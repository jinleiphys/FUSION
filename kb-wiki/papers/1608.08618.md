# Two-Particle Correlations in Heavy-Light Ion Collisions

> We study the initial, high-energy scatterings in heavy ion collisions using the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework. We focus on two-particle long-range rapidity correlations which are modeled as two-gluon correlations. We calculate the two-gluon production cross section using the saturation framework in the heavy-light ion regime, including all-order saturation effects in the heavy nucleus while considering only two-orders in the light ion. The two-gluon production cross section generates four types of long-range in rapidity correlations: (i) geometric correlations, (ii) Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) like correlations accompanied by a back-to-back maximum, (iii) near-side correlations, and (iv) away-side azimuthal correlations. The geometric correlations (i) are due to the fact that nucleons are correlated by simply being confined within the same nucleus. Correlations (iii) and (iv) have exactly the same amplitudes along with azimuthal and rapidity shapes: one centered around $Δϕ=0$ and the other one centered around $Δϕ=π$ (here $Δϕ$ is the azimuthal angle between the two produced gluons). The geometry dependence of the correlation function leads to stronger azimuthal near- and away-side correlations in the tip-on-tip U+U collisions than in the side-on-side U+U collisions, an exactly opposite behavior from the correlations generated by the elliptic flow of the quark-gluon plasma: a study of azimuthal correlations in the U+U collisions may help to disentangle the two sources of correlations. Finally we rewrite our result for the two-gluon production cross-section in a $k_T$-factorized form resulting in an expression involving a convolution of one- and two-gluon Wigner distributions over the transverse momenta and impact parameters. This differs from the $k_T$-factorized forms used in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper calculates two-gluon correlations in heavy-light ion collisions using the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework, finding that the resulting azimuthal correlations are symmetric and produce equal ridges on both the near-side (Δφ=0) and away-side (Δφ=π), with the caveat that these results are leading-order in saturation effects in the heavy nucleus but only two orders in the light ion, and sub-leading in the 1/Nc² expansion.

## Method

The author calculates the two-gluon production cross section in the heavy-light ion regime using the quasi-classical McLerran-Venugopalan/Golec-Biernat-Motyka (MV/GM) approximation, including all-order saturation effects in the heavy nucleus while considering only two orders in the light ion. The calculation involves evaluating correlators of Wilson lines in the adjoint representation, using a color basis decomposition of four s-channel gluons and exponentiating the interaction matrix in the large-Nc limit. The resulting cross section is analyzed for its azimuthal angular dependence to extract the correlation function.

## Context

This work extends the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework from single-gluon production in pA collisions (reviewed in the thesis) to two-gluon correlations in heavy-light ion collisions, building on prior work by Kovchegov (2012) and Kovchegov (2013). The theoretical prediction of an away-side ridge in gluon production was posted on the arXiv before the ALICE collaboration discovered the away-side ridge in p-Pb collisions at the LHC (Abelev, 2012). The results enable a way to distinguish correlations due to saturation effects from those due to hydrodynamic flow, particularly in U+U collisions where the geometry dependence is opposite. The paper also derives a kT-factorized form involving Wigner distributions (functions of both transverse momentum and impact parameter), which differs from the usual kT-factorized forms used in the literature.
