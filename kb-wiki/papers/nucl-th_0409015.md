# Spin asymmetry and GDH sum rule for real and virtual photons for the deuteron

> An explicit evaluation of the spin asymmetry of the deuteron and the associated GDH sum rule is presented which includes disintegration, single and double pion and eta production. For the GDH integral a large cancellation is found between the disintegration channel and the meson production channels. Furthermore, first results for the contribution of the disintegration channel to the generalized GDH integral at constant four-momentum transfer reveal a dominance of the isovector M1 transition to the $^1$S$_0$-state near threshold resulting in a negative contribution with a minimum around $Q^2\approx 0.2$ fm$^{-2}$ which is driven by the nucleon anomalous isovector magnetic moment.

# Spin asymmetry and GDH sum rule for real and virtual photons for the deuteron

## Key claim

The deuteron's Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule integral of 0.65 μb arises from a near-complete cancellation between a large negative contribution from photodisintegration (−381.52 μb) and large positive contributions from meson production channels (263.44 μb for single pion, 159.34 μb for double pion, and −13.95 μb for eta production), with the cancellation being sensitive to retardation effects in the potential and pion-exchange currents that were not fully accounted for in earlier static-interaction calculations.

## Method

The authors evaluated the spin asymmetry and GDH integral for the deuteron by separately calculating contributions from photodisintegration (γ + d → n + p), single and double pion production, and eta production. For photodisintegration, they used a retarded potential with retarded π-meson exchange currents, coupled-channel NΔ-dynamics, and relativistic contributions; for meson production, they employed the MAID model for elementary production operators with complete final-state interactions in the NN- and πN-subsystems. For virtual photons, they computed the generalized GDH integral at constant four-momentum transfer Q² for the electrodisintegration channel d(e,e')np using the Argonne V₁₈ potential with meson exchange currents, isobar configurations, and relativistic corrections.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron anomalous magnetic moment κ_d = −0.143 nuclear magnetons, giving a sum rule value I^{GDH}_d = 0.65 μb
- Sum of free proton and neutron GDH integrals: I^{GDH}_p + I^{GDH}_n = 438 μb
- Photodisintegration contribution to the finite GDH integral (integrated up to 0.8 GeV): −381.52 μb
- Single pion production contribution on deuteron (integrated up to 1.5 GeV): 263.44 μb
- Double pion production contribution on deuteron (integrated up to 2.2 GeV): 159.34 μb
- Eta production contribution on deuteron (integrated up to 1.5 GeV): −13.95 μb
- Total sum of all channels on deuteron: 27.31 μb, compared to the sum rule value of 0.65 μb
- For the generalized GDH integral of electrodisintegration, the minimum occurs at Q² ≈ 0.2 fm⁻², driven by the isovector M1 transition to the ¹S₀ state
- The minimum in the spin asymmetry for electrodisintegration occurs at final-state energy E_np ≈ 70 keV

## Context

This work extends earlier evaluations by Arenhövel and collaborators (1997) that used static interactions and exchange currents, and by Schwamb and Arenhövel (2001) that introduced retarded potentials. The results confirm that direct extraction of the neutron spin asymmetry from deuteron data is not possible due to significant differences between nucleon and deuteron spin asymmetries arising from Fermi motion and final-state interactions. The calculations enable future experimental tests of the generalized GDH integral for virtual photons and motivate independent checks using effective field theory, as well as extension to coherent and incoherent pion and eta electroproduction channels.
