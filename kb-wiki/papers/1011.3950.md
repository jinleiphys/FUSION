# Determining source cumulants in femtoscopy with Gram-Charlier and Edgeworth series

> Lowest-order cumulants provide important information on the shape of the emission source in femtoscopy. For the simple case of noninteracting identical particles, we show how the fourth-order source cumulant can be determined from measured cumulants in momentum space. The textbook Gram-Charlier series is found to be highly inaccurate, while the related Edgeworth series provides increasingly accurate estimates. Ordering of terms compatible with the Central Limit Theorem appears to play a crucial role even for nongaussian distributions.

# Determining source cumulants in femtoscopy with Gram-Charlier and Edgeworth series

## Key claim
The fourth-order source cumulant in coordinate space can be determined from measured cumulants in momentum space for noninteracting identical particles, but only if the Edgeworth series ordering is used rather than the textbook Gram-Charlier series; the Gram-Charlier series fails badly at any truncation order, while the Edgeworth series provides increasingly accurate estimates provided the measured momentum-space distribution is reasonably close to a gaussian.

## Method
The authors derived expressions for the second-order and fourth-order x-cumulants (coordinate-space cumulants) in terms of measured q-cumulants (momentum-space cumulants) using both Gram-Charlier Type A and Gauss-Edgeworth series expansions. They tested the accuracy of truncated expansions against exact analytic results from a Symmetric Normal Inverse Gaussian (SNIG) toy model, which provides closed-form expressions for both momentum-space and coordinate-space cumulants. The comparison was performed by computing percentage deviations of the truncated series approximations from the exact SNIG values as a function of the measured q-kurtosis.

## Key numbers
- At γ₄^q = 0 (gaussian limit), all series approximations become exact, with zero percentage deviation.
- For the Gram-Charlier series, even small nonzero values of γ₄^q lead to large percentage deviations in both κ₂^x and γ₄^x, and the deviations increase with increasing truncation order m.
- The Edgeworth series shows dramatically smaller percentage deviations than the Gram-Charlier series at all tested values of γ₄^q, and the Edgeworth approximations continue to improve as higher orders w are included.
- For the SNIG test case, the Edgeworth series simplifies such that the w=4 term contributions from ζ₄²H₈(0) = 0.091 γ₄² and ζ₆F₆H₆(0) = -0.104 γ₄² sum to -0.013 γ₄², demonstrating substantial cancellation within a given order.

## Context
This work extends prior femtoscopic imaging techniques (Brown and collaborators, 1997-2005) and cumulant-based approaches to nongaussian source shapes (Eggers and collaborators, 2006-2007). The authors show that the standard Gram-Charlier Type A series, commonly presented in textbooks (Kendall and Stuart, 1987; McCullagh, 1987), is unsuitable for extracting source cumulants from measured correlation functions, while the Edgeworth series derived by Cramer (1946) provides a robust alternative. The one-dimensional calculation establishes a framework that must be generalized to three dimensions using existing 3D machinery (Eggers and collaborators, 2006-2007) before application to experimental data, and the method avoids the ambiguity of a priori parametrization choices inherent in fitting approaches.
