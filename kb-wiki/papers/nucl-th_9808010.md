# Weak capture of protons by protons

> The cross section for the proton weak capture reaction $^1H(p,e^+ν_e)^2H$ is calculated with wave functions obtained from a number of modern, realistic high-precision interactions. To minimize the uncertainty in the axial two-body current operator, its matrix element has been adjusted to reproduce the measured Gamow-Teller matrix element of tritium $β$ decay in model calculations using trinucleon wave functions from these interactions. A thorough analysis of the ambiguities that this procedure introduces in evaluating the two-body current contribution to the pp capture is given. Its inherent model dependence is in fact found to be very weak. The overlap integral $Λ^2(E=0)$ for the pp capture is predicted to be in the range 7.05--7.06, including the axial two-body current contribution, for all interactions considered.

# Weak capture of protons by protons

## Key claim
The overlap integral Λ²(E=0) for the proton-proton weak capture reaction is predicted to lie in the narrow range 7.05–7.06 for all modern high-precision nucleon-nucleon interactions considered, after adjusting the axial two-body current operator to reproduce the measured Gamow-Teller matrix element of tritium β decay. The model dependence introduced by this adjustment procedure is found to be very weak.

## Method
The cross section for the reaction ¹H(p,e⁺νₑ)²H was calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation for the pp scattering and deuteron bound-state wave functions using five high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, CD-Bonn, Nijm-I, Nijm-II, and Reid93), including full electromagnetic interactions up to order α² (Coulomb, two-photon exchange, vacuum polarization, magnetic moment, and Darwin-Foldy terms). The axial two-body current operator was constrained by adjusting its cutoff masses and the N→Δ axial coupling constant to reproduce the experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium β decay, and then applied to compute the pp capture matrix element at zero energy.

## Key numbers
- Overlap integral Λ²(E=0) predicted range: 7.05–7.06 for all interactions considered, including the axial two-body current contribution.
- Ratio of D-wave to S-wave contribution to the one-body axial current matrix element at zero energy: approximately 0.00013 in the limit v_rel,n.r. → 0.
- Deuteron binding energy used: B_d = 2.224575 MeV, with corresponding γ = 0.2315380 fm⁻¹ from the relativistic relation.
- Fermi function parametrization at zero energy: f(0) = 0.144, about 3% smaller than the simple phase-space integral value of 0.148.
- Axial-to-vector coupling constant ratio: g_A = 1.2654 ± 0.0042, averaged from neutron decay measurements.
- Vector coupling constant: G_V = (1.14939 ± 0.00065) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻².
- Kinetic energy available to the final state at E=0: approximately 420 keV.
- Finite momentum transfer correction estimate: (q r_d)² ≈ (0.0042)², where q ≈ 420 keV and r_d ≈ 2 fm.

## Context
This work provides an integrated study of the pp weak capture reaction, extending the original calculation by Bethe and Critchfield (1938) and subsequent refinements by Salpeter (1952), Bahcall (1969), Gould and Guessoum (1990), and Kamionkowski and Bahcall (1994). It improves upon earlier work by using multiple modern high-precision nucleon-nucleon interactions with complete electromagnetic treatment and by systematically constraining the axial two-body current through tritium β decay data. The narrow predicted range for Λ² reduces theoretical uncertainty in the solar pp neutrino flux, which is critical for understanding the pp chain in main-sequence stars and for interpreting solar neutrino experiments.
