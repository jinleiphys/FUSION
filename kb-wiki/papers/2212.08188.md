# Ab initio in-medium similarity renormalization group for open-shell atomic systems

> Precise theoretical calculations of open-shell atomic systems are critical for extracting fundamental physics parameters from precision experiments. Here we present proof-of-principle calculations illustrating the effectiveness of the valence-space formulation of the ab initio in-medium similarity renormalization group, widely used in nuclear theory, as a new ab initio method for atomic systems. We adapt this approach to study properties of closed- and open-shell many-electron systems from helium to calcium. Ground-state energies, excitation spectra, and ionization energies are obtained for selected atoms, and reasonable agreement is found with benchmark coupled-cluster and many-body perturbation theory calculations, where available.

## Key claim

The valence-space formulation of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG), previously used in nuclear theory, is presented as a proof-of-principle non-perturbative many-body method for atomic systems; the authors find "reasonable agreement" with benchmark coupled-cluster and many-body perturbation theory calculations, but note that discrepancies grow for heavier elements and mid-period atoms due to missing relativistic and fine-structure corrections.

## Method

The authors apply the IMSRG to the non-relativistic many-electron Hamiltonian with a point-like nucleus, using a Laguerre orbital basis set (parameterized by truncation \(e_{\mathrm{max}}\) and inverse length scale \(\zeta\)) and normal-ordering with respect to the Hartree-Fock ground state. They employ the arctan variant of the White generator to evolve the Hamiltonian via a unitary flow, truncating induced three-body terms at the two-body level [IMSRG(2)], then decouple a valence-space Hamiltonian for open-shell atoms and diagonalize it exactly using the KSHELL code.

## Key numbers

- Helium ground-state energy: converged IMSRG values shown for \(e_{\mathrm{max}}=6,8\) as a function of \(\zeta\); Hartree-Fock ground state energy is \(E_{\mathrm{HF}}=-2.8616\ \mathrm{a.u.}\)
- Neon ground-state energy: converged IMSRG values shown for \(e_{\mathrm{max}}=8,10\); Hartree-Fock ground state energy is \(E_{\mathrm{HF}}=-128.5228\ \mathrm{a.u.}\); discrepancy with experiment is on the order of the fine-structure constant (\(\alpha \approx 1/137\))
- Ionization energies computed systematically up to atomic number \(Z=16\) for period 2 and period 3 elements, compared with NIST experimental data
- Two-electron binding energies for magnesium and calcium benchmarked against CI+MBPT calculations from Safronova (2009)

## Context

This work extends the VS-IMSRG method, originally developed for nuclear systems (Hergert 2016, Stroberg 2019), to atomic many-body systems as a complementary approach to coupled-cluster theory (Stopkowicz 2015) and CI+MBPT (Porsev 2001, Safronova 2009). The results enable future inclusion of relativistic corrections via Dirac-Hartree-Fock reference states and systematic calculations of isotope shifts relevant for laser-spectroscopy experiments, though the authors note that fine-structure contributions become relevant for elements beyond period 2 and that memory limitations currently restrict calculations to \(Z \leq 16\).
