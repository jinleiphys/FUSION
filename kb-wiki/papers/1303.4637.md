# Light-ion production from O, Si, Fe and Bi induced by 175 MeV quasi-monoenergetic neutron

> We have measured double-differential cross sections in the interaction of 175 MeV quasimonoenergetic neutrons with O, Si, Fe and Bi. We have compared these results with model calculations with INCL4.5-Abla07, MCNP6 and TALYS-1.2. We have also compared our data with PHITS calculations, where the pre-equilibrium stage of the reaction was accounted respectively using the JENDL/HE-2007 evaluated data library, the quantum molecular dynamics model (QMD) and a modified version of QMD (MQMD) to include a surface coalescence model. The most crucial aspect is the formation and emission of composite particles in the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Key claim

The paper reports that while most nuclear reaction models can reproduce proton production from 175 MeV neutron interactions, the crucial challenge is describing the formation and emission of composite particles (deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and alpha particles) in the pre-equilibrium stage, with no single model accurately reproducing all measured light-ion yields across all targets and angles.

## Method

Double-differential cross sections for light-ion (proton, deuteron, triton, helium-3, alpha) production were measured using the Medley spectrometer array at the The Svedberg Laboratory, employing a 175 MeV quasi-monoenergetic pulsed neutron beam with time-of-flight gating to suppress the low-energy neutron component. The experimental data were compared with calculations from INCL4.5-Abla07, MCNP6 (using CEM03.03), TALYS-1.2, and PHITS (with JENDL/HE-2007, QMD, and MQMD options), where all model calculations were folded with the experimental neutron spectrum for direct comparison.

## Key numbers

- Neutron beam peak energy: 175 MeV (quasi-monoenergetic)
- Detection angles: 20 degrees to 160 degrees in the laboratory system (eight angles)
- Proton identification threshold: 2 MeV
- Proton dynamic range: up to 170 MeV
- Targets measured: O, Si, Fe, and Bi (also C and U, but not reported in this paper)

## Context

This work extends earlier neutron-induced cross-section measurements at 96 MeV conducted at the same facility by Pomp and colleagues, addressing the scarcity of experimental data above 100 MeV identified by the nuclear data community (HPRL). The results enable benchmarking and improvement of models such as INCL4.5-Abla07, MCNP6, TALYS-1.2, and PHITS for accelerator-driven system applications, where reliable neutron-induced cross-section data in the 20-200 MeV intermediate energy region are needed. The paper specifically identifies that TALYS overestimates composite particle production via the Kalbach systematics, that MCNP6 underestimates forward-angle deuteron emission, and that introducing a coalescence model into QMD (MQMD) improves PHITS predictions but still lacks direct-like reaction mechanisms for forward-angle deuterons, tritons, and helium-3.
