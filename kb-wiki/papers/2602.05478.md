# Influence of Non-extensivity on the drag and diffusion coefficients of hadronic matter

> In this work, we investigate the drag and diffusion coefficients of various hadrons propagating through a hadronic thermal bath by employing the Fokker Planck equation within the framework of Tsallis nonextensive statistics. The nonextensive parameter $q$ accounts for the deviation from equilibrium and provides a more realistic description of the medium that is not perfectly thermalized. The hadronic bath, consisting of various mesonic and baryonic species, is characterized by different mass cutoffs that control the spectral composition of the medium. Our analysis shows that both the drag $F$ and momentum diffusion coefficients $Γ$ increases exponentially with temperature and increases systematically with increasing $q$ and mass cutoff. The spatial diffusion coefficient $D_x$ exhibits a decreasing trend with temperature $T$, $q$ and mass cutoff which highlights the significant influence of nonequilibrium effects and hadronic composition on the transport behaviour of hadrons, offering valuable insights into the thermal and dynamical properties of the hadronic phase preceding freezeout in heavy ion collisions. Additionally, we have studied the relaxation time of heavy mesons such as $D_0$, $J/ψ$ and $Υ$. We found that the heavier mesons relaxed later in comparison to the lighter mesons in the hadronic medium.

# Influence of Non-extensivity on the drag and diffusion coefficients of hadronic matter

## Key claim

The drag and diffusion coefficients of hadrons in a hadronic thermal bath increase with the Tsallis non-extensive parameter $q$ and with the hadronic mass cutoff, but only under the assumption that the medium is not perfectly thermalized and that the Fokker-Planck equation with constant cross sections provides a valid description; the spatial diffusion coefficient decreases with $q$ and mass cutoff, indicating stronger coupling in non-equilibrium conditions.

## Method

The authors employed the Fokker-Planck equation within Tsallis non-extensive statistics to compute drag coefficient $F$, momentum diffusion coefficient $\Gamma$, and spatial diffusion coefficient $D_x$ for hadrons propagating through a hadronic thermal bath. The hadronic bath was characterized by different mass cutoffs that control the spectral composition of the medium, and the Tsallis distribution $f_a^0 = [1-(q-1)E_a/T]^{q/(q-1)}$ was used instead of the Boltzmann-Gibbs distribution. Transport coefficients were calculated from thermal averages of scattering rates assuming constant cross sections, with the drag coefficient related to the inverse relaxation time via $F = \tilde{\tau}_a^{-1} = \sum_b n_b \langle \sigma_{ab} v_{ab} \rangle$.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient $F$ increases exponentially with temperature for all values of the Tsallis parameter $q$ and mass cutoffs studied.
- Relaxation-time ratio for $\Upsilon$ relative to $D_0$ lies in the range 2.3-2.9 over the considered temperature range.
- Relaxation-time ratio for $J/\psi$ relative to $D_0$ lies in the range 1.6-1.9 over the considered temperature range.
- Spatial diffusion coefficient $D_x$ decreases with increasing temperature, $q$, and mass cutoff, consistent with the relation $D_x = T/(mF)$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of drag and diffusion coefficients that assumed perfect thermal equilibrium described by Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, such as those using Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory (Laine, 2011) and empirical elastic scattering amplitudes (He, 2011). It also builds on effective field theory calculations (Ghosh, 2011; vanHees, 2004) and more recent work within the Color String Percolation Model (Goswami, 2022) and Van der Waals Hadron Resonance Gas framework (Goswami, 2023). The use of Tsallis statistics enables a more realistic description of the hadronic medium in heavy-ion collisions, where particle momentum spectra exhibit power-law tails rather than pure thermal distributions, and the results enable improved modeling of heavy meson transport through the hadronic phase preceding freeze-out.
