# Local chiral effective field theory interactions and quantum Monte Carlo applications

> We present details of the derivation of local chiral effective field theory interactions to next-to-next-to-leading order, and show results for nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and deuteron properties for these potentials. We then perform systematic auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations for neutron matter based on the developed local chiral potentials at different orders. This includes studies of the effects of the spectral-function regularization and of the local regulators. For all orders, we compare the quantum Monte Carlo results with perturbative many-body calculations and find excellent agreement for low cutoffs.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that local chiral effective field theory (EFT) nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) can be constructed and used in auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations of neutron matter, with the caveat that the excellent agreement with perturbative many-body calculations holds only for low cutoffs (coordinate-space cutoff $R_0 = 1.0-1.2$ fm).

## Method
The authors derived local chiral NN potentials by employing a local regulator in coordinate space and a spectral-function regularization (SFR) for two-pion exchange, with the contact interactions chosen to depend only on momentum transfer ${¥bf q}$ (except for a spin-orbit term). They fit the low-energy constants (LECs) to neutron-proton phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (PWA) at laboratory energies up to 150 MeV (or 100 MeV for $R_0=1.2$ fm) and to the proton-proton and neutron-neutron $^1S_0$ scattering lengths. They then performed systematic AFDMC calculations for neutron matter using these potentials at LO, NLO, and N$^2$LO, and compared the results to many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) calculations.

## Context
This work extends the local chiral potential approach first presented in Gezerlis (2013) by providing full details of the derivation, tables of LECs, and systematic AFDMC calculations for neutron matter. It enables quantum Monte Carlo simulations of neutron-rich systems using chiral EFT interactions, which were previously limited to nonlocal potentials unsuitable for such methods. The paper contrasts with the nonlocal chiral potentials of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (2005) by using a local regulator that preserves long-range physics while allowing continuum QMC simulations. The results for neutron matter agree with MBPT for low cutoffs, and the authors note that their fitting strategy differs from the optimized N$^2$LO potentials of Ekstrom (2013, 2014) by fitting only at low energies and taking the $c_i$ LECs from pion-nucleon scattering.
