# FUSION03, Concluding Remarks

> Fusion reactions below the Coulomb barrier provide new insights into multidimensional quantum tunneling, nuclear reaction dynamics and nuclear structure. These reactions are also of considerable interest to nuclear astrophysics. In this summary recent developments in the field are reviewed and open questions related to subbarrier fusion are presented.

## Key claim

This conference summary reviews recent developments in subbarrier fusion and presents open questions, noting that while the quality of experimental data has improved, many analysis techniques remain "simple-minded" and require more precise coupled-channels calculations.

## Method

The paper summarizes presentations and discussions from the FUSION03 conference, covering coupled-channels calculations, the nuclear potential puzzle (large surface diffuseness), fusion inhibition for heavy systems, fusion of unstable nuclei, electron screening in astrophysical reactions, and fusion in astrophysical settings. It reviews specific experimental results and theoretical approaches presented by conference speakers.

## Key numbers

- The surface diffuseness parameter needed to fit fusion data is typically 1.5 to 2 times the value required to fit elastic scattering data.
- Fusion inhibition is theoretically expected when the product of electric charges exceeds Z1Z2 ~ 1600, but experimental data show inhibition even at much smaller values.
- The system 60Ni+89Y shows a very steep fall-off of fusion data at extreme subbarrier energies that is hard to understand with the canonical diffuseness value.
- The 132Sn+64Ni system exhibits a very large subbarrier fusion enhancement.

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on subbarrier fusion reviewed by Balantekin (1997) and Adelberger (1998) for astrophysical applications. It highlights open questions including the electron screening puzzle where experimental data require larger shifts than the adiabatic approximation predicts, the need for better understanding of fusion inhibition and quasi-fission in symmetric systems, and the challenges of modeling fusion of halo nuclei. The paper enables future work by identifying specific theoretical problems such as coupling to resonances and the role of dissipative effects in coupled-channels codes.
