# Impact of the inelastic proton -- nucleus cross section on the prompt neutrino flux

> The description of the inelastic proton -- nucleus cross section at very high energies is still an open question. The current theoretical uncertainty has direct impact on the predictions of the cosmic ray and neutrino physics observables. In this paper we consider different models for the treatment of $σ_{inel}^{pA}$, compare its predictions at ultrahigh cosmic ray energies and estimate the prompt neutrino flux at the neutrino energies that have been probed by the IceCube Observatory. We demonstrate that depending of the model used to describe $σ_{inel}^{pA}$, the predictions for the prompt neutrino flux can differ by a factor of order of three. Such result demonstrate the importance of a precise measurement of the inelastic proton -- nucleus cross section at high energies.

## Key claim

The uncertainty in the modelling of the inelastic proton-Air cross section at high energies causes the predicted prompt neutrino flux at IceCube energies to vary by a factor of up to three, independent of the primary cosmic ray flux model used, demonstrating that this cross section is a major source of uncertainty that must be constrained by future measurements.

## Method

The authors used the semi-analytical Z-moment approach to solve coupled cascade equations for nucleons, heavy mesons, and leptons, calculating the prompt neutrino flux from charm hadron production. They considered four phenomenological models for the inelastic proton-Air cross section (QGSJET01, QGSJETII.03, Sibyll 2.1, and the EMM model) plus the MFEK analytical parametrization, using both a broken power-law (BPL) and the H3a spectrum for the primary cosmic ray flux, with charm production described by collinear factorization using CT14 parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The predictions for the inelastic proton-Air cross section from different models differ by approximately 40% at high energies, with the MFEK (EMM) prediction being an upper (lower) bound.
- The QGSJET01 and MFEK predictions for the prompt neutrino flux differ by a factor of approximately 3 in the kinematical range covered by the IceCube Observatory (neutrino energies less than about 3 x 10^6 GeV).
- The two versions of the QGSJET model (QGSJET01 and QGSJETII.03) differ by approximately 50% at low neutrino energies.
- The EMM model predicts a prompt neutrino flux that is smaller than the QGSJETII.03 prediction by approximately 40%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on prompt neutrino flux calculations (e.g., Rojo et al., Anna et al., Vicantoni et al.) that focused on heavy quark production factorization, parton distribution functions, and primary cosmic ray flux models, by specifically investigating the impact of the inelastic proton-Air cross section uncertainty. The results show that the modelling of this cross section is a critical source of uncertainty, complementing the Pierre Auger Collaboration (2012) measurement at sqrt(s) = 57 TeV, and motivate future precise measurements of the inelastic proton-Air cross section at cosmic ray energies to enable more reliable predictions for IceCube and future neutrino telescopes.
