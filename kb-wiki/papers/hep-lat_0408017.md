# Momentum dependence of the N to Delta transition form factors

> We present a new method to determine the momentum dependence of the N to Delta transition form factors and demonstrate its effectiveness in the quenched theory at $β=6.0$ on a $32^3 \times 64$ lattice. We address a number of technical issues such as the optimal combination of matrix elements and the simultaneous overconstrained analysis of all lattice vector momenta contributing to a given momentum transfer squared, $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new lattice method to determine the momentum dependence of the N to Delta transition form factors, demonstrating that the electric quadrupole (E2) and Coulomb quadrupole (C2) amplitudes are non-zero at low momentum transfer squared, but only within the quenched approximation at a single lattice spacing and with Wilson fermions.

## Method

The authors compute the N to Delta transition form factors using quenched lattice QCD on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice at \(\beta=6.0\) with Wilson fermions, employing a fixed-sink approach for the three-point function. They introduce two novel techniques: a symmetric combination of \(\Delta\) spin indices (sink \(S_1\)) that maximizes the number of statistically independent lattice measurements, and an overconstrained analysis that simultaneously fits all lattice momentum vectors contributing to a given \(Q^2\) using singular value decomposition. The form factors are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point correlation functions at three values of the hopping parameter \(\kappa\).

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: \(32^3 \times 64\) at \(\beta=6.0\), with inverse lattice spacing \(a^{-1} = 2.04(2)\) GeV set from the nucleon mass in the chiral limit.
- Three values of \(\kappa\) used: 0.1554, 0.1558, and 0.1562, corresponding to pion-to-rho mass ratios \(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.64\), 0.59, and 0.50 respectively.
- 200 configurations analyzed for the final results; the fixed-sink method initially produced errors almost three times larger than the fixed-current approach, but the new analysis reduced errors to match the fixed-current approach.
- The ratio EMR (E2/M1) in the chiral limit agrees with experimental measurements, while CMR (C2/M1) is less negative than experiment at low \(Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice calculations of N to Delta transition form factors by Leinweber and others, which used the fixed-current approach, by introducing a fixed-sink method that allows evaluation at all momentum transfers from a single inversion. The results are compared to experimental data extracted using the phenomenological MAID model, and the authors suggest that the discrepancy in CMR at low \(Q^2\) may be due to the quenched approximation, making it a probe of unquenching effects in future calculations.
