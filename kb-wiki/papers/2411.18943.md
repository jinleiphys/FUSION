# Enhanced nuclear Schiff and electric dipole moments in nuclei with an octupole deformation

> Deformed nuclei exhibit enhanced moments that violate time-reversal invariance ($T$) and parity ($P$). This paper focuses on the enhanced nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM) and Schiff moment present in nuclei with octupole deformation (pear-shaped nuclei). These moments, which are proportional to the octupole deformation, have a collective nature and are large in the intrinsic frame that rotates with the nucleus. However, in a state with definite angular momentum and parity, $T$ and $P$ conservation forbid their expectation values in the laboratory frame, as nuclear rotation causes them to vanish. In nuclei with octupole deformation, close opposite-parity rotational states with identical spin are mixed by $T$,$P$-violating nuclear forces. This mixing polarises the nuclear axis along the nuclear spin, allowing moments from the intrinsic frame to manifest in the laboratory frame, provided the nuclear spin $I$ is sufficiently large. Using half-life data for $E1$ transitions from the NuDat database, we calculate the intrinsic nuclear EDM $d_{\text{int}}$ for a range of nuclei theorised to exhibit octupole deformation. From these values, we independently estimate the intrinsic nuclear Schiff moment $S_{\text{int}}$ and the octupole deformation parameter $β_{3}$. Finally, we compare the magnitude of these collective moments in the laboratory frame with the contributions from valence nucleons, providing an estimate of the nuclear EDM and Schiff moment components unrelated to octupole deformation. The uncertainty of our estimates may exceed a factor of 10.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that in nuclei with octupole deformation (pear-shaped nuclei), the intrinsic nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM) and Schiff moment, which are large in the rotating nuclear frame, can manifest in the laboratory frame only when opposite-parity rotational states of identical spin are mixed by T,P-violating nuclear forces and the nuclear spin I is sufficiently large; the authors caution that the uncertainty of their estimates may exceed a factor of 10.

## Method

The authors used half-life data for E1 transitions from the NuDat database to calculate the intrinsic nuclear EDM d_int for a range of nuclei theorised to exhibit octupole deformation, employing the rotational model formula that links the reduced E1 transition probability to the intrinsic dipole moment. From these d_int values, they independently estimated the intrinsic nuclear Schiff moment S_int and the octupole deformation parameter beta_3 using relations derived from the hydrodynamic liquid-drop model. They then compared the magnitude of these collective moments in the laboratory frame with contributions from valence nucleons, providing estimates of nuclear EDM and Schiff moment components unrelated to octupole deformation.

## Key numbers

*   Intrinsic nuclear Schiff moment |S_int| for 153Eu: 9.5 e fm^3.
*   Octupole deformation parameter beta_3 for 153Eu: 0.095.
*   Laboratory-frame Schiff moment |S_lab| for 153Eu: 0.30 bar-theta e fm^3.
*   Laboratory-frame EDM |d_lab| for 153Eu: 0.37 x 10^{-2} bar-theta e fm.
*   Intrinsic nuclear EDM |d_int| for 225Ra: 0.24 e fm (nuclear spin I = 1/2).
*   Octupole deformation parameter beta_3 for 225Ra: 0.15.
*   Laboratory-frame Schiff moment |S_lab| for 225Ra: 2.2 bar-theta e fm^3.
*   Intrinsic nuclear EDM |d_int| for 233U: 0.82 e fm (nuclear spin I = 5/2).
*   Octupole deformation parameter beta_3 for 233U: 0.39.
*   The dimensionless strength constant eta of the nuclear T,P-violating potential is related to the QCD theta-term constant by eta_n = -eta_p = 4 x 10^5 bar-theta.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Auerbach (1996) and Spevak (1997), who first proposed that octupole-deformed nuclei exhibit enhanced Schiff moments due to mixing of opposite-parity doublet states by T,P-violating forces. The authors provide semi-empirical estimates for a wider range of candidate nuclei than previous numerical calculations by Engel (2003) and Dobaczewski (2018), which were limited to isotopes like 225Ra, and include stable and long-lived isotopes such as 153Eu, 155Gd, and 161Dy as proposed by Flambaum (2020) and Feldmeier (2020). The results are intended to guide future many-body nuclear calculations and experimental searches for T,P-violating effects, including those motivated by axion dark matter detection experiments such as CASPEr.
