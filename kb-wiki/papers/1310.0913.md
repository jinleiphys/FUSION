# Appearance of a quark matter phase in hybrid stars

> The appearance of quark matter in the core of hybrid stars is a fundamental issue in such compact stars. The central density of these stars is sufficiently high such that nuclear matter undergoes a further change into other exotic phases that consist of hyperons and quarks. However, the equation of state (EOS) for the high-density matter is still not clear and several recent observations have indicated the limitations of the EOSs; theoretical studies should try to elucidate the EOSs. It is believed that the inner regions of the stars should consist of a mixed hadron-quark phase. We study the mixed hadron-quark phase, taking into account finite-size effects, and find that that the mixed phase should be restricted to a narrower region. Therefore, a quark matter phase should appear in the central region.

# Appearance of a quark matter phase in hybrid stars

## Key claim
The mixed hadron-quark phase in hybrid stars is restricted to a narrower region when finite-size effects (specifically charge screening) are taken into account, which means that a pure quark matter phase should appear in the central region of such stars, contrary to the expectation that the inner regions consist entirely of a mixed phase.

## Method
The author uses density functional theory under the local density approximation with a sharp boundary between hadronic and quark phases (bag model) and a surface tension parameter σ, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for non-rotating stars and then extending the equation of state (EOS) to stationary rigidly rotating stars in general relativity. Calculations are performed both with and without charge screening to isolate its effect on the mixed phase structure.

## Key numbers
- The mass difference between screened and unscreened cases around the maximum mass is about 0.05 solar masses (0.05 M⊙), which is small compared to the total mass of the star.
- For the non-rotating case without screening, the hybrid star radius is 12.6 km; with screening, the radius is 12.9 km.
- For rotating stars, the equatorial radius (Req) and polar radius (Rp) become different at rotation rates of 400 Hz or faster.

## Context
This work extends the study of structured mixed phases (SMPs) in hybrid stars originally proposed by Heiselberg et al. and Glendenning and Pei, who suggested crystalline structures such as droplets, rods, slabs, tubes, and bubbles in the mixed phase. The author's previous work (Endo, 2013) developed the EOS with charge screening, and this paper applies that EOS to both static and rotating stars. The results enable comparison with observations of rapidly rotating neutron stars and open the question of whether quark matter spin polarization (Tatsumi, 2009) could explain magnetic fields in compact stars. The author notes that kaon condensation studies (Maruyama, 2012) show similar results to this work.
