# Double scattering production of two positron-electron pairs in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions

> We present first measurable predictions for electromagnetic (two-photon) double scattering production of two positron-electron pairs in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at LHC. Measureable cross sections are obtained with realistic cuts on electron/positron (pseudo)rapidities and transverse momenta for the ALICE and ATLAS or CMS experiments. The predictions for total and differential cross sections are presented. We show also two-dimensional distributions in rapidities of the opposite-sign (from the same or different subcollisions) and of the same-sign ($e^+ e^+$ or $e^- e^-$) electrons and in rapidity distance between them. Expected number of events are presented and discussed. Our calculations strongly suggest that relevant measurements with the help of ATLAS, CMS and ALICE detectors are possible in a near future.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first measurable predictions for double-scattering production of two electron-positron pairs in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, claiming that cross sections are large enough to be observed with realistic detector cuts, but only when transverse momentum cuts are applied to keep the per-collision probability small enough that unitarization corrections are not needed.

## Method

The authors calculate the double-scattering cross section by factorizing it into the product of two independent single-pair production probabilities, each obtained from the equivalent-photon approximation with realistic nuclear charge form factors. They first compute the differential probability for single \(e^+e^-\) production on a three-dimensional grid in impact parameter and lepton rapidities, then integrate the product of two such probabilities over impact parameter with a combinatorial factor of 1/2 for identical pairs. The numerical integration is performed using the VEGAS Monte Carlo program, with a grid-type integration used for cross-checks.

## Key numbers

* For PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV with \(p_t > 0.2\) GeV and no rapidity cut, the integrated cross section is 52.525 \(\mu\)b, corresponding to 52,525 events for an integrated luminosity of 1 nb\(^{-1}\).
* With ALICE-like cuts (\(p_t > 0.2\) GeV, \(|y_e| < 1\)), the cross section is 0.649 \(\mu\)b, giving 649 events per 1 nb\(^{-1}\).
* With ATLAS/CMS main tracker cuts (\(p_t > 0.5\) GeV, \(|y_e| < 2.5\)), the cross section is 0.235 \(\mu\)b, yielding 235 events per 1 nb\(^{-1}\).
* With ATLAS/CMS forward calorimeter coverage (\(p_t > 0.3\) GeV, \(|y_e| < 4.9\)), the cross section is 7.447 \(\mu\)b, giving 7,447 events per 1 nb\(^{-1}\).
* For \(p_t > 1\) GeV and \(|y_e| < 2.5\), the cross section drops to 9.8 nb, corresponding to 10 events per 1 nb\(^{-1}\).
* The four-pion background from double \(\rho^0\) production at \(p_{t,\text{min}} = 0.3\) GeV and \(|y| < 4.9\) is 2.954 mb, about 400 times larger than the four-electron signal (7.447 \(\mu\)b) under the same cuts.

## Context

This work extends the study of double-scattering mechanisms in ultraperipheral collisions, previously applied to \(\rho^0\rho^0\) production by Klein and Nystrand (2004) and by Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek (2014), to the electromagnetic production of two lepton pairs. The authors note that the double-scattering mechanism for \(\rho^0\rho^0\) was insufficient to explain STAR data on four-pion production, making the \(e^+e^-e^+e^-\) channel a cleaner candidate for first unambiguous verification of double scattering in UPC. The predictions enable experimental searches by ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS, and the authors suggest that future calculations of the single-scattering \(\gamma\gamma \to e^+e^-e^+e^-\) contribution will be needed for exact comparison with data.
