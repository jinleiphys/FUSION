# Asymmetry of Strange Sea in Nucleons

> Based on the finite-temperature field theory, we evaluate the medium effects in nucleon which can induce an asymmetry between quarks and antiquarks of the strange sea. The short-distance effects determined by the weak interaction can give rise to $δm\equiv Δm_s-Δm_{\bar s}$ where $Δm_{s(\bar s)}$ is the medium-induced mass of strange quark by a few KeV at most, but the long-distance effects by strong interaction are sizable. Our numerical results show that there exists an obvious mass difference between strange and anti-strange quarks, as large as 10-100 MeV.

# Asymmetry of Strange Sea in Nucleons

## Key claim
The asymmetry between strange quarks and antiquarks in the nucleon sea arises from the asymmetric environment of light quarks (u and d) in nucleons, not from any intrinsic asymmetry in the interactions themselves. The authors find that long-distance strong interaction effects mediated by kaons can produce a mass difference of 10-100 MeV, while short-distance weak interaction effects contribute at most a few KeV.

## Method
The authors employ finite-temperature field theory to compute the self-energy corrections to strange quark and antiquark masses in the asymmetric medium of a nucleon, where the net light quark numbers differ (two u and one d in proton; one u and two d in neutron). They evaluate two classes of contributions: short-distance effects from W and Z boson exchange at the quark level, and long-distance effects from kaon exchange using an effective chiral Lagrangian with a pseudovector coupling between quarks and mesons. The medium effects are incorporated through the thermal propagator with a Fermi-Dirac distribution function, and the results are expressed as functions of the effective nucleon radius R.

## Key numbers
- Short-distance contribution to the strange-antistrange mass difference in proton: 92 eV to 0.8 KeV, for effective nucleon radius R = 0.5-1.0 fm
- Short-distance contribution to the strange-antistrange mass difference in neutron: 0.38 KeV to 3.0 KeV, for effective nucleon radius R = 0.5-1.0 fm
- Long-distance contribution yields a mass difference of 10-100 MeV, consistent with the estimate from Ji and Tang (2001) of delta m approximately 40 MeV from CCFR data
- For current quark masses (m_s = 150 MeV, m_u = m_d = 6 MeV), delta m can reach approximately 100 MeV at R = 0.5 fm
- For constituent quark masses (m_s = 500 MeV, m_u = m_d = 350 MeV), the mass difference is even larger

## Context
This work extends the meson-cloud model approach of Signal and Thomas (1987) and the light-cone model of Brodsky and Ma (1996) for strange-antistrange asymmetry, using finite-temperature field theory as a formal framework. The authors also connect to the analysis of CCFR data by Ji and Tang (2001), who found m_s = 260 +/- 70 MeV and m_bar_s = 220 +/- 70 MeV from the data. The results suggest that the strange sea asymmetry is a natural consequence of the asymmetric light quark environment in nucleons, with the dominant mechanism being low-energy strong interactions via kaon exchange rather than weak interactions. This work enables further investigation of the strangeness content of nucleons and the role of chiral symmetry restoration at high temperature and density.
