# Light new physics and the $τ$ lepton dipole moments: prospects at Belle II

> While electron and muon dipole moments are well-established precision probes of physics beyond the standard model, it is notoriously challenging to test realistic new-physics (NP) scenarios for the $τ$ lepton. Constructing suitable asymmetries in $e^+e^-\toτ^+τ^-$ has emerged as a promising such avenue, providing access to the electric and magnetic dipole moment once a polarized electron beam is available, e.g., with the proposed polarization upgrade of the SuperKEKB $e^+e^-$ collider. However, this interpretation relies on an effective-field-theory (EFT) argument that only applies if the NP scale is large compared to the center-of-mass energy. In this Letter we address the consequences of the asymmetry measurements in the case of light NP, using light spin-0 and spin-1 bosons as test cases, to show how results can again be interpreted as constraints on dipole moments, albeit in a model-dependent manner, and how the decoupling to the EFT limit proceeds in these cases. In particular, we observe that the imaginary parts generated by light new particles can yield nonvanishing asymmetries even without electron polarization, which can again be interpreted as constraints on the $τ$ anomalous magnetic moment. This proposed measurement, thus, presents a novel opportunity for NP searches that can be realized already with present data at Belle II.

# Light new physics and the τ lepton dipole moments: prospects at Belle II

## Key claim
While asymmetry measurements in $e^+e^-\to\tau^+\tau^-$ at Belle II can probe the τ dipole moments, the direct interpretation as $a_\tau^\text{NP}$ and $d_\tau^\text{NP}$ via an effective-field-theory argument only holds if the new-physics scale is large compared to the center-of-mass energy; for light new physics (mediator masses $m_\text{NP}^2 \lesssim s$), the interpretation becomes model-dependent, though constraints can still be extracted. The imaginary parts generated by light new particles can yield nonvanishing asymmetries even without electron polarization, providing a novel opportunity that can be realized with present Belle II data.

## Method
The authors compute one-loop contributions of light spin-0 and spin-1 bosons to the electromagnetic form factors $F_2(s)$ and $F_3(s)$ of the $\tau\tau\gamma$ vertex in $e^+e^-\to\tau^+\tau^-$, parameterizing the new-physics interactions via effective Lagrangians with Yukawa-like couplings for scalars and vector/axial-vector couplings for vectors, including possible Chern-Simons terms. They then relate the real and imaginary parts of these form factors to the asymmetries $A_T^\pm$, $A_L^\pm$, $A_{N,F_3}^\pm$ (requiring polarized electron beams) and $A_N^\pm$, $A_{T,F_3}^\pm$ (accessible without polarization), and evaluate the sensitivity to $a_\tau$ and $d_\tau$ at $\sqrt{s_B}=10.58\ \text{GeV}$ assuming a future sensitivity of $10^{-6}$ on the effective form factors.

## Key numbers
- The assumed future sensitivity on $\Re F_{2,3}(s_B)$ and $\Im F_{2,3}(s_B)$ is $10^{-6}$ at the Belle II center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_B}=10.58\ \text{GeV}$.
- For large mediator masses, the sensitivity to $a_\tau$ via $\Re F_{2,3}$ approaches a constant value coinciding with the assumed $10^{-6}$ sensitivity on $\Re F_{2,3}^\text{eff}$.
- The typical sensitivity to $a_\tau$ stays below $10^{-5}$ even for small mediator masses, apart from accidental cancellations.
- The Higgs-mediated one-loop contribution to $\Im F_2$ in the Standard Model is estimated as $\Im F_2^h \simeq 10^{-9}$, well below the projected sensitivity of $10^{-6}$.
- Near-threshold enhancement at $\sqrt{s} \simeq 2m_\tau$ can improve sensitivity by up to one order of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the asymmetry-based approach for measuring τ dipole moments at $e^+e^-$ colliders, originally proposed by Bernabeu et al. (2004-2008) at the Υ resonances and later developed for Belle II by Crivellin et al. (2021) and the Belle II Group (2022), including radiative corrections by Gogniat et al. (2025). While previous work assumed heavy new physics where SMEFT applies, this paper addresses the case of light mediators (axions, axionlike particles, light scalars, and light vector bosons) that are actively studied in the literature. The results enable model-dependent constraints on τ dipole moments from Belle II data even without the planned polarization upgrade, and motivate similar measurements at BESIII or a future Super Tau-Charm Factory.
