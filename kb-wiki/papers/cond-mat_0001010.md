# Chaos in the thermodynamic limit

> We study chaos in the Hamiltonian Mean Field model (HMF), a system with many degrees of freedom in which $N$ classical rotators are fully coupled. We review the most important results on the dynamics and the thermodynamics of the HMF, and in particular we focus on the chaotic properties.We study the Lyapunov exponents and the Kolmogorov--Sinai entropy, namely their dependence on the number of degrees of freedom and on energy density, both for the ferromagnetic and the antiferromagnetic case.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chaos (quantified by the largest Lyapunov exponent and Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy) persists in the thermodynamic limit for the ferromagnetic Hamiltonian Mean Field model due to a second-order phase transition, but vanishes for the antiferromagnetic case where no phase transition occurs, contrasting with short-range systems where chaos always survives.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the equations of motion for N classical rotators (up to N=20000) using a fourth-order symplectic algorithm with energy conservation errors of order 10^-5 (or 10^-12 at very low energy). They computed the full Lyapunov spectrum via the standard method of Benettin et al., extracting the largest Lyapunov exponent and the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy as the sum of positive Lyapunov exponents, studying their dependence on energy density U and system size N for both ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the ferromagnetic case near U=0, the largest Lyapunov exponent scales as lambda_1 proportional to U^(1/2) with exponent beta=1/2
- For U much larger than the critical energy U_c=0.75, lambda_1 decays as N^(-1/3) in the ferromagnetic case
- The critical temperature for the ferromagnetic case is T_c=0.5, corresponding to critical energy U_c=0.75
- The antiferromagnetic ground state energy is U=-1/2
- For the antiferromagnetic case at very low energies, lambda_1 scales as (U+1/2)^(1/2)
- Superdiffusion with exponent alpha=1.38 +/- 0.05 is observed in the ferromagnetic case for energy range 0.5 less than U less than 0.75
- The antiferromagnetic case shows lambda_1 proportional to N^(-1/3) for all energies above the very low energy region

## Context

This work extends studies of Lyapunov exponents in Hamiltonian systems with short-range interactions (e.g., Fermi-Pasta-Ulam lattices) by Ruelle (1979) and Livi, Politi, and Ruffo (1992), which found that the largest Lyapunov exponent reaches a finite energy-dependent value in the thermodynamic limit. The paper contrasts with those results by showing that for long-range interactions, chaos can vanish in the thermodynamic limit when no phase transition is present. The findings enable deeper understanding of the connection between microscopic chaos and macroscopic thermodynamics, with relevance to nuclear multifragmentation studies by Bonasera, Latora, and Rapisarda (1995) and atomic cluster physics.
