# The role of hidden-charm pentaquark resonance $P^+_c(4450)$ in $J/ψ$ photoproduction on nuclei near threshold

> We study the $J/ψ$ photoproduction from nuclei at near-threshold incident photon energies of 5--11 GeV within the nuclear spectral function approach by considering incoherent direct ($γN \to {J/ψ}N$) and two-step ($γp \to P^+_c(4450)$, $P^+_c(4450) \to {J/ψ}p$) photon--nucleon charmonium creation processes. We calculate the absolute and relative excitation functions within the different scenarios for in-medium modification of the directly photoproduced $J/ψ$ mesons. We show that the overall subthreshold $J/ψ$ production in $γA$ reactions reveals some sensitivity to adopted in-medium modification scenarios for $J/ψ$ mesons only if branching ratio $Br[P^+_c(4450) \to {J/ψ}p]$ $\sim$ 1% and less. Our studies also demonstrate that the presence of the $P^+_c(4450)$ resonance in $J/ψ$ photoproduction produces above threshold additional enhancements in the behavior of the total $J/ψ$ creation cross section on nuclei, which could be also studied in the future JLab experiments at the upgraded up to 12 GeV CEBAF facility to provide further evidence for its existence.

## Key claim

The overall subthreshold $J/\psi$ production in $\gamma A$ reactions reveals sensitivity to adopted in-medium modification scenarios for $J/\psi$ mesons only if the branching ratio $Br[P^+_c(4450) \to J/\psi p]$ is $\sim$1% or less; for larger branching ratios (3–5%), the resonant contribution masks the mass shift signal.

## Method

The authors extended the nuclear spectral function approach of Paryev and Kiselev (previous work) to include both direct non-resonant $J/\psi$ photoproduction ($\gamma N \to J/\psi N$) and a two-step resonant process ($\gamma p \to P^+_c(4450)$, $P^+_c(4450) \to J/\psi p$) on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb targets. They computed absolute and relative excitation functions for incident photon energies of 5–11 GeV, adopting five scenarios for the $J/\psi$ in-medium mass shift at saturation density ($V_0 = 0, -25, -50, -100, -150$ MeV) and three options for the $P^+_c(4450) \to J/\psi p$ branching ratio (1%, 3%, 5%). The $P^+_c(4450)$ resonance was described with both free and in-medium Breit-Wigner spectral functions, including a collisional width from $P^+_c(4450)N$ inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- $J/\psi$ threshold photoproduction energy on a free nucleon at rest: 8.2 GeV.
- Resonant energy for $P^+_c(4450)$ photoproduction on a free proton at rest: 10.08 GeV.
- $P^+_c(4450)$ pole mass: 4.45 GeV; vacuum total decay width: 39 MeV.
- $J/\psi$–nucleon absorption cross section used: $\sigma_{J/\psi N} = 3.5$ mb.
- $P^+_c(4450)$–nucleon inelastic cross section estimated: $\sigma_{P_cN} \approx 33.5$ mb (sum of 3.5 mb $J/\psi N$ and 30 mb $pN$ inelastic).
- Effective $P^+_c(4450)$ absorption cross section: $\sigma^{\rm eff}_{P_cN} \approx 43.5$ mb (including decay contribution $\sigma_{\rm dec} \approx 10$ mb).
- Average collisional width for $P^+_c(4450)$: $\langle \Gamma_{\rm coll} \rangle \approx 120$ MeV for $^{12}$C and $\approx 190$ MeV for $^{208}$Pb at $E_\gamma = 10.08$ GeV.
- Free total cross section parametrization for $\gamma N \to J/\psi N$: $\sigma = 11.1 (1-x)^2$ nb, where $x = E^{\rm thr}_\gamma / E_\gamma$.
- Branching ratio relation: $Br[P^+_c(4450) \to \gamma p] = 7.5 \times 10^{-3} \times Br[P^+_c(4450) \to J/\psi p]$.

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach of Paryev and Kiselev (previous work) for direct $J/\psi$ photoproduction on nuclei by adding the two-step resonant channel via the $P^+_c(4450)$ pentaquark, which was discovered by the LHCb Collaboration (2015) in $\Lambda^0_b \to K^- (J/\psi p)$ decays. The study is motivated by planned JLab 12 GeV experiments using the SoLID, CLAS12, HMS, and SHMS detectors. The results indicate that subthreshold $J/\psi$ excitation functions can probe $J/\psi$ in-medium mass shifts of -50 MeV or more only if the $P^+_c(4450) \to J/\psi p$ branching ratio is 1% or less, while above-threshold enhancements from the resonance could provide further evidence for its existence.
