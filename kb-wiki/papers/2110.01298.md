# Single-particle and collective structures in neutron-rich Sr isotopes

> Neutron-rich Sr nuclei around N=60 exhibit a sudden shape transition from spherical ground state to strongly prolate-deformed. Recently, a lot of new insight into the structure of Sr isotopes in this region was gained through experimental studies of excited levels, transitions strengths and spectroscopic factors. In this work, a "classic" shell-model description of strontium isotopes from N=50 to N=58 is provided, using a natural valence space outside the 78Ni core. Both even-even and even-odd isotopes are adressed. In particular, spectroscopic factors are computed to shed more light on the structure of low-energy excitations and their evolution along the Sr chain. The origin of deformation at N=60 is commented in the context of the present and previous shell-model and Monte-Carlo shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper provides a "classic" shell-model description of strontium isotopes from N=50 to N=58 using a natural valence space outside the 78Ni core, showing that the sudden shape transition at N=60 can be understood within this framework, though the origin of deformation is commented on in the context of both the present results and previous shell-model and Monte-Carlo shell-model calculations rather than being fully resolved.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for even-even and even-odd Sr isotopes in the neutron-rich region from N=50 to N=58, using a valence space outside the doubly-magic 78Ni core. Spectroscopic factors were computed for low-energy excitations to study their structure and evolution along the Sr isotopic chain. The results were compared with experimental data on excited levels, transition strengths, and spectroscopic factors to assess the shell-model description.

## Key numbers

- The study covers Sr isotopes from N=50 to N=58, with the sudden shape transition occurring at N=60.
- Spectroscopic factors were computed for low-energy excitations across the Sr chain from N=50 to N=58.
- The valence space is defined outside the 78Ni core (Z=28, N=50).

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich Sr isotopes by providing a systematic description from N=50 to N=58 using a consistent valence space, complementing recent experimental studies of excited levels and transition strengths in this region. The results are compared with earlier shell-model and Monte-Carlo shell-model calculations that addressed the shape transition at N=60. The calculations enable future comparisons with experimental spectroscopic factors and provide a baseline for understanding how deformation develops as neutrons are added toward N=60.
