# Forward dijet and vector meson production at the LHC

> We first describe the predictions concerning jet production in the very forward region, especially in the FOCAL kinematic region of the ALICE collaboration at CERN as a test of saturation. We also compare vector meson production cross sections at the LHC in Pb Pb and p Pb collisions with the Balitsky Fadin Kuraev Lipatov and the Balitsky Kovchegov evolution equations and data favor the observation of saturation at the LHC.

## Key claim

Forward dijet and vector meson production data at the LHC favor the presence of gluon saturation effects, but the claim is conditional: for vector mesons, saturation is clearly favored only in PbPb collisions at high center-of-mass energy W, not in pPb collisions where the gluon density is probed in the proton; for forward jets, the model predicts lower saturation scales than the naive A^{1/3} dependence, but the predictions await experimental test with the FOCAL detector.

## Method

The authors compute forward jet cross sections in pPb collisions using a dipole model that mixes the AAMQS parametrization (without impact parameter dependence) with the IPSat model (with impact parameter dependence but no BK evolution), constructing the Pb thickness as the sum of Gaussian nucleon thicknesses. For exclusive vector meson production (J/Psi and Upsilon) in ultra-peripheral collisions, they factorize the gamma to q-qbar splitting from the dipole density in protons or Pb, evolving the dipole densities using both the linear BFKL and the non-linear BK evolution equations, with b-dependence included via a Gaussian thickness for protons and the Wood-Saxon formalism for Pb.

## Key numbers

- Forward jet kinematics probe x values close to 10^{-5} on the Pb side in pPb collisions.
- Forward CMS detector kinematics: 3.5 < y_jet < 4.5 with p_T^jet between 10-20 GeV, 20-40 GeV, or 40-80 GeV.
- CASTOR/FOCAL kinematics: 5.2 < y_jet < 6.6 with p_T^jet between 5-10 GeV or 10-20 GeV.
- Saturation scales are computed for p, O, Cu, Xe, and Pb at three x values: x = 2e-5, 0.0004, and 0.003; the model yields lower saturation scales than the naive A^{1/3} dependence.
- For J/Psi production in PbPb collisions, data favor the BK evolution over BFKL, with saturation effects appearing more important in data than predicted by BK.
- For Upsilon production, BFKL and BK give similar cross sections for gamma-p interactions, and smaller differences between BFKL and BK are observed in gamma-Pb interactions than for J/Psi due to the higher Upsilon mass.

## Context

This work extends the study of saturation effects at the LHC by combining forward jet predictions (using a novel b-dependent model that sums nucleon thicknesses) with vector meson production comparisons. The vector meson analysis builds on prior measurements from H1 and ZEUS at HERA and from CMS, ALICE, and LHCb at the LHC, comparing against BFKL and BK evolution equations. The forward jet predictions are designed to be testable with the upcoming FOCAL detector in ALICE, while the vector meson results indicate that precise Upsilon measurements in PbPb UPC collisions could probe the transition region between saturation and dilute regimes.
