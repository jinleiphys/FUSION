# Soft pions and transport near the chiral critical point

> During the expansion of a heavy ion collision, the system passes close to the $O(4)$ critical point of QCD, and thus the fluctuations of the order parameter $(σ, \vecπ)$ are expected to be enhanced. Our goal is to compute how these enhanced fluctuations modify the transport coefficients of QCD near the pseudo-critical point. We also make a phenomenological estimate for how chiral fluctuations could effect the momentum spectrum of soft pions. We first formulate the appropriate stochastic hydrodynamic equations close to the $O(4)$ critical point. Then, working in mean field, we determine the correlation functions of the stress tensor and the currents which result from this stochastic real time theory, and use these correlation functions to determine the scaling behavior of the transport coefficients. The hydrodynamic theory also describes the propagation of pion waves, fixing the scaling behavior of the dispersion curve of soft pions. We present scaling functions for the shear viscosity and the charge conductivities near the pseudo-critical point, and estimate the absolute magnitude of the critical fluctuations to these parameters and the bulk viscosity. Using the calculated pion dispersion curve, we estimate the expected critical enhancement of soft pion yields, and this estimate provides a plausible explanation for the excess seen in experiment relative to ordinary hydrodynamic computations. Our results motivate further phenomenological and numerical work on the implications of chiral symmetry on real time properties of thermal QCD near the pseudo-critical point.

## Key claim

The authors compute how enhanced fluctuations of the chiral order parameter near the O(4) critical point modify transport coefficients of QCD, working in a mean-field approximation that provides a qualitative understanding but is not expected to match full numerical simulations of stochastic hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors formulate stochastic hydrodynamic equations near the O(4) critical point, then work in a mean-field approximation to determine correlation functions of the stress tensor and currents from this stochastic real-time theory. They use these correlation functions to determine the scaling behavior of transport coefficients, and also analyze the hydrodynamic theory describing pion wave propagation to fix the scaling behavior of the soft pion dispersion curve.

## Key numbers

* The mean-field critical exponents are compared to O(4) scaling theory: mean-field gives beta = 1/2, delta = 3, Delta = 3/2, while O(4) scaling theory gives beta = 0.38, delta = 4.8, Delta = 1.83
* The ratio m_sigma_c^2 / m_c^2 on the critical line is 3 in mean-field theory versus 4.0 in O(4) scaling theory
* The pion screening mass on the critical line is m_c^2 = mfrak^2 h^{2/3}, where h is the reduced field
* The scaling variable is defined as z = t h^{-2/3} in mean field, where t is the reduced temperature

## Context

This work extends earlier results by Grossi et al. (2020) that computed contributions of soft pions to transport coefficients in the broken phase and estimated how these contributions evolve approaching the critical point from below. The hydrodynamic equations of motion were written down previously by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1992) using Poisson bracket methods, and the current paper provides a somewhat different derivation. The results motivate further phenomenological and numerical work on the implications of chiral symmetry for real-time properties of thermal QCD near the pseudo-critical point, including numerical simulations of the critical theory to find two-point functions of conserved currents.
