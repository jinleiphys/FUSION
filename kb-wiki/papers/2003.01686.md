# Wigner function formalism and the evolution of thermodynamic quantities in an expanding magnetized plasma

> By combining the Wigner function formalism of relativistic quantum kinetic theory with fundamental equations of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), we present a novel approach to determine the proper time evolution of the temperature and other thermodynamic quantities in a uniformly expanding hot, magnetized, and weakly interacting plasma. The aim is to study the contribution of quantum corrections to this evolution. We first determine the corresponding Wigner function in terms of the solution of the Dirac equation in the presence of a constant magnetic field. Using this function, we then compute the energy-momentum tensor of the above-mentioned plasma, which eventually yields its energy density and pressure. Plugging these quantities in the energy equation of relativistic MHD, we arrive, after choosing an appropriate coordinate system, at a differential equation for the temperature as a function of the proper time. The numerical solution of this equation leads finally to the proper time evolution of the temperature. The latter is then used to determine the evolution of a large number of thermodynamic quantities in this expanding and magnetized plasma. We compare our results with other existing results from relativistic MHD. We also comment on the effect of point to point decaying magnetic fields on the thermodynamic properties of this plasma.

## Key claim

By combining the Wigner function formalism of relativistic quantum kinetic theory with the energy equation of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), the authors present a novel approach to determine the proper time evolution of temperature and other thermodynamic quantities in a uniformly expanding hot, magnetized, and weakly interacting plasma, specifically aiming to include quantum corrections from Landau levels that arise in the presence of constant magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors first determine the Wigner function for a fermionic hot and magnetized plasma using the solution of the Dirac equation in a constant background magnetic field via the Ritus eigenfunction formalism. They then use this Wigner function to analytically compute the energy-momentum tensor, yielding the energy density, transverse pressure, and longitudinal pressure as functions of temperature. These thermodynamic quantities are plugged into the energy equation of ideal transverse MHD in Milne coordinates, producing a first-order differential equation for temperature as a function of proper time, which is solved numerically for both high-temperature (weak magnetic field) and low-temperature (strong magnetic field) limits, as well as for point-to-point decaying magnetic field profiles.

## Key numbers

* Initial proper time for the onset of hydrodynamics is taken as tau_0 ~ 0.2 fm/c after the collision.
* In the high-temperature limit (eB much less than T^2) and low-temperature limit (eB much greater than T^2), the proper time evolution of temperature is determined numerically for massless fermions.
* For the massive case, the analysis is performed in the limit M_n^2 much less than T^2, where M_n^2 = m^2 + 2neB is the magnetic mass.
* The Bjorken solution for 1+1 dimensional expansion is given by T = T_0 (tau_0 / tau)^(1/kappa) with kappa = 3 for an ideal gas equation of state epsilon = kappa p.
* The Gubser flow temperature is given by T = T_0 (tau_0 / tau)^(1/kappa) * G(r, r_0; tau, tau_0) with the Gubser parameter q = 1/4.3 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of relativistic MHD for uniformly expanding plasmas, particularly the "ideal transverse MHD" framework developed by Rischke and collaborators, and the nonideal transverse MHD framework introduced by Shokri and Sadooghi. It also generalizes the authors' earlier work (Tabatabaee and Sadooghi, 2019) that combined anisotropic hydrodynamics with the Boltzmann equation in a relaxation time approximation, which showed no significant difference from the Bjorken solution for temperature. The new approach enables the inclusion of quantum corrections from Landau levels that were absent in classical kinetic theory treatments, and allows determination of the proper time evolution of temperature, energy density, transverse and longitudinal pressures, speeds of sound, and magnetic susceptibility in both constant and decaying magnetic fields.
