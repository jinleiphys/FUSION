# Incoherent Photoproduction of $η$-mesons from the Deuteron near Threshold

> Incoherent photoproduction of the $η$-meson on the deuteron is studied for photon energies from threshold to 800 MeV. The dominant contribution, the $γ$N-$η$N amplitude, is described within an isobar model. The final state interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential is included and found to be important for the description of the production cross section close to threshold. Possible effects from the $ηN$ final state interaction are discussed.

## Key claim
The observed enhancement of the total cross section for incoherent photoproduction of η-mesons from the deuteron near threshold can be largely explained by the final state interaction between the outgoing neutron and proton, but a remaining discrepancy at very small excess energies may indicate an additional contribution from the η-nucleon final state interaction.

## Method
The authors model the elementary γN → ηN amplitude using only the S11(1535) resonance, with parameters fitted to γp → ηp data. They compute the γd → npη cross section in the impulse approximation using the deuteron wave function, then include the neutron-proton final state interaction by employing the half-shell t-matrix from the CD-Bonn potential for the 1S0 and 3S1 partial waves. Possible effects from the η-nucleon final state interaction are discussed by comparing the ratio of data to the calculation that includes only the nucleon-nucleon final state interaction.

## Key numbers
- Resonance parameters for the S11(1535) at the pole: mass M_R = 1544 MeV, total width Γ = 203 MeV, with branching ratios Γ_η/Γ = 0.45, Γ_π/Γ = 0.45, Γ_ππ/Γ = 0.1.
- Electromagnetic helicity amplitudes: A^p_{1/2} = 0.124 GeV^{-1/2} and A^n_{1/2} = -0.1 GeV^{-1/2}.
- The impulse approximation describes data reasonably well at photon energies above about 680 MeV, but substantially underestimates the data close to threshold.
- The additional enhancement seen in η-production via hadronic probes (pp → ppη and pn → dη) was a factor of 2 to 3, limited to excess energies below roughly 15 MeV and 10 MeV, respectively.

## Context
This work extends calculations by Arenhovel and collaborators (2000) which included both nucleon-nucleon and η-nucleon final state interactions but still underestimated the cross section near threshold, and contradicts the earlier calculation by Ueda (1999) which invoked a quasi-bound η-deuteron state and produced too strong an enhancement. The results enable future experimental studies with higher statistics and energy resolution to isolate the η-nucleon final state interaction, as suggested by the TAPS collaboration (Hejny, 2001).
