# Ab initio calculation of the electromagnetic and neutral-weak response functions of 4He and 12C

> Precise measurement of neutrino oscillations, and hence the determination of their masses demands a quantitative understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions. To this aim, two-body meson-exchange currents have to be accounted for along within realistic models of nuclear dynamics. We summarize our progresses towards the construction of a consistent framework, based on quantum Monte Carlo methods and on the spectral function approach, that can be exploited to accurately describe neutrino interactions with atomic nuclei over the broad kinematical region covered by neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that two-body meson-exchange currents (MEC) are essential for reproducing experimental electromagnetic response functions of 4He and 12C, but the framework combining quantum Monte Carlo methods with the spectral function approach remains limited to moderate momentum transfers (|q| less than or approximately 500 MeV) for the nonrelativistic treatment, while the relativistic extension via the impulse approximation still requires improvements to include final state interactions.

## Method

The authors employ Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods to compute the ground state wave functions of 4He and 12C using the AV18+IL7 nuclear Hamiltonian, then calculate Euclidean response functions as Laplace transforms of the energy-dependent response functions for electromagnetic and neutral-weak currents. For the moderate momentum transfer regime, they use nonrelativistic one- and two-body currents derived consistently with the Hamiltonian via the continuity equation; for the relativistic regime, they adopt the impulse approximation with spectral functions obtained from the local density approximation, combined with fully relativistic meson-exchange currents. The maximum entropy technique is used to invert the Euclidean response functions of 4He to recover the energy-dependent response.

## Key numbers

- Electromagnetic transverse Euclidean response function of 12C calculated at momentum transfer q = 570 MeV, compared to experimental data from Jourdan (1996)
- Electromagnetic transverse response function of 4He at q = 500 MeV, with experimental data from Carlson (2002)
- For the relativistic calculation of 12C, the two-hole spectral function of carbon was approximated using uniform nuclear matter at Fermi momentum k_F = 221 MeV
- The two-particle two-hole contributions to the spectral function normalization are 15-20%, located at large momenta and energies

## Context

This work extends the Green's Function Monte Carlo approach previously applied to light nuclei (Pervin 2007, Marcucci 2008, Schiavilla 2002) to compute response functions for 4He and 12C, addressing the discrepancy between MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic neutrino-carbon cross section data and relativistic Fermi gas model predictions (Aguilar 2008). The framework enables future calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for oscillation experiments such as DUNE, though the authors note that the relativistic calculations still require inclusion of final state interactions and that discrepancies remain between the nonrelativistic GFMC and relativistic spectral function approaches in both magnitude and energy dependence of two-body current contributions.
