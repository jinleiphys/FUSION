# The $1/N_c$ Operator Analysis of the Combined Octet and Decuplet Baryons Contact Interactions in SU(3) Chiral Effective Field Theory

> In this work, we construct the non-derivative four-point interactions for Octet and Decuplet baryons in the SU(3) Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChEFT) framework, and there are 104 coupling constant terms. The non-relativistic expansion of the baryon fields has been considered up to the Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) of the three-momentum expansion. We find 28 and 106 Low-Energy Constants (LECs) for Leading Order (LO) and NLO, respectively. Using the Hartree Hamiltonian of the $1/N_c$ expansion of the operator product up to Next-to-Next-to-Leading Order (NNLO), we can reduce the free parameters (LECs) of the ChEFT from 134 down to 24 up to NLO of the three-momentum expansion. Moreover, we will discuss the implications of the $1/N_c$ sum rules in $ΩΩ$ and $ΩN$ scatterings, where the future results from lattice QCD can be used to test our sum rules.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the non-derivative four-point interactions for combined octet and decuplet baryons in SU(3) Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChEFT) and shows that the large-\(N_c\) expansion reduces the number of independent low-energy constants (LECs) from 134 down to 24 up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the three-momentum expansion, but this reduction is conditional on using the Hartree Hamiltonian of the \(1/N_c\) operator product expansion up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).

## Method

The authors first constructed the minimal derivative chiral Lagrangians for all six types of contact interactions involving octet (\(B\)) and decuplet (\(\Delta\)) baryons in SU(3) ChEFT, yielding 104 coupling constant terms. They then performed a non-relativistic expansion of the baryon fields up to NLO in the three-momentum expansion (order \((Q/M)^2\)) to derive baryon-baryon potentials, which produced 134 LECs (28 at LO and 106 at NLO). Finally, they matched the spin-flavor structures of these potentials to those from the \(1/N_c\) Hartree Hamiltonian expanded up to NNLO, obtaining sum rules that reduce the independent LECs to 24.

## Key numbers

* The non-derivative chiral Lagrangian for octet and decuplet baryon contact interactions yields 104 coupling constant parameters: 15 for \(BB \rightarrow BB\), 2 for \(BB \rightarrow DB\), 20 for \(DB \rightarrow DB\), 8 for \(BB \rightarrow DD\), 3 for \(DB \rightarrow DD\), and 54 for \(DD \rightarrow DD\).
* After non-relativistic expansion up to NLO in the three-momentum expansion, the chiral potentials contain 134 LECs: 28 at LO and 106 at NLO.
* Using the \(1/N_c\) Hartree Hamiltonian up to NNLO, the number of independent LECs is reduced from 134 down to 24 up to NLO of the three-momentum expansion.

## Context

This work extends previous large-\(N_c\) analyses of nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions (Savage 2011, Beane 2011, Vonk 2025) by systematically incorporating decuplet baryon degrees of freedom alongside octet baryons within the SU(3) ChEFT framework, which had not been done before. The resulting parameter reduction significantly improves the predictive capacity of ChEFT for baryon-baryon interactions, and the derived sum rules for \(\Omega\Omega\) and \(\Omega N\) scatterings can be tested against future lattice QCD results and experimental data for exotic dibaryon states.
