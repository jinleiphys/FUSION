# Quarks and gluons in dense two-colour QCD

> We compute quark and gluon propagators in 2-colour QCD at large baryon chemical potential mu. The gluon propagator is found to be antiscreened at intermediate mu and screened at large mu. The quark propagator is drastically modified in the superfluid region as a result of the formation of a superfluid gap.

# Quarks and gluons in dense two-colour QCD

## Key claim
In two-colour QCD at large baryon chemical potential, the gluon propagator exhibits antiscreening at intermediate densities and screening at large densities, while the quark propagator shows dramatic modifications in the superfluid region due to the formation of a superfluid gap; however, the screening behaviour depends on the lattice spacing and the location of the deconfinement transition, with weaker screening observed on the finer lattice where deconfinement occurs at much higher chemical potential.

## Method
The study uses lattice QCD simulations with gauge group SU(2) and two degenerate flavours of Wilson fermion, including a diquark source term to study diquark condensation. Gauge configurations were generated on two lattices: a coarse lattice with volume 8^3 x 16 and lattice spacing 0.23 fm, and a fine lattice with volume 12^3 x 24 and lattice spacing 0.18 fm, both with m_pi/m_rho = 0.8. The gluon propagator in Landau gauge is decomposed into magnetic and electric form factors, while the quark propagator is analysed via the Gor'kov formalism with normal and anomalous components.

## Key numbers
- Coarse lattice: beta = 1.7, kappa = 0.178, volume V = 8^3 x 16, lattice spacing 0.23 fm
- Fine lattice: beta = 1.9, kappa = 0.168, volume V = 12^3 x 24, lattice spacing 0.18 fm
- Both lattices: m_pi/m_rho = 0.8
- Diquark source values: aj = 0.04 for all configurations, with additional configurations at aj = 0.02 and aj = 0.06 for selected chemical potentials
- Onset transition found at mu_o approximately equal to m_pi/2
- Chemical potentials studied on coarse lattice include a mu = 0.5 and a mu = 0.7

## Context
This work extends lattice studies of two-colour QCD at finite density, following earlier results on the coarse lattice reported in Hands (2006). The gluon propagator results are compared to vacuum studies by Leinweber (1998), Cucchieri (2007), and Bogolubsky (2007), which show infrared suppression even without medium effects. The quark propagator modifications are linked to the rotation of the chiral condensate into the diquark condensate in the superfluid phase, as previously observed by Kogut (2000). The results provide first-principles input for Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations of dense QCD, as pursued by Roberts (2000) and Nickel (2006), and enable future quantitative studies of the BEC-BCS crossover, including determination of the Fermi momentum and Cooper pair sizes.
