# Renormalizability and nonrenormalizability of nonlocal potentials

## Key claim
Using separable toy models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction inspired by chiral effective field theory, the authors demonstrate that nonlocality of the long-range forces either forces the introduction of nonlocal counter terms or makes the entire approach nonrenormalizable, contradicting the assumption that renormalizability is a universal property of quantum-mechanical interaction models.

## Method
The authors construct separable S-wave toy-model potentials with short-range contact interactions and long-range parts mimicking one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange contributions, regulated by a power-like form factor with cutoff Lambda of order the breakdown scale Lambda_b. The leading-order (LO) potential is treated nonperturbatively by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, while the next-to-leading-order (NLO) amplitude is computed perturbatively to identify power-counting-breaking (PCB) contributions proportional to positive powers of the cutoff. Renormalizability is tested by checking whether these PCB terms can be absorbed into a redefinition of the LO potential.

## Key numbers
- The cutoff Lambda is assumed to be of order the hard scale, Lambda ~ Lambda_b, where Lambda_b is the EFT breakdown scale.
- The LO short-range potential uses a power-like form factor F_Lambda(p) = Lambda^2 / (Lambda^2 + p^2).
- The loop function Sigma_{0,11}(p_on) = Lambda^3 / [4 Lambda_b (p_on + i Lambda)^2] is of order O(Q^0), while other Sigma_0 matrix elements are of order O(Q^1).
- The PCB term from Sigma_{2,21}(p_on) requires subtraction of its threshold value Sigma_{2,21}(0) = -Lambda^3 / (4 Lambda_b^3).
- The renormalized remainder Delta Sigma_{2,21}(p_on) = -[Lambda^3 / (4 Lambda_b (Lambda - i p_on)^2)] * (p_on^2 / Lambda_b^2) = O(Q^2).

## Context
This work extends the explicit demonstration of renormalizability of nuclear chiral EFT in the two-nucleon sector at LO and NLO by Gasparyan et al. (2021, 2023), which relied on the locality of long-range forces derived from the chiral Lagrangian. The authors show that nonlocal long-range interactions, as used in phenomenological models by Epelbaum et al. (2017) and Peng et al. (2021), can break renormalizability. The results imply that arbitrary phenomenological models of nuclear interactions cannot be assumed renormalizable, and that renormalizability is a specific feature of interactions based on an effective field theory.
