# Resonance decay effects on anisotropy parameters

> We present the elliptic flow $v_2$ of pions produced from resonance decays. The transverse momentum $p_T$ spectra of the parent particles are taken from thermal model fits and their $v_2$ are fit under the assumption that they follow number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling expected from quark-coalescence models. The $v_2$ of pions from resonance particle decays is found to be similar to the measured pion $v_2$. We also propose the measurement of electron $v_2$ as a means to extract open-charm $v_2$ and investigate whether a thermalized system of quarks and gluons (a quark-gluon plasma) is created in collisions of Au nuclei at RHIC.

## Key claim

When resonance decays are accounted for, the measured pion elliptic flow ($v_2$) may become consistent with the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling law that suggests partonic collectivity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but model uncertainties make extraction of the direct pion $v_2$ difficult.

## Method

The authors simulated secondary pions from decays of $\rho$, $\omega$, $K^*$, $K_S^0$, and $\Delta$ resonances, assuming the parent particles follow NCQ-scaling of $v_2$ parameterized by fits to published $K_S^0$ and $\Lambda$ $v_2$ data from minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV. The $p_T$ distributions of parent particles were taken from thermal model fits with slope parameters from measurements, and relative hadron abundances were fixed using chemical fits. The simulated decay pion $v_2$ was then compared to measured pion $v_2$ data from STAR and PHENIX.

## Key numbers

- At mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, as many as 80% of pions are from resonance decays.
- The fit parameters for the NCQ-scaled $v_2$ function are $a = 0.1$, $b=0.35$, $c = 0.2$, and $d=0.03$, with $p_T$ in GeV/c.
- NCQ-scaling works well for kaons, protons, and Lambdas within $0.6 \le p_T/n_q \le 1.5$ GeV/c, whereas pion $v_2$ deviates for all $p_T$.
- The STAR measured slope parameter for $\rho$ distributions in peripheral collisions is $319 \pm 4$ (stat.) $\pm 32$ (syst.) MeV.
- For electron $p_T > 1.5$ GeV/c, the $v_2$ of electrons from D-meson decays becomes similar to the parent D-meson $v_2$.
- The electron $v_2$ measurement within $1 \le p_T(\mathrm{e}) \le 3$ GeV/c corresponds to $2 \lesssim p_T(\mathrm{D}) \lesssim 5$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of NCQ-scaling of elliptic flow observed at RHIC, where pions appeared to violate the scaling that kaons, protons, $\Lambda$, and $\Xi$ follow (STAR Collaboration 2003; PHENIX Collaboration 2003). The paper addresses the discrepancy by showing that resonance decay contributions can bring pion $v_2$ into agreement with NCQ-scaling, supporting the quark-coalescence model explanation for hadron formation (Voloshin 2002; Fries et al. 2003). It also proposes electron $v_2$ measurements as a method to extract open-charm $v_2$, enabling investigation of whether charm quarks thermalize in the quark-gluon plasma, following similar suggestions by Greco et al. (2003) and Lin et al. (2003).
