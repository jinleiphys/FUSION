# Effective distributions of quasi-particles for thermal photons

> It has been found in recent heavy-ion experiments that the second and the third flow harmonics of direct photons are larger than most theoretical predictions. In this study, I construct effective parton phase-space distributions with in-medium interaction using quasi-particle models so that they are consistent with a lattice QCD equation of state. Then I investigate their effects on thermal photons using a hydrodynamic model. Numerical results indicate that elliptic flow and transverse momentum spectra are modified by the corrections to Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions.

# Effective distributions of quasi-particles for thermal photons

## Key claim

The paper claims that modifying the ideal Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein parton phase-space distributions to include in-medium interactions via a quasi-particle model, constrained to reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state, can enhance thermal photon elliptic flow and suppress the transverse momentum spectrum, but the effect is not large enough to fully explain the experimentally observed large direct photon anisotropy.

## Method

The author constructs a quasi-particle model where the effective one-particle distribution for quarks and gluons includes a temperature-dependent effective interaction term \(W_\mathrm{eff}(T)\), fitted to reproduce the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equation of state from Borsanyi et al. (2013). The effective fugacities \(\lambda_q = \lambda_g = e^{-W_\mathrm{eff}/T}\) are then substituted into the leading-order thermal photon emission rate for Compton scattering and pair annihilation. A (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with boost-invariance, using the same lattice QCD equation of state, provides the flow and temperature profiles for Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, and the thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow are computed by integrating the emission rate over space-time.

## Key numbers

- The effective interaction \(W_\mathrm{eff}\) is fitted for the temperature range \(0.15 < T < 0.6\) GeV; it has a peak structure slightly below the pseudo-critical temperature \(T_c = 0.17\) GeV, with values comparable to the temperature itself.
- The maximum energy density in the most central collisions is set to \(e_\mathrm{max} = 30\) GeV at initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/\(c\).
- The hadronic freeze-out temperature is \(T_f = 0.13\) GeV; the crossover width for interpolating QGP and hadronic emission rates is \(\Delta T = 0.017\) GeV.
- The impact parameter for the Au-Au collisions is \(b = 6\) fm.
- The in-medium corrections suppress the QGP photon emission by a factor of approximately \(e^{-2W_\mathrm{eff}/T}\) near the pseudo-critical temperature.

## Context

This work addresses the "photon \(v_2\) puzzle" observed in heavy-ion experiments at RHIC and LHC, where the second and third flow harmonics of direct photons exceed most theoretical predictions from conventional models using ideal gas distributions. It extends prior studies that introduced viscous corrections to the distribution (Dusling, 2008; Shen, 2014) and semi-QGP effects (Gale, 2014), and is related to earlier quasi-particle models (Schneider, 2001; Biro, 2001; Bluhm, 2004; Castorina, 2005; Mattiello, 2009; Chandra, 2011; Plumari, 2011). The results imply that non-ideal gas corrections to parton distributions must be included for quantitative descriptions of heavy-ion photon observables, and the formalism can be extended to study chemical non-equilibration effects, causal dissipative hydrodynamics, and anisotropic hydrodynamics compatible with lattice QCD.
