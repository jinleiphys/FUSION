# Confronting current NLO parton fragmentation functions with inclusive charged-particle spectra at hadron colliders

> The inclusive spectra of charged particles measured at high transverse momenta ($p_T\gtrsim$2GeV/c) in proton-proton and proton-antiproton collisions in the range of center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s}=200-7000$GeV are compared with next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations using seven recent sets of parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions (FFs). Accounting for the uncertainties in the scale choices and in the parton distribution functions, we find that most of the theoretical predictions tend to overpredict the measured LHC and Tevatron cross sections by up to a factor of two. We identify the currently too-hard gluon-to-hadron FFs as the probable source of the problem, and justify the need to refit the FFs using the available LHC and Tevatron data in a region of transverse momenta, $p_T\gtrsim$10GeV/c, which is supposedly free from additional non-perturbative contributions and where the scale uncertainty is only modest.

# Confronting current NLO parton fragmentation functions with inclusive charged-particle spectra at hadron colliders

## Key claim

Most next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations using seven modern sets of parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions overpredict the measured LHC and Tevatron charged-particle cross sections by up to a factor of two, with the probable source being currently too-hard gluon-to-hadron fragmentation functions that require refitting using data at transverse momenta above 10 GeV/c where scale uncertainties are modest.

## Method

The authors compared inclusive charged-hadron spectra from proton-proton and proton-antiproton collisions at center-of-mass energies from 200 to 7000 GeV with next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations using the INCNLO program, employing seven sets of parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions (Kretzer, KKP, BFGW, HKNS, AKK05, DSS, and AKK08) with CT10NLO parton distribution functions. They evaluated the agreement via chi-squared statistics for three different minimum transverse momentum cuts (1.3, 5.0, and 10.0 GeV/c) and three scale choices, accounting for scale uncertainties through 16 variations and parton distribution function uncertainties through the CT10NLO error sets.

## Key numbers

- The Kretzer fragmentation functions produced the lowest chi-squared values across most scale choices and transverse momentum cuts, with chi-squared per data point values ranging from 2.031 to 11.73 depending on scale and minimum transverse momentum
- The AKK05 fragmentation functions produced the highest chi-squared values, with chi-squared per data point values ranging from 21.00 to 208.4 depending on scale and minimum transverse momentum
- Scale uncertainty stabilizes below plus or minus 20 percent for transverse momenta above approximately 10 GeV/c
- The data span five orders of magnitude in perturbatively-accessible transverse momentum values from 2 to over 100 GeV/c
- The analysis included 368 data points above 1.3 GeV/c, 169 data points above 5.0 GeV/c, and 103 data points above 10.0 GeV/c from LHC, Tevatron, and UA1 experiments

## Context

This work extends comparisons of next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations to the latest LHC charged-hadron data from CMS (2011, 2012) and ALICE (2013), as well as Tevatron data from CDF (2009). The authors note that while inclusive jet and prompt photon measurements at LHC agree perfectly with next-to-leading order expectations (Wobisch 2011, Nagy 2003, d'Enterria 2012), the charged-hadron discrepancies are unexpected and problematic for heavy-ion collision baseline measurements. The findings contradict the earlier inclusion of lower-energy hadron collider data in global fits by DSS (deFlorian 2007) and AKK08 (Albino 2008), which used data at transverse momenta below 5 GeV/c where scale uncertainties are large, and suggest that future global fits should restrict to data above 10 GeV/c to avoid non-perturbative contributions and large scale uncertainties.
