# Improved opacity expansion for medium-induced parton splitting

> We present a new expansion scheme to compute the rate for parton splittings in dense and finite QCD media. In contrast to the standard opacity expansion, our expansion is performed around the harmonic oscillator whose characteristic frequency depends on the typical transverse momentum scale generated in the splitting. The first two orders account for the high frequency regime that is dominated by single hard scatterings together with the regime of multiple soft scatterings at low frequency. This work generalizes the findings of Ref. \cite{Mehtar-Tani:2019tvy} beyond the leading logarithmic approximation allowing to account also for the Bethe-Heitler regime and compare to the full numerical results from Ref. \cite{CaronHuot:2010bp}. We investigate the sensitivity of our results to varying the separation scale that defines the leading order. Finally, the application to Monte Carlo event generators is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new expansion scheme for medium-induced parton splitting rates that, unlike the standard opacity expansion, expands around a harmonic oscillator solution whose frequency depends on the typical transverse momentum scale generated in the splitting; the first two orders of this expansion account for both the high-frequency regime dominated by single hard scatterings and the low-frequency regime of multiple soft scatterings, generalizing the leading-logarithmic approximation of Mehtar-Tani (2019) to also describe the Bethe-Heitler regime.

## Method

The authors shift the expansion point of the medium-induced splitting spectrum from the vacuum solution (standard opacity expansion) to the harmonic oscillator (HO) solution, defined by an effective jet quenching parameter \(\hat q_\text{eff}\) that depends logarithmically on a subtraction scale \(Q_\text{fac}\). They treat the difference \(\delta v = v - v_\text{HO}\) as a perturbation, compute the leading-order (HO) spectrum analytically via the BDMPS-Z result \(z\,dI^{(0)}/dz = (2\alpha_s/\pi)\,zP(z)\,\ln|\cos(\Omega L)|\), and evaluate the next-to-leading order correction by integrating the dipole cross-section with the subtracted HO term over the medium length, using the Gyulassy-Wang model for the elastic scattering potential.

## Key numbers

* The characteristic frequency separating the LPM and single-hard regimes is \(\omega_c = \frac{1}{2}\hat q_\text{eff} L^2\).
* For \(\omega \ll \omega_c\), the leading-order spectrum scales as \(z\,dI^{(0)}/dz \propto \sqrt{\omega_c/(2\omega)}\).
* For \(\omega \gg \omega_c\), the leading-order spectrum scales as \(z\,dI^{(0)}/dz \propto \frac{1}{12}(\omega_c/\omega)^2\).
* In the high-frequency regime \(\omega \gg \hat q L^2\), the NLO-corrected spectrum gives \(\omega\,dI/d\omega \approx \bar\alpha\,(\pi/4)\,(\hat q L^2/\omega)\).
* In the Bethe-Heitler regime, the spectrum behaves as \(\omega\,dI/d\omega \approx 2\bar\alpha\,(\hat q_0 L/\mu^2)\left(\ln(\mu^2 L/(2\omega)) - 1 + \gamma_E\right)\).
* For a thermal medium with \(T=0.4\) GeV and \(\alpha_s \approx 0.3\), the bare quenching parameter is \(\hat q_0 = 1.83\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and the Debye mass is \(m_D = 0.9\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the leading-logarithmic approach of Mehtar-Tani (2019) beyond the leading-log approximation by incorporating hard scattering effects on top of multiple soft interactions, unifying the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) single-scattering regime and the BDMPS-Z multiple-soft-scattering regime. The results are compared to the full numerical solutions of Caron-Huot (2010) and enable semi-analytic interpolation between the Bethe-Heitler regime (\(\tau_\text{form} < \ell_\text{mfp}\)), the LPM regime (\(\ell_\text{mfp} < \tau_\text{form} < L\)), and the single-hard-scattering regime (\(L < \tau_\text{form}\)), with potential applications to Monte Carlo event generators for jet quenching phenomenology.
