# A new EFT approach to NN scattering problem

> We present the new, modified Weinberg approach to the NN scattering problem in effective field theory. Issues of renormalization are briefly discussed and the results of LO calculations are presented.

## Key claim

The authors propose a modified, renormalizable version of Weinberg's approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering in effective field theory, claiming that the standard Weinberg approach is not renormalizable because not all divergences from iterating the Lippmann-Schwinger equation can be absorbed into the leading-order potential parameters.

## Method

The authors start from a manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian for pions and nucleons and use time-ordered perturbation theory to define the NN potential as a sum of two-nucleon-irreducible diagrams. They expand the potential, two-nucleon propagator, and scattering amplitude in small parameters and solve order-by-order, with the leading-order amplitude obtained from an integral equation first derived by Kadyshevsky. The leading-order partial wave equations are solved, with a counter-term added for the \(^3P_0\) wave to ensure a unique solution.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^1S_0\) partial wave, the LO Weinberg approach yields an effective range \(r = 1.50\) fm (fit), shape parameters \(v_2 = -1.9\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 8.6(8)\) fm\(^5\), and \(v_4 = -37(10)\) fm\(^7\), compared to Nijmegen PWA values \(a = -23.7\) fm, \(r = 2.67\) fm, \(v_2 = -0.5\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 4.0\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -20\) fm\(^7\).
- For the \(^3S_1\) partial wave, the LO Weinberg approach yields an effective range \(r = 1.60\) fm (fit), shape parameters \(v_2 = -0.05\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 0.8(1)\) fm\(^5\), and \(v_4 = -4(1)\) fm\(^7\), compared to Nijmegen PWA values \(a = 5.42\) fm, \(r = 1.75\) fm, \(v_2 = 0.04\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 0.67\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -4.0\) fm\(^7\).
- The NLO KSW approach gives shape parameters for \(^1S_0\): \(v_2 = -3.3\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 18\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -108\) fm\(^7\); for \(^3S_1\): \(v_2 = -0.95\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 4.6\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -25\) fm\(^7\).

## Context

This work extends the original Weinberg approach (Weinberg, 1990) to NN scattering in chiral effective field theory, addressing the renormalization inconsistency noted by Savage (1998). The modified approach uses a Lorentz-invariant formulation that yields a \(1/r^2\) ultraviolet behavior rather than the more singular \(1/r^3\) behavior of non-relativistic EFT, enabling perturbative renormalization. The results enable systematic higher-order calculations of NN scattering and can be tested against the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (de Swart, 1995) and compared with the KSW scheme (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998).
