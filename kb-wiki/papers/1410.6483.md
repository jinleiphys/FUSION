# Resummation of Double-Differential Cross Sections and Fully-Unintegrated Parton Distribution Functions

> LHC measurements involve cuts on several observables, but resummed calculations are mostly restricted to single variables. We show how the resummation of a class of double-differential measurements can be achieved through an extension of Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET). A prototypical application is $pp \to Z + 0$ jets, where the jet veto is imposed through the beam thrust event shape ${\mathcal T}$, and the transverse momentum $p_T$ of the $Z$ boson is measured. A standard SCET analysis suffices for $p_T \sim m_Z^{1/2} {\mathcal T}^{1/2}$ and $p_T \sim {\mathcal T}$, but additional collinear-soft modes are needed in the intermediate regime. We show how to match the factorization theorems that describe these three different regions of phase space, and discuss the corresponding relations between fully-unintegrated parton distribution functions, soft functions and the newly defined collinear-soft functions. The missing ingredients needed at NNLL/NLO accuracy are calculated, providing a check of our formalism. We also revisit the calculation of the measurement of two angularities on a single jet in JHEP 1409 (2014) 046, finding a correction to their conjecture for the NLL cross section at ${\mathcal O}(α_s^2)$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that resummation of double-differential cross sections for observables such as beam thrust and transverse momentum can be achieved by extending Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to include additional collinear-soft modes in the intermediate kinematic regime, provided the two observables are parametrically related by a power law \(p_T \sim Q^{1-r} \Tau^r\) with \(1/2 < r < 1\).

## Method

The authors perform a factorization analysis of the Drell-Yan process \(pp \to Z + 0\) jets, where the jet veto is imposed via beam thrust \(\Tau\) and the \(Z\) boson transverse momentum \(p_T\) is measured. They identify three distinct kinematic regimes requiring different effective field theories: SCET\(_{\rm I}\) for \(p_T \sim Q^{1/2} \Tau^{1/2}\), SCET\(_{\rm II}\) for \(p_T \sim \Tau\), and a new intermediate SCET\(_+\) regime where collinear-soft modes are introduced. The matching of QCD onto SCET\(_+\) currents is performed, and factorization theorems are derived for each region. The missing ingredients needed for next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy are calculated, and the matching between the three factorization theorems is carried out to all orders.

## Key numbers

* The SCET\(_+\) power counting parameter is \(\lambda \sim \Tau/Q \sim (p_T/Q)^{1/r}\), with \(1/2 < r < 1\).
* The SCET\(_+\) factorization is valid for \(p_T \sim Q^{1-r} \Tau^r\); the distance from the SCET\(_{\rm I}\) boundary is measured by power corrections of size \(\lambda^{2r-1} \sim p_T^2/(Q \Tau)\), and from the SCET\(_{\rm II}\) boundary by \(\lambda^{2-2r} \sim \Tau^2/p_T^2\).
* The NLO cross section calculation provides a check of the formalism, finding corrections of the first type (\(p_T^2/(Q \Tau)\)) but not of the second type at this order.
* For the double angularity measurement on a single jet, the authors find a correction to the conjecture of Larkoski et al. (2014) for the NLL cross section at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based resummation framework for single-variable observables, such as beam thrust (Stewart et al., 2010) and transverse momentum (Collins et al., 1984), to double-differential measurements. It builds on the identification of boundary theories for two angularities on a single jet by Larkoski et al. (2014), correcting their NLL result at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\). The formalism enables resummed predictions for jet substructure studies and processes like \(pp \to H+1\) jet with a jet veto, where the transverse momentum of the jet becomes small.
