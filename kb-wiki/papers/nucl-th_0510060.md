# Low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction and shell-model calculations

> We discuss the use of the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon NN interaction V-low-k in the derivation of the shell-model effective interaction and emphasize its practical value as an alternative to the Brueckner G-matrix method. We present some selected results of our current study of exotic nuclei around closed shells, which have been obtained starting from the CD-Bonn potential. We also show some results of calculations performed with different phase-shift equivalent NN potentials, and discuss the effect of changes in the cutoff momentum which defines the V-low-k potential.

# Low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction and shell-model calculations

## Key claim

The low-momentum potential V-low-k, derived from a bare nucleon-nucleon potential by integrating out high-momentum modes down to a cutoff momentum Lambda, provides a practical and advantageous alternative to the traditional Brueckner G-matrix method for deriving shell-model effective interactions, but the quality of agreement with experiment depends on the choice of cutoff momentum and the input NN potential, with different phase-shift equivalent potentials yielding nearly identical nuclear structure results only after allowing for limited changes in Lambda.

## Method

The authors construct V-low-k from the CD-Bonn NN potential by imposing T-matrix equivalence below a cutoff momentum Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1, preserving deuteron binding energy and low-energy phase shifts. They then derive the shell-model effective interaction V_eff using a folded-diagram (Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff) expansion, with the Q-box including diagrams up to second order in V-low-k, and apply it to shell-model calculations for neutron-rich Sn isotopes around doubly magic ^132Sn and for ^134Te. For the study of potential dependence, they compare results from CD-Bonn, NijmegenII, and Argonne v18 potentials while varying Lambda from 1.5 to 2.5 fm^-1.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1 used for the main calculations of Sn isotopes
- For ^134Te, the rms deviation sigma between calculated and experimental spectra reaches a minimum at Lambda ~ 1.7 fm^-1 for CD-Bonn and at Lambda ~ 2 fm^-1 for NijmegenII and AV18
- For CD-Bonn, sigma remains below 150 keV for Lambda between 1.5 and 1.9 fm^-1; for NijmegenII and AV18, this occurs for Lambda between 1.8 and 2.2 fm^-1
- Calculated ground-state energy of ^134Te relative to ^132Sn: -20.793 MeV (NijmII), -20.778 MeV (AV18), -20.778 MeV (CD-Bonn); experimental value -20.560 MeV
- Calculated 2+ excitation energy in ^134Te: 1.342 MeV (NijmII), 1.325 MeV (AV18), 1.315 MeV (CD-Bonn); experimental 1.279 MeV
- Calculated 4+ excitation energy in ^134Te: 1.611 MeV (NijmII), 1.599 MeV (AV18), 1.603 MeV (CD-Bonn); experimental 1.576 MeV
- Calculated 6+ excitation energy in ^134Te: 1.741 MeV (NijmII), 1.726 MeV (AV18), 1.742 MeV (CD-Bonn); experimental 1.691 MeV
- Effective neutron charge of 0.70e used for B(E2) calculation in ^134Sn; effective neutron-hole charge of 0.78e used for ^128Sn and ^130Sn

## Context

This work extends the V-low-k approach introduced by Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators (2002) and applies it to shell-model calculations of exotic nuclei near doubly magic ^132Sn, a region well away from the valley of stability. The authors had previously performed similar calculations using the G-matrix formalism (Coraggio et al., 2002), and the present results show that V-low-k yields comparable or better agreement with experiment while avoiding the need to calculate the G matrix. The study of potential dependence confirms earlier findings by Bogner et al. (2003) and Holt et al. (2004) that V-low-k interactions derived from different phase-shift equivalent potentials are nearly identical, and it enables future systematic shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei using a unified low-momentum interaction framework.
