# Trinucleon Electromagnetic Form Factors and the Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics

> This contribution briefly illustrates preliminary calculations of the electromagnetic form factors of $^3$He and $^3$H, obtained within the Light-front Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics, adopting i) a Poincaré covariant current operator, without dynamical two-body currents, and ii) realistic nuclear bound states with $S$, $P$ and $D$ waves. The kinematical region of few $(GeV/c)^2$, relevant for forthcoming TJLAB experiments, has been investigated, obtaining possible signatures of relativistic effects for $Q^2>2.5 ~(GeV/c)^2$.

## Key claim

Preliminary calculations of the electromagnetic form factors of 3He and 3H within Light-front Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics, using a Poincare covariant current operator without dynamical two-body currents and realistic bound states including S, P, and D waves, show possible signatures of relativistic effects for momentum transfers Q^2 greater than 2.5 (GeV/c)^2, but these results are preliminary and do not yet include the two-body currents that the chosen Breit frame necessarily requires.

## Method

The authors employed the Bakamjian-Thomas procedure within Light-front Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics to construct Poincare covariant operators from non-relativistic wave functions. They evaluated matrix elements of a Poincare covariant current operator in a Breit frame with zero perpendicular momentum transfer (q_perp = 0), using realistic trinucleon bound states containing S, P, and D waves, and incorporated Wigner-Melosh rotations to relate the light-front spin basis to the canonical spin basis. The auxiliary current operator was built from the free one-body nucleon current plus a term involving the light-front spin operator of the whole system, without including dynamical two-body currents.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic effects become possible signatures for Q^2 greater than 2.5 (GeV/c)^2, in the kinematical region of a few (GeV/c)^2 relevant for forthcoming TJLAB experiments
- The nucleon current used is J^mu_N = -F^N_2(Delta^2) (p^mu + p'^mu)/(2M) + gamma^mu [F^N_1(Delta^2) + F^N_2(Delta^2)], where F^N_1 and F^N_2 are the Dirac and Pauli nucleon form factors respectively

## Context

This work extends previous Light-front RHD calculations of deuteron electromagnetic observables by Lev, Pace, and Salme (2000) and preliminary trinucleon calculations by Baroncini, Pace, and Salme (2007) that retained only S and S' waves, by now including P and D waves in the nuclear bound states. The approach is motivated by forthcoming experiments at TJLAB measuring 3He and 3H form factors in the few (GeV/c)^2 region, as discussed by Makis. The chosen Breit frame with q_perp = 0 necessarily produces a two-body current related to pair production, as noted by Adam, Beyer, and Neudatchin (2008), which must be included in future work to achieve a complete relativistic description.
