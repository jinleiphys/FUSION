# Azimuthal Asymmetry of Prompt Photons in Nuclear Collisions

> The azimuthal elliptic asymmetry v2 observed in heavy ion collisions, is usually associated with properties of the medium created in the final state. We compute the azimuthal asymmetry which is due to multiple interactions of partons at the initial stage of nuclear collisions, and which is also present in $pA$ collisions. In our approach the main source of azimuthal asymmetry is the combination of parton multiple interactions with the steep variation of the nuclear density at the edge of nuclei. We apply the light-cone dipole formalism to compute the azimuthal asymmetry of prompt photons yield from parton-nucleus, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The azimuthal elliptic asymmetry \(v_2\) of prompt photons can arise from multiple parton interactions at the initial stage of nuclear collisions, not only from final-state medium properties; however, the predicted \(v_2\) from this mechanism is extremely small and changes sign (becomes negative) for peripheral collisions, and the authors note that if PHENIX preliminary data showing larger \(v_2\) are confirmed, other sources must also contribute.

## Method

The authors apply the light-cone dipole formalism to compute the cross section for prompt photon bremsstrahlung from a quark interacting with nuclear matter. The azimuthal asymmetry is obtained from the second Fourier coefficient of the photon transverse-momentum distribution, where the key ingredient is the orientation of the color dipole relative to the impact parameter, encoded in the partial elastic amplitude \(\text{Im}f^{N}_{q\bar{q}}(\vec{s},\vec{r})\). The calculation is performed for quark-nucleus (qA), proton-nucleus (pA), and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at RHIC energy \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear profile (and a hard-sphere profile for comparison) and a parametrized unintegrated gluon density fixed to HERA data.

## Key numbers

- For qA collisions at RHIC energy with \(\alpha=1\) and Woods-Saxon profile, a pronounced elliptic anisotropy appears only for impact parameters close to the nuclear radius \(R_A = 6.5\) fm; central collisions yield \(v_2^{qA}\) identically zero.
- For the hard-sphere (HS) nuclear profile at impact parameter \(b = 7\) fm, the azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2^{qA}\) is significantly larger than for the Woods-Saxon profile at the same \(b\) (see Fig. 1; the enhancement is roughly an order of magnitude).
- For Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC energy with Woods-Saxon profile, the absolute value of \(v_2^{AA}\) is reduced compared to both \(v_2^{pA}\) and \(v_2^{qA}\) (see Fig. 3).
- At high \(p_T\), \(v_2\) for all qA, pA, and AA collisions approaches zero because the dipole size becomes very small and orientation effects vanish.
- The effective quark mass used is \(m_q = 0.2\) GeV; the dipole cross-section parameters are \(\sigma_0 = 23.03\) mb, \(R_0(x) = 0.4\ \text{fm} \times (x/x_0)^{0.144}\) with \(x_0 = 3.04 \times 10^{-4}\), and \(R_N^2 = 5\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the mechanism introduced in Kopeliovich et al. (2007) that generates azimuthal asymmetry from initial-state multiple parton interactions, in contrast to the usual assumption that \(v_2\) arises solely from final-state medium properties. The calculation uses the light-cone dipole formalism previously applied to prompt photon production in pp collisions at RHIC and Tevatron (Rezaeian et al., 2007) and provides predictions for pA and AA collisions. The results are compared to PHENIX measurements of direct photon \(v_2\) (PHENIX, 2006 and 2007 preliminary), which show larger values than the prompt-photon prediction, suggesting that thermal radiation or other sources may be needed. The work also highlights that the nuclear profile tail is poorly constrained by electron scattering, introducing significant uncertainty, and that the off-diagonal unintegrated gluon density is not uniquely determined by available data.
