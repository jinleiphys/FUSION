# Few-body bound states and resonances in finite volume

> Since the pioneering work of Lüscher in the 1980s it is well known that considering quantum systems in finite volume, specifically, finite periodic boxes, can be used as a powerful computational tool to extract physical observables. While this formalism has been worked out in great detail in the two-body sector, much effort is currently being invested into deriving analogous relations for systems with more constituents. This work is relevant not only for nuclear physics, where lattice methods are now able to calculate few- and many-nucleon states, but also for other fields such as simulations of cold atoms. This article discusses recent progress regarding the extraction of few-body bound-state and resonance properties from finite-volume calculations of systems with an arbitrary number of constituents.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general formalism for the finite-volume dependence of bound-state energies and resonance properties for systems with an arbitrary number \(N\) of constituents, extending the well-known two-body Lüscher formalism. The key hedge is that the derivation assumes finite-range interactions and that the leading exponential volume dependence is dominated by the smallest separation energy \(\kappa_{A|N-A}\) between any two-cluster breakup channel, with subleading terms that can be safely neglected except at very small volumes.

## Method

The author derives the asymptotic form of the \(N\)-body wavefunction in the region where one cluster of \(A\) particles is separated from the remaining \(N-A\) particles by a distance larger than the interaction range \(R\), using separation of variables and expansion in eigenstates of the subsystem Hamiltonians. The finite-volume correction to the binding energy \(\Delta B_N(L)\) is then obtained by matching this asymptotic behavior to the periodic boundary conditions of a cubic box of length \(L\), yielding a leading exponential dependence proportional to \((\kappa_{A|N-A}L)^{1-d/2} K_{d/2-1}(\kappa_{A|N-A}L)\). Numerical verification is performed by discretizing the Hamiltonian on a spatial lattice using finite-difference operators for the kinetic energy and local Gaussian potentials, with the code generating results for \(N=2,3,4,5\) particles in one, two, and three spatial dimensions.

## Key numbers

* For \(N=2\) in \(d=1\) with Gaussian potential (\(V_0=-1.0\), \(R=1.0\)): binding energy \(B_2 = 0.356\) (in units of particle mass \(m=1\)), fitted \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 0.59536(3)\) compared to expected \(\kappa_{1|1} = 0.59625\).
* For \(N=3\) in \(d=1\) with same potential: \(B_3 = 1.275\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 1.1062(14)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|2} = 1.1070\).
* For \(N=4\) in \(d=1\) with same potential: \(B_4 = 2.859\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 1.539(3)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|3} = 1.541\).
* For \(N=5\) in \(d=1\) with same potential: \(B_5 = 5.163\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 1.916(21)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|4} = 1.920\).
* For \(N=2\) in \(d=2\) with Gaussian potential (\(V_0=-1.5\), \(R=1.5\)): \(B_2 = 0.338\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 0.58195(6)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|1} = 0.58140\).
* For \(N=3\) in \(d=2\) with same potential: \(B_3 = 1.424\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 1.20409(3)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|2} = 1.20339\).
* For \(N=4\) in \(d=2\) with same potential: \(B_4 = 3.449\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 1.743(8)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|3} = 1.743\).
* For \(N=2\) in \(d=3\) with Gaussian potential (\(V_0=-5.0\), \(R=1.0\)): \(B_2 = 0.449\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 0.6694(2)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|1} = 0.6700\).
* For \(N=3\) in \(d=3\) with same potential: \(B_3 = 2.916\), \(\kappa_{\text{fit}} = 1.798(3)\) compared to \(\kappa_{1|2} = 1.814\).
* For a tuned \(N=4\) system in \(d=1\) with repulsive three-body and attractive four-body forces: extracted slope \(0.508(2)\) compared to \(\kappa_{2|2} = 0.502\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism originally developed by Lüscher (1985, 1986, 1990) for two-body systems to an arbitrary number of constituents, building on earlier studies of the triton and Efimov trimers in finite volume by Kreuzer (2010, 2012, 2013) and Meissner (2014). The derived volume dependence enables robust extrapolations of binding energies to infinite volume from a small set of small-volume lattice calculations, and the proportionality factor in the exponential correction is directly related to the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for low-energy capture processes relevant to nucleosynthesis. The numerical framework presented here can be used to search for few-body resonances, particularly in light of recent efforts to observe and calculate few-neutron resonances in nuclear physics.
