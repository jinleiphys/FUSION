# Liquid-gas coexistence vs. energy minimization with respect to the density profile in the inhomogeneous inner crust of neutron stars

> We compare two approaches to describe the inner crust of neutron stars: on the one hand, the simple coexistence of a liquid (clusters) and a gas phase, and on the other hand, the energy minimization with respect to the density profile, including Coulomb and surface effects. We find that the phase-coexistence model gives a reasonable description of the densities in the clusters and in the gas, but the precision is not high enough to obtain the correct proton fraction at low baryon densities. We also discuss the surface tension and neutron skin obtained within the energy minimization.

# Liquid-gas coexistence vs. energy minimization with respect to the density profile in the inhomogeneous inner crust of neutron stars

## Key claim

The simple phase-coexistence model gives a reasonable description of the neutron and proton densities in the clusters and in the gas, but the precision is not high enough to obtain the correct proton fraction at low baryon densities; the small corrections to mechanical and chemical equilibrium due to Coulomb and surface effects slightly modify the density of the neutron gas, resulting in a considerable reduction of volume and proton fractions.

## Method

The authors compare two approaches for describing the inner crust of neutron stars: a simple phase-coexistence model assuming mechanical and chemical equilibrium between a liquid (cluster) and gas phase, and a more complete energy minimization using a parametrized density profile within the Wigner-Seitz approximation. The energy is calculated using the semiclassical Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation with Skyrme energy-density functionals (SLy4, SLy7, BSk20, BSk22), including Coulomb energy, electron gas energy, and exchange terms, and the thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to nine parameters describing the density profile for spherical (3D), rod (2D), and slab (1D) geometries.

## Key numbers

- The transition from inner crust to uniform matter occurs at baryon density $\rho_b \approx 0.09~\text{fm}^{-3}$ with SLy4, while with BSk22 it is below $0.08~\text{fm}^{-3}$.
- In the energy minimization with SLy4, the most favorable phase is the crystal (3D) below $0.061~\text{fm}^{-3}$, rods (2D) from $0.061$ to $0.073~\text{fm}^{-3}$, and slabs (1D) between $0.073$ and $0.081~\text{fm}^{-3}$.
- The proton density in the gas phase is always less than $10^{-3}~\text{fm}^{-3}$ for all Skyrme parametrizations studied.
- In the low-density limit, the surface tension is consistent with the Bethe-Weizsacker semi-empirical mass formula value of $\sigma = 1.03$ MeV fm$^{-2}$.
- The proton number in the 3D phase varies smoothly from $Z\approx 34$ at low baryon density to $Z\approx 26$ at the transition to the 2D phase.
- For $\rho_b = 0.02~\text{fm}^{-3}$, there are about $800$ neutrons in the Wigner-Seitz cell, $200$ in the cluster, and only $23$ in the neutron skin.

## Context

This work extends the phase-coexistence approach used by Magierski and Bulgac (2004) and Di Gallo et al. (2011) for hydrodynamic modeling of collective modes in the inner crust, and compares with the more microscopic energy-minimization approach previously applied by Oyamatsu (1993) and Pearson et al. (2012). The results show that while the simple phase-coexistence model captures bulk densities reasonably well, it fails to predict cluster sizes, geometry transitions, and accurate proton fractions at low densities, which require inclusion of Coulomb and surface effects. The authors plan to use their calculated surface tension and neutron skin results to improve hydrodynamic descriptions of collective modes in the inner crust, following the approach of Di Gallo et al. (2011).
