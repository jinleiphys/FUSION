# A novel quark pairing in sQGP induced by the non-Abelian feature of the interaction

> We solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for quark propagator and quark gluon vertex in the Nambu-Gorkov basis which is widely applied to study the color superconductivity. After considering the non-Abelian feature in the off-diagonal part of quark gluon vertex, we acquire a quark pairing gap in chiral limit above the chiral phase transition temperature $T_c$. The gap persists up to $2-3\,T_c$ and vanishes at higher temperature. Such a quark pairing characterizes the strongly coupled quark gluon plasma phase as a new phase and distinct from the phase with quasi quarks and gluons. Its new features can be disclosed in the heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim
The authors claim that a novel quark pairing gap emerges in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) at temperatures above the chiral phase transition temperature \(T_c\), persisting up to \(2-3\,T_c\). This pairing is induced specifically by the non-Abelian (three-gluon vertex) contribution to the quark-gluon vertex, not by the conventional Cooper instability mechanism of color superconductivity, and the abstract's claim of a "new phase" is hedged by the condition that the result is obtained in the chiral limit and within a truncation that neglects momentum dependence of the gap and vertex.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark propagator and the quark-gluon vertex in the Nambu-Gorkov basis, which doubles the fermion degrees of freedom to describe pairing. They included the non-Abelian diagram (involving the three-gluon vertex) in the vertex DSE, while the Abelian diagram was neglected as \(1/N_c^2\) suppressed. The equations were solved in the infrared limit (vanishing spatial momentum and zeroth Matsubara frequency) for the pseudoscalar pairing channel (2SC channel), neglecting the quadratic contribution of the gap \(\Delta\) in the propagator for the analytic solution, and then including it for the numerical results.

## Key numbers
* The quark pairing gap \(\Delta\) vanishes at a critical temperature \(T_\Delta = 416\ \text{MeV}\).
* Near below \(T_\Delta\), the gap follows \(\Delta^2 / 0.37[\mathrm{GeV}^2] = 1 - (T/T_\Delta)^{2.16}\), with a mean-field critical exponent \(\beta = 1/2\).
* The vacuum gluon condensate used as input is \(\langle g^2 A^2 \rangle_{\text{vac}} = 4.4/(N_c^2 - 1)\ \text{GeV}^2\) with \(N_c = 3\).
* The pairing exists in the temperature range \(T \in [T_c, 3T_c]\) and at finite chemical potential extends the sQGP phase region.

## Context
This work extends functional QCD studies of the QCD phase diagram, particularly the DSE approach of Gao (2020) and Lu (2023) for the chiral phase transition, and the vertex analysis of Gao (2021). It contradicts the conventional picture that above \(T_c\) the QGP consists only of quasi-quarks and gluons, and instead supports earlier conjectures of a distinct sQGP phase from Shuryak (2003, 2014), Glozman (2022), and lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest (Borsanyi, 2020) and HotQCD (Bazavov, 2018) collaborations. The pairing mechanism is distinct from the color superconducting (CSC) phase at low temperature and high chemical potential studied by Hong (1999) and Nickel (2006). The results enable future investigations of the relation between this pairing and the chiral spin symmetry observed on the lattice, and may explain the small shear viscosity and thermodynamic properties of sQGP in heavy-ion collisions.
