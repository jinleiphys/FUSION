# Spin Observables in Coincidence Electron Scattering from Nuclei I: Reduced Response Functions

> A theoretical description of nucleon knockout reactions initiated by polarized electron scattering from polarized nuclei is presented. Explicit expressions for the complete set of reduced response functions (independent of the polarization angle) that can be experimentally obtained assuming plane waves for the electron are given in a general multipole expansion. The formalism is applied to the particular case of closed-shell-minus-one nuclei using two models for the ejected nucleon, including the final-state interaction phenomenologically with a complex optical potential and in the factorized plane-wave impulse approximation. Relativistic effects in the kinematics and in the electromagnetic current are incorporated throughout --- specifically a new expansion of the electromagnetic current in powers only of the struck nucleon momentum is employed. Results are presented for the nucleus 39K.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete theoretical formalism for the reduced response functions in polarized electron scattering from polarized nuclei, but the practical extraction of all such functions is "too much to hope for" experimentally in the general case, so the work aims to identify which specific observables are most sensitive to reaction mechanisms and thus most relevant to measure.

## Method

The authors perform a multipole expansion of the nuclear states and electromagnetic current in the plane-wave Born approximation, using \(jj\)-coupling and spherical harmonics of the polarization and emission angles to define a set of reduced response functions that depend only on momentum transfer \(q\), energy transfer \(\omega\), and missing momentum \(p\). They apply the formalism to closed-shell-minus-one nuclei, specifically \(^{39}\)K described as a \(d_{3/2}\) hole in \(^{40}\)Ca, using two models for the ejected nucleon: a complex optical potential to include final-state interactions phenomenologically, and the factorized plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA). Relativistic effects are incorporated via a new expansion of the electromagnetic current in powers only of the struck nucleon momentum \(\eta = p/M\), not in powers of \(q/2M\) or \(\omega/2M\).

## Key numbers

* The formalism is applied to the nucleus \(^{39}\)K, described as a \(d_{3/2}\) hole in the \(^{40}\)Ca closed-shell core.
* The multipole expansion of the nuclear wave function involves an infinite number of partial waves \(l\), with the index \(\mathcal{J}\) running from \(\mathcal{J}=0,\ldots,2J_i\) for the polarization expansion.
* The cross section is expressed as \(\frac{d\sigma}{d\epsilon'_e d\Omega'_e d\hat{p}'} = \sigma_M [v_L R^L + v_T R^T + v_{TL} R^{TL} + v_{TT} R^{TT} + h(v_{T'} R^{T'} + v_{TL'} R^{TL'})] = \Sigma + h\Delta\), where \(\sigma_M\) is the Mott cross section and \(h\) is the electron helicity.

## Context

This work extends the general formalism for polarized coincidence reactions presented in Ras89 to the specific case of \(\vec{A}(\vec{e},e'N)B\) reactions, building on the authors' prior work on inclusive polarized responses in Ama97. It provides a framework for extracting spin-dependent nuclear spectral functions from polarized targets, enabling studies of the spin distribution of single-particle orbitals in nuclei near closed shells, and goes beyond the PWIA treatment of Cab93 and Cab94 by including final-state interactions through an optical potential.
