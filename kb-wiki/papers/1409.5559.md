# Charmonium Transverse Momentum Distribution in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> The Charmonium transverse momentum distribution is more sensitive to the nature of the hot QCD matter created in high energy nuclear collisions, in comparison with the yield. Taking a detailed transport approach for charmonium motion together with a hydrodynamic description for the medium evolution, the cancelation between the two hot nuclear matter effects, the dissociation and the regeneration, controls the charmonium transverse momentum distribution. Especially, the second moment of the distribution can be used to differentiate between the hot mediums produced at SPS, RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the second moment of the charmonium transverse momentum distribution, \(\langle p_T^2\rangle\), can differentiate between the hot QCD mediums produced at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, because the cancellation between dissociation and regeneration controls the distribution in a way that the integrated yield does not.

## Method

The authors coupled a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model for the medium evolution with a transport equation for charmonium motion, including gluon dissociation and regeneration via detailed balance. The initial charmonium distribution was modified by cold nuclear matter effects (Cronin effect via Gaussian smearing and nuclear shadowing via the EKS98 parametrization), and charm quarks were treated as kinetically thermalized based on experimental evidence from D-meson \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow. The transport equation was solved analytically to obtain the final \(J/\psi\) transverse momentum distribution, \(R_{AA}(p_T)\), and \(\langle p_T^2\rangle\).

## Key numbers

*   The number of \(c\bar c\) pairs in central (0-10%) collisions is \(0.13 \pm 0.03\) at SPS (17.2 GeV Pb+Pb), \(18 \pm 4\) at RHIC (200 GeV Au+Au), and \(110 \pm 65\) at LHC (2.76 TeV Pb+Pb).
*   The maximum temperature of the medium at the starting time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c is \(T_0 = 484\) MeV for central 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions with \(dN_{ch}/dy = 1600\).
*   The critical temperature for the phase transition is taken as \(T_c = 165\) MeV at vanishing baryon number density.
*   The Cronin parameter \(a_{gN}\) is taken as 0.075 (GeV/c)\(^2\)/fm at SPS, 0.1 (GeV/c)\(^2\)/fm at RHIC, and 0.15 (GeV/c)\(^2\)/fm at LHC.
*   The nuclear overlap function \(T_{AA}\) for central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions is \(22 \pm 1.3\) mb\(^{-1}\) and for central Pb+Pb collisions is \(23 \pm 2.1\) mb\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the transport approach for charmonium production previously applied at SPS and RHIC (Xiang and Zhuang) to LHC energies, incorporating a hydrodynamic medium and thermalized charm quarks. It contrasts with static suppression models by dynamically treating both dissociation and regeneration, and it enables the use of the second \(p_T\) moment as a quantitative observable to distinguish the fireball properties across SPS, RHIC, and LHC, where the integrated yield \(R_{AA}\) shows a similar centrality trend despite different energies.
