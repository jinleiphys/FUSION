# Electric dipole polarizability and the neutron skin

> The recent high-resolution measurement of the electric dipole (E1) polarizability (alphad) in 208Pb [Phys. Rev. Lett. 107, 062502 (2011)] provides a unique constraint on the neutron-skin thickness of this nucleus. The neutron-skin thickness (rskin) of 208Pb is a quantity of critical importance for our understanding of a variety of nuclear and astrophysical phenomena. To assess the model dependence of the correlation between alphad and rskin, we carry out systematic calculations for 208Pb, 132Sn, and 48Ca based on the nuclear density functional theory (DFT) using both non-relativistic and relativistic energy density functionals (EDFs). Our analysis indicates that whereas individual models exhibit a linear dependence between alphad and rskin, this correlation is not universal when one combines predictions from a host of different models. By averaging over these model predictions, we provide estimates with associated systematic errors for rskin and alphad for the nuclei under consideration. We conclude that precise measurements of rskin in both 48Ca and 208Pb---combined with the recent measurement of alphad---should significantly constrain the isovector sector of the nuclear energy density functional.

## Key claim

While individual nuclear models show a strong linear correlation between the electric dipole polarizability and the neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb, this correlation is not universal when predictions from a host of different models are combined, yielding a correlation coefficient of only 0.77 across 48 energy density functionals.

## Method

The authors computed the electric dipole strength distribution using both relativistic and non-relativistic density functional theory with 48 different energy density functionals (EDFs), including Skyrme, DD-ME, and NL3/FSU families. For each EDF, the dipole polarizability was obtained from the inverse energy-weighted sum rule of the E1 strength calculated within the random-phase approximation. The neutron-skin thickness was computed as the difference between the root-mean-square neutron and proton radii for each model.

## Key numbers

- Measured electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb: (20.1 ± 0.6) fm³ (from Tamii et al., 2011)
- PREX measurement of neutron radius in 208Pb: r_n = 5.78^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm, corresponding to r_skin = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm
- Model-averaged prediction for r_skin in 208Pb (from 25 EDFs consistent with the RCNP polarizability measurement): (0.168 ± 0.022) fm
- Model-averaged prediction for r_skin in 132Sn: (0.232 ± 0.022) fm
- Model-averaged prediction for r_skin in 48Ca: (0.176 ± 0.018) fm
- Model-averaged prediction for dipole polarizability in 132Sn: (10.081 ± 0.150) fm³
- Model-averaged prediction for dipole polarizability in 48Ca: (2.306 ± 0.089) fm³
- Correlation coefficient across all 48 models for 208Pb: C_AB^models = 0.77
- Within individual systematically varied model families, correlation coefficients exceed 0.99

## Context

This work extends the correlation analysis between dipole polarizability and neutron-skin thickness previously performed within single models such as the Skyrme SV-min model by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010), which found a correlation coefficient of 0.98. By comparing 48 different EDFs, the authors demonstrate significant systematic model dependence that cannot be captured by single-model covariance analyses. The results enable future experimental programs: a higher-precision (1%) PREX measurement of the neutron radius in 208Pb and a measurement of the neutron skin in 48Ca would together provide strong constraints on the isovector sector of nuclear energy density functionals, which is critical for understanding neutron-star structure and atomic parity violation.
