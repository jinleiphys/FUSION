# CC1$π^+$ to CCQE cross sections ratio at accelerator energies

> MiniBooNE and K2K collaborations have recently reported the ratio for the $ν_μ$ induced charge current 1$π^+$ production cross section to the charged current induced quasielastic lepton production cross section i.e. $R(E)=\frac{σ^{CC1π^+}}{σ^{CCQE}}$. In this paper we study, theoretically, in a local density approximation the effect of nuclear medium in charged current quasielastic(CCQE) and charged current one pion production(CC1$π^+$) processes for the $ν_μ$ induced reaction in nuclei. The theoretical results have been compared with the results of experimental observations by the MiniBooNE and K2K collaborations. The ratio R(E) for $ν_μ$ induced reaction in water for the proposed T2K experiment is also presented.

## Key claim

The paper theoretically calculates the ratio R(E) = σ(CC1π⁺)/σ(CCQE) for ν_μ-induced reactions on carbon and oxygen nuclei, finding that nuclear medium effects (including modifications to Δ properties, final-state interactions, and RPA correlations) are essential to reproduce the experimentally observed ratio reported by MiniBooNE and K2K, with agreement better below 1.2 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross sections using a local Fermi gas model with Lindhard functions for Pauli blocking and Fermi motion, incorporating RPA correlations to renormalize weak transition strengths. For charged-current single pion production (CC1π⁺), they employed a Δ(1232) dominance model in the Local Density Approximation, including both incoherent and coherent pion production channels, with medium modifications to the Δ mass and width following Oset et al., and final-state interactions of pions treated via a Monte Carlo simulation for incoherent production and Eikonal approximation for coherent production.

## Context

This work extends theoretical treatments of neutrino-nucleus cross sections by simultaneously incorporating nuclear medium effects in both CCQE and CC1π⁺ channels, building on the delta resonance model of Oset et al. and the local Fermi gas approach of Singh et al. The calculations are compared with experimental results from MiniBooNE (using mineral oil) and K2K (using SciBar/polystyrene), and predictions are provided for the T2K experiment using water targets. The results enable improved interpretation of ν_μ disappearance searches where CC1π⁺ events contribute either as signal or background to CCQE signals, and are consistent with calculations by Leitner et al., Valverde et al., Benhar et al., and Alvarez-Ruso et al. for the respective channels.
