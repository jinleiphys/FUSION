# Searching for the QCD critical point through constant entropy density contours

> We propose a novel method to locate the QCD critical point by constructing an expansion along contours of constant entropy density. Applying two independent analysis of lattice QCD data at zero baryon chemical potential, we find a critical point at $T_c = 114 \pm 7$ MeV and $μ_{B_c} = 602 \pm 62$ MeV for an expansion truncated at order $μ_B^2$. This approach is consistent with recent lattice QCD results up to $μ_B/T \leq 3$. A more precise determination of the required expansion coefficients from lattice simulations will be essential for reliably establishing the location of the QCD critical endpoint.

# Searching for the QCD critical point through constant entropy density contours

## Key claim
The authors propose that the QCD critical point can be located at T_c = 114 ± 7 MeV and μ_B_c = 602 ± 62 MeV by extrapolating lattice QCD data from zero baryon chemical potential along contours of constant entropy density, but only for an expansion truncated at order μ_B^2 and with the caveat that more precise lattice coefficients are needed for a reliable determination.

## Method
The authors expand the temperature along contours of constant entropy density as a function of baryon chemical potential, truncated to order μ_B^2, using the second-order baryon number susceptibility χ_2^B and entropy density s from continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results at μ_B = 0. They then impose the conditions that at the critical point both the first and second derivatives of the temperature with respect to the zero-chemical-potential temperature vanish, yielding equations for the critical temperature and chemical potential. Two independent analyses are performed: one using parametric forms and another using smoothing spline interpolation of the lattice QCD input for s(T) and χ_2^B(T), with error propagation via Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers
- Critical point from parametric lattice input: T_c = 114.3 ± 6.9 MeV, μ_B_c = 602.1 ± 62.1 MeV
- Critical point from spline interpolation: T_c = 119.5 ± 5.4 MeV, μ_B_c = 556.5 ± 49.8 MeV
- Zero-chemical-potential critical temperature parameter: T_0_c = 140.9 ± 2.0 MeV
- The expansion shows agreement with lattice QCD results up to μ_B/T ≤ 3.5
- The S-shaped multi-valued behavior of entropy density develops at μ_B ≳ 602 MeV

## Context
This work extends recent efforts to locate the QCD critical point using lattice QCD data at zero and imaginary chemical potential, including model-based and data-driven approaches by Clarke (2024), Basar (2024), Hippert (2024), Fu (2020), Gunkel (2021), and Gao (2021). The method is consistent with lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal Budapest Collaboration (Borsanyi 2021, 2014) up to μ_B/T ≤ 3. The results suggest that heavy-ion collisions at center-of-mass energies around 4-6 GeV probe the region closest to the critical point. Future work incorporating higher-order terms, analytic continuation from imaginary μ_B (Borsanyi 2025), or direct simulations at non-zero μ_B will be needed to refine the constraints.
