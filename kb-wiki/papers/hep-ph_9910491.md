# Inverse meson mass ordering in color-flavor-locking phase of high density QCD

> We derive the effective Lagrangian for the low-energy massive meson excitations of the color-flavor-locking (CFL) phase of QCD with 3 flavors of light quarks. We compute the decay constants, the maximum velocities, and the masses of the mesons at large baryon chemical potential mu. The decay constants are linear in mu. The meson maximum velocities are close to that of sound. The meson masses in the CFL phase are significantly smaller than in the normal QCD vacuum and depend only on bare quark masses. The order of the meson masses is, to some extent, reversed compared to that in the QCD vacuum. In particular, the lightest particle is eta'.

## Key claim

The meson masses in the color-flavor-locking (CFL) phase of high-density QCD are significantly smaller than in the normal QCD vacuum and depend only on bare quark masses, with the order of masses reversed compared to the vacuum: in particular, the lightest particle is the eta' meson, and neutral kaons are lighter than charged kaons.

## Method

The authors derive the effective chiral Lagrangian for the ten low-energy (pseudo-)Goldstone bosons of the CFL phase at large baryon chemical potential mu, using symmetry arguments and the transformation properties of the diquark condensates X and Y. They compute the decay constants, maximum velocities, and masses by matching the effective theory to microscopic calculations using a low-energy effective theory of fermion modes near the Fermi surface, evaluating one-loop diagrams in the Nambu-Gorkov propagator basis. The mass terms are determined by matching the shift of the vacuum energy from perturbative calculations in the microscopic theory to the effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Decay constant for the pion octet: f_pi approximately 0.209 mu
- Decay constants for the H and eta' mesons: f_H = f_eta' approximately 0.195 mu
- Maximum velocity for all mesons: v_pi = v_H = v_eta' = 1/sqrt(3)
- Coefficient C in the mass term: C approximately 1.578
- Coefficient C' in the mass term: C' approximately 0.04216
- Ratio f_pi^2 / f_eta'^2 = 2(21-8 ln 2)/27 approximately 1.14
- Debye mass squared for the gluon from microscopic calculation: m_{A0}^2 = (21-8 ln 2)/18 * g^2 mu^2 / (2 pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the CFL phase first identified by Alford, Rajagopal and Wilczek (1998), who showed that the diquark condensate locks color and flavor symmetry rotations. The authors build on the effective Lagrangian approach of Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999) and the observation by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) that quark and nuclear matter might be continuously connected. The results enable quantitative predictions for the low-energy spectrum of dense QCD matter relevant to neutron star physics, and demonstrate that the meson mass ordering is inverted compared to the QCD vacuum, with the eta' being the lightest particle rather than the heaviest.
