# Short-distance constraints on hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon

> A key ingredient in the evaluation of hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $(g-2)_μ$ concerns short-distance constraints (SDCs) that follow from QCD by means of the operator product expansion. Here we concentrate on the most important such constraint, in the longitudinal amplitudes, and show that it can be implemented efficiently in terms of a Regge sum over excited pseudoscalar states, constrained by phenomenological input on masses, two-photon couplings, as well as SDCs on HLbL scattering and the pseudoscalar transition form factors (TFFs). Our estimate of the effect of the longitudinal SDCs on the HLbL contribution is: $Δa_μ^\text{LSDC}=13(6)\times 10^{-11}$. This is significantly smaller than previous estimates, which mostly relied on an ad-hoc modification of the pseudoscalar poles and led to up to a $40\%$ increase with respect to the nominal pseudoscalar-pole contributions, when evaluated with modern input for the relevant TFFs. We also comment on the status of the transversal SDCs and, by matching to perturbative QCD, argue that the corresponding correction will be significantly smaller than its longitudinal counterpart.

## Key claim

The longitudinal short-distance constraints on hadronic light-by-light scattering can be implemented via a Regge sum over excited pseudoscalar states, yielding an increase of $\Delta a_\mu^\text{LSDC}=13(6)\times 10^{-11}$, which is significantly smaller than previous estimates that suggested up to a 40% increase relative to the nominal pseudoscalar-pole contributions.

## Method

The authors construct a large-$N_c$ Regge model for the pseudoscalar transition form factors of $\pi^0$, $\eta$, and $\eta'$, including their radial excitations, with masses following a Regge trajectory and couplings constrained by phenomenological input on two-photon couplings and short-distance constraints from the operator product expansion. The model is matched to the perturbative QCD quark loop at a scale $Q_\text{match}=1.7$ GeV, and the contribution to $(g-2)_\mu$ from the mixed kinematic region is evaluated by integrating the BTT decomposition of the HLbL tensor.

## Key numbers

* The total increase from longitudinal short-distance constraints is $\Delta a_\mu^\text{LSDC}=13(6)\times 10^{-11}$.
* The excited-pion poles contribute $\Delta a_\mu^{\pi\text{-poles}}=2.7\,(0.4)_\text{Model}\,(1.2)_\text{syst}\times 10^{-11}$.
* The excited-eta poles contribute $\Delta a_\mu^{\eta\text{-poles}}=3.4^{\,+0.9}_{\,-0.7}\big\vert_\text{Model}\,(0.9)_\text{syst}\times 10^{-11}$.
* The excited-eta-prime poles contribute $\Delta a_\mu^{\eta'\text{-poles}}=6.5\,(1.1)_\text{Model}\,(1.7)_\text{syst}\times 10^{-11}$.
* The combined pseudoscalar-pole increase is $\Delta a_\mu^{\text{PS-poles}}=12.6(4.1)\times 10^{-11}$.
* The matching scale between the Regge model and pQCD is $Q_\text{match}=1.7$ GeV, varied within $\pm 0.5$ GeV.
* The asymptotic constraint for the longitudinal amplitude is $\lim_{Q\to\infty}Q^4\,\bar\Pi_1(Q,Q,Q)=-\frac{4}{9\pi^2}$.
* The mixed-region constraint is $\lim_{Q_3\to\infty}\lim_{Q\to\infty}Q^2Q_3^2\,\bar\Pi_1(Q,Q,Q_3)=-\frac{2}{3\pi^2}$.
* The two-photon coupling of the first excited pion is bounded by $F_{\pi(1300)\gamma\gamma}<0.0544(71)\,\text{GeV}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering developed by Colangelo et al. (2014-2017) and addresses the short-distance constraints originally formulated by Melnikov and Vainshtein (2003). The authors show that the earlier implementation, which neglected the momentum dependence of singly-virtual transition form factors, overestimates the effect by nearly a factor of three compared to the present Regge-model approach. The result reduces the largest systematic uncertainty in the HLbL contribution ahead of the Fermilab E989 experiment, and the matching to pQCD provides a framework for future improvements from higher-order OPE calculations and experimental data on two-photon couplings of excited states in the 1-2 GeV region.
