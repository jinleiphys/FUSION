# Charge-exchange reaction cross sections and the Gamow-Teller strength for double beta decay

> The proportionality between single charge-exchange reaction cross sections in the forward direction as found, for example from $(p,n)$ and $(^3$He,$t)$ and from $(n,p)$ and $(d,^2$He) reactions, and the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength into the same final nuclear states has been studied and/or assumed often in the past. Using the most physically justified theory we have at our disposal and for the specific example of the ${}^{76}$Ge-${}^{76}$Se system that may undergo double beta-decay, we demonstrate that the proportionality is a relative good assumption for reactions changing a neutron into a proton, i.e. ${}^{76}$Ge$(p,n){}^{76}$As. In this channel, the main contribution to the GT strengths comes from the removal of a neutron from an occupied single-particle (SP) state and putting a proton into an unoccupied SP state having either the same state quantum numbers or those of the spin-orbit partner. In contrast to this, in the second leg of the double beta decay a single proton must be taken from an occupied SP state and a neutron placed in an unoccupied one. This second process often is Pauli forbidden in medium-heavy nuclei and only can be effected if the Fermi surface is smeared out. Such is the case for ${}^{76}$Se$(n,p){}^{76}$As. Our results suggest that one may not always assume a proportionality between the forward-angle cross sections of the charge-exchange reactions and the GT strength in any such medium-heavy nuclei. The discrepancy originates from a pronounced effect of the radial dependence of the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction in connection with the Pauli principle on the cross sections in the $(n,p)$ reaction channel. Such a radial dependence is completely absent in the GT transition operator.

## Key claim

The proportionality between forward-angle charge-exchange reaction cross sections and Gamow-Teller (GT) strength, often assumed in the literature, holds well for the (p,n) reaction channel (neutron-to-proton conversion) in the ⁷⁶Ge-⁷⁶Se double-beta-decay system, but fails for the (n,p) channel (proton-to-neutron conversion) in the same medium-heavy nucleus due to Pauli blocking effects combined with the radial dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave approximation (DWA) calculations using the Melbourne effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (g-matrix from the Bonn B potential) and quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) nuclear structure with realistic forces from the Bonn CD potential, using a 9-level basis (pf and sdg major shells) with an oscillator length parameter b = 2.09 fm. They evaluated charge-exchange reaction cross sections for ⁷⁶Ge(p,n)⁷⁶As and ⁷⁶Se(n,p)⁷⁶As to 1⁺ states in the intermediate nucleus, comparing the forward-angle cross sections with the corresponding GT transition strengths B(GT) calculated from the same QRPA wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The particle-particle renormalization factor used in the QRPA was g_pp = 0.85 for the 9-level basis in ⁷⁶Ge.
- The unquenched values g_ph = 1 and g_A = 1.25 were used for the particle-hole channel renormalization factor and axial coupling constant, respectively.
- The harmonic oscillator length parameter for single-particle wave functions was b = 2.09 fm.
- The QRPA calculation for 1⁺ states in ⁷⁶As included 23 two-quasiparticle excitations from N=3 and N=4 oscillator shells.
- Of the 23 two-quasiparticle states, those with ID = 5, 6, 15, 16, 21, and 22 cannot be excited by the GT operator.

## Context

This work extends the proportionality studies of Taddeucci et al. (1987) and Ejiri (2000, 2001) by using more realistic reaction theory (g-folding optical potentials with the Melbourne force) and QRPA nuclear structure, rather than the simpler plane-wave or distorted-wave impulse approximations with free NN amplitudes used previously. The authors specifically address the mass-76 system relevant for double-beta decay, where previous proportionality studies for (n,p)-type reactions had only been investigated up to mass A = 12 (Ejiri, 2000). The results enable more reliable extraction of GT strengths from charge-exchange data for double-beta-decay nuclei and highlight the need for caution when using (n,p) reaction data to test nuclear wave functions for neutrinoless double-beta-decay matrix element calculations.
