# Duality in semi-inclusive pion electroproduction

> We explore quark-hadron duality in semi-inclusive pion electroproduction on proton and neutron targets. Using the spin-flavor symmetric quark model, we compute ratios of pi+ and pi- cross sections for both unpolarized and polarized scattering, and discuss realizations of duality in several symmetry breaking scenarios. The model calculations allow one to understand some of the key features of recent data on semi-inclusive pion production at low energies.

# Duality in Semi-Inclusive Pion Electroproduction

## Key claim

Quark-hadron duality in semi-inclusive pion electroproduction is realized in the spin-flavor symmetric quark model and in several symmetry-breaking scenarios, but only in the leading fragmentation approximation (valid at large $z = E_\pi/\nu$) and at specific $x$ values where the model assumptions hold; the quantitative comparison with data at intermediate $z$ requires including subleading fragmentation effects that do not follow from duality alone.

## Method

The authors computed ratios of $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$ semi-inclusive cross sections for both unpolarized and polarized scattering on proton and neutron targets using a hadronic basis within the SU(6) quark model. They summed coherently over excited nucleon resonances ($N_1^*$) in the $s$-channel intermediate state and final state resonances ($N_2^*$) for the process $\gamma N \to N_1^* \to \pi N_2^*$, where both $N_1^*$ and $N_2^*$ belong to the $\bm{56^+}$ ($L=0$, even parity) and $\bm{70^-}$ ($L=1$, odd parity) multiplets, and compared these hadronic-level results with parton model expectations. The analysis was extended to several SU(6) breaking scenarios: spin-1/2 dominance, scalar diquark dominance, and helicity-1/2 dominance.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(6) symmetric model, the unpolarized $\pi^-$ to $\pi^+$ ratio on the proton is ${\cal N}_p^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^+} = 1/8$, and on the neutron is ${\cal N}_n^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_n^{\pi^+} = 1/2$.
- For the SU(6) symmetric model, the spin-dependent $\pi^-$ to $\pi^+$ ratio on the proton is $\Delta{\cal N}_p^{\pi^-}/\Delta{\cal N}_p^{\pi^+} = -1/16$, and on the neutron is $\Delta{\cal N}_n^{\pi^-}/\Delta{\cal N}_n^{\pi^+} = -1$.
- For the SU(6) symmetric model, the ratio of neutron to proton cross sections for $\pi^+$ production is ${\cal N}_n^{\pi^+}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^+} = 1/2$, and ${\cal N}_n^{\pi^+}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^-} = 4$.
- For the spin-1/2 dominance scenario, the unpolarized $\pi^-$ to $\pi^+$ ratio on the proton is ${\cal N}_p^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^+} = 1/56$, and on the neutron is ${\cal N}_n^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_n^{\pi^+} = 7/2$.
- For the scalar diquark dominance scenario, ${\cal N}_p^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^+} = 0$ and ${\cal N}_n^{\pi^+}/{\cal N}_n^{\pi^-} = 0$, with ${\cal N}_n^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^+} = 1/4$.
- For the helicity-1/2 dominance scenario, the unpolarized $\pi^-$ to $\pi^+$ ratio on the proton is ${\cal N}_p^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^+} = 1/20$, and on the neutron is ${\cal N}_n^{\pi^-}/{\cal N}_n^{\pi^+} = 5/4$.
- For a deuteron target, the ratio of $\pi^+$ to $\pi^-$ cross sections generalizes to ${\cal N}_d^{\pi^+}/{\cal N}_d^{\pi^-} = (4+R)/(4R+1)$, where $R \equiv \overline D/D$ is the ratio of unfavored to favored fragmentation functions.
- The empirical ratio ${\cal N}_p^{\pi^+}/{\cal N}_p^{\pi^-}$ from Jefferson Lab Hall C data at $x=0.32$ and $Q^2 \approx 2.5$ GeV$^2$ is approximately 2 for $0.3 \lesssim z \lesssim 0.7$.

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron duality framework previously developed for inclusive structure functions by Close and Isgur (2001) and by Close and Melnitchouk (2003) to the semi-inclusive case of pion electroproduction. The earlier inclusive studies showed that summing over neighboring positive and negative parity excited states in the $\bm{56}$ and $\bm{70}$ multiplets reproduces parton model results, and the present work demonstrates that the same mechanism applies to semi-inclusive scattering with specific patterns of $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratios. The model calculations help interpret recent Jefferson Lab Hall C data on semi-inclusive pion production at low energies, and the results enable future experimental discrimination between different symmetry-breaking mechanisms by measuring $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratios on proton and neutron targets at large $x$ and large $z$, as well as providing predictions for polarized semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering measurements.
