# Low Mass Dileptons from Pb+Au Collisions at 158 AGeV

> The medium modification of vector meson properties in hot/dense hadronic matter and its role in explaining the CERES/NA45 dilepton data at different centralities are discussed.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of low-mass dilepton yield in Pb+Au collisions at 158 AGeV can be explained by either a quark-gluon plasma initial state or a purely hadronic initial state, provided that vector meson masses shift downward in the medium; the data alone cannot distinguish between these two scenarios.

## Method

The authors calculated dilepton emission rates from a thermal source using the imaginary part of the retarded electromagnetic current correlation function, expressed via vector meson dominance in terms of the spectral functions of the ρ and ω mesons. The space-time evolution of the expanding fireball was modeled using boost-invariant relativistic hydrodynamics, with medium effects incorporated through temperature- and density-dependent hadronic masses from either the universal scaling model or Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD). The resulting spectra were compared to CERES/NA45 data for two centrality bins corresponding to average charged-particle multiplicities ⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 250 and 350.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature Ti = 180 MeV for QGP initial state with ⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 250, evolving through a mixed phase with Tc = 190 MeV
- Initial temperature Ti = 190 MeV for hadronic initial state with ⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 250 in the universal mass scaling scenario
- Initial temperature Ti ≈ 195 MeV for hadronic initial state with ⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 350 and universal scaling
- Initial temperature Ti = 200 MeV for QGP initial state with ⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 350 and Tc = 190 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature TF = 120 MeV
- Assumed thermalization time τi = 1 fm/c
- Enhancement in dilepton yield observed in the mass region 0.3 to 0.6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis of WA98 photon data from Pb+Pb collisions (Sarkar, Alam, and Hatsuda, 2001) to the CERES/NA45 dilepton data from Pb+Au collisions at the same beam energy. The results are consistent with prior calculations showing that a substantial negative shift of vector meson masses (Brown and Rho, 1991) or a large broadening of the ρ meson spectral function due to baryon scattering (Rapp and Wambach, 1999) can explain the observed dilepton enhancement. The study enables further discrimination between competing models of in-medium hadron properties by comparing predictions for both photon and dilepton observables from the same dynamical evolution.
