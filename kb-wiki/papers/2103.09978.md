# $N^*$ states with hidden charm and a three-body nature

> In this work we study the formation of $N^*$'s as a consequence of the dynamics involved in the $ND\bar D^*-N\bar D D^*$ system when the $D\bar D^*-\bar D D^*$ subsystem generates $X(3872)$ in isospin 0 and $Z_c(3900)$ in isospin 1. States with isospin $I=1/2$ and mass in the energy region $4400-4600$ MeV are found to arise with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$, leading to predictions in this way of the existence of $N^*$ resonances with hidden charm and a three-body nature. We also discuss the possibility of the existence of $Δ_c$ states, i.e., $Δ$'s with hidden charm.

# N* states with hidden charm and a three-body nature

## Key claim
The authors predict the existence of N* resonances with hidden charm and positive parity in the energy region 4400–4600 MeV, arising from the three-body dynamics of the ND*D*-ND*D* system when the D*D*-D*D* subsystem forms X(3872) (isospin 0) or Zc(3900) (isospin 1). The claim is conditional on the validity of the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations and on the input two-body amplitudes that generate Lambda_c(2595) from the DN-D*N coupled channels.

## Method
The authors solve Faddeev equations within the fixed center approximation (FCA) for the NX(3872)-NZc(3900) coupled configurations of the ND*D*-ND*D* system. The D*D*-D*D* subsystem is treated as a cluster (X(3872) or Zc(3900)), and the nucleon rescatters successively off the D (D) and D* (D*) mesons forming the cluster. Two-body t-matrices for the ND, ND*, ND, and ND* interactions are taken from models based on SU(4) symmetry (Liang 2014) and SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry (GarciaRecio 2008), and the coupled-channel equations for the partitions T1 and T2 are solved to obtain the three-body T-matrix.

## Key numbers
- Two states with I=1/2, J^P=1/2^+ are found at masses 4410 MeV (width ~2 MeV) and 4560 MeV (width ~20 MeV) using the SU(4)-based model; using the SU(8) model gives 4404 MeV (width ~2 MeV) and 4556 MeV (width ~4 MeV).
- Two states with I=1/2, J^P=3/2^+ are found at 4467 MeV (width ~6 MeV) and 4565 MeV (width ~14 MeV) using the SU(4)-based model; using the SU(8) model gives 4513 MeV (width ~2.6 MeV) and 4558 MeV (width ~4.8 MeV).
- For I=3/2, J^P=1/2^+, two states are found at 4359 MeV (width ~1.5 MeV) and 4512 MeV (width ~4 MeV) using the SU(8) model.
- The ND*D*-ND*D* threshold lies around 4814 MeV.
- The cut-off Lambda used in the form factor is 700 MeV, varied in the range 700–770 MeV, producing shifts of ~3–5 MeV in the masses.

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of hidden-charm pentaquarks observed by LHCb (Aaij 2015, Aaij 2019), specifically Pc(4312), Pc(4440), and Pc(4457), by proposing a three-body mechanism rather than the usual two-body meson-baryon molecular picture. Prior studies (Burns 2019, Geng 2017) suggested that Sigma_c D* - Lambda_c(2595) D molecules could explain the Pc states, but those works treated the system as an effective two-body problem. The present work explicitly includes the three-body dynamics of the ND*D* system, generating positive-parity N* states in the same mass region as the observed Pc states. The I=3/2 states (Delta_c with hidden charm) are a new prediction whose existence depends on the yet-unconfirmed narrow negative-parity Sigma_c states near 2600 MeV predicted by GarciaRecio (2008). The results can be tested by analyzing Lambda_b decay data from LHCb (Aaij 2019, Aaij 2016) and at FAIR experiments.
