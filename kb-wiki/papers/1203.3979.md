# Neutron Rich Hypernuclei in Chiral Soliton Model

> The binding energies of neutron rich strangeness $S=-1$ hypernuclei are estimated in the chiral soliton approach using the bound state rigid oscillator version of the SU(3) quantization model. Additional binding of strange hypernuclei in comparison with nonstrange neutron rich nuclei takes place at not large values of atomic (baryon) numbers, $A=B\leq\sim 10$. This effect becomes stronger with increasing isospin of nuclides, and for "nuclear variant" of the model with rescaled Skyrme constant $e$. Total binding energies of (Lambda)He-8 and recently discovered (Lambda)H-6 satisfactorily agree with experimental data. Hypernuclei (Lambda)H-7, (Lambda)He-9 are predicted to be bound stronger in comparison with their nonstrange analogues H-7, He-9; hypernuclei (Lambda)Li-10, (Lambda)Li-11, (Lambda)Be-12, (Lambda)Be-13, etc. are bound stronger in the nuclear variant of the model.

## Key claim

The chiral soliton approach predicts that neutron-rich hypernuclei with strangeness S=-1 have additional binding energy compared to their nonstrange analogues, but this effect is limited to atomic numbers A ≤ ~10 and becomes stronger with increasing isospin; the quantitative reliability depends on using a rescaled Skyrme constant e=3.0 (the "nuclear variant") rather than the standard value e=4.12.

## Method

The author uses the rigid oscillator version of the SU(3) bound state chiral soliton model, where antikaon fields are bound by SU(2) skyrmions. The mass formula includes strangeness excitation energies, hyperfine splitting corrections, and two subtraction schemes to remove uncertainties from Casimir energies. Calculations are performed for two values of the Skyrme constant (e=4.12 and e=3.0) using previously computed moments of inertia and sigma terms for skyrmion configurations with baryon numbers B=1 through B=16.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of Λ⁶H: theoretical prediction 14.8 MeV (e=4.12) or 16 MeV (e=3.0), compared to experimental value 10.8 ± 1.1 MeV from FINUDA
- Binding energy of Λ⁸He: theoretical prediction 34.8 MeV (e=4.12) or 40 MeV (e=3.0), compared to experimental value 36.0 MeV
- Binding energy difference Δε for Λ⁶H relative to ⁶H: theoretical 9.0 MeV (e=4.12) or 11.2 MeV (e=3.0)
- Binding energy difference Δε for Λ⁸He relative to ⁸He: theoretical 3.4 MeV (e=4.12) or 8.9 MeV (e=3.0)
- For Λ⁷H: theoretical total binding energy 23 MeV (e=4.12) or 24 MeV (e=3.0), with experimental value for ⁷H being 8 MeV (preliminary)
- For Λ⁹He: theoretical total binding energy 30 MeV (e=4.12) or 37 MeV (e=3.0)
- Skyrme constant values used: e=4.12 (standard) and e=3.0 (nuclear variant)
- Pion decay constant Fπ = 186 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton approach previously applied to light hypernuclei by Kopeliovich (2010) and to nuclear isotope mass splittings by Kopeliovich, Shchepkin, and collaborators (2000s). It directly follows the discovery of Λ⁶H by the FINUDA Collaboration (Agnello et al., 2007, 2009) and earlier theoretical work by Dalitz and Levi-Setti (1960s) who first noted that the Lambda particle may act as additional glue for nuclear matter. The results are less precise than traditional methods such as the shell model or Hartree-Fock (Dalitz et al., Shimura et al.), but the CSA provides a unified framework for nuclei with different neutron excesses across a wide range of atomic numbers. The calculations can be extended to hypernuclei with arbitrary neutron excess and to beauty and charm hypernuclei.
