# A Relativistic Thomas-Fermi Description of Collective Modes in Droplets of Nuclear Matter

> Isoscalar collective modes in a relativistic meson-nucleon system are investigated in the framework of the time-dependent Thomas-Fermi method. The energies of the collective modes are determined by solving consistently the dispersion relations and the boundary conditions. The energy weighted sum rule satisfied by the model allows the identification of the giant ressonances. The percentage of the energy weighted sum rule exhausted by the collective modes is in agreement with experimental data, but the energies come too high.

## Key claim

The relativistic Thomas-Fermi model reproduces the percentage of the energy-weighted sum rule exhausted by collective modes in agreement with experimental data, but the calculated energies of the giant resonances come too high compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors extend a nuclear fluid-dynamical model to the Walecka model (quantum hadrodynamics) using a time-dependent Thomas-Fermi method. They linearize the Vlasov equation around equilibrium, introduce scalar and tensor fields to describe monopole and quadrupole deformations of the Fermi surface, and solve the coupled equations of motion for the meson fields and nucleon densities with boundary conditions at the nuclear surface. The eigenvalues are determined by solving dispersion relations consistently with the boundary conditions for a spherical nucleus with constant interior density.

## Key numbers

- For set I parameters (g²_s = 122.88, g²_v = 169.49, p_F = 1.3 fm⁻¹, M*/M = 0.522), two collective monopole modes are identified at 28 MeV and 35 MeV
- For set II parameters (g²_s = 91.64, g²_v = 136.20, p_F = 1.42 fm⁻¹, M*/M = 0.556), collective modes come at slightly higher energy than set I
- The surface tension used is σ_sup = 1.017 MeV/fm² from the liquid drop model
- For l=2⁺ and set I, a vector meson mode appears at ℏω = 984.56 MeV exhausting 27.30% of the EWSR
- In infinite nuclear matter at small momentum transfer, about 62% of the EWSR is exhausted by continuum nuclear modes and about 38% by vector meson modes
- Calculations are performed for a nucleus with A = 208

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approximation to the Walecka model developed by Nos1 and Nos2 (1990s) for nuclear matter to finite nuclei, using the fluid-dynamical model of Vos1 and Vos2 (1990s) that incorporates monopole and quadrupole Fermi surface distortions. It lifts the restrictions of irrotational flow and large vector meson mass assumed in the earlier work by Fuse (1990s). The approach enables the study of collective excitations in finite nuclear systems within relativistic quantum hadrodynamics while avoiding the problematic space-like poles that appear in one-loop meson propagator calculations.
