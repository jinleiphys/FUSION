# Polarization observables in dp backward elastic scattering at high and intermediate energies

> The tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ and the polarization transfer coefficients $κ_0 (= 3/2 K_y^y)$ and $K_{xz}^y$ are investigated for dp backward elastic scattering by the invariant-amplitude method. Discrepancies between the conventional calculations and the experimental data on $T_{20}$ and $κ_0$ at high and intermediate energies are mostly dissolved by including imaginary parts in the amplitudes. The quantity $K_{xz}^y$ is shown to be useful in criticizing nuclear force assumptions.

## Key claim

By including imaginary parts in the invariant amplitudes of the one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) model, the discrepancies between conventional calculations and experimental data for the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) and the polarization transfer coefficient \(\kappa_0\) in dp backward elastic scattering at high and intermediate energies are mostly dissolved, though the authors assume the phase parameters \(\Theta\) and \(\Theta'\) to be independent of momentum transfer \(k\) for convenience, with no justification for that assumption.

## Method

The authors derived exact formulae for the polarization observables \(T_{20}\), \(\kappa_0\), and \(K_{xz}^y\) using the invariant-amplitude method in a non-relativistic framework, assuming the ONE mechanism. They calculated the relative magnitudes \(R\) and \(R'\) of the tensor amplitudes via the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) using deuteron wave functions from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris, RSC, Nijmegen, Bonn B), while treating the phase angles \(\Theta\) and \(\Theta'\) as free parameters to incorporate imaginary parts. The observables were then evaluated as functions of the momentum transfer \(k\) by varying the ratio \(r = w(k)/u(k)\) of the D- to S-wave Fourier transforms of the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- The zero point of the S-wave Fourier transform \(u(k)\), denoted \(k_0\), is 0.39 GeV/c for the RSC potential and 0.45 GeV/c for the Bonn B potential.
- In the PWIA limit, at \(r=0\), \(T_{20} = 0\) and \(\kappa_0 = 1\); in the limit \(r \rightarrow \infty\), \(T_{20} = -1/\sqrt{2}\) and \(\kappa_0 = -1\).
- The experimental data for \(T_{20}\) show a local maximum in the range \(k = 0.25\)–\(0.45\) GeV/c.
- For the set \((\Theta, \Theta') = (135^\circ, 30^\circ)\), the calculations describe the \(T_{20}\) data very well except for the structure around \(k=0.44\) GeV/c.
- The quantity \(K_{xz}^y\) vanishes at \(k = k_0\), where \(k_0 = 0.39\) GeV/c for RSC and 0.45 GeV/c for Bonn B.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent formulae for \(T_{20}\) and \(\kappa_0\) in (d,p) reactions derived by Tanifuji et al. (1995), which were applicable only at low energies due to additional approximations, to treat dp backward elastic scattering at high and intermediate energies without such restrictions. The results contradict the earlier PWIA calculations by Vasan (1973) and Koike (1994), which predicted a circular relation in the \(\kappa_0\)–\(T_{20}\) plane that disagreed with experimental data from Punjabi et al. (1995). The findings enable future quantitative reproduction of data by allowing \(\Theta\) and \(\Theta'\) to vary with \(k\), and suggest that the \(\Theta\) effect (important at low energies) may originate from non-mesonic phenomena like virtual deuteron breakup, while the \(\Theta'\) effect (important at high energies) likely arises from mesonic effects including \(\Delta\) and other baryon resonances, as previously investigated by Boudard et al. (1985) and Nakamura et al. (1985).
