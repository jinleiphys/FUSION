# Collective perspective on advances in Dyson-Schwinger Equation QCD

> We survey contemporary studies of hadrons and strongly interacting quarks using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, addressing: aspects of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking; the hadron spectrum; hadron elastic and transition form factors, from small- to large-Q^2; parton distribution functions; the physics of hadrons containing one or more heavy quarks; and properties of the quark gluon plasma.

# Collective perspective on advances in Dyson-Schwinger Equation QCD

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework provides the only extant continuum approach that can simultaneously connect both meson and baryon observables with dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) via a single interaction kernel, but the quality of predictions depends critically on whether the dressed-quark mass-function and diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes possess QCD-like momentum-dependence rather than being momentum-independent.

## Method

The authors employ Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equations for baryons, which sum all possible exchanges between three dressed-quarks, retaining scalar (0+) and axial-vector (1+) diquark correlations as the dominant components for ground-state octet and decuplet baryons. The Faddeev equation kernel is built from dressed-quark propagators obtained from the gap equation and diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, with the interaction preserving QCD's one-loop renormalisation group behaviour. Nucleon electromagnetic form factors are computed using a conserved current constrained by vector Ward-Takahashi identities, with the current involving eight-dimensional integrals evaluated via Monte-Carlo techniques.

## Key numbers

- In the nucleon's rest frame, only 37% of the total spin is contained within components of the Faddeev amplitude that possess zero quark orbital angular momentum.
- The DSE study produces the Roper resonance as a radial excitation of the nucleon at 1.82 ± 0.07 GeV.
- The unified spectrum of u,d-quark hadrons produces a root-mean-square-relative-error per degree of freedom of 13% when compared with PDG values.
- The ratio d_v/u_v at x→1 is computed as 0.28 for a momentum-dependent mass-function and 0.18 for a constant mass-function, both consistent with the experimentally inferred value of 0.23 ± 0.09 (90% confidence level).
- The corresponding F_2^n/F_2^p ratio is 0.49 for momentum-dependent and 0.41 for constant mass-function, consistent with the inferred value of 0.45 ± 0.08.

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach to baryons by building on meson studies (Chang, 2011) and earlier Faddeev equation treatments (Eichmann, 2008; Cloet, 2008) to achieve a unified description of meson and baryon spectra and form factors. The results demonstrate that the Roper resonance, discovered in 1963 and previously unexplained by symmetry-preserving QCD tools, is the proton's first radial excitation whose dressed-quark core is obscured by a dense cloud of pions and other mesons, consistent with analyses from the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (Suzuki, 2009). The framework enables future feedback between experiments at JLab in the 12 GeV era and theory to refine understanding of the dressed-quark mass function and QCD's beta-function.
