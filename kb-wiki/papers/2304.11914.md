# Investigating the impact of extra resonance states in the van der Waals Hadron Resonance Gas Model

> We investigate, in addition to the experimentally established hadrons, how the inclusion of extra resonance states, through the Hagedorn mass spectrum (HS) or Quark Model (QM) predicated states, affects the thermodynamic and transport quantities of the hadronic system in the van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model. We found that the VDWHRG model with the HS provides the most accurate description of the lattice QCD results, both at zero and finite chemical potential. Moreover, the inclusion of these extra states has a significant impact on the van der Waals (VDW) parameters, which, in turn, affect the thermodynamic and transport quantities as well as the likely position of the liquid-gas phase transition critical point in the QCD phase diagram. Additionally, we infer that there is a strong correlation between the van der Waals parameters and the chemical potential. Overall, our study sheds light on the importance of considering extra resonance states and proper tuning of the VDW parameters in the VDWHRG model to enhance the accuracy and reliability of the model in the context of Ultra-relativistic heavy-ion physics.

## Key claim

The van der Waals Hadron Resonance Gas (VDWHRG) model that includes extra resonance states via the Hagedorn mass spectrum provides the most accurate description of lattice QCD results for thermodynamic quantities at both zero and finite baryon chemical potential, but the fitted van der Waals parameters show a strong dependence on the chemical potential and on which extra states are included.

## Method

The authors fitted four variants of the VDWHRG model (VDW, VDW+excluded-volume for mesons, VDW+EV+HS, and VDW+EV+QM) to lattice QCD data for pressure, energy density, entropy density, specific heat, and speed of sound at zero chemical potential (from Borsanyi 2013) and at finite chemical potential (from Bazavov 2017). The van der Waals parameters a (attraction) and b (repulsion), and the Hagedorn normalization constant C_H, were extracted via chi-squared minimization over a temperature range of 110 to 180 MeV. The Hagedorn spectrum used a threshold mass M_0 = 2 GeV, a = 5/4, and T_H = 180 MeV.

## Key numbers

* At zero chemical potential, the VDW+EV+HS model achieved the best fit with chi-squared/ndf = 0.15, compared to 0.65 for VDW, 0.28 for VDW+EV, and 0.42 for VDW+EV+QM.
* At zero chemical potential, the fitted van der Waals parameters for the VDW+EV+HS model were a = 1.33 +/- 0.018 MeV.fm^3 and b = 5.30 +/- 0.07 fm^3, with C_H = 0.060 +/- 0.003 GeV^(3/2).
* At finite chemical potential (mu_B/T = 1 and 2), the VDW+EV+HS model again gave the best fit with chi-squared/ndf = 0.11, with fitted parameters a = 0.710 +/- 0.012 MeV.fm^3 and b = 3.58 +/- 0.05 fm^3, and C_H = 0.065 +/- 0.002 GeV^(3/2).
* The repulsive parameter b decreased from 5.78 +/- 0.08 fm^3 (VDW at zero mu_B) to 3.80 +/- 0.05 fm^3 (VDW at finite mu_B), showing strong chemical potential dependence.

## Context

This work extends the VDWHRG model of Vovchenko (2015, 2016) by incorporating extra resonance states via the Hagedorn mass spectrum and Quark Model predictions, following earlier studies by Alba (2017) and Karthein (2021) that showed additional states improve lattice agreement. The finding that the Hagedorn normalization constant C_H is much smaller (0.06 GeV^(3/2)) than in previous work by Majumder (2010) (0.63 GeV^(3/2)) is attributed to the attractive interaction partially mimicking the effect of extra states. The results enable more reliable predictions of the QCD critical point location and thermodynamic properties for heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC BES, FAIR, and NICA.
