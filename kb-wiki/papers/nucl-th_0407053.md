# General Survey of Polarization Observables in Deuteron Electrodisintegration

> Polarization observables in inclusive and exclusive electrodisintegration of the deuteron using a polarized beam and an oriented target are systematically surveyed using the standard nonrelativistic framework of nuclear theory but with leading order relativistic contributions included. The structure functions and the asymmetries corresponding to the various nucleon polarization components are studied in a variety of kinematic regions with respect to their sensitivity to realistic $NN$-potential models, to subnuclear degrees of freedom in terms of meson exchange currents, isobar configurations and to relativistic effects in different kinematical regions, serving as a benchmark for a test of present standard nuclear theory with effective degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper systematically surveys polarization observables in deuteron electrodisintegration using a nonrelativistic framework with leading-order relativistic contributions, but the results serve as a benchmark for testing present standard nuclear theory with effective degrees of freedom rather than providing definitive predictions.

## Method

The authors compute structure functions and asymmetries for inclusive and exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration using a standard nonrelativistic nuclear theory framework with leading-order relativistic corrections included. They perform multipole expansions of the structure functions and form factors, and derive explicit expressions for the separation of specific structure functions from asymmetry measurements at various combinations of electron and deuteron polarization angles.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized form factors are given by \(F_L = \frac{16 \pi^2}{3} \sum_{Lj \mu }\frac{e^{-2\rho_\mu^j}}{2L+1} |C^L (\mu j) |^2\) and \(F_T = \frac{16 \pi^2}{3} \sum_{Lj \mu }\frac{e^{-2\rho_\mu^j}}{2L+1} (|E^L (\mu j) |^2 +|M^L (\mu j) |^2)\)
- The vector polarization form factor \(F_{LT} ^{\prime 1-1}\) equals \(32 \pi^2 \sqrt 2 \sum_{LL'j \mu } (-)^{j} \left(\begin{matrix} L'&L& 1 \cr 0 &-1&1 \cr\end{matrix}\right) \bigg\{ \begin{matrix} L'&L&1 \cr 1&1&j \cr\end{matrix} \bigg\}e^{-2\rho_\mu^j}\, \Re e [C^{L'} (\mu j)^* N^L_1 (\mu j)]\)
- The tensor polarization form factor \(F_{L} ^{20}\) equals \(-16 \pi^2 \sqrt{\frac{5}{3}} \sum_{LL'j \mu } (-)^{j} \left(\begin{matrix} L'&L& 2 \cr 0 &0&0 \cr\end{matrix}\right) \bigg\{ \begin{matrix} L'&L&2 \cr 1&1&j \cr\end{matrix} \bigg\}e^{-2\rho_\mu^j}\, \Re e [C^{L'} (\mu j)^* C^L (\mu j)]\)
- For a set of \(n\) independent \(t\)-matrix elements, there are exactly \((n-1)^2\) quadratic relations between observables, reducing the \(n^2\) linearly independent observables to \(2n-1\) independent ones
- The electron asymmetry \(A_e(X)\) contains only \(f_{LT}'(X)\), requiring just one asymmetry measurement for determination
- The angle \(\theta_d^0\) used in several separation formulas is \(\theta_d^0=\mbox{arcos}\,(1/\sqrt{3})\)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Arenhövel, Leidemann, and Tomusiak (2002) by providing a complete survey of polarization observables in deuteron electrodisintegration with explicit multipole expansions and separation schemes. The paper enables experimentalists to design measurements that isolate specific structure functions, with the multipole expressions for inclusive form factors having been reported previously in Leidemann and Tomusiak (1991). The results serve as a benchmark for testing standard nuclear theory with effective degrees of freedom, including realistic NN-potential models, meson exchange currents, and isobar configurations.
