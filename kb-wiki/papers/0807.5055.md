# Study of the derivative expansions for the nuclear structure functions

> We study the convergence of the series expansions sometimes used in the analysis of the nuclear effects in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) proccesses induced by leptons. The recent advances in statistics and quality of the data, in particular for neutrinos calls for a good control of the theoretical uncertainties of the models used in the analysis. Using realistic nuclear spectral functions which include nucleon correlations, we find that the convergence of the derivative expansions to the full results is poor except at very low values of $x$.

# Study of the derivative expansions for the nuclear structure functions

## Key claim
The derivative expansions commonly used to approximate nuclear effects in deep inelastic scattering converge poorly to the full results for realistic nuclear spectral functions that include nucleon correlations, except at very low values of $x$, contradicting the earlier assumption that the expansions are a good approximation for $x \leq 0.5$.

## Method
The authors computed the nuclear structure functions $F_2^A$ and $F_3^A$ for $^{40}Ca$ using the full convolution integral with realistic nuclear spectral functions (including nucleon correlations), then compared these to the results from Taylor-expanded approximations truncated to order $\epsilon/M$ and $\mathbf{p}^2/M^2$. Four different spectral functions were tested: three with correlations (Kulagin et al. 2004, Ankowski et al. 2007, Fernandez de Cordoba et al. 1991) and one mean-field model without correlations (Smith et al. 2002).

## Key numbers
- The mean removal energy $\langle \epsilon \rangle$ for the three correlated spectral functions ranges from -40 MeV (Ankowski et al. 2007) to -49 MeV (Kulagin et al. 2004).
- The mean kinetic energy per nucleon $\langle T \rangle = \langle \mathbf{p}^2 / 2M \rangle$ ranges from 26.2 MeV (Ankowski et al. 2007) to 30.1 MeV (Kulagin et al. 2004).
- The maximum deviation between the expansion and full result for $R_2 = F_{2A}/F_2$ is 3–4% around $x = 0.5$–$0.6$ at $Q^2 = 20$ GeV$^2$, which corresponds to increasing the deviation from unity by approximately 30%.
- For the mean-field spectral function (IV) without correlations, the expansion shows "almost perfect agreement" with the full result.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of derivative expansions for nuclear structure functions previously used by Frankfurt et al. (1985), Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1989, 1990, 1991), and Kulagin (1997). The expansions had been assumed valid for $x \leq 0.5$ based on earlier studies with simpler spectral functions. The authors show that including nucleon correlations, which produce high-momentum components, degrades convergence, and that the expansions systematically underestimate nuclear corrections at intermediate $x$ and overestimate them at high $x$. This finding is relevant for neutrino experiments (e.g., NuTeV) that require precise control of theoretical uncertainties in nuclear structure functions.
