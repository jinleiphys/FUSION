# Pseudospin symmetry and structure of nuclei with $Z\ge$ 100

> In the framework of the Relativistic Mean Field Approach a pseudospin dependence of the residual forces in nuclei is considered. It is shown that this dependence is relatively weak. As a consequence, a pseudospin dependence of the particle--core coupling is weak as well. This leads to a small splitting of the pseudospin doublets produced by a vector coupling of an odd particle pseudospin and a pseudo--orbital momentum of the core. Some possibilities for experimental investigations of the manifestations of the pseudospin symmetry in the spectra of odd nuclei with $Z\ge$ 100 are indicated.

## Key claim

The pseudospin dependence of the residual forces and of the particle-core coupling in nuclei is relatively weak, leading to small splittings of pseudospin doublets in odd deformed nuclei; however, this conclusion is conditional on the approximate equality of the scalar and vector potentials in the relativistic mean field, which is not exact because a perfect equality would eliminate the binding potential for nucleons.

## Method

The authors derive a separable residual interaction from the relativistic mean field approach by expanding the time-dependent mean field and density in powers of collective deformation parameters, then express the formfactor of the particle-core coupling in terms of pseudospin and pseudo-orbital momentum operators. Using empirical values for the scalar and vector potentials (V₀s and V₀v) and nuclear geometry parameters (R = 7 fm, a = 0.6 fm), they extract the coefficients c and b that quantify the pseudospin-independent and pseudospin-dependent parts of the interaction. The splitting of pseudospin doublets in rotational bands of odd deformed nuclei is then estimated from the ratio b/c and the average value of the pseudospin-orbit operator.

## Key numbers

- The scalar and vector potentials satisfy V₀s – V₀v ≈ 50 MeV and V₀s + V₀v = 700–800 MeV (inside the nucleus).
- The ratio Vₛ/Vᵥ ≈ –1.1 from QCD sum rules, with the sigma term σ_N ≈ 45 ± 8 MeV.
- For nuclear radius R = 7 fm and diffusion parameter a = 0.6 fm, the extracted coefficients are c ≈ 550 MeV and b ≈ 45 MeV.
- The pseudospin-dependent part of the particle-core coupling is weaker than the pseudospin-independent part by a factor of approximately 2(b/c)⟨l̃·s̃⟩ ≈ 1/360 (for N ≈ 30 particles in the open shell).
- The estimated splitting of pseudospin doublets in rotational bands of odd deformed nuclei is ∼7 keV (proportional to a constant l̃₀ of order unity), consistent with experimentally observed splittings of 10–30 keV.
- In the spectrum of ¹⁸⁷Os₁₁₁, the splitting of states in doublets does not exceed 10 keV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of pseudospin symmetry, originally identified by Arima, Hecht, and Ginocchio, by deriving the pseudospin dependence of the residual interaction and particle-core coupling from the relativistic mean field framework. The authors build on the demonstration by Ginocchio that pseudospin symmetry arises when Vₛ/Vᵥ ≈ –1, and on calculations by Ring and Arima showing that the symmetry improves with decreasing binding energy and pseudo-orbital momentum. The paper enables experimental searches for pseudospin symmetry manifestations in odd superheavy nuclei (Z ≥ 100), specifically in rotational bands based on pseudospin singlets or doublets with Λ = 1, as predicted by the calculations of Parchomenko and Sobiczewski for Md, Lr, and Z = 111 isotopes.
