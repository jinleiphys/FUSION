# A novel Bayesian approach to spectral function reconstruction

> We present a novel approach to the inference of spectral functions from Euclidean time correlator data that makes close contact with modern Bayesian concepts. Our method differs significantly from the maximum entropy method (MEM). A new set of axioms is postulated for the prior probability, leading to an improved expression, which is devoid of the asymptotically flat directions present in the Shanon-Jaynes entropy. Hyperparameters are integrated out explicitly, liberating us from the Gaussian approximations underlying the evidence approach of the MEM. We present a realistic test of our method in the context of the non-perturbative extraction of the heavy quark potential. Based on hard-thermal-loop correlator mock data, we establish firm requirements in the number of data points and their accuracy for a successful extraction of the potential from lattice QCD. An improved potential estimation from previously investigated quenched lattice QCD correlators is provided.

# A novel Bayesian approach to spectral function reconstruction

## Key claim

The authors present a Bayesian spectral function reconstruction method that replaces the Shannon-Jaynes entropy used in the maximum entropy method (MEM) with a new prior functional that lacks asymptotically flat directions, and integrates out the hyperparameter α explicitly rather than relying on Gaussian approximations; however, the method's success in extracting the heavy quark potential from lattice QCD correlators is demonstrated only for the real part at Nτ=32, while the imaginary part requires Nτ=128 with relative error ΔD/D=10⁻⁵ to achieve sub-20% accuracy.

## Method

The method inverts the convolution D(τ)=∫dω K(ω,τ)ρ(ω) connecting a Euclidean correlator to a spectral function ρ(ω)>0, using a likelihood probability P[D|ρ,I]=exp[-L-γ(L-Nτ)²] with γ→∞ to enforce L/Nτ=1. The prior probability P[ρ|I]∝exp[S] uses a new functional S=α∫dω (1-ρ/m+ln(ρ/m)) derived from axioms of subset independence, scale invariance, smoothness, and maximum at the prior; the hyperparameter α is integrated out from the joint probability P[ρ,D,α,m] assuming no prior knowledge (P[α]=1), and the maximum of the resulting α-independent posterior P[ρ|D,m] is found numerically using the LBFGS quasi-Newton algorithm.

## Key numbers

- Wilson Loop spectra: reconstruction uses Nω=1000 frequency points over ω∈[-126,189] GeV; Wilson line correlator spectra use Nω=1200 points over ω∈[-15.7,15.7] GeV
- At T=2.33Tc, the real part of the HTL inter-quark potential is reproduced from Wilson Loop correlators with sub-10% accuracy using Nτ=32 data points at relative error ΔD/D=10⁻⁴
- The real part from Wilson line correlators is reproduced with sub-1% accuracy using Nτ=32 at ΔD/D=10⁻²
- The imaginary part of the HTL potential requires Nτ=128 data points at ΔD/D=10⁻⁵ to achieve sub-20% deviation (shown for Wilson line correlators only)
- Lattice QCD data: quenched simulation at T=2.33Tc with Nτ=32, β=7, aσ=0.0039 fm, ξ=4

## Context

This work extends the maximum entropy method (MEM) as formulated by Jarrell and Gubernatis (1996) and previously applied to QCD spectral functions by Asakawa (2000, 2003), Nickel (2006), and Rothkopf (2011). The new prior functional cures the asymptotically flat directions present in the Shannon-Jaynes entropy that cause slow convergence and scale dependence in MEM, while the explicit integration of the hyperparameter α avoids the Gaussian approximation required in the MEM evidence approach of Jarrell (1996). The method enables more reliable extraction of the heavy quark potential from lattice QCD correlators, addressing challenges identified in Burnier (2013) where MEM failed to reproduce even the Lorentzian shape of HTL spectral peaks.
