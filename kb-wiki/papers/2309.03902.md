# Universality of $nn$ distributions of $s$-wave $2n$ halos and the unitary limit

> We calculate neutron-neutron relative-energy distributions of $s$-wave two-neutron ($2n$) halo nuclei using Halo Effective Field Theory (Halo EFT) at leading order. At this order these systems are described by the $2n$ separation energy, the neutron-core ($nc$) virtual-state energy and the neutron-neutron ($nn$) scattering length. We focus on knockout reactions where the removal of the core is sudden, such that the final-state interactions are dominated by the $nn$ interaction. We consider the neutron relative-energy distribution for the nuclei $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be, $^{17}$B, $^{19}$B, and $^{22}$C. We show that the ground-state neutron momentum distributions of all these nuclei stem from a single curve, which can be obtained by taking both the neutron-core and neutron-neutron interaction to the unitary limit. This universal description can be extended to the final distribution measured in experiment by including $nn$ final-state interactions via the approximate technique of enhancement factors. For all the nuclei considered we find good agreement between the full leading-order Halo EFT calculation and the universal prediction obtained in this way. The universality of the ground-state momentum distribution in two-neutron Borromean halos can thus be tested by dividing the experimental results from sudden core knockout by the enhancement factor and comparing to the unitary-limit prediction.

## Key claim

The ground-state neutron relative-energy distributions of s-wave two-neutron halo nuclei collapse onto a single universal curve when both the neutron-core and neutron-neutron interactions are taken to the unitary limit, but this universality is only visible after dividing out the overall normalization and is obscured in experimental observables by final-state interactions that introduce a different energy scale set by the neutron-neutron scattering length.

## Method

The authors performed leading-order Halo Effective Field Theory calculations of the neutron-neutron relative-energy distributions for five Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei (¹¹Li, ¹⁴Be, ¹⁷B, ¹⁹B, and ²²C). They solved the coupled Faddeev equations for the particle-dimer amplitudes, constructed the three-body wave functions, and computed both the ground-state distributions and the distributions after including neutron-neutron final-state interactions via the t-matrix. The unitary-limit predictions were obtained by setting the inverse scattering lengths of both the neutron-neutron and neutron-core interactions to zero while retaining a three-body force tuned to reproduce each nucleus's two-neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

* The two-neutron separation energies used are: ¹¹Li (369 keV), ¹⁴Be (1266 keV), ¹⁷B (1384 keV), ¹⁹B (90 keV), and ²²C (100 keV)
* The neutron-core virtual-state energies are: ¹¹Li (26 keV), ¹⁴Be (510 keV), ¹⁷B (83 keV), ¹⁹B (5 keV), and ²²C (68 keV)
* Deviations of the universal unitary-limit prediction from the full leading-order Halo EFT ground-state distributions are smaller than 20% for ¹¹Li, ¹⁴Be, ¹⁷B, and ¹⁹B in the energy range from 0 to 4 times the two-neutron separation energy, and slightly higher for ²²C
* The deviations of the exact neutron-neutron t-matrix from the unitary-limit t-matrix at the two-neutron separation energies are approximately 23% for ¹⁴Be and ¹⁷B, 36% for ¹¹Li, 52% for ²²C, and 53% for ¹⁹B
* The final-state enhancement factor approximation reproduces the full leading-order EFT calculations with neutron-neutron final-state interactions to better than 20% accuracy over almost the entire kinematic range

## Context

This work extends the philosophy of Konig et al. (2016), who proposed understanding light nuclei in pionless EFT as an expansion around the unitary limit with a three-body force, to clustered two-neutron halo systems. It builds on prior Halo EFT calculations for the p-wave halo ⁶He by Gobel et al. (2021) and on the Faddeev equation framework established by Canham (2008) and Hammer (2017). The results enable experimental tests at rare isotope beam factories: by dividing measured neutron relative-energy distributions from sudden core knockout reactions by the final-state enhancement factor, the universal ground-state prediction can be compared directly to data. The authors note that a kinematically complete measurement of the ⁶He(p,p'alpha)nn reaction is already approved at RIKEN for measuring the neutron-neutron scattering length.
