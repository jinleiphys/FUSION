# On Cooling of Neutron Stars With Stiff Equation of State Including Hyperons

> The existence of high mass ($\sim 2M_{\odot}$) pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 requires the compact star matter to be described by a stiff equation of state (EoS). Presence of hyperons in neutron stars leads to a softening of the EoS that results in a decrease of the maximum neutron-star mass below the measured values of masses for PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 pulsars if one exploits ordinary relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (hyperon puzzle). However, within an RMF EoS with a sigma-scaled hadron effective masses and coupling constants, the maximum neutron-star mass remains above $2M_{\odot}$ even when hyperons are included. Also, other important constraints on the equation of state, e.g. the flow constraint from heavy-ion collisions are to be fulfilled. We demonstrate how a satisfactory explanation of all existing observational data for the temperature-age relation is reached within the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario with a relativistic-mean-field EoS with a sigma-scaled hadron effective masses and coupling constants including hyperons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario can satisfactorily explain all existing observational data for the temperature-age relation of neutron stars, provided one uses a stiff relativistic-mean-field equation of state with sigma-scaled hadron effective masses and coupling constants that includes hyperons, thereby resolving the hyperon puzzle while maintaining a maximum neutron-star mass above 2 solar masses.

## Method

The authors used the MKVORHφ equation of state (EoS) from previous work (Maslov 2015), which includes hyperons and uses sigma-field-dependent hadron effective masses and coupling constants, to compute neutron star cooling histories. They adopted neutrino emissivities, specific heat, crust properties, and pairing gaps from earlier works on the nuclear medium cooling scenario, including medium-modified Urca (MMU) processes, medium-modified nucleon bremsstrahlung (MNB) processes, and pair-breaking-formation (PBF) processes, with proton pairing gaps from Grigorian (2016) and Λ hyperon pairing gaps from Takatsuka and Tamagaki (2000, 2006). The effective pion gap density dependence was taken from previous work (Grigorian 2016), with saturation assumed above a critical density varied from 1.5 to 3 times nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star mass for MKVOR model (without hyperons): 2.33 solar masses, with direct Urca reactions allowed for masses above 2.14 solar masses
- Maximum neutron-star mass for MKVORHφ model (with hyperons): 2.22 solar masses
- Λ hyperons appear at density n_c^(Λ) = 2.63 n_0 (where n_0 is nuclear saturation density)
- Ξ- hyperons appear at density n_c^(Ξ-) = 2.93 n_0
- Direct Urca reactions on Λ hyperons become allowed for neutron star masses above 1.43 solar masses
- Direct Urca reactions involving Ξ- hyperons become allowed for masses above 1.65 solar masses
- Reference masses for high-mass pulsars: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.928 ± 0.017 solar masses (Fonseca 2016) and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses (Antoniadis 2013)

## Context

This work extends the nuclear medium cooling scenario previously applied to the HHJ, HDD, DD2, and DD2vex equations of state (Blaschke 2004, 2011, 2013; Grigorian 2016) to the MKVORHφ model that includes hyperons while satisfying the heavy-ion collision flow constraints (Danielewicz 2002; Lynch 2009). The paper resolves the "hyperon puzzle" (Schaffner-Bielich 2008; Djapo 2008) by showing that a stiff EoS with sigma-scaled masses can simultaneously accommodate 2-solar-mass pulsars, flow constraints, and observed neutron star cooling data, enabling future comparisons with gravitational wave constraints from LIGO-Virgo (2017).
