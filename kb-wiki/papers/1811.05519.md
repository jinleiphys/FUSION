# Acoplanarity of Lepton Pair to Probe the Electromagnetic Property of Quark Matter

> We investigate the $P_T$-broadening effects in dilepton production through photon-photon scattering in heavy ion collisions. The QED multiple interaction effects with the medium is found to be consistent with a recent observation of low transverse momentum lepton pair from ATLAS collaboration at the LHC. We further comment on the magnetic effects and point out a number of ways to disentangle these two mechanisms. In particular, the rapidity dependence of the $P_T$-broadening effects provide a unique probe to the magnetic effects.

## Key claim

The observed acoplanarity of lepton pairs from photon-photon scattering in heavy ion collisions can be explained by QED multiple scattering of the leptons in the quark-gluon plasma, with the effective QED transport coefficient \(\langle \hat q_{QED}L\rangle\) ranging from \((50\ \mathrm{MeV})^2\) to \((100\ \mathrm{MeV})^2\), but the magnetic field contribution is not ruled out and can be disentangled via rapidity dependence.

## Method

The authors compute the transverse momentum broadening of dileptons produced via \(\gamma\gamma\to\ell^+\ell^-\) in heavy ion collisions by combining the photon flux from the colliding nuclei with all-order QED Sudakov resummation of soft photon radiation. For central collisions, they add a medium-induced broadening factor \(\exp(-\langle\hat q_{QED}L\rangle r_\perp^2/4)\) derived from a QED dipole formalism analogous to QCD jet quenching. They also model the magnetic field contribution as proportional to \([\tanh(y_+)-\tanh(y_-)]^2\) and compare the predicted acoplanarity distributions to ATLAS data.

## Key numbers

- Effective QED transport coefficient: \(\langle \hat q_{QED}L\rangle\) ranges from \((100\ \mathrm{MeV})^2\) in most central collisions to \((50\ \mathrm{MeV})^2\) in non-central collisions.
- Ratio of QED to QCD saturation scales for quark jets: \(\langle \hat q_{QED}L\rangle / \langle \hat q_{QCD}L\rangle = (\alpha_e^2/\alpha_s^2) \times (7/15)\) for \(N_f=3\).
- Typical transverse momentum width of incoming photon flux: approximately \(40\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from a Gaussian fit to STARlight simulation.
- Lepton transverse momentum cut: \(P_\perp > 4\ \mathrm{GeV}\); invariant mass range: \(10\ \mathrm{GeV} < M_{\mu\mu} < 100\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for the ATLAS comparison.
- Magnetic field contribution to pair \(P_T\)-broadening: \(\langle \Delta p_\perp^2\rangle_{\mu^+\mu^-}^{B} = \langle \mathbf{P}_m^2(b_\perp)\rangle [\tanh(y_+)-\tanh(y_-)]^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on dijet azimuthal correlations (Mueller 2016, Chen 2016, Chen 2018) to dilepton production, and provides a QED counterpart to QCD jet quenching analyses (Gyulassy 1993, Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996). The calculation reproduces the ATLAS ultra-peripheral collision data (ATLAS 2016) and explains the additional broadening observed in central collisions (ATLAS 2018, STAR 2018). The proposed rapidity-dependent measurement of acoplanarity would enable a direct probe of the magnetic field in the quark-gluon plasma, complementing studies of the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2009, Skokov 2016).
