# (Inverse) Magnetic Catalysis in Bose-Einstein Condensation of Neutral Bound Pairs

> The Bose-Einstein condensation of bound pairs made of oppositely charged fermions in a magnetic field is investigated. We find that the condensation temperature shows the magnetic catalysis effect in weak coupling and the inverse magnetic catalysis effect in strong coupling. The different responses to the magnetic field can be attributed to the competition between the dimensional reduction by Landau orbitals in pairing dynamics and the anisotropy of the kinetic spectrum of fluctuations (bound pairs in the normal phase)

## Key claim

The condensation temperature of neutral bound pairs (composite bosons) formed from oppositely charged fermions in a magnetic field exhibits magnetic catalysis (increase with field) in the weak-coupling regime and inverse magnetic catalysis (decrease with field) in the strong-coupling regime, depending on the competition between dimensional reduction from Landau orbitals and the anisotropy of the bosonic fluctuation spectrum.

## Method

The authors use a functional integral formulation for a nonrelativistic fermion system with a short-range attractive interaction in a uniform magnetic field, employing the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a pairing field. They compute the effective action to Gaussian order around the normal phase, analytically summing over all Landau levels in the BEC limit where the chemical potential is set by the bound-state condition. The critical temperature is then obtained from the number equation using the resulting anisotropic boson dispersion, with the anisotropy factor \(\kappa\) expressed in terms of Hurwitz zeta functions.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature is given by \(T_c = \kappa^{2/3} T_c^0\), where \(T_c^0 = [n/(2\zeta(3/2))]^{2/3} \pi/m\) is the ideal Bose gas condensation temperature at zero field.
* In the strong-coupling domain (positive scattering length \(a_s > 0\)), the anisotropy factor \(\kappa \simeq 1 - \frac{1}{4}(eB)^2 a_s^4\) for weak fields, giving a slight suppression of \(T_c\).
* In the weak-coupling domain (negative scattering length \(a_s < 0\)), the binding energy is \(|\bar\chi| \simeq \frac{1}{2} m \omega_B^2 a_s^2\) and the anisotropy factor \(\kappa \simeq 4 eB a_s^2 \ll 1\), strongly suppressing \(T_c\).
* The demarcation between the two regimes occurs at \(r_c \simeq 0.303\) for the ratio \(|\bar\chi|/\omega_B\), with \(\kappa \simeq 0.792\) in the large-field limit.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis from relativistic chiral symmetry breaking in QCD (Klimenko, Miransky) to a nonrelativistic BEC system of neutral bound pairs, providing a controlled platform to examine the competition between enhanced pairing and enhanced phase fluctuations. The authors follow the functional integral approach of Sademelo (1993) for the BCS/BEC crossover and analytically compute Gaussian fluctuations beyond mean-field theory, which is necessary because mean-field alone cannot capture the BEC transition. The results enable quantitative predictions for how an external magnetic field can either induce or suppress Bose-Einstein condensation depending on the coupling strength, and suggest that the inverse magnetic catalysis seen in lattice QCD (lattice calculations, 2014) may have a nonrelativistic analogue driven by fluctuation effects.
