# Three-Body Nuclear Forces from a Matrix Model

> We compute three-body nuclear forces at short distances by using the nuclear matrix model of holographic QCD proposed in our previous paper with P. Yi. We find that the three-body forces at short distances are repulsive for (a) aligned three neutrons with averaged spins, and (b) aligned proton-proton-neutron / proton-neutron-neutron. These indicate that in dense states of neutrons such as cores of neutron stars, or in Helium-3 / tritium nucleus, the repulsive forces are larger than the ones estimated from two-body forces only.

## Key claim

The three-body nuclear forces at short distances are repulsive for aligned three neutrons with averaged spins and for aligned proton-proton-neutron/proton-neutron-neutron configurations, but this result is obtained specifically within a large N_c holographic QCD matrix model and applies only in the short-range regime where the inter-nucleon distance r satisfies 1/sqrt(lambda) M_KK << r << 1/M_KK.

## Method

The authors used a U(k) matrix model derived from holographic QCD via gauge/string duality applied to a D4-D8 system, which describes k-body baryon systems. For k=3, they solved the ADHM constraint to obtain the configuration space, substituted the solution into the matrix model Lagrangian, integrated out the auxiliary gauge field A_0, and evaluated the Hamiltonian using tensor products of single-baryon states with quantum spin/isospin wave functions. The three-body potential was extracted by subtracting the one-body and two-body contributions for baryons aligned on a straight line with equal spacings.

## Key numbers

- The three-body potential scales as N_c/(lambda^2 r^4), where r is the inter-nucleon distance, N_c is the number of colors, and lambda is the 't Hooft coupling
- The two-body repulsive core scales as N_c/(lambda r^2), making the three-body potential suppressed relative to two-body by a factor of approximately 1/(lambda (r M_KK)^2)
- The region of validity is 1/sqrt(lambda) M_KK << r << 1/M_KK, where M_KK is approximately 1 GeV
- The classical value of the instanton size rho is 2^{-1/4} 3^{7/4} sqrt(pi) lambda^{-1/2} M_KK^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous matrix model approach with P. Yi (Hashimoto, Iizuka, and Yi, 2010) which derived the model and computed two-body nuclear forces, finding a universal repulsive core for any baryon states with two flavors. The three-body result is consistent with the soliton approach of Hashimoto (2009) which found vanishing three-body forces for classical spin/isospin configurations using 't Hooft instantons, but the present work goes beyond that by including quantum spin/isospin degrees of freedom. The results indicate that in dense neutron states such as neutron star cores, or in Helium-3 and tritium nuclei, repulsive forces are larger than estimates from two-body forces alone.
