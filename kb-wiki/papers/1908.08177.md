# New magicity $N=32$ and $34$ triggered by strong couplings between Dirac inversion partners

> Inspired by recent experiments, the successive new magicity $N = 32$ and $34$ in Ca isotopes are studied within the relativistic density functional theory. It is illustrated that the strong couplings between the $s_{1/2}$ and neutron ($ν$) $\nu2p_{1/2}$ orbits, here referred as "Dirac inversion partners" (DIPs), play a key role in opening both subshells $N = 32$ and $34$. Such strong couplings originate from the inversion similarity between the DIPs, that the upper component of the Dirac spinor of one partner shares the same orbital angular momentum as the lower component of the other, and vice versa. Following the revealed mechanism, it is predicted that the magicity $N = 32$ is reserved until $^{48}$S, but vanishes in $^{46}$Si.

## Key claim

The strong repulsive couplings between the neutron \(s_{1/2}\) and \(\nu 2p_{1/2}\) orbits, termed "Dirac inversion partners" (DIPs), are the essential mechanism that triggers both the \(N=32\) and \(N=34\) subshell closures in Ca isotopes, with the effect being particularly enhanced in the relativistic Hartree-Fock model PKA1 compared to other relativistic Lagrangians.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree and Hartree-Fock calculations using the PKA1, PKO3, and DD-ME2 Lagrangians for Ca isotopes and neighboring nuclei, with pairing correlations treated in the BCS scheme using the finite-range Gogny force D1S. They analyzed the evolution of two-neutron separation energies, single-particle levels, and nuclear density profiles from \(^{52}\)Ca to \(^{54}\)Ca, and decomposed the interaction matrix elements between the \(\nu 2p_{1/2}\) orbit and other orbits into upper-upper (UU) and upper-lower (UL) components of the Dirac spinors to isolate the DIP coupling contributions.

## Key numbers

- The \(2_1^+\) excitation energy in \(^{54}\)Ca is 2043 keV, revealing the magicity at \(N=34\).
- The two-neutron separation energies \(S_{2n}\) calculated by PKA1 show a significant descending from \(N=32\) to \(N=36\), reproducing the experimental evidence for successive magicity at \(N=32\) and \(N=34\).
- The \(\nu 2p\) spin-orbit splitting \(\Delta E_{\nu 2p}\) remains stable from \(^{52}\)Ca to \(^{48}\)S and suddenly decreases in \(^{46}\)Si, as calculated by PKA1.
- The measured \(2_1^+\) energy in \(^{52}\)Ar\(_{34}\) is 1656(18) keV, similar to that of \(^{46}\)Ar\(_{28}\) at 1554(1) keV, indicating persistence of magicity \(N=34\) on the proton-deficient side.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of magicity at \(N=32\) and \(N=34\) in Ca isotopes (Steppenbeck 2013, Michimasa 2018) and addresses the theoretical discrepancy between shell-model calculations using GXPF1A and KB3G interactions, as well as ab-initio calculations (Hagen 2012, Hergert 2014). The authors provide a unified relativistic interpretation using the PKA1 Lagrangian (Long 2007), which reproduces both magicity simultaneously, unlike the PKO3 and DD-ME2 Lagrangians. The mechanism of DIP couplings enables predictions for the persistence of \(N=32\) magicity down to \(^{48}\)S, consistent with the experimentally observed magicity in \(^{50}\)Ar (Steppenbeck 2015).
