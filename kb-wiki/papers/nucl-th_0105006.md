# The pairing Hamiltonian for one pair of identical nucleons bound in a potential well

> The problem of one pair of identical nucleons sitting in ${\cal N}$ single particle levels of a potential well and interacting through the pairing force is treated introducing even Grassmann variables. The eigenvectors are analytically expressed solely in terms of these with coefficients fixed by the eigenvalues and the single particle energies. When the latter are those of an harmonic oscillator well an accurate expression is derived for both the collective eigenvalue and for those trapped in between the single particle levels, for any strength of the pairing interaction and for any number of levels. Notably the trapped solutions are labelled through an index upon which they depend parabolically.

# The pairing Hamiltonian for one pair of identical nucleons bound in a potential well

## Key claim

The authors derive semi-analytical expressions for the eigenvalues of the pairing Hamiltonian for one pair of identical nucleons distributed across multiple single-particle levels in a harmonic oscillator well, claiming that the trapped (non-collective) solutions follow a parabolic dependence on an index and that the collective eigenvalue can be accurately represented by a cubic function of the coupling strength, but only for coupling strengths in the range relevant to empirical nuclear data (approximately G̃ = 0.05 to 0.1).

## Method

The authors introduce even Grassmann variables to represent fermion pairs and diagonalize the pairing Hamiltonian in a 2N-dimensional basis of states with seniority v=0 and v=2. For the harmonic oscillator well, they recast the secular equation into polynomial form and use Vieta's formulas to relate the trapped eigenvalues to the collective eigenvalue, then solve the resulting nonlinear system to obtain parabolic coefficients for the trapped solutions. The collective eigenvalue is obtained either from a cubic interpolation formula for small coupling or from an asymptotic expansion for large coupling, with iterative refinement available.

## Key numbers

* For N=5 levels at G̃=0.05, the trapped eigenvalues are approximately 4.2812 (N̄=0), 6.1056 (N̄=1), 8.1021 (N̄=2), and 10.271 (N̄=3), agreeing with exact numerical solutions to better than 0.27%.
* For N=5 levels at G̃=0.1, the trapped eigenvalues are approximately 3.4266 (N̄=0), 5.6237 (N̄=1), 7.8485 (N̄=2), and 10.101 (N̄=3).
* For N=5 levels at G̃=1, the trapped eigenvalues are approximately 3.1601 (N̄=0), 5.3621 (N̄=1), 7.6190 (N̄=2), and 9.9297 (N̄=3).
* For N=5 levels at G̃=5, the trapped eigenvalues are approximately 3.1510 (N̄=0), 5.3472 (N̄=1), 7.6015 (N̄=2), and 9.9140 (N̄=3).
* The collective energy at G̃=0 for any N is Ẽ_c = 3, and its derivative at G̃=0 is -2.
* The iterative refinement of the collective energy converges to better than 0.00001 after 4 iterations for most cases shown.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Grassmann-algebra treatment of the pairing Hamiltonian for n pairs in a single level (Barbaro et al., 2000) to the multi-level case, and connects to the Richardson-Gaudin approach (Richardson and Sherman, 1964) for the exactly solvable pairing model. The semi-analytical results for the harmonic oscillator well provide a practical tool for treating systems with many pairs in a deformed mean field, and the authors suggest extension to neutron-proton pairs in isospin singlet states, where the partners must occupy different shells at least 2ħω apart (Garrido et al., 2001).
