# Top-quark and Higgs boson perspectives at heavy-ion colliders

> The perspectives for measuring the top quark and the Higgs boson in nuclear collisions at the LHC and Future Circular Collider (FCC) are summarized. Perturbative QCD calculations at (N)NLO accuracy, including nuclear parton distribution functions, are used to determine their cross sections and visible yields after standard analysis cuts in PbPb and pPb collisions at the LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.5, 8.8 TeV) and FCC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39, 63 TeV). In their "cleanest" decay channels, $\rm t\bar{t}\to b\bar{b} 2 \ell 2ν$ and $\rm H\to γγ, 4\ell$, about 10$^3$ ($10^5$) top-quark and 10 (10$^3$) Higgs-boson events are expected at the LHC (FCC) for their total nominal integrated luminosities. Whereas the $\rm t \bar{t}$ observation is clearcut at both colliders, evidence for Higgs production, perfectly possible at the FCC, requires integrating $\times$30 more luminosities at the LHC.

## Key claim

Top-quark pairs should be clearly observable in heavy-ion collisions at both the LHC and FCC, but Higgs boson evidence at the LHC requires integrating about 30 times more luminosity than the nominal value, whereas the FCC can achieve a clear Higgs signal with its nominal integrated luminosities.

## Method

The author used perturbative QCD calculations at next-to-leading-order (NLO) for top-quark pair and single-top production, and at next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) for Higgs boson production, employing the MCFM program with CT10 proton PDFs and EPS09 nuclear PDFs for lead ions. Cross sections were computed for PbPb and pPb collisions at LHC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 and 8.8 TeV) and FCC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 39 and 63 TeV), then multiplied by nominal integrated luminosities and corrected for branching ratios, acceptance, and efficiency losses to obtain expected event yields.

## Key numbers

* Top-quark pair production cross section in PbPb at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV): 3.4 microbarns, yielding about 450 events in the dilepton channel after cuts
* Top-quark pair production cross section in PbPb at FCC (sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV): 300 microbarns, yielding about 150,000 events in the dilepton channel after cuts
* Higgs boson production cross section in PbPb at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV): 500 nanobarns, yielding about 6 diphoton events after cuts
* Higgs boson production cross section in PbPb at FCC (sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV): 11.5 microbarns, yielding about 450 diphoton events after cuts
* Cross sections increase by a factor of 55-90 for top-quark pairs and by a factor of 20 for the Higgs boson between LHC and FCC energies
* Antishadowing nPDF modifications increase total top-quark yields by 2-8% compared to pp collisions; Higgs cross sections are enhanced or depleted by about 3% at LHC or FCC respectively
* The significance of the Higgs diphoton signal at the LHC with nominal luminosities is 0.5-0.6 sigma; with 30 pb^-1 integrated luminosity in pPb it reaches 3 sigma; at the FCC with nominal luminosities it reaches 5.5 sigma

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by d'Enterria (2015) for top-quark production in nuclear collisions and by DdE for Higgs boson production, providing a unified perspective on measuring both particles at current and future heavy-ion colliders. The top-quark measurements would constrain nuclear gluon PDFs in the antishadowing and EMC regions using the Hessian reweighting technique of Paukkunen (2014), while Higgs boson measurements could probe final-state QGP density through suppression effects. The results enable planning for heavy-ion running at the HL-LHC and FCC, showing that top-quark studies are feasible now while Higgs studies require either the HL-LHC with enhanced pPb luminosity or the FCC.
