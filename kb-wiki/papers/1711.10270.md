# Spin-isospin excitation of ${}^3$He with three-proton final state

> Spin-isospin excitation of ${}^3$He nucleus by proton-induced charge-exchange reaction, ${}^3\mathrm{He}(p,n)ppp$, at forward neutron scattering angle is studied in a plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA). In PWIA, cross sections of the reaction is written in terms of proton-neutron scattering amplitudes and response functions of the transition from ${}^{3}$He to three-proton state by spin-isospin transition operators. The response functions are calculated with realistic nucleon-nucleon potential models using a Faddeev three-body method. Calculated cross sections agree with available experimental data in substance. Possible effects arising from the uncertainty of proton-neutron amplitudes and three-nucleon interactions in three-proton system are examined.

## Key claim

The plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations of the ${}^3\mathrm{He}(p,n)ppp$ reaction at forward neutron scattering angle show "reasonable agreement" with available experimental data, but the calculated energy-transfer dependence of the longitudinal polarization transfer coefficient $D_{LL}(0^\circ)$ is "much smoother" than the data, and the discrepancy cannot be resolved by introducing attractive $T=\frac{3}{2}$ three-nucleon potentials, even those strong enough to produce a $3p$ resonance.

## Method

The cross section and polarization transfer coefficients for the ${}^3\mathrm{He}(p,n)ppp$ reaction at $\theta_n=0^\circ$ are calculated within a plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA), where the observables are expressed in terms of $n(p,n)p$ scattering amplitudes (taken from the SP07 solution of the SAID database) and response functions for the transition from the ${}^3$He bound state to three-proton continuum states. The response functions are computed by solving the Faddeev three-body equations in coordinate space for the three-proton system, using realistic nucleon-nucleon potential models (AV18, AV8', AV14, dTRS and their charge-dependent versions) and including the Brazil model of the two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential tuned to reproduce the ${}^3$He binding energy.

## Key numbers

- At $T_p=346$ MeV, the $pn$ observables from the SP07 solution used are: $\sigma^{pn}(0^\circ)=11.32$ mb/sr, $D_{LL}^{pn}(0^\circ)=-0.3942$, $D_{NN}^{pn}(0^\circ)=-0.2396$.
- At $T_p=200$ MeV, the $pn$ observables used are: $\sigma^{pn}(0^\circ)=12.47$ mb/sr, $D_{LL}^{pn}(0^\circ)=-0.1831$, $D_{NN}^{pn}(0^\circ)=-0.3269$.
- The experimental data of Ref. (Wakasa 2008) suggest a $3p$ resonance in the $\frac{1}{2}^{-}$ state centered at $\omega_{r}=16 \pm 1$ MeV with width $\Gamma = 11 \pm 3$ MeV.
- From fitting the calculated response functions to experimental data at $T_p=346$ MeV, the extracted $pn$ amplitudes are: $\vert {\cal V}_c \vert^2 = 1.43 \pm 0.57$ mb/sr, $\vert {\cal V}_L \vert^2 = 2.90 \pm 0.38$ mb/sr, $\vert {\cal V}_T \vert^2 = 3.60 \pm 0.75$ mb/sr, yielding $\sigma^{pn}(0^\circ)=11.5 \pm 1.7$ mb/sr.
- The $T=\frac{3}{2}$ three-nucleon potential strength parameter $W_1(\frac{3}{2})$ required to bind the $4n$ system is $-36.14$ MeV, compared to $W_1(\frac{1}{2})=-2.55$ MeV for the $T=\frac{1}{2}$ state with AV18.
- A $3p$ resonance appears in the calculation only when the $T=\frac{3}{2}$ three-nucleon potential for the $J_0^\pi=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$ state is made extremely attractive ($W_1(\frac{3}{2})=-90$ MeV), producing a resonance at $\omega_{Lab}=9$ MeV with a width of about 2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous Faddeev calculations for proton-deuteron systems (Ishikawa 2009) and three-alpha systems (Ishikawa 2013) to the three-proton continuum in a charge-exchange reaction. It directly addresses the experimental results of (Pan 1998) at $T_p=200$ MeV and (Wakasa 2008) at $T_p=346$ MeV, who interpreted the deviation of $D_{LL}(0^\circ)$ from free $pn$ values as evidence for a $3p$ resonance. The study also examines the implications of the tetraneutron resonance claim by (Kisamori 2016) and the analysis of (Hiyama 2016) showing that such a state requires an extremely attractive $T=\frac{3}{2}$ three-nucleon potential. The results enable future experimental studies to clarify whether the observed $D_{LL}(0^\circ)$ behavior is due to nuclear interactions or other reaction mechanism effects, and stress the need for better knowledge of forward-angle $pn$ observables.
