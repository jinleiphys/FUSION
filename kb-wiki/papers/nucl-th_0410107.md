# Asymmetries in the Non-Mesonic Weak Decay of Polarized Lambda-Hypernuclei

> The non-mesonic weak decay of polarized Lambda-hypernuclei is studied for the first time by taking into account, with a Monte Carlo intranuclear cascade code, the nucleon final state interactions. A one-meson-exchange model is employed to describe the Lambda N-> n N processes in a finite nucleus framework. The relationship between the intrinsic Lambda asymmetry parameter a_Λand the asymmetry a^M_Λaccessible in experiments is discussed. A strong dependence of a^M_Λon nucleon final state interactions and detection threshold is obtained. Our results for a^M_Λare consistent with ^{11}_ΛB and ^{12}_ΛC data but disagree with observations in ^5_ΛHe.

## Key claim

The first theoretical estimate of the observable asymmetry parameter a^M_Λ (which accounts for nucleon final state interactions and detection thresholds) is obtained for ^5_ΛHe, ^11_ΛB, and ^12_ΛC; the results are consistent with data for the p-shell hypernuclei but disagree with the positive asymmetry observed for ^5_ΛHe, and the authors argue that only positive intrinsic Λ asymmetry values (not predicted by any existing meson-exchange model) could resolve this discrepancy.

## Method

The ΛN → nN weak transition is described with a one-meson-exchange potential (π, ρ, K, K*, ω, η mesons) from Parreno and Ramos (2002), and strong final state interactions between the weak decay nucleons are included via a scattering nN wave function from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using the Nijmegen Soft-Core NSC97 potentials. A Monte Carlo intranuclear cascade code simulates the propagation of primary and secondary nucleons inside the residual nucleus, and the observable asymmetry a^M_Λ is extracted from the simulated proton angular intensities fitted to a linear cosθ dependence.

## Key numbers

* For ^5_ΛHe with the full one-meson-exchange model and a 30 MeV proton detection threshold, a^M_Λ = -0.46; the experimental value from KEK-E462 (preliminary) is 0.07 ± 0.08.
* For ^12_ΛC with the full one-meson-exchange model and a 30 MeV threshold, a^M_Λ = -0.37; the experimental value from KEK-E508 (preliminary) is -0.44 ± 0.32.
* For ^11_ΛB with the full one-meson-exchange model and a 30 MeV threshold, a^M_Λ = -0.39; the experimental value from KEK-E508 (preliminary) is 0.11 ± 0.44.
* The intrinsic asymmetry a_Λ before FSI for the full OME model is -0.68 for ^5_ΛHe, -0.81 for ^11_ΛB, and -0.73 for ^12_ΛC.
* With zero detection threshold, a_Λ / a^M_Λ ≈ 2 for ^5_ΛHe and ≈ 4 for ^11_ΛB and ^12_ΛC; for a 70 MeV threshold, a_Λ / a^M_Λ ≈ 1 for all three hypernuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of hypernuclear non-mesonic weak decay (Sasaki et al., 2000; Parreno and Ramos, 2002; Itonaga et al., 2002) by including, for the first time, nucleon final state interactions in the calculation of the asymmetry from polarized Λ-hypernuclei. The results address the puzzle raised by KEK experiments (Ajimura et al., 2000; Maruta et al., 2004) where ^5_ΛHe shows a positive asymmetry while theoretical models predict negative values; the authors conclude that closer collaboration between theorists and experimentalists, similar to the recent resolution of the Γ_n/Γ_p ratio puzzle, is needed to resolve this asymmetry puzzle.
