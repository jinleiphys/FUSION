# Towards a New Strategy of Searching for QCD Phase Transition in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We reconsider the Hung and Shuryak arguments in favour of searching for the deconfinement phase transition in heavy ion collisions {\em downward} from the nominal SPS energy, at $E_{lab} \approx 30 \ GeV/A$ where the fireball lifetime is the longest one. Using the recent lattice QCD data and the mixed phase model, we show that the deconfinement transition might occur at the bombarding energies as low as $E_{lab}=3 - 5 \ GeV/A$. Attention is drawn to the study of the mixed phase of nuclear matter in the collision energy range $E_{lab}= 2-10 \ GeV/A$.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the softest point of the equation of state (EOS), where the fireball lifetime is longest, occurs at bombarding energies as low as 3-5 GeV/A, not at the 30 GeV/A previously advocated by Hung and Shuryak (1994). This conclusion is conditional on using recent lattice QCD data and a mixed phase model that accounts for baryon-rich matter, which suppresses the softest-point effect at higher baryon densities.

## Method
The authors used the statistical mixed phase model of Shanenko, Yukalova, and Yukalov (SYY), which treats the coexistence of hadron clusters and unbound quarks and gluons without spatial separation, and fitted its three parameters (A, γ, and the cluster-cluster interaction constant) to lattice QCD data for pure gluonic SU(2) and SU(3) matter. They then computed the pressure-to-energy-density ratio (p/ε) as a function of energy density for baryon-free matter and for matter at finite baryon density, comparing the resulting softest-point location to the earlier work of Hung and Shuryak (1994).

## Key numbers
- The softest point of the EOS from lattice QCD data (Kogut-Susskind and Wilson schemes) is at ε_sp ≈ 0.5–0.6 GeV/fm³, corresponding to Au beam energies of about 3–5 GeV/A.
- The mixed phase model predicts the softest point at ε_sp ≈ 0.3 GeV/fm³.
- Hung and Shuryak (1994) obtained ε_sp ≈ 1.5 GeV/fm³ at a beam energy of E_lab ≈ 30 GeV/A.
- The deconfinement temperature from lattice QCD is T_dec = 150–157 MeV; the mixed phase model gives T_dec = 150 MeV.
- The mixed phase model parameters used are γ = 0.62, A^(1/(3γ+1)) = 225 MeV, and the cluster-cluster interaction constant Φ̄₀ = 4.1 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻².
- The minimum in p/ε disappears at baryon density n_B ≈ n₀ (normal nuclear matter density).

## Context
This work extends the strategy of Hung and Shuryak (1994) for searching for the deconfinement phase transition by tuning beam energy to the softest point of the EOS, but corrects the predicted energy downward from 30 GeV/A to 3–5 GeV/A using lattice QCD data and the SYY mixed phase model. The analysis contradicts the earlier assumption of baryon-free matter and shows that the softest-point effect is suppressed at baryon densities near normal nuclear density. The results are consistent with hydrodynamic calculations by M. O. (1995) that found a shock-front enhancement at E_lab ≈ 6 GeV/A. The paper enables future experimental searches for the mixed phase of quarks and hadrons in the energy range E_lab = 2–10 GeV/A, where signals such as Λ-hyperon enhancement have already been observed at 3.5 GeV/A.
