# Relation between shrinkage effect and compression of rotational spectrum in $^7_Î›$Li hypernucleus

> It has been shown experimentally that the $^6$Li nucleus shrinks by adding a $Î›$ particle while its spectrum is also compressed. We discuss the compatibility of these two effects, contrasting also with a relation between the shrinkage effect and the stability of the spectrum in the $^9_Î›$Be hypernucleus. To this end, we employ two-body $d$-$^5_Î›$He and $Î±$-$^5_Î›$He cluster models for the $^7_Î›$Li and $^9_Î›$Be hypernuclei, respectively. We first argue that a Gaussian-like interaction between two clusters leads to a stabilization of the spectrum against an addition of a $Î›$ particle, even though the intercluster distance is reduced. In the case of $^7_Î›$Li, the spin-orbit interaction between the intercluster motion and the deuteron spin has to be considered also. We show that the shrinkage effect makes the expectation value of the spin-orbit potential larger, lowering the excitation energy for the $^6$Li($3^+)\otimes Î›(1/2^+)$ level.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shrinkage effect (reduction of intercluster distance) caused by adding a Lambda particle is compatible with the compression of the rotational spectrum in 7Î›Li, but only because the spin-orbit interaction between the intercluster motion and the deuteron spin becomes effectively larger due to the shrinkage, lowering the excitation energy; for 9Î›Be, the spectrum remains stable despite shrinkage because the Gaussian-like intercluster potential makes the level spacing approximately independent of the potential depth.

## Method

The authors employed two-body cluster models: an Î±-5Î›He cluster model for 9Î›Be and a d-5Î›He cluster model for 7Î›Li. For 9Î›Be, they constructed the intercluster potential from an Î±-Î± nuclear plus Coulomb potential combined with a double-folding potential for the Î±-Î› interaction, solved the Schrodinger equation for each angular momentum L, and used a bound-state approximation for resonance states. For 7Î›Li, they used a semi-microscopic cluster model where the central and spin-orbit intercluster potentials were derived from a core-plus-proton-plus-neutron three-body model, with the Lambda contribution estimated via a single folding potential.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the shrinkage effect in hypernuclei, first proposed by Motoba, Bando, and Ikeda (1983), and addresses a puzzle raised by high-precision gamma-ray spectroscopy measurements by Tamura (2001) and Ukai (2006). The paper contrasts the behavior of 7Î›Li and 9Î›Be with that of 13Î›C, where the spectrum is pushed up rather than compressed, as shown by Kohri (2002). The results enable future studies of sd-shell hypernuclei such as 19Î›F, for which gamma-ray spectroscopy has been planned at J-PARC.
