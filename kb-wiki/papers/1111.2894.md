# Relation between shrinkage effect and compression of rotational spectrum in $^7_Λ$Li hypernucleus

> It has been shown experimentally that the $^6$Li nucleus shrinks by adding a $Λ$ particle while its spectrum is also compressed. We discuss the compatibility of these two effects, contrasting also with a relation between the shrinkage effect and the stability of the spectrum in the $^9_Λ$Be hypernucleus. To this end, we employ two-body $d$-$^5_Λ$He and $α$-$^5_Λ$He cluster models for the $^7_Λ$Li and $^9_Λ$Be hypernuclei, respectively. We first argue that a Gaussian-like interaction between two clusters leads to a stabilization of the spectrum against an addition of a $Λ$ particle, even though the intercluster distance is reduced. In the case of $^7_Λ$Li, the spin-orbit interaction between the intercluster motion and the deuteron spin has to be considered also. We show that the shrinkage effect makes the expectation value of the spin-orbit potential larger, lowering the excitation energy for the $^6$Li($3^+)\otimes Λ(1/2^+)$ level.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shrinkage effect (reduction of intercluster distance) caused by adding a Lambda particle is compatible with the compression of the rotational spectrum in 7ΛLi, but only because the spin-orbit interaction between the intercluster motion and the deuteron spin becomes effectively larger due to the shrinkage, lowering the excitation energy; for 9ΛBe, the spectrum remains stable despite shrinkage because the Gaussian-like intercluster potential makes the level spacing approximately independent of the potential depth.

## Method

The authors employed two-body cluster models: an α-5ΛHe cluster model for 9ΛBe and a d-5ΛHe cluster model for 7ΛLi. For 9ΛBe, they constructed the intercluster potential from an α-α nuclear plus Coulomb potential combined with a double-folding potential for the α-Λ interaction, solved the Schrodinger equation for each angular momentum L, and used a bound-state approximation for resonance states. For 7ΛLi, they used a semi-microscopic cluster model where the central and spin-orbit intercluster potentials were derived from a core-plus-proton-plus-neutron three-body model, with the Lambda contribution estimated via a single folding potential.

## Key numbers

* The root-mean-square intercluster distance for the L=0 state of 8Be is 4.90 fm, while for 9ΛBe it is 3.24 fm, a reduction of about 34%.
* The excitation energy of the 2+ state in 8Be is 3.04 MeV, while the energy shift due to adding a Lambda particle in 9ΛBe is only 0.05 MeV.
* The spin-orbit energy shift for the L=2, I=3 state (first-order perturbation) is -1.22 MeV for 6Li and -1.84 MeV for 7ΛLi.
* The calculated B(E2) value from the 5/2+ member of the 6Li(3+)⊗Λ(1/2+) state to the ground state doublet is 3.03 e2fm4, compared to the experimental value of 3.6±0.5+0.5-0.4 e2fm4.
* The spin-averaged energy of the second doublet (5/2+,7/2+) in 7ΛLi is 1.858 MeV, smaller than the 2.186 MeV excitation energy of the first excited state of 6Li.
* The factor βℏ2/8μ from the Bohr-Sommerfeld formula is 0.574 MeV for the α+α system and 0.572 MeV for the α+5ΛHe system.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the shrinkage effect in hypernuclei, first proposed by Motoba, Bando, and Ikeda (1983), and addresses a puzzle raised by high-precision gamma-ray spectroscopy measurements by Tamura (2001) and Ukai (2006). The paper contrasts the behavior of 7ΛLi and 9ΛBe with that of 13ΛC, where the spectrum is pushed up rather than compressed, as shown by Kohri (2002). The results enable future studies of sd-shell hypernuclei such as 19ΛF, for which gamma-ray spectroscopy has been planned at J-PARC.
