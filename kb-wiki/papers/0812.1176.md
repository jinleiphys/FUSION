# Non-flow correlations in a cluster model

> We derive analytical forms for nonflow contributions from cluster correlation to the measurement of two-particle elliptic flow (v2{2}). We estimate nonflow contribution from rho->pi+pi decays and find it is negative but not a major contributor to the nonflow effect in v2{2}. We also estimate nonflow contribution from the recent STAR measurement of two-particle angular correlations.

## Key claim
The paper derives analytical forms for nonflow contributions to two-particle elliptic flow (v2{2}) from cluster correlations, showing that nonflow from rho meson decays is negative but small (a few percent) compared to the difference between v2{2} and v2{4} measurements at RHIC, indicating that resonance decays are not a major nonflow source.

## Method
The authors develop a cluster model decomposing particle pairs into four sources: hydro-medium pairs, same-cluster pairs, hydro-cluster cross pairs, and inter-cluster pairs. They derive analytical expressions for each contribution to the two-particle azimuthal correlation, then apply the formalism to estimate nonflow from rho->pi+pi decays using Monte Carlo sampling with assumed rho transverse momentum distributions, and to estimate nonflow from STAR's measured two-particle angular correlations parameterized as a two-dimensional Gaussian plus dipole term.

## Key numbers
- For rho decays with inverse slope T=0.3 GeV, the average cosine of twice the opening azimuthal angle between daughter pions (c_rho) is -0.17
- The fraction of pions from rho decays is taken as x=40% based on peripheral Au+Au measurements
- Nonflow contribution from rho decays is typically a few percent of v2{2}, e.g., for 10-20% centrality: 1 - v2_hy/v2 = -1.62% compared to 1 - v2{4}/v2{2} = 14.08%
- For 30-40% centrality: v2{2}=7.29%, v2{4}=6.33%, nonflow from rho decays is -1.63% while 1-v2{4}/v2{2}=13.17%
- For 60-70% centrality: v2{2}=7.25%, v2{4}=5.68%, nonflow from rho decays is -6.95% while 1-v2{4}/v2{2}=21.66%

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of nonflow effects in elliptic flow measurements at RHIC, building on earlier studies of flow methods (v2method, trainor) and four-particle cumulants (v4). The analytical framework enables separation of nonflow contributions from resonance decays versus other sources like minijet correlations and flow fluctuations from initial eccentricity fluctuations (Voloshin_fluc, PHOBOS). The results show that the large difference between v2{2} and v2{4} measurements cannot be explained by resonance decays, pointing to other nonflow mechanisms or flow fluctuations as the dominant source.
