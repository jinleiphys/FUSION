# Transport coefficients of hot magnetized QCD matter beyond the lowest Landau level approximation

> In this article, shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and thermal conductivity of a hot QCD medium have been studied in the presence of a strong magnetic field. To model the hot magnetized QCD matter, an extended quasi-particle description of the hot QCD equation of state in the presence of the magnetic field has been adopted. The effects of higher Landau levels on the temperature dependence of viscous coefficients (bulk and shear viscosities) and thermal conductivity have been obtained by considering the $1\rightarrow 2$ processes in the presence of the strong magnetic field. An effective covariant kinetic theory has been set up in (1+1)-dimensional that includes mean field contributions in terms of quasi-particle dispersions and magnetic field to describe the Landau level dynamics of quarks. The sensitivity of these parameters to the magnitude of the magnetic field has also been explored. Both the magnetic field and mean field contributions have seen to play a significant role in obtaining the temperature behaviour of the transport coefficients of hot QCD medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including higher Landau levels (beyond the lowest Landau level approximation) significantly modifies the temperature dependence of shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and thermal conductivity of hot QCD matter in a strong magnetic field, but this claim is conditional on the regime \(gT \ll \sqrt{|q_f eB|}\) where higher Landau level contributions are significant, and on the dominance of \(1 \rightarrow 2\) processes (gluon to quark-antiquark pair production/annihilation) over \(2 \rightarrow 2\) processes.

## Method

The authors employed an effective covariant kinetic theory in (1+1)-dimensions for quarks and antiquarks, incorporating the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) to encode hot QCD medium effects from lattice QCD and 3-loop HTLpt equations of state. The Chapman-Enskog method was used to solve the Boltzmann equation with a collision integral linearized via the relaxation time approximation, where the thermal relaxation time was computed for \(1 \rightarrow 2\) processes including full Landau level resummation up to 50 Landau levels. Mean field contributions from the quasiparticle dispersion relations were included through a force term arising from local conservation laws.

## Key numbers

* Thermal relaxation time \(\tau_{\text{eff}}\) for quarks shows a decreasing trend with increasing temperature at \(|eB| = 10 m_\pi^2\), considering up to 50 Landau levels.
* Bulk viscosity to entropy ratio \(\zeta/s\) and shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s\) were computed at \(|eB| = 10 m_\pi^2\) for temperatures above \(T_c\), with results compared to the zero magnetic field results of Mitra et al. (2018) and lattice data of Nakamura et al. (2004).
* Quark masses used: \(m_u = 3\) MeV, \(m_d = 5\) MeV, \(m_s = 100\) MeV.
* The magnetic field strength considered is \(|eB| = 10 m_\pi^2\) (approximately 0.2 GeV\(^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of transport coefficients in magnetized QCD matter beyond the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation used in earlier perturbative calculations by Hattori et al. (2017) and Kurian et al. (2018). It builds on the formalism of Fukushima and Hidaka (2017) for longitudinal conductivity with full Landau level resummation, and on the EQPM framework developed by Chandra et al. (2011) and Mitra et al. (2018) for hot QCD equations of state. The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where strong magnetic fields are present, and provide a baseline for future studies of transverse transport coefficients in moderate magnetic fields.
