# Dilepton low $p_T$ suppression as an evidence of the Color Glass Condensate

> The dilepton production is investigated in proton-nucleus collisions in the forward region using the Color Glass Condensate approach. The transverse momentum distribution ($p_T$), more precisely the low $p_T$ region, where the saturation effects are expected to increase, is analyzed. The ratio between proton-nucleus and proton-proton differential cross section for RHIC and LHC energies is evaluated, showing the effects of saturation at small $p_T$, and presenting a Cronin type peak at moderate $p_T$. These features indicate the dilepton as a most suitable probe to study the properties of the saturated regime and the Cronin effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the ratio of dilepton production cross sections in proton-nucleus versus proton-proton collisions, evaluated using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, shows saturation effects at low transverse momentum and a Cronin-type peak at moderate transverse momentum, but the specific shape of this ratio depends critically on whether a local or non-local Gaussian weight function is used for the color field correlator, with the non-local Gaussian producing suppression of the Cronin peak.

## Method

The authors compute the dilepton production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions in the forward region using the CGC approach, where the differential cross section is expressed as a convolution of a weight function (containing the proton structure function from CTEQ6L parametrization) with the color field correlator function C(l_T, x, A). The correlator function is evaluated using several models: the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with energy dependence introduced through the saturation scale, the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) Fourier transform, and a CGC fit parametrization, using both local and non-local Gaussian weight functions for the color sources. The ratio R_pA between proton-nucleus and proton-proton differential cross sections is then calculated for RHIC (sqrt(s)=350 GeV) and LHC (sqrt(s)=8.8 TeV) energies.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale for gold at x=10^{-3}: Q_s^2 = 4.114 GeV^2 (GBW fit1), 3.069 GeV^2 (CGC fit2 with m_q=10 MeV), 2.327 GeV^2 (CGC fit3 with m_q=140 MeV)
- GBW parameters: x_0 = 3 x 10^{-4}, lambda = 0.288
- CGC fit parameters (m_q=10 MeV): x_0 = 1.06 x 10^{-4}, lambda = 0.285
- CGC fit parameters (m_q=140 MeV): x_0 = 0.267 x 10^{-4}, lambda = 0.253
- Large saturation effects observed at p_T less than approximately 2 GeV for RHIC energies (sqrt(s)=350 GeV) and at p_T less than approximately 4 GeV for LHC energies (sqrt(s)=8.8 TeV)
- At large p_T, the difference between predictions using different saturation scale values is a factor of 2 when comparing the smallest value (fit3) with the GBW values

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism applied to dilepton production in proton-nucleus collisions, building on prior studies of dilepton production as a probe of saturation dynamics (Gelis and Jalilian-Marian, 2002; Rauf, 2003) and on the McLerran-Venugopalan model. The ratio R_pA analysis parallels the approach used by Blaizot, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2004) to study the Cronin effect in hadron production, where local Gaussian correlators produce a Cronin peak that disagrees with BRAHMS data at forward rapidities, while non-local Gaussian correlators suppress the peak. The results indicate that dilepton transverse momentum distributions can serve as a clean probe to distinguish between local and non-local Gaussian weight functions and to determine whether the Cronin effect is an initial or final state effect.
