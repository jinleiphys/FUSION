# Saturation and geometrical scaling -- from small $x$ deep inelastic ep scattering to high energy proton-proton and heavy ion collisions

> Gluon distributions of colliding hadrons saturate as a result of the non-linear evolution equations of QCD. As a consequence there exists the so called saturation momentum, which is related to the gluon density per unit rapidity per transverse area. When saturation momentum is the only scale for physical processes, different observables exhibit geometrical scaling (GS). We show a number of examples of GS and its violation in different reactions.

# Saturation and geometrical scaling, from small x deep inelastic ep scattering to high energy proton-proton and heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Geometrical scaling (GS) is observed in deep inelastic scattering, proton-proton collisions, and heavy ion collisions, but the scaling is approximate and limited in range; the exponent λ extracted from DIS (0.329) differs from that in pp collisions (0.22), and GS in heavy ion collisions is of worse quality than in pp.

## Method

The author reviews the dipole model of deep inelastic scattering, where the proton structure function F₂ exhibits geometrical scaling when the dipole-proton cross-section depends only on the product of dipole size and saturation momentum. For proton-proton collisions, inclusive gluon production is computed using k_T factorization with unintegrated gluon densities that depend on the ratio k_T²/Q_s²(x). For heavy ion collisions, the transverse area and saturation momentum are scaled with the number of participants N_part, and charged particle spectra from ALICE, STAR, and PHENIX are plotted against the scaling variable τ.

## Key numbers

- Exponent λ extracted from combined HERA DIS data in a model-independent way: 0.329
- Best quality GS in pp scattering at ALICE achieved for λ = 0.22
- GS in pp extends over a limited range up to √τ ∼ 4
- For heavy ion collisions, GS is shown with λ = 0.3
- Scaling relations for HI: S_⊥ ∼ N_part^(2/3) and Q_s² ∼ N_part^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the geometrical scaling concept from deep inelastic scattering, originally developed by Kwiecinski (2002) and Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998), to hadronic collisions. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate framework and the traveling wave solutions of nonlinear QCD evolution equations by Munier and Peschanski, and the JIMWLK/BK equations. The results enable further studies of GS violation at non-zero rapidity and for identified particles, and call for new LHC Run II data to test the universal scaling function F(τ).
