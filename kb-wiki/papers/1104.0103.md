# Initialization effect in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Based on the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model plus the Skyrme force parameters, initialization effect is studied in heavy-ion collision at intermediate energies. We find that there are moderate initialization effects in the observables of free neutron to proton ratio (n/p), pion-/pion + ratio, as well as neutron to proton differential flow (F^x_n-p). Effects of initialization are larger for charged pion-/pion ratios than n/p ratios. And the effects of initialization are more evident in nuclear reactions at lower incident beam energies. We do not see large effects of initialization for light reaction systems or large asymmetric (neutron-richer) reaction systems. We also see relatively large effects of initialization on the neutron to proton differential flow at relatively lower incident beam energies or with large impact parameters. These results may be useful for the delicate studies of Equation of Sate (EoS) of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper finds that initialization effects (choice of Skyrme force parameters for nuclear density distributions) produce only moderate changes in common symmetry-energy observables in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, but these effects are larger for charged pion ratios than for free neutron-to-proton ratios and are more evident at lower incident beam energies.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with Skyrme force parameters, comparing two initialization schemes: Skyrme M* (SM) and Skyrme 1 (S1) force parameters. They simulated central and semi-central collisions of ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca, ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at beam energies from 200 to 800 MeV/nucleon, and computed free neutron-to-proton ratios (n/p), charged pion ratios (π⁻/π⁺), and neutron-proton differential flow (Fˣₙ₋ₚ) as functions of beam energy and impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- For central reactions at 400 MeV/nucleon, the initialization effect on π⁻/π⁺ ratio was 1.6% for ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca, 2.9% for ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, and 3.5% for ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au.
- Free n/p ratios of preequilibrium nucleon emissions showed no significant initialization effect across all reaction systems at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- For ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at 400 MeV/nucleon with an impact parameter of 8 fm, the π⁻/π⁺ ratio increased with decreasing beam energy, and initialization effects on this ratio were larger at lower incident beam energies.
- Neutron-proton differential flow parameter showed large initialization effects at 200 MeV/nucleon, with effects diminishing at 800 MeV/nucleon for ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at impact parameter 8 fm.
- For ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at 400 MeV/nucleon, initialization effects on neutron-proton differential flow parameter increased with larger impact parameters.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of symmetry-energy probes (Li et al. 2000, Yong et al. 2006, Xiao et al. 2009) by quantifying how the choice of nuclear initialization affects observables like n/p ratios, π⁻/π⁺ ratios, and neutron-proton differential flow. The results indicate that initialization effects are generally small enough to neglect for most practical studies of the nuclear symmetry energy, but they should be considered when using subthreshold pion production at lower beam energies or when studying neutron-proton differential flow at lower energies or large impact parameters. This enables more reliable transport model simulations for constraining the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter.
