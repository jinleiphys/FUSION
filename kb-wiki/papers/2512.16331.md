# Unfolding Baryon Number Fluctuations from Correlations of Light Nuclei Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the baryon number, which is mostly carried by protons and neutrons, in relativistic heavy-ion collisions provide a sensitive probe for locating the conjectured critical point in the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) phase diagram. Since current experiments have limited access to neutron fluctuations because detectors are largely insensitive to neutrons, measurements of (net-)proton fluctuations are often used as a proxy for (net-)baryon number fluctuations. Although direct measurements of neutron fluctuations are challenging, their information are encoded in the production and correlations of light nuclei, when they are formed through coalescence of nucleons at kinetic freeze-out. Here, we propose to unfold neutron fluctuations from correlations among light nuclei produced in heavy-ion collisions. Model calculations validate this approach and show that baryon number fluctuations can be unfolded up to the third order. For fourth and higher-order cumulants, however, the uncertainties become sizable, indicating that further methodological developments and refinements are required.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that neutron number fluctuations, which are experimentally inaccessible because detectors are largely insensitive to neutrons, can be unfolded from correlations among light nuclei (deuterons, tritons, and helium-3) produced in heavy-ion collisions, thereby enabling reconstruction of baryon number fluctuations. The method is validated up to third-order cumulants, but for fourth and higher-order cumulants the uncertainties become sizable, indicating that further methodological developments are required.

## Method

The authors derive analytical expressions connecting proton and neutron number fluctuations to those of light nuclei formed via nucleon coalescence at kinetic freeze-out, then use these relations to express baryon number cumulants in terms of experimentally measurable proton and light-nuclei fluctuations and correlations. They validate the approach using the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model combined with a nucleon coalescence model to simulate central Au+Au collisions across the Beam Energy Scan energy range of 7.7–200 GeV, generating 5 million Monte Carlo events per energy. The unfolding procedure is applied to existing STAR Collaboration data for proton fluctuations and light-nuclei correlations.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron-to-proton ratio in central Au+Au collisions at BES energies ranges from approximately 0.3% to 2%, with the event-averaged proton number at mid-rapidity between 20 and 40.
- The deviation in experimental C2/C1 for protons from AMPT results near 27 GeV is 3 sigma lower than the model prediction.
- Unfolded isospin fluctuations exhibit deviations from the AMPT model at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV and 19.6 GeV, reaching approximately 2.5 sigma at these two energies.
- The fourth-order cumulant ratio calculated in AMPT is essentially unity, but reconstructed results from light nuclei have uncertainties exceeding the magnitude of the values themselves.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies by Vovchenko (2021) showing that baryon number fluctuations systematically deviate from proton fluctuations, indicating protons and neutrons are not statistically independent as previously assumed. The method enables experimental access to neutron fluctuations that have been inaccessible at RHIC and LHC detectors, and the unfolded isospin fluctuations show a possible double-peak energy dependence anticipated by QCD theory, with the first peak associated with the spinodal region (Steinheimer, 2012) and the second with the critical end point (Berdnikov, 1999). Future high-statistics measurements of light nuclei and improved modeling (Wang, 2023; ALICE, 2025; Zhang, 2025) together with machine learning techniques will be essential for extending this approach to higher-order cumulants.
