# Transversity Generalized Parton Distributions of $Δ$ with the Diquark Spectator Model

> We show quark transversity generalized parton pistributions (GPDs) of $Δ^+$ isobar by using the diquark spectator model for the first time. First, this model is tested by electric charge, magnetic-dipole and axial charge form factors, and it is used for calculating the transversity GPDs $H^{qT}_{1,3,5,7}$ of $Δ^+$. The quark transversity distribution $h_1$ is then obtained from the transversity GPDs in the forward limit. Then, helicity-flip amplitudes are shown numerically by using relations between the helicity amplitudes and the GPDs. Finally, by taking first moments of the GPDs, tensor form factors are obtained and we predict the tensor charge. Experimentally, $N$-$Δ$ transition GPDs are investigated in deeply virtual Compton scattering and virtual meson-production processes, and generalized distribution amplitudes, which correspond to the $s$-channel GPDs, could be investigated by the two-photon processes $γ^* γ\to Δ\barΔ$ at the electron-positron colliders. Therefore, the spin-3/2 $Δ$ GPDs could become interesting quantities experimentally in future.

## Key claim

The authors present the first numerical calculation of quark transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the spin-3/2 Δ⁺ isobar using the diquark spectator model, but only four of the sixteen possible transversity GPDs (H^{qT}_{1,3,5,7}) are non-zero because the hadron-quark-diquark vertex is simplified by setting parameters c₂ = c₃ = 0, which reduces the calculation to the spin-1/2 part of the structure.

## Method

The authors employ the diquark spectator model, treating the Δ⁺ as composed of a quark and a diquark (two quarks treated as a single entity), and calculate the quark transversity GPDs from the matrix element of the non-local quark operator with spin-flip using the Feynman diagram approach in the Breit frame. The hadron-quark-diquark vertex is simplified to g^{αβ} form by setting c₂ = c₃ = 0, which eliminates contributions from higher multipole terms. The d quark GPDs in Δ⁺ are computed as an example, and results for other quark flavors are obtained by counting the corresponding quark numbers.

## Key numbers

- Δ⁺ resonance mass M = 1.085 GeV, quark mass m_q = 0.4 GeV, diquark mass m_D = 0.76 GeV, cutoff mass m_R = 1.6 GeV
- The position x_max where ξ-even GPDs concentrate is x_max = 0.345 for ξ = 0, x_max = 0.476 for ξ = -0.2, and x_max = 0.607 for ξ = -0.4
- The tensor charge for the d quark in Δ⁺ is δd = 2[G^{qT}_1(0) - G^{qT}_2(0)] = 0.876
- Predicted tensor charges for Δ isobars: g_T^{Δ⁺⁺} = 2.628, g_T^{Δ⁺} = 0.876, g_T^{Δ⁰} = -0.876, g_T^{Δ⁻} = -2.628

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Fu et al., 2022, 2023, 2024) on the decomposition of spin-3/2 twist-2 GPDs, which previously covered unpolarized and longitudinal polarized GPDs, to now include transversity GPDs numerically. The calculations are motivated by experimental possibilities at Jefferson Lab and future Electron-Ion Colliders (EIC and EicC) for measuring N-Δ transition GPDs in deeply virtual Compton scattering and virtual meson production, as well as generalized distribution amplitudes in two-photon processes γ*γ → ΔΔ̄ at electron-positron colliders. The results enable future theoretical and experimental studies of spin-3/2 hadron structure, including comparisons with lattice QCD calculations such as those by Alexandrou et al. (2008, 2013).
