# Cascade production from antikaon induced reactions on lambda and sigma

> Using a gauged flavor SU(3) invariant hadronic Lagrangian, we study $Ξ$ production from $\bar K$ induced reactions on $Λ$ and $Σ$ in a coupled-channel approach. Including the four channels of $\bar KΛ$, $\bar KΣ$, $πΞ$, and $ηΞ$, we solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the $K$-matrix approximation by neglecting the off-shell contribution in the intermediate states. For the transition potential which drives the higher-order contribution, we consider all allowed Born diagrams in the $s$, $t$, and $u$ channels. With coupling constants determined from the SU(3) symmetry with empirical input, we find that the cross sections for the reactions $\bar KΛ\toπΞ$, $\bar KΣ\toπΞ$, $\bar KΛ\toηΞ$, and $\bar KΣ\toηΞ$ all have values of a few mb. In contrast to the results from the lowest-order Born approximation, the magnitude of these cross sections is less sensitive to the values of the cut-off parameters in the form factors. From the transition matrix in the coupled-channel approach, we have further evaluated the cross sections for the elastic process $\bar KΛ\to\bar KΛ$, $\bar KΣ\to\bar KΣ$, $πΞ\toπΞ$, and $ηΞ\toηΞ$ as well as for the inelastic processes $\bar KΛ\to\bar KΣ$ and $πΞ\toηΞ$. Implications of the reactions studied here in $Ξ$ production from relativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed.

# Cascade production from antikaon induced reactions on lambda and sigma

## Key claim

Using a gauged flavor SU(3) invariant hadronic Lagrangian in a coupled-channel approach with the K-matrix approximation (neglecting off-shell contributions in intermediate states), the authors find that cross sections for Ξ production from antikaon-induced reactions on Λ and Σ are a few mb and are less sensitive to the cutoff parameter in form factors than results from the lowest-order Born approximation, provided unitarity is preserved.

## Method

The authors constructed a transition potential from all allowed Born diagrams (s, t, and u channels) using a gauged flavor SU(3) invariant hadronic Lagrangian with empirical hadron masses and coupling constants determined from SU(3) symmetry. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the K-matrix approximation for a coupled-channel system including the four channels K̄Λ, K̄Σ, πΞ, and ηΞ, using an on-shell propagator and form factors with a monopole cutoff parameter Λ at all vertices. Total and differential cross sections were then obtained via partial wave expansion of the transition amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The universal pseudovector coupling constant g' = 9.2 GeV⁻¹, the universal vector meson coupling g = 13.0, and the F/D ratio parameter α = 0.56, determined from empirical values f_{πNN} = 1.00, g_{ρNN} = 3.25, and D/(D+F) = 0.64.
- The πΛΣ coupling constant f_{πΛΣ} = 0.741, the πΣΣ coupling constant f_{πΣΣ} = -0.722, and the πΞΞ coupling constant f_{πΞΞ} = 0.281.
- The KΛΞ coupling constant f_{KΛΞ} = 0.900 and the KΣΞ coupling constant f_{KΣΞ} = 3.54.
- The ηΛΛ coupling constant f_{ηΛΛ} = -1.06, the ηΣΣ coupling constant f_{ηΣΣ} = 4.26, and the ηΞΞ coupling constant f_{ηΞΞ} = -1.98.
- The K*ΛΞ vector coupling constant g_{K*ΛΞ} = 5.63 and the K*ΣΞ vector coupling constant g_{K*ΣΞ} = 3.25.
- The tensor coupling constant for ρNN is g^t_{ρNN} = 19.8.
- Cross sections for the reactions K̄Λ→πΞ, K̄Σ→πΞ, K̄Λ→ηΞ, and K̄Σ→ηΞ all have values of a few mb at energies much above threshold, with those involving pions in the final state about twice as large as those involving etas.
- The mixing angle between singlet and octet eta mesons was taken as θ = -23°, with a comparison case of θ = -10° showing essentially no change for pion-final-state reactions and slight variations for eta-final-state reactions.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel method previously applied by Lahiff and Kei to the empirically known reactions K̄N→πΛ and K̄N→πΣ, now applied to the less constrained Ξ production channels. The study directly tests the assumption made in the multiphase transport model by Pal (2002) that cross sections for these strangeness-exchange reactions have the same transition matrix elements as the measured K̄N→πΣ reaction. The results provide input cross sections needed for modeling multistrange baryon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where enhanced strangeness production has been suggested by Rafelski as a quark-gluon plasma signal.
