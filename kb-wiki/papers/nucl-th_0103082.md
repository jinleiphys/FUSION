# Medium effects in the production and pi0 gamma decay of omega-mesons in pA collisions in the GeV region

> The omega-resonance production and its pi0 gamma decay in p-A reactions close to threshold is considered within the Intranuclear Cascade (INC) Model. The pi0 gamma invariant mass distribution shows two components which correspond to the omega decay 'inside' and 'outside' the nucleus, respectively. The 'inside' component is distorted by medium effects, which introduce a mass shift as well as collisional broadening for the omega-meson and its decaying pion. The relative contribution of the 'inside' component is analyzed in detail for different kinematical conditions and nuclear targets. It is demonstrated that a measurement of the correlation in azimuthal angle between the pi0 and gamma momenta allows to separate events related to the 'inside' omega decay from different sources of background when uncorrelated pi0's and gamma's are produced.

# Wiki Page: Medium effects in the production and π⁰γ decay of ω-mesons in pA collisions in the GeV region

## Key claim

The π⁰γ invariant mass distribution from ω-meson decay in pA collisions contains two components corresponding to decays "inside" and "outside" the nucleus, where the "inside" component is distorted by medium effects including a mass shift and collisional broadening for both the ω-meson and its decaying pion; however, the authors note that the actual mass shift of the ω-meson at finite density is not known and the coefficient α = 0.18 used in their model is based on QCD sum rules for ω-mesons at rest, which may be momentum-dependent.

## Method

The authors employed an Intranuclear Cascade (INC) model to simulate ω-meson production and its π⁰γ decay in pA collisions near threshold, using a linearized kinetic equation solved numerically with the target nucleus treated as a mixture of degenerate neutron and proton Fermi gases in a spherical potential well. The ω-meson propagation through the nucleus was described using the eikonal approximation with an optical potential in the low-density approximation, and the ω mass shift was modeled according to Hatsuda and Lee (1995) as M*_R = M_R(1 − α ρ_A(r)/ρ₀) with α = 0.18, while collisional broadening was introduced via the optical theorem using σ_tot(ωN). The decay of ω to π⁰γ was simulated with the actual spectral shape, where for decays inside the nucleus the mass was generated according to a Breit-Wigner distribution with the in-medium mass and width calculated from the local nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- The collisional broadening for pions from ω decay is estimated as δΓ_π ≈ β_π σ_πN ρ₀ ≈ 100–120 MeV, with an upper limit of 200 MeV considered for off-shell effects.
- The total cross section for ω production in the final π⁰γ channel increases from 20 μb at T_p = 1.7 GeV to about 300 μb at 2.6 GeV for a Pb target, and from 3 μb to about 40 μb for a ¹²C target.
- The ratio R = N(M(π⁰,γ)=0.6–0.75 GeV)/N(M(π⁰,γ) ≥ 0.75 GeV) for Pb at T_p = 2.4 GeV without cuts changes from 0.029 (no medium effects) to 0.128 (with both collisional broadening and mass shift); with cuts (P_tot ≤ 0.5 GeV/c, P_t ≤ 0.2 GeV/c, φ = 180±30°) the ratio changes from 0.033 to 0.401.
- For ¹²C at T_p = 2.4 GeV, the ratio R without cuts changes from 0.008 (no medium effects) to 0.059 (with both effects); with cuts it changes from 0.020 to 0.183.
- The branching ratio BR(ω → π⁰γ) = 0.085 is used in the analysis.
- The probability P_Pb(inside) for ω to decay inside a Pb nucleus is approximately 0.4.
- The background cross section from NN → NΔπ reactions is estimated as σ_BG ≈ 5 μb per nucleon, while the signal cross section σ₀ for ω decay inside the nucleus is ≤ 7–10 μb per nucleon for a Pb target.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ω-meson medium modifications in pion-nucleus reactions by Golubeva et al. (2000) and Sibirtsev et al. (1999) to proton-induced reactions, specifically analyzing the π⁰γ decay channel which has a branching ratio about three orders of magnitude higher than the dilepton channel (BR(ω → e⁺e⁻) ≈ 7.1×10⁻⁵). The authors demonstrate that the azimuthal angle correlation between π⁰ and γ momenta provides a method to separate in-medium ω decay events from background sources such as π⁰ rescattering and non-resonant π⁰γ production, enabling future experiments at the COSY-Jülich accelerator with the ANKE spectrometer to probe the momentum dependence of the ω-meson mass shift in nuclear matter.
