# Discrete Symmetries on the Light Front and a General Relation Connecting Nucleon Electric Dipole and Anomalous Magnetic Moments

> We consider the electric dipole form factor, F_3(q^2), as well as the Dirac and Pauli form factors, F_1(q^2) and F_2(q^2), of the nucleon in the light-front formalism. We derive an exact formula for F_3(q^2) to complement those known for F_1(q^2) and F_2(q^2). We derive the light-front representation of the discrete symmetry transformations and show that time-reversal- and parity-odd effects are captured by phases in the light-front wave functions. We thus determine that the contributions to F_2(q^2) and F_3(q^2), Fock state by Fock state, are related, independent of the fundamental mechanism through which CP violation is generated. Our relation is not specific to the nucleon, but, rather, is true of spin-1/2 systems in general, be they lepton or baryon. The empirical values of the anomalous magnetic moments, in concert with empirical bounds on the associated electric dipole moments, can better constrain theories of CP violation. In particular, we find that the neutron and proton electric dipole moments echo the isospin structure of the anomalous magnetic moments, kappa^n ~ - kappa^p.

## Key claim

The authors derive a model-independent relation connecting the electric dipole form factor \(F_3(q^2)\) to the Pauli form factor \(F_2(q^2)\) Fock state by Fock state for any spin-1/2 system, with the caveat that this relation holds regardless of the fundamental mechanism generating CP violation, provided the light-front wave functions contain appropriate CP-violating phases.

## Method

The authors construct the light-front Fock expansion of the electromagnetic current matrix elements for a spin-1/2 system, deriving exact overlap formulas for the Dirac, Pauli, and electric dipole form factors \(F_1(q^2)\), \(F_2(q^2)\), and \(F_3(q^2)\) in the \(q^+=0\) frame. They then develop the action of parity, time-reversal, and charge-conjugation transformations on light-front wave functions, showing that time-reversal- and parity-odd effects are encoded as phases in the wave functions. From this formalism, they extract a relation between the Fock-state contributions to \(F_2\) and \(F_3\) that is independent of the specific CP-violating mechanism.

## Key numbers

* At \(q^2=0\), the universal relation for a spin-1/2 baryon is \(d_i = 2 \kappa_i \tan \beta_i\), where \(d_i\) is the electric dipole moment contribution from Fock state \(i\), \(\kappa_i\) is the anomalous magnetic moment contribution from that Fock state, and \(\beta_i\) is the CP-violating phase in that Fock state's light-front wave function.
* The electric dipole moment is defined as \(d = (e/M) F_3(0)\) and the anomalous magnetic moment as \(\kappa = (e/2M) F_2(0)\), where \(e = |e|\) is the fundamental unit of charge and \(M\) is the proton mass.
* The neutron and proton electric dipole moments echo the isospin structure of the anomalous magnetic moments, with \(\kappa^n \sim -\kappa^p\).

## Context

This work extends the light-front formalism for electromagnetic form factors originally developed by Drell and Yan (1970) and Brodsky and Drell (1980) to include the electric dipole form factor \(F_3(q^2)\). It provides a first-principles derivation of a relation that had been noted phenomenologically by Bigi and Uraltsev (1991) and observed in specific supersymmetric models by Feng, Matchev, and Shadmi (2001). The result enables constraints on theories of CP violation by combining empirical values of anomalous magnetic moments with bounds on electric dipole moments, and it applies to any spin-1/2 system including leptons, quarks, and baryons.
