# Elliptic Flow Suppression due to Hadron Mass Spectrum

> Hadron resonance gas models provide a good description of the equation of state of quantum chromodynamics determined by lattice QCD calculations at temperatures $T \sim 100-155$ MeV. In this paper we investigate the effects of an exponentially increasing hadron mass spectrum (Hagedorn spectrum) on the azimuthal anisotropy of the rapidly expanding matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. If the temperature at which the conversion from fluid degrees of freedom to hadrons is sufficiently close to the Hagedorn temperature, the production of Hagedorn resonances suppresses the differential elliptic flow of all hadron species.

## Key claim

If the switching temperature at which fluid degrees of freedom are converted to hadrons is sufficiently close to the Hagedorn temperature (around 155 MeV), the production of heavy Hagedorn resonances suppresses the differential elliptic flow of all hadron species, with the suppression magnitude comparable to that from viscous effects with eta/s ~ 1/4pi.

## Method

The authors solved boost-invariant ideal relativistic fluid dynamics with a single averaged optical Glauber initial condition for 20-30% most central RHIC events at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, using a Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) algorithm. They computed the differential elliptic flow v2(pT) of all hadron species emitted from isothermal hypersurfaces at switching temperatures Tsw=130 MeV and Tsw=155 MeV, comparing a standard Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model against three parametrizations of an exponentially increasing Hagedorn mass spectrum (rho1, rho2, rho3) with different Hagedorn temperatures. Particle decays were not included.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperatures used: T_H1 = 0.252 GeV, T_H2 = 0.167 GeV, T_H3 = 0.180 GeV
- Switching temperatures investigated: T_sw = 0.130 GeV and T_sw = 0.155 GeV
- The integrated v2 changed by only 4% with respect to the HRG value at T_sw = 0.155 GeV when Hagedorn states were included
- The differential v2 suppression becomes appreciable for pT > 1.5 GeV
- Results saturate when the maximum Hagedorn resonance mass M_max ~ 10 GeV
- The trace anomaly from lattice QCD (Borsanyi 2010) is described up to T ~ 155 MeV by the Hagedorn gas models

## Context

This work extends the study of Hagedorn resonances in hadron resonance gas models, which had previously been shown to affect chemical equilibration times (Noronha-Hostler 2007, 2009), particle ratios (Noronha-Hostler 2009), and shear viscosity (Noronha-Hostler 2008, Pal). The paper demonstrates that these resonances also impact dynamical observables in heavy ion collisions, specifically elliptic flow. The results indicate that current hybrid models coupling fluid dynamics to hadronic transport (such as those by Song, Hirano 2005, Niemi 2012, Bozek 2012) must account for Hagedorn resonances to correctly extract the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma. The suppression of differential elliptic flow from heavy resonance production is of the same order as that from viscous effects with eta/s ~ 1/4pi, motivating future event-by-event studies of higher flow harmonics.
