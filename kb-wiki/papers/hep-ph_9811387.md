# Quark and Nuclear Matter in the Linear Chiral Meson Model

> We present an analytical description of the phase transitions from a nucleon gas to nuclear matter and from nuclear matter to quark matter within the same model. The equation of state for quark and nuclear matter is encoded in the effective potential of a linear sigma model. We exploit an exact differential equation for its dependence upon the chemical potential $μ$ associated to conserved baryon number. An approximate solution for vanishing temperature is used to discuss possible phase transitions as the baryon density increases. For a nucleon gas and nuclear matter we find a substantial density enhancement as compared to quark models which neglect the confinement to baryons. The results point out that the latter models are not suitable to discuss the phase diagram at low temperature.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical description of both the nucleon gas to nuclear matter transition and the nuclear matter to quark matter transition within a single linear sigma model, but the authors emphasize that their treatment of quarks and baryons is "still very crude" and that a different picture may appear for two-flavor QCD, particularly due to the possible influence of other condensates.

## Method

The authors exploit an exact functional differential equation for the dependence of the effective action on the baryon chemical potential, which expresses the mu-derivative of the effective action in terms of the exact field-dependent fermion propagator. They approximate the fermion propagators using a simple ansatz with effective constituent masses and vector meson couplings, and separate the momentum integration into a high-momentum region described by quarks and a low-momentum region described by nucleons, with a transition momentum scale q_H. The mu-dependent part of the effective potential is then integrated analytically for vanishing temperature, while the mu-independent part is parametrized phenomenologically.

## Key numbers

* The contribution of a free gas of baryons to the dependence of the free energy on the chemical potential is enhanced by a factor of 27 compared to the contribution from a free gas of quarks.
* For nuclear matter, a typical value of the transition momentum between quark and baryon descriptions is q_H greater than or approximately 260 MeV.
* The first order line for the gas-liquid nuclear transition exhibits a critical endpoint for a temperature of about 10 MeV.
* The momentum integration for the chemical potential flow is restricted to momenta less than about 600 MeV, with the quark description used for momenta above q_H^2 and the baryon description for momenta below q_H^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of the QCD phase diagram at nonzero baryon density, including studies by Kleinschmidt (1992), Berges and Rajagopal (1998), and others cited in the paper. The authors argue that quark models which neglect confinement to baryons are not suitable for discussing the phase diagram at low temperature, because the nucleon enhancement factor of 27 from binding quarks into baryons plays a crucial quantitative role. The results enable future studies of the critical endpoint of the first order line between quark and nuclear matter, which could produce distinctive signatures in relativistic heavy ion collisions as discussed by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998).
