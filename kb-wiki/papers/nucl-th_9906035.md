# Axial Vector Current and Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling Constant on $μ^- p \to n ν_μ γ$ reaction

> The recent TRIUMF experiment for $μ^- p \to n ν_μ γ$ gave a surprising result that the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant $g_P$ was larger than the value obtained from $μ^- p \to n ν_μ$ experiment as much as 44 %. Reexamining axial vector current on the gauge theory, we found an additional term to the matrix element of Beder and Fearing which was used to extract the $g_P$ value from the measured photon energy spectrum. This additional term, which is self gauge invariant, plays a key role in restoring the reliability of $g_P (- 0.88 m_μ^2) = 6.77 g_A (0)$. Comparison with conventional approaches is also presented.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the discrepancy between the TRIUMF radiative muon capture (RMC) experiment, which extracted a value of the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant \( \hat{g}_P = 9.8 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.3 \), and the theoretically expected value \( \hat{g}_P = 6.77 \) from the Partially Conserved Axial Current (PCAC) hypothesis can be resolved by including an additional, previously omitted gauge-invariant term in the matrix element used to analyze the RMC photon energy spectrum.

## Method

The authors rederived the axial vector current in the presence of an external electromagnetic field using a gauge transformation of the linear sigma model Lagrangian. From this derivation, they obtained an additional term in the axial current (the fourth term in their equation 22) which contributes to the RMC amplitude. They then constructed a new RMC amplitude by adding a corresponding term, \(\Delta M_e\), to the standard Beder-Fearing amplitude, ensuring the total amplitude remains gauge invariant. The photon energy spectrum was calculated using this new amplitude for the theoretical value of \( \hat{g}_P = 6.77 \) and compared to the experimental spectrum from the TRIUMF group.

## Key numbers

*   The TRIUMF experiment extracted a value of \( \hat{g}_P \equiv g_P(-0.88 m_\mu^2) / g_A(0) = 9.8 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.3 \), which is 44% larger than the PCAC prediction.
*   The PCAC prediction for the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant is \( g_P(-0.88 m_\mu^2) = 6.77 g_A(0) \), or equivalently \( \hat{g}_P = 6.77 \).
*   For the photon energy spectrum with \( k \ge 60 \) MeV, the number of RMC photons observed was \( 279 \pm 26 \). The spectrum calculated with the new term for \( \hat{g}_P = 6.77 \) yielded 273 photons, while the spectrum from the TRIUMF best-fit curve (without the new term, for \( \hat{g}_P = 9.8 \)) yielded 299 photons.

## Context

This work addresses a puzzle created by the TRIUMF RMC experiment (Jonkmans et al., 1996), which found a value for the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant \( g_P \) that was 44% larger than the value obtained from ordinary muon capture (OMC) experiments (Bardin et al., 1981) and the prediction of the PCAC hypothesis. The authors reexamined the theoretical framework of Beder and Fearing (1987, 1980), which was used to extract the \( g_P \) value from the RMC data. By deriving the axial current from gauge theory, they found a missing term in the Beder-Fearing amplitude. Including this term allows the RMC data to be consistent with the standard PCAC value of \( \hat{g}_P = 6.77 \), thereby restoring the reliability of the PCAC prediction and resolving the discrepancy between the OMC and RMC results.
