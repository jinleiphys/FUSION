# Valence Quark Transversity at Small $x$

> In our previous work we established a formalism which allows one to determine the small-$x$ asymptotics of any transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution function (TMD PDF) of the proton at small values of strong coupling. In this paper we apply this formalism to the valence quark transversity TMD. We relate the valence quark transversity to the transversely polarized dipole scattering amplitude, written in terms of the fundamental transversely-polarized "Wilson line" operator, an expression for which we derive explicitly as well. We then write down the evolution equation for the transversely polarized dipole amplitude. Solving that equation we arrive at the following small-$x$ asymptotics of the valence quark transversity in the large-$N_c$ limit: \begin{align} h_{1T}^v (x, k_T^2) \sim h_{1T}^{\perp \, v} (x, k_T^2) \sim \left( \frac{1}{x} \right)^{-1 + 2 \, \sqrt{\frac{α_s \, N_c}{2 \, π}} } . \notag \end{align} This result is in agreement with one of the two possible small-$x$ asymptotics for the transverse structure function found previously by Kirschner, Mankiewicz, Schäfer, and Szymanowski.

## Key claim

The valence quark transversity TMDs \(h_{1T}^v (x, k_T^2)\) and \(h_{1T}^{\perp \, v} (x, k_T^2)\) both exhibit the same small-\(x\) asymptotic behavior \(\sim (1/x)^{-1 + 2 \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c / (2\pi)}}\) in the double logarithmic approximation (DLA) and large-\(N_c\) limit, which agrees with one of the two possible asymptotics previously found by Kirschner, Mankiewicz, Schäfer, and Szymanowski (1996) for the chiral-odd transversity PDF \(h_1(x, Q^2)\).

## Method

The authors apply a formalism developed in their prior work (Kovchegov and Sievert, 2018) to determine small-\(x\) asymptotics of transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs). They first derive an explicit expression for the transversely-polarized fundamental Wilson line operator, which involves both a gluon-exchange contribution proportional to the quark mass and a quark-exchange contribution. They then relate the valence quark transversity TMD to the transversely-polarized dipole scattering amplitude, write down the evolution equation for this amplitude in the double logarithmic approximation, and solve it in the large-\(N_c\) limit to obtain the small-\(x\) asymptotics.

## Key numbers

* The small-\(x\) asymptotics of the valence quark transversity TMDs is \(h_{1T}^v (x, k_T^2) \sim h_{1T}^{\perp \, v} (x, k_T^2) \sim \left( \frac{1}{x} \right)^{-1 + 2 \, \sqrt{\frac{\alpha_s \, N_c}{2 \, \pi}} }\), valid in the large-\(N_c\) limit and DLA.
* The transversely-polarized Wilson line operator is suppressed by a factor of \(1/s^2\) relative to the eikonal contribution, indicating that transversity TMDs are suppressed by \(x^2\) compared to unpolarized TMDs at small \(x\).
* The transversely-polarized Wilson line is proportional to the quark mass \(m\) in the gluon-exchange contribution, as shown in Eq. (VpolT2).

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Kovchegov and Sievert (2018) for determining small-\(x\) asymptotics of quark TMDs, which was previously applied to quark helicity TMDs (Kovchegov, 2015-2017). The result agrees with one of the two possible small-\(x\) asymptotics for the chiral-odd transversity PDF \(h_1(x, Q^2)\) found by Kirschner, Mankiewicz, Schäfer, and Szymanowski (1996); the other possibility \((1/x)^0\) from that work arises from leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) terms outside the DLA precision employed here. The findings are relevant to resolving the "transverse spin puzzle", the tension between tensor charge values extracted from global fits (Radici, 2018; Bacchetta, 2012) and lattice QCD results, by quantifying whether small-\(x\) quarks carry significant transverse spin.
