# Field Transformations and the Classical Equation of Motion in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The construction of effective Lagrangians commonly involves the application of the `classical equation of motion' to eliminate redundant structures and thus generate the minimal number of independent terms. We investigate this procedure in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. The use of the 'classical equation of motion' is interpreted in terms of field transformations. Such an interpretation is crucial if one wants to bring a given Lagrangian into a canonical form with a minimal number of terms. We emphasize that the application of field transformations not only eliminates structures, or, what is equivalent, expresses certain structures in terms of already known different structures, but also leads to a modification of coefficients of higher--order terms. This will become relevant, once one considers effective interaction terms beyond next--to--leading order, i.e., beyond $O(p^4)$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using the "classical equation of motion" to eliminate redundant terms in chiral perturbation theory is equivalent to a field transformation, but this simple replacement procedure only works correctly for identifying independent structures at a given order; it fails to produce the correct coefficients when transforming a non-canonical Lagrangian into canonical form beyond next-to-leading order (beyond \(O(p^4)\)).

## Method

The authors derive the change in the functional form of the chiral Lagrangian under a field transformation \(U = \exp(iS(U'))U'\), where the generator \(S\) is constrained by the transformation properties of the Goldstone boson field under \(SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R\), parity, and charge conjugation. They then construct explicit generators at \(O(p^2)\) and show how choosing the parameters \(\alpha_1\) and \(\alpha_2\) in \(S_2\) cancels the redundant terms in the \(O(p^4)\) Lagrangian. For higher orders, they describe an iterative algorithm analogous to the Foldy–Wouthuysen method, where successive transformations with generators of increasing chiral order (\(S_2, S_4, \dots\)) are applied, and all terms up to the desired final order must be kept consistently at each step.

## Key numbers

* The lowest-order Lagrangian \({\cal L}_2\) contains the pion decay constant in the chiral limit \(F_0\), appearing as \(F_0^2/4\) in the kinetic and mass terms.
* Two independent generators exist at \(O(p^2)\): \(S_2 = i\alpha_1(D^2U U^\dagger - U(D^2U)^\dagger) + i\alpha_2(\chi U^\dagger - U\chi^\dagger - \frac{1}{3}Tr(\chi U^\dagger - U\chi^\dagger))\), with arbitrary real parameters \(\alpha_1\) and \(\alpha_2\).
* The cancellation of the two redundant \(O(p^4)\) structures requires \(\alpha_1 = 4c_1/F_0^2\) and \(\alpha_2 = 4c_2/F_0^2\), where \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) are the coefficients of the non-canonical terms in \(\tilde{\cal L}_4\).
* The toy model uses a coupling constant \(g_{eff}\) with dimension energy\(^{-2}\) and produces a fermion-fermion scattering amplitude \(M = -ig_{eff}^2\left(\frac{t^2}{t-m^2} + \frac{u^2}{u-m^2}\right)\) at order \(g_{eff}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the formal discussion of field transformations and the equivalence theorem in effective field theories, as previously treated by Arzt (1993), Leutwyler (1994), and Simma (1993), by providing explicit practical algorithms for chiral perturbation theory. The paper directly builds on the canonical \(O(p^4)\) Lagrangian of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) and shows how to systematically transform non-canonical Lagrangians (which arise, for example, from bosonization of effective quark models by Balog, Ebert, Ball, and Belkov) into that standard form. The iterative method enables consistent comparison of coefficients from different models at orders beyond \(O(p^4)\), up to any desired order in the momentum expansion.
