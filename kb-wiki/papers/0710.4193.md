# Reaction cross sections of carbon isotopes incident on a proton

> We systematically study total reaction cross sections of carbon isotopes with N=6-16 on a proton target for wide range of incident energies, putting an emphasis on the difference from the case of a carbon target. The analysis includes the reaction cross sections of ^{19,20,22}C at 40 AMeV, the data of which have recently been measured at RIKEN. The Glauber theory is used to calculate the reaction cross sections. To describe the intrinsic structure of the carbon isotopes, we use a Slater determinant generated from a phenomenological mean-field potential, and construct the density distributions. To go beyond the simple mean-field model, we adopt two types of dynamical models: One is a core+n model for odd-neutron nuclei, and the other is a core+n+n model for 16C and 22C. We propose empirical formulas which are useful in predicting unknown cross sections.

## Key claim

The Glauber model, combined with mean-field and core-plus-valence-neutron descriptions of carbon isotopes, can systematically reproduce and predict total reaction cross sections on a proton target across 40–800 AMeV, but the model may not be reliable at 40 AMeV and the eikonal approximation gives about 15% larger cross sections than exact quantum-mechanical calculations at that energy.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber theory to calculate total reaction cross sections for carbon isotopes (N=6–16) on a proton target. Nuclear densities were generated from a Slater determinant using a phenomenological mean-field potential, supplemented by core+n models for odd-neutron isotopes and core+n+n models for 16C and 22C. The nucleon-nucleon profile function parameters (σtot, α, β for pp and pn) were taken from experimental data in the literature, and the optical limit approximation was used for most calculations after verifying that breakup effects were negligible (less than 1% difference between few-body and optical-limit results).

## Key numbers

- For proton-12C at 40 MeV: σR = 432 mb; at 800 MeV: σR = 243 mb
- For proton-22C at 40 MeV: σR = 957 mb (with S2n = 0.489 MeV, rm = 3.6 fm); at 800 MeV: σR = 442 mb
- For proton-22C at 40 MeV with S2n = 0.122 MeV (rm = 4.1 fm): σR = 1005 mb
- The empirical formula σR(p + 6+NC)/σR(p + 12C) = R(C) × (6σpptot + Nσpntot)/(6σpptot + 6σpntot) holds with R(C) = 0.96 ± 0.05 across 153 data points (9 isotopes × 17 energies)
- rms matter radii range from 2.31 fm (12C) to 3.58 fm (22C); rms neutron radii range from 2.30 fm (12C) to 3.92 fm (22C)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic Glauber-model analysis of carbon isotopes on a 12C target (Abu-Ibrahim et al., 2004) to proton targets, motivated by ongoing measurements at RIKEN for 19,20,22C at 40 AMeV (Tanaka, 2007). The study enables prediction of unknown cross sections for neutron-rich carbon isotopes and provides empirical formulas for rapid estimation. It also demonstrates that proton targets are more sensitive than carbon targets to the extended neutron surface of halo nuclei like 22C, because the pn interaction is longer-ranged and stronger than the nn or pp interaction at low energies.
