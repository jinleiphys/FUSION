# Electroproduction of the N*(1535) resonance at large momentum transfer

> We report on the first lattice calculation of light-cone distribution amplitudes of the N*(1535) resonance, which are used to calculate the transition form factors at large momentum transfers using light-cone sum rules. In the region Q^2 > 2 GeV^2, where the light-cone expansion is expected to converge, the results appear to be in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice calculation of light-cone distribution amplitudes (DAs) of the N*(1535) resonance, and uses them in light-cone sum rules to compute transition form factors; the results agree with experimental data only in the region Q² > 2 GeV², where the light-cone expansion is expected to converge.

## Method

The authors calculate moments of the leading-twist distribution amplitudes of the N*(1535) resonance using lattice QCD, extracting them from matrix elements of local three-quark operators with covariant derivatives on QCDSF/DIK configurations at β=5.40. These lattice-determined DAs are then used as input to light-cone sum rules (LCSRs) to compute the helicity amplitudes A₁/₂(Q²) and S₁/₂(Q²) for electroproduction of the N*(1535) resonance, with the sum rules taking the same functional form as those for the proton's Dirac and Pauli form factors after replacing m_N with m_N*.

## Key numbers

- Normalization constant f_N* = 4.544(117)(223) × 10⁻³ GeV², about 50% larger than f_N = 3.234(63)(86) × 10⁻³ GeV²
- First moment φ¹⁰⁰ = 0.4765(33)(155) for N*(1535), compared to 0.3999(37)(139) for the nucleon, indicating a more asymmetric DA
- Second moment φ²⁰⁰ = 0.2274(89)(307) for N*(1535) versus 0.1816(64)(212) for the nucleon
- Normalization constant λ₂* = 191.9(44)(391) × 10⁻³ GeV² for N*(1535), compared to λ₂ = 70.02(128)(268) × 10⁻³ GeV² for the nucleon
- All moments and normalization constants are given at μ²_MSbar = 1 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculation of nucleon distribution amplitudes by Braun et al. (2008) to the N*(1535) resonance, which appears as a byproduct of the same correlation functions due to parity projection. The approach combines lattice constraints with light-cone sum rules, previously applied to the nucleon by Braun et al. (2001, 2006), and enables quantitative predictions for transition form factors that were previously impossible due to the lack of DA information for nucleon resonances beyond the Δ(1232). The results suggest that the large asymmetry of the nucleon DA found in QCD sum rule calculations by Chernyak (1984) and King (1986) may be contaminated by opposite-parity states.
