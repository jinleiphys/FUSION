# Zero mode quantization of multi-Skyrmions

> A zero mode quantization of the minimal energy SU(2) Skyrmions for nucleon numbers four to nine and seventeen is described. This involves quantizing the rotational and isorotational modes of the configurations. For nucleon numbers four, six and eight the ground states obtained are in agreement with the observed nuclear states of Helium, Lithium and Beryllium. However, for nucleon numbers five, seven, nine and seventeen the spins obtained conflict with the observed isodoublet nuclear states.

## Key claim

Zero mode quantization of minimal-energy SU(2) Skyrmions yields ground-state spins and isospins that agree with observed nuclear states for nucleon numbers 4, 6, and 8, but for nucleon numbers 5, 7, 9, and 17 the predicted ground-state spins conflict with the observed isodoublet nuclear states, suggesting that inclusion of vibrational modes or alternative minima may be required.

## Method

The author performs semiclassical quantization of the rotational and isorotational zero modes of minimal-energy Skyrmion configurations for baryon numbers 4 through 9 and 17, using the moduli space approximation that truncates the field configuration space to the quotient (SU(2)×SU(2))/K, where K is a discrete subgroup encoding the symmetries of each classical solution. The Finkelstein-Rubenstein constraints are evaluated using the rational map ansatz for Skyrmions, which allows determination of whether closed loops in configuration space are contractible or noncontractible, thereby fixing the allowed quantum states. The moments of inertia are computed numerically from the rational-map-generated Skyrme fields, and the Hamiltonian takes the form of a rigid rotor in space and isospace.

## Key numbers

* For B=1, the moment of inertia is U_ij = V_ij = 106.4 δ_ij (in units of 1/e³ f_π), with rotational and isorotational moments equal due to spherical symmetry.
* For B=4, the isorotational moments of inertia are U_11 = U_22 = 254.0 and U_33 = 306.4, while the rotational moments are V_11 = V_22 = V_33 = 1162.9 (same units).
* U_ij and W_ij increase approximately linearly with baryon number B, while V_ij increases approximately as B².
* For B=5 and B=9, the zero-mode ground state is an isodoublet with spin 1/2, conflicting with the observed isodoublet ground states with spin 3/2.
* For B=7 and B=17, the zero-mode ground state is an isodoublet with spin 7/2, conflicting with the observed isodoublet ground states (spin 3/2 for B=7, spin 5/2 for B=17).

## Context

This work extends the zero-mode quantization approach previously applied to the axially symmetric B=2 Skyrmion (obtaining the deuteron ground state) and the tetrahedrally symmetric B=3 Skyrmion (obtaining spin-1/2, isospin-1/2 ground states matching ³H and ³He) by Baskerville (1995) and others. The analysis uses the rational map ansatz of Houghton, Manton, and Sutcliffe (1998) to determine Skyrmion symmetries and evaluate Finkelstein-Rubenstein constraints. The results enable future work incorporating vibrational modes (as described by Barnes, Baskerville, and Tchrakian (1997) for B=2 and B=4) to potentially resolve the ground-state discrepancies for odd baryon numbers, and motivate further numerical investigation of whether the proposed minimal-energy solutions for B=5, 7, 9, and 17 are truly well-defined global minima.
