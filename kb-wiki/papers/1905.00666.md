## Key claim

The authors compute the production of the $\Xi(1690)^-$ resonance via the $K^-p \to K^+K^-\Lambda$ reaction using an effective Lagrangian approach, but the calculation assumes a spin-parity $J^p = 1/2^-$ for $\Xi(1690)^-$ as suggested by the BaBar Collaboration and theoretical works, and sets unknown coupling constants involving two hyperon resonances to zero to avoid theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors employ tree-level Born approximation with effective Lagrangian densities for the $K^-p \to K^+K^-\Lambda$ reaction, including $s$- and $u$-channel $\Sigma/\Lambda$ ground states and resonances for the $\Xi$-pole contributions, and $s$-channel $\Lambda$, $u$-channel nucleon pole, and $t$-channel $K^-$-exchange for the $\phi$-pole contributions. The $\Xi$-pole includes $\Xi(1320)$, $\Xi(1535)$, $\Xi(1690)(J^p=1/2^-)$, and $\Xi(1820)(J^p=3/2^-)$, while the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ intermediate states include $\Lambda(1116)$, $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1520)$, $\Lambda(1670)$, $\Sigma(1192)$, and $\Sigma(1385)$. Coupling constants for ground-state hadrons are taken from the Nijmegen soft-core potential model (NSC97a), while those for $s$-wave resonances are obtained from the chiral unitary model, and the authors fit model parameters to the experimental data from Gay (year not specified) for the three-body reaction.

## Key numbers

- The Dalitz plot density $d^2\sigma/dM_{K^+K^-}dM_{K^-\Lambda}$ is computed at $p_{K^-} = 4.2$ GeV/$c$ ($E_{\rm cm} = 3.01$ GeV).
- The total cross section for $K^-p \to K^+\Xi(1690)^-$ peaks at $p_{K^-} = 2.6$ GeV/$c$ ($E_{\rm cm} = 2.47$ GeV) with a value of $1.5~\mu$b.
- The fit to experimental data is performed in the low-mass region below $M^2_{K^-\Lambda} = 3.3$ GeV$^2/c^4$.
- The authors present cross sections for the two-body reaction in a beam momentum range from 2.1 GeV/$c$ to 2.3 GeV/$c$ near threshold.
- The Dalitz plot analysis is demonstrated for $p_{K^-} = 1.915 \sim 2.065$ GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the study of $\Xi(1690)^-$ production, which has been observed by experiments such as those from the BaBar Collaboration (2010) and Belle Collaboration, but whose spin and parity have not been clearly determined. The authors note that the BaBar Collaboration favored a $J=1/2$ assignment from decay angular distribution, while theoretical works by Xiao (year not specified) and Khem (year not specified) suggested $J^p=1/2^-$. The calculation enables future $K^-$ beam experiments to perform Dalitz plot analysis for direct information on $\Xi(1690)^-$ production, avoiding the interference complications with $a_0(980)$ that appear in $\Lambda_c^+$ decay experiments.
