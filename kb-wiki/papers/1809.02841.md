# Effect of an absorbing medium on particle oscillations

> Particle oscillations in absorbing matter are considered. The approach based on the optical potential is shown to be inapplicable in the strong absorption region. Models with Hermitian Hamiltonian are analyzed. They give an increase of the process width in comparison with the model based on the optical potential.

## Key claim

The standard optical-potential approach to particle oscillations in absorbing matter contains double counting and is inapplicable in the strong absorption region; models with a Hermitian Hamiltonian give a process width that scales as \(\Gamma\) (the annihilation width), whereas the potential model gives a width that scales as \(1/\Gamma\) in the strong absorption limit.

## Method

The author compares three approaches to \(n \rightarrow \bar{n} \rightarrow M\) (antineutron annihilation) in nuclear matter: (1) the standard potential model, which uses a Schrödinger equation with an antineutron optical potential \(U_{\bar{n}}\); (2) a field-theoretical model with a bare antineutron propagator; and (3) a field-theoretical model with a dressed antineutron propagator, where a scalar field \(V_1\) is separated from the annihilation Hamiltonian. The process width is calculated directly from the off-diagonal matrix element in each case, without invoking the optical potential for the field-theoretical models.

## Key numbers

* For realistic parameters \(\Gamma = 100\ \text{MeV}\) and \(|\text{Re}V| = 10\ \text{MeV}\), the model with a dressed propagator gives a lower limit on the free-space \(n\bar{n}\) oscillation time of \(\tau = 1.2 \times 10^{9}\ \text{s}\).
* The model with a bare propagator gives a lower limit on the free-space \(n\bar{n}\) oscillation time of \(\tau = 10^{16}\ \text{yr}\).
* The ratio of widths \(r = \Gamma_s / \Gamma_{\text{pot}} = 1 + (\Gamma / (2\,\text{Re}V))^2\); when \(|\text{Re}V| = \Gamma/2\), \(r = 2\); when \(|\text{Re}V| = \Gamma/4\), \(r = 5\).
* In the strong absorption limit (\(\Gamma/2 \gg |\text{Re}V|\)), the potential model gives \(\Gamma_{\text{pot}} = 4\epsilon_{n\bar{n}}^2 / \Gamma\), while the Hermitian model gives \(\Gamma_s \sim \Gamma\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior field-theoretical treatment of \(n\bar{n}\) oscillations in matter (Nazaruk, 2015) and critiques the standard optical-potential approach used in earlier studies of \(n\bar{n}\) oscillations (Dover, 1983; Kondratyuk, 1985) and \(K^0\bar{K}^0\) oscillations. The analysis shows that the potential model contains double counting, a point previously argued qualitatively by Nazaruk (2015) on a finite time interval. The results enable a more reliable extraction of the free-space \(n\bar{n}\) oscillation time from experimental limits, and the author notes that similar reinforcement effects occur in \(K^0_S\) regeneration (Nazaruk, 2013).
