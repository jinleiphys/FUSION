# Nuclear magnetism in the deformed halo nucleus $^{31}$Ne

> Based on the point-coupling density functional, the time-odd deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (TODRHBc) is developed. Then the effects of nuclear magnetism on halo phenomenon are explored by taking the experimentally suggested deformed halo nucleus $^{31}$Ne as an example. For $^{31}$Ne, nuclear magnetism contributes 0.09 MeV to total binding energy, and the breaking of Kramers degeneracy results in 0-0.2 MeV splitting in canonical single-particle spectra. The blocked neutron level has a dominant component of $p$ wave and it is marginally bound. However, if we ignore nuclear magnetism, the level becomes unbound. This shows a subtle mechanism that nuclear magnetism changes the single-particle energies, causing a nucleus to become bound. Based on the TODRHBc results, a prolate one-neutron halo is formed around the near-spherical core in $^{31}$Ne. The nucleon current is mostly contributed by the halo rather than the core, except near the center of the nucleus. A layered structure in the neutron current distribution is observed and studied in detail.

## Key claim

Nuclear magnetism (the time-odd field) can change the single-particle energy of the blocked neutron orbital in the deformed halo nucleus 31Ne from unbound to bound, but this result may depend on numerical truncation conditions even though convergence has been checked with high accuracy.

## Method

The authors developed the time-odd deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (TODRHBc) by incorporating the time-odd field self-consistently into the existing DRHBc theory, using the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1. They performed calculations for 31Ne with each possible blocked orbital independently and took the result with the lowest total energy as the ground state, comparing with time-even DRHBc calculations that neglect nuclear magnetism via the equal-filling approximation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear magnetism contributes 0.09 MeV to the total binding energy of 31Ne.
- The breaking of Kramers degeneracy results in 0 to 0.2 MeV splitting in canonical single-particle spectra.
- The blocked neutron level in TODRHBc has single-particle energy epsilon = -0.087 MeV (bound), while in time-even DRHBc it is at epsilon = 0.049 MeV (unbound).
- After including rotational correction (0.93 MeV from cranking approximation), the one-neutron separation energy becomes 0.56 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.15(+0.16/-0.10) MeV.
- The quadrupole deformation of 31Ne is 0.170 in TODRHBc and 0.167 in time-even DRHBc.
- The core rms radius is 3.47 fm, the halo rms radius is 7.17 fm, and the halo quadrupole deformation is 4.21.
- The proton Fermi energy decreases by 0.06 MeV after including nuclear magnetism.

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc theory developed by Zhou (2010) and Li (2012) by adding the time-odd field, which had previously been neglected in odd-mass halo nuclei studies through the equal-filling approximation. The study of 31Ne builds on experimental halo evidence from Nakamura (2009, 2014) and Gaudefroy (2012), and on prior DRHBc calculations by Zhong (2022). The results demonstrate a mechanism by which nuclear magnetism can bind an otherwise unbound orbital, with implications for predicting the neutron drip line and understanding magnetic moments in exotic nuclei.
