# Heavy-quark potential in Gribov-Zwanziger approach around deconfinement phase transition

> The interaction potential between a pair of heavy quarks is calculated with resummed perturbation method in Gribov-Zwanziger approach at finite temperature. The resummed loop correction makes the potential complex. While the real part is, as expected, screened and becomes short-ranged in hot medium, the strength of the imaginary part increases with temperature and is comparable with the real part, which is very different from the previous calculation in HTL approach. This means that, both the color screening and Landau damping play important role in the dissociation of heavy flavor hadrons in hot medium.

## Key claim

The Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) approach, which incorporates gluon confinement via a modified gluon propagator, yields a heavy-quark potential whose imaginary part is much larger than that obtained from the Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) approach, and becomes comparable in strength to the real part near the deconfinement temperature, implying that Landau damping is as important as color screening for quarkonium dissociation in this regime.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop gluon self-energy using the GZ gluon propagator in Coulomb gauge at finite temperature, then resum the gluon and quark loops to obtain the full propagator. The static heavy-quark potential is extracted from the real-time Wilson loop in the limit of infinite time, following the method of Laine (2006). The Gribov mass parameter is determined self-consistently from a gap equation, using an interpolated running coupling that connects vacuum values (from potential model fits) to high-temperature lattice results.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum Gribov mass: \( m_G(0) = 0.55 \) GeV, chosen to reproduce the Cornell potential with parameters \(\alpha = 0.4105\) and \(\sigma = 0.2~\text{GeV}^2\).
- Debye screening length \( r_D \) decreases from infinity in vacuum to about 0.8, 0.6, and 0.4 fm at temperatures \( T = 0.15, 0.25, 0.35 \) GeV respectively.
- The critical temperature is taken as \( T_c = 170 \) MeV.
- At high temperature, the Gribov mass approaches the asymptotic form \( m_G(T) = (N_c / (2^{3/2} 3\pi)) g^2(T) T \).
- The renormalization scale in the gap equation is \(\mu = 10.92\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger approach, previously used to compute the vacuum heavy-quark potential at one-loop level by Golterman (2012), to finite temperature around the deconfinement transition. It contrasts with the HTL approach of Laine (2006) and Beraudo (2007), which is valid only at extremely high temperatures and fails to produce a confining potential at low temperatures. The results enable a perturbative description of quarkonium dissociation that includes both color screening and Landau damping, and can be compared with lattice extractions of the complex potential (e.g., Rothkopf (2011), Bazavov (2014)) and with machine-learning-based extractions (Shi (2021)).
