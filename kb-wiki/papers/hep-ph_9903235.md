# Imaging the Space-Time Evolution of High Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions with Bremsstrahlung

> The bremsstrahlung produced when heavy nuclei collide is estimated for central collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Bremsstrahlung photons with energies below 100 to 200 MeV are sufficient to discern the gross features of the space-time evolution of electric charge, if they can be separated from other sources of photons experimentally. This is illustrated explicitly by considering two very different models, one Bjorken-like, the other Landau-like, both of which are constructed to give the same final charge rapidity distribution.

## Key claim

Bremsstrahlung photons with energies up to 100–200 MeV can distinguish between different space-time evolution scenarios of electric charge in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC, even when the final charge rapidity distributions are identical; the ability to do so depends on experimental separation of these photons from other sources.

## Method

The authors computed classical bremsstrahlung from accelerated nuclear charges in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (100 GeV per nucleon in the center-of-mass frame) using a continuous charge distribution model. They constructed two extreme parameterizations of the space-time evolution, a Bjorken-like model with constant deceleration and a Landau-like model with instantaneous stopping followed by uniform acceleration, both tuned to produce the same flat final rapidity distribution of net electric charge. The bremsstrahlung intensity as a function of photon energy and emission angle was then evaluated numerically for each model.

## Key numbers

- RHIC beam energy: 100 GeV per nucleon, corresponding to Lorentz γ = 106 and a contracted gold nucleus diameter of 0.13 fm.
- Photon energy range considered: up to 100–200 MeV in the lab (center-of-mass) frame.
- For very soft photons (ω << 1/R = 30 MeV), the intensity peaks at θ ∼ 1° for central gold collisions at RHIC.
- In the Landau-like model with tf = 20 fm/c, at θ = 10° and ω = 60 MeV, the intensity shows an order-of-magnitude enhancement due to constructive interference compared to the soft photon limit.
- The Bjorken-like model used t0 = 0.5 fm/c and c0 values of 0.5, 1, and ∞, giving central core deceleration times of 27.3, 13.9, and 0.5 fm/c respectively.
- The Landau-like model used tf values of 1, 2, 10, and 20 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nucleus-nucleus bremsstrahlung by Kapusta (1993), Bjorken and McLerran (1980s), and Dumitru, McLerran and Stoecker (1990s), as well as a recent study by Jeon, Kapusta, Chikanian and Sandweiss (1999) that focused on photons below 5 MeV. The present paper shows that higher-energy bremsstrahlung can probe the time structure of the collision, not just the final charge distribution, which hadronic measurements alone cannot distinguish. The results suggest that experimental detection of bremsstrahlung photons up to 200 MeV could provide information on the deceleration or expansion time scales, motivating detector design efforts.
