# Fragmentation in Jets at NNLO

> Beam and jet functions in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory describe collinear initial- and final-state radiation (jets), and enter in factorization theorems for N-jet production, the Higgs pT spectrum, etc. We show that they may directly be calculated as phase-space integrals of QCD splitting functions. At NLO all computations are trivial, as we demonstrate explicitly for the beam function, the transverse-momentum-dependent beam function, the jet function and the fragmenting jet function. This approach also highlights the role of crossing symmetry in these calculations. At NNLO we reproduce the quark jet function and calculate the fragmenting quark jet function for the first time. Here we use two methods: a direct phase-space integration and a reduction to master integrals which are computed using differential equations.

## Key claim

The authors show that beam and jet functions in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) can be directly calculated as phase-space integrals of QCD splitting functions, and they present the first calculation of the fragmenting quark jet function at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), though the results are given in terms of plus-distributions and harmonic polylogarithms with the implicit condition that the calculation is performed in dimensional regularization.

## Method

The authors compute the relevant phase-space integrals of QCD splitting functions in dimensional regularization, using two complementary methods: a direct phase-space integration and a reduction to master integrals which are solved using differential equations. They demonstrate the approach at NLO for the beam function, the transverse-momentum-dependent beam function, the jet function, and the fragmenting jet function, then extend the calculation to NNLO for the quark jet function and the fragmenting quark jet function.

## Key numbers

- The quark jet function at NNLO is given to order epsilon, with the color-factor C_F contribution containing terms such as -16/epsilon^3 - 24/epsilon^2 + (1/epsilon)(-65 + 32 pi^2/3) at the finite part level.
- The fragmenting quark jet function at NNLO is presented for the first time, with results expressed in terms of plus-distributions L_n(s'/mu^2) and harmonic polylogarithms H_w(z).
- The NNLO beam function results include contributions from color structures C_F, C_A, and T_F n_f, with explicit expressions containing plus-distributions up to L_3(s/mu^2) and harmonic polylogarithms up to weight 3.
- The transverse-momentum-dependent beam function results at NNLO include terms with harmonic polylogarithms involving indices -1, 0, 1, 2, and 3.

## Context

This work extends the approach of calculating jet functions via splitting functions, previously applied at NLO, to NNLO for the fragmenting jet function for the first time. The method provides a unified framework for computing beam functions, jet functions, and fragmenting jet functions that appear in factorization theorems for N-jet production and the Higgs pT spectrum (as discussed in earlier SCET literature by Stewart, Tackmann, and Waalewijn). The results enable precision predictions for fragmentation processes in jets at NNLO, and the explicit expressions can be used in phenomenological studies of jet substructure and fragmentation functions.
