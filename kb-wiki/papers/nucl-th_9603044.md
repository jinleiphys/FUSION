# Reply to Comment of Gazdzicki and Heinz on Strangeness Enhancement in $p+A$ and $S+A$

> The Comment of Gazdzicki and Heinz is flawed because their assumed baryon stopping power in $pA$ is inconsistent with data and because they ignored half the analysis based on the VENUS model. The Comment continues the misleading presentation of strangeness enhancement by focusing on ratios of integrated yields. Those ratios discard essential experimental information on the rapidity dependence of produced $Λ$ and obscure discrepancies between different data sets. Our conclusion remains that the NA35 minimum bias data on $p+S\rightarrowΛ+X $ indicate an anomalous enhancement of central rapidity strangeness in few nucleon reactions that points to non-equilibrium dynamics as responsible for strangeness enhancement in nuclear reactions.

# Reply to Comment of Gazdzicki and Heinz on Strangeness Enhancement in p+A and S+A

## Key claim

The authors maintain that the NA35 minimum bias data on p+S → Λ + X show an anomalous enhancement of central rapidity strangeness in few-nucleon reactions, but they hedge this claim by noting that if future data converge toward the lower NA5 and NA36 ranges, then conventional hadron cascade models could account for most of the strangeness production and their conclusions would change.

## Method

The authors used two microscopic models, HIJING and VENUS, to quantify differences between p+p, p+A, and A+A reactions, focusing on the rapidity distribution of Λ particles reported by NA35 for minimum bias p+S reactions. They compared model predictions to existing data on p+A → p+X systematics from Busza and to earlier data from NA5 on p+Ar and preliminary NA36 data on p+Pb.

## Key numbers

- The central (y ≈ 3) rapidity density of Λ's in p+S was reported as 0.06 ± 0.01, which is four times the value of 0.016 ± 0.0005 in p+p.
- The cross section for selected p+S events (n_ch > 5) was 470 mb, corresponding to a minimum bias trigger with impact parameters less than 5 fm.
- The mean number of interacting S nucleons is ν ≈ 2.2 for this trigger.
- The average baryon rapidity shift in p+A reactions grows as Δy_B ≈ 1 + (ν − 1)/3, giving only half a unit greater shift in p+S than in p+p.
- The NA5 p+Ar → Λ+X central density is a factor of two lower than found in p+S by NA35.

## Context

This reply extends the authors' earlier analysis (Gyulassy, Topor Pop, Wang, 1995) and directly contradicts the Comment of Gazdzicki and Heinz, who argued that there is no strangeness enhancement in p+S reactions and that the authors' analysis should be dismissed because HIJING underestimates baryon stopping power. The work builds on NA35 data (1994) and earlier systematics from Busza (1984) on p+A → p+X, as well as NA5 data on p+Ar and p+Xe (Brick et al., 1988). The authors argue that ratios of integrated yields, as advocated by Gazdzicki and Heinz and by Rafelski, discard essential differential information and obscure discrepancies between data sets. Resolving these discrepancies will require additional pA experiments to understand whether non-equilibrium dynamics or conventional cascade models explain strangeness enhancement.
