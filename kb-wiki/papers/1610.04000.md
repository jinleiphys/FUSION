# Inversion doublets of reflection-asymmetric clustering in 28Si and their isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions

> [Background] Various cluster states of astrophysical interest are expected to exist in the excited states of $^{28}{\rm Si}$. However, they have not been identified firmly, because of the experimental and theoretical difficulties. [Purpose] To establish the $^{24}$Mg+$α$, $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C and $^{20}$Ne+2$α$ cluster bands, we theoretically search for the negative-parity cluster bands that are paired with the positive-parity bands to constitute the inversion doublets. We also offer the isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions as a promising probe for the clustering. We numerically show that these transition strengths from the ground state to the cluster states are very enhanced. [Method] The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with Gogny D1S effective interaction is employed to calculate the excited states of $^{28}{\rm Si}$. The isoscalar monopole and dipole transition strengths are directly evaluated from wave functions of the ground and excited states. [Results] Negative-parity bands having $^{24}$Mg+$α$ and $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C cluster configurations are obtained in addition to the newly calculated $^{20}$Ne+2$α$ cluster bands. All of them are paired with the corresponding positive-parity bands to constitute the inversion doublets with various cluster configurations. The calculation show that the band-head of the $^{24}$Mg+$α$ and $^{20}$Ne+2$α$ cluster bands are strongly excited by the isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions. [Conclusions] The present calculation suggests the existence of the inversion doublets with the $^{24}$Mg+$α$, $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C and $^{20}$Ne+2$α$ configurations.Because of the enhanced transition strengths, we offer the isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions as good probe for the $^{24}$Mg+$α$ and $^{20}$Ne+2$α$ cluster bands.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the ground state of ²⁸Si has a duality of oblate deformed mean-field and cluster configurations (²⁴Mg+α and ²⁰Ne+⁸Be), and that because of this duality, the isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions from the ground state to the inversion doublet cluster states are as strong as single-particle estimates, despite the strong influence of the spin-orbit interaction which causes the ground state to deviate from a pure SU(3) shell model state.

## Method

The authors employed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective interaction to calculate excited states of ²⁸Si. They used two constraint potentials: a βγ-constraint on quadrupole deformation to obtain mean-field configurations, and a d-constraint on the distance between quasi clusters to obtain ²⁴Mg+α, ¹⁶O+¹²C, and ²⁰Ne+⁸Be cluster configurations. After angular momentum projection, they performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations to obtain the eigenstates and directly evaluated isoscalar monopole and dipole transition strengths from the wave functions of the ground and excited states.

## Key numbers

- The oblate deformed ground state minimum has energy E = -235.7 MeV at (β,γ) = (0.36, 46°)
- The overlap between the ²⁴Mg+α (T) configuration and the oblate deformed ground state minimum is 0.96 at inter-cluster distance d = 2.0 fm
- The negative-parity Jπ=1⁻ state with the ²⁴Mg+α (T) configuration appears approximately 15 MeV above the ground state
- The ²⁰Ne+⁸Be negative-parity Jπ=1⁻ state appears approximately 17 MeV above the ground state with inter-cluster distance d = 3.0 fm
- The ¹⁶O+¹²C configuration with Jπ=0⁺ appears approximately 10 MeV above the ground state with inter-cluster distance d = 2.5 fm, and has overlap 0.90 with the prolate minimum at d = 2.5 fm
- The ²⁴Mg+α (A) configuration with Jπ=0⁺ has overlap 0.42 with the superdeformed configuration at inter-cluster distance d = 4.5 fm
- The calculated B(E2;2⁺→0⁺) for the ground-state band is 79.4 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 67 e²fm⁴)
- The calculated B(E2;4⁺→2⁺) for the ground-state band is 123 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 83 e²fm⁴)

## Context

This work extends the previous AMD study by Taniguchi (2009) which investigated positive-parity bands in ²⁸Si and found three bands with large overlap with cluster configurations. The present study extends the survey to negative-parity states to identify the inversion doublets that must accompany reflection-asymmetric cluster configurations, as originally proposed by Horiuchi (1968). The authors also build on analytical work by Yamada (2008) and Chiba (2016) showing that isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions from a pure SU(3) shell model ground state to ideal cluster states are as strong as single-particle estimates, and provide the first quantitative numerical calculation using a realistic nuclear model. This work enables experimental identification of cluster states in ²⁸Si through isoscalar monopole and dipole transition measurements, which could help establish the clustering systematics in mid sd-shell nuclei and inform astrophysical processes such as helium and carbon burning.
