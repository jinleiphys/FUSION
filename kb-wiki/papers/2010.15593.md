# Universal scaling properties of QCD close to the chiral limit

> We present a lattice QCD based determination of the chiral phase transition temperature in QCD with two massless (up and down) and one strange quark having its physical mass. We propose and calculate two novel estimators for the chiral transition temperature for several values of the light quark masses, corresponding to Goldstone pion masses in the range of $58~{\rm MeV}\lesssim m_π\lesssim 163~{\rm MeV}$. The chiral phase transition temperature is determined by extrapolating to vanishing pion mass using universal scaling relations. After thermodynamic, continuum and chiral extrapolations we find the chiral phase transition temperature $T_c^0=132^{+3}_{-6}$ MeV. We also show some preliminary calculations that use the conventional estimator for the pseudo-critical temperature and compare with the new estimators for $T_c^0$. Furthermore, we show results for the ratio of the chiral order parameter and its susceptibility and argue that this ratio can be used to differentiate between $O(N)$ and $Z_2$ universality classes in a non-parametric manner.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition temperature in QCD with two massless quarks and a physical strange quark is determined to be \(T_c^0 = 132^{+3}_{-6}\) MeV, based on novel estimators that are reliable at finite quark masses and extrapolated using universal scaling relations; the result is about 25 MeV lower than the pseudo-critical crossover temperature at physical quark masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate light quarks and a physical strange quark, using the HISQ action on lattices with temporal extents \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12\) and spatial extents up to \(N_\sigma = 56\). They defined two novel estimators for the chiral transition temperature: \(T_\delta\) from the condition \(H\chi_M/M = 1/\delta\) and \(T_{60}\) from \(\chi_M = 0.6\,\chi_M^{\text{max}}\), which are close to the scaling variable \(z=0\) and thus require smaller chiral extrapolations. The chiral condensate and susceptibility were computed for light quark masses corresponding to pion masses \(58\ \text{MeV} \lesssim m_\pi \lesssim 163\ \text{MeV}\), and the results were extrapolated to the thermodynamic limit, continuum limit, and chiral limit using \(O(4)\) universal scaling functions.

## Key numbers

- Chiral phase transition temperature: \(T_c^0 = 132^{+3}_{-6}\) MeV after thermodynamic, continuum, and chiral extrapolations.
- Pion mass range studied: \(58\ \text{MeV} \lesssim m_\pi \lesssim 163\ \text{MeV}\).
- The ratio \(M/\chi_M\) at \(T_p\) and \(T_{60}\) was compared to \(O(N)\) and \(Z_2\) scaling expectations; the data disfavor a \(Z_2\) endpoint down to \(H_c = 1/240\), corresponding to \(m_\pi \sim 45\) MeV.
- The chiral phase transition temperature is about 25 MeV smaller than the pseudo-critical crossover temperature at physical quark masses.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD determinations of the chiral crossover temperature (Aoki 2009, Bazavov 2011, Bonati 2015, Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2020) by providing the first direct determination of the chiral phase transition temperature in the massless limit using novel estimators. The results support the \(O(4)\) universality class scenario for the chiral transition and disfavor a first-order transition or a \(Z_2\) endpoint down to pion masses of about 45 MeV, consistent with the classic argument of Pisarski and Wilczek (1983). The new estimators enable more reliable chiral extrapolations and can be used to differentiate between universality classes in future studies.
