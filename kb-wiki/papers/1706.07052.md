# Effect of collisions on neutrino flavor inhomogeneity in a dense neutrino gas

> We investigate the stability, with respect to spatial inhomogeneity, of a two-dimensional dense neutrino gas. The system exhibits growth of seed inhomogeneity due to nonlinear coherent neutrino self-interactions. In the absence of incoherent collisional effects, we observe a dependence of this instability growth rate on the neutrino mass spectrum: the normal neutrino mass hierarchy exhibits spatial instability over a larger range of neutrino number density compared to that of the inverted case. We further consider the effect of elastic incoherent collisions of the neutrinos with a static background of heavy, nucleon-like scatterers. At small scales, the growth of flavor instability can be suppressed by collisions. At large length scales we find, perhaps surprisingly, that for inverted neutrino mass hierarchy incoherent collisions fail to suppress flavor instabilities, independent of the coupling strength.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a simplified two-dimensional, two-flavor dense neutrino gas, the assumption of spatial homogeneity is not sustained due to nonlinear neutrino self-interactions, and that for the inverted neutrino mass hierarchy, elastic incoherent collisions with a static background of nucleon-like scatterers fail to suppress flavor instabilities at large length scales, independent of the coupling strength.

## Method

The authors performed a linear stability analysis of the quantum kinetic equations for a two-dimensional, two-flavor neutrino gas with mono-energetic neutrinos, decomposing the density matrices into Fourier modes and seeking solutions of the form exp(-iΩt) to identify exponentially growing modes. They included elastic, angle-dependent incoherent collisions with a static background of heavy nucleon-like scatterers, using a collision term proportional to the identity matrix in flavor space with constants c₁ and c₂ fixed at ratio c₁/c₂ = 4.83. The stability was determined by solving a 6×6 matrix equation for the collective mode frequencies Ω_k, with instability indicated by Im(Ω_k) > 0.

## Key numbers

- The ratio c₁/c₂ for the collision term is fixed at 4.83, derived from equal numbers of protons and neutrons in the early universe.
- For the collisionless system with normal hierarchy, the homogeneous mode (k=0) shows instability over a larger range of μ/ω compared to the inverted hierarchy case.
- For small-scale inhomogeneity with |k| = 1000 ω and α = 0.99, the growth rate κ/ω is shown as a function of μ/ω for collision strengths c₁ = 0, c₁ = 1.0 ω, and c₁ = 10.0 ω.
- The homogeneous mode (k=0) solutions for the a-block are given by Ω_(0,±)^(a) = πμ(1-α) ± √(ω² + 2πμ(1+α)ω + π²μ²(1-α)²).
- The homogeneous mode solutions for the s and c blocks are Ω_(0,±)^(s,c) = (5/2)πμ(1-α) - iπ(2c₁+c₂) ± √(ω² - πμ(1+α)ω + (1/4)π²μ²(1-α)²).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spatial instabilities in anisotropic astrophysical environments (Duan 2014, Abbar 2015, Mirizzi 2015, Chakraborty 2015) to the early universe context, where previous studies (Dolgov 2002, Mangano 2005) assumed homogeneity and isotropy without examination. The finding that collisions fail to suppress instabilities for the inverted hierarchy at large scales challenges the assumption that incoherent scattering always damps flavor instabilities, and the authors note that detailed numerical simulations are needed to determine whether transient inhomogeneity could affect Big Bang nucleosynthesis, with such a code under development (Grohs 2015, 2016).
