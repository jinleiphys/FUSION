# Leading Chiral Contributions to the Spin Structure of the Proton

> The leading chiral contributions to the quark and gluon components of the proton spin are calculated using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. Similar calculations are done for the moments of the generalized parton distributions relevant to the quark and gluon angular momentum densities. These results provide useful insight about the role of pions in the spin structure of the nucleon, and can serve as a guidance for extrapolating lattice QCD calculations at large quark masses to the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The leading chiral (pionic) contributions to the quark and gluon components of the proton spin, \(J_{q,g}\), are calculated using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the total nonanalytic contribution to the proton spin must vanish because the total proton spin is independent of the pion mass. The results depend on unknown low-energy constants such as the chiral-limit values \(b_{(q,g)N}\) and \(b_{(q,g)\Delta}\), and the non-analytic corrections may be largely cancelled if \(J_{q}^{0} \approx \langle x \rangle_{q/\pi}/2\).

## Method

The authors matched the quark and gluon energy-momentum tensor operators onto hadronic operators (pionic, single-nucleon, and delta-resonance operators) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT). They computed the one-loop Feynman diagrams (pion loops with nucleon and delta intermediate states) that yield the leading nonanalytic chiral logarithms of the form \(m_\pi^2 \ln m_\pi^2\) to the form factors \(A_{q,g}(0,\mu^2)+B_{q,g}(0,\mu^2)=2J_{q,g}\). The calculation was extended to the moments of generalized parton distributions for \(n=4,6,\dots\).

## Key numbers

* The experimentally extracted quark spin contribution to the proton spin is \(\Delta \Sigma(\mu^2\sim 5\ {\rm GeV}^2) = 0.16 \pm 0.08\).
* The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 93\ {\rm MeV}\).
* The momentum fraction of the pion carried by quarks at \(\mu^2\sim 1\ {\rm GeV}^2\) is \(\langle x_q\rangle_\pi \simeq 0.5\).
* The delta-nucleon mass splitting is \(\Delta = M_\Delta - M_N \sim 300\ {\rm MeV}\).
* The leading chiral logarithm contribution to \(J_{q,g}\) contains the factor \(\frac{g_A^2 m_\pi^2}{(4\pi f_\pi)^2} \ln\left(\frac{m_\pi^2}{\Lambda_\chi^2}\right)\).
* The delta contribution involves the function \(K(m_\pi,\Delta)\) which includes terms like \((m_\pi^2 - 2\Delta^2)\log(m_\pi^2/\Lambda_\chi^2)\) and a term with \(2\Delta\sqrt{\Delta^2 - m_\pi^2}\log(\dots)\).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the proton spin puzzle following the EMC measurement (Ashman et al., 1988) which showed that only a small fraction of the proton spin comes from quark spins, contradicting the SU(6) quark model prediction (Close, 1979). The calculation provides a systematic chiral perturbation theory framework for the quark and gluon angular momentum contributions \(J_q\) and \(J_g\) defined via the Ji sum rule (Ji, 1997). The results are intended to guide extrapolations of lattice QCD calculations (Mathur et al., 2000; Ji and Balitsky, 1997) performed at large quark masses (pion masses above 500 MeV) down to the physical pion mass, and they enable future comparisons with experimental data from deeply virtual Compton scattering and exclusive meson production.
