# Open-Flavour Mesons from the Angle of Bethe, Dyson, Salpeter, Schwinger, et al

> Recently, we completed a comprehensive investigation of a huge part of the entire meson spectrum by considering both quarkonia and open-flavour mesons by means of a single common framework which unites the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation that describes mesons as quark-antiquark bound states and the Dyson-Schwinger equation that governs the full quark propagator: Adopting two (as a matter of fact, not extremely diverse) models that attempt to grasp all principal aspects of the effective strong interactions entering identically in both these equations, we derived within this unique setup, for all mesons analysed, their masses and leptonic decay constants as well as, for the pseudoscalar ones among these mesons, their in-hadron condensates. Here, as a kind of promotion or teaser, we give but a few examples of the resulting collections of data, laying the main emphasis on the dependence of our insights on the effective-interaction model underlying all such outcomes.

## Key claim

The authors present a unified Poincare-covariant treatment of both quarkonia and open-flavour mesons using the same Bethe-Salpeter / Dyson-Schwinger framework, but the results show an unexpectedly large model dependence between two similar effective-interaction models, manifesting even in the number of accessible bound states rather than only in numerical meson properties.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states together with the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow-ladder truncation, using two different ansatze for the effective strong interaction kernel G(k^2). They compute masses, leptonic decay constants, and in-hadron condensates for mesons spanning quark combinations from a fictitious massless quark (chi) up to the bottom quark, covering ground states and lowest radial excitations across multiple J^{P(C)} channels.

## Key numbers

* Strange meson masses M_{q s} and decay constants f_{q s} are computed using both interaction models, with one model (Ref. EI) producing an additional 0^+ excitation not present in the other model (Ref. EIa).
* Charmed-strange meson masses M_{s c} and decay constants f_{s c} show a 1^+ state present in one model (Ref. EIa) but absent in the other (Ref. EI).
* Charmonium masses M_{c c} and decay constants f_{c c} from the Ref. EI model reveal "many more ground and excited states" compared to the Ref. EIa model.
* In-hadron condensates (generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation) are presented numerically for pseudoscalar mesons under both interaction models.

## Context

This work extends prior Poincare-covariant meson studies (e.g., the authors' own earlier work in GRHKL and WL@) by treating quarkonia and open-flavour mesons within a single identical framework, whereas previous studies typically treated them separately. The results enable systematic uncertainty estimates from model dependence and provide comprehensive tables of predictions (available in Ref. GRHKL) for comparison with experiment (confronted with PDG data). The in-hadron condensate analysis generalizes the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation following the approach of MRT.
