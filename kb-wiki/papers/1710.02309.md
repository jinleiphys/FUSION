# Heavy flavour in high-energy nuclear collisions: overview of transport calculations

> Transport calculations are the tool to study medium modifications of heavy-flavour particle distributions in high-energy nuclear collisions. We give a brief overview on their state-of-the art as well as on the questions remaining open, from the evaluation of the transport coefficients to the effects of in-medium hadronization, from the rescattering in the hadronic phase to the possible presence of hot-medium effects also in small systems, like proton-nucleus collisions.

# Heavy flavour in high-energy nuclear collisions: overview of transport calculations

## Key claim
Transport calculations are the essential tool for studying medium modifications of heavy-flavour particle distributions, but the theoretical uncertainties in the transport coefficients and in the modeling of in-medium hadronization prevent unambiguous extraction of information about the partonic stage from final hadronic observables.

## Method
The paper reviews the multi-step setup for heavy-flavour transport calculations: initial heavy-quark pair production from QCD event generators, background medium evolution from hydrodynamic calculations, heavy-quark propagation via relativistic Langevin equations with momentum-diffusion coefficients, hadronization through recombination with thermal partons from the medium, and possible rescattering in the hadronic phase. The review compares results from weak-coupling calculations with Hard-Thermal-Loop resummation and lattice-QCD simulations for the transport coefficients, and examines model predictions for nuclear modification factors and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers
- Momentum-diffusion coefficient κ from weak-coupling NLO calculation receives large corrections for realistic αs values, arising from overlapping scattering processes with light partons (no explicit numerical value given for the correction magnitude)
- Lattice-QCD results for κ in a gluon plasma (Francis 2015) are affected by large systematic theoretical uncertainties from extracting real-time information from Euclidean spacetime simulations
- Spatial diffusion coefficient Ds = 2T²/κ is used to quantify coupling strength across models; a collection of Ds values from different theoretical frameworks is shown (Prino 2016)
- Transport calculations including in-medium hadronization produce a bump at moderate pT in the D-meson RAA and enhancement of v2, moving predictions closer to experimental data from Adamczyk (2014) and Abelev (2013)
- Transport coefficients of D/B-mesons in a hadron gas from effective chiral Lagrangians (Ozvenchuk 2014) join smoothly to partonic-phase results as a function of temperature
- Rescattering in the hadronic phase has a small effect on final observables, shown for elliptic flow of electrons from semi-leptonic heavy-flavour decays (Adare 2006)
- In small systems (d-Au at RHIC, p-Pb at LHC), transport model results with different coefficients (Beraudo 2015) show deviations from unity from nuclear PDFs, kT-broadening, transport, and in-medium hadronization, but large experimental uncertainties (Adare 2012, Abelev 2014) leave room for medium effects

## Context
This review extends the line of work on heavy-flavour transport in heavy-ion collisions, comparing weak-coupling calculations (CaronHuot 2008) and lattice-QCD results (Francis 2015) for transport coefficients, and discussing models for in-medium hadronization via recombination (Greco 2003, Gossiaux 2009, Beraudo 2014) and resonant-state formation (He 2014, Oh 2009). It enables future work by identifying open questions: the need for first-principle transport coefficients at experimentally relevant conditions, the theoretical uncertainties in modeling hadronization, and the possible extension of transport setups to small systems like high-multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions where collective effects are observed but jet-quenching evidence is lacking.
