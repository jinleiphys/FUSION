# Ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays: Anomalies, QCD, and LHC data

> Measurements of proton and nuclear collisions at the Large Hadron Collider at nucleon-nucleon c.m. energies up to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 13 TeV, have improved our understanding of hadronic interactions at the highest energies reached in collisions of cosmic rays with nuclei in the earth atmosphere, up to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 450$ TeV. The Monte Carlo event generators (EPOS, QGSJET, and SIBYLL) commonly used to describe the air showers generated by ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays (UHECR, with $E_{CR}\approx 10^{17}$--$10^{20}$ eV) feature now, after parameter retuning based on LHC Run-I data, more consistent predictions on the nature of the cosmic rays at the tail of the measured spectrum. However, anomalies persist in the data that cannot be accommodated by the models. Among others, the total number of muons (as well as their maximum production depth) remains significantly underestimated (overestimated) by all models. Comparisons of EPOS, QGSJET, and SIBYLL predictions to the latest LHC data, and to collider MC generators such as PYTHIA, indicate that improved description of hard multiple minijet production and nuclear effects may help reduce part of the data--model discrepancies, shed light on the UHECR composition approaching the observed $E_{CR}\approx 10^{20}$ eV cutoff, and uncover any potential new physics responsible of the observed anomalies.

## Key claim

After retuning Monte Carlo event generators (EPOS, QGSJET, and SIBYLL) based on LHC Run-I data, predictions for the nature of ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays at the highest energies have become more consistent, but anomalies persist that cannot be accommodated by the models, specifically the total number of muons remains significantly underestimated and their maximum production depth overestimated by all models.

## Method

The paper compares predictions from the Reggeon Field Theory based Monte Carlo generators EPOS-LHC, QGSJET-II, and SIBYLL 2.3c to recent LHC measurements of basic QCD observables in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at center-of-mass energies up to 13 TeV. It also interfaces the CONEX air transport program with the PYTHIA 6 collider Monte Carlo to study the impact of hard QCD jets and heavy-quark production on muon yields in extensive air showers, using a hydrogen atmosphere with the same density as air.

## Key numbers

- TOTEM measures total p-p cross section at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV as 110.6 +/- 3.4 mb, with approximately 72% inelastic and 28% elastic components
- Charged-particle pseudorapidity density at midrapidity in p-p collisions at 13 TeV is approximately 5.5
- At the highest cosmic-ray energies of 10^20 eV, different Monte Carlo predictions for charged-particle pseudorapidity density vary within approximately 10 to 25
- The observed number of muons in ultrahigh-energy cosmic ray showers is underestimated by approximately 30% by all models
- Switching off heavy-quark production in PYTHIA 6 leads to a 15% muon increase on the ground
- PYTHIA 6 muon yields are 10 to 30% larger at 600 to 1000 meters from the shower axis compared to other models
- EPOS-LHC, QGSJET-II, and QGSJET-01 feature approximately 25% increase in inelasticity between 10^14 eV and 10^20 eV

## Context

This work extends the comparisons of hadronic interaction models to LHC data previously reviewed in d'Enterria (2011) and d'Enterria (2016), finding that pre-LHC models overall bracketed experimental measurements and only small modifications were required for the new QGSJET-II, EPOS-LHC, and SIBYLL 2.3c releases. The study enables future work on improved description of hard multiple minijet production and nuclear effects to reduce data-model discrepancies, potentially shedding light on the ultrahigh-energy cosmic ray composition approaching the observed cutoff at approximately 10^20 eV and uncovering any potential new physics responsible for the observed anomalies.
