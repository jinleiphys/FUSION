# Comparison of the heavy-fermion and Foldy-Wouthuysen formalisms at third order

> We compare two non-relativistic (NR) reduction schemes (heavy-fermion and Foldy-Wouthuysen) that are used to derive low-energy effective-field-theory Lagrangians. We give the explicit transformation between the two types of fields to O(1/m^2), derived from a quite general, relativistic Lagrangian. Beyond leading order the NR reductions always involve the smaller components of the Dirac spinors that are to be integrated out to formulate the NR theory. Even so, the transformation between the NR Lagrangians can be carried out explicitly to O(1/m^2) using a field renormalization, as long as the lower components of the Lagrangian are known. The fixed coefficient corrections to some low-energy constants at O(1/m^2) will depend on the particular scheme chosen, but will match after the field renormalization.

## Key claim

The heavy-fermion (HF) and Foldy-Wouthuysen (FW) non-relativistic reduction schemes produce Lagrangians that differ at order \(1/m^2\), not at order \(1/m\) as previously assumed, but these differences can be absorbed by a field renormalization provided the lower components of the original relativistic Lagrangian are known.

## Method

The authors start from a general relativistic Lagrangian of the form \(\mathcal{L} = \bar\psi(i\not{\mathcal{D}}-m+\gamma_0 G)\psi\) and apply both the heavy-fermion method (splitting the fermion field into velocity-dependent large and small components, then integrating out the small components) and the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation (a sequence of unitary transformations that decouples upper and lower Dirac components) to order \(1/m^2\). They derive the explicit field transformation matrix \(M^{(i)}\) relating the HF fields \((H, h')\) to the FW fields \((N, n)\) and show how the upper-component Lagrangians transform into each other via \(U_{HH} = a^\dagger U_{NN} a\) at this order.

## Key numbers

* The explicit transformation between HF and FW fields is given to \(\mathcal{O}(1/m^2)\), with the leading difference between the two Lagrangians appearing first at \(\mathcal{O}(1/m^2)\) (the second line of Eq. (FW2)).
* The \(g_A^3/m^2\) corrections to the low-energy constants \(\hat{d}_{12}\) and \(\hat{d}_{13}\) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory differ between the HF and FW formalisms.
* The free FW propagator is \(\frac{i}{i\partial_0 - (\sqrt{m^2-\nabla^2}-m)} = \frac{i}{i\partial_0 + \frac{\nabla^2}{2m} + \frac{\nabla^4}{8m^3} + \ldots}\), while the HF propagator for an on-shell nucleon after emitting a pion of four-momentum \(q\) is \(\frac{i(1+(l_0-q_0)/2m)}{-q_0 - (l\cdot q)/m + q^2/(2m)}\).
* The field renormalization factor relating HF and FW upper components at \(\mathcal{O}(1/m^2)\) is \(1 + \frac{1}{8m^2}\tilde{B}\tilde{C}\).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of HF and FW methods beyond the \(\mathcal{O}(1/m)\) equivalence shown by Mannel (1992) and Korner and Thompson (1991) for heavy-quark effective field theory, and beyond the renormalizable-theory treatment of Das and Mathur (1993) which considered only a constant electric field in QED. The paper generalizes the equivalence proof to non-renormalizable effective field theories such as heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, where low-energy constants parametrize short-distance physics. The results enable practitioners to correctly compare LECs extracted using different non-relativistic reduction schemes, provided the lower-component structure of the original relativistic Lagrangian is known.
