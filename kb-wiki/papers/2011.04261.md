# One-loop Kubo estimations of the shear and bulk viscous coefficients for hot and magnetized Bosonic and Fermionic systems

> The expressions of the shear viscosity and the bulk viscosity components in the presence of an arbitrary external magnetic field for a system of hot charged scalar Bosons (spin-0) as well as for a system of hot charged Dirac Fermions (spin-$\frac{1}{2}$) have been derived by employing the one-loop Kubo Formalism. This is done by explicitly evaluating the thermo-magnetic spectral functions of the energy momentum tensors using the real time formalism of finite temperature field theory and the Schwinger proper time formalism. In the present work, a rich quantum field theoretical structure in the expressions of the viscous coefficients in non-zero magnetic field are found, which are different from their respective expressions obtained earlier via kinetic theory based calculations; though, in absence of magnetic field, the one-loop Kubo and the kinetic theory based expressions for the viscosities are known to be identical. We have identified that Kubo and kinetic theory based results of viscosity components follow similar kind of temperature and magnetic field dependency. The relaxation time and the synchrotron frequency in the kinetic theory formalism are realized to be connected respectively with the thermal width of propagator and the transitions among the Landau levels of the charged particles in the Kubo formalism. We believe that, the connection of latter quantities are quite new and probably the present work is the first time addressing this interpretation along with the new expressions of viscosity components, not seen in existing works.

## Key claim

The paper derives one-loop Kubo expressions for shear and bulk viscosity components in a magnetic field for charged scalar bosons and Dirac fermions, finding that these expressions differ from earlier kinetic-theory-based results in the presence of a magnetic field, although the two frameworks yield identical results when the magnetic field is zero. The authors claim that the relaxation time and synchrotron frequency in kinetic theory correspond respectively to the thermal width of the propagator and transitions among Landau levels in the Kubo formalism, a connection they believe is new.

## Method

The authors used the one-loop Kubo formalism, evaluating the thermo-magnetic spectral functions of the energy-momentum tensor via the real-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory and the Schwinger proper-time formalism to incorporate the external magnetic field. They considered two systems: charged scalar bosons (spin-0) and charged Dirac fermions (spin-1/2), and extracted the shear and bulk viscosity components from the spectral functions using the covariant tensor basis of Huang et al. (2011). The thermal width Gamma was introduced via a Breit-Wigner representation of the Dirac delta functions to obtain non-divergent results in the zero-momentum limit.

## Key numbers

* For scalar bosons at zero magnetic field, the shear viscosity is given by eta_Scalar = (2/(15T)) integral (d^3k/(2pi)^3) (k^4/(omega_k^2 Gamma)) f(omega_k)[1+f(omega_k)], where f is the Bose-Einstein distribution.
* For Dirac fermions at zero magnetic field, the shear viscosity is eta_Dirac = (4/(15T)) integral (d^3k/(2pi)^3) (k^4/(omega_k^2 Gamma)) f_tilde(omega_k)[1-f_tilde(omega_k)], where f_tilde is the Fermi-Dirac distribution.
* For scalar bosons at zero magnetic field, the bulk viscosity is zeta_Scalar = (2/(9T)) integral (d^3k/(2pi)^3) (1/(omega_k^2 Gamma)) [m^2 + (3theta-1)omega_k^2]^2 f(omega_k)[1+f(omega_k)].
* For Dirac fermions at zero magnetic field, the bulk viscosity is zeta_Dirac = (4/(9T)) integral (d^3k/(2pi)^3) (1/(omega_k^2 Gamma)) [m^2 + (3theta-1)omega_k^2]^2 f_tilde(omega_k)[1-f_tilde(omega_k)].
* The kinematic region for the unitary cuts in the scalar case at finite B is sqrt(q_z^2 + 4(m^2 + eB)) < q^0 < infinity, and for the Landau cuts it is |q^0| < sqrt(q_z^2 + (sqrt(m^2+eB) - sqrt(m^2+3eB))^2).
* The kinematic region for the unitary cuts in the Dirac case at finite B is sqrt(q_z^2 + 4m^2) < q^0 < infinity, and for the Landau cuts it is |q^0| < sqrt(q_z^2 + (m - sqrt(m^2+2eB))^2).

## Context

This work extends the one-loop Kubo calculations of shear and bulk viscosity, previously performed at zero magnetic field by Nicola (2009), Ghosh (2014), and others, to the case of an arbitrary external magnetic field. It contrasts with earlier kinetic-theory-based calculations for magnetized systems by Tuchin (2012), Denicol (2018), and others, showing that the Kubo expressions contain additional quantum field theoretical structures not present in the Landau-quantized relaxation-time approximation. The results enable future comparisons between quantum field theoretical and kinetic theory approaches for transport coefficients in strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.
