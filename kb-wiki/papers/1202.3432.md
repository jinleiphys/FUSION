# Lifting the Gribov ambiguity in Yang-Mills theories

> We propose a new one-parameter family of Landau gauges for Yang-Mills theories which can be formulated by means of functional integral methods and are thus well suited for analytic calculations, but which are free of Gribov ambiguities and avoid the Neuberger zero problem of the standard Faddeev-Popov construction. The resulting gauge-fixed theory is perturbatively renormalizable in four dimensions and, for what concerns the calculation of ghost and gauge field correlators, it reduces to a massive extension of the Faddeev-Popov action. We study the renormalization group flow of this theory at one-loop and show that it has no Landau pole in the infrared for some - including physically relevant - range of values of the renormalized parameters.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new one-parameter family of Landau gauges for Yang-Mills theories that avoids both the Gribov ambiguity and the Neuberger zero problem, but the claim is conditional: the resulting gauge-fixed theory reduces to a massive extension of the Faddeev-Popov action only for the calculation of ghost and gauge field correlators, and the absence of a Landau pole in the infrared holds only for some range of renormalized parameters, including physically relevant ones.

## Method

The authors implement a two-step averaging procedure: first averaging over all Gribov copies of each gauge field configuration with a weight factor proportional to exp(-β₀∫ tr(A_μ)²), then averaging over Yang-Mills field configurations with the usual Yang-Mills action. They employ a replica trick borrowed from disordered systems to formulate this procedure as a functional integral, and show that the resulting action describes a collection of gauged supersymmetric nonlinear sigma models. The perturbative renormalizability in four dimensions is proven using Slavnov-Taylor identities derived from the BRST and nonlinear symmetries of the theory.

## Key numbers

- The gluon propagator acquires a bare square mass m₀² = nβ₀, where n is the number of replicas and β₀ is the free parameter of the gauge-fixing procedure
- The ghost propagator retains the usual form δ^{ab}/p²
- The gluon propagator is transverse in momentum and given by δ^{ab}/(p² + nβ₀) × (δ_{μν} - p_μ p_ν/p²)
- The superfield propagator for Λ_k is δ^{ab}δ_{kl}δ(θ̅-θ̅')(θ-θ')/p²

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger approach (Gribov 1977, Zwanziger 1989) and the refined Gribov-Zwanziger framework (Dudal 2008) by providing a first-principles field-theoretical justification for the massive extension of the Faddeev-Popov action, specifically the Curci-Ferrari model (Curci 1976). The proposed gauge-fixing interpolates between the Faddeev-Popov gauge (β₀→0) and the absolute Landau gauge (β₀→∞), and the one-loop calculations in this model were shown in prior work (Tissier 2010, Tissier 2011) to reproduce lattice results for gluon and ghost propagators. This enables analytic calculations of infrared Yang-Mills correlation functions without the Landau pole problem that plagues standard perturbation theory.
