# Reanalyses for $^{42-51}$Ca scattering on a $^{12}$C target at $280$ MeV/nucleon based on chiral $g$ folding mode with Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov densities (published in Results in Physics)

> In the previous paper, we predicted reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}$ for $^{40-60,62,64}$Ca+$^{12}$C scattering at $280$~MeV/nucleon, since Tanaka {\it el al.} measured interaction cross sections $σ_{\rm I}$ for $^{42-51}$Ca in RIKEN and determined neutron skin $r_{\rm skin}({\rm RIKEN})$ using the optical limit of the Glauber model with the Woos-Saxon densities. Our purpose is to reanalyze the $r_{\rm skin}$ from the $σ_{\rm I}$. Our analysis is superior to theirs, since the chiral $g$-matrix folding model (the GHFB and GHFB+AMP densities) is much better than the optical limit of the Glauber model (the Woos-Saxon densities). Our model is the chiral $g$-matrix folding model with the densities scaled from the GHFB and GHFB+AMP densities. We scale the GHFB and GHFB+AMP densities so that the $σ_{\rm R}$ of the scaled densities can agree with the central values of $σ_{\rm I}$ under the condition that the proton radius of the scaled proton density equals the data determined from the isotope shift based on the electron scattering. The $r_{\rm skin}$ thus determined are close to their results $r_{\rm skin}^{42-51}({\rm RIKEN})$. For $^{48}$Ca, our value $r_{\rm skin}^{48}$ is 0.105 $\pm$ 0.06~fm, while their value is $r_{\rm m}^{48}({\rm RIKEN})=0.146 \pm 0.06$~fm. We take the weighted mean and its error of $r_{\rm skin}^{48}(σ_{\rm I})= 0.105 \pm 0.06$~fm and $r_{\rm skin}^{48}(E1{\rm pE}) =0.17 \pm 0.03$~fm of the high-resolution $E1$ polarizability experiment (E1{\rm pE}). Our final result is $r_{\rm skin}^{48}=0.157 \pm 0.027$~fm. Our conclusion is $r_{\rm skin}^{48}=0.157 \pm 0.027$~fm for $^{48}$Ca. For $^{42-47,49-51}$Ca, our results on $r_{\rm skin}$ are similar to theirs. Our result for $^{48}$Ca is related to CREX.

## Key claim

The chiral g-matrix folding model with Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (GHFB) densities provides a superior reanalysis of neutron skin values for calcium isotopes compared to the optical limit of the Glauber model used by Tanaka et al. (2019), but the resulting skin values are close to the original results except for ⁴⁸Ca, where the authors' value is smaller.

## Method

The authors used the Kyushu chiral g-matrix folding model with projectile and target densities calculated using the Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (GHFB) method, with angular momentum projection (AMP) applied for even-mass nuclei. They scaled the GHFB and GHFB+AMP proton and neutron densities so that the calculated reaction cross sections agreed with the central values of the measured interaction cross sections, under the constraint that the proton radius of the scaled density matched the experimental proton radius from electron scattering isotope shift data. For ⁴⁸Ca, they then took the weighted mean of their result and the result from the high-resolution E1 polarizability experiment.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca, the authors' reanalyzed neutron skin from interaction cross sections is r_skin(σ_I) = 0.105 ± 0.06 fm, compared to the Tanaka et al. (2019) value of r_skin(RIKEN) = 0.146 ± 0.06 fm.
- The final combined result for ⁴⁸Ca, taking the weighted mean with the E1 polarizability experiment result of r_skin(E1pE) = 0.17 ± 0.03 fm, is r_skin = 0.157 ± 0.027 fm.
- For ⁴²Ca, the reanalyzed neutron skin is r_skin(σ_I) = 0.066 ± 0.06 fm, compared to the original r_skin(RIKEN) = 0.049 ± 0.06 fm.
- For ⁵⁰Ca, the reanalyzed neutron skin is r_skin(σ_I) = 0.374 ± 0.05 fm, compared to the original r_skin(RIKEN) = 0.353 ± 0.05 fm.
- The average error on the interaction cross section data from Tanaka et al. (2019) is 1.1%.

## Context

This work reanalyzes the interaction cross section data for ⁴²⁻⁵¹Ca on ¹²C at 280 MeV/nucleon originally measured by Tanaka et al. (2019) at RIKEN, who used the optical limit of the Glauber model with Woods-Saxon densities. The authors apply the Kyushu chiral g-matrix folding model, previously successful for Ne and Mg isotopes (Sumi 2012, Watanabe 2014) and for ⁴He scattering (Toyokawa 2017), to obtain improved neutron skin values. The final result for ⁴⁸Ca is related to the ongoing CREX experiment and is consistent with the coupled-cluster result of Hagen et al. (2015) giving r_skin = 0.12 to 0.15 fm.
