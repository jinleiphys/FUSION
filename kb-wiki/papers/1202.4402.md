# N-body Efimov states from two-particle noise

> The ground state energies of universal N-body clusters tied to Efimov trimers, for N even, are shown to be encapsulated in the statistical distribution of two particles interacting with a background auxiliary field at large Euclidean time when the interaction is tuned to the unitary point. Numerical evidence that this distribution is log-normal is presented, allowing one to predict the ground-state energies of the N-body system.

## Key claim

The ground state energies of universal $2N$-body Efimov clusters (for even particle number) can be extracted from the statistical distribution of a two-particle correlator in an auxiliary field at large Euclidean time, provided the distribution is exactly log-normal. The paper presents numerical evidence that this distribution is log-normal to within approximately 2%, but acknowledges that small deviations from log-normality exist and that the predicted energies agree with prior numerical calculations only at the 10-30% level.

## Method

The author computes the two-particle correlation function for nonrelativistic fermions interacting through a point interaction tuned to the unitary point, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce an auxiliary field. The moments of the two-body correlator are shown to correspond to the ground state energies of $2N$-body systems via the relation $\mathcal{M}_N(T) \to \mathcal{Z}_{2N} e^{-E_0^{(2N)} T}$ at large Euclidean time. Lattice calculations on a $16^3 \times 1000$ lattice with momentum-dependent interactions tuned to remove lattice artifacts are used to generate the correlator distribution, and a cumulant expansion of $\ln C_2$ is employed to quantify deviations from log-normality.

## Key numbers

- The lattice data is log-normal to within approximately 2%, as measured by the discrepancy between the cumulant expansion cut off at $N_\kappa=2$ and the converged value.
- The predicted energies from the log-normal assumption, $E_0^{(2N)} = \frac{1}{2}N(N-1) E_0^{(4)}$, agree with numerical calculations from vonStecher (2009) at the 10-30% level, with greater discrepancy for larger $N$.
- The energy cutoff in the lattice formulation is approximately 335 times the three-particle ground state energy per particle.
- The three-body ground state binding energy is $E_0^{(3)} = -a_3 \Lambda_E$, where $\Lambda_E$ is the Efimov scale introduced by an effective three-body interaction.
- The four-body ground state energy is $E_0^{(4)} = -a_4 \Lambda_E$, with no relevant four-body scale.
- For the log-normal distribution, the parameters satisfy $\mu = \frac{1}{2} E_0^{(4)} T$ and $\sigma^2 = -E_0^{(4)} T$ at unitarity.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Endres (2011) that lattice correlators for strongly interacting nonrelativistic systems display log-normal distributions, and connects this statistical behavior to the universal Efimov physics described by Efimov (1970, 1971). The approach provides a new method for predicting energies of higher-body Efimov states (up to $2N$-body) without direct many-body calculations, complementing prior numerical studies by vonStecher (2009), Hammer (2006, 2007), and others. The results enable future work to develop perturbative expansions around the log-normal distribution and to systematically include corrections from higher cumulants, potentially improving agreement with numerical calculations for larger $N$.
