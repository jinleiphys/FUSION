# Microscopic structure of a vortex line in superfluid neutron star matter

> The microscopic structure of an isolated vortex line in superfluid neutron star matter is studied by solving the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations. Our calculation, which is the starting point for a microscopic calculation of pinning forces in neutron stars, shows that the size of the vortex core varies differently with density, and is in general smaller than assumed in some earlier calculations of vortex pinning in neutron star crusts. The implications of this result are discussed

## Key claim
The vortex core size in superfluid neutron star matter, as determined by the self-consistent coherence length ξ₂, varies differently with density than the BCS coherence length ξ₀ used in earlier pinning calculations, and is generally smaller than previously assumed, with the discrepancy being largest at low densities.

## Method
The authors solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations self-consistently for an isolated vortex line in infinite neutron matter, using a zero-range pairing interaction with the strength g fitted to reproduce the ¹S₀ pairing gap from the Bonn A potential. Quasiparticle states were expanded in a Bessel function basis, and the pairing gap Δ(ρ) was iterated to convergence, with the cutoff energy set to ℏΩ = 50 MeV and the effective mass m* taken equal to the bare neutron mass for dilute matter.

## Key numbers
- For k_F = 0.1 fm⁻¹: Δ_∞ = 0.06 MeV, ξ₀ = 26.80 fm, ξ₂ = 11.46 fm
- For k_F = 0.2 fm⁻¹: Δ_∞ = 0.38 MeV, ξ₀ = 8.73 fm, ξ₂ = 6.59 fm
- For k_F = 0.5 fm⁻¹: Δ_∞ = 1.91 MeV, ξ₀ = 4.42 fm, ξ₂ = 10.20 fm
- The coherence length ξ₂ remains between 6 and 10 fm across the density range studied, while ξ₀ varies by a factor of ten
- The BCS coherence length is defined as ξ₀ = 16.88 k_F / Δ_∞

## Context
This work extends the microscopic BdG approach previously applied to type-II superconductors by Gygi and Schluter (1991) and Hayashi et al. (1998) to the neutron star context, where the parameter ξ₀k_F is smaller and magnetic fields are absent. The results contradict the common assumption in pinning calculations by Alpar (1977), Takatsuka (1984), and Lazzari and de Blasio (1995) that the vortex core is a cylinder of normal matter of radius ξ₀, and instead show that the core size is more uniform across densities, disfavoring the super-weak pinning regime proposed by Pines and Alpar (1985). The work enables a microscopic calculation of pinning forces between vortices and nuclear clusters in the neutron star crust.
