# Impact of global monopole on heavy mesons in hot-dense medium

> This research study is primarily focus on investigating how the topological effects influence the eigenvalue solutions in the presence of a hot-dense medium. To accomplish this, we employ the non-relativistic Schrödinger wave equation, taking into consideration both the quantum flux field and an interaction potential. Through this approach, we determine the energy eigenvalues and their corresponding wave functions using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method. Our findings indicate that when we consider both the topological effects and the magnetic flux, $Φ$, there is a noticeable reduction in the binding energy within the hot-dense medium. Additionally, we analyze the role of the baryonic potential in shaping the binding energy within the $(T, u_b)$ plane. Interestingly, it is evident that the influence of the baryonic potential becomes more pronounced as its values decrease

## Key claim

The paper claims that topological effects from a point-like global monopole and an external magnetic flux reduce the binding energy of heavy mesons in a hot-dense medium, but this result is conditional on the approximation that the Debye mass times the radial coordinate is much less than one (m_D r << 1) and on a second-order series expansion of the interaction potential.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic Schrödinger wave equation for a quark-antiquark system (charmonium and bottomonium) in the background of a point-like global monopole, including a quantum flux field and a temperature- and density-dependent Cornell potential. The radial equation is transformed via a change of variable and series expansion around the characteristic meson radius, then solved analytically using the Nikiforov-Uvarov (NU) method to obtain energy eigenvalues and wave functions. The Debye mass is taken as a function of temperature and baryonic chemical potential, and the quarkonium mass is computed as twice the bare quark mass plus the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature used is T_c = 0.17 GeV.
- The binding energy is plotted for magnetic flux values Φ = 0.25, 0.50, and 0.75 (dimensionless, in units of Φ_0 = 2π e^{-1}).
- The global monopole parameter α is varied among values 0.25, 0.40, 0.75, and 1.0 (dimensionless, with α=1 corresponding to no topological defect).
- The baryonic chemical potential u_b is varied in the (T, u_b) plane, with specific plots at u_b = 0 and at finite values.
- The quarkonium mass formula reduces to the result of Ref. [2] when α=1 and Φ=0, and to the classical result of Ref. [31] when additionally T=0.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quarkonia in hot-dense media (e.g., Abu-Shady et al. (Ref. [2]) using the Cornell potential at finite temperature, and Potel et al. (Ref. [3]) using conformable fractional NU method) by incorporating topological effects from a global monopole and an external magnetic flux. It also builds on earlier quantum-mechanical studies of global monopoles by Abu-Shady and collaborators (Refs. [20-28]) who considered various potentials but without hot-dense medium. The results enable future investigations of how topological defects from the early universe might modify quarkonium suppression patterns in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
