# Study of effective interaction from single particle transfer reactions on f-p shell nuclei

> The present study concentrates on the average effective two-body interaction matrix elements being extracted, using sum-rule techniques, from transfer reactions on target states having single orbital as well as two orbitaloccupancy. This investigation deals with transfer reactions on f-p shell nuclei involving (i) $1f_{7/2}$ and $2p_{3/2}$ transfer on target states using $^{40}$Ca as inert core, and (ii) $2p_{3/2}$ and$1f_{5/2}$ transfer on states using $^{56}$Ni as core.

## Key claim

The paper extracts average effective two-body interaction matrix elements from single-particle transfer reactions on f-p shell nuclei, extending previous work by considering target states with two active orbitals (rather than just one) outside the inert cores \(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{56}\)Ni. The authors acknowledge that the results are subject to uncertainties from incomplete experimental spectra and the assumption of pure configurations in target states.

## Method

The authors apply energy-weighted sum-rule techniques to experimental data on single-nucleon stripping and pick-up reactions. For targets with \(^{40}\)Ca as the inert core, they analyze \(1f_{7/2}\) and \(2p_{3/2}\) transfers on nuclei with either \((1f_{7/2})^n\) or mixed \((1f_{7/2})^n(2p_{3/2})^m\) configurations; for targets with \(^{56}\)Ni as the inert core, they study \(2p_{3/2}\) and \(1f_{5/2}\) transfers on nuclei with \((2p_{3/2})^n\) or mixed \((2p_{3/2})^n(1f_{5/2})^m\) configurations. Linear equations relating the isospin centroids of residual nuclear states to the average interaction parameters are set up and solved via least-squares fits, combining equations from single- and two-orbital target states.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{40}\)Ca core case, the extracted average interaction parameters include \(\overline{W}^{T=0}_{1f_{7/2}1f_{7/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=1}_{1f_{7/2}1f_{7/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=0}_{1f_{7/2}2p_{3/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=1}_{1f_{7/2}2p_{3/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=0}_{2p_{3/2}2p_{3/2}}\), and \(\overline{W}^{T=1}_{2p_{3/2}2p_{3/2}}\) (numerical values given in Table III of the paper).
- For the \(^{56}\)Ni core case, the extracted parameters include \(\overline{W}^{T=0}_{2p_{3/2}2p_{3/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=1}_{2p_{3/2}2p_{3/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=0}_{2p_{3/2}1f_{5/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=1}_{2p_{3/2}1f_{5/2}}\), \(\overline{W}^{T=0}_{1f_{5/2}1f_{5/2}}\), and \(\overline{W}^{T=1}_{1f_{5/2}1f_{5/2}}\) (numerical values given in Table IV of the paper).
- The analysis for the \(^{40}\)Ca core used transfer reaction data from five targets with both \(1f_{7/2}\) and \(2p_{3/2}\) active orbits, combined with data from \((1f_{7/2})^n\) targets.

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach previously applied by the same group (Sharda et al., earlier works) to single-orbital cases, now incorporating two active orbitals to extract cross-shell interaction matrix elements. The results are compared with those from other authors (referenced in the paper as r64–r69), showing minor variations within uncertainties. The extracted matrix elements can be used as input for shell-model calculations in the f-p shell region, and the method enables the systematic study of effective interactions from transfer reaction data on more complex target configurations.
