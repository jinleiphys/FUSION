# Effective Field Theory of Nuclear Forces

> The application of the effective field theory (EFT) method to nuclear systems is reviewed. The roles of degrees of freedom, QCD symmetries, power counting, renormalization, and potentials are discussed. EFTs are constructed for various energy regimes of relevance in nuclear physics, and are used in systematic expansions to derive nuclear forces in terms of a number of parameters that embody information about QCD dynamics. Two-, three-, and many-nucleon systems, including external probes, are considered.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theory (EFT) provides a systematic framework for deriving nuclear forces from QCD symmetries, but the success of the expansion depends critically on the relative magnitude of pion effects compared to shorter-range dynamics, and the scale at which pions become non-perturbative (denoted \(M_{NN}\)) remains a matter of current debate.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of EFTs for nuclear systems by identifying relevant degrees of freedom (nucleons, pions, and possibly delta isobars) based on the momentum scale \(Q\) relative to the characteristic QCD scale \(M_{QCD} \sim 1\) GeV and the nuclear scale \(M_{nuc} \sim 100\) MeV. The method uses chiral symmetry to constrain interactions, a power counting scheme to organize contributions in powers of \(Q/M_{QCD}\), and renormalization to absorb short-distance physics into local interaction coefficients. For very low momenta (\(Q \lesssim M_{nuc}\)), all interactions are treated as contact terms; for low momenta (\(Q \sim M_{nuc}\)), pions are included explicitly as dynamical fields.

## Key numbers

* The characteristic QCD scale is \(M_{QCD} \sim 1\) GeV, the nuclear scale is \(M_{nuc} \sim 100\) MeV, and nuclear binding energies are of order \(M_{nuc}^2/M_{QCD} \sim 10\) MeV.
* In the \(^3S_1\) channel, the scattering length scale \(\aleph^{(^3S_1)} \sim 40\) MeV; in the \(^1S_0\) channel, \(\aleph^{(^1S_0)} \sim 8\) MeV, with an average \(\aleph \sim 30\) MeV.
* The deuteron binding energy is of order \(\aleph^2/M_{QCD}\), which is much smaller than \(M_{nuc}^2/M_{QCD}\).
* The pion mass \(m_\pi \sim M_{nuc}\) and the delta-nucleon mass difference \(\delta m \equiv m_\Delta - m_N \sim M_{nuc}\).

## Context

This review extends the EFT approach to nuclear systems, building on earlier work in chiral perturbation theory for single-nucleon systems and on the pioneering ideas of Bethe and Peierls (1930s) regarding the deuteron's large size relative to the nuclear force range. The program was initiated in works by van Kolck and collaborators in the mid-1990s, and it enables systematic calculations of two-, three-, and many-nucleon systems including external probes (photons, electrons, neutrinos), with the goal of replacing phenomenological nuclear models by a framework with controlled theoretical uncertainties.
