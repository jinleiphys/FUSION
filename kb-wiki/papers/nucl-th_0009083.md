# Effect of in-medium properties on heavy-ion collisions

> The properties of strange hadrons, i.e. of kaons and hyperons, in the nuclear medium are discussed in connection with neutron star properties and relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Firstly, the relevant medium modifications of a kaon in a medium as provided by heavy-ion collisions is critically examined within a coupled channel calculation. We demonstrate, that particle ratios for kaons are not a sensitive probe of in-medium effects while the K- flow is more suited to pin down the K- optical potential in dense matter. Secondly, the interaction between hyperons is studied and may form bound states which can be produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Signals for the detection of strange dibaryons by their decay topology and/or in the invariant mass spectra are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the in-medium effects probed in heavy-ion collisions are entirely different from those in neutron stars, and that particle ratios for kaons are not a sensitive probe of in-medium effects, while the K- flow is more suited to pin down the K- optical potential in dense matter.

## Method

The author performed coupled channel calculations for the K- in nuclear matter at finite relative momentum and finite temperature, using a simple separable SU(3) symmetric potential whose parameters were fixed by the shape of the Lambda(1405) resonance. For hyperons, the weak decay patterns of strange dibaryons were modeled using weak SU(3) symmetry for the parity-violating and parity-conserving amplitudes, with parameters fixed by the weak decay amplitudes of the octet hyperons. The results were then applied to relativistic heavy-ion collisions using the BUU transport model.

## Key numbers

- The K+ optical potential at normal nuclear density is repulsive: U_opt(K+) = +30 MeV at rho/rho_0
- The K- optical potential from coupled channel calculations is attractive: U_K- ≈ -100 MeV at rho_0
- The critical temperature for the K- optical potential to change sign at normal nuclear density is T_c = 32 MeV
- For twice normal nuclear density, the critical temperature is about twice this value (approximately 64 MeV)
- The slope parameter extracted from hadron spectra in relevant heavy-ion experiments at GSI is T = 90 MeV
- The Lambda optical potential at normal nuclear density is U_Lambda = -27 MeV
- The Xi optical potential at normal nuclear density is U_Xi = -20 to -25 MeV, with recent measurements indicating a smaller value of U_Xi = -14 MeV or less
- The decay lengths of different strange dibaryons from model calculations are in the range c*tau = 1-5 cm
- The decay length of the Sigma-Sigma- dibaryon is c*tau(Sigma-Sigma-) = c*tau(Sigma-)/2 = 2.2 cm
- Production rates of strange dibaryons for Au+Au collisions at RHIC vary between 10^-2 to 10^-4 per event

## Context

This work extends studies of kaon in-medium effects by Li (1997) and Koch (1994), and contradicts the interpretation that enhanced subthreshold K- production at GSI is due to a reduced effective energy of the K- from an attractive optical potential. The paper instead proposes that enhanced in-medium cross sections, particularly through the shifted Lambda(1405) resonance, explain the observed K- enhancement. For hyperons, the work builds on hyperon-nucleon interaction studies from hypernuclear data by Dover and Gal (1983) and Dover (1991), and extends the meson exchange model for weak nonmesonic decay of Lambda hypernuclei by Parreno (1997) to other hyperons. The results enable searches for strange dibaryons in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and provide a new observable (K- flow) to distinguish in-medium effects from statistical production.
