# Dual chiral density waves in nuclear matter

> We study inhomogeneous chiral phases in nuclear matter using a hadronic model with the parity doublet structure. With an extended ansatz for the dual chiral density wave off the chiral limit, we numerically determine the phase structure. A new type of dual chiral density wave where the condensate has nonvanishing space average is confirmed and it comes to occupy a wide range of low density region as the chiral invariant mass parameter is lowered.

# Dual chiral density waves in nuclear matter

## Key claim
Using a hadronic model with parity doublet structure that includes the chiral invariant mass parameter \(m_0\), the authors find that a new type of dual chiral density wave (sDCDW) with nonvanishing space-averaged condensate appears at low baryon density when \(m_0\) is below approximately 780 MeV, but this parameter range fails to reproduce stable nuclear matter at saturation.

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model for nuclear matter based on the parity doublet picture, treating \(N(939)\) and \(N^\ast(1535)\) as chiral partners, with scalar (\(\sigma\)), pseudoscalar (\(\pi\)), and vector (\(\omega\)) mesons included via hidden local symmetry. They adopt an extended DCDW ansatz \(\sigma = \delta\sigma + \sigma_0\cos(2fz)\), \(\pi_3 = \sigma_0\sin(2fz)\) that allows a nonzero space-averaged condensate \(\delta\sigma\), diagonalize the Bogoliubov-de Gennes Hamiltonian for nucleons in the periodic potential using Bloch's theorem, and determine the phase structure by numerically minimizing the effective potential as a function of baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) for several values of the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\).

## Key numbers
- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), binding energy per nucleon \(-16\ \text{MeV}\), and incompressibility \(K = 240\ \text{MeV}\) are used to fix model parameters for each \(m_0\).
- For \(m_0 = 700\ \text{MeV}\), the sDCDW phase appears in the range \(900\ \text{MeV} \lesssim \mu_B \lesssim 1020\ \text{MeV}\), with wavenumber \(f \sim 70\)–\(100\ \text{MeV}\) and shift \(\delta\sigma \sim 50\)–\(70\ \text{MeV}\), giving a chiral density wavelength \(\lambda = \pi/f \sim 6\)–\(9\ \text{fm}\).
- For \(m_0 = 800\ \text{MeV}\), the ordinary DCDW phase has wavenumber \(f \sim 200\)–\(300\ \text{MeV}\), corresponding to wavelength \(\lambda \sim 2\)–\(3\ \text{fm}\), and the space-averaged order parameter \(M_1\) is typically less than \(10\ \text{MeV}\).
- The threshold for sDCDW appearance is \(m_0 \lesssim 780\ \text{MeV}\); for \(m_0 \lesssim 670\ \text{MeV}\) the homogeneous chiral-symmetry broken phase disappears entirely.

## Context
This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral phases from quark-based models (e.g., NJL-type models by Nickel (2009), Carignano (2010), and Karasawa (2013)) to a hadronic model that can describe nuclear matter properties at saturation, following the parity doublet approach of Detar and Kunihiro (1988) and Jido (2001). The authors build on their own earlier work (Takeda, 2018) and the parameterization of Motohiro (2015). The finding of a shifted DCDW phase with spontaneously generated \(\delta\sigma\) suggests that such phases may survive in the chiral limit; the authors note that if \(m_0\) decreases with density as argued by Heinz (2013), the sDCDW phase could be realized in high-density nuclear matter. The work opens the question of whether a phonon mode (extra Nambu-Goldstone boson) appears in the sDCDW phase, unlike the ordinary DCDW where a combination of translation and chiral rotation remains unbroken.
