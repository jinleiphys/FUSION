# Hbt Analysis of Anisotropic Transverse Flow

> The effects of anisotropic transverse collective flow on the HBT correlation function is studied. There exist three different physics contributions related to flow which affect the correlation function: anisotropic source shape, anisotropic space-momentum correlations in pion emission, and the effects related to the HBT measurement of the size of a moving source in different reference frames. Resolution of these contributions experimentally can lead to a detailed understanding of both collective flow in nucleus-nucleus collisions and the HBT technique itself. A method is presented which permits the derivation of model independent relations between the radius of a source measured in a frame in which it is moving and in its rest frame.

# HBT Analysis of Anisotropic Transverse Flow

## Key claim
Anisotropic transverse flow in nucleus-nucleus collisions affects HBT correlation functions through three distinct physical mechanisms: anisotropic source shape, anisotropic space-momentum correlations in pion emission, and the effects of measuring a moving source in different reference frames. The paper argues that these three contributions can be disentangled experimentally to provide detailed understanding of both collective flow and the HBT technique, but the analysis is presented on a qualitative level without a complete set of formulae for the general case.

## Method
The authors use RQMD 1.08 generated events for Au+Au collisions at 11.4 GeV/nucleon to study production point distributions of pions with rapidity around y_lab ≈ 3, analyzing distributions in x, y, z, and t coordinates for pions emitted in the +x, -x, and y directions in the transverse plane. They derive expressions for HBT radii in the "long-side-out" coordinate system from second derivatives of the correlation function, and develop model-independent Lorentz transformation relations between source sizes measured in the moving frame and the source rest frame. Calculations are performed in the center-of-mass frame of the colliding nuclei, considering only pions produced within the first 50 fm/c after the collision.

## Key numbers
- Transverse flow velocity for pions emitted in the +x direction: v_x^{(+x)} ≈ 0.13 (in natural units, from RQMD simulation)
- Transverse flow velocity for pions emitted in the -x direction: v_x^{(-x)} ≈ -0.08 (from RQMD simulation)
- Transverse flow velocity for pions emitted in the +y direction: v_y^{(+y)} ≈ 0.10 (from RQMD simulation)
- Longitudinal flow velocity for all directions of pion emission: v_z ≈ 0.76 (from RQMD simulation)
- Effective source size in the "out" direction seen from the "in^+" direction: sqrt(12.8) fm (from RQMD simulation)
- Effective source size in the "out" direction seen from the "in^-" direction: sqrt(17.4) fm (from RQMD simulation)
- Effective source size in the "side" direction seen from the "in^+" direction: sqrt(19.1) fm (from RQMD simulation)
- Effective source size in the "side" direction seen from the "in^-" direction: sqrt(16.6) fm (from RQMD simulation)
- Mean transverse velocity of pions: approximately 0.38 (in the center-of-mass frame)
- Mean longitudinal velocity of pions: approximately 0.87 (in the center-of-mass frame)
- Average transverse flow velocity from experimental data: v_t ≈ 0.3 (cited from prior work)
- Average longitudinal flow velocity from experimental data: v_long ≈ 0.5 (cited from prior work)

## Context
This work extends the study of anisotropic transverse flow discovered at BNL AGS energies (reported in a 1993 Physical Review Letter) by examining its impact on HBT measurements of collision volumes. The paper builds on prior HBT formalism by Pratt (1984), the concept of "lengths of homogeneity" introduced by Sinyukov (1989), and the "on-shell" correlation function formulation. The authors note that about half of the difference between R_x^2 in the +x and -x cases is due to transverse flow, with the remainder attributable to source geometry differences. The work enables future experimental studies of the dependence of HBT correlation functions on the orientation of pion pair momentum with respect to the reaction plane, and suggests comparison with two-proton correlation techniques for source size measurements.
