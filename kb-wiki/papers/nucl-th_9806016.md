# Consistent BCS+RQRPA formalism with application to the double beta decay

> A new consistent analysis of the renormalized proton--neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation based on the simultaneous recalculation of the one--body density matrix and the pairing tensor has been used to study the double beta decay. We demonstrated that inclusion of the quasiparticle correlations at the BCS level reduces the ground state correlations in the particle--particle channel of the proton--neutron interaction. We also simplified the RQRPA equations significantly obtaining a low--dimensioned set of linear equations for the quasiparticle densities. The formalism was applied to the double beta decay in ^{76}Ge.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a self-consistent iteration between the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) and a modified BCS treatment (which recalculates the density matrix and pairing tensor using non-zero quasiparticle densities) reduces ground-state correlations in the particle-particle channel and stabilizes the calculated double-beta decay matrix elements, but only if all possible multipolarities are included in the renormalization factors.

## Method

The authors develop a consistent BCS+RQRPA (SRQRPA) formalism by iterating between the RQRPA equations and a modified BCS solution where the density matrix and pairing tensor depend on the quasiparticle densities \(n_a\). They derive a low-dimensional (\(2n \times 2n\)) linear system for the quasiparticle densities, replacing the previous \(n^2 \times n^2\) system, which allows inclusion of all multipolarities. The method is applied to the \(2\nu\beta\beta\) Gamow-Teller decay of \(^{76}\)Ge using the Bonn-B nucleon-nucleon potential and a Woods-Saxon single-particle basis with 16 levels above a \(^{16}\)O core.

## Key numbers

* The experimental half-life for \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay in \(^{76}\)Ge is \(T_{1/2}^{2\nu} = (1.42 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{21}\) yr, from the Heidelberg-Moscow collaboration.
* The Ikeda sum rule is violated by up to 20% in the RQRPA and up to 25% in the SRQRPA, compared to exact conservation in the usual QRPA.
* The physically acceptable region for the particle-particle renormalization parameter is \(0.8 \leq g_{\rm pp} \leq 1.2\).
* The single-particle basis consists of 16 levels with \(^{16}\)O as a core.
* Multipolarities up to \(11^+\) are included in the full calculations.

## Context

This work extends the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) formalism of Tsuhara (1995) and Schweiger (1996) by making the BCS treatment consistent with the non-zero quasiparticle densities of the RQRPA ground state, following the approach of the "gsc" reference. It addresses the instability of the standard QRPA, which requires fine-tuning of the \(g_{\rm pp}\) parameter to avoid collapse of the matrix elements. The method enables more predictive calculations of double-beta decay matrix elements and identifies the need to include scattering terms (B-modes) in the phonon operator to restore the Ikeda sum rule, as suggested by Krmar (1998) and Raduta (1998).
