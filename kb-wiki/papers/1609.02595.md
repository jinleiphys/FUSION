# Magnetic catalysis of a finite size pion condensate

> We study the Bose-Einstein condensation of a finite size pion gas subject to the influence of a magnetic field. We find the expressions for the critical chemical potential and temperature for the onset of condensation. We show that for values of the external magnetic flux larger than the elemental flux, the critical temperature is larger than the one obtained by considering only finite size effects. We use experimentally reported values of pion source sizes and multiplicities at LHC energies to show that if the magnetic flux, produced initially in peripheral heavy-ion collision, is at least partially preserved up to the hadronic phase, the combined finite size and magnetic field effects give rise to a critical temperature above the kinetic freeze-out temperature. We discuss the implications for the evolution of the pion system created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors show that for a finite-size pion gas in a weak magnetic field, the critical temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation increases above the increase caused by finite volume alone, provided the magnetic flux through the system exceeds the elemental flux \(\Phi_0\). The effect is conditional on the magnetic flux being at least partially preserved from the initial collision stage up to the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors treat a non-interacting gas of pions at finite temperature in a finite cubic volume \(V = L^3\), subject to a uniform magnetic field \(\vec{B} = B\hat{z}\) in the weak-field limit \(eB < mT\). They separate the particle density into ground-state and excited-state contributions, express the ground-state contribution in terms of the magnetic flux \(\Phi = V^{2/3}B\) relative to the elemental flux \(\Phi_0 = 2\pi/e\), and use the Euler-Maclaurin expansion to show that the excited-state contribution from charged pions reduces to that of neutral pions at leading order. The critical chemical potential and critical temperature are then obtained by solving the algebraic equation for \(\delta = \beta(m - \mu)\) near the condensation onset.

## Key numbers

* For a semi-central Pb+Pb collision at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV with source radius \(R \sim 7\) fm, the average thermal pion density is \(\langle \rho \rangle \sim 0.22\) fm\(^{-3}\), with a range \(0.2 < \rho < 0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The thermal freeze-out temperature range from blast-wave fits is \(95 < T_{th} < 106\) MeV for centrality classes 0-40%.
* A magnetic field with initial strength \(eB = \zeta m^2\) and \(\zeta \sim 1/2\) can produce fluxes \(\Phi/\Phi_0 > 1\) for semi-central collisions with nuclear radius \(R_N \sim 6-7\) fm and impact parameter \(b = R_N\).
* The critical chemical potential in the presence of magnetic flux is \(\mu_c^\Phi = m - \frac{1}{m} \left( \frac{2\pi^2}{V^2} \right)^{1/3} \left( \frac{1 + 2\Phi/\Phi_0}{3} \right)^{2/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume treatment of pion Bose-Einstein condensation by Begun (2015) and Begun (2008) to include magnetic field effects, and builds on earlier studies of magnetic catalysis of condensation in weakly interacting systems. The results enable further studies of whether the pion system in heavy-ion collisions can transit through a condensate state before kinetic freeze-out, with implications for the chaoticity parameter in interferometry measurements (BE-correlations studies). The authors note that for the isospin chemical potential, the opposite effect was found by Endrodi, where a higher charge imbalance is needed to sustain a condensate in a magnetic field.
