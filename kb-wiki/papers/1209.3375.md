# Nuclear Quantum Many-Body Dynamics: From Collective Vibrations to Heavy-Ion Collisions

> A summary of recent researches on nuclear dynamics with realistic microscopic quantum approaches is presented. The Balian-Vénéroni variational principle is used to derive the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation describing the dynamics at the mean-field level, as well as an extension including small-amplitude quantum fluctuations which is equivalent to the time-dependent random-phase approximation (TDRPA). Such formalisms as well as their practical implementation in the nuclear physics framework with modern three-dimensional codes are discussed. Recent applications to nuclear dynamics, from collective vibrations to heavy-ion collisions are presented. A particular attention is devoted to the interplay between collective motions and internal degrees of freedom. For instance, the harmonic nature of collective vibrations is questioned. Nuclei are also known to exhibit superfluidity due to pairing residual interaction. Extensions of the theoretical approach to study such pairing vibrations are now available. Large amplitude collective motions are investigated in the framework of heavy-ion collisions leading, for instance, to the formation of a compound system. How fusion is affected by the internal structure of the collision partners, such as their deformation, is discussed. Other mechanisms in competition with fusion, and responsible for the formation of fragments which differ from the entrance channel (transfer reactions, deep-inelastic collisions, and quasi-fission) are investigated. Finally, studies of actinide collisions forming, during very short times of few zeptoseconds, the heaviest nuclear systems available on Earth, are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, derived from the Balian-Vénéroni variational principle, is optimized for predicting expectation values of one-body observables but not for fluctuations or correlations of those observables; the latter require a separate prescription from the same variational principle, which yields results equivalent to the time-dependent random-phase approximation (TDRPA) in the small-amplitude limit.

## Method

The author derives the TDHF equation from the Balian-Vénéroni variational principle by restricting the variational space for the observable to one-body operators and the state to Slater determinants. For fluctuations and correlations of one-body observables, the variational space for the observable is extended to exponentials of one-body operators, leading to a prescription that involves evolving perturbed density matrices backward in time under the TDHF equation. The formalism is implemented with Skyrme energy density functionals in modern three-dimensional codes, and applications to collective vibrations and heavy-ion collisions are reviewed.

## Key numbers

- The time over which the Slater determinant picture breaks down due to residual interaction is estimated as τ ≈ 100–200 fm/c in the nuclear physics context.
- Actinide collisions forming the heaviest nuclear systems available on Earth last for "few zeptoseconds" (1 zeptosecond = 10⁻²¹ s).
- The paper reports that TDHF calculations are successful for one-body observables but fail to reproduce fluctuations of one-body operators, as shown in prior work by Koonin (1977), Davies (1978), and Das (1979).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on time-dependent mean-field approaches initiated by Dirac (1930) and formalized by Balian and Vénéroni (1981, 1984, 1992). It contrasts with semi-classical transport models such as the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) and Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) theories, which include two-body collision terms but poorly describe ground-state nuclear structure. The paper enables future studies of pairing vibrations via time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) and provides a framework for computing fragment mass and charge distributions in heavy-ion collisions, as demonstrated in Simenel (2011).
