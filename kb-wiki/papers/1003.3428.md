# No-core shell model for A = 47 and A = 49

> We apply the no-core shell model to the nuclear structure of odd-mass nuclei straddling $^{48}$Ca. Starting with the NN interaction, that fits two-body scattering and bound state data we evaluate the nuclear properties of $A = 47$ and $A = 49$ nuclei while preserving all the underlying symmetries. Due to model space limitations and the absence of 3-body interactions, we incorporate phenomenological interaction terms determined by fits to $A = 48$ nuclei in a previous effort. Our modified Hamiltonian produces reasonable spectra for these odd mass nuclei. In addition to the differences in single-particle basis states, the absence of a single-particle Hamiltonian in our no-core approach complicates comparisons with valence effective NN interactions. We focus on purely off-diagonal two-body matrix elements since they are not affected by ambiguities in the different roles for one-body potentials and we compare selected sets of $fp$-shell matrix elements of our initial and modified Hamiltonians in the harmonic oscillator basis with those of a recent model $fp$-shell interaction, the GXPF1 interaction of Honma, Otsuka, Brown and Mizusaki. While some significant differences emerge from these comparisons, there is an overall reasonably good correlation between our off-diagonal matrix elements and those of GXPF1.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model (NCSM), when supplemented with phenomenological two-body terms fitted to A=48 nuclei, produces reasonable spectra for odd-mass A=47 and A=49 nuclei despite model space limitations and the absence of three-body interactions, though the ground state spin of ⁴⁷K remains incorrect.

## Method

The authors applied the NCSM with a two-body effective Hamiltonian derived from the CD-Bonn NN interaction in a limited N_max=0 basis space with ℏΩ=10 MeV. They added three phenomenological NN interaction terms (central modified Gaussians with isospin-dependent strengths plus a tensor term) that were fitted to the total binding energies of ⁴⁸Ca, ⁴⁸Sc, and ⁴⁸Ti along with the lowest positive and negative parity excitations of ⁴⁸Ca. They then calculated ground state energies and excitation spectra for ⁴⁷Ca, ⁴⁹Ca, ⁴⁷Sc, and ⁴⁷K, and compared selected off-diagonal fp-shell two-body matrix elements with those of the GXPF1 interaction of Honma, Otsuka, Brown and Mizusaki.

## Key numbers

- Ground states calculated with the ab initio CD-Bonn effective Hamiltonian lie above experimental values by approximately 20 MeV for the A=47-49 nuclei.
- With the modified Hamiltonian (CD-Bonn + 3 terms), deviations from experimental binding energies are much less than 1%.
- The first excited state of ⁴⁹Sc is a 3/2⁻ at eigenvalue -425.151 MeV (compared to ground state -428.365 MeV) with about 51% occupancy of the 1p3 state; the 18th state at -422.803 MeV has 28% occupancy, and the 24th state at -422.440 MeV has 21% occupancy.
- In ⁴⁹Ca with the modified Hamiltonian, a nearly pure 1p₁/₂ single-particle state is obtained at 5.235 MeV excitation energy.
- The 0d₅/₂ single-hole state in ⁴⁷K appears at -386.17 MeV (13.36 MeV excitation energy) with 30% 0d₅/₂ vacancy.
- One of the "diff2" values (difference between ab initio Heff and modified Heff) reaches -1.2123 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM approach previously applied to A=48 nuclei (Vary, Negoita, and Stoica, 2006) to odd-mass A=47 and A=49 nuclei, testing whether the same modified Hamiltonian can describe these systems. The results are compared with full fp-shell calculations using the KB3 interaction by Martinez-Pinedo, Zuker, Poves and Caurier (1996) and with the GXPF1 interaction of Honma, Otsuka, Brown and Mizusaki (2004). The study enables future work with larger basis spaces, improved three- and four-body cluster calculations, and the introduction of true three-nucleon and four-nucleon potentials.
