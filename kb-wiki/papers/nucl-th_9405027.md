# Toward a Consistent Description of the PNC Experiments in A=18-21 Nuclei

> The experimental PNC results in $^{18}$F, $^{19}$F, $^{21}$Ne and the current theoretical analysis show a discrepancy . If one interprets the small limit of the experimentally extracted PNC matrix element for $^{21}$Ne as a destructive interference between the isoscalar and the isovector contribution, then it is difficult to understand why the isovector contribution in $^{18}$F is so small while the isoscalar + isovector contribution in $^{19}$F is relatively large. In order to understand the origin of this discrepancy a comparison of the calculated PNC matrix elements was performed. It is shown that the $^{18}$F and $^{21}$Ne matrix elements contain important contributions from 3$\hbar ω$ and 4$\hbar ω$ configuration and that the (0+1)$\hbar ω$ calculations give distorted results.

# Toward a Consistent Description of the PNC Experiments in A=18-21 Nuclei

## Key claim
The apparent discrepancy between experimental parity nonconservation (PNC) results in ¹⁸F, ¹⁹F, and ²¹Ne and existing theoretical calculations can be resolved if nuclear structure calculations include up to 3ℏω and 4ℏω configurations, rather than the previously used (0+1)ℏω or (0+1+2)ℏω truncations. The smallness of the ²¹Ne PNC matrix element is reinterpreted as arising from a very suppressed isoscalar contribution combined with a small isovector contribution, rather than from destructive interference between isoscalar and isovector terms.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations in the small ZBM model space using the F-psd interaction, decomposing the wave functions and PNC matrix elements into nℏω → (n+1)ℏω contributions for the parity-mixed doublets in ¹⁸F, ¹⁹F, and ²¹Ne. They compared these results with larger model space calculations for ¹⁸F using the Warburton-Brown interaction in the first four major shells including up to 3ℏω excitations, with center-of-mass spuriousity removed and short-range correlations included. The DDH best values for weak coupling constants were used throughout.

## Key numbers
- The experimentally extracted PNC matrix element for ²¹Ne has an upper limit of <0.029 eV.
- The isovector contribution in ¹⁸F has an upper limit of <0.09 eV.
- The isoscalar + isovector contribution in ¹⁹F is 0.40 ± 0.1 eV.
- From the analysis, the overlapping region for (α_IS, α_IV) values across all three nuclei lies in the range (0.6-1.2, 0.07-0.26).
- A renormalization factor β_IV = 0.59 was obtained from comparison with the ¹⁸Ne → ¹⁸F first forbidden beta decay result.
- A renormalization factor β_IS = 0.48 was estimated from a (0+1+2+3+4)ℏω calculation in ¹⁴N.

## Context
This work extends the earlier theoretical analysis by Haxton (reviewed in Adelberger and Haxton, 1985) which used (0+1+2)ℏω calculations for ¹⁸F and (0+1)ℏω calculations for ¹⁹F and ²¹Ne, and which interpreted the ²¹Ne result as destructive interference between isoscalar and isovector contributions. The present analysis contradicts the conclusions of Brandenburg (1978), who found a stable isoscalar contribution with fluctuating isovector contributions, and instead agrees with the recent analysis by Desplanques and Delorme (1993). The results enable a consistent extraction of weak nucleon-meson coupling constants from the combined A=18-21 data set, provided future calculations include sufficient nℏω configurations.
