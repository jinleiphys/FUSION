# Influence of charge asymmetry and isospin dependent cross-section on elliptical flow

> Using the isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics model, we study the effect of charge asymmetry and isospin dependent cross-section on different aspects of elliptical flow. Simulations have been carried out for the reactions of $^{124}X_{m}+^{124}X_{m}$, where m = (47, 50, 53, 57 and 59) and $^{40}X_{n}+^{40}X_{n}$, where n= (14, 16, 18, 21 and 23). Our study shows that elliptical flow depend strongly on the isospin of cross-section. The transition energy remains almost constant with increase in N/Z of the system. A good agreement is obtained with experimental measurements.

## Key claim
Elliptical flow in heavy-ion collisions depends strongly on whether the nucleon-nucleon cross-section is isospin dependent or isospin independent, but the transition energy from in-plane to out-of-plane emission remains almost constant as the neutron-to-proton ratio (N/Z) of the system increases.

## Method
The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state. They studied collisions of fixed-mass systems: \(^{124}X_m + ^{124}X_m\) (with m = 47, 50, 53, 57, 59) and \(^{40}X_n + ^{40}X_n\) (with n = 14, 16, 18, 21, 23) at incident energies from 30 to 100 MeV/nucleon. Elliptical flow was analyzed as a function of transverse momentum and beam energy for free nucleons, light mass fragments (LMFs, 2 ≤ A ≤ 4), and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, 5 ≤ A ≤ A_tot/6), using the minimum spanning tree method to identify fragments.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction \(^{124}Sn_{50}+^{124}Sn_{50}\) at E = 100 MeV/nucleon, simulations with the isospin-dependent cross-section reduced by 20% showed good agreement with experimental data from the INDRA@(GSI+GANIL) collaboration.
- The transition energy \(E_{Trans}\) (in MeV/nucleon) for elliptical flow was found to remain almost constant as the N/Z ratio of the system increased, for both the A=124 and A=40 reaction sets.
- At E = 50 MeV/nucleon and E = 100 MeV/nucleon, neutron-rich systems such as \(^{124}Ag_{47}+^{124}Ag_{47}\) and \(^{40}Si_{14}+^{40}Si_{14}\) exhibited weaker squeeze-out flow compared to neutron-deficient systems such as \(^{124}Pr_{59}+^{124}Pr_{59}\) and \(^{40}V_{23}+^{40}V_{23}\).
- The peak of the Gaussian-shaped elliptical flow distribution shifted towards lower values of average transverse momentum \(\langle P_t \rangle\) for heavier fragments compared to free nucleons.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions, particularly those by Yu-Ming Zheng et al. (studying \(^{48}Ca + ^{48}Ca\) from 30 to 100 MeV/nucleon), D. Persram et al. (implementing in-medium cross-sections in the BUU model), and Y. Zhang et al. (investigating elliptic flow excitation functions). The results confirm the findings of Zhang et al. that neutron-rich systems exhibit weaker squeeze-out flow. The study enables future experimental comparisons, particularly for the \(^{40}X_m + ^{40}X_m\) reactions at the SCC500 facility at VECC Kolkata (India), and supports the use of isospin-dependent cross-sections with a 20% reduction to match experimental data from the INDRA collaboration.
