# Microscopic statistical basis of classical Thermodynamics of finite systems

> Heat can flow from cold to hot at any phase separation. Therefore Lynden-Bell's gravo-thermal catastrophe must be reconsidered. The original objects of Thermodynamics, the separation of phases at first order phase transitions, like boiling water in steam engines, are not described by a single canonical ensemble. Inter-phase fluctuations are not covered. The basic principles of statistical mechanics, especially of phase transitions have to be reconsidered without the use of the thermodynamic limit. Then thermo-statistics applies also to nuclei and large astronomical systems. A lot of similarity exists between the accessible phase space of fragmenting nuclei and inhomogeneous multi stellar systems.

## Key claim

Heat can flow from cold to hot at any phase separation, which means Clausius's formulation of the second law ("heat always flows from hot to cold") is violated at phase separations even in macroscopic systems; this requires reconsideration of Lynden-Bell's gravo-thermal catastrophe because systems with negative heat capacity can equilibrate rather than run away.

## Method

The paper develops a microcanonical statistical mechanics without the thermodynamic limit, using the Boltzmann-Planck entropy definition \(S = k \ln W\) where \(W\) measures the phase-space volume of configurations consistent with fixed energy, particle number, and volume. It analyzes the curvature of the microcanonical entropy \(S(E)\) as a function of energy to identify phase separations, showing that convexity of \(S(E)\) (positive second derivative) and the resulting negative heat capacity are the generic and necessary signals of phase separation.

## Key numbers

- The entropy change for an ideal gas expanding from volume \(V_1\) to \(V_2\) is \(\Delta S = N \ln(V_2/V_1)\).
- The heat capacity at constant volume for an extensive system is given by \(C_V(E) = \partial E/\partial T = -(\partial S/\partial E)^2 / (\partial^2 S/\partial E^2) > 0\).
- At phase separation, the heat capacity at the energy minimum \(E_{min}\) becomes negative: \(C_V(E_{min}) = \partial E/\partial T = -(\partial S/\partial E)^2 / (\partial^2 S/\partial E^2) < 0\).
- The relative width of the canonical energy for a homogeneous phase scales as \(\Delta E(T)_\alpha \propto 1/\sqrt{N_\alpha}\).

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical approach to thermodynamics developed by Boltzmann (1872) and Clausius (1854, 1865), and directly challenges Lynden-Bell's (1968) gravo-thermal catastrophe argument for self-gravitating systems. It contradicts the standard assumption that canonical and microcanonical ensembles are equivalent for all systems, showing that at phase separations they are not equivalent and that negative heat capacities appear generically. The paper enables the study of phase transitions in finite systems such as fragmenting nuclei and multi-star systems, where the thermodynamic limit cannot be applied, and connects to experimental observations of negative heat capacities in nuclear fragmentation (d'Agostino, 2000) and atomic clusters (Schmidt, 2001).
