# Spin observables for the pd <-> pi+ t process around the Delta resonance

> The proton analyzing power Ay0 and the deuteron tensor analyzing power T20 are evaluated for the pd <-> pi+ t process, in the energy region around and above the Delta resonance. These calculations extend a previous analysis of the excitation function and differential cross-section, based on a model embodying one-- and two-body p-wave absorption mechanisms and isobar excitation. The three-nucleon bound state and the pd scattering state are evaluated through Faddeev techniques for both the Bonn and Paris potentials. The spin variables exhibit a greater sensitivity to the number of included three-nucleon partial waves than the cross-sections, while the role played by the initial-- or final-state interactions appears to be small. The results for the tensor analyzing power at backward angles show a non-negligible dependence on the potentials employed, consistently with what has been previously found for the cross-sections. The calculation of spin observables gives a clear indication that other reaction mechanisms (presumably s-wave two-body absorption) have to be included in the model, in order to reproduce the experimental data below the Delta-resonance, in analogy with the simpler pp <-> pi+ d process.

# Spin observables for the pd <-> pi+ t process around the Delta resonance

## Key claim
The proton analyzing power Ay0 and deuteron tensor analyzing power T20 for the pd <-> pi+ t reaction are calculated using a model with one- and two-body p-wave absorption mechanisms and isobar excitation, but the results indicate that additional reaction mechanisms (presumably s-wave two-body absorption) must be included to reproduce experimental data below the Delta resonance, in analogy with the simpler pp <-> pi+ d process.

## Method
The authors extended a previous analysis of excitation functions and differential cross-sections by calculating spin observables (Ay0 and T20) from the same transition amplitudes. The three-nucleon bound state and pd scattering state were evaluated through Faddeev-AGS techniques using both the Bonn and Paris potentials, with the transition amplitudes decomposed into three-nucleon partial waves while the pion-tritium motion was represented as a three-dimensional plane-wave state. The helicity formalism of Jacob and Wick was employed to transform the amplitudes from a jI coupling scheme into a channel-spin representation, from which the spin observables were derived.

## Key numbers
- The Faddeev-AGS calculation of three-nucleon dynamics in the initial state gave a 4% effect in the cross section, and a comparable 4% effect was found when increasing from 82 to 464 three-nucleon partial waves.
- The excitation function for the Paris potential was about 25% lower than the Bonn B result.
- At the resonance peak, the isobar width was set to the experimental value of 115 MeV.
- The calculations were extended up to eta approximately 4 (pion-nucleus c.m. momentum in units of pion masses times c), with the phenomenological width parameterization valid below eta approximately 2.5.
- The differential cross-section results at backward angles showed a dip around eta approximately 3.6 that the model failed to reproduce.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Canton et al. (1997) on the pd <-> pi+ t reaction, which had previously studied only excitation functions and differential cross-sections. The spin observables exhibit greater sensitivity to the number of included three-nucleon partial waves than the cross-sections, particularly requiring inclusion of P and D orbital states for the intermediate Delta N subsystem. The results suggest that non-perturbative effects in the off-shell Delta propagation are important near the resonance but decrease at higher energies, and that s-wave pion absorption mechanisms (involving pi rescattering on a second nucleon) need to be incorporated, as was shown for the simpler pp <-> pi+ d reaction by Canton et al. (1996) and others.
