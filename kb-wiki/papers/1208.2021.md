# Electromagnetic Structure of the Pion

> In this work, we analyze the electromagnetic structure of the pion. We calculate its electromagnetic radius and electromagnetic form factor in low and intermediate momentum range. Such observables are determined by means of a theoretical model that takes into account the constituent quark and antiquark of the pion within the formalism of light-front field theory. In particular, we consider a nonsymmetrical vertex in this model, with which we calculate the electromagnetic form factor of the pion in an optimized way, so that we obtain a value closer to the experimental charge radius of the pion. The theoretical calculations are also compared with the most recent experimental data involving the pion electromagnetic form factor and the results show very good agreement.

## Key claim

The nonsymmetrical vertex (NSV) model within light-front field theory, when tuned with a regulator mass of 1.0 GeV, yields a pion charge radius and electromagnetic form factor that agree with experimental data to within 0.2% deviation, but only for regulator masses between 0.8 GeV and 1.3 GeV; outside this range the model breaks down significantly.

## Method

The authors calculated the pion electromagnetic form factor and charge radius using a light-front field theory model with a nonsymmetrical vertex regulator function. They employed the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame, with constituent quark masses set to 0.220 GeV, and varied the regulator mass parameter m_R from 0.5 to 1.3 GeV to optimize agreement with experimental data. The pion decay constant was also computed as a consistency check.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius obtained with m_R = 0.946 GeV: ⟨r_π+⟩ ≈ 0.689 fm, about 2% larger than the experimental value of (0.672 ± 0.008) fm.
- Pion charge radius obtained with m_R = 1.0 GeV: ⟨r_π+⟩ ≈ 0.673 fm, with a 0.2% deviation from the experimental value.
- Pion decay constant for m_R = 1.0 GeV: f_π = 93.1 MeV, compared to the experimental value f_π^exp = 92.2 MeV.
- Constituent quark masses used: m_u = m_¯d = 0.220 GeV; pion mass: m_π = 0.140 GeV.
- Regulator mass values tested: m_R = 0.5, 0.8, 0.946, 1.0, and 1.3 GeV; form factor calculations extended up to Q^2 = 10 (GeV/c)^2.

## Context

This work extends the nonsymmetrical vertex model previously reported by Pacheco (1999) and applies it within the light-front formalism developed by Brodsky and others. The results are compared with experimental data from Brauel (1979), Tadevosyan (2001), Huber (2008), and Horn (2008). The model provides a framework for describing pion electromagnetic properties that can be extended to other light mesons or applied to studies of pion structure in reactions such as antiproton-proton annihilation.
