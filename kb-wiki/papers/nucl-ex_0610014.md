# Experimental observables on nuclear liquid gas phase transition

> Progress on nuclear liquid gas phase transition (LGPT) or critical behavior has been simply reviewed and some signals of LGPT in heavy ion collisions, especially in NIMROD data, are focused. These signals include the power-law charge distribution, the largest fluctuation of the fragment observables, the nuclear Zipf law, caloric curve and critical exponent analysis etc.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental signals of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT) in heavy-ion collisions, focusing on NIMROD data for quasi-projectile fragmentation of Ar + Al, Ti, and Ni at 47 MeV/nucleon, and argues that multiple coherent observables, rather than any single signal, are required to corroborate the observation of a phase transition or critical behavior in finite nuclei.

## Method

The author analyzes quasi-projectile (QP) fragments reconstructed from NIMROD 4π detector data using three-source fits and Monte Carlo sampling. Fragment charge distributions are fitted with a power law (Fisher droplet model) to extract the effective exponent τeff, and with an exponential form after removing the heaviest fragment. Zipf plots (mean fragment size vs. rank) are fitted with a power law to extract the Zipf parameter λ. The caloric curve is constructed from temperature vs. excitation energy, and critical exponents β, γ, and σ are extracted from scaling relations using the deduced critical temperature Tc = 8.3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Minimum τeff ≈ 2.3 observed in the excitation energy range 5–6 MeV/nucleon for QP from ⁴⁰Ar + ⁵⁸Ni at 47 MeV/nucleon, close to the Fisher droplet model prediction of τ ≈ 2.21.
- Peak in mean normalized second moment ⟨S₂⟩ and maximum in normalized variance of Zmax/ZQP (NVZ) occur around E*/A = 5.6 MeV/nucleon.
- Zipf law parameter λ ≈ 1 in the 5–6 MeV/nucleon excitation energy range for the same system.
- Critical temperature Tc = 8.3 ± 0.5 MeV determined from the caloric curve in the critical region at 5.6 MeV/nucleon excitation energy.
- Critical exponents extracted: β = 0.33 ± 0.01, γ = 1.15 ± 0.06, σ = 0.68 ± 0.04, compared to liquid-gas universality class values (β = 0.33, γ = 1.23, σ = 0.64) and 3D percolation values (β = 0.41, γ = 1.8, σ = 0.45).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on nuclear multifragmentation as a critical phenomenon, following the Purdue group's pioneering application of the Fisher droplet model to proton-nucleus collisions (Minich et al., 1982). The NIMROD results are compared with prior caloric curve measurements in heavier nuclei (Natowitz et al., 1997) and with critical exponent analyses from the EOS collaboration. The paper enables future searches for LGPT in finite nuclear systems by demonstrating that multiple coherent signals, including power-law charge distributions, fluctuation peaks, Zipf law, and critical exponents, must be observed together to confirm a phase transition.
