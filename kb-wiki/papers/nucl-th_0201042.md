# In-medium vector meson properties and low-mass dilepton production from hot hadronic matter

> The in-medium properties of the vector mesons are known to be modified significantly in hot and dense hadronic matter due to vacuum polarisation effects from the baryon sector in the Walecka model. The vector meson mass drops significantly in the medium due to the effects of the Dirac sea. In the variational approach adopted in the present paper, these effects are taken into account through a realignment of the ground state with baryon condensates. Such a realignment of the ground state becomes equivalent to summing of the baryonic tadpole diagrams in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). The approximation scheme adopted here goes beyond RHA to include quantum effects from the scalar meson and is nonperturbative and self--consistent. It includes multiloop effects, thus corresponding to a different approximation as compared to the one loop approximation of including scalar field quantum corrections. In the present work, we study the properties of the vector mesons in the hot and dense matter as modified due to such quantum correction effects from the baryon as well as scalar meson sectors. These medium modifications of the properties of the vector mesons are reflected, through the shifting and broadening of the respective peaks, in the low mass dilepton spectra. There is broadening of the peaks due to corrections from scalar meson quantum effects as compared to the relativistic Hartree approximation. It is seen to be rather prominent for the $ω$ meson in the invariant mass plot.

# In-medium vector meson properties and low-mass dilepton production from hot hadronic matter

## Key claim

The vector meson masses drop significantly in hot and dense hadronic matter due to Dirac sea effects, but including quantum corrections from the scalar meson sector (beyond the relativistic Hartree approximation) partially counteracts this drop, yielding higher vector meson masses and broader spectral peaks than predicted by the relativistic Hartree approximation alone.

## Method

The authors employ a nonperturbative variational approach within the Walecka model, using thermofield dynamics to study the finite-temperature ground state with baryon and sigma condensates. The vector meson self-energies are computed in the relativistic random phase approximation, including vacuum polarization from the baryon sector (Dirac sea) and quantum effects from the scalar meson sector via sigma condensates, which sum multiloop diagrams self-consistently. The in-medium masses are obtained by solving the dispersion relation including the real part of the self-energy, and decay widths are calculated for the processes ρ → ππ, ω → 3π, and ω → ρπ (the latter becoming kinematically allowed in medium).

## Key numbers

- At saturation density and zero temperature, the omega meson mass drop from its vacuum value is approximately 150 MeV in RHA, reducing to about 117 MeV for λ_R = 1.8 and 61 MeV for λ_R = 5 when scalar quantum effects are included.
- The quartic scalar self-coupling λ_R is taken as 1.8 and 5, yielding nuclear matter incompressibilities of 401 MeV and 329 MeV respectively (compared to 545 MeV in mean field and 450 MeV in RHA).
- The scaled coupling constants for λ_R = 0 (RHA), 1.8, and 5 are: C_S^2 = 183.3, 167.5, and 137.9; C_V^2 = 114.7, 96.45, and 63.7 respectively.
- The ρ meson decay width in vacuum (Γ_ρ = 151 MeV) is used to fix the g_{ρππ} coupling.
- The ω → 3π partial decay width in vacuum (7.49 MeV) is used to fix the g_{ω3π} coupling to 0.24.
- The g_{ωρπ} coupling is taken as 2.
- The ρ meson mass modification from pion loops is at most 1%.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vector meson in-medium modifications within Quantum Hadrodynamics, specifically the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) calculations by Hatsuda (1992), Hatsuda et al. (1993), and Jean et al. (1994) which showed that Dirac sea contributions dominate over Fermi sea effects in reducing vector meson masses. The authors build on their own nonperturbative variational framework (Mishra et al., 1999, 2000) that includes sigma condensates, going beyond the one-loop scalar quantum corrections considered by Serot and Pal (1998). The results contradict the simple universal scaling hypothesis of Brown and Rho (1991) and are relevant for interpreting low-mass dilepton enhancement observed in experiments such as HELIOS, CERES, and DLS, where the broadening of the ω peak due to scalar quantum effects may lead to its smearing and ultimate disappearance at finite densities as suggested by Weise (1999).
