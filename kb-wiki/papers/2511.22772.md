# Relations between three-particle interactions in nuclear matter to observable quantities

> In the first part of this paper, we use the framework of the Fermi liquid theory to derive model-independent relations between the slope parameters of the symmetry energy and of the incompressibility in nuclear matter to three-particle interaction parameters. Based on these relations, we present simple estimates and compare with the empirical information. In the second part, we discuss the general structure of the three-particle scattering amplitude in nuclear matter, and use methods similar to the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone theory to show how three-particle cluster diagrams emerge naturally in the Fermi liquid theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact, model-independent relations between the slope parameters of the symmetry energy (L) and of the incompressibility (J) in nuclear matter and three-particle interaction parameters within Fermi liquid theory, but the actual numerical estimates rely on semi-quantitative approximations and the assumption that higher-order terms in the Faddeev series may be needed to match empirical values.

## Method

The authors used the field-theoretic formulation of Fermi liquid theory due to Landau and Migdal to derive relations between the slope parameters L and J and the ℓ=0 moments of the three-nucleon forward scattering amplitude. They then made semi-quantitative estimates by approximating the leading term in the Faddeev series for the three-particle amplitude using simple two-body contact interactions of the Landau-Migdal type, and compared these estimates to empirical values from heavy nuclei, astrophysical observations, and heavy ion collisions. In the second part, they used methods similar to the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone theory to expand the self energy in powers of the ladder t-matrix and derive the structure of the three-particle scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* J = -255 ± 245 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (ρ = 0.155 fm⁻³), from Fig. 4 of Cai (2014)
* L = 59 ± 16 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, from Fig. 20 of Li (2018)
* K = 250 MeV (canonical value of incompressibility at normal nuclear matter density)
* a_s = 32 MeV (canonical value of symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density)
* M*/M = 0.85 ± 0.15 (range for the Landau effective mass at the Fermi surface)
* μ = 0.27 ± 0.25 (dimensionless parameter related to the isovector effective mass splitting, from Li (2018))
* H_0^(2pc) values for three extended Skyrme interaction sets: 0.528 (Set 1, M*/M=0.7), 0.762 (Set 2, M*/M=0.8), 1.095 (Set 3, M*/M=0.9)
* H_0'^(2pc) values: -0.063 (Set 1), -0.101 (Set 2), -0.135 (Set 3)

## Context

This work extends the Fermi liquid theory approach of Landau (1956, 1957) and Migdal (1967) to derive exact relations between three-body interactions and nuclear matter observables, building on earlier work by Bentz (2019, 2020). The results connect to the long history of three-particle interaction studies in nuclear matter by Bethe (1965) and Day (1981), and provide a framework for interpreting empirical constraints on the symmetry energy and incompressibility from heavy ion collisions, neutron star observations, and nuclear structure. The second part makes explicit connections to the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone theory of Bethe (1971), showing how three-particle cluster diagrams emerge naturally from the Fermi liquid theory, and suggests that higher-order terms beyond the leading Faddeev series may be important for understanding the skewness of nuclear matter.
