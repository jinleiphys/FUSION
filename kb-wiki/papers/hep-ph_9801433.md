# Equivalence of renormalized covariant and light-front perturbation theory: II. Transverse divergences in the Yukawa model

> Light-front dynamics can only become a viable alternative to the covariant approach if doubts about its covariance can be taken away. As a minimal requirement we take that the physical quantities calculated with light-front perturbation theory are the same as those obtained using covariant perturbation theory. If this situation occurs, we use the word equivalent to characterize it. For quantities that involve the calculation of superficially convergent diagrams, proofs of equivalence exist. For some types of divergent diagrams the proof of equivalence is complicated. Longitudinal divergences were dealt with in Part I. (hep-ph/9702311). In Part II we deal with diagrams with transverse divergences. Our method is based on minus regularization, which is inspired on BPHZ regularization. In a calculation using numerical methods we show how to obtain a rotationally invariant amplitude for two triangle diagrams contributing to the decay of a scalar boson in the Yukawa model. It concludes our proof of equivalence of covariant and light-front perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for two triangle diagrams in the Yukawa model (the fermion triangle and the one-boson exchange correction), minus regularization applied to light-front time-ordered diagrams yields a rotationally invariant amplitude that matches the covariant BPHZ-regularized result, contrary to earlier claims that naive light-cone quantization violates rotational invariance.

## Method

The authors apply minus regularization, a method inspired by BPHZ regularization, to the light-front time-ordered diagrams obtained by integrating covariant Feynman amplitudes over the light-front energy variable k^- using contour integration. For the two triangle diagrams with transverse divergences, they numerically evaluate the regularized light-front amplitudes using Fortran with IMSL Gaussian integration routines and compare the sum of all time-ordered contributions to the covariant BPHZ-regularized result for the decay of a scalar boson at rest.

## Key numbers

* The difference between the sum of the six minus-regularized light-front time-ordered fermion triangle diagrams and the covariant BPHZ-regularized result has a maximum of 0.03% (using MR1 method and 128 points per integration variable).
* Two of the six light-front time-ordered diagrams (those with one instantaneous fermion) are so small they require a scale enlarged by a factor of 100 to be visible in the figures.
* The sum of the six light-front time-ordered amplitudes for the fermion triangle is independent of the angle theta (the angle between the momentum of one outgoing boson and the z-axis), with the thick line in the figure representing a constant value of 125.

## Context

This work extends the proof of equivalence between covariant and light-front perturbation theory from convergent diagrams and self-energy diagrams (treated in Part I, hep-ph/9702311) to more complicated triangle diagrams with transverse divergences. It directly addresses the claim by Burkardt and Langnau (1991) that naive light-cone quantization leads to rotational invariance violation in the Yukawa model, showing instead that minus regularization restores covariance. The method enables future application of light-front perturbation theory to processes involving transverse divergences while maintaining full equivalence to covariant results.
