# Impacts of EMC effects on the D meson modification factor in equilibrating QGP

> In this article we employ the nuclear EMC effect to extract the parton distribution functions (PDFs) inside the Lead (Pb) and Gold (Au) nuclei. Extracted PDFs are utilized to obtain the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) ones, using the computing codes like Pythia 8 or MCFM-10. Through this procedure TMDPDFs for charm and bottom quarks in Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\;GeV$, Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\;TeV$ and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\;TeV$ are calculated. To evaluate the validity of results and investigate the influence of nuclear EMC effect, the numerated TMDs are used as input to estimate heavy quark modification factor $R_{AA}$ at transverse plane $P_T$. This observable is calculated through numerical solution of the Fokker-Planck equation. For this purpose we need to extract the drag and diffusion coefficients, using the hard thermal loop correction. It is done in the frame work of the relativistic hydrodynamics up to the third order approximation of gradient expansion. The results are compared with same solutions when the input PFDs are considered inside the unbounded protons where the nuclear effect is not included. The comparison indicates a significant improvement of computed $R_{AA}$ with available experimental data when the EMC effect is considered.

## Key claim
Including the nuclear EMC effect in the initial parton distribution functions (PDFs) for heavy quarks significantly improves the agreement of the computed D meson nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ with experimental data from RHIC and LHC, compared to calculations using free-proton PDFs without nuclear effects.

## Method
The authors extracted nuclear PDFs for Lead (Pb) and Gold (Au) using the nCTEQ15 model via the APFEL legacy program, then generated transverse-momentum-dependent PDFs (TMDPDFs) for charm and bottom quarks using Pythia 8. These TMDPDFs served as initial conditions for solving the Fokker-Planck equation to evolve heavy quark distributions through an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma, with drag and diffusion coefficients computed using hard thermal loop (HTL) corrections and third-order relativistic hydrodynamics for the medium evolution.

## Key numbers
- For Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the minimum $\chi^2$ for $R_{AA}$ fitting (with $P_T > 1.5$ GeV) was 3.04 with EMC effect and 10.57 without, using fitted parameters $\lambda_g=0.51$, $K=0.83$, $T_0=378$ MeV.
- For Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, the minimum $\chi^2$ (for $P_T > 3$ GeV) was 0.09 with EMC effect and 3.25 without, using $\lambda_g=0.41$ (with EMC) or 0.48 (without), $K=1.0$, $T_0=395$ MeV.
- For Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, the minimum $\chi^2$ was 0.02 with EMC effect and 1.07 without, using $\lambda_g=0.38$ (with EMC) or 0.40 (without), $K=1.3$, $T_0=403$ MeV.
- The freeze-out temperature was taken as $T_c=155$ MeV for LHC and $T_c=170$ MeV for RHIC.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of heavy quark energy loss and $R_{AA}$ in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., LBT and Duke group results) by incorporating the nuclear EMC effect into the initial parton distributions, which had not been done previously. The approach enables investigation of how the choice of colliding ion species (Au vs. Pb) and the nuclear medium environment affect heavy quark observables, and provides a framework for comparing calculations with experimental data from STAR, ALICE, and CMS collaborations.
