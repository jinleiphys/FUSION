# Microscopic Structure of High-Spin Vibrational States in Superdeformed A=190 Nuclei

> Microscopic RPA calculations based on the cranked shell model are performed to investigate the quadrupole and octupole correlations for excited superdeformed (SD) bands in even-even A=190 nuclei. The K=2 octupole vibrations are predicted to be the lowest excitation modes at zero rotational frequency. The Coriolis coupling at finite frequency produces different effects depending on the neutron and proton number of nucleus. The calculations also indicate that some collective excitations may produce J(2) moments of inertia almost identical to those of the yrast SD band. An interpretation of the observed excited bands invoking the octupole vibrations is proposed, which suggests those octupole vibrations may be prevalent in even-even SD A=190 nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that most observed excited superdeformed (SD) bands in even-even A=190 nuclei correspond to K=2 octupole vibrational states, rather than two-quasiparticle excitations, based on microscopic RPA calculations that predict these octupole vibrations are the lowest excitation modes at zero rotational frequency.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations based on the cranked shell model, using a Nilsson Hamiltonian with pairing fields and separable multipole residual interactions. The quadrupole deformation and pairing gaps were determined by the standard Strutinsky procedure at zero rotational frequency, with a phenomenological prescription for pairing gaps at finite frequency characterized by a critical frequency. The RPA eigenenergies were used to calculate the J(2) moments of inertia for excited SD bands by adding the relative difference j(2) = -d^2 hbar-Omega / d omega^2 to the experimental J(2) of the yrast band.

## Key numbers

- The K=2 octupole vibrations are predicted to have excitation energy E_x approximately 1 MeV and B(E3; 0+ -> 3-) approximately 10 single-particle units at zero rotational frequency in Hg isotopes.
- The gamma vibrations are predicted to be higher in energy (E_x greater than or equal to 1.4 MeV) and less collective (B(E2; 0+ -> 2+) less than 2-3 single-particle units) than the lowest octupole vibrations at zero rotational frequency.
- For Hg-194, the experimental Routhian of band 3 is approximately 0.8 MeV at zero rotational frequency, supporting the collective vibration interpretation over the predicted lowest two-quasiparticle states at approximately 1.5 MeV.
- In Pb-196, the band-head energy of bands 2 and 3 is around 870 keV, showing better agreement with calculations using octupole coupling strength f_3=1.05.
- For the lowest alpha=1 octupole state in Pb-196 at zero rotational frequency, the largest two-quasiparticle component has |psi|^2=0.14, while the largest component associated with neutron N=7 orbitals has |psi|^2=0.04.
- Experimental B(E1) values for Pb-196 are approximately 10^-6, 10^-5, and 10^-4 Weisskopf units for bands 2, 3, and 4 respectively, compared to calculated values of approximately 10^-7 and 10^-6 Weisskopf units for bands 2 and 3.
- The quadrupole deformation used for Pb calculations is epsilon=0.44, with pairing parameters Delta(0)=0.8 MeV for neutrons and 0.7 MeV for protons, both with hbar-omega_c=0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Nakatsukasa, Matsuyanagi, Mizutori, Shimizu, 1996) on quadrupole and octupole correlations in Hg-190,192,194 to all even-even SD nuclei observed in the A=190 region, including Pb-192,194,196,198. The paper contrasts the situation in SD nuclei, where collective states remain the lowest excitation modes even at high spin due to large shell gaps, with normal-deformed nuclei where aligned two-quasiparticle bands erase the energy gap and become dominant. The results enable experimental searches for octupole vibrational bands in Pb-192 and Pb-198, and suggest that extending observations to neutron numbers N less than 108 or N greater than 116 should reveal K=1 octupole vibrations with strong E1 decays, providing direct evidence for octupole collectivity.
