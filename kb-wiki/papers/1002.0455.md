# On the Spectrum of the Many-Body Pauli Projector

> Spectrum of the Pauli projector of a quantum many-body system is studied. It is proven that the kern of the complete many-body projector is identical to the kern of the sum of two-body projectors. Since the kern of the many-body Pauli projector defines an allowed subspace of the complete Hilbert space, it is argued that a truncation of the many-body model space following the two-body Pauli projectors is a natural way when solving the Schrödinger equation for the many-body system. These relations clarify a role of the many-body Pauli forces in a multicluster system.

## Key claim
The paper proves that the kernel (null space) of the sum of two-body Pauli projectors is identical to the kernel of the complete many-body Pauli projector, thereby justifying the truncation of the many-body model space using only two-body projectors even when the two-body projectors overlap strongly.

## Method
The author derives an algebraic relation between the complete many-body projector \(\hat{\Gamma}\) and the sum of two-body projectors \(\hat{P} = \sum_i \hat{P}_i\), showing that \(\hat{\Gamma} = 1 - \lim_{m \to \infty} (1 - \hat{P})^m\). Using mathematical induction and the non-commutativity of the two-body projectors, the proof establishes that any state in the kernel of \(\hat{P}\) is also in the kernel of \(\hat{\Gamma}\), and vice versa.

## Key numbers
- The overlap of the \(|0S\rangle\) forbidden states from different two-body subsystems in the \(3\alpha\) system is estimated as 1.367, indicating that terms like \(\hat{P}_i \hat{P}_j\) can give non-negligible contributions if they do not cancel.
- The paper references that in the \(3\alpha\) system, the BFW \(\alpha\alpha\) potential yields three Pauli forbidden states in partial waves \(|0S\rangle\), \(|2S\rangle\), and \(|2D\rangle\) for each two-body subsystem.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of Pauli projector methods in multicluster systems, particularly the \(3\alpha\) model of \(^{12}\)C, where earlier studies by Fujii (2006) identified "almost forbidden states" causing underbinding or overbinding. The result clarifies that the orthogonal condition model (OCM) and the method of orthogonalising pseudopotentials (OPP), which use only the first term of the complete projector expansion, are mathematically justified. It also explains why the supersymmetric transformation (SUSY) approach, used by Baye (1987), fails to reproduce experimental data for reactions such as \(\alpha + d \to ^6\text{Li} + \gamma\) as shown by Tursunov (2016, 2018, 2019), while OPP succeeds due to its retention of realistic nodal behavior.
