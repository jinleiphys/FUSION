# Evidence for charm-bottom tetraquarks and the mass dependence of heavy-light tetraquark states from lattice QCD

> We continue our study of heavy-light four-quark states and find evidence from lattice QCD for the existence of a strong-interaction-stable $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ $ud\bar{c}\bar{b}$ tetraquark with mass in the range of 15 to 61 MeV below $\bar{D}B^*$ threshold. Since this range includes the electromagnetic $\bar{D}Bγ$ decay threshold, current uncertainties do not allow us to determine whether such a state would decay electromagnetically, or only weakly. We also perform a study at fixed pion mass, with NRQCD for the heavy quarks, simulating $qq^\prime \bar{b}^\prime \bar{b}$ and $q q^\prime \bar{b}^\prime\bar{b}^\prime$ tetraquarks with $q,\, q^\prime =ud$ or $\ell s$ and variable, unphysical $m_{b^\prime}$ in order to investigate the heavy mass-dependence of such tetraquark states. We find that the dependence of the binding energy follows a phenomenologically-expected form and that, though NRQCD breaks down before $m_{b^\prime}=m_c$ is reached, the results at higher $m_{b^\prime}$ clearly identify the $ud\bar{b}^\prime \bar{b}$ channel as the most likely to support a strong-interaction-stable tetraquark state at $m_{b^\prime}=m_c$. This observation serves to motivate the direct $ud\bar{c}\bar{b}$ simulation. Throughout we use dynamical $n_f=2+1$ ensembles with pion masses $m_π=$415, 299, and 164 MeV reaching down almost to the physical point, a relativistic heavy quark prescription for the charm quark, and NRQCD for the bottom quark(s).

## Key claim

Lattice QCD provides evidence for a strong-interaction-stable $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ $ud\bar{c}\bar{b}$ tetraquark with mass 15 to 61 MeV below the $\bar{D}B^*$ threshold, but current uncertainties do not allow determination of whether this state would decay electromagnetically (via $\bar{D}B\gamma$) or only weakly, since the electromagnetic threshold lies within this binding energy range.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations using dynamical $n_f=2+1$ ensembles with pion masses 415, 299, and 164 MeV, employing a relativistic heavy quark prescription for the charm quark and NRQCD for the bottom quark(s). They construct $3\times 3$ and $2\times 2$ generalized eigenvalue problems (GEVPs) using diquark-antidiquark and meson-meson interpolating operators, extracting binding energies from single-exponential fits to the smallest eigenvalues of binding correlators. A separate variable-heavy-mass study uses NRQCD for unphysical bottom quarks with masses ranging from 0.60 to 6.29 times the physical bottom quark mass, fitting the resulting binding energies to phenomenologically motivated forms that incorporate color-Coulomb attraction, good-diquark binding, and residual heavy-light interactions.

## Key numbers

* The $ud\bar{c}\bar{b}$ tetraquark binding energy relative to $\bar{D}B^*$ threshold is in the range 15 to 61 MeV, corresponding to a mass of approximately 7154(23) MeV.
* The variable-heavy-mass study on the $E_M$ ensemble ($m_\pi=299$ MeV) yields binding energies for $ud\bar{b}'\bar{b}$ ranging from -86(10) MeV at $m_{b'}/m_b=0.594$ to -210(18) MeV at $m_{b'}/m_b=6.287$, and for $ud\bar{b}'\bar{b}'$ from -76(11) MeV to -438(20) MeV over the same mass range.
* The fit parameters for the heavy-quark mass dependence on the $E_M$ ensemble are $C_0=-82(6)$ MeV, $C_1^{ud}=-217(14)$ MeV, $C_2^{ud}=40(5)$ MeV, $C_1^{\ell s}=-116(10)$ MeV, and $C_2^{\ell s}=22(3)$ MeV.
* The NRQCD approximation is found to be reliable for $m_Q \gtrsim 0.594\, m_b$, with the charm quark at approximately $m_c\simeq 0.33\, m_b$ lying beyond the accessible range.
* The final fit ranges used for the $ud\bar{c}\bar{b}$ analysis are $t/a\in[10:23]$ for $E_L$, $t/a\in[14:20]$ for $E_M$, and $t/a\in[17:21]$ for $E_H$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior lattice QCD prediction of $ud\bar{b}\bar{b}$ and $\ell s\bar{b}\bar{b}$ tetraquarks (Francis 2016) to the charm-bottom sector, confirming the phenomenological expectation that tetraquark binding decreases as the heavy anti-diquark reduced mass decreases. The variable-heavy-mass study validates the physical picture where binding arises from color-Coulomb attraction between heavy antiquarks, good-light-diquark attraction, and residual heavy-light interactions, with the $ud\bar{c}\bar{b}$ channel identified as the most likely among the four possible charm-bottom combinations to support a strong-interaction-stable state. The results motivate experimental searches at LHCb, where $B_c$ production is already established, and suggest that $ud\bar{c}\bar{c}$, $\ell s\bar{c}\bar{b}$, and $\ell s\bar{c}\bar{c}$ tetraquarks are unlikely to be bound, consistent with Cheung (2017).
