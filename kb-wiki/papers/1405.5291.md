# Effective action for Bose-Einstein condensates

> We clarify basic properties of an effective action (i.e., self-consistent perturbation expansion) for interacting Bose-Einstein condensates, where field $ψ$ itself acquires a finite thermodynamic average $\langle ψ\rangle$ besides two-point Green's function $\hat G$ to form an off-diagonal long-range order. It is shown that the action can be expressed concisely order by order in terms of the interaction vertex and a special combination of $\langleψ\rangle$ and $\hat G$ so as to satisfy both Noether's theorem and Goldstone's theorem (I) corresponding to the first proof. The self-energy is predicted to have a one-particle-reducible structure due to $\langle ψ\rangle\neq 0$ to transform the Bogoliubov mode into a bubbling mode with a substantial decay rate.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the effective action (self-consistent perturbation expansion) for interacting Bose-Einstein condensates can be expressed concisely as a functional of a single combined quantity \(\hat{\cal G}\) that incorporates both the condensate wave function and the Green's function, thereby satisfying both Noether's theorem and Goldstone's theorem (I) simultaneously, but only if the self-energy is allowed to include one-particle-reducible diagrams that standard two-particle-irreducible approaches exclude.

## Method

The author extends the Luttinger-Ward \(\Phi\)-derivable formalism to Bose-Einstein condensates by constructing the \(\Phi\) functional order by order in the interaction, starting from normal-state diagrams and systematically adding anomalous diagrams characteristic of off-diagonal long-range order. The unknown weights of these diagrams are determined by requiring that the identities derived from the interaction energy and stationarity conditions be satisfied, which forces the inclusion of one-particle-reducible diagrams beyond the conventional two-particle-irreducible (2PI) category used in the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism.

## Key numbers

- The first-order coefficients for the \(\Phi^{(1)}\) functional are determined as \(c^{(1)}_{2b} = -1\), \(c^{(1)}_{1a} = -4\), and \(c^{(1)}_{1b} = 1\).
- The second-order coefficients for the \(\Phi^{(2)}\) functional include \(c^{(2)}_{4b} = -2\), \(c^{(2)}_{4c} = 1\), \(c^{(2)}_{3a} = -4\), \(c^{(2)}_{3b} = 2\), \(c^{(2)}_{3c} = 4\), \(c^{(2)}_{3d} = -2\), and \(c^{(2)}_{2a} = c^{(2)}_{2b} = 2\), \(c^{(2)}_{2c} = -4\), \(c^{(2)}_{2d} = 2\), \(c^{(2)}_{2e} = 1\).
- The self-energy is predicted to transform the Bogoliubov mode into a bubbling mode with a decay rate proportional to the \(s\)-wave scattering length \(a\) in the dilute limit.

## Context

This work extends the Luttinger-Ward (1960) and Baym (1962) conserving approximation scheme to Bose-Einstein condensates, resolving the "conserving vs. gapless" dilemma first pointed out by Hohenberg and Martin (1965). It contradicts the standard two-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action approach of Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis (1974) by showing that one-particle-reducible diagrams are necessary to satisfy both Noether's theorem and Goldstone's theorem (I) simultaneously. The formalism enables systematic higher-order calculations beyond Bogoliubov theory (1947) and the Lee-Huang-Yang (1957) corrections, predicting modifications to the ground-state energy and condensate density of dilute Bose gases, as well as a finite lifetime for the single-particle excitation mode.
