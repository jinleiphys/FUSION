# Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculation for Rotating Bose-Einstein Condensates

> A rotating bosonic many-body system in a harmonic trap is studied with the 3D-Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method at zero temperature, which has been applied to nuclear many-body systems at high spin. This method is a variational method extended from the Hartree-Fock theory, which can treat the pairing correlations in a self-consistent manner. An advantage of this method is that a finite-range interaction between constituent particles can be used in the calculation, unlike the original Gross-Pitaevskii approach. To demonstrate the validity of our method, we present a calculation for a toy model, that is, a rotating system of ten bosonic particles interacting through the repulsive quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in a harmonic trap. It is found that the yrast states, the lowest-energy states for the given total angular momentum, does not correspond to the Bose-Einstein condensate, except a few special cases. One of such cases is a vortex state, which appears when the total angular momentum $L$ is twice the particle number $N$ (i.e., $L=2N$).

# Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculation for Rotating Bose-Einstein Condensates

## Key claim

The yrast states (lowest-energy states for a given total angular momentum) of a rotating bosonic system in a harmonic trap do not correspond to a Bose-Einstein condensate except at L=0 and L=2N, where N is the particle number; the vortex state at L=2N is one such special case. This conclusion is drawn from a toy model of ten bosons with repulsive quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and the authors note that the model is "too simple to describe the detailed structure of realistic systems."

## Method

The authors applied the three-dimensional Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (3D-cranked HFB) method at zero temperature, originally developed for nuclear many-body systems at high spin, to a rotating bosonic system of ten particles in an isotropic harmonic oscillator potential. The two-body interaction was taken as a repulsive quadrupole-quadrupole interaction with no pairing term (g=0), and the single-particle basis consisted of harmonic oscillator states up to the 0h shell. The HFB state was determined through a variational principle with seven constraints: three components of total angular momentum, three quadrupole operators to fix the intrinsic axes, and the particle number, using a steepest-descent method with a convergence criterion of |d| < 1.0 x 10^{-7}.

## Key numbers

- Particle number: N = 10 bosons in the toy model
- Oscillator energy: hbar omega = 1.9 meV
- Quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strengths tested: kappa/A^2 = 0.1, 0.5, and 1.0 meV/micrometer^4
- Total angular momentum range explored: L from 0 to 20 (in steps of Delta L = 0.05)
- Vortex state appears at L = 2N = 20 (in units of hbar)
- Ratio Nv/hbar omega estimated to be of order 10^{-4} to 10^{-5} for the three interaction strengths, indicating a weakly interacting system
- Convergence criterion: |d| < epsilon = 1.0 x 10^{-7}

## Context

This work extends the 3D-cranked HFB method, previously applied to nuclear high-spin physics (3dCHFB, 3dCHFB2), to bosonic many-body systems as an alternative to the Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) equation approach. The authors build on Mottelson's prediction (Mottelson, 1999) that quadrupole excitation dominates at low angular momentum and that all bosons occupy the p state when L=N, which was numerically verified by Mottelson and collaborators using the 2D GP equation and by Bertsch and Papenbrock (2000) using exact diagonalization. The method enables the use of finite-range interactions (unlike the delta-function interaction in the GP equation) and does not assume a priori existence of a condensate, allowing the study of non-condensate yrast states. The results enable future investigations of more realistic inter-atomic potentials and the effect of pairing interactions in rotating bosonic systems.
