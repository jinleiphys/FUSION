# Color Screening and the Suppression of the Charmonium State Yield in Nuclear Reactions

> We discuss the new data for the production of the $ψ'$ meson in pA collisions at 450 GeV at CERN-SPS (of the NA50-collaboration) [1]. We extract from the CERN data $σ(ψ' N)\approx 8$ mb under the assumption that the $ψ'$ is produced as a result of the space-time evolution of a point-like $c\bar c$ pair which expands with time to the full size of the charmonium state. In the analysis we assume the existence of a relationship between the distribution of color in a hadron and the cross section of its interaction with a nucleon. However, our result is rather sensitive to the pattern of the expansion of the wave packet and significantly larger values of $σ(ψ' N)$ are not ruled out by the data. We show that recent CERN data confirm the suggestion of [2] that color fluctuations of the strengths in charmonium-nucleon interaction are the major source of suppression of the $J/ψ$ yield as observed at CERN in both pA and AA collisions.

# Color Screening and the Suppression of the Charmonium State Yield in Nuclear Reactions

## Key claim

The NA50 data for ψ' production in pA collisions at 450 GeV can be fitted with a ψ'-nucleon cross section of approximately 8 mb under the assumption that the ψ' is produced as a point-like c-cbar pair that expands with time, but significantly larger values of σ(ψ'N) are not ruled out by the data due to sensitivity to the expansion pattern of the wave packet.

## Method

The authors analyze NA50 data for ψ' production in pA collisions at 450 GeV using a semiclassical Glauber approximation that accounts for the space-time evolution of color fluctuations. They model charmonium states as point-like c-cbar pairs that expand linearly with distance from the production point over a formation length, with the cross section increasing from a small initial value to the full hadronic cross section. The suppression factor S is calculated by integrating over nuclear density profiles and accounting for the decay of higher charmonium resonances (χ, ψ') into J/ψ.

## Key numbers

- σ(ψ'N) ≈ 8 mb extracted from CERN data under the point-like expansion assumption
- Average transverse distances: √⟨r²⟩_{J/ψ} = 0.38 fm, √⟨r²⟩_χ = 0.57 fm, √⟨r²⟩_{ψ'} = 0.76 fm from charmonium wave functions of Eich (1985)
- σ(J/ψ-N) = 3.5 ± 0.8 mb from SLAC photoproduction data at E_inc ≈ 20 GeV
- Formation length of J/ψ at SPS energies: l_f ≈ 3 fm (proper formation time τ_f = 0.3 fm) for p = 30 GeV
- Constituent quark model predictions: σ(Cornell) = 2.2 mb (J/ψ), 9.1 mb (ψ'), 3.5-6.9 mb (χ); σ(log. potential) = 2.7 mb (J/ψ), 13 mb (ψ'), 4.4-8.7 mb (χ)
- Hard pQCD cross sections (b < 0.35 fm): σ(hard) = 0.42 mb (J/ψ), 0.13 mb (ψ'), 0.28-0.41 mb (χ)
- π-N cross section used for normalization: σ(πN) = 25 mb at both E_lab = 450 GeV and 920 GeV

## Context

This work extends the color fluctuation model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1985) and the earlier analysis of Gerland et al. (2000) to new NA50 data, confirming that color fluctuations in charmonium-nucleon interactions are the major source of J/ψ suppression in both pA and AA collisions. The results contradict the Vector Dominance Model prediction that σ(ψ'N) ≈ 0.7·σ(J/ψ-N), and instead support the QCD prediction that interaction strength depends on the volume occupied by color. The analysis enables predictions for HERA B at E_lab = 920 GeV, where measurements over a larger Feynman x_F range, especially negative x_F, will test the formation time dependence and provide new constraints on the relation between meson size and hadron-hadron interaction strength.
