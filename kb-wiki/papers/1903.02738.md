# Gravitational form factors of a spin one particle

> We define the form factors of the quark and gluon symmetric energy-momentum tensor (EMT). The static EMT is related to the spatial distributions of energy, spin, pressure and shear forces. They are obtained in the form of a multipole expansion. The relations between gravitational form factors and the generalised parton distributions are given.

## Key claim

The paper defines the complete set of nine gravitational form factors (GFFs) for a spin-1 particle, including three energy-momentum tensor (EMT) non-conserving form factors that were either not discussed or incomplete in prior work. The authors explicitly note that for spin-1 particles, the multipole expansion of the static EMT yields additional quadrupole-order terms in the stress tensor beyond those for spin-0 and spin-1/2 cases.

## Method

The authors define the quark and gluon symmetric energy-momentum tensor form factors through matrix elements of the EMT operator between spin-1 particle states, using covariant normalization and Breit frame kinematics. They express the static EMT components (energy density, spin distribution, and stress tensor) in terms of multipole expansions involving spin and quadrupole operators. The relations between the nine GFFs and the vector generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are derived by considering the Mellin moments of the GPDs and comparing with the ++ components of the EMT.

## Key numbers

- The total EMT form factor A_0(0) = 1, giving the normalization condition for the energy-momentum sum rule
- The total spin form factor J(0) = 1, giving the normalization for the spin sum rule for a spin-1 particle
- Free theory values of total EMT form factors: E_0(t) = 1, E_2(t) = 0, J(t) = 1, D_0(t) = 1/3 - 4h, D_2(t) = -1, D_3(t) = 0, where h is a parameter from the non-minimal gravitational coupling
- The D-term is defined as D = D_0(0) = 1/3 - 4h
- For a particle with integer spin J, there are (4J+2) conserving and (2J+1) non-conserving EMT form factors

## Context

This work extends the formalism of gravitational form factors from spin-0 and spin-1/2 particles (Polyakov 2002, Polyakov 2018) to spin-1 particles, providing a complete definition that includes three EMT-nonconserving form factors missing in earlier treatments by Holstein (2006), Abidin (2008), and Taneja (2011). The results coincide with the concurrent work of Cosyn (2019). The derived sum rules between GFFs and GPDs enable future lattice QCD calculations and experimental extractions of the mechanical properties (pressure, shear forces, energy distributions) of spin-1 hadrons such as the deuteron.
