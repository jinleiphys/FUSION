# Ab initio investigations of A=8 nuclei: $α{-}α$ scattering, deformation in $^8$He, radiative capture of protons on $^7$Be and $^7$Li and the X17 boson

> We apply the No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) that is capable of describing both bound and unbound states in light nuclei in a unified way with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions as the only input. The NCSMC can predict structure and dynamics of light nuclei and, by comparing to available experimental data, test the quality of chiral nuclear forces. We discuss applications of NCSMC to the $α{-}α$ scattering and the structure of $^8$Be, the p+$^7$Be and p+$^7$Li radiative capture and the production of the hypothetical X17 boson claimed in ATOMKI experiments. The $^7$Be(p,$γ$)$^8$B reaction plays a role in Solar nucleosynthesis and Solar neutrino physics and has been subject of numerous experimental investigations. We also highlight our investigation of the neutron rich exotic $^8$He that has been recently studied experimentally at TRIUMF with an unexpected deformation reported.

# Ab initio investigations of A=8 nuclei

## Key claim
The No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC), using only chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions as input, can simultaneously describe bound and unbound states in light nuclei and test chiral nuclear forces by comparison to experimental data, but the agreement with data depends on the specific choice of three-nucleon interaction model (e.g., NN+3N_lnl gives best agreement for alpha-alpha scattering, while the S-factor for 7Be(p,gamma)8B depends on whether N3LO*+3N_lnl or N4LO+3N_lnl* interactions are used).

## Method
The NCSMC solves the A-body Schrodinger equation for both bound and scattering boundary conditions using a trial wave function that combines a description of the aggregate system when all nucleons are close together with explicit sub-clusters that can separate. The method employs chiral Effective Field Theory nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions as the only input, with second-quantization techniques used to treat both target and projectile on the same footing for cluster-cluster parts. For 8He, the NN interaction (NN-N4LO) was renormalized by the SRG approach with an evolution parameter lambda_SRG = 2.4 fm^-1, and NCSMC calculations extended the NCSM basis using the 7He(gs)+n cluster.

## Key numbers
- 8He 2+_1 resonance: excitation energy 3.54(6) MeV with width 0.89(11) MeV from experiment; NCSMC calculation gives 3.58(6) MeV excitation energy and 750(50) keV width
- NCSM calculation of 8He 2+_1 state: neutron quadrupole moment Q_n = 6.15 e fm^2, proton quadrupole moment Q_p = 0.60 e fm^2
- 7Be(p,gamma)8B S-factor at zero energy: 19.8 +/- 0.3 eV barn from NCSMC calculations
- Currently recommended S_17(0) value: 20.8 +/- 0.7 (expt) +/- 1.4 (theory) eV barn
- 7He 3/2- ground state resonance width: 150 keV experimentally
- 8Be 2+_1 resonance in alpha-alpha scattering: region impacted by this resonance shows best agreement with NN+3N_lnl model

## Context
This work extends the NCSMC formalism developed by Baroni (2013) and Navratil (2016) to treat cluster-cluster scattering using second-quantization techniques, building on the approach of Kravvaris (2020) for alpha-alpha scattering and 12Be clustering. The calculations address the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction critical for solar neutrino physics, where the recommended S-factor from the review by Adelberger (2011) has theory-dominated uncertainty; the new NCSMC results reduce this theoretical uncertainty. The investigation of the X17 boson anomaly reported by the ATOMKI collaboration (Krasznahorkay 2016, Firak 2020) provides first-principles calculations of the p+7Li capture reaction, including internal pair conversion and hypothetical boson production, enabling comparison of vector, pseudoscalar, and axial vector candidates for the 17 MeV boson.
