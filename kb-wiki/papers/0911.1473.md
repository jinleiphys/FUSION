# First results for electromagnetic three-nucleon form factors from high-precision two-nucleon interactions

> The electromagnetic form factors of the three-nucleon bound states were calculated in Complete Impulse Approximation in the framework of the Covariant Spectator Theory for the new high-precision two-nucleon interaction models WJC-1 and WJC-2. The calculations use an approximation for the three-nucleon vertex functions with two nucleons off mass shell. The form factors with WJC-2 are close to the ones obtained with the older model W16 and to nonrelativistic potential calculations with lowest-order relativistic corrections, while the form factors with the most precise two-nucleon model WJC-1 exhibit larger differences. These results can be understood when the effect of the different types of pion-nucleon coupling used in the various models is examined.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic form factors of the three-nucleon bound states calculated in Complete Impulse Approximation (CIA) using the new high-precision two-nucleon interaction models WJC-1 and WJC-2 are presented, but only under the CIA-0 approximation which replaces vertex functions with two nucleons off mass shell by those with only one nucleon off mass shell; the authors caution that the quality of this approximation was tested only for the older W16 model, and that WJC-1's mixed pseudoscalar-pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling might introduce further differences between CIA and CIA-0 to which W16 is not sensitive.

## Method

The electromagnetic three-nucleon form factors were calculated in the framework of the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) using the Complete Impulse Approximation (CIA), which includes diagrams where the photon couples to single nucleons that can be off-shell or on-shell before or after the photon-nucleon vertex. Because vertex functions with two nucleons off mass shell were not available for the new WJC-1 and WJC-2 models, the authors introduced the CIA-0 approximation, which expands the vertex function in the off-shell energy of the second nucleon around its on-shell value and keeps only the positive-energy component in the pair rest frame. The form factors were computed numerically from three-nucleon vertex functions obtained by solving the CST Faddeev-type integral equations for the two-nucleon interaction models W16, WJC-1, and WJC-2, using the on-shell single-nucleon current with the Galster parameterization of nucleon form factors.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy reproduced by both WJC-1 and WJC-2 models: 8.48 MeV
- Momentum transfer range for form factor calculations: up to Q = 9 fm^{-1}
- WJC-1 fitted with 27 adjustable parameters; WJC-2 fitted with 15 adjustable parameters
- WJC-1 uses mixed pseudoscalar-pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling with mixing parameter for charged pions lambda_{pi+/-} = -0.312
- WJC-2 uses pure pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling (lambda = 0)
- W16 (CIA) charge radius of tritium: 1.718 fm; W16 (CIA-0): 1.720 fm; WJC-1 (CIA-0): 1.700 fm; WJC-2 (CIA-0): 1.722 fm; experimental: 1.755 +/- 0.086 fm
- W16 (CIA) charge radius of helium-3: 1.900 fm; W16 (CIA-0): 1.901 fm; WJC-1 (CIA-0): 1.879 fm; WJC-2 (CIA-0): 1.904 fm; experimental: 1.959 +/- 0.030 fm
- W16 (CIA) magnetic moment of tritium: 2.544 nuclear magnetons; WJC-1 (CIA-0): 2.441 n.m.; WJC-2 (CIA-0): 2.525 n.m.; experimental: 2.979 n.m.
- W16 (CIA) magnetic moment of helium-3: -1.747 n.m.; WJC-1 (CIA-0): -1.648 n.m.; WJC-2 (CIA-0): -1.742 n.m.; experimental: -2.128 n.m.

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory calculations of three-nucleon form factors previously performed by Pinto, Stadler, and Gross (2009) for a family of older models (including W16) to the new high-precision two-nucleon interaction models WJC-1 and WJC-2 from Gross and Stadler (2008). The results are compared to nonrelativistic impulse approximation with relativistic corrections (IARC) calculations by Marcucci and collaborators (1998, 2005) based on the AV18/UIX potential, and the close agreement between WJC-2, W16, and IARC is attributed to the suppression of Z-graphs through pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling. The work enables future inclusion of interaction currents (such as boson-in-flight terms and contact interactions) to fully evaluate the models against experimental data, and demonstrates that electromagnetic probes can distinguish otherwise on-shell equivalent two-nucleon interaction models.
