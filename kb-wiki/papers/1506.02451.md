# Decay analysis of compound nuclei with mass A$\sim 30-200$ formed in the reactions involving loosely bound projectiles

> The dynamics of the reactions forming compound nuclei using loosely bound projectiles is analysed within the framework of dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) of Gupta and Collaborators. We have analysed different reactions with $^{7}Li$, $^{9}Be$ and $^{7}Be$ as neutron rich and neutron deficient projectiles, respectively, on different targets at the three $E_{lab}$ values, forming compound nuclei within the mass region A$\sim 30-200$. The contributions of light particles LPs ($A\le4$) cross sections $Пғ_{LP}$, energetically favoured intermediate mass fragments IMFs ($5 \le A_2 \le 20$) cross sections $Пғ_{IMF}$ as well as fusion-fission $\it{ff}$ cross sections $Пғ_{ff}$ constitute the $Пғ_{fus}$ (=$Пғ_{LP}$+$Пғ_{IMF}$+$Пғ_{ff}$) for these reactions. The contribution of the emitted LPs, IMFs and ff fragments is added for all the angular momentum upto the $\ell_{max}$ value, for the resepctive reactions. Interestingly, we find that the $О”R^{emp}$, the only parameter of model and uniquely fixed to address the $Пғ_{fus}$ for all other reactions having $\it same$ loosely bound projectile at the chosen incident energy. It may be noted that the dynamical collective mass motion of preformed LPs, IMFs and ff fragments or clusters through the modified interaction potential barrier are treated on parallel footing. We see that the values of modified interaction barrier heights $О”V_{B}^{emp}$ for such reactions are almost of the same amount specifically at the respective $\ell_{max}$ values.

## Key claim

The dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) can describe fusion cross sections for reactions induced by loosely bound projectiles (вҒ·Li, вҒ·Be, вҒ№Be) on various targets using a single empirically-fitted neck length parameter О”R^emp that is uniquely fixed for all reactions sharing the same projectile at the same incident energy, provided the barrier modification О”V_B^emp is nearly constant at the respective в„“_max values.

## Method

The authors applied the dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) of Gupta and collaborators to 18 reactions forming compound nuclei in the mass range AвҲј30-200, using вҒ·Li, вҒ·Be, and вҒ№Be projectiles on targets including ВІвҒ·Al, ВІвҒёSi, вҒөвҒ№Co, вҒөвҒёNi, В№ВІвҒҙSn, В№вҒҙвҒҙSm, В№вҒ¶вҒ№Tm, and В№вҒёвҒ·Re at laboratory energies of 10, 17, and 28 MeV. The total fusion cross section Пғ_fus was calculated as the sum of light particle (AвүӨ4), intermediate mass fragment (5вүӨAвӮӮвүӨ20), and fusion-fission contributions, integrated over all angular momenta up to в„“_max, with the neck length parameter О”R^emp fitted to reproduce experimental Пғ_fus for one reaction and then applied unchanged to all other reactions with the same projectile and energy.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) previously applied by Gupta and collaborators to reactions across light to superheavy mass regions, now systematically applied to loosely bound projectiles where breakup and incomplete fusion complicate the reaction dynamics. The study contrasts with statistical models (Morreto 1975, Campo 1991, Matsus 1997) that treat light particles, intermediate mass fragments, and fission on different footings, whereas DCM treats all decay channels on equal footing through collective clusterization. The finding that a single О”R^emp works for all targets with the same projectile enables predictions of fusion cross sections for reactions where experimental data are unavailable, such as вҒ·Be+ВіВІS, вҒ·Be+вҒҙвҒ°Ca, вҒ·Be+вҒҙвҒёTi, and вҒ·Be+вҒ¶вҒөCu, and establishes a systematic basis for future studies of breakup and incomplete fusion channels.
