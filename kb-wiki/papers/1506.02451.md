# Decay analysis of compound nuclei with mass A$\sim 30-200$ formed in the reactions involving loosely bound projectiles

> The dynamics of the reactions forming compound nuclei using loosely bound projectiles is analysed within the framework of dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) of Gupta and Collaborators. We have analysed different reactions with $^{7}Li$, $^{9}Be$ and $^{7}Be$ as neutron rich and neutron deficient projectiles, respectively, on different targets at the three $E_{lab}$ values, forming compound nuclei within the mass region A$\sim 30-200$. The contributions of light particles LPs ($A\le4$) cross sections $σ_{LP}$, energetically favoured intermediate mass fragments IMFs ($5 \le A_2 \le 20$) cross sections $σ_{IMF}$ as well as fusion-fission $\it{ff}$ cross sections $σ_{ff}$ constitute the $σ_{fus}$ (=$σ_{LP}$+$σ_{IMF}$+$σ_{ff}$) for these reactions. The contribution of the emitted LPs, IMFs and ff fragments is added for all the angular momentum upto the $\ell_{max}$ value, for the resepctive reactions. Interestingly, we find that the $ΔR^{emp}$, the only parameter of model and uniquely fixed to address the $σ_{fus}$ for all other reactions having $\it same$ loosely bound projectile at the chosen incident energy. It may be noted that the dynamical collective mass motion of preformed LPs, IMFs and ff fragments or clusters through the modified interaction potential barrier are treated on parallel footing. We see that the values of modified interaction barrier heights $ΔV_{B}^{emp}$ for such reactions are almost of the same amount specifically at the respective $\ell_{max}$ values.

# Decay analysis of compound nuclei with mass A∼30-200 formed in the reactions involving loosely bound projectiles

## Key claim

The dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) can describe fusion cross sections for reactions induced by loosely bound projectiles (⁷Li, ⁷Be, ⁹Be) on various targets using a single empirically-fitted neck length parameter ΔR^emp that is uniquely fixed for all reactions sharing the same projectile at the same incident energy, provided the barrier modification ΔV_B^emp is nearly constant at the respective ℓ_max values.

## Method

The authors applied the dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) of Gupta and collaborators to 18 reactions forming compound nuclei in the mass range A∼30-200, using ⁷Li, ⁷Be, and ⁹Be projectiles on targets including ²⁷Al, ²⁸Si, ⁵⁹Co, ⁵⁸Ni, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹⁴⁴Sm, ¹⁶⁹Tm, and ¹⁸⁷Re at laboratory energies of 10, 17, and 28 MeV. The total fusion cross section σ_fus was calculated as the sum of light particle (A≤4), intermediate mass fragment (5≤A₂≤20), and fusion-fission contributions, integrated over all angular momenta up to ℓ_max, with the neck length parameter ΔR^emp fitted to reproduce experimental σ_fus for one reaction and then applied unchanged to all other reactions with the same projectile and energy.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷Be-induced reactions at E_lab∼17 MeV with ΔR^emp=1.130 fm: σ_fus for ⁷Be+²⁷Al→³⁴Cl* is 646.74 mb (experiment: 635±76 mb, Kalita 2006); for ⁷Be+⁵⁸Ni→⁶⁵Ge* is 62.84 mb (experiment: 61.1±6.9 mb, Martinez 2014)
- For ⁷Li-induced reactions at E_lab∼10 MeV with ΔR^emp=0.907 fm: σ_fus for ⁷Li+²⁷Al→³⁴S* is 437.13 mb (experiment: 415±67 mb, Kalita 2006); for ⁷Li+²⁸Si→³⁵Cl* is 352.64 mb (experiment: 352.81±8 mb, Sinha 2008); for ⁷Li+⁵⁹Co→⁶⁶Zn* is 2.82 mb (experiment: 3.97 mb, Beck 2003)
- For ⁹Be-induced reactions at E_lab∼28 MeV with ΔR^emp=1.138 fm: σ_fus for ⁹Be+²⁷Al→³⁶Cl* is 1130.823 mb (experiment: 1191 mb, Marti 2005); for ⁹Be+²⁸Si→³⁷Ar* is 892.69 mb (experiment: 945±94 mb, Bodek 1980); for ⁹Be+¹²⁴Sn→¹³³Xe* is 82 mb (experiment: 89.6±3.0 mb, Parkar 2010); for ⁹Be+¹⁴⁴Sm→¹⁵³Dy* is 0.77 mb (experiment: 1.72±1.02 mb, Gomes 2006); for ⁹Be+¹⁶⁹Tm→¹⁷⁸Ta* is 0.182 mb (experiment: 0.198 mb, Fang 2015); for ⁹Be+¹⁸⁷Re→¹⁹⁶Au* is 0.0263 mb (experiment: 0.030 mb, Fang 2015)
- Barrier modification ΔV_B for ⁷Be-induced reactions at ℓ_max ranges from -2.005 MeV (⁷Be+³²S) to -3.970 MeV (⁷Be+⁶⁵Cu), showing near-constancy for a given projectile

## Context

This work extends the dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) previously applied by Gupta and collaborators to reactions across light to superheavy mass regions, now systematically applied to loosely bound projectiles where breakup and incomplete fusion complicate the reaction dynamics. The study contrasts with statistical models (Morreto 1975, Campo 1991, Matsus 1997) that treat light particles, intermediate mass fragments, and fission on different footings, whereas DCM treats all decay channels on equal footing through collective clusterization. The finding that a single ΔR^emp works for all targets with the same projectile enables predictions of fusion cross sections for reactions where experimental data are unavailable, such as ⁷Be+³²S, ⁷Be+⁴⁰Ca, ⁷Be+⁴⁸Ti, and ⁷Be+⁶⁵Cu, and establishes a systematic basis for future studies of breakup and incomplete fusion channels.
