# Scale-Chiral Effective Field Theory for Nuclear Interactions in the Veneziano Limit

> Following Golterman and Shamir, we develop scale-chiral perturbation theory in the large $N_c$ and large $N_f$ Veneziano limit that incorporates both light-quark baryons and hidden local symmetric bosons and derive a leading-order scale symmetry Lagrangian applicable in nuclear physics. Some applications in the medium-free space and baryonic matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops scale-chiral perturbation theory in the large \(N_c\) and large \(N_f\) Veneziano limit following Golterman and Shamir, incorporating both light-quark baryons and hidden local symmetric bosons, and shows that at leading order in scale symmetry the resulting Lagrangian is identical to that obtained in the Crewther-Tunstall approach, suggesting that the emergent scale symmetry observed in dense nuclear matter is generic and independent of the precise nature of how scale symmetry arises in QCD.

## Method

The authors adopt the Golterman-Shamir (GS) scheme, which assumes an infrared fixed point at a critical number of flavors \(N_f^c\) and takes the Veneziano limit where both \(N_f\) and \(N_c\) go to infinity with \(N_f/N_c\) held fixed. They construct the scale-chiral Lagrangian to leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the scale-chiral counting, including hidden local symmetry (HLS) vector mesons and octet baryons, using the conformal compensator field \(\chi = f_\sigma e^{\sigma/f_\sigma}\) to implement scale symmetry. The power counting is set by \(\Delta = |n_f^c - n_f| \sim O(p^2)\) where \(n_f = N_f/N_c\), and the unknown potentials \(V_I(\sigma - \eta)\) are expanded in powers of \(\Delta\).

## Key numbers

- The dilaton mass in the chiral limit is given by \(m_\sigma^2 = -\frac{4}{f_\sigma^2} \tilde{c}_{d,11}(n_f - n_f^\ast)\), where \(\tilde{c}_{d,11}\) is an unknown constant and \((n_f - n_f^\ast)\) accounts for deviation from the IR fixed point.
- The leading-order dilaton potential takes the Coleman-Weinberg form: \(\tilde{\cal L}_{\chi{\rm PT}_\sigma;d}^{O(\Delta)} = -\frac{m_\sigma^2 f_\sigma^2}{4} \left(\frac{\chi}{f_\sigma}\right)^4\left(\ln \frac{\chi}{f_\sigma} - \frac{1}{4}\right)\).
- The sound velocity of massive neutron stars approaches the conformal value \(v_s^2/c^2 = 1/3\) at a density \(\sim 3n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is normal nuclear matter density) and stays unchanged up to \(\sim (6-7)n_0\).
- The tidal deformability \(\Lambda\) from neutron star mergers (GW170817) is brought below 800 by the half-skyrmion mechanism.

## Context

This work extends the scale-chiral effective field theory approach previously developed by Crewther and Tunstall (CT) and applied in Li, Ma, and Rho (LMR) (2016) to include baryons and hidden local symmetry, now reformulated in the Golterman-Shamir (GS) framework. The GS approach provides an alternative theoretical basis for the "leading-order scale symmetry" (LOSS) approximation that has successfully explained several nuclear phenomena, including the effective Gamow-Teller coupling constant in shell model (\(g_A^\ast = 1.0 \pm 0.1\)), Brown-Rho scaling, and the properties of massive compact stars. The equivalence of the two approaches at leading order suggests that the emergent scale symmetry in dense matter is robust, and the framework enables systematic calculations of nuclear interactions and dense matter properties that can be tested against future gravitational wave observations of neutron star mergers.
