# Gluon Parton Distribution of the Nucleon from 2+1+1-Flavor Lattice QCD in the Physical-Continuum Limit

> We present the first physical-continuum limit $x$-dependent nucleon gluon distribution from lattice QCD using the pseudo-PDF approach, on lattice ensembles with $2+1+1$ flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ), generated by MILC Collaboration. We use clover fermions for the valence action on three lattice spacings $a \approx 0.9$, 0.12 and 0.15~fm and three pion masses $M_π\approx 220$, 310 and 690~MeV, with nucleon two-point measurements numbering up to $O(10^6)$ and nucleon boost momenta up to 3~GeV. We study the lattice-spacing and pion-mass dependence of the reduced pseudo-ITD matrix elements obtained from the lattice calculation, then extrapolate them to the continuum-physical limit before extracting $xg(x)/\langle x \rangle_g$. We use the gluon momentum fraction $\langle x \rangle_g$ calculated from the same ensembles to determine the nucleon gluon unpolarized PDF $xg(x)$ for the first time entirely through lattice-QCD simulation. We compare our results with previous single-ensemble lattice calculations, as well as selected global fits.

## Key claim

The authors present the first determination of the $x$-dependent nucleon gluon parton distribution function (PDF) entirely through lattice QCD simulation that includes a physical-continuum limit extrapolation, using the pseudo-PDF approach; however, the resulting gluon PDF has much larger uncertainties (by a factor of 3--5) than those from single-lattice-spacing analyses, and the lattice results deviate from global-fit gluon distributions for $x<0.3$, likely due to the lack of large-Ioffe-time lattice data.

## Method

The calculation uses lattice ensembles with $2+1+1$ flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) generated by the MILC Collaboration, with clover fermions for the valence action on three lattice spacings ($a \approx 0.09$, 0.12 and 0.15 fm) and three pion masses ($M_\pi \approx 220$, 310 and 690 MeV). The authors compute nucleon two-point and three-point gluon correlators with nucleon boost momenta up to 3 GeV, extract ground-state matrix elements using two-state and two-sim fits, form reduced pseudo-Ioffe-time distributions (RpITDs), and extrapolate these to the physical-continuum limit using an ansatz linear in pion mass and lattice-spacing squared. The gluon PDF $xg(x)$ is then obtained by matching the RpITDs to the lightcone PDF via the pseudo-PDF matching kernel, using the gluon momentum fraction $\langle x \rangle_g$ calculated from the same ensembles.

## Key numbers

- Three lattice spacings used: $a \approx 0.0888(8)$ fm, $0.1184(10)$/$0.1207(11)$ fm, and $0.1510(20)$ fm
- Three pion masses: $M_\pi \approx 220$, $310$, and $690$ MeV (with physical pion mass $M_\pi=135$ MeV as target)
- Nucleon two-point measurements numbering up to $O(10^6)$ (specifically 1,466,944 for the a12m220 ensemble)
- Nucleon boost momenta up to 3.05 GeV
- Continuum-limit RpITD values from $O(a)$ extrapolation at $\nu=\pi/4$: 0.988(31) for light nucleon; at $\nu=\pi/2$: 0.95(12); at $\nu=\pi$: 0.70(32)
- Continuum-limit RpITD values from $O(a^2)$ extrapolation at $\nu=\pi/4$: 0.987(16) for light nucleon; at $\nu=\pi/2$: 0.950(60); at $\nu=\pi$: 0.75(16)
- Fit range for RpITD matching: Ioffe time $\nu \in [0,7]$
- Gluon PDF results consistent with global fits (CT18 and NNPDF3.1 NNLO) for $x \in [0.3,1]$

## Context

This work extends previous exploratory gluon PDF calculations by the same group (Fan 2020) on a single a12m310 ensemble with $10^5$ measurements, and by the HadStruc Collaboration (2021) on a single ensemble, by incorporating multiple lattice spacings and pion masses to perform the first physical-continuum limit extrapolation. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to systematically improve the small-$x$ gluon distribution by using larger nucleon boost momenta to reach larger Ioffe times, and to reduce uncertainties by an order of magnitude in all ensembles. The authors compare their results with global fits from CT18 (Hou 2019) and NNPDF3.1 (Ball 2017) NNLO analyses, as well as with the JAM20 gluon PDF (Moffat 2021).
