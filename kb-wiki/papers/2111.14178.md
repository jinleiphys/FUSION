# Two-photon transitions of charmonia on the light front

> We investigate the two-photon transitions $H_{c\bar c} \to γ^*γ$ of the charmonium system in light-front dynamics. The light-front wave functions were obtained from solving the effective Hamiltonian based on light-front holography and one-gluon exchange interaction within the basis light-front quantization approach. We compute the two-photon transition form factors as well as the two-photon decay widths for S- and P-wave charmonia, $η_c$ and $χ_{cJ}$ and their excitations. Without introducing any free parameters, our predictions are in good agreement with the recent experimental measurements by BaBar and Belle, shedding light on the relativistic nature of charmonium.

## Key claim
The authors compute two-photon transition form factors and decay widths for charmonia using light-front wave functions obtained from an effective Hamiltonian that combines light-front holography with one-gluon exchange, and they report good agreement with BaBar and Belle data without introducing any free parameters, though the basis sensitivity (difference between Nmax=8 and Nmax=16 results) provides an uncertainty band.

## Method
The light-front wave functions for charmonia were obtained by solving an effective Hamiltonian based on light-front holography and one-gluon exchange interaction within the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach. The two-photon transition form factors for S-wave (ηc) and P-wave (χc0, χc1, χc2) charmonia were computed from these wave functions using leading-order light-front perturbation theory, with the on-shell photon treated via the transverse current J⊥. The same wave functions, previously used for spectroscopy, decay constants, and other observables, were employed without parameter adjustment.

## Key numbers
- ηc(1S) two-photon width: 3.7(6) keV (BLFQ prediction) compared to experimental value 5.15(35) keV from PDG.
- ηc(2S) two-photon width: 1.9(4) keV (BLFQ prediction) compared to experimental value 2.1(1.6) keV from PDG.
- χc0(1P) two-photon width: 1.7(4) keV (BLFQ prediction) compared to experimental value 2.20(16) keV from PDG.
- χc2(1P) two-photon width: 0.70(13) keV (BLFQ prediction) compared to experimental value 0.56(5) keV from PDG.
- χc1(1P) reduced two-photon width: 3.0(5) keV (BLFQ prediction); experimental value for χc1(3872) is 20–500 eV from Belle.
- χc1(2P) reduced two-photon width: 3(1) keV (BLFQ prediction) for a pure c-cbar state, significantly above the Belle measurement for χc1(3872).
- χc2(2P) two-photon width: 0.58(25) keV (BLFQ prediction), consistent with PDG lower bound >0.17 keV for χc2(3930).
- Effective quark mass mf = 1.57 GeV determined from mass spectroscopy.
- UV resolution μUV ≈ κ√Nmax = 2.8 GeV for Nmax=8 basis.
- Monopole fit pole mass for ηc(1S) TFF: Λ² = 8.5 ± 0.6 ± 0.7 GeV² ≈ M_J/ψ² from BaBar data.

## Context
This work extends the light-front Hamiltonian approach previously applied to charmonium spectroscopy and other observables by Li, Li, and Vary (2017) to two-photon transitions, providing a unified relativistic framework. It addresses deficiencies in non-relativistic effective theories (e.g., NRQCD by Feng 2015, 2017) and potential models that show slow convergence and discrepancies with experiment, as reviewed by Babiarz (2019, 2020). The results enable comparisons with lattice QCD calculations (e.g., CLQCD 2016, 2020; Dudek 2006) and DSE/BSE calculations (Chen 2016), and provide predictions for excited states yet to be measured, such as ηc(2S) and χc2(2P).
