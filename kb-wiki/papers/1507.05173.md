# The Proton Mass and Scale-Invariant Hidden Local Symmetry for Compressed Baryonic Matter

> I discuss how to access dense baryonic matter of compact stars by combining hidden local symmetry (HLS) of light-quark vector mesons with spontaneously broken scale invariance of a (pseudo) Nambu-Goldstone boson, dilaton, in a description that parallels the approach to dilatonic Higgs. Some of the surprising observations are that the bulk of proton mass is not Nambu-Goldstonian, parity doubling emerges at high density and the EoS of baryonic matter can be soft enough for heavy-ion processes at low density and stiff enough at high density for $\sim 2$ solar mass neutron stars.

## Key claim

The bulk of the proton mass (≥70%) is not generated by spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry (the Nambu-Goldstone mechanism) but instead remains as a chiral-invariant mass even when the quark condensate vanishes on average at high density; this conclusion follows from combining hidden local symmetry (HLS) with scale-chiral symmetry, assuming an infrared fixed point for scale symmetry that may emerge in dense medium even if not present in vacuum.

## Method

The author constructs a scale-invariant hidden local symmetry (sHLS) Lagrangian by coupling the HLS Lagrangian for light-quark vector mesons (ρ, ω) with a conformal compensator field χ representing the dilaton (identified with f₀(500) as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken scale symmetry). Baryons are treated as skyrmions from this Lagrangian on a crystal lattice; the topological transition from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at density n₁/₂ (≈ 1.5–2.0 n₀) is used to demarcate two density regions (R-I and R-II), with the "bare" parameters of the effective Lagrangian scaling differently in each region based on the in-medium vacuum expectation value of the dilaton field and the vector manifestation fixed point for the ρ meson.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Half-skyrmion transition density: n₁/₂ ∼ (1.5–2.0)n₀, with n₁/₂ ∼ 2.0n₀ fitting well in numerical applications
- Chiral-invariant nucleon mass M₀ ≈ (0.7–0.8) m_N, meaning ≥70% of the proton mass remains when the bilinear quark condensate averages to zero
- Critical density for chiral restoration (VM fixed point): n_c ≈ (6–7)n₀
- In R-I, the density-scaling parameter Φ(n) ≈ 1/(1 + c_I n/n₀) with c_I < 1
- The ρ meson mass scales as m*_ρ/m_ρ ≈ (1 − n/n_c) in R-II

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2001) and the scale-chiral perturbation theory of Crewther and Tunstall (2012) to dense baryonic matter relevant for compact stars. It contradicts the long-held assumption that most of the proton mass arises from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (as reviewed by Schumacher, 2010) and instead supports the parity-doubling picture seen in skyrmion crystal simulations (Ma et al., 2013) and in renormalization-group analyses of parity-doublet models (Paeng et al., 2012). The framework enables predictions for the equation of state of neutron-star matter that can be soft at low density (relevant for heavy-ion collisions) and stiff at high density (accommodating ∼2 solar mass neutron stars), with the symmetry energy showing a characteristic cusp at n₁/₂ that can be tested by future heavy-ion experiments.
