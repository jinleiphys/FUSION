# Compositeness of the Delta(1232) resonance in pi N scattering

> We evaluate the $πN$ compositeness of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance so as to clarify the internal structure of $Δ(1232)$ in terms of the $πN$ component. Here the compositeness is defined as contributions from two-body wave functions to the normalization of the total wave function and is extracted from the $πN$ scattering amplitude. In this study we employ the chiral unitary approach with the interaction up to the next-to-leading order plus a bare $Δ$ term in chiral perturbation theory and describe $Δ(1232)$ in an elastic $πN$ scattering. Fitting the $πN$ scattering amplitude to the solution of the partial wave analysis, we obtain a large real part of the $πN$ compositeness for $Δ(1232)$ comparable to unity and non-negligible imaginary part as well, with which we reconfirm the result in the previous study on the $πN$ compositeness for $Δ(1232)$.

## Key claim

The πN compositeness of the Δ(1232) resonance has a large real part comparable to unity and a non-negligible imaginary part, confirming previous results; however, the extracted value depends on the choice of subtraction constant in the loop function, and the present calculation gives a minimal value of |XπN| under the constraint that the nucleon compositeness be non-negative.

## Method

The authors employ the chiral unitary approach for elastic πN scattering, using an interaction kernel from chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order (Weinberg–Tomozawa term, s- and u-channel nucleon exchange, contact term) plus a bare Δ term. They solve the scattering equation algebraically, evaluate the p-wave loop function via a dispersion relation with two subtraction constants, and fit the seven model parameters to the WI08 partial wave analysis solution for the S11, S31, P11, P31, P13, and P33 amplitudes up to w = 1.35 GeV. The πN compositeness is extracted from the residue of the scattering amplitude at the resonance pole using the derivative of the loop function.

## Key numbers

- Pole position of Δ(1232): w_pole = 1209.8 − 47.6 i MeV (naive fit) and 1206.9 − 49.6 i MeV (constrained fit)
- πN compositeness of Δ(1232): XπN = 0.69 + 0.39 i (naive fit) and 0.87 + 0.35 i (constrained fit)
- Coupling constant g for Δ(1232): 0.383 − 0.053 i MeV^{-1/2} (naive) and 0.395 − 0.061 i MeV^{-1/2} (constrained)
- χ²/N_d.o.f. = 486.3/809 for the naive fit and 1239.9/809 for the constrained fit
- πN compositeness of N(940): −0.18 (naive) and 0.00 (constrained)

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Aceti et al. (2014) on the πN compositeness of Δ(1232) by using a more refined chiral unitary approach with next-to-leading order interactions, rather than a simple model. The results reconfirm the large πN cloud contribution to the Δ(1232) internal structure, which complements quark-model descriptions where Δ(1232) is a genuine three-quark state. The study also addresses the ambiguity in the compositeness definition due to subtraction constant shifts, as discussed by Hyodo et al. (2008), and provides a minimal value for |XπN| under the constraint of a physical nucleon compositeness.
