# Collective behavior in random interaction

> Recent investigations have looked at the many-body spectra of random two-body interactions. In fermion systems, such as the interacting shell model, one finds pairing-like spectra, while in boson systems, such as IBM-1, one finds rotational and vibrational spectra. We discuss the search for random ensembles of fermion interactions that yield rotational and vibrational spectra, and in particular present results from a new ensemble, the ``random quadrupole-quadrupole ensemble''

## Key claim

The authors show that fermion systems with random two-body interactions (TBRE) exhibit some signatures of collective band structure, including previously unknown correlations between excitation energy ratios, but these signatures are much weaker than in boson systems; they propose a new "random quadrupole-quadrupole ensemble" (RQQE) that yields sharper collective signatures in fermion systems, though the ensemble is less random than the TBRE.

## Method

The authors generated random two-body interactions for fermion systems in the sd and pf valence spaces, diagonalized them using the shell-model code REDSTICK, and analyzed the resulting spectra for signatures of pairing, vibrational, and rotational collectivity. They introduced the RQQE, where the Hamiltonian is constructed from a random one-body quadrupole operator \(\tilde{Q}\) as \(\hat{H} = -\lambda \tilde{Q} \cdot \tilde{Q}\), with the two-body matrix elements derived from the random quadrupole matrix elements \(q_{ij}\). They compared the TBRE and RQQE results for excitation energy ratios \(R_{42}\) and \(R_{62}\), B(E2) transition strength ratios, and the distribution of angles between ground state wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- For pairing, vibrational, and rotational collectivity, the expected ratios are \(R_{42} = 1, 2,\) and \(3.33\), respectively.
- In the sd shell, the TBRE has 63 independent random two-body matrix elements, while the RQQE has only 5 independent parameters (assuming time-reversal symmetry).
- In the pf shell, the TBRE has 195 independent matrix elements and the RQQE has 9.
- The RQQE yields \(J=0\) ground states in \(> 99\%\) of cases for all even-even nuclides tested.
- For the TBRE in many-body spaces where the fraction of \(J=0\) states is 4-11%, the fraction of ground states with \(J=0\) is 45-70%.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of random interactions in many-body systems, particularly the finding of pairing-like spectra in fermion systems (Johnson, 1999; Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean, 2000) and rotational/vibrational spectra in boson systems (Bijker and Frank, 2000). The authors build on the "consistent-Q" formulation from the interacting boson model (Bijker and Frank, 2000) to construct the RQQE for fermions. The paper enables future work on understanding the origins of collectivity in nuclear spectra and testing whether specific algebraic components of the nuclear interaction are necessary for collective behavior.
