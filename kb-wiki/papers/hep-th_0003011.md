# Massive Spin 3/2 Electrodynamics

> We study the general non-minimally coupled charged massive spin 3/2 model both for its low energy phenomenological properties and for its unitarity, causality and degrees of freedom behaviour. When the model is viewed as an effective theory, its parameters (after ensuring the correct excitation count) are related to physical characteristics, such as the magnetic moment g factor, by means of low energy theorems. We also provide the corresponding higher spin generalisation. Separately, we consider both low and high energy unitarity, as well as the causality aspects of our models. None (including truncated N=2 supergravity) is free of the minimal model's acausality.

## Key claim

The paper shows that no non-minimal coupling choice for a charged massive spin-3/2 field, including the truncated N=2 supergravity model, can simultaneously satisfy the correct degrees-of-freedom count and avoid acausal propagation in external electromagnetic fields; the gyromagnetic ratio g is a free parameter in the effective theory, not fixed to g=2 by low-energy theorems unless one also imposes low-energy unitarity via the optical theorem.

## Method

The authors construct the most general parity-even, non-derivative Lagrangian for a charged massive Rarita-Schwinger field coupled to electromagnetism, parameterized by five constants (l1 through l5). They eliminate two parameters by requiring the model to maintain the correct number of propagating degrees of freedom (four physical states). They then compute soft-photon vertex and Compton scattering amplitudes using Gordon identities and on-shell relations to map the remaining parameters to the gyromagnetic ratio g. Causality is studied by analyzing the determinant of the matrix that determines the Lagrange multiplier ψ0 from the secondary constraint; a zero of this determinant signals acausal propagation.

## Key numbers

* Minimal coupling gives g = 2/3 (the well-known Belinfante result).
* The gyromagnetic ratio in the general model is g = 2/3 − (4/3)(l1 + 2l2).
* Truncated N=2 supergravity (l1 = −2, l2 = 1/2) yields g = 2.
* For a pure constant electric background with l5 = 0, the critical field where causality fails is |E|^2 = 3 (m^2 / [e |l1 + 2l2|])^2.
* For a pure constant magnetic background, the critical field satisfies −(3/2)m^2 + (e^2/(2m^2)) [2l2]^2 B^2 = ± e |1 − 2l2| |B|; for minimal coupling (l1 = l2 = l5 = 0) this gives B^2 = (3m^2/(2e))^2, and for truncated supergravity (l2 = 1/2) it gives B^2 = 3m^4/e^2.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing problem of causal propagation for charged higher-spin fields, originally identified by Johnson and Sudarshan (1961) and Velo and Zwanziger (1969) for the minimally coupled Rarita-Schwinger field. It generalizes the causality analysis to the full five-parameter family of non-minimal couplings, including the truncated N=2 supergravity model of Ferrara, Porrati, and Telegdi (1992). The paper shows that no choice of non-minimal parameters can eliminate acausality, contrary to earlier hopes that the supergravity-inspired model might be causal. The results enable phenomenological applications of spin-3/2 effective theories with arbitrary g, provided one accepts that the effective description breaks down at the critical field scale.
