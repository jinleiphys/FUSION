# Comment on "Three-dimensional study of the six-body bound-state for the case of effective three-body configuration model" [Int. J. Mod. Phys. E 25, 9 (2016) 1650072]

> The authors argue that the calculated $^6$He binding energies by the solution of the coupled Faddeev-Yakubovsky integral equation in a Three-dimensional scheme reported by E. Ahmadi Pouya and A. A. Rajabi [Int. J. Mod. Phys. E 25, 9 (2016) 1650072] are incorrect. The formalism of the paper has serious mistakes and the numerical results are quite misleading because such a calculation even with small grids for Jacobi momenta and the angle variables leads to a huge memory of 37.8 PB (petabyte) which cannot even be achieved on present supercomputers.

# Comment on "Three-dimensional study of the six-body bound-state for the case of effective three-body configuration model"

## Key claim

The calculated ⁶He binding energies reported by Ahmadi Pouya and Rajabi (2016) are incorrect because their formalism contains serious mistakes and the numerical implementation would require approximately 37.8 PB of memory, which exceeds the capabilities of any existing supercomputer.

## Method

The authors examine the formalism of the six-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in a three-dimensional (3D) scheme as presented by Ahmadi Pouya and Rajabi, identifying specific errors in the representation of Yakubovsky components, the placement of two-body t-matrices inside or outside the integral kernel, and the angular dependence of the equations. They also analyze the numerical memory requirements by counting the independent variables for each Yakubovsky component (5 Jacobi momentum magnitudes, 4 spherical angles, and 3 azimuthal angles) and computing the storage needed for the coupled system.

## Key numbers

- Each Yakubovsky component depends on 12 independent variables (5 Jacobi momentum magnitudes, 4 spherical angles, 3 azimuthal angles), not 10 as claimed in the original paper.
- With 20 mesh points for Jacobi momenta, 14 for spherical angles, and 14 for azimuthal angles, the size of the coupled Yakubovsky components is 6.75 × 10¹⁴.
- With at least 7 iterations, the total problem size is 4.72 × 10¹⁵, requiring 3.78 × 10¹⁶ bytes, or approximately 37.8 PB of memory.
- This memory requirement is about 28.3 times larger than the memory of the Sunway TaihuLight supercomputer (1.31 PB), ranked number one in the TOP500 list.
- Even using the incorrect size from the original paper's Eq. (5.1), the required memory for two Yakubovsky components would be about 192756 GB.

## Context

This comment directly challenges the work of Ahmadi Pouya and Rajabi (2016) published in Int. J. Mod. Phys. E, which claimed to solve the six-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for ⁶He in a 3D scheme. The commenters note that the correct formulation of Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for six-body bound states leads to five coupled equations, reducible to two for two-neutron halo nuclei like ⁶He, as shown by Witala (2010). The comment extends prior work on 3D representations of Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations by Elster (1998), Schadow (1998), Liu (2002), and others, and argues that the published formalism cannot reproduce the correct 3D representation given in those references. The comment enables future work by clarifying the correct variable count and memory requirements for genuine six-body calculations in a 3D scheme.
