# Excitation spectra of exotic nuclei in a self-consistent phonon-coupling model

> We explore giant resonance spectra and low-lying dipole strength in the Ni and Sn chains from proton rich to very neutron rich isotopes, relevant in astrophysical reaction chains. For the theoretical description we employ the random-phase approximation (RPA) plus many-body effects through a phonon coupling model with optimized selection of phonons. The nuclear force is based on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock energy functional carried consistently through all steps of modelling. The main effect of phonon coupling is a broadening of the spectral distributions (collisional width). This broadening is particularly dramatic for low-lying dipole strength in very neutron rich nuclei delivering there a qualitative change of the spectra.

## Key claim

Phonon coupling (via the time-blocking approximation) produces a dramatic broadening of low-lying dipole strength in very neutron-rich nuclei, qualitatively changing the spectral shape compared to RPA predictions, but this effect is much weaker for the giant resonance peaks themselves where the main improvement is smoothing of unphysical RPA fragmentation.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using two parametrizations (SV-bas and SV-m64k6), then built random-phase approximation (RPA) on top, and finally added phonon coupling through the time-blocking approximation (TBA) with an optimized phonon selection criterion based on dimensionless coupling strength (cutoff parameter v_min = 0.05). The phonon coupling was computed in a discrete basis while RPA and TBA included proper treatment of the nucleon continuum, and the effective interaction in TBA was renormalized by subtraction of the zero-frequency component to maintain consistency with the mean-field ground state.

## Key numbers

- For the phonon selection cutoff, v_min = 0.05 was used, with stable results demonstrated for v_min in the range 0.01–0.1
- The maximal phonon energy was set to E_max(phon) = 40 MeV
- The single-particle basis used maximal angular momentum l_max(sp) = 17; for giant resonances the maximal single-particle energy was epsilon_max(sp) = 100 MeV, while for low-lying dipole strength it was 200 MeV
- The radial grid used spacing 0.05 fm and box size 18 fm
- An additional smoothing of 400 keV width was applied for giant resonances and 10 keV for the pygmy dipole resonance region
- The effective mass for SV-bas is m*/m = 0.9 versus m*/m = 0.64 for SV-m64k6; the TRK sum rule enhancement (isovector effective mass) is kappa = 0.4 for SV-bas versus 0.6 for SV-m64k6; the symmetry energy is J = 30 MeV for SV-bas versus 27 MeV for SV-m64k6
- The phonon coupling produces a small downshift of average resonance energies by 0–1 MeV and increases widths by typically 1 MeV
- For the GMR, GQR, and GDR in the Sn chain, widths are smaller than the corresponding widths for Ni isotopes

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent TBA studies (Lyutorovich 2015, 2016; Tselyaev 2016, 2017, 2018) from stable doubly-magic nuclei to exotic neutron-rich and proton-rich isotopes in the Ni and Sn chains, which are relevant for astrophysical reaction chains (Arnould 2007). The paper provides systematic predictions for giant resonance properties and low-lying dipole strength across long isotopic chains, enabling future experimental tests of isovector aspects of Skyrme functionals (particularly the GDR isotopic trend problem noted by Erler 2010) and offering benchmarks for other approaches such as the coupled-cluster calculations of Hagen (2016) for Ni isotopes and the relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation of Robin (2017).
