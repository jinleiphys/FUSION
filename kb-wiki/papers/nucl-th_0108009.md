# A Role of the Axial Vector Mesons on the Photon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions and Their Relevant Decays

> A role of the axial vector mesons, such as $K_1$ and $a_1$, on the emitted photon spectrum in hot hadronic matter is studied through the channels $πρ\to a_1 \to πγ$ and $K ρ\to K_1 \to K γ$. They are shown to be dominant channels in this spectrum. This study is carried out with an effective chiral lagrangian which includes vector and axial-vector mesons and explains well their relevant decays simultaneously.

## Key claim

The axial vector mesons \(a_1\) and \(K_1\) are shown to be dominant intermediate states for photon production in hot hadronic matter via the channels \(\pi\rho \rightarrow a_1 \rightarrow \pi\gamma\) and \(K\rho \rightarrow K_1 \rightarrow K\gamma\), but the \(K_1\) contribution is at least one order of magnitude smaller than that of \(a_1\) at high photon energies above about 0.5 GeV, while both are competitive at low energies below 0.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors used an effective chiral Lagrangian based on a non-linear realization of \(SU(3)_L \otimes SU(3)_R\) symmetry, which includes vector and axial-vector mesons, to compute the decay widths of \(a_1\) and \(K_1\) mesons and the photon production rate from the s-channel processes \(\pi\rho \rightarrow a_1 \rightarrow \pi\gamma\) and \(K\rho \rightarrow K_1 \rightarrow K\gamma\) in a hot hadronic gas. The Lagrangian parameters \(G\), \(r\), and \(g\) were determined by fitting the calculated decay widths to experimental data. The photon production rate was then computed using a three-dimensional integral over the invariant amplitude, with Bose-Einstein distribution functions for the initial mesons, at temperatures of 100, 150, and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(a_1(1260)\) used: 1210 MeV; mass of \(K_1(1270)\) used: 1280 MeV.
- Calculated decay width \(\Gamma_{a_1 \rightarrow \rho\pi} = 488\) MeV (experimental range: 200–600 MeV).
- Calculated decay width \(\Gamma_{a_1 \rightarrow \pi\gamma} = 0.688\) MeV (experimental: \(0.64 \pm 0.28\) MeV).
- Calculated decay width \(\Gamma_{K_1 \rightarrow \rho K} = 47\) MeV (experimental: \(57 \pm 5\) MeV).
- Calculated decay width \(\Gamma_{K_1 \rightarrow K\gamma} = 0.350\) MeV (no experimental value given).
- Photon production rates were computed at three temperatures: \(T = 100, 150, 200\) MeV.
- The \(K_1\) contribution is at least one order of magnitude smaller than that of \(a_1\) in the high \(E_\gamma\) region up to 3–4 GeV.
- In the low \(E_\gamma\) region below 0.5 GeV, both \(a_1\) and \(K_1\) show competitive roles.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Xiong, Shuryak, and Brown (1992) and Haglin (1994) on photon production from hot hadronic matter, which had emphasized the role of the \(a_1\) meson but did not include the \(K_1\) meson or used a simple phenomenological Lagrangian that overestimated decay widths. The authors also build on the chiral Lagrangian framework of Shin et al. (2000) and compare their results to those of Song (1993) and Ko et al. (1996). The findings enable more accurate modeling of the photon spectrum from heavy-ion collisions, particularly in the low-energy region where strange axial-vector mesons contribute comparably to non-strange ones.
