# Strong decay of $Λ_c(2940)$ as a $2P$ state in the $Λ_c$ family

> Considering the mass, parity and $D^0 p$ decay mode, we tentatively assign the $Λ_c(2940)$ as the $P-$wave states with one radial excitation. Then, via studying the strong decay behavior of the $Λ_c(2940)$ within the $^3P_0$ model, we obtain that the total decay widths of the $Λ_{c1}(\frac{1}{2}^-,2P)$ and $Λ_{c1}(\frac{3}{2}^-,2P)$ states are 16.27 MeV and 25.39 MeV, respectively. Compared with the experimental total width $27.7^{+8.2}_{-6.0}\pm0.9^{+5.2}_{-10.4}~\rm{MeV}$ measured by LHCb Collaboration, both assignments are allowed, and the $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^-$ assignment is more favorable. Other $λ-$mode $Σ_c(2P)$ states are also investigated, which are most likely to be narrow states and have good potential to be observed in future experiments.

## Key claim
The paper assigns the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ resonance as a $\lambda$-mode $2P$ state in the $\Lambda_c$ family, finding that both $J^P = 1/2^-$ and $J^P = 3/2^-$ assignments are allowed by the experimental width, but the $J^P = 3/2^-$ assignment is more favorable. The claim is conditional on the mass, parity, and $D^0 p$ decay mode being consistent with a radially excited $P$-wave interpretation.

## Method
The authors used the $^3P_0$ quark pair creation model (also called the quark-pair-creation model) to calculate Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka-allowed two-body strong decay widths of the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ as $\Lambda_{c1}(1/2^-,2P)$ and $\Lambda_{c1}(3/2^-,2P)$ states. They employed simple harmonic oscillator wave functions, a spatially constant pair-production strength $\gamma$, and relativistic phase space. The overall parameter $\gamma = 9.83$ was fixed by reproducing the experimental width of $\Sigma_c(2520)^{++} \to \Lambda_c \pi^+$ (14.78 MeV). The harmonic oscillator parameters for mesons and baryons were taken from the relativized quark model, with light quark masses $m_{u/d}=220$ MeV, $m_s=419$ MeV, and $m_c=1628$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- Total decay width of $\Lambda_{c1}(\frac{1}{2}^-,2P)$: 16.27 MeV.
- Total decay width of $\Lambda_{c1}(\frac{3}{2}^-,2P)$: 25.39 MeV.
- Experimental total width of $\Lambda_c(2940)$ from LHCb: $27.7^{+8.2}_{-6.0}\pm0.9^{+5.2}_{-10.4}$ MeV.
- Partial decay width ratio for $J^P = 1/2^-$: $\Gamma[\Sigma_c \pi]:\Gamma[\Sigma_c^* \pi]:\Gamma[DN] = 1:0.52:6.91$.
- Partial decay width ratio for $J^P = 3/2^-$: $\Gamma[\Sigma_c \pi]:\Gamma[\Sigma_c^* \pi]:\Gamma[DN] = 1:3.22:22.03$.
- Mass splitting of $\Lambda_c(1P)$ states: $m[\Lambda_c(2625)] - m[\Lambda_c(2595)] = 36$ MeV.
- Predicted mass range for the $\Lambda_{c1}(\frac{1}{2}^-,2P)$ state if $\Lambda_c(2940)$ is the $3/2^-$ state: 2909 to 2945 MeV.
- Total decay widths of five $\lambda$-mode $\Sigma_c(2P)$ states range from about 28 to 69 MeV (using masses from Capstick and Isgur (1986)).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of $\Lambda_c(2940)$ that considered various interpretations, including $D^*N$ molecular states (He 2006, Ortega 2012, Ortega 2014, Zhao 2016, Yang 2015) and other quark-model assignments (Garcilazo 2007, Valcarce 2008, Ebert 2011, Lu 2016, Chen 2009, Chen 2014). The paper directly addresses the LHCb amplitude analysis (Aaij 2017) which determined the most likely $J^P = 3/2^-$ for $\Lambda_c(2940)$. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted $\Lambda_{c1}(1/2^-,2P)$ state in the $DN$ final state and for the narrow $\Sigma_c(2P)$ states with large $D^{(*)}N$ branching ratios.
