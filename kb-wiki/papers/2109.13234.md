# Rotation and deformation of strangeon stars in the Lennard-Jones model

> The strong interactions at low energy scales determine the state of the supranuclear matter in the pulsar-like compact objects. It is proposed that the bulk strong matter could be composed of strangeons, which are quark clusters with a nearly equal number of three light-flavor quarks. In this work, to characterize the strong-repulsive interactions at short distances and the non-relativistic nature of the strangeons, the Lennard-Jones model is used to describe the equation of state (EoS) of strangeon stars (SSs). We investigate the static, the slowly rotating, and the tidally deformed SSs in detail. The corrections resulted from the finite surface densities are considered crucially in the perturbative approaches. We also study the universal relations between the moments of inertia, the tidal deformabilities, and the quadrupole moments. Those results are ready to be used for various purposes in astrophysics, and possible constraints from contemporary observations on the parameter space of the Lennard-Jones model are discussed. Future observations of the pulsars' radio signals, the X-ray emissions from the hot spots on the surface of the stars, and the gravitational waves (GWs) from binary mergers can give tighter constraints or even verify or falsify the existence of SSs.

## Key claim
The Lennard-Jones model for strangeon stars (SSs) predicts that the maximal mass can exceed 3 solar masses and that the commonly used causality limit (R=2.824M) can be violated, while the dynamical sound speed remains subluminal due to the retarded-field nature of interactions in the solid-state lattice.

## Method
The authors used the Lennard-Jones potential to model the equation of state (EoS) of strangeon matter, treating strangeons as non-relativistic quark clusters with short-range repulsion and long-range attraction. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static spherical stars, then applied the Hartle-Thorne perturbative approach to third order in angular frequency for slowly rotating configurations, and separately computed tidal deformabilities using linear perturbation theory. Crucially, they included finite surface density corrections in the boundary matching conditions.

## Key numbers
- Maximal mass of SSs can exceed 3 solar masses for large parameter space (e.g., Nq=18, ns=0.24 fm^-3, epsilon=50 MeV)
- Surface baryon density ns ranges from 0.24 fm^-3 to 0.36 fm^-3, corresponding to 1.5 to 2.25 times nuclear saturation density n0=0.16 fm^-3
- Potential depth epsilon chosen in range 20-100 MeV, similar to internucleon potential well (50-120 MeV for the 1S0 channel)
- Quark mass taken as mq=300 MeV; strangeon quark numbers Nq=18 (quark-alpha) and Nq=9 considered
- The Hartle-Thorne approximation has relative errors less than 2% for spin frequencies below ~600 Hz for neutron star models

## Context
This work extends the Lennard-Jones model for strangeon stars previously developed by Lai (2009) and Xu (2009, 2018), and builds on the Hartle-Thorne formalism (Hartle 1967, 1968, 1973) for rotating relativistic stars. It also follows the tidal deformability calculations of LZX2019 for SSs and the universal I-Love-Q relations discovered by Yagi (2013). The results enable future constraints from pulsar timing, X-ray hot spot observations, and gravitational wave detections (e.g., GW170817) to test the strangeon star hypothesis against neutron star and quark star models.
