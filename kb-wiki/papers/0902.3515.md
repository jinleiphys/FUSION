# A Smoothing Method of Discrete Breakup S-matrix Elements in the Theory of Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channels

> We present a practical way of smoothing discrete breakup S-matrix elements calculated by the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (CDCC). This method makes the smoothing procedure much easier. The reliability of the smoothing method is confirmed for the three-body breakup reactions, 58Ni(d,pn) at 80 MeV and 12C(6He,4He2n) at 229.8 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents a practical smoothing formula for discrete breakup S-matrix elements obtained from continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations, using the complex scaling method (CSM) to construct the smoothing factors; the method is validated for three-body breakup reactions but is intended to be applicable to four-body breakup processes where exact smoothing factors are difficult to obtain.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the internal Hamiltonian of the projectile using L2-type Gaussian basis functions to obtain pseudostates for CDCC calculations, then construct smoothing factors using CSM by diagonalizing the complex-scaled Hamiltonian with real-range Gaussian basis functions. The smoothing formula combines the CDCC S-matrix elements with these CSM-based smoothing factors, which are computed from the overlap between pseudostates and exact continuum states via a spectral representation of the complex-scaled Green's function.

## Key numbers

- For the 58Ni(d,pn) reaction at 80 MeV: the breakup S-matrix elements are shown at grazing total angular momentum J=17 for channels (ℓ,L) = (0,17), (2,15), (2,17), and (2,19); the continuum is truncated at k=1.5 fm−1 with 2N=40 complex-range Gaussian basis functions (a1=1.0 fm, aN=30.0 fm) and N=30 real-range Gaussian basis functions for CSM.
- For the 12C(6He,4He2n) reaction at 229.8 MeV: the breakup S-matrix elements are shown at grazing total angular momentum J=25 for channels (ℓ,L) = (0,25), (2,23), (2,25), and (2,27); the continuum is truncated at k=1.5 fm−1 with the same basis parameters as the deuteron case.
- The CSM smoothing results converge to the exact solution as the scaling angle θ increases from 5° to 15° for both reactions.

## Context

This work extends the pseudostate discretization method for CDCC previously developed by Matsumoto, Egami, and collaborators (2006-2008), which was successful for elastic scattering of four-body systems but lacked a practical smoothing procedure for breakup S-matrix elements. The new CSM-based formula replaces an earlier smoothing method proposed by Egami (2008) that required heavy numerical calculations, and it is validated against exact smoothing factors obtained by solving the two-body Schrodinger equation numerically. The method enables future CDCC calculations of four-body breakup processes for three-body projectiles such as 6He, with planned applications to experimental data from Aumann, Wang, and Chulkov.
