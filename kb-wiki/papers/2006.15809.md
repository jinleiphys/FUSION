# Pileup corrections on higher-order cumulants

> We propose a method to remove the contributions of pileup events from higher-order cumulants and moments of event-by-event particle distributions. Assuming that the pileup events are given by the superposition of two independent single-collision events, we show that the true moments in each multiplicity bin can be obtained recursively from lower multiplicity events. In the correction procedure the necessary information are only the probabilities of pileup events. Other terms are extracted from the experimental data. We demonstrate that the true cumulants can be reconstructed successfully by this method in simple models. Systematics on trigger inefficiencies and correction parameters are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to remove pileup contributions from higher-order cumulants and moments, assuming that pileup events are superpositions of two independent single-collision events; the true moments in each multiplicity bin can be obtained recursively from lower multiplicity events using only the probabilities of pileup events, with other terms extracted from experimental data.

## Method

The authors derive relations connecting experimentally-observed moments (including pileup effects) with true moments by decomposing pileup events into sub-pileup events composed of two independent single-collision events. They propose a recursive reconstruction procedure that solves for true moments starting from the lowest multiplicity bin (m=0) and lowest order (r=1), then iterating upward in both multiplicity and moment order. The method is demonstrated using a Glauber and two-component model with negative binomial distributions for multiplicity and Poisson distributions for particle number, with pileup probability α=0.05.

## Key numbers

- Pileup probability α = 0.05 used in the model simulations
- Glauber model uses pp cross section of 33 mb for gold-gold collisions
- Hard component fraction x = 0.1 in the two-component model for multiplicity
- Negative binomial parameters: μ = 1.0 (mean particles per source), k = 1.0 (inverse width)
- Mean particle number in realistic case: ⟨N⟩ = 0.05 × Npart (number of participant nucleons)
- 10 million Au+Au collision events processed in the simulation
- For the simple Poisson model, true cumulants are ⟨N^r⟩ = 10 at all multiplicities
- Systematic study varies pileup probability by p from -10% to +10% of α=0.05

## Context

This work addresses a problem identified by Sombun (2017) and Garg (2017) that pileup events cause strong enhancement of fourth-order cumulants in central collisions, which could mimic or obscure signals of the QCD critical point. The method is designed for fixed-target experiments at RHIC, CBM, and J-PARC-HI where high collision rates increase pileup probability. The correction procedure is intended to be applied after standard detector efficiency corrections (as described by Bialas (1985), Kitazawa (2011), and others), and enables proper interpretation of higher-order fluctuation observables in the search for the QCD critical point.
