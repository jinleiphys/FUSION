# Hints of incomplete thermalization in RHIC data

> The large elliptic flow observed in Au-Au collisions at RHIC is often put forward as a compelling evidence for the formation of a strongly-interacting quark-gluon plasma. The main argument is that the measured elliptic flow is as large as the value given by fluid-dynamics models that assume complete thermalization. It is argued that this claim may not be justified, since a detailed examination of experimental data rather suggests that the system created is not fully equilibrated at the time when anisotropic flow develops.

# Hints of incomplete thermalization in RHIC data

## Key claim

The large elliptic flow measured in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, often cited as evidence for a strongly-interacting quark-gluon plasma, may not indicate complete thermalization; a detailed examination of experimental data suggests the system is not fully equilibrated at the time when anisotropic flow develops, and several features of the data are better explained by an out-of-equilibrium scenario.

## Method

The paper compares ideal fluid-dynamics predictions for anisotropic flow (assuming complete kinetic equilibrium, Knudsen number much less than 1) with an out-of-equilibrium scenario where the mean number of collisions per particle is insufficient for local equilibrium. It examines the scaling behavior of elliptic flow $v_2$ and the ratio $v_4/v_2^2$ as functions of system size, centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum, confronting these predictions with published RHIC data from the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The typical buildup time for elliptic flow in ideal fluid dynamics is $\bar R/c_s$, about 2–4 fm/$c$ for Au-Au or Cu-Cu collisions.
- The ideal-fluid prediction for the ratio $v_4/v_2^2$ is $1/2$; values significantly larger than $1/2$ indicate incomplete thermalization.
- The relation $\Kn^{-1} = (c_s/c)(\sigma/S)(dN/dy)$ connects the Knudsen number to the particle density at the time anisotropic flow builds up.
- In the out-of-equilibrium regime, $v_2/\epsilon$ scales linearly with $(1/S)(dN/dy)$, with no hint of saturation, extending down to values measured in Pb-Pb collisions at the CERN SPS.

## Context

This work extends the out-of-equilibrium framework of Bhalerao (2005) and challenges the interpretation of Kolb (2003) that RHIC data require thermalization within 0.6 fm/$c$. It contradicts the ideal-fluid paradigm by showing that $v_2/\epsilon$ is not constant across centralities, as would be required by scale invariance, and that $v_4/v_2^2$ exceeds the hydrodynamical value of $1/2$. The paper enables further transport-model studies to quantify how far RHIC data are from the hydrodynamical limit, and anticipates that LHC Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.5$ TeV will provide a discriminating test.
