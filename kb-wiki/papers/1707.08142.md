# A new statistical method for the structure of the inner crust of neutron stars

> We investigated the structure of the low density regions of the inner crust of neutron stars using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model to predict the proton content $Z$ of the nuclear clusters and, together with the lattice spacing, the proton content of the crust as a function of the total baryonic density $ﾏ＼b$. The exploration of the energy surface in the $(Z,ﾏ＼b)$ configuration space and the search for the local minima require thousands of calculations. Each of them implies an HFB calculation in a box with a large number of particles, thus making the whole process very demanding. In this work, we apply a statistical model based on a Gaussian Process Emulator that makes the exploration of the energy surface ten times faster. We also present a novel treatment of the HFB equations that leads to an uncertainty on the total energy of $\approx 4$ keV per particle. Such a high precision is necessary to distinguish neighbour configurations around the energy minima.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Gaussian Process Emulator (GPE) can reduce the number of required Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for mapping the energy surface of the inner crust by approximately a factor of ten, but only when combined with a novel treatment of continuum states that reduces the numerical uncertainty on the total energy to about 4 keV per particle, which is necessary to distinguish neighboring configurations around energy minima.

## Method

The authors used the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model to compute the total energy of nuclear clusters in the inner crust as a function of proton number Z and baryonic density ﾏ＼b. To suppress numerical noise from artificial discretization of continuum states, they performed HFB calculations in a box of radius R_B = 80 fm, larger than the Wigner-Seitz cell radius R_WS, and then used a Gaussian Process Emulator trained on approximately 8% of the configuration space to interpolate the full energy surface across the (Z, ﾏ＼b) plane.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty on the total energy from the novel treatment of the HFB equations is approximately 4 keV per particle.
- The error from discretization of continuum states (ﾏダbox) is approximately 1 keV per particle for a box radius of 80 fm, based on the spread of curves in pure neutron matter calculations.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering HFB calculations of the inner crust by Negele and Vautherin (1973) and later works by Baldo (2005) and Grill (2011), which suffered from numerical noise due to treatment of neutron gas states. The method improves upon the subtraction technique suggested by Margueron (2007) and avoids the approximations of semi-classical methods used by Pearson (2015). The approach enables systematic exploration of the energy surface for constructing unified equations of state for neutron star crusts, though the authors note that first-order phase transitions breaking the single-nucleus approximation, as discussed by Gulminelli (2015), are not considered here.
