# Effective Field Theory Power Counting at Finite Density

> Effective field theory is applied to finite-density systems with an unnaturally large scattering length, such as neutron matter. A new organizational scheme is identified and connected with an expansion in inverse powers of the number of space-time dimensions. This power counting allows for analytic calculations in many-body systems and reproduces the hole-line expansion of traditional nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a new organizational scheme for effective field theory (EFT) at finite density that expands in inverse powers of the number of space-time dimensions, which reproduces the hole-line expansion of traditional nuclear physics and allows for convergent analytic calculations even in D=4 dimensions, though the scheme is identified by working in a large number of dimensions.

## Method

The author applies non-relativistic EFT with contact interactions to neutron matter, using dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS) and a renormalization scale mu. The ground-state energy contributions from ring diagrams of bubbles with particle-particle, particle-hole, and hole-hole scattering are computed analytically and numerically, then organized by counting powers of 1/D where D is the number of space-time dimensions, with the suppression of each additional hole-line quantified.

## Key numbers

- The neutron scattering length gives |1/a_s| ~ 10 MeV
- The kinetic energy per particle of a free fermion gas is E_kin/A = (3/5)(k_F^2/2M)
- For the three-bubble diagrams, the 2pp-1hh contribution is 0.0641 (k_F a_s)^3 (k_F^2/2M) and the 1pp-2hh contribution is 0.0115 (k_F a_s)^3 (k_F^2/2M)
- For the four-bubble diagrams, the 3pp-1hh contribution is 0.0383 (k_F a_s)^4 (k_F^2/2M), the 2pp-2hh contribution is 0.00247 (k_F a_s)^4 (k_F^2/2M), and the 1pp-3hh contribution is -0.000683 (k_F a_s)^4 (k_F^2/2M)
- The leading-order ground-state energy per particle for neutron matter (g=2) is E/A = (3/5 + (2k_F a_s/3)/(pi-2k_F a_s)) (k_F^2/2M) + O(1/D)
- For neutrons with 1/a_s -> 0, the scale-invariant result is E/A = (4/15) k_F^2/2M
- The pole in E/A occurs at k_F a_s = pi/2 for positive scattering length

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach to finite-density systems with unnaturally large scattering lengths, building on the free-space power countings known as Lambda-counting and Q-counting from Weinberg (1991) and the PDS scheme from Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998). It connects to the hole-line expansion of traditional nuclear physics described by Fetter and Walecka (1971) and Negele and Orland (1988), and agrees with results from Hammer and Furnstahl (2000) for dilute Fermi systems. The new power counting enables systematic calculation of finite-density observables such as the ground-state energy, and can be extended to include explicit pions and three-body forces for exploring nuclear matter near saturation density.
