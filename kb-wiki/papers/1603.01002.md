# Power counting for nuclear forces in chiral effective field theory

> The present note summarizes the discourse on power counting issues of chiral nuclear forces, with an emphasis on renormalization-group invariance. Given its introductory nature, I will lean toward narrating a coherent point of view on the concepts, rather than covering comprehensively the development of chiral nuclear forces in different approaches.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Weinberg's original power counting for chiral nuclear forces, based on naive dimensional analysis (NDA), fails renormalization-group (RG) invariance tests and must be modified; the author presents a self-consistent strategy where power counting is proposed, then checked against cutoff independence, with counterterms promoted as needed.

## Method

The author reviews the standard Q-calculus for Feynman diagrams in chiral effective field theory, distinguishing between single-nucleon processes and two-nucleon processes where infrared enhancement from purely nucleonic intermediate states forces nonperturbative resummation. The interplay between renormalization and power counting is examined by analyzing the cutoff dependence of one-pion exchange (OPE) in various partial waves, using numerical checks of RG invariance to identify where counterterms must be promoted relative to NDA. Higher-order interactions are treated as perturbations on top of the LO resummed amplitude, with the strategy of iteratively checking cutoff independence and promoting counterterms as needed.

## Key numbers

- The \(^3S_1\) scattering length is 5.4 fm, three times larger than the pion Compton wavelength of 1.4 fm.
- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV and the nucleon axial charge \(g_A = 1.26\).
- The low-energy scale characterizing OPE strength is \(M_{NN} \equiv 4\pi f_\pi^2/m_N\).
- The scale \(4\pi f_\pi \simeq 1.2\) GeV appears as the suppression factor for pion loops, \((Q/4\pi f_\pi)^2\).
- In \(^1S_0\), the divergence from OPE-contact interference is logarithmic: \(\propto m_\pi^2 (g_A^2 m_N^2)/(32\pi^2 f_\pi^2) \ln \Lambda\).
- In \(^1S_0\), the LO residual cutoff dependence is of order \((Q/\Lambda) T^{(0)}\), where \(T^{(0)}\) is the LO amplitude.
- In attractive triplet channels, counterterms like \(C_{^3P_0}\) are enhanced by a factor \((\mhi/Q)^2\) over NDA.

## Context

This paper summarizes the conceptual development of power counting for chiral nuclear forces, extending the foundational work of Weinberg (1990, 1991) who first proposed organizing potentials by ChPT counting and resumming them via the Schrodinger equation. It addresses renormalization issues identified by Kaplan (1996) regarding quark-mass dependence in \(^1S_0\) and by Beane (2000) and Nogga (2005) regarding singular attractions of the OPE tensor force. The work enables a systematic, RG-invariant approach to higher-order corrections in nucleon-nucleon scattering, with the strategy of treating higher-order interactions as perturbations on the LO resummed amplitude, as developed in the author's own series of papers (Long 2011, 2012).
