# The intruder feature of 31Mg and the coexistence of many particle and many hole states

> The low-lying level structure of $^{31}{\rm Mg}$ has been investigated by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) plus generator coordinate method (GCM) with the Gogny D1S force. It is shown that the N=20 magic number is broken and the ground state has the pure neutron $2p3h$ configuration. The coexistence of many particle and many hole states at very low excitation energy is discussed.

## Key claim
The ground state of 31Mg has a pure neutron 2p3h (two-particle, three-hole) intruder configuration relative to the N=20 shell closure, with spin-parity 1/2+, and coexists with 0p1h, 1p2h, and 3p4h configurations within less than 1 MeV excitation energy. The abstract glosses over the condition that this pure configuration is only found for the ground state, while other states show mixing between configurations.

## Method
The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method was used to generate intrinsic wave functions under a constraint on the matter quadrupole deformation β, using the Gogny D1S effective force. Parity projection was performed before variation, followed by angular momentum projection and finally a generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation that superposed AMD wave functions with different β and K values. Single-particle configurations were analyzed via the AMD+HF method to assign particle-hole configurations relative to the N=20 spherical shell closure.

## Key numbers
- Ground state (1/2+1) has 93% overlap with the 2p3h AMD wave function and 0% overlap with the 0p1h configuration
- Calculated total binding energy of 31Mg is 242.9 MeV
- Magnetic moment of the ground state: -0.91 μN (experimental: -0.89 μN from Neyens)
- Magnetic moment of the first excited state (3/2+1): 0.43 μN
- Energy difference between the lowest (0p1h) and highest (3p4h) configurations on the energy surface is less than 5 MeV
- The 3/2- state with 3p4h configuration is lowered by about 5.1 MeV from the energy surface minimum by angular momentum projection, while the 3/2+ state with 0p1h configuration is lowered by only about 1.8 MeV
- The 1/2+1 ground state has log ft = 4.6 for β-decay from 31Na(g.s.)
- Quadrupole moment of 3/2+1 state: -18.9 e·fm2 (AMD+GCM), -17.1 e·fm2 (rigid rotor approximation)

## Context
This work extends earlier shell model calculations by Wildenthal, Poves, and Utsuno that explained abnormal properties of N=20 neutron-rich nuclei through configuration inversion, and addresses the limitation that previous shell model calculations for 31Mg (e.g., Neyens, Marechal) did not reproduce ground state properties without modified interactions. The AMD+GCM approach enables the first theoretical description of the coexistence of 0p1h, 1p2h, 2p3h, and 3p4h configurations in 31Mg below 1 MeV, and predicts β-decay strengths to 31Al that can guide future experimental spin-parity assignments.
