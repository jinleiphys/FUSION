# Various aspects of the Deformation Dependent Mass model of nuclear structure

> Recently, a variant of the Bohr Hamiltonian was proposed where the mass term is allowed to depend on the beta variable of nuclear deformation. Analytic solutions of this modified Hamiltonian have been obtained using the Davidson and the Kratzer potentials, by employing techniques from supersymmetric quantum mechanics. Apart from the new set of analytic solutions, the newly introduced Deformation-Dependent Mass (DDM) model offered a remedy to the problematic behaviour of the moment of inertia in the Bohr Hamiltonian, where it appears to increase proportionally to the square of beta. In the DDM model the moments of inertia increase at a much lower rate, in agreement with experimental data. The current work presents an application of the DDM-model suitable for the description of nuclei at the point of shape/phase transitions between vibrational and gamma-unstable or prolate deformed nuclei and is based on a method that was successfully applied before in the context of critical point symmetries.

## Key claim

The Deformation-Dependent Mass (DDM) model, when applied to shape/phase transitions between vibrational and gamma-unstable or prolate deformed nuclei, can reproduce the E(5) and X(5) critical point symmetry results for fixed values of the extra parameter \(a\), while leaving \(a\) to vary reveals interesting trends in the critical values of the parameters as angular momentum increases.

## Method

The authors apply an extremum method previously used for critical point symmetries (Dav, Var) to the DDM model with a Davidson potential. For each angular momentum L, they find the pair of parameters \((a,\beta_0)_{crit}\) that maximizes the partial derivative of the energy ratio \(R_L = E(L)/E(2)\) with respect to \(\beta_0\), treating \(a\) as an additional free parameter. This is done for both the spherical to gamma-unstable transition (using \(\Lambda = \tau(\tau+3)\)) and the spherical to deformed transition (using a harmonic oscillator in \(\gamma\) with stiffness parameter \(c\)).

## Key numbers

- For fixed \(a = 0.011\), the ground band energy ratios match E(5) results: e.g., \(R_4 = 2.19663\) (E(5): 2.199), \(R_6 = 3.58597\) (E(5): 3.59), \(R_8 = 5.16385\) (E(5): 5.169).
- For fixed \(a = 0.0035\), the ground band energy ratios match X(5) results: e.g., \(R_4 = 2.90566\) (X(5): 2.904), \(R_6 = 5.43696\) (X(5): 5.43), \(R_8 = 8.49561\) (X(5): 8.483).
- In the spherical to gamma-unstable transition, \((a)_{crit}\) remains zero for low L and jumps to a finite value at L=10; \((\beta_0)_{crit}\) increases linearly with L up to L=10, then continues at a lower rate.
- The moment of inertia formula is \(J = \beta^2 / (1 + a \beta^2)^2\); using critical \((a,\beta_0)\) values, the moment of inertia increases linearly up to L=10 and then decreases (downbending) when \(a\) becomes non-zero.

## Context

This work extends the DDM model (Bonatsos et al., 2015) to shape/phase transitions, building on the variational extremum method introduced by Dav and Var for critical point symmetries. The DDM model itself remedies the problematic \(\beta^2\) dependence of the moment of inertia in the standard Bohr Hamiltonian, bringing it into agreement with experimental data. The results enable further study of the parameter \(a\) as a control parameter for phase transitions, with connections to the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) limits as discussed in Panos and Aparam.
