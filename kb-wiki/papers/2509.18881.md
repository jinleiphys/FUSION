# Magnetization by Rotation: Spin and Chiral Condensates in the NJL Model

> The role of spin degrees of freedom in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) has attracted significant interest in recent years. Spin hydrodynamics extends conventional hydrodynamics by incorporating spin via the spin tensor. In the mean-field limit of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model under rigid rotation, spin degrees of freedom manifest naturally as axial-vector, or spin, condensate. We investigate the interplay between chiral and spin condensates in this framework. While rotation typically suppresses the formation of a chiral condensate, the presence of a spin condensate may counteract this effect, enhancing the chiral condensate. Moreover, it can alter the nature of the chiral transition from second to first order.

## Key claim

While rotation typically suppresses the chiral condensate, the presence of a spin (axial-vector) condensate can counteract this suppression and enhance the chiral condensate, and can alter the chiral transition from second-order to first-order, but only when the axial coupling constant exceeds a threshold value relative to the scalar coupling.

## Method

The authors study the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an added axial-vector interaction term under rigid rotation, using the metric of a rotating cylinder. They perform a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, apply the mean-field approximation, and compute the effective potential via the Ritus method with Matsubara sums, using Pauli-Villars regularization. The coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate sigma and the spin condensate s are solved self-consistently in the chiral limit with N_c=3 and N_f=2.

## Key numbers

* The Pauli-Villars cutoff is Lambda = 757.048 MeV, with the scalar coupling G = 6.002 / Lambda^2 chosen to reproduce a vacuum constituent quark mass sigma = 300 MeV at T=0, Omega=0, and s=0.
* For the axial coupling ratio r_A = G_A/G = 2.0, the critical temperature at Omega=0 is T_c approximately 165 MeV, and the critical angular velocity at T=0 is Omega_c approximately 33 MeV.
* For r_A = 3.33, a region of finite spin condensate appears at low temperatures and intermediate angular velocities, bounded by a second-order transition at lower Omega and a first-order transition at higher Omega.
* For r_A = 8.33, the spin condensate region extends to the temperature axis, with both boundaries exhibiting first-order phase transitions.
* At fixed Omega = 25 MeV for r_A = 3.33 and Omega = 20 MeV for r_A = 8.33, the temperature dependence of the condensates is shown; at fixed T = 40 MeV, the angular-velocity dependence is shown for both cases.

## Context

This work extends studies of spin-polarized phases in the NJL model at zero temperature and moderate baryon density (Tsue 2012, Matsuoka 2016, Morimoto 2018) to finite temperature and rotation, drawing an analogy to the Barnett effect where rotation aligns intrinsic spins. The results connect to spin hydrodynamics (Becattini 2022, Buzzegoli 2024) and show a conceptual analogy to (inverse) magnetic catalysis (Shovkovy 2007, Gusynin 1995, Bali 2012). Future work could employ functional renormalization group methods (Schaefer 2008), include boundary conditions (Chernodub 2016), and explore the Nambu-Goldstone modes analogous to magnons in condensed matter (Watanabe 2011, Beekman 2019).
