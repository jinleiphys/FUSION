# Advancing QCD-based calculations of energy loss

> We give a brief overview of the basics and current developments of QCD-based calculations of radiative processes in medium. We put an emphasis on the underlying physics concepts and discuss the theoretical uncertainties inherently associated with the fundamental parameters to be extracted from data. An important area of development is the study of the single-gluon emission in medium. Moreover, establishing the correct physical picture of multi-gluon emissions is imperative for comparison with data. We will report on progress made in both directions and discuss perspectives for the future.

## Key claim

This paper provides a brief overview of the current state and conceptual foundations of QCD-based calculations for radiative energy loss in a medium, emphasizing that while various model implementations agree qualitatively, theoretical uncertainties in the fundamental parameters (such as the transport coefficient \(\hat q\)) complicate quantitative extraction of medium properties from hard probe data.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for medium-induced single-gluon emission, starting from a path-integral formulation that resums multiple interactions with the medium (the BDMPS-Z formalism). It discusses two main approximation schemes: the opacity expansion (fixed-order in medium interactions) and the harmonic oscillator approximation (valid for multiple soft scatterings). The review then extends the formalism to the case of two color charges (the "antenna" setup) to study interference effects and decoherence, and finally surveys existing models for multi-gluon emissions, including quenching weights, modified DGLAP splitting functions, and Monte Carlo generators.

## Key numbers

* The transport coefficient \(\hat q\) represents the average transverse momentum broadening per unit length and is treated as a phenomenological parameter to be extracted from data.
* The formation time of a medium-induced gluon is \(\tform = \sqrt{\omega / \hat q}\).
* The characteristic transverse momentum accumulated via coherent interaction with the medium is \(\kform^2 = \sqrt{\omega \hat q}\).
* The largest momentum scale from broadening is \(\Qmed = \sqrt{\hat q L}\), where \(L\) is the medium longitudinal extension.
* The leading contribution to the medium-induced gluon energy spectrum is \(\omega \frac{dN}{d\omega} \big|_\text{med-induced} \simeq \frac{\alpha_s C_R}{\pi} \sqrt{ \frac{\hat q L^2}{\omega} }\).
* The decoherence timescale for an antenna with opening angle \(\theta_{bc}\) is \(t_\text{d} = (\hat q \theta_{bc}^2)^{-1/3}\).
* The hard scale governing the system is \(Q_\text{hard} = \max \left(Q_s, r_\perp^{-1}, \omega|\delta \n| \right)\).

## Context

This review extends the established BDMPS-Z framework for medium-induced radiation (Baier 1997, Zakharov 1997, Wiedemann 2000) and discusses recent progress in relaxing the eikonal and collinear approximations. It reports on the development of the in-medium antenna spectrum (Mehtar-Tani 2011, Casalderrey-Solana 2011) which introduces interference effects and a decoherence parameter, enabling a more rigorous treatment of multi-gluon emissions and jet cascading in heavy-ion collisions. The paper also notes that current Monte Carlo generators (Q-PYTHIA, Q-HERWIG, MARTINI, JEWEL) do not yet incorporate these interference effects, and that the interface between bremsstrahlung and medium-induced radiation remains highly model dependent.
