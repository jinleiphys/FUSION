# SOLVING THE BETHE--SALPETER EQUATION IN MINKOWSKI SPACE: SCALAR THEORIES

> The Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation for scalar-scalar bound states in scalar theories without derivative coupling is formulated and solved in Minkowski space. This is achieved using the perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR), which allows these amplitudes to be expressed as integrals over weight functions and known singularity structures and hence allows us to convert the BS equation into an integral equation involving weight functions. We obtain numerical solutions using this formalism for a number of scattering kernels to illustrate the generality of the approach. It applies even when the na\"ıve Wick rotation is invalid. As a check we verify, for example, that this method applied to the special case of the massive ladder exchange kernel reproduces the same results as are obtained by Wick rotation.

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar-scalar bound states in scalar theories without derivative coupling can be solved directly in Minkowski space using the perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR), even when the naive Wick rotation is invalid. The method is demonstrated for a number of scattering kernels, but the approach is limited to scalar theories without derivative coupling and requires that the weight functions satisfy specific boundary conditions.

## Method
The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the scattering kernel are both expressed using the perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR), which writes them as integrals over weight functions and known singularity structures. Substituting these representations into the Bethe-Salpeter equation and using the uniqueness theorem of PTIR converts the original singular integral equation into a real integral equation for the weight function in two variables (a mass parameter alpha and a kinematic parameter z). The resulting integral equation is solved numerically by discretizing the alpha and z axes and iterating from an initial assumed weight function, with singularities regularized using the Hadamard finite part prescription with a small epsilon parameter.

## Key numbers
- The regularization parameter epsilon in the Hadamard finite part was kept small but finite, typically ~10^{-5} or less.
- The eigenvalue lambda = g^2/(4pi)^2 was solved for a range of bound state masses P^2 and exchange masses m_sigma, and all solutions reproduced the eigenvalues obtained in Euclidean space by Linden and Mitter.
- For the generalized ladder kernel, the convergence and stability of eigenvalues with varying number of grid points, epsilon, and alpha_max were reported as excellent, as for the pure ladder case.

## Context
This work extends the PTIR method originally used by Wick and Cutkosky for the massless scalar exchange ladder model (the Wick-Cutkosky model) to a broader class of scattering kernels, including cases where Wick rotation becomes invalid due to complex ghost poles in dressed propagators (as studied in Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches). The method enables solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation directly in Minkowski space, which is necessary for calculating absorptive parts of scattering amplitudes, and avoids the difficulties of numerically rotating Euclidean solutions back to Minkowski space. The authors note that future work should investigate separable kernels (which have exact solutions), generalizations to nonperturbative constituent propagators, the heavy-light bound state limit, and extensions to include derivative couplings and fermions.
