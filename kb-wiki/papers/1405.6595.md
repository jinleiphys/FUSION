# Eccentricity distributions in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We propose a new parametrization of the distribution of the initial eccentricity in a nucleus-nucleus collision at a fixed centrality, which we name the Elliptic Power distribution. It is a two-parameter distribution, where one of the parameters corresponds to the intrinsic eccentricity, while the other parameter controls the magnitude of eccentricity fluctuations. Unlike the previously used Bessel- Gaussian distribution, which becomes worse for more peripheral collisions, the new Elliptic Power distribution fits several Monte Carlo models of the initial state for all centralities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the distribution of the initial eccentricity in nucleus-nucleus collisions can be well described by a universal two-parameter "Elliptic Power" distribution for all centralities, unlike the previously used Bessel-Gaussian distribution which fails for peripheral collisions. The authors hedge that this distribution provides a good fit to several Monte Carlo models of the initial state, but acknowledge that the fit is not perfect for the two-dimensional distribution, specifically overestimating the maximum density and slightly underestimating the width of the ε_y distribution.

## Method

The authors derived the Elliptic Power distribution from an exact analytic expression for the distribution of (ε_x, ε_y) under the assumptions of pointlike independent sources with a two-dimensional Gaussian spatial distribution. They then treated both the ellipticity parameter ε_0 and the power parameter α as free fit parameters, allowing arbitrary real positive values of α, and fitted this distribution to histograms of ε_2 from Monte Carlo Glauber and IP-Glasma simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV across multiple centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- The Elliptic Power distribution fits Monte Carlo Glauber simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair in the 75-80% centrality range with parameters ε_0 = 0.61 and α = 3.3
- For the fourth harmonic ε_4 in 75-80% central Pb-Pb collisions, the Elliptic Power fit gives α = 3.2 and ε_0 = 0.22
- For the third harmonic ε_3 in 75-80% central Pb-Pb collisions, a Power fit (ε_0 = 0) gives α = 3.6
- The relative eccentricity fluctuations σ_ε/⟨ε⟩ approach √(4/π - 1) ≈ 0.52 for central collisions for both ε_2 and ε_3
- The mean of the Power distribution for ε_3 is given by ⟨ε_3⟩ = √π Γ(α+1) / [2 Γ(α+3/2)]
- The mean square of the Power distribution is ⟨ε_3²⟩ = 1/(α+1)

## Context

This work extends the Power distribution introduced by Yan (2013) for fluctuation-only eccentricity to include a reaction-plane eccentricity component, creating the Elliptic Power distribution. It improves upon the Bessel-Gaussian distribution proposed by Voloshin (2007), which fails for peripheral collisions because it does not enforce the constraint that ε_2 < 1. The result enables characterization of any initial-state model by two numbers per centrality (ε_0 and α), and can be applied to the distribution of elliptic flow values measured at the LHC, potentially improving unfolding procedures that previously used the Bessel-Gaussian distribution.
