# Effect of bulk viscosity on interferometry correlations in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A temperature dependent bulk viscosity coefficient is used in 3+1D hydrodynamic simulations. I study the effect of the increase of bulk viscosity around the critical temperature on the system dynamics in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760$~GeV. With increasing bulk viscosity the life-time of the system increases slightly. Also the shape of the freeze-out hypersurface changes, the outer layers of the fireball live longer. This effect causes a small reduction of the ratio of two interferometry radii $R_{out}/R_{side}$, improving the agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

Contrary to the naive expectation that a bulk viscosity peak around the critical temperature would increase the $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio (by softening the effective equation of state), the author finds a small *decrease* in that ratio, improving agreement with experimental data from ALICE.

## Method

The author performs 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations of central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760$ GeV, using a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio $\zeta/s$ with a peak near $T_H=180$ MeV. Two scenarios are compared: one with only low-temperature bulk viscosity ($\zeta/s=0.02$ at $T_H$) and one with an additional peak ($\zeta/s=0.35$ at $T_H$). Pion pair correlation functions are constructed from the freeze-out hypersurface at 150 MeV using the Cooper-Frye formula with nonequilibrium corrections, and the three-dimensional HBT radii $R_{out}$, $R_{side}$, and $R_{long}$ are extracted via Gaussian fits.

## Key numbers

- The bulk viscosity peak height at $T_H=180$ MeV is $\zeta/s=0.35$ for the scenario with the peak, compared to $\zeta/s=0.02$ for the scenario without the peak.
- The freeze-out temperature used is 150 MeV; varying it down to 140 MeV does not change the conclusions.
- For smooth initial conditions, differences in HBT radii between the two scenarios are less than 5%.
- For event-by-event simulations with fluctuating initial conditions, the $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio is reduced by up to 3% when the bulk viscosity peak is included.
- The calculation uses a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of $\eta/s=0.08$.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of HBT correlations in heavy-ion collisions, which previously showed that a hard equation of state is needed to reproduce the $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio (Rischke 1996, Bozek 2009). The author notes that while bulk viscosity has been implemented in simulations by Denicol (2009), Song (2009), Ryu (2015), and Bernhard (2016), its effect on interferometry radii had not been studied. The result suggests that including HBT radii in Bayesian statistical analyses of transport parameters (Novak 2013, Pratt 2015, Bernhard 2015, Bernhard 2016) would be consistent with a bulk viscosity peak, and further insight could come from studying small systems like p+Pb collisions.
