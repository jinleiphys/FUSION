# The internal structures of the nucleon resonances $N(1875)$ and $N(2120)$

> A nucleon resonance with spin-parity $J^P=3/2^-$ and mass about 2.1 GeV is essential to reproduce the photoproduction cross sections for $Λ(1520)$ released by the LEPS and CLAS Collaborations. It can be explained as the third nucleon resonance state $[3/2^-]_3$ in the constituent quark model so that there is no position to settle the $N(1875)$ which is listed in the PDG as the third $N3/2^-$ nucleon resonance. An interpretation is proposed that the $N(1875)$ is from the interaction of a decuplet baryon $Σ(1385)$ and a octet meson $K$, which is favored by a calculation of binding energy and decay pattern in a Bethe-Salpeter approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nucleon resonance N(2120) with J^P = 3/2^- should be assigned as the third state [N3/2^-]_3 in the constituent quark model, leaving no room for N(1875) in that model; instead, N(1875) is proposed to be a hadronic molecular state, a bound state of Σ(1385) and K meson, based on a Bethe-Salpeter calculation of binding energy and decay pattern.

## Method

The author performed a mass scan of the N(2120) by fitting photoproduction data for Λ(1520) from the CLAS and LEPS Collaborations within a Regge-plus-resonance approach, comparing χ² behavior when assuming the resonance is the third versus the fourth N3/2^- state in the constituent quark model. For N(1875), a Bethe-Salpeter equation in the covariant spectator formalism was solved for the Σ(1385)K system with vector meson exchange (ρ, ω, φ) as the interaction kernel, using a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ; the resulting bound-state wave function was then used to compute two-body decay widths via hadron loop mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The N(2120) mass scan yields a minimum χ² at 2.13 GeV when assigned as state [N3/2^-]_3; when assigned as [N3/2^-]_4, χ² remains stable around 150, indicating poor reproduction of data.
- The best-fit cutoff for the Σ(1385)K bound state is Λ ≈ 1.80 GeV, giving a binding energy of 28 MeV and a total width of 155 MeV.
- For Λ = 1.80 GeV, the decay branching ratios of the Σ(1385)K bound state are: Nσ 55.6%, Nπ 22.8%, Nω 14.2%, Nρ 4.8%, ΛK 2.1%, ΣK 0.5%.
- The PDG values for N(1875) are mass 30^{+25}_{-25} MeV above threshold and total width 200^{+20}_{-20} MeV (BnGa analysis); the BnGa branching ratios are Nσ 60±12%, Nπ 3±2%, ΛK 4±2%, ΣK 15±8%.
- The constituent quark model predicts the [N3/2^-]_3 state at a bare mass of 1960 MeV, with a dominant Nρ decay channel (57.1%), conflicting with the N(1875) decay pattern.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of He (2014) and Xie (2013) on Λ(1520) photoproduction, confirming that a resonance near 2.1 GeV is essential to reproduce CLAS data. It contradicts the assignment of N(1875) as the third N3/2^- state in the constituent quark model, as argued by Klempt (2009) and supported by AdS/QCD (Brodsky, 2014), instead proposing a molecular interpretation analogous to the Λ(1405) as a N̄K bound state (Dalitz, 1960; Jido, 2010). The Bethe-Salpeter approach used here was previously applied to B̄B* and D̄D* systems (He, 2014). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted decay pattern of N(1875) as a Σ(1385)K molecule.
