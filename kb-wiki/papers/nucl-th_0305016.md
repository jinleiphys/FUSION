# Non-Scissors-Mode Behaviour of Isovector Magnetic Dipole Orbital Transitions Involving Isospin Transfer

> We study the response of isovector orbital magnetic dipole (IOMD) transitions to the quadrupole-quadrupole ($Q \cdot Q$) interaction, to the isospin-conserving pairing interaction (ICP) and to combinations of both. We find qualitatively different behaviours for transitions in which the final isospin differs from the initial isospin versus cases where the two isospins are the same. For $N=Z$ even-even nuclei with $J^π=0^+, T=0$ ground states such as $^8Be$ and $^{20}Ne$, the summed $T=0 \to T=1$ IOMD from the ground state to all the $J=1, T=1$ states in the $0 \hbar ω$ space does not vanish when the $Q \cdot Q$ interaction is turned off. The pairing interaction (ICP) alone leads to a finite transition rate. For nuclei with $J=0^+, T=1$ ground states such as $^{10}Be$ and $^{22}Ne$, the summed $T=1 \to T=1$ IOMD $does$ vanish when the $Q \cdot Q$ interaction is turned off, as is expected in a good scissors-mode behaviour. However this is not the case for the corresponding sum of the $T=1 \to T=2$ IOMD transitions. In $^{22}Ne$ (but not in $^{10}Be$) the sum of the $T=1 \to T=2$ IOMD transitions is remarkably insensitive to the strengths of both the $Q \cdot Q$ and the ICP interactions. In $^{22}Ne$ an energy weighted-sum is similarly insensitive. All our calculations were carried out in the $0 \hbar ω$ space.

# Non-Scissors-Mode Behaviour of Isovector Magnetic Dipole Orbital Transitions Involving Isospin Transfer

## Key claim
The summed isovector orbital magnetic dipole (IOMD) strength for transitions where the final isospin differs from the initial isospin does not vanish when the quadrupole-quadrupole (Q·Q) interaction is turned off, contrary to the standard scissors-mode expectation; in 22Ne specifically, the summed T=1→T=2 IOMD strength is remarkably insensitive to the strengths of both the Q·Q and isospin-conserving pairing (ICP) interactions.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed in the 0ħω space using the OXBASH code for four light even-even nuclei (8Be, 10Be, 20Ne, 22Ne) with a Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, harmonic oscillator single-particle terms, a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and an isospin-conserving pairing interaction. The summed isovector orbital B(M1) strength was calculated from the ground state to all J=1+ states with a given isospin T in the model space, varying the ratio R = G/(18χ̄) where G is the ICP strength and χ̄ is the scaled Q·Q strength.

## Key numbers
- For 8Be with pure Q·Q (G=0): summed T=0→T=1 IOMD strength = 2.547 (dimensionless B(M1) units)
- For 8Be with pure ICP (χ=0): summed T=0→T=1 IOMD strength = 0.728
- For 20Ne with pure Q·Q (G=0): summed T=0→T=1 IOMD strength = 4.673
- For 20Ne with pure ICP (χ=0): summed T=0→T=1 IOMD strength = 2.447
- For 10Be with pure Q·Q (G=0): summed T=1→T=1 IOMD strength = 0.358; summed T=1→T=2 IOMD strength = 0.596
- For 10Be with pure ICP (χ=0): summed T=1→T=1 IOMD strength = 0.000; summed T=1→T=2 IOMD strength = 0.218
- For 22Ne with pure Q·Q (G=0): summed T=1→T=1 IOMD strength = 3.659; summed T=1→T=2 IOMD strength = 1.098
- For 22Ne with pure ICP (χ=0): summed T=1→T=1 IOMD strength = 0.000; summed T=1→T=2 IOMD strength = 1.175
- For 22Ne T=1→T=2 summed strength: 1.098 at R=0, decreases to 0.985 at R≈0.4, then increases to 1.174 as R→∞
- For 22Ne, the energy-weighted sum A+B (T=1→T=1 plus T=1→T=2) varies by only 13% over the range 0<R<1, although A and B individually each decrease by close to a factor of three

## Context
This work extends earlier shell-model studies of the scissors mode in deformed nuclei, which was first proposed by Lo Iudice and Palumbo (1978) and experimentally observed in 156Gd by Bohle et al. (1984). The authors build on a previous result by Zamick showing that with a simple Q·Q interaction the summed energy-weighted isovector orbital M1 strength is proportional to the difference between isoscalar and isovector B(E2) sums. The present work introduces an isospin-conserving pairing interaction alongside the Q·Q interaction, revealing a new class of "insensitive modes" in 22Ne where the summed T=1→T=2 IOMD strength remains nearly constant regardless of the relative strengths of the two interactions. This enables future studies of isovector orbital M1 transitions in heavier nuclei with neutron excess, where such isospin-transfer analysis is not currently possible.
