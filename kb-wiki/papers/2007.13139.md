# Asymptotic normalization coefficient method for two-proton radiative capture

> The method of asymptotic normalization coefficients is a standard approach for studies of two-body non-resonant radiative capture processes in nuclear astrophysics. This method suggests a fully analytical description of the radiative capture cross section in the low-energy region of the astrophysical interest. We demonstrate how this method can be generalized to the case of three-body $2p$ radiative captures. It was found that an essential feature of this process is the highly correlated nature of the capture. This reflects the complexity of three-body Coulomb continuum problem. Radiative capture $^{15}$O+$p$+$p \rightarrow ^{\,17}$Ne+$γ$ is considered as an illustration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method can be generalized from two-body to three-body (ANC3) for two-proton radiative capture processes, but the generalization requires accounting for the highly correlated nature of the three-body Coulomb continuum, which the authors show is not captured by a naive hyperspherical harmonics (HH) approach and instead requires a simplified Hamiltonian (SH) approximation.

## Method

The authors develop an ANC3 formalism within both the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method and a simplified Hamiltonian (SH) approach for three-body core+proton+proton systems. For the HH method, they replace the ground-state wave function by its long-range asymptotics expressed via the three-body ANC value C3 and Whittaker functions, then derive analytical expressions for the E1 strength function. For the SH method, they solve an inhomogeneous Schrodinger equation with a simplified Hamiltonian that retains only the core-proton interaction in one Jacobi coordinate, obtaining semi-analytical expressions for the E1 strength function that incorporate the energy distribution between the two emitted protons. The radiative capture 15O+p+p -> 17Ne+gamma is used as an illustrative test case.

## Key numbers

- For 6He, the ANC3 value is C3 = 0.3 fm^{-1/2}
- For 17Ne, the ANC3 values are C3 = 13400 fm^{-1/2} with Z_hh = 27.5 and C3 = 5958 fm^{-1/2} with fitted Z_hh = 26.14
- The effective charges for 17Ne are Z_hh^{(000)} = 27.50 for K=0 and Z_hh^{(101)} = 27.41 for K=1
- In the SH approach for 17Ne, the saddle point parameters are epsilon_0 = 0.48 and Z_sh = 23.282
- The Coulomb correction energy E_cy = 3.67 MeV for the Y-coordinate motion
- The ANC3 approximation of the overlap integral deviates within about 7% from direct calculation in a broad energy range (E_T less than about 1 MeV)
- The saddle point integration accuracy is about 2% at 0.1 MeV and about 6% at 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ANC method, previously established for two-body non-resonant radiative capture processes (Xu 1994, Timofeyuk 2003, Pang 2007, Mukhamedzhanov 2019), to three-body 2p radiative captures. It builds on prior numerical three-body studies of 2n and 2p captures in 6He and 17Ne by Grigorenko (2006, 2007, 2018, 2020) and Parfenova (2018), which revealed the importance of near-threshold resonances in two-body subsystems and the highly correlated nature of the capture process. The work enables fully analytical calculations of astrophysical capture rates for 2p processes that previously required bulky numerical three-body calculations, though the authors note that for 2n captures the practical applicability of the ANC3 method remains questionable due to complications from Pauli-suppressed configurations and final-state neutron-neutron interactions.
