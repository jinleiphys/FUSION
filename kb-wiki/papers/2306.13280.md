# Disoriented isospin condensates may be the source of anomalous kaon correlations measured in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV

> The magnitude of fluctuations between charged and neutral kaons measured by the ALICE Collaboration in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC exceeds conventional explanation. Here it is shown that if the scalar condensate, which is typically associated with chiral symmetry, is accompanied by an isospin=1 field, then the combination can produce large fluctuations where $\langle \bar{u}u\rangle \ne \langle \bar{d}d\rangle$. Hadronizing strange and anti-strange quarks might then strongly fluctuate between charged ($u\bar{s}$ or $s\bar{u}$) and neutral ($d\bar{s}$ or $s\bar{d}$) kaons.

# Disoriented isospin condensates may be the source of anomalous kaon correlations measured in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV

## Key claim
If the scalar quark-antiquark condensate associated with chiral symmetry breaking is accompanied by an isospin=1 field, event-by-event fluctuations between $\langle \bar{u}u \rangle$ and $\langle \bar{d}d \rangle$ condensates can produce the large charged-versus-neutral kaon fluctuations observed by ALICE, provided the condensate fluctuations occur in domains of sufficient size and the fraction of quarks arising from condensates is substantial.

## Method
The authors model the scalar condensate fields using a 2+1 flavor linear sigma model potential with parameters fixed to reproduce vacuum pion and kaon decay constants ($f_\pi = 92.1$ MeV, $f_K = 110.1$ MeV) and lattice QCD temperature dependence from the HotQCD Collaboration. They compute the free-energy penalty $\Delta U/T$ for fluctuations away from the isospin-symmetric minimum $\sigma_u = \sigma_d$ as a function of temperature and the mixing angle $\theta$ between up and down condensates, then evaluate the relative statistical probability $e^{-V\Delta U/T}$ for different domain volumes. The resulting probability distribution for $\theta$ is used to calculate $\tilde{\nu}$, the contribution of two kaons from the same domain to the $\nu_{\rm dyn}$ observable, which is then related to the ALICE measurement through the number of independent domains and the fraction of quarks coming from condensates.

## Key numbers
- ALICE measured $\nu_{\rm dyn}/\alpha \approx 0.2$ for the most central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, about five times the expected value from charge conservation effects.
- The vacuum condensate parameters are $f_\pi = 92.1$ MeV and $f_K = 110.1$ MeV from the Particle Data Group.
- The light quark condensate temperature dependence is parameterized with $A=0.01984$, $T_0=161.7$ MeV, $\Delta T=9.009$ MeV; the strange quark condensate with $A=0.02402$, $T_0=194.0$ MeV, $\Delta T=22.25$ MeV.
- The linear sigma model parameters are $\lambda = 15.01$, $\lambda' = -2.176$, $\mu^2 = -(472.8\ \text{MeV})^2$, $c(0) = 1.732$ GeV, $c' m_q = 8.775\times10^5\ \text{MeV}^3$, and $c' m_s = 2.617\times10^7\ \text{MeV}^3$.
- At hadronization temperature $T\approx 160$ MeV, a pure $\langle \bar{u}u \rangle$ or pure $\langle \bar{d}d \rangle$ condensate is disfavored by a factor of approximately $1/2$ for a volume of 10 fm$^3$, and has vanishingly small probability for 100 fm$^3$.
- For the 10-15% centrality bin, if 25% of quarks arise from condensates spread over 6 domains with 125 kaons in the acceptance, the ALICE measurement can be explained if $\tilde{\nu}$ is close to $1/3$.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Kapusta (2022) which showed that conventional correlation sources (local charge conservation, particle decays, Bose-Einstein effects) could not explain the ALICE measurement of $\nu_{\rm dyn}$ for central collisions. It differs from earlier disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) searches by Anselm, Blaizot, Rajagopal, and Bjorken (1990s), which focused on pion fluctuations and found no evidence in experiments by WA98, NA49, and MiniMax. The proposed disoriented isospin condensate (DIC) mechanism is presented as less exotic than competing explanations involving strange DCC or coherent kaon fields from off-diagonal condensates such as $\langle \bar{u}s \rangle$, which are absent in the QCD vacuum. The authors predict that measuring fluctuations of other particle pairs rich in $u/\bar{u}$ versus $d/\bar{d}$, such as $\Xi^0$ and $\Xi^-$, could test and potentially verify the DIC interpretation.
