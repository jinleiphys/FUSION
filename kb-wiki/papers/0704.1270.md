# Core-Corona Separation in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Simple geometrical considerations show that the collision zone in high energy nuclear collisions may be divided into a central part (``core''), with high energy densities, and a peripheral part (``corona''), with smaller energy densities, more like in pp or pA collisions. We present calculations which allow to separate these two contributions, and which show that the corona contribution is quite small (but not negligible) for central collisions, but gets increasingly important with decreasing centrality. We will discuss consequences concerning results obtained in heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS).

# Core-Corona Separation in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The collision zone in high-energy nuclear collisions can be divided into a high-density central "core" and a low-density peripheral "corona," where the corona contribution is small but non-negligible for central collisions and becomes increasingly important with decreasing centrality; the centrality dependence of hadron yields is essentially determined by the relative weight of core versus corona contributions, which depends on hadron type.

## Method

The author used the EPOS parton model to simulate AuAu and PbPb collisions, identifying string segments at an early proper time τ₀ and classifying them as core or corona based on whether the local string segment density exceeds a critical density ρ₀. The corona part was treated using standard EPOS particle production (as in pp or pA collisions), while the core part was parameterized with covariant statistical hadronization including collective radial flow with a linear rapidity profile, azimuthal asymmetry proportional to initial spatial eccentricity, and microcanonical conservation of energy, momentum, and flavor.

## Key numbers

- Core formation density ρ₀ = 2/fm²
- Freeze-out energy density ε_FO = 0.22 GeV/fm³
- Maximum radial flow y_rad = 0.75 + 0.20 × log(√s/200 GeV) at RHIC energies
- Eccentricity coefficient f_ecc = 0.45
- Strangeness suppression factor γ_s = 1.3
- Core formation time τ₀ = 1 fm (results insensitive to variations up to a factor of 2)
- The combined core+corona approach describes all available p_t data (π⁺, K⁺, p, Λ̄, Ξ̄⁺) for p_t ≤ 5 GeV within 20%
- For SPS (17.3 GeV) compared to RHIC (200 GeV), the corona contribution is larger at the lower energy

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization models (Author, various years) and blast-wave fits (Author, various years) by enabling a global fit across all energies, centralities, and colliding systems with a single set of parameters, whereas previous approaches required separate fit parameters for each condition. The core-corona separation provides a mechanism to explain the centrality dependence of hadron yields and the nuclear modification factor R_AA, showing that the different behavior of baryons versus mesons arises from the contrast between statistical hadronization with flow (core) and string fragmentation (corona). This enables a unified understanding of RHIC and SPS data where the corona contribution washes out the extreme effects seen in R_AA when considering R_cp (central over peripheral) ratios.
