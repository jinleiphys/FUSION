# Charged kaon production by coherent scattering of neutrinos and antineutrinos on nuclei

> With the aim of achieving a better and more complete understanding of neutrino interactions with nuclear targets, the coherent production of charged kaons induced by neutrinos and antineutrinos is investigated in the energy range of some of the current neutrino experiments. We follow a microscopic approach which, at the nucleon level, incorporates the most important mechanisms allowed by the chiral symmetry breaking pattern of QCD. The distortion of the outgoing (anti)kaon is taken into account by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with realistic optical potentials. Angular and momentum distributions are studied, as well as the energy and nuclear dependence of the total cross section.

# Charged kaon production by coherent scattering of neutrinos and antineutrinos on nuclei

## Key claim
Coherent charged kaon production on nuclear targets via (anti)neutrino scattering has very small cross sections in the few-GeV region, with per-nucleon values roughly 40 times smaller than the corresponding free-nucleon cross sections at 2 GeV, because the large kaon mass forces high momentum transfers that suppress nuclear form factors. The authors qualify that their microscopic model is restricted to neutrino energies up to 2 GeV for K+ production and to antikaon momenta below 1 GeV/c for K- production.

## Method
The authors compute coherent K+ and K- production on nuclear targets by summing elementary kaon-production amplitudes on nucleons derived from chiral SU(3) Lagrangians, using a prescription that shares the momentum transfer equally between initial and final nucleons. The outgoing kaon or antikaon wave function is obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with realistic optical potentials: a t-rho approximation for K+ (repulsive real part, absorptive imaginary part from KN total cross sections) and a chiral unitary coupled-channel model for K- (including s-wave and p-wave contributions from hyperon-hole excitations). Nuclear densities are taken from electron scattering data and Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Key numbers
- At E_nu = 2 GeV, the coherent K+ cross section per nucleon on 12C is approximately 40 times smaller than the elementary cross section on free nucleons averaged over protons and neutrons.
- Reducing the kaon mass by a factor of 2 increases the integrated cross section by a factor of 10 at E_nu = 1 GeV and by a factor of 68 at E_nu = 1.5 GeV.
- The contact term (CT) contribution to the K+ coherent cross section is more than 25 times larger than the sum of all other mechanisms at E_nu = 2 GeV before interference.
- At E_antinu = 2 GeV, the coherent K- cross section per nucleon on 12C is approximately 40 times smaller than the elementary cross section averaged over protons and neutrons.
- The K- distortion reduces the cross section more strongly than K+ distortion due to the stronger antikaon-nucleus interaction.
- The optical potential for K+ uses C = 0.13 and normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3.
- The K- optical potential is restricted to antikaon momenta |p_K| <= 1 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic approach developed for coherent pion production by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2007) and Amaro et al. (2008) to the strangeness sector, using the elementary kaon production models on nucleons from Rafi Alam et al. (2010) for K+ and Alam et al. (2012) for K-. It contrasts with earlier theoretical studies that assumed single nucleon targets (Rafi Alam 2010, Alam 2012) and with the PCAC-based models used for coherent pion production (Paschos 2005, Berger 2008). The results provide baseline predictions for coherent kaon production that could be tested by MINERvA and other experiments, and contribute to understanding backgrounds for proton decay searches (where atmospheric neutrino-induced K+ production mimics p -> antinu K+) and for neutrino oscillation measurements.
