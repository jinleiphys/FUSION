# Normal-ordering approximations and translational (non) invariance

> Normal-ordering provides an approach to approximate three-body forces as effective two-body operators and it is therefore an important tool in many-body calculations with realistic nuclear interactions. The corresponding neglect of certain three-body terms in the normal-ordered Hamiltonian is known to influence translational invariance, although the magnitude of this effect has not yet been systematically quantified. In this work we study in particular the normal-ordering two-body approximation applied to a single harmonic-oscillator reference state. We explicate the breaking of translational invariance and demonstrate the magnitude of the approximation error as a function of model space parameters for $^4\rm{He}$ and $^{16}\rm{O}$ by performing full no-core shell-model calculations with and without three-nucleon forces. We combine two different diagnostics to better monitor the breaking of translational invariance. While the center-of-mass effect is shown to become potentially very large for $^4\rm{He}$, it is also shown to be much smaller for $^{16}\rm{O}$ although full convergence is not reached. These tools can be easily implemented in studies using other many-body frameworks and bases.

## Key claim

The single-reference normal-ordering two-body (SR-NO2B) approximation of three-nucleon forces explicitly breaks translational invariance of the Hamiltonian, and the magnitude of this center-of-mass contamination depends sensitively on the choice of harmonic-oscillator basis frequency and model-space truncation, with effects that can be very large for light systems like ⁴He but much smaller for ¹⁶O.

## Method

The authors performed full no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations for ⁴He and ¹⁶O using the NNLOsat chiral interaction, comparing results with full three-nucleon forces against those using the SR-NO2B approximation where the residual three-body term is discarded. They introduced two complementary diagnostics, the ξ_CM metric and the N_CM(ω_N) metric, to quantify center-of-mass contamination in the ground-state wave function, and benchmarked these metrics using an external harmonic-oscillator trap potential. The calculations were carried out for multiple model-space truncations N_max and basis frequencies ℏΩ to map the approximation error.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He, the center-of-mass effect from the NO2B approximation can become "potentially very large" depending on basis parameters; the study reached N_max ≤ 10 for full three-nucleon-force calculations (5.4 × 10⁹ matrix elements at N_max = 10, versus 66.5 × 10⁹ that would be required at N_max = 12)
- For ¹⁶O, the center-of-mass effect is "much smaller" than for ⁴He, though full convergence was not reached; calculations with full three-nucleon forces reached N_max ≤ 6 (D = 1.6 × 10⁶ basis states), while NO2B-approximated calculations reached N_max ≤ 8 (D = 6 × 10⁸ basis states)
- The benchmark with an external trap potential at ℏω_trap = 20 MeV showed that both ξ_CM and N_CM(ω_N) correctly identified decoupling for basis frequencies ℏΩ from 8 to 36 MeV, while the standard metric N_CM(Ω) only worked when Ω equaled the trap frequency

## Context

This work systematically quantifies the translational-invariance breaking in the SR-NO2B approximation, which had been noted but not systematically studied in prior benchmarks by Hagen (2007), Roth (2012), and Binder (2013) that were performed at fixed oscillator frequency. The study extends the center-of-mass diagnostics introduced by Parzuchowski (2017) and Hagen (2009) to the specific problem of normal-ordering approximations, and provides tools that can be implemented in other many-body frameworks such as coupled-cluster and configuration-interaction methods. The results enable better monitoring of approximation errors in medium-mass nuclear structure calculations that rely on normal-ordered three-nucleon forces.
