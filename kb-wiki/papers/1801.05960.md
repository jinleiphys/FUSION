# In-medium $\mbox{NN}\to \mbox{N}Δ$ cross section and its dependence on effective Lagrange parameters in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> The in-medium $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ cross section and its differential cross section in isospin asymmetric nuclear medium are investigated in the framework of the one-boson exchange model by including the isovector mesons, i.e., $δ$ and $ρ$ mesons. Our results show that the in-medium $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ cross sections are suppressed with density increasing, and the differential cross sections become isotropic with the density increasing at the beam energy around the $Δ$ threshold energy. The isospin splitting on the medium correction factor, $R=σ_{ NN\rightarrow NΔ}^*/σ_{NN\rightarrow NΔ}^{\text{free}}$ is observed for different channels of $NN\to NΔ$, especially around the threshold energy for all the effective Lagrangian parameters. By analyzing the selected effective Lagrangian parameters, our results show that the larger effective mass is, the weaker medium correction $R$ is.

## Key claim

The in-medium $NN\rightarrow N\Delta$ cross section is suppressed with increasing density, and the medium correction factor $R = \sigma^*_{NN\rightarrow N\Delta}/\sigma^{\text{free}}_{NN\rightarrow N\Delta}$ exhibits isospin splitting for different reaction channels, particularly near the $\Delta$ threshold energy; however, the magnitude of this medium correction depends on the effective Lagrangian parameters, with larger effective nucleon mass corresponding to weaker medium suppression.

## Method

The authors employed a one-boson exchange model within a relativistic Lagrangian framework including $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\delta$, and $\pi$ mesons, using three parameter sets (NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$, DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$, and DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$) that produce different nuclear matter properties. The in-medium cross sections were calculated by replacing free-space masses and momenta with effective Dirac masses and kinetic momenta in the matrix elements, and the $\Delta$ resonance was treated by averaging over its mass distribution using a spectral function with a decay width parameterization. The calculations were performed for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter with the isovector mesons $\rho$ and $\delta$ included to account for isospin dependence.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.160$ fm$^{-3}$ for NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$, 0.152 fm$^{-3}$ for DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$, and 0.153 fm$^{-3}$ for DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$
- Effective nucleon mass ratio $m^*_N/m_N = 0.75$ for NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$, 0.609 for DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$, and 0.55 for DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$ at saturation density
- Effective $\Delta$ pole mass ratio $m^*_\Delta/m_\Delta = 0.809$ for NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$, 0.702 for DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$, and 0.661 for DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$ at saturation density
- Symmetry energy coefficient $S_0 = 30.60$ MeV (NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$), 32.35 MeV (DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$), and 25.34 MeV (DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$)
- Slope of symmetry energy $L = 101.46$ MeV (NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$), 52.85 MeV (DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$), and 45.33 MeV (DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$)
- Incompressibility $K_0 = 240.0$ MeV (NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$), 219.1 MeV (DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$), and 240.2 MeV (DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$)
- Binding energy per nucleon $E/A = -16.00$ MeV (NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$), $-16.12$ MeV (DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$), and $-16.25$ MeV (DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$)
- Effective mass splitting $\Delta m^*_N = (m^*_p - m^*_n)/m_N = 0.0312$ (NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$), 0.0236 (DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$), and 0.0265 (DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$)
- Effective mass splitting $\Delta m^*_\Delta = (m^*_{\Delta^{++}} - m^*_{\Delta^+})/m_\Delta = 0.0079$ (NL$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$), 0.0060 (DDME$\delta$-$\Delta$), and 0.0068 (DDRH$\rho\delta$-$\Delta$)

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium $NN\rightarrow N\Delta$ cross sections by Li and Li (2017) in the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck framework, which showed isospin-dependent medium corrections without considering the $\Delta$ mass distribution. The present paper incorporates the $\Delta$ spectral function and examines three different effective Lagrangian parameter sets (NL$\rho\delta$, DDME$\delta$, DDRH$\rho\delta$) to connect the in-medium cross section to nuclear matter properties such as effective mass and symmetry energy slope. The results enable more consistent treatment of mean field and collision terms in transport models for heavy ion collisions near the $\Delta$ threshold energy, particularly for constraining the symmetry energy at suprasaturation density.
