# The Cheshire Cat Hadrons Revisited

> The notion of Cheshire Cat Principle in hadron structure developed in a 1993 lecture note is revisited by correcting nontrivial errors committed in the lecture and bringing up to date with the recent developments on the "vector manifestation" of chiral symmetry a la Harada-Yamawaki.

## Key claim

The Cheshire Cat Principle (CCP), the idea that low-energy hadron properties should not depend on the size of the confinement region, is revisited and corrected for errors in the 1993 lecture, and is shown to be exact for topological quantities in (3+1) dimensions while only approximate for nontopological observables, with the modern development involving "color flavor locking" and the matching of effective field theories to QCD via hidden gauge symmetry.

## Method

The paper uses the chiral bag model in (1+1) dimensions as a toy system where the CCP can be formulated exactly, then extends the reasoning to (3+1) dimensions. The method involves constructing an action with three terms, inside the bag (quarks), outside the bag (pions), and a boundary term, and showing that quantum anomalies cause fermion number to leak from the bag into the meson sector, with the total baryon charge remaining independent of bag size. The Wess-Zumino-Witten term is derived from the bag boundary condition using path-integral methods and point-splitting regularization.

## Key numbers

* In (1+1) dimensions, the pion decay constant is fixed as \( f = 1/\sqrt{4\pi} \).
* The scalar mass in the Schwinger model is obtained as \( m_\phi^2 = e^2/\pi \), derived from the CCP and the \( U_A(1) \) anomaly.
* The baryon charge partition in (1+1) dimensions is \( Q = 1 = Q_V + Q_{\tilde{V}} \), with \( Q_{\tilde{V}} = \theta/\pi \) and \( Q_V = 1 - \theta/\pi \), where \( \theta = \phi(R)/f \).

## Context

This work extends the Cheshire Cat Principle originally developed in the "little bag" model of Brown and Rho (1979) and the 1993 ELAF93 lectures, correcting errors in that earlier treatment. It connects to the Harada-Yamawaki "vector manifestation" of chiral symmetry and to the notion of "BR scaling" of hadron properties in density and temperature. The paper enables further work on gluon-meson/quark-baryon dualities and the matching of effective field theories with QCD, particularly for hadronic matter under extreme conditions.
