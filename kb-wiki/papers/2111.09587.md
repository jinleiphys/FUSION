# Application of the single-channel, single-energy AA/PWA method to $K^{+} Λ$ photoproduction

> The new single-channel, single-energy partial wave analysis method based on a simultaneous use of amplitude and partial wave analysis called AA/PWA, developed and tested on $η$ photoproduction in ref. Svarc et al, PRC 102, 064609 (2020), is applied to the $K^{+} Λ$ photoproduction for the center-of-mass energy range of 1625 MeV $< W <$ 2296 MeV. A complete set of multipoles has been created. The advantages of the method have been confirmed, and a comparison with the only existing single-energy partial wave analysis of $K^{+} Λ$ photoproduction given in refs. Anisovich et al PRL 119 062004 (2017) and Anisovich et al Eur. Phys. J. A 53: 242 (2017) is presented. We confirm the size and shape of Bonn-Gatchina multipoles, but we do not confirm the unambiguous interpretation of the structure in the $M_{1-}$ multipole as a $N(1880) \frac{1}{2}^{+}$ resonance. The decisive role of the self-consistency of the world database is emphasized.

## Key claim

The AA/PWA method, when applied to \(K^{+}\Lambda\) photoproduction, confirms the size and shape of the Bonn-Gatchina multipoles but does **not** confirm the unambiguous interpretation of the structure in the \(M_{1-}\) multipole as a \(N(1880) \frac{1}{2}^{+}\) resonance, emphasizing that the result depends critically on the self-consistency of the world database.

## Method

The authors applied the single-channel, single-energy AA/PWA method, which first performs an amplitude analysis of the database by fitting the moduli of the four reaction amplitudes while fixing their phases to those of the Bonn-Gatchina BG2017 energy-dependent coupled-channels model (thereby eliminating the continuum ambiguity). In the second step, a truncated partial wave analysis (TPWA) is performed, using a penalization technique to force the reaction amplitudes from the fitted multipoles to stay close to the amplitudes obtained in the first step, ensuring continuity in energy via the fixed phase and continuity in angle via the TPWA. The analysis was carried out on a world database of eight observables for \(K^{+}\Lambda\) photoproduction in the center-of-mass energy range 1625 MeV \(< W <\) 2296 MeV, using a two-dimensional interpolation of the data in energy and angle rather than standard binning.

## Key numbers

*   The analysis covers the center-of-mass energy range 1625 MeV \(< W <\) 2296 MeV.
*   The database includes 3615 data points for \(d\sigma/d\Omega\) (from CLAS 2007 and CLAS 2010), 400 for \(\Sigma\) (from GRAAL 2007 and CLAS 2016), 408 for \(T\) (from GRAAL 2007 and CLAS 2016), 1597 for \(P\) (from CLAS 2010 and GRAAL 2007), 415 each for \(O_{x'}\) and \(O_{z'}\) (from GRAAL 2007 and CLAS 2016), and 138 each for \(C_x\) and \(C_z\) (from CLAS 2007).
*   The penalty factor \(\lambda\) in the TPWA step was set to 250.
*   The analysis was performed on a grid of 141 energy points and 16 fixed angular points in \(\cos(\Theta)\) from -0.7 to 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the AA/PWA method, previously developed and tested on \(\eta\) photoproduction (Svarc et al., PRC 102, 064609, 2020), to the \(K^{+}\Lambda\) photoproduction reaction, which has the same isospin structure. It directly compares its results with the only existing single-energy partial wave analysis of \(K^{+}\Lambda\) photoproduction by the Bonn-Gatchina group (Anisovich et al., PRL 119, 062004, 2017 and Eur. Phys. J. A 53: 242, 2017), using the same database and the same constraining energy-dependent model (BG2017) obtained via private communication. The method enables a minimally model-dependent extraction of partial waves that can be compared with coupled-channels models, and it highlights the decisive role of data self-consistency in determining the uniqueness of the solution.
