# Quark model estimate of hidden-charm pentaquark resonances

> A quark model, which reproduces the ground-state mesons and baryons, i.e., the threshold energies, is applied to the $qqqc\bar c$ configurations, where $q$ is a light quark and~$c$ the charmed quark. In the calculation, several open channels are explicitly included such as $\Jpsi +N$, $η_c+N$, $Λ_c +D$, etc. To distinguish genuine resonances and estimate their width, we employ Gaussian Expansion Method supplemented by the real scaling method (stabilization). No resonance is found at the energies of the $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ pentaquarks. On the other hand, there is a sharp resonant state at 4690\,MeV with $J=1/2^-$ state and another one at 4920\,MeV with $J=3/2^-$ state, which have a compact structure.

## Key claim

Using a non-relativistic quark model with explicit coupling to open scattering channels, no resonant states are found at the masses of the LHCb \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) pentaquarks; instead, two narrow compact resonances appear at higher energies, 4690 MeV and 4920 MeV, which are too high to correspond to the observed states.

## Method

The authors solved the five-body Schrödinger equation for the \(uudc\bar{c}\) system using the Gaussian Expansion Method with four types of Jacobi coordinates, including both "connected" (color-confined) configurations and "scattering" configurations that allow fall-apart into color-singlet hadron pairs such as \(J/\psi+N\), \(\eta_c+N\), \(\Lambda_c+D\), and \(\Sigma_c+D\). To distinguish genuine resonances from discretized continuum states, they applied the real-scaling (stabilization) method, scaling the Gaussian range parameters of the scattering coordinates and identifying states whose energies remain stable as the scaling factor \(\alpha\) is varied.

## Key numbers

- No resonance found at the LHCb pentaquark energies of 4380 MeV and 4450 MeV.
- A sharp resonant state at 4690 MeV with \(J^P = 1/2^-\) (seed at 4708 MeV in the connected-only calculation).
- A sharp resonant state at 4920 MeV with \(J^P = 3/2^-\) (seed at 4896 MeV in the connected-only calculation).
- The lowest connected-only (no scattering) state for \(J^P = 1/2^-\) appears at 4119 MeV, above the \(\eta_c N\) and \(J/\psi N\) thresholds.
- The lowest connected-only state for \(J^P = 3/2^-\) appears at 4221 MeV.
- The light-quark (\(qq\)) correlation function peaks at about 0.8 fm, and the charm-quark (\(c\bar{c}\)) correlation function peaks at about 0.2 fm for the 4690 MeV resonance.
- The AL1 potential gives essentially the same resonance masses: 4690 MeV for \(J=1/2^-\) and 4920 MeV for \(J=3/2^-\).

## Context

This work extends the quark-model approach to hidden-charm pentaquarks by explicitly including coupling to multiple open meson-baryon channels, a technique previously applied by Hiyama and Hosaka (2005) to the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark. The results contradict molecular or diquark models that predict resonances near the LHCb masses (e.g., Maiani (2015), Karliner (2015), Chen (2015)), and instead suggest that the observed \(P_c\) states may require additional dynamics beyond the simple quark-quark potential used here. The method enables future quark-model studies to reliably identify genuine multiquark resonances rather than discretization artifacts.
