# Residual cut-off dependence and power counting: the deuteron as a case study

> Effective field theories (EFTs) require regularization and renormalization to gain predictive power. While regularization is inconsequential from the point of view of the observable predictions of EFT -- in a renormalized theory we expect predictions to be regulator-independent once the cutoff is removed -- the particular details of regulator dependence might provide interesting insights into the inner workings of an EFT. In fact, the analysis of regulator dependence has been frequently suggested as a tool to study the ordering scheme or power counting of EFTs. We show here that the choice of the regulator might impact the power law properties of the residual cutoff dependence. If this conclusion were to be confirmed, it would have consequences on the validity of this method as a tool to analyze power counting.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the choice of regulator in effective field theories can impact the power-law properties of the residual cutoff dependence, which if confirmed would challenge the validity of using regulator dependence as a tool to analyze power counting. The authors hedge that numerical complexities make it difficult to give a definite answer, and their conclusions are presented as suggestive rather than definitive.

## Method
The authors studied the leading-order one-pion exchange potential in the spin-triplet channel of chiral effective field theory, regularizing and renormalizing it using five different techniques: boundary condition at a cutoff radius, delta-shell regularization, local Gaussian regulator, semilocal regulator, and nonlocal momentum-space regulator. They calculated the deuteron binding energy (fixed at 2.2 MeV as the renormalization condition) and the spin-triplet neutron-proton scattering length, then analyzed the residual cutoff dependence by fitting the scattering length as a function of the coordinate-space cutoff to extract the power-law exponent of the convergence.

## Key numbers
- The asymptotic spin-triplet scattering length converges to a_t = 5.317 fm for all regularization schemes once the cutoff is sufficiently hard.
- The extracted power-law exponent n_opt for the residual cutoff dependence varies significantly across regulators: nonlocal gives 1.3 ± 0.2, semi-local gives 3.4 ± 1, local gives 6 ± 3, boundary condition gives 1.4 ± 0.35, and delta-shell gives 2 ± 0.9.
- The deuteron binding energy was fixed at 2.2 MeV as the renormalization condition for all schemes.
- The R ranges over which fits were performed differ: nonlocal [0.02, 0.084] fm, semi-local [0.03, 0.06] fm, local [0.07, 0.2] fm, boundary condition [0.17, 0.45] fm, delta-shell [0.15, 0.5] fm.

## Context
This work extends the debate on Weinberg counting versus alternative power counting schemes for chiral effective field theories, following prior work by Kaplan (1996), Nogga (2005), and the ongoing discussion between Epelbaum (2018, 2019), Pavon Valderrama (2019), Machleidt (2020), and van Kolck (2020). The analysis of residual cutoff dependence was previously proposed as a diagnostic tool by Long (2011, 2012), Griesshammer (2015, 2020), and Valderrama (2016), but this paper suggests that the method may be regulator-dependent and therefore unreliable. The results enable future work to develop more robust methods for determining power counting in non-perturbative EFTs, and the authors note that consistent comparisons might still be possible when using the same regulator across different systems, as done by Odell (2019).
