# Shell and alpha cluster structures in 8Be with tensor-optimized shell model

> We study the shell and alpha cluster structures in the ground and excited states of 8Be in terms of the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM). In TOSM, the tensor correlation is optimized in the full space of 2p2h configurations involving high-momentum components. The short-range correlation is treated with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). We use the effective interaction based on the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction AV8$^\prime$. The 8Be states consist of two groups of ground band states and highly excited states with the isospin T=0 and T=1. It is found that the tensor contributions of the ground band states are stronger than the highly excited states and that the kinetic energies and the central contributions of the ground band states are almost twice the 4He values. These features suggest two-alpha clustering for the ground band states in 8Be. We also estimate the correlation energy of the $α$ clustering using the alpha cluster model. In the highly excited states, the calculated spectrum in TOSM reproduces the experimental level order and the relative energies of each level. This agreement suggests that those states can be interpreted as shell-like states. The level order is found to be sensitive to the presence of the tensor force in comparison with the results using the Minnesota effective interaction without the tensor force. It is also found that the tensor contributions in the T=0 states are stronger than the T=1 states, which is consistent with the state dependence of the tensor force.

## Key claim

The ground band states of ⁸Be (0⁺₁, 2⁺₁, 4⁺₁) exhibit two-alpha clustering, evidenced by kinetic energies and central contributions that are almost twice the ⁴He values, while the highly excited states above 16.6 MeV can be interpreted as shell-like states; the tensor force contributions are stronger in the T=0 states than in the T=1 states, and the level order of highly excited states is sensitive to the presence of the tensor force.

## Method

The authors applied the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) combined with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) to ⁸Be, using an effective interaction (AV8′ₑff) based on the bare AV8′ nucleon-nucleon interaction with enhanced tensor (10%) and LS (40%) matrix elements to reproduce rigorous ⁴He results. The TOSM wave function includes 0p0h, 1p1h, and 2p2h configurations with high-momentum components, using Gaussian basis functions for particle states up to Lmax=12, and the short-range central correlation was treated via UCOM. Results were compared with calculations using the Minnesota effective interaction, which lacks a tensor force.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of ⁴He in TOSM+UCOM: −20.46 MeV; with S-UCOM: −22.30 MeV (rigorous SVM value: −25.92 MeV)
- Tensor contribution to ⁴He in TOSM+UCOM: −51.06 MeV; with S-UCOM: −54.55 MeV (SVM: −68.32 MeV)
- Matter radius of ⁴He: 1.52 fm (TOSM); 1.39 fm (Minnesota interaction)
- (0s)⁴ configuration probability in ⁴He: 84.1% (AV8′ₑff); 96.6% (Minnesota)
- With enhanced interaction (X_T=1.1, X_LS=1.4): ⁴He total energy −26.16 MeV, kinetic 95.45 MeV, central −56.17 MeV, tensor −62.43 MeV, LS −3.02 MeV
- Ground band states of ⁸Be: kinetic energies and central contributions are almost twice the ⁴He values
- Highly excited states in ⁸Be start from the 2⁺₂ state at E_x=16.6 MeV; decay widths of these states are almost less than 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TOSM studies of tensor correlations in He and Li isotopes (Myo 2007, 2009, 2011, 2012) to the 4N nucleus ⁸Be, which exhibits coexistence of alpha cluster and shell-like structures. The ground band states are understood as two-alpha cluster states following Ikeda (1968) and Pieper (2004), while the highly excited states are interpreted as shell-like states. The tensor force's role in producing the splitting between p3/2- and p1/2-dominant states in He and Li isotopes (Myo 2005, 2006, 2011, 2012) is extended here to explain the different structures in ⁸Be. This work enables future studies of tensor force effects in heavier 4N nuclei and the interplay between cluster and shell-model configurations.
