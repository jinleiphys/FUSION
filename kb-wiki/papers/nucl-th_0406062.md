# Gamow-Teller GT+ distributions in nuclei with mass A=90-97

> We investigate the Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the electron-capture direction in nuclei having mass A=90-97, assuming a 88Sr core and using a realistic interaction that reasonably reproduces nuclear spectroscopy for a wide range of nuclei in the region as well as experimental data on Gamow-Teller strength distributions. We discuss the systematics of the distributions and their centroids. We also predict the strength distributions for several nuclei involving stable isotopes that should be experimentally accessible for one-particle exchange reactions in the near future.

# Gamow-Teller GT+ distributions in nuclei with mass A=90-97

## Key claim

The authors calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the electron-capture direction for 36 nuclei with A=90-97 using a realistic interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential, but note that because their model space is not a complete 0ħω calculation, the total Gamow-Teller strength is overestimated and must be corrected by a phenomenological quenching factor of approximately 4.25, estimated from total absorption spectrometry data on ⁹⁷Ag.

## Method

Shell-model diagonalization calculations were performed in a model space using ⁸⁸Sr as a core, with valence protons in the 1p₁/₂ and 0g₉/₂ shells and valence neutrons in the 1d₅/₂, 2s₁/₂, 1d₃/₂, 0g₇/₂, and 0h₁₁/₂ shells. The effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn potential using many-body perturbation theory, and the method of moments was used to obtain strength distributions, with 33 Lanczos iterations per final Jᶠ for most nuclei. The calculations were performed using the new parallel shell-model code ORPAH (Oak Ridge PArallel shell model code) on up to 80 processors.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor h = 4.25 (range 3.74 to 4.90) derived from ⁹⁷Ag total absorption spectrometry data where ΣB(GT) = 3.00(40) was reported by Hu et al.
- For ⁹⁸Ag, total strength 2.7(4) gives hindrance factor h = 4.27 (range 3.72 to 5.01), with calculated B_QEC = 11.53 (92% of total GT strength inside Q_EC = 8.24 MeV window)
- Calculated total GT+ strength for ⁹⁰Zr is S(GT+) = 0.34, compared to experimental (n,p) value of 1.0 ± 0.3 from Raywood et al.
- Fraction of calculated GT strength inside Q_EC window: ⁹⁷Ag = 98%, ⁹⁸Ag = 92%, ⁹⁶Pd = 72%, ⁹⁵Rh = 67%, ⁹⁷Pd = 68%, ⁹⁶Rh = 57%, ⁹⁷Rh = 30%, ⁹⁴Ru = 19%, ⁹⁵Ru = 8%, ⁹³Tc = 2%, ⁹⁴Tc = 2%, ⁹⁵Tc = 1%, ⁹⁶Tc = 1%
- Centroid of experimental GT strength in ⁹⁷Ag: E_centr_exp = 4.3 MeV versus calculated E_centr_calc = 4.7 MeV
- Approximate formula for total GT+ strength: GT+ = 0.086(Z_v - 1.5)(20 - N_v), with χ² per degree of freedom = 0.05
- Single-particle estimate for total GT+ strength in ¹⁰⁰Sn: GT+(¹⁰⁰Sn) = 17.78

## Context

This work extends shell-model calculations of Gamow-Teller strength distributions from the 0f-1p shell (mass A=40-80, studied by Langanke (1995), Martinez (1996), and Caurier (1999)) into the mass A=90-97 region above ⁸⁸Sr. The calculations are motivated by proposed (t,³He) reaction measurements on stable Zr and Mo isotopes by Zegers et al. (2004), and the results are relevant for electron capture rates in type II supernova core collapse, following the work of Langanke (2003) and Hix (2003). The paper enables future experimental comparisons for one-particle exchange reactions on stable isotopes in this mass region and provides systematics of GT centroids that confirm the interpretation by Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000) that odd-even staggering in centroid energies disappears when measured from the parent ground state.
