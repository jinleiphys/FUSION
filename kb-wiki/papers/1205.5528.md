# Simulations of Pion Production in the DAEδALUS Target

> DAEδALUS, the Decay At-rest Experiment for δ_{CP} at a Laboratory for Underground Science will look for evidence of CP-violation in the neutrino sector, an ingredient in theories that seek to explain the matter/antimatter asymmetry in our universe. It will make a precision measurement of the oscillations of muon antineutrinos to electron antineutrinos using multiple neutrino sources created by low-cost compact cyclotrons. The experiment utilizes decay-at-rest neutrino beams produced by 800 MeV protons impinging a beam target of graphite and copper. Two well established Monte Carlo codes, MARS and GEANT4, have been used to optimise the design and the performance of the target. A study of the results obtained with these two codes is presented in this paper.

# Simulations of Pion Production in the DAEδALUS Target

## Key claim
MARS and GEANT4 simulations of pion production in the DAEδALUS target consistently agree on pion production rates within 30%, and both codes compare well to experimental data in the energy region of interest below 800 MeV, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that the GEANT4 Bertini model is closest to experimental data among the three GEANT4 models tested, and the comparison relies on thin-target benchmarks rather than the full meter-scale target design.

## Method
The authors simulated 800 MeV protons impinging on a 5 mm thin graphite target using the MARS code (with the Cascade Exciton Model) and three GEANT4 models (Bertini Cascade, Binary Cascade, and Liège INCL coupled with ABLA). They counted total π⁺ production and inelastic proton interactions to derive cross-sections, then compared these to experimental data for proton-on-carbon collisions at energies between 300 and 2500 MeV. The ratio of π⁺ production cross-section to inelastic cross-section was computed using a thin-target approximation where target thickness (5 mm) is much smaller than interaction lengths (~1 m).

## Key numbers
- MARS and GEANT4 agree on pion production rates within **30%** for the models presented.
- The DAEδALUS target uses **800 MeV** protons impinging on a graphite and copper target.
- The thin target used for benchmarking is **5 mm** thick, compared to the full-scale target which will be on the order of **1 m** thick.
- The copper reduces the ratio of electron antineutrino background to muon antineutrino signal to approximately **10⁻³**.
- The GEANT4 models are validated for incident energies up to **10 GeV** (Bertini and Binary Cascade) and **200 MeV – 3 GeV** (Liège INCL).
- Experimental thin carbon targets used for comparison have thickness between **1 – 1.79 g/cm²** or **4.5 – 8 mm**.

## Context
This work extends the benchmarking of Monte Carlo simulation codes for neutrino source design, specifically for the DAEδALUS experiment which will measure CP-violation in the neutrino sector. The study compares MARS and GEANT4 against experimental pion production data from references such as pion_0.73, pion_0.585, and pion_1.6_2.5_3.5, and against proton inelastic cross-section data from proton_table and proton_table2. The results enable reweighting of simulation data to more accurately reflect experimental measurements, which will be used to determine cyclotron power cycles and required running times for the DAEδALUS experiment's physics goals.
