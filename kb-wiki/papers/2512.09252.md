# Thermal liquid-gas phase transition in a quasi-one-dimensional dipolar Fermi gas

> We theoretically investigate thermodynamic properties in a quasi-one-dimensional single-component dipolar Fermi gas at finite temperatures. The self-bound fermionic droplet can be achieved by exchange correlations with the long-range dipole-dipole interactions under the quasi-one-dimensional confinement, where the interaction can be tuned by tilting the dipoles along the system coordinate. Using the Hartree-Fock approximation, we show how the liquid-gas phase transition occurs in this system, and elucidate the finite-temperature phase structure consisting of the gas phase, liquid phase, their coexistence, and the spinodal phase. We also discuss its similarity with the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter through the comparison with phenomenological models. Our results would be useful for an interdisciplinary understanding of self-bound fermionic matter as well as an analog quantum simulation of nuclear systems.

## Key claim

Using the Hartree-Fock approximation, the authors show that a quasi-one-dimensional dipolar Fermi gas exhibits a thermal liquid-gas phase transition with a finite-temperature phase structure consisting of gas, liquid, coexistence, and spinodal phases, and they find that the critical-to-flash temperature ratio is approximately 1.53, which deviates from the classical Jaqaman model prediction of 4/3.

## Method

The authors employ the Hartree-Fock approximation at finite temperature for a single-component dipolar Fermi gas confined in a quasi-one-dimensional geometry, where the dipole-dipole interaction is tuned by tilting the dipoles along the longitudinal axis. They solve the self-consistent equation for the Hartree-Fock self-energy and the number-density equation to obtain thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy per particle, and free energy density. The flash point and critical point are determined from the conditions P = (∂P/∂n)_T = 0 and (∂P/∂n)_T = (∂²P/∂n²)_T = 0, respectively, and the coexistence line is found by locating isothermal points where both phases have identical pressure and chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature study of self-bound dipolar fermions in quasi-one-dimensional geometry by Astrakharchik and Morigi (2013) to finite temperatures, and draws parallels with the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter studied within the Hartree-Fock approximation by Rios (2010). The results enable analog quantum simulation of nuclear systems, particularly for understanding thermal effects in heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical phenomena such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers. The authors compare their Hartree-Fock results with two phenomenological models used in nuclear physics: the Jaqaman model (1983) and the Kapusta model (1984), finding that the Kapusta model with one-dimensional Sommerfeld expansion better describes the behavior near the flash point.
