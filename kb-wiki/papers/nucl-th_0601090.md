# Transport study of charged current interactions in neutrino-nucleus reactions

> Within a dynamical transport approach we investigate charged current interactions in neutrino-nucleus reactions for neutrino energies of 0.3 - 1.5 GeV with particular emphasis on resonant pion production channels via the $Δ_{33}(1232)$ resonance. The final-state-interactions of the resonance as well as of the emitted pions are calculated explicitly for $^{12}C$ and $^{56}Fe$ nuclei and show a dominance of pion suppression at moderate momenta $p_π>$ 0.2 GeV/c. A comparison to integrated $π^+$ spectra for $ν_μ+ ^{12}C$ reactions with the available (preliminary) data demonstrates a reasonable agreement.

## Key claim

Within a dynamical transport approach, the authors investigate charged current neutrino-nucleus reactions for neutrino energies of 0.3–1.5 GeV, with particular emphasis on resonant pion production via the Δ₃₃(1232) resonance; the abstract's claim of "reasonable agreement" with preliminary data is hedged by noting that any agreement or disagreement is primarily due to the uncertain axial-vector form factor, such that no final conclusions can be drawn from the comparison.

## Method

The authors employ the CBUU (Coupled-Channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport approach, which propagates Δ resonances and their decay products (pions and nucleons) through the residual nucleus using a perturbative treatment. The elementary neutrino-nucleon interaction is described by the Rein-Sehgal model with modified form factors, using two parameter sets: set A (M_V = 0.843 GeV, M_A = 1.032 GeV) and set B (M_V = 1 GeV, M_A = 1.2 GeV). The Δ spectral function includes a W-dependent width with Γ₀ = 110 MeV and β = 0.3 GeV, and final-state interactions (FSI) are computed explicitly for ¹²C and ⁵⁶Fe targets.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy range studied: 0.3–1.5 GeV.
- For ¹²C target, integrated π⁺ absorption is 12% and π⁰ absorption is 14%; with a detection threshold of T_π = 100 MeV, relative absorption rises to 33% for π⁺ and 35% for π⁰.
- For ⁵⁶Fe target, integrated π⁺ absorption is 35% and π⁰ absorption is 42%; with a detection threshold of T_π = 100 MeV, relative absorption rises to 58% for π⁺ and 64% for π⁰.
- The ratio R₀ of final to initial π⁰ momentum spectrum for ν_μ + ⁵⁶Fe at E_ν = 1 GeV is roughly constant at R ∼ 0.4 for √(P_T² + P_z²) > 0.2 GeV/c.
- The ratio of π⁺ cross section from proton relative to neutron is 3, determined by a relative Clebsch-Gordon coefficient of √3 in the decay amplitude.
- The Δ resonance mass is M_Δ = 1.232 GeV, with vacuum width parameters Γ₀ = 110 MeV and β = 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the CBUU transport model, previously tested on π, p, and relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (Cassing 1990, 1999) as well as photo-nuclear and electron-induced reactions (Effenberger, Muehlich), to neutrino-nucleus reactions. It contrasts with event generators like NUANCE, NEUT, NEUGEN, and NUX-FLUCA that lack explicit resonance propagation and in-medium decay. The study enables future differential analyses of FSI in neutrino-nucleus reactions, with the authors noting that precise double differential data on free nucleons are mandatory before addressing in-medium effects on resonance transitions.
