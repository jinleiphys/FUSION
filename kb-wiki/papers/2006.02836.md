# Factorial cumulants from global baryon number conservation

> The proton, antiproton and mixed proton-antiproton factorial cumulants originating from the global conservation of baryon number are calculated analytically up to the sixth order. Our results can be directly tested in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper calculates analytic expressions for proton, antiproton, and mixed proton-antiproton factorial cumulants up to sixth order under the assumption that global baryon number conservation is the *only* source of correlations, providing a baseline that experimental results can be tested against.

## Method

The authors start with Poisson-distributed total baryon and antibaryon numbers and binomial-distributed observed proton and antiproton multiplicities, then enforce global baryon conservation via a Kronecker delta constraint. They derive the factorial cumulant generating function using modified Bessel functions and obtain exact closed-form expressions for factorial cumulants up to order six, as well as asymptotic expansions for the special case of zero net baryon number (B=0) applicable at high energies.

## Key numbers

- For B=0, the leading asymptotic behavior of the ratio R^(2,0)(z_c) = C^(2,0)/(p^2) is approximately -1/2 z_c + 1/8 + 1/32 z_c^{-1} + ..., where z_c = ⟨N_b⟩_c = ⟨N̅_b⟩_c
- For B=0, R^(1,1)(z_c) ~ 1/2 z_c + 1/8 + 1/32 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(3,0)(z_c) ~ 3/4 z_c - 5/16 - 3/32 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(2,1)(z_c) ~ -1/4 z_c - 1/16 - 1/32 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(4,0)(z_c) ~ -15/8 z_c + 33/32 + 45/128 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(3,1)(z_c) ~ 3/8 z_c + 3/32 + 9/128 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(2,2)(z_c) ~ 1/8 z_c + 1/32 + 5/128 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(5,0)(z_c) ~ 105/16 z_c - 279/64 - 105/64 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(4,1)(z_c) ~ -15/16 z_c - 15/64 - 15/64 z_c^{-1} + ...
- For B=0, R^(3,2)(z_c) ~ -3/16 z_c - 3/64 - 3/32 z_c^{-1} + ...

- Numerical results are also presented for B=300, corresponding to central collisions at low energies

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of global baryon number conservation effects on fluctuations, which were limited to lower-order cumulants (Bzdak 2012, Braun-Munzinger 2016, Rogly 2018, Vovchenko 2020). The factorial cumulants contain more information than the net-proton cumulants measured by STAR (Adamczyk 2013, Adam 2020) and ALICE (Acharya 2019), and the results enable more sophisticated experimental tests of whether observed correlations are consistent with global baryon conservation alone. The ALICE Collaboration (2019) measured the second-order net-proton cumulant and found consistency with global baryon conservation, but the authors note that second-order factorial cumulants would provide a more stringent test.
