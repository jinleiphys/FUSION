# Heavy flavor dissociation in the framework of multi-body Dirac equations

> We study heavy flavor properties at finite temperature in the framework of a relativistic potential model. With an improved method to solve the three-body Dirac equation, we determine a universal set of model parameters for both mesons and baryons by fitting heavy flavor masses in vacuum. Taking heavy quark potential from lattice QCD simulations in hot medium, we systematically calculate heavy flavor binding energies and averaged sizes as functions of temperature. The meson and baryons are separately sequentially dissociated in the quark-gluon plasma, and the mesons can survive at higher temperature due to the stronger potential between quark-antiquark pairs than that between quark-quark pairs.

# Heavy flavor dissociation in the framework of multi-body Dirac equations

## Key claim

Heavy flavor mesons and baryons are sequentially dissociated in the quark-gluon plasma, with mesons surviving to higher temperatures than baryons because the quark-antiquark potential is stronger than the quark-quark potential; however, the exact dissociation temperatures depend sensitively on whether the potential is taken as the free energy (slow dissociation limit) or the internal energy (rapid dissociation limit), with the latter yielding substantially higher dissociation temperatures.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Dirac equation for mesons and the three-body Dirac equation for baryons, using an improved expansion of baryon wave functions in terms of two-body spherical harmonic oscillator states to ensure completeness and orthogonality. Model parameters (quark masses and coupling strengths) were fixed by fitting heavy flavor hadron masses in vacuum, with the relations between quark-antiquark and quark-quark couplings set to α_qq = 0.45 α_q\bar q and σ_qq = 0.49 σ_q\bar q, close to one-gluon exchange and lattice QCD results. At finite temperature, the heavy quark potential was taken from lattice QCD simulations in two limits, the free energy F and the internal energy U, and the dissociation temperature was defined as the temperature where the binding energy vanishes or the averaged radius becomes infinite.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 0.135 GeV, m_s = 0.263 GeV, m_c = 1.400 GeV, m_b = 4.773 GeV
- Quark-antiquark Coulomb coupling: α_q\bar q = 0.20 × 2.22 = 0.444; string tension: σ_q\bar q = 0.09 × 2.04 = 0.1836 GeV²
- For charmonia and bottomed mesons, relative difference between calculated and experimental masses D_R < 1%
- For doubly charmed baryons Ξ_cc⁺ and Ξ_cc⁺⁺, relative difference D_R < 0.4%
- For Λ_c⁺, relative difference reaches ~7%
- Critical temperature for QCD deconfinement: T_c ≃ 155 MeV
- Scaled dissociation temperatures T_d/T_c for J/ψ: 1.42 (V=F) and 3.09 (V=U)
- Scaled dissociation temperatures T_d/T_c for Ω_ccc: 1.15 (V=F) and 2.18 (V=U)
- Scaled dissociation temperatures T_d/T_c for Λ_c: 1.02 (V=F) and 1.37 (V=U)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic potential model approach from two-body systems (mesons) studied via the two-body Dirac equation by Crater and Alstine (2002, 2008) and applied at finite temperature by Guo (2012) and Shi (2013), to three-body systems (baryons) using the three-body Dirac equation developed by Whitney and Crater (2011). It improves upon prior non-relativistic treatments of heavy flavor dissociation such as those by Satz (2005) and Burnier (2015), which used the Schrodinger equation for quarkonium, by including relativistic effects essential for baryons where constituent relative velocities approach c. The results enable systematic predictions of dissociation temperatures for all heavy flavor hadrons in heavy ion collisions, providing a more complete theoretical basis for interpreting experimental signals of quark-gluon plasma formation.
