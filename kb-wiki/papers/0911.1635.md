# Intermediate statistics as a consequence of deformed algebra

> We present a formulation of the deformed oscillator algebra which leads to intermediate statistics as a continuous interpolation between the Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistics. It is deduced that a generalized permutation or exchange symmetry leads to the introduction of the basic number and it is then established that this in turn leads to the deformed algebra of oscillators. We obtain the mean occupation number describing the particles obeying intermediate statistics which thus establishes the interpolating statistics and describe boson like and fermion like particles obeying intermediate statistics. We also obtain an expression for the mean occupation number in terms of an infinite continued fraction, thus clarifying successive approximations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a deformed oscillator algebra, parameterized by a complex number \(\kappa\) with \(|\kappa|^2 = 1\), can produce intermediate statistics that continuously interpolate between Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistics, but the authors explicitly restrict their formulation to particles that "may indeed differ from the anyons in the literature" because anyons arise only in two space dimensions while the oscillator algebra is valid in any number of dimensions.

## Method

The authors start from a generalized permutation or exchange symmetry for \(N\)-particle wave functions, where exchanging coordinates multiplies the wave function by a parameter \(\kappa\) (with \(\kappa = e^{i\alpha}\), \(\alpha\) real). They compute probability densities for two- and three-particle states of identical particles and show that these probabilities can be expressed in terms of basic (bracket) numbers \([n] = (\kappa^n - \kappa^{-n})/(\kappa - \kappa^{-1})\). They then demonstrate that the existence of these basic numbers naturally implies a deformed oscillator algebra, and they derive the mean occupation number for particles obeying intermediate statistics, expressing it as an infinite continued fraction.

## Key numbers

* The statistics-determining parameter \(\kappa\) takes the values \(\kappa = \pm 1\) (corresponding to \(\alpha = 0, \pi\)) which define the Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac cases respectively; intermediate values of \(\alpha\) give continuous interpolation.
* The basic number is defined as \([x] = (\kappa^x - \kappa^{-x})/(\kappa - \kappa^{-1})\) and can be expressed trigonometrically as \([n] = \sin(n\alpha)/\sin\alpha\).
* For the two-particle state, the probability density reduces to \(P^{(2)} = \frac{1}{2}(2 + [2])\,\Pi_2\), where \([2] = \kappa + \kappa^{-1}\).
* For the three-particle state, the probability density is \(P^{(3)} = \frac{1}{6}(6 + 7[2] + 4[3] + [4])\,\Pi_3\), which can also be written as \(P^{(3)} = \frac{1}{6}(2 + [2])(2 + 2[2] + [3])\,\Pi_3\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of intermediate statistics based on detailed balance (Refs. [RA-PNS1] and [RA-PNS2]) by providing a deformed oscillator algebra foundation for the same distribution functions. It also connects to the extensive literature on \(q\)-deformed quantum oscillators and quantum groups (Biedenharn, 1989; Macfarlane, 1989) and to prior thermostatistics of \(q\)-bosons and \(q\)-fermions (PNS; Lavagno and Swamy, 2002; 2008). The formulation enables future work on the exact thermostatistics of intermediate-statistics particles, since the mean occupation number derived here is exact, whereas earlier work (RA-PNS1) used only a first approximation.
