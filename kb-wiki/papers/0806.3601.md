# K anti-K N molecule state with I=1/2 and J^P=1/2^+ studied with three-body calculation

> A K \bar{K} N system with I=1/2 and J^P=1/2^+ is investigated with non-relativistic three-body calculations by using effective \bar K N, K \bar K and KN interactions. The \bar K N interaction describes the Lambda(1405) as a \bar K N molecule, and the K\bar K interaction is adjusted to give f_0(980) and a_0(980) states as K \bar K molecules. The present investigation suggests that a bound K \bar K N state can be formed below the K \bar K N threshold (1930 MeV) with a 90 ~ 100 MeV width of three-hadron decays, which are dominated by K bar K N -> K pi Sigma and pi eta N. It is found that the K \bar K N state is a weakly bound hadron molecular state with a size larger than an alpha particle because of the repulsive KN interactions.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a bound \(K\bar{K}N\) state with \(I=1/2\) and \(J^P=1/2^+\) can be formed below the \(K\bar{K}N\) threshold (1930 MeV), but this conclusion is conditional on the \(K\bar{K}\) interaction with \(I=1\) having sufficient attraction; the state becomes unbound if that attraction is less than 70% of the values used in the calculation.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic three-body calculations using a variational approach with a Gaussian expansion method to solve the Schrödinger equation for the \(K\bar{K}N\) system. They employed effective local two-body potentials for \(\bar{K}N\), \(K\bar{K}\), and \(KN\) interactions, with the \(\bar{K}N\) interaction taken from either the Hyodo-Weise (HW-HNJH) or Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) potential, and the \(K\bar{K}\) interaction adjusted to reproduce the masses and widths of \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) as \(K\bar{K}\) molecular states. The imaginary parts of the potentials were treated perturbatively to estimate decay widths.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the \(K\bar{K}N\) state measured from the \(K+\bar{K}+N\) threshold: \(-19\) MeV for parameter set (A) (HW-HNJH) and \(-41\) MeV for set (B) (AY).
* Total width of the \(K\bar{K}N\) state: approximately 90 MeV (from \(\Gamma = -2\,\text{Im}E\) with \(\text{Im}E = -44\) MeV for set (A) and \(-49\) MeV for set (B)).
* The \(K\bar{K}N\) bound state appears about 10 MeV below the lowest two-body threshold (\(\Lambda(1405)+K\)) in both parameter sets.
* Root-mean-square radius of the \(K\bar{K}N\) state: 1.7 fm for set (A) and 1.4 fm for set (B) with the \(KN\) interaction included.
* Without the \(KN\) repulsive interaction, the binding energy increases to \(-39\) MeV (set A) and \(-57\) MeV (set B), and the width increases to \(\Gamma = 130-140\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-hadron molecular states, particularly the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) system investigated by the same authors (Kanada-En'yo, 2008), by replacing one anti-kaon with a kaon to explore the \(K\bar{K}N\) system. It builds on the description of \(\Lambda(1405)\) as a \(\bar{K}N\) molecule (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002; Hyodo and Weise, 2007) and the treatment of \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) as \(K\bar{K}\) molecules (Weinstein, 1982). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted \(K\bar{K}N\) state through its dominant decay channels \(K\pi\Sigma\) and \(\pi\eta N\), and provide a benchmark for three-body calculations of hadronic molecules with competing attractive and repulsive interactions.
