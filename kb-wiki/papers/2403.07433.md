# Baryonic Vortex Phase and Magnetic Field Generation in QCD with Isospin and Baryon Chemical Potentials

> We propose a novel baryonic vortex phase in low energy dense QCD with finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials. It is known that the homogeneous charged pion condensate emerges as a ground state at finite isospin chemical potential, and therein arises the Abrikosov vortex lattice with an applied magnetic field. We first demonstrate that a vortex with the same quantized magnetic flux as the conventional Abrikosov vortex, carries a baryon number captured by the third homotopy group of Skyrmions, once we take into account a modulation of the neutral pion inside the vortex core. Such a vortex-Skyrmion state is therefore dubbed the baryonic vortex. We further reveal that when the baryon chemical potential is above a critical value, the baryonic vortex has negative tension measured from the charged pion condensation. It implies that the phase, in which such vortices emerge spontaneously without an external magnetic field, would take over the ground state at high baryon density. Such a new phase contributes to the comprehension of QCD phase diagram and relates to the generation of magnetic fields inside neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that when the baryon chemical potential exceeds a critical value, a "baryonic vortex", a vortex carrying quantized magnetic flux and a conserved baryon number, becomes the ground state of low-energy dense QCD, replacing the homogeneous charged pion condensate. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the specific vortex-Skyrmion ansatz and the inclusion of the Skyrme term to stabilize the soliton size.

## Method

The authors use the SU(2) chiral perturbation theory extended with the Skyrme term and coupled to Maxwell electrodynamics, with both isospin chemical potential μ_I and baryon chemical potential μ_B. They construct a static, cylindrically symmetric vortex-Skyrmion ansatz where the neutral pion field φ(z) winds along the vortex axis and the charged pion field α(ρ) interpolates from zero at the origin to π/2 at infinity, with the gauge field A_φ(ρ) producing a longitudinal magnetic field. The equations of motion are solved numerically to find the vortex profile, and the string tension is computed as a function of the longitudinal wave vector k, with the stable configuration determined by minimizing the tension.

## Key numbers

- At μ_I = 0.5 f_π s and μ_B = 0, the vortex string tension at its minimum is 86.75 f_π², corresponding to an energy per baryon number of 831 GeV.
- The critical baryon chemical potential for the transition to the baryonic vortex phase at μ_I = 0.5 f_π s is μ_B^c ≈ 2.518 f_π s.
- The pion decay constant is taken as f_π = 54 MeV and the Skyrme parameter s = 5.45, with the effective pion mass m_π = 139 MeV corresponding to a dimensionless isospin chemical potential of μ̃_I > 0.47.
- The validity of the chiral perturbation theory momentum expansion requires k ≪ 4π f_π, which translates to k ≪ 2.31 in the dimensionless units used.

## Context

This work extends the study of Abrikosov vortex lattices in charged pion condensates at finite isospin chemical potential, previously investigated by Adhikari (2015, 2018, 2022) and Gronli (2022), by incorporating a baryon chemical potential and a neutral pion winding that endows the vortex with baryon number. The baryonic vortex phase provides a mechanism for spontaneous magnetic field generation in neutron stars, contrasting with the conventional picture where magnetic fields arise from proton superconductivity during neutrino cooling (1969 Nature, Graber 2016). The results enable future studies of vortex lattices and the full QCD phase diagram at high baryon density with isospin asymmetry.
