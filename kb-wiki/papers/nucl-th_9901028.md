# Soft-core baryon-baryon potentials for the complete baryon octet

> SU(3) symmetry relations on the recently constructed hyperon-nucleon potentials are used to develop potential models for all possible baryon-baryon interaction channels. The main focus is on the interaction channels with total strangeness S=-2, -3, and -4, for which no experimental data exist yet. The potential models for these channels are based on SU(3) extensions of potential models for the S=0 and S=-1 sectors, which are fitted to experimental data. Although the SU(3) symmetry is not taken to be exact, the S=0 and S=-1 sectors still provide the necessary constraints to fix all free parameters. The potentials for the S=-2, -3, and -4 sectors, therefore, do not contain any additional free parameters, which makes them the first models of this kind. Various properties of the potentials are illustrated by giving results for scattering lengths, bound states, and total cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors use SU(3) symmetry relations, slightly broken, to extend hyperon-nucleon potentials fitted to existing S=0 and S=-1 scattering data to all baryon-baryon channels with strangeness S=-2, -3, and -4, producing the first such models with no additional free parameters for these unmeasured sectors.

## Method

The authors construct one-boson-exchange potentials for all two-baryon channels involving the \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+\) baryon octet, using an SU(3)-symmetric interaction Lagrangian whose coupling constants are fixed by fits to NN and YN scattering data for the S=0 and S=-1 sectors. They then apply isospin rotations to obtain potentials on the physical particle basis, accounting for coupled-channel thresholds and effective meson-mass corrections, and compute scattering lengths, bound states, and total cross sections for all strangeness sectors from S=0 to S=-4.

## Key numbers

- Threshold laboratory momentum for \(\Lambda p \rightarrow \Sigma^+ n\): 633.4 MeV/\(c\)
- Threshold laboratory momentum for \(\Lambda\Lambda \rightarrow \Xi^0 n\): 326.0 MeV/\(c\)
- Effective pion mass reduction in \(\Lambda B \rightarrow \Sigma B\) (B = N, \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\)): from 138.041 MeV/\(c^2\) to 132.58 MeV/\(c^2\)
- Effective pion mass reduction in \(\Sigma\Lambda \rightarrow \Lambda\Sigma\): from 138.041 MeV/\(c^2\) to 114.62 MeV/\(c^2\)
- \(\Lambda\Lambda(^1S_0)\) scattering lengths: small (mildly attractive), with an estimate from hypernuclear data giving \(a_{\Lambda\Lambda}(^1S_0) \approx -2.0\) fm
- Deuteron binding energies from NSC97 models: \(-2.19\) MeV (NSC97a) to \(-2.07\) MeV (NSC97f), compared to experimental \(-2.224575(9)\) MeV
- \(\Sigma^+\Sigma^+\) binding energies: \(-1.53\) MeV (NSC97a) to \(-3.07\) MeV (NSC97f)
- \(\Xi^0\Sigma^+\) binding energies in \(^1S_0\): \(-3.02\) MeV (NSC97a) to \(-16.5\) MeV (NSC97f)
- \(\Xi^0\Sigma^+\) binding energies in \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\): \(-5.64\) MeV (NSC97a) to \(-36.1\) MeV (NSC97f)
- \(\Xi^0\Xi^0\) binding energies: \(-0.10\) MeV (NSC97a) to \(-15.8\) MeV (NSC97f)

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core hyperon-nucleon potentials (NSC97a–f) of Rijken and Stoks (1999) to all strangeness sectors, providing the first parameter-free predictions for S=-2, -3, and -4 baryon-baryon interactions. The potentials are relevant for understanding strangeness-rich systems in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, astrophysical problems (e.g., neutron star interiors), and the existence of exotic multiquark states such as the H dibaryon predicted by Jaffe (1977). The results enable future studies of double-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei and \(\Xi\) hypernuclei, though the authors note that two-meson exchange contributions (Rijken, 1998) may be needed to reproduce the stronger \(\Lambda\Lambda\) attraction suggested by hypernuclear data.
