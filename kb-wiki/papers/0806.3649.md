# Final remarks to our study of $η$-photoproduction on protons in the resonance region

> The properties of previously discovered nucleon resonances are amended basing on the recent and more detailed experimental data about photoproduction of $η$-mesons on protons.

## Key claim

The properties of previously discovered nucleon resonances are amended based on recent GRAAL experimental data on η-photoproduction on protons, but the author cautions that claiming total agreement between calculation and data would be premature, even in the limited photon energy region below 1.5 GeV.

## Method

The author analyzed experimental data for the reaction γ + p → η + p using a dynamical model, fitting resonance parameters to describe differential cross sections, total cross sections, and beam asymmetry measurements from GRAAL and other experiments. The analysis required inclusion of a new S11 resonance (S11(1830)) in addition to the known S11(1535) and S11(1650), and extracted photoexcitation amplitudes and coupling parameters for multiple nucleon resonances.

## Key numbers

- S11(1535): resonance mass Wr = 1535 MeV, total width Γr = 156 MeV, Γpη/Γr = 0.5, photoexcitation amplitude A1/2 = 0.110 GeV^{-1/2}
- S11(1650): resonance mass Wr = 1642 MeV, total width Γr = 140 MeV, Γpη/Γr = 0.1, photoexcitation amplitude A1/2 = 0.102 GeV^{-1/2}
- S11(1830): resonance mass Wr = 1828 MeV, total width Γr = 150 MeV, Γpη/Γr = 0.1, photoexcitation amplitude A1/2 = 0.032 GeV^{-1/2}
- P13(1720): resonance mass Wr = 1730 MeV, total width Γr = 185 MeV, ξ^{1/2} = 0.245 × 10^{-1} GeV^{-1}, ξ^{3/2} = 0.630 × 10^{-1} GeV^{-1}
- GRAAL measurements covered the energy region 1100–1500 MeV for beam asymmetry and differential cross sections
- Anomalous behavior in beam asymmetry was observed in the region 1.05–1.2 GeV at θ = 90° and 142.5°

## Context

This work extends previous dynamical model analyses of η-photoproduction (references 6–10 in the paper) by incorporating newer GRAAL experimental data. The author notes that while some earlier works (references 12–13) found the P13(1720) resonance insignificant, more recent studies (references 14–16) and the author's own previous work (reference 7) report its essential contribution. The analysis enables further investigation of a possible narrow resonance (Γ < 25 MeV) in the second resonance region near W = 1680 MeV, which has been suggested as a member of the SU(3) flavor antidecuplet and would be more visible in η-photoproduction on neutrons.
