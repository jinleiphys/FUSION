# Neutron skins of atomic nuclei: per aspera ad astra

> The complex nature of the nuclear forces generates a broad range and diversity of observational phenomena. Heavy nuclei, though orders of magnitude less massive than neutron stars, are governed by the same underlying physics, which is enshrined in the nuclear equation of state. Heavy nuclei are expected to develop a neutron-rich skin where many neutrons collect near the surface. Such a skin thickness is strongly sensitive to the poorly-known density dependence of the symmetry energy near saturation density. An accurate and model-independent determination of the neutron-skin thickness of heavy nuclei would provide a significant first constraint on the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. The determination of the neutron-skin thickness of heavy nuclei has far reaching consequences in many areas of physics as diverse as heavy-ion collisions, polarized electron and proton scattering off nuclei, precision tests of the standard model using atomic parity violation, and nuclear astrophysics. While a systematic and concerted experimental effort has been made to measure the neutron-skin thickness of heavy nuclei, a precise and model-independent determination remains elusive. How to move forward at a time when many new facilities are being commissioned and how to strengthen the synergy with other areas of physics are primary goals of this review.

## Key claim
The neutron-skin thickness of heavy nuclei is strongly sensitive to the poorly-known density dependence of the symmetry energy near saturation density, but despite systematic experimental efforts, a precise and model-independent determination remains elusive; parity-violating electron scattering offers a clean determination of the weak form factor dominated by the neutron distribution, yet measuring asymmetries of order parts per million is both challenging and time-consuming.

## Method
The paper reviews multiple experimental techniques for determining neutron-skin thickness, including hadronic scattering, coherent pion photoproduction, measurements of electric dipole polarizabilities and pygmy dipole resonances, and parity-violating electron scattering. For parity-violating electron scattering, longitudinally polarized electrons are elastically scattered off unpolarized nuclei, and the parity-violating asymmetry is determined from the difference in cross sections between right- and left-handed electrons, which in Born approximation is directly proportional to the ratio of the weak to charge form factors of the nucleus. The review synthesizes results from a scientific program at the Mainz Institute for Theoretical Physics in 2016 that gathered theorists and experimentalists to quantify strengths and limitations of each technique through analysis of systematic errors.

## Key numbers
- The mean-square charge radius of $^{208}$Pb is known to better than 0.02%, with $R_{\rm ch}=5.5012(13)\,{\rm fm}$.
- The PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory reported a parity-violating asymmetry for $^{208}$Pb of $A_{\rm PV}^{\rm Pb} = 656 \pm 60(\mathrm{stat}) \pm 14(\mathrm{syst}) \,{\rm ppb}$ at an average momentum transfer of $\overline{Q}^{\,2}=(0.008\,80 \pm 0.000\,11) {\rm GeV}^{2}$.
- The extracted weak form factor of $^{208}$Pb from PREX is $F_{W}(\overline{Q}^{\,2})= 0.204 \pm 0.028$.
- PREX furnished the first credible estimate of the neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb to be $R_{\rm skin}^{208}={0.33}^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\,{\rm fm}$.
- Nuclear saturation density is approximately $\rhoz\approx 0.15\,{\rm fm}^{-3}$, with $R(A)=r_{0}A^{1/3}\approx (1.17\,{\rm fm})A^{1/3}$.
- The binding energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation is $\epsz\approx -16$ MeV, and the symmetry energy at saturation is $J\approx 23$ MeV.
- The PREX-II experiment aims for a determination of the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb with $\pm0.06\,{\rm fm}$ sensitivity, and CREX aims for $\pm0.02\,{\rm fm}$ sensitivity to the neutron radius of $^{48}$Ca.

## Context
This review extends the long-standing effort to determine neutron distributions that began with Hofstadter's pioneering electron scattering work in the 1950s, and it directly builds on the PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory (Abrahamyan 2012, Horowitz 2012) which provided the first model-independent determination of the weak form factor of $^{208}$Pb. The work connects to neutron star physics through the slope of the symmetry energy $L$, which is strongly correlated to both the neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb (RocaMaza 2011, Brown 2000, Furnstahl 2001, Centelles 2008) and neutron star radii (Horowitz 2000, Horowitz 2001, Carriere 2002, Fattoyev 2012, Chen 2014), and the review was motivated by the 2016 Mainz Institute for Theoretical Physics program on Neutron Skins of Nuclei. The upcoming PREX-II and CREX experiments at Jefferson Laboratory, combined with the first gravitational wave detection from binary neutron star merger GW170817 by LIGO-Virgo (2017), provide a path to constrain the equation of state of neutron-rich matter.
