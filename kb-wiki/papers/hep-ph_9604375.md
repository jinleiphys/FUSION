# Recent developments in rho-omega mixing

> The topic of rho-omega mixing has received renewed interest in recent years and has been studied using a variety of modern techniques. A brief history of the subject is presented before summarising recent developments in the field. The present status of our understanding is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the traditional extraction of the rho-omega mixing parameter from the pion form-factor is ambiguous because the experimental data cannot distinguish between mixing of the isospin-pure states and the intrinsic decay of the isospin-pure omega into two pions, leading to a wide range of possible values for the mixing parameter.

## Method

The paper reviews the history of rho-omega mixing within the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model, then summarizes recent model calculations using quark loops, QCD sum rules (QCDSR), and Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT). It describes a new general analysis that includes both the momentum dependence of the mixing parameter and the intrinsic decay of the isospin-pure omega into two pions, constructing a precise theoretical expression for the pion form-factor that is compared to existing experimental fits.

## Key numbers

* The mixing parameter extracted from the pion form-factor assuming no intrinsic decay contribution is \(\Pi_{\rho \omega} = -3960\) MeV\(^2\), compared to a previous value of \(-4520 \pm 600\) MeV\(^2\) from Coon and Barrett.
* When both mixing and intrinsic decay are allowed, the mixing parameter \(\Pi_{\rho\omega}\) can take values in the set \((-840, -6240)\) MeV\(^2\), reflecting the large experimental uncertainty.
* The QCD sum rule result from Maltman under-estimates the isospin-violation seen experimentally in the reaction \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing study of rho-omega mixing, which began with the observation of omega interference in the reaction \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\) by the Orsay group. It directly addresses the debate following Goldman et al. (1988) who predicted significant momentum dependence of the mixing, challenging the fixed-value approach used in nuclear charge symmetry violation (CSV) models by Coon et al. The paper enables a re-evaluation of the mixing parameter used in nuclear models and highlights the need for a completely consistent field theory description of isospin violation in both timelike (mixing) and spacelike (nuclear CSV) regimes.
