# Status of Alpha-Particle Condensate Structure of the Hoyle State

> The present understanding of the structure of the Hoyle state in 12C is reviewed. Most of the theoretical approaches to the Hoyle state are shortly summarized. The corresponding results are analyzed with respect to whether they give evidence to the alpha particle condensation structure of the Hoyle state (and other Hoyle-like states in heavier self-conjugate nuclei) or not.

## Key claim

The Hoyle state (the 0₂⁺ state at 7.65 MeV in ¹²C) can be considered to good approximation as a state where three α-particles are condensed into the center-of-mass 0S orbital, but the term "condensate" for a handful of α-particles is stretched to its limits and such states in finite nuclei are only precursors to what might happen in infinite nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors review theoretical approaches to the Hoyle state, focusing on the THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) wave function which is an antisymmetrized product of three identical α-particle wave functions with a common Gaussian width parameter B for the center-of-mass motion. They compare this approach with the Resonating Group Method (RGM) of Kamimura et al. (1977), the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) of Uegaki et al. (1977), the Orthogonal Condition Model (OCM) of Horiuchi (1974), Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) by Kanada-En'yo et al. (2007), Fermion Molecular Dynamics (FMD) by Chernykh et al. (2007), and pure bosonic approaches by Lazauskas et al. and Ishikawa. The bosonic occupation numbers of α-particles are evaluated from the single α-particle density matrix.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state lies at 7.65 MeV excitation energy in ¹²C.
- The optimal B parameter for the Hoyle state is B_H = 6.8 fm, compared to B_g = 2.5 fm for the ground state.
- The expectation value of the antisymmetrizer N(B) is approximately 0.62 for the Hoyle state and 0.007 for the ground state.
- The rms radius of the Hoyle state is 3.83 fm, compared to 2.4 fm for the ground state, giving a volume approximately 3-4 times larger.
- The three α-particles in the Hoyle state occupy the same 0S orbit to about 70 percent, with all other orbits down by at least a factor of ten.
- The THSR wave function has 98 percent squared overlap with the Kamimura wave function for the Hoyle state.
- In pure bosonic approaches, the 0S occupancy is approximately 80 percent.
- The simultaneous three α decay probability relative to sequential ⁸Be + α decay gives a branching ratio of 10⁻⁴.

## Context

This review extends the work of Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Röpke (2001) which first interpreted the Hoyle state as an α-particle condensate, building on earlier studies of quartet condensation in infinite nuclear matter by Röpke. It contrasts with the earlier chain-state hypothesis of Morinaga (1956) and confirms the interpretation of Horiuchi (1974) that the Hoyle state is a state of three weakly coupled α-particles in relative S-waves. The review enables future studies of Hoyle-analog states in heavier self-conjugate nuclei such as ¹⁶O, which are now being investigated both theoretically and experimentally by groups such as Itoh and collaborators.
