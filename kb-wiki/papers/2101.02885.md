# A unified description of the structure and electromagnetic breakup of $^{\bf 11}$Be

> We study both the static properties of $^{11}$Be and its reaction dynamics during electromagnetic breakup under a unified framework. A many-body approach - the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) is used to describe the structure of the neutron-halo nucleus, $^{11}$Be. The same AMD wave function is then adapted as an input to the fully quantum theory of Coulomb breakup under the aegis of the finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory. The calculated observables are also compared with those obtained with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential model wave function. The experimental core-valence neutron relative energy spectrum and dipole response along with other observables are well described by our calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unified description of both the static properties and electromagnetic breakup of the neutron-halo nucleus 11Be is achievable by combining antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) for structure with the finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) for reactions, but only after the AMD wave function is supplemented by the AMD plus resonating group method (AMD+RGM) to correct the asymptotics of the valence neutron wave function; without this correction, the AMD framework alone fails to bind 11Be and does not reproduce its halo nature.

## Method

The authors used the AMD framework with the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to generate intrinsic wave functions for 10Be and 11Be, then applied angular momentum projection and the generator coordinate method (GCM) to obtain excitation spectra. To properly describe the neutron halo asymptotics, they introduced an AMD+RGM approach that superposes 338 basis wave functions placing 10Be and a valence neutron on a grid. The resulting valence neutron overlap amplitude (with tail correction to match the experimental one-neutron separation energy) was used as input to the fully quantum mechanical FRDWBA theory of Coulomb breakup on a 208Pb target, and results were compared with those from a phenomenological Woods-Saxon (WS) potential model wave function.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy of 11Be: AMD+RGM calculation gives 0.48 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.50 MeV; the pure AMD calculation gives -0.22 MeV (unbound).
* Root mean square matter radius of 11Be: AMD+RGM gives 2.71 fm, AMD gives 2.59 fm, experimental value is 2.73 ± 0.05 fm.
* Root mean square charge radius of 11Be: AMD+RGM gives 2.55 fm, AMD gives 2.54 fm, experimental value is 2.463 ± 0.015 fm.
* Spectroscopic factor for the 10Be(0+1) ⊗ s1/2 channel: AMD+RGM gives 0.82, AMD gives 0.46, experimental values range from 0.72(4) to 1.0(2).
* Spectroscopic factor for the 10Be(2+1) ⊗ d3/2 channel: AMD+RGM gives 0.18, AMD gives 0.38, experimental value is 0.18.
* Root mean square matter radius of 11Be from the cluster model: WS wave function gives 2.79 fm, AMD wave function gives 2.78 fm, experimental value is 2.73 ± 0.05 fm.
* Root mean square charge radius of 11Be from the cluster model: WS wave function gives 2.35 fm, AMD wave function gives 2.35 fm, experimental value is 2.463 ± 0.015 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior AMD studies of Be isotopes by Kanada-En'yo (1999, 2003, 2012) and Kimura (2016) by adding the RGM technique to properly describe the neutron halo asymptotics, which was a known limitation of the standard AMD framework. The results are consistent with ab-initio no-core shell model calculations by Calci (2016) and Bonaccorso (2019) for the overlap amplitudes, and with experimental data from Nakamura (1994, 1997) on the dipole response and relative energy spectrum. The unified structure-reaction approach enables future studies of other halo nuclei such as 31Ne, where the AMD+RGM method has already been applied by Minomo (2011, 2012) and Sumi (2012).
