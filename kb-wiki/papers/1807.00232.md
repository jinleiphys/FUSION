# Transversity parton distribution functions from lattice QCD

> We present the first direct calculation of the transversity parton distribution function within the nucleon from lattice QCD. The calculation is performed using simulations with the light quark mass fixed to its physical value and at one value of the lattice spacing. Novel elements of the calculations are non-perturbative renormalization and extraction of a formula for the matching to light-cone PDFs. Final results are presented in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at a scale of $\sqrt{2}$ GeV.

# Transversity parton distribution functions from lattice QCD

## Key claim

This paper presents the first direct *ab initio* calculation of the isovector transversity parton distribution function $h_1^{u-d}(x)$ from lattice QCD, but the results are obtained at a single lattice spacing ($a \simeq 0.093$ fm) and a single pion mass ($m_\pi \simeq 130$ MeV), with nucleon momenta up to only 1.38 GeV, meaning that continuum and infinite-momentum extrapolations have not yet been performed.

## Method

The calculation uses one ensemble of $N_f=2$ twisted mass clover-improved fermions at physical pion mass on a $48^3 \times 96$ lattice. Bare matrix elements of a non-local operator with a Wilson line are computed for three nucleon momenta ($P = 0.83$, 1.11, 1.38 GeV) using momentum smearing and sequential inversions through the sink. The matrix elements are renormalized non-perturbatively using a RI'-type prescription, converted to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at $\sqrt{2}$ GeV, and then matched to light-cone PDFs using a one-loop matching kernel derived in this work, with target mass corrections applied as a final step.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: $a \simeq 0.093$ fm; spatial extent $L \simeq 4.5$ fm; pion mass $m_\pi \simeq 130$ MeV
- Three nucleon momenta used: $P = 6\pi/L \simeq 0.83$ GeV, $8\pi/L \simeq 1.11$ GeV, $10\pi/L \simeq 1.38$ GeV
- Source-sink separation: $T_{\rm sink} = 12a \simeq 1.12$ fm
- Statistics: 100 configurations (9600 measurements) for $P=0.83$ GeV; 425 configurations (38250 measurements) for $P=1.11$ GeV; 811 configurations (72990 measurements) for $P=1.38$ GeV
- Isovector tensor charge extracted from the $P=10\pi/L$ data: $g_T = 1.10(34)$ from integration of the PDF over $x \in [-1,1]$
- Renormalized matrix element at zero Wilson line length gives $g_T = 1.09(11)$, compatible with the integration result
- Final PDFs presented in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at scale $\mu = \sqrt{2}$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF approach of Ji (2013) to the transversity case, following the same methodology previously applied to unpolarized and helicity PDFs by Alexandrou et al. (2018). The results are compared to phenomenological fits of SIDIS data from Lin et al. (2017), which show much larger uncertainties; the lattice data also agree with the dedicated tensor charge calculation of Alexandrou et al. (2017). The direct lattice QCD computation of transversity PDFs provides independent first-principles input that can constrain global analyses of SIDIS data and dihadron fragmentation functions, and has implications for beyond-Standard-Model physics through the tensor charge. Future work requires higher nucleon momenta, multiple lattice spacings for continuum extrapolation, and techniques to control excited-state contamination at larger boosts.
