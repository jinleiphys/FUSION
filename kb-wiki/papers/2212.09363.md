# Studying the 3+1D structure of the Glasma using the weak field approximation

> We extend the weak field approximation for the Glasma beyond the boost-invariant approximation, which allows us to compute rapidity-dependent observables in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions. We show that in the limit of small fields, the weak field approximation agrees quantitatively with non-perturbative lattice simulations. Furthermore, we demonstrate that the rapidity profile of the transverse pressure is determined by longitudinal color correlations within the colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

The weak field approximation for the Glasma can be extended beyond the boost-invariant approximation to compute rapidity-dependent observables, and in the limit of small fields (g²μ/m ≲ 1) it agrees quantitatively with non-perturbative lattice simulations, but for larger non-linearity the weak-field pressure is generally overestimated while the rapidity profile shape is still correctly reproduced.

## Method

The authors linearize the Yang-Mills equations around non-interacting background fields and color currents of the two colliding nuclei, expanding to leading order in the color currents (the dilute limit) to obtain a 3+1D wave equation for the perturbative gauge field. They solve this wave equation using Green's function methods, obtaining explicit analytical expressions for the perturbative field strength tensor, and then compute the energy-momentum tensor. Expectation values are computed by inserting the one- and two-point functions of a 3+1D extension of the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, which introduces longitudinal color correlations characterized by a correlation length ξ and a longitudinal width R.

- The weak field approximation agrees with non-perturbative lattice simulations for g²μ/m ≲ 1 (left and center panels of Fig. 3), where g²μ/m is the non-linearity parameter.
- For larger values of g²μ/m (right panel of Fig. 3), the weak field result overestimates the overall transverse pressure but still accurately reproduces the rapidity profile.
- The longitudinal correlation length ξ (with ξ ≤ 2R) determines the width of the rapidity profile of transverse pressure, while the longitudinal system size R has only a weak effect on the profile width.
- In the coherent limit ξ = 2R, the transverse pressure is computed at late times τ ≫ R for various values of R and g²μ/m.

## Context

This work extends the weak field approximation (Ipp 2021) from 2+1D boost-invariant Glasma calculations to full 3+1D dynamics, enabling access to rapidity-dependent observables. It provides a semi-analytical alternative to fully numerical 3+1D lattice simulations (Ipp 2020, Schlichting 2020) that require large computational resources, and allows probing parameter regimes (intermediate correlation length ξ ≲ R) that are difficult to reach with lattice methods. The results enable future studies of more realistic nuclear geometries including nucleonic and sub-nucleonic structure using hot spots (Demirci 2021, Demirci 2022, Mantysaari 2022).
