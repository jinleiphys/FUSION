# News on Strangeness at Ultrarelativistic Energies - Review of Microscopic Models

> We review recent developments in the field of microscopic transport model calculations for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. In particular, we focus on the strangeness production, for example, the phi-meson and its role as a messenger of the early phase of the system evolution. Moreover, we discuss the important effects of the (soft) field properties on the multiparticle system. We outline some current problems of the models as well as possible solutions to them.

## Key claim

Microscopic transport models can describe strangeness production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions only if the effective string tension is dramatically enhanced from its vacuum value (approximately 1 GeV/fm to 3 GeV/fm) or if quark masses are reduced to current quark values, because the Schwinger mechanism for pair production is exponentially sensitive to these parameters.

## Method

The paper reviews several microscopic transport models applied to SPS and RHIC energies: the quark molecular dynamics (qMD) model, which uses a Cornell potential with color matrices to simulate colored quark dynamics and hadronization; the UrQMD hadron-string cascade, which treats particle production via string excitations and fragmentations with a Schwinger tunneling mechanism; the parton cascade model (PCM/VNI/BMS); the quark gluon string model (QGSM); and the AMPT multiphase transport model. Each model is evaluated against experimental data on strangeness observables, particularly phi-meson yields and antibaryon-to-baryon ratios.

## Key numbers

* Net strangeness in the quark phase reaches values up to approximately 10 net strange quarks in central Pb+Pb collisions at 30 AGeV, depending on the string tension (qMD model).
* The ratio of strange to light quark pair production probability gamma_s = P(s anti-s)/P(q anti-q) increases from 0.37 at kappa = 1 GeV/fm to 0.72 at kappa = 3 GeV/fm.
* The diquark production probability gamma_qq = P(qq anti-q anti-q)/P(q anti-q) increases from 0.093 at kappa = 1 GeV/fm to 0.45 at kappa = 3 GeV/fm.
* Quarks from the same original hadron recluster with a decay length of 2.2 fm, while quarks from different hadrons hadronize with a decay length of 4.8 fm (qMD for S+Au at 200 AGeV).
* The Regge slope for the Pomeron is alpha'_P approximately 0.4 GeV^{-2}, translating to an effective string tension larger than the vacuum value of approximately 1 GeV/fm.
* The intrinsic transverse momentum parameter sigma is varied from 0.55 GeV/c to 1.5 GeV/c in UrQMD studies of phi-meson production at RHIC.
* The (Lambda - anti-Lambda)/(p - anti-p) ratio is approximately 0.8 for the strong color field scenario (kappa = 3 GeV/fm), matching experimental values at RHIC.
* The QGSM underestimates the absolute yield of phi mesons at RHIC by a factor of 2.
* Approximately 88% of final anti-protons in central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV originate from 3-meson fusion reactions in the HSD transport approach.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on microscopic transport models for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, particularly the UrQMD model (Bass et al., 1998) and the HSD model (Cassing et al., 2001). It addresses the "phi puzzle" where different decay channels (kaonic vs. muonic) show different yields, studied by the AMPT model (Pal et al., 2002) and QGSM (Bravina et al., 2002). The strong color field scenario builds on earlier predictions by Biro et al. (1984) and Sorge et al. (1992). The review enables future work on multi-particle collisions, charm production in microscopic models, and the formulation of initial conditions using parton-based Gribov-Regge theory as in the NEXUS3 model (Drescher et al., 2000).
