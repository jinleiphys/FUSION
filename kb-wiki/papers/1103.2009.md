# Forward-backward elliptic anisotropy correlation in parton cascade

> A potential experimental probe, forward-backward elliptic anisotropy correlation ($C_{FB} $), has been proposed by Liao and Koch to distinguish the jet and true elliptic flow contribution to the measured elliptic flow ($v_2$) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Jet and flow fluctuation contribution to elliptic flow is investigated within the framework of a multi-phase transport model using the $C_{FB} $ probe. We found that the $C_{FB} $ correlation is remarkably different and is about two times of that proposed by Liao and Koch. It originates from the correlation between fluctuation of forward and backward elliptic flow at low transverse momentum, which is mainly due to the initial correlation between fluctuation of forward and backward eccentricity. This results in an amendment of the $C_{FB}$ by a term related to the correlation between fluctuation of forward and backward elliptic flow. Our results suggest that a suitable rapidity gap for $C_{FB} $ correlation studies should be around $\pm$ 3.5.

## Key claim

The forward-backward elliptic anisotropy correlation \(C_{FB}\) measured in AMPT simulations is about two times larger than the value predicted by Liao and Koch at low transverse momentum for mid-rapidity gaps, because the correlation between fluctuations of forward and backward elliptic flow is nonzero (close to 1/4) rather than zero as assumed in the original proposal.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with a partonic interaction cross section of 10 mb. They calculated the forward-backward correlation \(C_{FB}\) of the elliptic flow \(v_2\) between forward and backward pseudorapidity bins for several centrality classes (0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%) and rapidity gaps, and compared the results to the correlation of the initial eccentricity \(e_2\) in the same bins.

## Key numbers

- At low \(p_T\) (\(p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\)) for the 0-20% centrality bin with rapidity gap \(0.5 < |\eta| < 1.0\), \(C_{FB}\) is close to 2, which is about two times the value of unity predicted by Liao and Koch.
- For the same centrality and low \(p_T\), the correlation of forward-backward elliptic flow fluctuations \(\langle \eta^F \eta^B \rangle\) is close to 1/4.
- At large rapidity gap \(3.0 < |\eta| < 4.0\) for 0-20% centrality, \(C_{FB}\) falls to approximately 1.2 at low \(p_T\).
- At high \(p_T\) (\(p_T > 4\) GeV/\(c\)), \(C_{FB}\) is close to zero for all centrality bins and rapidity gaps studied.
- The relative nonstatistical fluctuations of \(v_2\) from experimental data are approximately 40%.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal of Liao and Koch (2009) by incorporating event-by-event fluctuations of elliptic flow, which were neglected in the original two-component parameterization. The results show that the original \(C_{FB}\) probe cannot directly measure jet contribution to \(v_2\) without knowledge of the forward-backward fluctuation correlation, especially for mid-rapidity gaps. The study suggests that a rapidity gap around \(\pm 3.5\) is suitable for clean extraction of jet contributions, and that the correlation of initial eccentricity fluctuations (as studied by Alver and collaborators, 2010) is efficiently transferred to final elliptic flow fluctuations at small rapidity gaps but not at large ones.
