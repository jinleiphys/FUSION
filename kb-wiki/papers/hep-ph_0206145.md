# Non-equilibrium Hadrochemistry in QGP Hadronization

> This survey offers an introductory tutorial for students of any age of the currently thriving field of hadrochemistry. We discuss the different chemical potentials, how the hadronic phase space is described and how one evaluates the abundance of hadrons at time of hadronization. We show that a rather accurate description of experimental data arises and we present results of fits to hadron yields at SPS and RHIC. We show that introduction of chemical non-equilibrium originating in a sudden hadronization of a QGP is favored strongly at SPS and is presently also emerging at RHIC. The low chemical freeze-out temperatures are consistent with the picture of single freeze-out scenario (chemical and thermal freeze-out coincide).

## Key claim

The paper argues that a chemically non-equilibrium description of hadronization, where the quark-gluon plasma undergoes sudden disintegration, is strongly favored at SPS energies and is also emerging at RHIC, with low chemical freeze-out temperatures consistent with a single freeze-out scenario where chemical and thermal freeze-out coincide.

## Method

The authors develop a statistical hadrochemistry framework using quark flavor fugacities (λ_u, λ_d, λ_s) and phase-space occupancy factors (γ_i) to describe particle yields in heavy-ion collisions. They distinguish between absolute chemical equilibrium (controlled by γ_i) and relative chemical equilibrium (controlled by chemical potentials for conserved quantum numbers), and apply this formalism to fit hadron yields measured at SPS and RHIC energies, analyzing transverse mass spectra and particle ratios.

## Key numbers

- QGP scattering time: τ_i^QGP = 0.2–2 fm, with particle densities ρ_i = 2–10 fm^{-3} and cross sections σ_i = 2–5 mb
- Fireball expansion time: τ^exp ≃ R_A/c ≃ 5–8 fm/c for Pb–Pb or Au–Au collisions
- Hagedorn temperature: T_H = 160 MeV
- Inverse slope T_⊥ = 232 ± 5 MeV for Λ, \barΛ, and K_S in S–W collisions at 200 A GeV (WA85)
- Inverse slopes for Pb–Pb at 158 A GeV: Λ = 289 ± 2 MeV, \barΛ = 287 ± 4 MeV, Ξ^- = 286 ± 9 MeV, \barΞ^+ = 284 ± 17 MeV, Ω^-+\barΩ^+ = 251 ± 19 MeV, φ = 305 ± 15 MeV
- Initial thermalization time: τ_0 ≡ τ_th ≃ 0.25–1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the statistical model of particle production originally proposed by Fermi (1950) and developed by Hagedorn (1965, 1968, 1973) into a multi-flavor hadrochemistry framework that accounts for chemical non-equilibrium during QGP hadronization. The authors contrast their approach with equilibrium-only models (such as those by Braun-Munzinger et al., 2001) that assume instantaneous absolute chemical equilibrium, showing that the choice of quark fugacity definitions significantly affects particle ratio predictions. The paper enables systematic study of chemical freeze-out conditions and the dynamics of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions from SPS to RHIC energies.
