# Energy loss of $B$ and $D$ mesons in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> We present the calculations of collisional and radiative energy loss of $B$ and $D$ mesons in the medium produced in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of $B$ and $D$ mesons including shadowing and energy loss are calculated and compared with the measured data. While the $D$ meson $R_{AA}$ can be described in terms of the radiative energy loss alone, both the collisional as well as radiative energy loss are required to explain the $B$ meson measurements.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of $D$ mesons in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV can be described by radiative energy loss alone, while both collisional and radiative energy loss are required to explain the $B$ meson measurements. This conclusion is conditional on the specific formalisms used (DGLV and a "present" generalized dead cone approach for radiative loss; Peigne-Peshier and Bjorken for collisional loss) and on the inclusion of shadowing effects.

## Method
The authors calculate the production cross sections of charm and bottom quark pairs to NLO in pQCD using CT10 parton densities, with charm mass 1.29 GeV and bottom mass 4.7 GeV. They compute collisional energy loss using both the Bjorken formalism and the Peigne-Peshier (PP) formalism, and radiative energy loss using both the DGLV (Djordjevic, Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev) reaction operator formalism and a generalized dead cone approach. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ is then obtained by combining these energy loss calculations with EPS09 NLO shadowing corrections and a Peterson fragmentation function, using an isentropic cylindrical expansion model for the medium evolution.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass: 1.29 GeV; bottom quark mass: 4.7 GeV.
- Peterson fragmentation parameter for charm: $\epsilon_c = 0.016$; for bottom: $\epsilon_b = 0.0012$.
- Strong coupling constant used: $\alpha_s = 0.3$.
- Number of active flavors: $N_f = 3$.
- Average path length for 0-20% centrality: 5.62 fm; for 0-100% centrality: 4.3 fm.
- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV for PbPb collisions.

## Context
This work extends calculations of heavy quark energy loss in quark-gluon plasma to $B$ and $D$ mesons at LHC energies, comparing with ALICE data for $D^0$ mesons and CMS data for $J/\psi$ from $B$ decays. It builds on prior formalisms for collisional energy loss by Braaten and Thoma (1991), Bjorken (1982), and Peigne and Peshier (2008), and for radiative energy loss by the DGLV group (Djordjevic, Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev) and the generalized dead cone approach of Abir (2012) and Saraswat (2015). The results enable further refinement of energy loss models by showing that different combinations of collisional and radiative mechanisms are needed for charm versus bottom quarks to match experimental data.
