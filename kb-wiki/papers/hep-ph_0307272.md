# Propagator of the interacting Rarita-Schwinger field

> We obtain in analytical form the dressed propagator of the Rarita-Schwinger field taking into account all spin components and discuss shortly its properties.

# Propagator of the interacting Rarita-Schwinger field

## Key claim
The authors derive an analytical closed-form expression for the dressed (full) propagator of the massive Rarita-Schwinger field that includes all spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 components, under the assumption that the self-energy is a known input (the "rainbow approximation"). The result is valid for any self-energy that respects Lorentz covariance, but no specific interaction or renormalization scheme is imposed.

## Method
The Dyson-Schwinger equation for the inverse propagator is solved by decomposing all spin-tensors (free propagator, self-energy, and full propagator) in a specially constructed basis of ten operators built from the projection operators of the Rarita-Schwinger field and the Dirac energy projectors Λ⁺ and Λ⁻. This Λ-basis has simple multiplicative properties (a multiplication table is given), which reduce the tensor inversion to a set of algebraic equations for scalar coefficients. The solution yields explicit expressions for all ten scalar coefficients of the dressed propagator in terms of the corresponding coefficients of the inverse free propagator and the self-energy.

## Key numbers
- The free propagator depends on an arbitrary parameter A in the Lagrangian (Eq. 1), with the point transformation invariance A → (A − 2α)/(1 + 4α) for α ≠ −1/4.
- The dressed propagator coefficients for the spin-3/2 part are: Ḡ₁ = 1/S̄₁, Ḡ₂ = 1/S̄₂.
- The spin-1/2 coefficients involve two denominator functions: Δ₁ = S̄₃S̄₆ − S̄₇S̄₁₀ and Δ₂ = S̄₄S̄₅ − S̄₈S̄₉, where Δ₂ = Δ₁(√p² → −√p²).
- The decomposition of an arbitrary spin-tensor S^{μν}(p) contains exactly 10 independent Lorentz-invariant coefficients s_i(p²) in the γ-matrix basis.

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to handle the extra spin-1/2 components of the Rarita-Schwinger field, which have been a longstanding difficulty since the formalism was introduced by Rarita and Schwinger. Previous approaches either dressed only the spin-3/2 part (Pas95, KS) or solved the system numerically (Kor, AKS). The analytical result enables systematic renormalization studies and provides a foundation for phenomenological applications such as Δ(1232) production in Compton scattering, where Pas95 showed that spin-1/2 components are necessary to reproduce experimental data. The authors note that the structure of the denominators Δ₁, Δ₂ resembles the joint dressing of two Dirac fermions with mutual transitions, rather than a single Dirac particle.
