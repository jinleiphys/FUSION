# Single-energy amplitudes for pion photoproduction in the first resonance region

> We consider multipole amplitudes for low-energy pion photoproduction, constructed with minimal model dependence, at single energies. Comparisons with fits to the full resonance region are made. Explanations are suggested for the discrepancies and further experiments are motivated.

## Key claim

Single-energy multipole amplitudes for low-energy pion photoproduction can be constructed with minimal model dependence from type-S data alone, but the consistency of these fits with energy-dependent solutions is sensitive to the quality of recoil polarization (P) data, and discrepancies at 280 MeV and 340 MeV suggest problems in the existing database rather than failures of the method.

## Method

Multipole amplitudes for π⁺n and π⁰p photoproduction were extracted from type-S observables (unpolarized cross section and single-polarization asymmetries P, Σ, T) using a truncated multipole expansion including E₀₊, M₁₋, E₁₊, and M₁₊, with higher-ℓ multipoles taken as real and given by Born plus vector-meson exchange terms from the MAID analysis. Fits were performed at single energies between 280 MeV and 420 MeV using a recent database, and the results were compared to the original Grushin (1980s) determinations and to single-energy solutions (SES) from the SAID energy-dependent analysis.

## Key numbers

- At 280 MeV for π⁺n: Re E₀₊ = 16.72(0.18) × 10⁻³/m_π (Fit1), compared to Grushin value 17.18(0.29) × 10⁻³/m_π and SES value 16.2 × 10⁻³/m_π
- At 280 MeV for π⁺n: Im E₀₊ = -3.41(0.87) × 10⁻³/m_π (Fit1), contradicting SAID, MAID, and Bonn-Gatchina results of (0.4 ± 0.2) × 10⁻³/m_π
- At 340 MeV for π⁺n: Im E₀₊ = 2.15(0.55) × 10⁻³/m_π (Fit1), contradicting SAID, MAID, and Bonn-Gatchina results of (0 ± 0.4) × 10⁻³/m_π
- At 340 MeV for π⁺n: Re M₁₊ = -16.24(0.24) × 10⁻³/m_π (Fit1), compared to SES value -14.85 × 10⁻³/m_π
- At 350 MeV for π⁰p: Im M₁₊ = 23.0 × 10⁻³/m_π (Fit1), compared to Grushin value 23.89(0.10) × 10⁻³/m_π and SES value 22.81 × 10⁻³/m_π
- Errors on fitted isospin multipoles were generally in the 2-5% range

## Context

This work reexamines the multipole extraction method of Grushin (1980s), which was previously considered the least biased determination of multipoles over the delta resonance region. The study extends that approach using a modern database including post-1990 measurements from Beck and others, and compares results to the SAID energy-dependent analysis (SAID), the MAID model (MAID), and the Bonn-Gatchina partial-wave analysis (Bonn-Gatchina). The findings motivate more precise measurements of recoil polarization P and double-polarization observable G to resolve discrepancies, and provide guidance for the current generation of complete or nearly-complete pion and kaon photoproduction experiments.
