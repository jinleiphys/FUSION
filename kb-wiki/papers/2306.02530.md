# Heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients in the pre-hydrodynamic QCD plasma

> Kinetic and chemical equilibrations play important roles in the formation of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (HICs). These processes further influence the production of hard and electromagnetic probes in HICs, in particular, the thermalization of heavy quarks, which are produced at an extremely early time before the formation of the QGP. We calculate the drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks in the pre-hydrodynamic quantum chromodynamic (QCD) plasma with the state-of-the-art QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) solver. We present the time, momentum, and angular dependencies of these coefficients for gluon and quark contributions separately, showing the effects of isotropization and chemical equilibration from the QCD plasma. We also provide a simple formula to estimate the heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients, as well as its energy loss, within the pre-hydrodynamic plasma at different coupling strengths based on the attractor theory. We then discuss the validity of these estimations with leading-order calculations and leading-logarithmic rescaling factors.

## Key claim

The paper provides first-principle calculations of heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients in the pre-hydrodynamic QCD plasma using the QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) solver, but the rescaling of these coefficients to strong coupling via attractor theory is validated only at weak coupling and breaks down at strong coupling, requiring regularization or alternative non-perturbative approaches.

## Method

The authors solve the QCD EKT with Bjorken expansion for gluon and light quark/antiquark distributions (Nf=3) at fixed 'tHooft coupling, then compute heavy quark (charm, mass 1.5 GeV) drag and diffusion coefficients from leading-order pQCD scattering amplitudes with dynamical screening masses fitted to Hard Thermal Loop calculations. They extract the time, momentum, and angular dependence of these coefficients for gluon and quark contributions separately, and test rescaling formulas based on attractor theory and leading-logarithmic factors against numerical EKT results at couplings lambda=5, 8, 10, 20, 40, 60.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m_HQ = 1.5 GeV throughout the calculations
- Default 'tHooft coupling lambda = 10 (alpha_s = lambda/(4pi N_c) with N_c=3)
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.134 fm from matching to LHC 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb data (dN_ch/deta = 1942, S_perp = 138 fm^2), giving tau_0 e_0 = 1.961 GeV^3 and Q_s = 1.496 GeV
- Hydrodynamization time for strongly coupled plasma (eta/s = 1/(4pi)) estimated as tau_h = 0.45-1.23 fm; for eta/s = 1/(2pi) as tau_h = 1.13-3.12 fm
- For weakly coupled plasma (lambda=10), eta/s = 1 and tau* = 100-275 Q_s^{-1} when universal time omega_tilde = 1-2
- Degeneracy factor nu_eff = 47.5 and attractor constant C_infty = 0.87

## Context

This work extends heavy quark thermalization studies beyond the hydrodynamic stage of heavy-ion collisions, where most prior simulations focus (Svetitsky 1987, Moore 2004, Rapp 2018). It builds on the QCD EKT framework developed by Kurkela (2018) and Du (2020) for full QCD kinetics including quarks, and uses attractor theory from Heller (2015) and Romatschke (2017) to connect weakly and strongly coupled regimes. The results enable inclusion of pre-hydrodynamic heavy quark energy loss in phenomenological simulations of heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies, though the rescaling to strong coupling requires further development beyond perturbative QCD.
