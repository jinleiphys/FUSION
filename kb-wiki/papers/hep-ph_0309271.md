# Penta-quark baryon from the QCD Sum Rule

> Exotic penta-quark baryon with strangeness +1, Θ^+, is studied in the QCD sum rule approach. We derive sum rules for the positive and negative parity baryon states with J=1/2 and I=0. It is found that the standard values of the QCD condensates predict a negative parity Θ^+ of mass \simeq 1.5 GeV, while no positive parity state is found. We stress the roles of chiral-odd condensates in determining the parity and mass of Θ^+.

## Key claim

Using the QCD sum rule approach with standard values of QCD condensates, the authors predict a negative-parity pentaquark Theta+ with mass around 1.5 GeV, while no positive-parity state is found; the result is sensitive to the dimension-5 mixed condensate parameter m_0^2, and if that parameter were as small as 0.4 GeV^2 the parity assignment would reverse.

## Method

The authors construct a local interpolating field operator for the pentaquark Theta+ (J=1/2, I=0, strangeness +1) using a combination of a scalar ud diquark and a pseudoscalar ud diquark. They compute the two-point correlation function via operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-6 operators, separate positive- and negative-parity spectral functions using the technique of Jido and Kodama, and apply a Borel-type sum rule with a Gaussian weight function to extract masses and residues.

## Key numbers

- Negative-parity Theta+ mass predicted at approximately 1.5 GeV, consistent with the experimentally observed mass of 1540 MeV/c^2.
- For continuum threshold s_th = 1.8 GeV, the extracted mass is 1.49 GeV (both at M = m_- and M = 1.5 GeV).
- Masses for negative-parity state range from 1.34 GeV (s_th = 1.6 GeV) to 1.65 GeV (s_th = 2.0 GeV).
- Standard QCD parameters used: m_s = 0.12 GeV/c^2, strange quark condensate <bar{s}s> = 0.8 x (-0.23 GeV)^3, m_0^2 = 0.8 GeV^2, gluon condensate <(alpha_s/pi) G^2> = (0.33 GeV)^4.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to exotic pentaquark baryons, following the experimental discovery of the Theta+ by the LEPS group at SPring-8 (Nakano, 2003) and subsequent confirmations by DIANA, CLAS, and SAPHIR. It improves upon prior sum rule analyses by Zhu (2003) and Matheus et al. (2003), who calculated the Theta+ mass but did not determine its parity; the authors show that the chiral-odd part of the correlation function, which those works neglected, is critical for parity determination. The result enables future lattice QCD and phenomenological studies to test the negative-parity assignment and the role of chiral symmetry breaking in exotic hadron spectroscopy.
