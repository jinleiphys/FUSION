# Multi-shell effective interactions

> Background: Effective interactions, either derived from microscopic theories or based on fitting selected properties of nuclei in specific mass regions, are widely used inputs to shell-model studies of nuclei. Until recently, most shell-model calculations have been confined to a single oscillator shell. Recent interest in nuclei away from the stability line, requires however larger shell-model spaces. Since the derivation of microscopic effective interactions has been limited to degenerate model spaces, there are both conceptual and practical limits to present shell-model calculations that utilize such interactions. Purpose: The aim of this work is to present a novel microscopic method to calculate effective interactions for the nuclear shell model. Its main difference from existing theories is that it can be applied not only to degenerate model spaces but also to non-degenerate model spaces. Methods: The formalism is presented in the form of many-body perturbation theory based on the recently developed Extended Kuo-Krenciglowa method. Our method enables us to microscopically construct effective interactions not only in one oscillator shell but also for several oscillator shells. Results: We present numerical results using effective interactions within (i) a single oscillator shell like the sd-shell and the pf-shell, and (ii) two major shells like the sdf7p3-shell and the pfg9-shell. We also present energy levels of several nuclei which have two valence nucleons on top of a given closed-shell core. Conclusions: Our results show that the present method works excellently in shell-model spaces that comprise several oscillator shells, as well as in a single oscillator shell. We show in particular that the microscopic inter-shell interactions are much more attractive than has been expected by degenerate perturbation theory. The consequences for shell-model studies are discussed.

# Multi-shell effective interactions

## Key claim

The Extended Kuo-Krenciglowa (EKK) method enables microscopic construction of effective interactions for non-degenerate model spaces comprising several oscillator shells, overcoming the limitation of previous degenerate perturbation theories that diverge when applied to multi-shell spaces. The authors show that inter-shell interactions obtained with this method are "much more attractive than has been expected by degenerate perturbation theory."

## Method

The authors formulate the effective interaction using many-body perturbation theory based on the Extended Kuo-Krenciglowa (EKK) method, which introduces an energy parameter E to shift the Hamiltonian and allows iterative solution of the decoupling equation for non-degenerate model spaces. They calculate the valence-linked Q-box diagrams up to third order in the nucleon-nucleon interaction V, using the low-momentum interaction Vlowk with a sharp cutoff Lambda = 2.5 fm^{-1} derived from the chiral N^3LO interaction of Entem and Machleidt. The total (P+Q) Hilbert space comprises harmonic oscillator basis states in the lowest seven major shells, and the final effective interactions are obtained in P-spaces of one or two major shells.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff for the low-momentum interaction Vlowk: Lambda = 2.5 fm^{-1}
- Q-box diagrams evaluated up to third order in the nucleon-nucleon interaction V
- Hilbert space composed of harmonic oscillator basis states in the lowest seven major shells
- Intermediate excitations included up to 10-20 oscillator quanta in the Q-box diagrams
- Energy parameter E for two-valence-nucleon systems initially estimated as E ~ 2 * epsilon_a^{min}, where epsilon_a^{min} is the minimum energy of active valence particle states
- Lowest pole of the Q-box(E) located at 2 * epsilon_a^{min} + 1 * hbar * omega

## Context

This work extends the Kuo-Krenciglowa (KK) method (Krenciglowa 1974) and the Lee-Suzuki scheme (Suzuki 1980), which were limited to degenerate model spaces consisting of typically one major oscillator shell. The EKK method enables microscopic effective interactions for multi-shell spaces needed to describe unstable nuclei, such as those in the island of inversion currently explained with empirical interactions (PhysRevC.70.044307). This development allows future shell-model studies to use microscopically derived interactions for spaces spanning several oscillator shells, addressing a key limitation noted in reviews of microscopic effective interactions (Hjorth-Jensen 1995).
