# Theoretical Conference Summary

> This talk reviews progress reported at Quark-Matter 2001 on various theoretical aspects of the quark-gluon plasma and discusses how the new results obtained at RHIC change the standard picture of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The first RHIC results do not qualitatively change the picture from earlier nucleus-nucleus collisions, but natural extrapolations fail in a few instances and intriguing new features suggest possible first glimpses of non-trivial QCD behavior in the bulk of the collisions.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical progress reported at Quark-Matter 2001, covering first-principles lattice QCD calculations of quark-gluon plasma thermodynamics, analytical approaches to the equation of state, the "standard picture" of nucleus-nucleus collisions from initial conditions through freeze-out, and specific observables including elliptic flow, strangeness, dileptons, J/Ψ production, and jet quenching. The talk synthesizes contributions from multiple speakers and compares theoretical expectations with the new RHIC data.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature for full QCD with dynamical quarks: T_c = (173 ± 8) MeV for N_f = 2 light flavors, T_c = (154 ± 8) MeV for N_f = 3 (statistical errors only)
- Energy density of the quark-gluon plasma near T_c: ε ≈ 700 MeV/fm³ with 40% uncertainty
- String breaking and screening near T_c occurs already for distances as small as r ≈ 0.3 fm
- Energy per particle at freeze-out remains approximately constant: E/N ≈ 1 GeV across a wide range of beam energies
- Saturation scale Q_s² estimated at ∼ 2 GeV² at present energies
- Elliptic flow v₂ shows hydrodynamical behavior at small p_T (p_T < 1 GeV) with saturation around p_T ∼ 2 GeV

## Context

This conference summary reviews progress reported at Quark-Matter 2001, extending the "standard picture" of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions developed from SPS data to the new RHIC energy regime. It discusses lattice results from Karsch, analytical approaches from Iancu and Schroeder, the saturation picture from Venugopalan and Kovchegov, and specific signal analyses from multiple groups. The work enables future comparisons between first-principles QCD calculations and RHIC data, particularly for jet quenching, elliptic flow, and J/Ψ production, while noting that the gap between theory and experiment is diminishing but remains significant.
