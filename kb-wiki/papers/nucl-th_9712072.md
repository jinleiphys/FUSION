# Lorentz Multiplet Structure of Baryon Spectra and Relativistic Description

> The pole positions of the various baryon resonances are known to reveal well-pronounced clustering, so-called Hoehler clusters. For nonstrange baryons the Hoehler clusters are shown to be identical to Lorentz multiplets of the type (j,j)*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] with j being a half-integer. For the Lambda hyperons below 1800 MeV these clusters are shown to be of the type [(1,0)+ (0,1)]*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] while above 1800 MeV they are parity duplicated (J,0)+(0,J) (Weinberg-Ahluwalia) states. Therefore, for Lambda hyperons the restoration of chiral symmetry takes place above 1800 MeV. Finally, it is demonstrated that the description of spin-3/2 particles in terms of a 2nd rank antisymmetric Lorentz tensor with Dirac spinor components does not contain any off-shell parameters and avoids the main difficulties of the Rarita-Schwinger description based upon a 4-vector with Dirac spinor components.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the well-known clustering of baryon resonance pole positions (Hohler clusters) is identical to specific Lorentz multiplets of the type {j,j} ⊗ [(1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2)] for nonstrange baryons, while for Lambda hyperons the clustering pattern changes above 1800 MeV from this structure to parity-duplicated (J,0)⊕(0,J) states, indicating chiral symmetry restoration above that energy threshold.

## Method

The author re-analyzed the symmetry of all reported nonstrange baryon excitations with masses below 2500 MeV using SL(2,C)⊗SU(2)_I classification rather than the conventional O(3)⊗SU(6) scheme, mapping the observed Hohler clusters onto specific Lorentz group representations. For Lambda hyperons, the classification was extended to include parity doublet patterns, and a comparison was made between the Rarita-Schwinger description of spin-3/2 particles (based on a 4-vector with Dirac spinor components) and an alternative formulation using a 2nd-rank antisymmetric Lorentz tensor with Dirac spinor components proposed by Fushchich and Nikitin.

## Key numbers

- The first Delta resonance cluster (n=4) contains seven Delta resonances with spins from 1/2- to 7/2+ concentrated in the mass region between 1900 MeV and 1950 MeV
- The nucleon S11, P11, P13, D13, D15, F15, and F17 (missing) states form a cluster at (1665 ± 25) MeV with width -(55 ± 15)i MeV
- The second nucleon cluster appears at (2110 ± 50) MeV with width -(180 ± 50)i MeV
- The Delta cluster at (1820 ± 30) MeV has width -(120 ± 30)i MeV
- The splitting between S01 and D03 Lambda resonances reaches 175 MeV, with a gap of about 150 MeV between their mass average and the P01 state
- Parity doublets in the Lambda hyperon spectrum appear at approximately 1800 MeV, including Lambda(1800;1/2-), Lambda(1810;1/2+), Lambda(1820;5/2+), and Lambda(1830;5/2-)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Kirchbach (1997) that first proposed O(1,3)⊗SU(2)_I symmetry for baryon spectra, and builds upon the systematic study of Lorentz multiplets by Weinberg for relativistic equations of motion for particles with any spin. The paper contradicts the conventional O(3)⊗SU(6) classification scheme based on the naive three-flavor quark model, and the parity doublet patterns observed in Lambda hyperons above 1800 MeV align with the reflection-asymmetric nucleon shape models of Donau and Reinhardt as well as the work of Iachello, Bijker, and Leviatan. The alternative spin-3/2 description avoids the off-shell parameter problems of the Rarita-Schwinger formalism noted by Bennm and Moldauer, and the parity doublet patterns for heavy flavor baryons were previously reported by Balachandran and Vaidya.
