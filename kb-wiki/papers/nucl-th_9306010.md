# Exact response of the non-relativistic harmonic oscillator

> Using Green$'$s function and operator techniques we give a closed expression for the response of a non-relativistic system interacting through confining, harmonic forces. The expression for the incoherent part permits rapid evaluation of coefficients in a 1/q expansion. A comparison is made with standard approximation methods.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed-form expression for the linear response of a non-relativistic system of particles bound by harmonic confining forces, which is exact for that specific system; the abstract does not emphasize that the method is limited to the harmonic oscillator and that the closed expression is only possible because the Hamiltonian separates in Jacobi variables, reducing the problem to a single relative coordinate in one dimension.

## Method

The authors treat a "di-quark" (two particles) with a harmonic confining interaction, which separates into center-of-mass and relative motion; they then solve for the relative motion only. They use two independent approaches: (i) a Green's function method, starting from the path-integral expression for the harmonic oscillator propagator, and (ii) an operator technique that evaluates the time-evolved coordinate operator \(\hat{x}(t)\) using the Glauber formula. Both methods yield identical closed-form expressions for the reduced response \(\phi(q,y)\) in terms of integrals over a variable \(s\), split into incoherent and coherent contributions.

## Key numbers

* The expansion parameter for the \(1/q\) series is \(2\beta/q\), where \(\beta\) is the inverse harmonic oscillator length.
* The leading term in the \(1/q\) expansion is \(F_0(y) = (1/\sqrt{\pi\beta^2})\exp(-y^2/\beta^2)\).
* The first correction term is \((m/q)F_1(y) = -(2\beta/q)(y/\beta)[1 - (2/3)(y/\beta)^2] F_0(y)\).
* The second correction term is \((m/q)^2F_2(y) = -(1/6)(2\beta/q)^2[1 - 9(y/\beta)^2 + 8(y/\beta)^4 - (4/3)(y/\beta)^6] F_0(y)\).
* The third correction term is \((m/q)^3F_3(y) = (1/2)(2\beta/q)^3(y/\beta)[1 - (47/9)(y/\beta)^2 + (74/15)(y/\beta)^4 - (4/3)(y/\beta)^6 + (8/81)(y/\beta)^8] F_0(y)\).

## Context

This work extends the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) \(1/q\) expansion method (Gersch, Rodriguez, and Smith, 1972) by providing an exact closed-form expression for the harmonic oscillator case, against which approximations can be tested. The authors compare their exact result with the first cumulant approximation (Gersch and Rodriguez, 1973) and with an iteration of \(F_1\) (Carraro and Koonin, 1990; Besprosvany, 1991), finding that only the \(1/q\) term of the iterant matches the exact expansion. The method is noted to be applicable to other systems with known closed-form Green's functions, such as the delta-shell potential and the infinite square well (Grosche and Steiner, 1993).
