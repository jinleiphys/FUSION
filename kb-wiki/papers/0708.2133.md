# Hard diffractive quarkonium hadroproduction at high energies

> We present a study of heavy quarkonium production in hard diffractive process by the Pomeron exchange for Tevatron and LHC energies. The numerical results are computed using recent experimental determination of the diffractive parton density functions in Pomeron and are corrected by unitarity corrections through gap survival probability factor. We give predictions for single as well as central diffractive ratios. These processes are sensitive to the gluon content of the Pomeron at small Bjorken-x and may be particularly useful in studying the small-x physics. They may also be a good place to test the different available mechanisms for quarkonium production at hadron colliders.

## Key claim

Hard diffractive heavy quarkonium production at Tevatron and LHC energies can be described by the Color Evaporation Model combined with Regge factorization, but the predictions depend critically on the gap survival probability factor that corrects for multiple-Pomeron exchange; the single diffractive ratio for J/Psi at Tevatron is predicted to be 1.12 +/- 0.19 percent, consistent with CDF measurements, but the theoretical uncertainty is dominated by the survival factor.

## Method

The author computes single and central diffractive cross sections for J/Psi and Upsilon production using the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) with NLO partonic cross sections, replacing one or both proton parton distributions by a convolution of the Pomeron flux factor and the H1 2006 DPDF Fit A diffractive parton densities. The single-Pomeron exchange results are then multiplied by gap survival probability factors from the KMR two-channel eikonal model: 0.15 (Tevatron) and 0.09 (LHC) for single diffraction, and 0.08 (Tevatron) and 0.04 (LHC) for central diffraction. The inclusive cross sections are computed with MRST 2004 NLO parton sets, using quark masses m_c = 1.2 GeV and m_b = 4.75 GeV with factorization and renormalization scales set to mu = 2 m_Q.

## Key numbers

- Single diffractive differential cross section for J/Psi at y=0: 85 nb (with survival factor) and 566 nb (single-Pomeron only) at sqrt(s) = 2 TeV; 159 nb (with survival) and 1770 nb (single-Pomeron) at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV
- Single diffractive differential cross section for Upsilon (B times dsigma/dy at y=0): 11 pb (with survival) and 72 pb (single-Pomeron) at sqrt(s) = 2 TeV; 35 pb (with survival) and 386 pb (single-Pomeron) at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV
- Central diffractive differential cross section for J/Psi at y=0: 18 nb at sqrt(s) = 2 TeV; 45 nb at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV
- Central diffractive differential cross section for Upsilon (B times dsigma/dy at y=0): 0.8 pb at sqrt(s) = 2 TeV; 3 pb at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV
- Single diffractive ratio R_SD for J/Psi: 0.93 percent (Tevatron) and 0.50 percent (LHC); numbers in parentheses for single-Pomeron only are 6.2 percent and 5.9 percent respectively
- Single diffractive ratio R_SD for Upsilon+Upsilon'+Upsilon'': 0.78 percent (Tevatron) and 0.39 percent (LHC); single-Pomeron only: 5.2 percent and 4.3 percent
- Central diffractive ratio R_CD for J/Psi: 0.2 percent (Tevatron) and 0.15 percent (LHC); single-Pomeron only: 2.5 percent and 3.7 percent
- Central diffractive ratio R_CD for Upsilon+Upsilon'+Upsilon'': 0.06 percent (Tevatron) and 0.03 percent (LHC); single-Pomeron only: 0.7 percent and 0.8 percent
- Gap survival probability factors from KMR model: single diffraction 0.15 (Tevatron) and 0.09 (LHC); central diffraction 0.08 (Tevatron) and 0.04 (LHC)
- CEM non-perturbative factors: F = 2.5 x 10^{-2} for J/Psi and F = 4.6 x 10^{-2} for Upsilon

## Context

This work extends the Ingelman-Schlein (IS) picture of hard diffraction to heavy quarkonium production, using the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) which has been successful in describing inclusive quarkonium data at RHIC and Tevatron. The predictions are compared to earlier calculations by Yuan (using color octet fragmentation), by KMR and GLM for survival probabilities, and to exclusive production via photon exchange and Odderon fusion studied by Bzdak and others. The results enable experimental searches at the LHC for diffractive quarkonium as a probe of the gluon content of the Pomeron at small Bjorken-x, though the author notes that theoretical uncertainties from the survival factor, quark masses, and scale choices remain large and that more detailed studies are needed.
