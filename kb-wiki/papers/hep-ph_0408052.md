# The Abelianization of QCD Plasma Instabilities

> QCD plasma instabilities appear to play an important role in the equilibration of quark-gluon plasmas in heavy-ion collisions in the theoretical limit of weak coupling (i.e. asymptotically high energy). It is important to understand what non-linear physics eventually stops the exponential growth of unstable modes. It is already known that the initial growth of plasma instabilities in QCD closely parallels that in QED. However, once the unstable modes of the gauge-fields grow large enough for non-Abelian interactions between them to become important, one might guess that the dynamics of QCD plasma instabilities and QED plasma instabilities become very different. In this paper, we give suggestive arguments that non-Abelian self-interactions between the unstable modes are ineffective at stopping instability growth, and that the growing non-Abelian gauge fields become approximately Abelian after a certain stage in their growth. This in turn suggests that understanding the development of QCD plasma instabilities in the non-linear regime may have close parallels to similar processes in traditional plasma physics. We conjecture that the physics of collisionless plasma instabilities in SU(2) and SU(3) gauge theory becomes equivalent, respectively, to (i) traditional plasma physics, which is U(1) gauge theory, and (ii) plasma physics of U(1)x U(1) gauge theory.

## Key claim

The authors argue that non-Abelian self-interactions between unstable soft gauge-field modes in QCD plasmas are ineffective at stopping instability growth, and that the growing non-Abelian gauge fields become approximately Abelian after a certain stage, suggesting that QCD plasma instabilities in the non-linear regime may have close parallels to traditional U(1) plasma physics rather than diverging from it.

## Method

The authors analyze the effective potential for soft gauge-field configurations that depend only on the spatial coordinate z, derived from the non-linear Vlasov equations for anisotropic quark-gluon plasmas. They then perform numerical simulations of a 1+1 dimensional toy model gauge theory that captures the essential dynamics, tracking the real-time evolution of the fields to test whether Abelianization occurs.

## Key numbers

* The non-Abelian scale for gauge field amplitude is A ~ k_soft/g, where k_soft is the soft momentum scale; the Abelian (hard-particle) scale is A ~ p_hard/g, with p_hard >> k_soft.
* The instability growth parameter mu^2 is defined as mu^2 = -Pi_xx(0,hat_e_z) = -Pi_yy(0,hat_e_z) > 0, where Pi is the self-energy of the linearized theory.
* In the effective potential for a simplified SU(2) configuration, static non-Abelian solutions exist at field amplitude A ~ mu/g, corresponding to the non-Abelian scale.
* The equilibration time for the bottom-up thermalization scenario is parametrically tau_eq ~ alpha^{-n} Q_s^{-1}, where the exponent n is unknown.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of plasma instabilities in QCD beyond the linearized regime studied by Mrowczynski (various works) and Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore (2003), who showed that such instabilities disrupt the original bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son. The paper's conjecture that QCD instabilities Abelianize enables future study of their non-linear development using techniques from traditional plasma physics, and suggests that the equilibration time exponent n remains unknown.
