# Phase shift formulas for baryon-baryon scattering in elongated boxes

> We have established the relations between the baryon-baryon scattering phase shifts and the two-particle energy spectrum in the elongated box. We have studied the cases with both the periodic boundary condition and twisted boundary condition in the center of mass frame. The framework is also extended to the system of nonzero total momentum with periodic boundary condition in the moving frame. This will be helpful to extract the phase shifts in the continuum from lattice QCD data using asymmetric volumes.

## Key claim

The authors derive generalized Lüscher's formulas that relate baryon-baryon scattering phase shifts to two-particle energy eigenvalues in elongated boxes (dimensions \(L \times L \times \eta L\)), extending prior work that was limited to cubic boxes or meson-meson/meson-baryon systems. The formulas apply to center-of-mass frame with periodic or twisted boundary conditions, and to moving frames with nonzero total momentum, but the derivations assume parity-conserving interactions and a cutoff angular momentum \(\Lambda = 2\).

## Method

The authors start from the nonrelativistic quantum-mechanical wave function for two baryons with spin, expand it in spin spherical harmonics, and match the asymptotic form to singular periodic solutions of the Helmholtz equation in an elongated box. The symmetry group reduces from \(O_h\) (cubic) to \(D_{4h}\) (elongated), and the generalized zeta function \(Z_{lm}(q^2,\eta)\) is defined with a modified index \(\tilde{\mathbf{n}} = (n_x, n_y, n_z/\eta)\). The resulting Lüscher formula is expressed as a determinant condition involving the S-matrix and the matrix \(\mathcal{M}^{(s)}_{JMl;J'M'l'}\), which is constructed from the zeta function and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The authors then specialize to cases A (parity \((-1)^{J+1}\), triplet states with \(l = J \pm 1\)) and case B (parity \((-1)^J\), \(l = J\) with singlet or triplet spin), and provide explicit phase shift formulas for each irreducible representation of \(D_{4h}\) up to \(J=2\).

## Key numbers

- Elongated box dimensions are \(L \times L \times \eta L\), where \(\eta\) is the elongation factor in the \(z\)-direction.
- The generalized zeta function is \(Z_{lm}(q^2,\eta) = \sum_{\mathbf{n}} \mathcal{Y}_{lm}(\tilde{\mathbf{n}}) / (\tilde{\mathbf{n}}^2 - q^2)\) with \(\tilde{\mathbf{n}} = (n_x, n_y, n_z/\eta)\).
- For case A in the \(A_1^-\) representation, the phase shift formula for \(\delta_{01}\) (ignoring mixing with \(J=2\)) is \(\cot\delta_{01} = \omega_{00} - (2\sqrt{30}/15)\omega_{22}\).
- For case A in the \(B_1^-\) representation, \(\cot\delta_{21} = \omega_{00} - (\sqrt{5}/5)\omega_{20} - (\sqrt{30}/5)\omega_{22}\).
- For case B with \(s=s'=1\) in the \(A_1^+\) representation, \(\cot\delta_2 = \omega_{00} - (4/7)\omega_{40} + (\sqrt{5}/7)\omega_{20} + (\sqrt{30}/7)\omega_{22} + (2\sqrt{10}/7)\omega_{42}\).
- For case B with \(s=s'=1\) in the \(A_2^-\) representation, \(\cot\delta_1 = \omega_{00} - (\sqrt{5}/5)\omega_{20} + (\sqrt{30}/5)\omega_{22}\).

## Context

This work extends the original Lüscher formula (Lüscher, 1986, 1990, 1991) for two-particle scattering in cubic boxes to elongated boxes for baryon-baryon systems, building on the asymmetric-box technique first applied to pion-pion scattering by Li (2007) and later to meson-baryon systems by Lee and Alexandru (2017). It also generalizes the moving-frame formalism of Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995) and the twisted-boundary-condition approach of Bedaque (2004) to baryon-baryon scattering in elongated volumes. The derived formulas enable lattice QCD practitioners to extract baryon-baryon phase shifts from simulations using asymmetric volumes, which provide better energy resolution and access to more momentum modes than cubic boxes, particularly for studying hyperon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon forces relevant to nuclear structure and neutron star properties.
