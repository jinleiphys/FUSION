# Few-body approach to structure of $\bar{K}$-nuclear quasi-bound states

> Structure of light antikaon-nuclear quasi-bound states, which consist of an antikaon $(\bar{K}=K^-,~\bar{K}^0)$ and a few nucleons $(N=p,~n)$ such as $\bar{K}NN$, $\bar{K}NNN$, $\bar{K}NNNN$ and $\bar{K}NNNNNN$ systems, is studied with full three- to seven-body calculations. Employing a realistic $\bar{K}N$ potential based on the chiral SU(3) effective field theory with the SIDDHARTA constraint, we show that the central nucleon densities of these systems increases when the antikaon is injected, by about factor of two at maximum. The $\bar{K}NNNN$ system shows the largest central density, about 0.74 fm$^{-3}$ even with the phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potential, which are not as high as those suggested in previous studies with approximate treatments of the few-body systems. We find the spin of the ground state of the $\bar{K}NNNNNN$ system depends on the strength of the $\bar{K}N$ attraction. Thus, the quantum number of the ground state can be another constraint on the $\bar{K}N$ interaction.

## Key claim

The central nucleon densities of light antikaon-nuclear systems increase by about a factor of two at maximum when the antikaon is injected, but the densities are not as high as those suggested in previous studies with approximate treatments of the few-body systems; the spin of the ground state of the $\bar{K}NNNNNN$ system depends on the strength of the $\bar{K}N$ attraction.

## Method

The authors performed full three- to seven-body calculations using the stochastic variational method (SVM) with correlated Gaussian basis for $\bar{K}NN$, $\bar{K}NNN$, $\bar{K}NNNN$, and $\bar{K}NNNNNN$ systems. They employed two types of $\bar{K}N$ interactions: an energy-dependent potential based on chiral SU(3) effective field theory with the SIDDHARTA constraint, and the energy-independent phenomenological Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) potential, combined with the Argonne V4' $NN$ potential.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar{K}NNNN$ system shows the largest central density, about 0.74 fm$^{-3}$, even with the phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potential
- For the $\bar{K}NN$ ($K^-pp$-$\bar{K}^0pn$) system with $J^\pi=0^-$ using the SIDDHARTA potential Type I: binding energy $B = 27.9$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 30.9$ MeV; with Type II: $B = 26.1$ MeV, $\Gamma = 59.3$ MeV; with the AY potential: $B = 48.7$ MeV, $\Gamma = 61.9$ MeV
- The $\bar{K}N$ pole positions from the SIDDHARTA potential are found at $1424-26i$ MeV and $1381-81i$ MeV
- The AV4' potential gives binding energies for ordinary nuclei: $^2$H = 2.24 MeV, $^3$H = 8.99 MeV, $^4$He = 32.1 MeV, $^6$He = 32.2 MeV, $^6$Li = 35.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaonic nuclei by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and Yamazaki (2002, 2007) that used optical potential or $g$-matrix approaches and predicted extremely dense systems about ten times higher than ordinary nuclear density. The present full few-body calculations show that those earlier high-density predictions are not realized when the few-body problem is accurately solved. The results enable future experimental constraints on the $\bar{K}N$ interaction through measurement of the ground state spin of the $\bar{K}NNNNNN$ system.
