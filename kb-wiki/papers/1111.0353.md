# Selected topics on parton distribution functions

> We report recent studies on structure functions of the nucleon and nuclei. First, clustering effects are investigated in the structure function F_2 of Be-9 for explaining an unusual nuclear correction found in a JLab experiment. We propose that high densities created by formation of clustering structure like 2*alpha+neutron in Be-9 is the origin of the unexpected JLab result by using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). There is an approved proposal at JLab to investigate the structure functions of light nuclei including the cluster structure, so that much details will become clear in a few years. Second, tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distributions are obtained by analyzing HERMES measurements on the structure function b_1 for the deuteron. The result suggests a finite tensor polarization for antiquark distributions, which is an interesting topic for further theoretical and experimental investigations. An experimental proposal exists at JLab for measuring b_1 of the deuteron as a new tensor-structure study in 2010's. Furthermore, the antiquark tensor polarization could be measured by polarized deuteron Drell-Yan processes at hadron facilities such as J-PARC and GSI-FAIR. Third, the recent CDF dijet anomaly is investigated within the standard model by considering possible modifications of the strange-quark distribution. We find that the shape of a dijet-mass spectrum changes depending on the strange-quark distribution. It indicates that the CDF excess could be partially explained as a PDF effect, particularly by the strangeness in the nucleon, within the standard model if the excess at m_{jj}~140 GeV is not a sharp peak.

## Key claim

The anomalously large nuclear modification observed in the structure function F2 of Be-9 at JLab can be explained by high densities created by cluster formation (2-alpha plus neutron structure) rather than by mean-field nuclear effects, but only if the Be-9 data point is plotted using the maximum local density from the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model rather than from the shell model.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and shell-model wave functions to calculate nucleon momentum distributions for Be-9, then computed nuclear structure functions via a convolution integral of the nucleon momentum distribution with the free nucleon structure function. For the tensor structure analysis, they parametrized tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distributions and fitted them to HERMES b1 data using two options: one with no antiquark tensor polarization and one allowing finite antiquark tensor polarization. For the CDF dijet anomaly, they calculated W+dijet, Z+dijet, top, WW, and WZ processes using the event generator GR@PPA with different strange-quark distributions (CTEQ6L1, HERMES-like soft, and a hard intrinsic-like distribution) evolved to Q2 = 2 MW2.

## Key numbers

- The HERMES measurement of b1 for the deuteron was analyzed; the set-2 analysis (allowing finite antiquark tensor polarization) gave ∫ dx b1(x) = 0.0058
- The CDF dijet anomaly was observed at mjj ≈ 140 GeV; the proton beam energy is 980 GeV (=1.96 TeV /2), so one dijet carries about 70 GeV (=140 GeV /2), corresponding to PDFs at x ≈ 0.1
- The strange-quark distribution is roughly 40% of the light antiquark distribution [ū(x)+d̄(x)]/2 in the CTEQ6L1 parametrization
- The average scale of the HERMES experiment is Q2 = 2.5 GeV2; distributions were evolved to Q2 = 2 MW2 for the CDF analysis

## Context

This work extends studies of nuclear clustering effects in structure functions, following the JLab measurement of nuclear modifications for light nuclei that showed an anomalously large effect in Be-9. The tensor structure analysis builds on the first measurement of b1 by the HERMES collaboration and uses a sum rule similar to the Gottfried sum to relate the b1 first moment to finite antiquark tensor polarization. The CDF dijet investigation addresses the anomaly reported by CDF (2011) which was not observed by D0, and suggests that PDF effects from the strange-quark distribution could partially explain the excess if it is not a sharp peak. The authors note that an approved JLab proposal exists to measure structure functions of light nuclei including Li-6, Li-7, B-10, and B-11 after the 12 GeV upgrade, and that future measurements at J-PARC and GSI-FAIR could probe antiquark tensor polarization via polarized deuteron Drell-Yan processes.
