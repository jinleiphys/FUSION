# Hydrodynamic afterburner for the Color Glass Condensate and the parton energy loss

> We take hydrodynamic initial conditions in relativistic heavy ion collisions from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) picture through the kT factorization formula. Gluon distributions produced from the CGC are found to provide good initial conditions for the hydrodynamic simulations in Au + Au collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energies. We reproduce the centrality, rapidity, and energy dependences of multiplicity within this approach. We also investigate the energy loss of high pT partons in the dense thermalized medium created from colliding two CGC's. We find that our results on the centrality dependence of nuclear modification factors for pions and back-to-back correlation for charged hadrons at midrapidity are consistent with the RHIC data up to semicentral events. Whereas, our approach in which jets are calculated from perturbative QCD 2->2 processes predicts less suppression at forward rapidity region compared to the BRAHMS data in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors combine Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial conditions with hydrodynamic evolution and parton energy loss to create a unified framework for relativistic heavy ion collisions, finding that this approach reproduces RHIC data for centrality dependence of nuclear modification factors and back-to-back correlations up to semicentral events, but predicts less suppression at forward rapidity compared to BRAHMS data.

## Method

The authors compute initial gluon distributions from the CGC using the kT factorization formula of Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (KLN), then assume these gluons thermalize to provide initial conditions for full three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with a bag model equation of state. High pT partons from pQCD 2→2 processes (generated via PYTHIA) are propagated through the hydrodynamic medium, losing energy via the first-order Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) radiative energy loss formula with opacity expansion.

## Key numbers

- Initial time τ0 = 0.6 fm/c for hydrodynamic simulations
- Saturation scale ⟨Qs²(x=0.01)⟩ ≈ 2.0 GeV²/c² in Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=0 at RHIC
- Cutoff momentum pT,cut = 3 GeV/c separating thermalized gluons from hard partons
- Initial transverse energy per particle ET/Ng ≈ 1.6 GeV at y=0 from the kT factorization formula
- Bag constant B = 486 MeV/fm³ for the equation of state
- Critical temperature Tc = 170 MeV for phase transition and chemical freezeout
- Kinetic freezeout temperature Tth = 100 MeV
- Screening mass μ = 0.5 GeV and typical medium length L = 3 fm in the GLV energy loss formula
- Energy loss coefficient C = 0.35 fixed by fitting PHENIX neutral pion RAA data at 10% centrality

## Context

This work extends the hydro+jet model previously developed by Hirano and Nara (2003-2004) by replacing parametrized initial conditions with CGC-based initial conditions from the KLN approach (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi, 2001-2002). It addresses the ambiguity problem of hydrodynamic initial conditions noted by Huovinen (1998) and connects CGC predictions to final-state observables including elliptic flow and jet quenching. The approach enables systematic study of how initial gluon saturation affects both bulk collective flow and hard probe suppression simultaneously, though the forward rapidity discrepancy with BRAHMS data indicates the need for improved treatment of nuclear shadowing or initial-state effects.
