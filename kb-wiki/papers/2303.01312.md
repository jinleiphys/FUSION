# Unitary interaction geometries in few-body systems

> We consider few-body systems in which only a certain subset of the particle-particle interactions is resonant. We characterize each subset by a {\it unitary graph} in which the vertices represent distinguishable particles and the edges resonant 2-body interactions. Few-body systems whose unitary graph is connected will collapse unless a repulsive 3-body interaction is included. We find two categories of graphs, distinguished by the kind of 3-body repulsion necessary to stabilize the associated system. Each category is characterized by whether the graph contains a loop or not: for tree-like graphs (graphs containing a loop) the 3-body force renormalizing them is the same as in the 3-body system with two (three) resonant interactions. We show numerically that this conjecture is correct for the 4-body case as well as for a few 5-body configurations. We explain this result in the 4-body sector qualitatively by imposing Bethe-Peierls boundary conditions on the pertinent Faddeev-Yakubovsky~decomposition of the wave function.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the type of three-body force required to stabilize a few-body system with resonant two-body interactions is determined by whether the graph representing those resonant interactions (the "unitary graph") contains a loop: tree-like graphs require the same three-body force as the three-body system with two resonant pairs, while graphs containing a cycle require the same three-body force as the fully resonant three-body system. The authors hedge that this conjecture is shown numerically for the four-body case and a few five-body configurations, and they provide a qualitative explanation only for the four-body sector.

## Method

The authors model distinguishable, equal-mass particles with Gaussian-regulated contact two-body potentials tuned to unitarity (scattering length exceeding 10^5 in mass units) and include Gaussian three-body contact potentials to prevent collapse. They solve the Schrodinger equation using the stochastic-variational method (SVM) for all N >= 3 systems, with the refined-resonating-group method (RGM) used as verification for a subset of cutoffs in the three- and four-body systems. The running of the two-body coupling c(lambda) is calibrated by solving the two-body Schrodinger equation at zero energy with a standard Numerov integration algorithm, while the three-body coupling d(lambda) is calibrated to produce a fixed three-body ground-state binding energy of -0.01 mass units for both the Lambda (two resonant pairs) and Delta (three resonant pairs) configurations.

## Key numbers

* The three-body ground-state energy without a stabilizing three-body force diverges as E_{3Lambda} ~ -0.000118 lambda^2 and E_{3Delta} ~ -0.0596 lambda^2, where lambda is the cutoff in mass units.
* The discrete scaling factor for the Efimov effect is (22.7)^2 for the fully resonant Delta configuration and (1986.1)^2 for the Lambda configuration with two resonant pairs.
* For the four-body configurations requiring the Delta-type three-body force: E_{4-full} = 4.4(1) E_{3Delta}, E_{4-slash} = 1.8(1) E_{3Delta}, E_{4-paw} ~ 1.0 E_{3Delta}, and E_{4-circle} ~ 0.2 E_{3Delta}.
* For the four-body configurations requiring the Lambda-type three-body force: E_{4-star} = 11(1) E_{3Lambda} and E_{4-line} = 8(1) E_{3Lambda}.
* The three-body ground-state binding energy used for calibration was E_3 = -0.01 m, where m is the particle mass.

## Context

This work generalizes the methods and findings of Contessi (2021) on AABB-type four-body systems to arbitrary few-body systems characterized by the geometry of their resonant interactions, represented as "unitary graphs." It extends the known Efimov effect for three-boson systems (Efimov, 1970) and the universal properties of unitary few-body systems (Braaten, 2004) to configurations where only a subset of particle pairs are resonant. The results enable predictions for the binding energies and Efimov scaling ratios of larger clusters based on their graph topology, with the combinatorial approximation for the N-body full configuration compared to prior calculations by von Stecher (2009) and Bazak (2016).
