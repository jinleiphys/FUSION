# QCD Shear Viscosity at (almost) NLO

> We compute the shear viscosity of QCD with matter, including almost all next-to-leading order corrections -- that is, corrections suppressed by one power of $g$ relative to leading order. We argue that the still missing terms are small. The next-to-leading order corrections are large and bring $η/s$ down by more than a factor of 3 at physically relevant couplings. The perturbative expansion is problematic even at $T \simeq 100$ GeV. The largest next-to-leading order correction to $η/s$ arises from modifications to the qhat parameter, which determines the rate of transverse momentum diffusion. We also explore quark number diffusion, and shear viscosity in pure-glue QCD and in QED.

## Key claim
The authors compute the shear viscosity of QCD with matter at almost next-to-leading order (NLO), finding that NLO corrections lower η/s by more than a factor of 3 at physically relevant couplings, but they acknowledge that the perturbative expansion remains problematic even at T ≃ 100 GeV and that their treatment is only "almost" NLO because they cannot compute the gain terms (momentum redistribution to other particles) using light-cone techniques.

## Method
The authors reorganize the leading-order collision operator into Fokker-Planck diffusion terms and conversion processes, then incorporate NLO corrections to the transverse momentum diffusion coefficient (δq̂), longitudinal diffusion, identity-changing processes, and the 1↔2 splitting rate. They solve the linearized Boltzmann equation using a variational method with a basis of six functions, and estimate the missing gain terms by analyzing their importance at leading order.

## Key numbers
- η/s at LO is ~0.5 for physically relevant couplings; NLO corrections bring η/s down by more than a factor of 3 at physically relevant couplings
- At T ~ 1000 GeV, NLO corrections lower η/s by a factor of two; at T ~ 250 MeV, by a factor of four
- The NLO correction to η/s arises primarily from modifications to the q̂ parameter (transverse momentum diffusion coefficient)
- The authors use 6 basis functions in their variational Ansatz, with results shifting by less than 10⁻⁴ when the basis is made larger

## Context
This work extends the leading-order perturbative calculations of shear viscosity by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000-2003), which found η/s ~ 0.5 but were frequently misinterpreted. The NLO corrections are large enough to bring perturbative results closer to the small η/s ~ 0.1-0.3 values inferred from RHIC and LHC hydrodynamic fits (Heinz 2013, Gale 2013) and lattice determinations (Meyer 2007, Pasztor 2016-2018). The calculation builds on Caron-Huot's (2008) technique for transverse momentum diffusion at NLO and on Ghiglieri, Moore, and Teaney's (2013-2015) sum-rule tools for longitudinal diffusion and collinear emission. The results enable better comparison between perturbative QCD and experimental data, though the large NLO corrections signal severe convergence problems in the perturbative expansion.
