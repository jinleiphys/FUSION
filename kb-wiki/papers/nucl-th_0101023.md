# Centrality Dependence of Hadron Multiplicities in Nuclear Collisions in the Dual Parton Model

> We show that, even in purely soft processes, the hadronic multiplicity in nucleus-nucleus interactions contains a term that scales with the number of binary collisions. In the absence of shadowing corrections, this term dominates at mid rapidities and high energies. Shadowing corrections are calculated as a function of impact parameter and the centrality dependence of mid-rapidity multiplicities is determined. The multiplicity per participant increases with centrality with a rate that increases between SPS and RHIC energies, in agreement with experiment.

# Centrality Dependence of Hadron Multiplicities in Nuclear Collisions in the Dual Parton Model

## Key claim
Even in purely soft processes, the hadronic multiplicity in nucleus-nucleus collisions contains a term that scales with the number of binary collisions, not just with the number of participants; however, at finite energies, energy-momentum conservation and shadowing corrections prevent the full binary-collision scaling from being realized, and the centrality dependence of the multiplicity per participant increases with energy from SPS to RHIC.

## Method
The authors use the Dual Parton Model (DPM), based on the Glauber-Gribov approach and the large-N expansion of non-perturbative QCD, to compute charged hadron multiplicities per unit rapidity in nucleus-nucleus collisions. They calculate the average number of binary collisions n(b) and participants n_A(b) from nuclear profile functions, then sum contributions from valence quark-diquark strings and sea quark-antiquark strings, with momentum distributions given by Regge propagators and fragmentation functions fixed from e+e- and pp data. Shadowing corrections are incorporated via triple Pomeron diagrams using a universal formalism from Gribov theory, with parameters taken from diffractive production data.

## Key numbers
- At SPS energy (sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV), the non-diffractive pp cross-section sigma_ND = 26 mb, k = 1.4, and dN_pp^ND/dy = 1.56.
- At RHIC energy (sqrt(s) = 130 GeV), sigma_ND = 33 mb, k = 2.0, and dN_pp^ND/dy = 2.72.
- At RHIC energy (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV), sigma_ND = 34 mb, k = 2.2, and dN_pp^ND/dy = 3.04.
- The multiplicity per participant at SPS increases by a factor of 1.15 between impact parameter b = 10 fm and b = 0.
- The corresponding increase at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV and 200 GeV is 1.30 and 1.31, respectively.

## Context
This work extends the Dual Parton Model of Capella et al. (1994) and the Quark Gluon String Model to the centrality dependence of hadron multiplicities in nuclear collisions, showing that the AGK cancellation (Abramovski, Gribov, Kancheli, 1973) would give binary-collision scaling at asymptotic energies but is violated at finite energies. The results are compared with WA98 data at SPS and PHENIX data at RHIC, and with alternative approaches such as the minijet model of Wang and Gyulassy (2001) and high-density QCD saturation models. The calculation enables predictions for the energy dependence of the centrality dependence from SPS to RHIC and beyond, and provides a baseline for separating soft and hard contributions to particle production.
