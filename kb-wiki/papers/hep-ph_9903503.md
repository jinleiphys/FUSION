# Quark Description of Hadronic Phases

> We extend our proposal that major universality classes of hadronic matter can be understood, and in favorable cases calculated, directly in the microscopic quark variables, to allow for splitting between strange and light quark masses. A surprisingly simple but apparently viable picture emerges, featuring essentially three phases, distinguished by whether strangeness is conserved (standard nuclear matter), conserved modulo two (hypernuclear matter), or locked to color (color flavor locking). These are separated by sharp phase transitions. There is also, potentially, a quark phase matching hadronic K-condensation. The smallness of the secondary gap in two-flavor color superconductivity corresponds to the disparity between the primary dynamical energy scales of QCD and the much smaller energy scales of nuclear physics.

# Quark Description of Hadronic Phases

## Key claim

The authors extend their earlier proposal that major universality classes of hadronic matter can be understood directly in microscopic quark variables, now allowing for splitting between strange and light quark masses. They argue that a surprisingly simple picture emerges with essentially three phases separated by sharp phase transitions, but the analysis is based on weak-coupling assumptions and schematic models whose quantitative predictions should not be regarded as definitive.

## Method

The authors analyze high-density QCD by assuming weak coupling at large Fermi surfaces, then studying pairing instabilities via BCS-type gap equations. They derive conditions for pairing between light and strange quarks using a schematic gap equation with a zero-range interaction, diagonalize the 18x18 color-flavor matrix for the transition between color-flavor locking and two-flavor superconductivity, and minimize the free energy in a mean-field approximation with a short-range interaction modeled on one-gluon exchange, using a sharp cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- The critical strange quark mass for the transition from color-flavor locking to two-flavor superconductivity is approximately given by the condition m_s^2 < 4 p_F Δ(μ), where Δ is the octet gap
- For μ = 0.5 GeV and Δ_us^0 = 25 MeV, the critical strange quark mass is very close to the estimate m_s = 2 sqrt(μ Δ_us^0)
- The primary gap in two-flavor color superconductivity is plausibly of order 100 MeV at several times nuclear density
- The secondary gap in the tensor channel for the third-color quarks is estimated to be in the range of several up to 100 keV
- The model calculation uses a cutoff Λ = 600 MeV and coupling K = 32 Λ^{-2} to reproduce a μ=0 quark constituent mass of 400 MeV
- For small strange quark masses around m_s ~ 50 MeV, the onset for strangeness is significantly above the onset for light quarks

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Schaefer and Wilczek, 1998) that color-flavor locking provides a unified description of hadronic matter, now incorporating realistic strange quark mass effects. It builds on the identification of color-flavor locking as the correct ground state at asymptotically large densities (Son, 1998) and on earlier two-flavor color superconductivity analyses (Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek, 1998; Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky, 1998). The paper enables a systematic framework for relating microscopic quark-gluon degrees of freedom to macroscopic hadronic phenomena, and suggests that the small energy scales of nuclear physics (few MeV gaps) may arise naturally from the disparity between the primary QCD scale (~100 MeV) and much smaller secondary gaps, without requiring a conspiracy of parameters.
