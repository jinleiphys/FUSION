# Ripples of the QCD Critical Point

> We investigate the impact of a critical end point (CEP) on the experimentally accessible baryon number fluctuations of different orders. By now, its potential location has been constrained fairly accurately within first principles functional QCD, together with the location of the chiral crossover line and further thermodynamic observables. This information is incorporated in an advanced QCD-assisted low energy effective theory which is used for the computation of baryon number fluctuations at the chemical freeze-out. This computation also takes care of global baryon number conservation at larger density, where the system changes from grand-canonical to canonical statistics. We observe a prominent peak structure, whose amplitude depends on the location of the CEP, while its position is more sensitive to the location of the freeze-out curve. Our results provide guidance for future low energy heavy-ion experiments.

# Ripples of the QCD Critical Point

## Key claim
The authors predict a pronounced peak structure in baryon number fluctuation ratios at beam energies between 3 and 7.7 GeV, whose height is sensitive to the location of the QCD critical end point (CEP) but whose position depends primarily on the freeze-out curve. This peak arises from the sharpening of the chiral crossover rather than from critical scaling, meaning the CEP can be inferred without relying on universal scaling behavior.

## Method
The authors construct a QCD-assisted low-energy effective theory (LEFT) by matching functional renormalization group (fRG) flows of first-principles QCD to those of the LEFT, incorporating results from functional QCD that constrain the CEP location. They compute baryon number susceptibilities at chemical freeze-out using three different freeze-out curves, and apply canonical corrections via the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) to account for global baryon number conservation at low collision energies. The parameter alpha in SAM is fixed by matching to STAR data for the ratio R32 at three beam energies.

## Key numbers
- CEP location in the QCD-assisted LEFT: (T_CEP, mu_B_CEP) = (98, 643) MeV
- Constraint from full functional QCD: 600 MeV less than or equal to mu_B_CEP less than or equal to 650 MeV
- SAM parameter alpha = 0 at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV, alpha = 0.14 at 7.7 GeV, alpha = 0.24 at 3 GeV
- Freeze-out baryon chemical potentials at the peak in R42: approximately 536 MeV (Andronic et al. freeze-out), 541 MeV (STAR Fit I), 486 MeV (STAR Fit II)
- CEP variations studied: (108, 604) MeV and (94, 704) MeV
- Peak height increases significantly when mu_B_CEP minus mu_B_peak is less than approximately 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends the QCD-assisted LEFT approach introduced by Fu et al. (2021) and builds on first-principles functional QCD studies using fRG and Dyson-Schwinger equations by Fu et al. (2019), Gao et al. (2020), and Gunkel et al. (2021) that constrained the CEP location. The results are compared with STAR collaboration data from the Beam Energy Scan program, including the 3.1 sigma significance non-monotonic kurtosis observation by STAR (2020). The approach enables future quantitative determination of the CEP location from experimental data without relying on critical scaling, and can be extended to scenarios where the onset regime involves a spatially modulated phase or moat regime as discussed by Fukushima (2010), Pisarski (2021), and Rennecke (2023).
