# Effect of relaxation time on the squeezed correlations of bosons for evolution sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Squeezed back-to-back correlation (SBBC) of a boson-antiboson pair is sensitive to the time distribution of the particle-emitting source, and the SBBC function for an evolving source is expected to be affected by the relaxation time of the system. In this paper, we investigate the effect of relaxation time on the SBBC function. We put forth a method to calculate the SBBC function with relaxation-time modification for evolving sources. The SBBC functions of $D^0\!{\bar D}^0$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are investigated by using a hydrodynamic model. It is found that the relaxation time reduces the amplitudes of the SBBC functions. This change becomes serious for a long relaxation time and large initial relative deviations of the chaotic and squeezed amplitudes from their equilibrium values, respectively, in the temporal steps.

## Key claim

The relaxation time of an evolving particle-emitting source reduces the amplitude of the squeezed back-to-back correlation (SBBC) function for boson-antiboson pairs, with the reduction becoming more serious for longer relaxation times and larger initial relative deviations of the chaotic and squeezed amplitudes from their equilibrium values.

## Method

The authors derived a relaxation-time modification to the SBBC function by incorporating a factor \([1+\delta^0_{c,s} e^{-t'/\bar\tau}]\) into the standard expressions for the chaotic and squeezed amplitudes in a hydrodynamic source. They applied this formalism using the VISH2+1 hydrodynamic model to compute the SBBC functions of \(D^0\bar D^0\) pairs in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, dividing the time evolution into discrete steps with uniform local time distribution.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV with 0–80% centrality, the SBBC function \(C(\Delta\phi)\) was calculated in momentum intervals 0.55–0.65 GeV/\(c\) and 1.15–1.25 GeV/\(c\).
- The relaxation-time parameter values used were \(\bar\tau = 0.2\) fm/\(c\) and \(\bar\tau = 0.4\) fm/\(c\), with initial relative deviation parameters \(\delta^0_c = \delta^0_s = \delta^0 = 0.25\) and 0.5.
- The freeze-out temperature was taken as \(T_f = 150\) MeV, the free \(D^0\) meson mass as 1.865 GeV/\(c^2\), and the time step width as 1 fm/\(c\).
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density of the QGP was set to 0.08.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of SBBC for evolving sources by Zhang (2015, 2016) and Yang (2018) by incorporating a relaxation-time approximation to account for deviations from local equilibrium in hydrodynamic evolution. The method enables more realistic calculations of the SBBC observable, which is sensitive to the time distribution of the source and may become a technique for extracting in-medium particle interactions, though no experimental data exist yet for comparison. The authors suggest future studies using cascade models such as a multi-phase transport model or hybrid models like hydro+UrQMD.
