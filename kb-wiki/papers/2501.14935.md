# Comparing strange and non-strange quark stars within resummed QCD at NLO

> We employ the renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) resummation method to evaluate the equation of state (EoS) for strange ($N_f=2+1$) and non-strange ($N_f=2$) cold quark matter at NLO. This allows us to obtain the mass-radius relation for pure quark stars and compare the results with the predictions from perturbative QCD (pQCD) at NNLO. Choosing the renormalization scale to generate maximum star masses of order $M=2 - 2.6 M_\odot$, we show that the RGOPT can produce mass-radius curves compatible with the masses and radii of some recently observed pulsars, regardless of their strangeness content. The scale values required to produce the desired maximum masses are higher in the strange scenario since the EoS is softer in this case. The possible reasons for such behavior are discussed. Our results also show that, as expected, the RGOPT predictions for the relevant observables are less sensitive to scale variations than those furnished by pQCD.

## Key claim

The renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) at NLO can produce mass-radius curves for pure quark stars compatible with observed pulsar masses and radii for both strange (Nf=2+1) and non-strange (Nf=2) quark matter, provided the renormalization scale parameter X is chosen to generate maximum star masses in the range M = 2–2.6 solar masses, with higher X values required in the strange scenario due to its softer equation of state.

## Method

The authors applied the RGOPT resummation method to evaluate the equation of state (EoS) for cold quark matter at NLO, considering both strange (Nf=2+1, with finite strange quark mass) and non-strange (Nf=2, massless) cases. They solved for the optimized variational mass parameter using a renormalization scheme change to obtain real solutions, then imposed beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions to obtain the EoS for neutron-star matter. The resulting EoS was used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for pure quark stars, with the renormalization scale set as Λ = X μB/3 where X is a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Maximum star masses of order M = 2–2.6 solar masses are generated by choosing the renormalization scale parameter X appropriately
- The RGOPT predictions are less sensitive to scale variations than perturbative QCD (pQCD) predictions
- The strange quark mass at 2 GeV is taken as ms(2 GeV) = 0.0935(8) GeV, with Λ_MSbar ≈ 0.335 GeV for Nf=3
- The pQCD NNLO pressure for Nf=2+1 is given by a pocket formula with coefficients c1=0.9008, d1=0.5034, d2=1.452, ν1=0.3553, ν2=0.9101
- The variational mass parameter η scales roughly as η ∼ O(g μ), increasing linearly with baryon chemical potential μB

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RGOPT application to non-strange quark stars (Restrepo 2022) by including strangeness via a finite strange quark mass, and compares with pQCD results at NNLO from Kurkela (2009) and Fraga (2013). The approach is motivated by the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis for strange quark matter and the alternative possibility of two-flavor quark stars advocated by Holdom (2017). The results enable comparison of strange and non-strange quark star properties and demonstrate that RGOPT provides improved scale dependence over standard pQCD, potentially allowing more reliable predictions for compact star observables from future gravitational wave and pulsar observations.
