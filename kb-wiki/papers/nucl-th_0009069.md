# Neutron-Proton Differential Flow as a Probe of Isospin-Dependence of Nuclear Equation of State

> The neutron-proton differential flow is shown to be a very useful probe of the isospin-dependence of the nuclear equation of state (EOS). # Neutron-Proton Differential Flow as a Probe of Isospin-Dependence of Nuclear Equation of State

## Key claim

The neutron-proton differential flow is introduced as a novel observable that can probe the isospin-dependence of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) by constructively combining information from both isospin fractionation and nuclear collective flow, while effectively avoiding uncertainties from other dynamical ingredients of heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies. The approach is demonstrated using transport model calculations with two specific parameterizations of the symmetry energy corresponding to curvature parameters of +61 MeV and -69 MeV, which are only about 1/7 of the full range predicted by many-body theories.

## Method

The study uses the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, where protons and neutrons are initialized according to density distributions from relativistic mean-field theory, and the reaction dynamics includes isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon scatterings, Pauli blockings, symmetry potentials, and the Coulomb potential for protons. The neutron-proton differential flow is defined as the isospin-averaged transverse momentum per nucleon at a given rapidity, with neutrons weighted by +1 and protons by -1, and is calculated for collisions between various tin isotopes at beam energies from 30 to 400 MeV/nucleon and impact parameters up to 5 fm.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density $S(\rho_0)$ is in the range of 27-36 MeV
- Two parameterizations of the symmetry energy curvature $K_{sym}$ are used: +61 MeV and -69 MeV, which lie within the full theoretical range of approximately -400 MeV to +466 MeV
- The constant $e_a$ in the symmetry potential is approximately 19 MeV
- A 40% variation of the isoscalar curvature $K_0$ parameter or a change by a factor of 2 of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections results in less than 7% change in the values of $F_{n-p}(y)$
- The degree of isospin fractionation increases with both the $(N/Z)_{sys}$ of the reaction system and the impact parameter, but decreases with beam energy
- The neutron-proton differential flow signal is strongest close to the lower energy end of the range studied (30-400 MeV/nucleon)

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of isospin fractionation (Muller, Li and Ko, Baran, Pawel, Cat) and collective flow (Danielewicz 1985, Gregoire 1986, Bertsch 1988, Gary, Pan, Reisdorf 1997, Li and Ko 1995) to introduce a combined observable that minimizes uncertainties from the isoscalar potential and in-medium cross sections. The approach enables experimental determination of the isospin-dependence of the nuclear EOS using rare isotope beams at facilities like the planned Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA), and is relevant to understanding Type II supernova explosions, neutron-star mergers, and the cooling rate of protoneutron stars.
