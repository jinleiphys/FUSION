# Extraction of the Sivers function from SIDIS, Drell-Yan, and $W^\pm/Z$ boson production data with TMD evolution

> We perform a global fit of the available polarized Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering (SIDIS), polarized pion-induced Drell-Yan (DY) and $W^\pm/Z$ boson production data at N$^3$LO and NNLO accuracy of the Transverse Momentum Dependent (TMD) evolution, and extract the Sivers function for $u$, $d$, $s$ and for sea quarks. The Qiu-Sterman function is determined in a model independent way via the operator product expansion from the extracted Sivers function. The analysis is supplemented by additional studies, such as the estimation of applicability region, the impact of the unpolarized distributions' uncertainties, the universality of the Sivers functions, positivity constraints, the significance of the sign-change relation, and the comparison with the existing extractions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Sivers function is universal across SIDIS, Drell-Yan, and W±/Z boson production processes when using TMD evolution at N3LO and NNLO accuracy, resolving a previous puzzle where analyses including TMD evolution had failed to simultaneously describe both SIDIS and DY data as well as parton-model-based extractions had.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of polarized SIDIS data from HERMES and COMPASS, polarized pion-induced Drell-Yan data from COMPASS, and W±/Z boson production data from STAR, using the ζ-prescription for TMD evolution at N3LO and NNLO accuracy. They used the SV19 extraction as input for unpolarized TMD distributions and the nonperturbative Collins-Soper kernel, and parametrized the Sivers function directly in b-space without assuming any relation to collinear PDFs. The Qiu-Sterman function was then determined in a model-independent way via operator product expansion from the extracted Sivers function.

## Key numbers

- The difference between NNLO and N3LO perturbative orders in the asymmetry is approximately 3% at typical HERMES kinematics (x=0.12, z=0.32, PhT=0.14 GeV)
- The difference between NLO and NNLO orders is approximately -7%, and between LO and NLO is approximately 50% at the same kinematics
- The analysis used data from HERMES, COMPASS SIDIS, COMPASS DY, and STAR W±/Z production, with a conservative data cut to ensure applicability of TMD factorization at small qT/Q

## Context

This work extends the SV19 extraction of unpolarized TMD distributions (Scimemi and Vladimirov, 2019) to the polarized case, and addresses a puzzle where previous TMD-evolution analyses (Echevarria et al., 2014; Echevarria et al., 2020) had not achieved better agreement with DY data than parton-model-based extractions (Anselmino et al., 2016; Cammarota et al., 2020). The successful simultaneous description of SIDIS, DY, and W±/Z data with TMD evolution enables future high-statistics measurements at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV Upgrade and the Electron-Ion Collider to further test the universality of the Sivers function and the sign-change relation between SIDIS and DY.
