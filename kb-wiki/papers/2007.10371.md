# Modeling the diffusive dynamics of critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point

> The experimental search for the QCD critical point by means of relativistic heavy-ion collisions necessitates the development of dynamical models of fluctuations. In this work we study the fluctuations of the net-baryon density near the critical point. Due to net-baryon number conservation the correct dynamics is given by the fluid dynamical diffusion equation, which we extend by a white noise stochastic term to include intrinsic fluctuations. We quantify finite resolution and finite size effects by comparing our numerical results to analytic expectations for the structure factor and the equal-time correlation function. In small systems the net-baryon number conservation turns out to be quantitatively and qualitatively important, as it introduces anticorrelations at larger distances. Including nonlinear coupling terms in the form of a Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional we observe non-Gaussian fluctuations quantified by the excess kurtosis. We study the dynamical properties of the system close to equilibrium, for a sudden quench in temperature and a Hubble-like temperature evolution. In the real-time dynamical systems we find the important dynamical effects of critical slowing down, weakening of the extremal value and retardation of the fluctuation signal. In this work we establish a set of general tests, which should be met by any model propagating fluctuations, including upcoming $3+1$ dimensional fluctuating fluid dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in small systems, exact net-baryon number conservation introduces anticorrelations at larger distances that significantly reduce the correlation length and fluctuation signals compared to infinite-system expectations, and that nonlinear Ginzburg-Landau couplings further suppress long-range correlations beyond what Gaussian models predict.

## Method

The authors solve a stochastic diffusion equation for net-baryon density fluctuations in one spatial dimension, extending the deterministic diffusion equation with a white noise stochastic term to satisfy the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. They implement a semi-implicit numerical scheme with periodic boundary conditions, studying both linear Gaussian models (with and without a surface tension term) and a nonlinear Ginzburg-Landau model with coupling coefficients mapped from the 3D Ising universality class. The system is initialized at constant density and allowed to equilibrate, then evaluated for static structure factors, equal-time correlation functions, local variance, and excess kurtosis at various temperatures and system sizes.

## Key numbers

- The thermodynamic correlation length ξ peaks at Tc = 0.15 GeV with a maximum value of about 3 fm near Tc
- The critical net-baryon density is set to nc = 1/(3 fm³)
- For the Gauss+surface model at T = 0.5 GeV, a system size of L = 5 fm is sufficient for the fitted correlation length to reach approximately the thermodynamic value ξ = ξ0 = 0.479 fm
- At T = Tc with L = 40 fm, the fitted correlation length in the Gauss+surface model approaches ξ ≈ 3 fm, while in the Ginzburg-Landau model it remains significantly smaller
- The local variance σ² in the Gauss+surface model at Tc is reduced in finite systems compared to the infinite-system continuum expectation of nc²/(2A√(K)m)
- The excess kurtosis κ is zero for Gaussian models and becomes nonzero in the Ginzburg-Landau model, with largest effects near Tc

## Context

This work extends previous studies of critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions by Nahrgang et al. (2017) and Bluhm et al. (2018, 2019) by providing a systematic benchmark framework for testing numerical implementations of fluctuating fluid dynamics. The authors establish general tests (static structure factor, equal-time correlation function, dynamic structure factor) that should be met by any model propagating fluctuations, including upcoming 3+1 dimensional fluctuating fluid dynamics simulations. The paper enables quantitative assessment of finite-size and finite-resolution effects in dynamical models of the QCD critical point search at RHIC and other facilities.
