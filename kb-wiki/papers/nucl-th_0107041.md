# Parity Violation in Neutron Capture Reactions

> In the last decade, the scattering of polarized neutrons on compound nucleus resonances proved to be a powerful experimental technique for probing nuclear parity violation. Longitudinal analyzing powers in neutron transmission measurements on p-wave resonances in nuclei such as $^{139}$La and $^{232}$Th were found to be as large as 10%. Here we examine the possibilities of carrying out a parallel program to measure asymmetries in the $(n,γ$) reaction on these same compound nuclear resonances. Symmetry-violating $(n,γ$) studies can also show asymmetries as large as 10%, and have the advantage over transmission experiments of allowing parity-odd asymmetries in several different gamma-decay branches from the same resonance. Thus, studies of parity violation in the $(n,γ)$ reaction using high efficiency germanium detectors at the Los Alamos Lujan facility, for example, could determine the parity-odd nucleon-nucleon matrix elements in complex nuclei with high accuracy. Additionally, simultaneous studies of the E1 and $V_{PNC}$ matrix elements invol ved in these decays could be used to help constrain the statistical theory of parity non-conservation in compound nuclei.

## Key claim

Parity-violating asymmetries in the $(n,\gamma)$ reaction on compound nuclear resonances can be as large as 10%, matching the size of asymmetries seen in neutron transmission experiments, and offer the additional advantage of allowing measurements on several individual gamma-decay branches from the same resonance, potentially yielding more precise determinations of the parity-nonconserving (PNC) matrix elements than transmission alone.

## Method

The authors examine the theoretical expressions for the longitudinal asymmetry $A_L^{\gamma_i}$ in neutron capture, derived from the work of Flambaum and Sushkov, and show that under common conditions it reduces to the same form as the transmission asymmetry $A_L^n$. They analyze existing gamma-ray spectroscopy data from $p$-wave resonances in $^{107}$Ag, measured at Geel, to demonstrate that the ratio of E1 gamma-ray intensities from $p$-wave resonances to M1 intensities from neighboring $s$-wave resonances can be large enough to make the approximation $A_L^\gamma \approx A_L^n$ valid. They then discuss experimental requirements for high-precision measurements using germanium detectors at spallation neutron sources such as LANSCE or nTOF at CERN.

## Key numbers

* Longitudinal analyzing powers in neutron transmission on $p$-wave resonances in $^{139}$La and $^{232}$Th were found to be as large as 10%.
* An asymmetry $A_L^\gamma$ of $9.5 \pm 0.3\%$ was measured in total neutron capture on $^{139}$La.
* A PNC mean-squared matrix element $M = 2.9^{+1.3}_{-0.9}$ meV was obtained from $J=1$ levels in $^{114}$Cd from neutron capture measurements.
* For $^{107}$Ag, the absolute intensity of a 6590.5 keV gamma-ray from the 125.1 eV $p$-wave resonance was $2.2 \pm 0.4\%$ per 100 neutron captures, compared to $0.022 \pm 0.005\%$ from the neighboring 144.2 eV $s$-wave resonance, giving a ratio of about 100.
* For $^{107}$Ag, the absolute intensity of a 7190.0 keV gamma-ray from the 259.9 eV $p$-wave resonance was $0.93 \pm 0.05\%$, compared to $0.015 \pm 0.003\%$ from the 251.3 eV $s$-wave resonance.
* The energy separation between opposite parity $s$-wave and $p$-wave resonances in the studied compound systems ranges from 0.1 to 100 eV.
* Calculated values of $M$ from Tomsovic et al. using DDH estimates are about a factor of 3 smaller than the experimental value for $^{238}$U and about a factor of 1.7 smaller for the Pd isotopes.
* E1 transitions are on average 7 times stronger than M1 transitions in the high-energy gamma-ray region ($E \approx 5-7$ MeV).

## Context

This paper extends the program of parity violation studies in compound nuclei from neutron transmission experiments, carried out by the TRIPLE collaboration on nuclei such as $^{238}$U, $^{232}$Th, $^{133}$Cs, and others, to the $(n,\gamma)$ reaction. The authors build on the statistical shell model developed by French et al. and extended by Tomsovic et al., and on prior $(n,\gamma)$ measurements by Adachi et al. on $^{139}$La and Seestrom et al. on $^{111}$Cd and $^{113}$Cd. The proposed measurements could help resolve two open theoretical issues: the unexplained common positive sign of asymmetries observed in $^{232}$Th by Bowman et al., and the treatment of the effective one-body PNC interaction in heavy nuclei, where the model space used by Tomsovic et al. for $^{238}$U predicts vanishing E1 and one-body $V_{PNC}$ matrix elements.
