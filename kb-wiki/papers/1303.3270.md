# Magnetar giant flare oscillations and the nuclear symmetry energy

> If the observed quasi-periodic oscillations in magnetar flares are partially confined to the crust, then the oscillation frequencies are unique probes of the nuclear physics of the neutron star crust. We study crustal oscillations in magnetars including corrections for a finite Alfven velocity. Our crust model uses a new nuclear mass formula that predicts nuclear masses with an accuracy very close to that of the finite range droplet model. This mass model for equilibrium nuclei also includes shell corrections and an updated neutron-drip line. We perturb our crust model to predict axial crust modes and assign them to observed giant flare quasi-periodic oscillation frequencies from the soft gamma-ray repeater SGR 1806-20. We find magnetar crusts that match observations for various magnetic field strengths, entrainment of the free neutron gas in the inner crust, and crust-core transition densities. We find that observations can be reconciled with smaller values of the symmetry energy slope parameter, L, if there is a significant amount of entrainment of the neutrons by the superfluid or if the crust-core transition density is large. We also find neutron star masses and radii which are in agreement with expectations from what is known about low-density matter from nuclear experiment. Matching observations with a field-free model we obtain the approximate values of M =1.35 Msun and R = 11.9 km. Matching observations using a model with the surface dipole field of SGR 1806-20 (B=2.4x10^15 G) we obtain the approximate values of M = 1.25 Msun and R = 12.4 km.

# Magnetar giant flare oscillations and the nuclear symmetry energy

## Key claim

If the observed quasi-periodic oscillations in magnetar flares are partially confined to the crust, then the oscillation frequencies can constrain the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter L, but only under specific assumptions about entrainment of the free neutron gas and crust-core transition density. Observations can be reconciled with smaller values of L if there is significant entrainment of neutrons by the superfluid or if the crust-core transition density is large.

## Method

The authors constructed magnetized neutron star crust models using a liquid droplet model with a new nuclear mass formula that includes shell corrections and an updated neutron-drip line, predicting nuclear masses to within 1.2 MeV. They solved the axial perturbation equation for torsional crust modes in the relativistic Cowling approximation, including corrections for a finite Alfven velocity, and assigned predicted fundamental (n=0, l=2) and first harmonic (n=1) modes to observed quasi-periodic oscillation frequencies from the soft gamma-ray repeater SGR 1806-20 (29 Hz and 626 Hz). The core equation of state was taken from a probability distribution determined by Steiner (2012) from observations of photosphere radius expansion bursts and quiescent emission of low-mass X-ray binaries, using Skyrme interactions SLy4 (L=46 MeV) and Rs (L=86 MeV) for the crust.

## Key numbers

- The SLy4 crust EOS has L = 46 MeV at nuclear saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3; the Rs crust EOS has L = 86 MeV at n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- For the field-free model matching SGR 1806-20 observations: M = 1.35 Msun and R = 11.9 km (with SLy4 crust EOS, full entrainment fent = 1.0, crust-core transition density nt = 0.12 fm^-3)
- For the model with surface dipole field of SGR 1806-20 (B = 2.4 x 10^15 G): M = 1.25 Msun and R = 12.4 km (SLy4 crust EOS, nt = 0.12 fm^-3)
- For the lower crust-core transition density nt = 0.08 fm^-3 with B = 2.4 x 10^15 G: M = 0.96 Msun and R = 13.5 km (SLy4 crust EOS)
- For the Rs crust EOS with B = 2.4 x 10^15 G and nt = 0.12 fm^-3: M = 1.10 Msun and R = 13.8 km
- For the Rs crust EOS with entrainment fraction fent = 0.25 and B = 0 G: M = 1.12 Msun and R = 12.0 km
- The crust model predicts nuclear masses to within 1.2 MeV of experimental values
- The Rs EOS gives fundamental frequencies between 15-20 Hz for the n=0, l=2 mode (assigned to the observed 18 Hz QPO)
- For the SLy4 crust EOS, entrainment fraction fent >= 0.75 is required for consistency with observed QPOs
- For the Rs crust EOS, entrainment fraction fent <= 0.30 is required for consistency with observed QPOs
- Magnetic fields B >= 4.0 x 10^15 G (for nt = 0.12 fm^-3) or B >= 2.0 x 10^15 G (for nt = 0.08 fm^-3) give no mass-radius solutions consistent at the 2-sigma level with constraints from PREs and LMXBs
- The field-free case gives a minimum radius R >= 11.9 km for SGR 1806-20

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Steiner (2009) and Sotani (2013) by using a modern equation of state with a more consistent treatment of nuclear shell effects to predict torsional mode frequencies in magnetars. The authors match observations for various magnetic field strengths, entrainment fractions, and crust-core transition densities, finding results consistent with the mass-radius constraints from Steiner (2012). The study finds smaller fundamental mode frequencies for crust equations of state with larger values of L, in agreement with Sotani (2013), but notes that recent neutron star radius measurements from Guillot (2013) suggesting radii less than 11.1 km would require either sufficiently large L or entrainment fraction to match torsional modes to observed QPOs. The work enables future studies of how entrainment in the crust might be correlated with L, and highlights the need for a complete evaluation of this correlation.
