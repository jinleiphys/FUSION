# Sterile Neutrino Hot, Warm, and Cold Dark Matter

> We calculate the incoherent resonant and non-resonant scattering production of sterile neutrinos in the early universe. We find ranges of sterile neutrino masses, vacuum mixing angles, and initial lepton numbers which allow these species to constitute viable hot, warm, and cold dark matter (HDM, WDM, CDM) candidates which meet observational constraints. The constraints considered here include energy loss in core collapse supernovae, energy density limits at big bang nucleosynthesis, and those stemming from sterile neutrino decay: limits from observed cosmic microwave background anisotropies, diffuse extragalactic background radiation, and Li-6/D overproduction. Our calculations explicitly include matter effects, both effective mixing angle suppression and enhancement (MSW resonance), as well as quantum damping. We for the first time properly include all finite temperature effects, dilution resulting from the annihilation or disappearance of relativistic degrees of freedom, and the scattering-rate-enhancing effects of particle-antiparticle pairs (muons, tauons, quarks) at high temperature in the early universe.

## Key claim

The paper identifies ranges of sterile neutrino mass, vacuum mixing angle, and initial lepton number that allow sterile neutrinos to constitute viable hot, warm, or cold dark matter candidates, but only under the condition that all observational constraints from supernovae, big bang nucleosynthesis, cosmic microwave background anisotropies, diffuse extragalactic background radiation, and lithium-6/deuterium overproduction are simultaneously satisfied.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation for sterile neutrino production in the early universe, including both resonant (MSW-enhanced) and non-resonant scattering production channels. They incorporated finite-temperature effects, the dilution from annihilation of relativistic degrees of freedom, and enhanced scattering rates from particle-antiparticle pairs (muons, tauons, quarks) at high temperature. The calculations explicitly included matter effects such as effective mixing angle suppression and enhancement, as well as quantum damping, and considered both non-resonant conversion (for small lepton asymmetry) and resonant conversion (for larger lepton asymmetries).

## Key numbers

* For the atmospheric neutrino anomaly, the best fit is maximal $\nu_\mu \rightleftharpoons \nu_\tau$ mixing with vacuum mass-squared difference $\delta m^2 \sim 3\times 10^{-3} \, \mathrm{eV}^2$.
* The best current limits on the mixing matrix element for sterile neutrinos from pion decay are $\vert U_{ex}\vert^2 < 10^{-7}$ for sterile neutrino masses $50 \, \mathrm{MeV} < m_s < 130 \, \mathrm{MeV}$ (Britton et al.).
* The observational bounds on primordial lepton asymmetries are $-4.1\times 10^{-2} \le L_{\nu_e} \le 0.79$ and $\vert L_{\nu_\mu,\nu_\tau}\vert \le 6.0$.
* The baryon-to-photon ratio is $\eta = n_b/n_\gamma \approx 2.79\times 10^{-8} \Omega_b h^2 \sim 10^{-10}$.
* The total weak potential experienced by an active neutrino is approximately $V(p,T) \approx (40.2 \, \mathrm{eV}) ({\cal L}/10^{-2}) (T/\mathrm{GeV})^3 - B p (T/\mathrm{GeV})^4$, with coefficient $B \approx 10.79 \, \mathrm{eV}$ for $\nu_e$ and $B \approx 3.02 \, \mathrm{eV}$ for $\nu_\mu,\nu_\tau$ at temperatures below $20 \, \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analytical estimates of Dodelson and Widrow (1994) for non-resonant sterile neutrino production and the resonant production mechanism proposed by Shi and Fuller (1999) that yields cool dark matter with a non-thermal spectrum skewed toward low energies. It also builds on constraints from core-collapse supernovae considered by Dolgov et al. and from big bang nucleosynthesis and cosmic microwave background limits. The paper enables future comparisons with Ly$\alpha$ forest power spectrum measurements and Sloan Digital Sky Survey data to further constrain warm dark matter scenarios.
