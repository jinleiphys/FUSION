# K-Mesic Nuclei Versus eta-Mesic Nuclei

## Key claim

The observed large differences in binding energies between K-mesic and eta-mesic nuclei are understood in terms of the separation of the involved energies and the resonance positions, but the experimental findings of broad K-mesic and narrow eta-mesic states are more difficult to explain. A phenomenological model for eta-N interactions, specifically the Helsinki K-matrix model, can explain the suppression of eta absorption in light nuclei when the subthreshold energy dependence of the eta-N scattering amplitude is taken into account.

## Method

The author uses the Helsinki K-matrix model incorporating eta-N, pi-N, and gamma-N channels, parameterized by two resonance pole terms (N*(1535) and N(1650)) plus a background matrix, to calculate the eta-N scattering amplitude as a function of center-of-mass energy. This amplitude is then evaluated at subthreshold energies appropriate for few-nucleon systems, where the center-of-mass energy argument includes nucleon binding energy, meson binding energy, and recoil energy of the meson-nucleon pair. The resulting effective eta-N scattering matrices are used in a multiple scattering series summed via an optical potential to calculate eta-He scattering lengths and eta binding in light nuclei.

## Key numbers

- eta-N scattering length from the Helsinki K-matrix model: a = 0.91(6) + i 0.27(2) fm, with effective range r0 = -1.33(15) - i 0.30(2) fm (all in fermi units)
- For eta in 4He: average subthreshold energy E_cm ≈ -37 MeV, giving T_eta,N(E_cm) = 0.45 + i 0.051 fm; calculated A(eta,4He) = -2.90 + i 0.35 fm, corresponding to a quasi-bound state with energy E ≈ -6 MeV and width Γ ≈ 3.0 MeV
- Experimental A(eta,4He) from GEM: ±3.1(5) + i 0.0(5) fm (Budzanowski 2009)
- For eta in 3He: average subthreshold energy E_cm ≈ -19 MeV, giving T_eta,N(E_cm) = 0.54 + i 0.077 fm
- Experimental A(eta,3He) from COSY-ANKE: ±10.7(±0.8 (+0.1)(-0.5)) + i 1.5(±2.6 (+1.0)(-0.9)) (Mersmann 2007)
- Experimental A(eta,3He) from older data: 4.24(29) + i 0.72(81) fm (Green and Wycech 2003)
- Photo-production result for eta-3He: E = -4.4(4.2) - i 12.8(3.1) MeV (Pfeiffer 2004)
- For eta in 25Mg: calculated binding about 18 MeV, width Γ ≈ 6.0 MeV, with T_eta,N(E_cm) ≈ 0.52 + i 0.07 fm
- Experimental result for eta-25Mg from COSY-GEM: E_B ≈ -13 - i 5 MeV (Budzanowski 2009)

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of eta-nuclear quasi-bound states by Haider and Liu (1986) and addresses the discrepancy between theoretical predictions of large widths from chiral models (Hirenzaki 2009, Garcia-Recio 2002) and recent experimental findings that indicate weaker absorption. The paper uses the Helsinki K-matrix model (Green and Wycech 2005) to explain the suppression of eta absorption in light nuclei, and compares the eta case with K-mesic nuclei where explicit treatment of decay channels (Shevchenko 2007, Wycech 2009) is necessary. The results enable improved calculations of eta-mesic states in few-nucleon systems and suggest that similar explicit decay channel treatments should be applied to the eta case.
