# Eigenvector continuation for emulating and extrapolating two-body resonances

> The study of open quantum systems (OQSs), i.e., systems interacting with an environment, impacts our understanding of exotic nuclei in low-energy nuclear physics, hadrons, cold-atom systems, or even noisy intermediate-scale quantum computers. Such systems often exhibit resonance states characterized by energy positions and dispersions (or decay widths), the properties of which can be difficult to predict theoretically due to their coupling to the continuum of scattering states. Dealing with this phenomenon poses challenges both conceptually and numerically. For that reason, we investigate how the reduced basis method known as eigenvector continuation (EC), which has emerged as a powerful tool to emulate bound and scattering states in closed quantum systems, can be used to study resonance properties. In particular, we present a generalization of EC that we call conjugate-augmented eigenvector continuation, which is based on the complex-scaling method and designed to predict Gamow-Siegert states, and thus resonant properties of OQSs, using only bound-state wave functions as input.

## Key claim

The authors present a generalization of eigenvector continuation (EC) called conjugate-augmented eigenvector continuation (CA-EC), which can predict Gamow-Siegert resonance states using only bound-state wave functions as input, provided the Hamiltonian is complex-scaled and the c-product is used for matrix elements.

## Method

The authors apply the uniform complex-scaling method (CSM) to transform the radial Schrodinger equation by rotating the coordinate contour by an angle phi, which makes resonance wave functions square-integrable. They then construct EC training vectors from bound-state wave functions at several values of a Hamiltonian parameter, compute Hamiltonian and norm matrix elements using the c-product (no complex conjugation), and solve the resulting generalized eigenvalue problem to extrapolate to resonance energies at target parameter values. For bound-state-to-resonance extrapolation, they augment the training set with complex-conjugated bound-state wave functions, which places them closer to the resonance regime in the complex-scaled momentum plane.

## Key numbers

* For S-wave resonance-to-resonance extrapolation using 4 training points, the real part of the energy at the target parameter c=0.65 is extrapolated to approximately 0.10 (dimensionless units) with 68.2% percentile intervals of about +/-0.01, while the imaginary part is extrapolated to approximately -0.04 with similar uncertainty.
* For P-wave resonance-to-resonance extrapolation with 4 training points at target c=0.65, the real part of the energy is extrapolated to approximately 0.10 and the imaginary part to approximately -0.02 (dimensionless units).
* For bound-state-to-resonance extrapolation of an S-wave system using 4 training points with conjugate augmentation, the real part of the energy at target c=0.65 is extrapolated to approximately 0.10 and the imaginary part to approximately -0.04 (dimensionless units).
* All calculations use a momentum cutoff of Lambda=8.0 (dimensionless units) with N=256 Gauss-Legendre mesh points and a complex-scaling angle phi=pi/6.

## Context

This work extends eigenvector continuation, originally introduced by Frame et al. (2017) for bound states and later applied to scattering by Furnstahl et al. (2020) and Drischler et al. (2021), to the domain of resonance states in open quantum systems. The method enables emulation of two-body resonances without solving the full scattering problem at each parameter value, and the conjugate-augmented variant allows extrapolation from bound-state calculations alone, which could enable future applications to many-body resonances where direct complex-scaling calculations become computationally prohibitive.
