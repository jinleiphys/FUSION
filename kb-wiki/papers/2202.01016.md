# Nuclear matter in 1+1 dimensions

> We review the solution of QCD in two spacetime dimensions. Following the analysis of Baluni, for a single flavor the model can be analyzed using Abelian bosonization. The theory can be analyzed in strong coupling, when the quarks are much lighter than the gauge coupling. In this limit, the theory is given by a Luttinger liquid.

## Key claim

For cold, dense QCD in 1+1 dimensions with a single flavor, the low-energy theory reduces to a single massless boson (a Luttinger liquid) when the chemical potential exceeds the soliton mass, but only after projecting out all massive mesonic degrees of freedom and assuming the mass scale is much smaller than the gauge coupling.

## Method

The authors review the Abelian bosonization approach following Baluni (1980), starting from the QCD Lagrangian in 1+1 dimensions with a single flavor and SU(N_c) color group. They work in a hybrid gauge where the gauge potential is off-diagonal and the electric field is diagonal, then bosonize the quark currents to obtain a Hamiltonian of coupled sine-Gordon models. An alternative approach integrates out the gauge field in axial gauge, yielding a current-current interaction that is then bosonized; projecting onto the ground state where all color fields are equal produces a single sine-Gordon model for the U(1) field Φ, and a nonzero chemical potential is incorporated by shifting this field.

## Key numbers

- The gauge coupling g has dimensions of mass in two spacetime dimensions.
- The meson/glueball mass scale is ∼ g.
- The chemical potential shift is k_0 = μ / N_c.
- At the edge of the Fermi surface (k_F → 0), the Luttinger parameter K̃ → 1 and the Fermi velocity v_F → 0.
- For asymptotically high density (k_F → ∞), the Luttinger parameter K̃ → 1/N_c and the Fermi velocity v_F → 1.

## Context

This pedagogical review extends the analysis of Baluni (1980) and builds on prior work by Steinhardt (1980), Cohen (1982), Gepner (1984), Frishman (1992), Abdalla (1995), Armoni (1997, 1998), and Dempsey (2021) on bosonized QCD in 1+1 dimensions. It contrasts with the large-N_c solution of 't Hooft (1974) and the extension to nonzero quark density by Bringoltz (2009), who found chiral density waves for massive quarks. The results enable speculation about whether the quarkyonic phase in 3+1 dimensions, as studied by McLerran (2007) and others, might exhibit Luttinger liquid behavior and non-Fermi liquid transport properties.
