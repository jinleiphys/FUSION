# Prolate-oblate shape transitions and O(6) symmetry in even-even nuclei: A theoretical overview

> Prolate to oblate shape transitions have been predicted in an analytic way in the framework of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), determining O(6) as the symmetry at the critical point. Parameter-independent predictions for prolate to oblate transitions in various regions on the nuclear chart have been made in the framework of the proxy-SU(3) and pseudo-SU(3) symmetries, corroborated by recent non-relativistic and relativistic mean field calculations along series of nuclear isotopes, with parameters fixed throughout, as well as by shell model calculations taking advantage of the quasi-SU(3) symmetry. Experimental evidence for regions of prolate to oblate shape transitions is in agreement with regions in which nuclei bearing the O(6) dynamical symmetry of the IBM have been identified, lying below major shell closures. In addition, gradual oblate to prolate transitions are seen when crossing major nuclear shell closures, in analogy to experimental observations in alkali clusters.

## Key claim

This review argues that prolate-to-oblate shape transitions in even-even nuclei have O(6) symmetry at the critical point within the Interacting Boson Model framework, but notes that the Bohr collective model places the critical point at a nearly triaxial Z(5) solution with γ=30°, with the apparent contradiction resolved by the proven equivalence between γ-instability and triaxiality for finite boson systems.

## Method

The paper surveys theoretical predictions for prolate-oblate shape transitions across multiple frameworks: algebraic models using bosons (IBM with its U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetries), special solutions of the Bohr collective model (including the Z(5) and Z(4) critical point symmetries), non-relativistic and relativistic mean field calculations (Skyrme, Gogny, and covariant density functional theory), and shell model approaches (including proxy-SU(3), pseudo-SU(3), and quasi-SU(3) symmetries). The review compares predictions from these approaches across series of isotopes and maps them to regions where O(6) dynamical symmetry has been identified experimentally.

## Key numbers

- The proxy-SU(3) symmetry predicts a prolate to oblate transition around N=114 in the rare earth region between Z=50-82 and N=82-126
- The proxy-SU(3) symmetry predicts a prolate to oblate transition around N=72 in the region Z=50-82 and N=50-82
- The ratio R_{3/2} = E(2_3^+)/E(2_2^+) is suggested as an order parameter, acquiring values close to unity on the prolate side and becoming much larger on the oblate side
- In the Z(5) critical point symmetry, the γ variable is centered around γ=30°, midway between prolate γ=0 and oblate γ=60°
- Shell model studies in the Sn-Sm region within the N=50-82 shell found the prolate to oblate shape phase transition occurs at N=76

## Context

This review extends the line of work on shape/phase transitions in nuclei that began with the IBM phase transition analysis of Feng (1981) and the introduction of critical point symmetries by Iachello (2000, 2001). It connects the O(6) dynamical symmetry of IBM, first identified by Arima (1979), to prolate-oblate transitions predicted by proxy-SU(3) symmetry (Bonatsos, 2017) and pseudo-SU(3) symmetry (Arima, 1969; Hecht, 1969). The review enables systematic comparison of predictions across algebraic, collective, mean-field, and shell-model approaches, and clarifies the relationship between γ-instability and triaxiality at the critical point.
