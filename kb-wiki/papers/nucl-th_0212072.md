# Spatial structure of a vortex in low density neutron matter

> We study in a fully selfconsistent approach the structure of a vortex in low density superfluid neutron matter. We determine that the matter density profile of a vortex shows a significant depletion in the region of the core, a feature never reported for a vortex state in a Fermi superfluid.

## Key claim

The matter density profile of a vortex in low-density superfluid neutron matter shows a significant depletion in the core region, a feature never reported for a vortex state in a Fermi superfluid; this depletion is likely significant because the pairing field is relatively stronger than in the case of electrons.

## Method

The authors used a nuclear energy density functional (NEDF) approach, with the normal part fitted to reproduce microscopic many-body calculations of homogeneous neutron matter and supplemented with a phenomenological gradient correction. The pairing correlations were described using a parametrization of the $^1S_0$ nn-phase shift from the Nijmegen potential, with induced interaction effects included as an additional factor of approximately 0.45. The normal and anomalous densities were evaluated using the technique described in Ref. stb, with no need to enclose the vortex in a cylinder or introduce periodic boundary conditions along the vortex axis.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap in neutron matter as a function of Fermi momentum has a maximum of approximately 1 MeV for a Fermi momentum of $k_F \approx 0.8$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The pairing gap vanishes for Fermi momenta larger than approximately 1.5 fm$^{-1}$.
- Neutron matter becomes dilute in the sense $\rho |a|^3 < 1$ only at densities smaller than approximately $6 \times 10^{-6}$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The asymptotic density changes roughly by a factor of forty from the lowest to the highest values of the chemical potential plotted, and the Fermi momentum changes by approximately a factor of three.

## Context

This work extends the only previous microscopic calculation of a vortex in low density neutron matter by De Blasio (1994) and contradicts the typical Ginzburg-Landau approach, which is expected to give mostly a qualitative picture. The finding of significant density depletion in the vortex core will affect estimates of the pinning energy of vortices on nuclei in neutron star crusts, which were previously calculated by Anderson and Itoh (1975) and others using crude estimates that neglected this effect.
