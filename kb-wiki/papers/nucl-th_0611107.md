# QMC calculations of symmetric nuclear matter

> We present an accurate numerical study of the equation of state of nuclear matter based on realistic nucleon--nucleon interactions by means of Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations. The AFDMC method samples the spin and isospin degrees of freedom allowing for quantum simulations of large nucleonic systems and represents an important step forward towards a quantitative understanding of problems in nuclear structure and astrophysics.

## Key claim

The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method can efficiently simulate symmetric nuclear matter with realistic tensor interactions, producing an equation of state that differs significantly from Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC/SOC) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) results, particularly at high densities; however, the calculations omit three-body forces and spin-orbit terms, and the fixed-phase approximation used does not guarantee an upper bound to the mixed energy.

## Method

The authors performed Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations of symmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne AV6' two-body potential (a truncation of AV8' dropping spin-orbit terms and keeping the first six operators). The method uses the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to linearize quadratic spin-isospin operators, replacing the sum over spin-isospin states with a sampling of rotations of four-component spinors, reducing the computational scaling from exponential to cubic in the number of nucleons. Calculations were performed with 28 nucleons in a periodic box, with finite-size effects checked using 76 and 108 nucleons at two densities.

## Key numbers

- Energy per particle at ρ/ρ₀ = 0.5: -7.64(3) MeV for 28 nucleons
- Energy per particle at ρ/ρ₀ = 3.0: -10.6(1) MeV for 28 nucleons
- Saturation energy from fit: E₀/A = -14.04(4) MeV at saturation density ρ̄/ρ₀ = 1.83(1)
- Compressibility at saturation: K ≈ 190 MeV
- Fit parameters: α = 3.09(6) MeV, β = -0.44(8) MeV
- Finite-size effects between 28 and 108 nucleons are within 3 percent at ρ = 0.08 fm⁻³ and ρ = 0.48 fm⁻³
- AFDMC reproduced the binding energy of ⁴He with AV6-class potentials within 0.3 MeV total energy

## Context

This work extends the AFDMC method, previously applied to pure neutron matter (Fantoni 2001, Sarsa 2003) and neutron drops (Gandolfi 2006, Pederiva 2004), to symmetric nuclear matter with both protons and neutrons. The results contradict earlier FHNC/SOC calculations (Bombaci 2005) which overbind at high density, and BHF two-hole-line calculations (Bombaci 2005) which show shallower binding; the authors show that including lowest-order elementary diagrams brings FHNC/SOC results close to AFDMC. This enables future calculations with spin-orbit terms, three-body forces, and asymmetric nuclear matter relevant to neutron star structure and supernovae.
