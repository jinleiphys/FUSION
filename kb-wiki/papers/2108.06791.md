# Electromagnetic response of hot QCD medium in the presence of background time-varying fields

> The response of the hot QCD medium in the presence of external time dependent electromagnetic fields has been studied within the relativistic Boltzmann transport theory. The impact of the time dependence of the electromagnetic fields and collisional aspects of the medium to the induced electric and Hall current densities has been explored. The non-equilibrium momentum distribution of degrees of freedom has been obtained in the presence of space-time varying electromagnetic fields. Further, the analysis has been extended to an anisotropic QCD medium while incorporating the in-medium interaction effects. It is observed that the electric charge transport is sensitive to the inhomogeneity of the fields and the momentum anisotropy of the QCD medium.

## Key claim

The response of the hot QCD medium to external electromagnetic fields is sensitive to the time dependence (inhomogeneity) of those fields and to the momentum anisotropy of the medium, with the additional current components being most pronounced at temperatures near the transition temperature \(T_c\), a nuance the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation, assuming a near-equilibrium distribution function ansatz linear in the electromagnetic fields and their first-order spacetime derivatives. They derived coupled differential equations for the transport coefficients \(\alpha_i\) and solved them analytically for three cases: constant fields, time-varying electric field with constant magnetic field, and fully time-varying electromagnetic fields. In-medium interactions were incorporated via the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM), and momentum anisotropy was introduced through a Romatschke–Strickland form with a small anisotropy parameter \(\xi\).

## Key numbers

- For constant electric and magnetic fields, the electrical conductivity \(\sigma_e\) and Hall conductivity \(\sigma_H\) were obtained; the results agree with Feng (2017) and Puglisi (2014).
- For time-varying electric field with constant magnetic field \(eB = 0.03\ \text{GeV}^2\), the additional Ohmic current coefficient \(\alpha_2 = \frac{q_{f_k} \epsilon [(\epsilon/\tau_R)^2 - (q_{f_k} B)^2]}{[(\epsilon/\tau_R)^2 + (q_{f_k} B)^2]^2}\) and Hall current coefficient \(\alpha_4 = \frac{2 q_{f_k}^2 \epsilon^2}{\tau_R [(\epsilon/\tau_R)^2 + (q_{f_k} B)^2]^2}\) were derived.
- For time-varying electromagnetic fields with magnetic field decay time \(\tau_B = 7\ \text{fm}\), the ratios \(R_e = j_e/(ET)\) and \(R_H = j_H/(EBT)\) were computed and compared with lattice data from Amato (2013), Aarts (2007), and the transport theory of Thakur (2019) at \(B=0\).
- The anisotropic corrections to the electric current \(\delta j_e^{(0)}\) and \(\delta j_e^{(1)}\) scale linearly with the anisotropy parameter \(\xi\) and involve integrals over momentum with the factor \(l = 1/(6\pi^2 T^2)\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of electric charge transport in the QCD medium under constant electromagnetic fields (Feng 2017, Das 2019, Thakur 2019, Kurian 2020) and the authors’ own earlier work on time-dependent electric fields (Gowthama 2020). It generalizes the formalism to include time-varying magnetic fields and momentum anisotropy, and it incorporates QCD equation-of-state effects via the EQPM (Chandra 2011). The results are compared with lattice QCD estimates (Amato 2013, Aarts 2007) and transport theory (Thakur 2019). The framework enables future studies of magnetohydrodynamics with realistic field profiles in heavy-ion collisions and of charge-dependent directed flow in asymmetric collisions (Gursoy 2018).
