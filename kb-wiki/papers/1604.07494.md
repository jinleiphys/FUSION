# Gauge angle dependence in TDHFB calculations of ${}^{20}$O + ${}^{20}$O head-on collisions with the Gogny interaction

> A numerical method to solve the TDHFB equations by using a hybrid basis of the two-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenfunctions and one-dimensional Lagrange mesh with the Gogny effective interaction is applied to the head-on collisions of the superfluid nuclei ${}^{20}$O's. Taking the energies around the barrier top energy, the trajectories, pairing energies, and numbers of transferred nucleons are displayed. Their dependence on the relative gauge angle at the initial time is studied by taking typical sample points of the gauge angle. It turned out that the functional form of the flux of the neutrons across a section plane is proportional to the sine of the two times of the gauge angle.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in TDHFB calculations of head-on collisions between two superfluid ${}^{20}$O nuclei, the flux of neutrons across a section plane is proportional to the sine of twice the relative gauge angle, but only when both nuclei are superfluid; no gauge-angle dependence appears when one nucleus is normal (non-superfluid) like ${}^{16}$O.

## Method
The authors solve the TDHFB equations using a hybrid basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenfunctions in the transverse plane and a one-dimensional Lagrange mesh along the collision axis, with the Gogny D1S effective interaction. Head-on collisions of two ${}^{20}$O nuclei are simulated at three center-of-mass energies (9.21, 9.41, and 9.61 MeV) around the barrier top, and the initial relative gauge angle between the two superfluid nuclei is varied among four representative values (0, 45, 90, and 135 degrees). Trajectories, pairing energies, and transferred nucleon numbers are extracted using a section-plane method following Washiyama (2008).

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energies studied: 9.21, 9.41, and 9.61 MeV, corresponding to boost energies of 4.8, 5.0, and 5.2 MeV respectively.
- Grid spacing: $\Delta z = 0.91$ fm; number of Lagrange mesh grid points: 46; harmonic oscillator quantum number cutoff: $n_x + n_y \leq N_{\rm shell} = 4$; harmonic oscillator parameter $\hbar\omega = 14.6$ MeV.
- Total basis size: 2760 degrees of freedom including spin and isospin; CPU time per time step: 4 minutes on 512 CPUs; one trajectory requires 8-10 days.
- For the sub-barrier case ($E_{\rm cm}=9.21$ MeV), the transferred neutron number reaches 0.55 at $R \sim 18$ fm, while transferred protons remain below 0.05; the neutron transfer jumps by 0.2 near the turning point at $R \sim 10.0$ fm.
- For the above-barrier case ($E_{\rm cm}=9.61$ MeV), the pairing energy drops from -13 MeV to -2 MeV after fusion and oscillates around -2 MeV.
- The pairing energy variation at the turning point for $E_{\rm cm}=9.21$ MeV is 0.15 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the TDHFB method with the Gogny interaction developed by Hashimoto and Scamps (2013, 2014) to study the role of the initial relative gauge angle in collisions of superfluid nuclei, a degree of freedom previously unexplored in mean-field treatments. The particle transfer mechanism is analogous to the Josephson effect in superconductors (Josephson, 1962, 1965), and the section-plane technique follows Washiyama (2008) from TDHF studies. The results enable future investigations of gauge-angle dependence in more complex superfluid collisions and provide a foundation for number-projection methods used by Simenel (2010), Scamps (2015), and Sekizawa (2014, 2015).
