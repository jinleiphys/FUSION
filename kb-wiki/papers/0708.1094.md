# Threshold Resonant Structure of the 232Th Neutron-Induced Fission Cross Section

> The structures observed in the sub-threshold neutron-induced fission of ^{232}Th were investigated employing a recent developed model. Theoretical single-particle excitations of a phenomenological two-humped barrier are determined by solving a system of coupled differential equations for the motion along the optimal fission path. A rather good agreement with experimental data was obtained using a small number of independent parameters. It is predicted that the structure at 1.4 and 1.6 MeV is mainly dominated by spin 3/2 partial cross-section with small admixture of spin 1/2, while the structure at 1.7 MeV is given by a large partial cross section of spin 5/2.

# Threshold Resonant Structure of the 232Th Neutron-Induced Fission Cross Section

## Key claim
The sub-threshold resonant structures observed in neutron-induced fission of 232Th at 1.4, 1.6, and 1.7 MeV can be explained by dynamical single-particle effects associated with vibrational resonances in the second well of a double-humped fission barrier, using only six barrier parameters and one internuclear velocity parameter, rather than requiring a triple-humped barrier interpretation.

## Method
The authors computed single-particle excitations along the optimal fission path for the partition 233Th → 98Sr + 135Te using the Superasymmetric Two Center Shell Model (STCSM) with an axial-symmetric shape parametrization of two intersected spheres joined by a neck. They solved a system of coupled differential equations for the time-dependent Schrodinger equation in a diabatic basis to obtain occupation probabilities for 40 different energy paths of the unpaired neutron, then added these excitations as specialization energies to a phenomenological double-humped barrier to compute fission cross sections via a statistical model with Clebsch-Gordan decomposition for spin projections.

## Key numbers
- Heights of the phenomenological double-humped barrier: inner barrier 6.81 MeV, second well 4.83 MeV, outer barrier 6.61 MeV
- Widths of the phenomenological barrier: inner barrier 1.2 MeV, second well 0.4 MeV, outer barrier 1.1 MeV
- Internuclear velocity used in the time-dependent calculation: 3.5 × 10^4 m/s, corresponding to a reaction time of approximately 5 × 10^-19 s
- The first well of the theoretical barrier is located at elongation R ≈ 5.5 fm, the first barrier at R = 10.57 fm, the second well at R = 13.69 fm, and the second barrier at R = 15.139 fm
- The structure at 1.4 and 1.6 MeV is mainly dominated by spin 3/2 partial cross-section with small admixture of spin 1/2
- The structure at 1.7 MeV is given by a large partial cross section of spin 5/2

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous exploratory investigation of 230Th neutron-induced fission threshold resonant structure (Mirea, 2005) by applying the same dynamical single-particle excitation formalism to 232Th. It offers an alternative to the triple-humped barrier interpretation proposed by James (1969) and Blons (1978) based on angular distribution analysis, which postulated an intermediate minimum in the potential energy surface. The results enable future experimental investigation of whether isotopic fragment distributions in fission change across individual resonances, which would provide direct evidence for the predicted mass-partition-dependent effects.
