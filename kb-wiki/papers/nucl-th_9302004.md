# Relativistic and Nuclear Structure Effects in Parity-Violating Quasielastic Electron Scattering

> The parity violating longitudinal asymmetry ${\cal A}$ is calculated for quasielastic electron scattering. We use a variety of relativistic mean field models for the response of nuclear matter and $^{12}$C at a momentum transfer of q=550 MeV/c. Relativistic effects from a reduced nucleon mass, RPA correlations and vacuum polarization can all change ${\cal A}$ by a relatively large amount. These large nuclear structure corrections could make it impossible to extract strange quark matrix elements or radiative corrections to weak axial currents.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects from a reduced nucleon mass, RPA correlations, and vacuum polarization can all change the parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry \( \mathcal{A} \) by a relatively large amount, potentially making it impossible to extract strange quark matrix elements or radiative corrections to weak axial currents from quasielastic electron scattering measurements.

## Method

The authors calculated the parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry \( \mathcal{A} \) for quasielastic electron scattering using a variety of relativistic mean field models for the response of nuclear matter and \( ^{12}\text{C} \) at a momentum transfer of \( q = 550 \) MeV/c. They computed the nuclear response functions from imaginary parts of polarization tensors, incorporating effects from a reduced nucleon mass (via scalar and vector mean fields), random phase approximation (RPA) correlations, and vacuum polarization, and expressed the asymmetry as a ratio of weak and electromagnetic response functions.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry scale factor \( a_0 \tau \) is set by \( a_0 = -G_F M^2 / (\sqrt{2} \pi \alpha) \simeq -3.172 \times 10^{-4} \).
- Calculations were performed at a momentum transfer of \( q = 550 \) MeV/c for both nuclear matter and \( ^{12}\text{C} \).
- The parity-violating asymmetry \( \mathcal{A} \) is decomposed into longitudinal, transverse, and transverse-axial contributions: \( \mathcal{A} = \mathcal{A}_L + \mathcal{A}_T + \mathcal{A}_{T'} \), with each term proportional to \( a_0 \tau \) and involving specific kinematic factors \( v_L \), \( v_T \), and \( v_{T'} \).

## Context

This work extends earlier results by Horowitz (1992) on relativistic effects in parity-violating quasielastic scattering, and it addresses concerns raised by Donnelly et al. (1992) that isospin ratio effects in the transverse response are small and under control. The paper shows that nuclear structure corrections from relativistic mean field models, RPA correlations, and vacuum polarization are large enough to hinder the extraction of strange quark matrix elements, which is a prerequisite for combining electron and neutrino scattering data to separate isoscalar strange quark contributions from radiative corrections to the isovector axial current.
