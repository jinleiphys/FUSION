# Fragmentation functions for axial-vector heavy tetraquarks: A TQ4Q1.1 update

> We present and discuss the release of novel sets of collinear, variable-flavor-number-scheme fragmentation functions for axial-vector, fully heavy $T_{4c}(1^{+-})$ and $T_{4b}(1^{+-})$ tetraquarks. Working within the single-parton approximation at leading power and employing nonrelativistic QCD factorization adapted for tetraquark Fock states, we model the initial-scale fragmentation input using a recent calculation for the constituent heavy-quark channel. Standard DGLAP evolution is then applied, ensuring consistent implementation of evolution thresholds. To support phenomenology, we investigate NLL/NLO$^+$ rates for tetraquark-jet systems at the HL-LHC and FCC from (sym)JETHAD. This study further integrates exotic matter explorations with precision QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents new sets of collinear, variable-flavor-number-scheme fragmentation functions for axial-vector fully heavy tetraquarks $T_{4c}(1^{+-})$ and $T_{4b}(1^{+-})$, but the initial-scale input is modeled only for the constituent heavy-quark channel, with gluon and light-parton channels set to zero at the starting scale and generated purely through DGLAP evolution.

## Method

Working within the single-parton approximation at leading power and employing nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization adapted for tetraquark Fock states, the authors model the initial-scale fragmentation input using a recent calculation for the constituent heavy-quark channel, with the short-distance coefficient rederived via the (sym)JETHAD framework. Standard DGLAP evolution is then applied using APFEL++ at NLO accuracy, ensuring consistent implementation of evolution thresholds through the heavy-flavor nonrelativistic evolution (HFNRevo) scheme. The resulting fragmentation functions are released in LHAPDF format.

## Key numbers

- Long-distance matrix elements: ${\cal O}^{T_{4c}(1^{+-})}([3,3]) = (0.0878 \pm 0.0098)\text{ GeV}^9$ and ${\cal O}^{T_{4b}(1^{+-})}([3,3]) \simeq (35.1 \pm 3.9)\text{ GeV}^9$
- Heavy-quark masses used: $m_c = 1.5$ GeV and $m_b = 4.9$ GeV
- Initial fragmentation scale: $\mu_{F,0} = 5 m_Q$ for the constituent heavy-quark channel
- Heavy-quark fragmentation functions peak near $z \simeq 0.85$
- Phenomenological predictions for tetraquark-jet systems at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV (HL-LHC) and $100$ TeV (FCC) with asymmetric transverse-momentum bins: $30 < |\vec \kappa_1| < 120$ GeV for the tetraquark and $50 < |\vec \kappa_2| < 120$ GeV for the jet

## Context

This work extends the TQ4Q1.1 set of fragmentation functions, previously released for scalar $T_{4Q}(0^{++})$ and tensor $T_{4Q}(2^{++})$ tetraquarks by Celiberto (2024), to axial-vector $1^{+-}$ states for both charm and bottom sectors. The initial-scale inputs are based on NRQCD calculations by Bai et al. (2024) and Feng et al. (2020), and the evolution scheme builds on the HFNRevo approach developed by Celiberto (2024). The new fragmentation functions enable next-to-leading-logarithmic precision studies of exotic tetraquark production at the HL-LHC and FCC, providing a cleaner probe of heavy-quark spin symmetry and multiquark dynamics compared to scalar and tensor states which are more susceptible to mixing with quarkonia or molecular configurations.
