# The role of the $S_{31}$ $πN$ partial wave in the three-nucleon force

> We re-examine the contribution of the $πN$ $S_{31}$ channel to the three-nucleon force for an energy dependent separable potential. Despite the lack of cancellation between the $S_{31}$ and the $P_{11}$ and $P_{33}$ channels, the total contribution of the three-nucleon force to the triton binding is still small.

# The role of the $S_{31}$ $\pi N$ partial wave in the three-nucleon force

## Key claim
The contribution of the $S_{31}$ $\pi N$ channel to the three-nucleon force is highly model-dependent due to uncertainty in the analytic continuation of the $\pi N$ amplitude from the physical region to the subthreshold region. Even when the $S_{31}$ contribution is suppressed by using an energy-dependent separable potential, the total three-nucleon force contribution to the triton binding energy remains too small to bridge the gap between experimental and theoretical values.

## Method
The authors constructed a rank-one separable potential for the $S_{31}$ $\pi N$ partial wave with an energy-dependent strength parameter of the form $c/(E - M)$, fitted to reproduce the scattering length and phase shifts up to 400 MeV pion energy. They then calculated the contribution of this partial wave to the three-nucleon force using first-order perturbation theory, employing a triton wave function from the Faddeev equation with the Paris-Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (PEST) potential and a $\pi NN$ form factor from the $P_{11}$ potential of McLeod and Afnan (1985).

## Key numbers
- The new $S_{31}$ potential yields a scattering length of $a_3 = -0.101 \, m_\pi^{-1}$, in better agreement with the empirical value of $a_3 = -0.101 \pm 0.004 \, m_\pi^{-1}$ compared to the previous potential's value of $a_3 = -0.091 \, m_\pi^{-1}$.
- The $S_{31}$ contribution to the three-nucleon force with full energy dependence is 4.1 keV in this work, compared to 31.7 keV in the previous calculation by Saito and Afnan (1994, 1995).
- The total three-body force contribution to the triton binding with full energy dependence is -29.9 keV in this work, compared to -2.3 keV in the previous calculation.
- The combined contribution from the $P_{11}$ and $P_{33}$ channels is -8.8 keV and -16.0 keV respectively, which is still too small to bridge the gap between the experimental triton binding energy of 8.45 MeV and the commonly reported value of 7.7 MeV for many realistic potentials (Friar et al., 1993).

## Context
This work extends the previous studies by Saito and Afnan (1994, 1995) which attributed the small three-body force contribution to cancellation between the repulsive $S_{31}$ and attractive $P_{11}$ and $P_{33}$ partial waves, energy dependence of the $\pi N$ amplitude, and a soft $\pi NN$ form factor. By introducing an energy-dependent separable potential for the $S_{31}$ channel, the authors demonstrate that the cancellation is reduced and the total three-body force becomes more attractive, but still insufficient to resolve the triton binding energy discrepancy. The work highlights the model dependence in extrapolating $\pi N$ amplitudes to the subthreshold region, which introduces uncertainty in three-nucleon force calculations.
