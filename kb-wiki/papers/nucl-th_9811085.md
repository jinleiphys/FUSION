# Survival Probabilities of Disoriented Chiral Domains in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> By using a dynamical transport code we show that chirally disoriented domains might survive the hadronization phase in a central relativistic heavy ion collision. A successful measurement of such domains after freeze-out will depend on the domain size and the manifestation of the disorientation in the low part of the charged pion momentum spectrum. We investigated the measurement of the transverse momentum and the pseudo-rapidity spectrum of pions below 200 MeV/c. In all cases the successful determination of a realistic domain requires first and second moment analyses of the phase space.

## Key claim

Using a dynamical transport code, the authors show that chirally disoriented domains might survive the hadronization and rescattering phases in a central relativistic heavy ion collision, but a successful measurement after freeze-out will depend on the domain size, its localization in coordinate space, and the manifestation of the disorientation in the low part of the charged pion momentum spectrum below 200 MeV/c.

## Method

The authors generated a DCC domain with specified size and energy density, embedded it in the hadronic background of a central Au-Au event at RHIC simulated by HIJING, and propagated the system through hadronization using a kinetic Monte Carlo cascade code that assumes strong binary collisions between hadrons including resonances. The domain was placed at the center of the fireball at a proper hadronization time of 1 fm/c, and the subsequent evolution followed the expansion rules of a Bjorken inside-outside cascade. A multi-resolution wavelet analysis with a Haar basis was applied to the pseudo-rapidity distributions to detect deviations from smooth background on an event-by-event basis.

## Key numbers

- Domain size of 3 fm was chosen as a conservative value for most simulations, with the mean transverse momentum of pions in the domain proportional to 1/r, giving about 100 MeV/c for a 3 fm radius.
- The energy density from explicit symmetry breaking in the linear sigma model is 40 MeV/fm^3, yielding approximately 32 pions in a 3 fm domain; the main calculation used 150 charged pions in a single domain with an average transverse momentum of about 100 MeV/c.
- For a 3 fm domain radius, domains with an energy density as low as 50 MeV/fm^3 can be detected via wavelet analysis even after rescattering.
- The background in the STAR detector acceptance (eta = +/-1) contains around 1500 charged pions, giving a signal-to-noise ratio of 1:10 for the 150-pion domain.
- The ratio of DCC to standard pions below p_T = 150 MeV/c is about 2:1, and the mean of the transverse momentum distribution shifts from about 330 MeV/c to 300 MeV/c.
- The freeze-out time for DCC pions is around 10 fm/c, while the hadronization time for the domain itself may be as large as 5 fm/c based on simulations by Rajagopal and Randrup.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation in the linear sigma model, particularly the quench scenario proposed by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and the dynamical evolution in a nonequilibrium bath studied by Gavin and Muller (1994), which suggested domain sizes of 3-6 fm. The authors address the experimental feasibility of detecting DCCs at RHIC, specifically with the STAR detector, by simulating the survival of domains through final-state interactions, in contrast to transport model results by V. Koch and the RQMD group (1997) which suggested domains cannot survive rescattering. The work enables experimental searches for DCC formation using charged pion observables (pseudo-rapidity and transverse momentum distributions) without requiring neutral pion measurements, and suggests that multi-dimensional correlation studies and wavelet analysis can detect domains even in the presence of multiple domains per event.
