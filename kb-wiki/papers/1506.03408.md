# Baryon Number Fluctuations from a Crossover Equation of State Compared to Heavy-Ion Collision Measurements in the Beam Energy Range $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 to 200 GeV

> Fluctuations of the proton number distribution in central Au-Au collisions have been measured by the STAR collaboration in a beam energy scan at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The motivation is a search for evidence of a critical point in the equation of state. It was found that the skewness and kurtosis display an interesting energy dependence. We compare these measurements to an equation of state which smoothly interpolates between an excluded volume hadron resonance gas at low energy density to a perturbative plasma of quarks and gluons at high energy density. This crossover equation of state agrees very well with the lattice QCD equation of state. The crossover equation of state can reproduce the data if the fluctuations are frozen at a temperature significantly lower than the average chemical freeze-out.

# Baryon Number Fluctuations from a Crossover Equation of State Compared to Heavy-Ion Collision Measurements in the Beam Energy Range sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV

## Key claim

The crossover equation of state, which smoothly interpolates between an excluded volume hadron resonance gas and a perturbative QCD plasma, can reproduce the STAR collaboration's measured skewness and kurtosis of net-proton distributions, but only if the baryon number fluctuations are assumed to freeze out at a temperature significantly lower (approximately 26 MeV lower) than the average chemical freeze-out temperature. Without this post-chemical freeze-out assumption, the crossover equation of state disagrees quantitatively with the data, even though it agrees very well with lattice QCD results for the pressure, trace anomaly, sound speed, and kurtosis at zero chemical potential.

## Method

The authors constructed a pressure function P(T,mu) that switches between a hadronic resonance gas piece (using two excluded volume models, exI and exII, plus a point-particle model) and a perturbative QCD piece via a smooth switching function S(T,mu). They fit the five parameters (two in the switching function, two in the pQCD piece, one in the excluded volume model) to lattice QCD data for the pressure and trace anomaly at zero chemical potential. They then computed the skewness (S sigma) and kurtosis (kappa sigma^2) of net-baryon number fluctuations from third and fourth derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potential, and compared these to STAR measurements at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV, first assuming fluctuations freeze out at the average chemical freeze-out temperature and then assuming a lower freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers

* The excluded volume model exI best fit gave T_0 = 177.1 MeV, epsilon_0^(1/4) = 306.5 MeV, and chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.34; exII gave T_0 = 177.6 MeV, epsilon_0^(1/4) = 279.7 MeV, and chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.34.
* The point particle model best fits gave T_0 = 145.3 MeV (r=4) or 157.4 MeV (r=5) with chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.56 or 0.62.
* The hard core radius of the proton and neutron from the excluded volume fits is 0.580 fm for exI and 0.655 fm for exII.
* The average chemical freeze-out temperature parameterization uses a = 0.166 GeV; the post-chemical freeze-out scenario uses a = 0.140 GeV, a reduction of 26 MeV.
* The STAR data cover beam energies sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV for central Au-Au collisions (0-5% centrality).
* The ratio kappa/S^2 shows excellent agreement at the four lowest beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, and 27 GeV) when using the lower freeze-out temperature.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous construction of crossover equations of state (Albright, Kapusta, Young, 2014) that interpolate between an excluded volume hadron resonance gas and perturbative QCD, which were shown to reproduce lattice QCD results for the pressure and trace anomaly. It directly compares to STAR collaboration beam energy scan measurements (STAR, 2014) of net-proton fluctuations. The study contrasts with Fukushima (2013), who used a point particle hadron resonance gas and a relativistic mean field model, and with Borsanyi et al. (2014), who compared lattice QCD results to second and third moments at selected energies. The finding that fluctuations must freeze out at lower temperatures than chemical freeze-out is consistent with known kinetic freeze-out temperatures from other studies (kinetic1, kinetic2). The results do not suggest evidence for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram in the energy range studied.
