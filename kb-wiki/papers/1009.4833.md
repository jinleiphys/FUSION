# Ab initio computation of circular quantum dots

> We perform coupled-cluster and diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of the energies of circular quantum dots up to 20 electrons. The coupled-cluster calculations include triples corrections and a renormalized Coulomb interaction defined for a given number of low-lying oscillator shells. Using such a renormalized Coulomb interaction brings the coupled-cluster calculations with triples correlations in excellent agreement with the diffusion Monte Carlo calculations. This opens up perspectives for doing ab initio calculations for much larger systems of electrons.

## Key claim

Coupled-cluster calculations with triples corrections (Lambda-CCSD(T)) and a renormalized Coulomb interaction defined for a limited number of low-lying oscillator shells yield energies for circular quantum dots that agree closely with diffusion Monte Carlo results, provided the renormalized interaction is used rather than the bare Coulomb interaction.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-cluster calculations (CCSD and Lambda-CCSD(T)) for circular quantum dots with N = 2, 6, 12, and 20 electrons confined by a parabolic potential, using a Hartree-Fock basis built from harmonic oscillator single-particle states. They constructed a renormalized two-body Coulomb interaction by exactly diagonalizing the two-electron problem in a centre-of-mass basis and applying a similarity transformation to obtain an effective interaction defined for the lowest 10-20 oscillator shells. For comparison, diffusion Monte Carlo calculations were performed using a trial wavefunction built from local-density-approximation orbitals with a Jastrow factor.

## Key numbers

* Energies were computed for N = 2, 6, 12, and 20 electrons in circular quantum dots with confinement frequencies omega = 0.28 a.u., 0.5 a.u., and 1.0 a.u.
* The effective interaction was defined for the lowest 10-20 oscillator shells; the full single-particle basis included up to 10 major oscillator shells (n = 110 single-particle states for N = 6).
* For GaAs quantum dots, the effective Bohr radius is approximately 97.93 Angstroms and the effective Hartree is approximately 11.86 meV.
* The magic numbers studied (N = 2, 6, 12, 20) correspond to closed harmonic oscillator shells.

## Context

This work extends coupled-cluster theory, previously successful in nuclear physics for nuclei up to Sn-132 (Hagen, 2008-2010), to circular quantum dots, where prior ab initio methods were limited to exact diagonalization for up to eight electrons (Eto, 1997; Maksym, 1990) or Monte Carlo methods for up to 24 electrons (Pederiva, 2001, 2003). The renormalized Coulomb interaction technique, adapted from nuclear many-body work (Navratil, 2000), enables coupled-cluster calculations to match diffusion Monte Carlo benchmarks, opening the possibility of ab initio calculations for quantum dots with up to 50 electrons without spherical symmetry.
