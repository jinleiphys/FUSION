# Tetraquarks at large $M$ and large $N$

> We study tetraquarks in large $N$ QCD with heavy quarks, in the domain where non-relativistic quantum mechanics offers an adequate approximation. Within the regime of validity of the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, we systematically study and explicitly construct tetraquark states. At leading order in the $1/N$ expansion, the bound spectrum consists of free mesons, while the $1/N$ corrections give rise to a Born-Oppenheimer potential that can bind the mesons into tetraquarks. We find two different types of tetraquarks, each endowed with distinct color-spatial wavefunctions. These states arise in the presence of an $\mathcal{O}(N)$ mass hierarchy between the quarks and the antiquarks. We provide a quantitative argument indicating that only for such a hierarchy is the ground state of the system a tetraquark. We discuss what the extrapolation of our results to realistic values of the parameters may imply for the QCD tetraquark states.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that stable tetraquark states exist in large \(N\) QCD with heavy quarks only when there is an \(\mathcal{O}(N)\) mass hierarchy between the quarks and antiquarks; outside this regime, no exactly stable tetraquark ground state exists, though metastable states were not systematically studied.

## Method

The authors work in the regime where all quark masses are much larger than \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) and the 't Hooft coupling \(\alpha = \alpha_s N/2\) is small, allowing a non-relativistic treatment via single-gluon-exchange Hamiltonian. They apply the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) approximation to the four-body problem with two heavy quarks (mass \(M\)) and two lighter antiquarks (mass \(m\)), solving first for the light degrees of freedom in the static background of the heavy quarks to generate an effective BO potential, then solving for the heavy-quark dynamics. The analysis uses the \(1/N\) expansion, where at leading order the spectrum consists of free mesons and the \(1/N\) corrections produce the BO potential that can bind mesons into tetraquarks.

## Key numbers

* The mass hierarchy condition for stable tetraquarks is \(M/m \gg N\) (the authors find this condition, while a prior study by Czarnecki (2017) found \(M/m \gg N^{3/2}\)).
* The observed \(X(3872)\) mass is within \(120\) keV of the \(D^0 \bar{D}^{*0}\) threshold.
* The observed \(T_{cc}^+\) mass is within approximately \(400\) keV of the \(D^0 D^{*+}\) threshold.
* Other candidate tetraquarks (e.g., \(Z_c(3900)\), \(Z_c(4020)\), \(Z_b(10650)\)) are found within \(\sim 10\) MeV of their corresponding two-meson thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the debate on tetraquark existence in large \(N\) QCD, originally argued against by Witten (1979) and Coleman (1985) on the basis that meson propagators factorize at leading order, but with a loophole pointed out by Weinberg (2013) that connected subleading diagrams could still contain tetraquark poles. The authors' type-I tetraquarks are the large \(N\) incarnation of states established for heavy enough quarks by Carlson (1987) and Manohar (1992), and relate to predictions for doubly-heavy tetraquarks by Karliner (2017), Eichten (2017), and Braaten (2020). The results enable a controlled analytic framework for studying tetraquark binding mechanisms and suggest that the observed \(T_{cc}^+\) may be more akin to the loosely bound type-II tetraquarks than to deeply bound type-I states.
