# In-medium properties of the phi meson with phi N resonant contributions

> Nuclear production experiments report missing absorption processes of the in-medium phi meson. Contributions arising from the K-bar K cloud have already been widely studied, and therefore we investigate the phi-meson properties in cold nuclear matter with the additional inclusion of resonant phi N interactions. Two models are considered which dynamically generate N*-like states close to the phi N threshold. We find that these states, together with the non-resonant part of the amplitude, contribute to the phi self-energy with the same order of magnitude as the K-bar K effects. At non-vanishing nuclear density, both models lead to an additional in-medium broadening of the phi, up to around 50 MeV. Furthermore, at least one of the models is compatible with a mass shift to lower energies of up to 35 MeV at threshold and normal matter density. Finally, a double-peak structure appears in the spectral function due to the mixing of resonance-hole modes with the $ϕ$ quasi-particle peak. These results converge into the direction of the experimental findings.

# In-medium properties of the phi meson with phi N resonant contributions

## Key claim

The inclusion of resonant phi N interactions, which dynamically generate N*-like states near the phi N threshold, contributes to the phi meson self-energy with the same order of magnitude as the previously studied K-bar K cloud effects, and can explain the missing absorption observed in nuclear production experiments. However, the results are model-dependent: only one of the two models (SU(6) approach) produces a significant attractive mass shift, while both models yield additional in-medium broadening.

## Method

The phi meson self-energy in cold nuclear matter was calculated by integrating the phi N scattering amplitude over the nucleon Fermi distribution, using a coupled-channel on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation. Two different models for the phi N interaction were compared: one based on Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) with vector-meson exchange, and another based on SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry extension of chiral perturbation theory. The loop function G was modified to include Pauli blocking effects from the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- Additional in-medium broadening of the phi meson up to around 50 MeV at non-vanishing nuclear density, for both models.
- Attractive mass shift of up to 35 MeV at threshold and normal matter density, compatible with the SU(6) model but not with the HLS model.
- Reported experimental widths of 23-100 MeV for phi absorption at half normal nuclear-matter density (from Wood et al., 2010).
- The phi N interaction magnitude at zero momentum is larger in the SU(6) model than in the HLS model, but the HLS contribution is substantial for typical production-experiment momenta around 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the phi meson self-energy from K-bar K cloud effects (Ko, 1992; Klingl, 1997; Holt, 2004; Alvarez-Ruso, 2002; Oset, 2000; Cabrera, 2002; Faessler, 2002) which predicted large width modifications but could not fully explain the experimental absorption seen in nuclear production experiments (Muto, 2005; Ishikawa, 2004; Wood, 2010; Hartmann, 2012) and heavy-ion collision experiments (Wada, 2013; Abelev, 2014). The results enable future calculations of the nuclear transparency ratio for different nuclei as a function of phi momentum, and suggest that phi production reactions in nuclei could discriminate between the HLS and SU(6) models due to their differing predictions for mass shift and spectral shape.
