# Effect of a repulsive three-body interaction on the $DD^{(*)}K$ molecule

> The hadronic molecular picture of the observed exotic states has inspired numerous investigations into few-body systems. Recently, the lattice effective field theory studied the effect of a three-body interaction on the binding energy of the $DD^{*}K$ system, revealing an intriguing phenomenon in the binding energy. This work uses the Gaussian expansion method to explore the underlying physics. Our results show that as the repulsive three-body interaction strengthens, the spatial size of the $DD^{(*)}K$ bound state gradually increases. Further enhancement of the three-body interaction causes the $DD^{(*)}K$ three-body bound state to break into a $D^{(*)}K$ two-body bound state, accompanied by a distant $D$ meson. The identical nature of the two $D$ mesons leads to the fact that the $DDK$ system consistently resembles an isosceles triangle-shaped spatial configuration.

## Key claim

The paper shows that as a repulsive three-body interaction in the \(DD^{(*)}K\) system is strengthened to infinity, the three-body bound state does not simply dissolve but instead breaks into a \(D^{(*)}K\) two-body bound state with a distant \(D\) meson, with the asymptotic binding energy matching that of the two-body subsystem. The abstract's claim that "the spatial size gradually increases" is qualified by the finding that this expansion is followed by a second stage where the \(D^*K\) distance actually decreases.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrodinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with Jacobi coordinates. Two-body \(DD^{(*)}\) potentials were taken from the one-boson exchange model with a monopole form factor (cutoff 1.01 GeV, fixed by the \(X(3872)\) binding energy), while \(D^{(*)}K\) potentials were described by the Weinberg-Tomozawa term with a Gaussian regulator (range \(b=1\) fm, strength \(C_L=-320.1\) MeV, fixed by the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) mass). The three-body interaction was a contact potential with variable strength \(C\) and effective ranges \(a_1=0.5\) fm and \(a_2=0.4\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Without three-body interaction, the \(DD^*K\) system has a binding energy of 77.8 MeV (consistent with 79.1 MeV from Zhang (2024)).
- Without three-body interaction, the \(DDK\) system has a binding energy of 71.2 MeV (consistent with Wu (2019)).
- With infinitely strong repulsive three-body interaction, the \(DD^*K\) binding energy asymptotically approaches 46.1 MeV, matching the \(D^*K\) two-body binding energy.
- With infinitely strong repulsive three-body interaction, the \(DDK\) binding energy asymptotically approaches 45.0 MeV.
- In the infinite-strength limit, the \(D^*K\) distance in the \(DD^*K\) system is 1.27 fm, identical to the characteristic size of the \(D^*K\) two-body bound state.
- The \(DD^*K\) system transitions between two stages at a three-body strength of approximately \(C \approx 2\times 10^5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the lattice effective field theory study by Zhang (2024), which first observed that the \(DD^*K\) binding energy asymptotically approaches about 45 MeV under infinite repulsive three-body interaction. The present study uses the Gaussian expansion method to reveal the spatial mechanism behind this behavior, showing that the system transitions from a genuine three-body bound state to a \(D^{(*)}K\) two-body bound state with a distant spectator meson. This enables future experimental studies of three-hadron interactions and provides a framework for understanding similar phenomena in other three-body molecular systems such as \(DDK\), \(D\bar{D}K\), and \(D^*D^*\bar{K}^*\).
