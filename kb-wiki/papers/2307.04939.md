# Masses and radiative decay widths of S- and P-wave singly-, doubly- and triply-heavy charm and bottom baryons

> We present a study of mass spectra and electromagnetic couplings of S- and P-wave baryons containing one, two or three heavy quarks, either charm (c) or bottom (b), in the framework of a non-relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model. A simultaneous fit to 41 known masses of heavy baryons (40 singly-heavy and 1 doubly-heavy) shows a r.m.s. deviation of 19 MeV. We present equal-spacing mass rules for excited baryons which may help to assign quantum numbers to experimentally observed heavy baryons. Explicit expressions are derived for electromagnetic couplings, both for spin-flavor matrix elements and radial integrals.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model that simultaneously describes the mass spectra and electromagnetic couplings of S- and P-wave baryons containing one, two, or three heavy quarks (charm or bottom), achieving a root-mean-square deviation of 19 MeV when fitted to 41 known heavy baryon masses, though the model is limited to 1S- and 1P-wave states and does not include the recently observed Omega_c(3185) and Omega_c(3327) states in the fitting procedure.

## Method

The authors employ a non-relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model with Jacobi coordinates, using a mass formula that includes constituent quark masses, harmonic oscillator terms, spin-spin interactions, spin-orbit coupling, isospin splitting, and SU(3) flavor terms in a Gursey-Radicati form. A maximum likelihood fit to 41 experimentally known Breit-Wigner masses (40 singly-heavy and 1 doubly-heavy baryon) determines 14 parameters: four constituent quark masses (u/d, s, c, b), the spring constant C, and the coefficients A, B, E, G separately for charm and bottom sectors. The orbital wave functions are constructed for ground states (n_rho+n_lambda=0) and first excited states (n_rho+n_lambda=1), distinguishing between rho-mode and lambda-mode excitations.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation of 19 MeV for the simultaneous fit to 41 known heavy baryon masses (40 singly-heavy and 1 doubly-heavy)
- Constituent quark masses: m_u=m_d = 291.53 +/- 0.60 MeV, m_s = 461.24 +/- 0.73 MeV, m_c = 1606.80 +/- 1.01 MeV, m_b = 4944.34 +/- 1.62 MeV
- Spring constant C = 0.02712 +/- 0.00008 GeV^3 for charm sector, C = 0.02377 +/- 0.00014 GeV^3 for bottom sector
- Spin-spin coefficient A = 22.08 +/- 0.19 MeV (charm), 7.37 +/- 0.25 MeV (bottom)
- Spin-orbit coefficient B = 21.84 +/- 0.28 MeV (charm), 4.33 +/- 0.55 MeV (bottom)
- Equal-spacing mass rule for lambda-mode excited sextet baryons: M(Omega_c) - M(Xi'_c) = 126 MeV (charm), 120 MeV (bottom), compared to experimental value of approximately 125 MeV
- Equal-spacing mass rule for lambda-mode excited anti-triplet baryons: M(Xi_c) - M(Lambda_c) = 161 MeV (charm), 164 MeV (bottom), compared to experimental value of approximately 195 MeV
- Mass splitting between S=3/2 and S=1/2 ground-state sextet baryons: approximately 65-70 MeV in charm sector, approximately 20 MeV in bottom sector
- Predicted mass of doubly-heavy Xi_cc(3621) state: 3621.40 +/- 0.72 MeV (experimental value cited from LHCb)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Omega_Q baryons (Ortiz-Pacheco, 2020; Santopinto, 2019) and Xi_Q and Xi'_Q baryons (Ortiz-Pacheco and Bijker, 2022) to include Sigma_Q, Lambda_Q, Xi_QQ, Omega_QQ, and Omega_QQQ baryons within a unified framework. The model provides equal-spacing mass rules that can help assign quantum numbers to experimentally observed heavy baryons, particularly the recently discovered Omega_c and Omega_b excited states from LHCb, Belle, and CMS collaborations. The calculated masses and radiative decay widths serve as benchmarks for future measurements and lattice QCD calculations, with the authors noting that the harmonic oscillator model has known limitations for light baryons (e.g., the Roper resonance problem) but is expected to work reasonably well for the 1S- and 1P-wave heavy baryons studied here.
