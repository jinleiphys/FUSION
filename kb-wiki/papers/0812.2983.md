# An attempt to explore the production routes of Astatine radionuclides: Theoretical approach

> In order to fulfil the recent thrust of Astatine radionuclides in the field of nuclear medicine various production routes have been explored in the present work. The possible production routes of $^{209-211}$At comprise both light and heavy ion induced reactions at the bombarding energy range starting from threshold to maximum 100 MeV energy. For this purpose, we have used the nuclear reaction model codes TALYS, ALICE91 and PACE-II. Excitation functions of those radionuclides, produced through various production routes, have been calculated using nuclear reaction model codes and compared with the available measured data. Contribution of various reaction mechanisms, like, direct, preequilibrium and equilibrium reactions, to the total reaction cross section has been studied using the codes. Result shows that equilibrium reaction mechanism dominates in all cases over other reaction mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper theoretically explores multiple production routes for astatine radionuclides 209-211At using light and heavy ion induced reactions, but the authors qualify that the equilibrium reaction mechanism dominates over preequilibrium and direct mechanisms in all cases for the energy range studied (threshold to 100 MeV), and that direct reactions have essentially no contribution in this energy range.

## Method

Excitation functions for 209-211At production were calculated using three nuclear reaction model codes: TALYS (using two-component exciton model for preequilibrium, Hauser-Feshbach formalism for equilibrium, and coupled channel analysis for direct reactions), ALICE91 (using geometry dependent hybrid model for preequilibrium and Weisskopf-Ewing formalism for equilibrium), and PACE-II (Monte-Carlo statistical code based on Hauser-Feshbach formalism for evaporation only). The reactions studied included alpha and 3He induced reactions on 209Bi, and 6,7Li and 9Be induced reactions on natural Pb and Tl targets, with calculated cross sections compared to available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- 211At half-life: 7.214 h, with alpha decay energy 5.867 MeV and electron capture 58.2%, alpha 41.8%
- 210At half-life: 8.1 h, with electron capture 99.82%, alpha 0.18%
- 209At half-life: 5.41 h, with electron capture 95.9%, alpha 4.1%
- Bombarding energy range studied: from threshold to maximum 100 MeV
- Q-value for 209Bi(alpha,2n)211At: -20.33 MeV, threshold 20.72 MeV
- Q-value for 209Bi(alpha,3n)210At: -28.07 MeV, threshold 28.61 MeV
- Q-value for 209Bi(alpha,4n)209At: -35.24 MeV, threshold 35.92 MeV
- Maximum production of 211At and 210At from alpha-induced reactions on 209Bi occurs at incident energies of 30 MeV and 40 MeV respectively
- ALICE91 produces 20% more 211At and 10% less 210At than TALYS for alpha-induced reactions
- For 3He-induced reactions on 209Bi, TALYS underpredicts measured 210At cross section by a factor of five

## Context

This work extends theoretical investigations of astatine production routes beyond the commonly used 209Bi(alpha,xn)209-211At reaction, exploring alternative light heavy ion induced reactions (6,7Li on Pb and 9Be on Tl) that may serve biological studies requiring minute amounts of radioactivity. The study compares three nuclear reaction model codes (TALYS, ALICE91, PACE-II) and finds that codes accounting for preequilibrium reactions (TALYS and ALICE91) reproduce experimental data better than PACE-II, which only considers equilibrium evaporation. The results enable optimization of production parameters for medical isotope applications and provide guidance for future experimental measurements of these production routes.
