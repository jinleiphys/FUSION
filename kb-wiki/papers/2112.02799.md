# Effect of thermal shear on longitudinal spin polarization in a thermal model

> By including the recently introduced thermal shear term that contributes to the spin polarization vector at local equilibrium, we determine longitudinal polarization of $Λ$ hyperons emitted from a hot and rotating hadronic medium using the thermal model with single freeze-out. In our analysis, we consider the RHIC top energies and use the model parameters which were determined in the earlier analyses of particle spectra and elliptic flow. We confirm that, unlike the previous calculations done by using only the thermal vorticity, the thermal shear term alone leads to the correct sign of the quadrupole structure of the longitudinal component of the polarization three-vector measured in experiments. However, we find almost complete cancellation between thermal shear and vorticity terms, which eventually leads to disagreement with the data. To clarify the role played by velocity and temperature gradient terms, we present a systematic analysis of different contributions to the longitudinal polarization.

## Key claim

The thermal shear term alone produces the correct sign of the quadrupole structure of longitudinal spin polarization, but when added to the thermal vorticity contribution the two terms nearly completely cancel, leading to disagreement with experimental data from STAR.

## Method

The authors used the thermal model with single freeze-out, employing previously determined model parameters for Au+Au collisions at RHIC top energies (√sNN = 130 GeV) that describe particle spectra and elliptic flow. They computed the longitudinal spin polarization of Λ hyperons by including both the thermal vorticity tensor (antisymmetric combination of fluid field gradients) and the recently introduced thermal shear tensor (symmetric combination), splitting each into velocity-gradient and temperature-gradient contributions. The temperature gradients were obtained from ideal hydrodynamic equations with speed of sound c_s = 1/√3, and the integration over the freeze-out hypersurface was performed at constant proper time τ_f.

## Key numbers

* For centrality 0-15%, the second azimuthal harmonic from total thermal vorticity (ϖ^I + ϖ^II) is -0.000052, while from total thermal shear (ξ^I + ξ^II) it is +0.000052, giving a net value of -3.9×10^-7
* For centrality 15-30%, the total thermal vorticity harmonic is -0.000138, total thermal shear is +0.000136, net value -1.8×10^-6
* For centrality 30-60%, the total thermal vorticity harmonic is -0.000290, total thermal shear is +0.000277, net value -0.0000134
* The freeze-out temperature used is T_f = 165 MeV, with proper times τ_f ranging from 4.266 fm (30-60% centrality) to 7.666 fm (0-15% centrality)
* The Λ hyperon mass used is m = 1.116 GeV, with transverse momentum integration range p_T = 0-3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the single freeze-out thermal model of Florkowski (2019) that previously included only thermal vorticity contributions to spin polarization. It directly addresses the "sign problem" identified by Becattini (2017) and Becattini (2020), where hydrodynamic model predictions for longitudinal spin polarization had the opposite sign compared to STAR experimental data. The study tests the recent proposal by Becattini (2021) and Fu (2021) that including shear-induced polarization could resolve the sign problem, but finds that in the full thermal model with both vorticity and shear, the cancellation between terms prevents agreement with data. The results enable further investigation into whether neglecting temperature gradients (as done in Becattini (2021)) or using constituent quark masses (as in Fu (2021)) can resolve the discrepancy.
