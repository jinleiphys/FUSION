# Investigations of the broken SU(3) symmetry in deformed even-even nuclei

> A collective vector-boson model with broken SU(3) symmetry is applied to several deformed even-even nuclei. The model description of ground and $γ$ bands together with the corresponding B(E2) transition probabilities is investigated within a broad range of SU(3) irreducible representations (irreps) $(λ,μ)$. The SU(3)-symmetry characteristics of rotational nuclei are analyzed in terms of the bandmixing interactions.

## Key claim
The broken SU(3) symmetry in deformed even-even nuclei is not characterized by a single fixed irreducible representation (λ,μ), but rather by physically favored regions in the (λ,μ) plane that depend on the energy splitting between the ground state band and the γ-band, measured by the ratio ΔE₂.

## Method
The authors applied a collective vector-boson model with a broken SU(3) symmetry Hamiltonian containing three parameters (g₁, g₂, g₃) to eight rare earth nuclei (¹⁶⁴Dy, ¹⁶⁴⁻¹⁶⁸Er, ¹⁶⁸,¹⁷²Yb, ¹⁷⁶,¹⁷⁸Hf) and one actinide nucleus (²³⁸U). They performed root mean square (RMS) fits of the ground state band and γ-band energy levels and B(E2) transition ratios across SU(3) irreps in the range 10 ≤ λ ≤ 160 and 2 ≤ μ ≤ 8, then analyzed the resulting σ_E and σ_B values as functions of the quantum numbers.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁶⁸Er (ΔE₂ = 9.3), the best energy description corresponds to the multiplet (20,2) with σ_E = 3.2 keV and σ_B = 0.28.
- For ¹⁶⁴Dy (ΔE₂ = 9.4), the best fit is at (16,2) with σ_E = 14.1 keV and σ_B = 0.52.
- For ¹⁶⁶Er (ΔE₂ = 8.8), the best fit is at (16,2) with σ_E = 5.8 keV and σ_B = 0.47.
- For ¹⁷²Yb (ΔE₂ = 17.6), no minimum is found; σ_E saturates at large λ ≥ 80 with σ_E ≈ 6.5 keV and σ_B = 0.12.
- For ²³⁸U (ΔE₂ = 22.6), the best fit occurs at λ ≥ 60 with σ_E = 1.6 keV and σ_B = 0.08.
- The splitting ratio ΔE₂ varies from 7 to 18 in the rare earth region and from 13 to 25 in the actinide region.

## Context
This work extends the vector-boson model with broken SU(3) symmetry to a systematic survey of deformed nuclei, building on prior SU(3) descriptions of rotational nuclei (Minkov et al., 1995). The results show that nuclei with small ΔE₂ (e.g., ¹⁶⁴⁻¹⁶⁸Er, ¹⁶⁴Dy, ¹⁶⁸Yb) favor low λ values (16-20) with strong band mixing, while nuclei with large ΔE₂ (e.g., ¹⁷²Yb, ¹⁷⁶Hf, ²³⁸U) favor large λ values (≥60-80) with weak band mixing, approaching the group contraction limit where SU(3) reduces to the triaxial rotor group T₅∧SO(3). This suggests a possible transition from the broken SU(3) scheme to the pure SU(3) scheme of the Interacting Boson Model (Arima and Iachello, 1975), which could be understood within the larger dynamical symmetry group Sp(6,ℝ).
