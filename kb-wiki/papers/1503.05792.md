# Broad resonances and beta-decay

> Beta-decay into broad resonances gives a distorted lineshape in the observed energy spectrum. Part of the distortion arises from the phase space factor, but we show that the beta-decay matrix element may also contribute. Based on a schematic model for p-wave continuum neutron states it is argued that beta-decay directly to the continuum should be considered as a possible contributing mechanism in many decays close to the driplines. The signatures in R-matrix fits for such decays directly to continuum states are discussed and illustrated through an analysis of the beta-decay of $^8$B into $2^+$ states in $^8$Be.

## Key claim

The paper argues that beta-decay directly to the continuum should be considered as a possible contributing mechanism in many decays close to the driplines, and that the beta-decay matrix element itself contributes to the distorted lineshape of broad resonances, not just the phase space factor.

## Method

The authors constructed a schematic single-particle model for p-wave continuum neutron states in a square well potential of radius 4 fm, varying the potential depth and initial state binding energy. They compared four different resonance definitions: the phase shift reaching pi/2, the resonant phase shift reaching pi/2, the maximum of the elastic cross-section, and the maximum of the beta-strength distribution. They then calculated the differential beta-strength B(E) as the product of the squared overlap matrix element between an initial bound p-wave neutron and the final continuum p-wave function, multiplied by the neutron phase space factor.

## Key numbers

- For a square well of radius 4.0 fm and potential depth 55.0 MeV, the resonance energy according to the elastic cross-section maximum is 0.266 MeV, while the beta-strength maximum (for initial binding energy 1 MeV) is 0.436 MeV.
- For potential depth 53.0 MeV, the elastic cross-section maximum is at 1.518 MeV, while the beta-strength maximum (for initial binding energy 1 MeV) is at 1.154 MeV.
- For potential depth 51.0 MeV, the elastic cross-section maximum is at 3.05 MeV, while the beta-strength maximum (for initial binding energy 1 MeV) is at 1.831 MeV.
- For the beta decay of 14Be, a neutron line of energy 288(1) keV and width 49(2) keV was reported, while a later 14Be(p,n)14B reaction experiment reported a level 304(4) keV above threshold with width 160(20) keV.
- For a potential depth of 55.4 MeV, elastic scattering gives a peak position at 305 keV with FWHM of 204 keV (single-particle strength), while for an initial binding energy in the range 0.5-1.0 MeV the beta strength peaks about 15 keV lower.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of broad resonances and their definitions found in earlier treatments by Blatt and Weisskopf (1952), Lane and Thomas (1958), and Dalitz (1970). It builds on the authors' previous work (Riisager et al. 2014) on beta-decay lineshapes and connects to R-matrix theory as developed by Barker (1994) for beta-decays. The paper enables improved analysis of beta-delayed particle emission from nuclei near the driplines, particularly for cases where the initial state is loosely bound, and suggests that parameters extracted from beta-decay data may not be directly transferable to modeling other nuclear processes proceeding through the same reaction channels.
