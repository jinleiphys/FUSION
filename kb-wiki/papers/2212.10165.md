# Conformality and percolation threshold in neutron stars

> Speed of sound is given attention in multi-messenger astronomy as it encodes information of the dense matter equation of state. Recently the trace anomaly was proposed as a more informative quantity. In this work, we statistically determine the speed of sound and trace anomaly and show that they are driven to their conformal values at the centers of maximally massive neutron stars. We show that the local peak in the speed of sound can be associated with deconfinement along with percolation conditions in QCD matter.

## Key claim

The authors statistically determine that the speed of sound and trace anomaly at the centers of maximally massive neutron stars are driven to their conformal values (c_s^2 = 1/3 and Δ = 0), and that the local peak in the speed of sound can be associated with deconfinement and percolation conditions in QCD matter, rather than a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct an ensemble of 4.62 × 10^5 equations of state (EoSs) using a piecewise-linear speed-of-sound parametrization introduced by Annala (2019). They impose consistency with perturbative QCD results down to approximately 40 times nuclear saturation density, the maximum-mass constraint M_TOV ≥ (2.08 ± 0.07) M_⊙ from the pulsar J0740+6620, and the tidal deformability constraint Λ_1.4 = 190^{+390}_{-120} from the GW170817 event. They then compute probability distribution functions for the speed of sound, trace anomaly, and net-baryon number susceptibility as functions of energy density.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at the peak of the speed of sound: ε_peak = 0.559^{+0.110}_{-0.088} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ confidence level
- Net-baryon density at the peak of the speed of sound: n_B,peak = 0.54^{+0.09}_{-0.07} fm^{-3} at 1σ confidence level
- Median central energy density of maximally massive neutron stars: ε_TOV = 1.163^{+0.107}_{-0.97} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ confidence level
- Speed of sound at the center of maximally massive neutron stars: c_s,TOV^2 = 0.28 ± 0.06 at 1σ confidence level
- Trace anomaly at the center of maximally massive neutron stars: Δ_TOV = -0.01 ± 0.03 at 1σ confidence level
- Critical percolation density from hadron size: n_c^per = 0.57^{+0.12}_{-0.09} fm^{-3}, using proton mass radius R_0 = 0.80 ± 0.05 fm
- Hadron density from Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV: n_c = 0.596 ± 0.065 fm^{-3}
- Median energy density at the global maximum of the dimensionless second-order cumulant of net-baryon density: ε_χ = 4.084^{+1.834}_{-1.275} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ confidence level

## Context

This work extends the statistical analysis of the neutron star equation of state performed by Annala (2019) and Altiparmak (2022), and builds on the proposal by Fujimoto (2022) that the trace anomaly scaled by energy density is a measure of conformality. The authors connect the non-monotonic behavior of the speed of sound to the percolation threshold of hadrons, as developed by Braun-Munzinger (2014) and others, and to the quarkyonic matter description of dense matter by McLerran (2007, 2018). The results enable future work linking multi-messenger astronomy observations to fundamental QCD properties such as chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement in neutron star cores.
