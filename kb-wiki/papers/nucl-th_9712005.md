# Mesonic and Binding Contributions to the Nuclear Drell-Yan Process

> We have evaluated the Drell-Yan cross section in nuclei paying special attention to the meson cloud contribution from pion and rho-meson, for which an accurate calculation using the meson nuclear spectral functions is used. Similarly, the nucleonic contribution is evaluated in terms of a relativistic nucleon spectral function. Fair agreement with experiment is found for different nuclei and the results show a sizeable contribution from the renormalized meson cloud. In order to reproduce the experiment a novel element is introduced, consisting of a gradual energy loss of the incoming proton in its pass through the nucleus which produces a strong A dependence at x_1 large.

# Mesonic and Binding Contributions to the Nuclear Drell-Yan Process

## Key claim

The meson cloud (pion and rho-meson) contributes sizeably to the nuclear Drell-Yan cross section, but agreement with experiment requires introducing a gradual energy loss of the incoming proton as it traverses the nucleus, a 3.5% fractional energy loss per collision, which produces a strong A dependence at large x₁ and explains why the expected mesonic enhancement is not observed in the data.

## Method

The Drell-Yan cross section for proton-nucleus collisions was evaluated using relativistic nucleon and meson spectral functions for the target nucleus, with the nucleonic contribution computed from a hole spectral function and the mesonic contributions from the imaginary parts of the in-medium pion and rho-meson propagators. The incoming proton's energy loss was modeled as an exponential decay with path length through the nucleus, using a fitted fractional loss β = 0.035 per nucleon-nucleon collision, with the total NN cross section taken as 40 mb. Results were averaged over experimental acceptance windows (4 GeV ≤ √Q² ≤ 9 GeV and √Q² ≥ 11 GeV) to avoid quarkonium resonances.

## Key numbers

- Fractional energy loss per collision: β = 0.035 (3.5%), fitted to reproduce one data point for ⁵⁶Fe at x₂ = 0.15
- Total NN cross section used: σ_NN = 40 mb
- Beam energy: E₁ ≈ 800 GeV
- For x₁ = 0.01, the ratio R = 2 dσ_A / A dσ_D shows very weak A dependence and values similar to the EMC effect
- For x₁ = 0.7, the ratio R shows strong A dependence, with heavier nuclei giving larger reductions due to energy loss
- At small x₂ with x₁ − x₂ = 0.26, energy loss reduces the cross section by about 10%
- The pion cloud contribution is slightly larger than the rho-meson cloud contribution in all nuclei studied (¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Fe)
- Differences between using the weighed cross section and the average x₁ − x₂ = 0.26 approximation are smaller than 10%

## Context

This work extends the approach of Marco and Oset (1996) on the EMC effect, using realistic meson spectral functions rather than the static approximation, to the Drell-Yan process. It contradicts earlier predictions by authors such as Berger and Coester (1987) and others who expected a sharp enhancement of the DY ratio at large x₁ from pionic effects alone, which was not seen in the experiment of Alde et al. (1990). The energy loss mechanism introduced here (β = 0.035 per collision) is consistent with independent estimates from J/ψ propagation studies (equivalent to 3.6% per collision) and with quark energy loss calculations (about 2.5% per collision for incoming quarks), and it enables future DY experiments at large x₁ to test the predicted strong A dependence.
