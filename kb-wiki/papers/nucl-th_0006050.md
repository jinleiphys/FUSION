# On the Delta-Delta component of the deuteron in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei

> The probability P(Delta-Delta) to find the Delta-Delta component inside the deuteron, where Delta stands for the Delta(1232) resonance, is calculated in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei. We obtain P(Delta-Delta) = 0.3%. This prediction agrees good with the experimental estimate P(Delta-Delta) < 0.4% at 90% of CL (D. Allasia et al., Phys. Lett. B174 (1986) 450).

## Key claim

The probability to find a Delta-Delta component inside the deuteron is calculated as 0.3% in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei, which agrees with the experimental upper limit of less than 0.4% at 90% confidence level, but this result is obtained at leading order in the large N_C expansion and for the Delta(1232) resonance on-mass shell, where the off-shell parameter Z does not contribute.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Delta-Delta-D interaction Lagrangian within the nuclear Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NNJL) model, using a one-pion exchange approximation for the N+N to Delta+Delta transition. They evaluated the effective coupling constants by integrating over nucleon loop diagrams in the large N_C expansion, neglecting momenta of interacting particles relative to the nucleon mass. The probability P(Delta-Delta) was then computed as the ratio of the differential decay rate for D to Delta+Delta to that for D to n+p, evaluated at leading order in the large N_C expansion with the cutoff Lambda set equal to the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- P(Delta-Delta) = 0.3% for the Delta(1232) resonance on-mass shell in the NNJL model, obtained at cutoff Lambda = M_N = 940 MeV.
- Experimental upper limit: P(Delta-Delta) < 0.4% at 90% confidence level (Allasia et al., 1986).
- Nucleon mass M_N = 940 MeV; Delta(1232) resonance mass M_Delta = 1232 MeV.
- Deuteron electric quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.286 fm, used to define the npD coupling constant g_V.
- Parameter Z = 0.473 reproduces the experimental cross section for neutron-proton radiative capture for thermal neutrons; at Z = 1/2 the agreement is within 3%.

## Context

This work extends the NNJL model, previously applied to low-energy nuclear reactions such as neutron-proton radiative capture and solar proton burning (Ivanov et al., 1995-1999), to the calculation of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the deuteron. The result P(Delta-Delta) = 0.3% agrees with the potential model approach of Dymarz and Khanna (1990), who found approximately 0.4-0.5%, and is consistent with the experimental bound from Allasia et al. (1986). The approach enables future calculations of the Delta-Delta component's contribution to amplitudes of low-energy nuclear reactions involving the deuteron, though the authors note that decomposition into specific orbital momentum states is not yet performed in a relativistically covariant manner.
