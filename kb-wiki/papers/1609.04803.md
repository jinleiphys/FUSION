# Hydrodynamization in kinetic theory: Transient modes and the gradient expansion

> We explore the transition to hydrodynamics in a weakly-coupled model of quark-gluon plasma given by kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation with conformal symmetry. We demonstrate that the gradient expansion in this model has a vanishing radius of convergence due to the presence of a transient (nonhydrodynamic) mode, in a way similar to results obtained earlier in strongly-coupled gauge theories. This suggests that the mechanism by which hydrodynamic behaviour emerges is the same, which we further corroborate by a novel comparison between solutions of different weakly and strongly coupled models. However, in contrast with other known cases, we find that not all the singularities of the analytic continuation of the Borel transform of the gradient expansion correspond to transient excitations of the microscopic system: some of them reflect analytic properties of the kinetic equation when the proper time is continued to complex values.

# Hydrodynamization in kinetic theory: Transient modes and the gradient expansion

## Key claim

The gradient expansion in relaxation time approximation (RTA) kinetic theory has a vanishing radius of convergence due to a transient nonhydrodynamic mode, similar to strongly-coupled gauge theories; however, not all singularities in the Borel transform of the gradient expansion correspond to physical transient excitations, some instead reflect analytic properties of the kinetic equation when proper time is continued to complex values.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) with conformal symmetry for Bjorken flow, expanding the distribution function and pressure anisotropy in a hydrodynamic gradient series in powers of the inverse dimensionless variable w = Tτ. They computed the expansion coefficients analytically up to order 426, then performed a Borel transform and Padé approximant analysis to locate singularities in the complex Borel plane. The analytic structure of the integral equation for the energy density was examined to interpret the origin of these singularities.

## Key numbers

- Leading gradient expansion coefficients: a₁ = 8/5 γ, a₂ = 32/105 γ², a₃ = −416/525 γ³
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = γ/5
- Branch point closest to origin in Borel plane: ξ₀ = 1.5000/γ (up to 5 decimal places)
- Complex singularities in Borel plane: ξ± ≈ (2.25016 ± 1.29898 i)/γ
- Zero-momentum transient mode decay rate: ω₀ = 1/γ
- Hydrodynamization occurs at rescaled variable w̃ ≡ w/(4π η/s) ≈ 1 across models
- Pressure anisotropy at hydrodynamization: 𝒜 ≈ 0.6–0.8
- EKT simulation used λ = 10 corresponding to η/s ≈ 0.642; holography used η/s = 1/(4π)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gradient expansion divergence previously found in strongly-coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory via holography (Heller 2013) and in hydrodynamic theories (Heller 2015, Basar 2015) to the weakly-coupled regime of kinetic theory. The finding that the divergence mechanism is the same, decay of nonhydrodynamic modes, suggests universality of hydrodynamization across coupling strengths. The paper also provides a novel comparison between RTA, effective kinetic theory (EKT) from Kurkela (2015), and holographic simulations from Jankowski (2014), showing semi-quantitative agreement when η/s is matched. The identification of unphysical Borel singularities arising from analytic continuation of the kinetic equation clarifies earlier work by Bazow (2015) and connects to resurgence phenomena studied by Dunne and Aniceto.
