# On attributes of a Rotating Neutron star with a Hyperon core

> We study the effect of rotation on global properties of neutron star with a hyperon core in an effective chiral model with varying nucleon effective mass within a mean field approach. The resulting gross properties of the rotating compact star sequences are then compared and analyzed with other theoretical predictions and observations from neutron stars. The maximum mass of the compact star predicted by the model lies in the range $(1.4-2.4) ~M_{\odot}$ at Kepler frequency $Ω_K$, which is consistant with recent observation of high mass stars thereby reflecting the sensitivity of the underlying nucleon effective mass in the dense matter EoS. We also discuss the implications of the experimental constraints from the flow data from heavy-ion collisions on the global properties of the rotating neutron stars.

# On attributes of a Rotating Neutron star with a Hyperon core

## Key claim

The maximum mass of a rotating neutron star with a hyperon core, computed in an effective chiral model with varying nucleon effective mass, lies in the range (1.4–2.4) solar masses at the Kepler frequency, but this range is conditional on the nucleon effective mass parameter: only the stiffest equation of state (set I, with m*/m = 0.80) reaches 2.4 solar masses, while the softest (set III, with m*/m = 0.90) yields only 1.4 solar masses, and the intermediate set II (m*/m = 0.85) gives 1.9 solar masses.

## Method

The authors employed a chiral sigma model Lagrangian with nonlinear scalar field interactions, generalized to include the lowest baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) interacting via sigma, omega, and rho mesons in a mean-field approximation. They solved the self-consistent field equations for beta-equilibrated, charge-neutral hyperonic matter at zero temperature, then computed rotating neutron star sequences using the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method implemented in the RNS code, integrating up to the Kepler (mass-shedding) frequency. Three parameter sets were used, all with fixed incompressibility K = 300 MeV but varying nucleon effective mass ratios m*/m = 0.80, 0.85, and 0.90, chosen to be consistent with experimental flow data from heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational masses at Kepler frequency: 2.4 solar masses (set I, m*/m = 0.80), 1.9 solar masses (set II, m*/m = 0.85), and 1.4 solar masses (set III, m*/m = 0.90)
- Central density at maximum mass for rotating stars: 7.0 x 10^14 g cm^-3 for sets I and II, and 8.0 x 10^14 g cm^-3 for set III, corresponding to (2.5–3.0) times nuclear saturation density
- Maximum baryonic masses at Kepler frequency: 2.56 solar masses (set I), 2.11 solar masses (set II), and 1.47 solar masses (set III)
- Rotation increases stellar mass by 17–20% compared to static counterparts for all three parameter sets
- Radii for the three equations of state lie in the range 24–26 km, giving compactness M/R in the range (0.05–0.09) solar masses per km
- Hyperons begin appearing at approximately 2 times nuclear saturation density for all parameter sets
- Nuclear saturation properties: energy per nucleon -16.3 MeV at saturation density 0.153 fm^-3, symmetry energy J = 32 MeV, incompressibility K = 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous static neutron star analysis (Jha, Mishra, and Sreekanth, 2006) by adding rotation up to the Kepler frequency, and builds on earlier chiral model studies of dense matter (Prakash, 1992; Dutta, 2004; Sahu, 2004). The results are compared with observations of massive neutron stars such as PSR J0751+1807 (2.1 +/- 0.2 solar masses), 4U 1636-536 (2.0 +/- 0.1 solar masses), and Vela X-1 (1.86 +/- 0.16 solar masses), showing that only parameter sets with lower nucleon effective mass (m*/m = 0.80–0.85) can accommodate these high masses. The equations of state are constrained by experimental flow data from heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz, 2002), which favor softer equations of state. The work enables future studies of how rotational properties of neutron stars can further restrict the nucleon effective mass parameter and hyperon coupling strengths in dense matter.
