# New perspectives on spectroscopic factor quenching from reactions

> The evolution of single-particle strengths as the neutron-to-proton asymmetry changes informs us of the importance of short- and long-range correlations in nuclei and has therefore been extensively studied for the last two decades. Surprisingly, the strong asymmetry dependence of these strengths and their extreme values for highly-asymmetric nuclei inferred from knockout reaction measurements on a target nucleus are not consistent with what is extracted from electron-induced, transfer, and quasi-free reaction data, constituting a two-decade old puzzle. This work presents the first consistent analysis of one-nucleon transfer and one-nucleon knockout data, in which theoretical uncertainties associated with the nucleon-nucleus effective interactions considered in the reaction models are quantified using a Bayesian analysis. Our results demonstrate that, taking into account these uncertainties, the spectroscopic strengths of loosely-bound nucleons extracted from both probes agree with each other and, although there are still discrepancies for deeply-bound nucleons, the slope of the asymmetry dependence of the single-particle strengths inferred from transfer and knockout reactions are consistent within $1σ$. Both probes are consistent with a small asymmetry dependence of these strengths. The uncertainties obtained in this work represent a lower bound and are already significantly larger than the original estimates.

# New perspectives on spectroscopic factor quenching from reactions

## Key claim
When theoretical uncertainties from nucleon-nucleus optical potentials are quantified using Bayesian statistics, the spectroscopic strengths extracted from one-nucleon transfer and one-nucleon knockout reactions agree for loosely-bound nucleons, and the slope of the asymmetry dependence of single-particle strengths from both probes is consistent within 1σ. However, discrepancies remain for deeply-bound nucleons, and the uncertainties obtained are a lower bound already significantly larger than original estimates.

## Method
The authors reanalyzed transfer data for 34,36,46Ar(p,d)33,35,45Ar(g.s.) at 33A MeV using the Adiabatic Wave Approximation (ADWA) and knockout data for 32,34,46Ar+9Be at 65.1A and 70A MeV using the eikonal method. They quantified uncertainties from the nucleon-nucleus optical potentials by sampling posterior distributions from the KDUQ global parameterization for transfer and from a Bayesian analysis of mock n-9Be elastic scattering data for knockout, propagating these to obtain credible intervals for angular distributions and momentum distributions.

## Context
This work addresses the two-decade-old puzzle of inconsistent spectroscopic factor quenching between knockout reactions (Gade 2008, Gade 2014, Gade 2021) and other probes including electron-induced (Kramer 2001), quasi-free (Gomez-Ramos 2018), and transfer reactions (Tostevin 2009, Lee 2006). Previous uncertainty estimates for transfer (NunesTr11) lacked rigorous statistical analysis. The results enable future consistent comparisons with (p,2p), (p,pn), and (e,e'p) data once similar uncertainty quantification is performed, and motivate improved optical potentials constrained by dispersion relations and charge-exchange data.
