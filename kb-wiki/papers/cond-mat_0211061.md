# Uniformity of the phase space and fluctuations in thermal equilibrium

> General relations are found between the measure of the uniformity of distributions on the phase space and the first moments and correlations of extensive variables for systems close to thermal equilibrium. The role played by the parameter of the Renyi entropy for the analysis of their fluctuations and correlations is studied. Analytical results are verified and illustrated by direct simulations of quantum systems of ideal fermions and bosons. Problems of finite statistics, usual in experiments and simulations, are addressed and discussed and solved by finding unbiased estimators for Renyi entropies and uniformities.

## Key claim

The paper establishes general relations between the Beck uniformity measure of phase-space distributions and the first moments, variances, and covariances of extensive thermodynamic variables (energy and particle number) for systems in or near thermal equilibrium, with the caveat that these relations hold specifically when the system is in a stationary state where temperature is a meaningful non-random parameter.

## Method

The authors derive analytical expressions linking the Renyi entropy \(I_q\) and the Beck uniformity \(\gamma_q\) to thermodynamic quantities such as specific heat and energy fluctuations in the canonical ensemble, and to variances of \(\beta E - \mu_c N\) in the grand canonical ensemble. They then construct unbiased estimators for \(p_i^q\) and for \(\gamma_q\) to handle finite-statistics problems in experiments and simulations. These theoretical results are verified by direct Monte Carlo simulations of ideal fermion (spin-1/2) and boson (spin-0) systems in a cubic box, using grand-canonical occupation-number sampling.

## Key numbers

* For fermions: system of \(N=10\) particles with \(M=12\) states, simulated with 10,000 configurations.
* For bosons: system of \(N=10\) particles with \(M=4\) states, simulated with 10,000 configurations at two temperatures: \(T=0.6\) K and \(T=3.6\) K.
* The Bose-Einstein condensation temperature for the boson system is 0.8 K.
* Simulations were performed for \(q\) in the range \([0.01, 10]\) in steps of 0.15.
* The maximum of \(\gamma_q = 1\) occurs at \(q=1\) for both fermions and bosons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Wislicki, 2001) that linked equilibrium thermodynamics to phase-space uniformity, which was originally formulated by Beck (1990) in the context of multifractal sets. The paper provides a practical method to determine the equilibrium temperature of a system from data taken at unknown temperature by fitting the shape of uniformity curves, which is particularly relevant for systems where temperature is not directly measurable, such as remnants of nuclear disassembly processes in high-energy collisions or stellar evolution remnants. The approach also enables the study of fluctuations and correlations with reduced systematic bias from background events by using high-\(q\) values to probe regions of the phase space with higher signal-to-noise ratio.
