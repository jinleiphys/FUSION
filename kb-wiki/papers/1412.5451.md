# MesonNet 2014 International Workshop. Mini-proceedings

> The MesonNet International Workshop was held in the Laboratori Nazionali di Frascati from September the 29th to October the 1st, 2014, being the concluding meeting of the MesonNet research network within EU HadronPhysics3 project. MesonNet is a research network focused on light meson physics gathering experimentalist and theoreticians from Europe and abroad. An overview of the research projects related to the scope of the network is presented in these mini-proceedings.

## Key claim

The workshop served as the concluding meeting of the MesonNet research network within the EU HadronPhysics3 project, reviewing and summarizing research on light meson physics, but the mini-proceedings present only an overview of the research projects related to the network's scope rather than a single unified result or claim.

## Method

The workshop consisted of 31 talks and 14 posters, organized by a program committee, covering light meson dynamics and decays, meson baryon interactions, and studies related to fundamental particle physics problems such as rare decays, the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model. The network coordinated light meson studies at European accelerator facilities including COSY (Jülich), DAΦNE (Frascati), ELSA (Bonn), GSI (Darmstadt), and MAMI (Mainz), with additional participants from experiments at VEPP-2000 (BINP), CEBAF (JLAB), and heavy flavor-factories (Babar, Belle II, BESIII).

## Key numbers

Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This workshop concludes the MesonNet network (2012-2014), which was a continuation and extension of the PrimeNet network (2009-2011) as described in Hoistadt (2011) and Adlarson (2012). The meeting builds upon two previous MesonNet workshops: a workshop on Meson Transition Form Factors in Cracow, Poland, May 2012 (Czerwinski, 2012) and an international workshop in Prague, June 2013 (Amaryan, 2013). The network's work enables future progress in determining light quark mass ratios from η→3π decays, improving calculations of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, and constraining physics beyond the Standard Model through rare meson decays; a proposal for a new EU network called "Hadron Precision Physics" is mentioned as a common initiative with the Working Group on Radiative Corrections and MC Generators for Low Energies (Actis, 2010).
