# Annihilation Mechanisms of Lepton Pair Production in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> The pion and quark annihilation mechanisms of dilepton production during relativistic nuclear collisions are studied. We focus on the modifications caused by the specific features of in-medium pion and quark states rather than by medium modification of the $ρ$-meson spectral density. The main ingredient emerging in our approach is a form-factor of the multi-pion (multi-quark) system. Replacing the usual delta-function the form-factor plays the role of distribution which, in some sense, "connects" the total 4-momenta of the annihilating and outgoing particles. The difference between the c.m.s. velocities attributed to annihilating and outgoing particles is a particular consequence of this replacement and results in the appearance of a new factor in the formula for the lepton pair production rate. We obtained that the form-factor of the multi-pion (multi-quark) system causes broadening of the rate which is most pronounced for small invariant masses, in particular, we obtain a growth of the rate for the invariant masses below two masses of the annihilating particles.

# Annihilation Mechanisms of Lepton Pair Production in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed enhancement of dilepton production at low invariant masses in relativistic nuclear collisions can be explained by medium-induced modifications of pion and quark states, specifically their confinement to a finite space-time volume, rather than by modifications of the ρ-meson spectral density. The key mechanism is a form-factor of the multi-pion or multi-quark system that replaces the usual delta-function for energy-momentum conservation, causing a broadening of the production rate that is most pronounced for invariant masses below two times the mass of the annihilating particles.

## Method

The authors model the pion (or quark) system as confined to a finite volume with a finite lifetime, using wave functions that satisfy Dirichlet boundary conditions on the surface of the reaction region. They calculate the S-matrix element for the annihilation chain π⁺π⁻ → ρ → γ* → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ (or qq̄ → γ* → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻) using a Gaussian form-factor ρ(x) = exp[(x² − 2(u·x)²)/(2R²)] to represent the spatial and temporal distribution of the emitting source. The dilepton production rate is then obtained by integrating over the pion (quark) and lepton phase spaces with thermal averaging, incorporating a correction factor from the Lorentz transformation between the pion-pion and lepton-pair center-of-mass systems.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the hot pion system used in evaluations: T = 180 MeV
- Enhancement of the dilepton production rate is observed for invariant masses M ≤ 800 MeV/c² in pion-pion annihilation
- Enhancement of the dilepton production rate is observed for invariant masses M ≤ 500 MeV/c² in quark-antiquark annihilation
- The real threshold for quark-antiquark annihilation is close to M = 2m_q ≈ 10 MeV/c² (two quark masses)
- The enhancement is more sensitive to variation in the spatial size R of the system than to variation in the system lifetime τ

## Context

This work addresses the enhancement of dilepton production with invariant masses of 200–800 MeV observed by the CERES collaboration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It extends the approach of Knoll (2000) and the author's own prior work (Anchishkin 2002, 2004, 2005) by focusing on modifications of pion and quark wave functions due to finite system size and lifetime, rather than on medium modifications of the ρ-meson spectral density as studied by other groups. The results show a similar tendency to the CERES data and suggest that quantum fluctuations in small systems can explain the low-mass dilepton enhancement, enabling further studies of the relationship between system size and dilepton spectra in both hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma phases.
