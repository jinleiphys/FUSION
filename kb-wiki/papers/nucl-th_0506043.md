# Random-phase approximation based on relativistic point-coupling models

> The matrix equations of the random-phase approximation (RPA) are derived for the point-coupling Lagrangian of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. Fully consistent RMF plus (quasiparticle) RPA illustrative calculations of the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole response of spherical medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, test the phenomenological effective interactions of the point-coupling RMF model. A comparison with experiment shows that the best point-coupling effective interactions accurately reproduce not only ground-state properties, but also data on excitation energies of giant resonances.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the point-coupling PC-F1 effective interaction, adjusted only to ground-state data, can reproduce giant resonance excitation energies in spherical nuclei, but the systematic overestimate of isoscalar monopole resonance energies suggests the nuclear matter compression modulus should be closer to 250 MeV rather than the PC-F1 value of 270 MeV.

## Method

The authors derive the relativistic random-phase approximation (RRPA) matrix equations from the point-coupling Lagrangian of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, including the Dirac sea of negative-energy states. They perform fully consistent RMF plus (quasiparticle) RPA calculations of the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole response in spherical medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, using the PC-F1 effective interaction with 9 coupling parameters adjusted to ground-state observables of 17 spherical nuclei. For open-shell nuclei, they employ the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the Gogny D1S pairing force in the particle-particle channel.

## Key numbers

- PC-F1 compression modulus: \(K_\infty = 270\) MeV
- ISGMR peak in \(^{208}\)Pb: \(E = 14.16\) MeV excitation energy
- Experimental ISGMR centroid in \(^{208}\)Pb: \(m_1/m_0 = 13.96 \pm 0.20\) MeV (from Young et al.)
- PC-F1 volume asymmetry: \(a_4 = 37.8\) MeV
- IVGDR peak in \(^{208}\)Pb with PC-F1: \(E = 13.0\) MeV
- Experimental IVGDR in \(^{208}\)Pb: \(E = 13.3 \pm 0.1\) MeV (from Ritz et al.)
- Recommended compression modulus range from density-dependent meson-exchange interactions: \(K_\infty \approx 250 - 270\) MeV
- DD-ME1 compression modulus: \(K_\infty = 245\) MeV
- DD-ME2 compression modulus: \(K_\infty = 251\) MeV
- Constrained volume asymmetry interval from RMF models: \(32\) MeV \(\le a_4 \le 36\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic RPA framework from meson-exchange models (e.g., NL3 by Lalazissis et al. 1997, density-dependent interactions by Typel and Wolter 1999, Niksic et al. 2002) to point-coupling interactions, specifically testing the PC-F1 interaction adjusted by Burvenich et al. (2002). The results enable future constraints on point-coupling effective interactions by including giant resonance data, as previously done for density-dependent meson-exchange forces in Niksic et al. (2002) and Lalazissis et al. (2005). The analysis shows that ground-state data alone cannot fully determine the isovector channel, and that isovector dipole properties do not distinguish between PC-F1, PC-F2, and PC-F4 interactions because their asymmetry energies differ only at densities above saturation.
