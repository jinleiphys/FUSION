# Sensitivity of Jet Observables to Molière Scattering Off Quasiparticles in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is a strongly coupled liquid when viewed at length scales of order the inverse of its temperature and longer. However, when it is probed at short enough length scales, asymptotic freedom mandates the presence of quark- and gluon-like quasiparticles. Partons in jets can trigger perturbative, high momentum-exchange $2\rightarrow2$ Molière scatterings off quasiparticles in the medium, making jets useful probes of the microscopic structure of QGP. Prior to this work, soft strongly coupled momentum-exchanges between jet partons and the QGP droplet produced in a heavy-ion collision, as well as the wakes that jets excite in the droplet, had been accounted for in the Hybrid Model of jet quenching. Here, we present a full calculation of Molière scattering off a QGP quasiparticle which results in the deflection of the jet parton and the excitation of a parton from the thermal medium that recoils after being kicked, and describe how it is implemented in the Hybrid Model. The scattered jet and recoil partons continue to propagate through the QGP, lose energy and momentum, excite wakes, and may further re-scatter. Using the Hybrid Model, we study how Molière scatterings impact jet shapes and fragmentation functions, the Soft Drop angle $R_g$, jet girth $g$, and observables that focus on the number and angular distribution of subjets within jets. We demonstrate that photon-tagged jets provide a particularly sensitive probe: selecting events by the photon energy mitigates the selection bias inherent in inclusive jet measurements and enhances sensitivity to rare large-angle scatterings. We find that Molière scatterings broaden both the $R_g$ and $g$ distributions when jets significantly softer than the photon are included. Our results point the way towards distinctive model-independent experimental signatures of hard scattering of jet partons off quasiparticles in QGP.

## Key claim

Molière scatterings, rare, large-angle, high-momentum-transfer 2→2 elastic collisions between jet partons and thermal quasiparticles in QGP, broaden the Soft Drop angle R_g and jet girth g distributions for photon-tagged jets in PbPb collisions, but only when jets significantly softer than the photon (x_J > 0.2) are included; this provides a distinctive model-independent experimental signature of the particulate structure of QGP at short length scales.

## Method

The authors implemented perturbative 2→2 Molière scattering probabilities (calculated from leading-order QCD matrix elements, following D'Eramo et al. (2018)) into the Hybrid Model of jet quenching, which previously included only nonperturbative strongly-coupled energy loss, soft Gaussian transverse momentum broadening, and hydrodynamic wakes. At each timestep of in-medium parton shower evolution, the probability of a hard scattering was computed and, when it occurred, the subprocess type and final-state kinematics were sampled from the appropriate probability distributions subject to the constraint |t|, |u| > a m_D² (with a=10); the scattered jet parton and recoiling thermal parton were then tracked as they continued to lose energy, excite wakes, and potentially re-scatter.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for perturbative Molière scattering is set by requiring momentum transfer squared |t|, |u| > a m_D², with a typically chosen as a=10.
- Photon-tagged jets were selected with p_T^γ > 150 GeV and x_J ≡ p_T^jet/p_T^γ > 0.2 (inclusive sample) or x_J > 0.8 (biased sample).
- Jet reconstruction radii of R=0.2 and R=0.6 were studied.
- The dimensionless parameter κ_sc governing strongly-coupled energy loss was fitted to data and found to be smaller by a factor of 3 to 4 in QCD compared to the strongly coupled gauge theory where the energy loss formula was derived.
- For R=0.2 photon-tagged jets with x_J>0.2, Molière scattering broadens both the R_g and g distributions in PbPb relative to pp, overcoming the narrowing associated with jet selection bias.

## Context

This work extends the Hybrid Model (Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2014-2025) by adding perturbative hard scattering processes to what was previously a purely strongly-coupled description of jet-medium interactions. It contrasts with other Monte Carlo treatments (Zapp et al., 2008-2013; He et al., 2015; Milhano et al., 2017; Park et al., 2018) that include both soft and hard perturbative processes, making it difficult to isolate the effects of hard scattering alone. The results point toward experimental measurements of photon-tagged jet substructure (following CMS 2024 measurements of R_g and g for R=0.2 jets with p_T^γ>100 GeV and x_J>0.4) that could reveal the short-distance quasiparticle structure of QGP and help understand how a strongly coupled liquid emerges from an asymptotically free gauge theory.
