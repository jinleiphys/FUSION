# Local covariant density functional constrained by the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> The recent progress in the localized covariant density functional constrained by the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory is briefly presented by taking the Gamow-Teller resonance in 90Zr as an example. It is shown that the constraints introduced by the Fock terms into the particle-hole residual interactions are straight forward and robust.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a localized covariant density functional derived from the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory via zero-range approximation and Fierz transformation can reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance properties of the original non-local RHF approach, with the caveat that the zero-range approximation introduces a slight difference in the strength distribution.

## Method

The authors start from the RHF parametrization PKO2, which includes sigma, omega, and rho mesons but no pions, and apply a zero-range reduction to the finite-range Yukawa propagators followed by Fierz transformation to express the Fock terms as local Hartree terms. This yields a new parametrization called PKO2-H. They then perform random phase approximation (RPA) calculations using the particle-hole residual interactions from this localized RHF equivalent functional to compute the Gamow-Teller resonance strength distribution in 90Zr.

## Key numbers

* The Gamow-Teller resonance peak energy in 90Zr calculated with the localized RHF equivalent RPA (PKO2-H) reproduces the experimental peak energy from Wakasa (1997).
* The diagonal matrix elements of the dominant particle-hole configuration (nu1g9/2)^{-1}(pi1g7/2)^1 are decomposed into contributions from the isovector tensor (tT) and isovector pseudovector (tPV) channels, with the net contribution approximately proportional to (1/4)[(g_sigma^2/m_sigma^2) - (g_omega^2/m_omega^2)].
* The coupling strength g_rho^2 is much smaller than g_omega^2, and m_rho^2 is approximately equal to m_omega^2.

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) by addressing known problems in the isovector channels of widely used relativistic Hartree (RH) parametrizations. Prior RHF calculations by Long (2006, 2007, 2008, 2009) successfully reproduced proton-neutron effective mass splitting, shell structure evolutions, and spin-isospin resonances, but introduced non-local potentials that limited applicability to deformed nuclei and beyond-mean-field treatments. The localized RHF equivalent functional PKO2-H, introduced in Liang (2012b), maintains the advantages of existing RH functionals while solving isovector channel problems, enabling future development of nuclear local covariant density functionals with proper isoscalar and isovector properties.
