# The antikaon nuclear potential in hot and dense matter

> The antikaon optical potential in hot and dense nuclear matter is studied within the framework of a coupled-channel self-consistent calculation taking, as bare meson-baryon interaction, the meson-exchange potential of the Jülich group. Typical conditions found in heavy-ion collisions at GSI are explored. As in the case of zero temperature, the angular momentum components larger than L=0 contribute significantly to the finite temperature antikaon optical potential at finite momentum. It is found that the particular treatment of the medium effects has a strong influence on the behavior of the antikaon potential with temperature. Our self-consistent model, in which antikaons and pions are dressed in the medium, gives a moderately temperature dependent antikaon potential which remains attractive at GSI temperatures, contrary to what one finds if only nuclear Pauli blocking effects are included.

## Key claim

The antikaon optical potential in hot and dense nuclear matter, calculated with a fully self-consistent coupled-channel model that dresses both antikaons and pions in the medium, remains attractive at temperatures up to 70 MeV and densities up to twice nuclear saturation density, contrary to what is found if only nuclear Pauli blocking effects are included (which would give repulsion at finite temperature).

## Method

The authors extended their previous zero-temperature self-consistent coupled-channel calculation to finite temperature, using the meson-exchange potential of the Jülich group as the bare meson-baryon interaction. The G-matrix equation was solved including partial waves up to L=4, with temperature effects incorporated through a finite-temperature nucleon Fermi distribution, temperature-dependent nucleon self-energies from a sigma-omega model, and temperature-dependent pion self-energies from a model including 1p-1h, 1Δ-1h, and 2p-2h excitations. Self-consistency was demanded for the antikaon optical potential at the quasiparticle energy.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³) and T=0, the nucleon attractive potential at k_N=0 is approximately -80 MeV; at T=70 MeV it reduces to approximately -40 MeV.
- The nucleon energy spectrum at k_N=0 goes from 860 MeV at T=0 to 900 MeV at T=70 MeV at saturation density.
- At T=70 MeV and ρ₀, the real part of U_K(k_K=0) loses only about 10 MeV of attraction compared to T=0.
- The imaginary part of the antikaon optical potential at T=70 MeV increases by about 25% at k_K=0 and by 50% at momenta around 500 MeV/c when higher partial waves (L>0) are added to the L=0 contribution.
- At a momentum of 500 MeV/c, the contribution of the L>0 components to the antikaon optical potential is as large as that of the L=0 ones.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature self-consistent model (Tolos, Ramos, Polls, 2001) to finite temperature, incorporating pion dressing in the intermediate πY loops as was done in chiral model calculations by Lutz (1998) and Oset (2000). The results contradict the findings of Schaffner-Bielich (2000), who found that the antikaon potential becomes repulsive at T=30 MeV when only Pauli blocking effects are included, demonstrating that full self-consistency has a tremendous influence on the temperature dependence. The attractive potential found here for finite density, temperature, and momentum is relevant for understanding the enhanced K⁻/K⁺ ratio measured by the KaoS collaboration at GSI, together with other mechanisms such as enhanced K⁻ production through πY collisions.
