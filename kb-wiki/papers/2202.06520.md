# Doubly heavy tetraquarks in a chiral-diquark picture

> Energy spectrum of doubly heavy tetraquarks, $T_{QQ}$ ($QQ \bar{q} \bar{q}$ with $Q = c, b$ and $q = u, d, s$), is studied in the potential chiral-diquark model. Using the chiral effective theory of diquarks and the quark-diquark-based potential model, the $T_{bb}$, $T_{cc}$, and $T_{cb}$ tetraquarks are described as a three-body system composed of two heavy quarks and an antidiquark. We find several $T_{bb}$ bound states, while no $T_{cc}$ and $T_{cb}$ (deep) bound state is seen. We also study the change of the $T_{QQ}$ tetraquark masses under restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper finds several bound states for the doubly-bottom tetraquark \(T_{bb}\), but no deep bound state for the doubly-charmed \(T_{cc}\) or the bottom-charmed \(T_{cb}\) tetraquark, with the binding assessed relative to the strong-decay threshold of two heavy mesons; the claim is conditional on the specific chiral-diquark model and the nonrelativistic potential used.

## Method

The authors describe the doubly heavy tetraquark \(T_{QQ}\) (\(QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}\)) as a three-body system of two heavy quarks and an antidiquark, using a chiral effective theory for scalar/pseudoscalar and vector/axial-vector diquarks. They construct two-body potentials: the \(AP1\) potential for the heavy-quark–heavy-quark interaction (scaled by a color factor of 1/2) and the Y-potential for the heavy-quark–antidiquark interaction (also scaled by 1/2). The Schrödinger equation is solved with the Gaussian expansion method, considering \(S\)-wave and \(P\)-wave orbital states.

## Key numbers

- The \(1S\) ground state of the nonstrange \(T_{bb}\) tetraquark (\(T_{bb;\bar u \bar d}\)) has a binding energy of **115 MeV** below the experimental \(B\)-\(B^*\) threshold.
- The \(1S\) ground state of the strange \(T_{bb}\) tetraquark (\(T_{bb;\bar n \bar s}\)) has a binding energy of **28 MeV** below the experimental \(B_{(s)}\)-\(B^*\) threshold.
- No bound state is found for the \(T_{cc}\) tetraquarks in the same model.
- The scalar diquark masses used are: \(M_{ud}(0^+) = 725\) MeV, \(M_{ns}(0^+) = 942\) MeV, \(M_{ud}(0^-) = 1406\) MeV, \(M_{ns}(0^-) = 1271\) MeV.
- The vector diquark masses used include: \(M_{nn}(1^+) = 973\) MeV, \(M_{ud}(1^-) = 1447\) MeV, \(M_{ns}(1^-) = 1776\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory of diquarks previously applied to singly heavy baryons by Kim, Oka, and Suzuki (2021) to the tetraquark sector, using the same diquark mass parameters and potential models. It contradicts predictions of a stable \(T_{cc}\) tetraquark from some earlier quark-model studies, while agreeing with most lattice QCD simulations (e.g., Bicudo et al., 2017; Leskovec et al., 2019) and chromomagnetic interaction models that find a stable \(T_{bb}\) but no stable \(T_{cc}\). The results enable future studies of chiral symmetry restoration effects on tetraquark masses and provide a framework for analyzing the recently observed \(T_{cc}^+\) state from LHCb (2021).
