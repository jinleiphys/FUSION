# Stochastic reconstructions of spectral functions: Application to lattice QCD

> We present a detailed study of the applications of two stochastic approaches, stochastic optimization method (SOM) and stochastic analytical inference (SAI), to extract spectral functions from Euclidean correlation functions. SOM has the advantage that it does not require prior information. On the other hand, SAI is a more generalized method based on Bayesian inference. Under mean field approximation SAI reduces to the often-used maximum entropy method (MEM), and for a specific choice of the prior SAI becomes equivalent to SOM. To test the applicability of these two stochastic methods to lattice QCD, firstly, we apply these methods to various reasonably chosen model correlation functions, and present detailed comparisons of the reconstructed spectral functions obtained from SOM, SAI and MEM. Next, we present similar studies for charmonia correlation functions obtained from lattice QCD computations using clover-improved Wilson fermions on large, fine, isotropic lattices at $0.75$ and $1.5T_c$, $T_c$ being the deconfinement transition temperature of a pure gluon plasma. We find that SAI and SOM give consistent results to MEM at these two temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two stochastic methods, stochastic optimization method (SOM) and stochastic analytical inference (SAI), produce spectral functions consistent with the maximum entropy method (MEM) for charmonia correlation functions from lattice QCD at 0.75 and 1.5 times the deconfinement temperature of a pure gluon plasma, but the abstract omits that the authors find stochastic methods have larger dependences on the temporal extent at the current noise level.

## Method

The authors applied SOM and SAI to reconstruct spectral functions from Euclidean correlation functions, first testing them on model correlation functions designed to mimic charmonium spectral functions, then on lattice QCD charmonia correlation functions computed using clover-improved Wilson fermions on large, fine, isotropic lattices. SOM uses a modified simulated annealing algorithm with a "kink" condition to avoid overfitting, while SAI uses Bayesian inference with Monte Carlo sampling over spectral configurations weighted by a likelihood function and a prior probability, with the regularization parameter α integrated out via the Wang-Landau algorithm for the density of states. The results were compared to MEM, which selects the most probable solution via the Shannon-Jaynes entropy.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD charmonia correlation functions were computed at 0.75 and 1.5 times the deconfinement transition temperature of a pure gluon plasma (T_c).
- The correlation functions were obtained on large, fine, isotropic lattices with clover-improved Wilson fermions.
- In model tests, the number of imaginary-time data points N_τ was varied, with typical values around O(10) discrete points.
- The α range for parallel tempering in SAI was divided with a constant ratio α_{i+1}/α_i = R.
- For SOM, the α parameter was decreased exponentially from 10^9 to 10^-6 in model tests, with the kink point appearing around α ~ 1.

## Context

This work extends the application of stochastic methods (SOM and SAI), previously used in condensed matter physics, to lattice QCD spectral function reconstruction, which is an ill-posed inverse problem. It provides a systematic comparison with the commonly used maximum entropy method (MEM) as implemented by Asakawa (2000) and others, and reanalyzes charmonia spectral functions in gluon plasma previously studied by Ding (2012) using MEM. The methods enable more robust estimation of systematic uncertainties in spectral functions from lattice QCD, which are essential for understanding in-medium properties of hadrons and transport coefficients like heavy quark diffusion.
