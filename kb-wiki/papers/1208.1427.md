# Measurement of Event Plane Correlations in Pb-Pb Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$=2.76 TeV with the ATLAS Detector

> A measurement of correlations between event-plane angles $Φ_n$ is presented as a function of centrality for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\mathrm{NN}}}}=2.76$ TeV. These correlations are estimated from observed event-plane angles $Ψ_n$ obtained from charged particle or transverse energy flow measured over a large pseudorapidity range $|η|<4.8$, followed by a resolution correction that accounts for the dispersion of $Ψ_n$ relative to $Φ_n$. Various correlators involving two or three event planes with acceptable resolution are measured. Significant positive correlations are observed for $4(Φ_2-Φ_4)$, $6(Φ_2-Φ_6)$, $6(Φ_3-Φ_6)$, $2Φ_2+3Φ_3-5Φ_5$, $2Φ_2+4Φ_4-6Φ_6$ and $-10Φ_2+4Φ_4+6Φ_6$. However, the measured correlations for $2Φ_2-6Φ_3+4Φ_4$ are negative. These results may shed light on the patterns of the fluctuation of the created matter in the initial state as well as the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution.

## Key claim

The paper reports significant correlations between event-plane angles in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but these correlations are derived from observed event-plane angles that require a resolution correction to account for the dispersion of the measured angles relative to the true event-plane angles; the results are therefore conditional on the accuracy of that correction.

## Method

Event-plane angles were measured using charged particles or transverse energy flow over a large pseudorapidity range (|η| < 4.8) in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV recorded by the ATLAS detector. The observed event-plane angles were corrected for finite resolution to estimate the true event-plane angles, and various two- and three-plane correlators were computed as a function of collision centrality.

## Key numbers

* Positive correlations were observed for 4(Φ₂ − Φ₄), 6(Φ₂ − Φ₆), 6(Φ₃ − Φ₆), 2Φ₂ + 3Φ₃ − 5Φ₅, 2Φ₂ + 4Φ₄ − 6Φ₆, and −10Φ₂ + 4Φ₄ + 6Φ₆ in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.
* Negative correlations were observed for the correlator 2Φ₂ − 6Φ₃ + 4Φ₄ in the same collision system and energy.

## Context

This measurement extends earlier studies of anisotropic flow and event-plane correlations in heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) to the higher energy of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The results provide constraints on models of initial-state fluctuations and the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, and can be compared to predictions from event generators such as those used by the ATLAS Collaboration.
