# The Mini-Jet Scale and Energy Loss at RHIC in the HIJING and RQMD Models

> The original default mini-jet scale p_0=2 GeV/c and energy loss, dE/dx=2 GeV/fm in HIJING lead to a too rapid growth of the multiplicity with energy. RQMD model without mini-jet leads on the other hand to a too slowly increasing multiplicity with energy. We show that a slight increase of p_0 from the default 2.0 GeV/c at sqrt(s)=130A GeV to 2.2 GeV/c at sqrt{s}=200A GeV is sufficient to account for the bulk observables. Jet quenching of pi^0 above p_T>4 GeV/c at 200A GeV is found to be well reproduced in central Au+Au by the original HIJING default prediction. However, the effective surface emission in the HIJING model formulation of energy loss over-predicts the quenching in more peripheral collisions. Neither HIJING nor HIJING/B{bar}B can account for the observed anomalous excess of moderate p_T<4 GeV/c baryons. The agreement of RQMD with data in this p_T region is shown to be fortuitous because, without mini-jets, it fails to reproduce the p+p spectrum.

# Wiki Page: The Mini-Jet Scale and Energy Loss at RHIC in the HIJING and RQMD Models

## Key claim

A modest 10% increase of the mini-jet scale p0 from 2.0 GeV/c at sqrt(s)=130A GeV to 2.2 GeV/c at sqrt(s)=200A GeV is sufficient to account for bulk observables in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but only if the energy loss parameter dE/dx is also reduced from the default 2 GeV/fm; the default HIJING parameters over-predict the growth of multiplicity with energy, while RQMD without mini-jets under-predicts it.

## Method

The authors compared predictions of the HIJING v1.37 event generator (which includes pQCD mini-jet production with a separation scale p0 and a simplified energy loss algorithm) and the RQMD v2.4 model (which simulates only soft multi-particle production without mini-jets) against RHIC data for Au+Au and p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV and 200A GeV. They systematically varied the mini-jet scale p0 and the energy loss parameter dE/dx in HIJING to test whether adjustments could bring the models into agreement with measured charged particle rapidity densities, transverse energy distributions, and nuclear modification factors R_AA for pions and hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Default HIJING mini-jet scale p0 = 2.0 GeV/c at sqrt(s)=130A GeV, increased to p0 = 2.2 GeV/c at sqrt(s)=200A GeV (a 10% increase) to match bulk observables
- Default HIJING energy loss dE/dx = 2 GeV/fm for gluons (1 GeV/fm for quarks) with mean free path lambda = 1 fm
- HIJING with dE/dx = 2 GeV/fm and p0 = 2.0 GeV/c over-predicts the ratio R_{200/130} for midrapidity dN_ch/dη in central collisions by 30%
- RQMD predicts a very small increase in charged particle density per participant pair over sqrt(s)=56 GeV to 200 GeV, while HIJING predicts an increase of more than a factor of 1.3
- HIJING with default energy loss reproduces the factor of ~5 suppression of π0 at pT > 4 GeV/c in central Au+Au at 200A GeV
- HIJING over-predicts quenching in peripheral (60-80%) collisions, predicting 30% suppression not seen in data
- The hard scattering component to charged particle production remains almost constant at (36 ± 6)% over the energy range sqrt(s)=130A GeV to 200A GeV

## Context

This work extends the HIJING model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1992) and RQMD model (Sorge, 1995) to RHIC energies, testing predictions made before the data were available. The study is motivated by saturation models such as the EKRT final state saturation model (Eskola et al., 2001) and the KLN initial state saturation model (Dima et al., 2001-2003), which proposed energy-dependent mini-jet scales. The paper finds that neither HIJING nor HIJING/B̅B can account for the observed anomalous excess of moderate pT < 4 GeV/c baryons, and that RQMD's agreement with data in that region is fortuitous because it fails to reproduce the p+p spectrum. The results enable future refinements of energy loss algorithms and motivate more realistic 3+1D hydrodynamic simulations with boost-variant initial conditions.
