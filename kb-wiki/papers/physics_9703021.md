# Exact Solution of selfconsistent Vlasov equation

> An analytical solution of the selfconsistent Vlasov equation is presented. The time evolution is entirely determined by the initial distribution function. The largest Lyapunov exponent is calculated analytically. For special parameters of the model potential positive Lyapunov exponent is possible. This model may serve as a check for numerical codes solving selfconsistent Vlasov equations. The here presented method is also applicable for any system with analytical solution of the Hamilton equation for the formfactor of the potential.

# Exact Solution of selfconsistent Vlasov equation

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical solution of the selfconsistent Vlasov equation for a separable multipole-multipole interaction potential, showing that selfconsistency can generate positive Lyapunov exponents, but only for attractive potentials (μ < 0) and specific model parameters; repulsive potentials (μ > 0) yield only oscillatory solutions with no chaotic behavior.

## Method

The author solves the Vlasov equation by first finding the nonselfconsistent solution via Hamilton's equations for the single-particle trajectories, expressing the general solution as a function of the integration constants of these equations. Selfconsistency is then imposed by requiring that the mean-field potential Q(t) equals the integral of the form factor g(r) weighted by the distribution function, which reduces to a second-order differential equation for Q(t) that is solved analytically for the linear form factor g(r) = a_x x + a_y y + a_z z.

## Key numbers

- The largest Lyapunov exponent is given analytically as λ = √(-μ n a_i² / m) for μ < 0, where n is the density of the initial distribution, a_i are the form factor coefficients, and m is the particle mass.
- The selfconsistent potential Q(t) evolves as Q(t) = ⟨r⟩ cosh(λ t) + ⟨p/m⟩ (1/λ) sinh(λ t), where ⟨r⟩ and ⟨p/m⟩ are the initial mean position and mean momentum.
- The parameter λ is defined as λ = √(-μ n a_i² / m), with the condition μ < 0 required for a positive Lyapunov exponent.

## Context

This work extends the numerical study of intrinsic chaoticity in mean-field models by providing an exactly solvable analytical model, complementing the numerical approach of Bauer et al. (1994) who studied the same separable multipole-multipole force model. The analytical solution enables direct verification of numerical codes that solve the selfconsistent Vlasov equation, particularly for testing the emergence of chaotic behavior from selfconsistency. The method is applicable to any system where the Hamilton equations for the form factor of the potential can be solved analytically.
