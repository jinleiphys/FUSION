# Callan-Symanzik equations and low-energy theorems with trace anomalies

> Basing on some new and concise forms of the Callan-Symanzik equations, the low-energy theorems involving trace anomalies à la Novikov-Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov, first advanced and proved in Nucl. Phys. \textbf{B165}, 67 (1980), \textbf{B191}, 301 (1981), are proved as immediate consequences. The proof is valid in any consistent effective field theories and these low-energy theorems are hence generalized. Some brief discussions about related topics are given.

## Key claim

The low-energy theorems involving trace anomalies à la Novikov-Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov (NSVZ) are proved as immediate consequences of new concise forms of the Callan-Symanzik equations (CSE), with the proof valid in any consistent effective field theory (EFT) that can be regularized and renormalized, not only in QCD.

## Method

The author reformulates the Callan-Symanzik equations by introducing an underlying theory perspective, where the trace operator \(\tilde{\Theta}\) includes both the canonical trace of the EFT and the trace anomalies arising from renormalization (represented by underlying parameters or their EFT "agent" constants). Using the new CSE form \(\{\lambda \partial_\lambda - d_{\Gamma^{(n)}}\} \Gamma^{(n)} = i\Gamma^{(n)}_{\tilde{\Theta}}\), the author derives the low-energy theorem \(\Pi_{\tilde{\Theta}O}(0) = -d_O \langle \text{vac}|O(0)|\text{vac}\rangle\) by noting that the vacuum expectation value of an operator \(O\) is momentum-independent, and then generalizes to multi-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- For pure 4-dimensional gluodynamics with \(O = \tilde{\Theta} = \frac{\beta(g_s)}{4g_s} G^a_{\mu\nu} G^{a\mu\nu}\), the low-energy theorem gives \(\Pi_{\tilde{\Theta}\tilde{\Theta}}(0) = -4 \langle \text{vac}| \tilde{\Theta} |\text{vac}\rangle\), where \(d_{\tilde{\Theta}} = 4\).
- For a general EFT operator \(O\), the low-energy theorem is \(\Pi_{\tilde{\Theta}O}(0) = -d_O \langle \text{vac}|O(0)|\text{vac}\rangle\), with \(d_O\) the canonical scale dimension of \(O\).
- For QCD with massive quarks, the theorem is cast as \(\Pi_{\tilde{\Theta}_{\text{gluon}}O}(0) = -d_O \langle \text{vac}|O(0)|\text{vac}\rangle [1 + \mathcal{O}(m)]\), matching Eq.(52) of NSVZ (1981).

## Context

This work extends the original proofs of the NSVZ low-energy theorems (Novikov, Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov, 1980, 1981) by establishing their direct connection to the Callan-Symanzik equations, which was not obvious in the original treatments where renormalization was either ignored or handled via a special regularization. The proof generalizes the theorems to any consistent EFT beyond QCD, and the author suggests future exploration of connections between CSE and QCD evolution equations such as DGLAP and JIMWLK, as well as applications to low-energy EFTs of QCD and electroweak sectors.
