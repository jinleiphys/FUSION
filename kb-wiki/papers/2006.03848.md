# Search for CP-violating nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment using the LuOH$^+$ cation

> The CP-violating interaction of the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) of the $^{175}$Lu nucleus with electrons in the molecular cation LuOH$^+$ is studied. The resulting effect is expressed in terms of CP-odd parameters, such as quantum chromodynamics angle $\barθ$, quark electric dipole moment (EDM) and chromo-EDM. For this we have performed a calculation of the nuclear MQM as well as the molecular constant that characterises the interaction of this MQM of $^{175}$Lu with electrons. Additionally, we predict the hyperfine structure constants for the ground electronic state of LuOH$^+$. We conclude that LuOH$^+$ is a promising system to measure the nuclear MQM.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the LuOH⁺ molecular cation is a promising system for measuring the CP-violating nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM), with the expected T,P-violating energy shift in \(^{175}\)LuOH⁺ and \(^{176}\)LuOH⁺ potentially a factor of 2 higher than in the isoelectronic neutral molecule \(^{173}\)YbOH, contingent on the nuclear MQM and molecular enhancement factor calculations.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic four-component coupled cluster calculations (CCSD(T)) to determine the equilibrium geometry and the molecular constant \(W_M\) that characterizes the interaction of the nuclear MQM with electrons in the ground electronic state of LuOH⁺. They also calculated the nuclear MQM of \(^{175}\)Lu and \(^{176}\)Lu using the deformed oscillator Nilsson model, expressing the MQM in terms of fundamental CP-violating parameters such as the QCD angle \(\bar{\theta}\) and quark (chromo-)EDMs. Additionally, they predicted the magnetic dipole hyperfine structure constants \(A_{||}\) and \(A_{\perp}\) to indirectly estimate the uncertainty of the \(W_M\) value.

## Key numbers

- The molecular constant \(W_M\) for LuOH⁺ is \(-1.251 \times 10^{33} \, \text{Hz}/(e \cdot \text{cm}^2)\), which is about 16% higher than the \(W_M\) value for YbOH.
- The \(^{175}\)Lu MQM is expressed as \(M(\bar{\theta}) \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-27} \, \bar{\theta} \, e \cdot \text{cm}^2\) and \(M(\tilde{d}) \approx 0.7 \times 10^{-10} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d) \, e \cdot \text{cm}\); the \(^{176}\)Lu MQM is 1.4 times larger.
- The expected energy shift for \(^{175}\)LuOH⁺ due to the QCD angle is \(\delta E(\bar{\theta}) \approx 20 \times 10^{10} \bar{\theta} \, \mu\text{Hz}\), and due to the chromo-EDM difference is \(\delta E(\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d) \approx 9 \times 10^{27} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d)/\text{cm} \, \mu\text{Hz}\).
- The equilibrium geometry parameters are \(R(\text{Lu--O}) = 1.873(20) \, \text{Å}\) and \(R(\text{O--H}) = 0.958(20) \, \text{Å}\).
- The final calculated hyperfine structure constants are \(A_{||}/g_{\text{Lu}} = 12763 \, \text{MHz}\) and \(A_{\perp}/g_{\text{Lu}} = 12327 \, \text{MHz}\).

## Context

This work extends the search for CP-violating nuclear MQMs from neutral molecules like YbOH (studied by Maison (2019) and Denis (2019)) to the molecular cation LuOH⁺, which offers advantages of ion trapping and sympathetic cooling. The calculated MQM and \(W_M\) values enable future experiments to set new constraints on fundamental CP-violating parameters such as \(\bar{\theta}\) and quark chromo-EDMs, potentially improving upon current limits from experiments like the ACME ThO experiment (2018) and the HfF⁺ experiment by Cornell (2017).
