# Systematic few-body analysis of eta-d, eta-3He and eta-4He interaction at low energies

> The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas N-body theory is used to study interaction of eta-mesons with d, 3He, and 4He. Separable expansion of the subamplitudes is adopted to convert the integral equations into the quasi-two-body form. The resulting formalism is applied to fit the existing data for low-energy eta production on few-nucleon targets. On the basis of this fitting procedure the scattering lengths a_{eta d}, a_{eta 3He}, a_{eta 4He} as well as the subthreshold behaviour of the elementary eta-N scattering amplitude are obtained.

# Systematic few-body analysis of eta-d, eta-3He and eta-4He interaction at low energies

## Key claim

The authors use a phenomenological ansatz for the eta-N scattering amplitude within the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas N-body theory to simultaneously reproduce the final-state interaction effects observed in eta production on few-nucleon targets (np to eta d, dp to eta 3He, dd to eta 4He), with the hedge that the extracted scattering lengths and subthreshold eta-N amplitude depend on the specific parameter set used to fit the data, and the analysis assumes the production mechanism is of short-range nature and independent of the final-state interaction.

## Method

The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) N-body integral equations were solved for the three-, four-, and five-body systems eta-d, eta-3He, and eta-4He using a separable pole expansion method to convert the equations into quasi-two-body form, with the energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) method providing convergence with six to eight separable terms. The eta-N interaction was modeled via excitation of the N(1535)1/2- resonance with coupling to the pi-N channel, using a rank-one separable potential for the NN interaction in the 1S0 and 3S1 configurations parametrized from the Paris potential. The parameters of the eta-N amplitude were then fitted so that the calculated eta-nuclear amplitudes squared reproduced the observed final-state interaction effects in eta production reactions on few-nucleon targets.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 3He: 8.64 MeV, calculated with the separable NN potential
- Binding energy of 4He: 31.17 MeV, calculated with the separable NN potential
- Set I parameters: g_eta = 1.91, beta_eta = 636 MeV, g_pi = 0.651, beta_pi = 850 MeV, M_0 = 1577 MeV, gamma_pipi = 4.0 MeV, yielding a_etaN = 0.93 + i 0.25 fm
- Set II parameters: g_eta = 1.66, beta_eta = 1524 MeV, g_pi = 0.977, beta_pi = 1057 MeV, M_0 = 1610 MeV, gamma_pipi = 0.10 MeV, yielding a_etaN = 0.67 + i 0.29 fm

## Context

This work extends previous few-body calculations of eta-nuclear systems by Fix and collaborators (FiAr2N, FiAr3N, FiKol4N) by using a realistic spin-dependent NN potential instead of spinless nucleons, and by focusing on fitting experimental data rather than only theoretical aspects. It contrasts with variational approaches by Barnea and collaborators (Barnea1, Barnea2, Barnea3) who used hyperphysical harmonics and stochastic variational methods. The combined analysis of all three systems (eta-d, eta-3He, eta-4He) within the same microscopic model enables determination of whether the observed near-threshold enhancements correspond to real bound states or virtual bound states, a question that individual reaction analyses cannot resolve.
