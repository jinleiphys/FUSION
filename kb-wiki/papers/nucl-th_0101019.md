# Excitation Spectrum in the Friedberg-Lee Model

> The excitation spectrum of the nucleon with the spin 1/2 is examined by using the Friedberg-Lee model containing the constituent quark and the scalar meson. An appropriate way of quantization for the non-linear meson field is employed by taking account of the non-topological soliton existed in the classical level. Our model space for the nucleon resonances includes the three-quark plus one-meson state in addition to the pure three-quark state. The excitation spectrum in this model space shows that the positive parity state appears as the first excited state associated with the 0s-excitation of the scalar meson. The meson excitation also generates the additional negative parity state apart from the well-known 0p-excitation of the quark.

# Excitation Spectrum in the Friedberg-Lee Model

## Key claim
The Friedberg-Lee model with a quantized scalar meson field, rather than a mean-field approximation, can produce the correct parity ordering of nucleon resonances (positive parity first excited state) when the model space includes three-quark-plus-one-meson states alongside pure three-quark states, but only for parameter sets where the meson-quark coupling constant g is between approximately 20 and 50.

## Method
The authors quantize the scalar meson field by introducing fluctuations around the non-topological soliton solution of the Friedberg-Lee model, solving coupled eigenvalue equations for quark and meson single-particle states. They construct a Fock space of single baryon states including both pure three-quark states and three-quark-plus-one-scalar-meson states, then diagonalize the Hamiltonian (excluding O(χ³, χ⁴) terms) to obtain excitation energies. Spurious center-of-mass states are removed using a harmonic oscillator projection technique.

## Key numbers
- The constituent quark mass is set to 300 MeV.
- The root mean square radius of the 0s-quark wave function is approximately 0.56 fm for all parameter values of g.
- The first excited state (positive parity) has an excitation energy of about 500 MeV, corresponding to the Roper resonance.
- The second excited state (negative parity) has an excitation energy of about 600 MeV, corresponding to the 0p-orbital excitation of one quark.
- The additional negative parity state (three-quark plus one meson) appears about 250 MeV above the two degenerate negative parity states.
- The energy difference between the 0s and 0p quark single-particle levels is constrained to 600 MeV.
- The meson-quark coupling constant g is explored in the range from approximately 20 to 50, where at least four bound quark states (0s, 0p, 0d, 1s) exist.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing "parity-ordering problem" in quark models, where conventional constituent quark models with harmonic oscillator confinement predict the first excited nucleon state to have negative parity, contrary to the observed Roper resonance (positive parity). The authors extend the Friedberg-Lee model (Friedberg and Lee, 1977) beyond the mean-field approximation used by Haider and Liu (1986) and Sskh (1993), instead quantizing the scalar meson field around the non-topological soliton. The approach is related to the breathing mode introduced in the MIT bag model by Brown et al. (1983) and to the monopole excitation analysis of Morsch et al. (1993). The work enables future inclusion of the pion field along the lines of Chen and Su (1995) and application to pion-nucleon reactions to test whether the predicted third negative parity state couples weakly to the pion-nucleon channel.
