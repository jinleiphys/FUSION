# Charmed hadrons in nuclear medium

> We study the properties of charmed hadrons in dense matter within a coupled-channel approach which accounts for Pauli blocking effects and meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner. We analyze the behaviour in this dense environment of dynamically-generated baryonic resonances as well as the open-charm meson spectral functions. We discuss the implications of the in-medium properties of open-charm mesons on the $D_{s0}(2317)$ and the predicted X(3700) scalar resonances.

## Key claim

The in-medium properties of open-charm mesons (D and D*) are calculated self-consistently using two different effective interaction models (SU(4) and SU(8) with heavy-quark symmetry), and the resulting modifications cause dynamically-generated charmed baryonic resonances and scalar mesons (D_s0(2317) and X(3700)) to acquire substantial widths in nuclear matter, though no clear mass shift is extracted.

## Method

The authors compute the self-energy and spectral function of D and D* mesons in nuclear matter by solving a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation self-consistently, incorporating Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings, and meson self-energies at finite temperature. Two bare interaction models are used: a broken SU(4) Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction with an attractive isoscalar-scalar term, and an SU(8) spin-flavor extension that includes pseudoscalar and vector mesons together with J=1/2+ and J=3/2+ baryons to respect heavy-quark symmetry. The in-medium T-matrix is then used to evaluate the self-energy by summing over the nucleon Fermi distribution, and the resulting spectral functions are analyzed for dynamically-generated baryonic resonances and scalar meson resonances.

## Key numbers

- The D_s0(2317) resonance, which has zero width in vacuum, develops a width of the order of 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
- The X(3700) resonance, which has a small width in vacuum, develops a width of the order of 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
- The SU(4) model generates the I=0 Lambda_c(2593) resonance and a new I=1 Sigma_c(2880) resonance.
- The SU(8) model generates additional J=3/2 resonances: Lambda_c(2660), Lambda_c(2941), Sigma_c(2554), and Sigma_c(2902), as well as new J=1/2 resonances Sigma_c(2823) and Sigma_c(2868).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium modifications of open-charm mesons, building on self-consistent coupled-channel approaches used for antikaons in hot dense nuclear matter by Tolos (2008). The SU(4) model follows the approach of Lutz (2006) and Mizutani (2006), while the SU(8) model with heavy-quark symmetry extends the spin-flavor formalism of Garcia-Recio (2005). The analysis of scalar resonances D_s0(2317) and X(3700) in nuclear matter builds on the dynamical generation of these states from pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interactions by Gamermann (2006) and Molina (2008). The results enable future experimental studies of transparency ratios at the CBM experiment at FAIR to measure the in-medium absorption rates of charmed hadrons.
