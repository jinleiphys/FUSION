# The shape of the proton at high energies

> We present first calculations of the fluctuating gluon distribution in a proton as a function of impact parameter and rapidity employing the functional Langevin form of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation. We demonstrate that when including effects of confinement by screening the long range Coulomb field of the color charges, the evolution is unitary. The large-x structure of the proton, characterized by the position of three valence quarks, retains an effect on the proton shape down to very small values of x. We determine the dipole scattering amplitude as a function of impact parameter and dipole size and extract the rapidity evolution of the saturation scale and the proton radius.

# The shape of the proton at high energies

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that when confinement effects are included by screening the long-range Coulomb field of color charges via an effective mass term, the JIMWLK evolution of the gluon distribution in a finite-size proton becomes unitary and respects the Froissart bound. The large-x structure of the proton, defined by the positions of three valence quarks, retains a noticeable imprint on the gluon distribution down to very small values of x, even after evolution over approximately 9 units of rapidity.

## Method
The authors solve the functional Langevin form of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation on a 2048x2048 transverse lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using a fixed coupling constant alpha_s = 0.3. They model the initial proton configuration at moderately small x by sampling gluon distributions around three constituent quark positions according to a McLerran-Venugopalan type model, and introduce an exponential infrared regulator via an effective mass scale m to suppress long-range gluon emission. The dipole scattering amplitude is computed as a function of impact parameter and dipole size to extract the saturation scale and effective proton radius as functions of rapidity.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale grows exponentially as Q_s ~ exp(lambda alpha_s Delta Y) with lambda ~ 1.4, extracted from simulations with N_t = 2048 lattice points and lattice spacing a_t = 4.167 x 10^{-3} fm.
- The effective proton radius grows linearly with rapidity as R_eff(Delta Y)/R_p ~ Gamma alpha_s Delta Y / (m R_p) with Gamma ~ 1.2, where R_p ~ 1 fm is the proton radius.
- The physical lattice size is 8.53 fm, with rapidity step size dY = 3.33 x 10^{-3}.
- The dipole scattering amplitude is evaluated for hadronic size dipoles with 0.4 < |r|/R_p < 0.6 and impact parameters up to |b| ~ R_p.
- The infrared mass scale is set to mR_p = 3 for the main results, with the dimensionless parameter g^2 mu_0 R_p = 30.

## Context
This work extends earlier numerical solutions of the JIMWLK equation by Rummukainen (2003), Lappi (2011), and Dumitru (2011), which assumed infinitely large homogeneous nuclei, by introducing for the first time an explicit impact parameter dependence for a finite-size proton. It addresses the problem of exponential growth of the hadron size identified by Kovner (2001) and Golec-Biernat (2003), which would violate the Froissart bound. The results enable event-by-event simulations of the fluctuating proton shape for use in classical Yang-Mills simulations of p+A collisions and fluid dynamic evolution, as well as for modeling deeply inelastic scattering at a future electron-ion collider.
