# Exclusive charge exchange reaction pD->n(pp) within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism

> The exclusive charge exchange reaction pD->n(pp) at intermediate and high energies is studied within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. The final state interaction in the detected pp pair at nearly zero excitation energy is described by the 1S0 component of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. Results of numerical calculations of polarization observables and differential cross-section persuade that, as in the non-relativistic case, this reaction (i) can be utilized as a ``relativistic deuteron polarimeter'' and (ii) delivers further information about the elementary nucleon-nucleon charge-exchange amplitude.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the exclusive charge exchange reaction \(p\vec{D} \to n(pp)\) can serve as a "relativistic deuteron polarimeter" and provide information about the elementary nucleon-nucleon charge-exchange amplitude, but only under the condition that the final \(pp\) pair is detected at nearly zero excitation energy and is dominated by the \(^1S_0\) component of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, with vector analyzing powers strictly zero in this approximation.

## Method

The reaction is studied within the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) formalism using a covariant generalization of the spectator mechanism. The deuteron bound state and the \(^1S_0\) final state of the \(pp\) pair are described by numerical solutions of the BS equation with a realistic one-boson exchange kernel (including \(\pi, \omega, \rho, \sigma, \eta, \delta\) mesons). The charge-exchange vertex is approximated by the on-shell elementary \(pn \to np\) amplitude, and final state interaction in the \(pp\) pair is treated via a one-iteration approximation to the inhomogeneous BS equation.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy \(E_x\) of the \(pp\) pair is taken in the range \(E_x = 0\)–\(8\) MeV, where the \(^1S_0\) configuration dominates.
- The internal relative momenta in the deuteron for which the BS and Gross approaches give almost identical amplitudes extend up to \(|\mathbf{k}| \sim 1.5\) GeV/c.
- The differential cross section is expressed as \(\frac{d^2\sigma}{dt\,ds_f} = \frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{64\pi\lambda(p,D)} \sqrt{1 - \frac{4m^2}{s_f}} \frac{1}{(2\pi)^2} |M_{fi}|^2\), where \(t = q^2\), \(s_f = (D - q)^2\), and \(m\) is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic spectator mechanism proposed by Bugg and Wilkin (1970s) for the reaction \(p\vec{D} \to n(pp)\) as a deuteron polarimeter, providing a fully covariant treatment using the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. It builds on the authors' previous impulse approximation study (Kaptari et al., 2001) and is motivated by experimental programs at COSY (Jülich) and the upgraded Dubna accelerator. The results enable relativistic calculations of polarization observables such as the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) for kinematical conditions relevant to these facilities, and allow comparison with non-relativistic calculations by Wilkin (1990s).
