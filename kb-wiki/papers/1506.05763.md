# Scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis near the critical point of nuclear matter

> The van der Waals (VDW) equation of state predicts the existence of a first-order liquid-gas phase transition and contains a critical point. The VDW equation with Fermi statistics is applied to a description of the nuclear matter. The nucleon number fluctuations near the critical point of nuclear matter are studied. The scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis diverge at the critical point. It is found that the crossover region of the phase diagram is characterized by the large values of the scaled variance, the almost zero skewness, and the significantly negative kurtosis. The rich structures of the skewness and kurtosis are observed in the phase diagram in the wide region around the critical point, namely, they both may attain large positive or negative values.

## Key claim

The scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis of nucleon number fluctuations diverge at the critical point of nuclear matter, but the specific singular values of the skewness and kurtosis depend on the path of approach to the critical point, not just on the critical point itself.

## Method

The authors applied the van der Waals (VDW) equation of state with Fermi statistics (quantum ideal gas expressions for pressure and density) to symmetric nuclear matter, fixing the VDW parameters a and b to reproduce ground-state properties (binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV, density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, and zero pressure at T=0). They then computed the scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis of net-nucleon number fluctuations in the grand canonical ensemble using derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potential, and mapped these quantities across the (T, μ) phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- Critical point temperature: Tc = 19.7 MeV; critical chemical potential: μc = 908 MeV; critical density: nc = 0.07 fm^-3.
- VDW parameters: a = 329 MeV·fm^3, b = 3.42 fm^3 (corresponding to a hard-core radius of 0.59 fm).
- Ground-state density of nuclear matter: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3; binding energy per nucleon: E_B = -16 MeV.
- For comparison, the Boltzmann approximation gives nc = 0.10 fm^-3 and Tc = 28.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quantum formulation of the VDW equation of state for nuclear matter (VdW-NM, 2015) to study higher-order fluctuation measures (skewness and kurtosis) near the nuclear matter critical point, complementing earlier studies of the QCD critical point using effective QCD models (flucQCD-2, QCD-1, QCD-2) and model-independent universality arguments (Stephanov 2009, Stephanov 2011). The results provide a baseline for identifying critical point signatures in heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly at low temperatures where deconfinement and pion production are negligible.
