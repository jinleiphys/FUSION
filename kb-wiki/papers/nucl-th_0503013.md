# K-Lambda and K-Sigma photoproduction in a coupled channels framework

> A coupled channels analysis, based on the K-matrix approach, is presented for photo-induced kaon production. It is shown that channel coupling effects are large and should not be ignored. The importance of contact terms in the analysis, associated with short range correlations, is pointed out. The extracted parameters are compared with SU(3)-model predictions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that channel coupling effects in photo-induced kaon production are large and cannot be ignored, and that the choice of gauge-invariance restoration procedure (Davidson-Workman, Ohta, or Janssen-Ryckebusch) introduces significant model dependence in the extracted resonance parameters, with the Davidson-Workman prescription yielding coupling constants closest to SU(3)-model predictions.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled channels K-matrix model using an effective Lagrangian with tree-level diagrams for the kernel, including s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions from baryon resonances (spin-1/2 and spin-3/2) and meson exchanges. They incorporated form factors with a dipole form (cutoff Lambda = 1.2 GeV^2 for most channels, 1.0 GeV^2 for kaon Born terms) and compared three gauge-restoration prescriptions (Davidson-Workman, Ohta, and Janssen-Ryckebusch) to enforce gauge invariance. The model simultaneously fitted pion-nucleon scattering, pion photoproduction, eta photoproduction, and kaon photoproduction data using a single parameter set.

## Key numbers

* Cutoff parameter for dipole form factors: Lambda = 1.2 GeV^2 for all channels except kaon Born contributions where Lambda = 1.0 GeV^2
* Bare masses of resonances included: N(0.939 GeV), Lambda(1.116 GeV), Sigma(1.189 GeV), S11(1.525 GeV, 1.690 GeV), S31(1.630 GeV), P11(1.480 GeV, 1.750 GeV), P13(1.750 GeV), P33(1.230 GeV, 1.855 GeV), D13(1.515 GeV, 1.700 GeV), D33(1.670 GeV)
* Coupling constant g_{NK Lambda} = 12 (positive sign, deviating from the customary negative value)
* Coupling constant g_{NK Sigma} = 8.7
* Pion-nucleon coupling constant g_{N pi} = 13.47

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels K-matrix approach previously applied to pion-nucleon scattering by Korchin et al. (1998) to strangeness photoproduction, addressing the need for a simultaneous fit across multiple reaction channels to reduce model dependence. The analysis contrasts with single-channel analyses such as those by Janssen and Ryckebusch (2002) and the unitary isobar model by Penner and Mosel (2002), and shows that the Ohta gauge-restoration prescription leads to an unbounded growth of the cross section at high energies while the Davidson-Workman prescription gives results closer to SU(3) symmetry predictions. The results enable future coupled-channels analyses of phi-meson photoproduction and provide a framework for interpreting new SAPHIR and CLAS data on strangeness production.
