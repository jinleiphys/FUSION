# New experimental $^{23}$Na($α,p$)$^{26}$Mg Reaction Rate for Massive Star and Type-Ia Supernova models

> The $^{23}$Na($α,p$)$^{26}$Mg reaction has been identified as having a significant impact on the nucleosynthesis of several nuclei between Ne and Ti in type-Ia supernovae, and of $^{23}$Na and $^{26}$Al in massive stars. The reaction has been subjected to renewed experimental interest recently, motivated by high uncertainties in early experimental data and in the statistical Hauser-Feshbach models used in reaction rate compilations. Early experiments were affected by target deterioration issues and unquantifiable uncertainties. Three new independent measurements instead are utilizing inverse kinematics and Rutherford scattering monitoring to resolve this. In this work we present directly measured angular distributions of the emitted protons to eliminate a discrepancy in the assumptions made in the recent reaction rate measurements, which results in cross sections differing by a factor of 3. We derive a new combined experimental reaction rate for the $^{23}$Na($α,p$)$^{26}$Mg reaction with a total uncertainty of 30% at relevant temperatures. Using our new $^{23}$Na($α,p$)$^{26}$Mg rate, the $^{26}$Al and $^{23}$Na production uncertainty is reduced to within 8%. In comparison, using the factor of 10 uncertainty previously recommended by the rate compilation STARLIB, $^{26}$Al and $^{23}$Na production was changing by more than a factor of 2. In type-Ia supernova conditions, the impact on production of $^{23}$Na is constrained to within 15%.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new combined experimental reaction rate for the 23Na(α,p)26Mg reaction with a total uncertainty of 30% at relevant temperatures, derived from three independent measurements corrected using directly measured proton angular distributions. This reduces the previously recommended factor-of-10 uncertainty from the STARLIB compilation to a constrained 30% uncertainty, though the rate itself agrees within uncertainty with Hauser-Feshbach predictions at higher temperatures while deviating below 1.2 GK.

## Method

The authors measured proton angular distributions from the 23Na(α,p)26Mg reaction at Aarhus University using a detector array covering laboratory angles of 60°–120° and 140°–164°, fitting the differential cross-sections with a sum of the first four even Legendre polynomials. These measured angular distributions were then applied to correct the cross-sections from two other independent experiments (Tomlinson 2015 at TRIUMF and Almaraz-Calderon 2015 at ANL), which had previously assumed different angular distributions. The three corrected cross-section datasets were integrated using the exp2rate code to obtain individual reaction rates, which were then combined via weighted averaging to produce the final recommended rate.

## Key numbers

- Total uncertainty of 30% on the new combined experimental reaction rate at relevant astrophysical temperatures
- The three independent measurements (Almaraz-Calderon 2015, Howard 2015, Tomlinson 2015) are consistent to within 30% with one another in the energy range E_cm = 1.7 – 3.0 MeV after angular distribution corrections
- Using the new rate, 26Al and 23Na production uncertainty in massive stars is reduced to within 8%, compared to changes of more than a factor of 2 using the previous factor-of-10 uncertainty from STARLIB
- In type-Ia supernova conditions, the impact on production of 23Na is constrained to within 15%
- The angular distribution corrections increase the p0 cross-sections by a factor of 1.4(3) and p1 cross-sections by a factor of 1.3(2) over the isotropic distribution assumed in Tomlinson (2015)
- For Almaraz-Calderon (2015), corrections increase p0 cross-sections by a factor of 4.3(9) and p1 cross-sections by a factor of 3.8(7) over the 27Al(α,p)30Si distribution previously assumed
- At E_cm = 1831 keV, the cross-section ratio σ/σ_ISO is 4.9(14) for p0 protons due to a strong resonance at 1800 keV
- The recommended reaction rate at T = 1.0 GK is 2.5×10^(-3) cm^3 s^(-1) mol^(-1), with lower limit 1.8×10^(-3) and upper limit 3.5×10^(-3)
- At T = 2.0 GK, the recommended rate is 6.8×10^(1) cm^3 s^(-1) mol^(-1), with lower limit 5.3×10^(1) and upper limit 8.7×10^(1)

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity study of Iliadis (2011), which identified the 23Na(α,p)26Mg reaction as having a significant effect on 26Al production in massive star models, and addresses the large uncertainties noted by Parikh (2013) in the application of Hauser-Feshbach models to low-mass alpha-induced reactions. The new rate resolves a factor-of-3 discrepancy between recent measurements by Almaraz-Calderon (2015), Tomlinson (2015), and Howard (2015) through direct angular distribution measurements, and enables more precise predictions of 23Na and 26Al nucleosynthesis in massive stars and type-Ia supernovae, with the rate now suitable for inclusion in reaction rate compilations such as REACLIB and STARLIB.
