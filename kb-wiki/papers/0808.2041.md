# Systematic Measurements of Identified Particle Spectra in pp, d+Au and Au+Au Collisions from STAR

> Identified charged particle spectra of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$ and $\pbar$ at mid-rapidity ($|y|<0.1$) measured by the $\dedx$ method in the STAR-TPC are reported for $pp$ and d+Au collisions at $\snn = 200$ GeV and for Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV, 130 GeV, and 200 GeV. ... [Shortened for arXiv list. Full abstract in manuscript.]

## Key claim

The paper reports systematic measurements of identified charged particle spectra across multiple collision systems and energies, finding that the average transverse momentum of each particle species follows a trend determined by the total charged particle multiplicity density, with the caveat that the extracted values depend on the specific functional form used to fit the spectra (e.g., Boltzmann, power-law, or Levy functions) and the transverse momentum range of the fit.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration used the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) to identify charged pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons via the specific energy loss (dE/dx) method at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.1). Particle spectra were measured in pp and d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, and in Au+Au collisions at 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV, covering a transverse momentum range from about 0.2 to 6 GeV/c. The spectra were corrected for detector acceptance, efficiency, and background contributions, and were fitted with various functional forms to extract yields, average transverse momenta, and particle ratios as a function of collision centrality.

## Key numbers

- Bjorken energy density estimate: at least several GeV/fm^3 for a formation time less than 1 fm/c in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
- Relative strangeness production in central Au+Au collisions is about 50% above the pp value at the same energy.
- Net-baryon density in Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV is significantly larger than at 130 GeV and 200 GeV, with a stronger increase with centrality.
- Antibaryon production relative to total particle multiplicity is constant over centrality but increases with collision energy.
- Strangeness production relative to total particle multiplicity is similar at the three measured RHIC energies (62.4, 130, and 200 GeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier STAR measurements of identified particle spectra in Au+Au collisions at 130 GeV (STAR Collaboration, 2001) and provides a systematic comparison across pp, d+Au, and Au+Au systems at multiple energies. The results enable tests of hydrodynamic models of the quark-gluon plasma expansion and provide baseline measurements for understanding parton energy loss and hadronization mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions. The data also serve as input for constraining the chemical freeze-out temperature and baryochemical potential in the statistical model framework.
