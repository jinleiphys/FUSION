# $Ω$ and $ϕ$ in Au + Au ~collisions at \srt~= 200 and 11.5 GeV from a multiphase transport model

> Within the framework of a multiphase transport model, we study the production and properties of $Ω$ and $ϕ$ in Au + Au collisions with a new set of parameters for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and with the original set of parameters for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 GeV, respectively. The AMPT model with the string melting version provides a reasonable description at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and while the AMPT model with default version describes the data well at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 GeV. It indicates that the system created at top RHIC energy is dominated by partonic interaction and while the hadronic interaction becomes important at lower beam energy, such as $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 GeV. The comparison of $N(Ω^++Ω^-)/[2N(ϕ)]$ ratio between data and calculations further supports the argument. Our calculations can generally describe the data of nuclear modification factor as well as elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with a newly tuned parameter set (Set C) for the string melting version describes Ω and φ production at √sNN = 200 GeV, while the default AMPT version describes the data at √sNN = 11.5 GeV, indicating a transition from partonic-dominated dynamics at top RHIC energy to hadronic-dominated dynamics at lower beam energy; however, the Ω yield is still underestimated by a factor of 5.0 even with the new parameters, leaving room for future model improvement.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with the string melting version (version 2.26t5) for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with a new parameter set (Set C: a=0.55, b=0.15 GeV⁻², αs=0.33, μ=3.2 fm⁻¹, corresponding to a parton cross section of 1.5 mb) and the default AMPT version with the original parameter set (Set A: a=2.2, b=0.5 GeV⁻², αs=0.47, μ=1.8 fm⁻¹) for √sNN = 11.5 GeV. They calculated the transverse momentum spectra, particle ratios, nuclear modification factors (RCP), and elliptic flow (v2) of Ω and φ, comparing with STAR experimental data.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 200 GeV, the AMPT string melting model with Set C underestimates the Ω yield by a factor of 5.0 compared to STAR data.
- The Ω/φ ratio at √sNN = 200 GeV shows an approximately linear increase with pT, consistent with the recombination model prediction RΩ/φth(p) = (4gΩCs)/(27gφ) × p.
- At √sNN = 200 GeV, the RCP values for both Ω and φ are smaller than 1 and suppressed at high pT, indicating medium effects.
- At √sNN = 11.5 GeV, the RCP of φ meson is close to 1, with the Ω RCP similar to φ's within large statistical uncertainty.
- The elliptic flow of φ at √sNN = 200 GeV increases from central collisions up to 40%-50% centrality and then decreases to 50%-80% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the RHIC Beam Energy Scan analysis by the STAR collaboration (OmegaphiSTAR, 2014) which observed that the Ω/φ ratio deviates from a consistent trend at lower collision energies, suggesting a change in strange quark dynamics below √sNN = 19.6 GeV. The study uses the AMPT model with parameter tuning following previous work by Lin et al. (2002, 2005) and the Lund string fragmentation parameters from earlier studies (Set A and Set B). The results support the quark coalescence/recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2003) and enable future comparisons with higher-precision data from the STAR Beam Energy Scan Phase-II runs in 2019-2020.
