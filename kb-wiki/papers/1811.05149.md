# $Ω_c$ excited states with heavy-quark spin symmetry

> We study the $C=1$, $S=-2$, $I=0$ sector, where five excited $Ω_c$ states have been recently observed by the LHCb Collaboration. We start from a recently developed unitarized baryon-meson model that takes, as bare baryon-meson interaction, an extended Weinberg-Tomozawa kernel consistent with both chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetries. This ${\rm SU(6)} \times {\rm HQSS}$ scheme leads to a successful description of the observed lowest-lying odd parity charmed $Λ_c$(2595) and $Λ_c$(2625) states, and bottomed $Λ_b$(5912) and $Λ_b$(5920) resonances. Within this model, five odd-parity $Ω_c$ states are dynamically generated, but with masses below 3 GeV, not allowing for an identification with the observed LHCb resonances. We revise this model and explore two different scenarios for the renormalization scheme, that is, using a modified common energy scale to perform the subtractions or utilizing a common ultraviolet cutoff to render finite the ultraviolet divergent loop functions in all channels. In both cases, we show that some (at least three) of the dynamically generated states can be identified with the experimental $Ω_c$, while having odd parity and $J=1/2$ or $J=3/2$. Two of these states turn out to be part of the same ${\rm SU(6)} \times {\rm HQSS}$ multiplets as the charmed and bottomed $Λ$ baryons.

## Key claim

By adjusting the renormalization scheme (either a modified subtraction scale or a common ultraviolet cutoff) in a unitarized baryon-meson model with SU(6) × HQSS Weinberg-Tomozawa kernel, at least three of the five dynamically generated odd-parity Ω_c states can be identified with the LHCb resonances, whereas the original scheme produced masses below 3 GeV that precluded any identification.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation using an s-wave SU(6) × HQSS extended Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the kernel for the C=1, S=-2, I=0 sector. They renormalize the ultraviolet-divergent loop functions either by one-subtraction at a modified energy scale (parameter α) or by a common sharp-cutoff Λ in momentum space for all baryon-meson channels. Pole positions on the first and second Riemann sheets are extracted to obtain masses and widths of dynamically generated states.

## Key numbers

- Five odd-parity Ω_c states predicted in the original scheme (Romanets 2012) with masses: 2810.9 MeV (J=1/2), 2814.3 MeV (J=3/2), 2884.5 MeV (J=1/2), 2941.6 MeV (J=1/2), 2980.0 MeV (J=3/2); all zero width.
- Using one-subtraction with α=1.16, two states shift to 2999.9 MeV (J=1/2, width 0.06 MeV) and 3036.3 MeV (J=3/2, width 0 MeV), near the experimental Ω_c(3000.4 MeV, width 4.5 MeV) and Ω_c(3050.2 MeV, width 0.8 MeV).
- Using a common cutoff Λ=1090 MeV, three states match experiment: 2994.26 MeV (J=1/2, width 1.85 MeV) near Ω_c(3000); 3048.7 MeV (J=3/2, width 0.0 MeV) near Ω_c(3050); 3116.81 MeV (J=1/2, width 3.72 MeV) near Ω_c(3119) or Ω_c(3090).
- For cutoffs below 800 MeV, all states become heavier and wider than experiment; for cutoffs above 1300–1350 MeV, states appear well below 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the SU(6) × HQSS unitarized model of Romanets (2012), which successfully described Λ_c(2595), Λ_c(2625), Λ_b(5912), and Λ_b(5920) but predicted Ω_c states too low in mass. It contrasts with molecular analyses by Montana (2017) and Debastiani (2017), which identified two J=1/2 states with Ω_c(3050) and Ω_c(3090) and a J=3/2 state with Ω_c(3119). The present model identifies J=1/2^- Ω_c(3000) and Ω_c(3119/3090) and J=3/2^- Ω_c(3050), placing two of these in the same SU(6) × HQSS multiplets as the charmed and bottomed Λ baryons. The results enable future experimental tests of the spin-parity assignments and molecular nature of the observed Ω_c states.
