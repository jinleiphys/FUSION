# Kaon condensation in the quark-meson coupling model and compact stars

> The properties of neutron stars constituted of a crust of hadrons and an internal part of hadrons and kaon condensate are calculated within the quark-meson-coupling model. We have considered stars with nucleons only in the hadron phase and also stars with hyperons as well. The results are compared with the ones obtained from the non-linear Walecka model for the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

Kaon condensation in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model softens the equation of state and reduces the maximum stellar mass, but the effect is smaller than in the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) because the QMC nucleon equation of state is already softer; the reduction is approximately 0.15 solar masses without hyperons and approximately 0.05 solar masses with hyperons.

## Method

The authors describe hadronic matter using the quark-meson coupling model, where baryons and kaons are treated as static MIT bags interacting through classical sigma, omega, and rho meson fields in the mean-field approximation. The kaon effective mass and kaon-meson couplings are computed self-consistently from the bag model, and the phase transition to a mixed phase of hadrons and kaon condensate is treated with Gibbs criteria for phase coexistence under global charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions. Neutron star structure is obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using the resulting equations of state, with the BPS equation of state for the crust.

## Key numbers

- Kaon condensation appears at densities greater than 3.5 times the nuclear saturation density (3.5 rho_0) when only nucleons are present; with hyperons included, kaon condensation appears at approximately 4 rho_0.
- The K^- optical potential at saturation density is -123 MeV for a nucleon bag radius of 0.6 fm; the K^+ potential is -22 MeV at the same density.
- Maximum gravitational mass for a star with nucleons only in the QMC model: 2.20 solar masses (radius 12.15 km, central energy density 5.74 fm^-4).
- Maximum gravitational mass for a star with nucleons plus kaon condensate in the QMC model: 2.05 solar masses (radius 12.94 km, central energy density 4.80 fm^-4).
- Maximum gravitational mass for a star with nucleons plus hyperons plus kaon condensate in the QMC model: 1.94 solar masses (radius 12.00 km, central energy density 5.47 fm^-4).
- For comparison, the NLWM gives a maximum gravitational mass of 2.40 solar masses for nucleons only, and 2.08 solar masses for nucleons plus kaon condensate.
- The kaon bag radius is 0.457 fm and the kaon bag parameter Z_K is 3.362, fixed from the vacuum kaon mass and stability condition for a nucleon bag radius of 0.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars that used the chiral model (Kaplan and Nelson) and the non-linear Walecka model (Thorsson, Ellis, Glendenning) by instead using the quark-meson coupling model, which treats baryons as MIT bags and determines kaon-meson couplings self-consistently from quark degrees of freedom. The results are compared directly with the NLWM calculations of Glendenning and collaborators, showing that the QMC model produces a softer equation of state and smaller mass reductions from strangeness inclusion. The work enables future studies of hybrid stars with kaon condensates using the QMC framework, and the authors note that existing mass-to-radius constraints from observations of EXO0748-676 (Cottam, 2002) and 1E 1207.4-5209 (Sanwal, 2002) do not exclude any of the equations of state considered.
