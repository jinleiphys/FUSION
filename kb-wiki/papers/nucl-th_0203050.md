# Classical Drop Phase Diagram and Cluster Distributions

> The phase diagram, ($T,ρ$), of a finite, constrained, and classical system is built from the analysis of cluster distributions in phase and configurational space. The obtained phase diagram can be split in three regions. One, low density limit, in which first order phase transition features can be observed. Another one, corresponding to the high density regime, in which fragments in phase space display critical behavior of 3D-Ising universality class type. And an intermediate density region, in which power-laws are displayed but can not be associated to the abovementioned universality class.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of a finite, constrained classical Lennard-Jones system can be split into three distinct density regions, but the critical behavior observed in the intermediate and high-density regions depends on using phase-space-based cluster definitions (ECRA) rather than simple configurational-space clusters; the abstract's claim of 3D-Ising universality class behavior applies only for densities above approximately 0.35 σ⁻³, not across the entire high-density regime.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of 147 particles interacting via a 6-12 Lennard-Jones potential, confined within a spherical container using a repulsive wall potential. They analyzed cluster distributions using three fragment recognition algorithms: Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) based on configurational space, Minimum Spanning Tree in Energy (MSTE) based on the Coniglio-Klein criterion, and the Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm (ECRA) which finds the most bound density fluctuation in phase space. Phase diagrams were constructed by identifying energies where power-law mass distributions appear, determined by χ² fitting of fragment spectra, and by analyzing caloric curves and specific heat from kinetic energy fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- System size: 147 particles interacting via Lennard-Jones potential with cutoff radius r_c = 3σ
- Densities studied: ρ = 0.01, 0.03, 0.07, 0.10, 0.20, 0.35, 0.50, 0.55, and 0.80 σ⁻³
- For ρ = 0.10 σ⁻³, the energy at which power-law mass distributions appear is E_* = 0.10 ± 0.05 ε₀
- The critical exponent τ = 2.21 (3D-Ising value) is matched for densities ρ > 0.05 σ⁻³
- The critical exponent γ = 1.23 (liquid-gas value) is achieved for densities ρ ≥ 0.35 σ⁻³
- Three density regions identified: Region A (ρ ≤ 0.05 σ⁻³) with first-order transition signals; Region B (0.05 σ⁻³ < ρ < 0.35 σ⁻³) with power laws but non-matching γ; Region C (ρ > 0.35 σ⁻³) with 3D-Ising critical exponents
- For ρ = 0.35 σ⁻³, a change in potential energy fluctuations behavior is observed, marking the onset of invariant interparticle distance distributions

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Campi et al. (previous studies using Coniglio-Klein clusters) by applying ECRA and MST cluster definitions to a confined Lennard-Jones system, revealing that the phase diagram depends critically on the cluster definition used. The study connects to earlier work by Dorso (1993) on the Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm and to the Fisher droplet model framework for phase transitions in finite systems. The results enable a more nuanced understanding of how volume constraints affect phase transitions in finite nuclear systems, showing that the commonly assumed liquid-gas transition picture must be qualified by the system density relative to the confining volume.
