# Centrality Dependence of Strangeness Enhancement in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions - a Core-Corona Effect

> In ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, the multiplicity of multi-strange baryons per participating nucleon increases with centrality in a different fashion for different systems and energies. At RHIC, for copper+copper (CuCu) collisions the increase is much steeper than for gold-gold (AuAu) collisions. We show that this system size dependence is due to a core-corona effect: the relative importance of the corona as compared to the core (thermalized matter) contribution varies and the contribution of a corona nucleon to the multiplicity differs from that of a core nucleon. $ϕ$ mesons follow in principle the same trend, but the difference between core and corona multiplicity is relatively small, and therefore the CuCu and AuAu results are quite similar. This simple geometrical explanation makes also a strong case in favor of the validity of Glauber geometry in the peripheral regions of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, which is crucial for understanding the early evolution of the system.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of strangeness enhancement in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can be explained as a purely geometrical core-corona effect, where the relative weight of core (thermalized matter) and corona (peripheral nucleon-nucleon-like collisions) varies with system size and centrality, without requiring any new parameters beyond those fixed by AuAu data.

## Method

The authors develop a toy model that separates participating nucleons into "core participants" (those that have scattered more than once and form a thermal fireball) and "corona participants" (those that have scattered only once and hadronize like pp strings). The multiplicity per wounded nucleon for each hadron species is given by a weighted average of fixed core and corona multiplicities, with the core fraction f(N_core) determined by Glauber geometry. The results are then compared with full three-dimensional EPOS 1.9 simulations that implement a space-time dependent core-corona separation based on local string segment density.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 200 AGeV, the corona multiplicity per wounded nucleon for Λ is 0.0193 ± 0.0018, while the core multiplicity is 0.05; for Ω+Ω̄ the corona value is 0.00017 ± 0.0001 and the core value is 0.0016
- At √s = 62 AGeV, the corona multiplicity per wounded nucleon for Λ is 0.0112 and the core multiplicity is 0.047; for Ω+Ω̄ the corona value is 0.0001 and the core value is 0.0012
- At √s = 17.2 AGeV (NA57 data), the corona multiplicity per wounded nucleon for Λ is 0.0137 ± 0.0002 and the core multiplicity is 0.057; for Ω+Ω̄ the corona value is 0.000064 ± 0.000024 and the core value is 0.00125
- The multiplicity of multi-strange baryons per participating nucleon in central heavy ion collisions is up to 20 times larger than in pp collisions at the same energy
- At √s = 200 AGeV, more than 30% of strings in pp collisions have an energy smaller than the threshold for Ω production

## Context

This work extends the core-corona idea previously proposed by Werner (2007) and applied to AuAu at RHIC and PbPb at SPS, by validating it against the newly available CuCu data at RHIC. The approach contradicts canonical statistical models (Redlich, 2001) which require an ad hoc N_part^(1/3) volume scaling to reproduce centrality dependence, and provides a physical interpretation for the empirical N_part/N_bin parameterization of Cai (2007). The results enable predictions for other systems and demonstrate that the core properties are independent of system size, implying that the expected quark-gluon plasma state could exist even in very small systems.
