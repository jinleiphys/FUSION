# Bayesian parameter estimation with a new three-dimensional initial-conditions model for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We extend the well-studied midrapidity TRENTo initial-conditions model to three dimensions, thus facilitating (3+1)D modeling and analysis of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. TRENTo-3D is a fast, parametric model of the 3D initial-state geometry, capable of providing initial conditions for (3+1)D models of quark--gluon plasma formation and evolution. It builds on TRENTo's success at modeling the initial nuclear participant thicknesses, longitudinally extending the initial deposition to form a central fireball near midrapidity and two fragmentation regions at forward and backward rapidities. We validate the new model through a large-scale Bayesian calibration, utilizing as observables the rapidity distributions of charged hadrons. For computational efficiency the present effort employs a (1+1)D linearized approximation of ideal hydrodynamics as a stand-in for quark--gluon plasma dynamics. This calibration serves as model validation and paves the way for utilizing TRENTo-3D as an initial-conditions model for state-of-the-art simulation incorporating (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim
The paper presents TRENTo-3D, a new parametric model that extends the 2D TRENTo initial-conditions model to three dimensions, but the validation is performed using a (1+1)D linearized approximation of ideal hydrodynamics as a stand-in for full quark-gluon plasma dynamics, rather than a full (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic simulation.

## Method
The authors extend the midrapidity TRENTo model by introducing a three-source ansatz for longitudinal energy deposition: a Gaussian-like central fireball near midrapidity flanked by two fragmentation regions at forward and backward rapidities, with energy-momentum conservation enforced locally. They sample nucleon configurations using Woods-Saxon distributions, compute participant and fragment thicknesses from subnucleonic constituents, and allocate energy between fireball and fragments via a beta-distributed fluctuation parameter. For validation, they connect the 3D initial conditions to final-state charged-hadron rapidity distributions using a (1+1)D linearized ideal hydrodynamics evolution model, and perform a Bayesian calibration to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is computed via the expression σ_NN^inel / fm² = 3.12 + 0.0735 ln(√s/GeV) - 0.193 [ln(√s/GeV)]², fit to Particle Data Group data.
- The fireball profile function includes a tapering factor (1 - (η_s/η_smax)⁴)⁴ that ensures the profile vanishes at finite spacetime rapidity.
- The maximum spacetime rapidity is defined as η_smax = arccosh(√s / 2 / k_Tmin), where k_Tmin is the transverse momentum scale parameter in GeV.
- The fragment profile function f_frag(x) = (-ln x)^α x^(β+1) exp(-2 k_Tmin / (x √s)) is motivated by parton distribution functions, with α and β as shape parameters.

## Context
This work extends the well-established 2D TRENTo model (Moreland, 2014) to three dimensions, building on earlier 3D formulations (Ke, 2016) and the three-source model for particle production (Wolschin, 2006). The model enables future (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, particularly for asymmetric collision systems where longitudinal structure is crucial. The Bayesian calibration to rapidity distributions of charged hadrons paves the way for more complete calibrations incorporating full (3+1)D hydrodynamics and additional observables such as anisotropic flow coefficients.
