# Mass Shift and Width Broadening of Rho-Mesons Produced in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The mass shift and width broadening of rho-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions is estimated using general formulae which relate the in-medium mass shift of a particle to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude of this particle on constituents of the medium and the width broadening to the corresponding cross section. It is found that the mass increases by some tens of MeV but, more importantly, the width becomes large, increasing by several hundred MeV at beam energies of a few GeV per nucleon and by twice that amount at beam energies of about a hundred GeV per nucleon.

## Key claim
The mass shift of rho-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions is estimated to be small (some tens of MeV), but the width broadening is predicted to be very large (several hundred MeV), potentially making the rho-meson peak unobservable in dilepton mass spectra; however, the authors emphasize that these results are only semiquantitative estimates due to uncertainties in the assumed nucleon density at the final stage of evolution and the simplified treatment of the hadronic medium.

## Method
The authors relate the in-medium mass shift of a rho-meson to the real part of its forward scattering amplitude on nucleons and pions, and the width broadening to the corresponding total cross sections, using formulae derived from the optical analogy. The rho-nucleon and rho-pion forward scattering amplitudes and cross sections are calculated by saturating with known resonances at low energies and using the vector dominance model (VDM) together with Regge pole fits to gamma-nucleon and gamma-pion data at high energies. The nucleon and pion densities at the final stage of the collision are taken from experimental multiplicities and assumed volumes, with averaging performed over the measured momentum distributions of nucleons and pions in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- For high-energy collisions (200 GeV·A, S+S): mass shift Δm_ρ = 16 MeV (18 MeV from ρN, -2 MeV from ρπ); width broadening ΔΓ_ρ = 550 MeV (150 MeV from ρN, 400 MeV from ρπ).
- For low-energy collisions (3 GeV·A, Ni+Ni): mass shift Δm_ρ = 27 MeV (37 MeV from ρN, -10 MeV from ρπ); width broadening ΔΓ_ρ = 280 MeV (245 MeV from ρN, 35 MeV from ρπ).
- At high energy, the averaged cross sections are ⟨σ_ρN⟩ ≈ 45 mb and ⟨σ_ρπ⟩ ≈ 20 mb; the averaged real parts are ⟨Re f_ρN⟩ ≈ -1.1 fm and ⟨Re f_ρπ⟩ ≈ 0.03 fm.
- At low energy, the averaged cross sections are ⟨σ_ρN⟩ = 45 mb and ⟨σ_ρπ⟩ = 13 mb; the averaged real parts are ⟨Re f_ρN⟩ = -0.54 fm and ⟨Re f_ρπ⟩ = 0.30 fm.
- The assumed nucleon density at the final stage is ρ_N^0 = 0.3 fm^{-3}, about twice the standard nuclear density.
- The pion-to-nucleon multiplicity ratio R_π is 5.3 for the high-energy case and 0.48 for the low-energy case.

## Context
This work extends the model-independent approach of Eletsky and Ioffe (1997) relating in-medium mass shifts to forward scattering amplitudes, applying it specifically to rho-mesons in heavy ion collisions rather than in infinite nuclear matter. The results contradict the expectation of a clear rho-meson peak in dilepton spectra and are in qualitative agreement with the measurement of e+e- pair production in heavy ion collisions by the CERES collaboration (1997), where no rho-peak was found. The paper enables future experimental tests: observation of a rho-peak would indicate that the hadronic density at the final stage of evolution is very low, even below normal nuclear density.
