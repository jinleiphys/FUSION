# Hadron-Hadron Interactions from Lattice QCD: Theory meets Experiments

> We summarize recent developments in the study of hadron-hadron interactions using lattice QCD near the physical pion mass ($m_π \simeq 146$ MeV), based on the HAL QCD method and its connection to experimental data. In particular, we focus on several key interaction channels shown below. Also, we examine the two-pion exchange (TPE) mechanism, which governs the long-range behavior of interactions between flavor-singlet hadrons, as well as between nucleons and flavor-singlet hadrons.

# Hadron-Hadron Interactions from Lattice QCD: Theory meets Experiments

## Key claim

Lattice QCD using the HAL QCD method at a pion mass of mπ ≃ 146.4 MeV can quantitatively reproduce experimental data on hadron-hadron interactions without fine-tuning, but the method relies on the assumption that higher-derivative terms of O(δ²∂t³) in the integro-differential equation are small and negligible for the systems studied.

## Method

The HAL QCD method extracts an energy-independent non-local potential from the spatiotemporal correlations of two hadrons using the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function derived from four-point correlation functions. The potential is obtained by solving an integro-differential equation for the R-correlator, which avoids the ground-state saturation problem that plagues the direct Lüscher finite-volume method. The non-local potential is expanded in a derivative expansion, with the leading-order local potential extracted at finite Euclidean time t, and the t-dependence of the result provides an estimate of systematic errors from truncation.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the K-configuration: mπ = 146.4 MeV, from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD on a volume of V = (8.12 fm)³ with lattice spacing a = 0.0846 fm
- The ΞN interaction in the spin-isospin singlet channel (¹¹S₀) is the most attractive, bringing the ΞN system close to unitarity
- The Tcc⁺ state is located approximately 360 keV below the D*⁺D⁰ threshold, with (I, JP) = (0, 1⁺)
- The φ-p bound state binding energy in the spin 1/2 channel is estimated to lie within the range of 12.8 to 56.1 MeV
- The J/ψ mass reduction in nuclear matter at normal density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is δm_{J/ψ} ≃ 19(3) MeV, using the spin-averaged scattering length
- The spatial effective energy Eeff(r) for φ-N, J/ψ-N, and ηc-N interactions approaches 2mπ = 292.8 MeV for r > 1.0 fm, indicating two-pion exchange dominance
- Low-energy constants from chiral EFT: c₁ = -1.10(3) GeV⁻¹, c₂ = 3.57(4) GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -5.54(6) GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 4.17(4) GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method (Hatsuda and collaborators, 2010-2024) from earlier quenched and heavier-pion calculations to near-physical pion mass, enabling the first quantitative comparisons with experimental data from ALICE (2020, 2021) and LHCb (2022). The baryon-baryon results connect to hypernuclear studies at J-PARC and femtoscopic correlation measurements at RHIC and LHC. The meson-meson results on D*-D interactions provide a lattice QCD foundation for understanding the doubly charmed tetraquark Tcc observed by LHCb. The two-pion exchange analysis builds on the chiral EFT framework of Machleidt (2011), Epelbaum (2008), and the TMO potential of Taketani, Machida, and Ohnuma (1951), extending it to flavor-singlet hadrons following the work of Peskin and Bhanot (1979) and Brambilla (2015). The results enable future femtoscopic analyses of J/ψ-p at LHC and searches for charmonium-nucleus bound states as proposed by Krein (2017).
