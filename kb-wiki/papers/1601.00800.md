# Effects of EoS in viscous hydro+cascade model for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan

> A state-of-the-art 3+1 dimensional cascade + viscous hydro + cascade model vHLLE+UrQMD has been applied to heavy ion collisions in RHIC Beam Energy Scan range $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7\dots 200$ GeV. Based on comparison to available experimental data it was estimated that an effective value of shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $η/s$ in hydrodynamic stage has to decrease from $η/s=0.2$ to $0.08$ as collision energy increases from $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7$ to $39$ GeV, and to stay at $η/s=0.08$ for $39\le\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\le200$ GeV. In this work we show how an equation of state with first order phase transition affects the hydrodynamic evolution at those collision energies and changes the results of the model as compared to "default scenario" with a crossover type EoS from chiral model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that replacing a crossover-type equation of state (chiral model EoS) with a first-order phase transition EoS (EoS Q) in the viscous hydro+cascade model vHLLE+UrQMD changes the hydrodynamic evolution at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, but the differences in final hadronic observables are largely smeared by the subsequent hadron cascade stage, making it unclear whether experimental data can discriminate between the two EoSs.

## Method

The authors used the 3+1 dimensional hybrid model vHLLE+UrQMD, where the initial stage and final hadronic stage are described by the UrQMD cascade, and the intermediate stage is modeled with relativistic viscous hydrodynamics in the Israel-Stewart framework. They performed two sets of simulations for 20-30% central Au-Au collisions across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range (sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV), using the same collision-energy-dependent parameters (including shear viscosity over entropy density ratio eta/s) but switching between the chiral model EoS (crossover) and EoS Q (first-order phase transition via Maxwell construction with bag constant B^(1/4)=230 MeV). Particlization from fluid to hadrons was set at a fixed energy density epsilon_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm^3 for both EoS scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio eta/s decreases from 0.2 to 0.08 as collision energy increases from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 39 GeV, and stays at eta/s = 0.08 for 39 <= sqrt(s_NN) <= 200 GeV.
- The first-order phase transition EoS Q has a transition temperature T_c(n_B=0) = 164 MeV at zero baryon density.
- Particlization energy density epsilon_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm^3 for both EoS scenarios.
- The relative change in average duration of the fluid stage between the two EoSs is maximal for the lowest collision energy simulated, sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV.
- EoS Q results in suppression of the average radial flow, decreasing mean p_T of hadrons (larger effect for heavier protons, smaller effect for lighter pions).
- The p_T integrated elliptic flow is suppressed in the EoS Q case by the same amount for all collision energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Karpenko et al., 2015) which used only the chiral model EoS to extract the collision-energy-dependent eta/s values from experimental data. The paper contrasts the chiral model EoS (crossover transition at all baryon chemical potentials, in qualitative agreement with lattice QCD at mu_B=0) with EoS Q (first-order phase transition via Maxwell construction between hadron resonance gas and ideal quark-gluon plasma, originally from Kolb et al., 2000). The results raise the open question of whether the model parameters can be readjusted to compensate for EoS-induced changes in elliptic flow and mean transverse momentum while preserving rapidity distributions, and thus whether experimental data can distinguish between the two EoS scenarios.
