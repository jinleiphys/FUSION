# Ginzburg-Landau phase diagram of QCD near chiral critical point - chiral defect lattice and solitonic pion condensate -

> We investigate the influence of the isospin asymmetry on the phase structure of quark matter near the chiral critical point systematically using a generalized version of Ginzburg-Landau approach. The effect has proven to be so profound that it brings about not only a shift of the critical point but also a rich variety of phases in its neighborhood. In particular, there shows up a phase with spatially varying charged pion condensate which we name the "solitonic pion condensate" in addition to the "chiral defect lattice" where the chiral condensate is partially destructed by periodic placements of two-dimensional wall-like defects. Our results suggest that there may be an island of solitonic pion condensate in the low temperature and high density side of QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a small isospin chemical potential (μI) near the chiral critical point not only shifts the critical point's location but also introduces a new "solitonic pion condensate" (SPC) phase and a "chiral defect lattice" (CDL) phase, with the SPC potentially forming an island in the low-temperature, high-density region of the QCD phase diagram. The key hedge is that these results are obtained within a generalized Ginzburg-Landau approach with perturbative expansion in μI, and the existence of the SPC island is suggested rather than definitively proven.

## Method

The author uses a generalized Ginzburg-Landau (GL) approach expanded up to sixth order in the chiral order parameter φ = (σ, π), incorporating derivative terms and a perturbative expansion in the isospin chemical potential μI. The GL coefficients are evaluated within the quark loop approximation, and the Euler-Lagrange equation is solved using a one-parameter family of ansatz solutions based on Jacobi elliptic functions, with the thermodynamic potential minimized over the elliptic modulus ν to determine the phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The Lifshitz critical point (LCP) at μI = 0 is located at (α₂^(LCP), α₄^(LCP)) = ( (5/4) * (3^(4/5) / 2^(2/5)), -5 / (2^(1/5) * 3^(3/5)) )
- For μI² = 0.01, the corresponding physical isospin chemical potential is estimated as μI ≈ 40 MeV (using h ~ m_q/Λ with m_q ~ 10 MeV and Λ ~ 1 GeV)
- The critical value for defect formation onset at α₄ = -4 is α₂c = 3.76 h^(4/5)
- The second-order transition from the σ_S-phase to the SPC takes over from the CDL for α₄ ≲ -6.16 at μI² = 0.01
- Phase diagrams are computed for μI² values of 0.01, 0.1, 0.2, and 0.5

## Context

This work extends the analysis of inhomogeneous chiral condensates near the chiral critical point, building on the finding by Nickel (2009) that the critical point becomes a Lifshitz critical point when inhomogeneity is allowed. The author uses the generalized GL approach developed by Nickel (2009) and Abuki (2011) to study how isospin asymmetry modifies the phase structure, connecting to studies of charged pion condensation at large isospin density by Son (2000) and to the physics of Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) superconductors. The results enable future studies of magnetic properties of inhomogeneous pion condensates and their potential phenomenological consequences for compact star physics.
