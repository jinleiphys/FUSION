# The Nuclear Yukawa Model on a Lattice

> We present the results of the quantum field theory approach to nuclear Yukawa model obtained by standard lattice techniques. We have considered the simplest case of two identical fermions interacting via a scalar meson exchange. Calculations have been performed using Wilson fermions in the quenched approximation. We found the existence of a critical coupling constant above which the model cannot be numerically solved. The range of the accessible coupling constants is below the threshold value for producing two-body bound states. Two-body scattering lengths have been obtained and compared to the non relativistic results.

# The Nuclear Yukawa Model on a Lattice

## Key claim

The quenched lattice Yukawa model for two identical fermions interacting via scalar meson exchange cannot produce two-body bound states for any accessible coupling constant, because a critical coupling exists above which the Dirac operator becomes ill-conditioned and numerical solution is impossible; the accessible region lies entirely below the threshold for bound state formation.

## Method

The authors discretize the scalar Yukawa Lagrangian on a Euclidean lattice using Wilson fermions for the nucleon field and a free Klein-Gordon action for the meson field, with the quenched approximation (setting the fermion determinant to unity) and no meson self-interaction. Meson field configurations are generated analytically via independent Gaussian Fourier modes, and fermion propagators are computed by inverting the interacting Dirac operator on these configurations. Two-body scattering lengths are extracted from the volume dependence of the two-fermion binding energy using Luscher's leading-order formula.

## Key numbers

- Critical lattice Yukawa coupling: \( g_L \lesssim 0.6 \); for typical \(\kappa = 0.1\) this corresponds to \( g \lesssim 3 \) or \(\alpha = g^2/(4\pi) \lesssim 0.7\).
- For \(a\mu = 0.1\) and \(\mu = 0.65\) GeV, the lattice spacing is \(a \approx 0.03\) fm.
- The dimensionless non-relativistic Yukawa coupling \(G = (g^2/4\pi)(m/\mu)\) has a critical value \(G_0 \approx 1.680\) for the appearance of the first bound state.
- For \(g_L = 0.3\), \(\kappa = 0.118\), and \(a\mu = 0.1\) (giving \(G = 0.193\)), the non-relativistic scattering length is \(a_0\mu = -0.214\), close to the Born approximation value.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the Yukawa model by de Soto et al. (2007) and investigates the two-fermion sector that previous studies of the phase structure (Montvay and references therein) did not address. The results contrast with non-relativistic potential models (such as the Nijmegen potential of 1993 and the Argonne AV18 potential of 1995) which produce bound states from ladder-sum approximations; the absence of bound states in the quenched lattice calculation suggests that fermion-antifermion pair creation (unquenching) may be essential for generating nuclear binding. The work enables future comparisons with unquenched simulations and with lattice effective field theory approaches (Lee, 2009) that have successfully described light nuclei.
