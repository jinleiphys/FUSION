# Renormalization Group Improved Gap Equation for Color Superconductors

> The renormalization group is used to resum leading logarithmic contributions of the form alpha_s^{n+1} beta_0^n log^n (Delta/mu) to the gap equation appropriate for high density QCD. The scale dependence of the strong coupling constant alpha_s increases the gap by a factor of exp(33/16 (pi^2/4 - 1)) ~ 20 in the small coupling limit.

## Key claim

The renormalization group is used to resum leading logarithmic contributions of the form alpha_s^{n+1} beta_0^n log^n (Delta/mu) to the gap equation for high density QCD. The scale dependence of the strong coupling constant increases the gap by a factor of exp(33/16 (pi^2/4 - 1)) ~ 20 in the small coupling limit, but the authors note this is an estimate because the beta-function for magnetic gluons below the Debye mass may deviate by about 20% and matching to the full QCD calculation has not been performed.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for momentum scales below 2mu and compute the leading diagrams contributing to the gap equation, regularizing the perpendicular gluon momentum with dimensional regularization and minimal subtraction. They exploit the scale dependence of the four-quark counterterm C to resum all leading log contributions, evolving the electric and magnetic gluon contributions separately by setting the renormalization scale to lambda_E for electric gluons and lambda_M for magnetic gluons. Using an approximation that converts the integral equation into a differential equation, they obtain an analytic solution in terms of Bessel functions and determine the gap by imposing the condition that dDelta/dalpha_p = 0 at alpha_p = alpha_Delta.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement factor from the running of the strong coupling is exp(33/16 (pi^2/4 - 1)) ~ 20 in the small coupling limit.
- For mu = 10^6 MeV with n_f = 2 and alpha_Lambda = bar_alpha, the gap is Delta = 4.4 MeV for beta_0 = 0 and Delta = 32.5 MeV for beta_0 = 11, giving an enhancement factor of ~7.
- For mu = 10^4 MeV with n_f = 2 and alpha_Lambda = bar_alpha, the gap is Delta = 5.7 MeV for beta_0 = 0 and Delta = 43.0 MeV for beta_0 = 11.
- The analytic result for the gap is Delta = (1024 sqrt(2) pi^4)/(n_f^{5/2} g_s^5) exp(-3 pi^2/(sqrt(2) g_s)) exp(3 beta_0/16 [pi^2/4 - 1]) exp(-d), where d is a number of order unity from the matching coefficient.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering renormalization group analysis of Son (1999), who found Delta ~ c g_s^{-5} exp(-3 pi^2/(sqrt(2) g_s)), and the Schwinger-Dyson equation approach of Schafer and Wilczek (1999). The authors show that simply changing the scale of g_s in the solution of the gap equation does not capture the largest contribution, and that the resummation of leading logs produces a factor ~20 enhancement that is independent of g_s. This work enables more precise predictions for the color superconducting gap at high density, though matching to the full QCD calculation at higher orders remains to be performed.
