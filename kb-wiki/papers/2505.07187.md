# Violation of NCQ scaling in hadron elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.0-7.7GeV

> We investigate the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow for various hadrons in non-central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\text{--}7.7\,\mathrm{GeV}\) using the AMPT model with string melting (SM) and pure hadron cascade (HC) modes. For the SM case, NCQ scaling is absent at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\) but is largely restored by \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =3.9\,\mathrm{GeV}\). Although quark coalescence occurs at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\), the lack of NCQ scaling is attributed to the insufficient development of quark elliptic flow and the limited production of strange quarks and antiquarks. This finding suggests that NCQ scaling could not be considered a definitive signature of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in the RHIC fixed-target energy region. For the HC case, as expected, no NCQ scaling is observed. However, a mass ordering in the elliptic flow emerges at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =4.5\,\mathrm{GeV}\), indicating that full thermalization may not be a prerequisite for mass ordering.

## Key claim
NCQ scaling of hadron elliptic flow is absent at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\) in the AMPT string melting model despite quark coalescence occurring, but is largely restored by \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.9\,\mathrm{GeV}\); this indicates that NCQ scaling cannot be considered a definitive signature of quark-gluon plasma formation in the RHIC fixed-target energy region.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model in both string melting (SM) mode, which includes partonic dynamics and quark coalescence hadronization, and pure hadron cascade (HC) mode, which excludes partonic degrees of freedom. They simulated non-central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\)–\(7.7\,\mathrm{GeV}\) with 10–40% centrality, computed elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) for \(p\), \(\Lambda^0\), \(\pi^+\), \(K^+\), and \(K^0_S\), and examined NCQ scaling by plotting \(v_2/n_q\) versus \((m_T - m_0)/n_q\). The analysis included varying parton scattering cross sections and removing the hadronic afterburner to isolate partonic effects.

## Key numbers
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\), NCQ scaling is entirely absent in the SM mode; it is largely restored by \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.9\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\), the number of strange quarks produced in midrapidity (\(|y| \le 0.5\)) is 0.175, compared to 7.23 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.5\,\mathrm{GeV}\); for strange antiquarks, the numbers are 0.100 and 3.89, respectively (more than a factor of 30 difference).
- Increasing the parton scattering cross section to \(30\,\mathrm{mb}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\) enhances quark elliptic flow development and makes NCQ scaling more pronounced.
- In the HC mode, a clear mass ordering in elliptic flow emerges at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.5\,\mathrm{GeV}\), but no mass ordering is observed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context
This work extends experimental observations from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, where STAR found NCQ scaling present at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.5\,\mathrm{GeV}\) (Abdallah 2021) but violated at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\,\mathrm{GeV}\) (Abdallah 2022). It systematically investigates the mechanism of NCQ scaling violation in the 3.0–7.7 GeV range using the AMPT model, building on earlier theoretical studies by Dunlop (2011) and Goudarzi (2020) that attributed violations to baryon stopping and flavor-dependent quark flow. The results challenge the use of NCQ scaling as a definitive QGP signature at low energies and motivate the search for less model-dependent phase transition observables, such as the yield ratio of identical strange particles in dual-reaction systems proposed by Zhu (2025).
