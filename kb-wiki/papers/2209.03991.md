# Bound states and electromagnetic radiation of relativistically rotating cylindrical wells

> We compute the effect of rigid rotation on the non-relativistic bound states. The energy levels of the bound states increase with the angular velocity of rotation until at certain value of the angular velocity they are completely pushed out into the continuum which corresponds to dissociation of the bound states. When the angular velocity exceeds the critical value at which the ground state disappears into the continuum, no bound state is possible. This effect should have important consequences for the phenomenology of the quark-gluon plasma. One of the ways to study it experimentally is to observe the electromagnetic radiation emitted by a rotating bound state. We compute the corresponding intensity of electromagnetic radiation and show that it strongly depends on the angular velocity of rotation.

# Bound states and electromagnetic radiation of relativistically rotating cylindrical wells

## Key claim

Rigid rotation increases the energy levels of non-relativistic bound states in a cylindrical well, eventually pushing them into the continuum at a critical angular velocity; the effect is computed for a model that treats the bound state non-relativistically and imposes a causal boundary condition at r = 1/Ω, with results that depend strongly on whether ΩR is larger or smaller than unity.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for a particle in a cylindrical well of radius R and constant depth U₀, rotating with constant angular velocity Ω about its center-of-mass axis. The Hamiltonian is H = H₀ − ΩJz, so the problem reduces to solving H₀ψ = (E + mΩ)ψ with boundary conditions that include causality (wave function vanishes at r = 1/Ω). The cases of slow rotation (R < 1/Ω) and rapid rotation (R > 1/Ω) are treated separately, with matching conditions at r = R for the slow case and a Dirichlet condition at r = 1/Ω for the rapid case. A two-dimensional attractive 1/r potential is also analyzed as a smoother alternative. The electromagnetic radiation intensity from transitions between bound states is computed in the electric dipole approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the shallow well limit (β² ≪ 1, m = 0, kz = 0), the ground state energy at Ω = 0 is E₀ = −(2/γ̃²MR²) exp(−2/MU₀R²), where γ̃ ≈ 1.78.
- The critical angular velocity for dissociation in the shallow well is Ωc = 2√(2M|E₀|)/ln(2π).
- For the 2D 1/r potential, the first azimuthally symmetric level (m = 0, n′ = 1) disappears at ℓ = ℓ₁,₀ = 0.72, where ℓ = αM/Ω; the next such level (n′ = 2) disappears at ℓ = ℓ₂,₀ = 3.84.
- For charmonium estimates: J/ψ binding energy is 0.59 GeV, reduced mass M = 0.63 GeV, giving α = 0.72; J/ψ dissociates at Ω ≈ 3 fm⁻¹, ψ′ at Ω ≈ 0.6 fm⁻¹.
- For the rapidly rotating infinite cylindrical well (ΩR > 1), the radiation intensity scales as Wfi = (RΩ)⁶ Wfi^(Ω=0).

## Context

This work extends studies of rotating quantum systems (Vilenkin 1980, Becattini 2007, Ambrus 2014, Chernodub 2016) motivated by the large local vorticity observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (STAR 2017, Deng 2016). The authors model the effect of rotation on bound states as a centrifugal effect that complements Debye screening in dissociating quarkonium states in the quark-gluon plasma. The results enable estimates of rotation-induced dissociation thresholds for charmonia and predict a steep increase in electromagnetic radiation intensity from rotating bound states, which could serve as an experimental diagnostic for plasma vorticity.
