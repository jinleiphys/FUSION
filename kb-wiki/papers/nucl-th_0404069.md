# Phi Meson Propagation and Decay at Finite Temperature

> We study spectral properties of the phi meson at finite temperature using an effective Lagrangian together with finite-temperature field theory as a basis for modeling. General field-theoretic arguments are then used to establish the lifetime of the phi in hot hadronic matter. We find from the model, and we therefore propose a scenario in which, the phi decays inside the fireball. Early decays into $μ^{+}μ^{-}$ occur at high temperature while measurable hadronic decays into $K^{+}K^{-}$ (not suffering rescattering) occur at freezeout, where flow could be substantial. These results provide a consistent picture, if not possible interpretation of the NA49 and NA50 data from CERN.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the phi meson decays inside the fireball at high temperature (roughly 5–8 fm/c lifetime), with early decays into muon pairs occurring at high temperature while measurable hadronic decays into kaon pairs occur at freezeout where flow is substantial; this scenario provides a possible interpretation of the NA49 and NA50 data, but the author notes the temperature and flow values are only indications and not the result of an exhaustive chi-square search.

## Method

The author uses a three-flavor chiral effective Lagrangian to model phi meson interactions with light mesons, computing the phi self-energy at one-loop (kaon bubble and tadpole graphs, pion-rho loop) and two-loop (scattering processes) levels using finite-temperature field theory with Matsubara frequency sums. The spectral function is constructed from the self-energy, and the finite-temperature decay rate is obtained by integrating the generalized Breit-Wigner distribution over all momenta and off-shell energies, then dividing by the phi number density to extract the lifetime. The transverse mass distribution for kaons is computed using a generalized Siemens-Rasmussen formula for radial flow with quantum statistics.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum phi meson lifetime is approximately 45 fm/c
- At high temperature, the phi lifetime drops to 5–8 fm/c in the model
- Muon pair production is dominated by temperatures around 210 MeV
- Kaon distribution is consistent with a freezeout temperature of 125 MeV and a flow velocity of v = 0.52c
- The collision rate increases especially rapidly past 150 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the "phi puzzle" from CERN experiments NA49 and NA50, where kaon spectra showed a larger inverse slope parameter than muon spectra, suggesting inconsistent flow effects. The author extends prior work on meson self-energies and collision rates (Haglin 1995, Volker 2002) and baryon contributions (Wang et al. 1998) to provide a scenario where early phi decays inside the fireball produce muon pairs at high temperature while kaons from later decays at freezeout carry flow signatures. The approach enables a consistent interpretation of the two experimental channels and opens the door for more complete branching ratio studies to be published separately.
