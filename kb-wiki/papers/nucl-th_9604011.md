# Relativistic view on the nuclear effects in the deep inelastic scattering on deuteron

> We analyze nuclear effects in the deep inelastic scattering on deuteron in the framework of the covariant approach. It is shown that this approach gives us the way to investigate the role of relativistic effects in the deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on deuteron, such as the relativistic kinematics and the off-mass-shell behavior of the Compton amplitude of nucleon in the consistent manner. We have obtained that taking into account of the nucleon amplitude off-mass-shell behavior gives us an analog of the interaction corrections in nonrelativistic models.

## Key claim

The covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach to deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron shows that the off-mass-shell behavior of the nucleon amplitude produces an analog of the interaction corrections found in nonrelativistic models, but the analysis neglects contributions from additional structure functions and from P-states in the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter formalism to write the deuteron hadron tensor as a convolution of the nucleon hadron tensor with distribution functions derived from the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function. They expand the nucleon propagator near its mass-shell pole, retaining the first derivative term, and express the deuteron structure function \(F_2^D\) in the laboratory frame in terms of the nucleon structure function \(F_2^N\) and its derivative, plus a term involving the derivative of the vertex function with respect to the nucleon relative energy. They then take the nonrelativistic limit by expanding in powers of \(\mathbf{p}^2/m^2\) to compare with standard nonrelativistic impulse-approximation results.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron structure function \(F_2^D(x_D)\) is expressed as an integral over the nucleon three-momentum \(\mathbf{k}\) with the Bjorken variable for the nucleon given by \(x_N = -q^2 / [(E - k_3) q_0]\) and for the deuteron by \(x_D = -q^2 / (M_D q_0)\).
- In the nonrelativistic limit, the expression for \(F_2^D\) contains the kinetic energy \(T = 2E - 2m\) and the binding energy \(\epsilon = M_D - 2m\), with the wave function normalized as \(\int d^3k/(2\pi)^3 \, \Psi^2(\mathbf{k}) = 1\).
- The expansion near mass-shell uses the pole approximation \(1/[(P/2 + k)_0 - E]^2 \simeq i\pi \delta'((P/2 + k)_0 - E)\), and terms with powers of \(\langle V \rangle / M_D\) larger than two are neglected, where \(\langle V \rangle\) is the mean nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Context

This work extends the covariant Bethe-Salpeter treatment of the deuteron (Burov and Molochkov, building on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism) to deep inelastic scattering, providing a framework to separate relativistic kinematics from off-mass-shell effects. It directly compares with nonrelativistic impulse-approximation calculations (e.g., the nonrelativistic field theory model cited as "nonrel" in the text) and shows that the relativistic impulse approximation contains both Fermi-motion effects and an analog of mesonic corrections. The results enable future investigation of the neglected contributions from additional structure functions and from P-states in the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function, which the authors state they will analyze next.
