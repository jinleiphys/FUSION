# Nucleon-antinucleon interaction

> A review is presented of the antinucleon-nucleon interaction, and some related issues such as fundamental symmetries, annihilation mechanisms, antinucleon-nucleus scattering, antiprotonic atoms and neutron-antineutron oscillations. The overall perspective is historical but the modern approaches are also presented.

## Key claim

This review paper presents a historical and modern overview of the antinucleon-nucleon interaction, but the abstract's claim of covering "modern approaches" is hedged: the author notes that chiral effective theory for the antinucleon-nucleon interaction "could perhaps be improved" because its low-energy constants are tuned to fit phase-shifts "that are not free from ambiguities," unlike modern nucleon-nucleon potentials which are fitted directly to observables.

## Method

The paper reviews the antinucleon-nucleon interaction by first describing the G-parity rule of Fermi and Yang (1959), which relates nucleon-nucleon amplitudes to antinucleon-nucleon amplitudes by flipping the sign of contributions from mesons with odd G-parity. It then surveys optical models where the long-range interaction is derived from meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials via the G-parity rule, supplemented by an empirical complex core to account for annihilation. The review also covers baryon-exchange mechanisms, quark-level rearrangement models, chiral effective field theory approaches, and applications to antiprotonic atoms, antinucleon-nucleus scattering, and neutron-antineutron oscillations in nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The charge to mass ratios |q|/m of antiproton and proton are identical to about 10^{-10}, and their magnetic moments |mu| to about 10^{-9} (Zyla 2020).
- Optical potentials require a very strong absorption up to at least 0.8 fm to fit antinucleon-nucleon data.
- The analyzing power of elastic antiproton-proton scattering at laboratory momentum 679 MeV/c shows moderate values (Kunne 1988).
- The asymmetry in antiproton-proton annihilation into pi pi or K K reaches |A_n| near 1 in some wide ranges of energy and angles (Hasan 1992).
- For the deuteron, the reduced lifetime T_r for neutron-antineutron oscillation is about 2.5 x 10^{22} s^{-1} (Dover 1982), with a chiral effective theory calculation giving 1.1 x 10^{22} s^{-1} (Oosterhof 2019) later revised to near agreement with Dover et al. (Haidenbauer 2019).
- For argon-40 relevant to the DUNE experiment, T_r is about 5.6 x 10^{22} s^{-1} (Barrow 2019).
- The free-neutron oscillation limit from the Grenoble experiment is about tau_{n anti-n} greater than 10^{-8} s (BaldoCeolin 1994).

## Context

This review extends the long tradition of phenomenological studies of the antinucleon-nucleon interaction dating back to the discovery of the antiproton in 1955 by Chamberlain and Segre, and the subsequent charge-exchange reaction giving access to the antineutron. It covers the development from early optical potentials (Bryan 1968, Dover-Richard 1980, Kohno-Weise 1986) through the LEAR experimental program at CERN to modern chiral effective field theory approaches (Dai 2017). The review enables future work with the PANDA experiment at Darmstadt and provides the nuclear physics foundation for interpreting neutron-antineutron oscillation searches in experiments such as DUNE and Super-Kamiokande.
