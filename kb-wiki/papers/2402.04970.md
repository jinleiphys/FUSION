# A global study of $α$-clusters decay in heavy and superheavy nuclei with half-life and preformation factor

> A detailed study of $α$-clusters decay is exhibited by incorporating crucial microscopic nuclear structure information into the estimations of half-life and preformation factor. For the first time, using the k-cross validation approach, two semi-empirical formulas for (i) $α$-decay half-life and (ii) $α$-particle preformation factor, are picked out and subsequently modified by including shell, odd-nucleon blocking, and asymmetry effects along with the usual dependence on $α$-decay energy ($Q_α$) and angular momentum of $α$-particle. Both the formulas are fitted for the two different regions separated by neutron number N$=$126, as from the experimental systematics the role of N$=$126 shell closure is found decisive in determining the trends of $Q_α$, $α$-decay half-life, and $α$-particle preformation factor. It is found that the inclusion of the above-mentioned degrees of freedom significantly reduces the errors in the estimations when compared with several other similar modified/refitted semi-empirical relations indicating the robustness of the proposed formulas. The predictions of $α$-decay half-life throughout the periodic chart have been made including the unknown territory, future probable decay chain of self-conjugate nucleus $^{112}$Ba terminated on $^{100}$Sn, decay chain of $^{208}$Pa through new isotope $^{204}$Ac as well as decay chains of awaiting superheavy nuclei $^{298}$Og and $^{299}$120.

## Key claim

The authors propose that including shell effects (separated by the N=126 neutron shell closure), odd-nucleon blocking, and isospin asymmetry in semi-empirical formulas for both α-decay half-life and α-particle preformation factor significantly reduces estimation errors compared to existing formulas, but the improvement is validated specifically through a k-cross validation approach on a dataset of 349 nuclei, with separate fitting for regions N≤126 and N>126.

## Method

The authors used a 5-fold cross-validation method on 349 nuclei from NUBASE2020 to compare 15 existing semi-empirical α-decay half-life formulas and 6 preformation factor formulas, refitting all on the same dataset. They then proposed a new modified Tagepera-Nurmia (NMTN) formula for half-life and a modified Deng-Zhang (MDZ) formula for preformation factor, each with separate coefficients for N≤126 and N>126 regions, incorporating terms for angular momentum (l), isospin asymmetry (I=(N-Z)/A), and odd-nucleon blocking (h). The preformation factors were extracted experimentally using the WKB approximation for barrier penetrability and the assault frequency formula.

## Key numbers

- The NMTN formula achieved an average RMSE of 0.4172 across 5 test sets in k-cross validation, compared to 0.4189 for the next best formula (Soylu 2021)
- The NMTN formula yielded χ² per degree of freedom = 0.15 with 12 total parameters, versus χ² = 0.61 for Soylu 2021 with 6 parameters
- The MDZ formula for preformation factor achieved an average RMSE of 0.4357 across 5 test sets, compared to 0.4596 for DZ 2021
- The NMTN formula RMSE for the full dataset of 349 nuclei was 0.38, with average prediction uncertainties of ±0.3821 for N≤126 and ±0.6324 for N>126 nuclei
- The NMTN formula coefficients for N≤126: a=0.7838, b=-20.4118, c=0.1630, d=2.9908, with h=0.1562 for odd-A and h=0.3515 for odd-odd nuclei
- The NMTN formula coefficients for N>126: a=0.7988, b=-18.3048, c=0.3041, d=-3.0399, with h=0.1198 for odd-A and h=0.0986 for odd-odd nuclei

## Context

This work extends the line of semi-empirical α-decay formulas originating from the Geiger-Nuttall law and the Viola-Seaborg systematics, including recent modifications by Soylu (2021), Royer (2020), and Deng and Zhang (2021). The authors specifically compare against formulas from Akrawy (2018, 2019), Singh (2020), and Sharma (2021), among others. The study enables reliable predictions of α-decay half-lives for unknown nuclei, including the decay chain of ¹¹²Ba terminating on ¹⁰⁰Sn, the decay chain of ²⁰⁸Pa through the new isotope ²⁰⁴Ac, and decay chains of superheavy nuclei ²⁹⁸Og and ²⁹⁹120.
