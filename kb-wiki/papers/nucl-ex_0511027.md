# Charge independence studied in $NN\to dπ$ reactions

> We review to what extent charge independence breaking (\emph{CIB}) in isospin related reactions of the type $NN\to dπ$ can or could be seen in existing data. In doing this we present fits to global and threshold cross sections including most recent data. Applying these we point out the probable impossibility to make model independent predictions even based solely on the different threshold energies and the well known $pp\to dπ^+$. A possible discrepancy is seen between the $np\to dπ^0$ and $pp\to dπ^+$ data, which may require invoking explicitly isospin symmetry breaking interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, while charge independence breaking (CIB) in the isospin-related reactions \(NN\to d\pi\) can be studied by comparing \(pp\to\pi^+d\) and \(np\to\pi^0d\) cross sections, it is probably impossible to make model-independent predictions based solely on threshold energy differences and the well-known \(pp\to\pi^+d\) data; a possible discrepancy between the two reactions may require invoking explicitly isospin symmetry breaking interactions.

## Method

The authors performed global fits to total cross section data for \(pp\to\pi^+d\) and \(np\to\pi^0d\) as functions of the dimensionless pion momentum \(\eta = q/m_{\pi^+}\), applying Coulomb corrections via Gamow factors. They extracted angle-integrated squared matrix elements \(R\) and compared the two reactions at low energies using a low-energy expansion \(R = \alpha_0(1+\alpha_1\eta^2)\), and also studied the anisotropy ratio \(a(\eta) = A_2/A_0\) from Legendre polynomial expansions of differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The threshold matrix element for \(np\to\pi^0d\) (multiplied by the isospin factor 2) is \(\alpha_0 = (185\pm 2)\times 10^3\ \mathrm{fm}^6\mathrm{MeV}^2\), while for \(pp\to\pi^+d\) it is \(\alpha_0 = (144.5\pm 4.7)\times 10^3\ \mathrm{fm}^6\mathrm{MeV}^2\).
- The \(p\)-wave parameter \(\alpha_1\) is \(3.97\pm 0.22\) for \(np\to\pi^0d\) and \(8.57\pm 0.52\) for \(pp\to\pi^+d\).
- The relative uncertainty in the total cross section fit parameter \(A_0\) is 2.7% for \(np\to\pi^0d\) at \(\eta=0.59\) and 0.76% for \(pp\to\pi^+d\) at \(\eta=0.51\).
- The relative uncertainty in the anisotropy \(a\) is 4.8% for \(np\to\pi^0d\) and 1.8% for \(pp\to\pi^+d\).
- The Coulomb correction factor \(|C_1^{\rm e}|^2/|C_0|^2\) differs from unity by less than 2% for \(\eta > 0.05\).
- The kinematic shift in the Mandelstam \(t\) variable between the two reactions is approximately 0.01065 GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin symmetry breaking in meson production, particularly the model calculations of Niskanen and Vestama (1997) and the global fit of Ritchie (1991). It also relates to measurements of the reactions \(pd\to\pi^0\,^3\mathrm{He}\) and \(pd\to\pi^+\,^3\mathrm{H}\) by Abdel-Bary (2003) and Betigeri (2001), which showed deviations from the isospin ratio of two. The paper enables future dedicated experiments to search for charge independence breaking by comparing \(np\to\pi^0d\) and \(pp\to\pi^+d\) with improved normalization and precision.
