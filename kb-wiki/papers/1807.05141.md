# Dynamical initialization and hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present a fully three-dimensional model providing initial conditions for energy and conserved charge density distributions in heavy ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) collision energies. The model includes the dynamical deceleration of participating nucleons or valence quarks. It provides a realistic estimation of the initial baryon stopping during the early stage of collisions. We also present the implementation of the model with 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamics, which involves the addition of source terms that deposit energy and net-baryon densities produced by the initial state model at proper times greater than the initial time for the hydrodynamic simulation. The importance of this dynamical initialization stage on hadronic flow observables at the RHIC BES is quantified.

## Key claim
The paper presents a fully three-dimensional dynamical initialization model that provides initial conditions for energy and conserved charge densities in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, with the key hedge being that the baryon stopping during the initial collision stage is parameterized using a classical string deceleration model rather than calculated from first principles.

## Method
The authors employ a classical string deceleration model where colliding nucleons pull out strings and decelerate according to a string tension constant, depositing lost energy and momentum uniformly along the string to form the hot medium. The remaining energy, momentum, and baryon number at the string endpoints are fed into 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic simulations as source terms at proper times greater than the initial hydrodynamic start time. Two parameterizations of the rapidity loss function are tested against experimental data for charged hadron pseudorapidity distributions.

## Key numbers
- The string deceleration time is fixed to Δτ = 0.5 fm/c
- For central Au+Au collisions at 19.6 GeV, the colliding nuclei take about τ_overlap = 1.5 fm/c to pass through each other
- The dynamical initialization results in a 5-10% reduction of the produced particle mean-p_T and elliptic flow coefficient compared to instantaneous hydrodynamization
- A variance of σ_μB ∼ 60 MeV in net baryon chemical potential is observed as the fireball crosses the chemical freeze-out line in the transition region for 0-5% Au+Au collisions at 19.6 GeV

## Context
This work extends the dynamical initialization frameworks proposed by Shen (2017) and Okai (2017) to connect pre-equilibrium dynamics with hydrodynamic evolution on a local collision-by-collision basis. It addresses challenges at low collision energies O(10) GeV where finite longitudinal extension of nuclei leads to substantial overlapping times, building on prior models by Cassing (2009), Karpenko (2015), and Denicol (2018). The framework enables detailed mapping of fireball trajectories on the T-μB phase diagram, which is essential for modeling how critical fluctuations evolve out-of-equilibrium as the fireball passes the critical region, following the event-by-event study first performed by Bass (2012).
