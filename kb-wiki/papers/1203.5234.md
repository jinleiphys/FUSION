# Realistic calculations of Kbar-N-N, Kbar-N-N-N, and Kbar-Kbar-N-N quasibound states

> Binding energies and widths of three-body KbarNN, and of four-body KbarNNN and KbarKbarNN nuclear quasibound states are calculated in the hyperspherical basis, using realistic NN potentials and subthreshold energy dependent chiral KbarN interactions. Results of previous K^-pp calculations are reproduced and an upper bound is placed on the binding energy of a K^-d quasibound state. A self consistent handling of energy dependence is found to restrain binding, keeping the calculated four-body ground-state binding energies to relatively low values of about 30 MeV. The lightest strangeness -2 particle-stable Kbar nuclear cluster is most probably KbarKbarNN. The calculated Kbar N -> pi Y conversion widths range from approximately 30 MeV for the KbarNNN ground state to approximately 80 MeV for the KbarKbarNN ground state.

# Realistic calculations of Kbar-N-N, Kbar-N-N-N, and Kbar-Kbar-N-N quasibound states

## Key claim
The binding energies of four-body Kbar nuclear clusters are restrained to relatively low values of about 30 MeV by self-consistent handling of the energy-dependent chiral KbarN interaction, in strong disagreement with earlier phenomenological predictions of over 100 MeV. The lightest strangeness -2 particle-stable Kbar nuclear cluster is most probably KbarKbarNN, but it is bound by less than 10 MeV with respect to decay into a pair of (KbarN)_{I=0} Lambda(1405)-like quasibound states.

## Method
The authors performed nonrelativistic calculations in the hyperspherical-harmonics basis for three-body KbarNN and four-body KbarNNN and KbarKbarNN systems, using the Argonne AV4' NN potential and subthreshold energy-dependent chiral KbarN interactions from the HNJH model. The energy dependence of the KbarN interaction was treated self-consistently by requiring that the expectation value of the Mandelstam variable sqrt(s) derived from the solution of the Schroedinger equation agrees with the input value used for the KbarN potential, iterating typically five cycles to convergence.

## Key numbers
- KbarNN (K^-pp) with I=1/2, J^pi=0^-: binding energy B = 15.7 MeV, conversion width Gamma = 41.2 MeV, with self-consistent <delta sqrt(s)> = -43 MeV
- KbarNNN with I=0, J^pi=1/2^+: B = 29.3 MeV, Gamma = 32.9 MeV, <delta sqrt(s)> = -61 MeV
- KbarNNN with I=1, J^pi=1/2^+: B = 18.5 MeV, Gamma = 31.0 MeV, <delta sqrt(s)> = -36 MeV
- KbarKbarNN with I=0, J^pi=0^+: B = 32.1 MeV, Gamma = 80.5 MeV, <delta sqrt(s)> = -46 MeV
- (KbarN)_{I=0} quasibound state: B = 11.4 MeV, Gamma = 43.6 MeV, <delta sqrt(s)> = -11.4 MeV
- For KbarKbarNN, using threshold KbarN interaction (delta sqrt(s)=0) gives B = 43.0 MeV, compared to the self-consistent value of 32.1 MeV
- The KbarKbarNN ground-state rms distances: r_NN = 1.84 fm, r_NK = 1.88 fm, r_KK = 2.31 fm

## Context
This work extends the chiral-model calculations of Dote, Hyodo, and Weise (2008) for K^-pp to four-body KbarNNN and KbarKbarNN systems, using the same HNJH chiral KbarN interaction model. The results contradict earlier phenomenological predictions by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) for KbarNNN and by Yamazaki, Dote, and Akaishi (2004, 2011) for KbarKbarNN, which gave binding energies exceeding 100 MeV. The self-consistency procedure for the energy-dependent KbarN interaction, refined from prescriptions used by Dote et al. (2008) and by Cieply, Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2011), provides a robust mechanism that restrains binding and suggests that strange dense matter is unlikely to be realized through Kbar mesons as previously argued.
