# Chiral and Diquark condensates at large magnetic field in two-flavor superconducting quark matter

> We study the effect of a large magnetic field on the chiral and diquark condensates in a regime of moderately dense quark matter. Our focus is on the inter-dependence of the two condensates through non-perturbative quark mass and strong coupling effects, which we address in a 2-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. For magnetic fields $eB\lesssim 0.01$ GeV$^2$ (corresponding to $B\lesssim 10^{18}$G), our results agree qualitatively with the zero-field study of Huang et al., who found a mixed broken phase region where the chiral and superconducting gap are both non-zero. For $eB\gtrsim 0.01$ GeV$^2$ and moderate diquark-to-scalar coupling ratio $G_D/G_S$, we find that the chiral and superconducting transitions become weaker but with little change in either transition density. For large $G_D/G_S$ however, such a large magnetic field disrupts the mixed broken phase region and changes a smooth crossover found in the zero-field case to a first-order transition at neutron star interior densities.

## Key claim

For magnetic fields above $eB \gtrsim 0.01$ GeV$^2$ and a large diquark-to-scalar coupling ratio $G_D/G_S$, the magnetic field disrupts the mixed broken phase region found in the zero-field study of Huang et al. (2001) and changes a smooth crossover into a first-order transition at neutron star interior densities.

## Method

The authors employed a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar-isoscalar and scalar diquark interaction terms, introducing bosonic fields for the chiral condensate $\sigma$ and the diquark condensate $\Delta$. They derived coupled gap equations by minimizing the zero-temperature thermodynamic potential in the presence of an external magnetic field, using a sharp three-momentum cutoff regularization and summing over Landau levels. The gap equations were solved self-consistently by numerical iteration for various values of the coupling ratio $R = G_D/G_S$ and chemical potential $\mu$, both at zero magnetic field and at large magnetic fields up to $eB = 0.05$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- For $B=0$ and $R=0.75$, the critical chemical potential is $\mu_c = 331.2$ MeV, with a chiral gap jump $\delta_\sigma = 237.9$ MeV and a superconducting gap jump $\delta_\Delta = 80.6$ MeV (first-order transition).
- For $B=0$ and $R=1.11$, the transition becomes second order with $\mu_c = 284.9$ MeV and zero jumps in both gaps.
- For $B=0$ and $R=1.20$, the transition is a smooth crossover with $\mu_{cp} \sim 269.7$ MeV.
- For $eB = 0.05$ GeV$^2$ ($B = 8.5 \times 10^{18}$ G) and $R=1.20$, the crossover becomes a first-order transition at $\mu_c = 263.2$ MeV, with $\delta_\sigma = 40.7$ MeV and $\delta_\Delta = 25.5$ MeV.
- For $eB = 0.05$ GeV$^2$ and $R=0.75$, the first-order transition weakens: $\mu_c = 329.5$ MeV, $\delta_\sigma = 158.4$ MeV, $\delta_\Delta = 53.4$ MeV.
- For magnetic fields $eB \lesssim 0.01$ GeV$^2$ ($B \lesssim 10^{18}$ G), results agree qualitatively with the zero-field case.

## Context

This work extends the zero-field study of Huang et al. (2001) on the competition between chiral and diquark condensates in two-flavor quark matter, which found a mixed broken phase region where both condensates are non-zero. The authors incorporate the effect of large magnetic fields relevant to magnetar interiors, where fields up to $10^{18}$ G may exist. The results enable future studies of heterogeneous nucleation and domain formation in superconducting quark matter, with potential applications to energetic events in neutron stars, and connect to work on magnetic effects in color superconductivity by Ferrer et al. (2005, 2006) and on bulk viscosities in magnetized strange quark matter by Huang et al. (2009).
