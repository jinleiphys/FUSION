# Possible eta-mesic 3He states within the finite rank approximation

> We extend the method of time delay proposed by Eisenbud and Wigner, to search for unstable states formed by eta mesons and the 3He nucleus. Using few body equations to describe eta-3He elastic scattering, we predict resonances and unstable bound states within different models of the eta-N interaction. The eta-3He states predicted within this novel approach are in agreement with the recent claim of the evidence of eta-mesic 3He made by the TAPS collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Eisenbud-Wigner time-delay method to negative energies to identify unstable bound states (quasibound states) of the eta-3He system, finding evidence for such states near threshold that are consistent with the TAPS collaboration's claim of eta-mesic 3He, but with the caveat that the exact peak positions depend on the chosen model of the eta-nucleon interaction and are subject to distortions from threshold singularities.

## Method

The authors solve few-body equations for eta-3He elastic scattering within a Finite Rank Approximation (FRA), where the 3He nucleus remains in its ground state in the intermediate state. They evaluate the time delay, defined as the energy derivative of the phase shift, from the complex T-matrix at both positive and negative energies using three different coupled-channel models of the elementary eta-nucleon interaction: one from Bhalerao and Liu (1995), one from Fix and collaborators (2005), and one from Green and Wycech (2005). The time delay at negative energies is computed using purely imaginary momenta corresponding to off-shell elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

* For the Fix model (2005) with eta-N scattering length a_etaN = (0.88, 0.41) fm, a peak is found at a positive energy of 0.5 MeV and another peak centered slightly below zero energy.
* For the Bhalerao-Liu model (1995) with a_etaN = (0.28, 0.19) fm, a broad hump is found around -5 MeV.
* The TAPS collaboration reported a binding energy of -4.4 +/- 4.2 MeV for eta-mesic 3He, which the authors note is in agreement with their findings.
* The 3He break-up threshold is 7.7 MeV, which limits the applicability of the FRA approach.
* The deuteron binding energy used as a test case is -2.224 MeV, with square well parameters V = 34.6 MeV and R = 2.07 fm.

## Context

This work extends the time-delay method previously applied by the same group to characterize meson-nucleon and meson-meson resonances (Kelkar et al., 2004) and to find the exotic pentaquark resonance around 1540 MeV from K+N data (Kelkar et al., 2005). The results are consistent with the TAPS collaboration's experimental claim of eta-mesic 3He from photoproduction reactions, and with an optical potential analysis by Sibirtsev and collaborators (2004) which found that if an eta-mesic 3He state exists, its binding energy should not exceed 5 MeV and its width should not exceed 10 MeV. The work enables future studies to use eta-mesic nuclei as a probe of the eta-nucleon interaction and the N*(1535) resonance properties.
