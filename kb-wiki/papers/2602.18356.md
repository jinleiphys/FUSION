# Constraining the $ΛΛ$ interaction with terrestrial and astronomical data

> Terrestrial double-$Λ$ hypernuclear data and astronomical observations of neutron stars provide complementary constraints on the $ΛΛ$ interaction. In this work, we investigate the $ΛΛ$ interaction within a Skyrme energy density functional framework based on the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) models. We employ a Skyrme-type $ΛΛ$ interaction that includes the standard $s$- and $p$-wave terms, as well as a density-dependent term that effectively represents an $NΛΛ$ three-body force. The $s$-wave terms are constrained using data on double-$Λ$ hypernuclei supplemented by pseudodata obtained from core + $2Λ$ three-body model calculations including heavier hypernuclei. We show that the data on heavier systems are essential to simultaneously constrain the two $s$-wave parameters. We further explore the impact of the $p$-wave and $NΛΛ$ components on the neutron-star properties and find that appropriate repulsive contributions of these terms yield consistency with current neutron-star mass-radius observations. These results indicate that the present framework provides phenomenologically acceptable equations of state for dense $(N,Λ)$ matter over a wide range of densities and highlight the importance of future experimental data on heavier double-$Λ$ hypernuclei.

# Constraining the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction with terrestrial and astronomical data

## Key claim
The $s$-wave parameters of the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction can be simultaneously constrained only when pseudodata for heavier double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei (from core + $2\Lambda$ three-body model calculations) are included alongside the limited experimental data for light systems; the $p$-wave and density-dependent terms remain poorly constrained by hypernuclear data alone but can be restricted using neutron-star mass-radius observations, provided the $p$-wave repulsion is sufficiently strong.

## Method
The authors employed a Skyrme-type $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction within the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) energy density functional framework, including $s$-wave terms ($\lambda_0$, $\lambda_1$), a $p$-wave term ($\lambda_2$), and a density-dependent term ($\lambda_3$) representing an effective $N\Lambda\Lambda$ three-body force. The $s$-wave parameters were fitted to experimental binding energies of $^{11}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be and calculated values for $^{9}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Li and $^{9}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be, supplemented by pseudodata for $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ of $^{18}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$O, $^{34}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$S, $^{42}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Ca, and $^{58}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Ni from core + $2\Lambda$ three-body model calculations. The remaining parameters ($\lambda_2$, $\lambda_3$) were then explored by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for neutron-star configurations and comparing with the $2M_\odot$ mass constraint and NICER data for PSR J0740+6620.

## Key numbers
- $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ for $^{11}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be: 19.07 MeV (experimental, from Ek19)
- $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ for $^{9}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Li: 14.55 MeV (from four-body cluster model calculations in Hi02)
- $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ for $^{9}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be: 14.40 MeV (from four-body cluster model calculations in Hi02)
- $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ pseudodata: 1.22 MeV ($^{18}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$O), 1.07 MeV ($^{34}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$S), 1.00 MeV ($^{42}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Ca), 0.86 MeV ($^{58}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Ni)
- Optimized $\lambda_0$ values for LL2 sets range from -253.39 MeV fm$^3$ (KIDS-C) to -232.01 MeV fm$^3$ (KIDS-A)
- Optimized $\lambda_1$ values for LL2 sets range from 268.19 MeV fm$^5$ (KIDS-A) to 314.53 MeV fm$^5$ (KIDS-C)
- MD values for LL2 fits: 0.89% (KIDS-C) to 2.80% (KIDS-A)
- Upper bound on $\lambda_3$ from hypernuclear data: $\sim$700 MeV fm$^6$ for KIDS-A, $\sim$1000 MeV fm$^6$ for KIDS-D
- Maximum neutron-star mass exceeds $2M_\odot$ for $\lambda_2 \gtrsim 300$ MeV fm$^5$ (KIDS-A) and $\lambda_2 \gtrsim 100$ MeV fm$^5$ (KIDS-D)
- Central $\Lambda$-hyperon fraction in massive neutron stars: $Y_{\Lambda,c} \sim 0.2-0.3$ for $\lambda_2=600$ MeV fm$^5$
- Acceptable parameter ranges: $-500 \lesssim \lambda_0 \lesssim -200$ MeV fm$^3$, $250 \lesssim \lambda_1 \lesssim 500$ MeV fm$^5$, $100 \lesssim \lambda_2 \lesssim 600$ MeV fm$^5$, $0 \leq \lambda_3 \lesssim 1000$ MeV fm$^6$

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme-type $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction framework originally proposed by Lanskaya (1998), who fit only two parameters to the single datum of $^{13}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$B. It complements a recent Bayesian analysis by Shin et al. (2026) that simultaneously constrained all five $\Lambda\Lambda$ parameters using both hypernuclear and neutron-star data. The present study uniquely incorporates pseudodata from core + $2\Lambda$ three-body model calculations for medium-mass hypernuclei, demonstrating that heavier systems are essential to separate bulk and surface contributions. The results enable future experimental data on heavier double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei to further refine the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction and improve predictions for hyperonic neutron-star matter.
