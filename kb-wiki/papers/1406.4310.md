# Baryon spectrum with $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermions

> The masses of the low lying baryons are evaluated using a total of ten ensembles of dynamical twisted mass fermion gauge configurations. The simulations are performed using two degenerate flavors of light quarks, and a strange and a charm quark fixed to approximately their physical values. The light sea quarks correspond to pseudo scalar masses in the range of about 210~MeV to 430~MeV. We use the Iwasaki improved gluonic action at three values of the coupling constant corresponding to lattice spacing $a=0.094$~fm, 0.082~fm and 0.065~fm determined from the nucleon mass. We check for both finite volume and cut-off effects on the baryon masses. We examine the issue of isospin symmetry breaking for the octet and decuplet baryons and its dependence on the lattice spacing. We show that in the continuum limit isospin breaking is consistent with zero, as expected. We performed a chiral extrapolation of the forty baryon masses using SU(2) $χ$PT. After taking the continuum limit and extrapolating to the physical pion mass our results are in good agreement with experiment. We provide predictions for the mass of the doubly charmed $Ξ_{cc}^*$, as well as of the doubly and triply charmed $Ω$s that have not yet been determined experimentally.

## Key claim

The authors compute the low-lying baryon spectrum using lattice QCD with \(N_f=2+1+1\) twisted mass fermions and, after taking the continuum limit and extrapolating to the physical pion mass, find good agreement with experiment; however, the largest systematic uncertainty in predicting masses at the physical point comes from the chiral extrapolation, not from discretization or volume effects.

## Method

The authors generate ten ensembles of dynamical twisted mass fermion gauge configurations with two degenerate light quarks and a strange and charm quark fixed near their physical values, using the Iwasaki improved gluonic action at three lattice spacings (\(a = 0.094\) fm, 0.082 fm, 0.065 fm). They compute two-point correlation functions for octet and decuplet baryons, apply Gaussian and APE smearing to suppress excited states, and extract ground-state masses via exponential and constant fits. For spin-3/2 baryons, a spin-3/2 projector is applied to remove contamination from spin-1/2 states. Chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass are performed using SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, and the continuum limit is taken assuming \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) scaling.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses in the sea range from about 210 MeV to 430 MeV across the ten ensembles.
* Lattice spacings are \(a = 0.0936(13)\) fm at \(\beta=1.90\), \(a = 0.0823(10)\) fm at \(\beta=1.95\), and \(a = 0.0646(7)\) fm at \(\beta=2.10\), determined from the nucleon mass.
* The lightest pion mass simulated is 213 MeV (at \(\beta=2.10\), \(a\mu_l=0.0015\)).
* The heaviest pion mass simulated is 432 MeV (at \(\beta=1.95\), \(a\mu_l=0.0075\)).
* The lattice volumes are \(L=3.0\) fm (\(32^3\times64\)) at \(\beta=1.90\), \(L=2.6\) fm (\(32^3\times64\)) at \(\beta=1.95\), and \(L=3.1\) fm (\(48^3\times96\)) at \(\beta=2.10\).
* The strange and charm valence quark masses are tuned using the experimental masses of the \(\Omega^-\) and \(\Lambda_c\) baryons, respectively.
* Isospin breaking effects in baryon masses are found to be consistent with zero in the continuum limit, as expected from \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) scaling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the low-lying baryon octet and decuplet using \(N_f=2\) twisted mass fermions (Alexandrou et al., 2009) to the \(N_f=2+1+1\) case, enabling a continuum extrapolation and assessment of volume effects. It complements other lattice QCD studies of charmed baryons that use staggered sea quarks with clover or domain wall valence quarks (Briceno et al., 2012; Na et al., 2008; Liu et al., 2009). The results provide predictions for the masses of the doubly charmed \(\Xi_{cc}^*\) and the doubly and triply charmed \(\Omega\) baryons, which have not yet been determined experimentally, and can serve as theoretical input for experiments at BES-III and PANDA at FAIR.
