# Inclusive studies of two- and three-nucleon short-range correlations in $^3$H and $^3$He

> Inclusive electron scattering at carefully chosen kinematics can isolate scattering from the high-momentum nucleons in short-range correlations (SRCs). SRCs are produced by the hard, short-distance interactions of nucleons in the nucleus, and because the two-nucleon (2N) SRCs arise from the same N-N interaction in all nuclei, the cross section in the SRC-dominated regime is identical up to an overall scaling factor. This scaling behavior has been used to identify SRC dominance and to measure the contribution of SRCs in a wide range of nuclei. We examine this scaling behavior over a range of momentum transfers using new data on $^2$H, $^3$H, and $^3$He, and find an expanded scaling region compared to heavy nuclei. Motivated by this improved scaling, we examine the $^3$H and $^3$He data in kinematics where three-nucleon SRCs may play an important role. The data for the largest struck nucleon momenta are consistent with isolation of scattering from three-nucleon SRCs, and suggest that the very highest momentum nucleons in $^3$He have a nearly isospin-independent momentum configuration.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the scaling region for two-nucleon short-range correlations (2N-SRCs) extends to lower momentum transfer ($Q^2$) and lower light-cone momentum ($\alpha$) in $^3$H and $^3$He compared to heavier nuclei, and that the $^3$H/$^3$He cross section ratio at the largest struck nucleon momenta is consistent with isolation of three-nucleon SRCs (3N-SRCs) and suggests a nearly isospin-independent momentum configuration, though the authors hedge that the data may not fully isolate 3N-SRC dominance and that additional measurements at multiple $Q^2$ values are needed for a definitive test.

## Method

Inclusive electron scattering cross sections were measured for $^2$H, $^3$H, and $^3$He targets in Jefferson Lab Hall A using the High Resolution Spectrometers at beam energies of 2.222, 4.332, 4.325, and 5.766 GeV, covering a mean $Q^2$ range of 0.6 to 2.6 GeV$^2$ and an $x$ range of 0.5 to 3. The cross section ratios ($^3$He/$^2$H, $^3$H/$^2$H, and $^3$H/$^3$He) were analyzed as functions of the light-cone momentum variables $\alpha_{2N}$ (for 2N-SRC studies) and $\alpha_{3N}$ (for 3N-SRC studies) to examine scaling behavior and isolate contributions from high-momentum nucleons in short-range correlated pairs and triples.

## Key numbers

- The 2N-SRC plateau in $^3$H/$^2$H and $^3$He/$^2$H ratios is observed at $\alpha_{2N} > 1.2$ for $Q^2 \ge 1$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to $k_{min} \approx 200$ MeV/c, compared to $\alpha_{2N} \approx 1.3$ ($k_{min} \approx 300$ MeV/c) in heavier nuclei.
- The $^3$H/$^3$He ratio shows 2N-SRC scaling down to $Q^2 = 0.6$ GeV$^2$, the lowest $Q^2$ value measured.
- The fitted 2N-SRC plateau value for $^3$H/$^3$He is $R = 0.854$ (from Li et al. 2022).
- The $^3$H/$^3$He ratio at $\alpha_{3N} > 1.4$ is consistent with a plateau near 0.7, as predicted for 3N-SRC dominance assuming an isospin-independent configuration.
- The $^3$H/$^3$He ratio at $\alpha_{3N} > 1.45$ rules out dominance of the singly-occurring nucleon in 3N-SRCs, which would yield a ratio of $\sigma_{ep} / \sigma_{en} \approx 2.5$.

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlation scaling from heavy nuclei (e.g., Frankfurt et al. 1993, Fomin et al. 2012) to the lightest mirror nuclei $^3$H and $^3$He, confirming the expectation of an expanded scaling region due to reduced Fermi motion and smaller final-state interactions. The comparison of $^3$H/$^3$He ratios at $x > 2$ provides the first experimental constraint on the isospin structure of three-nucleon SRCs, building on theoretical predictions from Sargsian (2019) and Day et al. (2018). The results enable future measurements at higher $\alpha_{3N}$ and multiple $Q^2$ values to definitively test 3N-SRC dominance and the nature of the three-nucleon interaction at short distances.
