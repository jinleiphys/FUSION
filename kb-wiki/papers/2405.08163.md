# Deep TOV to characterize Neutron Stars

> Astrophysical observations, theoretical models, and terrestrial experiments probe different regions of neutron star (NS) interior. Therefore, it is essential to consistently combine the information from these sources. This analysis requires multiple evaluations of Tolman Oppenheimer Volkoff equations which can become computationally expensive with a large number of observations. Further, multi-messenger astronomy requires rapid NS characterization via gravitational waves for efficient electromagnetic follow-up. In this work, we develop a novel neural network-based map from the EoS curve to the mass and radius of cold non-rotating NS. We estimate a speed-up of an order of magnitude when compared with the state-of-the-art RePrimAnd solver and an average error of 1e-3 when calculating the mass and radius of the neutron star. Additionally, we also develop neural network solvers for obtaining EoS curves from a physics conforming EoS model, FRZ$χ_{1.5}$. We utilize this efficient continuous map to measure the sensitivity of model parameters of FRZ$χ_{1.5}$ towards mass and radius. We show that 8 out of 18 parameters of this model are sensitive by at least three orders of magnitude higher than the remaining 10 parameters. This information will be useful in further speeding up, as well as probing the crucial parameter space, in the parameter estimation from astrophysical observations using this physics-conforming EoS model.

## Key claim

The authors develop a neural network-based map from the equation of state (EoS) curve to the mass and radius of cold non-rotating neutron stars, claiming a speed-up of an order of magnitude compared to the state-of-the-art RePrimAnd solver and an average error of 1e-3 for mass and radius calculations. However, this accuracy is achieved only for smooth EoS prescriptions; for piecewise polytropes and the FRZχ₁.₅ model, which have discontinuities in the speed of sound, the accuracy degrades to approximately 99% (relative error less than 0.01).

## Method

The authors train a physics-informed neural network (deep TOV) to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, mapping discretized pressure and energy density inputs to radial mass and distance outputs. They train the network using 26,782 EoS curves from a piecewise polytrope model with seven polytropes (four fixed for the crust, three varied for the core), and test it on spectral EoS curves and the physics-conforming FRZχ₁.₅ model. Additionally, they develop two auxiliary neural networks (EoS solver and Pc^max solver) to rapidly evaluate the FRZχ₁.₅ EoS model, using a physics-informed loss function that constrains the derivative of the network to match the speed of sound.

## Key numbers

- Speed-up of approximately 8 times compared to the RePrimAnd solver: RePrimAnd takes ~151 ms while deep TOV takes ~19 ms to evaluate masses and radii of 1000 neutron stars on a single-core CPU.
- Relative error less than 0.01 (99% accuracy) for computing total mass and radius from piecewise polytrope EoS curves.
- Average error of approximately 0.1% (99.9% accuracy) for both mass and radius when testing on spectral EoS curves.
- 8 out of 18 parameters of the FRZχ₁.₅ model have sensitivities (total effect index S_T) at least three orders of magnitude higher than the remaining 10 parameters, as quantified by variance-based Sobol sensitivity analysis.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to apply machine learning to neutron star characterization, such as the two-network approach of Soma et al. (2023) which lacked physical constraints, by incorporating the TOV equation directly into the neural network optimization. The authors also build on the physics-conforming FRZχ₁.₅ EoS model of Tiwari and Pai (2023) and the piecewise polytrope model of Read et al. (2008). The efficient mapping enables rapid parameter estimation from future multi-messenger observations (e.g., LIGO-Virgo-Kagra and NICER) and provides the first quantified sensitivity study of EoS model parameters, which can guide which parameters to prioritize in Bayesian inference.
