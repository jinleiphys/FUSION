# Dynamical supersymmetry for strange quark and $ud$ antidiquark in hadron mass spectrum

> Speculating that the $ud$ diquark with spin 0 has a similar mass to the constituent $s$ quark, we introduce a symmetry between the $s$ quark and the $\overline{ud}$ diquark. Constructing an algebra for this symmetry, we regard a triplet of the $s$ quarks with spin up and down and the $\overline{ud}$ diquark with spin 0 as a fundamental representation of this algebra. We further build higher representations constructed by direct products of the fundamental representations. We propose assignments of hadrons to the multiples of this algebra. We find in particular that $\{D_{s}, D_{s}^{*}, Λ_{c}\}$, $\{η_{s}, ϕ, Λ, f_{0}(1370)\}$ and $\{Ω_{c}, T_{sc}\}$ form a triplet, a nonet and a quintet, respectively, where $T_{sc}$ is a genuine tetraquark meson composed of $\overline{ud}sc$. We also find a mass relation between them by introducing the symmetry breaking due to the mass difference between the $s$ quark and the $\overline{ud}$ diquark. The masses of possible tetraquarks $\overline{ud}sc$ and $\overline{ud}sb$ are estimated from the symmetry breaking and the masses of $Ω_{c}$ and $Ω_{b}$ to be 2.942 GeV and 6.261 GeV, respectively.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a dynamical supersymmetry between the strange constituent quark and the spin-0 $\overline{ud}$ antidiquark, based on the speculation that these two objects have similar masses. The symmetry is not a fundamental symmetry of QCD but rather an emergent symmetry among effective constituents formed by quark-gluon dynamics, and the mass relations derived depend on the mass difference between the $s$ quark and the $\overline{ud}$ diquark.

## Method
The authors construct a V(3) algebra that treats a triplet of the $s$ quark with spin up and down and the $\overline{ud}$ antidiquark with spin 0 as a fundamental representation. They build higher representations by taking direct products of the fundamental representations, decomposing them into irreducible multiplets such as a nonet (for mesonic composites) and a quintet and quartet (for diquark-like composites). Hadrons are assigned to these multiplets, and a symmetry-breaking term is introduced to account for the mass difference between the $s$ quark and the $\overline{ud}$ diquark, leading to Gell-Mann Okubo type mass relations.

## Key numbers
- The masses of possible tetraquarks $\overline{ud}sc$ and $\overline{ud}sb$ are estimated to be 2.942 GeV and 6.261 GeV, respectively, from the symmetry breaking and the masses of $\Omega_{c}$ and $\Omega_{b}$.
- The $ud$ diquark mass is expected to be 0.4 to 0.6 GeV, similar to the constituent strange quark mass which may be 0.5 GeV, based on QCD sum rule results from Kim (2011) and constituent quark mass estimates.
- The hadron multiplets identified include $\{D_{s}, D_{s}^{*}, \Lambda_{c}\}$ as a triplet, $\{\eta_{s}, \phi, \Lambda, f_{0}(1370)\}$ as a nonet, and $\{\Omega_{c}, T_{sc}\}$ as a quintet.

## Context
This work extends the approach of flavor SU(3) symmetry, originally developed by Ne'eman (1961) and Gell-Mann (1962), by introducing a supersymmetry between a fermion (the $s$ quark) and a boson (the $\overline{ud}$ diquark). It builds on earlier supersymmetry ideas in hadron physics by Miyazawa (1966, 1968) using V(3) algebra, and on diquark studies by Jaffe (2004) and lattice QCD investigations. The paper enables predictions for the masses of genuine tetraquark states such as $T_{sc}$ ($\overline{ud}sc$) and $T_{sb}$ ($\overline{ud}sb$), which could be searched for experimentally.
