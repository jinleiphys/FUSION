# Quantum interference between nuclear excitation by electron capture and radiative recombination

> We investigate the quantum interference between the resonant process of nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) followed by the radiative decay of the excited nucleus, and radiative recombination (RR). In order to derive the interference cross section, a Feshbach projection operator formalism is used. The electromagnetic field is considered by means of multipole fields. The nucleus is described by a phenomenological collective model and by making use of experimental data. The Fano profile parameters as well as the interference cross section for electric and magnetic multipole transitions in various heavy ions are presented. We discuss the experimental possibility of discerning NEEC from the RR background.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quantum interference between nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) and radiative recombination (RR) produces a measurable Fano profile in the total recombination cross section, but the magnitude of this interference effect depends critically on the specific nuclear transition multipolarity and the collision system, with the interference cross section being proportional to the NEEC cross section multiplied by a Fano profile parameter that is real for both electric and magnetic cases.

## Method

A Feshbach projection operator formalism was used to separate the total Fock space into three subspaces: states with a free electron (P), states with an excited nucleus (Q), and states with a photon (R). The electromagnetic field was expanded in multipole fields, the nucleus was described using a phenomenological collective model with experimental data, and the electron dynamics were governed by the Dirac equation. The interference cross section was expressed in terms of the dimensionless Fano profile parameter Q_f, which was calculated separately for electric and magnetic multipole transitions by evaluating the relevant matrix elements for the electron-nucleus interaction, the nucleus-radiation field interaction, and the electron-radiation field interaction.

## Key numbers

- For electric E2 transitions, calculations were performed for nine even-even nuclei: 236U, 238U, 248Cm, 174Yb, 170Er, 154Gd, 156Gd, 162Dy, and 164Dy
- For 164Dy, 170Er, 174Yb, and 154Gd, capture into the K shell of bare ions was considered
- For the uranium isotopes (236U, 238U) and 248Cm, capture into the L shell of initially He-like ions was considered because the first excited nuclear state energy is too low for K-shell capture
- For 156Gd and 162Dy, capture into He-like ions was considered, with the nuclear excited state width including partial internal conversion rates
- The long-wavelength approximation kR_0 << 1 was used, where k is the photon wave number and R_0 is the nuclear radius
- The natural width of the nuclear excited state Γ_d was calculated as the sum of radiative rates A_r^{d→f} and internal conversion rates A^{d→i}_{IC}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic calculations of NEEC cross sections (Pálffy, 2006) by adding the interference term between NEEC and RR, which had been studied theoretically for the atomic analog of dielectronic recombination and RR interference (Zimmermann, 1990s) and experimentally confirmed (Knapp, Spies, 1990s). The paper enables experimental identification of NEEC by quantifying how the Fano interference profile modifies the RR background, which is essential because NEEC has not yet been experimentally observed despite theoretical proposals dating back to Goldanskii (1980s) and subsequent studies in plasmas (Harston, 1990s) and solid targets (Cue, Kimball, 1990s).
