# Current correlation functions, QCD sum rules and vector mesons in baryonic matter

> Based on an effective Lagrangian which combines chiral SU(3) dynamics with vector meson dominance, we have developed a model for the forward vector meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes. We use this as an input to calculate the low energy part of the current-current correlation function in nuclear matter. Its spectrum enters directly in the ``left hand side'' of QCD sum rules. For the isovector channel we find a significant enhancement of the in-medium spectral density below the rho resonance while the rho meson mass itself changes only slightly. The situation is different in the isoscalar channel, where the mass and peak position of the omega meson move downward while its width increases less drastically than in the rho meson case. For the phi meson we find almost no mass shift; the width of the peak broadens moderately. We observe a remarkable degree of consistency with the operator product expansion of QCD sum rules in all three channels. We point out, however, that these results cannot simply be interpreted, as commonly done, in terms of a universal rescaling of vector meson masses in matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in-medium vector meson spectral functions derived from an effective chiral Lagrangian with vector meson dominance are remarkably consistent with QCD sum rules without requiring the additional Brown-Rho (BR) scaling of vector meson masses, but the authors explicitly caution that their results cannot simply be interpreted in terms of a universal rescaling of vector meson masses in matter.

## Method

The authors constructed forward vector meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes using an effective Lagrangian combining chiral SU(3) dynamics with vector meson dominance, then used these amplitudes as input to calculate the low-energy part of the current-current correlation function in nuclear matter to leading order in baryon density. The in-medium spectral functions were computed for the isovector (rho), isoscalar (omega), and phi meson channels, and the results were compared with the operator product expansion side of QCD sum rules after Borel transformation.

## Key numbers

* The bare rho meson mass is 0.81 GeV, while the physical rho mass in vacuum is 0.77 GeV, shifted downward by coupling to the two-pion continuum
* The gluon condensate used is ⟨0|(α_S/π)𝒢²|0⟩ = 1.2 × 10⁻² GeV⁴
* The fitted condensate parameters for the rho and omega channels are c₂^(ρ,ω) = 0.04 GeV⁴ and c₃^(ρ,ω) = -0.071 GeV⁶, with continuum thresholds s_(ρ,ω) = 1.56 GeV²
* For the phi channel, the fitted parameters are c₁^φ = 0.07 GeV², c₂^φ = -0.10 GeV⁴, c₃^φ = -0.07 GeV⁶, with s_φ = 2.2 GeV²
* The pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV and charged pion mass m_π = 139.6 MeV are used throughout
* The rho-pion-pion coupling constant g = 6.05

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule analyses by Hatsuda and collaborators (mid-1990s) and Shifman et al. (1979) by replacing the simplified delta-function-plus-continuum parameterization of spectral functions with realistic in-medium spectra from a hadronic model. The results contradict the simple Brown-Rho scaling prediction of universal vector meson mass dropping in matter, instead finding that the rho meson mass changes only slightly while its width increases significantly, the omega meson mass moves downward with moderate broadening, and the phi meson shows almost no mass shift. The paper enables more reliable interpretation of dilepton production experiments at CERN (CERES and HELIOS) by providing realistic in-medium spectral functions for use in QCD sum rule analyses.
