# QCD Phase Diagram and the Finite Volume Fireball: A Model Study

> Experimental investigations of the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter involve collisions of heavy ions at ultrarelativistic velocities. The medium created in such a collision is often of dimensions a few fermi, in particular in the Beam Energy Scan experiments. An understanding of the effect of the finite volume and the boundary is important for connecting the experimental results to the phase diagram. Using the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model, an effective theory for the chiral transition of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), we have studied the effect of the finite volume of the fireball on the transition line at finite temperature and density using the MIT boundary condition to mimic the condition that the system is deconfined inside. The shift of the transition temperature for finite volume and finite volume effect on number density and its susceptibilities are studied. The volume effects should be considered when looking for signatures of the phase diagram in experiments.

## Key claim

Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with MIT boundary conditions on a spherical geometry, the authors find that the finite volume of the fireball created in heavy-ion collisions shifts the chiral transition line toward larger chemical potentials and lower temperatures, with the effect becoming significant only for system radii of 5 fm or smaller; the first-order transition region shrinks strongly with decreasing volume.

## Method

The authors study the two-flavor NJL model in the mean-field approximation, imposing the MIT boundary condition on a spherical volume to mimic a deconfined quark-gluon plasma droplet. The thermodynamic potential is computed using Schwinger's proper time regularization with a UV cutoff, and the chiral transition is tracked via the constituent quark mass M (equivalent to the chiral condensate). The crossover temperature is estimated from the peak of dM/dT, and the first-order transition is identified by a two-well structure in the mean-field free energy.

## Key numbers

- At zero chemical potential, the infinite-volume chiral crossover temperature is T_chi ≈ 186 MeV; for a sphere of radius R = 5 fm, the crossover shifts by about 7 MeV to lower temperature.
- At μq = 350 MeV, the infinite-volume system shows a first-order transition at T ≈ 47.3 MeV; for a 5 fm sphere the transition becomes a smooth crossover at T ≈ 72.2 MeV.
- The parameter set used is: m = 15 MeV, G = 17.2 GeV^{-2}, Λ = 645 MeV, giving a critical point at μ_c ≈ 340 MeV in infinite volume.
- For a 10 fm sphere, the critical point shifts to T ≈ 42 MeV; for a 5 fm sphere, the highest χ2 peak appears at T ≈ 20 MeV.
- At low temperatures (T = 20 MeV), a two-well structure in the thermodynamic potential persists down to R = 10 fm but disappears for R = 5 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of finite-volume effects on the chiral transition using antiperiodic or periodic boundary conditions (Palhares 2009, Bhattacharyya 2012, Almasi 2016, Klein 2017, Kovacs 2023) by instead applying the MIT boundary condition, which the authors argue better captures the physics of a deconfined fireball. The MIT condition was previously used for the chiral crossover at zero chemical potential on a sphere (Zhang 2019) and for rotating fermionic systems (Chernodub 2016, 2017). The results indicate that volume effects are much larger with MIT boundary conditions than with antiperiodic ones, and that the shift of the critical point and the smoothing of the first-order transition must be accounted for when interpreting experimental signatures from the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC.
