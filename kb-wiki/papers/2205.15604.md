# Low-energy parameterisations of the pion-nucleon phase shifts

> Compared in this work are a few sets of results, obtained from the pion-nucleon ($πN$) data at low energy (pion laboratory kinetic energy up to $100$ MeV) on the basis of the modelling of the $s$- and $p$-wave $K$-matrix elements (or of their reciprocal) via simple polynomials. The fitted values and uncertainties of the model parameters, as well as the corresponding Hessian (covariance) matrices, for three of these parameterisations are given in tabular form for two types of joint fits: to the measurements of the two elastic-scattering processes $π^\pm p \to π^\pm p$, and to those of the $π^+ p$ reaction and of the $π^- p$ charge-exchange reaction $π^- p \to π^0 n$. From these results, reliable and (largely) data-driven (hence model-independent) predictions, accompanied by uncertainties which reflect the statistical and systematic fluctuation of the input data, can be obtained for the low-energy constants of the $πN$ interaction (scattering lengths/volumes and range parameters), for the phase shifts, for the $K$-matrix elements, and for the partial-wave amplitudes in the (dominant at low energy) $s$ and $p$ waves. After the addition of the $d$- and $f$-wave contributions, and the inclusion of the electromagnetic effects, corresponding predictions can be obtained for the usual low-energy $πN$ observables, i.e., for the differential cross section and for the analysing power.

# Low-energy parameterisations of the pion-nucleon phase shifts

## Key claim

The paper compares four polynomial-based parameterisations of the s- and p-wave K-matrix elements for pion-nucleon scattering at laboratory kinetic energies up to 100 MeV, finding that three of them (effective-range expansion, ELW parameterisation, and ETH parameterisation) achieve comparable descriptions of the data, while a fourth scheme (using q^2 in denominators with epsilon expansions) provides a poorer description and is not pursued further. The results are presented as data-driven predictions with uncertainties reflecting both statistical and systematic fluctuations, but the author notes that the low-energy data cannot reliably determine more than seven parameters per isospin channel.

## Method

The author modelled the s- and p-wave K-matrix elements (or their reciprocals) using simple polynomial expansions in either q^2 (the squared centre-of-mass momentum) or epsilon (the pion centre-of-mass kinetic energy), with resonant contributions explicitly added for the Delta(1232) and N(1440) resonances in the P33 and P11 partial waves. The d- and f-wave phase shifts were fixed from the SAID XP15 solution, electromagnetic corrections from the University of Zurich programme were applied, and joint fits were performed to two combinations of reactions: the two elastic-scattering processes pi+- p -> pi+- p, and the pi+ p reaction together with the pi- p charge-exchange reaction pi- p -> pi0 n.

## Key numbers

- Pion laboratory kinetic energy range: up to 100 MeV
- For the effective-range expansion, ELW, and ETH parameterisations: chi-squared values for the joint elastic-scattering fit (718 degrees of freedom) were 859.94, 852.53, and 846.25 respectively
- For the effective-range expansion, ELW, and ETH parameterisations: chi-squared values for the joint pi+ p and pi- p charge-exchange fit (736 degrees of freedom) were 851.01, 842.13, and 844.92 respectively
- The resonant contribution to a^{3/2}_{1+} is about 36.0130 GeV^{-3} for the effective-range expansion, ELW, and Section 2.4 parameterisations
- The resonant contribution to a^{1/2}_{1-} is about 4.9616 GeV^{-3} for the same parameterisations
- The significance threshold p_min used for outlier removal corresponds to 2.5 sigma effects in the normal distribution, approximately 1.24 x 10^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the model-independent approach to low-energy pion-nucleon analysis that began with Fettes (1997), who sought simpler descriptions than the hadronic-exchange model of Goudsmit (1994). The parameterisations compared include the effective-range expansion from Hohler (1983), the polynomial K-matrix approach used by Ericson (2004), and the ETH parameterisation established by Fettes (1997) using pion kinetic energy as the expansion variable. The results enable reliable predictions for low-energy constants (scattering lengths/volumes and range parameters), phase shifts, K-matrix elements, and partial-wave amplitudes, which can be combined with d- and f-wave contributions and electromagnetic effects to predict differential cross sections and analysing power for the three experimentally accessible low-energy pion-nucleon reactions.
