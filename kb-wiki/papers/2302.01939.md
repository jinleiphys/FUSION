# Rescattering effects in nucleon-to-meson form factors and application to tau-lepton-induced proton decay

> Nucleon decays put extremely stringent bounds on baryon-number-violating interactions. However, in case the corresponding operators involve only $τ$ leptons, the direct two-body decays, e.g., $p\toπ^0 τ^+$, are kinematically not allowed and nucleon decay can only proceed via an off-shell $τ$, leading to $p\to π^0\ell^+ν_\ell\barν_τ$. To calculate such processes, the momentum dependence of the form factors for nucleon-to-meson transitions, which describe the hadronization of the underlying process at the quark level, is needed. In this work, we point out new isospin and Fierz relations among such proton-kaon matrix elements and calculate the momentum dependence of the nucleon-to-meson form factors from the universal final-state interactions in terms of pion-nucleon or kaon-nucleon scattering phase shifts. We use these results to derive novel limits on the Wilson coefficients of the baryon-number-violating dimension-$6$ operators involving a $τ$ lepton, which were previously unconstrained.

# Rescattering effects in nucleon-to-meson form factors and application to tau-lepton-induced proton decay

## Key claim

The authors show that baryon-number-violating dimension-6 operators involving only tau leptons, which were previously unconstrained because the direct two-body decays (e.g., p → π⁰ τ⁺) are kinematically forbidden, can be constrained by four-body decays (e.g., p → π⁰ ℓ⁺ νₗ ν̄_τ) mediated by an off-shell tau, provided the momentum dependence of the nucleon-to-meson form factors is calculated from dispersion theory including meson-nucleon rescattering effects.

## Method

The authors derive new isospin and Fierz relations among proton-to-kaon matrix elements, then calculate the momentum dependence of nucleon-to-meson form factors using an Omnès representation that incorporates unitarity constraints from pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering phase shifts. The form-factor normalizations are taken from lattice QCD results of Yoo et al. (2021), and the dispersive representation is constructed to satisfy unitarity relations while reproducing the tree-level chiral perturbation theory result in the limit of no rescattering. The resulting form factors are used to compute decay widths for four-body tau-induced proton decay and extract limits on the corresponding Wilson coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For the special case C^{AB}_{τ α} ≡ C^{LL}_{τ α} = C^{LR}_{τ α} = C^{RL}_{τ α} = C^{RR}_{τ α}, the partial width for p → π⁰ ℓ⁺ νₗ ν̄_τ is 14.3(1.8) × |C^{AB}_{τ udu}/(10¹⁰ GeV)^{-2}|² × (1/10³⁰ yr)⁻¹
- For n → π⁻ ℓ⁺ νₗ ν̄_τ, the partial width is 26.2(3.3) × |C^{AB}_{τ udu}/(10¹⁰ GeV)^{-2}|² × (1/10³⁰ yr)⁻¹
- For p → K⁰ ℓ⁺ νₗ ν̄_τ, the partial width is 0.81(1) × |C^{AB}_{τ usu}/(10¹⁰ GeV)^{-2}|² × (1/10³⁰ yr)⁻¹
- The resulting limits on Wilson coefficients are |C_α| ≲ (10⁻¹⁰/GeV)² at the nucleon scale, compared to |C_α| ≲ (10⁻¹⁵/GeV)² for two-body decays
- Without rescattering corrections, the corresponding coefficients would be 7.2, 13.3, and 0.84 respectively
- The form-factor normalizations from lattice QCD (Yoo et al., 2021) are given in units of GeV² at MS-bar scale μ = 2 GeV, e.g., W₀^{U_{1L}}(0) = 0.151(31) GeV², W₁^{U_{1L}}(0) = -0.134(18) GeV²

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining baryon-number-violating operators using nucleon decay, which has been pursued through lattice QCD calculations by Yoo et al. (2021) and earlier by the JLQCD and CP-PACS collaborations. The authors generalize the dispersive approach previously applied to baryon-number-conserving nucleon form factors (Hoferichter et al., 2012, 2015, 2016, 2018) to the baryon-number-violating case. The results enable the first constraints on tau-flavored dimension-6 SMEFT operators that were previously unconstrained, complementing the stringent limits from two-body decays (e.g., Super-Kamiokande limits on p → π⁰ e⁺ at 2.4 × 10³⁴ years) and providing a method to interpret inclusive nucleon decay searches such as p → e⁺ X.
