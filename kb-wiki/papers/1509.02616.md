# Generalized Second-Order Thomas-Fermi Method for Superfluid Fermi Systems

> Using the $\hbar$-expansion of the Green's function of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation, we extend the second-order Thomas-Fermi approximation to generalized superfluid Fermi systems by including the density-dependent effective mass and the spin-orbit potential. We first implement and examine the full correction terms over different energy intervals of the quasiparticle spectra in calculations of finite nuclei. Final applications of this generalized Thomas-Fermi method are intended for various inhomogeneous superfluid Fermi systems.

## Key claim

The authors extend the second-order Thomas-Fermi approximation to superfluid Fermi systems by including density-dependent effective mass and spin-orbit potential, which were previously absent in superfluid formulations. The method is intended as a semiclassical approximation for high-energy quasiparticle states in hybrid HFB calculations, with the hedge that the full second-order corrections are necessary only when the lower energy cutoff of the Thomas-Fermi window reaches into the low-energy region (e.g., 25 MeV).

## Method

The authors derive the second-order Thomas-Fermi corrections from an \(\hbar\)-expansion of the Green's function of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation, including spin-orbit terms and density-dependent effective mass. They implement these corrections in non-self-consistent perturbative calculations for the deformed nucleus \(^{238}\)U using the Skyrme SLy4 force and volume pairing, comparing the semiclassical densities to full coordinate-space HFB solutions from the HFB-AX solver. Finally, they perform fully self-consistent hybrid HFB calculations where low-energy quasiparticle states are treated with the full HFB method and high-energy states (from a lower cutoff up to 65 MeV) are treated with the second-order Thomas-Fermi approximation.

## Key numbers

- The second-order correction to the pairing density in \(^{238}\)U for quasiparticle energies from 55 to 65 MeV is very small, with the zeroth-order approximation underestimating the pairing density at the nuclear surface by 10% when the lower cutoff is lowered to 25 MeV.
- With a lower cutoff of 25 MeV, the deviation in total binding energy of \(^{238}\)U (total 1790.52 MeV) between hybrid HFB and full HFB is approximately 0.5 MeV.
- The number of eigenfunctions to be solved in hybrid HFB calculations is reduced by half when using a lower cutoff of 25 MeV, reducing computational cost by at least one order of magnitude.
- For the neutron pairing gap in \(^{120}\)Sn with SLy4 and volume pairing, the pairing strengths in BCS and HFB must be adjusted to 283 and 187 MeV fm\(^{-3}\), respectively, to reproduce a gap of 1.245 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the second-order superfluid Thomas-Fermi formalism of Schuck (1976), Gross (1989), and Csordas (2010) by including density-dependent effective mass and spin-orbit potential, which are essential for realistic nuclear density functionals such as the Skyrme force of Vautherin and Brink (1972). The method enables hybrid HFB calculations for large superfluid systems where full 3D coordinate-space HFB is computationally prohibitive, such as trapped cold atomic gases with \(10^{5-6}\) particles (Partridge, 2006; Shin, 2008) and neutron star crusts in large 3D cells (Mazy, 2007; Stone, 2007). The approach contrasts with the coarse-graining treatment of Simonucci (2013), which only includes the \(h\overleftrightarrow{\Lambda}^2\Delta\) term, whereas the present work shows that all second-order terms are needed for low-energy cutoffs.
