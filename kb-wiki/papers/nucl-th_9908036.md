# Vlasov Description Of Dense Quark Matter

> We discuss properties of quark matter at finite baryon densities and zero temperature in a Vlasov approach. We use a screened interquark Richardson's potential consistent with the indications of Lattice QCD calculations. We analyze the choices of the quark masses and the parameters entering the potential which reproduce the binding energy (B.E.) of infinite nuclear matter. There is a transition from nuclear to quark matter at densities 5 times above normal nuclear matter density. The transition could be revealed from the determination of the position of the shifted meson masses in dense baryonic matter. A scaling form of the meson masses in dense matter is given.

## Key claim

The paper claims a smooth transition from nuclear matter to quark matter at densities around 5 times normal nuclear matter density, but with the important qualifier that no first-order phase transition is found at zero temperature, and the transition is instead a continuous crossover.

## Method

The author solves the Vlasov equation for a system of quarks and antiquarks using a test-particle method, with a screened Richardson potential as the interquark interaction. The screening is implemented via a Debye radius that depends on the quark Fermi momenta and masses, with the strong coupling constant fixed to reproduce the binding energy of infinite nuclear matter at saturation density. The quark masses and potential parameters are first calibrated by fitting known meson masses in vacuum, then the same parameter sets are used to compute the equation of state of dense quark matter and the in-medium masses of mesons placed in that matter.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\); binding energy per nucleon: \(-16\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
* Transition from nuclear to quark matter estimated at baryon density \(\rho_{cb} \approx 0.75\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (about 5 times \(\rho_0\)), corresponding to a critical energy density \(\epsilon_c = 0.71\ \mathrm{GeV}\) from the bag model with \(B^{1/4} = 0.206\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
* For parameter set I (\(\Lambda = 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_u = m_d = 130\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\alpha_0 = 1.43\)): Debye radius at \(\rho_0\) is about \(0.5\ \mathrm{fm}\)
* Critical densities for meson dissolution (set II): \(\pi\) at \(\rho_c = 2.14\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(\eta_c(1S)\) and \(J/\psi\) at \(\rho_c \approx 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(\gamma(1S)\) at \(\rho_c = 17.15\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
* Scaling exponent for meson mass shift: \(\beta = 3.8\) for set II

## Context

This work extends the Vlasov approach previously applied to hadron masses by Bonasera (1999) to dense quark matter, using a screened Richardson potential consistent with lattice QCD. The results contradict the expectation of a first-order phase transition from nuclear to quark matter at zero temperature, instead finding a smooth crossover. The scaling of meson masses in dense matter is compared to the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) based on chiral symmetry, and the dissolution of charmonium states is discussed in relation to the QGP signature proposed by Satz (1990). The approach enables future dynamical studies of heavy-ion collisions from quark degrees of freedom, with the possibility of extending the calculations to finite temperature and to finite nuclei.
