# Quasiparticle Renormalization and Pairing Correlations in Spherical Superfluid Nuclei

> We present a detailed discussion of the solution of Nambu-Gor'kov equations in superfluid nuclei, which provide a consistent framework to deal with the interplay between particle-hole and particle- particle channel, including the effects of the fragmentation of the quasiparticle strength and of the pairing interaction induced by the exchange of collective vibrations. The coupling between quasiparticle and vibrations is determined from the experimental polarizability of the low-lying collective surface vibrations. This coupling is used to renormalize the properties of quasiparticles obtained from a BCS calculation using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. We apply the formalism to the case of the nucleus 120Sn, showing results for the low-energy spectrum and the quasiparticle strength distribution in neighbouring odd nuclei and for the neutron pairing gap.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the pairing gap in superfluid nuclei receives comparable contributions from the bare nucleon-nucleon force and from the phonon-mediated induced pairing interaction, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of mean field and on the frozen-phonon approximation that neglects renormalization of the vibrations themselves.

## Method

The authors solve the Nambu-Gor'kov equations for the superfluid nucleus 120Sn using a "prior scheme" that first performs a Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculation with the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, then adds renormalization effects from the coupling between quasiparticles and collective surface vibrations. The particle-vibration coupling vertices are determined from a Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculation using a separable force, with coupling constants tuned to reproduce the experimental polarizability of low-lying collective surface modes, following the collective model of Bohr and Mottelson. The resulting energy-dependent normal and abnormal self-energies are computed by diagonalizing a matrix that couples the BCS quasiparticle states to 1-quasiparticle-plus-1-phonon configurations, with iterative self-consistent treatment of the fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed for the nucleus 120Sn, using the SLy4 effective interaction for the mean field.
- The phonon coupling constants χλ are determined by fitting to experimental polarizabilities β²λ1/ħωλ1 of low-lying surface modes, with resulting χλ values close to 1.
- The bare pairing gap from BCS and the phonon-induced contribution to the pairing gap are found to have "comparable magnitudes" (no specific numerical gap value given for the total renormalized gap).
- The quasiparticle strength N_{a(n)} = x_{a(n)}² + y_{a(n)}² is less than 1 for each fragment, indicating fragmentation of the quasiparticle strength.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by the same group (Idini, Barranco, Vigezzi, as cited in references prl_noi through pastore:08) on the microscopic origin of pairing correlations in nuclei. It provides a detailed implementation of the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism for superfluid nuclei, which was previously applied in condensed matter physics for strong-coupling superconductivity (Gorkov, Nambu, Eliashberg). The approach enables future consistent calculations of one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions (Gregory) and makes contact with the Nuclear Field Theory formalism (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975). The paper notes that while similar renormalization effects have been extensively studied in normal nuclei (Mahaux, 1985; Soloviev), their consequences on pairing correlations have been much less investigated.
