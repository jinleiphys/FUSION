# Off-Shell Structures of Nucleon-Nucleon t-matrices and their Influence on Nucleon-Nucleus Elastic Scattering Observables

> The sensitivity of nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering observables to the off-shell structure of nucleon-nucleon t-matrices, derived from realistic NN potentials, is investigated within the context of a full-folding model based on the impulse approximation. Our study uses recently developed NN potential models, which describe a subset of the NN data base with a $χ^2$ per datum $\sim$1, which means that the NN t-matrices are essentially on-shell equivalent. We calculate proton-nucleus elastic scattering observables for $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{208}$Pb between 100 and 200 MeV laboratory energy. We find that the elastic scattering observables are insensitive to off-shell differences of the employed NN t-matrices. A more detailed investigation of the scattering equation and the optical potential as given in a factorized approximation reveals that the elastic scattering observables do not sample the NN t-matrices very far off-shell where they exhibit differences.

## Key claim

Proton-nucleus elastic scattering observables for targets between 16O and 208Pb at 100-200 MeV are insensitive to off-shell differences among NN t-matrices derived from the Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, and CD-Bonn potentials, even though those t-matrices exhibit significant off-shell differences at large momenta. The insensitivity arises because the nuclear density restricts the sampling of the NN t-matrix to a region close to the on-shell point, where the different potentials give nearly identical results.

## Method

The authors calculated first-order full-folding optical potentials in the impulse approximation using the spectator expansion of multiple scattering theory, employing fully off-shell NN t-matrices from the Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, and CD-Bonn potentials (all with chi-squared per datum = 1.03). They computed elastic scattering observables for proton scattering from 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb at laboratory energies between 100 and 200 MeV, using Dirac-Hartree nuclear density matrices. To isolate which off-shell regions of the NN t-matrix are sampled, they employed the optimum factorized (off-shell t-rho) approximation to the optical potential and analyzed the rescattering term of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation as a function of the integration cutoff kmax.

## Key numbers

- All three NN potentials (Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, CD-Bonn) describe the Nijmegen NN data base with a chi-squared per datum = 1.03.
- The on-shell momentum in the NN system at 200 MeV is k_NN = k'_NN = 1.55 fm^{-1}.
- For 16O at 200 MeV, integration up to kmax = k0 + 1.0 fm^{-1} (where k0 ~ 3 fm^{-1}) is sufficient to obtain the full result for the rescattering integral.
- For 90Zr at 200 MeV, integration only to kmax = k0 + 0.5 fm^{-1} is needed for the full result.
- The off-shell region sampled in NA elastic scattering is bounded to approximately k0 +/- 1.5 fm^{-1} in the NA system.
- The largest difference between Nijmegen I and CD-Bonn Wolfenstein amplitudes within the sampled region is about 6 percent of the total value of Re A.
- Nijmegen I and Nijmegen II show off-shell differences of 1 MeV fm^3 and larger for k_NN = k'_NN ~ 4 fm^{-1}, but these differences lie outside the sampled region.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Arellano et al. (1996) that used the Paris potential and inversion potentials, finding similar insensitivity of NA elastic scattering to off-shell effects. The study exploits the recent development of NN potentials (Nijmegen group, 1994; CD-Bonn, Machleidt et al., 1996) that are on-shell equivalent with chi-squared per datum near unity, enabling a clean test of off-shell sensitivity. The results show that first-order full-folding optical potentials cannot be improved by refining the off-shell behavior of realistic NN t-matrices; instead, higher-order effects in the multiple scattering expansion must be addressed to resolve remaining discrepancies with experimental data.
