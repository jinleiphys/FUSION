# Shear-induced spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the spin polarization generated by the hydrodynamic gradients. In addition to the widely studied thermal vorticity effects, we identify an undiscovered contribution from the fluid shear. This shear-induced polarization (SIP) can be viewed as the fluid analog of strain-induced polarization observed in elastic and nematic materials. We obtain the explicit expression for SIP using the quantum kinetic equation and linear response theory. Based on a realistic hydrodynamic model, we compute the differential spin polarization along both the beam direction $\hat{z}$ and the out-plane direction $\hat{y}$ in non-central heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, including both SIP and thermal vorticity effects. We find that SIP contribution always shows the same azimuthal angle dependence as experimental data and competes with thermal vorticity effects. In the scenario that $Λ$ inherits and memorizes the spin polarization of strange quark, SIP wins the competition, and the resulting azimuthal angle dependent spin polarization $P_y$ and $P_z$ agrees qualitatively with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a previously undiscovered contribution to spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions arising from fluid shear (shear-induced polarization, or SIP), which competes with the well-studied thermal vorticity effects. The key qualifier is that SIP wins this competition only in the "strange memory" scenario where Lambda hyperons inherit and memorize the spin polarization of strange quarks, leading to qualitative agreement with experimental data; in the "Lambda equilibrium" scenario, thermal vorticity effects still dominate.

## Method

The authors derived the explicit expression for shear-induced polarization using the quantum kinetic equation (chiral kinetic theory) for massless fermions and then extended the result to arbitrary mass using linear response theory with a one-loop calculation of the retarded correlator between the axial current and the stress-energy tensor. They implemented the resulting spin polarization formula in a 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic model (MUSIC) with AMPT initial conditions, calibrated to fit charged particle multiplicity, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The spin polarization vector was computed on the chemical freezeout surface using a Cooper-Frye type prescription, considering two limiting scenarios: "Lambda equilibrium" (immediate spin relaxation) and "strange memory" (polarization frozen from strange quarks).

## Key numbers

- Collision system: Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Lambda hyperon mass used: m = 1.116 GeV
- Strange quark mass used (benchmark value): m = 0.3 GeV
- The azimuthal angle dependence of P_z and P_y can be parametrized approximately as "b_z sin(2 phi_p)" and "a + b_y cos(2 phi_p)" respectively
- The factor |p_perp|^2 / epsilon_0^2 (square of typical fermion velocity) controls the relative importance of SIP versus thermal vorticity effects

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions beyond the widely studied thermal vorticity effects (Becattini 2013, Fang 2016, Pang 2016) that successfully describe global Lambda polarization but fail to reproduce the differential (azimuthal-angle-dependent) spin polarization observed by STAR (Niida 2018, Adam 2019). The shear-induced polarization is identified as the fluid analog of strain-induced polarization observed in elastic and nematic materials (Crooker 2005, 2005PhRvL..95j7203M). The results suggest that both SIP and the memory of strange quark polarization are required to resolve the "spin sign puzzle" in differential Lambda polarization, and enable future quantitative studies of hadronization and spin transport in the quark-gluon plasma.
