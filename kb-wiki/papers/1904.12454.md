# Quark matter symmetry energy effect on equation of state for neutron stars

> We study the equation of state of neutron-star matter including the effects of isovector-vector coupling in quark matter. We employ the relativistic mean-field theory with an extended TM1 parameter set to describe hadronic matter and the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with isovector-vector and hypercharge-vector couplings to describe the quark matter. The deconfinement hadron-quark phase transition is constructed under Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions. It is found that the isovector-vector and hypercharge-vector couplings in quark matter enhance the symmetry energy and hypercharge symmetry energy in neutron-star matter.

## Key claim
The isovector-vector and hypercharge-vector couplings in the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter enhance the symmetry energy and hypercharge symmetry energy in neutron-star matter, which delays and shrinks the hadron-quark phase transition; for sufficiently large couplings (G3=G8=10 G0), the phase transition does not occur inside neutron stars, leaving only pure hadronic matter.

## Method
The authors employed relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with an extended TM1 parameter set (TM1-50, with symmetry energy slope L=50 MeV) to describe hadronic matter, and the three-flavor SU(3) NJL model with isovector-vector (G3) and hypercharge-vector (G8) couplings to describe quark matter. The deconfinement hadron-quark phase transition was constructed under Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions, imposing global charge neutrality, beta equilibrium, and mechanical equilibrium between the two phases. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation was then solved with the resulting equation of state to obtain neutron-star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- TM1-50 parameter set yields symmetry energy slope L = 50 MeV at saturation density n0, with symmetry energy S0 fixed at density 0.11 fm^-3.
- For G3=G8=1.5 G0, the vector coupling contribution to the slope parameter is ΔL_V = 6.6 MeV; for G3=G8=10 G0, ΔL_V = 44 MeV.
- The mixed phase starting density n_b^(1) increases from 0.681 fm^-3 (G3=G8=0) to 0.757 fm^-3 (G3=G8=1.5 G0) and 0.938 fm^-3 (G3=G8=10 G0); the ending density n_b^(2) changes from 2.210 to 2.226 and 2.241 fm^-3 respectively.
- Maximum neutron-star mass for pure hadronic matter (TM1-50) is 2.120 M_sun; with NJL quark matter at G3=G8=0 it is 2.101 M_sun, at G3=G8=1.5 G0 it is 2.112 M_sun, and at G3=G8=10 G0 it is 2.120 M_sun.
- Radius of a 1.4 M_sun neutron star is 13.0 km for all cases considered; the central density at maximum mass ranges from 0.879 to 0.900 fm^-3.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars by incorporating isovector-vector and hypercharge-vector couplings in the NJL model, which were treated differently in prior work: G3=1.5 G0 was used in Wu (2017) and Rehberg (1996), G3=G0 in Ueda (2013), and G3=0 in Benic (2014). The TM1-50 hadronic model (Bao 2014) was chosen to match the symmetry energy slope L=50 MeV, consistent with experimental constraints from Tews (2017). The results enable more precise modeling of neutron-star interiors and can be tested against observational constraints from PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010, Fonseca 2016) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013), which require maximum masses above 2 M_sun, and radius constraints from Steiner (2016) and Guillot (2014).
