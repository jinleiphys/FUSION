# Role of the $螖^*(1940)$ in the $蟺^+ p \to K^+ 危^+(1385)$ and $pp \to n K^+ 危^+(1385)$ reactions

> The $p p \to n K^+ 危^+(1385)$ reaction is a very good isospin $3/2$ filter for studying $螖^{++*}$ resonance decaying to $K^+危^+(1385)$. Within the effective Lagrangian method, we investigate the $危(1385)$ (spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^+$) hadronic production in the $蟺^+ p \to K^+ 危^+(1385)$ and $p p \to n K^+ 危^+(1385)$ reactions. For $蟺^+ p \to K^+ 危^+(1385)$ reaction, in addition to the "background" contributions from $t$-channel $K^{*0}$ exchange, $u$-channel $螞(1115)$ and $危^0(1193)$ exchange, we also consider the contribution from the $s$-channel $螖^*(1940)$ resonance, which has significant coupling to $K危(1385)$ channel. We show that the inclusion of the $螖^*(1940)$ resonance leads to a fairly good description of the low energy experimental total cross section data of $蟺^+ p \to K^+ 危^+(1385)$ reaction. Basing on the study of $蟺^+ p \to K^+危^+(1385)$ reaction and with the assumption that the excitation of $螖^*(1940)$ resonance dominant the $pp \to n K^+ 危^+(1385)$ reaction, we calculate the total and differential cross sections of $p p \to n K^+ 危^+(1385)$ reaction. It is shown that the new experimental data support the important role played by the $螖^*(1940)$ resonance with a mass in the region of $1940$ MeV and a width of around $200$ MeV. We also demonstrate that the invariant mass distribution and the Dalitz Plot provide direct information of the $危^+(1385)$ production, which can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The $\Delta^*(1940)$ resonance, with spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$, provides a dominant contribution to both the $\pi^+ p \to K^+ \Sigma^+(1385)$ and $pp \to n K^+ \Sigma^+(1385)$ reactions, but the evidence for its existence is classified as "poor or only fair" by the Particle Data Group, and its total decay width and branching ratios are not experimentally known.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with an isobar model at tree level, calculating Feynman diagrams for the $\pi^+ p \to K^+ \Sigma^+(1385)$ reaction that included $s$-channel $\Delta^*(1940)$ resonance excitation, $t$-channel $K^{*0}$ exchange, and $u$-channel $\Lambda(1115)$ and $\Sigma^0(1193)$ exchange. For the $pp \to n K^+ \Sigma^+(1385)$ reaction, they assumed the production mechanism proceeds via $\pi^+$-meson exchange with the $\Delta^*(1940)$ resonance dominating, and performed a four-parameter $\chi^2$ fit to 11 experimental total cross section data points below $p_{\pi^+} = 4$ GeV to determine the resonance parameters and coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Fitted mass of $\Delta^*(1940)$: $M_{\Delta^*} = 1940 \pm 24$ MeV
- Fitted total decay width of $\Delta^*(1940)$: $\Gamma_{\Delta^*} = 172 \pm 94$ MeV
- Product of branching ratios: Br($\Delta^* \to N\pi$) $\times$ Br($\Delta^* \to \Sigma^* K$) = $(0.52 \pm 0.13)\%$
- Branching ratio Br($\Delta^* \to \Sigma^* K$) = $(10.4 \pm 4.9)\%$, corresponding to partial decay width $\Gamma_{\Delta^* \to \Sigma^* K} = 17.9 \pm 12.9$ MeV
- Strong coupling constants determined: $g_1 = -1.04 \pm 0.38$, $g_2 = 5.24 \pm 2.30$, and $g_{\pi N \Delta^*} = 0.35 \pm 0.12$
- Minimal $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.8$ achieved with cutoff parameters $\Lambda_t = \Lambda_u = 0.6$ GeV and $\Lambda_s = 0.9$ GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $\Delta^*$ resonances in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0(1385)$ reactions by Oh (2007), Gao (2010), Chen (2013), and He (2013), who found that a $\Delta^*$ resonance with spin-parity $3/2^-$ and mass around 2 GeV has significant coupling to the $K\Sigma(1385)$ channel. The authors build on quark model predictions by Capstick and Roberts (1998) that the $\Delta^*(1940)$ should contribute visibly to $K\Sigma(1385)$ production, and use new experimental data from the HADES Collaboration (Agakishiev, 2011) at 3.5 GeV beam energy. The results enable future experimental tests of the $\Sigma^+(1385)$ production mechanism through invariant mass distributions and Dalitz plots, and provide the first determination of the $\Delta^*(1940)$ coupling to the $K\Sigma(1385)$ channel.
