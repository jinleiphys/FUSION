# Continuum Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Green's function method for odd-$A$ nuclei

> To study the exotic odd nuclear systems, the self-consistent continuum Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory formulated with Green's function technique is extended to include blocking effects with the equal filling approximation. Detailed formula are presented.To perform the integrals of the Green's function properly, the contour paths $C_{\rm b}^{-}$ and $C_{\rm b}^{+}$ introduced for the blocking effects should include the blocked quasi-particle state but can not intrude into the continuum area. By comparing with the box-discretized calculations, the great advantages of the Green's function method in describing the extended density distributions, resonant states, and the couplings with the continuum in exotic nuclei are shown. Finally, taking the neutron-rich odd nucleus $^{159}$Sn as an example, the halo structure is investigated by blocking the quasi-particle state $1p_{1/2}$. It is found that it is mainly the weakly bound states near the Fermi surface that contribute a lot for the extended density distributions at large coordinate space.

## Key claim
The continuum Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Green's function method is extended to odd-\(A\) nuclei by incorporating blocking effects under the equal filling approximation, but the contour paths for the blocking integrals must be chosen so that they include the blocked quasi-particle state while excluding the continuum region to avoid unphysical contributions.

## Method
The authors solve the coordinate-space HFB equation for odd-\(A\) nuclei by constructing the generalized density matrix from Green's function integrals over complex-energy contour paths \(C_{E<0}\), \(C_{\rm b}^{-}\), and \(C_{\rm b}^{+}\). The radial HFB Green's function is built from independent solutions with regular boundary conditions at the origin and outgoing-wave boundary conditions at large radius, and the densities are obtained by integrating the Green's function along these contours. The blocking effect is implemented by adding and subtracting the contributions of the blocked quasi-particle state in the density expressions, with the equal filling approximation averaging over magnetic quantum numbers.

## Key numbers
- The lowest quasi-particle state in \(^{158}\)Sn is \(3p_{1/2}\) with energy around \(0.9\) MeV and Fermi energy around \(-1.0\) MeV.
- For \(^{159}\)Sn blocking the \(3p_{1/2}\) state, the blocking energy width \(E_{\rm b}=0.9\) MeV (satisfying \(E_{i_{\rm b}} < E_{\rm b} < -\lambda\)) yields correct results, while \(E_{\rm b}=1.2\) MeV (exceeding the continuum threshold \(-\lambda\)) produces an unphysical peak in the occupation number density and an increasing tail in the neutron density.
- The calculation uses a box size \(R=20\) fm, mesh size \(\Delta r=0.1\) fm, maximal angular momentum \(j_{\rm max}=25/2\), maximal quasi-particle energy \(E_{\rm cut}=60\) MeV, and contour height \(\gamma=0.1\) MeV with energy step \(\Delta E=0.01\) MeV.
- The pairing interaction uses parameters \(V_{0}=-458.4\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), \(\eta=0.71\), \(\alpha=0.59\), and \(\rho_{0}=0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context
This work extends the continuum Skyrme-HFB theory with Green's function method, previously formulated only for even-even nuclei by Zhang et al. (2011, 2012), to odd-\(A\) nuclei by including blocking effects. It builds on the Green's function HFB approach developed by Belyaev et al. (1987) and Matsuo (2001), and on the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory with Green's function by Sun et al. (2014, 2016). The method enables consistent treatment of pairing correlations, continuum coupling, and blocking in coordinate space for exotic odd nuclei, and is demonstrated for the neutron-rich halo candidate \(^{159}\)Sn.
