# Dynamics of critical fluctuations: Theory -- phenomenology -- heavy-ion collisions

> This report summarizes the presentations and discussions during the Rapid Reaction Task Force "Dynamics of critical fluctuations: Theory -- phenomenology -- heavy-ion collisions", which was organized by the ExtreMe Matter Institute EMMI and held at GSI, Darmstadt, Germany in April 2019. We address the current understanding of the dynamics of critical fluctuations in QCD and their measurement in heavy-ion collision experiments. In addition, we outline what might be learned from studying correlations in other physical systems, such as cold atomic gases.

# Dynamics of critical fluctuations: Theory – phenomenology – heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
This report summarizes the Rapid Reaction Task Force discussions on the dynamics of critical fluctuations in QCD, concluding that while multiple theoretical approaches exist (hydro-kinetic equations, stochastic diffusion, nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics, QCD-assisted transport, and mode-coupling theory), the quantitative connection between critical fluctuations and experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions remains incomplete due to non-universal translation of Ising model variables to QCD parameters and the need to account for finite-size effects, critical slowing-down, and spurious fluctuations from acceptance limitations.

## Method
The task force reviewed and compared five distinct theoretical frameworks: effective kinetic theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations (hydro-kinetic equations) for out-of-equilibrium noise in expanding systems; stochastic diffusion of critical net-baryon density fluctuations using a Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional mapped from the 3-dimensional Ising model; nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (NχFD) coupling a Langevin equation for the chiral order parameter to relativistic fluid dynamics; QCD-assisted transport using Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) to compute equilibrium correlation functions and spectral functions for Langevin-type transport equations; and modeling of time correlations with hydrodynamic fluctuations including mode-coupling theory for thermal conductivity near the critical point.

## Key numbers
- The finite correction to longitudinal pressure from hydrodynamic fluctuations scales as $1.08318 / [s (4\pi \gamma_{\eta}\tau)^{3/2}]$, where $\gamma_{\eta} = \eta/(e_0+p_0)$ and $\tau$ is proper time
- The critical correlation length growth is constrained by causality: for cooling time $\tau = 1$ fm, $\xi/\xi_0$ is limited to below 1.3; for $\tau = 5.5$ fm, $\xi/\xi_0$ is limited to below 1.9
- The quark-meson coupling constant is fixed as $g = 3.37$ from vacuum nucleon masses of 940 MeV; pion decay constant $f_\pi = 93$ MeV; pion mass $m_\pi = 138$ MeV; sigma mass $m_\sigma = 600$ MeV
- The initial correlation length far from transition temperature is $\xi_0 \sim 1.6$ fm
- Critical exponents used: $\alpha = 0.11$, $\nu = 0.63$, $z = 2 + \alpha/\nu$, $\beta = 0.326$, $\delta = 4.80$ from universality class arguments

## Context
This report synthesizes work extending from earlier studies of hydrodynamic fluctuations (Akamatsu 2016, 2017; Mazeliauskas 2017) and critical dynamics (Nahrgang 2018; Bluhm 2016, 2018, 2019) to provide a comprehensive overview of theoretical approaches for critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. It enables future work connecting these frameworks to experimental data from RHIC and SPS, particularly through finite-size scaling analyses (Fraga 2011) and the study of balance functions (Kapusta 2012; Plumberg 2017). The report identifies open questions including the need for non-universal mapping between Ising model and QCD variables, treatment of long-time tails in fluctuating hydrodynamics, and incorporation of colored noise to restore relativistic causality.
