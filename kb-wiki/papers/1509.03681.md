# Theory of electromagnetic reactions in light nuclei

> We briefly review the theory for electromagnetic reactions in light nuclei based on the coupled-cluster formulation of the Lorentz integral transform method. Results on photodisintegration reactions of 22O and 40Ca are reported on and preliminary calculations on the Coulomb sum rule for 4He are discussed.

# Theory of electromagnetic reactions in light nuclei

## Key claim

The coupled-cluster formulation of the Lorentz integral transform (LIT-CCSD) method can describe photodisintegration and electron-scattering reactions in light and medium-mass nuclei using only a chiral nucleon-nucleon force, but the theoretical predictions for the giant dipole resonance in 40Ca are slightly broader and lower in strength than experimental data, and the Coulomb sum rule calculations for 4He still miss the isoscalar monopole contribution.

## Method

The authors reformulated the Lorentz integral transform method within coupled-cluster theory, truncating both the cluster operator T and the excitation operator R at the single and double (SD) excitation level. They solved a bound-state equation derived from the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian to obtain the Lorentz integral transform of the nuclear response function, then inverted the transform to extract the physical response. For photodisintegration, they used the dipole operator in the long-wavelength limit; for electron scattering, they performed a multipole expansion of the charge operator into isoscalar and isovector Coulomb multipoles, each calculated separately for fixed momentum transfer values.

## Key numbers

- For 22O photodisintegration, a low-energy pigmy dipole resonance peak appears and agrees with data from Leistenschneider et al. (2001).
- For 40Ca photodisintegration, the theoretical giant dipole resonance prediction is slightly broader and lower in strength compared to data from Ahrens et al. (1975).
- For 4He, the isovector dipole Cv1 calculation from coupled-cluster theory agrees with exact hyperspherical harmonics up to about q = 200 MeV/c, but is 20% smaller at higher momenta.
- For the 4He Coulomb sum rule, at momentum transfer below q = 100 MeV/c, two or three multipoles suffice for convergence; at q = 500 MeV/c, nine multipoles are needed.
- Model space convergence for the isovector monopole Cv0 and isovector dipole Cv1 operators is reached already at Nmax = 14.

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz integral transform method, previously applied with exact hyperspherical harmonics to light nuclei like 4He (Barnea, 2000), to heavier and neutron-rich nuclei using coupled-cluster theory. The LIT-CCSD approach was benchmarked against exact hyperspherical harmonics calculations on 4He, where higher-order corrections to the T and R operators were found to be very small. The method enables ab-initio studies of photodisintegration and electron-scattering reactions in medium-mass nuclei such as 40Ca and 16O, the latter being relevant for neutrino-nucleus interactions in long-baseline neutrino experiments like T2K. Prior work using the LIT approach had suggested the existence of a low-energy dipole mode in 6He (Bacca, 2002), and the present study finds a similar pigmy resonance in 22O.
