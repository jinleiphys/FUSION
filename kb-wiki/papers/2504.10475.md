# Probing the Sivers Asymmetry with Transverse Energy-Energy Correlators in the Small-$x$ Regime

> We investigate transverse energy-energy correlators (TEECs) for both polarized and unpolarized targets in the small-$x$ regime at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Focusing on the approximately back-to-back electroproduction of a hadron-electron pair, we apply transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization formulas that incorporate TMD evolution for both event-shape observables and expand them in terms of the small-$x$ dipole amplitude. This allows us to write the TEEC off the transversely polarized proton in terms of a C-odd interaction, corresponding to an odderon exchange. Due to the charge-conjugation-odd nature of the small-$x$ quark Sivers function, we restrict the sum over final hadronic states to positively and negatively charged hadrons separately. We present numerical predictions for the TEEC Sivers asymmetry at the EIC and find the magnitude of the asymmetry to be on the $0.1 \%$ level. This channel offers a promising avenue for benchmarking the still largely unconstrained odderon amplitude.

## Key claim
The Sivers asymmetry in transverse energy-energy correlators (TEECs) at small Bjorken-x is directly proportional to the spin-dependent odderon amplitude, but the asymmetry is only non-zero when the sum over final-state hadrons is restricted to either positively or negatively charged hadrons separately, because the quark and antiquark Sivers functions have opposite signs under charge conjugation and would cancel in an inclusive sum.

## Method
The authors apply transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization formulas for TEECs in the back-to-back limit of electroproduction of a hadron-electron pair, incorporating TMD evolution at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy. They expand the quark TMDs at the initial scale in terms of the small-x dipole amplitude from the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory, separating the real part (pomeron) and imaginary part (odderon) of the dipole S-matrix. The dipole amplitudes are evolved to smaller x using the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with running coupling, and the TEEC jet function for charged hadrons is modeled by fitting to the NPC23 collinear fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers
- The predicted magnitude of the TEEC Sivers asymmetry at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) is on the 0.1% level.
- The initial condition for BK evolution uses a saturation scale Q_s0^2 = 0.061 GeV^2, with parameters C^2 = 4.97, e_c = 35.3, and B_perp = 14.1 mb from the fit in Casuga (2023).
- The non-perturbative Sudakov parameters are g_2 = 0.84, g_1 = 0.106 GeV^2 for unpolarized TMDs, g_1^s = 0.180 GeV^2 for the Sivers function, and g_1^D = 0.042 GeV^2 for fragmentation, with Q_0^2 = 2.4 GeV^2 and b_max = 1.5 GeV^{-1}.
- The odderon amplitude is modeled as O^perp_{1T,x}(r) = -P_x(r) * kappa * r^2 * Q_s0^3 / (8 M_p) with kappa = 1/3.

## Context
This work extends the TEEC formalism developed in Kang (2023) for unpolarized targets to the transversely polarized case, connecting the Sivers asymmetry at small x to the odderon amplitude discussed in Hatta (2005), Yao (2018), and Dong (2018). The C-odd nature of the small-x quark Sivers function requires charge-separated hadron measurements, similar to the approach in Mantysaari (2025) and Boer (2015). The proposed observable at the EIC provides a new channel to benchmark the still poorly constrained odderon amplitude, whose existence was demonstrated experimentally only recently by the D0 Collaboration (2020).
