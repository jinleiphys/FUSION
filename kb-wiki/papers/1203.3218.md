# Neutrino and antineutrino cross sections in $^{12}$C

> We extend the formalism of weak interaction processes, obtaining new expressions for the transition rates, which greatly facilitate numerical calculations, both for neutrino-nucleus reactions and muon capture. We have done a thorough study of exclusive (ground state) properties of $^{12}$B and $^{12}$N within the projected quasiparticle random phase approximation (PQRPA). Good agreement with experimental data is achieved in this way. The inclusive neutrino/antineutrino ($ν/\tildeν$) reactions $^{12}$C($ν,e^-)^{12}$N and $^{12}$C($\tildeν,e^+)^{12}$B are calculated within both the PQRPA, and the relativistic QRPA (RQRPA). It is found that the magnitudes of the resulting cross-sections: i) are close to the sum-rule limit at low energy, but significantly smaller than this limit at high energies both for $ν$ and $\tildeν$, ii) they steadily increase when the size of the configuration space is augmented, and particulary for $ν/\tildeν$ energies $> 200$ MeV, and iii) converge for sufficiently large configuration space and final state spin.

## Key claim

The paper extends the formalism for weak interaction processes to obtain new expressions for transition rates that facilitate numerical calculations, but the key hedge is that the inclusive cross-sections for neutrino and antineutrino reactions on 12C are close to the sum-rule limit only at low energy, while at high energies (above about 200 MeV) they are significantly smaller than this limit and steadily increase with configuration space size, only converging for sufficiently large configuration space and final state spin.

## Method

The authors calculated exclusive (ground state) properties of 12B and 12N using the Projected Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (PQRPA) with model spaces S2, S3, S4, and S6 (including 2, 3, 4, and 6 hbar-omega harmonic oscillator shells) and a delta-interaction, with single-particle energies and pairing strengths varied via chi-squared search to match experimental spectra of odd-mass nuclei. They also computed inclusive cross-sections for the reactions 12C(nu_e,e-)12N and 12C(antinu_e,e+)12B using both PQRPA and the Relativistic QRPA (RQRPA) with spaces S20 and S30, employing the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with DD-ME2 parameterization and Gogny force for pairing.

## Key numbers

- Exclusive cross-section (ECS) for 12C_gs(nu_e,e-)12N_gs plotted for incident neutrino energies up to 60 MeV (DAR region), up to 100 MeV (supernova neutrino signal search), and up to 250 MeV (LSND neutrino oscillation search), in units of 10^-42 cm^2.
- Inclusive cross-sections (ICS) for both neutrino and antineutrino reactions plotted in units of 10^-39 cm^2, with the sum rule limit SR_GT obtained using an average excitation energy of 17.34 MeV.
- For antineutrinos, spins contribute only up to J=11, while for neutrinos it is necessary to go up to J=14 to achieve saturation.
- Saturation of inclusive cross-sections requires going to energies larger than 600 MeV.
- The Fermi coupling constant used is G = (3.04545 +/- 0.00006) x 10^-12 (in natural units).
- The Coulomb energy difference between initial and final nuclei is given as approximately 1.45 Z A^{-1/3} MeV, and the neutron-proton mass difference is 1.29 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of weak interaction processes developed by Krmpotić (2002, 2005) and the Walecka group (1995), providing new expressions for transition rates that bridge the terminology gap between neutrino-nucleus scattering (Coulomb, longitudinal, transverse electric, transverse magnetic) and nuclear beta-decay/muon capture (vector and axial matrix elements with degrees of forbiddenness). The calculations are compared with previous RPA-like approaches including RPA by Volpe (2000), CRPA by Kolbe (1999), and RQRPA by Paar (2007), as well as shell model results by Volpe (2000). The results enable future studies of forbidden processes using low-energy neutrino and antineutrino beams, as proposed by Lazauskas (2007), but the authors conclude that such studies would require antineutrino fluxes with energies up to at least 150 MeV, whereas the proposed experiments only reach 80 MeV.
