# Relativistic corrections to hadron-hadron correlation function

> Femtoscopy offers a sensitive probe of hadron emission sources and hadronic interactions. In this study, we examine relativistic corrections to scattering phase shifts and correlation functions using the two-body Dirac equation framework. We analyze the impact of the Darwin term and spin-dependent potentials, showing that these relativistic effects, especially spin-related interactions, significantly enhance the proton-proton correlation function. Our findings emphasize the necessity of including relativistic corrections for precise femtoscopic analyses.

# Relativistic corrections to hadron-hadron correlation function

## Key claim

Relativistic corrections, particularly spin-dependent interactions from the two-body Dirac equation framework, significantly enhance the proton-proton correlation function compared to non-relativistic calculations; however, the magnitude of this enhancement depends on the spin state of the pair, with spin-singlet and spin-triplet states showing opposite effects that partially cancel in the spin-averaged observable.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Dirac equation for proton-proton scattering using a Yukawa-type potential with pion exchange parameters, reformulating the relativistic equation into Schrödinger-like form via the Crater–Van Alstine approach. Scattering phase shifts are extracted using the variable phase approach (VPA) for both uncoupled (spin-singlet) and coupled (spin-triplet) channels, and the Lednický–Lyuboshits (LL) model is then applied to compute two-particle correlation functions from the extracted scattering lengths and effective ranges.

## Key numbers

- Non-relativistic proton-proton scattering length: $a_0 = -18.69$ fm, effective range: $r_{\rm eff} = 2.82$ fm
- Relativistic case without spin-dependent potentials: $a_0 = -24.15$ fm, $r_{\rm eff} = 2.71$ fm
- Relativistic spin-singlet state: $a_0 = -13.43$ fm, $r_{\rm eff} = 2.99$ fm
- Relativistic spin-triplet state: $a_0 = -22.10$ fm, $r_{\rm eff} = -129.35$ fm
- Source size parameter used in correlation function calculations: $R = 1.0$ fm
- Pion mass parameter in Yukawa potential: $m_\pi = 0.165$ GeV; pion-nucleon coupling constant: $f_\pi = 0.2707$

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic femtoscopy formalism of Koonin (1977) and Pratt (1990) by incorporating relativistic corrections through the two-body Dirac equation, building on the relativistic constraint dynamics approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering by Liu (2002) and to meson-meson scattering by Crater (2004). The results contrast with the momentum-space relativistic propagator approach of Albaladejo (2024), offering a coordinate-space alternative. The findings enable more precise extraction of hadron emission source sizes and interaction parameters from experimental femtoscopy data at RHIC and LHC, particularly for light hadron pairs where relativistic effects are expected to be even more pronounced than in the proton-proton system studied here.
