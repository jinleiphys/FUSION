# Renormalization Group Flow Equations and the Phase Transition in O(N)-models

> We derive and solve flow equations for a general O(N)-symmetric effective potential including wavefunction renormalization corrections combined with a heat-kernel regularization. We investigate the model at finite temperature and study the nature of the phase transition in detail. Beta functions, fixed points and critical exponents β, ν, δand ηfor various N are independently calculated which allow for a verification of universal scaling relations.

## Key claim

The paper derives and solves renormalization group (RG) flow equations for a general O(N)-symmetric effective potential including wavefunction renormalization corrections, going beyond the local potential approximation; the authors claim that their choice of blocking functions (f_k^{(1)}) accelerates convergence of the flow equations compared to using the lowest-order blocking function f_k^{(0)}.

## Method

The authors combine a Wilsonian RG approach with heat-kernel (proper-time) regularization to derive coupled flow equations for the effective potential V and the wavefunction renormalization Z for a d-dimensional massless O(N) scalar theory. They introduce smooth blocking functions f_k^{(i)} to regulate momentum integrals, with different blocking functions required for different orders of the derivative expansion, and perform an RG improvement by replacing bare potentials with running k-dependent counterparts. The flow equations are solved numerically on a grid using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm with adaptive step size, starting from tree-level initial parameters at an ultraviolet scale Lambda = 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature T_c ≈ 140 MeV for the O(4) model, obtained from the full potential analysis including wavefunction renormalization contributions.
* Critical exponent β ≈ 0.4 for the order parameter near T_c in the O(4) model.
* Initial UV parameters: Λ = 800 MeV, Φ²_{0,Λ} = (140 MeV)², λ_Λ = 49, Z_Λ = 0.8.
* Perfect agreement with three-loop chiral perturbation theory expansion up to order O((T/f_π)^8) for temperatures up to T ≈ 45 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the local potential approximation by including wavefunction renormalization corrections (Z_1 and Z_2) for O(N)-symmetric models, building on prior RG approaches by Wilson and Kadanoff, and on the proper-time regularization method of Oleszczuk (1994). The results enable verification of universal scaling relations and critical exponents for various N, and the finite-temperature extension allows comparison with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for low temperatures and with Widom scaling functions near T_c. The authors note that their T_c value of ≈140 MeV is higher than the ≈100 MeV obtained by Wettich (1996) using a full potential analysis with exact RG, attributing the discrepancy to different implementations of threshold functions.
