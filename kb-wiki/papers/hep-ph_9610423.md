# Nuclear beams in HERA

> A study has been made of the physics interest and feasibility of experiments with nuclear beams in HERA. It is shown that such experiments widen considerably the horizon for probing QCD compared to that from free nucleon targets. In addition there is some sensitivity to physics beyond the standard model. Hence the option to include circulating nuclear beams in HERA allows a wide range of physics processes to be studied and understood.

## Key claim

The paper argues that circulating nuclear beams in HERA would allow a new regime of QCD to be probed experimentally for the first time, specifically the regime where the virtual photon interacts coherently with all nucleons at a given impact parameter, but this claim is conditional on achieving luminosities of 1-10 pb\(^{-1}\) per nucleon and on the assumption that nuclear beams can be stored simultaneously or changed frequently to minimize systematic errors.

## Method

The authors performed a feasibility study for deep inelastic electron-nucleus scattering at HERA, using the one-photon exchange formula with MRS(A) structure functions to compute expected counting rates and statistical errors. They assumed nuclear beams with Z/A = 1/2 (nucleon energy 410 GeV), electron energy 27.6 GeV, and 100% detection efficiency for scattered electrons with E' > 5 GeV and angle > 3 degrees or quarks scattered at > 10 degrees with energy > 5 GeV. Statistical precision was estimated for measurements of structure function ratios, gluon density ratios via 2+1 jet events, scaling violations, and inelastic J/ψ production, using luminosities of 1-10 pb\(^{-1}\) per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon beam energy for nuclear targets: 410 GeV (half the HERA proton energy of 820 GeV)
- Electron beam energy: 27.6 GeV
- Required luminosities for most studies: 1-10 pb\(^{-1}\) per nucleon
- For shadowing measurements with 2 pb\(^{-1}\) per nucleon, statistical errors on \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) ratios are < 1% in many bins
- For gluon density ratio measurements via 2+1 jet events: luminosity of 10 pb\(^{-1}/A\) for each nucleus
- For inelastic J/ψ production gluon density ratio: integrated luminosity of 10 pb\(^{-1}/A\) for each nucleus, probing scale approximately \(m_{J/\psi}^2 \approx 10\) GeV\(^2\)
- For diffractive structure function ratio measurements: statistical precision of order 5% with luminosities of \(10/A1\) and \(10/A2\) pb\(^{-1}\) for each nucleus
- Sensitivity to differences in gluon densities between bound and free nucleons: approximately 5% of the total gluon density at \(x \approx 10^{-3}\) from scaling violation method
- For 2+1 jet analysis: jets selected with invariant mass \(\overline{s} \geq 100\) GeV\(^2\)
- Energy loss of quarks and gluons traversing nuclear medium: expected below 350 MeV/fm (from Baier et al.)

## Context

This paper extends the physics case for nuclear beams at HERA beyond the fixed-target lepton-nucleus scattering data from NMC, E665, SLAC, and BCDMS, which only touched the surface of nonlinear QCD effects due to limited \(Q^2\) range at small \(x\). The work builds on the theoretical framework of Gribov (1969) on the paradox of high-energy nuclear interactions, Bjorken's Aligned Jet Model (1971), and Baier et al. on the Landau-Migdal-Pomeranchuk effect in QCD. It enables future experimental programs to study gluon shadowing, color transparency, perturbative color opacity, and parton energy loss in nuclear matter, with direct connections to heavy ion physics at RHIC and LHC where measurements of gluon shadowing are necessary for interpreting high \(p_t\) jet rates.
