# Low-energy quadrupole states in neutron-rich tin nuclei

> We present a study on the isoscalar quadrupole strength in tin nuclei, focusing mainly on the low-energy region. The calculations are performed using the Skyrme type energy density functionals within the fully self-consistent quasiparticle random phase approximation, allowing for a good description of the experimental data for the first 2$^+$ state and the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance. It is found that the first $2^+$ state and the low-energy quadrupole states between 3 and 6 MeV display an opposite behavior with increasing neutron number. While the strength of the first $2^+$ state decreases, some excited states start to accumulate between 3 and 6 MeV, and increase their strength with increasing neutron number. This low-energy region between 3 and 6 MeV is quite sensitive to the changes in the shell structure with increasing neutron number. In particular, between $^{116}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn, the filling of the neutron orbitals with large values of $j$, has an important impact on the low-energy region. Our analysis shows that the low-energy states have a non-collective character, except the first 2$^+$ state. In addition, the states in the low-energy region above 5 MeV display an interesting pattern: with the increase of the neutron number, their strength increases and their nature changes, namely they switch from proton excitations to neutron-dominated one. We conclude that the low-energy quadrupole states between 3 and 6 MeV can provide information about the shell evolution in open-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The low-energy quadrupole states between 3 and 6 MeV in neutron-rich tin nuclei show an opposite behavior to the first 2+ state with increasing neutron number, but the authors cannot positively conclude that these states constitute a "pygmy quadrupole resonance" because they lack the collective character and concentrated strength that would justify that classification.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations on top of Hartree-Fock BCS ground states using Skyrme-type energy density functionals (SkM*, SGII, and SLy5 interactions) with volume-type pairing. The calculations assumed spherical symmetry, used a discretized continuum in a 20 fm spherical box with 0.1 fm mesh size, and employed a quasiparticle energy cut-off of 100 MeV to satisfy the Energy Weighted Sum Rule. The discrete QRPA spectra were averaged with a Lorentzian width of 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The first 2+ state energy increases from approximately 1.18 MeV in 116Sn to higher values in 130Sn, while the reduced electric transition probability decreases correspondingly
- The neutron 1h11/2 orbital occupation probability increases from 0.22 in 116Sn to 0.75 in 128Sn, and reaches full occupation at the N=82 shell closure in 132Sn
- The isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance centroid energy decreases smoothly by about 0.7 MeV from 14.07 MeV in 116Sn to 13.36 MeV in 132Sn
- In 124Sn, the low-energy quadrupole states between 2 and 5 MeV exhaust 0.62% of the isoscalar EWSR within QRPA, but including states at 5.33 and 5.55 MeV brings the total to 4.26%, comparable to the experimental values of 3.8(5)% and 5.5(6)% and the QPM value of 4.1%
- In 128Sn, a low-energy state at 4.44 MeV has a reduced transition probability of 1459.3 fm4 and exhausts 2.71% of the EWSR, while a state at 5.0 MeV has 2761.8 fm4 and exhausts 5.78% of the EWSR
- The neutron 1h11/2-1h11/2 configuration contributes 70.52% to the first 2+ state in 128Sn, increasing from 26.25% in 120Sn

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of low-energy quadrupole strength in neutron-rich nuclei, including RPA calculations for 28O by Tarpanov (1997) and Yokoyama (1995), shell model and QRPA studies of Nickel isotopes by Lanza (2003), and quasiparticle phonon model calculations on tin nuclei by Tsoneva (2011) which first named these states as "pygmy quadrupole resonance." The calculations are compared with experimental data from the (17O,17O'gamma) reaction at 340 MeV by Pellegri (2015) and (alpha,alpha'gamma) and (gamma,gamma') experiments by Spieker (2016) on 124Sn. The work enables future investigations of how low-energy quadrupole states can serve as probes of shell evolution in open-shell nuclei, though the authors note that QRPA does not include more complex configurations beyond two quasiparticles, so coupling with more complicated configurations would change precise energies and strength fragmentation.
