# Octupole degree of freedom for the critical-point candidate nucleus $^{152}$Sm in a reflection-asymmetric relativistic mean-field approach

> The potential energy surfaces of even-even $^{146-156}$Sm are investigated in the constrained reflection-asymmetric relativistic mean-field approach with parameter set PK1. It is shown that the critical-point candidate nucleus $^{152}$Sm marks the shape/phase transition not only from U(5) to SU(3) symmetry, but also from the octupole-deformed ground state in $^{150}$Sm to the quadrupole-deformed ground state in $^{154}$Sm. By including the octupole degree of freedom, an energy gap near the Fermi surface for single-particle levels in $^{152}$Sm with $β_2 = 0.14 \sim 0.26$ is found, and the important role of the octupole deformation driving pair $ν2f_{7/2}$ and $ν1i_{13/2}$ is demonstrated.

## Key claim
The critical-point candidate nucleus $^{152}$Sm marks a shape/phase transition not only from spherical U(5) to axially deformed SU(3) symmetry, but also from the octupole-deformed ground state in $^{150}$Sm to the quadrupole-deformed ground state in $^{154}$Sm, with the two minima in $^{152}$Sm separated by only 0.33 MeV and a 0.5 MeV barrier.

## Method
The authors performed constrained reflection-asymmetric relativistic mean-field (RAS-RMF) calculations with the PK1 parameter set for even-even $^{146-156}$Sm isotopes. They mapped the potential energy surfaces in the ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) plane by simultaneously constraining the mass quadrupole and octupole moments, using a two-center harmonic-oscillator basis with 16 major shells and BCS pairing with a constant gap $\Delta = 11.2/\sqrt{A}$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state binding energy of $^{152}$Sm: 1254.46 MeV (calculated) vs 1253.10 MeV (experimental), a deviation of 0.2%
- Ground-state quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ of $^{152}$Sm: 0.20 (calculated) vs 0.31 (experimental)
- Ground-state octupole deformation $\beta_3$ of $^{152}$Sm: 0.15 (calculated)
- Energy difference between the octupole minimum at ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) = (0.20, 0.15) and the quadrupole minimum at ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) = (0.29, 0) in $^{152}$Sm: 0.33 MeV, with a 0.5 MeV barrier
- For $^{150}$Sm, the octupole-deformed global minimum at ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) = (0.19, 0.14) is 1.36 MeV deeper than the corresponding quadrupole-deformed state
- For $^{154}$Sm and $^{156}$Sm, ground states are well quadrupole deformed with ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) $\sim$ (0.33, 0)

## Context
This work extends earlier axially deformed RMF calculations by Meng (2005) on $^{144-158}$Sm that discussed the spherical-to-axially-deformed shape transition, by now including the octupole degree of freedom. The results provide a microscopic explanation for the recent experimental observation by Garrett (2009) of a repeating excitation pattern in $^{152}$Sm, which had been interpreted as shape coexistence rather than critical-point behavior. The calculations enable future generator coordinate method studies of the low-lying states in $^{152}$Sm based on the two minima in the ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) plane.
