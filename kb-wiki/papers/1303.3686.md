# Fluid dynamics near the QCD critical point

> We present a fully dynamical model to study the chiral and deconfinement transition of QCD simultaneously. The quark degrees of freedom constitute a heat bath in local equilibrium for both order parameters, the sigma field and a dynamical Polyakov loop. The nonequilibrium evolution of these fields is described by Langevin equations including dissipation and noise. In several quench scenarios we are able to observe a delay in the relaxation times near the transition temperature for a critical point as well as a first-order phase transition scenario. During the hydrodynamical expansion of a hot quark fluid we find a strong enhancement of thermal fluctuations at the first-order transition compared to a scenario with a critical point.

# Fluid dynamics near the QCD critical point

## Key claim
The authors present a fully dynamical model (Polyakov-chiral fluid dynamics, PχFD) that simultaneously treats the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions of QCD, and they observe that during the hydrodynamical expansion of a hot quark fluid, thermal fluctuations are strongly enhanced at a first-order phase transition compared to a scenario with a critical point, but only for a specific tuning of the quark-meson coupling parameter g.

## Method
The model couples a fluid-dynamically expanding medium of quarks and antiquarks (treated as a locally thermalized heat bath) to two order parameters: the sigma field (for chiral symmetry) and a dynamical Polyakov loop (for deconfinement). The nonequilibrium evolution of these fields is described by Langevin equations with temperature-dependent damping and stochastic noise terms, while the quark fluid evolves via ideal relativistic fluid dynamics with source terms for energy transfer from the fields. The authors tune the quark-meson coupling g to study either a first-order phase transition (g=4.7) or a critical point (g=3.52) at vanishing baryochemical potential.

## Key numbers
- First-order transition temperature: T_c = 172.9 MeV (for g=4.7)
- Critical point transition temperature: T_c = 180.5 MeV (for g=3.52)
- Polyakov loop damping coefficient: η_ℓ = 5/fm
- Initial temperature for fluid dynamic expansion: T = 200 MeV (above both transition temperatures)
- Equilibration study time: t = 24 fm (for intensity histograms of fluctuations)
- Domain formation snapshots shown at t = 1 fm, t = 4 fm, and t = 7 fm during first-order phase transition

## Context
This work extends the chiral fluid dynamics approach of Nahrgang (2011) and Mishustin (1998) by incorporating a dynamical Polyakov loop to model the deconfinement transition alongside the chiral transition, building on the Polyakov-loop-extended quark meson model of Schaefer (2007) and Ratti (2005). The results enable predictions for upcoming experiments at FAIR and NICA, where domain formation and clustering at the first-order phase transition could produce non-statistical fluctuations in hadron rapidity density within single events, as previously suggested by Mishustin (1998) and Sasaki (2007). The approach goes beyond standard hydrodynamics that includes the phase transition only in the equation of state, as done by Steinheimer (2007).
