# The First fm/c of Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present an introductory review of the early time dynamics of high-energy heavy-ion collisions and the kinetics of high temperature QCD. The equilibration mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma uniquely reflect the non-abelian and ultra-relativistic character of the many body system. Starting with a brief expose of the key theoretical and experimental questions, we provide an overview of the theoretical tools employed in weak coupling studies of the early time non-equilibrium dynamics. We highlight theoretical progress in understanding different thermalization mechanisms in weakly coupled non-abelian plasmas, and discuss their relevance in describing the approach to local thermal equilibrium during the first ${\rm fm}/c$ of a heavy-ion collision. Some important connections to the phenomenology of heavy-ion collisions are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

This review argues that the equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma in the first fm/c of a heavy-ion collision can be understood through weak-coupling QCD kinetic theory, but only under the assumption that the momentum distribution is isotropic; the treatment of anisotropic systems with plasma instabilities remains an open problem that the authors explicitly set aside.

## Method

The authors review the QCD Boltzmann equation with elastic 2-to-2 scattering and inelastic 1-to-2 collinear radiation, decomposing elastic processes into soft collisions described by a Fokker-Planck equation and hard collisions. They derive the drag and diffusion coefficients for isotropic systems and analyze the Landau-Pomenanchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect on gluon radiation, solving an integral equation for the splitting rate in both the Bethe-Heitler and deep LPM regimes. The review then applies this kinetic framework to the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario for overoccupied and underoccupied systems undergoing longitudinal expansion.

## Key numbers

- The elastic scattering rate for transverse kicks is given by (2π)² dΓ_el/d²q_⊥ = g² C_A T* [1/q_⊥² - 1/(q_⊥² + 2m²)], where m is the soft scale and T* is the hard scale.
- The drag coefficient η = (g² C_A m²)/(8π) log(μ_⊥²/m²) and the transverse diffusion coefficient q̂ = (g² C_A (2 T* m²))/(4π) log(μ_⊥²/(2m²)) for isotropic systems.
- The LPM energy scale ω_LPM ~ m⁴/q̂, below which the Bethe-Heitler regime applies and above which multiple scattering suppresses radiation.
- In the Bethe-Heitler limit, the differential splitting rate for soft gluons (z ≪ 1) is dΓ_inel^BH(Ps)/dz ~ α_s P_{g→g}^{soft}(z) q̂/m².
- In the deep LPM regime for soft gluons, the emission rate is dΓ_inel^LPM(Ps)/dz = (α_s/2π) P_{g→g}^{soft}(z) √[q̂/(P z(1-z))].
- The simulation initial condition uses dN/(ν_g d²x_⊥ dy) = 0.23 Q_s²/λ and √⟨p_T²⟩ = 1.8 Q_s, with λ = 4π α_s N_c.
- In equilibrium, the occupancy measure λ⟨p f_p⟩/⟨p⟩ reaches 0.11λ.

## Context

This review extends the leading-order QCD kinetic theory framework developed by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002) and the transport coefficient calculations of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2003) and Ghiglieri et al. (2018). It provides the theoretical foundation for the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario originally proposed by Baier et al. (2000) and numerically simulated by Kurkela and Zhu (2015). The review explicitly excludes plasma instabilities discussed by Mrowczynski (1993) and Romatschke and Strickland (2003), noting that their role in underoccupied systems remains unresolved and that current kinetic theory implementations have ignored them to date.
