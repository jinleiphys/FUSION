# Light-by-light scattering sum rules constraining meson transition form factors

> Relating the forward light-by-light scattering to energy weighted integrals of the γ* γ-fusion cross sections, with one real photon (γ) and one virtual photon (γ*), we find two new exact super-convergence relations. They complement the known super-convergence relation based on the extension of the GDH sum rule to the light-light system. We also find a set of sum rules for the low-energy photon-photon interaction. All of the new relations are verified here exactly at leading order in scalar and spinor QED. The super-convergence relations, applied to the γ* γ-production of mesons, lead to intricate relations between the γγ-decay widths or the γ* γ-transition form factors for (pseudo-) scalar, axial-vector and tensor mesons. We discuss the phenomenological implications of these results for mesons in both the light-quark sector and the charm-quark sector.

## Key claim

The paper derives two new exact super-convergence relations for forward light-by-light scattering, complementing the known Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH)-type sum rule, and shows that these relations impose intricate cancellations among meson transition form factors and decay widths across different quantum numbers, though the phenomenological verification relies on available data and perturbative QED checks rather than a full non-perturbative QCD proof.

## Method

The authors use dispersion relations to connect the forward \(\gamma^\ast\gamma^\ast\) scattering amplitudes to energy-weighted integrals of \(\gamma^\ast\gamma\) fusion cross sections, where one photon is real and the other has arbitrary space-like virtuality. They expand the low-energy side of these sum rules using an effective Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian (dimension-8 and dimension-10 operators) and verify the resulting relations at leading order in scalar and spinor QED. They then apply the sum rules to \(\gamma^\ast\gamma\) production of mesons (pseudo-scalars, scalars, axial-vectors, tensors) using available data for light-quark and charm-quark sectors.

## Key numbers

* The GDH-type sum rule: \(\int_{s_0}^\infty \frac{ds}{s} [\sigma_2(s) - \sigma_0(s)] = 0\), where \(s\) is the total energy squared and \(s_0\) is the first inelastic threshold for \(\gamma\gamma\) fusion.
* Low-energy constants from scalar QED at tree level: \(c_1 = \frac{\alpha^2}{m^4} \frac{7}{1440}\), \(c_2 = \frac{\alpha^2}{m^4} \frac{1}{1440}\).
* Low-energy constants from spinor QED at tree level: \(c_1 = \frac{\alpha^2}{m^4} \frac{1}{90}\), \(c_2 = \frac{\alpha^2}{m^4} \frac{7}{360}\).
* Spinor QED sum rule for dimension-10 LEC: \(c_4 = -\frac{\alpha^2}{m^6} \frac{1}{315}\), in agreement with Dicus (1997).
* Sum rule for \(c_1\) via longitudinal cross section: \(c_1 = \frac{1}{8\pi} \int_{s_0}^\infty ds \left[ \frac{\sigma_{LL}(s, Q_1^2, Q_2^2)}{Q_1^2 Q_2^2} \right]_{Q_1^2=Q_2^2=0}\), verified in perturbative field theory but with open questions about convergence in non-perturbative QCD.

## Context

This work extends the GDH-type sum rule for the photon target, originally inferred by Gerasimov (1973) and Brodsky (1995), to the case of one virtual photon, and adds two new super-convergence relations involving longitudinally polarized \(\gamma^\ast\gamma\) cross sections. It also generalizes the sum rules for low-energy light-by-light constants first established by Pascalutsa (2010). The results provide constraints on meson transition form factors relevant for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon \((g-2)_\mu\), and can be tested with data from BABAR, Belle, and future BES-III measurements. The paper enables estimates of unmeasured \(\gamma^\ast\gamma\) transition form factors for tensor mesons such as \(f_2(1285)\) and \(a_2(1320)\).
