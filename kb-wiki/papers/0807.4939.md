# Coalescence Models For Hadron Formation From Quark Gluon Plasma

> We review hadron formation from a deconfined quark gluon plasma (QGP) via coalescence or recombination of quarks and gluons. We discuss the abundant experimental evidence for coalescence from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and compare the various coalescence models advocated in the literature. We comment on the underlying assumptions and remaining challenges as well as the merits of the models. We conclude with a discussion of some recent developments in the field.

## Key claim

The paper reviews coalescence/recombination as the dominant mechanism for hadron formation from quark-gluon plasma at intermediate transverse momentum (1.5 < pT < 5 GeV/c) in heavy-ion collisions, arguing that this model uniquely explains the baryon enhancement and valence quark-number scaling of elliptic flow, though the exact degree of thermalization of the parton ensemble before hadronization remains uncertain.

## Method

The authors compare multiple coalescence model implementations (including ALCOR and light-cone wave function approaches) against experimental data from RHIC Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They analyze three key observables: baryon-to-meson ratios, nuclear modification factors (R_AA and R_CP), and elliptic flow parameter v2 for identified hadrons across centrality classes. The review evaluates how each model handles the transition from deconfined partons to color-neutral hadrons without requiring additional parton creation through splitting or string breaking.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for QGP formation: T_c approximately 185 MeV from lattice QCD calculations
- Core temperature in RHIC collisions: in excess of 300 MeV
- Intermediate transverse momentum region: 1.5 < pT < 5 GeV/c
- Baryon-to-meson ratio in Au+Au at pT = 3 GeV/c: approximately 1:1 (baryons and mesons in nearly equal proportion), compared to 1:3 in nucleon-nucleon collisions
- Baryon elliptic flow (v2) at intermediate pT: 50% larger than meson v2
- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s) = 200 GeV per nucleon-nucleon pair for Au+Au collisions

## Context

This review extends the recombination approach first developed in the 1970s for leading particle effects in hadronic collisions (Das 1977) and early nuclear collision applications by Gupt (1983) and the ALCOR model by Biro, Levai, and Zimanyi (1994-1999). It directly addresses the "baryon puzzle" from RHIC data that contradicted expectations from fragmentation models and hydrodynamic flow explanations (Vance 1998, Kolb 2003, Hirano 2003). The work enables future studies of deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration signatures by connecting hadronic observables to the pre-hadronic parton phase.
