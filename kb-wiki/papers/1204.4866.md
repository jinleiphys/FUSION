# The black disk to be observed in the Orear region

> It is argued that the very first signatures of the approach to the black disk asymptotical limit in hadron collisions may be observed in the differential cross section of elastic scattering. The exponentially decreasing with the angle (or $\sqrt {|t|}$) regime beyond the diffraction peak will become replaced by an oscillatory behavior or by the power-like falloff. Some estimates of energies where this can happen are presented.

# The black disk to be observed in the Orear region

## Key claim
The first signatures of approach to the black disk asymptotic limit in hadron collisions may appear as a replacement of the exponential falloff in the Orear region by oscillatory behavior or power-like falloff, but this transition is conditional on the ratio Z = 4πB/σ_t approaching 1/2 and on the product of real-to-imaginary ratios ρ_dρ_l tending to zero; the author estimates this could happen at energies around 75 TeV.

## Method
The author solves the unitarity integral equation for the imaginary part of the elastic scattering amplitude outside the diffraction peak, assuming the Gaussian shape of the amplitude in the diffraction cone and negligible overlap function from inelastic processes. The solution yields an exponential falloff in the Orear region whose slope depends on the parameter Z = 4πB/σ_t and the product ρ_dρ_l. The author then examines the analytic continuation of this solution when Z approaches 1/2, which replaces the exponential with a cosine-squared oscillatory term.

## Key numbers
- At LHC energy √s = 7 TeV, the ratio Z = 4πB/σ_t equals 1.00 (Table 1).
- At ISR energy √s = 62.5 GeV, Z = 1.50 (Table 1).
- The diffraction peak slope B increases from about 10 GeV⁻² at √s ≈ 6 GeV to about 20 GeV⁻² at LHC energies.
- The ratio ρ_0 predicted by the black disk formula π/ln(s/1 GeV²) gives 0.177 at 7 TeV, compared to dispersion relation estimates of 0.14; the value 0.14 is only reached at 75 TeV according to the asymptotic formula.
- The distance between oscillation zeros in the black disk limit is given by Δ(√|t|) = π / √(2B ln((1+ρ_dρ_l)/Z)).
- The fit to TOTEM 7 TeV data yields ρ_l ≈ -2 in the Orear region.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing black disk model of hadron collisions, where total cross sections grow as σ_t = 2πR² with R = R₀ ln s, and elastic and inelastic cross sections each approach 50% of the total. The author notes that current LHC data (TOTEM) show Z = 1.00 rather than the black disk value of 0.5, and that the diffraction peak slope B requires stronger-than-logarithmic energy dependence. The analysis builds on prior solutions of the unitarity equation by Andreev and Dremin (2009, 2010) and on fits to TOTEM data by Dremin et al. (2012). The paper suggests that the transition from exponential to oscillatory or power-like behavior in the Orear region may be the first observable signature of black disk asymptotics, but also notes that hard parton scattering could fill the Orear region at very high energies.
