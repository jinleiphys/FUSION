# Calculation of the N to Delta electromagnetic transition matrix element

> We present results on the ratio of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole amplitudes, $R_{EM}={\cal G}_{E2}/{\cal G}_{M1}$, for the transition $γN to Δ$ from lattice QCD. We consider both the quenched and the 2-flavor theory.

## Key claim

The paper reports lattice QCD calculations of the ratio of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole amplitudes, \(R_{EM}\), for the \(\gamma N \rightarrow \Delta\) transition, finding that unquenching (including dynamical quark effects) drives the ratio more negative and yields results consistent with experimental measurements, whereas a previous quenched study by Leinweber could not statistically exclude a zero value.

## Method

The authors computed three-point correlation functions on the lattice using the conserved electromagnetic current with Wuppertal smearing and equal re-weighting of \(U\) and \(U^*\) gauge configurations to reduce noise. The ratio \(R_{EM}\) was extracted from plateau values of the ratio \(R_\sigma\) in the rest frame of the \(\Delta\) at the lowest accessible momentum transfer, using 100 quenched configurations on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice at \(\beta=6.0\) and 100 unquenched (2-flavor) SESAM configurations on a \(16^3 \times 32\) lattice at \(\beta=5.6\) at four sea quark masses.

## Key numbers

- Quenched result at \(\kappa = 0.1558\): \(R_{EM} = (-0.9 \pm 0.8)\%\) with \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} \sim 0.4\), at momentum transfer \(q^2 \approx 0.14\ \text{GeV}^2\) on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice.
- Unquenched results at \(q^2 \sim 0.53\ \text{GeV}^2\) (with \(a^{-1}=1.85\ \text{GeV}\)): \(R_{EM} = (-2.24 \pm 0.46)\%\) at \(\kappa_{sea}=0.1560\) (\(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.83\)); \((-2.25 \pm 0.55)\%\) at \(\kappa_{sea}=0.1565\) (\(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.81\)); \((-3.40 \pm 0.61)\%\) at \(\kappa_{sea}=0.1570\) (\(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.76\)); \((-2.98 \pm 0.90)\%\) at \(\kappa_{sea}=0.1575\) (\(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.69\)).
- For \(\kappa_{sea}=0.1570\), the ratio of Coulomb quadrupole to magnetic dipole amplitudes \(R_{SM} = (-3.2 \pm 2.1)\%\).
- Experimental comparison: \(R_{EM} = (-2.1 \pm 0.2_{\text{stat}} \pm 2.0_{\text{mod}})\%\) at \(q^2 = 0.126\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the Athens experiment, and \((-1.6 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.4)\%\) at similar momentum transfer from Joo.

## Context

This work extends the earlier quenched lattice study by Leinweber, which had insufficient statistics to exclude a zero value for \(R_{EM}\), by using smearing techniques, a larger lattice, and more configurations. It presents the first full QCD (2-flavor) results for this transition, using SESAM lattices. The unquenched results, which are more negative than the quenched ones, point to pionic cloud contributions to baryon deformation and enable direct comparison with experimental measurements from the Athens and Joo collaborations.
