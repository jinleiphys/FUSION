# Systematic study of near yrast band structures in odd-mass $^{125-137}$Pr and $^{127-139}$Pm isotopes

> In the present work, the basis space in the triaxial projected shell model approach is expanded to include three and five quasiparticle configurations for odd-proton systems. This extension allows to investigate the high-spin band structures observed in odd-proton systems up to and including the second band crossing region, and as a first major application of this development, the high-spin properties are investigated for odd-mass $^{125-137}$Pr and $^{127-139}$Pm isotopes. It is shown that band crossings in the studied isotopes have mixed structures with first crossing dominated by one-proton coupled to two-neutron configuration for the lighter isotopes which then changes to three-proton configuration with increasing neutron number. Further, $γ$-bands based on quasiparticle states are also delineated in the present work, and it is predicted that these band structures built on three-quasiparticle configurations become favoured in energy for heavier systems in the high-spin region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by expanding the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) basis space to include three- and five-quasiparticle configurations for odd-proton systems, the high-spin band structures of odd-mass Pr and Pm isotopes can be described up to and including the second band crossing region, with the first crossing dominated by a one-proton coupled to two-neutron configuration for lighter isotopes changing to a three-proton configuration with increasing neutron number.

## Method

The authors extended the TPSM basis space for odd-proton systems to include one-proton, one-proton coupled to two-neutron, three-proton, and five-quasiparticle (three-proton plus two-neutron) configurations. They performed angular-momentum projection from triaxially deformed Nilsson intrinsic states and diagonalized a shell model Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions. Calculations were carried out for 13 odd-mass isotopes (125-137Pr and 127-139Pm) using deformation parameters adopted from earlier studies, with about 40 to 50 intrinsic states selected around the Fermi surface for each system.

## Key numbers

* Deformation parameters for 125Pr: axial quadrupole deformation ε = 0.300, triaxial deformation ε' = 0.100, γ = 18.4 degrees
* Deformation parameters for 139Pm: axial quadrupole deformation ε = 0.190, triaxial deformation ε' = 0.090, γ = 25.3 degrees
* For 125Pr, the three-quasiparticle band (one-proton plus two-neutron aligned) crosses the ground-state band and becomes yrast at I = 41/2
* For 127Pr, the first band crossing occurs at I = 39/2 due to two-neutron alignment
* For 129Pr, the first band crossing changes to two-proton alignment and occurs at I = 35/2
* For 131Pr, the band crossing also occurs at I = 35/2
* For heavier Pr isotopes (135Pr, 137Pr), the band crossing occurs at I = 43/2
* For 127Pm, the three-quasiparticle state crosses the ground-state band at I = 43/2
* For 129Pm and 131Pm, band crossing occurs at I = 39/2 due to two-neutron alignment
* The γ-band based on the ground-state (K = 7/2) lies at approximately 1.0 MeV excitation energy at I = 11/2 for 125Pr
* The γ-band built on the aligned three-quasiparticle state (K = 5/2) is located at approximately 2.5 MeV excitation energy at I = 11/2 for 125Pr
* For 125Pr, a five-quasiparticle state crosses the three-quasiparticle state at I = 59/2

## Context

This work extends the TPSM approach previously applied to even-even and odd-odd nuclei in the mass ~130 region (Sheikh et al., 2017; Bhat et al., 2014) to odd-proton systems by including proton-aligning configurations that were absent in earlier projected shell model (PSM) studies. The results address discrepancies noted in standard cranked shell model (CSM) calculations, which underpredicted alignment gains for lighter isotopes like 127Pr and 131Pm (Cederwall et al., 1998), and in earlier PSM calculations that neglected proton configurations and showed discrepancies for heavier Pm isotopes. The work enables future identification of γ-bands built on quasiparticle states in odd-mass nuclei, particularly predicting that three-quasiparticle band structures become favored at high spin in heavier systems, with 139Pm identified as the best candidate for experimental observation.
