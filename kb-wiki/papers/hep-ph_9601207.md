# Relativistic SU(4) and Quaternions

> A classification of hadrons and their interactions at low energies according to SU(4) allows to identify combinations of the fifteen mesons $π$, $ω$ and $ρ$ within the spin-isospin decomposition of the regular representation \rhdmulti{15}. Chirally symmetric SU(2)$\times$SU(2) hadron interactions are then associated with transformations of a subgroup of SU(4). Nucleon and Delta resonance states are represented by a symmetric third rank tensor \rhdmulti{20} whose spin-isospin decomposition leads to $4\oplus 16$ `tower states' also known from the large-N$_c$ limit of QCD. Towards a relativistic hadron theory, we consider possible generalizations of the stereographic projection {\bf S}$^{2}$ $\to$ {\bf C} and the related complex spinorial calculus {\it on the basis of the division algebras with unit element}. Such a geometrical framework leads directly to transformations in a quaternionic projective `plane' and the related symmetry group SL(2,{\bf H}). In exploiting the Lie algebra isomorphism sl(2,{\bf H}) $\cong$ su$*$(4) $\cong$ so(5,1), we focus on the Lie algebra su$*$(4) to construct quaternionic Dirac-like spinors, the associated Clifford algebra and the relation to SU(4) by Weyl's unitary trick. The algebra so(5,1) contains the de Sitter-algebra so(4,1) which can be contracted to the algebra of the Poincaré group.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that SU(4) symmetry can classify low-energy hadrons and their interactions, and that a relativistic extension of this framework can be constructed using quaternionic projective geometry and the Lie algebra su*(4), which is isomorphic to so(5,1) and contains the de Sitter algebra so(4,1) that contracts to the Poincare group. The hedge is that this is presented as a "possible generalization" of the stereographic projection based on division algebras, rather than a fully established relativistic hadron theory.

## Method

The author classifies hadrons and their low-energy interactions according to SU(4), identifying combinations of the fifteen mesons (pi, omega, rho) within the spin-isospin decomposition of the regular representation 15. Nucleon and Delta resonance states are represented by a symmetric third-rank tensor 20, whose spin-isospin decomposition yields 4 ⊕ 16 "tower states" known from the large-N_c limit of QCD. The paper then generalizes the stereographic projection S^2 → C using division algebras with unit element, leading to transformations in a quaternionic projective plane and the symmetry group SL(2,H), exploiting the Lie algebra isomorphism sl(2,H) ≅ su*(4) ≅ so(5,1) to construct quaternionic Dirac-like spinors and the associated Clifford algebra, connecting to SU(4) via Weyl's unitary trick.

## Key numbers

- The regular representation of SU(4) contains 15 mesons (pi, omega, rho) in its spin-isospin decomposition.
- The symmetric third-rank tensor representation of SU(4) is dimension 20, whose spin-isospin decomposition gives 4 ⊕ 16 "tower states".
- The Lie algebra isomorphism chain is sl(2,H) ≅ su*(4) ≅ so(5,1), with so(5,1) containing so(4,1) which contracts to the Poincare group algebra.

## Context

This work extends the classification of hadrons using SU(4) symmetry and connects it to the large-N_c limit of QCD, which predicts similar "tower states" for nucleons and Delta resonances. The paper builds on prior work using division algebras and stereographic projection to construct relativistic theories, and aims to provide a geometric foundation for hadron interactions that incorporates both chiral SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry and relativistic invariance. The approach enables a quaternionic formulation of Dirac spinors and Clifford algebras that may lead to new relativistic hadron models, though the paper does not present experimental predictions or numerical comparisons with data.
