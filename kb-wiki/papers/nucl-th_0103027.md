# rho -> pi pi decay in nuclear medium

> We calculate the medium modifications of the rho pi pi vertex in a relativistic hadronic framework, incorporating nucleons and Delta(1232) isobars. We find a substantial increase of the rho pi pi coupling, dominated by processes where the Delta is excited. The coupling depends significantly on the virtuality of the rho, which is related to analytic properties of the vertex function. We analyze the general case of a non-zero three-momentum of the rho with respect to the nuclear medium, and evaluate the resulting widths and spectral strength in the transverse and longitudinal channels for the rho -> pi pi decay. These widths are used to obtain the dilepton yields from rho decays in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper finds a substantial increase of the ρππ coupling in nuclear medium, dominated by processes where the Δ(1232) isobar is excited, but this result is obtained within a leading-density approximation that breaks down at high baryon densities, so the numerical results at saturation density and above should be treated as indications of possible large effects rather than accurate predictions.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium ρππ vertex using a fully relativistic hadronic framework with nucleons and Δ(1232) isobars, working at zero temperature and to leading order in baryon density. They evaluate the seven diagrams shown in Fig. 1 (bubble and triangle diagrams with nucleon and Δ propagators) using the leading-density approximation where the nucleon three-momentum is set to zero in the integrands, which simplifies the calculation to a factor of ρ_B/4 times the integrand evaluated at k=0. The analysis covers both the case of ρ at rest with respect to the medium and the general case of non-zero three-momentum, allowing separate treatment of transverse and longitudinal polarizations.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum ρππ coupling constant is g_ρ ≈ 5.26
- At the physical ρ mass (M = 776 MeV) and saturation density ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³, the effective coupling g_eff is roughly two times larger than the vacuum value, giving an in-medium width of about 600 MeV (a factor of 4 enhancement)
- The dominant contribution comes from the N-Δ bubble diagram (g), contributing 0.62 − 1.16i to the relative medium contribution at M = m_ρ with Γ_Δ = 120 MeV
- Singularities in the vertex function occur at M² = (0.657 GeV)² and M² = (0.021 GeV)² for Γ_Δ = 0
- The critical three-momentum for the appearance of a second kinematic branch is q_crit = M√(M² − 4m_π²)/(2m_π)
- The Δ width is parameterized as Γ_Δ(ρ_B) = Γ_Δ(1 + (40 MeV/Γ_Δ)(ρ_B/ρ_0))

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Herrmann, Friman, and Nörenberg (1998) who computed the ρππ vertex for ρ at rest in nuclear matter using non-relativistic couplings, and by Urban, Buballa, Rapp, and Wambach (2000) who extended that calculation to non-zero three-momenta and finite temperature. The authors' own previous work analyzed the ω → ππ decay in nuclear medium (Broniowski, Florkowski, Hiller, 2000-2001). The results are applied to compute dilepton yields from ρ decays for the CERES Pb+Au experiment, finding that the spreading of the width helps explain the low-mass enhancement of dilepton spectra but the overall normalization falls significantly short of experimental data.
