# Electroweak Corrections to the True Muonium Hyperfine Splitting

> In contrast to other atomic systems, in true muonium ($μ^+μ^-$) the leading-order $Z$ boson corrections to the hyperfine splitting are shown to be experimentally accessible in the near future. This contribution ($-109$ MHz) constitutes a necessary contribution to a full $\mathcal{O}(mα^7)$ calculation of the true muonium hyperfine splitting. This would enable a number of solutions to the muon problem to be constrained. Additionally, we compute the general expression for a pseudovector coupling to particle-antiparticle bound states at leading order, including the annihilation channel.

# Electroweak Corrections to the True Muonium Hyperfine Splitting

## Key claim

The leading-order Z boson corrections to the true muonium hyperfine splitting, which scale as O(mα^6), are shown to be experimentally accessible in the near future, but only if the full set of O(mα^6) and O(mα^7) QED corrections are also computed to reach the required 1 MHz precision. The electroweak contribution of -109 MHz is comparable in size to O(mα^7) corrections and constitutes a necessary part of a complete O(mα^7) calculation.

## Method

The author computes the leading-order energy shift to the hyperfine splitting from Z boson exchange and annihilation channels in true muonium using the non-relativistic limit of the weak neutral current Lagrangian, following the formalism of Eides (1995). The exchange contribution is derived from the t-channel Z boson exchange, while the annihilation channel is computed from the s-channel diagram, with both expressions evaluated using the non-relativistic wave function at the origin. The results are generalized to arbitrary pseudovector bosons (Z') and then specialized to the electroweak Z boson using the relation between the Fermi constant and gauge boson masses.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order Z boson correction to the true muonium 1s hyperfine splitting is -109 MHz
- The exchange channel contributes -2/3 of the total, and the annihilation channel contributes -1/3, with the total given by ΔE^Z = -(3G_F)/(8√2) × (m_μ^3 α^3)/(π n^3)
- The current theoretical prediction for the 1s hyperfine splitting is 42330577(800)(1200) MHz, where the first error is from model-dependent hadronic effects and the second is an estimate of unknown O(mα^6) corrections unique to true muonium
- The estimated O(mα^6) corrections from electron vacuum polarization effects unique to true muonium are approximately 1200 MHz
- The electroweak correction is -2.58 × 10^(-6) of the leading-order hyperfine splitting (42260692 MHz)
- The dimensionless electroweak coupling for true muonium scales as G_F m_μ^2 ≈ α^(3.2), compared to G_F m_e^2 ≈ α^(8.4) for electronic systems

## Context

This work extends the calculation of weak interaction effects in atomic systems from Eides (1995), who resolved the sign ambiguity for Z exchange in hydrogen and muonium, to the case of true muonium (μ^+μ^-), which was left untreated in that work. The paper also generalizes the result to arbitrary pseudovector bosons (Z'), building on constraints for positronium from Karshenboim (2010). The calculation is motivated by upcoming experiments including the Heavy Photon Search (HPS) and the DIRAC experiment, which may observe true muonium and measure its hyperfine splitting to O(100 MHz) precision, enabling discrimination of BSM solutions to the muon problem such as those proposed by Tucker-Smith (2010) and others.
