# Adiabatic Hydrodynamization: a Natural Framework to Find and Describe Prehydrodynamic Attractors

> The adiabatic hydrodynamization framework is a promising framework within which to describe and characterize pre-hydrodynamic attractors in a model-independent fashion. Using this framework, we define a procedure to identify a time-dependent change in coordinates which reveals a dynamical reduction in the number of active degrees of freedom. Applying this procedure to the kinetic theory of a Bjorken-expanding gas of gluons in the small angle elastic scattering limit, we are able to intuitively explain the self-similar evolution of the gluon distribution function long before the applicability of hydrodynamics, as well as the loss of memory of its initial condition.

## Key claim

The adiabatic hydrodynamization (AH) framework can quantitatively describe pre-hydrodynamic attractors in a more general kinetic theory than previously demonstrated, provided one optimizes the time-dependent momentum rescalings to maximize adiabaticity at each time step, even when analytic eigenstates are unknown and adiabaticity is not exactly satisfied.

## Method

The authors applied the AH framework to the kinetic theory of a Bjorken-expanding gas of gluons in the small-angle elastic scattering limit, using a Fokker-Planck collision kernel that includes a term proportional to \(I_b \nabla \cdot \hat{p} f\) not present in prior work. They recast the Boltzmann equation into a pseudo-Schrödinger equation with an effective Hamiltonian \(H_{\rm eff}\), expressed it in a basis of left and right states, and at each time step chose the momentum rescaling parameters \(B(y)\) and \(C(y)\) to minimize \(\braket{\partial_y \ket{0}_R | \partial_y \ket{0}_R}\) while setting the ground state energy to zero via \(A(y)\).

## Key numbers

* The scaling exponents for the gluon distribution function in the bottom-up thermalization scenario are \(\alpha = -2/3\), \(\beta = 0\), and \(\gamma = 1/3\) for the self-similar form \(f(\bm{p},\tau) = \tau^\alpha w_S (p_\perp \tau^{-\beta}, p_z \tau^{-\gamma})\).
* The adiabaticity condition is quantified by \(\delta_A^{n} = | \bra{ n}_L\partial_y \ket{0}_R / (\epsilon_n-\epsilon_0) | \ll 1\), where \(\epsilon_n\) are eigenvalues of \(H_{\rm eff}\).
* The effective Hamiltonian includes a factor \(q = g_s^4 N_c^2 / (4\pi) \log(p_{UV}/p_{IR})\) from the collision kernel, with UV cutoff \(p_{UV} = B(y)\) and IR cutoff \(p_{IR} = m_D = \sqrt{2N_c g_s^2 I_b}\).

## Context

This work extends the AH framework introduced by Brewer (2019) and applied to the over-occupied limit of small-angle scattering by Brewer (2022), by relaxing assumptions that allowed analytic eigenstates and exact adiabaticity. It reproduces scaling exponents previously found by Mazeliauskas (2018) using moment methods in QCD effective kinetic theory, and connects to the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier (2000). The results enable a unified adiabatic description of all phases of the bottom-up scenario, from early-time scaling through hydrodynamization, in upcoming work.
