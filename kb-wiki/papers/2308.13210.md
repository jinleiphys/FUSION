# Robust features of QCD phase diagram through a Contact Interaction model for quarks: A view from the effective potential

> Our research delves into the QCD phase diagram in the temperature $T$ and quark chemical potential $μ$ plane. We use a unique confining contact interaction effective model of quark dynamics that maintains the QCD symmetry intact. By embedding the model into a Schwinger-Dyson equations framework, within a Landau gauge rainbow-ladder-like truncation, we derive the gap equation. In order to accurately regulate the said equation, we utilize the Schwinger optimal time regularization scheme. We further derive the effective potential of the model by integrating the gap equation over the dynamical mass, which along with the confining length scale serve as parameters for the chiral and confinement deconfinement phase transitions, respectively. A cross-over transition is observed at low $μ$ and above a critical value of the temperature $T_c$, whilst a first order phase transition is found for low $T$ at high density. The critical end point is estimated to be located at $(μ_{E}/T_{c,0}=1.6, T_{E}/T_{c,0}=0.42)$, which falls within the range of other QCD effective models predictions. $T_{c,0} =208$ MeV is the critical temperature at vanishing $μ$. Screening effects of the medium which dilute the strength of the effective coupling are considered by including the vacuum polarization contribution due to quarks at high temperatures into the framework. It locates the critical end point at $( μ^{E}_{c}/T_c \approx2.6, T^{E}_{c}/T_c \approx 0.57)$, which hints for a deeper analysis of screening effects on models of this kind.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a confining contact interaction model, regularized via the Schwinger optimal time scheme and embedded in a Schwinger-Dyson equations framework, produces a QCD phase diagram with a cross-over at low chemical potential and a first-order transition at high density, with the critical end point (CEP) located at \((\mu_{E}/T_{c,0}=1.6, T_{E}/T_{c,0}=0.42)\); however, when screening effects from the medium are included via a temperature-dependent coupling, the CEP shifts to \((\mu^{E}_{c}/T_c \approx 2.6, T^{E}_{c}/T_c \approx 0.57)\), indicating that the precise location is sensitive to how medium effects are incorporated.

## Method

The authors used a confining contact interaction (a symmetry-preserving vector-vector version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) within the Schwinger-Dyson equations framework in Landau gauge with a rainbow-ladder-like truncation. They derived the gap equation and regulated it using the Schwinger optimal time regularization scheme, introducing an infrared cut-off \(\tau_{ir}\) (confinement scale) and an ultraviolet cut-off \(\tau_{uv}\). The effective thermodynamic potential was obtained by integrating the gap equation over the dynamical quark mass \(M\), and the phase transitions were analyzed by locating the minima of this potential as functions of temperature \(T\) and quark chemical potential \(\mu\). Screening effects were later included by making the effective coupling temperature-dependent via a vacuum polarization contribution.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at vanishing chemical potential: \(T_{c,0} = 208\) MeV (from the thermal gradient of the dynamical mass).
- Critical end point without screening: \((\mu_{E}/T_{c,0}=1.6, T_{E}/T_{c,0}=0.42)\), corresponding to \((\mu^{E}_{c} \approx 350\) MeV, \(T^{E}_{c} \approx 90\) MeV).
- Critical chemical potential at zero temperature (first-order transition): \(\mu_c \approx 380\) MeV.
- With screening effects included: critical temperature at \(\mu=0\) drops to \(T_c \approx 132\) MeV; CEP shifts to \((\mu^{E}_{c}/T_c \approx 2.6, T^{E}_{c}/T_c \approx 0.57)\), corresponding to \((\mu_E \approx 340\) MeV, \(T_E \approx 65\) MeV).
- Model parameters: \(\tau_{ir} = (240\ \mathrm{MeV})^{-1}\), \(\tau_{uv} = (905\ \mathrm{MeV})^{-1}\), \(\alpha_{\rm eff} = 5.739 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}\), bare quark mass \(m_{u/d} = 7\) MeV.
- Dynamical quark mass in vacuum: \(M = 358\) MeV (chiral limit) and \(M = 368\) MeV (with current quark mass \(m=7\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the confining contact interaction model previously developed by Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2010) and Roberts et al. (2011) for meson properties, applying it to the finite-temperature and finite-density QCD phase diagram. The results are consistent with the range of CEP locations predicted by other effective QCD models, such as those by Sasaki (2007), Costa (2008), and Fu (2007), which place the CEP in the region \((\mu_{E}/T_{c}=1.0-2.0, T_{E}/T_{c}=0.4-0.8)\) for two light quark flavors. The inclusion of screening effects via a temperature-dependent coupling, following the approach of Lo (2022), shifts the CEP and highlights the need for a deeper analysis of medium effects on such models.
