# The mixed phase quark core in massive hybrid stars

> We investigate the properties of hybrid star and the mixed phase core to explore the radius ratio of the mixed phase in hybrid star. In the context of observed massive neutron stars (NSs), we examine the internal structure, phase transitions, and the impacts of the equation of state (EOS) in maximum hybrid star. We investigate the stiffness changes in the EOS during the hadron-quark phase transition within the hybrid stars. The relativistic mean-field (RMF) model is used to describe hadronic matter, while to the represent quark matter, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is applied. We explore the strength of vector coupling in quark matter, which delays the onset density of the mixed phase and reduces the size of the mixed-phase core in a hybrid star, but does not exhibit a clear correlation with the central density. In a hybrid star with a maximum mass of approximately 2 solar masses ($M_\odot$), a mixed-phase core of $\sim$5 km may exist, comprising about $40\%$ of the total radius. However, our results do not support the existence of a sizable quark core containing the mixed phase ($R_{\rm{MP}}>1/2~R_{\rm{total}}$) for the maximum-mass hybrid star or for a 2~$M_\odot$ massive star.

## Key claim

The authors find that a mixed-phase quark core of approximately 5 km may exist in hybrid stars with maximum mass near 2 solar masses, comprising about 40% of the total radius, but their results do not support the existence of a sizable quark core exceeding half the total radius for the maximum-mass hybrid star or for a 2 solar mass star.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model and the RMF model with density-dependent isovector coupling (RMFL) to describe hadronic matter, employing parameter sets BigApple, NL3L-50, TM1e, and NL3. For quark matter, they apply the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector coupling strength G_V treated as a free parameter, and model the hadron-quark mixed phase using the Gibbs construction with global charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star mass reaches approximately 2.5 solar masses with G_V greater than or equal to 0.4 G_S using the NL3L-50 parameter set
- A mixed-phase core of approximately 5 km may exist in a hybrid star with maximum mass near 2 solar masses, comprising about 40% of the total radius
- With G_V = 0, the mixed-phase core radius can reach close to 6 km and contain around 0.3 solar masses for the maximum mass neutron star
- The squared sound velocity c_s^2 and polytropic index gamma for the mixed phase are the lowest among all three phases, with gamma values below 1.7 for all mixed phase and quark phase equations of state
- The onset density of the mixed phase shifts to higher densities with increasing vector coupling G_V

## Context

This work extends studies of hybrid stars containing quark matter, following the approach of Glendenning (1992) for Gibbs construction and using similar frameworks to Annala (2020) and Liu (2023) who investigated quark cores in massive neutron stars. The results enable future comparisons with observational constraints from NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, as well as gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 and GW190814, to further constrain the existence and size of mixed-phase quark cores in massive neutron stars.
