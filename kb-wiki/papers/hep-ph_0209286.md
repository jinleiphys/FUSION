# Thermal approach to RHIC

> Applications of a simple thermal model to ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are presented. We compute abundances of various hadrons, including particles with strange quarks, the pT-spectra, and the HBT radii for the pion. Surprising agreement is found, showing that the thermal approach can be used successfully to understand and describe the RHIC data.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a simple thermal model with a single universal freeze-out temperature (rather than separate chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures) can successfully describe all "soft" features of RHIC data, particle ratios, transverse-momentum spectra, and HBT radii, provided that the complete treatment of hadronic resonances from the Particle Data Table is included.

## Method

The model uses ideal-gas statistical distributions for all hadrons and resonances at freeze-out, with four adjustable parameters: temperature (T), baryon chemical potential (μ_B), freeze-out proper time (τ), and transverse size (ρ_max). Particle ratios at midrapidity fix T and μ_B via χ² fitting, while the geometric parameters τ and ρ_max are determined from the p⊥-spectra. Resonance decays are implemented exactly using a semi-analytic cascade method that transforms the thermal distribution of each parent resonance into the momentum distribution of its decay products, with all branching ratios taken from the Particle Data Table.

## Key numbers

- Temperature: T = 165 ± 7 MeV (fitted from particle ratios at √s_NN = 130 GeV Au+Au collisions)
- Baryon chemical potential: μ_B = 41 ± 5 MeV
- χ²/n = 0.97 for the fit to 16 data points of particle ratios
- Energy density at freeze-out: ε = 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Pressure at freeze-out: P = 0.08 GeV/fm³
- Baryon density at freeze-out: ρ_B = 0.02 fm⁻³
- For minimum-bias collisions: τ = 5.6 fm, ρ_max = 4.5 fm, giving ρ_max/τ = 0.81, maximum transverse flow β⊥^max = 0.62, average transverse flow ⟨β⊥⟩ = 0.46
- For 0-5% most central collisions: τ = 8.2 fm, ρ_max = 6.9 fm, giving ρ_max/τ = 0.84, β⊥^max = 0.64, ⟨β⊥⟩ = 0.47
- Predicted ratio φ/h⁻ = 0.019 (experiment: 0.021 ± 0.001)
- Predicted ratio Ω⁻/h⁻ = 0.0010 (experiment: 0.0012 ± 0.0005)
- Predicted ratio Ξ⁻/π⁻ = 0.0072 (experiment: 0.0085 ± 0.0020)

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach previously applied at SPS energies (Braun-Munzinger et al., Rafelski et al., and others) to the new RHIC data at √s_NN = 130 GeV. It contradicts the two-freeze-out concept advocated by Heinz and collaborators, showing that a single freeze-out temperature works when resonances are treated completely. The model enables systematic comparison of thermal parameters with lattice QCD results (Karsch: T_c = 154 ± 8 MeV for three massless flavors) and provides a baseline against which signatures of new phenomena (such as quark-gluon plasma formation) can be identified.
