# Strangelets in Cosmic Rays

> The properties of strangelets are reviewed and two experiments searching for them in cosmic rays are described. The prospects for strangelets as ultra-high energy cosmic rays beyond the classical GZK-cutoff are discussed.

# Strangelets in Cosmic Rays

## Key claim

Strangelets (small lumps of strange quark matter) may be absolutely stable or metastable down to some small limiting baryon number, and could exist in cosmic rays at detectable levels; however, their existence remains unconfirmed experimentally, and the theoretical stability depends sensitively on the bag constant, strange quark mass, and finite-size effects such as surface and curvature energies.

## Method

The paper reviews the MIT bag model formalism for strange quark matter, deriving bulk properties from the pressure equilibrium between quark Fermi gases and the confining bag pressure. For finite strangelets (baryon number A < 10^7), the author presents a multiple reflection expansion treatment that adds surface and curvature corrections to the bulk energy, along with Coulomb and zero-point energy terms. The formalism is applied to compute mass formulas for strangelets with varying strange quark mass, and the resulting charge-to-mass ratios are compared to experimental signatures for the AMS-02 space-based detector and the Lunar Soil Strangelet Search (LSSS) experiment.

## Key numbers

- Energy per baryon for bulk three-flavor massless strange quark matter: 829 MeV × B_{145}^{1/4}, where B_{145}^{1/4} ≡ B^{1/4}/145 MeV
- Energy per baryon for bulk two-flavor (u,d) quark matter: 934 MeV × B_{145}^{1/4}
- Mass formula for strangelets with massless s-quarks and B^{1/4}=145 MeV: M = 829A + 0A^{2/3} + 351A^{1/3} MeV
- Mass formula for strangelets with m_s=150 MeV and B^{1/4}=145 MeV: M = 874A + 77A^{2/3} + 232A^{1/3} MeV
- Charge-to-mass relation for unpaired strangelets with A << 700: Z = 0.1 × (m_s/150 MeV)^2 × A
- Charge-to-mass relation for unpaired strangelets with A >> 700: Z = 8 × (m_s/150 MeV)^2 × A^{1/3}
- Charge-to-mass relation for color-flavor locked strangelets: Z = 0.3 × (m_s/150 MeV) × A^{2/3}
- Predicted integrated flux of strangelets reaching the inner Solar System: F = 2×10^5 × A^{-1.067} × Z^{-0.6} per year per square meter per steradian
- LSSS sensitivity: 10^{-17} over a wide mass range from a 15-gram lunar soil sample
- GZK-cutoff energy for strangelets: E_Total ≈ 10^{19} × A eV, allowing energies beyond 3×10^{20} eV for large A

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for strange quark matter developed by Bodmer (1971), Chin (1979), Witten (1984), and Farhi and Jaffe (1984), and incorporates finite-size effects from multiple reflection expansion calculations by Berger and Jaffe (1986) and Madsen (1993, 1994). The paper connects these theoretical predictions to two ongoing experimental searches: AMS-02 (a space-based detector led by Samuel Ting) and LSSS (a lunar soil analysis led by Jack Sandweiss). The work enables quantitative flux predictions that can be tested by these experiments, and proposes strangelets as a candidate explanation for ultra-high energy cosmic rays beyond the GZK cutoff, circumventing both the acceleration limit (proportional to charge Z, which can be large) and the photo-disintegration energy threshold (which scales with baryon number A).
