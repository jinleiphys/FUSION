# Quark mass dependence of H-dibaryon in $ΛΛ$ scattering

> We study the quark mass dependence of the H-dibaryon in the strangeness $S=-2$ baryon-baryon scattering. A low-energy effective field theory is used to describe the coupled-channel scattering, in which the quark mass dependence is incorporated so as to reproduce the lattice QCD data by the HAL QCD collaboration in the SU(3) limit. We point out the existence of the Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole in the $ΛΛ$ scattering amplitude below the threshold in the SU(3) limit, which may cause the Ramsauer-Townsend effect near the $NΞ$ threshold at the physical point. The H-dibaryon is unbound at the physical point, and a resonance appears just below the $NΞ$ threshold. As a consequence of the coupled-channel dynamics, the pole associated with the resonance is not continuously connected to the bound state in the SU(3) limit. Through the extrapolation in quark masses, we show that the unitary limit of the $ΛΛ$ scattering is achieved between the physical point and the SU(3) limit. We discuss the possible realization of the "H-matter" in the unphysical quark mass region.

## Key claim

The H-dibaryon is unbound at the physical point, but a resonance appears just below the $N\Xi$ threshold; the pole associated with this resonance is not continuously connected to the bound state in the SU(3) limit due to coupled-channel dynamics, and the unitary limit of $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering is achieved between the physical point and the SU(3) limit.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions and a bare H-dibaryon field to describe coupled-channel $\Lambda\Lambda$, $N\Xi$, and $\Sigma\Sigma$ scattering in the $S=-2$, $J=0$, $I=0$ channel. Quark mass dependence is introduced linearly in the baryon masses (via leading-order chiral perturbation theory) and in the coupling constants, with parameters determined by fitting to HAL QCD lattice data in the SU(3) limit at three quark masses. The scattering amplitude is obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a sharp cutoff $\Lambda = 300$ MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the flavor singlet bound state in the SU(3) limit: 26 MeV/c$^2$ (contact model) and 27 MeV/c$^2$ (bare H model) at the HAL-1 lattice point
- $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length in the SU(3) limit: $-2.31$ fm (contact model) and $-2.32$ fm (bare H model)
- Best-fit parameters for baryon masses: $M_0 = 0.948$ GeV/c$^2$, $\alpha = -0.754$ GeV$^{-1}$c$^2$, $\beta = -0.644$ GeV$^{-1}$c$^2$, $\sigma = 0.0826$ GeV$^{-1}$c$^2$
- Contact model singlet coupling constants: $\lambda_0^{(1)} = -88.5$ GeV$^{-2}$c$^3$, $\lambda_1^{(1)} = -163$ GeV$^{-4}$c$^7$
- Bare H model parameters: $g^2 = 2350$ GeV$^{-1}$c$^2$, $M_{H,0} = 19.8$ GeV/c$^2$, $\sigma_H = -1.53$ GeV$^{-1}$c$^2$

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies by the HAL QCD collaboration (Inoue et al., 2011) on the H-dibaryon in the SU(3) limit to the physical point and unphysical quark mass regions. It contrasts with earlier approaches using chiral perturbation theory (Haidenbauer et al., 2011, 2015) and Nambu-Goldstone boson loop calculations (Shanahan et al., 2011, 2013) by employing a pionless EFT framework that captures the correct near-threshold scaling and coupled-channel dynamics. The finding of a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson pole in the $\Lambda\Lambda$ amplitude and the prediction of the unitary limit enable future studies of few-body and many-body phenomena (the "H-matter") in unphysical quark mass regions, analogous to the Efimov effect predicted for three pions by Hyodo (2013).
