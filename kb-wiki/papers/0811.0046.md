# Effective Kbar N interaction with strong pi Sigma dynamics constrained by chiral SU(3) symmetry

> We derive a single-channel effective Kbar N interaction from chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics, emphasizing the important role of the pi Sigma channel and the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance. The chiral low energy theorem requires strongly attractive interaction not only in the Kbar N channel but also in the pi Sigma channel. As a consequence of the strong pi Sigma dynamics, the equivalent potential in single Kbar N channel turns out to be less attractive than the one used in a purely phenomenological approach.

## Key claim

The effective single-channel Kbar N potential derived from chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics is less attractive than the purely phenomenological Akaishi-Yamazaki potential, because the chiral low energy theorem requires strong attractive interaction not only in the Kbar N channel but also in the pi Sigma channel, shifting the quasibound state in the Kbar N channel to around 1420 MeV rather than the nominal Lambda(1405) mass.

## Method

The authors start from the leading-order chiral SU(3) meson-baryon interaction in s-wave, given by the Weinberg-Tomozawa term with coupling strengths C_ij determined by group theory. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the coupled Kbar N, pi Sigma, eta Lambda, and K Xi channels in the I=0 sector, using dimensional regularization for the loop integrals. They then perform an exact transformation to derive an effective single-channel Kbar N interaction V^eff that incorporates the dynamics of the other channels, and construct an equivalent local potential in coordinate space with a Gaussian spatial form that reproduces the coupled-channel scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Poles of the full coupled-channel amplitude: z_1 = 1428 - 17i MeV, z_2 = 1400 - 76i MeV
- Single-channel Kbar N interaction alone generates a bound state at 1427 MeV
- Single-channel pi Sigma interaction alone generates a broad resonance at 1388 - 96i MeV
- The Kbar N quasibound state appears at approximately 1420 MeV, giving a "binding energy" of about 15 MeV measured from the Kbar N threshold (approximately 1435 MeV)
- The Akaishi-Yamazaki phenomenological potential has diagonal Kbar N strength of -436 MeV and off-diagonal Kbar N-pi Sigma strength of -412 MeV, with range parameter b approximately 0.66 fm

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach developed by Kaiser, Weise, and collaborators (1995) and Hyodo and others (2002), which successfully reproduces Kbar N scattering data and the Lambda(1405) resonance. It directly contrasts with the phenomenological Akaishi-Yamazaki potential (2002), which treats the Lambda(1405) as a Feshbach resonance with no diagonal pi Sigma interaction. The derived potential enables rigorous few-body calculations of kaonic nuclei such as K^-pp, which have been studied via Faddeev calculations by Shevchenko (2006) and Ikeda (2007) and variational calculations by Yamazaki (2007).
