# Entanglement entropy of a color flux tube in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory

> We construct a novel flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE$^2$), defined as the excess entanglement entropy relative to the vacuum of a region of color flux stretching between a heavy quark-anti-quark pair in pure gauge Yang-Mills theory. We show that FTE$^2$ can be expressed in terms of correlators of Polyakov loops, is manifestly gauge-invariant, and therefore free of the ambiguities in computations of the entanglement entropy in gauge theories related to the choice of the center algebra. Employing the replica trick, we compute FTE$^2$ for $SU(2)$ Yang-Mills theory in (2+1)D and demonstrate that it is finite in the continuum limit. We explore the properties of FTE$^2$ for a half-slab geometry, which allows us to vary the width and location of the slab, and the extent to which the slab cross-cuts the color flux tube. Following the intuition provided by computations of FTE$^2$ in (1+1)D, and in a thin string model, we examine the extent to which our FTE$^2$ results can be interpreted as the sum of an internal color entropy and a vibrational entropy corresponding to the transverse excitations of the string.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a novel "flux tube entanglement entropy" (FTE$^2$) defined as the excess Rényi entropy of a region of color flux between a heavy quark-antiquark pair relative to the vacuum, and demonstrates numerically for SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1)D that this quantity is finite in the continuum limit and manifestly gauge-invariant, thereby avoiding the ambiguities that plague standard entanglement entropy definitions in gauge theories.

## Method

The authors compute FTE$^2$ on the lattice using the replica trick, expressing it as the difference between the Rényi entropy in the presence of static quark sources (defined via gauge-invariant correlators of Polyakov loops) and the Rényi entropy of the Yang-Mills vacuum. They employ a "half-slab" geometry that allows systematic variation of the slab width, position relative to the quark-antiquark pair, and the extent to which the slab cross-cuts the color flux tube. The lattice simulations are performed for SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1)D, and the results are compared to expectations from a thin string model and from analytical results in (1+1)D Yang-Mills theory.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of entanglement entropy in Yang-Mills theory, which began with computations for SU(2) in (3+1)D by Buividovich (2008) and subsequent lattice studies by Velytsky (2008), Itou (2015), and others. The FTE$^2$ construction addresses the fundamental issue that the Hilbert space of gauge-invariant states cannot be expressed as a tensor product of subspaces, which was previously handled via the extended Hilbert space approach (Buividovich, 2008; Casini, 2013) or the algebraic approach (Casini, 2013; Radicevic, 2014). The paper enables future studies of the dependence of flux tube entanglement on quark-antiquark separation, number of colors, slab geometry, and temperature, and provides a framework for distinguishing internal color entropy from vibrational entropy of the string.
