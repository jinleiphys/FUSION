# Nonextensive critical effects in relativistic nuclear mean field models

> We present a possible extension of the usual relativistic nuclear mean field models widely used to describe nuclear matter towards accounting for the influence of possible intrinsic fluctuations caused by the environment. Rather than individually identifying their particular causes we concentrate on the fact that such effects can be summarily incorporated in the changing of the statistical background used, from the usual (extensive) Boltzman-Gibbs one to the nonextensive taken in the form proposed by Tsallis with a dimensionless nonextensivity parameter $q$ responsible for the above mentioned effects (for $q \rightarrow 1$ one recovers the usual BG case). We illustrate this proposition on the example of the QCD-based Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of a many-body field theory describing the behavior of strongly interacting matter presenting its nonextensive version. We check the sensitivity of the usual NJL model to a departure from the BG scenario expressed by the value of $| q - 1|$, in particular in the vicinity of critical points.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that intrinsic fluctuations in nuclear matter, which violate the assumptions of standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, can be summarily incorporated by replacing the statistical background with Tsallis nonextensive statistics characterized by a parameter \(q\). The authors check the sensitivity of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to small departures from the Boltzmann-Gibbs case (quantified by \(|q-1|\)), particularly in the vicinity of critical points, but note that the exact physical interpretation of \(q\) (e.g., temperature fluctuations for \(q>1\) or phase-space correlations for \(q<1\)) remains debated.

## Method

The authors extend the standard SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by replacing the Fermi-Dirac distributions for quarks and antiquarks with their \(q\)-generalized forms from Tsallis statistics, using a prescription that preserves particle-hole symmetry (requiring both \(q\) and \(2-q\) to appear). They then solve the self-consistent gap equations for the \(q\)-dependent constituent quark masses and condensates, and compute thermodynamic quantities such as pressure, energy density, and baryon number susceptibility for different values of \(q\) near the critical end point (CEP) in the temperature-chemical potential plane.

## Key numbers

* For \(q=1\) (Boltzmann-Gibbs case), the compression at the inflection point is \(\rho/\rho_0 = 1.67\) with chemical potential \(\mu = 318.5\) MeV.
* For \(q=1.01\), the chemical potential at the inflection point shifts to \(\mu = 321\) MeV; for \(q=1.02\), it shifts to \(\mu = 326.1\) MeV.
* For \(q=0.99\), the chemical potential at the inflection point shifts to \(\mu = 313\) MeV; for \(q=0.98\), it shifts to \(\mu = 307.7\) MeV and the compression shifts to \(\rho/\rho_0 = 1.72\).
* The critical exponent \(\epsilon\) for baryon number susceptibility changes from the mean-field value of \(2/3\) to approximately \(0.6\) for \(q=1.02\) and approximately \(0.8\) for \(q=0.98\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier \(q\)-NJL model (Rozynek and Wilk, 2010) and the \(q\)-version of the Walecka model by Pereira et al. (2009), which considered only \(q>1\) for nuclear matter. It contrasts with the standard NJL model of Sousa et al. (2009) by showing that nonextensive effects smear the critical end point into a critical area, which could affect the interpretation of baryon number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions and the equation of state for compact stars. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD (which corresponds to \(q=1\)) and with Monte Carlo methods based on nonextensive thermodynamics.
