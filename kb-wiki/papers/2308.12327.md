# Toward experimental determination of spin entanglement of nucleon pairs

> Nuclear entanglement is a flagship in the interdisciplinary direction of nuclear physics and quantum information science. Spin entanglement, a special kind of nuclear entanglement, is ubiquitous in nuclear structures and dynamics. Based on the idea of quantum state tomography, the problem of experimental determination of spin entanglement of two-nucleon pure states is studied directly within the scope of nuclear physics. It is shown that the amount of spin entanglement can be obtained by measuring three spin polarizations of one nucleon in polarization experiments. The errors from imperfect preparation of nucleon pairs are also analyzed. This work not only complements the existing literature of nuclear entanglement but also provides new opportunities for nuclear physics with polarized particles.

## Key claim

The amount of spin entanglement of a two-nucleon pure state can be determined experimentally by measuring three spin polarizations of one nucleon in polarization experiments, but this is presented as a proof of concept that requires nontrivial efforts before becoming a realistic experimental protocol.

## Method

The author applies quantum state tomography (QST) to the spin degrees of freedom of a neutron-proton pair, reconstructing the reduced density matrix of one nucleon from its three spin polarizations measured via nuclear scattering experiments. The transverse spin polarizations are obtained from left-right asymmetry measurements in single scattering of the nucleon against a spin-0 target, while the longitudinal spin polarization is extracted from a double scattering experiment using polarization transfer coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The spin concurrence C satisfies 0 ≤ C ≤ 1, with C = 0 for separable states and C = 1 for maximally entangled states.
- The concurrence is given by C = 2|α₀₀α₁₁ − α₀₁α₁₀| for the spin wave function |Ψ^spin_AB⟩ = α₀₀|0_A0_B⟩ + α₀₁|0_A1_B⟩ + α₁₀|1_A0_B⟩ + α₁₁|1_A1_B⟩.
- The concurrence can be expressed in terms of spin polarizations as C = √(1 − ⟨σ_x̂_A⟩²_A − ⟨σ_ŷ_A⟩²_A − ⟨σ_ẑ_A⟩²_A).
- The transverse spin polarization ⟨σ_ŷ_A⟩_A is determined by the left-right asymmetry formula ⟨σ_ŷ_A⟩_A = (1/A(θ)) × (N_L(θ) − N_R(θ))/(N_L(θ) + N_R(θ)), where A(θ) is the analyzing power.

## Context

This work extends the quantum information approach of Sancho and Huelga (2000) and the Walborn protocol (2006, 2007) for two-qubit entanglement measurement into low-energy nuclear physics, where photonic experimental techniques cannot be directly applied. The study complements theoretical nuclear entanglement work by groups including Kwasniewicz, Kanada-Enyo, Johnson, and Beane (various years 2013-2023), and builds on the experimental Bell test tradition of Lamehi-Rachti and Mittig (1976) and Sakai et al. (2006) using proton-proton scattering. It enables future experimental determination of spin entanglement in nucleon pairs produced by nucleon-nucleon scattering, dinucleon decays, or knockout reactions, and opens new opportunities for nuclear physics with polarized particles.
