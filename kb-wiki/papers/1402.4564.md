# Structure of Compact Stars in Pion Superfluid Phase

> The gross structure of compact stars composed of pion superfluid quark matter is investigated in the frame of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Under the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme, the uncertainty of the thermodynamic functions for inhomogeneous states is cured, and the LOFF state appeared in the hard cutoff scheme is removed from the phase diagram of pion superfluid. Different from the unpaired quark matter and color superconductor, the strongly coupled pion superfluid in the state close to the BCS limit is a possible candidate of compact stars with mass $M\simeq 3M_{\odot }$ and radius $R\simeq 14$ km.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strongly coupled pion superfluid quark matter, in a state close to the BCS limit, can serve as a candidate for compact stars with mass \(M\simeq 3M_{\odot}\) and radius \(R\simeq 14\) km, but only under the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme which removes the LOFF state from the phase diagram; the result is conditional on the regularization method and the assumption that the LOFF state is an artifact of the hard cutoff scheme.

## Method

The author uses the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at mean-field level to describe pion condensation at finite isospin and baryon chemical potentials. To avoid unphysical terms in the thermodynamic potential for inhomogeneous states, the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme is employed instead of the hard three-momentum cutoff. The equation of state from the NJL model is then input into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute the mass-radius relation for nonrotating, spherically symmetric compact stars.

## Key numbers

* Maximum star mass \(M \simeq 3 M_{\odot}\) and radius \(R \simeq 14\) km for a pion superfluid star at \(T=0\) and \(\mu_B = 600\) MeV, using the Pauli-Villars scheme.
* For comparison, normal quark matter yields maximum mass \(\sim 1.8 M_{\odot}\) and radius \(\sim 10\) km.
* Sound velocity squared in the pion superfluid saturates at \(c_s^2 = 0.63\) in the Pauli-Villars scheme and \(0.73\) in the hard cutoff scheme, both much larger than the normal quark matter value \(\sim 1/3\).
* The pion superfluid phase transition occurs at \(\mu_I = m_\pi = 134\) MeV at \(T = \mu_B = 0\).
* Model parameters are fixed by vacuum properties: quark condensate \(\langle u\bar u\rangle = \langle d\bar d\rangle = (-250\ \text{MeV})^3\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, and pion mass \(m_\pi = 134\) MeV.
* The MIT bag constant used at the star surface is \(B = 75\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends studies of compact stars composed of color superconductor quark matter (Shovkovy, 2005; Alford et al., 2008) which found that color superconductivity does not significantly change the mass-radius relation from unpaired quark matter, because the sound velocity remains near \(1/3\). The author shows that pion superfluid, with its much stiffer equation of state, can explain massive compact stars such as PSR J1311-3430 (Romani, 2012). The use of Pauli-Villars regularization resolves a technical problem with the hard cutoff scheme that produced an artificial LOFF state (He et al., 2006; Fukushima, 2008), and the removal of the LOFF state is consistent with the expectation that strongly coupled systems have no LOFF window. The results enable further study of the BEC-BCS crossover in pion superfluid and its astrophysical consequences.
