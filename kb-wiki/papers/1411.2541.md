# Continuum and Three-Nucleon Force Effects on 9Be Energy Levels

> We extend the recently proposed ab initio no-core shell model with continuum to include three-nucleon (3N) interactions beyond the few-body domain. The extended approach allows for the assessment of effects of continuum degrees of freedom as well as of the 3N force in ab initio calculations of structure and reaction observables of p- and lower-sd-shell nuclei. As first application we concentrate on energy levels of the 9Be system for which all excited states lie above the n-8Be threshold. For all energy levels, the inclusion of the continuum significantly improves the agreement with experiment, which was an issue in standard no-core shell model calculations. Furthermore, we find the proper treatment of the continuum indispensable for reliable statements about the quality of the adopted 3N interaction from chiral effective field theory. In particular, we find the 1/2+ resonance energy, which is of astrophysical interest, in good agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The inclusion of continuum degrees of freedom via the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) is indispensable for reliably assessing the quality of chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions in *ab initio* calculations of 9Be energy levels; standard no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations alone can give a misleading impression of the 3N force's performance.

## Method

The authors extended the NCSMC formalism to include explicit 3N interactions for arbitrary p- and lower-sd-shell target nuclei. They computed 9Be eigenstates using an expansion in NCSM eigenstates (describing localized configurations) and binary-cluster channel states (describing n-8Be scattering), solving the coupled Schrödinger equation via the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. The nuclear Hamiltonian used chiral NN interactions at N3LO (Entem & Machleidt, 2003) and 3N interactions at N2LO with a cutoff of 400 MeV/c, softened via the similarity renormalization group (SRG) with flow parameter α = 0.0625 fm⁴.

## Key numbers

- The experimental n-8Be threshold energy is 1.665 MeV.
- The 9Be ground-state (3/2⁻) binding energies are -49.17 MeV (NN+3N-induced) and -54.87 MeV (NN+3N-full) relative to the n-8Be threshold.
- For the 1/2⁺ state, inclusion of continuum shifts the energy by about 5 MeV at Nmax=7, bringing it to its experimental position slightly above threshold.
- The 5/2⁻ resonance has an experimental width of 0.78 keV, and the NCSMC calculations also yield a very narrow width for this state.
- The 7/2⁻ state shows continuum effects below 0.5 MeV, and its energy shift from the chiral 3N interaction has the "wrong sign" compared to experiment.
- For negative-parity states, NCSMC energy differences from the n-8Be threshold are reduced compared to NCSM, with changes well below 0.5 MeV when increasing Nmax from 6 to 12.

## Context

This work extends the NCSMC approach (Barnea et al., 2013) to include explicit 3N interactions beyond the few-body domain, building on prior NCSM/RGM studies (Hupin et al., 2013; Quaglioni & Navratil, 2009) that treated 3N forces only for A=5 systems. Earlier NCSM calculations of 9Be (Forssén et al., 2005; Burov et al., 2010) showed poor model-space convergence and overestimated positive-parity state energies, while Green's function Monte Carlo calculations (Pieper & Wiringa, 2002) indicated strong sensitivity of the 5/2⁻–1/2⁻ splitting to 3N interactions. The present results enable future calculations of E1 transitions and the n-8Be capture cross section, and will be generalized to multi-nucleon projectiles (deuteron, ³H, ³He, ⁴He).
