# $η-η^{\prime}$ Mixture in Chiral Bag

> The $η-η^{\prime}$ mixture is discussed in the chiral bag model to calculate the pseudoscalar octet-singlet mixing angle consistent with the experimental data. The color anomaly is taken into account with the modified boundary conditions, which shows the relation between the $η^{\prime}$ mass and the gluon condensate inside the chiral bag. We show, however, that $η-η^{\prime}$ mixing angle can follow the Cheshire Cat Principle, i.e., insensitivity to the bag radius.

## Key claim
The $\eta$-$\eta^{\prime}$ mixing angle is calculated in the chiral bag model to be $\theta = -12.7^{\rm o}$, which is consistent with the experimental range of $-10^{\rm o}$ to $-23^{\rm o}$, and the authors argue that this angle is insensitive to the bag radius, following the Cheshire Cat Principle, provided the meson masses inside and outside the bag scale at the same rate.

## Method
The authors extend the chiral bag model (CBM) to a U(3)×U(3) group structure, incorporating SU(3) chiral and flavor symmetry breaking terms in the Lagrangian. They diagonalize the bilinear kinetic and mass matrices in the $\pi_0$-$\pi_8$ channel in the weak field approximation to extract the $\eta$-$\eta^{\prime}$ mixing angle. The color anomaly is included via modified boundary conditions, relating the $\eta^{\prime}$ mass to the gluon condensate inside the bag, and the pseudoscalar octet masses are described via the quark condensate.

## Key numbers
- $\eta$-$\eta^{\prime}$ mixing angle: $\theta = -12.7^{\rm o}$, derived from the chiral bag model with parameters $f_K = 114$ MeV, $m_\pi = 140$ MeV, $m_K = 496$ MeV, $m_{\eta^{\prime}} = 960$ MeV, and $m_\eta = 550$ MeV.
- The parameter $\delta = 0.168$, where $\delta = \frac{1}{3}(\chi^2 - 1)$ and $\chi = f_K / f_\pi$.
- The coefficients in the mixing transformation: $a = -1.037$, $b = -1.790$.
- The experimental range for the mixing angle is given as $\theta_{exp} = -10^{\rm o} \sim -23^{\rm o}$.

## Context
This work extends the chiral bag model (CBM) of Brown et al. (1980s) and the SU(3) CBM of Hong and Min (1990s) to include $\eta$ degrees of freedom and the $\eta$-$\eta^{\prime}$ mixture, addressing the long-standing issue of the pseudoscalar octet-singlet mixing angle. The result is consistent with phenomenological analyses (e.g., the quadratic Gell-Mann-Okubo formula giving $\theta = -10^{\rm o}$ and the linear formula giving $\theta = -23^{\rm o}$). The paper enables further study of the Cheshire Cat Principle in the U(1) flavor sector and the role of gluon and quark condensates in meson masses within the bag model.
