# Superallowed $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ $β$ decay of $T =2$ $^{20}$Mg: $Q_{\textrm{EC}}$ value and $βγ$ branching

> \textbf{Background}: Superallowed $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ $β$ decays of isospin $T=2$ nuclides can be used to test theoretical isospin symmetry breaking corrections applied to extract the CKM matrix element $V_{ud}$ from $T = 0,1$ decays by measuring precise $ft$ values and also to search for scalar currents using the $β-ν$ angular correlation. Key ingredients include the $Q_{\textrm{EC}}$ value and branching of the superallowed transition and the half life of the parent. \textbf{Purpose}: To determine a precise experimental $Q_{\textrm{EC}}$ value for the superallowed $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ $β$ decay of $T=2$ $^{20}$Mg and the intensity of $^{20}$Mg $β$-delayed $γ$ rays through the isobaric analog state in $^{20}$Na. \textbf{Method}: A beam of $^{20}$Mg was produced using the in-flight method and implanted into a plastic scintillator surrounded by an array of high-purity germanium detectors used to detect $β$-delayed $γ$ rays. The high-resolution $γ$-ray spectrum was analyzed to measure the $γ$-ray energies and intensities. \textbf{Results}: The intensity of $^{20}$Mg $β$-delayed $γ$ rays through the isobaric analog state in $^{20}$Na was measured to be $(1.60 \pm 0.04_{\textrm{stat}} \pm 0.15_{\textrm{syst}} \pm 0.15_{\textrm{theo}}) \times 10^{-4}$, where the uncertainties are statistical, systematic, and theoretical, respectively. The $Q_{\textrm{EC}}$ value for the superallowed transition was determined to be $4128.7 \pm 2.2$ keV based on the measured excitation energy of $6498.4 \pm 0.2_{\textrm{stat}} \pm 0.4_{\textrm{syst}}$ keV and literature values for the ground-state masses of $^{20}$Na and $^{20}$Mg. \textbf{Conclusions}: The $β$-delayed $γ$-decay branch and $Q_{\textrm{EC}}$ value are now sufficiently precise to match or exceed the sensitivity required for current low-energy tests of the standard model.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of the beta-delayed gamma-ray intensity through the isobaric analog state in 20Na and a new precision QEC value for the superallowed 0+ to 0+ beta decay of 20Mg, but the authors note that the total uncertainty on the ft value is still dominated by the beta-delayed proton branch and half-life, not by the quantities measured here.

## Method

A beam of 20Mg was produced by projectile fragmentation of 170 MeV/u 24Mg on a beryllium target, isolated using the A1900 fragment separator, and implanted into a plastic scintillator surrounded by the Segmented Germanium Array (SeGA) of 16 high-purity germanium detectors. Beta-gamma coincidences were used to remove room background, and gamma-gamma coincidences between different SeGA detectors were used to interpret the decay scheme. The high-resolution gamma-ray spectrum was analyzed to measure gamma-ray energies and intensities.

## Key numbers

- Intensity of 20Mg beta-delayed gamma rays through the isobaric analog state in 20Na: (1.60 ± 0.04_stat ± 0.15_syst ± 0.15_theo) × 10^{-4}
- QEC value for the superallowed transition: 4128.7 ± 2.2 keV
- Excitation energy of the isobaric analog state in 20Na: 6498.4 ± 0.2_stat ± 0.4_syst keV
- Transition energy of the 5514-keV gamma ray: 5514.7 ± 0.2_stat ± 0.4_syst keV
- Transition energy of the 984-keV gamma ray: 983.73 ± 0.00_stat ± 0.10_syst keV
- Experimental ratio of partial widths Gamma_gamma/Gamma_p for the IAS: 0.0063 ± 0.0009
- Weighted average of published beta-delayed proton intensity through the IAS: I_beta_p = (2.54 ± 0.17) × 10^{-2}

## Context

This work extends precision measurements of T=2 superallowed beta decays beyond the most precisely measured case of 32Ar (Bhattacharya et al., 2008) and provides the first measurement of the beta-delayed gamma branch for 20Mg. The results enable tests of isospin symmetry breaking corrections calculated by different nuclear structure methods (Townsend et al., 2010; Hardy and Towner, 2015) and provide a prerequisite QEC value for future measurements of the beta-nu angular correlation to search for scalar currents, as previously done for 32Ar (Adeva et al., 1999; Araujo et al., 2019).
