# Analyzing N-point Energy Correlators Inside Jets with CMS Open Data

> Jets of hadrons produced at high-energy colliders provide experimental access to the dynamics of asymptotically free quarks and gluons and their confinement into hadrons. In this paper, we show that the high energies of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), together with the exceptional resolution of its detectors, allow multipoint correlation functions of energy flow operators to be directly measured within jets for the first time. Using Open Data from the CMS experiment, we show that reformulating jet substructure in terms of these correlators provides new ways of probing the dynamics of QCD jets, which enables direct imaging of the confining transition to free hadrons as well as precision measurements of the scaling properties and interactions of quarks and gluons. This opens a new era in our understanding of jet substructure and illustrates the immense unexploited potential of high-quality LHC data sets for elucidating the dynamics of QCD.

## Key claim

Using CMS Open Data, the authors demonstrate that multipoint energy correlators can be directly measured inside high-energy jets at the LHC for the first time, but they explicitly note that their results are an "analysis" rather than a "measurement" because detector effects have not been corrected.

## Method

The authors extracted jets from CMS 2011A Open Data (anti-\(k_t\) algorithm with \(R=0.5\), \(p_T \in [500,550]\) GeV, \(|\eta| < 1.9\)) and computed projected \(N\)-point energy correlators (ENCs) by integrating over the shape of the correlator while fixing the largest angular separation \(R_L\). They used charged hadron subtraction to mitigate pileup and restricted to particle flow candidates with \(p_T > 1\) GeV to minimize acceptance effects, focusing primarily on track-based observables.

## Key numbers

- Jets have transverse momenta \(p_T \in [500,550]\) GeV and pseudo-rapidity \(|\eta| < 1.9\), clustered with anti-\(k_t\) algorithm with \(R = 0.5\).
- The two-point correlator shows a clear break in scaling behavior at \(R_L \sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/p_T^{\text{jet}} \sim 10^{-2}\), corresponding to the confinement transition.
- Below the confinement scale, the two-point correlator exhibits \(R_L d\sigma/d R_L \propto R_L^2\) scaling, corresponding to uniformly distributed hadrons.
- The perturbative scaling regime extends for over a decade in \(R_L\) for \(R_L \gtrsim 0.5\) down to \(R_L \sim 10^{-2}\).
- The three-point correlator shape dependence is shown at a fixed slice \(R_L \sim 0.25\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical program initiated by Chen et al. (2020) to reformulate jet substructure in terms of energy correlators, building on earlier conformal field theory work by Hofman and Maldacena (2008) and Belitsky et al. (2013-2014). It enables direct imaging of the confinement transition in QCD jets using LHC data, complementary to the recent Lund plane measurement from ATLAS (2020), and opens the door to precision measurements of the strong coupling \(\alpha_s\) and tests of parton shower implementations such as Pythia 8.226.
