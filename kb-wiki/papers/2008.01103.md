# Effects of density-dependent scenarios of in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions in heavy-ion collisions

> Using a more reasonable separate density-dependent scenario instead of the total density-dependent scenario for in-medium $nn$, $pp$ and $np$ interactions, we examine effects of differences of in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions in two density-dependent scenarios on isospin-sensitive observables in central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. Moreover, to more physically detect the differences between the nucleon-nucleon interactions in two density-dependent scenarios, we also map the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the separate density-dependent scenario into that in the total density-dependent scenario through fitting the identical constraints for symmetric nuclear matter as well as the identical slope parameter of nuclear symmetry energy at the saturation density. It is shown that two density-dependent scenarios also lead to essentially different symmetry potentials especially at high densities although they can lead to the identical equation of state for the symmetry nuclear matter as well as the identical symmetry energy for the isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. Consequently, these isospin-sensitive observables are also appreciably affected by the different density-dependent scenarios of in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions. Therefore, according to these findings, it is suggested that effects of the separate density-dependent scenario of in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions should be taken into account when probing the high-density symmetry energy using these isospin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions.

# Effects of density-dependent scenarios of in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The separate density-dependent scenario for in-medium nn, pp, and np interactions (where the interaction depends on the densities of the two individual nucleons) leads to essentially different symmetry potentials compared to the total density-dependent scenario (where the interaction depends on the total density at the two-nucleon center), even when both scenarios are constrained to produce identical symmetric nuclear matter properties and identical symmetry energy at saturation density. This means that isospin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions are appreciably affected by which density-dependent scenario is chosen, independent of the symmetry energy setting.

## Method
The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with the momentum-dependent interaction (MDI) single-nucleon potential for the total density-dependent scenario and the improved MDI (IMDI) single-nucleon potential for the separate density-dependent scenario, both derived from Gogny effective interactions. They performed simulations of central 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, and mapped the IMDI interaction parameters to produce identical symmetry energy at saturation density as the MDI interaction, isolating the effects of the density-dependent scenario from the effects of different symmetry energy settings.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for central 197Au+197Au collisions
- Saturation density: ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: Esym(ρ0) = 30.5 MeV
- Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: E0(ρ0) = -16 MeV
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K0 = 212 MeV
- Mapped parameters for IMDI: x = -0.5874 (to match MDI with x = -1) and x = 0.2063 (to match MDI with x = 0)
- Free neutrons and protons defined as those with local densities less than ρ0/8

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Xu et al. (2010) and Chen et al. (2014) who examined effects of separate density dependence on symmetry potential and energy using Gogny effective interactions, but did not study heavy-ion collision observables. It also builds on the Brueckner theory framework (Brueckner, 1964; Dabrowski, 1973, 1974) which showed that the G-matrix of nucleon-nucleon interactions depends strongly on the respective Fermi momenta of neutrons and protons. The results suggest that future probes of high-density symmetry energy using isospin-sensitive observables such as free neutron-proton ratios and π-/π+ ratios should account for the separate density-dependent scenario, and that double ratios from two reaction systems (as proposed by Li et al., 2006 and Wei et al., 2019) could better discriminate between the two scenarios.
