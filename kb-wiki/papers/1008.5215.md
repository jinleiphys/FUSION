# Mini review of Poincaré invariant quantum theory

> We review the construction and applications of exactly Poincaré invariant quantum mechanical models of few-degree of freedom systems. We discuss the construction of dynamical representations of the Poincaré group on few-particle Hilbert spaces, the relation to quantum field theory, the formulation of cluster properties, and practical considerations related to the construction of realistic interactions and the solution of the dynamical equations. Selected applications illustrate the utility of this approach.

## Key claim

Exactly Poincaré invariant quantum mechanical models of few-degree-of-freedom systems can be constructed that share most axiomatic properties of quantum field theory except microscopic locality, and these models yield identical bound-state and scattering observables regardless of which kinematic subgroup (instant, point, or front form) is chosen for the dynamical representation.

## Method

The authors construct dynamical unitary representations of the Poincaré group on few-particle Hilbert spaces by adding interactions to the mass Casimir operator of non-interacting irreducible representations built from tensor products of single-particle irreducible representations. They formulate the two-body problem by diagonalizing the dynamical mass operator in a non-interacting irreducible basis, and extend this to three-body systems using scattering-equivalence transformations to restore the strong form of cluster properties while preserving the S-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The two-body invariant mass has a continuous spectrum starting from \(m_1 + m_2\) (the sum of the two particle masses).
- Single-particle Hilbert spaces are of the form \(\mathcal{H}_{mj} = L^2(\mathbb{R}^3) \otimes \mathbb{C}^{2j+1}\), where the spin dimension is \(2j+1\).
- The spectrum of a component of the spin vector takes discrete values in integer steps from \(-j\) to \(j\).
- The three-body mass operator is constructed as \(M = M_{(12)(3)} + M_{(23)(1)} + M_{(31)(2)} - 2M_0\), where each term is a \(2+1\)-body mass operator.

## Context

This mini review extends the foundational work of Wigner (1939) on unitary ray representations of the Poincaré group, Dirac (1949) on kinematic sub-algebras, and Bakamjian and Thomas (1953) on constructing the Poincaré Lie algebra for two interacting particles. It builds on the N-particle constructions of Coester and Polyzou (1982) and the three-nucleon bound state calculations of Glöckle, Coester and Lee (1986). The approach enables exact numerical calculations of model predictions at the few-GeV scale where perturbative QCD methods are not applicable, and provides a framework for computing few-nucleon observables including reactions with particle production.
