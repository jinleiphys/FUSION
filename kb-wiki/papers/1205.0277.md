# Three particles in a finite volume: The breakdown of spherical symmetry

> Lattice simulations of light nuclei necessarily take place in finite volumes, thus affecting their infrared properties. These effects can be addressed in a model-independent manner using Effective Field Theories. We study the model case of three identical bosons (mass m) with resonant two-body interactions in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, which can also be generalized to the three-nucleon system in a straightforward manner. Our results allow for the removal of finite volume effects from lattice results as well as the determination of infinite volume scattering parameters from the volume dependence of the spectrum. We study the volume dependence of several states below the break-up threshold, spanning one order of magnitude in the binding energy in the infinite volume, for box side lengths L between the two-body scattering length a and L = 0.25a. For example, a state with a three-body energy of -3/(ma^2) in the infinite volume has been shifted to -10/(ma^2) at L = a. Special emphasis is put on the consequences of the breakdown of spherical symmetry and several ways to perturbatively treat the ensuing partial wave admixtures. We find their contributions to be on the sub-percent level compared to the strong volume dependence of the S-wave component. For shallow bound states, we find a transition to boson-diboson scattering behavior when decreasing the size of the finite volume.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the finite-volume energy shift of three-body bound states in a cubic box is dominated by the S-wave component, with higher partial wave admixtures (from the breakdown of spherical symmetry) contributing at the sub-percent level, but only for box side lengths L between the two-body scattering length a and L = 0.25a, and for states with binding energies spanning one order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors use pionless Effective Field Theory for three identical bosons with resonant two-body interactions in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. They derive a homogeneous sum equation for the boson-diboson amplitude in finite volume, solve it numerically using two approaches (a Grid approach using discrete momenta and a Sum approach expanding in kubic harmonics), and develop two perturbative methods (partial resummation and strict perturbation theory) to treat the admixture of higher partial waves arising from the cubic symmetry.

## Key numbers

- A state with infinite-volume three-body energy of -3/(ma^2) is shifted to -10/(ma^2) at box side length L = a.
- The study covers box side lengths L between the two-body scattering length a and L = 0.25a.
- Higher partial wave contributions are found to be on the sub-percent level compared to the S-wave component.
- The ratio of Legendre functions Q_4(z)/Q_0(z) scales as z^{-4} for large arguments, giving a suppression factor of approximately (2π/(ΛL))^4 for the G-wave correction.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Kreuzer (2008, 2009, 2010) on three-body finite-volume effects, moving beyond bound-state calculations to access energies above the boson-diboson break-up threshold. It generalizes Lüscher's two-particle finite-volume formalism (Lüscher, 1990, 1991) to the three-body sector, enabling the removal of finite-volume effects from lattice QCD results for light nuclei and the extraction of infinite-volume scattering parameters. The approach can be straightforwardly generalized to the three-nucleon system in pionless EFT, which is relevant for interpreting lattice QCD calculations of the triton (Beane, 2009).
