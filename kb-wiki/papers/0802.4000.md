# Properties of $J/ψ$ at $T_c$: QCD second-order Stark effect

> Starting from the temperature dependence of the energy density and pressure from lattice QCD calculation, we extract the temperature dependence of the electric and magnetic condensate near $T_c$. While the magnetic condensate hardly changes across $T_c$, we find that the electric condensate increases abruptly above $T_c$. This induces a small but an equally abrupt decrease in the mass of $J/ψ$, can be calculated through the second-order Stark effect. Combining the present result with the previously determined QCD sum rule constraint, we extract the thermal width of $J/ψ$ above $T_c$, which also increases fast. These changes can be identified as the critical behavior of $J/ψ$ across $T_c$ associated with the phase transition. We find that the mass shift and with broadening of $J/ψ$ at 1.05 $T_c$ will be around -100 MeV and 100 MeV respectively.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the critical behavior of the QCD phase transition can be identified with a sudden increase in the electric gluon condensate near T_c, and that this translates into an abrupt but small decrease in the J/psi mass (around -100 MeV at 1.05 T_c) via the QCD second-order Stark effect, rather than a sudden disappearance of the J/psi peak.

## Method

The authors extract the temperature dependence of the electric and magnetic gluon condensates from lattice QCD data for the energy density and pressure in pure SU(3) gauge theory (Boyd 1996), using the energy-momentum tensor decomposition and the trace anomaly. They then apply the QCD second-order Stark effect formula, originally developed by Peskin (1979) for heavy quarkonium in nuclear matter, to compute the J/psi mass shift from the change in the electric condensate. Finally, they combine this mass shift with a previously determined QCD sum rule constraint (Morita and Lee 2008) to extract the thermal width of J/psi above T_c.

## Key numbers

- Mass shift of J/psi at T_c: around -50 MeV.
- Mass shift of J/psi at 1.05 T_c: around -100 MeV.
- Thermal width of J/psi at 1.05 T_c: around 100 MeV.
- Charm quark mass used in the Stark effect calculation: m_c = 1704 MeV.
- Bohr radius used for the Coulomb wave function: a = 0.271 fm.
- Running coupling constant used: alpha_s = 0.57.
- The QCD sum rule constraint from Morita and Lee (2008) gives -Delta m + Gamma_T approximately equal to 80 + 17(T - T_c) MeV in the range from T_c to 1.05 T_c.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Morita and Lee (2008), which could only provide a combined constraint on mass shift and width, by using the second-order Stark effect to separately determine the mass shift from lattice data. It contradicts the earlier expectation that J/psi disappears suddenly at T_c (Matsui and Satz 1986) and instead supports lattice findings using maximum entropy methods (Hatsuda 2004, Datta 2004) that the J/psi peak survives up to nearly 2 T_c. The results enable future experimental searches for the J/psi mass shift at RHIC upgrades and LHC, where the mass resolution for the dimuon channel is 35 MeV for CMS, around 70 MeV for ALICE and ATLAS, and potentially better for the dielectron channel.
