# Shell model studies of neutron rich nuclei

> We discuss the present status of the description of the structure of the very neutron rich nuclei, in the framework of modern large scale shell model calculations. Particular attention is paid to the interaction related issues, as well as to the problems of the shell model approach at the neutron drip line. We present detailed results for nuclei around N=20 and, more briefly, we discuss some salient features of the regions close to N=8, 28 and 40. We show that most experimental features can be understood in a shell model context.

## Key claim

Most experimental features of very neutron-rich nuclei can be understood in a shell model context, but the predictive power is seriously hindered by the lack of an "universal" shell model interaction, and the correctness of the monopole hamiltonian is crucial for success.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed in valence spaces spanning two contiguous major shells (e.g., sd-pf) to describe neutron-rich nuclei near N=8, 20, 28, and 40. The effective interaction was adjusted using experimental data, such as the excitation energy of the 3/2- state in 35Si measured at ISOLDE, to fix monopole terms of the cross-shell interaction. Results for normal (0p-0h) and intruder (np-nh) configurations were compared to experimental data on excitation energies, B(E2) values, and spectroscopic quadrupole moments.

## Key numbers

- In 11Be, the expected 0ħω normal state lies at 300 keV above the intruder ground state; the p-sd gap is reduced but still 4 MeV wide.
- The N=20 quasiparticle gap g decreases from 7 MeV in 40Ca to 5.6 MeV in 36S, 5.1 MeV in 34Si, and 3.6 MeV in 32Mg (experimental).
- In 32Mg, the 2p-2h intruder lies 1.4 MeV below the normal closed-shell configuration; the calculated 2+ excitation energy is 0.93 MeV (experiment: 0.89 MeV) and B(E2; 2+→0+) is 98 e²fm⁴ (experiment: 90(16) e²fm⁴).
- In 34Mg, the normal state is 1.1 MeV below the intruder; the 2+ excitation energy for the normal configuration is 1.09 MeV and for the intruder 0.66 MeV.
- In 34Si, the intruder 0+ state appears at 1.7 MeV (experiment: 2.1 MeV) and the 2+ at 3.0 MeV (experiment: 3.3 MeV).
- In 31Na, the intruder lies 1.6 MeV below the normal configuration; the ground state spin is 3/2+ and the 5/2+ state is at 0.28 MeV (experiment: 0.35(2) MeV).
- The 1d3/2–fp gap in 34Si is 4.7 MeV in this work, compared to 4.4 MeV in the QMCD calculation; for 28O the values are 3.4 MeV and 1.2 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the shell model description of neutron-rich nuclei pioneered by Wildenthal, Curtin and Brown (1983) using the USD interaction, and by earlier calculations of Wildenthal and Chung (1980) that first identified anomalous behavior in 31Na. The paper provides a systematic survey of the "island of inversion" around N=20, confirming that only Ne, Na, and Mg belong to it while F and Al sit at its edge. The results enable future experimental tests of shape coexistence and shell erosion at N=28 and N=40, and highlight the need for better experimental data on simple exotic nuclei to constrain the monopole hamiltonian.
