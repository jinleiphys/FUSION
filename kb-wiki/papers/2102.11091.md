# Confinement-Deconfinement transition and $Z_2$ symmetry in $Z_2+$Higgs theory

> We study the Polyakov loop and the $Z_2$ symmetry in the lattice $Z_2+$Higgs theory in 4D Euclidean space using Monte Carlo simulations. The results show that this symmetry is realised in the Higgs symmetric phase for large number of temporal lattice sites. To understand the dependence on the number of temporal sites, we consider a one dimensional model by keeping terms of the original action corresponding to a single spatial site. In this approximation the partition function can be calculated exactly as a function of the Polyakov loop. The resulting free energy is found to have the $Z_2$ symmetry in the limit of large temporal sites. We argue that this is due to $Z_2$ invariance as well as dominance of the distribution or density of states corresponding to the action.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $Z_2$ symmetry of the Polyakov loop, which is explicitly broken by the Higgs field in the action, can be effectively restored in the Higgs symmetric phase for sufficiently large temporal lattice size $N_\tau$ because the density of states (entropy) dominates over the Boltzmann factor that breaks the symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the lattice $Z_2+$Higgs theory in four-dimensional Euclidean space using the Metropolis algorithm, with lattice sizes ranging from $N_\tau=4$ to $24$ and spatial extent $N_s$ such that $N_s/N_\tau = 4$. They computed the Polyakov loop distributions, the thermal average of the temporal gauge-Higgs interaction term $sk_4$, and its susceptibility across the confinement-deconfinement transition and the Higgs transition. To understand the $N_\tau$ dependence, they constructed a $0+1$ dimensional model retaining only the temporal component of the gauge-Higgs interaction for a single spatial site, for which the partition function and density of states can be calculated exactly.

## Key numbers

* The pure gauge ($\kappa=0$) confinement-deconfinement transition occurs at critical gauge coupling $\beta_g \sim 0.4407$, as determined by duality transformations in previous work (Balian 1974).
* For $N_\tau=2$ at $\kappa=0.13$, the Polyakov loop histograms in the deconfined phase show no $Z_2$ symmetry, with two clearly separated peaks for the $L>0$ and $L<0$ sectors.
* For $N_\tau=8$ at $\kappa=0.13$, the Polyakov loop histograms for the two $Z_2$ sectors agree well with each other in both confined and deconfined phases.
* For $N_\tau=24$ at $\beta_g=0.435$, the two Polyakov loop sectors agree in the average $\langle sk_4 \rangle$ and susceptibility $\chi_{sk_4}$ up to the Higgs crossover point $\kappa<\kappa_c$.
* In the $0+1$ dimensional model, the density of states $\rho(sk_4)$ for both $L=\pm 1$ is well described by a Gaussian centered at $sk_4=0$ with standard deviation $\sqrt{N_\tau}$ for large $N_\tau$.

## Context

This work extends previous non-perturbative Monte Carlo studies of $SU(2)+$Higgs theory by Biswal (2015), which found that the $Z_2$ symmetry of the Polyakov loop is realised in the Higgs symmetric phase for large $N_\tau$. The paper uses the simpler $Z_2+$Higgs theory, which has been extensively studied by Balian (1974), Creutz (1979), and Jongeward (1980), as a tractable model to understand the mechanism of symmetry restoration. The results enable a deeper understanding of how the density of states can overcome explicit symmetry breaking in gauge-Higgs systems, with implications for the confinement-deconfinement transition in $SU(N)$ gauge theories with matter fields.
