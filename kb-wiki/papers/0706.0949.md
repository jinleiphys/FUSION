# Microscopic calculation of half lives of spherical proton emitters

> Half life values for proton radioactivity in nuclei have been calculated in the WKB approximation. The microscopic proton-nucleus potential has been obtained by folding the densities of daughter nuclei with two microscopic NN interactions, DDM3Y and JLM. The densities have been obtained in the Relativistic Mean Field approach in the spherical approximation using the force FSU Gold. No substantial modification of results has been observed if other common forces are employed. The calculated results for the decays from the ground state or the low-lying excited states in almost all the nuclei agree well with experimental measurements. Reasons for large deviations in a few cases have been discussed. Results in $^{109}$I and $^{112,113}$Cs show that the effect of deformation is small contrary to earlier calculations. Predictions for possible proton radioactivity have been made in two nuclei, $^{93}$Ag and $^{97}$In.

## Key claim

The authors calculate proton-emission half-lives using a fully microscopic proton-nucleus potential (obtained by folding RMF densities with DDM3Y or JLM interactions) and find that, contrary to earlier calculations using Woods-Saxon potentials, the effect of deformation is small for the decays of ¹⁰⁹I and ¹¹²,¹¹³Cs, and that the data can be reproduced without invoking deformation.

## Method

The proton-nucleus nuclear potential was constructed by folding the density of the daughter nucleus (obtained from a Relativistic Mean Field calculation in the spherical approximation using the FSU Gold force) with two microscopic nucleon-nucleon interactions: the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) interaction and the Jeukenne–Lejeune–Mahaux (JLM) interaction. The Coulomb potential was similarly obtained by folding the Coulomb interaction with the microscopic proton density. Half-lives were then calculated in the WKB approximation, with the assault frequency derived from the Q-value following the prescription of Poenaru et al. for cluster radioactivity extended to protons; no normalization factor was applied to the nuclear potential.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁰⁵Sb, the JLM interaction gives a half-life substantially different from the DDM3Y result, attributed to the very low Q-value of the decay; in all other cases with Q-values close to or greater than 1 MeV, the results from the two interactions are very close.
- For ¹⁸⁵Bi, the calculated admixture fraction of the 2p-2h state in the daughter ground state is ~0.08, consistent with values ≤0.09 from calculations in ¹⁸⁶,¹⁸⁸Pb.
- For the hypothetical decay from the 9/2⁻ state in ¹⁸⁵Bi (assuming the same Q-value as the observed 1/2⁺ decay), the predicted half-life is 26 ms (with l=5).
- For ¹⁸⁷Bi, the calculated half-life for proton radioactivity from the 1/2⁺ excited state (assuming a 9% 2p-2h admixture in the daughter ground state) is 10.9 seconds.
- For ⁸⁹Rh and ⁹³Ag, the lower limit of the experimental half-life is 1.5 μs; for ⁹⁷In, the half-life from systematics is 5 ms.

## Context

This work extends the single-folding model approach of Basu et al. (2005) by replacing the phenomenological daughter-nucleus densities with densities from a Relativistic Mean Field calculation (FSU Gold force), and by testing two microscopic NN interactions (DDM3Y and JLM). It contradicts the earlier conclusion of Maglione et al. (1998) that a deformation of β ~ 0.15 is essential to reproduce the half-lives of ¹⁰⁹I and ¹¹²,¹¹³Cs; the present calculation reproduces the ¹⁰⁹I data with considerable accuracy without deformation. The results enable predictions for possible proton radioactivity in ⁹³Ag and ⁹⁷In, and suggest that the failure of earlier Woods-Saxon calculations may be due to deficiencies of that potential far from stability rather than to deformation.
