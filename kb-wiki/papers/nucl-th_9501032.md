# Universality of Symmetry and Mixed-symmetry Collective Nuclear States

> The global correlation in the observed variation with mass number of the $E2$ and summed $M1$ transition strengths is examined for rare earth nuclei. It is shown that a theory of correlated $S$ and $D$ fermion pairs with a simple pairing plus quadrupole interaction leads naturally to this universality. Thus a unified and quantitative description emerges for low-lying quadrupole and dipole strengths.

## Key claim

The observed approximate universal behavior of $E2$ and summed $M1$ transition strengths across rare-earth nuclei can be reproduced quantitatively by the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (FDSM) using a single Hamiltonian with a simple pairing plus quadrupole interaction, without adjusting parameters to the electromagnetic strengths themselves.

## Method

The authors employed the FDSM, an $SO(8)$ or $Sp(6)$ truncation of the shell model built from correlated $S$ and $D$ fermion pairs, with a 5-parameter Hamiltonian containing proton and neutron pairing terms and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. They fitted the Hamiltonian parameters to reproduce experimental spectra (including $2^+_1$, $4^+_1$, $6^+_1$, $1^+$, $2^+_2$, $0^+_2$ states) for Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy, and Er isotopes using a gradient search, then computed $B(E2)$ and summed $B(M1)$ transition strengths from the resulting wavefunctions with fixed effective charges and g-factors.

## Key numbers

- Effective charges fixed globally at $e_\pi = 0.24$ eb (protons) and $e_\nu = 0.20$ eb (neutrons) for all rare-earth nuclei examined
- g-factors taken as $g_\pi = 1.0$ $\mu_N$ and $g_\nu = 0$ for all nuclei examined
- Fixed parameter set for all nuclei: $G'_{0\pi} = -0.074$ MeV, $G'_{0\nu} = 0.020$ MeV, $B'_{2\pi} = -0.001$ MeV, $B'_{2\nu} = 0.047$ MeV, $B_{2\pi\nu} = -0.243$ MeV
- The $M1$ saturation occurs at least 1 unit of $P$ (where $P \equiv N_p N_n/(N_p+N_n)$) lower than that for the $E2$ strength, which never completely saturates

## Context

This work extends the observation by Rangacharyulu et al. (1991) of a universal correlation between $E2$ and $M1$ strengths in rare-earth nuclei, and addresses the difficulty noted by those authors for the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-2) with $F$-spin symmetry to account for this universality. The FDSM approach contrasts with the IBM-2, where the $Q_\pi \cdot Q_\nu$ and Majorana interactions are separately fitted to states with different collective behaviors, whereas the fermion model describes all states with a single Hamiltonian. The results enable further investigation of whether other fermion models can accommodate this universality, and suggest that adjusting the effective $d$-boson number might allow an IBM-2 description of these systematics.
