# New parametrization for the nuclear covariant energy density functional with point-coupling interaction

> A new parametrization PC-PK1 for the nuclear covariant energy density functional with nonlinear point-coupling interaction is proposed by fitting to observables for 60 selected spherical nuclei, including the binding energies, charge radii and empirical pairing gaps. The success of PC-PK1 is illustrated in its description for infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei including the ground-state and low-lying excited states. Particularly, PC-PK1 improves the description for isospin dependence of binding energy along either the isotopic or the isotonic chains, which makes it more reliable for application in exotic nuclei. The predictive power of PC-PK1 is also illustrated for the nuclear low-lying excitation states in a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian in which the parameters are determined by constrained calculations for triaxial shapes.

## Key claim
The new parametrization PC-PK1 for the nuclear covariant energy density functional with point-coupling interaction improves the description of isospin dependence of binding energy along isotopic and isotonic chains compared to previous parametrizations, but the abstract's claim of reliability for exotic nuclei is hedged by the fact that the fit uses only 60 selected spherical nuclei and the predictive power for deformed nuclei requires additional rotational energy corrections.

## Method
The authors performed a multiparameter fit using the Levenberg-Marquardt method to determine nine coupling constants and pairing strength parameters by minimizing the square deviation between calculated and experimental binding energies, charge radii, and empirical pairing gaps for 60 selected spherical nuclei. The Dirac equation for nucleons was solved in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 20 major oscillator shells for spherical calculations and 16 shells for axially deformed cases, with pairing correlations treated by the BCS method using a density-independent delta-force.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16.12 MeV, with saturation density 0.154 fm^-3, compared to empirical values of -16±1 MeV and 0.166±0.018 fm^-3
- Incompressibility K0 = 238 MeV for PC-PK1
- Dirac mass M*_D/M between 0.55 and 0.60 for all effective interactions, consistent with spin-orbit splitting data
- PC-PK1 reproduces experimental binding energies within 1 MeV for Ca isotopes and within 2 MeV for Sn and Pb isotopes
- For Ni isotopes, even-even 58-64Ni show underestimation of 2.4-5.2 MeV
- Deviations within 1 MeV for N=82 isotones and within 2 MeV for N=20 and N=50 isotones
- Charge radii of Sn isotopes reproduced within 0.3% by all effective interactions
- PC-PK1 symmetry energy at saturation is 11% above the empirical value of approximately 32 MeV, compared to 16-18% for PC-F1, PC-LA, NL3*, and PK1

## Context
This work extends the relativistic point-coupling model parametrizations PC-LA (Nikolaus 1992) and PC-F1 (Burvenich 2002), which showed deficiencies in isospin dependence of binding energy, and the density-dependent parametrization DD-PC1 (Niksic 2008), which had larger deviations for spherical nuclei. The new PC-PK1 parametrization enables more reliable predictions for exotic nuclei near the drip lines and provides input for beyond-mean-field calculations of low-lying collective excited states using a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian.
