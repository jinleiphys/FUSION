# How Large is the "Natural" Magnetic Moment?

> The "natural" magntic moment of a particle of spin S is generally assumed to be that given by the Belinfante conjecture and has the value g=1/S for its gyromagnetic ratio. Thus, for the spin 1/2 electron we find the Dirac value $g_e=2$. However, in the standard model the charged W boson, a spin one particle, is found to have the value $g_{W^+}=2$. We show how this result comes about and argue that the "natural" value for any particle of spin S should be g=2, independent of spin.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "natural" gyromagnetic ratio for any fundamental particle of spin S should be g=2, independent of spin, contradicting the long-standing Belinfante conjecture which predicted g=1/S. The author acknowledges that this claim is supported primarily by theoretical arguments rather than experimental evidence, since only the electron (spin 1/2) and W boson (spin 1) exist as charged fundamental particles without strong interactions.

## Method

The author reviews the Belinfante conjecture and its derivation via minimal substitution for spin 1/2, spin 0, and spin 1 systems using both Lagrangian methods and nonrelativistic reduction. He then demonstrates that the charged W boson in the standard electroweak theory acquires an additional contribution to its magnetic moment from the Yang-Mills gauge interaction term in the SU(2) field tensor, raising its g-factor from the Belinfante value of 1 to the standard model value of 2. Three theoretical arguments are presented to generalize this result to arbitrary spin: unitarity constraints on Compton scattering at high energy, the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, and factorization of the graviton scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For the electron (spin 1/2), the Dirac value is g_e = 2, with small O(alpha) corrections from photon loops.
- The Belinfante conjecture predicts g = 1/S, giving g = 1 for spin 1 particles.
- The standard model predicts g_{W^+} = 2 for the charged W boson, confirmed experimentally as g_{W^pm} = 2.20 +/- 0.20.
- The proton has g_p = 5.58 and the neutron has g_n = -3.82, but these are composite systems with strong interaction corrections.
- The Compton scattering amplitude contains terms proportional to 1/m^2 that violate unitarity at photon energy omega_i ~ m unless g = 2.

## Context

This work extends the debate between the Belinfante conjecture (Belinfante, 1953; Case, 1955; Hagen and Hurley) and the alternative proposal that g=2 for all spins, which has been supported by Ferrara, Porrati, and Telegdi through Compton scattering unitarity arguments. The paper connects to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule (originally derived for nucleons) and its generalization by Weinberg, as well as to string theory results showing g=2 for all spins. The argument enables a unified understanding of magnetic moments across all fundamental particles and resolves the apparent contradiction between the Belinfante prediction and the observed W boson magnetic moment.
