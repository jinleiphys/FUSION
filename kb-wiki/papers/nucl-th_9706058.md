# New Source Term for QGP Formation in the Background-Field Model

> We consider pair production in a space-time-dependent background field and derive a source term, i.e., production rate in the one-particle phase space. Such a source term is required in Boltzmann-equation-based models of quark-gluon plasma formation and evolution. We compare the source term derived here with the one that has been used in the literature so far. Significant differences are observed.

# New Source Term for QGP Formation in the Background-Field Model

## Key claim

The authors derive a new source term for parton pair production in a space-time-dependent background electric field for use in Boltzmann-equation-based models of quark-gluon plasma formation, arguing that the previously used source term (which replaces the constant field in Schwinger's formula with a time-dependent field) is invalid because a time-dependent field can directly excite negative-energy particles across the mass gap by a perturbative mechanism, without tunneling. The new source term differs significantly from the old one, particularly in that it depends on the instantaneous field strength squared rather than linearly on the field, and contains a nonlocal dependence on field derivatives absent in the earlier formulation.

## Method

The authors compute the lowest-order perturbative amplitude for fermion pair production in a space-time-dependent background field (Fig. 1b), using the Fourier transform of the field and working in a gauge where the electric field is along the collision axis. They assume the electric field depends only on the boost-invariant proper time τ and takes an exponential form E(τ) = E₀ exp(-τ/τ₀), perform the integrations analytically, and use an integral representation to recover dependence on the instantaneous field strength |E(τ)|². The resulting production rate in one-particle phase space is obtained by identifying spatial and momentum rapidities via a Thomas-Fermi-like approximation.

## Key numbers

- Input parameters for numerical comparisons: particle mass m = 5 MeV, τ₀ = 0.1 fm, fine-structure constant α = 0.2, initial energy density ½E₀² = 10 GeV/fm³
- The new source term behaves as ∼ E² in both the small-E and large-E limits, while the old source term vanishes with all derivatives as E → 0 and behaves as ∼ E ln(E) in the large-E limit
- The ratio of new to old source terms increases with the strength of the electric field

## Context

This work extends the background-field model approach to QGP formation, which was developed by Kajantie and Matsui (1985), Gatoff, Kerman, and Matsui (1987), Bialas et al. (1988), Banerjee, Bhalerao, and Ravishankar (1989), Asakawa and Matsui (1991), and Eisenberg (1996). These models use Schwinger's (1951) nonperturbative pair production formula for a constant electric field, with the source term in the form given by Casher, Neuberger, and Nussinov (1979). The authors explicitly contradict the validity of replacing the constant field in that source term with a time-dependent field, which had been the standard practice. The new source term enables more accurate Boltzmann-equation simulations of QGP formation and evolution, and the authors note that extension to coloured quark and antiquark production and to gluon production in a space-time-dependent colour field is in progress (Nayak and Ravishankar, in preparation).
