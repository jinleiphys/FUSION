# Dependence of hadronic properties on Quark Masses and Constraints on their Cosmological Variation

> We follow our previous paper on possible cosmological variation of weak scale (quark masses) and strong scale, inspired by data on cosmological variation of the electromagnetic fine structure constant from distant quasar (QSO) absorption spectra. In this work we identify the {\em strange quark mass} $m_s$ as the most important quantity, and the {\em sigma meson mass} as the ingredient of the nuclear forces most sensitive to it. As a result, we claim significantly stronger limits on ratio of weak/strong scale ($W=m_s/Λ_{QCD}$) variation following from our previous discussion of primordial Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis ($|δW/W|<0.006$) and Oklo natural nuclear reactor ($|δW/W|<1.2 \cdot 10^{-10}$; there is also a non-zero solution $δW/W=(-0.56 \pm 0.05) \cdot 10^{-9}$) .

# Dependence of hadronic properties on Quark Masses and Constraints on their Cosmological Variation

## Key claim

The strange quark mass $m_s$ is the most important quark mass for hadronic and nuclear physics, and the sigma meson ($f_0(600)$) mass is the ingredient of nuclear forces most sensitive to it; this yields significantly stronger limits on variation of the ratio $W = m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}$ than previously obtained, with the caveat that the Oklo data also permit a non-zero solution for the variation.

## Method

The authors use the Walecka model of nuclear forces, which expresses the nucleon-nucleon potential as the difference between a scalar (sigma) exchange and a vector (omega) exchange term. They compute the sensitivity of the sigma meson mass to the strange quark mass by summing three contributions: the strange sea (common to all hadrons), the valence strangeness from the sigma being an approximate SU(3) singlet, and the mixing of the sigma with $\bar K K$ and $\eta\eta$ continuum states. They then differentiate the deuteron binding energy and the energy of neutron resonances (in Oklo and in $^5$He) with respect to the sigma, omega, and nucleon masses, and convert those derivatives into constraints on $\delta(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})$.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson mass sensitivity to strange quark mass: $\delta m_\sigma / m_\sigma \approx 0.54 \, (\delta m_s / m_s)$, compared to $\delta m_N / m_N \approx 0.19 \, (\delta m_s / m_s)$ for the nucleon.
- Deuteron binding energy sensitivity: $\delta Q_d / Q_d \approx -17 \, (\delta m_s / m_s)$ from summing sigma, omega, and nucleon contributions.
- Limit from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis: $|\delta (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) / (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})| < 0.006$.
- Limit from Oklo natural nuclear reactor: $|\delta (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) / (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})| < 1.2 \times 10^{-10}$; non-zero solution: $\delta (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) / (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) = (-0.56 \pm 0.05) \times 10^{-9}$.
- Rough limit from $^{12}$C resonance in stellar production: $|\delta (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) / (m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})| < 0.001$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (Flambaum and Shuryak, 2002) on cosmological variation of the weak scale, which was inspired by data suggesting variation of the electromagnetic fine structure constant from quasar absorption spectra (Webb et al., 2001). It identifies the sigma meson as the key hadron linking strange quark mass variation to nuclear observables, going beyond earlier studies that focused on pion contributions from light quark masses (Flambaum and Shuryak, 2002; Dmitriev et al., 2002; Beane and Savage, 2002). The derived limits on $m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}$ variation are orders of magnitude stronger than previous constraints and can be applied to future searches for time variation of fundamental constants, as well as to studies of the parameter space for "alternative Universes."
