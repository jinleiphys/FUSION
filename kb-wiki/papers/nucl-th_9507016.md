# Compressional properties of nuclear matter in the relativistic mean field theory with the excluded volume effects

> Compressional properties of nuclear matter are studied by using the mean field theory with the excluded volume effects of the nucleons. It is found that the excluded volume effects make it possible to fit the empirical data of the Coulomb coefficient $K_{c}$ of nucleus incompressibility, even if the volume coefficient $K$ is small($\sim 150$MeV). However, the symmetry properties favor $K=300\pm 50$MeV as in the cases of the mean field theory of point-like nucleons.

## Key claim

The excluded volume effects (EVE) of nucleons make it possible to fit the empirical Coulomb coefficient \(K_c\) of nucleus incompressibility even when the volume coefficient \(K\) is as low as \(\sim 150\) MeV, but the symmetry properties (specifically the volume-symmetry coefficient \(K_{vs}\)) still favor \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV, as in the mean field theory of point-like nucleons.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic mean field theory with the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model including nonlinear \(\sigma\) terms, and incorporate the excluded volume effects of nucleons by reducing the available configurational volume from \(V\) to \(V - N V_n\), where \(V_n\) is the nucleon volume. They fix the nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV, normal density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), and binding energy \(a_1 = 16\) MeV, then determine the remaining five parameters (nucleon radius \(R_n\), couplings \(C_s\), \(C_v\), and nonlinear coefficients \(B\), \(C\)) by imposing saturation conditions and given values of effective mass \(M^*_0\), incompressibility \(K\), and skewness \(K'\). The Coulomb coefficient \(K_c\) is computed via the scaling model relation \(K_c = -\frac{3 q_{el}^2}{5 R_0} \left( \frac{9 K'}{K} + 8 \right)\), and the volume-symmetry coefficient \(K_{vs}\) is calculated after adding standard \(\rho\)-meson terms.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\); nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV; binding energy \(a_1 = 16\) MeV.
- For \(K = 150\) MeV, parameter sets reproducing empirical \(K_c\) exist only for nucleon radius \(R_n = 0.81\)–\(0.84\) fm (EOS10) and \(R_n = 0.76\)–\(0.87\) fm (EOS11).
- For \(K = 300\) MeV and \(K_c = -3.990\) MeV, solutions exist in two \(R_n\) ranges: \(R_n \approx 0\)–\(0.72\) fm and \(R_n = 0.86\)–\(0.88\) fm.
- For \(K = 350\) MeV and \(K_c = -7.274\) MeV, solutions exist for \(R_n \approx 0\)–\(0.79\) fm and \(R_n = 0.85\)–\(0.88\) fm.
- The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model result (\(K = 200\) MeV, \(M^*_0 = 0.906 M\), \(K' \sim -85\) MeV) is reproduced by the EVE model with \(R_n = 0.568\) fm, smaller than the QMC bag radius \(R_B = 0.8\) fm.
- The upper bound for effective mass at normal density is \(M^*_0 < 0.94 M\) for \(k_{F0} = 1.4\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kouno et al., 1995) using relativistic mean field theory with nonlinear \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) terms, where they found \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV favorable for reproducing \(K\), \(K_c\), \(K_{vs}\), and symmetry energy \(a_4\) simultaneously. It contrasts with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (Saito et al., 1995) which yields a smaller incompressibility (\(\sim 200\) MeV). The paper enables future work to test whether the excluded volume effects can reconcile low incompressibility values with empirical Coulomb and symmetry coefficients, while noting that the scaling model and relativistic mean field framework may not drastically alter the conclusion favoring \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV.
