# Testing Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis for Non-Abelian Gauge Theories

> We report on progress in full quantum understanding of thermalization in non-Abelian gauge theories. Specifically, we test the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis for (2+1)-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory.

# Testing Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis for Non-Abelian Gauge Theories

## Key claim
The eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) is shown to hold for (2+1)-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory in three specific lattice setups, but only under finite-size and truncation constraints: a linear plaquette chain with electric field basis limited to j=0,1/2, a two-dimensional hexagonal lattice with the same Hilbert space constraint, and a three-plaquette chain with j up to 7/2 where energy eigenvalues converge.

## Method
The authors perform exact numerical diagonalization of the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian for (2+1)-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory on two lattice geometries: a linear chain of square plaquettes and a hexagonal plaquette plane, both with periodic boundary conditions. They use the electric basis to describe states on links, enforce Gauss's law at each vertex, and truncate the local electric basis at either j_max=1/2 or j_max=7/2. They compute energy eigenvalues and eigenstates, then analyze level statistics via nearest-gap distributions and restricted gap ratios compared to Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) predictions, diagonal matrix elements of local operators (1-plaquette and 2-plaquette operators) relative to microcanonical averages, and off-diagonal matrix elements of the electric energy operator.

## Key numbers
- Coupling values used: g^2=1.2 for the linear plaquette chain (setup a), g^2=0.75 for the hexagonal lattice (setup b), and g^2=0.8 for the three-plaquette chain with j_max=7/2 (setup c), all in lattice units.
- For the linear chain with j_max=1/2, level statistics were analyzed in the k=2pi/N momentum sector on a chain of N=19 plaquettes.
- For the hexagonal lattice with j_max=1/2, level statistics were analyzed in the k_x=2pi/N_x, k_y=2pi/N_y momentum sector on a 5x4 hexagonal lattice.
- For the three-plaquette chain with j_max=7/2, level statistics were analyzed in the zero momentum sector with positive left-right and top-bottom reflection parity on N=3 plaquettes.
- The microcanonical proxy for diagonal matrix elements used an average over 21 nearby states (alpha-10 to alpha+10).
- Exponential decrease in deviation of diagonal matrix elements from microcanonical averages was observed with increasing lattice size for both the plaquette chain and honeycomb lattice with j_max=1/2.

## Context
This work extends tests of the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis, previously applied mainly in condensed matter physics, to non-Abelian gauge theories relevant for understanding thermalization in quantum chromodynamics and heavy ion collisions. Prior studies by D'Alessio (2015) reviewed ETH in other contexts, while Yao (2023) and Muller (2023) provided the mapping to spin systems for j_max=1/2, and Ebner (2023) contributed the three-plaquette calculations with larger j_max. The results enable future extensions to SU(3) gauge theories, higher dimensions, and inclusion of dynamical fermions, as well as more detailed study of the onset of GOE behavior in off-diagonal matrix elements.
