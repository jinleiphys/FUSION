# Long-range collectivity in small collision-systems with two- and four-particle correlations @ STAR

> New STAR differential and integral v{2,3} measurements that explicitly account for non-flow contributions are reported for p/d/3He+Au, collisions at Roots=200 GeV. The measurements, which leverage the two-particle correlators for p/d/3He+Au and minimum-bias p+p collisions in tandem with three well-established methods of non-flow subtraction, are observed to be method-independent. For comparable multiplicities, they further indicate system-independent v2{2} and v3{2} values that are consistent with the critical role of both size (N{ch}) and the subnucleonic-fluctuations-driven eccentricities e{2,3} but are inconsistent with the notion of shape engineering in p/d/3He+Au collisions. The scaling function derived from the measurements, confirm the important role of final-state effects across a broad spectrum of collision-system sizes and energies and suggests an increase in eta/s(T,muB) for small collision-systems.

## Key claim

The paper reports that for comparable charged-particle multiplicities, the non-flow-mitigated elliptic flow v2{2} and triangular flow v3{2} values are system-independent across p+Au, d+Au, and 3He+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, consistent with the influence of both system size (Nch) and subnucleonic-fluctuation-driven eccentricities ε2,3, but inconsistent with the notion of shape engineering in these small collision systems.

## Method

The STAR collaboration measured two-particle correlation functions Y(Δφ) for p/d/3He+Au and minimum-bias p+p collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, with trigger and associated particles selected from 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and |η| < 0.9, imposing |Δη| > 1.0 to suppress near-side jet contributions. Three independent non-flow subtraction methods were employed: a template-fit method using the p+p correlator as a baseline, and two Fourier expansion methods that scale the p+p non-flow contributions by either the pair-yield coefficient ratio (c0pp/c0) or the c1 coefficient ratio (c1/c1pp). The resulting v2(pT), v3(pT), and pT-integrated v2{2} and v2{4} values were compared across all three methods and validated with AMPT model simulations.

## Key numbers

- v2(pT) and v3(pT) measurements for p/d/3He+Au at √sNN=200 GeV, with trigger and associated particles in 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and |η| < 0.9
- Non-flow contributions are system-dependent, but the non-flow-mitigated v2(pT) and v3(pT) are method-independent within uncertainties
- v3(pT) for p+Au and d+Au from STAR are about a factor of 3-4 larger than those reported by PHENIX (PHENIX, 2018), lying well outside statistical and systematic uncertainties
- pT-integrated v2{2} and v2{4} for 0-10% d+Au and 3He+Au are reported; statistics for p+Au precluded a statistically significant measurement of v2{4}
- The scaling function SFS(vn/εn, pT, Nch, η/s, q̂) shows a collapse of diverse vn measurements onto a single curve, with a jet-quenching branch for pT > 4 GeV/c and a viscous attenuation branch for pT < 4 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior measurements at RHIC by PHENIX (PHENIX, 2018) and at the LHC (Chatrchyan, 2013) for small collision systems, addressing the question of whether collective flow develops in p/d/3He+Au and p+Pb collisions or whether initial-state-driven mechanisms prevail. The results contradict the earlier PHENIX report of system-dependent v3(pT) and instead support the applicability of hydrodynamic expansion even for small systems, consistent with numerical simulations suggesting hydrodynamics remains applicable when system size R is of O(1/T) (Chesler, 2016). The scaling function analysis confirms the role of final-state effects across a broad spectrum of collision-system sizes and energies, and suggests an increase in the specific viscosity ⟨η/s(T, μB)⟩ for small collision systems, motivating future measurements with systems such as O+O at RHIC and the LHC.
