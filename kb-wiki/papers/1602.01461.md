# Comment on "Breakdown of the expansion of finite-size corrections to the hydrogen Lamb shift in moments of charge distribution"

> In a recent paper, Hagelstein and Pascalutsa examine the error associated with an expansion of proton structure corrections to the Lamb shift in terms of moments of the charge distribution. They propose a small modification to a conventional parameterization of the proton's charge form factor and show that this can resolve the proton radius puzzle. However, while the size of the "bump" they add to the form factor is small, it is large compared to the total proton structure effects in the initial parameterization, yielding a final form factor that is unphysical. Reducing their modification to the point where the resulting form factor is physical does not allow for a resolution of the radius puzzle.

## Key claim

Hagelstein and Pascalutsa (2015) proposed that a small, narrow bump added to the proton charge form factor at very low momentum transfer could resolve the proton radius puzzle, but the modification is unphysical because it makes the form factor exceed unity. Reducing the bump to a physically allowed size would not provide a significant improvement in the agreement between electronic and muonic hydrogen Lamb shift results.

## Method

Arrington compared the magnitude of the proposed form factor modification (a peak contribution of \(3 \times 10^{-5}\) to \(G_E\) at \(Q^2 \approx 10^{-6}\) GeV\(^2\)) against the total finite-size effect in the original parameterization of Arrington (2007), where \(|G_E - 1| = 3.5 \times 10^{-6}\) at the same \(Q^2\). He then examined the consequences of reducing the modification by an order of magnitude to avoid the unphysical \(G_E > 1\) condition, and considered the constraints from analyticity of form factors as discussed by Hill (2010).

## Key numbers

- The proposed modification to \(G_E\) has a peak contribution of \(3 \times 10^{-5}\) at \(Q^2 \approx 10^{-6}\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the original parameterization of Arrington (2007), \(|G_E - 1| = 3.5 \times 10^{-6}\) at \(Q^2 \approx 10^{-6}\) GeV\(^2\), meaning the proposed bump is roughly ten times larger than the total finite-size effect.
- Reducing the modification by an order of magnitude to avoid \(G_E > 1\) would not provide a significant improvement in agreement between electronic and muonic hydrogen Lamb shift results.
- Similar features near the electronic hydrogen sensitivity peak at \(Q^2 \approx 10^{-10}\) GeV\(^2\) would need to be \(10^5\) times smaller to avoid exceeding the full finite-size correction from Arrington (2007).

## Context

This comment directly addresses the proposal by Hagelstein and Pascalutsa (2015) that a small modification to the proton charge form factor could resolve the proton radius puzzle, which arises from the discrepancy between muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) and electronic hydrogen measurements (Parthey 2011, Mohr 2012) combined with electron scattering data (Sick 2003, Bernauer 2010, Zhan 2011, Lee 2015, Arrington 2015). The comment shows that the proposed modification is unphysical and that no similar modification can simultaneously resolve the discrepancy and satisfy constraints from analyticity of form factors (Hill 2010).
