# Gauge-invariant formulation of $N N \to N N γ$

> A complete, rigorous relativistic field-theory formulation of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) bremsstrahlung reaction is presented. The resulting amplitude is unitary as a matter of course and it is gauge invariant, i.e., it satisfies a generalized Ward-Takahashi identity. The novel feature of this approach is the consistent microscopic implementation of local gauge invariance across all interaction mechanisms of the hadronic systems, thus serving as a constraint for all subprocesses. The formalism is quite readily adapted to approximations and thus can be applied even in cases where the microscopic dynamical structure of the underlying interacting hadronic systems is either not known in detail or too complex to be treated in detail. We point out how the interaction currents resulting from the photon being attached to nucleon-nucleon-meson vertices can be treated by phenomenological four-point contact currents that preserve gauge invariance. In an advance application of the present formalism [K. Nakayama and H. Haberzettl, PRC 80, 051001(R) (2009)], such interaction currents were found to contribute significantly in explaining experimental data. In addition, we provide a scheme that permits the approximate treatment of current contributions resulting from pieces of the NN interaction that cannot be incorporated exactly. In each case, the approximation procedure ensures gauge invariance of the entire bremsstrahlung amplitude. We also discuss the necessary modifications when taking into account baryonic states other than the nucleon N; in detail, we consider the $Delta(1232)$ resonance by incorporating the couplings of the $NN$ to the $NΔ$ and $ΔΔ$ systems, and the $γN \to Δ$ transitions. We apply the formalism to the 280-MeV bremsstrahlung data from TRIUMF [PRD 41, 2689 (1990)] incorporating $γNΔ$ transition currents and find good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete, rigorous relativistic field-theory formulation of the nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung reaction that is both unitary and gauge invariant, but the authors acknowledge that the formalism is "quite readily adapted to approximations" and can be applied even when the microscopic dynamical structure of underlying hadronic systems is either not known in detail or too complex to be treated in detail.

## Method

The authors derive the bremsstrahlung current by attaching an outgoing photon to all reaction mechanisms of the nucleon-nucleon T-matrix using a gauge-derivative procedure equivalent to minimal substitution, then removing external hadron propagators via LSZ reduction. They formulate the full four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter scattering equations and then perform a covariant three-dimensional reduction that eliminates the energy variable from loop integrations while preserving gauge invariance through the introduction of additional gauge-invariance preserving (GIP) currents. The formalism incorporates phenomenological four-point contact currents for photon attachment to nucleon-nucleon-meson vertices and extends to include the Delta(1232) resonance through NN to NDelta and DeltaDelta couplings.

## Key numbers

- The formalism is applied to 280-MeV bremsstrahlung data from TRIUMF [Phys. Rev. D 41, 2689 (1990)] incorporating gamma-N-Delta transition currents, finding good agreement with the data.
- The hard bremsstrahlung process p + p to pp(1S0) + gamma constrains the invariant mass of the two protons in the final state to be less than 3 MeV above its minimum value of twice the proton mass.
- The HADES Collaboration studies di-electron production in heavy-ion and elementary NN collisions in the 1-2 GeV/u energy domain.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical approach of Haberzettl (1997) developed for pion photoproduction to the NN bremsstrahlung reaction, and follows an advance application by Nakayama and Haberzettl (2009) that resolved a longstanding discrepancy between high-precision KVI data and existing theoretical models. The formalism enables improved treatment of bremsstrahlung processes relevant to di-lepton production in heavy-ion collisions studied by the HADES Collaboration, and provides a framework for analyzing recent hard bremsstrahlung measurements from COSY-ANKE and CELSIUS-Uppsala.
