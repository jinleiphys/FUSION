# Resummation for the Field-theoretical Derivation of the Negative Magnetoresistance

> We show detailed derivation of the electric conductivity of quark matter at finite temperature and density under a magnetic field. We especially focus on the longitudinal electric conductivity along the magnetic direction and establish the field-theoretical description of the negative magnetoresistance as observed in chiral materials. With increasing magnetic field our microscopic calculation leads to changing behavior from approximately quadratic to asymptotically linear dependence of the electric conductivity, while the magnetic dependence is quadratic in the conventional relaxation time approximation. The presented formulation founds a firm basis for the physical interpretation of the negative magnetoresistance as manifestation of the chiral anomaly, as well as it offers general methodology applicable for various transport coefficients.

## Key claim
The paper provides a field-theoretical derivation of the longitudinal electric conductivity in quark matter under a magnetic field, showing that the negative magnetoresistance arises from the chiral anomaly without assuming the relaxation-time approximation. The calculation demonstrates a transition from approximately quadratic to asymptotically linear dependence of conductivity on magnetic field, but only under the specific hierarchy T ≳ √(qB) ≫ gT where the magnetic field is not the largest scale.

## Method
The authors use the Kubo formula for the electric conductivity tensor and perform a resummation of pinching singularities in the polarization tensor within the real-time Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. They evaluate the longitudinal conductivity by solving the Boltzmann equation derived from field-theoretical resummation, taking into account full Landau levels (not just the lowest Landau level) and 1↔2 scattering processes at leading order in the QCD coupling g. The calculation assumes a hierarchy T ≳ √(qB) ≫ gT and uses free quark propagators in a constant magnetic field with gluonic interactions treated perturbatively.

## Key numbers
- The Hall conductivity is σ_H = n_e / B, where n_e is the net electric charge density and B is the magnetic field strength.
- The transverse conductivity is parametrically σ_⊥ / T ∼ g²T²/|q_f B|, which is small under the hierarchy √(qB) ≫ gT.
- For the longitudinal conductivity, lattice QCD results at T = 1.1 T_c and B = 0 give 0.2 C_em ≲ σ/T ≲ 0.7 C_em, where C_em = Σ_f q_f².
- The numerical calculation uses T = 200 MeV at zero density with u and d quark flavors (q_u = 2e/3, q_d = -e/3) and includes Landau levels up to n_max = 5.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for the chiral magnetic effect originally proposed by Kharzeev (2007) and the negative magnetoresistance prediction by Son and Spivak (2012). It goes beyond the relaxation-time approximation used in earlier work by Son and Spivak (2012) and the chiral kinetic theory approach, which are appropriate only for small magnetic fields. The calculation provides a field-theoretical foundation for interpreting negative magnetoresistance as a manifestation of the chiral anomaly, and offers a methodology applicable to other transport coefficients in QCD matter. The results are consistent with lattice QCD simulations by Ding et al. (2016) at zero magnetic field, supporting the validity of the approach.
