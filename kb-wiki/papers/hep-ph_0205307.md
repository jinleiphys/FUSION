# Instanton Contribution to the Proton and Neutron Electric Form Factors

> We study the instanton contribution to the proton and neutron electric form factors. Using the single instanton approximation, we perform the calculations in a mixed time-momentum representation in order to obtain the form factors directly in momentum space. We find good agreement with the experimentally measured electric form factor of the proton. For the neutron, our result falls short of the experimental data. We argue that this discrepancy is due to the fact that we neglect the contribution of the sea quarks. We compare to lattice calculations and a relativistic version of the quark-diquark model.

## Key claim

The single instanton contribution, calculated in the single instanton approximation (SIA), can account for the correct magnitude of the proton electric form factor at intermediate momentum transfers \(Q^2 \sim 1-4~\text{GeV}^2\), but the authors do not claim the SIA results have the accuracy to explain high-\(Q^2\) precision data for the ratio \(G_E^p/G_M^p\). For the neutron, the connected single-instanton contribution falls short of experimental data, which the authors argue is due to the neglected sea quark (disconnected diagram) contribution.

## Method

The authors compute the instanton contribution to the proton and neutron electric form factors using the single instanton approximation (SIA) in a mixed time-momentum representation, working in the Breit frame. They evaluate the wall-to-wall (W2W) three-point and two-point nucleon correlation functions in the zero-mode approximation, where the non-zero mode part of the quark propagator is replaced by the free propagator. The electric form factor is extracted from the ratio of the three-point to the squared two-point correlator at a fixed Euclidean time \(t = 0.9~\text{fm}\), using standard instanton ensemble parameters (average instanton size \(\bar{\rho} = 1/3~\text{fm}\), average instanton density \(\bar{n} = 1~\text{fm}^{-4}\), and quark effective mass \(m^\star \approx 85~\text{MeV}\)).

## Key numbers

- The average instanton size in the Instanton Liquid Model (ILM) is \(\rho \approx 1/3~\text{fm}\) and the typical density is \(n \approx 1~\text{fm}^{-4}\).
- The quark effective mass used in the SIA is \(m^\star \approx 85~\text{MeV}\).
- The SIA calculation of the two-point correlator is reliable up to distances of \(\approx 0.9~\text{fm}\); the three-point function is reliable up to \(\approx 1.8~\text{fm}\).
- The SIA results for the proton electric form factor are reliable for momentum transfers \(1~\text{GeV}^2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 4~\text{GeV}^2\).
- At \(Q^2 \sim 1-4~\text{GeV}^2\), the proton electric form factor from JLab measurements is reduced by about \(20-50\%\) relative to the magnetic one.
- The dipole fit mass parameter is \(M_{dip} = 0.84~\text{GeV}\).
- The scalar diquark size in the ILM is estimated to be approximately \(0.4~\text{fm}\).
- The connected component of the Green function accounts for roughly half of the neutron electric form factor, in qualitative agreement with lattice results.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous single-instanton calculation of the pion form factor (Faccioli, Schwenk, Shuryak, 2002) to the nucleon, motivated by the similarity between the pion and scalar diquark channels. It compares to lattice QCD calculations by Dong et al. (2000), which found that the connected contribution accounts for roughly half of the neutron electric form factor, and to a relativistic quark-diquark model by Ma et al. (2000) using light-front dynamics. The results enable further tests of the instanton liquid model for other baryons, such as the proton-to-delta transition form factors, and suggest that the dipole behavior of the proton electric form factor can be explained by instanton-induced interactions.
