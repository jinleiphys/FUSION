# Effects of self-consistency violation in Hartree-Fock RPA calculations for nuclear giant resonances revisited

> We provide accurate assessments of the consequences of violations of self-consistency in Hartree-Fock (HF) based random phase approximation (RPA) calculations of the centroid energy $E_{cen}$ of isoscalar and isovector giant resonances of multi-polarities $L=0-3$ in a wide range of nuclei. This is done by carrying out highly accurate HF-RPA calculations neglecting the particle-hole (ph) spin-orbit or Coulomb interaction in the RPA and comparing with the fully self-consistent HF-RPA results. We find that the shifts in the value of $E_{cen}$ due to self-consistency violation associated with the spin-orbit and Coulomb interactions are comparable or larger than the current experimental errors in $E_{cen}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that violations of self-consistency in Hartree-Fock based random phase approximation (HF-RPA) calculations, specifically the omission of the particle-hole (ph) spin-orbit or Coulomb interactions from the RPA residual interaction, produce shifts in the centroid energy of giant resonances that are comparable to or larger than current experimental errors (0.1–0.3 MeV), with the largest effects (up to ~1.5 MeV) occurring for spin-unsaturated nuclei in the isoscalar giant monopole resonance.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent HF-RPA calculations for isoscalar and isovector giant resonances of multipolarities L=0–3 across a wide range of nuclei using the SGII Skyrme interaction. They then repeated the calculations while deliberately omitting either the ph spin-orbit interaction (LS violation) or the ph Coulomb interaction (CO violation) from the RPA residual interaction, and compared the resulting centroid energies, constrained energies, and scaling energies to the fully self-consistent results. The RPA calculations used a large computational box (30 fm) with a mesh size of 0.3 fm, and the strength functions were computed using the Q-P representation method with a detailed 1ph space up to 4 major shells, with convergence checked against 6 major shells.

## Key numbers

- For the ISGMR in 208Pb, omission of the ph spin-orbit interaction shifts the peak of the strength function to higher energy by ~0.7 MeV, while omission of the ph Coulomb interaction shifts it to lower energy by ~0.4 MeV.
- For spin-unsaturated nuclei, the shift in centroid energy due to LS violation is ~1.2 MeV for 100Sn and ~1.8 MeV for 56Ni.
- For the isovector giant dipole resonance in 208Pb, omission of the ph Coulomb interaction shifts the strength function to lower energy by ~0.4 MeV.
- The fully self-consistent RPA calculations reproduce the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) to within less than 0.3% for all modes and nuclei studied (40Ca, 90Zr, 208Pb).
- The constrained Hartree-Fock and RPA values of m_{-1} for the ISGMR agree within 0.6% for 40Ca and 208Pb.
- For 208Pb, the correction term κ_np in the isovector EWSR has an effect of 2% for L=3.
- For the ISGMR in 208Pb, if both ph LS and CO interactions are dropped simultaneously, the shift in centroid energy is δE_LS,CO = -0.30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier assessments of self-consistency violation effects, which were limited to the constrained energy and scaling energy of the ISGMR (Agrawal et al., 2003) or to specific nuclei and modes (Terasaki et al., 2005 for 120Sn; Nakatsukasa et al., 2001 for 16O). The paper provides a systematic survey across a wide range of nuclei and multipolarities (L=0–3) for both isoscalar and isovector modes, demonstrating that obtaining an m_1 value close to the EWSR does not guarantee a fully self-consistent calculation. The results establish that fully self-consistent HF-RPA calculations are necessary to achieve theoretical uncertainties below current experimental errors, particularly for extracting the nuclear incompressibility modulus K_∞ from ISGMR data.
