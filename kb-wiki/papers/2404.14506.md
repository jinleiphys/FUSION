# Shell-model study of $^{28}$Si: coexistence of oblate, prolate and superdeformed shapes

> We study the shape coexistence in the nucleus $^{28}$Si with the nuclear shell model using numerical diagonalizations complemented with variational calculations based on the projected generator-coordinate method. The theoretical electric quadrupole moments and transitions as well as the collective wavefunctions indicate that the standard USDB interaction in the $sd$ shell describes well the ground-state oblate rotational band, but misses the experimental prolate band. Guided by the quasi-SU(3) model, we show that the prolate band can be reproduced in the $sd$ shell by reducing the energy of the $0d_{3/2}$ orbital. Alternatively, in the extended $sdpf$ configuration space a modification of the SDPF-NR interaction that accommodates cross-shell excitations also reproduces the oblate and prolate bands. Finally, we address the possibility of superdeformation in $^{28}$Si within the $sdpf$ space. Our results indicate that superdeformed structures appear at about $18$-$20$~MeV.

## Key claim

The standard USDB interaction in the sd shell accurately describes the oblate ground-state rotational band of 28Si but fails to reproduce the experimental prolate band; the prolate band can be reproduced either by reducing the energy of the 0d3/2 orbital in the sd shell or by using a modified SDPF-NR interaction in the extended sdpf space that accommodates cross-shell excitations.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations using numerical diagonalizations (Lanczos method with the ANTOINE code) and variational calculations based on the projected generator-coordinate method (PGCM) with the Taurus suite. They employed the USDB interaction in the sd configuration space and a modified SDPF-NR interaction in the sdpf space, guided by quasi-SU(3) model predictions to adjust single-particle energies and reproduce the prolate band.

## Key numbers

- Experimental intrinsic quadrupole moment for the oblate band: Q0,t = -57.3 ± 0.7 e fm2 (from B(E2) transition strengths)
- Experimental intrinsic quadrupole moment for the prolate band: Q0,t = 72 ± 7 e fm2 (from B(E2) transition strengths)
- Superdeformed structures appear at about 18-20 MeV excitation energy in the sdpf space
- Ground-state energy from USDB diagonalization: -135.9 MeV relative to 16O core
- Ground-state energy from USDB-MOD diagonalization: -137.6 MeV relative to 16O core
- Ground-state energy from SDPF-NR* diagonalization: -145.1 MeV relative to 16O core
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the oblate 2+ state with USDB (PGCM and diagonalization): 18.5 e fm2 (experiment: 16 ± 3 e fm2)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the prolate 2+ state with USDB-MOD (PGCM and diagonalization): -19.2 e fm2

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of 28Si that focused primarily on the oblate ground-state band (Wildenthal 1973, Smirnova 2019, Li 2023) and addresses the long-standing challenge of describing the prolate band at ~6.5 MeV excitation. The quasi-SU(3) model (Zuker 1995) guides the modification of the USDB interaction, while the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics study (AMD 2018) previously predicted superdeformation in 28Si, which the present work finds at 18-20 MeV. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted superdeformed band and provide a benchmark for ab initio approaches to shape coexistence in the sd shell.
