# Description of superdeformed nuclei in the interacting boson model

> The interacting boson model is extended to describe the spectroscopy of superdeformed bands. Microscopic structure of the model in the second minimum is discussed and superdeformed bosons are introduced as the new building blocks. Solutions of a quadrupole Hamiltonian are implemented through the $1/N$ expansion method. Effects of the quadrupole parameters on dynamic moment of inertia and electric quadrupole transition rates are discussed and the results are used in a description of superdeformed bands in the Hg-Pb and Gd-Dy regions.

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM) can be extended to describe superdeformed bands by introducing superdeformed bosons (s, d, g) as building blocks, but the model's applicability is limited in the Gd-Dy region where single-particle degrees of freedom cause variations in the dynamic moment of inertia that the simple collective model cannot accommodate.

## Method

The authors used a Nilsson + particle-number-conserving BCS model to analyze the microscopic structure of superdeformed states, determining that the inert core is much smaller than for normal deformation, leading to about three times more valence bosons (N ~ 31). They then employed a quadrupole Hamiltonian in the sdg-IBM framework and solved it using a 1/N expansion method extended to third order in (L/N)^2, which provides analytical formulas for energies and E2 transition matrix elements. The model parameters (κ and q) were fitted to experimental data on dynamic moments of inertia and B(E2) values for superdeformed bands in the Hg-Pb and Gd-Dy regions.

## Key numbers

- For superdeformed states in ¹⁹⁴Hg, the boson number is N_super = 31, compared to N_normal = 8 (hole picture) or 15 (particle picture); for ¹⁵²Dy, N_super = 31 versus N_normal = 10.
- The deformation parameters are δ ~ 0.40 (axis ratio 5:3) for the Hg-Pb region and δ ~ 0.50 (axis ratio 2:1) for the Gd-Dy region.
- The oscillator frequency is ħω₀ ≈ 7.1 MeV for A ~ 190 (Hg-Pb) and 7.7 MeV for A ~ 150 (Gd-Dy).
- Intrinsic quadrupole moments calculated with N_max = 12 are 19 b (protons) and 29 b (neutrons) for ¹⁹⁴Hg, and 19 b (protons) and 27 b (neutrons) for ¹⁵²Dy; experimental values are 18 ± 3 eb for ¹⁵²Dy and 17 ± 2 eb for ¹⁹⁴Hg.
- In the superdeformed Λ-pair, S- and D-pairs account for about 80% probability, with G-pair having a sizable contribution; for normal deformation (δ = -0.13), S- and D-pairs account for 100%.
- The pairing gap for superdeformed states is Δ ≈ 0.5 MeV for both protons and neutrons in ¹⁹⁴Hg, using G = 0.06 MeV.
- The SU(3) limit quadrupole parameters are q₂₂ = -1.242, q₂₄ = 1.286, q₄₄ = -1.589.
- Effective boson charges for B(E2) fits in ¹⁹²⁻¹⁹⁴Hg are e = 0.12–0.14 eb.

## Context

This work extends the standard IBM, which was successful for normal deformed nuclei (Iachello 1987, Casten 1988), to the superdeformed regime by incorporating a much larger boson number and g-bosons based on microscopic calculations by Honma (1992). The 1/N expansion method was previously developed by Kuyucak (1988) and extended to higher orders by Kuyucak (1995). The results enable systematic phenomenological analysis of superdeformed bands, complementing microscopic theories, and the formalism can be used to investigate identical bands and C₄ symmetry in future work.
