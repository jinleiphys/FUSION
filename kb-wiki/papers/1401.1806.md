# Coherence effects between the initial and final state radiation in a dense QCD medium

> In these proceedings contribution we review our recent results of medium modifications to the coherence pattern between the initial and final state radiation. We study single gluon production off a highly energetic parton that undergoes a hard scattering and subsequently crosses a dense QCD medium of finite size. Multiple scatterings lead to a partial suppression of the interferences between different emitters that opens the phase space for large angle emissions. We briefly discuss the generalization of this setup to describe gluon production in the case of pA collisions by including finite length and energy corrections. The configuration studied here may have phenomenological consequences in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Medium-induced multiple scatterings partially suppress the interference between initial-state and final-state gluon radiation, opening phase space for large-angle emissions (antiangular ordering) up to a maximal angle set by the medium, while vacuum coherence is recovered for angles above that maximum.

## Method

The author solves the Classical Yang-Mills equations for a highly energetic quark that undergoes a hard scattering and then traverses a dense QCD medium of finite length, treating the radiated gluon as a fluctuation on a static background field. The gluon spectrum is decomposed into six components (direct emissions from the incoming and outgoing parton, and interference terms between them) and analyzed in two asymptotic regimes: the coherent regime (formation time much larger than medium length) and the incoherent regime (formation time much smaller than medium length). The calculation is performed in the light-cone gauge using the harmonic oscillator approximation for medium averages.

## Key numbers

* The medium-induced component of the gluon spectrum (in-in term) peaks around the momentum scale \(k_f = \sqrt{k^+ \hat q}\), where \(k^+\) is the gluon light-cone momentum and \(\hat q\) is the medium transport coefficient.
* The maximal accumulated transverse momentum from the medium is \(Q_s^2 = \hat q L^+\), where \(L^+\) is the medium length.
* The maximal angle for large-angle emissions is \(\theta_{\text{max}} = Q_s / k^+\).
* The formation time scale is \(\tau_f \sim |\Omega|^{-1}\), where \(\Omega = (1-i) \sqrt{\hat q / (2k^+)}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of color coherence in QCD media from the \(q\bar{q}\) antenna case (Mehtar-Tani 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2011, Mehtar-Tani 2011, Mehtar-Tani 2012) to a space-like (t-channel) scattering process with a single quark. It enables future phenomenological studies of jet production in pA collisions by incorporating finite-length and non-eikonal corrections, and may help clarify factorization issues in regimes where collinear factorization fails.
