# Three-body radiative capture reactions

> Radiative capture reaction rates for $^6$He, $^9$Be and $^{17}$Ne formation at astrophysical conditions are studied within a three-body model using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator method to calculate their states. An alternative procedure to estimate these rates from experimental data on low-energy breakup is also discussed

# Three-body radiative capture reactions

## Key claim
Three-body radiative capture reaction rates for the formation of 6He, 9Be, and 17Ne can be calculated within a three-body model using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) method, but the computed rates show important differences from previous sequential estimations, particularly at low temperatures where direct three-body capture dominates.

## Method
The authors computed photodissociation cross sections for the Borromean nuclei 6He, 9Be, and 17Ne using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) method to generate the three-body states. Reaction rates for the inverse radiative capture processes were then obtained from the photodissociation cross sections via the principle of detailed balance, including both E1 and M1 multipole transitions. An alternative method was also proposed to estimate reaction rates from inclusive low-energy breakup data on the compound nucleus, valid for E1-dominated processes at small scattering angles where the interaction is Coulomb dominated.

## Key numbers
- For 6He formation via the alpha(2n,gamma)6He reaction: the computed reaction rate shows important differences compared with the sequential estimation of Bartlett (2006), with the three-body direct capture dominating at low temperatures
- For 17Ne formation via the 15O(2p,gamma)17Ne reaction: the computed reaction rate is several orders of magnitude larger than previous calculations in Grigorenko (2006)
- For 9Be formation via the alpha(alpha n,gamma)9Be reaction: the calculations agree with sequential estimations at high temperature but show an enhancement at low temperature from three-body direct E1 capture to low-energy 1/2+ states
- The alternative method using breakup data for 6He + 208Pb can provide reaction rates in the temperature region between 1-3 GK, requiring data restricted to low-energy and small-angle kinematic conditions

## Context
This work extends the three-body radiative capture framework previously applied to Borromean nuclei by the same group (Casal 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016) and contrasts with sequential two-step reaction models used by Angulo (1999), Sumiyoshi (2002), and Bartlett (2006). The results show that direct three-body capture plays an important role at low temperatures where particles have no energy to populate intermediate resonances, as previously suggested by Garrido (2011). The proposed alternative method using inclusive breakup data, applied here to 6He and previously to 11Li (Casal 2016R), enables estimation of reaction rates from experimental data when direct theoretical calculations differ significantly.
