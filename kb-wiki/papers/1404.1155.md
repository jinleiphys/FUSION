# Eikonal reaction theory for two-neutron removal reactions

> The eikonal reaction theory (ERT) proposed lately is a method of calculating one-neutron removal reactions at intermediate incident energies in which Coulomb breakup is treated accurately with the continuum discretized coupled-channels method. ERT is extended to two-neutron removal reactions. ERT reproduces measured one- and two-neutron removal cross sections for 6He scattering on 12C and 208Pb targets at 240 MeV/nucleon and also on a 28Si target at 52 MeV/nucleon. For the heavier target in which Coulomb breakup is important, ERT yields much better agreement with the measured cross sections than the Glauber model.

## Key claim

The eikonal reaction theory (ERT), previously developed for one-neutron removal, is extended to two-neutron removal reactions and reproduces measured cross sections for 6He scattering on light and heavy targets with no free parameters; for heavier targets where Coulomb breakup is important, ERT yields much better agreement with experiment than the Glauber model, which omits Coulomb breakup.

## Method

The authors solve the four-body Schrödinger equation for the n+n+α+T system using the eikonal approximation, treating the nuclear interactions of the two neutrons and the α particle with the target under the adiabatic approximation while treating the Coulomb interaction of the α particle exactly via the continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). The S-matrix is factorized into separate operators for each neutron and the α particle, and the one- and two-neutron removal cross sections are decomposed into elastic breakup, one-neutron stripping, and two-neutron stripping contributions. The optical potentials for the projectile constituents are obtained by folding the Melbourne nucleon-nucleon g-matrix interaction with target densities.

## Key numbers

* For 6He scattering on 12C at 240 MeV/nucleon, ERT reproduces the measured one- and two-neutron removal cross sections with no free parameter; the Glauber model results are close to the ERT results for this light target.
* For 6He scattering on 208Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon, ERT yields much better agreement with the experimental data than the Glauber model, particularly for the elastic breakup cross section and the two-neutron removal cross section.
* For 6He scattering on 28Si at 52 MeV/nucleon, the deviation of the Glauber-model results from the ERT results for σ_{-2n} and σ_R is about 10%, while the error of the adiabatic approximation itself is 3% for these quantities.
* The optical potential U_α^(Nucl) for the 28Si target at 52 MeV/nucleon was determined by multiplying the real and imaginary parts of the folding potential by 0.91 and 1.39, respectively, to reproduce measured elastic and total reaction cross sections for 4He+28Si scattering at nearby energies.

## Context

This work extends the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) of Yahiro (2011) and Hashimoto (2011) from one-neutron to two-neutron removal reactions, building on four-body CDCC calculations for 6He scattering by Matsumoto (2010) and others. The Glauber model, which has been applied to two-neutron removal for light targets by Bertsch (1998) and others, fails for heavy targets because its adiabatic approximation makes the Coulomb phase diverge; ERT solves this problem by treating Coulomb breakup accurately with CDCC. The method enables consistent extraction of spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients from both light and heavy target data, and the authors propose a hybrid approach where stripping cross sections are calculated with the Glauber model and elastic breakup with CDCC.
