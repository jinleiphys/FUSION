# Triaxial projected shell model approach

> The projected shell model analysis is carried out using the triaxial Nilsson+BCS basis. It is demonstrated that, for an accurate description of the moments of inertia in the transitional region, it is necessary to take the triaxiality into account and perform the three-dimensional angular-momentum projection from the triaxial Nilsson+BCS intrinsic wavefunction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three-dimensional angular-momentum projection from a triaxial Nilsson+BCS intrinsic wavefunction is necessary to accurately describe the moments of inertia of transitional nuclei, where the axially symmetric projected shell model (PSM) fails. The authors show that triaxiality with gamma approximately 30 degrees reproduces the steep increase in moment of inertia at low spin, but note that the work is exploratory and that both deformation parameters epsilon and epsilon' should be varied consistently in future studies.

## Method

The authors developed a triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) by performing three-dimensional angular-momentum projection from a triaxial Nilsson+BCS quasiparticle vacuum state. They diagonalized a shell model Hamiltonian consisting of Q·Q plus monopole and quadrupole pairing forces within the space of projected states, using the triaxial Nilsson potential specified by deformation parameters epsilon and epsilon'. The method was applied to rare-earth nuclei using three major harmonic-oscillator shells (N=4,5,6 for neutrons; N=3,4,5 for protons) with standard pairing strengths G1=21.24 MeV and G2=13.86 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For 156Er, 158Yb, and 176W, the experimental moments of inertia are reproduced with triaxiality parameter epsilon' approximately 0.15, corresponding to gamma approximately 35 degrees
- For 184Os, the experimental moment of inertia is well reproduced with epsilon' approximately 0.15
- For 186Os and 188Os, the experimental moment of inertia can be described with epsilon' between 0.10 and 0.15
- The deformation parameters epsilon used were 0.20 for 156Er and 158Yb, and 0.225 for 176W (taken from earlier axially symmetric calculations)
- Pairing strengths used: G1 = 21.24 MeV and G2 = 13.86 MeV for the rare-earth region

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model (PSM) approach of Hara and Sun (1995), which assumed axial symmetry and failed to reproduce the steep increase in moments of inertia for transitional rare-earth nuclei such as 156Er, 158Er, 158Yb, 162Hf, 172W, 174W, and 176W. The authors note that a triaxial rotor model (Davydov and Filippov, 1958) had previously shown that backbending plots slant toward the vibrational limit as gamma increases, motivating the present three-dimensional projection. The work enables future studies where both epsilon and epsilon' are varied to find the energy minimum for each nucleus, and where projection from 2- and higher-quasiparticle states is included.
