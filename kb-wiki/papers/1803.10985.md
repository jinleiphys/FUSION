# Nuclear parton density functions from dijet photoproduction at the EIC

> We study the potential of dijet photoproduction measurements at a future electron-ion collider (EIC) to better constrain our present knowledge of the nuclear parton distribution functions. Based on theoretical calculations at next-to-leading order and approximate next-to-next-to-leading order of perturbative QCD, we establish the kinematic reaches for three different EIC designs, the size of the parton density function modifications for four different light and heavy nuclei from He-4 over C-12 and Fe-56 to Pb-208 with respect to the free proton, and the improvement of EIC measurements with respect to current determinations from deep-inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan data alone and when also considering data from existing hadron colliders.

## Key claim

Dijet photoproduction at a future EIC can improve constraints on nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) by a factor of 5 to 10 compared to current determinations, but only if the kinematic reach is sufficient to probe the shadowing region down to x-values of about 10^{-2} and scales up to 400 GeV^2, which is more limited than in inclusive jet DIS.

## Method

The authors performed NLO and approximate NNLO (aNNLO) perturbative QCD calculations of dijet photoproduction cross sections in electron-ion collisions, using the nCTEQ15 and EPPS16 nuclear PDF fits as baselines. They simulated differential cross sections in average jet transverse momentum, average rapidity, and reconstructed parton momentum fractions for four nuclei (He-4, C-12, Fe-56, Pb-208) across three EIC designs (eRHIC at sqrt(s)=80-90 GeV, MEIC at sqrt(s)=45 GeV), assuming anti-kT jet clustering with R=1 and a minimum jet pT of 5 GeV for the leading jet.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic reach: average jet transverse momentum up to 10 GeV at MEIC and 20 GeV at eRHIC, corresponding to scales up to 400 GeV^2
- x-values probed in the ion: down to about 10^{-2}, reaching into the shadowing region but not below
- Annual event counts at largest pT: between 10 and 10^3 events with luminosities of 100 fb^{-1} (MEIC) or 10 fb^{-1} (eRHIC)
- Nuclear modification relative to protons: increases with nuclear mass from a few percent up to a factor of two for Pb-208
- aNNLO corrections: do not exceed +7% or -7% at large jet pT and forward/backward rapidity
- Expected improvement: EIC measurements can reduce current nPDF uncertainties by a factor of 10 compared to nCTEQ15 (DIS+dy only) and by a factor of 5 compared to nCTEQ15 including RHIC pion data or EPPS16 including LHC dijet data

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of inclusive jet production in DIS at the EIC (Klasen 2017) to the complementary photoproduction regime (Q^2 approx 0), where both direct and resolved photons contribute. It builds on the nCTEQ15 fits (Kovarik 2015) and EPPS16 fits (Eskola 2016) that currently constrain nPDFs from DIS, Drell-Yan, RHIC pion data, and LHC dijet data. The results enable future experimental design decisions for EIC detectors and demonstrate that dijet photoproduction can probe the EMC effect, antishadowing, and the shadowing region, while also potentially constraining photon PDFs in complementary kinematic regions.
