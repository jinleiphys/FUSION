# Proton isovector helicity PDF at NNLO and the twist-3 moment $\tilde{d}_2$ from lattice QCD at physical quark masses

> We present a lattice quantum chromodynamics calculation of the $x$-dependent isovector quark helicity parton distribution function (PDF) of the proton in the large momentum effective theory (LaMET) framework. Through operator product expansion (OPE) we also extract the $\tilde{d}_2$ moment of the twist-3 PDF $g_T(x)$ for the first time in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, which is proportional to the average color Lorentz force experienced by the quark in the proton. This calculation is performed on a lattice of spacing $a$ = 0.076 fm at physical quark masses. The quasi-PDF matrix elements are measured in proton states boosted to momenta $P_z=\{0, 0.25, 1.02, 1.53\}$ GeV. We first extract the lowest few helicity PDF moments from the renormalization-group (RG) invariant ratios of the matrix elements with OPE. Combined with the matrix elements relevant for $g_T(x)$, we obtain $\tilde{d}_2^{u-d}(2\ {\rm GeV})=0.0024(46)$ at next-to-leading order in $\overline{\rm MS}$. Then, the helicity quasi-PDF matrix elements are renormalized in the hybrid scheme with linear renormalon resummation and Fourier transformed to the $x$-space after an asymptotic extrapolation. The quasi-PDF is perturbatively matched to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ PDF with RG and threshold resummations at next-to-leading power and next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracies. After resummations, we determine the PDF in the region $x\in[0.25,0.75]$ with controlled systematic uncertainties. The end-point regions are then parameterized, combined with the LaMET prediction at moderate $x$, and fitted to the short-distance matrix elements in coordinate space.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD extraction of the twist-3 moment \(\tilde{d}_2\) in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme, finding \(\tilde{d}_2^{u-d}(2\ {\rm GeV})=0.0024(46)\) at next-to-leading order, which is consistent with zero within its large uncertainty and indicates a small genuine twist-3 contribution to the proton spin structure.

## Method

The authors compute isovector quark helicity quasi-PDF matrix elements on a \(64^3\times64\) lattice with spacing \(a=0.076\) fm at physical quark masses, using proton states boosted to momenta \(P_z=\{0, 0.25, 1.02, 1.53\}\) GeV. They extract the lowest few helicity PDF moments from renormalization-group invariant ratios of matrix elements via operator product expansion, then obtain \(\tilde{d}_2\) from the short-distance behavior of transverse twist-3 correlators using NLO matching. The helicity quasi-PDF is renormalized in the hybrid scheme with linear renormalon resummation, Fourier transformed to \(x\)-space, and perturbatively matched to the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) PDF with RG and threshold resummations at next-to-leading power and next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing \(a = 0.076\) fm, determined using the \(f_K\) scale, on a \(64^3\times64\) lattice with 350 configurations
* Pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV (physical quark masses)
* Proton boosts: \(P_z = \{0, 0.25, 1.02, 1.53\}\) GeV
* Axial charge \(g_A^{u-d} = 1.211(12)\) from local matrix element analysis
* Helicity PDF moments at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: \(\langle x\rangle_{u-d} = 0.291(7)\), \(\langle x^2\rangle_{u-d} = 0.101(10)\), \(\langle x\rangle_{u+d} = 0.183(6)\), \(\langle x^2\rangle_{u+d} = 0.073(3)\)
* Twist-3 moment \(\tilde{d}_2^{u-d}(2\ {\rm GeV}) = 0.0024(46)\) at NLO in \(\overline{\rm MS}\)
* PDF determined in the region \(x \in [0.25, 0.75]\) with controlled systematic uncertainties

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of helicity PDFs by Alexandrou et al. (2020) and Holligan et al. (2024) by achieving both physical quark masses and a fine lattice spacing simultaneously, and by incorporating recent theory developments including NNLO matching and resummations. The \(\tilde{d}_2\) extraction builds on the twist-3 factorization framework developed by Braun et al. (2021) and provides a complementary first-principles constraint to direct local operator calculations by Gockeler et al. (2005), Burger et al. (2021), and Crawford et al. (2024), which typically require nonperturbative subtractions for power-divergent mixing. The results enable future comparisons with experimental determinations from Jefferson Lab and the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider, and the extracted moments can be used to test the Jaffe-Manohar and Ji decompositions of the proton spin.
