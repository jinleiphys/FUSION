# Proton tensor charges from a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation

> The proton's tensor charges are calculated at leading order in a symmetry-preserving truncation of all matter-sector equations relevant to the associated bound-state and scattering problems. In particular, the nucleon three-body bound-state equation is solved without using a diquark approximation of the two-body scattering kernel. The computed charges are similar to those obtained in contemporary simulations of lattice-regularised quantum chromodynamics, an outcome which increases the tension between theory and phenomenology. Curiously, the theoretical calculations produce a value of the scale-invariant ratio $(-δ_T d/δ_T u)$ which matches that obtained in simple quark models, even though the individual charges are themselves different. The proton's tensor charges can be used to constrain extensions of the Standard Model using empirical limits on nucleon electric dipole moments.

## Key claim

The proton's tensor charges are calculated using a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation solved without a diquark approximation, producing values similar to lattice QCD results and thereby increasing tension between theory and phenomenology; however, the scale-invariant ratio (−δ_T d/δ_T u) matches simple quark model predictions even though the individual charges differ from those models.

## Method

The nucleon three-body bound-state equation (a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation) was solved directly under the assumption that two-body interactions dominate, without using a diquark approximation of the two-body scattering kernel. The dressed-quark gap equation and inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark tensor vertex were solved using the same rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation interaction, with all equations solved in a symmetry-preserving manner at leading order. The quark-quark interaction kernel employed a form consistent with QCD's gauge sector, with parameters fixed by the pion's leptonic decay constant.

## Key numbers

- δ_T u = 0.912^(+42)_(−47) (proton, at renormalization scale ζ=2 GeV)
- δ_T d = −0.218^(+4)_(−5) (proton, at renormalization scale ζ=2 GeV)
- g_T^(1) = δ_T u − δ_T d = 1.130^(+42)_(−47) (isovector tensor charge)
- g_T^(0) = δ_T u + δ_T d = 0.694^(+42)_(−47) (isoscalar tensor charge)
- Dressed-quark tensor charge T_1(k^2=0; ζ_2) = 0.67(5) (light-quark tensor charge at zero momentum)
- Nucleon mass m_N = 0.932^(+5)_(−11) GeV (obtained from the three-body equation with no parameter variation)
- Renormalization-group-invariant current-quark masses: hat m_u = hat m_d = 6.6 MeV (corresponding to one-loop evolved values m_u^(ζ_2) = m_d^(ζ_2) = 4.4 MeV)

## Context

This work extends continuum bound-state methods for nucleon structure beyond the diquark approximation used in earlier approaches such as Cahill (1988), Burden (1988), and Reinhardt (1989), instead adapting the formulation of Eichmann (2009, 2011) to solve the three-valence-body problem directly. The computed tensor charges are similar to those from contemporary lattice QCD simulations by Bhattacharya (2015, 2016) and Alexandrou (2017), increasing the known tension between theoretical calculations and phenomenological extractions. The results enable improved constraints on extensions of the Standard Model through empirical limits on nucleon electric dipole moments, as the proton's EDM is determined by the combination δ_T u and δ_T d with quark-level EDM contributions.
