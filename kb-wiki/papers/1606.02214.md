# Chiral-symmetry breaking and pion structure in the Covariant Spectator Theory

> We introduce a covariant approach in Minkowski space for the description of quarks and mesons that exhibits both chiral-symmetry breaking and confinement. In a simple model for the interquark interaction the quark mass function is obtained and used in the calculation of the pion form factor. We study the effects of the mass function and of the different quark pole contributions on the pion form factor.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a covariant approach in Minkowski space that exhibits both chiral-symmetry breaking and confinement, but the pion form factor is surprisingly insensitive to the functional form of the strong quark form factors and quark mass function, and the results depend on the specific piecewise shape assumed for the mass function in the timelike region where no lattice data are available.

## Method

The authors use the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) to solve the Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator, keeping only quark propagator pole contributions in the loop integration. They employ a simple interaction kernel consisting of a linear confining potential and a constant potential with a strong quark form factor, which satisfies the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The dynamical quark mass function is obtained by fitting to lattice QCD data in the spacelike region and adopting a piecewise form in the timelike region, then used to calculate the pion form factor via triangle diagrams in impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

- The pion form factor is studied for momentum transfer up to \(Q^2 \approx 6\) GeV\(^2\), where the scaled pion form factor has a maximum according to Jefferson Lab measurements after the 12 GeV upgrade.
- For large pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.6\) GeV and large \(Q^2\), the active pole contributions are suppressed compared to spectator contributions by about 30%.
- Results are shown for pion masses \(m_\pi = 0.6\), 0.42, and 0.14 GeV, comparing running and fixed quark masses.

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory approach previously developed by Gross (1969) and applied to mesons by Biernat et al. (2015), and is similar to Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approaches used by Bicudo (1989) but solved in Minkowski space rather than Euclidean space. The results enable straightforward extension of pion form factor calculations from spacelike to timelike \(Q^2\) regions, which can help interpret dilepton production data from heavy ion collisions and resolve discrepancies between Babar and Belle results for the \(\pi \gamma^* \gamma\) transition form factor.
