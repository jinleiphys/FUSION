# Conformal symmetry and light flavor baryon spectra

> The degeneracy among parity pairs systematically observed in the N and Delta spectra is interpreted to hint on a possible conformal symmetry realization in the light flavor baryon sector in line with AdS_5/CFT_4. The case is made by showing that all the observed N and Delta resonances with masses below 2500 MeV distribute fairly well each over the first levels of a unitary representation of the conformal group, a representation that covers the spectrum of a quark-diquark system, placed directly on the AdS_5 cone, conformally compactified to R^1*S^3. The free geodesic motion on the S^3 manifold is described by means of the scalar conformal equation there, which is of the Klein-Gordon type. The equation is then gauged by the "curved" Coulomb potential that has the form of a cotangent function. Conformal symmetry is not exact, this because the gauge potential slightly modifies the conformal centrifugal barrier of the free geodesic motion. Thanks to this, the degeneracy between P11-S11 pairs from same level is relaxed, while the remaining states belonging to same level remain practically degenerate. The model describes the correct mass ordering in the P11-S11 pairs through the nucleon spectrum as a combined effect of the above conformal symmetry breaking, on the one side, and a parity change of the diquark from a scalar at low masses, to a pseudoscalar at higher masses, on the other. The quality of the wave functions is illustrated by calculations of realistic mean-square charge radii and electric charge form-factors on the examples of the proton, and the protonic P11(1440), and S11(1535) resonances. The scheme also allows for a prediction of the dressing function of an effective instantaneous gluon propagator from the Fourier transform of the gauge potential. We find a dressing function that is finite in the infrared and tends to zero at infinity.

## Key claim

The systematic degeneracy among parity pairs observed in the N and Delta spectra below 2500 MeV is interpreted as evidence for an approximate conformal symmetry realization in the light flavor baryon sector, consistent with AdS_5/CFT_4, though the symmetry is not exact because the gauge potential slightly modifies the conformal centrifugal barrier of the free geodesic motion.

## Method

The authors place a quark-diquark system on the AdS_5 cone, conformally compactified to R^1 x S^3, and describe the free geodesic motion on S^3 using the scalar conformal equation of Klein-Gordon type. They gauge this equation with a "curved" Coulomb potential having the form of a cotangent function, which respects conformal symmetry as a harmonic function on S^3. The resulting interacting Klein-Gordon equation is solved analytically, yielding wave functions expressed in terms of Romanovski polynomials and an algebraic mass formula. The model assigns N and Delta resonances to levels of a unitary representation of the conformal group, with the diquark treated as spinless and its parity changing from scalar at low masses to pseudoscalar at higher masses.

## Key numbers

- All observed N and Delta resonances with masses below 2500 MeV are distributed over the first levels of a unitary representation of the conformal group.
- Seven Delta resonances (S31(1900), P31(1910), P33(1920), D33(1940), F35(1905), D35(1930), F37(1950)) are squeezed in the narrow mass band between 1900 MeV and 1950 MeV, fitting into the K=3 multiplet.
- The N(1440), N(1535), N(1520) triplet matches quantum numbers of K=1 in the conformal representation.
- The model describes the correct mass ordering in the P11-S11 pairs through the nucleon spectrum.
- The cotangent potential on S^3 has finite range with r in [0,R] and x4 in [-R,R], where R is the S^3 hyper-radius and kappa = 1/R^2 is the curvature.
- The dressing function of an effective instantaneous gluon propagator is predicted to be finite in the infrared and tends to zero at infinity.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT approach to hadron physics pioneered by Brodsky and de Teramond (2008) within light-front QCD, and builds on prior quark-diquark models by Wilczek (2003) and others that reduce the number of "missing" states in baryon spectroscopy. The model implements conformal symmetry in a position space of finite volume, placing the quark-diquark system on the conformally compactified boundary of AdS_5, and provides a framework for calculating mean-square charge radii and electric charge form-factors of baryon resonances, demonstrated on the proton, P11(1440), and S11(1535).
