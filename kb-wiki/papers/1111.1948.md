# Decay studies of $^{288-287}115$ alpha-decay chains

> The $α$-decay chains of $^{288-287}115$ are studied along with the possible cluster decay modes by using the preformed cluster model (PCM). The calculated $α$-decay half-lives are compared with experimental data and other model calculations. The calculated Q-values, penetration probabilities and preformation probabilities factors for $α$-decay suggest that $^{283}_{170}113$,$^{287}_{172}115$ and $^{272}_{165}107$ parent nuclei are more stable against the $α$-decay. These alpha decay chains are further explored for the possibilities of cluster decay. Decay half lives of different cluster from different nuclei of the decay chains point to the extra stability near or at the deformed shells Z=108, N=162 and Z=100, N=152. The decay half-lives for $^{14}C$ and $^{48}Ca$ clusters are lower than the current experimental limit ($\approx$ $10^{28}$sec).

# Decay studies of $^{288-287}115$ alpha-decay chains

## Key claim
The preformed cluster model (PCM) calculations suggest that $^{283}_{170}113$, $^{287}_{172}115$, and $^{272}_{165}107$ are more stable against alpha decay, but this conclusion is qualified by the assumption that alpha decay proceeds from ground state to ground state, whereas decay from excited states may also occur and large experimental uncertainties exist for some nuclei in the chains.

## Method
The authors applied the preformed cluster model (PCM), which uses dynamical collective coordinates of mass and charge asymmetry, to calculate alpha-decay half-lives for the $^{288}115$ and $^{287}115$ decay chains. They computed Q-values from experimental data, the PCM model itself, the Myers-Swiatecki mass formula, and the Muntian nuclear data table, then compared the resulting half-lives with experimental data and other theoretical model calculations. For cluster decay, they calculated half-lives for clusters including $^{10}Be$, $^{14}C$, $^{20}O$, $^{23}F$, $^{26}Ne$, $^{28}Mg$, $^{34}Si$, and $^{48}Ca$ from nuclei in both alpha-decay chains.

## Key numbers
- The $^{288}115$ and $^{287}115$ decay chains were observed in 3n- and 4n-evaporation channels with cross sections of about 3 pb and 1 pb, respectively, using a $^{48}Ca$ beam with a $^{243}Am$ target.
- The decay half-lives for $^{14}C$ and $^{48}Ca$ clusters from nuclei in these decay chains are lower than the current experimental limit of approximately $10^{28}$ seconds.
- The cluster decay half-life of $^{48}Ca$ from all parent nuclei of the $^{287}115$ decay chain lies within the experimental limits of approximately $10^{28}$ seconds.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of the $^{288}115$ and $^{287}115$ decay chains by authors including Zhongzhou (2003), Geng (2003), Sankha (2004), Basu (2004), Sharma (2005), and Royer (2008), who studied ground state properties using various theoretical models. The PCM calculations point to extra stability near or at the deformed shells Z=108, N=162 and Z=100, N=152, consistent with earlier investigations by Lazarev (1994) and Greenlees (2008). The results enable future experimental searches for cluster decay branching in superheavy element decay chains, particularly for $^{14}C$ and $^{48}Ca$ emission, whose predicted half-lives fall within current experimental sensitivity.
