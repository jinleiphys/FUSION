# Effective Masses and Sizes of N(939), $Δ\!\!\mbox{\boldmath(1232)}$ and N(1440) in Nuclear Medium

> The effective masses and sizes of N(939), $Δ(1232)$ and N(1440) in nuclear medium are calculated for a model in which quarks are subjected to a confinement force quenched by the scalar part of nuclear interactions. At the nuclear matter density, the nucleon swells by 50\% relative to the free nucleon. The masses of $Δ(1232)$ and N(1440) decrease in nuclear medium more than the nucleon, so that the pion requires less energy to excite them in nuclear medium than it does for a free nucleon.

## Key claim

The nucleon swells by 50% at nuclear matter density, and the masses of Δ(1232) and N(1440) decrease more than the nucleon mass, but these results depend on a specific model where the confinement force is quenched by the scalar part of nuclear interactions and the bag parameters are adjusted to reproduce effective masses from the σω model.

## Method

The authors use a dynamical bag model with a breathing degree of freedom, where quarks are confined by an infinite potential well of radius R. The Hamiltonian includes a hyperfine interaction to split N and Δ states, a pion self-energy term, and a bag kinetic energy term. For nuclear medium effects, the bag constant BD, pion-nucleon coupling fN, pion mass mπ, and pion radius Rπ are quenched according to the σω model mean-field approximation, with the effective nucleon mass mN* determined self-consistently from the scalar field σ. The effective masses of Δ and N(1440) are then obtained by solving the same eigenvalue equation with the quenched parameters.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density (kF = 1.36 fm⁻¹), the nucleon rms radius swells by 50% relative to the free nucleon, giving ⟨r²⟩N¹/² = 1.29 fm
- The ratio of quark-core rms radii in medium to free is [⟨r²⟩N(q)¹/²]*/[⟨r²⟩N(q)¹/²] = 1.96 at nuclear matter density
- The effective mass parameter αN = mN*/mN takes values 0.691 for kF = 1.20 fm⁻¹ and 0.619 for kF = 1.36 fm⁻¹
- The mass difference between Δ and N decreases to 50% of the free value at nuclear matter density
- The three-quark probability P3q = 0.74 for free nucleon, 0.87 for kF = 1.20 fm⁻¹, and 0.91 for kF = 1.36 fm⁻¹
- The condition for swollen bag contact is reached at kF = 1.28 fm⁻¹, corresponding to 0.8 times normal nuclear matter density

## Context

This work extends the dynamical bag model of Brown, Durso and Johnson (1983) and Tomio and Nogami (1985) to nuclear medium, using the σω model of Serot and Walecka (1986) with parameters from Gmuca (1992) to define the effective nucleon mass. The predicted 50% nucleon swelling is close to the 45% obtained in the skyrmion picture by Rho (1985), while the 50% reduction in Δ-N mass difference is much larger than the 20% predicted in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Christov et al. (1990). The results enable predictions for π-nucleus scattering, suggesting that Δ and N(1440) excitations require less energy in nuclear medium than in free πN scattering.
