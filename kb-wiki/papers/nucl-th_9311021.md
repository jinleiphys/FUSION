# Rates for the reactions antiproton-proton --> pi phi and gamma phi

> We study antiproton-proton annihilation at rest into $πϕ$ and $γϕ$. Rescattering by $\overline{K^*}K+K^*\overline{K}$ and $ρ^{+}ρ^{-}$ for $\overline{p}p\rightarrowπϕ$ states is sizable, of order $(0.90\, {\rm to}\,2.6)\times 10^{-4}$ in the branching ratio, but smaller than experiment. For $\overline{p}p\rightarrowγϕ$ the rescattering contributions are negligible, but the $γϕ$ channel is well explained by a $ρϕ$ intermediate state combined with vector meson dominance.

# Rates for the reactions antiproton-proton → πφ and γφ

## Key claim

Rescattering via K*K and ρ⁺ρ⁻ intermediate states yields a branching ratio for pp̅ → πφ of (0.90 to 2.6)×10⁻⁴, which is sizable but still smaller than the experimental value of (4.0±0.8)×10⁻⁴; for pp̅ → γφ, rescattering contributions are negligible (∼10⁻⁷), but the γφ channel is well explained by a ρφ intermediate state combined with vector meson dominance, giving a branching ratio of 1.27×10⁻⁵.

## Method

The authors calculated two-meson rescattering contributions for pp̅ annihilation at rest into πφ and γφ using on-shell intermediate states (K*K, ρ⁺ρ⁻, π⁺π⁻) with loop integrals, form factors (monopole with cutoff Λ from 1.2 to 2.0 GeV), and experimental decay widths for the vertices. For γφ, they also evaluated vector meson dominance via ρφ and ωφ intermediate states, relating the γρ coupling to the universal constant fρ.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for pp̅ → πφ from rescattering: (0.90 to 2.6)×10⁻⁴ (range including form factor variation and experimental uncertainties)
- Experimental branching ratio for pp̅ → πφ: (4.0±0.8)×10⁻⁴ (from Braune)
- Branching ratio for pp̅ → γφ from K*K rescattering: 1.7×10⁻⁷ (on-shell), reduced to (3.0×10⁻⁸ to 9.4×10⁻⁸) with form factors
- Branching ratio for pp̅ → γφ from ρφ via vector meson dominance: 1.27×10⁻⁵
- Experimental branching ratio for pp̅ → γφ: (1.2±0.3)×10⁻⁵
- Branching ratio for pp̅ → γφ from ωφ via vector meson dominance: 2.8×10⁻⁶
- Branching ratio for pp̅ → γω from ωρ and ωω via vector meson dominance: 2.3×10⁻⁵ (destructive interference), compared to experimental (6.8±1.8)×10⁻⁵ (from Amsler)

## Context

This work extends the calculation of rescattering contributions for pp̅ → φφ in flight (Locher, Lu, Zou) to the reactions pp̅ → πφ and γφ at rest. The rescattering results for πφ are smaller than the experimental value, leaving room for additional mechanisms such as direct annihilation or other intermediate states. For γφ, the vector meson dominance approach successfully explains the experimental rate, consistent with earlier applications to pp̅ → γγ, γπ, γη, γω, and γη' (Amsler). The authors note that a preprint by Buzatu contained an error in the loop integration for the K*K contribution to πφ, leading to an overestimate.
