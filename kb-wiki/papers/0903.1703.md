# 3- and 4- body meson- nuclear clusters

> The binding energies and matter distributions for the 3- body systems like $ϕ$- meson + 2N, 2$ϕ$ + N and 4- body system like $ϕ$+3n are calculated. For the 3- particle systems two- dimensional Faddeev equations in the differential form are used. For the 4- body system $ϕ$+3n the folding model is applied.

# 3- and 4- body meson- nuclear clusters

Belyaev, V. B.; Sandhas, W.; Shlyk, I. I. (2009-03-10)

## Key claim

The paper claims that bound states of a phi-meson with two nucleons (phi nn, phi np, phi pp) and with two phi-mesons and one nucleon (phi phi n) exist, with binding energies large enough to close the main K-meson decay channels of the phi-meson. However, for the four-body system phi+3n, the folding model estimate yields no bound state, and the authors note this may be an underestimate compared to exact calculations.

## Method

For the three-body systems phi NN and phi phi N, the authors solved two-dimensional Faddeev equations in differential form, expanding the wave function into partial waves and retaining only the lowest partial wave. The phi-N interaction was modeled as a Yukawa potential with parameters from Gao (year not given), the phi-phi potential as a sum of two Yukawa potentials fitted to the f2(2010) resonance, and the N-N interactions used the MT III and modified MT I potentials. For the four-body system phi+3n, a folding model was applied using the phi nn cluster wavefunction to generate an effective potential for the third neutron.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the phi N system: E_phi n = -9.47 MeV, E_phi p = -9.40 MeV
- Binding energy of the phi n n system: E_phi n n = -21.8 MeV
- Binding energy of the phi n p system (np pair in triplet state): E_phi n p = -37.9 MeV
- Binding energy of the phi phi n system: -77 MeV
- nn-scattering length used to calibrate the singlet potential: a_nn = -18.5 fm
- Phi-N Yukawa potential parameters: alpha = 1.25, mu = 600 MeV
- Phi-phi potential parameters: V1 = 1000 MeV*fm, mu1 = 2.5 fm^-1, V2 = 1250 MeV*fm, mu2 = 3.0 fm^-1
- Numerical grid for stable results: N = 110 mesh points in hyperradius, M = 210 mesh points in hyperangle, hyperradius cutoff L = 9 fm

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of meson-nuclear clusters, particularly the strong attraction observed in K- few-nucleon systems, to the case of the phi-meson which contains two strange quarks. The authors note that earlier quark model calculations by Huang (year not given) and phenomenological models by Gao (year not given) predicted strong phi-N attraction. The results suggest that phi-meson clusters with two nucleons or two phi-mesons and one nucleon form compact bound states, with the phi np system having a binding energy larger than naive expectations from a phi+d configuration. The four-body phi+3n system remains an open question, as the folding model may underestimate binding compared to exact AGS-equation calculations previously performed for the analogous eta_c+3N system by Belyaev, Shevchenko, Fix, and Sandhas (year not given). The authors plan exact four-body calculations in the future.
