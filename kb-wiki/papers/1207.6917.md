# Centrality and initial formation time dependence of the emission of thermal photons from fluctuating initial conditions at RHIC and LHC

> Event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions (IC) in the ideal hydrodynamic calculation are known to enhance the production of thermal photons significantly compared to a smooth initial state averaged profile in the range $p_T >$ 1 GeV/$c$ for 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC and 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. The 'hotspots' or the over-dense regions in the fluctuating IC produce more high $p_T$ photons compared to the smooth IC due to the strong temperature dependent emission of the thermal radiation. This enhancement is expected to be more pronounced for peripheral collisions, for lower beam energies, and for larger values of plasma formation time. A suitably normalized ratio of central to peripheral yield of thermal photons ($R_{cp}^γ$) is a potential probe to study the density fluctuations and their size in the initial conditions.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions in ideal hydrodynamic calculations enhance thermal photon production compared to smooth initial state averaged profiles for $p_T > 1$ GeV/$c$, but this enhancement is less pronounced at LHC than at RHIC for the same centrality bin, and the $p_T$ spectra alone cannot distinguish between the effects of initial condition fluctuations and variations in the plasma formation time.

## Method

The authors used event-by-event ideal hydrodynamics with fluctuating initial conditions, combined with state-of-the-art photon emission rates (plasma rate from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) and hadronic rates from Turbet, Rapp, and Gale (2000)), to compute thermal photon production from 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC and 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC at different collision centralities. The initial formation time $\tau_0$ was taken as 0.17 fm/$c$ at RHIC and 0.14 fm/$c$ at LHC, with a default fluctuation size parameter $\sigma$ of 0.4 fm.

## Key numbers

- Thermal photon enhancement from fluctuating initial conditions is most significant in the range $2 \le p_T \le 4$ GeV/$c$ at both RHIC and LHC energies.
- Initial formation time $\tau_0$ = 0.17 fm/$c$ for 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- Initial formation time $\tau_0$ = 0.14 fm/$c$ for 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC.
- Default fluctuation size parameter $\sigma$ = 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamic framework developed by Holopainen, Niemi, and Eskola (2011) to study thermal photon production, building on earlier studies by Chatterjee, Holopainen, Renk, and Eskola (2011, 2012) that first showed the enhancement from fluctuating initial conditions. The results demonstrate that the ratio of central to peripheral yield of thermal photons ($R_{cp}^\gamma$) normalized by the number of binary collisions can serve as a probe of the fluctuation size parameter, offering a way to reduce uncertainties about initial conditions that cannot be resolved from $p_T$ spectra alone.
