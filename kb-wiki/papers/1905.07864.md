# Deep Inelastic Scattering on an Extremal RN-AdS Black Hole II: Holographic Fermi Surface

> We consider deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on a dense nucleus described as an extremal RN-AdS black hole with holographic quantum fermions in the bulk. We evaluate the 1-loop fermion contribution to the R-current on the charged black hole, and map it on scattering off a Fermi surface of a dense and large nucleus with fixed atomic number. Near the black hole horizon, the geometry is that of AdS$_2\times $R$^3$ where the fermions develop an emergent Fermi surface with anomalous dimensions. DIS scattering off these fermions yields to anomalous partonic distributions mostly at large-x, as well as modified hard scattering rules. The pertinent R-ratio for the black hole is discussed. For comparison, the structure functions and the R-ratio in the probe or dilute limit with no back-reaction on the geometry, are also derived. We formulate a hybrid holographic model for DIS scattering on heavy and light nuclei, which compares favorably to the existing data for Pb, Au, Fe, C and He over a wide range of parton-x.

## Key claim

Deep inelastic scattering on a dense nucleus modeled as an extremal RN-AdS black hole receives a next-to-leading order contribution from holographic quantum fermions that yields anomalous partonic distributions mostly at large-x, with the structure functions modified by an emergent Fermi surface near the black hole horizon, though the exact interpolation between the near-horizon (non-Fermi liquid) and far-horizon (Fermi liquid) regimes is approximated rather than constructed exactly.

## Method

The authors evaluated the 1-loop fermion contribution to the R-current on an extremal RN-AdS black hole by computing the bulk effective action for the R-field shift coupled to Dirac fermions, then mapped this to boundary structure functions via the holographic dictionary. The hard part of the fermion propagator was approximated by its vacuum AdS$_5$ result, while the soft part used the retarded propagator near the emergent Fermi surface from the AdS$_2\times$R$^3$ reduction of the near-horizon geometry. The structure functions were decomposed into leading order (classical black hole, order $N_c^2$) and sub-leading (quantum fermion, order $N_c^0$) contributions, with the R-ratio derived for both dense and dilute limits.

## Key numbers

* The leading order structure function at low-x scales as $F_2^{\rm BH}(x,q^2)/A \approx (C_T/x)(3x^2q^2/4m_N^2)^{2/3}$ with $C_T$ proportional to $N_c^2/(2^{17/3}\pi^2\Gamma^2(1/3)\tilde{\alpha}^{5/3})$
* The leading order structure function at large-x drops exponentially as $D(x) \approx C_D \exp(-\sqrt{q/m_N}(m_N/\mu)\sqrt{x})$ with $C_D \approx \exp(-2\Gamma^2(1/4)\sqrt{6\tilde{\alpha}}/3\sqrt{2\pi})$
* The chemical potential relates to nucleon mass as $\mu \approx (4/3)m_N$, and the nucleus rest energy is $E_A = (3/4)A\mu$
* The Bjorken-x for the nucleus is defined as $x_A = Q^2/(2E_A\omega) = x m_N/E_A$
* The transition from non-Fermi liquid to Fermi liquid occurs at $k^0_c$ satisfying $k^0_c \approx |v_F \Pi(k^0_c)|$, where $\Pi(k^0) = h_2 e^{i\gamma_{k_F}}(k^0)^{2\nu_k}$ with $\nu_k = (\sqrt{\tilde{\alpha}}/\mu)(k^2 - k_R^2)^{1/2}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of DIS on an extremal RN-AdS black hole (Mamo and Zahed, 2018) by adding the next-to-leading order fermionic contribution, and builds on holographic Fermi liquid studies by Faulkner et al. (2009) and Gubser and Ren (2012) who characterized the low-lying excitations near the AdS$_2\times$R$^3$ horizon. The hybrid holographic model formulated here enables comparison to existing DIS data for Pb, Au, Fe, C and He over a wide range of parton-x, complementing earlier holographic DIS work by Polchinski and Strassler (2003) at moderate-x and by Hatta et al. (2008) on thermal black holes at low-x.
