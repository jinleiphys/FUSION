# Quasiparticles and ion cooling by an electron beam in a strong magnetic field

> The frictional force arising from the interaction of ions with a magnetized electron beam in a strong magnetic field is investigated. The problem is reduced to the interaction of quasiparticles, or Larmor circles, with ions. A Hamiltonian describing this interaction is derived. The role of various impact parameters in the process of ion cooling is elucidated. The cases of both positively and negatively charged ions are considered.

# Quasiparticles and ion cooling by an electron beam in a strong magnetic field

## Key claim
The complex three-dimensional problem of ion-electron beam interaction in a strong magnetic field can be reduced to a simpler two-dimensional problem of quasiparticle-ion interaction using a random phase averaging method, enabling calculation of the frictional force beyond perturbation theory. The difference in frictional force between positively and negatively charged ions is entirely determined by the region of impact parameters where perturbation theory fails.

## Method
The authors replace the interaction of ions with individual magnetized electrons by an interaction with quasiparticles (Larmor circles), averaging over the random phase of electron cyclotron motion. They derive a Hamiltonian describing the quasiparticle-ion interaction in a reduced two-dimensional phase space, then compute the frictional force components F_y and F_z by integrating over impact parameters. The method is applied for both positively charged ions (sigma=1) and negatively charged ions (sigma=-1), and the results are compared with perturbation theory predictions from Derbenev and Skrinsky (1978) and Parkhomchuk (1984).

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field B = 4 × 10^3 G, corresponding to cyclotron frequency ω ∼ 7 × 10^10 s^{-1}
- For H± ions, characteristic length λ = 4 × 10^{-5} cm
- Typical ion velocity spread v^{(i)} ∼ 3 × 10^6 cm/s, giving β ∼ 1 for H± and β ∼ 0.2 for heavy ions
- Electron transverse velocity spread v^{(e)}_⊥ ∼ 3 × 10^7 cm/s, giving dimensionless Larmor radius ρ̄ ∼ 5 for H± and ρ̄ ∼ 1 for heavy ions
- For electron density n_e = 10^9 cm^{-3}, plasma frequency ω_p ∼ 1.7 × 10^9 s^{-1} and maximum impact parameter b_max ∼ 50
- Perturbation theory lower cutoff b_min = 2/β^2
- For σ = -1 (negatively charged ions) with β_⊥ = 0 and ρ̄ = 0, the friction force F_z = N × 8π/β^2
- The energy loss rate W is always negative, given by W = - (1/2) λ ω β_∥ N ∫ d²b (ΔP_z(b))²

## Context
This work extends the electron cooling theory originally proposed by Budker (1967) and addresses discrepancies between the perturbation theory results of Derbenev and Skrinsky (1978) and Parkhomchuk (1984), showing they have different ranges of applicability (ρ̄ ≫ b_min and ρ̄ ≪ b_min, respectively). The quasiparticle approach, inspired by Landau's quasiparticle concept in condensed matter theory, enables nonperturbative calculations that are essential for accurate friction force determination, particularly for negatively charged ions. The results are important for designing electron cooling facilities and suggest that cooling of positively charged ions by a positron beam would be significantly more efficient than by an electron beam, though technically challenging.
