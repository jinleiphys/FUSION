# Wilsonian renormalization group analysis of nonrelativistic three-body systems without introducing dimerons

> Low-energy effective field theory describing a nonrelativistic three-body system is analyzed in the Wilsonian renormalization group (RG) method. No effective auxiliary field (dimeron) that corresponds to two-body propagation is introduced. The Efimov effect is expected in the case of an infinite two-body scattering length, and is believed to be related to the limit cycle behavior in the three-body renormalization group equations (RGEs). If the one-loop property of the RGEs for the nonrelativistic system without the dimeron field, which is essential in deriving RGEs in the two-body sector, persists in the three-body sector, it appears to prevent the emergence of limit cycle behavior. We explain how the multi-loop diagrams contribute in the three-body sector without contradicting the one-loop property of the RGEs, and derive the correct RGEs, which lead to the limit cycle behavior. The Efimov parameter, $s_{0}$, is obtained within a few percent error in the leading orders. We also remark on the correct use of the dimeron formulation. We find rich RG-flow structure in the three-body sector. In particular, a novel nontrivial fixed point of the three-body couplings is found when the two-body interactions are absent. We also find, on the two-body nontrivial fixed point, the limit cycle is realized as a loop of finite size in the space of three-body coupling constants when terms with derivatives are included.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Efimov effect (limit cycle behavior in the three-body renormalization group equations) can be derived directly from a Wilsonian RG analysis of a nonrelativistic three-body system *without* introducing an auxiliary dimeron field, contrary to the apparent obstruction from the one-loop property of the RGEs. The key resolution is that multi-loop diagrams contribute to the three-body sector through the normal-ordering of counterterms generated by one-loop shell-mode integrations, even though the one-loop property of the RGEs is preserved.

## Method

The authors perform a Wilsonian RG analysis of a nonrelativistic bosonic effective field theory described by a single complex scalar field, using the operator-formalism implementation where the field momentum is bounded by a floating cutoff. They derive the RGEs for the three-body coupling constants by considering one-loop shell-mode integrations and then rewriting the resulting counterterms in normal-ordered form, which generates contributions from multi-loop diagrams where the additional loop momenta are forced to be related to the shell mode by Galilean invariance. The RGEs are solved analytically in the leading order (nonderivative three-body coupling) and numerically when derivative terms are included.

## Key numbers

* The Efimov parameter \(s_0\) is obtained within a few percent error in the leading orders (the exact Efimov value is \(s_0 = 1.00624\cdots\)).
* The ratio of binding energies of subsequent Efimov states is \(E_{n+1}/E_n \equiv e^{-2\pi/s_0} = 1/515.03\cdots\) (quoted from Efimov's original work).
* In the leading-order calculation with only the nonderivative three-body coupling, a nontrivial fixed point of the three-body coupling is found on the two-body trivial fixed point.
* On the two-body nontrivial fixed point, when terms with two derivatives are included, the limit cycle is realized as a loop of finite size in the space of three-body coupling constants.

## Context

This work extends the RG analysis of the Efimov effect originally performed by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998) using the dimeron field formulation, and by Moroz, Floerchinger, Schmidt, and Wetterich (2008) using functional RG with the dimeron field. The authors provide the first demonstration of the limit cycle behavior directly from RGEs without introducing a dimeron field, and they note that the dimeron formulation misses a certain contribution essential for obtaining the Efimov parameter within a few percent error. The analysis enables a systematic study of the effects of nonzero two-body effective range and off-shell parameters on the three-body binding energies, which had not been previously considered.
