# Effects of $ϕ_0$-meson on the EOS of hyperon star in the relativistic mean field model

> Nuclear effective interactions are considered as a vital tool to guide into the region of the high degree of isospin asymmetry and density. We take varieties of parameter sets of the RMF model to show the parametric dependence of the hyperon star properties. We add $ϕ_0$-meson to $σ$-$ω$-$ρ$ model. The effects of $ϕ_0$-meson on the equation of state and consequently on the maximum mass of the hyperon star are discussed. Due to the inclusion of $ϕ_0$-meson the threshold density of different hyperon production shift to higher density region. The effects of the hyperon-meson coupling constants on the maximum mass and radius of the hyperon stars are discussed.

## Key claim

Adding the $\phi_0$-meson (a vector meson mediating repulsive hyperon-hyperon interaction) to the standard $\sigma$-$\omega$-$\rho$ relativistic mean field (RMF) model stiffens the equation of state (EOS) of hyperon star matter and increases the maximum mass, but the effect is parametric: the magnitude depends on the chosen RMF parameter set and the hyperon-meson coupling constants, and the radius of the canonical 1.4 $M_\odot$ star changes by 13% when the hyperon-meson coupling constant is varied by 0.2.

## Method

The author solves the RMF model with an effective Lagrangian that includes $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, and $\phi_0$ mesons, using 22 different RMF parameter sets (G1, G2, IFSU, IFSU*, FSU, FSU2, TM1, TM2, PK1, NL3, NL3*, NL3-II, NL1, NL-RA1, SINPA, SINPB, GM1, GL97, GL85, L1, L3, and HS). Hyperon-meson coupling constants are fitted to reproduce hyperon potential depths ($U_\Lambda^{(N)} = -30$ MeV, $U_\Sigma^{(N)} = +40$ MeV, $U_\Xi^{(N)} = -28$ MeV) and $\rho$-meson couplings follow SU(6) symmetry. The resulting EOS is used as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute mass-radius relations for hyperon stars.

## Key numbers

- For the G2 parameter set, the $\Xi$ hyperon threshold density shifts from 0.3989 fm$^{-3}$ (without $\phi_0$) to 0.461 fm$^{-3}$ (with $\phi_0$); the $\Sigma^+$ threshold shifts from 0.914 fm$^{-3}$ to 1.038 fm$^{-3}$.
- For the G2 parameter set, the maximum mass of the hyperon star is 1.576 $M_\odot$ with $\phi_0$ and 1.299 $M_\odot$ without $\phi_0$; the corresponding radii are 9.622 km and 9.054 km, respectively.
- For the NL3 parameter set, the maximum mass of the hyperon star is 2.633 $M_\odot$ with $\phi_0$ and 2.529 $M_\odot$ without $\phi_0$; the corresponding radii are 13.012 km and 13.012 km, respectively.
- For the G2 parameter set, the central energy density at maximum mass is 3.387 $\times 10^{15}$ g cm$^{-3}$ with $\phi_0$ and 3.921 $\times 10^{15}$ g cm$^{-3}$ without $\phi_0$.
- The radius of the canonical 1.4 $M_\odot$ star changes from 10.0278 km to 11.275 km when $x_{\sigma\Lambda}$ is changed from 0.5 to 0.7 (with $U_\Lambda$ fixed at -30 MeV).
- The incompressibility of the parameter sets ranges from 211.4 MeV (NL1) to 626.3 MeV (L1); symmetry energy ranges from 21.7 MeV (L1) to 43.7 MeV (NL1).

## Context

This work extends the study of the "hyperon puzzle" (the difficulty of reproducing the observed 2 $M_\odot$ neutron star mass when hyperons are included) by examining the repulsive hyperon-hyperon interaction mediated by the $\phi_0$-meson, following earlier approaches such as those of Weissenborn (2012, 2014) and Oertel (2015). The author uses 22 RMF parameter sets to test the predictive capacity of the model, finding that only a few sets (FSU2, PK1, GL85, GL97, SINPA, SINPB, NL3, NL3*, IFSU*, and G2) can reproduce the canonical star radius in the range 9.7-13.9 km from Hebeler (2010). The results show that the $\phi_0$-meson systematically increases the maximum mass and compactness of hyperon stars, but the radius behavior is parameter-set dependent, and more hypernuclei data are needed to constrain the hyperon-meson coupling constants.
