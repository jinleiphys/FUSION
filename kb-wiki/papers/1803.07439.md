# Functional renormalization-group calculation of the equation of state of one-dimensional nuclear matter inspired by the Hohenberg--Kohn theorem

> We present the first successful functional renormalization group(FRG)-aided density-functional (DFT) calculation of the equation of state (EOS) of an infinite nuclear matter (NM) in (1+1)-dimensions composed of spinless nucleons. We give a formulation to describe infinite matters in which the 'flowing' chemical potential is introduced to control the particle number during the flow. The resultant saturation energy of the NM coincides with that obtained by the Monte-Carlo method within a few percent. Our result demonstrates that the FRG-aided DFT can be as powerful as any other methods in quantum many-body theory.

## Key claim
The authors present the first successful functional renormalization group (FRG)-aided density functional theory (DFT) calculation of the equation of state of infinite nuclear matter in (1+1)-dimensions, but only for spinless nucleons and with the caveat that the result depends on introducing a "flowing" chemical potential to control particle number during the flow.

## Method
The authors formulate the DFT-RG approach for infinite uniform systems by introducing a lambda-dependent ("flowing") chemical potential that controls the particle number as the interaction is gradually switched on. They solve the flow equation for the two-particle point-irreducible (2PPI) effective action up to second-order vertex expansion, starting from a free fermion system at lambda=0 and flowing to the fully interacting system at lambda=1. The calculation uses the two-nucleon interaction constructed by Alexander et al. (1989) for a (1+1)-dimensional spinless nuclear matter model.

## Key numbers
- The saturation energy of the one-dimensional nuclear matter obtained by the FRG-aided DFT coincides with that obtained by the Monte-Carlo method within a few percent.
- The second-order truncation in previous work by Kemler et al. (2017) gave only 30% accuracy compared to the Monte-Carlo simulation by Alexander et al. (1989).
- The calculation uses a (1+1)-dimensional system composed of spinless nucleons with mass set to 1 in the chosen units.

## Context
This work extends the DFT-RG approach pioneered by Polonyi, Sailer, and Schwenk (2002, 2004) and applied to quantum mechanical toy models by Kemler et al. (2013) and to a one-dimensional many-body model by Kemler et al. (2017). The authors improve upon the fixed particle-number formalism of Kemler et al. (2017), which underestimated the saturation energy by about 30%, by introducing a flowing chemical potential. This enables accurate calculation of the equation of state for infinite nuclear matter and demonstrates that the FRG-aided DFT can be competitive with Monte-Carlo methods for such systems.
