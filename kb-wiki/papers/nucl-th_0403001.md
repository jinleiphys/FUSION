# Final-State Interaction as the Origin of the Cronin Effect

> Instead of adhering to the usual explanation of the Cronin effect in terms of the broadening of the parton transverse momentum in the initial state, we show that the enhancement of hadron production at moderate $p_T$ in d+Au collisions is due to the recombination of soft and shower partons in the final state. Such a mechanism can readily explain the decrease of the Cronin effect with increasing rapidity. Furthermore, the effect should be larger for protons than for pions.

## Key claim

The Cronin effect (enhancement of hadron production at moderate transverse momentum in d+Au collisions) originates from the recombination of soft and shower partons in the final state during hadronization, rather than from initial-state broadening of parton transverse momentum via multiple scattering.

## Method

The authors apply the recombination model, decomposing the joint quark-antiquark distribution into thermal-thermal (TT), thermal-shower (TS), and shower-shower (SS) components. They fit the soft parton parameters C and T to low-pT PHENIX π+ data at η=0 for d+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV, then compute the full pion spectrum from the sum of all three components. The hard parton distributions are taken from a generic parameterization based on pQCD with EKS98 nuclear PDFs and a K-factor of 2.5.

## Key numbers

- Soft parton inverse slope T = 0.21 GeV/c, normalization C = 12 (GeV/c)^(-1) for 0-20% centrality d+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV
- Intrinsic parton transverse momentum width ⟨k_T^2⟩ = 0.09 (GeV/c)^2, compared to >1 (GeV/c)^2 needed in fragmentation models
- Soft pion component mean ⟨p_T⟩ = 0.42 GeV/c and ⟨p_T^2⟩ = 0.26 (GeV/c)^2
- Minimum hard parton momentum k_0 = 3 GeV/c for pQCD validity
- Inelastic cross section σ_in = 40.3 mb used for normalization
- C values for other centralities: 11 (20-40%), 7.8 (40-60%), 5.65 (60-90%) (GeV/c)^(-1)

## Context

This work directly contradicts the conventional initial-state broadening explanation of the Cronin effect, which had been the standard approach since the original observation by Cronin and collaborators. It extends the recombination model previously applied to Au+Au collisions by Hwa, Yang, and collaborators (2003-2004) to the d+Au system, showing that thermal-shower recombination is sensitive to soft parton density even in cold nuclear matter. The result enables quantitative predictions for the centrality and rapidity dependence of the Cronin effect, and naturally explains the larger effect for protons than pions observed by PHENIX, which is difficult to interpret in fragmentation models.
