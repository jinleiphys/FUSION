# Competing shape evolution, crossing configurations and single particle levels in nuclei

> The evolution of shape in the even-even zirconium (Zr) isotopes has been the subject of study for many years. However, the odd-mass isotopes have not been investigated as extensively due to limited experimental accessibility and computational challenges. This work, employing the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing, examines the effect of rapid shape evolution and normal-intruder configuration crossing -- both identified as quantum phase transitions -- alongside evolution in single particle energies, on the positive-parity spectrum of odd-mass $^\text{93-103}$Zr isotopes. Calculated energy levels, magnetic moments, $B(E2)$ values, and quadrupole moments are compared to experimental data, showing good agreement. The special case of $^{99}$Zr, which lies near the critical point of both quantum phase transitions, is also addressed, offering a new interpretation to the $7/2^+_1$ isomeric state and the occurrence of the type II shell evolution, in light of recent debates.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the rapid evolution of shapes, normal-intruder configuration crossing (both identified as quantum phase transitions), and single quasi-particle energies collectively determine the positive-parity spectrum of odd-mass ⁹³⁻¹⁰³Zr isotopes, and that type II shell evolution occurs in effective single particle energies but not in the bare single particle orbitals themselves, contrary to some prior claims.

## Method

The interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM) was employed, coupling an even-even boson core (described by the interacting boson model with normal 0p-0h and intruder 2p-2h configurations) to a valence neutron fermion. For ⁹³⁻⁹⁵Zr, valence neutrons were assigned to the ν1d₅/₂ orbital with fixed occupations; for ⁹⁷⁻¹⁰³Zr, BCS calculations determined single quasi-particle energies and occupation probabilities across the ν2s₁/₂, ν1d₃/₂, ν0g₇/₂, and ν0h₁₁/₂ orbitals. The Hamiltonian included boson, fermion, and Bose-Fermi terms with configuration-dependent parameters, and wave functions were analyzed for configuration content, single-particle occupation, and n_d boson number (deformation indicator).

## Context

This work extends the intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs) framework previously established for even-even Zr isotopes (Gavrielov 2019, 2020, 2022) and odd-mass Nb isotopes (Gavrielov 2022c, 2023a) to the odd-mass Zr chain. It directly addresses a debate between Boulay (2020), who proposed type II shell evolution lowering the ν2s₁/₂ single particle orbital in ⁹⁹Zr, and Garrett (2021), who argued against such a change. The paper resolves this by showing that configuration mixing and shape evolution, rather than bare single particle energy shifts, explain the ⁹⁹Zr spectrum, and it enables future experimental and theoretical work to disentangle shape, configuration, and single particle effects in odd-mass nuclei.
