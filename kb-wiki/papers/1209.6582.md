# Microscopic Optical Potentials for Helium-6 Scattering off Protons

> The differential cross section and the analyzing power are calculated for elastic scattering of $^6$He from a proton target using a microscopic folding optical potential, in which the $^6$He nucleus is described in terms of a $^4$He-core with two additional neutrons in the valence p-shell. In contrast to previous work of that nature, all contributions from the interaction of the valence neutrons with the target protons are taken into account.

## Key claim

The authors show that including the full spin-dependent contributions from the valence neutrons in the p-shell of $^6$He (beyond the standard central and spin-orbit terms used for closed-shell nuclei) produces only small changes to the differential cross section and analyzing power at 71 and 200 MeV/nucleon, and that these contributions are energy-dependent and partially cancel at the higher energy.

## Method

A first-order Watson optical potential was constructed by folding a fully-off-shell nucleon-nucleon t-matrix (from the Cd-Bonn potential) with a single-particle density matrix for $^6$He, modeled as a $^4$He core (closed s-shell) plus two valence neutrons in the $p_{3/2}$ shell described by harmonic oscillator wave functions. The expectation values of the spin-momentum operators for the valence neutrons were computed explicitly, and the contributions from all six Wolfenstein amplitudes (A, C, M, G, H, D) were evaluated, with the integrals involving (G+H) and D found to vanish for the valence neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator constant for s-shell: $\nu_s = 0.355$ fm$^{-2}$, corresponding to a $^6$He charge radius of 2.054 fm
- Oscillator constant for p-shell: $\nu_p = 0.3225$ fm$^{-2}$, corresponding to a matter radius of 2.32 fm
- Calculations performed at projectile laboratory energies of 71 MeV/nucleon and 200 MeV/nucleon
- At 71 MeV/nucleon, the valence neutron contribution to the central term is negligible, while the contribution from the M amplitude to the spin-orbit term is visible
- At 200 MeV/nucleon, the valence neutron contributions to the central and spin-orbit terms are of the same size but act in opposite directions, so the net contribution vanishes

## Context

This work extends previous folding model calculations for $^6$He scattering, which had failed to reproduce the negative analyzing power around 50 degrees observed experimentally by Sakaguchi (2011) at 71 MeV/nucleon. The authors had previously investigated a cluster description of $^6$He (Weppner, 2011) where the internal dynamics of the alpha-core plus two neutrons were treated explicitly, finding sensitivity of the analyzing power to cluster dynamics. The present approach complements work by Kaki (2012) using the Glauber approximation with sophisticated $^6$He wave functions, and the authors conclude that a microscopic understanding requires treating reaction dynamics and nuclear structure at an equal level of sophistication.
