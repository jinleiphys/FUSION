# Three-dimensional sizes and shapes of pion emission in heavy-ion collisions

> In the era of precision measurements in high-energy heavy-ion physics, there is an increasing expectation towards phenomenological and theoretical studies to provide a better description of data. In recent years, multiple experiments have confirmed through two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation measurements that the shape of the two-pion pair source can be well described by Levy-stable distributions. However, direct comparisons of new phenomenological results with the data are still needed to understand the underlying phenomena and learn more about the nature of pion emission. In this paper, we present a three-dimensional analysis of the two-pion source in Monte-Carlo simulations of Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon collision energy, and discuss a detailed comparison with the most recent centrality-dependent measurements from the PHENIX Collaboration.

## Key claim
The EPOS3 Monte-Carlo model reproduces the PHENIX data on pion source shapes and sizes for peripheral Au+Au collisions, but an increasing discrepancy is found for central collisions, particularly in the Levy index and the absolute correlation strength, while the scaled correlation strength is well described across all centralities.

## Method
The authors simulated 300,000 minimum-bias Au+Au events at 200 GeV per nucleon with EPOS3 (version 359), extracted freeze-out coordinates of identical charged pion pairs, and constructed three-dimensional pair source distributions in the out-side-longitudinal coordinate system. They fitted one-dimensional Levy-stable distributions simultaneously to the three projections in 11 transverse momentum bins and six centrality classes, then compared the extracted Levy index, Levy scale parameters, and correlation strength to PHENIX measurements.

## Key numbers
- Simulation: 300,000 minimum-bias Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with EPOS3 (version 359)
- Transverse momentum selection: 0.15 < p_T (GeV/c) < 1.0, pseudorapidity |eta| < 1
- 11 k_T classes: 50 MeV/c wide, ranging from 0.175 GeV/c to 0.725 GeV/c
- 6 centrality classes: 10% wide, between 0-60%
- Relative momentum selection: Q_LCMS < sqrt(0.15 GeV * m_T)
- Fit range upper limit: rho^fit_max = sqrt(2500/m_T) fm, with B = 2500 fm^2*GeV/c^2 (default)
- Normalization limit rho^lambda_max: varied from 1 mm to 50 cm
- Systematic uncertainty from centrality definition: < 1% change in parameters
- Variance within event blocks: 1-1.5% for central collisions, 0.5-1% for peripheral collisions

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological study of Kincses (2024) which showed that Levy-walk of hadrons during hadronic rescattering explains the long tail in pion pair sources, using the same EPOS3 model. It provides a direct comparison with the recent centrality-dependent PHENIX measurements (2024) of two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations. The results enable future investigations into whether Coulomb scattering, in-medium mass modifications, or additional hadronic dynamics are responsible for the discrepancies in central collisions, and demonstrate that the scaled correlation strength data do not require in-medium modifications as previously suggested by PHENIX (2024).
