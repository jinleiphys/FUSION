# Structure of A=10-13 nuclei with two- plus three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory

> Properties of finite nuclei are evaluated with two-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (NNN) interactions derived within chiral effective field theory (EFT). The nuclear Hamiltonian is fixed by properties of the A=2 system, except for two low-energy constants (LECs) that parameterize the short range NNN interaction. We constrain those two LECs by a fit to the A=3 system binding energy and investigate sensitivity of 4He, 6Li, 10,11B and 12,13C properties to the variation of the constrained LECs. We identify a preferred choice that gives globally the best description. We demonstrate that the NNN interaction terms significantly improve the binding energies and spectra of mid-p-shell nuclei not just with the preferred choice of the LECs but even within a wide range of the constrained LECs. At the same time, we find that a very high quality description of these nuclei requires further improvements to the chiral Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The chiral two-nucleon (NN) plus three-nucleon (NNN) interaction, with the two unknown low-energy constants (LECs) of the NNN contact terms constrained solely by the A=3 binding energies, significantly improves binding energies and spectra of mid-p-shell nuclei (A=10-13) over the NN-only interaction, but the description is not yet of very high quality and requires further improvements to the chiral Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors used the no-core shell model (NCSM) to solve the many-body Schrodinger equation for nuclei from A=3 to A=13, employing a chiral NN interaction at N3LO and a chiral NNN interaction at N2LO. The two unknown LECs (c_D and c_E) of the NNN contact terms were constrained by fitting to the binding energies of 3H and 3He, and the sensitivity of properties of 4He, 6Li, 10,11B, and 12,13C to variations of these constrained LECs was investigated. A regulator depending on momentum transfer was introduced in the NNN terms, making the chiral NNN interaction local, and the preferred LEC values were selected by comparing calculated observables to experiment across multiple nuclei.

## Key numbers

- 3H binding energy: 8.482 MeV; 3He binding energy: 7.718 MeV (used to constrain c_D and c_E LECs)
- Preferred LEC values: c_D = -1, c_E = -0.346 (selected as giving the best overall description)
- 6Li ground-state energy: 32.63 MeV with NN+NNN (experiment: 31.995 MeV); with NN only: 28.98 MeV
- 10B ground-state energy: 64.78 MeV with NN+NNN (experiment: 64.751 MeV); with NN only: 56.11 MeV
- 11B ground-state energy: 77.52 MeV with NN+NNN (experiment: 76.205 MeV); with NN only: 67.29 MeV
- 12C ground-state energy: 95.57 MeV with NN+NNN (experiment: 92.162 MeV); with NN only: 84.76 MeV
- 13C ground-state energy: 103.23 MeV with NN+NNN (experiment: 97.108 MeV); with NN only: 90.31 MeV
- Total energy rms deviation for 28 excited states: 1.314 MeV with NN+NNN; 1.671 MeV with NN only
- 6Li quadrupole moment Q(1_1+ 0): -0.124 e fm^2 with NN+NNN (experiment: -0.082(2) e fm^2)
- 12C B(M1; 1+1 -> 0+0): 0.913 mu_N^2 with NN+NNN (experiment: 0.951(20) mu_N^2); with NN only: 0.353 mu_N^2

## Context

This work extends the first NCSM application of chiral NN+NNN interactions by Nogga (2006) on 7Li, introducing a local chiral NNN interaction via a momentum-transfer regulator and using the A=3 binding energies rather than the 4He binding energy as the second constraint on the LECs. The results demonstrate that chiral NNN interactions significantly improve the description of mid-p-shell nuclei compared to NN-only calculations, but the over-correction seen in some states (e.g., the 1+0 and 4+0 states in 12C) indicates that sub-leading NNN interactions and four-nucleon interactions at N3LO (Epelbaum 2006) will be needed, along with larger basis spaces up to N_max=8 for A>6.
