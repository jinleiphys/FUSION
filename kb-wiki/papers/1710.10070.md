# Color dipole predictions for the exclusive vector meson photoproduction in $pp/pPb/PbPb$ collisions at Run 2 LHC energies

> In this paper we present a comprehensive analysis of exclusive vector meson photoproduction in $pp$, $pPb$ and $PbPb$ collisions at Run 2 LHC energies using the Color Dipole formalism. The rapidity distributions and total cross sections for the $ρ$, $ϕ$, $J/Ψ$, $Ψ(2S)$ and $Υ$ production are estimated considering the more recent phenomenological models for the dipole - proton scattering amplitude, which are based on the Color Glass Condensate formalism and are able to describe the inclusive and exclusive $ep$ HERA data. Moreover, we also discuss the impact of the modelling of the vector meson wave functions on the predictions. The current theoretical uncertainty in the Color Dipole predictions is estimated and a comparison with the experimental results is performed.

## Key claim
The Color Dipole formalism, using three phenomenological models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude (IIM, bCGC, IP-SAT) that describe HERA data, can predict exclusive vector meson photoproduction in pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions at Run 2 LHC energies, but the theoretical uncertainty is large (up to a factor 3 for light mesons) and depends on the vector meson wave function model and the dipole size probed.

## Method
The authors computed rapidity distributions and total cross sections for exclusive photoproduction of rho, phi, J/psi, psi(2S), and Upsilon mesons using the Color Dipole formalism with the equivalent photon approximation. They combined three dipole-proton scattering amplitude models (IIM, bCGC, IP-SAT) with two vector meson wave function models (Boosted Gaussian and Gaus-LC), and extended the proton amplitude to nuclear targets using a Glauber-Gribov approach with a Fermi distribution for the nuclear density.

## Key numbers
- For rho production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, the IIM prediction is smaller than the IP-SAT and bCGC predictions by a factor of 2 at midrapidity.
- The theoretical uncertainty in the rapidity distributions for light vector mesons at Y=0 in pp collisions reaches a factor of 3.
- For pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.1 TeV, the rapidity distribution is asymmetric due to the Z^2 enhancement of the nuclear photon spectrum.
- The maximum gamma-hadron center-of-mass energy reached at the LHC is W ~ 900 to 8000 GeV, compared to W_max ~ 200 GeV at HERA.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of exclusive vector meson photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions (Klein, 1999; Strikman, 2001; Goncalves and Machado, 2008) by providing predictions for Run 2 LHC energies using updated dipole models fitted to high-precision HERA data (Rezaeian, 2013; IP-SAT update, 2015). The analysis enables future global fits to discriminate between QCD dynamics models (IIM, bCGC, IP-SAT) and vector meson wave function models, and to quantify the need for additional corrections such as survival gap factors, next-to-leading order effects, and nuclear shadowing.
