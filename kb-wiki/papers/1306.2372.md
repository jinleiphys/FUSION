# The statistical multifragmentation model for liquid-gas phase transition with a compressible nuclear liquid

> We propose a new formulation of the statistical multifragmentation model based on the analysis of the virial expansion for a system of the nuclear fragments of all sizes. The developed model not only enables us to account for short-range repulsion, but also to calculate the surface free energy which is induced by the interaction between the fragments. We propose a new parameterization for the liquid phase pressure which allows us to introduce a compressible nuclear liquid into the statistical multifragmentation model. The resulting model is exactly solvable and has no irregular behavior of the isotherms in the mixed phase region that is typical for mean-field models. The general conditions for the 1-st and 2-nd (or higher) order phase transitions are formulated. It is shown that all endpoints of the present model phase diagram are the tricritical points, if the Fisher exponent $τ$ is in the range $\{3}{2} \le τ\le 2$. The treatment of nuclear liquid compressibility allows us to reduce the tricritical endpoint density of the statistical multifragmentation model to one third of the normal nuclear density. A specific attention is paid to of the fragment size distributions in the region of a negative surface tension at supercritical temperatures.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new formulation of the statistical multifragmentation model that introduces a compressible nuclear liquid, which reduces the tricritical endpoint density to one third of normal nuclear density, but only if the Fisher exponent τ is in the range 3/2 ≤ τ ≤ 2 and the parameter α > 1 to avoid unphysical behavior.

## Method

The authors derive a system of equations for the gaseous phase pressure and the induced surface tension coefficient from a virial expansion of a multicomponent system of nuclear fragments with hard-core repulsion, using a Van der Waals extrapolation to high densities. They introduce a new parameterization for the liquid phase pressure that depends on chemical potential raised to a power ν = 2, 3, or 4, allowing for non-zero isothermal compressibility, and analyze the conditions for first and second order phase transitions by studying the behavior of dimensionless variables ξ₁ and ξ₂ at the phase coexistence curve.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, used as the reference density for fragment volumes V₁ = 1/ρ₀
- Fisher exponent τ = 1.9 used in simulations, motivated by experimental data from ISIS and EOS collaborations
- Bulk binding energy per nucleon at vanishing temperature W₀ = 16 MeV
- Fermi-gas parameter ε₀ = 16 MeV for the temperature-dependent binding energy term W_Fm(T) = T²/ε₀
- Tricritical endpoint density reduced to one third of normal nuclear density (ρ ≈ ρ₀/3) when nuclear liquid compressibility is included
- The model predicts second order phase transitions for 3/2 ≤ τ ≤ 2, and higher order transitions for τ < 3/2

## Context

This work extends the simplified statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) originally developed by Bondorf (1995) and solved analytically by Bugaev (2000, 2000b), which treated nuclear fragments as incompressible objects. The authors address the conceptual problem that the standard SMM predicts critical and tricritical endpoints at the liquid phase density ρ = ρ₀, whereas ordinary substances have critical densities about one third of that value, as noted by Stanley (1971), Dillmann (1991), and LFK (1994). The new formulation enables more realistic modeling of nuclear matter at higher temperatures and densities relevant for heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical applications, and provides a framework for studying fragment size distributions at supercritical temperatures where surface tension becomes negative.
