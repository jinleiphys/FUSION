# Quarkonium formation time in quark-gluon plasma

> The quarkonium formation time in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is determined from the space-time correlator of heavy quark vector currents using the quarkonium in-medium mass and wave function obtained from heavy quark potentials extracted from the lattice QCD. It is found that the formation time of a quarkonium increases with the temperature of the QGP and diverges near its dissociation temperature. Also, the quarkonium formation time is longer if the heavy quark potential is taken to be the free energy from lattice calculations for a heavy quark pair, compared to that based on the more negative internal energy.

## Key claim

The formation time of a quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma increases with temperature and diverges near its dissociation temperature; the formation time is longer when the heavy quark potential is taken as the free energy from lattice QCD rather than the more attractive internal energy, with the actual values depending on which potential is used.

## Method

The authors computed the quarkonium formation time from the space-time correlator of heavy quark vector currents, using the dispersion relation to relate the real part of the heavy quark pair polarization function to its imaginary part (the spectral function). The in-medium masses and wave functions of bottomonia were obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with heavy quark potentials (free energy or internal energy) extracted from lattice QCD calculations, and the dielectron decay widths were calculated from the wave function at the origin. The formation time distribution was then derived from the ratio of the resonance contribution to the total correlator excluding lower-mass resonances.

## Key numbers

- Average formation time of $\Upsilon(1S)$ at $T=T_c$: 0.44 fm/$c$ for the free energy potential and 0.3 fm/$c$ for the internal energy potential.
- For comparison, the formation time of $\Upsilon(1S)$ in free space from prior work (Kharzeev 1999) is 0.32 fm/$c$.
- Bottom quark mass used: $m_b = 4.65$ GeV.
- The threshold energy for the continuum contribution was taken as $\sqrt{s_{\rm th}} = 2\widetilde{m}_Q$, where $\widetilde{m}_Q = m_Q + V(r=\infty, T)/2$.

## Context

This work extends the space-time correlator method of Kharzeev (1999) for quarkonium formation time in vacuum to the case of a quark-gluon plasma, using in-medium properties from lattice QCD potentials. The results are consistent with the earlier observation by Karsch (1987) that formation time scales with the quarkonium radius. The findings enable more accurate modeling of quarkonium production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as the longer formation time at high temperature means medium effects on the heavy quark pair must be treated before the bound state forms.
