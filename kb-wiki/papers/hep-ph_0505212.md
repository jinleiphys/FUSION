# The Fate of Non-Abelian Plasma Instabilities in 3+1 Dimensions

> Plasma instabilities can play a fundamental role in plasma equilibration. There are similarities and differences between plasma instabilities in abelian and non-abelian gauge theories. In particular, it has been an open question whether non-abelian self-interactions are the limiting factor in the growth of non-abelian plasma instabilities. We study this problem with 3+1 dimensional numerical simulations. We find that non-abelian plasma instabilities behave very differently from abelian ones once they grow to be non-perturbatively large, in contrast with earlier results of 1+1 dimensional simulations. In particular, they grow more slowly at late times, with linear rather than exponential dependence of magnetic energy on time.

## Key claim

Non-abelian plasma instabilities in 3+1 dimensions do not continue to grow exponentially once they reach the non-abelian scale where self-interactions become important; instead, the magnetic energy eventually grows only linearly with time, in contrast to earlier 1+1 dimensional simulations which showed resumed exponential growth.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional numerical simulations of the hard-loop effective theory for SU(2) gauge theory coupled to an anisotropic hard-particle distribution, discretizing velocity space via spherical harmonic expansion up to lmax = 24. They initialized the gauge fields with Gaussian noise having an exponential momentum cutoff at k0 = 2 m_infinity, set the electric fields and W fields to zero initially, and evolved the system in A0 = 0 gauge using the same algorithm as Bodeker, Moore, and Rummukainen (2000) for the isotropic case, with the addition of anisotropic source terms.

## Key numbers

- The dominant Weibel instability has wavenumber k = 0.827 m_infinity and growth rate gamma = 0.273 m_infinity for the chosen hard-particle distribution.
- The 3+1 dimensional simulation used lattice spacing a = 0.25 m_infinity^{-1}, volume L^3 = (64 a)^3 = (16 m_infinity^{-1})^3, and initial amplitude Delta = 0.02 m_infinity^{-1/2}.
- The 1+1 dimensional comparison simulation used length L = 8192 a = 2048 m_infinity^{-1} and Delta = 0.014 m_infinity^{+1/2}.
- In the linear growth regime, the ratio of electric to magnetic energy approaches unity, and the ratio of B_z^2 to total B^2 levels out near 0.29.

## Context

This work extends the 1+1 dimensional studies of Rebhan, Romatschke, and Strickland (2005) and the earlier toy model of Arnold and Lenaghan (2002), which had suggested that non-abelian fields would abelianize and resume exponential growth beyond the non-abelian scale. The 3+1 dimensional results contradict those expectations, showing that non-abelian self-interactions limit growth more severely than in lower dimensions. The findings are relevant for understanding plasma equilibration in quark-gluon plasmas produced in heavy-ion collisions, particularly in the context of the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier et al. (2001) and the instability-driven isotropization scenario of Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore (2003).
