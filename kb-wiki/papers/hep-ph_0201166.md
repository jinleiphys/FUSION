# Possible Evidence of Disoriented Chiral Condensates from the Anomaly in Omega and AntiOmega Abundances at the SPS

> No conventional picture of nucleus-nucleus collisions has yet been able to explain the abundance of Omega and AntiOmega in central collisions between Pb nuclei at 158 A GeV at the CERN SPS. We argue that such a deviation from predictions of statistical thermal models and numerical simulations is evidence that they are produced as topological defects in the form of skyrmions arising from the formation of disoriented chiral condensates. The estimated domain size falls in the right range to be consistent with the so far non-observation of DCC from the distribution of neutral pions.

# Possible Evidence of Disoriented Chiral Condensates from the Anomaly in Omega and AntiOmega Abundances at the SPS

## Key claim

The observed excess of Omega and AntiOmega yields in central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV at the CERN SPS, which cannot be explained by statistical thermal models or numerical simulations, may be evidence that these hyperons are produced as skyrmions (topological defects) arising from disoriented chiral condensates (DCC), provided that the DCC domain size is small enough (approximately 2 fm) to avoid detection via the neutral pion ratio method.

## Method

The authors estimate the number of Omega and AntiOmega produced per central collision by extrapolating WA97 measurements in a narrow rapidity window to 4π using NA49 Xi data and the ratio of Omega to Xi yields from WA97. They then apply a known probability per unit volume for forming non-trivial chiral field topology (0.04 ξ⁻³) from the linear sigma model, assuming SU(2) ≃ SU(3) flavor symmetry, to estimate the DCC domain size ξ required to produce the observed yield. Finally, they compute destruction time-scales for Omega via collisions with pions and kaons using thermal rates from the literature.

## Key numbers

- WA97 measured Omega + AntiOmega yield = 0.410 ± 0.08 in Δy = 1 around y = 0, with AntiOmega/Omega ratio = 0.383 ± 0.081
- Extrapolated to 4π, the average AntiOmega yield per central collision is 0.498
- Estimated DCC domain size ξ ∼ 2 fm, consistent with theoretical expectations of 1.5 fm (thermal equilibrium scenario) and 3–4 fm (annealing scenario)
- Destruction time-scale for Omega by pion collisions at T = 200 MeV is τ_π ∼ 257 fm; by kaon collisions τ_K ∼ 152 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of disoriented chiral condensates (originally introduced by Anselm (1988), Bjorken (1992), and others) by proposing a new observational signature via strange hyperons rather than pions. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between statistical model fits and measured Omega yields at the SPS, previously noted by Rafelski (1999) and others, and the failure of numerical models such as UrQMD to reproduce these yields without parameter adjustment. The result enables future experimental searches for DCC through hyperon production at low transverse momentum, complementing the existing pion-based search strategy that has so far yielded null results.
