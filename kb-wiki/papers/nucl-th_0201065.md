# On the Fermi and Gamow-Teller strength distribution in medium-heavy mass nuclei

> An isospin-selfconsistent approach based on the Continuum-Random-Phase-Approximation (CRPA) is applied to describe the Fermi and Gamow-Teller strength distributions within a wide excitation-energy interval. To take into account nucleon pairing in open-shell nuclei, we formulate an isospin-selfconsistent version of the proton-neutron-quasiparticle-CRPA (pn-QCRPA) approach by incorporating the BCS model into the CRPA method. The isospin and configurational splittings of the Gamow-Teller giant resonance are analyzed in single-open-shell nuclei. The calculation results obtained for $^{208}$Bi, $^{90}$Nb, and Sb isotopes are compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors present an isospin-selfconsistent Continuum-Random-Phase-Approximation (CRPA) approach, extended to include nucleon pairing via BCS theory for open-shell nuclei, to describe Fermi and Gamow-Teller strength distributions over a wide excitation-energy interval; however, the analysis is restricted to the particle-hole subspace and does not address the influence of 2p-2h configurations or the quenching effect, with the coupling to many-quasiparticle configurations simulated only via an appropriate smearing parameter.

## Method

The authors formulate an isospin-selfconsistent version of the proton-neutron-quasiparticle-CRPA (pn-QCRPA) by incorporating the BCS model into the CRPA method, using a model Hamiltonian with Landau-Migdal forces for the particle-hole interaction and a separable particle-particle interaction for nucleon pairing. The strength functions are calculated via inhomogeneous pn-QRPA equations in coordinate-space representation, using the full basis of single-particle states for the particle-hole channel. For single-open-shell nuclei with only proton pairing, the system reduces to equations for two radial effective operators. The isospin splitting of the Gamow-Teller giant resonance is analyzed by projecting RPA states onto states with definite isospin, using the strength function of the isovector M1 giant resonance in the parent nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the isospin-selfconsistent CRPA approach previously developed by Moukhai et al. (1999) and Rodin et al. (2001), which focused on direct-decay properties of giant resonances, to describe Fermi and Gamow-Teller strength distributions in both closed-shell and open-shell nuclei. It addresses shortcomings of earlier RPA-based studies by Auerbach and Klein (1984) and Van Giai et al. (1998), which did not treat the high-energy region of the isovector spin-monopole giant resonance, effects of nucleon pairing, or isospin splitting of the GTR. The results are compared with experimental data for 208Bi, 90Nb, and Sb isotopes from charge-exchange reaction studies by Jänecke et al. (1993) and Krasznahorkay et al. (2001), and the approach enables future inclusion of the spin-quadrupole part of the particle-hole interaction and a consistent treatment of the continuum for describing both low-energy beta-decay and high-energy giant resonance regions.
