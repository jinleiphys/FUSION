# Molecular Structure with Exotic Clusters in light Neutron-rich Nuclei

> The excited states of Be-12, Be-14 and B-15 have been studied with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. In the predicted excited states we find novel molecule-like structures with very exotic clusters such as 6He+8He in Be-14 and 6He+9Li in B-15. The origin of the He-6 cluster development in the very neutron-rich nuclei is understood by the new-type correlation among 4 neutrons and 2 protons. In this paper we also present our recent challenge to study $sd$-shell nuclei. Shape coexistence problems in Ar-36 and Ca-40 are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that novel molecule-like structures with exotic clusters (such as ⁶He+⁸He in ¹⁴Be and ⁶He+⁹Li in ¹⁵B) appear in excited states of neutron-rich nuclei, but these predictions are obtained within a bound-state approximation that does not fully account for particle decay widths.

## Method

The author applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) to calculate the ground and excited states of ¹²Be, ¹⁴Be, and ¹⁵B. For the heavier sd-shell nuclei ³⁶Ar and ⁴⁰Ca, variation after parity projection with a constraint on total oscillator quanta was used instead of full VAP to save computational time. The interactions employed were the modified Volkov No.1 central force (case 3), the G3RS spin-orbit force, and the Coulomb force, with a Majorana parameter m=0.65 and G3RS strength u₁=-u₂=3700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ¹²Be: 61.9 MeV (theoretical) vs 68.65 MeV (experimental)
- Binding energy of ¹⁴Be: 59.7 MeV (theoretical with m=0.65) and 67.7 MeV (with m=0.61) vs 69.77 MeV (experimental)
- Binding energy of ¹⁵B: 73.1 MeV (theoretical) vs 88.19 MeV (experimental)
- Excitation energy of 0⁺₂ in ¹⁴Be: 4.2 MeV (calculated with m=0.61)
- Deformation parameter β=0.49 for the 0⁺₁ state and β=0.64 for the 0⁺₂ state in ¹⁴Be
- Theoretical B(GT) value for ¹²Be(0⁺₁) to ¹²B(1⁺): 0.8, compared to experimental B(GT)=0.59
- Partial decay widths of 6⁺₂ and 8⁺₂ states in ¹⁴Be for the ⁸He+⁶He channel: less than 50 keV
- Deformation parameter β∼0.4 for the super deformed state in ⁴⁰Ca
- Excitation energy of the 1⁻ band head in the parity doublet of the super deformed band in ⁴⁰Ca: 12 MeV, about 3 MeV higher than the 0⁺ state in the super deformation band

## Context

This work extends earlier AMD studies of clustering in ¹⁰Be and ¹²C by Kanada-En'yo and collaborators, and it provides the first full microscopic calculation that systematically reproduces energy levels from the ground state to highly excited states in ¹²Be. The predicted exotic clustering structures (⁶He+⁸He in ¹⁴Be and ⁶He+⁹Li in ¹⁵B) suggest that such configurations may be common in neutron-rich nuclei, and the shape coexistence results for ³⁶Ar and ⁴⁰Ca connect to recent experimental gamma-ray measurements by Svensson (2001) and Ideguchi (2001). The work enables future studies of whether similar intruder states and clustering appear in neutron-rich sd-shell nuclei such as ³²Mg.
