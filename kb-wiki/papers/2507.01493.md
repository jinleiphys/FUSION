# Probing the tetrahedral $α$ clusters in relativistic $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O collisions

> Relativistic $^{16}$O +$^{16}$O collisions probe the Quark-Gluon Plasma formed in small systems, while their collective phenomena illuminate the structure of $^{16}$O. Recently, various configurations of $^{16}$O from \textit{ab initio} calculations were implemented in heavy-ion models, such as the hydrodynamic model and a multiphase transport model (AMPT) to study cluster effects in relativistic $^{16}$O +$^{16}$O collisions. However, divergent predictions across configurations and models complicate interpretations. In this Letter, we isolate the impact of multi-nucleon correlations in relativistic $^{16}$O +$^{16}$O collisions while fixing the one-body density distribution of $^{16}$O. Our results show that the normalized ratios ${\rm Norm}(v_{2}\{2\}/v_{2}\{4\})$ and ${\rm Norm}(v_{2}\{2\}/v_{3}\{2\})$ effectively probe the effects of one-body density (e.g., tetrahedral symmetry) and multi-nucleon correlations (e.g., $α$ clusters). These observables provide critical constraints for refining heavy-ion models, essential for investigating cluster configurations in light nuclei through relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the normalized ratios Norm(v₂{2}/v₂{4}) and Norm(v₂{2}/v₃{2}) can separately probe one-body density effects (e.g., tetrahedral symmetry) and multi-nucleon correlations (e.g., α clusters) in relativistic ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions, but only if the heavy-ion model's initial-state fluctuations are first controlled or calibrated.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber and TRENTo initial-state simulations of ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions at √sNN = 7 TeV, using Skyrme-DFT density profiles with (Q̂₃₂ = 40 fm³, β₃₂ = 0.339) and without tetrahedral symmetry. They introduced an effective compactness parameter χ to control α-cluster correlations while keeping the one-body density fixed, and computed the normalized ratios of initial spatial eccentricities ε₂{2}/ε₂{4} and ε₂{2}/ε₃{2}. Hydrodynamic validation was performed with the iEBE-VISHNU model for VMC densities.

## Key numbers

- The tetrahedral configuration has octupole deformation β₃₂ = 0.339 with Q̂₃₂ = 40 fm³, identified as the ground state of ¹⁶O from symmetry-restored Skyrme-DFT calculations.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section was set to σNN = 70.9 mb with nucleon width w = 0.4 fm.
- For χ varying from 0 to 2, the RMS of a cluster in oxygen ranges from 2.37 fm to 1.60 fm for the Q̂₃₂ = 40 fm³ case, and from 2.25 fm to 1.62 fm for the spherical case.
- The TRENTo fluctuation parameter k is approximately 1 in default simulations; disabling these fluctuations (k → ∞) makes TRENTo results consistent with MC Glauber predictions.
- Hydrodynamic simulations used 10M events of ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions at √sNN = 7 TeV with VMC densities and 10 oversamplings of UrQMD afterburner per event.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to extract nuclear structure from relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Li 2019, Xu 2021, Zhang 2021, Ryssens 2023, STAR 2024, Giacalone 2024) by addressing the problem that different ab initio configurations (VMC, NLEFT, EQMD) and different models (hydrodynamics, AMPT) give divergent predictions for cluster effects in ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions (Huang 2023). The paper resolves the ambiguity noted by Yuanyuan Wang (2024) regarding cluster compactness by fixing the one-body density while varying multi-nucleon correlations. The results enable experimental data from upcoming LHC and RHIC ¹⁶O+¹⁶O runs to constrain the tetrahedral structure of ¹⁶O, provided initial-state model uncertainties (particularly TRENTo gamma fluctuations) are first calibrated using Norm(v₂{2}/v₂{4}).
