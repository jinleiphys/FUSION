# Nonlinear relaxation field in charged systems under high electric fields

> The influence of an external electric field on the current in charged systems is investigated. The results from the classical hierarchy of density matrices are compared with the results from the quantum kinetic theory. The kinetic theory yields a systematic treatment of the nonlinear current beyond linear response. To this end the dynamically screened and field-dependent Lenard-Balescu equation is integrated analytically and the nonlinear relaxation field is calculated. The classical linear response result known as Debye - Onsager relaxation effect is only obtained if asymmetric screening is assumed. Considering the kinetic equation of one specie the other species have to be screened dynamically while the screening with the same specie itself has to be performed statically. Different other approximations are discussed and compared.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the nonlinear relaxation field in charged systems under high electric fields depends critically on whether the screening treatment is symmetric or asymmetric between species, and that the classical Debye-Onsager relaxation effect is recovered only when asymmetric screening is assumed, with the kinetic theory providing a systematic treatment beyond linear response.

## Method

The author integrates the dynamically screened and field-dependent Lenard-Balescu equation analytically, starting from the quantum kinetic theory formulated with gauge-invariant Green functions and the generalized Kadanoff-Baym ansatz. The results are compared with those from the classical BBGKY hierarchy under a local equilibrium approximation, both with and without background friction (mobility). The relaxation field is calculated for statically and dynamically screened approximations, and the analytical expressions are derived for the nonlinear current.

## Key numbers

- Debye limiting law: δE = E (1 − κ e² / (6 T)) for a single charge Z=1 (Equation labeled debye).
- Onsager correction: δE = E (1 − κ e² / (3 (2 + √2) T)) for Z=1 when ion dynamics are considered (Equation labeled ons).
- For Z=1 with background friction (hydrodynamical/Onsager result), the linear response relaxation field is δE/E = (κ e² / (3 T)) (Z q / (√q + 1)) with q = (b_e + Z b_i) / ((1+Z)(b_e + b_i)) (Equation erg1).
- For Z=1 without background friction (symmetric treatment), the linear response relaxation field is δE/E = − (e² κ_e) / (6 T) × 2 + o(E) (Equation erg2).
- The nonlinear Onsager result for Z=1 includes the function F_H(α) with α = eE/(T κ_e), giving δE = − (e² κ_e) / (3(1+√2) T) E F_H(α) (Equation 9).
- The spectral function for free particles in a uniform electric field is A₀(k,ω) = (2π/ε_E) Ai((k²/2m − ℏω)/ε_E) with ε_E = (ℏ² e² E² / (8m))^(1/3) (Equation spectrall).

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate between the Debye (1923) and Onsager (1927) relaxation effects in electrolyte theory, which had been addressed in linear response by various authors (e.g., Ebeling et al. 1976, Röpke 1988, Redmer 1998) and in kinetic theory by Morawetz (1992, 1997). The paper resolves the confusion by showing that the symmetry breaking introduced by assuming background friction (mobility) in the classical BBGKY hierarchy leads to the Onsager result, while the symmetric treatment without friction yields a different result; the quantum kinetic theory provides a systematic framework to treat these cases and enables calculation of the nonlinear relaxation field beyond linear response for arbitrary field strengths.
