# Hadronization of heavy diquark and light quark within NJL-Model

> We present a path-integral hadronization for doubly heavy baryons. The two heavy quarks in the baryon are approximated as a scalar or axial-vector diquark described by a heavy diquark effective theory. The gluon dynamics are represented by a NJL-Model interaction for the heavy diquarks and light quarks, which leads to an effective action of the baryon fields after the quark and diquark fields are integrated out. This effective action for doubly heavy baryon includes the electromagnetic and electroweak interactions, as well as the interaction with light mesons. We also verify the Ward-Takahashi identity at the baryon level, obtain the Isgur-Wise function for weak transitions, and calculate the strong coupling constant of the doubly heavy baryon and pion. Numerical studies are also performed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a path-integral hadronization for doubly heavy baryons that treats the two heavy quarks as a scalar or axial-vector diquark, but the derivation is performed only at leading power in the heavy diquark limit (order 1/m_X), and the NJL-type interaction used to represent gluon dynamics is an approximation that avoids the full nonlinear gluon sector.

## Method

The authors construct a heavy diquark effective theory (HDiET) for scalar and axial-vector diquarks, then introduce an NJL-model type current-current interaction to represent gluon dynamics between the heavy diquark and light quark. They linearize the resulting four-fermion interactions by introducing auxiliary baryon fields (a spinor field T and a spinor-vector field H_mu) via Gaussian integration, then integrate out the light quark and diquark fields to obtain an effective action for the doubly heavy baryon fields. The baryon fields are renormalized using the proper-time method with a momentum cutoff, and the effective electromagnetic, electroweak, and strong (pion) interactions are derived from the resulting generating functional.

## Key numbers

- The lowest-lying doubly heavy baryon Xi_cc has mass m_{Xi_cc^{++}} = 3621.40 MeV, as observed by LHCb in 2017.
- The spatial size of the two heavy quarks in a doubly heavy baryon is at order r_{QQ} ~ 1/(m_Q v), while the distance between a heavy quark and the light quark is at order r_{Qq} ~ 1/Lambda_QCD, with the ratio r_{QQ}/r_{Qq} ~ 1/(m_Q v) << 1.
- The Isgur-Wise function xi(w) for weak transitions satisfies xi(1) = 1 and is given by xi(w) = I^{EW}(0,0,w)/I^{EW}(0,0), where I^{EW}(0,0,w) contains the function r(w) = log[sqrt(w^2-1)+w]/sqrt(w^2-1).
- The renormalization factor for the pion field is Z_Phi^{-1} = I_1^Phi = (N_c/(4 pi^2)) (m_q^2/F_pi^2) Gamma(0, m^2/Lambda^2).

## Context

This work extends the path-integral hadronization approach previously applied to mesons and baryons (Cahill 1988, Reinhardt 1989, Ebert 1992-1996, Abu-Raddad 2002) to the specific case of doubly heavy baryons, building on the heavy diquark effective theory developed by Shi (2020). The method enables the derivation of effective electromagnetic and electroweak interactions for doubly heavy baryons from a fundamental quark-diquark level, and the resulting Isgur-Wise function is shown to be proportional to a universal soft function that should equal that for singly heavy meson transitions (such as B to D) in the heavy quark limit. The approach enables future calculations of doubly heavy baryon properties including strong couplings with pions and weak transition form factors.
