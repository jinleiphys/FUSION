# Mott Insulators, No-Double-Occupancy, and Non-Abelian Superconductivity

> SU(4) dynamical symmetry is shown to imply a no-double-occupancy constraint on the minimal symmetry description of antiferromagnetism and d-wave superconductivity. This implies a maximum doping fraction of 1/4 for cuprates and provides a microscopic critique of the projected SO(5) model. We propose that SU(4) superconductors are representative of a class of compounds that we term non-abelian superconductors. We further suggest that non-abelian superconductors may exist having SU(4) symmetry and therefore cuprate-like dynamics, but without d-wave hybridization.

# Mott Insulators, No-Double-Occupancy, and Non-Abelian Superconductivity

## Key claim

SU(4) dynamical symmetry in cuprate superconductors automatically enforces a no-double-occupancy constraint, implying a maximum doping fraction of 1/4 and providing a microscopic justification for why the Zhang SO(5) model requires Gutzwiller projection, the projection arises because the SO(5) formulation treats order parameters as classical fields rather than as operators within the full SU(4) algebra.

## Method

The authors construct a U(4) ⊃ SU(4) dynamical symmetry model with 16 generators representing spin-singlet d-wave pairs, spin-triplet pairs, spin operators, and staggered magnetization operators. They transform these operators to coordinate space and demonstrate that closure of the SU(4) algebra under commutation requires a no-double-occupancy condition that separates lattice sites into even and odd sublattices. They then show that the full minimal algebra for cuprates is SO(8), which reduces to SU(4) when on-site Coulomb repulsion suppresses S and S* pairs that would occupy the same site.

## Key numbers

- Maximum doping fraction preserving SU(4) symmetry: P_f = 1/4 (derived from counting hole pairs, where maximum holes Omega = Omega_e/4 with Omega_e the total number of lattice sites)
- Empirical maximum doping fraction for cuprate superconductivity: 0.23 to 0.27, consistent with the SU(4) symmetry prediction
- Probability of double occupancy when two hole pairs overlap at adjacent sites: 1/16 (for pairs separated by 2a or 2b lattice vectors)

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior SU(4) model (Guidry 1999, Wu 2003) and provides a microscopic critique of the projected SO(5) model of Zhang (1997), which predicts no charge gap at half filling and requires Gutzwiller projection to recover Mott insulator behavior. The paper shows that the Zhang SO(5) algebra is a subalgebra of SU(4), and that closure of the Zhang algebra also implies no double occupancy. The results enable a unified symmetry description of antiferromagnetism and d-wave superconductivity without projection, and predict that non-abelian superconductors with SU(4) symmetry could exist with pairing structures other than d-wave (such as extended s-wave or p-wave), provided the no-double-occupancy constraint holds.
