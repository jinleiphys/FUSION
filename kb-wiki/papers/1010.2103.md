# Early onset of ground-state deformation in the neutron-deficient polonium isotopes

> In-source resonant ionization laser spectroscopy of the even-$A$ polonium isotopes $^{192-210,216,218}$Po has been performed using the $6p^37s$ $^5S_2$ to $6p^37p$ $^5P_2$ ($λ=843.38$ nm) transition in the polonium atom (Po-I) at the CERN ISOLDE facility. The comparison of the measured isotope shifts in $^{200-210}$Po with a previous data set allows to test for the first time recent large-scale atomic calculations that are essential to extract the changes in the mean-square charge radius of the atomic nucleus. When going to lighter masses, a surprisingly large and early departure from sphericity is observed, which is only partly reproduced by Beyond Mean Field calculations.

## Key claim

The ground states of neutron-deficient polonium isotopes show a surprisingly large and early departure from sphericity, starting from \({}^{198}\)Po, which is larger in magnitude and occurs at higher neutron numbers than in the \(Z \leq 82\) isotones; this deviation is only partly reproduced by Beyond Mean Field calculations.

## Method

In-source resonant ionization laser spectroscopy was performed on even-\(A\) polonium isotopes \({}^{192-210,216,218}\)Po using the \(6p^3 7s\; ^5S_2 \rightarrow 6p^3 7p\; ^5P_2\) transition at \(\lambda = 843.38\) nm at the CERN ISOLDE facility. Isotope shifts were measured by scanning the narrowband laser frequency and monitoring yields via \(\alpha\)-decay detection, \(\beta\)-decay counting, or Faraday cup collection. The measured shifts were combined with a previous data set (Kowalski 1991) in a King plot analysis to test large-scale Dirac-Coulomb-Breit relativistic configuration interaction atomic calculations, and the changes in mean-square charge radii were extracted using the calculated electronic factor \(F\) and specific mass shift \(M_{SMS}\).

## Key numbers

- Isotope shifts \(\delta\nu^{A,210}_{exp}\) for the 843.38 nm transition range from \(-11.524(125)\) GHz (\({}^{218}\)Po) to \(+6.697(185)\) GHz (\({}^{192}\)Po), relative to \({}^{210}\)Po.
- Changes in mean-square charge radius \(\delta\langle r^2\rangle^{A,210}_{exp}\) range from \(+0.958(10)\{7\}\) fm\(^2\) (\({}^{218}\)Po) to \(-0.537(13)\{22\}\) fm\(^2\) (\({}^{192}\)Po), relative to \({}^{210}\)Po.
- The calculated atomic electronic factor \(F\) for the 843.38 nm transition is \(-12.786\) GHz/fm\(^2\); for the 255.8 nm transition it is \(+28.363\) GHz/fm\(^2\).
- The calculated specific mass shift \(M_{SMS}\) for the 843.38 nm transition is \(-311\) GHz\(\cdot\)amu; for the 255.8 nm transition it is \(+51\) GHz\(\cdot\)amu.
- The deviation from sphericity becomes marked starting at \({}^{198}\)Po (\(N=114\)), with \(\delta\langle r^2\rangle^{198,210}_{exp} = -0.619(12)\{13\}\) fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends laser spectroscopy measurements of polonium isotopes beyond the previous study by Kowalski (1991), which had to rely on the Finite Range Droplet Model to extract charge radii. The new data test large-scale atomic calculations for the first time in polonium, enabling model-independent extraction of mean-square charge radii. The observed early onset of deformation contrasts with the robust sphericity of neutron-deficient lead isotopes (De Witte 2007, Seliverstov 2009) and differs from the behavior in mercury (Ulm 1986); the results enable future comparisons with Beyond Mean Field calculations and motivate extending studies to more neutron-deficient radon and radium isotopes.
