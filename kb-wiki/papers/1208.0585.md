# Meson vacuum phenomenology in a three-flavor linear sigma model with (axial-)vector mesons

> We study scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons with non-strange and strange quantum numbers in the framework of a linear sigma model with global chiral $U(N_f)_L \times U(N_f)_R$ symmetry. We perform a global fit of meson masses, decay widths, as well as decay amplitudes. The quality of the fit is, for a hadronic model that does not consider isospin-breaking effects, surprisingly good. We also investigate the question whether the scalar $\bar{q}q$ states lie below or above 1 GeV and find the scalar states above 1 GeV to be preferred as $\bar{q}q$ states. Additionally, we also describe the axial-vector resonances as $\bar{q}q$ states.

## Key claim

The authors perform a global fit of meson masses, decay widths, and decay amplitudes within a three-flavor linear sigma model with (axial-)vector mesons and find that scalar quark-antiquark states above 1 GeV (specifically \(a_0(1450)\) and \(K_0^\star(1430)\)) are strongly favored over the low-mass candidates, but the scalar-isoscalar sector is excluded from the fit due to its complexity and the presence of a glueball state.

## Method

The authors construct an extended linear sigma model (eLSM) with global chiral \(U(3)_L \times U(3)_R\) symmetry, including scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector meson nonets, plus a dilaton field to encode the trace anomaly. They perform a global fit of 21 experimentally known quantities (masses, decay widths, decay amplitudes, and decay constants) and test all four possible assignments of the isotriplet and isodoublet scalar states to physical resonances. The scalar-isoscalar states are excluded from the fit due to the large number of physical resonances and the expected mixing with a glueball state.

## Key numbers

- The global fit describes all experimental quantities with an average error of 5%, and most quantities to much better precision.
- The masses of the two scalar-isoscalar quark-antiquark states in the large-\(N_c\) limit are found to be about 1.36 GeV and 1.53 GeV.
- The non-strange scalar-isoscalar state is identified with \(f_0(1370)\) and the heavier \(\bar{s}s\) state may be \(f_0(1710)\).
- Only the pair \(a_0(1450) / K_0^\star(1430)\) yields a good fit; the other three combinations (\(a_0(980) / K_0^\star(800)\), \(a_0(980) / K_0^\star(1430)\), \(a_0(1450) / K_0^\star(800)\)) do not.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor linear sigma model study of Parganlija et al. (2010) to three flavors, including strange mesons, to test whether the conclusion that scalar quark-antiquark states lie above 1 GeV holds when strange degrees of freedom are included. The model is the first to simultaneously incorporate scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons with both non-strange and strange quantum numbers within a single linear chiral framework. The results enable future studies of in-medium properties of hadrons and the coupling of the glueball to mesons, as well as investigations of chiral symmetry restoration at nonzero temperature and density.
