# Automated calculation of Jet fragmentation at NLO in QCD

> We present FMNLO, a framework to combine general-purpose Monte Carlo generators and fragmentation functions (FFs). It is based on a hybrid scheme of phase-space slicing method and local subtraction method, and accurate to next-to-leading order (NLO) in QCD. The new framework has been interfaced to MG5 aMC@NLO and made publicly available in this work. We demonstrate its unique ability by giving theoretical predictions of various fragmentation measurements at the LHC, followed by comparison with the data. With the help of interpolation techniques, FMNLO allows for fast calculation of fragmentation processes for a large number of different FFs, which makes it a promising tool for future fits of FFs. As an example, we perform a NLO fit of parton fragmentation functions to unidentified charged hadrons using measurements at the LHC. We find the ATLAS data from inclusive dijet production show a strong constraining power. Notable disparities are found between our gluon FF and that of BKK, DSS and NNFF, indicating the necessities of additional constraints and data for gluon fragmentation function.

## Key claim

FMNLO provides a framework for NLO-accurate QCD calculations of jet fragmentation that can be interfaced with general-purpose Monte Carlo generators like MG5 aMC@NLO, but the hybrid scheme relies on a phase-space slicing cutoff parameter lambda (recommended 0.02-0.04) and the validation shows agreement with analytical results at the few-per-mille level only when this cutoff is sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors developed a hybrid scheme combining phase-space slicing of collinear regions with local subtraction methods (CS dipole or FKS subtraction) to handle uncancelled collinear singularities in fragmentation processes. They implemented this scheme within the MG5 aMC@NLO framework, using HOPPET for DGLAP evolution and convolution of fragmentation functions, and employed interpolation techniques on a two-dimensional grid (50 points in x, 16 points in Q) to enable fast recalculation for different fragmentation function sets without repeating NLO partonic cross section calculations.

## Key numbers

* NLO predictions with cutoff lambda=0.04 differ from analytical results by at most two per mille in the range x_h from 0.01 to 1 for e+e- -> gamma* -> q qbar at center-of-mass energy Q=200 GeV
* For pp collisions at 7 TeV, NLO corrections reach 70% in lower p_T,h regions (60-220 GeV range studied)
* The interpolation grid uses N_x=50 points in x and N_Q=16 points in Q, with interpolation order n=4, achieving precision better than a few per mille
* For isolated-photon-tagged jets at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, NLO corrections contribute less than 20% in the region xi_T^gamma < 3.5, but rise rapidly to nearly 2 for xi_T^gamma > 3.5

## Context

This work extends the capabilities of NLO fragmentation calculations beyond the case-by-case implementations of prior approaches such as INCNLO (de Florian et al., various years) and the two-cutoff method of Harris and Owens (2001), by providing an automated framework that works for arbitrary hard processes within the Standard Model and beyond. The framework enables future global fits of fragmentation functions, as demonstrated by the authors' NLO fit to unidentified charged hadrons using LHC data, which found notable disparities between their gluon fragmentation function and those from BKK (Binnewies, Kniehl, Kramer), DSS (de Florian, Sassot, Stratmann), and NNFF (Bertone et al., 2017-2018), indicating the need for additional constraints on gluon fragmentation.
