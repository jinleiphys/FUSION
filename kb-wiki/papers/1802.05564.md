# Quantal Diffusion Description of Multi-Nucleon Transfers in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Employing the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, we develop a quantal diffusion description of the multi-nucleon transfer in heavy-ion collisions at finite impact parameters. The quantal transport coefficients are determined by the occupied single-particle wave functions of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock equations. As a result, the primary fragment mass and charge distribution functions are determined entirely in terms of the mean-field properties. This powerful description does not involve any adjustable parameter, includes the effects of shell structure and is consistent with the fluctuation-dissipation theorem of the non-equilibrium statistical mechanics. As a first application of the approach, we analyze the fragment mass distribution in $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{238}\mathrm{U}$ collisions at the bombarding energy $E_{\text{c.m.}}=193$ MeV and compare the calculations with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors develop a quantal diffusion description of multi-nucleon transfer in heavy-ion collisions at finite impact parameters, based on the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, which determines primary fragment mass and charge distributions entirely from mean-field properties without adjustable parameters. The approach is applied specifically to collisions in the tip geometry of the uranium nucleus, which the authors identify as the dominant contribution to the measured fragment distributions in the $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{238}\mathrm{U}$ system.

## Method

The authors linearize the Langevin equations derived from the SMF approach around the mean neutron and proton numbers obtained from time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) solutions, and derive coupled differential equations for the variances and co-variance of the fragment neutron and proton distributions. The quantal diffusion coefficients for neutrons and protons are computed from the occupied single-particle wave functions of the TDHF equations, using a diabatic approximation and closure relation to evaluate the correlation functions of the stochastic drift coefficients. The resulting Gaussian distribution functions for each impact parameter are integrated over the relevant range of impact parameters to obtain production cross-sections for nuclei with given neutron and proton numbers.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 193$ MeV for the $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{238}\mathrm{U}$ system
- Impact parameter $b = 2.8$ fm (initial orbital angular momentum $\ell = 54\hbar$) used for the density profile and drift coefficient illustrations
- Impact parameters $b = 2.1$ fm, $b = 2.6$ fm, and $b = 3.1$ fm (initial angular momenta $\ell = 40\hbar$, $50\hbar$, and $60\hbar$) used for the mean drift-path calculations
- Symmetric equilibrium state neutron number $N_0 = (N_P + N_T)/2 = 87$ and proton number $Z_0 = (Z_P + Z_T)/2 = 56$
- Gaussian dispersion parameter $\kappa = 1.0$ fm used in the numerical calculations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantal diffusion description for central collisions (Ayik 2016, Ayik 2017) to finite impact parameters, providing a microscopic framework for multi-nucleon transfer in deep-inelastic collisions and quasi-fission reactions. The approach contrasts with the particle projection method in TDHF (Simenel 2010, Sekizawa 2013, 2014, 2017), which works best for few-nucleon transfer and underestimates dispersions in dissipative collisions, and with phenomenological nucleon exchange models (Schroder 1981, Merchant 1981). The formalism enables calculation of primary fragment mass and charge distributions for heavy-ion collisions without adjustable parameters, potentially guiding experimental searches for neutron-rich isotopes.
