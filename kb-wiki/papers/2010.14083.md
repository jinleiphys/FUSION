# Final state interaction in the pn and nn decay channels of $^4_Λ$He

> We study the effects of final state interactions in the non-mesonic weak decay $ΛN \rightarrow nN$ (n is a neutron and N is either a neutron or a proton) of the hypernucleus $_Λ^4$He. Using a three-body model the effects of distortion of the interaction of the emitted nucleon pair with the residual nucleus is considered. We also study the influence of the final state interaction between the emitted nucleons using the Migdal-Watson model. The effect of spin symmetries in the final state of the pair is also considered. Based on our calculations, we conclude that final state interactions play a minor role in the kinetic energy spectrum of the emitted nucleon pair.

## Key claim

Final state interactions (FSI) play a minor role in the kinetic energy spectrum of the emitted nucleon pair from the non-mesonic weak decay of $_\Lambda^4$He, although the authors qualify that the Migdal-Watson enhancement function primarily affects very low relative energies of the pair and thus has little effect on the observed kinetic energy sums for proton-neutron and neutron-neutron pairs.

## Method

The authors employed a three-body Faddeev formalism to describe the final state of two emitted nucleons and a residual (A-2) nucleus, incorporating distorted waves for nucleon-nucleus interactions via eikonal and partial-wave methods, and nucleon-nucleon final state interactions via the Migdal-Watson model. The decay width was calculated using Fermi's Golden Rule with the weak interaction Hamiltonian, and the resulting kinetic energy sum spectra were compared to experimental data from the KEK-E462 experiment.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference driving the decay is $\Delta_n = M_\Lambda - M = 176$ MeV
- The free lambda decay rate is $\Gamma = 2.5 \times 10^{-6}$ eV with mean lifetime $263 \pm 2$ ps
- The weak coupling constant is $G_F m_\pi^2 = 2.21 \times 10^{-7}$, with constants $A_\pi = 1.05$ and $B_\pi = -7.15$ adjusted to reproduce free lambda decay
- The singlet neutron-neutron scattering length used is $a_{nn}^s = -18.9 \pm 0.4$ fm with effective range $r_{nn}^s = 2.819$ fm
- The neutron-proton scattering parameters are: $a_{np}^t = 5.4114(27)$ fm, $r_{np}^t = 1.7606(35)$ fm, $a_{np}^s = -23.7154(80)$ fm, and $r_{np}^s = 2.706(67)$ fm

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Hussein and collaborators (Bar08, Bau09, Bau09b, KGH10, Baue10) which showed that a simple plane-wave model without correlations describes the pn channel reasonably well but fails for the nn channel, where the peaking in the calculated sum kinetic energy spectrum is too high compared to data. The present study provides a more complete three-body treatment of final state interactions and demonstrates that the Migdal-Watson enhancement function primarily affects very low relative energies, enabling future experiments with improved neutron detector efficiency to more accurately determine the nn scattering length.
