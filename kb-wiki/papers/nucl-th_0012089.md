# Multi-gap superfluidity in nuclear matter

> It is shown that under lowering density or temperature a nucleon Fermi superfluid can undergo a phase transition to a new superfluid state corresponding to superposition of states with singlet-triplet (ST) and triplet-singlet (TS) pairing of nucleons (in spin and isospin spaces). Such states arise as a result of branching from one-gap solution of the self-consistent equations, describing ST pairing of nucleons. The density and temperature dependence of the order parameters for new two-gap solutions is determined in the model with Skyrme effective forces.

## Key claim

Under lowering density or temperature, a nucleon Fermi superfluid can undergo a phase transition to a new superfluid state corresponding to a superposition of singlet-triplet (ST) and triplet-singlet (TS) pairing of nucleons, but only when the coupling constants in the TS and ST pairing channels are of the same order; for the Skyrme potentials RATP and SkP, where the TS coupling constant is considerably larger than the ST coupling constant, no such two-gap solutions exist.

## Method

The authors use Landau's semiphenomenological Fermi-liquid theory to derive self-consistent equations for the order parameters of two-gap unitary states in symmetric nuclear matter, generalizing the BCS equations. They specify the energy functional using Skyrme effective forces and solve the resulting equations numerically at zero temperature and as a function of temperature, analyzing the branching of two-gap solutions from the one-gap ST solution. The analysis is performed in the logarithmic approximation and numerically for several Skyrme potentials (Ska, SkM, SkM*, RATP, SkP).

## Key numbers

- The cutoff energy for the interaction near the Fermi surface is set to θ = 0.1 ε_F (where ε_F is the Fermi energy).
- The function φ(α) that determines the existence of two-gap solutions has a minimum φ_min = -4 at α = 0 and a maximum φ_max = 4 at α = ±∞, giving the condition -4 < 1/g_1 - 1/g_2 < 4 for the existence of TS-ST mixed states in the logarithmic approximation.
- Two-gap TS-ST superfluid states arise at a critical density (for Ska, SkM, SkM* potentials) and at a critical temperature T_tsst below the ST critical temperature T_st, with the order parameters following the relation Δ_30 = (1/2)x(1+d(x,T)) and Δ_03 = (1/2)x(1-d(x,T)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of one-gap superfluid states in symmetric nuclear matter (Akhiezer et al., Ref. AIP) by analyzing phase transitions to multi-gap superfluid states. It uses Skyrme effective forces as the NN interaction potential, following the parameter sets from the work of Beiner et al. (Ref. BGH) and the SkP potential from the work of Dobaczewski et al. (Ref. DFT). The results enable future studies of the influence of asymmetry and real bound states (deuterons) on the thermodynamic properties of multi-gap superfluid states in nuclear matter.
