# Quantal rotation and its coupling to intrinsic motion in nuclei

> Symmetry breaking is an importance concept in nuclear physics and other fields of physics. Self-consistent coupling between the mean-field potential and the single-particle motion is a key ingredient in the unified model of Bohr and Mottelson, which could lead to a deformed nucleus as a consequence of spontaneous breaking of the rotational symmetry. Some remarks on the finite-size quantum effects are given. In finite nuclei, the deformation inevitably introduces the rotation as a symmetry-restoring collective motion (Anderson-Nambu-Goldstone mode), and the rotation affects the intrinsic motion. In order to investigate the interplay between the rotational and intrinsic motions in a variety of collective phenomena, we use the cranking prescription together with the quasiparticle random phase approximation. At low spin, the coupling effect can be seen in the generalized intensity relation. A feasible quantization of the cranking model is presented, which provides a microscopic approach to the higher-order intensity relation. At high spin, the semiclassical cranking prescription works well. We discuss properties of collective vibrational motions under rapid rotation and/or large deformation. The superdeformed shell structure plays a key role in emergence of a new soft mode which could lead to instability toward the $K^π=1^-$ octupole shape. A wobbling mode of excitation, which is a clear signature of the triviality, is discussed in terms of a microscopic point of view. A crucial role played by the quasiparticle alignment is presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that spontaneous symmetry breaking (SBS) in finite nuclei is never exact due to quantum fluctuations, but for heavy deformed nuclei (A ~ 200) the correlation time for rotational orientation (~10⁻¹⁹ s) is significantly larger than the single-particle Fermi motion period (~10⁻²² s), making the symmetry-broken deformed description practically valid and essential for understanding nuclear structure.

## Method

The authors use the cranking model Hamiltonian H' = H - ω_rot · J together with the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) in the rotating shell model to study the coupling between rotational and intrinsic motions. They analyze the unified model Hamiltonian of Bohr and Mottelson, employing the adiabatic (Born-Oppenheimer) approximation and field coupling techniques with separable residual interactions of the pairing-plus-quadrupole type. The self-consistency condition ω_x Σ_x = ω_y Σ_y = ω_z Σ_z for the harmonic oscillator model is used to relate deformation of the potential to that of the density distribution.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear rotational period in heavy nuclei is typically τ_rot = 10⁻¹⁹ to 10⁻²⁰ s, which is 100–1000 times larger than the single-particle Fermi motion period τ_F ~ 10⁻²² s
- At high spin, the rotational period can reach τ = 10⁻²¹ to 10⁻²² s, comparable to τ_F
- For a heavy deformed nucleus with A = 200 and single-particle overlaps |⟨φ_i⁰|φ_i^θ⟩| ~ 0.9, the coefficient C_{12...A} ~ (0.9)^200 ~ 7 × 10⁻¹⁰, showing rotational fluctuation is significantly hindered
- The zero-point kinetic energy per nucleon T_zpe/A ~ 1/(mR²) ~ 10 MeV, comparable to the nuclear binding energy B/A ~ 8 MeV
- For ⁸⁰Zr (Z = N = 40) with N_max = 3, the spherical kinetic energy T_0 = 150 ω_0 ~ 1.43 GeV using ω_0 ≈ 41A⁻¹/³ ~ 9.5 MeV; a prolate deformation of e^η = 1.1 increases kinetic energy by about 1% (14 MeV)
- The moment of inertia J is approximately order of A^(5/3)
- The correlation time τ_cor ~ J/ΔI ~ J amounts to 10⁻¹⁹ s for typical deformed actinide nuclei

## Context

This paper extends the unified model of Bohr and Mottelson (BM2, 1975) and the cranking model approach developed by the authors and collaborators (SM82, MSM90, NMMS96, SS09) for studying rapidly rotating nuclei. It builds on the pairing-plus-quadrupole model originally proposed by Bohr, Mottelson, and colleagues (BK65, KB68-1, BS69), and discusses the Jahn-Teller effect (JT37) as an analogy for the degeneracy-driven symmetry breaking mechanism. The work enables microscopic calculations of intrinsic moments entering the generalized intensity relation at low spin, and provides a framework for understanding wobbling motion observed in ¹⁶³Lu and neighboring nuclei, superdeformed shell structure, and octupole instability toward K^π = 1⁻ shapes.
