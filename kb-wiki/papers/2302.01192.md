# Tomography of pions and protons via transverse momentum dependent distributions

> We perform the first simultaneous extraction of parton collinear and transverse degrees of freedom from low-energy fixed-target Drell-Yan data in order to compare the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the pion and proton. We demonstrate that the transverse separation of the quark field encoded in TMDs of the pion is more than $4 σ$ smaller than that of the proton. Additionally, we find the transverse separation of the quark field decreases as its longitudinal momentum fraction decreases. In studying the nuclear modification of TMDs, we find clear evidence for a transverse EMC effect. We comment on possible explanations for these intriguing behaviors, which call for a deeper examination of tomography in a variety of strongly interacting quark-gluon systems.

## Key claim
The transverse separation of quark fields (average ⟨b_T|x⟩) in the pion is approximately 20% smaller than in the proton, with a statistical significance of more than 4σ, and this separation decreases as the longitudinal momentum fraction x decreases for both hadrons. The authors also report evidence for a "transverse EMC effect" in nuclei, but note that these findings call for deeper theoretical examination and are not yet definitive explanations.

## Method
The authors performed a simultaneous Bayesian Monte Carlo extraction of collinear and transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the pion and proton from fixed-target Drell-Yan (DY) data, using the JAM QCD analysis framework at next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic (N²LL) accuracy. They analyzed 524 data points including q_T-dependent and q_T-integrated πA and pA DY data from E288, E605, E615, E537, E772, and E866 experiments, plus leading neutron electroproduction data from HERA (H1 and ZEUS), with the TMDs modeled via a b_*-prescription and multiple nonperturbative parametrizations explored for the large-b_T region.

## Key numbers
- The average transverse separation ⟨b_T|x⟩ of the u-quark in the pion is approximately 20% smaller than in the proton, with a confidence level of (4–5.2)σ.
- The charge radius of the pion is r_π = 0.659(4) fm, compared to the proton charge radius r_p = 0.8409(4) fm (PDG values cited for reference).
- The analysis used a starting scale Q_0 = 1.27 GeV and b_max = 2e^{-γ_E}/Q_0 ≈ 0.88 GeV^{-1}.
- The total χ²/N for the fit was 1.12 with a Z-score of 1.86 across all 524 data points.
- For the bound proton in a tungsten nucleus, the average transverse separation is up to 12% smaller than that in a free proton (transverse EMC effect).

## Context
This work extends previous TMD extractions (e.g., Bacchetta et al. 2017, 2019, 2022; Bertone et al. 2019; Scimemi and Vladimirov 2019) by simultaneously extracting pion collinear PDFs and TMD PDFs for both pion and proton, rather than fixing collinear PDFs from external analyses. The results are consistent with earlier findings of Alrashed et al. (2022) on nuclear TMD effects but go beyond by including x-dependence and a combined collinear+TMD global analysis. The observed decrease of ⟨b_T|x⟩ with decreasing x is interpreted as possibly related to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (Schweitzer et al. 2012), and the transverse EMC effect mirrors the classic collinear EMC effect found by the European Muon Collaboration (1983). Future tagged SIDIS programs at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider could extend this tomography to kaons and neutrons.
