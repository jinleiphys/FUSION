# Doublet bands in $^{126}$Cs in the triaxial rotor model coupled with two quasiparticles

> The positive parity doublet bands based on the $πh_{11/2}\otimesνh_{11/2}$ configuration in $^{126}$Cs have been investigated in the two quasi-particles coupled with a triaxial rotor model. The energy spectra $E(I)$, energy staggering parameter $S(I)=[E(I)-E(I-1)]/2I$, $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ values, intraband $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios, $B(M1)_{\textrm{in}}/B(M1)_{\textrm{out}}$ ratios, and orientation of the angular momentum for the rotor as well as the valence proton and neutron are calculated. After including the pairing correlation, good agreement has been obtained between the calculated results and the data available, which supports the interpretation of this positive parity doublet bands as chiral bands.

## Key claim
After including pairing correlations via the BCS quasiparticle approximation, the two-quasiparticle-plus-triaxial-rotor model reproduces both the energy spectra and electromagnetic transition ratios of the positive parity doublet bands in \(^{126}\)Cs, supporting their interpretation as chiral bands, but only for spins \(9\hbar \leq I \leq 18\hbar\), with deviations at higher spin attributed to the Coriolis effect.

## Method
The authors used a particle-rotor model (PRM) in which two quasiparticles (a proton and a neutron in the \(h_{11/2}\) shell) are coupled to a triaxial rotor core. Pairing was included via the BCS quasiparticle approximation, replacing single-particle energies with quasiparticle energies and multiplying matrix elements by pairing factors \(u_\mu u_\nu + v_\mu v_\nu\). The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the laboratory frame to obtain energy spectra, staggering parameters, \(B(M1)\) and \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities, and angular-momentum orientations.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation parameters: \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.26\) (slightly larger than the \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.244\) from Tajima (1994)), \(\gamma = 24^\circ\).
- Moment of inertia: \(\mathcal{J} = 20\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}\).
- Pairing gap: \(\Delta = 1.0\ \text{MeV}\) for both protons and neutrons (from the empirical formula \(\Delta = 12/\sqrt{A}\)).
- Proton Fermi energy: \(\lambda_\text{p} = -2.293\ \text{MeV}\); neutron Fermi energy: \(\lambda_\text{n} = 0.8\ \text{MeV}\).
- Coupling parameter: \(C = 0.3\ \text{MeV}\).
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment: \(Q_0 = 3.5\ \text{eb}\); gyromagnetic ratios \(g_\text{R} = 0.44\), \(g_\text{p} = 1.21\), \(g_\text{n} = -0.21\).
- Calculated energy separation between the two bands: \(\sim 200\ \text{keV}\) for spins \(9\hbar \leq I \leq 18\hbar\).
- Effective angles between angular momenta: all three effective angles exceed \(45^\circ\) in the spin interval \(9\hbar \leq I \leq 15\hbar\), indicating aplanar rotation.

## Context
This work extends the particle-rotor model of Frauendorf and Meng (1997) and Peng et al. (2003a, 2003b) by including pairing correlations via the BCS quasiparticle approximation, enabling the model to handle multi-valence-neutron configurations. It specifically addresses the \(N=71\) isotone \(^{126}\)Cs, where the neutron Fermi level lies in the middle of the \(\nu h_{11/2}\) subshell, a different regime from the \(N=75\) and \(N=77\) Cs isotopes studied by Starosta et al. (2001), Koike et al. (2001), and others. The success of the two-quasiparticle model motivates development of a multi-proton and multi-neutron plus triaxial rotor model, which the authors state is in progress.
