# Hadron formation in high energy photonuclear reactions

> We present a new method to account for coherence length effects in a semi-classical transport model. This allows us to describe photo- and electroproduction at large nuclei (A>12) and high energies using a realistic coupled channel description of the final state interactions that goes beyond simple Glauber theory. We show that the purely absorptive treatment of the final state interactions can lead to wrong estimates of color transparency and formation time effects in particle production. As an example, we discuss exclusive rho^0 photoproduction on Pb at a photon energy of 7 GeV as well as K^+ production in the photon energy range 1-7 GeV.

## Key claim
The paper argues that purely absorptive final state interactions (FSI) as used in standard Glauber theory can lead to incorrect estimates of formation time and color transparency effects in photonuclear reactions; a coupled-channel transport model is necessary to correctly interpret these effects, particularly for mesons with long mean free paths.

## Method
The authors combine a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model for final state interactions with a quantum-mechanical treatment of coherence length effects in the initial state, using vector meson dominance (VMD) to describe the photon's hadronic fluctuations. The photon state is decomposed into vector meson components (\(\rho^0\), \(\omega\), \(\phi\)) and a bare photon component, with Glauber theory used to calculate how each component is shadowed as it propagates through the nucleus before the primary reaction. The transport model then propagates all produced particles through the nuclear medium, allowing for coupled-channel effects such as sidefeeding and regeneration that are absent in purely absorptive Glauber treatments.

## Key numbers
- Formation time \(\tau_f \gtrsim 0.8\) fm/c in the hadron rest frame, corresponding to formation lengths of about 30 fm for a 5 GeV pion, 7 fm for a 5 GeV kaon, and 7 fm for a 7 GeV \(\rho\) meson.
- For exclusive \(\rho^0\) photoproduction on \({}^{208}\)Pb at \(E_\gamma = 7\) GeV, about 60% of additional \(\rho^0\) mesons come from inelastic primary reactions (e.g., \(\gamma N \rightarrow \rho^0 \pi N\)) rather than elastic \(\gamma N \rightarrow \rho^0 N\).
- For \(K^+\) production on \({}^{208}\)Pb in the photon energy range 1–7 GeV, shadowing reduces the nuclear production cross section to about 65% at 7 GeV; the new treatment of shadowing increases the total yield at 7 GeV by about 20% compared to the earlier treatment in Eff00.
- For exclusive \(\rho^0\) photoproduction on Pb at 7 GeV, Glauber theory is reliable only for \(|t| > 0.1\) GeV\(^2\); below this threshold, the exclusivity measure cannot distinguish elastic from inelastic primary reactions.

## Context
This work extends the semi-classical transport model previously applied to heavy ion collisions (SIS energies) and hadron-induced reactions (Eff99a, Eff99b) to high-energy photonuclear reactions, improving upon the initial shadowing treatment in Eff00. It directly contradicts the purely absorptive FSI treatment of standard Glauber theory (Gla59Gla70, Yen71) for extracting formation times, showing that coupled-channel effects can mimic or mask formation time signatures. The results are relevant for interpreting exclusive \(\rho^0\) electroproduction data from the HERMES experiment (Ack00) and for planning future measurements at TJNAF and DESY, as the method can be extended to virtual photons and higher energies.
