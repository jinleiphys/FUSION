# Dynamics of Anti-Proton -- Protons and Anti-Proton -- Nucleus Reactions

> A short review of simulation results of anti-proton-proton and anti-proton-nucleus interactions within the framework of Geant4 FTF (Fritiof) model is presented. The model uses the main assumptions of the Quark-Gluon-String Model or Dual Parton Model. The model assumes production and fragmentation of quark-anti-quark and diquark-anti-diquark strings in the mentioned interactions. Key ingredients of the model are cross sections of string creation processes and an usage of the LUND string fragmentation algorithm. They allow one to satisfactory describe a large set of experimental data, especially, a strange particle production, Lambda hyperons and K mesons.

## Key claim

The Geant4 FTF (Fritiof) model, based on the Quark-Gluon-String Model / Dual Parton Model with LUND string fragmentation, can satisfactorily describe a large set of experimental data for anti-proton-proton and anti-proton-nucleus interactions, particularly strange particle production, but the authors note that at higher momenta (above 10 GeV/c) the experimental data for average multiplicities of Lambda hyperons and K mesons scatter too strongly to draw a solid conclusion.

## Method

The authors implemented the Dual Parton Model / Quark-Gluon-String Model in the Geant4 FTF framework, using cross sections for various quark-flow diagrams (string junction annihilation, quark-antiquark annihilation, pomeron exchange) with energy-dependent parameterizations. For anti-proton-nucleus interactions, they applied the Glauber approach for cross section calculations and coupled the FTF model with either the binary cascade model (BIC) or the precompound model (PRECO) to handle intra-nuclear cascading and secondary particle production.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for string junction annihilation (diagram "a"): sigma_a = 25 / sqrt(s - 4m^2) mb
- Cross section for quark-antiquark annihilation (diagram "b"): sigma_b = 15.65 + 700 * (2.172 - sqrt(s))^2.5 mb for sqrt(s) <= 2.172 GeV, and sigma_b = 34 / sqrt(s) mb for sqrt(s) > 2.172 GeV
- Cross section for pomeron exchange processes (diagram "e"): sigma_e = 140 / s mb
- Cross section for non-annihilation processes (FTF): sigma_FTF = 35 * (1 - 2.1 / sqrt(s)) mb
- The model is validated for anti-proton momenta from annihilation at rest up to approximately 1000 GeV/c
- Neutron distributions in anti-proton-nucleus interactions were calculated at P_lab = 1.22 GeV/c
- Rapidity distributions of Lambda hyperons and K0_S mesons were calculated at P_lab = 3.6, 12, and 100 GeV/c
- Anti-proton-Xe interactions were studied at 200 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the Geant4 FTF model, previously applied to hadron-nucleon and hadron-nucleus interactions, to anti-proton-proton and anti-proton-nucleus reactions. It builds on the classical Glauber approach for anti-proton-deuteron scattering by Franco and Glauber (1966) and later work by Dalkarov and Karmanov (1985) on anti-proton scattering on C, Ca, and Pb nuclei. The model enables simulations needed for cosmic ray experiments (PAMELA, BESS, AMS, CAPRISE) searching for anti-nuclei, and for the PANDA experiment at FAIR facilities studying hypernuclei production.
