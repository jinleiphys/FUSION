# Pion-Nucleon Scattering in a Large-N Sigma Model

> We review the large-N_c approach to meson-baryon scattering, including recent interesting developments. We then study pion-nucleon scattering in a particular variant of the linear sigma-model, in which the couplings of the sigma and pi mesons to the nucleon are echoed by couplings to the entire tower of I=J baryons (including the Delta) as dictated by large-N_c group theory. We sum the complete set of multi-loop meson-exchange πN --> πN and πN --> σN Feynman diagrams, to leading order in 1/N_c. The key idea, reviewed in detail, is that large-N_c allows the approximation of LOOP graphs by TREE graphs, so long as the loops contain at least one baryon leg; trees, in turn, can be summed by solving classical equations of motion. We exhibit the resulting partial-wave S-matrix and the rich nucleon and Delta resonance spectrum of this simple model, comparing not only to experiment but also to pion-nucleon scattering in the Skyrme model. The moral is that much of the detailed structure of the meson-baryon S-matrix which hitherto has been uncovered only with skyrmion methods, can also be described by models with explicit baryon fields, thanks to the 1/N_c expansion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the rich resonance structure of pion-nucleon scattering, previously accessible only through skyrmion methods, can also be described by models with explicit baryon fields when using the 1/N_c expansion, though both approaches share significant problems in low partial waves that remain unresolved.

## Method

The authors study a variant of the linear sigma-model where sigma and pion mesons couple not only to nucleons but to the entire tower of I=J baryons (including the Delta) as dictated by large-N_c group theory. They sum the complete set of multi-loop meson-exchange Feynman diagrams for πN → πN and πN → σN to leading order in 1/N_c by exploiting the large-N_c approximation that allows loop graphs containing at least one baryon leg to be replaced by tree graphs, which are then summed by solving classical equations of motion.

## Key numbers

- The proportionality rule for pion-baryon couplings relates g_πNN, g_πNΔ, g_πΔΔ, and other couplings up the I=J tower to a single underlying coupling constant, with corrections occurring at order 1/N_c^2 rather than 1/N_c
- The baryon mass formula M_J = M_0 + J(J+1)/(2I) + O(N_c^{-2}) holds, where M_0 and I are constants of order N_c that can be fixed by pegging M_{1/2} and M_{3/2} to experimental nucleon and Delta masses
- The pseudovector coupling g_πNN/(2M_N), the tensor coupling of the rho, and the vector coupling of the omega all scale as sqrt(N_c), while the vector coupling of the rho and tensor coupling of the omega scale as 1/sqrt(N_c)
- The sigma-model calculation uses parameters m_sigma = 600 MeV, kappa = 18.5, lambda = 214, g = 13.6, and a_N = 0.5 fm for the nucleon size parameter

## Context

This work extends the large-N_c approach to meson-baryon scattering developed by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and the exchange-graph formalism of Mattis (1992), while connecting to the Skyrme model results of Hayashi, Eckart, Holzwarth, and Schwesinger (1984) and others. The paper enables future realistic calculations incorporating vector mesons and provides a framework for comparing the explicit-baryon approach with skyrmion methods, though the complete resolution of problems in low partial waves remains a major technical hurdle.
