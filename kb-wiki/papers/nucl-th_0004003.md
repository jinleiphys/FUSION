# Tables of internal conversion coefficients for superheavy elements

## Key claim
The authors present calculated internal conversion coefficients (ICC) for superheavy elements with atomic numbers 104 ≤ Z ≤ 126, covering multipolarities E1–E4 and M1–M4 at 16 transition energies from 10 keV to 1 MeV. The abstract does not mention that the calculations rely on theoretical binding energies from Lu et al. (1971) rather than experimental binding energies, which are unavailable for these elements, and that the agreement with prior calculations for Z=104 is better than 1 percent in most cases.

## Method
The ICC were computed using the NICC program, which solves the Dirac equation for bound and free electron states using the formulae of Büring (1965) and performs direct integration to obtain conversion matrix elements. The atomic potential is Hartree–Fock–Slater, the nucleus is modeled with a Fermi charge distribution, and the potential of Lu et al. (1971) is used. Because experimental binding energies are absent for superheavy elements, the kinetic energy of the converted electron is derived from theoretical eigenvalues from Lu et al. (1971), which are close to experimental binding energies where available.

## Key numbers
- ICC are tabulated for Z = 104 through Z = 126, for multipolarities E1–E4 and M1–M4, at 16 transition energies from 10 keV to 1 MeV.
- For Z=104 and multipolarity E1, the K-shell ICC at 800 keV is 6.957E-03 and at 1000 keV is 4.916E-03; the L1-shell ICC at 800 keV is 1.100E-03 and at 1000 keV is 7.643E-04.
- For Z=104 and multipolarity E1, the M1-shell ICC at 10 keV is 5.727E-01 and at 15 keV is 3.545E-01; the M2-shell ICC at 10 keV is 1.095E+00 and at 15 keV is 6.630E-01.
- For Z=104, comparison with Rösel et al. (1978) shows agreement better than 1 percent in most cases, only exceptionally within 2–3 percent.

## Context
This work extends prior ICC calculations to superheavy elements beyond Z=104, where internal conversion strongly dominates over gamma-ray emission. The calculations are benchmarked against Rösel et al. (1978) and Band and Trzhaskovskaya (1978) for Z=104. The tables are motivated by recent experimental progress, including the identification of the ground-state band in 254No (Z=102) up to spin 14 by Reiter et al. (1999) and indications of element Z=118, A=293 by Ninov et al. (1999). The results enable gamma-ray spectroscopy of superheavy nuclei by providing the ICC needed to correct for conversion electron emission.
