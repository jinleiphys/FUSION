# Dynamical equilibration of strongly interacting "infinite" parton matter within the parton-hadron-string dynamics transport approach

> We study the kinetic and chemical equilibration in "infinite" parton matter within the parton-hadron-string dynamics off-shell transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) for partons matched to reproduce lattice QCD results-including the partonic equation of state-in thermodynamic equilibrium. The "infinite" parton matter is simulated by a system of quarks and gluons within a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, at different energy densities, initialized slightly out of kinetic and chemical equilibrium. We investigate the approach of the system to equilibrium and the time scales for the equilibration of different observables. We, furthermore, study particle distributions in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) including partonic spectral functions, momentum distributions, abundances of the different parton species and their fluctuations (scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis) in equilibrium. We also compare the results of the microscopic calculations with the ansatz of the DQPM. It is found that the results of the transport calculations are in equilibrium well matched by the DQPM for quarks and antiquarks, while the gluon spectral function shows a slightly different shape due to the explicit interaction of partons. The time scales for the relaxation of fluctuation observables are found to be shorter than those for the average values. Furthermore, in the local subsystem, a strong change of the fluctuation observables with the size of the local volume is observed. These fluctuations no longer correspond to those of the full system and are reduced to Poissonian distributions when the volume of the local subsystem becomes much smaller than the total volume.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, based on the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM), can reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics in equilibrium for quarks and antiquarks, while the gluon spectral function shows a slightly different shape due to the mass dependence of the gluon width generated by explicit parton interactions. The time scales for relaxation of fluctuation observables are shorter than those for average values, and fluctuations in local subsystems deviate from those of the full system, reducing to Poissonian distributions when the local volume becomes much smaller than the total volume.

## Method

The authors simulated "infinite" parton matter by placing quarks and gluons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions at various energy densities, initialized slightly out of kinetic and chemical equilibrium. They employed the off-shell PHSD transport approach based on generalized Kadanoff-Baym equations, with parton masses and widths taken from the DQPM which is matched to lattice QCD results. The system evolution was tracked through elastic interactions (qq, qg, gg scattering) and inelastic processes (g ↔ q+qbar, g ↔ g+g, g+g ↔ q+qbar), with cross sections determined self-consistently by iterating the coupled rate equations until equilibrium was reached.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 158 MeV used as reference for the DQPM running coupling parametrization
- Gluon-gluon elastic cross section parametrized as σ_gg(ε) ≈ 7.6e^(-ε/0.8) + 106.2e^(-ε/0.2) + 1.7e^(-ε/3.7) + 0.3, where ε is in GeV/fm^3 and cross sections in fm^2
- Quark-quark cross section scaling factor α_qq(ε) ≈ 0.3e^(-ε/2.6) + 0.4, with quark-gluon cross section fixed at σ_gq = (4/9)σ_gg
- DQPM relations at μ_q = 0: M_q = (2/3)M_g and Γ_q = (4/9)Γ_g for masses and widths of quarks relative to gluons
- Running coupling parameters λ = 2.42 and T_s/T_c = 0.56 extracted from fit to lattice QCD equation of state

## Context

This work extends prior studies of thermalization in strongly interacting matter using nonequilibrium approaches (Ref13-Ref18, Ref21, Ref23) by providing a fully microscopic transport description that incorporates both partonic and hadronic degrees of freedom with a dynamic phase transition. The DQPM framework builds on earlier quasiparticle models (quasi_models) but differs by including finite widths for partonic spectral functions, which is essential for obtaining the small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (shear_viscosity) that enables approximately hydrodynamic evolution. The results enable future studies of how finite-size effects in heavy-ion collisions affect fluctuation observables and the interpretation of experimental data in terms of thermodynamic limit predictions.
