# Heavy-Quarkonium Production in High Energy Proton-Proton Collisions at RHIC

> We update the study of the total Psi and Upsilon production cross section in proton-proton collisions at RHIC energies using the QCD-based Color-Singlet (CS) Model, including next-to-leading order partonic matrix elements. We also include charm-quark initiated processes which appear at leading order in alpha_s, but which have so far been overlooked in such studies. Contrary to earlier claims, we show that the CS yield is consistent with measurements over a broad range of J/Psi rapidities. We also find that charm-quark initiated processes, including both intrinsic and sea-like charm components, typically contribute at least 20% of the direct J/Psi yield, improving the agreement with data both for the integrated cross section and its rapidity dependence. The key signature for such processes is the observation of a charm-quark jet opposite in azimuthal angle phi to the detected J/Psi. Our results have impact on the proper interpretation of heavy-quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions and its use as a probe for the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Contrary to earlier claims, the Color-Singlet Model yield for J/psi production at RHIC is consistent with measurements over a broad range of rapidities, provided that charm-quark initiated processes (including both intrinsic and sea-like charm components) are included; these contribute at least 20% of the direct J/psi yield.

## Method

The authors computed total cross sections for J/psi, psi(2S), and Upsilon(1S) production in proton-proton collisions at RHIC using the Color-Singlet Model at leading order (alpha_s^3) and next-to-leading order (up to alpha_s^4) for gluon-gluon and quark-gluon fusion, using partonic matrix elements from Campbell, Maltoni and Tramontano (2007). They additionally computed charm-quark initiated processes (cg fusion) at leading order using the MADONIA tree-level matrix element generator, employing three different charm parton distribution functions: without intrinsic charm, with BHPS intrinsic charm (2% momentum fraction), and with sea-like intrinsic charm (2.4% momentum fraction). The theoretical uncertainties were obtained from combined variations of the heavy-quark mass (m_c = 1.5 ± 0.1 GeV, m_b = 4.75 ± 0.25 GeV) and the factorization and renormalization scales.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark initiated processes contribute at least 20% of the direct J/psi yield at RHIC
- The fraction of J/psi produced in association with a single c-quark via gc → J/psi c ranges from 10% up to 45% of the direct yield in the case of sea-like charm distribution
- For the J/psi, |R(0)|^2 = 1.01 GeV^3; for the psi(2S), |R(0)|^2 = 0.639 GeV^3; for the Upsilon(1S), |R(0)|^2 = 7.6 GeV^3
- The contribution from gg → J/psi c cbar at alpha_s^4 is at the level of 0.5% of the total cross section
- The BHPS intrinsic charm model uses <x>_{c+cbar} = 2%; the sea-like model uses <x>_{c+cbar} = 2.4%
- The direct J/psi feed-down fraction used was F^{direct}_{J/psi} = 59 ± 10%, based on chi_c feed-down of 30 ± 10% of the prompt yield

## Context

This work extends the Color-Singlet Model analysis of heavy-quarkonium production to RHIC energies, updating earlier studies that had claimed the CSM was insufficient to describe J/psi production. The authors show that the CSM at NLO accuracy, combined with charm-quark initiated processes, is consistent with PHENIX and STAR measurements, contradicting the conclusions of Cooper (2004) which had incorrectly assumed chi_c feed-down at alpha_s^2 to be the dominant source. The results enable future measurements of J/psi + charm jet azimuthal correlations as a probe of the charm content of the proton, and have implications for interpreting heavy-quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma.
