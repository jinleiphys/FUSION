# Axial Vector Current and Coupling of the Quark in the Instanton Model

> We compute the axial vector current, form factor, and coupling for quarks in the instanton liquid model with two light flavors. Non-local current corrections are derived, as required by the effective 't Hooft interaction. We obtain a pion-mediated axial form factor and an axial coupling which, when simply applied in the non-relativistic limit for constituent quarks, matches the experimental value to within a few percent, both in and out of the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The instanton liquid model (ILM) predicts a 25% reduction in the constituent quark axial coupling from unity, yielding values that match the experimental nucleon axial coupling to within a few percent when applied in the non-relativistic limit, both in and out of the chiral limit. The result depends on the assumption that relativistic wave function corrections to the constituent quark are nearly negligible.

## Method

The author computes the axial vector current for quarks in the instanton liquid model with two light flavors, deriving non-local current corrections required by the effective 't Hooft interaction to maintain current conservation. The axial coupling constant is then calculated by summing multi-instanton contributions including pion and sigma meson exchange in both local and non-local vertex diagrams, using the standard ILM parameters of instanton density (200 MeV)^4 and average instanton size ρ = 1/3 fm.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark axial coupling in the chiral limit: g_A = 0.73
- Constituent quark axial coupling with physical pion mass (m_π = 138 MeV): g_A = 0.77
- Nucleon axial coupling in the non-relativistic limit: G_A = 1.22 (chiral limit) and G_A = 1.28 (physical pion mass m_π = 140 MeV)
- Experimental nucleon axial coupling: |G_A/G_V| = 1.2670 ± 0.0035
- Pion-quark coupling constant: g_{πqq} = 3.78
- Sigma-quark coupling constant: g_{σqq} = 3.86
- Sigma mass: m_σ = 443 MeV (chiral limit) and m_σ = 540 MeV (physical pion mass)
- Instanton density: N/V = (200 MeV)^4
- Instanton size: ρ = 1/3 fm
- Constituent quark mass from gap equation: M = 345 MeV
- Individual diagram contributions to g_A in chiral limit: -0.102 (pion, local), -0.0425 (sigma, local), -0.101 (pion, non-local), -0.0236 (sigma, non-local)

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model approach to axial current phenomena, building on prior studies of non-local current corrections by Schwinger, Terning, and others. The results contradict earlier predictions of large negative corrections to g_A from the MIT bag model (CJJT), the large-N_c limit (Weinberg, BSL), and perturbative corrections (Duck). The paper enables a constituent quark-level explanation of the nucleon axial coupling without requiring relativistic wave function corrections, and suggests that the ILM contains the primary effects of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking responsible for the observed deviation of G_A from unity.
