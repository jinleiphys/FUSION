# Moments of the charge distribution observed through electron scattering in H3 and He3

> The moments of the charge distributions obtained by the analysis of electron-scattering data are examined in $^3$H and $^3$He. It is shown that the experimental form-factors are well explained, but the values of the moments of the charge distributions are not determined uniquly by the present analyses.

## Key claim
The experimental form factors of ³H and ³He are well reproduced by both the sum-of-Gaussians (SOG) and Fourier-Bessel (FB) analysis methods, but these "model-independent" methods yield different charge distributions from each other and therefore do not determine unique values for the moments of the charge distribution, because the available experimental data are limited to a finite range of momentum transfer and do not constrain the tail region of the charge distribution.

## Method
The authors compared charge distributions and form factors for ³H and ³He obtained from three sources: (1) SOG-analysis of world electron-scattering data from Ref. [am], (2) FB-analysis from Ref. [vries], and (3) ab initio calculations using the AV8'+3N potential solved by the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM). They constructed a corrected form factor by adding a parameterized function ΔF_c(q) to the theoretical AV8'+3N form factor to reproduce the SOG form factor, then examined the resulting charge densities and their moments, particularly in the tail region.

## Key numbers
- For ³H, the SOG method yields a root-mean-square charge radius of 1.76(4) fm, while the FB method yields 1.68(3) fm (from Ref. [vries]).
- For ³He, the SOG method yields 1.844(45) fm, while the FB method yields 1.877(19) fm (from Ref. [vries]).
- Ref. [am] provides 1.753(86) fm for ³H and 1.959(30) fm for ³He by SOG-analysis with updated world data.
- Recent laser spectroscopy of muonic ³He-ions gives the square root of the mean square radius as 1.97007(94) fm (from Ref. [atom]).
- The experimental data used in the SOG-analysis cover q² between 0.09 and 30 fm⁻² for ³H, and between 0.032 and 30 fm⁻² for ³He.
- The binding energies calculated with the AV8'+3N potential are 8.41 MeV for ³H and 7.74 MeV for ³He, compared to experimental values of 8.48 MeV and 7.72 MeV, respectively.
- The neutron charge distribution contributes to ⟨r⁶⟩_c by about 25% in ³H.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing effort to determine charge distributions of ³H and ³He from electron scattering, following the SOG-analysis of Ref. [am] and the FB-analysis of Ref. [vries]. The authors show that the SOG and FB methods, while successful for the gross shape of the charge distribution as shown in Ref. [am], fail to provide accurate moments because the tail region is not constrained by existing data. The paper calls for new accurate experimental data at momentum transfer squared less than 0.1 fm⁻² to resolve the ambiguity, and notes that recent muonic atom measurements (Ref. [atom]) provide a precise mean square radius for ³He that depends on higher moments determined via electron scattering.
