# Deep-inelastic multinucleon transfer processes in the $^{16}$O+$^{27}$Al reaction

> The reaction mechanism of deep-inelastic multinucleon transfer processes in the $^{16}$O+$^{27}$Al reaction at an incident $^{16}$O energy ($E_{\rm lab}=134$ MeV) substantially above the Coulomb barrier has been studied both experimentally and theoretically. Elastic-scattering angular distribution, total kinetic energy loss spectra and angular distributions for various transfer channels have been measured. The $Q$-value- and angle-integrated isotope production cross sections have been deduced. To obtain deeper insight into the underlying reaction mechanism, we have carried out a detailed analysis based on the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. A recently developed method, TDHF+GEMINI, has been applied to evaluate production cross sections for secondary products. From a comparison between the experimental and theoretical cross sections, we find that the theory qualitatively reproduces the experimental data. Significant effects of secondary light-particle emissions are demonstrated. Possible interplay between fusion-fission, deep-inelastic, multinucleon transfer and particle evaporation processes are discussed.

# Deep-inelastic multinucleon transfer processes in the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{27}\)Al reaction

## Key claim

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory combined with the statistical de-excitation model GEMINI (TDHF+GEMINI) qualitatively reproduces the experimental isotope production cross sections for the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{27}\)Al reaction at \(E_{\rm lab}=134\) MeV, but the agreement is only qualitative, not quantitative. The authors demonstrate that secondary light-particle emissions significantly affect the final yields, and that the reaction mechanism involves an interplay between deep-inelastic transfer, fusion-fission, and particle evaporation processes.

## Method

Elastic-scattering angular distributions, total kinetic energy loss (TKEL) spectra, and angular distributions for various transfer channels (producing projectile-like fragments from \(^{15,14}\)N down to \(^{6,7}\)Li) were measured using six silicon surface barrier detector telescopes in \(\Delta E\)-\(E\) configuration at the Pelletron-LINAC accelerator facility in Mumbai. The experimental data were compared with microscopic TDHF calculations using the Skyrme SL5y energy density functional, performed for three initial orientations of the triaxially deformed \(^{27}\)Al target over an impact parameter range of 0 to 12 fm. The TDHF+GEMINI approach was then applied to evaluate production cross sections for secondary products after statistical de-excitation.

## Key numbers

- Incident \(^{16}\)O laboratory energy: \(E_{\rm lab}=134\) MeV, with energy uncertainty \(\pm0.5\) MeV
- Ratio of incident energy to Coulomb barrier: \(E/V_{\rm B}\simeq5.3\)
- Target thickness: 540 \(\mu\)g/cm\(^2\) of \(^{27}\)Al foil
- Cumulative reaction cross section from optical-model fit: \(\sigma_{\rm R}=1379.3\) mb at \(E_{\rm lab}=134\) MeV
- Minimum impact parameter for binary reactions from TDHF: \(b_{\rm min}=7.025\) fm (shrinking-\(x\)), 6.348 fm (shrinking-\(y\)), and 6.899 fm (shrinking-\(z\)) orientation of \(^{27}\)Al
- Maximum TKEL from TDHF calculations: approximately 55 MeV
- Maximum experimental TKEL observed: approximately 70 MeV for the \(^{27}\)Al(\(^{16}\)O,\(^{6}\)Li) channel
- Angular resolution of telescopes: less than 1.4\(^\circ\)
- Uncertainty in absolute cross section from target thickness measurement: about 2%; from solid angle measurement: about 1%
- Uncertainty from angular distribution fitting procedure: 9–13% for most channels, 16–17% for \(^9\)Be and \(^{13}\)C

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{27}\)Al reaction at lower energies (88–100 MeV) by Mikumo (1980) and Cormier (1976), which established that deep-inelastic transfer processes dominate the reaction mechanism. It also addresses the conflicting conclusions of Puhlhofer (1972), who argued that fusion-fission contributes little up to 165 MeV, and Bhattacharya (2002), who found fusion-fission competitive with deep-inelastic processes at 116 MeV. The use of TDHF+GEMINI, a method recently developed by Sekizawa (2017), enables the first microscopic evaluation of secondary product cross sections for this light system, and the results enable future detailed comparisons of mass-angle correlations and Wilczynski plots with experimental data for deeper understanding of the reaction dynamics.
