# The Physics of Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay: A Primer

> Neutrinoless double beta decay is a hypothetical radioactive process which, if observed, would prove the neutrino to be a Majorana fermion: a particle that is its own antiparticle. In this lecture mini-series I discuss the physics of Majorana fermions and the connection between the nature of neutrino mass and neutrinoless double beta decay. We review Dirac and Majorana spinors, discuss methods of distinguishing between Majorana and Dirac fermions, and derive in outline the connection between neutrino mass and double beta decay rates. We conclude by briefly summarizing the experimental landscape and the challenges associated with searches for this elusive process.

## Key claim

Neutrinoless double beta decay, if observed, would prove the neutrino is a Majorana fermion, but the rate is suppressed by a factor of \(m_{\nu}^{2}/E^{2}\) relative to standard lepton-number-conserving processes, making the half-life at least \(\geq 10^{26}\) years in all practical isotopes.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations of neutrinoless double beta decay, beginning with Dirac and Majorana spinor formalism in the Weyl basis and the Majorana condition \(\psi^{c} = i\gamma^{2}\psi^{*} = \psi\). It then derives the connection between neutrino mass, chirality, and helicity to explain why the lepton-number-violating process is suppressed, and outlines how the nuclear pairing force enables certain even-even isotopes (e.g., \(^{136}\)Xe) to undergo double beta decay while forbidding single beta decay. The rate calculation is sketched using the effective Hamiltonian with Fermi constant, CKM matrix element, and hadronic currents including the axial coupling \(g_{A}\).

## Key numbers

* The experimentally allowed half-life for neutrinoless double beta decay is \(t_{1/2} \geq 10^{26}\) years in all practical isotopes.
* The lepton-number-violating process is suppressed relative to the lepton-number-conserving process by a factor of \(m_{\nu}^{2}/E^{2}\); for reactor neutrino experiments with energies of a few MeV and neutrino mass less than about 1 eV, at most one in every \(\sim 2 \times 10^{13}\) neutrinos might interact via the lepton-number-violating mode.
* The two-neutrino double beta decay mode goes at least \(10^{6}\) times faster than the predicted neutrinoless mode in xenon.
* Next-generation experiments require energy resolution of at most 2% FWHM to reduce background from two-neutrino double beta decay to around 0.1 counts per ton per year.

## Context

This primer extends pedagogical treatments of neutrino physics from standard textbooks such as Thomson (2013) and Halzen and Martin (2008), and reviews the theoretical framework connecting Majorana neutrinos to neutrinoless double beta decay as developed in works by Haxton (1984), Bilenky (2012, 2015), and Engel (2017). The paper enables students to understand why the neutrino beam thought experiment fails due to helicity suppression and why nuclear double beta decay provides a viable alternative, while pointing to experimental reviews by Gomez-Cadenas (2011), Dolinski (2019), and Elliott (2002) for details on search technologies.
