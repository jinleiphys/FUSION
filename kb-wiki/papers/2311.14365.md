# Contrasting observables related to the $N^*(1535)$ from the molecular or a genuine structure

> In this work we compare the predictions for the scattering length and effective range of the channels $K^0 Σ^+, K^+ Σ^0 , K^+ Λ$ and $ηp$, assuming the $N^*(1535)$ state as a molecular state of these channels, or an original genuine state, made for instance from three quarks. Looking at very different scenarios, what we conclude is that the predictions of these two pictures are drastically different, to the point that we advise the measurement of these magnitudes, accessible for instance by measuring correlation functions, in order to gain much valuable information concerning the nature of this state.

# Contrasting observables related to the $N^*(1535)$ from the molecular or a genuine structure

## Key claim
The predictions for scattering lengths and effective ranges of the $K^0\Sigma^+$, $K^+\Sigma^0$, $K^+\Lambda$, and $\eta p$ channels are drastically different depending on whether the $N^*(1535)$ is assumed to be a molecular state (dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions) or a genuine compact state (e.g., three-quark state). The authors conclude that measuring these observables via correlation functions can distinguish between the two pictures, but the claim is conditional: the genuine-state model cannot simultaneously reproduce the molecular-model predictions for all channels and the pole position, with $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.}$ values around 150-170 indicating extremely poor fits.

## Method
The authors first compute scattering lengths, effective ranges, and the pole position for the $N^*(1535)$ using the chiral unitary approach (molecular picture) with coupled channels $K^0\Sigma^+$, $K^+\Sigma^0$, $K^+\Lambda$, and $\eta p$, using a cutoff $q_{\mathrm{max}}=630$ MeV. They then construct a model where the $N^*(1535)$ is a genuine state with a bare mass $M_R$ and couplings $\tilde{g}_i$ to each channel, using a separable potential $V_{ij}^{(c)} = \tilde{g}_i\tilde{g}_j/(\sqrt{s}-M_R)$. The four free parameters (three couplings and the bare mass) are fitted to the molecular-model results under three strategies: fitting only channel 1 data, fitting channel 1 data plus the pole position, and fitting all channels' data plus the pole position.

## Key numbers
- Molecular picture pole position: $\sqrt{s}_R = 1519.52 - i\,82.21$ MeV
- Molecular picture scattering lengths (fm): $a_1 = 0.42(\pm0.04) - i\,0.60(\pm0.03)$ for $K^0\Sigma^+$; $a_2 = 0.3(\pm0.01) - i\,0.32(\pm0.02)$ for $K^+\Sigma^0$; $a_3 = 0.29(\pm0.02) - i\,0.20(\pm0.04)$ for $K^+\Lambda$; $a_4 = -0.72(\pm0.013)$ for $\eta p$
- Molecular picture effective ranges (fm): $r_{0,1} = -1.13(\pm0.2) - i\,2.68(\pm0.2)$; $r_{0,2} = -5.81(\pm1.4) + i\,8.91(\pm0.5)$; $r_{0,3} = -3.13(\pm0.3) - i\,0.24(\pm0.6)$; $r_{0,4} = -1.73(\pm0.13)$
- Genuine state fit (Strategy 3, all channels): $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} \approx 154.3$; bare mass $M_R = 1900.31$ MeV; couplings $\tilde{g}=4.51$, $\tilde{g}_3=1.80$, $\tilde{g}_4=2.92$
- Genuine state fit (Strategy 2, channel 1 + pole): $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} \approx 174.95$; bare mass $M_R = 1837.70$ MeV
- Genuine state fit (Strategy 1, channel 1 only): pole at $\sqrt{s}_R = 1679.28 - i\,26.50$ MeV, far from the molecular prediction

## Context
This work extends the approach of Song (2022) and Dai (2023), who showed that combined knowledge of binding energy, scattering length, and effective range can determine the molecular compositeness of states like the $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$. It contrasts with the three-quark interpretation of the $N^*(1535)$ supported by Liu (2015) and Abell (2023), which include $\eta N$ and $\pi N$ channels but omit the $K\Sigma$ channel essential for the molecular picture as shown by Molina (2023). The results provide strong motivation for experimental measurement of correlation functions for these channels at facilities like ALICE, CMS, and STAR, which would resolve the nature of the $N^*(1535)$.
