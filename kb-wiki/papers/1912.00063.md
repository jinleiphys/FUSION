# Minkowski-space solutions of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the fermion propagator with the rainbow-ladder truncation

> We solve the Minkowski-space Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) for the fermion propagator in quantum electrodynamics (QED) with massive photons. Specifically, we work in the quenched approximation within the rainbow-ladder truncation. Loop-divergences are regularized by the Pauli-Villars regularization. With moderately strong fermion-photon coupling, we find that the analytic structure of the fermion propagator consists of an on-shell pole and branch-cuts located in the timelike region. Such structures are consistent with the direct solution of the fermion propagator as functions of the complex momentum. Our method paves the way towards the calculation of the Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter amplitude using dressed fermion propagator.

## Key claim

The fermion propagator in Minkowski-space QED with massive photons, solved in the quenched rainbow-ladder truncation with Pauli-Villars regularization, has an analytic structure consisting of an on-shell pole and branch-cuts in the timelike region, but this result holds only for moderately strong coupling and under the specific truncation and regularization scheme used.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) for the fermion propagator directly in Minkowski space, working in the quenched approximation (gauge-boson propagator remains bare) and the rainbow-ladder truncation (fermion-photon vertex replaced by \(\gamma^\mu\)). Loop divergences are regularized using Pauli-Villars regularization, which introduces a regulator mass \(\Lambda\) and makes the self-energy vanish asymptotically. The spectral representation of the fermion propagator is assumed to consist of a mass-shell pole and branch-cuts, and the spectral functions are obtained by iteratively solving the resulting nonlinear integral equations for the imaginary parts of the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- Fermion on-shell mass \(m = 0.6502\) for input bare mass \(m_{\mathrm{B}} = 0.5\), coupling \(e^2/(4\pi) = 0.3\), photon mass \(m_{\mathrm{A}} = 1\), and Pauli-Villars regulator mass \(\Lambda = 10\).
- Pole residue \(Z = 0.9096\) for the same input parameters.
- The threshold for branch-cuts is set by the finite distance between the pole and branch points due to the massive photon in Feynman gauge.

## Context

This work extends the Minkowski-space SDE approach of Sauli (2004) and Jia (2017) to the fermion propagator in massive QED, and it directly compares its results to the un-Wick rotation technique of Frederico (2019), finding agreement within numerical uncertainties in the Euclidean region. The method enables future calculation of Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for two-fermion bound states using the dressed fermion propagator as input.
