# Quantum and stringy corrections to the equation of state of holographic QCD matter and the nature of the chiral transition

> We consider the finite temperature phase diagram of holographic QCD in the Veneziano limit (Nc large, Nf large with xf=Nf/Nc fixed) and calculate one string-loop corrections to the free energy in certain approximations. Such corrections, especially due to the pion modes are unsuppressed in the Veneziano limit. We find that under some extra assumptions the first order transition following from classical gravity solutions can become second order. If stringy asymptotics are of a special form and there are residual interactions it may even become of third order. Operationally these computations imply modelling the low temperature chiral symmetry breaking phase with a hadron gas containing Nf^2 massless Goldstone bosons and an exponential spectrum of massive hadrons. A third order transition is possible only if repulsive hadron interactions via the excluded volume effect are included.

## Key claim

Under specific extra assumptions about the form of stringy corrections and the inclusion of repulsive hadron interactions via the excluded volume effect, the first-order chiral transition predicted by classical gravity solutions can become second order or even third order, rather than remaining first order as the abstract might suggest.

## Method

The authors compute the high-temperature plasma phase pressure from a five-dimensional holographic V-QCD model in the Veneziano limit (large Nc, large Nf with xf=Nf/Nc fixed), using black hole solutions to obtain thermodynamics. For the low-temperature hadronic phase, they model the pressure using an ideal gas of Nf^2 massless Goldstone bosons plus an exponential Hagedorn mass spectrum of massive hadrons, with parameters constrained by matching to the plasma phase. They then introduce repulsive Van der Waals-type interactions via the excluded volume correction to modify the hadron gas equation of state and test whether continuity of the pressure and its first and second logarithmic derivatives can be achieved.

## Key numbers

* For xf=2, the plasma phase pressure vanishes at T/Lambda = 0.0925, and the Goldstone-boson-only hadron gas gives a first-order transition at T = 0.125 Lambda.
* The lowest vector meson masses (in units of Lambda) are 2.815, 4.731, 6.076, 7.188 for xf=1; 0.707, 1.156, 1.477, 1.744 for xf=2; and 0.0795, 0.1270, 0.1612, 0.1897 for xf=2.5.
* The lowest scalar meson masses (in units of Lambda) are 2.640, 4.568, 5.857, 6.958 for xf=1; 0.599, 1.102, 1.405, 1.670 for xf=2; and 0.06224, 0.1198, 0.1519, 0.1802 for xf=2.5.
* The toy model for the Hagedorn spectrum gives b m0 = pi sqrt(2) approximately 4.44 and a = -2, with rho0 approximately 0.177.
* For xf=1 with m0=2.8, a second-order transition can be fitted with T_c around 0.6 Lambda and b around 1-2 depending on parameters.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature study of V-QCD models in the Veneziano limit previously developed by the same authors (Alho et al. 2014, 2015) and by Jarvinen and Kiritsis (2012), where classical gravity solutions predicted either one or two phase transitions. The analysis of string-loop corrections builds on the observation that pion loop contributions are unsuppressed in the Veneziano limit, unlike in the 't Hooft limit. The paper enables future studies of how the chiral transition order depends on the number of flavors and on hadronic interactions, and it suggests that the transition could be of third order, similar to the large-N limit of two-dimensional lattice gauge theory (Gross and Witten 1980, Wadia 1980), rather than the first-order transition expected from epsilon-expansion universality arguments (Pisarski and Wilczek 1984).
