# Local energy-density functional approach to many-body nuclear systems with s-wave pairing

> The ground-state properties of superfluid nuclear systems with ^1S_0 pairing are studied within a local energy-density functional (LEDF) approach. A new form of the LEDF is proposed with a volume part which fits the Friedman- Pandharipande and Wiringa-Fiks-Fabrocini equation of state at low and moderate densities and allows an extrapolation to higher densities preserving causality. For inhomogeneous systems, a surface term with two free parameters is added. In addition to the Coulomb direct and exchange interaction energy, an effective density-dependent Coulomb-nuclear correlation term is included with one more free parameter, giving a contribution of the same order of magnitude as the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in Coulomb displacement energy. The root-mean-square deviations from experimental masses and radii with the proposed LEDF come out about a factor of two smaller than those obtained with the conventional functionals based on the Skyrme or finite-range Gogny force, or on the relativistic mean-field theory. The generalized variational principle is formulated leading to the self-consistent Gor'kov equations which are solved exactly, with physical boundary conditions both for the bound and scattering states. With a zero-range density-dependent cutoff pairing interaction incorporating a density-gradient term, the evolution of differential observables such as odd-even mass differences and staggering in charge radii, is reproduced reasonably well, including kinks at magic neutron numbers. An extrapolation to infinite nuclear matter is discussed. We study also the dilute limit in both the weak and strong coupling regime.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new local energy-density functional (LEDF) that reproduces ground-state properties of superfluid nuclei with s-wave pairing, achieving root-mean-square deviations from experimental masses and radii about a factor of two smaller than conventional Skyrme, Gogny, or relativistic mean-field functionals, but only after including a three-parameter fit to masses and radii of about 100 spherical nuclei and a density-dependent Coulomb-nuclear correlation term.

## Method

The authors construct a Kohn-Sham type energy-density functional with a volume term fitted to the Friedman-Pandharipande and Wiringa-Fiks-Fabrocini equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, a surface term with two free parameters in a fractional Padé-like form containing density gradients, and a Coulomb term with an effective density-dependent Coulomb-nuclear correlation term. They formulate a generalized variational principle leading to self-consistent Gor'kov equations solved exactly in coordinate space with physical boundary conditions for both bound and scattering states, using a zero-range density-dependent cutoff pairing interaction with a density-gradient term.

## Context

This work extends the local energy-density functional approach introduced by Fayans (1998) and builds on earlier formulations by Stancu, Brink, and Flocard (1988) for finite systems with s-wave pairing. The proposed functional (abbreviated FaNDF⁰) improves upon conventional Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic mean-field models by reducing rms deviations in masses and radii by roughly a factor of two, and it addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in Coulomb displacement energies through a Coulomb-nuclear correlation term. The approach enables extrapolation to higher densities preserving causality, relevant for neutron star crust calculations, and provides a framework for studying pairing in both finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter from the weak to strong coupling regime.
