# Interacting boson models of nuclear and nucleon structure

> Interacting boson models provide an elegant and powerful method to describe collective excitations of complex systems by introducing a set of effective degrees of freedom. We review the interacting boson model of nuclear structure and discuss a recent extension to the nucleon and its excited states.

# Interacting Boson Models of Nuclear and Nucleon Structure

## Key claim

Interacting boson models provide an elegant and powerful method to describe collective excitations of complex systems by introducing effective degrees of freedom, but the paper is a review that surveys existing models rather than presenting a single new result; the authors note that for nucleon structure, the energy domain (several GeV) belongs to the nonperturbative regime of QCD where no exact solution exists, so one must rely on models and symmetries.

## Method

The paper reviews the interacting boson model (IBM-1) for nuclear structure, in which low-lying collective states in even-even nuclei are described by a system of N interacting bosons with angular momentum and parity L^P = 0^+ (monopole) and L^P = 2^+ (quadrupole), spanning a six-dimensional space with group structure U(6). For nucleon structure, the authors discuss an extension using a U(7) algebraic model in which baryons are built from three constituent parts, with relative motion described by Jacobi coordinates and bosonic quantization introducing dipole bosons (L^P = 1^-) and an auxiliary scalar boson (L^P = 0^+). The classical limits of both models are studied via coherent states to obtain geometric interpretations of the dynamical symmetries.

## Key numbers

- The energy scale for nuclear structure is several MeV (10^6 eV), while for nucleon excitations it is several GeV (10^9 eV), differing by three orders of magnitude.
- For the nucleus 154Sm, the IBM treats it as a system of N = 6 + 5 = 11 interacting bosons, reducing the number of states with L^P = 0^+, 2^+, and 4^+ from shell model values by a factor of 10^12 to 10^13.
- A simultaneous fit to 25 well-established (3 and 4 star) nucleon and delta resonances using the U(7) model gives a root-mean-square deviation of 39 MeV.
- The N(1440) and N(1710) resonances are associated with vibrational excitations with quantum numbers (v1, v2) = (1,0) and (0,1), respectively.

## Context

This review extends the interacting boson model framework, originally developed for nuclear structure by Arima and Iachello (IBM), to the nucleon and its excited states, building on earlier algebraic approaches to baryon structure by Bijker, Leviatan, and collaborators (BIL). The work unifies various exactly solvable models of baryon structure within a single U(7) algebraic framework, enabling systematic study of baryon resonances; the authors note that the phase triangle for nucleon structure is similar to the nuclear case (spherical, deformed, and rigid deformed shapes), but whereas many nuclei populate different regions of the triangle, there is only one baryon spectrum, leaving open the question of where the nonstrange baryon mass spectrum fits.
