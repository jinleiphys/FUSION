# Role of charge equilibration in multinucleon transfer in damped collisions of heavy ions

> In this work, the charge equilibration process has been analyzed within the Langevin-type dynamical approach. Its duration and energy dependence are discussed. We have analyzed the isotopic distributions of final products obtained in the isospin-asymmetric 58Ni,40Ca + 208Pb reactions. Comparison of 58Ni,64Ni + 208Pb systems have been done in order to analyze the final yields of neutron-rich heavy nuclides.

## Key claim

The charge equilibration process in isospin-asymmetric heavy-ion collisions takes approximately 0.5 zs, and the Langevin-type dynamical model can reproduce experimental isotopic distributions only after introducing two modifications: equal sharing of excitation energy at the initial stage and increased fluctuations of the charge asymmetry degree of freedom until the excitation energy reaches 10 MeV.

## Method

The authors used a Langevin-type dynamical model with eight degrees of freedom (including charge asymmetry) to simulate collisions of 58Ni, 40Ca, and 64Ni projectiles with 208Pb targets at energies near the Coulomb barrier. They solved the Langevin equations numerically for many trajectories at each impact parameter, then fed the primary reaction products into a statistical model that simulated decay cascades (neutron, proton, alpha, and gamma emission plus sequential fission) to obtain final fragment distributions. The model was modified by introducing a time-dependent transition from equal to mass-proportional excitation energy sharing (with characteristic time τ = 2 zs) and by doubling the random force for the charge-asymmetry degree of freedom at the initial stage of collision.

## Key numbers

- Charge equilibration time extracted for the 58Ni + 208Pb reaction: 0.5 zs
- Characteristic transition time for excitation energy sharing: τ = 2 zs
- Threshold for doubled fluctuations of charge asymmetry: excitation energy reaches 10 MeV
- 58Ni + 208Pb reaction studied at Elab. = 328.4 MeV and Elab. = 345 MeV
- 40Ca + 208Pb reaction studied at Elab. = 249 MeV
- 64Ni + 208Pb reaction studied at Elab. = 350 MeV
- Angular ranges for 58Ni + 208Pb: 70° < θlab. < 110°; for 40Ca + 208Pb: 42° < θlab. < 100°

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Langevin-type model (Karpov and Saiko, 2018; Saiko and Karpov, 2019) which successfully described isospin-symmetric systems (136Xe + 198Pt,208Pb,209Bi; 160Gd + 186W; 208Pb + 208Pb; 238U + 238U,248Cm) but failed for isospin-asymmetric combinations. The charge equilibration time of 0.5 zs coincides with relaxation times from microscopic TDHF calculations (Umar, 2015). The improved model enables comparison of 58Ni and 64Ni + 208Pb reactions to identify which system is more favorable for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclides, concluding that the isospin-symmetric 64Ni + 208Pb system yields larger production cross sections of neutron-rich isotopes despite lower integral yields of heavy elements.
