# Hydrodynamization and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions: a kinetic theory perspective

> Understanding the applicability of fluid-dynamical models to describe the hot and dense matter produced in the early stages of hadronic collisions is a fundamental problem in the field. In particular, it is not clear to what degree this hydrodynamization process requires proximity to a local equilibrium state. In this contribution, we study this problem in kinetic theory considering an ultrarelativistic gas undergoing strong longitudinal expansion, assuming Bjorken flow. We solve the Boltzmann equation and verify that the system displays considerable deviations from local equilibrium, even though the energy-momentum tensor is well described by fluid dynamics. We further quantify this effect computing the emission of photons in the quark-gluon plasma and verify whether this deviation from equilibrium can be observed.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamization of the quark-gluon plasma can occur even when the system is far from local equilibrium: the energy-momentum tensor is well described by fluid dynamics while the single-particle distribution function itself shows considerable deviations from equilibrium, and these deviations may be observable through early-time thermal photon emission.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a massless classical gas undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion (Bjorken flow), using the method of moments with truncation at N=20 and L=10 to obtain an exact solution. They compared this exact solution to the 14-moment approximation (a fluid-dynamical truncation) and computed thermal photon emission rates from quark-antiquark annihilation and Compton scattering processes in the forward scattering approximation.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: proper time τ₀ = 0.1 fm, temperature T(τ₀) = 10 GeV, thermal potential α(τ₀) = 0
- Relaxation time assumed constant: τ_R = 1 fm
- Strong coupling constant: α_s = 0.3
- Comparison of exact and 14-moment solutions performed at τ = 0.5 fm for the distribution function and at τ = 0.8 fm for photon momentum anisotropy
- Photon emission rate formula includes factor 5/9 and logarithmic term ln(3.7388 k₀ / (g_s² T))

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach to hydrodynamization in heavy-ion collisions, building on the method of moments developed by Denicol (2012) and the resummation techniques for the moment expansion developed by de Brito (2024). The authors show that the 14-moment approximation, which is commonly used in fluid-dynamical models, significantly underestimates the exact nonequilibrium distribution function even when the energy-momentum tensor is well captured. This enables future work to use early-time photon emission as a probe of whether the quark-gluon plasma is actually close to local equilibrium, following the photon emission framework of Shen (2014) and Dusling (2009).
