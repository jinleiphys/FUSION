# Electron and Photon Scattering on Three-Nucleon Bound States

> A big spectrum of processes induced by real and virtual photons on the 3He and 3H nuclei is theoretically investigated through many examples based on nonrelativistic Faddeev calculations for bound and continuum states. The modern nucleon-nucleon potential AV18 together with the three-nucleon force UrbanaIX is used. The single nucleon current is augmented by explicit pi- and rho-like two-body currents which fulfill the current continuity equation together with the corresponding parts of the AV18 potential. We also employ the Siegert theorem, which induces many-body contributions to the current operator. The interplay of these different dynamical ingredients in the various electromagnetic processes is studied and the theory is compared to the experimental data. Overall we find fair to good agreement but also cases of strong disagreement between theory and experiment, which calls for improved dynamics. In several cases we refer the reader to the work of other groups and compare their results with ours. In addition we list a number of predictions for observables in different processes which would challenge this dynamical scenario even more stringently and systematically.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nonrelativistic Faddeev calculations using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus the UrbanaIX three-nucleon force, augmented by explicit pi- and rho-like two-body currents and the Siegert theorem, achieve fair to good agreement with experimental data for a wide range of photon-induced processes on 3He and 3H, but also show cases of strong disagreement that call for improved dynamics.

## Method

The authors solved nonrelativistic Faddeev equations for three-nucleon bound and continuum states using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and the UrbanaIX three-nucleon force. They employed a single-nucleon current operator augmented by explicit pi- and rho-like two-body currents that satisfy the current continuity equation with the corresponding parts of the AV18 potential, and also applied the Siegert theorem to induce many-body contributions to the current operator. The formalism was applied to elastic electron scattering, inclusive and exclusive electron-induced processes, photodisintegration, and nucleon-deuteron radiative capture on 3He and 3H.

## Key numbers

- The calculations used the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential together with the UrbanaIX three-nucleon force as the dynamical input
- The two-body currents were constructed to be consistent with the pi- and rho-like exchange parts of the AV18 potential
- The Siegert theorem was employed to incorporate additional many-body current contributions
- The paper reports "fair to good agreement" but also "cases of strong disagreement" between theory and experiment across the various processes studied

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations for three-nucleon systems that used simpler separable forces (Lehman 1969-1971, Barbour 1967-1970) or only single-nucleon currents, by incorporating realistic nuclear forces with consistent two-body currents. The approach builds on the method of Riska (1985) for associating two-body currents to nucleon-nucleon forces through the continuity equation. The results enable systematic tests of the dynamical scenario through predictions for observables in various electromagnetic processes, and the authors identify specific disagreements that motivate further theoretical development including the explicit inclusion of delta-isobar degrees of freedom as pursued by the Hanover group (Deltuva 2004).
