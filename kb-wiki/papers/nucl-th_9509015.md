# Effect of $NN$-correlations on predictions of nuclear transparencies for protons, knocked-out in high $Q^2\,(e,e'p)$ reactions

> We study the transparency $\cal T$ of nuclei for nucleons knocked-out in high-energy semi-inclusive $(e,e'p)$ reactions, using an improved theoretical input, discussed by Nikolaev et al. We establish that neglect of $NN$-correlations between the knocked-out and core nucleons reduces nuclear transparencies by $\approx 15 \%$ for light, to $\approx 10\%$ for heavy nuclei. About the same is predicted for transparencies, integrated over the transverse or longitudinal momentum of the outgoing proton. Hadron dynamics predicts a roughly constant $\cal T$ beyond $Q^2\approx $2 GeV$^2$, whereas for all targets the largest measured data point $Q^2$=6.7 GeV$^2$ appears to lie above that plateau. Large error bars on those data-points preclude a conclusion regarding the onset of colour transparency.

## Key claim

Neglect of NN-correlations between the knocked-out and core nucleons reduces nuclear transparencies by approximately 15% for light nuclei to approximately 10% for heavy nuclei, but the largest measured data point at Q^2 = 6.7 GeV^2 lies above the hadronic plateau for all targets, although large error bars preclude a conclusion regarding the onset of colour transparency.

## Method

The authors computed semi-inclusive (e,e'p) transparencies using Glauber theory for final-state interactions, incorporating binary collision terms in a multiple-scattering expansion of the FSI operator. They evaluated the energy-integrated transparency T^E and the transverse-momentum-integrated transparency T^{E,P_perp} for carbon, iron, and gold targets at the four Q^2 values of the NE18 experiment, both with and without NN-correlations included via the pair distribution function zeta_2 in the two-body density matrix.

## Key numbers

- Neglect of NN-correlations reduces transparencies by approximately 15% for light nuclei (carbon) and approximately 10% for heavy nuclei (gold), in a nearly Q^2-independent fashion.
- For Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, the equal-impact-parameter approximation considerably overestimates the proper T^E; for all other data points the overestimate is less than 10%.
- The largest measured data point is at Q^2 = 6.7 GeV^2, which lies above the predicted hadronic plateau for all targets.
- The NE18 experiment covered momentum transfers 1 <= Q^2 (GeV^2) <= 6.7 on various targets.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Nikolaev et al. (1993, 1995) by numerically assessing the effect of NN-correlations that those authors had neglected, and it also extends the earlier work of Rinat and Taragin (1995) which used an equal-impact-parameter approximation. The results enable comparison with the SLAC NE18 data (Makins et al., 1994; O'Neill et al., 1995) and show that current error bars cannot distinguish between predictions with or without correlations, nor confirm the onset of colour transparency.
