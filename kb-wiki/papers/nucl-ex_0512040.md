# $2α+t$ cluster structure in $^{11}$B

> The cluster structures of the excited states in $^{11}$B are studied by analyzing the isoscalar monopole and quadrupole strengths in the $^{11}$B($d$,$d'$) reaction at $E_d=200$ MeV. The excitation strengths are compared with the predictions by the shell-model and antisymmetrized molecular-dynamics (AMD) calculations. It is found that the large monopole strength for the $3/2^-_3$ state at $E_x=8.56$ MeV is well described by the AMD calculation and is an evidence for a developed $2α+t$ cluster structure.

## Key claim

The large isoscalar monopole strength observed for the \(3/2^-_3\) state at \(E_x = 8.56\) MeV in \(^{11}\)B is suggested to be evidence for a well-developed \(2\alpha + t\) cluster structure, based on comparison with antisymmetrized molecular-dynamics (AMD) calculations. The claim is qualified by noting that the AMD calculation tends to underestimate the long tail of the wave function at large radius, so the quantitative reproduction of isotopic shifts is not perfect, but the general trend and state-by-state dependence are well reproduced.

## Method

The isoscalar monopole and quadrupole excitation strengths in \(^{11}\)B were extracted via multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) of angular distributions from the \(^{11}\)B(\(d,d'\)) reaction at \(E_d = 200\) MeV, measured at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics, Osaka University. The cross sections were calculated in a macroscopic deformed potential model, and the multipole contributions with \(\Delta J \ge 3\) were neglected. The extracted strengths were compared with shell-model (SM) calculations using the SFO interaction within a \(0-2\hbar\omega\) configuration space and with AMD calculations using the variational-after-spin-parity-projection (VAP) method with MV1 and G3RS interactions.

## Key numbers

- The \(3/2^-_3\) state at \(E_x = 8.56\) MeV is excited with a large isoscalar monopole strength; the \(B(E0;IS)\) value extracted from reanalysis of \((e,e')\) data is \(90 \pm 3\) fm\(^4\).
- The \(B(E0)\) value from the reanalysis of \((e,e')\) data is \(18.7 \pm 0.7\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- The reported \(B(E2)\) value from \((e,e')\) for the \(3/2^-_3\) state is \(9.4 \pm 0.2\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (KAN75), but this is deemed unreliable because the \(E0\) transition was neglected.
- The AMD (VAP) calculation predicts a \(B(E2)\) value of \(0.84\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) for the \(3/2^-_3\) state.
- The \(0^+_2\) state in \(^{12}\)C at \(E_x = 7.65\) MeV has an isoscalar monopole strength of \(B(E0;IS) = 121 \pm 9\) fm\(^4\) (AJZE90).
- The \(3/2^-_3\) state in \(^{11}\)B is located 100 keV below the \(\alpha\)-decay threshold.

## Context

This work extends the study of dilute cluster states, previously established for self-conjugate \(N=4n\) nuclei such as the \(0^+_2\) state in \(^{12}\)C (Ikeda 1968; Tohsaki 2001; Funaki 2003, 2005), to the non-self-conjugate nucleus \(^{11}\)B. The \(3/2^-_3\) state was previously identified as having abnormally quenched Gamow-Teller and spin-flip \(M1\) strengths (Papa 2004; Fujita 2004; Hannon 2003), suggesting a non-shell-model structure. The present work enables future studies of cluster structures in \(N \neq 4n\) nuclei and motivates precise measurements of \(E2\) strengths for the \(5/2^-_2\) state to clarify the coexistence of shell-model-like and cluster components.
