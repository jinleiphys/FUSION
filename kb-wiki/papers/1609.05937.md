# Glue spin and helicity in proton from lattice QCD

> We report the first lattice QCD calculation of the glue spin in the nucleon. The lattice calculation is carried out with valence overlap fermions on 2+1 flavor DWF gauge configurations on four lattice spacings and four volumes including an ensemble with physical values for the quark masses. The glue spin $S_{G}$ in the Coulomb gauge in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme is obtained with the 1-loop perturbative matching. We find the results fairly insensitive to lattice spacing and quark masses. We also find that the proton momentum dependence of $S_G$ in the range $0\leq |\vec{p}| < 1.5$ GeV is very mild, and we determine it in the large momentum limit to be $S_{G}=0.251(47)(16)$ at the physical pion mass in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at $μ^2=10$ GeV$^2$. If the matching procedure in large momentum effective theory is neglected, $S_G$ is equal to the glue helicity measured in high-energy scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of the glue spin in the nucleon, finding that the gluon helicity at the physical pion mass is approximately 0.251(47)(16) in the MSbar scheme at 10 GeV^2, which constitutes about 50% of the total proton spin. However, this result neglects the 1-loop matching from large momentum effective theory (LaMET) because the perturbative matching factor is only 0.22 at the available momentum scale, indicating a convergence problem that makes the direct matching unreliable.

## Method

The calculation uses valence overlap fermions on 2+1 flavor domain-wall fermion gauge configurations from the RBC/UKQCD collaboration across four lattice spacings (0.0627 to 0.1431 fm) and four volumes, including an ensemble with physical quark masses. The glue spin operator is defined as the matrix element of the chromoelectric field crossed with the gauge potential in Coulomb gauge, computed from disconnected three-point functions with the nucleon interpolating field. The bare lattice results are renormalized using 1-loop perturbative matching with Cactus improvement to the MSbar scheme at 10 GeV^2, and the frame dependence is studied by computing the glue spin at proton momenta up to 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Glue spin in the large momentum limit: S_G = 0.251(47)(16) at the physical pion mass in the MSbar scheme at mu^2 = 10 GeV^2, where the first error is statistical and the second is systematic.
- The glue spin constitutes 50(9)(3)% of the total proton spin.
- The 1-loop LaMET matching factor between S_G and Delta G at mu^2 = 10 GeV^2 and |p| = 1.5 GeV is 0.22, indicating a convergence problem for the perturbative series.
- The global fit to the frame dependence uses data from five ensembles with pion masses ranging from 140 MeV (physical) to 370 MeV, lattice spacings from 0.0627 fm to 0.1431 fm, and volumes from 2.0^3 x 4.0 fm^4 to 5.5^3 x 11.0 fm^4.

## Context

This work provides the first direct lattice QCD determination of the glue spin in the nucleon, addressing the "proton spin crisis" where deep-inelastic scattering experiments (Accardi 2012, de Florian 2009, Aidala 2012) showed that quark spin contributes only about 30% of the proton spin. The result extends earlier phenomenological analyses of glue helicity from RHIC experiments (STAR 2013, PHENIX 2014) and COMPASS (Adolph 2015), which found evidence for non-zero glue helicity but with large uncertainties. The calculation enables future improvements through non-perturbative renormalization and higher-order LaMET matching, and suggests that the glue spin in temporal gauge could avoid the current matching difficulties (Hatta 2013).
