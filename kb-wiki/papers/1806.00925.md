# KN scattering amplitude revisited in a chiral unitary approach and a possible broad resonance in S=+1 channel

> We revisit the $KN$ scattering amplitude in order to investigate the possibility for the existence of a broad resonance in the $I=0$ $KN$ channel around the energy of 1617 MeV with 305 MeV width. We use the chiral unitary model to describe the $KN$ scattering amplitudes and determine the model parameters so as to reproduce the differential cross sections of the $K^{+}N$ scatterings and the $I=0$ and 1 total cross sections up to $p_{lab} = 800$ MeV/c, from which inelastic contributions start to be significant. Performing analytic continuation of the determined amplitude to the complex energy plane, we find a pole for a broad resonance state. We point out that the rapid increase appearing in the $I=0$ total cross section around $p_{lab}=500$ MeV/c is a hint of the possible broad resonance of strangeness $S=+1$.

## Key claim

The authors find a pole for a broad resonance state in the $I=0$ $KN$ channel after performing analytic continuation of the determined amplitude to the complex energy plane, but they hedge that this is a "possible" broad resonance and that the rapid increase in the $I=0$ total cross section around $p_{lab}=500$ MeV/c is only a "hint" of such a state, not a definitive observation.

## Method

The authors construct $KN$ scattering amplitudes using a chiral unitary approach, where the interaction kernel is derived from chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order and unitarized by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in partial waves up to D-wave. They determine the low-energy constants $b^I$, $d^I$, $g^I$, and $h^I$ for both $I=0$ and $I=1$ channels by fitting to experimental differential cross sections of $K^+p$ scattering and total cross sections for both isospin channels up to $p_{lab}=800$ MeV/c, then perform analytic continuation of the resulting amplitude to the complex energy plane to search for resonance poles.

## Key numbers

- The possible broad resonance in the $I=0$ $KN$ channel is found around an energy of 1617 MeV with a width of 305 MeV (from the abstract).
- The analysis is restricted to laboratory momenta up to $p_{lab}=800$ MeV/c, where inelastic contributions start to be significant.
- The rapid increase in the $I=0$ total cross section that hints at the resonance appears around $p_{lab}=500$ MeV/c.
- The kaon decay constant is taken as $f_K = 110.0$ MeV (with $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV).
- The subtraction constants for both $I=0$ and $I=1$ are fixed at $a^{I=0,1} = -1.150$ with regularization scale $\mu=1$ GeV.
- For Solution 1, the $I=1$ fit gives $\chi^2/N = 2.96$ and the $I=0$ fit gives $\chi^2/N = 4.54$; for Solution 2, the $I=1$ fit gives $\chi^2/N = 2.97$ and the $I=0$ fit gives $\chi^2/N = 4.06$.

## Context

This work revisits the possibility of a $Z^*$-type broad resonance with strangeness $S=+1$ and isospin $I=0$, which was discussed in earlier studies by Cool (1966), Tyson (1967), Bugg (1968), Abrams (1969), and Giacomelli (1972-1974). The authors use the chiral unitary approach, previously successful in describing the double pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance in the $S=-1$ channel by Oller (2000) and Jido (2003), and extend it to the exotic $S=+1$ channel where the leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg term vanishes for $I=0$, making next-to-leading order contributions responsible for any attraction. This work enables future experimental searches for broad $S=+1$ resonances in the energy region below where inelastic channels open significantly.
