# N$^3$LO Power Corrections for $0$-jettiness Subtractions With Fiducial Cuts

> We compute the leading logarithmic power corrections at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order for $0$-jettiness subtractions for color singlet production. We discuss how to disentangle these power corrections from those arising from the presence of fiducial and isolation cuts by using Projection-to-Born improved slicing. We present the results for Drell-Yan and Higgs production in gluon fusion differential in both the invariant mass and rapidity of the color singlet. Our results include all the channels contributing at leading logarithmic order for these processes, including the off-diagonal channels that receive contributions from soft quark emission. We study the numerical impact of the power corrections for Drell-Yan and Higgs production and find it to become negligible compared to the size of the N$^3$LO corrections only below $τ_\text{cut} \sim 10^{-5}$. We estimate that in a fully differential calculation at N$^3$LO combining the Projection-to-Born improved slicing method and our results for the leading logarithmic power corrections may allow for keeping the slicing uncertainties under control already with $τ_\text{cut} \lesssim 10^{-3}$, marking a significant improvement in efficiency for these methods. These results constitute a crucial ingredient for fully differential N$^3$LO calculations based on the $N$-jettiness subtraction scheme.

## Key claim

The paper computes the leading logarithmic (LL) power corrections at N$^3$LO for 0-jettiness subtractions in color singlet production, but the abstract's claim that these corrections enable efficient slicing with $\tau_\text{cut} \lesssim 10^{-3}$ is conditional on combining them with the Projection-to-Born improved slicing method, and the power corrections only become negligible compared to the N$^3$LO corrections themselves below $\tau_\text{cut} \sim 10^{-5}$.

## Method

The author uses consistency relations derived from infrared cancellation to determine the structure of the next-to-leading power (NLP) differential distribution in the 0-jettiness variable $\tau$, then computes the leading logarithmic contributions at N$^3$LO for both Drell-Yan and gluon-fusion Higgs production. The calculation employs soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to separate hard, collinear, and soft modes, and uses the leptonic definition of 0-jettiness to avoid large rapidity-dependent power corrections. The Projection-to-Born improved slicing method is used to disentangle dynamical power corrections from those arising from fiducial cuts.

## Key numbers

* For Drell-Yan and Higgs production, the leading logarithmic power corrections become negligible compared to the N$^3$LO corrections only below $\tau_\text{cut} \sim 10^{-5}$.
* Combining the Projection-to-Born improved slicing method with the computed leading logarithmic power corrections may allow keeping slicing uncertainties under control with $\tau_\text{cut} \lesssim 10^{-3}$.
* At N$^3$LO ($n=3$), the residual error from power corrections scales as $\Delta\sigma(\tau_\text{cut}) \sim \tau_\text{cut} \sum_{m=0}^{5} \ln^m \tau_\text{cut}$, with the leading logarithm being $\ln^5 \tau_\text{cut}$.
* The analytic control of the $\ln^5 \tau$ term at NLP can provide an improvement in numerical accuracy comparable to reducing the slicing parameter by approximately two orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the program of understanding power corrections in $N$-jettiness subtraction pioneered by Moult (2016, 2017) and Ebert (2018), who established the scaling behavior of power corrections at lower orders. It provides the necessary ingredients for fully differential N$^3$LO calculations based on the $N$-jettiness subtraction scheme, complementing recent progress on three-loop beam functions by Ebert (2020) and Baranowski (2022), and three-loop soft functions by Baranowski (2020, 2021, 2022). The results enable more efficient N$^3$LO calculations by allowing larger values of the slicing parameter $\tau_\text{cut}$ while maintaining control over theoretical uncertainties.
