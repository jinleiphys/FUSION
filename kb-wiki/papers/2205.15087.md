# An analysis of the spin density matrix of quarkonium in heavy ion collisions

> In this addendum to [1], we apply the techniques developed in that paper to the $J/Ψ$ and $Υ$ spin alignement measurements in [2]. We argue that while the data points to a maximally impure density matrix, consistent with Cooper-Frye/Statistical model freeze-out, a measurement of the dependence of the $ϕ$-sensitive coefficients on the azimuthal angle with respect to the reaction plane would be the crucial test of this conclusion.

# An analysis of the spin density matrix of quarkonium in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The existing ALICE data on J/Ψ and Υ spin alignment in Pb-Pb collisions are compatible with a maximally impure (incoherent) density matrix, consistent with Cooper-Frye/statistical model freeze-out; however, a measurement of the azimuthal-angle dependence of the polarization coefficients with respect to the reaction plane is the crucial test needed to confirm this conclusion, because a flat dependence would support thermal production while a harmonic modulation would indicate non-equilibrium between vorticity and spin density.

## Method
The authors apply the Gell-Mann matrix formalism developed in their prior work (Gonçalves and Torrieri, 2022) to the ALICE Collaboration measurements of the J/Ψ and Υ(1S) spin density matrix, using the relations between the experimentally reported polarization parameters λθ, λφ, λθφ and the density matrix elements ρ00, r1,-1, and r10. They solve the system of algebraic equations relating these parameters to the coherence variables n3 and n8 (or the combined variable ñ) and the reference angles θr and φr, then compare the extracted values to the pure-state condition ρ² = ρ.

## Key numbers
- For J/Ψ in the Collins-Soper frame, the combined variable ñ = 0.09 ± 0.10 for the range 4 < pT < 6 GeV/c², and ñ = 0.02 ± 0.07 for the range 6 < pT < 10 GeV/c².
- For J/Ψ in the helicity frame, ñ = 0.09 ± 0.11 for the range 6 < pT < 10 GeV/c².
- The J/Ψ density matrix values do not intersect the pure-state points in the n3-n8 plane, indicating an impure (incoherent) state.
- For Υ(1S), the uncertainties are too large to determine whether the density matrix represents a coherent state or not.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Gonçalves and Torrieri (2022) for analyzing vector meson spin alignment via off-diagonal density matrix coefficients, applying it to existing ALICE data on quarkonium polarization. The results support the statistical/thermal hadronization picture advocated by Zanna (2013) and Andronic et al. (2015), where the density matrix is maximally impure, as opposed to a coherent coalescence scenario in a vortical background. The authors call for future experimental measurements of the polarization coefficients as a function of the azimuthal angle with respect to the reaction plane, which would distinguish between a flat (thermal) and modulated (vorticity-driven) dependence, and suggest extending the analysis to K* and φ vector mesons measured by ALICE and STAR.
