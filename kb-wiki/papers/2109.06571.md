# Full solution of the medium-induced radiation spectrum

> New measurements of jet quenching observables at RHIC and at the LHC, such as jet substructure observables, demand an increased precision in the theory calculations describing medium-induced radiation of gluons. Closed expressions for the gluon spectrum including a full resummation of multiple scatterings have been known for the past 20 years. Still they have only been evaluated in specific limits either taking a few terms in an opacity expansion or by employing a Gaussian approximation for the interaction potential -- which misses essential physical effects. We present here a new flexible method to compute the full spectrum for a realistic interaction potential, thus allowing us for the first time to properly quantify the effect of the all-order resummation of multiple scatterings. This new approach paves the way for precision phenomenological studies including multiple scattering effects such as coherence phenomena.

## Key claim

The authors present a new flexible numerical method to compute the full medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum including all-order resummation of multiple scatterings for realistic interaction potentials, which for the first time allows proper quantification of the effect of multiple scatterings beyond the previously used harmonic oscillator and opacity expansion approximations.

## Method

The method reformulates the BDMPS-Z medium-induced gluon spectrum into a set of differential equations for auxiliary functions \(\vec{\phi}\) and \(\vec{\psi}_I\), which are solved numerically for a given collision rate \(V(\vec{q})\). The solution for \(\vec{\psi}_I\) is then integrated to obtain the \(\vec{k}\)-dependent spectrum, and the transverse momentum integration yields the gluon energy distribution, respecting the kinematical constraint \(k \ll \omega\). The approach works for any realistic interaction model, such as Yukawa or hard thermal loop (HTL) parton-medium interactions.

## Key numbers

- For a Yukawa interaction with \(\mu = 0.6\) GeV and linear density \(n_0 = 1\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\), the full resummed spectrum is compared to the GLV \(N=1\) approximation for varying path length \(L\); at large gluon energies \(\omega > \bar\omega_c \equiv \mu^2 L/2\), both spectra are suppressed as \(\bar\omega_c/\omega\).
- For the harmonic oscillator comparison, \(\hat{q}L = 1.3 (n_0 L) \mu^2\) is used to relate parameters; with \(\mu = 1.6\) GeV (\(\bar\omega_c = 39\) GeV) and \(\hat{q} = 2.8\ \mathrm{GeV^2/fm}\), or \(\mu = 0.9\) GeV (\(\bar\omega_c = 12.3\) GeV) and \(\hat{q} = 0.9\ \mathrm{GeV^2/fm}\), for a medium with \(n_0L = 5\) and \(L = 6\) fm.
- The HO approximation spectrum decreases proportionally to \(1/\omega^2\) at large energies, faster than the full resummed result.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z framework (Caron-Huot, 2010) by providing a numerical method to evaluate the full medium-induced gluon spectrum without the approximations used for the past 20 years: the harmonic oscillator (Gaussian) approximation and the opacity expansion (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev or GLV \(N=1\) approximation). The method enables precision phenomenological studies of jet quenching observables at RHIC and the LHC, particularly for coherence phenomena and jet substructure, where the discrepancies between approximations are significant.
