# Intermediate-mass dilepton spectra and the role of secondary hadronic processes in heavy-ion collisions

> We carry out a study of intermediate-mass (between 1 and 2.5 GeV) dilepton spectra from hadronic interactions in heavy-ion collisions. The processes considered are $ππ\to l{\bar l}$, $πρ\to l{\bar l}$, $πa_1\to l{\bar l}$, $πω\to l{\bar l}$, $K{\bar K}\to l{\bar l}$, and $K{\bar K^*}+c.c \to l{\bar l}$. The elementary cross sections for those are obtained from chiral Lagrangians involving pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons. The respective electromagnetic form factors are determined by fitting to experimental data for the reverse processes of $e^+e^-\to hadrons$. Based on this input we calculate cross sections and thermal dilepton emission rates and compare our results with those from other approaches. Finally we use these elementary cross sections with a relativistic transport model and calculate dilepton spectra in S+W collisions at SPS energies. The comparison of our results with experimental data from the HELIOS-3 collaboration indicates the importance of the secondary hadronic contributions to the intermediate-mass dilepton spectra.

## Key claim

Secondary hadronic processes (specifically meson-meson annihilations such as ππ, πρ, πa₁, πω, K̄K, and K̄K*+c.c.) can account for a significant portion of the observed enhancement in intermediate-mass (1–2.5 GeV) dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, but the authors note that contributions from charm meson decay, Drell-Yan processes, and quark-gluon plasma formation may also coexist and need quantitative assessment.

## Method

The authors calculated elementary dilepton production cross sections for six meson annihilation channels using chiral Lagrangians with electromagnetic form factors constrained by fitting to experimental data from the reverse processes e⁺e⁻ → hadrons. These cross sections were then used as input in a relativistic transport model to simulate dilepton spectra in S+W collisions at SPS energies, and the results were compared with experimental data from the HELIOS-3 collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The intermediate-mass region studied spans from 1 GeV to 2.5 GeV in dilepton invariant mass.
- For the ππ annihilation cross section, the pion electromagnetic form factor was parameterized with m_r = 0.775 GeV, m_r′ = 0.761 GeV, and Γ_r = 0.118 GeV (from Gale and Kapusta, 1987).
- Thermal dilepton emission rates were calculated at three temperatures: T = 120, 150, and 180 MeV.
- For the K̄K* form factor, the coupling constant g_{φ′K̄K*}/g_{φ′} = 0.19 GeV⁻¹ was determined by fitting to experimental data.
- The πa₁ channel was found to be the most important contributor to intermediate-mass dileptons, with three scenarios considered to bracket the form factor uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of low-mass dilepton enhancement (e.g., Li, Ko, and Brown; Brown and Rho, 1992; Hatsuda and Lee, 1992) into the intermediate-mass region, where earlier calculations by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1997) showed that background from primary vector meson decays, charm decays, and Drell-Yan processes underestimated the HELIOS-3 data for central S+W collisions. The results enable future discrimination between hadronic contributions and possible quark-gluon plasma signals in the intermediate-mass region, and the authors note that the πa₁ channel rates differ from those in Song (1994) and Song (1996) due to the use of new form factors from e⁺e⁻ data.
