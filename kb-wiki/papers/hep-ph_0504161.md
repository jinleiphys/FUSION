# Relations between quark and lepton mixing angles and matrices

> We discuss the relations between the mixing angles and the mixing matrices of quarks and leptons. With Raidal's numerical relations, we parametrize the lepton mixing (PMNS) matrix with the parameters of the quark mixing (CKM) matrix, and calculate the products of $V_{\mathrm{CKM}}U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}$ and $U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}V_{\mathrm{CKM}}$. Also, under the conjectures $V_{\mathrm{CKM}}U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}=U_{\mathrm{bimax}}$ or $U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}V_{\mathrm{CKM}}=U_{\mathrm{bimax}}$, we get the PMNS matrix naturally, and test Raidal's relations in these two different versions. The similarities and the differences between the different versions are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the product of the PMNS and CKM matrices is closer to the exact bimaximal mixing pattern when the PMNS matrix is multiplied on the left of the CKM matrix (deviation of order λ²) rather than on the right (deviation of order λ), and that Raidal's numerical relations between quark and lepton mixing angles hold more precisely under the conjecture \(U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}V_{\mathrm{CKM}} = U_{\mathrm{bimax}}\) than under \(V_{\mathrm{CKM}}U_{\mathrm{PMNS}} = U_{\mathrm{bimax}}\).

## Method

The authors parametrize the lepton mixing (PMNS) matrix using the Wolfenstein parameters of the quark mixing (CKM) matrix, assuming Raidal's numerical relations between the mixing angles of quarks and leptons. They then compute the products \(V_{\mathrm{CKM}}U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}\) and \(U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}V_{\mathrm{CKM}}\) expanded in powers of the Wolfenstein parameter \(\lambda\), and compare the deviations of each product from the exact bimaximal mixing matrix. Separately, they assume either \(V_{\mathrm{CKM}}U_{\mathrm{PMNS}} = U_{\mathrm{bimax}}\) or \(U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}V_{\mathrm{CKM}} = U_{\mathrm{bimax}}\), derive the PMNS matrix from each conjecture, and test whether Raidal's relations are satisfied.

## Key numbers

* The best fit values of the CKM mixing angles are \(\theta_1^{\mathrm{CKM}} = 2.4^\circ\), \(\theta_2^{\mathrm{CKM}} = 0.2^\circ\), and \(\theta_3^{\mathrm{CKM}} (\theta_{\mathrm{C}}) = 12.9^\circ\).
* The lepton mixing angles from experimental data are \(\theta_{\mathrm{atm}} = \theta_1^{\mathrm{PMNS}} = 45.0^\circ \pm 6.5^\circ\), \(\theta_{\mathrm{chz}} = \theta_2^{\mathrm{PMNS}} = 0^\circ \pm 7.4^\circ\), and \(\theta_{\mathrm{sol}} = \theta_3^{\mathrm{PMNS}} = 32.6^\circ \pm 1.6^\circ\).
* The Wolfenstein parameter \(\lambda = \sin\theta_{\mathrm{C}} = 0.2243 \pm 0.0016\), with best fit values \(A = 0.82\), \(\rho = 0.20\), and \(\eta = 0.33\).
* Under the conjecture \(U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}V_{\mathrm{CKM}} = U_{\mathrm{bimax}}\), the derived value is \(|U_{e3}^{\mathrm{PMNS}}| = 0.48\lambda^2 = 0.024\).
* Under the conjecture \(V_{\mathrm{CKM}}U_{\mathrm{PMNS}} = U_{\mathrm{bimax}}\), the derived value is \(|U_{e3}^{\mathrm{PMNS}}| = 0.68\lambda = 0.15\).
* The deviation of \(U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}V_{\mathrm{CKM}}\) from the exact bimaximal mixing matrix is of order \(\lambda^2\), while the deviation of \(V_{\mathrm{CKM}}U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}\) is of order \(\lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the quark-lepton complementarity (QLC) relation first pointed out by Smirnov (2004) and the three-generation numerical relations proposed by Raidal (2004), which link the mixing angles of quarks and leptons. The paper builds on the suggestion by Minakata and Smirnov (2004) that the product of the CKM and PMNS matrices may equal the bimaximal mixing matrix, and compares two different orderings of this product. The results enable a unified parametrization of both mixing matrices using only the Wolfenstein parameters of the CKM matrix, and provide testable predictions for the PMNS matrix element \(U_{e3}\) that can be compared with future neutrino oscillation experiments.
