# Quark Number Susceptibility and QCD Phase Transition in the Predictive Soft-wall AdS/QCD Model with Finite Temperature

> Based on the infrared improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model which can lead to a consistent prediction for the mass spectra of light resonance mesons, we extend it to the finite temperature system and carry out the calculation for the quark number susceptibility with finite quark mass. As a consequence, we show that the quark number susceptibility grows rapidly with a continuous blow-up in a narrow temperature interval as the temperature increases, and there is a peak when the temperature is around $T \simeq 160 \sim 190$ MeV depending slightly on the models, the resulting critical temperature is about 170MeV, which agrees remarkably with the lattice QCD calculation.

# Quark Number Susceptibility and QCD Phase Transition in the Predictive Soft-wall AdS/QCD Model with Finite Temperature

## Key claim
The quark number susceptibility grows rapidly with a continuous blow-up in a narrow temperature interval, with a peak around T ~ 160–190 MeV depending slightly on the model; the resulting critical temperature is about 170 MeV, which agrees with lattice QCD calculations, but the precise peak location and sharpness depend on the constituent quark mass and the infrared confinement scale parameter μ_g.

## Method
The authors extend the infrared-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model of Sui et al. (2009) to finite temperature by introducing a 5D black hole metric with Hawking temperature T = 1/(π z_0). They compute the quark number susceptibility χ_q from the retarded Green function of the U(1) gauge field dual to the quark number current, using the GKP-W relation and hydrodynamic expansion for small frequency and momentum. The dilaton field is solved analytically from the equation of motion for the scalar VEV v(z), with four different parameterizations of v(z) (Models Ia, Ib, IIa, IIb) that satisfy the same UV and IR boundary conditions.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperatures for the four models: Ia: T_c = 173.6 ± 3.2 (from σ^{1/3}) ± 11.7 (from μ_d) MeV; Ib: T_c = 164.4 ± 8.1 ± 5.2 MeV; IIa: T_c = 194.9 ± 7.4 ± 9.2 MeV; IIb: T_c = 159.5 ± 7.8 ± 9.4 MeV.
- The peak in χ_q/T^2 occurs in the temperature range T ~ 160–190 MeV across all models.
- The chiral condensation values used are σ^{1/3} = 275 MeV (Ia), 265 MeV (Ib), 265 MeV (IIa), 272 MeV (IIb), with ±25 MeV variations.
- Current quark masses: m_q = 4.16 MeV (Ia), 4.64 MeV (Ib), 4.44 MeV (IIa), 4.07 MeV (IIb).
- The infrared scale μ_d = 445 MeV (corresponding to μ_g = 363 MeV for Ia/IIa, μ_g = 257 MeV for Ib/IIb), varied by ±50 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the predictive soft-wall AdS/QCD model of Sui et al. (2009), which successfully reproduces light meson mass spectra, to finite temperature by computing the quark number susceptibility. The results agree with lattice QCD studies by McNeile et al. (1987) and Allton et al. (2005), which show a rapid continuous rise in χ_q/T^2 around T ~ 170 MeV at zero chemical potential. The findings enable further studies of the QCD phase transition with three flavors, finite chemical potential, and inclusion of quartic scalar interactions or back-reaction effects.
