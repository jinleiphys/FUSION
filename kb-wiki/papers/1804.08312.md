# Photoneutron cross sections for Ni isotopes: Toward understanding $(n,γ)$ cross sections relevant to the weak s-process nucleosynthesis

> Photoneutron cross sections were measured for $^{58}$Ni, $^{60}$Ni, $^{61}$Ni, and $^{64}$Ni at energies between the one-neutron and two-neutron thresholds using quasi-monochromatic $γ$-ray beams produced in laser Compton-scattering at the NewSUBARU synchrotron radiation facility. The new photoneutron data are used to extract the $γ$-ray strength function above the neutron threshold complementing the information obtained by the Oslo method below the threshold. We discuss radiative neutron capture cross sections and the Maxwellian-averaged cross sections for Ni isotopes including $^{63}$Ni, a branching point nucleus along the weak s-process path. The cross sections are calculated with the experimentally constrained $γ$-ray strength functions from the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov plus quasi-particle-random phase approximation based on the Gogny D1M interaction for both $E1$ and $M1$ components and supplemented with the $M1$ upbend.

## Key claim

The photoneutron cross sections for Ni isotopes, combined with Oslo-method data below the neutron threshold, provide experimentally constrained gamma-ray strength functions that enable more reliable calculations of radiative neutron capture cross sections for the weak s-process, including for the branching-point nucleus ⁶³Ni; however, the extracted strength functions require a phenomenological correction factor of 2/3 on the overall E1 strength to reproduce the peak photoneutron cross section, and the M1 upbend parameter C remains a free parameter adjusted between 3×10⁻⁸ and 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻³.

## Method

Photoneutron cross sections were measured for ⁵⁸Ni, ⁶⁰Ni, ⁶¹Ni, and ⁶⁴Ni using quasi-monochromatic gamma-ray beams from laser Compton scattering at the NewSUBARU facility, with energies between the one-neutron and two-neutron thresholds. The gamma-ray strength function above the neutron threshold was extracted from these data and combined with strength functions below the threshold obtained via the Oslo method from charged-particle reactions at the Oslo Cyclotron Laboratory (⁶⁰Ni(³He,αγ), ⁶⁰Ni(³He,³He'γ), ⁶⁴Ni(p,p'γ), and ⁶⁴Ni(d,p'γ)). The combined strength functions were used in statistical Hauser-Feshbach calculations with the TALYS code, employing Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov plus quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations based on the Gogny D1M interaction for both E1 and M1 components, supplemented with a phenomenological M1 upbend.

## Key numbers

- The one-neutron separation energies for the Ni targets: ⁵⁸Ni = 12.22 MeV, ⁶⁰Ni = 11.39 MeV, ⁶¹Ni = 7.82 MeV, ⁶⁴Ni = 9.66 MeV.
- The two-neutron separation energies: ⁵⁸Ni = 22.47 MeV, ⁶⁰Ni = 20.39 MeV, ⁶¹Ni = 19.21 MeV, ⁶⁴Ni = 16.50 MeV.
- The level density at the neutron separation energy ρ(Sn) for ⁵⁹Ni = 2536(520) MeV⁻¹, for ⁶⁰Ni = 5349(2031) MeV⁻¹, for ⁶⁵Ni = 1120(110) MeV⁻¹.
- The average radiative width ⟨Γγ,0⟩ for ⁵⁹Ni = 2030(800) meV, for ⁶⁰Ni = 2200(700) meV, for ⁶⁵Ni = 1090 ± 550 meV.
- The E1 damping width used in the QRPA calculations was 4.5 MeV, smaller than the systematics of ΓE1 = 7 − A/45 MeV due to the closed proton shell in Ni.
- The M1 upbend parameter C was tested at two values: 3×10⁻⁸ MeV⁻³ and 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the gamma-ray strength function method previously applied to other isotopic chains (Utsunomiya 2010, 2011, 2013; Filipescu 2014; Nyhus 2015) to the Ni isotopic chain, providing the first photoneutron data for the rare isotopes ⁶¹Ni and ⁶⁴Ni. The results enable improved calculations of (n,γ) cross sections for ⁶⁰Ni, ⁶³Ni (a branching-point nucleus with half-life 101 yr), and ⁶⁴Ni relevant to the weak s-process in massive stars, and also test the D1M+QRPA model predictions against experimental data. The study highlights the need for further experimental photodata on light nuclei to understand the origin of the 2/3 correction factor on the E1 strength and to better constrain the M1 upbend parameter.
