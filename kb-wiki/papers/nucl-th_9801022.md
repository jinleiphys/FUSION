# NN Scattering: Chiral Predictions for Asymptotic Observables

> We assume that the nuclear potential for distances larger than 2.5 fm is given just by the exchanges of one and two pions and, for the latter, we adopt a model based on chiral symmetry and subthreshold pion-nucleon amplitudes, which contains no free parameters. The predictions produced by this model for nucleon-nucleon observables are calculated and shown to agree well with both experiment and those due to phenomenological potentials.

## Key claim

The authors assume that the nuclear potential for distances larger than 2.5 fm is given only by one- and two-pion exchange, with the two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) derived from a chiral-symmetry model with no free parameters; they find that predictions for nucleon-nucleon observables agree well with experiment only for specific partial waves, and that the TPEP is dominated by contributions from interactions involving degrees of freedom beyond the pure pion-nucleon sector.

## Method

The authors used the variable phase method, which is equivalent to solving the Schrodinger equation, to compute phase shifts and mixing parameters for nucleon-nucleon scattering. The potential consisted of the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) plus a two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) derived from subthreshold pion-nucleon amplitudes using Hohler-Jacob-Strauss (HJS) coefficients, with the TPEP regularized by a step function at a cutoff radius r_R. The OPEP and the pure-nucleon part of the TPEP (V_N) were regularized by cutting them at a radius r_pi (varied between 0.8 and 1.0 fm), while the remainder part of the TPEP (V_R) was cut at radii r_R between 1.5 and 2.5 fm; results were considered predictions only when they varied by less than 5% with respect to the cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear potential is assumed to be given by one- and two-pion exchange only for distances larger than 2.5 fm.
- The regularization cutoff r_pi for OPEP and V_N was varied in the interval 0.8-1.0 fm; results were discarded if the contribution varied more than 1.0%.
- The cutoff r_R for V_R was varied between 1.5 fm and 2.5 fm; results with variation less than 5% were taken as predictions.
- For the wave ^1G_4, predictions extend up to 300 MeV laboratory energy.
- For the wave ^1D_2, predictions extend up to 50 MeV laboratory energy.
- The central component of V_R at distances around 1.0 fm is about -25 GeV when using the full TPEP; restricting to the leading HJS coefficients (nu^0 t^0 and nu^0 t^1) gives values around -150 MeV at 1.0 fm.
- The pi N coupling constant used was g^2/4pi = 14.28, while the SAID analysis uses g^2/4pi = 13.7.
- There is a 10% difference between experimental pn and pp solutions for the H waves.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral TPEP calculation (Robilotta and da Rocha, 1997) by testing its predictions against experimental nucleon-nucleon scattering data. It contrasts with the approach of Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994, 1996), who used a general effective Lagrangian with free parameters adjusted to NN data, whereas the present model contains no free parameters and relies on subthreshold pi N data via HJS coefficients. The results enable future work to identify which partial waves are dominated by long-range chiral dynamics and which require additional short-range physics; the authors note qualitative agreement with a similar chiral potential calculation by Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise (1997).
