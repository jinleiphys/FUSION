# Spontaneous fission of superheavy nucleus $^{286}$Fl

> The decimal logarithm of spontaneous fission half-life of the superheavy nucleus $^{286}$Fl experimentally determined is $\log_{10} T_f^{exp} (s) = -0.632$. We present a method to calculate the half-life based on the cranking inertia and the deformation energy, functions of two independent surface coordinates, using the best asymmetric two center shell model. In the first stage we study the statics. At a given mass asymmetry up to about $η=0.5$ the potential barrier has a two hump shape, but for larger $η$ it has only one hump. The touching point deformation energy versus mass asymmetry shows the three minima, produced by shell effects, corresponding to three decay modes: spontaneous fission, cluster decay and $α$~decay. The least action trajectory is determined in the plane $(R,η)$ where $R$ is the separation distance of the fission fragments and $η$ is the mass asymmetry. We may find a sequence of several trajectories one of which gives the least action. The parametrization with two deformation coordinates $(R,η)$ and the radius of the light fragment, $R_2$, exponentially decreasing with $R$ is compared with the simpler one, in which $R_2$~=constant. The latter is closer to the reality and reminds us about the alpha or cluster preformation at the nuclear surface.

# Spontaneous fission of superheavy nucleus $^{286}$Fl

## Key claim
The authors present a method to calculate the spontaneous fission half-life of $^{286}$Fl using cranking inertia and deformation energy with two independent surface coordinates, but the shape parametrization with exponentially decreasing light-fragment radius yields zero-point vibration energies too high to reproduce the experimental half-life; the best agreement is obtained with a linearly increasing light-fragment radius, which gives a reasonable zero-point vibration energy of 0.685 MeV.

## Method
The macroscopic-microscopic method was used to compute deformation energy as a sum of Yukawa-plus-exponential phenomenological energy and shell-plus-pairing corrections from the asymmetric two-center shell model, assuming spherical fragments. The cranking inertia tensor was calculated with BCS pairing correlations, and the least action trajectory was determined in the plane of separation distance $R$ and mass asymmetry $\eta$ by minimizing the WKB action integral. Four laws for the variation of the light fragment radius $R_2$ were compared: exponential decrease, linear decrease, linear increase, and constant $R_2$.

## Key numbers
- Experimental spontaneous fission half-life of $^{286}$Fl: $\log_{10} T_f^{exp} (s) = -0.632$
- Deepest minimum of deformation energy: $E_{def} = -3.49$ MeV at $x=0.074$, $\eta=0.00$
- First barrier height at symmetry ($\eta=0$): $B_1 = 1.810$ MeV (first maximum at $x=0.352$)
- Second barrier height at symmetry: $B_2 = 5.143$ MeV (second maximum at $x=0.741$)
- For the linearly increasing $R_2$ trajectory, zero-point vibration energy $E_v = 0.685$ MeV reproduces the experimental half-life
- For constant $R_2$ with split $^{132}$Sn ($\eta=0.0769$), $E_v = 1.3612$ MeV reproduces the experimental half-life
- For exponential decrease of $R_2$ at $\eta=0.087$, $E_v = 4.2835$ MeV is needed to reproduce the experimental half-life
- Parent nucleus radius: $R_0 = 7.6427$ fm (using $r_0 = 1.16$ fm)

## Context
This work extends previous calculations by the same group on $^{282}$Cn and $^{284}$Cn (Poenaru 2014) using cranking inertia, and contrasts with fission dynamics using the Werner-Wheeler nuclear inertia tensor (Poenaru 2013) which gave too small inertia values. The method enables systematic calculation of spontaneous fission half-lives for superheavy nuclei with $Z=104-118$, and the three minima in touching-point deformation energy correspond to the three decay modes (spontaneous fission, cluster decay, and alpha decay) previously identified for heavier superheavy nuclei with $Z>121$ (Poenaru 2012, 2011). The authors note that future work considering deformed fragments could further improve the method.
