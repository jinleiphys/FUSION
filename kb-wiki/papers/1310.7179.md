# The physical mechanism of AdS instability and Holographic Thermalization

> Gravitational falling in AdS has two characteristic properties \cite{ohsin}: i) A thick shell becomes a thin shell. ii) Any shape become spherical. Such focusing character of AdS, for the collapse of dusts, leads to the rapid thermalization mechanism in strongly interacting system. For the collapse of a wave, it explains the cascade of energy to UV through repeated bounces, which has been extensively discussed in recent numerical works. Therefore the focusing is the physical mechanism of instability of AdS. Such sharp contrast between the dust and wave in collapse, together with the experimental observation of rapid thermalization, suggest that the initial condition of created particles in RHIC is in a state with random character rather than a coherent one. Two time scales, one for thermalization and the other for hydro-nization are defined and calculated in terms of the total mass density and energy distribution of the initial particles. We find $t_{th}\sim (1-c_1/E^2)^{1/2}/T $ so that softer modes thermalize earlier. However, for hydro-nization, $t_{hyd} \sim 1/E^{2/3}T^{1/3}$ therefore harder modes come earlier. We also show that near horizon limit of Dp brane solutions have similar focusing effect which is enough to guarantee the early thermalization.

## Key claim

The focusing mechanism of AdS gravity, whereby any thick shell becomes thin and any shape becomes spherical during gravitational collapse, is the physical mechanism underlying both AdS instability and holographic thermalization. However, the author qualifies that for wave collapse (as opposed to dust collapse), black hole formation requires many repeated bounces because the uncertainty principle resists localization, so the "rapid thermalization at once" observed in RHIC experiments implies the initial state must be an incoherent dust-like state rather than a coherent wave configuration.

## Method

The author analyzes geodesic motion of non-interacting particles in AdS and near-horizon Dp brane geometries, deriving the radial equation of motion from the action of a massive particle in a gravitational background. For dust shells, the focusing effect is demonstrated by showing that particles starting from zero radial velocity at different initial heights arrive at the center simultaneously in global AdS, or that the leading term of the radial trajectory becomes independent of initial condition at late times in the Poincare patch and Dp brane backgrounds. Two distinct time scales are defined: thermalization time as the moment when the last particle passes the apparent horizon, and hydro-nization time as the moment when the radial spread of a shell falls within 10% of the horizon radius.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time: \( t_{th} \sim (1 - c_1/E^2)^{1/2} / T \), so softer modes thermalize earlier; when the highest energy scale \( r_0 \gg r_H \), \( t_{th} \simeq 1/(\pi T) \).
- Hydro-nization time: \( t_{hyd} \sim 1 / (E^{2/3} T^{1/3}) \), so harder modes hydro-nize earlier.
- For D4 brane background: \( r(t) = 4/t^2 - 8a/(\epsilon t)^3 + \cdots \) with \( a \simeq 0.86237 \), showing the leading term is independent of initial condition.
- For general Dp brane with \( p < 5 \): \( r(t) = (\beta / t)^\beta + \cdots \) with \( \beta = 2/(5-p) \).
- For p=5 brane: \( r(t) = r_0 / \sinh^2(t/2) \), so initial position differences decay exponentially as \( \Delta r \sim 4 \Delta r_0 \cdot e^{-t} \).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous paper (Oh and Sin, 2013) which first identified the focusing property of AdS, and addresses difficulties in existing holographic thermalization models: spherical scalar wave collapse (Danielsson 1999, Balasubramanian 2011) requires fine-tuned initial conditions, homogeneous null hyperplane sources (Minwalla) also assume special configurations, and colliding shock wave models (Chesler) set initial conditions before the collision where gravity dual is not relevant. The paper explains the numerical observations of repeated bounces and eventual black hole formation in scalar field collapse (Bizon 2011, Dias 2011, Garfinkle 2011, Buchel 2012, Bantilan 2012) as consequences of the focusing mechanism combined with wave dispersion. It enables quantitative distinction between thermalization and hydro-nization timescales, and suggests that the focusing mechanism is universal to all gravity duals with near-horizon Dp brane geometries, not just AdS.
