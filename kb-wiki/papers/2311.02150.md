# Precision three-dimensional imaging of nuclei using recoil-free jets

> In this study, we explore the azimuthal angle decorrelation of lepton-jet pairs in e-p and e-A collisions as a means for precision measurements of the three-dimensional structure of bound and free nucleons. Utilizing soft-collinear effective theory, we perform the first-ever resummation of this process in e-p collisions at NNLL accuracy using a recoil-free jet axis. Our results are validated against Pythia simulations. In e-A collisions, we address the complex interplay between three characteristic length scales: the medium length $L$, the mean free path of the energetic parton in the medium $λ$, and the hadronization length $L_h$. We demonstrate that in the thin-dilute limit, where $L \ll L_h$ and $L \sim λ$, this process can serve as a robust probe of the three-dimensional structure for bound nucleons. We conclude by offering predictions for future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider within this limit.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that using a recoil-free jet axis in lepton-jet correlations removes non-global logarithms, enabling the first resummation at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy for this observable in e-p collisions. For e-A collisions, they show that in the thin-dilute limit where the medium length L is much smaller than the hadronization length L_h and comparable to the mean free path lambda, the process can probe bound nucleon structure with medium effects reduced to transverse momentum broadening.

## Method
The authors employ soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive a factorization theorem for back-to-back lepton-jet configurations in e-p collisions, separating hard, beam, jet, and soft modes. They perform resummation of large logarithms to NNLL accuracy using renormalization group evolution equations for the hard function, TMD beam function, recoil-free jet function, and soft function. For e-A collisions, they model medium effects through Glauber interactions using a screened Coulomb potential and opacity expansion, considering the interplay of medium length L, mean free path lambda, and hadronization length L_h.

## Key numbers
- Resummation performed at NNLL accuracy for lepton-jet azimuthal distributions in e-p collisions, the first such calculation for this observable
- The thin-dilute limit for e-A collisions requires L much less than L_h and L approximately equal to lambda, quantified by the small parameter v = L/L_h = 2 M x_B A^{1/3}/Q^2 much less than 1
- The natural scale choices are mu_H = Q for the hard function and mu_f = mu_J = sqrt(zeta_fi) = sqrt(zeta_Ji) = mu_b = 2 e^{-gamma_E}/b for the TMD PDF and jet functions
- The Born cross section is sigma_0 = alpha_em^2/(S Q^2) * 2(hat{s}^2+hat{u}^2)/hat{t}^2

## Context
This work extends the lepton-jet correlation approach of Liu (2018) and Liu (2020), which used standard jet axes and achieved only NLL accuracy due to non-global logarithms. By adopting a recoil-free jet axis as developed by Bertolini (2013) and Larkoski (2014), the authors remove these complications and achieve NNLL accuracy, matching the perturbative precision of SIDIS analyses while avoiding dependence on non-perturbative fragmentation functions. The e-A analysis builds on the nTMD framework of Alrashed (2021) and the opacity expansion approach of Gyulassy (2002), enabling future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider to disentangle nuclear PDF modifications from dynamical medium effects.
