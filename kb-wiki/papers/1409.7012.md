# Three-particle quantization condition: an update

> We give an update on our derivation of a quantization condition relating the finite-volume spectrum of three particles in a cubic box to infinite-volume scattering quantities. We have discovered and fixed technical problems in the derivation sketched in the proceedings of last year's lattice conference [arXiv:1311.4848], and have presented a detailed description of the corrected derivation in Ref. [arXiv:1408.5933]. Here we give an overview of the problems and their solutions, and describe open questions.

## Key claim

The authors present a corrected derivation of a quantization condition relating the finite-volume spectrum of three identical scalar particles in a cubic box to infinite-volume scattering quantities, but acknowledge that the relation of the key infinite-volume quantity (Kdf,3) to the physical three-to-three scattering amplitude remains unclear, contrary to their earlier claim.

## Method

The derivation analyzes finite-volume correlation functions in Minkowski space for a relativistic field theory with a single scalar field of mass m and Z2 symmetry, working to all orders in perturbation theory. The authors replace finite-volume momentum sums with infinite-volume integrals plus a residue, using a modified principal value (PV) prescription to avoid cusps that arise at the two-particle threshold when using the standard iϵ prescription. The resulting quantization condition is expressed as det(F3^{-1} + i Kdf,3) = 0, where F3 depends on the two-particle K-matrix K2 and a regulated free non-relativistic propagator G, while Kdf,3 is defined as a sum of diagrams using the PV prescription with specified ordering of integrations and additional diagram decorations.

## Key numbers

- The two-particle quantization condition is given by det(F2^{-1} + i K2) = 0, where K2 is the two-particle K-matrix and F2 is a known kinematical factor dependent on the volume L
- The three-particle quantization condition is det(F3^{-1} + i Kdf,3) = 0, where F3 = (1/(2ω L^3))[-2F/3 + 1/(F^{-1} + (1 + K G)^{-1} K F)]
- Finite-volume momentum sums are replaced by integrals with corrections that give power-law dependence on L beginning at 1/L^4 when cusps are present
- Exponentially suppressed finite-volume effects proportional to e^{-mL} are ignored throughout

## Context

This work extends the two-particle quantization condition developed by Luscher (1986, 1991) and the form derived by Kim (2005) to the three-particle sector, correcting technical problems in the authors' earlier derivation presented at the 2013 lattice conference (Hansen, 2013). The formalism is needed for lattice QCD calculations of resonances coupling to three-particle channels (e.g., ω→3π, K*→Kππ, N(1440)→Nππ) and for studying three-body interactions in nuclear physics, with the most important unresolved issue being the relation of Kdf,3 to physical scattering amplitudes.
