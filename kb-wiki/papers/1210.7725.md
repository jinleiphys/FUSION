# Theoretical analysis of Lambda(1405) --> (Sigma pi)^0 mass spectra produced in p+p --> p + Lambda(1405) + K^+ reactions

> We formulated the Lambda(1405) (abbreviated as Lambda^*) --> (Sigma pi)^0 invariant-mass spectra produced in p+p --> p + Lambda^* + K^+ reactions, in which both the incident channel for a quasi-bound K^-p state and its decay process to (Sigma pi)^0 were taken into account realistically. We calculated M(Sigma pi) spectral shapes for various theoretical models for Lambda^*. They are asymmetric and skewed, and were compared with recent experimental data of HADES, yielding M(Lambda^*) = 1405 -9 +11 MeV/c^2 and Gamma = 62 +- 10 MeV, where the interference effects of the Kbar N-Sigma pi resonance with I= 0 and 1 Sigma pi continuum are considered. The nearly isotropic proton distribution observed in DISTO and HADES is ascribed to a short collision length in the production of Lambda^*, which justifies the high sticking mechanism of Lambda^* and the participating proton into K^-pp.

# Theoretical analysis of Lambda(1405) → (Sigma pi)^0 mass spectra produced in p+p → p + Lambda(1405) + K^+ reactions

## Key claim

The Lambda(1405) resonance mass extracted from HADES data is M = 1405 (+11, -9) MeV/c^2 with width Gamma = 62 ± 10 MeV, confirming the traditional single-pole value rather than the chiral double-pole prediction near 1420 MeV/c^2, but only when the spectral function is treated as an asymmetric, skewed shape arising from the coupled-channel formation and decay processes rather than a symmetric Breit-Wigner.

## Method

The authors formulated the Lambda(1405) → (Sigma pi)^0 invariant-mass spectra using a coupled-channel Kbar N - Sigma pi T-matrix approach with separable Yukawa-type potentials, where the spectral function S(x) = W_form(x) × G(x) is composed of a formation-process function W_form (calculated via impulse approximation for the pp collision) and a decay-process function G(x) (derived from the generalized optical potential formalism). They performed chi-squared fitting of the HADES missing-mass spectra for the sum of Sigma^+ pi^- and Sigma^- pi^+ channels at T_p = 3.50 GeV, using the AY model (Akaishi-Yamazaki) and the Chiral model (Hyodo-Weise) on equal footing, with the mass M and width Gamma as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit mass from HADES data: M = 1405 (+11, -9) MeV/c^2 with 68% confidence level
- Best-fit width from HADES data: Gamma = 62 ± 10 MeV
- Chi-squared for HKAY (present AY treatment) with PDG parameters (M = 1405.1 MeV/c^2, Gamma = 50 MeV): chi^2 = 11
- Chi-squared for Chiral model using T_21: chi^2 = 111
- Chi-squared for Chiral model using T_22: chi^2 = 40
- Incident proton kinetic energies considered: T_p = 2.50, 2.83, and 3.50 GeV
- Reaction threshold: T_p^thresh = 2.42 GeV
- Lower kinematic bound of G(x): M_l = M_Sigma + m_pi = 1328 MeV/c^2
- Upper kinematic bound of G(x): M_u = M_p + m_K- = 1432 MeV/c^2

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel Kbar N - Sigma pi formalism of Akaishi, Myint, and Yamazaki (2008) to analyze pp collision data, directly confronting the chiral double-pole prediction of Jido et al. (2003) and Magas et al. (2005) which places poles around 1420 and 1390 MeV/c^2. The analysis resolves the controversy from earlier ANKE data (Zychor et al., 2008), which Geng and Oset (2008) had interpreted as favoring the 1420 MeV/c^2 pole, but which Esmaili et al. (2011) found statistically inconclusive. The results enable future stopped-K^- experiments on deuterium at J-PARC (Suzuki, E31) to further discriminate between models, and support the high-sticking mechanism for K^-pp formation proposed by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2007).
