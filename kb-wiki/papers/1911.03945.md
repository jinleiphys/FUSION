# Dark photon manifestation in the triplet-like QED processes $γ+ \ell_i \to \ell^+_j \ell^-_j + \ell_i$, $i\neq j,$ $i=e, μ,$ $j=e, μ,τ$

> The triplet-like QED processes $γ+ \ell_i \to \ell^+_j \ell^-_j + \ell_i$ with $i\neq j,$ and $i=e, μ,$ $j=e, μ,τ$ has been investigated as the reactions where a dark photon, $A'$, is produced as a virtual state with subsequent decay into a $\ell^+_j \ell^-_j-$-pair. This effect arises due to the so-called kinetic mixing and is characterized by the small parameter $ε$ describing the coupling strength relative to the electric charge $e$. The main advantage of searching $A'$ in these processes is that the background to the $A'$ signal is pure QED. Concerning $A'$, we consider its contribution in the Compton-type diagrams only since, in this case, the virtual dark photon has time-like nature and its propagator has the Breit-Wigner form. So, near the resonance, $A'$ can manifest itself. The contribution of $A'$ in the Borsellino diagrams is negligible since, in this case, the virtual dark photon is space-like, the $A'$ propagator does not peak and the effect is proportional, at least, to $ε^2$. We calculate the distributions over the invariant mass of the produced $\ell^+_j \ell^-_j-$ pair and search for the kinematical region where the Compton-type diagrams contribution is not suppressed with respect to the Borsellino ones. The value of the parameter $ε$ is estimated as a function of the dark photon mass for a given number of events.

## Key claim

The dark photon \(A'\) can manifest as a resonance in the invariant mass distribution of the produced \(\ell^+_j \ell^-_j\) pair in the triplet-like QED processes \(\gamma + \ell_i \to \ell^+_j \ell^-_j + \ell_i\) (with \(i \neq j\)), but only when the virtual dark photon appears in the Compton-type diagrams (time-like propagator) and the kinematical region is restricted by a cut on the variable \(u\) to suppress the dominant Borsellino diagram background.

## Method

The authors calculated the double differential cross section over the invariant variables \(s_1\) (the squared invariant mass of the produced \(\ell^+_j \ell^-_j\) pair) and \(u\) for the QED background, separating the contributions from the Borsellino diagrams and the Compton-type diagrams. They identified the kinematical region where the Compton-type contribution exceeds the Borsellino contribution by imposing a cut \(u > u_0\) (with \(u_0\) a negative parameter) to remove events with small \(|t_2|\). The dark photon effect was then included by modifying the photon propagator in the Compton-type diagrams with a Breit-Wigner form, and the parameter \(\epsilon\) (kinetic mixing strength) was estimated as a function of the dark photon mass for a given number of measured events and a standard deviation of \(\sigma = 2\).

## Key numbers

*   For the process \(\gamma + \mu^- \to e^+ e^- + \mu^-\), with \(10^4\) measured events and \(\sigma = 2\), the limit on the mixing parameter is \(\epsilon^2 \leq 10^{-7}\) in the dark photon mass region \(520 < m_{A'} < 980\) MeV.
*   For the process \(\gamma + e^- \to \mu^+ \mu^- + e^-\), with \(10^4\) measured events and \(\sigma = 2\), the limit is \(\epsilon^2 \leq 10^{-7} \div 6 \times 10^{-8}\) in the dark photon mass region \(520 < m_{A'} < 980\) MeV.
*   For the process \(\gamma + \mu^- \to e^+ e^- + \mu^-\), with \(10^4\) measured events and \(\sigma = 2\), the limit is \(\epsilon^2 \leq 10^{-7}\) in the dark photon mass region \(30 < M_{A'} < 400\) MeV.
*   The ratio of the Compton-type diagram contribution to the Borsellino contribution, \(R^c_b\), is less than \(2 \cdot 10^{-2}\) over a wide range of \(s_1\) when the full kinematical region is allowed, for \(s = 6, 30, 60\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the search for dark photons via the triplet photoproduction process \(\gamma + e^- \to e^+ e^- + e^-\) previously investigated by Gakh (2018), but with the advantage that the final state leptons are not identical, simplifying experimental analysis. The proposed reactions \(\gamma + \ell_i \to \ell^+_j \ell^-_j + \ell_i\) offer a larger phase space and a purely QED background that can be calculated with high accuracy. This enables a complementary search for dark photons in the MeV to GeV mass range, similar in spirit to fixed-target experiments like those at JLab (APEX, DarkLight, HPS) and MAMI (A1 Collaboration), but using a photon beam colliding with a high-energy lepton beam.
