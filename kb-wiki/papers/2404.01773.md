# Measurement of the mesonic decay branch of the $\bar{K}\!N\!N$ quasi-bound state

> We conducted measurements of $K^- + {^3{\rm He}} \to π\!Y \!N + N'$ reactions using a $1~{\rm GeV}/c$ $K^-$-beam, with the objective of understanding the broad decay width of $\bar{K} \!N \!N$ (approximately twice as broad as that of $Λ(1405)$ considered to be the $\bar{K} \!N$ quasi-bound state). We successfully reproduced distributions of the $π\! Y \! N$ invariant mass and momentum transfer for $π\! Y \! N$ using model fitting functions for $\bar{K} \!N \!N$ formation and quasi-free $\bar{K}$ absorption (${\rm QF}_{\bar{K}-{\rm abs}}$) processes. The model can describe the experimental data quite well, and four $\bar{K} \! N \! N \to π\! Y \! N $ cross-sections were obtained. The results indicate that mesonic decay is the dominant decay branch of $\bar{K} \! N \! N$. The results also suggest that $Γ_{πΛN} \sim Γ_{πΣN}$, which indicates that the $I_{\bar{K} \! N}=1$ absorption channel, in addition to the $I_{\bar{K} \! N}=0$ absorption channel, substantially contribute to the $\bar{K} \! N \! N$ decay, making the $\bar{K} \! N \! N$ state approximately twice as unstable as $Λ$(1405).

## Key claim

The mesonic decay branch is the dominant decay mode of the $\bar{K}NN$ quasi-bound state, and the decay widths into $\pi\Lambda N$ and $\pi\Sigma N$ are approximately equal, indicating that both the $I_{\bar{K}N}=0$ and $I_{\bar{K}N}=1$ absorption channels substantially contribute to the $\bar{K}NN$ decay, making the state approximately twice as unstable as the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance.

## Method

The J-PARC E15 collaboration measured $K^- + {^3\mathrm{He}} \to \pi Y N + N'$ reactions using a $1\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ $K^-$ beam incident on a liquid $^3\mathrm{He}$ target, with final-state particles detected by a cylindrical detector system (CDS) consisting of a drift chamber and hodoscope counter in a $0.7\ \mathrm{T}$ magnetic field. Events were selected using a log-likelihood method combining kinematic fit $\chi^2$ and distances of closest approach between charged particles, with acceptance correction performed via a three-dimensional map in $m_{[\pi Y N]}$, $q_{[\pi Y N]}$, and $m_{[\pi Y]}$ using Monte Carlo simulation. The four measured final states were $\pi^- \Lambda pp$, $\pi^+ \Lambda nn$, $\pi^- \Sigma^+ pn$, and $\pi^+ \Sigma^- pn$, and the data were fitted with model functions for $\bar{K}NN$ formation and quasi-free $\bar{K}$ absorption processes.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the $\bar{K}NN$ state: $B_{\bar{K}NN} = 42 \pm 3\ (\mathrm{stat.})^{+3}_{-4}\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}$
* Decay width of the $\bar{K}NN$ state: $\Gamma_{\bar{K}NN} = 100 \pm 7\ (\mathrm{stat.})^{+19}_{-9}\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}$
* Mass of the $\bar{K}NN$ state: $2.328 \pm 0.003\ (\mathrm{stat.})^{+0.004}_{-0.003}\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{GeV}/c^2$
* Momentum transfer to the $\pi Y N$ system: $383 \pm 11\ (\mathrm{stat.})^{+4}_{-1}\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}/c$
* Cross-section for $\bar{K}NN \to \pi \Lambda N$: $9.3 \pm 0.8\ (\mathrm{stat.})^{+1.4}_{-1.0}\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mu\mathrm{b}$
* Cross-section for $\bar{K}NN \to \pi \Sigma N$: $5.3 \pm 0.4\ (\mathrm{stat.})^{+0.8}_{-0.6}\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mu\mathrm{b}$
* Integrated luminosity: $\mathcal{L} = 2.89 \pm 0.01\ \mathrm{nb}^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the J-PARC E15 collaboration's previous observation of the $\bar{K}NN$ state decaying into the non-mesonic $\Lambda p$ channel (Yamaga et al., 2019, 2020) by measuring the mesonic decay branches for the first time. The measured decay width of approximately $100\ \mathrm{MeV}$ is larger than theoretical predictions using chiral-SU(3)-based potentials (Dote et al., 2018; Barnea et al., 2012; Kanada-En'yo et al., 2021; Marri et al., 2019; Ohnishi et al., 2017; Revai et al., 2014; Maeda et al., 2013), which predicted widths in the range $40-70\ \mathrm{MeV}$, and is about twice the width of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance. The results enable improved modeling of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction below the $\bar{K}N$ mass threshold and provide constraints on the $I_{\bar{K}N}=1$ absorption channel contributions that are not included in most theoretical calculations (Sekihara et al., 2013, 2012).
