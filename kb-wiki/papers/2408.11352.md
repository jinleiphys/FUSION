# Photoproduction $γp \to f_0(980) p$ in an effective Lagrangian approach

> The most recent data on differential cross sections and photon beam asymmetries $Σ$ from the LEPS2/BGOegg Collaboration for the $γp \to f_0(980) p$ reaction are analyzed within a tree-level effective Lagrangian approach. The $t$-channel $ρ$ and $ω$ exchanges, the $u$- and $s$-channel $N$ exchanges, the interaction current, and the possible $s$-channel $N^*$ exchanges are taken into account in constructing the reaction amplitudes to reproduce the data. The results show that the contributions from either the $N(2040)3/2^+$ or the $N(2100)1/2^+$ resonance exchange in the $s$ channel are necessarily required to describe the LEPS2/BGOegg data and they dominate the differential cross sections of $γp \to f_0(980) p$. Further analysis shows that the contributions from the $t$-channel $ρ$ and $ω$ exchanges and the interaction current are rather small to both differential cross sections and photo beam asymmetries, and the contributions from $u$-channel $N$ exchange are considerable in the case of including $N(2040)3/2^+$ in the model while negligible in the case of including $N(2100)1/2^+$ in the model. Predictions of target asymmetries $T$ for $γp \to f_0(980) p$ are given, which can be examined by future experiments.

# Photoproduction $\gamma p \to f_0(980) p$ in an effective Lagrangian approach

## Key claim

The LEPS2/BGOegg data for $\gamma p \to f_0(980) p$ necessarily require the inclusion of either the $N(2040)3/2^+$ or the $N(2100)1/2^+$ resonance exchange in the $s$ channel to be described, but the available data are too limited to distinguish which resonance is responsible, and adding more than one resonance would produce too many fits with similar quality.

## Method

The authors constructed a tree-level effective Lagrangian model for $\gamma p \to f_0(980) p$ including $t$-channel $\rho$ and $\omega$ exchanges, $s$- and $u$-channel nucleon exchanges, an interaction current to preserve gauge invariance, and $s$-channel $N^*$ resonance exchanges. They fitted the model parameters to the LEPS2/BGOegg differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry data from threshold up to $E_\gamma \approx 2.4$ GeV, testing eight nucleon resonances with spin $J \leq 7/2$ individually and selecting the two best-fitting candidates for detailed analysis.

## Key numbers

- The three best fits were obtained with $N(2040)3/2^+$ ($\chi^2/N = 2.82$), $N(2100)1/2^+$ ($\chi^2/N = 3.02$), and $N(2300)1/2^+$ ($\chi^2/N = 3.88$) for $N = 38$ data points.
- The Born term (no $s$-channel resonance) gave $\chi^2/N = 14.40$, showing resonances are required.
- In Model I ($N(2040)3/2^+$): fitted resonance mass $M_R = 2038 \pm 1$ MeV, width $\Gamma_R = 350 \pm 15$ MeV; coupling product $g_{RNf_0}g_{RN\gamma}^{(1)} = -8.83 \pm 0.09$, $g_{RNf_0}g_{RN\gamma}^{(2)} = -5.60 \pm 0.11$.
- In Model II ($N(2100)1/2^+$): fitted resonance mass $M_R = 2088 \pm 2$ MeV, width $\Gamma_R = 320 \pm 56$ MeV; coupling product $g_{RNf_0}g_{RN\gamma}^{(1)} = 0.53 \pm 0.05$.
- The $t$-channel cutoff parameter was fixed at $\Lambda_M = 1070$ MeV from Xing (2018).
- The $f_0(980)$ mass was taken as $M_{f_0} = 990$ MeV; the $\pi\pi$ decay branching ratio was taken as the average of $0.52$, $0.75$, and $0.84$ from BaBar (2006), BES (2005), and Anisovich (2001).

## Context

This work provides the first theoretical analysis of the LEPS2/BGOegg data (2023) for $\gamma p \to f_0(980) p$, which cover a wider energy range (threshold to $E_\gamma \approx 2.4$ GeV) than the earlier CLAS data at $E_\gamma \approx 3.4$ GeV (CLAS, 2008-2018). Previous theoretical studies used Regge models (da Silva, 2013; Donnachie, 2015) or effective Lagrangian approaches (Lee, 2016; Xing, 2018) but only addressed the CLAS data. The present work extends these by including both differential cross sections and photon beam asymmetries, and by testing individual $N^*$ resonances. The predictions for target asymmetries $T$ are provided to distinguish between the $N(2040)3/2^+$ and $N(2100)1/2^+$ scenarios in future experiments.
