# Variations on the Deuteron

> We consider few problems which are related to the deuteron and have simple analytical solution. Relation is found between the deuteron electric quadrupole moment and the $np$-scattering amplitude. The degree of circular polarization of photons is calculated for the radiative capture of longitudinally polarized thermal neutrons. The anapole, electric dipole and magnetic quadrupole moments of the deuteron are calculated.

# Variations on the Deuteron

## Key claim
The deuteron, due to its anomalously small binding energy (2.23 MeV), allows many nontrivial problems to be solved via simple analytical calculations in the zero-range approximation, yielding relations between the deuteron electric quadrupole moment and the np-scattering amplitude, as well as predictions for the circular polarization of photons from radiative capture of polarized thermal neutrons and for the deuteron's parity-odd electromagnetic moments. The results are approximate, with the zero-range approximation introducing uncertainties of order 20% or more, and the derived quadrupole moment (0.18–0.24 fm²) is somewhat smaller than the directly measured value (0.286 fm²).

## Method
The author uses the zero-range approximation for the deuteron wave function, where the wave function is approximated by its asymptotic exponential form ψ_d = √(κ/2π) e^{-κr}/r with κ = 45.7 MeV and κ⁻¹ = 4.3 fm. For the quadrupole moment, the np-scattering amplitude is expanded in momenta and analytically continued to the bound-state pole to relate the D-wave admixture to the spin-dependent scattering parameters g₁+g₂. For the circular polarization, M1 and E2 transition matrix elements are calculated using the orthogonality of radial wave functions and the known deuteron magnetic moment. For the anapole, electric dipole, and magnetic quadrupole moments, the P-odd and P-odd/T-odd pion-exchange interactions are treated in perturbation theory with plane-wave intermediate states.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: ε = 2.23 MeV; corresponding κ = √(m_p ε) = 45.7 MeV; κ⁻¹ = 4.3 fm.
- Triplet scattering length: α_t = 5.42 fm; singlet scattering length: α_s = -23.7 fm.
- Effective interaction radius: r_t = 1.76 fm; correction factor 1 − κ r_t = 0.59.
- Derived deuteron quadrupole moment from dp scattering: Q₁ = 0.18 fm²; from np phase analysis: Q₁ = 0.20–0.24 fm²; experimental value: Q = 0.286 fm².
- Measured circular polarization of γ-quanta in np → dγ: P_γ = −(1.5 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³.
- Calculated M1 contribution to circular polarization: P_γ(M1) = −0.92 × 10⁻³.
- Calculated E2 contribution to circular polarization: P_γ(E2) = −0.25 × 10⁻³.
- Total calculated circular polarization: P_γ = −1.2 × 10⁻³.
- Parameter ξ = κ/m_π = 0.32.
- Strong πNN coupling constant: g = 13.45.
- Deuteron magnetic moment: μ_d = 0.8574.
- Proton magnetic moment: μ_p = 2.79; neutron magnetic moment: μ_n = −1.91.
- Derived effective axial constant from deuteron anapole moment at best value of P-odd constant: C^a_{2d} = 0.014 ± 0.003.
- Combined radiative correction and anapole contribution: C_{2d} = 0.028 ± 0.005.

## Context
This review extends classical textbook results on the deuteron (such as the relation between binding energy and triplet scattering length, and photodisintegration cross-sections from Bethe and Peierls (1935) and Bethe, Longmire, and Peierls) to more subtle problems involving D-wave admixture and parity violation. The relation between the deuteron quadrupole moment and np-scattering amplitude builds on work by Blinova, Khriplovich, and Korkin and by Ericson. The circular polarization calculation follows the experimental measurements by Vesna (1978) and Bazarov (1984) and theoretical treatments by Danilov and Burov. The anapole moment calculation extends the work of Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov on heavy nuclei to the deuteron, and the P-odd/T-odd moments follow earlier phenomenological work by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich. The results enable experimental searches for the deuteron electric dipole moment at storage rings and provide a test of the P-odd πNN constant and strange quark admixture in nucleons.
