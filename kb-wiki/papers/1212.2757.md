# Relativistic models for quasielastic electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Relativistic models developed within the framework of the impulse approximation for quasielastic (QE) electron scattering and successfully tested in comparison with electron-scattering data have been extended to neutrino-nucleus scattering. Different descriptions of final-state interactions (FSI) in the inclusive scattering are compared. In the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model FSI are described consistently with the exclusive scattering using a complex optical potential. In the relativistic mean field (RMF) model FSI are described by the same RMF potential which gives the bound states. The results of the models are compared for electron and neutrino scattering and, for neutrino scattering, with the recently measured charged-current QE (CCQE) MiniBooNE cross sections.

## Key claim
The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model can reproduce the MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross sections without increasing the nucleon axial mass above its standard value, provided that final-state interactions (FSI) are described by a complex optical potential whose imaginary part recovers the overall effect of inelastic channels. However, the RGF prediction is not univocally determined from elastic phenomenology because different parametrizations of the relativistic optical potential yield different results.

## Method
The authors compared four relativistic models for inclusive quasielastic electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering within the impulse approximation: relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA, no FSI), relativistic real optical potential (rROP, only real part of optical potential), relativistic mean field (RMF, same real potential for bound and scattering states), and relativistic Green's function (RGF, complex optical potential with flux-conserving imaginary part). For the RGF model, the hadron tensor was computed via a spectral representation using a biorthogonal expansion in eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical-model Hamiltonian and its Hermitian conjugate, with the imaginary part redistributing flux across all channels. Calculations were performed for carbon-12 targets at fixed kinematics (incident lepton energy 1 GeV, momentum transfers 500 and 1000 MeV/c) and for flux-averaged MiniBooNE CCQE cross sections.

## Key numbers
- For $^{12}$C$(e,e')$ at $\varepsilon=1$ GeV and $q=1000$ MeV/c, the RGF-EDAD1 cross section at the peak overshoots the RMF cross section by up to 40%.
- The standard nucleon axial mass world average is 1.03 GeV/$c^2$ (from deuteron data), while the RFG model requires 1.35 GeV/$c^2$ to fit MiniBooNE data.
- MiniBooNE CCQE data have an overall normalization error of 10.7%.
- The MiniBooNE neutral-current elastic measurement covers a $Q^2$ range up to approximately 1.65 (GeV/$c)^2$.

## Context
This work extends relativistic models previously tested on electron scattering data (e.g., Meucci (2001) for RDWIA, and earlier nonrelativistic Green's function models by Capuzzi (1991) and others) to neutrino-nucleus scattering. The RGF model is contrasted with the RMF model of Caballero (2003) and with the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model used by MiniBooNE. The results enable comparison with the MiniBooNE CCQE data (2010) and show that inelastic contributions recovered by the optical potential imaginary part can explain the data without enlarging the axial mass, consistent with alternative approaches that explicitly include two-particle-two-hole meson-exchange currents (Amaro (2011), Martini (2009), Nieves (2011)). The work also highlights the need for a better-determined phenomenological relativistic optical potential that closely fulfills dispersion relations.
