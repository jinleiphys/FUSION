# Anomalous hydrodynamics kicks neutron stars

> Observations show that, at the beginning of their existence, neutron stars are accelerated briskly to velocities of up to $1000$ km/s. We discuss possible mechanisms contributing to these kicks in a systematic effective-field-theory framework. Anomalies of the underlying microscopic theory result in chiral transport terms in the hydrodynamic description, and we identify these as explanation for the drastic acceleration. In the presence of vorticity or a magnetic field, the chiral transport effects cause neutrino emission along the respective axes. In typical scenarios, the transport effect due to the magnetic field turns out to be strong enough to explain the kicks. Mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies enter in a distinct way, and we also discuss their implications.

## Key claim

Anomalous chiral transport effects in hydrodynamics, specifically the chiral magnetic effect, can produce neutrino emission strong enough to accelerate proto-neutron stars to observed velocities of up to 1000 km/s, with the magnetic-field-driven effect dominating over the vorticity-driven effect by many orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors use an effective-field-theory framework of anomalous hydrodynamics, where the transport coefficients for chiral vortical and chiral magnetic effects are computed exactly from the anomaly coefficients of the underlying microscopic theory. They apply this framework to a proto-neutron star modeled as a rigidly rotating disk, considering the classically conserved lepton number currents (electrons and neutrinos) and their anomalies, including mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies. The neutrino flux from chiral transport is estimated using typical neutron star parameters (temperature, chemical potential, magnetic field, radius), and the resulting recoil velocity is computed from the momentum carried away by neutrinos that escape through the crust.

## Key numbers

- Observed neutron star kick velocities: up to 1000 km/s
- Neutron star vorticity estimate: ω ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ MeV (from Ω = 2π/ms)
- Magnetic field estimate: B = 10¹² G ≈ 0.1 MeV²
- Typical neutron star parameters used: μ^ℓ = 300 MeV, T = 10 MeV
- Estimated chiral magnetic current: J_ℓ5 = C μ^ℓ B ≈ 1 MeV³ (with C = 1/(2π²))
- Neutrino flux estimate: Ṅ_ν ≈ 10⁵⁴ /s
- Neutron star radius: r_N = 10 km
- Neutron star mass: m_NS = 3 × 10³⁰ kg
- Kick duration assumed: Δt ≈ 10 s
- Resulting velocity change: Δv ≈ 10³ km/s

## Context

This work extends earlier qualitative proposals linking neutron star kicks to asymmetric neutrino emission (Vilenkin 1979, Vilenkin 1978, PhysRevD.22.3080) by providing a systematic quantitative framework based on modern anomalous hydrodynamics (Son 2009, Erdmenger 2008, Banerjee 2008). It differs from previous microscopic approaches (Sagert 2007, Charbonneau 1, Charbonneau 2) by working directly with macroscopic parity-violating transport effects in the hydrodynamic description, avoiding thermal wash-out issues. The framework enables precise predictions relating neutron star properties to kick strength and direction, and offers a potential observational window into mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies through the temperature-dependent chiral vortical effect.
