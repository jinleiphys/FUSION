# Role of the possible $Σ^*(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ state in the $Λp \to Λp π^0$ reaction

> The $Λp \to Λp π^0$ reaction near threshold is studied within an effective Lagrangian method. The production process is described by single-pion and single-kaon exchange. In addition to the role played by the $Σ^*(1385)$ resonance of spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^+$, the effects of a newly proposed $Σ^*$ ($J^P = 1/2^-$) state with mass and width around $1380$ MeV and $120$ MeV are investigated. We show that our model leads to a good description of the experimental data on the total cross section of the $Λp \to Λp π^0$ reaction by including the contributions from the possible $Σ^*(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ state. However, the theoretical calculations by considering only the $Σ^*(1385)$ resonance fail to reproduce the experimental data, especially for the enhancement close to the reaction threshold. On the other hand, it is found that the single-pion exchange is dominant. Furthermore, we also demonstrate that the angular distributions provide direct information of this reaction, hence could be useful for the investigation of the existence of the $Σ^*(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ state and may be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including a hypothetical $\Sigma^*$ state with $J^P = 1/2^-$, mass around 1380 MeV, and width around 120 MeV, together with the established $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance, allows an effective-Lagrangian model to reproduce the measured total cross section of the $\Lambda p \to \Lambda p \pi^0$ reaction near threshold; calculations using only the $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance fail to describe the data, especially the enhancement close to threshold.

## Method

The reaction $\Lambda p \to \Lambda p \pi^0$ was modeled using tree-level Feynman diagrams with single-pion and single-kaon exchange, including contributions from the $\Sigma(1193)$ pole, the nucleon pole, the $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance ($J^P = 3/2^+$), and the proposed $\Sigma^*(1380)$ state ($J^P = 1/2^-$). Coupling constants were fixed from known decay widths and SU(3) flavor symmetry relations, and a final-state interaction enhancement factor derived from spin-averaged $\Lambda p$ scattering lengths was applied. Total and differential cross sections were computed and compared to existing bubble-chamber data.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the proposed $\Sigma^*(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ state: 1380 MeV; width: 120 MeV (assumed from fits in prior work).
- Mass of the $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance: 1384.57 MeV; total width: 37.13 MeV; branching ratio to $\pi \Lambda$: 0.87; coupling constant $g_{\pi \Lambda \Sigma^*_1} = 1.26$.
- Coupling constant for $\pi \Lambda \Sigma^*(1380)$: $g_{\pi \Lambda \Sigma^*_2} = 2.12$; for $K N \Sigma^*(1380)$: $g_{K N \Sigma^*_2} = 1.34$.
- Spin-averaged $\Lambda p$ scattering length: $\bar{a} = -1.75$ fm; effective range: $\bar{r} = 3.43$ fm; derived FSI parameters: $\alpha = -70.1$ MeV, $\beta = 185.1$ MeV.
- $\Lambda p$ FSI enhances the total cross section by a factor of 3 close to the reaction threshold.
- Beam momentum range studied: from just above threshold (0.9 GeV) to 2.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $\Sigma^*$ resonances in $K^- p \to \Lambda \pi^+ \pi^-$ reactions by Wu (2009) and in $K \Sigma^*(1385)$ photoproduction by Gao (2010) and Chen (2013), which had suggested evidence for a $\Sigma^*(1380)$ state. The paper provides a new test of the penta-quark picture of baryon excited states, which predicts a low-lying $J^P = 1/2^-$ $\Sigma^*$ around 1380–1405 MeV (Zhang 2004; Helminen 2000). The results enable future experiments to measure angular distributions in $\Lambda p \to \Lambda p \pi^0$ to distinguish the $S$-wave decay of the $\Sigma^*(1380)$ from the $P$-wave decay of the $\Sigma^*(1385)$, thereby clarifying the existence and properties of this proposed state.
