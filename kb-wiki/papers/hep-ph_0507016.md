# Sum Rules for Magnetic Moments and Polarizabilities in QED and Chiral Effective-Field Theory

> We elaborate on a recently proposed extension of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule which is achieved by taking derivatives with respect to the anomalous magnetic moment. The new sum rule features a {\it linear} relation between the anomalous magnetic moment and the dispersion integral over a cross-section quantity. We find some analogy of the linearized form of the GDH sum rule with the `sideways dispersion relations'. As an example, we apply the linear sum rule to reproduce the famous Schwinger's correction to the magnetic moment in QED from a tree-level cross-section calculation and outline the procedure for computing the two-loop correction from a one-loop cross-section calculation. The polarizabilities of the electron in QED are considered as well by using the other forward-Compton-scattering sum rules. We also employ the sum rules to study the magnetic moment and polarizabilities of the nucleon in a relativistic chiral EFT framework. In particular we investigate the chiral extrapolation of these quantities.

## Key claim

The paper elaborates on a recently proposed extension of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule, achieved by taking derivatives with respect to the anomalous magnetic moment, which yields a linear relation between the anomalous magnetic moment and a dispersion integral over a cross-section quantity; this linearized sum rule allows the Schwinger correction to be reproduced from a tree-level cross-section calculation rather than requiring one-loop input.

## Method

The authors derive a linearized GDH sum rule by introducing a trial Pauli term for the anomalous magnetic moment, taking derivatives of the GDH sum rule with respect to this trial parameter, and then setting the trial parameter to zero. They apply this sum rule to compute the one-loop anomalous magnetic moment in QED from tree-level cross-sections, and extend the approach to nucleon magnetic moments and polarizabilities within a relativistic chiral effective-field theory framework using single-pion photoproduction cross-sections in Born approximation. The connection between the linearized sum rule and sideways dispersion relations is also established.

## Key numbers

- Schwinger correction reproduced: \(\kappa = \alpha_{em}/2\pi\) from the linearized GDH sum rule using tree-level cross-section derivatives
- Leading-order heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory prediction for nucleon polarizabilities: \(\alpha_E^{(HBLO)} = 12.2 \times 10^{-4}~\text{fm}^3\), \(\beta_M^{(HBLO)} = 1.2 \times 10^{-4}~\text{fm}^3\)
- Experimental sum of polarizabilities from Baldin sum rule: \((\alpha_E+\beta_M)_p^{exp} = (13.69\pm0.14)\times 10^{-4}~\text{fm}^3\) for proton, \((\alpha_E+\beta_M)_n^{exp} = (14.40\pm0.66)\times 10^{-4}~\text{fm}^3\) for neutron
- Experimental proton polarizabilities: \(\alpha_E^{(exp)} = 12.1\pm 0.3 \,\text{(stat)}\mp 0.4 \,\text{(syst)} \pm 0.3\, \text{(mod)} \,[\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3]\), \(\beta_M^{(exp)} = 1.6 \pm 0.4 \,\text{(stat)}\pm 0.4 \,\text{(syst)} \pm 0.4\, \text{(mod)} \,[\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3]\)

## Context

This work extends the GDH sum rule framework introduced by Gerasimov, Drell, and Hearn (1960s) by providing a linearized version that simplifies perturbative calculations. The method is applied to QED to reproduce Schwinger's one-loop correction and outline a path to two-loop calculations, and to chiral effective-field theory for nucleon magnetic moments and polarizabilities. The results for nucleon polarizabilities from relativistic chiral loops are compared with heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory predictions (Bernard, Kaiser, Meissner, 1990s) and experimental data, while the chiral extrapolation of magnetic moments is contrasted with the infrared regularization approach of Kubis and Meissner (2000), noting discrepancies in analytic terms that can be reconciled by counter terms.
