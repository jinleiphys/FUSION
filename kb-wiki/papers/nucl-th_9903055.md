# Soliton formation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> The soliton formation is considered in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with local four quark interaction and various schemes to regularize the energy contribution of the polarized vacuum. No additional constraints are admitted in order to stabilize the soliton. While solitons are unstable in the proper-time regularized version the three momentum cut-off regularization apparently is more appropriate. Using a semi-classical approach multi-quark solitons obtained from that scheme are discussed. However, no self-consistent non-trivial unit baryon number configuration has been found. We also study a renormalizable extension of the model. In this case no stable multi-quark solitons are obtained within the semi-classical approach.

# Soliton formation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

## Key claim

Stable soliton solutions in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with local four-quark interaction exist only when using three-momentum cut-off regularization, and only for sufficiently large quark numbers (A ≥ 10 for Λ/m = 1.5); no self-consistent non-trivial unit baryon number configuration was found, and solitons are unstable under proper-time regularization or in the renormalizable extension.

## Method

The authors employed a semi-classical approach using the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion up to order ħ² to compute particle and energy densities for fermions in a scalar field background, treating the three-momentum cut-off regularization scheme. For multi-quark systems, they applied the Thomas-Fermi method with gradient corrections to study finite hadronic systems, solving the resulting non-linear differential equations for the scalar field profile σ(r) and Fermi momentum p_F(r). For unit baryon number, they performed a fully self-consistent calculation by diagonalizing the projected Dirac Hamiltonian in a basis of free Dirac spinors with momentum less than the cut-off, iterating the equations of motion for hedgehog profile functions σ(r) and π(r).

## Key numbers

- Hadronic matter becomes bound for Λ/m ≤ 1.80; for larger ratios no solitons exist.
- For Λ/m = 1.5, solitons are expected to exist for A ≥ 10 quarks.
- For Λ/m = 1.5 with A = 12 quarks, the scalar field at the radius R = 3.12 m⁻¹ is σ(R) = 0.943 m.
- Binding energy per quark in hadronic matter: −5.8 MeV (Λ/m = 1.7, m = 348 MeV), −12.5 MeV (Λ/m = 1.6, m = 365 MeV), −20.6 MeV (Λ/m = 1.5, m = 383 MeV), and −101.8 MeV (Λ/m = 1.0, m = 553 MeV).
- For Λ/m = 1.5 with A = 12 quarks, the second order Wigner-Kirkwood contribution to particle number and energy amounts to 20%.
- The pion decay constant was fixed at f_π = 90 MeV in the chiral limit.
- The critical ratio for binding hadronic matter is Λ/m = 1.80; the corresponding coupling constants from the gap equation are G = 1.60, 1.86, and 2.18 m² for Λ/m = 1.7, 1.6, and 1.5 respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of soliton formation in the NJL model, particularly addressing the instability found in proper-time regularization schemes by Tuebner (1994) and Bochum (1994), where the chiral circle condition σ² + π² = const. was required for stabilization. The authors build on the Thomas-Fermi method applied by Fiolhais et al. (1998) to show that three-momentum cut-off regularization can yield stable multi-quark solitons without additional constraints. The results enable further investigation of multi-baryon systems in the NJL model, though the failure to find a self-consistent unit baryon number solution leaves open the question of whether single baryons can be described as solitons in this framework.
