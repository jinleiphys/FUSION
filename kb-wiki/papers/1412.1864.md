# Hidden Color and the $b_1$ structure function of the deuteron

> The $b_1$ structure function is an observable feature of a spin-1 system sensitive to non-nucleonic components of the target nuclear wave function. A simple model for hidden-color, six-quark configurations is proposed and found to give substantial contributions for values of $ x>0.2$. Good agreement with Hermes data is obtained. Predictions are made for an upcoming JLab experiment.

## Key claim

Hidden-color six-quark configurations in the deuteron can reproduce the HERMES measurement of the $b_1$ structure function at $x=0.452$ with a probability amplitude $P_{6q}=0.0015$, which is small enough to evade any limits imposed by conventional nuclear theory calculations that make no explicit reference to such states.

## Method

The author constructs a non-relativistic quark model of the deuteron containing a six-quark hidden-color component, with the six quarks in an $S$-state and a $d$-state obtained by promoting one quark to a $d_{3/2}$ orbital. Harmonic oscillator wave functions are used with three parameters: the radius parameter $R$, quark mass $m$, and the six-quark probability $P_{6q}$. The $b_1$ structure function is computed from the light-cone momentum distribution of quarks in these six-quark configurations and combined with previously computed pion-exchange contributions to compare with HERMES data.

## Key numbers

- The HERMES measurement at $x=0.452$ gives $b_1 = -0.38 \pm 0.16 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.03 \text{ (sys)} \times 10^{-2}$ at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 4.69$ GeV$^2$
- The six-quark probability needed to reproduce the central value at $x=0.452$ is $P_{6q}=0.0015$, using $R=1.2$ fm and $m=338$ MeV
- The six-quark contribution at $x=0.452$ is $b_1^{6q} = -0.38 \times 10^{-2}$, matching the HERMES central value
- The six-quark contribution at $x=0.248$ is $b_1^{6q} = 0.41 \times 10^{-2}$, while pion-exchange contributions at that $x$ range from $-0.55$ to $0.13 \times 10^{-2}$ depending on the pion structure function model
- For $x < 0.2$, the six-quark contribution is negligible (less than $0.01 \times 10^{-2}$)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier paper (Miller 2013) that considered several effects contributing to the $b_1$ structure function, here focusing specifically on hidden-color components. The $b_1$ structure function was predicted by Jaffe (1989) to be sensitive to non-nucleonic components, and the HERMES collaboration (Airapetian 2005) measured it for $0.01 < x < 0.45$. The nucleonic contributions computed by Khan (1991) and Umnikov (1996) are very small, and conventional pion-exchange mechanisms cannot explain the negative value at $x=0.452$. The hidden-color model enables predictions for an upcoming Jefferson Laboratory experiment (JLab) that will measure $b_1$ for $0.16 < x < 0.49$ and $1 < Q^2 < 5$ GeV$^2$ with improved precision.
