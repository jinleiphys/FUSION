# Emergence of the geometric contribution to the superfluid density in the inner crust of neutron stars

> The geometric contribution to the superfluid density has been found to be of great importance in the inner crust of neutron stars. In this work we clarify how this contribution arises in the context of a band theory for neutrons. Specifically, we derive the dependence of the superfluid density on the magnitude of the pairing gap when the system has many bands cutting the Fermi energy, as it is the case for the neutrons in the inner crust. Also, in the perturbation theory framework, we find that it is essential to account for the corrections to the (Bogoliubov) quasi-particle states in order to get the geometric contribution. Accounting only for the corrections to the (Hartree-Fock) single-particle states leads to the conventional contribution only.

## Key claim

The geometric contribution to the superfluid density in the inner crust of neutron stars arises from the mixing of different bands and depends linearly on the magnitude of the pairing gap when many bands cut the Fermi energy; this contribution is missed if one accounts only for corrections to the Hartree-Fock single-particle states rather than the full Bogoliubov quasi-particle states.

## Method

The author applies linear response theory to a 3D periodic system described by a mean-field Hamiltonian, starting from a Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) ground state with constant pairing gap. The superfluid density is extracted by computing the first-order perturbative correction to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) quasi-particle states under a stationary flow perturbation \(V' = -\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{p}\), and comparing the resulting average momentum density with the two-fluid model expression. The analysis focuses on the limiting behavior of the geometric contribution for small pairing gaps by expanding the quasi-particle energy expressions in terms of the single-particle energies relative to the Fermi energy.

## Key numbers

- The geometric contribution to the superfluid fraction shows a linear dependence on the pairing gap \(\Delta\) in the range \(\Delta \in [0.001, 0.015]\) MeV, as demonstrated by linear fits to numerical results for two different baryon densities in the crystal phase of the inner crust.
- For single-particle bands with energies satisfying \(|\xi| \ll \Delta\) (conduction bands cutting the Fermi energy), the geometric contribution scales linearly with \(\Delta\) as \(\Delta (1 + \xi^2/\Delta^2)^{-1/2} \simeq \Delta\).
- For bands with \(|\xi| \gg \Delta\), the geometric contribution scales quadratically with the gap as \(\Delta^2/|\xi|\).
- For the case \(|\xi| \simeq \Delta\), the contribution is \(\Delta/\sqrt{2}\), still linear in the gap.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Almirante (2025) for the superfluid density in the inner crust of neutron stars, which identified the importance of the geometric contribution that was neglected in earlier calculations by Carter (2005) and Chamel (2024). The study clarifies how the geometric contribution emerges in systems with many bands cutting the Fermi energy, as is the case for neutrons in the inner crust, in contrast to condensed matter systems where few bands suffice (Liang 2017, Tian 2023). The results enable comparisons between neutron star crust physics and condensed matter systems (Julku 2016, Mojarro 2025) where a linear dependence of superfluid density on interaction strength has been found, and have implications for explaining pulsar glitches (Martin 2016) and neutron star thermal evolution (Page 2012).
