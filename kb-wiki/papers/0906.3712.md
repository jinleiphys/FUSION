# Thermal nature of charmonium transverse momentum spectra from Au-Au collisions at the highest energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC)

> We analyze the transverse momentum distribution of $J/ψ$ mesons produced in Au + Au collisions at the top RHIC energy within a blast-wave model that accounts for a possible inhomogeneity of the charmonium distribution and/or flow fluctuations. The results imply that the transverse momentum spectra of$J/ψ$, $ϕ$ and $Ω$ hadrons measured at the RHIC can be described well if kinetic freeze-out takes place just after chemical freeze-out for these particles.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum spectra of J/ψ, φ, and Ω hadrons at RHIC can be described well by a common freeze-out at T=160 MeV if one accounts for either inhomogeneity of the charmonium distribution or significant flow fluctuations; the paper argues that the apparent discrepancy in the low-pT J/ψ spectrum does not require a nonthermal contribution.

## Method

The authors fit a blast-wave model (boost-invariant parametrization with linear transverse rapidity profile) to midrapidity transverse momentum spectra of φ mesons and Ω hyperons from central Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, obtaining T=160 MeV and α=0.75 (vmax≈0.635c). They then apply the same parameters to J/ψ spectra and find deviations at low pT. To resolve this, they generalize the blast-wave model in two ways: (1) averaging over flow fluctuations using either a flat or Gaussian distribution of the maximum transverse rapidity α, and (2) replacing the homogeneous particle density with the initial spatial distribution of charm quarks from binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (proportional to T_A^2(r) from a Woods-Saxon distribution).

## Key numbers

- Temperature of chemical freeze-out used in fits: T = 160 MeV, close to lattice QCD Tc for zero-net-baryon-density matter.
- For φ and Ω spectra, best-fit blast-wave parameters: α = 0.75, implying vmax ≈ 0.635c.
- For the low-pT part of J/ψ spectra with the same T=160 MeV, a lower α = 0.55 (vmax ≈ 0.5c) is required.
- For the flat flow fluctuation distribution: α_min = 0.23 (vmax_min ≈ 0.23c), α_max = 1.0 (vmax_max ≈ 0.76c).
- For the Gaussian flow fluctuation distribution: α^0 = 0.55 (vmax^0 ≈ 0.5c), δ = 0.18.
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Au nucleus: c = 6.38 fm, a = 0.535 fm.
- Transverse size of bulk-matter distribution R estimated between 7 and 8 fm.
- The exponential slope parameter for φ mesons from STAR Collaboration: T_exp ≈ 359 MeV for the 0%-10% centrality bin.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model (SHM) approach of Braun-Munzinger et al. (2003, 2004) by addressing the previously noted failure of the simple blast-wave model to describe J/ψ transverse momentum spectra (Braun-Munzinger et al. 2007). It contrasts with the two-component model of Rapp (2003) and the coalescence model of Greco (2003), which require a significant primordial (nonthermal) J/ψ component of about 50%. The paper enables future dynamical hydrokinetic models that include resonance feed-down and more realistic freeze-out hypersurfaces, as noted in the discussion of forthcoming work.
