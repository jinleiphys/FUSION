# Semiclassical approaches to nuclear dynamics

> The extended Gutzwiller trajectory approach is presented for the semiclassical description of nuclear collective dynamics, in line with the main topics of the fruitful activity of V.G. Solovjov. Within the Fermi-liquid droplet model, the leptodermous effective surface approximation was applied to calculations of energies, sum rules and transition densities for the neutron-proton asymmetry of the isovector giant-dipole resonance and found to be in good agreement with the experimental data. By using the Strutinsky shell correction method, the semiclassical collective transport coefficients such as nuclear inertia, friction, stiffness, and moments of inertia can be derived beyond the quantum perturbation approximation of the response function theory and the cranking model.The averaged particle-number dependence of the low-lying collective vibrational states are described in good agreement with basic experimental data, mainly due to an enhancement of the collective inertia as compared to its irrotational flow value. Shell components of the moment of inertia are derived in terms of the periodic-orbit free-energy shell corrections. A good agreement between the semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi moments of inertia with shell corrections and the quantum results is obtained for different nuclear deformations and particle numbers. Shell effects are shown to be exponentially dampted out with increasing temperature in all the transport coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper presents semiclassical approaches (extended Gutzwiller trajectory approach, Fermi-liquid droplet model, Strutinsky shell-correction method) as alternatives to full quantum calculations for nuclear collective dynamics, claiming good agreement with experimental data and quantum results, but with the hedge that these semiclassical methods rely on the leptodermous (thin-surface) approximation and the condition that the dimensionless parameter \(k_F R \sim A^{1/3} \gg 1\) (heavy nuclei), and that shell effects are exponentially damped with increasing temperature.

## Method

The authors apply the extended Gutzwiller approach (EGA) of periodic-orbit theory (POT) using phase-space trace formulas and the improved stationary-phase method (ISPM) to derive semiclassical level densities, free-energy shell corrections, and transport coefficients. Within the Fermi-liquid droplet model (FLDM), they use the leptodermous effective-surface (ES) approximation of the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approach to calculate isovector giant-dipole resonance properties and collective transport coefficients (inertia, friction, stiffness, moments of inertia). The Strutinsky shell-correction method (SCM) is employed to separate smooth ETF components from oscillating shell corrections, with the latter expressed as sums over periodic orbits (POs) including bifurcation effects.

## Key numbers

* The semiclassical parameter for convergence is \(k_F R \sim A^{1/3} \gg 1\) (heavy nuclei), where \(k_F = p_F/\hbar\) is the wave number at the Fermi energy and \(R\) is the nuclear size.
* The leptodermic parameter (diffuse surface thickness relative to mean curvature radius) is proportional to \(A^{-1/3}\), where \(A\) is the nuclear particle number.
* Shell effects in all transport coefficients (inertia, friction, stiffness, moments of inertia) are shown to be exponentially damped with increasing temperature.
* The smooth ETF level density \(g_{\rm ETF}(\varepsilon)\) is obtained from the trace of the short-trajectory Green's function term \(G_{{\rm CT}_0}\).
* The oscillating level density \(\delta g_{\rm scl}(\varepsilon)\) is expressed as a sum over POs: \(\delta g_{\rm PO}(\varepsilon) = \Re \{ {\cal B}_{\rm PO} \exp[(i/\hbar) S_{\rm PO}(\varepsilon) - i\pi \mu_{\rm PO}/2] \}\), with action \(S_{\rm PO}(\varepsilon) = \oint d\mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{p}\) and Maslov phase \(\mu_{\rm PO}\).

## Context

This review extends the semiclassical periodic-orbit theory of Gutzwiller (1971) and the Strutinsky shell-correction method (Strutinsky, 1967; Fuhii, 1970) to nuclear collective dynamics, building on the Fermi-liquid droplet model of Kolomietz, Magner, and collaborators and the effective-surface approximation of Strutinsky, Tyapin, and Magner. It enables analytical calculations of transport coefficients (inertia, friction, stiffness, moments of inertia) for collective vibrations and rotations in heavy deformed nuclei, including shell effects and their temperature dependence, and provides a semiclassical understanding of the double-humped fission barrier in actinide nuclei via periodic-orbit bifurcations.
