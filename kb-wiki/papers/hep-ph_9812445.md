# Light Quarks in the Instanton Vacuum at Finite Baryon Density

> We consider the finite density, zero-temperature behaviour of quark matter in the instanton picture. Since the instanton-induced interactions are attractive in both $\bar{q}q$ and $qq$ channels, a competition ensues between phases of matter with condensation in either or both. It results in chiral symmetry restoration due to the onset of diquark condensation, a `colour supercondutor', at finite density. Also present is a state with both manners of condensation, however this phase is found to be thermodynamically disfavoured for equilibrium matter. Properties of quark matter in each phase are discussed, with emphasis on the microscopic effects of the effective mass and superconducting energy gap.

## Key claim

At finite baryon density, the instanton-induced interactions that are attractive in both the $\bar{q}q$ and $qq$ channels lead to a competition between chiral symmetry breaking and diquark condensation; the authors find that chiral symmetry is restored by the onset of diquark condensation (colour superconductivity) at a critical chemical potential, but caution that the transition occurs at quark densities below normal nuclear density, which "most probably contradicts the lore of conventional nuclear theory" and may require going beyond the mean-field approximation used.

## Method

The authors construct an effective low-momentum theory for quarks by averaging over an ensemble of instantons and anti-instantons, using the 't Hooft interaction vertex derived from the would-be zero modes of the Dirac operator at finite chemical potential. They derive a set of self-consistent Schwinger-Dyson-Gorkov equations for the normal and anomalous quark propagators to first order in the instanton density (the small packing fraction parameter), solving for the effective masses $M_{1,2}$ (chiral order parameters) and the superconducting gap $\Delta$ (diquark order parameter) as functions of chemical potential. The coupling constant $\lambda$ is determined self-consistently from a saddle-point condition relating it to the instanton density.

## Key numbers

- Average instanton size: $\bar\rho \approx 0.35\;\text{fm}$; average inter-instanton distance: $\bar R \approx 0.95\;\text{fm}$; ratio $\bar\rho/\bar R \approx 1/3$; packing fraction $f \approx 1/10$ (using $\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 280\;\text{MeV}$)
- Constituent quark mass at zero chemical potential: $2M(0) \approx 700\;\text{MeV}$ (the scalar diquark mass is $\sim 1/\bar\rho \sim 1\;\text{GeV}$)
- The transition to the colour superconducting phase occurs at quark densities less than normal nuclear density; after the system jumps into the superconducting phase the density appears to be about twice the nuclear density

## Context

This work extends the instanton-vacuum approach to finite baryon density, building on the variational instanton ensemble of Diakonov and Petrov (1986) and the observation by Berg and Lattimer that instantons induce attraction in both $\bar{q}q$ and $qq$ channels. It confirms the expectation of the Stony Brook group (1998) that diquark condensation becomes favoured at finite density, and refines the calculations of Diakonov, Forkel, and Lattimer (1996) who found only a metastable diquark-condensed state at $N_c \geq 3$ for zero chemical potential. The results suggest that ordinary nuclear matter may be a "boiling" mixture of the chiral broken and colour superconducting phases, as proposed by Brown and Rho, but the authors note this conflicts with conventional nuclear theory and that going beyond mean-field approximation is needed.
