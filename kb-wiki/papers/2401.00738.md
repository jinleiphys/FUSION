# Bottom-hadron production in high-energy $pp$ and heavy-ion collisions

> The hadro-chemistry of bottom quarks produced in hadronic collisions encodes valuable information on the mechanism of color-neutralization in these reactions. We first compute the chemistry of bottom-hadrons in high-energy $pp$ collisions employing statistical hadronization with a largely augmented set of states beyond the currently measured spectrum. This enables a comprehensive prediction of fragmentation fractions of weakly decaying bottom hadrons for the first time and a satisfactory explanation of the existing measurements in $pp$ collisions at the LHC. Utilizing the bottom hadro-chemistry thus obtained as the baseline, we then perform transport simulations of bottom quarks in the hot QCD matter created in PbPb collisions at the LHC energy and calculate the pertinent bottom-hadron observables. The transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependent modifications of the bottom baryon-to-meson ratio ($Λ_b^0/B^-$) relative to their $pp$ counterparts are highlighted as a result of bottom quark diffusion and hadronization in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). We finally summarize the heavy quark (charm vs bottom) diffusion coefficients as extracted from transport simulations and compare them to result from recent full lattice QCD computations.

# Bottom-hadron production in high-energy $pp$ and heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The statistical hadronization model with a largely augmented set of excited bottom-hadron states (beyond the currently measured PDG spectrum) can reproduce the measured fragmentation fractions of weakly decaying bottom hadrons in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions, but only when using the relativistic quark model (RQM) predictions for the missing excited states rather than the current PDG listings.

## Method
The authors compute bottom-hadron thermal densities at hadronization temperature $T_H \sim 170$ MeV using a grand-canonical statistical ensemble with strangeness suppression factor $\gamma_s \sim 0.6$, including primary production and feeddown from excited states via branching ratios estimated from the $^3P_0$ quark model. For PbPb collisions, they employ a strongly coupled transport approach featuring nonperturbative heavy quark diffusion in the QGP (with a $T$-matrix interaction and a $K=1.6$ factor to mimic radiative energy loss), resonance recombination hadronization at low to intermediate $p_T$, and heavy-hadron diffusion in the hadronic phase.

## Key numbers
- Hadronization temperature: $T_H \sim 170$ MeV used in the statistical hadronization model.
- Strangeness suppression factor: $\gamma_s \sim 0.6$ in elementary collisions.
- Thermal relaxation rate amplification factor: $K = 1.6$ applied to the $b$-quark thermal relaxation rate from the $T$-matrix approach to mimic radiative energy loss contributions.
- Peak of $\Lambda_b^0/B^-$ ratio in PbPb collisions occurs at $p_T \sim 6$ GeV and extends to $p_T \sim 15$ GeV.
- Extracted heavy quark spatial diffusion coefficient: $D_s(2\pi T) \sim 2$–$4$ at temperatures $1$–$1.5 T_c$ near the phase boundary.
- Collision energy for PbPb: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV.

## Context
This work extends the statistical hadronization approach previously applied to charm hadro-chemistry by He (2022) and the transport model for charm quarks in heavy-ion collisions by He (2019). The results contradict the assumption of universal heavy quark fragmentation fractions across collision systems, as earlier measurements by HFLAV (2019) showed different $f_{\rm baryon}$ values between $e^+e^-$ and hadronic collisions. The extracted bottom quark diffusion coefficients are compared to recent full lattice QCD computations by Altenkort (2023) and found to be very comparable, enabling future combined analyses of charm and bottom hadron observables as probes of the strongly-coupled QGP.
