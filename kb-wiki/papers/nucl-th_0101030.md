# Four-body DWBA calculations of the Coulomb breakup of 6He

> We investigate the Coulomb breakup of the two-neutron halo nucleus 6He by extending the framework of the finite range post form distorted wave Born approximation theory introduced earlier for the description of the breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei. The model can use the realistic three-body wave function for the 6He ground state. Calculations have been performed for energy, total and parallel momentum distributions of 4He fragment. The two-body 4He-neutron and neutron-neutron correlations have also been calculated. Our results are compared with those of an adiabatic model of the Coulomb breakup reactions. The two theories lead to almost identical results. Comparisons are also made with the available experimental data. It is found that the pure Coulomb breakup can describe the bulk of the data taken at below grazing angles. However, a rigorous description of all the available data requires the consideration of the nuclear breakup effects.

# Four-body DWBA calculations of the Coulomb breakup of 6He

## Key claim

The finite-range post-form distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) extended to four-body final states can describe the bulk of experimental Coulomb breakup data for 6He at below-grazing angles, but a rigorous description of all available data requires the inclusion of nuclear breakup effects; the FRDWBA and adiabatic (AD) model produce almost identical results, differing by approximately 20%.

## Method

The authors extended the finite-range post-form distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) framework, previously developed for one-neutron halo nuclei, to treat the Coulomb breakup of the two-neutron halo nucleus 6He as a four-body final state (core + two neutrons + target). Finite-range effects were included via an effective local momentum approximation (ELMA) applied to the Coulomb distorted wave of the charged core in the outgoing channel, and the formalism allowed the use of a realistic three-body ground-state wave function for 6He obtained from Faddeev calculations with hyperspherical harmonics. Calculations were performed for energy distributions, total and parallel momentum distributions of the 4He fragment, and two-body 4He-neutron and neutron-neutron correlations, with results compared to the adiabatic model and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of 6He predicted by the three-body model: 0.975 MeV
- Root mean square (rms) matter radius of 6He from the three-body model: 2.50 fm
- Alpha-particle rms radius used: 1.49 fm
- FRDWBA results for alpha-particle energy spectra at 63.2 MeV/nucleon on Au target at theta = 5 degrees are about 20% larger than AD model results near and above the peak region
- Mean square distance between alpha-particle and center of mass of 6He: 1.428 fm^2 for three-body wave function, 2.32 fm^2 for dineutron model
- Full width at half maximum (FWHM) of total momentum distributions changes systematically from 76 MeV/c to 98 MeV/c with increasing detection angle (0.65, 1.67, 3.02, and 4.55 degrees) at 64 MeV/nucleon on Au target
- FWHM of parallel momentum distributions at 24 MeV/nucleon on U and Pb targets: approximately 76 MeV/c
- Pure Coulomb two-neutron removal cross section on Pb target at 240 MeV/nucleon: 62.5 mb
- Peak of neutron-neutron relative energy spectrum at 240 MeV/nucleon on Pb target: approximately 0.45 MeV
- Grazing angle for 6He on Au at 63.2 MeV/nucleon: approximately 3.5 degrees
- Grazing angle for 6He on Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon: 1.1 degrees

## Context

This work extends the finite-range post-form DWBA theory developed by Chatterjee, Banerjee, and Shyam for one-neutron halo nuclei (2000) to the Borromean two-neutron halo nucleus 6He, which is a three-body system where all two-body subsystems are unbound. The calculations are compared with the adiabatic model of Coulomb breakup reactions (Tostevin, 1998) and with experimental data from several groups (Balamuth, 2000; Bush, 2000; Aumann, 2000; Warner, 2000; Aarts, 2000). The results enable future studies that include both Coulomb and nuclear breakup effects on the same footing, and allow breakup observables to serve as additional constraints on three-body structure models of Borromean nuclei. The work also highlights the inadequacy of the simpler dineutron model, which treats the two halo neutrons as a point-like cluster.
