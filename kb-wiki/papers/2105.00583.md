# Quark model analysis of the Weinberg operator contribution to the nucleon EDM

> The Weinberg operator (chromo-electric dipole moment of gluon) is a CP violating quantity generated in many candidates of new physics beyond the standard model, and it contributes to observables such as the electric dipole moments (EDM) of the neutron or atoms which are currently measured in experiments. In this proceedings contribution, we report on our result of the evaluation of the Weinberg operator contribution to the nucleon EDM in the nonrelativistic quark model using the Gaussian expansion method.

## Key claim

The irreducible contribution of the Weinberg operator to the nucleon electric dipole moment (EDM) is evaluated using the nonrelativistic quark model with the Gaussian expansion method, and it is found to be smaller than the reducible contribution obtained from QCD sum rules, with the total nucleon EDM determined with an uncertainty of about 60%.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon as a nonrelativistic three-quark system and solve the Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method, a variational approach based on Jacobi coordinates. The CP-odd interquark force generated by the Weinberg operator is derived from a one-loop level diagram with a heavy quark approximation and an assumed gluon mass of 350 MeV. Three different CP-even interquark potentials (Bhaduri, AP1, and AL1) are used to estimate systematic uncertainties, and the irreducible nucleon EDM is computed from the resulting wave function.

## Key numbers

- The irreducible neutron EDM from the Weinberg operator is \(d_n^{\rm (irr)} (w) \approx -w \times (4-5) \, e \, {\rm MeV}\).
- The irreducible proton EDM from the Weinberg operator is \(d_p^{\rm (irr)} (w) \approx w \times (4-5) \, e \, {\rm MeV}\).
- The total neutron EDM combining reducible and irreducible contributions is \(d_n (w) = w \times (20 \pm 12) \, e \, {\rm MeV}\).
- The total proton EDM combining reducible and irreducible contributions is \(d_p (w) = -w \times (18 \pm 11) \, e \, {\rm MeV}\).
- The reducible contribution from QCD sum rules (Haisch 2019) is \(d_n^{\rm (red)} (w) \approx w \times (25 \pm 13) \, e \, {\rm MeV}\) for the neutron and \(d_p^{\rm (red)} (w) \approx w \times (-23 \pm 12) \, e \, {\rm MeV}\) for the proton.
- The experimental upper limit on the neutron EDM is \(|d_n| < 1.8 \times 10^{-26} e \, {\rm cm}\) (Abel 2020).

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule calculations of the Weinberg operator contribution to the nucleon EDM (Demir 2002, Haisch 2019) by including the irreducible contribution that was previously neglected. The result provides the first quantified evaluation of the total nucleon EDM from the Weinberg operator, and the methodology can be applied to calculate the CP-odd nuclear force relevant for atomic and nuclear EDM analyses (Yamanaka 2017, Chupp 2017).
