# Dynamical Instability of Holographic QCD at Finite Density

> In this paper we study the dynamical instability of Sakai-Sugimoto's holographic QCD model at finite baryon density. In this model, the baryon density, represented by the smeared instanton on the worldvolume of the probe D8-\overline{D8} mesonic brane, sources the worldvolume electric field, and through the Chern-Simons term it will induces the instability to form a chiral helical wave. This is similar to Deryagin-Grigoriev-Rubakov instability to form the chiral density wave for large N_c QCD at finite density. Our results show that this kind of instability occurs for sufficiently high baryon number densities. The phase diagram of holographic QCD will thus be changed from the one which is based only on thermodynamics. This holographic approach provides an effective way to study the phases of QCD at finite density, where the conventional perturbative QCD and lattice simulation fail.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model at finite baryon density exhibits a dynamical instability to form a chiral helical wave phase, but only for sufficiently high baryon number densities; the instability is governed by the effective Chern-Simons coupling which depends on the 't Hooft coupling and is suppressed at large coupling.

## Method

The authors embed probe D8-anti-D8 branes in the near-horizon D4 brane geometry of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, with baryon density represented by smeared D4 branes (instantons) on the D8 worldvolume. They solve the DBI and Chern-Simons actions to obtain the background D8 embedding and electric field for both low-temperature (confined) and high-temperature (deconfined) phases, then expand the action to quadratic order in fluctuations of the D8 profile and gauge fields to derive a master equation for the magnetic field fluctuations that governs the dynamical instability.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the confinement-deconfinement transition is T_c = 0.0755 (in units where R=1), obtained from comparing free energies of the supergravity backgrounds.
* The D8-anti-D8 separation parameter is set to L = 0.53 and U_KK = 0.1 throughout the analysis.
* The effective Chern-Simons coupling is given by kappa = n_b / (4 pi^2 n_4) = 288 pi^2 (1/lambda^2) (U_KK / R), where lambda is the 't Hooft coupling.
* The 't Hooft coupling is set to lambda = 16.71 and M_KK = 950 MeV for numerical calculations.

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up holographic approach of Domokos and Harvey (2007) and Nakamura, Ooguri and Park (2010) to the top-down Sakai-Sugimoto model (2004-2005), providing a more systematic treatment of the Chern-Simons-induced instability. The instability is analogous to the Deryagin-Grigoriev-Rubakov instability for chiral density wave formation in large N_c QCD at finite density (1992). The results modify the purely thermodynamic phase diagram of Bergman (2007) by adding a spatially modulated phase at high baryon density, and the holographic approach enables study of QCD at finite density where perturbative QCD and lattice simulations fail.
