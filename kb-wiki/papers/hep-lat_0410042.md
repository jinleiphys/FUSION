# Volume dependences from lattice chiral perturbation theory

> The physics of pions within a finite volume is explored using lattice regularized chiral perturbation theory. This regularization scheme permits a straightforward computational approach to be used in place of analytical continuum techniques. Using the pion mass, decay constant, form factor and charge radius as examples, it is shown how numerical results for volume dependences are obtained at the one-loop level from simple summations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice-regularized chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) can be used to compute finite-volume effects on pion observables at one-loop order through straightforward numerical summations, reproducing known continuum results from dimensional regularization. The hedge is that this approach is presented as a practical computational alternative rather than a theoretical advance, and the authors explicitly note that the continuum limit of their lattice-regularized expressions is analytically identical to the dimensionally-regularized results.

## Method

The authors use a discretized SU(2) chiral Lagrangian with nearest-neighbor derivatives at leading order and symmetrized derivatives at next-to-leading order, maintaining exact chiral symmetry on the lattice. They compute one-loop Feynman diagrams for the pion two-point correlator, decay constant, electromagnetic form factor, and charge radius by converting loop momentum integrals into finite four-dimensional summations over lattice momenta on a hyper-rectangular lattice with spatial extent \(L = aN_s\) and temporal extent \(T = aN_t\). The volume dependence is extracted by taking the difference of each observable between two spatial volumes, which cancels the tree-level and counterterm contributions, leaving only the loop summation.

## Key numbers

* Fractional change in pion mass shown for \(M_\pi(L_\infty) = 100\), 140, and 300 MeV (corresponding to \(x_\pi = 100\), 142, and 321 MeV respectively) over spatial volumes \(2\ \text{fm} < L < 4\ \text{fm}\), with lattice spacing \(a = 0.1\ \text{fm}\), \(L_\infty = 8\ \text{fm}\), and \(N_t = 5N_s\)
* Fractional change in pion decay constant shown over same volume range \(2\ \text{fm} < L < 4\ \text{fm}\) with same lattice parameters, exhibiting the ratio \([f_\pi(L)/f_\pi(L_\infty)-1]/[M_\pi(L)/M_\pi(L_\infty)-1] = -4\) as expected from Gasser and Leutwyler (1987, 1988)
* Fractional change in pion form factor at \(q^2 = M_\pi^2\) shown over same volume range, with similar magnitude to mass and decay constant
* Fractional change in pion charge radius shown over same volume range, with dramatically larger magnitude because the charge radius has volume-dependent loop contributions at its first nonzero ChPT order
* The continuum limit of lattice-regularized results is analytically identical to dimensional-regularized expressions, verified explicitly for the pion mass difference between two volumes

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume ChPT framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1987, 1988) and the lattice ChPT formalism of Lee and Sharpe (1999) and Borasoy, Lewis, and Ouimet (2001) by providing a fully numerical computational scheme that avoids analytical evaluation of loop integrals. The method reproduces known continuum results for the pion mass and decay constant from Colangelo and Durr (2004) and Colangelo and Haefeli (2004), and presents new results for the volume dependence of the pion form factor and charge radius. The approach is intended to enable extension to two-loop calculations where analytical renormalization becomes substantially more involved, though the authors note that two-loop work will require determining numerical values for low-energy constants and their scale dependences, as well as understanding the interplay between power divergences \(1/a^n\) and volume dependences \(1/(aN_s)^n\).
