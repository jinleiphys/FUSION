# Chiral symmetry, scalar field and confinement : from nucleon structure to nuclear matter

> We discuss the relevance of the scalar modes appearing in chiral theories with spontaneous symmetry breaking such as the NJL model for nuclear matter studies. We show that it depends on the relative role of chiral symmetry breaking and confinement in the nucleon mass origin. It is only in the case of a mixed origin that nuclear matter can be stable and reach saturation. We describe models of nucleon structure where this balance is achieved. We show how chiral constarints and confinement modify the QCD sum rules for the mass evolution in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear matter can only achieve stability and saturation if the nucleon mass has a mixed origin, arising from both spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and confinement, rather than from chiral symmetry breaking alone. The authors show that the scalar response of the nucleon to the nuclear environment, which depends on the confinement mechanism, is essential for restoring saturation properties that would otherwise be destroyed by attractive three-body forces.

## Method

The authors formulate an effective chiral theory using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model framework, where the nuclear scalar field is identified with the chiral-invariant radial fluctuation of the condensate. They incorporate the nucleon's scalar response through a phenomenological parameter (kappa_NS) in the nucleon mass evolution, and perform Hartree mean-field calculations with pion-loop corrections via a many-body RPA approach that includes short-range correlations and Delta-hole excitations, as well as a full relativistic Hartree-Fock calculation including rho meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon sigma term is sigma_N approximately 50 MeV, with a pion cloud contribution of sigma_N^(pion cloud) approximately 20 MeV and a non-pionic piece of sigma_Nsigma^(no pion) approximately 30 MeV
- The lattice parameter a_4 is approximately -0.5 GeV^{-3}, which is much smaller than the chiral model value of (a_4)_Chiral = -3.5 GeV^{-3}
- The dimensionless scalar response parameter C = (F_pi^2 / 2 M_N) kappa_NS that reproduces lattice data is C approximately 1.25
- The correlation energy from spin-isospin loops is approximately -10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach initiated by Walecka (1986) by connecting the background scalar field to QCD condensates and chiral symmetry breaking. It addresses failures in pure chiral symmetry breaking models identified by Bodmer and Toki (2001), where attractive tadpole diagrams destroy matter stability, and resolves discrepancies with lattice data from Thomas et al. (2004) on the nucleon mass expansion. The approach enables a unified description from nucleon structure to nuclear matter properties, including saturation, compressibility, and asymmetry energy, with implications for modifying QCD sum rules for the nucleon mass evolution in nuclear matter.
