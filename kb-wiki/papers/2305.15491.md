# Predictions for the sPHENIX physics program

> sPHENIX is a next-generation detector experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, designed for a broad set of jet and heavy-flavor probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma created in heavy ion collisions. In anticipation of the commissioning and first data-taking of the detector in 2023, a RIKEN-BNL Research Center (RBRC) workshop was organized to collect theoretical input and identify compelling aspects of the physics program. This paper compiles theoretical predictions from the workshop participants for jet quenching, heavy flavor and quarkonia, cold QCD, and bulk physics measurements at sPHENIX.

## Key claim

This paper compiles theoretical predictions from a 2022 RIKEN-BNL Research Center workshop for the sPHENIX detector's physics program, covering jet quenching, heavy flavor, quarkonia, cold QCD, and bulk physics measurements, with the caveat that many predictions are preliminary and intended to motivate further quantitative theoretical work before first data arrives in 2023.

## Method

The authors organized a three-day plenary workshop at Brookhaven National Laboratory in July 2022 where heavy-ion theorists presented concrete predictions and highlighted interesting physics accessible with sPHENIX. Written contributions from theory speakers and their collaborators were collected and summarized in this paper, covering calculations using multiple theoretical frameworks including JetMed, JETSCAPE, and the Improved Opacity Expansion (IOE) for medium-induced radiation, as well as baseline perturbative QCD calculations using nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) sets EPPS21, nNNPDF3.0, and TUJU21.

## Key numbers

- Jet nuclear modification factor baseline (no energy loss) in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV shows nPDF uncertainties of order 10% at p_T^j=10 GeV, growing for larger momentum (especially for nNNPDF3.0)
- Hadron nuclear modification factor baseline in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV shows 90% confidence intervals contained in +/-25% bands across the studied momentum range
- JetMed predictions for the Soft Drop groomed jet radius theta_g in dijet events use effective qhat values varied between 0.4-1.2 GeV^2/fm
- JETSCAPE predictions show charged jets are more suppressed compared to inclusive jets at high jet p_T, with no strong jet cone size dependence for RHIC jets (unlike LHC)
- JETSCAPE predictions for photon-triggered jet I_AA show a rise below p_T^jet=12 GeV and strong suppression for p_T^jet>12 GeV
- Jet R_AA dependence on cone size R shows variations up to approximately 10% across the range R=0.1 to R=0.8
- Projected sPHENIX statistics: one million jets above 30 GeV and fifty thousand direct photons above 20 GeV in most central 0-10% Au+Au events

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical preparation for jet quenching measurements at RHIC, complementing prior work at the LHC by experiments such as ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS (Cunqueiro 2021, Apolinario 2022). The sPHENIX program aims to complete the scientific mission of RHIC by providing precision jet and heavy-flavor measurements not previously achievable at this facility, building on earlier RHIC results from BRAHMS (2004), PHOBOS (2004), STAR (2005), and PHENIX (2004). The kinematic coverage overlaps with LHC at high p_T while extending into a lower-p_T regime not previously accessible due to the smaller underlying event at RHIC energies.
