# What can we learn from recent $2νββ$ experiments?

> With recent measurements of the two neutrino double beta decay high precision electron spectra, combining with charge exchange or $β$-decay experimental data, we give severe constraints over the current nuclear many body calculations. Our calculation shows that QRPA approach can well reproduce the measured spectra for the two open shell nuclei, $^{82}$Se and $^{100}$Mo. For the closed shell nucleus $^{136}$Xe, QRPA can also reproduce the spectra with proper treatments. We also find that considering the high-lying state reduction, Nuclear Shell Model can also well reproduce the spectra as well as B(GT) strength under a unique quenched $g_A$. For $^{136}$Xe, we find that the flip of the sign for the decay strength will lead the spectra to go beyond HSD. These results call for future high precision measurement of charge-exchange reaction.

# What can we learn from recent $2\nu\beta\beta$ experiments?

## Key claim
The electron spectra from two-neutrino double beta decay can provide stringent constraints on nuclear many-body calculations, but the ability to distinguish between true single-state dominance and effective low-lying state dominance (where high-lying state contributions cancel) is limited without additional charge-exchange data. For the closed-shell nucleus $^{136}$Xe, a sign flip in the decay strength can push the calculated spectra outside the region bounded by the single-state dominance and high-lying state dominance hypotheses.

## Method
The authors performed full numerical calculations of the $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay electron spectra for $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, and $^{136}$Xe, solving the Dirac equation for electron wave functions and computing nuclear matrix elements using both the Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with realistic CD-Bonn force and the Nuclear Shell Model (NSM) using the NuShellX@MSU code. For NSM, the $jj44$ model space was used for $^{82}$Se and the $jj55$ model space for $^{136}$Xe, with the $jun45$, $jj44b$, and $jj55a$ interactions respectively. The calculated spectra were compared to experimental data from NEMO-3 and KamLAND-Zen, and the running sums of the nuclear matrix elements were analyzed to understand the contributions from different intermediate states.

## Key numbers
- For $^{82}$Se, the NEMO-3 measurement favors SSD with $\chi^2$/ndf = 12.34/16 compared to HSD with $\chi^2$/ndf = 35.32/16 for the single electron spectrum.
- For $^{136}$Xe, KamLAND-Zen rules out SSD at the 97% confidence level.
- For NSM calculations of $^{82}$Se, the fitted $g_A$ for double beta decay is approximately 0.55, slightly larger than the fitted value for $^{76}$Ge from Brown (2015).
- For NSM calculations of $^{82}$Se, a quenched value of $q \equiv g_A/g_{A0} = 0.6$ is needed to reproduce the low-lying B(GT) strength from charge-exchange experiments.
- For QRPA calculations of $^{82}$Se, the cancellation from high-lying states occurs around excitation energies of 7 MeV (pygmy GTR) and 12 MeV (giant GTR).

## Context
This work extends the combined analysis of $2\nu\beta\beta$ electron spectra and charge-exchange data previously performed by Simkovic (2018) for $^{136}$Xe, and addresses the discrepancy between the QRPA calculations of Moreno (2008) for $^{100}$Mo (which favored HSD) and the NEMO-3 experimental results (which favor SSD). The study also confronts the NSM calculations of Brown (2015) and Horoi (2007) with the new spectral data. The results call for future high-precision charge-exchange reaction measurements to resolve whether cancellations from high-lying states are present, which would enable better discrimination between nuclear models and improve the predictive power for neutrinoless double beta decay calculations.
