# Few body Calculation of Neutrino Neutral Inelastic scattering on 4He

## Key claim

The inelastic neutral-current neutrino-4He cross-section is calculated in the impulse approximation using two modern nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne V8' and V18), with full final-state interaction among the four nucleons via the Lorentz integral transform method. The results show a substantial enhancement and a steeper temperature dependence compared to earlier estimates by Woosley et al. (1990) and Haxton (1988), but the calculation neglects many-body currents and three-nucleon forces, which are estimated to introduce about 10% error.

## Method

The neutrino-nucleus scattering is treated in the impulse approximation using the effective weak Hamiltonian in the limit of small momentum transfer relative to the Z boson mass. The nuclear response functions are computed by combining the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method, which reduces the continuum problem to a bound-state-like problem, with the effective interaction hyperspherical-harmonic (EIHH) approach to solve the resulting Schrodinger-like equations. Two modern nucleon-nucleon potentials, Argonne V8' and Argonne V18, are used as the nuclear Hamiltonians, and the cross-sections are averaged over a Fermi-Dirac neutrino spectrum with zero chemical potential for various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Temperature-averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T = 4 MeV: 2.09 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (AV8') and 2.31 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (AV18)
- Temperature-averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T = 10 MeV: 7.85 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (AV8') and 8.81 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (AV18), compared to 6.78 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from Woosley et al. (1990)
- Temperature-averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T = 16 MeV: 8.52 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (AV8') and 9.48 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (AV18)
- Energy-transfer cross-section fit parameters for AV8': alpha = 0.64 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV, T₀ = 2.05 MeV, beta = 4.46
- Energy-transfer cross-section fit parameters for AV18: alpha = 0.72 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV, T₀ = 2.12 MeV, beta = 4.42
- Numerical accuracy of the calculation: about 1%
- Estimated total error from neglected many-body currents and three-nucleon force: about 10%
- Axial vector part contributes more than 90% of the cross-section
- Difference between current work and earlier estimates: 15-30% at T = 10 MeV, growing to 50-70% at T = 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the first microscopic calculation of the neutral-current neutrino-4He cross-section by the same authors (Gazit and Barnea, 2006) by providing a detailed energy-dependent calculation with two modern NN potentials. The results contradict earlier estimates by Woosley et al. (1990) and Haxton (1988), predicting substantially larger cross-sections and a steeper temperature dependence. The calculation enables improved hydrodynamic simulations of core-collapse supernovae, where neutrino-4He interactions are critical for shock revival and neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis of 7Li, 11B, and 19F; the authors note that the energy transfer from inelastic reactions is 1-2 orders of magnitude larger than from elastic reactions, making these cross-sections important for accurate modeling of the helium shell temperature.
