# Nucleon Spin in QCD: Old Crisis and New Resolution

> We discuss the shortfalls of existing resolutions of the long-standing gauge invariance problem of the canonical decomposition of the nucleon spin to the spin and angular momentum of quarks and gluons. We provide two logically flawless expressions of nucleon spin which have different physical meanings, using the gauge independent Abelian decomposition. The first one is based on the assumption that all gluons (binding and valence gluons) contribute to the nucleon spin, but the second one is based on the assumption that only the binding gluons (and the quarks) contribute to it. We propose the second expression to be the physically correct one.

# Nucleon Spin in QCD: Old Crisis and New Resolution

## Key claim

The paper provides two gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin into quark and gluon contributions, but argues that only the second expression, which excludes valence gluons and includes only binding gluons and quarks, is physically correct, based on the quark model of hadrons. The authors acknowledge that experiments must ultimately decide between the two expressions.

## Method

The authors apply the gauge-independent Abelian decomposition of the non-Abelian gauge potential, first separating the restricted (binding) potential from the valence potential, then further decomposing the restricted potential into vacuum and physical (transverse binding) parts. They construct two gauge-invariant angular momentum expressions: one (Eq. ns2) that includes all gluons, and another (Eq. ns3) that includes only binding gluons and quarks, derived from the restricted QCD (RCD) framework.

## Key numbers

- The canonical QCD angular momentum expression (Eq. ns0) contains four terms: quark spin, quark orbital angular momentum, gluon spin, and gluon orbital angular momentum; three of these terms are not gauge invariant individually.
- The EMC experiment on muon scattering from polarized proton indicated that the contribution of quark spin to proton spin was "much smaller than theoretical expectation" (no numerical value given in the text).
- The restricted potential (Eq. rp) is defined as $\hat A_\mu = A_\mu \n - (1/g) \n \times \partial_\mu \n$, where $A_\mu = \n \cdot \vec A_\mu$.
- The magnetic field strength $H_{\mu\nu}$ (Eq. rfs) is given by $H_{\mu\nu} = -(1/g) \hat{n} \cdot (\partial_\mu \hat{n} \times \partial_\nu \hat{n}) = \partial_\mu \tilde C_\nu - \partial_\nu \tilde C_\mu$.

## Context

This work extends the gauge-invariant decomposition approach of Chen et al. (2008, 2009) and addresses criticisms by Ji (2010) and Wakamatsu (2010) regarding the uniqueness and physical meaning of the vacuum potential. The authors use their earlier Abelian decomposition method (Cho 1980, 1981) to separate binding from valence gluons, building on the concept of Abelian dominance in QCD (t Hooft 1981). The paper enables experimental tests of whether valence gluons contribute to nucleon spin, which would either confirm or challenge the quark model of nucleons (Particle Data Group).
