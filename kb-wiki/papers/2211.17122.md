# Lifetime of the hypertriton

> Conflicting values of the hypertriton lifetime $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ were derived in relativistic heavy ion (RHI) collision experiments over the last decade. A very recent ALICE Collaboration measurement is the only experiment where the reported $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ comes sufficiently close to the free-$Λ$ lifetime $τ_Λ$,as expected naively for a very weakly bound $Λ$ in ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$. We revisited theoretically this ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$ lifetime puzzle, using ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$ and ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ wave functions computed within the abinitio no-core shell model employing interactions derived from chiral effective field theory to calculate the two-body decay rate $Γ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}\to{}^3\mathrm{He}+π^-)$. We found significant but opposing contributions arising from $ΣNN$ admixtures in ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$ and from $π^- -{}^3\mathrm{He}$ final-state interaction. To derive $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$, we evaluated the inclusive $π^-$ decay rate $Γ_{π^-}({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ by using the measured branching ratio $Γ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}\to{}^3\mathrm{He}+π^-)/Γ_{π^-}({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ and added the $π^0$ contributions through the $ΔI = \frac{1}{2}$ rule. The resulting $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ varies strongly with the rather poorly known $Λ$ separation energy $E_{\mathrm{sep}}({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ and it is thus possible to associate each one of the distinct RHI $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ measurements with its own underlying value of $E_{\mathrm{sep}}({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$.

## Key claim

The hypertriton lifetime varies strongly with the poorly known Λ separation energy, so that each of the conflicting experimental lifetime measurements from relativistic heavy ion collisions can be associated with its own underlying value of the Λ separation energy, rather than any single measurement being definitively correct.

## Method

The authors computed the two-body π⁻ decay rate Γ(³ΛH → ³He + π⁻) using ³ΛH and ³He wave functions from ab initio no-core shell model calculations with chiral effective field theory interactions. They included ΣNN admixtures in the hypertriton wave function and used distorted-wave π⁻–³He final-state interactions. The inclusive π⁻ decay rate was obtained via the measured branching ratio R₃ = 0.35 ± 0.04, and π⁰ contributions were added using the ΔI = 1/2 rule, with non-mesonic corrections of 1.5% and 0.8% applied.

## Key numbers

- The two-body π⁻ decay rate Γ(³ΛH → ³He + π⁻) ranges from 0.943 GHz (at UV cutoff 800 MeV) to 1.293 GHz (at UV cutoff 1400 MeV), corresponding to Λ separation energies from 69 keV to 162 keV.
- The resulting hypertriton lifetime τ(³ΛH) varies from 234 ± 27 ps (at Λ separation energy 69 keV) to 176 ± 20 ps (at Λ separation energy 162 keV).
- Replacing plane-wave pions with distorted-wave π⁻–³He interactions enhances the two-body decay rate by ≈ 15%.
- ΣNN admixtures (contributing ≤ 0.5% to the wave function norm) reduce the purely ΛNN decay rate by ≈ 10%.
- For the STAR Collaboration's reported Λ separation energy of 410 keV, the extrapolated two-body decay rate is 1.548 GHz and the lifetime is 147 ± 17 ps.
- The combined spread across 4 YN interactions and 42 NN+NNN interactions gives Λ separation energies 90–190 keV, two-body rates 1.0–1.4 GHz, and lifetimes 160–220 ps.

## Context

This work revisits the "hypertriton lifetime puzzle" where experiments from STAR (2017, 2021), HypHI (2013), and ALICE (2019, 2022) reported conflicting lifetimes ranging from 142 ps to 253 ps. It extends prior theoretical calculations by Kamada et al. (1997), Gal and Garcilazo (2018), and Pérez-Obiol et al. (2020), and finds that the ALICE (2022) measurement of τ = 253 ± 11 ± 6 ps is consistent with the authors' lowest Λ separation energy of 69 keV. The strong dependence on the poorly known Λ separation energy means future experiments at MAMI, JLab, J-PARC, and CERN with precision better than 50 keV are needed to resolve the puzzle.
