# The Kolmogorov-Smirnov test and its use for the identification of fireball fragmentation

> We propose an application of the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test for rapidity distributions of individual events in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The test is particularly suitable to recognise non-statistical differences between the events. Thus when applied to a narrow centrality class it could indicate differences between events which would not be expected if all events evolve according to the same scenario. In particular, as an example we assume here a possible fragmentation of the fireball into smaller pieces at the quark/hadron phase transition. Quantitative studies are performed with a Monte Carlo model capable of simulating such a distribution of hadrons. We conclude that the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test is a very powerful tool for the identification of the fragmentation process.

## Key claim

The Kolmogorov-Smirnov test can identify non-statistical event-by-event differences in rapidity distributions from ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, and is specifically sensitive to fireball fragmentation into droplets at the quark/hadron phase transition, provided the rapidity resolution is better than about 0.5 units.

## Method

The authors generate artificial events using the Monte Carlo event generator DRAGON, which simulates a fireball that can decay into droplets of variable size (volume parameter b) plus a bulk hadron gas. For each pair of events, they construct empirical cumulative distribution functions of the rapidity of final-state hadrons, compute the maximum vertical distance D between them, and transform it into the Kolmogorov-Smirnov statistic d. They then calculate the corresponding Q-value (the probability of observing a larger d under the null hypothesis that both events come from the same underlying distribution) and histogram the Q-values over many event pairs; a deviation from a uniform Q-distribution, quantified by the parameter R, signals non-statistical differences between events.

## Key numbers

* For RHIC Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV with dN/dy=1000, the R parameter for all hadrons is approximately 10 times larger when all particles come from droplets (b=10 fm^3) compared to the case with no droplets.
* For FAIR Au+Au at sqrt(s)=7.6 A GeV with dN/dy=1500 and no droplets, the Q-histogram shows no false positive signal, consistent with the RHIC no-droplet case.
* Droplet sizes down to b=5 fm^3 produce a clear low-Q peak in the Q-histogram for charged hadrons; the R value for b=5 fm^3 is still well above the no-droplet baseline.
* Even when only 25% of hadrons come from droplets (b=10 fm^3) and the rest from the bulk, the signal remains visible in the Q-histogram for charged hadrons.
* Smearing rapidities with a Gaussian of width 0.5 units weakens the low-Q peak to a level comparable to the resonance-only background; smearing of 0.1 units (typical experimental resolution) preserves the signal.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal of spinodal fragmentation at ultrarelativistic energies (Mishustin 1998, Scavenius 2000) and the hydrodynamic instability analysis of Torrieri (2008) by providing a practical statistical test to detect such fragmentation in data. It contrasts with the equilibrium crossover scenario from lattice QCD (Aoki 2006) and complements earlier fluctuation-based probes such as multiplicity fluctuations (Baym 1999), mean p_t fluctuations (Broniowski 2005), and two-pion femtoscopy (granular HBT, Torrieri 2007). The method enables experimental searches for fireball fragmentation at RHIC and LHC, and can also set upper limits on droplet formation if no signal is observed.
