# Rotating hybrid stars with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model

> We study rapidly rotating hybrid stars with the Dyson-Schwinger model for quark matter and the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory with realistic two-body and three-body forces for nuclear matter. We determine the maximum gravitational mass, equatorial radius, and rotation frequency of stable stellar configurations by considering the constraints of the Keplerian limit and the secular axisymmetric instability, and compare with observational data. We also discuss the rotational evolution for constant baryonic mass, and find a spinup phenomenon for supramassive stars before they collapse to black holes.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the spinup (backbending) phenomenon in rotating hybrid stars is not related to the hadron-quark phase transition, but instead occurs in supramassive stars before they collapse to black holes, which is possible in a narrow range of large mass for both hybrid and purely nucleonic neutron stars.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory with realistic two-body and three-body forces for nuclear matter (using the Bonn-B potential) and the Dyson-Schwinger quark model for quark matter, with a Gibbs construction for the hadron-quark phase transition. They then compute rotating stellar configurations using the KEH method and the public RNS code, determining stable configurations by considering both the Keplerian (mass-shedding) limit and the secular axisymmetric instability condition.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of static purely nucleonic stars (BOB EOS): 2.51 solar masses
- Maximum gravitational mass of static hybrid stars (DS1 EOS): 2.30 solar masses; (DS2 EOS): 2.02 solar masses; (DS3 EOS): 1.79 solar masses; (DS4 EOS): 1.60 solar masses; (DS10 EOS): 1.48 solar masses
- Maximum gravitational mass of Keplerian purely nucleonic stars (BOB EOS): 2.99 solar masses
- Maximum gravitational mass of Keplerian hybrid stars (DS1 EOS): 2.82 solar masses; (DS2 EOS): 2.47 solar masses; (DS3 EOS): 2.19 solar masses; (DS4 EOS): 1.95 solar masses; (DS10 EOS): 1.76 solar masses
- Maximum Keplerian frequency for purely nucleonic stars (BOB EOS): 1653 Hz
- Maximum Keplerian frequency for hybrid stars (DS2 EOS): 1399 Hz; (DS10 EOS): 1763 Hz
- Maximum quark matter fraction in hybrid stars with DS2 EOS: 8.39% for the heaviest possible static neutron star with baryonic mass 2.35 solar masses
- The baryon mass range for conversion from a purely nucleonic star to a hybrid star with DS2 EOS: 1.84 to 2.37 solar masses
- The Dyson-Schwinger model parameter used: bag constant B_DS = 90 MeV fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of static hybrid stars with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model (Chen 2011) by including the effects of rapid rotation. It uses the KEH method developed by Komatsu (1989) and the public RNS code for rotating stellar structure calculations. The results are compared with observational constraints from massive pulsars (heavy, heavy2, heavy3) and the fast-rotating pulsar PSR J1748-2446ad at 716 Hz. The paper finds that Dyson-Schwinger model equations of state with parameter alpha greater than or equal to 2 are ruled out by current two-solar-mass pulsar observations. The spinup phenomenon previously associated with the hadron-quark phase transition by Weber (1997) and Haensel (2016) is instead attributed to supramassive stars in this model.
