# Numerical calculation of 3-pion Coulomb scattering using scalar QED

> The Coulomb scattering between three charged pions is shown to be perturbatively calculable using scalar QED for the relative momenta accessible to high-energy experiments of particle collisions. For the triplet relative momenta, $Q_3 \gtrsim 50$ MeV/$c$, the calculated three-pion correlation function at $O(α^2)$ is very similar to the commonly-used triple-product of pair factors -- Riverside approximation. At lower $Q_3$, but above the lowest existing experimental measurement of $\sim15$ MeV/$c$, terms of $O(α^3)$ or higher are needed to obtain a convincing description of three-pion Coulomb interactions. This is in contrast to two-pion correlations, for which the known exact solution is shown to be saturated by the lower orders at comparable pair relative momenta.

# Numerical calculation of 3-pion Coulomb scattering using scalar QED

## Key claim

For triplet relative momenta Q3 greater than about 50 MeV/c, the three-pion Coulomb correlation function calculated at O(alpha^2) is very similar to the commonly-used Riverside approximation (triple product of pair Gamow factors); however, at lower Q3 down to about 15 MeV/c, terms of O(alpha^3) or higher are needed for a convincing description, in contrast to the two-pion case where lower orders saturate the exact solution at comparable pair momenta.

## Method

The author calculates two- and three-pion Coulomb scattering amplitudes using Feynman diagrams in scalar QED with dimensional regularization, restricting to straight-ladder diagrams which dominate for nonrelativistic final-state kinematics. The Feynman integrals are numerically evaluated using the sector decomposition method implemented in the pySecDec package, with integration by parts identities (via FIRE6) used to reduce computational complexity for some diagrams. For three-pion calculations, the correlation function is obtained by a weighted average over triplet configurations, sampling from asymmetric to equilateral configurations with weights proportional to the product of the three pair relative momenta.

## Key numbers

- The pion Bohr radius is a = 387.5 fm, and the binding energy of two oppositely charged pions is approximately 2 keV.
- For two-pion correlations, the expansion parameter alpha' = alpha * m / q is less than or approximately 0.2 for charged pions given the experimental lower bound of q approximately 5 MeV/c.
- The two-pion numerical calculation at O(alpha) and O(alpha^2) agrees with the Gamow factor to better than one percent for the nonrelativistic q values shown.
- For three-pion correlations, at Q3 greater than or approximately 0.05 GeV/c (50 MeV/c), the O(alpha^2) calculation is within 1% of the Riverside approximation.
- The O(alpha^3) contribution is calculated only for the symmetric configuration at Q3 = 0.105 GeV/c due to computational demands.
- The lowest existing experimental measurement of three-pion correlations reaches approximately 15 MeV/c in Q3.

## Context

This work extends perturbative QED techniques for Coulomb scattering from the two-body case, where the exact Gamow solution is known (Landau 1981, Lednicky 2005) and the resummation of ladder diagrams was shown by Baier (1969), to the three-body case which lacks a closed-form solution. The Riverside approximation (Liu 1986) and its generalized version used by the ALICE collaboration (2013, 2015) for three-pion Coulomb corrections in Bose-Einstein correlation measurements are tested against the perturbative calculation. The results enable future inclusion of finite source-size effects and suggest that the observed suppression of three-pion Bose-Einstein correlations at Q3 below about 50 MeV/c may require genuine three-body Coulomb effects beyond the Riverside approximation to be understood.
