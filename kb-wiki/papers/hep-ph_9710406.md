# Pre-equilibrium evolution of quark-gluon plasma

> We study the production and the evolution of quark-gluon plasma expected to be formed in ultra relativistic heavy-ion collisions, within the color flux-tube model. We introduce the gluonic component in the Boltzmann equation, which we solve in the phase space which is extended to include the SU(3) color degree of freedom. The color degree of freedom is shown to play a decisive role in equilibration, and in fixing the temperature of the plasma. We further find that the soft partons that we study here contribute substantially to the bulk properties. Finally, the model is shown to provide a detailed picture of how the quark-gluon plasma evolves and is driven towards a hydrodynamic flow, which starts occuring around 1 $fm$.

# Pre-equilibrium evolution of quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The color degree of freedom in SU(3) plays a decisive role in equilibration and in fixing the temperature of the plasma, with the system showing a trend towards hydrodynamic flow starting around 1 fm, but the authors note that the Bjorken scenario is probably valid only up to 3-4 fm and their results are restricted to proper times ≤ 1.5 fm.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation in an extended phase space that includes the SU(3) color degree of freedom, incorporating quark, antiquark, and gluon components within the color flux-tube model. The Schwinger mechanism provides the source terms for parton production from a background chromoelectric field, while collisions are treated via a relaxation time approximation with τ_c values of 0.001, 0.2, and 5 fm. The coupled transport equations are solved numerically using a double self-consistent method, with the initial energy density set to 300 GeV/fm^3 corresponding to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density: ε₀ = 300 GeV/fm³ (for LHC energies)
- Peak number density for quark-antiquark together (two flavors): approximately 60 /fm³
- Maximum temperature obtained: around 300 MeV
- For τ_c = 0.2 fm, the peak average energy per particle is around 2.0 GeV
- For τ_c = 0.001 fm (hydrodynamic limit), maximum number density is around 120 /fm³ at τ ≈ 1.0 fm
- For τ_c = 0.2 fm, maximum number density is around 80 /fm³
- For τ_c = 0.001 fm, maximum average energy per particle is around 1.0 GeV; for τ_c = 5.0 fm, it is around 4.0 GeV
- At τ ∼ 1 fm, the SU(3) number density is larger than SU(2) by a factor ∼ 10, and energy by a factor ∼ 5
- Hydrodynamic exponents found: n(τ) ∼ τ^(-0.7), ε(τ) ∼ τ^(-1.23), T(τ) ∼ τ^(-0.31) (ideal values would be -1, -4/3, and -1/3 respectively)
- Only 30 percent of the field has decayed in the hydrodynamic limit (τ_c = 0.001 fm) compared to larger decay for τ_c = 0.2 and 5 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(2) study (Nayak and Ravishankar, 1997) by including the gluonic component and the realistic SU(3) gauge group, which were absent in earlier flux-tube model studies by Baym, Kajantie, and Banerjee that were purely abelian. The model provides a non-perturbative complement to perturbative QCD-based approaches such as HIJING (Wang and Gyulassy) and the parton cascade model (PCM), which are insufficient for studying soft parton dynamics. The results enable future calculations of dilepton, direct photon, and strangeness production signatures from the pre-equilibrium stage, and suggest that a unified approach combining hard and soft components could be developed following the chromoviscous hydrodynamics approach of Eskola and Gyulassy.
