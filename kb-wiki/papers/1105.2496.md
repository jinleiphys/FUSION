# Searching for Perfect Fluids: Quantum Viscosity in a Universal Fermi Gas

> We measure the shear viscosity in a two-component Fermi gas of atoms, tuned to a broad s-wave collisional (Feshbach) resonance. At resonance, the atoms strongly interact and exhibit universal behavior, where the equilibrium thermodynamic properties and the transport coefficients are universal functions of the density $n$ and temperature $T$. We present a new calibration of the temperature as a function of global energy, which is directly measured from the cloud profiles. Using the calibration, the trap-averaged shear viscosity in units of $\hbar\,n$ is determined as a function of the reduced temperature at the trap center, from nearly the ground state to the unitary two-body regime. Low temperature data is obtained from the damping rate of the radial breathing mode, while high temperature data is obtained from hydrodynamic expansion measurements. We also show that the best fit to the high temperature expansion data is obtained for a vanishing bulk viscosity. The measured trap-averaged entropy per particle and shear viscosity are used to estimate the ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy density, which is compared that conjectured for a perfect fluid.

## Key claim

The paper measures the trap-averaged shear viscosity in a unitary Fermi gas as a function of reduced temperature, and shows that the best fit to high-temperature expansion data is obtained for a vanishing bulk viscosity, consistent with theoretical predictions; the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is compared to the conjectured perfect fluid bound of \(\eta/s \geq \hbar/(4\pi k_B)\).

## Method

The shear viscosity was determined using two complementary hydrodynamic methods: (1) at low temperatures, by measuring the damping rate of the radial breathing mode after a brief release and recapture of the cloud; (2) at high temperatures, by observing the time-dependent aspect ratio of the cloud after release from a deep optical trap. Universal hydrodynamic equations including both friction force and viscous heating were solved assuming a linear velocity field, and the trap-averaged viscosity parameter \(\bar{\alpha} = \int d^3\mathbf{x}\,\eta/(\hbar N)\) was extracted as the only free parameter by fitting to the data. Temperature was calibrated using a combination of experimental energy-versus-entropy data and the second virial coefficient approximation for the high-temperature regime.

## Key numbers

* Trap-averaged viscosity coefficient \(\bar{\alpha}\) versus reduced temperature \(\theta_0 = T_0/T_F(n_0)\) at the trap center was measured from nearly the ground state to the unitary two-body regime; the fit \(\bar{\alpha} = \bar{\alpha}_{3/2}\,\theta_0^{3/2} + \bar{\alpha}_{1/2}\,\theta_0^{1/2}\) yields \(\bar{\alpha}_{3/2} = 2.96(3)\) and \(\bar{\alpha}_{1/2} = 1.87(8)\) for the normal fluid regime (\(E > 0.7\,E_F\)).
* For the high-temperature expansion data at \(E/E_F = 3.3\), the best fit with pure shear viscosity gives \(\bar{\alpha}_S = 24.4\) with reduced \(\chi^2 = 1.6\), while the best fit with pure bulk viscosity gives \(\bar{\alpha}_B = 16.7\) with reduced \(\chi^2 = 8.6\); zero bulk viscosity yields the minimum reduced \(\chi^2\).
* Trap frequencies for the deep optical trap: \(\omega_z = 2\pi \times (182.7 \pm 0.5)\) Hz, \(\omega_x = 2\pi \times (5283 \pm 10)\) Hz, \(\omega_y = 2\pi \times (5052 \pm 10)\) Hz, \(\bar{\omega} = 2\pi \times (1696 \pm 9)\) Hz; total atom number ranges from \(N = 4.0 \times 10^5\) at \(E = 2.3\,E_F\) to \(N = 6.0 \times 10^5\) at \(E = 4.6\,E_F\).
* For the shallow trap used in breathing mode experiments: \(\omega_\perp = 2\pi \times 1696(10)\) Hz, \(\omega_x/\omega_y = 1.107(0.004)\), \(\omega_z = 2\pi \times 71(3)\) Hz, \(\bar{\omega} = 2\pi \times 589(5)\) Hz; typical Fermi temperature \(T_F \simeq 2.4\,\mu\)K; total atom number \(N = 2.0 \times 10^5\).
* The fit to energy-versus-entropy data yields critical parameters \(S_c = 2.04(39)\), \(E_c = 0.78(12)\,E_F\), and \(T_c = 0.24(8)\,T_{FI}\).

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of shear viscosity in unitary Fermi gases (CaoViscosity, TurlapovPerfect) and provides a precise calibration of temperature versus energy, enabling comparison with theoretical predictions for the temperature dependence of viscosity (BruunViscousNormalDamping, LevinViscosity, TaylorViscosity, ZwergerViscosity). The vanishing bulk viscosity result confirms theoretical predictions by SonBulkViscosity and EscobedoBulkViscosity. The measured \(\eta/s\) ratio is compared to the perfect fluid conjecture of Kovtun, which was originally derived using string theory methods and has been applied to the quark-gluon plasma produced in gold-ion collisions at 100 GeV per nucleon. The results enable future tests of the local density approximation and the relation between trap-averaged and local viscosity coefficients.
