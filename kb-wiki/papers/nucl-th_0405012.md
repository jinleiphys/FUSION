# Fragmentation path of excited nuclear systems

> We perform a study of the fragmentation path of excited nuclear sources, within the framework of a stochastic mean-field approach. We consider the reaction $^{129}$Xe + $^{119}$Sn at two beam energies: 32 and 50 MeV/A, for central collisions. It is observed that, after the compression phase the system expands towards a dilute configuration from which it may recontract or evolve into a bubble-like structure. Then fragments are formed through the development of volume and/or surface instabilities. The two possibilities co-exist at 32 MeV/A, leading to quite different fragment partitions, while at 50 MeV/A the hollow configuration is observed in all events. Large variances are recovered in a way fully consistent with the presence of spinodal decomposition remnants. Kinematical properties of fragments are discussed and suggested as observables very sensitive to the dominant fragment production mechanism. A larger radial collective flow is observed at 50 MeV/A, in agreement with experiments.

## Key claim
The fragmentation path of excited nuclear sources formed in central heavy-ion collisions is driven by volume and surface instabilities encountered during the expansion phase, but the specific outcome (recontraction versus bubble-like fragmentation) depends on beam energy and coexists at 32 MeV/A while being universal at 50 MeV/A.

## Method
The authors performed stochastic mean-field (Boltzmann-Langevin) simulations of central collisions of 129Xe + 119Sn at 32 and 50 MeV/A, using a Skyrme-like mean-field with a soft equation of state (compressibility K = 200 MeV). Density fluctuations were injected locally in coordinate space every 5 fm/c, scaled to match the kinetic equilibrium fluctuations of a Fermi gas at local temperature, and the system evolution was followed for 240 fm/c. Fragment distributions were obtained both at the freeze-out stage and after secondary de-excitation.

## Key numbers
- Reaction: 129Xe + 119Sn at 32 MeV/A; initial source: A = 247, Z = 103, T = 8.3 MeV, R = 6.6 fm, radial expansion velocity β = 0.09 r/R.
- Reaction: 129Xe + 119Sn at 50 MeV/A; initial source: A = 244, Z = 101, T = 10 MeV, R = 7.5 fm, radial expansion velocity β = 0.13 r/R.
- At 32 MeV/A, the system breaks into 5 fragments on average (Z ≥ 3) when fluctuations are included; without fluctuations it recontracts.
- At 50 MeV/A, the total charge of IMFs (Z ≥ 3) at freeze-out is 75, compared to 84 at 32 MeV/A.
- Fluctuations were injected every Δt = 5 fm/c; the collisional damping time τ_coll ≈ 50 fm/c at T ≈ 4 MeV.
- The characteristic growth time for unstable modes is τ ≈ 30 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends stochastic mean-field studies of multifragmentation (Colonna and collaborators, earlier BOB method) by implementing local density fluctuations in coordinate space, which allows application to charge-asymmetric systems. The results at 32 MeV/A agree with BOB calculations and with experimental data for the same reaction (John, 2000). The coexistence of recontraction and bubble-like fragmentation at 32 MeV/A is linked to observations of negative specific heat (Chomaz, 2001) and spinodal decomposition remnants (Borderie, 2001). The study enables future investigation of the nuclear equation of state at low density and the transition from fragmentation to vaporization at higher energies.
