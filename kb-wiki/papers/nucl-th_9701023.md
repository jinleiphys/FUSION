# What can we learn from three-pion interferometry ?

> We address the question which additional information on the source shape and dynamics can be extracted from three-particle Bose-Einstein correlations. For chaotic sources the true three-particle correlation term is shown to be sensitive to the momentum dependence of the saddle point of the source and to its asymmetries around that point. For partially coherent sources the three-pion correlator allows to measure the degree of coherence without contamination from resonance decays. We derive the most general Gaussian parametrization of the two- and three-particle correlator for this case and discuss the space-time interpretation of the corresponding parameters.

# What can we learn from three-pion interferometry?

## Key claim
For chaotic sources, the true three-pion correlation term is sensitive to the momentum dependence of the saddle point of the source and to its asymmetries around that point, but this sensitivity enters only at fourth order in the relative momenta, making the extraction of such structural information extremely difficult experimentally. For partially coherent sources, the three-pion correlator allows measurement of the degree of coherence without contamination from resonance decays, but the required extrapolation to vanishing relative momenta is sensitive to the assumed functional form of the correlator.

## Method
The authors derive analytic expressions for two- and three-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions in terms of the source Wigner density, expanding the exchange amplitudes in small relative momenta to isolate the phase contributions. For chaotic sources, they show that the normalized true three-pion correlator isolates a phase that depends on the first and second derivatives of the source saddle point with respect to momentum and on the third central space-time moments of the emission function. For partially coherent sources, they decompose the source into chaotic and coherent Wigner densities and derive the most general Gaussian parametrizations for the two- and three-particle correlators correct up to second order in relative momenta.

## Key numbers
- The normalized true three-pion correlation function for chaotic sources is given by r₃ = 2 cos Φ, where Φ is the sum of the phases of the three two-particle exchange amplitudes.
- For chaotic sources, the phase Φ enters at second order in relative momenta q (proportional to ∂⟨x_μ⟩/∂K^ν) and receives contributions from third central moments at order q³, making the normalized three-pion correlator sensitive to source asymmetries only at order q⁵.
- For partially coherent sources at vanishing relative momenta, the two-pion correlator intercept is R₂(p,p) = ε(p)(2 − ε(p)) and the three-pion correlator intercept is R₃(p,p,p) = 2 ε²(p)(3 − 2ε(p)), where ε(p) is the momentum-dependent chaotic fraction.
- The normalized three-pion correlator at vanishing relative momenta is r₃(p,p,p) = 2√ε(p) (3 − 2ε(p))/(2 − ε(p))^(3/2), which deviates from the chaotic limit of 2.
- The most general Gaussian parametrization for partially coherent sources depends on 16 parameters (all functions of the average momentum K), requiring measurement of 15 momentum components (6 for two-particle and 9 for three-particle spectra) plus the coherence parameter ε(K).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of multi-particle Bose-Einstein correlations by Zajc (1987), Pratt (1993), and Zhang (1993, 1995), and the covariant current formalism of Gyulassy, Kaufmann, and Wilson (1979) and Chapman and Heinz (1994). The authors show that the simple one- or two-parameter Gaussian parametrizations suggested by Białas, Bożek, and Mączka (1990) and Pratt, Rischke, and Wiedemann (1992) are not general enough, as they assume a pointlike coherent source. The results enable a framework for separating the space-time characteristics of chaotic and coherent source components, though the authors are pessimistic about short-term experimental prospects for extracting source asymmetries from three-pion correlations.
