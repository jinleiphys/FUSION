# Relativistic Spin-Flavor States in Light Front Dynamics

> Orthonormal spin-flavor wave functions of Lorentz covariant quark models of the Bakamjian-Thomas type are constructed for nucleon resonances. Three different bases are presented. The manifestly Lorentz covariant Dirac-Melosh basis is related to the Pauli-Melosh basis and the symmetrized Bargmann-Wigner basis that are manifestly orthogonal.

## Key claim

Orthonormal spin-flavor wave functions for nucleon resonances can be constructed in three different bases (Dirac-Melosh, Pauli-Melosh, and symmetrized Bargmann-Wigner) that are related to each other, but the Dirac-Melosh basis is not manifestly orthogonal, unlike the Pauli-Melosh and symmetrized Bargmann-Wigner bases, and the construction is limited to Bakamjian-Thomas-type Lorentz covariant quark models.

## Method

The authors construct relativistic three-quark spin-flavor wave functions for nucleon resonances by starting from nonrelativistic quark model states and applying Melosh rotations to transform instant-form spinors to light-front spinors. They then express the Pauli spinor matrix elements in terms of Dirac spinor matrix elements using projection operators, generalize the expressions to arbitrary nucleon momenta via Lorentz boosts, and symmetrize the resulting invariants in the uds basis where quarks are treated as distinguishable. The Dirac-Melosh basis is related to the Pauli-Melosh basis and the symmetrized Bargmann-Wigner basis through explicit transformation formulas.

## Context

This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) type covariant light cone quark models (Terent'ev, Kondratyuk and collaborators) by providing explicit relationships between three different bases for baryon spin-flavor wave functions: the Dirac-Melosh basis used by particle physicists (Azn, Wap, Dzi, Krusche and Weber), the Pauli-Melosh basis used by nuclear physicists (Terent'ev, Kondratyuk and collaborators), and the symmetrized Bargmann-Wigner basis. The paper enables future calculations of baryon form factors and decays in light-front dynamics by clarifying how to translate between these bases and by providing explicit wave functions for nucleon resonances including N*(1535), N*(1520), and N*(1675).
