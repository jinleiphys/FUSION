# Gauging the three-nucleon spectator equation

> We derive relativistic three-dimensional integral equations describing the interaction of the three-nucleon system with an external electromagnetic field. Our equations are unitary, gauge invariant, and they conserve charge. This has been achieved by applying the recently introduced gauging of equations method to the three-nucleon spectator equations where spectator nucleons are always on mass shell. As a result, the external photon is attached to all possible places in the strong interaction model, so that current and charge conservation are implemented in the theoretically correct fashion. Explicit expressions are given for the three-nucleon bound state electromagnetic current, as well as the transition currents for the scattering processes γHe3 -> NNN, Nd -> γNd, and γHe3 -> Nd. As a result, a unified covariant three-dimensional description of the NNN-γNNN system is achieved.

## Key claim

The authors derive relativistic three-dimensional integral equations for the three-nucleon system interacting with an external electromagnetic field that are unitary, gauge invariant, and conserve charge, by applying the "gauging of equations" method to the spectator equations where spectator nucleons are always on mass shell; the method automatically attaches the external photon to all possible places in the strong interaction model without introducing new spectator particles or double counting diagrams.

## Method

The authors start from the four-dimensional three-nucleon bound state equation and replace the Feynman propagator of the spectator particle with an on-mass-shell delta-function propagator to obtain the three-dimensional spectator equation. They then apply the gauging of equations method to this spectator equation, treating it as an eight-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation with some propagators represented by on-mass-shell delta-functions, to derive integral equations for the electromagnetic transition currents. Explicit expressions are given for the three-nucleon bound state electromagnetic current and for transition currents of the scattering processes gamma-He3 -> NNN, Nd -> gamma Nd, and gamma-He3 -> Nd.

## Key numbers

- The three-nucleon bound state current j^mu(K,Q) satisfies the current conservation relation q_mu j^mu = 0, where q is the four-momentum of the incoming photon
- The charge conservation condition j^mu(Q,Q) = 2e Q^mu is verified, where e is the physical charge of the three-body bound state and Q is the total four-momentum
- The normalization condition for the bound state vertex function is given as: -i bPhi_1^Q P_{12} delta_1 delta_2 d_3 (partial t_1 / partial Q^2) delta_2 d_3 P_{12} Phi_1^Q + i bPhi_1^Q delta_1 (partial(delta_2 d_3)/partial Q^2) P_{12} Phi_1^Q = 1, where Q^2 = M^2 and M is the mass of the three-nucleon bound state

## Context

This work extends the gauging of equations method previously introduced by the same authors for four-dimensional equations (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1993) and for three-dimensional equations within the spectator approach (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1993) to the three-nucleon spectator equations introduced by Gross (1969). The Gross spectator approach had been used in successful relativistic calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering (Holinde, 1990), elastic electron-deuteron scattering (Van Orden, 1990), pion photoproduction from the nucleon (Surya, 1992), and the triton binding energy (Stadler, 1995), but the expressions needed to calculate electromagnetic properties of the three-nucleon system were not previously available. The derived equations enable a unified covariant three-dimensional description of the NNN-gammaNNN system, allowing calculations of electromagnetic form factors of triton and He-3, as well as various photoproduction and electroproduction processes.
