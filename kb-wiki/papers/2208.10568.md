# Quantifying uncertainties due to irreducible three-body forces in deuteron-nucleus reactions

> \noindent{\bf Background:} Deuteron-induced nuclear reactions are an essential tool for probing the structure of nuclei as well as astrophysical information such as $(n,γ)$ cross sections. The deuteron-nucleus system is typically described within a Faddeev three-body model consisting of a neutron ($n$), a proton ($p$), and the target nucleus ($A$) interacting through pairwise phenomenological potentials. While Faddeev techniques enable the exact description of the three-body dynamics, their predictive power is limited in part by the omission of irreducible neutron-proton-nucleus three-body force ($n$-$p$-$A$ 3BF). {\bf Results:} By comparing the Faddeev and NCSM/RGM results, we show that the irreducible $n$-$p$-$α$ 3BF has a non-negligible effect on bound state and scattering observables alike. Specifically, the Faddeev approach %are yields a $^6$Li ground state that is approximately $600$~keV shallower than the one obtained with the NCSM/RGM. Additionally, the Faddeev calculations for $d$+$α$ scattering yield a $3^+$ resonance that is located approximately $400$~keV higher in energy compared to the NCSM/RGM result. The shape of the $d$+$α$ angular distributions computed using the two approaches also differ, owing to the discrepancy in the predictions of the $3^+$ resonance energy.

## Key claim

By comparing Faddeev three-body calculations (which omit irreducible three-body forces) with full six-body NCSM/RGM calculations for the d+alpha system, the authors show that the irreducible n-p-alpha three-body force arising from Pauli exclusion principle effects has a non-negligible impact on both bound state and scattering observables, accounting for approximately 600 keV of the Li-6 binding energy and shifting the 3+ resonance by about 400 keV.

## Method

The authors computed microscopic n-alpha and p-alpha momentum-space potentials using the no-core shell model coupled with the resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) with a similarity renormalization group evolved chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interaction at lambda_SRG = 1.5 fm^-1. These potentials were used as input to momentum-space Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations solved in a Coulomb basis to obtain three-body predictions for the d+alpha system. Simultaneously, six-body NCSM/RGM calculations of d+alpha scattering and the Li-6 ground state were performed using the same nucleon-nucleon interaction to serve as a benchmark that includes the full antisymmetrization effects.

## Key numbers

- The Faddeev approach yields a Li-6 ground state that is approximately 600 keV shallower than the one obtained with the NCSM/RGM
- The Faddeev calculations for d+alpha scattering yield a 3+ resonance that is located approximately 400 keV higher in energy compared to the NCSM/RGM result
- The NCSM/RGM model space was truncated at N_max = 12/13 for positive/negative parity partial waves with hbar_Omega = 14 MeV and lambda_SRG = 1.5 fm^-1
- The Woods-Saxon potential used for comparison required a Pauli projector strength Gamma = 1100 fm^-1 to remove a Pauli-forbidden bound state

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of three-body forces in deuteron-induced reactions, such as the work of Dinmore (2019) where the three-body force emerges from target excitation mechanisms. The study isolates contributions to the irreducible three-body force arising specifically from Pauli exclusion principle effects, disentangling them from target excitation and three-nucleon forces. The results lay the foundation for future parametrizations of the three-body force due to Pauli exclusion principle effects in improved three-body calculations of deuteron-induced reactions on heavier targets.
