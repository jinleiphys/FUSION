# Microscopic composite systems bound by strong gravity in extra dimensions as candidates for dark matter

> In the Arkani-Hamed-Dimopoulos-Dvali (ADD) model with n extra compactified dimensions, the gravitational potential scales as 1/r^{n+1} and becomes significantly stronger at short distances. We investigate the possibility of forming small-sized composite systems of Standard Model particles bound by this potential. Such bound states, composed of quarks, neutrinos, axions, or other particles, exhibit a small cross-section-to-mass ratio, making them viable candidates for dark matter.

## Key claim

In the ADD model with extra compactified dimensions, Standard Model particles (quarks, neutrinos, axions) could form small-sized gravitationally bound composite systems that serve as dark matter candidates, provided the number of constituent fermions exceeds approximately 1600, based on the assumption that gravitational and electromagnetic interactions become comparable at the cut-off distance.

## Method

The author uses a variational approach with a Gaussian ansatz for the particle number density to find the ground state energy of N fermions bound by the modified gravitational potential V(r) = -G m1 m2 R^n / r^(n+1) at short distances. The total energy is minimized as the sum of relativistic kinetic energy for an ideal ultrarelativistic Fermi gas and the gravitational energy, with the latter regularized by a cut-off parameter r_c for n>1. The condition for bound state formation (E_grav + E_kin < 0) yields estimates for the minimal number of particles, and the cut-off scale is estimated by equating the gravitational and electromagnetic potentials at distance r_c.

## Key numbers

* Minimal number of identical fermions needed to form a bound state: N > 1/alpha^(3/2) ≈ 1600, derived from equating gravitational and electromagnetic interactions at the cut-off distance.
* For n=1 extra dimension, gravitational energy scales as E_grav ≈ -N^2 G m^2 R_(1) / R^2.
* For n=2 extra dimensions, gravitational energy scales as E_grav ≈ -2 N^2 G m^2 R^2_(2) / (sqrt(pi) R^3) * ln(R/r_c).
* For n>2 extra dimensions, gravitational energy scales as E_grav ≈ -4 N^2 G m^2 R^n_(n) / (2^(n/2) sqrt(pi) (n-2) r_c^(n-2) R^3).
* To avoid black hole collapse for constituent quark mass m=300 MeV: N < 10^38 * (r_c / fm).
* For a nugget mass M ~ B m_p, the cross-section-to-mass ratio satisfies sigma/M < 0.1 cm^2/g ~ 10 fm^2/GeV for any baryon number B.
* For nuggets comprising all dark matter, one nugget per year per m^2 detector is expected when B ~ 10^19.

## Context

This work extends the ADD model (Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos, Dvali, 1998) for extra dimensions to the formation of composite dark matter candidates, building on prior studies of strangelets (Witten, 1984) and axion-quark nuggets (Zhitnitsky and collaborators, 2003-2023). The paper addresses the stability problem of quark nuggets by proposing that singular gravitational potential in extra dimensions provides the binding mechanism, in contrast to earlier models that relied on strange quarks or axion-pion domain walls. The work enables future theoretical development of a comprehensive theory incorporating generalized Einstein equations and new short-distance physics, and suggests that existing dark matter detectors (LZ, XENONnT, PandaX-4T, IceCube) and geophysical detection methods (seismic networks, meteor radars) could be sensitive to these gravitational nuggets, though the cross-section suppression factor B^(2/3) requires re-evaluation of existing limits.
