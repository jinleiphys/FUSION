# Confirmation of quark-hadron duality in the neutron F_2 structure function

> We apply a recently developed technique to extract for the first time the neutron F_2^n structure function from inclusive proton and deuteron data in the nucleon resonance region, and test the validity of quark-hadron duality in the neutron. We establish the accuracy of duality in the low-lying neutron resonance regions over a range of Q^2, and compare with the corresponding results on the proton and with theoretical expectations. The confirmation of duality in both the neutron and proton opens the possibility of using resonance region data to constrain parton distributions at large x.

## Key claim
The paper confirms that quark-hadron duality holds for the neutron F_2 structure function, establishing that the phenomenon is not accidental (as some arguments suggested for the proton) but a general property of nucleon structure functions; the agreement between resonance-region data and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) fits is better than 10% when integrated over the entire resonance region (W^2 < 4 GeV^2), with local deviations of 15-20% in individual resonance regions.

## Method
The authors extracted the neutron F_2^n structure function from inclusive proton and deuteron F_2 data in the nucleon resonance region using an iterative technique that applies nuclear corrections additively, accounting for Fermi motion, binding, off-shell effects, and quasi-elastic contributions. They used 514 data points from Jefferson Lab experiment E00-116 (Q^2 from 4.5 to 6.4 GeV^2) and SLAC experiments E49a6 and E49a10 (Q^2 from 0.6 to 2.4 GeV^2), centering data at fixed Q^2 using fits from Christy and Bosted (2010). They then computed truncated moments of F_2^n over specific W^2 intervals (1st, 2nd, 3rd resonance regions and the full region) and compared these to moments from global QCD fits to high-W^2 DIS data.

## Key numbers
- The extraction covers Q^2 from 0.6 to 6.4 GeV^2, using 514 data points total.
- The off-shell correction δ^(off) F_2^d was approximately -1.5% over most of the x range, contributing less than 2% to the total error on F_2^n.
- Global agreement between resonance data and the QCD fit (Alekhin et al., 2007) shows deviations of less than 10% over the entire Q^2 range for the full resonance region (W^2 < 4 GeV^2).
- Local deviations in individual resonance regions are generally less than 15-20%, with larger deviations only in the 1st resonance region at the highest Q^2 (6.4 GeV^2, x ~ 0.9).
- The ratio of truncated neutron to proton moments (M_2^n/M_2^p) shows good agreement with QCD fits from Alekhin et al. (2007) and MSTW (2009), except in the Δ resonance region where the fits underestimate the ratio.

## Context
This work extends the study of Bloom-Gilman duality, previously established for the proton F_2 and F_L structure functions by Malace et al. (2009), Ioana et al. (2007), and Liang et al. (2008), to the neutron for the first time. It contradicts arguments by Close and Isgur (2001) that duality in the proton might be due to accidental cancellations between quark charges that do not occur for the neutron. The confirmation of duality in both nucleons enables the use of resonance-region data to constrain parton distribution functions at large x, which is important for searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model and for neutrino oscillation experiments.
