# Renormalisation Group Flow and Kaon Condensation

> Functional renormalisation group approach is applied to a system of kaons with finite chemical potential. A set of approximate flow equations for the effective couplings is derived and solved. At high scale the system is found to be at the normal phase whereas at some critical value of the running scale it undergoes the phase transition (PT) to the phase with a spontaneously broken symmetry with the kaon condensate as an order parameter. The value of the condensate turns out to be quite sensitive to the kaon-kaon scattering length.

# Renormalisation Group Flow and Kaon Condensation

## Key claim
Using a functional renormalisation group (FRG) approach with a truncated ansatz for the effective action, the system of kaons at finite chemical potential undergoes a second-order phase transition to a condensed phase at a critical scale \(k \sim \mu\), but the quantitative value of the condensate is highly sensitive to the poorly known kaon-kaon scattering length, making precise predictions currently unreliable.

## Method
The author applies the functional renormalisation group to a linear sigma model with a complex doublet field representing kaons (\(K^+, K^0\)) and (\(K^-, \bar K^0\)) at finite chemical potential. A set of approximate flow equations for the effective couplings (mass term \(u_1\), quartic coupling \(u_2\), and sextic coupling \(u_3\)) and the wavefunction renormalisation \(Z_\phi\) is derived from the scale-dependent effective potential. The flow equations are solved numerically using a Litim-type cutoff function, with initial conditions at \(\Lambda = 50\) GeV fixed by vacuum properties including the kaon mass and the kaon-kaon scattering length.

## Key numbers
- The critical chemical potential for the phase transition is found at \(\mu = m\), where \(m\) is the kaon mass.
- The starting scale for the RG flow is \(\Lambda = 50\) GeV, chosen to be much larger than any other mass scale.
- The kaon-kaon scattering length from lattice calculations is \(a_{\text{lat}} = -0.310\, m^{-1}\) (where \(m\) is the kaon mass).
- The mass term vanishes and the system enters the broken phase at a running scale \(k \sim \mu\), with specific examples shown for \(\mu = 0.51\) GeV, 0.55 GeV, and 0.595 GeV.
- The condensate grows with chemical potential, with fastest growth near threshold \(\mu \simeq \mu_{\text{crit}}\), then slowing for larger \(\mu\).

## Context
This work extends the FRG approach previously applied to neutron matter (Krippa, earlier work), Bose-Einstein condensation (Wetterich, earlier work), and balanced/imbalanced fermion systems (Krippa, earlier work) to the specific case of kaon condensation. It builds on the analysis of Bedaque (earlier work) showing that nonzero quark masses in the colour-flavour locked (CFL) phase favour meson condensation, and on the dispersion relations derived by Miransky and Son (earlier work) for systems with broken Lorentz symmetry. The results contradict the possibility of a first-order phase transition (no indication found) and highlight that the condensate value depends strongly on the kaon-kaon scattering length, with values from lattice calculations, lowest-order chiral perturbation theory, and a phenomenological model giving noticeably different results. The work enables future studies of mixed pion-kaon condensation and more realistic effective actions including momentum-dependent vertices.
