# Moments of generalized Husimi distributions and complexity of many-body quantum states

> We consider generalized Husimi distributions for many-body systems, and show that their moments are good measures of complexity of many-body quantum states. Our construction of the Husimi distribution is based on the coherent state of the single-particle transformation group. Then the coherent states are independent-particle states, and, at the same time, the most localized states in the Husimi representation. Therefore delocalization of the Husimi distribution, which can be measured by the moments, is a sign of many-body correlation (entanglement). Since the delocalization of the Husimi distribution is also related to chaoticity of the dynamics, it suggests a relation between entanglement and chaos. Our definition of the Husimi distribution can be applied not only to the systems of distinguishable particles, but also to those of identical particles, i.e., fermions and bosons. We derive an algebraic formula to evaluate the moments of the Husimi distribution.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the moments of a generalized Husimi distribution, defined using group-theoretical coherent states, serve as a measure of many-body quantum complexity (entanglement) because delocalization of the Husimi distribution signals correlation among particles; the author notes that this measure is independent of the complexity of single-particle states and that the relation between delocalization and chaotic dynamics suggests a link between entanglement and chaos, but the claim is conditional on the index q being a positive integer for practical evaluation.

## Method

The author constructs generalized coherent states based on the single-particle transformation group (e.g., SU(2) for qubits, U(N) for identical particles) following Perelomov's group-theoretical approach, where coherent states correspond to separable states for distinguishable particles and bosons, or Slater determinants for fermions. The Husimi distribution is defined as the squared absolute value of the overlap between a quantum state and these coherent states, and its moments (Rényi-Wehrl entropy) are computed via an algebraic formula that expresses the q-th moment directly in terms of expansion coefficients of the state in an orthonormal basis, using the projection onto the irreducible representation D_{qλ} within the q-fold tensor product of the Hilbert space.

## Key numbers

* For bosons with m=2 in two single-particle states (spin 1 representation), the normalized second moment of the Husimi distribution for the entangled state |1,1⟩_n is 2/3, which is the minimum value; the maximum value is 1 for coherent states.
* For the same bosonic system (m=2), the condition for the maximum normalized second moment (value 1) is c_0^2 = 2c_1c_{-1}, which characterizes coherent states.
* For bosons in N single-particle states with m particles, the dimension of the representation D_{qλ} (denoted B^{(q)}[N,m]) is (N+qm-1)!/((N-1)! (qm)!), and the ratio dim D_{qλ}/dim D_λ equals Γ(N+qm)Γ(m+1)/(Γ(N+m)Γ(qm+1)).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous proposal (Sugita, 2001) for using Husimi distribution moments as a measure of quantum chaos in one-body systems to many-body systems, and it generalizes the concept of the Rényi-Wehrl entropy introduced by Zyczkowski (1990) following questions by Wehrl (1978) and Lieb (1978). The paper enables the quantitative study of entanglement in many-body systems (including fermions and bosons) through the moments of the Husimi distribution, and it suggests a connection between entanglement and chaotic dynamics via the stationary phase condition of the coherent state path integral, which defines classical equations of motion (e.g., TDHF for fermions).
