# Higher order anisotropies in the Buda-Lund model: Disentangling flow and density field anisotropies

> The Buda-Lund hydro model describes an expanding ellipsoidal fireball, and fits the observed elliptic flow and oscillating HBT radii successfully. Due to fluctuations in energy depositions, the fireball shape however fluctuates on an event-by-event basis. The transverse plane asymmetry can be translated into a series of multipole anisotropy coefficients. These anisotropies then result in measurable momentum-space anisotropies, to be measured with respect to their respective symmetry planes. In this paper we detail an extension of the Buda-Lund model to multipole anisotropies and investigate the resulting flow coefficients and oscillations of HBT radii.

## Key claim

The Buda-Lund hydro model is extended to include multipole anisotropies (beyond elliptic flow) in both the spatial density and expansion velocity fields, showing that the same values of flow coefficients $v_n$ and HBT radius oscillations can arise from different combinations of spatial ($\epsilon_n$) and velocity-field ($\chi_n$) anisotropy parameters, creating an ambiguity that cannot be resolved by flow measurements alone.

## Method

The authors generalize the Buda-Lund source function by introducing a scaling variable $s$ with multipole spatial anisotropy coefficients $\epsilon_n$ and a velocity potential $\Phi$ with multipole flow anisotropy coefficients $\chi_n$, satisfying the co-moving derivative condition $u^\mu \partial_\mu s = 0$ for consistency with hydrodynamics. They numerically compute single-particle spectra, flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$, and the azimuthal dependence of HBT correlation radii $R_\text{out}^2$ and $R_\text{side}^2$ for various combinations of $\epsilon_2$, $\chi_2$, $\epsilon_3$, and $\chi_3$, including averaging over the relative orientation $\Delta\alpha_{23}$ between second- and third-order event planes.

## Key numbers

- Default model parameters: particle mass $m = 140$ MeV, freeze-out time $\tau_0 = 7$ fm/$c$, central freeze-out temperature $T_0 = 170$ MeV, temperature-asymmetry parameter $a^2 = 0.3$, spatial slope parameter $b = -0.1$, transverse size $R = 10$ fm, longitudinal size $Z = 15$ fm, transverse expansion $\dot R = 1$, longitudinal expansion $\dot Z = 0.94$
- Flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$ are computed at $p_t = 300$ MeV/$c$ for various $\epsilon_n$ and $\chi_n$ values
- For the $\alpha_3=\psi_3=0$ case, the change in average radius $R_{i,3,0}^2$ can be as large as 30% over the investigated interval of $\epsilon_3$ and $\chi_3$
- The third-order scaled amplitude $R_{\text{side},3,3}^2/R_{\text{side},3,0}^2$ shows a dependence mainly on spatial anisotropy $\epsilon_3$, with a wavy structure as a function of $\chi_3$

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund model (Csörgő 1995, Csanád 2003) from second-order (elliptic) anisotropies to third-order (triangular) and higher multipole anisotropies, building on earlier studies of how shape and flow anisotropies contribute to $v_2$ and HBT radius oscillations (Csanád 2008, Ster 2010). The results enable disentangling spatial density anisotropies from flow velocity anisotropies in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where higher-order anisotropies are now measurable (Adare 2011, Aamodt 2011, Adamczyk 2013, Adare 2014). The approach contrasts with the blast-wave model (Retière 2003) which did not consider third-order anisotropies or differences between second- and third-order event planes.
