# Neutrino Quantum Kinetic Equations

> Neutrinos propagate in astrophysical and cosmological environments modifying their flavor in intriguing ways. The study of neutrino propagation in media is based on the mean-field, extended mean-field and Boltzmann equations. We summarise salient features of these evolution equations and the methods employed so far to derive them. We emphasize applications to situations of observational interest.

## Key claim

This review argues that the most general mean-field equations for neutrino propagation in media must include neutrino-antineutrino pairing correlations and helicity coherence terms (from non-zero neutrino masses or magnetic moments), which have been neglected in most prior studies; these terms could produce flavor conversion between neutrino and antineutrino sectors and may become relevant in the transition region between the high-density Boltzmann regime and the dilute mean-field regime in core-collapse supernovae.

## Method

The paper reviews three theoretical frameworks used to derive neutrino evolution equations in media: the density matrix approach (with the Born-Bogoliubov-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon hierarchy), the coherent-state path integral formalism, and the Keldysh Closed-Time-Path formalism. It presents the general mean-field equations obtained from the Ehrenfest theorem for the two-point correlators (particle density matrix, antiparticle density matrix, and pairing correlators) and shows how they reduce to the standard Liouville-von Neumann equations under assumptions of homogeneity, isotropy, and neglect of pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- The measured neutrino mass squared differences are $\Delta m^2_{23} = 7.6 \times 10^{-3}$ eV$^2$ and $\Delta m^2_{12} = 2.4 \times 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$, which fix the locations of MSW resonances in supernovae.
- In a core-collapse supernova burst of about ten seconds, typically $10^{58}$ neutrinos are emitted with an average energy of about 10 MeV.
- Only about $10\%$ of neutrinos still undergo some collisions outside the neutrino-sphere.
- The pairing mean-field contributions are proportional to the ratio $(m/E_{\nu})^2$, where $m$ and $E_{\nu}$ are the neutrino mass and energy respectively.
- The matter contribution to the mean-field Hamiltonian responsible for the MSW effect is $h^{mat} = \sqrt{2} G_F \rho_e$, where $\rho_e$ is the electron number density.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical foundation of neutrino flavor evolution in media beyond the standard mean-field equations used in studies of the MSW effect (Wolfenstein 1977, Mikheev 1986) and collective neutrino oscillations (Duan 2010, Duan 2009). It highlights that the most general mean-field equations derived by Volpe (2013) and Serreau (2014) include pairing correlations analogous to BCS theory in superconductivity, which have been neglected in prior work such as Sigl (1992) and Cardall (2007). The paper enables future work on solving quantum kinetic equations that simultaneously account for collisions and flavor evolution in the transition region of core-collapse supernovae, which is necessary to determine whether flavor instabilities can occur behind the shock and impact supernova dynamics or r-process nucleosynthesis.
