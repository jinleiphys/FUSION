# From resonantly interacting fermions with effective range to neutron matter

> A density functional theory is proposed for strongly interacting fermions with arbitrary large negative scattering length. The functional has only two parameters that are directly fixed to reproduce the universal properties of unitary gas: the so-called "Bertsch parameter" $ξ_0$ and a parameter $η_e$ related to the possible influence of the effective range $r_e$ at infinite scattering length $a$. Using most recent quantum Monte-Carlo (QMC) estimates of these two parameters, it is shown that the functional properly reproduces the experimental measurements of interacting Fermi systems not only at unitarity but also away from this limit over a wide range of $(ak_F)^{-1}$ values. The functional is applied to obtain an expression of the Tan's contact parameter including the effect of $r_e$. Application is finally made to neutron matter. It is shown that most recent QMC results are well reproduced.

# From resonantly interacting fermions with effective range to neutron matter

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new density functional theory (DFT) for strongly interacting fermions that, when constrained by the Bertsch parameter ξ₀ and an effective-range parameter ηₑ from quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations at unitarity, reproduces experimental measurements of interacting Fermi systems over a wide range of (akF)⁻¹ values and also matches QMC results for neutron matter, but only if the effective range re is explicitly included; without re effects, the agreement with neutron matter QMC results is significantly worse.

## Method

The author constructs a resummed energy functional for spin-degenerate fermions with s-wave interaction, parameterized by scattering length a and effective range re, that has the correct Lee-Yang low-density expansion and is constrained at unitarity by two parameters: the Bertsch parameter ξ₀ and the effective-range coefficient ηₑ. The functional is then applied to compute the energy per particle and the Tan contact parameter for uniform systems, and compared to experimental cold-atom data and QMC calculations for both unitary Fermi gases and neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter ξ₀ ≈ 0.37 (from QMC, range 0.37–0.44 considered); effective-range parameter ηₑ = 0.127 from fixed-node QMC (For 2011, For 2012)
- For unitary gas with ξ₀ = 0.37, the expansion coefficients around unitarity are ζ = 1.12 and ν = 1.19; for ξ₀ = 0.44, ζ = 0.89 and ν = 0.84
- Neutron matter parameters: a ≈ −18.9 fm, re ≈ 2.7 fm, giving |re/a| ≈ 0.14
- The functional reproduces QMC neutron matter results up to |akF| ≈ 5; deviations appear for |akF| > 5

## Context

This work extends earlier density functionals for unitary Fermi gases (Pap 2005, Pap 2006, Bul 2007, Bul 2012) by including finite scattering length and effective range effects, motivated by neutron matter where |re/a| is much larger than in cold atoms. It builds on effective field theory resummation techniques (Sch 2005, Kai 2011) and fixed-node QMC calculations of effective-range effects (For 2011, For 2012). The functional enables a unified description from the BCS-BEC crossover through unitarity to neutron matter, and provides predictions for the effective-range dependence of the Tan contact parameter that could be tested experimentally.
