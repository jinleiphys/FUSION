# Density and Temperature of Fermions from Quantum Fluctuations

> A novel method to determine the density and temperature of a system is proposed based on quantum fluctuations typical of Fermions in the limit where the reached temperature T is small compared to the Fermi energy $ε_f$ at a given density $ρ$. Quadrupole and particle multiplicity fluctuations relations are derived in terms of $\frac{T}{ε_f}$. This method is valid for infinite and finite fermionic systems, in particular we apply it to heavy ion collisions using the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) approach which includes the Fermi statistics. A preliminary comparison to available experimental data is discussed as well. We stress the differences with methods based on classical approximations. The derived 'quantum' temperatures are systematically lower than the corresponding 'classical' ones. With the proposed method we may get important informations on the Equation of State (EOS) of quantum Fermi systems to order O($\frac{T}{ε_f})^3$, in particular near the Liquid-Gas (LG) phase transition and at very low densities where quantum effects are dominant.

# Density and Temperature of Fermions from Quantum Fluctuations

## Key claim

The paper proposes that density and temperature of fermionic systems can be determined from quadrupole and multiplicity fluctuations using quantum statistics, but only in the limit where temperature T is small compared to the Fermi energy ε_f (to order O(T/ε_f)^3); the derived "quantum" temperatures are systematically lower than those obtained from classical approximations, contradicting many prior analyses of heavy-ion collision data that assumed classical Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the variance of transverse quadrupole momentum fluctuations (σ²_xy) and the variance of particle multiplicity fluctuations (<(ΔN)²>/N̄) for fermions using a Fermi-Dirac distribution expanded to O(T/ε_f)^4. They apply these relations to simulated ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions at fixed impact parameter b=1 fm and beam energies from 4 MeV/A to 100 MeV/A using the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) approach, which includes Fermi statistics, and extract density and temperature from the fluctuation observables for protons and neutrons separately.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.145–0.17 fm⁻³, with compressibility K = 180–240 MeV and binding energy 15–16 MeV/nucleon
- Fermi energy of nuclear matter: ε_f = 36(ρ/ρ₀)^(2/3) MeV
- Quadrupole fluctuation variance for fermions: σ²_xy = N̄ [ (16m²ε_f²)/35 (1 + (7/6)π²(T/ε_f)²) + O(T/ε_f)⁴ ]
- Multiplicity fluctuation relation: <(ΔN)²>/N̄ = (3/2)(T/ε_f) + O(T/ε_f)³
- Classical quadrupole result: σ²_xy = N̄ · 4m²T_cl²
- Back-bending in caloric curve observed at T ≈ 3 MeV for protons, indicating liquid-gas phase transition
- Rapid variation of energy density observed around T ≈ 2 MeV for neutrons and T ≈ 3 MeV for other cases
- Ratio T/ε_f is always smaller than one, confirming validity of the low-T expansion

## Context

This work extends the fluctuation-based thermometry method of Albergo (1985) and the classical quadrupole fluctuation approach of Pochodzalla (1995) by incorporating genuine quantum statistics rather than the classical Maxwell-Boltzmann approximation typically used in heavy-ion collision analyses. The method is inspired by similar fluctuation suppression observed in trapped Fermi gases by Shin (2008). The results enable extraction of the nuclear Equation of State near the liquid-gas phase transition and at very low densities where quantum effects dominate, and suggest that prior experimental temperatures derived from classical approximations (e.g., Pochodzalla 1995, Albergo 1985) may be systematically overestimated. The approach also allows comparison with Bauer's (1988) approximation relating apparent classical temperatures to input Fermi-Dirac temperatures.
