# Dynamical Screening of $α$-$α$ Resonant Scattering and Thermal Nuclear Scattering Rate in a Plasma

> We use effective field theory and thermal field theory to study the dynamical screening effect in the QED plasma on the $α$-$α$ scattering at the $^8$Be resonance. Dynamical screening leads to an imaginary part of the potential which results in a thermal width for the resonance and dominates over the previously considered static screening effect. As a result, both the resonance energy and width increase with the plasma temperature. Furthermore, dynamical screening can have a huge impact on the $α$-$α$ thermal nuclear scattering rate. For example, when the temperature is around $10$ keV, the rate is suppressed by a factor of about $900$. We expect similar thermal suppressions of nuclear reaction rates to occur in those reactions dominated by an above threshold resonance with a thermal energy. Dynamical screening effects on nuclear reactions can be relevant to cosmology and astrophysics.

## Key claim

Dynamical screening in a QED plasma, arising from collisions with medium particles that produce an imaginary part in the α-α potential, dominates over the previously considered static screening effect and causes both the resonance energy and width of the ⁸Be resonance to increase with plasma temperature, in contrast to the static-only prediction of a decreasing resonance energy.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory (EFT) and thermal field theory to compute the imaginary part of the α-α interaction potential from the thermal photon propagator in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, including a finite electron mass. They analytically continued the vacuum Coulomb Green's function to a complex energy shift containing both the real static screening term and the imaginary dynamical screening term, then fitted the resulting scattering amplitude to a Breit-Wigner form to extract the temperature-dependent resonance energy and width. The thermally averaged α-α nuclear scattering rate was computed by numerically integrating the energy-dependent cross section (including both static and dynamical screening) over a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution.

## Key numbers

* The ⁸Be resonance in vacuum has a center-of-mass energy E₀ = 91.84 ± 0.04 keV and a width Γ₀ = 5.57 ± 0.25 eV.
* At a plasma temperature of about 10 keV, the α-α thermal nuclear scattering rate is suppressed by a factor of about 900 when dynamical screening is included.
* The thermal width from dynamical screening becomes comparable to the intrinsic vacuum width Γ₀ = 5.57 eV at a temperature of approximately 160 eV.
* The effective range expansion parameters fitted to reproduce the resonance and S-wave phase shift up to ECM = 3 MeV are: scattering length a = -2.029 × 10³ fm, effective range r₀ = 1.104 fm, and shape parameter P₀ = -1.824 fm³.
* The α particle mass is M ≈ 3727.38 MeV.
* The most significant dynamical screening effect on the thermal scattering rate occurs in the temperature range 1 keV < T < 75 keV.

## Context

This work extends the static screening analysis of α-α resonant scattering by Yao, Mehen, and Müller (2016) by adding the previously neglected dynamical screening effect, which was studied for quark-gluon plasma systems by Laine (2006), Brambilla (2008), and Beraudo (2007) and applied to quarkonia dynamics by Petreczky (2010), Brambilla (2013), Blaizot (2015), and Burnier (2015). The results suggest that dynamical screening could significantly modify nuclear reaction rates dominated by above-threshold resonances in thermal plasmas, with potential implications for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis predictions of primordial ⁷Li abundance, which is currently over-predicted by a factor of about 3 (Cyburt 2003, Cyburt 2015, Fields 2011).
