# Production of Bound States with Hidden Charm at J-PARC and JLab

> The cross sections of the production of bound states with hidden charm in reactions induced by pions and photons are presented. To facilitate the experimental efforts to determine the $J/Ψ$-N interactions, we present results for $J/Ψ$ production on the deuteron.

# Production of Bound States with Hidden Charm at J-PARC and JLab

## Key claim

The cross sections for producing bound states of a J/Ψ meson with a light nucleus (³He or a six-quark cluster q⁶) are predicted to be 2–3 times larger with pion beams than with photon beams, but the absolute magnitudes depend strongly on the poorly-constrained strength of the J/Ψ–N interaction, as quantified by the s-wave scattering length a.

## Method

The authors use an impulse-approximation framework in which the production amplitude for γ/π + A → N + [B]_{J/Ψ} factorizes into a J/Ψ–nucleon elementary amplitude and a bound-state wavefunction. The π + N → J/Ψ + N amplitude is computed from a ρ-exchange mechanism using a Lagrangian with coupling constants g_{J/Ψ,ρπ} = 0.032, g_{ρNN} = 6.23, and κ_ρ = 1.825, while the γ + N → J/Ψ + N amplitude is taken from prior work (Wu and Lee, 2012). For the deuteron target, the full amplitude is the coherent sum of three mechanisms: an impulse term, an NN re-scattering term, and a J/Ψ–N re-scattering term, with the J/Ψ–N potential taken as a Yukawa form v = –α e^{-μr}/r (μ = 630 MeV) whose strength is varied to produce different scattering lengths a.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g_{J/Ψ,ρπ} = 0.032 is determined from the J/Ψ → ρ + π decay width.
- For the ³He–J/Ψ bound state, the Yukawa potential parameters are α₃ = 0.33 and μ = 257 MeV, taken from the Pomeron-quark coupling model of Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond (1990).
- Pion-induced cross sections for producing [³He]_{J/Ψ} and [q⁶]_{J/Ψ} are a factor of 2–3 larger than the corresponding photon-induced cross sections.
- For the deuteron reaction γ/π + d → J/Ψ + n + p at forward proton angle θ_p = 0, the differential cross section for a J/Ψ–N scattering length a = –8.83 fm is about a factor of 10 larger than that for a = –0.24 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ earlier predictions for photo-production of hidden-charm bound states on ⁴He and ³He targets (Wu and Lee, 2012) to pion-induced reactions, which are relevant for the J-PARC facility. The calculations rely on a J/Ψ–N potential whose strength is uncertain, with prior estimates coming from effective field theory (Peskin, 1979; Luke, Manohar, and Savage, 1992), the Pomeron-quark coupling model (Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond, 1990), and Lattice QCD (various authors). The deuteron-target predictions are designed to enable experimental extraction of the J/Ψ–N scattering length at JLab and J-PARC, with the photon case dominated by the J/Ψ–N re-scattering term and the pion case requiring careful treatment of the impulse term as well.
