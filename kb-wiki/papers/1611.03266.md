# Does the Gogny interaction need a third Gaussian?

> By considering infinite matter constraints only, we suggest in this paper that the Gogny interaction should benefit from a third Gaussian in its central part. A statistical analysis is given to select the possible ranges which are compatible with these constraints and which minimize a $χ^2$ function.

# Does the Gogny interaction need a third Gaussian?

## Key claim
The authors suggest that adding a third Gaussian to the central part of the Gogny interaction can significantly improve the reproduction of the spin-isospin decomposition of the equation of state in symmetric nuclear matter when compared to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, but they explicitly note that these results are not conclusive because finite-nuclei constraints have not been considered.

## Method
The authors performed a statistical chi-squared analysis using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock data on the spin-isospin channel decomposition of the equation of state as observables, fitting the parameters of a modified Gogny interaction that includes a third Gaussian in the central term. They froze the long-range parameter at 1.2 fm and the density dependence power at alpha=1/3, while allowing the other two ranges and all other parameters to vary freely, and then compared the resulting equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter against the BHF reference.

## Key numbers
- The long-range Gaussian was fixed at mu_1 = 1.2 fm and the density dependence power at alpha = 1/3
- An example set of ranges chosen from the chi-squared contour was mu_2 = 0.8 fm and mu_3 = 0.25 fm
- The effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation increased from 0.73 (for the D1S parametrisation) to 0.87 for the three-Gaussian interaction

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Davesne et al., 2016) showing that standard Gogny interactions cannot reproduce the spin-isospin decomposition of the equation of state even after re-fitting parameters without finite-nuclei constraints. The study is motivated by earlier work (Davesne et al., 2015, 2016) demonstrating that finite-range interactions can be expanded in partial waves and that extended Skyrme interactions with higher-order gradients can capture complex BHF results. The results suggest that adding a third Gaussian could be explored in existing Gogny codes without major modifications, though the authors note that additional tensor terms (Anguiano et al., 2011, 2012) would also improve some aspects of the interaction, and that finite-nuclei constraints must be incorporated before definitive conclusions can be drawn.
