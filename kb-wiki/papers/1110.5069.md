# Jet quenching in shock waves

> We study the propagation of an ultrarelativistic light quark jet inside a shock wave using the holographic principle. The maximum stopping distance and its dependency on the energy of the jet is obtained.

## Key claim

The maximum stopping distance for an ultrarelativistic light quark jet inside a shock wave scales as \(E^{1/3}\), the same energy dependence as in a static, thermalized medium, because both the null geodesic trajectory and the trailing string solution near the boundary are dominated by the geometry near the AdS boundary, which is identical for the shock wave and thermal AdS-Schwarzschild metrics.

## Method

The authors model an ultrarelativistic light quark jet as an open string in an asymptotically \(AdS_5\) background dual to a shock wave metric with a step function \(\theta(x^-)\). They approximate the string at late times by a trailing null string whose endpoints follow null geodesics, solve the linearized equations of motion for a perturbation \(\delta x\) to obtain a finite energy configuration, and compute the maximum stopping distance by integrating the endpoint trajectory from the boundary to the horizon.

## Key numbers

- The maximum stopping distance scales as \((\Delta x(E_*))_{max} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu}} \left( \frac{2\pi \sqrt{\mu} E_*}{\sqrt{\lambda} \mathcal{J}} \right)^{1/3}\), where \(\mu = \pi^2 \Lambda^4 \gamma_{\parallel}^2\) with \(\Lambda\) the typical particle momentum of the shock wave and \(\gamma_{\parallel}\) its Lorentz boost factor.
- In terms of the shock wave parameters, \((\Delta x(E_*))_{max} = \frac{1}{\gamma_{\parallel}^{2/3}} \left( \frac{2 E_*}{\sqrt{\lambda} \mathcal{J} \pi \Lambda^4} \right)^{1/3}\).
- The null geodesic path length near the boundary scales as \(x(z_*) \simeq 1/(\sqrt{\mu} z_*)\) for initial radial position \(z_* \simeq 0\).
- The bulk coordinate is confined to \(z^4 < 1/\mu\) for the null geodesic to be real.

## Context

This work extends the holographic jet quenching approach of Chesler et al. (2008, 2009) from static, thermalized media to the non-equilibrium shock wave geometry used to model early-stage heavy ion collisions by Gubser et al. (2008), Lin et al. (2009), Kovchegov et al. (2009), and Chesler et al. (2010). It also builds on the heavy quark energy loss in shock waves studied by Horowitz et al. (2009) and the light-ray-in-bulk method of Hatta et al. (2007, 2008) and Stoffers et al. (2011). The result enables modeling of jet quenching at early times after a heavy ion collision, before thermalization, using the same \(E^{1/3}\) scaling found in equilibrated plasmas.
