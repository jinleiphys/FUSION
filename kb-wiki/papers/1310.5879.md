# Modeling nuclear parton distribution functions

> The presence of nuclear medium and collective phenomena which involve several nucleons modify the parton distribution functions of nuclei (nPDFs) compared to those of a free nucleon. These modifications have been investigated by different groups using global analyses of high energy nuclear reaction world data resulting in modern nPDF parametrizations with error estimates, such as EPS09(s), HKN07 and nDS. These phenomenological nPDF sets roughly agree within their uncertainty bands, but have antiquarks for large-$x$ and gluons for the whole $x$-range poorly constrained by the available data. In the kinematics accessible at the LHC this has negative impact on the interpretation of the heavy-ion collision data, especially for the $p + A$ benchmarking runs. The EMC region is also sensitive to the proper definition of $x$, where the nuclear binding effects have to be taken into account, and for heavy nuclei one also needs to take into account that a fraction of the nucleus momentum is carried by the equivalent photons which modifies the momentum sum rule. We study how these effects affect the predictions for the nuclear modification ratios at the LHC kinematics using a model where we combine theoretical input for the leading twist nuclear shadowing (the FGS model) and the EKS98s/EPS09s nPDF set where the spatial dependence is formulated as a power series of the nuclear thickness functions $T_A$.

# Modeling nuclear parton distribution functions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two often-neglected nuclear effects, the proper definition of the Bjorken scaling variable x (accounting for nuclear binding energy) and the momentum carried by equivalent photons in heavy nuclei, produce measurable corrections to nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) predictions at LHC kinematics, particularly for forward-rapidity pion production in p+Pb collisions, even when the underlying nPDF parametrizations otherwise agree within their uncertainty bands.

## Method

The authors combine the leading-twist nuclear shadowing model FGS (Frankfurt, Guzey, Strikman) with the phenomenological EKS98/EPS09 nPDF set, applying a correction to shift from the free-proton Bjorken variable x_p to the proper nuclear variable x_shift using the nuclear binding energy and a rescaling of gluon distributions to account for the momentum fraction carried by equivalent photons. They then compute leading-order inclusive π^0 production cross sections in p+Pb collisions at LHC energies (√s = 5500 GeV) using QCDNUM DGLAP evolution and compare nuclear modification ratios with and without these corrections.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear binding energy: ε_A ≈ 7.88 MeV for Pb, 7.68 MeV for C
- Fraction of nucleus momentum carried by equivalent photons: η_γ(¹²C) = 0.11%, η_γ(²⁰⁸Pb) = 0.7%
- Gluon scaling factor at Q₀² = 2.5 GeV²: g_scale ≈ 0.984 for Pb, g_scale ≈ 0.997 for C
- Decrease in momentum carried by gluons after rescaling: 0.88% for Pb, 0.14% for C
- Gluon momentum fraction in CTEQ5L proton: 41.80%
- Gluon momentum fraction for Pb using x_shift: 43.58% (EKS98), 43.81% (FGS1+EKS98), 43.91% (FGS2+EKS98)
- Change in scaling factor with scale: ~0.4% between Q² = 2.5 GeV² and Q² = 10000² GeV²

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological nPDF parametrizations EPS09 (Eskola 2009), HKN07 (Hirai 2007), and nDS by incorporating theoretical input from the FGS leading-twist shadowing model (Frankfurt 2011) and correcting for nuclear binding effects and the equivalent photon field, which are neglected in standard global analyses. The results show that forward-rapidity π⁰ production at the LHC is particularly sensitive to the proper x-definition, enabling future impact-parameter-dependent nPDF sets (to be released in a forthcoming paper) that can be tested against LHC p+Pb benchmark data and centrality-dependent measurements.
