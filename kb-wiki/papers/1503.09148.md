# Light Quarks in the Screened Dyon-Anti-Dyon Coulomb Liquid Model II

> We discuss an extension of the dyon-anti-dyon liquid model that includes light quarks in the dense center symmetric Coulomb phase. In this work, like in our previous one, we use the simplest color SU(2) group. We start with a single fermion flavor $N_f=1$ and explicitly map the theory onto a 3-dimensional quantum effective theory with a fermion that is only U$_V(1)$ symmetric. We use it to show that the dense center symmetric plasma develops, in the mean field approximation, a nonzero chiral condensate, although the ensuing Goldstone mode is massive due to the U$_A(1)$ axial-anomaly. We estimate the chiral condensate and $σ,η$ meson masses for $N_f=1$. We then extend our analysis to several flavors $N_f>1$ and colors $N_c>2$ and show that center symmetry and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking disappear simultaneously when $x=N_f/N_c\geq 2$ in the dense plasma phase. A reorganization of the dense plasma phase into a gas of dyon-antidyon molecules restores chiral symmetry, but may preserve center symmetry in the linearized approximation. We estimate the corresponding critical temperature.

## Key claim

The dense center-symmetric dyon-anti-dyon Coulomb plasma develops a nonzero chiral condensate in the mean field approximation, but the resulting Goldstone mode is massive due to the U$_A(1)$ axial anomaly; center symmetry and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking disappear simultaneously when the flavor-to-color ratio $x=N_f/N_c \geq 2$ in the dense plasma phase.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier dyon-anti-dyon liquid model by including light quarks in the dense center-symmetric Coulomb phase for SU(2) color. They map the theory onto a 3-dimensional quantum effective theory with a fermion that is only U$_V(1)$ symmetric, using fermionization and bosonization techniques to handle the fermionic determinant from zero mode hopping between L-dyons and anti-L-dyons. The effective potential is then analyzed in the mean field approximation for constant fields, and the analysis is extended to multiple flavors and colors.

## Key numbers

* The dyon density is given by $n_D/T^3 = C \, e^{-\pi/\alpha_s} / \alpha_s^2$, with $\pi/\alpha_s = (10/3) \ln(T / 0.36 T_c)$ for $N_c=2$ and $N_f=1$.
* The model is estimated to be applicable in the range $0.5\,T_c < T < T_c$.
* The critical flavor-to-color ratio for simultaneous loss of center symmetry and chiral symmetry breaking is $x = N_f/N_c \geq 2$.
* The classical Coulomb interaction strength between dyons and anti-dyons is $C_D/\alpha_s = 2.46/\alpha_s$, with a core size $a_{D\bar D} \approx 1/T$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Liu, Shuryak, and Zahed, 2015) of gauge topology in the confining phase of SU(2) gauge theory, which modeled the confining phase as a dense instanton-dyon plasma. It builds on the KvBLL instanton discovery (Kraan and van Baal, 1998) and the dyon-anti-dyon liquid model proposed by Diakonov and Petrov (2000), as well as the streamline solutions derived by Larsen and Shuryak (2014). The results enable future study of the interplay between chiral symmetry breaking and center symmetry in dense topological ensembles, and suggest a simultaneous restoration transition when the flavor-to-color ratio exceeds 2.
