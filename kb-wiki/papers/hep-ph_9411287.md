# Neutral pion photoproduction off nucleons revisited

> We investigate threshold pion photoproduction in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We give the expansion of the electric dipole amplitude $E_{0+}$ to three orders in $μ$, the ratio of the pion to nucleon mass, and show that it is slowly converging. We argue that this observable is not a good testing ground for the chiral dynamics of QCD. In contrast, we exhibit new and fastly converging low--energy theorems in the P--waves which should be used to constrain the data analysis. We also discuss the importance of polarization observables to accurately pin down certain multipoles and give predictions for the reaction $γn \to π^0 n$.

## Key claim

The chiral expansion of the electric dipole amplitude \(E_{0+}\) for neutral pion photoproduction converges very slowly, making it a poor test of QCD chiral dynamics; instead, the P-wave multipoles obey new, fastly converging low-energy theorems that should be used to constrain data analysis.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to compute the S-wave electric dipole amplitude \(E_{0+}\) to order \(q^4\) (three orders in \(\mu = M_\pi/m\)) and the P-wave combinations \(P_1, P_2, P_3\) to order \(q^3\). They included one-loop diagrams, tree-level contributions from the chiral Lagrangian up to \({\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(4)}\), and estimated the low-energy constants via resonance saturation from vector mesons (\(\rho, \omega\)) and the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance. Isospin-breaking effects from the pion mass difference were incorporated in the loops.

## Key numbers

*   For \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\), the threshold value of the electric dipole amplitude is \(E_{0+, \text{thr}}^p = -1.0 \cdot 10^{-3} / M_{\pi^+}\) (using the best-fit sum of low-energy constants \(a_1^p(m) + a_2^p(m) = 2.7\) GeV\(^{-4}\)), with the \(\mu\)-expansion showing terms \(1 - 1.26 + 0.55 + \dots\).
*   For \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\), the threshold electric dipole amplitude is \(E_{0+, \text{thr}}^n = 2.59 \cdot 10^{-3} / M_{\pi^+}\) (using the same low-energy constants), with a better-converging expansion \(1 - 0.29 + \dots\).
*   The slope of the P-wave \(P_1\) for the proton at threshold is \(|\vec q|^{-1} P_{1,\text{thr}}^p = 0.480\) GeV\(^{-2}\), with the leading term \(0.512\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and a first correction of \(-0.062\).
*   The slope of the P-wave \(P_2\) for the proton at threshold is \(|\vec q|^{-1} P_{2,\text{thr}}^p = -0.512\) GeV\(^{-2}\), with a negligible first correction of \(-0.0008\).
*   The vector meson contribution to the low-energy constant \(b_P\) for the proton is \(b_P^V = 3.13\) GeV\(^{-3}\); the static \(\Delta\) contribution is \(b_P^{\Delta, \text{static}} = 12.3\) GeV\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1993) by using the heavy baryon formulation to achieve a strict power counting and to compute the complete order \(q^4\) expression for \(E_{0+}\). It contradicts the long-held belief that the electric dipole amplitude is a clean test of chiral dynamics, showing instead that the P-waves provide rapidly converging low-energy theorems. The results enable improved data analysis for \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\) experiments at MAMI and SAL, and provide predictions for the experimentally difficult reaction \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\) to test isospin decomposition.
