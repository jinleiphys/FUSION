# Two-body dissipation effects on synthesis of superheavy elements

> To investigate the two-body dissipation effects on the synthesis of superheavy elements, we calculate low-energy collisions of the $N=50$ isotones ($^{82}$Ge, $^{84}$Se, $^{86}$Kr and $^{88}$Sr) on $^{208}$Pb using the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM). TDDM is an extension of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory and can determine the time evolution of one-body and two-body density matrices. Thus TDDM describes both one-body and two-body dissipation of collective energies. It is shown that the two-body dissipation may increase fusion cross sections and enhance the synthesis of superheavy elements.

## Key claim
Two-body dissipation, modeled via the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), can expand the fusion energy window and lower the extra-push energy for certain projectile-target combinations, thereby potentially enhancing the synthesis of superheavy elements; however, the results are exploratory rather than quantitative, and the effect is not universal (e.g., no fusion was seen for the \({}^{88}\)Sr + \({}^{208}\)Pb system in TDDM with the standard residual interaction).

## Method
The authors performed head-on collisions of the \(N=50\) isotones (\({}^{82}\)Ge, \({}^{84}\)Se, \({}^{86}\)Kr, and \({}^{88}\)Sr) on a \({}^{208}\)Pb target using TDDM, which extends time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) by coupling the one-body and two-body density matrices. The calculations used the Skyrme III force for the mean field and a contact residual interaction (\(v_0 = -500\) MeV fm\(^3\)) to compute two-body correlations, with a limited set of about 20 bound single-particle states near the Fermi level. Fusion was defined as the system remaining stuck together beyond \(T_f = 4000\) fm/c.

## Key numbers
- For \({}^{82}\)Ge + \({}^{208}\)Pb (total charge \(Z=114\)): TDHF fusion occurs in two energy windows (300–380 MeV and 470–620 MeV), while TDDM widens the fusion range to 285–650 MeV; the lowest TDDM fusion energy is about 28 MeV above the Coulomb barrier (estimated at 257.5 MeV for a similar system).
- For \({}^{84}\)Se + \({}^{208}\)Pb (\(Z=116\)): TDHF fuses only at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 299 \pm 1\) MeV, whereas TDDM fuses from 300 to 400 MeV; the fusion threshold is about 30 MeV above the Coulomb barrier.
- For \({}^{86}\)Kr + \({}^{208}\)Pb (\(Z=118\)): TDHF does not fuse at any energy (contact time up to 2300 fm/c at 650 MeV), but TDDM fuses in a narrow window at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 341 \pm 1\) MeV; the extra push is about 54 MeV above the Coulomb barrier, roughly half the prediction of the Swiatecki macroscopic model for the effective fissility.
- For \({}^{88}\)Sr + \({}^{208}\)Pb (\(Z=120\)): No fusion occurs in either TDHF or TDDM with the standard residual interaction; a stronger interaction (\(v_0 = -1000\) MeV fm\(^3\)) did induce fusion at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 340\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends TDHF studies of heavy-ion fusion (e.g., Guo and Nakatsukasa (2012) for \({}^{70}\)Zn + \({}^{208}\)Pb) by incorporating two-body dissipation via TDDM, which is missing in standard mean-field approaches. The results suggest that two-body dissipation can lower the extra-push energy and widen fusion windows for systems with total charge up to \(Z=118\), but the method is limited by the small single-particle space and the simplified contact residual interaction. Future work requires larger single-particle bases and improved residual interactions to obtain quantitative predictions for superheavy element synthesis, particularly for systems like \({}^{88}\)Sr + \({}^{208}\)Pb.
