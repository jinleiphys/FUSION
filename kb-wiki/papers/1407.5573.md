# Disentangling phase transitions and critical points in the proton-neutron interacting boson model by catastrophe theory

> We introduce the basic concepts of catastrophe theory needed to derive analytically the phase diagram of the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2). Previous studies [1,2,3] were based on numerical solutions. We here explain the whole IBM-2 phase diagram including the precise order of the phase transitions in terms of the cusp catastrophe.

# Disentangling phase transitions and critical points in the proton-neutron interacting boson model by catastrophe theory

## Key claim

The authors claim that catastrophe theory can determine analytically the order of all phase transitions in the IBM-2 phase diagram, resolving ambiguities from prior numerical studies. The key qualifier is that the analysis is restricted to a simplified Consistent-Q Hamiltonian and focuses only on the critical surfaces previously identified numerically, not the full parameter space.

## Method

The authors apply catastrophe theory to the semiclassical (mean-field) energy surface of the IBM-2 Consistent-Q Hamiltonian, using a product of proton and neutron boson condensates as the intrinsic state. They identify the fundamental degenerate critical point (non-Morse point) for each critical surface, perform Taylor expansions around that point, and transform the energy surface into canonical catastrophe forms using nonlinear changes of variables. The order of each phase transition is then determined by whether the energy surface lies near a Maxwell set (first-order) or a bifurcation set (second-order), with the cusp catastrophe (germ z^4, perturbation a z + b z^2) identified as the relevant elementary catastrophe for the entire IBM-2 phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The critical control parameter for the spherical-axially deformed transition is given by ξ_c = (28 + 2χ²)/(35 + 2χ²), yielding ξ_c = 4/5 for χ = 0 and ξ_c = 9/11 for χ = ±√7/2.
- The Hessian matrix eigenvalues for the spherical-axially deformed surface are 5ξ − 4 and ξ, with the essential variable β₁ having eigenvalue vanishing at ξ = 4/5.
- For the e−x* line, the Hessian eigenvalue associated with β is 5ξ − 4, vanishing at ξ = 4/5, with all γ derivatives vanishing at the fundamental root.
- The expression for ξ_c is independent of χ′ = (χ_π − χ_ν)/2, meaning the first-order critical surface separating spherical and axially deformed shapes propagates vertically in the IBM-2 parameter space.

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of the IBM-2 phase diagram by Garcia-Ramos (2004), Caprio (2005), and others, which could not unambiguously classify the order of phase transitions due to finite-size limitations (systems with at most 10 proton and 10 neutron bosons). The analytic catastrophe theory approach confirms the numerical classifications and provides rigorous proof. The method parallels earlier catastrophe theory analyses of IBM-1 by Lopez-Moreno (1996) and of IBM with configuration mixing by Hellemans (2007, 2009). This work enables unambiguous classification of quantum phase transitions in two-fluid boson models and provides a template for applying catastrophe theory to other nuclear structure models with multiple order parameters.
