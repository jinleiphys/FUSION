# Pion-only, chiral light-front model of the deuteron

> We investigate the symptoms of broken rotational invariance, caused by the use of light front dynamics, for deuterons obtained using one- and two-pion-exchange potentials. A large mass splitting between states with m=0 and m=1 is found for the deuteron obtained from the one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential. The size of the splitting is smaller when the chiral two-pion-exchange (TPE) potential is used. When the TPE potential constructed without chiral symmetry is used, the deuteron becomes unbound. These results arise from significant relativistic effects which are much larger than those of the Wick-Cutkosky model because of the presence of the tensor force.

## Key claim

The light-front deuteron exhibits a large mass splitting between magnetic substates (m=0 and m=1) when using a one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential, and this splitting is reduced but not eliminated when a chirally symmetric two-pion-exchange (TPE) potential is added; the tensor force from pseudoscalar coupling makes these relativistic effects much larger than those seen in the scalar Wick-Cutkosky model.

## Method

The authors constructed a light-front nucleon-nucleon potential from a chiral Lagrangian containing only nucleons and pions, then computed deuteron binding energies and wave functions for the OPE potential alone, the OPE plus chirally symmetric TPE potential, and the OPE plus non-chiral TPE (ncTPE) potential. They used three different pion-nucleon form factors (n=1 with Λ=1.9 GeV, n=1 with Λ=2.1 GeV, and n=2 with Λ=2.9 GeV) to verify robustness, and solved the light-front bound-state equations for states with m=0 and m=1.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy for OPE potential with Λ=1.9 GeV: m=0 state at 2.29 MeV, m=1 state at 1.43 MeV (mass splitting of 0.86 MeV)
- Deuteron binding energy for OPE+TPE potential with Λ=1.9 GeV: m=0 state at 2.37 MeV, m=1 state at 2.08 MeV (mass splitting of 0.29 MeV)
- OPE+ncTPE potential with Λ=1.9 GeV: deuteron becomes unbound (binding energy zero or very small)
- D-state probability for OPE+TPE potential with Λ=1.9 GeV: approximately 6% (consistent with the non-relativistic Friar-Gibson-Payne model)
- Nucleon mass M = 938.958 MeV, pion mass m_π = 138.03 MeV, pion decay constant f_0^2 = 0.079 (corresponding to g_π^2/4π = 14.6228)

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic OPE deuteron model of Friar, Gibson, and Payne (1984) by incorporating full light-front dynamics and adding two-pion-exchange contributions with chiral symmetry. The results show that chiral symmetry is essential for obtaining a bound deuteron when TPE potentials are included, and that the tensor force from pseudoscalar coupling produces much larger relativistic effects than the scalar Wick-Cutkosky model studied earlier by Cooke and Miller (2000). The findings enable future light-front calculations of few-nucleon systems using chiral effective field theory potentials.
