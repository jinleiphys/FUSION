# Mass of the H-dibaryon

> Recent lattice QCD calculations have reported evidence for the existence of a bound state with strangeness -2 and baryon number 2 at quark masses somewhat higher than the physical values. By developing a description of the dependence of this binding energy on the up, down and strange quark masses that allows a controlled chiral extrapolation, we explore the hypothesis that this state is to be identified with the $H$-dibaryon. Taking as input the recent results of the HAL and NPLQCD Collaborations, we show that the $H$-dibaryon is likely to be unbound by $13 \pm 14$ MeV at the physical point.

## Key claim

The H-dibaryon is likely to be unbound by 13 ± 14 MeV at the physical quark masses, even though lattice QCD simulations at larger quark masses show it as bound; the extrapolation to physical masses using chiral perturbation theory with finite range regularization produces this result.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous chiral extrapolation of octet baryon masses (from PACS-CS Collaboration lattice data) and H-dibaryon binding energies (from HAL and NPLQCD Collaborations) using finite range regularization (FRR) with a dipole regulator. The fit used an SU(3) chiral expansion for the octet masses to determine the quark-mass insertion parameters α, β, and σ, and then applied these to describe the H-dibaryon mass as an SU(3) singlet with its own chiral loop corrections, minimizing χ² for both datasets simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- H-dibaryon is unbound by 13 ± 14 MeV at the physical point (mass above Λ-Λ threshold)
- Best-fit regulator mass Λ = 1.02 ± 0.06 GeV
- Best-fit H-dibaryon chiral coefficient C_H = 5.65 ± 0.09 GeV⁻²
- Octet baryon masses at the physical point from the fit: N = 0.959 ± 0.023 GeV, Λ = 1.129 ± 0.014 GeV, Σ = 1.188 ± 0.011 GeV, Ξ = 1.325 ± 0.006 GeV (compared to physical values 0.939, 1.116, 1.193, 1.318 GeV)
- χ² per degree of freedom for octet fit: 0.49 (7.3 divided by 15)
- HAL Collaboration data points used at pseudoscalar masses 1.015, 0.837, and 0.673 GeV on a 3.87 fm lattice
- NPLQCD data point at m_π = 389 MeV and m_K = 553 MeV (after infinite volume extrapolation)
- Octet-decuplet mass splitting δ = 0.292 GeV used for H-dibaryon loop corrections

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of the H-dibaryon by the HAL Collaboration (Inoue 2010) and NPLQCD Collaboration (Beane 2010), which found binding at heavier quark masses, and addresses the earlier suggestion by Jaffe (1976) that the H-dibaryon should be deeply bound. The chiral extrapolation using FRR, previously applied to baryon masses by Young (2009) and Leinweber (2003), enables a controlled extrapolation to physical quark masses that respects chiral symmetry, in contrast to simple linear fits in m_π or m_π². The result that the H-dibaryon is near threshold (unbound by 13 ± 14 MeV) motivates further studies of doubly strange hypernuclei and dense matter equations of state.
