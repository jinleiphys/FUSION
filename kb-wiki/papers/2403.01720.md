# Eigenphase shift decomposition of the RPA strength function based on the Jost-RPA method

> The S-matrix which satisfies the unitarity, giving the poles as RPA excited states, is derived using the extended Jost function within the framework of the RPA theory. An analysis on the correspondence between the component decomposition of the RPA strength function by the eigenphase shift obtained by diagonalisation of the S-matrix and the S- and K-matrix poles was performed in the calculation of the $^{16}$O quadrupole excitations. The results show the possibility that the states defined by the eigenphase shift can be expressed as RPA-excited eigenstates corresponding to the S-matrix poles in the continuum region.

## Key claim

The paper derives a unitary S-matrix within the Jost-RPA framework and shows that the eigenphase shifts obtained by diagonalizing this S-matrix can be used to decompose the RPA strength function into components that correspond to RPA-excited eigenstates, but only under the condition that the eigenphase shifts are redefined to correspond to the RPA eigenstates rather than to the free-particle scattering boundary conditions.

## Method

The authors extended the Jost function method within the Random-Phase-Approximation (RPA) theory to derive an S-matrix that satisfies unitarity, using a flux-adjusted definition involving momentum-dependent factors. They then diagonalized this S-matrix to obtain eigenphase shifts and used these eigenphase shifts to decompose the RPA strength function into components. The method was applied to calculate quadrupole excitations in \(^{16}\)O, with the particle-hole excitation configuration consisting of 8 neutron and 8 proton configurations (N=16 total).

## Key numbers

* The particle-hole excitation configuration for \(^{16}\)O quadrupole excitations has \(N_n = 8\) and \(N_p = 8\) for neutrons and protons respectively, giving \(N = N_n + N_p = 16\) total configurations.
* The S-matrix is defined as \(\bvec{\mathcal{S}}(\bvec{\mathcal{K}}) \equiv \bvec{\mathcal{K}}^{\frac{1}{2}} \bvec{\mathcal{J}}^{(+)-1} \bvec{\mathcal{J}}^{(-)} \bvec{\mathcal{K}}^{-\frac{1}{2}}\), where \(\bvec{\mathcal{K}}\) is a diagonal matrix with momentum components \(k_{1,\alpha}=\sqrt{\frac{2m}{\hbar^2}(e_\alpha+E)}\) and \(k_{2,\alpha}=\sqrt{\frac{2m}{\hbar^2}(e_\alpha-E)}\).
* The condition for S-matrix poles in the positive energy region is \(\det \left(\bvec{\mathcal{J}}^{(+)}_{11} - \bvec{\mathcal{J}}^{(+)}_{12} \bvec{\mathcal{J}}^{(+)-1}_{22} \bvec{\mathcal{J}}^{(+)}_{21}\right) = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the Jost function method previously developed within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework by Mizuyama et al. (2015) and within the RPA framework (Jost-RPA method) by the same group. It addresses the problem that the S-matrix defined directly from the Jost function does not satisfy unitarity, which was a limitation in the earlier Jost-RPA formulation. The method enables classification of resonances in nuclear collective excitations by decomposing strength functions into eigenphase shift components, and can be applied to analyze the correspondence between S-matrix poles and RPA excited states in the continuum region.
