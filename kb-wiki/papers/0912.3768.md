# Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass term

> The Bohr Hamiltonian describing the collective motion of atomic nuclei is modified by allowing the mass to depend on the nuclear deformation. Exact analytical expressions are derived for spectra and wave functions in the case of a gamma-unstable Davidson potential, using techniques of supersymmetric quantum mechanics. Numerical results in the Xe-Ba region are discussed.

# Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass term

## Key claim

The Bohr Hamiltonian for collective nuclear motion can be modified by allowing the mass parameter to depend on the deformation coordinate β as B(β) = B₀/(1 + aβ²)², and exact analytical solutions for spectra and wave functions can be obtained for γ-unstable Davidson potentials using supersymmetric quantum mechanics, but only for the specific choice of deformation function f(β) = 1 + aβ² and for potentials independent of γ.

## Method

The authors start from the von Roos two-parameter form of the kinetic energy operator for position-dependent masses and apply it to the Bohr Hamiltonian in five-dimensional Bohr-Wheeler coordinates. They assume a γ-unstable Davidson potential u(β) = β² + β₀⁴/β² and a deformation-dependent mass B(β) = B₀/(1 + aβ²)², then separate variables in β and γ. The β-equation is solved by factorizing the Hamiltonian using SUSYQM techniques, imposing shape invariance conditions to obtain exact energy eigenvalues and wave functions for the ground state band (ν=0) and quasi-β₁ band (ν=1).

## Key numbers

- The mass parameter takes the form B(β) = B₀/(1 + aβ²)², where B₀ is a constant and a is a free parameter controlling the deformation dependence.
- The Davidson potential is u(β) = β² + β₀⁴/β², where β₀ indicates the position of the minimum (β₀ = 0 corresponds to the simple harmonic oscillator).
- The eigenvalue equation for the γ-part yields Λ = τ(τ+3), where τ is the seniority quantum number characterizing irreducible representations of SO(5).
- The energy formula for excited bands is ϵ_ν = ϵ₀ + 2ν(c₀ - a\bar{c}₀) + 2aν², where ν=0 gives the ground state band and ν=1 gives the quasi-β₁ band.
- The quality measure for fits is σ = √[Σᵢ(Eᵢ(exp)-Eᵢ(th))² / ((n-1)E(2₁⁺)²)].
- For ¹³⁴Xe and ¹³²Xe, the fitting leads to a=0 and β₀=0 (pure harmonic behaviour).
- For ¹³⁰Xe and ¹²⁸Xe, nonzero β₀ values are needed but a=0 still suffices.
- Beyond ¹²⁶Xe, both nonzero β₀ and nonzero a become necessary.

## Context

This work extends the standard Bohr Hamiltonian (Bohr, 1952) by addressing the long-standing puzzle that moments of inertia increase more slowly with β² than the constant-mass prediction (Ring, 1980). It is motivated by the geometrical limit of the Interacting Boson Model (Iachello and Arima, 1987), where terms of the form β²π² appear in the O(6) limiting symmetry and U(5)-O(6) transition region (van Roosmalen, 1982). The approach builds on prior work on position-dependent effective masses (Quesne and Tkachuk, 2004) and supersymmetric quantum mechanics (Cooper, Khare, and Sukhatme, 1995). The numerical application to Xe-Ba isotopes (Casten, 1985) provides a first test, and the authors indicate that extension to axially symmetric well-deformed nuclei is in progress.
