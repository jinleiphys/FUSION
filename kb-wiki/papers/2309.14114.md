# Hybrid Strangeon Stars

> It was conjectured that the basic units of the ground state of bulk strong matter may be strange-clusters called strangeons, and they can form self-bound strangeon stars that are highly compact. Strangeon stars can develop a strange quark matter (SQM) core at high densities, particularly in the color-flavor-locking phase, yielding a branch of hybrid strangeon stars. We explore the stellar structure and astrophysical implications of hybrid strangeon stars. We find that hybrid strangeon stars can meet various astrophysical constraints on pulsar masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities. Finally, we show that the strangeon-SQM mixed phase is not preferred if the charge-neutrality condition is imposed at the strangeon-SQM transition region.

# Hybrid Strangeon Stars

## Key claim

Hybrid strangeon stars, compact objects with a strangeon matter crust and a color-flavor-locked (CFL) strange quark matter core, can satisfy current astrophysical constraints on pulsar masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities, but only if the CFL phase is sufficiently stiff to maintain radial stability; the strangeon-SQM mixed phase is not preferred when charge neutrality is imposed at the transition region.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for strangeon matter using a Lennard-Jones potential between strangeons and for CFL quark matter using a unified thermodynamic potential with pQCD corrections and color-superconductivity gap parameter Delta. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with Maxwell construction (sharp interface) at the chemical-potential crossing point to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, then compare against NICER and GW170817 constraints. They also examine Gibbs construction for a mixed phase by requiring equal pressures in both phases as functions of baryon and electron chemical potentials under global charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- Strangeon surface baryon number density: n_s = 0.22 or 0.30 fm^{-3} (two benchmark values)
- Strangeon interaction parameter: epsilon/N_q = 80/9 MeV (approx 8.9 MeV) or 120/9 MeV (approx 13.3 MeV)
- CFL bag constant B = 60 or 80 MeV/fm^3; gap parameter Delta sampled at 60, 80, 100, 120 MeV
- At maximum mass, strangeon crust widths are 1-5 km with masses 0.1-1.0 solar masses
- At M = 2.0 solar masses for B = 80 MeV/fm^3: CFL core mass = 1.13 solar masses (Delta = 100 MeV) or 1.86 solar masses (Delta = 120 MeV)
- At M = 2.0 solar masses for B = 60 MeV/fm^3: CFL core mass = 0.27 solar masses (Delta = 60 MeV) or 1.50 solar masses (Delta = 80 MeV)
- Transition mass density ranges from approximately nuclear saturation density (157 MeV/fm^3) to 1.5 times saturation density
- Mass density jumps at transition vary from 100 to 370 MeV/fm^3
- GW170817 constraint Lambda(1.4 solar masses) less than or equal to 800 is met by selected benchmark examples
- Mixed phase exists only in a tiny segment with mu_B variation smaller than 1 MeV and mu_e lifted to 6-8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the strangeon star model of Xu (2003) and Lai (2009, 2017) by introducing a CFL quark matter core, analogous to conventional hybrid neutron stars studied by Alford (2004, 2013) but with a solid strangeon crust instead of a hadronic one. The hybrid configuration can naturally address the pulsar glitch crisis (Andersson 2012, Chamel 2012) through starquakes in the thick strangeon crust, and the large density discontinuity at the SM-CFL interface may produce distinct g-mode gravitational waves (Flores 2013, Ranea-Sandoval 2018) and crust-core interfacial modes (Lau 2020, Zhu 2022) that could differentiate these objects from conventional hybrid stars or inverted hybrid stars.
