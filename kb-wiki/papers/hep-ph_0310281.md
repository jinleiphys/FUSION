# Spectroscopy of pentaquark states

> We construct a complete classification of pentaquark states in terms of the spin-flavour SU(6) representations. We find that only some definite SU(3) representations are allowed, singlets, octets, decuplets, anti-decuplets, 27-plets and 35-plets. The latter three contain exotic states, which cannot be constructed from three quarks only. This complete classification scheme is general and model independent and is useful both for model builders and experimentalists. The mass spectrum is obtained from a Gursey-Radicati type mass formula, whose coefficients have been determined previously by a study of qqq baryons. The ground state pentaquark which is identified with the recently observed Theta(1540) state, is predicted to be an isosinglet anti-decuplet state. Its parity depends on the interplay between the spin-flavour and orbital contributions to the mass operator.

# Spectroscopy of pentaquark states

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete, model-independent classification of pentaquark (qqqq\bar{q}) states in terms of spin-flavour SU(6) representations, but the parity of the ground state pentaquark (identified with the Theta(1540)) depends on the interplay between spin-flavour and orbital contributions to the mass operator and cannot be determined from symmetry alone.

## Method

The authors construct the allowed SU_sf(6) representations for the q^4\bar{q} system using Young tableau techniques, then decompose these into SU_f(3) flavour and SU_s(2) spin states. They apply a Gursey-Radicati type mass formula with coefficients previously determined from the qqq baryon spectrum to calculate energy splittings of exotic pentaquark states, and discuss the symmetry properties of the orbital wave function for excitations up to N=1 quantum.

## Key numbers

- The coefficients of the Gursey-Radicati mass formula, determined from qqq baryons, are: B = 21.2 MeV, C = 38.3 MeV, D = -197.3 MeV, E = 38.5 MeV.
- The coefficient A (from the SU_sf(6) Casimir term) is determined as A = 55.1 MeV from the energy difference between N(1535) and N(1440).
- For all spin-flavour configurations, the lowest pentaquark state is a ^2\overline{10} anti-decuplet with spin s=1/2 and isospin I=0, identified with the Theta(1540).
- The anti-decuplet state with strangeness S=-2 and isospin I=3/2 is calculated at 2305 MeV, compared to a candidate observed at 1862 MeV.
- Excited pentaquark states belonging to the 27-plet are predicted at 1660 MeV and 1775 MeV.
- The threshold for determining ground state parity is Delta_1 > 4A = 220 MeV (negative parity) versus Delta_1 < 220 MeV (positive parity).

## Context

This work extends the classification of multiquark states using SU(6) spin-flavour symmetry, building on the Gursey-Radicati mass formula approach previously applied to qqq baryons. The paper provides a general basis that can be used by constituent quark models (such as those by Stancu, Helminen, Glozman, and others), diquark-diquark-antiquark approaches, and lattice QCD studies. The results enable systematic searches for exotic pentaquark states beyond the Theta(1540), particularly in the flavour anti-decuplet, 27-plet, and 35-plet representations, and highlight that the parity determination of the Theta(1540) will distinguish between different theoretical approaches including the Skyrme model of Diakonov et al., QCD sum rules, and lattice QCD calculations.
