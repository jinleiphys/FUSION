# Interference Effect Between Neutron Direct and Resonance Capture Reactions For Neutron-Rich Nuclei

> Interference effect of neutron capture cross section between the compound and direct processes is investigated. The compound process is calculated by resonance parameters and the direct process by the potential mode. The interference effect is tested for neutron-rich $^{82}$Ge and $^{134}$Sn nuclei relevant to $r$-process and light nucleus $^{13}$C which is neutron poison in the $s$-process and produces long-lived radioactive nucleus $^{14}$C ($T_{1/2}=5700$ y). The interference effects in those nuclei are significant around resonances, and low energy region if $s$-wave neutron direct capture is possible. Maxwellian averaged cross sections at $kT=30$ and $300$ keV are also calculated, and the interference effect changes the Maxwellian averaged capture cross section largely depending on resonance position.

## Key claim

The interference effect between compound (resonance) and direct neutron capture processes can significantly alter the total neutron capture cross section for neutron-rich nuclei such as \(^{82}\)Ge and \(^{134}\)Sn, but the magnitude and sign of the effect depend on the resonance position and the incident neutron energy, with the phase of the interference term chosen to be constructive below a resonance and destructive above it.

## Method

The compound process was calculated using resonance parameters from TENDL-2015 for \(^{82}\)Ge and \(^{134}\)Sn and from JENDL for \(^{13}\)C, while the direct capture process was calculated using a potential model with Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions and the Kunieda optical potential for the scattering wave function. The total cross section was computed via the \(R\)-matrix formalism including an interference term between the Breit-Wigner resonance amplitudes and the direct capture amplitude, with the phase of the interference term fixed by comparison to known behavior in \(^{16}\)O. Maxwellian averaged cross sections were then evaluated at \(kT = 30\) and \(300\) keV.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{13}\)C, the thermal neutron capture cross section used to calibrate the Woods-Saxon potential was \(\sigma_\gamma(th) = 1.496\) b (from Firestone).
- For \(^{134}\)Sn, the first resonance appears at \(E = 102.1\) keV, where the interference effect gives a significant change.
- For \(^{13}\)C, a resonance is located at \(E = 152.4\) keV with \(J = 2\).
- Maxwellian averaged cross section at \(kT = 30\) keV for \(^{134}\)Sn: Com+Dir+Int = 0.3511 mb, Com+Dir = 0.2690 mb, a variation of +30%.
- Maxwellian averaged cross section at \(kT = 30\) keV for \(^{82}\)Ge: Com+Dir+Int = 0.3574 mb, Com+Dir = 0.3412 mb, a variation of +4.7%.
- Maxwellian averaged cross section at \(kT = 30\) keV for \(^{13}\)C: Com+Dir+Int = 0.0700 mb, Com+Dir = 0.0692 mb, a variation of +1.1%.
- Maxwellian averaged cross section at \(kT = 300\) keV for \(^{13}\)C: Com+Dir+Int = 0.0776 mb, Com+Dir = 0.0780 mb, a variation of -0.5%.
- Maxwellian averaged cross section at \(kT = 300\) keV for \(^{134}\)Sn: Com+Dir+Int = 0.2566 mb, Com+Dir = 0.2591 mb, a variation of -1.0%.
- Maxwellian averaged cross section at \(kT = 300\) keV for \(^{82}\)Ge: Com+Dir+Int = 0.6367 mb, Com+Dir = 0.6115 mb, a variation of +4.1%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the interference effect in neutron capture, which had been demonstrated for \(^{16}\)O by Mengoni and Otsuka (2000), to neutron-rich nuclei relevant to the \(r\)-process (\(^{82}\)Ge and \(^{134}\)Sn) and to the \(s\)-process neutron poison \(^{13}\)C. The results suggest that the interference effect can change Maxwellian averaged cross sections by up to 30% at astrophysically relevant temperatures, which may impact nucleosynthesis calculations; however, the authors note that the use of artificial resonance parameters, the phase ambiguity, and the need for experimental data on partial \(\gamma\)-decay widths remain open problems requiring further measurement.
