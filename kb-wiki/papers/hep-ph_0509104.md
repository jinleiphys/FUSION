# Phenomenological study of two-meson couplings of Theta+

> We evaluate two-meson couplings of Theta+, using experimental information of nucleon resonances decaying into pi pi N channels, in which the two pions are in scalar- and vector-type correlations. We examine two assignments of spin and parity of J^P=1/2^+ and 3/2^-, for which the experimental spectra of known resonances with exotic baryons are properly reproduced by an octet-antidecuplet representation mixing scheme. With the obtained coupling constants, total cross sections of the reactions pi- p -> K- Theta+ and K+ p -> pi+ Theta+ are calculated. Substantial interference of two terms may occur in the reaction processes for the J^P=1/2^+ case, whereas the interference effect is rather small for the 3/2^- case.

## Key claim

The two-meson couplings of the Theta+ are evaluated using experimental nucleon resonance decay data and SU(3) flavor symmetry, but the extracted coupling constants depend strongly on the assumed spin-parity assignment and on the relative phase between the two nucleon resonance contributions, with the self-energy constraint limiting the allowed parameter space.

## Method

The authors extracted the antidecuplet components of scalar and vector two-meson coupling constants from the experimental partial decay widths of nucleon resonances N(1440) and N(1710) for J^P=1/2^+, and N(1520) and N(1700) for J^P=3/2^-, using an octet-antidecuplet representation mixing scheme with mixing angles theta_N=29 degrees and 33 degrees respectively. They then applied flavor SU(3) symmetry to relate these couplings to the Theta+ K pi N vertex and calculated tree-level total cross sections for the reactions pi- p -> K- Theta+ and K+ p -> pi+ Theta+.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle theta_N = 29 degrees for J^P=1/2^+ case, theta_N = 33 degrees for J^P=3/2^- case
- For J^P=1/2^+: |g^s_{N(1440)}| = 4.28, |g^v_{N(1440)}| < 3.68, |g^s_{N(1710)}| = 1.84, |g^v_{N(1710)}| = 0.31
- For J^P=1/2^+: antidecuplet scalar coupling |g^s_{1/2^+}| = 0.47 or 3.68 depending on relative phase, with self-energy constraint |g^s_{1/2^+}| < 3.07 and |g^v_{1/2^+}| < 1.05
- For J^P=3/2^-: |g^s_{N(1520)}| = 3.56, |g^v_{N(1520)}| = 1.11, |g^s_{N(1700)}| < 2.66, |g^v_{N(1700)}| < 0.32
- For J^P=3/2^-: self-energy constraint gives |g^s_{3/2^-}| < 1.51 and |g^v_{3/2^-}| < 0.76
- The scalar-type amplitude gives the same sign for both reactions, while the vector-type amplitude gives opposite signs

## Context

This work extends the octet-antidecuplet mixing analysis of Hyodo and Hosaka (2005) by incorporating two-meson couplings, and builds upon the self-energy study of Hosaka (2004) which assumed pure antidecuplet representation. The calculated coupling constants and reaction cross sections enable comparison with ongoing experiments at KEK for pi- p -> K- Theta+ and with high-resolution K+ p -> pi+ Theta+ experiments, potentially helping to resolve the controversial experimental status of the Theta+ reported by Nakano (2003) and questioned by Hicks (2005).
