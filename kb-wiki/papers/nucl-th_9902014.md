# Shell-Model Effective Operators for Muon Capture in ^{20}Ne

> It has been proposed that the discrepancy between the partially-conserved axial-current prediction and the nuclear shell-model calculations of the ratio $C_P/C_A$ in the muon-capture reactions can be solved in the case of ^{28}Si by introducing effective transition operators. Recently there has been experimental interest in measuring the needed angular correlations also in ^{20}Ne. Inspired by this, we have performed a shell-model analysis employing effective transition operators in the shell-model formalism for the transition $^{20}Ne(0^+_{g.s.})+μ^- \to ^{20}F(1^+; 1.057 MeV) + ν_μ$. Comparison of the calculated capture rates with existing data supports the use of effective transition operators. Based on our calculations, as soon as the experimental anisotropy data becomes available, the limits for the ratio $C_P/ C_A$ can be extracted.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between the PCAC prediction and shell-model calculations of the ratio \(C_P/C_A\) in muon capture, previously observed in \({}^{28}\)Si, can be at least partly resolved in \({}^{20}\)Ne by using renormalized (effective) one-body transition operators; the method's success is supported by comparison of calculated capture rates with existing data, but the final extraction of \(C_P/C_A\) limits awaits experimental anisotropy data.

## Method

The authors performed full 1s0d-shell-model calculations for the transition \({}^{20}\)Ne(0\(^+_{\text{g.s.}}\)) + \(\mu^- \to {}^{20}\)F(1\(^+\); 1.057 MeV) + \(\nu_\mu\) using three different two-body interactions: the phenomenological USD interaction and two microscopic effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn and Nijm-I nucleon-nucleon potentials. Effective one-body transition operators for muon capture were computed through second-order in the \(G\)-matrix using a perturbative \(\hat{Q}\)-box approach with \({}^{16}\)O as the closed-shell core, and the resulting reduced nuclear matrix elements were used to calculate capture rates and the angular-correlation parameter \(x = M_1(2)/M_1(-1)\).

## Key numbers

- The renormalization effects on the one-body transition matrix elements are of the order of 10–30%, with almost all cases showing a reduction in absolute value; the Gamow–Teller-type single-particle matrix element [101] reduces by roughly 10%.
- For the bare USD interaction, the extracted interval for \(C_P/C_A\) is \(-4.9 \le C_P/C_A \le -3.7\), far from the PCAC prediction.
- For the renormalized CD-Bonn and Nijm-I interactions, the ratio \(C_P/C_A\) agrees almost exactly with the PCAC prediction of \(C_P/C_A \approx 7\).
- If \(C_P/C_A \approx 7\), then \(x \sim 0.35\) for all interactions employed; if \(C_P/C_A \sim 5\) (as indicated by \({}^{28}\)Si results), then \(x \sim 0.30\) for the present reaction.

## Context

This work extends the effective-operator method proposed by Siiskonen et al. (1999) for \({}^{28}\)Si to the case of \({}^{20}\)Ne, motivated by experimental plans for angular-correlation measurements announced by Brudanin et al. (1999). The calculations address the long-standing anomaly where shell-model matrix elements using the USD interaction gave \(C_P/C_A = 0.0 \pm 3.2\) (Mofiah et al., 1997; Junker et al., 1996), far from the PCAC prediction of \(\approx 7\) and from values extracted using matrix elements of Ciechanowicz (1976) and Parthasarathy (1981). The results enable future extraction of \(C_P/C_A\) from the \({}^{20}\)Ne anisotropy data once published, and support the near interaction independence of the renormalization effects.
