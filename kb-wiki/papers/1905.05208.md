# Symmetry, symmetry breaking, and pion parton distributions

> Pion valence, glue and sea distributions are calculated using a continuum approach to the two valence-body bound-state problem. Since the framework is symmetry preserving, physical features of the distributions are properly expressed. The analysis reveals that the emergent phenomenon of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking causes a hardening of the valence-quark distribution function, ${q}^π(x)$. Nevertheless, this distribution exhibits the $x\simeq 1$ behaviour predicted by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). At the scale $ζ_2:=2\,$GeV, the following momentum fractions are predicted: $\langle x_{\rm valence} \rangle = 0.48(3)$, $\langle x_{\rm glue} \rangle = 0.41(2)$, $\langle x_{\rm sea} \rangle = 0.11(2)$. Evolving to $ζ=5.2\,$GeV, the result for ${q}^π(x)$ agrees with that computed using lattice QCD. These outcomes should both spur improved analyses of existing experiments and stimulate efforts to obtain new data on the pion distribution functions using available and envisioned facilities.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) hardens the pion's valence-quark distribution function, but the distribution still exhibits the $(1-x)^2$ behavior predicted by QCD at $x\simeq 1$; the complete symmetry-preserving treatment requires summing both the handbag diagram and a "pierced" Bethe-Salpeter amplitude contribution, which together satisfy the baryon-number and momentum sum rules.

## Method

The authors used a rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to compute the pion's valence-quark distribution function at the hadronic scale $\zeta_H = 0.30$ GeV, employing a Ward-identity approximation for the dressed-quark-photon vertex and the "pierced" pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. They computed the lowest six independent Mellin moments of the distribution and reconstructed the pointwise $x$-dependence, then evolved the result to higher scales ($\zeta = 2$ GeV and $\zeta = 5.2$ GeV) using DGLAP evolution to obtain the glue and sea distributions.

## Key numbers

- At scale $\zeta_2 = 2$ GeV: $\langle x_{\rm valence} \rangle = 0.48(3)$, $\langle x_{\rm glue} \rangle = 0.41(2)$, $\langle x_{\rm sea} \rangle = 0.11(2)$.
- The hadronic scale is determined as $\zeta_H = 0.30$ GeV, via a connection between the saturation value of QCD's process-independent effective charge and the one-loop running coupling.
- At $\zeta = 5.2$ GeV, the predicted valence-quark distribution $q^\pi(x)$ agrees with lattice QCD results.
- The valence-quark distribution satisfies the sum rules: $\int_0^1 dx\, q^\pi(x;\zeta_H) = 1$ and $\int_0^1 dx\, x\, q^\pi(x;\zeta_H) = 1/2$.
- The distribution exhibits the symmetry $q^\pi(x;\zeta_H) = q^\pi(1-x;\zeta_H)$.

## Context

This work extends the continuum analysis of Hecht (2000) and the symmetry-preserving corrections identified by Chang (2014) by using realistic numerical solutions for the dressed-quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude rather than algebraic models. It unifies the treatment of pion parton distributions with previous successful calculations of the charged-pion elastic and neutral-pion transition form factors (Chang 2013, Raya 2015, Gao 2017, Ding 2018). The results agree with lattice QCD calculations (Xu 2018, Chen 2018, Karthik 2018, Sufian 2019, Joo 2019) after evolution, and should spur improved analyses of existing Drell-Yan data from the E615 experiment (Conway 1989) and motivate new measurements at Jefferson Lab and other facilities.
