# Color path-integral Monte Carlo simulations of quark-gluon plasma

> Thermodynamic properties of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) of constituent quasiparticles are studied by a color path-integral Monte Carlo simulations (CPIMC). For our simulations we have presented QGP partition function in the form of color path integral with new relativistic measure instead of Gaussian one used in Feynman and Wiener path integrals. For integration over color variable we have also developed procedure of sampling color variables according to the group SU(3) Haar measure. It is shown that this method is able to reproduce the available quantum lattice chromodynamics (QCD) data.

# Color Path-Integral Monte Carlo Simulations of Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The color path-integral Monte Carlo (CPIMC) method, using a new relativistic measure instead of the Gaussian measure of Feynman and Wiener path integrals and sampling color variables according to the SU(3) Haar measure, is able to reproduce available quantum lattice chromodynamics (QCD) data for the thermodynamic properties of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) of constituent quasiparticles, provided that the quasiparticle masses and coupling constant are taken as input from lattice data or appropriate models.

## Method

The grand partition function of a multi-component QGP (dressed quarks, antiquarks, and gluons) is expressed as a color path integral with a relativistic measure derived from the relativistic kinetic energy operator K = sqrt(p^2 + m^2(T, mu_q)), replacing the Gaussian measure of standard Feynman path integrals. Each quasiparticle is represented by a set of spatial "beads" and a single 8-dimensional color vector in SU(3), with color variables sampled according to the SU(3) Haar measure under quadratic and cubic Casimir conditions; the interaction is a color Coulomb potential with a Kelbg-type effective quantum potential, and Fermi (Bose) statistics are accounted for via determinants (permanents) in the density matrix.

## Key numbers

- Quasiparticle masses satisfy m >= T at zero net-baryon density, based on analysis of lattice data from Lattice02 and LiaoShuryak.
- The coupling constant alpha_s = g^2/(4pi) used in simulations is taken from phenomenological QCD estimations by Prosperi.
- The plasma coupling parameter is defined as Gamma = (q2_bar * g^2)/(4pi * r_s * T), where q2_bar = N_c^2 - 1 is the average quadratic Casimir value.
- The degeneracy parameter chi = n_q * lambda_q^3, where lambda_q = sqrt(2*pi*beta/m_q) is the thermal wavelength of quark quasiparticles.
- The Wigner-Seitz radius r_s is defined by r_s^3 = 3/(4*pi*n), where n is the total density of all quasiparticles.

## Context

This work extends previous classical nonrelativistic molecular dynamics simulations of a color Coulomb interacting QGP by Shuryak and Zahed (2004-2005) into the quantum regime, using path integral techniques previously applied to strongly coupled electrodynamic plasmas by Filinov, Bonitz, and collaborators (2001-2004). The CPIMC method reproduces the QCD lattice equation of state from Fodor (2009) and related thermodynamic functions, and is intended to be combined with a Wigner phase-space approach to compute transport properties such as diffusion coefficients and viscosity, which are not accessible from CPIMC alone.
