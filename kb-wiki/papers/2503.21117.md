# MPD physics performance studies in Bi+Bi collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=9.2$ GeV

> The Multi-Purpose Detector (MPD) is one of the three experiments of the Nuclotron Ion Collider-fAcility (NICA) complex, which is currently under construction at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna. With collisions of heavy ions in the collider mode, the MPD will cover the energy range 4-11 GeV to scan the high baryon-density region of the QCD phase diagram. With expected statistics of 50-100 million events collected during the first run, MPD will be able to study a number of observables, including measurements of light hadrons and hypernuclei production, particle flow, correlations and fluctuations, have a first look at dielectron production, and modification of vector-meson properties in dense matter. In this paper, we present selected results of the physics feasibility studies for the MPD experiment in Bismuth-Bismuth collisions at an energy of 9.2 GeV, the system considered as one of the first available at the NICA collider.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the MPD detector at NICA will be able to perform femtoscopic and correlation measurements in Bi+Bi collisions at 9.2 GeV, with reconstructed parameters agreeing within statistical uncertainties with model-level values, but only after accounting for finite momentum resolution effects that significantly alter source size and correlation strength parameters.

## Method

The authors used UrQMD in cascade mode to generate five million central Bi+Bi collisions at energies of 4.0, 5.8, 7.7, and 9.2 GeV, then applied the CRAB after-burner to introduce quantum statistical correlations. Two-pion correlation functions were fitted with Levy distributions to extract source size R_inv, Levy index alpha, and correlation strength lambda, with separate analyses for primary pions, secondary pions, and the complete set, both with ideal resolution and with a 10 MeV momentum smearing to simulate MPD detector resolution. Charge balance functions were also computed for inclusive charged hadrons in the 0-80% centrality class at 9.2 GeV, comparing reconstructed events to generator-level UrQMD data.

## Key numbers

- Five million Bi+Bi central collisions were generated at each of four energies: sqrt(s_NN) = 4.0, 5.8, 7.7, and 9.2 GeV
- MPD minimum momentum resolution is about 1.5% for particles with total momentum around 0.2 GeV, corresponding to a relative momentum resolution Delta_q = 10 MeV
- Finite resolution reduces the source size R_inv by about 2% for primary pions and about 21% for secondary pions
- Finite resolution reduces the intercept parameter lambda by about 8% for primary pions and about 39% for secondary pions
- Finite resolution decreases the Levy index alpha for primary pions by about 8% and increases it for secondary pions by about 10%
- Track selection cuts for charge balance function analysis: 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c and |eta| < 1, with at least 15 TPC hits and DCA < 3 cm

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies previously performed at SPS (Porfy 2023), RHIC (Kincses 2016), and LHC (CMS 2023) into the NICA energy range, building on earlier MC studies for Bi+Bi collisions by Ayala (2023). The results enable future studies incorporating an Equation of State to search for signals of critical behavior and the Critical End Point in the QCD phase diagram, and provide benchmarks for distinguishing first-order phase transitions from crossover using factorial moment analysis as proposed by Kodolova (2021) and Cheremnova (2022).
