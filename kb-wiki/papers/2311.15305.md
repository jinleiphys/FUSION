# Multireference covariant density-functional theory for the low-lying states of odd-mass nuclei

> We extend multireference covariant density-functional theory (MR-CDFT) based on a relativistic point-coupling energy functional to describe the low-lying states of odd-mass nuclei. The nuclear wave function is constructed as a superposition of quadrupole-octupole deformed mean-field configurations, with projection onto angular momentum, particle numbers, and parity within the framework of the generator coordinate method. Using $^{25}$Mg as an example, we calculate the energy spectrum, electric multipole, and magnetic dipole transition strengths based on three different schemes for the mean-field configurations of odd-mass nuclei. We find that the low-energy structure of $^{25}$Mg is reasonably reproduced in all three schemes. In particular, the effect of octupole correlation is illustrated in the application to the low-lying parity doublets of $^{21}$Ne. This work demonstrates the success of the MR-CDFT for the low-lying states of odd-mass nuclei with possible strong quadruple-octupole correlations.

## Key claim
The paper extends multireference covariant density-functional theory (MR-CDFT) to odd-mass nuclei, demonstrating that the low-energy structure of $^{25}$Mg is "reasonably reproduced" across three different schemes for constructing the mean-field configurations, with the caveat that the results are shown for only two example nuclei ($^{25}$Mg and $^{21}$Ne) and the method’s general applicability is asserted rather than proven.

## Method
The nuclear wave function is constructed as a superposition of quadrupole-octupole deformed mean-field configurations, with projection onto angular momentum, particle numbers, and parity within the generator coordinate method (GCM). Three schemes for the mean-field configurations of odd-mass nuclei are compared: an even-mass-core (EAC) scheme, a false quantum vacuum (FQV) scheme, and the equal filling approximation (EFA). The method is applied to calculate the energy spectrum, electric multipole, and magnetic dipole transition strengths for $^{25}$Mg and the low-lying parity doublets of $^{21}$Ne.

## Key numbers
*   The impact of time-odd fields in odd-mass nuclei is reported to be on the order of 100–200 keV, based on prior studies by Schunck (2010) and Giuliani (2023).
*   The harmonic oscillator basis used for the Dirac spinor expansion comprises 8 major shells, with an oscillator frequency defined as $\hbar\omega=41A^{-1/3}$.
*   For the angular momentum projection, the number of mesh points for the Euler angle $\theta$ in the interval $[0, \pi]$ is set to $N_\theta=12$.
*   For the particle-number projection, choosing $N_\varphi=5$ mesh points for the gauge angles in the interval $[0, \pi]$ is stated to yield a convergent solution for the total energy of $^{24}$Mg.

## Context
This work extends the MR-CDFT framework, previously successful for even-even nuclei (e.g., Yao 2015, Zhou 2016, Rong 2023), to the more complex case of odd-mass nuclei. It builds upon earlier studies using angular-momentum projection with Skyrme-type EDFs for $^{25}$Mg (Bally 2014) and Gogny forces for odd-mass magnesium isotopes (Borrajo 2016, 2017, 2018). The success of this method enables fully microscopic and self-consistent studies of low-lying states in odd-mass nuclei with strong quadrupole-octupole correlations, moving beyond hybrid models like the particle-rotor model or the interacting boson-fermion model.
