# From the microscopic to the macroscopic world: from nucleons to neutron stars

> Recent observations of neutron-star properties, in particular the recent detection of gravitational waves emitted from binary neutron stars, GW 170817, open the way to put strong constraints on nuclear interactions. In this paper, we review the state of the art in calculating the equation of state of strongly interacting matter from first principle calculations starting from microscopic interactions among nucleons. We then review selected properties of neutron stars that can be directly compared with present and future observations.

## Key claim

This review argues that multi-messenger observations of neutron stars, particularly the gravitational-wave event GW170817, can place strong constraints on nuclear interactions, but the connection between microscopic nuclear physics and macroscopic neutron-star properties requires careful modeling that accounts for theoretical uncertainties in chiral effective field theory and many-body calculations.

## Method

The authors review the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method for calculating the equation of state (EOS) of pure neutron matter from microscopic nuclear Hamiltonians, including both phenomenological potentials (AV8'+UIX) and local chiral effective field theory interactions. They then describe how the pure neutron matter EOS is extended to beta-equilibrium conditions with leptons, and how general extrapolation schemes using piecewise polytropes or speed-of-sound parametrizations connect these microscopic calculations to neutron-star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities. The paper also presents a new phenomenological EOS framework (the DSH formalism) that simultaneously matches virial expansion coefficients, nuclear mass and radii data, quantum Monte Carlo calculations, and astrophysical observations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ ≈ 2.7 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³
- Symmetry energy from AV8'+UIX Hamiltonian: E_sym = 35.1 MeV with slope L = 63.6 MeV
- Symmetry energy from chiral N²LO (mid) with 1.0 fm cutoff: E_sym = 32.5 MeV with L = 40.5 MeV
- Symmetry energy from AV8' alone: E_sym = 30.5 MeV with L = 31.3 MeV
- Tidal polarizability constraint from GW170817: originally Λ̃ ≤ 800 at 90% confidence, later revised to 70 ≤ Λ̃ ≤ 700
- Radius range for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star from GW170817: 8.4-13.6 km
- Constraining tidal polarizability to ΔΛ̃ ≈ 200 reduces radius uncertainty to ≈ 2 km
- Previous analysis found 43 < L < 52 MeV at 68% confidence from neutron-star observations (Steiner 2012)

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting microscopic nuclear interactions to neutron-star properties that began with Burbidge (1957) and Cameron (1959). It builds on mass measurements of ∼2 solar mass neutron stars by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), and on the first gravitational-wave detection from a binary neutron-star merger, GW170817, by LIGO/Virgo (2017). The paper enables future Bayesian inference approaches to determine the nuclear EOS directly from multi-messenger observations, and provides a framework for systematically exploring the impact of nuclear physics uncertainties on kilonova light curves, nucleosynthetic yields, and gravitational-wave signals from neutron-star mergers.
