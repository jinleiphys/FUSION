# Exotic break-up modes in heavy ion reactions at low energies

> New reaction mechanisms occurring in heavy ion collisions at low energy (10- 30 MeV/A) are investigated within the Stochastic Mean Field model. We concentrate on the analysis of ternary breakup events, of dynamical origin, occurring in semi-central reactions, where the formation of excited systems in various conditions of shape and angular momentum is observed. We show how this fragmentation mode, which can be considered as a precursor of the neck emission observed at higher beam energies, emerges from the combined action of surface (neck) instabilities and angular momentum effects. Interesting perspectives are opening towards the investigation of this mechanism in neutron-rich (or exotic) systems, with the possibility to access information on the low-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ternary breakup events in semi-central heavy ion collisions at 10-30 MeV/nucleon arise from the combined action of surface (neck) instabilities and angular momentum effects, but the authors acknowledge that the Stochastic Mean Field model does not actually observe multiple breakup over the simulated time scales (≈1000 fm/c), instead inferring breakup probability from the time evolution of shape observables such as quadrupole and octupole moments.

## Method

The authors employed the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model, an approximate solution to the Boltzmann-Langevin equation, to simulate 197Au + 197Au collisions at 15 and 23 MeV/nucleon for semi-central impact parameters (4-6 fm). They used 100 test particles per nucleon and 100 events per set of initial conditions, with a Skyrme-like effective interaction (SKM*) and a stiff symmetry energy parameterization (C = 36 MeV). Fragment properties were reconstructed from the one-body density distribution using a coalescence procedure with a density threshold of ρ0/6, and the probability of subsequent breakup was estimated from the time evolution of quadrupole (Q2) and octupole (Q3) moments of the primary projectile-like and target-like fragments.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range investigated: 10-30 MeV/nucleon, with detailed simulations at 15 and 23 MeV/nucleon
- Impact parameter window: 4-6 fm
- Saturation density: ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³; binding energy: E/A = -16 MeV/nucleon; compressibility: 200 MeV
- Mean-field parameters: A = -356 MeV, B = 303 MeV, exponent α = 1/6, isovector coefficient C = 36 MeV
- Simulation time scales: t_stop = 1250 (900) fm/c for 15 MeV/A at b = 4 (6) fm; t_stop = 900 (750) fm/c for 23 MeV/A at b = 5 (6) fm
- Average mass of PLF/TLF fragments: ≈150 mass units
- Distance between peaks of A1 and A2 mass distributions: about 30% of the DF fragment mass
- System initial asymmetry: N/Z = 1.49 for Au nuclei; final average N/Z ≈ 1.43

## Context

This work extends experimental investigations of ternary breakup in 197Au + 197Au collisions at 15 and 23 MeV/nucleon reported by the CHIMERA collaboration (Isa, Cap, wilc, wilc2), and complements similar theoretical studies using the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model (QMD_calc). The authors interpret the observed ternary breakup as a precursor of the neck fragmentation mechanism observed at higher beam energies (30-50 MeV/nucleon) as studied by Baran (2004), Def, and Sylvie. The work opens perspectives for investigating neutron-rich systems to access information on the low-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, though the authors note that their SMF calculations overestimate dissipation from nucleon emission and underestimate fluctuations compared to experimental data.
