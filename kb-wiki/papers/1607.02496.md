# Mass ordering of spectra from fragmentation of saturated gluon states in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions

> The mass ordering of mean transverse momentum $\left<p_T\right>$ and of the Fourier harmonic coefficient $v_2 (p_T)$ of azimuthally anisotropic particle distributions in high energy hadron collisions is often interpreted as evidence for the hydrodynamic flow of the matter produced. We investigate an alternative initial state interpretation of this pattern in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC. The QCD Yang-Mills equations describing the dynamics of saturated gluons are solved numerically with initial conditions obtained from the Color Glass Condensate based IP-Glasma model. The gluons are subsequently fragmented into various hadron species employing the well established Lund string fragmentation algorithm of the PYTHIA event generator. We find that this ab initio initial state approach reproduces characteristic features of bulk spectra, in particular the particle mass dependence of $\left<p_T\right>$ and $v_2 (p_T)$.

# Mass ordering of spectra from fragmentation of saturated gluon states in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions

## Key claim

The mass ordering of mean transverse momentum and the Fourier harmonic coefficient v2(pT) for identified hadrons in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions can be reproduced by an initial state framework combining the IP-Glasma model with Lund string fragmentation, without requiring hydrodynamic flow or final state rescattering. The authors explicitly note that this provides an alternative interpretation to the standard hydrodynamic flow explanation, but hedge that the approach has not yet been tested for the convergence of multiparticle anisotropic Fourier coefficients or extended to light-heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations numerically with initial conditions from the IP-Glasma model, which incorporates color charge fluctuations and saturation scales constrained by HERA deep inelastic scattering data. The resulting gluon fields are evolved to proper time tau ~ 1/QS, and gluon momenta are sampled from the computed multiplicity density dNg/dyd^2kT. These gluons are grouped into strings (18 gluons per string) and fragmented into hadrons using the Lund string fragmentation algorithm in PYTHIA's hadron-stand-alone mode with default parameters, including color reconnection.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale fluctuations parameter sigma = 0.5, constrained by inclusive charged particle multiplicity and rapidity distributions in p+p collisions and by HERA DIS data
- Non-perturbative constant xi in the range 0.45 <= xi <= 0.75, constrained by global analysis of multiplicity distributions
- Default PYTHIA fragmentation parameters a = 0.68 and b = 0.98, with transverse momentum smearing width sigma_pT = 0.33 GeV
- Beam rapidity y_max = 8.9 at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, with maximum sampled gluon transverse momentum k_T,max = 10 GeV
- Charged hadron multiplicity after fragmentation is about 50-75% larger than gluon multiplicity dNg/dy, depending on coupling scheme
- Mean charged hadron multiplicity for ALICE data over |eta| < 0.5 is <N_ch> = 5.98; for CMS data over |eta| < 2.4 is <N_ch> = 30
- Using fixed coupling decreases <pT> by about 10-15%; turning off color reconnections decreases <pT> by about 5-10%
- Varying sigma_pT from 0.33 GeV to 0 changes v2{2}(pT) by a factor of 1.5-2 within the pT range shown

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012) and earlier CGC-based approaches that either compared gluon distributions directly to data or used fragmentation functions unreliable at low pT (Esposito 2015). It contrasts with hydrodynamic interpretations (Bozek 2013) that attribute mass splitting patterns to collective flow, and with PYTHIA color-reconnection schemes (Ortiz 2013) that reproduced the <pT> mass ordering but not the v2(pT) mass ordering. The CGC+Lund framework enables future studies of multiparticle anisotropic Fourier coefficients and extension to light-heavy ion collisions, addressing whether initial state correlations can describe phenomena previously attributed to final state rescattering.
