# Weak-coupling techniques for the thermodynamics of the quark-gluon plasma

> We describe some of the recent progress in the calculation of thermodynamic quantities in QCD at high temperatures and densities by weak-coupling techniques and extrapolation to realistic coupling strength. We argue that a (mostly) weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma at temperatures only a few times the transition temperature is not yet ruled out by the observed fast apparent thermalization at RHIC, as nonabelian plasma instabilities might provide an efficient mechanism for fast isotropization even in a collisionless plasma.

## Key claim

Weak-coupling techniques, when combined with appropriate resummations, can quantitatively reproduce lattice results for the equation of state of the quark-gluon plasma at temperatures only a few times the deconfinement temperature, and the observed fast isotropization at RHIC does not rule out a weakly coupled plasma because nonabelian plasma instabilities might provide an efficient isotropization mechanism even in a collisionless plasma.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of thermal field theory to QCD thermodynamics, focusing on resummation techniques that reorganize the perturbative expansion to tame the poorly convergent plasmon term of order \(g^3\). Two main approaches are described: screened perturbation theory (Andersen, Braaten, Strickland) and a \(\Phi\)-derivable two-loop approximation for the entropy density (Blaizot, Iancu, Rebhan), both of which incorporate hard-thermal-loop quasiparticle properties such as Debye screening. At finite chemical potential, hard-dense-loop resummations are used to capture non-Fermi-liquid behaviour at low temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The strictly perturbative expansion of the thermal pressure for pure-glue QCD fails to converge even at temperatures as high as \(10^5 T_c\), where the order-\(g^3\) term spoils apparent convergence.
- The perturbative result for the pressure of a quark-gluon plasma with \(N_f\) massless quarks has been computed up to order \(g^6 \ln(g)\), with the explicit coefficient \(1134.8 + 65.89 N_f + 7.653 N_f^2\) for the \(g^6 \ln(1/g)\) term in pure-glue QCD.
- The soft-sector contribution to the pressure at four-loop order contains an inherently nonperturbative constant \(c\) that requires a 3-d lattice calculation to determine.
- At finite chemical potential, the difference \(\Delta P = P(T,\mu) - P(T,0)\) from the unexpanded three-loop dimensional reduction result reproduces lattice data (Allton 2003) within expected errors for \(\mu/T = 0.2\) to \(1.0\).
- The low-temperature entropy density at high chemical potential has a perturbative expansion with anomalous terms: \(\mathcal{S}/\mu^2 T = N_f + (4\alpha_s N_f / 9\pi) \ln(2.227 \sqrt{\alpha_s N_f/\pi} \, \mu/T) - 1.383 (\alpha_s N_f/\pi)^{2/3} (T/\mu)^{2/3} + 1.041 (\alpha_s N_f/\pi)^{1/3} (T/\mu)^{4/3} + O(T^3 \ln T)\) for \(T \ll g\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the program of applying weak-coupling techniques to QCD thermodynamics, building on earlier perturbative calculations by Arnold (1995), Zhai (1995), and Braaten (1996) and on dimensional reduction methods by Kajantie (2002). It addresses the long-standing failure of strict perturbation theory, which even occurs in scalar field theory in the large-\(N\) limit (Drummond 1997). The resummation approaches described here enable quantitative comparisons with lattice results (Boyd 1996, Okamoto 1999) at temperatures \(T \ge 2 T_c\) and provide a framework for exploring the equation of state at finite chemical potential, with potential applications to neutron star cooling. The paper also discusses the resolution of a discrepancy between earlier lattice results on off-diagonal quark-number susceptibilities (Gavai 2001, 2002) and perturbative predictions, which has since been resolved by more recent lattice data (Bernard 2004, Gavai 2004, Allton 2005).
