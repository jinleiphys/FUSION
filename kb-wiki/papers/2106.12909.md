# Unitary $p$-wave Fermi gas in one dimension

> We elucidate universal many-body properties of a one-dimensional, two-component ultracold Fermi gas near the $p$-wave Feshbach resonance. The low-energy scattering in this system can be characterized by two parameters, that is, $p$-wave scattering length and effective range. At the unitarity limit where the $p$-wave scattering length diverges and the effective range is reduced to zero without conflicting with the causality bound, the system obeys universal thermodynamics as observed in a unitary Fermi gas with contact $s$-wave interaction in three dimensions. It is in contrast to a Fermi gas with the $p$-wave resonance in three dimensions in which the effective range is inevitably finite. We present the universal equation of state in this unitary $p$-wave Fermi gas within the many-body $T$-matrix approach as well as the virial expansion method. Moreover, we examine the single-particle spectral function in the high-density regime where the virial expansion is no longer valid. On the basis of the Hartree-like self-energy shift at the divergent scattering length, we conjecture that the equivalence of the Bertsch parameter across spatial dimensions holds even for a one-dimensional unitary $p$-wave Fermi gas.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas near a $p$-wave Feshbach resonance can reach a unitarity limit (where the $p$-wave scattering length diverges and the effective range is reduced to zero) without conflicting with the causality bound, unlike the three-dimensional case, and that this system exhibits universal thermodynamics analogous to the three-dimensional unitary $s$-wave Fermi gas. The hedge is that this unitarity would be realized at zero temperature, and the conjecture about the equivalence of the Bertsch parameter across spatial dimensions is stated as a conjecture, not a proven result.

## Method

The authors employ a two-channel Hamiltonian model for a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas near a $p$-wave Feshbach resonance, using a many-body $T$-matrix approach (also called the in-medium $T$-matrix approach) to compute the self-energy of fermions and the dressed molecular propagator. They also apply the virial expansion method to obtain the equation of state in the low-density limit, and perform analytic continuation of the thermal Green's function to real frequencies to compute the single-particle spectral function and density of states.

## Key numbers

* The second-order virial coefficient $b_2$ for the unitary $p$-wave Fermi gas in one dimension is exactly zero ($b_2 = 0$), in contrast to the three-dimensional unitary Fermi gas where $b_2 = \frac{3}{4\sqrt{2}}$.
* The dimensionless range parameter $R = |r_{\rm eff} k_T|$ is taken as $R = 0.01$ to describe the universal regime (sufficiently small effective range).
* For $R \gtrsim 0.1$, the fraction of molecules in the closed channel, $n_{\rm b}/n$, becomes no longer negligible, and $n_{\rm b}$ becomes exponentially large as $R$ increases, scaling as $n_{\rm b} \propto z^2 e^{-\alpha/R}$ with a constant $\alpha > 0$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of unitary Fermi gases, previously studied for three-dimensional $s$-wave interactions (e.g., by Ho, Baker, and the Bertsch parameter community), to a one-dimensional $p$-wave system. It contrasts with three-dimensional $p$-wave Fermi gases, where Wigner's causality bound prevents the effective range from being reduced to zero, as noted by Wigner (1955). The paper builds on theoretical work on $p$-wave interactions in one dimension by Cui (2016), Sekino (2018, 2020), and Valiente, and on the classification of low-energy scattering by the combination $2L+d$ from Hammer. The results enable future experimental searches for unitary behavior in one-dimensional $p$-wave Fermi gases and further theoretical investigation of the Bertsch parameter across different spatial dimensions and interaction symmetries.
