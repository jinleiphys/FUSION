# Constraining cross sections for unstable $^{153,159}$Gd$(n,γ)$ and their astrophysical implications

> Neutron capture $(n,γ)$ cross sections of Gadolinium (Gd) isotopes are critical to astrophysics research, nuclear reactor designs, and medical applications. However, the available $(n,γ)$ data on unstable Gd isotopes are scarce and direct measurement is challenging. In this work, we propose an approach to infer the $(n,γ)$ cross sections for unstable $^{153,159}$Gd isotopes by constraining both the $γ$-ray strength functions ($γ$SFs) and nuclear level densities (NLDs). Specifically, the key $γ$SF parameters are adjusted to match the available experimental data, and the NLD parameters are determined by renormalizing microscopic level densities through a Bayesian optimization method. Our approach is verified by comparing our predictions with the experimental $(n,γ)$ data for the stable $^{155,157}$Gd isotopes. We then infer the unstable $^{153,159}\text{Gd}(n,γ)$ cross sections within the neutron energy range of 0.01--5.0 MeV. The resulting uncertainty is about $30\%$, which is significantly reduced by a factor of 5.5 compared to a large uncertainty of $\sim167\%$ predicted with different nuclear models in TALYS. We further calculate the astrophysical reaction rates for the $^{153,159}\text{Gd}$ isotopes. It is found that the $^{159}\text{Gd}(n,γ)$ rate is larger by a factor of $\sim$2.9 than the JINA REACLIB recommendation. This enhancement increases the neutron capture branching ratio at $^{159}$Gd. Consequently, the resulting $^{160}$Gd abundance is increased by a factor of $\sim$2 compared to predictions using the JINA REACLIB rate in $s$-process nucleosynthesis simulations. Our approach is promising for extracting $(n,γ)$ data on a wider range of unstable isotopic chains as well as for essential astrophysical reaction network calculations and nuclear science applications.

## Key claim

The authors infer neutron capture cross sections for unstable $^{153,159}$Gd by constraining both the gamma-ray strength functions and nuclear level densities using experimental data for neighboring stable isotopes, achieving a reduction in uncertainty from approximately 167% to about 30%, but only within the neutron energy range of 0.01 to 5.0 MeV and under the assumption that the constrained parameters for the compound nuclei $^{154,156,158,160}$Gd are transferable to the unstable targets.

## Method

The authors fitted a double-peaked Standard Lorentzian model to experimental E1 gamma-ray strength function data from the IAEA Photon Strength Function Database for the compound nuclei $^{154,156,158,160}$Gd using nonlinear least-squares minimization. They renormalized microscopic HFB+c nuclear level densities via a Bayesian optimization method to simultaneously match known discrete low-energy levels and the total level density at the neutron separation energy derived from s-wave resonance spacings, using two different spin-cutoff prescriptions (FG05 and FG09) to define an uncertainty band. These constrained inputs were then implemented in the TALYS-2.0 reaction code to calculate $(n,\gamma)$ cross sections for $^{153,155,157,159}$Gd, with validation against experimental data for the stable isotopes $^{155,157}$Gd.

## Key numbers

- The resulting uncertainty in the inferred $(n,\gamma)$ cross sections for $^{153,159}$Gd is about 30%, reduced by a factor of 5.5 compared to the approximately 167% uncertainty from different nuclear model combinations in TALYS.
- The $^{159}$Gd$(n,\gamma)$ astrophysical reaction rate is larger by a factor of approximately 2.9 than the JINA REACLIB recommendation.
- The resulting $^{160}$Gd abundance is increased by a factor of approximately 2 compared to predictions using the JINA REACLIB rate in s-process nucleosynthesis simulations.
- The fitted SLO parameters for $^{154}$Gd include peak 1: $\sigma_1 = 171.23 \pm 10.15$ mb, $E_1 = 12.09 \pm 0.12$ MeV, $\Gamma_1 = 3.25 \pm 0.27$ MeV; peak 2: $\sigma_2 = 241.56 \pm 13.97$ mb, $E_2 = 15.12 \pm 0.09$ MeV, $\Gamma_2 = 2.81 \pm 0.15$ MeV.
- The estimated s-wave resonance spacing for $^{160}$Gd from systematics is $D_0 = 7.8$ eV.
- NLD variations yield an average uncertainty factor of approximately 2.8 for the four Gd isotopes in the 0.01–5 MeV energy range; E1 gamma-ray strength function variations yield an average uncertainty factor of approximately 3.3.

## Context

This work extends the approach of constraining gamma-ray strength functions and nuclear level densities to reduce uncertainties in neutron capture cross sections for unstable nuclei, previously applied by Zhang (2025) and others, to the specific case of gadolinium isotopes relevant to s-process nucleosynthesis. The authors validate their method against experimental data for stable $^{155,157}$Gd from Wisshak (1995) and others, then apply it to unstable $^{153,159}$Gd where direct measurements are challenging due to radioactivity and the lack of free-neutron targets. The enhanced $^{159}$Gd$(n,\gamma)$ rate increases the neutron capture branching ratio at $^{159}$Gd, which affects the production of the s-only isotope $^{160}$Gd in stellar nucleosynthesis models; the approach is promising for extracting $(n,\gamma)$ data on a wider range of unstable isotopic chains for astrophysical reaction network calculations.
