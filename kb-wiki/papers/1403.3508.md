# Log-periodic oscillations of transverse momentum distributions

> Large p_T transverse momentum distributions exhibit apparently a power-like behavior. We argue that, under closer inspection, this behavior is in fact decorated with some log-periodic oscillations. Assuming that this is genuine effect and not experimental artefact, it suggests that either the exponent of the power-like behavior is in reality complex number or that there is a scale parameter which exhibits specific log-eriodic oscillations. This problem is discussed using Tsallis distributions with scale parameter being a temperature T. At this stage we consider both possibilities on equal footing.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparently power-law behavior of large-\(p_T\) transverse momentum distributions in \(pp\) collisions is actually decorated with log-periodic oscillations, and that this genuine effect (if not an experimental artifact) can be explained either by a complex power exponent (complex nonextensivity parameter \(q\)) or by a scale parameter (temperature \(T\)) that itself oscillates log-periodically; at this stage both possibilities are considered on equal footing.

## Method

The authors fit Tsallis distributions (in either the original form with parameters \(q\) and \(T\) or the QCD-inspired Hagedorn form with parameters \(m\) and \(T\)) to large-\(p_T\) data from CMS at 0.9 and 7 TeV, then examine the data/fit ratio. They model the observed oscillations by multiplying the Tsallis distribution by a log-periodic factor \(R(E) = a + b\cos[c\ln(E+d)+f]\). They derive this factor from two distinct mechanisms: (1) allowing the power index \(m\) (or \(q\)) to be complex, leading to a hierarchy of scales; (2) keeping \(q\) real but allowing the temperature \(T\) to oscillate log-periodically, which they connect to a stochastic Langevin equation with energy-dependent noise or energy-dependent relaxation time.

## Key numbers

- For CMS \(pp\) collisions at 0.9 TeV, fit parameters for the oscillating factor \(R\) are: \(a=0.865\), \(c=2.1\); at 7 TeV: \(a=0.909\), \(c=1.86\); for both energies \(b=0.166\), \(d=0.948\), \(f=-1.462\).
- The Tsallis/Hagedorn fit parameters for 0.9 TeV are \((T=0.135, m=8)\); for 7 TeV \((T=0.145, m=6.7)\).
- The observed oscillation frequency parameter \(c \simeq 2\) is more than an order of magnitude smaller than the expected value \(2\pi/\ln(1+\alpha)\); the discrepancy is resolved by introducing a hierarchy factor \(\kappa \simeq 22\) (for \(\alpha \simeq 0.15\) and \(c \simeq 2\)).
- For the oscillating-temperature mechanism, the temperature oscillation amplitude is about \(\bar{b}/\bar{a} \sim 3\%\) of the mean temperature: at 0.9 TeV \(\bar{a}=0.132\), \(\bar{b}=0.0035\), \(\bar{c}=2.2\); at 7 TeV \(\bar{a}=0.143\), \(\bar{b}=0.0045\), \(\bar{c}=2.0\).

## Context

This work extends the use of Tsallis distributions for fitting transverse momentum spectra in multiparticle production, as previously applied by Cleymans (2011), Biro (2012), and Deppman (2013) among others, and specifically follows the successful fits to LHC data from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE. The observation of log-periodic oscillations in the data/fit ratio is a new finding that the authors interpret as evidence for either complex nonextensivity (building on earlier work by Wilk and Wlodarczyk on complex \(q\)) or an oscillating temperature derived from a stochastic Langevin equation with energy-dependent noise. The paper opens the question of which mechanism is physically preferred and suggests possible connections to QCD Coulomb phase shift effects discussed by Pire (1988).
