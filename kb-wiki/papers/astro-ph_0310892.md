# Cosmological variation of deuteron binding energy, strong interaction and quark masses from big bang nucleosynthesis

> We use Big Bang Nucleosynthesis calculations and light element abundance data to constrain the relative variation of the deuteron binding energy since the universe was a few minutes old, $δQ = Q(BBN)-Q(present)$. Two approaches are used, first treating the baryon to photon ratio, $η$, as a free parameter, but with the additional freedom of varying $δQ$, and second using the WMAP value of $η$ and solving only for $δQ$. Including varying $Q$ yields a better fit to the observational data than imposing the present day value, rectifying the discrepancy between the $^4He$ abundance and the deuterium and $^7Li$ abundances, {\it and} yields good agreement with the independently determined $η_{WMAP}$. The minimal deviation consistent with the data is significant at about the 4-$σ$ level; $δQ/Q= -0.019 \pm 0.005$. If the primordial $^4$He abundance lies towards the low end of values in the literature, this deviation is even larger and more statistically significant. Taking the light element abundance data at face-value, our result may be interpreted as variation of the dimensionless ratio $X=m_s/Λ_{QCD}$ of the strange quark mass and strong scale: $δX/X=(1.1 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-3}$. These results provide a strong motivation for a more thorough exploration of the potential systematic errors in the light element abundance data.

## Key claim

The paper finds that allowing the deuteron binding energy to vary during Big Bang Nucleosynthesis provides a better fit to observed light element abundances than using the present-day value, resolving an internal inconsistency between the measured helium-4 abundance and the deuterium and lithium-7 abundances while simultaneously yielding agreement with the independently determined baryon-to-photon ratio from WMAP. The minimal deviation is significant at about the 4-sigma level, but the authors caution that systematic errors in the helium-4 abundance data could imitate the effect.

## Method

The authors modified a standard BBN code (Kawano 1992) to allow the deuteron binding energy Q to vary as a free parameter in the reaction p+n to d+gamma, then performed likelihood analyses comparing predicted abundances of helium-4, deuterium, and lithium-7 against observational data compilations. Two approaches were used: first treating both the baryon-to-photon ratio eta and delta Q as free parameters, and second fixing eta to the WMAP value and solving only for delta Q, with the likelihood function incorporating both experimental uncertainties and theoretical errors from reaction rate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Relative variation of deuteron binding energy (using high helium-4 mass fraction and fixed eta from WMAP): delta Q/Q = -0.019 +/- 0.005
- Best-fit deuteron binding energy from combined analysis with WMAP eta: Q_BBN = (25.34 +/- 0.12) x 10^9 K (present value is 25.82 x 10^9 K)
- Best-fit baryon-to-photon ratio from simultaneous fit: eta = (6.51 +0.77 -0.66) x 10^{-10}, compatible with WMAP value eta_0 = (6.14 +/- 0.25) x 10^{-10}
- For low helium-4 mass fraction subset: delta Q/Q = -0.048 +/- 0.004 (fixed eta) and delta Q/Q = -0.059 +/- 0.007 (free eta)
- For full 14-point helium-4 sample with increased errors: delta Q/Q = -0.033 +/- 0.006 (fixed eta)
- Inferred variation of strange quark mass to QCD scale ratio: delta X/X = (1.1 +/- 0.3) x 10^{-3}, where X = m_s / Lambda_QCD

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions by Flambaum and Shuryak (2002), Dmitriev and Flambaum (2002), and Flambaum and Shuryak (2003) on how strong scale variation affects BBN yields, and builds on studies of deuteron binding energy variation in BBN by Dys71, Davies, Barrow, and others. The results provide strong motivation for improved measurements of primordial light element abundances and more thorough exploration of systematic errors, particularly in helium-4 data from Izotov and Thuan (2003) and others, and suggest that searches for variation of fundamental constants should prioritize the strange quark mass to QCD scale ratio over the fine structure constant or Lambda_QCD/M_Planck.
