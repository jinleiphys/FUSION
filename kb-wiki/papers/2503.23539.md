# Pfaffian formulas for non equivalent bases

> Pfaffian formulas used to compute overlaps necessary to carry out generator coordinate method calculations using a set of Hartree- Fock- Bogoliubov wave functions, is generalized to the case where each of the HFB states are expanded in different arbitrary bases spanning different sub-space of the Hilbert space. The formula obtained is compared with previous results proving to be completely equivalent to them. A discussion of equivalent formulas obtained in the literature is carried out.

# Pfaffian formulas for non equivalent bases

## Key claim

The paper derives a Pfaffian formula for the overlap between two Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions that is valid when each HFB state is expanded in a different arbitrary basis spanning a different subspace of the Hilbert space; the formula depends only on quantities defined in the original finite bases and is independent of any formal extension to an infinite basis, contrary to earlier derivations that required such extensions.

## Method

The author starts by formally embedding each finite basis into an infinite Hilbert space by adding complementary basis states with zero occupancy, then uses the Pfaffian formula for overlaps in the infinite-dimensional space. By applying block matrix manipulations and the "move and shift" operation to the Pfaffian argument, the expression is reduced to one involving only the finite-basis quantities: the restricted overlap matrix R (the N x N matrix of overlaps between the two finite bases) and the matrices M^(i) = (V_i U_i^{-1})* constructed from the finite-basis Bogoliubov amplitudes. The derivation is then compared with previous results from Robledo (2012), Avez et al. (2012), and Scamps et al. (2013), showing equivalence while pointing out inconsistencies in the Avez et al. derivation.

## Key numbers

- The final Pfaffian formula (Eq. labeled eq:PfaffFin) involves a matrix of dimension (2N) x (2N), where N = 2n is the dimension of the finite bases (assumed equal for both states, with N taken as the larger of the two basis sizes).
- The overlap is expressed as s_N pf( [R M^(1) R^T, -I; I, -M^(0)*] ), where R is the N x N restricted overlap matrix and M^(i) are N x N matrices derived from the finite-basis Bogoliubov amplitudes.
- The derivation uses an integer M (the dimension of the extended Hilbert space) which is allowed to tend to infinity; the final result is independent of M.
- The equivalence to the result of Avez et al. (2012) yields the expression <phi_0|phi_1> = (-1)^n det T pf( [M^(1), T^{-1}; -T^{-1}, -M^(0)*] ), where T is the overlap matrix between the two bases.

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian formalism for HFB overlaps originally developed for equivalent bases (Robledo, 2009; PhysRevC.79.021302) to the common situation where HFB states are expanded in non-equivalent single-particle bases, such as harmonic oscillator bases with different oscillator lengths (Robledo, 2022). It corrects and clarifies the derivation of Avez et al. (2012), which the author argues contains inconsistencies in its treatment of pseudo-identities and the removal of non-occupied states. The result enables consistent symmetry restoration and generator coordinate method calculations in contexts where the basis changes, including angular momentum projection using full HO major shells (Marevic, 2020) and particle number restoration in fission fragments (Simenel, 2010). The paper also shows that the formula of Scamps et al. (2013) for BCS overlaps, originally derived without considering non-equivalent bases, accidentally gives the correct result for overlaps but would fail for general operator matrix elements.
