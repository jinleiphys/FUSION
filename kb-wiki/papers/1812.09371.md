# Role of the spectator system in electromagnetic effects

> The electromagnetic effects on charged pion ($π^+,π^-$) spectra provide new, independent information on the space-time evolution of the ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision. The spectator life time and its excitation energy may also be of importance for the understanding of the space-time evolution of the participant zone. This paper gives an overview of our coordinated effort to understand the interplay between electromagnetic phenomena and processes related to the fragmentation of the spectator system at forward rapidity in peripheral $Pb$+$Pb$ collisions at top CERN SPS energies. Our study includes on one hand the experimental analysis of electromagnetic effects and corresponding phenomenological Monte Carlo simulations, and on the other hand dedicated theoretical calculations based on the Abrasion-Ablation model ABRABLA and the 4D Langevin approach.

## Key claim

The spectator system's expansion and fragmentation, not just its static charge, must be considered to accurately model the electromagnetic distortion of charged pion spectra in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies; the optimal description of the data requires a shift in spectator rapidity (Δy = -0.11) and a non-zero expansion velocity, suggesting that participant charge also contributes to the total charged cloud responsible for the effect.

## Method

The authors combined experimental data from the NA49 experiment on the π⁺/π⁻ ratio in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon with phenomenological Monte Carlo simulations that varied the distance between pion emission zone and spectator (d_E), spectator surface expansion velocity (β), and spectator rapidity shift (Δy). Separately, they performed theoretical calculations using the Abrasion-Ablation model ABRABLA to obtain spectator excitation energies and de-excitation channels, and the 4D Langevin approach to simulate the time evolution of spectator evaporation processes.

## Key numbers

- The optimal description of NA49 data for peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon was obtained for d_E = 0.75 fm, with a spectator rapidity shift Δy = -0.11 from the original beam rapidity.
- The estimated kinetic energy range for the expanding charged sphere corresponding to the optimal description of EM effects (β = 0.2 ± 0.05) is comparable to the ABRABLA excitation energy of order 100 MeV.
- For impact parameter b = 10.5 fm, the mean multiplicity of emitted neutrons is 26, while that of protons is 6.
- Nucleons are mostly evaporated in a time below 600 fm/c in the spectator center-of-mass system, comparable to the estimated 400-2000 fm/c that fast pions at p_T = 0 spend in close spectator vicinity.
- The Lorentz boost factor at this collision energy is γ ≈ 9.2.

## Context

This work extends the studies of spectator-induced electromagnetic effects reported by Sputowska (2018) for Pb+Pb collisions and Kielbowicz (2018) for Ar+Sc collisions at the CERN SPS, and by Rybicki (2007, 2011) on charged pion spectra distortions. The paper connects the phenomenological modeling of EM effects with dedicated nuclear theory (ABRABLA by Gaimard (1991) and Kelic (2008), and the 4D Langevin approach) to address the previously neglected time evolution of the spectator system. The results enable future studies of the interplay between spectator de-excitation and electromagnetic effects, and may provide verification of the collective model based on energy-momentum conservation presented at the same workshop.
