# Folding potential with modern nuclear density functionals and application to 16O+208Pb reaction

> Double folding potential is constructed using the M3Y interaction and the matter densities of the projectile and target nuclei obtained from four microscopic energy density functional (EDF) models. The elastic scattering cross sections for the 16O+208Pb system are calculated using the optical model with the double folding potentials of the four EDF models. We focus on the correlation between the matter densities and the behavior the double folding potential and the elastic scattering cross sections. First, the matter and charge densities are examined by comparing the results of the four EDF models. There is a slight difference in the density in the internal region, but it is negligible in the outer region. Next, we calculate the double folding potential with the matter densities obtained from the four EDF models. Differences between the models are negligible in the outer region, but the potential depth in the internal region shows model dependence, which can be understood from the behavior of matter densities in the internal region. Another point is that the double folding potential is shown to be weakly dependent on the incident energy. Finally, the elastic scattering cross sections have no significant model dependence except for the slight difference in the backward angle.

## Key claim

The elastic scattering cross sections for the 16O+208Pb system calculated with double folding potentials from four different energy density functional (EDF) models show no significant model dependence except for slight differences at backward angles, suggesting that within the EDF models considered, theoretical predictions are not sensitive to a specific model.

## Method

Double folding potentials were constructed using the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction and matter densities of 16O and 208Pb obtained from four microscopic EDF models: two nonrelativistic Skyrme force models (SLy4 and KIDS) and two relativistic mean field models (QHD and QMC). The elastic scattering cross sections were then calculated using the optical model with these double folding potentials, where the imaginary part of the optical potential was fitted to experimental data using the SLy4 and QHD models and then applied to the other models.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, model dependence in matter density becomes negligible at r greater than or equal to 2.5 fm; for 208Pb, at r greater than or equal to 6 fm.
- The double folding potential was calculated at Elab = 80, 90, and 192 MeV, corresponding to below, near, and above barrier energies.
- The energy-dependent correction factor in the M3Y potential is 1 - 0.005 * Elab / A1, giving 0.975 at Elab = 80 MeV and 0.94 at Elab = 192 MeV, a difference of 3.5%.
- Optimal imaginary potential depths WI from fitting: at Elab = 80 MeV, SLy4 gives 9.705 MeV and QHD gives 11.130 MeV; at Elab = 90 MeV, SLy4 gives 12.068 MeV and QHD gives 13.025 MeV; at Elab = 192 MeV, SLy4 gives 16.411 MeV and QHD gives 16.763 MeV.
- Chi-squared values for the fits: at Elab = 80 MeV, SLy4 gives 0.207 and QHD gives 0.309; at Elab = 90 MeV, SLy4 gives 8.458 and QHD gives 3.574; at Elab = 192 MeV, SLy4 gives 2.102 and QHD gives 0.805.

## Context

This work extends the application of microscopic EDF models, previously used to calculate nuclear properties across the nuclear chart (Heo et al., 2023) and applied to lepton-nucleus scattering, to nuclear reactions by replacing the phenomenological Woods-Saxon density function in the double folding potential. The study uses the well-known 16O+208Pb system as a first step, and the weak model dependence observed opens questions about whether such insensitivity will hold for reactions of neutron-rich rare isotopes relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis.
