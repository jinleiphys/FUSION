# Eta-mesic nuclei in relativistic mean-field theory

> With the eta-nucleon (eta N) interaction Lagrangian deduced from chiral perturbation theory, we study the possible eta-mesic nuclei in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory. The eta single-particle energies are sensitive to the eta N scattering length, and increase monotonically with the nucleon number A. If the scattering length is in the range of a^{eta N}=0.75-1.05 fm and the imaginary potential V_{0}-15 MeV, some discrete states of C, O and Ne eta bound states should be identified in experiments. However, when the scattering length a^{eta N}< 0.5 fm, or the imaginary potential V_{0} > 30 MeV, no discrete eta meson bound states could be observed in experiments.

## Key claim

If the eta-nucleon scattering length is in the range of 0.75 to 1.05 fm and the imaginary potential depth is about 15 MeV, discrete eta bound states in carbon-12, oxygen-16, and neon-20 should be identifiable in experiments; however, if the scattering length is below 0.5 fm or the imaginary potential exceeds 30 MeV, no discrete eta meson bound states could be observed.

## Method

The authors constructed an eta-nucleon interaction Lagrangian from heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order, with the off-shell term parameter kappa determined by the eta-nucleon scattering length. They combined this Lagrangian with the standard relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian for nucleons and mesons (sigma, omega, rho, and photon), using the NL-SH parameter set, and solved the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently for spherical nuclei. An imaginary potential of the "t-rho" form was added phenomenologically to account for absorption, with a suppression factor derived from phase-space reduction for the mesonic and non-mesonic decay channels.

## Key numbers

- For scattering length a^{eta N} = 0.20 fm, 1s state binding energies are (9 +/- 7) MeV, increasing with nucleon number A; widths are much larger than binding energies even at V0 = 15 MeV.
- For a^{eta N} = 0.50 fm, ground-state single-particle binding energies are (26 +/- 10) MeV; with V0 = 15 MeV, decay widths are comparable to binding energies.
- For a^{eta N} = 0.75 fm, 1s binding energies range from 46.2 MeV (in eta-12C) to 56.3 MeV (in eta-44Ti) at V0 = 15 MeV; 1p binding energies range from 6.6 MeV to 36.8 MeV.
- For a^{eta N} = 1.05 fm, 1s binding energies range from 69.8 MeV (in eta-12C) to 80.7 MeV (in eta-44Ti) at V0 = 15 MeV; 1p binding energies range from 23.4 MeV to 59.0 MeV.
- For a^{eta N} = 0.75 to 1.05 fm and V0 = 15 MeV, the sum of half-widths of 1s and 1p states is narrower than the separation between these states for C, O, and Ne nuclei.
- Widths of 1s states across all calculations are in the range (28 +/- 7) to (104 +/- 14) MeV; widths of 1p states are (26 +/- 3) to (89 +/- 7) MeV, for V0 = 15 to 50 MeV.
- Changing V0 from 15 to 50 MeV decreases single-particle binding energies by about 3 to 8 MeV for both 1s and 1p states.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of eta-mesic nuclei by Haider and collaborators, who first proposed such systems, and addresses the strong model dependence of the eta-nuclear potential, which spans from about -20 to -100 MeV across different theoretical approaches. The authors use a chiral perturbation theory derived interaction, in contrast to earlier phenomenological potentials, and their results are compared to experimental searches by Sokol et al. (who claimed observation of eta-11C) and Pfeiffer et al. (who claimed evidence for eta-3He). The study enables future work with more realistic eta-nucleon interactions that incorporate the N*(1535) resonance explicitly and more fundamental treatments of the imaginary potential.
