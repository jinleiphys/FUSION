# Standard convolution description of deuteron tensor spin structure

> Spin-1 hadrons have additional structure functions not present for spin 1/2 hadrons. These could probe novel aspects of hadron structure and QCD dynamics. For the deuteron, the tensor structure function $b_1$ inherently mixes quark and nuclear degrees of freedom. These proceedings discuss two standard convolution models applied to calculations of the deuteron $b_1$ structure functions. We find large differences with the existing HERMES data and other convolution model calculations. This leaves room for non-standard contributions to $b_1$ in the deuteron. We also discuss the influence of higher twist nuclear effects in the model calculations and data extraction at kinematics covered in HERMES and Jefferson Lab.

# Standard convolution description of deuteron tensor spin structure

## Key claim
The two standard convolution models for the deuteron tensor structure function \(b_1\) produce results that differ significantly from each other and from the existing HERMES data, but the large experimental error bars mean that non-standard contributions (such as shadowing, pionic, or hidden color mechanisms) cannot be ruled out and may still be required.

## Method
Two convolution approaches were applied to calculate the deuteron \(b_1\) structure function. Theory 1 uses scaling-limit relations between virtual photon-hadron helicity amplitudes to express \(b_1\) as a convolution of a polarized deuteron momentum distribution with the average nucleon structure function \(F_1^N\). Theory 2 is based on the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) framework, which does not assume scaling-limit relations and thus automatically includes higher twist nuclear effects, yielding an expression with additional \(F_2^N\) terms. Both calculations used the MSTW2008 LO parametrization for \(F_2^N\), the SLAC-R1998 parametrization for the ratio \(R = \sigma_L/\sigma_T\), and the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers
- At \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) (representative of the average HERMES kinematics), both convolution calculations fail to reproduce the size or trend of the HERMES data for \(xb_1\) as a function of \(x\).
- The difference between Theory 1 and Theory 2 becomes larger for smaller \(Q^2\) values, with calculations shown at \(Q^2 = 1.0\), 2.5, and 5.0 GeV\(^2\).
- The \(SD\)-wave interference term in these calculations has opposite sign compared to the earlier calculations of Hoodbhoy (1988) and Khan (1991), and a non-zero \(b_1\) is found for \(x > 1\) where it was identically zero in those earlier results.

## Context
This work extends the sparse literature of standard convolution calculations for the deuteron \(b_1\) structure function, specifically contrasting with the earlier calculations of Hoodbhoy (1988) and Khan (1991). The large discrepancies with HERMES data (Airapetian, 2005) motivate further investigation of non-standard mechanisms such as shadowing (Frankfurt, 1983; Nikolaev, 1996; Edelmann, 1997; Bora, 1997), eikonal final-state interactions (Cosyn, 2017), and pionic or hidden color contributions (Miller, 2013). The results also highlight that at the kinematics of HERMES and the upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments (Slifer, 2013), the Callan-Gross-like relation \(b_2 = 2x_T b_1\) is violated and the higher twist structure functions \(b_3, b_4\) are of similar magnitude to the leading twist ones, warranting inclusion of higher twist effects in future data extraction procedures.
