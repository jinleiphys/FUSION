# Vector quarkonia at the LHC with JETHAD: A high-energy viewpoint

> In this review we discuss and extend the study of the inclusive production of vector quarkonia, $J/ψ$ and $Υ$, emitted with large transverse momenta and rapidities at the LHC. We adopt the novel ZCW19$^+$ determination to depict the quarkonium production mechanism at the next-to-leading level of perturbative QCD. This approach is based on the nonrelativistic QCD formalism well adapted to describe the production of a quarkonium state from the collinear fragmentation of a gluon or a constituent heavy quark at the lowest energy scale. We rely upon the NLL/NLO$^+$ hybrid high-energy and collinear factorization for differential cross sections, where the standard collinear formalism is enhanced by the BFKL resummation of next-to-leading energy logarithms arising in the $t$-channel. We employ the JETHAD method to analyze the behavior of rapidity distributions for double inclusive vector-quarkonium and inclusive vector-quarkonium plus jet emissions. We discovered that the natural stability of the high-energy series, previously observed in observables sensitive to the emission of hadrons with heavy flavor detected in the rapidity acceptance of LHC barrel calorimeters, becomes even more manifest when these particles are tagged in forward regions covered by endcaps. Our findings brace the important message that vector quarkonia at the LHC via the hybrid factorization offer a unique chance to perform precision studies of high-energy QCD, as well as an intriguing opportunity to shed light on the quarkonium production puzzle.

## Key claim

The hybrid high-energy and collinear factorization, when applied to vector quarkonium production at the LHC, shows that the natural stability of the high-energy series becomes even more manifest when quarkonia are tagged in forward regions covered by endcaps, compared to previous observations in barrel calorimeters.

## Method

The author adopts the ZCW19$^+$ determination of fragmentation functions based on nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) to describe quarkonium production at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, using collinear fragmentation of a gluon or a constituent heavy quark at the lowest energy scale. The NLL/NLO$^+$ hybrid high-energy and collinear factorization is employed, where the standard collinear formalism is enhanced by Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) resummation of next-to-leading energy logarithms arising in the $t$-channel. The JETHAD method is used to analyze rapidity distributions for double inclusive vector-quarkonium and inclusive vector-quarkonium plus jet emissions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The J/psi meson has a mass around 3.1 GeV, as discovered in 1974 at SLAC and BNL
- The first bottomonium particle (Upsilon) was observed in 1977
- The scale hierarchy for the semi-hard regime requires sqrt(s) >> {Q} >> Lambda_QCD, where s is the center-of-mass energy squared and {Q} is a set of process-characteristic hard scales
- At large transverse momenta one has mu ~ |p_T| >> m_Q, where m_Q is the mass of the constituent heavy quark

## Context

This work extends the hybrid high-energy and collinear factorization formalism previously applied to semi-inclusive forward-backward hadroproductions (Colferai 2010, Celiberto 2020, Bolognino 2021, Celiberto 2021, Celiberto 2022), now enriched with single-parton quarkonium fragmentation following the scheme proposed by Celiberto (2022). The study is complementary to the work by Boussarie (2017) on inclusive semi-hard J/psi plus jet processes within the short-distance approximation. The approach enables precision studies of high-energy QCD and addresses the quarkonium production puzzle, with future prospects including matching between the hybrid factorization and the ABF formalism (Ball 1995, Altarelli 2001).
