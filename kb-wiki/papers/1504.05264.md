# Spin Physics at J-PARC

> Spin-physics projects at J-PARC are explained by including future possibilities. J-PARC is the most-intense hadron-beam facility in the high-energy region above multi-GeV, and spin physics will be investigated by using secondary beams of kaons, pions, neutrinos, muons, and antiproton as well as the primary-beam proton. In particle physics, spin topics are on muon $g-2$, muon and neutron electric dipole moments, and time-reversal violation experiment in a kaon decay. Here, we focus more on hadron-spin physics as for future projects. For example, generalized parton distributions (GPDs) could be investigated by using pion and proton beams, whereas they are studied by the virtual Compton scattering at lepton facilities. The GPDs are key quantities for determining the three-dimensional picture of hadrons and for finding the origin of the nucleon spin including partonic orbital-angular-momentum contributions. In addition, polarized parton distributions and various hadron spin topics should be possible by using the high-momentum beamline. The strangeness contribution to the nucleon spin could be also investigated in principle with the neutrino beam with a near detector facility.

## Key claim

J-PARC could enable a wide range of hadron-spin physics projects using secondary beams and the primary proton beam, but the paper emphasizes that these are future possibilities with no approved proposals at the time of writing; the high-momentum beamline needed for many hadron-spin studies was expected to be ready in the Japanese fiscal year of 2016.

## Method

The paper surveys spin-physics projects at J-PARC, covering both particle-physics topics (muon g-2, electric dipole moments, time-reversal violation in kaon decay) and hadron-spin topics (generalized parton distributions, transverse-momentum-dependent distributions, tensor-polarized PDFs, elastic spin asymmetries). For hadron-spin studies, the author discusses specific experimental channels such as exclusive Drell-Yan with pion beams, exclusive hadron-production reactions with proton beams, and Drell-Yan processes with polarized or tensor-polarized targets, all using the planned high-momentum beamline at the hadron experimental facility.

## Key numbers

- Muon anomalous magnetic moment: current BNL-E821 value is \(a_\mu = 0.00116592089(63)\), with a 3.3\(\sigma\) discrepancy from the theoretical value \(0.00116591828(49)\); the proposed J-PARC experiment aims to improve the measurement to 0.1 ppm (a factor of five improvement).
- Muon electric dipole moment: current limit from BNL-E821 is \(1.9 \times 10^{-19}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; the J-PARC experiment aims to reach \(10^{-21}\) e\(\cdot\)cm (two orders of magnitude improvement).
- Neutron electric dipole moment: current limit from a Grenoble experiment is \(3 \times 10^{-26}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; with J-PARC's spallation UCN sources, the expected UCN density is \(10^3\)-\(10^4\) UCN/cm\(^3\) (compared to 0.7 UCN/cm\(^3\) in Grenoble), aiming for a limit of \(10^{-28}\) e\(\cdot\)cm.
- Transverse muon polarization in kaon decay: KEK-E246 result was \(P_T = -0.0017 \pm 0.0023 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.0017 (\text{syst})\); the proposed J-PARC experiment expects an error of \(\delta P_T = 10^{-4}\).
- High-momentum beamline: primary proton beam intensity of \(10^{10}\)-\(10^{12}\)/sec at 30 GeV; typical intensity for 10 GeV/c pions is \(10^7\)/sec and for 15 GeV/c pions is \(10^6\)/sec.
- J-PARC proton beam power: 1 MW in the 3-GeV synchrotron, 0.75 MW expected in the 50-GeV synchrotron (currently operating at 30 GeV).

## Context

This paper extends discussions of spin physics from lepton-facility experiments (such as deeply virtual Compton scattering for GPDs) to hadron-facility possibilities at J-PARC, complementing work at RHIC (which probes smaller \(x\) regions) and Fermilab (E866 and E906/SeaQuest for antiquark distributions). The proposed measurements of GPDs via exclusive Drell-Yan and exclusive hadron-production reactions would provide complementary access to the ERBL region, while tensor-polarized PDF measurements via Drell-Yan with tensor-polarized deuteron targets would extend the HERMES and JLab \(b_1\) measurements to antiquark tensor polarization. The paper enables future experimental proposals at J-PARC once the high-momentum beamline becomes operational.
