# Self-consistent account for phonon induced corrections to quadrupole moments of odd nuclei. Pole and non-pole diagrams

> Recent results of the description of quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei are briefly reviewed. They are based on the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems with account for the phonon-particle coupling (PC) effects. The self-consistent model for describing the PC effects was developed previously for magnetic moments. Account for the non-pole diagrams is an important ingredient of this model. In addition to previously reported results for the odd In and Sb isotopes, which are the proton-odd neighbors of even tin nuclei, we present new results for odd Bi isotopes, the odd neighbors of even lead isotopes. In general, account for the PC corrections makes the agreement with the experimental data significantly better.

## Key claim

The paper claims that explicitly calculating only the fluctuating part of phonon-particle coupling (PC) corrections, those that depend non-regularly on the nucleus and single-particle state, significantly improves agreement with experimental quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei, but this improvement is conditional on the cancellation of singular terms between the Z-factor renormalization and the induced interaction, and on the inclusion of non-pole diagrams for the phonon quadrupole moment contribution.

## Method

The authors use the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) based on the Fayans energy density functional (EDF) DF3-a as the generator of the self-consistent basis. They compute PC corrections to quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei in the \(g_L^2\) approximation, where \(g_L\) is the vertex for creating the \(2^+_1\) phonon, considering four diagrammatic contributions: the Z-factor renormalization of the ends, the induced interaction (GGD diagram), the phonon quadrupole moment (GDD diagram including its non-pole counterpart), and the non-diagonal end correction. The model is applied to odd In and Sb isotopes (proton-odd neighbors of even Sn) and new results are presented for odd Bi isotopes (proton-odd neighbors of even Pb).

## Key numbers

- For the sample of 18 In and Sb nuclei, the rms deviation between theoretical and experimental quadrupole moments decreases from \(<\delta Q>_{\text{rms}} = 0.27\) b without PC corrections to \(<\delta \tilde{Q}>_{\text{rms}} = 0.16\) b with PC corrections.
- The \(2^+_1\) phonon excitation energy in \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) is \(\omega_2^{\text{th}} = 4.747\) MeV (theoretical) and \(\omega_2^{\text{exp}} = 4.086\) MeV (experimental), much higher than in lighter Pb isotopes where \(\omega_2 < 1\) MeV.
- For \(^{209}\text{Bi}\), the Z-factor is \(Z = 0.996\) and the total PC correction \(\delta Q_{\text{ph}} = -0.001\) b, confirming negligible contribution from the \(2^+_1\) phonon.
- For \(^{111}\text{In}\) (\(1g_{9/2}\) state), the Z-factor is \(Z = 0.488\) and the PC correction \(\delta Q_{\text{ph}} = -0.170\) b, changing the quadrupole moment from \(Q_{\text{th}} = +1.165\) b to \(\tilde{Q}_{\text{th}} = +0.99\) b (experiment: \(+0.80(2)\) b).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent model previously developed for magnetic moments (Saperstein et al., 2015-2016) to quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei. It builds on the mean-field calculations using the Fayans EDF DF3-a that successfully described magnetic moments (Tolokonnikov et al., 2011-2013) and quadrupole moments (Saperstein et al., 2016-2017) without PC corrections. The approach contrasts with the strategy of Fayans et al., who designed EDF parameters to implicitly include PC effects on average. The results enable future inclusion of contributions from \(3^-\) phonons and motivate experimental clarification of Bi isotope quadrupole moments.
