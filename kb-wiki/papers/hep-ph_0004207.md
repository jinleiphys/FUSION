# Low Energy Theory for 2 flavors at High Density QCD

> We construct the effective Lagrangian describing the low energy excitations for Quantum Chromodynamics with two flavors at high density. The non-linear realization framework is employed to properly construct the low energy effective theory. The light degrees of freedom, as required by 't Hooft anomaly conditions, contain massless fermions which we properly include in the effective Lagrangian. We also provide a discussion of the linearly realized Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The authors construct the low-energy effective Lagrangian for two-flavor Quantum Chromodynamics at high density using the non-linear realization framework, but the effective theory is valid only for energies much smaller than the gap parameter \(\Delta\), and the construction explicitly includes massless fermions required by 't Hooft anomaly conditions.

## Method

The authors employ the non-linear realization method (Callan-Coleman-Wess-Zumino formalism) to parameterize the coset space \(G/H\) where \(G = SU(3) \times U_V(1)\) is the full symmetry group and \(H = SU(2) \times \widetilde{U}_V(1)\) is the unbroken subgroup after diquark condensation. They construct the Maurer-Cartan one-form from the Goldstone fields, decompose it into parallel and perpendicular parts relative to the unbroken subgroup, and write the most general two-derivative invariant Lagrangian. Fermions are introduced via a field redefinition \(\widetilde{\psi} = \mathcal{V}^\dagger \psi\) that transforms under the unbroken subgroup, and a Majorana mass term is added. The Lagrangian is then generalized to account for broken Lorentz invariance at finite density by allowing different coefficients for temporal and spatial indices.

## Key numbers

* The gap parameter \(\Delta \sim \frac{\mu}{g^5} e^{-\alpha/g}\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential, \(g\) is the coupling constant, and \(\alpha\) is a calculable constant.
* The hierarchy of scales at high density: \(\Delta \ll m_{\text{gluon}} \ll \mu\), with the dynamically generated gluon mass \(m_{\text{gluon}} \sim g\mu\).
* In the linearly realized Lagrangian at dimension four, the squared gluon mass ratio between the eighth gluon and the other massive gluons is predicted to be \(m_8^2 / m_i^2 = 4/3\) (with \(i = 4,5,6,7\)).
* In the non-linear realization, the squared gluon mass ratio becomes \(m_8^2 / m_i^2 = (1/3)(\widetilde{v}^2 / v^2)\), and using one-loop perturbation theory from Rischke (2000) gives \(a_2 \approx 1/2\) (i.e., \(\widetilde{v} \approx \sqrt{2}\,v\)).

## Context

This work extends the non-linear realization approach previously applied to three-flavor QCD at high density by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999) to the two-flavor case, where 't Hooft anomaly conditions require the presence of massless fermions in the low-energy spectrum. The authors show that the non-linear realization yields a consistent derivative-counting scheme for the Goldstone boson terms, in contrast to the linearly realized Lagrangian where a dimension-six term must be added to match the perturbative gluon mass ratio calculated by Rischke (2000) and Schafer (2000). The effective theory enables future dynamical calculations of the low-energy coefficients at asymptotically high densities and provides a framework for studying the properties of dense quark matter relevant to neutron star interiors.
