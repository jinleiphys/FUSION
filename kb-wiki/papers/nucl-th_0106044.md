# Nucleon momentum distribution in deuteron and other nuclei within the light-front dynamics method

> The relativistic light-front dynamics (LFD) method has been shown to give a correct description of the most recent data for the deuteron monopole and quadrupole charge form factors obtained at the Jefferson Laboratory for elastic electron-deuteron scattering for six values of the squared momentum transfer between 0.66 and 1.7 (GeV/c)$^{2}$. The good agreement with the data is in contrast with the results of the existing non-relativistic approaches. In this work we firstly make a complementary test of the LFD applying it to calculate another important characteristic, the nucleon momentum distribution $n(q)$ of the deuteron using six invariant functions $f_{i}$ $(i=1,...,6)$ instead of two ($S$- and $D$-waves) in the nonrelativistic case. The comparison with the $y$-scaling data shows the decisive role of the function $f_{5}$ which at $q\geq$ 500 MeV/c exceeds all other $f$-functions (as well as the $S$- and $D$-waves) for the correct description of $n(q)$ of the deuteron in the high-momentum region. Comparison with other calculations using $S$- and $D$-waves corresponding to various nucleon-nucleon potentials is made. Secondly, using clear indications that the high-momentum components of $n(q)$ in heavier nuclei are related to those in the deuteron, we develop an approach within the natural orbital representation to calculate $n(q)$ in $(A,Z)$-nuclei on the basis of the deuteron momentum distribution. As examples, $n(q)$ in $^{4}$He, $^{12}$C and $^{56}$Fe are calculated and good agreement with the $y$-scaling data is obtained.

## Key claim

The light-front dynamics (LFD) method can simultaneously describe both the deuteron charge form factors (already shown to agree with Jefferson Lab data) and the nucleon momentum distribution, provided the six-component relativistic wave function (specifically the function \(f_{5}\), related mainly to pion exchange) is included; without \(f_{5}\), the high-momentum tail of \(n(q)\) for \(q>2\) fm\(^{-1}\) cannot be explained.

## Method

The authors calculated the angle-averaged nucleon momentum distribution \(n(q)\) of the deuteron using the six invariant functions \(f_i\) (\(i=1,\dots,6\)) from the covariant light-front dynamics formalism, with the kernel computed from a relativistic one-boson-exchange model (pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector mesons). They then extended this to heavier nuclei (\(^{4}\)He, \(^{12}\)C, \(^{56}\)Fe) by combining a mean-field (Woods-Saxon) hole-state contribution with the high-momentum particle-state part taken from the deuteron's \(f_5\) contribution, using the natural orbital representation of the one-body density matrix.

## Key numbers

* The LFD calculation of the deuteron momentum distribution agrees with \(y\)-scaling data for momenta \(q<2\) fm\(^{-1}\) using only the \(f_1\) and \(f_2\) functions, but requires the \(f_5\) function for \(q>2\) fm\(^{-1}\); the deviation of the total \(n(q)\) from the sum \(n_{12}=n_1+n_2\) starts at \(q\approx 1.8\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* The function \(f_5\) exceeds all other \(f\)-functions (and the non-relativistic \(S\)- and \(D\)-waves) at \(q\geq 500\) MeV/c.
* For the heavier nuclei, the scaling constant \(\alpha_A = N_A \cdot A/2\) takes values 8.335, 8.352, and 8.372 for \(^{4}\)He, \(^{12}\)C, and \(^{56}\)Fe, respectively.
* The LFD results for \(n(q)\) deviate strongly from those of non-relativistic NN potentials (e.g., Argonne v\(_{18}\), Nijmegen-II, Paris 1980) for \(q>3\) fm\(^{-1}\); the non-relativistic impulse approximation with Argonne v\(_{18}\) fails to describe the Jefferson Lab data on tensor polarization \(t_{20}\) and charge form factors \(G_C\) and \(G_Q\).

## Context

This work extends the successful LFD description of deuteron electromagnetic form factors (Abbott et al., 2000) to the nucleon momentum distribution, providing a complementary test of the method. It contrasts with non-relativistic potential approaches (e.g., Argonne v\(_{18}\) by Wiringa et al., 1995; Nijmegen potentials by Stoks et al., 1994; Paris 1980 by Lacombe et al., 1980) which, while describing \(n(q)\) up to \(q\approx 3\) fm\(^{-1}\), cannot simultaneously reproduce the charge form factors. The method for heavier nuclei builds on the observation by Faralli et al. (2000) that high-momentum tails of \(n_A(q)\) are rescaled versions of the deuteron distribution, and on the natural orbital representation framework of Stoitsov et al. (1993). It enables practical calculations of momentum distributions for any \((A,Z)\) nucleus using only mean-field wave functions and the deuteron's \(f_5\) component.
