# Fluctuations of Conserved Quantities in High Energy Nuclear Collisions at RHIC

> Fluctuations of conserved quantities in heavy-ion collisions are used to probe the phase transition and the QCD critical point for the strongly interacting hot and dense nuclear matter. The STAR experiment has carried out moment analysis of net-proton (proxy for net-baryon (B)), net-kaon (proxy for net-strangeness (S)), and net-charge (Q). These measurements are important for understanding the quantum chromodynamics phase diagram. We present the analysis techniques used in the moment analysis by the STAR experiment and discuss the moments of net-proton and net-charge distributions from the first phase of the Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

# Fluctuations of Conserved Quantities in High Energy Nuclear Collisions at RHIC

## Key claim
The STAR experiment observes the most significant deviation of skewness (S) and kurtosis (κ) of net-proton distributions from Skellam (Poisson) expectations at collision energies of 19.6 and 27 GeV for central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions, but the statistical errors are large at the lowest energies (7.7 and 11.5 GeV), preventing a definitive conclusion about a non-monotonic signal of the QCD critical point.

## Method
The STAR experiment measured event-by-event moments (up to fourth order) of net-proton (proton minus antiproton, as a proxy for net-baryon) and net-charge multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from the first phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. Protons and antiprotons were identified using ionization energy loss in the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) within transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c and mid-rapidity |y| < 0.5, while charged particles for net-charge were measured within 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c and |η| < 0.5. The analysis applied centrality bin width correction, novel centrality determination to avoid auto-correlation effects, and efficiency correction with error estimation based on the Delta theorem.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies studied: √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions
- Net-proton kinematic acceptance: transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c, mid-rapidity |y| < 0.5
- Net-charge kinematic acceptance: transverse momentum 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c, pseudo-rapidity |η| < 0.5
- Most significant deviation of net-proton S and κ from Skellam expectation observed at 19.6 and 27 GeV for 0-5% central collisions
- Resonance decay effects on net-proton kurtosis (κσ²) estimated at the 2% level based on hadron resonance gas model calculations
- Statistical error of cumulants scales as Δ(Cn) ~ O(σⁿ), where σ is the width of the distribution
- BES-II (planned for 2018) will increase luminosity by 3-10 times using stochastic electron cooling

## Context
This work extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, which systematically varies collision energy to probe the QCD phase diagram across a range of baryon chemical potentials (μB). The measurements of conserved quantity fluctuations (net-baryon, net-charge, net-strangeness) are motivated by theoretical predictions that higher moments are sensitive to the correlation length near the QCD critical point and are directly connected to susceptibilities computed in lattice QCD. The results are compared to UrQMD transport model calculations (which show monotonic energy dependence without a critical point) and to Skellam/Poisson expectations for uncorrelated particle production. The second phase of the Beam Energy Scan (BES-II) starting in 2018 will provide higher statistics at energies below 20 GeV, with detector upgrades including the inner TPC (iTPC) and Event Plane Detector (EPD) to improve tracking efficiency and centrality determination.
