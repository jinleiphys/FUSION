# Probing the fission properties of neutron-rich actinides with the astrophysical $r$ process

> We review recent work examining the influence of fission in rapid neutron capture ($r$-process) nucleosynthesis which can take place in astrophysical environments. We briefly discuss the impact of uncertain fission barriers and fission rates on the population of heavy actinide species. We demonstrate the influence of the fission fragment distributions for neutron-rich nuclei and discuss currently available treatments, including recent macroscopic-microscopic calculations. We conclude by comparing our nucleosynthesis results directly with stellar data for metal-poor stars rich in $r$-process elements to consider whether fission plays a role in the so-called `universality' of $r$-process abundances observed from star to star.

## Key claim

Fission may explain the observed "universality" of r-process abundances in metal-poor stars, but this connection requires careful qualification: the universality observed among lanthanide elements relative to each other does not require fission participation, whereas the co-production of light precious metals (such as silver and palladium) with lanthanides via late-time fission deposition could account for the flat trends seen in stellar [Ag/Eu] and [Pd/Eu] ratios.

## Method

The authors performed r-process nucleosynthesis calculations using the LANL suite of Hauser-Feshbach codes with four distinct theoretical fission barrier sets, applying fission rates for neutron-induced, beta-delayed, and spontaneous fission channels. They compared fission fragment distributions from the macroscopic-microscopic FRLDM approach against data-driven phenomenological models (GEF2016, Kodama and Takahashi, and 50/50 symmetric splits) for multiple astrophysical conditions including merger tidal-tail ejecta and accretion disk wind ejecta. The resulting elemental abundance patterns were compared directly with observational data from r-process enhanced, metal-poor stars (r-I and r-II stars) from the JINAbase stellar database.

## Key numbers

- Fission fragment distributions can lead to differences as large as an order of magnitude in r-process abundances depending on the yield prescription used
- The order of magnitude threshold at which r-process abundances are sensitive to fission yields is at the 0.1% level; calculations cutting off yields at the 1% level produce different abundance results
- Spontaneous fission of the long-lived species 254Cf has the potential to impact merger light curves on the order of 10 to 100 days
- Spontaneous fission rates become very low until reaching Z > 100 for the Karpov phenomenological formula and the Swiatecki power law fit
- The Xu and Ren parameterization predicts a very large region of fast spontaneous fission rates at Z greater than or approximately 94 just above the N = 184 predicted shell closure
- A spontaneous fission Q-value of approximately 200 MeV transfers heat to the astrophysical environment

## Context

This work reviews and extends prior studies on fission in r-process nucleosynthesis, building on the fission barrier comparisons of Vassh (2019) and the first prediction of multi-chance beta-delayed fission by Mumpower (2016). The authors demonstrate that the macroscopic-microscopic FRLDM fission yields from Mumpower (2018) produce wider fragment distributions than phenomenological models like GEF2016, enabling co-production of light precious metals (palladium, silver) with lanthanides. This co-production could explain the "universality" of r-process abundances observed by Sneden (2008) in metal-poor stars, though the authors caution that lanthanide-to-lanthanide ratios do not require fission. The work enables future multi-messenger studies of neutron star mergers, particularly regarding the 254Cf spontaneous fission heating signature in kilonova light curves.
