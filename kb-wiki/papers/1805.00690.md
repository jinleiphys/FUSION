# Marchenko method with incomplete data and singular nucleon scattering

> We apply the Marchenko method of quantum inverse scattering to study nucleon scattering problems. Assuming a $β/r^2$ type repulsive core and comparing our results to the Reid93 phenomenological potential we estimate the constant $β$, determining the singularity strength, in various spin/isospin channels. Instead of using Bargmann type S-matrices which allows only integer singularity strength, here we consider an analytical approach based on the incomplete data method, which is suitable for fractional singularity strengths as well.

## Key claim
The Marchenko inverse scattering method, combined with an extrapolation of phase shifts above 350 MeV LAB energy using a fixed singularity strength parameter ν, can reconstruct nucleon-nucleon potentials whose repulsive core behaves as β/r², and the optimal ν values are not necessarily integers despite earlier Bargmann-type analyses that forced integer values.

## Method
The authors applied the Marchenko quantum inverse scattering formalism, generalized to singular potentials, to reconstruct nucleon-nucleon potentials from experimental np scattering phase shifts up to 350 MeV LAB energy. They extrapolated the phase shifts to higher energies using a three-parameter asymptotic form constrained by the generalized Levinson's theorem, which relates the asymptotic phase shift to the singularity strength ν. The reconstructed potentials were compared to the Reid93 phenomenological potential in the 500-1000 MeV energy range to determine the best-fit ν values for each channel.

## Key numbers
- In the ¹S₀ channel, the best-fit singularity strength is ν = 2.0, corresponding to β₀ = 6.0.
- In the ³P₁ channel, the best-fit ν = 2.3, corresponding to β₀ = 5.6.
- In the ³P₀ channel, the best-fit ν = 3.2, corresponding to β₀ = 11.4.
- In the ³D₂ channel, the best-fit ν = 2.3, corresponding to β₀ = 1.6.
- The extrapolation boundary is at k₀ = 2.054 fm⁻¹, corresponding to 350 MeV LAB energy.
- The reduced mass for the np system is m = 469.459 MeV/c².

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Bargmann-type S-matrix analysis (Elbistan et al., 2017) which only allowed integer ν values, by using the more general Marchenko method that accommodates fractional singularity strengths. The study is motivated by lattice QCD results (Ishii, 2007) and perturbative QCD calculations showing a 1/r² behavior at short distances, as well as holographic QCD predictions. The results enable a more precise determination of the short-distance behavior of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and provide input for the systematic study of coupled channels such as ³S₁-³D₁, which the authors plan to address in a separate publication.
