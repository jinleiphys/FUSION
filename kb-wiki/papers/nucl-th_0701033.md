# Large-amplitude Qn-Qp collectivity in the neutron-rich oxygen isotope 20O

> By means of HFB calculations with independent constraints on axial neutron and proton quadrupole moments Q_n and Q_p, we investigate the large amplitude isoscalar and isovector deformation properties of the neutron-rich isotope 20O. Using the particle-number and angular-momentum projected Generator Coordinate Method, we analyze the collective dynamics in the {Q_n, Q_p} plane. The parameterization SLy4 of the Skyrme interaction is used for all calculations in connection with a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction. Our results show that already for this moderately neutron-rich nucleus the transition moments are modified when independent neutron and proton collective dynamics are allowed.

## Key claim
For the neutron-rich oxygen isotope 20O, allowing independent neutron and proton quadrupole deformations in the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) modifies transition moments compared to a one-dimensional constraint on the total mass quadrupole moment, even though the main features of the collective wave functions remain similar. The agreement with experimental data for the B(E2) value is not satisfactory, and the model would likely be improved by including states that break time-reversal invariance.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with independent constraints on the axial neutron and proton quadrupole moments (Q_n and Q_p) to generate a two-dimensional collective space. They then applied particle-number and angular-momentum projection, followed by the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) to mix these projected states and obtain the ground state and excited states for J=0 and J=2. The Skyrme interaction SLy4 was used for the particle-hole channel, with a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction.

## Key numbers
- The excitation energy of the lowest 2+ state calculated with the two-dimensional {Q_n, Q_p} space is 3.2 ± 0.2 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.7 MeV.
- The B(E2↑) value calculated with the two-dimensional {Q_n, Q_p} space is 180 ± 50 e² fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 28 ± 2 e² fm⁴.
- The ratio of neutron to proton transition matrix elements is M_n/M_p ≈ 2.1, which is greater than N/Z = 12/8 = 1.5.
- The energy gain from the full isospin dynamics (using the two-dimensional space versus the one-dimensional path) is about 800 keV for both the ground state and the lowest 2+ state.

## Context
This work extends mean-field investigations of collective dynamics in oxygen isotopes, which previously only considered excitations about the lowest configuration with zero mass quadrupole moment (Khan et al., 2000; 2002). It also builds on the projected GCM method applied to 16O by Bender et al. (2003). The results enable future studies of isovector quadrupole modes in neutron-rich nuclei, but the authors note that the variational space for the 2+ state needs to be enlarged by including states that break time-reversal invariance to improve agreement with experiment.
