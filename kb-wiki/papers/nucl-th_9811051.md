# Proton Elastic Scattering and Neutron Distribution of Unstable Nuclei

> We study theoretically how we can determine the neutron density distributions of unstable nuclei from proton elastic scattering. We apply the relativistic impulse model to study the sensitivities of the observables to the density distributionswhich are expressed in Woods-Saxon form. We find that the both radius and diffuseness of densities can be determined from restricted elastic scattering data in principle. We think this result is helpful to design future experiments.

# Proton Elastic Scattering and Neutron Distribution of Unstable Nuclei

## Key claim
The authors claim that both the radius and diffuseness parameters of neutron density distributions in unstable nuclei can be determined from only two experimental observables, the reaction cross section and the first dip angle of the differential cross section, provided these quantities can be measured accurately enough. This conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the proton density distribution is already known precisely, and the method is demonstrated for the specific cases of ⁴⁰Ca and ⁶⁰Ca using Woods-Saxon parameterizations.

## Method
The authors apply the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) to calculate Dirac optical potentials from nuclear density distributions, then solve the Dirac equation to obtain observables for proton elastic scattering. They use Woods-Saxon forms for neutron densities, varying the radius parameter r₀ and diffuseness parameter a, while fixing proton densities from relativistic mean field theory (RMFT). For ⁴⁰Ca, they generate contour plots of reaction cross section and first dip angle in the r₀–a plane and compare with experimental values (σ_R = 51 fm², θ_dip = 15.3 deg) to extract the neutron density. For ⁶⁰Ca, they use RMFT-calculated values (σ_R = 75.43 fm², θ_dip = 12.22 deg) as mock experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴⁰Ca at T = 300 MeV: experimental reaction cross section σ_R = 51 fm² and first dip angle θ_dip = 15.3 deg, with assumed 2% experimental errors.
- For ⁶⁰Ca: RMFT-calculated reaction cross section σ_R = 75.43 fm² and first dip angle θ_dip = 12.22 deg, used as simulated data.
- For ⁶⁰Ca neutron density: r₀ parameter fixed at 4.48 fm (half-density radius from RMFT), with a varied from 0.5 to 2.0 fm.
- For ⁴⁰Ca neutron density: r₀ varied from 3.2 to 4.3 fm and a varied from 0.1 to 0.8 fm.
- Estimated experimental errors for unstable nuclei: several percent for reaction cross sections and several milliradian for first dip angles, based on the case of ⁵⁶Ca (reference [14] in the paper).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of unstable nuclei using radioactive ion beams, where interaction cross sections and neutron-removal cross sections have been used to extract radii (references [1,2] in the paper). The authors specifically build on the finding that radii of both proton and neutron distributions can be extracted when charge distribution information is available (references [4,5]). The paper aims to go beyond previous work by determining not just radii but also the shape (diffuseness) of neutron densities, using only restricted elastic scattering data. This prescription is intended to help design future experiments with unstable nuclei where electron scattering is impractical and beam intensities are low. The relativistic impulse model used here was previously shown to describe proton-nucleus elastic scattering well for stable targets at intermediate energies (reference [9]).
