# Fluctuations around Bjorken Flow and the onset of turbulent phenomena

> We study how fluctuations in fluid dynamic fields can be dissipated or amplified within the characteristic spatio-temporal structure of a heavy ion collision. The initial conditions for a fluid dynamic evolution of heavy ion collisions may contain significant fluctuations in all fluid dynamical fields, including the velocity field and its vorticity components. We formulate and analyze the theory of local fluctuations around average fluid fields described by Bjorken's model. For conditions of laminar flow, when a linearized treatment of the dynamic evolution applies, we discuss explicitly how fluctuations of large wave number get dissipated while modes of sufficiently long wave-length pass almost unattenuated or can even be amplified. In the opposite case of large Reynold's numbers (which is inverse to viscosity), we establish that (after suitable coordinate transformations) the dynamics is governed by an evolution equation of non-relativistic Navier-Stokes type that becomes essentially two-dimensional at late times. One can then use the theory of Kolmogorov and Kraichnan for an explicit characterization of turbulent phenomena in terms of the wave-mode dependence of correlations of fluid dynamic fields. We note in particular that fluid dynamic correlations introduce characteristic power-law dependences in two-particle correlation functions.

## Key claim
The paper establishes that fluctuations in all fluid dynamic fields (including vorticity) around a Bjorken flow background can be treated analytically, and that under conditions of large Reynolds numbers the dynamics becomes effectively two-dimensional and turbulent at late times, introducing characteristic power-law dependences in two-particle correlation functions.

## Method
The authors formulate linearized equations of motion for fluctuations in energy density and flow velocity around a Bjorken expanding background, solving them analytically for laminar flow conditions. For the turbulent regime at large Reynolds numbers, they perform coordinate transformations to map the relativistic equations onto non-relativistic Navier-Stokes type evolution that becomes effectively two-dimensional at late times, allowing application of Kolmogorov-Kraichnan turbulence theory. They also construct a Monte Carlo Glauber model with wounded nucleons to generate fluctuating initial conditions in all fluid fields.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s is related to Reynolds numbers that are "typically larger than unity" but "Re < O(100)" in heavy ion collisions
- Bjorken energy density scales as ε_Bj(τ) = ε_Bj(τ0) (τ0/τ)^(4/3) for ideal equation of state ε = 3p
- Bjorken temperature scales as T_Bj(τ) = T_Bj(τ0) (τ0/τ)^(1/3)
- The ratio ν_Bj(τ)/τ = ν_Bj(τ0)/τ0 (τ0/τ)^(2/3) decreases with proper time
- Smearing parameter σ = 0.4 fm used for initial energy density fluctuations in the Monte Carlo Glauber model
- Average transverse velocity width ⟨|v|⟩ = 0.1 c used for generating fluctuating initial flow fields

## Context
This work extends the discussion of fluctuating initial conditions in heavy ion collisions beyond the standard focus on initial density inhomogeneities, treating fluctuations in all fluid fields democratically. It builds on the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1982) and responds to the suggestion by Mishra et al. (2008) and Mocsy and Sorensen (2010) that harmonic flow coefficients v_n might provide information about the initial state similar to CMB power spectra. The paper contrasts with the CMB case where Hubble expansion dampens vorticity fluctuations, showing that in Bjorken-type expansion, small fluctuations can become seeds of turbulent behavior, limiting the applicability of linear treatments. This enables a framework for understanding when and how turbulence may onset in heavy ion collisions, complementing event-by-event simulations by groups such as Holopainen et al. (2010) and Schenke et al. (2010).
