# Short-range production of three bottom mesons

> Previous investigations of the three-body dynamics of $B$ mesons have shown that no Efimov effect arises in systems composed of three $B$ and $B^*$ mesons. This implies that the properties of such three-body systems can be described reliably within nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) with short-range interactions using only two-body input, as three-body forces are strongly suppressed. In this work, we present leading-order predictions for the three-body point production rates of systems consisting of three $B$ and $B^*$ mesons. These predictions provide a novel way to experimentally probe the $B^{(*)}$-$\bar{B}^{(*)}$ interactions, which play a crucial role in the hadronic-molecule interpretation of the $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10610)$ and $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10650)$ states. Moreover, they provide a way to test the approximate conformal symmetry predicted for such systems at low energies experimentally.

## Key claim
The authors present leading-order predictions for short-range production rates of three-bottom-meson systems, arguing that the absence of the Efimov effect in these systems allows parameter-free predictions within nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) using only two-body input. The key hedge is that the NREFT framework becomes marginal for the $ZB$ system (where $\gamma/M_\pi \approx 1$) and is used as a phenomenological model, while the $Z^\prime B^*$ system ($\gamma^\prime/M_\pi \approx 0.5$) is more reliably described.

## Method
The authors employ a nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) with short-range contact interactions at leading order, treating the $Z$ and $Z^\prime$ states as shallow $S$-wave hadronic molecules (dimers) of $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$, respectively. They solve Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equations for the short-range production amplitudes in both single-channel ($ZB$, $Z^\prime B^*$) and coupled-channel ($ZB^*$ and $Z^\prime B$) configurations, then construct the three-body production amplitudes by combining dimer-particle amplitudes with dimer propagators and transition vertices. The production rate functions are obtained by integrating the squared amplitudes over the three-body phase space.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies adopted: $E_B = 5$ MeV for the $Z$ state and $E_B^\prime = 1$ MeV for the $Z^\prime$ state, taken from Cleven (2011).
- Binding momenta: $\gamma = 162$ MeV for the $Z$ state and $\gamma^\prime = 72$ MeV for the $Z^\prime$ state, using bottom-meson masses $M_B = 5279.41$ MeV and $M_{B^*} = 5324.75$ MeV from the Particle Data Group (2024).
- Mass ratio parameter: $r = M_B/M_{B^*} = 0.991485$ for the $Z$ dimer and $r^\prime = 1$ for the $Z^\prime$ dimer.
- Ratio $\gamma^\prime/M_\pi \approx 0.5$ for the $Z^\prime B^*$ system, indicating reasonable applicability of pionless EFT; $\gamma/M_\pi \approx 1$ for the $ZB$ system, indicating marginal applicability.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of three $B$ meson systems by Lin (2017), which found no Efimov effect in these systems, to instead investigate unparticle behavior through short-range production. The approach builds on the unparticle concept proposed by Georgi (2007) and its nonrelativistic realizations in nuclear and hadronic reactions by Hammer (2021), Braaten (2021), and Braaten (2023). The predictions provide a way to experimentally probe the $B^{(*)}-\bar{B}^{(*)}$ interactions relevant for the hadronic-molecule interpretation of the $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10610)$ and $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10650)$ states, and to test the approximate conformal symmetry predicted for these systems at low energies.
