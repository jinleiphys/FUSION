# Synergy between NP and HEP research goals and efforts in fundamental symmetries and interactions

> The aim of this white paper is to highlight several areas for which the Department of Energy's Office of Nuclear Physics has primary stewardship or significant investment and expertise, and for which there is also significant interest and expertise within the HEP community. These areas of overlap offer exciting opportunities for collaboration.

## Key claim

This white paper argues that there are multiple areas of overlap between Nuclear Physics (NP) and High Energy Physics (HEP) research in fundamental symmetries where collaborative efforts can accelerate progress, but the specific opportunities are limited to four main areas identified during the 2021 Snowmass process: neutrinoless double beta decay, the neutron electric dipole moment, tests of CKM unitarity, and lepton-nucleus scattering, with additional mention of searches for scalar and tensor interactions and neutron-antineutron oscillations.

## Method

The authors summarize the physics goals, open challenges, and collaborative opportunities across four areas identified through the 2021 Snowmass process, drawing on four joint NP-HEP contributions and associated topical group reports. They describe how lattice QCD and effective field theory methods serve as common theoretical tools connecting experimental results to beyond-the-standard-model physics, and they outline the specific experimental programs and theoretical calculations needed in each area.

## Key numbers

- The SNS EDM experiment at Oak Ridge is designed to reach a neutron electric dipole moment sensitivity of \(d_n \sim 3 \times 10^{-28}\) e-cm
- A \(\sim 3\sigma\) tension with the Standard Model has been revealed in tests of CKM unitarity from superallowed beta decays and kaon decays
- Lattice QCD has achieved permille accuracy on the form factor \(f_+(0)\) and on the ratio \(f_{K^+}/f_{\pi^+}\) needed for extracting \(V_{us}\) and \(V_{us}/V_{ud}\)
- The current limit on the free neutron oscillation time is \(\tau_{N \overline N} \gtrsim 10^8\) seconds, corresponding to new physics scales of \(10^2-10^3\) TeV
- Future measurements of the neutron lifetime with uncertainty \(\Delta\tau_n \sim 0.1\) seconds and of the ratio \(\lambda = g_A/g_V\) with uncertainty \(\Delta\lambda/|\lambda|\sim 0.03\%\) will allow extraction of \(V_{ud}\) from neutron decay with accuracy comparable to superallowed beta decay

## Context

This white paper extends the collaborative framework established during the 2021 Snowmass process, building on four joint contributions (USQCD 2022, Ruso 2022, Alarcon 2022, Cirigliano 2022) and associated topical group reports (Davoudi 2022, deGouvea 2022, Blum 2022). It identifies specific areas where the DOE Office of Nuclear Physics has primary stewardship or significant investment that overlap with HEP community interests, enabling future joint efforts in experimental programs such as DUNE, the SNS EDM experiment, LEGEND-1000, CUPID, and nEXO, as well as theoretical developments in lattice QCD and effective field theory.
