# Color superconductivity: Continuity of quark and hadron matter, the role of the strange quark mass, and perturbative results

> We summarize some recent results on the structure of QCD at very high baryon density.

## Key claim
The paper argues that in QCD with three degenerate light flavors, the high-density color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase and the low-density nuclear phase may be continuously connected without an intervening phase transition, but this continuity depends on the strange quark mass being small enough that the Fermi surface mismatch does not destroy pairing.

## Method
The author analyzes the structure of the CFL order parameter and its symmetry breaking pattern, then studies the effect of a nonzero strange quark mass on pairing between light and strange quarks. The gap equation is solved in the Eliashberg formalism including both electric and magnetic gluon exchanges in the hard dense loop approximation, using the one-loop running coupling constant.

## Key numbers
- The superconducting gap from instanton-induced interactions is expected to be \(\Delta\simeq 100\) MeV at densities \(\rho\sim 5\rho_0\).
- The quark constituent mass in the CFL phase is \(\Sigma\simeq 10\) MeV, significantly smaller than the gap \(\Delta\simeq 50\) MeV.
- The critical condition for strange-light quark pairing is \(\Delta > m_s^2/(2\mu)\).
- The perturbative gap scales as \(\Delta \simeq c\mu g^{-5}\exp(-3\pi^2/(\sqrt{2}g))\) with \(c\simeq 256\pi^4\).
- For \(\mu<500\) MeV, the gap reaches \(\Delta\simeq 100\) MeV, though the coupling \(g\simeq (2-4)\) makes this result unreliable.

## Context
This work extends the earlier discovery by Bailin and Love (1984) that QCD at large density is a color superconductor, and the subsequent realization by Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998) and Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) that the gap could be large. The paper builds on the CFL phase proposed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and the continuity argument with Schafer and Wilczek (1998). It enables future work on whether realistic neutron star densities fall in the CFL phase, which requires accurate estimates of the gap and dynamically generated strange quark mass.
