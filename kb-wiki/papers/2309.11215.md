# Critical Point from Shock Waves Solution in Relativistic Anisotropic Hydrodynamics

> Solutions of shock waves in anisotropic relativistic hydrodynamics in the absence of refraction of the flow passing through the shock wave are considered. The existence of a critical value of the anisotropy parameter is shown. This value is the upper limit below which an adequate description of shock waves is possible. Shock wave solutions also provide a mechanism for system isotropization.

# Critical Point from Shock Waves Solution in Relativistic Anisotropic Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Shock wave solutions in relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics exist only when the anisotropy parameter ξ is below a critical value ξ_crit(α) that depends on the polar angle α; above this critical value, the solutions become discontinuous in the isotropic limit and exhibit rarefaction shock wave behavior, making an adequate description impossible.

## Method

The author derives shock wave solutions from the energy-momentum conservation matching conditions across a discontinuity surface, using the Romatschke-Strickland form of the anisotropic distribution function with a one-dimensional anisotropy parameter ξ. The key assumption is that the polar angle α of the flow relative to the beam axis remains unchanged across the shock (α' = α), which eliminates flow refraction. The system of three equations for the downstream velocity v' and anisotropy ξ' is solved as a function of the input parameters σ (pressure ratio), α, and upstream ξ, with particular attention to the boundary cases α = 0 (longitudinal) and α = π/2 (transverse).

## Key numbers

- Critical anisotropy parameter for transverse shock waves (α = π/2): ξ_crit ≈ 2.62143, obtained from the equation ∂R_∥/∂ξ = ∂R_⊥/∂ξ at σ = 1.
- Critical anisotropy parameter for longitudinal shock waves (α = 0): ξ_crit ≈ 5.47941.
- For purely longitudinal expansion with zero initial anisotropy, the maximum anisotropy ξ_max = ξ_crit(π/2) ≈ 2.62143 corresponds to shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/S ≈ 0.17179.
- For purely longitudinal expansion with zero initial anisotropy, ξ_max = ξ_crit(0) ≈ 5.47941 corresponds to η/S ≈ 0.35619.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analytical solutions for longitudinal and transverse shock waves in anisotropic hydrodynamics with constant anisotropy (Kovalenko 2022), which assumed ξ' = ξ and allowed flow deflection. By instead fixing α' = α, the present study recovers the more familiar behavior of isotropic shock waves while revealing a critical anisotropy bound. The results connect to the broader program of relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics developed by Strickland, Florkowski, and collaborators (Strickland; Florkowski 2013), and to earlier work on shock waves in quark-gluon matter by Rischke (1995) and Dumitru (1999). The critical point provides a constraint on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/S for which shock wave formation is possible throughout the system's evolution, enabling estimates of transport properties from shock observables.
