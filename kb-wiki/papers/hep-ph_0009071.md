# The Similarity Renormalization Group

> Quantum field theories require a cutoff to regulate divergences that result from local interactions, and yet physical results can not depend on the value of this cutoff. The renormalization group employs a transformation that changes the cutoff to isolate hamiltonians that produce cutoff-independent eigenvalues. The similarity renormalization group is based on similarity transformations that regulate off-diagonal matrix elements, forcing the hamiltonian towards a band-diagonal form as the cutoff is lowered. This avoids pathologies that plagued tradition transformations acting on hamiltonians, making it possible to produce a well-behaved perturbative approximation of renormalized hamiltonians in asymptotically free theories. We employ a simple two-dimensional delta function example to illustrate this new renormalization technique.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) provides a unitary transformation that runs a cutoff on energy differences rather than on individual states, thereby avoiding the small-energy-denominator pathologies that plagued earlier Hamiltonian renormalization schemes such as the Bloch transformation used by Wilson (1965, 1970). The authors illustrate the method with a two-dimensional delta-function potential, showing that perturbative SRG calculations contain three interdependent sources of error (truncation in momentum expansion, truncation in the running coupling, and approximation of the running coupling itself) that scale as inverse powers of logarithms of the cutoff.

## Method

The authors apply Wegner's formulation of the SRG, defined by the flow equation \(dH_s/ds = [H_s, [H_s, h]]\) with flow parameter \(s = 1/\lambda^4\), to a nonrelativistic two-body system in two dimensions with an attractive delta-function potential. They solve the flow equation perturbatively for the reduced interaction \(\bar{V}_s(p,p')\) expanded in powers of the running coupling \(\alpha_s\), using coupling coherence to fix counterterms, and then compute the bound-state energy and on-shell T-matrix from the renormalized Hamiltonian truncated at various orders.

## Key numbers

* The exact bound-state energy for the two-dimensional delta-function potential is given by \(E_0 = \Lambda^2 / (e^{4\pi/\alpha_0} - 1)\), where \(\Lambda\) is a momentum cutoff and \(\alpha_0\) is the bare coupling.
* The renormalized running coupling that keeps the binding energy fixed as the cutoff is removed is \(\alpha_\Lambda = 4\pi / \ln(1 + \Lambda^2/E_0)\).
* The exact on-shell T-matrix for S-wave scattering is \(T_0(k) = -2 / [\ln(k^2/E_0) - i\pi]\).
* The exact S-wave phase shift satisfies \(\cot \delta_0 = (1/\pi) \ln(k^2/E_0)\).
* The Wegner similarity function is \(f_{sij} = e^{-s \Delta_{ij}^2}\) with \(\Delta_{ij} = \epsilon_i - \epsilon_j\), and the relation between the flow parameter and momentum cutoff is \(s = 1/\lambda^4\).

## Context

This work extends the SRG formalism originally developed by Glazek and Wilson (1993, 1994) and independently by Wegner (1994), applying it to a simple two-dimensional delta-function potential that exhibits logarithmic ultraviolet divergences analogous to those in QED and QCD. The pedagogical example demonstrates dimensional transmutation (Coleman, 1973) and asymptotic freedom, and the error analysis shows how perturbative SRG calculations produce inverse-logarithmic errors in addition to power-law errors, which would be eliminated in a realistic calculation by fitting the coupling to data.
