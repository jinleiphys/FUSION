# Isospin Effect on the Process of Multifragmentation and Dissipation at Intermediate Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> In the simulation of intermediate energy heavy ion collisions by using the isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics, the isospin effect on the process of multifragmentation and dissipation has been studied. It is found that the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments $N_{imf}$ for the neutron-poor colliding system is always larger than that for the neutron-rich system, while the quadrupole of single particle momentum distribution $Q_{zz}$ for the neutron-poor colliding system is smaller than that of the neutron-rich system for all projectile-target combinations studied at the beam energies from about 50MeV/nucleon to 150MeV/nucleon. Since $Q_{zz}$ depends strongly on isospin dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and weakly on symmetry potential at the above beam energies, it may serve as a good probe to extract the information on the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section. The correlation between the multiplicity $N_{imf}$ of intermediate mass fragments and the total numer of charged particles $N_c$ has the behavior similar to $Q_{zz}$, which can be used as a complementary probe to the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section.

# Isospin Effect on the Process of Multifragmentation and Dissipation at Intermediate Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The quadrupole of single particle momentum distribution $Q_{zz}$ depends strongly on the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and weakly on the symmetry potential for beam energies from about 50 MeV/nucleon to 150 MeV/nucleon, making it a good probe for extracting information on the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section; however, this conclusion holds specifically for reversible kinetic reactions with heavy projectiles on light targets and for small impact parameters.

## Method
The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, which includes a density-dependent mean field with symmetry potential and Coulomb potential, an isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section with a density-dependent reduction factor, and Pauli blocking that distinguishes neutrons and protons. They compared two pairs of reversible reaction systems with the same projectile and target masses but different neutron-to-proton ratios: $^{120}_{48}Cd+^{40}_{18}Ar$ (neutron-rich) versus $^{120}_{54}Xe+^{40}_{20}Ca$ (neutron-poor), and $^{76}_{30}Zn+^{40}_{18}Ar$ (neutron-rich) versus $^{76}_{36}Kr+^{40}_{20}Ca$ (neutron-poor), at beam energies from 15 MeV/nucleon to 200 MeV/nucleon and various impact parameters. Clusters were constructed using an isospin-dependent coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff $p_0 = 300$ MeV/c and relative distance cutoff $R_0 = 3.5$ fm.

## Key numbers
- The multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments $N_{imf}$ for neutron-poor systems is always larger than for neutron-rich systems at beam energies above 50 MeV/nucleon for all projectile-target combinations studied.
- $Q_{zz}$ for neutron-poor systems is smaller than for neutron-rich systems at beam energies from 50 MeV/nucleon to 150 MeV/nucleon for all reversible colliding systems studied.
- The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section uses the empirical expression $\sigma^{med}_{NN} = (1 + \alpha \frac{\rho}{\rho_0}) \sigma^{free}_{NN}$ with $\alpha \approx -0.2$, where the free neutron-proton cross section is about three times larger than the free proton-proton or neutron-neutron cross section below 300 MeV.
- The symmetry potential strength $c$ is taken as 32 MeV, with three forms: $F_1(u) = u$, $F_2(u) = u^2$, and $F_3(u) = u^{1/2}$, where $u \equiv \rho/\rho_0$.
- Intermediate mass fragments are defined with charge number $Z = 3$ to 13 for heavy systems and $Z = 3$ to 8 for medium mass systems.

## Context
This work extends the isospin-dependent transport theory studies by Li et al. (2000) on nuclear symmetry energy and by Pak and Li (2000) on collective flow and balance energy, which showed that isospin dependence of these observables originates mainly from the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section. The authors had previously found in Liu et al. (2000) that nuclear stopping can probe the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, but it was unclear how stopping depends on the neutron-proton ratio. This paper demonstrates that $Q_{zz}$ increases slightly with increasing neutron-proton ratio, and establishes the correlation between $N_{imf}$ and the total number of charged particles $N_c$ as a complementary probe. The results enable experimental extraction of the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section using multifragmentation observables in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions.
