# Examination of $N^*(1535)$ as a probe to observe the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter

> We investigate modifications of mass and decay width of $N^*(1535)$ in nuclear matter in a chiral symmetric way. The nucleon and $N^*(1535)$ are introduced by a parity doublet model, and nuclear matter is constructed by one-loop diagrams of the nucleon and $N^*(1535)$. The decay width of $N^*(1535)$ is studied respecting chiral symmetry. Our calculations show that the partial width of $Γ_{N^*\to Nπ}$ is slightly broadened by a collisional broadening, and that of $Γ_{N^*\to Nη}$ is drastically suppressed at density. As a result, the total decay width $Γ_{\rm tot}$ gets small at density. These modifications, especially the drastic narrowing of partial width of $Γ_{N^*\to Nη}$, together with the dropping of mass of $N^*(1535)$ provide experiments for observing the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter by means of $N^*(1535)$ resonance with useful information.

## Key claim

The paper claims that modifications of the mass and decay width of \(N^*(1535)\) in nuclear matter, particularly the drastic suppression of the partial width \(\Gamma_{N^*\to N\eta}\) and the dropping of the \(N^*(1535)\) mass, can serve as experimental probes for observing the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the chiral invariant mass parameter \(m_0\) (values of 500, 700, and 900 MeV are tested) and on the assumption that \(N^*(1535)\) is the chiral partner of the nucleon within the parity doublet model.

## Method

The author constructs a parity doublet model including nucleon and \(N^*(1535)\) fields, \(\sigma\) and \(\pi\) mesons, and an \(\eta\) meson as a chiral singlet, with parameters fixed by vacuum properties (masses, decay widths, axial charge) and nuclear matter properties (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility). Nuclear matter is described at one-loop level for nucleons and \(N^*(1535)\) with mean fields for \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) mesons, and the gap equation for the \(\sigma\) mean field is solved to obtain density-dependent masses. Fluctuations of \(\pi\) and \(\eta\) mesons are then incorporated to compute the in-medium self-energies and decay widths of \(N^*(1535)\) into \(N\pi\) and \(N\eta\) channels.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum masses used as inputs: nucleon mass \(m_+^{\rm vac} = 939\) MeV, \(N^*(1535)\) mass \(m_-^{\rm vac} = 1535\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV, \(\eta\) meson mass \(m_\eta = 547\) MeV, \(\omega\) meson mass \(m_\omega = 783\) MeV.
- Vacuum decay widths: \(\Gamma_{N^*\to N\pi} \sim 75\) MeV and \(\Gamma_{N^*\to N\eta} \sim 75\) MeV, with total width \(\Gamma_{\rm tot} \sim 150\) MeV.
- Nuclear matter inputs: saturation density \(\rho_B^* = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(E/A - m_+^{\rm vac} = -16\) MeV, incompressibility \(K = 240\) MeV.
- The chiral invariant mass \(m_0\) is varied as 500, 700, and 900 MeV; for \(m_0 = 500\) MeV, the mean field \(\sigma_0\) decreases with density, and the mass difference between \(N^*(1535)\) and the nucleon becomes smaller as density increases.
- The cutoff parameter for the form factor is \(\Lambda = 300\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model of Motohiro (2015), which reproduced nuclear matter properties at mean field level, by including fluctuations of mesons to study in-medium modifications of \(N^*(1535)\). It builds on prior studies of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter using effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (reviewed by Hatsuda (1994)) and on investigations of \(N^*(1535)\) in medium by Ghosh (2014), Kim (1998), and Ohtani (2016). The results are relevant for experiments on \(\eta\) mesic nuclei, first proposed by Haider and Liu (1986), and for photo-production experiments on nuclei by Yorita (2000), Maruyama (2002), and Lehr (2003). The work enables future experimental searches for partial chiral symmetry restoration using \(N^*(1535)\) resonance properties in nuclear matter.
