# $Ω^-$ production as a probe of equation of state of dense matter near the QCD phase transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The production of doubly strange hyperon $Ξ^-$ and trebly strange hyperon $Ω^-$ in relativistic Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 4.2 GeV is explored based on a relativistic transport model that is interweaved with hadronic mean-field potentials for heavy-ion collisions. Upon comparison, it appears that relative to the double strangeness observable $Ξ^-$, the yield and collective flows of the triple strange $Ω^-$ exhibit a higher sensitivity to the equation of state (EoS) of dense matter. This characteristic makes the $Ω^-$ an essential observable for studying the properties of densely formed matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The trebly strange hyperon $\Omega^-$ shows higher sensitivity to the equation of state (EoS) of dense nuclear matter than the doubly strange $\Xi^-$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but this conclusion is based on varying the incompressibility coefficient $K_0$ between 200 and 400 MeV (soft vs. stiff EoS) without aiming to constrain the actual EoS, and the stiff EoS with $K_0 = 400$ MeV may violate causality at high baryon density.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT-HC transport model, a pure hadron cascade version of the Multi-Phase Transport model with hadronic mean-field potentials, to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.2$ GeV. They employed a Skyrme-type density-dependent mean-field potential $U(\rho)=\alpha\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}+\beta(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0})^\gamma$ with incompressibility coefficients $K_0 = 200$ MeV (soft EoS) and $K_0 = 400$ MeV (stiff EoS), and also considered an extremely soft EoS with $K_0 = 100$ MeV for comparison. The production and collective flows of $\Xi^-$ and $\Omega^-$ were computed for central (impact parameter $b = 2$ fm) and semi-central ($b = 7$ fm) collisions, and results were cross-checked with the PHSD transport model.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon density in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.2$ GeV reaches approximately 4.3–5.8 $\rho/\rho_0$, depending on the EoS chosen.
- $\Xi^-$ production begins at around 2.5 fm/c when central compression density hits about 4.5 $\rho/\rho_0$, while $\Omega^-$ production begins near 5 fm/c, aligning with central maximum compression density of approximately 5.5 $\rho/\rho_0$ (for soft EoS $K_0 = 200$ MeV).
- The $\Xi^-$ production rate is roughly 250 times higher than that of $\Omega^-$.
- The relative sensitivity $RS = (M_{soft} - M_{stiff})/M_{stiff}$ of $\Omega^-$ is approximately 2.5 times that of $\Xi^-$.
- The EoS sensitivity of $\Omega^-$ is about twice that of $\Xi^-$ as verified by the PHSD transport model.
- The transverse momentum ranges where $\Omega^-$ shows notable EoS sensitivity are 0.25 to 1 GeV for $sgn(y)*v_1$ and 0 to 0.8 GeV for $v_2$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Yong (2021, 2022) that identified $\Xi^-$ as an effective sensor for the nuclear EoS at high densities, and demonstrates that $\Omega^-$ provides even greater sensitivity due to its production in denser matter and its production channel $\bar{K}+\Xi \rightleftharpoons \pi+\Omega$, which combines the sensitivities of single strangeness $\bar{K}$ and double strangeness $\Xi$. The findings are relevant for upcoming experiments at facilities such as CBM and HADES at GSI/FAIR, J-PARC-HI, NICA, RHIC-STAR BES-II, and HIAF, which aim to explore the QCD phase structure and EoS at high baryon densities.
