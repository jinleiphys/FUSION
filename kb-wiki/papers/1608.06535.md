# Heavy Exotic Molecules with Charm and Bottom

> We revisit the formation of pion-mediated heavy-light exotic molecules with both charm and bottom and their chiral partners under the general strictures of both heavy-quark and chiral symmetry. The chiral exotic partners with good parity formed using the $(0^+, 1^+)$ multiplet are about twice more bound than their primary exotic partners formed using the $(0^-,1^-)$ multiplet. The chiral couplings across the multiplets $(0^\pm, 1^\pm)$ cause the chiral exotic partners to unbind, and the primary exotic molecules to be about twice more bound, for $J\leq 1$. Our multi-channel coupling results show that only the charm isosinglet exotic molecules with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ binds, which we identify as the reported neutral $X(3872)$. Also, the bottom isotriplet exotic with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ binds, which we identify as a mixture of the reported charged exotics $Z^+_b(10610)$ and $Z^+_b(10650)$. The bound isosinglet with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ is suggested as a possible neutral $X_b(10532)$ not yet reported.

## Key claim

The paper claims that under the combined constraints of heavy-quark and chiral symmetry, only the charm isosinglet exotic molecule with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ binds (identified as $X(3872)$), and the bottom isotriplet exotic with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ binds (identified as a mixture of $Z_b^+(10610)$ and $Z_b^+(10650)$), while the chiral cross-coupling between the $(0^\pm,1^\pm)$ multiplets causes the chiral exotic partners to unbind and the primary exotic molecules to be about twice more bound for $J\leq 1$.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-relativistic Hamiltonian for heavy-light meson molecules using one-pion exchange potentials derived from chiral and heavy-quark symmetry Lagrangians, with a regulated monopole form factor and a core cutoff of 1 GeV. They solved the multi-channel coupled Schrodinger problem for $J=0$ and $J=1$ channels, including mixing between the $(0^-,1^-)$ and $(0^+,1^+)$ multiplets, for both charm and bottom systems with isosinglet and isotriplet configurations.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the charm isosinglet $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ state: $E=3.867$ GeV for cutoff $\Lambda=1$ GeV, identified as $X(3872)$
- Binding energy of the bottom isosinglet $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ state: $E=10.509$ GeV for cutoff $\Lambda=1$ GeV
- Binding energy of the bottom isosinglet $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$ state: $E=10.555$ GeV for cutoff $\Lambda=1$ GeV
- Binding energy of the bottom isosinglet $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ state: $E=10.532$ GeV for cutoff $\Lambda=1$ GeV, suggested as possible neutral $X_b(10532)$
- Binding energy of the bottom isosinglet $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ state: $E=10.550$ GeV for cutoff $\Lambda=1$ GeV
- Empirical masses used: $m_{D^\pm}=1.870$ GeV, $m_{D^0}=1.865$ GeV, $m_{D^{*\pm}}=2.010$ GeV, $m_{D^{*0}}=2.007$ GeV; $m_B=5.279$ GeV, $m_{\vec B}=5.325$ GeV
- Mass splittings: $\Delta_1=(m_{\tilde D^{\star}}-m_{D^{\star}})\approx 410$ MeV, $\Delta_2=(m_{\tilde D}-m_{D})\approx 530$ MeV
- Pion coupling constant: $g_H=0.6$, pion decay constant $f_\pi=93$ MeV, pion mass $m_\pi=137$ MeV

## Context

This work extends molecular descriptions of heavy exotic mesons (deusons) by Thorsson (1995), Karliner (2011), and others, incorporating the full chiral multiplet mixing between $(0^-,1^-)$ and $(0^+,1^+)$ heavy-light mesons that was only partially considered in prior studies. The results support the molecular interpretation of $X(3872)$ and the charged $Z_b$ states, and predict a new neutral bottom exotic $X_b(10532)$ near the $B\bar B^*$ threshold, which could be searched for by the DO collaboration at Fermilab or the LHCb collaboration at CERN.
