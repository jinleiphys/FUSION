# On the covariant relativistic separable kernel

> Two different methods of the covariant relativistic separable kernel construction in the Bethe-Salpeter approach are considered. One of them leads in the center-of-mass system of two particles to the quasipotential equation. The constructed 4-dimensional covariant functions are used to reproduce neutron-proton phase shifts for total angular momenta $J=0,1$. Obtained results are compared with other model calculations.

## Key claim

Two methods for constructing covariant relativistic separable kernels in the Bethe-Salpeter approach are presented; one yields a 4-dimensional kernel with poles in the relative-energy complex plane, while the other yields a 3-dimensional (quasipotential) kernel with no such poles, and both are used to reproduce neutron-proton phase shifts for total angular momenta J=0,1, though the agreement with experimental data is limited to specific energy ranges for each partial wave.

## Method

The authors construct one-rank separable kernels for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction using two covariant generalizations of nonrelativistic Yamaguchi-type form factors: the first replaces the 3-momentum squared by the 4-momentum squared (giving functions g_p with poles on the real p_0 axis), and the second introduces a formal 4-vector Q defined from the relative and total 4-momenta (giving functions g_Q with no poles on the real p_0 axis). The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved for partial states with total angular momenta J=0,1 using these kernels, and the parameters are fitted to reproduce neutron-proton phase shifts and low-energy observables (scattering length, effective range, deuteron binding energy). Both modified Yamaguchi (MY/MYQ) and modified extended Yamaguchi (MEY/MEYQ) forms are tested.

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length a_s = -23.69 fm (MYQ) and -23.49 fm (MEYQ), compared to experimental -23.748(10) fm.
- Triplet scattering length a_t = 5.37 fm (both MYQ and MEYQ), compared to experimental 5.424(4) fm.
- Singlet effective range r_0s = 2.22 fm (MYQ) and 2.30 fm (MEYQ), compared to experimental 2.75(5) fm.
- Triplet effective range r_0t = 1.71 fm (both MYQ and MEYQ), compared to experimental 1.759(5) fm.
- Deuteron binding energy E_d = 2.2246 MeV (both MYQ and MEYQ), compared to experimental 2.224644(46) MeV.
- Maximum laboratory energy T_Lab^max for the fits: 0.999 GeV for ^1S_0^+, 1.1 GeV for ^3S_1^+, 1.1 GeV for ^1P_1^+, 0.999 GeV for ^3P_0^+, 0.999 GeV for ^3P_1^+ (MYQ); 0.999 GeV for ^1S_0^+, 1.1 GeV for ^3S_1^+, 1.1 GeV for ^1P_1^+, 2.83 GeV for ^3P_0^+, 0.999 GeV for ^3P_1^+ (MEYQ).
- Phase shifts for ^3P_0^+ are described up to T_Lab < 3 GeV with MEY/MEYQ functions.
- Phase shifts for ^1P_1^+ and ^3S_1^+ are described up to T_Lab < 1.2 GeV with MEY/MEYQ functions.
- Phase shifts for ^1S_0^+ and ^3P_1^+ are described up to T_Lab < 1.1 GeV with MEY/MEYQ functions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous parametrization (Bondarenko et al., 2008, JETPL) by introducing a second covariant generalization method (the Q-vector approach) and by increasing the complexity of the form factors from modified Yamaguchi to modified extended Yamaguchi forms. The results are compared with the nonrelativistic CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt, 2001) and the SP07 partial-wave analysis (Stoks and Rijken, 2007). The new kernels are intended for use in relativistic calculations of electromagnetic reactions with the deuteron, such as elastic lepton-deuteron scattering and deuteron electrodisintegration, and the authors note that higher-rank kernels are needed to improve agreement with data up to 3 GeV for the ^1S_0^+ and ^3P_1^+ channels.
