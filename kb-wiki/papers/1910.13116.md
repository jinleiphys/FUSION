# Saturation and forward jets in proton-lead collisions at the LHC

> We investigate the forward-jet energy spectrum within the Color Glass Condensate framework at 5 TeV center-of-mass energy. In particular, we focus on the kinematic range covered by the CMS-CASTOR calorimeter. We show that our saturation-model calculations are compatible with the CASTOR measurements and that to optimally reproduce the data, effects of multi-parton interactions need to be included. We predict a significant nuclear suppression - reaching down to 50% at the lowest considered jet energies $E_{\rm jet} \sim 500 \, {\rm GeV}$.

## Key claim

The authors show that their Color Glass Condensate (CGC) saturation-model calculations are compatible with the CMS-CASTOR forward-jet measurements at 5 TeV, but only if effects of multi-parton interactions are included; the abstract's claim of compatibility is conditional on this additional modeling.

## Method

The authors compute forward-jet energy spectra in the hybrid formalism of the CGC effective field theory, where the dilute proton is described by collinear parton distribution functions (CTEQ6) and the dense target (proton or lead nucleus) is described by a dipole amplitude evolved with the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. For proton-lead collisions, the nuclear dipole amplitude is obtained by applying the Glauber model to the proton dipole initial condition fitted to HERA data, with no additional nuclear parameters. Multi-parton interactions are modeled by a Poisson distribution of partonic scatterings, with jet merging probabilities computed from azimuthal angle constraints within a cone radius R=0.5.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear suppression factor reaches down to \(R_{pA} \approx 0.5\) (50%) at the lowest considered jet energies \(E_{\rm jet} \sim 500\) GeV in proton-lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV.
* The CASTOR calorimeter covers pseudorapidity \(5.2 < \eta_{\rm lab} < 6.6\) in the laboratory frame, which is boosted by \(y_{\rm shift} = 0.465\) relative to the center-of-mass frame.
* Bjorken-\(x\) values probed in the lead nucleus range from \(x_A \sim 10^{-6}\) to \(x_A \sim 10^{-5}\) for jet energies \(500\) GeV \(< E < 3000\) GeV in the CASTOR acceptance.
* The proton transverse area parameter from the HERA fit is \(\sigma_0/2 = 16.36\) mb, and the initial saturation scale squared is \(Q_{s0}^2 = 0.06\) GeV\(^2\).
* The effective cross section for double-parton scattering in proton-proton collisions is \(\sigma_{\rm eff} \approx 15\) mb, consistent with \(\sigma_0/2\).
* The minimum transverse momentum cutoff is chosen as \(p_{T,\rm min} = 1.0\) GeV, with sensitivity studied at 0.5 GeV and 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework previously applied to inclusive particle production at RHIC and LHC (Lappi and Mantysaari, 2013) to the very forward rapidity region covered by the CMS-CASTOR calorimeter, probing Bjorken-\(x\) values down to \(10^{-6}\) in the lead nucleus. The results are compatible with the CASTOR measurements (Sirunyan et al., 2018) and predict significant nuclear suppression, complementing similar saturation signals seen in D-meson production by LHCb (Aaij et al., 2017, 2019) and in ultra-peripheral collisions. The calculations enable future comparisons with electron-ion collider measurements and provide a baseline for distinguishing saturation effects from collinear factorization approaches using nuclear PDFs (Eskola et al., 2019).
