# Investigation on the 48Ca+249-252Cf reactions synthesizing isotopes of the superheavy element 118

> The study of the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249,250,251,252}$Cf reactions in a wide energy interval around the external barrier has been achieved with the aim of investigating the dynamical effects of the entrance channel via the $^{48}$Ca induced reactions on the $^{249-252}$Cf targets and to analyze the influence of odd and even neutron composition in target on the capture, quasifission and fusion cross sections. Moreover, we also present the results of the individual evaporation residue excitation functions obtained from the de-excitation cascade of the various even-odd and even-even $^{297-300}$118 superheavy compound nuclei reached in the studied reactions, and we compare our results of the $^{294}$118 evaporation residue yields obtained in the synthesis process of the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249,250}$Cf reactions with the experimental data obtained in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Cf experiment carried out at the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions of Dubna.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed yield of the superheavy nucleus 294118 in the 48Ca+249Cf experiment at Dubna can be fully explained only when one accounts for the combined contributions from both the 3-neutron evaporation channel of the 297118 compound nucleus (from the 48Ca+249Cf reaction) and the 4-neutron evaporation channel of the 298118 compound nucleus (from the 48Ca+250Cf reaction), because the presence of 250Cf in the target is inevitable.

## Method

The authors performed model calculations of capture, quasifission, and fusion cross sections for the 48Ca+249,250,251,252Cf reactions by solving equations of motion for the relative radial coordinate, surface vibrations of 48Ca, and orbital angular momentum, using a dinuclear system (DNS) concept with friction coefficients and nucleus-nucleus potentials dependent on single-particle states. The evaporation residue cross sections for the 297-300118 compound nuclei were then calculated using a statistical de-excitation cascade model, employing two different sets of theoretical masses and fission barriers (from the Warsaw group and from Moller et al.) to compare with the experimental data from the Dubna experiment.

## Key numbers

- The maximum values of cross sections for the 2n, 3n, and 4n emission channels from the 297118 CN in the 48Ca+249Cf reaction are in the 0.3-1.2 pb range.
- The calculated evaporation residue cross sections for the 3n channel from 297118 CN at excitation energies E*_CN = 29.2 and 34.4 MeV are close to the experimental data within error bars.
- The 295118 ER nucleus from the 48Ca+250Cf reaction (3n emission from 298118 CN) reaches a maximum yield of about 4 pb at E*_CN = 28 MeV.
- The 297118 ER formation from the 48Ca+252Cf reaction (3n channel) should reach about 2 pb at E*_CN = 27-28 MeV.
- The contributions of the 294118 ER nucleus formed in the 48Ca+251,252Cf reactions are lower than 10^-9 and 10^-6 pb, respectively, in the 29.2-34.4 MeV excitation energy interval.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied by the same group (Nasirov et al., 2010; Nasirov et al., 2009) to reactions aiming at Z=120 superheavy elements, now applied to the 48Ca+249-252Cf systems that produce isotopes of element 118. The calculations are compared with the experimental data from Oganessian et al. (2006) at the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions in Dubna, which reported the synthesis of 294118. The results enable predictions for future experiments using a mixed target of 249-252Cf isotopes, showing that the 48Ca+252Cf reaction is a useful accessible reaction to obtain 297118 and 296118 ER nuclei.
