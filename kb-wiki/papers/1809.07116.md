# A cross-validation check in the covariance analysis of isospin sensitive observables from heavy ion collision

> This paper focuses on two problems. The first is related to the consistency checks of the complete correlation coefficients between two groups of variables obtained in Phys. Lett. B 749, 262 (2015). The first group collects parameters that describe properties of nuclear matter referring to the transport model involved. The second one is a group of observables adopted in the heavy ion collision experiments. The second problem concerns the method of determining the values of pure correlations between some variables in heavy ion collision. The application of this method for the analysis of the correlations of the isospin sensitive variables is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper performs a cross-validation (out-of-sample) check on complete correlation coefficients between nuclear-matter force parameters and isospin-sensitive observables previously reported in Phys. Lett. B 749, 262 (2015), finding that some pairs of observables yield inconsistent results that may indicate either statistical or theoretical inconsistencies in the transport model.

## Method

The authors use formal relations between complete and partial correlation coefficients (Eq. 1) to derive complete correlation coefficients among the force parameters \(K_0\), \(S_0\), and \(L\) from the previously published complete correlation coefficients between these parameters and five isospin-sensitive observables \(B_a\) (single n/p ratio, double n/p ratio, isoscaling ratios for neutrons and protons, and isospin transport ratio \(R_{diff}\)). They then impose consistency conditions requiring all derived complete and partial correlation coefficients to lie within the interval \([-1, 1]\); violations of this condition indicate inconsistency between the results obtained from different observables. The analysis is performed separately for beam energies of 50 MeV/u and 120 MeV/u using data from the ImQMD-Sky transport model applied to \({}^{124}Sn + {}^{124}Sn\) and \({}^{112}Sn + {}^{112}Sn\) reactions.

## Key numbers

- For 50 MeV/u, the partial correlation between \(S_0\) and \(L\) controlling for \(K_0\) is \(r_{SL|K} \in (-0.45, 0.34)\).
- For 120 MeV/u, the partial correlation \(r_{SL|K}\) becomes much stronger, lying in \((-0.92, -0.71)\), except for the observable pair involving \(B_5\) (\(R_{diff}\)) with \(B_2\), which gives \(r_{SL|K} = 0.17\).
- For 50 MeV/u, the partial correlation between \(K_0\) and \(S_0\) controlling for \(L\) is \(r_{SK|L} \in (-0.59, 0.37)\); for 120 MeV/u, \(r_{SK|L} \in (0.33, 0.56)\) except for the \(B_2\)-\(B_5\) pair where \(r_{SK|L} = -0.76\).
- For 50 MeV/u, the partial correlation between \(L\) and \(K_0\) controlling for \(S_0\) is \(r_{LK|S} \in (-0.22, 0.81)\); for 120 MeV/u, \(r_{LK|S} \in (0.02, 0.46)\).
- The multiple correlation coefficient \(r_{S|LK}\) (linear correlation of \(S_0\) with both \(L\) and \(K_0\) together) lies in \((0.13, 0.65)\) for 50 MeV/u and in \((0.77, 0.98)\) for 120 MeV/u.

## Context

This work extends the covariance analysis of Zhang et al. (2015) by introducing a cross-validation methodology to test whether complete correlation coefficients obtained from one set of HIC observables are consistent with those from another independent set. The results suggest caution when comparing results from isospin transport ratio \(R_{diff}\) (observable \(B_5\)) with those from other isospin-sensitive observables, as the former shows systematic inconsistencies, particularly at higher energy. The analysis also builds on the compilation of 142 Skyrme interactions by Baldo (2016), which found very weak correlation between \(S_0\) and \(L\) under binding-energy constraints, and shows that stronger correlations emerge when symmetry-energy-sensitive constraints from HIC data are applied.
