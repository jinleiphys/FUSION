# TENDL-astro: a new nuclear data set for astrophysics interest

> In this work, we are presenting a new database of astrophysical interest, based on calculations performed with the nuclear reaction code TALYS. Four quantities are systematically calculated for over 8000 nuclides: cross sections, reaction rates, Maxwellian Averaged Cross Sections (or MACS) at 30 keV and partition functions. For cross sections and reaction rates, nine reactions are considered, induced by neutron, proton or alpha. The main complement of this database compared to existing ones is that the impact of reaction models ({\it e.g.} level density, gamma strength function, and optical model) is estimated by varying 9 different models, and by proposing calculated values for each of them, together with averages, standard deviations and other statistical quantities. This new database, called TENDL-astro, version 2023, is available online (https://tendl.web.psi.ch/tendl\_2023/astro/astro.html) and linked to the well-known TENDL database, used in a variety of applications.

## Key claim

The TENDL-astro database provides systematically calculated cross sections, reaction rates, Maxwellian Averaged Cross Sections (MACS) at 30 keV, and partition functions for over 8000 nuclides, with the novel feature of quantifying model uncertainties by varying 9 different reaction models; however, the direct capture contribution is not included, and no individual resonance behavior is calculated at low energies below a few tens of keV.

## Method

The authors used the TALYS 1.96 nuclear reaction code with the Hauser-Feshbach compound nucleus formalism to calculate cross sections for nine reactions (n,gamma; n,p; n,alpha; p,gamma; p,n; p,alpha; alpha,gamma; alpha,n; alpha,p) for incident energies from 10^-5 eV to 20 MeV. Reaction rates and normalized partition functions G(T) were computed for temperatures from 10^5 to 10^10 K by integrating cross sections over a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution including target excited states, while MACS at 30 keV were calculated for the target ground state only. Model uncertainties were estimated by varying 9 different models (including level densities, gamma strength functions, and optical potentials) across 480 combinations for non-fissile nuclides and 960 for fissile ones.

## Key numbers

- Over 8000 nuclides are included in the database
- Nine reactions are considered: (n,gamma), (n,p), (n,alpha), (p,gamma), (p,n), (p,alpha), (alpha,gamma), (alpha,n), (alpha,p)
- Incident energy grid spans from 10^-5 eV up to 20 MeV
- Temperature range for reaction rates and partition functions: 10^5 K to 10^10 K
- MACS values are calculated at kT = 30 keV, corresponding to T = 3.48 x 10^9 K
- 480 model combinations for non-fissile nuclides, 960 for fissile nuclides

## Context

This work extends the TENDL library series (Koning 2019) and prior MACS uncertainty studies (Rochman 2017, Rochman 2010) by systematically applying model variations to astrophysical quantities. The database complements existing resources such as the KADoNiS database (Dillmann 2014, Dillmann 2006) by providing calculated values with quantified model uncertainties for a much larger set of nuclides, including unstable species relevant to stellar nucleosynthesis. The database enables users to assess the impact of nuclear model choices on astrophysical reaction rates and cross sections, which is particularly important for nuclides where experimental data are unavailable.
