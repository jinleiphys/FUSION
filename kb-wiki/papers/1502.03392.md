# Solid-state calculation of crystalline color superconductivity

> It is generally believed that the inhomogeneous Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase appears in a color superconductor when the pairing between different quark flavors is under the circumstances of mismatched Fermi surfaces. However, the real crystal structure of the LOFF phase is still unclear because an exact treatment of 3D crystal structures is rather difficult. In this work we present a solid-state-like calculation of the ground-state energy of the body-centered cubic (BCC) structure for two-flavor pairing by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix in the Bloch space without assuming a small amplitude of the order parameter. We develop a computational scheme to overcome the difficulties in diagonalizing huge matrices. Our results show that the BCC structure is energetically more favorable than the 1D modulation in a narrow window around the conventional LOFF-normal phase transition point, which indicates the significance of the higher-order terms in the Ginzburg-Landau approach.

## Key claim

The body-centered cubic (BCC) crystal structure is energetically more favorable than the one-dimensional (1D) modulation in a narrow window around the conventional LOFF-normal phase transition point, but this advantage is limited to a small region and the BCC-normal transition is of rather weak first order, contrary to earlier Ginzburg-Landau predictions of a strong first-order transition.

## Method

The authors performed a solid-state-like calculation of the ground-state energy of the BCC structure for two-flavor quark pairing by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix in Bloch space without assuming a small amplitude of the order parameter. They developed a computational scheme that decomposes the huge Hamiltonian matrix into smaller blocks by neglecting a small amount of off-diagonal couplings, enabling diagonalization for the BCC structure at cutoff parameters up to D~35. The grand potential was evaluated using a Pauli-Villars-like regularization scheme and compared with the 1D LO and FF states.

## Key numbers

- The upper critical field for the FF state is δμ₂ = 0.754Δ₀ (where Δ₀ is the pairing gap at vanishing mismatch).
- The lower critical field for the FF state is δμ₁ = 0.707Δ₀.
- For the BCC structure, the phase transition to the normal state occurs at δμ* − δμ₂ ≃ 0.03Δ₀, with the gap parameter Δ ≃ 0.1Δ₀ at the transition.
- The optimal pair momentum for the BCC structure is q ~ δμ, and the BCC-normal transition is of weak first order with the upper critical field only about 4% higher than δμ₂.
- Calculations were performed for Δ₀ = 60, 80, and 100 MeV at μ = 400 MeV, with results nearly identical when scaled by (δμ − δμ₂)/Δ₀.

## Context

This work extends the study of crystalline color superconductivity beyond the 1D LOFF structures (FF and LO states) analyzed by Buballa (2009) and the Ginzburg-Landau approach of Bowers and Rajagopal (2002), who investigated 23 crystal structures up to O(Δ⁶) and predicted a strong first-order BCC-normal transition at δμ* ≃ 3.6Δ₀. The results are consistent with the quasiclassical equation approach of Combescot and Mora (2004, 2005), who predicted a weak first-order transition. The computational scheme enables future studies of other 3D crystal structures like FCC, though the FCC calculation remains infeasible with current resources.
