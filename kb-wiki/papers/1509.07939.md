# Novel Collective Phenomena in High-Energy Proton-Proton and Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> The observation of long-range collective correlations for particles emitted in high-multiplicity pp and pPb collisions has opened up new opportunities of investigating novel high-density QCD phenomena in small colliding systems. We review experimental results related to the studies of collective phenomena in small systems from RHIC and the LHC over the past several years. Latest development in theoretical interpretations motivated by different frameworks are also reviewed, and confronted with the experimental data. Perspectives on possible future directions are discussed, with the aim of further exploring the rich emergent QCD phenomena.

## Key claim

The observation of long-range collective correlations in high-multiplicity pp and pPb collisions suggests novel high-density QCD phenomena in small systems, but the authors caution that while the experimental signatures show striking similarities to PbPb collisions, strong conclusions about hydrodynamic interpretation require detailed model comparisons that are still ongoing.

## Method

The authors review experimental results from RHIC and the LHC on two-particle and multi-particle azimuthal correlations, identified particle spectra, flow factorization, femtoscopic HBT correlations, and heavy flavor production in pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions. They compare these measurements to theoretical interpretations from relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, the color glass condensate framework, and PYTHIA with color reconnections.

## Key numbers

- The near-side ridge in pPb collisions is about four times larger than in pp collisions for Ntrk > 110
- v2 and v3 values in pPb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV rise with pT up to around 3 GeV and then fall off, similar to PbPb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- v3 harmonics as a function of pT for pPb and PbPb systems are almost identical across the full multiplicity range
- The femtoscopic radii in pPb collisions are about 15-20% larger than in pp collisions at similar multiplicities
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio extracted from ultra-central PbPb collisions is eta/s ~ 0.08-0.2
- Up to 20% deviation from unity of the factorization ratio r2 has been observed in ultra-central (0-0.2% centrality) PbPb collisions
- The Upsilon(2S) to Upsilon(1S) yield ratio shows suppression as multiplicity increases in pp and pPb collisions
- Open charm meson yields in pp at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV increase faster than linear above 3-4 times the average minimum bias multiplicity

## Context

This review extends the study of collective phenomena from large heavy-ion collision systems (AA) to small colliding systems (pp and pA), building on the discovery of the ridge in pp collisions and the subsequent observation of similar long-range correlations in pPb. The work connects to prior experimental results from RHIC and the LHC on elliptic flow in AA collisions (Ollitrault 1992) and the discovery of triangular flow (Alver 2010), while confronting data with hydrodynamic calculations using IP-Glasma initial conditions (Schenke 2010) and Glauber initial conditions (Heinz 2013). The review enables future studies to differentiate between hydrodynamic, color glass condensate, and color reconnection models, and to explore whether similar collective phenomena appear in even smaller systems like high-multiplicity pp collisions.
