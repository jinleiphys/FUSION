# The general harmonic-oscillator brackets: compact expression, symmetries, sums and Fortran code

> We present a very simple expression and a Fortran code for the fast and precise calculation of three-dimensional harmonic-oscillator transformation brackets. The complete system of symmetries for the brackets along with analytical expressions for sums, containing products of two and three brackets, is given.

## Key claim

The paper presents a compact, highly symmetric expression for three-dimensional harmonic-oscillator transformation brackets (HOBs) that is simpler than prior formulations, along with a complete system of symmetries and analytical expressions for sums of products of two and three brackets; the claimed simplicity and speed of calculation depend on precomputing binomial and trinomial coefficient arrays and using the specific expression in Eqs. (23)-(24) rather than the more complicated eight-index sum of Buck and Merchant (1990).

## Method

The authors start from the Buck-Merchant (1990) expression for the general harmonic-oscillator bracket and rewrite it using trinomial coefficients and products of four G-functions, each containing a Clebsch-Gordan coefficient with zero angular-momentum projections and a square root of trinomial ratios. They derive the complete set of four symmetry relations for the brackets (Eqs. 12.1-12.4) and obtain analytical formulas for sums of products of two and three HOBs by composing orthogonal transformations of Jacobi coordinates. A Fortran code is implemented that precomputes arrays of binomial and trinomial coefficients, then evaluates the 6-j and 9-j symbols via binomial-based expressions to compute HOBs rapidly.

## Key numbers

- For maximum oscillator energy \(E_0 = 3\), the code computed 4,672 HOBs with a summed absolute deviation \(\delta(E_0) = 0.2956 \times 10^{-14}\) in 0.28 seconds on a personal computer.
- For \(E_0 = 6\), 658,000 HOBs were computed with \(\delta(E_0) = 0.2394 \times 10^{-11}\) in 74.7 seconds.
- For \(E_0 = 10\), 51,349,192 HOBs were computed with \(\delta(E_0) = 0.1306 \times 10^{-6}\) in 25,100 seconds.
- For \(E_0 = 12\), 273,872,766 HOBs were computed with \(\delta(E_0) = 0.3481 \times 10^{-4}\) in 248,000 seconds.

## Context

This work extends the earlier expression for harmonic-oscillator brackets by Buck and Merchant (1990) and the general formalism of Talmi (1952), Moshinsky (1959), and Smirnov (1962) for transforming between single-particle and relative-plus-center-of-mass coordinates. The compact expression and symmetry relations enable efficient antisymmetrization of translationally invariant many-fermion wave functions in Jacobi coordinates, as needed in nuclear shell-model calculations and hadron structure studies. The Fortran code and analytical sum rules (Eqs. 35-36) directly support calculations of permutation operator matrix elements in the translationally invariant oscillator basis, as required by the approach of Navratil, Kamuntavicius, and Barrett (1997).
