# Numerical approach to the nuclear deformation energy

> A numerical method close to the Strutinsky procedure (but better) is proposed to calculate the deformation energy of nuclei. Quadrupole (triaxial) deformations are considered. Theoretical as well as practical aspects of the method are reviewed in this paper. A complete fortran program illustrates the feasibility of the method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a numerical method for calculating nuclear deformation energy that improves upon the standard Strutinsky procedure by replacing the Strutinsky smoothed level density with a semiclassical level density derived from the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion, thereby avoiding the dependence on the order and smearing parameters that plague the original Strutinsky method.

## Method

The method follows the macroscopic-microscopic approach: the liquid-drop model deformation energy is computed analytically for triaxial ellipsoidal shapes using elliptic integrals, then shell corrections are added by solving the Schrodinger equation for a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit coupling in an oscillator basis. The shell correction is obtained as the difference between the sum of occupied single-particle energies and the semiclassical energy calculated from the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion (Thomas-Fermi plus corrections up to order 1/hbar), with pairing corrections added via the BCS approximation using a pairing strength deduced from the empirical gap 12/sqrt(A).

## Key numbers

- Liquid drop model parameters: r0 = 1.275 fm, e^2 = 1.4399764 MeV, reduced fissility zeta = 52.8(1 - 2.84 I^2) where I = (N-Z)/(N+Z)
- For N=126 with Vo=44 MeV, RV=Rso=7.52 fm, aV=aso=0.67 fm, kappa_j=-0.7491: total semiclassical energy Esc = -2282.56 MeV (scdefor routine) and -2282.61 MeV (sclspher1 routine), compared to -2282.5 MeV from Jennings (1975)
- For N=184 with Vo=43 MeV, RV=Rso=8.48 fm, aV=aso=0.67 fm, kappa_j=-0.7321: total semiclassical energy Esc = -3228.76 MeV (scdefor) and -3228.78 MeV (sclspher1), compared to -3230.0 MeV from Jennings (1975)
- Relative contributions to total semiclassical energy: lambda*N0 ~ 30.5%, E_{-3}^0 ~ 66%, E_{-1}^0 ~ 2%, E_{-1}^{SO} ~ 1.25%, E_1^0 ~ 0.05%, E_1^{SO} ~ 0.2%
- In deformed test cases (N=54 and N=80), the relative error between semiclassical and Strutinsky calculations is less than 1 MeV per 1300-1500 MeV in the plateau region

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic method pioneered by Strutinsky (1967) by replacing the Strutinsky smoothing procedure with a semiclassical approach based on the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion, following the theoretical demonstration by Mohammed-Azizi (2006) that the Strutinsky level density approximates the semiclassical level density. The method builds on the Woods-Saxon potential calculations of Mohammed-Azizi (2004) and the semiclassical formalism of Jennings (1975). The code enables systematic calculations of deformation energy surfaces for large numbers of nuclei without the parameter-dependence issues of the standard Strutinsky method, and can serve as a benchmark for future constrained Hartree-Fock calculations.
