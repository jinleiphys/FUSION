# Azimuthal flow of decay photons in relativistic nuclear collisions

> An overwhelming fraction of photons from relativistic heavy ion collisions has its origin in the decay of $π^0$ and $η$ mesons. We calculate the azimuthal asymmetry of the decay photons for several azimuthally asymmetric pion distributions. We find that the $k_T$ dependence of the elliptic flow parameter$v_2$ for the decay photons closely follows the elliptic flow parameter $v_2^{π^0}$ evaluated at $p_T \approx k_T+δ$, where $δ\approx$ 0.1 -- 0.2 GeV, for typical pion distributions measured in nucleus-nucleus collisions at relativistic energies. Similar results are obtained for photons from the 2-$γ$ decay of $η$ mesons. Assuming that the flow of $π^0$ is similar to those for $π^+$ and $π^-$ for which independent measurements would be generally available, this ansatz can help in identifying additional sources for photons. Taken along with quark number scaling suggested by the recombination model, it may help to estimate $v_2$ of the parton distributions in terms of azimuthal asymmetry of the decay photons at large $k_T$.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) of decay photons from \(\pi^0\) and \(\eta\) mesons closely follows the \(v_2\) of the parent mesons evaluated at a transverse momentum shifted by \(\delta \approx 0.1\)–\(0.2\) GeV, provided the parent meson distribution is azimuthally asymmetric and the photon transverse momentum \(k_T > 0.2\) GeV. This empirical relation holds for several parameterizations of the pion distribution and for the \(v_2(p_T)\) shape, including cases where \(v_2\) peaks and then decreases.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluated the Lorentz-invariant cross-section for decay photons from \(\pi^0\) and \(\eta\) mesons using a delta-function decay kinematics (Eq. 1) and azimuthally asymmetric meson distributions. They considered exponential and power-law parameterizations for the meson transverse momentum spectra, with the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2(p_T)\) modeled as a saturating function \(v_2(p_T)=a[1-\exp(-p_T/b)]\) with \(a=0.2\) and \(b=1\) GeV, and also a peaked form \(v_2(p_T)=0.4\,p_T^2\exp(-p_T)\). For the recombination model section, they used a thermal parton distribution with quark mass \(m_q=260\) MeV, transition temperature \(T=175\) MeV, and transverse rapidity \(\tanh\eta_T=0.55\), multiplying by \([1+4v_2^q(p_T/2)\cos(2\phi)]\) to account for elliptic flow of mesons formed from recombining partons.

## Key numbers

- The shift parameter \(\delta\) between the photon transverse momentum \(k_T\) and the parent pion transverse momentum \(p_T\) is \(\delta \approx 0.1\)–\(0.2\) GeV for \(k_T > 0.2\) GeV, with accuracy better than 1–3%.
- For the exponential pion distribution, the slope parameter \(T_0\) is taken as \(\sim 290\) MeV; for the power-law distribution, \(p_0 \sim 5\) GeV and \(n \approx 29\).
- The saturating \(v_2\) parameters are \(a=0.2\) and \(b=1\) GeV.
- For the peaked \(v_2\) shape \(v_2(p_T)=0.4\,p_T^2\exp(-p_T)\), the shift \(\delta\) is about 0.27 GeV.
- The maximum kinetic energy of a \(\pi^0\) in the rest frame of an \(\eta\) meson is \(\approx 80\) MeV.
- The \(\eta/\pi^0\) ratio is about 0.44 for \(p_T > 2\) GeV.
- In the recombination model, the quark mass is \(m_q=260\) MeV, the transition temperature is \(T=175\) MeV, and \(\tanh\eta_T=0.55\).

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow of thermal photons (phot_flow) by providing a quantitative ansatz for the dominant background from decay photons, which must be subtracted to identify additional photon sources such as thermal radiation. The authors note that the WA98 group (WA98) had earlier observed similar ratios \(v_2^\gamma/v_2^{\pi^0}\) and that a relation between \(p_T\)-integrated anisotropy for \(\pi^0\) and decay photons (rani) was used in WA98 experiment analysis. The results enable experimental extraction of the parton elliptic flow \(v_2^q\) via the recombination model scaling (Fries et al., 2003) combined with the shifted relation between decay photon and pion \(v_2\).
