# Investigating the broadening phenomenon in two-particle correlations induced by gluon saturation

> It has been found that the gluon density inside the proton grows rapidly at small momentum fractions. Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) predicts that this growth can be regulated by nonlinear effects, ultimately leading to gluon saturation. Within the color glass condensate framework, nonlinear QCD effects are predicted to suppress and broaden back-to-back angular correlations in collisions involving heavy nuclei. While suppression has been observed in various experiments in $d/p$$+$A collisions compared to $p$$+$$p$ collisions, the predicted broadening remains unobserved. This study investigates the contributions of intrinsic transverse momentum ($k_T$), which is associated with saturation physics, as well as parton showers and transverse motion from fragmentation ($p_T^{\mathrm{frag}}$), which are not saturation dependent, to the width of the correlation function. Our findings show that the non-saturation dependent effects, especially the initial-state parton shower and $p_T^{\mathrm{frag}}$, which occur independently of the collision system, smear the back-to-back correlation more than gluon saturation does, making the broadening phenomenon difficult to observe.

# Investigating the broadening phenomenon in two-particle correlations induced by gluon saturation

## Key claim
Non-saturation-dependent effects, particularly the initial-state parton shower and transverse momentum from fragmentation ($p_T^{\mathrm{frag}}$), smear the back-to-back correlation function more than gluon saturation does, making the predicted broadening from the color glass condensate framework difficult to observe in experiments.

## Method
The authors used the PYTHIA-6 Monte Carlo generator with the standard STAR tune, CTEQ6L parton distribution functions, and JETSET for fragmentation to simulate 200 GeV $p$+$p$ collisions. They sequentially added initial-state parton showers, final-state parton showers, and $p_T^{\mathrm{frag}}$ (modeled as a Gaussian with width 0.4 GeV/$c$) to the baseline simulation, then varied the intrinsic $k_T$ Gaussian width from 0.25 to 1.0 GeV/$c$ to mimic saturation effects, measuring the resulting width of the away-side peak in the di-$\pi^0$ azimuthal correlation function.

## Key numbers
- The simulated $p$+$p$ collisions are at a center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV, with trigger hadron $p_T$ range 2.0–2.5 GeV/$c$ and associated hadron $p_T$ range 1.0–1.5 GeV/$c$, at pseudo-rapidity $2.6 < \eta < 4$.
- The Gaussian width of $p_T^{\mathrm{frag}}$ was set to 0.4 GeV/$c$, extracted from a global analysis of EMC, HERMES, and COMPASS data.
- Intrinsic $k_T$ values of 0.5 and 1.0 GeV/$c$ were assigned for $p$+$p$ and $p$+$Au$ collisions, respectively, motivated by saturation scale estimates at RHIC energies where the average $x$ probed is $10^{-2}$.
- The mean value of $Q^2$ probed is 2.5 GeV$^2$, and the probed $x_2$ covers a wide range from $10^{-4}$ to approximately 0.5.
- The extracted width of the away-side peak ($\sigma_{\mathrm{away}}$) remained approximately constant as $k_T$ was varied from 0.25 to 1.0 GeV/$c$ when parton showers and $p_T^{\mathrm{frag}}$ were included.

## Context
This work addresses the puzzle that while RHIC experiments (STAR, 2021; PHENIX, 2011) and LHC experiments (ATLAS, 2019; LHCb, 2017, 2023) have observed the predicted suppression of back-to-back correlations in $p$+A and $d$+A collisions compared to $p$+$p$ collisions, none have observed the predicted broadening. The study extends earlier work by Zheng (2014) and Lappi (2013) on intrinsic $k_T$ modeling, and responds to next-to-leading-order CGC calculations by Hanninen (2023), Mantysaari (2022), and Caucal (2023) that show NLO contributions like parton showers also broaden the correlation. The results imply that future CGC calculations of di-hadron correlations must include NLO contributions to make meaningful comparisons with experimental data.
