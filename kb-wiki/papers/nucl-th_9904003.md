# Reply to Comment on ``Determination of pion-baryon coupling constants from QCD sum rules''

> In response to Kim's comment (nucl-th/9903040) on the sum rules for pion-baryon coupling constants obtained in hep-ph/9512259 and hep-ph/9606471, we point out that our treatment of the continuum is consistent with duality and with the fact that the correlator in the presence of an external meson or field should be represented by a double dispersion relation.

# Reply to Comment on "Determination of pion-baryon coupling constants from QCD sum rules"

## Key claim

The authors defend their earlier sum-rule treatment of pion-baryon coupling constants against Kim's criticism, arguing that their continuum modelling is consistent with duality provided one uses a double dispersion relation (rather than a single one) for the correlator in the presence of an external meson, even in the chiral limit where a single momentum flows through the correlator.

## Method

The authors show that the correlator in the presence of an external pion must be represented by a double dispersion relation in the two hadron momenta \(p_1^2\) and \(p_2^2\), because the external meson can induce transitions between different hadronic states. They demonstrate their continuum treatment explicitly for the dimension-5 term in the operator-product expansion (OPE), which produces a logarithmic term \(C\ln(-p^2)\); inserting the corresponding spectral density \(\rho_{\rm OPE}(s_1,s_2,0) = -C s_1 \delta(s_1 - s_2)\) into the double dispersion relation and Borel transforming yields a continuum factor \(E_1(S/M^2)\) multiplying the leading term, consistent with their original paper. They also note that the dimension-7 condensate term, which has no logarithm, should not receive a continuum factor, but this correction is numerically negligible.

## Key numbers

- The dimension-5 continuum contribution after Borel transform takes the form \(-C M^2 (1 + S/M^2) \exp(-S/M^2)\), which replaces the bare term \(-C M^2\) by \(-C M^2 E_1(S/M^2)\) in the sum rule.
- The dimension-3 term in the sum rule acquires a continuum factor \(E_2(S/M^2)\).
- The dimension-7 condensate term, which behaves as \(1/p^2\) with no logarithm, should have its continuum factor \(E_0(S/M^2)\) replaced by 1; this correction leaves the numerical results of the original paper "practically unchanged."

## Context

This reply responds to Kim's Comment (1999) on the sum rules for pion-baryon coupling constants derived by Krippa and Birse (1995, 1996). The authors clarify that the correct formalism for external-meson correlators follows Ioffe (1995), who stressed the necessity of double dispersion relations to capture the double pole at \(p^2 = M_N^2\) and the single pole from nucleon-to-continuum transitions. The work defends the standard QCD sum-rule approach for coupling constants against criticisms about continuum modelling and duality, and it enables future applications of these sum rules to pion-baryon couplings with confidence in the continuum treatment.
