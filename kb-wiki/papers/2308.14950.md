# Note on the electromagnetic radius of proton

> We have analyzed the proton form factor data by using a number of phenomenological parameterizations (models) and extracting the proton electric and magnetic radii. To this end we performed a global fit to all available form factor data, with the virtual photon momentum squared $Q^2$ from $0.0002$ to nearly 10 GeV$^2$ for electric form factor and from $0.015$ to 31 GeV$^2$ for magnetic one. Special attention was given to the small structure shown by the form factor data near $Q^2 = 0.2$ GeV$^2$. It was found that different models yield different structures with different numbers of minimum at this kinematics. Since the slope of form factor in the limit of $Q^2\to 0$ is influenced by this structure, the extracted proton radii are consequently different for different models. Our finding recommends that future experiments should focus on this kinematics instead of low $Q^2$. Experimental data with accuracies comparable to those of the latest data at low $Q^2$ would clearly help to clarify the effect of this structure on the proton charge radius. Interestingly, most of the extracted proton charge radii were found to be closer to the value obtained from the muonic hydrogen atom spectroscopy.

## Key claim
Different phenomenological parameterizations of the proton form factor yield different extracted proton charge and magnetic radii because the slope at \(Q^2\to 0\) is influenced by a small structure in the form factor data near \(Q^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\); most extracted charge radii are closer to the value from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy, but the authors recommend future experiments focus on this intermediate \(Q^2\) region rather than on very low \(Q^2\).

## Method
The authors performed a global fit to all available proton electric and magnetic form factor data using four phenomenological parameterizations: dipole, double-dipole, Friedrich-Walcher (FW), and Arrington models. The fit covered \(Q^2\) from 0.0002 to nearly 10 GeV\(^2\) for the electric form factor and from 0.015 to 31 GeV\(^2\) for the magnetic one, using the MINUIT code with SIMPLEX and MIGRAD minimizers. Special attention was given to the structure near \(Q^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) by plotting the form factor relative to the standard dipole form factor \(G_{E,p} - G_{\rm SD}\).

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius from the dipole fit to all data: \(r = 0.8388 \pm 0.0003\) fm (cut-off \(\Lambda = 0.8145 \pm 0.0003\) GeV).
- Proton charge radius from the double-dipole fit: \(r = 0.8714 \pm 0.0093\) fm.
- Proton charge radius from the Friedrich-Walcher fit: \(r = 0.8362 \pm 0.0110\) fm.
- Proton charge radius from the Arrington fit: \(r = 0.8650 \pm 0.0003\) fm.
- The structure in the electric form factor is located at \(Q_b = 0.1969 \pm 0.0085\) GeV (FW fit).
- The PRad collaboration data (Xiong 2019) gave an extracted radius of \(r_E = 0.831 \pm 0.014\) fm.
- The Mainz-2010 experiment (Bernauer 2010) gave \(r_E = 0.879 \pm 0.008\) fm and \(r_M = 0.777 \pm 0.017\) fm.
- The muonic hydrogen experiment (Pohl 2010) gave \(r_E = 0.84184 \pm 0.00067\) fm.

## Context
This work extends the global fit approach of Arrington (2007) and the Friedrich-Walcher (2003) analysis of the \(Q^2 \approx 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) structure, which was interpreted as a pion cloud contribution. The authors show that the extracted proton radius depends on the choice of parameterization and on the inclusion of higher-\(Q^2\) data, not just the low-\(Q^2\) slope. This finding suggests that the proton radius puzzle, reviewed by Gao and Vanderhaeghen (2022), may be partially resolved by focusing new experiments on the \(Q^2 \approx 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) region with precision comparable to the latest low-\(Q^2\) data.
