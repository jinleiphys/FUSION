# Exploring Three-Nucleon Forces in Lattice QCD

> Three-nucleon forces (3NF) are investigated from two-flavor lattice QCD simulations. We utilize the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function to determine two-nucleon forces (2NF) and 3NF in the same framework. As a first exploratory study, we extract 3NF in which three nucleons are aligned linearly with an equal spacing. This is the simplest geometrical configuration which reduces the huge computational cost of calculating the NBS wave function. Quantum numbers of the three-nucleon system are chosen to be (I, J^P)=(1/2,1/2^+) (the triton channel). Lattice QCD simulations are performed using N_f=2 dynamical clover fermion configurations at the lattice spacing of a = 0.156 fm on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with a large quark mass corresponding to m_π= 1.13 GeV. We find repulsive 3NF at short distance in the triton channel. Several sources of systematic errors are also discussed.

## Key claim

This exploratory lattice QCD study finds repulsive three-nucleon forces (3NF) at short distance in the triton channel, but only for the simplest geometrical configuration where three nucleons are aligned linearly with equal spacing, and only under the condition that parity-odd two-nucleon forces can be neglected by using a spin/isospin wave function that is antisymmetric for any nucleon pair.

## Method

The authors used two-flavor dynamical clover fermion configurations from the CP-PACS Collaboration to compute Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for two-nucleon and three-nucleon systems. They extracted 3NF by solving a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for the linear configuration (three nucleons equally spaced on a line) in the triton channel (I=1/2, J^P=1/2^+), subtracting the two-nucleon forces obtained from separate lattice simulations. The spin/isospin wave function was chosen to be fully antisymmetric so that only parity-even two-nucleon forces contribute.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.156 fm on a 16^3 x 32 lattice
- Pion mass: m_pi = 1.13 GeV (corresponding to heavy quark mass)
- Nucleon mass: m_N = 2.15 GeV
- Delta mass: m_Delta = 2.31 GeV
- Lattice spatial size: (2.5 fm)^3
- Sink time slices used for analysis: (t-t_0)/a = 8 and 9
- Two-nucleon ground state energies: E_2N^10 ~ -3 MeV and E_2N^01 ~ -3 MeV
- Three-nucleon ground state energy: E_3N ~ -3 MeV
- Total energy shift for 3NF: delta_E = E_3N - 3/2 E_2N^10 - 3/2 E_2N^01 ~ 5 MeV, with systematic error estimated at less than or equal to 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous lattice QCD approach for two-nucleon forces (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2010) to three-nucleon systems, providing the first direct calculation of 3NF from QCD rather than from phenomenological models such as the Fujita-Miyazawa two-pion exchange or chiral effective field theory (Weinberg 1992, Epelbaum 2009). The results enable future systematic lattice QCD studies of 3NF with more general geometrical configurations and lighter quark masses, and may inform the short-range repulsive component phenomenologically required in nuclear matter calculations for neutron stars (Akmal 1998).
