# Coulomb Excitation of Deuteron in Peripheral Collisions with a Heavy Ion

> We develop an ab initio, non-perturbative, time-dependent Basis Function (tBF) method to solve the nuclear structure and scattering problems in a unified manner. We apply this method to a test problem: the Coulomb excitation of a trapped deuteron by an impinging heavy ion. The states of the deuteron system are obtained by the ab initio nuclear structure calculation implementing a realistic inter-nucleon interaction with a weak external trap to localize the center of mass and to discretize the continuum. The evolution of the internal state of the deuteron system is directly solved using the equation of motion for the scattering. We analyze the excitation mechanism of the deuteron system by investigating its internal transition probabilities and observables as functions of the exposure time and the incident speed. In this investigation, the dynamics of the Coulomb excitation are revealed by the time evolution of the system's internal charge distribution.

## Key claim

The authors develop a non-perturbative, time-dependent Basis Function (tBF) method that unifies ab initio nuclear structure and scattering calculations, but they demonstrate it only on a simplified test problem where the deuteron's center of mass is fixed by an external trap and the continuum is discretized, with the trap strength and basis strength both set to 5 MeV and only three interaction channels retained.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for a trapped deuteron in the time-varying Coulomb field of a passing heavy ion (U\(^{92+}\)) using a basis of eigenstates obtained from an ab initio structure calculation with the JISP16 realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator representation. The time evolution is computed via the MSD2 numerical scheme, which is symmetric in time and conserves norm, and the results are compared with first-order perturbation theory. Only the E1 multipole component of the Coulomb field is retained, and the magnetic interaction is neglected.

## Key numbers

- The trap strength and basis strength are both set to 5 MeV, with truncation parameter \(N_{\text{max}} = 60\) for the 3DHO basis.
- The impact parameter is chosen as \(b = 5\) fm, and the projectile is fully stripped uranium (U\(^{92+}\)) with incident speeds of 0.1, 0.2, and 0.4 (in natural units).
- The exposure time is fixed from \(-5\) MeV\(^{-1}\) to \(5\) MeV\(^{-1}\), approximately \(6.582 \times 10^{-21}\) sec.
- The r.m.s. charge radius of the target before scattering is 1.472 fm, about 25% smaller than the experimental measurement of 1.975(3) fm for a natural deuteron.
- The average intrinsic energy of the scattered target increases by at least 0.7 MeV for incident speeds 0.2 and 0.4, and by about 10% for incident speed 0.1.
- The full width half maximum of the first quantum fluctuation dip for the initial state at \(v=0.4\) is \(\Delta t > 0.1\) MeV\(^{-1}\), with transition energy \(\Delta E > 12.7\) MeV, yielding a product greater than unity consistent with the uncertainty principle.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent approach previously applied in quantum field theory by Vary (2009), Zhao (2013), Chen (2017), and Du (2017) to nuclear structure and scattering problems. The tBF method is contrasted with existing ab initio approaches such as the no-core shell model with resonating group method (Quaglioni 2008, Navratil 2009), the Faddeev method (Witala 2000), and the Green's function Monte Carlo method (Lynn 2015), which the authors argue may be challenged to retain full non-perturbative quantum coherence over all relevant intermediate states. The present demonstration with a trapped deuteron and only E1 transitions is intended as a proof-of-principle; future work will remove the trap, include higher multipole components and magnetic transitions, and treat more complex targets and reactions.
