# A new crystalline phase in magnetar crusts

> We show that ions at the low densities and high magnetic fields relevant to the outer crust of magnetars form a novel crystalline phase where ions are strongly coupled along the magnetic field and loosely coupled in the transverse direction. The underlying cause is the anisotropic screening of the Coulomb force by electrons in the presence of a strongly quantizing magnetic field which leads to Friedel oscillations in the ion-ion potential. In particular, the Friedel oscillations are much longer-ranged in the direction of the magnetic field than is the case in the absence of magnetic fields, a factor that has been neglected in previous studies. These "Friedel crystals" have very anisotropic elastic moduli, with potentially interesting implications for the Quasi-periodic Oscillations seen in the X-ray flux of magnetars during their giant flares. We find the minimum energy configuration of ions taking into account these anisotropic effects and find that, depending on the density, temperature and magnetic field strength, different lattice structures (fcc, hcp or bcc oriented in different ways in relation to the magnetic field) are favored.

# A new crystalline phase in magnetar crusts

## Key claim

The authors show that ions in the outer crust of magnetars can form a novel "Friedel crystal" phase where ions are strongly coupled along the magnetic field direction and loosely coupled in the transverse direction, but only in the low-temperature regime where the long-range oscillatory Friedel part of the ion-ion potential dominates over the short-range Yukawa part; the absence of this phase in certain regions of parameter space does not mean the material is not solid, as standard Coulomb crystals are expected there instead.

## Method

The authors computed the minimum energy configuration of ions by numerically summing the anisotropic screened Coulomb potential (including both Yukawa and Friedel oscillatory terms) over several thousand ions in the longitudinal direction and a few in the transverse direction, for five candidate lattice structures (simple cubic, fcc, bcc, rotated-bcc with the diagonal axis along the magnetic field, and hcp) at various orientations relative to the magnetic field. The lattice constant along the magnetic field direction was varied over the range 0 to 5π/(2k_e) while keeping the density fixed by adjusting the transverse spacing, and the contribution from distant ions beyond the explicit sum was estimated by an integral approximation.

## Key numbers

- The electron Fermi momentum in the longitudinal direction is given by n_e = (eB)/(2π²) k_e, relating electron density to magnetic field strength.
- The mass density at which electrons become relativistic is ρ ≈ 0.79 × 10⁸ g/cm³ × (A₆₆/Z₂₈) × B₁₅, where B₁₅ = B/10¹⁵ G, A₆₆ = A/66, and Z₂₈ = Z/28.
- The condition for strongly quantizing magnetic fields (most electrons in the lowest Landau level) is ρ ≲ 5.2 × 10⁸ g/cm³ × (A₆₆/Z₂₈) × B₁₅^(3/2).
- The thermal cutoff length for the Friedel oscillations is λ_T ≈ 1.6 × 10⁻⁷ cm × (Z₂₈/A₆₆) × (ρ₈/(B₁₅ T₁)), where T₁ = T/(1 keV) and ρ₈ = ρ/(10⁸ g/cm³).
- The Debye screening length is 1/m_D ≈ 1.3 × 10⁻¹⁰ cm × √(Z₂₈ ρ₈/A₆₆) × (1/B₁₅).
- The inverse electron Fermi momentum is 1/k_e ≈ 3.0 × 10⁻¹¹ cm × (A₆₆/Z₂₈) × (B₁₅/ρ₈).
- The magnetic length is 1/√(eB) ≈ 8.1 × 10⁻¹² cm × (1/√B₁₅).
- The melting temperature criterion from the Friedel potential gives T_melt ∼ 1 MeV × (A₆₆² B₁₅³/ρ₈²).
- The parameter space explored covers densities 10⁵ g/cm³ < ρ < 10¹⁰ g/cm³, magnetic fields 10¹³ G < B < 10¹⁶ G, and temperatures 0.1 keV < T < 10 keV (1.16 × 10⁶ K to 1.16 × 10⁸ K).
- Friedel crystals form at low temperatures (10⁶ K to 5 × 10⁷ K), and exist up to T ∼ 5 × 10⁸ K for some values of B and ρ.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Bedaque et al. (2013) which used a simple model to predict the formation of strongly coupled ion filaments along the magnetic field in magnetar crusts, but could not determine the transverse crystal structure. The present paper provides a more rigorous calculation that determines the favored lattice structures (fcc, hcp, bcc, or rotated-bcc depending on density, temperature, and magnetic field strength), building on the anisotropic electronic screening formalism developed by Stein et al. (2011). The results enable computation of all elastic moduli of magnetar crusts, which is relevant for modeling quasi-periodic oscillations observed in the X-ray flux of magnetars during giant flares, and may also affect estimates of the breaking strain of the crust for gravitational wave physics.
