# QCD critical point and hydrodynamic fluctuations in relativistic fluids

> These lecture notes consist of two major connected parts. The first part (Sections 1, 2), after a brief historical introduction, deals with the physics of critical points in thermodynamic equilibrium. The features of the fluctuations relevant for the QCD critical point search are highlighted. The second part (Sections 3, 4) focuses on the recent developments in the description of the fluctuation dynamics especially relevant for the QCD critical point search in heavy-ion collisions.

# QCD critical point and hydrodynamic fluctuations in relativistic fluids

## Key claim
The non-monotonic dependence of fluctuation cumulants on collision energy, particularly the quartic cumulant, is proposed as a signature of the QCD critical point; however, the magnitude of these cumulants depends sensitively on the equation of state and is therefore hard to predict, meaning that while recent STAR data exclude a monotonic dependence at 3.1 sigma, this is not yet a definitive indication of the critical point's existence.

## Method
The paper develops a deterministic approach to hydrodynamic fluctuations by expanding around the average background and deriving evolution equations for multipoint Wigner-transformed correlation functions of conserved densities. For relativistic fluids with nontrivial velocity gradients, a confluent formalism is introduced that defines equal-time correlators in the local rest frame using boost transformations and a confluent derivative, yielding ultraviolet-finite renormalized hydrodynamic equations. The hierarchy of evolution equations for two-point, three-point, and four-point correlation functions is derived explicitly for a diffusion problem and generalized to arbitrary relativistic flow.

## Key numbers
- The variance of the order parameter scales as $\langle\phi^2\rangle \sim \xi^{2.}$, the third cumulant as $\langle\phi^3\rangle \sim \xi^{4.5}$, and the fourth cumulant as $\langle\phi^4\rangle^{\rm c} \sim \xi^{7.}$ (approximate universal powers due to fluctuations).
- The singular contributions to cumulants of specific entropy fluctuations follow the same scaling: $\Delta \langle(\delta m)^2 \rangle\sim \xi^{2.}$, $\Delta \langle(\delta m)^3 \rangle\sim \xi^{4.5}$, $\Delta \langle(\delta m)^4 \rangle^c \sim \xi^{7.}$.
- STAR collaboration experimental measurements exclude a monotonic dependence of quartic cumulants at the 3.1$\sigma$ level.

## Context
These lecture notes extend the foundational work on critical fluctuations in QCD by Stephanov (1998, 1999) and the universal non-Gaussian fluctuation signatures proposed by Stephanov (2008). The deterministic approach to hydrodynamic fluctuations builds on recent developments for Bjorken flow by Akamatsu (2017, 2018) and Martinez (2018), and for arbitrary relativistic flow by Stephanov (2017) and An (2019, 2020, 2022). The mapping between Ising model variables and QCD variables follows the standardized parameterization of Parotto (2018). The work enables quantitative connection between equilibrium thermodynamic fluctuations and experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, by providing evolution equations that account for memory effects and the transformation from hydrodynamic quantities to particle multiplicity fluctuations.
