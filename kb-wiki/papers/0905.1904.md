# Rates of K-shell Electron Capture Decays of 180Re and 142Pm Atoms

> We propose a theoretical analysis of the experimental data on the time behaviour K-shell electron capture (EC) decays of atoms 180Re and 142Pm in solid targets, obtained recently by Faestermann et al. Phys. Lett. B 672, 227 (2009) and Vetter et al., Phys. Lett. B 670, 149 (2008). We show that the absence of the time modulation in these data rules out the explanation of the "GSI Oscillations" (Yu. A. Litvinov et al., Phys. Lett. B 664, 162 (2008)) by means of two closely spaced ground mass-eigenstates of mother nuclei.

## Key claim
The absence of time modulation in the K-shell electron capture decay rates of neutral atoms 180Re and 142Pm in solid targets rules out the explanation of the "GSI Oscillations" that invokes two closely spaced ground mass-eigenstates of the mother nuclei, but is consistent with an explanation based on interference of neutrino mass-eigenstates.

## Method
The authors calculate the time-dependent K-shell electron capture decay rates for 180Re and 142Pm atoms using time-dependent perturbation theory, considering two alternative frameworks: (1) an initial state described by a coherent superposition of two closely spaced ground mass-eigenstates of the mother nucleus, and (2) a standard weak interaction theory with massive neutrinos where the final-state electron neutrino is a coherent superposition of neutrino mass-eigenstates. For each framework, they compute the predicted modulation period and compare with experimental time spectra from Faestermann et al. (2009) and Vetter et al. (2008).

## Key numbers
- For the two-mass-eigenstate hypothesis applied to 180Re and 142Pm atoms, the predicted modulation period is T_EC approximately 5 s, based on the energy difference between mass-eigenstates Delta E = 8.38(9) x 10^-16 eV derived from the GSI data for 140Pr.
- For the neutrino mass-eigenstate interference framework, the predicted modulation periods are T_EC = 1.43 x 10^-4 s for 180Re and T_EC = 1.84 x 10^-4 s for 142Pm, calculated using Delta m^2_21 = 2.19 x 10^-4 eV^2 and Q-values of 3800 keV (180Re) and 4870 keV (142Pm).
- The experimental time spectra for 180Re (binned at 1 s/bin) and 142Pm (binned at 0.5 s/bin) show no time modulation, consistent with the neutrino interference prediction but inconsistent with the 5 s modulation predicted by the two-mass-eigenstate hypothesis.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical debate over the "GSI Oscillations" observed in H-like heavy ions by Litvinov et al. (2008), where electron capture decay rates of 142Pm60+, 140Pr58+, and 122I52+ showed periodic modulation with periods following T_EC = A/20 s. The authors rule out the alternative explanation proposed by Giunti (2008) and Kienert et al. (2008) involving two closely spaced nuclear ground mass-eigenstates, using new experimental data on neutral atoms from Faestermann et al. (2009) and Vetter et al. (2008). The results support the authors' own neutrino mass-eigenstate interference mechanism (Ivanov et al., 2008) and enable further tests of neutrino properties in electron capture decays.
