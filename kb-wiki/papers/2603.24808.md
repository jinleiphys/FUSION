# Diffractive and photon-induced processes at the LHC: from the odderon discovery, the evidence for saturation to the search for axion-like particles

> We discuss first the discovery of the odderon by the TOTEM and D0 collaborations. We then describe the gap between jets measurements sensitive to the high gluon density regime and the possible observation of saturation phenomenon in Pb Pb interactions. We also mention the sensitivity to beyond standard model physics and to the production of axion-like particles via photon photon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent results in diffractive and photon-induced processes at the LHC, claiming that the odderon has been discovered via a comparison of elastic pp and p-pbar cross sections at the 3.4 sigma level, and that evidence for saturation in PbPb collisions is favored by exclusive J/Psi production data, though alternative explanations such as nuclear PDFs with shadowing cannot be ruled out.

## Method

The author compares elastic differential cross sections dσ/dt measured by TOTEM in pp collisions at 2.76, 7, 8, and 13 TeV with D0 measurements in p-pbar collisions at 1.96 TeV, extrapolating the TOTEM data down to the Tevatron energy using fits to characteristic points (dip, bump, etc.). For saturation studies, exclusive vector meson (J/Psi, Upsilon) production cross sections in γp and γPb interactions are computed using dipole models with either the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation including saturation or the linear Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) equation, and compared to data from HERA, ALICE, CMS, and LHCb.

## Key numbers

- The bump-over-dip ratio for pp elastic collisions is flat above √s = 100 GeV; for D0 p-pbar at 1.96 TeV the ratio is 1.00 ± 0.21, yielding a >3 sigma difference between pp and p-pbar.
- The comparison between extrapolated TOTEM pp data and D0 p-pbar data at 1.96 TeV gives a χ² test p-value of 0.00061, corresponding to 3.4 sigma significance; combined with TOTEM ρ measurements this reaches 5.3 to 5.7 sigma.
- The extrapolated pp total cross section at 1.96 TeV is σ_tot = 82.7 ± 3.1 mb, leading to dσ/dt(t=0) = 357.1 ± 26.4 mb/GeV² from TOTEM data.
- The exclusive J/Psi production cross section in γPb interactions shows clear differences between BK and BFKL predictions, with data favoring the BK (saturation) model; for γp interactions the differences are small.
- The exclusive Upsilon production cross section in γPb interactions shows small differences between BK and BFKL predictions for W ~ 1 TeV, consistent with CMS measurements.
- For diffractive c-cbar production in γPb interactions at W ~ 1 TeV, the BK evolution predicts a factor of about two lower cross section than BFKL.
- The ratio of diffractive to inclusive c-cbar production in γPb interactions is predicted to be about 21% using BK evolution.
- The Standard Model exclusive WW production cross section is σ_WW = 95.6 fb, with σ_WW(W > 1 TeV) = 5.9 fb.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for the odderon, following earlier indications at ISR energies (ISR collaboration) and theoretical predictions by Nicolescu, Martynov, Landshoff, and Leader. The saturation studies build on the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation and dipole model approaches developed by Bialas and Peschanski, and are compared to measurements from H1, ZEUS, ALICE, CMS, and LHCb. The results enable future measurements with the Precision Proton Spectrometer (PPS) and ATLAS Forward Proton (AFP) detectors, as well as complementary studies at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) using different ion species to map the transition to the saturation regime.
