# Zero-sound modes for the nuclear equation of state at supra-normal densities

> The meaningful correlations between the zero-sound modes and the stiffness of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) are uncovered in nuclear matter with the relativistic mean-field theory. It is demonstrated that the high-density zero-sound modes merely exist in models with the stiff EOS. While the stiff EOS can be softened by including ω-meson self-interactions (the ω4 term), the weakened coupling of the ω-meson self-interactions reignites the zero sound at high density. These results suggest that the high-density zero-sound modes can be used to probe the stiffness of the EOS at supra-normal densities. The implications and effects of zero sounds are also discussed in heavy ion collisions and neutron stars.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that high-density zero-sound modes exist only in models with a stiff nuclear equation of state (EOS), but this correlation is weakened in neutron star matter due to the complexity of isospin asymmetry and internal composition.

## Method
The authors employed relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) based on relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory to calculate zero-sound modes in nuclear matter. They used four parameter sets (NL3w03, GM1, TM1w02, FSUGarnet) classified as stiff or soft EOS, and systematically adjusted the ω-meson self-interaction coupling constant c₃ to modulate the stiffness of the high-density EOS while keeping the incompressibility nearly unchanged.

## Key numbers
- Zero-sound modes appear at density 2.05 ρ₀ for q=10 MeV in NL3w03, compared to 2.2 ρ₀ for q=80 MeV with q₀=61.2 MeV
- In TM1w02-based models, high-density zero-sound modes appear when c₃ is reduced to 35, corresponding to sound velocity square v²₂.₀=0.254 at 2ρ₀
- In FSUGarnet-based models, high-density zero-sound modes appear when c₃ is reduced to 25, corresponding to v²₂.₀=0.382 at 2ρ₀
- The maximum neutron star mass ranges from 2.066 M⊙ (FSUGarnet original) to 2.752 M⊙ (FSUGarnet with c₃=1)
- The radius of 1.4 M⊙ neutron star ranges from 11.706 km (FSUGarnet original) to 13.818 km (TM1w02 with c₃=1)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of zero-sound modes in nuclear matter by Chin (1977) and Lim (1989) using relativistic RMF theory, and connects to constraints on the high-density EOS from neutron star observations such as NICER (Riley 2021, Miller 2019) and gravitational-wave detections (Abbott 2017, 2018, 2019). The results suggest that zero-sound modes could serve as a probe of EOS stiffness in heavy-ion collisions, and may influence neutron star cooling processes as discussed by Bedaque (2003) and Leinson (2011).
