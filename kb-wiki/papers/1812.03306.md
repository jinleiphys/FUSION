# Implications of nuclear interaction for nuclear structure and astrophysics within the relativistic mean-field model

> Thesis based on the papers Phys. Rev. C 2015, 92, 054314; Int. J. Mod. Phys. E 2016, 25, 1650062; Phys. Rev. C 2017, 95, 015801; Phys. Rev. C 2017, 96, 034623; Nucl. Phys. A 2017, 966, 197; Phys. Rev. C 2018, 97, 045806; arXiv:1701.00731; and Lecture on Tides in binary stars by T. Hinderer

## Key claim

The binary mass distributions of neutron-rich thermally fissile nuclei such as $^{250}$U and $^{254}$Th can be predicted using a statistical model where excitation energies are obtained from the temperature-dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) formalism, but the results are only relative fragmentation probabilities because the dynamics of the fission process and secondary emissions are ignored.

## Method

The author used the statistical model of Fong (1956) with excitation energies and level density parameters calculated self-consistently from the temperature-dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) formalism, solving the Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations with axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis expansions (N$_F$ = 12, N$_B$ = 20). The level density of each fragment was computed from the excitation energy using the Bethe formula, and the folding level density for each binary fragmentation was obtained via a convolution integral; relative yields were then normalized to a total of 2. For comparison, the same statistical model was applied using single-particle energies from the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller (1995) retrieved from the RIPL-3 library.

## Key numbers

- For $^{236}$U at T = 1 MeV, the most favorable symmetric fragmentation in TRMF is $^{118}$Pd + $^{118}$Pd with relative yield 0.949 (normalized to 2).
- For $^{250}$U at T = 1 MeV in TRMF, the most favorable fragmentation is $^{141}$Te + $^{109}$Zr with relative yield 1.454.
- For $^{254}$Th at T = 3 MeV in TRMF, the symmetric fragmentation $^{127}$Rh + $^{127}$Rh has the largest yield at 0.803.
- For $^{232}$Th at T = 3 MeV in TRMF, the most favorable fragmentation is $^{132}$Sb + $^{100}$Y with relative yield 0.886.
- The inverse level density parameter $K$ for $^{236}$U, $^{250}$U, $^{232}$Th, and $^{254}$Th shows maximum fluctuations up to 30 MeV at T = 1 MeV, reducing to 10-13 MeV at T = 2 MeV or above.
- The level density parameter $a$ from TRMF is close to the empirical value $a = A/10$, while FRDM level density parameters are appreciably lower.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model of Fong (1956) and the approach of Rajasekaran and Devanathan (1981) by replacing Nilsson-model single-particle energies with self-consistent TRMF excitation energies. It builds on prior RMF studies of fission by Rutz (1995) and Patra (2010), and on the author's own earlier work on ternary mass distributions for $^{252}$Cf, $^{242}$Pu, and $^{236}$U. The results enable predictions of binary fragmentation yields for the range of neutron-rich thermally fissile nuclei $^{246-264}$U and $^{244-262}$Th, which could be relevant for energy production if these nuclei prove viable for achieving critical conditions faster than normal fissile nuclei.
