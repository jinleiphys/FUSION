# Are There Low-Lying Intruder States in ^8Be?

> We allow up to $4 \hbarω$ excitations in $^8Be$ relative to the basic configuration $(0s)^4(0p)^4$ in order to see if there are low-lying intruder states in $^8Be$. We use three interactions $-χQ \cdot Q, -χQ \cdot Q$ + spin-orbit, and a realistic (x,y) interaction, where for $x=1,y=1$ one gets values for the spin-orbit and tensor interactions which correspond closely to those of a non-relativistic $G$ matrix such as Bonn A. For the three interactions considered, if we allow up to $2 \hbar ω$ excitations we get the energy of the lowest $J=0^+$ intruder state to be at 32.1 MeV, 30.1 MeV and 33.8 MeV, respectively. If we allow up to $4 \hbarω$ excitations the corresponding values are 26.5 MeV, 26.5 MeV and 28.7 MeV. We thus support the statement made by E. Warburton in his 1986 $R$ matrix analysis of the $β^{\mp}$-delayed alpha spectra from the decay of $^8Li$ and $^8B$ that ``satisfactory fits are obtained without introducing intruder states below 26-MeV excitations'' (Phys. Rev. C 33, 303(1986)). In $^{10}Be$ however, with the first two ``$Q \cdot Q$'' interactions, we get (with up to $2 \hbar ω$ excitations) low-lying intruder states at 9.7 MeV and 11.9 MeV respectively. Thus the presence of low-lying intruder states in $^{10}Be$ (the $0^+_2$ state at 6.179 MeV may or may not be an intruder) does not imply that there are low-lying intruder states in $^8Be$.

## Key claim

The authors support Warburton's (1986) claim that no intruder states exist below 26 MeV excitation in ^8Be, but they hedge that this conclusion depends on the interaction used and the model space: with up to 4ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder state appears at 26.5-28.7 MeV depending on the interaction, and these energies are "very high" and "argue against" Barker's suggestion of low-lying intruder states in ^8Be.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model calculations for ^8Be allowing up to 4ħω excitations relative to the (0s)^4(0p)^4 configuration, and for ^10Be allowing up to 2ħω excitations. They used three interactions: a pure quadrupole-quadrupole (-χQ·Q) interaction, the same plus a spin-orbit term, and a realistic (x,y) interaction with x=1, y=1 that approximates the Bonn A non-relativistic G matrix. Intruder states were operationally defined as those with less than 50% 0ħω occupancy.

## Key numbers

- For ^8Be with the pure Q·Q interaction and up to 4ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder state is at 26.5 MeV excitation energy
- For ^8Be with the Q·Q plus spin-orbit interaction and up to 4ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder state is at 26.5 MeV
- For ^8Be with the realistic (x,y) interaction and up to 4ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder state is at 28.7 MeV
- For ^8Be with only up to 2ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder states are at 32.1 MeV (Q·Q), 30.1 MeV (Q·Q+spin-orbit), and 33.8 MeV (realistic)
- For ^10Be with the pure Q·Q interaction and up to 2ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder state is at 9.7 MeV
- For ^10Be with the Q·Q plus spin-orbit interaction and up to 2ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder state is at 11.4 MeV
- For ^10Be with the realistic (x,y) interaction and up to 2ħω excitations, the lowest J=0^+ intruder state is at 31.0 MeV
- The ground state of ^8Be with the Q·Q interaction has 62.8% 0ħω, 25.7% 2ħω, and 11.5% 4ħω occupancy

## Context

This work directly addresses a debate between Warburton (1986), who found no intruder states below 26 MeV in ^8Be from R-matrix analysis of beta-delayed alpha spectra, and Barker (1988, 1989), who argued from systematics of neighboring nuclei (^16O, ^12C, ^10Be with 0^+_2 states at 6.05, 7.65, and 6.18 MeV respectively) that ^8Be should also have a low-lying intruder state. The calculations extend the authors' prior work (Fayache, Sharma, and Zamick, 1996) by including up to 4ħω excitations in ^8Be. The results support Warburton's conclusion for ^8Be but leave open the question of whether the 6.179 MeV 0^+ state in ^10Be is an intruder, noting that the presence of low-lying intruders in ^10Be does not imply their existence in ^8Be.
