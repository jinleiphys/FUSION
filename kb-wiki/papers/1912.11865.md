# Analysis of $^4$He($γ,p)$T and $^4$He($γ,n)$$^3$He Reactions with Linearly Polarized Photons in the Energy Range up to 100\,MeV

> In a number of investigations, one can find the data on the $^4$He($γ$,p)T and $^4$He($γ$,n)$^3$He reaction cross sections in the collinear geometry, which are due to spin {\it S}=1 transitions of the final-state particles. The ratio of the differential cross section in the collinear geometry to the differential reaction cross section at the nucleon emission angle $θ_N$=90$^\circ$, and specified by the {\it S}=0 electric dipole transition at photon energies in the range 20$\le E_γ\le$100\,MeV, is independent of the photon energy, within the experimental error. In the meantime, experiments were made to measure the asymmetry of the cross section $Σ(θ_N)$, for the mentioned reactions with linearly polarized photons. It has been found that in the energy range between 20 and 90\,MeV, the $Σ(θ_N)$ value is also independent of the photon energy, within the experimental error. These data are in agreement with the assumption that transitions with spin {\it S}=1 can be due to the contribution of $^3P_0$ states of the $^4$He nucleus, and are inconsistent with the assumption that the spin-flip of the particle system occurred during the reaction as a result of the meson exchange current contribution. The available measured data on the collinear geometry reaction cross sections and the ones on the cross-section asymmetry of the reaction with linearly polarized photons do not agree between themselves. The above mentioned reactions seem to be more convenient for measuring the degree of photon beams linear polarization than the deuteron photodisintegration reactions.

# Analysis of $^4$He($\gamma$,p)T and $^4$He($\gamma$,n)$^3$He Reactions with Linearly Polarized Photons in the Energy Range up to 100 MeV

## Key claim

The ratio of the differential cross section in collinear geometry (due to spin S=1 transitions) to the differential cross section at nucleon emission angle $\theta_N=90^\circ$ (due to S=0 electric dipole transitions) is independent of photon energy between 20 and 100 MeV within experimental error, and the cross-section asymmetry $\Sigma(\theta_N)$ for linearly polarized photons is similarly energy-independent between 20 and 90 MeV. These data are consistent with S=1 transitions arising from $^3P_0$ states of $^4$He, but inconsistent with the hypothesis that spin-flip occurs via meson exchange current contributions.

## Method

The author performed a multipole analysis of the $^4$He($\gamma$,p)T and $^4$He($\gamma$,n)$^3$He reactions in the E1, E2, and M1 approximation, deriving expressions for the differential cross section and cross-section asymmetry $\Sigma(\theta_N)$ in terms of multipole amplitudes. Experimental data from prior measurements of differential cross sections in $4\pi$ geometry (using diffusion chambers in magnetic fields on bremsstrahlung beams) and from measurements of cross-section asymmetry on linearly polarized photon beams (produced via coherent bremsstrahlung and planar channeling) were re-analyzed using the least-squares method to extract the coefficients $A$, $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$, $\varepsilon$, and $\nu$, which encode the contributions of S=0 and S=1 transitions.

## Key numbers

- Mean coefficient $\overline{\nu}_p = 0.019 \pm 0.002$ and $\overline{\nu}_n = 0.028 \pm 0.003$ for the ($\gamma$,p) and ($\gamma$,n) channels respectively, from data in the photon energy range 20–100 MeV (statistical errors only).
- After correcting for angular resolution ($\delta(\theta_N)=1^\circ$), the mean values become $\overline{\nu}_p = 0.01 \pm 0.002$ and $\overline{\nu}_n = 0.015 \pm 0.003$.
- The true value of the coefficient $\nu$ is estimated to lie within $\nu = 0.010 \pm 0.005$.
- Effective degrees of photon polarization for the coherent bremsstrahlung beam: $P_\gamma^{\text{eff}} = 0.62$ (34–46 MeV), 0.71 (46–65 MeV), and 0.75 (65–90 MeV), with statistical error $\Delta P_\gamma^{\text{eff}} = \pm 0.03$.
- At $\theta_N=90^\circ$, the calculated asymmetry for the dominant $E1^3P_1$ or $M1^3S_1$ transition is $\Sigma(90^\circ) = 0.9901 \pm 0.005$ using $\nu=0.01\pm0.005$.
- The $^3P_1E1$ transition cross-section from radiative capture data at $E_p=2$ MeV ($E_\gamma=21.25$ MeV) was $0.72^{+0.29}_{-0.18}\%$ of the total reaction cross section.
- From Nogga et al. (2003), the $^4$He $P$-state probability ranges from 0.22% (CD-Bonn potential) to 0.75% (AV18+Urbana IX), with inclusion of 3N forces nearly doubling the $P$-state probability.

## Context

This work extends the multipole analysis of $^4$He photodisintegration reactions previously performed by the author and collaborators (Lyakhno, 2006) using data from diffusion chamber experiments (Gorbunov et al., 1961; Gorbunov, 1968) and radiative capture measurements (King et al., 1995; Tortorici et al., 2004). The analysis contradicts the meson exchange current explanation for S=1 transitions proposed by Gari and Hebach (1974), and instead supports the interpretation from Nogga et al. (2003) that S=1 transitions arise from $^3P_0$ states in the $^4$He nuclear structure. The findings suggest that $^4$He photodisintegration may be more convenient than deuteron photodisintegration for measuring the degree of linear polarization of photon beams, but the systematic inconsistency between collinear geometry cross-section data and asymmetry data requires further experimental clarification with improved angular resolution and polarization calibration.
