# Hidden charm pentaquark and $Λ(1405)$ in the $Λ^0_b \to η_c K^- p (πΣ)$ reaction

> We have performed a study of the $Λ^0_b \to η_c K^- p$ and $Λ^0_b \to η_c πΣ$ reactions based on the dominant Cabibbo favored weak decay mechanism. We show that the $K^- p$ produced only couples to $Λ^*$ states, not $Σ^*$ and that the $πΣ$ state is only generated from final state interaction of $\bar{K}N$ and $ηΛ$ channels which are produced in a primary stage. This guarantees that the $πΣ$ state is generated in isospin $I=0$ and we see that the invariant mass produces a clean signal for the $Λ(1405)$ of higher mass at $1420$ MeV. We also study the $η_c p$ final state interaction, which is driven by the excitation of a hidden charm resonance predicted before. We relate the strength of the different invariant mass distributions and find similar strengths that should be clearly visible in an ongoing LHCb experiment. In particular we predict that a clean peak should be seen for a hidden charm resonance that couples to the $η_c p$ channel in the invariant $η_c p$ mass distribution.

## Key claim

The $\Lambda^0_b \to \eta_c K^- p$ and $\Lambda^0_b \to \eta_c \pi \Sigma$ reactions produce a clean signal for the higher-mass pole of the $\Lambda(1405)$ at 1420 MeV and a clear peak for a predicted hidden charm resonance around 4265 MeV in the $\eta_c p$ invariant mass distribution, with the caveat that the absolute normalization is unknown and only relative strengths of the different invariant mass distributions are compared.

## Method

The authors model the weak decay of $\Lambda^0_b$ at the quark level via a Cabibbo-favored mechanism producing an $sud$ cluster, which hadronizes into $K^- p$, $\bar{K}^0 n$, and $\eta \Lambda$ states. Final state interactions of the $\eta_c p$ system are described using a Breit-Wigner form for a dynamically generated resonance $R$ from coupled-channel calculations of $\bar{D}\Sigma_c$, $\bar{D}\Lambda_c$, and $\eta_c N$ channels, while the $K^- p$ and $\pi \Sigma$ final state interactions are treated with a chiral unitary approach via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The Dalitz plot and invariant mass distributions are computed from the double differential width formula.

## Key numbers

* The dynamically generated hidden charm resonance $R$ has mass $M_R = 4265$ MeV and width $\Gamma_R = 23.2$ MeV (from $M_R - i\Gamma_R/2 = 4265 - i11.6$ MeV).
* The $\pi \Sigma$ invariant mass distributions peak at 1420 MeV, corresponding to the higher-mass pole of the $\Lambda(1405)$.
* The coupling of the resonance $R$ to $\bar{D}\Sigma_c$ is $g_{R,\bar{D}\Sigma_c} = 2.96 - i0.21$, to $\eta_c p$ is $g_{R,\eta_c p} = -0.94 + i0.03$, and to $\bar{D}\Lambda_c$ is $g_{R,\bar{D}\Lambda_c} = -0.08 + i0.06$.
* The rate of $\Lambda^0_b \to \eta_c K^- p$ production with $K^- p$ in $S$-wave is predicted to be three times larger than for $\Lambda^0_b \to J/\psi K^- p$, apart from phase space.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework applied to the $\Lambda^0_b \to J/\psi K^- p$ reaction studied by LHCb (Aaij 2015), where the $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ pentaquark states were observed, and follows the approach of Roca (2015) for the $J/\psi$ final state. The predictions are directly tied to the molecular hidden charm states predicted by Wu (2010) from $\bar{D}\Sigma_c$ and $\bar{D}\Lambda_c$ interactions. The results are intended to guide the ongoing LHCb analysis of $\Lambda^0_b \to \eta_c K^- p$ and to provide a clean test of the two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ free from isospin $I=1$ contamination.
