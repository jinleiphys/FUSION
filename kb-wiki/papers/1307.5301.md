# Gauge turbulence, topological defect dynamics, and condensation in Higgs models

> The real-time dynamics of topological defects and turbulent configurations of gauge fields for electric and magnetic confinement are studied numerically within a 2+1D Abelian Higgs model. It is shown that confinement is appearing in such systems equilibrating after a strong initial quench such as the overpopulation of the infrared modes. While the final equilibrium state does not support confinement, metastable vortex defect configurations appear in the gauge field which are found to be closely related to the appearance of physically observable confined electric and magnetic charges. These phenomena are seen to be intimately related to the approach of a non-thermal fixed point of the far-from-equilibrium dynamical evolution, signalled by universal scaling in the gauge-invariant correlation function of the Higgs field. Even when the parameters of the Higgs action do not support condensate formation in the vacuum, during this approach, transient Higgs condensation is observed. We discuss implications of these results for the far-from-equilibrium dynamics of Yang-Mills fields and potential mechanisms how confinement and condensation in non-abelian gauge fields can be understood in terms of the dynamics of Higgs models. These suggest that there is an interesting new class of dynamics of strong coherent turbulent gauge fields with condensates.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a 2+1D Abelian Higgs model, metastable vortex defect configurations and transient electric confinement appear during far-from-equilibrium evolution after a strong initial quench, even though the final equilibrium state does not support confinement; these phenomena are linked to the approach of a non-thermal fixed point and transient Higgs condensation can occur even when the vacuum parameters do not support condensate formation.

## Method

The authors performed real-time semi-classical simulations of the 2+1D Abelian Higgs model on cubic lattices of 512² to 2048² spatial points, using a compact U(1) gauge field formulation. They studied the dynamical equilibration starting from two types of initial conditions: a tachyonic scenario with negative mass squared and vanishing Higgs expectation value, and overpopulated initial states with high infrared mode occupancy. The Ginzburg-Landau parameter ξ = 6e²/λ was varied to control the transition between type I and type II superconducting phases, and gauge-invariant two-point correlation functions of the Higgs field were used to define occupation numbers and dispersions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice sizes used: 512² to 2048² spatial points
- The Ginzburg-Landau parameter ξ = 6e²/λ controls the transition between type I (ξ > 1/2) and type II (ξ < 1/2) superconducting phases
- The tachyonic instability populates modes with k < |m|, where m² = -λv²/6

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-thermal fixed points and turbulent cascades in pure scalar relativistic theories (Berges 2008, Berges 2012) and pure gauge systems (Berges 2008, Berges 2012, Schlichting 2012, Kurkela 2012) to the Abelian Higgs model. The results establish a link between topological defect dynamics in Higgs models and confinement phenomena in Yang-Mills theories, suggesting that the far-from-equilibrium dynamics of gauge theories can be understood through the Higgs model reformulation of Yang-Mills theory in diagonalisation gauges, as discussed by Ford (1998) and others. The findings enable future studies of how confinement and condensation in non-abelian gauge fields emerge from turbulent, strongly coherent gauge field configurations.
