# Kinetic approach to polarization-vorticity coupling and hydrodynamics with spin

> Recently introduced equilibrium Wigner functions for spin-one-half particles are used in the semiclassical kinetic equations to study the relation between spin polarization and vorticity. It is found, in particular, that such a framework does not necessarily imply that the thermal-vorticity and spin polarization tensors are equal. Subsequently, a procedure to formulate the hydrodynamic framework for particles with spin-one-half, based on the semiclassical expansion, is outlined.

## Key claim

The kinetic approach using equilibrium Wigner functions for spin-one-half particles does not necessarily imply that the thermal-vorticity and spin polarization tensors are equal; they can differ in extended global equilibrium where both are constant but not identical, and in extended local equilibrium where they are spacetime-dependent and unequal.

## Method

The author uses the semiclassical kinetic equations for Wigner functions of spin-one-half particles, employing the equilibrium distribution functions introduced by Becattini (2013). The Wigner functions are expanded in powers of \(\hbar\), and the zeroth-order equations for the scalar coefficient \(\mathcal{F}_{(0)}\) and axial-vector coefficient \(\mathcal{A}^\nu_{(0)}\) are solved to determine constraints on hydrodynamic variables. The resulting conservation laws for charge, energy-momentum, and spin tensors are derived in both the de Groot–van Leeuwen–van Weert (GLW) and canonical formulations, and the connection between them via pseudo-gauge transformations is examined.

## Key numbers

- The spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) and thermal vorticity \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}\) are related by \(\varpi_{\mu\nu} = -\frac{1}{2}(\partial_\mu \beta_\nu - \partial_\nu \beta_\mu)\), where \(\beta_\mu = u_\mu/T\).
- The parameter \(\zeta = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}\omega_{\mu\nu}\omega^{\mu\nu}}\) is interpreted as the ratio of spin chemical potential \(\Omega\) to temperature \(T\), i.e., \(\zeta = \Omega/T\).
- The equilibrium Wigner function expansion coefficient \(M^\pm = \cosh(\zeta) \pm \frac{\sinh(\zeta)}{2\zeta} \omega_{\mu\nu}\Sigma^{\mu\nu}\) is used for the spin-dependent part.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic approach to spin polarization and vorticity developed by Becattini (2013, 2015, 2016, 2017) and the hydrodynamic framework with spin proposed by Florkowski (2017, 2018). It provides a critical comparison between thermodynamic and kinetic approaches, showing that the kinetic framework allows for distinct spin polarization and thermal vorticity tensors. The results enable the construction of a complete hydrodynamic theory for spin-one-half particles, with conservation laws for charge, energy-momentum, and spin tensors that can be applied to describe \(\Lambda\)-hyperon spin polarization measurements in heavy-ion collisions by the STAR Collaboration (2017, 2018).
