# Spectral Properties of the k-Body Embedded Gaussian Ensembles of Random Matrices

> We consider $m$ spinless Fermions in $l > m$ degenerate single-particle levels interacting via a $k$-body random interaction with Gaussian probability distribution and $k <= m$ in the limit $l$ to infinity (the embedded $k$-body random ensembles). We address the cases of orthogonal and unitary symmetry. We derive a novel eigenvalue expansion for the second moment of the Hilbert-space matrix elements of these ensembles. Using properties of the expansion and the supersymmetry technique, we show that for $2k > m$, the average spectrum has the shape of a semicircle, and the spectral fluctuations are of Wigner-Dyson type. Using a generalization of the binary correlation approximation, we show that for $k << m << l$, the spectral fluctuations are Poissonian. This is consistent with the case $k = 1$ which can be solved explicitly. We construct limiting ensembles which are either fully integrable or fully chaotic and show that the $k$-body random ensembles lie between these two extremes. Combining all these results we find that the spectral correlations for the embedded ensembles gradually change from Wigner-Dyson for $2k > m$ to Poissonian for $k << m << l$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the spectral fluctuations of the k-body embedded Gaussian ensembles EGOE(k) and EGUE(k) gradually transition from Wigner-Dyson type when 2k > m to Poissonian when k << m << l, contrary to the common belief that these ensembles always follow Wigner-Dyson statistics in the limit of infinite matrix dimension.

## Method

The authors derive a novel eigenvalue expansion for the second moment of the Hilbert-space matrix elements of the k-body embedded Gaussian ensembles, using the invariance of these ensembles under orthogonal or unitary transformations of the single-particle basis. They apply the supersymmetry method to solve saddle-point equations for the regime 2k > m, and generalize the binary correlation approximation of Mon and French (1975) to calculate level correlation functions for the regime k << m << l. Limiting ensembles with minimum and maximum numbers of independent random variables are constructed to bracket the behavior of the embedded ensembles.

## Key numbers

- Dimension of Hilbert space: N = C(l, m), where l is the number of single-particle states and m is the number of fermions
- Number of links per vertex: M = sum_{s=1}^k C(m, s) C(l-m, s)
- Minimum number of links to connect any two vertices: L_min <= [m/k], where brackets denote the floor function
- Number of independent random variables: K_beta = (beta/2) C(l, k) [C(l, k) + delta_{beta,1}]
- Eigenvalue for s=0 class: Lambda^{(0)}(k) = C(m, k) C(l-m+k, k)
- Eigenvalue for s=1 class: Lambda^{(1)}(k) = C(m-1, k) C(l-m+k-1, k)
- Eigenvalue for general class s: Lambda^{(s)}(k) = C(m-s, k) C(l-m+k-s, k)
- Dimension of class s: D^{(s)} = C(l, s)^2 - C(l, s-1)^2 for s >= 1, with D^{(0)} = 1

## Context

This work addresses a gap identified by Brody et al. (1981) regarding the lack of a comprehensive analytical theory for spectral fluctuations of embedded Gaussian ensembles. It extends the foundational work of Mon and French (1975) who introduced the k-body embedded Gaussian ensembles, and the binary correlation method they developed. The results enable understanding of when realistic many-body systems with few-body interactions (such as atomic nuclei with two-body forces) can be expected to follow Wigner-Dyson statistics versus Poissonian statistics, and explain why numerical simulations may show apparent agreement with random matrix theory due to finite-size effects that disappear in the l to infinity limit.
