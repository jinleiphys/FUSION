# In-medium Hadrons - Properties, Interaction and Formation

> In this talk various aspects of in-medium behavior of hadrons are discussed with an emphasis on observable effects. Examples for theoretical predictions of in-medium spectral functions are given and the importance of resonance-hole excitations is stressed. It is also stressed that final state interactions can have a major effect on observables and thus have to be considered as part of the theory. This is demonstrated with examples from neutrino-nucleus interactions. Finally, the possibility to access hadron formation times in high-energy photonuclear (or neutrino-induced) reactions is illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in-medium modifications of hadrons (such as vector mesons) are best studied not through simple mass shifts but through changes in spectral functions, and that any theoretical prediction must include final-state interactions treated with the same accuracy as the in-medium effects themselves to be compared with observables.

## Method

The authors employ a resonance-hole model to calculate in-medium spectral functions for rho and omega mesons, using coupling constants from nucleon resonance decays to meson-nucleon channels. For neutrino-nucleus reactions, they implement final-state interactions using the GiBUU semiclassical transport method, including elastic and inelastic rescattering, particle production, and sidefeeding. For hadron formation studies, they use a transport model with a time-dependent prehadronic cross section that rises linearly with time, as predicted by perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- The D13(1520) resonance has a partial decay width into rho N of about 20%, lying about 200 MeV below the nominal threshold for rho N decay
- The omega spectral function at normal nuclear density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^3) shows considerable broadening but hardly any shift
- The CBELSA/TAPS experiment found a lowering of the omega mass by about 14% at rho_0 and a width of about 55 MeV for slow omegas
- For neutrino-induced pion production on Fe at 1 GeV, the ratio of cross sections with and without final-state interactions drops to about R = 0.4 for kinetic energies beyond about 200 MeV
- The simplified formation-time model uses a formation time of about 0.5 fm/c in the rest frame of the hadron being formed

## Context

This work extends the resonance-hole approach previously applied to pions (the Delta-hole model from Ericson and Weise (1988)) to vector mesons, building on earlier QCD sum rule predictions by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) that were later refined by Leupold and Mosel (1998). The paper connects nucleon resonance physics with in-medium effects, and the transport calculations using GiBUU enable comparison with experiments such as CBELSA/TAPS for omega mesons (Trnka et al., 2005) and neutrino-nucleus measurements. The hadron formation studies address the discrepancy between HERMES and EMC attenuation data that was problematic in earlier work by Falter (2004).
