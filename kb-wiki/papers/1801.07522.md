# Uncertainties in radiative neutron-capture rates relevant to the $A\sim 80$ $r$-process peak

> The rapid neutron-capture process ($r$-process) has for the first time been confirmed to take place in a neutron-star merger event. A detailed understanding of the rapid neutron-capture process is one of the holy grails in nuclear astrophysics. In this work we investigate one aspect of the $r$-process modelling: uncertainties in radiative neutron-capture cross sections and astrophysical reaction rates for isotopes of the elements Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Ga, Ge, As, and Se. In particular, we study deviations from standard libraries used for astrophysics, and the influence of a very-low $γ$-energy enhancement in the average, reduced $γ$-decay probability on the ($n,γ$) rates. We find that the intrinsic uncertainties are in some cases extremely large, and that the low-energy enhancement, if present in neutron-rich nuclei, may increase the neutron-capture reaction rate significantly.

## Key claim

The intrinsic uncertainties in radiative neutron-capture cross sections and reaction rates for isotopes of Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Ga, Ge, As, and Se are in some cases extremely large, and the low-energy enhancement in the gamma-ray strength function, if present in neutron-rich nuclei, may increase the neutron-capture reaction rate significantly.

## Method

The authors used the nuclear-reaction code TALYS-1.8 to calculate radiative neutron-capture cross sections and astrophysical reaction rates for isotopes of Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Ga, Ge, As, and Se. They varied all available combinations of level density models (six models), gamma-ray strength function models (eight models), neutron optical-model potentials (two models), and mass models (four models) to determine the spread in predicted cross sections and rates, and compared the results to experimental data from the EXFOR database and to the JINA REACLIB and BRUSLIB libraries.

## Key numbers

- For stable and near-stable nuclei, the lower and upper TALYS cross sections span a factor of approximately 5 to 10.
- For neutron-rich nuclei at astrophysical temperature T = 0.1 GK, the ratio of maximum to minimum TALYS reaction rates reaches approximately 2000 for 81Ni(n,gamma), approximately 500 for 76Ni(n,gamma), and approximately 200 for 75Co(n,gamma).
- At T = 2.0 GK, the ratio of maximum to minimum TALYS rates is approximately 2000 for 81Ni(n,gamma) and approximately 200 for 85Ge(n,gamma).
- The 82Ni(n,gamma) rate at T = 0.1 GK had a TALYS minimum rate of zero, so the ratio could not be computed.
- The variation in reaction rates for some neutron-rich nuclei exceeds the factor of 100 considered in the sensitivity study of Surman et al. (2014).

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity study of Surman et al. (2014), which identified certain (n,gamma) reaction rates for A ~ 80 nuclei as having significant impact on final r-process abundances, and the earlier work of Larsen and Goriely (2010) on the low-energy enhancement in the gamma-ray strength function. The results underline the urgent need for improved microscopic models for level densities, gamma-ray strength functions, and neutron optical-model potentials, as the phenomenological models currently used in standard libraries such as JINA REACLIB and BRUSLIB have predictive power of only a factor of 5 to 10 even for stable nuclei, and much larger uncertainties for neutron-rich nuclei.
