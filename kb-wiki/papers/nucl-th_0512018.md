# Sound Wave in Hot Dense Matter Created in Heavy Ion Collision

> A model to study the sound wave in hot dense matter created in heavy ion collisions by jet is proposed.The preliminary data of jet shape analysis of PHENIX Collaboration for all centralities and two directions is well explained in this model. Then the wavelength of the sound wave, the natural frequency of the hot dense matter and the speed of sound wave are estimated from the fit.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the double-peak structure observed in two-particle azimuthal correlations in non-peripheral Au+Au collisions can be explained by a sound wave model where the perturbation induced by a fast parton propagates as a coherent wave in the hot dense medium, with the caveat that the model uses a speed of sound (0.129c) much smaller than the value typically used in Mach cone calculations (0.33c).

## Method

The authors model the energy deposited by a fast parton as a sound wave obeying a standard wave equation in the hot dense medium, treating the medium as homogeneous and at rest. They introduce three parameters: the speed of sound in the medium, the velocity of a co-moving system around the quenched parton (accounting for Doppler effects), and the ratio of the source size to the sound wavelength. The resulting diffraction pattern is compared to PHENIX jet shape data for different centralities and for both forward and backward jets.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound in the hot dense matter: \(c_s = 0.129c\) (from fit to PHENIX data, much smaller than the 0.33c used in Mach cone calculations)
- Wavelength of the sound wave: \(\lambda = 13.3\) fm (estimated using a source size of 5 fm from double HBT radius for central collisions)
- Natural frequency of the hot dense matter: \(2.91 \times 10^{21}\) Hz (calculated as \(c_s/\lambda\))
- Particle momentum used in calculation: 2.5 GeV (average of the experimental momentum range 2-3 GeV)
- Ratio of source size to wavelength for 0-10% centrality forward jet: \(s/\lambda = 0.15\); for 60-92% centrality forward jet: \(s/\lambda = 0.08\)
- Co-moving system velocity for 0-10% centrality forward jet: \(c_c = 0.92c\); for 60-92% centrality forward jet: \(c_c = 0.95c\)
- Intensity ratios between forward and backward jets: 0.60 for 0-10% centrality, 0.51 for 30-40% centrality, 0.67 for 60-92% centrality

## Context

This work extends the conical flow model proposed by Casalderrey-Solana (2004) and the colored sound mode ideas of Ruppert and Muller (2004-2005), which were developed to explain the double-peak structure in away-side correlations observed by PHENIX. The model introduces a more microscopic treatment of the sound wave as an intrinsic collective excitation of the hot dense matter, and it enables future tests at LHC where higher-momentum triggers should produce a decrease in the separation between the two peaks in the backward jet due to Lorentz transformation effects.
