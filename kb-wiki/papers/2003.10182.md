# Radiative $p_{\perp}$-broadening of fast partons in an expanding quark-gluon plasma

> We study contribution of radiative processes to $p_{\perp}$-broadening of fast partons in an expanding quark-gluon plasma. It is shown that the radiative correction to $\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle$ for the QGP produced in $AA$-collisions at RHIC and LHC may be negative, and comparable in absolute value with the non-radiative contribution. We have found that the QGP expansion enhances the radiative suppression of $p_\perp$-broadening as compared to the static medium.

## Key claim

The radiative correction to the mean squared transverse momentum \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle\) of fast partons in an expanding quark-gluon plasma may be negative and comparable in absolute value to the non-radiative contribution, with the QGP expansion enhancing this radiative suppression relative to the static medium case.

## Method

The author uses the light-cone path integral (LCPI) formalism to compute the radiative contribution to \(p_{\perp}\)-broadening from real \(q\to qg\) splitting and virtual \(q\to qg\to q\) processes, including Glauber factors for initial and final state interactions. The QGP expansion is modeled via Bjorken's scaling with a transport coefficient \(\hat{q}(\tau)=\hat{q}_0(\tau_0/\tau)\) for \(\tau>\tau_0\), and the oscillator approximation is employed for the three-body Hamiltonian. Numerical calculations are performed for conditions corresponding to central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV), with the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_0\) calibrated to reproduce the quark energy loss from previous analyses of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\).

## Key numbers

* For the expanding QGP at RHIC with \(m_q=300\) MeV, \(m_g=400\) MeV and \(\hat{q}'=r\hat{q}\): \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_r/\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = -0.757\), with \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = 7.96\) GeV\(^2\).
* For the expanding QGP at LHC with \(m_q=300\) MeV, \(m_g=400\) MeV and \(\hat{q}'=r\hat{q}\): \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_r/\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = -0.836\), with \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = 11.85\) GeV\(^2\).
* For the static medium at RHIC with the same masses and \(\hat{q}'=r\hat{q}\): \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_r/\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = -0.63\), with \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = 5.3\) GeV\(^2\).
* For the static medium at LHC with the same masses and \(\hat{q}'=r\hat{q}\): \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_r/\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = -0.719\), with \(\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle_0 = 7.89\) GeV\(^2\).
* The transport coefficient at formation time \(\tau_0=0.5\) fm is \(\hat{q}_0\approx 0.551\) GeV\(^3\) at \(E=30\) GeV for RHIC and \(\hat{q}_0\approx 0.719\) GeV\(^3\) at \(E=100\) GeV for LHC.
* The ratio of transport coefficients for the Glauber factor to the oscillator Hamiltonian is \(r\approx 2\) for RHIC and \(r\approx 2.3\) for LHC.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of radiative \(p_{\perp}\)-broadening in static media (Zakharov, 2019, 2020) to the more realistic case of an expanding QGP, following the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1983). It contradicts the expectation from Mueller (2000) that the radiative correction is positive and comparable to the non-radiative contribution, by showing that inclusion of initial-state Glauber factors yields a negative net radiative correction. The results suggest that the absence of a clear jet deflection signal in STAR (STAR Collaboration, 2017) and ALICE (ALICE Collaboration, 2019) data may be due to compensation between radiative and non-radiative contributions, and they enable more accurate modeling of jet \(p_{\perp}\)-broadening in heavy-ion collisions.
