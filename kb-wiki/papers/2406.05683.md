# Simulating Parton Fragmentation on Quantum Computers

> Parton fragmentation functions (FFs) are indispensable for understanding processes of hadron production ubiquitously existing in high-energy collisions, but their first principle determination has never been realized due to the insurmountable difficulties in encoding their operator definition using traditional lattice methodology. We propose a framework that makes a first step for evaluating FFs utilizing quantum computing methodology. The key element is to construct a semi-inclusive hadron operator for filtering out hadrons of desired types in a collection of particles encoded in the quantum state. We illustrate the framework by elaborating on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with numeral simulations. Remarkably, We show that the semi-inclusive hadron operator can be constructed efficiently with a variational quantum algorithm. Moreover, we develop error mitigation techniques tailed for accurately calculating the FFs in the presence of quantum noises. Our work opens a new avenue for investigating QCD hadronization on near-term quantum computers.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a framework for the first direct evaluation of parton fragmentation functions using quantum computing, but the demonstration is limited to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in 1+1 dimensions as a proof of concept, not full QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a semi-inclusive hadron operator that filters out identified hadrons from multi-particle states encoded in qubits on a spatial lattice. They use a variational quantum algorithm with a Hamiltonian variational ansatz to prepare physical vacuum and one-particle states from bare computational basis states, then convert the fragmentation function into multi-point dynamical correlators. The approach is validated by simulating the quantum circuits classically for the one-flavor 1+1D Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model discretized with staggered fermions and mapped to qubits via the Jordan-Wigner transformation.

## Key numbers

* Quark fragmentation functions were calculated for system sizes of N=10, N=14, N=18, and N=22 qubits, with dressed quark mass m_q a = 0.4 and hadron mass m_h a = 0.6.
* The total circuit depth for the proposed quantum algorithm scales as O(N^3/epsilon) for N qubits and desired accuracy epsilon.
* Error mitigation simulations used a depolarization noise model with noise level p = 10^{-3} per gate, with Richardson extrapolation up to 7th order (c_j = (1+0.1j) x 10^{-3}, j=1,...,7).
* For the error mitigation demonstration, N=6 qubits were used with m a = 1, g = 0.8, and quantum gate fidelity 0.999.

## Context

This work extends the quantum computing approach for parton distribution functions from light-cone correlators (Li 2021) to fragmentation functions, addressing a long-standing challenge where lattice QCD methods have been unable to compute FFs from first principles due to sign problems and the difficulty of encoding semi-inclusive hadron operators. The results qualitatively agree with extracted quark fragmentation functions (Gao 2024) and NJL model calculations (Yang 2020), and the framework enables future calculations on near-term quantum processors for QCD hadronization.
