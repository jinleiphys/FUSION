# Hot Nuclear Matter in the Quark Meson Coupling Model

> We study here hot nuclear matter in the quark meson coupling (QMC) model which incorporates explicitly quark degrees of freedom, with quarks coupled to scalar and vector mesons. The equation of state of nuclear matter including the composite nature of the nucleons is calculated at finite temperatures. The calculations are done taking into account the medium-dependent bag constant. Nucleon properties at finite temperatures as calculated here are found to be appreciably different from the value at $T=0.$

# Hot Nuclear Matter in the Quark Meson Coupling Model

## Key claim

The authors study hot nuclear matter using the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model with a medium-dependent bag constant, finding that the effective nucleon mass increases monotonically with temperature up to 250 MeV, in contrast to the Walecka model where it falls sharply near 200 MeV. This behavior arises because thermal excitations of quarks inside the nucleon bag dominate over the sigma field contribution, a result that depends on the specific ansatz chosen for the density-dependent bag constant.

## Method

The authors generalize the QMC model to finite temperatures by allowing thermal excitation of quarks and antiquarks inside the MIT bag to higher angular momentum states, with a temperature-dependent bag radius determined by minimizing the effective nucleon mass. The scalar and vector mean fields are determined self-consistently through minimization of the thermodynamic grand potential, using a specific exponential ansatz for the medium-dependent bag constant B = B0 exp(-4 g_sigma^B sigma / M_N). The model parameters (g_omega^2/4pi = 5.24, (g_sigma^B)^2/4pi = 3.69) are fitted to saturation properties of nuclear matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon bag constant: B0^(1/4) = 188.1 MeV, with Z = 2.03 and current quark mass m_q^0 = 0
- Vector meson coupling fitted from saturation: g_omega^2/4pi = 5.24
- Additional bag parameter fitted from saturation: (g_sigma^B)^2/4pi = 3.69
- Scalar sigma field becomes nonzero at a temperature of 200 MeV, indicating a phase transition to baryon-antibaryon pairs
- Effective nucleon mass increases monotonically with temperature up to at least 250 MeV, unlike the Walecka model where it falls sharply at T ~ 200 MeV
- Bag constant grows with temperature for densities greater than approximately 0.1 fm^-3
- Free nucleon bag radius R0 = 0.6 fermis; the nucleon bag shrinks with increasing temperature
- Pressure at zero baryon density becomes nonzero at and above a temperature of 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the QMC model of Guichon (1988) and the modified QMC model with medium-dependent bag constant of Jin and collaborators (1992) to finite temperatures. It directly contrasts with the Walecka model calculations of Furnstahl and Serot (1990), where the effective nucleon mass first rises then falls rapidly near 200 MeV, and where the phase transition is sharper. The results enable further study of the nuclear matter phase diagram at finite temperature, though the authors note the difficulty of obtaining a baryon that swells with temperature, a problem also encountered in thermal skyrmion calculations by Dey and collaborators (1990).
