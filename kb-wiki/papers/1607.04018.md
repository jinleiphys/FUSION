# Unravelling the physical meaning of the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition of the nucleon spin

> A general consensus now is that there are two physically inequivalent complete decompositions of the nucleon spin, i.e. the decomposition of the canonical type and that of mechanical type. The well-known Jaffe-Manohar decomposition is of the former type. Unfortunately, there is a wide-spread misbelief that this decomposition matches the partonic picture, which states that motion of quarks in the nucleon is approximately free. In the present monograph, we reveal that this understanding is not necessarily correct and that the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition is not such a decomposition, which natively reflects the intrinsic (or static) orbital angular momentum structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The Jaffe-Manohar decomposition of the nucleon spin is widely believed to match the partonic picture of approximately free quarks, but this understanding is not necessarily correct; the decomposition does not natively reflect the intrinsic (or static) orbital angular momentum structure of the nucleon.

## Method

The author analyzes the Landau problem of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field to contrast the physical meaning of canonical versus mechanical orbital angular momentum (OAM), then applies the nonabelian Stokes theorem to the QCD case to relate the path dependence of Wigner distributions to the difference between canonical and mechanical quark OAM in the nucleon. The relation between the two types of OAM derived by Burkardt (2013) is rederived using the nonabelian Stokes theorem following the suggestion of Tiwari (2015).

## Key numbers

- In the Landau problem, the quantized energy levels are \(E_n = \left(n + \frac{1}{2}\right) \omega\) with \(\omega = eB/m\), and the cyclotron radius is quantized as \(r_n = \sqrt{\frac{1}{eB}} \cdot \sqrt{2n+1}\).
- The expectation value of the mechanical OAM in a Landau state \(|n, n+m\rangle\) is \(\langle L_{mech} \rangle = -(2n+1)\), independent of the quantum number \(m\).
- The expectation value of the canonical OAM in the same state is \(\langle L_{can} \rangle = m\), which depends on the gauge choice and does not appear in the observable energy.
- In the Landau gauge, the expectation value of the mechanical OAM is \(\langle L_{mech} \rangle = -(2n+1)\), reproducing the symmetric-gauge result, while the canonical OAM expectation value differs (\(\langle L_{can} \rangle = k_x^2/(eB)\)).

## Context

This work extends the debate on gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin, specifically challenging the widespread belief that the Jaffe-Manohar (1990) decomposition is compatible with the partonic picture. The author contrasts the canonical-type decomposition with the mechanical-type decomposition previously advocated in Wakamatsu (2010, 2011, 2012, 2014), and uses the Landau problem to argue that the mechanical OAM is the physically meaningful quantity under strong color-magnetic fields. The analysis builds on the nonabelian Stokes theorem as applied by Tiwari (2015) and the relation between canonical and mechanical OAM derived by Burkardt (2013).
