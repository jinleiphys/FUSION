# The GDH sum rule for the deuteron

> As first topic, the GDH sume rule is discussed in the context of a more general class of sum rules associated with the various contributions to the total photoabsorption cross section for target and beam polarization. Then I address the question of whether the GDH sum rule for the neutron can be determined from the one for the deuteron. It appears that this will not be possible in a simple manner. The spin response of the deuteron is calculated including contributions from the photodisintegration channel and from coherent and incoherent single pion production as well, and the GDH integral is evaluated up to a photon energy of 550 MeV. The photodisintegration channel converges fast enough and gives a large negative contribution, essentially from the $^1S_0$ state near threshold and its absolute size is about the same than the sum of proton and neutron GDH values. It is only partially cancelled by the single pion production contribution. But the incoherent channel has not reached convergence at 550 MeV.

## Key claim

The GDH sum rule for the deuteron cannot be used to extract the neutron GDH sum rule in a simple manner because the neutron is not fully polarized in a vector-polarized deuteron target, and the spin asymmetry of the deuteron is not an incoherent sum of proton and neutron contributions due to binding, final state interactions, and coherent pion production effects.

## Method

The GDH integral for the deuteron was evaluated by explicit integration up to a photon energy of 550 MeV, including three separate channels: photodisintegration (gamma d -> n p), coherent single pion production (gamma d -> pi0 d), and incoherent single pion production (gamma d -> pi N N). The photodisintegration channel was calculated nonrelativistically with the Bonn potential, including one-body currents, meson exchange contributions, isobar configurations, and leading-order relativistic contributions. The coherent pion production was modeled with a coupled-channels approach for the N-Delta, NN-pi, and NN channels, while the incoherent channel used a spectator nucleon model with pseudovector Born terms and Delta resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_d = -0.143, giving a GDH prediction of I^GDH_d = 0.65 microbarn
- Photodisintegration channel contribution to GDH integral at 550 MeV: -413 microbarn (with full model including relativistic contributions)
- Coherent pi0 production contribution at 550 MeV: +63 microbarn
- Incoherent pion production total contribution at 550 MeV: +167 microbarn (from the model calculation)
- Sum of proton and neutron GDH values from multipole analysis (fit SM95) at 550 MeV: 331 microbarn
- Total deuteron GDH integral at 550 MeV using model values: -183 microbarn
- Total deuteron GDH integral at 550 MeV using multipole analysis for incoherent channel: -19 microbarn
- Photodisintegration contribution with nucleon anomalous magnetic moment switched off: +7.3 microbarn

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Gerasimov (1965) and Drell and Hearn (1966) for the GDH sum rule to the deuteron, addressing the question of whether the neutron sum rule can be extracted from deuteron measurements. The calculation contradicts the simple assumption that the deuteron spin asymmetry is an incoherent sum of proton and neutron contributions, as had been suggested for extracting the neutron value. The results enable future experimental tests of the low-energy spin asymmetry in photodisintegration and provide a benchmark for more complete theoretical models that include two-body effects and higher-energy contributions beyond 550 MeV.
