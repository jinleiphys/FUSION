# Narrow deeply bound $K^-$ atomic states

> Using optical potentials fitted to a comprehensive set of strong interaction level shifts and widths in $K^-$ atoms, we predict that the $K^-$ atomic levels which are inaccessible in the atomic cascade process are generally narrow, spanning a range of widths about 50 - 1500 keV over the entire periodic table. The mechanism for this narrowing is different from the mechanism for narrowing of pionic atom levels. Examples of such `deeply bound' $K^-$ atomic states are given, showing that in many cases these states should be reasonably well resolved. Several reactions which could be used to form these `deeply bound' states are mentioned. Narrow deeply bound states are expected also in $\bar{p}$ atoms.

## Key claim

Using optical potentials fitted to a comprehensive set of strong interaction level shifts and widths in $K^-$ atoms, the authors predict that $K^-$ atomic levels inaccessible in the atomic cascade process are generally narrow (widths about 50–1500 keV over the periodic table), due to a mechanism of effective repulsion induced by strong absorptive potentials rather than the repulsive real potential that narrows pionic atom levels.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation for $K^-$ atoms using phenomenological density-dependent (DD) and $t\rho$ optical potentials fitted to 65 data points of kaonic atom strong interaction level shifts and widths across the periodic table, with $\chi^2$ per point around 1.6. They calculated binding energies and widths for deeply bound atomic states (those below the lowest levels reached by X-ray cascades) for nuclei including carbon, nickel, and lead, and tested sensitivity by comparing results between the two potential forms and between macroscopic and single-particle nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- For the $1s$ atomic state in kaonic carbon: width = 62 keV, binding energy = 270 keV, making it resolved from the 2 keV broad $2p$ level bound by 111 keV.
- The width of the $1s$ state in carbon is 50 times smaller than the 3.1 MeV width that would result from the Coulomb wave function alone.
- For the $5g$ level in kaonic Pb, the width saturates such that the difference between calculations with and without the real part of the optical potential becomes less than 50% at the nominal value of Im $b_0$.
- For circular $l=1...5$ levels in Pb, widths change by less than 5% when replacing the $t\rho$ potential with the DD potential; level positions change by less than 12 keV.
- The binding energy of the $1s$ level varies by up to 40 keV between potential models, which is only 2.5% of the level width.

## Context

This work extends the study of deeply bound hadronic atom states, previously observed only for pions in experiments on $^{208}$Pb using the ($d$, $^3$He) recoilless reaction (Yamazaki 1996, 1998; Toki 1988). The mechanism for narrowing in $K^-$ atoms is different from the repulsive $s$-wave potential that narrows pionic states (Friedman 1985; Toki 1988), and instead relies on absorptive-potential-induced repulsion first noted by Krell (1971). The predictions enable experimental searches for deeply bound kaonic states using low-momentum-transfer reactions such as ($K^-,p$) with $K^-$ from $\phi$(1020) decay at DA$\Phi$NE or via quasi-free $\bar{p}p$ annihilation at GSI.
