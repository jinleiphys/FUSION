# Dihadron Fragmentation and the Confinement Transition in Energy Correlators

> In this letter, we relate the factorization for $e^+e^- \to h_1 h_2 X$ to the factorization for energy-energy correlators in the collinear limit. This enables us to give a nonperturbative proof of factorization for the energy correlators, relate the energy correlator jet function to transverse-momentum-sensitive dihadron fragmentation functions, and provide a rigorous description of the confinement transition region.

## Key claim

The paper provides a nonperturbative proof of factorization for energy-energy correlators (EEC) in the collinear limit, valid even in the confinement transition region where \(Q^2 z \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2\), by relating the EEC jet function to transverse-momentum-sensitive dihadron fragmentation functions; the abstract's claim of a "nonperturbative proof" is qualified by the fact that the proof relies on the operator definitions of the fragmentation functions and the refactorization of those functions in the perturbative regime.

## Method

The authors start from the standard factorization for \(e^+e^- \to h_1 h_2 X\) dihadron production in terms of hard coefficients \(H_i\) and transverse-momentum-sensitive dihadron fragmentation functions \(D_{i\to h_j h_k}\). They then express the EEC in the collinear region (\(z\ll 1\)) as a derivative of an integral over these dihadron and single-hadron cross sections, using the relation between the angular variable \(z\) and the transverse momenta of the two hadrons. This yields a factorization formula for the EEC where the jet function \(J_i(x^2 Q^2 z,\mu)\) is explicitly written as a sum over weighted integrals of the dihadron and single-hadron fragmentation functions. The RG evolution of the jet function is derived from the timelike DGLAP evolution of the fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

* The EEC jet function \(J_i\) depends on the combination \(x^2 Q^2 z\), and the factorization is valid for \(Q^2 z \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2\).
* The nonperturbative jet function is modeled as \(J_i(Q^2 z,\mu_0) = \int_0^{Q^2 z} d\nu \, \frac{N_i}{1+(\nu/\Lambda_i)^{1+\delta_i}}\), with the fit performed at \(\mu_0 = 10\ {\rm GeV}\).
* The starting input for the fit uses Pythia simulations at \(Q = m_Z = 91.2\ {\rm GeV}\).
* The LL and NLL RG evolution results are shown for \(Q = m_Z/2\), \(m_Z\), and \(2 m_Z\), with a dramatic reduction in perturbative uncertainty at NLL.

## Context

This work extends the factorization of the EEC in the collinear region, previously derived in the OPE region (\(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2/Q^2 \ll z \ll 1\)) by Dixon et al. (2019), into the nonperturbative confinement transition region. It provides a rigorous QCD foundation for recent phenomenological studies of the confinement transition by Liu et al. (2024) and Barata et al. (2024), which used TMD-factorization-inspired models or collinear factorization with a nonperturbative Sudakov factor. The results enable predictions of the \(Q\) dependence of the EEC in the small-angle regime through perturbative RG evolution, and establish a direct connection between energy correlators and multi-hadron fragmentation functions, opening the door to studying confinement with different quantum numbers (spin, charge, heavy flavor) and in jet and nuclear substructure.
