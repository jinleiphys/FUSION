# Protonium annihilation densities in a unitary coupled channel model

> We consider a unitary coupled channel model to describe the low energy proton-antiproton scattering and the lower Coulomb-like protonium states. The existence of deeper quasi-bound states of nuclear nature is found to be a consequence of the experimental data. The properties of these states as well as the protonium annihilation densities are described and the difference with respect to the optical models description are manifested.

## Key claim

The existence of deeper quasi-bound states of nuclear nature in the proton-antiproton system is a consequence of experimental data, and the unitary coupled channel model predicts that annihilation has an attractive effect on these states (increasing their real energy), contrary to the repulsive effect seen in optical models.

## Method

The authors constructed a unitary coupled channel model coupling the p̅p and n̅n channels to an explicit meson-meson channel via a short-range Yukawa-like annihilation potential with range r_a = 1/m_p ≈ 0.21 fm, using a real and hermitian potential matrix to preserve unitarity. The real part of the N̅N interaction was taken from a modified version of the Bryan-Phillips meson-exchange potential (Model I) and alternatively from the Nijmegen N̅N potential (Model II), with short-range singularities regularized by imposing a C^1 polynomial matching below a cutoff radius r_c. The model parameters (cutoff radius r_c and annihilation strength λ) were adjusted per partial wave to reproduce experimental p̅p scattering lengths extracted from protonium level shifts via the Trueman expansion.

## Key numbers

- Annihilation potential range: r_a = 1/m_p ≈ 0.21 fm
- For the ¹¹S₀ quasi-nuclear state without annihilation (W₀=0), the bound state energy is E = -54.7 MeV with rms radius R = √⟨r²⟩ = 1.3 fm
- Experimental singlet S-wave protonium energy shift: ΔE(¹S₀) = 0.440(75) - i 0.600(125) keV
- Experimental triplet S-wave protonium energy shift: ΔE(³SD₁) = 0.785(35) - i 0.470(40) keV
- Extracted singlet p̅p scattering length: a₀ = 0.49(9) - i 0.73(14) fm
- Extracted triplet p̅p scattering length: a₁ = 0.93(4) - i 0.60(5) fm
- KW optical model predictions: a₀ = 0.57 - i 0.77 fm, a₁ = 0.92 - i 0.63 fm
- Meson masses used in Model I: π (138.2 MeV), η (548 MeV), ρ (760 MeV), ω (782 MeV), σ₀ (770 MeV), σ₁ (560 MeV)
- πN coupling constant: g = 13.5

## Context

This work extends the unitary coupled channel approach previously applied to baryon-antibaryon states by Dalkarov et al. (1990) and Carbonell et al. (1993, 1994) to describe protonium annihilation densities, which had only been computed using optical models such as those by Carbonell et al. (1989). The study contrasts the unitary approach with optical models like Dover-Richard (1980, 1982), Kohno-Weise (1985), and the Paris N̅N potential (1982, 1994, 1999, 2009), showing that unitarity and short annihilation range produce qualitatively different behavior for quasi-nuclear states. The results are relevant for interpreting annihilation data in the PUMA project at CERN, which aims to correlate annihilation with peripheral neutron density distributions in nuclei.
