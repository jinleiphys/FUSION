# Z(3) metastable states in Polyakov Quark Meson model

> We study the existence of Z(3) metastable states in the presence of the dynamical quarks within the ambit of Polyakov quark meson (PQM) model. Within the parameters of the model, it is seen that for temperatures $T_m$ greater than the chiral transition temperature $T_c$, Z(3) metastable states exist ( $T_{m} \sim 310$ MeV at zero chemical potential). At finite chemical potential $T_m$ is larger than the same at vanishing chemical potential. We also observe a shift of ($\sim 5^\circ$) in the phase of the metastable vacua at zero chemical potential. The energy density difference between true and Z(3) metastable vacua is very large in this model. This indicates a strong explicit symmetry breaking effect due to quarks in PQM model. We compare this explicit symmetry breaking in PQM model with small explicit symmetry breaking as a linear term in Polyakov loop added to the Polyakov loop potential. We also study about the possibility of domain growth in a quenched transition to QGP in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Z(3) metastable states exist in the Polyakov Quark Meson (PQM) model only for temperatures above approximately 310 MeV at zero chemical potential, not at all temperatures above the chiral transition temperature T_c, indicating a strong explicit symmetry breaking effect due to dynamical quarks rather than the small symmetry breaking assumed in earlier models.

## Method

The authors use the Polyakov Quark Meson (PQM) model in a mean-field approximation, integrating out quark degrees of freedom to obtain an effective thermodynamic potential that couples the Polyakov loop order parameter to the chiral condensate. They minimize this potential with respect to the real and imaginary parts of the Polyakov loop and the sigma field to locate the true vacuum (at θ=0) and search for local minima in the second and third quadrants of the complex Polyakov loop plane to identify metastable Z(3) vacua. Two parameterizations of the pure-gauge Polyakov loop potential are compared: one with a large barrier between Z(3) vacua (parameter set 1 from Ref. psrsk2) and one with a small barrier (parameter set 2 from Ref. scfer).

## Key numbers

- Z(3) metastable states first appear at T_m ∼ 310 MeV at zero chemical potential, which is above the chiral transition temperature T_c ∼ 180 MeV
- The phase of the metastable vacua is shifted by ∼ 5° from the ideal Z(3) angles (appearing at 115° and 245° instead of 120° and 240°)
- The free energy density difference between true and metastable vacua at T ∼ 310 MeV is ∼ 0.3 GeV fm⁻³, rising to ∼ 4.0 GeV fm⁻³ at the onset temperature
- The chiral crossover occurs at T = 180 MeV, and the Polyakov loop modulus reaches a value of 1.11 at high temperature
- The pion mass is 138 MeV, the sigma mass is 600 MeV, and the constituent quark mass in vacuum is 300 MeV (from g = 3.2 and f_π = 93 MeV)

## Context

This work extends studies of Z(3) metastable states in QCD with dynamical quarks, which were previously examined using perturbative calculations by Dixit and Ogilvie (1990s) and in lattice QCD by Deka (2010s) that suggested metastable states only exist at T ≥ 750 MeV. The authors compare their PQM model results with the approach of Pisarski, Skokov, and collaborators (2010s) who added a small linear term in the Polyakov loop to model explicit symmetry breaking; in that approach metastable states exist at all T > T_c with negligible phase shift, whereas the PQM model shows strong symmetry breaking with metastable states only appearing above 310 MeV. This work enables studies of domain growth and non-equilibrium effects in quenched transitions to quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions, building on numerical simulations by Ranjita and Mishra (2015).
