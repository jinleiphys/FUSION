# Jets and elliptic flow correlations at low and high transverse momenta in ultrarelativistic A+A collisions

> Data from the Large Hadron Collider on elliptic flow correlations at low and high $p_T$ from Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$~TeV are analyzed and interpreted in the framework of the HYDJET++ model. This model allows us to describe simultaneously the region of both low and high transverse momenta and, therefore, to reproduce the experimentally observed nontrivial centrality dependence of elliptic flow correlations. The origin of the correlations between low and high-$p_T$ flow components in peripheral lead-lead collisions is traced to correlations of particles in jets.

## Key claim

The HYDJET++ model reproduces the experimentally observed centrality dependence of elliptic flow correlations between low and high transverse momenta in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but only when jet quenching effects are included; the origin of these correlations in peripheral collisions is traced to particle correlations within jets rather than to collective flow.

## Method

The authors used the two-component HYDJET++ event generator, which combines a soft hydro-type thermal hadronic state with a hard state from in-medium multiparton fragmentation via the PYQUEN energy loss model, to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. They calculated elliptic flow coefficients v2{4}(pT) using the Q-cumulant method for four-particle correlations, comparing the model-generated "true" v2^RP(pT) (with respect to the known reaction plane) against the cumulant-restored values and CMS data across seven centrality bins from 5-10% to 50-60%.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Low-pT interval used for correlations: 1.0 < pT < 1.25 GeV/c
- High-pT interval used for correlations: 14 < pT < 20 GeV/c
- Statistics: 2 x 10^6 events for semicentral to 7 x 10^6 events for very peripheral collisions
- The model reproduces CMS data well for pT <= 1.25 GeV/c and pT >= 14 GeV/c; in the intermediate region 4 < pT < 10 GeV/c, both v2^RP(pT) and v2{4}(pT) from HYDJET++ lie below CMS data
- At high pT (>10 GeV/c), v2{4}(pT) from HYDJET++ is systematically larger than v2^RP(pT) for all centralities, with deviations becoming significant for centralities larger than 30-40%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of elliptic flow correlations first reported by the CMS Collaboration (2019) and also seen in ATLAS data, using the HYDJET++ model which is among the few event generators (alongside EPOS and QGSJET) that can simultaneously describe soft and hard physics in heavy-ion collisions. The study clarifies that in peripheral collisions (beyond 30-40% centrality), the four-cumulant method measures primarily particle correlations inside jets rather than genuine collective flow from jet quenching, resolving why v2{4} exceeds the true v2^RP at high pT. The results enable future improvements to the model in the intermediate pT region (4-10 GeV/c) where simple superposition of soft and hard components is insufficient.
