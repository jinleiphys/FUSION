# Dynamical Pair Production at Sub-Barrier Energies for Light Nuclei

> In the collision of two heavy ions, the strong repulsion coming from the Coulomb field is enough to produce $e^+e^-$ pair(s) from vacuum fluctuations. The energy is provided by the kinetic energy of the ions and the Coulomb interaction at the production point. If, for instance, the electron is located at the center of mass (C.M.) of the two ions moving along the \emph{z}-axis, and the positron is at a distance \emph{x} from the electron, the ions can be accelerated towards each other since the Coulomb barrier is lowered by the presence of the electron. This screening results in an increase in the kinetic energy of the colliding ions and may result in an increase in the fusion probability of light ions above the adiabatic limit.

## Key claim
Dynamical electron-positron pair production in sub-barrier light-ion collisions can screen the Coulomb repulsion between the nuclei, potentially increasing the fusion probability above the adiabatic limit, but only if the electron is located at the center of mass and the positron tunnels away on a perpendicular axis; the effect is predicted to occur for center-of-mass energies above about 1 MeV for carbon-carbon collisions.

## Method
The authors model pair production using the Schwinger mechanism, treating the positron as tunneling through an effective potential derived from the Klein-Gordon equation. They place the electron at the center of mass of two identical nuclei and the positron on a line perpendicular to the beam axis, compute the tunneling probability via the imaginary-action method, and estimate the cross-section by combining the tunneling probability with a time-dependent factor accounting for the number of tunneling attempts during the ion-ion collision.

## Key numbers
- For two \(^{12}\)C nuclei with surfaces touching, the maximum transverse mass for dynamical pair production is \(m_{T,\text{max}} = 3.14\) MeV.
- For two \(^{238}\)U nuclei with surfaces touching, \(m_{T,\text{max}} = 17.8\) MeV.
- The expected number of virtual pairs available to tunnel for two uranium nuclei with surfaces touching is \(N_p \approx 14\); for carbon in the same configuration, \(N_p \approx 1.2\).
- The tunneling probability \(\Pi_t\) is taken as 0.5 for distances between the classical turning point \(R_i\) and the cutoff distance \(R_x\).
- For \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C, the maximum number of pairs produced is attained near \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 4\) MeV (for \(\Delta E_k = 2m_e\)).
- The predicted cross-sections are of the order of millibarns or less.
- The effect is predicted to occur for \(E_{\text{c.m.}} \ge 1\) MeV for \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C collisions.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous perturbative treatment of vacuum polarization in nuclear collisions (Settlemyre, 2021) to a non-perturbative calculation of real pair production. It builds on the Schwinger formalism for pair production in strong fields and on prior studies of sub-barrier fusion enhancement in light systems, particularly carbon-carbon fusion relevant to stellar nucleosynthesis (Kimura, 2004; Krauss, 1987; Engstler, 1988; Shoppa, 1993; Musumarra, 2001; Kimura, 2005). The results call for experimental coincidence measurements of pair production with fusion fragments to test the predicted enhancement.
