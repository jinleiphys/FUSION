# 3alpha clustering in the excited states of 16C

> The alpha cluster states of 16C are investigated by using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. It is shown that two different types of alpha cluster states exist: triangular and linear-chain states. The former has an approximate isosceles triangular configuration of alpha particles surrounded by four valence neutrons occupying sd-shell, while the latter has the linearly aligned alpha particles with two sd-shell neutrons and two pf-shell neutrons. It is found that the structure of the linear-chain state is qualitatively understood in terms of the 3/2 pi- and 1/2 sigma- molecular orbit as predicted by molecular-orbital model, but there exists non-negligible Be+alpha+2n correlation. The band-head energies of the triangular and linear-chain rotational bands are 8.0 and 15.5 MeV, and the latter is close to the He+Be threshold energy. It is also shown that the linear-chain state becomes the yrast sstate at J=10 with excitation energy 27.8 MeV owing to its very large moment-of-inertia comparable with hyperdeformation.

# 3α clustering in the excited states of ¹⁶C

## Key claim
Two distinct types of 3α cluster states exist in ¹⁶C, triangular and linear-chain configurations, but the linear-chain state exhibits a non-negligible ¹⁰Be+α+2n correlation and parity-asymmetric structure, meaning its interpretation as a pure linear chain is qualified by this cluster substructure. The band-head energies are predicted at 8.0 MeV (triangular) and 15.5 MeV (linear-chain), with the latter close to the ⁴He+¹²Be and ⁶He+¹⁰Be threshold energies.

## Method
The authors performed variational calculations using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, constraining the quadrupole deformation parameter β and later γ to probe stability against bending motion. After angular momentum projection, they applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) using β as the generator coordinate to obtain excitation spectra and band structures. Neutron single-particle orbits were analyzed by constructing the Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian from the optimized intrinsic wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Band-head energy of the triangular rotational band: 8.0 MeV (0⁺₂ state)
- Band-head energy of the linear-chain rotational band: 15.5 MeV (0⁺₅ state)
- Linear-chain 10⁺ yrast state excitation energy: 27.8 MeV
- Linear-chain configuration deformation parameters: (β, γ) = (1.10, 0 degrees)
- Triangular configuration deformation parameters: (β, γ) = (0.70, 41 degrees)
- Ground state binding energy: −110.6 MeV (calculated), compared to observed −110.8 MeV
- Linear-chain moment-of-inertia parameter: ℏ/2ℑ = 112 keV
- Intra-band B(E2; 2⁺₁₀ → 0⁺₅) for linear-chain band: 182.4 e²fm⁴
- Overlap of 0⁺₅ state with linear-chain basis wave function: 0.92 at γ = 3.1 degrees
- Distance between left and center α clusters in linear-chain: 3.5 fm; between right and center: 3.8 fm
- Single-particle energy of most bound valence neutron in linear-chain (orbit e): −5.32 MeV
- Single-particle energy of least bound valence neutron in linear-chain (orbit f): −4.18 MeV

## Context
This work extends prior molecular-orbital model predictions by Itagaki et al. (2001) that the linear-chain configuration in ¹⁶C with (3/2⁻_π)²(1/2⁻_σ)² valence neutron occupation should be stable against bending, but the AMD calculation reveals additional ¹⁰Be+α+2n correlation not captured by the simpler molecular-orbital picture. The triangular 3α configuration is analogous to cluster states previously suggested in ¹³C and ¹⁴C by Suhara et al. (2010) and others. The results provide quantitative predictions, band-head energies, rotational band structure, and large B(E2) strengths, that could guide experimental searches using ⁴He+¹²Be and ⁶He+¹⁰Be reaction channels, and the authors note that a forthcoming paper will extend the stability analysis to other carbon isotopes.
