# Resummation of nuclear enhanced higher twist in the Drell Yan process

> We investigate higher twist contributions to the transverse momentum broadening of Drell Yan pairs in proton nucleus collisions. We revisit the contribution of matrix elements of twist-4 and generalize this to matrix elements of arbitrary twist. An estimate of the maximal nuclear broadening effect is derived. A model for nuclear enhanced matrix elements of arbitrary twist allows us to give the result of a resummation of all twists in closed form. Subleading corrections to the maximal broadening are discussed qualitatively.

# Resummation of nuclear enhanced higher twist in the Drell Yan process

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear enhanced higher twist contributions to transverse momentum broadening of Drell Yan pairs in proton-nucleus collisions can be resummed to all twists in closed form, but only for the contributions that lead to maximal broadening, thereby establishing an upper bound on nuclear effects; subleading corrections that give less broadening are discussed only qualitatively and remain uncalculated.

## Method

The author revisits the twist-4 (double scattering) calculation for Drell Yan in both light cone and covariant gauge, confirming previous results by Guo (1998). The calculation is then generalized to arbitrary twist by considering planar diagrams with n pairs of soft gluons in covariant gauge, applying a 2n-th order collinear expansion in transverse momenta, and taking all derivatives on the transverse delta-function to obtain the maximal broadening contribution. A model for nuclear enhanced matrix elements of arbitrary twist is used to give a closed form for the resummed result.

## Key numbers

- The soft-hard matrix element for twist-4 is approximated as $T_{qg}(\xi_1) \approx \lambda^2 A^{4/3} f_q(\xi_1)$ with $\lambda^2 = 0.01$ GeV$^2$
- The transverse momentum broadening to twist-4 accuracy is $\Delta \langle q_\perp^2 \rangle = \frac{4\pi^2 \alpha_s}{N_c} \lambda^2 A^{1/3}$
- The twist-4 contribution to the second moment of the transverse momentum spectrum is $(Q_\perp^2)_1 = \frac{4\pi c_q^2 \alpha^2}{3 N_c Q^2 S} \int_B^1 \frac{d \xi_2}{\xi_2} T_{qg}(\xi_1) f_{\bar q}(\xi_2) \frac{4\pi^2 \alpha_s}{N_c}$
- The zeroth moment of the transverse momentum spectrum from twist-4 vanishes: $(Q_\perp^0)_1 = 0$
- For arbitrary twist, the cross section for n gluon pairs is $\frac{d \sigma_n}{d Q^2 d q_\perp^2} = \frac{4\pi c_q^2 \alpha^2}{3 N_c S Q^2} \left[ - \frac{4\pi^2 \alpha_s}{N_c} \frac{d}{d q_\perp^2} \right]^n \delta(q_\perp^2) \int_B^1 \frac{d \xi_2}{\xi_2} T_{qg^n}(\xi_1) f_{\bar q}(\xi_2)$

## Context

This work extends the twist-4 calculations for Drell Yan in proton-nucleus collisions by Guo (1998) and Fries, Schafer, Schaffner-Bielich, and Muller (1999, 2000), and generalizes the framework of Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1992, 1994) who first pointed out the nuclear enhancement mechanism based on generalized factorization theorems by Qiu and Sterman (1991). The resummation enables an upper bound estimate for nuclear broadening effects in Drell Yan, relevant for understanding hard processes at RHIC and LHC, though the author notes that QCD factorization fails at twist-6 for symmetric colliding systems as shown by Doria, Frenkel, and Taylor, but remains valid in the proton-nucleus limit.
