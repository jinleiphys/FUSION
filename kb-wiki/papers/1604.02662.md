# In Search of a Pristine Signal for (Scale-)Chiral Symmetry in Nuclei

> I describe the long-standing search for a "smoking-gun" signal for the manifestation of (scale-)chiral symmetry in nuclear interactions. It is prompted by Gerry Brown's last unpublished note, reproduced verbatim below, on the preeminent role of pions and vector ($ρ$,$ω$) mesons in providing a simple and elegant description of strongly correlated nuclear interactions. In this note written in tribute to Gerry Brown, I first describe a case of an unambiguous signal in axial-charge transitions in nuclei and then combine his ideas with the more recent development on the role of hidden symmetries in nuclear physics. What transpires is the surprising conclusion that the Landau-Migdal fixed point interaction $G_0^\prime$, the nuclear tensor forces and Brown-Rho scaling, all encoded in scale-invariant hidden local symmetry, as Gerry put, "run the show and make all forces equal."

## Key claim

The paper argues that a "pristine signal" for (scale-)chiral symmetry in nuclei exists in axial-charge beta decay transitions, where the enhancement factor from soft-pion exchange currents combined with intrinsic density dependence (IDD) from Brown-Rho scaling matches experimental data, but this signal depends on the controversial assumption that the scalar meson \(f_0(500)\) can be interpreted as a dilaton from spontaneously broken scale symmetry, which remains unconfirmed by lattice QCD for \(N_f \leq 3\).

## Method

The author constructs a scale-invariant hidden local symmetry (sHLS) Lagrangian that includes pions, vector mesons (\(\rho, \omega\)), and a dilaton scalar \(\phi\) as explicit degrees of freedom, matched to QCD via operator product expansion at the chiral scale \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 4\pi f_\pi\). The "bare" parameters of this Lagrangian acquire intrinsic density dependence (IDD) from the density dependence of QCD condensates, leading to Brown-Rho scaling where hadron masses scale as \(m_H^*/m_H \approx \Phi(n) = f_\pi^*/f_\pi\). A double-decimation renormalization-group approach using \(V_{lowk}\) is then applied to compute Fermi-liquid fixed-point parameters and tensor forces in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor for axial-charge transitions in lead nuclei (\(A=205-212\)): \(\epsilon_{MEC} \approx 2.0\), agreeing with experimental value \(\epsilon^{exp}_{MEC} = 2.01 \pm 0.05\) at nuclear matter density \(n_0\)
- Enhancement factor for \(A=12\) system: \(\epsilon^{exp}_{MEC} = 1.64 \pm 0.05\)
- Enhancement factor for \(A=16\) system: \(\epsilon^{exp}_{MEC} \approx 1.7\)
- Dilaton condensate scaling at nuclear matter density: \(\Phi(n_0) \approx 0.8\) from pionic atom data
- Ratio of two-body to one-body matrix elements at \(n_0\): \(R(n_0) \approx 0.5\)
- Predicted topological transition density from skyrmion matter to half-skyrmion matter: \(n_{1/2} \sim (2-3)n_0\)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of Brown and Rho (1970s-1990s) on meson-exchange currents and Brown-Rho scaling, building on the soft-pion theorem prediction by Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1978) for axial-charge transitions. It contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory approaches that treat vector mesons as higher-order effects, arguing that explicit vector and scalar degrees of freedom in a scale-invariant hidden local symmetry framework provide a more efficient description, particularly for the Landau-Migdal parameter \(G_0'\) and nuclear tensor forces. The approach enables predictions for the C14 dating problem (Holt et al., 2008) and provides a framework for extending calculations to densities relevant for compact stars, though the dilaton interpretation of \(f_0(500)\) remains contested by the "no-go school" citing the QCD trace anomaly.
