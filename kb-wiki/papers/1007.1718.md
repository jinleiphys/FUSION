# Nature of the sigma meson as revealed by its softening process

> The pi pi scattering is studied in two-flavor chiral models with a finite pion mass to investigate the nature of the sigma meson which is observed as the lowest scalar-isoscalar resonance. We compare several models with different origins of the sigma meson, such as the chiral partner of the pion and the dynamically generated pi pi molecule. We find that the dynamically generated sigma meson exhibits a novel pattern of the threshold enhancement reflecting the s-wave nature of the resonance, which is qualitatively different from the softening of the chiral partner introduced as a bare field. This behavior around the threshold energy region is universal as far as the bare sigma pole stays away from the threshold throughout the symmetry restoration process. On the other hand, for m_pi=0, the dynamically generated sigma behaves similarly to the chiral partner in the symmetry restoration limit, implying the possibility of the dynamically generated chiral partner.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a dynamically generated sigma meson (a pi-pi molecule) exhibits a qualitatively different softening pattern as chiral symmetry is restored compared to a sigma meson that is the chiral partner of the pion, but in the chiral limit (m_pi = 0) the dynamically generated sigma behaves similarly to the chiral partner, suggesting the possibility of a dynamically generated chiral partner.

## Method

The authors study pi-pi scattering in the I=J=0 channel using two-flavor chiral models, constructing tree-level amplitudes from a linear sigma model Lagrangian and then unitarizing them via the N/D method. They introduce a parameter x that interpolates between a model with a bare sigma pole (x=1, the chiral partner scenario) and a model with only contact interactions (x=0, the dynamically generated scenario), and simulate chiral symmetry restoration by reducing the chiral condensate <sigma> from its vacuum value toward zero.

## Key numbers

* The critical value of the parameter x at which the contact interaction at threshold changes sign is C = 0.134, for m_pi = 140 MeV and m_sigma = 550 MeV.
* The subtraction constant a in the loop function G(s) is fixed to a = -pi/sqrt(3) by imposing G(s)=0 at s = m_pi^2.
* The chiral condensate is parametrized as <sigma> = Phi <sigma>_0, with Phi ranging from 1 (vacuum) to 0 (restoration limit).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of sigma meson softening by Jido (2000) and Yokokawa (2002), using a more systematic classification of the sigma meson's origin (chiral partner, dynamically generated molecule, or CDD pole). The approach enables discrimination between these structures through the pattern of threshold enhancement during symmetry restoration, and it addresses the question raised by Oller (2000) and Leupold (2008) about whether a dynamically generated state can serve as the chiral partner of the pion.
