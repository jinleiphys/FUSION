# Isospin equilibration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the mixing and the kinetic equilibration of projectile and target nucleons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the energy regime between 150~AMeV and 2~AGeV in a coupled-channel BUU (CBUU) approach. We find that equilibrium in the projectile-target degrees of freedom is in general not reached even for large systems at low energy where elastic nucleon-nucleon collisions dominate. Inelastic nucleon excitations are more favorable for equilibration and their relative abundance increases both with energy and mass. Experimentally, the projectile/target admixture can be determined by measuring the degree of isospin equilibration in isospin asymmetric nuclear collisions. For one of the most promising systems currently under investigation, ${}^{96}_{44}{\rm Ru}+{}^{96}_{40}{\rm Zr}$, we investigate the influence of the equation of state and the inelastic in-medium cross section.

# Isospin equilibration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Full equilibration of projectile and target nucleons is practically never achieved in central heavy-ion collisions between 150 AMeV and 2 AGeV, even for large systems like Au+Au; the degree of mixing depends critically on the number of inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions, which is limited by the fireball lifetime and system size, and the signal can be accessed experimentally via isospin ratios in asymmetric systems like Ru+Zr.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (CBUU) transport model that includes nucleons, baryonic resonances up to 1.95 GeV/c², and mesons (π, η, ρ, σ) with explicit isospin degrees of freedom. They simulate central collisions of Ni+Ni, Au+Au, and Ru+Zr systems at bombarding energies from 150 AMeV to 2 AGeV, tracking the origin (projectile or target) of each hadron to compute the target/projectile ratio as a function of rapidity. Complementary calculations in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions using two shifted Fermi spheres provide equilibration times for comparison with the fireball lifetimes in realistic collisions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at 150 AMeV, the rapidity distributions of projectile and target nucleons remain clearly separated; only about 1/4 of NN collisions occur between identical particles (same spin and isospin) that cannot be distinguished by the Pauli principle.
- The mean transverse-to-longitudinal energy ratio E_rat for central Au+Au reaches a maximum of 1.92 at 400 AMeV, decreasing to 1.22 at 2 AGeV, indicating maximum stopping around 400 AMeV.
- For Ru+Zr at 400 AMeV, the target/projectile ratio reaches up to 0.6 at projectile rapidities; at 1.5 AGeV the ratio is close to zero up to normalized rapidity y_0 ≈ 0.6 and reaches a maximum of 0.4 at projectile rapidity.
- The final isospin ratios for Ru and Zr at 1.5 AGeV shift from initial values of ~0.85 and ~0.71 to approximately 0.90 and 0.81 respectively, due to charged pion production.
- Equilibration (defined as global ratio ≤ 10% of maximum) is reached for Au+Au at 1 AGeV but not for Ni+Ni at any energy studied.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of equilibration in heavy-ion collisions at low energies (≤ 50 AMeV, reviewed by W. Bauer) to the relativistic regime up to 2 AGeV, using the CBUU approach previously validated against pion spectra and multiplicities (Teis et al., 1996). The authors find that elastic collisions alone are insufficient for isospin equilibration due to forward-peaked angular distributions, contradicting simple thermal models that assume full mixing (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1997; Reisdorf et al., 1997). The results enable experimental extraction of the nuclear equation of state and in-medium cross sections by measuring N/Z ratios versus rapidity in very central collisions of asymmetric systems such as Ru+Zr, currently under investigation at SIS (Yvonne et al., 1998).
