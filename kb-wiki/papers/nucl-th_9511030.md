# RPA Description of the Electric Polarizability of the Nucleon

> Excited states of the nucleon are described as RPA configurations on a mean--field ground state taken from the MIT bag model. A residual interaction of a structure as in the Nambu--Jona--Lasinio model is used. The particle-hole states are coupled to good total angular momentum and isospin. Valence excitations of particle-hole type and quark--antiquark ($q\overline{q}$) states from the Dirac--sea are included. The dependence of the baryon spectrum and multipole response functions on the coupling constant $G$ is studied. At critical values of $G$ the 3q--ground state becomes degenerate with strongly collective $q\overline{q}$ modes. The model is used to calculate the electric polarizability of the neutron $α_N$. Without residual interaction $α_N=7\cdot 10^{-4}fm^3$ is found. With residual interaction the value increases to $α_N=(7-11)\cdot 10^{-4}fm^3$. The measured value of $α_N$ is reproduced within experimental error bars.

# RPA Description of the Electric Polarizability of the Nucleon

## Key claim
The electric polarizability of the neutron can be reproduced within experimental error bars using a relativistic RPA model that includes both valence particle-hole excitations and quark-antiquark excitations from the Dirac sea, but only when the residual interaction is included; without it, the calculated value falls below the experimental range. The coupling constant is constrained to the region G = 3 to 6 x 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}.

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic RPA model on a mean-field ground state taken from the MIT bag model, using a residual color-current interaction of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type. Particle-hole states from valence excitations and quark-antiquark states from the Dirac sea are coupled to good total angular momentum and isospin, and the RPA eigenvalue equations are solved by exact diagonalization within a model space of about 40 unperturbed 1p1h and q-qbar states with energies up to 2200 MeV. The electric polarizability is obtained from the inverse energy weighted RPA sum rule, with the bare vacuum polarizability subtracted to obtain the physical value.

## Key numbers
- Neutron electric polarizability without residual interaction: alpha_N = 7 x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Neutron electric polarizability with residual interaction: alpha_N = (7-11) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Measured neutron polarizability from Compton scattering: alpha_N = (12 +/- 1.5 +/- 2) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Bag radius used: R_bag = 0.9 fm, corresponding to quark core rms radius <r^2>_c^{1/2} = 0.65 fm
- Critical coupling constant for J^pi = 0^- channel: G_crit = 6.8 x 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}
- Critical coupling constant for J^pi = 0^+ channel: G_crit = 6.4 x 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}
- Constrained coupling constant region: G = 3 to 6 x 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of nucleon polarizability in the MIT bag model (which gave alpha_N ~ 8 x 10^{-4} fm^3) and chiral quark models (which gave alpha_N ~ 8-10 x 10^{-4} fm^3) by including dynamical correlations beyond perturbative treatments. The authors note that a perturbative treatment of residual interactions, as used in some earlier approaches, is unreliable because the matrix elements are of the order of unperturbed excitation energies. The model enables future studies of collective mesonic modes as dynamically correlated q-qbar excitations in baryonic systems, and suggests that the polarizability is particularly sensitive to the quark core rather than vacuum effects.
