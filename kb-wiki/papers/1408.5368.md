# Manifestations of nuclear CP-violation in ThO molecule

> Investigations of CP violation in hadron sector may be done using measurements in the ThO molecule. Recent measurements in this molecule improved the limit on electron EDM by an order of magnitude. Another time reversal (T) and parity (P) violating effect in $^{229}$ThO is induced by the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment. We have performed nuclear and molecular calculations to express this effect in terms of the strength constants of T,P-odd nuclear forces, neutron EDM, QCD vacuum angle $θ$, quark EDM and chromo-EDM.

# Manifestations of nuclear CP-violation in ThO molecule

## Key claim

The nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) in the ThO molecule can produce measurable energy shifts that are sufficiently large to compete with, and potentially improve, current limits on the QCD vacuum angle θ̃ and the difference of quark chromo-EDMs (d̃_u − d̃_d), provided that experiments currently performed on ²³²ThO for electron EDM searches are extended to the ²²⁹ThO isotope. The authors hedge that the proton contribution to the MQM is small due to an accidental cancellation of contributions from different orbitals, and that the neutron EDM contributions to MQM are one to two orders of magnitude smaller than the contributions from nucleon CP-odd interactions.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear structure calculations using the deformed-nucleus model of Bohr to determine occupation numbers for neutrons and protons in ²²⁹Th, then computed the nuclear MQM in the laboratory frame. For the molecular part, they used a two-step approach combining generalized relativistic effective core potential (GRECP) molecular calculations at the CCSD(T) level with a nonvariational restoration procedure to recover the four-component electronic wavefunction near the thorium nucleus, from which the molecular parameter W_M was evaluated.

## Key numbers

- Final calculated value of W_M for the H³Δ₁ state of ThO: 1.66 × 10³³ Hz / (e·cm²)
- MQM of ²²⁹Th expressed in terms of the QCD vacuum angle: M(θ) = −4 × 10⁻²⁸ θ̃ · e·cm²
- MQM expressed in terms of quark chromo-EDMs: M(d̃) = −2 × 10⁻¹¹ (d̃_u − d̃_d) · e·cm
- Energy shift for the largest coefficient (F=5/2, J=1): 0.16 W_M M = −11 × 10¹⁰ θ̃ · μHz
- Energy shift for the largest coefficient: 0.16 W_M M = −5 × 10²⁷ (d̃_u − d̃_d)/cm · μHz
- Current limits on |θ̃| < 2.4 × 10⁻¹⁰ and |d̃_u − d̃_d| < 6 × 10⁻²⁷ cm correspond to shifts |0.16 W_M M| < 26 μHz and 30 μHz, respectively
- Current accuracy in ²³²ThO electron EDM measurements: 700 μHz, anticipated to improve by ∼2 orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and others (1984–2014) that paramagnetic molecules with ³Δ₁ electron terms are promising for measuring T,P-violating effects from nuclear MQM, and builds on the recent experimental breakthrough by the ACME collaboration (2014) that improved the electron EDM limit using ThO by more than an order of magnitude. The calculations enable a new method to search for CP-violation in the hadron sector, including the QCD θ-term and quark chromo-EDMs, using molecular spectroscopy of ²²⁹ThO, complementing existing limits from Hg atom EDM measurements (Griffin et al., 2000; Swallows et al., 2013) and direct neutron EDM searches.
