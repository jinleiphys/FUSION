# Contribution of the $a_1$ meson to the axial nucleon-to-$Δ$ transition form factors

> We analyze the low-$Q^2$ behavior of the axial form factor $G_A(Q^2)$, the induced pseudoscalar form factor $G_P(Q^2)$, and the axial nucleon-to-$Δ$ transition form factors $C^A_5(Q^2)$ and $C^A_6(Q^2)$. Building on the results of chiral perturbation theory, we first discuss $G_A(Q^2)$ in a chiral effective-Lagrangian model including the $a_1$ meson and determine the relevant coupling parameters from a fit to experimental data. With this information, the form factor $G_P(Q^2)$ can be predicted. For the determination of the transition form factor $C^A_5(Q^2)$ we make use of an SU(6) spin-flavor quark-model relation to fix two coupling constants such that only one free parameter is left. Finally, the transition form factor $C^A_6(Q^2)$ can be predicted in terms of $G_P(Q^2)$, the mean-square axial radius $\langle r^2_A\rangle$, and the mean-square axial nucleon-to-$Δ$ transition radius $\langle r^2_{ANΔ}\rangle$.

## Key claim

The authors show that including the $a_1(1260)$ axial-vector meson in a chiral effective-Lagrangian model provides a description of the low-$Q^2$ behavior of the axial nucleon-to-delta transition form factors $C_5^A$ and $C_6^A$, but the model has a restricted domain of validity (up to about $Q^2_{\rm max}=0.6$ GeV$^2$) and the parametrization diverges as $Q^2\to\infty$, which is an unphysical feature.

## Method

The authors first fit the axial form factor $G_A(Q^2)$ to experimental data from pion electroproduction using a parametrization that includes a linear term and an $a_1$ meson pole contribution. They then use an SU(6) spin-flavor quark-model relation to fix two coupling constants for the nucleon-to-delta transition, leaving one free parameter for $C_5^A(Q^2)$. Finally, they predict $C_6^A(Q^2)$ in terms of $G_P(Q^2)$, the mean-square axial radius, and the mean-square axial nucleon-to-delta transition radius.

## Key numbers

- Axial-vector coupling constant: $g_A = 1.2723$ (from Patrignani 2016)
- Axial mass from dipole fit to pion electroproduction data with $Q^2_{\rm max}=0.6$ GeV$^2$: $M_A = 1.084 \pm 0.020$ GeV, corresponding to $\langle r_A^2\rangle = 0.398 \pm 0.015$ fm$^2$
- For the $a_1$ model fit with $Q^2_{\rm max}=0.6$ GeV$^2$: $\tilde{c}_1 = -1.78 \pm 0.09$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\tilde{c}_2 = -5.31 \pm 0.69$, $\langle r_A^2\rangle = 0.416 \pm 0.021$ fm$^2$, $\langle r_A^4\rangle = 0.383 \pm 0.050$ fm$^4$
- The $a_1$ contribution to $\langle r_A^2\rangle$ for $Q^2_{\rm max}=0.6$ GeV$^2$ is $0.781$ fm$^2$, with the LEC contribution being $-0.366$ fm$^2$, giving a total of $0.415$ fm$^2$
- Pion mass: $M_\pi = 139.57$ MeV; nucleon mass: $m_N = 938.92$ MeV; $a_1$ mass: $M_{a_1} = 1260$ MeV; pion-decay constant: $F_\pi = 92.2$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis of Schindler (2006) for nucleon axial form factors to the nucleon-to-delta transition, following the approach of including vector mesons that was successful for electromagnetic form factors (Kubis 2000, Schindler 2005, Bauer 2012). The $a_1$ meson contribution to $C_5^A$ was envisaged decades ago by Sehgal (1979), who questioned the common use of a dipole form. The results enable predictions for weak single-pion production in the delta-resonance region, which is relevant for neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments.
