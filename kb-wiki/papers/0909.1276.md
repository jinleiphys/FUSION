# Particle vibrational coupling in covariant density functional theory

> A consistent combination of covariant density functional theory (CDFT) and Landau-Migdal Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) is presented. Both methods are in principle exact, but Landau-Migdal theory cannot describe ground state properties and density functional theory does not take into account the energy dependence of the self-energy and therefore fails to yield proper single-% particle spectra as well as the coupling to complex configurations in the width of giant resonances. Starting from an energy functional, phonons and their vertices are calculated without any further parameters. They form the basis of particle-vibrational coupling leading to an energy dependence of the self-energy and an induced energy-dependent interaction in the response equation. A subtraction procedure avoids double counting. Applications in doubly magic nuclei and in a chain of superfluid nuclei show excellent agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a consistent combination of covariant density functional theory (CDFT) and Landau-Migdal Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) that, starting from an energy functional without additional parameters, calculates phonons and their vertices to describe particle-vibrational coupling; the method relies on a subtraction procedure to avoid double counting between the correlations introduced by particle-vibration coupling and those already contained in the phenomenological density functional.

## Method

The authors start from a covariant density functional (the relativistic mean-field model with meson exchange) and compute the static residual interaction as the second derivative of the energy functional with respect to the density matrix. Using this interaction, they calculate phonon vertices and frequencies within relativistic RPA, then construct the energy-dependent self-energy from particle-phonon coupling via a convolution of the coupling amplitude with the mean-field Green's function. The nuclear response is obtained from a Bethe-Salpeter equation solved in the Time Blocking Approximation (TBA), which restricts the propagation to 1p1h and 1p1h⊗phonon configurations, and a subtraction technique removes double counting of ground-state correlations.

## Key numbers

* In nuclear matter, the effective mass from mean-field models is roughly m*/m ≈ 0.8, while coupling to low-lying collective surface vibrations enhances it to m*/m ≈ 1 near the Fermi surface.
* The phonon vertices γ^μ are determined by the relation γ^μ_{kl} = Σ_{k'l'} Ṽ_{kl',lk'} δρ_{k'l'}, where Ṽ is the second derivative of the energy functional with respect to the density matrix.
* The energy-dependent interaction in the response equation is given by V^e_{kl',lk'}(ω,ε,ε') = Σ_{μ,σ} σ γ^{μ(σ)*}_{k'k} γ^{μ(σ)}_{l'l} / [ε - ε' + σ(Ω^μ - iη)], where Ω^μ are the phonon frequencies.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (K. S. and S. S. 1979, 1980; F. T. T. 2000) to the relativistic framework, combining covariant density functionals (Ring 1996; V. A. L. R. 2005) with the particle-phonon coupling techniques of Nuclear Field Theory (B. M. 1975; B. B. B. 1977, 1983) and the Time Blocking Approximation (Tse. 1989). The method enables the calculation of single-particle spectra with proper level densities near the Fermi surface and the spreading width of giant resonances, going beyond the relativistic RPA which is limited to 1p1h configurations.
