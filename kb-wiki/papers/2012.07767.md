# Octet and decuplet baryon self-energies in relativistic SU(3) chiral effective theory

> The self-energies of the full set of flavor SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons are computed within a relativistic chiral effective theory framework. The leading nonanalytic chiral behavior is derived for the octet and decuplet masses, and a finite-range regularization consistent with Lorentz and gauge invariance is applied to account for the finite size of the baryons. Using a four-dimensional dipole form factor, the relative importance of various meson-baryon loop contributions to the self-energies is studied numerically as a function of the dipole range parameter and meson mass, and comparison is made between the relativistic results and earlier approximations within the heavy baryon limit.

## Key claim

The paper computes the self-energies for the complete set of flavor SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons using a fully relativistic chiral effective theory with a four-dimensional dipole form factor, finding that the leading nonanalytic chiral behavior is preserved and that the relativistic results differ from earlier heavy-baryon approximations, particularly for transitions where the initial and intermediate baryon masses differ.

## Method

The authors start from the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian with octet and decuplet baryon fields and pseudoscalar mesons, then compute the self-energy operators for all four transition types (octet-octet, octet-decuplet, decuplet-octet, decuplet-decuplet) by taking spin traces of the loop integrals. A four-dimensional dipole form factor with regulator mass Λ is inserted into the integrands to regularize divergences while preserving Lorentz and gauge invariance, and the loop integrals are evaluated analytically using light-front coordinates and contour integration, with careful treatment of end-point contributions from light-front zero-modes.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar decay constant: f_φ = 93 MeV
- For the N → Nπ self-energy with equal initial and intermediate baryon masses, the heavy-baryon limit yields Σ_{N→Nπ}^{HB} = −(3 g_A² (Λ − m_π)⁴ (Λ² + 4Λ m_π + m_π²)) / (512 π f_π² Λ³)
- The coefficient of the O(m_π³) leading nonanalytic term from the heavy-baryon result is Σ_{N→Nπ}^{LNA} = −(3 g_A²)/(32 π f_π²) m_π³
- For the proton external state, the NNπ coupling constant squared is C_{NNπ}² = (3/4) (D+F)² = (3/4) g_A²

## Context

This work extends previous chiral loop calculations that focused primarily on nucleon external states (Ghaderi 2018) or relied on heavy-baryon approximations (Young 2003, Shanahan 2012) to the full SU(3) octet and decuplet baryon spectrum using a manifestly covariant framework. The results enable future applications to meson-baryon splitting functions needed for parton distribution function calculations (Salamu 2018, Wang 2020) and to scalar matrix elements and σ-terms for all octet and decuplet baryons, as well as providing a benchmark for comparing relativistic versus heavy-baryon treatments of chiral corrections.
