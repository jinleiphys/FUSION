# Evolution of pion HBT radii from RHIC to LHC -- Predictions from ideal hydrodynamics

> We present hydrodynamic predictions for the charged pion HBT radii for a range of initial conditions covering those presumably reached in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. We study central (b=0) and semi-central (b=7fm) collisions and show the expected increase of the HBT radii and their azimuthal oscillations. The predicted trends in the oscillation amplitudes reflect a change of the final source shape from out-of-plane to in-plane deformation as the initial entropy density is increased.

## Key claim
The hydrodynamic model predicts that the normalized azimuthal oscillation amplitudes of pion HBT radii in non-central collisions will change sign between RHIC and LHC energies, indicating a transition from out-of-plane to in-plane deformation of the freeze-out source, but the authors caution that the model fails to reproduce the absolute HBT radii measured at RHIC, so only the trends (not the magnitudes) may be trustworthy.

## Method
The authors used the ideal hydrodynamic code AZHYDRO with a quark-hadron phase transition equation of state (RappEoS) to simulate central (b=0) and semi-central (b=7 fm) Pb+Pb collisions. They varied the initial peak entropy density s0 at thermalization time τ0 to cover final charged multiplicities from dNch/dy = 680 (RHIC) to dNch/dy = 2040 (LHC), keeping T0τ0 constant. Pion HBT radii were computed from space-time variances of the Cooper-Frye emission function at decoupling (Tdec = 100 MeV), with the understanding that this overestimates the longitudinal radius Rl by 20-25%.

## Key numbers
- Final charged multiplicity at RHIC: dNch/dy(y=b=0) = 680; at LHC: dNch/dy = 2040
- Initial entropy density for RHIC conditions: s0 = 117 fm^-3 at τ0 = 0.6 fm/c; for LHC conditions: s0 = 602 fm^-3 at τ0 = 0.35 fm/c
- Critical temperature for phase transition: Tc = 164 MeV; decoupling temperature: Tdec = 100 MeV; decoupling energy density: edec = 0.075 GeV/fm^3
- In central collisions, Rs increases by approximately 30% at low KT from RHIC to LHC, while Rl changes hardly at all
- For an ideal massless gas equation of state (no phase transition), Rl increases by 25-30% from RHIC to LHC, compared to negligible change with the phase transition equation of state
- A specific calculation for photons and dileptons used s0 = 1735 fm^-3 at τ0 = 0.1 fm/c, yielding dNch/dy = 2035

## Context
This work extends earlier hydrodynamic predictions by Heinz (2002) that used an ideal quark-gluon gas without phase transition and unrealistically high initial temperatures (2 GeV) to show the possibility of the out-of-plane to in-plane shape transition. The present study uses a more realistic equation of state (RappEoS) and initial conditions. The model had previously failed to reproduce the absolute HBT radii measured at RHIC (Lisa, 2005) but successfully predicted the normalized oscillation amplitudes (Retiere and Lisa, 2003). The results enable experimental tests at LHC of whether the freeze-out source shape changes from out-of-plane elongated at RHIC to in-plane elongated at LHC, which would be a qualitative signature of the quark-hadron phase transition.
