# Producing the Deuteron in Stars: Anthropic Limits on Fundamental Constants

> Stellar nucleosynthesis proceeds via the deuteron (D), but only a small change in the fundamental constants of nature is required to unbind it. Here, we investigate the effect of altering the binding energy of the deuteron on proton burning in stars. We find that the most definitive boundary in parameter space that divides probably life-permitting universes from probably life-prohibiting ones is between a bound and unbound deuteron. Due to neutrino losses, a ball of gas will undergo rapid cooling or stabilization by electron degeneracy pressure before it can form a stable, nuclear reaction-sustaining star. We also consider a less-bound deuteron, which changes the energetics of the $pp$ and $pep$ reactions. The transition to endothermic $pp$ and $pep$ reactions, and the resulting beta-decay instability of the deuteron, do not seem to present catastrophic problems for life.

# Producing the Deuteron in Stars: Anthropic Limits on Fundamental Constants

## Key claim
The most definitive boundary dividing probably life-permitting universes from probably life-prohibiting ones is between a bound and unbound deuteron, but a less-bound deuteron that makes the pp and pep reactions endothermic, or causes beta-decay instability of the deuteron, does not seem to present catastrophic problems for life.

## Method
The authors extended the stellar model of Adams (2008) to include an unstable deuteron, endothermic pp and pep reactions, and neutrino losses. They solved the coupled differential equations for hydrostatic equilibrium, mass conservation, heat transport, and energy generation numerically, calculating equilibrium abundances of deuterium and neutrons from the reaction network including pp, pep, pn, Dp, and deuteron decay channels. The central temperature was found by solving the energy transport equation with a constant opacity approximation, and the reaction rates for endothermic reactions were computed by numerically integrating over the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution with a kinetic energy threshold.

## Key numbers
- In our universe, the pp reaction releases Q_pp = 0.420 MeV, the pep reaction releases Q_pep = 1.442 MeV, and the deuteron binding energy is B_D = 2.2245 MeV.
- The deuteron becomes unbound (B_D < 0) when the sum of light quark masses exceeds m_u + m_d > 19.1 MeV, based on the parameterization B_D = 2.2 MeV - 1.3 MeV * ((m_u + m_d)/(m_u,0 + m_d,0) - 1) where m_u,0 = 2.3 MeV and m_d,0 = 4.8 MeV.
- A 5-8% decrease in the strength of the strong force will unbind the deuteron.
- The net reaction 4p + 2e- -> He-4 + 2 nu_e releases Q_He4 = 26.73 MeV in our universe.
- For the unstable deuteron case with lambda_D = 10^18 s^-1, stars are not possible when neutrino losses are taken into account; with lambda_D = 10^16 s^-1, the minimum stellar mass increases as the deuteron becomes less stable.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Barnes 2015) of diproton-burning stars to the opposite scenario of unbinding the deuteron, following earlier arguments by Barrow and Tipler (1986) and Davies (1983) that a weaker nuclear force would remove a key link in nucleosynthesis. The dependence of deuteron binding on fundamental constants builds on calculations by Davies (1972), Agrawal (1998), and Barr and Khan (2007), among others. The results enable future work on the full parameter space of fundamental constants that permit stable, nuclear-burning stars and thus potentially life-bearing universes.
