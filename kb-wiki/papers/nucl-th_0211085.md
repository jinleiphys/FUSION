# Medium effects to the N(1535) resonance and eta mesic nuclei

> The structure of eta-nucleus bound systems (eta mesic nuclei) is investigated as one of the tools to study in-medium properties of the N(1535) (N*) resonance. We show that, as a general consequence, the eta-nucleus potential has a repulsive core at the nuclear center with an attractive part at the nuclear surface, if sufficient reduction of the mass difference of N and N* stems from the in-medium effects to N*. The (d,3He) spectra are evaluated for the formation of these bound states to investigate the experimental feasibility.

## Key claim

The paper shows that if the in-medium mass difference between the nucleon and the N(1535) resonance is sufficiently reduced (by about 10-30% of the vacuum value at saturation density), the eta-nucleus optical potential develops a repulsive core at the nuclear center with an attractive pocket at the surface, rather than being purely attractive as in the low-density T-rho approximation.

## Method

The authors construct an eta-nucleus optical potential in the N*-dominance model, analogous to the Delta-hole model for pions, using the chiral doublet model to describe the density dependence of the N and N* masses and the N* decay width. They calculate the eta-nucleus potential for finite nuclei using a Fermi distribution for the nuclear density, and evaluate the (d,3He) reaction spectra for 12C at a deuteron kinetic energy of 3.5 GeV to test experimental observability of the predicted potential shape.

## Key numbers

- The eta-NN* coupling constant is taken as g_eta approximately 2 (isoscalar scalar coupling).
- The C parameter, representing the strength of chiral restoration at saturation density, is varied between 0.1 and 0.3 (with C=0.2 as the expected value).
- The eta optical potential is evaluated at eta energies from m_eta (approximately 547 MeV) down to m_eta minus 50 MeV.
- The (d,3He) reaction is calculated for 12C at T_d = 3.5 GeV.
- For C=0.2, the repulsive core prevents formation of eta bound states; for C=0.0 (no medium modifications), bound state level structure is similar to that obtained in Hayano (1999).

## Context

This work extends the systematic studies of eta-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu (1986) and Chiang, Oset and Liu (1991), who predicted attractive eta-nucleus potentials and bound states based on the eta-nucleon scattering length. The paper introduces medium modifications to the N(1535) mass and width using the chiral doublet model of DeTar and Kim, showing that chiral restoration effects can qualitatively change the potential shape. The results enable experimental searches for the predicted repulsive core signature in (d,3He) reactions, and connect eta-mesic nuclei to the study of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter.
