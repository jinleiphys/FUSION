# Modeling Neutrino Quasielastic Cross Sections on Nucleons and Nuclei

> We calculate the total and differential quasielastic cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on nucleons using up to date fits to the nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors $G_E^p$, $G_E^n$, $G_M^p$, $G_M^n$, and $F_A$ and pseudoscalar form factors. We compare predictions of the cross sections for nucelons and nuclei to experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using updated nucleon electromagnetic form factors (BBA-2003) improves the agreement with neutrino quasielastic cross section data compared to the older dipole approximation, but the comparison to data carries a normalization uncertainty of approximately 10% and requires nuclear corrections (Pauli blocking and binding effects) to match measurements on carbon targets.

## Method

The authors calculate total and differential quasielastic cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on nucleons using the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis to relate electron scattering form factors (G_E^p, G_E^n, G_M^p, G_M^n) to the vector form factors for neutrino scattering, combined with a dipole axial form factor F_A with axial mass M_A = 1.00 GeV. They compare predictions using their BBA-2003 form factors to experimental data from multiple experiments, applying a Fermi gas model for carbon with 25 MeV binding energy and 220 MeV Fermi momentum for nuclear corrections, and also consider a model by Tsushima et al. that includes nuclear binding effects on nucleon form factors.
