# Impact of correlations on nuclear binding energies

> A strong effort will be dedicated in the coming years to extend the reach of ab initio nuclear-structure calculations to heavy doubly open-shell nuclei. In order to do so, the most efficient strategies to incorporate dominant many-body correlations at play in such nuclei must be identified. With this motivation in mind, the present work pedagogically analyses the inclusion of many-body correlations and their impact on binding energies of Calcium and Chromium isotopes. Employing an empirically-optimal Hamiltonian built from chiral effective field theory, binding energies along both isotopic chains are studied via a hierarchy of approximations based on polynomially-scaling expansion many-body methods. The corresponding results are compared to experimental data and to those obtained via valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations at the normal-ordered two-body level that act as a reference in the present study. The spherical mean-field approximation is shown to display specific shortcomings in Ca isotopes that can be understood analytically and that are efficiently corrected via the consistent addition of low-order dynamical correlations on top of it. While the same setting cannot appropriately reproduce binding energies in doubly open-shell Cr isotopes, allowing the unperturbed mean-field state to break rotational symmetry permits to efficiently capture the static correlations responsible for the phenomenological differences observed between the two isotopic chains. Eventually, the present work demonstrates in a pedagogical way that polynomially-scaling expansion methods based on unperturbed states that possibly break (and restore) symmetries constitute an optimal route to extend ab initio calculations to heavy closed- and open-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that polynomially-scaling expansion many-body methods based on unperturbed states that may break (and restore) symmetries constitute an optimal route to extend ab initio calculations to heavy closed- and open-shell nuclei, but the success depends critically on whether the unperturbed mean-field state is allowed to break rotational symmetry for doubly open-shell systems.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (sHFB) mean-field calculations plus second-order Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (sBMBPT(2)) and spherical Bogoliubov coupled cluster with singles and doubles (sBCCSD) for Calcium and Chromium isotopes, using the EM 1.8/2.0 chiral Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis with e_max=12. For doubly open-shell Chromium isotopes, they additionally employed axially-deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (dHFB) plus second-order Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (dBMBPT(2)) to capture static correlations via symmetry breaking. Results were compared to valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG(2)) calculations and experimental data.

## Key numbers

* sHFB underbinds 48Ca by more than 100 MeV relative to experimental data
* Two-neutron shell gap at N=20 reduced from 13 MeV in sHFB to 8.6 MeV in VS-IMSRG(2), compared to experimental value of 9.1 MeV; sBMBPT(2) and sBCCSD give 12.4 MeV
* S2n in 42Ca improved from 13.3 MeV (sHFB) to 18.0 MeV (sBMBPT(2)), 18.1 MeV (sBCCSD), and 18.6 MeV (VS-IMSRG(2)), versus experimental 19.8 MeV
* Root-mean-square error to experimental binding energies along Ca chain: 1.9 MeV for VS-IMSRG(2), 7.3 MeV for BMBPT(2), 8.8 MeV for BCCSD
* Spherical mean-field energy curvature coefficient beta_v for 0f7/2 shell in Ca: -0.290 MeV (negative, indicating wrong concavity)
* Spikes in two-neutron shell gap at N=20 and N=28 overestimated by 4.0 MeV and 2.1 MeV respectively in sHFB

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of many-body correlations in ab initio nuclear structure, building on prior studies using chiral Hamiltonians such as Hebe11fits (2011) and expansion methods including BMBPT (Tichai 2018) and BCCSD (Signoracci 2014). The pedagogical comparison of spherical versus deformed reference states for Calcium (singly open-shell) and Chromium (doubly open-shell) isotopes demonstrates that while dynamical correlations from low-order perturbation theory correct the wrong curvature of spherical mean-field energies in semi-magic nuclei, static correlations from symmetry breaking are essential for doubly open-shell systems. This enables future extension of ab initio calculations to heavy open-shell nuclei where both correlation types are important.
