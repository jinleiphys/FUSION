# Many-body correlations for nuclear physics across scales: from nuclei to quark-gluon plasmas to hadron distributions

> It is an experimental fact that multi-particle correlations in the final states of high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions are sensitive to collective correlations of nucleons in the wave functions of the colliding nuclei. Here, I show that this connection is more direct than it intuitively seems. With an energy deposition scheme inspired by high-energy quantum chromodynamics, and within a linearized description of initial-state fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma, I exhibit relations between $N$-particle correlations in the final states of nuclear collisions and $N$-nucleon density distributions in the colliding nuclei. This result formally justifies the sensitivity of the outcome of high-energy collisions to features such as nuclear deformations. It paves the way, thus, to systematic studies of the impact of state-of-the-art nuclear interactions in such processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that under a linearized description of initial-state fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma and using a glasma-inspired energy deposition scheme, N-particle correlations measured in final states of high-energy nuclear collisions can be directly linked to N-nucleon density distributions in the colliding nuclei, formally justifying the sensitivity of such collisions to nuclear deformations.

## Method

The author employs a background-fluctuation splitting of the energy density field in the transverse plane and truncates at first nontrivial order in the perturbation to relate final-state multi-particle correlations to connected correlation functions of the initial energy density. Using the IP-Jazma model where the energy density is given by the product of transverse nuclear profiles, the author then expresses these energy-density correlators in terms of one-, two-, and three-nucleon densities in the transverse plane of the colliding nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon form factor used in the transverse nuclear profile is a two-dimensional Gaussian with width w = 0.5 fm (Eq. 6)
- The Woods-Saxon profile parameters used in the example are half-width radius R = 5 fm and surface diffuseness a = 0.5 fm (Eq. 4)
- The example considers nuclei with mass number A = 96 nucleons distributed independently according to a one-nucleon density
- The Lorentz factor at top RHIC and LHC energies satisfies γ > 100, making the longitudinal extent of nuclei negligible
- Soft particle production occurs typically at p_t < 2 GeV, with typical charged hadron multiplicity N_ch ∼ O(1000) per event

## Context

This work extends previous connections between ab initio nuclear structure calculations and high-energy collisions, such as those by Nagle (2013) and Lim (2018) for light nuclei from deuteron to 16O, by providing a formal theoretical framework linking multi-hadron correlation observables to N-nucleon correlations. The results enable systematic studies of how state-of-the-art nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory impact high-energy collision outcomes, complementing existing Monte Carlo approaches that sample fully-correlated A-body wave functions.
