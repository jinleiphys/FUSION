# Lattice QCD and Baryon-Baryon Interactions

> In this chapter, the current status on baryon-baryon interactions such as nuclear forces in lattice Quantum ChromoDynamics (QCD) is reviewed. In studies of baryon-baryon interactions in lattice QCD, the most reliable method so far is the potential method, proposed by the Hadrons to Atomic nuclei from Lattice QCD (HAL QCD) collaboration, whose formulation, properties and extensions are explained in detail. Using the HAL QCD potential method, potentials between nucleons (proton and neutron, denoted by $N$) in the derivative expansion have been extracted in various cases. The lattice QCD results shown in this chapter include a Leading Order (LO) central potential in the parity-even $NN(^1S_0)$ channel, LO central and tensor potentials in the parity-even $NN(^3S_1$-$^3D_1)$ channel, and a Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) spin-orbit potential as well as LO potentials in the parity-odd channels. Preliminary results at the almost physical pion and kaon masses, in addition to exploratory studies on three-nucleon potentials, are presented. Interactions between generic baryons including hyperons, made of one or more strange quarks as well as up and down quarks, have also been investigated. Universal properties of potentials between baryons become manifest in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit, where masses of three quarks, up, down and strange, are all equal. In particular, it is observed that one bound state, traditionally called the $H$-dibaryon, appears in the flavor singlet representation of SU(3). A fate of the $H$ dibaryon is also discussed with flavor SU(3) breaking taken into account at the almost physical point. Finally, various kinds of dibaryons, bound or resonate states of two baryons, including charmed dibaryons, have been predicted by lattice QCD simulations at the almost physical point.

## Key claim

This chapter reviews the current status of baryon-baryon interactions from lattice QCD using the HAL QCD potential method, which the authors argue is the most reliable approach for such systems. The abstract's claim that the method is "most reliable" is hedged by the text's explicit acknowledgment that both the finite volume method and the HAL QCD method are "theoretically equivalent" but have "their own pros and cons in practice," and that the HAL QCD method has been applied mainly to baryon-baryon systems in the center-of-mass frame while extension to moving frames has just begun.

## Method

The HAL QCD potential method extracts a non-local but energy-independent potential from the equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function for two baryons, defined from QCD correlation functions. The non-local potential is expanded in terms of velocity (derivative) with local coefficient functions, where the leading order (LO) central and tensor potentials are obtained from a single NBS wave function, while next-to-leading order (NLO) spin-orbit potentials require two NBS wave functions at different energies. A time-dependent method is employed to avoid the need for ground-state saturation, instead requiring only elastic-state saturation at much smaller time separations, and the method extends naturally to coupled-channel systems using multiple source operators.

## Key numbers

- Central potential in the \(^1S_0\) channel obtained at pion masses \(m_\pi \simeq 411\), 570, and 701 MeV, with corresponding nucleon masses \(m_N \simeq 1215\), 1412, and 1584 MeV, on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice at lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.091\) fm (\(a^{-1} = 2.176(31)\) GeV)
- Central and tensor potentials in the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channel obtained at \(m_\pi \simeq 146\) MeV (almost physical pion mass) on a \(96^4\) lattice at \(a \simeq 0.0846\) fm, though with large statistical fluctuations
- LO and NLO potentials in parity-odd channels calculated at \((m_\pi, m_N) \simeq (1133, 2158)\) MeV on a \(16^3 \times 32\) lattice at \(a \simeq 0.156\) fm, using 2-flavor lattice QCD
- The spin-orbit potential \(V_{\rm LS}^{I=1}(r)\) in parity-odd channels is found to be strong and negative at all distances, producing an attractive phase shift in the \(^3P_2\) channel

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD potential method developed by Ishii (2006), Aoki (2009), and collaborators, which resolved a previous controversy with the finite volume method over whether nucleon-nucleon systems are bound at heavier pion masses. The chapter reports that it is now "the Lattice QCD community's consensus that \(NN\) systems are unbound at heavier pion masses in both channels," following corrections to finite volume extractions by Iritani (2016) and Aoki (2016). The results enable future calculations of parity-odd potentials at lighter pion masses to see if tensor and spin-orbit potentials become large enough to reproduce experimental scattering phase shifts, and provide a framework for first-principles determination of three-nucleon interactions needed for nuclear structure and neutron star properties.
