# Shear and bulk viscous coefficients of a hot and chirally imbalanced quark matter using NJL model

> The shear $η$ and bulk $ζ$ viscous coefficients have been calculated in a hot and chirally asymmetric quark matter quantified in terms of a chiral chemical potential (CCP) using the two-flavor Nambu-Jona--Lasinio (NJL) model. This is done by employing the one-loop Green-Kubo formalism where the viscous coefficients have been extracted from the long-wavelength limit of the in-medium spectral function corresponding to the energy momentum tensor (EMT) current correlator calculated using the real time formalism of finite temperature field theory. The momentum dependent thermal width of the quark/antiquark that enters into the expression of the viscosities as a dynamical input containing interactions, has been obtained from the $2\to2$ scattering processes mediated via the collective mesonic modes in scalar and pseudoscalar chanels encoded in respective in-medium polarization functions having explicit temperature and CCP dependence. Several thermodynamic quantities such as pressure, energy density, entropy density $(s)$, specific heat and isentropic speed of sound have also been calculated at finite CCP. The temperature and CCP dependence of the viscosity to entropy density ratios $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ have also been studied.

## Key claim

The shear and bulk viscosities of a hot, chirally imbalanced quark matter are calculated using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, but the results depend on a finite, momentum-dependent thermal width for quarks and antiquarks, which is obtained from 2-to-2 scattering processes mediated by collective mesonic modes; without this finite width, the viscosities would diverge due to the "pinch singularity" problem, meaning the calculation only works for an interacting system.

## Method

The authors employed the one-loop Green-Kubo formalism within the real-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory, extracting the shear and bulk viscous coefficients from the long-wavelength limit of the in-medium spectral function of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) current correlator. The momentum-dependent thermal width of quarks and antiquarks, which serves as a dynamical input containing interactions, was calculated from 2-to-2 scattering processes mediated by collective mesonic modes in the scalar (sigma) and pseudoscalar (pion) channels, using in-medium polarization functions with explicit temperature and chiral chemical potential (CCP) dependence. Thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy density, entropy density, specific heat, and isentropic speed of sound were also computed at finite CCP within the mean-field approximation of the NJL model.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of viscous coefficients in hot and/or dense QCD matter using NJL-like models, such as those by Zhuang (1995), Rehberg (1996), Iwasaki (2007), Alberico (2007), Sasaki (2008), Marty (2013), Lang (2013, 2015), and Harutyunyan (2017), which used kinetic theory, Kubo formula, or relaxation time approximation, but did not include a chiral chemical potential (CCP). It also builds on the authors' prior work on the electromagnetic spectral function at finite CCP (Ghosh, 2022) and on the quark thermal width at finite CCP (Ghosh, 2024). The results enable future studies of dissipative hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions where local chiral imbalance may occur, particularly in the context of the chiral magnetic effect.
