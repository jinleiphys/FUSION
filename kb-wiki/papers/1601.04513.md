# Transverse momentum-flow correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The correlation between the transverse momentum and the azimuthal asymmetry the flow is studied. A correlation coefficient is defined between the average transverse momentum of hadrons emitted in an event and the square of the elliptic or triangular flow coefficient. The hydrodynamic model predicts a positive correlation of the transverse momentum with the elliptic flow, and almost no correlation with the triangular flow in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies. In p-Pb collisions the new correlation observable is very sensitive to the mechanism of energy deposition in the first stage of the collision.

# Transverse momentum-flow correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The hydrodynamic model predicts a positive correlation between the average transverse momentum and the square of the elliptic flow coefficient in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, but almost no correlation with the triangular flow coefficient. The correlation coefficient defined in the paper is designed to be largely free of self-correlation and non-flow effects, unlike the standard Pearson coefficient.

## Method
The author defines a correlation coefficient ρ(v_n{2}^2, [p_⊥]) between the event-averaged transverse momentum and the square of the elliptic (n=2) or triangular (n=3) flow coefficient, using separate pseudorapidity intervals to suppress non-flow correlations. The covariance and variances are computed using dynamical (self-correlation-subtracted) estimators. The observable is tested in 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, with statistical hadron emission to produce realistic finite-multiplicity events.

## Key numbers
- The flow-transverse momentum correlation coefficient ρ(v_2{2}^2, [p_⊥]) reaches 0.25 for Pb-Pb collisions at centrality 30-40%, indicating a significant positive correlation.
- For p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the correlation between average transverse momentum and initial eccentricity is ρ([p_⊥], ε_2) = -0.04 ± 0.03 and ρ([p_⊥], ε_3) = -0.13 ± 0.04 for the compact source scenario, versus ρ([p_⊥], ε_2) = 0.14 ± 0.03 and ρ([p_⊥], ε_3) = 0.05 ± 0.03 for the standard Glauber model at 0-3% centrality.
- The initial Gaussian smearing width for entropy deposition from each participant nucleon is σ = 0.4 fm, with a mixing parameter α = 0.15 for the number-of-collisions contribution.
- The r.m.s. radius of the fireball in p-Pb collisions is approximately 1.5 fm for the standard Glauber scenario and 0.9 fm for the compact source scenario.

## Context
This work extends the study of nonlinearities in the hydrodynamic response to initial geometry, complementing event shape engineering techniques used by the ALICE Collaboration (Adam 2015) and higher-order moment analyses (Bhalerao 2014). The proposed correlation coefficient provides a quantitative measure that can distinguish between different initial-state models, particularly in small systems like p-Pb collisions where the mechanism of energy deposition remains debated. The observable is sensitive to the smoothing scale in initial entropy deposition, which has been studied by Petersen (2010), Renk (2014), and Noronha-Hostler (2015), and could help discriminate between hydrodynamic expansion and partonic cascade mechanisms (Bzdak 2014) in small collision systems.
