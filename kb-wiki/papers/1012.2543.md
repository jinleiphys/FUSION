# Thermal effects on neutrino-nucleus inelastic scattering in stellar environments

> Thermal effects for inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering off even-even nuclei in the iron region are studied. Allowed and first-forbidden contributions to the cross sections are calculated within the quasiparticle random phase approximation, extended to finite temperatures within the Thermo-Field-Dynamics formalism. The GT$_0$ strength distribution at finite temperatures is calculated for the sample nucleus $^{54}$Fe. The neutral-current neutrino-nucleus inelastic cross section is calculated for relevant temperatures during the supernova core collapse. The thermal population of the excited states significantly enhances the cross section at low neutrino energies. In agreement with studies using a large scale shell-model approach the enhancement is mainly due to neutrino up-scattering at finite temperatures.

## Key claim
Thermal population of excited states significantly enhances the neutral-current inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section at low neutrino energies (below about 10 MeV), with the enhancement mainly due to neutrino up-scattering at finite temperatures, in agreement with large-scale shell-model studies.

## Method
The authors applied the thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA) within the Thermo-Field-Dynamics (TFD) formalism to calculate allowed (GT₀, 1⁺) and first-forbidden (0⁻, 1⁻, 2⁻) contributions to the inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section for the sample nucleus ⁵⁴Fe. The nuclear Hamiltonian used was that of the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model, including mean fields, BCS pairing, and separable multipole and spin-multipole particle-hole interactions. Cross sections were computed for temperatures relevant to supernova core collapse stages, using momentum-dependent multipole operators.

## Key numbers
- GT₀ strength centroid in ⁵⁴Fe shifts downward by 1.1 MeV at T = 1.72 MeV relative to T = 0.
- At neutrino energies below 10 MeV, thermal effects can increase the low-energy cross section by up to two orders of magnitude when temperature rises from 0.86 MeV to 1.72 MeV.
- At E_ν = 35 MeV, the INNS cross section calculated with the q-dependent 1⁺ operator is 20% less than that calculated with the GT₀ operator; at E_ν = 50 MeV the difference is about a factor of 2.
- For E_ν = 30 MeV, up to 20% of the cross section is due to first-forbidden transitions; at E_ν = 40 MeV, allowed and forbidden transitions contribute about equally; at E_ν = 50 MeV, first-forbidden transitions contribute nearly twice as much as 1⁺ transitions.
- The lowest 1⁺ state in ⁵⁴Fe at T = 0 has an excitation energy of approximately 7.5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Haxton (1988) on inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering from cold nuclei, and the large-scale shell-model calculations by Juodagalvis et al. (2002, 2005) which first analyzed thermal effects for hot nuclei. The TQRPA approach avoids assuming the Brink hypothesis for the down-scattering component, unlike the LSSM studies, and includes thermal effects for first-forbidden transitions as well as allowed ones. The results enable more accurate evaluation of neutrino opacities and thermalization during supernova core collapse, and the approach can be extended to calculate angular-dependent cross sections.
