# Pairing dynamics and solitonic excitations in collisions of medium-mass, identical nuclei

> We present results of collisions of $^{90}$Zr+$^{90}$Zr and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr obtained within time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) extended to superfluid systems, known as time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA). We discuss qualitatively new features occurring in collisions of two superfluid nuclei at energies in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier. We show that a \textit{solitonic excitation} -- an abrupt pairing phase distortion -- reported previously [P.~Magierski et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. \textbf{119}, 042501 (2017)], increases the barrier for capture generating effective repulsion between colliding nuclei. Moreover we demonstrate that pairing field leads to qualitatively different dynamics at the Coulomb barrier which manifests itself in a slower evolution of deformation towards a compact shape. Last but not least, we show that magnitude of pairing correlations can be dynamically enhanced after collision. We interpret it as a dynamically induced $U(1)$ symmetry breaking, which leads to large-amplitude oscillations of pairing field and bear similarity to the pairing Higgs mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper shows that collisions of superfluid medium-mass nuclei at energies near the Coulomb barrier produce a solitonic excitation, an abrupt pairing phase distortion, that increases the barrier for capture and generates effective repulsion between the colliding nuclei, but the magnitude of this effect is weaker than previously reported in Magierski (2017) because the present work uses a realistic Skyrme energy density functional rather than the Fayans functional without spin-orbit term.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) calculations for head-on collisions of identical zirconium isotopes (⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr and ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr) on a spatial lattice with 1.25 fm grid spacing, using the Skyrme SkM* functional for the mean-field term and a regularization scheme for the pairing coupling constant. They solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for both protons and neutrons, varying the relative gauge angle Δφ between the pairing fields of the two colliding nuclei and scanning center-of-mass energies to determine threshold energies for capture.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr with average neutron pairing gap 1.98 MeV, the threshold energy for capture is 179 MeV for Δφ=0 and 185 MeV for Δφ=π, giving ΔE_s = 6 MeV
- For ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr with average neutron pairing gap 2.44 MeV, the threshold energy is 178 MeV for Δφ=0 and 187 MeV for Δφ=π, giving ΔE_s = 9 MeV
- For ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr with average neutron pairing gap 2.94 MeV, the threshold energy is 178 MeV for Δφ=0 and 187 MeV for Δφ=π, giving ΔE_s = 9 MeV
- For ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr (average neutron pairing gap 0.00 MeV), the threshold energy is 184 MeV for both Δφ=0 and Δφ=π, giving ΔE_s = 0 MeV
- Total excitation energy for ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr at 178 MeV (just below threshold) with pairing gap 1.98 MeV is 37 MeV for Δφ=0 and 25 MeV for Δφ=π
- Total excitation energy for ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr at 177 MeV with pairing gap 2.94 MeV is 34 MeV for Δφ=0 and 8 MeV for Δφ=π
- Energy conservation during evolution was maintained within 8×10⁻⁴ relative deviation (less than 1.3 MeV)
- The spatial grid used 20×20×64 points for ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr and 24×24×64 points for ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr, with 1.25 fm spacing

## Context

This work extends the studies reported in Magierski (2017) by using a realistic Skyrme energy density functional (SkM*) instead of the Fayans functional, and by examining collisions of medium-mass nuclei (⁹⁰Zr and ⁹⁶Zr) rather than lighter systems. The results confirm the existence of solitonic excitations predicted in that earlier work but find a weaker dependence of the threshold energy on the relative gauge angle, consistent with the experimental analysis of Scamps (2018) suggesting a modest impact of superfluidity on fusion barriers. The paper also demonstrates that pairing correlations can be dynamically enhanced after collision, interpreted as a dynamically induced U(1) symmetry breaking analogous to the pairing Higgs mechanism, and shows that pairing dynamics significantly delays the evolution of deformation toward a compact shape compared to the pairing-free case.
