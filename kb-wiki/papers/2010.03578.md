# A New Paradigm for Topological or Rotational Non-Abelian Gauge Fields from Einstein-Skyrme Holography

> We report analytically known states at non-zero temperature which may serve as a powerful tool to reveal common topological and thermodynamic properties of systems ranging from the QCD phase diagram to topological phase transitions in condensed matter materials. In the holographically dual gravity theory, these are analytic solutions to a five-dimensional non-linear-sigma (Skyrme) model dynamically coupled to Einstein gravity. This theory is shown to be holographically dual to $\mathcal{N}=4$ Super-Yang-Mills theory coupled to an $SU(2)$-current. All solutions are fully backreacted asymptotically Anti-de Sitter~(AdS) black branes or holes. One family of global AdS black hole solutions contains non-Abelian gauge field configurations with positive integer Chern numbers and finite energy density. Larger Chern numbers increase the Hawking-Page transition temperature. In the holographically dual field theory this indicates a significant effect on the deconfinement phase transition. Black holes with one Hawking temperature can have distinct Chern numbers, potentially enabling topological transitions. A second family of analytic solutions, rotating black branes, is also provided. These rotating solutions induce states with propagating charge density waves in the dual field theory. We compute the Hawking temperature, entropy density, angular velocity and free energy for these black holes/branes. These correspond to thermodynamic data in the dual field theory. For these states the energy-momentum tensor, (non-)conserved current, and topological charge are interpreted.

## Key claim

The paper reports analytic, fully backreacted asymptotically Anti-de Sitter black brane and black hole solutions to a five-dimensional Einstein-Skyrme model, which are holographically dual to strongly coupled field theory states with topological or rotating non-Abelian gauge field configurations at non-zero temperature; the abstract's claim of a "powerful tool" is conditioned on these solutions being analytic and thus enabling explicit computation of thermodynamic and topological properties, but the authors note that the Chern-Simons term required for full supersymmetry of the dual $\mathcal{N}=4$ Super-Yang-Mills theory is omitted for simplicity.

## Method

The authors construct analytic solutions to the Einstein-Skyrme action in five-dimensional asymptotically Anti-de Sitter spacetime, which is shown via an invertible map to be equivalent to an Einstein-Yang-Mills theory with meron (half-instanton) gauge field configurations. Three families of solutions are obtained: a static black brane with Skyrmion hair yielding a Reissner-Nordstrom-like metric, a rotating black brane with Skyrmion hair, and a global AdS black hole carrying non-trivial topological charge (integer Chern numbers). The Hawking temperature, entropy density, angular velocity, and free energy are computed for these solutions and interpreted as thermodynamic data in the dual field theory.

## Key numbers

* Static Skyrme-AdS black brane blackening factor: \( A(r) = -\frac{m_s}{r^2} - \frac{\chi_2^2 \omega^2}{6 e^2 r^4} + \frac{r^2}{L^2} \), where \(m_s\) is the black brane mass, \(\omega\) is the angular velocity in internal \(SU(2)\) space, and \(\chi_2\) is an integration constant.
* Skyrmion field solution: \(\chi(r) = \arccos\left(-\frac{\chi_2}{2r^2} - \chi_1\right)\), with \(\chi_1\) and \(\chi_2\) integration constants.
* The meron topological charge is half the Skyrmion topological charge: \(\mathpzc{q}_\text{meron} = \frac{1}{2} \times \mathpzc{q}_\text{\,Skyrmion}\).
* The topological charge (second Chern number) for pure gauge \(SU(2)\) Yang-Mills solutions in four dimensions is given by \(\mathpzc{q} = \frac{g_{\text{\tiny YM}}^2}{8\pi^2} \int D d^4 x = \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \oint_{S^3} \hat n_i J^i d^3 x\), taking integer values for pure gauge fields.

## Context

This work extends prior holographic approaches to topological configurations: Son and Stephanov (2003) considered five-dimensional instantons in a non-dynamical metric background, while Sakai and Sugimoto (2004) used probe D4-branes in string theory to model Skyrmions as baryons. The present paper differs by dynamically coupling Skyrmions to gravity in the Anti-de Sitter bulk, providing fully backreacted analytic solutions at non-zero temperature, which enables the study of topological phase transitions and deconfinement transitions in strongly coupled field theories, including potential applications to the QCD phase diagram and condensed matter topological phases.
