# Nuclear effects in dAu collisions from recent RHIC data

> Neutral pion (pi0) production is calculated in a leading order (LO) perturbative QCD-based model in pp and dAu collisions at s^1/2=200 AGeV at midrapidity. The model includes the transverse component of the initial parton distribution. We compare our results for pp collision to experimental data at RHIC energy. We repeat our calculation for the dAu collision and investigate the interplay between shadowing and multiple scattering. In central $]dAu collisions the influence of possible jet energy loss on cold nuclear matter is discussed and numerical results are displayed.

## Key claim

In central dAu collisions at RHIC, the nuclear modification factor R_dAu shows a ±10% deviation from unity across all centrality bins, which overlaps with systematic errors and makes it impossible to exclude a null effect scenario; however, the data in the most central bin (0-20%) display a suppression at high p_T that may indicate moderate jet energy loss in cold nuclear matter, though the large error bars prevent a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors calculate neutral pion production in pp and dAu collisions at √s = 200 AGeV using a leading-order perturbative QCD-based parton model that includes a Gaussian transverse momentum component for initial parton distributions. For dAu collisions, they incorporate nuclear multiscattering via broadening of the parton transverse momentum distribution and compare three different nuclear shadowing parameterizations (EKS, HIJING, and HKM) within a Glauber geometrical framework. The nuclear modification factor R_dAu is computed as the ratio of the dAu cross section with nuclear effects to that without nuclear effects, and the results are compared to preliminary PHENIX data in four centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit value for the parton transverse momentum imbalance in pp collisions is ⟨k_T²⟩ = 2.5 GeV², yielding ⟨p_T,pair²⟩ = 5 GeV², compatible with di-hadron correlation measurements.
- The nuclear multiscattering broadening parameter C = 0.4 GeV², with a maximum broadening of approximately 1 GeV².
- The nuclear modification factor R_dAu shows deviations from unity within ±10% across all centrality bins in the transverse momentum region 2-20 GeV/c.
- In the most central dAu collisions (0-20% centrality), the data can accommodate a 10-15% suppression at high p_T, consistent with moderate jet energy loss in cold nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the LO pQCD-based approach of Wang (2001) and Zhang (2002) for describing nuclear effects in pA collisions, and builds on earlier studies by Levai (2003) showing that large k_T imbalance is required in both LO and NLO calculations. The results are compared to preliminary PHENIX data from dAu collisions at RHIC, and the observed suppression in central collisions is discussed in relation to cold nuclear matter energy loss seen in HERMES experiments and investigated theoretically by Wang (2004), Arleo (2003), and Accardi (2006). The paper enables future quantitative analysis of jet energy loss in cold nuclear matter once final data with smaller error bars become available.
