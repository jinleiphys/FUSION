# Deeply virtual pion production through two-loop order

> Deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) is among the most prominent channels to extract the nucleon's generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at $ep$ scattering facilities such as {\tt JLab} and the upcoming {\tt EIC/EicC} experiments, which plays a vital role in unravelling the three-dimensional internal structure of nucleon. In this work we calculate for the first time the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD radiative corrections to the DV$π$P processes $γ_L^* p\to π^+ n$ and $γ_L^* p\to π^0 p$ in the generalized Bjorken limit $Q^2\gg \vert t\vert, Λ_{\text{QCD}}^2$, accurate at the leading twist within collinear factorization framework. The impact of the two-loop QCD corrections appears to be positive and substantial, including which considerably improves the agreement between the perturbative QCD prediction and the available {\tt JLab} data. In addition to the differential longitudinal DV$π$P cross section, we also study the impact of the two-loop QCD corrections on the transverse single-spin asymmetries (TSSA) in some benchmark kinematics at {\tt JLab}, {\tt EIC} and {\tt EicC}.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD radiative corrections to deeply virtual pion production, finding that these two-loop corrections are positive and substantial, and that including them considerably improves the agreement between perturbative QCD predictions and available JLab data, though the improvement depends on the choice of GPD parametrization model.

## Method

The authors computed the NNLO hard-scattering kernels for the non-singlet channel of deeply virtual pion production by relating them to the known NNLO corrections to the pion electromagnetic form factor via an analytic continuation formula, and performed an independent two-loop calculation for the pure singlet contribution in neutral pion production using automated multi-loop tools (QGRAF, FIRE, AmpRed) with the method of differential equations for master integrals. The resulting coefficient functions were convolved with the twist-2 pion distribution amplitude (expanded in Gegenbauer polynomials with lattice QCD input from RQCD Collaboration) and nucleon axial-vector GPDs from two parametrization models (Goloskokov-Kroll and GUMP) to produce predictions for the longitudinal cross section and transverse single-spin asymmetry at benchmark kinematics for JLab, EIC, and EicC.

## Key numbers

- The NNLO correction to the longitudinal cross section for DVπ⁺P at JLab kinematics (Q² = 4 GeV², x_B = 0.5) is positive and substantial, even as significant as the NLO correction, with the relative importance decreasing as Q² increases.
- For DVπ⁺P at EIC kinematics (Q² = 30 GeV², x_B = 0.05), the perturbative convergence within the GK model appears satisfactory.
- The NNLO correction for DVπ⁰P is also positive and significant, with relative importance compared to NLO decreasing with increasing Q².
- The pure singlet contribution to DVπ⁰P, calculated for the first time at two-loop order, has modest phenomenological impact.
- The transverse single-spin asymmetry for both π⁺ and π⁰ production remains stable against NNLO corrections, but shows strong sensitivity to the underlying GPD parametrization at low t and small x_B.

## Context

This work extends the NLO QCD calculations for deeply virtual meson production that were first available about two decades ago (Belitsky 2001, Ivanov 2004) and brings them to NNLO accuracy, matching recent progress in NNLO calculations for deeply virtual Compton scattering (Braun 2022, Ji 2023, Braun 2025). The results enable more reliable extraction of nucleon GPDs from DVMP data at current JLab facilities and upcoming EIC and EicC experiments, and open the door to NNLO studies of a broader class of DVMP processes including neutrino-induced meson production.
