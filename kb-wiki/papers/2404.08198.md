# Spherical-like spectra for the description of the normal states of $^{108-120}$Cd in the SU3-IBM and the $Q_{2_{1}^{+}}$ anomaly

> The Cd puzzle implies that the phonon excitation of a spherical nucleus should be questioned and refuted. For understanding this spherical-like $γ$-soft mode newly found experimentally, a possible answer was proposed recently in the SU3-IBM. In this paper, the evolutions of the normal states in $^{108-120}$Cd are investigated and compared with the experimental results. For better explaining the nearly zero B(E2) values between the $0_{2}^{+}$ state and the $2_{1}^{+}$ state, except for the SU(3) second-order and third-order Casimir operators, other SU(3) higher-order interactions are also considered in detail. It can be found that the results of theoretical fitting and experimental data agree well with simple parameter selection. The spherical-like spectra really exist. The deficiency may come from the lack of configuration mixing. The realistic spectra characteristics of the spherical-like spectra are found for $^{118,120}$Cd and the electric quadrupole moments of the $2_{1}^{+}$ state are predicted. The $Q_{2_{1}^{+}}$ anomaly in $^{108-116}$Cd are also discussed.

## Key claim

The SU3-IBM with SU(3) higher-order interactions beyond the second- and third-order Casimir operators can reproduce the spherical-like gamma-soft spectra of the normal states in Cd isotopes, but the nearly zero B(E2) values between the 0₂⁺ state and the 2₁⁺ state in ¹¹²⁻¹¹⁶Cd are partly induced by configuration mixing with intruder states, and only for ¹¹⁸,¹²⁰Cd can the coupling between normal and intruder states be ignored.

## Method

The authors used the SU3-IBM Hamiltonian including the d-boson number operator, the SU(3) second-order and third-order Casimir operators, and additional higher-order interactions: the square of the second-order Casimir operator, the O(3) scalar shift operator Ω = [L × Q × L]⁽⁰⁾, and the fourth-order interaction Λ = [(L × Q)⁽¹⁾ × (L × Q)⁽¹⁾]⁽⁰⁾. They systematically varied the coefficients of these higher-order terms at a perturbation level for boson number N=7 and studied the evolution of energy levels and B(E2) values as a function of the control parameter η from 0.0 to 2.0, then fitted the model to experimental data for ¹⁰⁸⁻¹²⁰Cd.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹²Cd, the experimental B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) is 0.0121(17) W.u., while the theoretical value without configuration mixing is 4.04 W.u., showing the need for mixing in lighter isotopes.
- For ¹¹⁴Cd, the experimental B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) is 0.0026(4) W.u., compared to a theoretical value of 3.65 W.u. without mixing.
- For ¹¹⁸Cd, the B(E2) between the 0⁺ state and the 2⁺ state in the normal states changes from nearly zero to around 5.3 W.u., indicating the coupling to intruder states can be ignored.
- The quadrupole moment Q(2₁⁺) for ¹⁰⁸Cd is experimentally -0.45 eb, with theory giving -0.086 eb; for ¹¹⁰Cd it is -0.40 eb (theory -0.076 eb); for ¹¹²Cd it is -0.38 eb (theory -0.045 eb); for ¹¹⁴Cd it is -0.35 eb (theory -0.018 eb); for ¹¹⁶Cd the experimental value is not given but the trend is reproduced.
- The effective charges used in the B(E2) calculations range from e=1.41 (W.u.)¹/² for ¹¹⁴Cd to e=1.78 (W.u.)¹/² for ¹⁰⁸Cd.

## Context

This work extends the SU3-IBM framework previously introduced by Wang (2022) for ¹¹⁰Cd, which only used the SU(3) second-order and third-order Casimir operators. It addresses the Cd puzzle identified by Wood (2011, 2016, 2018), where the normal states of Cd isotopes show a spherical-like gamma-soft rotational mode rather than the expected phonon excitation of a spherical nucleus. The paper shows that the SU3-IBM can reproduce the unique evolution trend of the quadrupole moments of the 2₁⁺ state in ¹⁰⁸⁻¹¹⁶Cd, which constitutes the Q(2₁⁺) anomaly, and predicts the electric quadrupole moments for ¹¹⁸,¹²⁰Cd. The results enable future configuration mixing calculations between normal and intruder states in the lighter Cd isotopes.
