# Relativistic Effects in the Electromagnetic Current at GeV Energies

> We employ a recent approach to the non-relativistic reduction of the electromagnetic current operator in calculations of electronuclear reactions. In contrast to the traditional scheme, where approximations are made for the transferred momentum, transferred energy and initial momentum of the struck nucleon in obtaining an on-shell inspired form for the current, we treat the problem exactly for the transferred energy and transferred momentum. We calculate response functions for the reaction $^2H(e,e'p)n$ at CEBAF (TJNAF) energies and find large relativistic corrections. We also show that in Plane Wave Impulse Approximation, it is always possible to use the full operator, and we present a comparison of such a limiting case with the results incorporating relativistic effects to the first order in the initial momentum of the struck nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for electronuclear reactions at GeV energies, the traditional non-relativistic reduction of the electromagnetic current operator, which assumes transferred momentum smaller than the nucleon mass and transferred energy much smaller than the nucleon mass, yields large discrepancies compared to a treatment that expands only in the initial nucleon momentum while treating transferred energy and momentum exactly.

## Method

The authors perform a non-relativistic reduction of the on-shell electromagnetic current operator by expanding in powers of the dimensionless initial nucleon momentum η = p/m_N, while keeping the transferred momentum κ = q/2m_N and transferred energy λ = ω/2m_N exact. They calculate response functions for the reaction ²H(e,e'p)n using the Bonn wave function for the deuteron, comparing results from the full current operator, the operator expanded to first order in η, and the strict non-relativistic reduction.

## Key numbers

- For momentum transfer q = 2m_N (κ = 1) under quasielastic conditions, the transferred energy ω ≈ 1.2 GeV (λ ≈ 0.6, τ ≈ 0.6), giving an extra factor κ/√τ ≈ 1.3 in the current matrix element, leading to a 60% increase in observables that depend on the square of this factor.
- For momentum transfer q = m_N (κ = 0.5), the transferred energy ω ≈ 400 MeV (λ ≈ 0.2, τ ≈ 0.2), giving an extra factor κ/√τ ≈ 1.1, leading to a 20% increase.
- The dimensionless Fermi momentum η_F for deuterium is approximately 0.06 (p_F = 55 MeV/c), while for heavy nuclei it ranges up to about 0.28.
- The factor √τ/κ for the transverse response at fixed q = 1.4 GeV/c (κ = 0.75) takes values 0.90, 0.85, and 0.74 for kinematics below, at, and above the quasielastic peak respectively.
- The approximation to O(η) agrees well with the full operator up to missing momenta p_m ≈ 1.3-1.5 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to improve the non-relativistic reduction by expanding in powers of q/m_N (McVoy and Van Hove; Boffi et al.), which are insufficient when both transferred energies and momenta reach values comparable to the nucleon mass. The approach has been used in other recent publications (cited as PV and related works), but those studies had not explicitly checked the quality of the approximations using a realistic nuclear model. The paper enables systematic evaluation of relativistic corrections in electron scattering experiments at TJNAF (now Jefferson Lab) with its 4 GeV electron beam, particularly for near-quasielastic kinematics where traditional approximations fail.
