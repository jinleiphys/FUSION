# Beam energy dependence of squeeze-out effect on the directed and elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at high baryon density region

> We present a detailed analysis of the beam energy dependence of the mechanisms for the generation of directed and elliptic flows in Au+Au collisions focusing on the role of hadronic rescattering and spectator shadowing within a microscopic transport model JAM with different equation of state. A systematic study of the beam energy dependence is performed for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =2.3 - 62.4$ GeV. The transition of the dynamical origin of the directed flow is observed. We find that the initial Glauber type nucleon-nucleon collisions generate negative $v_1$ for nucleons at midrapidity due to the presence of spectator matter, and this negative nucleon $v_1$ is turned to be positive by the meson-baryon interactions at the beam energy region of $\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 30$ GeV. In contrast, above 30 GeV there is no spectator shadowing at midrapidity, and initial nucleon-nucleon collisions do not generate directed flow, but subsequent rescatterings among produced particles generate negative $v_1$ for nucleons. It is demonstrated that negative pion-directed flows are mostly generated by the interaction with the spectator matter. It is also shown that the squeeze-out effect is largely suppressed in the case of softening, which leads to the enhancement of elliptic flow around $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5-7$ GeV. The elliptic flow at midrapidity above 10 GeV is not influenced by the squeeze-out due to spectator matter, while its effect is seen at the forward rapidity range of $y/y_\mathrm{c.m.}>0.5$, which decreases as beam energy increases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dynamical origin of directed flow in Au+Au collisions undergoes a transition around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 30\) GeV: below this energy, initial Glauber-type nucleon-nucleon collisions generate negative nucleon \(v_1\) at midrapidity due to spectator shadowing, which is then turned positive by meson-baryon interactions; above 30 GeV, spectator shadowing is absent at midrapidity and subsequent rescatterings among produced particles generate negative \(v_1\) for nucleons.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic hadronic transport model JAM to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.3 - 62.4\) GeV, implementing different equations of state via nuclear mean-field potentials and a modified two-body scattering style that controls pressure (including an "attractive orbit" mode to mimic a first-order phase transition). They systematically disabled meson-baryon and meson-meson scatterings, as well as interactions between participant and spectator nucleons, to isolate the contributions of hadronic rescattering and spectator shadowing to directed and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range studied: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.3 - 62.4\) GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Transition energy for the dynamical origin of directed flow: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 30\) GeV (spectator shadowing dominant) vs. above 30 GeV (no spectator shadowing at midrapidity)
- Enhancement of elliptic flow around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5-7\) GeV due to suppression of squeeze-out by softening
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) GeV, spectator effects reduce nucleon and pion elliptic flow by 20-40% at midrapidity
- Passing time of two nuclei at 27 GeV: \(t_\text{pass} \approx 0.9\) fm/c
- Nuclear incompressibility used in mean-field mode: \(K = 272\) MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of spectator shadowing effects on anisotropic flow, which were previously known at lower beam energies (AGS energies, \(E_\text{lab} \leq 5\) AGeV) from studies by Bass (1993) and Hartnack (1994), to a systematic beam energy scan up to RHIC-BES energies. The results are compared with experimental data from the E895 Collaboration (directed flow at AGS energies) and the STAR Collaboration (directed and elliptic flow at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7 - 27\) GeV). The finding that softening of the equation of state suppresses squeeze-out and enhances elliptic flow around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5-7\) GeV provides a possible signature of a first-order phase transition, building on predictions by Chen (2017) and Nara (2017). The inability of the hadronic model to reproduce the similar directed flow of protons and \(\Lambda\)s measured by STAR suggests the need for partonic degrees of freedom in future models.
