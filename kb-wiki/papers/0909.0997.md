# Regularities and symmetries of subsets of collective 0+ states

> The energies of subsets of excited 0+ states in geometric collective models are investigated and found to exhibit intriguing regularities. In models with an infinite square well potential, it is found that a single formula, dependent on only the number of dimensions, describes a subset of 0+ states. The same behavior of a subset of 0+ states is seen in the large boson number limit of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model near the critical point of a first order phase transition, in contrast to the fact that these 0+ state energies exhibit a harmonic behavior in all three limiting symmetries of the IBA. Finally, the observed regularities in 0+ energies are analyzed in terms of the underlying group theoretical framework of the different models.

# Regularities and symmetries of subsets of collective 0+ states

## Key claim

The energies of a specific subset of excited 0+ states in geometric collective models with infinite square well potentials follow a single formula depending only on the number of dimensions D, but this formula is exact only for D=3 and is a very accurate approximation (deviations less than 0.1% through n=10) for other dimensions. In the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model near the critical point of the first-order phase transition between U(5) and SU(3), the same subset of 0+ states exhibits the n(n+3) behavior found in five-dimensional geometric models, in contrast to the harmonic (linear) behavior these states show in all three IBA limiting symmetries.

## Method

The authors analyzed the energy eigenvalues of subsets of 0+ states in five geometric models using infinite square well potentials in the beta degree of freedom (E(5), X(5), Z(5), Z(4), X(3)), normalizing energies to the first excited 0+ state rather than the usual 2+ state to reveal underlying regularities. They performed IBA calculations using the Hamiltonian H(zeta,chi) with large boson number N_B=250 along the U(5)-SU(3) leg (chi = -sqrt(7)/2) near the critical point zeta=0.473, comparing the resulting 0+ state energies to the n(n+3) formula and ground state band energies to a J(J+2) formula.

## Key numbers

- In E(5), Z(5), and X(5) models, the 0+ state energies normalized to the first excited 0+ state are exactly identical across all three models; the n(n+3) formula approximates these energies with deviations less than 0.1% through n=10.
- In X(3) (D=3), the 0+ state energies follow exactly E(0+_m) = A n(n+2) with n=m-1.
- In Z(4) (D=4), the 0+ state energies follow approximately E(0+_m) = A n(n+2.5) with n=m-1.
- The general formula for infinite square well models is E(0+_m) = A n(n + (D+1)/2) with n=m-1, where D is the number of dimensions.
- In IBA calculations with N_B=250, chi=-sqrt(7)/2, zeta=0.473, the 0+ state energies normalized to E(2_1+)=1.0 give: E(0_2+)=6.08 (analytic 6.00), E(0_3+)=14.85 (analytic 15.00), E(0_4+)=27.57 (analytic 27.00), E(0_5+)=42.55 (analytic 42.00) using the 3n(n+3)/2 formula.
- In the same IBA calculation, ground state band energies normalized to E(2_1+)=1.0 give: E(4_1+)=3.05 (analytic 3.00), E(6_1+)=6.08 (analytic 6.00), E(8_1+)=10.00 (analytic 10.00), E(10_1+)=14.73 (analytic 15.00) using the J(J+2)/8 formula.
- Approximate degeneracies hold: E(6_1+) = E(0_2+), E(10_1+) = E(0_3+), E(14_1+) = E(0_4+), E(18_1+) = E(0_5+) to the 10% level at J=18.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of 0+ state energies from the critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello 2000) and X(5) (Iachello 2001) to a broader class of infinite square well models including Z(5), Z(4), and X(3), and connects these geometric results to the IBA model near its first-order phase transition. The regularities are interpreted through the Euclidean group E(D) and its second-order Casimir operator, showing that the 0+ states in these models preserve a partial dynamical symmetry of Type I as defined by Alhassid and Leviatan. The results enable experimental tests of these predicted 0+ state patterns in nuclei near phase transitional regions and suggest further investigation of the degeneracies between 0+ states and alternate yrast band members.
