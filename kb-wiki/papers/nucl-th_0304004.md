# The Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q Potential

> Lattice QCD simulations of the potential between two baryons, each containing a heavy quark and two light quarks, such as the Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q potential, will provide insight into the nucleon-nucleon interaction. As one-pion exchange does not contribute to the Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q potential, the long-distance behavior is dominated by physics that contributes to the intermediate-range attraction between two nucleons. We compute the leading long-distance contributions to the Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q potential in QCD and in partially-quenched QCD in the low-energy effective field theory.

## Key claim

The long-distance component of the Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q potential is dominated by two-pion exchange rather than one-pion exchange, because the Lambda_Q baryon is an isosinglet with no Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q-pion coupling at leading order in the heavy-quark expansion. This makes the Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q system a cleaner probe of the intermediate-range attraction in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the potential falls off with a mass scale set by twice the pion mass and becomes quite small for baryon separations greater than about 1.5 fm.

## Method

The authors computed the leading long-distance contributions to the Lambda_Q-Lambda_Q potential in both QCD and partially-quenched QCD using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. They evaluated the box and crossed-box diagrams involving intermediate Sigma_Q and Sigma_Q* baryons, Fourier transformed the results to position space, and obtained integral representations for the potential. For the partially-quenched case, they also computed the modification to the crossed-box diagram from ghost and sea quark contributions, as well as the leading hairpin contribution from eta-meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- The spin-weighted average of the Sigma_c-Lambda_c and Sigma_c*-Lambda_c mass splittings is approximately 211 MeV, used as the value of the mass splitting parameter in the heavy-quark limit
- The axial coupling g_3 is determined from the measured width of the Sigma_c*++ to be |g_3| = 0.95 plus or minus 0.08 plus or minus 0.08 in the charmed sector
- The pion decay constant is taken as f_pi approximately 132 MeV
- The hairpin contribution to the potential at r = 1 fm is approximately -1 keV, and at r = 2 fm is approximately -0.06 keV, for c_1 = c_2 = 1, Lambda_chi approximately 1 GeV, and m_SS = 600 MeV
- At asymptotically large distances, the QCD potential falls as - (3 g_3^4 m_pi^(9/2) / (16 pi^(5/2) r^(5/2) Delta^2 f_pi^4)) e^(-2 m_pi r)

## Context

This work extends the program of using heavy-light hadrons on the lattice to study nuclear forces, previously focused on heavy meson pairs such as BB by the JLab/MIT group. It contrasts with the nucleon-nucleon potential, where one-pion exchange dominates at long distances and partially-quenched calculations are plagued by large quenching artifacts from one-eta exchange, as shown by Beane and Savage (2002). The computed potentials enable chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD simulations performed with unphysically large sea quark masses, and the authors note that the simplifications from having only two light quarks and an infinitely-heavy quark make this system a prime candidate for studying inter-baryon interactions.
