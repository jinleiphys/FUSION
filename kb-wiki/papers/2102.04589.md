# Two $^9$Li clusters connected with two valence neutrons in $^{20}$C

> Many preceding works have shown in $^{11}$Li the presence of the halo structure comprised of the weakly bound two neutrons around $^9$Li, and it is intriguing to see how this halo structure changes when another $^9$Li approaches. In this study, we introduce a four-body model for $^{20}$C with two $^9$Li clusters and two valence neutrons. The recent development of the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) makes it possible to generate $jj$-coupling shell-model wave functions from $α$ cluster models. Here, $jj$-coupling shell model wave function of $^9$Li is regarded as a cluster, which corresponds to the subclosure configuration of $p_{3/2}$ for the neutrons, and we discuss how the two neutrons connect two $^9$Li clusters. Until now, most of the clusters in the conventional models have been limited to the closures of the three-dimensional harmonic oscillators, such as $^4$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca; however, owing to AQCM, it is feasible to utilize the $jj$-coupling shell model wave functions as plural subsystems quite easily. The appearance of a rotational band structure with a cluster structure around the four-body threshold energy is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in $^{20}$C, a cluster state with two $^9$Li cores separated by about 3.75 fm appears below the four-body threshold energy, and that this state serves as the band head of a rotational band; however, this identification relies on bound-state approximation and the authors caution that some states above the neutron threshold could be continuum states, with only those showing rapid energy convergence considered candidates for resonance states.

## Method

The authors construct a four-body model for $^{20}$C consisting of two $^9$Li clusters and two valence neutrons, using the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) to generate $jj$-coupling shell-model wave functions for each $^9$Li cluster (subclosure configuration of $p_{3/2}$ for neutrons). The two $^9$Li clusters are separated by distances randomly generated between 0.5 fm and 5.0 fm, and the Gaussian center parameters for the two valence neutrons are generated using random numbers with an exponential distribution. The Hamiltonian includes the Tohsaki central interaction and the G3RS spin-orbit interaction, and the total wave function is obtained by superposing angular-momentum-projected Slater determinants and solving the Hill-Wheeler equation.

## Key numbers

- The lowest $0^+$ state of $^{20}$C converges to $-31.4$ MeV from the four-body ($^9$Li+$^9$Li+$n$+$n$) threshold; experimentally the ground state is at $-28.5$ MeV from threshold.
- The candidate cluster $0^+$ state appears at $-2.96$ MeV from the four-body threshold, with a proton principal quantum number $N$-proton = 6.12, corresponding to a $^9$Li-$^9$Li distance of about 3.75 fm.
- The two valence neutrons in the cluster state have an estimated $N$ value of about 4.0 (each sharing $\sim$2.0), indicating two-node orbits.
- The $2^+$ state of the rotational band is at $-2.29$ MeV and the $4^+$ state at $0.33$ MeV from the four-body threshold.
- The size parameter $\nu = 0.20$ fm$^{-2}$ is used for the Gaussian wave functions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AQCM studies of cluster structures in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly their preceding work on $^{18}$C (Itagaki et al., 2020) which showed a $^9$Li+$^9$Li cluster state below threshold. The study is motivated by the known halo structure of $^{11}$Li (Hansen and Jonson, 1985) and the molecular-orbital motion of neutrons around $\alpha$ clusters in Be isotopes (Itagaki and Okabe, 2000; Itagaki et al., 2008). The results enable future investigation of molecular-orbital states in $^{20}$C and connection to nuclear reaction experiments to populate these states.
