# Low energy proton reactions of astrophysical interest in A$\sim90-100$ region

> Semimicroscopic optical potentials for low energy proton reactions in mass 90-100 region have been obtained by folding the density dependent M3Y interaction with relativistic mean field densities. Certain parameters in the potential have been deduced by comparing calculated results with the data for elastic scattering. Low energy proton reactions in this mass region have been studied in the formalism with success. Rates of important astrophysical reaction in the mass region have been calculated.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a semimicroscopic optical potential, obtained by folding the density-dependent M3Y interaction with relativistic mean field densities from the FSU Gold Lagrangian, can reasonably reproduce low-energy proton reaction cross sections in the mass 90–100 region, except for one anomalous case. The claim is hedged by the fact that the potential parameters were fixed by fitting elastic scattering data and then applied uniformly across the region without individual reaction fitting, and that the quality of agreement improves at lower energies relevant to astrophysics.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field calculations using the FSU Gold Lagrangian in coordinate space to obtain nuclear densities for nuclei in the mass 90–100 region, assuming spherical symmetry. These densities were folded with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction in the Local Density Approximation to construct semimicroscopic optical model potentials. The real and imaginary parts of the potential were multiplied by fixed factors of 0.81 and 0.15, respectively, determined by fitting low-energy elastic proton scattering data on Zr and Mo isotopes. Cross sections and astrophysical reaction rates were then calculated using the Hauser-Feshbach formalism implemented in the code TALYS1.4, employing Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov level densities and E1 gamma strength functions from Hilaire.

## Key numbers

- Charge radii were calculated and compared with experimental data; the text states they are "reasonably well produced" but gives no explicit numerical values for radii.
- The real and imaginary parts of the optical potential were multiplied by factors of 0.81 and 0.15, respectively, to fit elastic scattering data.
- For the $^{98}$Ru$(p,\gamma)$ reaction, the measured cross section becomes larger than calculated values by "more than one order of magnitude" around 1.6 MeV proton energy.
- For $^{89}$Y$(p,\gamma)$, no experimental value differs from calculation by more than a factor of two.
- The proton energy range relevant to a typical $p$-process temperature of 1–3 GK for nuclei in this mass region lies between 1 and 4 MeV.
- For $^{96}$Zr$(p,\gamma)$, the text notes that numerical values in a reference were larger by a factor of $10^3$ than the values shown in the figure of that reference.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semimicroscopic optical model calculations, which were successful in the mass 60–80 region for rapid proton process reactions (Lahiri and Gangopadhyay, earlier works), to the mass 90–100 region relevant to the $p$-process. The study addresses the need for theoretical reaction rates identified by Rapp et al. for photodisintegration reactions producing $^{91}$Nb, $^{95}$Tc, and $^{99}$Rh. The calculated rates are compared with the widely used NON-SMOKER rates (Rauscher and Thielemann) and found to be very similar, suggesting that the present results can be reproduced with commonly used NON-SMOKER rates. The work enables more constrained theoretical predictions for $p$-process nucleosynthesis in this mass region, though the anomalous behavior of $^{98}$Ru$(p,\gamma)$ remains unexplained.
