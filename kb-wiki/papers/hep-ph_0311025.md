# Spontaneous rotational symmetry breaking and roton like excitations in gauged sigma-model at finite density

> The linear $σ$-model with a chemical potential for hypercharge is a toy model for the description of the dynamics of the kaon condensate in high density QCD. We analyze the dynamics of the gauged version of this model. It is shown that spontaneous breakdown of $SU(2) \times U(1)_{Y}$ symmetry, caused by the chemical potential, is always accompanied by spontaneous breakdown of both rotational symmetry and electromagnetic $U(1)_{em}$. The spectrum of excitations in this model is rich and, because of rotational symmetry breakdown, anisotropic. It is shown that there exist excitation branches that behave as phonon like quasiparticles for small momenta and as roton like ones for large momenta. This suggests that this model can be relevant for anisotropic superfluid systems.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a gauged linear sigma-model with a chemical potential for hypercharge, the spontaneous breakdown of \(SU(2) \times U(1)_Y\) symmetry is always accompanied by spontaneous breakdown of both rotational symmetry \(SO(3)\) (down to \(SO(2)\)) and electromagnetic \(U(1)_{em}\), and that the resulting spectrum contains excitation branches that behave as phonon-like quasiparticles for small momenta and as roton-like ones for large momenta, suggesting relevance for anisotropic superfluid systems.

## Method

The authors analyze the gauged linear sigma-model with a chemical potential \(\mu\) for hypercharge, using both global and local \(U(1)_Y\) symmetry. They derive the ground state solutions by minimizing the effective potential with an ansatz that breaks rotational symmetry, then expand the Lagrangian to quadratic order in fluctuations around the ground state to compute the spectrum of excitations. The dispersion relations are obtained analytically in the infrared region and numerically for larger momenta, for both positive and negative \(m^2\) cases.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential for spontaneous symmetry breaking is \(\mu^2 > m^2\) for \(m^2 > 0\).
- For \(m^2 < 0\), the second stage of symmetry breaking occurs when \(\mu^2 > \frac{g^2}{16\lambda}|m^2|\).
- The gaps (masses at zero momentum) for the excitations in the \(g' = 0\) case include: \(\Delta^2 = 0\) (3 modes), \(\Delta^2 = 2\mu\phi\) (2 modes), \(\Delta^2 = 2\mu\phi + 3\phi^2\) (2 modes), \(\Delta^2 = 4\mu^2\) (1 mode), \(\Delta^2 = \delta_{-}^2\) (1 mode), and \(\Delta^2 = \delta_{+}^2\) (1 mode), where \(\phi^2 = g^2\varphi_0^2/2\).
- For the gauged \(U(1)_Y\) case, the gap for two new states is \(\Delta^2 = \mu\phi + \phi_b^2/2 - \sqrt{(\mu\phi - \phi_b^2/2)^2 + \phi^2\phi_b^2}\), where \(\phi_b^2 = (g')^2\varphi_0^2/2\).
- The roton-like local minimum in the dispersion relation occurs at \(k_3 \sim m\) (in units of \(m\)).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ungauged linear sigma-model with a chemical potential for hypercharge studied by Miransky and Shovkovy (2002) and Schafer et al. (2001), where a kinetic seesaw mechanism leads to an unconventional number of Nambu-Goldstone bosons. The authors show that in the gauged version, the Nambu-Goldstone and Anderson-Higgs mechanisms become conventional, with three (or two for gauged \(U(1)_Y\)) gapless modes with linear dispersion. The presence of roton-like excitations, absent in the ungauged model, connects this work to superfluid systems and suggests the model may be relevant for anisotropic superfluids. The two-stage symmetry breaking pattern for \(m^2 < 0\) is compared to tumbling gauge theories.
