# Markov Chain Monte Carlo Predictions of Neutron-rich Lanthanide Properties as a Probe of $r$-process Dynamics

> Lanthanide element signatures are key to understanding many astrophysical observables, from merger kilonova light curves to stellar and solar abundances. To learn about the lanthanide element synthesis that enriched our solar system, we apply the statistical method of Markov Chain Monte Carlo to examine the nuclear masses capable of forming the $r$-process rare-earth abundance peak. We describe the physical constraints we implement with this statistical approach and demonstrate the use of the parallel chains method to explore the multidimensional parameter space. We apply our procedure to three moderately neutron-rich astrophysical outflows with distinct types of $r$-process dynamics. We show that the mass solutions found are dependent on outflow conditions and are related to the $r$-process path. We describe in detail the mechanism behind peak formation in each case. We then compare our mass predictions for neutron-rich neodymium and samarium isotopes to the latest experimental data from the CPT at CARIBU. We find our mass predictions given outflows that undergo an extended (n,$γ$)$\rightleftarrows$($γ$,n) equilibrium to be those most compatible with both observational solar abundances and neutron-rich mass measurements.

## Key claim

The paper uses Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to infer nuclear masses of neutron-rich lanthanides that can reproduce the solar r-process rare-earth abundance peak, but the mass solutions found are dependent on the specific outflow conditions assumed, meaning the method does not yield a unique set of nuclear masses but rather identifies which outflow dynamics are most compatible with both solar abundances and experimental mass measurements.

## Method

The authors applied the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm within a Markov Chain Monte Carlo framework to explore the multidimensional parameter space of nuclear masses for neutron-rich lanthanide isotopes, using the parallel chains method for convergence diagnostics. They implemented physical constraints including mass smoothness conditions and compared the resulting nucleosynthetic yields to solar r-process abundance residuals for three distinct types of moderately neutron-rich astrophysical outflows with different r-process dynamics. The mass predictions for neutron-rich neodymium and samarium isotopes were then compared to experimental data from the Canadian Penning Trap (CPT) at CARIBU.

## Key numbers

- The study focused on the rare-earth abundance peak near mass number A ~ 164 in the solar r-process residuals
- Three distinct astrophysical outflow conditions were considered, each with different r-process dynamics (extended (n,gamma) <-> (gamma,n) equilibrium, and other types)
- Mass predictions for neutron-rich neodymium and samarium isotopes were compared to experimental data from the CPT at CARIBU
- Outflows undergoing extended (n,gamma) <-> (gamma,n) equilibrium produced mass predictions most compatible with both observational solar abundances and neutron-rich mass measurements

## Context

This work extends the "reverse engineering" approach first introduced in earlier studies (REMM1, REMM2) that used statistical methods to connect nuclear properties and rare-earth abundances. It builds on prior work by Sneden and Frebel on metal-poor star abundances, and by Kasen and Barnes on kilonova light curves. The method enables future studies to use nuclear physics measurements to discriminate between different astrophysical outflow conditions responsible for r-process nucleosynthesis, and can be applied to upcoming data from facilities like the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB).
