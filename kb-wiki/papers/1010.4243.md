# Ginzburg-Landau phase diagram for dense matter with axial anomaly, strange quark mass, and meson condensation

> We discuss the phase structure of dense matter, in particular the nature of the transition between hadronic and quark matter. Calculations within a Ginzburg-Landau approach show that the axial anomaly can induce a critical point in this transition region. This is possible because in three-flavor quark matter with instanton effects a chiral condensate can be added to the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase without changing the symmetries of the ground state. In (massless) two-flavor quark matter such a critical point is not possible since the corresponding color superconductor (2SC) does not break chiral symmetry. We study the effects of a nonzero but finite strange quark mass which interpolates between these two cases. Since at ultra-high density the first reaction of CFL to a nonzero strange quark mass is to develop a kaon condensate, we extend previous Ginzburg-Landau studies by including such a condensate. We discuss the fate of the critical point systematically and show that the continuity between hadronic and quark matter can be disrupted by the onset of a kaon condensate. Moreover, we identify the mass terms in the Ginzburg-Landau potential which are needed for the 2SC phase to occur in the phase diagram.

## Key claim
The axial anomaly can induce a critical point in the transition between hadronic and quark matter, but only in three-flavor quark matter where a chiral condensate can be added to the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase without changing the ground state symmetries. This critical point is disrupted by the onset of a kaon condensate when the strange quark mass is nonzero, potentially breaking the continuity between hadronic and quark matter.

## Method
The authors constructed a Ginzburg-Landau free energy for the chiral condensate, diquark condensate, and neutral kaon condensate, based on the symmetries of QCD including the axial anomaly. They derived the potential up to fourth order in the order parameters and first order in the strange quark mass, then solved the stationarity equations for the three order parameters to determine the ground state phase diagram in the parameter space of the Ginzburg-Landau coefficients.

## Key numbers
- The kaon mass squared in the high-density effective theory is given by \( m_{K^0}^2 \equiv \frac{2B(m_d+m_s)}{f_\pi^2} + a m_u(m_s+m_d) \), where the instanton contribution is linear in the quark masses and the other contribution is quadratic.
- The effective kaon chemical potential is \( \mu \equiv \frac{m_s^2 - m_d^2}{2\mu_q} \), and kaon condensation occurs when \( \mu > m_{K^0} \).
- The critical strange quark mass for the onset of kaon condensation scales at high density as \( m_s \sim m_u^{1/3} \Delta^{2/3} \).
- The pion decay constant in the CFL phase is \( f_\pi^2 = \frac{21-8\ln 2}{18} \frac{\mu_q^2}{2\pi^2} \), with the pion velocity \( v_\pi^2 = 1/3 \).

## Context
This work extends previous Ginzburg-Landau studies by Hatsuda (2006) and Yamamoto (2007) that established the possibility of a critical point in the quark-hadron transition due to the axial anomaly in three-flavor matter. It incorporates a nonzero strange quark mass and kaon condensation, which was predicted by Bedaque and Schafer (2000) to occur in CFL matter at high density. The results show that the quark-hadron continuity proposed by Schafer (1998) and Alford (1999) can be disrupted by kaon condensation, and the paper identifies the mass terms needed for the 2SC phase to appear in the phase diagram.
