# Pions in the nuclear medium

> We discuss various aspects of pion physics in the nuclear medium. We first study s-wave pion-nucleus interaction in connection with chiral symmetry restoration and quark condensate in the nuclear medium. We then adress the question of p-wave pion-nucleus interaction and collective pionic modes in nuclei and draw the consequences for in medium $ππ$ correlations especially in the scalar-isoscalar channel. We finally discuss the modification of the rho meson mass spectrum at finite density and/or temperature in connection with relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Pions in the nuclear medium

## Key claim
Chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter is partially compensated by the medium's reaction against it, but higher-order exchange contributions exactly cancel this compensation, leaving pion exchange as the main source of deviation from the first-order density result. The paper argues that the 30% restoration at normal nuclear matter density predicted by the first-order formula is robust, but the precise value depends on the nuclear sigma term whose experimental determination lies in the range 3–9 MeV.

## Method
The author derives the quark condensate ratio R = ⟨q̄q(ρ)⟩/⟨q̄q⟩_vac using the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation and the PCAC soft-pion theorem, expressing R in terms of the full soft-pion-nucleus optical potential Π(0). Various medium corrections to the pion self-energy are evaluated: pion exchange effects from correlated ground-state wave functions, incoherent rescattering with short-range correlations in a linear sigma model, and higher-order contact terms in a non-linear sigma model. For p-wave interactions, a two-level model coupling pions to Δ-hole states is used to compute collective pionic modes and their effect on two-pion propagation and rho meson properties.

## Key numbers
- Quark condensate in vacuum: ⟨q̄q⟩_vac ≈ −(220 MeV)^3
- Pion-nucleon sigma commutator: Σ_N ≈ 45 MeV
- First-order chiral restoration at normal density: R = 1 − ρ Σ_N / (f_π² m_π²) → 30% restoration at ρ₀
- Pion exchange correction to R: δR ≈ −0.04 (ρ/ρ₀)², a 4% effect at normal nuclear matter density
- Birse and Mac Govern linear sigma model contribution: δR = −0.25 (ρ/ρ₀)² (ruled out by phenomenology)
- Nuclear sigma term deviation from bare nucleon sigma term: 3–9 MeV range
- Short-range correlation parameter: g'_ΔΔ ≈ 0.5
- Downward shift of delta peak in (³He,T) data: about 70 MeV
- Pion pair condensation threshold density: 1.3 ρ₀ (in models without chiral constraints)
- Critical temperature for chiral restoration: T_c ≈ 150–200 MeV
- Gluon condensate: ⟨(α_s/π) G^a_μν G_a^{μν}⟩_vac ≈ (360 MeV)^4
- CERES dilepton data: enhancement in the 500 MeV invariant mass region in S+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon
- Initial fireball conditions in transport model: temperature 170 MeV, nucleon and delta densities 1.0 ρ₀ each

## Context
This paper extends the first-order chiral symmetry restoration result of Cohen (1992) by incorporating medium corrections to the pion-nucleus sigma commutator, building on the work of Ericson (1993) who proposed the reaction of the medium against restoration. The collective pionic modes (pisobars) discussed here were previously studied by Chanfray and collaborators (1994) and are compared to (³He,T) experiments at SATURNE by Contardo (1996). The two-pion correlation analysis extends the Jülich model of Lohse (1990) and addresses the CHAOS collaboration data from TRIUMF. The rho meson discussion connects to the CERES collaboration dilepton data and compares the scaling law approach of Brown and Rho (1991) with conventional many-body mechanisms from Chanfray and Schuck (1994). The work enables future dilepton experiments with the HADES detector at SIS to distinguish between vacuum modification and many-body effects.
