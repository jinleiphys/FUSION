# Transverse momentum dependent feed-down fractions for bottomonium production

> We extract transverse momentum dependent feed-down fractions for bottomonium production using a data-driven approach. We use data published by the ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb Collaborations for sqrt(s) = 7 TeV proton-proton collisions. Based on this collected data, we produce fits to the differential cross sections for the production of both S- and P-wave bottomonium states. Combining these fits with branching ratios for excited state decays from the Particle Data Group, we compute the feed-down fractions for both the Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) as a function of transverse momentum. Our results indicate a strong dependence on transverse momentum, which is consistent with prior extractions of the feed-down fractions. When evaluated at the average momentum of the states, we find that approximately 75% of Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) states are produced directly. Our results for the transverse momentum dependent feed-down fractions are provided in tabulated form so that they can be used by other research groups.

## Key claim

The authors extract transverse momentum dependent feed-down fractions for Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) production in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, finding that approximately 75% of both states are produced directly when evaluated at their average transverse momentum. This result rules out the possibility that ground state Upsilon suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions could be solely due to excited state suppression, which was based on an outdated understanding of bottomonium feed-down fractions.

## Method

The authors performed fits to published ATLAS and CMS differential cross section data for S-wave bottomonium states (Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), Upsilon(3S)) using a functional form f(p_T, a, b, c, d) = a * p_T^b / (p_T^2 + c^2)^d. They combined these fits with LHCb measurements of P-wave to S-wave production ratios and branching ratios from the Particle Data Group to extract differential cross sections for chi_b(1P) and chi_b(2P) states. The feed-down fractions were then computed by inverting a feed-down matrix that relates experimentally observed cross sections to direct production cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For the ATLAS+LHCb extraction, the Upsilon(1S) integrated cross section is 69.069 +/- 0.035 nb with average p_T of 5.771 +/- 0.010 GeV
- For the CMS+LHCb extraction, the Upsilon(1S) integrated cross section is 72.34 +/- 0.17 nb with average p_T of 5.75 +/- 0.04 GeV
- The Upsilon(1S) direct production feed-down fraction at average p_T is 0.763 +/- 0.010 (ATLAS+LHCb) and 0.767 +/- 0.010 (CMS+LHCb)
- The Upsilon(2S) direct production feed-down fraction at average p_T is 0.774 +/- 0.018 (ATLAS+LHCb) and 0.758 +/- 0.019 (CMS+LHCb)
- The chi_b(1P) feed-down contribution to Upsilon(1S) at average p_T is 0.127 +/- 0.009 (ATLAS+LHCb) and 0.129 +/- 0.009 (CMS+LHCb)
- The chi_b(2P) feed-down contribution to Upsilon(2S) at average p_T is 0.183 +/- 0.018 (ATLAS+LHCb) and 0.195 +/- 0.019 (CMS+LHCb)

## Context

This work extends prior extractions of momentum-independent feed-down fractions used in phenomenological studies of bottomonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, such as those described in Brambilla (2020) and Yao (2020). The results are consistent with previous findings reported by Wohri (2015) regarding the strong transverse momentum dependence of feed-down fractions. The tabulated results enable other research groups to implement a unified picture of bottomonium feed-down, reducing uncertainty in analyses of bottomonium suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and can be applied at lower energies such as sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV due to linear scaling of bottomonium production cross sections with collision energy at LHC energies.
