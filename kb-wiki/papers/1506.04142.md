# High-precision determination of the pion-nucleon $Пғ$-term from Roy-Steiner equations

> We present a determination of the pion-nucleon ($ПҖN$) $Пғ$-term $Пғ_{ПҖN}$ based on the Cheng-Dashen low-energy theorem (LET), taking advantage of the recent high-precision data from pionic atoms to pin down the $ПҖN$ scattering lengths as well as of constraints from analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry in the form of Roy-Steiner equations to perform the extrapolation to the Cheng-Dashen point in a reliable manner. With isospin-violating corrections included both in the scattering lengths and the LET, we obtain $Пғ_{ПҖN}=(59.1\pm 1.9\pm 3.0)$ MeV $=(59.1\pm 3.5)$ MeV, where the first error refers to uncertainties in the $ПҖN$ amplitude and the second to the LET. Consequences for the scalar nucleon couplings relevant for the direct detection of dark matter are discussed.

## Method
The authors combine high-precision ПҖN scattering lengths extracted from pionic hydrogen and deuterium level shifts and decay widths with a complete solution of Roy-Steiner equations for ПҖN scattering, which enforce analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry. The Cheng-Dashen low-energy theorem is used to relate the Born-term-subtracted isoscalar amplitude at the Cheng-Dashen point to the scalar form factor of the nucleon, with isospin-violating corrections included both in the scattering lengths and in the low-energy theorem itself. The Roy-Steiner equations are solved by minimizing a ПҮВІ-like function that compares partial-wave phase shifts to the right-hand side of the dispersion relations, with the scattering lengths imposed as constraints.

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy between the earlier determination of ПғПҖN вүҲ 45 MeV by Gasser (1990) using the Koch-Hohler partial-wave analysis and the later value of (64 Вұ 8) MeV by Pavan (2001). The difference is traced to the modern pionic-atom scattering lengths and the Roy-Steiner constraint on the P-wave scattering volume a1+^+, which eliminates the need for independent input for that parameter. The precise Пғ-term enables improved predictions for scalar nucleon couplings relevant to dark matter direct detection, particularly for Higgs-mediated WIMP-nucleon interactions, and provides a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon sigma term.
