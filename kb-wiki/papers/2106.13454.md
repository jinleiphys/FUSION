# Deformation effect on nuclear density profile and radius enhancement in light- and medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei

> Mass number dependence of the nuclear radii is closely related to the nuclear matter properties. It is known that the most of nuclei exhibit some deformation. We discuss how the nuclear density profile is modified by the nuclear deformation to elucidate the enhancement mechanism of the nuclear radii through a systematic investigation of neutron-rich Ne, Mg, Si, S, Ar, Ti, Cr, and Fe isotopes. Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations are performed in a three-dimensional Cartesian grid to describe the nuclear deformation in a non-empirical way. The role of the nuclear deformation on the nuclear density profiles is explored in comparison to calculations with spherical limit. We find correlations between the nuclear deformation and the internal nuclear density. The evolution of the nuclear radii appears to follow the core swelling mechanism recently proposed in spherical nuclei [Phys. Rev. C 101, 061301(R) (2020)], and the radius is further enhanced by the nuclear deformation. This study demands further theoretical and experimental investigations for the internal density.

# Deformation effect on nuclear density profile and radius enhancement in light- and medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim
The evolution of nuclear radii in neutron-rich isotopes follows the core swelling mechanism previously proposed for spherical nuclei (Horiuchi 2020), but nuclear deformation further enhances the radius beyond the spherical-core prediction. The correlation between deformation and internal density change is approximately quadratic in the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂, with correlation coefficients ranging from 0.54 to 0.77 depending on the Skyrme interaction used.

## Method
Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations were performed on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid (sphere radius 20 fm) to obtain fully deformable ground-state wave functions for even-even neutron-rich Ne, Mg, Si, S, Ar, Ti, Cr, and Fe isotopes. Spherical constrained Hartree-Fock calculations using the filling approximation were also performed as reference states. Four Skyrme parameter sets (SkM*, SLy4, SkI3, and SIII) were employed, with SkM* results primarily discussed due to its validated description of deformation in Ne and Mg isotopes.

## Key numbers
- Correlation coefficients between β₂² and |Δ[ρ(0)]|: 0.77 (SkM*), 0.73 (SLy4), 0.66 (SkI3), 0.54 (SIII)
- For ⁶²Cr, the central density of the [000]1/2 neutron orbit from deformed HF is approximately 10% higher than the spherical 0s₁/₂ orbit; the proton central density enhancement is approximately 1%
- Single-particle energies for ⁶²Cr: -44.41 MeV (deformed [000]1/2 orbit) versus -43.29 MeV (spherical 0s₁/₂ orbit)
- Nuclear matter saturation densities for the four interactions: 0.1602 fm⁻³ (SkM*), 0.1595 fm⁻³ (SLy4), 0.1577 fm⁻³ (SkI3), 0.1453 fm⁻³ (SIII)
- Incompressibility K₀ values: 216.40 MeV (SkM*), 229.90 MeV (SLy4), 257.96 MeV (SkI3), 355.35 MeV (SIII)
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L: 45.80 MeV (SkM*), 45.94 MeV (SLy4), 100.49 MeV (SkI3), 9.91 MeV (SIII)
- The simple volume-conservation estimate gives Δ[ρ(0)] ≈ -0.01 to -0.05 for |β₂| = 0.1 to 0.3

## Context
This work extends the core swelling mechanism proposed by Horiuchi (2020) for spherical nuclei to deformed neutron-rich systems, where that earlier study showed that core density swells to reduce internal density for energy gain in Ca isotopes. The present study systematically covers isotopes with proton numbers 8 < Z < 28, building on prior deformation studies of Ne and Mg isotopes (Takechi 2012, Minomo 2011, Horiuchi 2012) and the known prolate dominance in medium-mass nuclei (Kumar 1970, Tajima 2001, Hamamoto 2009). The results enable future experimental investigations of internal nuclear density via electron scattering (SCRIT facility) and demand inclusion of pairing correlations and beyond-mean-field effects for quantitative comparison with data.
