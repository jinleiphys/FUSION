# Quadrupole shape invariants in the interacting boson model

> In terms of the Interacting Boson Model, shape invariants for the ground state, formed by quadrupole moments up to sixth order, are studied in the dynamical symmetry limits and, for the first time, over the whole structural range of the IBM-1. The results are related to the effective deformation parameters and their fluctuations in the geometrical model. New signatures that can distinguish vibrator and gamma-soft rotor structures, and one that is related to shape coexistence, are identified.

## Key claim

The paper maps the behavior of quadrupole shape invariants (up to sixth order) over the full structural range of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-1) for the first time, showing that these invariants can serve as signatures to distinguish vibrator (U(5)), gamma-soft rotor (O(6)), and rigid rotor (SU(3)) structures, with the caveat that the results depend on boson number N and the structural parameters zeta and chi.

## Method

The authors calculated shape invariants K_n (n=3-6) and fluctuations sigma_beta and sigma_gamma from moments of the quadrupole operator in the ground state, using analytical expressions in the dynamical symmetry limits (U(5), SU(3), O(6)) of the IBM-1. For the full parameter space between symmetry limits, they performed numerical diagonalizations of the Extended Consistent Q Formalism (ECQF) Hamiltonian using the PHINT code, then evaluated the invariants with a FORTRAN code (QINVAR) for N=10 bosons over a grid of the structural parameters zeta and chi.

## Key numbers

* In the U(5) limit for N=infinity: K_4 = 7/5, sigma_beta = 2/5, sigma_gamma = 21/25
* In the SU(3) limit for N=infinity: K_3 = 1, K_4 = 1, K_6 = 1, sigma_beta = 0, sigma_gamma = 0
* In the O(6) limit for N=infinity: K_4 = 1, K_6 = 1/3, sigma_beta = 0, sigma_gamma = 1/3
* Effective gamma deformation: gamma_eff = 0 degrees for SU(3), 30 degrees for both U(5) and O(6)
* The approximation K_4 approx = (7/10) * B(E2;4_1+ -> 2_1+) / B(E2;2_1+ -> 0_1+) is valid within about 10% for the ECQF-square and boson numbers N >= 5

## Context

This work extends the shape invariant formalism introduced by Kumar (1972) and Cline (1986) for analyzing Coulomb excitation data, and builds on approximation formulae by Jolos et al. (1997) and Palchikov et al. (1998) developed within the Q-phonon scheme. The results enable experimental determination of effective deformation parameters beta_eff and gamma_eff and their fluctuations from only a few B(E2) values, which is particularly important for exotic nuclei studied with radioactive beams where data are sparse. The invariant K_4 and the fluctuation sigma_gamma = K_6 - K_3^2 are identified as signatures that can distinguish U(5) from O(6) symmetry, which is otherwise difficult, and the region of rapid change in sigma_gamma corresponds to shape coexistence as seen in 152Sm.
