# Dynamical solution of the strong CP problem within QCD ?

> The strong CP problem is inseparably connected with the topology of gauge fields and the mechanism of color confinement, which requires nonperturbative tools to solve it. In this talk I present results of a recent lattice investigation of QCD with the $θ$ term in collaboration with Yoshifumi Nakamura. The tool we are using to address the nonperturbative properties of the theory is the gradient flow, which is a particular realization of momentum space RG transformations. The novel result is that within QCD the vacuum angle $θ$ is renormalized, together with the strong coupling constant, and flows to $θ= 0$ in the infrared limit. This means that CP is conserved by the strong interactions.

## Key claim
Within QCD itself, the vacuum angle θ is renormalized together with the strong coupling constant and flows to θ = 0 in the infrared limit, meaning strong CP conservation emerges dynamically rather than requiring new physics such as axions. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the gradient flow analysis of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, with the expectation that it extends to full QCD with massive quarks.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory using the plaquette action at β = 6.0 on 16^4, 24^4, and 32^4 lattices, with 4000–5000 configurations per volume. They applied the gradient flow to evolve gauge fields as a function of flow time t, which acts as a momentum-space renormalization group transformation, and computed the renormalized coupling α_V(θ, μ) via the energy density and a discrete Fourier transform over topological charge sectors.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.082(2) fm at β = 6.0, with √t₀ = 0.147(4) fm setting the scale.
- √t₀ Λ_MS̅ = 0.217(7), in agreement with a dedicated lattice calculation of 0.220(3).
- String tension √σ = 445(19) MeV, consistent with Regge phenomenology.
- Topological susceptibility √t₀ χ_t^{1/4} = 0.162(3), agreeing with the value 0.161(4) from Ce et al. (2015).
- Screening radius λ_S ≈ 0.31/|θ| fm, derived from the running coupling expression α_V(θ, μ) = α_V(μ)[1 − α_V(μ)(D/λ)θ²]^λ with D ≈ 0.10 and λ ≈ 0.75 on the 24^4 lattice.

## Context
This work extends the RG flow picture from the CP^{N-1} model at large N (Pruisken 2000, Levine 1983) and from instanton vacuum analyses (Knizhnik 1984) to full lattice QCD using the gradient flow. It contradicts the prevailing view that the strong CP problem requires beyond-Standard-Model solutions such as the Peccei-Quinn axion (Peccei and Quinn 1977). The results enable future lattice studies of the θ-dependent RG flow at smaller lattice spacings and with dynamical quarks, and raise questions about the consistency of the axion mechanism with color confinement.
