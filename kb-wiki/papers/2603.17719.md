# ALPHANSO: Open-Source Modeling of ($α$,n) Neutron Source Terms

> Applications ranging from nuclear safeguards to dark matter detection require accurate predictions of neutron yields and energy spectra produced by ($α$,n) reactions. Legacy tools like SOURCES-4C remain widely used despite significant limitations, including outdated nuclear data, missing target nuclides, and restricted accessibility. Here, we present ALPHANSO, an open-source Python package for calculating ($α$,n) neutron source terms. ALPHANSO incorporates modern nuclear data libraries and formats covering all naturally occurring target nuclides and provides a transparent, modular framework for updating or extending the data as new evaluations are released. Comparison with an updated version of SOURCES-4A, NeuCBOT, and experimental measurements across a range of elements and materials shows that ALPHANSO reproduces neutron yields and spectra in good agreement with experimental data and state-of-the-art ($α$,n) calculations. These results demonstrate that ALPHANSO is a reliable, accessible, and modern alternative to legacy ($α$,n) source term codes such as SOURCES-4C. Its open-source design and modular data handling make it readily extensible to future evaluated nuclear data and low-background applications.

## Key claim

ALPHANSO reproduces neutron yields and spectra in good agreement with experimental data and state-of-the-art (alpha,n) calculations, but the paper acknowledges that for some target materials (such as K2CO3) both ALPHANSO and SOURCES-4A substantially underpredict the reference yield, attributed to limited reliability of underlying beam data for potassium targets.

## Method

ALPHANSO uses the same thick-target, continuous slowing-down approximation as SOURCES-4C, computing neutron yield by integrating the ratio of (alpha,n) cross section to stopping power over the alpha-particle energy range. The code incorporates modern evaluated nuclear data libraries (ENDF/B-VIII.1, JENDL-5, TENDL-2023, and Parvu et al. re-evaluations) in GNDS format, with stopping powers from ASTAR and SRIM. Neutron energy spectra are constructed from two-body kinematics assuming isotropic emission in the center-of-mass frame, with branching ratios derived from individual level cross sections in the evaluated data files.

## Key numbers

* ALPHANSO shows a factor of approximately 400x speedup compared to the Monte Carlo code SaG4n
* SOURCES-4C is limited to alpha-particle energies below 6.5 MeV, while ALPHANSO extends to higher energies covering 21 nuclides in natural decay chains
* ALPHANSO reproduces experimental neutron yields from West (1982) for alpha beams spanning 4 to 9 MeV incident on thick targets of Be, C, Mg, and Al
* For homogeneous mixtures, ALPHANSO shows Calculated/Experimental (C/E) ratios for (alpha,n) reactions on light elements and compounds for the 235U, 238U, and 232Th alpha decay series in secular equilibrium

## Context

This work extends the capabilities of legacy deterministic codes like SOURCES-4C (Wilson, 2002) by incorporating modern nuclear data libraries and removing the 6.5 MeV energy limit that excluded high-energy alpha emitters critical for dark matter experiments (Mei, 2009). It improves upon NeuCBOT (Westerdale, 2017), which uses exclusively TALYS-based cross sections and has lower accuracy for some important target materials (Mendoza, 2020), and upon the modified SOURCES-4A (Tomasello, 2008; Parvu, 2025) by providing an open-source, modular framework that supports GNDS-formatted data and immediate integration of new evaluations. The code enables reproducible (alpha,n) source term calculations for nuclear safeguards, waste management, criticality safety, and low-background experiments.
