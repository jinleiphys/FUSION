# Compton Scattering on Light Nuclei

> Compton scattering on light nuclei ($A=2,3$) has emerged as an effective avenue to search for signatures of neutron polarizabilities, both spin--independent and spin--dependent ones. In this discussion I will focus on the theoretical aspect of Compton scattering on light nuclei; giving first a brief overview and therafter concentrating on our Compton scattering calculations based on Chiral effective theory at energies of the order of pion mass. These elastic $γ$d and $γ$He-3 calculations include nucleons, pions as the basic degrees of freedom. I will also discuss $γ$d results where the $Δ$-isobar has been included explicitly. Our results on unpolarized and polarization observables suggest that a combination of experiments and further theoretical efforts will provide an extraction of the neutron polarizabilities.

## Key claim

Compton scattering on light nuclei (deuteron and helium-3) can enable extraction of all six neutron polarizabilities (electric, magnetic, and four spin polarizabilities), but this extraction requires a combination of multiple experimental measurements and further theoretical efforts, as no single observable is sensitive to only one polarizability.

## Method

The author employs Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) at energies of order the pion mass, with nucleons and pions as basic degrees of freedom, to calculate elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron and on helium-3. For the deuteron, the calculation includes explicit Delta-isobar contributions within the Small Scale Expansion scheme and resummation of intermediate NN-rescattering states. For helium-3, the calculation treats the nucleus as a (2+1) nucleon system using Faddeev decomposition, with one-body and two-body operators folded with chiral NNLO wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability: alpha_p = (12.0 +/- 0.6) x 10^-4 fm^3
- Proton magnetic polarizability: beta_p = (1.9 +/- 0.5) x 10^-4 fm^3
- Iso-scalar nucleon polarizabilities from deuteron Compton scattering: alpha^s = 11.3 +/- 0.7_stat +/- 0.6_Baldin +/- 1_th, beta^s = 3.2 +/- 0.7_stat +/- 0.6_Baldin +/- 1_th (with Baldin sum rule constraint alpha^s + beta^s = 14.5 +/- 0.6)
- Neutron backward spin polarizability: gamma_pi = (58.6 +/- 4.0) x 10^-4 fm^4 from quasi-free Compton scattering on the deuteron
- ChPT prediction for neutron backward spin polarizability: 57.4 x 10^-4 fm^4
- Neutron forward spin polarizability: gamma_0 = -0.38 x 10^-4 fm^4 from VPI-FA93 multipole analysis
- Deuteron Compton scattering calculations cover photon energies from 45 to 125 MeV (lab frame)
- Helium-3 Compton scattering calculations at omega_cm = 60, 80, and 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory approach to Compton scattering from the proton (Beane 2002, Hildebrandt 2004) to the deuteron (Hildebrandt 2005) and helium-3 (Chen 2006, Chen 2007, Shukla 2009), with the goal of extracting neutron polarizabilities that cannot be obtained from free-neutron targets. The calculations enable planning of experiments at MAXlab and HIgammaS facilities, and the results suggest that polarized helium-3 behaves as an effective neutron target, allowing direct access to neutron spin polarizabilities through double-polarization observables.
