# Soft dipole mode in $^{11}$Li and three body continuum

> Properties of the neutron rich $^{11}$Li nucleus are calculated in the framework of the cluster model $^{9}$Li $+n+n$. The formalism of the harmonic oscillator representation of the scattering theory is used for the description of bound and continuum spectrum states in the three-body-democratic-decay approximation. It is shown that this approach allows one to take into account adequately the long asymptotic tail of the $^{11}$Li wave function ({\em neutron halo}) and to reproduce correctly the binding energy, radius and $^{11}$Li electromagnetic dissociation cross-section on target nuclei. The shape and the energy position of the $B(E1)$ peak corresponding to the soft dipole mode are also in agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The cluster model \(^{9}\text{Li}+n+n\) with the harmonic oscillator representation of scattering theory, applied in the three-body-democratic-decay approximation, reproduces the binding energy, radius, and electromagnetic dissociation cross-section of \(^{11}\text{Li}\) and yields the shape and energy position of the \(B(E1)\) soft dipole mode peak in agreement with experiment, provided the long asymptotic tail of the wave function (neutron halo) is accounted for via \(S\)-matrix pole location rather than pure variational diagonalization.

## Method

The authors expand the three-body wave function of \(^{11}\text{Li}\) (treated as a \(^{9}\text{Li}\) core plus two valence neutrons) in six-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenfunctions. They solve the coupled hyperspherical equations by truncating the potential energy matrix at a maximum number of oscillator quanta \(\tilde{N}\) and using the \(J\)-matrix (oscillator representation of scattering theory) to treat the infinite asymptotic region: for bound states they locate the \(S\)-matrix pole via \(\det A^{(+)}=0\), and for continuum states they compute the \(S\)-matrix at positive energies. Only democratic decay channels (no bound two-body subsystems) are allowed, and the \(n\)-\(^{9}\text{Li}\) interaction is described by the shallow potential of Johansen et al. (1990) with a Gaussian \(NN\) potential.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\text{Li}\): experimental value \(\varepsilon_{2n}=247\pm80\) keV; calculated ground state energy with \(\tilde{N}=24\) is \(-0.240\) MeV (compared to experimental \(-0.247\pm0.080\) MeV).
* Root-mean-square radius of \(^{11}\text{Li}\): experimental \(3.16\pm0.11\) fm; calculated with \(\tilde{N}=24\) and asymptotic tail is \(3.32\) fm (variational result without tail is \(3.04\) fm).
* Root-mean-square radius of \(^{9}\text{Li}\): experimental \(2.32\pm0.02\) fm.
* Electromagnetic dissociation cross section of 0.8 GeV/nucleon \(^{11}\text{Li}\) on Pb target: calculated 0.966 barn (experimental \(0.890\pm0.110\) barn); on Cu target: calculated 0.132 barn (experimental \(0.21\pm0.04\) barn).
* The soft dipole mode exhausts about 90% of the cluster EWSR, which is \(1/12 \approx 8.3\%\) of the total EWSR.
* The \(B(E1)\) peak maximum lies at excitation energy \(\approx 1\)–2 MeV.
* The minimal impact parameter used in the equivalent photon method: 9.0 fm for Pb target, 6.8 fm for Cu target.

## Context

This work extends the cluster-model studies of \(^{11}\text{Li}\) by Johansen et al. (1990) and others, who used variational approaches that could not reproduce the long asymptotic tail of the halo wave function. It also contrasts with calculations that allow only two-body decay channels (e.g., Lenske et al., 1993), which failed to reproduce both the position and form of the \(B(E1)\) peak. The method enables accurate description of the soft dipole mode and the electromagnetic dissociation cross section, and it can be applied to other weakly-bound three-body systems (Barromean nuclei) such as those studied in the \(\alpha+N+N\) model.
