# Neutrino-nucleus interactions and the short-range structure of nuclei

> Improvements in theoretical modeling of Short Range structures and phenomena, and comparisons with data, will require sustained collaboration between nuclear theorists and neutrino experimentalists. The extensive history of studying this area of nuclear physics in electron- and hadron-scattering experiments, coupled with the transformative capabilities of LArTPCs to identify neutrinos, will provide a ripe opportunity for new discoveries that will further our understanding of the nucleus.

## Key claim

Short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs in nuclei can be probed via neutrino-nucleus interactions in liquid argon time projection chambers (LArTPCs), but the intrinsic uncertainty on the incident neutrino energy limits the precision compared to electron scattering; the paper argues that sustained collaboration between nuclear theorists and neutrino experimentalists is required to improve theoretical modeling and Monte Carlo treatment of these short-range phenomena.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical description of short-range correlations (SRCs) in nuclei, focusing on the tensor character of the nucleon-nucleon potential and its effects on pair momentum distributions. They then analyze data from the ArgoNeuT experiment, which operated a LArTPC in the NuMI neutrino beam at Fermilab, to identify events with back-to-back proton pairs in the final state from charged-current neutrino and antineutrino interactions on argon. The analysis reconstructs initial-state nucleon momenta by subtracting the momentum transfer vector from the higher-momentum proton, analogous to triple-coincidence electron scattering analyses.

## Key numbers

- For high relative momenta (400–500 MeV/c) in carbon, approximately 90% of SRC pairs are isosinglet (np) pairs, while about 5% are pp pairs and, by isospin symmetry, about 5% are nn pairs (from JLab experiments on 12C).
- The ArgoNeuT experiment detected events with two protons in a strictly back-to-back, high-momentum configuration in the final state, with one example showing proton momenta of 552 MeV/c and 500 MeV/c.
- The detection threshold for protons in the ArgoNeuT LArTPC is approximately 20 MeV, well below the Fermi momentum in argon of about 220 MeV/c.
- The MicroBooNE experiment, with about 70 tons of active LAr mass (compared to ArgoNeuT's less than 0.5 tons), is expected to begin operations on the Booster neutrino beam (0.8 GeV average energy) at Fermilab in early 2015.

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations from electron scattering experiments at JLab (Arrington, Science) to neutrino scattering, using the ArgoNeuT experiment as a proof of principle. The paper builds on theoretical calculations of nucleon momentum distributions in s- and p-shell nuclei (Wiringa 2013, Alvioli 2013) and pair momentum distributions (Schiavilla 2007), and on the AV18/IL7 Hamiltonian developed for ab initio quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Pieper 2001, Pieper 2012). The results enable future high-statistics studies with larger LArTPC detectors like MicroBooNE, and motivate the inclusion of SRC effects in neutrino event generators for current and future neutrino oscillation experiments.
