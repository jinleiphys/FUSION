# Renormalization of the Off-shell chiral two-pion exchange NN interactions

> The renormalization, finiteness and off-shellness of short distance power like singular interactions is discussed. We show analytically that the renormalizability of the off-shell scattering amplitude relies completely on the corresponding on-shell amplitude without proliferation of new counterterms. We illustrate the result by complementary calculations both in coordinate as well as in momentum space in the simplest 1S0 channel for chiral np interactions including Two Pion Exchange.

## Key claim

The paper shows analytically that the off-shell scattering amplitude for chiral two-pion exchange NN interactions can be renormalized using only on-shell information, without requiring new counterterms for off-shell quantities, provided the same short-distance boundary condition is applied to both on-shell and off-shell wave functions.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space and the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation in coordinate space for the ¹S₀ channel of chiral np interactions including Two Pion Exchange at NLO, N2LO, and N3LO. They impose a common short-distance boundary condition on on-shell and off-shell wave functions, derived from the zero-energy on-shell problem, and remove the short-distance cutoff \( r_c \to 0 \) to demonstrate finiteness analytically using WKB approximations for the singular potentials.

## Key numbers

- The short-distance singular potentials scale as \( V(r) \to 1/(M_N^m f_\pi^n r^{n+m+1}) \) with \( n=5,6,7 \) corresponding to NLO, N2LO, and N3LO respectively.
- The finite cutoff effect scales as \( \mathcal{O}(r_c^{n/2-1}) \) for \( n=5,6,7 \), giving fast convergence as \( r_c \to 0 \).
- The on-shell point for the half-off-shell R-matrix at \( T_{\rm LAB}=50 \) MeV corresponds to \( p=153.2 \) MeV.
- The scattering length for the contact-interaction fit is \( \alpha_0 = -23.74 \) fm with \( r_c = 0.9 \) fm.

## Context

This work extends the on-shell renormalization program for singular chiral potentials developed by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2004, 2007) and the momentum-space counterterm approach of Entem and Ruiz Arriola (2007) to the off-shell case. It addresses the long-standing issue of off-shell ambiguities in NN forces (reviewed by Srivastava, 1975) and shows that, unlike in some effective field theories where off-shell finiteness is not guaranteed (Appelquist, 1980), the chiral two-pion exchange interactions can be renormalized off-shell using only on-shell conditions. This enables consistent treatment of off-shell quantities needed for three-body problems, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, and nuclear matter calculations.
