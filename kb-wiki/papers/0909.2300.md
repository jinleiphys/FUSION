# Relativistic RPA in axial symmetry

> Covariant density functional theory, in the framework of self-consistent Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and Relativistic Random Phase approximation (RPA), is for the first time applied to axially deformed nuclei. The fully self-consistent RMF+RRPA equations are posed for the case of axial symmetry and non-linear energy functionals, and solved with the help of a new parallel code. Formal properties of RPA theory are studied and special care is taken in order to validate the proper decoupling of spurious modes and their influence on the physical response. Sample applications to the magnetic and electric dipole transitions in $^{20}$Ne are presented and analyzed.

## Key claim

This work presents the first fully self-consistent application of covariant density functional theory (Relativistic Mean Field plus Relativistic Random Phase Approximation) to axially deformed nuclei, using a new parallel code that solves the RMF+RRPA equations for non-linear energy functionals. The authors caution that the method is demonstrated only for sample applications to magnetic and electric dipole transitions in \({}^{20}\)Ne, and that the proper decoupling of spurious modes must be validated for each calculation.

## Method

The authors formulate the relativistic RPA equations in a basis of axially deformed Dirac spinors, using the NL3 parameter set for the non-linear meson coupling functional. They solve the static RMF equations for the deformed ground state, then construct the RPA matrices including particle-hole and antiparticle-hole configurations (the latter required by the no-sea approximation), and diagonalize the non-Hermitian RPA matrix to obtain excitation energies and transition strengths. The residual interaction is derived from meson exchange (sigma, omega, rho, and photon) with Yukawa propagators, and for the non-linear sigma field the propagator is obtained numerically from a modified Helmholtz equation with a position-dependent mass term.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of \({}^{20}\)Ne is calculated using the NL3 parameter set, which includes the non-linear sigma self-interaction terms with parameters \(g_2\) and \(g_3\) in the potential \(U(\sigma) = \frac{g_2}{3}\sigma^3 + \frac{g_3}{4}\sigma^4\).
* The RPA equations include configurations where holes in the Fermi sea occupy empty levels in the Dirac sea; neglecting these antiparticle-hole configurations can shift the energy of the giant monopole resonance in \({}^{208}\)Pb from the experimental value at 14 MeV down to 2-3 MeV (cited from MGT.97).
* The orthogonality relation for RPA states is \(\sum_{mi} X_{mi}^{(\nu)*} X_{mi}^{(\nu')} - Y_{mi}^{(\nu)*} Y_{mi}^{(\nu')} = \delta_{\nu\nu'}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic RPA framework, previously restricted to spherical nuclei (Fur.85, MWG.02), to axially deformed systems, following only a few non-relativistic deformed RPA calculations based on Skyrme forces (HHR.98, NKK.06) or Gogny forces (PGB.07). The ability to compute the electric dipole response of deformed nuclei provides valuable input for astrophysical r-process and s-process calculations (Gor.98), which pass through large regions of deformed nuclei. The authors note that most nuclei in the nuclear chart are deformed, so this formalism enables systematic studies of collective excitations across the periodic table.
