# Study of Delta-Delta excitations in the reaction n+p -> d+pi+pi

> The deuteron spectrum in n+p -> d+pi+pi at k_n = 1.88 GeV/c and theta_d = 0 deg is explained by considering a Delta-Delta excitation as the reaction mechanism for the 2pi production. We study the influence of intermediate Delta-Delta and Delta-Nucleon interactions on the spectrum. The angular distribution of the pions is predicted.

## Key claim

The paper explains the deuteron spectrum in the reaction n + p → d + ππ at a neutron momentum of 1.88 GeV/c and deuteron angle of 0 degrees by assuming a double Δ(1232) excitation as the dominant reaction mechanism, but the actual agreement depends on including both intermediate ΔΔ and ΔN interaction potentials in the coupled-channel calculation.

## Method

The authors applied a coupled-channel approach derived from Δ-hole models, using the source function formalism to set up integro-differential equations for the correlated ΔΔ wavefunction solved in configuration space with the Lanczos method. The Δ resonance was treated as a quasi-particle with an energy-dependent width, and the ΔΔ and ΔN interaction potentials were constructed within a meson exchange model including π, ρ, ω, and σ mesons, with all parameters determined from other reaction studies such as single pion production and charge exchange reactions on the deuteron (Mosbacher 1997).

## Key numbers

- Neutron beam momentum: 1.88 GeV/c
- Deutron detection angle: θ_d = 0 degrees
- The low-mass enhancement in the cross section occurs at a dipion invariant mass M_{ππ} = 2m_π (approximately 280 MeV)
- The high-mass enhancement corresponds to the maximum possible dipion invariant mass, where the two-pion center-of-mass system is identical to the overall center-of-mass system
- Angular distributions are shown for three specific deuteron momenta in the lab frame: k_d = 1.1, 1.5, and 1.9 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the ABC effect in n + p → d + ππ first observed by Abashian (1963) and measured at 1.88 GeV/c by Plouin (1978), which had been explained by a double Δ excitation mechanism proposed by Risser (1973) and Barnir (1975). The paper enables future closer examination of ΔΔ and ΔN interaction potentials by showing that this reaction is sensitive to these interactions, and it provides predictions for the angular distribution of the pions that can be tested experimentally.
