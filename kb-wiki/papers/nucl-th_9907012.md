# Damping of IVGDR - Fermi-liquid or Fermi-gas ?

> Collisional relaxation rates of collective modes in nuclei are calculated using the Levinson equation for the reduced density matrix with a memory dependent collision term. Linearizing the collision integral two contribution have to be distinguished, the one from the quasiparticle energy and the one from occupation factors. The first one yields the known Landau formula of zero sound damping and the second one leads to the Fermi gas model of Ref.1 with the additional factor 3 in front of the frequencies. Adding both contribution we obtain a final relaxation rate for the Fermi liquid model. Calculations of the temperature dependence of the damping rates and of the shape evolution of IVGDR are in good agreement with the experiment and show only minor differences between both models.

# Damping of IVGDR - Fermi-liquid or Fermi-gas ?

## Key claim
The collisional relaxation rate for the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) can be derived from a Fermi liquid model that adds quasiparticle energy contributions to the Fermi gas result, yielding a final relaxation rate that differs from the Fermi gas prediction by a factor of 2 in the frequency-squared term; however, calculations show only minor differences between both models when compared to experimental data, and the temperature dependence remains too flat relative to measurements.

## Method
The authors start from the Levinson equation for the reduced density matrix with a memory-dependent collision term and linearize the collision integral with respect to an external disturbance. They separate two contributions: one from occupation factors (Fermi gas model) and one from the quasiparticle energy variation (additional Fermi liquid contribution). The relaxation times are computed via thermal averaging, then inserted into a Mermin-type polarization function to determine the IVGDR damping rates and strength functions from the zeros of the dielectric function.

## Key numbers
- Fermi gas relaxation rate (thermally averaged): 1/tau_gas = (1/tau_0) * [1 + 3*(omega/(2*pi))^2]
- Fermi liquid relaxation rate (thermally averaged): 1/tau_liq = (2/tau_0) * [1 + 2*(omega/(2*pi))^2]
- At vanishing temperature, the Fermi liquid relaxation rate is twice that of the Fermi gas model (factor 2 vs factor 1 in the constant term)
- The Fermi gas model gives a temperature dependence proportional to 3*Omega^2, while the Fermi liquid gives 2*Omega^2
- The Landau formula for zero sound damping is gamma proportional to [1 + (Omega/(2*pi*T))^2]

## Context
This work extends the Fermi gas model of Fuhrmann, Morawetz and Walke (1998) by adding the quasiparticle contribution to obtain a full Fermi liquid description, resolving the discrepancy between the Fermi gas result and the Landau damping formula of zero sound from Abrikosov and Khalatnikov (1959). The calculations are applied to IVGDR damping in 120Sn and 208Pb and compared with experimental data from Ramakrishnan et al. (1996) and Baumann et al. (1998). The results show that both models give similar predictions, with the Fermi liquid model yielding a slightly lower relaxation rate at finite temperature, but neither fully captures the steepness of the experimental temperature dependence.
