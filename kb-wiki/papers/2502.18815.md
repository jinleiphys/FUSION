# Forbidden non-unique $β^{-}$ transitions and $g_A$-sensitive electron spectral-shapes

> In the present work, we have done a systematic study of beta decay properties such as electron spectral-shapes, shape factors, and log$ft$ values for the higher forbidden non-unique $β^{-}$ transitions in the mass region A=85-123. We have performed the nuclear shell model (SM) calculations to explore the sensitivity of the electron spectral-shapes for different axial-vector coupling constants $g_{A}=0.8-1.27$. The effective interactions GWBXG, G-matrix, SNET and SN100PN are used for different model spaces. In the present work, we have computed the electron spectral-shapes of $^{85}$Br, $^{87}$Rb, $^{93}$Zr, $^{97}$Zr, $^{101}$Mo, $^{115}$Cd, $^{117}$Cd, $^{119}$In, $^{123}$Sn and $^{135}$Cs by constraining the small relativistic nuclear matrix element from conserved vector-current hypothesis (CVC). We have found that the electron spectral-shapes are strongly dependent on $g_{A}$ except the second forbidden non-unique $β^{-}$ transition $^{93}$Zr.

## Key claim

The electron spectral-shapes of higher forbidden non-unique beta transitions in the mass region A=85-123 are strongly dependent on the axial-vector coupling constant g_A, except for the second forbidden non-unique transition of 93Zr, where the dependence is weak; this sensitivity is evaluated by constraining the small relativistic nuclear matrix element using the conserved vector-current (CVC) hypothesis.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations using the KSHELL code with four effective interactions (GWBXG, G-matrix, SNET, SN100PN) across different model spaces to compute one-body transition densities for ten beta-decaying nuclei. The electron spectral-shapes, shape factors, and log ft values were computed using the Behrens-Buhring formalism, with the small relativistic nuclear matrix element (s-NME) constrained via the CVC relation rather than set to zero as in standard 0-hbar-omega shell model calculations. The spectra were normalized to unit area and evaluated for g_A values ranging from 0.80 to 1.27 with g_V fixed at 1.0.

## Key numbers

- 93Zr(5/2+ → 9/2+) second forbidden transition: log ft = 11.847 (SM+CVC, g_A=1.00) and 11.752 (g_A=1.27); Q-value = 90.8(15) keV; branching ratio 27%
- 135Cs(7/2+ → 3/2+) second forbidden transition: log ft = 12.935 (SM+CVC, g_A=1.00) and 12.809 (g_A=1.27); Q-value = 268.70(29) keV
- 87Rb(3/2- → 9/2+) third forbidden transition: log ft = 16.430 (SM+CVC, g_A=1.00) and 16.311 (g_A=1.27); Q-value = 282.275(6) keV; experimental log ft = 17.514(7)
- 115Cd(1/2+ → 9/2+) fourth forbidden transition: log ft = 19.569 (SM+CVC, g_A=1.00) and 19.452 (g_A=1.27); Q-value = 1451.9(7) keV
- 123Sn(11/2- → 1/2+) fifth forbidden transition: log ft = 26.742 (SM+CVC, g_A=1.00) and 26.616 (g_A=1.27); Q-value = 1408.2(24) keV
- CVC-constrained s-NME for 93Zr: V_M(0)_211(CVC) = -0.2577; l-NME V_M(0)_220 = -13.8324
- CVC-constrained s-NME for 123Sn: V_M(0)_541(CVC) = -12.227; l-NME V_M(0)_550 = -1290.0080

## Context

This work extends the spectrum shape method (SSM) for extracting the effective g_A value, previously applied to 113Cd and 115In by Suhonen (2017) and to sd-shell nuclei 36Cl and 24Na by Kumar (2020), into the A=85-123 mass region for higher forbidden non-unique transitions. The study contrasts with earlier MQPM calculations by Haaranen (2017) for 135Cs and 123Sn, finding agreement when s-NMEs are zero but divergence when CVC is applied; it also complements recent shell model studies by Ramalho (2024) in the A=86-99 region. The results identify candidates for future shape measurement experiments that could help determine the effective g_A value relevant for neutrinoless double beta decay searches.
