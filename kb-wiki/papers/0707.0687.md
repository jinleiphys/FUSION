# The Dissociation of 8B in the Coulomb Field and the Validity of the CD Method

> The GSI1, GSI2 (as well as the RIKEN2 and the corrected GSI2) measurements of the Coulomb Dissociation (CD) of 8B are in good agreement with the most recent Direct Capture (DC) 7Be(p,g)8B reaction measurement performed at Weizmann and in agreement with the Seattle result. Yet it was claimed that the CD and DC results are sufficiently different and need to be reconciled. We show that these statements arise from a misunderstanding (as well as misrepresentation) of CD experiments. We recall a similar strong statement questioning the validity of the CD method due to an invoked large E2 component that was also shown to arise from a misunderstanding of the CD method. In spite of the good agreement between DC and CD data the slope of the astrophysical cross section factor (S17) can not be extracted with high accuracy due to a discrepancy between the recent DC data as well as a discrepancy of the three reports of the GSI CD data. The slope is directly related to the d-wave component that dominates at higher energies and must be subtracted from measured data to extrapolate to zero energy. Hence the uncertainty of the measured slope leads to an additional uncertainty of the extrapolated zero energy cross section factor, S17(0). This uncertainty must be alleviated by future experiments to allow a precise determination of S17(0), a goal that so far has not be achieved in spite of strong statement(s) that appeared in the literature.

## Key claim

Despite claims of significant disagreement between Coulomb Dissociation (CD) and Direct Capture (DC) measurements of the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction, the CD and DC data are actually in good agreement; however, the slope of the astrophysical S17 factor cannot be extracted with high accuracy due to discrepancies among DC data sets and among the three reports of GSI CD data, leading to additional uncertainty in the extrapolated zero-energy cross section factor S17(0).

## Method

The author compares published Coulomb Dissociation data (GSI1, GSI2, corrected GSI2, RIKEN2) with Direct Capture data (Seattle, Weizmann, Bochum, Orsay) for the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction. The slope parameter S' = dS/dE is extracted from both CD and DC data in the energy range 300–1500 keV, and the extrapolated S17(0) values from CD experiments are compared using the Descouvemont and Baye extrapolation procedure. The analysis examines systematic disagreements among data sets and the impact of excluding CD data above 425 keV.

## Key numbers

- The 1/σ weighted average of S17(0) from four CD results is 20.0 ± 0.7 eV-b with χ² = 0.5, in excellent agreement with the Weizmann DC measurement and in agreement with the Seattle DC result.
- The d-wave contribution is approximately 6% at zero energy but dominates around 1.0 MeV.
- A reasonable systematic error of +0.0 –3.0 eV-b due to extrapolation is required by current data.
- The overall agreement between CD and DC slopes is 1.7 sigma, which is better than the agreement among specific DC data sets.
- Individual data points from the Bochum group and the Seattle group disagree by as much as five sigma.

## Context

This work extends the debate over the validity of the Coulomb Dissociation method for the 8B system, following earlier disputes about E2 contributions raised by Langanke (1996) and the MSU group, which were resolved by exclusive measurements from the GSI2 collaboration (Schumann 2003). The paper directly contradicts claims by Esbensen, Bertsch, and Snover (2005) that CD data require substantial corrections and that CD-derived S17(0) values are on average 10% lower than modern direct measurements. The analysis enables future experiments to resolve the slope discrepancies among DC data (Seattle, Weizmann, Bochum, Orsay) and among GSI CD data sets, which are necessary for an accurate extrapolation of S17(0) to solar burning energies.
