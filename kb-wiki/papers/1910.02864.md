# Possible lightest $Ξ$ Hypernucleus with Modern $ΞN$ Interactions

> Experimental evidence exists that the $Ξ$-nucleus interaction is attractive. We search for $NNΞ$ and $NNNΞ$ bound systems on the basis of the AV8 $NN$ potential combined with either a phenomenological Nijmegen $ΞN$ potential or a first principles HAL QCD $ΞN$ potential. The binding energies of the three-body and four-body systems (below the $d+Ξ$ and $^3{\rm H}$/$^3{\rm He}+Ξ$ thresholds, respectively) are calculated by a high precision variational approach, the Gaussian Expansion Method. Although the two $ΞN$ potentials have significantly different isospin ($T$) and spin ($S$) dependence, the $NNNΞ$ system with quantum numbers $(T=0, J^π=1^+$) appears to be bound (one deep for Nijmegen and one shallow for HAL QCD) below the $^3{\rm H}$/$^3{\rm He}+Ξ$ threshold. Experimental implications for such a state are discussed.

## Key claim

The lightest bound Ξ hypernucleus is predicted to be the four-body NNNΞ system with quantum numbers (T=0, J^π=1^+), but the binding mechanism and energy depend critically on which ΞN potential is used: the phenomenological Nijmegen ESC08c potential gives a deep bound (~10 MeV) driven by the ³³S₁ channel, while the first-principles HAL QCD potential gives only a shallow, Coulomb-assisted bound state (0.36 MeV) driven by the ¹¹S₀ channel.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body NNΞ and four-body NNNΞ Schrödinger equations using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), a high-precision variational approach with Gaussian basis functions. They employed the AV8 NN potential combined with either the Nijmegen ESC08c ΞN potential (a meson-exchange phenomenological model) or the HAL QCD ΞN potential (derived from lattice QCD simulations at nearly physical quark masses). For the NNNΞ system, a phenomenological three-body force was added to reproduce the ³H binding energy, and Coulomb interactions were included for the charged particles.

## Key numbers

- NNΞ binding energy with ESC08c: 7.20 MeV for (T=1/2, J^π=3/2^+) below the d+Ξ threshold; no bound state for (T=1/2, J^π=1/2^+)
- NNNΞ binding energy with ESC08c: 10.20 MeV for (T=0, J^π=1^+), 3.55 MeV for (T=1, J^π=0^+), and 10.11 MeV for (T=1, J^π=1^+) below the ³H/³He+Ξ threshold
- NNNΞ binding energy with HAL QCD: 0.36 (16)(26) MeV for (T=0, J^π=1^+) at t/a=12, where the first parenthesis is the statistical error and the second is the systematic error from t/a=11 and 13 data
- HAL QCD expectation values for the (T=0, J^π=1^+) state at t/a=12: ⟨V_ΞN^strong⟩ = -2.06 MeV, ⟨V_ΞN^Coulomb⟩ = -0.38 MeV
- Decay width Γ of NNNΞ (0,1^+) from ΞN-ΛΛ coupling: 0.89 MeV for ESC08c; 0.06, 0.05, 0.03 MeV for HAL QCD at t/a=11,12,13 respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Ξ hypernuclei motivated by the KISO event (a candidate for ¹⁵_ΞC) and femtoscopic data from LHC indicating attractive ΞN interactions. It contrasts with an earlier Faddeev calculation by Garcilazo et al. using an ESC08c-inspired effective ΞN potential, which found different NNΞ binding energies due to different NN potentials and treatment of the ΞN interaction. The results provide a first step toward answering which is the lightest Strangeness=-2 nucleus, and suggest that the NNNΞ (0,1^+) state could be produced in heavy-ion reactions at GSI and CERN LHC, decaying into d+Λ+Λ or the double Λ hypernucleus ⁴_ΛΛH.
