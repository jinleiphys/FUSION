# How AMPT generates large elliptic flow with small cross sections

> We resolve the long-standing open question of how the transport model AMPT manages to generate sufficiently high elliptic flow (v2) in A+A reactions with only few-millibarn 2->2 partonic cross sections - in apparent contradiction with an early study by Molnar and Gyulassy. Through detailed comparisons with the covariant Molnar's Parton Cascade (MPC), we pinpoint which features of initial conditions, interactions, and dynamics encoded in the partonic stage of AMPT allow it to circumvent the "opacity puzzle" at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper resolves the long-standing "opacity puzzle" by showing that AMPT generates large elliptic flow with small (3 mb) elastic parton cross sections not because of non-covariant dynamics, but primarily due to two features of its string-melting initial conditions: a high initial parton rapidity density (about 2.5 times higher than earlier studies) and a low initial effective temperature (about 0.23 GeV, three times lower than the 0.7 GeV used in the earlier Molnar and Gyulassy study), which together increase the transport opacity.

## Method

The author performed detailed comparisons between AMPT version 2.26t5 and the covariant Molnar's Parton Cascade (MPC) code version 1.9 for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 8 fm. The study systematically modified MPC simulations to become progressively more "AMPT-like" by adjusting the transverse profile from binary collision to wounded nucleon, increasing the rapidity density to match AMPT's dN/dy = 645, and lowering the initial temperature to T0 = 0.23 GeV to match AMPT's average quark pT. The author also tested covariance by running MPC with different parton subdivision factors (ell = 1, 5, 20) and by feeding AMPT's event-by-event initial conditions into MPC.

## Key numbers

- AMPT uses elastic parton-parton cross sections of sigma_qq = 3 mb (with alpha_s = 0.33 and Debye mass mu = 0.447 GeV), compared to the 40-50 mb needed in the earlier Molnar and Gyulassy study
- The initial quark rapidity density in AMPT is dN/dy = 645 at midrapidity, about 2.5 times higher than the minijet dN_g/dy = 240 used in the earlier study
- The initial effective temperature of the bulk quark distribution in AMPT is T0 = 0.23 GeV (average pT = 0.54 GeV), three times lower than the T0 = 0.7 GeV used in the earlier study
- With the low temperature T0 = 0.23 GeV, the transport cross section ratio sigma_tr/sigma_tot = 0.5 (at mu/T = 1.95), compared to sigma_tr/sigma_tot = 0.18 at the high temperature T0 = 0.7 GeV (mu/T = 0.64)
- Quark elliptic flow from AMPT plateaus at about 6 percent, while covariant MPC with the low minijet dN/dy = 240 gives only v2 = 1.5 percent
- Noncovariant artifacts from the naive cascade algorithm (no subdivision) give less than 10 percent relative error in pT-differential elliptic flow for AMPT-like conditions
- The average formation time of quarks in AMPT is about 0.22 fm

## Context

This work resolves the "opacity puzzle" posed by the discrepancy between AMPT's success with small cross sections and the earlier study by Molnar and Gyulassy (2001) which found that 40-50 mb cross sections were needed. The findings are in qualitative agreement with Lin (2014) which showed quarks in AMPT are oversaturated. The paper enables future scrutiny of AMPT's quark coalescence hadronization algorithm, which amplifies the 6 percent quark v2 to match measured hadron v2, and suggests the low-temperature, near-isotropic scattering regime in AMPT is reminiscent of the chromomagnetic monopole picture proposed by Liao and Shuryak (2006).
