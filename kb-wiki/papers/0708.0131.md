# On the Density Dependent Nuclear Matter Compressibility

> In the present work we apply a quantum hadrodynamic effective model in the mean-field approximation to the description of neutron stars. We consider an adjustable derivative-coupling model and study the parameter influence on the dynamics of the system by analyzing the full range of values they can take. We establish a set of parameters which define a specific model that is able to describe phenomenological properties such as the effective nucleon mass at saturation as well as global static properties of neutron stars (mass and radius). If one uses observational data to fix the maximum mass for neutron stars by a specific model, we are able to predict the compression modulus of nuclear matter K = 257,2MeV.

# On the Density Dependent Nuclear Matter Compressibility

## Key claim

The paper claims that by using observational data to fix the maximum mass of neutron stars within a specific quantum hadrodynamic model, one can predict the compression modulus of nuclear matter as K = 257.2 MeV, but this result depends on a particular choice of model parameters (λ = 0.06, β = 0.00, γ = -1.00) and the assumption that the largest observed neutron star masses may contain exotic matter rather than purely hadronic matter.

## Method

The authors apply a quantum hadrodynamic effective model in the mean-field approximation, including the full baryon octet (proton, neutron, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) and leptons (electron and muon), with adjustable derivative couplings parameterized by λ, β, and γ that modify the sigma, omega, and rho meson coupling strengths. They vary these parameters across three coupling schemes, solve the chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions at each density, compute the equation of state, and then integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations for comparison with observational data.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of nuclear matter: K = 257.2 MeV, predicted for the best-fit model with parameters λ = 0.06, β = 0.00, γ = -1.00
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: 0.7 M_N (where M_N = 939 MeV is the bare nucleon mass) for the best-fit model
- Maximum neutron star mass for the best-fit model: 1.48 solar masses (M_⊙)
- Binding energy at saturation: -16 MeV, assumed at saturation density of 0.17 fm⁻³
- Asymmetry coefficient: 32.5 MeV
- Acceptable parameter range for coupling scheme 1 (λ varies, β = γ = 0): 0.06 < λ < 0.19
- Acceptable parameter range for coupling scheme 3 (all vary independently): 0.00 < λ < 20.22 and 0.00 < β < 1.35
- Maximum mass for the Walecka model (λ = β = γ = 0): 1.9 M_⊙, with effective mass M* ∼ 0.5 M and compression modulus K ∼ 500 MeV
- Maximum mass range for coupling scheme 1 with acceptable phenomenology: 1.46 to 1.22 M_⊙
- Neutron star radii from the model: between 10 km and 14 km
- Largest observed pulsar mass cited: 2.1 ± 0.2 M_⊙
- A change in binding energy of 0.25 MeV implies a decrease by 0.5 solar masses for the maximum mass

## Context

This work extends the Walecka model (1974) and its derivatives (the ZM1 model, ZM3 model, and exponential model) by introducing a generalized derivative-coupling scheme with three adjustable parameters that can recover these earlier models as special cases. The paper builds on prior work by Dexheimer, Vasconcellos, and Bodmann (2006) and uses SU(6) flavor spin symmetry to fix hyperon-meson couplings. The result enables future calculations of neutron star properties including radius, redshift, maximum rotational frequency, moment of inertia, and cooling properties, while the predicted compression modulus of 257.2 MeV falls within the range of 200-300 MeV from other analyses.
