# Superfluid Response and the Neutrino Emissivity of Neutron Matter

> {We calculate the neutrino emissivity of superfluid neutron matter in the inner crust of neutron stars. We find that neutrino emission due to fluctuations resulting from the formation of Cooper pairs at finite temperature is highly suppressed in non-relativistic systems. This suppression of the pair breaking emissivity in a simplified model of neutron matter with interactions that conserve spin is of the order of $v_F^4$ for density fluctuations and $v_F^2$ for spin fluctuations, where $v_F$ is the Fermi velocity of neutrons. The larger suppression of density fluctuations arises because the dipole moment of the density distribution of a single component system does not vary in time. For this reason, we find that the axial current response (spin fluctuations) dominates. In more realistic models of neutron matter which include tensor interactions where the neutron spin is not conserved, neutrino radiation from bremsstrahlung reactions occurs at order $v_F^0$. Consequently, even with the suppression factors due to superfluidity, this rate dominates near $T_C$. Present calculations of the pair-breaking emissivity are incomplete because they neglect the tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

# Superfluid Response and the Neutrino Emissivity of Neutron Matter

## Key claim

The pair-breaking and formation (PBF) neutrino emissivity in superfluid neutron matter is highly suppressed in the vector channel (by order v_F^4) due to current conservation and the absence of time-varying dipole moment in a single-component system, but the axial current response dominates at order v_F^2; however, in realistic models with tensor interactions where spin is not conserved, neutron-neutron bremsstrahlung occurs at order v_F^0 and dominates the PBF rate for temperatures T >= T_C/5, making all current PBF calculations incomplete because they neglect the tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors calculated the neutrino emissivity from superfluid neutron matter using the relation between emissivity and the imaginary parts of the density-density (vector) and spin-density (axial) response functions in the non-relativistic limit. They employed a simplified model Hamiltonian with a zero-range four-fermion interaction and solved the vertex equation in the random-phase approximation (RPA) to restore current conservation, comparing mean-field, approximate vertex, and full RPA results for the vector response. For the axial response, they used the one-loop mean-field polarization tensor and compared the resulting emissivities with the bremsstrahlung rate from the literature, including superfluid suppression factors from Yakovlev (1999).

## Key numbers

- The suppression factor for the pair-breaking emissivity in the simplified model is of order v_F^4 for density fluctuations and v_F^2 for spin fluctuations, where v_F is the Fermi velocity of neutrons.
- Numerical results were displayed at k_F^2/(2m) = mu = 30 MeV with a momentum cutoff Lambda = 2 k_F and a superfluid gap Delta = 1 MeV.
- The pairing gap in the neutron star crust rises from zero at neutron drip density (4 x 10^12 g/cm^3) to a maximum of about 1 MeV when the neutron Fermi momentum k_F ~ 200 MeV.
- For temperatures of relevance to accreting neutron stars (T ~ 5 x 10^8 K at a column depth of about 10^12 g/cm^2), the PBF process was previously found to cool the crust below temperatures required for carbon ignition.
- The bremsstrahlung rate dominates the PBF rate for T >= T_C/5, where T_C is the critical temperature for superfluidity.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Leinson and Perez (2006), who found that earlier PBF calculations violated vector-current conservation and obtained a suppression factor v_F^4, and Sedrakian, Muther, and Schuck (2006), who found a suppression factor ~ T/M using Landau Fermi liquid theory. The authors show that the v_F^4 suppression is not generic but specific to single-component systems, and that in multicomponent systems (such as the neutron star crust where neutrons coexist with a lattice of neutron-rich ions) the density fluctuations occur at order v_F^2. The paper enables future work incorporating tensor interactions (e.g., pion exchange) into the axial response of the superfluid, which the authors identify as the missing key aspect in all current PBF calculations.
