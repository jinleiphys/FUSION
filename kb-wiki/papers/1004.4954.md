# Cluster structures of excited states in $^{14}$C

> Structures of excited states in $^{14}$C are investigated with a method of $β$-$γ$ constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics in combination with the generator coordinate method. Various excited states with the developed $3α$-cluster core structures are suggested in positive- and negative-parity states. In the positive-parity states, triaxial deformed and linear-chain structures are found to construct excited bands. Interestingly, $^{10}$Be+$α$ correlation is found in the cluster states above the $^{10}$Be+$α$ threshold energy.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that excited states of $^{14}$C exhibit developed three-alpha cluster core structures, including triaxial deformed and linear-chain configurations in positive-parity states, but the authors qualify that these are theoretical predictions from a model without assuming clusters a priori, and the linear-chain structure is found to be less favored in negative-parity states than in positive-parity states.

## Method

The authors applied the beta-gamma constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to investigate the ground and excited states of $^{14}$C. They performed variational calculations with constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameters beta and gamma at 196 mesh points on the beta-gamma plane, then superposed the parity and angular-momentum projected AMD wave functions using the Hill-Wheeler equation. The Hamiltonian used the Volkov No. 2 central force and the G3RS spin-orbit interaction with parameters adjusted to reproduce alpha-alpha and alpha-n phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The minimum point of the positive-parity energy surface before angular-momentum projection is at (beta cos gamma, beta sin gamma) = (0.00, 0.00), indicating a spherical shape; after projection to 0+ states, the minimum shifts to (0.23, 0.04), indicating a triaxial shape.
- The minimum point of the negative-parity energy surface is at (0.08, 0.04), and after projection to 3- states, the minimum is at (0.20, 0.09).
- A flat region in the positive-parity energy surface exists around (beta cos gamma, beta sin gamma) = (0.80-0.90, 0.00), and after projection a local minimum emerges at (0.90, 0.04).
- The calculated B(E2) strength for the 2+1 to 0+1 transition is 4.4 e2fm4, compared to the experimental value of 3.6 +/- 0.6 e2fm4.
- The calculated root-mean-square radius for the 0+1 ground state is 2.22 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.30 +/- 0.07 fm.
- The expectation value of squared intrinsic proton spin for the triaxial deformed state at (0.45, 0.17) is 0.15, while for the linear-chain state at (0.93, 0.04) it is less than 0.10.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of cluster structures in $^{12}$C by Horiuchi (1974), Uegaki (1977), and others, as well as earlier cluster model calculations for $^{14}$C by Itagaki et al. (2004) who predicted equilateral-triangular 3-alpha structures forming K-pi=0+ and K-pi=3- rotational bands. The present study uses a model-free AMD approach rather than assuming clusters, and finds that excess neutrons in $^{14}$C occupy sd-like orbitals around two of the three alpha clusters, indicating $^{10}$Be+alpha correlations above the $^{10}$Be+alpha threshold energy. This enables future experimental searches for the predicted linear-chain and triaxial deformed bands in $^{14}$C.
