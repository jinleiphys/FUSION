# On the physics of massive neutrinos

> Massive neutrinos open up the possibility for a variety of new physical phenomena. Among them are oscillations and double beta decay. Furthermore they influence several fields from particle physics to cosmology. In this article the concept of massive neutrinos is given and the current status of experimental research is extensively reviewed. This includes astrophysical studies of solar, supernova and very high energy neutrinos. Future perspectives are also outlined.

# On the physics of massive neutrinos

## Key claim

Massive neutrinos would enable a variety of new physical phenomena including oscillations and double beta decay, but the review makes clear that while several Grand Unified Theories predict neutrino masses, it remains an open question whether neutrinos are Dirac or Majorana particles, and the experimental status at the time of writing had not yet definitively established a non-zero mass for any neutrino flavor.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for neutrino mass generation, including the see-saw mechanism and extensions to the Standard Model such as Higgs triplets, the Zee model, and SO(10) Grand Unified Theories. It then surveys experimental searches for neutrino masses through kinematical tests (tritium beta decay, pion decay, tau decay) and through neutrinoless double beta decay experiments using semiconductor detectors (Heidelberg-Moscow, IGEX), scintillators, cryogenic bolometers, time projection chambers, and geochemical methods.

## Key numbers

- LEP measurement of Z0-width yields 2.993 ± 0.011 neutrino flavors for masses below 45 GeV
- Tritium beta decay endpoint energy is about 18.6 keV, with only 10⁻⁹ of all electrons in a 20 eV region below the endpoint
- Muon neutrino mass limit from pion decay at PSI: m(νμ) < 170 keV (90% CL)
- Tau neutrino mass limit from ALEPH: m(ντ) < 18.2 MeV (95% CL)
- Electron neutrino mass limit from internal bremsstrahlung in ¹⁶³Ho electron capture: m(νe) < 225 eV (95% CL)
- Heidelberg-Moscow experiment using 11 kg of enriched germanium (86% ⁷⁶Ge) achieved background of 0.2 counts/year/kg/keV at the peak position
- Heidelberg-Moscow limit on neutrinoless double beta decay half-life: T₁/₂ > 1.1 × 10²⁵ years, corresponding to effective Majorana mass ⟨mν⟩ < 0.47 eV (90% CL)
- Radiochemical detection of ²³⁸U double beta decay with half-life of 2.0 ± 0.6 × 10²¹ years
- Higgs triplet vacuum expectation value ratio constraint: v₃/v₂ < 0.07 from gauge boson mass relation

## Context

This review extends the theoretical and experimental status summarized in earlier works by Boehm and Vogel (1992) and by Mohapatra (1991) on neutrino mass generation in Grand Unified Theories. The paper covers the period up to 1998, including results from the Mainz and Troitzk tritium beta decay experiments, the Heidelberg-Moscow germanium double beta decay experiment, and tau neutrino mass limits from LEP collaborations (ALEPH, DELPHI, OPAL). The review enables future searches for neutrino mass by documenting the experimental sensitivities achieved and the remaining challenges, particularly the need for improved statistics in tau decay measurements and the importance of understanding systematic effects in tritium beta decay that produced negative mν² fit values.
