# The two-photon exchange contribution to elastic electron-nucleon scattering at large momentum transfer

> We estimate the two-photon exchange contribution to elastic electron-proton scattering at large momentum transfer by using a quark-parton representation of virtual Compton scattering. We thus can relate the two-photon exchange amplitude to the generalized parton distributions which also enter in other wide angle scattering processes. We find that the interference of one- and two-photon exchange contribution is able to substantially resolve the difference between electric form factor measurements from Rosenbluth and polarization transfer experiments. Two-photon exchange has additional consequences which could be experimentally observed, including nonzero polarization effects and a positron-proton/electron-proton scattering asymmetry. The predicted Rosenbluth plot is no longer precisely linear; it acquires a measurable curvature, particularly at large laboratory angle.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange contribution, estimated using a quark-parton representation of virtual Compton scattering and related to generalized parton distributions (GPDs), can substantially resolve the discrepancy between proton electric form factor measurements from Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer experiments, though the calculation relies on the handbag approximation which neglects "cat's ears" diagrams where the two photons couple to different quarks.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-photon exchange amplitude for elastic electron-proton scattering at large momentum transfer by first computing the hard scattering amplitude for electron-quark scattering via direct and crossed box diagrams, then embedding this partonic amplitude within the nucleon using the handbag approximation, where both photons interact with the same quark. The resulting amplitude was expressed as a convolution of the hard scattering with generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon, using the symmetric frame with $q^+ = 0$ to define parton light-front momentum fractions. The soft (infrared-divergent) part of the two-photon exchange was combined with electron-proton bremsstrahlung contributions to obtain an infrared-finite result, while the hard part was evaluated for kinematics where $s$, $-u$, and $Q^2$ are large compared to the hadronic scale.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange correction to the reduced cross section $\sigma_R$ is given by $2 G_M \mathcal{R}(\delta \tilde G_M + \varepsilon \frac{\nu}{M^2} \tilde F_3) + 2 \frac{\varepsilon}{\tau} G_E \mathcal{R}(\delta \tilde G_E + \frac{\nu}{M^2} \tilde F_3)$, where the correction terms are of order $e^2$ relative to the one-photon exchange amplitude.
- The soft part of the two-photon exchange contribution $\delta_{2\gamma, soft}$ contains a $\pi^2/2$ term, and the difference between this soft contribution plus bremsstrahlung and the standard Mo and Tsai (1968) radiative corrections was found to be below $10^{-3}$ for all kinematics considered.
- The normal spin asymmetry $A_n$ at the quark level is given by $A_n = \frac{e_q e^2}{4\pi} \frac{m_q}{2Q} \frac{\sqrt{2\varepsilon(1+\varepsilon)}}{1+4\varepsilon m_q^2/Q^2} \frac{Q^2(Q^2+4m_q^2)}{\hat s(\hat s - \hat u)}$, which is infrared finite.
- The partonic Mandelstam variables for the quark-level scattering are $\hat s = \frac{(x+\eta)^2}{4 x \eta} Q^2$ and $\hat u = -\frac{(x-\eta)^2}{4 x \eta} Q^2$, where $x$ is the parton light-front momentum fraction and $\eta = k^+/P^+$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2003) which considered only the elastic nucleon intermediate state for two-photon exchange, by using generalized parton distributions to effectively sum all possible inelastic nucleon intermediate states. The handbag approximation used here has been phenomenologically successful in describing wide-angle real Compton scattering at moderate energies and momentum transfers, as shown by Brooks and Dixon and by Vanderhaeghen et al. (2000). The results enable quantitative predictions for several experimentally observable consequences of two-photon exchange beyond the form factor discrepancy, including nonzero single-spin asymmetries, a positron-proton versus electron-proton scattering asymmetry, and measurable curvature in Rosenbluth plots at large laboratory angles.
