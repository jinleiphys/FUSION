# Comment on the manuscript 1806.02080v1 entitled "Spurious finite-size instabilities of a new Gogny interaction suitable for astrophysical applications"

> The conclusions of the manuscript 1806.02080v1 questioning the adequacy of the recently proposed Gogny D1M* interaction for finite nuclei calculations using harmonic oscillator (HO) basis are revised. Several convergence and stability studies are performed with HO basis of different sizes and oscillator parameters and the results show the robustness of the D1M* results for finite nuclei. This analysis is also extended to beyond mean-field calculations of generator-coordinate-method type with D1M*. On the other hand, the existence of a finite-size instability in finite nuclei when coordinate space methods are used to solve the HF equations (as shown in 1806.02080v1) is independently confirmed for D1M* using an in-house computer code based on a quasilocal approximation to the HF exchange potential. We confirm that the most affected quantity in the coordinate space calculation is the spatial density at the origin, but integrated quantities like binding energies or radii show a plateau against the number of iterations, where they are consistent with the values from the HO basis calculation, before diverging for a larger number of iterations. A connection between the last occupied s-orbital in the nucleus and the appearance of instabilities in coordinate space is observed.

## Key claim
The authors argue that the D1M* Gogny interaction is robust for finite-nuclei calculations when a harmonic oscillator (HO) basis is used, because the HO basis acts as a natural ultraviolet cutoff that regulates finite-size instabilities; however, they independently confirm that the same instabilities do appear in coordinate-space mesh calculations, where the central density is most affected but integrated quantities like binding energies and radii show a plateau before diverging.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations for spherical doubly magic nuclei using both a harmonic oscillator basis (with up to 20 major shells) and a coordinate-space mesh employing a quasilocal approximation to the HF exchange potential derived from the extended Thomas-Fermi expansion of the density matrix. They compared binding energies, neutron and proton densities, and rms radii for D1M* and D1M across multiple nuclei, and extended the analysis to beyond-mean-field Generator-Coordinate-Method (GCM) calculations for neutron-rich Ra and Th isotopes using the octupole moment as the collective coordinate.

## Key numbers
- D1M* HF binding energy for \(^{208}\)Pb: 1636.73 MeV (HO basis) vs. 1637.83 MeV (quasilocal approximation at 100 iterations) and 1638.18 MeV (at 150 iterations)
- D1M* HF binding energy for \(^{40}\)Ca: 342.55 MeV (HO basis) vs. 341.33 MeV (quasilocal approximation at 100 iterations) and 341.35 MeV (at 150 iterations)
- D1M* HF binding energy for \(^{16}\)O: 128.32 MeV (HO basis) vs. 127.29 MeV (quasilocal approximation at 100 and 150 iterations)
- D1M HF binding energy for \(^{208}\)Pb: 1636.08 MeV (HO basis) vs. 1637.96 MeV (quasilocal approximation)
- D1M HF binding energy for \(^{40}\)Ca: 341.67 MeV (HO basis) vs. 340.53 MeV (quasilocal approximation)
- The critical density for instability is \(\rho_c \simeq 0.20\) fm\(^{-3}\) at a momentum transfer of about 2.5 fm\(^{-1}\), as previously reported by Martini (2018) and De Pace (2016)
- For \(^{40}\)Ca, the energy gap between the 2\(s_{1/2}\) level and the Fermi level is \(\sim\)1.5 MeV; for \(^{56}\)Ni it is \(\sim\)7 MeV; for \(^{16}\)O it is \(\sim\)20 MeV; for \(^{100}\)Sn it is \(\sim\)16 MeV
- In \(^{176}\)Sn, the proton 2\(s_{1/2}\) level is at \(\sim\)12 MeV from the Fermi level and the neutron 3\(s_{1/2}\) level is at \(\sim\)10 MeV from the last occupied orbit
- The rms radii for \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{40}\)Ca show less than 0.5% variation in the range of iterations where the energy is quasi-stable

## Context
This comment responds to the manuscript by Martini (2018) which claimed that the D1M* Gogny interaction suffers from spurious finite-size instabilities in the S=0, T=1 channel, detected through fully antisymmetrized RPA calculations of nuclear matter response functions (De Pace, 2016). The present work extends the analysis of Gonzalez-Boquera (2018) by demonstrating that HO basis calculations remain robust for D1M* across a wide range of nuclei and basis sizes, and that the instabilities seen in coordinate-space mesh calculations (using the FINRES\(_4\) code of Bennaceur, 2018) are nucleus-dependent and connected to the occupation of s-orbitals near the Fermi level. The results enable continued use of D1M* for finite-nuclei and beyond-mean-field calculations (such as GCM) within the HO basis framework, while confirming that coordinate-space methods require caution for this interaction.
