# Hydrodynamics formalism with Spin dynamics

> We review the key steps of the relativistic fluid dynamics formalism with spin degrees of freedom initiated recently. We obtain equations of motion of the expansion of the system from the underlying definitions of quantum kinetic theory for the equilibrium phase space distribution functions. We investigate the dynamics of spin polarization of the system in the Bjorken hydrodynamical background.

## Key claim

This paper reviews a relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics formalism that includes spin degrees of freedom, and shows that within a Bjorken flow background the spin polarization tensor components evolve independently of each other. The abstract's claim of "investigating the dynamics of spin polarization" is hedged by the explicit statement that the Bjorken set-up is too simple to address experimental measurements, and that the longitudinal component of the mean polarization vector vanishes in this framework, which disagrees with the quadrupole structure seen in experiment.

## Method

The author derives equations of motion for the baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor from quantum kinetic theory, using equilibrium phase-space distribution functions for spin-1/2 massive particles based on the de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) formalism. The spin polarization tensor is decomposed into electric-like and magnetic-like components, and the conservation laws are projected onto basis vectors appropriate for a longitudinally boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous Bjorken expansion. The resulting six coupled differential equations for the scalar functions describing spin polarization are solved numerically alongside the conservation equations for charge and energy-momentum.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic hydrodynamics with spin framework initiated by Florkowski et al. (2017-2019) and builds on the equilibrium distribution function approach of Becattini et al. (2013). It contrasts with thermal-based models (Becattini 2016, Karpenko 2016, Li 2017, Xie 2017) that assume spin polarization is determined solely by thermal vorticity at freeze-out, without considering its independent evolution during fluid expansion. The results enable future studies that go beyond the Bjorken symmetry to address the quadrupole structure of longitudinal spin polarization observed by the STAR Collaboration (2017-2019).
