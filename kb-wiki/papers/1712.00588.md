# Development of heavy-flavour flow-harmonics in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We employ the POWLANG transport setup, developed over the last few years, to provide new predictions for several heavy-flavour observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to LHC center-of-mass energies. In particular, we focus on the development of the flow-harmonics $v_2$ and $v_3$ arising from the initial geometric asymmetry in the initial conditions and its associated event-by-event fluctuations. Within the same transport framework, for the sake of consistency, we also compare the nuclear modification factor of the $p_T$ spectra of charm and beauty quarks, heavy hadrons and their decay electrons. We compare our findings to the most recent data from the experimental collaborations. We also study in detail the contribution to the flow harmonics from the quarks decoupling from the fireball during the various stages of its evolution: although not directly accessible to the experiments, this information can shed light on the major sources of the final measured effect.

## Key claim

The paper provides new predictions for heavy-flavour flow harmonics (v₂ and v₃) using the POWLANG transport setup, but the final flow signal arises from a non-trivial interplay of opposite-sign contributions from quarks decoupling at different stages of the fireball evolution, rather than being dominated by early-escaping particles as suggested in some prior studies.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic Langevin equation to model heavy-quark propagation through a hydrodynamically expanding background medium, using either weak-coupling Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed transport coefficients or non-perturbative lattice-QCD coefficients. The initial heavy-quark production is generated via a pQCD event-generator, while the background medium is initialized with Glauber-model entropy density profiles (both optical and Monte Carlo implementations) and evolved using the ECHO-QGP code in 2+1 dimensions with longitudinal boost-invariance. For triangular flow studies, event-by-event fluctuations in nucleon positions are used to generate anisotropic initial conditions, with events rotated to align the participant-plane angles ψ₂ or ψ₃ along the x-axis before averaging.

## Key numbers

- Initialization time τ₀ = 1 fm/c at √s_NN = 200 GeV and τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- Entropy density normalization s₀ = 84 fm⁻³ at √s_NN = 200 GeV and s₀ = 400 fm⁻³ at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for optical-Glauber initialization
- For Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, Kτ₀ = 6.37 with τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c
- Gaussian smearing width σ = 0.2 fm for entropy density from each nucleon-nucleon collision
- Approximately 6000 Pb-Pb collisions generated for Monte Carlo initial conditions
- Decoupling temperature T_dec = 155 MeV assumed for heavy quarks
- About 10% of charm quarks spend less than 4 fm/c in the medium; half escape after τ ≳ 7 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous POWLANG studies (Beraudo 2014, Beraudo 2015) from nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow to include triangular flow predictions for heavy-flavour particles. It contrasts with the findings of He (2015) who argued that anisotropic escape probability dominates flow signals in a pure transport setup with mild cross-sections, questioning the strongly-interacting medium interpretation. The paper enables future event-by-event studies selecting collisions with different initial eccentricities within the same centrality class to further constrain heavy-quark coupling to the medium.
