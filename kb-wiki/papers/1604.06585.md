# Exploring universality of transversity in proton-proton collisions

> We consider the azimuthal correlations of charged hadron pairs with large total transverse momentum and small relative momentum, produced in proton-proton collisions with one transversely polarized proton. One of these correlations directly probes the chiral-odd transversity parton distribution in connection with a chiral-odd interference fragmentation function. We present predictions for this observable based on previous extractions of transversity (from charged pion pair production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering) and of the interference fragmentation function (from the production of back-to-back charged pion pairs in electron-positron annihilations). All analyses are performed in the framework of collinear factorization. We compare our predictions to the recent data on proton-proton collisions released by the STAR collaboration at RHIC, and we find them reasonably compatible. This comparison confirms for the first time the predicted role of transversity in proton-proton collisions and it allows to test its universality.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that the transversity parton distribution function extracted from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data, combined with the interference fragmentation function from electron-positron annihilation data, can predict the transverse spin asymmetry observed in proton-proton collisions at RHIC, thereby testing the universality of transversity. However, the agreement is qualified: while overall reasonable, some discrepancies appear in forward kinematics, and the predictions include only valence quarks because those are the only components extractable from current fixed-target SIDIS data.

## Method

The authors compute the transverse spin asymmetry A_UT for the process p + p^uparrow -> (pi+ pi-) + X at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 200 GeV using collinear factorization. They use previously extracted parametrizations of the transversity distribution h_1 (from SIDIS data on transversely polarized proton and deuteron targets by HERMES and COMPASS) and the interference fragmentation function H_1^sphericalangle (from BELLE e+e- data), evolving each replica via DGLAP evolution at leading order to the STAR momentum scales. The theoretical predictions are integrated over experimental bin widths and compared to STAR data for the asymmetry as a function of invariant mass M_h and pseudorapidity eta.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy of proton-proton collisions: sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Transverse momentum range of the hadron pair: 3 <= |P_T| <= 13 GeV/c
- Pseudorapidity range for forward kinematics: -1.4 <= eta <= 0
- Invariant mass range of the pion pair: 0.3 <= M_h <= 1.2 GeV
- QCD evolution starting scale: Q_0^2 = 1 GeV^2
- Value of strong coupling constant used: alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.139
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for replicas that closely reproduce forward data: replica "6" = 2.04, replica "31" = 1.52, replica "43" = 2.02

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach for di-hadron production, previously applied to SIDIS (Bacchetta et al., 2011, 2012; Radici et al., 2015) and e+e- annihilation (Courtoy et al., 2012), to proton-proton collisions, where TMD factorization is known to break down for single hadron production (Rogers, 2010). The comparison with STAR data (Adamczyk et al., 2015) enables a test of transversity universality across different hard processes and opens the possibility of using hadronic collision data in future global fits of transversity, particularly as more STAR data from runs at sqrt(s) = 200 and 500 GeV become available.
