# Is there a flavor hierarchy in the deconfinement transition of QCD?

> We present possible indications for flavor separation during the QCD crossover transition based on continuum extrapolated lattice QCD calculations of higher order susceptibilities. We base our findings on flavor specific quantities in the light and strange quark sector. We propose a possible experimental verification of our prediction, based on the measurement of higher order moments of identified particle multiplicities. Since all our calculations are performed at zero baryochemical potential, these results are of particular relevance for the heavy ion program at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence from continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD simulations that the characteristic temperatures for the deconfinement transition differ between light and strange quarks by approximately 15 MeV, with strange quarks deconfining at a higher temperature; this flavor hierarchy is proposed as a prediction that can be tested experimentally at the LHC through measurements of higher-order moments of identified particle multiplicities.

## Method

The Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration performed lattice QCD simulations using a tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action and a staggered fermionic action with 2-level stout improvement, with physical quark masses and the physical strange-to-light quark mass ratio (m_s/m_ud ≈ 28). Continuum extrapolations were carried out at each temperature using four lattice spacings corresponding to N_t = 6, 8, 10, 12 (lattice spacings a = 0.22, 0.16, 0.13, and 0.11 fm at T_c). The authors computed second- and fourth-order diagonal and off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities and their ratios at zero baryochemical potential, and compared these to Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model predictions.

## Key numbers

- The inflection point of the light quark susceptibility χ_2^L occurs at 150 MeV, while that of the strange quark susceptibility χ_2^s occurs at 165 MeV, giving a difference of approximately 15 MeV.
- A rescaling transformation between the light and strange quark susceptibilities yields a preferred rescaling factor x = 1.11, corresponding to a temperature difference of (x-1)·T_c ≈ 15 MeV.
- The characteristic temperature for the onset of strange quark liberation, based on the v_1^f and v_2^f observables, is T ≳ 157 MeV.
- First experimental results from ALICE indicate that the chemical freeze-out temperature of strange hadrons is about 16 MeV higher than that of light hadrons (164 vs 148 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the 2006 finding by the Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration that the characteristic temperature in the strange sector is about 20 MeV higher than in the light quark sector (Aoki 2006), and builds on the collaboration's earlier high-precision determination of χ_2^u and χ_2^s (Borsanyi 2011). The paper generalizes susceptibility combinations v_1 and v_2 proposed by Bazavov (2013) to any flavor, and introduces a new combination w^f that shows the strongest flavor separation. The results enable experimental verification at LHC and RHIC through measurements of higher-order moments of identified particle multiplicities, as proposed by Karsch (2012) and others, and are directly relevant to the observed strangeness enhancement and hypernuclei formation at RHIC and LHC.
