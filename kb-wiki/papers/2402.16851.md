# Medium-induced photon bremsstrahlung in neutrino-nucleus, antineutrino-nucleus, and electron-nucleus scattering from multiple QED interactions

> Interactions of charged leptons with nuclei and the naive tree-level kinematics of these processes are affected by radiation of photons induced by the QED nuclear medium. We evaluate cross-section modifications at leading orders of the number of correlated interactions inside the nucleus, known as the opacity expansion. We derive results for soft and collinear types of the bremsstrahlung at the first three orders in opacity and generalize them to higher orders. We present the leading in opacity energy spectra of soft and collinear photons and radiative energy loss inside the nucleus for experiments with lepton kinematics in the GeV energy range. At leading power of the Glauber soft-collinear effective field theory, the soft radiation is further resummed to all orders both in opacity and in the electromagnetic coupling constant. We find that the soft and collinear medium-induced radiation is vacuumlike, and additional corrections are power suppressed. Despite the negligible modification to the induced photon spectra, the nuclear medium-induced radiation sizably affects the broadening of charged leptons in the direction orthogonal to their propagation.

## Key claim

The authors find that soft and collinear medium-induced photon radiation in neutrino-nucleus, antineutrino-nucleus, and electron-nucleus scattering is vacuumlike, with additional corrections from the QED nuclear medium being power suppressed; despite negligible modification to the induced photon spectra, the nuclear medium-induced radiation sizably affects the broadening of charged leptons in the direction orthogonal to their propagation.

## Method

The authors evaluate cross-section modifications at leading orders of the number of correlated interactions inside the nucleus using the opacity expansion, deriving results for soft and collinear bremsstrahlung at the first three orders in opacity and generalizing to higher orders. At leading power of the Glauber soft-collinear effective field theory (SCET_G), the soft radiation is resummed to all orders both in opacity and in the electromagnetic coupling constant. The collinear radiation is evaluated using the electron-photon splitting function analogous to the Altarelli-Parisi splitting, with the collinear divergence regularized by the nonzero charged lepton mass.

## Key numbers

- Soft-photon corrections suppress cross sections at the 10%-30% level for electrons of GeV energies, shown for an incoming beam energy of 2 GeV and soft-photon energy cutoff of 10 MeV as a function of squared momentum transfer.
- The soft-photon energy cutoff used in numerical estimates is ΔE = 10 MeV.
- The incoming beam energy used for numerical estimates is E_beam = 2 GeV.
- The atomic screening scale is given by ζ ≈ (m_e Z^(1/3))/192, where Z is the nuclear charge and m_e is the electron mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous evaluation of cross-section modifications for elastic scattering off nucleons inside a large nucleus due to photon exchange with the nuclear medium (Tomalak 2022), where they found permille-level effects in (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering and up to percent-level corrections in electron-nucleus scattering in forward kinematics. The authors subsequently included multiple rescattering of charged leptons within the effective field theory approach (Tomalak 2023) and found sizable QED nuclear medium-induced redistribution of charged particle tracks in the transverse direction. This work takes the next step by evaluating soft and collinear bremsstrahlung induced by lepton interactions with the nuclear medium, and the results differ both qualitatively and quantitatively from the QCD case of medium-induced gluon radiation.
