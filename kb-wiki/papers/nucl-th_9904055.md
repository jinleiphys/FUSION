# Effect of nucleon structure variation on the longitudinal response function

> Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, we study the longitudinal response function for quasielastic electron scattering from nuclear matter. In QMC the coupling constant between the scalar ($σ$) meson and the nucleon is expected to decrease with increasing nuclear density, because of the self-consistent modification of the structure of the nucleon. The reduction of the coupling constant then leads to a smaller contribution from relativistic RPA than in the Walecka model. However, since the electromagnetic form factors of the in-medium nucleon are modified at the same time, the longitudinal response function and the Coulomb sum are reduced by a total of about 20% in comparison with the Hartree contribution. We find that the relativistic RPA and the nucleon structure variation both contribute about fifty-fifty to the reduction of the longitudinal response.

## Key claim

Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the longitudinal response function and Coulomb sum for quasielastic electron scattering from nuclear matter are reduced by about 20% compared to the Hartree contribution, with relativistic RPA correlations and nucleon structure variation each contributing roughly half of that reduction.

## Method

The authors calculate the longitudinal response function for infinite, isosymmetric nuclear matter within the QMC model, which treats nucleons as MIT bags containing confined quarks that couple to scalar (σ) and vector (ω) mesons. They incorporate three modifications to the standard Quantum Hadrodynamics (Walecka model) approach: (1) a monopole meson-nucleon vertex form factor with cutoff Λ_N = 1.5 GeV, (2) a density-dependent modification of the proton electric form factor parametrized from QMC calculations, and (3) a density-dependent σ-nucleon coupling constant g_s(ρ_B) obtained by solving the nuclear matter ground state self-consistently. The longitudinal response is computed in the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) including vacuum polarization, with the isoscalar and isovector parts combined.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, the σ-nucleon coupling constant g_s decreases by about 9% relative to its free-space value.
- The effective nucleon mass at ρ₀ is M*/M = 0.81, with nuclear matter saturating at binding energy −15.7 MeV and incompressibility K = 281 MeV (for quark mass m_q = 5 MeV).
- The in-medium proton electric form factor at ρ₀ is parametrized as R_{pE}(ρ₀, Q²) ≈ 1 − 0.26 × Q² (Q² in GeV²), corresponding to a 5.5% swelling of the proton rms radius.
- The Coulomb sum at high momentum transfer is reduced by about 20% in the full calculation compared to the Hartree result; this is smaller than the approximately 30% reduction found in QHD.
- For constituent quark mass m_q = 300 MeV, the effective nucleon mass at ρ₀ is 723 MeV and incompressibility is 345 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to various nuclear problems by Saito, Tsushima, and Thomas, to the longstanding problem of the quenched longitudinal response in quasielastic electron scattering. Earlier nonrelativistic approaches and the Walecka model (QHD) had difficulty reproducing the observed quenching; Horowitz and others (mid-1980s) showed that RRPA with vacuum polarization was important in reducing the Coulomb sum. The present calculation shows that nucleon structure variation (modification of electromagnetic form factors and density-dependent coupling) contributes comparably to RRPA correlations, and that the total reduction is about 20% rather than the 30% found in QHD. The results enable future calculations for finite nuclei using local density approximation, allowing direct comparison with experimental data from Jordan and others.
