# Meson Properties in the Quark Model: A Look at Some Outstanding Problems

> This thesis examines three problems of the quark model, split between two general areas. The general areas are: 1) models of meson decay ($^3P_0$ and flux-tube breaking), 2) final state interactions (FSI's). The three specific problems are: 1) identification of the $f_4(2220)$ state, 2) determination of the mixing angle between the $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$ mesons, 3) examination of FSI's in the reaction $γγ\rightarrow ππ$ near threshold. A more complete abstract is included in the thesis.

## Key claim
This thesis examines three specific problems in the quark model, but the abstract's claim of addressing "models of meson decay" and "final state interactions" is hedged by the fact that the full text is truncated and only appendices and introductory material are provided; the actual numerical results for the three problems (identification of the f4(2220), K1 mixing angle determination, and FSI in gamma gamma -> pi pi) are not presented in the available text.

## Method
The work uses the ^3P_0 and flux-tube breaking models of meson decay to calculate decay amplitudes, with meson wavefunctions expressed as simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) wavefunctions and as relativized quark model (RQM) wavefunctions from Godfrey and Isgur (1985). Colour, flavour, and spin overlaps for the decay amplitudes are evaluated using matrix trace methods and 9j symbols, and the amplitudes are converted to partial wave amplitudes via recoupling calculations or the Jacob-Wick formula. The mixing angle between K1(1270) and K1(1400) is treated as a rotation between the ^1P_1 and ^3P_1 basis states, with the angle determined by comparing model predictions to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Quark constituent masses used: m_u = 220 MeV, m_d = 220 MeV, m_s = 419 MeV (from the Godfrey-Isgur relativized quark model).
- For K1 meson mixing, a mixing angle of theta = 45 degrees is taken for K1(1270)^+ and K1(1400)^+ (and their neutral counterparts), with -45 degrees for the negatively charged and anti-mesons.
- The K*(1410)-K*(1680) mixing matrix is given as: K*(1410) = 1.00 * K(2^3S_1) + 0.04 * K(1^3D_1), K*(1680) = -0.04 * K(2^3S_1) + 1.00 * K(1^3D_1).
- For eta-eta' mixing, ideal mixing corresponds to theta_P = -54.7 degrees, perfect mixing to theta_P = -9.7 degrees, and data from two-photon decay suggests theta_P approximately -20 degrees.

## Context
This thesis extends the quark model work of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) on meson spectroscopy and of Kokoski and Isgur (1987) on meson decay parameters, applying the ^3P_0 and flux-tube breaking decay models to specific outstanding problems. The determination of the K1(1270)-K1(1400) mixing angle builds on the mixing formalism described in the Review of Particle Properties (Montanet 1994). The work enables improved identification of meson resonances and better understanding of final state interactions in two-photon processes, though the truncated text does not present the actual numerical results for the three specific problems.
