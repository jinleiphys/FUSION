# Hadron Physics and the Dyson--Schwinger Equations of QCD

> We use the Bethe-Salpeter equation in rainbow-ladder truncation to calculate the ground state mesons from the chiral limit to bottomonium, with an effective interaction that was previously fitted to the chiral condensate and pion decay constant. Our results are in reasonable agreement with the data, as are the vector and pseudoscalar decay constants. The meson mass differences tend to become constant in the heavy-quark limit. We also present calculations for the pion and rho electromagnetic form factors, and for the single-quark form factors of the η_c and J/ψ.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, using an effective interaction previously fitted only to the chiral condensate and pion decay constant, produces meson masses and decay constants in reasonable agreement with data from the chiral limit to bottomonium, but the mass splitting between pseudoscalar and vector mesons is too large for heavy quarkonium states, and scalar and axialvector masses are systematically too small.

## Method

The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved in rainbow-ladder truncation for ground-state mesons, using a two-parameter effective interaction that models the infrared enhancement of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel as a Gaussian plus a perturbative ultraviolet term. The light quark masses were fitted to the pion and kaon masses, while the charm and bottom quark masses were fixed by the J/ψ and Υ vector meson masses. Electromagnetic form factors were computed from the dressed quark-photon vertex obtained by solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation with the same kernel, combined with the impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 0.1385 GeV (experiment: 0.135, 0.140 GeV); pion decay constant: 0.131 GeV (experiment: 0.131 GeV)
- Rho meson mass: 0.743 GeV (experiment: 0.775 GeV); rho decay constant: 0.207 GeV (experiment: 0.221 GeV)
- J/ψ mass: 3.098 GeV (experiment: 3.097 GeV); J/ψ decay constant: 0.421 GeV (experiment: 0.416 GeV)
- Υ mass: 9.46 GeV (experiment: 9.46 GeV); Υ decay constant: 0.62 GeV (experiment: 0.715 GeV)
- η_c mass: 2.908 GeV (experiment: 2.980 GeV); η_c decay constant: 0.381 GeV (experiment: 0.335 ± 0.075 GeV)
- Pion charge radius: 0.44 fm² (experiment: 0.44 ± 0.01 fm²)
- For the pseudoscalar meson mass trajectory: C₀ = 0, C₁ = 6.94, C₂ ≈ 4.6; for vector mesons: C₀ = 0.51, C₁ = 7.27, C₂ ≈ 4.6; for scalar mesons: C₀ = 0.38, C₁ = 8.65, C₂ ≈ 4.6 (fit to M²_meson = C₀ + C₁ m_q + C₂ m_q²)
- The vector-meson pole in the dressed quark-photon vertex occurs at Q² = -0.55 GeV² for light mesons and at Q² = -9.5 GeV² for charmonium

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger approach of Maris and Tandy (1999), which was fitted to light-meson observables, to the full range from chiral quarks to bottomonium, demonstrating that the same effective interaction can describe heavy as well as light mesons. The results for light and charmonium states are consistent with those of Alkofer et al. (2002) using a slightly different model interaction. The calculations enable systematic study of electromagnetic form factors of heavy mesons and provide a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations such as those by Dudek et al. (2006), with which the static electromagnetic properties of charmonium agree well.
