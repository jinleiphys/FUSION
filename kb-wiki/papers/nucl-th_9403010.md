# Quark-Gluon-Plasma Formation at SPS Energies?

> By colliding ultrarelativistic ions, one achieves presently energy densities close to the critical value, concerning the formation of a quark-gluon-plasma. This indicates the importance of fluctuations and the necessity to go beyond the investigation of average events. Therefore, we introduce a percolation approach to model the final stage ($τ> 1$ fm/c) of ion-ion collisions, the initial stage being treated by well-established methods, based on strings and Pomerons. The percolation approach amounts to finding high density domains, and treating them as quark-matter droplets. In this way, we have a {\bf realistic, microscopic, and Monte--Carlo based model which allows for the formation of quark matter.} We find that even at SPS energies large quark-matter droplets are formed -- at a low rate though. In other words: large quark-matter droplets are formed due to geometrical fluctuation, but not in the average event.

## Key claim

Even at SPS energies, large quark-matter droplets can be formed due to geometrical fluctuations, but only at a low rate and not in the average event; the critical energy density at SPS is close enough to the percolation threshold that fluctuations become crucial.

## Method

The author introduces a two-stage percolation approach: the initial stage (up to about 1 fm/c) is treated with the VENUS string model based on Pomeron exchange and string fragmentation, while the final stage uses a percolation model to identify high-energy-density domains as quark-matter droplets. At a fixed proper time, particles are assigned a critical volume V0 (or equivalently a critical energy density epsilon0), and overlapping volumes in (s, r_x, r_y) space define quark-matter clusters, which then undergo longitudinal expansion and eventual hadronization.

## Key numbers

- For central S+S collisions at 200 GeV, the maximum possible cluster volume at tau = 2 fm/c is V_max approximately 600 fm^3.
- For epsilon0 = 1 GeV/fm^3, the cluster volume distribution peaks at small values and drops very fast; with 1200 simulations, clusters up to 65 fm^3 are observed.
- Reducing epsilon0 to 0.50 GeV/fm^3 widens the distribution by roughly a factor of two compared to the 1 GeV/fm^3 case.
- At epsilon0 = 0.15 GeV/fm^3 (approximately nuclear matter density), the distribution develops a maximum at large volumes, with the tail almost reaching V_max = 600 fm^3.
- The best current estimate for the realistic critical energy density is epsilon0* = 1 GeV/fm^3, based on comparisons with proton, pion, and strange particle yields for S+S and S+Ag scattering.

## Context

This work extends the VENUS string model (Werner, 1993) by adding a percolation stage to model the formation of quark-gluon plasma droplets, going beyond average-event analyses that are insufficient near the critical energy density. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as hadronic cascades (Ranft, 1993; Toneev, 1993; Sorge, 1993) and string fusion (Andersson, 1993; Pajares, 1993; Sorge, 1992), and is presented as the first realistic, microscopic, Monte-Carlo-based model allowing quark matter formation. The approach enables future event-by-event searches for "miniplasmas" and studies of particle correlations to determine source radii, which could further constrain the critical energy density.
