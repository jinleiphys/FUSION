# Radiative Correction to Lepton Proton Scatterings in Manifestly Lorentz-Invariant Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory is used to calculate the radiative correction of low energy elastic lepton proton scatterings. Corrections of differential cross section and charge asymmetry are given at chiral next-to-leading order $(\mathcal{O}(p^2))$ with a nonzero lepton mass, which are infrared and ultraviolet finite. The results are basically consistent with previous predictions based on hadron model calculation, but they are somewhat different from calculations based on heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, especially in charge asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper calculates radiative corrections to elastic lepton-proton scattering using manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) at chiral next-to-leading order (𝒪(p²)), keeping the lepton mass nonzero; the results are basically consistent with hadron model predictions but differ from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) calculations, especially in charge asymmetry.

## Method

The authors computed one-loop virtual QED corrections (two-photon exchange, vertex corrections, vacuum polarization) and single soft-photon bremsstrahlung for elastic ℓ±p scattering, using manifestly Lorentz-invariant BχPT up to chiral 𝒪(p²). Dimensional regularization was employed to handle both chiral and QED divergences, with infrared divergences canceled systematically at each chiral order. The calculation included only the elastic proton intermediate state, omitting Δ(1232) and higher nucleon resonances, and used the chiral Lagrangian with low-energy constants gA = 1.267, c6 = 3.706/(4M), c7 = −0.120/(2M).

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range considered: Q² ≈ 0.0016–0.082 GeV² for electrons and 0.0016–0.080 GeV² for muons, corresponding to MUSE experiment kinematics with beam momenta 115, 153, and 210 MeV and lab scattering angles 20°–100°.
- Chiral expansion scale: Q/(4πFπ) ∼ Q/M, with Fπ = 92.4 MeV.
- The critical case ε = 1 (photon polarization parameter) corresponds to Q² = 2m² ≈ 0.022 GeV² for muon beam.
- The IR divergence of the two-photon exchange contribution arises only from chiral leading order (𝒪(p)), with no new IR divergence at any higher chiral order.

## Context

This work extends previous radiative correction calculations for lepton-proton scattering beyond the ultrarelativistic approximation (Maximon and Tjon, 2000) and beyond heavy baryon χPT (Talukdar et al., 2019, 2020), which suffers from nonrelativistic expansion issues (Bernard, 1996; Becher, 1999). The results are relevant for the MUSE experiment at PSI, which aims to measure the proton charge radius at better than 1% precision, and for resolving the proton radius puzzle and the proton form factor puzzle. The approach enables systematic chiral expansion of radiative corrections at low momentum transfer (Q² ∼ mπ²), where hadron model calculations (Blunden, 2003; Kondratyuk, 2005; Tomalak, 2014) have uncontrolled uncertainties from unphysical timelike regions.
