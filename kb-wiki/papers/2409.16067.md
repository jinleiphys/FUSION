# Evidence of collinear factorization breaking due to collinear-to-soft Glauber exchanges for a $2 \to 3$ exclusive process at leading twist

> We exhibit an exclusive process, namely the photoproduction of a $π^{0}γ$ pair with large invariant mass, which violates collinear factorization. We explicitly demonstrate that this is due to the fact that there exists diagrams with gluon exchange in $t$ channel, contributing at the leading power, for which Glauber gluons are trapped. This is caused by the pinching of the contour integration of both the plus and minus light-cone components of the Glauber gluon momentum. We argue that this leads to the observed ``endpoint-like'' divergence of the convolution integral at leading order and leading power when collinear factorization is naïvely assumed.

## Key claim

The photoproduction of a $\pi^{0}\gamma$ pair with large invariant mass violates collinear factorization at leading twist because diagrams with a gluon exchange in the $t$ channel, contributing at leading power, trap Glauber gluons via pinching of the contour integration of both the plus and minus light-cone components of the Glauber gluon momentum; this leads to an "endpoint-like" divergence in the convolution integral when collinear factorization is naively assumed.

## Method

The authors performed a power-counting analysis using Libby-Sterman rules and Landau equations on a specific two-loop diagram for the process $\gamma (q) + N(p_{N}) \to \gamma (q') + \pi^{0}(p_{\pi}) + N'(p_{N'})$, identifying pinch singular surfaces (PSSs) in the loop momentum space. They analyzed the pole structure of the loop momenta $l$ and $k$ in both the collinear region and the Glauber region, demonstrating that the Glauber pinch occurs for a $\bar{n}$-collinear to soft Glauber exchange between a spectator parton from the nucleon and a soft spectator parton connecting the incoming photon and outgoing pion. The power counting was performed in light-cone gauge to isolate the leading contributions.

## Key numbers

* The Glauber region scaling for the loop momentum $l$ is $l \sim (\lambda, \lambda^2, \lambda)Q$, where $\lambda \ll 1$ is the ratio of the small scale (e.g., $\sqrt{|t|}, m_{\pi}, m_N, \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}$) to the hard scale $Q$.
* The overall power of the Glauber region contribution is $\lambda$, which is the same leading power as the standard collinear region contribution.
* The soft region where $l,k \sim (\lambda, \lambda, \lambda)$ is power-suppressed at $O(\lambda^2)$ relative to the leading power.
* The collinear region scaling for $F_A$ (gluon GPD part) is $\lambda^0$, for $F_B$ (pion DA part) is $\lambda^1$, and for $F_H$ (hard part) is $\lambda^0$, giving an overall scaling of $\lambda$.

## Context

This work extends the proof of factorization for $2 \to 3$ exclusive processes derived by Qiu and Yu (2022), showing that it fails for the specific case of $\pi^{0}\gamma$ photoproduction due to a Glauber pinch. The Glauber pinch is analogous to the classic Glauber pinch in the Drell-Yan process (Collins, 1997), but here it occurs between a collinear spectator parton from the nucleon and a soft spectator parton from the photon-pion system, rather than between two collinear spectators. The result implies that the crossed process of $\pi^{0}$-nucleon scattering to two photons (Qiu, 2022) also suffers from the same Glauber problem, while processes involving only quark GPDs (such as charged pion or rho meson photoproduction studied by Duplancic (2018, 2022, 2023) and Boussarie (2016)) remain factorizable. The authors suggest that going beyond collinear factorization by introducing $k_T$-dependent distributions is necessary to save the phenomenology.
