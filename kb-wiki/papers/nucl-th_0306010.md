# The Extended Wronskian Determinant Approach and the Iterative Solutions of One-Dimensional Dirac Equation

> An approximation method, namely, the Extended Wronskian Determinant Approach, is suggested to study the one-dimensional Dirac equation. An integral equation which can be solved by iterative procedure to find the wave functions is established. We employ this approach to study the one-dimensional Dirac equation with one-well potential, and give the energy levels and wave functions up to the first order iterative approximation. For double-well potential, the energy levels up to the first order approximation are given.

## Key claim

The Extended Wronskian Determinant Approach (EWDA) provides an iterative method to solve the one-dimensional Dirac equation with a perturbation potential, using the exact solution of the corresponding homogeneous Dirac equation as the zeroth-order wave function; the method yields energy levels and wave functions up to any desired order in principle, but the paper only demonstrates results up to the first-order iterative approximation for one-well and double-well potentials.

## Method

The authors extend the Wronskian Determinant Approach (WDA), previously developed for the Schrödinger equation, to the one-dimensional Dirac equation. They construct a second linearly independent solution ψ₂ from a known bound-state solution ψ₁ of the homogeneous Dirac equation using an extended Wronskian determinant, then derive an integral equation for the perturbed wave function. This integral equation is solved by an iterative procedure, with the energy shift ε determined by a "resonant condition" that enforces the boundary condition that the divergent component of the wave function vanishes at infinity.

## Context

This work extends the Wronskian Determinant Approach (WDA) previously developed by the same authors for the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation (Xu, Lu, and Su, 2003) to the relativistic case of the Dirac equation. It also builds on the Friedberg-Lee-Zhao (FLZ) method (Friedberg, Lee, and Zhao, 2001-2003) for solving the Schrödinger equation by quadrature along a single trajectory. The EWDA enables the study of Dirac equations with double-well potentials, which have applications in the soliton bag model, instantons, and anti-instantons, and can be combined with variational methods to optimize trial wave functions.
