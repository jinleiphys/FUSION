# U_A(1) Anomaly in Hot and Dense QCD and the Critical Surface

> We discuss the chiral phase transition in hot and dense QCD with three light flavors. Inspired by the well known fact that the U_A(1) anomaly could induce first order phase transitions, we study the effect of the possible restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry at finite density. In particular, we explore the link between the U_A(1) restoration and the recent lattice QCD results of de Forcrand and Philipsen, in which the first order phase transition region near zero chemical potential (mu) shrinks in the quark mass and mu space when mu is increased. Starting from the Ginzburg-Landau theory for general discussions, we then use the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model for quantitative studies. With the partial U_A(1) restoration modeled by the density dependent 't Hooft interaction, we fit the shrinking of the critical surface found in de Forcrand and Philipsen's lattice calculation at low mu. At higher mu, the critical surface might shrink or expand, depending on the scenarios. This raises the possibility that despite the shrinking of the critical surface at lower mu, the QCD critical end point might still exist due to the expansion at higher mu. In this case, very high precision lattice data will be needed to detect the back-bending of the critical surface with the currently available analytic continuation or Taylor expansion approaches. Lattice computations could, however, test whether the U_A(1) restoration is responsible for the shrinking of the critical surface by computing eta' mass or the topological susceptibility at small mu.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observed shrinking of the first-order chiral phase transition region at low chemical potential (mu) in lattice QCD can be explained by partial restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry, but at higher mu the first-order region might either shrink or expand depending on the competition between U_A(1) restoration and density effects, leaving open the possibility that the QCD critical end point (CEP) could still exist.

## Method

The authors first use Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory to analyze how the U_A(1) anomaly term (the determinant term) influences the order of the chiral phase transition in three-flavor QCD. They then perform quantitative calculations using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, modeling partial U_A(1) restoration by making the 't Hooft coupling constant density-dependent with a Gaussian form g_D*(mu) = g_D*(0) exp(-mu^2/mu_0^2). The critical surface in the (mu, m_ud, m_s) space is computed for various values of the free parameter mu_0 and compared to the lattice QCD results of de Forcrand and Philipsen (2006).

## Key numbers

- The lattice QCD expansion of the critical curve near mu=0 is: m_c(mu)/m_c(0) = 1 - 3.3(3)(mu/(pi T_c))^2 - 47(20)(mu/(pi T_c))^4 - ..., with T_c = 135 MeV at mu=0.
- The NJL model parameters fitted to vacuum observables give: m_ud = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 135.7 MeV, g_S = 3.67 Lambda^{-2}, Lambda = 631.4 MeV, and g_D = -9.29 Lambda^{-5}.
- The NJL model with g_D*(0) = g_D yields m_c(0) ~ 1.1 MeV, while with g_D*(0) = 3.5 g_D it yields m_c(0) ~ 26 MeV.
- The range of mu_0 that reproduces the lattice curvature is 253-379 MeV for both choices of g_D*(0).
- A rough estimate from instanton physics gives mu_0 ~ 1/(sqrt(N_f) rho_0) ~ 380 MeV, where N_f = 3 and rho_0 ~ 0.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD results of de Forcrand and Philipsen (2006), who found that the first-order phase transition region near zero chemical potential shrinks as mu increases, challenging the conventional picture of a QCD critical end point. The paper builds on earlier studies of U_A(1) restoration effects by Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) and Shuryak (1993), and on NJL model analyses by Fukushima (2001) that reproduced lattice topological susceptibility data. The results suggest that lattice computations of the eta' mass or topological susceptibility at small mu could test whether U_A(1) restoration is responsible for the shrinking, and that very high precision lattice data would be needed to detect the possible back-bending of the critical surface at higher mu.
