# Elliptic flow in ultra-relativistic collisions with light polarized nuclei

> Estimates for elliptic flow in collisions of polarized light nuclei with spin $j\ge1$ with a heavy nucleus are presented. In such collisions the azimuthal symmetry is broken via polarization of the wave function of the light nucleus, resulting in nonzero one-body elliptic flow coefficient evaluated relative to the polarization axis. Our estimates involve experimentally well known features of light nuclei, such as their quadrupole moment and the charge radius, yielding the one-body elliptic flow coefficient in the range from 1\% for collisions with the deuteron to 5\% for for collisions with $^{10}$B nucleus. Prospects of addressing the issue in the upcoming fixed-target experiment at the Large Hadron Collider are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that elliptic flow in ultra-relativistic collisions of light polarized nuclei (spin j ≥ 1) with a heavy nucleus can be generated via the polarization of the light nucleus wave function, yielding a nonzero one-body elliptic flow coefficient relative to the polarization axis. The effect is estimated to range from 1% for deuteron collisions to 5% for ¹⁰B collisions, but these values depend on the degree of polarization achievable in experiment and on the assumption that collective flow transmutes the initial geometric deformation into final-state momentum anisotropy.

## Method

The authors estimate the ellipticity of the fireball formed in collisions of polarized light nuclei (deuteron, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, ¹⁰B) with a heavy Pb target using the wounded nucleon model with a binary component, implemented in the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo code. They relate the ellipticity of the nuclear distribution to the experimentally known quadrupole moment Q₂ and mean squared charge radius of each light nucleus, then apply a quenching factor from the wounding profile of nucleon-nucleon interactions and a linear response coefficient k ≈ 0.2 to convert ellipticity to elliptic flow v₂{Φ_P}. Glauber simulations at √s_NN = 72 GeV (corresponding to planned LHC fixed-target experiments) are performed for central collisions.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron with j₃ = 0, the ellipticity of the nuclear distribution is ε₂^{|Ψ|²_{j₃=0}}{Φ_P} ≈ 0.14; for j₃ = ±1, ε₂^{|Ψ|²_{j₃=1}}{Φ_P} ≈ −0.07.
- The estimated elliptic flow coefficient for most central deuteron+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 72 GeV is in the range −0.5% ≲ v₂{Φ_P} ≲ 1%, depending on the tensor polarization P_zz (accessible range −1.5 ≲ P_zz ≲ 0.7).
- For ¹⁰B, the leading-order estimate of the nuclear distribution ellipticity from the quadrupole moment formula is −25% for the j₃ = ±3 state.
- The quenching factor for the ellipticity due to Pb nucleon contributions is 70–80% for the light nuclei considered.
- The response coefficient used to convert ellipticity to flow is k ∼ 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal for polarized deuterons (Bozek and Broniowski, 2018) to other light nuclei with spin j ≥ 1, including ⁷Li, ⁹Be, and ¹⁰B, which have larger quadrupole moments relative to their radii and thus produce larger effects. The method provides a controlled way to probe the geometry of the initial fireball in small systems, complementing earlier studies of collectivity in p+A, d+A, and ³He+A collisions (Bozek, 2011; Nagle, 2013) and addressing the debate between hydrodynamic and Color Glass Condensate explanations of angular correlations. If confirmed experimentally, the nonzero v₂{Φ_P} would support the late-stage collective expansion picture over early-stage gluon production mechanisms.
