# The heavy-quark hybrid meson spectrum in lattice QCD

> Recent findings on the spectrum of heavy-quark mesons from computer simulations of quarks and gluons in lattice QCD are summarized, with particular attention to quark-antiquark states bound by an excited gluon field. The validity of a Born-Oppenheimer treatment for such systems is discussed. Recent results on glueball masses, the light-quark 1-+ hybrid meson mass, and the static three-quark potential are summarized.

# The heavy-quark hybrid meson spectrum in lattice QCD

## Key claim

The leading Born-Oppenheimer (LBO) approximation provides a valid description of both conventional and hybrid heavy-quark mesons, with level splittings agreeing within 10% when compared to direct NRQCD simulations, but only in the absence of light sea quarks; the inclusion of light quark loops drastically changes the long-range behavior of the static potentials and likely prevents the formation of the dense spectrum of states predicted by the quenched approximation.

## Method

The gluonic adiabatic potentials V_Q\bar{Q}(r) for a static quark-antiquark pair were computed using Markov-chain Monte Carlo lattice QCD simulations with anisotropic lattices, improved actions, and large operator sets with correlation matrix techniques. The leading Born-Oppenheimer approximation was then applied by solving the radial Schrödinger equation separately for each potential, neglecting retardation effects, heavy-quark spin interactions, and mixings between different adiabatic surfaces. The validity of this approximation was tested by comparing LBO level splittings with results from simulations using a leading-order non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) heavy-quark action that includes retardation and allows surface mixings.

## Key numbers

- Level splittings between LBO predictions and direct NRQCD simulations agree within **10%** (for conventional 2S and 1P states and four hybrid states, in terms of the hadronic scale r_0 and with respect to the 1S state)
- Hybrid/S-wave mixing due to quark spin effects in bottomonium: ⟨1H|Υ⟩ ≈ **0.076–0.11**, ⟨1H|η_b⟩ ≈ **0.13–0.19**
- Hybrid/S-wave mixing due to quark spin effects in charmonium: ⟨1H|J/Ψ⟩ ≈ **0.18–0.25**, ⟨1H|η_c⟩ ≈ **0.29–0.4**
- The bottom quark mass used in LBO calculations: M_b = **4.58 GeV**
- The charm quark mass used in LBO calculations: M_c = **1.20 GeV**
- The hadronic scale r_0^{-1} used for bottomonium: **450 MeV**; for charmonium: **415 MeV**
- The scale r_0 ≈ **0.5 fm** for unquenched potential comparisons
- Lattice spacing for unquenched potential calculations: a ≈ **0.08 fm**
- Lattice spacing for light-quark hybrid calculations: a ≈ **0.09 fm**
- Excitation energy of the first gluonic excitation in the presence of three static quarks: near **1 GeV**
- String tension used for three-quark potential: σ = **0.89 GeV/fm**
- Glueball masses given in terms of r_0, with r_0^{-1} = **410(20) MeV** assumed for GeV scale

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the static quark-antiquark potential (earlier1, earlier2, jkm2) and provides the first systematic test of the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for heavy-quark hybrid mesons, originally proposed by Hasenfratz and collaborators. The results validate the LBO picture in the quenched approximation but highlight the need for unquenched calculations, as demonstrated by the SESAM collaboration's finding that the Σ_g^+ and Π_u potentials change little below 1 fm when sea quarks are included. The study enables future calculations of hybrid meson properties with dynamical quarks and provides a framework for interpreting experimental candidates such as the exotic 1^{-+} resonances observed by the E852 collaboration.
