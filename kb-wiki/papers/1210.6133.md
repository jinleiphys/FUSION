# Extracting the impact parameter dependence of the nPDFs from the EKS98 and EPS09 global fits

> As all the globally fitted nuclear PDFs (nPDFs) have been so far impact parameter independent, it has not been possible to calculate the hard process cross sections in different centrality classes consistently with the global analyses. In \cite{Helenius:2012wd} we have offered a solution to this problem by determining two spatially dependent nPDF sets, \texttt{EPS09s} and \texttt{EKS98s}, using the $A$-systematics of the earlier global fits EPS09 and EKS98 and an assumption that the spatial dependence can be written as a power series of the nuclear thickness function. For a data comparison, we have calculated the nuclear modification factor of inclusive neutral pion production in d+Au collisions at RHIC in four centrality bins at midrapidity and compared these to a PHENIX measurement. In addition, we have also performed a similar calculation for inclusive photon production in d+Au collisions at RHIC.

# Extracting the impact parameter dependence of the nPDFs from the EKS98 and EPS09 global fits

## Key claim
The authors produce spatially dependent nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) sets, EPS09s and EKS98s, by assuming the spatial dependence can be written as a power series of the nuclear thickness function and fitting to the A-systematics of the earlier global fits EPS09 and EKS98; the resulting sets allow calculation of hard-process cross sections in different centrality classes consistently with the global analyses, but the spatial form is an assumption not uniquely constrained by the data.

## Method
The authors parameterize the spatially dependent nuclear modification r_i^A(x,Q^2,s) as a power series in the nuclear thickness function T_A(s), with coefficients c_j^i(x,Q^2) that depend only on x and Q^2, not on A. They determine these coefficients by minimizing a chi-squared function that compares the spatial average of r_i^A to the A-dependent R_i^A values from the existing EPS09 and EKS98 global fits, using A >= 16 nuclei and the first four non-trivial terms of the series. The fitting is performed for both the central sets and the 30 error sets of EPS09 at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), and for EKS98.

## Key numbers
- The power series ansatz uses the first four non-trivial terms (j = 1 to 4) for accurate fitting across the full x and Q^2 region for all parton flavors and all sets considered.
- The nuclear modification factor R_dAu^pi0 for inclusive neutral pion production in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and y = 0 is calculated in four centrality classes at midrapidity and compared to PHENIX data from Adler (2006).
- The same calculation is performed for inclusive photon production R_dAu^gamma in d+Au collisions at RHIC, using the BFG (set II) fragmentation functions for the fragmentation component.

## Context
This work extends the impact-parameter-independent global nPDF analyses EPS09 (Eskola 2009) and EKS98 (Eskola 1998) by introducing a spatial dependence based on the nuclear thickness function. It enables consistent calculation of nuclear modification factors in different centrality classes for heavy-ion collisions, as demonstrated for d+Au collisions at RHIC. The resulting EPS09s and EKS98s sets are made publicly available and can be used for predictions in p+Pb collisions at the LHC, as noted in the companion paper Helenius (2012).
