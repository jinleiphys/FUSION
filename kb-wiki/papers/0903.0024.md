# Signature of QCD critical point: Anomalous transverse velocity dependence of antiproton-proton ratio

> We formulate the QCD critical point focusing effect on transverse velocity ($β_{t}$) dependence of antiproton to proton ($\bar{p}/p$) ratio, which was recently proposed by Asakawa {\it et al.} as an experimental signature of QCD critical point in high energy heavy ion collisions (HICs). For quantitative analysis, Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model and THERMal heavy-IoN generATOR (THERMINATOR) are applied to calculate the corresponding $β_{t}$ dependence of $\bar{p}/p$ ratio for three gedanken focused isentropic trajectories with different focusing degree on QCD phase diagram. Finally, we obtained an observable anomaly in $β_{t}$ dependence of $\bar{p}/p$ ratio, which can be employed as a signature of QCD critical point.

## Key claim
The QCD critical point focusing effect, which deforms isentropic trajectories on the QCD phase diagram, can produce an observable anomaly in the transverse velocity dependence of the antiproton-to-proton ratio, but only if the isentropic trajectory of the colliding system passes through the vicinity of the critical point.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD transport model to extract the transverse velocity versus emission time distributions for antiprotons and protons in Pb+Pb 40 AGeV fixed target collisions. They then used the THERMINATOR thermal model to calculate the time evolution of antiproton and proton numbers along three gedanken isentropic trajectories with different focusing degrees, all passing through a common QCD critical point at (550, 149) MeV and chemical freeze-out point at (440, 145) MeV. The UrQMD-derived beta_t-t distributions were modified by replacing the time distributions with those from the THERMINATOR calculations, and the resulting beta_t dependence of the antiproton-to-proton ratio was computed.

## Key numbers
- QCD critical point location used: (mu_c, T_c) = (550, 149) MeV
- Chemical freeze-out point used: (mu_ch, T_ch) = (440, 145) MeV, from statistical model fit to Pb+Pb 40 AGeV fixed target data at SPS/CERN
- The normal (no QCP) beta_t dependence of antiproton-to-proton ratio from UrQMD shows a monotonous increase with beta_t up to 0.7
- The anomalous beta_t dependence from QCP focusing shows a completely opposite pattern compared to the UrQMD calculation, with steeper dependence for higher focusing degree

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Asakawa et al. that the QCD critical point focusing effect could produce an observable signature in the antiproton-to-proton ratio. The authors provide the first quantitative calculation of this effect using UrQMD and THERMINATOR models, demonstrating that the anomaly (enhanced ratio at low beta_t and suppressed at high beta_t) is robust for different focusing degrees. The results suggest that existing antiproton p_T spectra from the NA49 collaboration at SPS/CERN may already show hints of this effect through a steeper exponential slope, and the authors propose using inverse Laplace transform on p_T spectra to extract anomalous structures as an excitation function for QCP searches.
