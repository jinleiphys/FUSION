# Effective potential for quantum scalar fields on a de Sitter geometry

> We study the quantum theory of an O(N) scalar field on de Sitter geometry at leading order in a nonperturbative 1/N-expansion. This resums the infinite series of so-called superdaisy loop diagrams. We obtain the de Sitter symmetric solutions of the corresponding, properly renormalized, dynamical field equations and compute the complete effective potential. Because of its self-interactions, the field acquires a strictly positive square mass which screens potential infrared divergences. Moreover, strongly enhanced ultralong-wavelength fluctuations prevent the existence of a spontaneously broken symmetry state in any dimension.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, at leading order in the 1/N expansion, an O(N) scalar field on de Sitter geometry always acquires a strictly positive effective square mass from self-interactions, which screens potential infrared divergences and prevents the existence of a spontaneously broken symmetry state in any dimension, regardless of the tree-level mass or coupling.

## Method

The author studies an O(N) scalar field theory on a de Sitter background using the large-N limit, which resums the infinite series of superdaisy (cactus) loop diagrams. The self-consistent field equations are solved for de Sitter-invariant Bunch-Davies vacuum states, with renormalization performed using a physical ultraviolet cutoff on momenta. The effective potential is computed from the self-consistent mass equation, and analytic approximations are derived for the small mass limit M^2/H^2 << 1.

## Key numbers

- The curvature-induced mass for the case of a tree-level massless minimally coupled field at weak coupling is M^2/H^2 = sqrt(lambda_R)/4pi - (c_3 lambda_R)/(48 pi^2) + O(lambda_R^{3/2}), where c_3 = 2 - gamma - ln 2 approximately equals 0.9
- For the case mu_R^2 + lambda_R phi^2/6 = 0 at phi^2 = 0, the generated mass squared is M^2/H^2 = - (c_3 lambda_R)/(48 pi^2) + sqrt(lambda_R/(16 pi^2) + (c_3 lambda_R/(48 pi^2))^2)
- For mu_R^2 < 0 at phi^2 = 0, the mass squared scales as M^2/H^2 = lambda_R H^2/(16 pi^2 |mu_R^2|) + O(lambda_R^2)
- In the small mass limit, the relevant wavelengths for IR fluctuations are as large as exp(3H^2/2M^2)

## Context

This work extends the stochastic approach of Starobinsky and Yokoyama (1994) by deriving curvature-induced mass generation directly from basic quantum field theory using the large-N expansion, confirming their result that self-interacting scalar fields acquire an effective mass on de Sitter. The paper also relates to the dynamical renormalization group analysis of Burgess (2009) and the 2PI and large-N techniques applied by Riotto and Sloth (2008). The results enable calculation of parametrically O(sqrt(lambda_R)) non-Gaussianities and corrections to the power spectrum in inflationary cosmology, and raise the question of whether similar nonperturbative effects persist beyond the large-N limit.
