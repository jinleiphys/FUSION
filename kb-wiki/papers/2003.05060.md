# Role of individual components of two-nucleon interaction in nuclear matrix elements of $2־½־²־²$ and $0־½־²־²$ of $^\textbf{48}$Ca: Beyond the closure approximation

> In the present work, we examine the role of central (C), spin-orbit (SO) and tensor (T) components of two-nucleon interaction in the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of the two-neutrino double beta decay ($2־½־²־²$) and the light neutrino-exchange mechanism of neutrinoless double beta decay ($0־½־²־²$) of $^{48}$Ca in closure approximation and nonclosure approach. The NMEs are calculated in the nuclear shell-model framework using two-nucleon effective interaction GXPF1A used for $pf$ shell. The decomposition of the shell model two-nucleon interaction into its individual components is performed using the spin-tensor decomposition (STD). The NMEs for $2־½־²־²$ are calculated in running nonclosure method. The NMEs for $0־½־²־²$ are calculated with four different methods, namely, closure, running closure, running nonclosure, and mixed method. Results show that the magnitude of NMEs for $2־½־²־²$ decreases about 7\% with the C+SO component of the interaction as compared to the C component. The magnitude of NMEs is further decreased about 9\% by adding T component to the C+SO component. The NMEs of $0־½־²־²$ calculated in running nonclosure method are enhanced by about 8-10\%, 8-10\%, and 9-12\%, respectively, as compared to corresponding NMEs calculated in running closure method with C, C+SO components and total (C+SO+T) GXPF1A interaction for different SRC parametrization. For both $2־½־²־²$ and $0־½־²־²$, the NMEs calculated with C+SO component is in opposite phase with the NMEs calculated with C component and the total GXPF1A interaction.

## Key claim

The magnitude of nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for both $2\nu\beta\beta$ and $0\nu\beta\beta$ of $^{48}$Ca depends significantly on which component (central, spin-orbit, tensor) of the two-nucleon interaction is included, and the sign of the NMEs calculated with the central-plus-spin-orbit component is opposite to that calculated with the central component alone or with the full interaction; these trends hold beyond the closure approximation when using running nonclosure methods.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations for $^{48}$Ca using the GXPF1A effective interaction in the $pf$ shell, decomposing it into central (C), spin-orbit (SO), and tensor (T) components via spin-tensor decomposition (STD). For $2\nu\beta\beta$, NMEs were calculated using the running nonclosure method, summing over $1^+$ states of the intermediate nucleus $^{48}$Sc. For $0\nu\beta\beta$ (light neutrino-exchange mechanism), NMEs were calculated with four methods: closure, running closure, running nonclosure, and mixed method, using different short-range correlation (SRC) parametrizations (Miller-Spencer, CD-Bonn, AV18) and including finite nucleon size and higher-order current effects.

## Key numbers

- For $2\nu\beta\beta$ of $^{48}$Ca, the NME magnitude decreases by about 7% when the SO component is added to the C component (from 0.070 to -0.065), and further decreases by about 9% when the T component is added to C+SO (from -0.065 to 0.059).
- For $0\nu\beta\beta$ of $^{48}$Ca, total NMEs calculated with the C component alone in the running nonclosure method are about 8-10% larger than those in the running closure method, depending on SRC parametrization.
- For $0\nu\beta\beta$ with the C+SO component, running nonclosure NMEs are about 8-10% larger than running closure NMEs.
- For $0\nu\beta\beta$ with the total (C+SO+T) GXPF1A interaction, running nonclosure NMEs are about 9-12% larger than running closure NMEs.
- For $0\nu\beta\beta$, adding the SO component to the C component decreases total NMEs by about 13-15% (closure), 10-11% (running closure), 10-11% (running nonclosure), and 12-15% (mixed method) for different SRC parametrizations.
- Adding the T component to C+SO further decreases total $0\nu\beta\beta$ NMEs by about 5-6% (closure), 10-12% (running closure), 9-11% (running nonclosure), and 4-5% (mixed method).
- The $2\nu\beta\beta$ NME with the C component alone is 0.070; with C+SO it is -0.065; with the full interaction (C+SO+T) it is 0.059.
- The closure energy used was $\langle E\rangle = 7.72$ MeV for closure and running closure methods.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $2\nu\beta\beta$ and $0\nu\beta\beta$ NMEs for $^{48}$Ca that used only the total effective interaction, such as those by Caurier et al. (1990), and by Sen'kov and Horoi (2013, 2014, 2016). It goes beyond the recent study by Kumar et al. (2020) that examined the role of individual interaction components for $0\nu\beta\beta$ of $^{48}$Ca using only the closure approximation, by also applying nonclosure methods. The results enable a more detailed understanding of how central, spin-orbit, and tensor forces contribute to double-beta decay NMEs, which can inform the development of more reliable nuclear models for heavier candidate nuclei.
