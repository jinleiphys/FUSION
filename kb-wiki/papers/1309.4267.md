# The Relativistic Green's Function Model for Quasielastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> A model based on the relativistic impulse approximation for quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering is presented. The effects of the final-state interactions (FSI) between the emitted nucleon and the residual nucleus are described by the relativistic Green's function model where FSI are treated consistently with the exclusive scattering and using the same complex optical potential. The results of the model are compared with the results of different descriptions of FSI and with available data for neutrino-nucleus scattering.

# The Relativistic Green's Function Model for Quasielastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model can reproduce charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) MiniBooNE neutrino-nucleus scattering data without increasing the nucleon axial mass above the standard value of 1.03 GeV/c², provided that the imaginary part of the complex optical potential (ROP) redistributes flux across all final-state channels. The model's enhancement over other relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) approaches arises from the translation to inclusive strength of inelastic channel effects, though the use of phenomenological ROPs prevents disentangling the specific reaction processes responsible.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive quasielastic lepton-nucleus cross sections within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA), where the hadron tensor is expressed via the imaginary part of the A-body Green's function projected onto single-nucleon knockout channels. Using projection operator techniques, the full propagator is reduced to a single-particle optical-model Green's function, whose spectral representation is evaluated through a biorthogonal expansion in eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. Bound nucleon states are self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions from a relativistic mean-field approach, and final-state interactions (FSI) are described by complex phenomenological ROPs (EDAD1, EDAI, and DEM parameterizations) fitted to elastic proton-nucleus scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Standard nucleon axial mass used in all calculations: M_A = 1.03 GeV/c²
- For the ¹⁶O(e,e′) reaction: beam energy ε = 1080 MeV, scattering angle ϑ = 32°
- For the ⁵⁶Fe(e,e′) reaction: beam energy ε = 2020 MeV, scattering angle ϑ = 20°
- rROP results are typically 15% lower than RPWIA results for the ¹²C(ν̅_μ, μ⁺) reaction
- NCE data on CH₂ span a Q² range up to approximately 1.65 (GeV/c)²
- RGF-EDAI cross section for NCE scattering is in accordance with the shape and magnitude of the data, while RGF-EDAD1 lies below the data at the smallest Q² values

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Green's function model, originally developed for quasielastic electron scattering (Giusti et al., various years), to charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering. The RGF model contrasts with the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), which neglects FSI entirely, and with the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model (Caballero et al., 2003), which uses only a real, energy-independent potential and includes only purely nucleonic contributions. The RGF results are larger than those from RMF and other IA-based models and can reproduce MiniBooNE CCQE data (MiniBooNE Collaboration, 2010) without enlarging the axial mass, unlike the relativistic Fermi gas model which requires M_A significantly above 1.03 GeV/c². The approach enables consistent treatment of FSI in both exclusive and inclusive scattering, though disentangling the role of multinucleon processes and two-body currents (Amaro et al., 2011) requires explicit microscopic calculations beyond the present phenomenological framework.
