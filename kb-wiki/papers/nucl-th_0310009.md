# Test of Pseudospin Symmetry in Deformed Nuclei

> Pseudospin symmetry is a relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian with scalar and vector mean fields equal and opposite in sign. This symmetry imposes constraints on the Dirac eigenfunctions. We examine extensively the Dirac eigenfunctions of realistic relativistic mean field calculations of deformed nuclei to determine if these eigenfunctions satisfy these pseudospin symmetry constraints.

## Key claim

Pseudospin symmetry in deformed nuclei is only approximate: the predicted relations between Dirac eigenfunction components are satisfied to varying degrees, with the conditions on lower amplitudes and the differential relation between dominant upper components holding approximately, while the relation between small upper components and the differential equations linking dominant to small upper components are not well satisfied.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree calculations for the deformed nucleus ¹⁶⁸Er using the NL3 parameter set in a Woods-Saxon basis, with pairing treated via BCS approximation. They extracted Dirac eigenfunctions for neutron pseudospin doublets and tested the predicted pseudospin symmetry relations, specifically the vanishing of certain lower components, the equality of others, and first-order differential equations connecting upper components, by comparing amplitudes as functions of ρ and z at several fixed coordinate values.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical average binding energy for ¹⁶⁸Er is B/A = 8.107 MeV, with quadrupole deformation β = 0.3497 and root mean square radius R = 5.376 fm.
- The [402]5/2 and [404]7/2 doublet (Λ̃=3) has a large energy splitting of about 2 MeV, with single-particle energies ε = -12.083 MeV and ε = -14.160 MeV respectively.
- The [400]1/2 and [402]3/2 doublet (Λ̃=1) has a small energy splitting of about 0.4 MeV, with ε = -10.2073 MeV and ε = -10.603 MeV respectively.
- The [501]3/2 and [503]5/2 doublet (Λ̃=2) has a small energy splitting less than 0.4 MeV, with ε = -1.349 MeV and ε = -0.9603 MeV respectively.
- The [510]1/2 and [512]3/2 doublet (Λ̃=1) has a tiny energy splitting less than 0.01 MeV, with ε = -3.8436 MeV and ε = -3.8378 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier tests of pseudospin symmetry in spherical nuclei (Ginocchio 1997, Ginocchio and Leviatan 1998, Meng et al. 1998) to the deformed case, where previous studies had been limited and focused primarily on lower Dirac components (Tanabe et al. 1990, 1991, 1992). The results confirm that pseudospin symmetry improves with decreasing binding energy and decreasing pseudo-orbital angular momentum projection, consistent with spherical-nucleus findings, and provide a foundation for understanding superdeformation and identical bands in deformed nuclei as discussed by Dudek (1990), Twin (1991), Stephens (1992), and Zeng (1993).
