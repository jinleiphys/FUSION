# Three-nucleon system: Irreducible and reducible contributions of the three-nucleon force

> The three-nucleon bound and scattering equations are solved in momentum space for a coupled-channel Hamiltonian. The Hamiltonian couples the purely nucleonic sector of Hilbert space with a sector in which one nucleon is excited to a $Δ$ isobar. The interaction consists of irreducible two-baryon and irreducible three-baryon potentials. The calculation keeps only the purely nucleonic one among the irreducible three-baryon potentials. The coupled-channel two-baryon potential yields additional reducible contributions to the three-nucleon force. The Coulomb interaction between the two protons is included using the method of screening and renormalization. Three-nucleon force effects on the bound-state energies and on observables of elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering and breakup are studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that adding an irreducible three-nucleon (3N) potential to a coupled-channel Hamiltonian with explicit Delta-isobar excitation can improve the description of three-nucleon bound-state energies, but simultaneously fitting the triton binding energy and the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length requires unnaturally large attractive and repulsive 3N force parameters, suggesting a fundamental difficulty rather than a model-specific problem.

## Method

The authors solve three-nucleon bound and scattering equations in momentum space using Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for a coupled-channel Hamiltonian coupling the purely nucleonic Hilbert space with a sector containing one Delta isobar. The two-baryon potential is the CD Bonn + Delta potential, and an irreducible 3N potential based on the Urbana IX form is added with modified strength parameters A2π, C2π, and B0. The Coulomb interaction between protons is included using screening and renormalization, and calculations are performed for energies up to the pion-production threshold.

## Key numbers

- CD Bonn + Delta alone yields triton binding energy |E(3H)| = 8.306 MeV (experimental value 8.482 MeV), missing it by 176 keV
- CD Bonn + Delta alone gives neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length a2 = 0.695 fm (experimental value 0.65 ± 0.04 fm)
- The irreducible 3N potential U3 requires A2π = -0.02598 MeV, C2π/A2π = 1.0000, B0 = 0.01250 MeV to fit both |E(3H)| = 8.482 MeV and a2 = 0.639 fm
- The irreducible 3N potential U1 uses A2π = -0.00266 MeV, C2π/A2π = 0.5000, B0 = 0, yielding |E(3H)| = 8.482 MeV but a2 = 0.559 fm
- Two-baryon partial waves with total pair angular momentum jx ≤ 5 and three-baryon states with total 3N angular momentum J ≤ 59/2 are included for convergence

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Nemoto (1998) and Deltuva (2003) that used only coupled-channel two-baryon potentials without irreducible 3N forces, and combines them with the approach of Deltuva (2009) that used irreducible two- and three-baryon forces in a purely nucleonic framework. The difficulty in simultaneously fitting the triton binding energy and neutron-deuteron scattering length mirrors findings by Kievsky (2010) with the Argonne V18 potential and by Epelbaum (2014) with chiral effective field theory, suggesting this is a general feature of current 3N force models. The paper enables future calculations with more complete dynamics, including explicit pion degrees of freedom.
