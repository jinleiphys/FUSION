# Transition from weak to strong coupling in thermal gauge theories: Lessons from ${\cal N}=4$ SYM Theory

> We investigate the transition between weak and strong coupling in thermal ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory as a function of 't Hooft coupling $λ$ for several quantities of phenomenological interest for which next-to-leading order calculations are available in both regimes. We use Padé approximants to interpolate between the weak and strong coupling expansions and determine the location and width of the transition between the asymptotic regimes.

## Key claim

The transition between weak and strong coupling in thermal N=4 SYM theory is not a sharp phase transition but a gradual crossover, with the central "pseudocritical" coupling lambda_c taking somewhat different values for different quantities of interest, though their transition regions overlap.

## Method

The author uses next-to-leading order (NLO) weak and strong coupling expansions for the normalized entropy density, kinematic shear viscosity, normalized diffusion constant, and normalized transverse broadening rate (jet quenching parameter). Generalized Pade approximants are constructed to interpolate between the two asymptotic regimes, and the transition point lambda_c is located by finding the extremum of the first derivative (for entropy density) or second derivative (for transport coefficients) of the Pade interpolation with respect to ln(lambda).

## Key numbers

- Normalized entropy density s(lambda)/s0: lambda_c = 3.14, with transition bounds lambda_- = 0.54 and lambda_+ = 12.95
- Kinematic shear viscosity eta/s: lambda_c = 5.28, with transition bounds lambda_- = 1.69 and lambda_+ = 16.38
- Normalized diffusion constant 2pi T D_s: lambda_c = 11.90, with transition bounds lambda_- = 5.62 and lambda_+ = 27.81
- Normalized momentum broadening coefficient qhat/T^3: lambda_c = 4.36, with transition bounds lambda_- = 1.39 and lambda_+ = 13.25
- Averaging over all four quantities gives a general estimate for the weak-to-strong coupling transition region: 3 less than or approximately equal to lambda less than or approximately equal to 14

## Context

This work extends the interpolation approach of Andersen (2021) for the normalized entropy density to three transport coefficients in N=4 SYM theory. The study uses known NLO results from Caron-Huot (2008) for the diffusion coefficient, Ghiglieri (2018) for the jet quenching parameter, and Buchel (2004) and Zhang (2012) for strong coupling corrections. The finding that the transition region (lambda approximately 3 to 14) includes the value lambda approximately 12 often used in applying SYM results to quark-gluon plasma physics suggests that neither weak nor strong coupling expansions are reliable in this physically interesting range, questioning the blanket use of strong coupling SYM results for modeling bulk QGP physics.
