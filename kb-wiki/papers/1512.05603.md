# Influence of kinematic cuts on the net charge distribution

> The higher moments of the net charge distributions, e.g. the skewness and kurtosis, are studied within an infinite hadronic matter calculation in a transport approach. By dividing the box into several parts, the volume dependence of the fluctuations is investigated. After confirming that the initial distributions follow the expectations from a binomial distribution, the influence of quantum number conservation in this case the net charge in the system on the higher moments is evaluated. For this purpose, the composition of the hadron gas is adjusted and only pions and $ρ$ mesons are simulated to investigate the charge conservation effect. In addition, the effect of imposing kinematic cuts in momentum space is analysed. The role of resonance excitations and decays on the higher moments can also be studied within this model. This work is highly relevant to understand the experimental measurements of higher moments obtained in the RHIC beam energy scan and their comparison to lattice results and other theoretical calculations assuming infinite matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a hadronic transport simulation of infinite matter, kinematic cuts in momentum space increase fluctuations of the net charge distribution, while local charge conservation from resonance formation suppresses them; these competing effects must be disentangled before experimental higher-moment measurements can be interpreted as signals of critical behavior.

## Method

The authors used the SMASH hadronic transport code to simulate infinite hadronic matter in a cubic box (volume 10^3 fm^3) with periodic boundary conditions, first with only pions undergoing elastic scattering and then with pions that can form and decay from rho mesons. They divided the box into 10 spatial intervals of 1 fm each, computed the higher moments (variance, skewness, kurtosis) of the net charge distribution from final particles after resonance decays, and applied a kinematic cut of 0.3 < p < 2 GeV on the absolute three-momentum to mimic experimental acceptance cuts.

## Key numbers

- Box volume: 10^3 fm^3, divided into 10 intervals of Δx = 1 fm.
- Simulations used on the order of 5 million events to reduce statistical errors on kurtosis.
- For a box with 400 pions and elastic cross-section σ = 10 mb, the kurtosis saturated after 10 fm/c of time evolution and showed no temperature dependence in the range T = 120–200 MeV; all subsequent calculations used t = 10 fm/c and T = 160 MeV.
- For the π–ρ system with 200 π⁺ and 200 π⁻, the kinematic cut 0.3 < p < 2 GeV shifted the skewness and kurtosis values relative to the binomial expectation, while resonance formation suppressed fluctuations compared to the Skellam distribution.

## Context

This work extends the study of conserved-charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, motivated by the search for a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram as pursued by the STAR Beam Energy Scan (Adamczyk 2014). It builds on prior theoretical work by Asakawa (2015), Berdnikov (1999), and Skokov (2012) linking higher moments to the correlation length, and addresses experimental complications such as volume fluctuations, global conservation laws, and detector efficiency discussed by Luo (2013), Bzdak (2012, 2013), and Karsch (2015). The results enable future studies of more complex systems with baryonic and mesonic degrees of freedom to disentangle the effects of baryon diffusion, conservation laws, and kinematic cuts on net baryon, net proton, and net charge distributions.
