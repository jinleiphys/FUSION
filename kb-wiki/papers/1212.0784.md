# Magnetized hot neutron matter: lowest order constrained variational calculations

> We have studied the spin polarized hot neutron matter in the presence of strong magnetic field. In this work, using the lowest order constrained variational method at finite temperature and employing $AV_{18}$ nuclear potential, some thermodynamic properties of spin polarized neutron matter such as spin polarization parameter, free energy, equation of state and effective mass have been calculated. It has been shown that the strong magnetic field breaks the symmetry of the free energy, leading to a magnetized equilibrium state. We have found that the equation of state becomes stiffer by increasing both magnetic field and temperature. The magnetic field dependence of effective mass for the spin-up and spin-down neutrons has been investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in spin-polarized hot neutron matter, a strong magnetic field breaks the symmetry of the free energy as a function of spin polarization, leading to a magnetized equilibrium state; however, the effect of magnetic fields below about \(10^{18}\) G is almost insignificant, and the stiffening of the equation of state and the shift in effective mass become pronounced only above that threshold.

## Method

The authors use the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method at finite temperature with the \(AV_{18}\) nucleon-nucleon potential to calculate the free energy, equation of state, spin polarization parameter, and effective masses of spin-up and spin-down neutrons in a uniform magnetic field. They treat the effective masses as variational parameters, minimize the free energy subject to a normalization constraint on the two-body correlation function, and compute the entropy from the Fermi-Dirac distribution to obtain the free energy per particle. The pressure is then derived from the density derivative of the free energy.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium spin polarization parameter at \(T = 10\) MeV, \(\rho = 0.3\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), and \(B = 5 \times 10^{18}\) G is \(\delta_{B_{\mathrm{max}}} = -0.23\).
- At the same conditions (\(T = 10\) MeV, \(\rho = 0.3\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(B = 5 \times 10^{18}\) G), the mass shift between spin-up and spin-down effective masses is \(\Delta (m^*/m) \approx 0.02\) relative to the unpolarized case.
- The free energy decreases rapidly for magnetic fields above \(B \simeq 10^{18}\) G, while below this value the decrease is slow.
- At low densities (\(\rho \leq 0.2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)), the magnitude of the spin polarization parameter decreases with increasing temperature; at higher densities, the temperature dependence is nearly identical to the zero-temperature result.
- The ratio \(T/\varepsilon_f^*\) (where \(\varepsilon_f^*\) is the average Fermi energy) rises with increasing magnetic field, making finite-temperature effects more significant at strong fields.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous zero-temperature studies of magnetized neutron matter (Bordbar et al., various years) and their finite-temperature studies of spin-polarized neutron matter without magnetic fields (Bordbar et al., various years) by combining both finite temperature and strong magnetic fields. It also builds on earlier finite-temperature calculations using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Bombaci et al.), Skyrme interactions (Rios et al.), and self-consistent Green’s functions (Rios et al.), as well as on magnetic-field studies by Perez-Garcia using Skyrme and Gogny interactions. The results enable more realistic modeling of protoneutron star interiors, including the effects of magnetic fields on the equation of state, maximum neutron star mass, and thermal evolution.
