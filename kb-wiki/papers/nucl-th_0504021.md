# Polarization effects in the reaction $\bar p+p\to e^++e^-$ in presence of two-photon exchange

> Polarization observables for the reaction $\bar p+p\to e^++e^-$ are given in terms of three independent complex amplitudes, in presence of two photon exchange. General expressions for the differential cross section and the polarization observables are given and model independent properties are derived. Polarization effects depending on the polarization of the antiproton beam, the target and of the electron in the final state, have been calculated.

## Key claim

The paper derives general, model-independent expressions for polarization observables in the reaction $\bar p+p\to e^++e^-$ when two-photon exchange (TPE) is included, showing that TPE introduces new structure functions and non-zero single-spin asymmetries even at scattering angles where the Born approximation predicts zero, though the TPE contributions are expected to be of order $\alpha$ (the fine-structure constant) and thus small.

## Method

The authors parametrize the hadronic current for $\bar p+p\to e^++e^-$ using three complex invariant amplitudes ($\tilde G_M$, $\tilde F_2$, $F_3$) following the tensor-form approach of Rekalo and collaborators, which is analytically continued from the space-like region of elastic electron-nucleon scattering. They decompose the amplitudes into Born (one-photon-exchange) terms plus TPE corrections ($\Delta G_M$, $\Delta G_E$, $F_3$), then compute the hadronic tensor and differential cross section in the center-of-mass system, keeping only terms linear in the TPE amplitudes. Expressions for single-spin asymmetries (polarized beam or target), final-electron polarization, polarization transfer coefficients, and double-spin correlation coefficients are derived and analyzed at threshold and at $\theta = 90^\circ$.

## Key numbers

- The TPE effect in elastic electron-deuteron scattering was predicted to represent a 10% effect at $q^2 \cong 1.3$ GeV$^2$ (citing Fr73).
- The ratio $G_E/G_M$ data from JLab (citing JG00) cover the region $0.3$ GeV$^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 5.6$ GeV$^2$, showing a remarkable fall with $Q^2$.
- A VMD-inspired model (citing Du04) predicts $G_E$ will vanish around $q^2 = -15$ GeV$^2$.
- The single-spin asymmetry $A_y(\theta)$ at $\theta = 90^\circ$ is given by $A_y(90^0)=2\frac{\sqrt{\tau -1}}{\overline D} \text{Im}\, G_E F_3^*$, where $\tau = q^2/(4m^2)$ and $\overline D = D(\theta=90^0)$.
- The final-electron transverse polarization $P_y^{(e)}$ is suppressed by a factor $m_e/m$ (electron mass over nucleon mass), but for $\mu^+\mu^-$ production the factor is larger by $m_\mu/m_e \approx 200$.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Rekalo et al. (2004) for elastic electron-nucleon scattering with TPE to the time-like annihilation channel $\bar p+p\to e^++e^-$, which is the crossing channel of $e+p\to e+p$. It generalizes earlier calculations by Bilenky et al. (1993) and Tomasi-Gustafsson (2005) that assumed one-photon exchange, and provides model-independent expressions that can be used to extract nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region even when TPE is present. The results are directly applicable to planned experiments at the FAIR facility at GSI, where the first measurement of the relative phase of the proton magnetic and electric form factors in the time-like region is planned via single-spin asymmetry measurements.
