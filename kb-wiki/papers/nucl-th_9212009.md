# Cross Section Fluctuations and Chaoticity in Heavy-Ion Dynamics

> Cross section fluctuations in nuclear scattering are briefly reviewed in order to show the main important features. Then chaotic scattering is introduced by means of a very simple model. It is shown that chaoticity produces the same kind of irregular fluctuations observed in light heavy--ion collisions. The transition from order to chaos allows a new general framework for a deeper understanding of reaction mechanisms.

## Key claim

Chaotic scattering, arising from a non-integrable classical Hamiltonian with a strong coupling between rotational and relative motion, produces irregular cross-section fluctuations in light heavy-ion collisions that are qualitatively identical to the non-statistical fluctuations observed experimentally, but a systematic quantitative analysis and more refined experiments are still needed to draw definitive conclusions.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data on cross-section fluctuations in light heavy-ion systems and then introduces a classical model of a deformed nucleus scattering from a spherical one, described by a Hamiltonian with a central and a non-central term. The classical equations of motion are solved numerically for the system \(^{28}\text{Si}+{}^{24}\text{Mg}\) to show fractal fluctuations in final observables. The quantal analogue is then studied using a 2-dimensional coupled-channels model and more realistic 3-dimensional calculations with the code FRESCO, which produce excitation functions and angular distributions that are compared to the experimental phenomenology.

## Key numbers

- Coherence lengths extracted from nucleon-nucleus scattering are 10-50 KeV, while those from light heavy-ion systems like \(^{12}\text{C}+{}^{12}\text{C}\) are 100-300 KeV.
- In the quantal coupled-channels calculations for \(^{28}\text{Si}+{}^{24}\text{Mg}\), the energy step used was 20 KeV, and no structures below this step were found.
- Coherence lengths extracted from the classical and quantal calculations for \(^{28}\text{Si}+{}^{24}\text{Mg}\) were comparable within a factor of two and of the order of 100-250 KeV.
- In the 3-dimensional FRESCO calculations for \(^{28}\text{Si}+{}^{24}\text{Mg}\), the total angular momentum used was \(L=15\hbar\) with an initial rotor spin of \(I=0\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of deterministic chaos from nonlinear dynamics to nuclear scattering, providing a new framework to explain the long-standing puzzle of non-statistical cross-section fluctuations in light heavy-ion collisions, which were previously reviewed by Erb and Bromley (1984) and Braun-Munzinger and Barrette (1982). The results connect the experimental anomalies to the theoretical concept of "soft chaos" (a mixed regime between order and hard chaos), suggesting that standard models like the optical model and Ericson's statistical theory apply only in the extreme regimes of order and complete chaos, respectively. This framework enables a reinterpretation of existing reaction mechanisms and opens the door to more refined experiments, such as detecting irregular fluctuations in gamma multiplicities, and to a deeper understanding of the transition from order to chaos in quantum systems.
