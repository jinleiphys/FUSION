# Contribution of single pion electroproduction to the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for the deuteron

> The generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule is evaluated for the contribution of single pion production on the deuteron by explicit integration up to an energy of 1.5 GeV for both coherent and incoherent production. As elementary $γ^* N\toπN$ amplitude the MAID2003 model has been used. For incoherent production final state interaction is included in the final $NN$ and $πN$ subsystems. The resulting contribution to the generalized transverse GDH sum rule is considerably smaller than the negative contribution from the disintegration channel $d(e,e')np$ which dominates the sum rule at low $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The contribution of single pion electroproduction to the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for the deuteron is considerably smaller than the negative contribution from the electrodisintegration channel \(d(e,e')np\), which dominates the sum rule at low \(Q^2\), but the authors note that the generalization of the GDH integral to virtual photons is to a certain extent arbitrary, with the factor \(g(\omega^{lab},Q^2)\) chosen here as unity.

## Method

The authors evaluated the generalized GDH sum rule for single pion production on the deuteron by explicit integration up to a maximal lab virtual photon energy of 1.5 GeV, covering both coherent (\(d(e,e'\pi^0)d\)) and incoherent (\(d(e,e'\pi)NN\)) production channels. The elementary \(\gamma^* N \to \pi N\) amplitude was taken from the MAID2003 model, and for incoherent production, final state interaction (FSI) was included in the final \(NN\) and \(\pi N\) subsystems using separable representations of the Paris potential for \(NN\) scattering and a separable model for \(\pi N\) scattering. The relevant form factor \(F_T^{\prime 10}\) was computed via integration over the final phase space of the small \(t\)-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The electrodisintegration channel \(d(e,e')np\) contributes a deep negative minimum of \(I_{\gamma^* d\to np}^{GDH} = -9.5\) mb at low \(Q^2 \approx 0.006\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- Above \(Q^2 = 0.8\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), the integral \(I^{GDH}_{\gamma^*d\to np}(Q^2)\) approaches zero rapidly.
- At the photon point \(Q^2=0\), the sum rule value for the deuteron is \(I_d^{GDH} = 0.65\) \(\mu\)b, dictated by the very small anomalous magnetic moment of the deuteron.
- The photodisintegration channel contributes about \(-380\) \(\mu\)b at \(Q^2=0\), requiring cancellation to three leading decimals by pion production channels.
- The ratio of E1 contributions for charged channels is approximately \(\Sigma_{\gamma^*d\to\pi^+nn}^{E1} / \Sigma_{\gamma^*d\to\pi^-pp}^{E1} \approx 0.77\).
- The helicity amplitude ratios from MAID for the \(D_{13}(1520)\) and \(F_{15}(1680)\) resonances are \((A^{p(D_{13})}_{3/2}/A^{p(D_{13})}_{1/2})^2 \approx 31\) and \((A^{p(F_{15})}_{3/2}/A^{p(F_{15})}_{1/2})^2 \approx 29\), respectively.
- The integral \(I^{GDH}_{\gamma^*d\to\pi^+nn}(Q^2)\) becomes negative at about \(Q^2 = 0.3\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the evaluation of the generalized GDH sum rule for the deuteron beyond the electrodisintegration channel studied by Arenhövel (2004) to include single pion electroproduction, following the formalism developed by Tammam et al. (2006) for polarization observables. The calculation addresses the question of whether the large negative contribution from electrodisintegration at low \(Q^2\) is canceled by meson electroproduction, similar to the cancellation observed at the photon point in the work of Arenhövel, Fix, and Schwamb. The results enable future studies of the neutron spin structure via the deuteron and highlight the need for improved data on the \(Q^2\) dependence of resonance helicity amplitudes, particularly for higher resonances beyond the \(P_{33}(1232)\).
