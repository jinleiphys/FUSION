# Sum-rules and Goldstone modes from extended RPA theories in Fermi systems with spontaneously broken symmetries

> The Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach is elaborated for cases with a continuously broken symmetry, this being the main focus of the present article. Correlations beyond standard RPA are summed up correcting for the quasi-boson approximation in standard RPA. Desirable properties of standard RPA such as fullfillment of energy weighted sum rule and appearance of Goldstone (zero) modes are kept. We show theoretically and, for a model case, numerically that, indeed, SCRPA maintains all properties of standard RPA for practically all situations of spontaneously broken symmetries. A simpler approximate form of SCRPA, the so-called renormalised RPA, also has these properties. The SCRPA equations are first outlined as an eigenvalue problem, but it is also shown how an equivalent many body Green's function approach can be formulated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach, which corrects for the quasi-boson approximation in standard RPA by using correlated occupation numbers, preserves the energy-weighted sum rule and the appearance of Goldstone (zero) modes for systems with spontaneously broken symmetries, provided the symmetry operator has no diagonal matrix elements in the single-particle basis.

## Method

The authors derive SCRPA from an equation-of-motion approach, minimizing a normalized energy-weighted sum rule to obtain an eigenvalue problem with the same mathematical structure as standard RPA but with matrices A and B evaluated using the correlated ground state. They express the single-particle occupation numbers and two-body correlation functions in terms of the RPA amplitudes X and Y, establishing self-consistency, and also introduce a simplified version called renormalised RPA (r-RPA) that retains only the correlated occupation numbers while discarding two-body correlations. The formalism is applied to the Hubbard model and a three-level pairing model to test the appearance of Goldstone modes.

## Key numbers

* For the half-filled 6-site Hubbard chain, the SCRPA ground state energy shows a maximum error of about 0.8 percent at U/t = 3.5, compared to the exact solution.
* The paper reports that SCRPA yields good agreement with the exact excitation spectrum for the half-filled 6-site chain at momentum transfer |q| = pi, with similar quality for |q| = 2pi/3 and pi/3.
* For the 2-site Hubbard molecule at half filling, SCRPA solves the problem exactly.

## Context

This work extends the Self-Consistent RPA formalism previously developed by the authors and others (Delion 2005, Dukelsky 1990, Hirsch 2002, Catara 1996, Stoyanov 2003) to cases with continuously broken symmetries, addressing a known defect of standard RPA where fermion occupation numbers are uncorrelated despite RPA describing ground-state correlations. The approach contrasts with the Phi-derivable functional method of Kadanoff and Baym (1961), which is difficult to implement numerically beyond the lowest order. The results enable future work on extending the theory to include two-body sectors in the RPA operator for finite superfluid systems where the particle-number symmetry operator has diagonal elements.
