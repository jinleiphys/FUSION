# Symmetry and Surface Symmetry Energies in Finite Nuclei

> A study of properties of the symmetry energy of nuclei is presented based on density functional theory. Calculations for finite nuclei are given so that the study includes isospin dependent surface symmetry considerations as well as isospin independent surface effects. Calculations are done at both zero and non-zero temperature. It is shown that the surface symmetry energy term is the most sensitive to the temperature while the bulk energy term is the least sensitive. It is also shown that the temperature dependence terms are insensitive to the force used and even more insensitive to the existence of neutron skin. Results for a symmetry energy with both volume and surface terms are compared with a symmetry energy with only volume terms along the line of $β$ stability. Differences of several MeV are shown over a good fraction of the total mass range in $A$. Also given are calculations for the bulk, surface and Coulomb terms.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the surface symmetry energy term is the most sensitive to temperature while the bulk energy term is the least sensitive, but this temperature dependence is insensitive to the specific Skyrme force used and even more insensitive to the existence of a neutron skin (difference between proton and neutron radii).

## Method

The authors used density functional theory with a Skyrme Hamiltonian to calculate the energy of finite nuclei along the beta stability line from 20Ne to 208Pb. They minimized the Helmholtz free energy for each nucleus at temperatures from 0 to 3 MeV using a Fermi function density distribution with equal proton and neutron radii, then extracted the coefficients of the Weizsacker mass formula expansion including volume symmetry and surface symmetry terms. Two Skyrme interactions were compared: SKM(m*=m) with no effective mass and SLy4 with a density-dependent effective mass of m*/m = 0.7.

## Key numbers

- For SKM(m*=m) at T=0: bulk coefficient B = 15.310 MeV, surface coefficient E_S = 18.303 MeV, volume symmetry coefficient S_V = 19.685 MeV, surface symmetry coefficient S_S = -33.176 MeV
- For SLy4 at T=0: B = 15.308 MeV, E_S = 20.008 MeV, S_V = 31.113 MeV, S_S = -41.035 MeV
- The T^2 coefficient for the surface symmetry term is -2.055 MeV for SKM(m*=m) and -2.595 MeV for SLy4, compared to the bulk T^2 coefficient of -0.0394 MeV and -0.0115 MeV respectively
- At A = 100 and T = 0, the SLy4 interaction gives a total symmetry energy of 43.09 MeV, while a pure volume calculation with S_V = 24 MeV gives 46.44 MeV
- For 208Pb with SLy4, the central density ranges from 0.14286 fm^-3 at T=0 to 0.12519 fm^-3 at T=5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the liquid droplet model of Myers and Swiatecki (1966) and the Weizsacker mass formula by including explicit temperature dependence of the symmetry energy coefficients. The study builds on prior calculations of the surface symmetry energy at zero temperature by various authors (2000-2005) and complements investigations of isospin fractionization in nuclear phase transitions. The results enable better extrapolation of the symmetry energy to neutron star conditions where only the volume term is important, and provide temperature-dependent coefficients for modeling rare isotope beam experiments and heavy ion collisions.
