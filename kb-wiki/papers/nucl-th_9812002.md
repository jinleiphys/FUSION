# Dirac Structure of the Nucleus-Nucleus Potential in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate nuclear matter properties in the relativistic Brueckner approach. The in-medium on-shell T-matrix is represented covariantly by five Lorentz invariant amplitudes from which we deduce directly the nucleon self-energy. To enforce correct Hartree-Fock results we develop a subtraction scheme which treats the bare nucleon-nucleon potential exactly in accordance to the different types of meson exchanges. For the higher order correlations we employ two different covariant representations in order to study the uncertainty inherent in the approach. The nuclear matter bulk properties are only slightly sensitive on the explicit representation used. However, we obtain new Coester lines for the various Bonn potentials which are shifted towards the empirical region of saturation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that by separating the leading-order single-meson exchange from the full T-matrix and treating the remaining ladder kernel with either a pseudo-scalar or pseudo-vector representation, the ambiguity in the Dirac structure of the nucleus-nucleus potential is narrowed, yielding new Coester lines for the Bonn potentials that are shifted closer to the empirical saturation region of nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Thompson equation for the in-medium T-matrix in the ladder approximation, using the Bonn A, B, and C one-boson exchange potentials as the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. They develop a subtraction scheme that treats the bare potential exactly according to meson type (pseudo-vector for pion and eta, pseudo-scalar for sigma, omega, rho, delta) and then represent the remaining subtracted T-matrix (the higher-order ladder correlations) either with a pseudo-scalar or a complete pseudo-vector set of five Lorentz-invariant amplitudes. The nucleon self-energy is extracted from the covariant T-matrix via trace formulas, and the nuclear matter equation of state is computed self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density, the binding energy per particle differs by only 0.5 MeV between the pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector representations of the subtracted T-matrix.
- For Bonn A, the compression modulus is K = 230 MeV, which agrees with the empirical value of K = 210 ± 30 MeV.
- For Bonn B, the compression modulus is K ≈ 150 MeV; for Bonn C, K ≈ 115 MeV.
- The two representation methods lead to a variation of 0.1–0.2 fm⁻¹ in the Fermi momentum at saturation and about 30 MeV in the effective mass at saturation density.
- The incompressibility values from the two representations differ by less than 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach of Horowitz and Serot (1987) and Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) by addressing the ambiguity in the covariant representation of the on-shell T-matrix. The authors previously found (Fuchs et al., 1998) that the momentum dependence of the nucleon self-energy is dominated by one-pion exchange, which motivated the separate treatment of the pion contribution. The new Coester lines obtained here are shifted toward the empirical saturation region compared to the earlier results of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990), and the refined treatment brings the more realistic Bonn B potential closer to the empirical region. The remaining uncertainty band is now much narrower, but the authors note that the ambiguity is not fully resolved and suggest that off-shell T-matrix elements may provide further insight.
