# Low-energy neutron-deuteron reactions with N3LO chiral forces

> We solve three-nucleon Faddeev equations with nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces derived consistently in the framework of chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. In this first investigation we include only matrix elements of the three-nucleon force for partial waves with the total two-nucleon (three-nucleon) angular momenta up to 3 (5/2). Low-energy neutron-deuteron elastic scattering and deuteron breakup reaction are studied. Emphasis is put on Ay puzzle in elastic scattering and cross sections in symmetric-space-star and neutron-neutron quasi-free-scattering breakup configurations, for which large discrepancies between data and theory have been reported.

## Key claim

The full chiral N3LO three-nucleon force, when combined with the N3LO chiral nucleon-nucleon potential, does not resolve the low-energy Ay puzzle in neutron-deuteron elastic scattering; instead it lowers the maximum of Ay, increasing the discrepancy with data, contrary to the partial improvement seen with standard phenomenological three-nucleon force models.

## Method

The authors solved three-nucleon Faddeev equations for neutron-deuteron elastic scattering and breakup using a nuclear Hamiltonian consisting of the N3LO chiral nucleon-nucleon potential (two cut-off sets: 450/500 MeV and 450/700 MeV) and the full N3LO chiral three-nucleon force, including all long-range, intermediate-range, and short-range components. The two low-energy constants cD and cE in the three-nucleon force were fixed by requiring reproduction of the triton binding energy and the doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length 2and. Calculations were restricted to partial waves with total two-nucleon angular momenta up to 3 and total three-nucleon angular momenta up to 5/2 for the three-nucleon force, and incoming neutron laboratory energies below 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The triton binding energy was used to fix pairs of (cD, cE); for the 201 (450/500 MeV cut-off) N3LO potential, the values that also reproduce 2and are (cD=13.78, cE=0.372); for the 204 (450/700 MeV cut-off) potential, (cD=9.095, cE=-0.0845).
- A pole in the doublet scattering length was found for the 201 potential at cD approximately -8, indicating an excited state of the three-nucleon Hamiltonian.
- The N2LO values of cD and cE for the same cut-off sets are (cD=-0.14, cE=-0.319) for 450/500 MeV and (cD=2.43, cE=0.113) for 450/700 MeV.
- At Elab,n = 13 MeV, the symmetric-space-star breakup cross section with the full N3LO force lies only slightly below the pure nucleon-nucleon prediction, showing small three-nucleon force effects.
- For the symmetric-space-star configuration at 13 MeV, nd data lie approximately 20% above theory, while pd data lie approximately 10% below theory and approximately 30% below nd data.

## Context

This work extends the consistent chiral perturbation theory approach to three-nucleon reactions from N2LO (Epelbaum 2002) to N3LO, using the N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials of Epelbaum (2005) and Machleidt (2011) together with the N3LO three-nucleon force derived by Bernard (2008, 2011). It addresses the longstanding Ay puzzle in elastic nd scattering and discrepancies in symmetric-space-star and neutron-neutron quasi-free-scattering breakup configurations that could not be resolved with standard phenomenological forces such as AV18, CD Bonn, or Nijmegen potentials combined with TM99 or Urbana IX three-nucleon force models. The results enable future studies with N4LO three-nucleon forces and suggest that the missing spin-isospin-momentum structures or the Delta(1232) resonance contributions, which enter at higher chiral orders, may be needed to resolve the remaining discrepancies.
