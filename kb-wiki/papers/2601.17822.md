# The role of the surface energy in nuclear octupole excitations

> Octupole excitations of atomic nuclei can be viewed as fluctuations around an equilibrium shape. These fluctuations in turn can be seen as probes of nuclear matter properties to the extent that the shape changes explore changes in compression, surface to volume ratio, or isospin overlap. In the present work we use a series of Skyrme interactions, which were fitted to provide a systematic range of surface energies, to explore the surface energy dependence of octupole excitations in $^{208}$Pb. We find a strong positive linear corelation between the surface energy of a Skyrme interaction and its prediction of the first $3^-$ octupole excitation energy.

# The role of the surface energy in nuclear octupole excitations

## Key claim
The paper finds a strong positive linear correlation between the surface energy coefficient of a Skyrme interaction and its prediction of the first 3⁻ octupole excitation energy in ²⁰⁸Pb, but the authors caution that the experimental value (2614 keV) is substantially lower than any of their calculated values, and that other systematic effects in the Skyrme fits must be taken into account before extrapolating.

## Method
The authors used time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) via the Sky3d code with eight Skyrme interactions from the SLy5sX series (X=1…8), which were fitted to have systematically varied surface energies while holding other nuclear matter properties constant. For each interaction, they generated a Hartree-Fock ground state of ²⁰⁸Pb, applied an instantaneous octupole boost of the form exp(ikr³Y₃₀) to each single-particle wave function, evolved the system in time, and extracted the octupole excitation energy from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent octupole operator response.

## Key numbers
- The eight SLy5sX forces have surface energies a_surf^(HF) ranging from 17.55 MeV (SLy5s1) to 18.89 MeV (SLy5s8).
- The corresponding calculated 3⁻ excitation energies in ²⁰⁸Pb range from 3.122 MeV (SLy5s1) to 3.323 MeV (SLy5s8).
- The linear fit between surface energy and excitation energy is given by E_3⁻ = 0.1506 a_surf^(HF) + 0.4812 MeV.
- The experimental value for the first 3⁻ energy in ²⁰⁸Pb is 2614 keV, which is lower than all calculated values.
- The boost strength used was k = 0.0001 fm⁻³ to remain in the linear RPA limit.

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of surface energy variation in Skyrme interactions developed by Jodon et al. (2016) and Islam et al. (2014), applying it for the first time to shape vibrations. The authors chose ²⁰⁸Pb because it is doubly-magic and spherical, with a well-studied first excited octupole state (Kibedi and Spear, 2002). The results suggest that octupole vibrations probe the nuclear surface energy because shape fluctuations change the surface-to-volume ratio, and the linear correlation found could provide a low-cost way to include pseudodata in future fits of effective interactions. The authors propose extending this study to other nuclei and other shape vibrations, such as quadrupole vibrations in spherical even-even nuclei (Verney et al., 2016).
