# Scattering in a Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics

> A Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics is discussed. Representations of the Hilbert space inner product and Poincaré generators are all expressed in terms of Euclidean space-time variables. The formulation does not require analytic continuation and can be used to directly calculate scattering observables. A toy model is used to demonstrate the feasibility of performing scattering calculations using the suggested computational methods.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that scattering observables can be computed directly in a Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics without analytic continuation, using a toy model with a separable potential to show convergence of the method to better than 1% error for momenta between 50 MeV and 2 GeV, provided the wave packet width, polynomial degree, and Euclidean time parameter are chosen in the correct order.

## Method
The authors construct a Hilbert space and Poincaré generators entirely from Euclidean Green functions and Euclidean space-time variables, using reflection positivity to ensure a positive-definite inner product. They then apply the invariance principle to replace real-time evolution with polynomial approximations of \(e^{-\beta H}\), which in Euclidean space corresponds to simple time translations. The method is tested on an exactly solvable separable potential model with a pion-exchange range and Deuteron-like binding energy, extracting sharp-momentum transition matrix elements from S-matrix elements using narrow wave packets, Chebyshev polynomial expansions, and the Cook condition to verify convergence.

## Key numbers
- The toy model potential uses a range set by the pion mass \(m_\pi\) and a strength \(\lambda\) chosen to produce a bound state with the binding energy of a Deuteron.
- Convergence was achieved for momenta between 50 MeV and 2 GeV, with better than 1% error in the scattering amplitude.
- The \(n\) values (number of Euclidean time steps) were a few hundred, with polynomial degree slightly higher, and \(\beta\) chosen so that \(\beta E\) was of order unity.
- Table 1 shows 21 sharp-momentum transition matrix elements from \(k0 = 0.05\) to \(2.0\) (units not specified but context implies GeV), with percent errors all between 0.0952% and 0.0997%.
- At \(k0 = 0.05\): Real T = 2.18499e-1, Im T = -1.03160e+0, error = 0.0982%.
- At \(k0 = 1.0\): Real T = -4.85708e-4, Im T = -4.37769e-6, error = 0.0967%.
- At \(k0 = 2.0\): Real T = -3.20000e-5, Im T = -3.80004e-8, error = 0.0966%.

## Context
This work extends the Euclidean reconstruction program of Osterwalder and Schrader (1970s) by showing that scattering calculations can be performed without analytic continuation, using only Euclidean Green functions and the invariance principle of Simon (1970s). It directly addresses the difficulty of formulating controlled approximations that preserve relativistic invariance in strong-interaction regimes, as noted by Coester (prior work on cluster properties). The main open challenge is incorporating realistic dynamics: the Euclidean Green functions must be computed or modeled, and a structure theorem for reflection-positive distributions is needed to identify acceptable models, particularly for QCD where cluster properties and reflection positivity are limited to local color singlets.
