# Radiative corrections to the forward-backward asymmetry in $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-$

> We present a calculation of the $C$-odd radiative corrections to $e^+e^-\to π^+π^-$ in a dispersive formalism, concentrating on the leading pion-pole contribution in the virtual box diagrams. In particular, we show how the effect of a general pion vector form factor in the loop integral can be incorporated in a model-independent way and how the cancellation of infrared singularities proceeds in this case. The numerical results, dominated by the infrared enhanced contributions, indicate significant corrections beyond scalar QED, essentially confirming recent findings in generalized vector-meson-dominance models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent dispersive calculation of the C-odd radiative corrections to the forward-backward asymmetry in \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\), showing that the dominant corrections arise from infrared-enhanced contributions and that the naive approach of multiplying the scalar-QED result by the squared pion vector form factor is a poor approximation, while the results confirm recent findings in generalized vector-meson-dominance models.

## Method

The authors use fixed-s dispersion relations to isolate the pion-pole contribution in the virtual box diagrams, expressing the virtual correction in terms of scalar loop functions \(C_0\) and \(D_0\). They decompose the correction into pole-pole, pole-dispersive, and dispersive-dispersive contributions, where the pion vector form factor enters the loop integrals through a dispersive representation that replaces the photon propagators. The infrared singularities are regularized with a photon mass \(\lambda\) and cancel against the real emission contribution, with the cancellation requiring a subtle interplay between the real and imaginary parts of the mixed pole-dispersive correction.

## Context

This work extends the scalar-QED calculation of the forward-backward asymmetry by Arbuzov (2020) and addresses the observation by Ignatov (2022) that multiplying the point-like result by \(|F_\pi^V|^2\) was insufficient to describe preliminary CMD-3 data, which motivated a generalized vector-meson-dominance approach. The dispersive formalism provides a model-independent foundation for these corrections, enabling improved treatment of radiative corrections in precision measurements of \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\) that are critical for the data-driven determination of hadronic vacuum polarization and the muon \(g-2\) anomaly.
