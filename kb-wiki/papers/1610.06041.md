# SU(3) Polyakov linear-sigma model: Conductivity and viscous properties of QCD matter in thermal medium

> In mean field approximation, the grand canonical potential of SU(3) Polyakov linear-$σ$ model (PLSM) is analysed for chiral phase-transition, $σ_l$ and $σ_s$ and for deconfinement order-parameters, $ϕ$ and $ϕ^*$ of light- and strange-quarks, respectively. Various PLSM parameters are determined from the assumption of global minimization of the real part of the potential. Then, we have calculated the subtracted condensates ($Δ_{l,s}$). All these results are compared with recent lattice QCD simulations. Accordingly, essential PLSM parameters are determined. The modelling of the relaxation time is utilized in estimating the conductivity properties of the QCD matter in thermal medium, namely electric [$σ_{el}(T)$] and heat [$κ(T)$] conductivities. We found that the PLSM results on the electric conductivity and on the specific heat agree well with the available lattice QCD calculations. Also, we have calculated bulk and shear viscosities normalized to the thermal entropy, $ξ/s$ and $η/s$, respectively, and compared them with recent lattice QCD. Predictions for $(ξ/s)/(σ_{el}/T)$ and $(η/s)/(σ_{el}/ T)$ are introduced. We conclude that our results on various transport properties show some essential ingredients, that these properties likely come up with, in studying QCD matter in thermal and dense medium.

## Key claim

The SU(3) Polyakov linear-sigma model (PLSM), when combined with a suitable modeling of the relaxation time, can reproduce lattice QCD results for electric conductivity and specific heat, and provide predictions for bulk and shear viscosities normalized to entropy, but only under the assumption of global equilibrium and at vanishing baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The grand canonical potential of the SU(3) PLSM was analyzed in mean field approximation to determine chiral condensates (σ_l, σ_s) and deconfinement order parameters (φ, φ*) for light and strange quarks, using both logarithmic and polynomial-logarithmic Polyakov-loop potentials. The relaxation time was modeled from the thermal average of elastic scattering cross sections assuming a T⁻² dependence for the cross section, and then used within the relaxation time approximation to calculate electric conductivity σ_el(T), heat conductivity κ(T), and bulk and shear viscosities (ξ, η) normalized to entropy density s.

## Key numbers

- Chiral critical temperature for light quarks: T_χ^l = 176.0 MeV (from peak of ∂σ_l/∂T)
- Chiral critical temperature for strange quarks: T_χ^s = 286.0 MeV (from peak of ∂σ_s/∂T)
- Deconfinement critical temperature from logarithmic Polyakov potential: T_χ^{φ_Log} = 96.0 MeV
- Deconfinement critical temperature from polynomial-logarithmic Polyakov potential: T_χ^{φ_PolyLog} = 186.0 MeV
- Light constituent quark mass: m_l ≈ 300 MeV (from m_l = g σ_l/2 with g = 6.5)
- Strange constituent quark mass: m_s ≈ 433 MeV (from m_s = g σ_s/√2 with g = 6.5)
- Vacuum chiral condensates: σ_{l0} = 92.4 MeV, σ_{s0} = 94.5 MeV
- PLSM parameters at m_σ = 800 MeV: c = 4807.84 MeV, h_l = (120.73)³ MeV³, h_s = (336.41)³ MeV³, m² = -(306.26)² MeV², λ₁ = 13.49, λ₂ = 46.48

## Context

This work extends previous PLSM calculations of chiral phase structure and meson masses by Tawfik et al. (2014, 2015, 2016) to transport properties, and compares with lattice QCD simulations using asqtad and p4 improved staggered fermion actions from Orginos et al. (2009) and Heller et al. (1999). The results enable future studies of QCD matter transport coefficients in dense medium and finite electromagnetic fields, building on prior work using PNJL (Bratkovskaya) and DQPM (Bratkovskaya, 2014) approaches.
