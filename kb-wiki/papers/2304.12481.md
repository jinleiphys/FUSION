# Double Parton Distributions from Euclidean Lattice

> We show that double parton distributions, which are important in describing double parton scattering processes in hadron collisions, can be directly computed from correlations of equal-time nonlocal Euclidean operators on the lattice in the large hadron momentum limit. We demonstrate this by taking the unpolarized color singlet quark double parton distribution as an example, and present a factorization formula connecting the corresponding lightcone and Euclidean correlations. This opens a new possibility of studying multiparton interactions on the Euclidean lattice.

# Double Parton Distributions from Euclidean Lattice

## Key claim
Double parton distributions can be directly computed from correlations of equal-time nonlocal Euclidean operators on the lattice in the large hadron momentum limit, with the difference between Euclidean and lightcone correlators being perturbatively calculable due to asymptotic freedom. The derivation assumes that renormalization has been performed for each operator separately and converted to the MSbar scheme, and that power-suppressed terms from the operator product expansion can be neglected.

## Method
The author derives a factorization formula connecting the Euclidean correlator of two equal-time nonlocal quark bilinear operators (with spatial separations and gamma^z Dirac structure) to the lightcone correlator defining the unpolarized color singlet quark double parton distribution. The derivation uses the operator product expansion for each spatial nonlocal operator, matching the double Mellin moments of the Euclidean and lightcone correlators, and expresses the relation in both coordinate space and momentum space (analogous to pseudo-distribution and quasi-distribution approaches for single parton distributions). The hard coefficients are perturbatively calculable and have been computed at one-loop order in prior work.

## Key numbers
- The one-loop hard coefficient takes the form: C_{q_i}(u_i, mu_i^2 z_i^2) = delta(1-u_i)(1 + (3/2)(alpha_s C_F/(2pi)) L_i) - (alpha_s C_F/(2pi)) ((1+u_i^2)/(1-u_i))_+ L_i + rem., where L_i = ln(-mu_i^2 z_i^2 / (4 e^{-2 gamma_E}))
- The lowest double Mellin moment with n1=n2=1 has been calculated for the nucleon and the pion in prior lattice work (Bali 2020, Bali 2021)

## Context
This work extends the large-momentum effective theory approach (Ji 2013, Ji 2014) from single parton distributions to double parton distributions, which are much less known than single parton distributions. Prior lattice calculations of double parton distributions were limited to the lowest double Mellin moment (Bali 2020, Bali 2021), while phenomenological extractions from experiments at the LHC, Tevatron, and CERN ISR have required model assumptions such as parton independence. The approach enables direct computation of the x-dependence of double parton distributions from first-principles lattice QCD, and can be generalized to transverse-momentum-dependent double parton distributions and general multiparton physics.
