# Coulomb corrections in quasi-elastic scattering based on the eikonal expansion for electron wave functions

> An eikonal expansion is developed in order to provide systematic corrections to the eikonal approximation through order 1/k^2, where k is the wave number. The expansion is applied to wave functions for the Klein-Gordon equation and for the Dirac equation with a Coulomb potential. Convergence is rapid at energies above about 250 MeV. Analytical results for the eikonal wave functions are obtained for a simple analytical form of the Coulomb potential of a nucleus. They are used to investigate distorted-wave matrix elements for quasi-elastic electron scattering from a nucleus. Focusing factors are shown to arise from the corrections to the eikonal approximation. A precise form of the effective-momentum approximation is developed by use of a momentum shift that depends on the electron's energy loss.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic eikonal expansion through order 1/k² for electron wave functions in a Coulomb potential, showing that convergence is rapid at energies above about 250 MeV, and that focusing factors arise naturally from the corrections rather than requiring separate WKB-based treatment.

## Method

The authors iteratively solve the Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations for electron wave functions in a Coulomb potential by expanding the complex eikonal phase in powers of 1/k, where k is the electron wave number. They obtain analytical results for the eikonal phases using a simple analytical form of the nuclear Coulomb potential V(r) = -αZ/√(r²+R²), and apply these distorted waves to compute matrix elements for quasi-elastic electron scattering from a nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Convergence is rapid at energies above about 250 MeV
- For a 500 MeV electron scattering from ²⁰⁸Pb, the Coulomb potential at the nucleus center is approximately V(0) = 25 MeV
- The leading-order eikonal phase χ₀ ≈ 3Zα ≈ 2 for ²⁰⁸Pb
- The second-order correction χ₂ ≈ 0.0025 χ₀ ≈ 0.005 for 500 MeV electrons on ²⁰⁸Pb
- The focusing factor at r=0 for Dirac waves is approximately f^(D±)(0) ≈ 1 - V(0)/E
- For Klein-Gordon waves, the focusing factor at r=0 is approximately f^(KG±)(0) ≈ 1 - V(0)/(2E)
- The electron mass m = 0.511 MeV, so for 500 MeV electrons k ≈ E and v ≈ 1 within a part per million
- Helicity conservation holds to about one part per thousand for a 500 MeV electron

## Context

This work extends the eikonal approximation for electron scattering, which was used by Glauber (1959) and by Czyz and Gottfried (1963), and provides systematic corrections that earlier approaches lacked. The authors show that focusing factors derived by Yennie, Boos, and Ravenhall (1965) using WKB approximation arise naturally from the eikonal expansion. The work enables a precise formulation of the effective-momentum approximation (EMA) used by Traini (1988, 2001), with a momentum shift that depends on the electron's energy loss, and addresses disagreements in nuclear response function determinations noted by Morgenstern (2001) and Benhar (2006).
