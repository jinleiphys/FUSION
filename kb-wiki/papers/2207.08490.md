# The $X(3960)$ seen in $D_{s}^{+} D_{s}^{-}$ as the $X(3930)$ state seen in $ D^{+} D^{-} $

> We perform a calculation of the interaction of the $ D \bar{D} $, $ D_{s} \bar{D}_{s} $ coupled channels and find two bound states, one coupling to $ D \bar{D} $ and another one at higher energies coupling mostly to $D_{s}^{+} D_{s}^{-}$. We identify this latter state with the $X_{0}(3930)$ seen in the $D^{+} D^{-}$ mass distribution in the $B^+ \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{+} $ decay, and also show that it produces an enhancement of the $D_{s}^{+} D_{s}^{-}$ mass distribution close to threshold which is compatible with the LHCb recent observation in the $B^+ \to D_{s}^{+} D_{s}^{-} K^{+} $ decay which has been identified as a new state, $X_{0}(3960)$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the recently observed $X(3960)$ state in $D_{s}^{+} D_{s}^{-}$ is not a new resonance but rather the same state as the $X_{0}(3930)$ seen in $D^{+} D^{-}$, provided the transition potential between the $D\bar{D}$ and $D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}$ channels is weakened to about 70% of its nominal strength.

## Method

The authors compute the $S$-wave interaction of the $D\bar{D}$ and $D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}$ coupled channels using vector-meson exchange from the local hidden gauge approach, with the potential unitarized via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. They then calculate the $D^{+}D^{-}$ and $D_{s}^{+}D_{s}^{-}$ mass distributions in $B^{-} \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{-}$ and $B^{-} \to D_{s}^{+} D_{s}^{-} K^{-}$ decays, where the $c\bar{c}$ pair from the weak decay hadronizes into a superposition of $D\bar{D}$ and $D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}$ that undergo final-state interaction.

## Key numbers

- The $X_{0}(3930)$ state has mass $M'_{0}=3924 \pm 2$ MeV and width $\Gamma'_{0}=17 \pm 5$ MeV from $B^{+} \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{+}$ decays.
- The LHCb $X(3960)$ signal has mass $M_{0}=3955 \pm 6 \pm 11$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{0}=48 \pm 17 \pm 10$ MeV.
- The $D_{s}^{+} D_{s}^{-}$ threshold is at 3937 MeV.
- With subtraction constant $a_{D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}}=-1.58$ and transition strength factor $\alpha=0.7$, the model produces a pole (Pole II) at 3936 MeV with width 11 MeV, identified as the $X_{0}(3930)$, coupling to $D\bar{D}$ with $|g|=2858$ MeV and to $D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}$ with $|g|=9076$ MeV.
- A lower pole (Pole I) at 3699 MeV couples more strongly to $D\bar{D}$ ($|g|=14516$ MeV) than to $D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}$ ($|g|=5897$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical study of $D\bar{D}$ and $D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}$ coupled channels from Dani et al. (2019), which found only a $D\bar{D}$ bound state and no state near the $D_{s}\bar{D}_{s}$ threshold. The authors note that lattice QCD results from Prelovsek et al. (2021) found a $0^{++}$ bound state coupling strongly to $D_{s}^{+}D_{s}^{-}$ and weakly to $D^{+}D^{-}$, which motivates the present calculation. The conclusion that the $X(3960)$ signal is actually the $X_{0}(3930)$ eliminates the need to invoke a new resonance and can be tested by further experimental studies of the $D_{s}^{+}D_{s}^{-}$ line shape.
