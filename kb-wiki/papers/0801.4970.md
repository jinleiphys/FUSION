# Hadrons in Medium -- Theory meets Experiment

> In this talk I give a short review of theoretical results on the properties of hadrons in cold, equilibrium nuclear matter. I then discuss the observable consequences of any changes of these properties inside the medium in actual experiments. I demonstrate that any experimental verification of in-medium effects requires a state-of-the-art treatment of the reaction dynamics and, in particular, also the final state interactions.

# Hadrons in Medium -- Theory meets Experiment

## Key claim
In-medium modifications of hadron properties (masses, widths) are not directly observable; any experimental verification requires a state-of-the-art treatment of reaction dynamics and final state interactions, and the observed spectral function is a product of the primary production cross section, the spectral function itself, and the partial decay probability.

## Method
The author reviews self-consistent calculations of in-medium selfenergies for pions, rho mesons, eta mesons, and omega mesons in cold nuclear matter, generated by nucleon-hole and resonance-hole excitations with short-range correlations. These theoretical results are then compared to experimental data from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration using the GiBUU semiclassical transport code, which provides a full event analysis including final state interactions and background contributions.

## Key numbers
- For the rho meson, the low-density approximation breaks down at about 0.3 rho_0 (where rho_0 is nuclear saturation density).
- The omega meson selfenergy extracted from omega-N scattering gives a broadening of about 60 MeV at rho_0 and a small upward shift of the peak mass.
- The CBELSA/TAPS data on omega photoproduction can be explained if a lowering of the omega meson mass in medium by about 16 percent is assumed together with collisional broadening.
- For phi mesons, inelastic cross sections about a factor 3 larger than theoretically expected are needed to explain transparency data from SPRING8.

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions by Bernard and Meissner (NJL model), Brown and Rho, and Hatsuda and Lee on vector meson mass dropping in medium, as well as calculations by Klingl and collaborators on scalar 2pi strength. The author's self-consistent approach solves the self-consistency problem for meson selfenergies for the first time, and the GiBUU transport code incorporates 20 years of experience from Giessen group simulations. The analysis of TAPS 2pi0 data shows that pion rescattering effects, rather than chiral symmetry restoration, can explain the observed lowering of scalar strength, contradicting earlier interpretations by the Valencia group and the Lyon group. The results enable future experimental searches for in-medium mass shifts to properly account for production and decay distortions.
