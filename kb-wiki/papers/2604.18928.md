# A novel approach to proton-boron-11 fusion

> Proton-boron-11 (p-$^{11}$B) fusion is a highly attractive aneutronic pathway for clean energy production, offering abundant fuel, negligible neutron activation, and the potential for direct energy conversion of charged $α$ particles. However, its practical implementation is severely hindered by the extremely high Coulomb barrier, necessitating ignition temperatures far beyond those of conventional deuterium-tritium reactions. In this work, we propose a novel approach to enhance the low-energy fusion cross-section by introducing a negative muon ($μ$). Instead of relying on the thermal equilibrium formation of a muonic molecule, we investigate a kinetic scenario in which a muonic hydrogen atom (p$μ$) is formed first and subsequently bombarded with a $^{11}$B nucleus. We quantitatively characterize the dynamic screening of the proton's Coulomb field by the tightly bound $μ$ cloud, the resulting modified Coulomb potential substantially lowers the effective barrier at intermediate separations. We also evaluate the penetrability, reaction cross-section, and reactivity of the p$μ$-$^{11}$B system, the results indicate that the inclusion of $μ$ enhances the tunneling probability by several orders of magnitude at incident energies below 100~keV, thereby significantly reducing the threshold for the nuclear reaction. This mechanism offers a promising alternative perspective for catalyzing p-$^{11}$B fusion, and also suggests a potential ignition pathway.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a negative muon, bound to a proton to form a muonic hydrogen atom (pμ) which is then bombarded with a ¹¹B nucleus, can enhance the p-¹¹B fusion tunneling probability by several orders of magnitude at incident energies below 100 keV, but the abstract omits the crucial qualifier that this enhancement becomes negligible above approximately 100 keV and does not extend to the dominant resonance region around 148 keV or to the 200–300 keV energy window relevant for magnetic confinement fusion.

## Method

The authors treat the muon as a static 1s screening cloud around the proton and derive a distance-dependent effective charge and modified Coulomb potential experienced by the incident ¹¹B nucleus. They calculate the minimum approach distance between p and ¹¹B as a function of incident energy, then evaluate the tunneling probability using the WKB approximation for energies below 33.5 keV and an Airy-function matching procedure for energies between 33.5 keV and 107.43 keV. The reaction cross-section is obtained by multiplying the tunneling probability by a high-precision empirical S-factor fit to experimental data, and the thermonuclear reactivity is computed by integrating the cross-section over a Maxwellian velocity distribution.

## Key numbers

* The first Bohr radius of the muon is a_μ ≈ 284.6 fm.
* The sum of the nuclear radii of p and ¹¹B used as the inner turning point is r_n = 3.284 fm.
* The WKB approximation is considered reliable for action S ≥ 10, which corresponds to incident energy E ≤ 33.50 keV; the action drops to S = 5 at E = 113.16 keV and to S = 1 at E = 658.86 keV.
* The Airy approximation tunneling probability intersects the bare-nucleus tunneling probability at E = 107.43 keV; above this energy the muon is taken to provide no enhancement.
* The S-factor fit includes a resonance at E_L = 148 keV with width δE_L = 2.35 keV and amplitude A_L = 1.82×10⁴ MeV·b.
* The reaction rate enhancement from muon screening is significant below 10 keV but decays rapidly; beyond 40 keV the rate shows no significant enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the concept of muon-catalyzed fusion (MCF), established for D-T systems by Frank (1947), Jones (1986), and Breunlich (1989), to the aneutronic p-¹¹B reaction, which has been studied extensively by Margarone (2022), Liu (2023), Magee (2023), and others. The paper addresses the fundamental difficulty noted by Schaller (1993) that boron's high nuclear charge (Z=5) creates a "muon trap" that renders standard thermal MCF ineffective; the proposed kinetic scenario of pre-forming pμ and then bombarding it with ¹¹B avoids this trapping problem. The results suggest that muon-assisted barrier mitigation could serve as a potential ignition mechanism for advanced aneutronic fuels, though the enhancement is confined to sub-100-keV energies and does not reach the 200–300 keV window relevant for magnetic confinement fusion systems.
