# Gluon Condensate via Dirac Spectral Density: IR Phase, Scale Anomaly and IR Decoupling

> Quark and gluon scalar densities, $\langle \barψ ψ\rangle$ and $\langle F^2 \rangle$, reflect the degree of scale-invariance violations in SU(N) gauge theories with fundamental quarks. It is known that $\langle \barψ ψ\rangle$ can be usefully scale-decomposed via spectral density $ρ(λ)$ of Dirac modes. Here I give such formula for $\langle F^2 \rangle$, which reveals that gluon condensate is a strictly UV quantity. For the recently-found IR phase [1,2], where the infrared (IR) degrees of freedom separate out and become independent of the system's bulk, it implies that $\langle F^2 \rangle$ due to this IR part vanishes. Its glue thus doesn't contribute to scale anomaly of the entire system and is, in this sense, scale invariant consistently with the original claim. Associated formulas are used to define IR decoupling of glue, which may serve as an alternative indicator of IR phase transition. Using the simplest form of coherent lattice QCD, we express the effective action of full QCD entirely via Dirac spectral density.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new formula expressing the gluon condensate \(\langle F^2 \rangle\) via the Dirac spectral density, which reveals that the gluon condensate is a strictly ultraviolet (UV) quantity; for the recently-discovered infrared (IR) phase, this implies that the IR component's glue contributes nothing to the scale anomaly of the entire system and is therefore scale invariant, consistent with the original claim of Alexandru (2019).

## Method

The author starts from a lattice QCD setup where a local gauge operator for \(F^2(x)\) is constructed from the trace of the lattice Dirac operator \(D\), using the relation \(\operatorname{Tr}_{cs} \hat{D}_{x,x}(U) - \operatorname{Tr}_{cs} \hat{D}_{x,x}(\mathbb{1}) = c_S \, a^4 \operatorname{Tr}_c F_{\mu\nu}F_{\mu\nu}(x,A) + \mathcal{O}(a^6)\). By taking the QCD average and expressing the trace of \(D\) as an integral over the surface spectral density \(\rho_s(\lambda)\) of Dirac eigenvalues in the complex plane, the author obtains \(\langle F^2 \rangle = (a/c_S) \int d\mathcal{S} \, \lambda \, \rho_s^{\text{eff}}(\lambda)\), where \(\rho_s^{\text{eff}}\) subtracts the free-field density. This formula is then specialized to the overlap Dirac operator, yielding explicit regularized expressions for both the gluon and quark condensates in terms of the spectral density along the eigenvalue circle.

## Key numbers

* The gluon condensate formula for overlap operators: \(\langle F^2 \rangle_{a,L} = \frac{a^2}{c_S \Delta} \int_0^{(2\Delta/a)^-} d\sigma \, \sigma^2 \, \rho^{\text{eff}}(\sigma, a, L) + T \frac{\langle n_0 \rangle_{a,L}}{L^3} \frac{2\Delta}{c_S}\), where \(\Delta \in (0,2)\) is the overlap parameter and \(c_S \neq 0\) is a constant.
* The quark condensate formula for overlap operators: \(-m \langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle_{a,L} = \int_{0^+}^{(2\Delta/a)^-} d\sigma \, \frac{m^2 + m^2 + a\sigma^2 m/\Delta}{m^2 + \sigma^2 + a\sigma^2 m/\Delta} \, \rho^{\text{eff}}(\sigma, a, L) + T \frac{\langle n_0 \rangle_{a,L}}{L^3} \left(1 + \frac{am}{2\Delta + am}\right)\).
* The contribution of Dirac scales up to any finite renormalized value \(\lambda_{\text{ren}}\) to the gluon condensate vanishes in the continuum limit: \(\langle F^2 \rangle_L [\lambda_{\text{ren}}] = \lim_{a \to 0} \frac{a^2 Z_S^2}{c_S \Delta} \int_0^{\lambda_{\text{ren}}} d\sigma_{\text{ren}} \, \sigma_{\text{ren}}^2 \, \rho_{\text{ren}}^{\text{eff}}(\sigma_{\text{ren}}, a, L) = 0\) for all \(0 < \lambda_{\text{ren}} < \infty\).
* The IR phase is classified by the power-law behavior \(\rho(\lambda) \propto \lambda^p\) as \(\lambda \to 0\), with \(p < 0\) for the IR phase, \(p = 0\) for the B (confined) phase, and \(p > 0\) for the UV phase.

## Context

This work extends the classification of phases in SU(3) gauge theories with fundamental quarks introduced by Alexandru (2015) and Alexandru (2019), which identified the IR phase as a regime where deep-infrared degrees of freedom separate and decouple from the bulk. The new spectral-density formula for the gluon condensate provides a field-theoretic tool to quantify the IR-bulk decoupling and to test the conjecture that the IR component's glue is scale invariant, enabling future lattice studies to use the vanishing of the IR contribution to \(\langle F^2 \rangle\) as an alternative indicator of the IR phase transition.
