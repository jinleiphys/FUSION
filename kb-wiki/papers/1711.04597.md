# Rotational bands in Quadrupole-Octupole Collective Model

> A collective bands of positive and negative parity could be composed of the vibrations and rotations. The rotations of the octupole configurations can be based either on the axial or the non-axial octupole vibrations. A consistent approach to the quadrupole-octupole collective vibrations coupled with the rotational motion enables to distinguish between various scenarios of disappearance of the E2 transitions in negative-parity bands. The here presented theoretical estimates are compared with the recent experimental energies and transition probabilities in and between the ground-state and low-energy negaive-parity bands in $^{156}$Dy. A realistic collective Hamiltonian contains the potential energy term obtained through the macroscopic-microscopic Strutinsky-like method with particle-number-projected BCS approach and deformation-dependent mass tensor defined in vibrational-rotational, nine-dimensional collective space. The symmetrization procedure ensures the uniqueness of the Hamiltonian eigensolutions with respect to the laboratory coordinate system. This quadrupole-octupole collective approach may also allow to find and/or verify some fingerprints of possible high-order symmetries (e.g. tetrahedral, octahedral,...) in nuclear collective bands.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quadrupole-octupole collective model that can distinguish between different scenarios for the disappearance of E2 transitions in negative-parity bands, but the authors cannot definitively identify which of the three energetically close octupole one-phonon bands (axial α₃₀, non-axial α₃₁, or tetrahedral α₃₂) best reproduces the experimental "band 2" in ¹⁵⁶Dy.

## Method

The authors construct a collective Hamiltonian in a nine-dimensional vibrational-rotational space using the adiabatic approximation to separate vibrational and rotational motions, with the potential energy obtained from the macroscopic-microscopic Strutinsky method using a Woods-Saxon mean field and particle-number-projected BCS pairing. The mass tensors for quadrupole and octupole motions are calculated independently using the cranking approximation, and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a basis symmetrized with respect to the D₄y group to ensure uniqueness of eigensolutions in the laboratory frame. The collective space includes quadrupole variables (α₂₀, α₂₂) and four real octupole variables (α₃₀, α₃₁, α₃₂, α₃₃), with the dipole variables determined from the center-of-mass condition.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state quadrupole deformation of ¹⁵⁶Dy is α₂₀ = 0.25 with α₂₂ = 0.0
- The negative-parity bands built on axial α₃₀ and non-axial α₃₁ one-phonon excitations are separated in energy by about 70 keV, while the band built on the tetrahedral α₃₂ phonon lies higher by approximately 150 keV
- The mesh for quadrupole variables spans α₂ν ∈ (-1.0; 1.0) with step Δα₂ν = 0.05, and for octupole variables α₃μ ∈ (-0.3; 0.3) with step Δα₃μ = 0.1, giving about two million mesh points
- The intra-band B(E2) values in the band built on the axial α₃₀ mode are lower by approximately 40% than in the tetrahedral band
- The predicted γ-ray energy for the (5⁻ → 4⁺) transition in the α₃₀ band is 1115 keV, compared to the experimental value of 1121 keV
- The predicted γ-ray energy for the (7⁻ → 6⁺) transition in the α₃₀ band is 1098 keV, compared to the experimental value of 1039 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of ¹⁵⁶Gd (Dobrowolski, 2016) to higher spins in ¹⁵⁶Dy, addressing the question posed by experimentalists using the Gammasphere array (Har, 2017) about whether the observed negative-parity "band 2" arises from octupole vibrations or tetrahedral symmetry. The model builds on the Bohr Hamiltonian approach (Pro, 2009; Bizzeti, 2010) and the Interacting Boson Model (Eng, 1985; Eng, 1987), but uses a macroscopic-microscopic method with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop formula (KPD, 2003) for the liquid-drop energy. The approach may enable identification of fingerprints of high-order symmetries such as tetrahedral or octahedral symmetries in nuclear collective bands.
