# Anharmonic properties of double giant dipole resonance

> A systematic microscopic study of the anharmonic properties of the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) has been carried out, for the first time, for nuclei with mass number $A$ spanning the whole mass table. It is concluded that the corrections of the energy centroid of the $J^π = 0^+$ and $2^+$ components of the DGDR from its harmonic limit are negative, have a value of the order of few hundred keV and follow an $A^{-1}$ dependence.

## Key claim

The energy centroid of the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) is shifted downward from the harmonic limit by a few hundred keV, and this shift follows an A⁻¹ dependence across the mass table. The authors conclude that the "Coulomb excitation anomaly" (large enhancement of cross sections) is likely not explained by these anharmonicities and must be found elsewhere.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic microscopic calculation of the DGDR spectrum using a Hamiltonian with a mean field, monopole pairing, and separable multipole-multipole forces, diagonalized in a complete basis of one- and two-phonon states (up to 40-50 MeV excitation energy) for nuclei spanning the mass table. The one-phonon basis was obtained from the quasiparticle random phase approximation, and the two-phonon basis included states built from 0⁺, 1⁻, 2⁺, 3⁻, and 4⁺ phonons coupled to J^π = 0⁺ and 2⁺. The strength of the isovector dipole-dipole term was fixed by fitting the observed GDR energy centroid in each nucleus or using the systematics 80·A⁻¹/³ MeV.

## Key numbers

* Energy shift of the DGDR 0⁺ component in ⁴⁰Ca: -643 keV from the harmonic limit.
* Energy shift of the DGDR 2⁺ component in ⁴⁰Ca: -740 keV from the harmonic limit.
* Energy shift of the DGDR 0⁺ component in ²⁰⁸Pb: -108 keV from the harmonic limit.
* Energy shift of the DGDR 2⁺ component in ²⁰⁸Pb: -158 keV from the harmonic limit.
* Energy shift of the DGDR 0⁺ component in ¹²⁰Sn: -199 keV from the harmonic limit.
* Energy shift of the DGDR 2⁺ component in ¹²⁰Sn: -194 keV from the harmonic limit.
* From a χ² analysis weighting 0⁺ and 2⁺ components equally: ΔE = b·A⁻ᵅ with α = 1.08 ± 0.06 and b = -37 ± 8 MeV.
* The GDR in ⁴⁰Ca exhausts 104% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn EWSR; the DGDR exhausts 103%.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of DGDR anharmonicity by Catara, Chomaz, and Van Giai (1989), Nishizaki and Wambach (1995), and Lanza et al. (1997), which were limited to doubly-magic nuclei like ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb. The present systematic study across the mass table resolves the previously unclear mass-number dependence of the energy shifts, establishing an A⁻¹ behavior consistent with Pauli principle corrections estimated by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and Hamamoto (1999), and contradicting the A⁻⁵/³ dependence predicted by Bertsch and Feldmeier (1997). The results enable future work to focus on other mechanisms (such as changes in transition matrix elements or multi-step excitation paths) to explain the observed enhancement of Coulomb excitation cross sections for the DGDR.
