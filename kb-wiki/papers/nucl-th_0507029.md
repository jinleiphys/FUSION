# Interactions of the solar neutrinos with the deuterons

> Starting from chiral Lagrangians, possessing the SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R local chiral symmetry, we derive weak axial one-boson exchange currents in the leading order in the 1/M expansion (M is the nucleon mass). We apply these currents in calculations of the cross sections for the disintegration of the deuterons by the low energy neutrinos. The nuclear wave functions are derived from a variant of the OBEPQB potential and from the Nijmegen 93 and Nijmegen I nucleon-nucleon interactions. The comparison of our cross sections with those obtained within the pionless effective field theory and other potential model calculations shows that the solar neutrino-deuteron cross sections can be calculated within an accuracy of 3.3 %.

# Interactions of the solar neutrinos with the deuterons

## Key claim
Solar neutrino-deuteron cross sections can be calculated within an accuracy of approximately 3.3% when weak axial one-boson exchange currents derived from chiral Lagrangians with local SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R symmetry are combined with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, provided the Coulomb interaction between protons in the final state is treated correctly in the charged-current channel.

## Method
The authors constructed weak axial one-boson exchange currents (WAECs) of pion, rho, omega, and a1 meson range from chiral Lagrangians in the tree approximation, satisfying the nuclear form of partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC). These currents were applied to calculate cross sections for neutrino-deuteron disintegration reactions using deuteron and 1S0 final-state wave functions generated from three one-boson exchange potentials: a variant of OBEPQB (extended to include a1 exchange), Nijmegen 93, and Nijmegen I. The extracted values of the constant L1,A entering the pionless effective field theory (EFT) cross sections were obtained by least-squares fit, and the results were compared with prior potential model calculations and pionless EFT predictions.

## Key numbers
- The solar neutrino-deuteron cross sections can be calculated within an accuracy of approximately 3.3% across all channels.
- For the neutral-current reaction nu_x + d -> nu'_x + n + p, cross sections at E_nu = 10 MeV are 1.093 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (NijmI potential), with differences from EFT cross sections ranging from +1.3% at 4 MeV to -2.9% at 20 MeV.
- For the charged-current reaction nu_e + d -> e^- + p + p, cross sections at E_nu = 10 MeV are 2.655 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (NijmI), with agreement with EFT within 3.3% except at the lowest energies.
- The extracted constant L1,A for the neutral-current reaction (NCN) is 4.6 (NijmI), 5.2 (Nijm93), and 4.8 (OBEPQG); for the charged-current reaction (CCN) it is 4.1 (NijmI) and 5.0 (Nijm93).
- The deuteron binding energy used is epsilon_B = -2.2245 MeV.
- The Fermi constant used is G_F = 1.166 x 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} and the axial coupling g_A = -1.26 (for EFT comparison) or g_A = -1.254 (for comparison with NSGK and YHH).

## Context
This work extends the standard nuclear physics approach to weak axial exchange currents, building on earlier formulations by Ivanov and Truhlik (various years), Towner (various years), and the chiral Lagrangian framework of the authors' previous work (Mosconi, Ricci, Truhlik, 2005). The calculations are directly relevant to the SNO detector results (SNO Collaboration, 2001-2004) and address the 5%-10% discrepancies between prior potential model calculations by Nakamura et al. (2001) and Ying et al. (1980). The results enable more precise interpretation of solar neutrino oscillation data by providing cross sections consistent with both the standard nuclear physics approach and pionless EFT (Butler and Chen, 2000; Kong and Ravndal, 2000), while identifying the Coulomb treatment in the charged-current channel as a remaining issue for the EFT calculations.
