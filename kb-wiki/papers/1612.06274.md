# Correlated wounded hot spots in proton-proton interactions

> We investigate the effect of non-trivial spatial correlations between proton constituents, considered in this work to be gluonic hot spots, on the initial conditions of proton-proton collisions from ISR to LHC energies, i.e. $\sqrt s\!=\!52.6,7000,13000$ GeV. The inclusion of these correlations is motivated by their fundamental role in the description of a recently observed new feature of $pp$ scattering at $\sqrt s\!=\!7$ TeV, the hollowness effect. Our analysis relies on a Monte-Carlo Glauber approach including fluctuations in the hot spot positions and their entropy deposition in the transverse plane. We explore both the energy dependence and the effect of spatial correlations on the number of wounded hot spots, their spatial distribution and the eccentricities, $\varepsilon_n$, of the initial state geometry of the collision. In minimum bias collisions we find that the inclusion of short range repulsive correlations between the hot spots reduces the value of the eccentricity ($\varepsilon_2$) and the triangularity ($\varepsilon_3$). In turn, upon considering only the events with the highest entropy deposition i.e. the ultra-central ones, the probability of having larger $\varepsilon_{2,3}$ increases significantly in the correlated scenario. Finally, the eccentricities show a quite mild energy dependence.

## Key claim

The inclusion of short-range repulsive correlations between gluonic hot spots inside the proton reduces the eccentricity (ε₂) and triangularity (ε₃) in minimum-bias proton-proton collisions, but in ultra-central collisions (the 0–1% highest-entropy events) the same correlations increase the probability of larger ε₂ and ε₃ values, with the net effect depending on centrality.

## Method

A Monte-Carlo Glauber event generator was developed for proton-proton collisions, where each proton is composed of three gluonic hot spots whose positions are sampled from a distribution that includes a repulsive short-range correlation term controlled by a core radius r_c. The probability of a hot spot–hot spot collision is given by an inelasticity density derived from a Gaussian parametrization of the elastic scattering amplitude, and wounded hot spots deposit entropy via a double Gamma distribution fitted to measured charged-particle multiplicity distributions at each collision energy.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: √s = 52.6 GeV (ISR), 7000 GeV (LHC Run I), 13000 GeV (LHC Run II).
- Repulsive core radius considered: r_c = 0.4 fm for the correlated scenario; r_c = 0 for the uncorrelated scenario.
- The average number of wounded hot spots is reduced by approximately 5% in the correlated (r_c = 0.4 fm) case relative to the case with fixed mean hot spot position ⟨s₁⟩.
- In minimum-bias collisions, the probability distribution P(ε₂) is shifted to smaller values when correlations are included; in ultra-central (0–1% entropy) collisions, the probability of larger ε₂ and ε₃ increases significantly in the correlated scenario.
- The parameters R_hs (hot spot radius) and R_p (proton radius) at √s = 7 TeV are R_hs = 0.3 fm and R_p = 0.75 fm for r_c = 0.4 fm, versus R_hs = 0.39 fm and R_p = 0.76 fm for r_c = 0.
- The maximum impact parameter for collisions is b_max = 2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the geometrical model of Albacete et al. (2016) that explained the hollowness effect in pp elastic scattering at √s = 7 TeV via correlated hot spots with energy-dependent radii. It contrasts with earlier initial-state models for pp collisions that assumed independent subnucleonic constituents, such as the IP-Glasma model of Schenke et al. (2012) or the MC-rcBK approach of Albacete et al. (2011, 2012). The results show that the effect of short-range repulsive correlations on eccentricities is qualitatively similar to that found in nucleus-nucleus collisions by Denicol et al. (2014), but quantitatively larger in the smaller pp system, and they enable future studies of how such correlations affect collective flow observables like v₂ and v₃ in high-multiplicity pp events.
