# Beta-decay in odd-A and even-even proton-rich Kr isotopes

> Beta-decay properties of proton-rich odd-A and even-even Krypton isotopes are studied in the framework of a deformed selfconsistent Hartree-Fock calculation with density-dependent Skyrme forces, including pairing correlations between like nucleons in BCS approximation. Residual spin-isospin interactions are consistently included in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels and treated in Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation. The similarities and differences in the treatment of even-even and odd-A nuclei are stressed. Comparison to available experimental information is done for Gamow-Teller strength distributions, summed strengths, and half-lives. The dependence of these observables on deformation is particularly emphasized in a search for signatures of the shape of the parent nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that deformed selfconsistent Hartree-Fock+BCS+QRPA calculations with Skyrme forces can reproduce beta-decay properties of proton-rich Kr isotopes, but the agreement depends on the assumed nuclear shape (oblate, prolate, or spherical) and the coupling strength of the particle-particle residual interaction is fixed at a single value (0.07 MeV) for all nuclei rather than fitted per case, which affects even-even and odd-A nuclei differently.

## Method

The authors performed deformed selfconsistent Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme force SG2, including BCS pairing correlations, to generate single-particle wave functions and energies for even-even and odd-A Kr isotopes. Residual spin-isospin interactions in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels were treated in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), with the particle-hole part derived selfconsistently from the Skyrme interaction and the particle-particle coupling strength fixed at 0.07 MeV. For odd-A nuclei, the mean field was obtained from the closest even-even neighbor with the odd nucleon's orbital blocked, and GT transition amplitudes were computed for both one-quasiparticle (1qp) and three-quasiparticle (3qp) excitations.

## Key numbers

- The particle-particle coupling strength was fixed at $\kappa^{pp}_{GT} = 0.07$ MeV for all nuclei, obtained from fitting even-even half-lives in the mass region A~70.
- The particle-hole coupling strength was $\chi^{ph}_{GT} = 0.37$ MeV for the SG2 force.
- The neutron pairing gap in Kr isotopes is approximately $\Delta \sim 1.5-2$ MeV, meaning the low-energy region below 3-4 MeV in odd-A nuclei corresponds to 1qp transitions.
- For $^{70}$Kr, the oblate and prolate minima are separated by about 1 MeV, with the oblate being deepest for both SG2 and Sk3 forces.
- The width parameter used to avoid singularities in the 1qp transition amplitude was $d = 0.5$ MeV.
- The GT strength distributions were folded with a Gaussian width of $\Gamma = 1$ MeV for visualization.
- A quenching factor of approximately $g_{s,\text{eff}} \approx 0.7\ g_{s,\text{free}}$ was applied, corresponding to a reduction factor of about two in strength distributions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous deformed HF+BCS+QRPA calculations on even-even exotic nuclei (Sarriguren et al., 1999-2001) to include odd-A Kr isotopes for the first time, and adds the new even-even isotope $^{70}$Kr to the chain studied earlier. The approach builds on spherical QRPA models (Ref. "sph"), Nilsson-potential treatments including deformation (Ref. "kru"), and Woods-Saxon based calculations (Ref. "moll"), while providing a fully selfconsistent framework suitable for extrapolation toward the proton drip line. The results enable discrimination between competing nuclear shapes (oblate vs. prolate) in this mass region through comparison of predicted GT strength distributions with experimental data, and are relevant for understanding the rp-process nucleosynthesis path, particularly at waiting points like $^{72}$Kr.
