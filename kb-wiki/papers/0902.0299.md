# Triton Clustering in Neutron Rich Nuclei

> Recently, it has been reported that as one goes from oxygen to fluorine, just the addition of one more proton, provides extraordinary stability to fluorine which can bind six more neutrons beyond what oxygen can. It is shown here that this surprising stability can be understood if neutron rich nuclei, $^{24}O$ and $^{27}F$ are treated as bound states of eight and nine-tritons respectively. Also the recently discovered $^{42}Si$ is predicted to have a bound state structure of fourteen tritons.

# Triton Clustering in Neutron Rich Nuclei

## Key claim
The surprising stability of neutron-rich fluorine isotopes (specifically that fluorine can bind six more neutrons than oxygen) can be explained if neutron-rich nuclei such as ²⁴O and ²⁷F are treated as bound states of eight and nine tritons respectively, with ²⁴O acting as a saturated, magic system that cannot bind additional neutrons while ²⁷F is weakly bound and can accept extra neutrons.

## Method
The authors treat nuclei of the form ³Z_Z X_2Z as bound states of Z tritons (³H), treating tritons as elementary entities. They compute single-triton separation energies S_t = BE(Z,N) - BE(Z-1,N-2) - BE(t) and two-triton separation energies S_2t as functions of triton number, using experimental mass data from Audi, Wapstra and Thibault (2003). They interpret the resulting plots for evidence of shell structure and magicity in the triton clustering picture.

## Key numbers
- ²⁴O is the heaviest bound isotope of oxygen, while ²⁸O is unbound.
- ²⁴O is treated as a bound state of 8 tritons; ²⁷F as a bound state of 9 tritons.
- ⁴²Si is predicted to be a bound state of 14 tritons.
- The single-triton separation energy shows a sharp peak at N_t = 8 (²⁴O) and a sharp dip at N_t = 9 (²⁷F), indicating magicity at N_t = 8.
- The two-triton separation energy plot shows a prominent kink at N_t = 8, confirming the magic character of 8 tritons.
- The authors predict that ⁶⁰Ca, as a bound state of 20 tritons, would be a magic nucleus.
- For triton numbers 12, 14, 15, 16, and 17, the masses are estimated rather than experimental, and the authors argue these estimated masses require correction.

## Context
This work extends the triton clustering model previously proposed by Abbas (2001, 2005), which treats nuclei with equal numbers of protons and neutrons as bound states of tritons. The paper addresses the puzzle raised by Sakurai (2002) regarding the sudden change in stability from oxygen to fluorine, and the magicity of N=28 in ⁴²Si requiring Z=14 to be magic as reported by Friedmann et al. (2005). The triton clustering picture provides a unified explanation for these phenomena and predicts ⁶⁰Ca as a magic nucleus, suggesting future experimental searches for this system.
