# Nuclear response for the Skyrme effective interaction with zero-range tensor terms. II. Sum rules and instabilities

> The formalism of linear response theory for Skyrme forces including tensor terms presented in article [1] is generalized for the case of a Skyrme energy density functional in infinite matter. We also present analytical results for the odd-power sum rules, with particular attention to the inverse energy weighted sum rule, $M_{-1}$, as a tool to detect instabilities in Skyrme functionals.

## Key claim

The paper extends the linear response formalism for Skyrme forces including tensor terms (presented in article I by Davesne et al. (2009)) to the case of a general Skyrme energy density functional in infinite matter, and demonstrates that the inverse energy weighted sum rule M_{-1} can serve as a practical tool to detect finite-size instabilities in Skyrme functionals by identifying poles in the response function as zeros in the denominator of this sum rule.

## Method

The authors generalized the linear response theory for Skyrme interactions with zero-range tensor terms, originally formulated for an effective force, to the framework of a Skyrme energy density functional in infinite symmetric nuclear matter. They derived analytical expressions for the odd-power sum rules of the response function, with particular focus on the inverse energy weighted sum rule M_{-1}, and showed that finding poles of the response function reduces to finding roots of a real function (the denominator of M_{-1}). The formalism was applied to detect instabilities across all scalar/vector and isoscalar/isovector channels at non-zero transferred momentum.

## Key numbers

- The paper presents analytical results for odd-power sum rules, but no specific numerical results (such as critical densities, coupling constant values, or instability thresholds) are given in the provided text.
- Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This work extends the linear response formalism of Davesne et al. (2009) (article I) for Skyrme forces with tensor terms, which previously only considered the central part of the interaction. It builds on earlier instability studies by Lesinski et al. (2006) in the scalar-isoscalar channel of the SkP functional and by Hellemans et al. (2011) in the vector channel of several Skyrme functionals. The inverse sum rule approach enables systematic detection of finite-size instabilities in infinite matter, which can then be related to unphysical configurations observed in finite-nucleus Hartree-Fock and RPA calculations, such as proton-neutron separation or spontaneous polarization in cranked HFB calculations.
