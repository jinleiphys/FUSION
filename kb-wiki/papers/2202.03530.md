# Finite-Volume Pionless Effective Field Theory for Few-Nucleon Systems with Differentiable Programming

> Finite-volume pionless effective field theory provides an efficient framework for the extrapolation of nuclear spectra and matrix elements calculated at finite volume in lattice QCD to infinite volume, and to nuclei with larger atomic number. In this work, it is demonstrated how this framework may be implemented via a set of correlated Gaussian wavefunctions optimised using differentiable programming and via solution of a generalised eigenvalue problem. This approach is shown to be significantly more efficient than a stochastic implementation of the variational method based on the same form of correlated Gaussian wavefunctions, yielding comparably accurate representations of the ground-state wavefunctions with an order of magnitude fewer terms. The efficiency of representation allows such calculations to be extended to larger systems than in previous work. The method is demonstrated through calculations of the binding energies of nuclei with atomic number $A\in\{2,3,4\}$ in finite volume, matched to lattice QCD calculations at quark masses corresponding to $m_π=806$ MeV, and infinite-volume effective field theory calculations of $A\in\{2,3,4,5,6\}$ systems based on this matching.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that differentiable programming (gradient-based optimisation) can optimise correlated Gaussian wavefunctions for finite-volume pionless effective field theory calculations of few-nucleon systems, achieving comparably accurate ground-state wavefunction representations with an order of magnitude fewer terms than the stochastic variational method, but only for the specific approximation where spatial and spin-isospin wavefunctions are factorised.

## Method

The authors implement a two-stage variational optimisation: first, differentiable programming with automatic differentiation and gradient descent optimises the parameters of N'-term correlated Gaussian wavefunctions at fixed low-energy constants (LECs); second, multiple sets of optimised Gaussians are combined and the linear coefficients are determined by solving a generalised eigenvalue problem. The Hamiltonian includes leading-order two-body and three-body contact interactions regulated with Gaussian smearing in a finite cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions. The method is applied to determine binding energies of nuclei with atomic number A in {2,3,4} in finite volume matched to lattice QCD results at m_pi = 806 MeV, and infinite-volume predictions for A in {2,3,4,5,6}.

## Key numbers

- DP wavefunctions with N' greater than or equal to 4 outperform the N' = 100 term wavefunction of the stochastic variational method for the deuteron binding energy at C_S = -132 MeV * fm^3 and L = 4.5 fm
- For the deuteron at C_S = -132 MeV * fm^3 and L = 4.5 fm, combining alpha = 8 sets of N' = 4 Gaussians yields results within 0.1% of the minimum energy achieved with alpha = 16 groups
- The two-body LEC C_0 = -131.0(21) MeV * fm^3, C_1 = -1.7(8) MeV * fm^3, and the three-body LEC D_0 = 20.0(24) MeV * fm^6 for a cutoff r_0 = 0.2 fm
- Finite-volume calculations are performed at L in {3.4, 4.5, 6.7} fm matched to lattice QCD results from Beane (2012)

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume pionless effective field theory framework previously implemented with the stochastic variational method by Eliyahu (2019) and Detmold (2021) to a differentiable programming approach that yields more compact wavefunction representations. The method enables extrapolation of lattice QCD results for light nuclei (A less than or equal to 4) to infinite volume and to larger nuclear systems (A in {5,6}) that are computationally intractable in direct lattice QCD calculations. The approach is restricted to states representable in pionless EFT and cannot describe states involving pions, Delta-resonances, or particle-antiparticle excitations.
