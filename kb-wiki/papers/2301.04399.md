# Point Production of a Nonrelativistic Unparticle Recoiling Against a Particle

> A nonrelativistic unparticle can be defined as an excitation created by an operator with a definite scaling dimension in a nonrelativistic field theory with an approximate conformal symmetry. The point production rate of an unparticle has power-law dependence on its total energy with an exponent determined by its scaling dimension. We use the exact result for the 3-point function of primary operators in a nonrelativistic conformal field theory to derive the contribution to the point production rate of the unparticle from its decay into another unparticle recoiling against a particle. In the case where the conformal symmetry is broken by a large positive scattering length, we deduce the exponent of the energy in the point production rate of the loosely bound two-particle state recoiling against a particle with large relative momentum.

# Point Production of a Nonrelativistic Unparticle Recoiling Against a Particle

## Key claim

The point production rate of a nonrelativistic unparticle decaying into another unparticle recoiling against a single particle has power-law dependence on the total energy, with the exponent determined by the scaling dimensions of the operators involved; this result is derived from the exact 3-point function of primary operators in a nonrelativistic conformal field theory (NRCFT), and in the case where conformal symmetry is broken by a large positive scattering length, the exponent for the production rate of a loosely bound two-particle state recoiling against a particle is $\Delta_3 - 3$.

## Method

The authors used the analytic result for the 3-point function of primary operators in a NRCFT, first derived by Henkel (1993) using Schrodinger symmetry, and performed Fourier transforms in both space and time to obtain the energy-momentum representation. They then applied the optical theorem to extract the differential production rate from the imaginary part of the Green function, specializing to the case where one operator annihilates a single particle (scaling dimension $\Delta_1 = D/2$) and evaluating the amputated amplitude on shell. For the bound state case, they introduced a deformation of the NRCFT that produces a large positive scattering length $a$, modifying the propagator to include a pole corresponding to the bound state.

## Key numbers

- The lowest scaling dimension for the 3-neutron unparticle is $\Delta_3 = 4.27272$; for 4 neutrons it is 5.07; for 5 neutrons it is 7.6.
- The lowest scaling dimensions for 3-charm-meson unparticles are $\Delta_3 = 3.10119$ for $D^0 \bar{D}^{*0} D^0$ and $\bar{D}^0 D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$, and $\Delta_3 = 3.08697$ for $D^0 \bar{D}^{*0} \bar{D}^{*0}$ and $\bar{D}^0 D^{*0} D^{*0}$.
- The $X(3872)$ binding energy relative to the $D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$ threshold is $\varepsilon_X = -0.07 \pm 0.12$ MeV, implying $|\varepsilon_X| < 0.22$ MeV at 90% confidence level.
- The energy scales for $X$ plus $D$ production are $\varepsilon_{D^0X} = 0.82$ keV and $\varepsilon_{D^{*0}X} = 0.26$ keV.
- The scattering lengths for $D^0X$ and $D^{*0}X$ are $a_{D^0X} = -9.7\, a$ and $a_{D^{*0}X} = -16.6\, a$, where $a$ is the $D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$ scattering length.
- The exponent for the bound state production rate at large energy is $\Delta_3 - 3$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nonrelativistic unparticles introduced by Hammer and Son (2021), who showed that low-energy neutron systems produced by short-distance reactions provide physical examples of nonrelativistic unparticles, and that the invariant energy distribution of $N$ neutrons has scaling behavior $E^{\Delta_N - 5/2} dE$. The authors previously applied this framework to neutral charm mesons and the $X(3872)$ resonance (Braaten, 2021), where the large positive scattering length in the $D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$ channel produces bound state components. The present paper derives the general formalism for the production rate of an unparticle recoiling against a particle, and specifically the rate for producing a loosely bound two-particle state (such as $X(3872)$) plus a single particle, which enables predictions for reactions like $B^\pm \to K^\pm + (\text{anything})$ and prompt production of $X(3872)\, D^0$ at the Large Hadron Collider. The predicted invariant energy distribution for four neutrons is not consistent with the resonance-like structure at $E/\varepsilon_{nn} \approx 20$ observed by Duer et al. (2022) in the $^8$He$(p,p\alpha)4n$ reaction, though the applicability of the point-production approximation is questionable for that process.
