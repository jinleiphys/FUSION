# Pentaquark Baryons in String Theory

> The recently observed pentaquark baryons $Θ^{+}$ and $Ξ^{--}$ are studied in the dual gravity theory of QCD. By developing a general formulation useful for studying the branched string web in a curved space, simple mass formulae of the pentaquark baryons are derived in the Maldacena prototype model for supersymmetric QCD and a more realistic model for ordinary QCD. Even though the approximations adopted here in deriving these formulae are extremely naive, the predicted values of the masses do not differ significantly from their experimental values. We thus conclude that this approach is promising for the purpose of obtaining a description of the observed masses of pentaquarks and of their extremely narrow decay widths. With the aim of constructing more reliable mass formulae, a preliminary sketch is given of how spin, the hyperfine interaction and parity are considered in the string picture.

## Key claim

The authors derive simple mass formulae for pentaquark baryons using the dual gravity theory of QCD, but explicitly state that the approximations adopted are "extremely naive" and that the predicted masses do not differ significantly from experimental values only within this crude framework.

## Method

The authors develop a general formulation for branched string webs in curved five-dimensional background spaces derived from the AdS/CFT correspondence, applying it to two models: Maldacena's prototype supersymmetric model (AdS5 x S5) and a more realistic QCD-like model with broken supersymmetry via compactification of one spatial dimension following Witten's approach. They evaluate the static energy of pentaquark configurations by calculating the total length of colored strings connecting quarks and junctions in the curved background metric, using connection conditions at string junctions where three strings meet with opening angles of 2π/3.

## Key numbers

- Observed mass of Θ+ pentaquark: M(Θ+) = 1,540 ± 10 MeV, with width Γ(Θ+) ≤ 25 MeV
- Observed mass of Ξ−− pentaquark: M(Ξ−−) = 1,862 MeV, with width Γ(Ξ−−) ≤ 18 MeV
- Predicted mass of Θ+ from chiral soliton model by Diakonov et al.: M(Θ+) = 1,530 MeV, with width Γ(Θ+) ≤ 15 MeV
- The AdS5 radius R is given by R^4 = 4π g_s N_c = 8π α_c N_c, where α_c is the QCD coupling and N_c = 3
- In the QCD-like model, the metric parameters are R'^3 = 2π α_c N_c / M_KK and U_KK = (8π/9) α_c N_c M_KK, where M_KK is the Kaluza-Klein mass scale

## Context

This work extends the application of the Maldacena (1998) duality between QCD and classical gravity to exotic hadrons, specifically pentaquarks that were experimentally observed at SPring-8 and CERN NA49. The authors compare their string theory approach to prior models: the chiral soliton prediction by Diakonov et al., the diquark model by Jaffe and Wilczek, and the cluster model by Karliner and Lipkin. The paper provides a preliminary framework for incorporating spin, hyperfine interactions, and parity into the string picture, aiming toward more reliable mass formulae for pentaquarks and their extremely narrow decay widths.
