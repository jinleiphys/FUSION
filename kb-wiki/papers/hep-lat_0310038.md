# A BCS Gapped Superfluid on the Lattice

> We discuss results of numerical analyses of the 3+1 dimensional Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at non-zero baryon density. By studying relevant order parameters, it is shown that the model goes through a transition between a vacuum with broken chiral symmetry, and a chirally restored phase at high baryon chemical potential (mu) with a diquark condensate roughly proportional to the area of the Fermi surface and a broken U(1) baryon number symmetry. The condensation of diquark pairs from the fermionic spectrum is shown to lead to an energy gap (Delta) about the Fermi energy (E_F), which is found to be approximately 15% of the vacuum fermion mass and roughly independent of mu in the chirally restored phase. The ground-state is believed, therefore, to be that of a BCS superfluid. We also discuss the effect of simulating on a finite volume, and any caveats this places on the conclusions made above.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first direct observation of a BCS energy gap in a lattice simulation of a relativistic quantum field theory, specifically the 3+1 dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at high baryon chemical potential. The gap is found to be approximately 15% of the vacuum fermion mass and roughly independent of chemical potential in the chirally restored phase, though this conclusion relies on disregarding data at low diquark source strengths (j < 0.3) due to suspected finite volume effects.

## Method

The authors performed hybrid Monte-Carlo simulations of the lattice NJL model with staggered fermions, using a partially quenched approximation where the diquark source j is set to zero during field updates but varied during measurement. They extracted the fermion dispersion relation E(k) from the Gor'kov time-slice propagator on lattices with elongated spatial extent (L_x = 96, L_y,z = 12) and varying temporal extent (L_t = 16, 20, 24), fitting normal and anomalous propagator components to extract excitation energies. The energy gap Delta was determined from the minimum of the dispersion relation after extrapolating to zero temperature (L_t^{-1} -> 0) and zero diquark source (j -> 0).

## Key numbers

- The energy gap at mu a = 0.8 is Delta a = 0.053(6), giving Delta / Sigma_0 = 0.15(2) where Sigma_0 is the vacuum fermion mass.
- Assuming Sigma_0 ~ 400 MeV, the physical gap is Delta ~ 60 MeV.
- The lattice spacing is a^{-1} = 720 MeV, with bare quark mass am = 0.006 and coupling a^2 g^{-2} = 0.495, yielding vacuum parameters Sigma_0 = 400 MeV, f_pi = 93 MeV, and m_pi = 138 MeV.
- Simulations were performed on volumes V = L_t x L_s^3 with L_t = L_s = 12, 16, and 20 for order parameter measurements, and on L_t x 96 x 12^2 lattices for dispersion relation measurements.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the NJL model (Walters, earlier work) that identified a diquark condensate in the dense phase, and provides non-perturbative evidence supporting analytic predictions from self-consistent treatments (Klevansky) and colour superconductivity estimates (Berges) that predicted gaps of order 50-100 MeV. The results are interpreted as evidence for BCS colour superconductivity in QCD with two degenerate flavours, since both theories share the same global symmetry structure, and are particularly relevant while the sign problem prevents direct lattice QCD simulations at non-zero baryon density. The main open question is resolving finite volume effects that cause deviations at low diquark source strengths, which may require full simulations with non-zero j during field updates.
