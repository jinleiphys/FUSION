# The Parton Bubble Model(PBM) is Connected to the Glasma Flux Tube Model(GFTM), and predicts the Ridge and Strong CP violation

> In an earlier paper we developed a Parton Bubble Model (PBM) for RHIC/LHC, based on a substructure of a ring of localized bubbles which initially contain 3-4 partons of almost entirely gluons. A Glasma Flux Tube Model (GFTM) which formed longitudinal flux tubes in the transverse plane of two colliding sheets of Color Glass Condensate (CGC) which pass through one another had been developed. These sheets create boost invariant flux tubes of longitudinal color electric and magnetic fields. A blast wave gives the tubes near the surface transverse flow in the same way it gave flow to the bubbles in the PBM. In this paper we connect the equivalent characteristics of the PBM to the GFTM and show that the intermediate transverse momentum charged-two-particle angular correlations of the most central and highest energy RHIC Au + Au collisions are explained. When one considers a 3-4 GeV/c transverse momentum tagged charged trigger particle in combination with other intermediate transverse momentum charged particles, the ridge correlation is generated and explained. The longitudinal color electric and magnetic fields of GFTM have a non-zero topological charge density $F \widetilde $F. These fields cause a local strong CP violation which effects charged particle production coming from quarks and anti-quarks created in the tube or bubble. We developed four-charged-particle correlations which show this CP effect and accumulate from bubble to bubble independent of whether particles are pushed or pulled and rotated in a right or left handed direction, We demonstrate strong evidence for the predicted color electric field using previously published experimental data. We developed 4 particle correlations for obtaining evidence for the predicted color magnetic field.

## Key claim

The Parton Bubble Model (PBM) and the Glasma Flux Tube Model (GFTM) are directly connected by assuming that the final state at kinetic freezeout of a flux tube is a PBM final state bubble, and this connection allows the PBM to explain the ridge phenomenon when a jet trigger is added and to predict strong CP violation effects via four-charged-particle correlations.

## Method

The authors connected the PBM, which uses a ring of twelve 2-fm-radius bubbles (gluonic hot spots) on the fireball surface at midrapidity, to the GFTM by assuming flux tubes expand from an initial transverse size of approximately 1/4 fm to the bubble size of approximately 2 fm at kinetic freezeout. They added a jet trigger (3-4 GeV/c charged trigger particle paired with particles having p_t above 1.1 GeV/c) to the PBM generator, using HIJING to determine the expected number of jets reduced by 80% to account for jet quenching. Four-charged-particle correlations were developed to detect the color electric and magnetic fields arising from the non-zero topological charge density in the GFTM.

## Key numbers

- The PBM bubble ring radius was estimated to be approximately 8 fm, with each bubble having a radius of approximately 2 fm.
- The PBM fit to angular correlation data for Au + Au central (0-10%) collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV was within a few percent of the observed correlations.
- The average observed angles from the PBM were approximately 30 degrees in Delta phi and 70 degrees in Delta eta, originating from a source size of about 2 fm radius.
- The initial transverse size of a GFTM flux tube is about 1/4 fm (since Q_s is around 1 GeV/c), and the phi angle wedge of the flux tube is approximately 1/20 radians or about 3 degrees.
- The HBT source radii reduce from about 6 fm at p_t approximately 0.2 GeV/c to about 2 fm at p_t approximately 1 GeV/c.
- The number of jets was reduced by 80% to account for jet quenching, consistent with single pi0 suppression.
- 4% of Au + Au central events (0-5% centrality) at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV have a charged particle with p_t between 3.0 and 4.0 GeV/c with at least one other charged particle with p_t greater than 1.1 GeV/c from the same jet.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Parton Bubble Model (Lindenbaum and Longacre, 2005) and its extension PBME (Lindenbaum and Longacre, 2007), which explained charged-particle-pair correlations in central and non-central Au + Au collisions at RHIC. The connection to the Glasma Flux Tube Model (Dumitru et al., 2006) provides a theoretical foundation for the bubble substructure and enables predictions for the ridge phenomenon observed at RHIC and for strong CP violation effects via color electric and magnetic fields, building on earlier work by Kharzeev and Pisarski (2000) on CP-odd bubbles of metastable vacuum.
