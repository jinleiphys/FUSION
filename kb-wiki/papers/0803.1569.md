# Partial Dynamical Symmetries in the g9/2 Shell-Progress and Puzzles

> We present analytic proofs of the properties of four particle states in the g9/2 shell which have seniority v=4 and angular momentum I=4 or 6.We show in particular that the number of pairs with angular momentum I is equal to one for these states.

# Partial Dynamical Symmetries in the g9/2 Shell - Progress and Puzzles

## Key claim

The paper provides analytic proofs that certain four-particle states in the j=9/2 shell with seniority v=4 and angular momentum I=4 or 6 have the property that they cannot mix with v=2 states under any arbitrary interaction, and that the number of pairs with angular momentum I equals one for these states. However, the authors acknowledge that one key relation (involving ratios of coefficients of fractional parentage for v=3 and v=5 states) still lacks an analytic proof and was only verified numerically.

## Method

The authors use analytic manipulations of coefficients of fractional parentage (CFPs) for identical fermions in a single-j shell, specifically applying the Redmond recursion relation and a proportionality relation between v-to-(v+1)-particle and (v+1)-to-(v+2)-particle CFPs. They define special linear combinations of the two degenerate v=4 states such that a specific three-to-four-particle CFP vanishes, then prove that the off-diagonal matrix element between v=2 and the special v=4 state vanishes for all five two-body interaction components (lambda = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8) in the j=9/2 shell.

## Key numbers

- For the j=9/2 shell, there are five two-body interaction matrix elements corresponding to lambda = 0, 2, 4, 6, and 8.
- The energy expression for the special v=4, I=4 state is: E = (68/33)*nu_2 + nu_4 + (13/15)*nu_6 + (114/55)*nu_8.
- The energy expression for the special v=4, I=6 state is: E = (19/11)*nu_2 + (12/13)*nu_4 + nu_6 + (336/143)*nu_8.
- For the j=7/2 shell, the energy expression for v=4, I=2 is: E = nu_2 + (42/11)*nu_4 + (13/11)*nu_6.
- For the j=7/2 shell, the energy expression for v=4, I=4 is: E = (7/3)*nu_2 + nu_4 + (8/3)*nu_6.
- The coefficient of nu_{lambda=I} in the energy expression equals 1 for the special states, meaning the number of pairs with angular momentum I equals one.

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical findings by Escuderos and Zamick (2006) that certain v=4 states in the j=9/2 shell have vanishing mixing with v=2 states, and builds on symbolic computation results by Van Isacker (2008) that gave closed energy expressions. The paper provides analytic proofs using the formalism of de-Shalit and Talmi (1963) and Talmi (1993) for coefficients of fractional parentage, and the Redmond recursion relation (1954). The results enable a deeper understanding of partial seniority symmetry in the j=9/2 shell, though the authors note that an analytic proof of one relation (involving ratios of CFPs for v=3 and v=5 states) remains an open puzzle, and a simple derivation of energy expressions for the j=9/2 solvable states is not yet available.
