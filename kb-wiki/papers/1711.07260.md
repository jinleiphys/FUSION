# Critical point of nuclear matter and beam energy dependence of net proton number fluctuations

> The beam energy dependence of net baryon number susceptibilities is studied in the framework of the hadron resonance gas model with the attractive and repulsive van der Waals interactions between baryons. The collision energy dependences for the skewness $Sσ$ and kurtosis $κσ^2$ deviate significantly from the Poisson baseline and demonstrate the existence of rich structures at moderate collision energies. This behavior may result from the critical end point of the nuclear liquid-gas first order phase transition. In particular, $κσ^2$ shows a non-monotonic energy dependence, and, in contrast to the standard scenario for the QCD critical point, it does not decrease at low collision energies. It is also found that the measurable net proton fluctuations differ significantly from the net baryon fluctuations when interactions between baryons cannot be neglected. The results are compared with the experimental net proton number fluctuations measured by the STAR collaboration.

## Key claim

The beam energy dependence of net baryon number fluctuations, calculated within the hadron resonance gas model with van der Waals interactions between baryons, shows that the kurtosis κσ² of net baryon fluctuations increases with decreasing collision energy at moderate energies, in contrast to the standard scenario for the QCD critical point where it would decrease; this behavior may result from the critical end point of the nuclear liquid-gas first order phase transition rather than the QCD chiral critical point.

## Method

The authors use the Quantum van der Waals (QvdW) hadron resonance gas model, which includes attractive and repulsive van der Waals interactions between all baryons and between all antibaryons, with parameters a = 329 MeV fm³ and b = 3.42 fm³ fitted to nuclear ground state properties. They calculate the normalized skewness Sσ = χ₃/χ₂ and kurtosis κσ² = χ₄/χ₂ of net baryon number fluctuations along the chemical freeze-out curve parameterized by Cleymans et al. (2005), applying a binomial filter with acceptance parameter q = 0.2 to model finite acceptance effects for net protons. The results are compared with STAR collaboration data for net proton fluctuations at midrapidity.

## Key numbers

- The van der Waals parameters are a = 329 MeV fm³ and b = 3.42 fm³, obtained from fitting nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and binding energy E/A = -16 MeV.
- The nuclear liquid-gas critical point in the QvdW-HRG model is at T_c ≅ 19.7 MeV and μ_B^c ≅ 908 MeV.
- The chemical freeze-out curve parameters are: a₁ = 0.166 ± 0.002 GeV, a₂ = 0.139 ± 0.016 GeV⁻¹, a₃ = 0.053 ± 0.021 GeV⁻³, b₁ = 1.308 ± 0.028 GeV, b₂ = 0.273 ± 0.008 GeV⁻¹.
- Resonance decay feeddown accounts for about 10% of observed protons at √s_NN = 2.4 GeV and about 50% at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV.
- The STAR data correspond to net proton fluctuations at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) in transverse momentum intervals 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c and 0.4 < p_T < 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the QvdW-HRG model previously developed by Vovchenko et al. (2015, 2016) to study higher-order baryon number fluctuations along the chemical freeze-out line, building on earlier studies by Fukushima (2014) and Mukherjee et al. (2016) that pointed out the influence of nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclear liquid-gas criticality. The results are compared with STAR Beam Energy Scan data and contrast with the "standard scenario" for the QCD critical point described by Stephanov (2011), where κσ² decreases with decreasing collision energy; here κσ² increases as the freeze-out point approaches the nuclear critical point. The work enables future experimental discrimination between these two scenarios using fluctuation measurements at moderate collision energies from HADES, NA61/SHINE, STAR fixed-target, CBM at FAIR, and NICA.
