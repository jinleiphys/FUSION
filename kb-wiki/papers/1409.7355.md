# Spin-orbit decomposition of ab initio wavefunctions

> Although the modern shell-model picture of atomic nuclei is built from single-particle orbits with good total angular momentum $j$, leading to $j$-$j$ coupling, phenomenological models suggested decades ago that for $0p$-shell nuclides a simpler picture can be realized via coupling of total spin $S$ and total orbital angular momentum $L$. I revisit this idea with large-basis, no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using modern \textit{ab initio} two-body interactions, and dissect the resulting wavefunctions into their component $L$- and $S$-components. Remarkably, there is broad agreement with calculations using the phenomenological Cohen-Kurath forces, despite a gap of nearly fifty years and six orders of magnitude in basis dimensions. I suggest $L$-$S$ may be a useful tool for analyzing \textit{ab initio} wavefunctions of light nuclei, for example in the case of rotational bands.

## Key claim

Despite a gap of nearly fifty years and six orders of magnitude in basis dimensions, large-basis no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using modern *ab initio* two-body interactions show broad agreement with phenomenological Cohen-Kurath calculations in the L-S decomposition of wavefunctions for 0p-shell nuclides, though the author notes this agreement does not mean either calculation is correct and that states with known difficulties show the largest discrepancies.

## Method

The author performed configuration-interaction (CI) diagonalization using the BIGSTICK code, computing wavefunctions for 9Be, 10B, 11B, and 12C in two frameworks: the phenomenological Cohen-Kurath interaction within the 0p-shell space, and *ab initio* NCSM calculations using a chiral two-body force evolved via similarity renormalization group (SRG) to lambda = 2.0 fm^-1 with N_max = 6 (and N_max = 8 for 10B). The L-S decomposition was carried out using a modified Lanczos trick, where the wavefunction of interest serves as the pivot vector for Lanczos iteration with the L^2 or S^2 operator, allowing direct read-off of the amplitude for each L or S component.

## Key numbers

- Cohen-Kurath calculations in the 0p space for 11B had a dimension of 62; the NCSM calculation had a dimension of 20 million
- For 10B, NCSM calculations at N_max = 6 had dimension 12 million; at N_max = 8, dimension 166 million; the Cohen-Kurath calculation had dimension 84
- For 9Be, NCSM calculations had dimension 5.2 million; Cohen-Kurath calculations had dimension 62
- NCSM calculations used oscillator frequency hbar-Omega = 20 MeV for 9Be and 22 MeV for other nuclides, with SRG evolution parameter lambda = 2.0 fm^-1
- For 7Li robustness tests, model spaces ranged from N_max = 6 (dimension 663,527) up to N_max = 12 (dimension 252 million)
- The 1+ state in 10B dropped below the 3+ state when going from N_max = 6 to N_max = 8, yet the L-decomposition remained nearly identical

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological understanding from Inglis (1953) and Kurath (1956) that p-shell nuclei are intermediate between j-j and L-S coupling, and the Cohen-Kurath interaction (1965) which showed strong dominance by a few L-S components. The author applies L-S decomposition as a diagnostic tool for *ab initio* NCSM wavefunctions, complementing recent studies of rotational band structure in NCSM calculations by Caprio et al. (2013) that used E2 and M1 transition strengths. The method enables comparison between phenomenological and *ab initio* calculations, and the author suggests it will be useful for examining how results change when three-body forces are included in future work, particularly for problematic states like the Hoyle state in 12C and the 1+;0 state in 12C.
