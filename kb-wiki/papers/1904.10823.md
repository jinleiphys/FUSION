# Saturation momentum scale extracted from semi-inclusive transverse spectra in high-energy pp collisions

> Geometric scaling is well confirmed for transverse momentum distributions observed in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. We introduced multiplicity dependence on a saturation momentum of the geometrical scaling, assuming the scaling holds for semi-inclusive distributions as well as for inclusive distributions. The saturation momentum is usually given by Bjorken's $x$ variable, but redefinition of the scaling variable can make the saturation momentum a function of collision energy $W$. We treat the energy as a free parameter (denoted $W^*$ to distinguish it from $W$) and associate the energy-dependent saturation momentum $Q_{\rm sat}(W^*)$ with particle number density. By using $Q_{\rm sat}(W^*)$ for a scaling variable $τ$, we show semi-inclusive distributions can be geometrically scaled. i.e., all semi-inclusive spectra observed at $W$=0.90, 2.76 and 7.00 TeV overlap one universal function. The particle density dependences of mean transverse momentum $\langle p_{\rm T} \rangle$ for LHC energies scales in terms of $Q_{\rm sat}(W^*)$. Furthermore, our model explains a scaling property of event-by-event $p_{\rm T}$ fluctuation measure $\sqrt{C_m}/\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle$ at LHC energies for pp collisions, where $C_m$ is two-particle transverse momentum correlator. Our analysis of the $p_{\rm T}$ fluctuation makes possible to evaluate a non-perturbative coefficient of the gluon correlation function.

## Key claim

Geometric scaling (GS), previously confirmed for inclusive transverse momentum distributions in pp collisions at LHC energies, is shown to hold also for semi-inclusive distributions (i.e., distributions with fixed multiplicity or limited multiplicity class) when the saturation momentum is redefined as a function of an effective energy parameter \(W^*\) rather than the actual collision energy \(W\).

## Method

The authors assume that semi-inclusive \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra scale to the same universal Tsallis-type function \({\cal F}(\tau)\) as inclusive spectra, using a scaling variable \(\tau = (p_{\rm T}/Q_{\rm sat}(W^*))^{2+\lambda}\). They fit this function to ALICE and CMS semi-inclusive \(p_{\rm T}\) data at \(W = 0.90\), 2.76, and 7.00 TeV for various multiplicity classes, extracting the effective energy \(W^*\) and effective transverse cross-sectional area \(S^*_{\rm T}\) for each class. The extracted \(Q_{\rm sat}(W^*)\) values are then used to analyze the scaling of mean transverse momentum \(\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) and the normalized event-by-event \(p_{\rm T}\) fluctuation measure \(\sqrt{C_m}/\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\), with the latter modeled via a Bose-Einstein correlation (BEC) function inspired by color flux tube geometry.

## Key numbers

*   For inclusive distributions, \(Q_{\rm sat}(W) = 0.99\), 1.11, and 1.21 GeV/\(c\) at \(W = 0.90\), 2.76, and 7.00 TeV, respectively.
*   The universal function \({\cal F}(\tau)\) is fitted with non-extensive parameter \(q = 1.134\), \(\kappa = 0.1293\), and \(\lambda = 0.22\); the effective cross-sectional area for inclusive spectra is \(S_{\rm T} = 22.66\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
*   For semi-inclusive spectra at \(W = 0.90\) TeV with ALICE multiplicity class \(n_{\rm acc} = 3\), the extracted \(Q_{\rm sat}(W^*) = 0.84^{+0.06}_{-0.06}\) GeV/\(c\) and effective radius \(R_{\rm T} = 0.63^{+0.09}_{-0.07}\) fm; for \(n_{\rm acc} = 17\), \(Q_{\rm sat}(W^*) = 1.24^{+0.08}_{-0.08}\) GeV/\(c\) and \(R_{\rm T} = 0.82^{+0.08}_{-0.07}\) fm.
*   For CMS data at \(W = 7.00\) TeV with \(n_{\rm tracks} = 131\), \(Q_{\rm sat}(W^*) = 1.23^{+0.07}_{-0.06}\) GeV/\(c\) and \(R_{\rm T} = 1.18^{+0.07}_{-0.07}\) fm.
*   The extracted \(Q_{\rm sat}(W^*)\) and \(R_{\rm T}\) scale as \((dn_{\rm ch}/dy)^{1/6}\) and \((dn_{\rm ch}/dy)^{1/3}\), respectively.
*   From fits to the \(p_{\rm T}\) fluctuation measure, the BEC model parameters are \(\sigma = 1.14\), \(n = -0.71\) at 0.90 TeV; \(\sigma = 1.01\), \(n = -0.73\) at 2.76 TeV; \(\sigma = 1.01\), \(n = -0.81\) at 7.00 TeV; the extracted non-perturbative coefficient \(\kappa_2\) is 1.79, 1.14, and 1.14 for these energies, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the geometric scaling hypothesis, previously established for inclusive pp spectra by Praszalowicz (2015) and others, to semi-inclusive distributions by introducing a multiplicity-dependent effective energy \(W^*\). It builds on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework and the Glasma flux tube picture of Dumitru (2008) and Lappi (2009), using a BEC-type correlation function to model two-particle correlations. The extracted non-perturbative coefficient \(\kappa_2\) of the gluon correlation function, comparable to Glasma framework estimates, enables a new phenomenological link between saturation momentum and event-by-event \(p_{\rm T}\) fluctuations, providing a method to classify events by saturation scale.
