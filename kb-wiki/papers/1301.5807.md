# Relativistic mean field study of 'islands of inversion' in neutron rich Z = 17 - 23, 37 - 40 and 60 - 64 nuclei

> We study the extremely neutron-rich nuclei for $Z = 17 - 23$, $37 - 40$ and $60 - 64$ regions of the periodic table by using axially deformed relativistic mean field formalism with NL3* parametrization. Based on the analysis of binding energy, two neutron separation energy, quadrupole deformation and root mean square radii, we emphasized the speciality of these considered regions which are recently predicted islands of inversion.

## Key claim

The paper uses axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations with the NL3* parameter set to identify three regions (Z = 17-23, 37-40, and 60-64) as "islands of inversion" where neutron-rich nuclei exhibit extra binding energy and large deformation compared to their neighboring isotopes, though the authors note that the RMF results often diverge from both FRDM and INM model predictions, especially at higher mass numbers.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the NL3* parameter set with a BCS pairing correlation, using constant pairing gaps for protons and neutrons. They used 12 harmonic oscillator basis quanta for regions I and II, and 14 basis quanta for region III, and computed binding energies, two-neutron separation energies, quadrupole deformation parameters, and root mean square radii for neutron-rich nuclei in the three regions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{69}$V (region I), the RMF binding energy is 531.5 MeV with quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 = 0.249$, compared to FRDM binding energy of 530.9 MeV and INM binding energy of 532.5 MeV
- For $^{119}$Zr (region II), the RMF binding energy is 922.3 MeV with $\beta_2 = 0.011$, compared to FRDM binding energy of 922.9 MeV and INM binding energy of 922.0 MeV
- For $^{185}$Gd (region III), the RMF binding energy is 1408.4 MeV with $\beta_2 = 0.114$, compared to FRDM binding energy of 1407.7 MeV and INM binding energy of 1408.0 MeV
- In the Z = 17-23 region, a sharp drop in two-neutron separation energy occurs at neutron number N = 42 for all members
- In the Z = 37-40 region, a sharp drop in two-neutron separation energy occurs at N = 68
- In the Z = 60-64 region, a sharp drop in two-neutron separation energy occurs at N = 112
- For $^{62}$Ti, the RMF binding energy is 499.7 MeV with $\beta_2 = 0.000$ (spherical), while FRDM gives 497.9 MeV and INM gives 495.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier RMF study by Patra (1991) that explained the failure of shell model calculations for neutron-rich Ne, Na, and Mg nuclei due to large deformation. The study is motivated by predictions from the Infinite Nuclear Matter (INM) model (2012) that identified new island of inversion regions around Z = 60, and by experimental evidence from Tarasov (2009) and Lenzi (2010) supporting an island of inversion centered around $^{62}$Ti. The results enable further investigation of neutron magic numbers at N = 42, 68, and 112 in neutron-drip-line nuclei.
