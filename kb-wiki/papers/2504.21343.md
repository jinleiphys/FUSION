# Analysis of $Σ^*$ via isospin selective reaction $K_Lp \to π^+Σ^0$

> The isospin-selective reaction $K_Lp \to π^+Σ^0$ provides a clean probe for investigating $I=1$ $Σ^*$ resonances. In this work, we perform an analysis of this reaction using an effective Lagrangian approach for the first time, incorporating the well-established $Σ(1189) 1/2^+$, $Σ(1385) 3/2^+$, $Σ(1670) 3/2^-$, $Σ(1775) 5/2^-$ states, while also exploring contributions from other unestablished states. By fitting the available differential cross section and recoil polarization data, adhering to partial-wave phase conventions same as PDG, we find that besides the established resonances, contributions from $Σ(1660) 1/2^+$, $Σ(1580) 3/2^-$ and a $Σ^*(1/2^-)$ improve the description. Notably, a $Σ^*(1/2^-)$ resonance with mass around 1.54 GeV, consistent with $Σ(1620)1/2^-$, is found to be essential for describing the data in this channel, a stronger indication than found in previous analyses focusing on $πΛ$ final states. While providing complementary support for $Σ(1660) 1/2^+$ and $Σ(1580) 3/2^-$, our results highlight the importance of the $Σ(1620) 1/2^-$ region in $K_Lp \to π^+Σ^0$. Future high-precision measurements are needed to solidify these findings and further constrain the $Σ^*$ spectrum.

# Analysis of $\Sigma^*$ via isospin selective reaction $K_Lp \to \pi^+\Sigma^0$

## Key claim

Using an effective Lagrangian approach applied to the isospin-selective reaction $K_Lp \to \pi^+\Sigma^0$ for the first time, the authors find that a $\Sigma^*(1/2^-)$ resonance with mass around 1.54 GeV, consistent with the controversial $\Sigma(1620)1/2^-$ state, is essential for describing the available differential cross section and recoil polarization data, providing a stronger indication than found in previous analyses focusing on $\pi\Lambda$ final states.

## Method

The authors performed a tree-level effective Lagrangian analysis of the reaction $\overline{K}^0p \to \pi^+\Sigma^0$ (projected from $K_Lp$ scattering), incorporating $s$-channel $\Sigma$ and $\Sigma^*$ resonance exchanges up to spin-5/2, $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange, and $u$-channel nucleon exchange, with form factors applied at each vertex. They fitted the model parameters to available differential cross section data from four experimental datasets and $\Sigma^0$ recoil polarization data at one energy point, constraining coupling constants within ranges estimated from SU(3) flavor symmetry and PDG decay widths, while adhering to partial-wave phase conventions consistent with the PDG.

## Key numbers

- Optimal fit yields $\chi^2/\text{D.o.F} = 1.606$ ($\chi^2 = 358.104$) for 245 data points with 22 tunable parameters
- Fitted mass of $\Sigma(1620) 1/2^-$: $1.541 \pm 0.003$ GeV; fitted width: $0.129 \pm 0.002$ GeV
- Fitted mass of $\Sigma(1660) 1/2^+$: $1.696 \pm 0.010$ GeV; fitted width: $0.108 \pm 0.021$ GeV
- Fitted coupling $\sqrt{\Gamma_{\overline{K}N}\Gamma_{\pi\Sigma}}/\Gamma_{\text{tot}}$ for $\Sigma(1580) 3/2^-$: $+0.032 \pm 0.005$
- Baseline fit without additional resonances (only four-star states plus backgrounds): $\chi^2/\text{D.o.F} = 2.789$ with 12 parameters
- Fit excluding $\Sigma(1660) 1/2^+$ and $\Sigma(1580) 3/2^-$ but including $\Sigma(1620) 1/2^-$: $\chi^2/\text{D.o.F} = 1.774$ with 16 parameters

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of $\Sigma^*$ resonances by the same group (Gao et al., 2010, 2012) that studied $K^-p \to \pi^0\Lambda$ and $K^-n \to \pi^-\Lambda$ reactions, where $\Sigma(1660) 1/2^+$ was favored but $\Sigma(1620) 1/2^-$ was not required by data. The $K_Lp \to \pi^+\Sigma^0$ channel isolates pure isospin $I=1$ amplitudes, unlike $K^-p$ reactions which mix $I=0$ and $I=1$ contributions. The results provide complementary support for $\Sigma(1660) 1/2^+$ and $\Sigma(1580) 3/2^-$ while highlighting the $\Sigma(1620) 1/2^-$ region as essential in this channel, motivating future high-precision measurements with $K_L$ beams to further constrain the $\Sigma^*$ spectrum.
