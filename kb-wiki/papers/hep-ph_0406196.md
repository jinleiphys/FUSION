# A study of pentaquarks on the lattice with overlap fermions

> We present a quenched lattice QCD calculation of spin-1/2 five-quark states with $uudd\bar{s}$ quark content for both positive and negative parities. We do not observe any bound pentaquark state in these channels for either I = 0 or I =1. The states we found are consistent with KN scattering states which are checked to exhibit the expected volume dependence of the spectral weight. The results are based on overlap-fermion propagators on two lattices, 12^3 x 28 and 16^3 x 28, with the same lattice spacing of 0.2 fm, and pion mass as low as ~ 180 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper reports a quenched lattice QCD calculation of spin-1/2 five-quark states with uudd\bar{s} quark content and finds no bound pentaquark state in either the I=0 or I=1 channels for either positive or negative parity; the states observed are consistent with KN scattering states, and the authors explicitly note that their data do not favor a pentaquark state between the ground and first excited scattering states, though they cannot preclude a pentaquark nearly degenerate (within 100 MeV) with the KN threshold.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD calculations using overlap fermion propagators on two lattices (12^3 x 28 and 16^3 x 28) with a lattice spacing of 0.200(3) fm, corresponding to box sizes of 2.4 fm and 3.2 fm. They computed correlation functions for spin-1/2 pentaquark interpolating fields of both KN-type and diquark-diquark-antiquark type for I=0 and I=1 channels, covering pion masses from 182(8) MeV to 1293(20) MeV, and used the sequential empirical Bayes method for fitting excited states while accounting for quenched ghost states (NKη') in the positive-parity channel.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass as low as 182(8) MeV, with the lowest pion mass finite-volume error estimated at approximately 2.7%
- Lattice spacing a = 0.200(3) fm, determined from fπ, on both 12^3 x 28 and 16^3 x 28 lattices
- KN S-wave threshold energy at mπ=182 MeV is 1.47 GeV for both lattice sizes
- KN P-wave threshold energy (n=1) at mπ=182 MeV is 1.80 GeV on the 2.4 fm lattice and 1.67 GeV on the 3.2 fm lattice
- First excited state in the I(J^P)=0(1/2^-) channel near the chiral limit is approximately 1.85 GeV, substantially higher than the experimentally reported pentaquark mass of 1540 MeV
- Ratio of spectral weights between the two lattices is 2.37, consistent with the expected volume dependence for two-particle KN scattering states
- Estimated energy shift for I=1 KN scattering from experimental S-wave scattering length is about 8 MeV on the 16^3 x 28 lattice and about 18 MeV on the 12^3 x 28 lattice

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of pentaquarks by Csikor et al. (2003) and Sasaki (2004) using Wilson fermions, and directly contradicts the results of Chiu and Hsieh (2004) who used overlap fermions and reported a bound pentaquark state. The authors find qualitative agreement with Csikor et al. and Sasaki in that no bound pentaquark is observed below the KN threshold, but disagree with Sasaki's claim of a low-lying pentaquark state between the ground and first excited KN scattering states. The study enables future investigations of possible pentaquark states nearly degenerate with the KN threshold using a two-channel approach, and provides a framework for extracting KN scattering information from lattice simulations.
