# Lorentz-covariant kinetic theory for massive spin-1/2 particles

> We construct a matrix-valued spin-dependent distribution function (MVSD) for massive spin-1/2 fermions and study its properties under Lorentz transformations. Such transformations result in a Wigner rotation in spin space and in a nontrivial matrix-valued shift in space-time, which corresponds to the side jump in the massless case. We express the vector and axial-vector components of the Wigner function in terms of the MVSD and show that they transform in a Lorentz-covariant manner. We then construct a manifestly Lorentz-covariant Boltzmann equation which contains a nonlocal collision term encoding spin-orbit coupling. Finally, we obtain the spin-dependent distribution function in local equilibrium by demanding detailed balance.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a Lorentz-covariant kinetic theory for massive spin-1/2 fermions by introducing a matrix-valued spin-dependent distribution function (MVSD) that, under Lorentz transformations, undergoes both a Wigner rotation in spin space and a matrix-valued shift in space-time analogous to the side-jump effect in massless particles; this framework is claimed to form the theoretical foundation for numerical simulations of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions and may potentially contribute to solving the sign problem of the longitudinal polarization, but the authors note that results including the shear-induced contribution appear sensitive to the equation of state and other parameters.

## Method

The authors define a matrix-valued spin-dependent distribution function (MVSD) in phase space via a Fourier transform of the momentum-space density matrix with respect to relative momentum, then relate it to the Wigner function through a gradient expansion keeping terms of first order in ℏ. They derive the Lorentz transformation properties of the MVSD by demanding covariance of the density matrix under boosts, obtaining a transformation law that includes both a Wigner rotation and a frame-dependent shift term Δμu0u. A manifestly Lorentz-covariant Boltzmann equation is constructed with a nonlocal collision term that encodes spin-orbit coupling, and the spin-dependent distribution function in local equilibrium is obtained by demanding detailed balance.

## Context

This work extends the Chiral Kinetic Theory (CKT) framework of Son (2012), Stephanov (2012), and Chen (2012) to massive spin-1/2 particles, building on prior efforts by Zhang (2019), Li (2019), Liu (2020), Yang (2020), Weickgenannt (2020), Manuel (2021), and Sheng (2021) to derive kinetic theory for massive particles with spin. The paper addresses the "sign problem of the longitudinal polarization" observed by the STAR collaboration (2019), where the azimuthal-angle dependence of Λ hyperon polarization shows opposite sign to theoretical predictions; recent proposals by Becattini (2021), Liu (2021), and Fu (2021) suggest a shear-induced contribution at freeze-out may resolve this issue. The formalism enables consistent numerical simulations of spin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions and smoothly connects to CKT in the massless limit by replacing m n^μ(p) → p^μ.
