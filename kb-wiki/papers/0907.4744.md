# Addressing the physics of the ridge by 2- and 3-particle correlations at STAR

> We present new results on 2-particle azimuthal ($Δϕ$) correlation relative to event plane and 3-particle pseudorapidity ($Δη$) correlation at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{{\it s}_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, measured by the STAR experiment. While jet-like correlation is symmetric, ridge is found to be asymmetric when trigger particle azimuth is between in- and out-of-plane. The charge ordering properties between associated and trigger particles are exploited to separate jet-like and ridge contributions in 3-particle $Δη$-$Δη$ correlations. We found that like-sign triplets are dominated by ridge. The separated ridge, while narrow in $Δϕ$, is extremely broad in $Δη$. The ridge particles are not only uncorrelated to the trigger particle in $Δη$, but also uncorrelated between themselves.

## Key claim

The ridge in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is asymmetric in azimuth when the trigger particle is between in-plane and out-of-plane, and the ridge particles are uncorrelated with each other in pseudorapidity; these features are qualitatively consistent with the Correlated Emission Model but the model lacks dynamics depending on particle transverse momentum or mechanisms for long-range pseudorapidity correlations.

## Method

Two-particle azimuthal correlations were measured relative to the event plane in 20-60% centrality Au+Au collisions, with the jet-like component isolated by subtracting the large-|Δη| region (|Δη|>0.7) from the small-|Δη| region (|Δη|<0.7). Three-particle Δη-Δη correlations were analyzed in 0-12% Au+Au collisions using charge ordering: like-sign triplets (same charge for all three particles) were identified as ridge-dominated, allowing separation of jet-like and ridge components without assuming the ridge shape in Δη.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, trigger pT range 3-4 GeV/c and associated pT range 1-2 GeV/c for dihadron correlations
- Trigger pT range 3-10 GeV/c and associated pT range 1-3 GeV/c for three-particle correlations
- Jet-like component Gaussian width σ_Δη = 0.25 ± 0.09 in 0-12% Au+Au collisions
- Ridge component Gaussian width σ_Δη = 1.53 ± 0.41 in 0-12% Au+Au collisions
- Ridge asymmetry parameter peaks at φ_s ∼ 45° for 20-60% Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends previous ridge studies by the STAR collaboration (Aoqi, QM08) by adding event-plane-dependent asymmetry measurements and charge-based separation of jet and ridge components in three-particle correlations. The asymmetry results are compared to the Correlated Emission Model by Chiu and Hwa (2009), which attributes the ridge to aligned jet propagation and medium flow. The finding that ridge particles are uncorrelated with each other in Δη constrains theoretical models of ridge formation and motivates further study of the interplay between jet propagation and radial flow in heavy-ion collisions.
