# Rotational alignment near N=Z and proton-neutron correlations

> The effects of the residual proton-neutron interactions on bandcrossing features are studied by means of shell model calculations for nucleons in a high-j intruder orbital. The presence of an odd-nucleon shifts the frequency of the alignment of two nucleons of the other kind along the axis of rotation. It is shown that the anomalous delayed crossing observed in nuclei with aligning neutrons and protons occupying the same intruder subshell can be partly attributed to these residual interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the anomalous delayed bandcrossing observed in nuclei where aligning neutrons and protons occupy the same high-j intruder subshell can be *partly* attributed to residual proton-neutron interactions, not solely to deformation changes induced by an odd nucleon as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations for protons and neutrons confined to a single high-j intruder orbital (e.g., h11/2, g9/2, f7/2) interacting via a short-range delta force, while moving in a deformed rotating potential generated by nucleons outside the shell. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian numerically to obtain the expectation value of the angular momentum component along the rotation axis as a function of rotational frequency, identifying crossing frequencies from the inflection points of the alignment curves.

## Key numbers

- For a deformation β=0.25 in the h11/2 shell, the alignment of a neutron pair shows a step-like rise in angular momentum from small values to about 10 (units of ℏ).
- In the f7/2 shell systematic study, the critical frequency for neutron alignment first grows with the number of added protons, reaches its maximum when Z=N, and then decreases when Z becomes larger than N.
- The delay of the first backbend in the even-even N=Z nucleus 72Kr is attributed to the T=1 correlations between protons and neutrons.

## Context

This work extends earlier cranked shell model (CSM) studies by Bengtsson and Frauendorf (1979) and Frauendorf (1980), which assumed independent motion of protons and neutrons and could not explain the observed delays. The paper contradicts the conclusion of Wyss et al. (1995) that a QQ-type p-n interaction in the particle-hole channel produces only very small shifts. It enables future disentanglement of deformation-change effects from p-n correlation effects in bandcrossing phenomena, and calls for more realistic shell model calculations in larger configuration spaces.
