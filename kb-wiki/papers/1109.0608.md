# Towards a model-independent partial wave analysis for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction

> Amplitude and partial wave analyses for pion, eta or kaon photoproduction are discussed in the context of `complete experiments'. It is shown that the model-independent helicity amplitudes obtained from at least 8 polarization observables including beam, target and recoil polarization can not be used to determine underlying resonance parameters. However, a truncated partial wave analysis, which theoretically requires only 5 observables will be possible with minimal model input.

## Key claim

A complete set of 8 polarization observables can determine the moduli and relative phases of the four helicity amplitudes for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, but the resulting amplitudes are useless for nucleon resonance analysis because an unknown overall phase remains that cannot be fixed by experiment or by unitarity. However, a truncated partial wave analysis requires only 5 observables theoretically and can be performed with minimal model input, making it the viable path to extract resonance parameters.

## Method

The author derives the mathematical framework for both amplitude analysis and truncated partial wave analysis (PWA) of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, using expansions of observables in Legendre or cosθ series with truncation at a maximal angular momentum ℓ_max. A numerical demonstration is performed with pseudo-data generated from the MAID2007 model for γ,π⁰ and γ,π⁺ over Elab = 200–1200 MeV, using 10 MeV energy bins and 10° angular bins, with single-energy fits performed using the SAID PWA tools in a three-step process: energy-dependent analysis, fixing phases to search absolute values of S,P,D,F multipoles, then a final phase search.

## Key numbers

- At least 8 polarization observables are required for a model-independent amplitude analysis, but the resulting amplitudes have an unknown overall phase φ₁(W,θ) that cannot be determined.
- A truncated partial wave analysis theoretically requires only 5 observables from only 2 types of polarization measurements.
- For an S,P wave truncation (ℓ_max=1), there are 4 complex multipoles (E₀₊, E₁₊, M₁₊, M₁₋) leading to 7 free real parameters plus an arbitrary phase.
- Pseudo-data were generated over Elab = 200–1200 MeV with beam energy bins of ΔEγ = 10 MeV and angular bins of Δθ = 10°, with typically 5×10⁶ events generated per observable over the full energy range.
- The Watson theorem is applied for the P₃₃ partial wave up to W ≈ 1550 MeV, where it remains elastic, while P₁₁ becomes inelastic near W ≈ 1300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the classical "complete experiments" framework of Barker, Donnachie and Storrow (1975) and the discrete ambiguity studies of Fasano, Tabakin and Saghai (1992) and Keaton (1996). It directly contradicts the notion that a complete amplitude analysis can yield useful resonance parameters, instead advocating the truncated PWA approach pioneered by Grushin (1980s) for the Δ(1232) region. The numerical demonstration uses the MAID2007 model of Drechsel (2007) and the SAID PWA tools of Arndt (1989), and the method is being applied to kaon photoproduction by Sandorfi (2010) and is relevant for eta photoproduction analyses.
