# Impact of hyperons on structural properties of neutron stars and hybrid stars within the regularized four-dimensional Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity

> We investigate the impact of hyperons and phase transition to quark matter on the structural properties of neutron stars within the regularized four-dimensional Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity (4DEGB). We employ the density-dependent relativistic mean-field model (DDME2) for the hadronic phase and the density-dependent quark mass (DDQM) model for the quark phase to construct hadronic and hybrid equations-of-state (EoSs) that are consistent with the astrophysical constraints. The presence of hyperons softens the EoS and with a phase transition, the EoS further softens, and the speed of sound squared drops to around 0.2 for the maximum mass configuration, which lies in the pure quark phase. Adjusting the Gauss-Bonnet coupling constant, $α$, within its allowed range results in a decrease in the mass-radius relationship for negative $α$, and an increase for positive $α$. In addition, functions are fitted to the maximum mass and its associated radius as a function of the constant $α$ to observe its impact on these properties. We find that positive values of $α$ support massive stars consistent with the 2\,$M_{\odot}$ constraint and NICER measurements, while negative values, although compatible with low-mass radius observations, fail to reach the observed maximum mass, particularly for EoSs involving phase transitions. Therefore, astrophysical observations may be used to effectively constrain the allowed range of $α$.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how hyperons and phase transitions to quark matter affect neutron star structure within regularized four-dimensional Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity, finding that positive values of the Gauss-Bonnet coupling constant support massive stars consistent with the 2 solar mass constraint and NICER measurements, while negative values fail to reach the observed maximum mass, particularly for equations of state involving phase transitions.

## Method

The authors employed the density-dependent relativistic mean-field model (DDME2) for the hadronic phase and the density-dependent quark mass (DDQM) model for the quark phase to construct hadronic and hybrid equations of state. They integrated the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the regularized 4DEGB gravity, varying the Gauss-Bonnet coupling constant in the range from -5 km^2 to +5 km^2, and used Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark deconfinement phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound squared drops to around 0.2 for the maximum mass configuration, which lies in the pure quark phase
- The Gauss-Bonnet coupling constant was varied in the range [-5 km^2, +5 km^2]
- The EoS with hyperons starts to deviate from the pure nucleonic one around 380 MeV/fm^3, marking the appearance of hyperons
- For the hybrid nucleonic EoS, the mixed phase region spans from around 600 MeV/fm^3 to 900 MeV/fm^3, a jump of 300 MeV/fm^3
- For the hybrid EoS with hyperons, the mixed-phase region is around 80 MeV/fm^3
- The hyperon potentials used were U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = 30 MeV, and U_Xi = -4 MeV
- The DDME2 parameter set gives saturation density n_0 = 0.152 fm^{-3}, binding energy -B/A = 16.4 MeV, compressibility K_0 = 252 MeV, symmetry energy S_0 = 32.3 MeV, and symmetry energy slope L_0 = 51 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron stars in modified gravity theories, specifically building on the regularized 4DEGB gravity formalism developed by Fernandes and Mann (2021) and the stellar structure equations derived by Mann (2021) and Ayan (2022). The paper addresses the "hyperon puzzle" identified by Glendenning (1997) and Bombaci (2017), where hyperons soften the equation of state and reduce the maximum mass. The results enable future work incorporating rotation and magnetic anisotropies, which the authors note require significant extension beyond the modified TOV equations to coupled partial differential equations.
