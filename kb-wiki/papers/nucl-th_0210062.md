# A parameter-free optical potential for the heavy-ion elastic scattering proces

> Thirty elastic scattering angular distributions for seven heavy-ion systems, in wide energy ranges, have been studied with the aim of systematizing the optical potential, real and imaginary parts, in a global way. The framework is: i) an extensive systematization of nuclear densities, ii) the energy dependence of the bare potential accounted by a model based on the nonlocal nature of the interaction, and iii) the real and imaginary parts of the optical potential assumed to have the same radial shape.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a parameter-free optical potential, with the imaginary part taken as a constant multiple (approximately 0.78) of the real part derived from a nonlocal model, can describe thirty elastic scattering angular distributions for seven heavy-ion systems across wide energy ranges, though the imaginary part's details are not critical to the fit quality.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was obtained from a nonlocal model that accounts for Pauli exchange between projectile and target nucleons, using a systematization of nuclear densities based on two-parameter Fermi distributions with radius R0 = 1.31A^(1/3) - 0.84 fm and average diffuseness a = 0.56 fm. The imaginary part was assumed to have the same radial shape as the real part, scaled by a single parameter Ni that was adjusted to fit elastic scattering angular distributions for systems including 12C + 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, 208Pb; 16O + 208Pb; and 40Ar + 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- Average value of the scaling parameter Ni = 0.78, found to be approximately system-independent across all studied systems
- Nuclear density radius parametrization: R0 = 1.31A^(1/3) - 0.84 fm
- Average nuclear matter diffuseness: a = 0.56 fm
- Experimental potential strengths described within 25% precision by the systematization
- Reaction cross sections from optical model calculations agree with geometrical barrier penetration model within about 20% precision
- For 12C + 12C at 16 MeV: optical model reaction cross section = 542 mb, barrier penetration model = 508 mb
- For 12C + 208Pb at 96 MeV: optical model reaction cross section = 1854 mb, barrier penetration model = 1498 mb
- For 16O + 208Pb at 129.5 MeV: optical model reaction cross section = 1993 mb, barrier penetration model = 1553 mb

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal model for the real part of the nucleus-nucleus interaction developed by Chamon et al. (2002) and Candido Ribeiro et al. (1997) by applying the same radial shape to the imaginary part, motivated by the Lax-type interaction used by Brandan et al. (1997) at intermediate energies. The parameter-free approach enables reliable estimates of heavy-ion elastic scattering and reaction cross sections without the six free parameters typically required in Woods-Saxon optical model analyses, and the authors indicate extension to halo-nuclei systems is being pursued.
