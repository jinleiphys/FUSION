# Semi-inclusive Pion Production and the d/u Ratio

> We explore the feasibility of directly extracting the large-x valence d/u ratio through a measurement of pions in the current fragmentation region of semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering from protons.

# Semi-inclusive Pion Production and the d/u Ratio

## Key claim

The valence d/u ratio at large x can be extracted from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on protons alone by measuring the ratio of pi- to pi+ yields, provided the pion fragmentation functions are known and the measurement is performed at sufficiently high z (above 0.5). This avoids the problematic nuclear corrections that plague deuteron-based extractions, though the statistical accuracy will be lower than when combining proton and deuteron data.

## Method

The authors compute the ratio R^pi = N_p^{pi-} / N_p^{pi+} for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on proton targets, using the parton model expression that relates the pion yields to products of quark distribution functions and fragmentation functions. They employ the CTEQ4 parton distributions at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 and parameterizations of the leading and non-leading fragmentation functions D(z) and D̅(z) from EMC data. The ratio is evaluated both in the z→1 limit, where R^pi → (1/4) d/u, and integrated over realistic z ranges (z > 0.3 and z > 0.5) to assess experimental feasibility. For comparison, they also compute the bound-neutron-to-proton ratio R_np that would be obtained from deuteron targets, including smearing corrections for Fermi motion and binding effects.

## Key numbers

- The modified d/u ratio approaches 1/5 in the limit x → 1, consistent with perturbative QCD expectations from Farrar and Jackson (1975)
- The correction term to the CTEQ4 d/u ratio at Q^2 ≈ 10 GeV^2 is parameterized as Δ(x) ≈ 0.2 x^2 exp(-(1-x)^2)
- The leading fragmentation function is parameterized as D(z) = 0.7 (1-z)^1.75 / z
- The non-leading fragmentation function is parameterized as D̅(z) = (1-z)/(1+z) D(z)
- At small x, the difference between bound and free nucleon quark distributions is less than about 2%
- For inclusive DIS, a leading twist analysis at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 is valid up to x ∼ 0.8
- Calculations at Q^2 = 100 GeV^2 show only small differences from the results at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to extract the d/u ratio from inclusive DIS on deuterons (Whitlow et al., 1992; European Muon Collaboration, 1987), which suffer from significant nuclear corrections at large x due to Fermi motion and binding effects (Melnitchouk, Schreiber and Thomas, 1994). The authors argue that the HERMES approach of combining proton and deuteron semi-inclusive data (Ackerstaff, 1996) does not avoid these nuclear uncertainties. Instead, they propose using only proton targets with known fragmentation functions from EMC (European Muon Collaboration, 1982, 1983), which enables a cleaner extraction. The method could resolve the discrepancy between current parton distribution fits and the perturbative QCD prediction of d/u → 1/5 as x → 1 (Farrar and Jackson, 1975), and has implications for charged current event rates at HERA (Melnitchouk and Thomas, 1997).
