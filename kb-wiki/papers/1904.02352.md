# Decay $X(3872)\toπ^0π^+π^-$ and $S$-wave $D^0\bar D^0 \toπ^+π^-$ scattering length

> The isospin-breaking decay $X(3872)\to(D^*\bar D+ \bar D^*D) \toπ^0D\bar D\toπ^0π^+π^-$ is discussed. In its amplitude there is a triangle logarithmic singularity, due to which the dominant contribution to $BR(X(3872)\toπ^0π^+π^-)$ comes from the production of the $π^+π^-$ system in a narrow interval of the invariant mass $m_{π^+π^-}$ near the value of $2m_{D^0} \approx 3.73$ GeV. The analysis shows that $BR(X(3872)\toπ^0 π^+π^-)$ can be expected at the level of $10^{-3}$--$10^{-4}$. This estimate includes, in particular, the assumption that the $S$-wave inelastic scattering length $|α''_{D^0\bar D^0 \toπ^+π^-}|\approx1/(2m_{D^{*+}})\approx0.25\ \mbox{GeV}^{-1}$.

## Key claim

The branching ratio of the decay \(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-\) is estimated at the level of \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-4}\), but this estimate depends on the assumption that the \(S\)-wave inelastic scattering length \(|\alpha''_{D^0\bar D^0 \to\pi^+\pi^-}|\) is approximately \(0.25\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), a value the authors describe as a rough guess that experiment must test.

## Method

The authors calculate the decay \(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-\) via a triangle-diagram mechanism: \(X(3872)\to(D^*\bar D+\bar D^*D)\to\pi^0 D\bar D\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-\). They evaluate the loop integrals for both neutral (\(D^{*0}\bar D^0\)) and charged (\(D^{*+}D^-\)) intermediate states, accounting for the finite widths of the \(D^*\) mesons and the \(X(3872)\) propagator with its couplings to \(D^*\bar D\) channels. The \(S\)-wave \(D^0\bar D^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\) amplitude is approximated by a constant scattering length, and the branching ratio is obtained by integrating the differential width over the \(X(3872)\) resonance line shape.

## Key numbers

* The branching ratio \(BR(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-)\) is estimated in the range \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-4}\).
* For \(g^2_A/(16\pi)=0.25\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(\Gamma_{non}=1\ \text{MeV}\), \(BR(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-)\approx 5.14\times 10^{-4}\) (Table I, central mass \(m_X=3.87169\ \text{GeV}\)).
* The \(\pi^+\pi^-\) invariant mass spectrum peaks in a narrow interval near \(2m_{D^0}\approx 3.73\ \text{GeV}\), with the logarithmic singularity located at \(\sqrt{s_2}=3.72982\ \text{GeV}\) in the stable-\(D^{*0}\) limit.
* The \(S\)-wave inelastic scattering length is taken as \(|\alpha''_{D^0\bar D^0\to\pi^+\pi^-}|\approx 1/(2m_{D^{*+}})\approx 0.25\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), corresponding to \(m_{\pi^+}|\alpha''|\approx 0.0347\) in dimensionless units.
* The width \(\Gamma(X\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-;m_X^2)\) at the resonance peak is approximately \(3\ \text{keV}\) for the chosen parameters.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective-Lagrangian studies of \(X(3872)\) decays (Achasov and Shestakov, 2014–2018), which treat the \(X(3872)\) as a compact charmonium state \(\chi_{c1}(2P)\). The proposed decay channel offers a new signature for \(X(3872)\) that does not involve charmed particles or charmonium in the final state, complementing searches for decays such as \(X(3872)\to p\bar p\) (LHCb, 2017). The calculation relies on the triangle-logarithmic-singularity mechanism previously discussed by Landau (1959) and others; the predicted narrow \(\pi^+\pi^-\) peak near 3.73 GeV could be tested at LHCb, Belle II, or PANDA.
