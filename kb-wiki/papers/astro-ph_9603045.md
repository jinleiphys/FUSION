# No Crisis for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> Contrary to a recent claim, the inferred primordial abundances of the light elements are quite consistent with the expectations from standard big bang nucleosynthesis when attention is restricted to direct observations rather than results from chemical evolution models. The number of light neutrino (or equivalent particle) species ($N_ν$) can be as high as 4.53 if the nucleon-to-photon ratio ($η$) is at its lower limit of $1.65 \times 10^{-10}$, as constrained by the upper bound on the deuterium abundance in high redshift quasar absorption systems. Alternatively, with $N_ν= 3$, $η$ can be as high as $8.90 \times 10^{-10}$ if the deuterium abundance is bounded from below by its interstellar value.

## Key claim

Contrary to the claim by Hata et al. (1995) that standard big bang nucleosynthesis is inconsistent with observed light element abundances, the standard model remains viable when one uses direct observational data rather than results from chemical evolution models, and when one accounts for larger systematic uncertainties in the primordial helium-4 mass fraction.

## Method

The authors perform a Monte Carlo analysis of big bang nucleosynthesis predictions, varying nuclear cross-sections and the neutron lifetime, to determine allowed ranges for the number of light neutrino species (N_ν) and the nucleon-to-photon ratio (η). They adopt observational constraints from direct measurements: a new determination of the primordial helium-4 mass fraction (Y_p = 0.243 ± 0.003) using updated helium emissivities, deuterium abundances from quasar absorption systems, and lithium-7 abundances from Population II and Population I stars. They do not use chemical evolution model results for the primordial abundances.

## Key numbers

- Primordial helium-4 mass fraction: Y_p = 0.243 ± 0.003 (statistical), with 95% C.L. range 0.236 – 0.250, based on 27 HII regions in 23 blue compact galaxies using new helium emissivities
- Upper bound on number of light neutrino species: N_ν < 4.53 (95% C.L.), derived using Y_p < 0.25, D/H < 2.5 × 10^{-4}, and ⁷Li/H < 2.6 × 10^{-10}
- For N_ν = 3, the nucleon-to-photon ratio η can be as high as 8.90 × 10^{-10} when using the interstellar deuterium lower limit (D/H > 1.1 × 10^{-5}) and the Population I lithium upper limit (⁷Li/H < 1.5 × 10^{-9})
- For N_ν = 3, the lower limit on η is 2.02 × 10^{-10} when requiring Y_p > 0.236
- High deuterium measurement in quasar absorption system Q0014+813: D/H ≈ (1.4 – 2.5) × 10^{-4}
- Low deuterium measurement in QAS towards Q1937-1009 and Q1009+2956: D/H = (2.4 ± 0.3 stat ± 0.3 syst) × 10^{-5}
- Interstellar deuterium abundance: D/H ≈ (1.5 ± 0.2) × 10^{-5}
- Population II lithium-7 abundance (95% C.L.): ⁷Li/H = (1.05 – 2.63) × 10^{-10}
- Population I lithium-7 abundance (95% C.L.): ⁷Li/H = (9.54 – 15.1) × 10^{-10}

## Context

This paper directly responds to the claim by Hata et al. (1995) of a "crisis" in big bang nucleosynthesis, which found N_ν = 2.1 ± 0.3 using chemical evolution model results. It also addresses the Bayesian analysis by Copi et al. (1995) which required systematic offsets in the helium abundance. The work uses new helium emissivity calculations by Smits (1996) and new observational data on deuterium in quasar absorption systems, showing that the standard model (N_ν = 3) is consistent with direct observations. The relaxed bounds on N_ν permit the existence of a gauge singlet neutrino as suggested in solutions to solar and atmospheric neutrino problems, while the higher allowed η values can make the nucleon density consistent with observed nucleon fractions in clusters of galaxies.
