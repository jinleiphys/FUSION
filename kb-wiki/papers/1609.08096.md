# Chiral study of the $a_0(980)$ resonance and $πη$ scattering phase shifts in light of a recent lattice simulation

> We investigate the $a_0(980)$ resonance within chiral effective field theory through a three-coupled-channel analysis, namely $πη$, $K\bar{K}$ and $πη'$. A global fit to recent lattice finite-volume energy levels from $πη$ scattering and relevant experimental data on a $πη$ event distribution and the $γγ\toπη$ cross section is performed. Both the leading and next-to-leading-order analyses lead to similar and successful descriptions of the finite-volume energy levels and the experimental data. However, these two different analyses yield different $πη$ scattering phase shifts for the physical masses for the $π, K, η$ and $η'$ mesons. The inelasticities, the pole positions in the complex energy plane and their residues are calculated both for unphysical and physical meson masses.

## Key claim

The chiral effective field theory analysis of recent lattice QCD data for πη scattering, combined with experimental data, shows that the a0(980) resonance corresponds to a pole on the fourth Riemann sheet both at the unphysical pion mass used in the lattice simulation (mπ ≈ 391 MeV) and when extrapolated to physical meson masses, but the leading-order and next-to-leading-order analyses yield different πη scattering phase shifts for physical masses.

## Method

The authors performed a three-coupled-channel analysis (πη, K anti-K, and πη') using unitarized U(3) chiral perturbation theory, directly fitting lattice finite-volume energy levels from Dudek et al. (2016) rather than the extracted phase shifts. They included finite-volume effects by modifying the two-point loop function G(s) with the difference between discrete momentum sums and continuous integrals, accounting for moving frames via Lorentz transformation of the Jacobian. A global fit was performed to the lattice energy levels together with experimental data on a πη event distribution from Armstrong et al. (1991) and the γγ → πη cross section from Uehara et al. (2009), using parameters c1, c2 for the event distribution and c1', c2' for the diphoton cross section to model production mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The lattice simulation used a pion mass mπ ≈ 391 MeV, with three different lattice volumes and many moving frames to obtain discrete energy levels.
- The subtraction constant a(μ) in the loop function G(s) is defined at a renormalization scale μ = 770 MeV.
- The finite-volume correction ΔG for the πη channel changes by less than one percent when increasing the momentum cutoff parameter nmax from 20 to 30 for L around 2 fm (mπL ≈ 4).
- The leading-order mixing angle θ is calculated from the formula involving M0 (the singlet η0 mass in the chiral limit), the leading-order kaon mass m_K, and the leading-order pion mass m_π, with Δ² = m_K² - m_π².

## Context

This work extends the unitarized chiral perturbation theory approach of Guo et al. (2011), which previously determined parameters by fitting ππ, πK, and πη scattering data, to directly analyze the lattice QCD results of Dudek et al. (2016) for πη scattering with coupled K anti-K and πη' channels. The finding that the a0(980) corresponds to a fourth Riemann sheet pole at physical masses agrees with the earlier study by Guo et al. (2011), while the lattice results at mπ ≈ 391 MeV also show a fourth sheet pole, unlike a canonical resonance pole on the third sheet. This enables chiral extrapolation of the lattice results to physical meson masses and provides a framework for comparing lattice QCD calculations with experimental data on the a0(980) resonance.
