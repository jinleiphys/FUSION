# Influence of 2p-2h configurations on beta-decay rates

> The effects of the phonon-phonon coupling on the beta-decay rates of neutron-rich nuclei are studied in a microscopic model based on Skyrme-type interactions. The approach uses a finite-rank separable approximation of the Skyrme-type particle-hole (p-h) residual interaction. Very large two-quasiparticle spaces can thus be treated. A redistribution of the Gamow-Teller (G-T) strength is found due to the tensor correlations and the 2p-2h fragmentation of G-T states. As a result, the beta-decay half-lives are decreased significantly. Using the Skyrme interaction SGII together with a volume-type pairing interaction we illustrate this reduction effect by comparing with available experimental data for the Ni isotopes and neutron-rich N=50 isotones. We give predictions for 76Fe and 80Ni in comparison with the case of the doubly-magic nucleus 78Ni which is an important waiting point in the r-process.

## Key claim

The inclusion of 2p-2h configurations via phonon-phonon coupling, together with tensor correlations, significantly reduces beta-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei compared to QRPA calculations alone; the reduction is demonstrated for Ni isotopes and N=50 isotones using the SGII Skyrme interaction, but the quantitative agreement with experimental data is not fully satisfactory, suggesting the tensor interaction strength may need adjustment.

## Method

The authors extend the finite-rank separable approximation (FRSA) of the Skyrme-type particle-hole residual interaction to include coupling between one-phonon and two-phonon configurations in the wave functions of Gamow-Teller states. Starting from Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations of the parent ground state, they solve QRPA equations in very large two-quasiparticle spaces, then construct wave functions as linear combinations of one-phonon and two-phonon configurations, with the Hamiltonian matrix elements coupling these configurations computed from the same Skyrme energy-density functional. The SGII Skyrme interaction is used for the particle-hole channel together with a zero-range tensor interaction (alpha_T = -180 MeV fm^5, beta_T = 120 MeV fm^5) and a volume-type pairing interaction with V_0 = -270 MeV fm^3.

## Key numbers

- For the tensor interaction parameters: alpha_T = -180 MeV fm^5 and beta_T = 120 MeV fm^5
- The Ikeda sum rule for 82Ge: S_- = 56.85 and S_+ = 2.84 with tensor correlations in QRPA; S_- = 54.46 and S_+ = 0.45 without tensor interaction
- The energy shift of the lowest 1+ state due to phonon-phonon coupling: 1.1 MeV for 82Ge and 0.8 MeV for 80Zn, compared to 0.2 MeV for 78Ni
- The tensor interaction shifts the [1_1+]_{QRPA} state downward by up to 2.7 MeV for 80Ni
- The log ft value for the [1_1+]_{QRPA} state decreases from 2.3 for 70Ni to 2.0 for 80Ni when tensor interaction is included
- Predicted half-lives: 0.7 ms for 76Fe and 2.5 ms for 80Ni
- Experimental half-life of 84Se: T_{1/2} = 3.26 +/- 0.10 min
- The phonon-phonon coupling reduces half-lives by a factor of approximately 30
- Half-lives calculated with tensor force are about 60-2000 times shorter than those calculated without

## Context

This work extends the FRSA approach previously developed for charge-exchange excitations (Severyukhin et al., 2012) and tensor correlations (Severyukhin and Sagawa, 2013) to include phonon-phonon coupling, following the quasiparticle-phonon model framework of Soloviev. It builds on earlier Skyrme-RPA plus particle-vibration coupling studies (Niu et al., 2012) and addresses the need to account for 2p-2h fragmentation to resolve discrepancies between QRPA predictions and experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions. The results enable predictions for the r-process waiting point 78Ni and its neighbors 76Fe and 80Ni, and suggest that further work with different Skyrme parametrizations and inclusion of T=0 pairing is needed for quantitative agreement.
