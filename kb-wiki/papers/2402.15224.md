# Distinguishing fission-like events from deep-inelastic collisions

> We propose two functions to distinguish fission-like events from quasi-elastic (QE) scattering and deep inelastic collisions (DIC), for a better analysis of the measured mass-total kinetic energy distributions of binary fragments formed in fusion-fission reactions. We note that the ratio of capture to DIC events evidently decreases with the decreasing of the depth of the capture pocket predicted from the Skyrme energy density functional, with which the capture pocket could be extracted from the measured mass-energy distributions. Together with the improved quantum molecular dynamics simulations, in which the typical contact time of the reaction partners is smaller than 200 fm/c for QE and is larger than 600 fm/c for fission-like events, we find that the ratio of capture to touching cross section systematically increases with the pocket depth.

## Key claim
The authors propose two empirical functions to uniformly separate fission-like events from quasi-elastic scattering and deep-inelastic collisions in measured mass-total kinetic energy distributions, but the method works best for systems where the capture pocket depth is non-zero; for reactions with very shallow or vanishing capture pockets (such as those induced by $^{86}$Kr), the boundaries become less distinct and the DIC yields dominate.

## Method
The authors define an inverted parabolic curve (F1) from the reaction Q-value and compound-nucleus excitation energy to set the upper TKE limit for fission-like fragments, and a linear function (F2) based on the center-of-mass energy and mass transfer to separate DIC from fission-like events. They apply these functions to 13 fusion-fission reactions and compare the resulting event classification with contour lines from prior experimental analyses. They also compute the capture pocket depth (quasi-fission barrier height Bqf) using the Skyrme energy density functional with the SkM* parameter set and the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF2) approach under frozen density approximation, and simulate capture and DIC processes with the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model using contact-time thresholds (200 fm/c for QE, 600 fm/c for fission-like events).

## Key numbers
- For $^{36}$S+$^{238}$U: capture pocket depth Bqf = 9.38 MeV, effective fissility (Z²/A)eff = 30.3.
- For $^{40}$Ca+$^{144}$Sm: Bqf = 9.22 MeV, (Z²/A)eff = 32.0.
- For $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U: Bqf = 5.85 MeV, (Z²/A)eff = 33.3.
- For $^{86}$Kr+$^{208}$Pb: Bqf = 0.26 MeV, (Z²/A)eff = 43.7; calculated ratio σcap/σT = 0.39 at the Bass barrier.
- For $^{40}$Ca+$^{144}$Sm: calculated ratio σcap/σT = 0.86 at the Bass barrier.
- The energy loss per exchanged proton in DIC is approximately 3 MeV, consistent with the coefficient Ec.m./80 ≈ 3 ± 1 MeV used in F2.
- In ImQMD simulations for $^{64}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb: contact time < 200 fm/c for QE, > 600 fm/c for fission-like events.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of mass-energy distributions for heavy and super-heavy fusion-fission reactions, where distinguishing quasi-fission from deep-inelastic collisions has been ambiguous, as noted for $^{86}$Kr+$^{198}$Pt in Kozulin et al. (2019) and for $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu in Kozulin et al. (2014). The proposed method enables systematic extraction of capture cross sections and the capture pocket depth from measured distributions, which is relevant for estimating fusion probabilities in super-heavy element synthesis (e.g., reactions producing elements 119 and 120). The relationship between the ratio of capture to touching cross section and the pocket depth provides a new observable for probing the nucleus-nucleus potential, complementing earlier work on quasi-fission barrier heights by Wang et al. (2011) and the dinuclear system model of Adamian et al. (1997).
