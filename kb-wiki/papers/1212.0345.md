# Renormalization of coherent state variables, within the geometric mapping of algebraic models

> We investigate the geometrical mapping of algebraic models. As particular examples we consider the Semimicriscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) and the Phenomenological Algebraic Cluster Model (PACM), which also contains the vibron model, as a special case. In the geometrical mapping coherent states are employed as trial states. We show that the coherent state variables have to be renormalized and not the interaction terms of the Hamiltonian, as is usually done. The coherent state variables will depend on the total number of bosons and the coherent state variables. The nature of these variables is extracted through a relation obtained by comparing physical observables, such as the distance between the clusters or the quadrupole deformation of the nucleus, to their algebraic counterpart.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the geometrical mapping of algebraic models using coherent states, the coherent state variables themselves must be renormalized (with factors depending on the total boson number N) rather than the interaction terms of the Hamiltonian, contrary to the standard procedure found in prior literature such as Refs. [roosmalen1, roosmalen2] and the review by Jolie (2004). This distinction becomes critical when the total boson number N represents a cutoff that must tend to infinity, as in cluster models.

## Method

The authors analyze the geometrical mapping of the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) and the Phenomenological Algebraic Cluster Model (PACM), which includes the vibron model as a special case. They compute expectation values of algebraic operators (such as the π-boson number operator and an algebraic distance operator) with respect to coherent trial states, and compare these to corresponding geometric expressions. From this comparison, they derive a relation that expresses the coherent state variable α in terms of a physical variable (e.g., inter-cluster distance) and the total boson number N, leading to the redefinition α_m = δ_m / √N.

## Key numbers

- The expectation value of the π-boson number operator in the PACM coherent state is ⟨n_π⟩ = N α² / (1 + α²), which must equal n_π (the average number of π-bosons), implying n_π << N rather than of order N.
- The expectation value of the algebraic distance operator ⟨r_m^a⟩ = √(2Nħ/(mω)) α_m / (1 + α²), defining the algebraic distance r_m^a.
- The square of the variation of the algebraic distance operator in the large N limit is ⟨(r_0^a)²⟩ − ⟨r_0^a⟩² → ħ/(2mω), giving fluctuations of order 1 fm.
- In the SACM, the minimal distance between clusters at α = 0 is given by r_0 ∼ √n_0, where n_0 is the minimal number of π-bosons required by the Pauli principle (Wildermuth condition).

## Context

This work extends the geometrical mapping formalism previously applied to the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) by Ginocchio and Kirson (1980) and to the vibron model by van Roosmalen et al. (1982). It directly challenges the standard renormalization prescription advocated in the review by Jolie (2004) and in Gilmore's textbook (1979), which divides n-body interaction terms by powers of N. The proposed renormalization of coherent state variables enables a consistent geometric interpretation of algebraic cluster models (SACM and PACM) when the cutoff N must approach infinity, and resolves conceptual difficulties in relating algebraic observables (such as inter-cluster distance or quadrupole deformation) to their geometric counterparts.
