# Complete spin structure of the pion-nucleon-loop delta self-energy

## Key claim

The full relativistic calculation of the pion-nucleon-loop contribution to the delta self-energy and dressed propagator, using the most general pion-nucleon-delta vertex including an off-shell parameter, can reproduce the P33 pion-nucleon phase shift with excellent agreement up to 500 MeV pion laboratory momentum, but the off-shell parameter a cannot be uniquely determined from this fit alone.

## Method

The author computed the one-loop delta self-energy from the pion-nucleon loop in vacuum using the most general form of the pion-nucleon-delta vertex, which includes an off-shell parameter a. The imaginary parts of the ten Lorentz-scalar coefficients of the self-energy were calculated analytically in closed form, while the real parts were obtained numerically via a dispersion relation to preserve causality. The dressed delta propagator was then derived by solving the Dyson equation using spin-projection operators, and the result was applied to compute the pion-nucleon phase shift in the spin-3/2 isospin-3/2 channel.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant g = 19, cutoff Lambda = 0.97 GeV, and bare delta mass M_Delta = 1.279 GeV gave an excellent fit to the P33 phase shift up to pion laboratory momentum of 500 MeV.
- An alternative fit with g = 20, Lambda = 0.92 GeV, and M_Delta = 1.273 GeV also produced a very good fit.
- The off-shell parameter a in the range from -1 to 0 gave excellent fits, while values much larger or much smaller destroyed the agreement.
- The sum-rule for the dressed delta propagator was satisfied to better than 1% accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of the delta self-energy beyond the approximations used in Xia, Siemens, and Soyeur (1994), where a simplified self-energy was employed, and contrasts with the nonrelativistic approach of Korpa and Malfliet (1995). The calculation provides a fully relativistic dressed delta propagator with the correct analytic properties, which can serve as a starting point for in-medium calculations of delta properties in hot nuclear matter created in heavy-ion collisions, and for studying other processes involving the delta such as Compton scattering on a nucleon or pion electroproduction.
