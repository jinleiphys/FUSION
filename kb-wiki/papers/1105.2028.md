# Variation of jet quenching from RHIC to LHC and thermal suppression of QCD coupling constant

> We perform a joint jet tomographic analysis of the data on the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ from PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE at LHC. The computations are performed accounting for radiative and collisional parton energy loss with running coupling constant. Our results show that the observed slow variation of $R_{AA}$ from RHIC to LHC indicates that the QCD coupling constant is suppressed in the quark-gluon plasma produced at LHC.

## Key claim

The observed slow variation of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ from RHIC to LHC energies indicates that the QCD coupling constant $\alpha_s$ is suppressed in the hotter quark-gluon plasma produced at LHC, with the data for $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV agreeing better with a freezing value $\alpha_s^{fr}$ smaller by about 20-30% than that obtained from the PHENIX data at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV.

## Method

The author performed a joint jet tomographic analysis of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV (PHENIX data) and $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV (ALICE data), accounting for both radiative and collisional parton energy loss with a running coupling constant. The induced gluon radiation was treated within the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach, multiple gluon emission was accounted for using Landau's method, and the collisional energy loss was included as a perturbation via renormalization of the initial plasma temperature. The QGP was described in the Bjorken model with initial temperature $T_0 \approx 300$ MeV for RHIC and $T_0 \approx 400$ MeV for LHC, and the in-medium suppression of $\alpha_s$ was modeled by reducing the freezing value $\alpha_s^{fr}$ from its vacuum value of about 0.7.

## Key numbers

- For central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, the initial temperature $T_0 \approx 300$ MeV, using $dN_{ch}/d\eta = 687$ from PHENIX.
- For central $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, the initial temperature $T_0 \approx 400$ MeV, using $dN_{ch}/d\eta = 1601$ from ALICE.
- The data for $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV can be described with $\alpha_s^{fr} \approx 0.6 \div 0.7$ for an equilibrium plasma.
- The data for $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV agree better with $\alpha_s^{fr} \approx 0.4 \div 0.5$.
- The collisional energy loss suppresses $R_{AA}$ only by about 15-25%.
- The initial entropy at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV is bigger by a factor of about 2.2 than that at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, translating into about 30% growth of the initial temperature.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous jet tomographic analysis (Zakharov 2008) of the PHENIX data, which used a larger total entropy from hydrodynamic simulations (Heinz) and found better agreement with $\alpha_s^{fr} \approx 0.5$. The present joint analysis of RHIC and LHC data provides quantitative evidence for the thermal suppression of $\alpha_s$ in the hotter QGP at LHC, a possibility previously discussed qualitatively by Horowitz and Gyulassy. The results enable more robust extraction of medium properties from jet quenching across different collision energies, though the author notes the crude approximation of using a single temperature-independent freezing value and leaves more accurate calculations with a temperature-dependent parametrization of $\alpha_s$ for future work.
