# Nucleon mass and pion loops: Renormalization

> Using Dyson--Schwinger equations, the nucleon propagator is analyzed nonperturbatively in a field--theoretical model for the pion--nucleon interaction. Infinities are circumvented by using pion--nucleon form factors which define the physical scale. It is shown that the correct, finite, on--shell nucleon renormalization is important for the value of the mass--shift and the propagator. For physically acceptable forms of the pion--nucleon form factor the rainbow approximation together with renormalization is inconsistent. Going beyond the rainbow approximation, the full pion--nucleon vertex is modelled by its bare part plus a one--loop correction including an effective $Δ$. It is found that a consistent value for the nucleon mass--shift can be obtained as a consequence of a subtle interplay between wave function and vertex renormalization. Furthermore, the bare and renormalized pion--nucleon coupling constant are approximately equal, consistent with results from the Cloudy Bag Model.

## Key claim

Using a Dyson-Schwinger equation with proper on-shell renormalization, the nucleon mass shift from pion loops is shown to be consistent with one-loop results (around -200 MeV) only when the rainbow approximation is abandoned and the full pion-nucleon vertex includes a one-loop correction with an effective Delta resonance; the rainbow approximation with renormalization is inconsistent for physically acceptable pion-nucleon form factors.

## Method

The nucleon propagator was analyzed nonperturbatively via the Dyson-Schwinger equation for a pseudovector pion-nucleon Lagrangian, with infinities circumvented by introducing dipole or exponential pion-nucleon form factors. The rainbow approximation (bare vertex) was tested with on-shell renormalization conditions, then replaced by a model where the full vertex consists of the bare part plus a one-loop correction including an effective Delta resonance described by Rarita-Schwinger spinors, using free nucleon and Delta propagators. The Dyson-Schwinger equation was solved iteratively for complex momenta to evaluate renormalization conditions on the nucleon mass shell.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass shift in the renormalized rainbow treatment is larger than the unrenormalized result by a factor of 1.8 (for dipole cutoff lambda = 0.96 GeV) to 2.9 (for lambda = 1.2 GeV)
- For the vertex-improved Dyson-Schwinger solution with dipole form factor, the mass shift stays around 200 MeV for lambda in the physically interesting region [0.95, 1.05] GeV
- For an exponential form factor, the self-consistent mass shift flattens around Lambda = 0.8 GeV at approximately 190 MeV
- The dipole width parameter from neutrino scattering experiments is lambda = 1.03 +/- 0.04 GeV
- Physical masses used: M_N = 0.94 GeV, M_Delta = 1.23 GeV
- Coupling constants from SU(6) quark model: g_pi N Delta = sqrt(72/25) * g, g_pi Delta Delta = (6/5) * g, with g = g_A * M_N / f_pi and g_A = 1.26

## Context

This work extends a previous study by Hecht et al. (2002) that analyzed covariant Euclidean nucleon mass shift calculations in rainbow approximation without renormalization, which suggested almost all nucleon mass shift comes from one-loop pion dressing. The present results are consistent with one-loop results from the Cloudy Bag Model (Thomas, 1981) and Chiral Perturbation Theory (Langacker, 1973; Becher, 1999), and the finding that bare and renormalized coupling constants are approximately equal agrees with Cloudy Bag Model analyses. The work enables more reliable extrapolation of nucleon lattice data to physical quark masses and constrains development of covariant nucleon models.
