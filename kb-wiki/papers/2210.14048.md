# Crustal failure as a tool to probe hybrid stars

> It is currently unknown if neutron stars (NSs) are composed of nucleons only or are hybrid stars, i.e., in addition to nucleonic crusts and outer cores, they also possess quark cores. Quantum chromodynamics allows for such a possibility, but accurate calculations relevant for compact stars are still elusive. Here we investigate some crust-breaking aspects of hybrid stars. We show that the crust-breaking frequency and maximum fiducial ellipticity are sensitive to the quark-hadron density jump and equation of state stiffness. Remarkably, the crust-breaking frequency related to static tides scales linearly with the mass of the star (for a given companion's mass), and its slope encompasses information about the microphysics of the star. However, for precise crust-breaking frequency predictions, relativistic corrections to Kepler's third law and the Newtonian tidal field should not be ignored. When a liquid quark core touches an elastic hadronic phase (the result of a significant energy-density jump), the maximum ellipticity can increase around an order of magnitude when compared to a liquid quark core touching a liquid hadronic phase. That is relevant because it would increase the odds of detecting continuous gravitational waves from NSs. Our order-of-magnitude analysis also suggests that a given upper limit to the ellipticity(crust-breaking frequency) could have representatives in stars with either small or intermediate(large) energy-density jumps. Therefore, when upper limits to the ellipticity for isolated stars are better constrained or electromagnetic radiation (e.g., gamma-ray precursors) is detected along with gravitational waves in inspiraling binary systems, they may help constrain some aspects of phase transitions in NSs.

## Key claim

The crust-breaking frequency and maximum fiducial ellipticity of hybrid stars are sensitive to the quark-hadron density jump and equation of state stiffness, but the paper's order-of-magnitude analysis suggests that a given upper limit to the ellipticity or crust-breaking frequency could have representatives in stars with either small or intermediate (large) energy-density jumps, meaning that these observables alone may not uniquely determine the phase transition parameters without additional information.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hybrid star models with a sharp first-order (Maxwell construction) phase transition between a hadronic phase and a quark phase, using both toy-model equations of state (linear MIT-bag-like for quarks, polytropic for hadrons) and more realistic EOSs (SLy4 crust joined to polytropic hadronic cores and MIT-bag-like quark cores). They computed static perturbations in general relativity with elasticity confined to the hadronic crust, using the von Mises scalar strain criterion (breaking when Θ > 0.1) to determine the maximum ellipticity for isolated stars and the orbital frequency at which crust failure begins in binary systems.

## Key numbers

- The crust-breaking GW frequency scales linearly with neutron star mass for a given companion mass, with deviations up to 2-3% for c_s^2 = 1/3 and 0.2-0.3% for c_s^2 = 1 in toy-model EOSs.
- For realistic EOSs with a 1.4 M_sun companion, crust-breaking GW frequencies range from below 1 kHz (stiff EOSs) to above 2 kHz (softer EOSs), with the merger reference frequency around 2 kHz for 1.4 M_sun, 10 km radius stars.
- When a liquid quark core touches an elastic hadronic phase (due to a significant energy-density jump), the maximum net fiducial ellipticity can increase by around an order of magnitude compared to when it touches a liquid hadronic phase.
- For toy-model EOSs, the critical density jump where the quark core starts touching the elastic hadronic phase is η_crit ≈ 2.352 for M = 1.4 M_sun with c_s^2 = 1.
- Tidal deformabilities for 1.4 M_sun hybrid stars with toy-model EOSs (1/3 ≤ c_s^2 ≤ 1) are in the range 450-550, consistent with GW170817 constraints.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of crustal failure in purely hadronic neutron stars (Gittins et al. 2020, 2021) to hybrid stars with quark cores, and builds on the formalism for static perturbations in general relativity with elasticity developed by Pereira et al. (2020) and Gittins et al. (2021). The results enable future constraints on phase transitions in neutron stars by combining upper limits on ellipticity from continuous gravitational wave searches (LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA) with potential electromagnetic precursors (e.g., gamma-ray bursts) from inspiraling binary systems, complementing tidal deformability constraints from binary inspiral events like GW170817.
