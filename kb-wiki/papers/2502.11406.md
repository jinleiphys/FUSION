# Flavor dependence of Energy-energy correlators

> Energy-energy correlators (EECs) within high energy jets serve as a key experimentally accessible quantity to probe the scale and structure of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The CMS Collaboration's first measurement of the modification to the EEC within single inclusive jets in Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p collisions reveals a significant enhancement at small angles, which may arise from jet transverse momentum $p_T$ selection biases due to jet energy loss. We investigate the dependence of jet EECs on the flavor of the initiating parton. The EEC distribution of a gluon jet is broader and the peak of transition from perturbative to non-perturbative regime occurs at a larger angle than a quark jet. Such flavor dependence leads to the different EECs for $γ$-jets and single inclusive jets due to their different flavor composition. It is also responsible for a colliding energy dependence of EECs of single inclusive jets at fixed jet energy. We also investigate the impact of flavor composition variation on the $p_T$ dependence of the jet EEC. We further propose that a change in the gluon jet fraction in A+A collisions compared to p+p can also contribute to a non-negligible enhancement of the medium modified EEC at small angles. Using the \textsc{Jewel} model, we predict the reduction of the gluon jet fraction in A+A collisions and estimate its impact on the EEC.

## Key claim

The enhancement of the energy-energy correlator (EEC) at small angles in Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p collisions arises not only from jet transverse momentum selection bias due to energy loss, but also from a reduction in the gluon jet fraction in A+A collisions compared to p+p collisions, which contributes a non-negligible additional enhancement.

## Method

The authors use the PYTHIA 8.3 Monte Carlo event generator to simulate vacuum parton showers and the JEWEL 2.3 model (with initial mean temperature 550 MeV and 0-10% centrality) to simulate medium-induced effects in heavy-ion collisions. Jets are reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with radius R=0.4 via FastJet. The EEC distributions for quark-initiated and gluon-initiated jets are computed separately, and the gluon jet fraction is extracted from PYTHIA simulations of di-jet events and compared to the fraction after quenching in JEWEL.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of EEC inside quark jets to that inside gluon jets at small angles decreases from 3.0 in vacuum to approximately 2.3 after medium modification in the JEWEL model.
- The initial gluon jet fraction for di-jet events with jet pT in (120-140) GeV/c is approximately 66% in PYTHIA; after accounting for jet quenching in JEWEL, this fraction decreases to 64.5%.
- A 10% change in the gluon jet fraction leads to approximately a 10% variation in the EEC distribution at small angles.
- The medium-induced flavor selection contributes approximately 6% of the enhancement of the EEC at small angles observed at LHC energies.
- The quark/gluon fraction for single inclusive jets at pT in (200-250) GeV/c is approximately 45%/55%.

## Context

This work extends the CMS Collaboration's first measurement of EEC modification in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV (CMS 2024), which showed enhancement at small angles that was previously attributed primarily to jet pT selection bias (Andres 2024, Xing 2024, Chen 2024). The authors demonstrate that flavor composition changes between A+A and p+p collisions constitute an additional non-negligible contribution. The results enable more precise interpretation of EEC measurements as probes of the quark-gluon plasma and motivate comparisons of EEC at RHIC energies (accessible to sPHENIX and STAR) versus LHC energies (accessible to ALICE) to study the x-dependence of jet EECs.
