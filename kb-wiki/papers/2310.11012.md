# A Data-Driven Density Functional Model for Nuclear Systems

> Through ensemble learning with multitasking and complex connection neural networks, we aggregated nuclear properties, including ground state charge radii, binding energies, and single-particle state information obtained from the Kohn-Sham auxiliary single-particle systems. Compared to traditional density functional theory, our model can more accurately characterize nuclear ground state information. Aiming at binding energy, the root mean square error is reduced to 450 keV. Although the complexity involving the nuclear interaction is skipped, the model has not completely devolved into a black box. Leveraging the correlation between densities and binding energies, we calculate the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb to be 0.223 fm. This model will advance our understanding of nuclear properties and accelerate the integration of machine learning into modern nuclear physics.

## Key claim
The model achieves a root-mean-square error of 450 keV for nuclear binding energies by using ensemble learning with multiple density-to-energy networks, but this accuracy depends on combining model-dependent densities (from Kohn-Sham networks calibrated to charge radii) with non-model-dependent shell and parity features, and the authors note that the description for nuclear masses was initially disappointing at 1.6 MeV before ensemble averaging.

## Method
The authors constructed a density-to-energy network (DTEN) that takes as input six densities (spatial, kinetic, and spin-orbit densities for both protons and neutrons) from Kohn-Sham single-particle wave functions, plus ten shell and parity features derived from proton and neutron numbers. The densities were processed through convolutional layers while the shell features went through fully connected layers, with all branches concatenated and passed through additional fully connected layers to predict binding energy. To improve accuracy, they employed weighted ensemble learning across multiple DTENs with identical architecture but different random initializations, using Bayesian weighting based on each model's RMS error.

## Key numbers
- Root-mean-square error for binding energy: 450 keV (0.455 MeV) after ensemble learning, compared to approximately 10 MeV/c² for the initial SHF+BCS theory
- The initial single DTEN gave an RMS error of 1.6 MeV before ensemble averaging
- Neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb calculated to be 0.223 fm, falling on the edge of the experimental measurement range from Adhikari (2021)
- Training used approximately 2400 nuclei with proton numbers greater than 40, with an 8:2 split for training and validation sets
- The energy variation near the minimum neutron skin thickness is less than 100 keV

## Context
This work extends the Kohn-Sham Network (KSN) approach from Yang (2023) by establishing neural network mappings from densities to binding energies, replacing the search for a parameterized energy density functional while retaining insights from traditional functionals. It improves upon prior machine learning approaches for nuclear masses including Bayesian neural networks (Utama 2016, Niu 2018), radial basis functions (Niu 2013), kernel ridge regression (Wu 2020), and Gaussian processes (Neufcourt 2019, 2020). The model enables exploration of correlations between densities and binding energies, particularly for inferring neutron skin thickness, and the authors identify future directions including three-dimensionalizing the model and introducing adversarial neural networks.
