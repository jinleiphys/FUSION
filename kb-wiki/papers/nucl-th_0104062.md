# Dynamical description of the breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei 11Be and 19C

> We investigate the breakup of the one-neutron halo nuclei 11Be and 19C within a dynamical model of the continuum excitation of the projectile. The time evolution of the projectile in coordinate space is described by solving the three-dimensional time dependent Schroedinger equation, treating the projectile-target (both Coulomb and nuclear) interaction as a time dependent external perturbation. The pure Coulomb breakup dominates the relative energy spectra of the fragments in the peak region, while the nuclear breakup is important at higher relative energies. The coherent sum of the two contributions provides a good overall description of the experimental spectra. Cross sections of the first order perturbation theory are derived as a limit of our dynamical model. The dynamical effects are found to be of the order of 10-15% for the beam energies in the range of 60 - 80 MeV/nucleon. A comparison of our results with those of a post form distorted wave Born approximation shows that the magnitudes of the higher order effects are dependent on the theoretical model.

## Key claim

The dynamical (nonperturbative) treatment of the breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei shows that higher-order effects in the Coulomb breakup are generally small (10-15% reduction of the first-order peak cross sections), but the magnitude of these higher-order effects depends on the theoretical model used for comparison, contradicting claims of substantial higher-order effects from other approaches.

## Method

The time evolution of the projectile in coordinate space was described by solving the three-dimensional time-dependent Schroedinger equation, treating the projectile-target Coulomb and nuclear interactions as a time-dependent external perturbation. The projectile center of mass was assumed to follow a hyperbolic trajectory (not a straight line), and spins of the particles were included explicitly in the nuclear model. First-order perturbation theory results were derived as a limit of the same dynamical model by keeping couplings only between the initial channel and all possible final channels in the perturbation potential.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical effects (higher-order corrections) are of the order of 10-15% for beam energies in the range of 60-80 MeV/nucleon
- For 11Be on 208Pb at 72 MeV/nucleon: ground state binding energy of 504 keV for the 2s1/2 neutron coupled to 10Be core; angular integration over projectile c.m. angles 0-3 degrees corresponding to minimum impact parameter of about 12 fm
- For 19C on 208Pb at 67 MeV/nucleon: calculations performed with two binding energy values of 530 keV and 650 keV; the 530 keV case overestimates peak cross sections by 35-40%, while the 650 keV case agrees with data
- The 530 keV binding energy with spectroscopic factor 0.67 (from first-order perturbation theory analysis in prior work) is compared to the dynamical model result with binding energy 650 keV and spectroscopic factor 1
- Higher-order effects reduce peak cross sections of first-order theory by about 10% for both binding energy values of 19C

## Context

This work extends previous time-dependent Schroedinger equation calculations for halo nuclei breakup (Esbensen 1995, Kido 1996) by including hyperbolic trajectories instead of straight-line approximations, explicit spin dependence, and both Coulomb and nuclear breakup contributions without perturbative approximations. The results contradict the conclusion of Typel (2001) that higher-order effects are substantial for 19C breakup, and show that the finite-range DWBA calculations of Chatterjee (2000) give different higher-order effects than the dynamical model, indicating model dependence. The findings enable more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors and binding energies from breakup data, and motivate further experimental studies with tagged core states as in Maddalena (2001).
