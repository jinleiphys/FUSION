# Study of excited nucleon states at EBAC: status and plans

> We present an overview of a research program for the excited nucleon states in Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at Jefferson Lab. Current status of our analysis of the meson production reactions based on the unitary dynamical coupled-channels model is summarized, and the N* pole positions extracted from the constructed scattering amplitudes are presented. Our plans for future developments are also discussed.

# Study of excited nucleon states at EBAC: status and plans

## Key claim

The EBAC dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) analysis of meson production reactions can extract N* pole positions from scattering amplitudes, but the current model finds that all extracted N* states originate from bare N* states introduced in the transition potential, with no meson-baryon molecular type resonances generated dynamically from pure meson-exchange processes; the analysis is limited to energies up to W=2 GeV and has not yet incorporated all processes with the ππN three-body unitary cut.

## Method

The EBAC-DCC model solves coupled-channels integral equations for partial wave amplitudes of meson-baryon scattering, treating the πN, ηN, πΔ, ρN, and σN channels below W=2 GeV. The transition potential is decomposed into a meson-exchange part derived from phenomenological Lagrangians and a resonant part from bare N* states; the hadronic parameters were first determined by fitting to SAID πN partial wave amplitudes up to W=2 GeV using the MINUIT library, then electromagnetic parameters were fixed separately by analyzing γp→πN and p(e,e'π)N reactions up to W=1.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- 16 bare N* states were introduced in the current EBAC-DCC model, chosen as the minimum number needed to explain the data
- The analysis of πN scattering was performed up to W=2 GeV, fitting to SAID partial wave amplitudes
- The γp→πN and p(e,e'π)N analyses were performed up to W=1.6 GeV
- The coupled-channels effect on photoproduction cross sections contributes about 30-40% in all W region up to 1.6 GeV
- The electroproduction analysis was performed at Q²=0.4 (GeV/c)²
- Extracted pole positions include: S11 at (1540, 191) MeV and (1642, 41) MeV; P11 at (1357, 76) MeV and (1364, 105) MeV; P33(Δ) at (1211, 50) MeV; D13 at (1521, 58) MeV; D15 at (1654, 77) MeV; F15 at (1674, 53) MeV; S31 at (1563, 95) MeV; D33 at (1604, 106) MeV; F35 at (1738, 110) MeV and (1928, 165) MeV; F37 at (1858, 100) MeV
- No resonance pole was found for P33(1600), P13, or P31 in the complex energy region Re(E)≤2000 MeV and -Im(E)≤250 MeV
- Two P11 poles were found near the Roper resonance region: (1357, 76) MeV and (1364, 105) MeV

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels approach proposed by Matsuyama, Sato, and Lee (2007) for analyzing meson production reactions to extract N* properties. The EBAC program aims to go beyond partial-wave analysis by connecting extracted N* parameters to hadron structure calculations such as constituent quark models and Lattice QCD simulations. The finding of two P11 poles near the Roper resonance agrees with independent analyses by the GWU group and the Jülich group (both 2006-2009), despite their different theoretical frameworks, and the next step is to determine whether both poles contribute to the physical Roper resonance by examining their residues.
