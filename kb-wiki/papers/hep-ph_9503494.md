# Bose-Einstein Correlations for Expanding Finite Systems

> There are two length-scales present simultaneously in all the principal directions for three-dimensionally expanding, finite systems. These are discussed in detail for the case of a longitudinally expanding system with a transverse flow and a transverse temperature profile. For systems with large geometrical sizes we find an $m_t$-scaling for the parameters of the Bose-Einstein correlation function, which may be valid in the whole transverse mass region for certain model parameters. In this limit, the Bose-Einstein correlations view only a small part of the source. The large geometrical sizes can be inferred from a simultaneous analysis of the invariant momentum distribution and the Bose-Einstein correlation function. A preliminary analysis of the NA44 data indicates that instead of a small fireball we are observing a big and expanding snowball in $S + Pb $ reactions at CERN SPS energies.

# Bose-Einstein Correlations for Expanding Finite Systems

## Key claim
For three-dimensionally expanding finite systems, two length-scales (geometrical and thermal) are simultaneously present in all principal directions; when geometrical sizes are large, the Bose-Einstein correlation function parameters exhibit an $m_t$-scaling that may be valid across the entire transverse mass region for certain model parameters, and the large geometrical sizes can only be inferred from a combined analysis of the invariant momentum distribution and the correlation function.

## Method
The authors model the emission function for high-energy heavy ion reactions with a longitudinally expanding system that includes transverse flow (parameter $b$) and a transverse temperature profile (parameter $a$), plus a finite duration $H(\tau)$. The integrals are evaluated using the saddle-point method in the longitudinally comoving system (LCMS) under approximations $\eta_s \ll 1$ and $r_{x,s} \ll \tau_0$, keeping leading-order terms for the radius parameters while summing sub-leading terms from the $\cosh(\eta-y)$ pre-factor for the invariant momentum distribution.

## Key numbers
- For central $S + Pb$ reactions at CERN SPS with 200 AGeV, the intercept parameter $\lambda_{\pi^+} = 0.56 \pm 0.02$ at $m_t = 150$ MeV and $0.55 \pm 0.02$ at $m_t = 450$ MeV, suggesting the halo contains $1 - \sqrt{\lambda} = 25 \pm 2\%$ of all pions.
- Preliminary analysis yields large geometrical source sizes for pions: $R_G(\pi) \approx 8$ fm.
- Freeze-out time $\tau_0 \approx 8$ fm/c, with vanishing duration $\Delta \tau$ for both pions and kaons.
- Freeze-out temperature $T \approx 80$ MeV.
- Kaons appear from a smaller region: $R_G(K) \approx 3.5$ fm.
- The fit to NA44 data for both pions and kaons gives $\chi^2 / NDF = 2.2$.

## Context
This work extends earlier treatments of one-dimensionally expanding finite systems (Csorgo and Lorstad, 1994) and three-dimensionally expanding cylindrically symmetric finite models (Csorgo and Lorstad, 1995) by including both transverse flow and transverse temperature gradients simultaneously. The analysis contradicts the interpretation of a small fireball in $S + Pb$ reactions, instead suggesting a large expanding "snowball" with a possible hot core. The results enable future data analyses to extract both geometrical sizes and temperature/flow profiles from combined measurements of Bose-Einstein correlation functions and invariant momentum distributions, building on the halo formalism of Bolz et al. (1993).
