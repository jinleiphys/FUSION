# Surpassing Wigner's causality bound in relativistic scattering with zero-range interaction

> It is shown that the relativistic zero-range potential scattering surpasses Wigner's causality bound, while being consistent with causality. The relativistic theory shows in addition a richer analytic structure, such as a $K$-matrix pole necessarily accompanying the bound-state solution. Implications of these results for the effective-field theory of nuclear forces are briefly considered.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a relativistic zero-range potential scattering can yield positive effective-range parameters while remaining consistent with causality, in direct opposition to Wigner's non-relativistic causality bound which requires the effective range to be non-positive. The result holds provided the coupling constant lies outside the acausal interval \(-8\pi^2 < \lambda < 0\).

## Method

The author solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a constant (zero-range) potential \(\lambda\) in the equal-mass case, obtaining an algebraic scattering amplitude \(T(s)\). Causality is enforced via the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for light-by-light scattering, which is satisfied only for \(\lambda \leq -8\pi^2\) or \(\lambda \geq 0\). The effective-range expansion parameters are then derived analytically from the relativistic scattering amplitude and compared to the causal domain.

## Key numbers

* The causal domain for the coupling constant is \(-\infty < \lambda \leq -8\pi^2\) or \(\lambda \geq 0\); the acausal domain is \(-8\pi^2 < \lambda < 0\).
* The effective range satisfies \(r_1 \geq 2/(\pi m)\), where \(m\) is the particle mass.
* For a shallow bound state, the momentum at which the phase shift crosses 90 degrees is approximately \(k_{\pi/2} \approx B^{1/4} m^{3/4}\), where \(B = 2m - M\) is the binding energy.
* The bound-state mass for a shallow state is \(M^2 \simeq 4m^2 / [1 + (16\pi)^2 \lambda^{-2}]\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of zero-range forces in effective field theory, which was previously limited by the non-relativistic Wigner bound derived by Wigner (1955) and applied to nuclear forces by Phillips and Cohen (1996). The relativistic treatment resolves the long-standing problem that zero-range forces in non-relativistic EFT yield negative effective-range parameters, in disagreement with nucleon-nucleon scattering data. The results suggest a possible revival of the "perturbative pions" approach of Kaplan (1998) within a relativistic framework, and enable a new power-counting scheme where finite-range forces (such as one-pion exchange) could be deferred to subleading orders.
