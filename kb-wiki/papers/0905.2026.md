# Determination of the 3He+alpha\to 7Be asymp. normalization coefficients (nucl. vertex constants) and their application for extrapolation of the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be astroph. S-factors to the solar energy region

> A new analysis of the modern precise measured astrophysical $S$ factors for the direct capture $^3He(α,γ)^7{\rm {Be}}$ reaction [B.S. Nara Singh {\it et al.}, Phys.Rev.Lett. {\bf 93}, 262503 (2004); D. Bemmerer {\it et al.}, Phys.Rev.Lett. {\bf 97}, 122502 (2006); F.Confortola {\it et al.}, Phys.Rev.C {\bf 75}, 065803 (2007), T.A.D.Brown {\it et al.}, Phys.Rev. C {\bf 76}, 055801 (2007) and A Di Leva, {\it et al.},Phys.Rev.Lett. {\bf 102}, 232502 (2009)] populating to the ground and first excited states of $^7{\rm Be}$ is carried out based on the modified two - body potential approach. New estimates are obtained for the $^{\glqq}$indirectly determined\grqq\, values of the asymptotic normalization constants (the nuclear vertex constants) for $^3{\rm {He}}+α\to{\rm {^7Be}}$(g.s.) and $^3{\rm {He}}+α\to{\rm {^7Be}}$(0.429 MeV) as well as the astrophysical $S$ factors $S_{34}(E)$ at E$\le$ 90 keV, including $E$=0. The values of asymptotic normalization constants have been used for getting information about the $α$-particle spectroscopic factors for the mirror (${\rm{^7Li}}{\rm {^7Be}}$)-pair.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the modified two-body potential approach (MTBPA) allows extraction of asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for the virtual decays \(^3\mathrm{He}+\alpha\to{}^7\mathrm{Be}\) directly from precise experimental astrophysical \(S\) factors, with the condition that the reaction is peripheral (dominated by the nuclear surface and external region) over the energy range 92.9–1200 keV, thereby reducing ambiguities from the choice of Woods–Saxon potential parameters that plague standard two-body potential model calculations.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed modern precise experimental astrophysical \(S\) factors for the \(^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}\) reaction populating the ground state and first excited state (0.429 MeV) of \(^7\mathrm{Be}\) from five experimental datasets (Nara Singh 2004, Bemmerer 2006, Confortola 2007, Brown 2007, Di Leva 2009) using the modified two-body potential approach (MTBPA). In MTBPA, the astrophysical \(S\) factor is expressed directly in terms of the ANCs for \(^3\mathrm{He}+\alpha\to{}^7\mathrm{Be}\) rather than through spectroscopic factors; the bound-state and scattering wave functions were calculated with a Woods–Saxon potential split with parity-dependent spin-orbit term, with geometric parameters varied in physically acceptable ranges (\(R\) = 1.62–1.98 fm, \(a\) = 0.63–0.77 fm) around standard values (\(R\) = 1.80 fm, \(a\) = 0.70 fm). The ANCs were extracted by fitting the calculated ratio functions to the experimental \(S\) factors, and the resulting ANCs were then used to extrapolate the astrophysical \(S\) factors to solar energies (\(E \le 90\) keV, including \(E = 0\)).

## Key numbers

- Weighted mean ANC squared for \(^3\mathrm{He}+\alpha\to{}^7\mathrm{Be}\)(g.s.): \(C_{1,3/2}^2 = 24.7^{+2.5}_{-3.1}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (from all experimental data, with asymmetric uncertainty due to systematic discrepancy between datasets).
- Weighted mean ANC squared for \(^3\mathrm{He}+\alpha\to{}^7\mathrm{Be}\)(0.429 MeV): \(C_{1,1/2}^2 = 20.6^{+2.2}_{-2.7}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (from all experimental data).
- Weighted mean total astrophysical \(S\) factor at zero energy: \(S_{34}(0) = 0.548^{+0.027}_{-0.030}\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\) (from all experimental data).
- Weighted mean total astrophysical \(S\) factor at 23 keV: \(S_{34}(23\ \mathrm{keV}) = 0.560^{+0.028}_{-0.031}\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\).
- Ratio of ANCs for the mirror pair: \(R_{C;3/2} = 1.83^{+0.18}_{-0.25}\) and \(R_{C;1/2} = 1.77^{+0.19}_{-0.24}\) for ground and first excited states, respectively.
- Ratio of single-particle ANCs for the mirror pair: \(R_{C^{(sp)};3/2} = 1.37 \pm 0.02\) and \(R_{C^{(sp)};1/2} = 1.40 \pm 0.09\).
- Ratio of spectroscopic factors for the mirror pair: \(R_{Z;3/2} = 1.34^{+0.13}_{-0.18}\) and \(R_{Z;1/2} = 1.26^{+0.16}_{-0.19}\) for ground and first excited states, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the modified two-body potential approach previously applied to radiative proton and alpha-particle capture by light nuclei (Igamov 2007, Igamov 2008, Artemov 2009) to the \(^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}\) reaction, which is a critical link in the \(pp\)-chain of solar hydrogen burning. The results improve upon earlier analyses by Igamov (1997) that underestimated contributions from the nuclear interior and used less precise experimental data, and they differ noticeably from ANC values obtained by Nollett (2001) using non-self-consistent potentials and by the \(R\)-matrix method of Descouvemont (2004) which assumed equal ANCs for ground and excited states. The extracted ANCs enable extrapolation of the astrophysical \(S\) factor to solar energies with reduced model dependence, and the derived spectroscopic factor ratios for the mirror (\(^7\mathrm{Li},^7\mathrm{Be}\)) pair provide empirical constraints on mirror symmetry breaking for alpha-particle spectroscopic factors, complementing microscopic cluster model calculations by Timofeyuk (2007) and resonating-group method results by Kajino (1986).
