# Finite Temperature Many-Body Theory with the Lipkin Model

> We have compared exact numerical results for the Lipkin model at finite temperature with Hartree-Fock theory and with the results of including in addition the ring diagrams. In the simplest version of the Lipkin model the Hartree-Fock approach shows a ``phase transition" which is absent in the exact results. For more realistic cases, Hartree-Fock provides a very good approximation and a modest improvement is obtained by adding the ring diagrams.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock approximation predicts a spurious "phase transition" in the simplest version of the Lipkin model (only the V interaction nonzero) that is absent in the exact finite-temperature results; however, when all interaction parameters are nonzero, Hartree-Fock provides a very good approximation to the exact internal energy, with only modest improvement from adding ring diagrams.

## Method

The authors solved the Lipkin model exactly by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the quasi-spin formulation for a system of 16 particles in 32 single-particle states (half-filled). They compared the exact grand canonical ensemble results with finite-temperature Hartree-Fock (HF) theory and with a treatment that adds the particle-particle hole-hole ring diagrams (random phase approximation, RPA) to the HF grand potential, including the necessary thermodynamic derivatives of the HF eigenvalues, wavefunctions, and RPA eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- For the case with only V = -0.65 (U=W=0), the HF critical temperature is T_c = 2.43 (in units of the single-particle spacing xi=1), where a spurious phase transition occurs.
- For the case with U=W=-0.02 and V=-0.65, the HF curve becomes smooth and the ring diagrams bring the internal energy close to the exact result across all temperatures.
- For the case with U=W=-0.2 and V=-0.65, the internal energy at low temperature is roughly doubled compared to the unperturbed case, and pure HF gives very good agreement with the exact answer.
- In the infinite temperature limit, the internal energy approaches E = -(1/4)pW, which for the V-only case gives zero.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature study of the Lipkin model by Yang, Heyer, and Kuo (1986) to finite temperature, where they found HF gave a very good approximation to the exact ground state energy and the ring series gave a small improvement. The finite-temperature Lipkin model had previously been studied by Da Providencia (1975), Hatsopoulos (1975), and Abe (1975) for the simplest version, focusing on excited states and boson expansions. The results enable assessment of the accuracy of finite-temperature many-body approximations for more realistic nuclear Hamiltonians, particularly for hot nuclei in heavy-ion collisions and supernova explosions.
