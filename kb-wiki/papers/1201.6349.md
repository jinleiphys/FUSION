# Hadronization and Hadronic Freeze-Out in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We analyze hadrochemical freeze-out in central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies, employing the hybrid version of the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model, which describes the transition from a hydrodynamic stage to hadrons by the Cooper-Frye mechanism, and matches to a final hadron-resonance cascade. We fit the results both before and after the cascade stage using the Statistical Model, to assess the effect of the cascade phase. We observe a strong effect on antibaryon yields except anti-Omega, resulting in a shift in T and mu_B of the freeze-out curve. We discuss indications of a similar effect in SPS and RHIC data, and propose a method to recover the bulk hadron freeze-out conditions.

# Hadronization and Hadronic Freeze-Out in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The hadron-resonance cascade afterburner in the UrQMD hybrid model does not cool the system in chemical equilibrium from the parton-hadron transition temperature down to the empirical freeze-out curve; instead, it selectively suppresses antibaryon yields (except anti-Omega) at SPS energies, distorting the hadron yield distribution away from equilibrium and producing an apparent downward shift in the statistical model freeze-out parameters (T, mu_B) that is not a genuine cooling effect.

## Method

The authors employed the hybrid version of the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, which uses a 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion terminated by the Cooper-Frye mechanism when the energy density of flow cells falls below a critical value of 0.8 GeV/fm^3, followed by an optional hadron-resonance cascade afterburner. They fitted the total hadron multiplicities from central Pb+Pb collisions at five SPS energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3, 7.6, 8.7, 12.3, and 17.3 GeV) using the grand canonical statistical model, both before and after the cascade stage, and compared the resulting (T, mu_B) freeze-out parameters.

## Key numbers

- At 158 AGeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV), the statistical model fit to UrQMD results shifts from (T = 160 MeV, mu_B = 246 MeV) before the cascade to (T = 151 MeV, mu_B = 277 MeV) after the cascade, with chi^2/DOF worsening from 4.7 to 28.2.
- Antibaryon suppression in the cascade ranges from 45% to 25% for antiprotons, anti-Lambda, and anti-Xi at 40 and 158 AGeV, while other hadron yields remain essentially constant.
- The critical energy density for the Cooper-Frye transition was set to epsilon_c = 0.8 GeV/fm^3.
- Statistical model fit to NA49 data at 158 AGeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV) yields T = 150.5 ± 1.7 (3.1) MeV, mu_B = 266 ± 6 (11) MeV, gamma_S = 0.915 ± 0.034 (0.063), with chi^2/DOF = 26.9/10.
- Chemical equilibration in a box with constant energy density requires upward of 25 fm/c, longer than the cascade expansion time.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bass and Dumitru (2000) on antibaryon suppression in hadronic cascades and by Becattini et al. (previous publication) on the freeze-out curve, correcting a premature conclusion about an alternate QCD phase. The results challenge the interpretation that the empirical freeze-out curve from statistical model analysis represents a genuine chemical equilibrium cooling from the parton-hadron coexistence line inferred from lattice QCD (at low mu_B) and chiral restoration theory (at high mu_B). The proposed method of excluding antiprotons, anti-Lambda, and anti-Xi from statistical model fits recovers freeze-out conditions closer to the lattice hadronization curve, enabling revision of the QCD phase diagram in the intermediate mu_B region (250-450 MeV) and motivating reanalysis of upcoming STAR beam energy scan data at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV.
