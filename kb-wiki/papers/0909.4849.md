# Dispersion sum rules for pion polarizabilities

> An analysis of different dispersion sum rules (DSRs) for the dipole and quadrupole pion polarizabilities is carried out. We prove the absence of additional spurious singularities in these approaches. The results of the calculations of the polarizabilities in the framework of DSRs are compared with ChPT predictions and with data obtained in different experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proves that dispersion sum rules (DSRs) for pion polarizabilities do not contain additional spurious singularities, contrary to a claim by Pasquini, Drechsel, and Scherer (PDS). The authors show that the zero-width approximation for meson resonance contributions yields results practically identical to the full calculation, confirming the absence of such singularities.

## Method

The authors constructed DSRs for the dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities of charged and neutral pions using dispersion relations at fixed u = mu^2 for the helicity amplitudes M_{++} and M_{+-}. The imaginary parts of the amplitudes were approximated by Breit-Wigner expressions for meson resonances (rho, omega, phi, a1, a2, b1, f0, sigma) with energy-dependent decay widths and coupling constants. The DSR results were compared with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions and experimental data from processes including gamma p -> gamma pi+ n, pi- A -> gamma pi- A, and gamma gamma -> pi pi.

## Key numbers

- For charged pions, the DSR prediction for the difference of dipole polarizabilities (alpha_1 - beta_1) is 13.60 +/- 2.15 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3), compared to the two-loop ChPT value of 5.7 +/- 1.0
- For neutral pions, the DSR prediction for (alpha_1 - beta_1) is -3.49 +/- 2.13 (10^{-4} fm^3), compared to ChPT value of -1.9 +/- 0.2
- The sum (alpha_1 + beta_1) from Baldin's DSR gives 0.166 +/- 0.024 for charged pions and 0.802 +/- 0.035 for neutral pions (10^{-4} fm^3)
- The quadrupole polarizability difference (alpha_2 - beta_2) for charged pions from DSR is 25.75 +/- 7.03 (10^{-4} fm^5), while the fit to data gives 25.0^{+0.8}_{-0.3}
- For neutral pions, the quadrupole difference from DSR is 39.72 +/- 8.01 (10^{-4} fm^5), compared to two-loop ChPT value of 37.6 +/- 3.3
- The MAMI (2005) experiment for gamma p -> gamma pi+ n gives (alpha_1 - beta_1) = 11.6 +/- 1.5_stat +/- 3.0_syst +/- 0.5_mod (10^{-4} fm^3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DSR calculations (Fil'kov and Kashevarov, 2006) and directly addresses the criticism by Pasquini, Drechsel, and Scherer (PDS) regarding spurious singularities in the dispersion approach. The paper compares DSR predictions with two-loop ChPT calculations by Gasser et al. (2002) and Burgi (1996), finding significant disagreement for the charged pion dipole polarizability difference. The results enable further experimental tests, particularly through improved analysis of the COMPASS and MAMI data, and motivate three-loop ChPT calculations to resolve the quadrupole polarizability discrepancies.
