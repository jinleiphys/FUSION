# Relativistic Calculations for Photonuclear Reactions (III): A Consistent Relativistic Analysis of the (e,e'p) and (gamma,p) Reactions

> Relativistic calculations for the quasifree electron scattering process (e, e'p) and the direct knockout contribution to (gamma, p) reactions are presented. The spectroscopic factors determined from the former reaction are used to fix the magnitude of the knockout contribution to the (gamma, p) reaction at 60 MeV. The results obtained for several nuclei indicate that the knockout contributions are much larger in magnitude and hence closer to the data than predicted in an earlier comparison based on non-relativistic calculations. We discuss the sensitivity of the results to the choice of parameters for the binding and final state interactions. We find these uncertainties to be more pronounced at the larger missing momenta explored by the (gamma, p) reaction. The implications of the present results for the size of contributions due to meson exchange currents are discussed.

## Key claim

Relativistic calculations for the direct knockout (DKO) contribution to (gamma,p) reactions at 60 MeV, with spectroscopic factors fixed from (e,e'p) data, yield cross sections that lie much closer to the experimental data than the non-relativistic calculations of Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994), who found DKO calculations fell below data by an average factor of 5.8; the authors find that the factors required to bring their relativistic calculations to the data are consistently less than 2, implying that meson exchange currents may not play as dominant a role as previously suggested.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted wave calculations for (e,e'p) reactions on 12C, 16O, 40Ca, and 51V using Dirac-Hartree bound state wave functions from Horowitz and Serot (1986) and complex phenomenological optical potentials from Cooper et al. (1993) for the outgoing proton. Spectroscopic factors were extracted by normalizing the (e,e'p) calculations to the right-hand peak of experimental momentum distributions, and these factors were then used without adjustment to predict the direct knockout contribution to (gamma,p) reactions at 60 MeV on the same nuclei. The sensitivity of the results was tested by varying the choice of bound state potentials (Hartree versus Woods-Saxon) and optical potentials (energy-dependent versus energy-plus-mass-dependent parameterizations).

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for 12C 1p3/2 knockout: 2.00 (energy-dependent optical potential) and 2.24 (energy-plus-mass-dependent optical potential), compared to non-relativistic value of 1.825 from Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994)
- Spectroscopic factor for 16O 1p1/2 knockout: 1.26 (energy-dependent) and 1.38 (energy-plus-mass-dependent), compared to non-relativistic value of 1.124 from Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994)
- Spectroscopic factor for 40Ca 1d3/2 knockout: 3.24 (energy-dependent) and 3.12 (energy-plus-mass-dependent), compared to non-relativistic value of 2.698 from Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994)
- Spectroscopic factor for 51V 1f7/2 knockout: 0.46 (energy-plus-mass-dependent), compared to non-relativistic value of 0.384 from Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994)
- The non-relativistic analysis of Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994) reported an average factor of 5.8 for the ratio between (gamma,p) data and DKO calculations for light nuclei
- The relativistic calculations require factors consistently less than 2 to bring DKO calculations to the data
- Variation in (gamma,p) cross sections due to changes in binding potential depth (less than 3% change) can yield up to 30% change in cross section
- Differences of up to 15% in spectroscopic factors obtained using different optical potentials
- Combined sensitivities in the (gamma,p) calculations lead to possible variation in cross section magnitude by up to a factor of two

## Context

This work extends the comparative analysis of (e,e'p) and (gamma,p) reactions performed by Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994) from a non-relativistic to a relativistic mean field framework, using the Dirac-Hartree potentials of Horowitz and Serot (1986) and the optical potentials of Cooper et al. (1993). The study is part of a series of relativistic calculations for photonuclear reactions by the authors, building on earlier work by Lotz and Sherif (1988) and Hedayati-Poor et al. (1995) that identified medium modifications to interaction Hamiltonians absent in non-relativistic treatments. The results enable a reassessment of the role of meson exchange currents in (gamma,p) reactions, suggesting that their contribution may be less dominant than previously inferred from non-relativistic analyses, and motivate future complete relativistic calculations including meson exchange current corrections.
