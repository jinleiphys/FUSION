# Neutrino emission from triplet pairing of neutrons in neutron stars

> Neutrino emission due to the pair breaking and formation processes in the bulk triplet superfluid in neutron stars is investigated with taking into account of anomalous weak interactions. We consider the problem in the BCS approximation discarding Fermi-liquid effects. In this approach we derive self-consistent equations for anomalous vector and axial-vector vertices of weak interactions taking into account the $^{3}P_{2}- ^{3}F_{2}$ mixing. Further we simplify the problem and consider the pure $^{3}P_{2}$ pairing with $m_{j}=0$, as is adopted in the minimal cooling paradigm. As was expected because of current conservation we have obtained a large suppression of the neutrino emissivity in the vector channel. More exactly, the neutrino emission through the vector channel vanishes in the nonrelativistic limit $V_F=0$. The axial channel is also found to be moderately suppressed. The total neutrino emissivity is suppressed by a factor of $1.9\times10^{-1}$ relative to original estimates using bare weak vertices.

## Key claim

The total neutrino emissivity from pair breaking and formation (PBF) processes in triplet-paired neutron superfluid is suppressed by a factor of \(1.9\times10^{-1}\) relative to original estimates that used bare weak vertices, because current conservation causes the vector channel contribution to vanish in the nonrelativistic limit and the axial channel is moderately suppressed.

## Method

The author derived self-consistent Dyson equations for anomalous vector and axial-vector weak-interaction vertices in a triplet superfluid, incorporating \(^{3}P_{2}-^{3}F_{2}\) mixing, within the BCS approximation and discarding Fermi-liquid effects. The calculation was then simplified to the pure \(^{3}P_{2}\) pairing state with \(m_{j}=0\), as adopted in the minimal cooling paradigm, and the neutrino emissivity was evaluated from the imaginary part of the weak polarization tensor using the standard model of weak interactions.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior findings on suppression of PBF neutrino emission in singlet (\(^{1}S_{0}\)) superfluids (Leinson 2006, Leinson 2009) to the triplet (\(^{3}P_{2}\)) case relevant for the bulk neutron superfluid in neutron stars. The original triplet PBF emissivity was derived by Yakovlev, Kaminker, and Levenfish (1999) using bare weak vertices, which violated vector current conservation as previously shown for the singlet case. The corrected emissivity enables more accurate modeling of neutron star cooling in the minimal cooling paradigm (Page 2009), particularly affecting early-time (\(t<1000\) years) thermal evolution and crust relaxation times.
