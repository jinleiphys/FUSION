# Signature splitting in $^{173}$W with triaxial particle rotor model

> A particle rotor model with a quasi-neutron coupled with a triaxially deformed rotor is applied to study signature splitting for bands with intruder orbital $\nu7/2^{+} [633]$ and non-intruder orbital $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$ in $^{173}$W. Excellent agreement with the observed energy spectra has been achieved for both bands. Signature splitting for band $\nu7/2^{+} [633]$, and band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$ before the onset of signature inversion, is satisfactorily reproduced by introducing the $γ$ degree of freedom. The phase and amplitude of signature splitting in band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$ is attributed to strong competition between $2f_{7/2}$ and $1h_{9/2}$ components. However, the explanation of signature inversion in band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$ self-consistently is beyond the present one quasi-neutron coupled with a triaxially deformed rotor.

# Signature splitting in $^{173}$W with triaxial particle rotor model

## Key claim

The triaxial particle rotor model, with a quasi-neutron coupled to a triaxially deformed rotor, reproduces the energy spectra and signature splitting for both the intruder band $\nu7/2^{+}[633]$ and the non-intruder band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$ in $^{173}$W, but the signature inversion observed at spin $35/2\hbar$ in the $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$ band cannot be explained within this one-quasiparticle framework.

## Method

A particle rotor model (PRM) Hamiltonian was constructed with a triaxial rotor core coupled to a valence neutron via a Nilsson-type single-particle Hamiltonian, including BCS pairing correlations. The model was diagonalized in a strong-coupling basis, with the triaxiality parameter $\gamma$, Coriolis attenuation factor $\xi$, and a variable moment of inertia ${\cal J}_0(I)={\cal J}_0 \sqrt{1+bI(I+1)}$ adjusted to reproduce the experimental energy spectra and signature splitting $S(I)=[E(I)-E(I-1)]-\frac{1}{2}[E(I+1)-E(I)+E(I-1)-E(I-2)]$ for both bands.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter $\varepsilon_2 = 0.24$ used for both bands, taken from Moller (1995).
- Triaxiality parameter $\gamma = 15^\circ$ for band $\nu7/2^{+}[633]$ and $\gamma = 12^\circ$ for band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$.
- Pairing gap parameter $\Delta_n = 0.81$ MeV.
- Calculated energy difference between $7/2^{+}[633]$ and $5/2^{-}[512]$ states is 67.6 keV at $\varepsilon_2=0.24$, compared with observed bandhead energy difference of 85.5 keV.
- Moment of inertia parameter ${\cal J}_0 = 35$ MeV$^{-1}\hbar^2$ for band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$.
- Coriolis attenuation factor $\xi = 0.7$ used for both bands.
- Variable moment of inertia parameter $b = 0.002$ for band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$.
- Signature inversion observed at spin $I = 35/2\hbar$ in band $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$.
- At the bandhead of $\nu7/2^{+}[633]$, the dominant orbital $|4\rangle$ contributes $\sum_K |C_4^{IK}|^2 \sim 75\%$, decreasing to $\sim 21\%$ at $I=41/2$.
- At the bandhead of $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$, the dominant orbital $|3\rangle$ contributes $\sum_K |C_3^{IK}|^2 \sim 96\%$, decreasing to $\sim 40\%$ at $I=41/2$.
- The orbital $5/2^{-}[512]$ has $\sim 64\%$ $2f_{7/2}$ component and $\sim 25\%$ $1h_{9/2}$ component.

## Context

This work extends earlier axial rotor model calculations by Walker (1978) for the $\nu7/2^{+}[633]$ band in $^{173}$W, which required selective Coriolis attenuation to reproduce signature splitting without triaxiality. The present study demonstrates that triaxiality provides a more natural explanation for the signature splitting amplitude in both bands. The results are relevant to understanding signature inversion in neighboring odd-odd nuclei such as $^{174}$Re, where Zhang (2007) observed signature inversion in the $\pi h_{9/2}\otimes\nu 5/2^{-}[512]$ configuration. The inability to reproduce the signature inversion at $35/2\hbar$ in the $\nu5/2^{-}[512]$ band indicates that a self-consistent description likely requires multi-quasiparticle configurations or band crossing effects beyond the present one-quasiparticle PRM.
