# Coulomb-nuclear interference in pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung

> Pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung offers a possibility of measuring the structure functions of pion-Compton scattering from a study of the small-momentum-transfer region where the bremsstrahlung reaction is dominated by the single-photon-exchange mechanism. The corresponding cross-section distribution is characterized by a sharp peak at small momentum transfers. But there is also a hadronic contribution which is smooth and constitutes an undesired background. In this communication the modification of the single-photon exchange amplitude by multiple-Coulomb scattering is investigated as well as the Coulomb-nuclear interference term.

## Key claim

The Coulomb-nuclear interference in pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung is investigated, showing that the Coulomb form factor from multiple-Coulomb scattering can reduce the cross section at the Coulomb peak by as much as 50% for heavy nuclei, and that the hadronic contribution dominates the Coulomb contribution for transverse momentum transfers above approximately 0.002 (GeV/c)^2.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the Coulomb amplitude including multiple-Coulomb scattering effects via the Glauber model, incorporating a Coulomb phase function and a form factor h_C(z) that depends on the ratio of transverse to total momentum transfer. They decomposed the total bremsstrahlung amplitude into Coulomb, nuclear, and polarizability contributions, calculating the nuclear amplitude by replacing the Coulomb potential with the sum of Coulomb and nuclear potentials in the Glauber formalism, and evaluated the resulting form factors numerically for carbon, iron, and lead nuclei using uniform charge and matter distributions with radius R_u = 1.1 A^(1/3) fm.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb form factor h_C(z) for lead at z=0.5 (the peak region where q_perp = q_parallel) has value 0.717 - i0.272, and at z=0.0 (very forward direction) has value 0.588 - i0.352
- For carbon at z=0.5, h_C = 0.998 - i0.030, and at z=0.0, h_C = 0.997 - i0.044
- For iron at z=0.5, h_C = 0.963 - i0.125, and at z=0.0, h_C = 0.943 - i0.179
- The hadronic contribution dominates the Coulomb contribution for transverse momentum transfers q_perp^2 greater than or equal to 0.002 (GeV/c)^2
- The ratio R(q_1) between polarizability and Born contributions remains approximately 1 for q_perp^2 less than or equal to 0.001 (GeV/c)^2
- The longitudinal momentum transfer q_min is given by q_min = (m_pi^2)/(2E_1) * x/(1-x), where x = omega_2/E_1

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies by Faldt and Tengblad (2007, 2008) on pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung and by Gal'perin et al. on related topics, providing the first analytic expression for the Coulomb form factor that accounts for the fixed longitudinal momentum transfer in bremsstrahlung. The results are directly applicable to the COMPASS experiment at CERN, which aims to measure the electromagnetic structure functions of pion-Compton scattering, and may also be important for interpreting data from Antipov et al. The analysis enables experimenters to extract polarizability contributions by restricting measurements to momentum transfers below 0.001 (GeV/c)^2, where the ratio between polarizability and Born contributions remains essentially the same as in free pion Compton scattering.
