# Effects of electromagnetic fluctuations in plasmas on solar neutrino fluxes

> We explore the effects of electromagnetic (EM) fluctuations in plasmas on solar neutrino fluxes exploiting the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. We find that the EM spectrum in the solar core is enhanced by the EM fluctuations due to the high density of the Sun, which increases the radiation energy density and pressure. By the EM fluctuations involving the modified radiation formula, the central temperature decreases when the central pressure of the Sun is fixed. With a help of the empirical relation between central temperature and neutrino fluxes deduced from the numerical solar models, we present the change in each of the solar neutrino fluxes by the EM fluctuations. We also discuss the enhanced radiation pressure and energy density by the EM fluctuations for other astronomical objects.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic (EM) fluctuation effect in the solar core enhances the radiation energy density and pressure by a factor of 1.22 relative to the blackbody case, which leads to a central temperature decrease of only \(3.7 \times 10^{-3}\%\) when the central pressure is held fixed; this change is too small to be distinguished from current uncertainties in standard solar models.

## Method

The authors computed the electromagnetic spectrum in the solar core using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem with a transverse dielectric permittivity derived from the Vlasov equation with a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision term. They integrated the spectrum over wavevector space, adopting a cutoff parameter \(k_{\rm max}\) equal to the inverse distance of closest approach between a test particle and an electron, to avoid divergence at low frequency. Using the resulting modified radiation constant \(a_{\rm EM}(T,\rho,X_i)\), they derived the central temperature change under the assumption of fixed total pressure, and then applied an empirical scaling law \(\phi_i \propto T_c^{m_i}\) from Bahcall (1996) to estimate the fractional change in each solar neutrino flux.

## Key numbers

- Radiation energy density and pressure in the solar core are 1.22 times higher than the blackbody case (for central density \(\rho_c = 1.48 \times 10^2\,{\rm g/cm^3}\) and central temperature \(T_c = 1.56 \times 10^7\,{\rm K}\)).
- Central temperature decreases by \(3.7 \times 10^{-5}\) (i.e., \(3.7 \times 10^{-3}\%\)) relative to the standard solar model.
- Fractional change in neutrino fluxes: \(\phi_{pp}\) changes by \(-4.03 \times 10^{-3}\%\), \(\phi_{pep}\) by \(-8.80 \times 10^{-3}\%\), \(\phi_{^7{\rm Be}}\) by \(+0.037\%\), \(\phi_{^8{\rm B}}\) by \(+0.088\%\), \(\phi_{^{13}{\rm N}}\) by \(+0.089\%\), \(\phi_{^{15}{\rm O}}\) by \(+0.099\%\), and \(\phi_{^{17}{\rm F}}\) by \(+0.102\%\).
- Solar luminosity decreases by about 0.1% due to the EM fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of EM fluctuations in plasmas by Tajima (1992), Cable (1992), and Opher and Opher (1997), which focused on the early universe and cold/warm plasmas, by applying the formalism to the high-density, moderate-temperature conditions of the solar core. The results are compared with standard solar models (SSMs) of Bahcall (1988, 1996) and with current uncertainty estimates from Orebi Gann (2021) and Vinyoles (2017). The paper provides an analytical fitting formula for the modified radiation constant over a wide parameter space, enabling future inclusion of EM fluctuation effects in stellar evolution codes and suggesting that these effects could serve as an energy loss mechanism in non-standard solar models involving exotic particles such as axions.
