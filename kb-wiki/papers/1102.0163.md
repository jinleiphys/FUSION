# Selection Rule for Electromagnetic Transitions in Nuclear Chiral Geometry

> In order to find the selection rules that can be applied to the electromagnetic transitions when the chiral geometry is achieved, a model for a special configuration in triaxial odd-odd nuclei is constructed which exhibits degenerate chiral bands with a sizable rotation. A quantum number obtained from the invariance of the Hamiltonian is given and the selection rule for electromagnetic transition probabilities in chiral bands is derived in terms of this quantum number. Among the available candidates for chiral bands of odd-odd nuclei, in which the near degeneracy of two $ΔI = 1$ bands is observed, the measured electromagnetic properties of the two bands in $^{128}_{55}$Cs$_{73}$ and $^{126}_{55}$Cs$_{71}$ are consistent with the rules, while those of $^{134}_{59}$Pr$_{75}$ and $^{132}_{57}$La$_{75}$ are not.

# Selection Rule for Electromagnetic Transitions in Nuclear Chiral Geometry

## Key claim

The paper derives a selection rule based on a quantum number A (eigenvalue of an operator combining a 90-degree rotation about the 3-axis with exchange of the valence proton and neutron) that governs electromagnetic transitions in chiral bands of triaxial odd-odd nuclei, but the rule applies strictly only under the special model assumptions of a single-j-shell configuration with γ = 90° and a hydrodynamical moment of inertia; the measured electromagnetic properties in ¹²⁸Cs and ¹²⁶Cs are consistent with the rule, while those in ¹³⁴Pr and ¹³²La are not.

## Method

A particle-rotor model was constructed for a triaxially deformed core with γ = 90° coupled to one proton particle and one neutron hole in the same single-j-shell (j = h₁₁/₂). The total Hamiltonian was diagonalized numerically using parameters simulating the A ≈ 130 region (A=130, Z=55, β=0.3, ℑ₀ = 8.55 ℏ²/MeV), and the quantum number A was identified from the invariance of the Hamiltonian under the combined operation of a π/2 rotation about the 3-axis and exchange of the valence proton and neutron. The selection rules for E2 and M1 transitions were derived by analyzing the allowed changes in the quantum numbers R₃ and C (exchange symmetry) that determine A.

## Key numbers

- For the numerical diagonalization: β = 0.3 and ℑ₀ = 8.55 ℏ²/MeV were used to simulate a chiral nucleus in the A ≈ 130 region.
- Approximate degeneracy of the two lowest bands was found for 13 < I < 24 in the calculated level scheme.
- For the M1 operator with g_R = 0.5: (g_ℓ − g_R) = 0.5 for protons and −0.5 for neutrons; (g_s^eff − g_R) = 2.848 for protons and −2.792 for neutrons.
- In ¹³⁴Pr, the observed B(E2; I → I−2) values of two intra-band transitions differed by at least a factor of two, violating the equality expected for chiral pair bands.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear chirality first pointed out by Frauendorf and Meng (1997) and builds on the particle-rotor model treatment published in KSH04. The selection rule provides a more definitive test for chiral geometry beyond the primary indication of near degeneracy of two ΔI = 1 bands, which had been observed in several A ≈ 130 odd-odd nuclei. The paper identifies ¹²⁸Cs and ¹²⁶Cs as the only current candidates whose measured electromagnetic transitions agree with the rule, while ruling out ¹³⁴Pr and ¹³²La as chiral pair bands, and calls for extending the selection rule to odd-A nuclei such as Nd isotopes where the proton particle is replaced by rotationally-aligned angular momentum from the S-band.
