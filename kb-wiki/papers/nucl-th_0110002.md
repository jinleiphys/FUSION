# Doorway states for one-nucleon transfer reactions as a test for current approaches to nuclei

## Key claim
The naturalness concept of effective field theory (EFT) is not confirmed by the doorway-state analysis of one-nucleon transfer reactions, because the contributions from two-, three-, and four-particle forces to the isoscalar static field are of comparable magnitude (ratio 1:1.2:1.3) rather than the EFT-expected hierarchy of 1:10^{-3/2}:10^{-3}.

## Method
The authors compute doorway states as eigenstates of a nucleon in the static field of a nucleus, where the static field is the convolution of free-space nucleon-nucleon forces with nucleon density distributions deduced from electron-nucleus and proton-nucleus elastic scattering data. They introduce an auxiliary scalar-isoscalar field with a nonlinear Lagrangian density containing cubic and quartic self-interactions to account for many-particle forces of all ranks, and determine the model parameters from the doorway-state energies. The resulting parameters are then compared with the naive dimensional analysis (NDA) naturalness prescription for the scalar field potential energy.

## Key numbers
- Contributions to the isoscalar part of the static field: U2 ≈ -80 MeV, U3 ≈ +96 MeV, U4 ≈ -104 MeV, giving a ratio |U2|:|U3|:|U4| ≈ 1:1.2:1.3.
- EFT naturalness expectation for the same quantities: V2 ≈ 30 MeV, V3 ≈ 1 MeV, V4 ≈ 0.03 MeV, giving a ratio 1:10^{-3/2}:10^{-3}.
- Extracted parameters: ρ1 = 0.146 fm^{-3}, Wm = 11.393 MeV, x = 16.004, Λ = 986.64 MeV, β = 5.583 fm^5.
- Naturalness coefficients from the scalar field potential: κ3 = -1.6, κ4 = -20.5 (κ2 = 1 by definition), contradicting the naturalness expectation that all κn are of order unity.

## Context
This work extends the doorway-state formalism developed in Birbrair and Ryazanov (Ref. 1) to test the Walecka model of nuclear relativity and the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) approach of Serot and Walecka (Refs. 4-6). The authors find that the dominant isovector contribution arises from many-particle forces rather than the ρ-meson exchange assumed in QHD, because ρ and δ meson contributions from two-particle forces nearly cancel. The results contradict the naturalness and naive dimensional analysis principles of effective field theory as formulated by Georgi (Ref. 10) and Manohar (Ref. 11), and instead suggest that the smallness of many-particle forces in few-nucleon systems (as seen in Ref. 12) arises from cancellation between forces of different ranks rather than from a hierarchy of decreasing strength. The approach enables model-independent determination of the scalar field parameters, in contrast to the model-dependent relativistic mean-field approach of Reinhard (Ref. 14) and Ring (Ref. 15).
