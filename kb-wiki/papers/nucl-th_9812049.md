# Effect of In-Medium Meson Masses on Nuclear Matter Properties

> Masses of hadrons (baryons as well as mesons) are modified in the nuclear medium because of their interactions. In this paper we investigate the effect of in-medium meson masses on the properties of the nuclear matter. The calculations are performed in the Walecka model. We find that with the inclusion of meson mass modification, the computed equation of state becomes softer. We also find that the decrease in the nucleon as well as meson masses in the medium is smaller than that obtained in the case when the in-medium meson masses are not taken into account.

## Key claim

The authors show that when the in-medium modification of meson masses is included self-consistently in the Walecka model calculation of nuclear matter properties, the resulting equation of state becomes softer and the decrease in nucleon and meson masses is smaller than in the standard (non-self-consistent) Walecka model.

## Method

The authors perform a self-consistent calculation in the Walecka model with σ and ω mesons, using the Relativistic Hartree Approximation (RHA) for the nucleon self-energy and the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) for the meson self-energies. The coupling constants are fitted to reproduce the nuclear matter saturation density of 0.15 fm⁻³ and binding energy of -15.75 MeV per baryon. The equations for the effective nucleon mass, effective σ and ω meson masses, and energy density are solved self-consistently as a function of nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ_NM = 0.15 fm⁻³, with binding energy -15.75 MeV per baryon.
- Self-consistent coupling constants: g_σ = 6.716, g_ω = 7.64; standard Walecka model values: g_σ = 8.69, g_ω = 9.9.
- At ρ_NM, self-consistent σ mass = 471 MeV, ω mass = 686 MeV; non-self-consistent σ mass = 445 MeV, ω mass = 632 MeV.
- At ρ_NM, self-consistent effective nucleon mass = 0.79 m_N; non-self-consistent = 0.73 m_N.
- Self-consistent nuclear matter incompressibility = 460 MeV; standard Walecka model value = 610 MeV.
- In the self-consistent calculation, the change in σ and ω masses is less than 15%, whereas in the standard model the maximum change is more than 20%.

## Context

This work extends the standard Walecka model (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986) and its extensions with nonlinear meson interactions and derivative coupling, which have been used extensively for nuclear structure and neutron star calculations. Prior calculations of in-medium meson masses in the Walecka model (e.g., by the same group) had not included the back-reaction of those mass changes on the nuclear matter properties. The present self-consistent approach yields a softer equation of state and lower incompressibility, which the authors argue may lead to better estimates for neutron star masses. The authors note that similar calculations for the ρ meson, relevant for quark-gluon plasma diagnostics, are in progress.
