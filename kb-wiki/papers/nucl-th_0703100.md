# Finite amplitude method for the RPA solution

> We propose a practical method to solve the random-phase approximation (RPA) in the self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) and density-functional theory. The method is based on numerical evaluation of the residual interactions utilizing finite amplitude of single-particle wave functions. The method only requires calculations of the single-particle Hamiltonian constructed with independent bra and ket states. Using the present method, the RPA calculation becomes possible with a little extension of a numerical code of the static HF calculation. We demonstrate usefulness and accuracy of the present method performing test calculations for isoscalar responses in deformed 20Ne.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "finite amplitude method" (FAM) that solves the random-phase approximation (RPA) equations by numerically evaluating residual interactions through finite differences of the Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian, rather than explicitly computing particle-hole matrix elements; the method is demonstrated to produce results identical to conventional RPA to three or four digits of accuracy for isoscalar responses in deformed 20Ne.

## Method

The method linearizes the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation in the frequency domain, then computes the induced field δh(ω) via finite difference: δh(ω) = (1/η)[h[⟨ψ'|,|ψ⟩] - h[⟨φ|,|φ⟩]], where the bra and ket single-particle states are independently varied with forward amplitudes X_i(ω) and backward amplitudes Y_i(ω). The resulting linear equations are solved iteratively using the bi-conjugate gradient method (Bi-CGM) for each frequency ω, requiring only operations of the HF Hamiltonian on single-particle orbitals. Test calculations were performed for 20Ne in a three-dimensional coordinate-space representation with a sphere of radius 10 fm and mesh spacing 0.8 fm, using the Bonche-Koonin-Negele (BKN) interaction.

## Key numbers

- Mesh spacing: Δx = Δy = Δz = 0.8 fm, with 8217 grid points in a sphere of radius 10 fm.
- Frequency range: ω from 0 to 40 MeV with spacing Δω = 200 keV (201 points).
- Smoothing parameter: Γ = 500 keV (ω → ω + iΓ/2).
- Finite difference parameter η = 10⁻⁵ / max{N(X), N(Y)}.
- Convergence condition for Bi-CGM: ratio of remaining difference to right-hand side less than 10⁻⁵.
- Convergence condition for Coulomb and Yukawa potential solvers: ratio of remaining difference to right-hand side less than 10⁻²³.
- Iterations required: 10 to 100 for ω < 10 MeV; more than 500 for ω > 30 MeV.
- Lowest intrinsic excitation: K=2 mode at ω = 8 MeV; next lowest K=0 mode at Ex = 9.6 MeV.
- Lowest 1p1h excitation energy: Ex = 4.5 MeV.
- Quadrupole deformation of 20Ne ground state: β ≈ 0.4.
- Accuracy of FAM compared to conventional RPA: three to four digits.

## Context

This work extends the real-time TDHF method of Nakatsukasa and Yabana (2005) and Inakura et al. (2004), which suffered from numerical instability due to zero-energy Nambu-Goldstone modes in time evolution, by providing a frequency-domain alternative. The method enables RPA calculations for deformed nuclei with full self-consistency, which had previously been limited to spherical nuclei in works such as Terasaki (2005), Giai (2003), and others. The approach requires only minor modification of existing static HF codes and is applicable to any energy-density functional, enabling systematic studies of giant resonances and low-lying states in deformed nuclei.
