# Impact parameter dependence of color charge correlations in the proton

> The impact parameter dependence of color charge correlators in the proton is obtained from the light front formalism in light cone gauge. We include NLO corrections due to the $|qqqg\rangle$ Fock state via light-cone perturbation theory. Near the center of the proton, the $b$-dependence of the correlations is very different from a "transverse profile function". The resulting $t$-dependence of exclusive $J/Ψ$ photoproduction transitions from exponential to power law at $|t| \approx 1$ GeV$^2$. This prediction could be tested at upcoming DIS facilities or in nucleus-proton ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs).

## Key claim

The impact parameter dependence of color charge correlations in the proton is very different from a "transverse profile function" near the center of the proton, with the two-point correlation function becoming negative at small impact parameter; this non-trivial dependence leads to a transition from exponential to power-law fall-off in exclusive J/Psi photoproduction at |t| approximately 1 GeV^2, which could be tested at upcoming DIS facilities or in ultraperipheral collisions.

## Method

The authors compute the two-point color charge correlator in the proton using the light front formalism in light cone gauge, including next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections from the |qqqg⟩ Fock state via light-cone perturbation theory (LCPT). They perform a Fourier transform of the correlator with respect to momentum transfer to obtain its impact parameter dependence, then use this correlator to compute the dipole scattering amplitude and the cross section for exclusive J/Psi photoproduction in the two-gluon exchange approximation. The results are compared to the IPsat model, which assumes a factorized Gaussian spatial profile function.

## Key numbers

- The transition from exponential to power-law dependence in dσ/dt for exclusive J/Psi photoproduction occurs at |t| ≈ 1 GeV^2 at x_P = 0.01 (corresponding to W ≈ 30 GeV).
- The NLO corrections to the color charge correlator are small at x ≈ 0.1 but become the dominant contribution at x ≈ 0.01.
- The collinear cutoff parameter m is varied over the range 0.1 to 0.4 GeV, producing the bands shown in the results.
- The IPsat model uses a Gaussian spatial profile T_p(b) = (1/(2πB)) e^{-b^2/(2B)} with an exponential fall-off in |t|.
- The cross section exhibits exponential fall-off up to |t| ≈ 0.8 GeV^2 before transitioning to power-law behavior.

## Context

This work extends the light front formalism for color charge correlators in the proton developed by Dumitru (2018) and the NLO corrections computed by Dumitru (2020). It contrasts with the IPsat model of Kowalski (2003) and Mantysaari (2018), which assumes a factorized Gaussian spatial profile that cannot reproduce the sign change or sum rule of the correlator. The results enable predictions for exclusive vector meson production at upcoming DIS facilities and in nucleus-proton ultraperipheral collisions, and relate to the dipole form factor approach proposed by Frankfurt (2002) for non-exponential slopes at small momentum transfer.
