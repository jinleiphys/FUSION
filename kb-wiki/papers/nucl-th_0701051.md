# Hadron Correlation in Jets

> We review some recent experimental and theoretical work on the correlation among hadrons produced at intermediate $p_T$ at RHIC. The topics include: forward and backward asymmetry with and without trigger at mid-rapidity, associated-particle distribution on the near side, the $Ω$ puzzle and its solution, associated particles on the away side, and two-jet recombination at LHC.

## Key claim

The recombination model (RM) can explain a wide range of hadron correlation phenomena at intermediate transverse momentum (p_T < 8 GeV/c) at RHIC, including the forward-backward asymmetry in d+Au collisions, the associated-particle distributions on the near and away sides, and the "Ω puzzle," without requiring parton fragmentation or saturation physics; however, the model's predictions for the Ω trigger's associated-particle distribution initially appeared to be contradicted by data, requiring the introduction of the "phantom jet" concept (ridge dominance) to resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data from RHIC (primarily STAR and PHENIX) on hadron correlations in jets at intermediate p_T (roughly 2–8 GeV/c) and interprets them using the recombination model (RM), where hadrons form by combining thermal partons from the medium and shower partons from hard-scattered partons. For the forward-backward asymmetry in d+Au collisions, the RM uses rapidity-dependent thermal-parton densities to compute B/F ratios. For the away-side structure, a Markovian parton scattering (MPS) Monte Carlo simulation treats the recoil parton's trajectory as a series of random scattering steps with energy loss, producing a dip-bump structure in Δφ. For LHC predictions, the RM calculates shower-shower (SS) recombination from two neighboring jets.

## Key numbers

- In d+Au collisions, the B/F ratio for uncorrelated yields is >1 for all p_T up to 6 GeV/c, with the RM calculation agreeing with data up to p_T ≈ 2 GeV/c and subsequently revised data matching the prediction for p_T > 2 GeV/c.
- For correlated yields in d+Au collisions with trigger at mid-rapidity, the B/F ratio is approximately 2 for the rapidity range 2.7 < |η| < 3.9, compared to roughly 1.3 for |η| < 0.75, giving a thermal-shower (TS) ratio R_TS = 1.7; the thermal-thermal (TT) ratio from soft hadron yields is R_TT = 2.9, nearly the square of R_TS.
- For central-to-peripheral ratios (R_CP) of associated particles in Au+Au collisions, the enhancement factor relative to pp is about 3 at p_T ≈ 1 GeV/c.
- For h-h correlation with 3 < p_T^{trig} < 6 GeV/c and p_T^{assoc} as low as 1 GeV/c, the jet-to-ridge ratio J/R is about 10–15%; for Λ-h correlation, J/R is about 0.04.
- For two-jet recombination at LHC in the range 10 < p_T < 20 GeV/c, the p/π ratio is estimated to vary from 20 down to 5.

## Context

This review extends the recombination model approach previously applied to single-particle spectra (e.g., the p/π ratio exceeding 1 at p_T ~ 3 GeV/c in central Au+Au collisions) to two-particle correlations, contradicting the expectation that parton fragmentation (even medium-modified) could explain the data. It builds on earlier work by Hwa and collaborators (2005) on correlation in jets and by the STAR collaboration on forward-backward asymmetry and associated-particle distributions. The resolution of the "Ω puzzle" (exponential spectrum yet with associated particles) via the phantom jet/ridge concept enables a unified description of baryon- and meson-triggered yields and predicts distinctive signatures for LHC, such as the p/π ratio from two-jet recombination, which would challenge jet tomography at higher p_T.
