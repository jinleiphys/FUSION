# Tunneling times and bremsstrahlung in alpha decay

> A semi-classical model based on quantum time concepts is presented for the evaluation of bremsstrahlung emission probabilities in alpha decay of nuclei. The contribution to the bremsstrahlung emission from the different regions in tunneling is investigated using realistic double folded nuclear and Coulomb potentials. Within this model, the contribution from the radiation emitted in front of the barrier before tunneling is much larger than that while leaving the barrier. A comparison with the data on $^{210}$Po shows that the results are sensitive to the nuclear potential and the rectangular well used in many of the quantum mechanical approaches can even give qualitatively different results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a semi-classical model based on quantum tunneling times to evaluate bremsstrahlung emission probabilities in alpha decay, finding that the contribution from radiation emitted in front of the barrier before tunneling is much larger than that while leaving the barrier; however, the results are sensitive to the nuclear potential used, and the rectangular well approximation common in quantum mechanical approaches can give qualitatively different results.

## Method

The authors construct a semi-classical model using the stationary concepts of dwell time and traversal time for a particle tunneling through a realistic double-folded nuclear potential (using M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction) and Coulomb potential. They define average velocities in three spatial regions (before, within, and after the barrier) based on these tunneling times, then compute the photon emission probability using the classical formula for bremsstrahlung with an added alpha cluster preformation factor. The model is applied to the alpha decay of 210Po, and the results are compared with experimental data from Kasagi et al., Boie et al., and Eremin et al.

## Key numbers

- For 210Po decay, the effective charge for dipole transitions is Zeff = 0.4.
- The alpha cluster preformation factor for 210Po is determined to be Pα = 0.03 when comparing experimental and theoretical half-lives using the realistic double-folded potential.
- Using a rectangular well potential with V0 = 16.7 MeV and r0 = 8.76 fm (as in Papenbrock et al.), the experimental half-life is reproduced only after including Pα = 0.03; adjusting the well depth to satisfy the Bohr-Sommerfeld condition gives V0 = 75 MeV and Pα = 0.016.
- The global quantum number used for 210Po calculations is G = 22; using G = 24 for 226Ra and 214Po slightly overestimates the emission probabilities compared to data.

## Context

This work extends the application of quantum tunneling time concepts (dwell time and traversal time) from previous studies of particle resonances and nuclear half-lives to the problem of bremsstrahlung in alpha decay. It directly addresses discrepancies between earlier theoretical approaches: the semi-classical model of Dyakonov and Gornyi (which gave conflicting results in different implementations), the fully quantum mechanical treatment of Papenbrock et al. (which found Coulomb acceleration dominant), and the work of Takigawa et al. (which emphasized interference between spatial regions). The paper enables future experimental tests by highlighting the need for new, reliable data to resolve whether alpha particles emit radiation during tunneling, and it provides a framework that can be applied to other nuclei such as 214Po, 226Ra, and 244Cu.
