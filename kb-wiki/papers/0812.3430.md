# Finite-size and confinement effects in spin-polarized trapped Fermi gases

> We calculate the energy of a single fermion interacting resonantly with a Fermi sea of different-species fermions in asymmetric traps, and show that finite particle numbers and the trap geometry impact the phase structure and the critical polarization. Our findings contribute to understanding some experimental discrepancies in spin-polarized Fermi gases as finite-size and confinement effects.

# Finite-size and confinement effects in spin-polarized trapped Fermi gases

## Key claim

The paper shows that the energy of a single spin-down fermion interacting resonantly with a spin-up Fermi sea (the Fermi polaron) depends significantly on the total particle number and the trap aspect ratio, and that these finite-size and confinement effects can partially explain the observed differences in critical polarization between the MIT and Rice experiments on spin-polarized Fermi gases.

## Method

The authors calculate the polaron energy variationally using a wavefunction that includes one-particle-one-hole (1p1h) excitations, generalized from the uniform-system approach of Chevy (2006) to anisotropic harmonic traps. They solve a self-consistent equation for the polaron energy in traps with different aspect ratios and particle numbers, using a contact interaction regulated by separable cutoff functions in momentum space. The resulting polaron energies are then used within a free-energy functional to compute the critical polarization and critical density ratio for the superfluid-to-normal phase transition.

## Key numbers

- For the uniform system at unitarity, the variational polaron energy parameter is η = -0.607 (Chevy, 2006).
- For an isotropic trap (α=1) with total particle number Ntot=10^7 and using Monte-Carlo quasiparticle parameters, the critical polarization reaches the uniform-system value Pc = 0.74 and critical density ratio xc = 0.47.
- For fixed Ntot=10^4 and increasing aspect ratio from α=1 to α=35, Pc increases from 0.82 to 0.89 and xc decreases from 0.40 to 0.31 (with Monte-Carlo m* and B values).
- For fixed α=35, Pc increases with decreasing Ntot from 0.82 (Ntot=10^5) to 0.89 (Ntot=10^4) to 0.96 (Ntot=10^3), and xc decreases from 0.39 to 0.31 to 0.18.
- The MIT experiment observed critical polarization Pc = 0.70(3) in a trap with aspect ratio α ~ 5 and total particle numbers Ntot ~ 10^6-10^7.
- The Rice experiment observed Pc ≳ 0.9 in a highly elongated trap with α ~ 35-45 and lower Ntot ~ 10^5.

## Context

This work provides a microscopic explanation for the discrepancies between the MIT experiments (Zwierlein 2005, 2006; Shin 2006) and the Rice experiments (Hulet 2006, 2007) on the phase structure and critical polarization of spin-polarized Fermi gases. The MIT experiments found Pc = 0.70(3) in nearly spherical traps with large particle numbers, while the Rice experiments found Pc ≳ 0.9 in highly elongated traps with smaller particle numbers. The paper extends the variational polaron approach of Chevy (2006) and the LDA-based phase diagram analysis of Recati (2008) to include finite-size and trap-geometry effects, showing that these effects naturally produce the observed differences. The results enable future density-functional calculations that could incorporate surface tension (Mueller 2008) and gradient terms for a more complete description.
