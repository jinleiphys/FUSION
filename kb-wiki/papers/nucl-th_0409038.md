# Quark recombination and elliptic flow

> Elliptic flow systematics for different hadron species have been explained by quark coalescence models. It has been argued that the elliptic asymmetry v_2 should scale with the number of quarks that comprise the hadron. We show how these arguments are sensitive to the relative role of asymmetries in the phase space density vs. asymmetries in the effective volume of emission. We also discuss the degree to which coalescence arguments differ from thermal models. Illustrative calculations based on solving the Boltzmann equation are presented along with the results of blast-wave models. Although the issue is complicated, ambiguities might be clarified by measurements of source-size parameters for nucleons at higher transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the widely cited scaling relation \(v_2^{(n_q)}(p_t)=n_q v_2^{(q)}(p_t/n_q)\) for elliptic flow in quark coalescence models is only valid under the specific assumption that the elliptic asymmetry originates from variations in the average phase space density, not from variations in the effective volume of emission; when asymmetries in the effective volume dominate, the scaling relation breaks down and \(v_2\) becomes approximately independent of the number of constituent quarks.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for elliptic flow in a coalescence framework, separating contributions from asymmetries in phase space density and effective volume. They then perform illustrative calculations using two types of blast-wave models (a cylindrical-shell parameterization and the Retiere-Lisa parameterization) and a hydrodynamically inspired blast-wave model, as well as solving the Boltzmann equation for partonic evolution, to test the conditions under which quark-number scaling of \(v_2\) holds.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass set to \(m_q = 350\) MeV for all calculations; hadron masses are \(M_C = n_q m_q\) for \(n_q = 1, 2, 3\).
- For the cylindrical-shell blast-wave model with temperature \(T = 100\) MeV and collective velocity parameters, scaling is strongly violated when asymmetry is introduced via the shell thickness (effective volume), with \(v_2\) approximately independent of \(n_q\).
- For the Retiere-Lisa blast-wave model with \(T = 100\) MeV, \(\rho_0 = 0.9\), \(\rho_a = 0.1\), and \(R_y/R_x = 1.2\), quark-number scaling is satisfied rather well; with \(R_y/R_x = 0.8\), scaling is strongly violated and \(v_2\) becomes negative in some instances.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of quark coalescence models for elliptic flow, which were previously used to explain the scaling of \(v_2\) with quark number by Voloshin (2003), the Mueller group, and the Ko group. The paper shows that the scaling relation is sensitive to whether the asymmetry arises from phase space density or emission volume, a distinction not addressed in earlier coalescence arguments. The results enable future experimental clarification through measurements of source-size parameters for nucleons at higher transverse momenta, as suggested by correlation measurements from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations.
