# Strangeness in the nucleon and the ratio of proton-to-neutron neutrino-induced quasi-elastic yield

> The electroweak form factors of the nucleon as obtained within a three flavor pseudoscalar vector meson soliton model are employed to predict the ratio of the proton and neutron yields from $^{12}C$, which are induced by quasi-elastic neutrino reactions. These predictions are found to vary only moderately in the parameter space allowed by the model. The antineutrino flux of the up-coming experiment determining this ratio was previously overestimated. The corresponding correction is shown to have only a small effect on the predicted ratio. However, it is found that the experimental result for the ratio crucially depends on an accurate measurement of the energy of the knocked out nucleon.

## Key claim

The ratio of proton-to-neutron quasi-elastic yields from carbon-12 induced by neutrinos and antineutrinos is predicted to lie between 1.1 and 1.2 using form factors from a three-flavor pseudoscalar vector meson soliton model, but this prediction is only valid if the energy thresholds for the knocked-out nucleon are set identically for protons and neutrons; a relative shift of 1 MeV between the thresholds introduces a 6% error in the ratio.

## Method

The authors employed a continuum random phase approximation (RPA) nuclear model to compute neutrino- and antineutrino-induced quasi-elastic cross sections on carbon-12, using nucleon form factors obtained from an SU(3) Skyrme model with vector mesons. The strange quark vector and axial-vector form factors were computed at zero and finite momentum transfer by Fourier-transforming radial profile functions from the soliton model and evaluating the spin and flavor matrix elements using exact SU(3) eigenfunctions of the collective Hamiltonian. The computed cross sections were folded with updated LAMPF neutrino and antineutrino energy spectra to predict the yield ratio.

## Key numbers

- The predicted ratio for the combined neutrino and antineutrino beam is \(1.1 \le R_y(\tilde \nu) \le 1.2\) from the Skyrme model with vector mesons.
- The strange axial charge at zero momentum transfer is predicted to be \(G_A^s(0) = -0.030\) (best fit to baryon masses), \(-0.015\) (reproducing experimental \(G_A(0)\)), or \(-0.028\) (best fit to \(G_A(0), G_M^p(0), G_M^n(0)\)).
- The strange magnetic moment at zero momentum transfer is predicted to be \(G_M^s(0) = -0.055\) (best fit to baryon masses), \(-0.035\) (reproducing experimental \(G_A(0)\)), or \(-0.050\) (best fit to \(G_A(0), G_M^p(0), G_M^n(0)\)).
- Using SU(3) symmetric wavefunctions instead of exact eigenfunctions increases the strange magnetic moment to \(G_M^s(0) = -0.559\).
- The antineutrino-induced cross sections decreased by a factor of approximately 4.3 when using the improved antineutrino spectrum compared to the old estimate.
- A relative shift of 1 MeV between the proton and neutron energy thresholds (e.g., \(E_p^0 = 60.0\) MeV, \(E_n^0 = 59.0\) MeV) changes the neutrino-induced ratio from 1.004 to 0.943, a change of approximately 6%.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Garino et al. (1992, 1993) that established the ratio \(R_y\) as a probe of strange quark matrix elements in the nucleon, by incorporating more accurate LAMPF beam spectra and form factors from a vector meson Skyrme model. The results are consistent with the finding of Barbaro et al. (1996) that \(R_y\) is insensitive to the nuclear model but sensitive to the strange axial charge \(G_A^s(0)\). The predicted small negative strange form factors contrast with the preliminary MIT-Bates measurement of \(G_M^s(0) = 0.46 \pm 0.36 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.18\) and with other calculations predicting large negative values, such as those by Leinweber et al. (1995) and Kim et al. (1996). The work enables the LSND experiment at LAMPF to discriminate between nucleon models through a precise measurement of \(R_y\), provided the energy thresholds for protons and neutrons are accurately determined.
