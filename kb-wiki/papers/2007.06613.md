# Why is LaMET an effective field theory for partonic structure?

> Partons are effective degrees of freedom describing the structure of hadrons involved in high-energy collisions. Familiar theories of partons are QCD light-front quantization and soft-collinear effective theory, both of which are intrinsically Minkowskian and appear unsuitable for classical Monte Carlo simulations. A ``new'' form of the parton theory has been formulated in term of the old-fashioned, Feynman's infinite momentum frame, in which the parton degrees of freedom are filtered through infinite-momentum external states. The partonic structure of hadrons is then related to the matrix elements of static (equal-time) correlators in the state $|P^z=\infty\rangle$. This representation lays the foundation of large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) which approximates parton physics through a systematic $M/P^z$ expansion of the lattice QCD matrix elements at a finite but large momentum $P^z$, and removes the residual logarithmic-$P^z$ dependence by the standard effective-field-theory matching and running.

# Why is LaMET an effective field theory for partonic structure?

## Key claim

LaMET is an effective field theory for partonic structure because it approximates the infinite-momentum limit of a hadron through a systematic Taylor expansion in $(M/P^z)^2$ of Euclidean lattice QCD matrix elements at finite but large momentum $P^z$, with the residual logarithmic-$P^z$ dependence removed by standard EFT matching and running; the author acknowledges that this reverses the logic of a standard EFT by using the full QCD Lagrangian as the effective one, and that the expansion does not converge uniformly for all $x$ because the effective parameter is $M^2/(k^z)^2$ where $k^z$ is the parton momentum.

## Method

The paper formulates parton distribution functions (PDFs) as the Fourier transform of spatial correlations in an infinite-momentum hadron state $|P^z=\infty\rangle$, expressed as equal-time correlators $\langle P^z=\infty |\overline{\psi}(z)\Gamma \psi(0)|P^z=\infty\rangle$ that are amenable to Euclidean lattice QCD simulation. This representation is related to the standard light-front correlator by an infinite Lorentz boost. The practical implementation approximates $P^z=\infty$ by a finite but large momentum $P^z$ on the lattice, computes the momentum distribution $f(k^z,P^z)$, and then matches to the physical PDF $f(x)$ through perturbative QCD matching that removes the difference between the two $P^z\to\infty$ limits caused by ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is $\epsilon = (M/P^z)^2$, where $M$ is a hadron mass scale on the order of 1 GeV; for $P^z\sim 2$ GeV, $\epsilon = 0.25$, and for $P^z\sim 3-5$ GeV (accessible with exascale computing), $\epsilon$ becomes as small as $0.03$.
- The smallest accessible $x$ in a LaMET calculation is $x_{\rm min}\sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}/P^z$, and the reliable range is $[x_{\rm min}, x_{\rm max}\sim 1-x_{\rm min}]$, which approaches $[0,1]$ as $P^z\to\infty$.
- The correlation length $\xi$ in the critical region is proportional to $P^z/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$.

## Context

This work extends the author's 2013 proposal for calculating parton physics on a Euclidean lattice (Ji, 2013) and the 2014 paper explaining the effective field theory principle (Ji, 2014). It contrasts with light-front quantization (Wilson, 1994; Brodsky, 1997) and soft-collinear effective theory (Bauer, 2000-2001), which are intrinsically Minkowskian and unsuitable for classical Monte Carlo simulations. The paper argues that the infinite-momentum limit is analogous to a critical point in condensed matter systems, where correlation functions decay algebraically as $f(\lambda)\sim \lambda^{\alpha-1}$, and that LaMET provides the first practical and systematic framework to calculate parton physics through Euclidean lattice QCD, enabling predictions for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (Accardi, 2012).
