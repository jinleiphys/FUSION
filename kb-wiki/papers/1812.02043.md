# The Roper resonance as a meson-baryon molecular state

> The recently proposed mechanism for the formation of the Roper resonance, in which a dynamically generated state as well as a genuine three-quark resonant state play an equally important role, is confronted with the model proposed almost twenty years ago in which the Roper is pictured as a molecular state of the nucleon and the $σ$ meson.

# The Roper resonance as a meson-baryon molecular state

## Key claim
The Roper resonance is primarily a dynamically generated molecular state of the nucleon and the σ meson, with the resonance mass determined by the molecular state energy, but an admixture of a genuine three-quark (1s)²(2s)¹ excited state around 2 GeV is crucial to reproduce the experimental width and modulus of the resonance pole.

## Method
The author uses a coupled-channel approach including πN, πΔ, and σN channels, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for meson amplitudes to all orders with a separable kernel approximation. The Cloudy Bag Model fixes the quark-pion vertices, while the s-wave σ-baryon vertex is introduced phenomenologically with the coupling strength gσ as a free parameter. The evolution of the lowest eigenvalue of the system matrix and the resonance S-matrix pole in the complex energy plane are studied using the Laurent-Pietarinen expansion.

## Key numbers
- The dynamically generated state mass appears typically 100 MeV below the nominal nucleon-sigma threshold.
- For gσ = 2.00, the pole position is Re Wp = 1.358 GeV and -2 Im Wp = 0.111 GeV.
- For gσ = 1.95, the pole position is Re Wp = 1.383 GeV and -2 Im Wp = 0.112 GeV.
- For gσ = 1.80, the pole position is Re Wp = 1.397 GeV and -2 Im Wp = 0.157 GeV.
- The PDG reference values for the Roper resonance are Re Wp = 1.370 GeV and -2 Im Wp = 0.175 GeV.
- Two choices for the σ meson mass and width are used: mσ = Γσ = 0.6 GeV and mσ = Γσ = 0.5 GeV.
- With gσ ≈ 1.5, the results for position, modulus, and phase come close to the PDG value.

## Context
This work extends the molecular state model proposed by the author and collaborators in 2001 (Golli, 2001), where the Roper was pictured as a nucleon-σ meson molecular state using a chiral version of the Chromodielectric Model. The present coupled-channel approach is motivated by lattice QCD simulations from the Graz-Ljubljana and Adelaide groups (Lang, 2016; Kiratidis, 2017) that found no evidence for a dominant three-quark configuration below 1.65 GeV. The results enable a deeper understanding of the Roper resonance formation mechanism by reconciling the molecular state picture with the need for a three-quark admixture to reproduce experimental decay properties.
