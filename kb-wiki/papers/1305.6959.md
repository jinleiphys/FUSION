# Charge form factor and sum rules of electromagnetic response functions in Carbon-12

> An "ab initio" calculation of the Carbon-12 elastic form factor, and sum rules of longitudinal and transverse response functions measured in inclusive (e,e') scattering, is reported, based on realistic nuclear potentials and electromagnetic currents. The longitudinal elastic form factor and sum rule are found to be in satisfactory agreement with available experimental data. A direct comparison between theory and experiment is difficult for the transverse sum rule. However, it is shown that the calculated one has large contributions from two-body currents, indicating that these mechanisms lead to a significant enhancement of the quasi-elastic transverse response. This fact may have implications for the anomaly observed in recent neutrino quasi-elastic charge-changing scattering data off Carbon-12.

## Key claim

The paper reports an ab initio calculation of the elastic form factor and sum rules for Carbon-12 that shows satisfactory agreement with experimental data for the longitudinal response, but the transverse sum rule comparison is difficult because the calculated result has large contributions from two-body currents that significantly enhance the quasi-elastic transverse response, which may have implications for the anomaly observed in neutrino quasi-elastic scattering data.

## Method

The authors performed Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of the Carbon-12 ground state using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potential, then computed the longitudinal elastic form factor and sum rules of the longitudinal and transverse response functions from inclusive electron scattering. The charge and current operators included one-body terms from non-relativistic expansion of the single-nucleon four-current and two-body terms derived from the static part of the NN potential, with additional contributions from Delta-resonance excitation and the rho-pi-gamma transition mechanism. The calculations were parallelized using the Asynchronous Dynamic Load Balancing (ADLB) library on Argonne's IBM Blue Gene/Q (Mira) supercomputer, averaging over 12 directions of the momentum transfer vector for each of 21 momentum transfer magnitudes.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy: -93.3(4) MeV, compared to experimental value of -92.16 MeV
- RMS charge radius: 2.46(2) fm, compared to experimental value of (2.471 +/- 0.005) fm
- At q=2.5 fm^-1, two-body contributions increase the one-body transverse sum rule by about 50%
- For q=1.53, 1.94, and 2.90 fm^-1, the maximum energy transfer in the longitudinal (transverse) case corresponds to 140, 210, and 345 (140, 180, 285) MeV respectively
- Two-body contributions to the longitudinal elastic form factor become appreciable only for q > 3 fm^-1
- The calculations used more than 250,000 MPI processes with 80% efficiency for energy calculations, and more than 32,000 MPI processes with above 95% efficiency for operator calculations

## Context

This work extends the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) from few-nucleon systems (A <= 4) to Carbon-12, building on prior GFMC calculations by Pieper (2008) and others that successfully reproduced energy spectra and static properties of light nuclei. The results contradict claims from analyses using simpler models (Fermi gas or local density approximations) that suggested in-medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, as advocated by the quark-meson coupling model of Lu (1999) and Guichon (2004). The finding that two-body currents significantly enhance the transverse response may help explain the anomaly observed in neutrino quasi-elastic charge-changing scattering data on Carbon-12 reported by Aguilar (2008), and enables future direct QMC calculations of the response functions themselves.
