# Transverse Momentum Spectra of $J/ψ$ and $ψ^{\prime}$ Mesons from Quark Gluon Plasma Hadronization at CERN SPS

> A popular derivation of the apparent temperature of the particle transverse momentum spectra and its inconsistency are considered. An improved formula for the apparent temperature is discussed. It is shown that recent results on transverse mass spectra of $J/ψ$ and $ψ^{\prime}$ mesons support a hypothesis of statistical production of charmonia at hadronization and suggest the early thermal freeze-out of these mesons. Using the apparent temperature formula the collective transverse velocity of the hadronizing quark gluon plasma is extracted to be $<v_T > \approx 0.2$. Predictions for transverse mass spectra of hidden and open charm mesons along with bottomonium at SPS and RHIC energies are discussed.

# Wiki Page: Transverse Momentum Spectra of J/ψ and ψ′ Mesons from Quark Gluon Plasma Hadronization at CERN SPS

## Key claim
The transverse mass spectra of J/ψ and ψ′ mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV support the hypothesis of statistical charmonium production at hadronization and suggest that these mesons undergo thermal freeze-out simultaneously with hadronization, provided the apparent temperature formula is corrected to account for the finite size of the system and the cylindrical geometry (with coefficient α = 2/π rather than the naive α = 1/2).

## Method
The author derives an improved formula for the apparent temperature T* of transverse mass spectra by starting from the cut-off distribution function for particle emission from a freeze-out hypersurface, then expanding the Bessel function for small pT vT γT / T and averaging over the freeze-out surface. For heavy particles with mT ≥ 1 GeV, the naive derivation (Heinz, 1999) is shown to produce negative apparent temperatures when applied to space-like hypersurfaces; the corrected derivation (Sinyukov, 1999) suppresses large-radius contributions with an additional exponential factor, yielding T* = TF + (2/π) · m · ⟨vT⟩². The author applies this formula to NA50 data on J/ψ and ψ′ mesons, assuming their thermal freeze-out coincides with QGP hadronization, to extract the collective transverse flow velocity of the hadronizing QGP.

## Key numbers
- Apparent temperature of J/ψ mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV: T*(J/ψ) = 245 ± 5 MeV (from NA50 data).
- Apparent temperature of ψ′ mesons in the same system: T*(ψ′) = 258 ± 5 MeV.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from light hadron systematics: TH = 175 ± 10 MeV.
- Extracted mean transverse flow velocity of QGP at hadronization: ⟨v_T^H⟩ ≈ 0.19 (from T*(J/ψ) = 245 MeV and TH = 175 MeV using the corrected formula).
- Predicted apparent temperature for D mesons at SPS: T*(D) ≈ 217 MeV.
- Predicted apparent temperature for Υ mesons at SPS: T*(Υ) ≈ 392 MeV.
- Predicted apparent temperature for J/ψ at RHIC (using ⟨v_T^H⟩ ≈ 0.30 from hydrodynamic calculations): T*(J/ψ) ≈ 350 MeV.
- Predicted apparent temperature for D mesons at RHIC: T*(D) ≈ 280 MeV.
- Predicted apparent temperature for Υ at RHIC: T*(Υ) ≈ 720 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the statistical coalescence model for charmonium production (Gorenstein and Gazdzicki, 1999; Braun-Munzinger et al., 2000) by addressing the previously neglected question of transverse mass spectra and freeze-out. The author resolves an inconsistency between two derivations of the apparent temperature formula: the naive result α = 1/2 from Heinz (1999) and the corrected result α = 2/π from Sinyukov (1999), showing that the naive derivation can produce unphysical negative apparent temperatures. The finding that J/ψ and ψ′ freeze out early, at hadronization rather than at the later pion freeze-out (TF = 120 ± 12 MeV, ⟨vT⟩ = 0.5 ± 0.12), is consistent with their low hadronic interaction cross sections. This enables predictions for open and hidden charm and bottom meson spectra at both SPS and RHIC energies, providing a testable signature of statistical hadronization.
