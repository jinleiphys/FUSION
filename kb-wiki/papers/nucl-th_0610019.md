# Four-body structure of $^7_Λ$Li and $ΛN$ spin-dependent interaction

> Two spin-doublet states of %$3/2^+$-$1/2^+$ and $7/2^+$-$5/2^+$ in $^7_Λ$Li are studied on the basis of the $α+Λ+n+p$ four-body model. We employ the two-body interactions which reproduce the observed properties of any subsystems composed of $αN$, $αΛ$ and $αNN$, and $αΛN$. Furthermore, the $ΛN$ interaction is adjusted so as to reproduce the $0^+$-$1^+$ splitting of in $^4_Λ$H. The calculated energy splittings of $3/2^+$-$1/2^+$ and $7/2^+$-$5/2^+$ states in $^7_Λ$Li are 0.69 MeV and 0.46 MeV, which are in good agreement with the resent observed data. The spin-dependent components of the $ΛN$ interaction are discussed.

# Four-body structure of $^7_\Lambda$Li and $\Lambda N$ spin-dependent interaction

## Key claim

The calculated energy splittings of 0.69 MeV for the $3/2^+$-$1/2^+$ doublet and 0.46 MeV for the $7/2^+$-$5/2^+$ doublet in $^7_\Lambda$Li agree with recent observed data, but only after the odd-state central $\Lambda N$ interaction is adjusted phenomenologically (by adding an attractive spin-spin correction of $\Delta v_{ss} = -100.0$ MeV in the second range) and the spin-orbit interactions (SLS and ALS) are tuned to reproduce the $^9_\Lambda$Be $5/2^+$-$3/2^+$ splitting.

## Method

The authors performed a four-body $\alpha + \Lambda + n + p$ cluster-model calculation using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with nine rearrangement channels in the Jacobian coordinate system. The $\alpha N$ interaction was taken from an effective potential reproducing low-lying $\alpha n$ states, the $NN$ interaction was based on a modified AV'8 potential adjusted to reproduce $^6$Li energies, the $\alpha\Lambda$ interaction was derived by folding a $\Lambda N$ G-matrix (Nijmegen model F) into the $\alpha$ density, and the $\Lambda N$ interaction was constructed to simulate NSC97f phase shifts with even-state strengths adjusted to reproduce the $0^+$-$1^+$ splitting in $^4_\Lambda$H and odd-state strengths tuned to the $^7_\Lambda$Li ground doublet.

## Key numbers

- $3/2^+$-$1/2^+$ splitting in $^7_\Lambda$Li: 0.69 MeV (calculated), in agreement with the observed value of 0.69 MeV.
- $7/2^+$-$5/2^+$ splitting in $^7_\Lambda$Li: 0.46 MeV (calculated), in agreement with the observed value of 0.47 MeV.
- Ground-state energy of $^7_\Lambda$Li ($1/2^+$): $-9.29$ MeV measured from the $\alpha+\Lambda+n+p$ threshold, corresponding to a $\Lambda$ binding energy of 5.58 MeV.
- Calculated $^6$Li $1^+$ and $3^+$ energies: $-3.7$ MeV and $-1.6$ MeV, respectively, measured from the $\alpha+n+p$ threshold.
- ALS parameter $\alpha = 0.83$ (where $V^{ALS} = -\alpha V^{SLS}$) chosen to reproduce the $^9_\Lambda$Be $5/2^+$-$3/2^+$ splitting.
- $^{13}_\Lambda$C $1/2^-$-$3/2^-$ splitting calculated to be 0.2 MeV using the same SLS/ALS interactions.

## Context

This work extends earlier cluster-model calculations for $^7_\Lambda$Li, specifically the $\alpha+d+\Lambda$ model (Motoba 1983, Wang 1987, Yamamoto 1991) and the $^5_\Lambda$He$+n+p$ model (Hiyama 1996), by treating the full $\alpha+n+p+\Lambda$ four-body system without frozen-cluster approximations. The results are consistent with shell-model calculations by Millener (2005) using phenomenological interaction matrix elements. The analysis constrains the odd-state spin-spin part of the $\Lambda N$ interaction and demonstrates that the SLS and ALS interactions, previously applied to $^9_\Lambda$Be and $^{13}_\Lambda$C (Hiyama 2000), can simultaneously reproduce the $^7_\Lambda$Li doublets. The work leaves open the question of how $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ coupling effects should be treated consistently across $^4_\Lambda$H and $^7_\Lambda$Li, as noted by Fetisov (1999) and Millener (2005).
