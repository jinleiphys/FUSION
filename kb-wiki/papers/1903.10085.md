# On resonance contribution to balance functions

> It is known that resonance decays influence the shape of the charge-balance functions measured in hadronic collisions. That is reflected in their rapidity and azimuthal widths and the integral, and therefore has consequences for different model interpretations. In this paper, we show that the contribution from neutral resonance decays can be removed from the balance function in an analytical way, and test the performance of the removal procedure with PYTHIA events. Prospects for applications of the procedure to real data analysis of balance functions are also discussed.

# On resonance contribution to balance functions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the contribution from neutral resonance decays (ρ⁰, ω, η) to the charge balance function can be removed analytically using a master equation, provided that resonance yields and single- and two-particle densities of their decay products are known; the procedure is tested only on PYTHIA events, not on real experimental data.

## Method

The author derives a master equation (Eq. 1) that expresses the balance function as a sum over neutral source types, each weighted by the average number of sources per event and their single- and two-particle densities. Using PYTHIA8 events for pp collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV with ALICE-like kinematic cuts (|y| < 0.8, 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c), the contributions from ρ⁰, ω, and η decays are subtracted from the numerator and denominator of the balance function to extract the "bulk" contribution. The procedure is tested by comparing the extracted bulk balance function to the true bulk balance function obtained by selecting only pions not originating from those resonances.

## Key numbers

- About 38% of charged pions within the chosen acceptance originate directly from quarks or gluons in PYTHIA8, while approximately 35% come from decays of neutral resonances ρ⁰, ω, and η.
- The balance function integral changes from 0.361 for all pions to 0.330 for the "bulk" after removing ρ⁰, ω, and η contributions.
- Relative fractions of 2ρ⁰/(π⁺ + π⁻) measured by ALICE are about 0.1, with ω yields being very similar.
- The ρ⁰ lifetime is cτ ≈ 1.3 fm/c, indicating that daughter pions can rescatter in the surrounding medium.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of resonance decay influence on balance functions from Bozek et al. (2005), who found that neutral resonance decays give about half of the pion pairs in the rapidity window considered and estimated resonance contributions in Au-Au collisions using STAR data. The analytical removal procedure builds on the observation by Bialas (2004) that in a model where particles are produced by decays of neutral clusters, only pairs from the same cluster contribute to the balance function. The method enables future application to real data from ALICE in pp, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions, where resonance yields and spectra are measured, and could also be used to remove contamination from weak decays such as K⁰S → π⁺π⁻ and Λ⁰ → πp.
