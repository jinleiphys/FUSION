# Nucleon resonances in πN scattering up to energies \sqrt(s) < 2.0 GeV

> A meson-exchange model for pion-nucleon scattering was previously constructed using a three-dimensional reduction scheme of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a model Lagrangian involving π, η, N, Δ, ρ, and σfields. We thereby extend our previous work by including the ηN channel and all the πN resonances with masses ~ 2 GeV, up to the F waves. The effects of the ππN channels are taken into account by introducing an effective width in the resonance propagators. The extended model gives an excellent fit to both πN phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in all channels up to the F waves and for energies below 2 GeV. We present a new scheme to extract the properties of overlapping resonances. The predicted values for the resonance masses and widths as well as resonance pole positions and residues are compared to the listing of the Particle Data Group.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous meson-exchange model for πN scattering to energies up to √s < 2.0 GeV by including the ηN channel and all πN resonances up to F waves, achieving an excellent fit to phase shifts and inelasticity parameters, but the fit requires four additional broad resonances not listed by the Particle Data Group and cannot describe the F17 channel.

## Method

The authors use a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a model Lagrangian involving π, η, N, Δ, ρ, and σ fields, extending their previous work by coupling the ηN channel and including resonances up to F waves. The effects of ππN channels are accounted for by introducing a phenomenological effective width term in the resonance propagators. A new scheme is developed to extract the properties of overlapping resonances in a completely symmetrical way, where the dressing of each resonance receives contributions from all other resonances in the same channel.

## Key numbers

- The model describes πN scattering data up to a center-of-mass energy of 2.0 GeV and partial waves up to F waves
- For the S11 channel, four resonances are needed to fit the data, including an additional S11(1878) not listed by the PDG
- The extracted width for the first S11 resonance increases from 90 MeV to 130 MeV compared to earlier work, and for the third S11 resonance from 265 MeV to 508 MeV, depending on the definition of resonance mass
- For the isospin-3/2 P31(1750) and P33(1920) resonances, extracted widths are about 500 MeV and 800 MeV respectively, both above PDG values
- The model requires four additional broad resonances not in the PDG listing: S11(1878), D13(2152), P13(2204), and P31(2100)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous meson-exchange model (Chen 2003) which was limited to the S11 channel up to 2 GeV, and earlier models (Hung 2001) that described πN scattering only up to 400 MeV. The approach assumes all nucleon resonances are fundamentally three-quark states dressed by coupling to meson-nucleon channels, in contrast to the Jülich model (Schutz 1998, Krehl 2000) where the Roper resonance P11(1440) is generated dynamically, and to the work of Gross (1993) where S11(1535) is generated dynamically. The results enable improved dynamical descriptions of pion photoproduction and provide resonance parameters that can be compared to lattice QCD calculations.
