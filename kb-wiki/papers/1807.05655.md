# Unified framework for heavy flavor and quarkonium production in high multiplicity p+p and p+A collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We discuss the production of $D$-mesons and $J/ψ$ in high multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions within the Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) framework. We demonstrate that the modification of the LHC data on $D$ and $J/ψ$ yields in high multiplicity events relative to minimum bias events arise from a significant enhancement of the gluon saturation scales of the corresponding rare parton configurations in the colliding protons and nuclei. For a given event multiplicity, we predict these relative yields to be energy independent from $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV at RHIC to the highest LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the observed enhancement of D-meson and J/psi yields in high-multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, relative to minimum-bias events, can be explained within the Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) framework by allowing the gluon saturation scales of the colliding protons and nuclei to fluctuate to larger values for rare parton configurations. The authors predict that these relative yields, for a given event multiplicity, are energy independent from sqrt(s)=200 GeV at RHIC to the highest LHC energies.

## Method

The authors compute charged hadron multiplicity by convoluting inclusive gluon production cross-sections, expressed in terms of unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDFs) encoding the saturation scales, with fragmentation functions. Heavy flavor production is calculated using a kT-factorized cross-section for c-cbar pairs, with single D-meson yields obtained via heavy quark fragmentation functions and J/psi yields computed using both the CGC+NRQCD formalism and the CGC+improved color evaporation model (ICEM). The rapidity dependence of the dipole amplitudes is obtained by solving the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, with initial saturation scales Q_sp,0^2=0.168 GeV^2 for the proton and Q_sA,0^2=2 Q_sp,0^2 for nuclei, determined from global fits to HERA DIS data.

## Key numbers

* Initial proton saturation scale: Q_sp,0^2 = 0.168 GeV^2, determined from DIS global fits to HERA data at x_0=0.01.
* Initial nuclear saturation scale: Q_sA,0^2 = 2 Q_sp,0^2, used as baseline for proton-nucleus collisions.
* D-meson results are presented for transverse momentum bins 1 < p_T < 2 GeV and 2 < p_T < 4 GeV in both p+p and p+A collisions at LHC energies.
* J/psi production results are shown for RHIC at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and LHC energies, with the CGC+NRQCD framework showing that the ^3S_1^[8] intermediate state dominates over ^1S_0^[8] and ^3P_J^[8] channels for all dN_ch/deta values.
* The unintegrated gluon distribution in high-multiplicity events, after QCD evolution to x=10^-4, peaks at transverse momenta of approximately 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the CGC effective field theory framework previously applied to minimum-bias heavy flavor and quarkonium production in p+p and p+A collisions (Ma 2014, Ma 2015, Ma 2017, Watanabe 2015, Fujii 2013, Fujii 2015, Fujii 2017) to the study of rare high-multiplicity events. The results enable systematic "event engineering" with heavy quarks to distinguish between initial-state (CGC saturation) and final-state effects in the ridge-like correlations observed in high-multiplicity collisions, and suggest future extensions to psi(2S) and Upsilon production, though these will require incorporating additional effects such as comover partons and Sudakov factors.
