# Stability constraint for spin equation of state

> A generalized Frenkel condition is proposed for use in spin hydrodynamics to relate the spin density and spin polarization (or spin chemical potential) tensors. It allows for independent treatment of electric- and magnetic-like components of the spin density tensor, which helps to fulfill the stability conditions recently derived in the literature. The generalized Frenkel condition extrapolates between the original Frenkel condition, where only the magnetic-like part of the spin tensor is present, and the case where the spin density tensor is directly proportional to the spin polarization tensor. We also demonstrate that our approach is supported by the result of a microscopic calculation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalized Frenkel condition that allows the electric-like and magnetic-like components of the spin density tensor to depend on the spin polarization tensor through two independent coefficients (S₁ and S₂), thereby satisfying the recently derived stability conditions χₛ > 0 and χ_b < 0 which the simpler linear relation S^{μν} ∝ ω^{μν} cannot fulfill.

## Method

The authors introduce a Lorentz-covariant ansatz S^{γδ} = S₁ (k^γ u^δ − k^δ u^γ) + S₂ ε^{γδρσ} u_ρ ω_σ, where k and ω are space-like vectors orthogonal to the flow u, and S₁, S₂ are functions of temperature T and chemical potential μ. They then extract the GLW spin tensor from relativistic kinetic theory (de Groot, van Leeuven, van Weert formalism) and compute the coefficients S₁ and S₂ explicitly for a gas of massive spinless particles, verifying the sign conditions S₁ < 0 and S₂ > 0.

## Key numbers

* For small mass-to-temperature ratio (m/T → 0): S₁ = −8 C T⁵ / (π² m²) and S₂ = 8 C T⁵ / (π² m²), giving S₂ = −S₁ exactly opposite.
* For large mass limit (m/T → ∞): S₁ = −(C T³ / π^{3/2}) √(m/(2T)) e^{−m/T} and S₂ = (C m T² / (2 π^{3/2})) √(m/(2T)) e^{−m/T}, so S₁ ≪ S₂ and the magnetic part dominates.
* The coefficients are expressed in terms of modified Bessel functions: S₁ = −C (T³/π²) [4 K₂(x) + x K₁(x)] and S₂ = C (T³/(2π²)) [(8+x²) K₂(x) + 2x K₁(x)], where x = m/T and C = cosh(μ/T).

## Context

This work resolves a paradox in spin hydrodynamics where the linear relation S^{μν} ∝ ω^{μν} (used by Wang (2021) and Biswas (2022)) forces χₛ = χ_b, contradicting the stability conditions χₛ > 0 and χ_b < 0 derived by Daher (2022), Sarwar (2022), and Xie (2023). The generalized Frenkel condition extrapolates between the original Frenkel condition (S₁ = 0, only magnetic part) and the proportional case (S₁ = S₂). The microscopic verification uses the GLW spin tensor from de Groot, van Leeuven, and van Weert (1980) as computed by Florkowski (2019). This enables stable spin-hydrodynamic simulations and suggests that future work should examine more general spin tensor forms and extend stability analysis to high-momentum modes.
