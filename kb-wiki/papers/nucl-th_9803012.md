# One- and two-neutron capture reactions of light nuclei in nuclear astrophysics

> We discuss models to calculate one- and two-neutron capture reactions on light nuclei. These are applied to calculate the reaction rates of 15N(n,gamma)16N, 16N(n,gamma)17}N and 4He(2n,gamma)6He. The possible astrophysical importance is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents models for one- and two-neutron capture reactions on light nuclei and applies them to calculate reaction rates for 15N(n,gamma)16N, 16N(n,gamma)17N, and 4He(2n,gamma)6He, but the astrophysical importance is only discussed qualitatively and the two-neutron capture rate on 4He is found to be dominated by the competing 4He(alpha n,gamma)9Be reaction under typical alpha-rich freeze-out conditions.

## Method

One-neutron capture cross sections were calculated using a hybrid model combining direct capture evaluated with real folding potentials in a potential model and resonant capture described by the Breit-Wigner formula, with spectroscopic factors taken from experiment or shell-model calculations. For the two-neutron capture reaction 4He(2n,gamma)6He, the rate was computed as a sequential two-step process (the 6He equivalent to alpha+n+n approach), where the first step forms unstable 5He via 4He+n and the second step treats 5He+n to 6He+gamma as a two-body problem in the initial state and a three-body problem in the final state. The rate was also deduced from the E1-strength function of Danilin et al. (1993) calculated in an alpha+n+n three-body model for 6He.

## Key numbers

- For 15N(n,gamma)16N: Q-value = 2.491 MeV; direct capture parameters A = 3.18, B = 3783.4, C = 335.2, D = 1.716 (rate in cm^3 mole^-1 s^-1 with T_9 in GK)
- For 16N(n,gamma)17N: Q-value = 5.883 MeV; direct capture parameters A = 0 (no s-wave contribution), B = 3649.9, C = 437.5, D = 1.633
- For 4He(2n,gamma)6He: Q_1 = -0.89 MeV, Q_2 = 1.87 MeV, total Q_12 = 0.98 MeV; width of intermediate state Gamma_c = 0.76 MeV
- The 4He(2n,gamma)6He rate from the alpha+n+n approach is larger by more than three orders of magnitude compared to the calculation of Fowler et al. (1975)
- The rate deduced from the E1-strength function of Danilin et al. (1993) is approximately two orders of magnitude larger than the alpha+n+n approach for T_9 > 0.2

## Context

This work extends the calculation of neutron capture rates on light nuclei relevant to inhomogeneous Big Bang models and the alpha-rich freeze-out in type II supernovae, building on prior work by Meissner et al. (1996) for 15N(n,gamma)16N and Rauscher et al. (1994) for 16N(n,gamma)17N. The two-neutron capture on 4He is compared with earlier calculations by Fowler et al. (1975) and Goerres et al. (1995), and with the photodisintegration approach of Danilin et al. (1993). The results enable improved reaction network calculations for primordial nucleosynthesis and supernova nucleosynthesis, though the authors note that even the enhanced 4He(2n,gamma)6He rate is dominated by the competing 4He(alpha n,gamma)9Be reaction under typical conditions.
