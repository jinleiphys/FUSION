# Self-interaction errors in nuclear energy density functionals

> When applied to a single nucleon, nuclear energy density functionals may yield a non-vanishing internal energy thus implying that the nucleon is interacting with itself. It is shown how to avoid this unphysical feature for semi-local phenomenological functionals containing all possible bilinear combinations of local densities and currents up to second order in the derivatives. The method outlined in this Rapid Communication could be easily extended to functionals containing higher order terms, and could serve as a guide for constraining the time-odd part of the functional.

# Self-interaction errors in nuclear energy density functionals

## Key claim
Semi-local phenomenological nuclear energy density functionals containing all possible bilinear combinations of local densities and currents up to second order in derivatives can be made free of self-interaction errors in the one-nucleon limit by imposing four specific constraints on the coupling constants, but this does not guarantee the absence of many-body self-interactions or self-pairing errors.

## Method
The author derived the conditions for vanishing nuclear energy in the one-nucleon limit by inserting a single-nucleon density matrix into the general semi-local functional form, applying gauge invariance relations, and requiring that the resulting expression vanish for any single-nucleon wavefunction. The analysis was performed for functionals containing all bilinear combinations of local densities, currents, and their derivatives up to second order, with the coupling constants treated as free parameters rather than derived from an underlying Skyrme force.

## Key numbers
- The rms deviation of phenomenological nuclear functionals fitting experimental nuclear mass data falls below 0.6 MeV (citing Goriely et al. 2009).
- The spurious self-interaction energy density in fully polarized neutron matter is given by δE = (C₀^ρ + C₁^ρ + C₀^s + C₁^s)ρ², which grows with increasing density.
- The kinetic density for polarized neutron matter is τ_n^pol = (3/5)(6π²)^(2/3)ρ^(5/3).

## Context
This work extends the discussion of self-interaction errors in nuclear energy density functionals, which were recognized early by Ripka (1969) and Stringari (1978) but barely discussed until recently. The paper contrasts the traditional Skyrme-force approach, where self-interactions cancel automatically due to the Hartree-Fock structure, with newer phenomenological functionals developed by Fayans (2000), Carlson et al. (2008), and Baldo et al. (2010) that are not directly constructed from effective forces. The derived constraints (Eqs. 16-19) provide a guide for constraining the time-odd part of the functional, which remains poorly known, and the method can be extended to higher-order functionals. The work also notes that many-body self-interactions from density-dependent terms, such as the t₃ term in Skyrme forces, require additional constraints beyond those derived here.
