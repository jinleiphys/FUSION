# Different particle alignments in N \approx Z Ru isotopes studied by the shell model

> Experimentally observed heaviest $N \approx Z$ nuclei, Ru isotopes, are investigated by the shell model on a spherical basis with the extended $P+QQ$ Hamiltonian. The energy levels of all the Ru isotopes can be explained by the shell model with a single set of force parameters. The calculations indicate enhancement of quadrupole correlations in the $N=Z$ nucleus $^{88}$Ru as compared with the other Ru isotopes, but the observed moments of inertia seem to require much more enhancement of quadrupole correlations in $^{88}$Ru. It is discussed that the particle alignment takes place at $8^+$ in $^{90}$Ru but is delayed in $^{88}$Ru till $16^+$ where the simultaneous alignments of proton and neutron pairs take place. The calculations present interesting predictions for $^{89}$Ru that the ground state is the $1/2^-$ state and there are three $ΔJ=2$ bands with different particle alignments including the T=0 $p-n$ pair alignment.

## Key claim

The spherical shell model with the extended P+QQ Hamiltonian, using a single set of force parameters, can reproduce the energy levels of Ru isotopes, but the observed moments of inertia for the N=Z nucleus 88Ru require much more enhancement of quadrupole correlations than the calculations provide, suggesting that the model space may need to be expanded or that the quadrupole correlations are specially enhanced in 88Ru compared to neighboring isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations in the hole space (1g9/2, 2p1/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2) using the extended P+QQ Hamiltonian, which includes monopole corrections, pairing forces, isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) and octupole-octupole forces. They treated single-hole energies as parameters and determined a single set of force parameters to reproduce overall energy levels of Ru isotopes, then systematically varied the QQ force strength (including isovector QQ contributions) to study the effect on backbending and moments of inertia. Additional calculations were performed in a truncated space (2p1/2, 1g9/2, 2d5/2) to examine the effect of including the 2d5/2 orbit.

## Key numbers

- The adopted single-hole energies (in MeV) are: εh9/2 = 0.0, εh1/2 = 1.1, εh5/2 = 5.5, εh3/2 = 6.0
- The force strengths (in MeV) are: g0 = 0.26(92/A), g2 = 0.12(92/A)5/3, χ02 = 0.26(92/A)5/3, χ03 = 0.04(92/A)2
- The maximum shell model dimension is 165 × 106 for 88Ru
- The calculation reproduces observed energies within an error of 0.8 MeV
- The deformation parameter from the projected shell model is 0.23 for 88Ru and 0.16 for 90Ru (the former is 1.4 times as large as the latter)
- B(E2: 2→0) for 88Ru ranges from 460 to 612 e2fm4 across calculations A through E, while for 90Ru it ranges from 296 to 482 e2fm4
- The ratio of B(E2) values between 88Ru and 90Ru is more than 1.6 in calculation A

## Context

This work extends the shell model study of N≈Z nuclei using the extended P+QQ Hamiltonian, which previously succeeded for 64Ge (Kaneko, 2002) and lighter 1f7/2-subshell nuclei (Hasegawa, 1997, 1999). The results are consistent with the projected shell model calculations of Marginean (2002) and Sun (2002), which used fixed deformations of 0.23 for 88Ru and 0.16 for 90Ru. The paper predicts that 89Ru has a 1/2- ground state and three ΔJ=2 bands with different particle alignments including T=0 p-n pair alignment, and suggests that including the 2d5/2 orbit in the model space could improve agreement with experiment for 88Ru.
