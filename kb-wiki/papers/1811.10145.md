# $α$ Clustering slightly above ${}^{100}$Sn in the Light of the New Experimental Data on the Superallowed $α$ Decay

> The recently observed $α$-decay chain ${}^{108}\text{Xe}\to{}^{104}\text{Te}\to{}^{100}\text{Sn}$ [K.~Auranen \emph{et al.}, Phys.\ Rev.\ Lett.\ {\bf121}, 182501 (2018)] could provide valuable information on the $α$ clustering in even-even nuclei slightly above the major shells $Z=50$ and $N=50$. In this work, this $α$-decay chain is studied theoretically within the framework of the density-dependent cluster model plus the two-potential approach. We calculate the $α$-decay half-lives of ${}^{104}$Te and ${}^{108}$Xe and study in detail their dependence on the $Q$ value and the density-profile parameters of the core nucleus. Various physical properties of ${}^{104}$Te, the heaviest nucleus with a doubly magic self-conjugate core $+$ $α$, are calculated, with two different assumptions on the renormalization factor of the double-folding potential, which could be a useful reference for future experimental studies.

## Key claim

The density-dependent cluster model (DDCM) with a double-folding potential can reproduce the newly measured alpha-decay half-lives of 108Xe and 104Te, but the agreement depends sensitively on the Q-value uncertainty and the assumed density-profile parameters of the core nucleus; the upper limit of the experimental half-life for 104Te (less than 18 ns) favors a Q value in the upper part of its 0.2 MeV error bar.

## Method

The authors treat the parent nucleus as a binary alpha-core system and compute the effective alpha-core potential using a double-folding integral with M3Y nucleon-nucleon interactions and a Coulomb term, renormalized by a factor lambda. The alpha-decay half-life is then estimated via the two-potential approach, with the alpha formation probability set to unity; the Wildermuth condition (global quantum number G=16) enforces the Pauli principle. The authors also study the rotational band of 104Te by solving the two-body Schrodinger equation in the quasibound-state approximation, calculating both alpha-decay widths and E2 gamma-decay widths for states up to 16+.

## Key numbers

- 108Xe: experimental Q_alpha = 4.6(2) MeV, experimental half-life T_1/2 = 58(+106)(-23) microseconds; theoretical half-life range (benchmark) = (4.1-213) x 10^3 ns, hindrance factor range = 0.07-4.
- 104Te: experimental Q_alpha = 5.1(2) MeV, experimental half-life upper bound T_1/2 < 18 ns; theoretical half-life range (benchmark) = 7-166 ns, hindrance factor range = 0.4-9.
- Benchmark center-value theoretical half-lives: T_1/2(108Xe) = 28 x 10^3 ns for Q_alpha = 4.6 MeV; T_1/2(104Te) = 32 ns for Q_alpha = 5.1 MeV.
- For the 2+ state of 104Te (constant lambda = 0.64417): excitation energy E_x = 0.12 MeV, alpha-decay width Gamma_alpha = 1.59 x 10^(-14) MeV, gamma-decay width Gamma_gamma = 6.31 x 10^(-15) MeV, alpha branching ratio b_alpha = 72%.
- For the 4+ state of 104Te (constant lambda): E_x = 0.39 MeV, b_alpha = 3%.
- With L-dependent renormalization (g = 5 x 10^(-3)): 2+ state E_x = 0.75 MeV, b_alpha = 2%; 4+ state E_x = 1.66 MeV, b_alpha = 15%.
- RMS charge radius of the alpha particle used: 1.67 fm; RMS charge radius of 100Sn (benchmark): 4.41 fm; empirical RMS charge radius of 100Sn: 4.44 fm.

## Context

This work reanalyzes the alpha-decay chain 108Xe -> 104Te -> 100Sn, first observed by Auranen et al. (2018), using a modified density-dependent cluster model that differs slightly from the earlier theoretical study by Xu and Ren (2006). The paper extends prior systematic DDCM studies of alpha emitters near 100Sn (Xu and Ren, 2006) and provides the first detailed calculation of the rotational band and decay branching ratios for 104Te, the heaviest nucleus with a doubly magic self-conjugate core plus an alpha particle. The results enable future experimental work to constrain the Q value of 104Te more precisely and to optimize production of 104Te in its ground state by understanding the competition between alpha and gamma decay in excited states.
