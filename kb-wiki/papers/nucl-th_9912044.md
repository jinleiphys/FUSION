# Pion-Baryon Couplings

> We have extended and applied a general QCD parameterization method to the emission of pions from baryons. We use it to calculate the strength and sign of the coupling of pions to the octet and decuplet of baryons. Certain relations between octet and decuplet couplings are pointed out.

# Pion-Baryon Couplings

## Key claim

The Morpurgo QCD parameterization method can be extended to pion-baryon couplings despite the pion's internal quark structure, and the inclusion of two-body quark operators yields significant corrections to the additive quark model predictions. The results satisfy several sum rules relating octet and decuplet couplings, including new relations that connect octet, decuplet, and octet-decuplet transition couplings for any value of the strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors apply the Morpurgo unitary transformation method, which maps exact QCD matrix elements onto matrix elements evaluated with auxiliary three-quark SU(6) spin-flavor wave functions, absorbing all orbital and color degrees of freedom into unknown parameters. They parameterize the pion-baryon coupling operator as a sum of one-body and two-body quark operators (neglecting three-body terms), where the two-body term includes an SU(3) symmetry breaking factor r = m_u/m_s. The constants A1 and A2 are fixed using the experimental πN coupling f^2/4π = 0.08 and the Δ(1232) decay width of about 130 MeV, which gives f_{NΔπ} ≈ 2f.

## Key numbers

- The one-body constant is fixed as A1 = (3/5)f from the πN coupling f^2/4π = 0.08
- With two-body terms included, A1 ≈ 0.53 f and A2 ≈ -0.18 f, giving A2/A1 ≈ 1/3
- The SU(3) symmetry breaking parameter r ≈ 330/550 ≈ 0.6 (ratio of light to strange quark masses)
- The π^0Δ^+Δ^+ coupling changes from the first-order value f_{πΔΔ} = (4/5)f to the total result f_{πΔΔ} = 0.23 f
- For the transition Δ^{++} → p π^+: 4√3(A-B)/3; for Σ^{*+} → Σ^0 π^+: -2√2(A-(2r-1)B)/3; for Σ^{*+} → Λ^0 π^+: -2√6(A-B)/3
- Ratio Δ^{++} → p over Σ^{*+} → Σ^0 = -√6 (with SU(3) breaking: -3.06)
- Ratio Σ^{*+} → Σ^0 over Σ^{*+} → Λ^0 = -1/√3 (with SU(3) breaking: -0.46)

## Context

This work extends the Morpurgo QCD parameterization method (Morpurgo 1989, 1992), previously applied to baryon masses, magnetic moments, and electromagnetic form factors (Dillon and Morpurgo 1999), to the strong interaction of pions with baryons. The results agree with the large N_c approach of Dashen et al. (1994) for transition couplings, and the numerical values tend to be closer to those from baryon-nucleon scattering fits by Stoks and Rijken (using one-boson exchange potentials) than to QCD sum rule results from Kim et al. The new relations in Eq. (13) enable predictions of decuplet baryon-pion couplings from experimentally better known octet and octet-decuplet transition couplings, independent of the strange quark mass.
