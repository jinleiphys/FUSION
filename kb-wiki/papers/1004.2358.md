# Lowest-order contributions of chiral three-nucleon interactions to pairing properties of nuclear ground states

> We perform a systematic study of the odd-even mass staggering generated using a pairing interaction computed at first order in low-momentum interactions. Building on previous work including the (nuclear plus Coulomb) two-nucleon interaction only, we focus here on the first-order contribution from chiral three-nucleon forces. We observe a significant repulsive effect from the three-nucleon interaction. The combined contribution from two- and three-nucleon interactions accounts for approximately 70% of the experimental pairing gaps. This leaves room for higher-order contributions to the pairing kernel and the normal self-energy that need to be computed consistently.

## Key claim
The first-order contribution from chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions to the pairing kernel systematically lowers theoretical pairing gaps by about 30%, and the combined two-nucleon plus three-nucleon first-order result accounts for approximately 70% of experimental pairing gaps, leaving room for higher-order contributions.

## Method
The authors constructed a density-dependent two-body interaction from chiral 3N forces at next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) by summing one particle over occupied Fermi-sea states, then added this to a low-momentum two-nucleon (NN) interaction \(V_{{\rm low}\,k}\) with cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.8 \, {\rm fm}^{-1}\). The resulting first-order pairing kernel was represented as a sum of separable terms and applied within a local density approximation to compute odd-even mass staggering (three-point mass differences) for semi-magic spherical nuclei. The normal self-energy part of the energy density functional was taken from a Skyrme parameterization with effective masses constrained by Hartree-Fock calculations using the same NN and 3N interactions.

## Key numbers
- The 3N contribution lowers pairing gaps systematically by about **30%** relative to the NN-only result.
- The combined NN+3N first-order pairing interaction accounts for approximately **70%** of experimental pairing gaps.
- The low-momentum NN cutoff was \(\Lambda = 1.8 \, {\rm fm}^{-1}\) and the chiral 3N cutoff was \(\Lambda_{\rm 3N} = 2.0 \, {\rm fm}^{-1}\).
- The Skyrme isoscalar effective mass at saturation density was constrained to the range **0.67–0.73** for the normal self-energy part.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of finite-nuclei ground states using only low-momentum two-nucleon interactions (Duguet 2008, Lesinski 2009, Duguet 2009) by adding the first-order contribution from chiral three-nucleon forces. The repulsive effect of the 3N interaction in the \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel was previously noted by Hebeler (2009) and Lesinski (2010). The results indicate that consistent inclusion of higher-order contributions to both the pairing kernel and the normal self-energy, from the same low-momentum NN and 3N interactions, is necessary to achieve a fully predictive non-empirical energy density functional.
