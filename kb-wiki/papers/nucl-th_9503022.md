# Toroidal quadrupole transitions associated to collective rotational-vibrational motions of the nucleus

> In the frame of the algebraic Riemann Rotational Model one computes the longitudinal, transverse and toroidal multipoles corresponding to the excitations of low-lying levels in the ground state band of several even-even nuclei by inelastic electron scattering (e,e'). Related to these transitions a new quantity, which accounts for the deviations from the Siegert theorem, is introduced. The intimate connection between the nuclear vorticity and the dynamic toroidal quadrupole moment is underlined. Inelastic differential cross-sections calculated at backscattering angles shows the dominancy of toroidal form-factors over a broad range of momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the toroidal quadrupole transition moment, derived from a multipole decomposition that goes beyond the Siegert theorem, is directly linked to nuclear vorticity and can be observed in inelastic electron scattering at backscattering angles, where the toroidal form factor dominates the cross-section over a broad range of momentum transfer. The key hedge is that this dominance holds specifically for the rigid-rotor (RR) submodel of the Riemann Rotational Model, while for the irrotational-flow (IF) model the approximation works well only up to a certain momentum transfer (q < 400 MeV/c for quadrupole transitions).

## Method

The author computes longitudinal, transverse electric, and toroidal multipole form factors for low-lying quadrupole and hexadecupole excitations in the ground-state band of even-even nuclei (152Sm, 154Sm, 166Er) using the algebraic Riemann Rotational Model. The velocity field is taken as a convex combination of rigid-rotor and irrotational-flow contributions, parameterized by a rigidity parameter r̃ ranging from 0 (irrotational) to 1 (rigid). The differential cross-section for inelastic electron scattering at 180° (backscattering) is calculated in the plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA), and the toroidal contribution is isolated by subtracting the Siegert limit.

## Key numbers

- For 152Sm (β = 0.246), the toroidal quadrupole transition moment T2 in arbitrary units is: 5.2×10⁻⁴ for IF (r̃ = 0), 1.32×10⁻³ for r̃ = 0.5, and 1.72×10⁻² for RR (r̃ = 1).
- For 154Sm (β = 0.270), T2 is: 5.6×10⁻⁴ for IF, 1.42×10⁻³ for r̃ = 0.5, and 1.56×10⁻² for RR.
- For 166Er (β = 0.287), T2 is: 5.2×10⁻⁴ for IF, 1.59×10⁻³ for r̃ = 0.5, and 1.52×10⁻² for RR.
- The toroidal quadrupole moment is two orders of magnitude larger in the RR case than in the IF case.
- The deviation from the Siegert theorem (η₂) is studied in the range q = 0 to 150 MeV/c, where it increases with rigidity and mass number, and oscillates around η = 1 above q = 150 MeV/c.
- For the IF model, the approximate cross-section (toroidal only) agrees with the exact one for q < 400 MeV/c for λ = 2 and q < 250 MeV/c for λ = 4.

## Context

This work extends the Riemann Rotational Model (Riemann, 1860; Rosenkilde, 1967) to include toroidal multipole moments, building on the multipole decomposition formalism of Dubovik and Cheshkov (1974) and the concept of the toroidal dipole (anapole) introduced by Zel'dovich (1958). It contrasts with the standard Bohr-Mottelson collective model, which assumes irrotational flow, by showing that rigid-rotor currents produce measurable toroidal quadrupole transitions at high momentum transfer. The results enable experimental tests of nuclear vorticity via backscattering electron scattering experiments, as suggested by measurements of normal-parity transverse excitations at 180° (reported by various authors in the 1980s and 1990s).
