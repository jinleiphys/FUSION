# Production of Strange, Non-strange particles and Hypernuclei in an Excluded-Volume Model

> We present a systematic study of production of strange and non-strange hadron yields and their ratios obtained in various experiments using our thermodynamically consistent excluded-volume model. We also analyze the production of light nuclei, hypernuclei and their antinuclei in terms of our excluded-volume model over a broad energy range starting from Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS) to Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. Further, we extend our model for studying rapidity spectra of hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions.

# Production of Strange, Non-strange particles and Hypernuclei in an Excluded-Volume Model

## Key claim
The authors' thermodynamically consistent excluded-volume model provides a good description of experimental data on hadron yield ratios and rapidity distributions across a broad energy range (AGS to LHC), but only after incorporating longitudinal flow; the model fails for multistrange particles, suggesting a different production mechanism (e.g., coalescence) is needed.

## Method
The authors use a grand canonical partition function with an excluded-volume correction for baryons (hard-core radius 0.8 fm) while treating mesons as pointlike particles, including all hadrons and resonances up to 2 GeV/c². Strangeness conservation is enforced via a strangeness neutrality condition. Rapidity distributions are calculated from a thermal model and then modified by integrating over a longitudinal flow velocity parameter η_max. The model is compared to experimental data for hadron ratios, light nuclei, hypernuclei, and their antinuclei from AGS to LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- Hard-core radius for baryons: r = 0.8 fm
- Longitudinal flow velocity β_L = 0.922 at √s_NN = 200 GeV for central Au-Au collisions, extracted from fitting antiproton rapidity distributions with η_max = 3.20
- The "correction term" in the thermodynamic consistency check is shown to be negligible at constant μ_B = 400 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier excluded-volume model (Tiwari 2012) and addresses a criticism by Gorenstein (2012) regarding thermodynamic consistency. The model is compared against the Cleymans-Suhonen model (1987), the RGSG model (Rischke 1991), the statistical hadron gas model (Andronic 2006), and the UrQMD transport model (Bleicher 1999). The analysis of rapidity distributions with longitudinal flow builds on experimental data from Bearden (2004). The results enable further study of hypernuclei production and suggest that thermal models alone are insufficient for multistrange particles, pointing toward coalescence mechanisms as a next step.
