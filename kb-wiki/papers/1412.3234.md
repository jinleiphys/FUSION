# Photoproduction of the $f_2(1270)$ resonance

> We have performed a calculation of the $纬p \to 蟺^+ 蟺^- p$ reaction, where the two pions have been separated in $D$-wave producing the $f_2(1270)$ resonance. We use elements of the local hidden gauge approach that provides the interaction of vector mesons in which the $f_2(1270)$ resonance appears as a $蟻$-$蟻$ molecular state in $L=0$ and spin 2. The vector meson dominance, incorporated in the local hidden gauge approach converts a photon into a $蟻^0$ meson and the other meson connects the photon with the proton. The picture is simple and has no free parameters, since the parameters of the theory have been constrained in the previous study of the vector-vector states. In a second step we introduce new elements, not present in the local hidden gauge approach, adapting the $蟻$ propagator to Regge phenomenology and introducing the $蟻NN$ tensor coupling. We find that both the differential cross section as well as the $t$ dependence of the cross section are in good agreement with the experimental results and provide support for the molecular picture of the $f_2(1270)$ in the first baryonic reaction where it has been tested.

# Photoproduction of the $f_2(1270)$ resonance

## Key claim

The $f_2(1270)$ resonance, modeled as a $\rho$-$\rho$ molecular state in $L=0$ and spin 2 within the local hidden gauge approach, can describe the $\gamma p \to \pi^+ \pi^- p$ reaction data from Jefferson Lab without free parameters, but only when the $\rho$ propagator is adapted to Regge phenomenology and the $\rho NN$ tensor coupling is introduced does the model achieve good agreement with both the differential cross section and the $t$ dependence.

## Method

The authors compute the $\gamma p \to \pi^+ \pi^- p$ reaction where the two pions are produced in $D$-wave via the $f_2(1270)$ resonance. The resonance is treated as a dynamically generated $\rho$-$\rho$ molecular state, with the photon converting to a $\rho^0$ meson via vector meson dominance, and the second $\rho^0$ mediating the interaction with the proton through $\rho NN$ vertices. Three models are compared: Model A uses only the vector $\rho NN$ coupling with a normal $\rho$ propagator; Model B adds the tensor $\rho NN$ coupling; Model C replaces the normal $\rho$ propagator with a Regge propagator while keeping both vector and tensor couplings.

## Key numbers

- $g_T^2 = 150$ GeV$^2$: the squared coupling of the $f_2(1270)$ to $\rho\rho$ in isospin $I=0$ and spin $S=2$, evaluated from the $\rho\rho$ amplitude generated in earlier work (Raquelvec).
- $M_{f_2} = 1275$ MeV: the mass of the $f_2(1270)$ resonance used in the width parametrization.
- $\Gamma^{\rm on}_{f_2} = 185$ MeV: the total on-shell decay width of the $f_2(1270)$.
- $\Gamma^{\rm on}_{\pi} = 105$ MeV: the partial on-shell decay width of the $f_2(1270)$ into $\pi\pi$.
- $g_{\rho NN}^2/4\pi = 0.9$ and $\kappa_{\rho} = 6.1$: the vector and tensor coupling constants for the $\rho NN$ vertex from the Bonn potential (Machleidt 1987).
- $\widehat{f} = 1.43$: the overall normalization factor for the Reggeon exchange contribution in Model C, fitted to the data.
- $\Lambda = 0.75$ GeV: the cutoff parameter for the dipole form factor applied to the $\rho NN$ vertex in Model B.
- $E_{\gamma} = 3.3$ GeV and $t = -0.55$ GeV$^2$: the specific kinematic point at which the invariant mass distribution is compared to CLAS data (Battaglieri 2009).
- $\alpha_{\rho}(t) = 0.55 + 0.8 t$: the Regge trajectory for the $\rho$ meson.

## Context

This work extends the molecular picture of the $f_2(1270)$ as a $\rho$-$\rho$ composite state, previously tested in mesonic reactions such as $f_2(1270) \to \gamma\gamma$ (Yamagata), $J/\psi$ decays (Alberzou, Geng 2009), and $\bar B^0_s \to J/\psi f_2(1270)$ (Xie), into the baryonic sector for the first time. It directly confronts the CLAS photoproduction measurement (Battaglieri 2009) and contrasts with the approach of Robert (2009), which generates the $f_2(1270)$ from final-state $D$-wave $\pi\pi$ interaction rather than from $\rho\rho$ dynamics. The success of the model enables future tests of the molecular nature of tensor mesons in baryonic reactions, particularly through total cross section measurements at CLAS.
