# Radiative decay of the sub-threshold $1_1^-$ and $2_1^+$ states of $^{16}$O in cluster effective field theory

> Radiative decay of the sub-threshold $1_1^-$ and $2_1^+$ states of $^{16}$O is studied in cluster effective field theory. The wave function normalization factors for initial and final states of the radiative decay amplitudes are deduced by using the phase shift data of elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering for $l=0,1,2$, which are related to the asymptotic normalization coefficients of $0_1^+$, $1_1^-$, $2_1^+$ states of $^{16}$O for the two-body $α$-$^{12}$C channel; then only one unfixed parameter remains in each of the radiative decay amplitudes. We fit the parameters to the experimental radiative decay rates and apply the fitted parameters to the study of radiative $α$ capture on $^{12}$C, $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O. The order of magnitude of astrophysical $S$ factors of $E1$ and $E2$ transitions of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O is reproduced compared to the experimental data, and we discuss an improvement in the calculation of $S$ factors of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the order of magnitude of astrophysical S factors for E1 and E2 transitions of the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction can be reproduced using cluster effective field theory with parameters fixed from radiative decay data of the sub-threshold 1₁⁻ and 2₁⁺ states of 16O, but the calculation requires improvement to achieve quantitative precision.

## Method

The author constructs a cluster effective field theory with α and 12C as non-relativistic point-like scalar fields, introducing auxiliary fields for bound states of 16O with angular momenta l=0,1,2. Wave function normalization factors (equivalent to asymptotic normalization coefficients) for the 0₁⁺, 1₁⁻, and 2₁⁺ states of 16O are extracted from phase shift data of elastic α-12C scattering for l=0,1,2, leaving one unfixed parameter in each radiative decay amplitude. These remaining parameters are fitted to experimental radiative decay rates of the 1₁⁻ and 2₁⁺ states, and the fitted parameters are then applied to calculate the astrophysical S factors for 12C(α,γ)16O.

## Key numbers

* Binding momenta: γ₀ = 200.1 MeV (0₁⁺ state), γ₁ = 15.88 MeV (1₁⁻ state), γ₂ = 37.00 MeV (2₁⁺ state) of 16O
* Inverse Bohr radius: κ = 245 MeV for the α-12C system
* Reduced mass of α and 12C: μ = 2795 MeV
* Asymptotic normalization coefficient of the 0₁⁺ ground state: |C_b|₀₁ = 44.5(3) fm⁻¹/²
* Asymptotic normalization coefficient of the 1₁⁻ state: |C_b|₁ = 1.727(3) × 10¹⁴ fm⁻¹/²
* Asymptotic normalization coefficient of the 2₁⁺ state: |C_b|₂ = 3.1(6) × 10⁴ fm⁻¹/²
* Experimental radiative decay rate of the 1₁⁻ state: Γ₁_exp = 0.055(3) eV
* Experimental radiative decay rate of the 2₁⁺ state: Γ₂_exp = 0.097(3) eV
* Expansion parameter: Q/Λ_H = 1/4, where Λ_H = 160 MeV and Q = 40 MeV at the Gamow-peak energy E_G = 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous cluster EFT studies of elastic α-12C scattering (Ando, 2018, 2023) and E1 transitions in 12C(α,γ)16O (Ando, 2019) by incorporating radiative decay data to constrain the capture amplitudes. The approach improves upon the previous sharp-cutoff regularization method by using dimensional regularization for logarithmic divergences, yielding milder cutoff dependence. The extracted ANC for the 0₁⁺ state (44.5 fm⁻¹/²) is compared to the R-matrix analysis value of 58 fm⁻¹/² from deBoer et al. (2017), while the ANC for the 2₁⁺ state is noted to be about four times smaller than other literature values, indicating sensitivity to the fit conditions for l=2 phase shifts. The work enables parameter-free calculations of the astrophysical S factors for 12C(α,γ)16O, though the author discusses that further improvement is needed for quantitative precision.
