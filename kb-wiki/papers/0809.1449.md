# Applicability of the Linear delta Expansion for the lambda phi^4 Field Theory at Finite Temperature in the Symmetric and Broken Phases

> The thermodynamics of a scalar field with a quartic interaction is studied within the linear delta expansion (LDE) method. Using the imaginary-time formalism the free energy is evaluated up to second order in the LDE. The method generates nonperturbative results that are then used to obtain thermodynamic quantities like the pressure. The phase transition pattern of the model is fully studied, from the broken to the symmetry restored phase. The results are compared with those obtained with other nonperturbative methods and also with ordinary perturbation theory. The results coming from the two main optimization procedures used in conjunction with the LDE method, the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS) and the Fastest Apparent Convergence (FAC) are also compared with each other and studied in which cases they are applicable or not. The optimization procedures are applied directly to the free energy.

# Applicability of the Linear delta Expansion for the lambda phi^4 Field Theory at Finite Temperature in the Symmetric and Broken Phases

## Key claim

The linear delta expansion (LDE) applied to the lambda phi^4 theory at finite temperature, when optimized using either the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS) or the Fastest Apparent Convergence (FAC) criterion applied directly to the free energy, produces nonperturbative results that converge quickly at order delta^2 and correctly reproduce the expected second-order phase transition pattern, but the applicability of each optimization criterion depends on whether nontrivial solutions exist for the variational parameter eta.

## Method

The authors used the imaginary-time formalism to compute the free energy of the lambda phi^4 scalar field theory up to second order in the LDE, which required evaluating vacuum diagrams up to three loops and background field dependent contributions. They applied two optimization procedures, the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS) and the Fastest Apparent Convergence (FAC), directly to the free energy to fix the arbitrary mass parameter eta, generating nonperturbative results for the pressure, critical temperature, and temperature-dependent vacuum expectation value in both the symmetric and broken phases.

## Key numbers

- Pressure results at order delta and delta^2 using PMS optimization show convergence, with both orders not differing too much, in contrast to perturbative results up to O(lambda^2) which exhibit typical alternating behavior indicating poor convergence.
- The pressure at order delta^2 from either PMS or FAC shows excellent agreement with the 2PI two-loop result of Berges and Reinosa (2005).
- The optimum eta bar becomes closer to the one-loop thermal mass m_T as the order in delta increases.
- The critical temperature T_c, pressure P, and background dependent free energy F were obtained in an explicit calculation up to order delta^2.

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the LDE to thermal field theories, particularly the Gross-Neveu model studied by Kneur and Pinto (2005) and the O(N) model studied by Kneur, Pinto, and Ramos (2007), where convergence was demonstrated. It directly compares the PMS and FAC optimization criteria, which had not been systematically contrasted for the lambda phi^4 theory at finite temperature. The results enable benchmarking the LDE against other nonperturbative methods such as two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective actions (Berges and Reinosa, 2005), screened perturbation theory (Andersen, Braaten, and Strickland, 2001), and the Phi-derivable technique (Iancu, 1998), showing that the LDE is operationally simpler while producing equivalent results.
