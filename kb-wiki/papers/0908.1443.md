# Theoretical study of lepton events in the atmospheric neutrino experiments at SuperK

> Super-Kamiokande has reported the results for the lepton events in the atmospheric neutrino experiment. These results have been presented for a 22.5kT water fiducial mass on an exposure of 1489 days, and the events are divided into sub-GeV, multi-GeV and PC events. We present a study of nuclear medium effects in the sub-GeV energy region of atmospheric neutrino events for the quasielastic scattering, incoherent and coherent pion production processes, as they give the most dominant contribution to the lepton events in this energy region. We have used the atmospheric neutrino flux given by Honda et al. These calculations have been done in the local density approximation. We take into account the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Coulomb effect, renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium in the case of the quasielastic reactions. The inelastic reactions leading to production of leptons along with pions is calculated in a $Δ$- dominance model by taking into account the renormalization of $Δ$ properties in the nuclear medium and the final state interaction effects of the outgoing pions with the residual nucleus. We present the results for the lepton events obtained in our model with and without nuclear medium effects, and compare them with the Monte Carlo predictions used in the simulation and the experimentally observed events reported by the Super-Kamiokande collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Coulomb corrections, RPA renormalization of weak transition strengths, and modification of Δ properties in the medium) in the calculation of sub-GeV atmospheric neutrino event rates at Super-Kamiokande brings the predicted lepton event numbers closer to the experimentally observed values than the Monte Carlo predictions used in the Super-K simulation, though the comparison is made only for sub-GeV events and the model still shows some discrepancies.

## Method

The authors calculated charged-current quasielastic scattering and incoherent and coherent single-pion production cross sections for atmospheric neutrinos on oxygen (¹⁶O) in the local density approximation, using the atmospheric neutrino flux of Honda et al. For quasielastic scattering, nuclear medium effects were included via the Lindhard function for Pauli blocking and Fermi motion, RPA correlations through π and ρ exchange with a Landau-Migdal parameter g′ = 0.7, and Coulomb corrections via an effective momentum approximation. For pion production, a Δ-dominance model was used with in-medium modifications of the Δ mass and width, plus final-state interactions of outgoing pions (absorption and scattering). Event rates were obtained by integrating flux-folded cross sections over the Super-Kamiokande detector response for a 22.5 kT water fiducial mass over 1489 live days.

## Key numbers

- Super-Kamiokande exposure: 22.5 kT water fiducial mass, 1489 live days
- Sub-GeV events defined as lepton energy E_l < 1.33 GeV, with minimum observed momenta of 100 MeV for electrons and 200 MeV for muons
- Axial dipole mass M_A = 1.1 GeV used in the calculation, consistent with the value used in Super-K analysis (Mitsuka)
- World average value for axial dipole mass: M_A = 1.026 ± 0.020 GeV
- NOMAD result for M_A: 1.05 ± 0.02 ± 0.06 GeV
- K2K SciBar detector result: M_A = 1.14 ± 0.11 GeV
- K2K SciFi detector result: M_A = 1.20 ± 0.12 GeV
- MiniBooNE result: M_A = 1.23 ± 0.2 GeV
- Landau-Migdal parameter g′ = 0.7 used, with variation studied at g′ = 0.63 and 0.77
- Axial charge F_A(0) = -1.267
- Vector dipole mass M_V = 0.84 GeV
- Cabibbo angle θ_C = 13.1°
- Fermi coupling constant G_F = 1.16639 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nuclear medium effects in neutrino-nucleus scattering on targets including ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ar, and ⁵⁶Fe (Quasi1–Coh1) to the specific case of atmospheric neutrino events at Super-Kamiokande. The Super-K Monte Carlo simulation uses the Smith-Moniz model (SmithMoniz) for quasielastic scattering, which includes Pauli blocking and Fermi motion in a Fermi gas model but not nucleon-nucleon correlations, and the Rein-Sehgal model (Rein, Rein1) for pion production with final-state interactions. The present calculation improves upon these by incorporating RPA correlations and in-medium Δ modifications. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments using water Cherenkov detectors, relevant for T2K (t2k), NOvA (nova), and future experiments with beta beams and neutrino factories.
