# Replica Symmetry Breaking for the Integrable Two-Site Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev Model

> We analyze a two-body nonhermitian two-site Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model with the couplings of one site complex conjugated to the other site. This model, with no explicit coupling between the sites, shows an infinite number of phase transitions which is a consequence of the partition function factorizing into a product over Matsubara frequencies. We calculate the quenched free energy in two different ways, first in terms of the single-particle energies, and second by solving the Schwinger-Dyson equations. The first calculation can be done entirely in terms of a one-site model. The conjugate replica enters due to non-analyticities when Matsubara frequencies enter the spectral support of the coupling matrix. The second calculation is based on the replica trick of the two-site partition function. Both methods give the same result. The free-fermion partition function can be rephrased as a matrix model for the coupling matrix. Up to minor details, this model is the random matrix model that describes the chiral phase transition of QCD, and the order parameter of the two-body model corresponds to the chiral condensate of QCD. Comparing to the corresponding four-body model, we are able to determine which features of the free energy are due to chaotic nature of the four-body model. The high-temperature phase of both models is entropy dominated, and in both cases is determined by the spectral density. The four-body SYK model has a low-temperature phase whose free energy is almost temperature-independent, signaling an effective gap of the theory even though the actual spectrum does not exhibit a gap. However the low-temperature free energy of the two-body SYK model is not flat, in fact it oscillates to arbitrarily low temperature. This indicates a less desirable feature that the entropy of the two-body model is not always positive, which most likely is a consequence of the nonhermiticity.

## Key claim

The two-site nonhermitian Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model with two-body (q=2) interactions exhibits an infinite number of second-order phase transitions, in contrast to the single first-order transition of the chaotic four-body model, because the partition function factorizes into a product over Matsubara frequencies; the low-temperature free energy oscillates to arbitrarily low temperature and the entropy becomes negative, which the authors attribute to nonhermiticity rather than a failure of the replica trick.

## Method

The quenched free energy was calculated in two independent ways: first, from the single-particle eigenvalues of the free-fermion Hamiltonian using the averaged spectral density of the antisymmetric coupling matrix (constant inside an ellipse in the complex plane); second, by solving the Schwinger-Dyson equations in the ΣG formulation of the two-site model with one replica and one conjugate replica. The free-fermion partition function was also rephrased as a matrix model for the coupling matrix, which up to minor details matches the random matrix model describing the chiral phase transition of QCD.

## Key numbers

- The averaged spectral density of the coupling matrix is constant inside an ellipse with semi-axes ε₀ = v/√(1+k²) and υ₀ = k²v/√(1+k²), where v sets the physical scale and k controls nonhermiticity.
- Critical temperatures occur at T_{c,m} = (2υ₀/π)/(2m+1) for non-negative integers m; for k=1, υ₀ = v/√2.
- For k=1, the free energy per particle is given by −F_L/(N/4)T = log 2 + Σ_{0<ω_n<2υ₀} [−1 + ω_n²/(4υ₀²) − log(ω_n²/(4υ₀²))], where ω_n = 2π(n+1/2)/β are Matsubara frequencies.
- For 0<k<1, the free energy expression splits into sums over ω_n ≥ 2υ₀ and 0<ω_n<2υ₀, with the first sum matching the Hermitian SYK result with ε₀ replaced by √(ε₀²−υ₀²).
- Numerical diagonalizations used N/2=128 and N/2=256 Majorana fermions with 1000 configurations.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the nonhermitian two-site SYK model from the chaotic four-body (q=4) case studied by Garcia-Garcia et al. (2021, 2022) to the integrable two-body (q=2) case. The q=4 model shows a first-order phase transition with a nearly temperature-independent low-temperature free energy due to universal two-point eigenvalue correlations in the Ginibre universality class. The q=2 model, being a Fermi liquid with Poisson statistics and a vanishing Lyapunov exponent (Garcia-Garcia et al., 2018), instead exhibits an infinite sequence of second-order transitions and an oscillating low-temperature free energy, demonstrating that the flat low-temperature free energy of the q=4 model is a consequence of quantum chaos. The matrix model formulation connects to the random matrix description of the QCD chiral phase transition.
