# In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group for Nuclei

> We present a new ab-initio method that uses similarity renormalization group (SRG) techniques to continuously diagonalize nuclear many-body Hamiltonians. In contrast with applications of the SRG to two- and three-nucleon interactions in free space, we perform the SRG evolution "in medium" directly in the $A$-body system of interest. The in-medium approach has the advantage that one can approximately evolve $3,...,A$-body operators using only two-body machinery based on normal-ordering techniques. The method is nonperturbative and can be tailored to problems ranging from the diagonalization of closed-shell nuclei to the construction of effective valence shell-model Hamiltonians and operators. We present first results for the ground-state energies of $^4$He, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca, which have accuracies comparable to coupled-cluster calculations.

## Key claim
The in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) can approximately evolve three- through A-body operators using only two-body machinery by normal-ordering with respect to a finite-density reference state, enabling ab-initio ground-state calculations of closed-shell nuclei with accuracies comparable to coupled-cluster calculations, provided that certain higher-order terms in the flow equations are omitted to preserve partial cancellations against neglected three-body normal-ordered contributions.

## Method
The authors perform a continuous unitary transformation of the nuclear Hamiltonian using the similarity renormalization group directly in the A-body system (in-medium), rather than in free space. They normal-order all operators with respect to a finite-density Hartree-Fock reference state, which allows the dominant induced three- and higher-body interactions to be absorbed into the normal-ordered zero-, one-, and two-body terms. The flow equations are then truncated to normal-ordered two-body operators [IM-SRG(2)] and integrated using either the Wegner generator or White's generator, with the underlined terms in the flow equations omitted for consistency with the truncation.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of $^4$He: IM-SRG(2) results using the Wegner and White generators agree within 20 keV, and the $e_{\text{max}}=8$ energy is within 20 keV of the exact no-core shell model diagonalization.
- Ground-state energy of $^4$He starting from a "bare" N$^3$LO potential: converged IM-SRG(2) result agrees well with the CCSD coupled-cluster result.
- Ground-state energies of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca: IM-SRG(2) calculations are well converged and closely track CCSD energies, using a smooth-cutoff $V_{\text{low}\,k}$ with $\Lambda = 1.8$ fm$^{-1}$ for $^{16}$O and a free-space SRG potential with $\lambda = 1.8$ fm$^{-1}$ for $^{40}$Ca.
- The IM-SRG(2) equations scale as $\mathcal{O}(N^6)$ with the number of single-particle orbitals.

## Context
This work extends the similarity renormalization group approach, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions in free space (Glazek and Wilson 1993; Wegner 1994), to an in-medium framework that avoids the technical challenge of explicitly evolving three-body operators in free space (Jurgenson et al. 2009). The method is nonperturbative and size-extensive, with the IM-SRG(2) energy being third-order exact in perturbation theory, analogous to the CCSD approximation in coupled-cluster theory (Hagen et al. 2007). The approach enables future calculations including three-nucleon forces and the construction of effective valence shell-model Hamiltonians for open-shell systems.
