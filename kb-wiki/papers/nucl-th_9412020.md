# Fragment Flow and the Nuclear Equation of State

> We use the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model with a momentum-dependent nuclear mean field to simulate the dynamical evolution of heavy ion collisions. We re-examine the azimuthal anisotropy observable, proposed as sensitive to the equation of state of nuclear matter. We obtain that this sensitivity is maximal when the azimuthal anisotropy is calculated for nuclear composite fragments, in agreement with some previous calculations. As a test case we concentrate on semi-central $^{197}{\rm Au}\ +\ ^{197}{\rm Au}$ collisions at 400 $A$ MeV.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of the azimuthal anisotropy ratio to the nuclear equation of state (compressibility coefficient K) is maximal when the observable is calculated for nuclear composite fragments rather than for free nucleons; free nucleons alone are essentially insensitive to variations in K, contrary to earlier claims.

## Method

The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent nuclear mean field (MDYI parameterization) to simulate semi-central 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 A MeV. They applied a coordinate-space coalescence prescription to distinguish free nucleons from composite fragments, with a critical distance parameter d_c = 3.2 fm and a coalescence time t_c chosen when transverse momentum generation saturates. The maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio r(θ) and the global anisotropy ratio R were calculated for free nucleons, all BUU test particles, and clusters separately, using five different values of the nuclear compressibility coefficient K (100, 150, 215, 250, and 380 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Impact parameter range integrated: 0 ≤ b (fm) ≤ 6.2 for semi-central 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 A MeV.
- Coalescence critical distance parameter: d_c = 3.2 fm.
- Compressibility coefficients used: K = 100, 150, 215, 250, and 380 MeV.
- Rapidity window for analysis: 0.7 ≤ (y/y_beam)_c.m. ≤ 1.2 (and also 0.4 ≤ (y/y_proj)_cm ≤ 0.8 for correlation function analysis).
- Spectator cut: momentum of BUU test particles > 0.25 GeV/c in projectile rest frame and laboratory frame, removing particles with θ < 12° and θ > 30°.
- The change in results from imposing a kinematic Fermi-sphere check on identified clusters was less than 1%.

## Context

This work resolves an apparent discrepancy between earlier calculations by Madey et al. (1993) and Elaa et al. (1994) regarding the sensitivity of neutron azimuthal anisotropy to the nuclear equation of state. It extends the original proposal by Gale, Welke, and Das Gupta (1988) that the azimuthal anisotropy ratio is sensitive to K, by showing that this sensitivity resides in the composite fragment component rather than free nucleons. The results enable future experiments, particularly with the EOS Time Projection Chamber and GSI/SIS facilities, to extract the nuclear compressibility coefficient by measuring the azimuthal anisotropy of nuclear clusters, using either reaction-plane-based methods or azimuthal pair correlation functions that avoid reaction-plane determination ambiguities.
