# Investigation of spatial manifestation of $α$ clusters in $^{16}$O via $α$-transfer reactions

> Recently, we have determined surface distributions of $α$ clusters in the ground state of $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ from $α$-transfer cross sections, without investigating the properties of its excited states. In this paper we extend our comprehension of $α$-cluster structures in excited states of nuclei through reaction studies. In particular we focus on $^{16}\mathrm{O}$, for which attention has been paid to advances of structure theory and assignment regarding $4^+$-resonance states. We study the surface manifestation of the $α$-cluster states in both the ground and excited states of $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ from the analysis of the $α$-transfer reaction $^{12}\mathrm{C}(^6\mathrm{Li},d)^{16}\mathrm{O}$. The $α$-transfer reaction is described by the distorted-wave Born approximation. We test two microscopic wave functions as an input of reaction calculations. Then a phenomenological potential model is introduced to clarify the correspondence between cluster-wave functions and transfer-cross sections. Surface peaks of the $α$-wave function of $^{16}\mathrm{O}(0^+)$ are sensitively probed by transfer-cross sections at forward angles, while it remains unclear how we trace the surface behavior of $^{16}\mathrm{O}(4^+)$ from the cross sections. We are able to specify that the $α$-cluster structure in the $0_1^+$ and $0_2^+$ states prominently manifests itself at the radii $\sim 4$ and $\sim 4.5$~fm, respectively. It is remarkable that the $4_1^+$ state has the $^{12}\mathrm{C}+α$-cluster component with the surface peak at the radius $\sim 4$ or outer, whereas the $^{12}\mathrm{C}+α$-cluster component in the $4_2^+$ state is found not to be dominant. The $4_2^+$ state is difficult to be interpreted by a simple potential model assuming the $^{12}\mathrm{C}+α$ configuration only.

## Key claim

The surface-peak position of the alpha-cluster wave function in the ground and excited states of oxygen-16 can be extracted from the angular distribution of the alpha-transfer reaction carbon-12(lithium-6,deuteron)oxygen-16, but the method works clearly only for the 0+ states; for the 4+ states the spatial manifestation cannot be uniquely determined from the transfer cross sections alone.

## Method

The alpha-transfer reaction carbon-12(lithium-6,deuteron)oxygen-16 was described by the finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA). Two microscopic wave functions for the carbon-12-alpha relative motion were tested as inputs: one from the orthogonality condition model (OCM) and one from a five-body model (5BM). A phenomenological potential model (PM) based on a Woods-Saxon potential was introduced to clarify the correspondence between cluster-wave functions and transfer-cross sections. The distorted waves were calculated with optical potentials from the literature, and the lithium-6 wave function was computed using an alpha-deuteron model.

## Key numbers

* The alpha-cluster structure in the 0_1+ state of oxygen-16 manifests itself at a radius of approximately 4 fm, as determined from the transfer cross sections at forward angles.
* The alpha-cluster structure in the 0_2+ state of oxygen-16 manifests itself at a radius of approximately 4.5 fm.
* The 4_1+ state has a carbon-12-plus-alpha cluster component with a surface peak at a radius of approximately 4 fm or outer.
* The 5BM wave function for the 0_1+ state has a surface peak shifted outward by approximately 1.5 fm compared to the OCM wave function.
* The experimental data were analyzed at two incident energies: 42.1 MeV and 48.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determination of surface distributions of alpha clusters in the ground state of neon-20 (Fukui et al., 2016) to excited states of oxygen-16. It tests two microscopic models: the OCM (Suzuki, 1976) which assumes a carbon-12-plus-alpha configuration with core excitation, and the 5BM which describes alpha cluster dissociation but treats carbon-12 as a closed core. The results support the 5BM for the ground state but the OCM for the 0_2+ state, indicating the need for a calculation that simultaneously addresses alpha dissociation and core polarization of carbon-12. The analysis addresses the controversial assignment of the 4+ resonance states, which have been interpreted differently by AMD calculations (Kanada-En'yo, 2012) and the algebraic approach of Bijker and Iachello (2014).
