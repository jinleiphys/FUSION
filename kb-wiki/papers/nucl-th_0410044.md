# Particle Number Fluctuations in the Microcanonical Ensemble

> Particle number fluctuations are studied in the microcanonical ensemble. For the Boltzmann statistics we deduce exact analytical formulae for the microcanonical partition functions in the case of non-interacting massless neutral particles and charged particles with zero net charge. The particle number fluctuations are calculated and we find that in the microcanonical ensemble they are suppressed in comparison to the fluctuations in the canonical and grand canonical ensembles. This remains valid in the thermodynamic limit too, so that the well-known equivalence of all statistical ensembles refers to average quantities, but does not apply to fluctuations. In the thermodynamic limit we are able to calculate the particle number fluctuations in the system of massive bosons and fermions when the exact conservation laws of both the energy and charge are taken into account.

## Key claim

Particle number fluctuations in the microcanonical ensemble (MCE) are suppressed compared to those in the canonical ensemble (CE) and grand canonical ensemble (GCE) even in the thermodynamic limit, so that the well-known equivalence of statistical ensembles applies to average quantities but not to fluctuations. The suppression persists for both massless and massive particles, and for Boltzmann, Bose, and Fermi statistics.

## Method

The authors derived exact analytical expressions for the microcanonical partition functions of non-interacting massless neutral particles and charged particles with zero net charge using Boltzmann statistics, expressed in terms of generalized hypergeometric functions. For massive bosons and fermions in the thermodynamic limit, they employed the method of microscopic correlators, imposing exact conservation of energy (and charge) via a Gaussian distribution of occupation numbers with a delta-function constraint, which modifies the microscopic correlator from its GCE form.

## Key numbers

- For massless neutral particles with Boltzmann statistics in the thermodynamic limit, the MCE scaled variance is ω_{m.c.e.} = 1/4, compared to ω_{g.c.e.} = 1 in the GCE.
- For massless charged particles with zero net charge and Boltzmann statistics in the thermodynamic limit, the MCE scaled variance for positively (or negatively) charged particles is ω_{m.c.e.}^{±} = 1/8, compared to ω_{g.c.e.}^{±} = 1 and ω_{c.e.}^{±} = 1/2.
- The asymptotic expansion of the MCE scaled variance for neutral massless Boltzmann particles is ω_{m.c.e.} ≃ (1/4)(1 - 1/(8N̅) + ...), where N̅ is the GCE average particle number.
- The asymptotic expansion of the MCE scaled variance for charged massless Boltzmann particles is ω_{m.c.e.}^{±} ≃ (1/8)(1 - 49/(1152 N̅_{±}^2) + ...), where N̅_{±} is the GCE average number of positive (negative) particles.

## Context

This work extends the results of Ce-fluc (2004), which first demonstrated that particle number fluctuations differ between the CE and GCE in the thermodynamic limit, by providing analytical calculations for the MCE. The paper enables the use of fluctuation observables in event-by-event analysis of nucleus-nucleus collisions to distinguish between statistical ensembles, which is relevant for the statistical hadron gas model applied to A+A, pp, p-pbar, and e+e- collisions. The method of microscopic correlators from Steph (year not specified in text) is used to treat massive bosons and fermions with quantum statistics.
