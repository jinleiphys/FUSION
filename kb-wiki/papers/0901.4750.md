# Chiral three-nucleon interaction and the carbon-14 dating beta decay

> We present a shell model calculation for the beta decay of 14-C to the 14-N ground-state, treating the relevant nuclear states as two 0p-holes in an 16-O core. Employing the universal low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential V(low-k) only, one finds that the Gamow-Teller matrix element is too large to describe the known (very long) lifetime of 14-C. As a novel approach to the problem, we invoke the chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) at leading order and derive from it a density-dependent in-medium NN interaction. Including this effective in-medium NN interaction, the Gamow-Teller matrix element vanishes for a nuclear density close to that of saturated nuclear matter. The genuine short-range part of the three-nucleon interaction plays a particularly important role in this context, since the medium modifications to the pion propagator and pion-nucleon vertex (due to the long-range 3NF) tend to cancel out in the relevant observable. We discuss also uncertainties related to the off-shell extrapolation of the in-medium NN interaction. Using the off-shell behavior of V(low-k) as a guide, we find that these uncertainties are rather small.

## Key claim

The anomalously long beta-decay lifetime of carbon-14 can be explained by including a density-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force, which causes the Gamow-Teller matrix element to vanish at a nuclear density close to saturated nuclear matter; the short-range component of the three-nucleon force plays a particularly important role, while the long-range contributions tend to cancel.

## Method

The authors performed a shell-model calculation treating the nuclear states of carbon-14 and nitrogen-14 as two 0p-hole configurations in an oxygen-16 core, using the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential V(low-k) derived from the N3LO chiral potential. They derived six analytic contributions to a density-dependent in-medium NN interaction from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force at one-loop order, including two-pion exchange, one-pion exchange, and short-range contact terms, and implemented these as effective interactions in the shell model using a folded-diagram formalism up to second order.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow-Teller matrix element using only the bare V(low-k) potential is M_GT = -0.88, far too large to describe the known lifetime.
- The carbon-14 half-life is approximately 5730 years, requiring a Gamow-Teller matrix element on the order of 2 x 10^-3.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter is chosen as hbar-omega = 14 MeV, and the 0p orbital energy splitting is epsilon = 6.4 MeV.
- The density of saturated nuclear matter is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3.
- The low-energy constants of the chiral three-nucleon force at cutoff Lambda_low-k = 2.1 fm^-1 are c_D = -2.062 and c_E = -0.625; at 2.3 fm^-1 they are c_D = -2.785 and c_E = -0.822.
- The two-pion exchange parameters are c_1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c_3 = -3.2 GeV^-1, c_4 = 5.4 GeV^-1.
- The one-pion-exchange contact term reduces the bare 1pi-exchange by about 16% at normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological studies of the carbon-14 beta decay, including the finding by Jancovici and Talmi that a tensor force is needed, and the no-core shell model calculation by Aroua et al. using the Argonne V8' interaction which showed suppression but not convergence. It also builds on the suggestion by Holt et al. that the transition is sensitive to density-dependent interactions modeled with Brown-Rho scaling. The present approach provides a systematic, chiral effective field theory foundation for the density-dependent corrections, and the resulting framework can be applied to other nuclear structure calculations near doubly-magic nuclei.
