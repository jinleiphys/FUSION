# Equilibrium and non-equilibrium effects in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The hypothesis of local equilibrium (LE) in relativistic heavy ion collisions at energies from AGS to RHIC is checked in the microscopic transport model. We find that kinetic, thermal, and chemical equilibration of the expanding hadronic matter is nearly reached in central collisions at AGS energy for $t \geq 10$ fm/$c$ in a central cell. At these times the equation of state may be approximated by a simple dependence $P \cong (0.12-0.15) ε$. Increasing deviations of the yields and the energy spectra of hadrons from statistical model values are observed for increasing bombarding energies. The origin of these deviations is traced to the irreversible multiparticle decays of strings and many-body $(N \geq 3)$ decays of resonances. The violations of LE indicate that the matter in the cell reaches a steady state instead of idealized equilibrium. The entropy density in the cell is only about 6% smaller than that of the equilibrium state.

## Key claim

Local equilibrium (kinetic, thermal, and chemical) is nearly reached in central collisions at AGS energy for times \(t \geq 10\) fm/\(c\) in a central cell, but at higher bombarding energies (SPS, RHIC) the deviations from equilibrium increase; the matter in the cell reaches a steady state rather than idealized equilibrium, with the entropy density only about 6% smaller than that of the equilibrium state.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) microscopic transport model to simulate central heavy ion collisions at energies from AGS to RHIC. They selected a cubic cell of volume \(V = 125\) fm\(^3\) centered at the origin of the center-of-mass system and compared the time evolution of hadron yields, energy spectra, pressure components, and entropy density in the cell to predictions of a statistical thermal model (SM) of an equilibrated ideal hadron gas with the same energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic equilibration (isotropy of pressure tensor components) in the central cell occurs at \(t \cong 10\) fm/\(c\) for AGS, 8 fm/\(c\) for SPS, and 4 fm/\(c\) for RHIC.
- At AGS energy, the equation of state in the cell may be approximated by \(P \cong (0.12-0.15) \varepsilon\) for \(t \geq 10\) fm/\(c\).
- At AGS energy, the difference between UrQMD and SM results for baryons lies within the 10% range of accuracy.
- At 160 AGeV (SPS), the nucleon "temperature" from a Boltzmann fit is about 30 MeV below the SM temperature, and the pion "temperature" is about 50 MeV below the SM temperature.
- The total entropy density in the cell is only about 6% smaller than the SM total entropy density.
- The limiting temperature of hadrons in the steady state is \(T_{lim} \leq 145\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of local equilibrium in heavy ion collisions by using the UrQMD model to systematically check kinetic, thermal, and chemical equilibration from AGS to RHIC energies. The finding that the system reaches a steady state rather than full equilibrium, due to irreversible multiparticle decays of strings and many-body (\(N \geq 3\)) decays of resonances, contradicts the assumption of detailed balance used in equilibrium statistical models. The results support the applicability of relativistic hydrodynamics (since the entropy per baryon ratio remains constant during expansion) but indicate that non-equilibrium thermodynamics of irreversible processes, not equilibrium thermodynamics, should be applied to study high-energy heavy ion collisions. The work builds on prior box calculations by Belkacem (1998) and earlier statistical model studies by the authors (1998, 1999).
