# Theory of neutrino emission from nucleon-hyperon matter in neutron stars: Angular integrals

> Investigations of thermal evolution of neutron stars with hyperon cores require neutrino emissivities for many neutrino reactions involving strongly degenerate particles (nucleons, hyperons, electrons, muons). We calculate the angular integrals $I_n$ (over orientations of momenta of $n$ degenerate particles) for major neutrino reactions with $n$ =3, 4, 5 at all possible combinations of particle Fermi momenta. The integrals $I_n$ are necessary ingredients for constructing a uniform database of neutrino emissivities in dense nucleon-hyperon matter. The results can also be used in many problems of physical kinetics of strongly degenerate systems.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete analytic classification of the angular integrals \(I_\Omega^{(n)}\) for \(n=3,4,5\) degenerate fermions, which are necessary ingredients for constructing a uniform database of neutrino emissivities in dense nucleon-hyperon matter; the integrals depend only on the Fermi momenta of the reacting particles and are independent of specific interparticle interactions, but the results are derived under the assumption that the system is non-superfluid and that the squared matrix elements can be approximated as angle-independent.

## Method

The authors decompose the neutrino emissivity integral into an energy integral \(I_\epsilon\) and an angular integral \(I_\Omega\) over orientations of particle momenta placed on their Fermi surfaces, neglecting neutrino momenta in the momentum-conserving delta function. They then compute \(I_\Omega^{(n)}\) for \(n=3,4,5\) by using a recursive technique that splits the system into two subsystems connected by a momentum transfer vector, applying the known result for \(n=3\) (the triangle condition) as the basic building block. For \(n=4\) and \(n=5\), the integrals reduce to expressions involving the difference of maximum and minimum momentum transfers (\(Q^{(4)}\)) or the area in the \((q,k)\)-plane (\(Q^{(5)}\)), which are evaluated analytically for all possible orderings of the Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

* For \(n=3\) fermions satisfying the triangle condition \(p_1 < p_2 + p_3\), the angular integral is \(I_\Omega^{(3)} = 2(2\pi)^2 / (p_1 p_2 p_3)\).
* For \(n=4\) fermions, the general expression is \(I_\Omega^{(4)} = 2(2\pi)^3 / (p_1 p_2 p_3 p_4) \times Q^{(4)}\), where \(Q^{(4)} = q_\mathrm{max} - q_\mathrm{min}\) with \(q_\mathrm{max} = p_3 + p_4\) and \(q_\mathrm{min} = \max(p_1 - p_2,\; p_3 - p_4)\).
* For four equal Fermi momenta \(p_1 = p_2 = p_3 = p_4\), the result simplifies to \(Q^{(4)} = 2 p_1\).
* For \(n=5\) fermions, the general expression is \(I_\Omega^{(5)} = 2(2\pi)^4 / (p_1 p_2 p_3 p_4 p_5) \times Q^{(5)}\), where \(Q^{(5)}\) is the area in the \((q,k)\)-plane; for five equal momenta, \(Q^{(5)} = (5/2) p_1^2\).
* For the neutron-branch modified Urca process in nucleon matter when the direct Urca process is forbidden (\(p_{\rm n} > p_{\rm p} + p_\ell\)), the angular integral is \(I_\Omega^{(5)} = 8(2\pi)^4 / p_{\rm n}^3\).
* For the proton-branch modified Urca process in case 1 (\(p_{\rm n} > 3p_{\rm p} - p_\ell\)), the angular integral is \(I_\Omega^{(5)} = (2\pi)^4 / (p_{\rm n} p_{\rm p}^3 p_\ell) \times (3p_{\rm p} + p_\ell - p_{\rm n})^2\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of angular integrals for degenerate Fermi systems, previously applied to nucleon matter in neutron stars (Yakovlev et al. 2001; Shapiro and Teukolsky 1983), to the full set of reactions involving hyperons (\(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\)) that appear in dense neutron star cores. The results enable the construction of a uniform database of neutrino emissivities for all major neutrino reactions (direct Urca, baryon-baryon bremsstrahlung, and modified Urca processes) in nucleon-hyperon matter, which is needed for modeling the thermal evolution of neutron stars with hyperon cores; the specific emissivities for direct Urca processes in hyperon matter were previously calculated by Lattimer et al. (1991) and Prakash et al. (1992), while the bremsstrahlung and modified Urca processes involving hyperons remain much less elaborated.
