# Predictions for p+Pb at 5.02A TeV to test initial state nuclear shadowing at the Large Hadron Collider

> Collinear factorized perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) model predictions are compared for $p+{\rm Pb}$ at 5.02$A$ TeV to test nuclear shadowing of parton distribution at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The pseudorapidity distribution the nuclear modification factor (NMF), $R_{p{\rm Pb}}(y=0,p_T<20\;{\rm GeV}/{\it c}) = dn_{p{\rm Pb}} /(N_{\rm coll}(b)dn_{pp})$ and the pseudorapidity asymmetry $Y_{asym}^{h}(p_T)=R^h_{pPb}(p_T, η<0)/R^h_{pPb}(p_T,η>0)$ are computed using {\small HIJING/BB v2.0 model} and a pQCD improved parton model kTpQCD\_v2.0 which embedded generalized parton distribution functions (PDFs). These results are updated calculations of those presented in Phys. Rev. C {\bf 85}, 024903 (2012).

# Predictions for p+Pb at 5.02A TeV to test initial state nuclear shadowing at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim

The paper predicts that with only 10^6 events of p+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV, measurements of the nuclear modification factor R_pPb(p_T) and the pseudorapidity asymmetry Y_asym^h(p_T) can distinguish between different nuclear shadowing parametrizations, but these predictions are model-dependent and rely on specific choices of minijet cutoff parameters and string tension values that are themselves constrained by fits to p+p and Pb+Pb data from RHIC to LHC.

## Method

The authors use two complementary Monte Carlo models: HIJING/BB v2.0, which implements collinear factorized pQCD minijet production with string fragmentation and strong color field (SCF) effects, and kTpQCD_v2.0, a pQCD-improved parton model with intrinsic-k_T broadening and generalized parton distribution functions. Both models incorporate nuclear shadowing via scale-independent parametrizations of the shadowing function S_a/A(x), with the HIJING/BB v2.0 model using a fixed Q^2 shadowing function from HIJING1.0 and the kTpQCD_v2.0 model testing multiple shadowing parametrizations including EKS99, EPS08, HKN, and HIJING parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: 5.02A TeV for p+Pb at the LHC
- Transverse momentum range studied: p_T < 20 GeV/c
- For HIJING/BB v2.0 at 5.02A TeV: minijet cutoff p_0 = 3.1 GeV/c and string tension kappa = 2.1 GeV/fm for p+Pb; for p+p at 5.02 TeV: p_0 = 2 GeV/c and kappa = 1.9 GeV/fm
- Number of binary collisions for minimum bias p+Pb: N_coll(MB) = 6.4; for central 0-20% collisions: N_coll approximately 12
- Nuclear modification factor R_pPb at mid-pseudorapidity with shadowing and SCF effects: approximately 0.7 in the 5 to 10 GeV/c region
- In kTpQCD_v2.0: intrinsic k_T width <k_T^2> = 2.5 GeV^2/c^2 for pp collisions; nuclear broadening parameter C = 0.4 GeV^2/c^2 with maximum number of semihard collisions nu_m between 3 and 4
- Required event sample: 10^6 events for definitive constraints

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions from Barnafoldi et al. (2012) in Phys. Rev. C 85, 024903, providing updated calculations for p+Pb at LHC energies. The paper contrasts its approach with the HIJING2.0 model of Deng et al. (2010), which uses stronger gluon shadowing (s_g = 0.22-0.23) and neglects jet quenching, and with Color Glass Condensate models such as KKT04 by Kharzeev et al. (2003). The predictions are designed to be testable with a short LHC run and would enable direct constraints on nuclear shadowing that are currently only indirectly inferred from Pb+Pb centrality dependence, with implications for interpreting both bulk and hard probe measurements in nucleus-nucleus collisions.
