# Heavy quark transport at RHIC and LHC

> We calculate the heavy quark evolution in heavy ion collisions and show results for the elliptic flow $v_2$ as well as the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ at RHIC and LHC energies. For the calculation we implement a Langevin approach for the transport of heavy quarks in the UrQMD (hydrodynamics + Boltzmann) hybrid model. As drag and diffusion coefficients we use a Resonance approach for elastic heavy-quark scattering and assume a decoupling temperature of the charm quarks from the hot medium of $130\,\text{MeV}$. At RHIC energies we use a coalescence approach at the decoupling temperature for the hadronization of the heavy quarks to D-mesons and B-mesons and a sub-following decay to heavy flavor electrons using PYTHIA. At LHC we use an additional fragmentation mechanism to account for the higher transverse momenta reached at higher collision energies.

# Heavy quark transport at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The authors show that a Langevin transport model for heavy quarks, using a Resonance approach for drag and diffusion coefficients with a decoupling temperature of 130 MeV, can simultaneously describe the elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor of heavy-flavor decay electrons at RHIC and D-mesons at LHC, but only when coalescence hadronization is used at RHIC while at LHC both coalescence and fragmentation mechanisms are needed to cover the full transverse momentum range.

## Method

The authors implemented a relativistic Langevin equation for charm and bottom quark propagation through the quark-gluon plasma, using the UrQMD hybrid model (combining ideal (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics with a Boltzmann transport cascade) to simulate the bulk medium evolution. Drag and diffusion coefficients were computed from a Resonance approach, assuming D-meson and B-meson states exist in the QGP phase, and from a T-Matrix approach using quark-antiquark potentials. At a decoupling temperature of 130 MeV, heavy quarks were hadronized via a coalescence mechanism at RHIC energies and via both coalescence and Peterson fragmentation at LHC energies, with subsequent decays to heavy-flavor electrons handled by PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- Decoupling temperature for charm quarks from the hot medium: 130 MeV
- RHIC collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, centrality 20%-40%, rapidity cut |y| < 0.35
- LHC collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, centrality 30%-50% for v2 and 0%-20% for RAA, rapidity cut |y| < 0.35
- At LHC, the coalescence approach yields a maximum of RAA at about 2 GeV followed by a decline to an RAA of about 0.2 at high pT
- Peterson fragmentation parameter epsilon_Q = 0.05

## Context

This work extends earlier heavy-quark transport studies using a thermal fireball model with combined coalescence-fragmentation (vanHees 2007, Greco 2007, Rapp 2009), ideal hydrodynamics with lattice-QCD equation of state (He 2012), the BAMPS model (Uphoff 2011), and the MARTINI model (Young 2011). By embedding the Langevin approach into the UrQMD hybrid model, which has been validated from AGS to LHC energies (Petersen 2008, Steinheimer 2009), the authors enable more realistic comparisons to PHENIX and ALICE data. The results highlight the need for new low-pT measurements at LHC to distinguish between coalescence and fragmentation hadronization mechanisms.
