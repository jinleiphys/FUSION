# Investigation of Gamow Teller Transition Properties in 56-64Ni Isotopes Using QRPA Methods

> Weak rates in nickel isotopes play an integral role in the dynamics of supernovae. Electron capture and $β$-decay of nickel isotopes, dictated by Gamow-Teller transitions, significantly alter the lepton fraction of the stellar matter. In this paper we calculate Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions for isotopes of nickel, $^{56-64}$Ni, using QRPA methods. The GT strength distributions were calculated using four different QRPA models. Our results are also compared with previous theoretical calculations and measured strength distributions wherever available. Our investigation concluded that amongst all RPA models, the pn-QRPA(C) model best described the measured GT distributions (including total GT strength and centroid placement). It is hoped that the current investigation of GT properties would prove handy and may lead to a better understanding of the presupernova evolution of massive stars.

# Investigation of Gamow-Teller Transition Properties in 56-64Ni Isotopes Using QRPA Methods

## Key claim
Among four QRPA models tested, the pn-QRPA(C) model (performed in a deformed basis with separable GT forces in both particle-particle and particle-hole channels) best reproduces measured GT strength distributions, total GT strength, and centroid placement for nickel isotopes 56-64, though the abstract's claim of "best description" is qualified by noting that the Large Scale Shell Model (LSSM) with KB3G interaction also shows very good agreement with experimental data, and the pn-QRPA(C) model overestimates total GT strength for 56Ni (5.34 units calculated versus 3.8 units measured).

## Method
The authors calculated Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for 56-64Ni isotopes using four QRPA formalisms: Pyatov Method (PM(B)), Schematic Model (SM(B) and SM(C)), and proton-neutron QRPA (pn-QRPA(C)). PM(B) and SM(B) used spherical basis with mean-field Hamiltonians defined only in particle space, while SM(C) and pn-QRPA(C) used deformed basis in quasiparticle space. The calculated GT strength distributions, total strengths, centroids, and widths were compared with experimental data from charge-exchange reactions ((p,n), (n,p), (3He,t), (d,2He), (t,3He)) and with previous theoretical calculations including shell model (KB3G, GXPF1, GXPF1J, FPD6 interactions), RPA, HF+BCS, and LSSM approaches.

## Key numbers
- Total B(GT)- strength for 56Ni: pn-QRPA(C) calculates 5.34 units, compared to measured (p,n) data of 3.8 units (Sas12)
- Total B(GT)- strength for 58Ni: pn-QRPA(C) calculates 6.89 units, compared to measured value of 7.4 units (Rap83)
- Total B(GT)- strength for 60Ni: pn-QRPA(C) calculates 7.38 units, compared to measured value of 7.2 units (Rap83)
- Total B(GT)+ strength for 58Ni: pn-QRPA(C) calculates 4.73 units, compared to measured (t,3He) data of 4.1 units (Col06)
- Total B(GT)+ strength for 60Ni: pn-QRPA(C) calculates 3.06 units, compared to measured (n,p) data of 3.11 units (Will95)
- Total B(GT)+ strength for 62Ni: pn-QRPA(C) calculates 2.43 units, compared to measured (n,p) data of 2.5 units (Will95)
- Total B(GT)+ strength for 64Ni: pn-QRPA(C) calculates 1.86 units, compared to measured (n,p) data of 1.72 units (Will95)
- GT centroid for 56Ni (beta- direction): pn-QRPA(C) centroid at 2.26 MeV, SM(B) at 3.86 MeV, PM(B) at 5.65 MeV, SM(C) at 3.66 MeV, compared to experimental centroid of 4.1 MeV (Sas12)
- A quenching factor of f_q^2 = (0.6)^2 was applied in all calculations
- Ikeda sum rule: B(GT)- - B(GT)+ approximately equals 3f_q^2(N-Z)

## Context
This work extends previous QRPA studies by Cakmak et al. (2014) that classified eight sub-models and identified four better models for GT strength description. It builds on experimental measurements of GT strength distributions via charge-exchange reactions including Sasano et al. (2011) for 56Ni(p,n), Fujita et al. (2002, 2007) for 58Ni(3He,t), and Williams et al. (1995) for (n,p) reactions on 60,62,64Ni. The pn-QRPA(C) model's ability to handle arbitrarily heavy nuclei makes it advantageous over LSSM for calculating GT strength distributions for hundreds of nuclei needed in presupernova evolution modeling, where weak rates of nickel isotopes significantly affect the lepton fraction and electron-to-baryon ratio in stellar matter as shown by Heger et al. (2001) and Aufderheide et al. (1994).
