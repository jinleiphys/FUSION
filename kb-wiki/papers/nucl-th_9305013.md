# Proof of stability of the hydrogen molecule

> We sketch two rigorous proofs of the stability of the hydrogen molecule in quantum mechanics. The first one is based on an extrapolation of variational estimates of the groundstate energy of a positronium molecule to arbitrary mass ratios. The second one is an extension of Heitler-London theory to nuclei of finite mass.

# Proof of stability of the hydrogen molecule

## Key claim
The hydrogen molecule (pp e⁻ e⁻) is proven to be stable in non-relativistic quantum mechanics for the physical mass ratio m/M ≈ 1/1836, using two complementary rigorous proofs that cover the range m/M ≤ 0.6 and m/M ≤ 0.144 respectively, rather than relying on the Born-Oppenheimer limit alone.

## Method
The first proof extrapolates variational upper bounds on the groundstate energy of the positronium molecule (e⁺ e⁺ e⁻ e⁻) to arbitrary mass ratios by decomposing the Hamiltonian into a charge-conjugation even part H_S and an odd part H_A, then showing that the expectation value of H_A vanishes for symmetric trial wavefunctions. The second proof extends Heitler-London theory to finite nuclear mass by constructing a variational wavefunction with a radial factor |Y| e^{-β|Y|} multiplying the Heitler-London orbital singlet function, then evaluating the expectation value of the full four-body Hamiltonian in nuclear center-of-mass coordinates.

## Key numbers
- The variational upper bound for the positronium molecule groundstate energy is E₀(e⁺ e⁺ e⁻ e⁻) ≤ -2.0168 E₀(e⁺ e⁻), with the minimum reached near β² = 0.48.
- The first proof establishes binding for mass ratios m/M ≤ 0.6, which includes the physical hydrogen molecule.
- The second proof yields a variational upper bound ⟨ψ, H^{(4)} ψ⟩ < -2.082 for the hydrogen molecule with physical masses.
- For the H⁻ ion (p e⁻ e⁻), a lower bound E₀(H^{(3)}) > -1.2507 is obtained for the physical mass ratio.
- For the H₂⁺ ion (p p e⁻), a lower bound E₀(p p e⁻) > -1.623 is obtained for the physical mass ratio.
- The continuous spectrum threshold for the four-body system is E_c = -2 (twice the hydrogen atom groundstate energy) for m/M ≤ 0.6.

## Context
This work fills a gap in the literature by providing the first rigorous proofs that the hydrogen molecule is stable within non-relativistic quantum mechanics, a fact previously taken for granted without mathematical proof. The first proof builds on variational calculations by Hylleraas and Ore (1947) for the positronium molecule, while the second proof extends the Heitler-London theory (1927) beyond the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to finite nuclear masses. The methods can be extended to predict stability of exotic mesons (QQ q̄ q̄) in quark models with flavor-independent potentials, as noted by Badhuri et al. (1980).
