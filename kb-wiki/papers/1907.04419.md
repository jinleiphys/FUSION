# Toward an effective theory of quarkonium production in nuclear matter

> These proceedings are dedicated to Miklos Gyulassy's 70$^{\rm th}$ birthday. In his long and distinguished career he has made seminal contributions to many areas of heavy ion theory, including early papers on quarkonium phenomenology in fixed-target heavy ion experiments. Theoretical and experimental studies of the $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ states have evolved considerably in the past decades, and I describe a recent generalization of non-relativistic Quantum Chromodynamics to include interactions in a generic nuclear medium. NRQCD with Glauber gluons aims to provide a universal microscopic description of the interaction of heavy quarkonia in a range of phases that include cold nuclear matter, dense hadron gas, and quark-gluon plasma. Such effective field theory is an important step toward understanding the common trends in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus data on quarkonium suppression.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a simple energy-loss model for quarkonium suppression in nuclear matter fails to describe experimental data, and that a more rigorous effective field theory, NRQCD with Glauber gluons (NRQCD$_{\rm G}$), is needed to provide a universal microscopic description of heavy quarkonium interactions across cold nuclear matter, hadron gas, and quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author generalizes non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) to include interactions with a nuclear medium by adding terms to the vNRQCD Lagrangian that describe exchanges of Glauber and Coulomb gluons between heavy quarks and medium quasiparticles. The derivation is cross-checked using three independent approaches: a background field method, a matching procedure from full QCD, and a hybrid method extracting Feynman rules from single-gluon insertion diagrams. The resulting Lagrangian is given to leading and subleading power for interactions with collinear, static, and soft sources.

## Key numbers

- The branching fractions for feed-down to $J/\psi$ are: $\psi(2S) \to J/\psi + X$ at $61.4 \pm 0.6\%$, $\chi_{c1} \to J/\psi + \gamma$ at $34.3 \pm 1.0\%$, and $\chi_{c2} \to J/\psi + \gamma$ at $19.0 \pm 0.5\%$.
- In the energy loss model comparison to ATLAS data from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions, the discrepancy between theory and data at high $p_T$ is as large as a factor of 3.
- The experimental suppression of $\psi(2S)$ relative to $J/\psi$ is 2 to 3 times larger for the excited state, while the energy loss model predicts slightly less suppression for $\psi(2S)$ than for $J/\psi$.
- The leading-power NRQCD factorization is expected to hold for transverse momenta $p_T \sim 10$ GeV for charmonia.

## Context

This work extends the earlier development of soft collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons (SCET$_{\rm G}$) by Ovanesyan (2011) and Kang (2016), which was applied to light and open heavy flavor final states, to the case of heavy quarkonium. It directly contradicts the energy-loss approach to quarkonium suppression proposed by Spousta (2016) and Arleo (2017), showing that such models cannot describe the hierarchy of excited-to-ground state suppression observed in LHC data. The new NRQCD$_{\rm G}$ framework enables rigorous calculations of medium-induced transitions between quarkonium states, extending earlier phenomenological work by Sharma (2012) and Aronson (2017) that used only the leading medium correction with diagonal state-to-state transitions.
