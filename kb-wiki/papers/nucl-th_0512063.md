# Nucleon Polarizabilities from Deuteron Compton Scattering within a Green's-Function Hybrid Approach

> We examine elastic Compton scattering from the deuteron for photon energies ranging from zero to 100 MeV, using state-of-the-art deuteron wave functions and NN-potentials. Nucleon-nucleon rescattering between emission and absorption of the two photons is treated by Green's functions in order to ensure gauge invariance and the correct Thomson limit. With this Green's-function hybrid approach, we fulfill the low-energy theorem of deuteron Compton scattering and there is no significant dependence on the deuteron wave function used. Concerning the nucleon structure, we use Chiral Effective Field Theory with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom within the Small Scale Expansion up to leading-one-loop order. Agreement with available data is good at all energies. Our 2-parameter fit to all elastic $γd$ data leads to values for the static isoscalar dipole polarizabilities which are in excellent agreement with the isoscalar Baldin sum rule. Taking this value as additional input, we find α_E^s= (11.3+-0.7(stat)+-0.6(Baldin)) x 10^{-4} fm^3 and β_M^s = (3.2-+0.7(stat)+-0.6(Baldin)) x 10^{-4} fm^3 and conclude by comparison to the proton numbers that neutron and proton polarizabilities are essentially the same.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the static isoscalar electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of the nucleon from elastic deuteron Compton scattering data using a Green's-function hybrid approach that includes full two-nucleon rescattering, thereby achieving the correct Thomson limit and good agreement with data from zero to 100 MeV, unlike previous perturbative effective-field-theory calculations that broke down below 50-60 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron for photon energies from zero to 100 MeV by combining state-of-the-art deuteron wave functions (AV18 and NNLO chiral potentials) with a Green's-function treatment of nucleon-nucleon rescattering between the two photon interactions, ensuring gauge invariance and the correct Thomson limit. The single-nucleon structure is described using Chiral Effective Field Theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom within the Small Scale Expansion up to leading-one-loop order (O(epsilon^3)), and the photon is also coupled to one-pion exchange currents via Siegert's theorem. A two-parameter fit of the isoscalar short-distance operators to all existing elastic gamma-d data yields the static polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar electric dipole polarizability: alpha_E^s = (11.3 +/- 0.7 (stat) +/- 0.6 (Baldin) +/- 1 (theory)) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Isoscalar magnetic dipole polarizability: beta_M^s = (3.2 -/+ 0.7 (stat) +/- 0.6 (Baldin) +/- 1 (theory)) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- The Baldin sum rule value used as constraint: alpha_E^p + beta_M^p = (13.8 +/- 0.4) x 10^{-4} fm^3 (from Olmos)
- Proton polarizabilities from the authors' prior fit (HGHP): alpha_E^p = (11.04 +/- 1.36 (stat) +/- 0.4 (Baldin)) x 10^{-4} fm^3, beta_M^p = (2.76 -/+ 1.36 (stat) +/- 0.4 (Baldin)) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Previous isoscalar fit from deuteron paper (deuteronpaper): alpha_E^s = (12.6 +/- 0.8 (stat) +/- 0.7 (wf) +/- 0.6 (Baldin)) x 10^{-4} fm^3, beta_M^s = (1.9 -/+ 0.8 (stat) -/+ 0.7 (wf) +/- 0.6 (Baldin)) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Photon energy range studied: zero to 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid effective-field-theory approach of deuteron Compton scattering by including full two-nucleon rescattering via Green's functions, correcting the failure of earlier perturbative calculations (deuteronpaper, Phillips, McGPhil1, McGPhil2) to reach the correct Thomson limit below 50-60 MeV. It improves upon the calculations of Lvov and Karakowski and Miller by retaining the full energy dependence of single-nucleon Compton multipoles including the explicit Delta(1232) resonance. The extracted isoscalar polarizabilities agree with the isoscalar Baldin sum rule and indicate that neutron and proton polarizabilities are essentially the same, consistent with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions (BKKM). The approach enables reliable extraction of neutron polarizabilities from existing and future deuteron Compton data at all energies below 100 MeV, including forthcoming experiments at TUNL/HIgammas, MAXlab, and S-DALINAC.
