# Spin structure of the nucleon at low energies

> The spin structure of the nucleon is analyzed in the framework of a Lorentz-invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory. The structure functions of doubly virtual Compton scattering are calculated to one-loop accuracy (fourth order in the chiral expansion). We discuss the generalization of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule and moments of these. We give predictions for the forward and the longitudinal-transverse spin polarizabilities of the proton and the neutron at zero and finite photon virtuality. A detailed comparison to results obtained in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory is also given.

## Key claim
The paper provides parameter-free predictions for the spin structure functions of doubly virtual Compton scattering at one-loop accuracy (fourth order in the chiral expansion) using a Lorentz-invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the results are restricted to photon virtualities \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) due to convergence limits of the infrared regularization scheme.

## Method
The authors calculate the spin-dependent structure functions \(\bar{S}_1(\nu, Q^2)\) and \(\bar{S}_2(\nu, Q^2)\) of forward doubly virtual Compton scattering off nucleons to one-loop order using infrared-regularized baryon chiral perturbation theory. From these structure functions, they derive the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule, and their moments, as well as the forward and longitudinal-transverse spin polarizabilities at zero and finite photon virtuality. Resonance contributions from the \(\Delta(1232)\) and vector mesons are estimated separately and added to the chiral results.

## Key numbers
- The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule at \(Q^2=0\) gives \(-204 \,\mu\)b for the proton and \(-232 \,\mu\)b for the neutron (from the low-energy theorem, not a new calculation).
- The photon virtuality range for reliable predictions is bounded by \(Q^2_{\text{max}} = 0.3 \ldots 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), depending on the observable.
- The chiral prediction for the moment \(I_A^n(Q^2)\) supplemented with resonance contributions agrees with Jefferson Laboratory data for virtualities \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\).
- The Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule is fulfilled within the Lorentz-invariant approach; the fifth-order result for the neutron BC sum rule agrees with the product of form factors for \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.15\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends earlier analyses of nucleon spin structure using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT), such as those by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1990s) and by Ji and collaborators, by employing a Lorentz-invariant formulation that automatically satisfies analyticity constraints. The results enable comparison with ongoing experimental programs at Jefferson Laboratory, MAMI, ELSA, and GRAAL that measure spin-dependent photoabsorption cross sections and sum rules, and they provide a framework for testing the transition from non-perturbative to perturbative QCD in the spin sector.
