# Nucleon-Nucleon scattering from the dispersive N/D method: next-to-leading order study

> We consider nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions from Chiral Effective Field Theory applying the $N/D$ method. The dynamical input is given by the discontinuity of the $NN$ partial-wave amplitudes across the left-hand cut (LHC) calculated in Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) by including one-pion exchange (OPE), once-iterated OPE and leading irreducible two-pion exchange (TPE). We discuss both uncoupled and coupled partial-waves. We show algebraically that the resulting integral equation has a unique solution when the input is taken only from OPE because it is of the Fredholm type with a squared integrable kernel and an inhomogeneous term. Phase shifts and mixing angles are typically rather well reproduced, and a clear improvement of the results obtained previously with only OPE is manifest. We also show that the contributions to the discontinuity across the LHC are amenable to a chiral expansion. Our method also establishes correlations between the $S$-wave effective ranges and scattering lengths based on unitarity, analyticity and chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the dispersive N/D method applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering, using the discontinuity across the left-hand cut calculated from chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order (including one-pion exchange, once-iterated one-pion exchange, and leading irreducible two-pion exchange), yields phase shifts and mixing angles in good agreement with the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis, with the caveat that the method's integral equation has a unique solution only when the input is restricted to one-pion exchange (where the kernel is quadratically integrable), while higher-order contributions require regularization via a small parameter epsilon to ensure Fredholm-type solvability.

## Method

The authors apply the N/D method to separate each NN partial-wave amplitude into a numerator function N(A) with only a left-hand cut and a denominator function D(A) with only a right-hand cut. The dynamical input is the discontinuity Delta(A) across the left-hand cut, calculated in chiral perturbation theory from one-pion exchange (OPE, O(p^0)), once-iterated OPE, and leading irreducible two-pion exchange (TPE, both O(p^2)). The resulting coupled integral equations for D(A) and N(A) are solved numerically by discretization, with subtraction constants fitted to reproduce phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis, while higher partial waves (ell >= 2) are treated using the principle of maximal smoothness to fix all but one subtraction constant.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts and mixing angles are reproduced up to laboratory kinetic energy E_lab ~ 200 MeV, with clear improvement over previous OPE-only results from Guo et al. (2013) and Oller et al. (2013).
- The left-hand cut for one-pion exchange extends for A <= -M_pi^2/4, where M_pi is the pion mass; two-pion exchange contributes for A <= -M_pi^2.
- The once-iterated OPE diagram contributes to Delta(A) only for A <= -n^2 M_pi^2/4, where n is the number of pion ladders (n=2 for twice-iterated OPE).
- For the once-subtracted integral equation with Delta(A) ~ lambda (-A)^gamma, a unique solution exists for gamma < -1/2; OPE gives gamma <= -1, satisfying this condition.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive N/D method previously applied to NN scattering with only OPE input by Guo et al. (2013) and Oller et al. (2013) to include next-to-leading order chiral contributions (irreducible and reducible two-pion exchange). It contrasts with the standard Lippmann-Schwinger approach to chiral NN potentials (Weinberg 1990, Ordonez et al. 1994, Entem and Machleidt 2003), which suffers from regulator dependence and requires promoting counterterms to lower orders for attractive singular tensor forces (Nogga et al. 2005, Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola 2006). The method avoids regulator dependence by using convergent dispersive integrals and establishes a systematic chiral expansion for the left-hand cut discontinuity, enabling future inclusion of higher-order contributions and correlations between S-wave effective ranges and scattering lengths based on unitarity, analyticity, and chiral symmetry.
