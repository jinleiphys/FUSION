# Probing the Non-exponential Decay Regime in Open Quantum Systems

> The most important law of radioactivity is that of the exponential decay. In the realm of quantum mechanics, however, this decay law is neither rigorous nor fundamental. The deviations from the exponential decay have been observed experimentally at the early stage of a decay process, but there is little evidence for non-exponential behavior at long times. Yet such long-term non-exponentiality is expected theoretically to probe the non-resonant background components of the initial wave function which preserve the structural interference and the memory of how the state was created. In this paper, we propose new observables that can be used for experimental investigations of the post-exponential decay regime, including the decay of threshold resonances, particle correlations in three-body decays, and interference between near-lying resonances. While the specific examples presented in this work pertain to atomic nuclei, the properties of non-exponential decay are generic, i.e., they apply to other many-body open quantum systems, such as hadrons, atoms, molecules, and nanostructures.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the long-time (post-exponential) decay regime in open quantum systems can be probed indirectly through observables such as threshold resonance decay, particle correlations in three-body decays, and interference between near-lying resonances, rather than requiring a direct measurement of the survival probability at very long times. The authors hedge that while the specific examples pertain to atomic nuclei, the properties are generic and apply to other many-body open quantum systems.

## Method

The authors use a time-dependent approach based on the Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) method with the Berggren ensemble in complex-momentum space to generate initial resonant states, which are then projected onto real-energy scattering states via Fourier-Bessel transformation within a 15 fm spherical box. The survival probability is evaluated through the Fourier transformation of the spectral function ρ(E), which is obtained by expanding the wave function in real-energy eigenstates or analyzing the asymptotic wave function at long times. For three-body decays, the energy and angular correlations of emitted particles are computed from the asymptotic wave function propagated for long times, with the spectral function decomposed into individual Jacobi-coordinate configurations.

## Context

This work extends theoretical arguments for non-exponential decay at long times (Khalfin 1958, Fonda 1978, Nicolaides 1996, Torrontegui 2010) by proposing experimentally accessible observables in dripline nuclei, where previous experimental evidence for post-exponential decay of isolated resonances has been missing (Nikolaev 1969, Norman 1988, Kelkar 2004). The paper builds on the time-dependent approach of Wang (2021) and the Gamow coupled-channel method (Wang 2019, 2022) to connect the non-exponential decay regime to measurable quantities such as nucleon-nucleon correlations in three-body decays (Egorova 2012) and interference effects between overlapping resonances (Volya 2003, Kravvaris 2017), enabling future experimental searches in proton-rich and neutron-rich nuclei near the dripline.
