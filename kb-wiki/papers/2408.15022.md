# A multi-band study of pulsar glitches with Fermi-LAT and Parkes

> Pulsar glitch is a phenomenon characterized by abrupt changes in the spin period over less than a minute. We present a comprehensive analysis of glitches in four gamma-ray pulsars by combining the timing observation data from \textit{Fermi} Large Area Telescope (\textit{Fermi}-LAT) and Parkes 64 m radio telescope. The timing data of five pulsars, namely PSRs J1028$-$5819, J1420$-$6048, J1509$-$5850, J1709$-$4429 (B1706$-$44) and J1718$-$3825, are examined over 14 yr of observations for each. A total of 12 glitches are identified in four pulsars, including a previously unreported glitch. That is, a new small glitch is identified for PSR J1718$-$3825 in MJD $\sim$ 59121(8), with a fractional glitch size of $Δν/ν\sim 1.9(2) \times 10^{-9}$. For PSR J1420$-$6048, our investigation confirms the presence of two linear recovery terms during the evolution of $\dotν$ following glitches 4, 6 and 8. Moreover, an exponential recovery process was identified after glitch 8, with a recovery fraction ($Q$) of $Q = 0.0131(5)$ and a corresponding timescale of $τ_{\rm d} = 100(6)$ d. Regarding the fourth glitch of PSR J1709$-$4429, our analysis reveals the presence of two exponential recovery terms with degree of recovery and decay time-scales $Q$1 = 0.0104(5), $τ_{\rm d1}=72(4)$ d and $Q$2 = 0.006(1), $τ_{\rm d2}=4.2(6)$ d, respectively. For the remaining previously reported glitches, we also refine the glitch epochs and recovery process through precise fitting of the timing data. We discuss how multi-band data of glitches can help better characterize the glitch recoveries and constrain the underlying physics of glitch events. Our findings demonstrate that the accumulation of observational data reveals the rich complexity of the glitch phenomenon, aiding in the search for a well-established interpretation.

## Key claim

By combining 14 years of *Fermi*-LAT and Parkes radio timing data, the authors identify 12 glitches across four gamma-ray pulsars, including one previously unreported small glitch in PSR J1718$-$3825, and refine the recovery parameters of known glitches using multi-band observations that enable better characterization of post-glitch relaxation processes.

## Method

The authors performed timing analysis on five gamma-ray pulsars (PSRs J1028$-$5819, J1420$-$6048, J1509$-$5850, J1709$-$4429, and J1718$-$3825) using *Fermi*-LAT gamma-ray data from 2008–2022 and Parkes 64 m radio telescope data from 2007–2020. They combined radio and gamma-ray times-of-arrival (ToAs) using TEMPO2, applied EFAC and EQUAD corrections for uncertainty, and fitted glitch parameters using a model that includes permanent frequency and frequency-derivative changes plus exponential recovery terms. The vortex creep model was then applied to interpret the post-glitch relaxation in terms of linear and non-linear response regimes in the neutron star superfluid.

## Context

This work extends previous glitch studies of gamma-ray pulsars by Ng et al. (2016), Zhao et al. (2017), and Lin et al. (2021), and builds on the vortex creep model developed by Alpar et al. (1984) and Alpar (1989) to interpret post-glitch recovery. The multi-band approach combining gamma-ray and radio timing data enables more precise characterization of glitch recovery processes than single-wavelength studies, and the refined parameters allow testing of theoretical predictions for inter-glitch waiting times from the vortex creep model, as previously applied by G\"ugercino\u{g}lu et al. (2022).
