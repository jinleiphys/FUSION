# Nucleon-Nucleon Potential and its Non-locality in Lattice QCD

> By the quenched lattice QCD simulation for two nucleons with finite scattering energy, validity of the delivative expansion of the general nucleon-nucleon potential U(r,r') = V(r, {\nabla}_r) δ^3(r-r') is studied. The relative kinetic energy between two nucleons is introduced through the anti-periodic boundary condition in the spatial directions. On a hypercubic lattice with the lattice spacing a ~ 0.137 fm and the spatial extent L_s ~ 4.4 fm with the pion mass m_π ~ 530 MeV, the local potentials for two different energies (E ~ 0 MeV and 45 MeV) are compared and found to be identical within statistical errors, which validates the local approximation of U(r,r') up to E=45 MeV for the central and tensor potentials. Central potentials in the spin-singlet channel for different orbital angular momentums (l=0 and l=2) at E ~ 45 MeV are also found to be the same within the errors, which also supports the local approximation.

## Key claim

The leading-order local approximation of the general non-local nucleon-nucleon potential is validated for energies up to 45 MeV and orbital angular momenta up to l=2, under the specific condition of a heavy pion mass (m_pi ~ 530 MeV) in quenched lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 32^3 x 48 lattice with lattice spacing a ~ 0.137 fm and spatial extent L_s ~ 4.4 fm, using the standard plaquette gauge action at beta=5.7 and Wilson quark action with hopping parameter kappa=0.1665. They introduced finite scattering energy between two nucleons by imposing anti-periodic boundary conditions in the spatial directions (giving E ~ 45 MeV) and compared the resulting local potentials with those obtained under periodic boundary conditions (E ~ 0 MeV). The Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions were extracted from four-point nucleon correlation functions, and the leading-order central and tensor potentials were derived from the Schrodinger equation using the derivative expansion formalism.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a ~ 0.137 fm (inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} = 1.44(2) GeV)
- Spatial extent L_s ~ 4.4 fm (32 lattice sites)
- Pion mass m_pi ~ 530 MeV (0.53 GeV)
- Nucleon mass m_N ~ 1.33 GeV (1.33 GeV)
- Two scattering energies compared: E ~ 0 MeV (periodic boundary condition) and E ~ 45 MeV (anti-periodic boundary condition)
- For the APBC case, the lowest lying state has nucleon momentum |k| ~ sqrt(3)*pi/L_s ~ 245 MeV, corresponding to E ~ 45 MeV
- 4000 gauge configurations were accumulated, separated by 200 sweeps
- Wave functions and potentials were evaluated at time separation t-t_0 = 9

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to nucleon-nucleon potentials developed by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009), which defined the NN potential from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function. The method has previously been applied to baryon-baryon interactions with strangeness (Nemura 2008, Inoue 2010), three-nucleon interactions (Doi 2010), and meson-baryon interactions (Ikeda 2010, Kawanai 2010). The present study provides the first explicit check of the validity of the derivative expansion (local approximation) for the NN potential at finite scattering energy, which enables future applications of the lattice-derived potentials to nuclear structure calculations at low energies. The authors note that the heavy pion mass used (530 MeV) may contribute to the small energy dependence observed, and that checks at higher energies or lighter pion masses are needed to determine where next-to-leading order contributions become significant.
