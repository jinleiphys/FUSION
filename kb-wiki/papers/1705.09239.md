# Comment on "Are two nucleons bound in lattice QCD for heavy quark masses? - Sanity check with Lüscher's finite volume formula -"

> In this comment, we address a number of erroneous discussions and conclusions presented in a recent preprint by the HALQCD collaboration, arXiv:1703.07210. In particular, we demonstrate that lattice QCD determinations of bound states at quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of $m_π= 806$ MeV are robust, and that the phases shifts extracted by the NPLQCD collaboration for these systems pass all of the 'sanity checks' introduced in arXiv:1703.07210.

## Key claim

The NPLQCD collaboration argues that lattice QCD determinations of two-nucleon bound states at a pion mass of 806 MeV are robust, and that the phase shifts they extracted pass all of the "sanity checks" introduced by the HALQCD collaboration (Iritani et al., 2017), contrary to the HALQCD claims that the results are invalid.

## Method

The NPLQCD collaboration extracted two-nucleon energy levels from two-point correlation functions on multiple lattice volumes (L=24, 32, and 48) at a pion mass of 806 MeV, using the finite-volume formalism of Luscher (1986, 1990) to determine infinite-volume binding energies and phase shifts. They performed effective range expansion (ERE) fits to the extracted energies, including both ground states (n=1) and first excited states (n=2), and tested the consistency of these fits against three specific "sanity checks" proposed by HALQCD: source independence of energy levels, consistency of ERE between positive and negative momentum-squared regions, and the sign of the S-matrix residue at the bound-state pole.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the lattice ensembles: m_pi = 806 MeV
- Three lattice volumes used: L = 24, 32, and 48 (in lattice units), with spatial volumes varying by a factor of eight
- In the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel, the slope condition from the S-matrix residue check is satisfied at more than 3 sigma significance
- In the 1S0 channel, the slope condition is satisfied at the 1 sigma level

## Context

This comment responds to a preprint by the HALQCD collaboration (Iritani et al., 2017) that questioned the validity of multi-baryon lattice QCD calculations. The NPLQCD collaboration demonstrates that their earlier results (Beane et al., 2012, 2013) and a refined re-analysis (Wagman et al., 2017) pass all HALQCD sanity checks, and that the HALQCD criticisms were based on errors including misidentifying an excited state as the ground state. The work enables continued confidence in lattice QCD studies of multi-nucleon systems and suggests that the HALQCD potential method requires further investigation to understand its failure to identify two-nucleon bound states.
