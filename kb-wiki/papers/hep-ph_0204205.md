# Scalar mesons above and below 1 GeV

> We show that two nonets and a glueball provide a consistent description of data on scalar mesons below 1.7 GeV. Above 1 GeV the states form a conventional (q bar q) nonet mixed with the glueball of lattice QCD. Below 1 GeV the states also form a nonet, as implied by the attractive forces of QCD, but of more complicated nature. Near the center they are 4 quark states of the Jaffe type in S-wave, with some (q bar q) in P-wave, but further out they rearrange in colour to two colourless (q bar q) pairs and finally as meson-meson states. A simple effective chiral model for such a system with two scalar nonets can be made involving two coupled linear sigma models. One of these could be looked upon as the Higgs sector of nonpertubative QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the scalar meson spectrum below 1.7 GeV can be consistently described by two nonets and a glueball, but the interpretation depends critically on mass region: above 1 GeV the states form a conventional quark-antiquark nonet mixed with a glueball, while below 1 GeV the states form a nonet of more complicated nature involving four-quark and meson-meson components, with the exact nature of broad states like the sigma and kappa remaining uncertain.

## Method

The authors synthesize lattice QCD predictions for the scalar glueball mass (approximately 1.6 GeV in the quenched approximation) with phenomenological data on scalar meson production and decays from multiple experiments, including charmed meson decays (E791, E687), radiative J/psi decays, central production in proton-proton collisions, and phi radiative decays from KLOE at DAΦNE. They compare the observed flavor patterns of scalar states above and below 1 GeV to the predictions of quark-antiquark, four-quark (Jaffe-type), and meson-molecule models, and examine the mixing between the glueball and nearby quark-antiquark states as well as isospin mixing between the f0(980) and a0(980).

## Key numbers

- The scalar glueball mass in the quenched approximation is approximately 1.6 GeV
- The sigma pole position is near 500-i250 MeV from most analyses in the 2000 Review of Particle Physics
- A BES experiment on J/psi to sigma omega to pi pi omega finds a sigma Breit-Wigner mass of 390 (+60/-36) MeV and width of 282 (+77/-50) MeV
- The CLEO analysis of tau to a1 nu to sigma pi nu to 3 pi nu finds a sigma Breit-Wigner mass of approximately 555 MeV and width of 540 MeV
- The E791 study of D to 3 pi finds a sigma with mass approximately 478 MeV and width approximately 324 MeV, with 46% of the D+ to 3 pi Dalitz plot explained by sigma pi
- The f0(980)-a0(980) mixing intensity from central production data is 8 +/- 3%
- The KLOE preliminary ratio Gamma(phi to gamma f0)/Gamma(phi to gamma a0) is 4.1 +/- 0.4, and in a more recent report 6.1 +/- 0.6
- KLOE preliminary branching ratios: br(phi to gamma f0) = (2.4 +/- 0.1) x 10^(-4) and br(phi to gamma a0) = (0.6 +/- 0.05) x 10^(-4)
- Later KLOE results: br(phi to gamma f0) = (4.5 +/- 0.2) x 10^(-4) and br(phi to gamma a0) = (0.75 +/- 0.07) x 10^(-4)
- The linear sigma model predicts the sigma at 620 MeV with a width of approximately 600 MeV, the a0(980) at 1128 MeV, the f0(980) at 1190 MeV, and the kappa at 1120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on the nature of light scalar mesons, which has been controversial for over thirty years, by proposing that two distinct nonets exist below and above 1 GeV. It builds on Jaffe's (1977) proposal of four-quark states, lattice QCD predictions for the scalar glueball mass from Bali (1993), Sesam (1995), and other groups, and the mixing analysis of Close and Kirk (2001) for the glueball-quarkonium system above 1 GeV. The paper enables future experimental tests through charmed meson decays, radiative phi decays at DAΦNE, and J/psi decays at BES, and calls for coupled-channel analyses incorporating unitarity to resolve discrepancies in the KLOE data on phi to gamma f0 and gamma a0.
