# Soft-core meson-baryon interactions. II. $πN$ and $K^+ N$ scattering

> The $πN$ potential includes the t-channel exchanges of the scalar-mesons $σ$ and f_0, vector-meson $ρ$, tensor-mesons f_2 and f_2' and the Pomeron as well as the s- and u-channel exchanges of the nucleon N and the resonances $Δ$, Roper and S_{11}. These resonances are not generated dynamically. We consider them as, at least partially, genuine three-quark states and we treat them in the same way as the nucleon. The latter two resonances were needed to find the proper behavior of the phase shifts at higher energies in the corresponding partial waves. The soft-core $πN$-model gives an excellent fit to the empirical $πN$ S- and P-wave phase shifts up to T_{lab}=600 MeV. Also the scattering lengths have been reproduced well and the soft-pion theorems for low-energy $πN$ scattering are satisfied. The soft-core model for the $K^+ N$ interaction is an SU_f(3)-extension of the soft-core $πN$-model. The $K^+ N$ potential includes the t-channel exchanges of the scalar-mesons a_0, $σ$ and f_0, vector-mesons $ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$, tensor-mesons a_2, f_2 and f_2' and the Pomeron as well as u-channel exchanges of the hyperons $Λ$ and $Σ$. The fit to the empirical $K^+ N$ S-, P- and D-wave phase shifts up to T_{lab}=600 MeV is reasonable and certainly reflects the present state of the art. Since the various $K^+ N$ phase shift analyses are not very consistent, also scattering observables are compared with the soft-core $K^+ N$-model. A good agreement for the total and differential cross sections as well as the polarizations is found.

## Key claim

The paper presents a soft-core meson-exchange model for πN and K⁺N scattering where the Δ, Roper, and S₁₁ resonances are treated as at least partially genuine three-quark states (not dynamically generated), and the K⁺N model is an SU_f(3) extension of the πN model. The fit to πN S- and P-wave phase shifts up to T_lab = 600 MeV is described as "excellent," while the fit to K⁺N S-, P-, and D-wave phase shifts up to the same energy is described as "reasonable" and "reflects the present state of the art," with the caveat that K⁺N phase shift analyses are not very consistent.

## Method

The πN potential includes t-channel exchanges of scalar mesons (σ, f₀), vector meson (ρ), tensor mesons (f₂, f₂′), and the Pomeron, plus s- and u-channel exchanges of the nucleon, Δ, Roper, and S₁₁ resonances. The K⁺N potential is constructed as an SU_f(3) extension of the πN model, including t-channel exchanges of scalar mesons (a₀, σ, f₀), vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ), tensor mesons (a₂, f₂, f₂′), and the Pomeron, plus u-channel exchanges of Λ and Σ hyperons. Gaussian-type form factors are used, and the resonances are included explicitly with renormalization at their poles, while the scalar mesons are treated as belonging to an SU_f(3) nonet rather than as effective correlated two-pion exchange.

## Key numbers

- The πN model fits empirical S- and P-wave phase shifts up to T_lab = 600 MeV.
- The K⁺N model fits empirical S-, P-, and D-wave phase shifts up to T_lab = 600 MeV.
- The Δ resonance mass is given as M_Δ = 1232 MeV.
- The exotic S = 1 resonance in the I = 0 K⁺N system has √s ≈ 1540 MeV and Γ ≤ 25 MeV, with a predicted mass of about 1530 MeV and width less than 15 MeV from Diakonov et al. (1997).
- The σ₀ meson introduced in prior Jülich models has a mass of 1200 MeV.
- The Jülich K⁺N model (Bütgen et al., 1990) required a 60% increase in the ω coupling to obtain enough short-range repulsion.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core (NSC) meson-exchange approach, previously applied to NN and YN interactions (Rijken et al., 1999, 2002), to the πN and K⁺N channels. It differs from prior πN models by Gross and Surya (1993), Schütz et al. (1994), and Pascalutsa and Tjon (2000) in its treatment of resonances as explicit three-quark states and its use of Gaussian form factors and Pomeron exchange. For K⁺N, it contrasts with the Jülich models (Bütgen et al., 1990; Hoffmann et al., 1995) by using only one-particle exchanges and including tensor-meson exchange, and with quark models (Barnes and Swanson, 1994; Silvestre-Brac et al., 1995, 1997; Lemaire et al., 2001, 2002) which fail to describe higher partial waves. The model enables theoretical estimates for the upper limit of the decay width of the recently discovered exotic resonance in the isospin zero K⁺N system.
