# Next-to-Leading QCD Factorization for Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering at Twist-4

> Within the framework of a high-twist approach, we calculate the next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD corrections to the transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive hadron production in deeply inelastic $e+A$ collisions, as well as lepton pair production in $p+A$ collisions. With explicit calculations of both real and virtual contributions, we verify, for the first time, the factorization theorem at twist 4 in NLO for the nuclear-enhanced transverse momentum weighted differential cross section and demonstrate the universality of the associated twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function. We also identify the QCD evolution equation for the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function in a large nucleus, which can be solved to determine the scale dependence of the jet transport parameter in the study of jet quenching.

## Key claim

The authors verify for the first time the factorization theorem at twist-4 in next-to-leading order (NLO) for the nuclear-enhanced transverse momentum weighted differential cross section in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan production, but only for contributions proportional to the nuclear size \(A^{1/3}\) (neglecting other \(A\)-independent higher-twist contributions).

## Method

Within the high-twist formalism using collinear expansion, the authors calculate both real and virtual one-loop QCD corrections to the \(\ell_{hT}^2\)-weighted differential cross section from double parton scattering in SIDIS off a large nucleus. They evaluate 57 diagrams for quark-gluon double scatterings (including soft-soft, hard-hard, and interference contributions) plus photon+gluon channel contributions, using dimensional regularization in \(n=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions to isolate infrared and collinear divergences. The collinear divergences are absorbed into renormalized fragmentation functions and a twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function, with the remaining finite pieces defining the NLO hard coefficient functions.

## Key numbers

- The LO result for the \(\ell_{hT}^2\)-weighted cross section contains \(\sigma_h = (4\pi^2\alpha_{\rm s}z_h^2/N_c) \sigma_0\) with \(\sigma_0 = \frac{2\pi\alpha_{\rm em}^2}{Q^2}\frac{1+(1-y)^2}{y} (1-\epsilon)\) in \(n=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions.
- The NLO virtual correction multiplies the LO result by \(\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} C_F \left(\frac{4\pi\mu^2}{Q^2}\right)^{\epsilon} \frac{1}{\Gamma(1-\epsilon)} \left[-\frac{2}{\epsilon^2}-\frac{3}{\epsilon}-8\right]\).
- The final NLO result contains divergences proportional to \(1/\hat\epsilon = 1/\epsilon - \gamma_E + \ln(4\pi\mu^2/\mu_f^2)\) where \(\mu_f\) is the factorization scale in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme.
- The evolution equation for the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function involves splitting kernels \(P_{qq}(\hat x)\), \(P_{qg}(\hat x)\), and a new kernel \(\mathcal{P}_{qg\to qg}\) with terms proportional to \(C_A/2\) and factors like \(4/(1-\hat x)_+\) and \((1+\hat x)/(1-\hat x)_+\).

## Context

This work extends the high-twist approach to parton energy loss and transverse momentum broadening (Guo 1998, Wang 2001, Kang 2008) by providing the first NLO verification of factorization at twist-4, which had previously only been proven at leading order (Qiu 1990). The results demonstrate the universality of the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function across SIDIS and Drell-Yan processes, and the derived evolution equation enables determination of the scale dependence of the jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}\), which is essential for quantitative NLO studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions (Armesto 2011). The work also identifies the connection between soft-hard interference contributions and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect (Wang 2001, Xing 2011).
