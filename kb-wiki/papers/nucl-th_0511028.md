# Fine structure of the 0.7 MeV resonance in the 230Th neutron--induced cross section

> The fine structure of the 0.7 MeV resonance in the 230Th neutron-induced cross section is investigated within the hybrid model. A very good agreement with experimental data is obtained. It is suggested that fine structure of the cross section quantify the changes of the intrinsic states of the nucleus during the disintegration process.

## Key claim

The fine structure of the 0.7 MeV resonance in the 230Th neutron-induced fission cross section can be explained by the existence of several barriers associated with different single-particle excitations of the unpaired neutron, without appealing to a triple-humped barrier interpretation. The authors suggest that dynamical single-particle effects, rather than a static triple-humped barrier, are responsible for the fine structure phenomenon.

## Method

The authors used the Hybrid Model (HM), which combines a phenomenological double-humped fission barrier with microscopic single-particle excitations computed using the Superasymmetric Two-Centre Shell Model (STCSM). They calculated the fission trajectory by minimizing the action integral in a three-dimensional configuration space (elongation, necking, mass-asymmetry), then solved coupled-channel equations to determine occupation probabilities of excited single-particle states via Landau-Zener effect at avoided level crossings. These excitations were added to a phenomenological double-humped barrier to construct new barriers, and the cross section was computed using the detailed balance principle with an imaginary potential in the second well to account for non-fission de-excitation channels.

## Key numbers

- Phenomenological barrier parameters: first barrier height V_A = 6.24 MeV, second well depth V_II = 2.37 MeV, second barrier height V_B = 6.29 MeV; stiffnesses ħω_A = 0.65 MeV, ħω_II = 1 MeV, ħω_B = 1.37 MeV.
- The three excited barriers of interest produce β-resonances within an energy interval smaller than 0.05 MeV, giving three main fine peaks in the fission cross section.
- The fine structure is found in the neutron energy region [0.7, 0.75] MeV, with the simulation agreeing very well with experimental data from Bol (year not given in text) and Lynn (year not given in text).
- The outer barrier is about three MeV lower in energy in the present STCSM version than in the previous version by Mirea (2004) and Mirea (2002).

## Context

This work extends the Hybrid Model previously developed by Mirea (2004) to explain intermediate structure in fission cross sections, and offers an alternative to the triple-humped barrier interpretation proposed by Bol (year not given in text) and Lynn (year not given in text) for the fine structure of 230Th neutron-induced fission. The authors show that the fine structure arises naturally from single-particle excitations near the top of the second barrier, where the level density is very high, and note that current evaluated data files (ENDF/B-VI.8 and JENDF-3.3) fail to reproduce this fine structure. This enables future studies of how the complex resonant structure of fission cross sections carries information about nuclear structure and dynamics during the disintegration process.
