# Structure of near-threshold resonances with new interpretation scheme of complex compositeness

> The nature of near-threshold resonances is quantitatively studied with a new interpretation scheme using the complex compositeness. A difficulty was known in the understanding of the internal structure of unstable resonances because their complex compositeness is not an interpretable measure. To overcome this problem, we develop a new interpretation scheme respecting the ambiguous aspects of the identification of the internal structure of resonances. We then apply the interpretation scheme to the near-threshold resonances slightly above the threshold, described by the effective range expansion. With the new interpretation scheme, we show that near-threshold resonances are dominated by the non-molecular component. Namely, even in the near-threshold region, the nature of resonances is sharply contrasted with bound states whose internal structure is usually molecular dominant.

## Key claim

Near-threshold resonances (unstable states above threshold) are generally dominated by the non-molecular component, in sharp contrast to shallow bound states below threshold which are usually molecular dominant; this result holds under a new interpretation scheme that introduces an "uncertain identification" category to handle the complex compositeness of unstable resonances.

## Method

The authors develop an interpretation scheme for unstable resonances that replaces the complex compositeness \(X\) (which cannot be interpreted as a probability) with three real probabilities: \(\mathcal{X}\) (certain molecular), \(\mathcal{Z}\) (certain non-molecular), and \(\mathcal{Y}\) (uncertain identification). They apply this scheme to near-threshold resonances described by the effective range expansion (ERE) truncated at \(k^2\) order, using the scattering length \(a_0\) and effective range \(r_e\) to compute the complex compositeness \(X = -i \tan \theta_k\) for resonances in the region \(0 < -\theta_k \leq \pi/4\) of the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(\alpha\) controlling the degree of ambiguity is fixed to \(\alpha_0 \approx 1.1318\), which excludes resonances with decay width \(\Gamma\) larger than the real part of the eigenenergy Re \(E\) (i.e., \(|\theta_E| > |\arctan(-1/2)|\)).
- In the interpretable region (\(|\theta_E| < |\arctan(-1/2)|\)), all resonances satisfy \(\mathcal{Z} \gtrsim 0.8\), meaning they are non-molecular dominant.
- On the real axis (\(-\theta_E = 0\)), the probabilities are \(\mathcal{X} = 0\), \(\mathcal{Y} = 0\), and \(\mathcal{Z} = 1\).
- For a resonance to exist in the ERE with \(0 < -\theta_k \leq \pi/4\), the condition \(|a_0| \leq |r_e|\) must hold (derived from \(r_e/a_0 \geq 1\) with \(r_e < 0\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of near-threshold states beyond the well-established low-energy universality for shallow bound states (Braaten 2004, Naidon 2016), which predicts molecular dominance for bound states below threshold (Hyodo 2014, Hanhart 2014). The authors contrast their result with the threshold energy rule (PTPS.E68.464) and prior compositeness analyses of bound states like the deuteron (Weinberg 1965, Kamiya 2017). The new interpretation scheme, inspired by Berggren (1970) on resonance extraction in nuclear reactions, enables quantitative classification of unstable resonances and can be applied to exotic hadron candidates such as \(\Xi(1620)\) (Sarti 2023) with excitation energy \(+3.68\) MeV from the \(K^-\Lambda\) threshold.
