# Dynamical Evolution, Hadronization and Angular De-correlation of Heavy Flavor in a Hot and Dense QCD Medium

> We study heavy flavor evolution and hadronization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The in-medium evolution of heavy quarks is described using our modified Langevin framework that incorporates both collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms. The subsequent hadronization process for heavy quarks is calculated with a fragmentation plus recombination model. We find significant contribution from gluon radiation to heavy quark energy loss at high $p_T$; the recombination mechanism can greatly enhance D meson production at medium $p_T$. Our calculation provides a good description of D meson nuclear modification at the LHC. In addition, we explore the angular correlation functions of heavy flavor pairs which may provide us a potential candidate for distinguishing different energy loss mechanisms of heavy quarks inside QGP.

## Key claim
The paper claims that incorporating both collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms for heavy quarks in a modified Langevin framework, combined with a fragmentation-plus-recombination hadronization model, provides a good description of D meson nuclear modification at the LHC; however, the authors note that tuning the transport coefficient allows either collisional or radiative energy loss alone to also provide a reasonable (though less accurate) description of the data.

## Method
The authors used a modified Langevin equation that includes a drag term, a thermal random force, and a recoil force term for medium-induced gluon radiation, with the gluon emission probability calculated using the Higher-Twist energy loss formalism. Heavy quark evolution was simulated inside a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic medium (VISH2+1) for 2.76 TeV PbPb collisions at the LHC, with initial heavy quark distributions from leading-order pQCD using CTEQ parton distribution functions and EPS08 nuclear shadowing. Hadronization was modeled using a hybrid fragmentation plus coalescence (recombination) model, and angular correlation functions of heavy flavor pairs were analyzed for different energy loss scenarios.

## Key numbers
- The spatial diffusion coefficient of heavy quarks in QGP was set to D = 6/(2π T).
- A lower cutoff ω₀ = π T was applied for gluon energy to incorporate the balance between emission and absorption.
- The gluon transport coefficient is related to the momentum space diffusion coefficient via q̂ = 2κ C_A/C_F, and the spatial diffusion coefficient relates via D = 2T²/κ.
- The study used 2.76 TeV PbPb collisions at the LHC.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Cao 2011, Cao 2012, Cao 2013) on heavy quark evolution and hadronization in heavy-ion collisions, using a modified Langevin framework that simultaneously incorporates collisional and radiative energy loss. It builds upon the Higher-Twist energy loss formalism (Zhang 2003) and uses the VISH2+1 hydrodynamic model (Song 2007, Song 2007) with parameter tunings from Qiu (2011). The angular correlation analysis of heavy flavor pairs is proposed as a potential observable to distinguish between different energy loss mechanisms, complementing the single-particle nuclear modification factor R_AA measurements from the ALICE collaboration.
