# Variational Theory and Parquet Diagrams for Nuclear Systems: A Comprehensive Study of Neutron Matter

> To deal with the problem of realistic nuclear interactions we have combined techniques of the Jastrow-Feenberg variational method and the local parquet-diagram theory. In the language of diagrammatic perturbation theory, ``commutator diagrams'' can be identified with non-parquet diagrams. We examine the physical processes described by these terms and include the relevant diagrams in a way that is suggested by the Jastrow-Feenberg approach. We show that the corrections from non-parquet contributions are, at short distances, larger than all other many-body effects. We examine here neutron matter as a prototype of systems with state-dependent interactions. Calculations are carried out for neutrons interacting via the so-called $v_8$ version of four popular interactions. We determine the structure and effective interactions and apply the method to the calculation of the energetics, structure and dynamic properties such as the single-particle self-energy and the dynamic response functions as well as BCS pairing in both singlet and triplet states. We find that many-body correlations lead to a strong reduction of the spin-orbit interaction, and, therefore, to a suppression of the $^3P_2$ and $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ gaps. We also find pairing in $^3P_0$ states; the strength of the pairing gap depends sensitively on the potential model employed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that non-parquet ("commutator") diagrams, which arise from the state-dependent operator structure of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and have been mostly neglected in prior work, contribute corrections at short distances that are larger than all other many-body effects; the authors incorporate these diagrams using a synthesis of the Jastrow-Feenberg variational method and local parquet-diagram theory.

## Method

The authors combine the Jastrow-Feenberg variational method with local parquet-diagram theory, treating neutron matter as a prototype system with state-dependent interactions. They use the operator representation of four nucleon-nucleon potentials (Reid 68, Argonne v6' and v8', and a chiral N2LO interaction) in their v8 form, and solve the coupled equations for the pair distribution function, static structure function, and particle-hole interaction via the FHNC-EL (Fermi Hypernetted Chain Euler-Lagrange) procedure. The non-parquet commutator diagrams are identified and included in a way suggested by the variational approach, and the method is extended to compute dynamic properties (dynamic response, single-particle self-energy) and BCS pairing gaps in both singlet and triplet channels.

## Key numbers

- The static structure function S(q) for fermions is given by S(q) = S_F(q) / sqrt(1 + 2 S_F^2(q) / t(q) * V_ph(q)), where S_F(q) = 3q/(4k_F) - q^3/(16 k_F^3) for q < 2k_F and S_F(q) = 1 for q >= 2k_F.
- The particle-hole interaction V_ph(r) = [1 + Gamma_dd(r)] [v(r) + V_e(r)] + (hbar^2/m) |nabla sqrt(1+Gamma_dd(r))|^2 + Gamma_dd(r) w_I(r), where Gamma_dd is the direct correlation function.
- The induced interaction w_I(q) = -t(q) (S(q) - 1) for bosons, with t(q) = hbar^2 q^2/(2m).
- The energy per particle is obtained by coupling-constant integration: E/N = (rho/2) integral d^3r v(r) integral_0^1 dlambda g_lambda(r).
- The chiral N2LO interaction uses a cutoff of 1.0 fm.
- The spin-orbit force is strongly reduced by many-body correlations, leading to suppression of the 3P2 and 3P2-3F2 gaps and enhancement of 3P0 pairing.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies (Krotscheck and Wang, 2023-2025) on the nuclear many-body problem using variational and parquet-diagram methods, specifically building on their earlier treatment of v3 and v4 interactions. It addresses the long-standing difficulty of handling state-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions in microscopic many-body theory, where simple Jastrow-Feenberg methods used for electrons and quantum fluids (helium-3, helium-4) are inadequate. The approach enables calculation of ground-state energetics, structure, dynamic response, and BCS pairing in neutron matter without phenomenological input, and it identifies that commutator (non-parquet) diagrams are essential for correctly describing short-range correlations in systems with strong spin/isospin dependence.
