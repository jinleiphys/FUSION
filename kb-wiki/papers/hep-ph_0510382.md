# Novel evaluation of the two-pion contribution to the nucleon isovector form factors

> We calculate the two-pion continuum contribution to the nucleon isovector spectral functions drawing upon the new high statistics measurements of the pion form factor by the CMD-2, KLOE, and SND collaborations. The general structure of the spectral functions remains unchanged, but the magnitude increases by about 10%. Using the updated spectral functions, we calculate the contribution of the two-pion continuum to the nucleon isovector form factors and radii. We compare the isovector radii with simple rho-pole models and illustrate their strong underestimation in such approaches. Moreover, we give a convenient parametrization of the result for use in future form factor analyses.

## Key claim
The two-pion continuum contribution to the nucleon isovector spectral functions increases in magnitude by about 10% compared to previous analyses when using the new high-statistics pion form factor data from CMD-2, KLOE, and SND, but the general structure of the spectral functions remains unchanged. The authors note that discrepancies exist among the three experimental data sets, particularly at the rho-resonance peak, and they take all three sets at face value to estimate the resulting error.

## Method
The authors used the unitarity relation to express the two-pion contribution to the isovector spectral functions for the Sachs form factors G_E^V and G_M^V in terms of the pion charge form factor F_pi(t) and the P-wave pi pi to N bar N amplitudes f^1_plus(t) and f^1_minus(t), as tabulated by Hoehler et al. (1976). They inserted updated parametrizations of the pion form factor of the Gounaris-Sakurai type, fitted to the new high-statistics data from CMD-2, KLOE, and SND, while keeping the pi pi to N bar N amplitudes fixed. The resulting spectral functions were then used in unsubtracted dispersion relations to compute the two-pion continuum contribution to the nucleon isovector form factors and radii, integrated up to t = 50 M_pi^2.

## Key numbers
- The magnitude of the two-pion spectral function increases by about 10% compared to the previous analysis by Hoehler et al. (1976).
- The two-pion continuum contribution to the Dirac isovector radius <r^2>^V_1 is 0.32 to 0.33 fm^2, and to the Pauli isovector radius <r^2>^V_2 is 1.77 to 1.80 fm^2, with the range indicating the error from the different pion form factor data sets.
- Simple rho-pole models underestimate the full two-pion continuum contribution to the isovector radii by about 30-40%; for example, the rho-pole parametrization from Ref. [Norbert] gives <r^2>^V_1 = 0.19 fm^2 and <r^2>^V_2 = 1.10 fm^2.
- The logarithmic singularity on the second Riemann sheet is located at t_c = 3.98 M_pi^2, very close to the two-pion threshold at t = 4 M_pi^2.
- The parametrization constants for the form factor fit (Eq. 13) are given, with errors of order 4% or less: a_1=1.10788, b_1=0.109364, c_1=0.36963, d_1=0.553034, a_2=5.724253, b_2=1.111128, c_2=0.27175, d_2=0.611258.

## Context
This work updates the classic dispersion analysis of the two-pion continuum contribution to nucleon isovector form factors originally performed by Hoehler and Pietarinen (1975) and Hoehler et al. (1976), and later updated in the mid-1990s by Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996) and Hammer, Meissner, and Drechsel (1999). The new high-statistics pion form factor measurements from CMD-2, KLOE, and SND allow a more precise determination of this contribution, which is the dominant long-range part of the isovector spectral function up to about 1 GeV. The results provide a fixed, parameter-free input for future dispersion analyses of the full nucleon form factors, reducing the number of free parameters, and the authors state that a new dispersion-theoretical analysis using this updated two-pion continuum is in preparation. The findings also confirm the earlier conclusion by Hammer, Meissner, and Drechsel (2003) that a long-range pion cloud extending to 2 fm is not compatible with the known two-pion contribution.
