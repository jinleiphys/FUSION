# Chiral Symmetry in the Confinement Phase of QCD

> Based on the Pomeranchuk theorem, one constructs the $δ(s)$ parameter to measure the difference between experimental data for the particle-particle and particle-antiparticle total cross section at the same energy. The experimental data for the proton-proton and proton-antiproton total cross section were used to show that, at the same energy, this parameter tends to zero as the collision energy grows. Furthermore, one assumes a classical description of the total cross section, dividing it into a finite number of non-interacting disjoint cells, each one containing a quark-antiquark pair subject to the confinement potential. Near the minimum of the total cross section, one associates $δ(s)$ with the entropy generated by these cells, analogous to the XY model. Using both the Quigg-Rosner and Cornell confinement potentials and neglecting other energy contributions, one can calculate the internal energy of the hadron. One obtains that both the entropy and internal energy possess the same logarithmic dependence on the spatial separation between the pairs in the cell. The Helmholtz free energy is used to estimate the transition temperature, which is far from the temperature widely related to the Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

Based on the Pomeranchuk theorem, the paper constructs a $\delta(s)$ parameter from the ratio of proton-antiproton to proton-proton total cross sections and, by interpreting the total cross section classically as a collection of disjoint cells each containing a quark-antiquark pair, argues that the entropy and internal energy of the hadron possess the same logarithmic dependence on spatial separation, leading to a topological phase transition analogous to the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) transition, but the estimated transition temperature is "far from the temperature widely related to the Quark-Gluon Plasma."

## Method

The author constructs the parameter $\delta(s) = \ln[\sigma_{tot}^{p\bar{p}}(s)/\sigma_{tot}^{pp}(s)]$ using experimental data for proton-proton and proton-antiproton total cross sections at the same energy. Near the minimum of the total cross section, $\delta(s)$ is associated with the entropy generated by non-interacting disjoint cells, each containing a quark-antiquark pair subject to a confinement potential. The internal energy of the hadron is calculated by integrating the Quigg-Rosner and Cornell confinement potentials, neglecting kinetic, chemical, and other energy contributions. The Helmholtz free energy is then used to estimate the transition temperature.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s}=62.7$ GeV, $\delta(s)=0.0128\pm0.0001$, indicating the parameter tends to zero as collision energy grows.
- The Quigg-Rosner confinement potential uses $\gamma\approx 0.75$ GeV.
- The confinement scale $r_0=\sqrt{r_{u\bar{u}}r_{d\bar{d}}}\approx 0.6$ fm, where $r_{u\bar{u}}$ and $r_{d\bar{d}}$ are the confinement scales for $u\bar{u}$ and $d\bar{d}$ pairs respectively.
- The experimental data for $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ total cross sections are used in the energy range $3.5$ GeV $<\sqrt{s}<63.0$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) phase transition framework, originally developed for two-dimensional systems in solid-state physics (Berezinskii 1970; Kosterlitz and Thouless 1973), to high-energy hadronic scattering. It uses the Pomeranchuk theorem (Pomeranchuk 1958) and the Quigg-Rosner (Quigg and Rosner 1977) and Cornell (Eichten et al. 1975, 1978, 1980) confinement potentials to connect total cross section data to thermodynamic quantities. The paper suggests that the phase transition described may correspond to chiral symmetry restoration in the confinement phase of QCD, and it enables a thermodynamic interpretation of the hadronic total cross section minimum distinct from the conventional Quark-Gluon Plasma transition associated with the Hagedorn temperature (Hagedorn 1965, 1968).
