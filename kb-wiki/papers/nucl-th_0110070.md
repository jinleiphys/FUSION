# Self-Consistent Harmonic Oscillator Model and Tilted Rotation

> The three dimensional harmonic oscillator model including a cranking term is used for an energy variational calculation. Energy minima are found under variation of the three oscillator frequencies determining the shape of the system for given values of the three components of the rotational vector which determines the orientation of the angular momentum in the intrinsic frame. Tilted rotations are established by numerical means for triaxial nuclei. The onset of tilted rotations is related to an instability of the mean field as verified by the random phase approximation. Analytic expressions are derived for the critical rotational frequencies associated with the mean field instability.

## Key claim

Tilted rotations (non-principal-axis rotations) occur in even-even nuclei if and only if the nucleus has a triaxial shape in its ground state; the onset of tilted rotation is signaled by the vanishing of a wobbling excitation mode at a critical rotational frequency, but this transition cannot occur for nuclei that are oblate at zero rotation.

## Method

The authors used a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator model with a cranking term for an energy variational calculation, minimizing the total energy under variation of the three oscillator frequencies (subject to a fixed volume constraint) for given components of the rotational vector. They solved the random phase approximation (RPA) equations in the rotating frame to identify bifurcation points where collective vibrational frequencies vanish, and derived analytic expressions for the critical rotational frequencies from the secular equations.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleus ²⁰Ne (10 particles), the global minimum at Ω=0 occurs for a prolate shape with configuration (Σ₁,Σ₂,Σ₃)=(7,7,11); at Ω≈4.55 a lower minimum appears with configuration (5,5,25), and the oblate shape occurs at Ω_crit^(1)≈5.65 as a local minimum only.
- The critical ratio r_crit = (√27+√2)/(√27-√2) determines the boundary for prolate-to-oblate shape transition under rotation.
- For the oblate case with N=16 and configuration (Σ₁,Σ₂,Σ₃)=(30,40,44), with ω_x=20.7 and ω_⊥=14.5, the critical frequency for tilted rotation is Ω_crit^(2)=0.93 for r=1.1.
- For oblate systems with r=1 (Σ₃=Σ₂), the critical frequency for the λ=±1 modes is Ω_cr = ±√(ω_x²+ω_⊥²), which exceeds the maximal allowed rotational frequency.

## Context

This work extends the triaxial cranked oscillator (TCO) model of Frauendorf (1993) by enforcing self-consistency between rotation and shape evolution, which was a serious drawback of earlier TCO calculations that used fixed triaxial deformation. The paper provides a microscopic foundation for the phenomenological two-phase model of Marshalek (1978) and connects the onset of tilted rotation to the softening of wobbling excitations, as previously discussed by Janssen (1978) and Marshalek (1979). The results enable future studies of tilted rotation in triaxial nuclei and clarify why oblate nuclei cannot support such rotations.
