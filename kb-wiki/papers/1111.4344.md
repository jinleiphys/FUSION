# Nuclear energy density optimization: Large deformations

> A new Skyrme-like energy density suitable for studies of strongly elongated nuclei has been determined in the framework of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory using the recently developed model-based, derivative-free optimization algorithm POUNDerS. A sensitivity analysis at the optimal solution has revealed the importance of states at large deformations in driving the parameterization of the functional. The good agreement with experimental data on masses and separation energies, achieved with the previous parameterization UNEDF0, is largely preserved. In addition, the new energy density UNEDF1 gives a much improved description of the fission barriers in 240Pu and neighboring nuclei.

## Key claim

The new UNEDF1 Skyrme-like energy density functional, optimized using the derivative-free POUNDerS algorithm within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework, preserves the good agreement with experimental masses and separation energies achieved by the previous UNEDF0 parameterization while providing a much improved description of fission barriers in 240Pu and neighboring nuclei, but only when the center-of-mass correction is neglected and fission isomer excitation energies are added to the fit dataset.

## Method

The authors optimized 12 parameters of a Skyrme-like energy density functional using the model-based, derivative-free optimization algorithm POUNDerS within the HFB theory, implemented with the HFBTHO code on axially symmetric deformed harmonic oscillator bases. The experimental dataset was expanded from the UNEDF0 fit by adding three ground-state masses of deformed nuclei (238U, 240Pu, 242Cm) and four fission isomer excitation energies (bandheads) with weights of 0.5 MeV, while the center-of-mass correction was removed from the functional to avoid conceptual problems in fission studies. The optimization ran 218 total HFBTHO simulations for each nucleus in the dataset using 80 compute nodes (640 cores) for 5.67 hours on the Fusion cluster at Argonne National Laboratory.

* The fission isomer data represent less than 4% of the total number of data points but account for typically 30% of the variation of the parameter set; for the symmetry energy coefficient this percentage is 75%

## Context

This work extends the UNEDF0 parameterization (Kortelainen et al., 2010) by adding fission isomer data to constrain surface properties of the functional, following the conclusion of Nikolov et al. (2010) that data on strongly deformed nuclear states should be considered in the optimization protocol. Unlike earlier efforts such as the SkM* functional (Bartel et al., 1982) which was optimized for fission using a semi-classical Thomas-Fermi approach with shell corrections, or the D1S parameterization of the Gogny force (Berger et al., 1989) which used a manual readjustment of the surface coefficient, UNEDF1 is the first EDF systematically optimized at the deformed HFB level without phenomenological corrections by explicitly considering constraints on states at large deformations. The new functional enables self-consistent fission and fusion studies without the conceptual problems associated with the center-of-mass correction, and the sensitivity analysis reveals that the isovector surface coupling constants C1ρΔρ and C1ρ∇J remain relatively unconstrained, requiring future constraints from spin-orbit splitting in neutron-rich nuclei.
