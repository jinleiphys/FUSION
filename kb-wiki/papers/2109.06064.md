# Quenching of Gamow-Teller strengths and two particle -- two hole configurations

> We apply the charge-exchange subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA), based on Skyrme functionals, to investigate Gamow-Teller resonances in several closed-shell and closed-subshell nuclei, located in different regions of the nuclear chart. After having discussed the SSRPA findings obtained within different approximation schemes in $^{48}$Ca, we compare our results with {\it{ab-initio}} coupled-cluster predictions available for C and O isotopes, where two-body currents are included. Our integrated strenghts, obtained by using one-body transition operators, are lower compared to the corresponding {\it{ab-initio}} results. This indicates that, within our model, quenching effects are mainly driven by the inclusion of two particle - two hole configurations and that the role of a two-body contribution in the transition operator is less important than in the coupled-cluster approach. By analyzing heavier nuclei, $^{90}$ Zr and $^{132}$Sn, we confirm the same conclusions that we have recently drawn for $^{48}$Ca: the inclusion of two particle - two hole configurations is very effective in our model for providing strengths which are significantly more quenched than in other theoretical models and, thus, in better agreement with the experimental measurements. This occurs because two particle - two hole configurations have a density which strongly increases with the excitation energy. Their inclusion thus pushes a significant amount of the strength to higher energies, compared to what happens in other theoretical models, reducing in this way the cumulative sum of the strength up to excitation energies around 20-30 MeV.

# Quenching of Gamow-Teller strengths and two particle -- two hole configurations

## Key claim

The inclusion of two particle - two hole (2p2h) configurations within the charge-exchange subtracted second random-phase approximation (CE-SSRPA) provides Gamow-Teller strengths that are significantly more quenched than in other theoretical models, leading to better agreement with experimental measurements without requiring ad-hoc quenching factors in the transition operator. The authors claim that within their model, quenching effects are mainly driven by 2p2h configurations and that the role of a two-body contribution in the transition operator is less important than in the coupled-cluster approach, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of one-body transition operators and Skyrme functionals.

## Method

The authors applied the charge-exchange subtracted second random-phase approximation (CE-SSRPA) based on the Skyrme SGII effective interaction, performing Hartree-Fock calculations as a starting point. The cutoff on one particle-one hole (1p1h) configurations was set at 60 MeV, while the cutoff on 2p2h configurations was chosen between 40 and 50 MeV. For lighter nuclei (48Ca, 14C, 22O), a full inversion of the A22 matrix in the subtraction procedure was required, while for heavier nuclei (90Zr, 132Sn) a diagonal approximation in the matrix to invert was found to be sufficient. The GT transition operators used were one-body operators, and the discrete spectra were folded with a Lorentzian having a width of 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca, the SSRPAFF (full calculation) spectrum showed visible improvement over RPA, with the cumulative sum up to 20 MeV significantly reduced compared to RPA.
- For 90Zr, the SSRPA integrated strength at 25 MeV was 22.3, compared to the RPA value near 30 and the experimental value slightly above 20.
- For 132Sn, the SSRPA integrated strength at 20 MeV was approximately 10 units lower than the particle-vibration coupling prediction of 80 from Niu et al.
- For 90Zr, the experimental GT- strength integrated up to 50 MeV was 29.3, very close to the full Ikeda sum rule value of 3(N-Z) = 30, while the GT+ strength integrated to the same energy was 2.9.
- For 14C and 22O, the RPA cumulative sums at 10 MeV were above the 70-80% intervals of the reduced sum rules from Ekstrom et al., while SSRPA values were located below these intervals.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CE-SSRPA analysis of 48Ca and 78Ni (Gambacurta 2020) to additional closed-shell and closed-subshell nuclei across the nuclear chart. The results are compared with ab-initio coupled-cluster predictions for 14C and 22O from Ekstrom et al., where two-body currents were included in the transition operator. The SSRPA results show better agreement with experimental data than other beyond-mean-field approaches, including the relativistic particle-vibration coupling calculations of Robin et al. and the non-relativistic particle-vibration coupling of Niu et al. The work enables future applications of the CE-SSRPA model to nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay, where using the bare value of the axial-vector coupling constant gA would be more reliable if ad-hoc quenching procedures are not required.
