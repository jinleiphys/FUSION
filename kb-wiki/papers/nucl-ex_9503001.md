# Lifetime Measurements in 120Xe

> Lifetimes for the lowest three transitions in the nucleus $^{120}$Xe have been measured using the Recoil Distance Technique. Our data indicate that the lifetime for the $2_{1}^{+} \to 0_{1}^{+}$ transition is more than a factor of two lower than the previously adopted value and is in keeping with more recent measurements performed on this nucleus. The theoretical implications of this discrepancy and the possible reason for the erroneous earlier results are discussed. All measured lifetimes in $^{120}$Xe, as well as the systematics of the lifetimes of the 2$_{1}^{+}$ states in Xe isotopes, are compared with predictions of various models. The available data are best described by the Fermion Dynamic Symmetry Model (FDSM).

## Key claim

The lifetime of the \(2_{1}^{+} \to 0_{1}^{+}\) transition in \(^{120}\)Xe is measured to be 64(5) ps, more than a factor of two lower than the previously adopted value of 122 ps, and this discrepancy is attributed to a long-lived (approximately 3500 ps) side-feeding transition into the \(6^{+}\) or higher state that was not properly accounted for in earlier measurements.

## Method

Lifetimes were measured using the Recoil Distance Method (RDM) with the \(^{106}\)Pd(\(^{18}\)O, 4n)\(^{120}\)Xe reaction at 70 MeV beam energy. Gamma-ray spectra were recorded in singles mode using four Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors at 24 target-to-stopper distances ranging from 18 \(\mu\)m to 2600 \(\mu\)m. Decay curves were fitted with exponential functions using the computer code LIFETIME, with corrections applied for solid angle changes, relativistic motion, angular distribution attenuation, ion slowing in the stopper, detector efficiency, internal conversion, and cascade feeding from both observed yrast transitions and unobserved side-feeding.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the \(2_{1}^{+} \to 0_{1}^{+}\) transition in \(^{120}\)Xe: \(\tau = 64 \pm 5\) ps, with \(B(E2) = 0.36 \pm 0.03\) \(e^{2}b^{2}\)
- Lifetime of the \(4^{+} \to 2^{+}\) transition: \(\tau = 8.1 \pm 0.8\) ps, with \(B(E2) = 0.42 \pm 0.004\) \(e^{2}b^{2}\)
- Lifetime of the \(6^{+} \to 4^{+}\) transition: \(\tau = 2.5 \pm 0.3\) ps, with \(B(E2) = 0.44 \pm 0.005\) \(e^{2}b^{2}\)
- Previously adopted lifetime for the \(2_{1}^{+} \to 0_{1}^{+}\) transition from Kutschera et al. (1972): \(\tau = 124 \pm 15\) ps
- Long-lived side-feeding transition into the \(6^{+}\) or higher state: \(\tau \sim 3500\) ps
- Target thickness: 762 \(\mu\)g/cm\(^{2}\) \(^{106}\)Pd; stopper thickness: 2.9 mg/cm\(^{2}\) Au; shortest target-stopper distance: 18 \(\mu\)m

## Context

This work resolves a factor-of-two discrepancy in the lifetime of the \(2_{1}^{+}\) state in \(^{120}\)Xe between earlier measurements by Kutschera et al. (1972) and Chaudhury et al. (1985) (both giving \(\tau \sim 122\) ps) and more recent results from Dewald et al. (1989, 1990) at Koln. The new measurement agrees with the Koln results and with a contemporaneous measurement by Mantica et al. (1992) using fast-timing coincidence. The corrected \(B(E2; 0^{+} \to 2^{+})\) value removes the previously observed "saturation" effect in Xe isotopes near mid-shell (N=66) that was described by Raman et al. (1987, 1991) and explained by IBM-2 with Pauli factors from Otsuka et al. (1990). The data are now best described by the Fermion Dynamic Symmetry Model (FDSM) rather than IBM-2, and this enables further tests of FDSM predictions for transitional nuclei in the A=120 region.
