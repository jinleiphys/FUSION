# Sum rule study for Double Gamow-Teller states

> We study the sum rules of double Gamow-Teller (DGT) excitations through double spin-isospin operator $(σt_-)^2$. In general, $2^+$ states in the granddaughter nuclei have dominant transition strength in DGT excitations and $0^+$ states are weak, except in $T=1$ mother nuclei in which $0^+$ strength is competitive with 2$^+$ strength. The isospin selection of DGT is also discussed among 5 possible isospin states in granddaughter nuclei. A possibility to extract the unit cross section for the DGT transition strength is pointed out in the $(σt_-)^2$ excitation of double isobaric analog state (DIAS) in $T=1$ nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper derives sum rules for double Gamow-Teller (DGT) excitations and shows that, while 2+ states generally dominate the DGT strength, 0+ strength becomes competitive with 2+ strength specifically in T=1 mother nuclei, and that the double isobaric analog state (DIAS) excited via the double spin-isospin operator may provide a way to extract the unit cross section for DGT transitions in these T=1 nuclei.

## Method

The authors derived sum rules for DGT transitions to Jπ=0+ and Jπ=2+ final states using the double spin-isospin operator (σt−)2, including the spin sum rule term Σ·Σ. They evaluated these sum rules for ten nuclei using shell model calculations in p-shell (CKII interaction), sd-shell (USDB interaction), and pf-shell (GXPF1A interaction) model spaces, as well as single-particle values for 90Zr. They also calculated the DGT transition strength to the DIAS state and the isospin selection amplitudes for the five possible isospin states in granddaughter nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca, the J=0 sum rule value is 135.5 and the J=2 sum rule value is 501.2 (with upper/lower limits of 144 and 480 respectively without the spin sum rule term)
- For 6He (T=1), the J=0 sum rule is 12.0 and the J=2 sum rule is 0.0, showing 0+ strength dominates over 2+ strength
- For 14C (T=1), the J=0 sum rule is 8.98 and the J=2 sum rule is 7.55, showing competitive 0+ and 2+ strengths
- The DIAS(DGT) strength for 14C is 4.56, comparable to the J=0 sum rule of 8.98
- For T=1 mother nuclei, the isospin probability distribution gives 60% to T''=T, 33.3% to T''=T+1, and 6.7% to T''=T+2
- For T=22 mother nuclei, 91.1% of DGT strength goes to T''=T-2 states

## Context

This work extends earlier sum rule studies of single Gamow-Teller transitions (which are proportional to 3(N-Z)) and prior DGT sum rule calculations by Vogel (1988) and Zheng/Zamick (1989), who calculated only the J=0 channel and obtained values a factor 3 larger due to lack of exact angular momentum projection. The results provide theoretical guidance for planned heavy-ion double charge exchange experiments at RIKEN RIBF using the (12C, 12Be(0+2)) reaction at 250 MeV/u, and enable extraction of unit cross sections for DGT strength in T=1 nuclei like 14C and 18O where the DIAS state may be clearly distinguished from other DGT states.
