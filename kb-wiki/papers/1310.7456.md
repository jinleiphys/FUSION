# Error Analysis of Nuclear Matrix Elements

> We estimate the expected errors of nuclear matrix elements coming from the uncertainty on the NN interaction. We use a coarse grained (GR) interaction fitted to NN scattering data, with several prescriptions for the long-part of the interaction, including one pion exchange and chiral two-pion exchange interactions.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that errors in nuclear matrix elements arising from NN interaction uncertainties are moderate but not negligible, setting a theoretical limit on the precision achievable in nuclear physics calculations. The abstract glosses over the finding that systematic errors are generally more than twice as large as statistical errors in most observables, and that low-energy fits produce softer potentials but increase theoretical errors.

## Method

The authors used a coarse-grained (GR) NN interaction parameterized as a sum of Dirac delta functions with a resolution scale of 0.6 fm, fitted to NN scattering data below pion production threshold. They extracted statistical errors from a partial wave analysis yielding χ²/d.o.f. = 1.06, and estimated systematic errors by comparing phase shifts from several high-quality potentials (including those of Machleidt (1996), Stoks (1993), Wiringa (1995), Friar (1993), and Gross (2008)). They then propagated these uncertainties to nuclear matrix elements computed on harmonic oscillator wave functions, and separately investigated the effect of including chiral two-pion exchange (χTPE) by reducing the cutoff radius to 1.8 fm.

## Key numbers

- The coarse-grained potential uses a resolution scale Δr = 0.6 fm and a cutoff radius r_c = 3 fm for the OPE prescription.
- The partial wave analysis of NN scattering data yielded χ²/d.o.f. = 1.06 for the GR potential.
- The estimated error in nuclear binding energy per particle is ΔB/A = 0.1 – 0.4 MeV across a range of mass numbers.
- When χTPE is included with r_c = 1.8 fm, the χ²/d.o.f. value increases to 1.10.
- For low-energy fits with T_LAB ≤ 125 MeV, the resolution scale becomes Δr ∼ 1.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous error analysis of nuclear two-body forces based on coarse graining (Navarro Perez et al., 2012, 2013) by propagating uncertainties to nuclear matrix elements and binding energies. It contrasts with typical shell model calculations that do not quantify input uncertainties, and it enables a priori estimates of the accuracy needed in many-body calculations. The inclusion of chiral two-pion exchange interactions connects to modern chiral perturbation theory studies (Epelbaum, 2009; Machleidt, 2011) and to optimized chiral potentials fitted to low-energy data (Ekstrom et al., 2013).
