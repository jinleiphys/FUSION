# Probing the Nuclear Symmetry Energy with Heavy-Ion Reactions Induced by Neutron-Rich Nuclei

> Heavy-ion reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei provide a unique means to investigate the equation of state of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, especially the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. In particular, recent analyses of the isospin diffusion data in heavy-ion reactions have already put a stringent constraint on the nuclear symmetry energy around the nuclear matter saturation density. We review this exciting result and discuss its implications on nuclear effective interactions and the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei. In addition, we also review the theoretical progress on probing the high density behaviors of the nuclear symmetry energy in heavy-ion reactions induced by high energy radioactive beams.

## Key claim

The paper reviews that recent analyses of isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions have placed a stringent constraint on the nuclear symmetry energy around saturation density, specifically finding that a density-dependent symmetry energy of approximately \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho) \approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.69}\) best reproduces the data, though this constraint applies primarily at sub-normal densities and the high-density behavior remains largely undetermined.

## Method

The authors review the IBUU04 isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model based on the Boltzmann-Uhling-Uhlenbeck equation, which uses a single-nucleon potential derived from a modified Gogny effective interaction (MDI) with a parameter \(x\) that adjusts the stiffness of the symmetry energy. The model includes nucleons, \(\Delta\) and \(N^{\ast}\) resonances, pions, and isospin-dependent dynamics, with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections estimated via the effective mass scaling model. The analysis of isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions is performed by comparing experimental data with IBUU04 simulations using different values of \(x\) to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is known to be around 30 MeV from the empirical liquid-drop mass formula.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter at \(\rho_0\) is determined to be \(231 \pm 5\) MeV from nuclear giant monopole resonances.
- A density-dependent symmetry energy of \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho) \approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.69}\) best reproduces both isospin diffusion and isoscaling data as well as the neutron-skin thickness in \(^{208}\)Pb.
- For the MDI interaction with \(x=1\), the slope parameter \(L = 16.4\) MeV and curvature parameter \(K_{\text{sym}} = -270.4\) MeV; for \(x=0\), \(L = 62.1\) MeV and \(K_{\text{sym}} = -88.6\) MeV; for \(x=-1\), \(L = 107.4\) MeV and \(K_{\text{sym}} = 94.1\) MeV; for \(x=-2\), \(L = 153.0\) MeV and \(K_{\text{sym}} = 276.3\) MeV.
- The isospin-dependent part of the incompressibility \(K_{\text{asy}}\) is constrained to \(-566 \pm 1350 < K_{\text{asy}} < 139 \pm 1617\) MeV from earlier giant monopole resonance studies.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the equation of state of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, particularly the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which has been studied using many-body theories such as Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF), Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF), and variational methods. The results from isospin diffusion data (Tsa04, Che05a, LiBA05c) provide the best phenomenological constraints on the symmetry energy at sub-normal densities and are consistent with neutron-skin thickness measurements in \(^{208}\)Pb (Ste05b, LiBA06a, Che05b) and isoscaling analyses (She07). Future high-energy radioactive beams will enable determination of the symmetry energy at supra-normal densities, which remains an open question.
