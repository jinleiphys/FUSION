# A new evaluation of the Baldin sum rule

> The Baldin sum rule for the nucleon has been recalculated at the light of the most recent photoabsorption cross section measurements. The proton value (alpha + beta)_p =13.69 +/- 0.14 is smaller but consistent with the one usually quoted in literature. However, the value for the neutron (alpha + β)_n =14.40 +/- 0.66 turns out to be three standard deviations away from the previously calculated one.

## Key claim

The Baldin sum rule for the nucleon has been recalculated using the most recent photoabsorption cross section measurements; the proton value is smaller but consistent with the previously quoted value, while the neutron value is three standard deviations away from the earlier calculation.

## Method

The authors divided the integration domain for both proton and neutron into four energy regions (threshold, resonance, high-energy, and asymptotic) and evaluated the integral of the photoabsorption cross section weighted by the inverse square of the photon energy. For the proton, they fitted 138 data points from Daresbury and Mainz using six Breit-Wigner resonances plus a smooth background parametrization, and used parametrizations from the Particle Data Group and the Donnachie-Landshoff model for the high-energy and asymptotic regions. For the neutron, they derived the cross section from deuteron data by subtracting the proton contribution under specific assumptions about resonance parameters and coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Proton polarizability sum: (alpha + beta)_p = 13.69 +/- 0.14 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Neutron polarizability sum: (alpha + beta)_n = 14.40 +/- 0.66 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Previous proton value from Damashek and Gilman (1970): (alpha + beta)_p = 14.2 +/- 0.3
- Previous neutron value from L'vov and Petrun'kin (1979): (alpha + beta)_n = 15.8 +/- 0.5
- Contribution to proton sum from threshold region: 1.25 +/- 0.02
- Contribution to proton sum from resonance region: 11.71 +/- 0.13
- Contribution to proton sum from high-energy region: 0.72 +/- 0.03
- Contribution to neutron sum from resonance region: 11.95 +/- 0.66
- Contribution to neutron sum from threshold region: 1.86 +/- 0.02

## Context

This work updates the Baldin sum rule calculations originally performed by Damashek and Gilman (1970) for the proton and by L'vov and Petrun'kin (1979) for the neutron, using improved photoabsorption cross section data from Mainz and Daresbury. The new results bring the proton and neutron values closer together, supporting the claim by Maiani and Preziosi that no isotopic effect should be expected for the sum of electric and magnetic polarizabilities. The findings enable more precise constraints on nucleon polarizabilities extracted from low-energy Compton scattering experiments and provide a test of chiral perturbation theory predictions, with the O(q^3) prediction of 13.3 being consistent with both the new proton and neutron values.
