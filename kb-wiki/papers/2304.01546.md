# Nuclear mass table in density functional approach inspired by neutron-star observations

> Background: Nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) approach has been widely used to describe nuclear-matter equations of state (EoS) and properties of finite nuclei. Recent advancements in neutron-star (NS) observations have put constraints on the nuclear EoS. The Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) functional has been then developed to satisfy the NS observations and applied to homogeneous nuclear matter and spherical nuclei. Purpose: We examine the performance of the KIDS functional by calculating the masses and charge radii of even-even nuclei towards the drip lines. Method: The Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov equation is solved by taking into account the axial deformation. Results: The root-mean-square deviation of the binding energy and the charge radius for the KIDS functional is 4.5--5.1 MeV and 0.03--0.04 fm, which is comparable to that for existing EDFs. The emergence and development of nuclear deformation in open-shell nuclei are well described. The location of the neutron drip line is according to the nuclear-matter parameter characterizing the low-mass NS. Conclusions: The NS-observation-inspired EDF offers a reasonable reproduction of the structures of finite nuclei. A future global optimization including more nuclear data will give better accuracy and high predictive power of neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) density functional, constructed primarily from neutron-star observations rather than extensive nuclear data, reproduces finite-nucleus binding energies and charge radii with an accuracy comparable to existing Skyrme functionals (RMSD 4.5–5.1 MeV for binding energy), but is inferior to globally fitted mass models; the location of the neutron drip line correlates with the nuclear-matter parameter η_τ that characterizes low-mass neutron stars.

## Method

The Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov equation was solved using the hfbtho code with axial deformation included, for even-even nuclei with atomic number Z from 8 to 110 and neutron number N from the proton drip line to N = 3Z. Four KIDS functionals (A, B, C, D) were employed, each with 11 parameters: seven adjusted to nuclear saturation properties and the neutron-star radius R_1.4, two fitted to binding energies and charge radii of ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb, and two pairing parameters adjusted to odd-even staggering in ¹⁵⁶Dy. Calculations used N_max = 20 oscillator shells with a quasiparticle energy cutoff at 60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- RMSD of binding energy: 4.50 MeV (KIDS-A), 4.86 MeV (KIDS-B), 4.70 MeV (KIDS-C), 5.08 MeV (KIDS-D), evaluated over 632, 630, 628, and 623 even-even nuclei respectively
- RMSD of charge radius: 0.0323 fm (KIDS-A), 0.0356 fm (KIDS-B), 0.0345 fm (KIDS-C), 0.0384 fm (KIDS-D), evaluated over 344, 344, 343, and 343 nuclei respectively
- Mean R(E) values: 0.011% (KIDS-A), -0.073% (KIDS-B), -0.127% (KIDS-C), -0.211% (KIDS-D), indicating average deviation of calculated binding energy from experiment
- Nuclear matter parameters: incompressibility K_0 = 230–260 MeV, symmetry energy J = 30–33 MeV, slope parameter L = 47–66 MeV, symmetry energy curvature K_τ = -420 to -360 MeV across the four models
- Neutron-star radius constraint: R_1.4 = 11.8–12.5 km (radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star)
- η_τ parameter: 89.8 MeV (KIDS-A), 80.7 MeV (KIDS-B), 78.6 MeV (KIDS-C), 66.0 MeV (KIDS-D), correlating with neutron drip line location

## Context

This work extends the KIDS functional approach, previously applied to homogeneous nuclear matter and spherical nuclei (Gil et al., 2022 on Nd isotopes), to the full nuclear landscape of even-even nuclei. The RMSD of 4.5–5.1 MeV is comparable to the SLy4 model (4.8 MeV) but larger than globally fitted mass models such as UNEDF0 (1.45 MeV) and the DRHBc calculation (0.032 fm for charge radius). The results enable future global optimization of the KIDS functional including more nuclear data to achieve accuracy comparable to existing globally-fitted energy-density functional models, and establish a connection between neutron-star observables and the location of the neutron drip line through the η_τ parameter.
