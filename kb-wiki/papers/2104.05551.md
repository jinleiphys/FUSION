# Interpreting the charge-dependent flow and constraining the chiral magnetic wave with event shape engineering

> The charge asymmetry (Ach) dependence of anisotropic flow serves as an important tool to search for the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) in heavy-ion collisions. However, the background effect, such as the local charge conservation (LCC) entwined with collective flow, has not yet been unambiguously eliminated in the measurement. With the help of two models, the AMPT with initial quadrupole moment and the blast wave (BW) incorporating LCC, we discuss the features of the LCC-induced and the CMW-induced correlations between Ach and the flow. More importantly, we first propose to use the Event Shape Engineering (ESE) technique to distinguish the background and the signal for the CMW study. This method would be highly desirable in the experimental search for the CMW and provides more insights for understanding the charge-dependent collective motion of the quark-gluon plasma.

# Interpreting the charge-dependent flow and constraining the chiral magnetic wave with event shape engineering

## Key claim

The slope of the charge asymmetry ($A_{\rm ch}$) dependence of elliptic flow ($v_2$) induced by local charge conservation (LCC) scales with the magnitude of $v_2$, while the chiral magnetic wave (CMW)-induced slope does not exhibit strong $v_2$ dependence; this difference allows the Event Shape Engineering (ESE) technique to disentangle the LCC background from the CMW signal, provided that the experimental data contain both contributions and the linear fit intercept at zero $v_2$ can be interpreted as the CMW fraction.

## Method

The authors used two models: the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with an artificially imposed initial electric quadrupole moment to simulate the CMW signal, and a blast-wave (BW) model incorporating local charge conservation (LCC) by forcing charged particles to be emitted in pairs with zero net charge at the same spatial point. They computed the $A_{\rm ch}$-$v_2$ correlation slope and the integral three-particle correlator as functions of the average elliptic flow $\langle v_2 \rangle$, using the ESE technique to select events with different flow magnitudes in the AMPT sample and by varying the BW parameter $\rho_2$ directly.

## Key numbers

- In the BW+LCC model, $\rho_2$ values of 0.1, 0.3, and 0.8 correspond to integrated $v_2$ values of 0.02, 0.06, and 0.14 at midrapidity, respectively; the slope of $A_{\rm ch}$-$\Delta v_2$ increases with $\rho_2$.
- In the AMPT with quadrupole moment, switching approximately 3% of total quarks generates a slope comparable to experimental results in semi-central collisions.
- The differential three-particle correlator for the second harmonic in AMPT with quadrupole shows a clear separation between positively and negatively charged flow particles, but no $\Delta\eta$ dependence; the third harmonic correlator is consistent with zero.
- For the ESE analysis, the AMPT+quadrupole results show no $\langle v_2 \rangle$ dependence; a linear fit gives a significantly positive intercept at zero $\langle v_2 \rangle$, while the BW+LCC results are proportional to $\langle v_2 \rangle$ with an intercept consistent with zero for the discrete $\rho_2$ method.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing search for the chiral magnetic wave in heavy-ion collisions, following measurements by ALICE (2016) and STAR (2015) that observed a linear $A_{\rm ch}$-$v_2$ relation, and the CMS (2019) finding of a similar relation in p-Pb collisions and for triangular flow. The authors build on earlier studies showing that LCC entwined with collective flow produces a background (Bzdak 2013, Voloshin 2014, Wu 2021), and adapt the ESE technique previously used to constrain the chiral magnetic effect fraction (CMS 2019) to the CMW problem. The proposed method enables future experimental extraction of the CMW signal fraction and provides a baseline for interpreting the differential three-particle correlator measured by ALICE (2016).
