# Deuteron disintegration by reactor antineutrinos

> The existence of a new interaction involving the electron neutrino and the nucleons, which has received a convincing confirmation through a good agreement between the theoretical and experimental results concerning all observable processes with solar neutrinos, should also inevitably manifest itself in the deuteron disintegration by reactor antineutrinos neutral currents. In this paper, the analytical and numerical characteristics of such a disintegration are presented. The attention is drawn to the problem of finding the neutron registration efficiency, discussed in the preparation of the experiment at the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory and in a number of special studies, to the role of quenching gas in proportional counters with helium-3 and to three performed reactor experiments with heavy water.

# Deuteron disintegration by reactor antineutrinos

## Key claim

The semi-weak interaction involving the electron neutrino and nucleons, previously invoked to explain solar neutrino data, should also produce a measurable contribution to deuteron disintegration by reactor antineutrinos via neutral currents, with a cross-section comparable to the standard electroweak contribution. The author argues that existing reactor experiments show inconsistencies in neutron registration that may obscure this signal, and calls for new experiments modeled on the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory methodology.

## Method

The author calculates cross-sections for deuteron disintegration by reactor antineutrinos via two non-interfering sub-processes: the standard Z-boson exchange (electroweak) and a proposed semi-weak interaction mediated by a massless pseudoscalar boson. Using the semi-weak coupling constant product extracted from solar neutrino data, the author computes integral cross-sections per fission for four reactor isotopes (²³⁵U, ²³⁸U, ²³⁹Pu, ²⁴¹Pu) and averages them over typical VVER-1000 reactor fuel compositions. The resulting event rates are compared with published results from three past reactor experiments (Savannah River Plant, Bugey-5, and Rovno), and the neutron registration efficiency problems in those experiments are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Semi-weak coupling constant product: g_{νe ps} g_{N ps} / (4π) = (3.2 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁵, extracted from solar neutrino data with n₀ = 11 effective collisions
- Deuteron binding energy B = 2.2246 MeV; neutron-proton mass difference δ = -1.2933 MeV
- Cross-section for Z-boson exchange sub-process at ω = 8.0 MeV: σ_Z^{ν̄_e} = 5.320 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²; for semi-weak sub-process at same energy: σ_ps = 3.315 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²
- Weighted integral cross-section for semi-weak sub-process (central value): Σᵣ_ps = 0.954 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fission with total relative uncertainty ±21%
- Weighted integral cross-section for combined neutral-current processes: Σᵣ_{Z+ps} = 1.25 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fission with total relative uncertainty ±15%
- Weighted integral cross-section for charged-current process: Σᵣ_cc = 0.099 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fission with ±4.0% uncertainty
- Neutron capture cross-section on ³He: σ = (847.5 ± 1.5) × (E/eV)⁻¹/² barns for E < 11 eV; at thermal energy 0.0253 eV, σ = 5300 barns
- Neutron registration efficiency at SNO: 0.211 ± 0.007 (²⁴Na method) and 0.210 ± 0.003 (Monte Carlo with ²⁵²Cf/AmBe sources)
- Ratio R₂ₙ/R₁ₙ in past experiments: 0.046 ± 0.038 (Savannah River), 0.064 ± 0.011 (Bugey), 0.215 ± 0.050 (Rovno)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Slad, 2015) of a semi-weak interaction to explain solar neutrino observations across five experiment types (³⁷Cl, ⁷¹Ga, νe scattering, and both charged- and neutral-current deuteron disintegration at SNO). The paper directly addresses the three past reactor experiments on deuteron disintegration: Savannah River Plant (Reines et al., 1976, 1979), Bugey-5 (Cavaignac et al., 1984), and Rovno (Korovkin et al., 1984, 1988), which the author argues contain unresolved methodological issues. The analysis enables future experiments to test the semi-weak hypothesis by comparing neutral-current deuteron disintegration rates with the predictions of the standard electroweak model, using the SNO's established neutron detection techniques (including ³He proportional counters with CF₄ quenching gas) as a methodological benchmark.
