# Recent results on Light Flavor from STAR

> These proceedings present an overview of the recent results on light flavor by the STAR experiment at RHIC.

## Key claim

These proceedings present an overview of recent STAR light-flavor results, but the measurements are described as showing trends that "may bear important implications" or "call for further studies," rather than providing definitive conclusions; the possible Chiral Magnetic Effect signal is estimated to be small, within 1-2 sigma from zero.

## Method

STAR used the large-acceptance detector at mid-rapidity (|η| < ~1) at RHIC to measure light-flavor hadrons, including strange particles, light nuclei, resonances, and dileptons, across multiple collision systems (Au+Au, p+Au, d+Au) and beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. The Beam Energy Scan program and fixed-target mode extended coverage to baryon chemical potentials μB ≈ 420-720 MeV. Analyses included flow decorrelations using the Forward Meson Spectrometer, cumulants of net-proton and net-Λ distributions, coalescence parameters for light nuclei, nuclear modification factors RCP, and novel data-driven methods for the Chiral Magnetic Effect correlator Δγ.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal flow decorrelation parameters r2 and r3 measured in 5-10% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV show a stronger decrease with normalized rapidity than LHC results.
- Net-proton cumulant ratio C6/C2 for central Au+Au collisions at 54.4 GeV is positive, while at 200 GeV it is negative, both with large uncertainties.
- Strange hadron RCP shows no suppression of K0s up to pT = 3.5 GeV/c, and particle-type dependence of RCP is smaller at √sNN ≤ 11.5 GeV.
- Coalescence parameter B2 for light nuclei first decreases then increases with collision energy, with a minimum around 20 GeV.
- Neutron density fluctuation Δn extracted from light nuclei shows a non-monotonic behavior with collision energy, with a peak around 20 GeV.
- Hypertriton binding energy and mass difference between hypertriton and antihypertriton provide the first CPT symmetry test in light hypernuclei, with no deviation observed.
- Global Λ polarization measured across BES energies shows positive polarization of order a few percent, increasing with decreasing collision energy, with systematic splitting between Λ and Λ̄.
- Possible CME signal extracted via invariant mass method in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions is estimated to be within 1-2 σ from zero.

## Context

This proceedings paper summarizes STAR results presented at the Strangeness in Quark Matter 2019 conference, extending the Beam Energy Scan program (BES-I) that collected data from 7.7 to 200 GeV starting in 2010. The measurements of net-proton cumulants up to sixth order build on prior lattice QCD calculations and O(4) scaling functions, while the longitudinal flow decorrelation results provide new constraints on (3+1)D hydrodynamic calculations that cannot simultaneously describe r2 and r3 at both RHIC and LHC. The light nuclei measurements and strange hadron RCP results point to the beam energy region below 19.6 GeV for further investigation of the deconfinement phase transition, and the CME analysis using novel data-driven methods (invariant mass method from Zhao (2019) and other approaches) indicates that the possible signal is small, motivating future isobar runs (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) taken in 2018.
