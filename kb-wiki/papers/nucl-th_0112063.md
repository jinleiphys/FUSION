# Considerations on rescattering effects for threshold photo- and electro-production of $π^0$ on deuteron

> We show that for the S-state $π^0$-production in processes $γ+d\to d+π^0$ and $e^-+d\to e^-+d+π^0$ the rescattering effects due to the transition: $ γ+d\to p+p+π^-$ (or $n+n+π^+)\to d+π^0$ are cancelled out due to the Pauli principle. The large values for these effects predicted in the past may result from the fact that the spin structure of the corresponding matrix element and the necessary antisymmetrization induced by the presence of identical protons (or neutrons) in the intermediate state was not taken into account accurately. One of the important consequences of these considerations is that $π^0$ photo- and electro-production on deuteron near threshold can bring direct information about elementary neutron amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for S-state π⁰ production in γ+d→d+π⁰ and e⁻+d→e⁻+d+π⁰, rescattering effects from the intermediate processes γ+d→p+p+π⁻ (or n+n+π⁺)→d+π⁰ cancel exactly due to the Pauli principle, contrary to earlier predictions of large rescattering effects. This cancellation means that π⁰ photo- and electro-production on deuteron near threshold can provide direct information about elementary neutron amplitudes, provided the Pauli principle is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin structure and parity constraints of the threshold amplitudes for γ+d→d+π⁰, showing that the intermediate ppπ⁻ (or nnπ⁺) system with ℓ₁=ℓ₂=0 (threshold conditions) must have 𝒥ᴾ=0⁻, while the final dπ⁰ system requires 𝒥ᴾ=1⁻ for S-wave production. They demonstrate that the standard rescattering diagrams (where a pion photoproduced on one nucleon scatters from another) must be summed with diagrams where the pion scatters from the same nucleon to satisfy the Pauli principle, and that this sum cancels for the imaginary parts of the threshold amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for γ+d→d+π⁰ is E_γ = 139.8 MeV
- The threshold energy for γ+d→p+p+π⁻ is E_γ = 145.8 MeV
- The threshold energy for γ+d→n+n+π⁺ is E_γ = 148.5 MeV
- The extracted threshold amplitude from deuteron photoproduction data is E_d = (−1.45 ± 0.09) × 10⁻³/m_π
- The experimental value of E_{0+}^{pπ⁰} = −1.13 × 10⁻³/m_π (from Be97)
- For ChPT predictions: E_{0+}^{pπ⁰} = −1.16 × 10⁻³/m_π and E_{0+}^{nπ⁰} = 2.13 × 10⁻³/m_π, giving a ratio |F(γd→d_sπ⁰)|²/|F(γd→dπ⁰)|² ≈ 10
- For dispersion relations approach (Ha97): E_{0+}^{pπ⁰} = −1.22 × 10⁻³/m_π and E_{0+}^{nπ⁰} = 1.19 × 10⁻³/m_π, giving a ratio |F(γd→d_sπ⁰)|²/|F(γd→dπ⁰)|² ≈ 6450

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem of rescattering effects in coherent π⁰ photoproduction on the deuteron, which had been predicted to be large by earlier calculations (Ko77, Bo78, Fa80) due to the fact that |E_{0+}^{nπ⁺}| ≈ 20 |E_{0+}^{pπ⁰}|. The authors show these earlier calculations violated the Pauli principle by not properly antisymmetrizing the intermediate state with identical nucleons. The result explains the experimentally observed absence of a unitary cusp in the energy dependence of the E_{0+} amplitude for γ+d→d+π⁰ (Be98), which contrasts with the cusp seen in γ+p→p+π⁰ (Be97, Fu96). The work also connects to recent electro-production data from the A1 collaboration at MAMI (Ew01) at Q²=0.1 GeV², which showed no evidence of the cusp that would be expected if rescattering were important. This enables model-independent extraction of neutron π⁰ production amplitudes from deuteron data, which is essential for testing Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions (VB96) and resolving discrepancies with heavy baryon ChPT calculations (Ber98) for electro-production.
