# The role of Glauber Exchange in Soft Collinear Effective Theory and the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov Equation

> In soft collinear effective theory (SCET) the interaction between high energy quarks moving in opposite directions involving momentum transfer much smaller than the center-of-mass energy is described by the Glauber interaction operator which has two-dimensional Coulomb-like behavior. Here, we determine this $n$-$\bar{n}$ collinear Glauber interaction operator and consider its renormalization properties at one loop. At this order a rapidity divergence appears which gives rise to an infrared divergent (IR) rapidity anomalous dimension commonly called the gluon Regge trajectory. We then go on to consider the forward quark scattering cross section in SCET. The emission of real soft gluons from the Glauber interaction gives rise to the Lipatov vertex. Squaring and adding the real and virtual amplitudes results in a cancelation of IR divergences, however the rapidity divergence remains. We introduce a rapidity counterterm to cancel the rapidity divergence, and derive a rapidity renormalization group equation which is the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov Equation. This connects Glauber interactions with the emergence of Regge behavior in SCET.

## Key claim

Glauber interactions between collinear quarks moving in opposite directions and Regge behavior are intimately connected in SCET; the rapidity renormalization group equation that resums large rapidity logarithms from these Glauber interactions is the leading-logarithmic Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) equation, but this analysis is perturbative and only a first step toward a comprehensive all-orders treatment.

## Method

The author matches QCD forward quark-quark scattering onto SCET by expanding the QCD amplitude in the forward region (small momentum transfer t relative to center-of-mass energy s), identifying the n-anti-n collinear Glauber interaction operator. The operator is renormalized at one loop, where rapidity divergences appear and are regulated using the formalism of Chiu (2011, 2012). Real soft gluon emission from the Glauber interaction is computed to derive the Lipatov vertex, and the virtual and real amplitudes are combined to calculate the NLO forward scattering cross section. A rapidity counterterm is introduced to cancel the rapidity divergence, yielding a rapidity renormalization group equation that is identified as the BFKL equation.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level matching gives the Glauber operator coefficient as -8 pi alpha_s(mu) / (k_perp^2), with the operator reproducing the QCD amplitude in the forward region.
- The one-loop rapidity divergent integral I(k_perp) contains a term proportional to (1/eta) ln(k_perp^2 / m_g^2) when regulated with a gluon mass m_g.
- The leading-log rapidity anomalous dimension is (alpha_s N_c / pi^2) times the kernel [1/(k_perp - k'_perp)^2 - (1/2) delta^(2)(k_perp - k'_perp) integral d^2 q_perp (k_perp^2)/(q_perp^2 (q_perp - k_perp)^2)].
- The solution of the BFKL equation gives a cross section sigma ~ s^(alpha_p - 1) with the pomeron intercept alpha_p = 1 + (4 alpha_s(mu) N_c / pi) ln 2.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to include Glauber interactions in SCET by Liu (2008) and Bauer (2010), and addresses the concern raised by Donoghue (2009) that Regge behavior appeared to fall outside SCET's organizing scheme. The paper connects Glauber interactions between n and anti-n collinear quarks to the BFKL equation of Fadin (1975) and Balitsky (1978), demonstrating that the rapidity RGE in SCET reproduces the leading-logarithmic BFKL evolution. This enables future work toward an all-orders treatment of Glauber interactions in SCET and a non-perturbative understanding of Regge behavior.
