# Comprehensive analysis of the wave function of a hadronic resonance and its compositeness

> We develop a theoretical framework to investigate the two-body composite structure of a resonance as well as a bound state from its wave function. For this purpose, we introduce both one-body bare states and two-body scattering states, and define the compositeness as a fraction of the contribution of the two-body wave function to the normalization of the total wave function. Writing down explicitly the wave function for a resonance state obtained with a general separable interaction, we formulate the compositeness in terms of the position of the resonance pole, the residue of the scattering amplitude at the pole and the derivative of the Green function of the free two-body scattering system. At the same time, our formulation provides the elementariness expressed with the resonance properties and the two-body effective interaction, and confirms the sum rule showing that the summation of the compositeness and elementariness gives unity. In this formulation the Weinberg's relation for the scattering length and effective range can be derived in the weak binding limit. The extension to the resonance states is performed with the Gamow vector, and a relativistic formulation is also established. As its applications, we study the compositeness of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance and the light scalar and vector mesons described with refined amplitudes in coupled-channel models with interactions up to the next to leading order in chiral perturbation theory. We find that $Λ(1405)$ and $f_{0}(980)$ are dominated by the $\bar{K} N$ and $K \bar{K}$ composite states, respectively, while the vector mesons $ρ(770)$ and $K^{\ast} (892)$ are elementary. We also briefly discuss the compositeness of $N (1535)$ and $Λ(1670)$ obtained in a leading-order chiral unitary approach.

## Key claim

The paper develops a framework to quantify the "compositeness" of a hadronic resonance (the fraction of its wave function coming from two-body scattering states) and its "elementariness" (the fraction from one-body bare states), showing that these quantities can be extracted from the pole position and residue of the scattering amplitude without detailed knowledge of the two-body interaction, provided the loop function (Green function of the free two-body system) is fixed.

## Method

The authors introduce a Hamiltonian with both one-body bare states and two-body scattering states, assume a separable interaction, and solve the Schrodinger equation analytically for bound states and resonances. They define compositeness as the integrated squared two-body wave function and elementariness as the squared overlap with the bare state, then express both in terms of the scattering amplitude's pole residue, the derivative of the loop function, and (for elementariness) the derivative of the effective interaction. The formalism is extended to coupled channels, multiple bare states, resonance states via Gamow vectors, and relativistic covariant formulations.

- The bound state condition is given by det[1 - v^eff(M_B) G(M_B)] = 0 for coupled channels
- The residue of the scattering amplitude at the pole is expressed as |g|^2 = -1 / [dG/dE + (1/(v^eff)^2) dv^eff/dE] evaluated at E = M_B

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronic molecular structure using field renormalization constants (Weinberg 1965) and wave function analyses (Aceti 2012, Gamermann 2009, Yamagata-Sekihara 2010) by explicitly including one-body bare states that were omitted in those prior approaches. The formalism enables quantitative classification of hadronic resonances as either composite (molecular) or elementary (compact) states, and is applied to resonances described by chiral coupled-channel models with next-to-leading order interactions. The results for N(1535) and Lambda(1670) are briefly discussed using a leading-order chiral unitary approach, indicating open questions about their structure.
