# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering Observables in Large-$N_c$ QCD

> Nucleon-nucleon scattering observables are considered in the context of the large $N_c$ limit of QCD for initial states with moderately high momenta ($p \sim N_c$). The scattering is studied in the framework of the time-dependent mean-field approximation. We focus on the dependence of those observables on the spin and isospin of the initial state which may be computed using time-dependent mean-field theory. We show that, up to corrections, all such observables must be invariant under simultaneous spin and isospin flips ({\it i.e.} rotations through $π/2$ in both spin and isospin) acting on either particle. All observables of this class obtained from spin unpolarized measurements must be isospin independent up to $1/N_c$ corrections. Moreover, it can be shown that the leading correction is of relative order $1/N_c^2$ rather than $1/N_c$.

# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering Observables in Large-\(N_c\) QCD

## Key claim

All nucleon-nucleon scattering observables that can be computed via time-dependent mean-field theory (TDMFT) in the large-\(N_c\) limit, for initial momenta \(p \sim N_c\), must be invariant under simultaneous spin and isospin flips (rotations through \(\pi/2\) in both spin and isospin) acting on either particle, up to \(1/N_c\) corrections. The leading correction to this spin-isospin structure is of relative order \(1/N_c^2\) rather than \(1/N_c\), because spin and isospin operators enter only in pairs when they appear.

## Method

The authors analyze nucleon-nucleon scattering in the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD using the time-dependent mean-field approximation, following Witten's argument that baryon-baryon interactions are of order \(N_c\) and properly described by TDMFT. They express scattering observables as integrals over impact parameter \(\vec{b}\) and hedgehog orientation parameters \(A_1, A_2\) (elements of SU(2) describing the collective rotations of each baryon), weighted by the squared modulus of Wigner \(D\)-matrices that encode the initial spin and isospin projections. The key step is showing that the large moment of inertia of the hedgehogs (of order \(N_c\)) makes the leading-order operator independent of spin and isospin, so the only spin-isospin dependence comes from the collective wave functions, leading to the symmetry constraint via the property \(|D^{1/2}_{m,n}(A)|^2 = |D^{1/2}_{-m,-n}(A)|^2\).

## Key numbers

- The baryon mass is of order \(N_c\) and the baryon size is of order unity (no explicit numerical value given).
- The moment of inertia \(\mathcal{I}\) of the hedgehog baryons is of order \(N_c\) (i.e., \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\)).
- The interaction time for two baryons to substantially overlap is of order \(N_c^0\) (i.e., order unity in \(N_c\) scaling).
- The characteristic rotational period of the hedgehogs is of order \(N_c\), which is much longer than the interaction time of order unity.
- The leading correction to the spin-isospin structure is of relative order \(1/N_c^2\) rather than \(1/N_c\).

## Context

This work extends Witten's (1979) analysis of baryon-baryon interactions in the large-\(N_c\) limit, which argued that the interaction strength is of order \(N_c\) and the proper framework is TDMFT. The authors build on the hedgehog mean-field picture for single baryons and the collective quantization method of Cohen and Broniowski (1986). The paper provides model-independent constraints on spin-isospin dependence of certain scattering observables (such as semi-inclusive baryon differential cross sections) that are accessible in TDMFT, while noting that elastic cross sections and total cross sections are not calculable in this framework. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on semi-inclusive differential cross sections, which the authors note would be very interesting to test the predicted spin-isospin structure.
