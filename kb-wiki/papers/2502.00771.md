# The nucleon structure from an AdS/QCD model in the Veneziano limit

> We employ the VQCD model, a holographic approach that dynamically simulates essential QCD characteristics, including linear mass spectra, confinement, asymptotic freedom, and magnetic charge screening, while incorporating quark flavor effects. Using this model, we first calculate the proton mass spectrum and the wave function, incorporating anomalous dimensions to refine our results. Next, we compute the proton structure functions across a range of Bjorken $x$ values using consistent parameters. Furthermore, we derive the proton electromagnetic form factor by solving the electromagnetic field's motion equation, accounting for background effects, and demonstrate qualitative consistency with results from free electromagnetic fields coupled to fermions. Finally, we calculate the gravitational form factors by introducing an effective graviton mass $m$ arising from chiral symmetry breaking and the proton energy-momentum tensor. Our calculations yield results that are in excellent agreement with experimental data and lattice QCD computations, validating the VQCD model as a robust tool for studying proton properties.

## Key claim

The VQCD model, a holographic approach incorporating quark flavor effects in the Veneziano limit, can simultaneously calculate the proton mass spectrum, structure functions, electromagnetic form factors, and gravitational form factors with results that agree well with experimental data and lattice QCD computations, provided that the five-dimensional mass and effective coupling constants are adjusted separately for different physical processes.

## Method

The authors solved Einstein's equations and equations of motion for the VQCD model, which combines an improved holographic QCD (IHQCD) action for gluons with a tachyon DBI action for quark flavors, using infrared asymptotic expansion with parameter c=0.25 and lambda_0=58pi^2. They then solved a Schrodinger-like equation for the proton as a massive spinor field in the deformed AdS_5 metric to obtain the mass spectrum, computed structure functions from deep inelastic scattering amplitudes using the optical theorem, derived electromagnetic form factors by solving the electromagnetic field's equation of motion in the DBI action, and calculated gravitational form factors by introducing an effective graviton mass from chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- Proton ground state mass: m_5 = 0.279 GeV used for mass spectrum calculation, with excited state masses within 3% error of experimental data from PDG
- For structure functions, five-dimensional mass m_5 = 0.229 GeV obtained from ground state mass; effective coupling constants: 2.92 (x=0.56), 2.42 (x=0.65), 1.66 (x=0.75), and 1.08 (x=0.85)
- For gravitational form factor A(Q^2), effective graviton mass squared m^2 = 0.02; for B(Q^2), m^2 = 0.08
- Dirac form factor effective charges in SU(6) approximation: g_R^p = 1, g_L^p = 0, g_R^n = -1/3, g_L^n = 1/3; Pauli form factor coefficients: eta_p = 1.793, eta_n = -1.913

## Context

This work extends previous holographic QCD approaches such as the soft wall model (Karch, 2006) and hard wall model (Polchinski, 2001) by incorporating the Veneziano limit where both N_C and N_f approach infinity with fixed ratio x_f, enabling treatment of quark flavor effects that were neglected in earlier models. The results improve upon the light-front holographic model (Brodsky, 2014) for electromagnetic form factors at high momentum transfer and show better agreement with lattice data (Hackett, 2023) for gravitational form factors than the hard-wall model (Abidin, 2009). This enables future studies of nucleon structure using a single consistent holographic framework that can simultaneously address the proton mass spectrum, parton distributions, charge distributions, and internal force distributions.
