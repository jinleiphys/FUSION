# Hyperspherical few-body model calculation for cascade $Ξ$ exotic hypernuclei with reference to nuclear drip lines

> In this paper, we presented the ground state energies calculated for some single and double cascade hyperon($Ξ^{-}$) hypernuclei. For hypernuclei of the type $_Ξ^{A_c+1}$X we applied $Ξ+^A_c$X two-body model and for hypernuclei of the type $_{ΞΞ}^{A_c+2}$X we adopted $Ξ+Ξ+^A_c$X three-body model. A two-term Gaussian (Isle type) potential with adjustable parameters is chosen as the $Ξ$-nucleus interaction, while a two-term Yukawa-type potential has been chosen for the $ΞΞ$ pair. In the three-body model calculation, we adopted the hyperspherical harmonics expansion (HHE) formalism. The resulting two- and three-body Schr{ö}dinger equations subject to appropriate boundary condition's have been solved numerically to get the ground-state energies and wavefunctions. Computed values are in excellent agreement with the observed ones as found in the literature. Analysis of the calculated observables indicates that the hyperons stay close to the core-nucleus. Hypernuclei formed as a result of absorption of one or more $Ξ$-hyperon(s) by a normal nucleus (bound or unbound) are found to have more stability than those formed as a result of absorption of one or more $Λ$ hyperon(s) by the same nucleus. This fact flashes towards the possibility of the existence of a valley of strange hypernuclei near the drip lines.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hypernuclei formed by absorbing one or more cascade (Ξ) hyperons into a normal nucleus (bound or unbound) are more stable than those formed by absorbing Λ hyperons into the same nucleus, suggesting a possible "valley of strange hypernuclei" near the drip lines. This claim is hedged by the fact that the calculations are performed with a specific adjustable potential and truncated basis (Kmax = 24), and the authors note that full convergence is not achieved but can be extrapolated.

## Method

The authors applied a two-body model (Ξ + core nucleus) for single-Ξ hypernuclei and a three-body model (Ξ + Ξ + core nucleus) for double-Ξ hypernuclei, using the hyperspherical harmonics expansion (HHE) formalism. The Ξ-nucleus interaction was modeled with a two-term Gaussian (Isle type) potential with adjustable attractive depth, while the ΞΞ interaction used a two-term Yukawa-type potential from the Nijmegen extended softcore model (ESC08c). The coupled differential equations were solved numerically for ground-state energies and wavefunctions, with the basis truncated at Kmax = 24.

## Key numbers

* Single-Ξ separation energy B_Ξ for Ξ⁻⁵He: 3.3396 MeV (present work), compared to 3.61 MeV from Filikhin et al.
* Single-Ξ separation energy B_Ξ for Ξ⁻¹⁵C: 4.3894 MeV (present work), compared to experimental value 4.38 ± 0.25 MeV from Nakazawa et al. (2015)
* Single-Ξ separation energy B_Ξ for Ξ⁻¹⁷O: 16.0320 MeV (present work), compared to 16.5 MeV from Lalazissis et al.
* Double-Ξ separation energy B_ΞΞ for ΞΞ⁶He: 10.3149 MeV (present work, Kmax = 24)
* Double-Ξ separation energy B_ΞΞ for ΞΞ¹⁶C: 11.6269 MeV (present work, Kmax = 24)
* Double-Ξ separation energy B_ΞΞ for ΞΞ¹⁸O: 38.5102 MeV (present work, Kmax = 24)
* ΞΞ bond energy ΔB_ΞΞ for ΞΞ⁶He: 3.6357 MeV
* Three-body RMS radius R_3B for ΞΞ⁶He: 2.1341 fm, compared to 2.57 ± 0.1 fm for normal ⁶He from Chulkov et al.
* Relative convergence ratio ΔB/B for ΞΞ⁶He at Kmax = 24: 0.0043

## Context

This work extends the few-body hyperspherical harmonics approach previously applied by Khan et al. to atomic and nuclear systems, and builds on experimental observations of Ξ hypernuclei by Aoki et al. (1995), Fukuda et al. (1998), Khaustov et al. (2000), and Nakazawa et al. (2015). The calculations use the core-Ξ potential from Filikhin et al. (2008, 2017) and the ΞΞ potential from Garcilazo and Valcarce (2016). The results enable predictions for double-Ξ hypernuclei where experimental data are scarce, and suggest that Ξ hyperons can stabilize otherwise unbound nuclei near the drip lines, potentially allowing the study of exotic nuclei beyond the normal drip lines.
