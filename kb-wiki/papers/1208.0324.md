# Dipole resonances and the nuclear Schiff moment

> The nuclear Schiff moment creates a mechanism of transfer of the violation of parity and time-reversal invariance by weak interaction in nuclei into the atomic electric dipole moment. We point out an additional contribution to the Schiff moment generated by the mixing of single-particle states through the low-lying nuclear dipole resonances. An estimate shows that this contribution is by order of magnitude comparable to single-particle contributions and can be enhanced if the low-lying resonance has collective nature.

# Dipole resonances and the nuclear Schiff moment

## Key claim

The mixing of single-particle states through low-lying nuclear dipole resonances (such as the pygmy dipole resonance or isoscalar dipole resonance) generates an additional contribution to the nuclear Schiff moment that is comparable in magnitude to the standard single-particle contributions, and can be further enhanced if the low-lying resonance has collective character.

## Method

The authors consider an odd-A spherical nucleus where the ground state of positive parity is mixed with dipole-excited states of opposite parity from the neighboring even-even core via parity-violating weak interaction. They derive the admixture amplitude using the effective weak nucleon-nucleon interaction in contact form, project this interaction onto the collective dipole operator (either the isovector dipole moment or the Schiff operator itself), and compute the resulting expectation value of the Schiff moment in the mixed ground state. The formalism is applied to the isoscalar dipole resonance (ISDR) strength distribution from quasiparticle random phase approximation calculations.

## Key numbers

- For a nucleus with A=201 and the extra nucleon in the p1/2 orbital, the ISDR strength is concentrated in two peaks: about 10^5 fm^6 in the lower peak (centroid around 10 MeV) and about 2 x 10^5 fm^6 in the upper peak (centroid around 20 MeV).
- Using R = 6 fm for the nuclear radius, the resulting Schiff moment is S = 0.8 x 10^-8 eta e fm^3, where eta is the dimensionless weak interaction strength parameter.
- This value is of the same size as Schiff moments evaluated in single-particle models and core polarization calculations.

## Context

This work extends the known collective mechanisms for Schiff moment enhancement from static octupole deformation (Auerbach, Flambaum, and Spevak, 1996; Spevak, Auerbach, and Flambaum, 1997), soft octupole vibration (Flambaum and Zelevinsky, 2003), and combined soft quadrupole and octupole modes (Zelevinsky, Auerbach, and Sen'kov, 2008) to the case of low-lying dipole resonances. The proposed mechanism enables future microscopic calculations using the inverse energy weighted sum rule in the random phase approximation or constrained Hartree-Fock with the Schiff operator as the constraint, and suggests that nuclei away from stability or deformed nuclei with lower-lying dipole strength could show enhanced Schiff moments.
