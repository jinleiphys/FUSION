# Longitudinal Resolution in a Large Relativistic Nucleus: Adding a Dimension to the McLerran-Venugopalan Model

> We extend the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the gluon distribution functions of very large nuclei to larger values of the longitudinal momentum fraction xF. Because gluons with larger values of xF begin to resolve the longitudinal structure of the nucleus, we find that it is necessary to set up a fully three-dimensional formalism for performing the calculation. We obtain a relatively compact expression for the gluon number density provided that the nucleus is sufficiently large and consists of color-neutral nucleons. Our expressions for the gluon number density saturate at small transverse momenta. The nuclear dependence we obtain is such that the number of gluons increases more slowly than the number of nucleons is increased.

## Key claim

The authors extend the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for gluon distribution functions to larger values of the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x_F\), where gluons begin to resolve the longitudinal structure of the nucleus, requiring a fully three-dimensional formalism; the result is valid only when the nucleus is sufficiently large and consists of color-neutral nucleons, and the gluon number density saturates at small transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors set up a classical Yang-Mills framework with a source representing a nucleus moving at speed \(\beta < 1\), using rest-frame variables to keep all quantities of order unity. They solve for the vector potential in covariant gauge, transform to light-cone gauge via a path-ordered exponential, and compute the gluon number density using a Gaussian ensemble average of the two-point charge density correlation function, imposing a color-neutrality condition to ensure infrared finiteness.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon radius is \(a \sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^{-1}\), with \(a/R \approx A^{-1/3} \ll 1\) for a large nucleus.
- The upper limit on \(x_F\) for weak coupling is \(x_F \lesssim 3C_F / (\pi m_n a)\), which with \(m_n a \approx 5\) gives \(x_F \lesssim 0.25\).
- For large \(x_F\), the lower limit on transverse momentum is \(q_t^2 / x_F \gtrsim (\pi m a / 3C_F) \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2\).
- For large \(x_F\), the upper limit on transverse momentum is \(x_F q_t^2 \lesssim (3C_F / m a) \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2\).
- The color charge per unit area at small \(x_F\) is \(\kappa^2 = 3A C_F / (\pi R^2) \approx 3A^{1/3} C_F / (\pi a^2)\).
- The normalization constant for the correlation function is \(\mathcal{K}^3 = 3A C_F / [(N_c^2 - 1) V] = 3A / (2N_c V)\).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) to larger \(x_F\) values, building on earlier calculations that assumed small \(x_F\) and effectively two-dimensional geometry. The authors previously showed (Lam and Mahlon, 2000) that infrared divergences in the MV model can be cured by incorporating confinement effects via a color-neutrality condition. This extension enables calculations of gluon distributions in a broader kinematic region relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
