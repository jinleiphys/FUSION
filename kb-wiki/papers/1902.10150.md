# Quark mass dependence of $╬│^{*}ŽĆ\rightarrowŽĆŽĆ$

> Usually the simulation of scattering processes in lattice QCD is carried out at unphysically high values of the quark masses. Hence, a method to extrapolate data obtained in lattice calculations to physical masses is needed to allow for comparison between theory and experiment. To obtain a sound extrapolation, dispersion relations and chiral perturbation theory can be invoked. While a simple combined approach known as the inverse amplitude method allows for a successful extrapolation of $ŽĆŽĆ\toŽĆŽĆ$ data, a more complicated framework is needed for inelastic processes such as $╬│^{*}ŽĆ\toŽĆŽĆ$. By employing a well-established dispersive description, the extrapolation can be performed for $╬│^{*}ŽĆ\toŽĆŽĆ$ both for on-shell as well as virtual photons, the decay $╬│^*\rightarrowŽĆŽĆŽĆ$ is also within the range of applicability. This particular process is interesting due to both its contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon and its connection to the axial anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dispersive framework for extrapolating lattice QCD data for the process ╬│*ŽĆ ŌåÆ ŽĆŽĆ from unphysically high quark masses to the physical point, but notes that the NLO inverse amplitude method (IAM) for the required ŽĆŽĆ input does not describe existing lattice data satisfactorily, making NNLO contributions necessary.

## Method

The authors combine chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with dispersion relations, specifically using the inverse amplitude method (IAM) to describe the MŽĆ-dependence of the ŽĆŽĆ p-wave scattering amplitude t1 and phase ╬┤. For ╬│*ŽĆ ŌåÆ ŽĆŽĆ, they employ a fixed-t dispersion relation and the reconstruction theorem to express the amplitude in terms of basis functions that depend on the Omn├©s function constructed from the ŽĆŽĆ phase, then solve the resulting KhuriŌĆōTreiman equations via matrix inversion and iterative methods, using the IAM to provide t1 in the complex plane.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive description of ╬│*ŽĆ ŌåÆ ŽĆŽĆ developed by Hoferichter (2012) to include quark mass dependence, enabling extrapolation of lattice data to the physical point. It builds on the IAM approach previously applied to ŽĆŽĆ ŌåÆ ŽĆŽĆ (Truong 1991, Dobado 1993, Dobado 1997, Gomez Nicola 2008) and adapts the KhuriŌĆōTreiman equation method from Gasser (2018) for ╬Ę ŌåÆ ŽĆŽĆŽĆ. The framework enables future determination of Žü resonance pole positions and radiative couplings from lattice data, and is relevant for the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon and tests of the axial anomaly prediction (Adler 1971), which is currently tested only at the 10% level.
