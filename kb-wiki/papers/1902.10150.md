# Quark mass dependence of $γ^{*}π\rightarrowππ$

> Usually the simulation of scattering processes in lattice QCD is carried out at unphysically high values of the quark masses. Hence, a method to extrapolate data obtained in lattice calculations to physical masses is needed to allow for comparison between theory and experiment. To obtain a sound extrapolation, dispersion relations and chiral perturbation theory can be invoked. While a simple combined approach known as the inverse amplitude method allows for a successful extrapolation of $ππ\toππ$ data, a more complicated framework is needed for inelastic processes such as $γ^{*}π\toππ$. By employing a well-established dispersive description, the extrapolation can be performed for $γ^{*}π\toππ$ both for on-shell as well as virtual photons, the decay $γ^*\rightarrowπππ$ is also within the range of applicability. This particular process is interesting due to both its contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon and its connection to the axial anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dispersive framework for extrapolating lattice QCD data for the process γ*π → ππ from unphysically high quark masses to the physical point, but notes that the NLO inverse amplitude method (IAM) for the required ππ input does not describe existing lattice data satisfactorily, making NNLO contributions necessary.

## Method

The authors combine chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with dispersion relations, specifically using the inverse amplitude method (IAM) to describe the Mπ-dependence of the ππ p-wave scattering amplitude t1 and phase δ. For γ*π → ππ, they employ a fixed-t dispersion relation and the reconstruction theorem to express the amplitude in terms of basis functions that depend on the Omnès function constructed from the ππ phase, then solve the resulting Khuri–Treiman equations via matrix inversion and iterative methods, using the IAM to provide t1 in the complex plane.

## Key numbers

- Existing lattice calculations of γ*π → ππ were carried out at Mπ ≈ 400 MeV (Briceno 2015, Briceno 2016) and Mπ ≈ 320 MeV (Alexandrou 2018)
- The physical pion mass is Mπp = 139.57 MeV
- Fits of NLO IAM to individual lattice ensembles yield χ²/d.o.f. values ranging from 0.69 (D200 ensemble at Mπ ≈ 200 MeV) to 3.21 (32 ensemble at Mπ ≈ 236 MeV)
- The NLO IAM contains 1 free parameter (the LEC combination l2r − 2l1r); the NNLO IAM contains 6 free parameters
- The pion decay constant is set to F = 86.67 MeV
- The NNLO IAM is known to break down for pion masses larger than 300–350 MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive description of γ*π → ππ developed by Hoferichter (2012) to include quark mass dependence, enabling extrapolation of lattice data to the physical point. It builds on the IAM approach previously applied to ππ → ππ (Truong 1991, Dobado 1993, Dobado 1997, Gomez Nicola 2008) and adapts the Khuri–Treiman equation method from Gasser (2018) for η → πππ. The framework enables future determination of ρ resonance pole positions and radiative couplings from lattice data, and is relevant for the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon and tests of the axial anomaly prediction (Adler 1971), which is currently tested only at the 10% level.
