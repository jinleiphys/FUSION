# Collective flow in heavy ion reactions and the properties of excited nuclear matter

> Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) calculations of central collisions between heavy nuclei are used to study fragment production and the creation of collective flow. It is shown that the final phase space distributions are compatible with the expectations from a thermally equilibrated source, which in addition exhibits a collective transverse expansion. However, the microscopic analyses of the transient states in the intermediate reaction stages show that the event shapes are more complex and that equilibrium is reached only in very special cases but not in event samples which cover a wide range of impact parameters as it is the case in experiments. The basic features of a new molecular dynamics model (UQMD) for heavy ion collisions from the Fermi energy regime up to the highest presently available energies are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while final-state particle spectra from central heavy-ion collisions can be fitted assuming a thermally equilibrated source with collective transverse expansion, microscopic analysis of the intermediate reaction stages shows that equilibrium is reached only in very special cases and not in event samples covering a wide range of impact parameters as in experiments.

## Method

Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) calculations were performed for central Au+Au collisions at 150 MeV/nucleon, with nucleons represented as Gaussian wavefunctions propagated via canonical equations of motion and supplemented by hard binary collisions with Pauli blocking. The complete space-time history of the collisions was analyzed, comparing the final phase-space distributions to fits assuming a thermalized source with a linearly increasing transverse flow velocity profile. A new extended model (UQMD) was outlined for simulations from the Fermi energy regime up to 200 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Transverse momentum spectra of fragments from Au+Au at 150 MeV/nucleon and b=0 are compatible with "temperatures" between 20 and 25 MeV and averaged collective flow velocities of 0.10–0.13 c
- Heavy fragment multiplicities suggested much lower temperatures on the order of 8 MeV
- At densities around 0.1–0.5 nuclear matter saturation density, where freeze-out is expected, temperatures have dropped below 10 MeV
- For light ejectiles like protons, collective energy amounts to only 30% of the total energy; for heavy fragments, 70–80% of the kinetic energy is collective
- Even in late expansion stages, the central reaction volume still contains approximately 1/3 of the mass of the entire Au+Au system
- For calculations with potentials, the integrated directed transverse momentum push per nucleon is more than twice as high for the heavier system compared to cascade simulations

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic predictions of collective flow in heavy ion collisions (Stoecker 1986) and experimental observations at the Bevalac (Doss 1986, Gutbrod 1989). The paper resolves a long-standing puzzle between "temperatures" of 20–25 MeV from spectral fits and much lower temperatures (around 8 MeV) inferred from heavy fragment multiplicities (Kuhn 1993), showing that the high-momentum tails come from fast-moving cooled cells rather than a hot source. The UQMD model enables systematic excitation function studies across four orders of magnitude in energy, from 20 MeV/nucleon to 200 GeV/nucleon, and can be used to search for signatures of the quark-hadron phase transition through abnormal behavior in collective flow strength.
