# Exotic hadrons: review and perspectives

> The physics of exotic hadrons is revisited and reviewed, with emphasis on flavour configurations which have not yet been investigated. The constituent quark model of multiquark states is discussed in some detail, as it can serve as a guide for more elaborate approaches.

# Exotic hadrons: review and perspectives

## Key claim
This review argues that the definition of an exotic hadron remains vague and time-dependent, with the consensus being that a hadron is exotic if it cannot be understood as mainly made of quark-antiquark or three quarks, but the word "mainly" leaves room for interpretation as more advanced pictures include expansions in Fock space with additional quark-antiquark pairs.

## Method
The paper surveys experimental and theoretical results on exotic hadrons across meson and baryon sectors, organized by flavour content (light, single heavy-flavour, double heavy-flavour, hidden heavy-flavour). It critically discusses the constituent quark model of multiquark states as a guide for more elaborate approaches, and reviews specific candidate states including XYZ mesons, pentaquarks, and dibaryons, with emphasis on flavour configurations that have not yet been investigated.

## Key numbers
- The $\phi$ meson has mass 1.02 GeV with a width of about 4 MeV, decaying preferentially into kaons despite pions being energetically easier
- The $J/\psi$ particle has mass about 3.1 GeV with width much less than 1 MeV
- The $D^*_{s,0}(2317)$ and $D^*_{s,1}(2460)$ were discovered with masses lower than predicted in quark-model calculations
- The $\eta_c(1s)$ lies about 112 MeV below the $J/\psi$
- The LHCb pentaquark candidates in $\Lambda_b\to J/\psi+p+K$ have masses $4380\pm 8\pm 29$ MeV (width $205\pm 18\pm 86$ MeV) and $4449.8\pm 1.7\pm 2.5$ MeV (width $39\pm 5\pm 19$ MeV)
- The $\Omega_b(bss)$ mass was measured around 6050 MeV by CDF and LHCb, in closer agreement with expectations than the initial D0 measurement of about 6165 MeV
- Charmed baryon widths include $\Gamma\sim 6$ MeV for $\Lambda_c(2880)$, $\sim 17$ MeV for $\Lambda_c(2940)$, $\sim 4$ MeV for $\Xi_c(3123)$
- The $1^{-+}$ states have been found between 1 and 2 GeV by VES, E852, and COMPASS

## Context
This review extends the extensive literature on exotic hadrons including reviews by Swanson (2006), Nielsen (2009), Hambrock (2013), and Chen (2016) for hidden-charm exotics, and the PDG review by Agashe (2014) for general hadron properties. It critically discusses the history of light pentaquark claims following Nakano (2003) and the Diakonov model (1997), the subsequent non-confirmation by higher-statistics experiments, and the recent LHCb hidden-charm pentaquark discovery (2015). The paper enables future searches for doubly-heavy exotics and double-charm baryons, which have not yet been confirmed despite predictions from multiple theoretical approaches.
