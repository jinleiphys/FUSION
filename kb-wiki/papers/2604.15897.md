# Delineating neutral and charged mesons in magnetic fields

> We investigate the properties of neutral and charged mesons in magnetic fields, from weak-field to strong-field regimes. To develop analytic insights, we employ a non-relativistic quark model with a confining potential of the harmonic oscillator type. Short-range correlations, such as Coulomb and color-magnetic interactions, are treated as perturbations. In particular, we focus on the magnetic field dependence of the relative and the center-of-mass motions. The qualitative trends differ significantly between neutral and charged mesons: for neutral mesons, the transverse momenta are conserved and continuous, while charged mesons exhibit quantized transverse dynamics. The Zeeman effects, arising from intrinsic spins and orbital angular momenta, are carefully examined. In particular, for charged mesons with spins $s\ge 1$, we discuss how the zero-point energy in the internal quark motion cancels the Zeeman energy from the orbital angular momentum, ensuring the energetic stability of mesons with high spins. The effectively reduced dimensionality of these mesons in the strong-field limit is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for charged mesons with spins \(s \ge 1\) in strong magnetic fields, the zero-point energy from internal quark motion cancels the Zeeman energy from orbital angular momentum, ensuring energetic stability of high-spin mesons; this cancellation is a qualitative trend that differs fundamentally from neutral mesons, where transverse momenta remain continuous rather than quantized.

## Method

The authors employ a non-relativistic quark model with a harmonic oscillator confining potential, treating short-range Coulomb and color-magnetic interactions as perturbations. They decompose the two-body Hamiltonian into center-of-mass and relative coordinates, solve for transverse motion using pseudomomentum and guiding center coordinates in the symmetric gauge, and compute expectation values of short-range potentials using Gaussian wavefunctions with running coupling \(\alpha_s(Q^2)\) to avoid logarithmic divergences at large magnetic field. Parameters are fitted to reproduce vacuum masses of \(\pi\), \(K\), \(\rho\), \(K^*\), and \(\phi\) mesons.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark masses: \(m_{u,d} = 0.264\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.425\) GeV; confining strength \(\lambda^{1/3} = 0.170\) GeV; QCD scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.250\) GeV; color-magnetic factor \(C_M = 1.22\).
* At \(B=0\), calculated masses: \(\pi = 0.147\) GeV (exp. 0.140 GeV), \(\rho = 0.764\) GeV (exp. 0.775 GeV), \(K = 0.486\) GeV (exp. 0.494 GeV), \(K^* = 0.911\) GeV (exp. 0.892 GeV), \(\phi = 1.03\) GeV (exp. 1.019 GeV).
* For neutral mesons, the effective transverse kinetic energy from confining potential is \(\lambda K_\perp^2 / (B_q^2 + 2M\lambda)\), which at large \(B\) reduces to \(\lambda (\bm{X}_1 - \bm{X}_2)^2 / (1 + 2M\lambda/B_q^2)\).
* The effective transverse oscillator frequency for neutral mesons is \(\mathcal{B}_q^2 = B_q^2 + 8\mu\lambda\), where \(\mu\) is the reduced mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior investigations (Hattori 2015, Kojo 2021) by providing a more comprehensive and conceptually refined treatment of pseudo-momentum and guiding center coordinates for charged mesons, and by developing physically motivated treatments of short-range correlations that require special care in the large-\(B\) limit. It builds on non-relativistic quark model approaches (Isgur 1979, DeRujula 1975, Sakharov 1980, Zeldovich 1967) and complements lattice QCD studies (Ding 2026, Bali 2012, Endrodi 2015) and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio or quark-meson model calculations (Coppola 2023, Gomez Dumm 2023, Carlomagno 2022) by providing analytic insights into how internal quark dynamics affect meson masses and transverse kinetic terms in magnetic fields, laying groundwork for future studies of multi-quark systems and hadronic media.
