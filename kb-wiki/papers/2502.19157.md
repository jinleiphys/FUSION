# Proper effective temperature and order parameters in relativistic non-equilibrium steady states

> We examine the concept of temperature in non-equilibrium steady states. Using the D3-D5 model of gauge/gravity duality, we investigate spontaneous symmetry breaking in a relativistic (2+1)-dimensional defect moving at constant velocity within a (3+1)-dimensional heat bath. We find that the dependence of the order parameter on both the heat bath temperature and the defect velocity can be captured by a single variable -- the proper effective temperature -- for the moving defect. Our results suggest that the proper effective temperature is an essential parameter for a class of relativistic non-equilibrium steady states.

## Key claim

The chiral condensate (order parameter) in a relativistic non-equilibrium steady state (NESS) of the D3-D5 model depends on the heat bath temperature \(T\) and the defect velocity \(v\) only through a single combined variable, the proper effective temperature \(T_{\rm eff}^{\rm prop}\), such that the order parameter is fully determined by the three variables \((T_{\rm eff}^{\rm prop}, B, \rho)\) rather than the four variables \((T, v, B, \rho)\). This holds within good numerical precision, but the authors note that an analytic proof remains elusive because the conversion from \(T\) to \(T_{\rm eff}^{\rm prop}\) requires knowledge of the solution itself.

## Method

Using the gauge/gravity duality (D3-D5 model), the authors study a \((2+1)\)-dimensional defect moving at constant velocity \(v\) in a \((3+1)\)-dimensional heat bath of temperature \(T\), with finite \(U(1)_B\) charge density \(\rho\) and external magnetic field \(B\). They solve the nonlinear equations of motion for the D5-brane embedding \(\theta(u)\) numerically via a shooting method, imposing the boundary condition of massless hypermultiplet (\(m=0\)) and the reality condition at the effective horizon \(u_* = u_h (1-v^2)^{1/4}\). The chiral condensate \(\sigma\) is extracted from the asymptotic expansion of \(\theta(u)\) near the AdS boundary.

## Key numbers

- The effective horizon is located at \(u_{*}=u_h (1-v^2)^{1/4}\), where \(u_h = 1/(\pi T)\).
- For \(\rho=B=0\) and preserved \(SU(2)_V\) symmetry, the effective temperature is \(T_{\mathrm{eff}} = T (1-v^2)^{1/4}(1+v^2)^{1/2}\).
- The proper effective temperature is defined as \(T_{\rm eff}^{\rm prop} = \gamma T_{\rm eff}\) with \(\gamma = (1-v^2)^{-1/2}\).
- Numerical data for \(\sigma\) at \(B=2\), \(\rho=1\) for temperatures \(T = 0.02, 0.06, 0.10, 0.14, 0.18, 0.22\) collapse onto a single curve when plotted against \(T_{\rm eff}^{\rm prop}\).
- A direct comparison of brane profiles for \((T,v) = (0.2, 0.3)\) and \((T,v) = (0.2052, 0)\) with common \((T_{\rm eff}^{\rm prop}, B, \rho) = (0.2052, 3, 0.02)\) shows the profiles coincide to high accuracy despite different effective horizon locations.

## Context

This work extends the study of spontaneous symmetry breaking in the D3-D5 model from thermal equilibrium (Jensen 2010, Evans 2010, Evans 2011) to non-equilibrium steady states where the defect moves at constant velocity. The concept of effective temperature in gauge/gravity duality was previously developed by Gubser (2006), Casalderrey-Solana (2007), Gursoy (2010), Das (2010), Kim (2011), Sonner (2012), and Nakamura (2013). The proper effective temperature was introduced by Hoshino (2018) as the NESS analog of the relativistic temperature defined by Landsberg (1966) and van Kampen (1968), in contrast to the Einstein-Planck definition. The results suggest that for this class of systems, the equilibrium phase diagram can be extended to NESS by replacing \(T\) with \(T_{\rm eff}^{\rm prop}\), enabling predictions of non-equilibrium order parameters from equilibrium data. Open questions include whether this property is general to other setups and whether a free energy can be defined for these NESSs.
