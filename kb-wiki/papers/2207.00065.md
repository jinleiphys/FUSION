# Non-equilibrium charmonium regeneration in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> The evaluation of quarkonium regeneration in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (URHICs) requires the knowledge of the heavy-quark phase space distributions in the expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fireball. We employ a semi-classical charmonium transport approach where regeneration processes explicitly account for the time-dependent spectra of charm quarks via Langevin simulations of their diffusion. The inelastic charmonium rates and charm-quark transport coefficients are computed from the same charm-medium interaction. The latter is modeled by perturbative rates, augmented with a $K$-factor to represent nonperturbative interaction strength and interference effect. Using central 5.02~TeV Pb-Pb collisions as a test case we find that a good description of the measured $J/ψ$ yield and its transverse-momentum dependence can be achieved if a large $K\gtrsim5$ is employed while smaller values lead to marked discrepancies. This is in line with open-charm phenomenology in URHICs, where nonperturbative interactions of similar strength are required. Our approach establishes a common transport framework for a microscopic description of open and hidden heavy-flavor (HF) observables that incorporates both nonperturbative and non-equilibrium effects, and thus enhances the mutual constraints from experiment on the extraction of transport properties of the QGP.

# Non-equilibrium charmonium regeneration in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
A coupled Langevin-Boltzmann transport framework for open and hidden charm, using the same charm-medium scattering amplitude for both sectors, can describe the measured $J/\psi$ yield and transverse-momentum dependence in central 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions only if a large nonperturbative $K$-factor of at least 5 is employed; smaller values lead to marked discrepancies, consistent with open-charm phenomenology requiring similar interaction strength.

## Method
The authors couple a stochastic Langevin simulation of charm-quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma to a Boltzmann equation for charmonium (J/ψ, χc, ψ′) evolution. The inelastic charmonium reaction rates (dissociation and regeneration) and charm-quark transport coefficients are computed from the same underlying charm-medium scattering amplitude, modeled using perturbative QCD Born amplitudes with a phenomenological $K$-factor to represent nonperturbative interaction strength and an interference factor for charmonium states. The time-dependent charm-quark phase-space distributions from the Langevin simulation are injected into the charmonium regeneration rate, replacing earlier thermal approximations.

## Key numbers
- A $K$-factor of 5 (compared to $K=1$) is required to describe the inclusive $J/\psi$ $R_{\rm AA}(p_T)$ in central 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at forward rapidity, consistent with ALICE data (Adam 2016).
- The spatial diffusion coefficient for $K=5$ yields ${\cal D}_s(2\pi T) \lesssim 6$, while $K=1$ gives ${\cal D}_s(2\pi T) \sim 30$.
- For $p_T \gtrsim 6$ GeV, the ALICE data are underestimated with $K=5$, consistent with a momentum-dependent $K$ factor.
- The critical temperature for the mixed phase is taken as $T_c = 180$ MeV, corresponding to a time window from approximately 5.5 to 9 fm/c in the fireball evolution.

## Context
This work extends previous charmonium transport calculations by Du and Rapp (2015) and earlier rate-equation approaches (Grandchamp 2001, Zhao 2010) by replacing thermal or blastwave approximations for charm-quark spectra with explicit Langevin simulations of their diffusion. The approach establishes a common transport framework for open and hidden heavy-flavor observables, enhancing mutual constraints on QGP transport properties. The required $K$-factor of at least 5 is consistent with independent extractions from open-charm phenomenology (Rapp 2018) and bottomonium suppression (Du 2019). Future improvements include replacing the schematic $K$-factor with explicit nonperturbative $T$-matrix calculations constrained by lattice QCD, and incorporating quantum effects in the transport equations.
