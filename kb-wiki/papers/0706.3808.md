# Birefringence (rotation of polarization plane and spin dichroism) of deuterons in carbon target

> Birefringence phenomenon for deuteron with energy up 20 MeV in carbon target is considered. The estimation for spin dichroism and for angle of rotation of polarization plane of deuterons is presented. It is shown that magnitude of the phenomenon strongly depends on behavior of the deuteron wave functions on small distance between nucleon in deuteron.

## Key claim

The birefringence effect (rotation of the polarization plane and spin dichroism) for deuterons up to 20 MeV in a carbon target is estimated using the eikonal approximation, and the magnitude of the phenomenon is shown to depend strongly on the behavior of the deuteron wave functions at small internucleon distances, particularly through the parameter G which is sensitive to the D-state component.

## Method

The authors applied the eikonal approximation to compute the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude for deuterons on carbon, treating the deuteron-nucleus interaction as the sum of proton-carbon and neutron-carbon strong interactions plus the proton-carbon Coulomb interaction. The deuteron wave function was decomposed into S-wave and D-wave components, and the difference in nucleon density distributions for spin states m=±1 versus m=0 was used to calculate the difference in forward scattering amplitudes. Two models were considered: one treating the Coulomb interaction at the proton position and another averaging the Coulomb interaction over the deuteron ground state.

## Key numbers

- For a carbon target with thickness z = 0.1 g/cm² and deuteron energies in the range 6-13 MeV, the spin dichroism is about 0.01.
- The angle of rotation of the polarization plane for the same carbon target at slightly higher energy is about -0.5 degrees.
- The total nucleon-carbon cross-section calculated by optical potential and eikonal approximation at 5.25 MeV is about 1.2 barn.
- The parameter G, computed from deuteron wave functions based on the CD-Bonn potential, is about 0.05.
- The characteristic time of nuclear deuteron-carbon interaction at 10 MeV is τ^N ≈ 5×10⁻²² s, while the characteristic period of nucleon oscillation in the deuteron is τ ≈ 2×10⁻²¹ s.
- The characteristic time of Coulomb deuteron-carbon interaction at 10.5 MeV is τ^C ≈ 2×10⁻¹⁸ s.
- The D-state weight in the deuteron wave functions used is 4.85%.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for deuteron birefringence originally developed by Baryshevsky (1992, 1993), which predicted that particles with spin S≥1 passing through unpolarized isotropic matter exhibit a difference in forward scattering amplitudes for spin projections m=0 and m=±1. The calculations are compared with experimental measurements of deuteron spin dichroism in carbon targets performed at the HVEC tandem Van-de-Graaff accelerator in Cologne (Exp1, Exp2) and later at 5.5 GeV/c on the Nuclotron in Dubna (dub). The results enable the use of birefringence measurements as an additional probe of deuteron wave functions at small internucleon distances (r < 2 fm), where discrepancies exist between different nuclear potential models.
