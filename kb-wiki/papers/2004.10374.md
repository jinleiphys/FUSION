# $2νββ$-decay to first $2^+$ state with partial isospin symmetry restoration from spherical QRPA calculations

> With partially restored isospin symmetry, we calculate the nuclear matrix element for a special decay mode of $2νββ$ (two neutrino double beta decay) -- the decay to the first $2^+$ excited states. With the realistic CD-Bonn nuclear force, we analyze the dependence of the nuclear matrix elements on the iso-vector and iso-scalar parts of proton-neutron particle-particle interaction. The dependence on the different nuclear matrix element is observed and the results are explained. We also give the phase space factors with numerical electron wave functions and properly chosen excitation energies. Finally we give our results for the half-lives of this decay mode for different nuclei.

# $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay to first $2^+$ state with partial isospin symmetry restoration from spherical QRPA calculations

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements for $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay to the first $2^+$ excited state depend on both the iso-vector ($T=1$) and iso-scalar ($T=0$) channels of the proton-neutron particle-particle interaction, unlike decays to ground states where only the iso-scalar channel matters; this dependence is analyzed with partially restored isospin symmetry, but the treatment of isospin symmetry restoration alters the final NMEs by only several percent for most nuclei except $^{136}$Xe.

## Method

The authors use spherical quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) with the CD-Bonn realistic nuclear force to construct both $1^+$ intermediate states (via charge-exchange pn-QRPA) and $2^+$ excited states of final nuclei (via charge-conserving CC-QRPA). The iso-vector and iso-scalar particle-particle interaction strengths are separately parametrized: $g_{pp}^{T=0}$ is fitted to reproduce experimental $2\nu\beta\beta$ Gamow-Teller NMEs to ground states, and $g_{pp}^{T=1}$ is fitted by requiring a vanishing Fermi NME, with the CC-QRPA iso-vector strength set equal to that of pn-QRPA for consistency. Phase space factors are calculated using numerical electron wave functions from the RADIAL package with the long wavelength approximation and electron wave functions evaluated at the nuclear surface.

## Key numbers

- For $^{100}$Mo, the predicted half-life for decay to the first $2^+$ state is $6.63 \times 10^{21}$ yr, the shortest among the 8 nuclei studied, with a branching ratio of $1.1 \times 10^{-3}$ relative to ground-state decay.
- For $^{128}$Te, the predicted half-life is $2.05 \times 10^{31}$ yr, the longest among the 8 nuclei, with a branching ratio of $9.8 \times 10^{-8}$.
- The nuclear matrix elements for decay to $2^+$ states range from $-9.05 \times 10^{-2}$ MeV$^{-3}$ ($^{116}$Cd) to $1.31 \times 10^{-2}$ MeV$^{-3}$ ($^{82}$Se), with $^{136}$Xe having the smallest magnitude at $-3.04 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-3}$.
- Phase space factors for the SSD (single state dominance) choice range from $1.813 \times 10^{-29}$ yr$^{-1}\cdot$MeV$^6$ ($^{128}$Te) to $2.989 \times 10^{-20}$ yr$^{-1}\cdot$MeV$^6$ ($^{100}$Mo).
- The phase space factor for $^{116}$Cd changes by about $30\%$ when varying the average excitation energy $\tilde{A}$ from the SSD value to higher energies.

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA calculations of $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay to excited states (e.g., Civitarese and Suhonen (1994), Aunola and Suhonen (1996), Stoica and collaborators (1997, 1998)) by implementing partial isospin symmetry restoration following the approach of Rodin and Faessler (2011) and Simkovic, Rodin, and Faessler (2013). The study systematically separates iso-vector and iso-scalar particle-particle interaction channels, which was not done in earlier QRPA calculations such as those of Barabash (2015) for ground-state decays. The results enable experimental searches for $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay to $2^+$ states, particularly identifying $^{100}$Mo, $^{96}$Zr, and $^{130}$Te as the most promising candidates for detection, with $^{100}$Mo having the current experimental limit within one order of magnitude of the predicted half-life.
