# Gaining (Mutual) Information about Quark/Gluon Discrimination

> Discriminating quark jets from gluon jets is an important but challenging problem in jet substructure. In this paper, we use the concept of mutual information to illuminate the physics of quark/gluon tagging. Ideal quark/gluon separation requires only one bit of truth information, so even if two discriminant variables are largely uncorrelated, they can still share the same "truth overlap". Mutual information can be used to diagnose such situations, and thus determine which discriminant variables are redundant and which can be combined to improve performance. Using both parton showers and analytic resummation, we study a two-parameter family of generalized angularities, which includes familiar infrared and collinear (IRC) safe observables like thrust and broadening, as well as IRC unsafe variants like $p_T^D$ and hadron multiplicity. At leading-logarithmic (LL) order, the bulk of these variables exhibit Casimir scaling, such that their truth overlap is a universal function of the color factor ratio $C_A/C_F$. Only at next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) order can one see a difference in quark/gluon performance. For the IRC safe angularities, we show that the quark/gluon performance can be improved by combining angularities with complementary angular exponents. Interestingly, LL order, NLL order, Pythia 8, and Herwig++ all exhibit similar correlations between observables, but there are significant differences in the predicted quark/gluon discrimination power. For the IRC unsafe angularities, we show that the mutual information can be calculated analytically with the help of a nonperturbative "weighted-energy function", providing evidence for the complementarity of safe and unsafe observables for quark/gluon discrimination.

## Key claim

The paper argues that mutual information, rather than simple correlation measures, reveals why two largely uncorrelated discriminant variables can still share the same "truth overlap" with quark/gluon identity, meaning they provide no improvement when combined; this is demonstrated for generalized angularities where at leading-logarithmic order the truth overlap is a universal function of the color factor ratio \(C_A/C_F\), with differences only appearing at next-to-leading-logarithmic order.

## Method

The authors define a two-parameter family of jet substructure observables called generalized angularities \(\lambda^\kappa_\beta = \sum_i z_i^\kappa (R_i/R_0)^\beta\), where \(z_i\) is the momentum fraction and \(R_i\) the angle to the winner-take-all axis. They compute the mutual information \(I(T;A)\) between an observable and the quark/gluon truth label using analytic resummation at leading-logarithmic (LL) and next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) order, and compare these results to parton shower simulations from Pythia 8 and Herwig++ for jets with \(p_T > 400\) GeV and jet radius \(R_0 = 0.6\) at the 8 TeV LHC.

## Key numbers

* For any observable exhibiting Casimir scaling, the truth overlap for an equal admixture of quarks and gluons with \(C_A/C_F = 9/4\) is \(I(T;A)_{f=1/2} \simeq 0.103\) bits.
* The Casimir scaling ROC curve is \(\text{ROC} = x^{C_A/C_F} = x^{9/4}\), where \(x\) is the quark jet efficiency and \(x^{9/4}\) is the gluon jet mistag rate.
* The mutual information for Casimir-scaling observables is a universal function of the color factor ratio \(C_A/C_F = 9/4\) for QCD.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark/gluon discrimination using jet substructure (Gallicchio 2011, Gallicchio 2012, Krohn 2012, Larkoski 2013) and resolves the apparent conflict between studies showing improved performance from combining observables (ATLAS 2013) and those showing little gain despite low correlations (CMS 2013). The mutual information framework enables analytic calculation of discrimination power for generalized angularities, including IRC unsafe variants like \(p_T^D\) and hadron multiplicity, and provides a method to identify redundant variables for future tagging studies.
