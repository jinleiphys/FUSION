# Post-formation in alpha emission from nuclei

> We propose a novel mechanism to explain nuclear decay by emission of an alpha particle. We show that the famous Geiger-Nuttall law can be explained by post-forming an alpha particle outside the range of the nuclear interaction with the daughter nucleus. This contrasts with the commonly accepted mechanism of first alpha particle pre-formation followed by emission through barrier penetration. We predict that the post-formation mechanism is more likely to occur for alpha-particles with higher energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Geiger-Nuttall law for alpha decay can be explained by individual nucleons (or clusters such as deuterons) tunneling through the Coulomb barrier separately and then forming an alpha particle *outside* the range of the nuclear interaction, rather than by a pre-formed alpha particle tunneling as a single entity. The authors hedge that this post-formation mechanism is predicted to be more likely for alpha particles with higher energy, and that the post-formation factor is expected to be largest at small Q-values.

## Method

The authors extend the Gamow model by calculating the product of individual WKB penetration probabilities for the four nucleons (two protons and two neutrons) composing an alpha particle, and also for alternative constituent combinations such as two deuterons. They compare the resulting logarithmic half-life dependence on Q-value to experimental data for even Polonium isotopes (using a ppnn product) and Uranium isotopes (using a dd product), assuming a post-formation factor of unity and using asymptotic Whittaker and Bessel functions for the bound nucleon wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For Polonium isotopes (mass number A=186-208), calculations using the ppnn post-formation formula with l=0 and f_post=1 reproduce the experimental Geiger-Nuttall trend of log half-life versus 1/sqrt(Q_alpha).
- For Uranium isotopes (mass number A=222-238), calculations using the dd post-formation hypothesis similarly reproduce the Geiger-Nuttall law.
- The ratio f_post/f_pre is estimated using WKB to scale as exp{ - (4 pi Z e^2 sqrt(m_N)/hbar) [ sqrt(2/Q_alpha) - sqrt(1/Q_d) ] }, where Q_d ~ Q_alpha - 20 MeV.
- For Q_alpha ~ 40 MeV, the estimate gives f_post ~ f_pre; for larger Q_alpha, f_pre dominates over f_post.

## Context

This work proposes an alternative to the standard Gamow theory (1928) of alpha decay via pre-formation and tunneling, which was used to explain the empirical Geiger-Nuttall law (1911). The paper extends recent microscopic alpha-clustering studies using relativistic density functional models by Typel (2014) and quasi-free alpha knockout experiments by Tanaka (2019) on tin isotopes. The post-formation mechanism raises the question of whether alpha particles are pre-formed inside the nucleus or post-formed at the nuclear surface, which the authors suggest could be tested experimentally via alpha-knockout reactions on radioactive projectiles in inverse kinematics.
