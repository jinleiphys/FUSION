# Mode entanglement and isospin pairing in two-nucleon systems

> In this study, we explore the entanglement and correlation in two-nucleon systems using isospin formalism. With the help of Slater decomposition, we derive analytical expressions for various entanglement measures. Specifically, we analyse the one- and two-mode entropies, mutual informations, and a basis-independent characteristic known as the one-body entanglement entropy. To understand the impact of pairing, we consider interactions involving isovector and isoscalar L=0 pairing terms. Our findings show that certain pairing interactions can maximize one-body entanglement entropy of ground states when both total angular momentum and total isospin have zero projections. We provide numerical examples for the sd shell and explore the mutual informations in LS coupled and jj coupled single-particle bases. We find that the shell structure and angular momentum coupling significantly impact the measures of entanglement. We outline the implications of conserving angular momentum and isospin on one-mode entropies, irrespective of particle number.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, for two-nucleon systems in the isospin formalism, certain isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions can maximize the one-body entanglement entropy of ground states, but only when both total angular momentum and total isospin have zero projections; the shell structure and angular momentum coupling significantly affect entanglement measures.

## Method

The authors used Slater decomposition to derive analytical expressions for one-mode and two-mode entropies, mutual informations, and one-body entanglement entropy for two-nucleon states in the isospin formalism. They applied these measures to single-configuration and configuration-interaction wave functions in the sd shell, considering interactions with isovector and isoscalar L=0 pairing terms, and analyzed results in both LS-coupled and jj-coupled single-particle bases.

## Key numbers

* One-mode entropy upper bound: 1 (achieved when mode occupation number is 1/2); two-mode entropy upper bound: 2.
* Maximum two-mode entropy for nonassociated modes in a Slater basis: log2(3) ≈ 1.58, reached when both modes have occupation number 1/3.
* For identical subshells with J=0, T=1, Tz=0, and j=1/2, the one-mode entropy of all modes in the single configuration equals 1 (the theoretical upper bound).
* The one-body entanglement entropy is given by S^(1B) = sum_{s=1}^{n} 2 h(4λ_s^2), where h(x) = -x log2(x) - (1-x) log2(1-x) and λ_s are Slater decomposition coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kovacs et al., 2021) which examined proton-proton and neutron-neutron two-body systems (T=1, Tz=±1) to now include proton-neutron systems with T=0,1 and Tz=0. It builds on mode-entanglement approaches developed by Zanardi (2002), Leggio (2003), and others, and connects to nuclear shell model studies of entanglement by Kruppa (2021, 2022), Stetcu (2022), and Tichai (2023). The results enable better understanding of how isospin symmetry and pairing interactions affect entanglement in nuclear systems, and may help develop improved truncation methods for shell model calculations.
