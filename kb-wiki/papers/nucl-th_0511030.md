# Low Momentum NN-interactions and Nuclear Matter Calculations

> Separable nucleon-nucleon potentials are calculated using inverse scattering techniques as presented in previously published work. The dependence of the potentials on the momentum cut-off of the scattering phase-shifts is studied. Some comparison is made with the V-low-k potential. The effect of the cut-off on nuclear matter binding energy calculated by standard Brueckner theory is also presented. It is foumd that a cut-off larger than about 4 fm-1 will keep the error to within one Mev around saturation density. While the potentials are cut-off dependent the effective interaction represented by the Brueckner K-matrix is less sensitive to this cut-off. This is in particular found to be the case for the 1S0 state.

# Low Momentum NN-interactions and Nuclear Matter Calculations

## Key claim

A momentum cut-off larger than about 4 fm⁻¹ in the construction of separable nucleon-nucleon potentials from phase-shifts keeps the error in nuclear matter binding energy to within one MeV around saturation density, but the effective Brueckner K-matrix is less sensitive to this cut-off than the potentials themselves, especially for the ¹S₀ state.

## Method

Separable nucleon-nucleon potentials were constructed using inverse scattering techniques that exactly fit the Arndt experimental phase-shifts (defined up to 1.6 GeV lab energy, corresponding to k ≈ 4.3 fm⁻¹ in the center-of-mass system). The potentials were calculated for different values of the momentum cut-off Λ, with phase-shifts extrapolated by a straight line to the chosen cut-off when Λ exceeded 4.3 fm⁻¹. Nuclear matter binding energies were then computed using standard Brueckner theory with continuous energy spectrum and self-consistent mean fields.

## Key numbers

- A cut-off larger than about 4 fm⁻¹ keeps the error in nuclear matter binding energy to within one MeV around saturation density.
- At the limit Λ → 0, the separable potential V(0,0) equals -23.73 (the scattering length) for the ¹S₀ state.
- The Arndt phase-shifts are defined up to 1.6 GeV lab energy, which converts to k ≈ 4.3 fm⁻¹ in the center-of-mass system.
- For a fixed density at k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹, the ¹S₀ contribution to potential energy per particle is almost constant down to a cut-off as low as k_F.
- At k_F = 1.7 fm⁻¹, the lowest acceptable cut-off has not shifted appreciably from the k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹ case, but potential energies below 5 fm⁻¹ increase more drastically.
- For the ³S₁ state, a slight decrease in binding is seen already at 5 fm⁻¹, followed by a sharp increase below 2.5 fm⁻¹.
- With cut-offs above 5 fm⁻¹, binding energy curves as a function of density are well clustered; at a cut-off of 4 fm⁻¹ there is a noticeable difference.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous inverse scattering separable potential calculations (Kwo95) and compares with the V_low-k approach developed by Bogner (2001, 2003) and Schwenk (2003). The study re-examines the relationship between nuclear matter saturation and the momentum cut-off of scattering phase-shifts, a question related to the Moszkowski-Scott separation method (Mos60) and the concept that short-ranged repulsions can be integrated out. The results enable further investigation of off-shell effects in the in-medium K-matrix by increasing the rank of separable potentials while keeping on-shell data intact, and suggest that the ³S₁-³D₁ states (with their deuteron D-state probability uncertainty) will require separate treatment.
