# Chiral-Transport-Induced Collective Modes in Strong Magnetic Fields and Their Implications for Neutron Star Phenomenology

> In this thesis, we study the collective modes induced by the chirality of elementary particles in magnetized media and their implications for neutron star phenomenology. We theoretically predict that the chiral magnetic wave can arise in quark matter inside neutron stars, resulting in the emergence of novel types of seismic oscillations and associated gravitational waves in the context of asteroseismology. We also investigate the response to dynamical electromagnetic fields and find that a dynamical screening, distinct from the conventional Landau damping, occurs due to the chiral anomaly.

## Key claim

The thesis predicts that the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) can arise in quark matter inside strongly magnetized neutron stars, leading to new types of seismic oscillations and associated gravitational waves, but this prediction depends on the assumption that the magnetic field is sufficiently strong and that chirality flipping from finite quark masses does not completely suppress the chiral transport.

## Method

The author performs a hydrodynamic analysis incorporating chiral transport phenomena (chiral magnetic effect and chiral separation effect) to study collective modes in quark matter inside magnetized neutron stars and neutrino matter in rotating supernova cores. For the collisionless regime, the author derives dispersion relations using chiral kinetic theory in the weak magnetic field limit and the lowest Landau level approximation in the strong magnetic field limit, incorporating dynamical electromagnetic fields and the chiral anomaly.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear density threshold: $\rho_0 \sim 10^{14}~{\rm g/cm^3}$ (density above which quark matter may exist in neutron star cores)
- QCD scale: $\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \simeq 200~{\rm MeV}$ (characteristic energy scale for nonperturbative QCD phenomena)
- Up quark mass: $m_{\rm u} \simeq 3~{\rm MeV}$; down quark mass: $m_{\rm d} \simeq 5~{\rm MeV}$; strange quark mass: $m_{\rm s} \simeq 100~{\rm MeV}$ (quark masses relevant for chirality flipping estimates)
- Temperature scale for QCD phase diagram: $T \sim 10^{12}~{\rm K}$ (corresponding to $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of chiral transport phenomena in relativistic plasmas, including the chiral magnetic effect (Vilenkin 1980, Fukushima 2008) and the chiral magnetic wave, by applying them to neutron star asteroseismology. It builds on the framework of chiral kinetic theory (Son 2012, Stephanov 2012) and previous work on collective modes in magnetized plasmas (Gorsky 2012, Stephanov 2014). The thesis introduces the concept of "chiral asteroseismology" as a new framework connecting microscopic chiral quantum effects to macroscopic stellar oscillations, and it enables observational tests of dense matter properties through gravitational wave astronomy, particularly following the first detection of binary neutron star mergers by LIGO (2017).
