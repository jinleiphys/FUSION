# Study of $^{44}$Ti in a Mixed--Symmetry Basis

> The structure of $^{44}$Ti is studied in an oblique-basis that includes spherical and SU(3) shell-model basis states. The results show that the oblique-basis concept is applicable, even though the strong spin-orbit interaction, which breaks the SU(3) symmetry, generates significant splitting of the single-particle levels. Specifically, a model space that includes a few SU(3) irreducible representations (irreps), namely, the leading (12,0) and next to the leading (10,1) irreps -- including spin S=0 and 1 configurations of the latter, plus spherical shell-model configurations (SSMC) that have at least two valence nucleons confined to the $f_{7/2}$ orbit -- the SM(2) case, yield results that are comparable to SSMC with at least one valence nucleon confined to the $f_{7/2}$ orbit -- the SM(3) case.

## Key claim

The oblique-basis concept, which combines spherical and SU(3) shell-model basis states, is applicable to $^{44}$Ti in the lower $pf$-shell even though the strong spin-orbit interaction badly breaks SU(3) symmetry; in this regime the spherical shell-model configurations are dominant and SU(3) is recessive, the opposite of the situation previously found in the $sd$-shell for $^{24}$Mg.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for $^{44}$Ti using the KB3 interaction in an oblique (non-orthogonal) basis that mixes spherical shell-model configurations (SSMC) with a few SU(3) irreducible representations: the leading (12,0) irrep and the next-to-leading (10,1) irrep including both S=0 and S=1 configurations. They compared results from pure spherical bases SM(n) (with n valence particles free in the $pf$-shell and the rest confined to $f_{7/2}$), pure SU(3) bases, and combined oblique bases, evaluating ground-state energies, low-lying spectra, and wave-function overlaps with the exact full $pf$-shell calculation.

## Key numbers

- The SM(2)+(12,0)&(10,1) oblique basis uses 49.8% of the full $pf$-shell space (47.7% from SM(2) plus 2.1% from the SU(3) irreps) and yields a ground-state energy of -13.76 MeV, comparable to the SM(3) result of -13.74 MeV which uses 84% of the full space.
- Adding the SU(3) irreps to the SM(1) basis (increasing from 14.5% to 16.6% of the full space) lowers the ground-state energy by 0.85 MeV relative to SM(1) alone.
- The SM(2)-type calculations yield on average more than 85% overlap with the exact full-space eigenstates, while SM(3) shows overlaps greater than 97%.
- The pure SU(3) eigenstates have overlaps with the exact states that are often less than 40%.
- The total M_J=0 dimensionality of the (12,0) and (10,1) SU(3) irreps is 84, which is 2.1% of the full 4000-dimensional $pf$-shell space.

## Context

This work extends the oblique-basis concept previously introduced for $^{24}$Mg in the $sd$-shell (Gueorguiev et al., earlier paper) to the $pf$-shell nucleus $^{44}$Ti, where SU(3) symmetry is badly broken by the strong spin-orbit interaction. The results demonstrate that even when one symmetry is recessive, combining a few SU(3) irreps with truncated spherical shell-model configurations accelerates convergence and yields results comparable to much larger spherical bases. This enables efficient calculations for heavier $pf$-shell nuclei where full-space diagonalization is computationally prohibitive, and complements prior comparative studies of SU(3) and projected Hartree-Fock approaches.
