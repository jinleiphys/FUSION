# Ultraperipheral nuclear interactions

> Large-distance ultraperipheral collisions of two relativistic ions are considered. The clouds of photons surrounding the ions are responsible for their distant electromagnetic interaction. The perturbative approach and the method of equivalent photons are described. It is shown that the total cross section of these collisions increases rapidly with increasing energy and is especially large for heavy ions. Some experimental data and their comparison with theoretical approaches are described. Further proposals are discussed.

## Key claim

Ultraperipheral nuclear collisions, where ions interact electromagnetically via their surrounding photon clouds at impact parameters larger than the sum of their radii, have total cross sections that increase as the cube of the logarithm of the Lorentz factor (ln³γ), with the preasymptotic behavior and numerical coefficients depending sensitively on the ultraperipherality parameter u(Z) that incorporates form factors, impact parameter suppression, and radiative corrections.

## Method

The paper reviews the perturbative (Born approximation) approach using Feynman diagrams for two-photon processes, and the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) which factorizes the cross section into photon fluxes and the dynamical cross section of the photon-photon interaction. The EPA is formulated with form factors describing the charge distribution inside colliding nuclei and a cutoff factor P(b) that enforces the condition b > R₁ + R₂ to exclude strong interactions, with the ultraperipherality parameter u(Z) encoding these effects. The Racah formula for electron-positron pair production and its generalization to dilepton and resonance production are presented, along with Coulomb corrections and comparisons to experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for PbPb collisions producing e⁺e⁻ pairs is 1.4 kb at √sₙₙ = 10 GeV, 22.8 kb at 100 GeV, and 97.5 kb at 1 TeV (from the Racah formula, Eq. rac2)
- The Coulomb correction for Pb ions changes the numerical factor in the ln³ term from 8.3 to approximately 16, reducing the cross section by more than a factor of two at 10 GeV and by about 13% at LHC energies
- The total ultraperipheral cross section for lead nuclei at LHC can be as large as 200 kb, while the bound-free capture cross section is about 200 b
- For pp collisions at 13 TeV producing μ⁺μ⁻ pairs, the fiducial cross section after cuts is 3.35 pb from EPA, corrected to 3.06 ± 0.05 pb with absorptive effects, compared to the ATLAS measured value of 3.12 ± 0.07 (stat.) ± 0.10 (syst.) pb
- For PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair, the measured fiducial cross sections for muon pairs are on the microbarn scale due to Z⁴ enhancement

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Fermi (1924), Weizsacker and Williams (1934), Landau and Lifshitz (1934), and Racah (1937) on electromagnetic interactions of relativistic charged particles and the equivalent photon method. It connects to modern experimental programs at RHIC (STAR Collaboration) and LHC (ALICE, ATLAS Collaborations) where ultraperipheral collisions are used to study strong electromagnetic fields, test QED at high energies, and search for new physics. The paper enables future studies at NICA and FAIR facilities (with √sₙₙ about 10 GeV) where preasymptotic behavior becomes important, and motivates improved treatment of the ultraperipherality parameter u(Z) through comparison with experimental data on dilepton production.
