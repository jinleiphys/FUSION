# Dimensional reduction and the generalized pion in a magnetic field within the NJL model

> In this work, the mass of the neutral pion is investigated in the presence of background magnetic fields in the framework of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Taking into account the anisotropic four-fermion interactions, a tensor current arises in the magnetized QCD system, which forms an anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) coupling in the Dirac equation for the quarks. By solving the gap equations, we find that the sign of the dynamically generated AMM is opposite to the sign of the quark's charge and its magnitude is definitely smaller than the constituent mass. We construct two generalized Nambu-Goldstone pions, which emerge as combinations of the quantum fluctuations around the conventional scalar and the emergent tensor chiral condensates. We analytically demonstrate that the Goldstone nature has been spoiled by the dimensional reduction in the two-particle state and the corresponding decreasing mass of the lighter generalized pionic mode is a remnant of the infrared dynamics.

## Key claim

The neutral pion's Goldstone nature is spoiled by dimensional reduction in the two-particle state, and the decreasing mass of the lighter generalized pionic mode is a remnant of infrared dynamics, contrary to the expectation that Goldstone's theorem would protect the pion mass in a magnetic field.

## Method

The authors employ the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with anisotropic four-fermion interactions that include both scalar and tensor channels, derived from Fierz transformations accounting for the reduced O(2) rotational symmetry in a magnetic field. They solve coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate and the dynamically generated anomalous magnetic moment (AMM), then construct the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the neutral pion as a two-by-two matrix mixing scalar and tensor quark-antiquark fluctuations in the random phase approximation.

## Key numbers

- The dynamically generated AMM magnitude is definitely smaller than the constituent quark mass (no explicit numerical ratio given, but the condition kappa < M is established analytically)
- The lowest Landau level contribution I_1 remains finite as (M, kappa) approach zero, demonstrating dimensional reduction from 3+1 to 2+1 dimensions in the one-point correlator
- For the case M_l^2 < kappa^2 < M_{l+1}^2 with l=1, the authors find no solution exists for G_S >= G_T, establishing the condition kappa < M as the only viable scenario

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations by Xu (2020) that showed AMM effects cause a monotonic decrease in neutral pion mass with magnetic field strength, consistent with lattice QCD simulations by Bali (2017, 2018) and Ding (2020). The paper contradicts the expectation from Coleman (1973) and Gusynin (1995) that dimensional reduction does not affect neutral NG modes, and instead demonstrates that the presence of two condensates (scalar and tensor) leads to mixing that spoils the Goldstone nature. The results enable future studies of generalized pionic modes as proper degrees of freedom in magnetized QCD matter, following the framework of Yamamoto (2007) and Song (2017).
