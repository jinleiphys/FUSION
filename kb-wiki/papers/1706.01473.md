# Analysis of vector boson production within TMD factorization

> We present a comprehensive analysis and extraction of the unpolarized transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions, which are fundamental constituents of the TMD factorization theorem. We provide a general review of the theory of TMD distributions, and present a new scheme of scale fixation. This scheme, called the $ζ$-prescription, allows to minimize the impact of perturbative logarithms in the large range of scales and does not generate undesired power corrections. Within $ζ$-prescription we consistently include the perturbatively calculable parts up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), and perform the global fit of the Drell-Yan and Z-boson production, which include the data of E288, Tevatron and LHC experiments. The nonperturbative part of the TMDs are explored checking a large variety of models. We support the obtained results by a study of theoretical uncertainties, perturbative convergence, and dedicated study of the range of applicability of the TMD factorization theorem. The considered nonperturbative models present significant differences in the fitting behavior, which allow us to clearly disfavor most of them. The numerical evaluations are provided by the "arTeMiDe" code, which is introduced in this work and that can be used for current/future TMD phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new scheme of scale fixation called the ζ-prescription, which minimizes the impact of perturbative logarithms over a large range of scales without generating undesired power corrections, and claims that this scheme allows a consistent global fit of Drell-Yan and Z-boson production data from low to high energies at NNLO precision for the first time.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of unpolarized transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) using the ζ-prescription for scale fixation, including perturbative calculations up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They analyzed Drell-Yan and Z-boson production data from the E288, Tevatron, and LHC experiments, testing a variety of non-perturbative models for the TMDs. The numerical evaluations were carried out using the arTeMiDe code, which was introduced in this work.

## Key numbers

- The lowest energy measurements included in the fits have (Q, sqrt(s)) = (4, 19.4) GeV (E288 experiment)
- The most energetic measurements have (Q, sqrt(s)) = (116-150, 8 x 10^3) GeV (ATLAS collaboration)
- The Z-boson mass used is M_Z = 91.2 GeV, with width Gamma_Z = 2.5 GeV, and Weinberg angle s^2_W = 0.2313
- The TMD anomalous dimensions are known up to order a_s^3 (three-loop order)
- The matching coefficient function C is known up to two-loop order

## Context

This work extends previous TMD phenomenology studies based on the ResBos code (Landry, 2002) and the DYqT/DYRes codes (Catani, 2007; Bozzi, 2010; Catani, 2015) by including NNLO perturbative ingredients and introducing the ζ-prescription to solve the problem of perturbative convergence at large transverse distance b. The authors also include LHC data in a TMD fit for the first time, and their findings disfavor the commonly used Gaussian ansatz for the non-perturbative TMD shape, instead finding that low-energy data prefer an exponential type of non-perturbative model. The arTeMiDe code is made available for future TMD phenomenology studies.
