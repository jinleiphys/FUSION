# Importance of Multiplicity Fluctuations in Entropy Scaling

> One of the greatest uncertainties in heavy-ion collisions is the description of the initial state. Different models predict a wide range of initial energy density distributions based on their underlying assumptions. Final flow harmonics are sensitive to these differences in the initial state due to the nearly linear mapping between eccentricities and anisotropic flow harmonics. The Trento code uses a model-agnostic approach by phenomenologically parameterizing the initial state and constraining those parameters from a Bayesian analysis. There the multiplicity fluctuations were determined by a one parameter $Γ$ distribution. However, initial-state models arising from the Color-Glass Condensate (CGC) framework lead to an initial energy density which is outside the functional form considered in Trento and its later Bayesian analyses because they rely on log-normal multiplicity fluctuations. We compare $T_{A}T_{B}$ scaling (CGC-like) to $\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}$ scaling (preferred from a Trento Bayesian analysis) and find that the $T_A T_B$ form together with log-normal fluctuations is a reasonable candidate to describe the multiplicity fluctuations but leads to larger eccentricities, which would affect the extraction of viscosity in small systems.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a Color-Glass Condensate-like linear scaling of initial energy density ($T_A T_B$) combined with log-normal multiplicity fluctuations can reproduce experimental multiplicity distributions, but this combination leads to larger initial eccentricities than the Trento-preferred $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ scaling with Gamma fluctuations, which would affect the extraction of shear viscosity in small systems.

## Method

The authors modified the Trento initial-state code to implement both linear scaling ($\epsilon \propto T_A T_B$) and log-normal multiplicity fluctuations, then compared these to the standard Trento form ($s \propto \sqrt{T_A T_B}$) with Gamma fluctuations. They tuned the fluctuation parameter $k$ for each combination to best reproduce STAR data for deuteron-gold (dAu) collisions at 200 GeV, then computed the two-particle eccentricities $\varepsilon_2\{2\}$ and $\varepsilon_3\{2\}$ as functions of centrality.

## Key numbers

- For dAu collisions at 200 GeV, all four combinations of scaling and fluctuation distributions agree with STAR multiplicity data below $N_{ch}/\langle N_{ch}\rangle = 4$
- The $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ scaling with log-normal fluctuations overpredicts the high-multiplicity tail of the dAu multiplicity distribution by a significant amount
- Linear scaling ($T_A T_B$) favors a narrow distribution (few fluctuations) while $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ prefers a wider distribution (many fluctuations) to match the same data
- The two functional forms produce noticeably different magnitudes of $\varepsilon_2\{2\}$ and $\varepsilon_3\{2\}$, with $T_A T_B$ leading to larger eccentricities except for a dip in $\varepsilon_3\{2\}$ in central events

## Context

This work extends the Trento Bayesian analysis by Moreland (2018) which assumed a Gamma distribution for multiplicity fluctuations and found preference for $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ scaling. The paper directly compares to the CGC-like linear scaling investigated by Nagle (2018) and others, showing that the Trento parameter space may have been overly restrictive. The results indicate systematic uncertainty in the extraction of QGP viscosities from small systems, suggesting that future Bayesian analyses should expand the functional space of initial-state models.
