# Quantum Numbers of Recently Discovered $Ω^{0}_{c}$ Baryons from Lattice QCD

> We present the ground and excited state spectra of $Ω^{0}_{c}$ baryons with spin up to 7/2 from lattice quantum chromodynamics with dynamical quark fields. Based on our lattice results, we predict the quantum numbers of five $Ω^{0}_{c}$ baryons, which have recently been observed by the LHCb Collaboration. Our results strongly indicate that the observed states $Ω_c(3000)^0$ and $Ω_c(3050)^0$ have spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^{-}$, the states $Ω_c(3066)^0$ and $Ω_c(3090)^0$ have $J^P = 3/2^{-}$, whereas $Ω_c(3119)^0$ is possibly a $5/2^{-}$ state.

## Key claim
The authors use lattice QCD to predict that the five newly observed Omega_c baryons have negative parity, with the specific assignments: Omega_c(3000) and Omega_c(3050) as 1/2-, Omega_c(3066) and Omega_c(3090) as 3/2-, and Omega_c(3119) as possibly 5/2-. The hedge is that the 5/2- assignment for Omega_c(3119) is tentative ("possibly"), and the results depend on the lattice parameters used (pion mass ~391 MeV, not physical).

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using anisotropic gauge field configurations (16^3 x 128 lattice, a_t ~0.035 fm, m_pi ~391 MeV) with dynamical light and strange quarks. They constructed a large set of baryonic interpolating fields transforming under lattice irreducible representations, computed correlation matrices using the distillation method with 64 eigenvectors, and extracted energy levels via a variational analysis solving a generalized eigenvalue problem. Spin identification was performed by analyzing overlap factors of operators onto extracted states, comparing results across different lattice irreps (G1, G2, H). A second independent calculation using MILC HISQ ensembles with overlap fermions (three lattice spacings: ~0.12, 0.09, 0.06 fm) was performed for cross-validation.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a_t ~0.035 fm with anisotropy 3.5; lattice size 16^3 x 128; pion mass m_pi ~391 MeV; 96 configurations
- Energy splitting of ground state Omega_c(1/2+) from half of eta_c mass: 1209(7) MeV (lattice L1) vs 1203(2) MeV (experiment)
- Hyperfine splitting between 3/2+ and 1/2+ ground state: 65(11) MeV (lattice L1) vs 70.7(1) MeV (experiment)
- Energy splittings from ground state for the five new states (lattice L1): 304(17) MeV (1/2-), 341(18) MeV (1/2-), 383(21) MeV (3/2-), 409(19) MeV (3/2-), 464(20) MeV (5/2-)
- Experimental masses of the five states: 3000, 3050, 3066, 3090, 3119 MeV (each with 1 MeV uncertainty)
- Scattering thresholds on lattice: Xi_c K at 373 MeV, Xi'_c K at 488 MeV above ground state

## Context
This work provides the first nonperturbative lattice QCD prediction of quantum numbers for the five Omega_c baryons discovered by LHCb (Aaij 2017), extending prior lattice studies that focused mainly on ground states of heavy baryons (Mathur 2002, Lewis 2001, Durr 2012). The methodology follows the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration's established approach for extracting excited baryon spectra (Edwards 2011, Edwards 2012), previously applied to triply charmed (Padmanath 2013) and doubly charmed baryons (Padmanath 2015). The results contradict most nonlattice calculations (Ebert 2007, Ebert 2011, Garcilazo 2007, Valcarce 2008, Roberts 2007, Vijande 2012, Yoshida 2015, Shah 2016) which predicted seven levels in this region, while a potential model calculation (Karliner 2017) made a similar spin-parity assignment. The work enables experimental confirmation of these quantum numbers through decay angular distribution studies at LHCb.
