# Recent Experimental Results on Nuclear Cluster Physics

> Knowledge on nuclear cluster physics has increased considerably since the pioneering discovery of 12C+12C resonances half a century ago and nuclear clustering remains one of the most fruitful domains of nuclear physics, facing some of the greatest challenges and opportunities in the years ahead. The occurrence of "exotic" shapes and/or Bose-Einstein alpha condensates in light N-Z alpha-conjugate nuclei is investigated. Evolution of clustering from stability to the drip-lines examined with clustering aspects persisting in light neutron-rich nuclei is consistent with the extension of the "Ikeda-diagram" to non alpha-conjugate nuclei.

## Key claim

Nuclear clustering remains a fruitful domain of nuclear physics, but the existence of specific exotic structures such as linear chain states in ¹⁶O and Bose-Einstein alpha condensates in medium-mass nuclei has not yet been definitively observed, despite theoretical predictions and ongoing experimental searches.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from resonant scattering, transfer reactions, and gamma-ray spectroscopy on light alpha-conjugate and neutron-rich nuclei, comparing excitation functions and angular distributions to theoretical models such as the cranked alpha-cluster model and Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations. It surveys data from reactions including ¹²C(α,α)¹²C*, ³²S+²⁴Mg, and alpha-particle inelastic scattering on ¹²C, as well as studies of oxygen isotopes from ¹⁷O to ²⁰O using radioactive ion beams and particle-gamma coincidence techniques.

## Key numbers

- The predicted 0⁺₆ state in ¹⁶O, with a structure analogous to the Hoyle state coupled to an alpha particle, lies at about 15.1 MeV, approximately 700 keV above the 4α-particle breakup threshold.
- Nuclear shapes with major-to-minor axis ratios of 2:1 correspond to a quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ ≈ 0.6 for superdeformation in light nuclei.
- Octupole-unstable 3:1 nuclear shapes (hyperdeformation) correspond to β₂ ≈ 1.0 for actinide nuclei.
- The 2⁺₂ "Hoyle" rotational excitation in ¹²C has been observed.
- The ¹²C(α,α)¹²C* reaction populates a new state compatible with an equilateral triangle configuration of three alpha particles.

## Context

This review extends the original Ikeda-diagram (Ikeda, 1968) to non-alpha-conjugate and neutron-rich nuclei, following the modification by von Oertzen (2001) and further revision to include ¹⁴C cluster cores. It builds on earlier experimental work by Chevallier (1967) on 4α-chain states in ¹⁶O and recent studies by Curtis (2013) that found no evidence for such chain configurations. The paper enables future searches for Bose-Einstein alpha condensates in medium-mass nuclei such as ²⁴Mg and for molecular chain structures using more sophisticated particle-gamma coincidence setups.
