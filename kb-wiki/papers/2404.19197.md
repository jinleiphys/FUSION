# Does the Electron EDM Preclude Electroweak Baryogenesis ?

> Electroweak baryogenesis (EWBG) constitutes a theoretically compelling and experimentally testable mechanism for explaining the origin of the baryon asymmetry of the universe (BAU). New results for the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the electron place significant constraints on the beyond Standard Model CP-violation needed for successful EWBG. Using a specific model illustration, we show how new developments in EWBG quantum transport theory that include CP-violating sources first order in gradients imply more relaxed EDM constraints -- and thereby greater EWBG viability -- than implied by previous approximation formulations. We also illustrate how these developments enable a more realistic treatment of CP-conserving interactions that can also have a decisive impact on the predicted BAU.

## Key claim

Using a specific model illustration, the authors show that a consistent treatment of CP-violating sources first order in gradients (the "vev resummation" or VR framework) yields a significantly larger baryon asymmetry than predicted by the semiclassical (SC) approximation, and can even exceed the "vev insertion approximation" (VIA) result by a factor of four or more, thereby relaxing electron EDM constraints on electroweak baryogenesis viability.

## Method

The authors solve the Kadanoff-Baym transport equations for scalar field density matrices using the vev resummation (VR) framework, which includes CP-violating sources that are first order in gradients of the background fields, avoiding both the theoretical inconsistencies of the VIA and the approximations of the SC method. They apply this formalism to a specific "Two-Step EWBG" model with two Higgs doublets, a real triplet, and a singlet scalar, discretizing momentum and angular variables to solve the resulting system of coupled integro-differential equations via the relaxation method. The baryon asymmetry is then obtained by integrating the left-handed fermion density ahead of the bubble wall weighted by the weak sphaleron rate.

## Key numbers

- The VR baryon asymmetry near the resonance \(m_{H_1} \approx m_{H_2}\) is more than **four times larger** in magnitude than the VIA value.
- For a representative momentum \(k = T\) at the resonance peak, the ratio of oscillation frequency to effective damping rate is \(\Delta\omega_k / \Gamma_{\text{eff}}(k) \approx 2.1\).
- The VR approach predicts a significantly larger BAU than the VIA for wall velocities \(v_w \lesssim 0.13\), while VIA results may exceed VR results for larger velocities.
- The present electron EDM bound is \(|d_e| < 4.1 \times 10^{-30} \, e \, \text{cm}\), which excludes parameter space above the solid black line in the \(\delta_\Sigma\) vs. \(m_{h_2}\) plane.

## Context

This work extends the VR framework developed by Cirigliano et al. (2009, 2011) for treating CP-violating transport during electroweak baryogenesis, and directly addresses the order-of-magnitude spread between VIA and SC predictions noted by Cline et al. (2020) and Kainulainen et al. (2021). The results demonstrate that EWBG remains viable under current electron EDM limits from the ACME collaboration (2018) and the updated bound from Alarcon et al. (2022), motivating continued experimental EDM searches and providing a more robust theoretical foundation for assessing the EWBG-EDM interface in any model with background field-induced CPV mixing between two species.
