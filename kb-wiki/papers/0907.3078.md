# Effects of Bulk Viscosity on $p_T$-Spectra and Elliptic Flow Parameter

> We estimate the effects of viscosity on the phase space distribution appearing in the Cooper-Frye formula within the framework of the Grad's fourteen moment method and find that there are non-trivialities in the discussion of a multi-component system. We calculate the viscous corrections of particle spectra and elliptic flow coefficients from the distortion of the distribution using the flow and the hypersurface taken from a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulation. We see that the bulk viscosity have visible effects on particle spectra. The results suggest we should treat both shear and bulk viscosity carefully when constraining the transport coefficients from experimental data.

# Effects of Bulk Viscosity on \(p_T\)-Spectra and Elliptic Flow Parameter

## Key claim
Bulk viscosity has visible effects on particle spectra and elliptic flow when estimated through the distortion of the distribution function at freezeout, but the authors caution that the viscous corrections may be overestimated because they use the Navier-Stokes limit for the bulk pressure and ideal hydrodynamic flow profiles rather than a fully viscous hydrodynamic simulation.

## Method
The authors generalize Grad's 14-moment method to a relativistic multi-component gas to obtain a unique and consistent expression for the distortion of the phase space distribution \(\delta f^i\) at freezeout. They then compute the viscous corrections to particle spectra and elliptic flow coefficients using the Cooper-Frye formula, taking the flow and freezeout hypersurface from a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulation of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, with a 16-component hadron resonance gas equation of state and the bulk pressure estimated in the Navier-Stokes limit \(\Pi = -\zeta \partial_\mu u^\mu\).

## Key numbers
- Freezeout temperature \(T_f = 0.160\) GeV
- Shear viscosity \(\eta = 1.31 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\) at freezeout (using \(\eta = s/4\pi\))
- Bulk viscosity \(\zeta = 4.37 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\) at freezeout when the free parameter \(\alpha = 15\)
- Impact parameter for Au+Au collisions: 7.2 fm
- Negative pion mass used in calculations: \(m = 0.139\) GeV

## Context
This work extends the ideal hydrodynamic models of Hirano (2002) and Hirano and Tsuda (2002) that were successful at RHIC, by adding viscous corrections to the distribution function at freezeout. It builds on the Grad 14-moment method developed by Israel (1979) for single-component systems and generalizes it to multi-component hadron resonance gas. The authors note that the quadratic ansatz of Dusling (2008) leads to thermodynamic instability because the matching conditions are not satisfied. A (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model is identified as necessary to accurately constrain transport coefficients from experimental data.

**Review-type paper: no original numerics.**
