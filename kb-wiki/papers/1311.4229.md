# Functional renormalization group approach to conventional theory of superfluidity and beyond

> Fermionic functional renormalization group (FRG) is applied to describe the superfluid phase transition of the two-component fermionic system with attractive contact interaction. Connection between the fermionic FRG approach and the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory with its Gorkov and Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) correction is made clear, and the FRG flow of the fermion self-energy is also studied to go beyond the BCS+GMB theory. The superfluid transition temperature and the associated chemical potential are calculated in the region of the negative scattering length using fermionic FRG.

## Key claim

The fermionic functional renormalization group (f-FRG) approach, without introducing an auxiliary bosonic field, reproduces the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory with the Gorkov and Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) correction as a limiting case, and including the RG flow of the fermion self-energy yields further small corrections to the superfluid transition temperature and a significant reduction of the chemical potential ratio, particularly near the unitary regime.

## Method

The authors applied fermionic functional renormalization group (f-FRG) to a non-relativistic two-component fermion system with an attractive contact interaction, using a vertex expansion of the scale-dependent 1PI effective action up to the four-point vertex. They derived and solved coupled flow equations for the fermion self-energy (taken as a constant, momentum-independent part) and the four-point vertex, including both particle-particle (PP) and particle-hole (PH) loop contributions, and determined the superfluid transition temperature via the Thouless criterion (divergence of the fermion-fermion scattering matrix at zero total momentum) together with the number equation.

## Key numbers

- BCS transition temperature in the weakly coupled regime: \(T_c^{\mathrm{BCS}}/\varepsilon_F = 0.613 \exp(-\pi/(2 k_F |a_s|))\)
- GMB correction factor: the transition temperature is reduced by a factor \((4e)^{1/3} \approx 2.2\), giving \(T_c^{\mathrm{GMB}} = T_c^{\mathrm{BCS}} / 2.2\)
- At unitarity (\((k_F a_s)^{-1} = 0\)), the f-FRG result including self-energy gives \((T_c/\varepsilon_F, \mu/\varepsilon_F) = (0.237, 0.367)\)
- For comparison, previous FRG results using an auxiliary bosonic field at unitarity gave \((T_c/\varepsilon_F, \mu/\varepsilon_F) \simeq (0.248, 0.51)\)
- Monte Carlo simulation results quoted for comparison at unitarity: \((0.152(7), 0.493(14))\) from PhysRevLett.96.160402 and PhysRevLett.101.090402; \((0.15(1), 0.43(1))\) from PhysRevA.78.023625; \((0.171(5), 0.429(9))\) from PhysRevA.82.053621

## Context

This work extends the conventional BCS theory and its GMB correction by providing a non-perturbative f-FRG framework that clarifies the connection between these approaches and allows systematic inclusion of self-energy effects beyond the BCS+GMB level. The results enable comparison with previous FRG studies that used bosonization (PhysRevA.81.063619) and with Monte Carlo simulations, and the analysis shows that the PH correlation is the dominant source of \(T_c\) reduction while the self-energy correction mainly affects the chemical potential ratio, with both effects becoming more important toward the unitary regime.
