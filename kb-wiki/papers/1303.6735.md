# Coupling strength of the $N^*(1535)S_{11}$ to the $K^+Λ$ channel

> The $N^*(1535)S_{11}$ coupling strength to the $K^+Λ$ channel, $g_{N^*(1535)ΛK^+}$, is extracted from the latest and largest $K^+Λ$ photoproduction database by using an isobar model. It is found that the coupling is small. In term of the coupling ratio the best result is $R\equiv |g_{N^*(1535)ΛK^+}/g_{N^*(1535)ηp}|=0.460\pm 0.172$, much smaller than that obtained from the isobar analysis of the $J/ψ$ decays, i.e., $1.3\pm 0.3$, but consistent with the results of the unitary chiral approach of the same decay processes as well as the partial wave analysis of kaon photoproduction, i.e., $R=0.5\sim 0.7$. The different results of $R$ found here and in literature to date suggest that a more solid definition of the coupling constant, especially in the case of $N^*(1535)S_{11}$ state, is urgently required, before a fair comparison can be made.

# Coupling strength of the N*(1535)S11 to the K+Λ channel

## Key claim

The coupling ratio R = |g_{N*(1535)ΛK+} / g_{N*(1535)ηp}| is extracted from the largest K+Λ photoproduction database to date and found to be 0.460 ± 0.172, which is significantly smaller than the value of 1.3 ± 0.3 obtained from isobar analysis of J/ψ decays, but the result is not conclusive due to conceptual differences in how coupling constants are defined across different analyses.

## Method

An isobar model was constructed from s-, u-, and t-channel Born terms along with vector mesons (K*+(892), K1(1270)), hyperon resonances (Λ*(1800)S01, Λ*(1810)P01, and Λ*(1600)P01), and nine nucleon resonances in the s-channel. The model was fitted to a database of more than 3500 data points including differential cross sections, recoil polarization, beam-recoil double polarization, photon Σ asymmetry, and target T asymmetry from CLAS, GRAAL, and LEPS collaborations. The electromagnetic coupling g_{γpN*} was fixed using the PDG helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^p = 0.090 ± 0.030 GeV^{-1/2}, and the hadronic coupling g_{N*(1535)ηp} was calculated from the PDG branching ratio Γ(Nη)/Γ_total = 0.42 ± 0.10.

## Key numbers

- Coupling ratio R = 0.460 ± 0.172 from the most reliable model (A2), compared to 0.354 ± 0.138 (model A1) and 0.253 ± 0.099 (model B1)
- Extracted hadronic coupling g_{N*(1535)ΛK+} = 0.853 ± 0.298 (model A2), 0.656 ± 0.240 (model A1), and 0.469 ± 0.172 (model B1)
- Product of electromagnetic and hadronic couplings g_{γpN*} g_{N*(1535)ΛK+} = 0.286 ± 0.030 (model A2), 0.220 ± 0.033 (model A1), and 0.157 ± 0.024 (model B1)
- g_{N*(1535)ηp} = 1.853 ± 0.244, calculated from PDG branching ratio
- χ² values: 8716 (model A2), 9035 (model A1), 9480 (model B1) for databases of over 3500 data points
- Correlation coefficient between N*(1535)S11 and N*(1650)S11 resonances is approximately 0.94 for model A2

## Context

This work extends previous isobar model analyses of K+Λ photoproduction (Mart 2012) by including the N*(1535)S11 resonance and refitting the largest available database. The result R = 0.460 ± 0.172 is consistent with the unitary chiral approach (Geng 2009) and partial wave analysis of kaon photoproduction (Sarantsev 2005), which found R = 0.5-0.7, but contradicts the larger value R = 1.3 ± 0.3 from isobar analysis of J/ψ decays (Liu and Zou 2006). The paper enables future extractions that use the residue of the transition amplitude at the pole position, as suggested in the 2012 Review of Particle Physics, to resolve conceptual differences in coupling constant definitions.
