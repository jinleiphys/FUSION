# Exotic dibaryons with a heavy antiquark

> We discuss the possible existence of exotic dibaryons with a heavy antiquark, being realized three-body systems, Dbar^(*)NN and B^(*)NN. These are genuinely exotic states with no quark-antiquark annihilation. We consider the heavy quark spin and chiral symmetries, and introduce the one pion exchange potential between a Dbar^(*) (B^(*)) meson and a nucleon N. As for the NN interaction, we employ the Argonne v^\prime_8 potential. By solving the coupled-channel equations for PNN and P^* NN (P^(*)=Dbar^(*) and B^(*)), we find bound states for (I,J^P)=(1/2,0^-) as well as resonant states for (I,J^P)=(1/2,1^-) both in Dbar^(*)NN and B^(*)NN systems. We also discuss the heavy quark limit, and find that the spin degeneracy is realized in the bound states with (I,J^P)=(1/2,0^-) and (1/2,1^-).

## Key claim

The paper claims that three-body systems of a heavy anti-meson (anti-D* or B*) and two nucleons can form bound states (for quantum numbers I=1/2, J^P=0^-) and resonant states (for I=1/2, J^P=1^-), but this result depends on using only the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) for the meson-nucleon interaction while neglecting short-range repulsions that other studies have argued may prevent binding.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channel Schrodinger equations for the PNN and P*NN systems (where P* = anti-D* or B*) using the Gaussian expansion method. They employed the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) for the heavy meson-nucleon interaction, respecting heavy quark spin and chiral symmetries, and used the Argonne v'_8 potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Bound and resonant states were identified by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and using the complex scaling method for resonances.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of anti-D*NN: -5.2 MeV below the anti-DN(1/2^-) + N threshold
- Binding energy of B*NN: -26.2 MeV below the BN(1/2^-) + N threshold
- Two-body anti-DN(1/2^-) binding energy: -1.6 MeV for J^P=1/2^- and I=0
- Resonance energy for anti-D*NN (J^P=1^-): 111.2 MeV measured from anti-DNN threshold, with decay width 18.6 MeV
- Resonance energy for B*NN (J^P=1^-): 6.8 MeV measured from BNN threshold, with decay width 0.4 MeV
- In the heavy quark limit (m_Q -> infinity), bound states for both J^P=0^- and 1^- have the same binding energy: -38.5 MeV measured from P*_QNN threshold
- Tensor force expectation value for anti-DN-anti-D*N mixing: -47.1 MeV (dominant contribution)
- Tensor force expectation value for BN-B*N mixing: -92.0 MeV (dominant contribution)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of two-body anti-D*N and B*N molecules (Yasui 2009, Yamaguchi 2011) to three-body systems with baryon number two, which had not been investigated before for heavy flavor sectors. The results contradict studies by Haidenbauer (2007) and Carames (2012) that suggested repulsive short-range interactions might prevent binding. The paper enables experimental searches for these exotic dibaryons at RHIC, LHC, J-PARC, and GSI-FAIR, and demonstrates that spin degeneracy from heavy quark symmetry survives in multi-hadron systems.
