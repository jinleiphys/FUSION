# Polarized deuteron charge-exchange reaction $dp->{pp}_sNπ$ in the Delta-isobar region

> Mechanisms of the charge exchange reaction $dp\to \{pp\}_{\!s} Nπ$, where $\{pp\}_{\!s}$ is a two-proton system at low excitation energy, are studied at beam energies 1 -- 2 GeV and for invariant masses $M_X$ of the final $Nπ$ system that correspond to the formation of the $Δ(1232)$ isobar. The direct mechanism, where the initial proton is excited into the $Δ(1232)$, dominates and explains the existing data on the unpolarized differential cross section and spherical tensor analyzing power $T_{22}$ for $M_X> 1.2$ GeV/$c^2$. However, this model fails to describe $T_{20}.

## Key claim
The direct one-pion-exchange mechanism, where the initial proton is excited into the Δ(1232) isobar, dominates the charge-exchange reaction and explains the unpolarized differential cross section and spherical tensor analyzing power T₂₂ for invariant masses M_X > 1.2 GeV/c², but this model fails to describe the tensor analyzing power T₂₀.

## Method
The authors employed Feynman diagram techniques to calculate the direct (D) and exchange (E) mechanisms of the dp → {pp}_s Nπ reaction, using both π and ρ meson exchange for the elementary pN → ΔN amplitudes. The d → {pp}_s transition form factor was evaluated using the CD-Bonn interaction potential, and the vertex form factors were taken in monopole form with cut-off parameters fixed from fits to exclusive pp → pnπ⁺ data. The Cartesian tensor analyzing powers A_xx and A_yy were computed as functions of the transverse momentum transfer q_t and integrated over the invariant mass M_X of the final Nπ system.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies studied: 1.6, 1.8, and 2.3 GeV (from ANKE@COSY data)
- Invariant mass range where the D-mechanism explains the cross section shape: M_X > 1.2 GeV/c²
- Cut-off parameter for π exchange: Λ = 0.5 GeV
- Cut-off parameters for ρ exchange: Λ_ρNN = Λ_ρNΔ = 0.7 GeV (standard) and 1.3 GeV (increased)
- Low excitation energy of the {pp}_s pair: E_pp < 3 MeV
- At q_t = 170 MeV/c, A_xx ≈ 0.5 for the π+ρ model with standard cut-off parameters

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of the dp → {pp}_s n reaction at ANKE@COSY (Chiladze, Kacharava) into the Δ-isobar region, where the reaction produces an additional pion. The direct mechanism was previously applied to the p(³He,t)Δ⁺⁺ reaction (Ellegaard) and to the dp → {pp}_sΔ⁰ reaction at SATURNE (Ellegaard, Dmitriev). The failure of the π+ρ exchange model to describe T₂₀ suggests that a more sophisticated NN → NΔ amplitude beyond undistorted meson exchange is needed, and that explicit treatment of the quasi-three-body final states (π⁰n{pp}_s and π⁻p{pp}_s) may be required for lower invariant masses M_X < 1.2 GeV/c².
