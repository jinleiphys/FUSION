# Phenomena at the QCD phase transition in nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (N$χ$FD)

> Heavy-ion collisions performed in the beam energy range accessible by the NICA collider facility are expected to produce systems of extreme net-baryon densities and can thus reach yet unexplored regions of the QCD phase diagram. Here, one expects the phase transition between the plasma of deconfined quarks and gluons and the hadronic matter to be of first order. A discovery of the first-order phase transition would as well prove the existence of the QCD critical point, a landmark in the phase diagram. In order to understand possible signals of the first-order phase transition in heavy-ion collision experiments it is very important to develop dynamical models of the phase transition. Here, we discuss the opportunities of studying dynamical effects at the QCD first-order phase transition within our model of nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nonequilibrium effects, particularly supercooling and spinodal decomposition, are crucial for understanding signals of a first-order QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collisions at NICA energies, but the authors hedge that quantitative predictions require improved treatment of the hadronic phase and realistic initial conditions.

## Method

The authors use nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (NχFD), coupling stochastic equations of motion for the chiral order parameter (sigma field) and Polyakov loop to fluid-dynamical equations for the quark-antiquark background. The sigma field evolves via a Langevin equation with a damping coefficient calculated from scattering with quarks, while the Polyakov loop uses a phenomenological relaxation equation. The fluid sector includes a stochastic source term from the sigma field evolution to ensure energy-momentum conservation. The model is tested in static box calculations with a sudden temperature quench and in expanding systems with spherical initial conditions, using both the Polyakov-loop extended quark-meson (PQM) model and a chiral quark-hadron (QH) model.

## Key numbers

- Damping coefficient for the Polyakov loop: ηℓ = 5/fm, with results "rather independent of the precise value"
- In the static box, the first-order phase transition scenario shows relaxation significantly delayed compared to the critical point scenario, with fluctuation intensity of low modes enhanced only during relaxation
- For the expanding system, snapshots are shown at times t c/fm = 0.88, 6.48, 11.28, 14.32 (from left to right in Figure 2)
- The sigma field fluctuation distribution is shown at t = 12 fm/c and t = 24 fm/c after equilibration in box calculations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of NχFD (Nahrgang et al., 2011) which showed nonequilibrium effects on order parameter fluctuations. It contrasts with thermodynamic treatments using Maxwell construction (Kolb et al., 2000; Kolb et al., 2003) and addresses the debate on how fast QGP expansion affects critical signatures (Nahrgang et al., 2014; Mukherjee et al., 2015). The paper enables future quantitative predictions for first-order phase transition signals at NICA and FAIR, particularly for enhanced flow coefficients and deuteron-to-proton ratios, by incorporating realistic hadronic interactions and initial baryon stopping.
