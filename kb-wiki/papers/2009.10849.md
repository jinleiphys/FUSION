# Variational and parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter. II. Twisted Chain Diagrams

> We develop a manifestly microscopic method to deal with strongly interacting nuclear systems that have different interactions in spin-singlet and spin-triplet states. In a first step we analyze variational wave functions that have been suggested to describe such systems, and demonstrate that the so-called commutator contributions can have important effects whenever the interactions in the spin-singlet and the spin-triplet states are very different. We then identify these contributions as terms that correspond, in the language of perturbation theory, to non-parquet diagrams. We include these diagrams in a way that is suggested by the Jastrow-Feenberg approach and show that the corrections from non-parquet contributions are, at short distances, larger than all other many-body effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that commutator contributions arising from symmetrization of operator products in variational wave functions for neutron matter correspond to non-parquet diagrams in perturbation theory, and these corrections are larger than all other many-body effects at short distances when interactions in spin-singlet and spin-triplet states are very different.

## Method

The authors analyze the symmetrized operator product (SOP) wave function for neutron matter with state-dependent interactions, identifying commutator terms that mix different operator channels. They establish a correspondence between Jastrow-Feenberg diagrams and Goldstone diagrams, then develop a localized Bethe-Goldstone equation that includes "twisted chain" diagrams as corrections to standard parquet-diagram summations. The twisted chain corrections are computed by evaluating commutators of spin operators in the basis of longitudinal (L) and transverse (T) tensor operators, with the induced interaction constructed from an energy-independent approximation to the particle-hole reducible diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The Reid interaction has different core sizes in spin-singlet and spin-triplet states, shown at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.0 fm^{-1} (Figure 1)
* The commutator correction for two longitudinal operators (L-L) vanishes: -2ν L̂²(a,b) + 2ν t^{(L)}_{αβ}t^{(L)}_{αβ} = 0
* The commutator correction for longitudinal and transverse operators (L-T) yields: -2ν L̂(a,b)T̂(a,b) + 2ν t^{(L)}_{αβ}t^{(T)}_{αβ} = 2ν T̂(1,2)
* The commutator correction for two transverse operators (T-T) yields: -2ν T̂(a,b)² + 2ν t^{(T)}_{αβ}t^{(T)}_{αβ} = 4ν L̂(a,b)

## Context

This work extends the parquet-diagram summation approach of Smith and Jackson (1970s) and the FHNC-EL method of Fantoni and Rosati (1980s) to include non-parquet diagrams that were previously omitted. The analysis builds on the variational wave function approach of Pandharipande and Bethe (1970s) and the localized Bethe-Goldstone equation developed in the authors' previous work (Ref. v3eos). The results enable systematic inclusion of commutator corrections in microscopic calculations of neutron matter, which is essential for accurate equation-of-state predictions relevant to neutron star physics.
