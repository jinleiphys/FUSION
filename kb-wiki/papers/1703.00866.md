# Nuclear dipole polarizability from mean-field modeling constrained by chiral effective field theory

> We construct a new Skyrme interaction Sk$χ$m$^*$ by fitting the equation of state and nucleon effective masses in asymmetric nuclear matter from chiral two- and three-body forces as well as the binding energies of finite nuclei. Employing this interaction to study the electric dipole polarizabilities of $^{48}$Ca, $^{68}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb in the random-phase approximation, we find that the theoretical predictions are in good agreement with experimentally measured values without additional fine tuning of the Skyrme interaction, thus confirming the usefulness of the new Skyrme interaction in studying the properties of nuclei. We further use this interaction to study the neutron skin thicknesses of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, and they are found to be consistent with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The new Skyrme interaction Skχm* can reproduce experimental electric dipole polarizabilities of medium-to-heavy nuclei without additional fine-tuning, but only because the fit includes constraints on the isovector effective mass from chiral effective field theory, which previous Skyrme interactions (Skχ414, Skχ450, Skχ500) lacked and therefore predicted effective masses too large for giant dipole resonance studies.

## Method
The authors constructed a Skyrme interaction by fitting to the equation of state and nucleon effective masses in asymmetric nuclear matter from chiral two- and three-body forces (N3LO two-body, N2LO three-body) as well as the binding energies of seven doubly-closed-shell nuclei. They then calculated electric dipole polarizabilities of ⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁸Ni, ¹²⁰Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb using the random-phase approximation (RPA) with the new interaction.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1651 ± 0.0025 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy at saturation E_sym(ρ₀) = 30.94 ± 0.46 MeV
- Density slope of symmetry energy L = 45.6 ± 2.5 MeV
- Isovector effective mass m*_{v,0}/m = 0.694 ± 0.020
- Electric dipole polarizability of ²⁰⁸Pb: 19.87 ± 0.29 fm³ (experiment: 19.6 ± 0.6 fm³)
- Electric dipole polarizability of ⁴⁸Ca: 2.27 ± 0.05 fm³ (experiment: 2.07 ± 0.22 fm³)
- Electric dipole polarizability of ⁶⁸Ni: 4.06 ± 0.07 fm³ (experiment: 3.88 ± 0.31 fm³)
- Electric dipole polarizability of ¹²⁰Sn: 9.28 ± 0.14 fm³ (experiment: 8.59 ± 0.36 fm³)
- Neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.170 ± 0.005 fm
- Neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca: 0.167 ± 0.004 fm

## Context
This work extends the approach of Lim (2017), who constructed Skyrme interactions (Skχ414, Skχ450, Skχ500) from chiral EOS but found them unsuitable for giant dipole resonance studies due to overly large effective masses. The new interaction improves upon those by including constraints on isoscalar and isovector effective masses from Holt (2013, 2016) and by fitting the central values of the chiral EOS rather than individual cutoffs. The results enable consistent mean-field studies of isovector properties in heavy nuclei where ab initio calculations with chiral forces are not yet feasible, and the predicted neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb is consistent with constraints from Tarbert (2014) and the PREX experiment (Abrahamyan, 2012).
