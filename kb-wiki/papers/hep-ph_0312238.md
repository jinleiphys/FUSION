# Formulation of Vector Manifestation in Hot and/or Dense Matter

> The vector manifestation (VM) was proposed as a novel manifestation of chiral symmetry in which the massless vector meson becomes the chiral partner of pion. In this write-up, I briefly summarize the following main ingredients to formulate the VM in hot and/or dense matter: Effective field theory (EFT) based on the hidden local symmetry; Wilsonian matching between the EFT and QCD; Intrinsic thermal and/or dense effects.

## Key claim

The vector manifestation (VM) proposes that at the chiral phase transition point in hot and/or dense matter, the massless vector meson (rho) becomes the chiral partner of the pion, rather than the scalar meson as in the conventional Ginzburg-Landau or Gell-Mann-Levy picture; this requires that the intrinsic temperature and/or density dependence of the bare parameters of the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian, derived through Wilsonian matching to QCD, drives the gauge coupling to zero and the parameter a to 1 at the critical point.

## Method

The author formulates the VM using an effective field theory based on hidden local symmetry (HLS), which includes the rho meson as a gauge boson and the pion as a Nambu-Goldstone boson. The bare parameters of the HLS Lagrangian are determined at a matching scale Lambda via Wilsonian matching between the HLS current correlators and the operator product expansion in QCD, incorporating temperature and/or density dependence through the quark and gluon condensates. Renormalization group equations (RGEs) for the leading-order parameters F_pi, a, and g are then used to evolve the bare parameters to on-shell values, with the fixed point (g,a) = (0,1) playing a central role. For dense matter, a quasiquark field is added to the HLS Lagrangian, and the coupled RGEs for g, a, and the quasiquark mass m_q are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The VM fixed point is characterized by (g, a) = (0, 1) for hot matter, and (g, a, m_q) = (0, 1, 0) for dense matter.
- The rho pole mass in hot matter near T_c is given by m_rho^2(T) = M_rho^2 + g^2 N_f (15 - a^2)/144 T^2, where the second term vanishes as g -> 0 and a -> 1 at T_c.
- The rho pole mass in dense matter near mu_c is given by m_rho^2(mu) = M_rho^2(mu) + g^2 (1 - kappa)^2/(6 pi^2) mu^2, which vanishes as M_rho -> 0 and g -> 0 at mu_c.
- The Wilsonian matching condition for the bare pion decay constant is F_pi^2(Lambda; T, mu)/Lambda^2 = 1/(8 pi^2)[1 + alpha_s/pi + (2 pi^2/3) <(alpha_s/pi) G_{munu}G^{munu}>_{T,mu}/Lambda^4 + pi^3 (1408/27) alpha_s <bar{q} q>^2_{T,mu}/Lambda^6].

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation originally proposed by Harada and Yamawaki (2001) for vacuum QCD to finite temperature and density, building on the hidden local symmetry framework of Bando, Kugo, Uehara, Yamawaki, and Yanagida (1985). The VM contrasts with the conventional Ginzburg-Landau or Gell-Mann-Levy picture where the scalar meson becomes the chiral partner of the pion, and with the Brown-Rho scaling scenario (1991) that predicts dropping rho meson masses. The formulation enables predictions for hot matter such as equal vector and axial-vector susceptibilities, violation of vector dominance in the pion electromagnetic form factor, and a pion velocity near the speed of light at the critical temperature, the latter differing from the standard chiral theory result of Son and Stephanov (2002) that predicts the pion velocity goes to zero.
