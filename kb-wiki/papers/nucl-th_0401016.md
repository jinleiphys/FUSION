# Neutron Matter: A Superfluid Gas

> We review recent progress in the theory of neutron matter with particular emphasis on its superfluid properties. Results of quantum Monte Carlo calculations of simple and realistic models of uniform superfluid neutron gas are discussed along with those of neutrons interacting in a potential well chosen to approximate neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. The properties of dilute superfluid Fermi gases that may be produced in atom traps, and their relations with neutron matter, are illustrated. The density dependence of the effective interaction between neutrons, used to describe neutron-rich systems with the mean field approximations, is also discussed.

# Neutron Matter: A Superfluid Gas

## Key claim
Neutron matter at low densities behaves as an unusual superfluid gas with positive pressure at all densities, and its energy per particle is close to approximately 0.44 times the noninteracting Fermi gas energy over a wide density range, provided the dimensionless parameter |ak_F| is much greater than 1. However, the pairing gaps at densities above 0.1 times nuclear saturation density are sensitive to the details of the neutron-neutron interaction, including repulsive cores and tensor components, and cannot be predicted solely from the scattering length and effective range.

## Method
The authors performed quantum Monte Carlo calculations, including Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC), for uniform superfluid neutron gas in periodic boxes using realistic bare two- and three-neutron interactions (Argonne v18 and Illinois-2). For neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, they modeled neutrons in a Woods-Saxon external potential well designed to approximate the effect of proton interactions, and calculated bound-state energies for up to 14 neutrons. The pairing gap was extracted from the energy difference between odd and even particle numbers.

## Key numbers
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is a = -18 fm, and the effective range is r_nn ~ 2.8 fm.
- Nuclear saturation density is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The energy of dilute superfluid Fermi gas with ak_F approaching infinity is approximately 0.44 E_FG, where E_FG = (3/10)(hbar^2/m)k_F^2 is the noninteracting Fermi gas energy per particle.
- At density rho_0/4, the pairing gap from GFMC calculations is approximately 2.8(5) MeV, in agreement with the BCS result of 2.6(1) MeV using realistic bare interactions.
- The BCS equation predicts a maximum gap of 3 MeV at 0.1 rho_0 and disappearance of ^1S_0 pairing at approximately 0.7 rho_0.
- For neutron drops in the external well, the 0s state is at approximately -12.1 MeV and the 0p state at approximately -4.8 MeV.
- The energy of the seventh pair in the periodic box at rho_0/4, E(14)-E(12), is approximately 3.2 MeV, while the eighth pair, E(16)-E(14), is approximately 26.8 MeV.
- Most realistic calculations give E(rho) ~ 0.5 E_FG(rho) in the density range 0.01 to 0.1 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work reviews and extends quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter, building on decades of study in the context of neutron stars, with earlier reviews cited as references. The results for dilute Fermi gases connect to experiments on cold atoms in traps that can tune the scattering length via Feshbach resonances, as discussed by the atom trap community. The density-dependent effective interactions discussed here relate to Skyrme-type mean-field models used in nuclear structure, such as those by Friedman and Pandharipande (1981) and the SLy4 interaction, and the pairing treatment connects to the work of Leggett (1980) and Randeria (1995) on the BCS-to-BEC crossover. The neutron drop calculations provide a new approach to modeling neutron-rich oxygen isotopes that complements traditional shell-model treatments.
