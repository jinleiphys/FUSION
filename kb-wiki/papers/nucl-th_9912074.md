# The new mechanism for intermediate- and short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Arguments against the traditional Yukawa-type approach to $NN$ intermediate- and short-range interaction due to scalar-isoscalar meson exchange are presented. Instead of the Yukawa mechanism for intermediate-range attraction some new approach based on formation of the symmetric six-quark bag in the state $|(0s)^6[6]_X,L=0>$ dressed due to strong coupling to $π$, $σ$ and $ρ$ fields are suggested. These new mechanism offers a strong intermediate-range attraction which replaces the effective $σ$-exchange (or excitation of two isobars in the intermediate state) in traditional force models. A similar mechanism with vector $ρ$-meson production in the intermediate six-quark state is expected to lead to a strong short-range spin-orbital nonlocal interaction in the $NN$ system, which may resolve the long-standing puzzle of the spin-orbit force in baryons and in two-baryon systems. Illustrative examples are developed which demonstrate clearly how well the suggested new model can reproduce $NN$ data. Strong interrelations have been shown to exist between the proposed microscopic model and the one-component Moscow $NN$ potential developed by the authors previously and also with some hybrid models and the one-term separable Tabakin potential. The new implications of the proposed model for nuclear physics are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the traditional Yukawa meson-exchange mechanism for intermediate- and short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction should be replaced by a mechanism based on formation of a symmetric six-quark bag in the state $|(0s)^6[6]_X,L=0\rangle$ dressed by strong coupling to $\pi$, $\sigma$ and $\rho$ fields, where the scalar-isoscalar "$\sigma$" exists only as a quasiparticle excitation inside the six-quark bag rather than as a free particle in vacuum.

## Method

The authors develop a group-theoretical analysis of six-quark system symmetries, distinguishing between fully symmetric $|(0s)^6[6]_X\rangle$ configurations and mixed-symmetry $|(0s)^4(1p)^2[42]_X\rangle$ states, and propose that the fully symmetric six-quark state, due to enhanced quark density, undergoes partial chiral symmetry restoration leading to strong additional attraction. They replace the traditional $t$-channel meson-exchange diagrams with $s$-channel dressed bag diagrams where intermediate $\pi$, $\sigma$ and $\rho$ mesons are treated explicitly, and combine this six-quark mechanism with conventional Yukawa $\pi$-, $2\pi$- and $\rho$-exchanges for separations beyond the bag region and for high partial waves ($L>3$).

## Key numbers

- The range of $\pi$-exchange force $\lambda_{\pi}\simeq 1.45$ fm is much larger than the nucleon quark radius $<r_N> \simeq 0.6$ fm
- Heavy-meson exchange with masses $m\ge 600$ MeV occurs at distances $r_m \simeq 0.2\div 0.8$ fm where nucleons strongly overlap
- The cut-off parameter $\Lambda_{\pi NN}^{\rm OBE}$ in OBE models lies in the interval $1.3 \div 2.0$ GeV, while direct estimates give $\Lambda_{\pi NN}^{\pi N + \rm theor} \simeq 0.4 \div 0.8$ GeV
- A small value $\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 0.528$ GeV was found by Cohen (1986) in the Skyrme model, and $\Lambda_{\pi NN} =0.63$ GeV (with $\Lambda_{\rho NN} =0.7$ GeV) was extracted from $NN \to N\Delta$ experiments
- The $\sigma$-meson mass estimates are $m_{\sigma}=400 \div 1200$ MeV with width $\Gamma_{\sigma} = 300 \div 500$ MeV
- The $\Delta\Delta$ channel threshold is approximately 600 MeV in the center-of-mass system
- The dressed bag model describes lower $NN$ phase shifts in the energy range $0 \div 600$ MeV
- The intermediate-range attraction operates at distances $r_{NN} \sim 0.7 \div 1.2$ fm where the dressed six-quark bag is localized

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed Moscow $NN$ potential and the hybrid quark compound bag (QCB) model, and shows interrelations with the one-term separable Tabakin potential. It directly challenges the traditional one-boson-exchange (OBE) models of Machleidt (1987, 1989) and the Nijmegen group, arguing that recent chiral perturbation theory calculations by Kaiser et al. (1997, 1998) and Oset et al. (1999) demonstrate that correlated $\pi\pi$ $s$-wave exchange gives repulsion rather than attraction, contradicting the conventional $\sigma$-exchange picture. The proposed mechanism enables a resolution of long-standing puzzles in few-nucleon systems, including the $A_y$ analyzing power puzzle in $nd$ and $pd$ scattering, the Sagara puzzle in backward $Nd$ elastic scattering, and discrepancies in ${}^3{\rm He}(e,e'p)$ reactions, by providing a new type of spin-dependent three-nucleon force.
