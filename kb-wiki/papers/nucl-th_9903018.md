# Hadron production in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV

> A Fermi statistical model analysis of hadron abundances and spectra obtained in several relativistic heavy ion collision experiments is utilized to characterize a particle source. Properties consistent with a disintegrating, hadron evaporating, deconfined quark-gluon plasma phase fireball are obtained, with a baryochemical potential μ_B=200-210MeV, and a temperature T_f\simeq 140-150MeV, significantly below previous expectations.

# Hadron production in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV

## Key claim

The hadron abundances and transverse mass spectra observed in 158A GeV Pb-Pb collisions at CERN-SPS are consistent with a disintegrating, hadron evaporating deconfined quark-gluon plasma fireball, but only when chemical non-equilibrium for strange and light quark flavors and collective flow are included in the analysis, yielding a chemical freeze-out temperature of T_f = 144 ± 2 MeV, significantly below the 180 MeV found in earlier equilibrium analyses such as Becattini (1998).

## Method

The authors performed a Fermi statistical model analysis of 15 hadron yield ratios from the NA49 and WA97 experiments, using six parameters: chemical freeze-out temperature T_f, light quark fugacity λ_q (equivalent to baryochemical potential μ_B = 3 T_f ln λ_q), strange quark fugacity λ_s, strange quark phase space occupancy γ_s, light quark phase space occupancy γ_q, and collective expansion velocity v_c. They fitted the model simultaneously to particle abundance ratios and to the mean inverse slope of strange baryon transverse mass spectra, using a χ² minimization procedure, and separately analyzed data sets including or excluding the four Ω and anti-Ω particle ratios.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_f = 144 ± 2 MeV for Pb-Pb collisions (and 144 ± 2 MeV for S-induced reactions)
- Baryochemical potential: μ_B = 203 ± 5 MeV for Pb-Pb, compared to μ_B = 178 ± 5 MeV for S-induced reactions
- Collective expansion velocity: v_c = 0.58 ± 0.04 c for Pb-Pb, compared to v_c = 0.49 ± 0.02 c for S-induced reactions
- Light quark fugacity: λ_q = 1.60 ± 0.02 for Pb-Pb
- Strange quark fugacity: λ_s = 1.10 ± 0.02 for Pb-Pb, consistent with the Coulomb-deformed strangeness conservation condition λ_s λ_Q^{1/3} = 1
- Light quark phase space occupancy: γ_q = 1.7 ± 0.5 for Pb-Pb, near the pion condensation limit γ_q^c = exp(m_π/2T_f)
- Entropy per baryon: S_f/B = 38 ± 3 for Pb-Pb
- Strangeness per baryon: s_f/B = 0.78 ± 0.04 for Pb-Pb
- Energy per baryon in emitted hadrons: E_f/B = 7.0 ± 0.5 GeV for Pb-Pb, close to the collision available energy of 8.6 GeV
- Energy density at freeze-out: ε = O(0.5) GeV/fm³
- Mean strange baryon inverse slope used as constraint: T̄_⊥^{Pb} = 260 ± 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chemical non-equilibrium statistical model analysis previously applied to 200A GeV S-induced reactions by Letessier and Rafelski (1999), and contradicts the higher freeze-out temperature of 180 MeV found in the equilibrium analysis of Becattini, Gazdzicki and Sollfrank (1998). The results enable a consistent interpretation of both the wide-acceptance NA49 data and the central-rapidity WA97 multi-strange baryon data, showing that the only consistent interpretation is direct hadronization from an exploding deconfined quark-gluon plasma without subsequent chemical re-equilibration. The analysis also finds that the Ω and anti-Ω yields cannot be described by the statistical model and require an additional production mechanism beyond the Fermi model.
