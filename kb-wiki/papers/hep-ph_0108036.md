# Color-flavor locked strangelets

> Finite lumps of color-flavor locked strange quark matter (CFL-strangelets) are significantly more stable than strangelets without color-flavor locking for wide ranges of parameters, increasing the likelihood of strangelet metastability, or even absolute stability beyond some minimum baryon number $A_{min}$. Whereas bulk CFL strange quark matter is electrically neutral, CFL-strangelets are positively charged, with $Z\approx 0.3 A^{2/3}$. This is quite different from ``ordinary'' strangelets and may provide a possible test of color-flavor locking if strangelets are detected in upcoming cosmic-ray space experiments.

# Color-flavor locked strangelets

## Key claim

Finite lumps of color-flavor locked strange quark matter (CFL-strangelets) are significantly more stable than strangelets without color-flavor locking for wide ranges of parameters, increasing the likelihood of strangelet metastability or even absolute stability beyond some minimum baryon number A_min, but this conclusion depends on the specific values of the bag constant B, the strange quark mass m_s, and the pairing gap Δ, with the allowed parameter range for stability being wider than for ordinary strange quark matter.

## Method

The author calculates the energy and charge of finite-size CFL-strangelets using the MIT bag model with α_S=0, incorporating surface and curvature corrections to the density of states for massless up and down quarks and massive strange quarks. The color-flavor locked phase is modeled by enforcing equal Fermi momenta for all three quark flavors to maximize pairing energy, with the pairing contribution to the free energy approximated as Ω_pair ≈ -3Δ²μ²/π². The equilibrium radius and charge are found by minimizing the total energy with respect to radius at fixed baryon number, assuming weak equilibrium with zero electron chemical potential for systems with A ≪ 10⁷.

## Key numbers

- CFL-strangelets may be absolutely stable (E/A < 930 MeV) for bag constants B^{1/4} as high as 180 MeV for low strange quark mass, 170 MeV for m_s ≈ 150 MeV, and 160 MeV for m_s ≈ 300 MeV, whereas stability without color-flavor locking requires B^{1/4} < 163 MeV even for massless s-quarks.
- The charge of CFL-strangelets follows Z ≈ 0.3 A^{2/3}, in contrast to ordinary strangelets where Z ≈ 0.1 A for small A and Z ≈ 8 A^{1/3} for systems larger than the Debye screening length (≈ 5 fm; A ≈ 150).
- The pairing gap Δ is taken as approximately 10–100 MeV, with the main calculations using Δ = 100 MeV.
- The lower bound on B^{1/4} to avoid spontaneous nuclear decay into two-flavor color superconducting quark matter increases from 146 MeV (for normal quark matter) to 156 MeV for CFL quark matter with Δ = 100 MeV.
- Up to 100 MeV per baryon is gained in binding energy for some parameter choices due to color-flavor locking.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color-flavor locked quark matter, previously studied for bulk systems by Rajagopal and Wilczek (2001) and Alford, Rajagopal, Reddy, and Wilczek (2001), to finite-size lumps (strangelets). It builds on earlier calculations of ordinary strangelet properties by Farhi and Jaffe (1984), Berger and Jaffe (1987), and Madsen (2000), showing that CFL-strangelets have a qualitatively different charge-mass relation (Z ∝ A^{2/3} rather than Z ∝ A for small systems). The distinctive charge signature could allow experimental tests of color-flavor locking using upcoming cosmic-ray space experiments such as the Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer (AMS) and ECCO, and calls for re-analysis of limits from previous strangelet searches.
