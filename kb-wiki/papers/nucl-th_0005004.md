# Azimuthal asymmetry in transverse energy flow in nuclear collisions at high energies

> The azimuthal pattern of transverse energy flow in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies is considered. We show that the probability distribution of the event-by-event azimuthal disbalance in transverse energy flow is essentially sensitive to the presence of the semihard minijet component.

# Azimuthal asymmetry in transverse energy flow in nuclear collisions at high energies

## Key claim
The probability distribution of event-by-event azimuthal disbalance in transverse energy flow is sensitive to the presence of semihard minijet components, but the paper qualifies that this conclusion holds specifically for the HIJING event generator framework with quenching turned on and a minijet infrared cutoff of 2 GeV; the asymmetry signal is robust to changes in the cutoff but the quantitative width values depend on whether quenching is on or off.

## Method
The authors used the HIJING Monte-Carlo event generator to simulate central AuAu collisions at RHIC energy of 200 GeV and central PbPb collisions at LHC energy of 5.5 TeV. They computed the probability distribution of the difference in transverse energy deposited into two oppositely azimuthally oriented cones with angular opening π, both at the partonic level and at the level of final hadrons, with semihard interactions and jet quenching turned on and off. The analysis extends beyond earlier leading-twist collinearly factorized QCD calculations (Leonidov and Ostrovsky 2000) by including multiple parton collisions, initial and final state radiation, hadronization, and jet quenching.

## Key numbers
- For AuAu at √s = 200 GeV with p0 = 2 GeV and quenching on: √⟨δE²⟩ = 16 GeV for hadrons, 18 GeV for partons, 7 GeV for soft hadrons only.
- For PbPb at √s = 5.5 TeV with p0 = 2 GeV and quenching on: √⟨δE²⟩ = 61 GeV for hadrons, 65 GeV for partons, 15 GeV for soft hadrons only.
- For PbPb at √s = 5.5 TeV with p0 = 2 GeV and quenching off: √⟨δE²⟩ = 71 GeV for hadrons.
- For PbPb at √s = 5.5 TeV with p0 = 4 GeV and quenching on: √⟨δE²⟩ = 69 GeV for hadrons, 76 GeV for partons, 16 GeV for soft hadrons.
- Switching off minijets narrows the asymmetry distribution by a factor of 2.3 at RHIC and by a factor of 4.1 at LHC energy (with quenching on).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Leonidov and Ostrovsky (2000), which used leading-twist collinearly factorized QCD for binary parton-parton collisions, by incorporating more realistic mechanisms from the HIJING event generator such as multiple parton collisions, radiation, hadronization, and jet quenching. The study is related to earlier work on p⊥ dependence of two-particle correlation functions due to minijets (XNW2) and to the broader program of using semihard degrees of freedom to explain features of high-energy hadron collisions (HIJING, XNW1). The results enable experimental calorimetric measurements at RHIC and LHC to discriminate between different dynamical models of the initial stage of nuclear collisions, such as minijet physics versus the dual parton model.
