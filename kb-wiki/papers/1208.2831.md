# Extracting generalized neutron parton distributions from 3He data

> An impulse approximation analysis is described of the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) H and E of the 3He nucleus, quantities which are accessible in hard exclusive processes, such as coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS). The calculation is based on the Av18 interaction. The electromagnetic form factors are correctly recovered in the proper limits. The sum of the GPDs H and E of 3He, at low momentum transfer, is largely dominated by the neutron contribution, thanks to the unique spin structure of 3He. This nucleus is therefore very promising for the extraction of the neutron information. By increasing the momentum transfer, however, this conclusion is somehow hindered by the the fast growing proton contribution. Besides, even when the neutron contribution to the GPDs of 3He is largely dominating, the procedure of extracting the neutron GPDs from it could be, in principle, nontrivial. A technique is therefore proposed, independent on both the nuclear potential and the nucleon model used in the calculation, able to take into account the nuclear effects included in the IA analysis and to safely extract the neutron information at values of the momentum transfer large enough to allow the measurements. Thanks to this observation, coherent DVCS should be considered a key experiment to access the neutron GPDs and, in turn, the orbital angular momentum of the partons in the neutron.

## Key claim

The sum of the generalized parton distributions H and E of ³He is dominated by the neutron contribution at low momentum transfer, making ³He a promising target for extracting neutron GPDs, but this dominance diminishes as momentum transfer increases, and the extraction procedure is nontrivial even when the neutron contribution dominates.

## Method

The authors performed an impulse approximation (IA) analysis of the GPDs H and E for the ³He nucleus, using the Av18 nuclear interaction to evaluate spin-dependent non-diagonal spectral functions. They computed the quantity G̃_M^(3,q) = H_q^3 + E_q^3 via convolution-like equations relating nuclear GPDs to nucleon GPDs, using three different nucleon GPD models (a double distribution model, a constituent quark model, and a MIT bag model) to test model independence. The calculation was performed in the nuclear Breit-frame for low momentum transfer values where coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) measurements may be feasible.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic form factor of ³He, G_M^3(Δ²), obtained from the integral of G̃_M^3, agrees with experimental data for -Δ² ≲ 0.15 GeV²
- At -Δ² = 0.1 GeV², the ratio of proton to neutron contribution to G̃_M^3 is slowly varying in x₃ and nearly identical across three different nucleon GPD models
- The neutron point-like form factor contribution to ³He at Δ² = 0 is 0.879 (Av18) and 0.874 (Av14), while the proton contribution is -0.044 (Av18) and -0.049 (Av14)
- At -Δ² = 0.1 GeV², the neutron point-like form factor contribution is 0.305 (Av18) and 0.297 (Av14), while the proton contribution is 0.040 (Av18) and 0.038 (Av14)
- The magnetic moment of ³He is μ₃ ≈ -2.13 μ_N, close to the neutron value μ_n ≈ -1.91 μ_N

## Context

This work extends the IA calculation of ³He GPDs from Rinaldi and Scopetta (2012) and earlier work by the same authors (2011), where only H_q was evaluated, to include the helicity-flip GPD E_q. The approach builds on the formalism for nuclear GPDs developed for the deuteron by Cano and Pire (2001) and for spin-0 nuclei by Guzey and Teckentrup (2006). The unique spin structure of ³He, where the system is in a configuration with the two protons paired with opposite spins approximately 90% of the time (Friar, 1993), makes it an effective polarized neutron target, similar to its use in extracting neutron spin structure functions (Ciofi degli Atti and Scopetta, 1997; Scopetta, 1999). The proposed extraction technique, independent of the nuclear potential and nucleon model, enables coherent DVCS off ³He to be considered a key experiment for accessing neutron GPDs and, through Ji's sum rule, the orbital angular momentum of partons in the neutron.
