# Improved Standard-Model predictions for $╬Ę^{(\prime)}\to \ell^+ \ell^-$

> The rare decays $╬Ę^{(\prime)}\to\ell^+\ell^-$, $\ell\in\{e,╬╝\}$, are highly suppressed in the Standard Model, both by their chirality structure and the required loop attaching the lepton line to the $╬Ę^{(\prime)}\to╬│^*╬│^*$ matrix element. The latter is described by a single scalar function, the transition form factor, which has recently been studied in great detail for $╬Ę^{(\prime)}$ in the context of the pseudoscalar-pole contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. Based on these results, we evaluate the corresponding prediction for the $╬Ę^{(\prime)}$ dilepton decays, supplemented by an improved evaluation of the asymptotic contributions including pseudoscalar mass effects. In particular, the dispersive representation for the $╬Ę^{(\prime)}$ transition form factors allows us, for the first time, to perform a robust evaluation of the imaginary parts due to subleading channels besides the dominant two-photon cut. Our final results are $\text{Br}[╬Ę\to e^+e^-]=5.37(4)(2)[4]\times 10^{-9}$, $\text{Br}[╬Ę\to ╬╝^+╬╝^-]=4.54(4)(2)[4]\times 10^{-6}$, $\text{Br}[╬Ę'\to e^+e^-]=1.80(2)(3)[3]\times 10^{-10}$, and $\text{Br}[╬Ę'\to ╬╝^+╬╝^-]=1.22(2)(2)[3]\times 10^{-7}$, where the errors refer to the uncertainty in the normalized branching fraction, the one propagated from $\text{Br}[╬Ę^{(\prime)}\to╬│╬│]$, and the total uncertainty, respectively. The branching fraction for $╬Ę\to╬╝^+╬╝^-$ exhibits a mild $1.6Žā$ tension with experiment, and we explore the bounds that can be derived on physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The authors present improved Standard-Model predictions for the branching fractions of ╬Ę and ╬ĘŌĆ▓ decays into electron and muon pairs, using a dispersive representation of the transition form factors that, for the first time, allows a robust evaluation of imaginary parts from subleading intermediate states beyond the dominant two-photon cut. The branching fraction for ╬Ę ŌåÆ ╬╝Ōü║╬╝Ōü╗ shows a mild 1.6Žā tension with experiment, which the authors use to derive bounds on physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors decompose the ╬Ę(ŌĆ▓) ŌåÆ ╬│*╬│* transition form factor into isovector, isoscalar, effective-pole, and asymptotic contributions. The isovector part is reconstructed dispersively via a double-spectral representation based on two-pion intermediate states, using the ╬Ę(ŌĆ▓) ŌåÆ ŽĆŌü║ŽĆŌü╗╬│ decay amplitudes fitted to KLOE and BESIII data. The isoscalar part is described by a vector-meson-dominance ansatz for Žē and Žå resonances, and the asymptotic contribution is implemented with a dispersion relation that includes pseudoscalar mass corrections. The reduced amplitude for the dilepton decay is then evaluated by integrating the double-spectral density with a kernel function expressed in terms of standard loop functions, and the Z-boson exchange contribution is added as a small correction.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach previously applied to ŽĆŌü░ ŌåÆ eŌü║eŌü╗ by Hoferichter (2021) to the ╬Ę and ╬ĘŌĆ▓ mesons, using transition form factors developed in the context of pseudoscalar-pole contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering for the muon gŌłÆ2 by Holz (2024). The results improve upon earlier calculations using chiral perturbation theory (Savage 1992, GomezDumm 1998), vector-meson-dominance approaches (Ametller 1993, Knecht 1999), and Canterbury approximants (Masjuan 2015), and enable more stringent tests of physics beyond the Standard Model through comparison with current and future experimental measurements from KLOE, BESIII, and NA62.
