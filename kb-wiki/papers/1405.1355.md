# Bulk viscous evolution within anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We derive a system of moment-based dynamical equations that describe the 1+1d space-time evolution of a cylindrically symmetric massive gas undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. Extending previous work, we introduce an explicit degree of freedom associated with the bulk pressure of the system. The resulting form generalizes the ellipsoidal one-particle distribution function appropriate for massless particles to massive particles. Using this generalized form, we obtain a system of partial differential equations that can be solved numerically. In order to assess the performance of this scheme, we compare the resulting anisotropic hydrodynamics solutions with the exact solution of the 0+1d Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. We find that the inclusion of the bulk degree of freedom improves agreement between anisotropic hydrodynamics and the exact solution for a massive gas.

## Key claim

The paper extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) framework to include an explicit bulk pressure degree of freedom for massive particles, showing that this addition improves agreement with the exact solution of the 0+1d Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, though the improvement is primarily at later times rather than early times.

## Method

The authors generalize the ellipsoidal one-particle distribution function by introducing a bulk degree of freedom Φ into the anisotropy tensor Ξ^{μν}, alongside the traceless shear tensor ξ^{μν}. They derive dynamical equations from the zeroth, first, and second moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric massive gas, then specialize to a transversally homogeneous 0+1d system to compare numerical solutions with exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

- For a massive gas with m/T = 1, the inclusion of the bulk degree of freedom improves agreement between aHydro and the exact solution compared to the ellipsoidal formalism without the bulk degree of freedom.
- The system is tested at early times τ ≲ 2 fm/c where pressure anisotropies are largest, with transverse pressure exceeding longitudinal pressure.
- The relaxation time τ_eq is assumed constant in the Boltzmann equation treatment.
- The expansion scalar θ = 1/τ for the 0+1d transversally homogeneous case.

## Context

This work extends the ellipsoidal aHydro formalism of Tinti and Florkowski (2013) from massless to massive particles, and addresses the failure of both spheroidal and ellipsoidal aHydro to reproduce early-time bulk pressure dynamics reported by Florkowski, Tinti, and two of the current authors (2014). The new formalism enables more accurate modeling of bulk viscous effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for systems where finite particle mass breaks conformal symmetry and generates bulk pressure.
