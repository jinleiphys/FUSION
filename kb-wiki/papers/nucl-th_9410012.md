# Theory of fermion to boson mappings: old wine in new bottles

> After a brief review of various mappings of fermion pairs to bosons, we rigorously derive a general approach. Following the methods of Marumori and Otsuka, Arima, and Iachello, our approach begins with mapping states and constructs boson representations that preserve fermion matrix elements. In several cases these representations factor into finite, Hermitian boson images times a projection or norm operator that embodies the Pauli principle. We pay particular attention to truncated boson spaces, and describe general methods for constructing Hermitian and approximately finite boson image Hamiltonians, including effective operator theory to account for excluded states. This method is akin to that of Otsuka, Arima, and Iachello introduced in connection with the Interacting Boson Model, but is more rigorous, general, and systematic.

## Key claim

The authors rigorously derive a general approach for mapping fermion pairs to bosons that preserves fermion matrix elements, claiming their method is "more rigorous, general, and systematic" than the Otsuka-Arima-Iachello (OAI) mapping used for the Interacting Boson Model, though they acknowledge the approach follows the same state-mapping philosophy as Marumori and OAI.

## Method

The authors begin by constructing fermion-pair states and calculating matrix elements (overlaps, one-body, and two-body operators) using a generalized Wick's theorem derived from fermion coherent states and Grassmann integration. They then map these fermion states directly onto boson states and construct boson representations of operators that exactly reproduce the fermion matrix elements, including a norm operator that enforces the Pauli principle. For the full boson Fock space, the representations factor into a finite, Hermitian boson image times the norm operator, and the authors show how to extend this approach to truncated boson spaces using effective operator theory.

## Key numbers

* The norm of a state with 5 J=0 pairs in a single j-shell with Ω = j+1/2 is 5! (1 - 10/Ω + 35/Ω² - 50/Ω³ + 24/Ω⁴), which agrees with the general result from the commutation relation [A₀, A₀†] = 1 - N̂/Ω.
* For a single j-shell with only J=0 pairs, the k-contraction c_k = tr(A₀A₀†)ᵏ = (2Ω)⁻ᵏ⁺¹, and the coefficients w⁰₂ = -1/(2Ω), w⁰₃ = 1/(3Ω²), w⁰₄ = 1/(8Ω²) - 1/(4Ω³), w⁰₅ = -1/(6Ω³) + 1/(5Ω⁴).
* For an axial rotor limit, the average number of d-bosons in the ground state band is 2/3 the total number of bosons.

## Context

This work extends the state-mapping methods of Marumori (1960) and Otsuka, Arima, and Iachello (1978) for the Interacting Boson Model, providing a more systematic framework that can handle arbitrary truncations and many-body contributions. The authors contrast their approach with the Belyaev-Zelevinskii (1962) operator-mapping method and the Dyson mapping, noting that in the full space all mappings yield equivalent results. The paper enables systematic calculation of higher-order boson interactions beyond the standard OAI prescription, which is needed for deformed nuclei where the OAI mapping of only n_d = 0,1,2 states is insufficient.
