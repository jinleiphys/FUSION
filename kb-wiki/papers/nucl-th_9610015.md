# Baryon-to-entropy ratio in very high energy nuclear collisions

> We compute as a function of rapidity $y$ the baryon number carried by quarks and antiquarks with $p_T > p_0 \approx$ 2 GeV produced in Pb+Pb collisions at TeV energies. The computation is carried out in lowest order QCD perturbation theory using structure functions compatible with HERA results. At $p_0=2$ GeV the initial gluon density is both transversally saturated and thermalised in the sense that the energy/gluon equals to that of an ideal gas with the same energy density. Even at these high energies the initial net baryon number density at $y=0$ at $τ=0.1$ fm will be more than the normal nuclear matter density but the baryon-to-entropy ratio is only $(B-\bar B)/S\sim 1/5000$. Further evolution of the system is discussed and the final baryon-to-entropy ratio is estimated.

## Key claim

The initial net baryon-to-entropy ratio in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies is only about 1/5000 at τ=0.1 fm, but this value may increase by up to a factor of 4 due to non-perturbative soft components and dissipation during the subsequent evolution of the system.

## Method

The authors computed the rapidity distributions of gluons, quarks, and antiquarks with pT > p0 = 2 GeV produced in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 5500 GeV using lowest-order QCD perturbation theory with GRV 94 LO parton distribution functions compatible with HERA results. The pp cross sections were transformed to Pb+Pb yields using the nuclear overlap function TAA(0) = 32/mb, neglecting nuclear shadowing, and the initial parton densities were evaluated at formation time τ = 1/p0 = 0.1 fm. The further evolution of the system was discussed using hydrodynamical expansion with possible shear viscosity and a decomposition into hard and soft components.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon number in central Pb+Pb at |y| < 0.5: 4350 gluons, with transverse energy 12960 GeV
- Initial quark and antiquark numbers: 200 quarks and 190 antiquarks, with transverse energies 620 GeV and 590 GeV respectively
- Initial number densities: n_g = 325/fm^3, n_q = 14.9/fm^3, n_antiquark = 14.2/fm^3
- Initial energy densities: ε_g = 967 GeV/fm^3, ε_q = 46.3 GeV/fm^3, ε_antiquark = 43.9 GeV/fm^3
- Initial net baryon number density: n_(B-Bbar) = 0.21/fm^3 at τ = 0.1 fm
- Initial baryon-to-entropy ratio: (B-Bbar)/S ≈ 1.8 × 10^(-4) ≈ 1/5000
- Energy per gluon: 2.98 GeV, corresponding to an ideal gas temperature T_eq = 1.10 GeV
- Initial asymmetry (B-Bbar)/(B+Bbar): 2.1% to 2.6% for √s = 5500 GeV (varying p0 from 1 to 3 GeV)
- Final baryon-to-entropy ratio estimate including soft component: ~8 × 10^(-4) for LHC
- For RHIC at √s = 200 GeV: initial (B-Bbar)/S ~ 2 × 10^(-3), final estimate ~9 × 10^(-3)

## Context

This work extends perturbative QCD calculations of minijet production in heavy ion collisions, building on the transverse saturation framework of Blaizot and Mueller (1987) and earlier parton production estimates by Eskola, Kajantie, and collaborators. The result that the central rapidity region is essentially depleted of net baryon number at very high energies agrees qualitatively with the expectations of Hwa and Shuryak, and Ukawa. The paper contrasts with the much larger baryon-to-entropy ratios of 1/20 to 1/50 observed at SPS energies (√s ≈ 20 GeV) and with the gluon field model of Kharzeev which argued that baryon stopping would persist to LHC energies. The work enables estimates of final multiplicities and transverse energies for LHC and RHIC, and sets the stage for next-to-leading order corrections and more refined treatments of nuclear shadowing and thermalization.
