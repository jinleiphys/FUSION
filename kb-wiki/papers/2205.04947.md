# Transport Equation for Small Systems and Nonadditive Entropy

> The nonadditive entropy introduced by Tsallis in 1988 has been used in different fields and generalizes the Boltzmann entropy extending the possibilities of application of the statistical methods developed in the context of Mechanics. Here we investigate one of the last points of the theory that still are under discussion: the source term of the nonextensive transport equation. Based on a simple system, we show that the nonadditivity is a direct consequence of the phase space topology, and derive the source term that leads to the nonextensive transport equation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nonadditive (Tsallis) entropy and its associated transport equation arise naturally from the phase space topology of a finite system, rather than being an ad hoc generalization. The source term of the nonextensive transport equation, previously proposed in the literature without full justification, is shown to follow directly from the composition law of probability densities derived from the phase space structure of a small system.

## Method

The authors analyze the statistical mechanics of an ideal gas with a finite number \(N\) of point-like particles in a volume \(V\), calculating the number of microstates as a function of energy via integration over momentum space. They derive the probability density for energy fluctuations in a partition of the system and show it takes the form of a \(q\)-exponential distribution, where the entropic index \(q\) is related to the number of particles. Using this probability density, they derive the composition law for two subsystems and show it follows the \(q\)-algebra. This composition law is then inserted into the collision term of the Boltzmann transport equation to obtain the nonextensive source term.

## Key numbers

* The entropic index for QCD, predicted from fundamental parameters (number of colors \(n_C=3\) and number of flavors \(n_f=6\)), is \(q = 8/7\) (approximately 1.143).
* For a quark moving in a QGP medium, a phenomenological fit gave \(q \sim 1.1\) for \(n_f=3\).
* The relation between the entropic index and the number of particles in the small system is given by \((1-q_A)^{-1} = 3N_B/2 - 1\), where \(N_B\) is the number of particles in the complementary partition.
* The probability density for the energy of a partition \(A\) is \(\rho(N_A,E_A) dE_A = \frac{\Gamma(3N/2)}{\Gamma(3N_A/2)\Gamma(3N_B/2)} \frac{dE_A}{E} \left(\frac{E_A}{E}\right)^{3 N_A/2 - 1} \left[1-\frac{E_A}{E}\right]^{3 N_B/2 - 1}\).

## Context

This work extends the line of research on the foundations of Tsallis statistics, particularly addressing a criticism summarized by Kapusta regarding the transport equation. It builds on prior work by Lima and Plastino (1998) and Lavagno (2002) that proposed a nonextensive transport equation but did not derive the source term from first principles. The paper shows that the source term derived from phase space topology reduces to the form proposed in those earlier works under appropriate conditions, specifically for thermofractals where the probability density function is independent of particle number. This result enables further study of dissipative transport coefficients in kinetic theory and potential applications to black hole thermodynamics and the AdS/CFT correspondence.
