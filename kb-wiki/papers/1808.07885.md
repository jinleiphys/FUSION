# Dynamical topological transitions in the massive Schwinger model with a θ-term

> Aiming at a better understanding of anomalous and topological effects in gauge theories out-of-equilibrium, we study the real-time dynamics of a prototype model for CP-violation, the massive Schwinger model with a $θ$-term. We identify dynamical quantum phase transitions between different topological sectors that appear after sufficiently strong quenches of the $θ$-parameter. Moreover, we establish a general dynamical topological order parameter, which can be accessed through fermion two-point correlators and, importantly, which can be applied for interacting theories. Enabled by this result, we show that the topological transitions persist beyond the weak-coupling regime. Finally, these effects can be observed with table-top experiments based on existing cold-atom, superconducting-qubit, and trapped-ion technology. Our work, thus, presents a significant step towards quantum simulating topological and anomalous real-time phenomena relevant to nuclear and high-energy physics.

## Key claim

The paper identifies dynamical quantum phase transitions between different topological sectors in the massive Schwinger model after sufficiently strong quenches of the theta parameter, and establishes a general dynamical topological order parameter that remains valid in interacting theories, not just in the weak-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors study the real-time evolution of the massive Schwinger model (1+1D QED) after an abrupt quench of the topological theta angle, preparing the system in the ground state of H_theta and switching to another value theta'. At weak coupling (e/m to 0), they solve the free fermionic theory analytically. For non-vanishing coupling, they perform non-perturbative real-time lattice simulations using Exact Diagonalization (ED) with staggered fermions on lattices of 8 to 20 sites with periodic boundary conditions, computing the topological invariant from fermion two-point correlators using a formalism adapted from computing Chern numbers in momentum space.

## Key numbers

* The critical quench strength for topological transitions is |Delta theta| > pi/2, with Delta theta = (theta - theta') in (-pi, pi].
* At weak coupling, vortices form at critical times t_c^(n) = (2n-1) * pi / [2 omega(k_c)] with n in N, where omega(k) = sqrt(k^2 + m^2) and critical modes are at k_c = +/- m sqrt(-cos(Delta theta)).
* The first topological transition occurs at times t_c * m ~ 1-2, within coherence times achievable with existing quantum simulators.
* The topological transition persists up to e/m less than or approximately 1, and appears to vanish for e/m greater than or approximately 2.
* Simulations use lattice spacing a*m = 0.8 and N = 8 sites as relevant for first quantum-simulator experiments.

## Context

This work extends studies of topological effects in gauge theories out of equilibrium, building on the massive Schwinger model (Coleman 1976) as a prototype for QCD's topological vacuum structure and CP violation. The dynamical topological order parameter generalizes previous constructions of topological invariants for dynamical quantum phase transitions (DQPTs) that were limited to non-interacting theories (Budich 2016, Tian 2018, Xu 2018). The results enable near-future quantum simulation experiments using trapped ions (Martinez 2016), superconducting qubits (Klco 2018), and cold neutral atoms (Zache 2018) to probe topological real-time phenomena relevant to nuclear and high-energy physics, including the conjectured Chiral Magnetic Effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008).
