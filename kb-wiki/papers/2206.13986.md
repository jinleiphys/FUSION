# Hyperon productions from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV

> We evaluate centrality dependence of hyperon, $Λ, Ξ, Ω$ and $K$ meson yields from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV using rate equation and compare with experimental observations. An increase in the yield per participating nucleon is observed with centrality. Higher rate of multistrange productions compared to single-strange are also observed in case of central collisions. Using rate equation or momentum integrated Boltzmann equation we discuss the yield microscopically which are then normalised with thermal pions to get the yield ratio,($H_s+\bar{H_s}$)/($π^++π^-$) at various centralities. We have also evaluated the relative yield of anti hyperons per participant at various centralities and compared with observations made at RHIC. Increase in the rate of $Ξ$ yield compared to $Λ$ is realised in most central collisions. We find multi strange $Ξ,Ω$ freeze-out at a temperature very close to $T_c$ and $Λ, K$ do little later.

## Key claim

Using a rate-equation (momentum-integrated Boltzmann) approach with a Bjorken-expanding hadronic background, the authors find that multistrange hyperons ($\Xi$, $\Omega$) chemically freeze out at temperatures very close to the QCD critical temperature $T_c$ (156 MeV), while single-strange $\Lambda$ and $K$ mesons freeze out slightly later at lower temperatures, and this sequential freeze-out pattern depends on centrality.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled set of first-order rate equations for the yields of $K$, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, and $\Omega$ in a hadronic gas undergoing Bjorken expansion, using hadronic cross sections (including isospin combinations) and detailed balance for inverse reactions. The initial number densities are treated as parameters (set to 0.85 or 0.7 times the equilibrium density at the initial temperature $T_i$), and the temperature and baryochemical potential evolve via energy-momentum conservation and baryon-number conservation. The calculated yields are normalized to thermal pion densities to obtain the yield ratio $(H_s+\bar{H_s})/(\pi^++\pi^-)$ and the relative yield per participant for various centrality classes, then compared to STAR data from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- For the 0-5% centrality class ($N_{part}=352$), the freeze-out temperatures that best explain the data are: $T_{ch}(K_s^0)=148.3$ MeV, $T_{ch}(\Lambda)=152$ MeV, $T_{ch}(\Xi)=154.5$ MeV, $T_{ch}(\Omega)=155$ MeV.
- For the 60-80% centrality class ($N_{part}=21$), the freeze-out temperatures are: $T_{ch}(K_s^0)=139$ MeV, $T_{ch}(\Lambda)=139$ MeV, $T_{ch}(\Xi)=139$ MeV.
- The yield ratio $(\Lambda+\bar{\Lambda})/(\pi^++\pi^-)$ for 0-5% centrality is $0.0453 \pm 0.003$.
- The yield ratio $(\Xi^-+\bar{\Xi^+})/(\pi^++\pi^-)$ for 0-5% centrality is $0.0061 \pm 0.0005$.
- The yield ratio $(\Omega^-+\bar{\Omega^+})/(\pi^++\pi^-)$ for 0-5% centrality is $0.00081 \pm 0.00009$.
- The relative yield per participant for $\bar{\Xi^+}$ in 0-5% centrality (normalized to 60-80% centrality) is $1.7897 \pm 0.565$, compared to $1.578 \pm 0.476$ for $\bar{\Lambda}$.

## Context

This work extends the rate-equation approach previously applied by the same group to LHC energies (Ghosh et al., 2020) down to RHIC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, and directly compares with STAR measurements of hyperon yields (Adam et al., 2007) and kaon yields (Agakishiev et al., 2012). The analysis addresses the observed enhancement of multistrange relative to single-strange baryon production in central collisions, a signature originally proposed as a quark-gluon plasma signal (Rafelski, 1982). The sequential freeze-out picture contrasts with the common freeze-out assumption of the Hadron Resonance Gas model and enables future comparisons with three-dimensional expansion models and with data from LHC Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV (ALICE Collaboration, 2014).
