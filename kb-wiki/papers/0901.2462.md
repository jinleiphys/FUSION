# Tomography of the Quark Gluon Plasma by Heavy Quarks

> Using the recently published model \cite{Gossiaux:2008jv,goss2} for the collisional energy loss of heavy quarks in a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), based on perturbative QCD (pQCD), we study the centrality dependence of $R_{AA}$ and $R_{AA}(p_T^{min})$, %= \frac{dN_{AA}/dp_T}{<N_{binary}> dN_{pp}/dp_T}$ measured by the Phenix collaboration, and compare our model with other approaches based on pQCD and on Anti de Sitter/ Conformal Field Theory (AdS/CFT)

## Key claim

Using a pQCD-based model with a running nonperturbative coupling constant and an infrared regulator determined by hard thermal loop calculations, the authors reproduce the centrality dependence of $R_{AA}$ and $R_{AA}(p_T^{min})$ measured by PHENIX, but the most peripheral events show a decrease in $R_{AA}$ that the model does not reproduce and known mechanisms do not account for.

## Method

The authors model heavy quark propagation through an expanding QGP described by ideal hydrodynamics, solving the Boltzmann equation for the heavy quark distribution. The elementary interactions between heavy quarks and plasma partons (light quarks and gluons) are computed using pQCD with a running coupling constant that remains finite at low momentum transfer and an infrared regulator set to $\mu = 0.2 m_D$ (the Debye mass), with a K-factor of 1.8 applied to the cross section to account for the omission of radiative energy loss. The Cronin effect is included via a Gaussian momentum broadening with variance $\sigma^2 = 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.

## Key numbers

- A K-factor of 1.8 applied to the cross section describes central and minimum bias data for $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ from STAR and PHENIX.
- The Cronin effect broadening parameter is set to $\sigma^2 = 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
- The drag coefficient for c-quarks and b-quarks is computed at a plasma temperature of 300 MeV and compared across models; the AdS/CFT drag coefficient is momentum independent, while all pQCD-based drag coefficients decrease with increasing $p_T$.
- For large $p_T$, the deviation of $R_{AA}$ from one in the authors' model is twice as large as when using the drag coefficient of van Hees and Rapp in an otherwise unchanged model.
- The elliptic flow $v_2$ is reduced by a factor of two when replacing the authors' drag coefficient with that of van Hees and Rapp, for minimum bias events at impact parameter b=7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Gossiaux and Aichelin, 2008) for collisional energy loss of heavy quarks in a QGP, comparing with data from the PHENIX collaboration (Adare et al., 2006) and STAR collaboration (Abelev et al., 2006). The model differs from prior pQCD approaches by using a running nonperturbative coupling constant (following Dokshitzer, 1995) and an infrared regulator from hard thermal loop calculations (extending the approach of Braaten and Thoma, 1991). The authors compare their drag coefficients with those from Moore and Teaney (2004), van Hees and Rapp (2004, 2005), Peshier and Peigne (2008), and AdS/CFT calculations by Gubser (2006) and Horowitz (2008). The work enables disentangling the effects of heavy quark-plasma interactions from the plasma expansion dynamics, showing that different models for the expansion could explain discrepancies between calculations that use different drag coefficients.
