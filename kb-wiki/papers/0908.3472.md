# Coulomb Dissociation of $^{15}$C and Radiative Neutron Capture on $^{14}$C

> The semiclassical, dynamical description of diffraction dissociation of weakly bound nuclei is applied to analyze the decay-energy spectra of $^{15}$C that have been measured at 68 MeV/nucleon on a Pb target. The optical potentials that are used to describe the nuclear interaction of $^{15}$C with the target nucleus are realistic because the fits to the two measured spectra, one with a small and one with a very large acceptance angle, are consistent and of similar quality. The cross section for the radiative neutron capture on $^{14}$C to the 1/2$^+$ ground state of $^{15}$C is deduced from the analysis. When combined with an estimated contribution from the capture to the 5/2$^+$ excited state of $^{15}$C, an excellent agreement with a recent direct capture measurement is achieved.

## Key claim

The semiclassical dynamical description of diffraction dissociation can reproduce the measured decay-energy spectra of 15C at both small and large acceptance angles with consistent scaling factors, and when combined with an estimated 4% contribution from capture to the 5/2+ excited state, it resolves the 15% discrepancy between the Coulomb dissociation and direct radiative capture measurements of the neutron capture cross section on 14C.

## Method

The author applied a semiclassical dynamical model where the projectile-target relative motion follows a classical Coulomb trajectory, while the breakup of 15C into 14C plus a neutron is calculated by solving the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the valence neutron relative to the core. The two-body wave function was expanded on angular momentum eigenstates up to Lmax=4, and the time evolution was propagated using a unitary operator that includes the core-target Coulomb interaction, core-target nuclear interaction based on the 17O+Pb optical potential, and the Perey-Perey neutron-target interaction. The calculated decay-energy spectra were folded with the experimental energy resolution (Gaussian with 1-sigma width of 0.23*sqrt(Erel)) and scaled by a factor Sc to optimize the chi-squared fit to the data from Nakamura et al. (2009).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 68 MeV/nucleon for 15C on a Pb target
- Neutron separation energy of 15C ground state: 1.218 MeV
- Binding energy of the 5/2+ excited state of 15C: 0.478 MeV
- Scaling factor Sc for the dynamic calculation with Lmax=4 at theta<2.1 degrees: 0.973
- Scaling factor Sc for the dynamic calculation with Lmax=4 at theta<6.0 degrees: 0.984
- MAC cross section from direct measurement (Reifarth et al.): 7.1 +/- 0.5 microbarn at kT = 23.3 keV
- MAC cross section from first-order E1 Coulomb dissociation analysis: 6.45 +/- 0.50 microbarn (ground state only)
- MAC cross section from dynamic calculation analysis: 6.67 +/- 0.50 microbarn (ground state only)
- Estimated contribution from capture to 5/2+ state: 0.26 microbarn (about 4% of total)
- Total MAC cross section from dynamic analysis including 5/2+ contribution: 6.92 +/- 0.50 microbarn
- Experimental cross section at 23.3 keV: 4.86 +/- 0.34 microbarn
- Peak height of dipole strength distribution from first-order analysis: 0.32 e2fm2/MeV

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical dynamical description previously applied to 8B and 17F (Esbensen 1995, 1996) to the neutron halo nucleus 15C, addressing a 15% discrepancy between the first-order Coulomb dissociation analysis of Nakamura et al. (2009) and the direct capture measurement of Reifarth et al. The analysis shows that the dynamic polarization effect is much weaker for neutron halo nuclei than for proton halo nuclei, and that the corrected straight-line trajectory approximation used in the experimental analysis is well justified. The results are consistent with the CDCC calculations of Nunes (2008), and a direct comparison between the semiclassical and CDCC approaches is planned (Capel). This work enables reliable extraction of neutron capture cross sections from Coulomb dissociation experiments for neutron-rich nuclei.
