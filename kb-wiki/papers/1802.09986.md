# Re-examining the transition into the N=20 island of inversion: structure of $^{30}$Mg

> Intermediate energy single-neutron removal from $^{31}$Mg has been employed to investigate the transition into the N=20 island of inversion. Levels up to 5~MeV excitation energy in $^{30}$Mg were populated and spin-parity assignments were inferred from the corresponding longitudinal momentum distributions and $γ$-ray decay scheme. Comparison with eikonal-model calculations also permitted spectroscopic factors to be deduced. Surprisingly, the 0$^{+}_{2}$ level in $^{30}$Mg was found to have a strength much weaker than expected in the conventional picture of a predominantly $2p - 2h$ intruder configuration having a large overlap with the deformed $^{31}$Mg ground state. In addition, negative parity levels were identified for the first time in $^{30}$Mg, one of which is located at low excitation energy. The results are discussed in the light of shell-model calculations employing two newly developed approaches with markedly different descriptions of the structure of $^{30}$Mg. It is concluded that the cross-shell effects in the region of the island of inversion at Z=12 are considerably more complex than previously thought and that $np - nh$ configurations play a major role in the structure of $^{30}$Mg.

## Key claim

The transition into the N=20 island of inversion at Z=12 is considerably more complex than previously thought, with the 0⁺₂ level in ³⁰Mg exhibiting a spectroscopic factor much weaker than expected for a predominantly 2p-2h intruder configuration, and with np-nh configurations playing a major role in the structure of ³⁰Mg.

## Method

Intermediate energy single-neutron removal from a ³¹Mg secondary beam (55.1 MeV/nucleon) on a carbon target was employed to populate levels up to 5 MeV excitation energy in ³⁰Mg. Spin-parity assignments were inferred from longitudinal momentum distributions measured with the SPEG spectrometer and the γ-ray decay scheme detected by an array of 8 EXOGAM Ge clover detectors, with spectroscopic factors deduced by comparison to eikonal-model calculations.

## Context

This work extends studies of the N=20 island of inversion, where earlier work by Thibault and Detraz first identified intruder configurations, and where shell-model calculations by Poves, Warburton, Utsuno, Otsuka, and Nowacki have been used to explain enhanced binding energies and low 2⁺ excitation energies. The results contradict the conventional picture from Terry and from Mach and Schwerd that placed ³⁰Mg outside the island of inversion with a spherical 0p-0h ground state coexisting with a 2p-2h intruder-dominated 0⁺₂ isomer. The findings support the EEdf1 calculations by Tsunoda (2017) which predict significant 4p-4h admixtures, in contrast to the SDPF-U-MIX calculations by Caurier, and enable future investigations using d(³⁰Mg,p) neutron transfer reactions and improved high-energy γ-ray detection.
