# Shape vibrations and quasiparticle excitations in the lowest 0+ excited state of the Erbium isotopes

> The ground and first excited 0+ states of the {156-172}Er isotopes are analyzed in the framework of the generator coordinate method. The shape parameter beta is used to generate wave functions with different deformations which together with the two-quasiparticle states built on them provide a set of states. An angular momentum and particle number projection of the latter spawn the basis states of the generator coordinate method. With this ansatz and using the separable pairing plus quadrupole interaction we obtain a good agreement with the experimental spectra and E2 transition rates up to moderate spin values. The structure of the wave functions suggests that the first excited 0+ states in the soft Er isotopes are dominated by shape fluctuations, while in the well deformed Er isotopes the two-quasiparticle states are more relevant. In between both degrees of freedom are necessary .

## Key claim

The first excited 0+ states in Er isotopes transition from being dominated by shape fluctuations in soft isotopes (neutron number N<98) to being dominated by two-quasiparticle excitations in well-deformed isotopes (N>=98), with both degrees of freedom necessary for transitional nuclei (N=92-96).

## Method

The authors used the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the deformation parameter beta as the generator coordinate, combined with two-quasiparticle states built on each HFB vacuum. After angular momentum and particle number projection, they solved the Hill-Wheeler equation using a separable pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian with three major harmonic oscillator shells as the single-particle space.

## Key numbers

- The monopole pairing strength is G_M = (18.75 ∓ 13.00(N-Z)/A)/A, with the minus sign for neutrons and plus for protons
- The quadrupole pairing strength is G_Q = 0.16 G_M
- The quadrupole-quadrupole strength parameter is chi' = 70 A^{-1.4} MeV
- The two-quasiparticle energy cutoff condition is E_i(beta) + E_j(beta) + E_0(beta) <= E(beta_min) + 3.5 MeV
- The calculated interband B(E2,0+_2 -> 2+_1) for 156Er (N=88) is approximately 100 W.u., suggesting shape vibration dominance
- The calculated interband B(E2,0+_2 -> 2+_1) for 168Er is less than 0.4 W.u., suggesting two-quasiparticle dominance
- The rotational constants A used for subtracting rigid rotor energy range from 0.057 MeV/hbar^2 for 156Er to 0.013 MeV/hbar^2 for 166-172Er

## Context

This work extends earlier GCM calculations (GCM2013) that only considered shape vibrations and failed for heavier Er isotopes with N>98. It addresses the long-standing problem of the nature of 0+_2 states in deformed nuclei, which have been treated as beta-vibrations in the Bohr collective model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) and as one-phonon states in the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) and projected shell model (PSM) calculations for specific nuclei like 158Gd and 168Er. The results enable systematic understanding of how collective and single-particle degrees of freedom combine across the transition from soft to well-deformed nuclei.
