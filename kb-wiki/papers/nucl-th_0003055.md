# Quark Condensate in the Deuteron

> We study the changes produced by the deuteron on the QCD quark condensate by means the Feynman-Hellmann theorem and find that the pion mass dependence of the pion-nucleon coupling could play an important role. We also discuss the relation between the many body effect of the condensate and the meson exchange currents, as seen by photons and pions. For pion probes, the many-body term in the physical amplitude differs significantly from that of soft pions, the one linked to the condensate. Thus no information about the many-body term of the condensate can be extracted from the pion-deuteron scattering length. On the other hand, in the Compton amplitude, the relationship with the condensate is a more direct one.

# Quark Condensate in the Deuteron

## Key claim

The many-body contribution to the deuteron sigma commutator from the nucleon-nucleon interaction is about 10 MeV (roughly five times the binding energy), but the pion-deuteron scattering length cannot be used to extract this condensate information because the meson-exchange correction linked to the sigma commutator is reduced by a factor of three when going from soft to physical pions, and non-static corrections further obscure the signal; the Compton amplitude provides a more direct probe.

## Method

The authors apply the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to the deuteron mass, expressing the deuteron sigma-term as the derivative of the mass with respect to the squared pion mass, and separate the two-nucleon interaction contribution from the sum of two free-nucleon sigma-terms. They evaluate the expectation values of the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) and its derivative with respect to pion mass squared using deuteron wave functions from the Argonne v14 and super-soft-core C potentials, as well as a regularized toy potential with monopole form factors. The meson-exchange contributions to the pion-deuteron scattering length and the forward Compton amplitude are then computed and related to the sigma commutator.

## Key numbers

- The expectation value of the OPEP in the deuteron ranges from -14.94 MeV (SSC potential) to -19.83 MeV (Argonne v14) for the regularized potential, and from -29.27 MeV to -33.63 MeV for the perturbative (unregularized) OPEP.
- The expectation value of the derivative term (m^2 dV_pi/d m^2) ranges from 1.48 MeV (SSC) to 1.52 MeV (Argonne v14) for the regularized potential.
- The many-body sigma commutator sigma_epsilon is 5.96 MeV (SSC) and 7.47 MeV (Argonne v14), about five times the deuteron binding energy of 2.2250 MeV.
- The meson-exchange contribution to the soft-pion deuteron scattering length a_mec(0) is -0.0019 m^{-1} for both realistic potentials.
- The meson-exchange contribution to the physical-pion deuteron scattering length a_mec(m) is -0.0011 m^{-1} for both realistic potentials.
- The exchange contribution to the forward Compton amplitude F_A^{exch}(0) is 0.057 e^2 m^{-1} (SSC) and 0.071 e^2 m^{-1} (Argonne v14).
- The coefficient c in the expression for sigma_epsilon is -0.30, dominated by the derivative of the pi-N coupling constant.

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD condensate modifications in nuclear matter (Chanfray and Ericson 1993) to the specific case of the deuteron, where the nucleon-nucleon interaction is dominated by one-pion exchange. The authors contrast their results with those of Gammal and Frederico (1998) using the Skyrme model, which gave a similar magnitude but opposite sign for sigma_epsilon. The paper establishes that while the pion-deuteron scattering length cannot provide empirical access to the condensate (contradicting the naive PCAC expectation), the Compton amplitude offers a viable path for experimental determination, following the framework of Ericson, Rosa-Clot, and Kulagin (1996) for the Bethe-Levinger sum rule.
