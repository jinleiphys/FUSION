# Neutron-proton pairing in Nuclear Matter

> The self-energy effect on the neutron-proton (np) pairing gap is investigated up to the third order within the framework of the extend Bruecker-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach combined with the BCS theory. The self-energy up to the second-order contribution turns out to reduce strongly the effective energy gap, while the \emph{renormalization} term enhances it significantly. In addition, the effect of the three-body force on the np pairing gap is shown to be negligible. To connect the present results with the np pairing in finite nuclei, an effective density-dependent zero-range pairing force is established with the parameters calibrated to the microscopically calculated energy gap.

## Key claim

The self-energy effect on the neutron-proton (np) pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter is investigated up to the third order in the hole-line expansion; the second-order contribution strongly reduces the effective gap, but the third-order "renormalization" term enhances it significantly, and the three-body force (3BF) effect is negligible.

## Method

The authors used the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach combined with BCS theory, employing the Argonne V18 two-body interaction and a microscopic 3BF constructed via the meson-exchange current approach. The self-energy was calculated up to the third order in the hole-line expansion (including the lowest-order M1, the rearrangement term M2, and the renormalization term M3), and the off-shell BCS gap equation was solved self-consistently with the density constraint for the coupled 3SD1 channel. The quasiparticle approximation was used to derive an effective gap equation involving the quasiparticle strength Z factor.

## Key numbers

* Maximum effective energy gap Z_F * Delta_F = 2.8 MeV at density rho = 0.055 fm^{-3} (k_F = 0.93 fm^{-1}) when including M1+M2+M3 with two-body force only.
* Effective energy gap at saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}) is approximately 0.75 MeV with M1+M2+M3.
* The depletion parameter kappa is about 0.25 for symmetric nuclear matter in the density range rho in (0.4 rho_0, 2.3 rho_0).
* For the effective density-dependent zero-range pairing force, two parameter sets are given: v_0 = -502.77 MeV with cutoff epsilon_c = 80 MeV, and v_0 = -440.73 MeV with cutoff epsilon_c = 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the self-energy effect on np pairing, which had only considered the lowest-order approximation M1 (Sig3, Sig4), by including the rearrangement term M2 and the renormalization term M3. It also adds the first investigation of 3BF effects on T=0 np pairing. The results are compared with predictions from the self-consistent in-medium T-matrix approximation (Sig4), showing similar behavior but slightly larger gaps at moderate densities. The effective density-dependent zero-range pairing force provided can be used in calculations of np pairing in finite nuclei, extending the approach of Bertsch and Esbensen (Be).
