# A Primary Study of Heavy Baryons Lambda_Q, Xi_Q, Sigma_Q and Omega_Q

> We perform a preliminary study of the 1/2+ and 3/2+ ground-state baryons containing a heavy quark in the framework of the chiral SU(3) quark model. By using the calculus of variations, masses of Lambda_Q, Sigma_Q, Xi_Q, Omega_Q, Sigma_Q^*, Xi_Q^* and Omega_Q^*, where Q means c or b quark, are calculated. With taking reasonable model parameters, the numerical results of established heavy baryons are generally in agreement with the available experimental data, except that those of Xi_Q are somewhat heavier. For Omega_b with undetermined experimental mass and nobserved Xi_b^*, Omega_b^*, reasonable theoretical predictions are obtained. Interactions inside baryons are also discussed.

## Key claim
The chiral SU(3) quark model can reproduce the masses of established heavy baryons generally in agreement with experimental data, except that the calculated masses of Xi_Q (Xi_c and Xi_b) are systematically heavier than the observed values by about 62-95 MeV.

## Method
The authors used the chiral SU(3) quark model with a Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, one-gluon-exchange (OGE), confinement, and chiral field exchange terms. For heavy-light quark pairs, Goldstone boson exchanges were omitted. Model parameters for light quarks were taken from prior work (Zhang 1997), while heavy-quark OGE couplings and confinement strengths were fitted to reproduce heavy meson masses (D, D*, D_s, D_s*, B, B*, B_s, B_s*). Masses of the ground-state baryons were then calculated by the calculus of variations using a harmonic-oscillator width as the variational parameter.

## Key numbers
- Lambda_c mass: calculated values range from 2305.9 to 2309.3 MeV across parameter sets, compared to the experimental value of 2286.5 MeV (about 20 MeV higher).
- Sigma_c mass: closest calculated value is 2452.3 MeV (with g_c=0.60, m_c=1430 MeV), compared to the experimental 2453.8 MeV; largest deviation is 64 MeV (2518.2 MeV with g_c=0.53, m_c=1870 MeV).
- Xi_c mass: calculated values range from 2533.8 to 2559.4 MeV, all 62-88 MeV above the experimental 2471.0 MeV.
- Omega_c mass: calculated values range from 2681.3 to 2713.4 MeV, with the exact experimental value 2697.5 MeV reproduced at g_c=0.53, m_c=1550 MeV.
- Lambda_b mass: calculated values range from 5643.6 to 5646.2 MeV, about 25 MeV above the experimental 5620.2 MeV.
- Sigma_b mass: closest calculated value is 5815.0 MeV (with g_b=0.60, m_b=4720 MeV), compared to the experimental 5807.8 MeV; other values are 29-55 MeV heavier.
- Xi_b mass: calculated values range from 5868.8 to 5887.5 MeV, all 76-95 MeV above the experimental 5792.4 MeV.
- Omega_b mass: calculated values range from 6023.3 to 6046.1 MeV, with the average result 6054.4 MeV compatible with the observed value from Ref. omb2.
- Sigma_c* mass: closest calculated value is 2516.8 MeV (with g_c=0.60, m_c=1430 MeV), compared to the experimental 2518.0 MeV.
- Xi_c* mass: closest calculated value is 2648.6 MeV (with g_c=0.58, m_c=1550 MeV), compared to the experimental 2646.6 MeV.
- Omega_c* mass: closest calculated value is 2769.6 MeV (with g_c=0.60, m_c=1550 MeV), compared to the experimental 2768.3 MeV.
- Sigma_b* mass: closest calculated value is 5838.3 MeV (with g_b=0.60, m_b=4720 MeV), compared to the experimental 5829.0 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for light baryons, deuteron binding, and NN/YN scattering (Zhang 1997; Huang), to singly-heavy baryons. It follows earlier applications of the same model to heavy tetraquarks and heavy mesons (Zhang 2009; Wang 2010). The results are compared with other theoretical approaches including quark models (Ebert 2005), QCD sum rules (Zhu 2008; Wang; Zhang 2008), and lattice QCD (Mathur 2002). The predictions for unobserved Xi_b* and Omega_b* baryons provide targets for future experimental searches, and the authors note that vector meson exchange effects remain to be included.
