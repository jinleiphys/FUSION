# Scaling properties of proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections

> We study the scaling properties of proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections for stable nuclei and propose an approximate expression in proportion to Z^{2/3}σ_{pp}^{total} + N^{2/3} σ_{pn}^{total}. Based on this expression, we can derive a relation that enables us to predict a total reaction cross section for any stable nucleus within 10% uncertainty at most, using the empirical value of the total reaction cross section of a given nucleus.

# Scaling properties of proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections

## Key claim
The authors propose an approximate expression for proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections proportional to Z^{2/3} σ_pp^total + N^{2/3} σ_pn^total, which is merely a parameterization without any microscopic foundation. Using this expression, they derive a relation that predicts the total reaction cross section for any stable nucleus within 10% uncertainty at most, but only if one accepts the factorization of an energy-dependent coefficient C(E) that is independent of mass number.

## Method
The authors parameterize the total reaction cross section σ_R for proton-nucleus reactions as σ_R = π C(E) [Z^{2/3} σ_pp^total + N^{2/3} σ_pn^total], where C(E) is an energy-dependent coefficient averaged over mass number. They extract C(E) values from experimental data at six energies between 40 and 860 MeV, then derive a parameter-free relation that predicts σ_R for any stable nucleus using the known cross section of a reference nucleus at the same energy.

## Key numbers
- The coefficient πC(E) ranges from 0.3764 ± 0.051 at 40 MeV to 1.2144 ± 0.066 at 345 MeV, then decreases to 0.8334 ± 0.068 at 860 MeV
- The uncertainty Δ in the parameter-free prediction is approximately 0.1 (10%)
- Reference nuclei and their cross sections used for predictions include: ⁶⁴Zn at 1092 ± 23 mb, ⁶⁴Cu at 835 ± 23 mb, ⁵⁶Fe at 662 ± 19 mb, and ⁶⁴Cu at 728 ± 17 mb
- The formula is applicable in the energy range from 40 MeV to 1000 MeV, with caution needed at 40 MeV for A ≥ 100 due to large deviations

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier empirical formula for carbon isotopes (Abu-Ibrahim et al., 2009) to stable nuclei across the entire mass-number region. The proposed surface-type scaling (Z^{2/3} + N^{2/3}) improves upon their earlier volume-type formula (Z + N), which was limited to restricted mass-number ranges. The parameter-free relation enables prediction of unknown reaction cross sections using only one measured cross section, which could be useful for estimating cross sections for nuclei where direct measurements are difficult.
