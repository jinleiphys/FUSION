# Spinodal instabilities of baryon-rich quark matter in heavy ion collisions

> Using the test-particle method to solve the transport equation derived from the Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model, we study how phase separation occurs in an expanding quark matter like that in a heavy ion collision. To test our method, we first investigate the growth rates of unstable modes of quark matter in a static cubic box and find them to agree with the analytical results that were previously obtained using the linear response theory. In this case, we also find the higher-order scaled density moments to increase with time and saturate at values significantly larger than one, which corresponds to a uniform density distribution, after the phase separation. The skewness of the quark number event-by-event distribution in a small sub-volume of the system is also found to increase, but this feature disappears if the sub-volume is large. For the expanding quark matter, two cases are considered with one using a blast-wave model for the initial conditions and the other using initial conditions from a mulple-phase transport (AMPT) model. In both cases, we find the expansion of the quark matter is slowed down by the presence of a first-order phase transition. Also, density clumps appear in the system and the momentum distribution of partons becomes anisotropic , which can be characterized by large scaled density moments and non-vanishing anisotropic elliptic and quadrupolar flows, respectively. The large density fluctuations further lead to an enhancement in the dilepton yield. In the case with the AMPT initial conditions, the presence of a first-order phase transition also results in a narrower rapidity distribution of partons after their freeze out. These effects of density fluctuations can be regarded as possible signals for a first-order phase transition that occurs in the baryon-rich quark matter formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Spinodal instabilities of baryon-rich quark matter in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Using test-particle simulations of the NJL-model transport equation, the authors show that density fluctuations from spinodal instabilities during a first-order phase transition in expanding baryon-rich quark matter produce measurable signals (enhanced scaled density moments, anisotropic flow, and dilepton yield), but these signals depend on the system spending sufficient time inside the spinodal region, only about 0.6 fm/c for the central region with AMPT initial conditions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.5 GeV, versus about 10 fm/c for blast-wave initial conditions.

## Method
The authors solve the Boltzmann equation derived from the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model using the test-particle method, including both mean-field potentials (scalar and vector interactions) and parton scatterings with Pauli blocking. They first validate the method by comparing growth rates of unstable sinusoidal density modes in a static cubic box against analytical linear response theory results from their earlier work (Li and Ko, 2016). They then simulate expanding quark matter using two sets of initial conditions: a blast-wave model with Wood-Saxon density profile (R=5 fm, a=0.5 fm) and initial conditions from the AMPT model with string melting for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.5 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Growth rates extracted for wave numbers k = 0.63, 0.31, 0.21, 0.16, 0.13 fm^{-1} in quark matter at density rho=0.7 fm^{-3} and temperature T=45 MeV, agreeing with linearized Boltzmann equation results.
- For box simulations at T=20 MeV and net quark density n_q=0.5 fm^{-3} with G_V=0: dilute phase density approximately 0.25 fm^{-3}, dense phase density approximately 1.0 fm^{-3}.
- Scaled density moments after phase separation in the box: <rho^2>/<rho>^2 ≈ 1.22, <rho^4>/<rho>^4 ≈ 2.11, <rho^6>/<rho>^6 ≈ 3.75.
- Skewness of quark number distribution in a 0.6 fm^3 sub-volume increases from 0.11 at t=0 to 0.60 at 20 fm/c and 0.75 at 40 fm/c; in a 30 fm^3 sub-volume skewness changes from -0.001 to 0.086 to 0.132.
- For blast-wave expanding quark matter with initial density rho_0=1.5 fm^{-3} and T=70 MeV: system enters spinodal region at about 6.5 fm/c and leaves at about 17.4 fm/c.
- Dilepton invariant mass spectrum peaks at sqrt(s) ≈ 0.5 GeV with peak value about 3.5 × 10^{-4} GeV^{-1} for the case with first-order phase transition.
- For AMPT initial conditions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.5 GeV: central part enters spinodal region at about 4.4 fm/c and exits at about 5.0 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous linear response study of spinodal instabilities in the PNJL model (Li and Ko, 2016) by going beyond the small-amplitude limit to simulate the full nonlinear evolution of density fluctuations. It builds on the test-particle method for solving transport equations (Wong, 1982) and the geometric scattering treatment (Bertsch and Das Gupta, 1988). The scaled density moments approach follows earlier work on density fluctuations as phase transition signals (PhysRevC.87.054903, 2013). The study enables future experimental searches for first-order phase transition signatures in baryon-rich quark matter at RHIC Beam Energy Scan and FAIR, particularly through measurements of scaled density moments, anisotropic flow coefficients v_2 and v_4, and dilepton yields.
