# On nonlinearity in hydrodynamic response to the initial geometry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> In the context of event-by-event hydrodynamic description, we analyze the implications of two models characterized by distinct initial conditions. The initial energy density of the first model adopts a Gaussian-type distribution, while those of the second one are features by high energy peripheral tubes. We calibrate the initial conditions of both models so that their initial probability distribution of eccentricity are mostly identical. Subsequently, the resultant scaled probability distributions of collective flow and the correlations between flow harmonic and eccentricity coefficients are investigated. Besides, the calculations are carried out for particle correlations regarding the symmetric cumulant, mixed harmonics, and nonlinear response coefficients. Although the resultant two-particle correlations possess similar shapes, numerical calculations indicate a subtle difference between the two models. To be specific, the difference resides in more detailed observables such as the probability distributions of elliptic flow as well as Pearson correlation coefficient regarding higher-order harmonics. We discuss several essential aspects concerning the linearity and nonlinearity between initial eccentricities and final state anisotropies. Further implications are addressed.

## Key claim

Although two distinct initial-condition models (a peripheral-tube model and an anisotropic Gaussian model) are calibrated to produce nearly identical probability distributions of initial eccentricities, the resulting hydrodynamic flow observables show subtle but systematic differences, particularly in the probability distributions of elliptic flow and in the Pearson correlation coefficients for higher-order harmonics, indicating that the mapping between initial geometry and final flow is not fully captured by eccentricity moments alone.

## Method

The authors prepared two sets of event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (20%-25% centrality): a peripheral tube model (background plus three high-energy tubes near the surface) and an anisotropic Gaussian model whose radial profile is Gaussian with azimuthal modulation parameterized by Fourier coefficients C_n. The anisotropic Gaussian parameters were tuned so that its eccentricity distributions matched those of the tube model. Both sets of initial conditions were evolved with the SPheRIO hydrodynamic code, and the resulting flow harmonics, probability distributions, symmetric cumulants, mixed harmonics, and nonlinear response coefficients were computed and compared.

## Key numbers

- The peripheral tube model parameters: K = 103.9, L = -89, M = 28.5, a = 0.077, b = 0.033, c = 2, A_tube = 30, r_0 = 1.3, R_tube = 1.1 (dimensionless units for energy density and length).
- The anisotropic Gaussian model parameters: R_0 = 3.1, Z = 133; for n=2: sigma_n = 0.075, M_n = 0.39; for n=3: sigma_n = 0.095, M_n = 0.045; for n=4: sigma_n = 0.145, M_n = 0.073; for n=5: sigma_n = 0.128, M_n = 0.063.
- Pearson correlation coefficient rho_n for epsilon_2 vs. v_2 and epsilon_3 vs. v_3 is significantly positive; rho_4 drops nearly an order of magnitude compared to rho_3.
- Nonlinear response coefficient chi_4: peripheral tube model = 1.50, anisotropic Gaussian model = 1.69 (CMS/ATLAS data: 0.818).
- Nonlinear response coefficient chi_5: peripheral tube model = 2.44, anisotropic Gaussian model = 2.794 (CMS/ATLAS data: 1.878).
- Nonlinear response coefficient chi_62: peripheral tube model = 3.46, anisotropic Gaussian model = 2.97 (CMS/ATLAS data: 0.715).
- Nonlinear response coefficient chi_63: peripheral tube model = 2.33, anisotropic Gaussian model = 1.12 (CMS/ATLAS data: 0.878).
- Symmetric cumulant SC(4,2): peripheral tube model = 0.043 x 10^{-6}, anisotropic Gaussian model = -0.252 x 10^{-6} (opposite signs).
- Normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(4,2): peripheral tube model = 0.035, anisotropic Gaussian model = -0.063.
- Mixed harmonic <v_4 v_2^2 cos(4[Psi_4 - Psi_2])>: peripheral tube model = 4.09 x 10^{-5}, anisotropic Gaussian model = 8.39 x 10^{-5}.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing investigation of the linearity and nonlinearity of the hydrodynamic response to initial geometry fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the cumulant decomposition approach of Teaney and Yan (2011) and the Bessel-Fourier expansion of Gardim et al. (2012). The peripheral tube model was previously introduced by the authors (Wen et al., 2017, 2018) as an alternative to global moment-based descriptions. The results show that even when eccentricity distributions are matched, higher-order observables such as symmetric cumulants and nonlinear response coefficients can differ significantly, implying that future studies should explore whether such differences can be discriminated by experimental data from the LHC (CMS and ATLAS Collaborations) and what constraints they place on the nature of initial-state fluctuations.
