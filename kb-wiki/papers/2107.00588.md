# Constraining the hadronic spectrum and repulsive interactions in a hadron resonance gas via fluctuations of conserved charges

> We simultaneously incorporate two common extensions of the hadron resonance gas model, namely the addition of extra, unconfirmed resonances to the particle list and the excluded volume repulsive interactions. We emphasize the complementary nature of these two extensions and identify combinations of conserved charge susceptibilities that allow to constrain them separately. In particular, ratios of second-order susceptibilities like $Ï‡_{11}^{BQ}/Ï‡_2^B$ and $Ï‡_{11}^{BS}/Ï‡_2^B$ are sensitive only to the baryon spectrum, while fourth-to-second order ratios like $Ï‡_4^B/Ï‡_2^B$, $Ï‡_{31}^{BS}/Ï‡_{11}^{BS}$, or $Ï‡_{31}^{BQ}/Ï‡_{11}^{BQ}$ are mainly determined by repulsive interactions. The modified hadron resonance gas model presented here significantly improves the description of lattice QCD susceptibilities at chemical freeze-out and can be used for the analysis of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.

# Constraining the hadronic spectrum and repulsive interactions in a hadron resonance gas via fluctuations of conserved charges

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas model can simultaneously incorporate both extra unconfirmed resonances and excluded volume repulsive interactions, and these two extensions affect different observables in complementary ways. The analysis of available lattice QCD results suggests the presence of both extra states in the baryon-strangeness sector and repulsive baryonic interactions, with indications that hyperons have a smaller repulsive core than non-strange baryons.

## Method

The authors extended the standard hadron resonance gas (HRG) model by adding unconfirmed resonances from the PDG2016+ list (including * and ** states) and the Quark Model (QM) list, and by implementing excluded volume (EV) repulsive interactions only for baryon-baryon and antibaryon-antibaryon pairs using a common EV parameter b. They calculated conserved charge susceptibilities and their ratios, identifying specific combinations that isolate either the hadronic spectrum sensitivity (ratios of second-order susceptibilities like Ï‡11^{BQ}/Ï‡2^B and Ï‡11^{BS}/Ï‡2^B) or the repulsive interaction sensitivity (fourth-to-second order ratios like Ï‡4^B/Ï‡2^B, Ï‡31^{BS}/Ï‡11^{BS}, and Ï‡31^{BQ}/Ï‡11^{BQ}), then compared these to lattice QCD results.
