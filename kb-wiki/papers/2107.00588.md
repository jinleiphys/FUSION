# Constraining the hadronic spectrum and repulsive interactions in a hadron resonance gas via fluctuations of conserved charges

> We simultaneously incorporate two common extensions of the hadron resonance gas model, namely the addition of extra, unconfirmed resonances to the particle list and the excluded volume repulsive interactions. We emphasize the complementary nature of these two extensions and identify combinations of conserved charge susceptibilities that allow to constrain them separately. In particular, ratios of second-order susceptibilities like $χ_{11}^{BQ}/χ_2^B$ and $χ_{11}^{BS}/χ_2^B$ are sensitive only to the baryon spectrum, while fourth-to-second order ratios like $χ_4^B/χ_2^B$, $χ_{31}^{BS}/χ_{11}^{BS}$, or $χ_{31}^{BQ}/χ_{11}^{BQ}$ are mainly determined by repulsive interactions. Analysis of the available lattice results suggests the presence of both the extra states in the baryon-strangeness sector and the repulsive baryonic interaction, with indications that hyperons have a smaller repulsive core than non-strange baryons. The modified hadron resonance gas model presented here significantly improves the description of lattice QCD susceptibilities at chemical freeze-out and can be used for the analysis of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.

# Constraining the hadronic spectrum and repulsive interactions in a hadron resonance gas via fluctuations of conserved charges

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas model can simultaneously incorporate both extra unconfirmed resonances and excluded volume repulsive interactions, and these two extensions affect different observables in complementary ways. The analysis of available lattice QCD results suggests the presence of both extra states in the baryon-strangeness sector and repulsive baryonic interactions, with indications that hyperons have a smaller repulsive core than non-strange baryons.

## Method

The authors extended the standard hadron resonance gas (HRG) model by adding unconfirmed resonances from the PDG2016+ list (including * and ** states) and the Quark Model (QM) list, and by implementing excluded volume (EV) repulsive interactions only for baryon-baryon and antibaryon-antibaryon pairs using a common EV parameter b. They calculated conserved charge susceptibilities and their ratios, identifying specific combinations that isolate either the hadronic spectrum sensitivity (ratios of second-order susceptibilities like χ11^{BQ}/χ2^B and χ11^{BS}/χ2^B) or the repulsive interaction sensitivity (fourth-to-second order ratios like χ4^B/χ2^B, χ31^{BS}/χ11^{BS}, and χ31^{BQ}/χ11^{BQ}), then compared these to lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- The excluded volume parameter b was varied over the range 0.4–1 fm^3 for comparisons with lattice data at Nτ=12 from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2018).
- The ratios χ4^B/χ2^B, χ31^{BS}/χ11^{BS}, and χ31^{BQ}/χ11^{BQ} are all equal to 1 in the ideal HRG model regardless of extra states; suppression relative to unity is directly sensitive to the EV parameter b.
- At temperatures below 150 MeV, the three fourth-to-second order ratios sit on top of each other in the lattice data; statistically significant differences emerge at T ≳ 160 MeV.
- For the QM list, best agreement with lattice data for χ4^S/χ2^S and χ11^{us} required b ≃ 1 fm^3; for the PDG2016+ list, best agreement required smaller excluded volumes (upper part of the b=0–1 fm^3 band).
- The PDG2016+ and QM lists allowed extending agreement with lattice data for χ11^{us} to T = 160–165 MeV when baryon excluded volumes of up to b ≃ 0.4 fm^3 and b ≃ 1 fm^3 were used, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that separately investigated either extra resonances (Alba 2017, Alba 2020) or excluded volume interactions (Vovchenko 2016, Vovchenko 2017) in the HRG model, and addresses discrepancies between lattice QCD susceptibilities and the standard HRG model observed at temperatures T ∼ 150–160 MeV (Bazavov 2014, Borsanyi 2018, Bellwied 2021). The modified HRG model presented here improves the description of lattice QCD susceptibilities at chemical freeze-out and can be used for analyzing event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC and future experiments at NICA and GSI-FAIR.
