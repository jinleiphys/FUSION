# Scaling Flows and Dissipation in the Dilute Fermi Gas at Unitarity

> We describe recent attempts to extract the shear viscosity of the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity from experiments involving scaling flows. A scaling flow is a solution of the hydrodynamic equations that preserves the shape of the density distribution. The scaling flows that have been explored in the laboratory are the transverse expansion from a deformed trap ("elliptic flow"), the expansion from a rotating trap, and collective oscillations. We discuss advantages and disadvantages of the different experiments, and point to improvements of the theoretical analysis that are needed in order to achieve definitive results. A conservative bound based on the current data is that the minimum of the shear viscosity to entropy density ration is that eta/s is less or equal to 0.5 hbar/k_B.

# Scaling Flows and Dissipation in the Dilute Fermi Gas at Unitarity

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the unitary Fermi gas has a minimum value of η/s ≤ 0.5 ħ/k_B based on current experimental data, but this is a conservative bound rather than a definitive measurement; the authors emphasize that improvements in theoretical analysis are needed to achieve definitive results, and that the bound is derived from scaling flow experiments that each have specific advantages and disadvantages.

## Method
The authors analyze three types of scaling flow experiments (transverse expansion from a deformed trap or "elliptic flow", expansion from a rotating trap, and collective oscillations) by solving the Euler and Navier-Stokes equations for a dilute Fermi gas at unitarity. They derive equations of motion for the scale parameters describing the cloud expansion, compute the energy dissipated by shear viscosity perturbatively, and compare the predicted crossing time of transverse and longitudinal radii to experimental data from O'Hara et al. (2002). The analysis uses the local density approximation and assumes the fluid remains isothermal during expansion.

## Key numbers
- The universal parameter ξ = 0.40(2) from quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Carlson, 2005) for the zero-temperature equation of state
- The critical temperature for superfluidity is T_c/T_F^hom ≃ 0.15, corresponding to y_c ≃ 0.72
- The integral ∫₀^∞ dt (ḃ_⊥/b_⊥)² = 0.87 ω_⊥ for the transverse expansion rate
- The dissipated energy ratio ΔE/E_int(0) = -2.32·β, where β is a dimensionless parameter defined in terms of the trap-averaged viscosity
- The predicted sensitivity of crossing time to dissipation: (δt/t)_cross = 0.008·(⟨α_s⟩/(1/(4π)))·(2×10⁵/N)^(1/3)·(0.045/λ)^(1/3)·((S/N)/2.2)·(0.83/(E_0/E_F))
- For ⟨α_s⟩ = 0.5, the crossing time shift reaches about 5%, with a numerical solution giving (δt/t)_cross = 6.5%
- The entropy generated is (ΔS/N) ≃ 0.27 for the conditions of the O'Hara et al. (2002) experiment
- The kinetic theory result for the shear viscosity coefficient is η_0 = 15/(32√π)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of hydrodynamic flow in the unitary Fermi gas first observed by O'Hara et al. (2002) and builds on the theoretical framework of Danielewicz and Gyulassy (1984) regarding the quantum bound on viscosity, as well as the holographic bound η/s ≥ ħ/(4πk_B) conjectured by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004). The paper reviews experiments by Kinast et al. (2004, 2005), Altmeyer et al. (2006), Wright et al. (2007), Clancy et al. (2007), and Riedl et al. (2008), and points to the need for improved theoretical analysis including two-fluid hydrodynamics below T_c and relaxation time effects. The results enable future experiments to extract the shear viscosity more precisely and test whether the unitary Fermi gas approaches the conjectured perfect fluid bound.
