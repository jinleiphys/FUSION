# How to Measure the $Q\overline{Q}$ Potential in a Light-Front Calculation

> A scheme is developed which shows how one would, given a light-front Hamiltonian for QCD, extract the $Q\overline{Q}$ potential, i.e. the quantity which corresponds to the potential between two infinitely heavy quarks in a rest frame, from a light-front calculation. The resulting potential will in general depend on the direction along which the infinitely heavy sources are separated and thus provides a direct probe of violations of rotational invariance in a physical observable. Furthermore, easy comparison to data from $c\overline{c}$ and $b\overline{b}$ spectroscopy and to lattice data is possible. The scheme may thus be very helpful in constructing a light-front Hamiltonian through an iterative procedure.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general scheme for extracting the static $Q\overline{Q}$ potential from a light-front Hamiltonian calculation, but the resulting potential will in general depend on the direction along which the infinitely heavy sources are separated, thus providing a direct probe of violations of rotational invariance in a physical observable.

## Method

The scheme treats two infinitely heavy quarks at fixed separation as one extended heavy source moving with constant velocity on the light front, then derives an effective light-front Hamiltonian for the heavy pair interacting with the "brown muck" (light degrees of freedom). The lowest eigenvalue of this effective Hamiltonian yields the potential $V(\vec{R})$, which is equivalent to the potential extracted from an asymmetric rectangular Wilson loop in lattice calculations. The formalism includes form factors arising from the kinematic displacement operator and requires cutoffs on light constituent momenta and kinetic energy differences to ensure the heavy quark limit commutes with loop integrations.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal separation in the rest frame $\Delta x^3$ transforms to $\Delta x^- = \sqrt{2}\Delta x^3$ on the light front for the case where charges move with constant four-velocity $v^\mu$ and ${\vec v}_\perp=0$ in the rest frame.
- For the more general case with constant four-velocity $v^\mu$, the longitudinal separation becomes $\Delta x^- = \Delta x^3/v^+$.
- The canonical Hamiltonian yields a static potential term $P^-_{HH} = g^2\,{\chi_Q^\dagger}' T^a \chi_Q\, \chi_{\bar{Q}}^\dagger T^a {\chi_{\bar{Q}}}'\, \delta^{(2)}({\vec R}_\perp)|R^3|v^+$ between the two heavy quarks.
- The photon distribution from an infinitely heavy electron falls off at large $k^+$ as $\rho(k^+) \sim 1/\left(v^+\Lambda {k^+}^4\right) + 1/{k^+}^5\log \left(\Lambda v^+/k^+\right)$.

## Context

This work extends the heavy quark limit formalism developed by Glazek and Perry (1992) and Burkardt (1992) to the two-source case, enabling direct comparison with quenched lattice data and fits to charmonium and bottomonium spectra. The scheme is designed to help construct a light-front Hamiltonian through an iterative procedure by using rotational invariance of the $Q\overline{Q}$ potential as a constraint on noncovariant counterterm functions, and it can also be applied to fix the transverse scale and renormalization constants in the transverse lattice formulation of light-front QCD.
