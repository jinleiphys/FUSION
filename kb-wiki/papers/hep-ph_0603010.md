# Testing the theory of QGP-induced energy loss at RHIC and the LHC

> We compare an analytic model of jet quenching, based on the GLV non-Abelian energy loss formalism, to numerical results for the centrality dependent suppression of hadron cross sections in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC. Simulations of neutral pion quenching versus the size of the colliding nuclear system are presented to high transverse momentum pT. At low and moderate pT, we study the contribution of medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung to single inclusive hadron production. In Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, the redistribution of the lost energy is shown to play a critical role in yielding nuclear suppression that does not violate the participant scaling limit.

# Testing the theory of QGP-induced energy loss at RHIC and the LHC

## Key claim

The GLV non-Abelian energy loss formalism, when implemented with running coupling and kinematic constraints, predicts that the logarithm of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ scales approximately as $N_{\text{part}}^{2/3}$ across different nuclear systems, but only when the analytic model is normalized to a reference numerical calculation in central A+A collisions at the same $p_T$, $y$, and $\sqrt{s}$.

## Method

The authors compare an analytic model of jet quenching, based on the GLV opacity expansion formalism, to numerical simulations of medium-induced gluon radiation evaluated to third order in opacity with (1+1)D Bjorken expansion of the plasma. The energy loss is implemented as a kinematic modification of the momentum fraction in fragmentation functions, with a Poisson-based probabilistic distribution $P(\epsilon)$ for fractional energy loss that incorporates kinematic constraints and running of the strong coupling constant $\alpha_s$ at the Debye screening scale $\mu \simeq gT$.

## Key numbers

- In central Au+Au collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV), the nuclear modification factor shows approximately 5-fold suppression, corresponding to an effective fractional energy loss $\epsilon_{\text{eff}} \approx 0.2$ for 10 GeV quark jets.
- The fractional energy loss for quark jets at RHIC scales with system size as $N_{\text{part}}^{0.60-0.63}$, based on power law fits for $E_{\text{jet}} = 10, 20,$ and $30$ GeV across $N_{\text{part}}$ values from 9 to 328.
- At the LHC ($\sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV), the gluon feedback term leads to $\geq 100\%$ correction to the low and moderate $p_T$ $\pi^0$ production rate, compared to only $\sim 25\%$ modification at RHIC in the $p_T \sim 1-2$ GeV range.
- The analytic model predicts a factor $\sim 2$ suppression in central Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC, based on $N_{\text{part}}^{2/3} = 22$ for Cu compared to 48 for Au.

## Context

This work extends the GLV formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev, 2000) to compare analytic and numerical models of jet quenching across different nuclear systems at RHIC and the LHC. It provides predictions for Cu+Cu collisions that were subsequently compared to PHENIX data (Shimomura, 2005) and STAR data (Dunlop, 2005), showing good agreement within experimental uncertainties. The inclusion of gluon feedback from medium-induced bremsstrahlung resolves the violation of participant scaling that affected earlier calculations (Vitev, 2002; Turbide, 2005) and enables consistent treatment of jet suppression from high $p_T$ down to the region where bulk particle production dominates.
