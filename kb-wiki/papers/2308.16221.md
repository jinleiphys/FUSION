# One-loop matching of the $CP$-odd three-gluon operator to the gradient flow

> The calculation of the neutron electric dipole moment within effective field theories for physics beyond the Standard Model requires non-perturbative hadronic matrix elements of effective operators composed of quark and gluon fields. In order to use input from lattice computations, these matrix elements must be translated from a scheme suitable for lattice QCD to the minimal-subtraction scheme used in the effective-field-theory framework. The accuracy goal in the context of the neutron electric dipole moment necessitates at least a one-loop matching calculation. Here, we provide the one-loop matching coefficients for the $CP$-odd three-gluon operator between two different minimally subtracted 't Hooft-Veltman schemes and the gradient flow. This completes our program to obtain the one-loop gradient-flow matching coefficients for all $CP$-violating and flavor-conserving operators in the low-energy effective field theory up to dimension six.

## Key claim
The paper provides the one-loop matching coefficients for the CP-odd three-gluon operator between the gradient flow and two different 't Hooft-Veltman minimal-subtraction schemes, completing the one-loop gradient-flow matching for all CP-violating and flavor-conserving operators in the low-energy effective field theory up to dimension six. The matching coefficients depend on the choice of dimensional scheme for the MS operators, but the dimensional scheme for the flowed operators does not affect the matching coefficients of the non-evanescent sector when evanescent operators are correctly renormalized.

## Method
The matching coefficients were extracted from gluon two-point and three-point functions, quark chromo-electric dipole moment (qCEDM) diagrams, and quark-antiquark-gluon-gluon four-point functions computed at one loop in perturbation theory using the method of regions with the inverse flow time 1/t as the hard scale. The calculation was performed for generic gauge parameters xi and alpha_0, with gauge-parameter dependence cancelling in the final results, and multiple independent implementations were compared for consistency. The operator basis followed the 't Hooft-Veltman scheme definitions of Cirigliano (2020), adapted to Euclidean conventions, and included physical operators, nuisance operators (class-IIa vanishing by equations of motion and class-IIb BRST-exact operators), and evanescent operators.

## Key numbers
- Power-divergent matching coefficient to the QCD theta term: C_theta = - (9 alpha_s C_A)/(16 pi) * (1/t)
- Self-matching coefficient for the CP-odd three-gluon operator in the HV scheme: C_tildeG = 1 + (alpha_s C_A)/(12 pi) + (3 alpha_s C_A log(8 pi mu^2 t))/(2 pi)
- Self-matching coefficient in the 4D scheme: barC_tildeG = 1 + (3 alpha_s C_A log(8 pi mu^2 t))/(2 pi), with no finite one-loop contribution at matching scale mu = (8 pi t)^{-1/2}
- Matching coefficient to the quark chromo-EDM operator in HV scheme: C_CE = (31 i alpha_s C_A)/(192 pi) + (3 i alpha_s C_A log(8 pi mu^2 t))/(32 pi)
- Matching coefficient to the total-derivative operator in HV scheme: C_pG = - (179 alpha_s C_A)/(96 pi)
- Matching coefficient to the QCD theta term vanishes: C_BoxTheta = 0

## Context
This work completes the one-loop gradient-flow matching program for the CP-violating and flavor-conserving sector of the low-energy effective field theory (LEFT) relevant for the neutron electric dipole moment, extending previous one-loop calculations by Mereghetti (2021) and Buhler (2023) for other operators. The results enable lattice QCD computations of hadronic matrix elements of flowed operators to be converted into the MS scheme used in effective field theories, with the target of 10-25% uncertainty on hadronic matrix elements as motivated by Alarcon (2022) and experimental prospects for improved neutron EDM sensitivities. The perturbative uncertainty motivates future extension to two-loop matching, as discussed by Artz (2019) and Harlander (2022).
