# A novel interpretation of the "$Θ^{+}(1540)$ pentaquark" peak

> We use a theoretical model of the $γ~d \to ~K^+ K^- ~n ~p $ reaction adapted to the experiment done at LEPS where a peak was observed and associated to the $Θ^{+}(1540)$ pentaquark. The study shows that the method used in the experiment to associate momenta to the undetected proton and neutron, together with the chosen cuts, necessarily creates an artificial broad peak in the assumed $K^+ n$ invariant mass in the region of the claimed $Θ^{+}(1540)$, such that the remaining strength seen for the experimental peak is compatible with a fluctuation of 2$σ$ significance.

# A novel interpretation of the "Θ⁺(1540) pentaquark" peak

## Key claim

The peak observed in the K⁺n invariant mass spectrum around 1526 MeV in the LEPS γ d → K⁺ K⁻ n p experiment is an artifact of the minimum momentum spectator approximation (MMSA) used to assign momenta to undetected nucleons, combined with the specific kinematic cuts; the remaining excess above the calculated background is compatible with a 2σ statistical fluctuation, not a genuine pentaquark resonance.

## Method

The authors constructed a theoretical model of the γ d → K⁺ K⁻ n p reaction including φ meson production on both proton and neutron, Λ(1520) production on the proton, and kaon rescattering. They simulated the LEPS experimental setup by generating events at twenty random photon energies between 2 GeV and 2.4 GeV, imposing the forward spectrometer angular cut (kaon angles ≤ 20° relative to the photon), the MMSA prescription for nucleon momenta, and the LEPS cuts on K⁺K⁻ invariant mass and effective photon energy. The resulting K⁺n invariant mass distributions were compared to the LEPS data and to distributions computed using the true momenta from the Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- The LEPS peak was observed at approximately 1526 MeV in the K⁺n invariant mass distribution.
- The MMSA prescription combined with LEPS cuts produces an artificial broad peak in the K⁺n spectrum that can be fitted by two Gaussians: one peaking at 1520 MeV with a width of 80 MeV and another at 1660 MeV with a width of 185 MeV.
- The accumulated strength of the three bins constituting the "Θ⁺" peak above the calculated background is about 35 events.
- The calculated background below the "Θ⁺" peak is approximately 36 events per bin, compared to the 22 events per bin assumed in the LEPS best fit.
- The statistical significance of the "Θ⁺" peak relative to the calculated background is about 2σ, based on Monte Carlo runs producing about 2000 events each (matching the LEPS statistics).
- The K⁻p mass distribution shows a similar peak-dip structure around 1650 MeV with about 33-40 accumulated events, which the LEPS collaboration itself dismissed as a statistical fluctuation.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of the γ d → K⁺ K⁻ n p reaction from the authors' earlier model (Torres and Oset, 2010) and directly addresses the LEPS experiment (Nakano et al., 2009) that claimed evidence for the Θ⁺(1540) pentaquark. The study contradicts the interpretation of the LEPS collaboration, who reported a 5σ significance for the peak, by showing that the assumed background was too low. The results are consistent with the null result from the CLAS experiment (Kinnon et al., 2010) which used the same reaction with complete kinematics and ten times more statistics but found no peak. The paper enables a resolution of the long-standing pentaquark controversy by demonstrating that the LEPS peak can be fully explained as an artifact of the MMSA reconstruction method and statistical fluctuation, without invoking any exotic resonance.
