# Exotic Theta^+ baryon production induced by a photon and a pion

> We investigate the photoproduction of the $Θ^+(1540)$ on a nucleon ($γn \to K^- Θ^+$, $γp \to \bar{K}^0 Θ^+$) and the pion-induced $Θ^+$ production reaction on the proton ($π^- p \to K^- Θ^+$). The total cross sections near threshold are estimated by using hadronic models with effective interaction Lagrangians and form factors that preserve gauge-invariance of the electromagnetic current. The photoproduction cross sections are found to be a few hundred nb, with the cross section on the proton being larger than that on the neutron. The pion-induced production cross section is found to be around a few hundred $μ$b but sensitive to the $K^* N Θ$ coupling whose value is not yet known. We also study the production cross section assuming that the $Θ^+$ has negative parity. The cross sections are then found to be much suppressed compared to the case where $Θ^+$ has positive parity. Hence, the interpretation of the $Θ^+$ as an odd-parity pentaquark state seems to be disfavored from the estimates of cross section for the photon-proton reaction from the SAPHIR experiment.

# Exotic Theta^+ baryon production induced by a photon and a pion

## Key claim

The photoproduction and pion-induced production cross sections of the Theta^+(1540) baryon are estimated using hadronic models, and the results favor positive parity for the Theta^+ over negative parity; however, the cross sections depend sensitively on the unknown K* N Theta coupling constant and on the choice of form factors, so the conclusions are qualitative rather than definitive.

## Method

The authors constructed effective interaction Lagrangians for the Theta^+ baryon, nucleon, kaon, and K* meson, including electromagnetic couplings with gauge-invariant form factors. They calculated tree-level Feynman diagrams for the reactions gamma n -> K- Theta^+, gamma p -> anti-K^0 Theta^+, and pi- p -> K- Theta^+, using two different form factor prescriptions: a center-of-mass energy-dependent form factor from Liu and Ko (2003) and a covariant momentum-transfer-dependent form factor from kaon photoproduction analyses. The coupling constant g_KNTheta was fixed from a decay width of 5-10 MeV, while g_K*NTheta and the anomalous magnetic moment kappa_Theta were treated as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g_KNTheta is estimated as 2.2 (using Gamma_Theta->KN = 5 MeV from the chiral quark soliton model) or in the range 2.2-3.11 (for Gamma = 5-10 MeV).
- For positive-parity Theta^+ with the covariant form factor (Lambda = 1.8 GeV) and g_K*NTheta = +2.2, the gamma p -> anti-K^0 Theta^+ cross section is about 200 nb near threshold, consistent with the SAPHIR experiment's observation that the cross section is similar to phi photoproduction.
- For negative-parity Theta^+, the photoproduction cross sections are less than 20 nb with the covariant form factor (Lambda = 1.8 GeV), much suppressed compared to the positive-parity case.
- For pi- p -> K- Theta^+ with positive-parity Theta^+, the cross section reaches a few hundred microbarns depending on g_K*NTheta, but without K* exchange the peak is only about 25 microbarns with the covariant form factor.
- The K* -> K gamma coupling constants are g_K*Kgamma = 0.388 GeV^{-1} (neutral decay) and 0.254 GeV^{-1} (charged decay); the K* -> K pi coupling is g_K*Kpi = 3.28.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic model calculations of Liu and Ko (2003) and Nam, Hosaka, and Kim (2003) for Theta^+ photoproduction by including K* exchange contributions, tensor couplings, and a more consistent form factor treatment that preserves gauge invariance. The results contradict the earlier claim by Liu and Ko (2003) that the photon-neutron cross section is substantially larger than the photon-proton cross section; instead, the authors find the photon-proton cross section is generally larger. The study also addresses the parity of the Theta^+, which was debated between chiral quark soliton model predictions (positive parity) and some QCD sum rule and lattice QCD studies (negative parity). The finding that negative-parity Theta^+ production is suppressed disfavors the odd-parity interpretation based on comparison with SAPHIR experimental data. The pion-induced reaction results are relevant for the KEK experiment searching for Theta^+ and for future studies at the GSI pion beam facility.
