# Efficient emulator for solving three-nucleon continuum Faddeev equations with chiral three-nucleon force comprising any number of contact terms

> We demonstrate a computational scheme which drastically decreases the required time to get theoretical predictions based on chiral two- and three-nucleon forces for observables in three-nucleon continuum. For a three-nucleon force containing N short-range terms all workload is reduced to solving N+1 Faddeev-type integral equations. That done, computation of observables for any combination of strengths of the contact terms is done in a flash. We demonstrate on example of the elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering observables the high precision of the proposed emulator and its capability to reproduce exact results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new computational emulator that reduces the time to compute three-nucleon continuum observables for any combination of chiral three-nucleon force contact-term strengths to seconds, after solving only N+1 Faddeev-type integral equations once. The emulator reproduces exact results with approximately 1-2% precision, though it is slightly less precise than the earlier perturbative approach it improves upon.

## Method

The authors split the three-nucleon force into a parameter-free part V(θ₀) and a sum of N contact terms c_i ΔV_i. They first solve the full Faddeev equation exactly for the parameter-free term alone (θ₀), then solve N additional integral equations for each contact term individually (ΔT_i) within a restricted set of partial-wave channels β (all two-nucleon states with total angular momentum j ≤ 2). For any desired combination of strengths c_i, the full T-matrix is obtained by trivial linear summation of the precomputed solutions, and observables are computed from these sums.

## Key numbers

* Reduction in computer time by a factor of approximately 50 compared to the earlier perturbative approach of Witała et al. (2021) for N=2 contact terms.
* Computation of all elastic scattering observables for a particular set of strengths requires approximately 10 seconds on a personal computer, versus approximately 8 minutes in the perturbative approach.
* The emulator reproduces exact results with approximately 1-2% deviation for 51 nonvanishing nd elastic scattering observables at both E=70 MeV and E=190 MeV, tested over six sets of (c_D,c_E) strengths.
* The χ² minimum in the (c_D,c_E) plane for nd elastic scattering cross section at E=70 MeV yields c_D = 2.910 ± 0.140 and c_E = 0.385 ± 0.015, obtained from approximately 5000 strength combinations.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approach of Witała et al. (2021) by introducing a more efficient linearization scheme that reduces the required number of Faddeev equation solves from many to exactly N+1, independent of the number of desired strength combinations. The method is demonstrated using the SMS N⁴LO⁺ chiral NN potential of the Bochum group (Reinert et al., 2018) combined with the chiral N²LO three-nucleon force, and enables rapid fine-tuning of three-nucleon Hamiltonian parameters against available three-nucleon scattering data.
