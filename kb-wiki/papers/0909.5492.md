# Gamow shell-model calculations of drip-line oxygen isotopes

> We employ the Gamow shell model (GSM) to describe low-lying states of the oxygen isotopes 24O and 25O. The many-body Schrodinger equation is solved starting from a two-body Hamiltonian defined by a renormalized low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, and a spherical Berggren basis. The Berggren basis treats bound, resonant, and continuum states on an equal footing, and is therefore an appropriate representation of loosely bound and unbound nuclear states near threshold. We show that such a basis is necessary in order to obtain a detailed and correct description of the low-lying 1+ and 2+ excited states in 24O. On the other hand, we find that a correct description of binding energy systematics of the ground states is driven by proper treatment and inclusion of many-body correlation effects. This is supported by the fact that we get 25O unstable with respect to 24O in both oscillator and Berggren representations starting from a 22O core. Furthermore, we show that the structure of these loosely bound or unbound isotopes are strongly influenced by the 1S0 component of the NN interaction. This has important consequences for our understanding of nuclear stability.

# Gamow shell-model calculations of drip-line oxygen isotopes

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model (GSM) with a Berggren basis is necessary to correctly describe the low-lying 1+ and 2+ excited states in 24O, but a correct description of binding energy systematics of the ground states is driven by proper treatment and inclusion of many-body correlation effects rather than continuum coupling alone.

## Method

The authors solved the many-body Schrödinger equation using the Gamow shell model with a spherical Berggren basis that treats bound, resonant, and continuum states on an equal footing, starting from a 22O closed-shell core. They constructed the effective shell-model interaction using many-body perturbation theory through second order, employing a renormalized low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction derived from chiral effective field theory at N3LO with a 500 MeV cutoff, and used a Woods-Saxon potential to generate the single-particle Berggren basis.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical spacing between the 1+ and 2+ states in 24O is 540 keV, compared to the experimental value of 600 keV.
- The calculated widths for the 1+ and 2+ resonant states in 24O are 0.44 MeV and 0.26 MeV, respectively; experimentally, Hoffman (2008) reported widths of 0.03+0.12-0.03 MeV for the 1+ state and 0.05+0.21-0.05 MeV for the 2+ state.
- The ground state of 25O is calculated at E = 2.1 MeV with width Gamma = 0.5 MeV, compared to experimental values of E = 0.77 MeV and Gamma = 0.17 MeV.
- Single-particle energies in the Woods-Saxon Gamow basis (in MeV): epsilon_nu0d5/2 = -6.53 (-6.85 experimental), epsilon_nu1s1/2 = -2.73 (-2.74 experimental), epsilon_nu0d3/2 = 1.43 - 0.01i (1.26 - 0.2i experimental).
- The 1+ - 2+ splitting increases to 530 keV when the 1S0 partial wave strength is at its full value (alpha = 1), close to experiment.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of oxygen isotopes that used harmonic oscillator bases and fitted interactions, such as those by Brown (2005, 2007) and Coraggio (2007), which produced too compressed spectra in 22O and incorrectly predicted all isotopes 17-28O as bound. The results complement coupled-cluster calculations by Hagen et al. (2007) showing that chiral NN interactions produce a flat binding energy curve for 24-28O, and shell-model calculations by Otsuka (2009) showing that effective three-nucleon forces add repulsion in heavier oxygen isotopes. The finding that continuum coupling is not the dominant mechanism for the drip line position, but is crucial for excited state properties, enables future work to disentangle many-body correlation effects from continuum effects in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line.
