# Three-neutron resonance trajectories for realistic interaction models

> Three-neutron resonances are investigated using realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models. The resonance pole trajectories are explored by first adding an additional interaction to artificially bind the three-neutron system and then gradually removing it. The pole positions for the three-neutron states up to J=5/2 are localized in the third energy quadrant-Im (E)<=0, Re (E)<=0-well before the additional interaction is removed. Our study shows that realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models exclude any possible experimental signature of three-neutron resonances.

## Key claim

Realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models exclude any possible experimental signature of three-neutron resonances, as all resonance poles for states up to J=5/2 move into the third energy quadrant (Re(E) ≤ 0, Im(E) ≤ 0) before the artificial binding interaction is fully removed, meaning they become subthreshold and physically unobservable.

## Method

The authors solved the three-neutron Faddeev equations in configuration space using realistic NN interaction models (AV14, AV18, Nijm II, and Reid 93). They employed two complementary methods to trace resonance pole trajectories: complex scaling (CS) and analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC). To artificially bind the three-neutron system, they either enhanced specific NN partial-wave interactions (primarily the ³PF₂ channel) or added a phenomenological Yukawa-type three-body force, then gradually reduced the enhancement to follow the pole trajectories into the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- Critical enhancement factor γ_c required to bind the J^π = 3/2⁻ three-neutron state via ³PF₂ channel enhancement: 3.74 for Reid 93, 3.61 for Nijm II, 3.86 for AV14, 3.98 for AV18
- Corresponding dineutron resonance positions at these γ_c values: 5.41-2.52i MeV (Reid 93), 5.31-2.41i MeV (Nijm II), 5.20-2.49i MeV (AV14), 4.83-2.31i MeV (AV18)
- Critical Yukawa 3NF strengths W₀ (in MeV·fm) to bind three-neutron in various states: 307 (1/2⁺), 1062 (3/2⁺), 809 (5/2⁺), 515 (1/2⁻), 413 (3/2⁻), 629 (5/2⁻)
- W' values (strength at which resonances become subthreshold): 152 (1/2⁺), 329 (5/2⁺), 118 (1/2⁻), 146 (3/2⁻), 277 (5/2⁻)
- Dineutron ³P₀ resonance at physical γ=1 for Reid 93: -14.2-18.5i MeV
- Dineutron ³PF₂ resonance at physical γ=1 for Reid 93: -15.9-39.9i MeV

## Context

This work extends the studies of Gloeckle and Witala (1990s) and Gloeckle and Hem (1990s), who investigated three-neutron resonances using simplified interactions or rank-2 separable potentials, but could not fully treat realistic NN forces due to numerical instabilities. The authors complement those earlier efforts by using four realistic local NN interaction models (AV14, AV18, Nijm II, Reid 93) and exploring all three-neutron states up to J=5/2. The results definitively exclude observable three-neutron resonances, contradicting earlier suggestions from experiments such as the double charge exchange studies that proposed broad resonances at E=2-6i MeV or E≈20-20i MeV. This work enables closure on the long-standing question of three-neutron resonant states and provides benchmark trajectories for testing future theoretical approaches.
