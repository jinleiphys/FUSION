# Model-dependence of the $γZ$ dispersion correction to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic $ep$ scattering

> We analyze the dispersion correction to elastic parity violating electron-proton scattering due to $γZ$ exchange. In particular, we explore the theoretical uncertainties associated with modeling contributions of hadronic intermediate states. Taking into account constraints from low- and high-energy, parity-conserving electroproduction measurements, choosing different models for contributions from the non-resonant processes, and performing the corresponding flavor rotations to obtain the electroweak amplitude, we arrive at an estimate of the uncertainty in the total contribution to the parity-violating asymmetry. At the kinematics of the Q-Weak experiment, we obtain a correction to the asymmetry equivalent to a shift in the proton weak charge of $(0.0054\pm0.0020)$. This should be compared to the value of the proton's weak charge of $\qwp=0.0713\pm0.0008$ that includes SM contributions at tree level and one-loop radiative corrections. Therefore, we obtain a new Standard Model prediction for the parity-violating asymmetry in the kinematics of the Q-Weak experiment of $(0.0767\pm0.0008\pm0.0020_{γZ})$. The latter error leads to a relative uncertainty of 2.8% in the determination of the proton's weak charge, and is dominated by the uncertainty in the isospin structure of the inclusive cross section. We argue that future parity-violating inelastic $ep$ asymmetry measurements at low-to-moderate $Q^2$ and $W^2$ could be exploited to reduce the uncertainty associated with the dispersion correction. Because the corresponding shift and error bar decrease monotonically with decreasing beam energy, a determination of the proton's weak charge with a lower-energy experiment or measurements of "isotope ratios" in atomic parity-violation could provide a useful cross check on any implications for physics beyond the Standard Model derived from the Q-Weak measurement.

## Key claim
The $\gamma Z$ dispersion correction to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic $ep$ scattering is model-dependent, and the associated theoretical uncertainty is larger (by roughly a factor of two) than estimated in some prior works, primarily due to the uncertainty in the isospin structure of the inclusive cross section.

## Method
The authors used a forward dispersion relation to relate the real part of the $\gamma Z$ box diagram to an integral over the imaginary part, which is expressed in terms of $\gamma Z$ interference structure functions $F^{\gamma Z}_{1,2,3}$. To obtain these structure functions, they performed an isospin rotation of parity-conserving electroproduction data from low- and high-energy measurements, using different models for the non-resonant contributions and the flavor rotation to the electroweak amplitude. The resulting uncertainty was estimated by comparing the outcomes of these different modeling choices.

## Key numbers
- The dispersion correction to the asymmetry is equivalent to a shift in the proton weak charge of $(0.0054 \pm 0.0020)$ at the kinematics of the Q-Weak experiment.
- The Standard Model prediction for the parity-violating asymmetry at Q-Weak kinematics is $(0.0767 \pm 0.0008 \pm 0.0020_{\gamma Z})$.
- The error from the $\gamma Z$ correction leads to a relative uncertainty of 2.8% in the determination of the proton's weak charge.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the $\gamma Z$ box correction first highlighted by Gorchtein and Horowitz (2010) and subsequently studied by Sibirtsev et al. (2010) and Carlson et al. (2011). While all recent results are consistent within their quoted error bars, this study obtains a larger uncertainty by roughly a factor of two by more thoroughly accounting for hadronic model-dependence. The authors argue that future parity-violating inelastic $ep$ asymmetry measurements at low-to-moderate $Q^2$ and $W^2$ could be exploited to reduce this uncertainty, and that a lower-energy experiment or atomic parity-violation measurements could provide a useful cross check.
