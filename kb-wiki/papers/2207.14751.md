# Bayesian quantification of the Quark-Gluon Plasma: Improved design and closure demonstration

> We present a demonstration of the design sampling and closure for the first comprehensive Bayesian model-to-data comparison of heavy-ion measurements with IP-Glasma initial conditions, in which we combine with state-of-the-art hydrodynamics (MUSIC), particlization (iS3D), and transport (SMASH). We further introduce a systematically-improvable method of sampling design points with better projection properties to explore the parameter space of the model for the first time in heavy-ion collisions.

# Bayesian quantification of the Quark-Gluon Plasma: Improved design and closure demonstration

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that an ordered Maximum Projection Latin hypercube design enables reliable intermediate Bayesian analyses with partial design subsets, whereas the commonly used maximin Latin hypercube produces centrally-clustered subsets that are unreliable until the full design is complete. This claim is conditional on the specific hybrid model combining IP-Glasma initial conditions with MUSIC hydrodynamics, iS3D particlization, and SMASH transport.

## Method

The authors constructed a Bayesian model-to-data comparison framework using IP-Glasma initial conditions, (2+1)D MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics, iS3D 14-moment viscous corrections for particlization, and SMASH hadronic afterburner. They introduced an ordered Maximum Projection Latin hypercube design for sampling the 11-dimensional parameter space, which minimizes the Maximum Projection criterion to ensure coverage of all lower-dimensional projections. They performed closure (self-consistency) tests by training Gaussian process surrogate models on partial and full design subsets and recovering known input parameters from pseudodata.

## Key numbers

- 11-dimensional parameter space was sampled with 350 design points using the Maximum Projection Latin hypercube design
- The viscous posterior for both shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) and bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio (ζ/s) became more constrained around the underlying truth when increasing from the first 100 design points (partial design) to the full 350-point design
- The collision evolution occurs in approximately 10 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion collisions that used maximin Latin hypercube designs, as in the studies by the SIMSPR collaboration (2016, 2019). The authors introduce a Maximum Projection design method, originally developed by Joseph, Gul, and Ba (2015), to the heavy-ion community for the first time. This enables reliable intermediate analyses before completing the full computationally expensive design, conserving resources and allowing earlier physics output. The results establish the foundation for a forthcoming complete Bayesian analysis of the Quark-Gluon Plasma with IP-Glasma initial conditions.
