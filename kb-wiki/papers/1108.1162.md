# Symmetries of the Similarity Renormalization Group for Nuclear Forces

> We analyze the role played by Long Distance Symmetries within the context of the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) approach, which is based on phase-shift preserving continuous unitary transformations that evolve hamiltonians with a cutoff on energy differences. We find that there is a SRG cutoff for which almost perfect fulfillment of Wigner symmetry is found. We discuss the possible consequences of such finding.

## Key claim

The authors find that for the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) cutoff of about 600 MeV (3 fm\(^{-1}\)), an extremely accurate fulfillment of Wigner SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry emerges in the evolved nucleon-nucleon potential, even though the initial high-quality potentials were not designed to implement this symmetry.

## Method

The authors solve Wegner's SRG flow equation for the Argonne AV18 potential using a discretized momentum-space basis with 200 Gaussian mesh points and an ultraviolet cutoff at 30 fm\(^{-1}\), evolving the potential with the kinetic energy as the SRG generator. They project the evolved potentials onto partial waves and construct linear combinations of triplet partial-wave matrix elements to extract central, tensor, and spin-orbit components, then compare singlet and triplet central combinations to test Wigner symmetry. The evolution is performed for several SRG cutoff values ranging from 5 fm\(^{-1}\) down to 1 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

* The SRG cutoff for best Wigner symmetry fulfillment in S-waves is \(\lambda_{\rm Wigner} = 3~{\rm fm}^{-1}\) (approximately 600 MeV), where the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) diagonal and fully off-diagonal matrix elements become practically identical.
* The deuteron bound-state pole lies at an imaginary momentum scale \(p_d \sim i~0.23~{\rm fm}^{-1}\), while the \(^1S_0\) virtual bound-state pole lies at \(p_v \sim i~0.04~{\rm fm}^{-1}\).
* For D-waves, Wigner symmetry is well fulfilled for momenta \(p',p < 0.7~{\rm fm}^{-1}\) at a similarity cutoff of \(2~{\rm fm}^{-1}\).
* For G-waves, better fulfillment of Wigner symmetry occurs at a similarity cutoff of \(4~{\rm fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of long-distance symmetries previously studied in the momentum-space \(V_{\rm low k}\) approach by Calle Cordon, Ruiz Arriola, and collaborators (2008, 2009), where Wigner SU(4) symmetry was found to emerge in effective interactions at low momenta. The paper tests whether the SRG approach, which is an alternative renormalization method based on continuous unitary transformations, also displays the same symmetry pattern. The finding that Wigner symmetry is nearly perfectly realized at a specific SRG cutoff suggests the possibility of constructing nuclear interactions based explicitly on this symmetry, which could have implications for nuclear structure and reaction calculations.
