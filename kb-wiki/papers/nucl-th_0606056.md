# Strangeness s = -3 dibaryons in a chiral quark model

> The structures of $NΩ_{(2,1/2)}$ and $ΔΩ_{(3,3/2)}$ with strangeness $s=-3$ are dynamically studied in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model by solving a resonating group method (RGM) equation. The first model parameters are taken from our previous work, which gave a satisfactory description of the energies of the baryon ground states, the binding energy of the deuteron, the nucleon-nucleon(NN) scattering phase shifts, and the hyperon-nucleon (YN) cross sections. The effect from the vector meson fields is very similar to that from the one-gluon exchange interaction, both in the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, the $NΩ_{(2,1/2)}$ and $ΔΩ_{(3,3/2)}$ systems are wealy bound states. The second model parameters are also taken from our previous work by fitting the KN scattering process. when the mixing of scalar mesons are considered, the $NΩ_{(2,1/2)}$ and $ΔΩ_{(3,3/2)}$ systems change into unbound state.

# Strangeness s = -3 dibaryons in a chiral quark model

## Key claim
The NΩ(2,1/2) and ΔΩ(3,3/2) systems are weakly bound states in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model when parameters are fitted to NN scattering, but become unbound when scalar meson mixing is included and parameters are fitted to KN scattering instead.

## Method
The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for the NΩ and ΔΩ two-baryon systems using two variants of a chiral SU(3) quark model: the original model with one-gluon exchange (OGE) and pseudoscalar/scalar meson exchanges, and an extended model where vector meson exchanges largely replace OGE. Model parameters were taken from previous fits to baryon ground-state energies, deuteron binding, NN phase shifts (first set), or from fits to KN scattering with scalar meson mixing (second set). The relative wave function was expanded in Gaussian basis functions to solve the bound-state RGM equation.

## Key numbers
- In the chiral SU(3) quark model (NN-fit parameters): NΩ binding energy = 3.0 MeV, RMS radius = 1.2 fm; ΔΩ binding energy = 0.6 MeV, RMS radius = 1.3 fm.
- In the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, set I (no tensor coupling, g_chv = 2.351): NΩ binding energy = 20.4 MeV, RMS = 0.9 fm; ΔΩ binding energy = 25.6 MeV, RMS = 0.9 fm.
- In the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, set II (tensor coupling f_chv/g_chv = 2/3, g_chv = 1.973): NΩ binding energy = 12.1 MeV, RMS = 1.0 fm; ΔΩ binding energy = 14.4 MeV, RMS = 0.9 fm.
- The σ meson mass used: 595 MeV (chiral SU(3) model), 535 MeV (extended set I), 547 MeV (extended set II).
- The OGE coupling constants g_u^2 drop from 0.766 (chiral SU(3)) to 0.056 (extended set I) and 0.132 (extended set II).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of dibaryons in the chiral SU(3) quark model (Dai et al., 2003) and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model (Dai et al., 2004) to the strangeness s = -3 sector. It contradicts the prediction of deeply bound NΩ and ΔΩ states by Wang et al. (2005). The results show that the short-range interaction mechanism (OGE versus vector meson exchange) does not qualitatively change the binding character, but the treatment of scalar meson mixing (as required for KN scattering fits) can turn these weakly bound states into unbound ones. This enables future experimental searches for NΩ bound states to discriminate between different parameterizations of the quark-quark interaction.
