# The Flavor and Spin Structure of Hyperons from Quark Fragmentation

> We systematically study the hadron longitudinal polarizations of the octet baryons at large $z$ from quark fragmentations in $e^+e^-$-annihilation, polarized charged lepton deep inelastic scattering (DIS) process, and neutrino (antineutrino) DIS process, based on predictions of quark distributions for the octet baryons in the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model and a perturbative QCD based counting rule analysis. We show that the $e^+e^-$-annihilation and polarized charged lepton DIS process are able to distinguish between the two different predictions of the hyperon polarizations. We also find that the neutrino/antineutrino DIS process is ideal in order to study both the valence content of the hyperons and the antiquark to hyperon (quark to anti-hyperon) fragmentations, which might be related to the sea content of hyperons.

## Key claim

The paper systematically predicts the longitudinal polarizations of all octet baryons (not just the Lambda) produced in three different fragmentation processes, using two distinct theoretical models for the quark distributions inside hyperons. The authors show that while the two models give similar predictions for Lambda and Xi polarizations, they produce qualitatively different predictions for Sigma polarizations in e+e- annihilation, which could be experimentally distinguished with available techniques.

## Method

The authors calculate quark distributions for all octet baryons using two approaches: a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model with Wigner-Melosh rotation effects, and a perturbative QCD counting rule analysis with leading-order valence quark distributions constrained by SU(3) symmetry. They then connect these quark distributions to fragmentation functions via the Gribov-Lipatov relation D_q^h(z) ~ z q_h(z), which is treated as a phenomenological Ansatz valid near z -> 1, and compute the resulting hadron polarizations in e+e- annihilation at LEP I (91 GeV) and LEP II (200 GeV), polarized charged lepton DIS, and neutrino/antineutrino DIS processes.

## Key numbers

- At LEP I energy (sqrt(s) = 91.187 GeV), the initial quark polarizations are P_q = -0.67 for u and c quarks, and P_q = -0.94 for d, s, and b quarks
- At LEP II energy (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV), the initial quark polarizations become P_q = -0.29 for u and c quarks, and P_q = -0.71 for d, s, and b quarks
- The ratio r_{d/u} = C_d/C_u is 1.29 at the Z-pole (LEP I) and 0.61 at LEP II
- In the pQCD counting rule analysis, the ratio u(x)/s(x) approaches 1/2 as x -> 1 for the Lambda, while in the quark-diquark model it approaches 0
- For the nucleon, the pQCD analysis predicts d(x)/u(x) = 1/5 as x -> 1, while the quark-diquark model predicts F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x) = 1/4
- The pQCD analysis predicts Delta q/q = 1 as x -> 1 for any quark flavor, while the quark-diquark model predicts Delta d(x)/d(x) = -1/3 as x -> 1 for the nucleon

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Lambda hyperon polarizations (Ma 1999, Burkardt 1993) to all octet baryons, building on the authors' earlier predictions for quark distributions in hyperons (Ma, Schmidt, Yang 2000) and Drell-Yan processes. The paper enables experimental tests of different models of hyperon spin structure by measuring Sigma polarizations in e+e- annihilation, which the authors argue can be done with existing experimental techniques at facilities like ALEPH, DELPHI, and OPAL. The results also provide predictions for Xi polarizations that could be tested at HERMES, E665, and NOMAD experiments.
