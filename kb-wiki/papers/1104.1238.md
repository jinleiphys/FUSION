# PACIAE 2.0: An updated parton and hadron cascade model (program) for the relativistic nuclear collisions

> We have updated the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE for the relativistic nuclear collisions, from based on JETSET 6.4 and PYTHIA 5.7 to based on PYTHIA 6.4, and renamed as PACIAE 2.0. The main physics concerning the stages of the parton initiation, parton rescattering, hadronization, and hadron rescattering were discussed. The structures of the programs were briefly explained. In addition, some calculated examples were compared with the experimental data. It turns out that this model (program) works well.

## Key claim

The updated PACIAE 2.0 model, now based on PYTHIA 6.4 instead of PYTHIA 5.7 and JETSET 7.4, reproduces experimental data for relativistic nuclear collisions, but the authors note that the connection with PYTHIA 8.1 is left for the future because PYTHIA 8.1 "does not yet in every respect replace the old code" and the physical differences are not important for nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Method

The PACIAE 2.0 model simulates relativistic nuclear collisions through four sequential stages: parton initiation (using PYTHIA 6.4 with string fragmentation switched off and diquarks broken into quarks), parton rescattering via 2-to-2 LO-pQCD parton-parton cross sections, hadronization via a Monte Carlo coalescence model, and hadron rescattering using parametrized hadron-hadron cross sections including approximately 600 inelastic channels. The model introduces a mechanism to increase effective string tension and reduce strange quark suppression, and includes deexcitation of energetic quarks in the coalescence model via iterative quark-antiquark pair generation.

## Key numbers

- Running 1000 events for inelastic pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV by PACIAE 2.0a to reproduce PHOBOS data of rapidity density at mid-rapidity, dNch/dy=2.25(+0.37/-0.30), spends approximately 3 minutes.
- Running 0-6% most central Au+Au collision at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV by PACIAE 2.0b and PACIAE 2.0c to reproduce PHOBOS data of charged multiplicity of 5060 spends approximately 13 seconds/event and approximately 265 seconds/event, respectively.
- Assumed constant total cross sections: sigma_tot^NN=40 mb (RHIC energy region) or 76 mb (LHC energy region), sigma_tot^piN=25 mb, sigma_tot^kN=21 mb, sigma_tot^pipi=10 mb, with inelastic to total cross section ratio of 0.85.
- Normal nuclear density assumed as rho_0=0.16 fm^{-3}.
- Parameters for effective string tension increase: a=3.5 GeV and s_0=0.8 GeV, determined by fitting hadron-hadron collision data.

## Context

This work extends the earlier PACIAE model (Sa, 2009) by updating the underlying event generator from PYTHIA 5.7/JETSET 7.4 to PYTHIA 6.4, which includes new physics and improvements. The model belongs to a class of parton and hadron cascade models that includes AMPT (string melting) (Ko, 2005), BAMPS (Zheng, 2005), PHSD (Cassing, 2009), and MARTINI (Schenke, 2009). The updated model enables studies of limiting fragmentation, fluctuations and correlations, direct photon production, QCD matter properties at RHIC and LHC energies, and charged particle elliptic flow in pp collisions at LHC energies.
