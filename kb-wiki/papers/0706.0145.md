# Cluster structures in Oxygen isotopes

> Cluster structure of 16O,18O and 20O is investigated by the antisymmettrized molecular dynamics (AMD) plus generator coordinate method (GCM). We have found the K^π=0$_2^+$ and 0$_1^-$ rotational bands of 18O that have the prominent 14C+αcluster structure. Clustering systematics becomes richer in 20O. We suggest the K^π=0$_2^+$ band that is the mixture of the 12C+α+4n and 14C+6He cluster structures, and the K^π=0$_1^-$ band that has the 14C+6He cluster structure. The K^π=0$_3^+$ and 0$_2^-$ bands that have the prominent 16C+αcluster structure are also found.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have identified cluster structures in \(^{18}\)O and \(^{20}\)O using the AMD+GCM framework without assuming any pre-existing clusters, but they note that the calculated excitation energies of the cluster bands overestimate experimental values, and agreement with experiment is only achieved when energies are measured from the theoretical cluster threshold rather than absolute excitation energies.

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with a generator coordinate method (GCM) to calculate the structure of \(^{16}\)O, \(^{18}\)O, and \(^{20}\)O. They performed energy variation under a constraint on the matter quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta\), then projected the optimized wave functions onto good parity and total angular momentum, and finally superposed the projected states to diagonalize the Hamiltonian using the Hill-Wheeler equation. The effective nuclear force used was the Modified Volkov force (MV1 case 3) for the central part and the G3RS force for the spin-orbit part.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical \(^{12}\)C+\(\alpha\) threshold energy for \(^{16}\)O is 17.1 MeV, evaluated from the calculated energies of \(^{12}\)C (\(-83.5\) MeV) and \(^{4}\)He (\(-26.7\) MeV) without projections.
- The theoretical \(^{14}\)C+\(\alpha\) threshold energy for \(^{18}\)O is 10.3 MeV (calculated \(^{14}\)C energy is \(-100.5\) MeV), while the experimental value is 6.24 MeV.
- For \(^{18}\)O, the energies of the \(K^{\pi}=0_2^+\) band members measured from the theoretical \(^{14}\)C+\(\alpha\) threshold are: \(0_2^+\) at \(-2.1\) MeV, \(2_3^+\) at \(-0.6\) MeV, \(4_2^+\) at 0.7 MeV, and \(6_1^+\) at 4.8 MeV; the experimental candidates are at \(-2.61\), \(-1.00\), 0.87, and 5.45 MeV from the experimental threshold.
- For \(^{18}\)O, the energies of the \(K^{\pi}=0_1^-\) band members measured from the theoretical \(^{14}\)C+\(\alpha\) threshold are: \(1_3^-\) at 2.5 MeV, \(3_2^-\) at 3.0 MeV, and \(5_2^-\) at 7.28 MeV; the experimental candidates are at 1.80, 3.43, and 5.38 MeV from the experimental threshold.
- The calculated binding energies are: \(^{16}\)O 127.3 MeV (experiment 127.62 MeV), \(^{18}\)O 137.5 MeV (experiment 139.81 MeV), and \(^{20}\)O 153.2 MeV (experiment 151.36 MeV).

## Context

This work extends AMD studies of cluster structures in neutron-rich nuclei, previously applied to Be isotopes (AMD studies by Kanada-En'yo and others, early 2000s) and \(^{22}\)Ne, to the oxygen isotope chain. It builds on earlier cluster model calculations for \(^{18}\)O (GCM calculations by Descouvemont and Baye, and OCM calculations) that predicted \(^{14}\)C+\(\alpha\) molecular bands, and on experimental \(\alpha\)-transfer and breakup reactions on \(^{14}\)C (Curtis and Ashwood, 2000s). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted \(K^{\pi}=0_2^+\) and \(0_1^-\) bands in \(^{20}\)O, particularly the \(^{16}\)C+\(\alpha\) parity doublet bands near the \(^{16}\)C+\(\alpha\) threshold, and suggest that valence neutron dynamics can produce molecular-orbital-like bands below the corresponding cluster threshold.
