# Wigner Function for Spin-1/2 Fermions in Electromagnetic Fields

> We study the Wigner function for massive spin-1/2 fermions in electromagnetic fields. Dirac form kinetic equation and Klein-Gordon form kinetic equation are obtained for the Wigner function, which are derived from the Dirac equation. The Wigner function and its kinetic equations are expanded in terms of the generators of Clifford algebra and a complicated system of partial differential equations is obtained. We prove that some component equations are automattically satisfied if the rest ones are fulfilled. In this thesis two methods are proposed for calculating the Wigner function, which are proved to be equivalent. The Wigner function is analytically calculated following the standard second-quantization procedure in the following cases: free fermions with or without spin imbalance, in constant magnetic field, in constant electric field, and in constant parallel electromagnetic field. Strong-field effects, such as the Landau levels and Schwinger pair-production are reproduced using the Wigner function approach. For an arbitrary space-time dependent field configuration, a semi-classical expansion with respect to the reduced Planck's constant $\hbar$ is performed. We derive general expressions for the Wigner function components at linear order in $\hbar$, in which order the spin corrections start playing a role. A generalized Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi (BMT) equation and a generalized Boltzmann equation are obtained for the undetermined polarization density and net fermion number density, which can be used to construct spin-hydrodynamics in the future. We also make a comparison between analytical results and the ones from semi-classical expansion, which shows coincidence for weak electromagnetic fields and small spin imbalance.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Wigner-function-based kinetic theory for massive spin-1/2 fermions in electromagnetic fields, showing that the semi-classical expansion to linear order in ℏ reproduces known strong-field effects (Landau levels, Schwinger pair-production) and yields a generalized BMT equation and Boltzmann equation, but only for weak electromagnetic fields and small spin imbalance where the semi-classical and analytical results coincide.

## Method

The covariant Wigner function (a 4×4 matrix in 8-dimensional phase space {x^μ, p^μ}) is constructed from the Dirac field operator, and kinetic equations are derived in both Dirac form (first-order differential operators) and Klein-Gordon form (second-order differential operators). The Wigner function and its kinetic equations are expanded in generators of the Clifford algebra, yielding a system of partial differential equations whose redundancy is exploited to reduce the independent degrees of freedom. For arbitrary space-time dependent fields, a semi-classical expansion in ℏ is performed to linear order, while for constant fields (free fermions, constant magnetic field, constant electric field, constant parallel electromagnetic fields) the Wigner function is computed analytically via second-quantization using orthonormal single-particle wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC with collision energy √s = 200 GeV per nucleon, the magnetic field can reach several m_π^2 ~ 10^18 G
- The total angular momentum is about 10^6 ℏ for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and impact parameter b = 10 fm, with about 10% left in the QGP
- The chiral magnetic effect current is given by J = (μ_5 / 2π^2) q B, where q is the electric charge
- For isobaric collisions of ⁴⁴₉₆Ru and ⁴⁰₉₆Zr, the magnetic field differs by 10% between the two species

## Context

This work extends the Wigner-function approach from massless chiral fermions (Son 2012, Stephanov 2012, Chen 2012, Hidaka 2016) to massive spin-1/2 fermions, recovering the Chiral Kinetic Theory in the massless limit and reproducing the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation and BMT equation in the classical limit. The results enable construction of spin-hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions, where the QGP exhibits chiral effects (CME, CSE) and Schwinger pair-production in strong electromagnetic fields, and provide a foundation for kinetic theory in strong background fields where the semi-classical expansion may require careful validation.
