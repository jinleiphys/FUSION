# Two-photon exchange in (muonic) deuterium at N3LO in pionless effective field theory

> We present a study of the two-photon-exchange ($2γ$-exchange) corrections to the $S$-levels in muonic ($μ$D) and ordinary (D) deuterium within the pionless effective field theory (pionless EFT). Our calculation proceeds up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in the pionless EFT expansion. The only unknown low-energy constant entering the calculation at this order corresponds to the coupling of a longitudinal photon to the nucleon-nucleon system. To minimise its correlation with the deuteron charge radius, it is extracted using the information about the hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift. We find the elastic $2γ$-exchange contribution in $μ$D larger by several standard deviations than obtained in other recent calculations. This discrepancy ameliorates the mismatch between theory and experiment on the size of $2γ$-exchange effects, and is attributed to the properties of the deuteron elastic charge form factor parametrisation used to evaluate the elastic contribution. We identify a correlation between the deuteron charge and Friar radii, which can help one to judge how well a form factor parametrisation describes the low-virtuality properties of the deuteron. We also evaluate the higher-order $2γ$-exchange contributions in $μ$D, generated by the single-nucleon structure and expected to be the most important terms beyond N3LO. The uncertainty of the theoretical result is dominated by the truncation of the pionless EFT series and is quantified using a Bayesian approach. The resulting extractions of the deuteron charge radius from the $μ$D Lamb shift, the $2S-1S$ transition in D, and the $2S-1S$ hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift, with the respective $2γ$-exchange effects evaluated in a unified pionless EFT approach, are in perfect agreement.

## Key claim

The two-photon-exchange contribution in muonic deuterium is found to be larger by several standard deviations than obtained in other recent calculations, which ameliorates the mismatch between theory and experiment; this discrepancy is attributed to the parametrisation of the deuteron elastic charge form factor used to evaluate the elastic contribution, not to the underlying nuclear physics.

## Method

The two-photon-exchange corrections to the S-levels in muonic deuterium and ordinary deuterium were calculated within pionless effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). The only unknown low-energy constant at this order, corresponding to the coupling of a longitudinal photon to the nucleon-nucleon system, was extracted using the hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift to minimise its correlation with the deuteron charge radius. The theoretical uncertainty was quantified using a Bayesian approach based on the truncation of the pionless EFT series.

## Key numbers

- Elastic two-photon-exchange contribution to the 2S level in muonic deuterium: -0.4463(77) meV (pionless EFT N3LO)
- Elastic two-photon-exchange contribution using chiral EFT N4LO deuteron charge form factor: -0.4456(18) meV
- Elastic two-photon-exchange contribution using empirical form factor parametrisation of Abbott et al. (2000): -0.417(2) meV
- Elastic two-photon-exchange contribution using Sick and Trautmann form factor parametrisation: -0.4509 meV
- Low-energy constant l1^{C0_S} extracted from isotope shift: -1.80(38) x 10^{-3}
- Deuteron charge radius from muonic deuterium: 2.12562(78) fm (CREMA)
- Deuteron charge radius from hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift and muonic hydrogen: 2.12771(22) fm
- Deuteron charge radius from CODATA 2018: 2.12799(74) fm

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory framework previously applied to deuteron Compton scattering and electromagnetic form factors (Chen 1999, Lensky 2021) to the two-photon-exchange corrections in muonic and ordinary deuterium. It addresses the "deuteron radius puzzle" by providing a model-independent evaluation that resolves the tension between the muonic deuterium extraction and the combined hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift extraction. The results enable consistent extractions of the deuteron charge radius from muonic deuterium Lamb shift, the 2S-1S transition in deuterium, and the hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift using a unified theoretical approach, and identify a correlation between the deuteron charge and Friar radii that can judge the quality of form factor parametrisations at low virtuality.
