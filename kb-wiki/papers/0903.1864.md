# Flavor Superconductivity from Gauge/Gravity Duality

> We give a detailed account and extensions of a holographic flavor superconductivity model which we have proposed recently. The model has an explicit field theory realization as strongly coupled N=2 Super Yang-Mills theory with fundamental matter at finite temperature and finite isospin chemical potential. Using gauge/gravity duality, i.e. a probe of two flavor D7-branes in the AdS black hole background, we show that the system undergoes a second order phase transition with critical exponent 1/2. The new ground state may be interpreted as a rho meson superfluid. It shows signatures known from superconductivity, such as an infinite dc conductivity and a gap in the frequency-dependent conductivity. We present a stringy picture of the condensation mechanism in terms of a recombination of strings. We give a detailed account of the evaluation of the non-Abelian Dirac-Born-Infeld action involved using two different methods. Finally we also consider the case of massive flavors and discuss the holographic Meissner-Ochsenfeld effect in our scenario.

## Key claim

Using gauge/gravity duality with a probe of two flavor D7-branes in the AdS black hole background, the system undergoes a second order phase transition with critical exponent 1/2, and the new ground state may be interpreted as a rho meson superfluid showing signatures known from superconductivity such as infinite dc conductivity and a gap in the frequency-dependent conductivity.

## Method

The authors model strongly coupled N=2 Super Yang-Mills theory with fundamental matter at finite temperature and finite isospin chemical potential by introducing a probe of two flavor D7-branes in the AdS black hole background. They evaluate the non-Abelian Dirac-Born-Infeld action using two different methods: an expansion to fourth order in the field strength and a new adapted symmetrized trace prescription. The equations of motion for the gauge field fluctuations are derived and solved to compute the frequency-dependent conductivity and spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent for the second order phase transition is 1/2.
- The superconducting density n_s vanishes linearly near the critical temperature, with n_s proportional to (1 - T/T_c) for T approximately equal to T_c.
- For massive quarks at fixed mu/M_q = 3, the real part of conductivity shows prominent peaks inside the gap at temperatures T/T_c = 0.6 (green curve) and T/T_c = 0.5 (orange curve).
- At higher quark mass parameter m = 2.842 and chemical potential mu_t = 3.483, the spectral function peaks approach the supersymmetric mass spectrum from above, with the first peak near omega approximately 15.

## Context

This work extends the holographic superconductor models previously studied in the gauge/gravity duality literature by providing an explicit field theory realization as N=2 Super Yang-Mills theory with fundamental matter. The authors compare their results to the supersymmetric mass formula obtained by Kruczenski (2003) and to earlier work on vector mesons by Erdmenger (2007). The model enables future construction of holographic s-wave superconductors by applying these methods to transverse scalars, and may be extended to find purely fermionic superconductors using the configuration discussed by Myers (2008).
