# Eta-meson light nucleus scattering and Charge Symmetry Breaking

> The η-meson collision with light nuclei, ^2H, ^3H, ^3He, and ^4He, is considered on the basis of a microscopic approach which utilises Faddeev type and Lippmann-Schwinger equations. The nuclear hamiltonian is approximated by the finite rank approximation which amounts to the coherent approximation. The η-nucleus scattering length and the resonance and bound--state poles of the amplitude describing elastic scattering of η-meson by these nuclei are obtained. For each of the nuclei considered, the minimal factor enhancing the ηN--attraction that moves the poles to the quasi--bound state area of the complex k--plane, is determined. It is shown that within the existing uncertainties in the elementary ηN iteraction all these nuclei can support a quasi--bound state which can result in a formation of an eta-mesic nucleus. Various aspects of the eta-nucleus physics are discussed with specific emphasis given to the η--nucleus effective interaction in configuration space (constructed via the Marchenko inverse scattering method), to the Okubo--Zweig--Iizuka (OZI) rule, and to the charge symmetry breaking problem.

## Key claim

Within the existing uncertainties in the elementary eta-nucleon interaction, all light nuclei considered (deuteron, triton, helium-3, and helium-4) can support a quasi-bound state, which could result in the formation of an eta-mesic nucleus; however, genuine bound states are impossible because the inelastic channel eta N to N pi is always open, making the Hamiltonian non-Hermitian and allowing only quasi-bound states.

## Method

The authors employed the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian, which truncates the spectral expansion to retain only the ground state of the nucleus (the coherent approximation), and solved the resulting one-dimensional integral equation for the elastic scattering amplitude. The nuclear wave functions were obtained via integro-differential equations (IDEA) with an S-wave Malfliet-Tjon NN potential, which coincide with exact Faddeev equations. The eta-nucleon interaction was represented by a separable form with a pole at the N*(1535) S11 resonance, and the Marchenko inverse scattering method was used to construct a local eta-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- The eta-nucleon scattering length is not accurately known; different analyses give Re a_etaN in the range [0.27, 0.98] fm and Im a_etaN in [0.19, 0.37] fm.
- The N*(1535) resonance parameters used: E_0 = 1535 MeV - (m_N + m_eta), Gamma = 150 MeV, range parameter alpha = 2.357 fm^{-1}.
- The eta-nucleon scattering lengths were calculated for eight values of Re a_etaN from 0.3 fm to 1.0 fm in steps of 0.1 fm, with Im a_etaN fixed at 0.3 fm.
- The mixing angle for eta-pi0 charge symmetry breaking is theta = 0.013.
- The cross section for the reaction d+d to alpha+pi0 at incident deuteron energy T_d = 1100 MeV was measured as 0.97 pb/sr, compared to a prediction of 0.12 pb/sr by Coon and Preedom.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of eta-meson scattering from light nuclei using the Finite Rank Approximation, previously applied to eta-d, eta-3H, eta-3He, and eta-4He systems (Rakityansky et al., various years). It addresses the charge symmetry breaking problem raised by the Goldzahl et al. (1992) experiment on d+d to alpha+pi0, where the cross section was much larger than expected from pure electromagnetic production, and follows the suggestion by Wilkin (1993) that the eta-alpha final state interaction enhances the amplitude. The results enable quantitative microscopic calculations of eta-nucleus scattering lengths and quasi-bound state properties, which can be used to interpret future experiments on eta-mesic nuclei and to test the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule and charge symmetry breaking mechanisms.
