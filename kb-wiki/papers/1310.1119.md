# Reanalysis of the $e^+ e^- \to (D^* \bar D^*)^{\pm} π^{\mp}$ reaction and the claim for the $Z_c (4025)$ resonance

> In this paper we study the reaction $e^+ e^- \to (D^* \bar D^*)^{\pm} π^{\mp}$ in which the BESIII collaboration has claimed the existence of a $1^+$ resonance, named $Z_c(4025)$, in the $D^*\bar D^*$ invariant mass spectrum with a mass around 4026 MeV and width close to 26 MeV. We determine the $D^*\bar D^*$ invariant mass distribution and find that although the explanation considered by the BESIII collaboration is plausible, there are others which are equally possible, like a $2^+$ resonance or a bound state. Even more, we find that the data can be explained without the existence of a resonance/bound state. In view of the different possible interpretations found for the BESIII data, we try to devise a strategy which could help in identifying the origin of the signal reported by the BESIII collaboration. For this, we study the dependence of the $D^*\bar D^*$ spectrum considering the different options as a function of the total center of mass energy. We arrive to the conclusion that increasing the center of mass energy from 4.26 GeV to 4.6 GeV can be useful to distinguish between a resonance, a bound state or just a pure background as being responsible for the signal found. This information should be useful for future experiments.

## Key claim
The BESIII collaboration's claim of a $1^+$ resonance $Z_c(4025)$ in the $D^*\bar D^*$ invariant mass spectrum is not unique; the data can be equally well explained by a $2^+$ resonance, a $2^+$ bound state, or even a pure $D$-wave background without any resonance or bound state.

## Method
The authors model the $e^+ e^- \to (D^* \bar D^*)^{\pm} \pi^{\mp}$ reaction at $\sqrt{s}=4.26$ GeV by parametrizing the $D^*\bar D^*$ invariant mass distribution with a Breit-Wigner amplitude for a resonance or bound state, plus a constant background and a wrong-sign background from the BESIII data. They perform $\chi^2$ fits to the experimental data for five scenarios: a $1^+$ resonance in $S$-wave, a $1^+$ bound state in $S$-wave, a $2^+$ resonance in $D$-wave, a $2^+$ bound state in $D$-wave, and a pure $D$-wave background. They then study how the predicted spectra change when the center-of-mass energy is increased to 4.4 GeV and 4.6 GeV.

## Key numbers
- BESIII reported a $Z_c(4025)$ resonance with mass around 4026 MeV and width close to 26 MeV.
- Best fit for a $1^+$ resonance in $S$-wave gives mass $M_R = 4030$ MeV and width $\Gamma_R = 34$ MeV, with $\chi^2/\text{n.d.o.f} \sim 1.09$.
- Best fit for a $2^+$ bound state in $D$-wave gives mass $M_R = 3990$ MeV and width $\Gamma_R = 160$ MeV, with $\chi^2/\text{n.d.o.f} \sim 1.2$.
- Best fit for a $2^+$ resonance in $D$-wave gives mass $M_R = 4030$ MeV and width $\Gamma_R = 80$ MeV, with $\chi^2/\text{n.d.o.f} \sim 1.1$.
- Pure $D$-wave background yields $\chi^2/\text{n.d.o.f} \sim 1.1$.
- The $1^+$ bound state in $S$-wave gives the worst fit, with $\chi^2/\text{n.d.o.f} \sim 1.4$ (without background).
- The study proposes varying $\sqrt{s}$ from 4.26 GeV to 4.6 GeV to distinguish between scenarios.

## Context
This work reanalyzes the BESIII claim of $Z_c(4025)$ (BESIII Collaboration, 2013) in the context of earlier theoretical predictions: a $2^{++}$ $D^*\bar D^*$ bound state near 3920 MeV from hidden gauge approaches (R. Molina and E. Oset, 2009), and similar threshold-bump misinterpretations in $e^+ e^- \to J/\psi D \bar D$ (Belle Collaboration, 2007) later explained as a $D \bar D$ molecular state below threshold (D. Gamermann et al., 2007). The results weaken the case for $Z_c(4025)$ as a genuine resonance and motivate future experiments at higher center-of-mass energies to discriminate between the competing interpretations.
