# Update on Partial-Wave Analysis

## Key claim

Progress has been made in "model-independent" partial-wave analyses for near-threshold pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction where a single resonance dominates, but more model-dependent approaches covering broader energy ranges are required to convincingly establish the existence of missing resonances, and these ambitious fits face fundamental difficulties in distinguishing between models with fitted parameters and data-fits with theoretical constraints.

## Method

The paper reviews partial-wave analyses of pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction, comparing near-threshold truncated multipole expansions (S- and P-waves) for reactions such as π⁰, η, and η' photoproduction against broader energy-range fits that require model-dependent treatments of Born contributions. The behavior of Born terms is examined through comparisons of the SAID and MAID analyses, including their handling of form factors, unitarization, and cancellation with the P₃₃(1232) multipole, and these approaches are contrasted with constraints from fixed-t dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- At a photon energy of 1 GeV, the π⁺n differential cross section shows a forward peak and a dip near t = -μ², with the Born term contribution reproducing this forward peak but having entirely the wrong shape away from it.
- The Born contribution alone exceeds the experimental total cross section just a few hundred MeV above threshold in π⁺n photoproduction.
- At higher energies (10 GeV), a simple Born approximation continues to give a qualitative description of the forward peak in π⁺n photoproduction.
- The cancellation of the unitarized Born and P₃₃ multipoles in π⁺n photoproduction reproduces the forward peak up to the limit of the MAID analysis (1 GeV).
- The simple model of Born plus P₃₃(1232) cancellation gives a qualitative description of very forward (4°) π⁰p differential cross sections over the same energy region.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing program of partial-wave analyses by the SAID group (Arndt, Workman, and collaborators) and compares with the MAID analysis (Drechsel and collaborators). The paper addresses the Baryon Resonance Analysis Group (BRAG) benchmark comparison of seven different fits to a common dataset, and discusses the need for mutually consistent photo-couplings across different reactions (π, η, K⁺, η' photoproduction) to resolve disagreements such as the conflicting N(1520) and N(1535) photo-decay amplitudes from η versus π photoproduction fits. The results weigh against approaches using phenomenological form factors to separate Born and resonance contributions, and suggest that fixed-t dispersion relation constraints (Engels, Barbour) should be used to test models, enabling more reliable extraction of photo-couplings for the many missing resonances predicted by quark models.
