# Superfluidity and excitations at unitarity

> We present lattice results for spin-1/2 fermions at unitarity, where the effective range of the interaction is zero and the scattering length is infinite. We measure the spatial coherence of difermion pairs for a system of 6, 10, 14, 18, 22, 26 particles with equal numbers of up and down spins in a periodic cube. Using Euclidean time projection, we analyze ground state properties and transient behavior due to low-energy excitations. At asymptotically large values of t we see long-range order consistent with spontaneously broken U(1) fermion-number symmetry and a superfluid ground state. At intermediate times we see exponential decay in the t-dependent signal due to an unknown low-energy excitation. We probe this low-energy excitation further by calculating two-particle correlation functions. We find that the excitation has the properties of a chain of particles extending across the periodic lattice.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice evidence that the ground state of spin-1/2 fermions at unitarity is superfluid, exhibiting off-diagonal long-range order consistent with spontaneously broken U(1) fermion-number symmetry, but only at asymptotically large Euclidean projection times; at intermediate times the signal shows exponential decay due to an unidentified low-energy excitation that behaves like a quasi-1D chain of particles extending across the periodic lattice.

## Method

The authors used a lattice transfer matrix projection method with auxiliary Hubbard-Stratonovich fields to simulate unpolarized spin-1/2 fermions in a periodic cube at unitarity (zero effective range, infinite scattering length). They started from a free Fermi ground state for systems of 6, 10, 14, 18, 22, and 26 particles (N=1,3,5,7,9,11,13 per spin) and applied Euclidean time projection to filter out high-energy states. They measured the spatial correlation function of the difermion pair operator as a function of projection times t1 and t2, and analyzed both the asymptotic large-time behavior and the transient intermediate-time decay to extract the energy gap and spatial structure of low-energy excitations.

## Key numbers

- Systems studied: 6, 10, 14, 18, 22, 26 particles (N=1,3,5,7,9,11,13 per spin) in a periodic cube with equal numbers of up and down spins.
- Lattice parameters: fermion mass 939 MeV, spatial lattice spacing a = (50 MeV)^(-1), temporal lattice spacing a_t = (24 MeV)^(-1).
- Unitary limit interaction coefficient: C_phys = -1.203 x 10^(-4) MeV^(-2).
- Fermi energy defined as E_F = (6 pi^2 N)^(2/3) / (2 m L^2) approximately 7.596 N^(2/3) / (m L^2).
- For the noninteracting 7,7 system (L=4), the bare difermion correlation function at large t gave values 1.19629 x 10^(-2) at n_x=0, 6.10352 x 10^(-3) at n_x=1, and 2.19727 x 10^(-3) at n_x=2, matching free grand canonical calculations to six-digit precision.
- For weak coupling (C_phys = -1.25 x 10^(-5) MeV^(-2), k_F a_scatt = -0.043) in the 7,7 system, simulation results at n_x=0 were 1.3715(1) x 10^(-2), at n_x=1 were 6.999(2) x 10^(-3), and at n_x=2 were 2.536(1) x 10^(-3), agreeing with perturbative calculations within estimated O(k_F^2 a_scatt^2) corrections.

## Context

This work extends lattice simulation techniques previously applied to the unitary limit by Lee (2005) for ground state energy measurements, now focusing on spatial correlations and low-energy excitations. The unitary limit is relevant to the BCS-BEC crossover studied in ultracold atomic gases (Tiesinga 1993, Stwalley 1976, Courteille 1998, Inouye 1998) and to superfluid neutron matter in neutron star crusts (Pethick 1995, Lattimer 2004). The finding of a quasi-1D chain-like excitation opens questions about the nature of low-energy modes in finite systems at unitarity and how they connect to the thermodynamic limit where gapless modes appear.
