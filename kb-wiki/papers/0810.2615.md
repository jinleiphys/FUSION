# Nuclear condensation and symmetry energy of dilute nuclear matter: an S-matrix approach

> Based on the general analysis of the grand canonical partition function in the S-matrix framework, the calculated results on symmetry energy, free energy and entropy of dilute warm nuclear matter are presented. At a given temperature and density, the symmetry energy or symmetry free energy of the clusterized nuclear matter in the S-matrix formulation deviates, particularly at low temperature and relatively higher density, in a subtle way, from the linear dependence on the square of the isospin asymmetry parameter $X=(ρ_n-ρ_p)/(ρ_n+ρ_p)$, contrary to those obtained for homogeneous nucleonic matter. The symmetry coefficients, in the conventional definition, can then be even negative. The symmetry entropy similarly shows a very different behavior.

# Nuclear condensation and symmetry energy of dilute nuclear matter: an S-matrix approach

## Key claim

The symmetry energy of clusterized dilute nuclear matter, calculated in the S-matrix framework, deviates from the linear dependence on the square of the isospin asymmetry parameter X² that is characteristic of homogeneous nucleonic matter; at low temperature and relatively higher density, the symmetry coefficients can even become negative, contrary to mean-field results.

## Method

The authors compute the grand canonical partition function using the S-matrix formalism of Dashen, Ma, and Bernstein (1969), decomposing it into contributions from stable single-particle states of bound nucleon clusters (including ground and excited states) and from two-particle scattering channels. The calculation incorporates all possible nuclear species up to mass A=1000 (SNC model without Coulomb) or approximately 9000 nuclei up to A=339 (SMF model with Coulomb), using experimental binding energies and a liquid-drop mass formula, with scattering contributions from NN, Nt, NHe³, Nα, and αα channels evaluated via phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon density considered: 0.01 fm⁻³
- Maximum cluster mass A_max = 1000 in SNC calculations; A_max = 339 with Z_max = 136 in SMF (approximately 9000 ground-state nuclei)
- Level density parameter a = A/8 MeV⁻¹
- Excited-state integration limits: E₀ = 2 MeV, E_s = 8 MeV; resonance integration extends to E_r ≈ 12 MeV
- In the SNC model at higher densities, the symmetry energy coefficient C_E saturates to approximately 28 MeV, the value for normal nuclear matter in the liquid-drop mass formula used
- Temperatures studied: T = 2, 4, and 8 MeV
- Densities studied: ρ = 0.0001, 0.001, and 0.01 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the virial expansion approach of Horowitz and Schwenk (2006) and Kowalski et al. (2007), who considered only light species (n, p, d, t, He³, α), by incorporating all possible nuclear clusters up to heavy masses in the S-matrix framework. The results show that heavy cluster formation significantly enhances symmetry coefficients compared to mean-field calculations and to light-species-only models, particularly at low temperature and higher density. The findings have direct implications for supernova dynamics, where the symmetry energy and its temperature and density dependence sensitively affect electron-capture rates, neutrino luminosities, and explosive nucleosynthesis yields.
