# Hydrodynamic attractors, initial state energy and particle production in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We exploit the concept of hydrodynamic attractors to establish a general relation between the initial state energy and the produced particle multiplicities in high-energy nuclear collisions. When combined with an ab initio model of energy deposition, the entropy production during the pre-equilibrium phase naturally explains the universal centrality dependence of the measured charged particle yields in nucleus-nucleus collisions. We further estimate the energy density of the far-from-equilibrium initial state and discuss how our results can be used to constrain non-equilibrium properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

# Hydrodynamic attractors, initial state energy and particle production in relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The paper establishes a direct relation between initial-state energy density and final-state charged particle multiplicity in high-energy nuclear collisions by exploiting the concept of hydrodynamic attractors, but this relation depends on the assumption of (nearly-)complete equilibration and is subject to uncertainties in the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio and the attractor constant.

## Method

The authors integrate the energy-momentum conservation equation for a boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous conformal system using the hydrodynamic attractor framework, which provides an effective constitutive relation between longitudinal pressure and energy density far from equilibrium. They then combine this with an ab initio model of initial-state energy deposition based on color-glass condensate effective theory and kT-factorization, where the initial energy density is proportional to products of saturation scales of the colliding nuclei. The resulting relation is applied to compute charged particle multiplicities and initial-state energies for various collision systems at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density for central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV: e0 ≈ 270 GeV/fm^3 at tau0=0.1 fm/c, with dependencies on parameters including (eta/s)^(-1/2) and (dN_ch/deta)^(3/2)
- The initial energy density is nearly three orders of magnitude higher than the QCD crossover energy density ec ≈ 0.346(41) GeV/fm^3 (for 2+1 flavor QCD)
- Entropy per charged particle at freeze-out: S/N_ch ≈ 6.7–8.5
- Jacobian factor between particle rapidity and pseudorapidity: J ≈ 1.1
- Transverse area for central Pb-Pb collisions: A⊥ ≈ π R_Pb^2 ≈ 138 fm^2
- Upper limit on shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s ≲ 0.4 for the most favorable choice of all other parameters
- Variation of the attractor constant C across different microscopic theories is at approximately 10% level

## Context

This work extends previous parametric estimates of entropy production based on particular microscopic scenarios (Baier 2002, Baier 2011, van der Schee 2015, Berges 2017) by providing a macroscopic description based on hydrodynamic attractors that is insensitive to microscopic details. The results enable constraints on non-equilibrium properties of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, by comparing reconstructed initial-state energy to experimentally measured final-state energy. The approach can be further refined using the KøMPøST pre-equilibrium package (Kurkela 2018, Kurkela 2018) and extended to smaller collision systems such as p-A or O-O collisions.
