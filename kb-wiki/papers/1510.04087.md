# Nuclear polarization effects in Coulomb excitation studies

> New polarization potentials have been determined based on: 1) the latest photo-neutron cross section evaluation and a missing factor of two in previous work, and 2) the mass dependency of the symmetry energy, $a_{sym}(A)$. The magnitude of the first one is 35\% stronger than the currently accepted polarization potential. The second one opens up the possibility for a parameter-free polarization potential. Both polarization potentials are essentially the same for heavy nuclei. The polarization effect on quadrupole collectivity is more substantial than previously assumed for light nuclei. Particular cases are discussed where long-standing discrepancies between high-precision Coulomb-excitation and lifetime measurements still remain. A solution to the long-standing discrepancy between $B(E2; 0^+_1\rightarrow 2^+_1)$ values determined in $^{18}$O by several Coulomb-excitation studies and a high-precision lifetime measurement is provided in favor of the latter. Polarization effects in light nuclei also influence the determination of spectroscopic quadrupole moments in Coulomb-excitation measurements. The hindrance of polarizability observed in the photo-neutron cross section for single-closed shell nuclei is calculated to have a negligible effect on quadrupole collectivity, within the existing experimental uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the currently accepted polarization potential used in Coulomb-excitation codes is too weak by 54% due to a missing factor of two in previous work, and that a new parameter-free polarization potential based on the mass dependency of the symmetry energy provides a natural explanation for enhanced quadrupole collectivity in light nuclei, though the hydrodynamic model remains untested for the lightest nuclei with A < 10.

## Method

The author re-derived the E1 polarization potential from first principles using the correct form of the sum rule relating the static nuclear polarizability α to the (-2) moment of the total electric-dipole photo-absorption cross section σ₋₂, correcting a factor-of-two error in Alder and Winther's formulation. A new empirical formula for σ₋₂ was determined from the 1988 photo-neutron cross-section evaluation using monoenergetic photons, and a parameter-free polarization potential was derived by incorporating the mass-dependent symmetry energy a_sym(A) from Tian et al. (2013) into the hydrodynamic model. The effects were tested using the semi-classical coupled-channel Coulomb excitation code GOSIA for specific cases including ¹⁸O.

## Key numbers

- The corrected polarization potential is 54% stronger than the currently accepted one, with the z parameter changing from z = 0.005 κ (E_p A_p)/(Z_p²(1+A_p/A_t)) to z = 0.0077 κ (E_p A_p)/(Z_p²(1+A_p/A_t))
- For κ=1, the new polarization potential yields approximately 3% increase in previously extracted B(E2) values
- The long-standing discrepancy in ¹⁸O between Coulomb-excitation B(E2; 0⁺₁→2⁺₁) = 0.00421(9) e²b² and the lifetime measurement value of 0.00476(11) e²b² is resolved using κ≈1.8, yielding approximately 10% increase in the B(E2) value
- The mass-dependent symmetry energy is given by a_sym(A) = 28.32(1 - 1.27A⁻¹/³) MeV, with bulk symmetry energy coefficient S_v ≈ 28.32 MeV and surface-to-volume ratio S_s/S_v ≈ 1.27
- The parameter-free σ₋₂ formula is σ₋₂(A) = (1.83 A²)/(A¹/³ - 1.27) μb/MeV for A≥3
- The polarizability parameter κ for ¹⁷O is 8.3(6) from Coulomb-excitation measurements
- The renormalised κ=1.2 for the 2⁺₁ state in ¹⁰Be yields a shift of approximately +0.05 eb in the spectroscopic quadrupole moment

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic model of Migdal (1944) and Levinger (1957) by correcting a factor-of-two error in the polarization potential formulation used in standard Coulomb-excitation codes such as GOSIA (Czosnyka et al.) and Winther-de Boer (Drake et al.). The paper provides a solution to the long-standing discrepancy between Coulomb-excitation and lifetime measurements in ¹⁸O, first noted by Ball et al. (1982) and Hermans et al. (1982). The parameter-free polarization potential derived from the mass-dependent symmetry energy of Tian et al. (2013) enables future Coulomb-excitation studies of radioactive nuclei where no photo-absorption cross-section information is available, and opens up tests of the hydrodynamic model for light nuclei through high-precision Coulomb-excitation measurements.
