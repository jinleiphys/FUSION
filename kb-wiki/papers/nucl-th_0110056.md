# Statistical theory of the many-body nuclear system

> A recently proposed statistical theory of the mean fields associated with the ground and excited collective states of a generic many-body system is extended by increasing the dimensions of the P-space. In applying the new framework to nuclear matter, in addition to the mean field energies we obtain their fluctuations as well, together with the ones of the wavefunctions, in first order of the expansion in the complexity of the Q-space states. The physics described by the latter is assumed to be random. To extract numerical predictions out of our scheme we develop a schematic version of the approach, which, while much simplified, yields results of significance on the size of the error affecting the mean fields, on the magnitude of the residual effective interaction, on the ground state spectroscopic factor and on the mixing occurring between the vectors spanning the P-space.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed statistical theory of nuclear mean fields by enlarging the P-space from one dimension to include both the ground state and 2p-2h excitations, but the numerical predictions remain dependent on a free parameter (the energy averaging width epsilon) and the approach is applied only to nuclear matter, not finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors partition the nuclear Hilbert space into a P-space (deterministic physics) and a Q-space (random, chaotic physics), then perform an energy average over the Q-space using a Lorentz-like distribution. They derive mean field equations and fluctuation equations for the ground state energy, expanding in the complexity of Q-space states (first order: 2p-2h states for the one-dimensional P-space, 4p-4h states for the enlarged P-space). A schematic model is developed to extract numerical predictions by correlating known observables (binding energy, level density) with the unknown mean field energy and residual interaction.

## Key numbers

- The number of 2p-2h states N_2 is computed using the Ericson formula for the density of N-particle–N-hole nuclear states: rho^{(N)}_{ph}(E,J) = g(gE)^{N-1}/(p!h!(N-1)!) * (2J+1)/sqrt(8pi)sigma^3 N^{3/2} * exp[-(2J+1)^2/(8N sigma^2)], with g = (3/2)(A/epsilon_F) and sigma^2 = F sqrt(E/a)/hbar^2, where a = A/8 MeV^{-1}
- The mean field energy per particle is given by E0_bar = 1/2 {(E_HF + E + epsilon) - sqrt[(E_HF - E - epsilon)^2 + 4 beta^2]}, valid for epsilon < E_HF - E
- The parameter epsilon (energy averaging width) is taken as epsilon = 7 in the bi-dimensional Hilbert space example and epsilon = 10 in the tri-dimensional examples (arbitrary units)
- The residual effective interaction matrix elements squared (beta^2) are predicted to be quenched by an order of magnitude compared to the bare force matrix elements

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous statistical theory (De Pace et al., 1999; Feshbach, 1996; Carra et al., 1998) from a one-dimensional P-space to a multi-dimensional P-space, allowing computation of wave function fluctuations in addition to energy fluctuations. The approach is inspired by the optical model of nuclear reactions (Feshbach, 1992) and aims to provide an alternative to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and Green's function Monte Carlo methods for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei. The results enable estimates of the error associated with mean field energies, the magnitude of the residual effective interaction, and the ground state spectroscopic factor, though the theory remains parameter-dependent and limited to nuclear matter.
