# Ab initio Translationally Invariant Nonlocal One-body Densities from No-core Shell-model Theory

> [Background:] It is well known that effective nuclear interactions are in general nonlocal. Thus if nuclear densities obtained from {\it ab initio} no-core-shell-model (NCSM) calculations are to be used in reaction calculations, translationally invariant nonlocal densities must be available. [Purpose:] Though it is standard to extract translationally invariant one-body local densities from NCSM calculations to calculate local nuclear observables like radii and transition amplitudes, the corresponding nonlocal one-body densities have not been considered so far. A major reason for this is that the procedure for removing the center-of-mass component from NCSM wavefunctions up to now has only been developed for local densities. [Results:] A formulation for removing center-of-mass contributions from nonlocal one-body densities obtained from NCSM and symmetry-adapted NCSM (SA-NCSM) calculations is derived, and applied to the ground state densities of $^4$He, $^6$Li, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O. The nonlocality is studied as a function of angular momentum components in momentum as well as coordinate space [Conclusions:] We find that the nonlocality for the ground state densities of the nuclei under consideration increases as a function of the angular momentum. The relative magnitude of those contributions decreases with increasing angular momentum. In general, the nonlocal structure of the one-body density matrices we studied is given by the shell structure of the nucleus, and can not be described with simple functional forms.

## Key claim

The paper derives a method for removing center-of-mass contributions from nonlocal one-body densities obtained from no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations, and demonstrates that the nonlocal structure of these densities is determined by the shell structure of the nucleus and cannot be described by simple functional forms, though the results are presented as a "proof-of-principle" study using a single interaction (JISP16) rather than a comprehensive benchmark.

## Method

The authors start from space-fixed nonlocal one-body density matrix elements calculated in the NCSM framework using harmonic oscillator single-particle states, then apply a Fourier transformation to momentum space and use Talmi-Moshinsky transformations to express the density in terms of the momentum transfer q and the average momentum K. The center-of-mass contribution, which factorizes exactly in the NCSM harmonic oscillator basis, is removed analytically by multiplying the space-fixed density by exp(b²q²/4A), where b is the oscillator length and A the mass number. The resulting translationally invariant nonlocal densities are studied in both momentum space (as functions of q and K) and coordinate space (as functions of the local coordinate ζ and nonlocal coordinate Z) for different angular momentum components.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C, the K=0 component of the local proton density is normalized at q=0 to the number of protons (6 for ¹²C)
- The integrated nonlocal density values agree with directly constructed local densities to at least six significant figures
- For ⁶Li, model space studies were carried out at N_max = 6, 10, 12, and 14 with ℏω = 20 MeV
- For ⁶Li, oscillator parameter dependence was studied at ℏω = 15 MeV, 20 MeV, and 25 MeV with N_max = 12
- The center-of-mass contribution is given by exp(-b²q²/4A), where A is the number of nucleons
- For ¹²C and ¹⁶O, the maximum of the l_q=0 slice is located around K ~ 1 fm⁻¹ and q = 0
- For ¹²C, the l_q=0 component exhibits a minimum around q ~ 2 fm⁻¹ at K = 0

## Context

This work extends the procedure for removing center-of-mass contributions from local densities, previously developed by Navratil (2004), Cockrell (2012), and Dytrych (2015), to the nonlocal case for the first time. The nonlocal densities are needed for microscopic folding model calculations of optical potentials (Arellano, 1990; Elster, 1996) and for treating antisymmetrization between clusters in reaction theory (Navratil, 2009). The method enables the use of ab initio NCSM wave functions in reaction calculations that require nonlocal density input, and the authors note that further studies with different NN interactions will be needed to determine if the observed nonlocal structures persist.
