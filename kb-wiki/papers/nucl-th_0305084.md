# Hydrodynamic description of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Relativistic hydrodynamics has been extensively applied to high energy heavy-ion collisions. We review hydrodynamic calculations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies and provide a comprehensive comparison between the model and experimental data. The model provides a very good description of all measured momentum distributions in central and semiperipheral Au+Au collisions, including the momentum anisotropies (elliptic flow) and systematic dependencies on the hadron rest masses up to transverse momenta of about 1.5--2 GeV/c. This provides impressive evidence that the bulk of the fireball matter shows efficient thermalization and behaves hydrodynamically. At higher p_t the hydrodynamic model begins to gradually break down, following an interesting pattern which we discuss. The elliptic flow anisotropy is shown to develop early in the collision and to provide important information about the early expansion stage, pointing to the formation of a highly equilibrated quark-gluon plasma at energy densities well above the deconfinement threshold. Two-particle momentum correlations provide information about the spatial structure of the fireball (size, deformation, flow) at the end of the collision. Hydrodynamic calculations of the two-particle correlation functions do not describe the data very well. Possible origins of the discrepancies are discussed but not fully resolved, and further measurements to help clarify this situation are suggested.

# Hydrodynamic description of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Relativistic hydrodynamics provides a very good description of all measured momentum distributions in central and semiperipheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies up to transverse momenta of about 1.5–2 GeV/c, but the model fails to describe two-particle correlation functions (HBT radii) and the discrepancies remain unresolved despite attempts to modify freeze-out conditions, initial conditions, and include dissipative effects.

## Method
The authors performed hydrodynamic calculations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies using a longitudinally boost-invariant source with Cooper-Frye freeze-out on a sharp freeze-out hypersurface, computing single-particle spectra, elliptic flow, and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions. They compared these calculations to experimental data from STAR and PHENIX collaborations, and explored modifications including earlier freeze-out, seeding transverse flow earlier, breaking longitudinal boost invariance, and adding first-order viscous corrections.

## Key numbers
- Hydrodynamic model describes data up to transverse momenta of about 1.5–2 GeV/c
- At RHIC, the ratio R_o/R_s is approximately 1 in the entire accessible K_T region up to 0.7 GeV/c, and holds true out to K_T greater than about 1.2 GeV/c
- For the in-plane elongated source calculation, initial central temperature was 2 GeV at tau_eq = 0.1 fm/c with decoupling at T_dec = 100 MeV
- The non-Gaussian corrections to the longitudinal radius R_l are only a fraction of 1 fm and not large enough to bridge the discrepancy with data
- Viscosity values needed to produce the desired reduction in R_l reduced elliptic flow v_2 almost by a factor of 2, incompatible with data

## Context
This review extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations by Landau (1953) and Rischke (1998) and addresses the "RHIC HBT puzzle" where hydrodynamic predictions for two-particle correlation functions disagree with STAR (2001) and PHENIX (2002) data. The work enables future tests of dissipative effects and hybrid calculations combining hydrodynamics with hadronic cascades as in SBD (2001), and motivates further measurements of azimuthal HBT oscillations to constrain the spatial deformation of the fireball at freeze-out.
