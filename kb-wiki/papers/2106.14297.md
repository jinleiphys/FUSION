# Hadron gas in the presence of a magnetic field using non-extensive statistics: A transition from diamagnetic to paramagnetic system

> Non-central heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies are unique in producing magnetic fields of the largest strength in the laboratory. Such fields being produced at the early stages of the collision could affect the properties of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) matter formed in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The transient magnetic field leaves its reminiscence, which in principle, can affect the thermodynamic and transport properties of the final state dynamics of the system. In this work, we study the thermodynamic properties of a hadron gas in the presence of an external static magnetic field using a thermodynamically consistent non-extensive Tsallis distribution function. Various thermodynamical observables such as energy density ($ε$), entropy density ($s$), pressure ($P$) and speed of sound ($c_{\rm s}$) are studied. Investigation of magnetization ($M$) is also performed and this analysis reveals an interplay of diamagnetic and paramagnetic nature of the system in the presence of a magnetic field of varying strength. Further, to understand the system dynamics under equilibrium and non-equilibrium conditions, the effect of the non-extensive parameter ($q$) on the above observables is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hadron gas in the presence of a static magnetic field, described using non-extensive Tsallis statistics, exhibits a transition from diamagnetic to paramagnetic behavior as the magnetic field strength increases from \(eB = m_{\pi}^{2}\) to \(eB = 15m_{\pi}^{2}\), but only when the system is far from equilibrium (non-extensive parameter \(q = 1.15\)); this transition is not observed for near-equilibrium systems (\(q = 1.001\)).

## Method

The authors formulated a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution function for a hadron gas composed of particles up to a mass of 1.2 GeV in the presence of an external static magnetic field. They calculated thermodynamic observables (energy density, pressure, entropy density, speed of sound, and magnetization) by summing over Landau levels for charged particles and using standard phase-space integrals for neutral particles, with vacuum contributions regularized via dimensional regularization and renormalization of the electric charge and magnetic field. The study was performed for varying values of the non-extensive parameter \(q\) (ranging from 1.001 to 1.15) and magnetic field strengths (\(eB = 0\), \(m_{\pi}^{2}\), and \(15m_{\pi}^{2}\)).

## Key numbers

- The maximum magnetic field generated in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC is of order \(eB \sim m_{\pi}^{2} \sim 10^{18}\) G, and at LHC it is \(eB \sim 15m_{\pi}^{2}\).
- For near-equilibrium (\(q = 1.001\)) at \(eB = 0\) GeV\(^2\), the total energy density agrees with HRG results up to \(T \sim 150\) MeV; at \(eB = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\), agreement holds up to \(T \sim 110\) MeV.
- For the system away from equilibrium (\(q = 1.15\)) at a lower magnetic field \(eB = m_{\pi}^{2}\), magnetization is negative (diamagnetic), while at a higher field \(eB = 15m_{\pi}^{2}\), magnetization becomes positive (paramagnetic).
- The squared speed of sound for near-equilibrium (\(q = 1.001\)) decreases with temperature for all magnetic fields, while for \(q = 1.15\) it increases with temperature for almost all magnetic field cases.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadron gas in magnetic fields using standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, such as those by Endrodi (2013) and Kadam (2019), by incorporating non-extensive Tsallis statistics to describe systems away from equilibrium, as observed in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The results enable further investigation into the interplay between magnetic field strength and non-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the diamagnetic-to-paramagnetic transition, which warrants deeper theoretical study.
