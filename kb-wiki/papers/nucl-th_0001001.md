# Exploring vector meson masses in nuclear collisions

> The formalism developed earlier by us for the propagation of a resonance in the nuclear medium in proton-nucleus collisions has been modified to the case of vector boson production in heavy-ion collisions. The first part of the talk describes this formalism. The formalism includes coherently the contribution to the observed di-lepton production from the decay of a vector boson inside as well as outside the nuclear medium. The calculated invariant rho mass distributions are presented for the $ρ$-meson production using optical potentials estimated within the VDM and the resonance model. In the second part of the talk we write a formalism for coherent rho production in proton nucleus collisions and explore the sensitivity of the (p,p$^\prime ρ^0$) reaction cross section to medium mass modification of the rho meson.

# Exploring vector meson masses in nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The formalism for resonance propagation in nuclear matter, previously developed for proton-nucleus collisions, is extended to heavy-ion collisions and includes coherent addition of resonance decay inside and outside the nuclear medium. The calculated mass shifts for the rho meson using optical potentials from the vector meson dominance (VDM) and resonance model are small and in the opposite direction compared to those indicated in high-energy heavy-ion experiments; only with an arbitrarily chosen real potential of -120 MeV (versus the model value of approximately +25 MeV) does the distribution begin to show features resembling experimental indications.

## Method

The authors modify their earlier formalism for resonance propagation in proton-nucleus collisions to the case of vector boson production in heavy-ion collisions, incorporating the resonance self-energy in the medium via an optical potential. The invariant mass distribution of di-leptons from rho-meson decay is computed by coherently summing the contributions from decay inside and outside the nuclear medium, using optical potentials estimated from the VDM and resonance model following Kondratyuk et al. For proton-nucleus collisions, a formalism for coherent rho production in the (p,p'ρ⁰) reaction is developed, where the rho-meson scattering wave function includes medium effects through an optical potential, and the cross section is calculated for a ¹²C target at 1.5 GeV beam energy.

## Key numbers

- Free width of the rho meson taken as Γ_ρ = 150 MeV; free rho mass m_ρ = 770 MeV.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at rho speed 0.6c, the coherent and incoherent cross sections differ only in peak cross section; at 0.9c, both shape and peak cross section differ.
- For the (p,p'ρ⁰) reaction on ¹²C at 1.5 GeV beam energy, the calculated peak cross section is approximately 0.34 μb/MeV.
- Optical potentials for rho meson at μ = 770 MeV: at v/c = 0.04, U = -20.4 MeV, W = -40.8 MeV; at v/c = 0.6, U = 37.9 MeV, W = -50.6 MeV; at v/c = 0.9, U = 25.8 MeV, W = -54.7 MeV.
- For the (p,p'ρ⁰) reaction, optical potentials for rho momentum k_ρ = 30 MeV/c: U = -20.75 MeV, W = -26.51 MeV; for k_ρ = 500 MeV/c: U = 33.49 MeV, W = -46.73 MeV.
- The sharp peak in the proton energy spectrum for a purely real potential appears at ω/Δm ≈ 3.7 for Δm = 100 and 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Jain et al. (1999) and Boreskov et al. (1999) for resonance propagation in proton-nucleus collisions to heavy-ion collisions, incorporating coherent rather than incoherent addition of inside and outside decay contributions. The optical potentials are taken from Kondratyuk et al., who used VDM at high energies and the resonance model at low energies to generate ρN scattering parameters. The results enable future extraction of in-medium rho-meson properties from heavy-ion and proton-nucleus data, provided the reaction dynamics are correctly described and experimental uncertainties are reduced; the authors note that existing heavy-ion data contain contributions from several other di-lepton processes beyond those considered here.
