# From three-body resonances to bound states in a continuum: pole trajectories

> We investigate the formation of three-body bound states in the continuum by tracing pole trajectories in the complex energy plane under variation of system parameters. Using a one-dimensional model of two identical bosons and a distinguishable particle interacting via Gaussian potentials, we systematically vary the interaction strength, interaction range, and mass ratio. Our results confirm the parametric nature of few-body bound states in a continuum (BIC) and extend this characterization to a broader set of system parameters. Specifically, we find that variations of both interaction parameters and the mass ratio can lead to the formation of at least one three-body BIC. However, the exact shape of trajectories differs, and for the mass ratio variation we find a more regular pattern with multiple BIC locations. These results suggest that the mechanism of few-body BIC formation is more sensitive to the kinematic structure of the problem than to the specific details of the two-body interaction.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that three-body bound states in the continuum (BICs) can form from resonances under variation of interaction strength, interaction range, and mass ratio, but the exact shape of the pole trajectories differs between parameters; the mass ratio variation produces a more regular pattern with multiple BIC locations, while interaction parameter variations yield only a single BIC within the accessible range.

## Method

The author solves the three-body Schrodinger equation for a one-dimensional system of two identical bosons and a distinguishable particle interacting via Gaussian potentials, using the complex scaling method (CSM) to transform resonances into discrete eigenvalues. The Gaussian expansion method (GEM) is employed via the open-source Julia package FewBodyToolkit.jl to expand the three-body state into superpositions of centered Gaussian functions. Pole trajectories are traced in the complex energy plane by systematically varying the interaction strength, interaction range, and mass ratio.

## Key numbers

- For the interaction strength variation (v0 from -1.81 to -14.51, with mu_g = 1 and beta = 5 fixed), the resonance pole moves from near the three-body breakup threshold (E^(3) = 0) to lower energies, with a single BIC formation where the width vanishes.
- For the interaction range variation (mu_g from 2.22 to 0.3, with v0 = -4 and beta = 5 fixed), the trajectory is qualitatively similar to the strength variation, with the BIC occurring at nearly the same real energy.
- For the mass ratio variation (beta from 1 to 20, with v0 = -4 and mu_g = 1 fixed), the trajectory extends nearly two orders of magnitude larger in imaginary part than the interaction parameter scans, with multiple BIC locations in a regular oscillatory pattern.
- All three parameter variations show a common BIC near p_rel approximately 2.2, where the width drops close to 10^-6 in the natural units of the problem.
- The numerical convergence tests show that for n_max = N_max greater than or equal to 26, results are converged within numerical precision of about 10^-6.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Happ 2024) on three-body resonances in a mass-imbalanced one-dimensional system, and builds on a subsequent two-channel model study (Happ 2025) that identified the mechanism for increasing resonance lifetimes to infinity. The results confirm the single-resonance parametric nature of three-body BICs reported in that prior work and extend the characterization to a broader parameter space including interaction strength and range. The finding of a common BIC at the same relative momentum value across all parameter variations suggests some universality in the formation mechanism, though developing an analytical understanding of this behavior and testing its generality beyond Gaussian potentials remain open questions for future investigation.
