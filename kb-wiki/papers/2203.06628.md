# Helicity-dependent distribution of strange quarks in the proton from nonlocal chiral effective theory

> The helicity-dependent strange quark distribution in the proton, $Δs$, is calculated in a nonlocal chiral SU(3) effective field theory. The hadronic proton to meson plus octet or decuplet baryon splitting functions are derived at the one-loop level, with loop integrals rendered finite by correlation functions introduced in the nonlocal Lagrangian. Within the convolution framework, the proton strange helicity distribution is obtained using spin-flavor symmetry to constrain the input valence quark distributions in the hadronic intermediate states. The polarized strange quark distribution is found to be quite small, with the lowest moment of $Δs$ negative, but consistent with recent global QCD analyses.

## Key claim

The polarized strange quark distribution in the proton, Δs, is found to be quite small, with the lowest moment negative, consistent with recent global QCD analyses; however, the calculation relies on a nonlocal chiral effective theory with dipole form factors and SU(6) symmetry assumptions for the input valence quark distributions, and the results depend on regulator parameters Λ_B = 1.0–1.2 GeV and Λ_T = 0.7–0.9 GeV determined from unpolarized baryon production data.

## Method

The authors derived spin-dependent hadronic splitting functions at one-loop level from a nonlocal chiral SU(3) effective Lagrangian, including octet rainbow, tadpole, Kroll-Ruderman, decuplet rainbow, and octet-decuplet transition diagrams. The loop integrals were rendered finite by dipole correlation functions in the nonlocal Lagrangian, and the proton strange helicity distribution was obtained via convolution of these splitting functions with input valence quark distributions in the intermediate hadronic states, related to the proton's polarized Δu and Δd PDFs using SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The regulator parameters were taken as Λ_B = 1.1(1) GeV for octet baryon-meson vertices and Λ_T = 0.8(1) GeV for decuplet baryon-meson vertices, determined from fitting pp → BX cross section data in Salamu (2018, 2019).
- The lowest moment (integrated polarization) of Δs is negative, consistent with the JAM global QCD analysis value Δs⁺_JAM = −0.03 ± 0.10 at Q² = 1 GeV² (Ethier, 2017).
- The lattice QCD result cited for comparison is Δs⁺_latt = −0.046 ± 0.008 (Alexandrou, 2020).
- The nonsinglet axial charge from hyperon beta decays is a₈ = 0.58 ± 0.03 (Close, 1993), while a cloudy bag model re-evaluation gives a₈ = 0.46 ± 0.05 (Bass, 2009).

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal chiral effective theory framework previously applied to unpolarized strange quark PDFs (Salamu, 2018, 2019) and transverse momentum dependent distributions (He, 2019) into the polarized sector. The results are consistent with the small negative strange quark polarization found in recent global QCD analyses by the JAM Collaboration (Ethier, 2017) and lattice QCD calculations (Alexandrou, 2020), and contradict earlier phenomenological assumptions of a large negative Δs ≈ −0.1. The formalism enables future studies of the flavor and helicity structure of the proton sea, and can be extended to other polarized sea quark distributions and to comparisons with experimental data from Jefferson Lab, COMPASS, and future electron-ion colliders.
