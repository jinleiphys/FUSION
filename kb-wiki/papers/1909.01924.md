# A time-dependent Hartree-Fock study of triple-alpha dynamics

> Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations have been performed for fusion reactions of He-4 + He-4 -> Be*-8, followed by He-4 + Be*-8 . Depending on the orientation of the initial state, a linear chain vibrational state or a triangular vibration is found in 12C, with transitions between these states observed. The vibrations of the linear chain state and the triangular state occur at ~9 and 4 MeV respectively.

## Key claim

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations of the triple-alpha reaction produce identifiable linear chain and triangular vibrational states in \(^{12}\)C at approximately 9 and 4 MeV respectively, with transitions between these states observed; however, the authors note that the SLy4d interaction underbinds the alpha particle by about 40% compared to experiment, which could strongly influence the results, and a fuller study with a better-fitted interaction is warranted.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the Skyrme SLy4d interaction in the Sky3D code, first simulating \(^4\)He + \(^4\)He fusion to produce \(^8\)Be\(^*\) at a center-of-mass energy of 1.0 MeV with zero impact parameter. The wave functions of the \(^8\)Be\(^*\) nucleus were saved at two different configurations (near maximum and minimum quadrupole moment) and used as starting points for subsequent \(^4\)He + \(^8\)Be\(^*\) collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 2.0 MeV, with impact parameters of 0 and 1 fm and with the \(^8\)Be\(^*\) oriented either along its long axis ("tip") or short axis ("side"). The resulting quadrupole oscillations of the compound \(^{12}\)C nucleus were analyzed via Fourier power spectra to extract vibrational energies.

## Key numbers

- The ground state alpha particle binding energy from static Hartree-Fock with SLy4d is 17.67 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 28.30 MeV (underbinding by ~40%).
- In the \(^4\)He + \(^4\)He collision at \(E_{CM}=1.0\) MeV and \(b=0\) fm, two states appear in the quadrupole oscillation spectrum of \(^8\)Be\(^*\): one at 8.5 MeV and another at 14.7 MeV.
- The lowest known \(2^+\) resonance in \(^8\)Be is at 3.03 MeV \(\pm\) 10 keV, and the broad \(4^+\) resonance is at 11.35 MeV (Tilley 2004).
- The linear chain vibrational state in \(^{12}\)C oscillates at approximately 9 MeV, and the triangular vibrational state oscillates at approximately 4 MeV.
- In a \(b=1\) fm tip collision, the nucleus initially oscillates in the 9 MeV chain state before decaying to the 4 MeV triangular state within approximately 2 zs.

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of the triple-alpha reaction by Umar (2010) and Ichikawa (2011), using a two-step process and spectral analysis of the compound nuclei, and qualitatively agrees with Umar (2010) on the existence of chain and triangular states. The results also relate to earlier work on shape isomers in \(^{24}\)Mg by Umar (1986) and to recent TDHF calculations on helium nuclei by Iwata (2019). The authors identify the need for future work including angular momentum projection, a fuller mapping of the parameter space (center-of-mass energy, impact parameter, orientation), and use of a Skyrme interaction that better reproduces the alpha particle binding energy.
