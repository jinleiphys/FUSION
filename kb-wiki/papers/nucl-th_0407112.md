# Short-range nucleon-nucleon correlation effects in photon- and electron-nucleus reactions

> The role played by nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations in photon- and electron-nucleus reactions is analyzed with a model which includes all the diagrams containing a single correlation function. The excitation of low-lying high-spin states, the reactions (e,e'), (e,e'N) and ($γ$,N) and the two-proton emission by both electrons and real photons are investigated. The results obtained for doubly-closed shell nuclei show that ($γ$,pp) appears to be the most adequate process to identify short-range correlations in the clearer way.

## Key claim

Short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations produce very small effects in inclusive (e,e') and one-nucleon emission processes, within the uncertainties of the input parameters, but the (γ,pp) reaction appears to be the most adequate process to identify short-range correlations in a clearer way, provided kinematics are chosen to minimize competing Δ-current contributions.

## Method

The authors use a Correlated Basis Function approach with a scalar correlation operator F = ∏ f_ij, truncating the cluster expansion to retain only diagrams containing a single correlation function h_ij = f²_ij − 1. They calculate the linear response function ξ_n(q) for 1p1h and 2p2h final states, including Meyer-like diagrams (one-point, two-point, and three-point diagrams), and apply the model to inclusive (e,e'), (e,e'N), (γ,N), (e,e'pp), and (γ,pp) reactions on doubly-closed shell nuclei (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb). Three different correlation functions (Gaussian G, S3, and V8) are tested to assess sensitivity to the correlation details.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O(e,e') at momentum transfers 300, 400, and 500 MeV/c, the maximum variation in the quasi-elastic peak due to SRC is around 2%.
- For ¹⁶O(e,e'p) with ω=128 MeV and |p|=444 MeV/c, the correlated and uncorrelated cross sections overlap almost exactly; relative differences are shown but no single percentage is quoted as dominant.
- For ¹⁶O(γ,p), the G correlation produces SRC effects about a factor 4 larger than those from S3 or V8 correlations.
- For ¹⁶O(e,e'pp) at incident electron energy 800 MeV, energy transfer 100 MeV, momentum transfer 400 MeV/c, the S3 and V8 correlations give cross sections a factor 2 smaller than the G correlation.
- For ¹⁶O(γ,pp) at ω=100 MeV, the ratio S(SRC)/S(SRC+Δ) is close to unity for most final states, indicating small Δ-current effects; at ω=215 MeV the Δ effects are larger.
- For ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca (γ,pp) to the ground state of the residual nucleus, the integrated S-factor ratios S(S3)/S(G) and S(V8)/S(G) range from 0.10±0.11 to 0.73±0.11 depending on nucleus and final state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own previous model for electromagnetic responses of nuclei with A>4 (Co' 1998, Mokhtar 2000, Co' 2001) to include two-nucleon emission processes. It contradicts the claim of Pandharipande, Papanicolas, and Wambach (1984) that SRC produce significant quenching in low-lying high-spin state excitations; the authors find quenching factors change by less than 5% when SRC are included. The results enable future experimental searches for SRC signatures in (γ,pp) reactions with careful kinematic selection to suppress Δ-current backgrounds, and suggest that (e,e'pp) longitudinal response could provide a clean signal but requires experimentally difficult Rosenbluth separation.
