# Ambiguities from nuclear interactions in the $^{12}$C($p,2p$)$^{11}$B reaction

> We investigate the impact of ambiguities coming from the choice of optical potentials and nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections on the spectroscopic factors extracted from the $^{12}$C($p,2p$)$^{11}$B reaction. These ambiguities are evaluated by analyzing the cross sections of the $^{12}$C($p,2p$)$^{11}$B reaction at 100 and 200 MeV within the framework of the distorted-wave impulse approximation with realistic choices of nuclear inputs. The results show that the studied ambiguities are considerably large in this energy region and careful choices of nuclear inputs used in the reaction calculations are required to extract reliable structure information.

## Key claim

The ambiguities in spectroscopic factors extracted from the $^{12}$C($p,2p$)$^{11}$B reaction due to the choice of optical potentials and nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections are considerably large at 100 and 200 MeV, but the extracted values remain consistent with the $(e,e'p)$ reference within about 20% deviation, with the agreement deteriorating as incident energy decreases.

## Method

The authors analyzed the triple differential cross sections of the $^{12}$C($p,2p$)$^{11}$B reaction at 100 and 200 MeV within the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework. They compared calculations using three types of optical potentials (microscopic single-folding Melbourne $g$-matrix, Koning-Delaroche phenomenological, and EDAD1 Dirac-based phenomenological) and three parametrizations of the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section (Franey-Love, Reid93, and SP07). The $p$-state spectroscopic factors were extracted by normalizing the calculated triple differential cross sections to experimental data at quasifree kinematic conditions.

## Key numbers

- At 200 MeV, the extracted $p$-state spectroscopic factor is 1.71(16), with an uncertainty of about 9% from the choice of nuclear inputs.
- At 100 MeV, the extracted $p$-state spectroscopic factor is 1.50(27), with an uncertainty of about 18% from the choice of nuclear inputs.
- The $(e,e'p)$ reference value for the $p$-state spectroscopic factor of $^{12}$C is 1.98(11) (Kramer et al., 2001; van der Steenhoven et al., 1988).
- For the 200 MeV case, the EDAD1 optical potential gives a spectroscopic factor of 1.82, while the Koning-Delaroche and $g$-matrix folding potentials both give 1.60, a difference of about 6%.
- For the 100 MeV case, the extracted $p$-state spectroscopic factors are 1.61 ($g$-matrix folding), 1.69 (Koning-Delaroche), and 1.32 (EDAD1), giving a 12% uncertainty from the optical potential choice.
- The differences between the three $NN$ cross section sets at 200 MeV are less than 3% at the peak region for the quasifree angle pair.
- At 100 MeV, a maximum deviation of 12% is observed between the Franey-Love and SP07 $NN$ cross sections in most of the angular region.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $^{12}$C($p,2p$)$^{11}$B reaction by using more modern and varied nuclear inputs than the original experimental analyses by Cowley et al. (1989) at 200 MeV and Devins et al. (1979) at 100 MeV. The results are compared with DWIA analyses of the same reaction at 197 and 392 MeV from Wakasa et al. (2017) and Noro et al. (2020), which used similar ingredients. The study shows that while the DWIA framework can provide reasonable spectroscopic factors down to 100 MeV, the optimal energy range for $(p,2p)$ reactions is around 400-600 MeV, and the 100 MeV energy represents the lowest limit of validity for the partial-wave DWIA formalism.
