# $Ξ(1690)$ as a $\bar{K} Σ$ molecular state

> We show that a $Ξ^{\ast}$ pole can be dynamically generated near the $\bar{K} Σ$ threshold as an $s$-wave $\bar{K} Σ$ molecular state in a coupled-channels unitary approach with the leading-order chiral interaction. This $Ξ^{\ast}$ state can be identified with the $Ξ(1690)$ resonance with $J^{P} = 1/2^{-}$. We find that the experimental $\bar{K}^{0} Λ$ and $K^{-} Σ^{+}$ mass spectra are qualitatively reproduced with the $Ξ^{\ast}$ state. Moreover we theoretically investigate properties of the dynamically generated $Ξ^{\ast}$ state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\Xi(1690)$ resonance can be dynamically generated as an $s$-wave $\bar{K} \Sigma$ molecular state near the $\bar{K} \Sigma$ threshold, but this generation requires multiple scatterings through coupled channels (especially $\eta \Xi$) because the chiral $\bar{K} \Sigma$ interaction alone is not attractive enough to produce a bound state in a single-channel problem.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channels unitary approach using the Bethe-Salpeter equation in algebraic form with the leading-order chiral (Weinberg-Tomozawa) interaction for $S=-2$ meson-baryon scatterings. They considered six two-body channels ($K^{-} \Sigma^{+}$, $\bar{K}^{0} \Sigma^{0}$, $\bar{K}^{0} \Lambda$, $\pi^{+} \Xi^{-}$, $\pi^{0} \Xi^{0}$, $\eta \Xi^{0}$) for neutral states and similarly six channels for charged states, with subtraction constants as the only model parameters. The scattering amplitude was solved to find pole positions, residues, and compositeness, and the mass spectra were fitted to experimental data from $\Lambda_{c}^{+}$ decay.

## Key numbers

- Pole position for neutral $\Xi(1690)$ with parameter set $\Lambda$: $1682.6 - 0.8 i$ MeV
- Pole position for neutral $\Xi(1690)$ with fitted parameters (Fit): $1684.3 - 0.5 i$ MeV
- $\bar{K} \Sigma$ compositeness for neutral $\Xi(1690)$ with Fit: $X_{K^{-} \Sigma^{+}} = 0.83 - 0.31 i$, $X_{\bar{K}^{0} \Sigma^{0}} = 0.12 + 0.17 i$
- Elementariness $Z$ for neutral $\Xi(1690)$ with Fit: $0.06 + 0.11 i$
- Pole position for charged $\Xi(1690)^{-}$ with Fit: $1693.4 - 10.5 i$ MeV
- Experimental branching fraction ratio $R = \mathcal{B}[\Lambda_{c}^{+} \to \Xi(1690)^{0} K^{+} \to (K^{-} \Sigma^{+}) K^{+}] / \mathcal{B}[\Lambda_{c}^{+} \to \Xi(1690)^{0} K^{+} \to (\bar{K}^{0} \Lambda) K^{+}] = 0.62 \pm 0.33$
- Theoretical $R$ value for Fit: $1.06$
- $\chi^{2} / N_{\rm d.o.f.}$ for Fit: $59.0 / 57$
- $K^{-} \Sigma^{+}$ threshold: $1683.05$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral unitary approach studies of the $S=-2$ sector by Ramos (2002), GarciaRecio (2003), and Gamermann (2011), which obtained $\Xi^{\ast}$ poles including $\Xi(1620)$, $\Xi(1690)$, and $\Xi(1950)$. The paper specifically focuses on the $\Xi(1690)$ resonance near the $\bar{K} \Sigma$ threshold, showing that it can be understood as a $\bar{K} \Sigma$ molecular state rather than a conventional $qqq$ baryon. This interpretation naturally explains the experimentally observed small decay width and the small ratio $\Gamma(\pi \Xi) / \Gamma(\bar{K} \Sigma) < 0.09$, which are difficult to reconcile with a naive quark model picture. The work enables future experimental determination of the branching fraction ratio $R$ to further constrain the $\bar{K} \Sigma$ scattering amplitude and the structure of $\Xi(1690)$.
