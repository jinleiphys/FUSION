# A region of high-spin toroidal isomers

> The combined considerations of both the bulk liquid-drop-type behawior and the quantized angular momentum reveal that high-spin toroidal isomeric states may have general occurrences for light nuclei with 28 <= A <= 48. High-spin N=Z toroidal isomers in this mass region have been located theoretically using cranked self-consistent constraint Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model calculations.

## Key claim

High-spin toroidal isomeric states may have general occurrences for light nuclei with 28 ≤ A ≤ 48, based on the combined considerations of bulk liquid-drop-type behavior and quantized angular momentum. The claim is hedged by the fact that these states have been located theoretically using cranked self-consistent constraint Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model calculations, and the existence of these isomers depends on the specific quantized angular momentum values that arise from single-particle state diagrams.

## Method

The authors used cranked self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with the SkM* force, without pairing, to locate high-spin toroidal isomers in even-even N=Z nuclei from 28Si to 48Cr. They first mapped the energy landscape for axially-symmetric toroidal shapes under quadrupole moment Q20 and angular momentum I constraints, using fine grids in Q20 and non-collective rotations from 0 to 120 ħ. When a local energy minimum was found, the quadrupole constraint was removed and free-convergence was tested to confirm the toroid was a true figure of equilibrium.

## Context

This work extends earlier liquid-drop model predictions by Wong (1978) that rotation about the symmetry axis above a threshold angular momentum can stabilize toroidal nuclei, and previous microscopic work by Ichikawa et al. (2012) that found only a single case of 40Ca(I=60 ħ) as a toroidal isomer. The present systematic search reveals many more toroidal high-spin isomers across the mass region 28 ≤ A ≤ 48, demonstrating their general occurrence. The results enable future experimental searches for these nuclei and may allow extraction of bulk properties of this new type of nuclear fluid, as well as possible utilization as a source of energy from decay to the ground state.
