# Effects of hyperons in binary neutron star mergers

> Numerical simulations for the merger of binary neutron stars are performed in full general relativity incorporating both nucleonic and hyperonic finite-temperature equations of state (EOS) and neutrino cooling for the first time. It is found that even for the hyperonic EOS, a hypermassive neutron star is first formed after the merger for the typical total mass $\approx$ 2.7M\bigodot, and subsequently collapses to a black hole (BH). It is shown that hyperons play a substantial role in the post-merger dynamics, torus formation around the BH, and emission of gravitational waves (GWs). In particular, the existence of hyperons is imprinted in GWs. Therefore, GW observations will provide a potential opportunity to explore the composition of the neutron star matter.

## Key claim

Even for a hyperonic equation of state that softens neutron star matter, a hypermassive neutron star is first formed after the merger of a binary neutron star system with total mass ≈ 2.7 M⊙, and subsequently collapses to a black hole; the presence of hyperons is imprinted in gravitational waves through a time-increasing characteristic frequency, but the adopted hyperonic equation of state cannot produce a stable neutron star with mass as high as 1.97 M⊙ (the mass of PSR J1614-2230).

## Method

Full general-relativistic numerical simulations of equal-mass binary neutron star mergers were performed using the BSSN-puncture formulation with fourth-order finite differencing and a conservative shock-capturing scheme for hydrodynamics. A finite-temperature equation of state including Λ hyperons (Hyp-EOS) was employed alongside a nucleonic equation of state (Shen-EOS) for comparison, and neutrino cooling was treated via a general relativistic leakage scheme for electron neutrinos, electron antineutrinos, and μ/τ neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- Total binary mass ≈ 2.7 M⊙ for the typical merger scenario.
- Maximum mass of zero-temperature spherical neutron stars: M_max,Hyp ≈ 1.8 M⊙ for Hyp-EOS; M_max,Shen ≈ 2.2 M⊙ for Shen-EOS.
- Gravitational mass of single neutron stars in isolation: 1.35 M⊙ and 1.5 M⊙ for Hyp-EOS models (H135 and H15).
- Time from merger to black hole formation: t_BH - t_merge ≈ 11.0 ms for H135, 3.1 ms for H15, and 8.9 ms for S16 (Shen-EOS with 1.6 M⊙).
- Torus mass in quasistationary state: ≈ 0.082 M⊙ for H135, 0.035 M⊙ for H15, and 0.10 M⊙ for S16.
- Gravitational wave amplitude: h_+ ≲ 2 × 10^(-22) for a source at distance D = 100 Mpc.
- Characteristic GW frequency for Shen-EOS models: f_GW ≈ 2.1 kHz for S135 and ≈ 2.5 kHz for S16; for H135, f_GW increases from ≈ 2.0 kHz at t_ret - t_merge = 2 ms to ≈ 2.5 kHz at t_ret - t_merge = 10 ms (a factor of 20–30% increase).

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of binary neutron star mergers with nucleonic equations of state (Sekiguchi 2011) and earlier work on exotic phases in core-collapse supernovae (Nakazato 2008, Sagert 2009, Sumiyoshi 2008) by incorporating Λ hyperons into a finite-temperature equation of state for the first time in merger simulations. The results show that hyperons soften the equation of state, leading to shorter hypermassive neutron star lifetimes and smaller torus masses compared to the nucleonic Shen-EOS, which has implications for short gamma-ray burst models. The finding that the gravitational wave frequency increases with time due to hyperon emergence suggests that future gravitational wave observations with Advanced LIGO or LCGT could distinguish between nucleonic and hyperonic compositions of neutron star matter, though the adopted equation of state cannot reproduce the 1.97 M⊙ neutron star mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010).
