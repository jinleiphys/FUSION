# Electron Dynamics in Neutron Scattering with Hydrogen Atoms

> In neutron-proton (n-p) scattering experiments, gas targets have been used to measure scattering length by detecting neutrons and recoil protons. Changes in electron dynamics within the gas target have a negligible effect on dynamics of neutrons and protons. However, electron dynamics are sensitive to the specific form of the n-p interaction during the scattering process, providing additional information to derive parameters in nuclear interaction models. We propose a theoretical approach to obtain these parameters from the momentum spectrum of ionized electrons within a hydrogen atomic gas target. This approach is based on a three-body scattering involving a neutron, a proton and an electron. We model the n-p interaction as the Yukawa potential and obtain the momentum spectrum of ionized electrons through the solution of the Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation. Electron dynamics exhibit significant differences at various potential parameters. These parameters can be determined by comparing numerical calculations with experimental results. Moreover, this approach offers insights into detecting ultrafast scattering processes.

## Key claim

The momentum spectrum of ionized electrons from a hydrogen gas target is sensitive to the force range parameter of the Yukawa potential used to model the singlet neutron-proton interaction, and this parameter can be determined by comparing numerical calculations with experimental results, provided the incident effective kinetic energy is fixed at 1.078 MeV.

## Method

The authors model the three-body system (neutron, proton, electron) in Jacobi coordinates and adopt a semi-classical approach: Newton's second law governs the classical motion of the reduced nucleus (neutron and hydrogen atom), while the Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation (TDSE) describes the electron's wave function. The n-p interaction is taken as the Yukawa potential with two parameters (force range λ and field strength V₀), and the electron's ionized momentum spectrum is obtained by Fourier transforming the ionized wave function after subtracting bound-state contributions. The total spectrum is aggregated over impact parameters b from -R₀ to R₀.

## Key numbers

- Incident effective kinetic energy fixed at 1.078 MeV for all calculations.
- Force range λ values studied: 0.8 fm, 1.0 fm, 1.2 fm, and 1.4 fm.
- Upper limit of force range estimated as λ_max = ħ/(m_π c) ≈ 1.4 fm, based on the pion mass.
- Reduced mass ratio μ_e/μ_N ≈ 1/918, justifying the semi-classical treatment.
- The ratio α = m_e/(m_p + m_e) = 1/1837, used in the Jacobi coordinate transformation.
- The scattering process time duration is less than one zeptosecond (10⁻²¹ s).

## Context

This work extends the study of low-energy (below 5 MeV) singlet n-p scattering, where the Yukawa potential (Yukawa, 1935) is the standard model for the ¹S₀ interaction. Prior experiments using gas targets (Huhn, 2000; Johansson, 2005) measured scattering lengths from neutron and proton dynamics but could not determine the force range from cross-section alone. The authors propose using the COLTRIMS technique (Doerner, 2000; Ullrich, 2003) to measure the electron momentum spectrum, which provides additional constraints. This approach enables indirect probing of ultrafast nuclear scattering processes on timescales below a zeptosecond, beyond the reach of current attosecond pulse techniques (Paul, 2001; Hentschel, 2001).
