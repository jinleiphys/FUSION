# Confinement contains condensates

> Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and its connection with the generation of hadron masses has historically been viewed as a vacuum phenomenon. We argue that confinement makes such a position untenable. If quark-hadron duality is a reality in QCD, then condensates, those quantities that were commonly viewed as constant empirical mass-scales that fill all spacetime, are instead wholly contained within hadrons; viz., they are a property of hadrons themselves and expressed, e.g., in their Bethe-Salpeter or light-front wave functions. We explain that this paradigm is consistent with empirical evidence, and incidentally expose misconceptions in a recent Comment.

## Key claim

Confinement makes the traditional view of QCD condensates as spacetime-independent vacuum constants untenable; instead, condensates are wholly contained within hadrons and expressed in their Bethe-Salpeter or light-front wave functions, a paradigm that is consistent with all empirical evidence.

## Method

The authors derive exact QCD identities showing that the chiral-limit vacuum quark condensate is equivalent to the chiral-limit value of the in-pseudoscalar-meson condensate, obtained from gauge-invariant hadron-to-vacuum transition amplitudes. They analyze the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation to demonstrate that the connection between pion mass and a vacuum quark condensate is a theoretical artifice, and they examine a mean-field soliton model to expose inconsistencies in the traditional vacuum-condensate picture.

## Key numbers

- The zero-momentum dynamical quark mass and effective gluon mass satisfy \(M(0) \lesssim m_g(0) =: m_{\rm c} \sim 2\Lambda_{\rm QCD}-3 \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), giving a containment radius \(r_{\rm c} \sim 1/m_{\rm c}\)
- The critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration at zero temperature is \(\mu_{\rm cr} \approx 0.3\,{\rm GeV}\)
- Vacuum saturation for the four-quark condensate underestimates the correct result by approximately 65%

## Context

This work extends the light-front framework arguments of Casher (1974) and Brodsky (2008, 2009, 2010) that DCSB must be a property of hadron wave functions, not the vacuum. It contradicts the traditional Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov sum-rule approach (1979) where condensates are treated as universal vacuum parameters, and it directly refutes the critique by Reinhardt and Weigel (2012) regarding soliton models. The in-hadron condensate paradigm removes the problem of QCD condensates contributing a cosmological constant \(10^{46}\) times too large, and it enables a unified Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of meson and baryon spectra, form factors, and distribution functions.
