# Hadron Resonance Gas Equation of State from Lattice QCD

> The Monte Carlo results in lattice QCD for the pressure and energy density at small temperature $T < 155$ MeV and zero baryonic chemical potential are analyzed within the hadron resonance gas model. Two extensions of the ideal hadron resonance gas are considered: the excluded volume model which describes a repulsion of hadrons at short distances and Hagedorn model with the exponential mass spectrum. Considering both of these models one by one we do not find the conclusive evidences in favor of any of them. The controversial results appear because of rather different sensitivities of the pressure and energy density to both excluded volume and Hagedorn mass spectrum effects. On the other hand, we have found a clear evidence for a simultaneous presence of both of them. They lead to rather essential contributions: suppression effects for thermodynamical functions of the hadron resonance gas due to the excluded volume effects and enhancement due to the Hagedorn mass spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper finds that neither the excluded volume model nor the Hagedorn mass spectrum alone can be conclusively established from lattice QCD data at T < 155 MeV, but when both effects are included simultaneously they clearly improve the agreement with lattice results for pressure and energy density.

## Method

The authors compared lattice QCD Monte Carlo results for pressure and energy density at zero baryonic chemical potential and T < 155 MeV (from Borsanyi et al., 2010) with three variants of the hadron resonance gas model: ideal HRG, excluded volume HRG with a common hard-core radius r for all hadrons, and EV-HRG augmented by an exponential Hagedorn mass spectrum with a single free parameter C. They performed chi-squared fits of the model calculations to the lattice data points for both p/T^4 and epsilon/T^4 simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for 3-flavor QCD is sigma_SB = 19*pi^2/12 ≈ 15.6, giving p_SB/T^4 ≈ 5.2
- A hard-core radius r ≥ 0.13 fm is required to keep the EV-HRG pressure below the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at all T > T_c
- For the EV-HRG model alone, r = 0.3 fm gives chi^2_p/N_df ≈ 0.4 for pressure but chi^2_epsilon/N_df ≈ 2.5 for energy density
- In the combined EV-HRG-H model, chi^2/N_df ≤ 1 is achieved for r ≤ 0.4 fm and C ≤ 0.2 GeV^(3/2)
- The Hagedorn mass spectrum parameters used are M_0 = 2 GeV, T_H = 160 MeV, m_0 = 0.5 GeV, and a = 5/4

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of HRG models with lattice QCD data, where the excluded volume model (studied by Andronic et al., 2012 and others) and the Hagedorn mass spectrum (studied by Noronha-Hostler et al., 2012 and others) were treated separately. The key advance is showing that neither effect alone can be clearly established from the lattice data, but their simultaneous inclusion resolves the tension between pressure and energy density fits. This enables improved equations of state for hydrodynamic modeling of nucleus-nucleus collisions and provides constraints on the hadron hard-core radius and Hagedorn spectrum parameters from lattice QCD.
