# Diagrammatic ab initio methods for infinite nuclear matter with modern chiral interactions

> A comparative study of the equation of state for pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter is presented using three ab initio methods based on diagrammatic expansions: coupled-cluster theory, self-consistent Green's functions, and many-body perturbation theory. We critically evaluate these methods by employing different chiral potentials at next-to-next-to-leading-order -- all of which include both two- and three-nucleon contributions -- and by exploring various many-body truncations. Our investigation yields highly precise results for pure neutron matter and robust predictions for symmetric nuclear matter, particularly with soft interactions. Moreover, the new calculations demonstrate that the $\rm{ NNLO_{sat} }(450)$ and $Δ\rm{NNLO_{go}}(394)$ potentials are consistent with the empirical constraints on the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. Additionally, this benchmark study reveals that diagrammatic expansions with similar architectures lead to consistent many-body correlations, even when applied across different methods. This consistency underscores the robustness of the diagrammatic approach in capturing the essential physics of nucleonic systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three diagrammatic ab initio methods (coupled-cluster, self-consistent Green's functions, and many-body perturbation theory) yield consistent results for infinite nuclear matter when using modern chiral interactions, but the agreement is significantly better for pure neutron matter than for symmetric nuclear matter, particularly with harder potentials where triples corrections become substantial.

## Method

The authors perform benchmark calculations of the equation of state for both pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature using three diagrammatic methods: coupled-cluster theory at the CCD and CCD(T) levels, self-consistent Green's functions with the ADC(3) and ADC(3)-D truncations, and many-body perturbation theory at third order [MBPT(3)]. All calculations employ a finite model space with periodic boundary conditions (66 neutrons for PNM, 132 nucleons for SNM) and use chiral potentials at next-to-next-to-leading order that include both two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions, with three-nucleon forces incorporated via the normal-ordered two-body approximation.

## Key numbers

- For PNM with NNLOsat(450), correlation energies increase in magnitude from approximately -1 MeV per nucleon at ρ = 0.04 fm⁻³ to about -2.5 MeV per nucleon at ρ = 0.20 fm⁻³, with differences between methods of at most a few hundred keV
- For SNM with ΔNNLOgo(394), the triples correction contributes about -0.8 MeV per nucleon around saturation density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³)
- For SNM with ΔNNLO(500), the triples correction is approximately -2.3 MeV per nucleon at ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, increasing to -3.3 MeV per nucleon at ρ = 0.20 fm⁻³
- For SNM with ΔNNLOgo(394), ADC(3) and CCD(T) differ by about 200 keV or less around saturation density
- Matter densities between 0.04 and 0.28 fm⁻³ are considered across all calculations

## Context

This work extends previous benchmark studies of infinite nuclear matter (Baldo 2012, Piarulli 2020, Lovato 2022) by providing a systematic comparison between coupled-cluster theory, self-consistent Green's functions, and many-body perturbation theory using the same finite model space discretization. The results enable future studies to quantify many-body uncertainties separately from interaction uncertainties in chiral effective field theory, and demonstrate that the NNLOsat(450) and ΔNNLOgo(394) potentials are consistent with empirical constraints on the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter, building on prior work by the Goteborg and Oak Ridge groups (NNLOsat 2015, DeltaGo 2020).
