# Gapless color superconductivity at zero and at finite temperature

> We present a detailed study of the recently proposed gapless color superconductivity in neutral two-flavor quark matter in beta-equilibrium at zero as well as at finite temperature. We clarify the nature of the fermionic quasiparticles, and discuss the physical properties of this gapless phase of quark matter. The appearance of two "secondary" spin-1 condensates in the gapless ground state is pointed out. The gluon screening properties are also discussed. It is found that finite temperature properties of the gapless color superconductor are rather unusual. One of the most striking results is that the ratio of the critical temperature to the gap at zero temperature is not a universal number. Moreover, this ratio could become arbitrarily large at some values of the coupling constant. In addition, the value of the gap parameter is not a monotonic function of temperature, although the phase transition is a second order phase transition. In an extreme case, in fact, a nonzero finite temperature gap can appear even if its zero temperature value is vanishing.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in neutral two-flavor quark matter under beta-equilibrium, a gapless color superconducting (g2SC) phase exists where the ratio of the critical temperature to the zero-temperature gap is not a universal constant and can become arbitrarily large for certain coupling strengths, contrary to the standard BCS expectation.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, with a momentum cutoff Λ = 653.3 MeV and scalar coupling G_S = 5.0163 GeV⁻², setting the diquark coupling G_D = η G_S (with η typically 0.75). They enforce electrical and color charge neutrality conditions alongside the gap equations for the diquark condensate Δ and the constituent quark mass m (taken in the chiral limit m₀ = 0). The thermodynamic potential is evaluated at zero and finite temperature, and the quasiparticle dispersion relations are analyzed to identify gapless modes when the mismatch parameter δμ exceeds Δ.

## Key numbers

- The quark chemical potential μ is taken in the range 350 to 450 MeV, with a representative value μ = 400 MeV used for numerical results.
- The electrical chemical potential in the normal neutral phase is μ_e^(1) ≈ 0.2196 μ, giving 87.84 MeV at μ = 400 MeV.
- The intersection of the neutrality line with Δ = δμ occurs at μ_e^(2) ≈ (3/5)μ = 240 MeV and Δ^(2) ≈ (3/10)μ = 120 MeV at μ = 400 MeV.
- For η = 0.75 at μ = 400 MeV, the lower branch of the gap equation exists for μ_e between approximately 107.9 MeV (μ_e^(min)) and 205.3 MeV (μ_e^(max)).
- The difference in densities of pairing quarks at zero temperature in the g2SC phase is given by (n_dg - n_ur)|_(T=0) = θ(δμ - Δ) * (2/(3π²)) * √((δμ)² - Δ²) * (3μ̄² + (δμ)² - Δ²).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal of gapless color superconductivity (Huang and Shovkovy, 2003) by providing a detailed finite-temperature analysis and clarifying the nature of quasiparticles. It contrasts with the conventional 2SC phase where the ratio T_c/Δ is universal (as in BCS theory) and where pairing quark densities are equal (enforced pairing, as argued by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek). The results enable future studies of g2SC matter in protoneutron star evolution and suggest that mixed phases (as considered by Buballa, Shovkovy, and others) may compete with the homogeneous g2SC phase depending on surface tension.
