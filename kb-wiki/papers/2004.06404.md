# Study of $Δ$ excitations in medium-mass nuclei with peripheral heavy ion charge-exchange reactions

> Isobaric single charge-exchange reactions, changing nuclear charges by one unit but leaving the mass partitions unaffected, have been for the first time investigated by peripheral collisions of $^{112}$Sn ions accelerated up to 1\textit{A} GeV at the GSI facilities. The high-resolving power of the FRS spectrometer allows us to obtain $(p, n)$-type isobaric charge-exchange cross sections with an uncertainty of $3.5\%$ and to separate quasi-elastic and inelastic components in the missing-energy spectra of the ejectiles. The inelastic component is associated to the excitation of the $Δ$(1232) isobar resonance and the emission of pions in s-wave both in the target and projectile nucleus, while the quasi-elastic contribution is associated to the nuclear spin-isospin response of nucleon-hole excitations. An apparent shift of the $Δ$-resonance peak of $\sim$63 MeV is observed when comparing the missing-energy spectra obtained from the measurements with proton and carbon targets. A detailed analysis, performed with a theoretical model for the reactions, indicates that this observation can be simply interpreted as a change in the relative magnitude between the contribution of the excitation of the resonance in the target and in the projectile.

## Key claim

The apparent shift of the Delta-resonance peak of approximately 63 MeV between proton and carbon targets is not a genuine in-medium effect but can be interpreted as a change in the relative magnitude between the contribution of the resonance excitation in the target and in the projectile, as shown by a theoretical model for the reactions.

## Method

Isobaric single charge-exchange reactions were induced by peripheral collisions of 112Sn ions at 1A GeV on polyethylene (CH2) and carbon targets at the GSI facilities, using the FRS zero-degree magnetic spectrometer to identify ejectile fragments and measure their momenta. The missing-energy spectra were obtained from the measured momentum of residual nuclei, with the carbon-target contribution subtracted from the CH2 data to isolate the proton-target component. A theoretical model based on response function theory in the local density approximation, including quasi-elastic nucleon-hole-nucleon-particle excitations and inelastic nucleon-hole-Delta-particle excitations mediated by pi- and rho-meson exchange, was used to analyze the spectra.

## Key numbers

* Isobaric charge-exchange cross sections were obtained with an uncertainty of 3.5 percent.
* The FRS spectrometer had a resolving power of B rho / Delta B rho = 1500 and an angular acceptance of plus or minus 15 mrad.
* The missing-energy spectrum resolution was approximately 10 MeV, a factor of 2 better than obtained at SATURNE experiments.
* An apparent shift of the Delta-resonance peak of approximately 63 MeV was observed between the missing-energy spectra from proton and carbon targets.
* The dispersion at the middle focal plane was D_{F2} = -7.48 plus or minus 0.02 cm per percent.
* The identification resolution achieved was Delta Z / Z = 6 times 10^{-3} and Delta A / A = 1 times 10^{-3} (FWHM).
* The charged pion emission threshold opens at omega_N approximately 139 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of Delta-resonance excitation in nuclear matter from light-ion reactions, such as the (3He,t) measurements at SATURNE by Contardo et al. (1986) and (p,n) reactions by Bonner et al. (1978) and Gaarde et al. (1991), to medium-mass nuclei using peripheral heavy-ion charge-exchange reactions. The results enable future investigations of in-medium properties of nucleon resonances and their role in three-body forces, Gamow-Teller strength quenching, and neutron star matter composition, with the theoretical framework providing a basis for analyzing similar reactions at facilities like FAIR.
