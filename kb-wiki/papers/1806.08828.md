# Astrophysical factors of ${}^{12}{\rm C}+{}^{12}{\rm C}$ fusion from Trojan horse method

> Carbon-carbon burning plays an important role in many stellar environments. Recently, using the indirect Trojan horse method A. Tumino {\em et al.} reported [Nature {\bf 557} 687 (2018)] a strong rise of the astrophysical factor for the carbon-carbon fusion at low resonance energies. In this paper, we demonstrate that this rise is the artifact of using an invalid plane-wave approximation. It is shown that the calculated renormalization factor decreases the astrophysical factor from [A. Tumino {\em et al.}, Nature {\bf 557} 687 (2018)] at the resonance energies of $E=0.8-0.9$ MeV by as much as $\approx 10^{3}$ times.

## Key claim

The strong rise of the astrophysical factor for carbon-carbon fusion at low resonance energies reported by Tumino et al. (2018) is an artifact of using an invalid plane-wave approximation; when Coulomb distorted waves are properly included via distorted-wave Born approximation, the renormalization factor decreases the astrophysical factor at resonance energies of 0.8–0.9 MeV by as much as approximately 10³ times.

## Method

The authors analyzed the Trojan horse method (THM) reaction ¹⁴N + ¹²C → d + ²⁴Mg* using distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) with Coulomb distorted waves and Coulomb plus nuclear distorted waves in the initial and final states, calculated with the FRESCO code. They derived a renormalization factor R(E) by taking the ratio of the zero-range DWBA differential cross section at the normalization energy of 2.664 MeV to that at lower resonance energies, and applied this factor to the astrophysical S*-factors reported by Tumino et al. (2018).

## Key numbers

- The renormalization factor decreases the THM astrophysical factors from Tumino et al. (2018) at resonance energies of E = 0.8–0.9 MeV by as much as ≈ 10³ times.
- The THM experiment was performed at a relative ¹⁴N–¹²C energy of E_aA = 13.845 MeV.
- The normalization interval chosen by Tumino et al. (2018) was E = 2.5–2.63 MeV, with the normalization energy used in this work being E = 2.664 MeV.
- For the resonance at E_(R)¹²C¹²C⁽⁰⁾ = 2.664 MeV, the relative kinetic energy of the deuteron and the center of mass of the p + ²³Na system is E_d²⁴Mg = 0.9 MeV, well below the Coulomb barrier of about 3 MeV.
- The reaction rate calculated by Tumino et al. (2018) at temperature T ~ 2 × 10⁸ K exceeds the adopted value from Fowler and Iliadis by a factor of 500.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the findings of Tumino et al. (2018), who reported a strong rise in the astrophysical factor for ¹²C + ¹²C fusion using the Trojan horse method with a plane-wave approximation. The authors demonstrate that the plane-wave approximation is invalid for this reaction because the deuteron energies are well below the Coulomb barrier, requiring proper treatment of Coulomb distorted waves. This correction enables more reliable determination of carbon-carbon fusion rates for astrophysical scenarios, though the authors note that the extrapolation of the S*-factor beyond E = 3.56 MeV as done in a related preprint is difficult to justify.
