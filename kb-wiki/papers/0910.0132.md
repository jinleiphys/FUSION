# Novel features of nuclear forces and shell evolution in exotic nuclei

> Novel simple properties of the monopole component of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction are presented, leading to the so-called monopole-based universal interaction. Shell structures are shown to change as functions of $N$ and $Z$ consistently with experiments. Some key cases of this shell evolution are discussed, clarifying the effects of central and tensor forces. The validity of the present tensor force is examined in terms of the low-momentum interaction V$_{low k}$ and the Q$_{box}$ formalism.

## Key claim

The monopole component of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction can be decomposed into a flat, global contribution from a Gaussian central force and local variations from the tensor force, together forming the "monopole-based universal interaction" (V_MU). The abstract glosses over the condition that this decomposition works specifically for the monopole component and that the central force parameters were tuned to reproduce the GXPF1A interaction's monopole matrix elements.

## Method

The authors analyzed the monopole matrix elements of realistic shell-model interactions (GXPF1A for the pf-shell and SDPF-M for the sd-shell) and compared them to a G-matrix interaction and a tensor force from π+ρ meson exchange. They subtracted the tensor-force contribution from the realistic interactions and found the residual to be nearly flat, which they reproduced with a Gaussian central force with range μ=1.0 fm and fitted strengths. The resulting V_MU interaction was then applied to calculate single-particle energy evolution across several isotopic chains, and the tensor force's persistence was tested using low-momentum interactions (V_low k) and Q_box folded-diagram calculations starting from the AV8' bare interaction.

## Key numbers

- For T=0 monopole matrix elements in the pf-shell, GXPF1A values range from -2.5 to -1 MeV; for T=1 they range from -0.3 to 0.2 MeV.
- The Gaussian central force uses μ=1.0 fm with strengths f_{0,0}=f_{1,0}=166 MeV for T=0 channels.
- For T=1, the Gaussian central force uses f_{0,1}=0.6×f_{0,0} and f_{1,1}=-0.8×f_{0,0}.
- The f_{7/2}-f_{5/2} splitting in Ni isotopes is reduced by 2 MeV from N=40 to N=50.
- For the AV8' tensor force, V_low k with cutoff Λ=2.1 fm^{-1} gives monopole matrix elements very close to the bare tensor contribution.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion of tensor-force-driven shell evolution by Otsuka et al. (2005) and the monopole interaction formalism of Bansal and French (1964) and Poves and Zuker (1981). It introduces a unified description where the central force provides the smooth monopole background while the tensor force generates local variations, enabling predictions of shell evolution from stable nuclei to exotic systems such as the N=20 gap evolution from Z=8 to 20, the Ni isotopic chain from ⁶⁸Ni to ⁷⁸Ni, and the Zr-Sn region from ⁹⁰Zr to ¹⁰⁰Sn. The work enables future full shell-model calculations using V_MU and tests of its multipole components.
