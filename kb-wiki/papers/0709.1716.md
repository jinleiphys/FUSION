# Consistent Determination of Particle Production in p-A, d-A and A-A Collisions using Color Glass Condensate

> The success of nonviscous hydrodynamics in describing the collective flow properties of bulk low $p_\perp$ observables at RHIC has led to the claim that a novel form of {\it strongly coupled} Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) is created in 200 AGeV Au+Au collisions. This success depends strongly, however, on the initial conditions assumed in the calculations. In particular, agreement with data is only obtained assuming Glauber nuclear reaction plane (participant) geometry. The KLN model of Color Glass Condenstate (CGC) initial conditions require the existence of nonvanishing viscous effects. We develop an improved model of CGC that is more internally consistent between the central and diffuse edge regions. The improved model is shown to describe bulk rapidity distributions for a wide assortment of system sizes, geometries and energies. The self consistency forces a specific $\sqrt{s}$ dependence of the multiplicity which leads to surprisingly low particle production predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC COM energies. These predictions are similar to those made using simple linear extrapolations of multiplicity attempted by the PHOBOS collaboration.

# Consistent Determination of Particle Production in p-A, d-A and A-A Collisions using Color Glass Condensate

## Key claim

The improved Color Glass Condensate model (elfKLN) can describe bulk rapidity distributions across multiple system sizes, geometries, and energies, but only if the saturation momentum is defined locally and universally rather than via participant density; this self-consistency forces a specific sqrt(s) dependence of multiplicity that yields surprisingly low particle production predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, similar to simple linear extrapolations by the PHOBOS collaboration.

## Method

The authors extend the factorized KLN (fKLN) approach by implementing a local density evolution of the saturation momentum Q_s that depends only on the Glauber thickness function T_A of each nucleus, not on the partner nucleus. They compute charged particle multiplicities using k_T-factorized unintegrated gluon distribution functions with a Lorentzian form, converting the p-p production formula into a local transverse space density by dividing by the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma_NN, then integrating over the transverse plane for nuclear collisions. The model includes a self-consistent high-x depletion factor (1-x)^n in both the unintegrated gluon distributions and the determination of Q_s, with parameters fixed to reproduce UA5 p-pbar data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section set to sigma_NN = 42 mb at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and 70 mb at sqrt(s)=5500 GeV
- Lambda = 0.288 and K = 0.56 in the integrated gluon distribution function to reproduce average Q_s,A^2(x=0.01) ~ 2 GeV^2
- Kappa ~ 0.02 set to reproduce UA5 p-pbar data
- Effective gluon mass m_g ~ 0.35 GeV used in the rapidity-to-pseudorapidity Jacobian
- For Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the model reproduces dN_ch/deta across centrality classes from BRAHMS data, with the per-participant-pair production extrapolating correctly to the p-p limit as N_Part/2 approaches 1
- For Pb+Pb at sqrt(s)=5500 GeV, predictions are lower than previous saturation models by approximately a factor of 70/42 ~ 1.66 due to the inclusion of the 1/sigma_NN factor
- For d-Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the model under-predicts absolute multiplicities but reproduces the shape of the rapidity distribution; after dividing by participant pairs, agreement improves significantly across centralities
- For p-Pb at sqrt(s)=5500 GeV, predicted charged particle production is smaller by a factor of about 2 compared to conventional KLN model predictions

## Context

This work extends the factorized KLN (fKLN) model introduced by Drescher et al. (2006) to create the "extended local" fKLN (elfKLN) model, addressing the non-universality problem in the original KLN model by Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2004) where Q_s was defined proportional to participant density and dropped to zero at the nuclear edge. The model contradicts the claim that CGC initial conditions require viscous effects to describe RHIC data (Hirano et al., 2005), instead showing that a consistent treatment of the nuclear edge yields better agreement. The results enable predictions for LHC Pb+Pb and p-Pb collisions that are significantly lower than previous CGC-based estimates, and the authors note that a full description of asymmetric collisions likely requires a fully fluctuating Monte Carlo model such as MCKLN (Drescher et al., 2006).
