# Chiral model for $\bar{K}N$ interactions and its pole content

> We use chirally motivated effective meson-baryon potentials to describe the low energy $\bar{K}N$ data including the characteristics of kaonic hydrogen. Our results are examined in comparison with other approaches based on the unitarity and dispersion relation for the inverse of the T-matrix. We demonstrate that the movements of the poles generated by the model upon varying the model parameters can serve as a tool to get additional insights on the dynamics of the strongly coupled $πΣ$-$\bar{K}N$ system.

## Key claim

Chirally motivated coupled-channel potentials generate not only two isoscalar poles related to the Λ(1405) resonance but also two isovector poles, one of which (the πΣ isovector pole) is reported for the first time; however, the authors note that the inclusion of next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections spoils the agreement among models on the position of the second isoscalar pole and may shift it above the K⁻p threshold, questioning which pole dominates the subthreshold physics.

## Method

The authors construct chirally motivated separable potentials for coupled meson-baryon channels (πΛ, πΣ, K̄N, ηΛ, ηΣ, KΞ) with coupling coefficients C_ij derived from chiral SU(3) symmetry, and fit the inverse-range parameters α_i to low-energy K̄N data including K⁻p cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and kaonic hydrogen characteristics. They then study the pole trajectories on the complex energy plane by scaling the nondiagonal interchannel couplings from zero (the zero-coupling limit) to the physical value, assigning each pole to a specific channel based on its origin.

## Key numbers

- For the WT1 model (leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction, f_π = 107 MeV, α = 650 MeV): kaonic hydrogen shift ΔE_N = 366 eV, width Γ = 696 eV; branching ratios γ = 2.366, R_c = 0.636, R_n = 0.188.
- Experimental kaonic hydrogen values (DEAR): ΔE_N = 193(43) eV, Γ = 249(150) eV.
- Isoscalar poles for WT1 model: z₁ = (1360, -54) MeV, z₂ = (1431, -21) MeV on the [-,+] Riemann sheet.
- In the zero-coupling limit (x=0): πΣ isoscalar pole at z = (1357 - i 85) MeV (resonant); K̄N isoscalar pole at z = 1433 MeV (bound state, below K̄N threshold).
- Isovector poles for WT1 model at physical coupling (x=1): πΣ pole at z = (1393 - i 189) MeV on [-,+] sheet; K̄N pole at z = (1401 - i 33) MeV on [+,-] sheet.

## Context

This work extends the chirally motivated coupled-channel approach to K̄N interactions previously developed by the authors (Cieply and Smejkal, 2010) and compares with leading-order-only models such as those of Jido et al. (2003) and Hyodo and Weise (2008), as well as NLO models like BNW (Borasoy et al., 2005). The pole-trajectory analysis using the zero-coupling limit provides a new tool to identify the channel origin of each pole, which can help resolve the long-standing ambiguity about the nature of the Λ(1405) resonance and guide interpretation of upcoming SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data.
