# Moment of inertia for pair rotation: Interplay between order parameter and shell structure

> [Background] Pair condensation in finite nuclei generates a collective motion known as pair rotation. The moment of inertia of pair rotation (P-MoI) has been used as an indicator of pair condensation. [Purpose] We aim to elucidate the fundamental properties of the P-MoI, particularly its dependence on the particle number and the order parameter. [Method] The P-MoI was evaluated using the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) calculations with a monopole pairing Hamiltonian and Skyrme density functional theory calculations with different density-dependent pairing energy density functionals. [Results] In open-shell nuclei, a negative correlation was found between the P-MoI and the pair amplitude, which is the order parameter for the transition from the normal phase to the superconducting phase. Analysis based on the decomposition of the P-MoI into the orbital contributions within the BCS approximation shows that its orbital dependence is very similar to that of the pairing gap. [Conclusions] The P-MoI reflects the influence of both the level density near the Fermi energy and the pair amplitude.

## Key claim

The moment of inertia for pair rotation (P-MoI) shows a negative correlation with the pair amplitude (the order parameter) in open-shell nuclei, which is opposite to the positive correlation between deformation and the moment of inertia for spatial rotation, and this behavior arises from the interplay between the single-particle level density near the Fermi energy and the pairing gap.

## Method

The authors evaluated the P-MoI using the three-point finite-difference formula from ground-state energies of even-even nuclei, computed with both the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approximation for a monopole pairing Hamiltonian and Skyrme density functional theory (DFT) calculations with volume, mixed, and surface types of density-dependent pairing energy density functionals. They analyzed the P-MoI for neutron pairing in proton-magic Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes, and performed orbital decomposition of the P-MoI within the BCS approximation to understand its dependence on the pair amplitude and level density.

## Key numbers

- The pairing force strength for the BCS Hamiltonian was set to \(G = 27.7/A\) MeV for Ni, \(22.5/A\) MeV for Sn, and \(18.9/A\) MeV for Pb isotopes, fitted to experimental odd-even mass staggering in \(^{68}\)Ni, \(^{116}\)Sn, and \(^{188}\)Pb.
- The Skyrme DFT pairing strengths \(V_0\) for volume-type pairing were \(-226.5\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Ni), \(-177.3\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Sn), and \(-185.5\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Pb); for mixed-type pairing, \(-331.0\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Ni), \(-267.9\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Sn), and \(-290.0\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Pb); for surface-type pairing, \(-414.0\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Ni), \(-431.0\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Sn), and \(-530\) MeV fm\(^3\) (Pb).
- In the open-shell nucleus \(^{110}\)Sn, the experimental P-MoI constrains the pair amplitude to within about 10% difference across different density-dependent pairing EDFs.
- For the closed-subshell nucleus \(^{120}\)Sn, the Skyrme DFT model with SLy4 parameters could not reproduce the experimental P-MoI with any type of pairing EDF.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of pair rotation as the Nambu-Goldstone mode associated with spontaneous gauge symmetry breaking in finite nuclei, building on the analogy between spatial rotation and gauge-space rotation discussed by Brink and Broglia (2005) and Broglia (2000). The P-MoI, which is equivalent to the inverse of the two-nucleon empirical shell gap studied by Lunney et al. (2003), provides an observable free from the ambiguities of odd-even mass staggering that affect comparisons of pairing gaps in DFT calculations (Bender et al., 2000). The results enable the use of P-MoI in open-shell superfluid nuclei to constrain the density dependence of pairing energy density functionals, as previously explored by Hinohara (2016, 2018) and Reinhard (2017, 2024), and suggest that open-shell nuclei are more suitable than closed-subshell nuclei for determining pairing coupling constants.
