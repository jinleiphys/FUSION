# Transverse and longitudinal spin alignment from color fields in heavy ion collisions

> We analyze the spin alignment of vector mesons stemming from spin correlation of the quark and antiquark induced by background color fields in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The quark-coalescence equation relating the collision kernel of the vector-meson kinetic equation to spin alignment is expanded to the relativistic case. Focusing on the color-octet contribution, the spin alignment for $ϕ$ mesons from glasma fields with momentum dependence is investigated, where the scenarios for different spin quantization axes are considered. Moreover, we qualitatively analyze the spin alignment from isotropic color fields in the quark gluon plasma phase for comparison. In particular, we propose that the experimental measurement of spin alignment along the beam direction, dubbed as the longitudinal spin alignment, could be useful to identify the dominance of longitudinal spin correlation potentially led by the glasma effect in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the experimentally measurable longitudinal spin alignment (along the beam direction) of vector mesons could serve as a specific probe to identify the dominance of longitudinal spin correlation potentially caused by glasma fields in high-energy nuclear collisions, rather than being a definitive signature of such fields.

## Method

The authors expand the quark-coalescence equation relating the collision kernel of the vector-meson kinetic equation to spin alignment to the relativistic case, lifting previous non-relativistic and equal-mass approximations. They then evaluate the color-octet contribution to the spin alignment of ϕ mesons from glasma fields with momentum dependence, considering different spin quantization axes, and qualitatively compare this with spin alignment from isotropic color fields in the quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Key numbers

- The spin alignment of ϕ mesons from glasma fields is estimated to yield ρ₀₀ < 1/3 at small transverse momenta, potentially explaining the magnitude observed at LHC.
- The non-relativistic approximation used in prior work (Kumar 2023) violates momentum conservation, which the present work corrects by deriving a general expression applicable for m_q ≠ m_¯q.
- The spin relaxation time τ^o_R is introduced as a constant parameter characterizing the suppression of the glasma-induced spin correlation from collisions in the QGP phase.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kumar 2023) of spin alignment for non-relativistic ϕ mesons from glasma fields to the relativistic case with proper momentum conservation. It contrasts with the proposal by Sheng (2022) that late-time fluctuating vector-meson fields describe global spin alignment data at RHIC energies, and with the turbulent color field proposal by Muller (2021) that also emphasizes late-time QGP effects. The paper enables future experimental discrimination between early-time glasma effects and late-time QGP effects on spin alignment through momentum-dependent measurements.
