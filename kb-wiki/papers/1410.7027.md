# On the extraction of the intrinsic light-quark sea in the proton

> The HERMES collaboration recently reported a reevaluation of the strange-quark parton distribution, $S(x)$, based on kaon production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering. Two distinct results on $S(x)$ at the $x> 0.1$ region, one with a sizable magnitude and another with a vanishing content, were reported. We show that the latter result is due to a particular assumption adopted in the analysis. The impact of the new HERMES $S(x)$ result on the extraction of intrinsic light-quark sea in the proton is discussed. Given the large uncertainty in the kaon fragmentation function, we find that the latest HERMES data do not exclude the existence of a significant intrinsic strange-quark sea in the proton. The $x$ dependence of the $(s+\bar s)/(\bar u+ \bar d)$ ratio is also in qualitative agreement with the presence of intrinsic strange-quark sea.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the HERMES collaboration's result of a vanishing strange-quark distribution \(S(x)\) at \(x > 0.1\) is not robust; it arises from a specific assumption in the analysis that neglects a small but non-negligible term in the extraction formula. Given the large uncertainty in kaon fragmentation functions, the latest HERMES data do not exclude the existence of a significant intrinsic strange-quark sea in the proton.

## Method

The authors reanalyze the HERMES charged-kaon multiplicity data from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) on a deuteron target using Eq. (2), which relates the strange-quark distribution \(S(x) = s(x) + \bar s(x)\) to the measured kaon multiplicity and the kaon fragmentation functions \(D_Q^K\) and \(D_S^K\). They compare three extraction methods: (1) using the DSS kaon fragmentation functions from a global fit (HERMES2014-set1); (2) assuming \(S(x) \approx 0\) for \(x > 0.1\) to extract \(\int D_Q^K dz\) from the data (HERMES2014-set2); and (3) a new approach (HERMES2014-set3) that accounts for a finite correction term in Eq. (3), estimated from the average ratio of the second term to the first term (4.8%) using CTEQ6L, NNPDF2.3L, and MMHT2014L PDFs. The extracted \(S(x)\) values are then used in the BHPS (Brodsky-Hoyer-Peterson-Sakai) five-quark Fock state model to extract the probabilities \({\cal P}_5^{u \bar u}\), \({\cal P}_5^{d \bar d}\), and \({\cal P}_5^{s \bar s}\) for intrinsic light-quark sea components.

## Key numbers

- The HERMES kaon multiplicity data at \(x > 0.1\) are fit as \({dN^K}/{dN^{DIS}} = (0.102 \pm 0.002) + (0.013 \pm 0.010)x\), yielding \(\int_{0.2}^{0.8} D_Q^K dz = 0.514 \pm 0.010\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) under the vanishing \(S(x)\) assumption.
- The DSS fragmentation function gives \(\int_{0.2}^{0.8} D_Q^K dz = 0.435\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), about 20% smaller than the HERMES-derived value.
- The ratios of the second term to the first term in Eq. (3) at \(x = 0.25\) are 6.2% (CTEQ6L), 3.5% (NNPDF2.3L), and 4.4% (MMHT2014L), with an average of 4.8% used for HERMES2014-set3.
- Extracted intrinsic strange-sea probability \({\cal P}_5^{s \bar s}\) ranges from 0.000 (HERMES2014-set2, \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV) to 0.111 (HERMES2014-set1, \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV), with HERMES2014-set3 giving 0.031 (\(\mu = 0.5\) GeV) and 0.039 (\(\mu = 0.3\) GeV).
- The difference \({\cal P}_5^{d \bar d} - {\cal P}_5^{u \bar u}\) is constrained to \(0.118 \pm 0.012\) from the E866 Drell-Yan measurement of \(\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)\).
- The ATLAS collaboration determined the strange-to-down sea-quark ratio \(r_s = 1.00^{+0.25}_{-0.28}\) at \(x = 0.023\) and \(Q^2 = 1.9\) GeV\(^2\) from inclusive \(W\) and \(Z\) production.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Chang and Peng (2011) that first extracted intrinsic light-quark sea probabilities using the BHPS model (Brodsky et al., 1980) from HERMES 2008 data and E866 Drell-Yan data. The paper directly addresses the tension between two HERMES 2014 extractions of \(S(x)\): one with a sizable strange sea at large \(x\) (using DSS fragmentation functions) and one with vanishing strangeness (using a data-driven assumption). The results enable future global fits that incorporate the latest HERMES and COMPASS kaon SIDIS data to better constrain kaon fragmentation functions and the intrinsic strange sea.
