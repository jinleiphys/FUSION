# The calculation of total reaction cross sections induced by intermediate energy $α$-particles with BUU Model

> The Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) Model, which includes the Fermi motion, the mean field, individual nucleon-nucleon (N-N) interactions and the Pauli blocking effect etc., is used to calculate the total reaction cross section $σ_R$ induced by $α$-particles on different targets in the incident energy range from 17.4 to 48.1 MeV/u. The calculation result can reproduce the experimental data well. The nucleus-nucleus interaction radius parameter $r_0$ was extracted from experimental $σ_R$. It is found that $r_0$ becomes constant with increasing the mass number of target.

# The calculation of total reaction cross sections induced by intermediate energy α-particles with BUU Model

## Key claim

The Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model can reproduce experimental total reaction cross sections for α-particles on targets from ⁹Be to ²⁰⁸Pb at 17.4 to 48.1 MeV/u, but only when a sufficiently large number of test particles is used, the minimum required test particle number decreases with increasing target mass number, from 1000 for ⁹Be down to 200 for ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Method

The BUU equation was solved using test particle and grid methods, incorporating Fermi motion, mean field, individual nucleon-nucleon interactions, and Pauli blocking. The average nucleon-nucleon collision number as a function of impact parameter was obtained from the BUU simulation, and the total reaction cross section was computed via Poisson statistics as σ_R = 2π ∫ [1 − exp(−N)] b db, where N is the average collision number. Cugnon's parameterization for the free nucleon-nucleon cross section, a soft equation of state, and square-type density distributions were used for all stable target and projectile nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: 17.4 to 48.1 MeV/u for α-particles on targets from ⁹Be to ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The nucleus-nucleus interaction radius parameter r₀ extracted from BUU calculations at 48.1 MeV incident energy stays essentially constant at around 1.0 fm for heavy nuclei.
- For ⁹Be target, at least 1000 test particles are needed to obtain a steady total reaction cross section; for ¹²C, 400 test particles are sufficient; for ²⁰⁸Pb, 200 test particles are enough.
- Beam energy spread was approximately 100 keV (FWHM) with typical intensity of 2×10⁴ particles per second in the experiments used for comparison.

## Context

This work extends earlier BUU model calculations for medium-mass projectiles (Cai 2000) to light ions such as α-particles, where the Glauber model had systematically underestimated total reaction cross sections at intermediate energies. The results enable extraction of the nucleus-nucleus interaction radius parameter r₀ and show that BUU calculations can be applied even for few-nucleon projectiles provided sufficient test particles are used. The extracted r₀ values show similar trends to the parametrized formula of Shen (1989), and the authors suggest future study of isospin dependence of nuclear charge radii following the work of Zhang and Meng (2002).
