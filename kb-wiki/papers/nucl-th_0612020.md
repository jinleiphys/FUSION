# Cluster structure of a low-energy resonance in tetraneutron

> We theoretically investigate the possibility for a tetraneutron to exist as a low-energy resonance state. We explore a microscopic model based on the assumption that the tetraneutron can be treated as a compound system where $^3$n+n and $^2$n+$^2$n coupled cluster configurations coexist. The influence of the Pauli principle on the kinetic energy of the relative motion of the neutron clusters is shown to result in their attraction. The strength of such attraction is high enough to ensure the existence of a low-energy resonance in the tetraneutron, provided that the oscillator length is large enough.

## Key claim
The tetraneutron can exist as a low-energy resonance state only if the oscillator length \(r_0\) is large enough (on the order of 10 fm), because the effective attraction generated by kinetic exchange terms from the Pauli principle is sufficient to create such a resonance only at those large values, while the nucleon-nucleon potential contributes negligibly.

## Method
The authors used the Algebraic Version of the resonating-group method (AVRGM) to study the \(^4\)n system as a coupled-channel problem involving \(^3\)n+n and \(^2\)n+\(^2\)n cluster configurations. They expanded the relative-motion wave function in a basis of harmonic-oscillator states allowed by the Pauli principle, solved the resulting linear equations for the scattering \(S\)-matrix, and analyzed the phase shifts of elastic \(^2\)n+\(^2\)n scattering. The effective cluster-cluster potentials were generated by Serber and Ripka nucleon-nucleon forces, and the eigenvalues of the antisymmetrizer were computed to quantify Pauli-induced attraction or repulsion.

## Key numbers
- The resonance appears only at oscillator length values \(r_0\) of the order of 10 fm (e.g., \(r_0 = 10b\) with \(b = 1.48\) fm).
- The difference in intrinsic potential energy between the \(^3\)n cluster and two dineutrons is \(\Delta_u = -0.5 V_{13} (1 + 2 r_0^2 / b_0^2)^{-3/2}\), where \(V_{13}\) is the strength of the neutron-pair interaction in the singlet spin state.
- The kinetic energy difference between clusters is \(\Delta_t = 0.5 \hbar^2 / m r_0^2\), with \(m\) the nucleon mass.
- The effective \(^2\)n-\(^2\)n potential generated by the Serber interaction was computed for \(r_0 = 0.15\) fm, 0.25 fm, 0.35 fm, and 0.5 fm (with \(b_0 = 1.48\) fm).
- The phase shift of elastic \(^2\)n+\(^2\)n scattering shows resonance behavior only when \(\Delta_u = 0\) or when \(r_0 = 10b\) with \(\Delta_u \neq 0\); at \(r_0 = 5b\) and \(r_0 = b\) no resonance is observed.

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical studies of the tetraneutron, particularly the hyperspherical functions method (HSFM) calculations of Timofeyuk (2002) and the Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations of Lazauskas (2005), which found no bound tetraneutron with realistic Hamiltonians. It also addresses the RGM-based study by Nesterov (2005) that reported a resonance at 1-3 MeV using the Volkov potential, which the authors note is inappropriate because it binds the dineutron. The present results are consistent with the experimental data of Marques (2002) and the ab initio calculations of Pieper (2003), both of which allow for a broad low-energy tetraneutron resonance. The work enables future experimental searches for such a resonance by specifying the conditions (large oscillator length) under which it may appear.
