# Temperature and finite-size effects in collective modes of superfluid Fermi gases

> We study the effects of superfluidity on the monopole and quadrupole collective excitations of a dilute ultra-cold Fermi gas with an attractive interatomic interaction. The system is treated fully microscopically within the Bogoliubov-de Gennes and quasiparticle random-phase approximation methods. The dependence on the temperature and on the trap frequency is analyzed and systematic comparisons with the corresponding hydrodynamic predictions are presented in order to study the limits of validity of the semiclassical approach.

## Key claim

The paper systematically tests the validity of superfluid hydrodynamics for describing monopole and quadrupole collective modes in a dilute Fermi gas, finding that hydrodynamics works well at zero temperature when the pairing gap is much larger than the trap level spacing, but fails at intermediate temperatures below the critical temperature where strong Landau damping and mode fragmentation appear.

## Method

The authors solve the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for the static ground state of a spherical harmonic trap containing approximately 17000 atoms, then compute the linear response within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) using a regularization scheme for the zero-range interaction. They compare these fully microscopic results with semiclassical predictions from superfluid hydrodynamics (at zero temperature) and from the Vlasov equation with a generalized scaling ansatz (for the normal phase above the critical temperature).

## Key numbers

- For the parameter set with chemical potential μ = 32 ℏΩ and coupling constant g = -0.965 (in harmonic oscillator units), the number of particles is approximately 17000 and the pairing gap at the trap center at zero temperature is approximately 6 ℏΩ.
- At zero temperature, the QRPA monopole frequency is 1.9 Ω, compared to the hydrodynamic prediction of 1.88 Ω; the QRPA quadrupole frequency is 1.4 Ω, compared to the hydrodynamic prediction of √2 ≈ 1.414 Ω.
- Above the critical temperature (T = 3 ℏΩ/k_B), the QRPA monopole frequency is 1.9 Ω (Vlasov prediction 1.88 Ω) and the quadrupole frequency is 2.2 Ω (Vlasov prediction 2.22 Ω).
- The critical temperature for this parameter set is approximately T_c ≈ 2.8 ℏΩ/k_B.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic QRPA treatment of collective modes in trapped Fermi gases previously developed by Bruun and Mottelson (2002) for spin-dipole and quadrupole modes, and by the same authors for monopole modes. It systematically addresses the temperature dependence and finite-size effects (governed by the trap frequency Ω) that are neglected in the hydrodynamic approach of Menotti, Stringari, and Baranov (2002). The results enable a quantitative assessment of when the widely used semiclassical hydrodynamic description can be trusted for interpreting experiments on breathing modes, such as those by Bartenstein et al. (2004) across the BCS-BEC crossover.
