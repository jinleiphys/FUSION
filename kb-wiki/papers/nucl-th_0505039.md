# Precision calculation of $γd\to π^+ nn$ within chiral perturbation theory

> The reaction $γd\to π^+ nn$ is calculated up to order $χ^{5/2}$ in chiral perturbation theory, where $χ$ denotes the ratio of the pion to the nucleon mass. Special emphasis is put on the role of nucleon--recoil corrections that are the source of contributions with fractional power in $χ$. Using the known near threshold production amplitude for $γp\to π^+ n$ as the only input, the total cross section for $γd\to π^+ nn$ is described very well. A conservative estimate suggests that the theoretical uncertainty for the transition operator amounts to 3 % for the computed amplitude near threshold.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete chiral perturbation theory calculation of $\gamma d\to \pi^+ nn$ up to order $\chi^{5/2}$, where the nucleon recoil corrections, which enter at fractional powers of the expansion parameter $\chi = m_\pi/M_N$, are included without approximation; the authors find that the static approximation commonly used in previous calculations misses important non-analytic contributions from three-body $\pi NN$ intermediate states that scale as $\sqrt{m_\pi/M_N}$ relative to the leading rescattering term.

## Method

The calculation uses chiral perturbation theory expanded in two small parameters: $\chi_m = m_\pi/M_N$ and $\chi_Q = k_\pi/m_\pi$, with the counting $\chi \sim \chi_m \sim \chi_Q^2$. The one-body production operator is fixed using the known threshold amplitude $E_{0+}$ for $\gamma p \to \pi^+ n$ from chiral perturbation theory at order $\chi_m^3$, and the energy dependence of the $s$-wave amplitude is included via a slope parameter $E_{0+}^{\,\prime}$ calculated from the pion-in-flight diagram. The rescattering contributions include pion $s$-, $p$-, and $d$-waves, with the $nn$ final state interaction included for $S$- and $P$-waves using phenomenological $NN$ wavefunctions from the Bonn potential.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical uncertainty for the transition operator near threshold is estimated at 3% for the computed amplitude, combining a 2% uncertainty from few-body corrections with the uncertainty of the input $E_{0+}$ value.
- The input threshold amplitude $E_{0+}$ for $\gamma p \to \pi^+ n$ is taken as $(28.2 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-3} m_\pi^{-1}$ from chiral perturbation theory at order $\chi_m^3$.
- The slope parameter $E_{0+}^{\,\prime} = -11.3 \times 10^{-3} m_\pi^{-1}$, calculated from the pion-in-flight diagram including leading and next-to-leading order corrections.
- For the $\pi d$ system, the recoil corrections for the isovector $\pi N$ interaction (Pauli forbidden intermediate state) give $I_- = -0.19 I_0$, while for the isoscalar interaction (Pauli allowed) $I_+ = -0.88 I_0$, showing near cancellation of the static term in the latter case.
- The calculation is valid for excess energies $Q = \sqrt{s} - (2M_N + m_\pi) \le 20$ MeV, where data from MIT exist for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nucleon recoil effects in pion-nuclear reactions previously studied for $\pi d$ scattering by the same group (Lensky et al., 2005), where it was shown that the static approximation works well for Pauli-forbidden intermediate states but fails for Pauli-allowed ones. The calculation provides a controlled framework for extracting the $\gamma n \to \pi^0 n$ amplitude from deuteron data, complementing the approach using $\gamma d \to \pi^0 d$ discussed in earlier chiral perturbation theory work on neutral pion production. The results enable future extractions of the $nn$ scattering length from $\pi^+$ production data with quantified theoretical uncertainties.
