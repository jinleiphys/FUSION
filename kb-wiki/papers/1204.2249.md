# Collision centrality and $τ_0$ dependence of the emission of thermal photons from fluctuating initial state in ideal hydrodynamic calculation

> Fluctuations in the initial QCD matter density distribution are found to enhance the production of thermal photons significantly in the range 2 \leq pT \leq 4 GeV/c compared to a smooth initial state averaged profile in ideal hydrodynamic calculation for 200 AGeV Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and 2.76 ATeV Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The thermal emission of photons is strongly dependent on the initial temperature of the system where the presence of 'hotspots' in the initial state translates into enhanced production of photons compared to a smooth profile. The effect of fluctuations in the initial state is found to be stronger for peripheral collisions and for lower beam energies. The pT spectra are found to be quite sensitive to the value of the initial formation time of the plasma which is not known unambiguously and which may vary with collision centralities at a particular beam energy. Increase in the value of the formation time lowers the production of thermal photons compared to the results from a shorter formation time. However, the relative enhancement from fluctuating initial tates (compared to a smooth initial state) is found to be stronger for the larger values of formation time. The pT spectra alone are found to be insufficient to quantify the fluctuations in the initial density distribution due to the uncertainties in the initial conditions. A suitably normalized ratio of central-to-peripheral yield as a function of collision centrality and pT can be a useful measure of the fluctuation size scale.

## Key claim

Initial-state density fluctuations enhance thermal photon production in the range 2 ≤ pT ≤ 4 GeV/c compared to smooth initial conditions, but the pT spectra alone are insufficient to quantify these fluctuations due to the unknown value of the initial formation time τ0; a suitably normalized ratio of central-to-peripheral yield can serve as a useful measure of the fluctuation size scale.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic calculations with longitudinal boost invariance using the SHASTA algorithm, employing a lattice-based equation of state with a sharp crossover transition. Initial conditions were generated from a Monte Carlo Glauber model with a wounded-nucleon entropy density profile (sWN), where the fluctuation size was controlled by a Gaussian smearing parameter σ. Thermal photon emission was computed by integrating state-of-the-art rates from the quark-gluon plasma phase and hadronic matter over the space-time evolution, averaging over 40-80 random events depending on centrality.

## Key numbers

- For 200 AGeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC with τ0 = 0.17 fm/c and σ = 0.4 fm, the exponential slope of the photon pT spectra from fluctuating IC is about 10%, 12%, and 16% flatter than from smooth IC for the 0-20%, 20-40%, and 40-60% centrality bins respectively, in the region 2 < pT < 4 GeV/c.
- Thermal photon production increases by about a factor of 4 at pT = 1 GeV/c to a factor of more than 10 at pT = 4 GeV/c from RHIC (0-20% centrality) to LHC (0-20% centrality).
- For the LHC case study with centrality-dependent τ0 from the EKRT model, τ0 values are 0.140 fm/c for 0-5% centrality, 0.164 fm/c for 20-30% centrality, and 0.188 fm/c for 40-50% centrality, representing only about a 30% change across these bins.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section was taken as 42 mb for √sNN = 0.2 TeV (RHIC) and 64 mb for √sNN = 2.76 TeV (LHC).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Chatterjee et al., 2011) which showed substantial enhancement of thermal photons from fluctuating initial conditions for the 0-20% centrality bin at RHIC, and generalizes it to multiple centrality classes and beam energies. The results contradict the assumption that smooth initial-state averaged profiles are sufficient for photon observables, and show that the enhancement from fluctuations is stronger for peripheral collisions and lower beam energies. The proposed Rcpγ ratio provides a path to quantify fluctuation sizes, addressing the degeneracy between fluctuation effects and formation time uncertainties that has been noted in prior hydrodynamic modeling (Hannu Holopainen et al., 2011; Niemi et al., 2008).
