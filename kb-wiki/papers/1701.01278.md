# Time-dependent density-functional calculation of nuclear response functions

> Basic issues of the time-dependent density-functional theory are discussed, especially on the real-time calculation of the linear response functions. Some remarks on the derivation of the time-dependent Kohn-Sham equations and on the numerical methods are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard derivation of the time-dependent Kohn-Sham (TDKS) equations from the stationary action principle is not fully rigorous because it implicitly assumes orthonormal orbitals before taking variations, and that a proper derivation naturally introduces gauge degrees of freedom (a time-dependent Hermitian matrix \(\lambda_{ij}(t)\)) which are often overlooked in the literature.

## Method

The author presents two proper derivations of the TDKS equations: first, by starting from an action integral that does not assume orthonormal orbitals and using the generator coordinate method formalism; second, by using Lagrange multipliers to enforce the orthonormality constraints during variation. The paper then discusses practical numerical aspects, including the imaginary-time method for preparing the initial ground state, the real-time calculation of linear response strength functions via Fourier transform of the time-dependent expectation value of an operator after an instantaneous external perturbation, and the choice of gauge functions \(\lambda_{ij}(t)\) to simplify time evolution.

## Key numbers

- The strength function \(S(E;F)\) is obtained by the Fourier transform: \(S(E;F) = \frac{1}{\pi \eta} \int_0^\infty \sin(Et) \{ \langle \Psi(t) | F | \Psi(t) \rangle - \langle \Phi_0 | F | \Phi_0 \rangle \} dt\), where \(\eta\) is a small parameter for the linear approximation.
- To obtain a smooth strength function, the damping factor \(\gamma\) must satisfy \(\gamma \gtrsim 2\pi/T\), where \(T\) is the integration time duration.
- The imaginary-time evolution step uses \(\exp(-\Delta t \, h[\rho^{(n)}]) \approx (1 - \Delta t \, h[\rho^{(n)}])\) for a small time step \(\Delta t\).

## Context

This work extends the standard time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT) formalism, which is conceptually similar to the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory with density-dependent effective interactions in nuclear physics (Neg82). The paper clarifies formal issues not well addressed in published articles and recent reviews (BHR03, Nak12, Sim12, MRSU14). It enables more rigorous numerical implementations of real-time TDDFT for nuclear response calculations, particularly for linear response strength functions and beyond-linear-regime simulations such as heavy-ion collisions.
