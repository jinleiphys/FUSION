# State Dependent Effective Interaction for the Hyperspherical Formalism with Noncentral Forces

> The recently developed effective interaction method for the hyperspherical harmonic formalism is extended to noncentral forces. Binding energies and radii of three- and four-body nuclei are calculated with AV6 and AV14 NN potentials. Excellent results for the convergence of the expansion are found, particularly for the three-nucleon system. Due to the higher density the convergence rate is a bit slower for the alpha particle. In comparison to central potential models there is only a very slight deterioration of the convergence due the tensor force, while other potential terms have no visible effect on the convergence. The obtained values for binding energy and radii also agree well with the results in the literature obtained with other few-body techniques.

## Key claim

The effective interaction method for the hyperspherical harmonic (EIHH) formalism, previously demonstrated only for central potentials, is extended to noncentral nuclear forces and yields excellent convergence for three- and four-body nuclei, though the convergence rate for the alpha particle is slightly slower than for the triton due to higher density.

## Method

The authors construct a state-dependent effective two-body interaction within the hyperspherical harmonic basis by solving a two-body Hamiltonian that depends on the hyperradius and on the quantum numbers of the residual A-2 particle subsystem. They apply the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method to derive the effective interaction for a given model space defined by a maximum hyperspherical quantum number K_max, then diagonalize the resulting A-body Hamiltonian to obtain ground state energies, radii, and two-body correlation functions for 3H and 4He using the AV6 and AV14 NN potentials.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with AV6: 7.1602(3) MeV
- Triton binding energy with AV14: 7.6814(3) MeV
- Triton matter radius with AV6: 1.77475(5) fm
- Triton matter radius with AV14: 1.77615(3) fm
- 4He binding energy with AV6 (no Coulomb): 25.61(4) MeV
- 4He binding energy with AV14 (with Coulomb): 24.34(5) MeV
- 4He matter radius with AV6 (no Coulomb): 1.4739(4) fm
- 4He matter radius with AV14 (with Coulomb): 1.5297(3) fm
- For triton, deviations from converged binding energy are less than 0.1 MeV for K >= 8 and less than 0.01 MeV for K >= 14
- For triton radius, deviations from converged results are less than 0.005 fm for K >= 6
- For 4He at K=8, about 1 MeV of binding energy is still missing (compared to about 0.1 MeV for triton at the same K)
- The highest K value used for triton was K=32 (374 hyperangular basis functions); for 4He, K=18 (1975 hyperangular basis functions)

## Context

This work extends the EIHH method previously developed by Barnea, Leidemann, and Orlandini (2000) for central potentials to noncentral forces, including the realistic AV14 potential. The method is an alternative to correlation function approaches (Fenin and Efros 1972; Krivec and Mandelzweig 1990; Rosati, Kievsky, and Viviani 1992) and to no-core shell-model calculations using harmonic oscillator bases (Navratil and Barrett 1998). The results agree well with other few-body techniques including Faddeev calculations (Chen et al. 1985), Green's function Monte Carlo (Carlson 1987), stochastic variational method (Varga, Ohbayasi, and Suzuki 1997), and hyperspherical harmonic calculations (Kievsky et al. 1997). The authors note that further benchmark studies are needed to resolve remaining differences in 4He binding energies between methods.
