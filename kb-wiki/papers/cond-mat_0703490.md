# The Thermodynamical Limit of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Model

## Key claim
The paper introduces a method based on Majorana polynomial roots to compute the full spectrum of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model in the thermodynamical limit, revealing four qualitatively different regions in parameter space rather than the two phases distinguished by ground-state analysis alone. However, this result applies only to the maximum spin sector s=N/2 with N even, and the analysis is limited to the parity sector with m even, which is isospectral to the odd sector in the thermodynamical limit.

## Method
The authors map the LMG Hamiltonian onto a linear second-order differential equation for the Majorana polynomial representing each eigenstate in the spin coherent states basis. They convert this into a Riccati-like first-order differential equation for the logarithmic derivative G of the polynomial, then solve perturbatively in 1/s to obtain the leading-order term G_0. The integrated density of states in the thermodynamical limit is computed as a contour integral of G_0 around the branch cut C_1, expressed in terms of elliptic integrals, and the density of states is obtained by differentiation.

## Key numbers
- The ground state phase transition occurs at h = γ_x, where for h > γ_x the ground state is unique with lim_{s→∞} ⟨S_z⟩/s = 1, and for h < γ_x the ground state is two-fold degenerate with lim_{s→∞} ⟨S_z⟩/s = h/γ_x.
- In zone II, the density of states diverges at ε_0 = -h, where the integrated density of states is given by N^{II}_0(-h) = 1 - (2/(π sqrt(γ_x γ_y))) [A_h^+ tan^{-1}(A_h^+/B_h^0) - A_h^- tan^{-1}(A_h^-/B_h^+)], with A_h^± = h ± sqrt(γ_x γ_y).
- In zone III, the density of states has two singularities at ε_0 = ±h; the location at ε_0 = h is given by N^{III}_0(h) = (2/(π sqrt(γ_x γ_y))) [A_h^+ tan^{-1}(A_h^+/B_{-h}^0) - A_h^- tan^{-1}(A_h^-/B_{-h}^-)].
- In zone IV, at ε_0 = -h, the integrated density of states is N^{IV}_0(-h) = 1 - h/sqrt(γ_x γ_y), and the density of states is discontinuous in the thermodynamical limit.
- In zone IV, the energy gap between consecutive levels spreads over two branches depending on parity, and the average gap in the thermodynamical limit is Δ_0(ε_0) = 1/ρ_0(ε_0).

## Context
This work extends numerical studies by Heiss (2005) and Castanos (2006) that suggested singular points in the density of states and nontrivial level spacing distribution for the LMG model. The method provides an exact analytical computation of the full spectrum in the thermodynamical limit, going beyond previous approaches such as variational methods (Lipkin 1965), bosonization (Dusuel 2004, Dzhioev 2004), and coherent state treatments (Kuriyama 2003) that focused on the low-energy spectrum. The results enable the calculation of finite-size corrections and can be extended to other models with higher-order interaction terms where the mapping onto a one-dimensional potential fails.
