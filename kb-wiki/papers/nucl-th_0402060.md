# Large-angle scattering and quasi-elastic barrier distributions

> We study in detail the barrier distributions extracted from large-angle quasi-elastic scattering of heavy ions at energies near the Coulomb barrier. Using a closed-form expression for scattering from a single barrier, we compare the quasi-elastic barrier distribution with the corresponding test function for fusion. We examine the isocentrifugal approximation in coupled-channels calculations of quasi-elastic scattering and find that for backward angles, it works well, justifying the concept of a barrier distribution for scattering processes. This method offers an interesting tool for investigating unstable nuclei. We illustrate this for the $^{32}$Mg + $^{208}$Pb reaction, where the quadrupole collectivity of the neutron-rich $^{32}$Mg remains to be clarified experimentally.

## Key claim

The quasi-elastic barrier distribution extracted from large-angle scattering is a valid and practical alternative to the fusion barrier distribution for probing nuclear structure, but it is slightly less well-behaved due to nuclear distortion effects that cause a small shift in peak position and a low-energy tail, unlike the symmetric fusion test function.

## Method

The authors derived an analytic semi-classical formula for the quasi-elastic test function using perturbation theory, accounting for nuclear distortion of the classical trajectory. They performed coupled-channels calculations using the isocentrifugal approximation (replacing channel angular momenta by total angular momentum J) and compared these with exact solutions for the ¹⁶O + ¹⁴⁴Sm system. For the ³²Mg + ²⁰⁸Pb system, they used the code CQUEL to compute quasi-elastic cross sections and barrier distributions assuming either rotational or vibrational coupling models.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁶O + ¹⁴⁴Sm reaction at θ=π, the peak position of the quasi-elastic test function deviates from the barrier height B by 0.265 MeV.
- The quasi-elastic test function width is approximately 0.56 ħΩ, where ħΩ is the curvature of the Coulomb barrier.
- For the ³²Mg + ²⁰⁸Pb system, the barrier height is B=106.6 MeV using the Akyüz-Winther potential.
- The measured B(E2) value for ³²Mg is 454±78 e²fm⁴ (MIA95) and 622±90 e²fm⁴ (CGL01), with the first 2⁺ state at 885 keV excitation energy.
- The energy ratio E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) for ³²Mg is 2.6.
- The coupling strength β₂ for ³²Mg estimated from the B(E2) value is 0.51.
- The isocentrifugal approximation works well for scattering angles θ_cm > 130° in the ¹⁶O + ¹⁴⁴Sm system.

## Context

This work extends the barrier distribution method originally proposed by Rowley, Satchler, and Stelson (1991) for fusion reactions to quasi-elastic scattering, following the experimental suggestion by Timmers et al. (1995). The authors validate the isocentrifugal approximation for backward-angle scattering, which had been questioned for scattering processes due to long-range Coulomb interactions (Esbensen et al., 1987; Thompson et al., 1991). The method enables nuclear structure studies of unstable nuclei with low-intensity radioactive beams, as demonstrated for the ³²Mg + ²⁰⁸Pb system where the quadrupole collectivity of ³²Mg remains debated between rotational and vibrational interpretations.
