# Systematics on production of superheavy nuclei $Z = 119-122$ in fusion-evaporation reactions

> The fusion dynamics on the formation of superheavy nuclei is investigated thoroughly within the dinuclear system model. The Monte Carlo approach is implemented into the nucleon transfer process for including all possible orientations, at which the dinuclear system is assumed to be formed at the touching configuration of dinuclear fragments. The production cross sections of superheavy nuclei Cn, Fl, Lv, Ts and Og are calculated and compared with the available data from Dubna. The evaporation residue excitation functions in the channels of pure neutrons and charged particles are analyzed systematically. The combinations with $^{44}$Sc, $^{48,50}$Ti, $^{49,51}$V, $^{52,54}$Cr, $^{58,62}$Fe and $^{62,64}$Ni bombarding the actinide nuclides $^{238}$U, $^{244}$Pu, $^{248}$Cm, $^{247,249}$Bk, $^{249,251}$Cf, $^{252}$Es and $^{243}$Am are calculated for producing the superheavy elements with Z=119-122. It is found that the production cross sections sensitively depend on the neutron richness of reaction system. The structure of evaporation residue excitation function is related to the neutron separation energy and fission barrier of compound nucleus.

# Systematics on production of superheavy nuclei Z = 119-122 in fusion-evaporation reactions

## Key claim

The production cross sections for superheavy elements Z=119-122 depend sensitively on the neutron richness of the reaction system and on the collision orientation of the dinuclear fragments, with the tip-tip orientation giving results consistent with available Dubna data but yielding cross sections roughly two orders of magnitude lower than waist-waist orientations.

## Method

The dinuclear system (DNS) model was used with a Monte Carlo approach to sample all possible collision orientations of the two deformed fragments at the touching configuration. The master equations for nucleon transfer were solved stochastically, and the evaporation residue cross sections were calculated by summing over partial waves, incorporating the penetration probability, fusion probability (including quasifission competition), and survival probability via statistical evaporation of neutrons, protons, and alpha particles.

## Key numbers

- Maximal yield of SHN $^{283}$Cn in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U reaction: 3 pb in the 3n channel via tip-tip collisions at excitation energy 35 MeV
- Maximal cross section for $^{288}$Fl: 8 pb; for $^{294}$Og: 0.6 pb in $^{48}$Ca induced reactions
- Optimal system for element Z=119: $^{252}$Es($^{44}$Ca,3n)$^{293}$119 with cross section 4.32 pb at excitation energy 35 MeV (from Li18)
- Optimal system for element Z=120: $^{44}$Sc+$^{252}$Es in the 3n channel with cross section 3.18 pb at excitation energy 35 MeV (this work)
- Production cross sections for elements Z=121 and Z=122: below 1 fb in massive fusion reactions
- For $^{249}$Cf($^{45}$Sc,3n)$^{291}$119: 0.99 pb at 37 MeV (this work)
- For $^{251}$Cf($^{50}$Ti,3n)$^{298}$120: 0.11 pb at 33 MeV (this work)
- For $^{248}$Cm($^{52}$Cr,2n)$^{300}$120: 0.37 pb at 25 MeV (this work)

## Context

This work extends previous DNS model calculations for superheavy element production (Fei et al. 2006, 2009, 2011; Adamian et al. 1997, 2020) by implementing stochastic collision orientations via Monte Carlo sampling, which had previously been treated with fixed tip-tip orientations. The results are compared with experimental data from Dubna (Oganessian et al. 1999, 2006, 2015) and with predictions from other models including the fusion-by-diffusion model (Siwek-Wilczynska et al. 2008) and multidimensional Langevin-type equations (Zagrebaev et al. 2008, 2015; Liu et al. 2011). The systematic survey of projectile-target combinations for Z=119-122 provides guidance for upcoming experiments at facilities such as HIAF (China), RIBF (Japan), and FRIB (USA), particularly identifying $^{44}$Sc+$^{252}$Es as the most promising for element 120 and $^{45}$Sc+$^{249,251}$Cf as feasible for element 119.
