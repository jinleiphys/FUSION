# Probing QCD chiral cross over transition in heavy ion collisions with fluctuations

> We argue that by measuring higher moments of the net proton number fluctuations in heavy ion collisions (HIC) one can probe the QCD chiral cross over transition experimentally. We discuss the properties of fluctuations of the net baryon number in the vicinity of the chiral crossover transition within the Polyakov loop extended quark-meson model at finite temperature and baryon density. The calculation includes non-perturbative dynamics implemented within the functional renormalization group approach. We find a clear signal for the chiral crossover transition in the fluctuations of the net baryon number. We address our theoretical findings to experimental data of STAR Collaboration on energy and centrality dependence of the net proton number fluctuations and their probability distributions in HIC.

## Key claim

By measuring higher moments (beyond the fourth order) of net baryon number fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, one can experimentally probe the QCD chiral crossover transition even at vanishing baryon chemical potential, because the singular part of the free energy from O(4) critical scaling increasingly dominates higher-order cumulant ratios as the crossover temperature is approached.

## Method

The authors calculated net baryon number cumulants and their ratios (kurtosis R_{4,2}, and higher-order ratios chi_6^B/chi_2^B and chi_8^B/chi_2^B) within the Polyakov loop extended quark-meson (PQM) model at physical pion mass, using the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach to include non-perturbative quantum and thermal fluctuations. They compared these model results to the hadron resonance gas (HRG) baseline predictions and to experimental data from the STAR Collaboration on net proton number fluctuations in Au-Au collisions at various energies.

## Key numbers

- The curvature parameter of the chiral transition line is kappa_q = 0.06, determined from scaling analysis in (2+1)-flavor QCD.
- The critical exponent alpha = -0.2131(34) in the 3-dimensional O(4) universality class, meaning the fourth-order moment does not diverge at the chiral transition; the first divergent moment at mu_q/T = 0 is for n = 6.
- At mu_q/T = 0, the singular contribution to chi_{n,s}^B scales as -(2*kappa_q)^{n/2} * h^{(2-alpha-n/2)/(beta*delta)} * f_s^{(n/2)}(z).
- For mu_q/T > 0, the singular contribution scales as -(2*kappa_q)^n * (mu_q/T)^n * h^{(2-alpha-n)/(beta*delta)} * f_s^{(n)}(z), with the first divergent moment at n = 3.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD results on the chiral crossover temperature at vanishing chemical potential from Lattice QCD (2006) and the curvature of the chiral transition line from Lattice QCD (2012), applying O(4) scaling arguments to fluctuation observables. It enables experimental searches for chiral criticality using higher-order cumulant measurements from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC, where preliminary data on chi_6^B/chi_2^B ratios show strong deviations from HRG expectations, and where the observed non-monotonic energy dependence of chi_4^B/chi_2^B in central Au-Au collisions may signal remnants of the chiral crossover.
