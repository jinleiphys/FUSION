# Does shape matter? v_2 vs eccentricity in small x gluon production

> We study analytically and numerically effects of spatial eccentricity of the projectile shape on the second flow harmonic in inclusive gluon production in p-A collisions in the CGC framework. Keeping the collision area fixed, we find that the two quantities are anti-correlated.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework and keeping the collision area fixed, the second flow harmonic \(v_2\{2\}\) in inclusive gluon production is anti-correlated with the spatial eccentricity of the projectile shape, although this anti-correlation is mild (a variation of eccentricity from 0 to about 0.9 produces only about a 10% change in \(v_2\{2\}\)).

## Method

The authors study inclusive two-gluon production in p-A collisions using the dense-dilute CGC approach, averaging over projectile color charge densities with a modified McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model that incorporates spatial eccentricity while preserving the total projectile area. Analytical calculations are performed in the large \(N_c\) approximation with a soft momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) for gluons in the projectile wave function, separately analyzing Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) and Bose enhancement (BE) contributions. Numerical calculations are performed for SU(3) on a lattice without the large \(N_c\) approximation or the soft cutoff, using Gaussian density profiles with eccentricity parameter \(a\).

## Key numbers

* For the HBT contribution at small eccentricity parameter \(a\), the angular integral of \(|\mu^2(\v{k}_1-\v{k}_2)|^2\) weighted by \(e^{2i(\phi_1-\phi_2)}\) scales as \((S_\perp \mu_0^2)^2 \frac{8 \sqrt{2 \pi } a \ln(2/a)}{ (2\pi)^2 k R}\) for \(a \to 0\) and \(kR \gg 1\).
* Varying the eccentricity from 0 (\(a=1\)) to about 0.9 (\(a=2\) or \(a=1/2\)) produces only about a 10% change in the quantity \(\left( \int d\phi_{1,2} |\mu^2(\Delta k)|^{2} e^{2i \delta \phi} \right)^{1/2}\).
* Numerical calculations are performed for two values of the ratio \(\mu/\mu_{\rm t} = 1/4\) and \(\mu/\mu_{\rm t} = 1/2\), with the projectile radius fixed as \(R = \mu^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends semi-qualitative discussions of quantum interference effects (Bose enhancement and HBT) in gluon production within the CGC framework, which were previously identified in Refs. [qi] and [Kovchegov (2018)]. It addresses the dependence of \(v_2\{2\}\) on projectile geometry, a question not properly addressed in prior work, and notes that no correlations between initial \(v_2\{2\}\) and collision geometry were observed in classical field simulations by Schenke (2015) and Greif (2017). The results enable a detailed analysis of p-Au, d-Au, and \(^3\)He-Au collisions, which the authors report is ongoing and will be published elsewhere.
