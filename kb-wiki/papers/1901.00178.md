# Comments on the chemical and kinetic equilibration in heavy ion collisions

> I argue that perturbative scattering of quarks and gluons are incompatible with lattice and heavy ion data on QGP properties. The non-perturbative mechanisms for quasiparticle rescattering and quark production are briefly discussed, as well as experiments needed to measure matter anisotropy and quark density at early stages of the collisions.

# Comments on the chemical and kinetic equilibration in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The author argues that perturbative QCD (pQCD) scattering of quarks and gluons is incompatible with lattice and heavy ion data on QGP properties, and that weak-coupling kinetic calculations cannot be successfully extrapolated to the strong coupling regime relevant for experimentally produced QGP. The claim is conditional on the observation that lattice data show electric screening masses much larger than pQCD predicts, which invalidates the small-angle scattering regime assumed in weak-coupling equilibration scenarios.

## Method

The paper reviews and critiques the weak-coupling "hot glue" scenario originally proposed by Shuryak (1992), which posited that kinetic equilibration of gluons occurs faster than chemical equilibration of quarks. The author compares the transport cross sections and total cross sections for gluon-gluon scattering and gluon-to-quark-pair conversion, examining how the electric screening mass regulates infrared divergences. Lattice data on electric and magnetic screening masses from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration are used to argue that the screening mass is much larger than pQCD predicts, placing the system in a large-angle scattering regime. The paper also discusses non-perturbative mechanisms including monopole-gluon scattering and instanton/sphaleron-driven quark production as alternatives.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of gluon-gluon to gluon-to-quark-pair cross sections at large angles is approximately 200 (30/0.14), from the original "hot glue" calculation.
- The electric screening mass from lattice data is around 7.5 T, not the small value predicted by pQCD.
- For the three values of t'Hooft coupling studied in Kurkela (2018) (lambda = 0.1, 1, 10, corresponding to alpha_s approximately 1/300, 1/30, 1/3), the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio eta/s equals 1900, 35, and 1 respectively.
- The empirical eta/s value is about a factor of 6 smaller than the weak-coupling result at the strongest coupling studied.
- Typical gluon momenta in QGP are p ~ 3T ~ 1 GeV, comparable to the screening mass scale.
- The sphaleron mechanism produces 6 units of axial charge per event.

## Context

This paper extends the long-standing debate between weak-coupling and strong-coupling scenarios of heavy ion collisions, which was intense around the year 2000 before the RHIC era. It directly contradicts the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) and the weak-coupling cascade calculations of Kurkela (2018), arguing that these approaches cannot reproduce the observed elliptic flow and viscosity. The paper instead supports the strongly-coupled QGP paradigm established by RHIC data, and points to monopole-based explanations from Ratti (2008) and instanton/sphaleron mechanisms from Shuryak (1992) and Mace (2016) as more promising directions. It enables future experimental tests including dilepton polarization measurements proposed by Shuryak (2012) and chiral magnetic effect studies using RHIC isotope runs.
