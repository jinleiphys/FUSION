# Energy-density functionals inspired by effective-field theories: Applications to neutron drops

> New energy-density functionals (EDFs) inspired by effective-field theories (EFTs) have been recently proposed. The present work focuses on three of such functionals which were developed to produce satisfactory equations of state for nuclear matter. We aim to extend these functionals to treat finite systems including a spin-orbit contribution and pairing correlations. We illustrate here a first step towards this direction, namely a generalization of such functionals tailored to perform applications to neutron gases confined in harmonic traps. Sets of available \textit{ab initio} results are used as benchmark pseudo-data for adjusting the additional parameters (with respect to the nuclear matter case) that have to be introduced for finite-size systems. Several quantities are predicted and compared to \textit{ab initio} and other EDF results such as, for instance, total energies, potentials, and density profiles. The associated effective masses are also analyzed. In cases where \textit{ab initio} results are available, two of these functionals globally provide predictions which are close one to the other as well as to \textit{ab initio} values. It is shown that, in general, this is not the case for several currently used Skyrme functionals. Directions for improving the third functional are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper extends three EFT-inspired energy-density functionals (YGLO, KIDS, ELYO) from nuclear matter to finite neutron drops by introducing spin-orbit and pairing contributions, with the additional parameters adjusted to reproduce the average of available *ab initio* results for neutron numbers 8, 12, 14, 16, and 20 in a 10 MeV harmonic trap; the authors find that two of these functionals (YGLO and KIDS) globally provide predictions close to each other and to *ab initio* values, while the third (ELYO) requires further improvement.

## Method

The authors generalized three EFT-inspired EDFs (YGLO, KIDS, ELYO) to finite systems by adding spin-orbit and pairing terms, then solved Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in spherical symmetry for neutron drops confined in isotropic harmonic traps. The new parameters (splitting coefficient W, spin-orbit coupling constant V_so, and pairing strength V_pp) were adjusted by fitting the scaled energies E/(hbar*omega*N^(4/3)) to the average of *ab initio* results for N = 8, 12, 14, 16, and 20 at hbar*omega = 10 MeV. For the YGLO functional, the resummed term was directly transposed by extending functions to functionals Y(rho) -> Y[rho(r)], while for ELYO the density-dependent scattering length a_s[rho(r)] was introduced.

## Key numbers

- The YGLO functional has 7 parameters for reproducing the equation of state of matter, compared to 9 for Skyrme functionals; the ELYO functional has only 5 parameters.
- The ELYO functional uses a neutron-neutron scattering length a_s = -18.9 fm and effective range r_s = 2.75 fm in the low-density regime, with r_s = -4.5 fm at higher densities where |a_s k_F| > Lambda with Lambda <= 1.
- The fitted spin-orbit coupling constants (in MeV fm^5) are: YGLO = 138.2, KIDS = 110.0, ELYO = 55.0, compared to Sly5 = 125.0, SkM* = 130.0, and UNEDF0 = 91.3.
- The fitted pairing strengths (in MeV fm^3) are: YGLO = -275.1, KIDS = -183.9, ELYO = -152.5, compared to Sly5 = -213.1, SkM* = -233.9, and UNEDF0 = -170.4.
- The splitting parameter W is: YGLO = -0.084, KIDS = 0.110, ELYO = 0.396.
- The YGLO and KIDS PNM equations of state are almost identical for densities rho < 0.15 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the YGLO functional (Yang Grasso Lacroix Orsay, 2015), the KIDS functional (Korea IBS Daegu Sungkyunkwan, 2013-2017), and the ELYO functional (Extended Lee-Yang Orsay, 2016) from nuclear matter to finite neutron drops, building on previous applications of KIDS to doubly magic nuclei (KIDS2, KIDS3, KIDS4). The neutron drop results are compared to *ab initio* calculations including quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) with AV8' and three-body forces, no-core shell model and coupled-cluster calculations with chiral EFT interactions (NCSMEFT, 2016), and configuration-interaction calculations with the JISP16 force (NCSMQMC, 2013). The work enables benchmarking of EFT-inspired functionals against *ab initio* pseudo-data for neutron-rich systems and provides a step toward applying these functionals to finite nuclei with spin-orbit and pairing correlations.
