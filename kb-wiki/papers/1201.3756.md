# Effects of pseudoscalar-baryon channels in the dynamically generated vector-baryon resonances

> We study the interaction of vector mesons with the octet of stable baryons in the framework of the local hidden gauge formalism using a coupled channels unitary approach, including also the pseudoscalar-baryon channels which couple to the same quantum numbers. We examine the scattering amplitudes and their poles, which can be associated to known $J^P=1/2^-,3/2^-$ baryon resonances, and determine the role of the pseudoscalar-baryon channels, changing the width and eventually the mass of the resonances generated with only the basis of vector-baryon states.

## Key claim

The inclusion of pseudoscalar-baryon channels in the coupled-channels unitary approach for vector-baryon interactions primarily widens the dynamically generated resonances, with a negligible effect on their masses except in a few specific cases, and breaks the spin degeneracy between J^P=1/2^- and 3/2^- states.

## Method

The authors use the local hidden gauge formalism to compute the vector-baryon (VB) interaction potential via vector-meson exchange, then add a box-diagram contribution where an external vector meson decays into two pseudoscalar mesons, one of which is exchanged with the baryon, creating an intermediate pseudoscalar-baryon (PB) state. They also include contact terms (Kroll-Ruderman terms) required by gauge invariance, which only contribute to J=1/2 states. The total potential is used as input to the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, and poles are searched in the second Riemann sheet or extracted from the T-matrix on the real axis.

## Key numbers

- The subtraction constant a_l(μ) = -2 is used for the loop function G, with regularization scale μ = 630 MeV.
- The pseudoscalar-baryon coupling constants are F = 0.51 and D = 0.75.
- The vector-meson decay constant f = 93 MeV.
- The form factor cutoff Λ = 1 GeV is used in the box diagram.
- The ρ meson width Γ_ρ = 149.4 MeV and K* width Γ_{K*} = 50.5 MeV are used for convolution of the loop function.
- For S=0, I=1/2, the J^P=1/2^- peak shifts from around 1700 MeV to around 1650 MeV, allowing association with N*(1650)(1/2^-), while the J^P=3/2^- peak remains near 1700 MeV, associated with N*(1700)(3/2^-).
- For S=-1, I=0, the peak around 1780 MeV shifts to lower energies and broadens for J^P=1/2^- compared to J^P=3/2^-.

## Context

This work extends the vector-baryon interaction study of Garzon and Oset (2012) (Ref. [angelsvec]) by including the coupling to pseudoscalar-baryon channels, which was previously neglected. It follows the approach of Kanchan et al. (Ref. [kanchan2]) for the S=-1 sector but uses the standard local hidden gauge formalism rather than a contact term alone. The results enable better association of dynamically generated resonances with known PDG states, particularly by explaining the larger widths of J^P=1/2^- resonances compared to their J^P=3/2^- counterparts, and by providing partial decay widths into pseudoscalar-baryon channels for comparison with experiment.
