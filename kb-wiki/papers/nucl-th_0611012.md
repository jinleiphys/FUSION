# Peculiarities in the Structure of Two-Particle States within the Bethe-Salpeter Approach

> The two-fermion bound system is an attractive subject of atomic and sub-atomic physics. Despite these systems are rather simple the study of two-particle bound states is challenging and still remains a source of progress in quantum theory. Here we present a new method of solving the Bethe Salpeter equations for the bound states of spinor particles by using the expansion of the vertex functions over the complete set of four-dimensional hyperspherical harmonics. Within this method the BS equation is treated in a ladder approximation for the cases of scalar, pseudoscalar and vector meson exchanges with corresponding form factors. This method is shown to be effective and stable.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-fermion bound states using a hyperspherical harmonic expansion, but the method yields stable solutions only for coupling constants below a critical value, above which the solution becomes strongly dependent on numerical parameters and effectively disappears.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for spinor particles in the ladder approximation with one-boson exchange kernels (scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector mesons) including form factors. They expand the vertex functions over a complete set of four-dimensional hyperspherical harmonics after performing a partial wave expansion and Wick rotation, reducing the equation to a system of one-dimensional integral equations that are then discretized via Gaussian quadrature to form a linear system.

## Key numbers

- For scalar meson exchange with meson mass \(\mu = 0.15\) GeV, the minimum coupling constant for a bound state to appear is \(g^2_{min} = 4.023\).
- For binding energy \(B = 1\) MeV, the coupling constant in the Bethe-Salpeter approach is \(\alpha_{BS} = 0.362\), while in Light-Front Dynamics it is \(\alpha_{LFD} = 0.331\).
- For binding energy \(B = 100\) MeV, the ratio \(\alpha_{BS}/\alpha_{LFD} \sim 1.3\).
- The critical coupling constant for the \(^1S_0\) channel is \(g^2_{cr} = 40.3\), with a theoretical estimate of \(g_{cr}^2 = 4\pi^2 \approx 40\).
- The critical coupling constant for the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel is \(g^2_{cr} \approx 65\).
- For meson mass \(\mu > 0.1\) GeV and coupling constant \(g^2 < 40\), taking \(N_{max} \sim 4-5\) hyperspherical terms and a Gaussian mesh of \(N_G = 64\) is sufficient for convergence.

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical harmonic method previously applied to scalar particles by fb-tjon and others to the case of spinor particles, and is inspired by the stability analysis of bound states in Light-Front Dynamics by Karmanov. The method enables systematic study of the stability of bound states within the Bethe-Salpeter approach, revealing a critical coupling beyond which solutions become unstable, analogous to the bound state collapse in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics for potentials behaving like \(1/r^2\).
