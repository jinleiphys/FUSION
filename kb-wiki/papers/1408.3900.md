# Υ Production in Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC

> We investigate the Υ production in heavy ion collisions at LHC energy in the frame of a dynamical transport approach. Both the initial production and in-medium regeneration and both the cold and hot nuclear matter effects are included in the calculations. In comparison with the ground state Υ(1s), the excited state Υ(2s) is much more sensitive to the heavy quark potential at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the excited state Υ(2s) is much more sensitive to the heavy quark potential at finite temperature than the ground state Υ(1s), and that comparison with CMS data supports a potential close to the internal energy (V = U) rather than the free energy (V = F), but only when regeneration is included does the model match the data for the excited state.

## Method

The authors used a dynamical transport approach based on a Boltzmann equation to model Υ production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy. They included both initial production and in-medium regeneration via gluon dissociation and its inverse process, with the temperature dependence of cross sections derived from the average size of bottomonium states calculated from a potential model. The medium evolution was described by relativistic hydrodynamics, and the bottom quark density was evolved via a conservation law with initial conditions from nuclear geometry and PYTHIA simulations.

## Key numbers

- The dissociation temperatures for the internal energy limit (V=U) are T_d(Υ(1S))/T_c = 4.1, T_d(Υ(1P))/T_c = 1.8, and T_d(Υ(2S))/T_c = 1.6.
- The dissociation temperatures for the free energy limit (V=F) are T_d(Υ(1S))/T_c = 3.0, T_d(Υ(1P))/T_c = 1.1, and T_d(Υ(2S))/T_c = 1.0.
- The directly produced Υ(1s) contribute 51% of the final observed Υ(1s), with 37% from Υ(1p) decay and 12% from Υ(2S) decay.
- The initial Υ rapidity density at y=0 is dσ_Υ^{pp}/dy|_{y=0} = 40 nb, and at y=3.25 it is 16 nb, consistent with LHCb measurement.
- The bottom quark production cross section is dσ_{b bbar}^{pp}/dy|_{y=0} = 20 μb from FONLL.
- For V=U, the maximum regeneration fraction of R_AA for Υ(2s) reaches 0.1 in most central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the transport approach previously applied to J/ψ production at LHC (Zhou et al.) to the bottomonium system, and contrasts with calculations at RHIC energy where regeneration is negligible (Grandchamp et al., Liu et al., Strickland). The results enable discrimination between different heavy quark potential models from lattice QCD, showing that the CMS data for Υ(2s) support the internal energy (V=U) over the free energy (V=F) limit. The next step would be to improve the high-p_T description and to incorporate more precise bottom quark thermalization.
