# Manifestation of Hidden Symmetries in Baryonic Matter: From Finite Nuclei to Neutron Stars

> The hadron-quark/gluon duality formulated in terms of a topology change at a density $n\gsim 2n_0$ $n_0\simeq 0.16$fm$^{-3}$ is found to describe the core of massive compact stars in terms of quasiparticles of fractional baryon charges, behaving neither like pure baryons nor like deconfined quarks. This can be considered as the Cheshire-Cat mechanism~\cite{CC} for the hadron-quark continuity arrived at bottom-up from skyrmions that is equivalent to the "MIT-bag"-to-skyrmion continuity arrived at top-down from quarks/gluons. Hidden symmetries, both local gauge and pseudo-conformal (or broken scale), emerge and give rise both to the long-standing "effective $g_A^\ast\approx 1$" in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions at $\lsim n_0$ and to the pseudo-conformal sound velocity $v_{pcs}^2/c^2\approx 1/3$ at $\gsim 3n_0$. It is suggested that what has been referred to, since a long time, as "quenched $g_A$" in light nuclei reflects what leads to the dilaton-limit $g_A^{\rm DL}=1$ at near the (putative) infrared fixed point of scale invariance. These properties are confronted with the recent observations in Gamow-Teller transitions and in astrophysical observations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a topology change at density \(n \gtrsim 2n_0\) (where \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)), realized as a skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition, provides a unified description of baryonic matter from finite nuclei to neutron stars without requiring a deconfinement phase transition, with the caveat that the precise transition density \(n_{1/2}\) cannot be determined from theory alone and must be fixed by phenomenology.

## Method

The authors combine hidden local symmetry (HLS) for vector mesons with hidden scale symmetry via a dilaton field \(\chi\) (identified with \(f_0(500)\)) in a chiral-scale effective Lagrangian (\(\psi d\)HLS). They incorporate topology change from skyrmion to half-skyrmion matter, extracted from crystal simulations of skyrmions, into the density-dependent parameters of the hadronic Lagrangian. This is then treated via a Wilsonian renormalization-group approach that maps to Landau Fermi-liquid theory at mean-field level, with \(1/\bar{N}\) corrections included via \(V_{lowk}\) RG.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Topology change density: \(n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0\) (determined phenomenologically)
- Pseudo-conformal sound velocity: \(v_{pcs}^2/c^2 \approx 1/3\) at densities \(n \gtrsim 3n_0\)
- Dilaton-limit axial coupling: \(g_A^{\rm DL} = 1\) at the infrared fixed point of scale invariance
- Effective axial coupling in Gamow-Teller transitions: \(g_A^* \approx 1\) at densities \(\lesssim n_0\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GnEFT approach (Rho and Ma, multiple papers) that combines hidden local symmetry and scale symmetry, and contrasts with standard chiral EFT (sChEFT) which breaks down above \(\sim 2n_0\). The topology-change mechanism provides a bottom-up realization of hadron-quark continuity (Cheshire-Cat mechanism) that is equivalent to the top-down MIT-bag-to-skyrmion continuity. The results enable predictions for neutron star equations of state that simultaneously satisfy softness constraints from tidal polarizability (from gravitational wave observations) and hardness required for \(\gtrsim 2 M_\odot\) neutron stars.
