# Leading chiral logarithms for the nucleon mass

> We give a short introduction to the calculation of the leading chiral logarithms, and present the results of the recent evaluation of the leading logarithm series for the nucleon mass within the heavy baryon theory. The presented results are the first example of leading logarithm calculation in the nucleon ChPT. We also discuss some regularities observed in the leading logarithmical series for nucleon mass. The talk has been presented at "Quark Confinement and the Hadron Spectrum XI".

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of the leading chiral logarithm (LLog) series for the nucleon mass within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), up to four-loop order for the LLog coefficients and five-loop order for terms non-analytic in the quark mass; however, the highest-order coefficients (k12, k14, k16) rely on conjectures about the cancellation of higher powers of the axial coupling gA and a relation to the pion mass LLog series, and are thus marked as tentative.

## Method

The calculation uses the heavy baryon formulation of chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) and the renormalization-group method for extracting leading logarithms, where the LLog coefficient at a given order is obtained by iterating one-loop beta-function insertions into the nucleon propagator. The procedure was automated using the FORM system, evaluating nearly 10^4 one-loop diagrams to reach the four-loop LLog coefficient for the nucleon mass. A strong check was performed by parallel calculation with different parameterizations of the Lagrangians, and coefficients up to k6 agree with known results from Bernard (1995), McGovern (1998), and Schindler (2007).

## Key numbers

* The LLog expansion for the physical nucleon mass is given as M_phys = M + (m^2/M) sum_{n=1}^∞ k_{2n} L^{n-1} + π m (m^2/(4πF)^2) sum_{n=1}^∞ k_{2n+1} L^{n-1}, where L = m^2/(4πF)^2 log(μ^2/m^2).
* The coefficients k_3 = -3/2 g_A^2, k_4 = 3/4 (g_A^2 + (c_2+4c_3-4c_1)M) - 3c_1 M, and k_5 = (3g_A^2/8)(3 - 16 g_A^2) are the one-loop and two-loop results.
* The four-loop coefficient k_11 = (g_A^2/2)[ -95 g_A^8 + (5187407/20160) g_A^6 - (449039/945) g_A^4 + (16733923/60480) g_A^2 - 298785521/1935360 ].
* The conjectured six-loop coefficient k_14 = - (186515/1536)(g_A^2 + M(c_2+4c_3-4c_1)) + (186515/2304) c_1 M.
* The pion mass LLog series up to six-loop order is m^2_phys = m^2 [1 - 1/2 L + 17/8 L^2 - 103/24 L^3 + 24367/1152 L^4 - 8821/144 L^5 + 1922964667/6220800 L^6 + ...].

## Context

This work extends the method for computing leading chiral logarithms, previously applied to meson ChPT by Buchler (2003), Kivel (2008, 2009), and Bijnens (2009, 2010), to the nucleon sector for the first time. The results enable future studies of the mathematical structure of non-renormalizable theories at high orders, and the observed regularities (such as the absence of higher powers of g_A in even coefficients and the relation to the pion mass LLog series) suggest deeper connections between LLog approximation and Lorentz invariance. The practical numerical convergence is excellent, as shown by plotting individual terms at m=138 MeV, though the authors note that LLog contributions are not necessarily dominant except in specific contexts like small-x parton distributions.
