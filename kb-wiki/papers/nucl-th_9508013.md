# sd-Shell Study with a Multi-Configuration Mixing Approach designed for Large Scale Nuclear Structure Calculations

> A systematic numerical investigation of a recently developed nuclear structure approach is presented which diagonalizes the Hamiltonian in the space of the symmetry-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) vacuum and symmetry-projected quasiparticle excitations with respect to it. The underlying HFB transformation, which is assumed to be time-reversal and axially symmetric, is determined by variation after the projection. The model allows the use of large basis systems. It has been applied to the calculation of energy spectra of several even-even, odd-odd and odd mass nuclei in the sd shell with mass numbers reaching from A=20 to 30. The Chung-Wildenthal interaction has been used. Good agreement with the exact shell model diagonalization and a considerable improvement on a previous approach, where the HFB transformation was significantly more restricted, is obtained.

## Key claim

The complex MONSTER approach, which diagonalizes the Hamiltonian in a space of symmetry-projected HFB vacua and two-quasiparticle excitations, yields an excellent approximation to exact shell model results for even-even, odd-odd, and odd-mass nuclei in the sd shell, with a clear improvement over the older real MONSTER approach; however, for odd-mass nuclei the spectrum must be built on a HFB transformation from a neighbouring even-mass nucleus, and the quality of the approximation depends on the choice of that reference.

## Method

The authors performed systematic numerical calculations using the complex MONSTER (Model for handling many Number- and Spin-projected Two-quasiparticle Excitations with Realistic interactions) approach, which diagonalizes the effective Hamiltonian in a space consisting of a symmetry-projected HFB vacuum and all symmetry-projected two-quasiparticle excitations built on it. The underlying HFB transformation, assumed time-reversal and axially symmetric, was determined by variation after projection in a preceding VAMPIR calculation for an yrast state. The method was applied to even-even, odd-odd, and odd-mass nuclei in the sd shell (A=20 to 30) using the mass-dependent Chung-Wildenthal interaction, and results were compared to exact shell model diagonalizations and to the older real MONSTER approach.

## Key numbers

- For even-even nuclei, the complex MONSTER average deviation of yrast state energies (spins 0+ to 5+) from shell model results ranged from 0% (for 20Ne) to 1.43% of the shell model ground state energy (for 26Mg); the real MONSTER deviations ranged from 0.87% (22Ne) to 2.42% (24Mg).
- For 28Si specifically, the complex MONSTER average deviation for yrast states 0+ to 6+ was 1085 keV (0.79% of the 137.495 MeV shell model ground state energy), compared to 1746 keV (1.27%) for real MONSTER.
- For odd-odd nuclei, the largest complex MONSTER average deviation was 2.21% of the shell model ground state energy (for 24Na); for 22Na the complex MONSTER yrast state deviations ranged from 83 keV (7+ state) to 362 keV (0+ state).
- For 30P, the complex MONSTER yrast state deviations ranged from 1157 keV (0.74% of ground state energy, for 0+) to 1579 keV (1.01%, for 2+).
- The complex MONSTER provides 57 configurations for spin 0+ states, 151 for 1+, 223 for 2+, 259 for 3+, 275 for 4+, and 277 for all higher spins; the real MONSTER provides fewer configurations per spin (e.g., 21 for I=0, 30 for I=1, 61 for I=2).
- For odd-mass nuclei, the complex MONSTER configuration space has 12 configurations for I=1/2, 20 for I=3/2, and 24 for higher spins; the real MONSTER has only 3, 5, and 6 configurations respectively.

## Context

This work extends the complex MONSTER approach, previously tested only on 20Ne and 22Ne (Bender et al., 1995), to a systematic study of heavier even-even, odd-odd, and odd-mass nuclei in the sd shell, addressing the open question of its performance when the number of shell model states greatly exceeds the number of MONSTER configurations. The approach improves upon the older real VAMPIR and real MONSTER approaches (Schmid, 1984, 1986, 1987, 1989), which imposed more restrictive symmetry conditions on the HFB transformation, and demonstrates that the complex MONSTER can approximate exact shell model results well enough to be used for large-scale nuclear structure calculations where full diagonalization is impractical.
