# Invariant Amplitudes for Pion Electroproduction

> The invariant amplitudes for pion electroproduction on the nucleon are evaluated by dispersion relations at constant t with MAID as input for the imaginary parts of these amplitudes. In the threshold region these amplitudes are confronted with the predictions of several low-energy theorems derived in the soft-pion limit. In general agreement with Chiral Perturbation Theory, the dispersive approach yields large corrections to these theorems because of the finite pion mass.

## Key claim

The invariant amplitudes for pion electroproduction on the nucleon are evaluated using fixed-t dispersion relations with MAID as input for the imaginary parts, and the results show large corrections to low-energy theorems derived in the soft-pion limit due to the finite pion mass, in general agreement with Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Method

The authors used fixed-t dispersion relations to compute the real parts of the six invariant amplitudes for pion electroproduction from the imaginary (absorptive) parts, using the MAID05 global fit to pion photoproduction data up to center-of-mass energies of W=2.2 GeV as input. The dispersive amplitudes were evaluated at threshold kinematics and compared with predictions from low-energy theorems and Chiral Perturbation Theory, including the Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti sum rule relating the Pauli form factor to a dispersion integral over the first amplitude A1, and the Nambu sum rule connecting the axial and Dirac form factors to the longitudinal amplitude A6.

## Key numbers

- Physical threshold for pion electroproduction occurs at nu_thr approximately 0.136 GeV and t_thr approximately -0.016 GeV^2 for real photons
- The FFR discrepancy Delta_1^N at the origin of the Mandelstam plane (nu=0, t=0) is compatible with zero for real photons
- The difference of isovector charge and axial radii from the Adler-Gilman dispersive calculation was 0.152 fm^2, compared to the modern value of (0.14 +/- 0.03) fm^2
- For neutral pion electroproduction at Q^2=0, the dispersive contribution to E_{0+} is approximately 8.5 x 10^{-3}/M_{pi+}, while the pole contribution is approximately -7.9 x 10^{-3}/M_{pi+}
- For the isospin (-) channel at Q^2=0, the dispersive contribution to E_{0+} is approximately 0.8 x 10^{-3}/M_{pi+}, compared to the pole contribution of approximately 21.0 x 10^{-3}/M_{pi+}
- The slope of E_{0+,disp}^{(-)thr} contains information on the difference of squared radii <r^2>_1^V - <r^2>_A^V = 0.141 fm^2, with a loop correction of 0.046 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersion relation studies of pion photoproduction (Pasquini et al., 2005, 2006) to the electroproduction case with virtual photons, introducing the additional variable Q^2 and two longitudinal amplitudes. The results are compared with low-energy theorems from Kroll and Ruderman (1954), Nambu et al. (1962), and Fubini, Furlan, and Rossetti (1965), as well as with Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations by Bernard et al. (1991-2005) and Lehnhart (2007). The dispersive approach enables evaluation of amplitudes outside the physical region and provides a framework for determining low-energy constants of baryon ChPT through comparison with the sub-threshold expansion.
