# Relativistic Coulomb scattering of spinless bosons

> The relativistic scattering of spin-0 bosons by spherically symmetric Coulomb fields is analyzed in detail with an arbitrary mixing of vector and scalar couplings. It is shown that the partial wave series reduces the scattering amplitude to the closed Rutherford formula exactly when the vector and scalar potentials have the same magnitude, and as an approximation for weak fields. The behavior of the scattering amplitude near the conditions that furnish its closed form is also discussed. Strong suppressions of the scattering amplitude when the vector and scalar potentials have the same magnitude are observed either for particles or antiparticles with low incident momentum. We point out that such strong suppressions might be relevant in the analysis of the scattering of fermions near the conditions for the spin and pseudospin symmetries. From the complex poles of the partial scattering amplitude the exact closed form of bound-state solutions for both particles and antiparticles with different scenarios for the coupling constants are obtained. Perturbative breaking of the accidental degeneracy appearing in a pair of special cases is related to the nonconservation of the Runge-Lenz vector.

# Relativistic Coulomb scattering of spinless bosons

## Key claim

The partial-wave series for the Klein-Gordon equation with spherically symmetric Coulomb fields reduces exactly to the closed Rutherford scattering formula when the vector and scalar potentials have the same magnitude (|g_v| = |g_s|), and as an approximation for weak fields; strong suppressions of the scattering amplitude occur for both particles and antiparticles with low incident momentum near this condition, which the authors suggest might be relevant for analyzing fermion scattering near spin and pseudospin symmetry conditions.

## Method

The authors solve the time-independent Klein-Gordon equation with vector and scalar Coulomb potentials of the form V_v = ħc g_v / r and V_s = ħc g_s / r, expressing the radial solutions in terms of Whittaker functions. The relativistic Coulomb phase shifts are derived from the asymptotic behavior of the confluent hypergeometric function, and the partial-wave series for the scattering amplitude is summed analytically when γ_l = l + 1/2 (corresponding to |g_v| = |g_s|). Perturbative expansions of the scattering amplitude are performed for small pure vector couplings, small pure scalar couplings, and small deviations from the equal-magnitude condition using digamma function identities. Bound-state energies are obtained from the complex poles of the partial scattering amplitude, yielding solutions expressed as second-degree algebraic equations with eigenfunctions in terms of generalized Laguerre polynomials.

## Key numbers

- The condition for real bound-state energies with a pure vector Coulomb field requires |g_v| = Zα < 1/4, in contrast to fermions where g_v = -1 leads to diving into the negative-energy continuum.
- The nonrelativistic limit for small couplings gives E_n / mc² ≈ ±[1 - (g_s ± g_v)² / (2n²)] for g_s ± g_v < 0, where n = N + l + 1 = 1, 2, 3, ...
- For g_v = ε g_s (g_s < 0), the energy expansion to next-to-leading order is E_ν / mc² = ±[1 - (g_s/ν)²]^{1/2} - ε (g_s/ν)² + O(ε²).
- For g_s = ε g_v, the energy expansion gives E_ν / mc² = -sgn(g_v) / [1 + (g_v/ν)²]^{1/2} - ε (g_v/ν)² / [1 + (g_v/ν)²] + O(ε²), with the limit |g_v| → 1/2 yielding energies approaching -mc² sgn(g_v)/√2.
- Near the condition |g_v| = |g_s| with small g_Δ = g_v - g_s, the positive-energy bound states are E_ν^(+)/mc² = 1 - 2(g_Σ/2ν)² / [1 + (g_Σ/2ν)²] + ε_Δ, while negative-energy states scale as E_ν^(-)/mc² = -1 + g_Δ²/(2ν²) + O(g_Δ³).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of relativistic Coulomb scattering for spinless bosons beyond the pure vector coupling treated by Kang and Brown (1962), Hetherington (1963), and Rawitscher (1964), and fills a gap left by Cooper, Jeppesen and Johnson (1988) who provided approximate expressions for the Klein-Gordon equation with vector Coulomb fields. The mixed vector-scalar Coulomb field was previously studied for bound states by Liu et al. and for scattering phase shifts by Chen et al., but the present work provides the first detailed examination of the full scattering amplitude and its closed-form summation when |g_v| = |g_s|. The results enable future analysis of meson-nucleus scattering with more realistic scalar-vector coupling schemes, and the authors suggest that the strong suppression effects near equal-magnitude conditions could be relevant for understanding fermion scattering in the context of spin and pseudospin symmetries as discussed by Ginocchio (1997) and others.
