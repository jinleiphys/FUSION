# Coherence dynamics in low-energy nuclear fusion

> Low-energy nuclear fusion reactions have been described using a dynamical coupled-channels density matrix method, based on the theory of open quantum systems. For the first time, this has been combined with an energy projection method, permitting the calculation of energy resolved fusion probabilities. The results are benchmarked against calculations using stationary Schrödinger dynamics and show excellent agreement. Calculations of entropy, energy dissipation and coherence were conducted, demonstrating the capability of this method. It is evident that the presence of quantum decoherence does not affect fusion probability. This framework provides a basis for quantum thermodynamic studies using thermal environments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the coupled-channels density matrix (CCDM) method combined with an energy projection technique can reproduce stationary Schrodinger fusion probabilities, and that transient quantum decoherence caused by the fusion environment localised in the nucleus-nucleus potential pocket does not affect the fusion probability.

## Method

The authors model low-energy head-on collisions of \(^{16}\)O + \(^{144}\)Sm using a dynamical coupled-channels density matrix approach based on the Lindblad master equation, treating compound nucleus formation as an irreversible "fusion environment" coupled to the reduced system. They apply the window operator (energy spectral decomposition method) to the final density matrix to obtain energy-resolved fusion probabilities, and compare these with results from the static coupled-channels code CCFULL. The dynamics of quantum coherence are studied via the time-dependent purity and von Neumann entropy of the reduced-system density matrix.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier height between \(^{16}\)O and \(^{144}\)Sm is \(V_B = 61.1\) MeV.
- The Gaussian wave packet calculations reach energy-resolved fusion probabilities of order \(10^{-8}\) at deep sub-barrier energies (\(E_{c.m.}/V_B < 0.9\)).
- The Coulomb wave packet calculations achieve fusion probabilities of \(\sim 10^{-6}\) at sub-barrier energies.
- The energy resolution parameter \(\epsilon\) for the window operator was set to 0.35 MeV.
- The time step used in propagation was \(1 \times 10^{-22}\) s, with initial radial centroid \(r_0 = 70\) fm and spatial dispersion \(\sigma_0 = 10\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels density matrix approach previously introduced for nuclear collisions by Diaz-Torres (2008, 2010) and applies it for the first time to low-energy fusion with energy resolution via the window operator. The results are benchmarked against the static coupled-channels code CCFULL (Hagino et al., 1999). The framework enables future studies of environmental effects on fusion reactions, particularly plasma environments in stellar fusion, and may address open questions such as the experimentally observed hindrance of fusion at sub-barrier energies and dissipative multi-nucleon transfer reactions.
