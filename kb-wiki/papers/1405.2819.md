# Effective-energy budget in multiparticle production in nuclear collisions

> The dependencies of charged particle pseudorapidity density and transverse energy pseudorapidity density at midrapidity on the collision energy and on the number of nucleon participants, or centrality, measured in nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied in the energy range spanning a few GeV to a few TeV per nucleon. The model in which the multiparticle production is driven by the dissipating effective energy of participants is introduced. The model is based on the earlier proposed approach, combining the constituent quark picture together with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics shown to interrelate the measurements from different types of collisions. Within this model, the dependence on the number of participants in heavy-ion collisions are found to be well described in terms of the effective energy defined as a centrality-dependent fraction of the collision energy. For both variables under study, the effective energy approach reveals a similarity in the energy dependence obtained for the most central collisions and centrality data in the entire available energy range. Predictions are made for the investigated dependencies for the forthcoming higher energy measurements in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a model in which multiparticle production in nucleus-nucleus collisions is driven by the dissipating effective energy of participants, defined as a centrality-dependent fraction of the collision energy, and claims that this approach reveals a similarity in the energy dependence obtained for the most central collisions and centrality data across the entire available energy range from a few GeV to a few TeV per nucleon.

## Method

The authors extend a previously proposed approach combining the constituent quark picture with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics to describe the centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density and transverse energy pseudorapidity density at midrapidity. They define an effective center-of-mass energy as a function of centrality, where the most central collisions correspond to the full collision energy while peripheral collisions correspond to a reduced fraction, and apply this effective energy to relate measurements in nucleus-nucleus collisions to those in hadronic collisions using a factor of 1/3 energy scaling between nucleon-nucleon and central heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The charged particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair at midrapidity in central nucleus-nucleus collisions is fitted by a hybrid function: ρ(0) = (-0.306 ± 0.027) + (0.364 ± 0.009) ln(s_NN) + (0.0011 ± 0.0011) s_NN^(0.50 ± 0.06), where s_NN is in GeV.
- The centrality data are fitted by a hybrid function: ρ(0) = (0.002 ± 0.080) + (0.646 ± 0.022) ln(ε_NN) + (0.0003 ± 0.0001) ε_NN^(1.158 ± 0.034), where ε_NN is the effective energy in GeV.
- The midrapidity density in the most central collisions at √s_NN = 5.52 TeV is predicted to be about 12.0 within 10% uncertainty.
- PHOBOS data at √s_NN = 200 GeV multiplied by 2.12 and CMS data at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV multiplied by 1.43 are shown for comparison with the model calculations.
- The effective energy for a centrality interval is defined as ε_NN = √s_NN (1 - α), where α = 0.025 for the 0-5% centrality class.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark participant model combined with Landau hydrodynamics previously proposed by two of the authors (Sarkisyan and Sakharov) to describe the centrality dependence of bulk observables, building on earlier wounded nucleon models that failed to describe the increase of multiplicity with participant number at RHIC and LHC energies. The approach enables predictions for higher-energy heavy-ion collisions at the LHC at √s_NN = 5.52 TeV and demonstrates a transition from logarithmic to power-law behavior in the energy dependence around 600-700 GeV per nucleon, which constrains theoretical models and the universality picture of multiparticle production.
