# Global Deep Neural Network Modeling of Compton Form Factors Constrained from Local $χ^2$ Maps Fits

> Over the past two decades, intense experimental efforts have focused on measuring observables that contribute to a three-dimensional description of the nucleon. Generalized Parton Distributions provide complementary insights into the internal structure and dynamics of hadrons, including information about the orbital angular momentum carried by quarks. The most direct process to access these distributions is Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering, in which the cross section can be expressed in terms of Compton Form Factors. These quantities are defined as convolutions of the Generalized Parton Distributions with coefficient functions derived in perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics. We extract the Compton Form Factors from Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering data collected at Jefferson Lab, including the most recent measurements in Hall A, using a novel local fitting technique based on $χ^2$ mapping to constrain the real parts of the Compton Form Factors $\mathcal{H}, \mathcal{E}$ and $\widetilde{\mathcal{H}}$. They are determined independently in each kinematic bin for the unpolarized beam-target configuration under the twist-2 approximation, following the formalism developed by Belitsky, Müller, and Kirchner. The extracted Compton Form Factors are then used to train and regularize a deep neural network, enabling a global determination of their behavior with minimal model dependence. This procedure is validated and systematically studied using pseudodata generated with kinematics matching those of the experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The authors extract the real parts of the Compton Form Factors $\mathcal{H}$, $\mathcal{E}$, and $\widetilde{\mathcal{H}}$ from unpolarized DVCS data using a local $\chi^2$ mapping technique that determines them independently in each kinematic bin, then use these local extractions to train a deep neural network for a global determination; the method is validated only on pseudodata generated with kinematics matching the experimental measurements, and the authors note that using only the unpolarized cross-section represents "the least constrained scenario."

## Method

The authors perform local fits of the real parts of three Compton Form Factors (Re $\mathcal{H}$, Re $\mathcal{E}$, Re $\widetilde{\mathcal{H}}$) to unpolarized beam-target DVCS cross-section data from Jefferson Lab Hall A and Hall B, using the twist-2 formalism of Belitsky, Müller, and Kirchner (BKM10) and neglecting helicity-flip gluonic amplitudes. The locally extracted CFF values from each kinematic bin are then used as training data for a deep neural network, which provides a global determination of the CFF behavior across kinematics. The procedure is tested and validated using pseudodata generated from the KM15$^*$ GPD model, which employs the scaling hypothesis (no QCD evolution) and a double-distribution ansatz with spectator-model t-dependence.

## Key numbers

* 195 distinct kinematic conditions (defined by $Q^2$, $x_B$, $t$) from JLab data are used for the local fits.
* The Hall A E12-06-114 experiment provides 1080 data points covering $Q^2$ from 2.71 to 8.51 GeV$^2$, $-t$ from 0.204 to 1.373 GeV$^2$, and $x_B$ from 0.363 to 0.617.
* The Hall A E07-007 experiment provides 404 data points with $Q^2$ from 1.49 to 2.00 GeV$^2$, $-t$ from 0.177 to 0.363 GeV$^2$, and $x_B$ from 0.356 to 0.361.
* The Hall A E00-110 experiment provides 468 data points with $Q^2$ from 1.82 to 2.37 GeV$^2$, $-t$ from 0.171 to 0.372 GeV$^2$, and $x_B$ from 0.336 to 0.401.
* The Hall B e1-DVCS1 experiment provides 1933 data points with $Q^2$ from 1.11 to 3.77 GeV$^2$, $-t$ from 0.11 to 0.45 GeV$^2$, and $x_B$ from 0.126 to 0.475.
* The total dataset comprises 1952 data points from Hall A experiments and 1933 from Hall B, for a total of 3885 data points across 195 kinematic sets.

## Context

This work extends the local fitting approach previously used by Guidal (2008, 2010), Moutarde (2009), and others, who extracted CFFs as free parameters at individual kinematic points, and the global fitting approach of Kumericki and Mueller (KM09, KM15, KM20) which uses GPD-based models. The novel contribution is combining local $\chi^2$ map extractions with deep neural network training to achieve a global determination with reduced model dependence compared to traditional GPD parametrizations like GK, VGG, and KM. The method is intended to enable interpolation and extrapolation into kinematic regions where experimental data is unavailable, and the authors note that future inclusion of additional observables (polarized cross-sections, beam-spin asymmetries) will provide stronger constraints.
