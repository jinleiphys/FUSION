# Microscopic optical potentials including breakup effects for elastic scattering

> We construct a microscopic optical potential including breakup effects for elastic scattering of weakly-binding projectiles within the Glauber model, in which a nucleon-nucleus potential is derived by the $g$-matrix folding model. The derived microscopic optical potential is referred to as the eikonal potential. For $d$ scattering, the calculation with the eikonal potential reasonably reproduces the result with an exact calculation estimated by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method. As the properties of the eikonal potential, the inaccuracy of the eikonal approximation used in the Glauber model is partially excluded. We also analyse the $^6$He scattering from $^{12}$C with the eikonal potential and show its applicability to the scattering with many-body projectiles.

## Key claim

The eikonal potential model, derived from the Glauber model, can reproduce the results of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for elastic scattering of weakly-bound projectiles, but only when the incident energy per nucleon is sufficiently high; at low energies (40 MeV/nucleon or below), the discrepancy is mainly due to the inaccuracy of the adiabatic approximation, not the eikonal approximation.

## Method

The authors construct a microscopic optical potential (the eikonal potential) by first calculating the eikonal S-matrix for the projectile-target system using the Glauber model, which incorporates the adiabatic and eikonal approximations. The nucleon-nucleus optical potentials for the projectile constituents are derived from the g-matrix folding model using the Melbourne g matrix. The eikonal S-matrix is then inverted via an Abel transform to obtain a local, spherical optical potential, which is used in a standard two-body Schrodinger equation to compute elastic scattering cross sections. The method is tested for deuteron scattering (p+n+T three-body model) and 6He scattering (4He+n+n+T four-body model) on 12C and 208Pb targets.

## Key numbers

- For deuteron scattering on 12C and 208Pb at incident energies of 20–200 MeV/nucleon, the eikonal potential model reproduces CDCC results well for energies at or above 80 MeV/nucleon.
- At 40 MeV/nucleon and below, the eikonal potential model shows reasonable but less accurate agreement with CDCC for deuteron scattering; the discrepancy is attributed to the adiabatic approximation.
- For 6He scattering on 12C at 20–200 MeV/nucleon, the eikonal potential model reproduces CDCC results well even at 40 MeV/nucleon and below.
- The dynamical polarization potential for 6He + 12C at 40 MeV/nucleon shows a repulsive real part in the peripheral region and an absorptive imaginary part in the whole region.

## Context

This work extends the g-matrix folding model approach (Melbourne group, 2000; Toyokawa et al., 2015) to include breakup effects in a microscopic optical potential, building on earlier target-density approximation (TDA) and double-single folding (DSF) models for 3,4He scattering (Egashira et al., 2014; Toyokawa et al., 2015). The eikonal potential model is compared to CDCC (Kamimura et al., 1989; Austern et al., 1987), which is the standard exact method for breakup-inclusive scattering of few-body projectiles. The approach enables the analysis of scattering for many-body projectiles (e.g., 8He) where full CDCC calculations are computationally prohibitive, and opens the door to including Coulomb breakup effects in future work.
