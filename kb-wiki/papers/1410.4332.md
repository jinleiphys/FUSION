# Chaoticity parameter $λ$ in two-pion interferometry in an expanding boson gas model

> We investigate the chaoticity parameter $λ$ in two-pion interferometry in an expanding boson gas model. The degree of Bose-Einstein condensation of identical pions, density distributions, and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlation functions are calculated for the expanding gas within the mean-field description with a harmonic oscillator potential. The results indicate that a sources with thousands of identical pions may exhibit a degree of Bose-Einstein condensation at the temperatures during the hadronic phase in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. This finite condensation may decrease the chaoticity parameter $λ$ in the two-pion interferometry measurements at low pion pair momenta, but influence only slightly the $λ$ value at high pion pair momentum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an expanding boson gas source with thousands of identical pions may exhibit a degree of Bose-Einstein condensation at temperatures in the hadronic phase (170–60 MeV) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and that this finite condensation can decrease the chaoticity parameter λ in two-pion interferometry at low pion pair momenta while influencing λ only slightly at high pion pair momentum. The hedge is that this result depends on the specific parameterization of the expanding source (e.g., the proportional parameter C₁) and the particle number N, and that the variation of λ with temperature is more complicated than for static sources.

## Method

The authors model an expanding relativistic boson gas of identical pions within a time-dependent mean-field harmonic oscillator potential, using a quasi-static adiabatic approximation where the system is treated as static at each evolution time. The temperature-radius relation is parameterized from relativistic hydrodynamic calculations (T = T₀R₀^δ / R^δ with α = 0.62 and δ = 1.88). They calculate the fugacity parameter, condensation fraction f₀, one- and two-body density matrices in configuration and momentum space, and then the two-pion HBT correlation functions and chaoticity parameter λ for sources with N = 500 and 2000 pions at temperatures from 60 to 170 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The temperature-radius relation is parameterized as T = T₀R₀^δ / (R₀ + αt)^δ with α = 0.62 and δ = 1.88, for an initial radius R₀ = 6 fm and initial temperature T₀ = 170 MeV.
- For a source with N = 2000 and C₁ = 0.35, the condensation fraction f₀ is finite at T = 160 MeV and increases as temperature decreases; for C₁ = 0.40 and for a static source with a = 2.5 fm, f₀ approaches zero at T ~ 160 MeV.
- For N = 500 and C₁ = 0.35, the expanding source has only a small condensation fraction at low temperatures; for C₁ = 0.40, the system is uncondensed in the whole temperature region (60–170 MeV).
- At T = 160 MeV for N = 2000 and C₁ = 0.35, the function F_N(p) shows an abrupt change at p ~ 200 MeV/c, corresponding to the momentum where the density distribution ρ(p) changes abruptly.
- The root-mean-squared radius (RMSR) of the source with N = 2000 is sensitive to C₁; values of C₁ between 0.35 and 0.40 give RMSR of order 10 fm, consistent with Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Bose-Einstein condensation effects on HBT interferometry from static boson gas sources (Wong and Zhang, 2007; Liu and Zhang, 2013) to an expanding source model with a time-dependent harmonic oscillator potential. It also builds on the pion laser model of Pratt (1993) and the Gaussian treatment of condensation effects by Csörgő and Zimányi (1998). The results enable comparison with experimental HBT data from the ALICE collaboration (2013) at LHC energies, where identical pion multiplicities can reach several thousands, and suggest that condensation effects on λ should be considered in analyses of source coherence.
