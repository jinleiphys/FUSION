# Dynamical equilibration in strongly-interacting parton-hadron matter

> We study the kinetic and chemical equilibration in 'infinite' parton-hadron matter within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons matched to reproduce lattice-QCD results - including the partonic equation of state - in thermodynamic equilibrium. The 'infinite' matter is simulated within a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions initialized at different baryon density (or chemical potential) and energy density. The transition from initially pure partonic matter to hadronic degrees of freedom (or vice versa) occurs dynamically by interactions. Different thermodynamical distributions of the strongly-interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) are addressed and discussed.

# Dynamical equilibration in strongly-interacting parton-hadron matter

## Key claim
The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, based on a dynamical quasiparticle model matched to lattice-QCD results, demonstrates that infinite parton-hadron matter can achieve both kinetic and chemical equilibrium dynamically, but only for energy densities either sufficiently below or above a critical range; in the intermediate regime from 0.48 to 1.3 GeV/fm³, no stable equilibrium is reached due to large fluctuations between hadronic and partonic configurations.

## Method
The authors simulated infinite parton-hadron matter within a cubic box of 9³ fm³ with periodic boundary conditions, initialized with light (u,d,s) quarks, antiquarks, and gluons at various baryon densities and energy densities. The system was evolved using the PHSD transport approach, which incorporates off-shell Kadanoff-Baym equations and the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) for partons, with covariant transition rates for hadronization and parton fusion. Elastic and inelastic collisions (gluon splitting and quark-antiquark fusion) drove the system toward thermal and chemical equilibrium, monitored through reaction rates, particle abundances, and energy spectra.

## Key numbers
- Energy density of 0.4 GeV/fm³: system initialized with only partons slightly below the critical energy density for deconfinement; most partons had formed hadrons by 40 fm/c, with a small fraction remaining unhadronized.
- Energy density of 2.18 GeV/fm³: system clearly above critical energy density; no hadrons observed at 40 fm/c, with different parton species in thermal and chemical equilibrium.
- Energy density of 2.87 GeV/fm³: system achieved chemical and thermal equilibrium after a few fm/c, with reaction rates constant and obeying detailed balance.
- Energy density of 5.37 GeV/fm³: equilibrium energy spectra for off-shell u quarks, s quarks, and gluons described by a Boltzmann distribution with temperature T = 240 MeV in the high energy regime.
- Energy density of 9.43 GeV/fm³: chemical equilibration reached after about 15 fm/c.
- Critical energy density for deconfinement phase transition: approximately 0.5 GeV/fm³.
- Unstable equilibrium regime: energy densities from 0.48 to 1.3 GeV/fm³, where no stable equilibrium was found due to large fluctuations.
- At energy densities above approximately 2 GeV/fm³, the hadron fraction becomes negligible.

## Context
This work extends prior nonequilibrium transport studies (e.g., Refs. [Ref13-Ref21] in the paper) by using the PHSD approach, which incorporates lattice-QCD matched partonic equations of state, to systematically investigate equilibration timescales in infinite matter. The results provide microscopic justification for the assumption of local thermal and chemical equilibrium used in hydrodynamic models (e.g., Refs. [Ref9-Ref12]), while also identifying a problematic intermediate energy density range where equilibrium assumptions break down. This enables future studies of equilibration in expanding systems and more realistic heavy-ion collision simulations, with further work needed to understand the fluctuating regime between 0.48 and 1.3 GeV/fm³.
