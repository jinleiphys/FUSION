# Calculation of two-photon exchange effects for $Δ$ production in electron-proton collisions

> We present an evaluation of a two-photon exchange correction to the cross section for unpolarized $Δ$ isobar production in electron-proton collisions, using a relativistic, crossing symmetric and gauge invariant approach. The calculated box and crossed-box diagrams include nucleon and $Δ$ intermediate states. We find a relation between the angular nonlinearity of the two-photon exchange contribution and the value of the $γΔΔ$ coupling constant.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange correction to the unpolarized cross section for Δ isobar production in electron-proton collisions is generally much smaller than the Born contribution, but its angular nonlinearity as a function of the virtual photon polarization ε is sensitive to the value and sign of the magnetic γΔΔ coupling constant gΔ, with a linear ε-dependence suggesting a negligible intermediate Δ contribution.

## Method

The authors computed the two-photon exchange correction δ2γ from box, crossed-box, and contact-term loop diagrams using a relativistic, crossing-symmetric, and gauge-invariant hadronic model. Intermediate states included a nucleon and a Δ isobar, with the γNN, γNΔ, and γΔΔ vertices defined in specific forms; the γΔΔ vertex was restricted to a magnetic dipole component. Loop integrals were evaluated analytically as sums of Passarino-Veltman dilogarithms, with ultraviolet convergence ensured by dipole form factors (cutoff Λ = 0.84 GeV) and infrared divergences cancelled by soft-photon emission contributions incorporated into the contact term.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange correction δ2γ was calculated at Q² = 1 GeV² and Q² = 3 GeV², shown as a function of ε for three values of the γΔΔ coupling constant gΔ (including gΔ = 0, corresponding to no intermediate Δ state).
- The dipole form factor cutoff was set to Λ = 0.84 GeV, chosen to be compatible with the nucleon mean-square radius.
- The calculation used nucleon mass M and Δ mass MΔ, with the kinematic variable ν defined as ν = (MΔ² − M² + Q²)/(2M).

## Context

This work extends the study of two-photon exchange corrections from elastic electron-proton scattering (Blunden 2003, Guichon 2003, Chen 2004, Kondratyuk 2005) to the inelastic channel of Δ production, where such corrections are necessary for precise analyses of Rosenbluth separations. The approach is complementary to the generalized parton distribution formalism of Pasquini (2006) and provides a hadronic framework that can be extended to include higher-mass resonances in future studies. The results enable improved extraction of the γNΔ transition current from experimental data (Liang 2004, Tvaskis 2005) and motivate further experimental searches for ε-nonlinearities to constrain the γΔΔ coupling.
