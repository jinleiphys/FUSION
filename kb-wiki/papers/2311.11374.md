# Rapidity scan approach for net-baryon cumulants with a statistical thermal model

> Utilizing rapidity-dependent measurements to map the QCD phase diagram provides a complementary approach to traditional beam-energy-dependent measurements around midrapidity. The changing nature of thermodynamic properties of QCD matter along the beam axis in heavy-ion collisions at low collision energies both motivates and poses challenges for this method. In this study, we derive the analytical cumulant-generating function for subsystems within distinct rapidity windows, while accounting for global net-baryon charge conservation of the full system. Rapidity-dependent net-baryon cumulants are then calculated for a system exhibiting inhomogeneity along the beam axis, and their sensitivity to finite acceptances through changing rapidity bin widths is explored. We highlight the nontrivial behaviors exhibited by these cumulants, underscoring their importance in establishing a noncritical baseline for interpreting net-proton cumulants in the search for the QCD critical point. Finally, we discuss the implications of the rapidity scan for mapping the QCD phase diagram within the current context.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical cumulant-generating function for net-baryon cumulants within finite rapidity windows that accounts for global net-baryon charge conservation, showing that rapidity-dependent cumulants exhibit nontrivial behaviors in systems with inhomogeneous thermodynamic properties along the beam axis, which the abstract presents as establishing a noncritical baseline for interpreting net-proton cumulants in the search for the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors treat the full collision system as a canonical ensemble with conserved total net-baryon number, while each rapidity window subsystem is treated as a grand canonical ensemble. They derive the cumulant-generating function by summing over Poisson-distributed baryon and antibaryon emissions from multiple thermal sources distributed along rapidity, using Graf's addition formula to obtain a closed-form expression. The method is applied to a continuous-source thermal model with rapidity-dependent freeze-out profiles (temperature, baryon chemical potential, and volume) taken from fits to pion and net-proton yields at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6\) GeV.

* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6\) GeV, the thermodynamic profiles for temperature \(T_s\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu_s\), and volume \(\dif{V_s}/\dif{y_s}\) are taken from fits shown in FIG. 2 of the paper, covering source rapidities \(y_s\) from 0 to 3.
* Three rapidity bin widths are compared: \(w=0.01\), \(0.25\), and \(1.00\), with acceptance windows spanning from \([0, w)\) up to \([3-w, 3]\).

## Context

This work extends the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) framework of Vovchenko (2020) and the statistical thermal model baseline established by Braun-Munzinger (2020) and Vovchenko (2021) for net-proton cumulants at beam energies above \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=27\) GeV. The rapidity scan approach complements the traditional beam energy scan method used by the STAR Collaboration (2020, 2021, 2022) and addresses the complication that at lower beam energies (7.7 to 27 GeV), thermodynamic properties vary significantly along rapidity, making midrapidity-only measurements an average over changing conditions. The results enable establishing a noncritical baseline for upcoming BES-II rapidity-dependent measurements and provide a framework for extracting effective temperature and baryon chemical potential values from rapidity-dependent cumulant data.
