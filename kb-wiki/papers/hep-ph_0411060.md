# The Ghost-Gluon Vertex in Landau Gauge Yang-Mills Theory

> Possible mechanisms for gluon confinement in Yang-Mills theory in covariant gauges are discussed briefly. Implications for the infrared behavior of the Green's functions are outlined. Results for the gluon and Faddeev-Popov ghost propagators from Dyson-Schwinger studies and lattice calculations are presented. The importance of the ghost-gluon vertex in these studies is discussed. Numerical results for a non-perturbatively constructed ghost-gluon vertex in four and three space-time dimensions are presented.

## Key claim

The ghost-gluon vertex in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory remains very close to its tree-level (bare) form at all momenta, especially in the infrared, confirming Zwanziger's hypothesis that a bare vertex suffices at leading order; however, subleading renormalization-group effects may generate small deviations of the observed magnitude.

## Method

The authors performed one iteration step in the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the ghost-gluon vertex, using non-perturbatively dressed gluon and ghost propagators from earlier DSE studies (Fischer 2002, Maas 2004) while keeping all vertices bare. The calculation was carried out in both four and three Euclidean space-time dimensions, neglecting the ghost-gluon scattering kernel and four-point functions. The resulting transverse part of the vertex, defined via a projection that removes the gluon momentum component, was evaluated across various kinematic configurations.

## Key numbers

- In four dimensions, the normalized transverse part of the ghost-gluon vertex (1+A) deviates from the tree-level value of 1 by only a few percent at all momenta, with the smallest deviations in the deep infrared.
- In three dimensions, the vertex reduces to the bare value in the ultraviolet, consistent with asymptotic freedom, and remains near tree-level throughout the momentum range shown.
- The infrared limit of the vertex satisfies the Taylor condition: \(\lim_{p\to 0} \Gamma^{abc}_\mu(k;q,p) = i g_d f^{abc} q_\mu\), where \(p\) is the incoming ghost momentum.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger truncation scheme of von Smekal (1997) and Fischer (2002) by directly testing the assumption of a bare ghost-gluon vertex used in those propagator calculations. The results support the Zwanziger-Gribov confinement scenario, which requires ghost dominance and a bare vertex in the infrared, and are consistent with independent lattice results by Cucchieri (2004). The findings contradict the older Mandelstam approximation (Mandelstam 1979), which neglected ghost contributions and predicted a diverging gluon dressing function. The nearly tree-level vertex enables future studies of the running coupling and extensions to QCD with dynamical quarks.
