# Double deeply virtual Compton scattering with positron beams at SoLID

> Double Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DDVCS) is the only experimental channel for the determination of the dependence of the Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) on both the average and the transferred momentum independently. The physics observables of the electron induced di-muon production reaction $\vv{e}^{\pm}p \to e^{\pm}pμ^+μ^-$ off unpolarized hydrogen are discussed. Their measurement with the high luminosity and large acceptance SoLID spectrometer at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility, using polarized and unpolarized positron and electron beams at 11 GeV is investigated. This experimental configuration is shown to provide unprecedented access to the GPDs with the determination of the real and imaginary parts of the Compton Form Factor ${\mathcal H}$ in an unexplored phase space, and to enable an exploratory investigation of higher twist effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that measuring the double deeply virtual Compton scattering (DDVCS) process with both polarized electron and positron beams at the SoLID spectrometer provides unprecedented access to the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) by enabling the determination of the real and imaginary parts of the Compton form factor H in an unexplored phase space; however, this claim is qualified by the fact that the quantitative evaluation depends on the GPD model used to generate pseudo-data, and the twist-3 contribution to the polarized cross section vanishes in the current theoretical approach.

## Method

The authors performed a simulation study of the reaction e±p → e±pμ+μ− using the SoLID spectrometer at Jefferson Lab with 11 GeV beams, incorporating a muon detector for forward and large angle detection. They generated pseudo-data from the VGG model of GPDs, applied detector acceptance and efficiency corrections, and developed a local fitting method to extract Compton form factors from the φ-distributions of experimental observables. The study compared two experimental scenarios: a 2-observables scenario using only polarized electron beams, and a 4-observables scenario requiring both polarized electron and positron beams.

## Key numbers

- Luminosity scenarios of 10^36 cm−2·s−1 and 10^37 cm−2·s−1 were compared for the fitting study
- The experiment would operate at a luminosity of 1.2×10^37 cm−2·s−1, corresponding to a 3 μA beam intensity at 11 GeV
- Electron beam polarization was considered 85%; positron beam polarization was 30% based on source simulations
- Total data taking period of 100 days equally shared between electron and positron beams
- The solenoid field difference between opposite polarities is at the 10−5 level
- Kinematic bins centered at Q2 = 1.25 GeV2 with ΔQ2 = ±0.25 GeV2, −t = 0.15 GeV2 with Δt = ±0.05 GeV2, ξ = 0.135 with Δξ = ±0.015, and φ bins of ±15°
- Five ξ′ values were studied: −0.060, −0.015, 0.015, 0.045, and 0.075

## Context

This work extends the experimental program of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) measurements at Jefferson Lab, where DVCS is limited to accessing GPDs along the diagonals x = ±ξ. The DDVCS process, originally suggested by PhysRevLett.90.012001 and PhysRevLett.90.022001, decouples the experimental x- and ξ-dependences, enabling off-diagonal investigation of GPDs. The study builds on the local fitting technique developed for DVCS by Guidal (2008) and applies it to DDVCS for the first time. The results enable future global fits of GPDs using the VGG model framework and demonstrate the necessity of positron beams for unambiguous separation of the interference and DDVCS amplitudes, similar to the approach advocated by Voutier (2014) for DVCS.
