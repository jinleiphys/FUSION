# Quenching jets increases their flavor

> The widespread notion that jets quenched in a Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) are similar in their parton flavor composition to jets in vacuum is critically examined. We demonstrate that while the soft to semi-hard [low to intermediate transverse momentum ($p_T$)] sector of vacuum jets are predominantly bosonic i.e., composed of gluons, \emph{sufficiently} quenched jets can have an intermediate momentum sector that is predominantly fermionic, dominated by quarks and antiquarks. We demonstrate, using leading order perturbative QCD processes, that the rate of flavor conversion from a gluon traversing the QGP as part of a jet, to a quark or antiquark, versus the reverse process, grows steadily with falling $p_T$. Simple diagrammatic estimates are followed by a variety of realistic simulations in static media. The relation of this increase in flavor to the observed baryon enhancement at intermediate $p_T$ is studied in a fully realistic simulation.

## Key claim
The paper challenges the assumption that jets quenched in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) retain a parton flavor composition similar to vacuum jets, demonstrating that *sufficiently* quenched jets can develop an intermediate momentum sector that is predominantly fermionic (quarks and antiquarks) rather than bosonic (gluons), with the effect depending on the degree of quenching and the momentum range considered.

## Method
The authors used leading-order perturbative QCD to compute flavor-conversion rates for semi-hard partons scattering in a static QGP medium, considering both QCD annihilation processes (gluon fusion to quark-antiquark pairs and reverse) and QCD Compton scattering (gluon-quark scattering and reverse). They solved the Boltzmann equation using the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) kinetic approach for a hard gluon evolving in an infinite static medium at temperature T=0.25 GeV, and also performed realistic simulations using the JETSCAPE framework with LBT and MATTER+MARTINI event generators.

## Key numbers
- Initial gluon energy E0 = 25 GeV, QGP temperature T = 0.25 GeV, QCD coupling g = 2
- For annihilation processes with Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics: ratio of gluon-to-quark conversion rate to reverse process = Nf/CF = 2.25 (with Nf=3, CF=4/3)
- Using full quantum statistics increases the annihilation ratio by more than 50% and introduces mild energy dependence
- Combined rate for a semi-hard gluon (2-5 GeV) to convert to a quark or antiquark: R(g→q+q̄) ≈ 0.07 GeV ≈ 0.35/fm
- This rate implies conversion within approximately 3 fm of QGP traversal at T = 0.25 GeV
- At late times (t=10 fm/c), fermions dominate over bosons in all momentum ranges and angles as chemical equilibration is reached

## Context
This work extends the understanding of jet modification beyond the traditional focus on energy loss and gluon radiation, which was established by earlier formalisms such as those of Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (2000), Salgado-Wiedemann (2002), Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (2002), and Wang (2001). The authors build on the AMY kinetic theory approach (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2002) and the multi-stage jet modification framework developed within JETSCAPE (Cao et al., 2017; JETSCAPE Collaboration, 2021, 2022). The results enable future studies connecting the fermion enhancement in quenched jets to the observed baryon enhancement at intermediate transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions, and suggest that jets could be used as probes of chemical equilibration in the QGP.
