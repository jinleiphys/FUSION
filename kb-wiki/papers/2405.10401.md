# Analytical insights into the interplay of momentum, multiplicity and the speed of sound in heavy-ion collisions

> We introduce a minimal model of ultracentral heavy-ion collisions to study the relation between the speed of sound of the produced plasma and the final particles' energy and multiplicity. We discuss how the particles' multiplicity $N_{\textrm{tot}}$ and average energy $E_{\textrm{tot}}/N_{\textrm{tot}}$ is related to the speed of sound $c_s$ by $c_s^2=d \ln (E_{\textrm{tot}}/N_{\textrm{tot}})/d\ln N_{\textrm{tot}}$ if the fluid is inviscid, its speed of sound is constant and all final particles can be measured. We show that finite rapidity cuts on the particles' multiplicity $N$ and energy $E$ introduce corrections between $c_s^2$ and $d \ln (E/N)/d\ln N$ that depend on the system's lifetime. We study analytically these deviations with the Gubser hydrodynamic solution, finding that, for ultrarelativistic bosons, they scale as the ratio of the freezeout temperature $T_{\mathrm{FO}}$ over the maximum initial temperature of the fluid $T_{0}$; the non-thermodynamic aspect of these corrections is highlighted through their dependence on the system's initial conditions.

## Key claim

For an inviscid fluid with constant speed of sound, the speed of sound squared equals the logarithmic derivative of average energy per particle with respect to total multiplicity, but this identity only holds exactly when all final particles are measured; finite rapidity cuts introduce corrections that depend on the system's lifetime and vanish only in the limit of asymptotically low freezeout temperature.

## Method

The authors introduce a minimal model of ultracentral heavy-ion collisions using inviscid relativistic hydrodynamics with a constant speed of sound, studying a one-parameter family of solutions where the temperature field is globally rescaled. They derive analytical expressions for the relation between speed of sound and particle observables, then test these relations numerically using 3+1D hydrodynamic simulations with MUSIC and analytically using the Gubser hydrodynamic solution for ultrarelativistic bosons.

## Key numbers

- For the Gubser solution, the slope observable deviates from the true speed of sound as: \( d\ln\langle p_T\rangle/d\ln N_{\mathrm{mr}} \approx 1/3 - (2/3)/(1+q^2\tau_0^2) (T_{\mathrm{FO}}/T_0)^{3/2} \) for \(c_s^2=1/3\)
- In typical ultracentral collisions at RHIC or LHC, \(T_{\mathrm{FO}}/T_0 \approx 0.15\)–\(0.4\), leading to appreciable deviations between \(c_s^2\) and its estimator for \(T_{\mathrm{FO}}/T_0 \geq 0.3\)
- The 1+1D model uses a Gaussian initial temperature profile \(T(\tau_0, r) = T_0 e^{-r^2/(2\sigma^2)}\) with \(\sigma = 5\) fm and \(T_0^\star = 0.500\) GeV, varying the normalization by factors \(\alpha = 1.000, 1.005, \ldots, 1.100\)

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Gardim et al. (2020) that the speed of sound can be extracted from the relation between mean transverse momentum and multiplicity in ultracentral collisions, which was subsequently applied by the CMS collaboration (2024). The authors provide analytical understanding of the limitations identified in numerical studies by Nijs et al. (2023), showing that finite rapidity cuts and nonzero freezeout temperature introduce corrections that depend on initial conditions and system lifetime, rather than purely thermodynamic effects.
