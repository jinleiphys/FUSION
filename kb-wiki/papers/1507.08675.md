# Range corrections in Proton Halo Nuclei

> We analyze the effects of finite-range corrections in halo effective field theory for S-wave proton halo nuclei. We calculate the charge radius to next-to-leading order and the astrophysical S-factor for low-energy proton capture to fifth order in the low-energy expansion. As an application, we confront our results with experimental data for the S-factor for proton capture on Oxygen-16 into the excited $1/2^+$ state of Fluorine-17. Our low-energy theory is characterized by a systematic low-energy expansion, which can be used to quantify an energy-dependent model error to be utilized in data fitting. Finally, we show that the existence of proton halos is suppressed by the need for two fine tunings in the underlying theory.

## Key claim

Finite-range corrections in halo effective field theory for S-wave proton halo nuclei are systematically included up to fifth order in the low-energy expansion, and the existence of proton halos is suppressed because they require two fine tunings in the underlying theory (the scattering length and the effective range must both be large), unlike neutron halos which require only one.

## Method

The authors constructed a halo effective field theory (Halo EFT) Lagrangian with an auxiliary halo field, including Coulomb interactions between the core and valence proton through the full Coulomb Green's function. They calculated the charge form factor to next-to-leading order (NLO) and the astrophysical S-factor for low-energy radiative proton capture to fifth order in the low-energy expansion, using dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS) and matching to the Coulomb-modified effective range expansion.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb momentum is defined as \(k_\mathrm{C} = Z_\mathrm{c} \alpha m_\mathrm{R}\), where \(\alpha\) is the fine structure constant, \(Z_\mathrm{c}\) is the core proton number, and \(m_\mathrm{R}\) is the reduced mass of the proton-core system.
- The LO asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) is given by \(A_\mathrm{LO} = \sqrt{6 k_\mathrm{C} / \tilde{H}(\gamma, k_\mathrm{C})} \, \Gamma(1 + k_\mathrm{C}/\gamma)\), where \(\gamma = \sqrt{2 m_\mathrm{R} B}\) is the binding momentum and \(B\) is the one-proton separation energy.
- The Coulomb-modified effective range is obtained from the ANC as \(r_0 = (1/(3 k_\mathrm{C})) [\tilde{H}(\gamma, k_\mathrm{C}) - 6 k_\mathrm{C} \Gamma(1 + k_\mathrm{C}/\gamma)^2 / A^2]\).
- The Coulomb-modified scattering length is given by \(a_0 = -2 / [4 k_\mathrm{C} H(-i k_\mathrm{C}/\gamma) + \gamma^2 r_0]\).
- The function \(\tilde{H}(\gamma, k_\mathrm{C})\) expands as \(\tilde{H}(\gamma, k_\mathrm{C}) = 1 - \gamma^2/(5 k_\mathrm{C}^2) + \gamma^4/(7 k_\mathrm{C}^4) + \dots\) for \(\gamma \ll k_\mathrm{C}\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculation of proton halo nuclei in Halo EFT by Ryberg et al. (2013) to include finite-range and higher-order electromagnetic effects. It also builds on the inclusion of Coulomb interactions in EFT by Kong and Ravndal (1999) and the study of Coulomb momentum scaling by Higa et al. (2008). The results enable systematic, order-by-order fitting to experimental radiative capture data with quantified theoretical uncertainties, as demonstrated for proton capture on Oxygen-16 into the excited \(1/2^+\) state of Fluorine-17.
