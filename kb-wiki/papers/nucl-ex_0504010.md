# First observation of in-medium modifications of the omega meson

> The photoproduction of omega mesons on nuclei has been investigated using the Crystal Barrel/TAPS experiment at the ELSA tagged photon facility in Bonn. The aim is to study possible in-medium modifications of the omega meson via the reaction A(gamma, omega)X. Results obtained for Nb are compared to a reference measurement on a liquid hydrogen target. While for recoiling, long-lived mesons (pi, eta and etaprime), which decay outside of the nucleus, a difference in the lineshape for the two data samples is not observed, we find a significant enhancement towards lower masses for omega mesons with low momenta produced on the Nb target.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of a downward shift in the omega meson mass when produced in a nuclear medium (niobium target) compared to vacuum, but only for omega mesons with momenta less than 500 MeV/c; the effect is a superposition of vacuum decays at the nominal mass with modified decays inside the nucleus, and the extracted in-medium mass depends strongly on assumptions about the fraction of decays occurring in vacuum.

## Method

The CBELSA/TAPS collaboration photoproduced omega mesons on a niobium target and a liquid hydrogen reference target using a tagged photon beam from the ELSA accelerator, detecting the omega meson via its decay into pi0 gamma. The invariant mass distributions of pi0 gamma pairs were compared between the two targets after background subtraction, with a momentum cut of |p_omega| < 500 MeV/c applied to select low-momentum omega mesons that are more likely to decay inside the nucleus. The vacuum-decay component was subtracted by matching the high-mass side of the niobium spectrum to the liquid hydrogen spectrum, and the remaining signal was fitted with a Voigt function to extract the in-medium mass.

## Key numbers

* In-medium omega meson mass at an estimated average nuclear density of 0.6 rho_0: M_medium = [722_{-2}^{+2}(stat)_{-5}^{+35}(syst)] MeV/c^2
* This corresponds to a lowering of the omega mass by 8% relative to the vacuum value of 782 MeV/c^2
* The width of the in-medium signal is Gamma = 55 MeV/c^2 (FWHM), dominated by experimental resolution
* The fraction of decays occurring inside the nuclear medium was estimated at 16% from transport code calculations, with 3% of events distorted by final state interactions in the mass range 0.6 GeV/c^2 < M_pi0gamma < 0.9 GeV/c^2
* The vacuum-decay fraction subtracted from the niobium spectrum was 75% in the central analysis, varied between 80% and 45% for systematic uncertainty estimation

## Context

This work extends previous dilepton measurements of vector meson in-medium modifications by the CERES collaboration at CERN (Agakichiev et al., 1995) and the KEK-PS E325 collaboration (Ozawa et al., 2001), which suffered from small branching ratios and difficulty isolating the omega signal from the rho meson. The use of the omega to pi0 gamma decay channel (branching ratio ~9%) provides a cleaner probe than dilepton channels, as proposed by Muhlich et al. (2004) and Messchendorp et al. (2002). The result enables systematic studies of the momentum dependence of the omega-nucleus potential and tests of theoretical predictions for mass shifts ranging from 20 to 150 MeV/c^2 (Klingl et al., 1997; Rapp et al., 1997).
