# G-Matrix Equation in the Resonating-Group Method

> The G-matrix equation is most straightforwardly formulated in the resonating-group method if the quark-exchange kernel is directly used as the driving term for the infinite sum of all the ladder diagrams. The inherent energy-dependence involved in the exchange term of the normalization kernel plays the essential role to define the off-shell T-matrix uniquely when the complete Pauli-forbidden state exists. We analyze this using a simple solvable model with no quark-quark interaction, and calculating the most general T-matrix in the formulation developed by Noyes and Kowalski. This formulation gives a certain condition for the existence of the solution in the Lippmann-Schwinger resonating-group method. A new procedure to deal with the corrections for the reduced masses and the internal-energy terms in the Lambda N - Sigma N coupled-channel resonating-group equation is proposed.

## Key claim

The G-matrix equation in the resonating-group method can be formulated most straightforwardly by using the quark-exchange kernel directly as the driving term for the infinite sum of ladder diagrams, provided that the inherent energy-dependence in the exchange term of the normalization kernel is properly handled to define the off-shell T-matrix uniquely when a complete Pauli-forbidden state exists.

## Method

The authors analyze a simple solvable orthogonality condition model (OCM) with no quark-quark interaction, deriving the complete off-shell T-matrix analytically using the Noyes and Kowalski formulation. They show that the Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation for the RGM can be solved by decomposing the potential into a term containing the Pauli-forbidden state projection and a term that acts only in the Pauli-allowed space. A new procedure is proposed to handle corrections for reduced masses and internal-energy terms in the Lambda N - Sigma N coupled-channel resonating-group equation.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell T-matrix for the simple OCM is given by \(\langle \omega | T(\omega,\varepsilon) |\omega \rangle = -\langle \omega |u\rangle \langle u|\omega \rangle / \langle u| G^{(+)}_0(\omega) |u \rangle\), where \(u\) is the Pauli-forbidden state and \(G^{(+)}_0(\omega)\) is the free Green function.
- The complete off-shell T-matrix for the simple OCM is \(T(\omega,\varepsilon) = -|u\rangle \langle u| / \langle u| G^{(+)}_0(\omega) |u \rangle + (\omega-H_0)|u\rangle [1/(\omega-\varepsilon)] \langle u|(\omega-H_0)\).
- The modified RGM equation uses \(\varepsilon^{\rm exp} = E - E^{\rm exp}_{\rm int}\) and \(H^{\rm exp}_0 = -\hbar^2/(2\mu^{\rm exp}) (\partial/\partial \mathbf{r})^2\) with empirical masses, requiring an extra modification term \(\Delta G = \Lambda (\Delta E_{\rm int} + \Delta H_0) \Lambda - (\Delta E_{\rm int} + \Delta H_0)\).

## Context

This work extends the orthogonality condition model originally developed by Saito (1968, 1977) and the resonating-group method formalism of Wheeler (1937) and Wildermuth and Tang (1977). The Noyes (1965) and Kowalski (1965) method is applied to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the RGM. The proposed modification procedure for reduced masses and internal energies is applied to the Lambda N - Sigma N coupled-channel RGM calculations previously reported by Fujiwara et al. (1995, 2000), enabling a realistic treatment of baryon-baryon scattering kinematics while preserving the essential Pauli principle structure.
