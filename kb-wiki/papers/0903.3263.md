# Away-side asymmetry of jet correlation relative to reaction plane: a sensitive probe for jet in-medium modifications

> We proposed a new observable based on two particle azimuth correlation to study the away-side medium response in mid-central Au+Au collisions. We argue that a left/right asymmetry may appear at the away-side by selecting triggers separately in the left and right side of the reaction plane. A simple model estimation suggests that the magnitude of such asymmetry could reach 30% with details depends on the medium response mechanisms. This asymmetry, if observed, can help to distinguish competing theoretical models.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a left/right asymmetry in the away-side dihadron correlation can appear when triggers are selected separately on the left and right side of the reaction plane in mid-central Au+Au collisions, with the magnitude of such asymmetry depending on the specific medium response mechanism. The observable is presented as a way to distinguish competing theoretical models, but the authors note that residual flow effects can produce a comparable asymmetry at the 40-50% level, potentially larger than the real medium-induced asymmetry.

## Method

The authors performed a model estimation using a Glauber-based jet absorption picture with Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry, where dijets are generated according to binary collision density profiles with uniform orientation and a kT smearing of 0.4 radian. The survival probability for each jet is calculated as e^{-kappa I}, where the matter integral I uses a quadratic dependence of absorption in a longitudinally expanding medium with formation time tau=0.2 fm/c and kappa=0.7 to reproduce the centrality dependence of R_AA. For quenched away-side jets, medium responses are generated uniformly between the production point and the quenching point at angles pi +/- 1.1 relative to the trigger, with attenuation applied according to the same absorption formula.

## Key numbers

- The estimated magnitude of the away-side asymmetry could reach 30% in a simple model estimation for 30-35% centrality Au+Au collisions
- The average attenuation of medium response is <e^{-kappa c tau integral rho dl}> approximately e^{-1.3} for 30-35% centrality Au+Au collisions
- A typical reaction plane resolution of 0.8 used by RHIC experiments reduces the observed asymmetry by about 50%
- Residual flow assumed at 5% of the measured v_2^{assoc} can lead to an away-side asymmetry at the 40-50% level
- The kT smearing used in the model is 0.4 radian based on p+p data
- The fragmentation spread of the survived jet is chosen as 0.3 radian

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet quenching and medium response in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, specifically building on observations of the away-side double-shouldered structure at pi +/- 1.1 reported by PHENIX (Adler 2006) and STAR (Adams 2007). The authors note that previous reaction plane dependent studies by Jia (2005), Feng (2008), and McCumber (2008) did not observe left/right asymmetry because they folded trigger angle bins into the 0-pi/2 range. The proposed observable could help distinguish between competing medium response mechanisms such as conical flow (Casalderrey-Solana 2006) versus large-angle gluon radiation (Polosa 2007), and may also help understand possible influences on v_2 measurements from jet-medium coupling.
