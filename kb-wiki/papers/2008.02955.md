# Constraining the nuclear symmetry energy and properties of neutron star from GW170817 by Bayesian analysis

> Based on the distribution of tidal deformabilities and component masses of binary neutron star merger GW170817, the parametric equation of states (EOS) are employed to probe the nuclear symmetry energy and the properties of neutron star. To obtain a proper distribution of the parameters of the EOS that is consistent with the observation, Bayesian analysis is used and the constraints of causality and maximum mass are considered. From this analysis, it is found that the symmetry energy at twice the saturation density of nuclear matter can be constrained within $E_{sym}(2{ρ_{0}})$ = $34.5^{+20.5}_{-2.3}$ MeV at 90\% credible level. Moreover, the constraints on the radii and dimensionless tidal deformabilities of canonical neutron stars are also demonstrated through this analysis, and the corresponding constraints are 10.80 km $< R_{1.4} <$ 13.20 km and $133 < Λ_{1.4} < 686$ at 90\% credible level, with the most probable value of $\bar{R}_{1.4}$ = 12.60 km and $\barΛ_{1.4}$ = 500, respectively. With respect to the prior, our result (posterior result) prefers a softer EOS, corresponding to a lower expected value of symmetry energy, a smaller radius and a smaller tidal deformability.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy at twice saturation density is constrained to \(E_{sym}(2\rho_0) = 34.5^{+20.5}_{-2.3}\) MeV at 90% credible level, but this result depends on the specific parametric EOS model and the prior assumptions about the EOS parameters \(K_0\), \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)\), and \(L\) being fixed to their most probable values.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent parametric EOS model for neutron star matter, with the symmetric nuclear matter energy and symmetry energy expanded around saturation density using parameters \(K_0\), \(J_0\), \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)\), \(L\), \(K_{sym}\), and \(J_{sym}\). They generated two million EOS via Monte Carlo sampling, screened them by causality and the requirement of supporting a maximum mass of at least 2.14 \(M_\odot\) (from PSR J0740+6620), then performed Bayesian inference using the joint distribution of tidal deformabilities and component masses from GW170817 as the likelihood.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at twice saturation density: \(E_{sym}(2\rho_0) = 34.5^{+20.5}_{-2.3}\) MeV at 90% credible level (posterior), compared to prior of \(54.5^{+4.0}_{-21.5}\) MeV.
- Radius of canonical (1.4 \(M_\odot\)) neutron star: \(10.80\ \mathrm{km} < R_{1.4} < 13.20\ \mathrm{km}\) at 90% credible level, with most probable value \(\bar{R}_{1.4} = 12.60\ \mathrm{km}\).
- Dimensionless tidal deformability of canonical neutron star: \(133 < \Lambda_{1.4} < 686\) at 90% credible level, with most probable value \(\bar{\Lambda}_{1.4} = 500\).
- Prior distribution of \(R_{1.4}\): \(11.30\ \mathrm{km} \leq R_{1.4} \leq 13.30\ \mathrm{km}\) at 90% credible level, with most probable value 12.9 km.
- Prior distribution of \(\Lambda_{1.4}\): \(217 \leq \Lambda_{1.4} \leq 723\) at 90% credible level, with most probable value 620.
- Parameter space constraints at 90% credible level: \(-400.0\ \mathrm{MeV} < K_{sym} < 22.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(-30.0\ \mathrm{MeV} < J_{sym} < 800.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(-98.0\ \mathrm{MeV} < J_0 < 400.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian approach of Lim et al. (2019) and De et al. (2018), who used chiral effective field theory and GW170817 data respectively, by employing a parametric EOS that directly extracts the symmetry energy. The results are consistent with the range of \(E_{sym}(2\rho_0)\) between 30 and 60 MeV found in prior studies by Xie and Li (2019), Wen et al. (2019), Zhang and Li (2019), and Zhou et al. (2020), but the posterior prefers a softer EOS with lower symmetry energy, smaller radius, and smaller tidal deformability than the prior. The constraints on the EOS parameter space reduce it to about 50% of the original volume, enabling tighter predictions for neutron star structure from future gravitational wave events.
