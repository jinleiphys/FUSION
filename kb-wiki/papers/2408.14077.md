# The effect of vorticity on the dynamical magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions

> Magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions are pivotal and subject to diverse factors. In this study, we quantitatively investigate the impact of fluid vorticity on the evolution of magnetic fields in the 20-50\% centrality class in Au+Au collisions, with collision energies of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=(7.7, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200)$ GeV. Our results indicate that fluid vorticity leads to a delay in the evolution of the magnetic field, in which this effect becomes more pronounced as the collision energy decreases. Additionally, we have calculated the mean magnetic field values on the freeze-out hypersurface for various collision energies. Our simulation results align with the values inferred from experimental data of $\barΛ-Λ$, within the error margins.

## Key claim

Fluid vorticity in the quark-gluon plasma significantly slows the decay of the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, with this effect becoming more pronounced as collision energy decreases; however, the contribution of vorticity to the magnetic field on the freeze-out hypersurface is relatively small because the hydrodynamic evolution starts late, after the magnetic field has already decayed substantially.

## Method

The authors solve Maxwell's equations separately from viscous hydrodynamics using the weak-field approximation, where the back reaction of electromagnetic fields on the medium is neglected. The (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics framework provides the background fluid velocity, temperature, baryon number density, and baryon current, with initial conditions generated from the SMASH hadronic transport model averaged over 5000 events per centrality bin. Maxwell's equations are solved in Milne coordinates using a Yee-grid algorithm, with the medium current including the charge density term \(n_q u^\mu\) and Ohm's law term \(\sigma F^{\mu\nu}_M u_\nu\), while the chiral conductivity is set to zero.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = (7.7, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200)\) GeV for Au+Au collisions at 20-50% centrality
- Impact parameters for the 20-50% centrality bin: \(b = (8.873, 8.844, 8.856, 8.861, 8.846, 8.892, 8.889)\) fm across the seven collision energies
- Initial proper times for hydrodynamics: \(\tau_0 = 3.2\) fm at 7.7 GeV, decreasing to \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm at 200 GeV
- Electrical conductivity set to \(\sigma/T = 0.4\)
- Freeze-out condition: local energy density \(e_{\rm frz} = 0.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Charge density relation: \(n_Q = 0.4 e \, n_B\), where the factor 0.4 is the proton-to-nucleon ratio in Au+Au collisions
- The magnetic field on the freeze-out hypersurface from simulation agrees within error margins with values inferred from \(\bar{\Lambda}\)-\(\Lambda\) spin polarization splitting using the formula \(eB \approx (M_N T / 0.613)(P_{\bar{\Lambda}} - P_{\Lambda})\) with \(M_N = 0.938\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Huang 2022) on dynamical magnetic fields in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, which found that longitudinal QGP expansion suppresses the magnetic field. It builds on the qualitative estimate by Guo (2019) that fluid vorticity induces a magnetic field proportional to \(eB = (e^2/4\pi) n_q A \omega\). The results are compared with experimental data on \(\bar{\Lambda}\)-\(\Lambda\) spin polarization splitting from the STAR Collaboration (2017, 2023) and STAR preliminary results. The framework enables future investigation of more sensitive magnetic field probes such as directed flow, and highlights the need to include medium effects during the pre-equilibrium stage, particularly at lower collision energies where hydrodynamics starts later.
