# LSND anomaly from CPT violation in four-neutrino models

> The LSND signal for \barν_μto \barν_e oscillations has prompted supposition that there may be a fourth light neutrino or that CPT is violated. Neither explanation provides a good fit to all existing neutrino data. We examine the even more speculative possibility that a four-neutrino model with CPT violation can explain the LSND effect and remain consistent with all other data. We find that models with a 3+1 mass structure in the neutrino sector are viable; a 2+2 structure is permitted only in the antineutrino sector.

## Key claim

A four-neutrino model with CPT violation can explain the LSND anomaly while remaining consistent with all other neutrino data, but only if the neutrino sector has a 3+1 mass structure; a 2+2 structure is viable only in the antineutrino sector, and a pure 2+2 structure in the neutrino sector is strongly disfavored even with CPT violation.

## Method

The authors constructed a four-neutrino framework with separate unitary mixing matrices U and Ubar for neutrinos and antineutrinos, allowing different mass-squared differences and mixing angles for particles and antiparticles. They derived approximate oscillation probability formulas for 3+1 and 2+2 mass structures, then compared the allowed parameter regions against existing experimental constraints from LSND, KARMEN, Bugey, CDHSW, GALLEX, CCFR, solar neutrino experiments, KamLAND, and atmospheric neutrino data. They evaluated the viability of pure 3+1, pure 2+2, and hybrid (3+1 for neutrinos, 2+2 for antineutrinos or vice versa) configurations.

## Key numbers

- The LSND signal for antineutrino oscillations is at the 3.3 sigma level.
- The allowed LSND parameter region lies along the line sin^2(2theta_L) * (delta m^2_L)^1.64 = 0.0025 between delta m^2_L ~ 0.2 and 1 eV^2, with a small allowed region near delta m^2_L ~ 7 eV^2 and sin^2(2theta_L) = 0.004.
- For 3+1 models with CPT conservation, the goodness of fit to all data is at most 5.6 x 10^{-3}.
- For 2+2 models with CPT conservation, the goodness of fit to all data is 1.6 x 10^{-6}.
- The 99% C.L. limit on sterile content in solar neutrino oscillations is cos^2(alpha) <= 0.45 using the standard solar model; this relaxes to cos^2(alpha) <= 0.61 if the ^8B flux is allowed to be free.
- The 99% C.L. limit from atmospheric data is (2/3) sin^2(alpha) + (1/3) sin^2(bar-alpha) <= 0.35.
- For the 3+1 model with CPT violation, the allowed delta bar m^2_L values must be near 1 or 7 eV^2 if the antineutrino sector is 3+1; the entire LSND-allowed range is possible if the antineutrino sector is 2+2.

## Context

This work extends prior attempts to explain the LSND anomaly using either four-neutrino models with a sterile neutrino (which were disfavored by combined Bugey and CDHSW constraints, as shown by Grimus and Schwetz (2001) and others) or three-neutrino models with CPT violation (which were excluded at the 3 sigma level by atmospheric data, as shown by Murayama (2002)). The paper combines both ideas into a single framework. The results enable experimental tests: a positive MiniBooNE result above the GALLEX+CDHSW upper bound would rule out 3+1 models with CPT violation, while a null result or one below that bound would leave such models viable. The MiniBooNE experiment, then taking data, could distinguish between the scenarios.
