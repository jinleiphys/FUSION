# Analysis of the Pion-Nucleus Scattering within the Folding and the Kisslinger Type Potentials

> Elastic cross sections are calculated and compared with the data on $π^{\pm}$ scattering on $^{28}$Si, $^{40}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{208}$Pb nuclei in the energy range from 130 to 290 MeV. To this end, both the folding optical potential (OP) and the local modified Kisslinger-type OP were calculated, and then the $πA$ cross sections were obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation to account for the relativization and distortion wave effects. In the folding OPs, the parameters of the elementary $πN$ amplitude were fitted when describing the $πA$ scattering data, and thus the essential in-medium effect on the parameters of the $πN$ amplitude was established since the pion is scattered not on a free but on a bound nuclear nucleon. Fairly good agreement with experimental data was obtained for both models of optical potentials and their forms turn out to be in coincidence in the region of their surfaces.

## Key claim

The folding optical potential and the local modified Kisslinger-type potential both yield fairly good agreement with experimental pion-nucleus elastic scattering data across the Delta-33 resonance region (130-290 MeV), but the folding model reveals that the in-medium pion-nucleon total cross section is about 1.6 times smaller than the free pion-nucleon cross section, an effect the abstract attributes to the pion scattering on bound rather than free nucleons.

## Method

Elastic differential cross sections for pi+ and pi- scattering on 28Si, 40Ca, 58Ni, and 208Pb were calculated using two microscopic optical potentials: a folding potential that integrates the nuclear density with a three-parameter pion-nucleon amplitude (total cross section sigma, real-to-imaginary ratio alpha, slope beta), and a local modified Kisslinger-type potential with 12 fitted parameters. Both potentials were used to solve the Klein-Gordon equation, and the folding model parameters were fitted by chi-squared minimization against experimental data at scattering angles below 80 degrees.

## Key numbers

* For pi- + 28Si at 130 MeV: fitted sigma = 7.00 fm^2, alpha = 0.84, beta = 0.93 fm^2 (chi^2/k = 0.122).
* For pi- + 28Si at 162 MeV: fitted sigma = 9.05 fm^2, alpha = 0.49, beta = 0.50 fm^2 (chi^2/k = 0.398).
* For pi- + 28Si at 180 MeV: fitted sigma = 8.97 fm^2, alpha = 0.37, beta = 0.44 fm^2 (chi^2/k = 1.262).
* For pi- + 28Si at 291 MeV: fitted sigma = 4.86 fm^2, alpha = -0.80, beta = 0.39 fm^2 (chi^2/k = 1.169).
* For pi- + 208Pb at 162 MeV: fitted sigma = 10.00 fm^2, alpha = 0.30, beta = 1.01 fm^2 (chi^2/k = 0.644).
* For pi- + 208Pb at 291 MeV: fitted sigma = 4.97 fm^2, alpha = -0.93, beta = 0.64 fm^2 (chi^2/k = 0.592).
* The in-medium pion-nucleon total cross section sigma_av is about 1.6 times smaller than the free pion-nucleon cross section near the resonance maximum at about 170 MeV.
* The slope parameter beta_av for pion scattering on bound nucleons in 28Si is about 1.6 times smaller than for free nucleons at energies near 170 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion-nucleus scattering using the Glauber multiple-scattering theory (Faldt 1968, Bjornenak 1970) and the Kisslinger potential (Kisslinger 1955) by applying both a folding potential (Lukyanov, Zemlyanaya, Lukyanov 2006) and a local Kisslinger-type potential (John Satchler 1996) to a consistent set of nuclei across the Delta-33 resonance. The results enable systematic comparison of in-medium pion-nucleon parameters with free values from Carter (1971) and Arndt (1985), and suggest that combined analysis of elastic and inelastic scattering to low-lying collective states could distinguish between the two potential models.
