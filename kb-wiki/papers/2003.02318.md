# Generalized Contact Formalism Analysis of the $^4$He$(e,e'pN)$ Reaction

> Measurements of short-range correlations in exclusive $^4$He$(e,e'pN)$ reactions are analyzed using the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF). We consider both instant-form and light-cone formulations with both the AV18 and local N2LO(1.0) nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials. We find that kinematic distributions, such as the reconstructed pair opening angle, recoil neutron momentum distribution, and pair center of mass motion, as well as the measured missing energy, missing mass distributions, are all well reproduced by GCF calculations. The missing momentum dependence of the measured $^4$He$(e,e'pN)$ / $^4$He$(e,e'p)$ cross-section ratios, sensitive to nature of the $NN$ interaction at short-distacnes, are also well reproduced by GCF calculations using either interaction and formulation. This gives credence to the GCF scale-separated factorized description of the short-distance many-body nuclear wave-function.

## Key claim

The Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) calculations, using either instant-form or light-cone formulations with either the AV18 or local N2LO(1.0) nucleon-nucleon potentials, reproduce the measured kinematic distributions and cross-section ratios of the $^4$He$(e,e'pN)$ reaction, supporting the scale-separated factorized description of the short-distance many-body nuclear wave-function. The analysis shows that the seemingly constant $^4$He$(e,e'pp)$ / $^4$He$(e,e'p)$ ratio reported by Korover (2014) is consistent with the increasing ratio observed by Schmidt (2020) when both datasets are analyzed within the same theoretical framework that accounts for different kinematics and experimental acceptances.

## Method

The authors implemented a weighted Monte Carlo event generator based on GCF cross-section expressions in both instant-form and light-cone formulations, using the AV18 and local N2LO(1.0) nucleon-nucleon potentials. They modeled radiative effects using the peaking approximation and accounted for reaction mechanism effects including nuclear transparency and single charge exchange (SCX) reactions, then propagated the pseudo-events through a model of the experimental setup including spectrometer acceptances and event selection criteria identical to those used in the original analysis by Korover (2014).

## Key numbers

- $Q^2 \approx 2$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$ and $x_B > 1.1$ for the kinematics of the measurements analyzed
- Three kinematic settings covering missing momentum ranges of [400-600], [540-720], and [660-820] MeV/$c$
- The width of the SRC pair center-of-mass momentum distribution $\sigma_{CM} = 100 \pm 20$ MeV/$c$, as extracted from the original analysis by Korover (2014)
- SCX probability $P^{SCX}_A = 1.5 \pm 1.5\%$, taken from the original analysis by Korover (2014)
- Nuclear transparency $T_A = 0.7$ with an assumed $\pm 20\%$ uncertainty, taken from the original analysis by Korover (2014)
- Momentum cut-off $k_{cut-off}$ varied uniformly between 200-300 MeV/$c$
- Excitation energy $E^*$ of the residual $A-2$ nucleus varied uniformly between 0-10 MeV
- Normalization constants for AV18 and N2LO calculations differ by factors of 1.06, 0.78, and 0.52 for the $p_{miss} \approx$ 500, 625, and 750 MeV/$c$ settings, respectively

## Context

This work extends the GCF analysis framework previously applied to heavier nuclei by Schmidt (2020) to the $^4$He$(e,e'pN)$ measurements from small-acceptance spectrometers reported by Korover (2014). The analysis resolves an apparent inconsistency between the constant $^4$He$(e,e'pp)$ / $^4$He$(e,e'p)$ ratio reported by Korover (2014) and the increasing ratio observed by Schmidt (2020) in heavier nuclei from $^{12}$C to $^{208}$Pb, demonstrating that proper accounting for phase-space and acceptance effects reconciles the two measurements. The results enable future studies of the transition from tensor to scalar dominance in the nucleon-nucleon interaction at shorter distances and support the use of both AV18 and N2LO potentials in calculations of high-density nuclear systems.
