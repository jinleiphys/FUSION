# Baryonic matter and the medium modification of the baryon masses

> We investigate the properties of baryonic matter within the framework of the in-medium modified chiral soliton model by taking into account the effects of surrounding baryonic environment on the properties of in-medium baryons. The internal parameters of the model are determined based on nuclear phenomenology at nonstrange sector and fitted by reproducing nuclear matter properties near the saturation point. We discuss the equations of state in different nuclear environments such as symmetric nuclear matter, neutron and strange matters. We show that the results for the equations of state are in good agreement with the phenomenology of nuclear matter. We also discuss how the SU(3) baryons masses undergo changes in these various types of nuclear matter.

# Baryonic matter and the medium modification of the baryon masses

## Key claim

The authors extend the pion mean-field approach to describe SU(3) baryon masses in symmetric nuclear matter, asymmetric nuclear matter, neutron matter, and strange baryonic matter, using density-dependent functionals parametrized and fitted entirely in the nonstrange (SU(2) sector) based on nuclear phenomenology near saturation. The results for equations of state agree with phenomenology only within the linear-density approximation up to about twice normal nuclear density, beyond which higher-order corrections would be needed.

## Method

The authors start from the collective Hamiltonian of the chiral quark-soliton model in free space, which includes classical soliton mass, rotational 1/Nc corrections, and flavor SU(3) symmetry-breaking terms. They introduce density-dependent functionals f_cl, f_1, f_2, f_0, and f_s that modify the classical soliton mass, moments of inertia, and symmetry-breaking parameters in medium, parametrized as linear functions of normalized baryon density λ = ρ/ρ0 (with additional isospin-asymmetry dependence for f_0). The three parameters for symmetric matter (C_cl, C_1, C_2) are fitted to reproduce the volume energy a_V = -16 MeV, zero pressure, and compressibility K_0 = 240 MeV at saturation; the two parameters for asymmetric matter (C_num, C_den) are fitted to the symmetry energy a_sym = 32 MeV and its slope L_sym = 60 MeV (Set I) or 50 MeV (Set II). The binding energy per baryon is then computed from the in-medium baryon masses using Eq. (13), and the equations of state are compared with Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) predictions and experimental constraints.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ0 ~ 0.16–0.17 fm⁻³ (normal nuclear matter density)
- Volume energy a_V = -16 MeV (from Bethe-Weizsäcker formula)
- Compressibility K_0 = 240 MeV (chosen based on giant monopole resonance data)
- Fitted parameters for symmetric matter: C_cl = -0.0561, C_1 = 0.6434, C_2 = -0.1218
- Predicted skewness Q = -182 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation a_sym = 32 MeV (average value from 30–34 MeV range)
- Slope parameter L_sym = 60 MeV (Set I) or 50 MeV (Set II)
- Fitted parameters for asymmetry: Set I: C_num = 65.60, C_den = 0.60; Set II: C_num = 78.72, C_den = 0.92
- K_sym = -135 MeV (from Set I); K_tau = -495 MeV; K_(0,2) = -450 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon in symmetric matter at saturation: -16 MeV (by construction)
- Results shown up to λ = 3 (three times normal nuclear density)

## Context

This work extends the pion mean-field approach (Diakonov 1987, Witten 1979, 1983) and the medium-modified Skyrme model approach (Rakhimov 1996, Yakhshiev 2010, 2013, 2015) to consistently describe SU(3) baryon masses in various baryonic environments. It builds on prior work by Yang (2010, 2015, 2016, 2018, 2019, 2020) that applied the pion mean-field approach to free-space baryon properties, and on Jung (2012, 2013) and Hong (2018) who studied nucleon and Delta isobar properties in medium. The results for symmetric matter and neutron matter equations of state are compared with APR predictions (Akmal 1998), and the symmetry energy is compared with constraints from isobaric analog states (Danielewicz 2013). The approach enables future investigation of neutron star properties and strange baryonic matter at higher densities, though the authors note the linear-density approximation may require higher-order corrections beyond λ = 2.
