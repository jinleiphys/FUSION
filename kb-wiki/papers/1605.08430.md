# Supporting the search for the CEP location with nonlocal PNJL models constrained by Lattice QCD

> We investigate the possible location of the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram based on nonlocal covariant PNJL models including a vector interaction channel. The form factors of the covariant interaction are constrained by lattice QCD data for the quark propagator. The comparison of our results for the pressure including the pion contribution and the scaled pressure shift $ΔP / T^4$ vs $T/T_c$ with lattice QCD results shows a better agreement when Lorentzian formfactors for the nonlocal interactions and the wave function renormalization are considered. The strength of the vector coupling is used as a free parameter which influences results at finite baryochemical potential. It is used to adjust the slope of the pseudocritical temperature of the chiral phase transition at low baryochemical potential and the scaled pressure shift accessible in lattice QCD simulations. Our study, albeit presently performed at the meanfield level, supports the very existence of a critical point and favors its location within a region that is accessible in experiments at the NICA accelerator complex.

## Key claim

The study supports the existence of a critical endpoint (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram and favors its location within a region accessible to the NICA accelerator complex, but this conclusion is conditional on the choice of nonlocal form factors and vector coupling strength, and is presently performed only at the meanfield level.

## Method

The authors use nonlocal covariant PNJL models with two quark flavors, including scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector interaction channels, where the form factors are constrained by lattice QCD data for the quark propagator. The thermodynamic potential is computed in the mean-field approximation using the Matsubara imaginary time formalism, with the Polyakov loop potential taken as a function of temperature and chemical potential. The vector coupling strength \(G_V\) is treated as a free parameter and tuned to reproduce either the curvature of the pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD or the scaled pressure shift.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential: \(T_c(0) = 154 \pm 9\) MeV from lattice QCD (Bazavov 2011).
- Curvature of the pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD (Taylor expansion): \(\kappa = 0.059 (2) (4)\) (Kaczmarek 2011).
- Curvature from imaginary chemical potential method: \(\kappa = 0.1341 \pm 0.019\) (Bellwied 2015) and \(\kappa = 0.1215 \pm 0.018\) (Bonati 2015).
- For Set C with \(\eta_V = 0.0\): \((T_{\rm CEP}, \mu_{\rm CEP}) = (128.6\ \text{MeV}, 223.3\ \text{MeV})\).
- For Set C with \(\eta_V = 0.555\): \((T_{\rm CEP}, \mu_{\rm CEP}) = (69.9\ \text{MeV}, 319.1\ \text{MeV})\).
- \(T_c(0)\) values for different sets: 169.9 MeV (Set A), 171.3 MeV (Set B), 173.2 MeV (Set C), 200.9 MeV (local model).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonlocal PNJL models (Contrera 2010, 2012) by systematically comparing different form factor parametrizations (exponential and Lorentzian) against lattice QCD data for the quark propagator and thermodynamics. It contrasts with local NJL models (Bratovic 2012) which found no CEP for finite vector coupling, showing that nonlocal models with Lorentzian form factors (Set C) predict a robust CEP even with strong vector interactions. The results enable predictions for the beam energy scan programs at NICA and FAIR, suggesting that the CEP may be accessible at collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 6\) GeV, which is within the planned range of the MPD experiment at NICA.
