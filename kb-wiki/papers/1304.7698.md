# Constraints on a possible dibaryon from combined analysis of the $pn \to d π^+ π^-$ and $pn \to pn π^+ π^-$ cross sections

> We use recent data that show a narrow peak around $\sqrt{s}=2.37\ \text{GeV}$ in the $pn \to d π^+ π^-$ cross section, with about double strength at the peak than in the analogous $pn \to d π^0 π^0$ reaction, and, assuming that it is due to the excitation of a dibaryon resonance, we evaluate the cross section for the $pn \to pn π^+ π^-$ reaction, with the final $pn$ unbound but with the same quantum numbers as the deuteron. We use accurate techniques to determine the final state interaction in the case of the $pn$ forming a deuteron or a positive energy state, which allow us to get the $pn \to pn π^+ π^-$ cross section with $pn$ in I=0 and S=1, that turns out to be quite close or saturates the experimental $pn \to pn π^+ π^-$ total cross section around $\sqrt{s} = 2.37\ \text{GeV}$, depending on the angular momentum assumed. We then parametrize a background with different methods, and the sum of the resonant and background contributions is fitted to present data. The resulting cross section exceeds the experimental results in the region of the resonant peak, showing a problem in the dibaryon hypothesis. Yet, in view of the dispersion of present experimental data, and the scarce information around $\sqrt{s} = 2.37\ \text{GeV}$, a call is made for precise measurements of the $pn \to pnπ^+π^-$ reaction around this energy, to further clarify this issue.

## Key claim

Assuming that the narrow peak observed in the $pn \to d \pi^+ \pi^-$ cross section around $\sqrt{s}=2.37\ \text{GeV}$ is due to a dibaryon resonance, the predicted resonant contribution to the $pn \to pn \pi^+ \pi^-$ cross section nearly saturates the experimental total cross section at that energy; when a fitted background is added, the sum exceeds the experimental data, posing a problem for the dibaryon hypothesis, though the authors call for more precise measurements due to data dispersion and scarcity.

## Method

The authors used a chiral unitary approach with on-shell factorization of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to relate the $pn \to d \pi^+ \pi^-$ and $pn \to pn \pi^+ \pi^-$ cross sections via final-state interactions of the $pn$ pair with deuteron quantum numbers ($I=0$, $S=1$). They extracted the $pn$ scattering amplitude from experimental phase shifts (Arndt analysis) and the deuteron coupling from its binding energy, then computed the resonant cross section for the unbound $pn$ case for both $L=0$ and $L=2$ orbital angular momenta between the pion pair and the nucleon system. They parametrized the non-resonant background using either a phase-space form or a polynomial, and fitted the sum of resonant and background contributions to existing $pn \to pn \pi^+ \pi^-$ data.

## Key numbers

- The peak cross section for $pn \to d \pi^+ \pi^-$ is taken as $0.44\ \text{mb}$ at $\sqrt{s}=2.37\ \text{GeV}$ (after subtracting $I=1$ background and phase-space corrections from the measured $0.5\ \text{mb}$).
- The predicted ratio $\sigma^{(pn)}/\sigma^{(d)}$ for the charged-pion reactions is $1.2$ for $L=2$, compared to $0.9$ in the earlier work of Wilkin for the neutral-pion case.
- For $L=2$, the predicted resonant $pn \to pn \pi^+ \pi^-$ cross section is about $25\%$ below the experimental datum at $\sqrt{s} \simeq 2.37\ \text{GeV}$; for $L=0$ it is barely below the datum.
- The $pn \to d \pi^0 \pi^0$ peak cross section used for the $NN$ inelasticity test is $0.27\ \text{mb}$ (updated from the earlier $0.4\ \text{mb}$ used in Wilkin (2012)).
- The dibaryon resonance is assumed to have mass $M_R = 2.37\ \text{GeV}$ and width $\Gamma = 70\ \text{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the test proposed by Wilkin (2012), who related the $pn \to d \pi^0 \pi^0$ and $pn \to pn \pi^0 \pi^0$ cross sections under the dibaryon hypothesis, but used approximate final-state interaction techniques and had no data for the latter reaction. The present paper uses improved chiral unitary methods for final-state interactions and exploits new data from Clement et al. (2013) for $pn \to d \pi^+ \pi^-$, which show a peak twice as strong as in the neutral channel as required by isospin symmetry, allowing direct comparison with existing $pn \to pn \pi^+ \pi^-$ data from Dakhno et al. (1973), Brunt et al. (1969), Tsuboyama et al. (2000), and Besliu et al. (1986). The results challenge the dibaryon interpretation and motivate precise measurements of $pn \to pn \pi^+ \pi^-$ around $\sqrt{s}=2.37\ \text{GeV}$ at facilities such as COSY or HADES, as well as theoretical models for $NN \to NN \pi\pi$ mechanisms involving light-meson exchange and deuteron wave-function effects.
