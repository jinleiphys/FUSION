# Structure of the ground and excited states in $_Λ^{9}$Be nucleus

> We investigate properties of bound and resonance states in the $_Λ^{9}$Be nucleus. To reveal the nature of these states, we use a three-cluster $2α+Λ$ microscopic model. The model incorporates Gaussian and oscillator basis functions and reduces a three-cluster Schrödinger equation to a two-body like many-channel problem with the two-cluster subsystems ($_Λ^{5}$He and $^8$Be) being in a bound or a pseudo-bound state. Influence of the cluster polarization on the energy and widths of resonance states in $_Λ^{9}$Be and on elastic and inelastic $_Λ^{5}$He+$α$ scattering is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates bound and resonance states in the hypernucleus _Λ⁹Be using a microscopic three-cluster 2α+Λ model, with the specific aim of analyzing how the polarization of the two-cluster subsystems (⁵_ΛHe and ⁸Be) affects the energy and widths of resonance states and the scattering matrix elements, going beyond the common approximation of treating these subsystems as rigid.

## Method

The authors employ the AMGOB (Algebraic Model of scattering with Gaussian and Oscillator Bases) microscopic three-cluster model, reducing the three-cluster Schrödinger equation to a two-body like many-channel problem. The wave function is expanded over Gaussian basis functions for the two-cluster subsystems and oscillator basis functions for the relative motion of the third cluster, with the Pauli principle between α-clusters fully taken into account. Bound states are obtained by diagonalization of the three-cluster Hamiltonian, while resonance parameters are extracted from the energy dependence of the two-channel S-matrix for α-⁵_ΛHe and Λ-⁸Be scattering, using the eigenphase shift method.

## Key numbers

- The energy of the 1/2⁺ ground state in _Λ⁹Be is -3.12 MeV with respect to the lowest binary decay threshold _Λ⁹Be → ⁵_ΛHe + α, and -6.63 MeV relative to the three-cluster 2α+Λ threshold.
- The nucleon mass used is m = 938.919 MeV/c², and the Λ hyperon mass is m_Λ = 1115.683(6) MeV/c², giving a mass ratio m̄_Λ = m_Λ/m = 1.18826.
- The oscillator length is chosen as b = 1.317 fm, and the Majorana parameter of the modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential is set to m = 0.4389.
- The spin-doublet splitting from the 2⁺ state in ⁸Be is reported experimentally as nearly degenerate, with the higher J state at slightly lower excitation energy; the paper notes that the LO chiral YN interactions reproduce the near degeneracy but give the wrong order of levels, while the Julich'04 interaction gives a significant splitting in contradiction to experiment.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic cluster model studies of _Λ⁹Be, particularly the calculations by Kato et al. (2018) which used a generator coordinate method for the ⁸Be core and treated core polarization by artificially enhancing the central NN potential, and the three-body model calculations by Lashko et al. (2019) which employed the complex scaling method for resonances. The present study enables a more complete understanding of how cluster polarization affects both bound and resonance states in _Λ⁹Be, and provides a framework for analyzing elastic and inelastic ⁵_ΛHe+α scattering that can be compared with future experimental data.
