# Some remarks about the transition gluon radiation

> The electrodynamical formulae for the transition radiation are applied to the case of partons crossing the surface of the deconfined region in heavy ion collisions. The chromopermittivity is used in place of the dielectric permittivity. The corresponding energy-angular distributions of emitted gluons are discussed. They could be of interest at LHC energies.

# Some remarks about the transition gluon radiation

## Key claim
Transition radiation formulae from electrodynamics can be applied to gluon emission when partons cross the surface of the deconfined region in heavy ion collisions, but the resulting distributions depend strongly on the unknown frequency dependence of the chromopermittivity; the most spectacular prediction, nearly monochromatic gluon subjets, relies on a specific model of the chromopermittivity (Equation eom) that may not hold in reality.

## Method
The Ginzburg-Frank formula for transition radiation is adapted to QCD by replacing the dielectric permittivity with the chromopermittivity. Two models for the chromopermittivity are considered: a constant value (inspired by fits to RHIC data) and a frequency-dependent form with a threshold (Equation eom, motivated by hadronic forward scattering amplitudes). The resulting energy-angular distributions of emitted gluons are derived analytically.

## Key numbers
- For the constant-chromopermittivity model at RHIC: ε_b ≈ 6, ε_a = 1 (vacuum); the spectrum is Bremsstrahlung-like with dN ∝ (dω/ω)(dθ/θ), independent of ε_b when ε_b ≫ ε_a.
- For the threshold model (Equation eom): dN ∝ (dω/ω⁵)(dθ/θ) Θ(ω − ω_th), producing a strong peak near ω_th.
- The angular divergence in both cases is replaced at finite energies by a maximum at θ ≈ γ⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends the classical Ginzburg-Frank (1945) transition radiation concept to gluons in heavy-ion collisions, following earlier successful applications of in-medium QCD to Cherenkov gluons (Dremin et al., 2005) and wakes (Holzmann et al., 2006; Ryazantsev et al., 2007). The constant-chromopermittivity model reproduces RHIC double-humped events (Fries and Werner, 2006; Adler et al., 2006), while the threshold model is inspired by cosmic-ray data (Apanasenko et al., 2001; Dremin, 2005). The paper suggests that if the threshold model is correct, LHC experiments could observe peaks in forward μ⁺μ⁻ pair mass spectra near ω_th, but cautions that confinement and hadronization effects near the QGP surface may modify or obscure the signal.
