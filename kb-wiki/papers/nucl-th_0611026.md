# Charged-particle rapidity density in Au+Au collisions in a quark combination model

> Rapidity/seudorapidity densities for charged particles and their centrality, rapidity and energy dependence in Au+Au collisions at RHIC are studied in a quark combination model. Using a Gaussian-type rapidity distribution for constituent quarks as a result of Landau hydrodynamic evolution, the data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130, 200$ GeV at various centralities in full pseudorapidity range are well described, and the charged particle multiplicity are reproduced as functions of the number of participants. The energy dependence of the shape of the $dN_{ch}/dη$ distribution is also described at various collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200, 130, 62.4$ GeV in central collisions with same value of parameters except 19.6 GeV. The calculated rapidity distributions and yields for the charged pions and kaons in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV are compared with experimental data of the BRAHMS Collaboration.

# Charged-particle rapidity density in Au+Au collisions in a quark combination model

## Key claim
The quark combination model, using a Gaussian-type rapidity distribution for constituent quarks derived from Landau hydrodynamic evolution, can describe charged-particle pseudorapidity densities across multiple centralities and collision energies at RHIC, but only if the sound velocity square is changed from $c_s^2=1/4$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \geq 62.4$ GeV to $c_s^2=1/7$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6$ GeV, indicating a possible change in the properties of the produced matter between these energies.

## Method
The authors first determine the number of constituent quarks from an effective energy equation that includes quark masses and a two-body interquark potential, scaling the quark number with the number of participant nucleon pairs. They then assign a Gaussian rapidity distribution to these quarks based on the Landau hydrodynamic model with a single parameter (sound velocity square $c_s^2$), and combine quarks into initial hadrons (including resonances) using a one-dimensional combination rule in rapidity space. All resonances are subsequently decayed into final-state hadrons using the PYTHIA 6.3 event generator.

## Key numbers
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV in central Au+Au collisions (0-5%): model yields for charged pions and kaons are $\pi^+ = 1676$, $\pi^- = 1680$, $K^+ = 280$, $K^- = 242$, compared to BRAHMS data ($\pi^+ = 1660 \pm 15 \pm 133$, $\pi^- = 1683 \pm 16 \pm 135$, $K^+ = 286 \pm 5 \pm 23$, $K^- = 242 \pm 4 \pm 19$).
- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u = m_d = 0.34$ GeV, $m_s = 0.5$ GeV, giving average mass $m = 0.36$ GeV; strangeness suppression factor $\lambda_s = 0.55$.
- Parameter $\beta = 3.6$ GeV$^{-1}$ (from $e^+e^-$ data); net quark numbers at 130 and 200 GeV are about 420 and 360, respectively.
- Sound velocity square: $c_s^2 = 1/4$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4, 130, 200$ GeV; $c_s^2 = 1/7$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV.
- Quark rapidity range: $y \in [-4.2, 4.2]$ at 130 and 200 GeV; $y \in [-3.2, 3.2]$ at 19.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' quark combination model, previously applied to $e^+e^-$ and $pp$ collisions (Xie 1988, Liang 1991, Wang 1995) and to RHIC hadron multiplicity ratios and $p_T$ spectra (Shao 2004) and elliptic flows (Yao 2006), to describe the full rapidity/pseudorapidity dependence of charged particles in Au+Au collisions. It uses the Landau hydrodynamic picture (Landau 1953) to motivate the Gaussian rapidity distribution of quarks, and the results are compared with PHOBOS data (Back 2002) and BRAHMS data (Bearden 2004, Staszel 2005). The finding that a different sound velocity is required at 19.6 GeV suggests a possible change in the equation of state of the produced matter between SPS and RHIC energies, enabling further study of the transition region.
