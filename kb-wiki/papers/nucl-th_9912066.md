# Hyperon stars in the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory

> In the framework of the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory, we determine a fully microscopic equation of state for asymmetric and $β$-stable nuclear matter containing $\sim$ and $\la$ hyperons. We use the Paris and the new Argonne $Av_{18}$ two-body nucleon interaction, whereas the nucleon-hyperon interaction is described by the Njimegen soft-core model. We stress the role played by the three-body nucleon interaction, which produces a strong repulsion at high densities. This enhances enormously the hyperon population, and produces a strong softening of the equation of state, which turns out almost independent on the nucleon-nucleon interaction. We use the new equation of state in order to calculate the structure of static neutron stars. We obtain a maximum mass configuration with $M_{\rm max}$ = 1.26 (1.22) when the Paris ($Av_{18}$) nucleon potential is adopted. Central densities are about 10 times normal nuclear matter density. Stellar rotations, treated within a perturbative approach, increase the value of the limiting mass by about 12%.

# Hyperon stars in the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory

## Key claim

The inclusion of three-body nucleon forces (TBF) in the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone calculation for hyperonic matter does not significantly change the equation of state compared to the case with only two-body forces, because the repulsive TBF at high density enhances hyperon populations, which counteracts the stiffening effect. This makes the equation of state almost independent of the chosen nucleon-nucleon interaction when hyperons are present.

## Method

The authors extended the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) scheme to include nucleons (n, p) and hyperons (Sigma-minus and Lambda) in beta-stable matter, solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently for the single-particle potentials of all baryon species. They used the Paris and Argonne Av18 nucleon-nucleon potentials with the Urbana three-body force model for nucleons, and the Nijmegen soft-core potential for nucleon-hyperon interactions, with no hyperon-hyperon interaction included. The equilibrium composition was determined by imposing chemical equilibrium, charge neutrality, and baryon number conservation, and the resulting equation of state was used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of static neutron stars: M_max = 1.26 solar masses with the Paris potential, and 1.22 solar masses with the Argonne Av18 potential, both including three-body forces and hyperons.
- Central densities of maximum mass configurations: about 10 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 approximately 0.17 fm^-3).
- Stellar rotation increases the limiting mass by about 12 percent when treated perturbatively at the Kepler frequency.
- Onset density for Sigma-minus hyperon formation: approximately 0.4 fm^-3 (about 2.4 times nuclear matter density) in the free hyperon case without three-body forces.
- Onset density for Lambda hyperon formation: approximately 0.82 fm^-3 (about 4.8 times nuclear matter density) in the free hyperon case without three-body forces.
- With three-body forces included, hyperon onset densities shift to 2-3.5 times normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic investigation (Baldo, Burgio, Schulze, 1998) of hyperon onset densities in neutron star matter to full equations of state and neutron star structure calculations. It contrasts with pure nucleonic calculations (Baldo et al., 1997) where three-body forces stiffen the equation of state dramatically, showing that hyperon formation acts as a pressure control mechanism that makes the equation of state nearly independent of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results enable more realistic neutron star models that include hyperonic cores, and suggest that kaon condensation (Ellis et al., 1995; Mishustin et al., 1997) is suppressed by the dominant hyperon formation and consequent deleptonization.
