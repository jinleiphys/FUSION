# Universality in the physics of cold atoms with large scattering length

> Effective field theories exploit a separation of scales in physical systems in order to perform systematically improvable, model-independent calculations. They are ideally suited to describe universal aspects of a wide range of physical systems. I will discuss recent applications of effective field theory to cold atomic and molecular few-body systems with large scattering length.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) provides a systematically improvable, model-independent framework for describing universal aspects of cold atomic few-body systems with large scattering length, but the approach requires a three-body parameter that cannot be predicted from the EFT itself and must be taken from experiment.

## Method

The author constructs a nonrelativistic EFT for bosonic atoms with large S-wave scattering length \(a\), using a Lagrangian with contact two-body and three-body interaction terms. The two-body scattering amplitude is summed analytically from bubble diagrams, while the atom-dimer scattering amplitude is obtained by solving an integral equation with an ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda\), where the three-body force strength \(H(\Lambda)\) runs on a limit cycle governed by the transcendental number \(s_0 = 1.00624\). The three-body parameter \(\Lambda_*\) is introduced by dimensional transmutation and must be fixed from a single three-body observable.

## Key numbers

- The transcendental number governing the limit cycle is \(s_0 = 1.00624\); when the cutoff \(\Lambda\) is increased by a factor of \(\exp(\pi/s_0) \approx 22.7\), the three-body force returns to its original value.
- For \(^4\)He atoms with \(a/r_e \approx 15\), the EFT prediction for the trimer ground state binding energy \(B_3^{(0)}\) ranges from 118 to 138 mK across four modern potentials, compared to direct calculations from 116.7 to 132.5 mK.
- The atom-dimer scattering length for \(a>0\) is given by \(a_{AD} = a\, (1.46 - 2.15\tan [s_0 \ln(a\Lambda_*) + 0.09])\,(1 + \mathcal{O}(r_e/a))\).
- The three-body recombination constant at threshold for recombination into the shallow dimer is \(\alpha = 67.1 \,(a^4/m)\,\sin^2 [s_0 \ln (a\Lambda_*) + 0.19]\,(1 + \mathcal{O}(r_e/a))\) for \(a>0\).
- For \(^{85}\)Rb atoms near a Feshbach resonance at \(B = 155\) G, the three-body loss rate coefficient \(K_3 = 3\alpha\) is compared to experimental data, with \(\Lambda_* = 5\)/a.u. preferred.

## Context

This work extends the universal Efimov physics originally derived by Efimov (1971) to cold atomic systems using the EFT framework developed by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999). The EFT reproduces the accumulation of infinitely many three-body bound states (Efimov states) as \(a \to \pm\infty\) and provides a practical calculational tool for three-body observables. The approach enables predictions for three-body recombination rates in cold atomic gases near Feshbach resonances, as demonstrated for \(^{85}\)Rb data from the JILA group, and sets the stage for extensions to four-body systems, the stability of Bose-Einstein condensates near resonances, and possible coexisting condensates of atoms, dimers, and trimers.
