# Production of $־˜^+$ Hypernuclei with the $(K^+,ֿ€^+)$ reaction

> We present results on the production of bound states of $־˜^+$ in nuclei using the $(K^+,ֿ€^+)$ reaction. By taking into account the states obtained within a wide range of strength of the $־˜^+$ nucleus optical potential, plus the possibility to replace different nucleons of the nucleus, we obtain an excitation spectra with clearly differentiated peaks. The magnitude of the calculated cross sections is well within reachable range.

## Key claim

The paper claims that bound states of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark in nuclei can be produced via the $(K^+, \pi^+)$ reaction with cross sections large enough to be experimentally observable, but this conclusion depends on assuming an attractive $\Theta^+$-nucleus optical potential of 60ג€“120 MeV at normal nuclear matter density and on a particular choice of the relative sign between two interfering amplitudes in the production mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for the $(K^+, \pi^+)$ reaction on a $^{12}$C target using the distorted-wave eikonal approximation, with kaon and pion distortions obtained from $t\rho$ approximations and optical theorem inputs. The $\Theta^+$ bound-state wave functions are obtained by solving the Schrֳ¶dinger equation with two Woods-Saxon-type potentials of strengths גˆ’60 MeV and גˆ’120 MeV at nuclear matter density, and the production amplitude is derived from two effective Lagrangians coupling the $\Theta^+$ to $K\pi N$ channels, with the relative sign of the two terms chosen based on constraints from $(\pi^-, K^-)$ production data.

## Key numbers

- Incident kaon kinetic energy fixed at 300 MeV to minimize momentum transfer, which reaches a minimal value of about 450 MeV/c for an initial $K^+$ momentum of around 620 MeV/c.
- For the shallow potential ($V = -60 \rho/\rho_0$ MeV), the formation cross section is around a few $\mu$b/sr MeV, with three isolated peaks in the spectrum.
- For the deep potential ($V = -120 \rho/\rho_0$ MeV), the cross section is about 10 times larger than the shallow case, with six clear peaks in the spectrum.
- Assuming a free $\Theta^+$ width of 15 MeV, the width in the nuclear medium is smaller than 6ג€“7 MeV due to Pauli blocking.
- The real parts of the distortion potentials make the cross sections larger by about a factor of two for both $\Theta$ potential cases.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of Nagahiro et al. (2004) that $\Theta^+$ bound states exist in nuclei with attractive potentials of 60ג€“120 MeV, which contradicted the earlier result of Kim (2003) that found only a weak potential from $KN$ decay alone. The $(K^+, \pi^+)$ reaction is analogous to the $(K^-, \pi^-)$ reaction used to produce $\Lambda$ hypernuclei and the only known $\Sigma$ hypernucleus, and the paper suggests that measurements of $\Theta^+$ binding energies and decay widths in nuclei could provide precise information on the $K\pi N$ component of the $\Theta^+$ wave function.
