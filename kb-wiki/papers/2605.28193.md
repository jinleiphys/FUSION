# Pion off-shell form factors

> In the paper, the electromagnetic off-shell pion form factors in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a separable kernel are considered. Different types of vertex functions of a pion are investigated. The separable kernel of the quark-antiquark interaction is used to obtain an analytical solution of the equation. The pion constants and the form factors on both the on-shell and off-shell surfaces are calculated. The differential cross section of the reaction $^1H(e,e' π^ +) n$ is also calculated in the paper. All the obtained results are compared with experimental data. The fulfillment of the Ward-Takahashi identity for the off-shell form factors $F_1(Q^2,t)$ and $F_2(Q^2,t)$ of a pion is verified.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a separable kernel can describe both on-shell and off-shell pion electromagnetic form factors, but the agreement with experimental data for the longitudinal differential cross section of the Sullivan process is limited at low momentum transfer, suggesting that additional contributions such as the gamma-rho-pi vertex are needed.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion vertex function using a rank-one separable kernel of the quark-antiquark interaction, with monopole and dipole functional forms for the radial part. The electromagnetic current is constructed from two contributions: a relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) term describing photon-quark interactions and an interaction (INT) term from minimal substitution in the nonlocal kernel. The model parameters (quark mass m and size parameter Lambda) are fixed by fitting the pion decay constant, transition form factor, and charge radius, after which the on-shell and half-off-shell form factors F1(Q^2,t) and F2(Q^2,t) are computed and the Ward-Takahashi identity is verified numerically.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV (used as input on-shell condition)
* Monopole model: quark mass m = 260 MeV, Lambda = 550 MeV; dipole model: m = 245 MeV, Lambda = 1100 MeV
* Pion decay constant: monopole gives f_pi = 143.363(1) MeV, dipole gives f_pi = 135.962(1) MeV (experimental: 130.410(2) MeV)
* Pion charge radius: monopole gives <r^2_pi> = 0.459(1) fm^2, dipole gives 0.438(1) fm^2 (experimental: 0.434(8) fm^2)
* Two-photon decay width: monopole gives Gamma = 7.25(1) eV, dipole gives 7.74(1) eV (experimental: 7.82(1) eV)
* g(0,m_pi^2) = 1.97(2) (GeV)^{-2} for monopole, 1.79(3) (GeV)^{-2} for dipole (experimental: 1.86(5) (GeV)^{-2})
* Transition form factor radius: monopole gives r_pi_gamma = 0.640(1) fm, dipole gives 0.652(1) fm (experimental: 0.659(4) fm)
* At Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, the RIA and INT contributions to F_pi are comparable; for the dipole model the INT contribution exceeds the RIA contribution

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach with separable kernels originally developed by Ito and collaborators (1991) for on-shell pion form factors to the off-shell regime relevant for the Sullivan process. It builds on recent investigations by Choi (2019) and Leao (2024) of space-like off-shell pion form factors within the Bethe-Salpeter framework. The calculated form factors are compared with experimental data from Jefferson Lab (2008, 2006) for pion electroproduction on the proton, and the results enable extraction of the off-shell form factor g(Q^2,t) which the authors identify as a physically observable quantity accessible in experiments.
