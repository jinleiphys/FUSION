# QCD jets in a hot and dense medium: a study of shower formation time and collision kernels

> With the use of MARTINI, a model which considers evolving QCD jets against a fluid dynamical background, it is shown that the introduction of formation time to the parton shower after the initial hard scattering is essential for a simultaneous description of charged hadron and jet $R_{AA}$. This inclusion also improves jet shape ratio at small angle and jet fragmentation function ratios of leading charged hadrons. The MARTINI framework is then aimed at a study of the leading order, next-to-leading-order, and non perturbative collision kernels. Sizable differences in the modification of jet substructure observables, i.e., jet shape and fragmentation functions are observed. Such differences are caused by the difference in the radiation rates of relatively soft gluons that survive in the evolution in medium.

# QCD jets in a hot and dense medium: a study of shower formation time and collision kernels

## Key claim
The inclusion of formation time in the high-virtuality parton shower is essential for a simultaneous description of charged hadron and jet nuclear modification factors (R_AA), but this improvement is parameter-independent and also model-independent given similar observations in the LBT model. However, the present schematic prescription for formation time underestimates the amount of interactions experienced by the evolving parton distribution, particularly for softer segments of jet substructure observables.

## Method
The authors used the MARTINI Monte Carlo framework, which solves the rate equation for evolving hard parton distributions in a dynamically expanding QGP medium modeled by event-by-event relativistic hydrodynamics (IP-Glasma+MUSIC). They computed radiative energy loss rates using the AMY formalism with leading-order (LO), next-to-leading-order (NLO), and non-perturbative (NP) collision kernels for transverse momentum broadening, and compared simulations with and without a formation time prescription for the high-virtuality parton shower. They also performed multi-stage simulations using the JETSCAPE framework where MATTER handles the high-virtuality stage before MARTINI handles low-virtuality evolution.

## Key numbers
- Charged hadron R_AA and jet R_AA at 0-5% centrality in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 ATeV: all five simulation curves (MARTINI with/without formation time, MATTER+MARTINI with two kappa sets, and MATTER vacuum+MARTINI) reproduce the data, with the MATTER vacuum case predictably above all other curves due to no energy loss in its high-virtuality stage.
- Jet shape ratio separation occurs at r approximately 0.08 for both simulations with and without formation time, with ordering R_LO > R_NLO > R_NP without formation time and different ordering with formation time (LO above, NLO at bottom, NP between).
- For jet fragmentation function ratios of leading charged hadrons without formation time: R_NP > R_NLO > R_LO; with formation time: R_NLO > R_NP > R_LO.
- The MATTER high-virtuality evolution uses a virtuality cutoff Q_min = 2 GeV and momentum cutoff p_cut = 2 GeV for energy loss, recoil, and radiative events.
- The temperature cutoff for jet freeze-out from the hydrodynamic background is T_cut = 160 MeV, reached at approximately tau = 14 fm/c in central collisions.
- Single shower studies at hat_p_T = 500 GeV show that by tau = 14 fm/c, high-p_T regions of parton distributions with and without formation time are nearly identical, with differences only in the lower zeta region (zeta less than or equal to 0.2, corresponding to p_T less than or equal to 100 GeV).
- For hat_p_T = 25 GeV showers, models with and without formation time converge by tau = 2.4 fm/c then diverge again, with the difference primarily in the gluon population.

## Context
This work extends previous studies using the MARTINI framework (Schenke 2009) and builds on the authors' prior work (Yazdi 2022) comparing LO, NLO, and NP collision kernels. The necessity of shower formation time was also observed in the LBT model (He 2022) and for a different transport model (Ke 2020), establishing model-independence of this requirement. The multi-stage JETSCAPE framework comparison uses parameters from a landmark Bayesian study of heavy ion collisions (Bernhard 2019) and previous MARTINI+JETSCAPE studies (Park 2019, Shi 2022). The results enable future multi-probe studies using jet-medium photon spectra (Modarresi-Yazdi 2023) and mini-jets (Pablos 2022) to access the evolving parton distribution in the low to intermediate p_T region where differences between collision kernels are most visible.
