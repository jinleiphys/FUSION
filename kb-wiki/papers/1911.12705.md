# Deconfined, Massive Quark Phase at High Density and Compact Stars: A Holographic Study

> In Hoyos et al. (arXiv:1603.02943) a holographic D3/D7 system was used to describe a deconfined yet massive quark phase of QCD at finite density, concluding that the equation of state of such a phase was not stiff enough to support exotic dense stars. That analysis used a hard quark mass to represent the dynamical mass and assumed a conformal gauge background. Here we phenomenologically adjust the D3/D7 system to include a running anomalous dimension for the quark condensate. This introduces a dynamical mechanism for chiral symmetry breaking yet the model still has a deconfined massive phase at intermediate densities. We show that these systems, dependent on the running profile in the deep IR, generate much stiffer equations of state and non-monotonic behaviour in the speed of sound. The results suggest that these equations of state may be closer to supporting hybrid stars with quark cores.

## Key claim
By phenomenologically adjusting the holographic D3/D7 system to include a running anomalous dimension for the quark condensate, the resulting equations of state for a deconfined, massive quark phase are much stiffer than in the conformal case studied by Hoyos et al. (2016), and may be closer to supporting hybrid stars with quark cores, though they are still not quite sufficient to construct a convincing description of both the heaviest neutron stars and new stable hybrid stars.

## Method
The authors use a bottom-up D3/D7 probe brane model in an AdS$_5$ background, modifying the DBI action with an effective dilaton term, $h$, that depends on the radial coordinate and the brane embedding field. This dilaton is chosen to produce a running anomalous dimension for the quark bilinear, which is zero in the UV and runs to a fixed point value in the IR, thereby introducing a dynamical chiral symmetry breaking mechanism. The equations of motion for the brane embedding and the gauge field (dual to quark density) are solved numerically at zero temperature to compute the free energy and the resulting equation of state.

## Key numbers
*   The speed of sound squared in the deconfined massive quark phase can reach as high as $c_s^2 = 0.55$.
*   The hard quark mass in the earlier D3/D7 model of Hoyos et al. (2016) was set to 308.55 MeV to place the transition to the deconfined phase at the same chemical potential as the transition to nuclear matter.
*   The nuclear saturation density is given as $n_s \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
*   The maximum pressures for the three nuclear matter equations of state from Hebeler et al. (2013) are 312.6 MeV fm$^{-3}$ (stiff), 637.2 MeV fm$^{-3}$ (medium), and 666.5 MeV fm$^{-3}$ (soft).

## Context
This work extends the holographic study of dense quark matter for compact stars initiated by Hoyos et al. (2016), who used a conformal D3/D7 system with a hard quark mass and found the equation of state too soft to support exotic stars. The present model introduces a dynamical running of the quark anomalous dimension, a mechanism for chiral symmetry breaking seen in models like those of Jarvinen (2011) and Alvares (2012), and generates a phase structure with a deconfined massive quark phase at intermediate densities. The results suggest that further refinements, such as including colour superconducting phases, may be necessary to produce equations of state stiff enough to support hybrid stars with quark cores.
