# Scalar and tensor resonances in $J/ψ$ radiative decays

> We perform a systematic analysis of the $J/ψ\to γπ^0π^0$ and $\to γK_S^0 K_S^0$ partial waves measured by BESIII. We use a large set of amplitude parametrizations to reduce the model bias. We determine the physical properties of seven scalar and tensor resonances in the 1-2.5 GeV mass range. These include the well known $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$, that are considered to be the primary glueball candidates. The hierarchy of resonance couplings determined from this analysis favors the latter as the one with the largest glueball component.

## Key claim
The analysis of BESIII data on $J/\psi \to \gamma \pi^0\pi^0$ and $\to \gamma K_S^0 K_S^0$ favors the $f_0(1710)$ over the $f_0(1500)$ as the resonance with the largest glueball component, based on the hierarchy of resonance couplings determined from the fits, though the authors note that the region below 1 GeV is excluded due to incompatibility with Watson's theorem.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic partial-wave analysis of BESIII mass-independent data for $J/\psi \to \gamma \pi^0\pi^0$ and $\to \gamma K_S^0 K_S^0$ in the 1-2.5 GeV mass range, using a large set of amplitude parametrizations based on a K-matrix formalism with CDD poles to reduce model bias. They first conducted two-channel fits (including only $\pi\pi$ and $K\bar K$) and then extended to three-channel fits by adding an effective $\rho\rho$ channel to account for $4\pi$ contributions, with statistical uncertainties determined via bootstrap resampling of $O(10^4)$ pseudodatasets.

## Key numbers
- Seven scalar and tensor resonances were identified in the 1-2.5 GeV mass range from the three-channel fits
- The $f_0(1500)$ pole mass from three-channel fits: [1437, 1471] MeV; width: [93, 126] MeV
- The $f_0(1710)$ pole mass from three-channel fits: [1756, 1785] MeV; width: [139, 171] MeV
- The $f_2(1270)$ pole mass from three-channel fits: [1256, 1279] MeV; width: [182, 214] MeV
- The $f_2'(1525)$ pole mass from three-channel fits: [1488, 1517] MeV; width: [68, 101] MeV
- The $f_0(2020)$ pole mass from three-channel fits: [1955, 2098] MeV; width: [206, 427] MeV
- The $f_2(1950)$ pole mass from three-channel fits: [1862, 2084] MeV; width: [217, 539] MeV
- A new $f_0(2330)$ was found in three-channel fits with mass: [2313, 2525] MeV; width: [180, 411] MeV
- Two-channel fits produced $\chi^2/\text{dof} \sim 1.7-2$; three-channel fits improved to $\chi^2/\text{dof} \sim 1.1-1.2$
- In $K\bar K$, the peak associated to $f_0(1710)$ is roughly six times stronger than the $f_0(1500)$ peak

## Context
This work extends previous studies of scalar glueball candidates by the JPAC Collaboration and others, specifically addressing the $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$ which are considered primary glueball candidates in lattice QCD and phenomenological analyses (Giacosa 2005, Albaladejo 2008, Janowski 2014). The analysis improves upon earlier two-channel fits by including a third $\rho\rho$ channel, resolving discrepancies in the description of the $f_2'(1525)$ lineshape and enabling the identification of a new $f_0(2330)$ resonance. The results enable future studies of the mixing between scalar mesons and the glueball, and provide coupling ratios that can be compared with PDG branching fractions and quark model predictions.
