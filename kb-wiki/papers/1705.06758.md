# Proof of Factorization of $χ_{cJ}$ Production in Non-Equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC in Color Singlet Mechanism

> Recently we have proved the factorization of NRQCD S-wave heavy quarkonium production at all orders in coupling constant. In this paper we extend this to prove the factorization of infrared divergences in $χ_{cJ}$ production from color singlet $c{\bar c}$ pair in non-equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC at all orders in coupling constant. This can be relevant to study the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proves that infrared divergences in \(\chi_{cJ}\) production from a color-singlet \(c\bar{c}\) pair factorize at all orders in coupling constant in non-equilibrium QCD, extending a previous proof for S-wave NRQCD production; the proof relies on the closed-time path integral formalism and applies specifically to the non-equilibrium conditions expected at RHIC and LHC, where a temperature may not be definable.

## Method

The author uses the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism to construct generating functionals for non-equilibrium QCD, both without and with a background field. Infrared divergences from eikonal gluon exchange are isolated by showing that light-like eikonal lines produce pure gauge fields (analogous to the Grammer-Yennie approximation in QED), and the factorization is demonstrated by changing integration variables in the path integral to absorb these divergences into gauge-invariant non-perturbative matrix elements containing light-like gauge links.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energies cited: \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV for Au-Au collisions at RHIC, and \(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\) TeV for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC.
- Temperature threshold for quark-gluon plasma formation cited: \(\ge 200\) MeV.
- Hadronization time scale in QCD cited: \(\sim 10^{-24}\) seconds.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior proof of factorization for NRQCD S-wave heavy quarkonium production (Nayak, 2017) to P-wave \(\chi_{cJ}\) production in non-equilibrium QCD, addressing the non-canceling infrared divergences previously identified in color-singlet P-wave heavy quarkonium hadroproduction and decay (Bodwin, 1995; Chao, 1995). The result provides a first-principles framework for defining gauge-invariant non-perturbative matrix elements for \(\chi_{cJ}\) production, which is needed to study quark-gluon plasma signatures at RHIC and LHC without relying on equilibrium assumptions or lattice QCD at finite temperature.
