# Gamow-Teller Strength distributions in $^{76}\textrm{Ge}$, $^{76,82}\textrm{Se}$, and $^{90,92}\textrm{Zr}$ by the Deformed Proton-neutron QRPA

> We developed the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and applied to the evaluation of the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength distributions including high-lying excited states, which data becomes recently available beyond one or two nucleon threshold by charge exchange reactions using hundreds of MeV projectiles. Our calculations started with single-particle states calculated by a deformed axially symmetric Woods-Saxon potential. Neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing correlations are explicitly taken into account at the deformed Bardeen Cooper Schriffer theory. Ground state correlations, and two-particle and two-hole mixing states are included in the deformed QRPA. In this work, we use a realistic two-body interaction given by the Brueckner $G$-matrix based on the CD Bonn potential to reduce the ambiguity on the nucleon-nucleon interactions inside nuclei. We applied our formalism to the GT transition strengths for $^{76}$Ge, $^{76,82}$Se, and $^{90,92}\textrm{Zr}$, and compared to available experimental data. The GT strength distributions were sensitive on the deformation parameter as well as its sign, {\it i.e.}, oblate or prolate. The Ikeda sum rule, which is usually thought to be satisfied under the one-body current approximation irrespective of nucleon models, is used to test our numerical calculations and shown to be satisfied without introducing the quenching factor, if high-lying GT excited states are properly taken into account. Most of the GT strength distributions of the nuclei considered in this work turn out to have the high-lying GT excited states beyond one nucleon threshold, which are shown to be consistent with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Ikeda sum rule is satisfied without introducing a quenching factor, provided that high-lying Gamow-Teller excited states beyond one nucleon threshold are properly included in the deformed proton-neutron QRPA calculation. This result depends on the explicit treatment of deformation and the use of a realistic two-body interaction derived from the CD Bonn potential.

## Method

The authors developed a deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) starting from single-particle states calculated with a deformed axially symmetric Woods-Saxon potential. Neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing correlations were treated at the deformed Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) level, and ground state correlations as well as two-particle and two-hole mixing states were included in the QRPA. The residual nucleon-nucleon interaction was taken from the Brueckner G-matrix based on the CD Bonn potential, and the formalism was applied to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions for 76Ge, 76,82Se, and 90,92Zr.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge, 76Se, 82Se, 90Zr, and 92Zr, the GT strength distributions were calculated and compared to available experimental data; the calculations show that most GT strength resides in high-lying excited states beyond one nucleon threshold.
- The Ikeda sum rule was tested numerically and found to be satisfied without a quenching factor when high-lying GT states are included; no specific numerical sum-rule value is quoted in the truncated text.
- The particle model space was exploited up to N = 5 hbar-omega for the deformed basis, and up to 10 hbar-omega in the spherical basis expansion.
- The deformation parameter beta_2 was varied; the GT strength distributions were sensitive to both the magnitude and sign (oblate or prolate) of beta_2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous spherical QRPA (Ch93) to a deformed QRPA framework, following earlier deformed QRPA studies that used the Nilsson basis with effective separable forces (Simkovic) or Skyrme forces (Sarriguren and collaborators, various years). The key advance is the use of a realistic G-matrix interaction from the CD Bonn potential within the deformed QRPA, reducing ambiguity in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results enable more reliable calculations of GT strength distributions for deformed nuclei relevant to supernova and r-process nucleosynthesis, and they provide a framework for future studies of neutrino-induced reactions and double-beta decay in deformed nuclei.
