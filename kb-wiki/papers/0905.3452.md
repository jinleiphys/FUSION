# Nucleon QCD sum rules in nuclear matter including radiative corrections

> We calculate the nucleon parameters in nuclear matter using the QCD sum rules method. The radiative corrections to the leading operator product expansion terms are included, with the corrections of the order α_s beyond the logarithmic approximation taken into account. The density dependence of the influence of radiative corrections on the nucleon parameters is obtained. At saturation density the radiative corrections increase the values of vector and scalar self-energies by about 40 MeV, and 30 MeV correspondingly. The results appear to be stable with respect to possible variations of the value of Λ_{QCD}.

# Nucleon QCD sum rules in nuclear matter including radiative corrections

## Key claim
The radiative corrections to the leading operator product expansion terms in QCD sum rules for nucleons in nuclear matter exhibit a large compensation between the logarithmic corrections (α_s ln q²)^n and the corrections of order α_s beyond the logarithmic approximation; at saturation density the net effect of including both types of corrections simultaneously is to increase the vector self-energy by about 40 MeV and the scalar self-energy by about 30 MeV, rather than the much larger shifts that would occur if only the logarithmic corrections were included.

## Method
The authors apply the QCD sum rules method to the nucleon polarization operator in symmetric nuclear matter, using the Ioffe current for the proton. They include radiative corrections to the leading OPE terms: the logarithmic corrections (α_s ln q²)^n are resummed via the factor L^{-γ} reflecting anomalous dimensions, while the corrections of order α_s beyond the leading logarithmic approximation (BLLA) are included perturbatively for the dimension d=3 scalar and vector condensates, with coefficients c^q = 7/2, c^p = 15/4, and c^I = 3/2. The Borel transformed sum rules are solved for the vector self-energy Σ_v, the Dirac effective mass m*, the in-medium residue λ²_m, and the continuum threshold W²_m, using the same input parameters as in the authors' prior work Drukarev et al. (2007) and comparing several approximation schemes (no corrections, LLA only, BLLA, and perturbative inclusion of all α_s corrections).

## Key numbers
- At saturation density ρ₀ with radiative corrections totally neglected: Σ_v = 229 MeV, m* − m = −329 MeV, λ²_m = 1.10 GeV⁶, W²_m = 1.72 GeV².
- At saturation density with leading logarithmic corrections only (LLA): Σ_v = 160 MeV, m* − m = −380 MeV, λ²_m = 1.10 GeV⁶, W²_m = 1.77 GeV².
- At saturation density with corrections beyond LLA (BLLA) included: Σ_v = 271 MeV, m* − m = −300 MeV, λ²_m = 1.41 GeV⁶, W²_m = 1.75 GeV².
- At saturation density with perturbative inclusion of all α_s corrections (PIRC-1, factorization at M₀² = 1 GeV²): Σ_v = 334 MeV, m* − m = −251 MeV, λ²_m = 1.47 GeV⁶, W²_m = 1.80 GeV².
- For α_s(1 GeV²) = 0.55 (Λ_QCD = 280 MeV) in BLLA: Σ_v ≈ 264 MeV, m* − m ≈ −289 MeV, λ²_m = 1.81 GeV⁶, W²_m = 1.99 GeV².
- Vacuum nucleon mass in BLLA: m = 0.91 GeV, λ² = 2.03 GeV⁶, W² = 1.96 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum rules approach to nucleons in nuclear matter, originally developed by Ioffe et al. (1980s) and applied to the in-medium case by Drukarev, Ryskin, and Sadovnikova (2007) and others, by including radiative corrections beyond the leading logarithmic approximation. The authors build on their own prior calculation (Drukarev et al., 2007) and on the vacuum radiative correction results of Ovchinnikov and Pivovarov (1988) and Jamin and Lange (2002). The finding that the BLLA corrections largely compensate the LLA shifts resolves an apparent tension with earlier nuclear physics estimates and enables more reliable extraction of nucleon self-energies from QCD sum rules; the results are stable within a few percent against variations of Λ_QCD between 150 MeV and 280 MeV.
