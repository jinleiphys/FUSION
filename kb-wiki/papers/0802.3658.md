# The Giant Dipole Resonance as a quantitative constraint on the symmetry energy

> The possible constraints on the poorly determined symmetry part of the effective nuclear Hamiltonians or effective energy functionals, i.e., the so-called symmetry energy S(rho), are very much under debate. In the present work, we show that the value of the symmetry energy associated with Skyrme functionals, at densities rho around 0.1 fm^{-3}, is strongly correlated with the value of the centroid of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) in spherical nuclei. Consequently, the experimental value of the GDR in, e.g., 208Pb can be used as a constraint on the symmetry energy, leading to 23.3 MeV < S(rho=0.1 fm^{-3}) < 24.9 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the centroid energy of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) in spherical nuclei is strongly correlated with the symmetry energy at densities around 0.1 fm\(^{-3}\), not at saturation density. Using the experimental GDR centroid in \(^{208}\)Pb, the authors constrain the symmetry energy to 23.3 MeV < S(\(\rho\)=0.1 fm\(^{-3}\)) < 24.9 MeV, but this result depends on the uncertain value of the enhancement factor \(\kappa\) which they estimate to lie between 0.18 and 0.26.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for the GDR in \(^{208}\)Pb using 20 different Skyrme effective interactions, selected to have incompressibility \(K_\infty\) between 210 and 270 MeV and to reproduce the experimental GDR energy within \(\pm\)2 MeV. They computed the RPA centroid energy \(E_{-1} = \sqrt{m_1/m_{-1}}\) and correlated it with the quantity \(f(0.1) = \sqrt{S(\rho=0.1\ \text{fm}^{-3})(1+\kappa)}\), where \(\kappa\) is the enhancement factor from the energy-weighted sum rule. The experimental GDR centroid (13.46 MeV) and the estimated range for \(\kappa\) were then used to extract the allowed range for the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- The experimental GDR centroid in \(^{208}\)Pb is \(E_{-1}(\text{exp}) = 13.46\) MeV, from photoabsorption measurements.
- The correlation coefficient between \(E_{-1}(\text{RPA})\) and \(f(0.1)\) for the 20 Skyrme forces is \(r = 0.909\).
- The extracted constraint on the symmetry energy is 23.3 MeV < \(S(\rho=0.1\ \text{fm}^{-3})\) < 24.9 MeV.
- The enhancement factor \(\kappa\) is estimated to lie between \(\kappa_{\text{min}} = 0.18\) and \(\kappa_{\text{max}} = 0.26\), based on the integrated photoabsorption cross section up to 26.4 MeV in \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The correlation coefficient between the quantity \(g_{A=208}(\rho_0)\) and \(S(\rho=0.1\ \text{fm}^{-3})\) is \(r = 0.981\).

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamical approaches by Krivine (1980) and Danielewicz (2004) that connected the GDR to the symmetry energy using the Steinwedel-Jensen model, which the authors argue is less reliable than their fully microscopic self-consistent RPA framework. The result provides a nuclear-structure constraint on the symmetry energy at subsaturation density, complementing constraints from neutron skin thickness studies (Typel and Brown, 2001; Furnstahl, 2002), heavy-ion collisions, and neutron star observables. The authors note that their constraint applies specifically to Skyrme functionals and suggest testing whether other classes of effective functionals yield similar results, while emphasizing the need to study the interplay between constraints from different density regimes.
