# Nuclear response Functions with Realistic Interactions

> Linear density response functions are calculated for symmetric nuclear matter of normal density by time-evolving two-time Green's functions in real time. The feasability and convenience of this approach to this particular problem has been shown in previous publications. Calculations are here improved by using more 'realistic' interactions derived from phase-shifts by inverse scattering. Of particular interest is the effect of the strong correlations in the nuclear medium on the response. This as well as the related energy weighted sum rule, dependence on mean field and effective mass are some of the main objects of this investigation. Comparisons are made with the collision-less limit, the HF+RPA method. The importance of vertex corrections is demonstrated.

## Key claim

Linear density response functions for symmetric nuclear matter at normal density can be calculated by time-evolving two-time Green's functions with realistic non-local separable interactions derived from phase-shifts by inverse scattering, but the present work includes only S-states (singlet and triplet) and neglects the tensor coupling, which is known to be vital for nuclear matter saturation.

## Method

The author time-evolves Kadanoff-Baym equations for two-time Green's functions in real time, starting from an uncorrelated zero-temperature Fermi distribution at normal nuclear density (k_F = 1.25 fm^-1). Self-energies are calculated to second order using rank-one separable potentials for the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 channels, constructed by inverse scattering with a momentum cutoff Lambda = 2 fm^-1. The system is evolved for 10 fm/c until fully correlated, then an external perturbation is applied and the linear response is extracted from the G_10 Green's functions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter binding energy at second order with cutoff Lambda = 2 fm^-1: 17.12 MeV per nucleon, compared to 17.33 MeV per nucleon in all-order summation (Table 1)
- Fermi momentum used throughout: k_F = 1.25 fm^-1
- Effective mass m** from HF+RPA with self-consistent mean field: 0.66 (from sum-rule inversion, Fig. HF)
- Effective mass m** from full correlated calculation including all self-energies: 0.61 (Fig. full)
- Momentum transfer for all response calculations: q_0 = 0.4 fm^-1
- Depletion factor kappa for cutoff Lambda = 2.6 fm^-1: 0.124; for Lambda = 9.8 fm^-1: 0.175 (from prior work by Kohler and Moszkowski)
- Initial kinetic energy at t = -10 fm/c: 19.4 MeV; initial mean field energy: -37.4 MeV
- Changes after correlation: kinetic energy +4.0 MeV, interaction energy -4.0 MeV, mean field +2.0 MeV, correlation energy E_corr = 6.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Kohler (2016, 2017) that used local Gaussian interactions for response functions via the Kadanoff-Baym method, by implementing non-local separable interactions derived from inverse scattering that reproduce NN phase shifts. The results contrast with the vast majority of published response calculations that use the HF+RPA method with Skyrme or Gogny effective forces (Gogny 1977, Garrido 1992, Margueron 2005-2008, among others), which ignore pre-existing correlations in the nuclear medium. The demonstration that vertex corrections (the Sigma_10 self-energies) are essential to satisfy the energy-weighted sum rule follows the theoretical framework of Baym and Kadanoff (1962). The separable potentials used are shown to produce binding energies almost identical to the Bonn-B potential for S-state contributions (Kwon 1997), and the momentum-space cutoff approach is compared to the V_low-k interaction and the Moszkowski-Scott separation method (1960).
