# Three-body model calculations of Nucleon-Delta and Delta-Delta dibaryon resonances

> Three-body hadronic models with separable pairwise interactions are formulated and solved to calculate resonance masses and widths of L=0 N-Delta and Delta-Delta dibaryons using relativistic kinematics. For N-Delta, I(JP)=1(2+) and 2(1+) resonances slightly below threshold are found by solving pi-N-N Faddeev equations. For Delta-Delta, several resonances below threshold are found by solving pi-N-Delta Faddeev equations in which the N-Delta interaction is dominated by the 1(2+) and 2(1+) resonating channels. The lowest Delta-Delta dibaryon resonances found are for I(JP)=0(3+) and 3(0+), the former agreeing well both in mass and in width with the relatively narrow D_{03}(2370) resonance observed recently by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration. Its spin-isospin symmetric partner D_{30} is predicted with mass around 2.4 GeV and width about 80 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the lowest Delta-Delta dibaryon resonances, with quantum numbers I(JP)=0(3+) and 3(0+), can be described by solving pi-N-Delta Faddeev equations, with the former agreeing well with the observed D_{03}(2370) resonance; however, this agreement depends on the specific choice of separable-potential parameters and the treatment of quantum-statistics suppression factors that reduce the expected decay width.

## Method

The authors formulated and solved three-body Faddeev equations with separable pairwise interactions using relativistic kinematics: for N-Delta dibaryons they solved pi-N-N Faddeev equations with pi-N P33 and N-N S-wave interactions, while for Delta-Delta dibaryons they solved pi-N-Delta Faddeev equations where the N-Delta interaction was dominated by the 1(2+) and 2(1+) resonating channels. The two-body interactions were fitted to experimental phase shifts from Arndt et al. (2006, 2007), and the integral equations were extended into the complex plane to search for resonance poles.

## Key numbers

- For N-Delta dibaryon D_{12} (I(JP)=1(2+)): pole position at 2147 - i60 MeV (type I form factor) and 2159 - i70 MeV (type II form factor)
- For N-Delta dibaryon D_{21} (I(JP)=2(1+)): pole position at 2165 - i64 MeV (type I) and 2169 - i69 MeV (type II)
- The D_{03} (I(JP)=0(3+)) resonance mass agrees with the observed D_{03}(2370) at sqrt(s)=2.37 GeV, with binding energy of about 90 MeV relative to the Delta-Delta threshold
- The D_{30} (I(JP)=3(0+)) partner is predicted with mass around 2.4 GeV and width about 80 MeV
- Width-suppression factors x_{IJ} for Delta-Delta dibaryons: x_{03}=2/3 and x_{30}=2/3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hadronic model for the D_{03} dibaryon (Gal and Garcilazo, 2013) to systematically study all s-wave N-Delta and Delta-Delta dibaryon candidates, building on the SU(6) predictions of Dyson and Xuong (1964). The calculations reproduce the phenomenologically deduced D_{12} pole positions from Refs. Igor (1984), Arndt (1987), and Hoshizaki (1992), and provide the first explanation of the small width of the D_{03}(2370) resonance observed by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration, which previous one-boson-exchange potential models and quark-based models could not explain. The results enable predictions for the as-yet-unobserved D_{30} dibaryon and provide a framework for studying other B-Delta systems where B stands for N, Delta, or hyperons.
