# Dilepton Spectroscopy of QCD Matter at Collider Energies

> Low-mass dilepton spectra as measured in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are a unique tool to obtain spectroscopic information about the strongly interacting medium produced in these reactions. Specifically, in-medium modifications of the vector spectral function, which is well known in the vacuum, can be deduced from the thermal radiation off the expanding QCD fireball. This, in particular, allows to investigate the fate of the $ρ$ resonance in the dense medium, and possibly infer from it signatures of the (partial) restoration of chiral symmetry, which is spontaneously broken in the QCD vacuum. After briefly reviewing calculations of thermal dilepton emission rates from hot QCD matter, utilizing effective hadronic theory, lattice QCD or resummed perturbative QCD, we focus on applications to dilepton spectra at heavy-ion collider experiments at RHIC and LHC. This includes invariant-mass spectra at full RHIC energy with transverse-momentum dependencies and azimuthal asymmetries, as well as a systematic investigation of the excitation function down to fixed-target energies, thus making contact to previous precision measurements at the SPS. Furthermore, predictions for the energy frontier at the LHC are presented in both dielectron and dimuon channels.

## Key claim

Low-mass dilepton spectra at collider energies can reveal in-medium modifications of the vector spectral function, particularly the melting of the ρ resonance, which may signal (partial) restoration of chiral symmetry; however, the interpretation depends on the specific hadronic many-body framework used and the superposition of varying temperatures in the fireball expansion.

## Method

The author calculates thermal dilepton emission rates using effective hadronic many-body theory for the in-medium ρ spectral function and lattice-QCD-inspired rates for the quark-gluon plasma, extended to finite three-momentum via matching to perturbative QCD photon rates. These rates are integrated over a space-time evolution of heavy-ion collisions using relativistic hydrodynamics or simplified fireball parametrizations with an equation of state matched to lattice QCD results, including non-thermal sources such as Drell-Yan and hadronic final-state decays.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature for QCD crossover: T_pc ≈ 160 MeV
- Chemical freezeout temperature at SPS energies: T_ch ≈ 160 MeV with baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 250 MeV
- Initial temperature in minimum-bias Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV: 330 MeV
- Initial temperature in central Au-Au at 200 GeV: ~380 MeV
- Initial temperature in central Pb-Pb at 2.76 ATeV: ~560 MeV
- Initial temperature in central Pb-Pb at 5.5 ATeV: ~620 MeV
- Thermalization time at full RHIC energy: 0.33 fm/c
- Thermalization time at LHC energies: 0.2 fm/c
- Electric conductivity from lattice QCD fit: σ_EM/T ≈ (0.37 ± 0.01) C_EM
- Electric conductivity from the author's construction with K=2: σ_EM/T ≈ 0.23 C_EM

## Context

This work extends the successful dilepton program from fixed-target experiments at the CERN-SPS (CERES/NA45 and NA60) to collider energies at RHIC and LHC, where the medium has negligible net baryon density (μ_B ≪ T). The hadronic many-body approach of Rapp (2000) and Rapp (2009) is updated with lattice-QCD results for the equation of state from Borsanyi (2010) and Cheng (2009), and for QGP dilepton rates from Ding (2010) and Brandt (2012). The calculations enable predictions for ongoing and upcoming experiments at RHIC and LHC, including the beam energy scan program.
