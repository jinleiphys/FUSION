# Probing phase transition in neutron stars via the crust-core interfacial mode

> Gravitational waves emitted from the binary neutron star (BNS) systems can carry information about the dense matter phase in these compact stars. The crust-core interfacial mode is an oscillation mode in a neutron star and it depends mostly on the equation of the state of the matter in the crust-core transition region. This mode can be resonantly excited by the tidal field of an inspiraling-in BNS system, thereby affecting the emitted gravitational waves, and hence could be used to probe the equation of state in the crust-core transition region. In this work, we investigate in detail how the first-order phase transition inside the neutron star affects the properties of the crust-core interfacial mode, using a Newtonian fluid perturbation theory on a general relativistic background solution of the stellar structure. Two possible types of phase transitions are considered: (1) the phase transitions happen in the fluid core but near the crust-core interface, which results in density discontinuities; and (2) the strong interaction phase transitions in the dense core (as in the conventional hybrid star case). These phase transitions' impacts on interfacial mode properties are discussed. In particular, the former phase transition has a minor effect on the M-R relation and the adiabatic tidal deformability, but can significantly affect the interfacial mode frequency and thereby could be probed using gravitational waves. For the BNS systems, we discuss the possible observational signatures of these phase transitions in the gravitational waveforms and their detectability. Our work enriches the exploration of the physical properties of the crust-core interfacial mode and provides a promising method for probing the phase transition using the seismology of a compact star.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how first-order phase transitions inside neutron stars affect the crust-core interfacial mode, finding that phase transitions near the crust-core interface can significantly alter the interfacial mode frequency even when they have a minor effect on the mass-radius relation and tidal deformability, thereby providing a gravitational-wave probe of such phase transitions that would be otherwise undetectable.

## Method

The authors use Newtonian fluid perturbation theory on a general relativistic background solution of the stellar structure obtained by numerically solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. They compute the eigenfrequencies and eigenfunctions of the crust-core interfacial mode using the standard shooting method, considering two types of first-order phase transitions: (1) phase transitions in the fluid core near the crust-core interface producing density discontinuities, and (2) hadron-quark phase transitions in the deep core (hybrid star case). The mode frequencies are obtained by solving the homogeneous perturbation equations \( (\mathcal{\hat L} - \rho \omega_{\alpha}^{2}) \vec{\xi}_{\alpha} = 0 \) with the Cowling approximation, and the mode eigenfunctions are normalized as \( \int d^3x |\vec{\xi}_{\alpha}|^2 = MR^2 \).

## Key numbers

- The crust-core interfacial mode \( i_{\mu} \) (shear modulus discontinuity only) has a typical frequency around 50 Hz for different equations of state.
- The \( i_{\rho} \) mode (density discontinuity only) has a typical frequency larger than 100 Hz.
- For the unified relativistic mean field EoSs FSU2R, FSU2H, and TM1e, where the shear modulus and density discontinuities coincide, the \( i_{\mu\rho} \)-mode frequencies for a \( 1.4 M_{\odot} \) star are 176.6 Hz, 177.2 Hz, and 187.4 Hz respectively.
- The relative energy density jumps for these unified EoSs are 16.4% (FSU2R), 15.4% (FSU2H), and 14.2% (TM1e), with the number density at the bottom of the crust being \( 0.083 \, \text{fm}^{-3} \), \( 0.087 \, \text{fm}^{-3} \), and \( 0.089 \, \text{fm}^{-3} \) respectively.
- For the SLy4-based EoS with a first-order phase transition, the phase transition occurs at \( n_0 = 0.095 \, \text{fm}^{-3} \) with a relative energy density jump of 1.21%.
- Bayesian inference from Xie (2021) shows a large likelihood (at 68% confidence level) of phase transitions at \( \rho \approx 1.6^{+1.2}_{-0.4} \rho_{\mathrm{sat}} \), close to the crust-core transition region.

## Context

This work extends the study of interfacial modes in neutron stars, building on the foundational work of McDermott (1985, 1988) who first characterized these modes, and the later astrophysical discussions by Tsang (2012) and Pan (2020) on the \( i_{\mu} \)-mode. It differs from the work of Lau (2021), who investigated interfacial modes at the crystalline quark core-neutron fluid interface in hybrid stars with typical frequencies around 500 Hz, by focusing on the lower-frequency crust-core interfacial mode that is more observationally feasible for current gravitational wave detectors. The paper also connects to the twin-star scenario discussed by Alford (2013), where hybrid stars with quark cores can have the same mass but different radii, providing a way to probe high-density matter states through differences in interfacial mode properties and their gravitational wave signatures.
