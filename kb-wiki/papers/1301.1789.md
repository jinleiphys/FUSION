# The refractive index in the viscous quark-gluon plasma

> Under the framework of the viscous chromohydrodynamics, the gluon self-energy is derived for the quark-gluon plasma with shear viscosity. The viscous electric permittivity and magnetic permeability are evaluated from the gluon self-energy, through which the refraction index %in the %viscous quark-gluon plasma is investigated. The numerical analysis indicates that the refractive index becomes negative in some frequency range. The start point for that frequency range is around the electric permittivity pole, and the magnetic permeability pole determines the end point. As the increase of $η/s$, the frequency range for the negative refraction becomes wider.

## Key claim

The refractive index of gluons in a quark-gluon plasma with shear viscosity becomes negative in a frequency range bounded by the electric permittivity pole (start) and the magnetic permeability pole (end), and this frequency range widens as the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) increases; however, the authors note that in the negative-refraction frequency region there are no propagation modes because the dispersion relation is not satisfied.

## Method

The authors derived the gluon self-energy using viscous chromohydrodynamics, which extends ideal chromohydrodynamics by incorporating a shear-viscosity-modified distribution function for quarks and gluons. From the self-energy, they obtained the viscous electric permittivity and magnetic permeability, then computed the Depine-Lakhtakia index (n_eff = ε_r |μ_M| + μ_r |ε|) to determine the sign of the real part of the refractive index. Numerical evaluations were performed for η/s = 0, 1/(4π), and 0.2, with wave number k = 0.2 ω_p and temperature T = ω_p.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic permeability pole shifts from approximately 0.65 ω_p for the HTL (hard thermal loop) case to approximately 0.8 ω_p for the viscous cases (η/s = 0, 1/(4π), 0.2).
- The electric permittivity pole occurs at ω_d = k (with k = 0.2 ω_p), and its position shows no appreciable distinction among viscous cases or from the HTL result.
- The frequency range for negative refraction becomes wider as η/s increases, with values of η/s ≤ 0.2 considered, where the lower bound 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08 is the AdS/CFT bound.
- A frequency gap ω ∈ [ω_m, ω_g] exists where n^2 < 0 and n_eff = 0, indicating no propagation; this gap diminishes with increasing η/s.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of negative refraction in QGP: Amariti et al. (2010) discussed negative refraction in charged fluid systems using AdS/CFT, and Juan Liu et al. (2012) studied the refractive index of light in weakly coupled QGP using hard thermal loop perturbation theory. The present paper is the first to incorporate shear viscosity into the chromohydrodynamic framework (previously developed by Manuel and collaborators for the ideal case) to study gluon refraction. The results enable further investigation of how viscous effects modify color electromagnetic properties and parton propagation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
