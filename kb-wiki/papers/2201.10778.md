# Analysis of the $Оіn \to K^+ ОЈ^-(1385)$ photoproduction

> In our previous work [Phys. Rev. D $\bf{101}$, 074025 (2020)], the photoproduction $Оіp \to K^+ ОЈ^0(1385)$ has been investigated within an effective Lagrangian approach. There, the reaction amplitudes were constructed by considering the $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast(892)$ exchanges, $s$-channel $N$ contribution, $u$-channel $Оӣ$ exchange, generalized contact term, and a minimum number of $s$-channel $N$ and $О”$ resonance diagrams. It was found that the inclusion of one of the $N(1895){1/2}^-$, $О”(1900){1/2}^-$, and $О”(1930){5/2}^-$ resonances is essential to reproduce the available differential and total cross-section data for $Оіp \to K^+ ОЈ^0(1385)$. In the present work, we employ the same model to study the photoproduction $Оіn \to K^+ ОЈ^-(1385)$, with the purpose being to understand the reaction mechanism and, in particular, to figure out which one of the $N(1895){1/2}^-$, $О”(1900){1/2}^-$, and $О”(1930){5/2}^-$ resonances is really capable for a simultaneous description of the data for both $K^+ ОЈ^0(1385)$ and $K^+ ОЈ^-(1385)$ photoproduction reactions. The results show that the available data on differential and total cross sections and photo-beam asymmetries for $Оіn \to K^+ ОЈ^-(1385)$ can be reproduced only with the inclusion of the $О”(1930){5/2}^-$ resonance rather than the other two. The generalized contact term and the $t$-channel $K$ exchange are found to dominate the background contributions. The resonance $О”(1930){5/2}^-$ provides the most important contributions in the whole energy region considered, and it is responsible for the bump structure exhibited in the total cross sections.

# Analysis of the Оіn вҶ’ KвҒә ОЈвҒ»(1385) photoproduction

## Key claim

The available differential cross-section and photo-beam asymmetry data for Оіn вҶ’ KвҒә ОЈвҒ»(1385) can be reproduced only with the inclusion of the О”(1930)5/2вҒ» resonance, rather than the N(1895)1/2вҒ» or О”(1900)1/2вҒ» resonances that were each individually sufficient for the proton-target reaction Оіp вҶ’ KвҒә ОЈвҒ°(1385). The claim is conditional on releasing the resonance electromagnetic coupling constants and the t-channel K exchange cutoff as fit parameters, whereas the fixed values from the earlier proton-target analysis failed to reproduce the negative beam asymmetries.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with reaction amplitudes constructed from t-channel K and K*(892) exchanges, s-channel N contribution, u-channel ОЈ(1385) exchange, a generalized contact term to ensure gauge invariance, and s-channel resonance diagrams. They tested three models, each including one of the N(1895)1/2вҒ», О”(1900)1/2вҒ», or О”(1930)5/2вҒ» resonances, by fitting to differential cross-section data from the LEPS and CLAS Collaborations and photo-beam asymmetry data from LEPS for Оіn вҶ’ KвҒә ОЈвҒ»(1385).

## Key numbers

- The cutoff parameter for t-channel K exchange was fitted to Оӣ_K = 805 Вұ 7 MeV, reduced from 950 MeV in the earlier proton-target model.
- The cutoff parameter for u-channel ОЈ(1385) exchange was fitted to Оӣ_ОЈ* = 915 Вұ 97 MeV.
- The product of resonance electromagnetic and hadronic coupling constants for О”(1930)5/2вҒ» were fitted as g^(1)_RNОі Г— g^(1)_RОЈ*K = -8.7 Вұ 0.3 and g^(2)_RNОі Г— g^(1)_RОЈ*K = -46.5 Вұ 1.8.
- The auxiliary current strength parameter was fitted as AвӮҖ = -0.056 Вұ 0.009.
- The О”(1930)5/2вҒ» resonance contribution dominates cross sections below E_Оі = 2000 MeV (photon laboratory energy).
- The LEPS beam-asymmetry data show negative values for photon energies E_Оі > 1.9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective Lagrangian analysis of Оіp вҶ’ KвҒә ОЈвҒ°(1385) (Wang, 2020) to the neutron-target reaction, aiming to discriminate among three candidate resonances. It contradicts the Regge-model analysis of Xiaoyun (2016), which described the cross-section data without any s-channel baryon resonances but did not consider the LEPS beam-asymmetry data, and the Regge model of Byung (2017), which qualitatively described both proton and neutron data but left room for improvement due to the lack of resonance contributions. The results enable a unified description of ОЈ(1385) photoproduction on both proton and neutron targets with the О”(1930)5/2вҒ» resonance, and the authors note that a self-consistent fit with common parameters for both targets requires freeing the resonance hadronic coupling constant g^(2)_RОЈ*K, which was set to zero in both analyses.
