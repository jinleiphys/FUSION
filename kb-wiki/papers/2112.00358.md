# Perturbative and non-perturbative effects in ultraperipheral production of lepton pairs

> Perturbative and non-perturbative terms of the cross sections of ultraperipheral production of lepton pairs in ion collisions are taken into account. It is shown that production of low-mass $e^+e^-$ pairs is strongly enhanced (compared to perturbative estimates) due to the non-perturbative Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov (SGS) factor. Coulomb attraction of the non-relativistic components of those pairs leads to the finite value of their mass distribution at lowest masses. Their annihilation can result in the increased intensity of 511 keV photons. It can be recorded at the NICA collider and is especially crucial in astrophysical implications regarding the 511 keV line emitted from the Galactic center. The analogous effect can be observed in lepton pairs production at LHC. Energy spectra of lepton pairs created in ultraperipheral nuclear collisions and their transverse momenta are calculated.

# Perturbative and non-perturbative effects in ultraperipheral production of lepton pairs

## Key claim
The cross section for ultraperipheral production of low-mass electron-positron pairs in ion collisions is strongly enhanced compared to perturbative (Breit-Wheeler) estimates, and the differential mass distribution remains finite at the lowest masses, because the non-perturbative Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov (SGS) factor accounts for Coulomb attraction between the non-relativistic pair components. This effect is most important near the production threshold and can influence the intensity of 511 keV annihilation photons observed from the Galactic center.

## Method
The total cross section and differential distributions for lepton pair production in ultraperipheral nuclear collisions are calculated using the equivalent photon approximation, where the Breit-Wheeler cross section for two-photon fusion is multiplied by the SGS factor (the Coulomb wave-function normalization factor for unbound pairs). The resulting expressions are integrated over the photon fluxes from the colliding ions, with the ultraperipherality parameter u(Z) determined by nuclear form factors. Energy spectra and transverse momentum distributions of the produced leptons are derived from the same formalism.

## Key numbers
- The Breit-Wheeler cross section for two-photon production of lepton pairs is given by σ_{γγ}^{BW}(X) = (πα²/m²)(1-v²)[(3-v⁴)ln((1+v)/(1-v))-2v(2-v²)], where v = √(1-4m²/M²) is the relative velocity of the pair components and m is the electron mass.
- The SGS factor is T = 2πα / [v(1-exp(-2πα/v))], where α is the fine-structure constant.
- The differential distribution dσ/dv² for e⁺e⁻ pairs in ultraperipheral collisions includes a factor ln³[u√(s_{nn}(1-v²))/(2m)], where √(s_{nn}) is the center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair.
- The threshold kinetic energy for e⁺e⁻ pair production in the rest system of one nucleon is E_{k,t} = 4m(1 + m/(2m_n)) ≈ 2.05 MeV.
- The total cross section threshold for proton-proton collisions is √s_t = 2(m_p + m) ≈ 1.88 GeV.
- The transverse momentum distribution for ultraperipheral production at high p_t scales as dσ^{up}/dp_t ∝ (Zα)⁴ / p_t³ × ln³(u²s_{nn}/p_t²).

## Context
This work extends the perturbative treatment of two-photon lepton pair production originally calculated by Landau and Lifshitz (1934) and the Breit-Wheeler cross section (1934), by incorporating the non-perturbative SGS factor known from non-relativistic quantum mechanics (Sommerfeld, Gamow, Sakharov). The approach follows the Sakharov recipe of multiplying the perturbative cross section by the Coulomb wave-function normalization factor, as previously applied in studies by Baier, Iengo, Cassing, and others. The results are directly relevant to planned measurements at the NICA collider and to astrophysical interpretations of the 511 keV annihilation line from the Galactic center, where low-energy positrons from ultraperipheral processes in stellar explosions may contribute. The formalism also applies to lepton pair production at the LHC.
