# Hydrodynamic response to jets with a source based on causal diffusion

> We study the medium response to jet evolution in the quark-gluon plasma within the JETSCAPE framework. Recoil partons' medium response in the weakly coupled description is implemented in the multi-stage jet energy-loss model in the framework. As a further extension, the hydrodynamic description is rearranged to include in-medium jet transport based on a strong-coupling picture. To interface hydrodynamics with jet energy-loss models, the hydrodynamic source term is modeled by a causal formulation employing the relativistic diffusion equation. The jet shape and fragmentation function are studied via realistic simulations with weakly coupled recoils. We also demonstrate modifications in the medium caused by the hydrodynamic response.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a causal diffusion equation can be used to construct a hydrodynamic source term for jet energy deposition in the quark-gluon plasma, preserving causality by ensuring the signal velocity remains at or below the speed of light; the results are based on simulations within the JETSCAPE framework and are presented as a proof-of-concept rather than a definitive quantitative prediction.

## Method

The authors implemented two approaches for medium response within the JETSCAPE event generator: a weakly coupled description using 2-to-2 partonic scatterings (MATTER and LBT modules) that generates recoil partons and energy-momentum holes, and a strongly coupled description where soft partons below an energy cutoff are thermalized and their energy-momentum is evolved via a causal relativistic diffusion equation before being transferred as a source term to (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC). The diffusion equation includes a diffusion coefficient and relaxation time chosen to keep the signal velocity causal, and the source term is constructed by summing the diffused energy-momentum densities of all deposited particles at a thermalization time.

## Key numbers

* Jet shape function for PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV: the simulation agrees with CMS data, showing broadening at both small and large radial distance r, where recoil contribution is significant.
* Jet fragmentation function ratio (PbPb/pp) at 2.76 TeV: the simulation qualitatively captures the ATLAS data trend of enhancement at both small z and large z.
* Fluid temperature modification shown at proper time tau = 9.3 fm/c and spatial rapidity eta_s = 0 in the transverse plane, with a higher temperature region following the jet and a lower temperature region from a diffusion wake.
* The causal diffusion formulation ensures no modification appears outside the light-cone, as demonstrated in the tau-x plane.

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE collaboration's multi-stage jet energy-loss framework (Cao 2017, Putschke 2019, Kumar 2019) by adding a hydrodynamic medium response based on causal diffusion, building on prior studies of hydrodynamic source terms (Tachibana 2017, Chen 2017, Chang 2019, Tachibana 2020). The weakly coupled recoil approach successfully describes jet shape and fragmentation function data from CMS (2018) and ATLAS (2017), but the authors note that applying recoil prescriptions at thermal momentum scales is inconsistent with the hydrodynamic description of the bulk medium. The hydrodynamic response approach enables future systematic studies of how jet-correlated structures in the bulk medium influence jet substructure, though the authors emphasize that such fully integrated simulations require careful background subtraction and high numerical precision.
