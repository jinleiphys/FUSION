# Detailed description of exclusive muon capture rates using realistic two-body forces

> Starting from state-by-state calculations of exclusive rates of the ordinary muon capture (OMC), we evaluated total muon-capture rates for a set of light- and medium-weight nuclear isotopes. We employed a version of the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA, for short) which uses as realistic nuclear forces the Bonn C-D one boson exchange potential. Special attention was paid on the percentage contribution to the total muon-capture rate of specific low-spin multipolarities resulting by summing over the corresponding multipole transitions. The nuclear method used offers the possibility of estimating separately the individual contributions to the total and partial rates of the polar-vector and axial-vector components of the weak interaction Hamiltonian for each accessible final state of the daughter nucleus. One of our main goals is to provide a reliable description of the charge changing transitions matrix elements entering the description of other similar semileptonic nuclear processes like the charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions, the electron capture on nuclei, the single \b{eta}-decay mode, etc., which play important role in currently interesting laboratory and astrophysical applications like the neutrino-detection through lepton- nucleus interaction probes, and neutrino-nucleosynthesis. Such results can be also be useful in various ongoing muon-capture experiments at PSI, Fermilab, JPARC and RCNP.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that state-by-state pn-QRPA calculations using the Bonn C-D realistic two-body force can reliably describe exclusive muon capture rates for light- and medium-weight nuclei, but the total Gamow-Teller strength from the calculation differs significantly (more than a factor of 2.5) from experimental values, even though the energy positions are well reproduced.

## Method

The authors employed the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with the Bonn C-D one boson exchange potential as the residual two-body interaction, using a Woods-Saxon mean field with the IOWA parametrization. They solved BCS equations to determine the ground state of the parent nucleus and pn-QRPA equations to obtain excited states of the daughter nucleus, then computed exclusive muon capture rates using the Donnelly-Walecka multipole decomposition formalism with eight independent irreducible tensor operators. The pn-QRPA energy spectrum was shifted for each multipolarity to match experimental low-lying energies of the daughter nucleus.

## Key numbers

* Total muon capture rates were evaluated for six isotopes: $^{28}Si$, $^{32}S$, $^{48}Ti$, $^{56}Fe$, $^{66}Zn$, and $^{90}Zr$, covering light- and medium-weight nuclei
* Pairing parameters $g_{pair}^{p}$ and $g_{pair}^{n}$ were adjusted to reproduce experimental energy gaps, e.g., for $^{28}Si$: $\Delta_{n}^{exp}=3.1428$ MeV reproduced as $\Delta_{n}^{theor}=3.1429$ MeV, $\Delta_{p}^{exp}=3.0375$ MeV reproduced as $\Delta_{p}^{theor}=3.0377$ MeV
* For $^{90}Zr$: $\Delta_{n}^{exp}=1.8567$ MeV reproduced as $\Delta_{n}^{theor}=1.8568$ MeV, $\Delta_{p}^{exp}=1.1184$ MeV reproduced as $\Delta_{p}^{theor}=1.1183$ MeV
* The particle-particle ($g_{pp}$) and particle-hole ($g_{ph}$) parameters lie in the region 0.65-1.20 for most multipolarities, with exceptions for $1^+$ and $2^-$ in some isotopes where values are 0.2-0.6
* Calculations were performed with two values of the axial-vector coupling constant: $g_A = 1.262$ (free nucleon value) and $g_A = 1.135$ (quenched value)
* The total GT strength from the calculation differs from experimental values by more than a factor of 2.5

## Context

This work extends earlier muon capture calculations that relied on closure approximation (Rose 1977, 1978; Primakoff 1959) or continuum RPA methods (Kolbe et al. 1994) by providing detailed state-by-state exclusive rates using realistic two-body forces. The results are intended to inform ongoing muon capture experiments at PSI, Fermilab, JPARC, and RCNP, and to provide nuclear matrix elements for related semileptonic processes such as charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions, electron capture in supernovae, and single beta decay. The paper builds on the Donnelly-Walecka projection method (Donnelly and Walecka 1972, 1973, 1976) and the pn-QRPA framework previously applied to double-beta decay and neutrino reactions (Rodin and Faessler 2006; Yousry and Faessler 2009).
