# Vacuum structure in QCD and Correlation functions

> We discuss here a model of QCD vacuum in terms of quark antiquark and gluon condensates alongwith their fluctuations. The correlation functions of hadronic currents in such a vacuum are evaluated to extract hadron properties. The presence of fluctuations of the condensates are emphasized. The structure of vacuum is then generalised to finite temperatures to study correlation functions at finite temperatures. Finally, we discuss the vacuum structure at finite densities with diquark condensates in a Nambu JonaLasinio type model. The coupled mass gap and superconducting gap equations are solved selfconsistently. For certain parameters of the model nontrivial solutions of both the gap equations are obtained. The equation of state is also computed.

## Key claim

The QCD vacuum must include not only quark-antiquark and gluon condensates but also their fluctuations (irreducible four-point structure) to reproduce the correct phenomenology of hadronic correlation functions, particularly the strong attraction in the pseudoscalar channel where condensates alone give a ratio of about 1.2 compared to the phenomenological value of about 100 at a separation of one fermi.

## Method

The author constructs a variational ansatz for the QCD vacuum using Bogoliubov pair creation operators for quarks and gluons, parametrizing the condensate functions with Gaussian forms and minimizing the energy density subject to constraints from the pion decay constant and gluon condensate value. The equal-time propagator and mesonic current correlation functions are computed from this vacuum, and the missing pseudoscalar attraction is restored by introducing composite fields representing condensate fluctuations. The formalism is extended to finite temperature using thermofield dynamics, and to finite density by adding diquark condensates in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type model with coupled mass gap and superconducting gap equations solved self-consistently.

## Context

This work extends the variational approach to the QCD vacuum developed in Mishra (1999) and earlier studies of condensate structure, and addresses the failure of pure condensate models to reproduce pseudoscalar correlation functions found in lattice simulations (Neglecor) and instanton liquid model calculations (Surcor). The finite temperature results are compared with chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) for low temperatures and lattice simulations near the critical temperature, and with the operator product expansion and sum rule studies by Hatsuda (1995) and others. The finite density analysis extends the color superconductivity studies by Bailin (1984), Wilczek (1998), and Pisarski (1998), and the results enable predictions for vector meson mass shifts in heavy ion collisions and the equation of state for neutron star matter.
