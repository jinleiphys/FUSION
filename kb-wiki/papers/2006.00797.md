# Strength of pairing interaction for hyperons in multistrangeness hypernuclei

> Pairing correlations play a very important role in atomic nuclei. Although several effective pairing interactions have been used in mean field calculations for nucleons, little is known about effective pairing interactions for hyperons. Based on the quark model, we propose a relationship between effective pairing interactions for hyperons and for nucleons; e.g., for $Λ$s, the strength of the pairing interaction is 4/9 of that for nucleons. A separable pairing force of finite range which has been widely applied to describing pairing correlations in normal nuclei is used to investigate pairing effects in multi-$Λ$ Ca, Sn and Pb hypernuclei.

## Key claim

Based on the quark model, the authors propose that the strength of the effective pairing interaction for hyperons is related to that for nucleons by the ratio \(n_{\mathrm{u/d}}^2/9\), where \(n_{\mathrm{u/d}}\) is the number of u/d quarks in the hyperon; for \(\Lambda\) hyperons this ratio is \(4/9\). The authors note that this ratio is probably very rough and that other factors such as violation of the OZI rule, medium effects, and mass splittings may alter it.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with a separable pairing force of finite range to study \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pairing in multistrangeness hypernuclei. For the particle-hole channel, they adopt the effective interactions NLSH-A and PK1-Y1, which have been used previously for \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei. The pairing strength for \(\Lambda\)s is set to \(G_\Lambda = 4/9 \cdot G_N = 323.56\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), where \(G_N = 728\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) is the nucleon pairing strength obtained from fitting the Gogny force.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength for \(\Lambda\)s is taken as \(G_\Lambda = 4/9 \cdot G_N = 323.56\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), with the nucleon pairing strength \(G_N = 728\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) and effective range \(a = 0.644\) fm.
- For \(^{44}_{4\Lambda}\)Ca, the maximal \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pairing gap is a bit smaller than 0.8 MeV, while for an open shell nucleus with \(A=44\), the nucleon pairing gap is about 1.8 MeV according to the empirical formula \(\Delta_N \approx 12 A^{-1/2}\) MeV.
- The maximal \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pairing gaps for \(^{40-S}_{-S\Lambda}\)Ca, \(^{132-S}_{-S\Lambda}\)Sn, and \(^{208-S}_{-S\Lambda}\)Pb are a bit smaller than 0.8 MeV, around 0.6 MeV, and smaller than 0.6 MeV, respectively.
- The two-Lambda separation energy \(S_{2\Lambda}\) shows sudden drops at strangeness numbers \(-S = 2, 8, 20, 34, 40, 58\), which are identified as magic numbers for \(\Lambda\)s.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing correlations in hypernuclei, which have been much less explored than pairing in normal nuclei. The authors propose a quark-model-based relationship for hyperon pairing strengths, building on earlier work that used the quark-meson coupling model for the particle-hole channel (Tsushima 1997) and the observation that the \(\Lambda\) potential depth is roughly 2/3 of that for nucleons (Bouyssy 1976, Dover 1987, Millener 1988). The results are compared with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations of Guven (2018), who used a zero-range \(\delta\) force with separately adjusted pairing strengths for different core nuclei; the present work uses a global finite-range separable force and finds smaller \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pairing gaps that decrease faster with mass number. This enables future systematic studies of hyperon pairing in multistrangeness hypernuclei and may be applicable to non-relativistic mean field models as well.
