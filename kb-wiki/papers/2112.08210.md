# Ab-initio coupled-cluster calculations of ground and dipole excited states in 8He

> We perform coupled-cluster calculations of ground- and dipole excited-state properties of the 8He halo nucleus with nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory, both with and without explicit delta degrees of freedom. By increasing the precision in our coupled-cluster calculations via the inclusion of leading order three-particle three-hole excitations in the cluster operator, we obtain a ground-state energy and a charge radius that are consistent with experiment, albeit with a slight under-binding. We also investigate the excited states induced by the electric dipole operator and present a discussion on the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn and cluster sum rules. Finally, we compute the electric dipole polarizability, providing a theoretical benchmark for future experimental determinations that will study this exotic nucleus.

## Key claim

Coupled-cluster calculations with chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions, including leading-order three-particle three-hole excitations, produce ground-state energies and charge radii for 8He that are consistent with experiment, though the nucleus is slightly underbound; the authors note that static correlations associated with possible ground-state deformation are not accurately captured in their spherical coupled-cluster approach, which may explain the underbinding.

## Method

The authors performed ab-initio coupled-cluster calculations for the 8He halo nucleus using a harmonic oscillator basis, with the cluster operator truncated at singles and doubles (D) and including leading-order triples (T-1). They employed three chiral effective field theory interactions (NNLOsat and the delta-full interactions DeltaNNLO_GO(394) and DeltaNNLO_GO(450)) and computed ground-state properties, the discretized dipole response function via the Lorentz integral transform coupled-cluster method, and the energy-weighted and inverse-energy weighted dipole sum rules.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 8He: -31(1) MeV for NNLOsat, -30(1) MeV for DeltaNNLO_GO(450), -30.3(8) MeV for DeltaNNLO_GO(394); experimental value is -31.60972(11) MeV (Brodeur 2012)
- Charge radius of 8He: 1.92(2) fm for DeltaNNLO_GO(450), with agreement to within 10^-4 fm of the most recent experimental determination (Krauth 2021)
- Energy-weighted dipole sum rule m1: 63(1) MeV fm^2 for NNLOsat, 62(1) MeV fm^2 for DeltaNNLO_GO(450), 60.5(9) MeV fm^2 for DeltaNNLO_GO(394)
- Enhancement factor kappa: 1.02(3) for NNLOsat, 1.00(3) for DeltaNNLO_GO(450), 0.94(3) for DeltaNNLO_GO(394)
- Spin-orbit correction to charge radius R_so^2: -0.143(6) fm^2 for NNLOsat, -0.134(9) fm^2 for DeltaNNLO_GO(450), -0.141(6) fm^2 for DeltaNNLO_GO(394)
- Cluster sum rule for NNLOsat: 6.0(3) MeV fm^2, corresponding to approximately 30% of the total E1 strength arising from core-halo relative motion

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-cluster studies of 8He (Ekstrom 2015) by including triples correlations and three-nucleon forces in the dipole response calculation, and by comparing delta-full and delta-less chiral interactions. The calculations provide theoretical benchmarks for ongoing and planned experimental measurements of the 8He excitation spectrum (Holl 2021; Aumann) and electric dipole polarizability, and improve upon earlier shell-model (Ong 2010) and complex-energy configuration interaction (Papadimitriou 2011) estimates of the spin-orbit correction to the charge radius.
