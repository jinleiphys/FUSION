# Instanton interpolating current for $σ$--tetraquark

> We perform a QCD sum rule analysis for the light scalar meson $σ$ ($f_0(600)$) with a tetraquark current related to the instanton picture for QCD vacuum. We demonstrate that instanton current, including equal weights of scalar and pseudoscalar diquark-antidiquarks, leads to a strong cancelation between the contributions of high dimension operators in the operator product expansion (OPE). Furthermore, in the case of this current direct instanton contributions do not spoil the sum rules. Our calculation, obtained from the OPE up to dimension 10 operators, gives the mass of $σ$--meson around 780MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a specific tetraquark interpolating current for the sigma meson (f0(600)), constructed from the instanton-induced quark-quark interaction with equal weights for scalar and pseudoscalar diquark-antidiquark components, leads to cancellation of high-dimension operator contributions in the operator product expansion (OPE) and avoids destabilizing direct instanton contributions, yielding a stable QCD sum rule result for the sigma mass around 780 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed a QCD sum rule analysis for the sigma meson as a tetraquark state, using an interpolating current derived from the 't Hooft instanton-induced four-quark interaction. The current includes scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor diquark-antidiquark components with coefficients α:β:γ = 1:-1:1/4. The OPE was calculated up to dimension-10 operators, and direct instanton contributions were evaluated using Shuryak's instanton liquid model. A two-resonance approximation was applied to the phenomenological spectral representation to avoid threshold dependence.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson mass: 782.2 MeV (for threshold s0 = 2.0 GeV), with values ranging from 738.0 MeV (s0 = 1.8 GeV) to 826.1 MeV (s0 = 2.6 GeV)
- First radial excitation mass: 1775.6 MeV (for s0 = 2.0 GeV), interpreted as f0(1710)
- Condensate values used: ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.23 GeV)^3, ⟨ig q̄σ·Gq⟩ = 0.8 GeV^2 ⟨q̄q⟩, ⟨g^2 G^2⟩ = 0.5 GeV^4
- Average instanton size: ρc = 1.6 GeV^{-1}
- Residue for sigma: f1 = 4.399 × 10^{-3} GeV (for s0 = 2.0 GeV)
- Residue for first excitation: f2 = 0.395 × 10^{-3} GeV (for s0 = 2.0 GeV)

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of light scalar mesons as tetraquarks by Brito (2005) and Chinese group (2005), which considered only scalar or pseudoscalar diquark-antidiquark currents up to dimension-6 operators and found unstable sum rules. The authors build on the observation by Lee (2005, 2006) and Ioffe (2004) that higher-dimensional operators can destabilize multiquark sum rules. The proposed instanton current resolves these instabilities and enables a stable determination of the sigma mass, consistent with the PDG range of 400-1200 MeV, while also suggesting that f0(1370) may be a conventional q̄q state and f0(1500) a glueball candidate.
