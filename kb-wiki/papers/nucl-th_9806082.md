# Half-lives and pre-supernova weak interaction rates for nuclei away from the stability line

> A detailed model for the calculation of beta decay rates of the $fp$ shell nuclei for situations prevailing in pre-supernova and collapse phases of evolution of the core of massive stars leading to supernova explosion has been extended for electron-capture rates. It can also be used to determine the half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei in the $fp/fpg$ shell. The model uses an averaged Gamow-Teller (GT) strength function. But it can also use the experimental log ft values and GT strength function from $(n,p)$ reaction studies wherever available. The calculated rate includes contributions from each of the low-lying excited states of the mother including some specific resonant states ("back resonance") having large GT matrix elements.

# Half-lives and pre-supernova weak interaction rates for nuclei away from the stability line

## Key claim
The model extends a previously developed averaged Gamow-Teller (GT) strength function approach from beta-decay rates to electron-capture rates for fp-shell nuclei in pre-supernova and collapse conditions, but the calculated rates depend on a free width parameter (set globally to 6.3 or 7.5 MeV) and on a quenching factor that differs between beta-decay (0.6) and electron-capture (0.5).

## Method
The model uses a statistically averaged allowed Gamow-Teller strength function, assuming a Gaussian distribution in energy whose centroid is fixed by a phenomenological formula based on (p,n) and (n,p) reaction data. For beta-decay, the GT sum rule is computed from neutron-particle and proton-hole occupancies using spectral distribution theory, with a quenching factor of 0.6; for electron-capture, the sum rule uses proton occupancy and neutron-hole occupancy with a quenching factor of 0.5. Contributions from low-lying excited states of the mother nucleus and from specific resonant "back resonance" states are included, and the electron chemical potential is computed numerically with finite-temperature and pair-production corrections.

## Key numbers
- For $^{62}$Fe, the unquenched GT sum-rule values are $S_{\beta^-} = 36.7$ and $S_{\beta^+} = 6.7$; the quenched values are 21.5 and 3.4, respectively.
- The width parameter for the Gaussian GT distribution in beta-decay calculations is set to either 6.3 MeV or 7.5 MeV (with skewness -0.3 in the latter case).
- For electron-capture rates, the width of the Gaussian strength distribution is taken as an average value of 2 MeV.
- At high densities above $10^9$ g cm$^{-3}$, excited-state contributions to beta-decay rates can be an order of magnitude larger than ground-state contributions.
- For $^{56}$Fe electron-capture rates, results using 28 excited states are compared to contributions from the lowest 4 excited states.

## Context
This work extends the beta-decay rate model of Kar, Ray and Sarkar (1998) to electron-capture rates for fp-shell nuclei important in pre-supernova and supernova evolution. It builds on the spectral distribution theory framework of French and collaborators and uses the phenomenological centroid formula from Sutaria and Ray (1998) fitted to (n,p) data. The model enables calculation of weak interaction rates for nuclei with $50 < A < 80$ that are needed to determine the electron fraction during silicon burning and collapse, and can be extended to predict half-lives for very neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line and for r-process and s-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
