# Algebraic description of the triaxially to axially rotational shape phase transition

> Within the framework of the interacting boson model, we propose a novel algebraic scheme to describe spin-dependent structural evolutions in triaxial nuclei. Our analysis demonstrates that a triaxially to axially rotational shape phase transition can be induced by the high-order correction which is microscopically motivated by the pairing interaction on the $γ$-unstable rotation. This prescription is further applied to describe the structural evolutions along the yrast sequences of $^{132}$Ba and $^{130}$Xe, providing an exemplary case for demonstrating a unified explanation of both low-energy collective structures and high-spin phenomena in O(6)-like nuclei.

# Algebraic description of the triaxially to axially rotational shape phase transition

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a triaxial-to-axial rotational shape phase transition along the yrast line can be induced by a high-order O(5) term in the interacting boson model Hamiltonian, provided the ratio of the high-order to two-body O(5) coefficients satisfies |B₁/B₂| ≥ 1/(2N²+6N); otherwise only a minor energy compression occurs without a phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct an interacting boson model Hamiltonian in the O(6) dynamical symmetry limit by adding a symmetry-conserving (C₂[O(5)])² term to the standard two-body O(6) Hamiltonian, preserving analytic solvability. They derive the energy spectrum analytically and identify the critical angular momentum Lc = 2τc where the yrast configuration switches from τ = L/2 states to τ = N (d-boson condensate) states. The model is extended to O(6)-breaking cases using a generalized quadrupole operator and analyzed via the cranking method to study the free energy and angular momentum as functions of cranking frequency.

## Key numbers

- Critical seniority number τc = -3/2 + √(9 - 4(B₂/B₁ + N² + 3N))/2 for the O(6) limit, with critical angular momentum Lc = 2τc.
- For the M II case with N=12, the critical value is τc = 5, giving Lc = 10.
- The parameter constraint for observing the QPT is 1/(2N²+6N) ≤ |B₁/B₂| ≤ 1/(N²+3N); for N=12 this gives |B₁/B₂| between approximately 0.0028 and 0.0056.
- The pairing-motivated high-order term strength satisfies |w₂/w₁| ∼ 1/N², consistent with the model constraints.
- For ¹³²Ba, fitted parameters (in MeV): A = -0.038, B₁ = -0.0005, B₂ = 0.080, C = 0.014; effective charge eα = 0.076 eb.
- For ¹³⁰Xe, fitted parameters (in MeV): A = -0.056, B₁ = -0.0008, B₂ = 0.093, C = 0.013; effective charge eα = 0.08 eb.
- The cranking analysis shows a discontinuous jump in Lx from Lx ∼ 12-16 to Lx = 24 (the maximum for N=12) when |B₁| ≥ 0.00017.
- The first derivative of the free energy ∂F_Min/∂ω shows a discontinuous change near ω ∼ 0.46 for the case (χ, B₁) = (-1.32, -0.00022).

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-driven quantum phase transitions in the interacting boson model, previously applied to spherical-to-rotational transitions in A∼110 nuclei by Regan (2003) and Cejnar (2004), and to axial-to-triaxial transitions in A∼150 rare-earth nuclei by Zhang (2021). The high-order O(5) term is microscopically motivated by the pairing interaction, following the OAI mapping approach of Otsuka (1978) and the projection technique of Harter (1985), similar to how Yoshida (1991) introduced high-order O(3) terms to describe spin-dependent moments of inertia. The model is applied to ¹³²Ba and ¹³⁰Xe, providing a unified description of low-energy O(6)-like collective structures and high-spin phenomena, and enables future experimental searches for the predicted sudden drop in B(E2; Lc+2 → Lc) transitions as a signature of the phase transition.
