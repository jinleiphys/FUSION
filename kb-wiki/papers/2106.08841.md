# Variational approximations to exact solutions in shell-model valence spaces: systematic calculations in the $sd$-shell

> We study the ability of variational approaches based on self-consistent mean-field and beyond-mean-field methods to reproduce exact energies and electromagnetic properties of the nuclei defined within the $sd$-shell valence space using the non-trivial USD Hamiltonian. In particular, Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB), variation after particle-number projection (VAPNP) and projected generator coordinate methods (PGCM) are compared to exact solutions {obtained by} the full diagonalization of the Hamiltonian. We analyze the role played by the proton-neutron ($pn$) mixing as well as the quadrupole and pairing degrees of freedom (including both isoscalar and isovector channels) in the description of the spectra of even-even, even-odd and odd-odd nuclei in the whole $sd$-shell.

## Key claim

Variational methods based on self-consistent mean-field and beyond-mean-field approaches can closely approximate exact shell-model diagonalization results in the sd-shell, but the quality of the approximation depends critically on whether proton-neutron mixing is included in the Bogoliubov transformations and on the specific variational method used (HFB, VAPNP, or PGCM).

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB), variation after particle-number projection (VAPNP), and projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) calculations using the USD Hamiltonian in the sd-shell valence space (0d5/2, 1s1/2, 0d3/2 orbits). They compared three types of intrinsic wave functions: axial without pn-mixing, general without pn-mixing, and general with pn-mixing, using constraints on quadrupole and pairing degrees of freedom (both isoscalar and isovector channels). The variational results were benchmarked against exact solutions from full diagonalization using the ANTOINE code, with the TAURUS code used for the PGCM calculations.

## Key numbers

- For 24Ne (even-even), the VAPNP calculation with general pn-yes seeds gave the absolute energy minimum, lower than both the HFB minimum and VAPNP minima without pn-mixing; the HFB minimum was independent of seed type
- For 25Ne (even-odd), the lowest VAPNP energy was obtained with general pn-yes seeds, below general pn-no seeds, which was itself below axial pn-no seeds; only in the axial case was there a strong dependence on the initial blocked state, with the lowest energy at |K_a| = 1/2
- For 24Na (odd-odd), the lowest energy with axial blocked seeds was found at |K| = 4, which matches the total angular momentum of the exact ground state
- The HFB method with general pn-yes seeds produced a fake odd-odd vacuum for 24Na, with largest contributions from even-even nuclei with Z = Z0 +/- 1 and N = N0 +/- 1, making it useless for describing odd-odd nuclei without additional constraints
- Effective charges used for electromagnetic transitions: e_p = 1.5 for protons and e_n = 0.5 for neutrons

## Context

This work extends the PGCM approach previously applied to pf-shell Ca isotopes by Bally (2019), where only quadrupole deformations and neutron-neutron pairing were considered due to the absence of protons in the valence space. The present study systematically investigates the role of proton-neutron mixing and pairing correlations across the full sd-shell, including even-even, even-odd, and odd-odd nuclei. The results enable future applications of variational methods to larger valence spaces where exact diagonalization becomes computationally prohibitive, and establish the conditions under which these approximations can reliably reproduce exact shell-model results.
