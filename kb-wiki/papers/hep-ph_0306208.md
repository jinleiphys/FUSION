# Thermodynamics at Non-Zero Baryon Number Density: A Comparison of Lattice and Hadron Resonance Gas Model Calculations

> We compare recent lattice studies of QCD thermodynamics at non-zero quark chemical potential with the thermodynamics of a hadron resonance gas. We argue that for T < Tc the equation of state derived from Monte--Carlo simulations of two flavour QCD at non-zero chemical potential can be well described by a hadron resonance gas when using the same set of approximations as used in current lattice calculations. We estimate the importance of truncation errors arising from the use of a Taylor expansion in terms of the quark chemical potential and examine the influence of unphysically large quark masses on the equation of state and the critical conditions for deconfinement.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for temperatures below the deconfinement transition temperature (T ≤ T_c), the equation of state from lattice QCD at non-zero chemical potential can be well described by a hadron resonance gas model, but only when using the same set of approximations (Taylor expansion truncated at O((μ_q/T)^4) and unphysically large quark masses) as used in the lattice calculations.

## Method

The authors compare lattice QCD results from 2-flavor simulations using a Taylor expansion of the pressure in powers of μ_q/T up to O((μ_q/T)^4) with a hadron resonance gas model in the Boltzmann approximation. They account for the distortion of the baryon mass spectrum due to unphysically large quark masses using a quadratic parametrization m(m_π)/m_H ≈ 1 + A (m_π^2/m_H^2), with A estimated from lattice studies of nucleon, delta, and their parity partners. The resonance gas model includes only non-strange baryons to match the 2-flavor lattice calculations, and the Taylor expansion is truncated to the same order as in the lattice studies.

## Key numbers

- The transition temperature at μ_q = 0 for the quark masses used in the lattice calculation is T_0 ≈ 200 MeV.
- The chiral limit extrapolation of the transition temperature in 2-flavor QCD is about 170 MeV.
- In the resonance gas model, the ratio of expansion coefficients c_4/c_2 = 3/4, which is temperature independent.
- For μ_q/T = 1, the truncated Taylor expansion for the pressure differs by only 15% from the complete resonance gas result, while the truncation error for χ_q/T^2 rises to about 80%.
- The 15% uncertainty in the parametrization of the baryonic mass spectrum (parameter A between 0.9 and 1.2) results in a 20% error on c_2(T) at T = T_0.
- The pion mass in the lattice calculation corresponds to m_π/√σ = 1.84 ± 0.04, giving a pion mass of about 770 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Karsch, Redlich, Tawfik, 2003) of the hadron resonance gas description of lattice QCD at zero chemical potential to the case of non-zero baryon density. It directly compares with lattice results from Ejiri et al. (2003) using the Taylor expansion method, and with the exact matrix inversion approach of Fodor et al. (2003). The paper shows that the resonance gas model provides a robust description of the hadronic phase, and suggests that differences between lattice and resonance gas predictions should appear at O((μ_q/T)^6), which could signal the approach to a critical point. The work also enables future studies using the imaginary chemical potential approach of Philipsen et al. to test resonance gas predictions.
