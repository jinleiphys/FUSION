# Bridging the Chiral symmetry and Confinement with Singularity

> We consider a holographic quark model where the confinement is a consequence of the quark condensate. Surprisingly, the equation of motion of our holographic model can be mapped to the old spin-less bag model. Both models correctly reproduce the linear Regge trajectory of hadrons for zero quark mass. For the case of non-zero quark mass, the model lead us to Heun's equation. The mass term is precisely the origin of the higher singularity, which changes the system behavior drastically. Our result can shed some light on why the chiral transition is so close to the confinement transition. In the massive case, the Schroedinger equation is exactly solvable, but only if a surprising new quantization condition, additional to the energy quantization, is applied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a specific holographic quark model, the quark condensate (the order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking) simultaneously generates linear confinement, so that the confinement and chiral transitions are necessarily coincident; however, this result is explicitly derived only in AdS₄ (2+1 boundary dimensions) and the authors note that extending it to AdS₅ (3+1 dimensions) requires an anomalous dimension of -1 for the quark bilinear operator.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic model in AdS₄ with a Dirac fermion coupled to a neutral scalar field whose bulk profile encodes both the bare quark mass (source term) and the quark condensate (vev term). They derive the equation of motion for the fermion and map it, via a formal change of variables, to the radial Schroedinger equation of the old spin-less bag model of Lichtenberg et al. (1982). For non-zero quark mass, the resulting equation is a bi-confluent Heun equation; the authors impose polynomial normalizability, which yields both the usual energy quantization and an additional quantization condition linking the quark mass and the string-tension parameter.

## Key numbers

* For zero quark mass, the holographic model reproduces the linear Regge trajectory: \( m_n^2 = 4 q M (n + m + 1/2) \), where \( qM \) sets the slope.
* For non-zero quark mass in the bag model mapping, the string tension \( b_\tau \) is quantized; for odd N: \( b_\tau \approx \frac{8}{5} \left( N + \frac{6}{5}L + \frac{5}{3} \right) m_q^2 \); for even N: \( b_\tau \approx \frac{2}{5} \left( N + \frac{6}{5}L + \frac{5}{3} \right) m_q^2 \).
* The energy spectrum for the bag model at zero quark mass is \( E_{N,L}^2 = 4 b_\tau (N + L + 3/2) \).
* For the bi-confluent Heun equation with \( N=4 \) and \( b_0=1 \), the five quantized values of \( a_0 \) for \( L=0 \) are: -7.50342, -2.26852, 2.5487, 7.93985, 14.2834.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical bag model of Lichtenberg et al. (1982) and the earlier analytic result by Gursey (showing linear Regge trajectories for massless quarks) to the massive quark case, where the spectrum becomes highly nonlinear. The authors also connect their holographic model to the numerical study of the bag model by Bag (2019). The key mathematical result, that the Heun equation requires an extra quantization condition beyond energy quantization, is a general feature of potentials with both even and odd powers of the radial coordinate. The paper suggests that the singular massless limit of the spectrum explains why chiral and confinement transitions are so close in QCD, but leaves the dynamical mechanism that suppresses the quark mass as an open question.
