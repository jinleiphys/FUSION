# Resonance energy of the barKNN-piYN system

> The resonance energies of strange dibaryons are investigated with the use of the \bar{K}NN-πY N coupled-channels Faddeev equation. It is found that the pole positions of the predicted three-body amplitudes are significantly modified when the three-body coupled-channels dynamics is approximated, as is done in the literature, by the effective two-body \bar{K}N interactions.

## Key claim

The pole positions of the predicted three-body amplitudes for the strange dibaryon resonance are significantly modified when the three-body coupled-channels dynamics is approximated by the effective two-body \(\bar{K}N\) interactions, as is done in the literature; the full Faddeev treatment gives a binding energy 15 to 25 MeV larger than the effective potential approach (EPA).

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equation for the coupled \(\bar{K}NN \oplus \pi Y N\) (\(Y = \Sigma, \Lambda\)) system with separable two-body interactions derived from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian. They then simulated the effective potential approach (EPA) by approximating the \(\pi\Sigma N\) Green function to neglect the momentum dependence of the spectator nucleon, and compared the resulting resonance pole positions to the full Faddeev calculation.

## Key numbers

- For Model (A), the full Faddeev calculation gives a pole energy of \(-63.3 - i22.2\) MeV (binding energy \(B = 63.3\) MeV, half-width \(\Gamma/2 = 22.2\) MeV); for Model (B), the pole is at \(-44.4 - i22.8\) MeV.
- The EPA calculation for Model (A) gives a pole at \(-41.8 - i35.4\) MeV, and for Model (B) at \(-31.5 - i26.3\) MeV; the EPA binding energy is 15 to 25 MeV smaller than the exact calculation.
- The two-body \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) resonance (the \(\Lambda(1405)\)) is at \(1405.8 - i25.2\) MeV for Model (A) and at \(1414.2 - i18.6\) MeV for Model (B).
- The FINUDA collaboration reported a resonance with binding energy \(B \sim 115\) MeV and width \(\Gamma \sim 67\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev study (Ikeda, 2007) and contrasts with earlier variational and effective-potential calculations by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), Dote et al. (2004), and the chiral unitary approach of Hyodo and Weise (2007). The paper demonstrates that the effective potential approach (EPA), which truncates the Fock space to \(\bar{K}NN\) and uses effective two-body \(\bar{K}N\) interactions, systematically underestimates the binding energy and misrepresents the pole trajectory as the \(\bar{K}N\) strength is increased. The full Faddeev treatment is essential for making precise predictions for the strange dibaryon resonance, which will be tested at facilities such as J-PARC and Spring-8.
