# Helicity-dependent generalization of the JIMWLK evolution

> We generalize the JIMWLK evolution equation to include the small-$x$ evolution of helicity distributions. To derive helicity JIMWLK, we use the method devised by A.H. Mueller for the usual unpolarized JIMWLK, and apply it to the helicity evolution equations derived earlier. We obtain a functional evolution equation for the generalized JIMWLK weight functional. The functional now is a sum of a helicity-independent term and a helicity-dependent term. The kernel of the new evolution equation consists of the standard eikonal leading-order JIMWLK kernel plus new terms containing derivatives with respect to the sub-eikonal quark and gluon fields. The new kernel we derive can be used to generate the standard JIMWLK evolution and to construct small-$x$ evolution equations for flavor-singlet operators made of any number of light-cone Wilson lines along with one Wilson line containing sub-eikonal helicity-dependent local operator insertions (a "polarized Wilson line").

## Key claim

The authors generalize the JIMWLK evolution equation to include the small‑\(x\) evolution of helicity distributions, deriving a functional evolution equation for a generalized weight functional that depends on both eikonal gluon fields and sub‑eikonal quark and gluon fields; the derivation relies on the leading‑logarithmic (and double‑logarithmic) approximation in which the ordering of light‑cone \(x^-\) lifetimes is assumed, so that subsequent evolution after each emission is absorbed in a shock wave of shorter \(x^-\) extent than the emitted parton’s lifetime.

## Method

The authors adapt the method of Mueller (2001) for the unpolarized JIMWLK equation to the helicity‑dependent case. They first construct the rapidity evolution of test operators consisting of one polarized Wilson line (containing sub‑eikonal helicity‑dependent insertions) and any number of standard unpolarized Wilson lines, using the helicity evolution equations derived earlier by Kovchegov et al. (2015–2018). The color generators appearing in the evolution diagrams are replaced by functional derivatives with respect to the eikonal field \(\alpha\) and the sub‑eikonal fields \(\beta\), \(\psi\), and \(\bar\psi\); the resulting kernel is then converted into an evolution equation for the target weight functional by integration by parts.

## Key numbers

* The sub‑eikonal interaction vertices are of order \(1/s\) with \(s\) the center‑of‑mass energy squared (leading sub‑eikonal approximation).
* The factor \(\eta_R\) in the definition of the polarized Wilson line for a gluon exchange is \(\eta_R = \delta_{R=F} + \delta_{R=\bar{F}} + 2\delta_{R=A}\).
* The kernel of the helicity JIMWLK equation contains the standard eikonal leading‑order JIMWLK kernel plus new terms with derivatives with respect to sub‑eikonal fields; the eikonal kernel is proportional to \(\alpha_s/\pi^2\) times an integral over transverse coordinates.

## Context

This work extends the unpolarized JIMWLK evolution (Jalilian‑Marian et al., 1997–2001; Weigert, 2000; Iancu et al., 2000–2001; Ferreiro et al., 2001) to include helicity dependence, building on the helicity evolution equations for polarized dipoles derived by Kovchegov et al. (2015–2018). It enables the numerical evaluation of small‑\(x\) asymptotics of helicity distributions beyond the large‑\(N_c\) and large‑\(N_c \& N_f\) approximations, and allows construction of evolution equations for operators such as the polarized color quadrupole (one polarized Wilson line and three standard Wilson lines). The flavor non‑singlet case is left for future work.
