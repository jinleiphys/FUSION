# Intermittency of charged particles in the hybrid UrQMD+CMC model at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Within the framework of intermittency analysis, a search for critical fluctuations is ongoing to locate the possible critical point in the quantum chromodynamics phase diagram. In this study, self-similar critical fluctuations from a critical Monte Carlo (CMC) model have been incorporated into the cascade ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model. This hybrid UrQMD+CMC model exhibits a clear power-law behavior of scaled factorial moment for charged particles in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 7.7-200 GeV. By comparing the UrQMD+CMC model results with those from the STAR experiment, it is found that the value of a calculated scaling exponent falls in the range of the experimental measurement when 1-2 \% signal of intermittency fluctuations is added into the UrQMD sample.

## Key claim

The hybrid UrQMD+CMC model can reproduce the scaling exponent measured by the STAR experiment when only 1-2% of intermittency signal from the CMC model is added into the UrQMD background sample, but the model cannot reproduce the non-monotonic energy dependence of the scaling exponent observed by STAR because a fixed replacing fraction is used across all energies.

## Method

The authors combined the cascade UrQMD model (version 3.4), which simulates hadronic dynamics without critical fluctuations, with the CMC model that generates self-similar density fluctuations via a Levy random walk algorithm. They replaced a fraction lambda (1-2%) of particles in UrQMD events with CMC particles having the same multiplicity and transverse momentum distributions, then calculated scaled factorial moments (SFMs) and the correlator Delta F_q(M) in two-dimensional transverse momentum space for charged particles (p, anti-p, K^pm, pi^pm) in the most central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7-200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponent nu from the pure CMC model is 1.03 +/- 0.01, slightly larger than the Ising model expectation of 1.0
- The replacing fraction lambda = 1.7% produces Delta F_q(M) values that exhibit good power-law behaviors at all seven collision energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV)
- The experimentally measured scaling exponent nu from STAR falls in the range 0.35-0.6 for the most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7-200 GeV
- The UrQMD+CMC model with lambda between 1% and 2% produces nu values that fall within the STAR experimental range
- The NA49 experiment in Si+Si collisions at 158A GeV required lambda = 1% to reproduce their observed power-law behavior

## Context

This work extends prior intermittency studies using the UrQMD model (which showed no critical fluctuations) and the CMC model (which showed clean intermittency but no dynamical evolution). The authors compare their results to preliminary STAR experiment measurements at RHIC BES-I energies (STARIntermittency, QMSTARIntermittency) and to the NA49 experiment's finding that 1% CMC signal reproduced Si+Si data (NA49EPJC). The hybrid model enables quantitative estimation of the fraction of critical fluctuations present in experimental data, but cannot yet explain the non-monotonic energy dependence of the scaling exponent observed by STAR, which requires energy-dependent replacing fractions in future work.
