# Constraining the hadronic spectrum through QCD thermodynamics on the lattice

> Fluctuations of conserved charges allow to study the chemical composition of hadronic matter. A comparison between lattice simulations and the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model suggested the existence of missing strange resonances. To clarify this issue we calculate the partial pressures of mesons and baryons with different strangeness quantum numbers using lattice simulations in the confined phase of QCD. In order to make this calculation feasible, we perform simulations at imaginary strangeness chemical potentials. We systematically study the effect of different hadronic spectra on thermodynamic observables in the HRG model and compare to lattice QCD results. We show that, for each hadronic sector, the well established states are not enough in order to have agreement with the lattice results. Additional states, either listed in the Particle Data Group booklet (PDG) but not well established, or predicted by the Quark Model (QM), are necessary in order to reproduce the lattice data. For mesons, it appears that the PDG and the quark model do not list enough strange mesons, or that, in this sector, interactions beyond those included in the HRG model are needed to reproduce the lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the well-established hadronic states listed in the Particle Data Group (PDG) are insufficient to reproduce lattice QCD results for partial pressures in the confined phase; additional states, either from the PDG's one-star entries or from Quark Model predictions, are needed, but for strange mesons even the Quark Model may not list enough states, or interactions beyond the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model may be required.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 4stout staggered fermions at zero baryon and electric chemical potentials and at imaginary strangeness chemical potential, in the temperature range 135 MeV to 165 MeV. They extracted partial pressures of hadrons grouped by baryon number and strangeness by fitting the imaginary-chemical-potential dependence of low-order susceptibilities to an HRG-based ansatz, then compared these lattice results to HRG model calculations using four different hadronic spectra: PDG2016 (2-4 star states), PDG2016+ (including 1-star states), PDG2016+ plus hypercentral Quark Model (hQM) states, and PDG2016+ plus non-relativistic Quark Model (QM) states.

## Key numbers

* The total number of measured particles and antiparticles (excluding charm and bottom) is 608 states with two, three and four stars in PDG2016, and 738 states when one-star states are included.
* The Quark Model predicts 1446 states (non-relativistic QM) and 1237 states (hypercentral QM), compared to the PDG listings.
* Lattice simulations covered the temperature range 135 MeV ≤ T ≤ 165 MeV, with aspect ratio LT=4 and lattice spacings corresponding to N_t = 10, 12, and 16.
* The QCD transition temperature is independently determined on the lattice to be approximately 155 MeV.
* For baryons with |S|=2, inclusion of one-star PDG states considerably improves agreement with lattice data; for |S|=1 and |S|=3 baryons, even the hQM states are insufficient and the full QM spectrum is needed to match lattice results up to T ≈ 145 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between lattice QCD and the HRG model that suggested missing strange resonances (Bazavov 2014). It resolves ambiguities from prior studies where adding Quark Model states improved some observables like the ratio μ_S/μ_B but worsened others like χ_4^S/χ_2^S and the up-strange correlator χ_11^{us} (Bellwied 2015). The results provide guidance for experimental searches at RHIC, LHC, and JLab to focus on undetected hadronic resonances, and enable improved thermal fits of heavy-ion collision freeze-out by identifying which sectors require additional states.
