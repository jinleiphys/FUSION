# Color Glass Condensate at RHIC ?

> Heavy-ion collisions at the BNL-RHIC collider can probe whether gluon saturation effects in nuclei at small x have set in, or whether leading-twist perturbative estimates of particle production are still applicable. I discuss that soon to come data from pA collisions at RHIC may provide more systematic insight into this problem than present data on the energy and centrality dependence of hadron production from Au+Au collisions. Results from pA collisions at RHIC could also help to understand some controversial and puzzling results from ion-ion collisions, for example the large azimuthal asymmetry at high p_t, as measured by the STAR collaboration.

## Key claim
Heavy-ion collisions at RHIC may have shown first signs of gluon saturation in large nuclei, but the author argues it is too early to draw definite conclusions from Au+Au data alone; upcoming pA collisions could provide more systematic insight by testing distinct scaling laws predicted by the Color Glass Condensate model.

## Method
The paper derives analytic expressions for gluon production in pA collisions by solving the Yang-Mills equations in the forward light-cone to all orders in the background field of the nucleus (treated as a Color Glass Condensate) but to leading order in the field of the weak proton. The resulting gluon distribution is averaged over Gaussian color charge distributions for both sources, yielding formulas that interpolate between the perturbative regime at high transverse momentum and the saturation regime at intermediate momentum.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is estimated as \(Q_s^2(y=0)\simeq 2\) GeV\(^2\), giving \(Q_s^2/\Lambda^2\simeq 50\).
- In the perturbative regime at high \(k_\perp\), the gluon distribution scales as \(\sim \alpha_s^3/k_\perp^4\) with a logarithmic correction \(\log(k_\perp/Q_s^{(2)})\).
- In the saturation regime \(Q_s^{(1)} < k_\perp < Q_s^{(2)}\), the distribution scales as \(\sim 1/k_\perp^2\) with a factor \(\log(k_\perp/Q_s^{(1)})\).
- The average transverse momentum in the saturation regime is given by \(\langle k_\perp\rangle = 2 Q_s^{(2)} (\xi-1-\log\xi)/\log^2\xi\) where \(\xi = Q_s^{(1)}/Q_s^{(2)}\).
- The \(k_\perp\)-integrated multiplicity in the nonperturbative regime scales as \(\log^2(Q_s^{(2)}/Q_s^{(1)})\).
- The azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2\) is measured by STAR at \(p_\perp \simeq 2-6\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework (McLerran-Venugopalan, 1994) to asymmetric pA collisions at RHIC, contrasting with leading-twist perturbative QCD estimates (Kharzeev-Levin, 2001; Eskola-Kajantie-Ruuskanen-Tuominen, 2000). The author argues that pA collisions can test saturation scaling laws more cleanly than Au+Au data, and suggests that the large azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2\) observed by STAR at high \(p_\perp\) may arise from the harder \(p_\perp\) distribution in regions where one nucleus is saturated and the other is dilute, combined with radiative energy loss. The results enable experimental tests of the rapidity evolution of the saturation scale using HERA parametrizations (Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff, 1998) and could help distinguish between saturation and perturbative models.
