# Baryon-charge Chemical Potential in AdS/CFT

> We present a closed framework of AdS/CFT with finite U(1)B-charge chemical potential. We show how the gauge-invariant identification of the chemical potential with the bulk gauge field emerges from the standard AdS/CFT dictionary. Physical importance and necessity of the Minkowski embeddings within the present framework is also shown numerically in the D3-D7 systems. We point out that the D3-D7 model with only the black-hole embeddings does not have the low-temperature and low-chemical-potential region in the grand-canonical ensemble, hence it is incomplete. A physical interpretation that explains these numerical results is also proposed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a gauge-invariant formulation of the U(1)B-charge chemical potential in AdS/CFT, showing that the chemical potential is defined as the integral of the bulk gauge field strength rather than the boundary value of the gauge potential itself. The authors argue that Minkowski embeddings are physically necessary in the D3-D7 system, contrary to the claim in KMMMT that they are unphysical, because the model with only black-hole embeddings lacks the low-temperature and low-chemical-potential region in the grand-canonical ensemble.

## Method

The authors construct a closed framework for AdS/CFT with finite U(1)B-charge chemical potential by introducing a Lagrange multiplier in the D7-brane DBI action that enforces the chemical potential as the integral of the gauge field strength Fρt from the boundary to the minimum radius ρmin. They solve the equations of motion for the D7-brane embedding y(ρ) and the gauge field A0(ρ) in the Euclidean AdS-Schwarzschild background, using the Gauss-law constraint to relate the electric flux to the quark-number density Q. The grand potential and Helmholtz free energy are computed from the on-shell action for both Minkowski embeddings (branes not touching the horizon) and black-hole embeddings (branes reaching the horizon), with numerical solutions obtained for fixed quark mass and 't Hooft coupling.

## Key numbers

* The Hawking temperature is given by T = U0/(πR²) = U0/(√(2λ)πls²), where U0 is the horizon radius and λ is the 't Hooft coupling.
* The quark mass is related to the asymptotic brane separation L by mq = (1/2)√(λ)TL.
* The coefficient a in the AdS/CFT dictionary for the gauge field is determined as a = 2/(T²NcNf).
* The dimensionless charge parameter is defined as Q̃ = (mT/τ7)Q = 16/(√(λ)NcNfT³)Q.
* The numerical analysis uses λ = 16² and mq = 8, with free-energy densities in units of 8NcNf, chemical potential and Q in units of 8 and NcNf respectively.

## Context

This work extends the holographic description of baryon chemical potential initiated in KSZ and HT, and directly addresses the debate between NSSY and KMMMT regarding the existence of Minkowski embeddings at finite baryon density. The authors show that their gauge-invariant formulation resolves the inconsistency, enabling a complete thermodynamic description of the D3-D7 system with both Minkowski and black-hole embeddings, where the Maxwell construction works non-trivially between the two embedding types. The framework is applicable to general AdS/CFT setups with flavor branes and enables future studies of phase transitions in holographic quark matter.
