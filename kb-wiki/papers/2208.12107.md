# Production of neutron-rich actinide nuclides in isobaric collisions via multinucleon transfer reactions

> We have calculated the multinucleon transfer reactions of $^{208}$Os, $^{208}$Pt, $^{208}$Hg, $^{208}$Pb,$^{208}$Po, $^{208}$Rn, $^{208}$Ra,$^{132,136}$Xe bombarding on $^{232}$Th and $^{248}$Cm at Coulomb barrier energies within the dinuclear system model, systematically. The results are in good agreement with the available experimental data. Coulomb effect and shell effect on production of actinides in these reactions have been investigated thoroughly. Potential energy surface and total kinetic energy mass distributions in the reactions $^{208}$Hg, $^{208}$Pb and$^{208}$Po colliding on $^{248}$Cm and $^{232}$Th are calculated and analyzed, respectively. It is found that PES and TKE spectra manifest the fragment formation mechanism in the multinucleon transfer reactions. The isospin effect and shell effect are shown in PES and TKE. Production cross-sections of multinucleon transfer products are highly dependent on the isobar projectiles with mass number $A=208$. The isobar projectiles with larger N/Z ratios are favorable for creating the neutron-rich target-like fragments. The isobar projectiles with larger charge number induced products tend to shift to proton-rich region. Coulomb potential coupled to shell effect is shown in production cross-sections of actinide isotopes. Based on the radioactive projectiles induced reactions, we have predicted massive new actinide isotopes around nuclear drip lines, even could access the superheavy nuclei region.

## Key claim
The authors systematically calculate multinucleon transfer reactions for a range of isobaric projectiles (A=208) on actinide targets, finding that production cross-sections of neutron-rich actinide nuclides depend strongly on the N/Z ratio of the projectile, with the caveat that the predictions rely on the dinuclear system model and assume Coulomb barrier energies (E_c.m. = 1.1 × V_B) rather than being experimentally verified for most systems.

## Method
The dinuclear system (DNS) model was used to calculate multinucleon transfer reactions for projectiles 208Os, 208Pt, 208Hg, 208Pb, 208Po, 208Rn, 208Ra, 132Xe, and 136Xe bombarding 232Th and 248Cm targets at Coulomb barrier energies. The model solves master equations for proton and neutron transfer, incorporating potential energy surfaces (PES), total kinetic energy (TKE) distributions, and de-excitation via statistical evaporation to obtain secondary fragment cross-sections. Calculations were benchmarked against available experimental data for 129,132,136Xe + 248Cm reactions.

## Key numbers
- Production cross-sections of actinide isotopes (Z=93-100) from 132,136Xe + 248Cm range from 100 millibarn to 10 nanobarn, consistent with experimental data from PhysRevC.35.204 and PhysRevC.33.1315.
- For 208Pb + 248Cm at E_c.m. = 1.1 × V_B, predicted cross-sections for new actinide isotopes include: 237,238,239,240Ac at 24 nb, 7 nb, 7 nb, 0.1 nb; 239,240,241,242Th at 216 nb, 64 nb, 9 nb, 0.9 nb; 240,241,242,243,244,245Pa at 7790 nb, 4890 nb, 1080 nb, 171 nb, 17 nb, 1 nb; 243,244,245,246U at 9190 nb, 2290 nb, 478 nb, 28 nb; 245,246,247,248,249,250Np at 0.25 mb, 0.08 mb, 3070 nb, 371 nb, 26 nb, 2 nb.
- For 208Pb + 248Cm, predicted cross-sections for new mendelevium isotopes: 261,262,263,264,265,266,267Md at 1940 nb, 625 nb, 1250 nb, 194 nb, 73 nb, 4 nb, 1 nb; for nobelium: 261,262,263,264,265,266,267,268No at 54 nb, 64 nb, 205 nb, 131 nb, 86 nb, 16 nb, 2.6 nb, 0.4 nb; for lawrencium: 267,268,269Lr at 15 nb, 0.5 nb, 0.1 nb.
- Predicted cross-sections for superheavy nuclei with Z=104-116 exceed 10 picobarn, particularly in 208Po + 248Cm, where neutron subshell N=162 plays a crucial role.
- TKE-mass distributions span a kinetic range of 500 to 800 MeV and mass region of 160 to 280, indicating transfer of more than 30 nucleons.

## Context
This work extends the dinuclear system model approach previously applied by the Lanzhou group (Feng, 2006; Chen, 2016, 2017) to systematically study isobaric projectile effects on multinucleon transfer reactions, complementing experimental efforts at HIRFL, JINR, GSI, GANIL, and ANL to synthesize new nuclides around drip lines and superheavy regions. The calculations predict massive new actinide isotopes near nuclear drip lines and access to superheavy nuclei, building on prior DNS model studies of quasifission and fusion-fission dynamics (Adamian, 1997, 1998) and providing guidance for future experiments with radioactive beams.
