# Nonequilibrium evolution in scalar O(N) models with spontaneous symmetry breaking

> We consider the out-of-equilibrium evolution of a classical condensate field and its quantum fluctuations for a scalar O(N) model with spontaneously broken symmetry. In contrast to previous studies we do not consider the large N limit, but the case of finite N, including N=1, i.e., plain $λϕ^ 4$ theory. The instabilities encountered in the one-loop approximation are prevented, as in the large-N limit, by back reaction of the fluctuations on themselves, or, equivalently, by including a resummation of bubble diagrams. For this resummation and its renormalization we use formulations developed recently based on the effective action formalism of Cornwall, Jackiw and Tomboulis. The formulation of renormalized equations for finite N derived here represents a useful tool for simulations with realistic models. Here we concentrate on the phase structure of such models. We observe the transition between the spontaneously broken and the symmetric phase at low and high energy densities, respectively. This shows that the typical structures expected in thermal equilibrium are encountered in nonequilibrium dynamics even at early times, i.e., before an efficient rescattering can lead to thermalization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for scalar O(N) models with spontaneous symmetry breaking at finite N (including N=1, 4, and 10), the back-reaction of quantum fluctuations on themselves prevents the instabilities encountered in the one-loop approximation, and that the phase structure expected in thermal equilibrium (spontaneously broken phase at low energy density, symmetric phase at high energy density) emerges in nonequilibrium dynamics even at early times, before efficient rescattering can lead to thermalization.

## Method

The authors separate the field into a classical condensate component and quantum fluctuations, then derive renormalized equations of motion and gap equations using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) effective action formalism restricted to the Hartree approximation (two-loop graphs of leading order in λ and 1/N). They solve the coupled system of the classical field equation and the mode equations for the quantum fluctuations, with the trial masses determined self-consistently via gap equations that incorporate bubble diagram resummation, using dimensional regularization with counterterms introduced via the trial masses.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant λ = 1 for all simulations
- For N=4, λ=1: initial field amplitudes studied include φ₀ = 1.3v (broken symmetry phase), φ₀ = 1.445v ≈ √2 v (critical region), and φ₀ = 1.6v (symmetric phase)
- Classical minimum of energy density E₀ = -λv⁴/4 = -0.25 for λ=1
- Energy conservation verified with typical precision of five significant digits
- Late-time averaging performed at t = 300 (in units of v⁻¹)
- For N=4, λ=1: the classical mass squared 𝒎_cl² is approximately zero for v < φ₀ < √2 v and increases for larger φ₀ values

## Context

This work extends previous large-N nonequilibrium studies (Cooper 1997, Boyanovsky 1996-1999, Baacke 2000) to finite N, including the physically relevant cases N=4 (O(4) sigma model for low-energy meson interactions) and N=1 (plain λφ⁴ theory). The formalism builds on the renormalized bubble resummation techniques developed by Nemoto (2000) and Verschelde (2001) for equilibrium quantum field theory, adapting them to nonequilibrium dynamics. The results enable simulations of realistic models such as the O(4) sigma model relevant for RHIC phenomenology and larger N values relevant for grand unified theories in inflationary cosmology.
