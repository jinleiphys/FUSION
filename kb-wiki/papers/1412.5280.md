# Polaronic atom-trimer continuity in three-component Fermi gases

> Recently it has been proposed that three-component Fermi gases may exhibit a new type of crossover physics in which an unpaired Fermi sea of atoms smoothly evolves into that of trimers in addition to the ordinary BCS-BEC crossover of condensed pairs. Here we study its corresponding polaron problem in which a single impurity atom of one component interacts with condensed pairs of the other two components with equal populations. By developing a variational approach in the vicinity of a narrow Feshbach resonance, we show that the impurity atom smoothly changes its character from atom to trimer with increasing the attraction and eventually there is a sharp transition to dimer. The emergent polaronic atom-trimer continuity can be probed in ultracold atoms experiments by measuring the impurity spectral function. Our novel crossover wave function properly incorporating the polaronic atom-trimer continuity will provide a useful basis to further investigate the phase diagram of three-component Fermi gases in more general situations.

# Polaronic atom-trimer continuity in three-component Fermi gases

## Key claim
The impurity atom in a three-component Fermi gas smoothly changes its character from atom to trimer with increasing attraction, but only in the presence of condensed pairs; there is a sharp transition to a dimer state at sufficiently strong coupling. The smooth atom-trimer continuity is possible because the condensation of Cooper pairs violates particle number conservation in units of two, making atoms and trimers indistinguishable in a superfluid state.

## Method
The author develops a variational approach in the vicinity of a narrow Feshbach resonance, described by a two-channel Hamiltonian with fermionic atoms and bosonic molecules. The background superfluid of the first two components is treated with the ordinary mean-field BCS-BEC crossover wave function. A single impurity atom of the third component is described by a trial wave function that superposes an atom-like state and a trimer-like state, where the trimer-like component includes the most general three-body wave function from the vacuum limit. The variational parameters are determined by solving coupled integral equations derived from minimizing the energy expectation value.

## Key numbers
- The atomic fraction Z = |z_{k=0}|^2 serves as the indicator of atom-trimer continuity, with Z ~ 1 in the atom-like region and Z ~ 0 in the trimer-like region; the crossover region is defined by 0.2 ≲ Z ≲ 0.8.
- In the weak coupling limit (1/a k_F → -∞), the polaron energy reduces to ε_AT(0) → (4πa/m)(n_1 + n_2), the usual mean-field polaron energy.
- In the broad resonance limit (R_* k_F → 0), the polaron energy becomes ε_AT(0) → E_3 - 2μ, where E_3 is the trimer binding energy in the vacuum.
- The phase boundary for trimer formation in the vacuum occurs at the threshold R_*/a = -0.0917249.
- At R_* k_F = 0.1, the twofold degenerate dimer states have lower energy than the polaronic atom-trimer state for 1/a k_F > 3.6.
- The dimer energy at zero center-of-mass momentum is ε_D(0) = μ.

## Context
This work extends the concept of atom-trimer continuity originally proposed by Nishida (2012) for three-component Fermi gases, which was inspired by the quark-hadron continuity in superfluid nuclear matter (Schaefer 1999, Wilczek 2007). The study addresses the difficulty that the ordinary mean-field approximation fails for three-component Fermi gases because it misses three-body correlations (Bedaque 2009, Chang 2006, Rapp 2007). The polaron problem studied here is analogous to the polaron-molecule transition in two-component Fermi gases (Prokof'ev 2008) but differs fundamentally because the impurity atom is dressed by pairs extracted from the condensate rather than particle-hole excitations from a Fermi sea (Chevy 2006, Combescot 2007). The results enable experimental probing via inverse radio-frequency or microwave spectroscopy (Frohlich 2011, Kohstall 2012) and provide a basis for investigating the phase diagram of three-component Fermi gases with unequal masses, populations, or inter-component interactions.
