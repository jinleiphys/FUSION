# Contribution of the rho meson and quark sub-structure to the nuclear spin-orbit potential

> The microscopic origin of the spin-orbit (SO) potential in terms of sub-baryonic degrees of freedom is explored and discussed for application to nuclei and hyper-nuclei. We thus develop a chiral relativistic approach where the coupling to the scalar- and vector-meson fields are controlled by the quark substructure. This approach suggests that the isoscalar and isovector density dependence of the SO potential can be used to test the microscopic ingredients which are implemented in the relativistic framework: the quark substructure of the nucleon in its ground-state and its coupling to the rich meson sector where the $ρ$ meson plays a crucial role. This is also in line with the Vector Dominance Model (VDM) phenomenology and the known magnetic properties of the nucleons. We explore predictions based on Hartree and Hartree-Fock mean field, as well as various scenarios for the $ρ$-nucleon coupling, ranked as weak, medium and strong, which impacts the isoscalar and isovector density dependence of the SO potential. We show that a medium to strong $ρ$ coupling is essential to reproduce Skyrme phenomenology in $N=Z$ nuclei as well as its isovector dependence. Assuming an SU(6) valence quark model our approach is extended to hyperons and furnishes a microscopic understanding of the quenching of the $NΛ$ spin-orbit potential in hyper-nuclei. It is also applied to other hyperons, such as $Σ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the microscopic origin of the nuclear spin-orbit (SO) potential can be traced to quark substructure and meson couplings, and that a medium to strong rho-meson tensor coupling, not just the vector coupling, is essential to reproduce Skyrme phenomenology in N=Z nuclei and its isovector dependence, with the specific hedge that the quark polarization effect on the scalar field (important for saturation) is a subleading correction neglected in this study.

## Method

The authors develop a chiral relativistic approach based on the Field Correlator Method (FCM) and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, where scalar (sigma) and vector (omega, rho) meson fields couple locally to quarks confined by a scalar potential. They evaluate the nucleon-meson couplings from quark wave functions in a bag model, derive the spin-orbit potential as the sum of a boost contribution and Thomas precession, and express it in terms of nucleon densities for spherical nuclei. They explore three scenarios for the rho-nucleon coupling, weak (no rho-tensor), medium (Vector Dominance Model, kappa_rho=3.7), and strong (kappa_rho=6.6 from pion-nucleon scattering), and extend the approach to hyperons using SU(6) valence quark model.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar and isovector nucleon magnetic moments are mu_S = 0.88 and mu_V = 4.70 (from experimental neutron and proton magnetic moments), giving the SU(6) bag model ratio mu_V/mu_S = 5.
- The weak axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.26 (experimental value), with the MIT bag model predicting g_A = 1.09.
- For the rho meson, the medium coupling (pure VDM) gives kappa_rho = 3.7, while the strong coupling (from pion-nucleon scattering data) gives kappa_rho = 6.6.
- The omega coupling from VDM phenomenology gives g_omega/m_omega approximately 2 fm; the effective Lagrangian PKA1 gives g_omega/m_omega = 2.7 fm.
- The ratio W_1/W_2 for the Hartree contribution ranges from 1 (no rho) to approximately 1.1 (weak rho) to less than or equal to 3 (medium rho) to greater than or equal to 3 (strong rho).
- The half-sum (W_1 + W_2)/2 for the Hartree contribution is approximately 0.18 fm^4 across all rho scenarios; for the Hartree-Fock contribution it ranges from approximately 0.23 fm^4 (no rho) to approximately 0.42 fm^4 (strong rho).

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and the spin-orbit derivation by Guichon et al., providing a chiral framework where the sigma field has a well-defined chiral status (unlike the original QMC model). It addresses the functional difference between Skyrme and relativistic mean field (RMF) models noted by Reinhard and Flocard (1995): Skyrme SO potential combines isoscalar and isovector density gradients, while RMF with only sigma and omega mesons is purely isoscalar. The paper shows that including the rho-tensor coupling and Fock terms reconciles relativistic approaches with Skyrme phenomenology, and extends the microscopic understanding to hyper-nuclei, explaining the quenching of the N-Lambda spin-orbit potential.
