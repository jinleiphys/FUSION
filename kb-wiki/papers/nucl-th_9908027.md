# On the Origin of the Multiplicity Fluctuations in High Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> Multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions obtain comparable contributions both from initial stage of the collisions, and from final stage interaction. We calculate the former component, using the ``wounded nucleon'' model and standard assumptions about nuclei and NN cross section. Combining it with the second one, calculated previously by Stephanov,Rajagopal and Shuryak, ref.2, we obtain good quantitative description of experimental data (ref.3) from NA49 collaboration at CERN on central PbPb collisions.

# On the Origin of the Multiplicity Fluctuations in High Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Initial-state fluctuations contribute about 20% to the multiplicity fluctuation ratio, compared to 50% from random Poisson statistics and 25% from final-state resonance correlations, and together these components explain the NA49 data at about the 10% accuracy level. The dominant initial-state effect is fluctuations in the number of punched-through spectator nucleons, not the purely geometric impact-parameter variation previously argued by Baym and Heiselberg (1999).

## Method

The authors simulated PbPb collisions using the wounded nucleon model with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution (parameters for Pb: n0 = 0.17 fm^-3, R = 6.52 fm, a = 0.53 fm) and an eikonal probability for nucleons to become spectators. They included three sources of initial-state fluctuations: impact parameter variation, fluctuations in the number of punched-through spectators, and fluctuations in the individual NN cross section (with dispersion Δσ_NN ≈ 0.5 ⟨σ_NN⟩ at collision energy ~100 GeV). The resulting distribution of participant number N_part was convolved with final-state resonance decay effects from Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1999), assuming mean charged multiplicity ⟨N_ch(N_part)⟩ = 0.75 N_part and dispersion ΔN_ch(N_part)^2 = 1.5 ⟨N_ch(N_part)⟩.

## Key numbers

- The NA49 experimental value for the ratio ⟨ΔN_ch^2⟩/⟨N_ch⟩ is 2.0–2.2 for 5% centrality PbPb collisions at CERN.
- Initial-state fluctuations contribute about 20% to this ratio; random Poisson statistics contribute 50%; final-state resonance correlations contribute about 25%.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for Pb are n0 = 0.17 fm^-3, R = 6.52 fm, a = 0.53 fm.
- The dispersion in NN cross section at collision energy ~100 GeV is Δσ_NN ≈ 0.5 ⟨σ_NN⟩.
- The mean charged multiplicity per participant is C = 0.75, determined from the observed mean multiplicity.
- For a hypothetical scenario with mean observed multiplicity of 1000 (C = 2.7), the ratio ⟨ΔN_ch^2⟩/⟨N_ch⟩ would increase to approximately 2.57.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, which was motivated by possible connections to thermodynamic observables and critical fluctuations at the tricritical point, as discussed by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1999). It directly contradicts the conclusion of Baym and Heiselberg (1999) that purely geometric impact-parameter variation can account for the observed multiplicity fluctuations; the authors find that for the 5% centrality cut, the geometric distribution is inadequate and the dominant effect is spectator fluctuations. The results enable future studies of detector acceptance effects and predict that initial-state fluctuations will become more important at higher multiplicities expected at RHIC and LHC.
