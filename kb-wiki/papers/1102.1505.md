# Theoretical study of the $KK\bar K$ system and dynamical generation of the K(1460) resonance

> The $K K\bar K$ system is investigated with a coupled channel approach based on solving the Faddeev equations considering the $KK\bar K$, $Kππ$ and $Kπη$ channels and using as input two-body $t$-matrices that generate dynamically the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances. In the present calculation, a quasibound state around 1420 MeV with total isospin $I=1/2$ and spin-parity $J^π=0^-$ is found below the three kaon threshold. This state can be identified with the K(1460) resonance listed by the Particle Data Group. We also study the $KK\bar K$ system in a single channel three-body potential model with two-body effective $KK$ and $K\bar K$ interactions, in which the $K\bar K$ interaction is adjusted to reproduce the properties of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances as $K\bar K$ bound states, obtaining a very similar result to the one found in the Faddeev approach.

# Theoretical study of the $KK\bar K$ system and dynamical generation of the K(1460) resonance

## Key claim
A quasibound state around 1420 MeV with total isospin I=1/2 and spin-parity J^π=0^- is found below the three kaon threshold in the coupled-channel Faddeev calculation, which can be identified with the K(1460) resonance listed by the Particle Data Group; however, the width obtained (~50 MeV) is much smaller than the PDG values (~250 MeV), and the authors note that the PDG widths may not be very precise due to poor experimental information in this energy region.

## Method
The authors solve the Faddeev equations for the coupled channels $KK\bar K$, $K\pi\pi$, and $K\pi\eta$, using as input two-body t-matrices obtained from solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with chiral Lagrangians that dynamically generate the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances. They also perform a separate single-channel nonrelativistic potential model calculation for the $KK\bar K$ system using Gaussian effective potentials with parameters fixed to reproduce the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ as $K\bar K$ bound states and the $KK$ scattering length from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers
- A quasibound state is found at $\sqrt s \sim 1420$ MeV with a width of $\sim 50$ MeV in the Faddeev calculation
- The state appears when the invariant mass of the $K\bar K$ subsystem with isospin 0 is $\sqrt{s_{23}^{0}} \sim 983$ MeV (forming the $f_0(980)$) or with isospin 1 at $\sqrt{s_{23}^{1}} \sim 950$ MeV
- The three-kaon threshold is at $3 m_K \sim 1488$ MeV (using $m_K = 496$ MeV)
- In the potential model, the quasibound state has mass 1467 MeV, width 110 MeV, and binding energy 21 MeV
- The root mean squared radius of the $KK\bar K$ quasibound state is 1.6 fm
- The average $K$-$K$ distance is 2.8 fm, and the distance between the $KK$ cluster and $\bar K$ is 1.7 fm
- The $K\bar K$ interaction strengths used are $U^{I=0,1}_{K\bar K} = -1155-283i$ MeV with range $b=0.47$ fm, and the $KK$ interaction strength is $U^{I=1}_{KK} = 313$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the study of few-kaon systems previously investigated by the same group, including the $K\bar K N$ system which predicted a $N^*(1910)$ resonance (Jido, Kanada-En'yo, 2008) and the $\bar K\bar K N$ system. The approach follows the Faddeev formalism developed in earlier works (Martinez Torres et al., 2008-2010) that successfully described resonances such as $\phi(2170)$ and $Y(4260)$. The results are compared with a potential model approach similar to that used for $K\bar K N$ and $\bar K\bar K N$ systems. The state found at 1420 MeV can be identified with the K(1460) resonance listed by the Particle Data Group, though with a significantly smaller width. The authors note that a similar two-body study of $f_0(980)K$ and $a_0(980)K$ systems by Oller and Roca found a resonant peak at 1460 MeV with 100 MeV width.
