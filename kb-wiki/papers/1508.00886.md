# Two-Nucleon Higher Partial-Wave Scattering from Lattice QCD

> We present a determination of nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts for l >= 0. The S, P, D and F phase shifts for both the spin-triplet and spin-singlet channels are computed with lattice Quantum ChromoDynamics. For l > 0, this is the first lattice QCD calculation using the Luscher finite-volume formalism. This required the design and implementation of novel lattice methods involving displaced sources and momentum-space cubic sinks. To demonstrate the utility of our approach, the calculations were performed in the SU(3)-flavor limit where the light quark masses have been tuned to the physical strange quark mass, corresponding to m_pi = m_K ~ 800 MeV. In this work, we have assumed that only the lowest partial waves contribute to each channel, ignoring the unphysical partial wave mixing that arises within the finite-volume formalism. This assumption is only valid for sufficiently low energies; we present evidence that it holds for our study using two different channels. Two spatial volumes of V ~ (3.5 fm)^3 and V ~ (4.6 fm)^3 were used. The finite-volume spectrum is extracted from the exponential falloff of the correlation functions. Said spectrum is mapped onto the infinite volume phase shifts using the generalization of the Luscher formalism for two-nucleon systems.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts for partial waves with l > 0 using the Luscher finite-volume formalism, but only under the assumption that mixing from higher partial waves can be ignored, an assumption the authors acknowledge may require further investigation for the range of energies explored.

## Method

The calculations were performed on isotropic Wilson lattices with lattice spacing b = 0.145(2) fm at the SU(3)-flavor symmetric point where m_pi = m_K ~ 800 MeV, using two spatial volumes of V ~ (3.5 fm)^3 and V ~ (4.6 fm)^3. Novel lattice methods involving spatially displaced nucleon operators at the source and momentum-space cubic sinks were designed and implemented to achieve overlap with higher partial waves. The finite-volume spectrum was extracted from the exponential falloff of correlation functions and mapped onto infinite-volume phase shifts using the generalization of the Luscher formalism for two-nucleon systems, with the assumption that only the lowest partial waves contribute to each channel.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 800 MeV (tuned to physical strange quark mass in SU(3)-flavor limit)
- Lattice spacing: b = 0.145(2) fm
- Two spatial volumes: V ~ (3.5 fm)^3 (L/b = 24) and V ~ (4.6 fm)^3 (L/b = 32)
- Binding energy in ^1S_0 channel: B = 21.8(+3.2/-5.1)(+0.8/-2.8) MeV
- Binding energy in ^3S_1 channel: B = 30.7(+2.4/-2.5)(+0.5/-1.6) MeV
- Second near-threshold pole in ^3S_1 channel: B = 3.3(+1.0/-0.9)(+0.6/-0.2) MeV
- Inverse scattering length for ^3P_2 channel: 1/(a_0 m_pi^3) = -0.317(49) (LO ERE fit)
- Inverse scattering length for ^1D_2 channel: 1/(a_0 m_pi^5) = -0.047(12) (LO ERE fit)
- Inverse scattering length for ^3D_3 channel: 1/(a_0 m_pi^7) = -0.046(20) (LO ERE fit)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon-nucleon S-wave interactions by Beane (2013) on the same gauge configurations to include P, D, and F partial waves for the first time using the Luscher formalism. The methodology enables future calculations of higher partial wave nucleon-nucleon scattering at physical quark masses, which will be necessary to reliably extract three- and four-body nuclear forces from lattice QCD. The authors note that the challenge of partial wave mixing has only been addressed in two-meson calculations by Dudek (2012, 2014) and Wilson (2014, 2015), and will need to be addressed in subsequent publications for the nucleon-nucleon system.
