# Microscopic study of the deformed neutron halo of 31Ne

> The properties of the deformed halo of 31Ne were discussed using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics plus resonating group method (AMD+RGM). The AMD+RGM calculations describe a large neutron radius for the ground state and reveal that the ground state is dominated by core-excited components. The resonant states were also investigated by applying the analytical continuation of the coupling constant. It was found that the first excited state (the 5/2- state) is also dominated by core-excited components and has a small decay width, whereas the second excited state (the 7/2- state) is dominated by the valence neutron in the $f$-wave coupled to the ground state of 30Ne.

## Key claim

The ground state of 31Ne is dominated by core-excited components (3p2h configuration) rather than the simple valence neutron coupled to the 30Ne ground state, and the AMD+RGM framework combined with ACCC successfully describes both the bound halo ground state and the resonant excited states, though the calculated one-neutron separation energy of 610 keV overestimates the experimental value of 150 ± 160 keV.

## Method

The authors applied the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics plus resonating group method (AMD+RGM) using the Gogny D1S effective interaction to describe 31Ne as a 30Ne + n system, with the valence neutron placed on a grid inside a 12 fm radius sphere. The analytical continuation of the coupling constant (ACCC) method was introduced to determine resonance energies and widths by varying the strength of the two-body spin-orbit interaction as an auxiliary potential, using a Pade approximation with N=M=7 to analytically continue eigenvalues from the bound region to the physical point.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of 31Ne: 0.61 MeV (AMD+RGM), compared to -0.36 MeV (AMD+GCM) and experimental 150 ± 160 keV
- Neutron distribution radius: 3.69 fm (AMD+RGM) versus 3.45 fm (AMD+GCM)
- Proton distribution radius: 3.11 fm (AMD+RGM) versus 3.06 fm (AMD+GCM)
- Quadrupole moment of protons: 11.2 fm^2 (AMD+RGM); quadrupole moment of neutrons: 31.1 fm^2 (AMD+RGM)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 0+1 ⊗ p3/2 channel: 0.30 (AMD+RGM), compared to experimental 0.32 ± 0.21
- 5/2-1 resonance: separation energy -0.12 MeV, decay width 0.07 MeV
- 7/2-1 resonance: separation energy -0.69 MeV, decay width 0.18 MeV
- 3/2-2 resonance: separation energy -1.76 MeV, decay width 0.29 MeV
- 7/2-2 resonance: separation energy -1.60 MeV, decay width 0.21 MeV

## Context

This work extends the AMD+RGM framework previously applied by Minomo (2011, 2012) and Sumi (2012) to describe the halo structure of 31Ne, now combined with ACCC to overcome the difficulty of identifying resonances. The results are consistent with the Nilsson model analysis by Hamamoto (2010), which identified the [321 3/2] Nilsson orbit as the primary candidate for the ground state configuration, and with particle-rotor model calculations by Urata (2011, 2012) and shell model calculations using the SDPF-M interaction by Nakamura (2009, 2014). The successful combination of AMD+RGM and ACCC enables future studies of other deformed haloes such as 37Mg and nuclei beyond the neutron drip line such as 26O.
