## Key claim

The pronounced structure seen in the $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum near the $\Sigma N$ threshold in production reactions like $K^-d \to \pi^- \Lambda p$ and $pp\to K^+ \Lambda p$ is consistent with a genuine cusp at the $\Sigma^+ n$ threshold, signaling an inelastic virtual state in the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ partial wave of the $\Sigma N$ isospin 1/2 channel, rather than a deuteron-like unstable bound state. The authors hedge that while the mean peak position of $2128.7\pm 0.3$ MeV coincides with the $\Sigma^+ n$ threshold, a second peak or shoulder at roughly 10-15 MeV above the $\Sigma N$ threshold varies significantly between data sets, making its theoretical interpretation difficult.

## Method

The authors analyzed published data from the strangeness exchange reaction $K^-d\to \pi^-\Lambda p$ (from Tan (1969), Braun (1977), Eastwood (1971), Pigot (1985), and others) and from the associated strangeness production reaction $pp\to K^+\Lambda p$ (from Siebert (1994), Budzanowski (2010), and TOF (2012)). For each data set, they subtracted a smooth background (either a polynomial fit to the yield below the peak or a phase-space distribution modified by final-state interactions) to isolate the peak structure, then fitted one or two incoherent Breit-Wigner distributions to determine the centroid mass and width of the enhancement(s).

## Key numbers

*   The mean peak position of the prominent structure, deduced from fits to data from both reactions, is $2128.7\pm 0.3$ MeV, which coincides with the threshold energy of the $\Sigma^+ n$ channel (2128.94 MeV).
*   For the $K^-d\to \pi^-\Lambda p$ data from Tan (1969), a single Breit-Wigner fit yields a centroid of $2129.55\pm 0.18$ MeV and a width $\Gamma = 12.0\pm 0.46$ MeV.
*   For the $pp\to K^+\Lambda p$ data from Pigot (1985), the peak position is $2129\pm 0.2\pm 0.2$ MeV and the width is $16.7\pm 1.9\pm 2$ MeV.
*   The mean centroid from one-Breit-Wigner fits across all data sets (excluding TOF I) is $2130.3\pm 0.6$ MeV, with a mean width of $\Gamma=12.2\pm 1.3$ MeV.
*   The $\Sigma^+ n$ threshold is at 2128.94 MeV and the $\Sigma^0 p$ threshold is at 2130.9 MeV.
*   The second peak or shoulder appears at roughly 10-15 MeV above the $\Sigma N$ threshold.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $\Lambda p$ interaction near the $\Sigma N$ threshold, a topic studied since the late 1960s with experiments like those of Tan (1969) and Cline (1968). It directly compares the predictions of modern coupled-channel $YN$ interaction models, such as the Juelich potentials (Haidenbauer 2005, Holzenkamp 1989) and Nijmegen potentials (Rijken 1999, Maessen 1989), which either produce a deuteron-like unstable bound state (e.g., NSC97f, Juelich 1989) or an inelastic virtual state (e.g., NSC89, Juelich 2005). The finding that the peak coincides with the $\Sigma^+ n$ threshold supports the inelastic virtual state interpretation, enabling future high-resolution exclusive measurements of $pp\to K^+\Lambda p$ to resolve the nature of the second, higher-mass structure.
