# The role of bulk energy in nuclear multifragmentation

> Because of thermal expansion and residual interactions, hot nuclear fragments produced in multifragmentation reactions may have lower nucleon density than the equilibrium density of cold nuclei. In terms of liquid-drop model this effect can be taken into account by reducing the bulk energy of fragments. We study the influence of this change on fragment yields and isotope distributions within the framework of the statistical multifragmentation model. Similarities and differences with previously discussed modifications of symmetry and surface energies of nuclei are analyzed.

## Key claim
The paper investigates whether reducing the bulk energy coefficient of hot primary fragments (due to thermal expansion and residual interactions lowering their nucleon density below normal nuclear density) can explain observed fragment yields and isotope distributions in multifragmentation, and finds that this effect enhances alpha-particle production and neutron richness of heavy fragments but does not change the shape of intermediate-mass fragment mass distributions.

## Method
The authors used the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) in its grand-canonical formulation to simulate multifragmentation of a gold source at excitation energies of 2–12 MeV per nucleon. They varied the bulk energy coefficient \(W_0\) from 16 MeV (normal density) down to 12 MeV, using a density-dependent expression based on the nuclear compressibility modulus \(K \approx 260\) MeV, and then applied a secondary deexcitation stage that gradually restores normal nuclear properties during evaporation.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density is \(\rho_0 \approx 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\); typical multifragmentation conditions involve densities \(\rho \approx 0.1-0.3 \rho_0\) and temperatures \(T \approx 3-8\) MeV.
- The bulk energy coefficient at normal density is \(W_0 = 16\) MeV; reducing fragment density to \(\rho_f \approx 0.6 \rho_0\) gives \(W_0 \approx 14\) MeV via the compressibility formula.
- The symmetry energy coefficient \(\gamma\) drops from 25 MeV for isolated cold nuclei to \(\approx 15\) MeV for hot primary fragments at multifragmentation, as found in prior experiments.
- The exponent \(n\) in the density-dependent symmetry energy \(\gamma(\rho_f) \propto (\rho_f/\rho_0)^n\) lies between 0.7 and 0.9 from recent analyses; to achieve a 40% drop in \(\gamma\) requires \(\rho_f \approx (0.45-0.55) \rho_0\), below the pasta-phase threshold.
- The freeze-out density was fixed at \(1/3 \rho_0\) for the SMM calculations.

## Context
This work extends prior studies by Botvina et al. (2006) and others (LeFevre, Iglio, Souliotis) that found modifications of symmetry and surface energies of hot fragments in multifragmentation. It specifically addresses the bulk energy term, which had not been explicitly varied in SMM before, and shows that reduced bulk energy enhances alpha-particle production and neutron enrichment of heavy fragments, complementing the previously observed reduction of symmetry energy. The results enable future multi-component analyses of experimental data to disentangle contributions from bulk, surface, and symmetry energy modifications in the freeze-out environment.
