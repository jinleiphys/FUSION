# Nucleon-Deuteron Scattering from an Effective Field Theory

> We use an effective field theory to compute low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering. We obtain the quartet scattering length using low energy constants entirely determined from low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering. We find $a_{th}=6.33$ fm, to be compared to $a_{exp}=6.35\pm 0.02$ fm.

# Nucleon-Deuteron Scattering from an Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
The quartet scattering length in neutron-deuteron scattering can be predicted from an effective field theory (EFT) without pions, using only low-energy constants determined from two-nucleon data, but the prediction is only valid at leading order and the doublet channel is expected to be more sensitive to pion-scale physics and is deferred to future work.

## Method
The authors constructed a non-relativistic EFT of nucleons and dibaryon fields (spin-0 and spin-1) that resums the leading contact interactions to all orders, reproducing the effective range expansion for NN scattering. They fixed the four dibaryon parameters from experimental NN scattering lengths and effective ranges in the triplet and singlet channels, then solved the resulting integral equation for zero-energy nd scattering in the quartet (J=3/2) channel using the Nystrom method.

## Key numbers
- Theoretical quartet scattering length: a_th = 6.33 fm, with an estimated uncertainty from higher orders of ±0.10 fm
- Experimental quartet scattering length: a_exp = 6.35 ± 0.02 fm (from Dilg, reference in text)
- Triplet NN scattering length: ³a = 5.42 fm; triplet effective range: ³r₀ = 1.75 fm (from Nijmegen group)
- Singlet NN scattering lengths: ¹a_pp = -17.3 fm, ¹a_np = -23.75 fm, ¹a_nn = -18.8 fm; singlet effective ranges: ¹r₀_pp = 2.85 fm, ¹r₀_np = 2.75 fm, ¹r₀_nn = 2.75 fm (from Jerry, reference in text)
- Dibaryon parameters from triplet channel: Δ_D = 8.7 MeV, g_D² = 1.6 × 10⁻³ MeV; from singlet channel: Δ_T = -1.5 MeV, g_T² = 1.0 × 10⁻³ MeV
- Resulting deuteron binding energy: B = 2.28 MeV
- Dimensionless parameter η = ³r₀ √(MB)/2 = 0.40
- Zero-range limit (η=0) gives ⁴a = 5.09 fm, reproducing Skornyakov and Ter-Martirosyan (reference in text)

## Context
This work extends the EFT approach of Weinberg (1990, 1991) and Kaplan (1997) for nuclear forces to the three-nucleon system, showing that the quartet nd scattering length can be predicted without free parameters once NN data are used as input. It builds on the universal or model-independent approaches of Efimov and Amado, but claims the EFT formulation is easier to implement conceptually and practically. The result enables systematic calculation of other few-nucleon observables within a QCD-consistent framework, with the doublet channel and three-nucleon forces left for future work.
