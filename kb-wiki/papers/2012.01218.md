# Nuclear pasta structures and symmetry energy

> In the framework of the relativistic mean field model with Thomas-Fermi approximation, we study the structures of low density nuclear matter in a three-dimensional geometry with reflection symmetry. The numerical accuracy and efficiency are improved by expanding the mean fields according to fast cosine transformation and considering only one octant of the unit cell. The effect of finite cell size is treated carefully by searching for the optimum cell size. Typical pasta structures (droplet, rod, slab, tube, and bubble) arranged in various crystalline configurations are obtained for both fixed proton fractions and $β$-equilibration. It is found that the properties of droplets/bubbles are similar in body-centered cubic (BCC) and face-centered cubic (FCC) lattices, where the FCC lattice generally becomes more stable than BCC lattice as density increases. For the rod/tube phases, the honeycomb lattice is always more stable than the simple one. By introducing an $ω$-$ρ$ cross coupling term, we further examine the pasta structures with a smaller slope of symmetry energy $L = 41.34$ MeV, which predicts larger onset densities for core-crust transition and non-spherical nuclei. Such a variation due to the reduction of $L$ is expected to have impacts on various properties in neutron stars, supernova dynamics, and binary neutron star mergers.

# Nuclear pasta structures and symmetry energy

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that reducing the slope of the symmetry energy from L = 89.39 MeV to L = 41.34 MeV, by introducing an ω-ρ cross coupling term in the relativistic mean field model, predicts larger onset densities for both the core-crust transition and the appearance of non-spherical nuclei in neutron star crusts, but this result depends on the specific choice of the ω-ρ coupling strength and the associated readjustment of the ρ-meson coupling constant to maintain consistency with pure neutron matter constraints at n_b = 0.1 fm^{-3}.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations with Thomas-Fermi approximation in a three-dimensional periodic unit cell, expanding the mean fields via fast cosine transformation and considering only one octant of the cell to improve numerical efficiency. They search for the optimal cell size at fixed baryon density and proton fraction, and compare the energies of droplets in body-centered cubic (BCC) and face-centered cubic (FCC) lattices, rods/tubes in simple and honeycomb configurations, and slabs. Two parameter sets are used: Set 0 (L = 89.39 MeV, no ω-ρ coupling) and Set 1 (L = 41.34 MeV, with ω-ρ coupling constant Λ_v = 0.34), both fitted to the same isoscalar nuclear matter properties at saturation density n_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}.

## Key numbers

- Slope of symmetry energy for Set 0: L = 89.39 MeV; for Set 1: L = 41.34 MeV (Table I).
- Symmetry energy at saturation: S = 32.46 MeV (Set 0) and S = 31.85 MeV (Set 1) (Table I).
- Saturation density: n_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}; binding energy per baryon at saturation: B(n_0) = -16.3 MeV; incompressibility: K(n_0) = 240 MeV; effective nucleon mass: m_N^*(n_0) = 0.78 m_N (Section III B).
- Neutron skin thickness of ^{208}Pb: ΔR_np = 0.195 fm (Set 0) and 0.157 fm (Set 1) (Table I).
- Maximum neutron star mass: M_max = 2.02 M_⊙ (Set 0) and 1.98 M_⊙ (Set 1) (Table I).
- Radius of 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star: R_{1.4} = 13.1 km (Set 0) and 11.9 km (Set 1) (Table I).
- Tidal deformability of 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star: Λ_{1.4} = 624 (Set 0) and 331 (Set 1) (Table I).
- Optimal lattice constant for BCC droplet phase at the example case: a = 30.71 fm (Figure 2).
- Convergence criterion: sum of squared deviations of local chemical potentials < 0.1 keV^2, giving energy per baryon uncertainty ΔE/A ≲ 0.001 keV (Section III A).

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional Thomas-Fermi calculations of nuclear pasta by Okamoto et al. (2012, 2013) and Maruyama et al. (2005), which used a larger symmetry energy slope (L = 89.39 MeV). The authors introduce an ω-ρ cross coupling term to reduce L to 41.34 MeV, consistent with the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 (Λ_{1.4} between 70 and 580) and the pure neutron matter constraint at n_b = 0.1 fm^{-3} from Brown et al. (2013). The results enable more realistic predictions for neutron star crust properties, supernova dynamics, and binary neutron star merger simulations, and complement earlier spherical/cylindrical Wigner-Seitz cell studies by Oyamatsu et al. (2007) and Grill et al. (2012) that found similar L-dependence of the core-crust transition density.
