# Angularity in Higgs boson decays via $\boldsymbol{H\to gg}$ at NNLL$'$ accuracy

> We present improved predictions of a class of event-shape distributions called angularity for a contribution from an effective operator $H\to gg$ in Higgs hadronic decay that suffers from large perturbative uncertainties. In the frame of Soft-Collinear Effective Theory, logarithmic terms of the distribution are resummed at NNLL$'$ accuracy, for which 2-loop constant of gluon-jet function for angularity is independently determined by a fit to fixed-order distribution at NLO corresponding to ${\mathcal{O}}(α_s^2)$ relative to the Born rate. Our determination shows reasonable agreement with the value in a thesis recently released. In the fit, we use an asymptotic form with a fractional power conjectured from recoil corrections at one-loop order and it improves the accuracy of determination in positive values of angularity parameter $a$. The resummed distribution is matched to the NLO fixed-order results to make our predictions valid at all angularity values. We also discuss the first moment and subtracted moment of angularity as a function of $a$ that allow to extract information on leading and subleading nonperturbative corrections associated with gluons.

## Key claim
The authors present improved predictions for angularity distributions in Higgs boson decays via the gluon channel \(H\to gg\), achieving next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic-prime (NNLL\('\)) accuracy by independently determining the two-loop constant of the gluon-jet function through a fit to fixed-order NLO distributions, with the caveat that the fit uses an asymptotic form including fractional-power recoil corrections conjectured from one-loop order, which improves accuracy for positive angularity parameter \(a\).

## Method
The authors employ Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to factorize the angularity distribution into hard, jet, and soft functions, resumming Sudakov logarithms in Laplace space. The two-loop constant of the gluon-jet function is determined by fitting the nonsingular part of the NLO fixed-order distribution (obtained from Monte Carlo simulations with \(10^{11}\) events) to an asymptotic form that includes recoil-correction terms with fractional powers for \(a>0\), using Tikhonov regularization to handle ill-posed fits. The resummed distribution at NNLL\('\) accuracy is then matched to the NLO fixed-order result to produce predictions valid across all angularity values.

## Key numbers
* The two-loop constant \(\cjt\) of the gluon-jet function is determined at seven values of \(a = -1 + n/4\) for \(n = 0,\dots,6\), with values such as \(65.43 \pm 0.91\) at \(a = -1\) and \(-36.84 \pm 0.72\) at \(a = 0\) for the quark channel (used for validation), and corresponding gluon-channel values derived from \(\Delta_a^\text{gluon} = \{-0.1598, -0.1474, -0.1319, -0.1106, -0.0787, -0.0258, 0.0695\}\) at \(\alpha_s = 0.1127\).
* The fit to the remainder function \(r_c(\tau_a,1)\) uses data down to \(\tau_a \sim 10^{-4}\) with an IR cutoff \(\alpha_\text{cut} = 10^{-11}\) on the dipole subtraction variable, and the systematic uncertainty is estimated from the maximum deviation of \(r_c\) over a set of fit regions.
* The recoil-correction coefficients at one loop are given as \(c_1 = -1\), \(c_2 = \frac{1}{2}(3-2a)\), and \(c_3 = -\frac{1}{6}(20-27a+9a^2)\) for \(0<a<1\).

## Context
This work extends previous resummation studies of angularity in Higgs decays, which were available at NLL\('\) for the gluon channel (byan, 2022) and at NNLL\('\) for the quark channel (Bell, 2018; Brune, 2022). The improved NNLL\('\) predictions for the gluon channel reduce perturbative uncertainties and enable more precise extraction of nonperturbative corrections from the first moment and subtracted moment of angularity as a function of \(a\), relevant for future Higgs factories such as CEPC and FCC-ee.
