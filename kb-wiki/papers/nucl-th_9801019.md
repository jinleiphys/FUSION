# Effects of isospin-symmetry violation on tests of the standard model using parity-violating electron scattering

> The effects of isospin-symmetry breaking on the observables for parity-violating electron scattering are investigated within the framework of the nuclear shell model for 12C, 16O, and 28Si. Contributions due to mixing with low-lying states as well as admixtures of 1p-1h configurations (via the radial wave functions) are accounted for. It is found that isospin-mixing can be important, and the consequences regarding precision tests of the standard model are addressed.

# Effects of isospin-symmetry violation on tests of the standard model using parity-violating electron scattering

## Key claim
Isospin-mixing corrections to the parity-violating electron scattering asymmetry can exceed 1% for momentum transfers above approximately 0.9 fm⁻¹ in ¹²C, contradicting earlier estimates that such corrections would remain below 1% for essentially all momentum transfers. However, the correction factor Γ(q) crosses through zero near the second maximum of the charge form factor, a feature that might be exploited to minimize nuclear-structure uncertainties in experiments on heavier nuclei.

## Method
Shell-model calculations in proton-neutron formalism were performed for ¹²C (p-shell), ¹⁶O (closed p-shell), and ²⁸Si (sd-shell), including empirically determined isospin-nonconserving (INC) interactions constrained to reproduce binding energy differences within isospin multiplets. Radial wave functions from Hartree-Fock calculations using Skyrme-type interactions were refined by scaling the mean-field potential to match experimental separation energies from intermediate states of the A-1 system, following the same procedure used for superallowed Fermi beta decay corrections.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²C, the correction factor |Γ(q)| exceeds 1% at approximately 0.9 fm⁻¹ momentum transfer
- The standard model asymmetry in the isospin-symmetry limit is given by 𝒜₀ = 3.22 × 10⁻⁶ q², with q² measured in fm⁻²
- The weak mixing angle used is sin²θ_W ∼ 0.230 ± 0.005
- For superallowed Fermi beta decay, isospin-symmetry breaking corrections are ≤ 0.7%
- The oscillator parameter for center-of-mass corrections is parameterized as ħω = 45A⁻¹/³ − 25A⁻²/³ MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier estimates by Donnelly (1989) that used a two-level model with a "worst-case" philosophy assuming isospin-mixing matrix elements of order 300 keV, finding corrections below 1% for ¹²C. The improved treatment incorporates realistic shell-model calculations with empirically constrained INC interactions and proper treatment of radial wave functions via intermediate A-1 states, closely mirroring the methodology used for superallowed Fermi beta decay corrections by Towner and Hardy (1990s). The results enable design of parity-violating electron scattering experiments at TJLAB that either minimize nuclear-structure uncertainties by exploiting the zero-crossing of Γ(q) or account for isospin-mixing corrections when testing the standard model or probing strangeness content in the nucleon.
