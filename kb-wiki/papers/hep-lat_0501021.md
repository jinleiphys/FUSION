# Baryon Masses in Lattice QCD with Exact Chiral Symmetry

> We investigate the baryon mass spectrum in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry. For 100 gauge configurations generated with Wilson gauge action at $ β= 6.1 $ on the $ 20^3 \times 40 $ lattice, we compute (point-to-point) quark propagators for 30 quark masses in the range $ 67 {MeV} \le m_q \le 1790 {MeV} $. For baryons only composed of strange and charm quarks, their masses are extracted directly from the time correlation functions, while for those containing $ u (d) $ light quarks, their masses are obtained by chiral extrapolation to $ m_π= 135 $ MeV. Our results of baryon masses are in good agreement with experimental values, except for the negative parity states of $ Λ$ and $ Λ_c $. Further, our results of charmed (including doubly-charmed and triply-charmed) baryons can serve as predictions of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper reports baryon masses computed in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry, finding good agreement with experimental values for most states, but with the explicit caveat that the negative parity states of Λ and Λ_c (specifically Λ(1405) and Λ_c(2593)) deviate significantly from experiment, warranting further study with higher statistics, larger lattices, smaller light quark masses, and dynamical fermions.

## Method

The authors generated 100 gauge configurations using the Wilson gauge action at β = 6.1 on a 20³ × 40 lattice, employing optimal domain-wall fermions to achieve exact chiral symmetry. Point-to-point quark propagators were computed for 30 quark masses in the range 67 MeV ≤ m_q ≤ 1790 MeV; baryon masses for states composed only of strange and charm quarks were extracted directly from time correlation functions, while masses for baryons containing u(d) light quarks were obtained by linear chiral extrapolation in m_π² to the physical pion mass of 135 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Inverse lattice spacing: a⁻¹ = 2.237(76) GeV, determined from the pion correlation function with experimental input f_π = 132 MeV
- Strange quark bare mass: m_s a = 0.08; charm quark bare mass: m_c a = 0.8, fixed to reproduce experimental masses of φ(1020) and J/ψ(3010) respectively
- Nucleon (1/2⁺): 958(36) MeV (experiment: 939 MeV)
- Λ (1/2⁻): 1854(47) MeV (experiment: 1405 MeV)
- Λ_c (1/2⁻): 3027(63) MeV (experiment: 2593 MeV)
- Ξ_cc⁺ (1/2⁺): 3522(16) MeV (experiment: 3520 MeV from SELEX)
- Ω_c⁰ (1/2⁺): 2677(30) MeV (experiment: 2697 MeV)
- Ω_ccc (3/2⁺): 4681(28) MeV (prediction, no experimental value)
- Ω_ccc (3/2⁻): 5066(48) MeV (prediction, no experimental value)

## Context

This work extends quenched lattice QCD calculations of baryon masses using optimal domain-wall fermions, which provide exact chiral symmetry at finite lattice spacing, improving upon earlier formulations with approximate chiral symmetry. The results for charmed baryons (singly-charmed, doubly-charmed, and triply-charmed) serve as first-principles predictions of QCD, with the Ξ_cc⁺ (1/2⁺) mass agreeing with the SELEX observation (Mattson, 2002). The significant discrepancy for the negative parity Λ(1405) and Λ_c(2593) highlights the need for future studies with dynamical fermions, larger volumes, and lighter quark masses to resolve excited-state contamination in the correlation functions.
