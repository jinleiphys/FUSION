# Multifragmentation of non-spherical nuclei : Analysis of central Xe + Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucl

> The influence of shape of expanding and rotating source on various characteristics of the multifragmentation process is studied. The analysis is based on the extension of the statistical microcanonical multifragmentation model. The comparison with the data is done for central Xe+Sn collisions at 50 A MeV as measured by INDRA Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the failure of standard spherical statistical multifragmentation models to reproduce kinetic observables in central Xe+Sn collisions at 50 A MeV can be resolved by assuming a prolate, slowly rotating, and collectively expanding source shape, with the caveat that the shape effect is only visible in angular and kinematic observables, not in fragment-size distributions.

## Method

The authors extended the microcanonical Metropolis Monte Carlo (MMMC) multifragmentation model to treat axially symmetric ellipsoidal freeze-out configurations (prolate and oblate), including exact Coulomb energy calculations for deformed shapes, angular momentum conservation with a rotation axis perpendicular to the reaction plane, and a radial collective expansion velocity profile with a power-law exponent α. They simulated a ¹⁹⁷Au source with 6 A MeV thermal excitation energy and L=40 ħ at break-up density ρ≈ρ₀/6, then filtered the events through the INDRA detector software replica and applied experimental centrality conditions (≥80% detected charge and momentum, Θ_flow ≥ π/3) for comparison with data.

## Key numbers

- Source angular momentum: L=40 ħ for the equilibrated source formed in central Xe+Sn collisions at 50 A MeV.
- Break-up density: ρ≈ρ₀/6, giving effective spherical radius R_sys=12.8 fm for the ¹⁹⁷Au source.
- Deformation ratios: prolate shape with ℛ=0.6 and oblate shape with ℛ=1.667 (where ℛ=R_x/R_z).
- Experimental mean kinetic energy per nucleon of IMFs: 5.2±0.1 MeV/nucleon, reproduced with blast velocities v_b=0.088 c (prolate) and v_b=0.063 c (oblate) for α=2.
- Blast velocity profile exponent: α=2 favored over α=1 by the kinetic energy data.
- Scale parameter for expansion: R₀=0.7 R_sys in all studies.

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation approach of Gross (1990) and Bondorf (1996) by incorporating non-spherical source shapes, addressing discrepancies between spherical models and INDRA Collaboration data for central Xe+Sn collisions at 50 A MeV (Bougault, 1998; Arnaud, 1998; Salou, 1998). The results enable future investigations of velocity and angular correlations between fragments as more sensitive probes of freeze-out shape, as noted by the authors in their ongoing work.
