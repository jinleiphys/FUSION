# Deuteron electromagnetic form factors in transverse plane with a phenomenological Lagrangian approach

> A phenomenological Lagrangian approach is employed to study the electromagnetic properties of deuteron. The deuteron is regarded as a loosely bound state of a proton and a neutron. The deuteron electromagnetic form factors are expressed in light-front representation in the transverse plane. The transverse charge density of the deuteron is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple phenomenological Lagrangian approach, treating the deuteron as a loosely bound S-wave state of a proton and neutron with only one free parameter (the size parameter Lambda_D), can qualitatively reproduce the deuteron electromagnetic form factors and transverse densities in the low Q^2 region, though discrepancies grow at higher Q^2 due to the oversimplified Gaussian wave function and the neglect of the D-wave component.

## Method

The deuteron is modeled as a proton-neutron bound state via an effective Lagrangian with a Gaussian correlation function characterized by a single size parameter Lambda_D. The coupling constant g_D is fixed by the compositeness condition (Weinberg-Salam). The electromagnetic form factors G_C and G_M are computed in light-front representation from loop diagrams where the photon couples to the proton and neutron constituents, using the Dirac and Pauli form factors of the nucleons. The transverse charge and magnetic densities rho_C(b) and rho_M(b) are obtained as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of the form factors in impact parameter space.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: epsilon_D ~ 2.22 MeV
- Normalization at zero recoil: G_C(0) = 1, G_Q(0) = M_D^2 * Q_D = 25.83, G_M(0) = (M_D/M_N) * mu_D = 1.714
- Model parameter: Lambda_D ~ 0.23 GeV
- Transverse charge density at b=0: maximum value 0.324 (this work), compared to 0.720 and 0.426 from two different parameterizations
- Mean-square charge radius (3D): R_C* ~ 2.75 fm (this work), compared to R_C* ~ 2.56 fm from parameterizations and experimental R_C* = 2.128 +/- 0.11 fm
- Mean-square magnetic radius (3D): R_M* ~ 2.16 fm (this work), compared to R_M* ~ 1.93 fm from parameterizations and experimental R_M* = 1.90 +/- 0.14 fm

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Lagrangian approach previously applied to hadronic molecules like X(3872) and Lambda_c(2940) (Dong et al.) to the deuteron, and follows the transverse-plane analysis pioneered for pions (Soper, Burkardt, Diehl, Carlson, Miller) and protons. The computed transverse charge density shows a positive central peak and a long positive tail, similar to the proton case, attributed to the positive pion cloud; the results are compared to parameterizations from Tomasi et al. and experimental data. The approach enables further study of deuteron structure in impact parameter space, though a more realistic calculation including the D-wave component is needed to address the quadrupole form factor.
