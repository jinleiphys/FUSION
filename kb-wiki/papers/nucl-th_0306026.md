# Closed-flavor pi + J/psi and pi + Upsilon Cross Sections at Low Energies from Dipion Decays

> The scale of low energy c-cbar and b-bbar cross sections on light hadrons is of great importance to searches for the quark gluon plasma using the heavy-quarkonium suppression signature. Unfortunately, little is known about these near-threshold cross sections at present, and recent theoretical estimates span many orders of magnitude. Here we use experimental data on the four observed closed-flavor heavy quarkonium hadronic decays psi' -> pi pi J/psi, Upsilon' -> pi pi Upsilon, Upsilon'' -> pi pi Upsilon and Upsilon'' -> pi pi Upsilon', combined with simple models of the transition amplitudes, to estimate the pion scattering cross sections of c-cbar and b-bbar mesons near threshold. Specifically we consider the closed-flavor reactions pi J/psi -> pi psi', pi Upsilon -> pi Upsilon', pi Upsilon -> pi Upsilon'' and pi Upsilon' -> pi Upsilon'' and their time-reversed analogues. Our results may be useful in constraining theoretical models of the strong interactions of heavy quarkonia, and can be systematically improved through future detailed studies of dipion decays, notably psi' -> pi pi J/psi and Upsilon'' -> pi pi Upsilon.

# Closed-flavor π + J/ψ and π + Υ Cross Sections at Low Energies from Dipion Decays

## Key claim
Using crossing symmetry and experimental dipion decay widths, the authors estimate near-threshold cross sections for closed-flavor pion scattering on heavy quarkonia (π J/ψ → π ψ', π Υ → π Υ', etc.), but the results depend strongly on the assumed model for the decay amplitude, with different models giving cross sections that diverge rapidly above a few hundred MeV from threshold.

## Method
The authors relate the experimentally measured partial widths of four dipion decays (ψ' → ππ J/ψ, Υ' → ππ Υ, Υ'' → ππ Υ, and Υ'' → ππ Υ') to the corresponding pion scattering cross sections via crossing symmetry, using the same invariant amplitude. Three simple models for the squared invariant amplitude are assumed: a constant amplitude approximation, a scalar anomaly/gluon radiation model with ⟨|A|²⟩ ∝ t² (where t = m_{ππ}²), and a meson exchange model with a Breit-Wigner form for a scalar f₀ meson modified by the scalar anomaly soft-pion factor t².

## Key numbers
- The partial width for ψ' → π⁺π⁻ J/ψ is 91.5 ± 9.0 keV, with a Dalitz plot area of 0.436 GeV⁴.
- The partial width for Υ' → π⁺π⁻ Υ is 8.3 ± 1.3 keV, with a Dalitz plot area of 1.023 GeV⁴.
- The partial width for Υ'' → π⁺π⁻ Υ is 1.2 ± 0.2 keV, with a Dalitz plot area of 6.679 GeV⁴.
- The partial width for Υ'' → π⁺π⁻ Υ' is 0.7 ± 0.2 keV, with a Dalitz plot area of 0.027 GeV⁴.
- In the constant amplitude approximation, the cross section for π⁺ J/ψ → π⁺ ψ' a few hundred MeV above threshold is about 20 μb, and for the exothermic π⁺ ψ' → π⁺ J/ψ it is about 0.1 mb.
- In the constant amplitude approximation, the cross section for π⁺ Υ → π⁺ Υ' a few hundred MeV above threshold is about 2 μb, and for π⁺ Υ' → π⁺ Υ it is about 10 μb.
- For π⁺ J/ψ → π⁺ ψ', all three models give consistent cross sections of about 5-15 μb at E_cm = 3.9 GeV, but predictions diverge rapidly with increasing invariant mass.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical estimates of heavy quarkonium dissociation cross sections on light hadrons, which had spanned many orders of magnitude, by using experimental dipion decay data as a direct input. The authors compare their results to prior calculations by Sorge, Shuryak and Zahed (1997) using scalar meson exchange with a high-mass f₀(1400), by Chen and Savage (1997) using a gluon radiation model, and by Fujii and Kharzeev (1999) using a color-dipole scattering model. The results can be systematically improved through future high-statistics studies of dipion decays, notably ψ' → ππ J/ψ and Υ'' → ππ Υ, and may help constrain theoretical models of the strong interactions of heavy quarkonia relevant to quark-gluon plasma searches.
