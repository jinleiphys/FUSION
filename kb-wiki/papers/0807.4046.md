# New anatomy of quantum holonomy

> We present a unified formulation of quantum holonomy which is capable of describing all of its known varieties. The full role of non-Abelian gauge connection is elucidated. As examples, solvable models of quantum kicked spin are analyzed. They are shown to exhibit spiral holonomy, as well as Berry phase and Wilczek-Zee holonomy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that all known varieties of quantum holonomy (Berry phase, Wilczek-Zee holonomy, and spiral eigenvalue holonomy) can be described within a single unified formalism based on the non-Abelian Mead-Berry gauge connection, with the caveat that the spiral holonomy, previously considered an outlier, is shown to arise from the same gauge structure when the holonomy matrix takes the form of a permutation matrix that can exchange eigenstates of different energies.

## Method

The authors derive a general expression for the holonomy matrix M(C) = W(C) B(C) for an adiabatic cyclic parameter variation, where W(C) is a path-ordered exponential of the full non-Abelian Mead-Berry gauge connection A(α) and B(C) is a diagonal matrix constructed from the diagonal part of A(α). They apply this formalism to solvable models of quantum kicked spin: a spin-1/2 system and a spin-3/2 system, both with periodic delta-function kicks, where the Floquet operator and its eigenstates are obtained analytically.

## Key numbers

- For the kicked spin-1/2 system with Hamiltonian H = T σ₃ + λV Σ δ(t-n-1/2), the quasienergy eigenvalues are εₙ = (p/2)λ + (-1)ⁿ E, where E = cos⁻¹(cos λ cos T − sin λ sin T cos γ), with integer p and spherical parameters γ, ξ.
- For the cyclic path C_λ (λ → λ+2π) in the spin-1/2 system, the holonomy matrix is M(C_λ) = cos[(2-p)π/2] − i Σ₂ sin[(2-p)π/2], which for odd p gives pure eigenstate exchange.
- The winding number for spiral holonomy in the spin-1/2 system is Δn(C_λ) = p, with Δn(C_ξ) = 0 and Δn(C_γ) = 0.
- For the spin-3/2 system, the doubly-degenerate quasienergies are εₙ = (p/2)λ + (-1)ⁿ E, with the same functional form of E as the spin-1/2 case.

## Context

This work extends the geometric phase framework of Berry (1984) and Wilczek and Zee (1984) by incorporating the spiral eigenvalue holonomy previously reported by Cheon (1998) and Tanaka and Miyamoto (2007) into a unified gauge-theoretic description. The formalism generalizes Fujikawa's (2005) path-integral approach to quantum holonomy and provides a gauge-invariant expression that treats all three holonomy types on equal footing, enabling future applications to adiabatic quantum control and holonomic quantum computing as suggested by Zanardi and Rasetti (1999) and others.
