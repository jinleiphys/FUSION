# Mass shifts of $^3P_J$ heavy quarkonia due to the effect of two-gluon annihilation

> In this work, we calculate the nonrelativistic asymptotic behavior of the amplitudes of $q\overline{q}\rightarrow 2g \rightarrow q\overline{q}$ in the leading order of $α_s$ (LO-$α_s$) with $q\overline{q}$ in the $^3P_J$ channels. In the practical calculation we take the momenta of quarks and antiquarks on-shell and expand the amplitudes on the three-momentum of the quarks and antiquarks to order 6 and get three nonzero terms. The imaginary parts of the first term and the second term are the old. The real parts of the results have IR divergence. When applying the results to the heavy quarkonia, the corresponding amplitude of $q\overline{q}\rightarrow1g\rightarrow q\overline{q}$ with $q\overline{q}$ in the color octet $^3S_1$ channel is considered to absorb the IR divergence in a unitary way in the leading order of $v$ (LO-$v$). The finial results can be used to estimate the mass shifts of the $^3P_J$ heavy quarkonia due to the effect of two-gluon annihilation. The numerical estimation shows that the contributions to the mass shifts of $χ_{c0,c1,c2}$ are about $1.23\sim1.58$ MeV, $1.57\sim1.86$ MeV and $5.92\sim5.45$ MeV when taking $α_s\approx 0.25\sim0.35$.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the real part of the nonrelativistic asymptotic behavior of two-gluon annihilation amplitudes for heavy quarkonia in the \(^3P_J\) channels at leading order in \(\alpha_s\), finding that the resulting mass shifts are positive for all \(^3P_J\) states (including the \(^3P_1\) state, whose decay width to two gluons is zero) and that the infrared divergence in the real part can be absorbed by a one-gluon exchange amplitude in the color octet \(^3S_1\) channel in a unitary way at leading order in \(v\).

## Method

The authors compute the on-shell scattering amplitudes \(q\bar{q} \rightarrow 2g \rightarrow q\bar{q}\) for \(q\bar{q}\) in the \(^3P_J\) channels and \(q\bar{q} \rightarrow 1g \rightarrow q\bar{q}\) for \(q\bar{q}\) in the color octet \(^3S_1\) channel, at leading order in \(\alpha_s\). They expand the amplitudes in the three-momentum \(p\) of the quarks and antiquarks up to order \(p^6\), perform the loop integrals in \(D=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions using FeynCalc and FIESTA, and extract the coefficients of the expansion. The infrared-divergent parts of the \(^3P_J\) amplitudes are absorbed into the \(^3S_1\) amplitude to obtain finite results for the mass shifts.

## Key numbers

* Mass shift of \(\chi_{c0}(1P)\): \(5.92 \sim 5.45\) MeV for \(\alpha_s \approx 0.25 \sim 0.35\).
* Mass shift of \(\chi_{c1}(1P)\): \(1.57 \sim 1.86\) MeV for \(\alpha_s \approx 0.25 \sim 0.35\).
* Mass shift of \(\chi_{c1}(2P)\): \(1.23 \sim 1.58\) MeV for \(\alpha_s \approx 0.25 \sim 0.35\).
* Ratio \(\Delta M(^3P_0)/\Gamma(^3P_0) \approx 0.27\) (assuming small \(\overline{H}_8\) contribution).
* Ratio \(\Delta M(^3P_2)/\Gamma(^3P_2) \approx 0.06\) (assuming small \(\overline{H}_8\) contribution).
* Imaginary parts of expansion coefficients: \(\text{Im}[c_{0,2}] = 8\pi\), \(\text{Im}[c_{2,2}] = 32\pi/15\), \(\text{Im}[c_{0,4}] = -56\pi/3\), \(\text{Im}[c_{2,4}] = -64\pi/15\).
* Real parts of expansion coefficients (finite parts after subtracting \(C_{IR}\) terms): \(\text{Re}[c_{0,2}^{fin}] = -8/3 - 16\log 2\), \(\text{Re}[c_{2,2}^{fin}] = 32/15 - (64/15)\log 2\), \(\text{Re}[c_{1,2}^{fin}] = -16/9\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation for the \(^1S_0\) channel (Cao and Zhou, 2018) to the \(^3P_J\) channels, where gauge invariance prevented a direct extension of the earlier off-shell method. The results provide the real parts of the NRQCD four-fermion coefficients for \(^3P_J\) states at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) and leading order in \(v\), complementing the known imaginary parts used for decay widths (Petrelli et al., 1997). The numerical estimates for \(\chi_{cJ}\) mass shifts (up to about 6 MeV) indicate that two-gluon annihilation effects should be considered in precise heavy quarkonium spectroscopy, especially when decay channels with large widths are open. The formalism also enables extraction of the nonperturbative matrix elements \(H_1\) and \(\overline{H}_8\) from experimental decay data, which can then be used to predict mass shifts for other \(^3P_J\) states such as bottomonia.
