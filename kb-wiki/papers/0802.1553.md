# Detection of supernovae neutrinos with neutrino-iron scattering

> The $ν_e-^{56}$Fe cross section is evaluated in the projected quasiparticle random phase approximation (PQRPA). This model solves the puzzle observed in RPA for nuclei with mass around $^{12}$C, because it is the only RPA model that treats the Pauli principle correctly. The cross sections as a function of the incident neutrino energy are compared with recent theoretical calculations of similar models. The average cross section weighted with the flux spectrum yields a good agreement with the experimental data. The expected number of events in the detection of supernova neutrinos is calculated for the LVD detector leading to an upper limit for the electron neutrino energy of particular importance in this experiment

## Key claim

The Projected Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (PQRPA) yields a smaller neutrino-iron cross section than almost all other RPA models for neutrino energies up to 60 MeV, and the smallest cross section of all models considered for energies between 60 and 100 MeV, but the agreement with experimental data is not sufficient to select the best nuclear structure calculation because experimental errors are very large.

## Method

The authors calculated the inclusive ν_e-⁵⁶Fe cross section using both QRPA and PQRPA within a configuration space of 12 single-particle levels spanning the 2ℏω, 3ℏω, and 4ℏω oscillator shells, employing a δ-interaction with different coupling constants for the particle-hole, particle-particle, and pairing channels. They folded the cross sections with the Michel energy spectrum for DAR neutrinos and with zero-pinched Fermi-Dirac distributions for supernova neutrino fluxes, then computed the expected number of events for the LVD detector as a function of neutrino temperature.

## Key numbers

* Flux-averaged cross section ⟨σ_e⟩ for ⁵⁶Fe(ν_e,e⁻)⁵⁶Co in the DAR region: QRPA gives 264.6 × 10⁻⁴² cm², PQRPA gives 197.3 × 10⁻⁴² cm², compared to the experimental value of 256 ± 83(stat) ± 42(syst) × 10⁻⁴² cm² (KARMEN, 1999).
* Theoretical B(GT⁻) strength for ⁵⁶Fe: PQRPA gives 17.7, overestimating the experimental value of 9.9 ± 2.4 (Rapaport, 1983).
* Supernova parameters assumed: total binding energy E_b = 3 × 10⁵³ erg, distance D = 10 kpc, with energy equipartition f_α = 1/6 per flavor, and antineutrino temperature T_ν̅_e in the interval [4, 7] MeV.
* The relevant energy interval for the ν_e-⁵⁶Fe reaction in the LVD detector is E_ν ≤ 60 MeV for the adopted astrophysical parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of ν-⁵⁶Fe cross sections using the Hybrid model (Kolbe, 1999), QRPA with Skyrme force (Lazauskas, 2007), and relativistic QRPA (Paar, 2007), and is the first application of PQRPA to this nucleus. The results are directly relevant to the LVD detector's supernova neutrino signal analysis (Agafonova, 2007), which previously used the Hybrid model cross section, and the authors note that the PQRPA cross section lies below most other models in the energy region most important for LVD.
