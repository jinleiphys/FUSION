# The Octet of Goldstone-Bosons in the SU(3) Linear sigma Model in the QRPA

> A symmetry conserving, non-perturbative treatment based on a variational squeezed vacuum state in conjunction with a well-defined class of RPA fluctuations is applied to the SU(3) linear-sigma-model. It is shown that the Goldstone theorem holds exactly both at zero and finite temperature. The approach represents a systematic procedure which avoids problems of the Gaussian Functional with the symmetries.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Goldstone theorem holds exactly for the full octet of pseudo-scalar mesons in the SU(3) linear sigma model at both zero and finite temperature when using the Quasi-particle Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA) built on a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean field, correcting the symmetry violations that plague the Gaussian functional approach (GFA) alone.

## Method

The authors apply a variational squeezed vacuum state (equivalent to the Gaussian functional approach) to define a self-consistent HFB mean field for the SU(3) linear sigma model. They then construct RPA excitation operators that include the symmetry generators, solving the finite-temperature RPA equations to obtain the masses of the pseudo-scalar meson nonet. The RPA equations are shown to yield zero-energy spurious solutions for the octet of pseudo-scalars in the chiral limit, proving that the Goldstone theorem is satisfied.

## Key numbers

- The octet of pseudo-scalar mesons (π, K, η) acquires zero mass in the chiral limit (c₀ = 0) at all temperatures below the transition temperature T_c, as shown by the relation m_π^{T²} = 0 when c₀ = 0 and ξ₀^T ≠ 0.
- The quasi-particle energies for the octet are degenerate: ℰ_{(p)_k}^T = ℰ_π^T and ℰ_{(s)_k}^T = ℰ_σ^T for k = 1,...,8, while the singlet states ℰ_{(p)_0}^T and ℰ_{(s)_0}^T are distinct from the octet.
- The pion mass satisfies the Ward identity: -D_π^{-1}(0) = -c₀ / ⟨σ₀⟩, linking the Goldstone two-point function to the condensate.

## Context

This work extends the HFB-RPA approach previously applied to the SU(2) linear sigma model by Aouissat et al. (1996) to the more complicated SU(3) case. It addresses the failure of the Gaussian functional approach (GFA) to preserve chiral symmetry, a problem noted in early GFA studies, and corrects the symmetry violations found in the leading-order 1/N_f expansion treatment of the three-flavor linear sigma model by Orts et al. (1997), where Goldstone bosons acquired masses from thermal fluctuations. The approach enables systematic non-perturbative studies of chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature in three-flavor effective theories.
