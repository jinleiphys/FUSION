# Hypernuclear production by ($γ, K^+$) reaction within a relativistic model

> Within a fully covariant model based on an effective Lagrangian picture, we investigate the hypernuclear production in photon-nucleus interaction on $ ^{16}$O target. The explicit kaon production vertex is described via creation, propagation and decay into relevant channel of $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710), and $N^*$(1720) intermediate baryonic resonance states in the initial interaction of the incident photon with one of the target protons. Bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation. Using vertex parameters determined in the previous studies, contributions of the $N^*$(1710) baryonic resonance dominate the total production cross sections which are found to peak at photon energies below 1 GeV. The results show that photoproduction is the most appropriate means for studying the unnatural parity hypernuclear states, thus accessing the spin dependence of the hyperon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

Within a fully covariant model using an effective Lagrangian, the $N^*(1710)$ baryonic resonance dominates the total cross section for hypernuclear production via the $(\gamma, K^+)$ reaction on $^{16}$O, with cross sections peaking at photon energies below 1 GeV; the authors note that their absolute cross sections may be uncertain by about 10% because nucleon intermediate states (Born terms) were omitted.

## Method

The authors calculate the $^{16}$O$(\gamma,K^+)$$^{16}\!\!\!_\Lambda$N reaction within a fully covariant model where the incident photon excites a bound proton to $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1710)$, and $N^*(1720)$ baryonic resonance intermediate states, which then decay into a $\Lambda$ hyperon and a $K^+$ meson. Bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with scalar and vector Woods-Saxon potentials in momentum space, and the outgoing kaon is treated in the plane-wave approximation. The nuclear structure is described by pure single-particle-single-hole ($\Lambda N^{-1}$) configurations, and the differential cross section is computed from the coherent sum of resonance amplitudes using vertex parameters determined in prior studies.

## Key numbers

- The binding energies of $\Lambda$ single-particle states in $^{16}\!\!\!_\Lambda$N are 13.20 MeV ($1s_{1/2}$), 2.50 MeV ($1p_{3/2}$), and 2.04 MeV ($1p_{1/2}$).
- The binding energies of nucleon hole states in $^{16}$O are 18.40 MeV ($1p_{3/2}$) and 12.13 MeV ($1p_{1/2}$).
- The threshold for kaon photoproduction on $^{16}$O is about 680 MeV, and the momentum transfer at zero degrees is above 500 MeV/c.
- Total cross sections peak at photon energies around 900 MeV, about 200 MeV above the production threshold.
- Contributions of the $N^*(1650)$ resonance are at least one order of magnitude smaller than those of $N^*(1710)$, and $N^*(1720)$ contributions are smaller by 3-4 orders of magnitude.
- Within the $(1p_{3/2}^{-1},1p_{3/2}^\Lambda)$ group, the cross section of the $3^+$ state is larger than that of the $2^+$ state by about a factor of 2.5 and by more than an order of magnitude than that of the $0^+$ state.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used are: reduced radius $r_s = r_v = 0.983$ fm, diffuseness $a_s = 0.70$ fm and $a_v = 0.58$ fm; the depths for $\Lambda$ states range from about -205 to -252 MeV (scalar) and 166 to 204 MeV (vector), while for nucleon hole states they are -445.0 MeV (scalar) and 360 MeV (vector).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of the $(\gamma,K^+)$ reaction on nuclei by Bennhold and collaborators (1989) and Lee and collaborators (2001), among others, by providing a fully covariant treatment with Dirac bound-state spinors and explicit $s$-channel resonance contributions. The results confirm the finding of Shyam and collaborators (2004) for the $(p,K^+)$ reaction that the $N^*(1710)$ resonance dominates production. The paper shows that photoproduction preferentially excites unnatural parity hypernuclear states, which are only weakly populated in hadronic reactions such as $(K^-,\pi^-)$ and $(\pi^+,K^+)$, thereby enabling tighter constraints on the spin-dependent part of the $\Lambda N$ interaction. Future work should include kaon-nucleus distortion effects and Born terms to improve the absolute cross section predictions.
