# Nuclear properties with semilocal momentum-space regularized chiral interactions beyond N2LO

> We present a comprehensive investigation of few-nucleon systems as well as light and medium-mass nuclei up to $A=48$ using the current Low Energy Nuclear Physics International Collaboration two-nucleon interactions in combination with the third-order (N$^2$LO) three-nucleon forces. To address the systematic overbinding of nuclei starting from $A \sim 10$ found in our earlier study utilizing the N$^2$LO two- and three-nucleon forces, we take into account higher-order corrections to the two-nucleon potentials up through fifth order in chiral effective field theory. The resulting Hamiltonian can be completely determined using the $A=3$ binding energies and selected nucleon-deuteron cross sections as input. It is then shown to predict other nucleon-deuteron scattering observables and spectra of light $p$-shell nuclei, for which a detailed correlated truncation error analysis is performed, in agreement with experimental data. Moreover, the predicted ground state energies of nuclei in the oxygen isotopic chain from $^{14}$O to $^{26}$O as well as $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca show a remarkably good agreement with experimental values, given that the Hamiltonian is fixed completely from the $A \leq 3$ data, once the fourth-order (N$^3$LO) corrections to the two-nucleon interactions are taken into account. On the other hand, the charge radii are found to be underpredicted by $\sim 10\%$ for the oxygen isotopes and by almost $20\%$ for $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca.

## Key claim

The systematic overbinding of nuclei starting from A ~ 10 found in the earlier N2LO study is resolved by including higher-order corrections to the two-nucleon potential up through N4LO+, even though the three-nucleon force is still only included at N2LO; however, charge radii remain underpredicted by about 10% for oxygen isotopes and almost 20% for 40Ca and 48Ca.

## Method

The authors combined semilocal momentum-space regularized (SMS) chiral two-nucleon (NN) interactions at orders N3LO, N4LO, and N4LO+ with the three-nucleon force (3NF) at N2LO only. The low-energy constants cD and cE of the 3NF were fixed from the triton binding energy and the neutron-deuteron differential cross-section minimum at 70 MeV. Few-nucleon systems were solved using Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations in momentum space, while light p-shell nuclei were treated with the No-Core Configuration Interaction (NCCI) method and medium-mass nuclei up to A=48 with the in-medium no-core shell model (IM-NCSM).

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy fixed to experimental value of -8.482 MeV by construction; 3He binding energy predicted as -7.740 MeV (N4LO+, Lambda=450 MeV) compared to experimental -7.718 MeV, a difference of about 20 keV
- Neutron-deuteron total cross-section at 70 MeV and 135 MeV: N4LO+ NN + N2LO 3NF predictions underestimate data by about 4% at 70 MeV and about 7% at 135 MeV
- Charge radii underpredicted by about 10% for oxygen isotopes and by almost 20% for 40Ca and 48Ca
- Deuteron structure radius from related work cited as 1.9729 (+0.0015/-0.0012) fm
- N4LO+ NN potentials achieve chi^2/datum = 1.01 for neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data below Elab = 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the LENPIC collaboration's previous N2LO study (Maris et al., 2020) which found systematic overbinding of p-shell nuclei starting around A=10. By incorporating higher-order NN interactions while keeping the 3NF at N2LO, the authors demonstrate that the overbinding issue originates from deficiencies in the NN force rather than missing higher-order 3NF contributions. The results enable future work on consistent N3LO three-nucleon forces and improved charge radius predictions, with ongoing efforts toward developing consistent many-body forces and exchange current operators beyond N2LO as noted in Epelbaum et al. (2022).
