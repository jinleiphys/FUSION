# What Do We Learn from Atomic Physics about Fundamental Symmetries?

> Valuable information on interactions violating $P$- and $T$-invariance can be extracted from atomic experiments. The hypothesis of a large weak matrix element between single-particle states in heavy nuclei, $\sim 100$ eV, is ruled out by measurements of parity nonconservation in atoms. Experimental upper limit on the electric dipole moment of the $^{199}$Hg atom strictly constrains parameters of $CP$-violation models. Upper limit on the $T$-odd, $P$-even admixture to nuclear forces is set: $α_T\,<\,10^{-11}$.

## Key claim

Atomic experiments provide stringent constraints on fundamental symmetry violations, ruling out the hypothesis of a large weak matrix element (∼100 eV) between single-particle states in heavy nuclei and setting an upper limit on the T-odd, P-even admixture to nuclear forces at α_T < 10^{-11}, though the latter bound is derived indirectly through radiative corrections rather than direct measurement.

## Method

The paper analyzes parity nonconserving (PNC) optical activity measurements in atomic lead vapor to extract an upper limit on the weak mixing matrix element in ^{207}Pb, using the anapole moment formalism and the Landau-Migdal effective potential approach. For CP-violation, the author combines the experimental upper limit on the electric dipole moment (EDM) of ^{199}Hg with nuclear structure calculations of the Schiff moment and atomic Hartree-Fock calculations to constrain the CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction constant ξ_{np}. For T-odd, P-even (TOPE) interactions, the paper calculates two-loop radiative corrections that transform TOPE fermion-fermion interactions into T- and P-odd operators, then uses experimental EDM limits to derive improved bounds.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on the nuclear-spin-dependent part of PNC optical activity in lead: P_{NSD}/P < 0.02 at 95% confidence level
- Upper limit on the anapole constant for ^{207}Pb: κ(^{207}Pb) < 1
- Upper limit on the effective neutron PNC constant: g_n < 10
- Upper limit on the weak mixing matrix element in ^{207}Pb: ⟨3d 5/2^+|W| 2f 5/2^-⟩ < 14 eV
- Upper limit on the ^{199}Hg atomic EDM: d(^{199}Hg)/e < 9 × 10^{-28} cm
- Upper limit on the CP-odd nuclear interaction constant: ξ_{np} < 1.7 × 10^{-3}
- Upper limit on the effective CP-odd neutral pion constant: \bar{g}_{πNN}^0 < 2 × 10^{-11}
- Upper limit on the quark chromoelectric dipole moment: d^c < 2.4 × 10^{-26} cm
- Upper limit on the electron EDM: d_e/e < 10^{-26} cm
- Upper limit on the neutron EDM: d(n)/e < 10^{-25} cm
- Upper limit on the TOPE electron-electron interaction constant: β_{ee} < 10^{-7}
- Upper limit on the TOPE quark-electron interaction constant (derivative in quark vertex): β_{qe} < 10^{-6}
- Upper limit on the TOPE electron-quark interaction constant (derivative in electron vertex): β_{eq} < 3 × 10^{-8}
- Upper limit on the TOPE quark-quark interaction constants: β_{qq} < 3 × 10^{-7}

## Context

This work extends the program of using atomic physics to probe fundamental symmetries, building on earlier theoretical frameworks by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984) for the anapole moment, and by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1986) for nuclear CP-odd moments. The paper contradicts the hypothesis of a large weak matrix element (∼100 eV) proposed by Bowman, Garvey, and collaborators (1990s) to explain common-sign asymmetries in neutron scattering from ^{232}Th. The derived limits on TOPE interactions improve by many orders of magnitude over direct experimental bounds from Commins (1994) and others, and the constraints on the spontaneous CP-violation Higgs model are stronger than those from neutron EDM experiments alone. The results enable future experiments such as the proposed PNC asymmetry measurement in the M4 γ-transition in ^{207}Pb (expected sensitivity 5-13 eV) to further test these symmetry-violating interactions.
