# Heavy Quark Energy Loss in High Multiplicity Proton Proton Collisions at LHC

> One of the most promising probes to study deconfined matter created in high energy nuclear collisions is the energy loss of (heavy) quarks. It has been shown in experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) that even charm and bottom quarks, despite their high mass, experience a remarkable medium suppression in the Quark Gluon Plasma. In this exploratory investigation we study the energy loss of heavy quarks in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. Although the colliding systems are smaller than compared to those at RHIC (p+p vs. Au+Au) the higher energy might lead to multiplicities comparable to Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC. Recently the CMS collaboration has shown that these particles likely interact among each other. The interaction of charm quarks with this environment gives rise to a non-negligible suppression of high momentum heavy quarks in elementary collisions.

## Key claim

In high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, charm quarks may experience a non-negligible suppression of about 10% at high transverse momentum if a deconfined medium is formed, but this prediction should be considered with caution because the heavy quarks undergo a transient regime before losing energy proportional to path-length, an effect not implemented in the simulations.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS model with a hydrodynamical phase to simulate proton-proton collisions and generate an averaged energy density profile of the medium. Heavy quarks were then propagated through this medium according to the Boltzmann equation using the MC@sHQ model, which includes both collisional and radiative energy loss with a running coupling constant and infrared regulator matched to hard-thermal-loop calculations. The ratio of the medium-modified spectra to vacuum spectra (where reinteraction is neglected) was computed to quantify the quenching, and a new observable R_HM/LM was defined as the ratio of high-multiplicity to low-multiplicity spectra scaled by their respective multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- For collisions with ν=5 elementary scatterings, the average charged particle multiplicity at mid-rapidity dN_ch/dy|_(y=0) is 16.05; for ν=10 it equals 29.
- For low-multiplicity collisions with ν=1, dN_ch/dy|_(y=0) = 3.62.
- The deconfined phase region is roughly 2-3 fm in diameter, defined for energy densities above 1.5 GeV/fm^3, with a mixed phase between 1.5 and 0.4 GeV/fm^3.
- A 10% suppression of charm quark spectra is predicted at large p_T (p_T > 7 GeV) in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions.
- For p_T > 10 GeV, the R(z) observable shows a decrease of roughly 10-15% with increasing centrality/multiplicity.

## Context

This exploratory study extends the heavy quark energy loss framework previously applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Gossiaux 2008, 2009, 2010) to the new regime of high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. It builds on the CMS collaboration's experimental indication (Khachatryan 2010) that particles in high-multiplicity p+p collisions likely interact among themselves, and on prior work showing that Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC produce a partially deconfined state (Alver 2006, Lu 2008, Abelev 2008, 2009, 2010). The results suggest that heavy quarks can serve as a probe to detect deconfined matter formation in elementary collisions, and the proposed R_HM/LM and R(z) observables enable experimental tests at LHC experiments.
