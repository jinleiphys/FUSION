# Nuclear response functions with finite range Gogny force: tensor terms and instabilities

> A fully-antisymmetrized random phase approximation calculation employing the continued fraction technique is performed to study nuclear matter response functions with the finite range Gogny force. The most commonly used parameter sets of this force, as well as some recent generalizations that include the tensor terms are considered and the corresponding response functions are shown. The calculations are performed at the first and second order in the continued fraction expansion and the explicit expressions for the second order tensor contributions are given. Comparison between first and second order continued fraction expansion results are provided. The differences between the responses obtained at the two orders turn to be more pronounced for the forces including tensor terms than for the standard Gogny ones. In the vector channels the responses calculated with Gogny forces including tensor terms are characterized by a large heterogeneity, reflecting the different choices for the tensor part of the interaction. For sake of comparison the response functions obtained considering a G-matrix based nuclear interaction are also shown. As first application of the present calculation, the possible existence of spurious finite-size instabilities of the Gogny forces with or without tensor terms has been investigated. The positive conclusion is that all the Gogny forces, but the GT2 one, are free of spurious finite-size instabilities. In perspective, the tool developed in the present paper can be inserted in the fitting procedure to construct new Gogny-type forces.

# Nuclear response functions with finite range Gogny force: tensor terms and instabilities

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that all commonly used Gogny force parametrizations (D1, D1S, D1N, D1M) and most tensor-extended versions (D1ST, D1ST2a, D1ST2b, D1ST2c, D1MT, D1MT2c) are free of spurious finite-size instabilities in nuclear matter, with the sole exception of the GT2 force. The convergence of the continued fraction expansion is slower for tensor-including forces than for standard Gogny forces, and the vector-channel responses show large heterogeneity reflecting different choices for the tensor part.

## Method

The authors performed fully-antisymmetrized random phase approximation (RPA) calculations of nuclear matter response functions using the continued fraction (CF) technique up to second order, applied to finite-range Gogny forces. They computed the polarization propagator in the CF expansion at first and second order, providing explicit expressions for the second-order tensor contributions, and evaluated the response functions for standard Gogny parametrizations (D1, D1S, D1N, D1M) and tensor-extended versions (GT2, D1ST, D1ST2a, D1ST2b, D1ST2c, D1MT, D1MT2c) at momentum transfers q = 27 MeV/c and q = 270 MeV/c at Fermi momentum k_F = 270 MeV/c. The critical densities for finite-size instabilities were extracted from the zero-energy response functions as a function of transferred momentum.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used throughout: k_F = 270 MeV/c
- Momentum transfers studied: q = 27 MeV/c and q = 270 MeV/c
- For D1N in the (0,1) channel, the critical density reaches approximately ρ_c ≈ 1.2 ρ_0 (where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3})
- For D1S and D1M in the (0,1) channel, critical densities reach approximately ρ_c ≈ 1.5 ρ_0
- For D1N at q = 0, the lowest density instability appears in the (1,1) channel at ρ_c ≈ 2.5 ρ_0
- For D1S at q = 0, the lowest density instability appears in the (0,1) channel at ρ_c ≈ 3.5 ρ_0
- In the D1 case, the critical density in the (0,1) channel is never lower than 2 ρ_0
- For D1S, D1N and D1M, critical densities in the (1,1) channel are never lower than 2 ρ_0 even at large q

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear matter response functions from Skyrme forces, where tensor terms and instabilities have been extensively investigated (Lesinski 2006, Pastore 2012, Pastore 2014), to finite-range Gogny forces, which had received less attention due to the analytical complexity of exchange terms. The CF technique had previously been applied to Gogny forces only for the D1 parametrization (Margueron 2008), and tensor-extended Gogny forces had only been studied in the Landau-Migdal approximation (Pastore 2013) or for spin susceptibilities (Navarro 2013). The present work provides the first systematic study of finite-size instabilities for Gogny forces at finite momentum transfer, and the tool developed can be incorporated into fitting procedures for constructing new Gogny-type forces. The results for the (1,1,1) channel show agreement with G-matrix calculations, opening the perspective of using these Gogny-type forces for neutrino-nucleus cross section calculations and neutrino mean-free path in nuclear matter.
