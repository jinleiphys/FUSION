# An approach to chemical freeze-out scenario of identified particle spectra at 200AGeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC

> Thermal model fit indicates early chemical freeze-out of multi-strange hadrons with small collective velocities at 200AGeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC. In this work, we present our recent results by SPheRIO hydrodynamical calculations inspired by this picture. In our model, multi-strange hadrons go through chemical freeze-out when the system reaches some temperature close to the phase transition, stopping to make inelastic collisions, and their abundances are therefore determined only by partonic EOS. At a lower temperature thermal freeze-out takes place where elastic collisions are brought to a halt. We calculate the spectra for various hadrons at different centrality windows, with chemical and thermal freeze-out temperature being fit as a function of centrality. As it is shown, the result provides a reasonable panoramic description of the spectra of identified particles. Chemical freeze-out gives good correction of the multiplicity of certain species of particles, especially for multi-strange hadrons.

# An approach to chemical freeze-out scenario of identified particle spectra at 200AGeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

The authors show that a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic model (SPheRIO) with a two-stage freeze-out scenario, where multi-strange hadrons undergo chemical freeze-out at a temperature close to the phase transition, followed by a lower-temperature thermal freeze-out for all hadrons, can reproduce the transverse-momentum spectra of identified particles across multiple centrality windows, but only if the chemical and thermal freeze-out temperatures are treated as adjustable parameters tuned to experimental data for each centrality.

## Method

The authors used the SPheRIO smoothed-particle hydrodynamic code with initial conditions generated by the NEXUS event simulator. They implemented strangeness conservation by incorporating a strangeness chemical potential and built a new equation of state combining a hadronic resonance model (with finite volume correction, including resonances from the Particle Data Tables) and an ideal gas model for the quark-gluon plasma phase. Chemical freeze-out was applied only to strange hadrons (Λ, Ξ, Ω, φ) and anti-protons, assuming complete chemical equilibrium with no strangeness saturation factor γ_S; thermal freeze-out at a lower temperature was then applied to all hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Thermal freeze-out temperature ranges from T_th = 135 MeV for most central collisions to T_th = 150 MeV for most peripheral collisions, determined by fitting the shape of the transverse-momentum distribution of all charged particles.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch is around 160 MeV for 200AGeV Au-Au collisions, close to the phase boundary, as cited from prior thermal model fits.
- The pseudo-rapidity distribution for all charged particles was computed for six different centrality windows and compared with PHOBOS Collaboration data.
- Transverse-momentum spectra were calculated for pions, protons, kaons, Λ, Ξ, and Ω at mid-rapidity and compared with BRAHMS and STAR Collaboration data.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic calculations that neglected strangeness or used simplified freeze-out treatments, such as those by the same collaboration (Qian et al., 2007) which showed that hydrodynamic models without chemical freeze-out reproduce light hadron spectra but fail on hyperon and anti-proton multiplicities. It contrasts with hybrid models (e.g., those coupling hydrodynamics to hadronic cascades) where the chemical freeze-out temperature is not studied explicitly, and with other hydrodynamic calculations that assumed Bjorken scaling or zero baryon chemical potential and covered only some centrality windows. The results enable systematic extraction of centrality-dependent freeze-out temperatures from full 3D hydrodynamic simulations, and the authors note that further work at large rapidity (where baryon density differs from zero) is in progress.
