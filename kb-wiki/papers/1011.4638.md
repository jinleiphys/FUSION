# Generalisation of Gunion-Bertsch Formula for Soft Gluon Emission

> We generalise the most extensively used Gunion-Bertsch formula for the soft gluon emission in a perturbative QCD, which could be very important for the phenomenology of the hot and dense matter produced in the heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalised Gunion-Bertsch formula for soft gluon emission from gluon-gluon scattering ($gg\rightarrow ggg$) that includes correction terms up to ${\cal O}(t^3/s^3)$, which the authors claim are more important than a previously published correction by Das et al. (2010). The generalisation reduces to the original Gunion-Bertsch formula only in the limit of very large centre-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}$.

## Method

The authors start from the full invariant amplitude for $gg\rightarrow ggg$ (25 topologies, reduced to 12 symmetric terms) expressed in terms of Mandelstam variables. They take the soft gluon limit ($k_5\rightarrow 0$) where $t'\rightarrow t$, $s'\rightarrow s$, $u'\rightarrow u$, and expand the matrix element in powers of $t/s$ up to ${\cal O}(t^3/s^3)$, factorising it into a $2\rightarrow 2$ matrix element times a generalised infrared factor. The resulting gluon multiplicity distribution is then obtained by regulating the infrared divergence with the Debye screening mass $m_D$, following the procedure of Gunion and Bertsch (1982) and Wong (1996).

## Key numbers

- The correction up to ${\cal O}(t^3/s^3)$ is around **50%** over the usual GB formula at $\alpha_s=0.3$ (Fig. 2).
- The correction terms are significant when $\sqrt{s} \le 6$ GeV (Fig. 1).
- For the temperature range $1\le T/T_C\le 5$, the correction is $\ge 40\%$ over the GB formula and $\ge 30\%$ compared to Das et al. (2010), using temperature-dependent $\alpha_s$ with momentum scale $Q=4\pi T$ (Fig. 3).
- The lower limit of $\sqrt{s}\sim 0.8$ GeV is restricted by the relation $s\ge 4m_D^2$ at $T=200$ MeV and $\alpha_s=0.3$.

## Context

This work extends the original Gunion-Bertsch formula (1982) for soft gluon emission from quark-quark scattering, which was later applied to gluon-gluon scattering by Biro et al. (1993) and Wong (1996). It corrects and improves upon a recent correction by Das et al. (2010), which the authors show misses the leading ${\cal O}(t/s)$ term and has an incorrect coefficient at ${\cal O}(t^2/s^2)$. The generalised formula is intended for use in heavy-ion phenomenology including hot glue scenarios, chemical equilibration of gluons, partonic matter viscosity, and radiative energy-loss of energetic partons.
