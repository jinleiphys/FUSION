# Rotational properties of $^{252, 253, 254}$No. Influence of pairing correlations

> Rotational bands of $^{252, 253, 254}$No and the fission barriers of $^{254}$No at spin 0$\hbar$ and 20$\hbar$ are calculated with the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov theory and the Lipkin-Nogami approximate particle number projection. The SLy4 Skyrme force is used in the particle-hole channel. A zero-range force with and without density-dependence is used in the particle-particle channel. The experimental ground state deformation (${\cal{Q}}_{20}$ = 32.8 b) is reproduced as well as the increase of the dynamical moment of inertia with frequency both for $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No. The rotational band of $^{253}$No is also calculated. Fission barriers of $^{254}$No at spin 0$\hbar$ and 20$\hbar$ show the robustness of shell-corrections against rotation in these heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov theory with the Lipkin-Nogami approximate particle number projection, using the SLy4 Skyrme force and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force, can reproduce the experimental rotational properties of Nobelium isotopes, but the agreement for the dynamical moment of inertia depends more on whether the pairing is active in the surface or volume region than on the precise strength of the pairing force.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations with the Lipkin-Nogami approximate particle number projection (HFBLN) in the coordinate representation. The SLy4 Skyrme force was used in the particle-hole channel, and two types of zero-range pairing forces were tested in the particle-particle channel: a density-independent volume pairing and a density-dependent surface-peaked pairing. Rotational bands were calculated for 252No, 253No, and 254No, and fission barriers were computed for 254No at spins 0 hbar and 20 hbar with an additional constraint on the quadrupole moment.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ground state quadrupole deformation of 254No: Q20 = 32.8 b, corresponding to beta2 = 0.27 +/- 0.02
- For the surface pairing, the neutron and proton strengths were V0,n(surf) = -1250 MeV.fm3 and V0,p(surf) = -1250 MeV.fm3, with saturation density rho_c = 0.16 fm-3
- For the volume pairing, the strengths were V0,n(vol) = -250 MeV.fm3 and V0,p(vol) = -290 MeV.fm3
- The first fission barrier of 254No at spin 0 hbar is 12.6 MeV above the first minimum when constrained to axial deformation, lowered by 3 MeV when allowing triaxiality (gamma reaches 10 degrees at the barrier)
- At spin 20 hbar, the first fission barrier height is 11.1 MeV
- The second minimum in the fission barrier of 254No at spin 0 hbar is at Q20 = 106 b with an excitation energy of 2.4 MeV
- The calculated beta2 for 254No is 0.264 after corrections for beta4
- The signature splitting of the quasi-proton [633]7/2+ orbitals is 0.2 MeV at hbar omega = 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends self-consistent cranked HFBLN calculations, previously applied to super-deformed bands in the A approximately 150 region by Rigollet (1999) and the A approximately 190 region by Terasaki (1995) and Fallon (1995), to the heavy Nobelium isotopes just below the super-heavy element region. The surface pairing force was originally fitted on the low-spin behavior of moments of inertia in the A approximately 150 super-deformed bands by Rigollet (1999). The results enable predictions for the rotational band of 253No, for which experiments were planned, and confirm the robustness of shell corrections against rotation in heavy nuclei, supporting the observation of the rotational band of 254No up to spin 22 hbar by Reiter (1999) and Leino (1999). The calculations also confirm the existence of a deformed shell closure at N = 162 found in previous microscopic calculations by Cwiok (1996) and macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Patyk (1990).
