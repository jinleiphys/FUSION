# Role of net baryon density on rapidity width of identified particles from the lowest energies available at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron to those at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Widths of the rapidity distributions of various identified hadrons generated with the UrQMD-3.4 event generator at all the Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) energies have been presented and compared with the existing experimental results. An increase in the width of the rapidity distribution of $Λ$ could be seen with both Monte Carlo (MC) and experimental data for the studied energies. Using MC data, the study has been extended to Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. A similar jump, as observed in the plot of rapidity width versus rest mass at Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS) and all SPS energies, persists even at RHIC and LHC energies, confirming its universal nature from AGS to the highest LHC energies. Such observation indicates that pair production may not be the only mechanism of particle production at the highest LHC energies. However, with MC data, the separate mass scaling for mesons and baryons is found to exist even at the top LHC energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the jump in rapidity width of Lambda baryons relative to other hadrons, previously observed at AGS and low SPS energies, persists at all SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies in UrQMD-3.4 model calculations, suggesting that pair production may not be the only mechanism of particle production even at the highest LHC energies. The hedge is that this conclusion is drawn from Monte Carlo data at RHIC and LHC, as no experimental rapidity distributions of identified particles are available at those energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD-3.4 event generator to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at SPS beam energies of 20, 30, 40, 80, and 158 A GeV, and Au-Au/Pb-Pb collisions at RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 62.4, 130, 200 GeV) and LHC energies (2.76 and 5.02 TeV). Rapidity distributions of pi-, K-, phi, Lambda, Xi-, and Omega- were fitted with a double Gaussian function to extract the width (r.m.s.) values. At RHIC and LHC, weak decays were switched off to match experimental pseudorapidity distributions of charged particles from PHOBOS and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energy 20 A GeV, UrQMD-3.4 rapidity width of Lambda is 0.806 +/- 0.0024 (experimental: 0.70 +/- 0.01); at 158 A GeV, UrQMD width is 1.327 +/- 0.0065 (experimental: 1.97 +/- 0.35)
- At RHIC energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, UrQMD rapidity width of Lambda is 2.866 +/- 0.0380; at LHC energy 2.76 TeV, width is 3.807 +/- 0.0094; at 5.02 TeV, width is 4.247 +/- 0.0313
- At LHC energy 2.76 TeV, UrQMD rapidity width of pi- is 3.603 +/- 0.0010; of K- is 3.399 +/- 0.0036; of phi is 3.240 +/- 0.02010; of Xi- is 3.320 +/- 0.0340; of Omega- is 3.053 +/- 0.0340
- At LHC energy 5.02 TeV, UrQMD rapidity width of pi- is 4.026 +/- 0.0072; of K- is 3.735 +/- 0.0100; of phi is 3.460 +/- 0.0480; of Xi- is 3.509 +/- 0.0456; of Omega- is 3.241 +/- 0.0878

## Context

This work extends the study of rapidity width mass scaling from AGS and low SPS energies (Deysir, 2014) to the full SPS range and to RHIC and LHC energies using the UrQMD model. The finding that Lambda baryons show an enhanced rapidity width compared to other hadrons, and that this width follows the net baryon density distribution, contradicts the expectation that pair production dominates at high energies. The results enable further investigation of particle production mechanisms at LHC energies and suggest that associated strangeness production remains relevant even at the highest collision energies.
