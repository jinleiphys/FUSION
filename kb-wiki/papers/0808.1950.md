# Density, short-range order and the quark-gluon plasma

> We study the thermal part of the energy density spatial correlator in the quark-gluon plasma. We describe its qualitative form at high temperatures. We then calculate it out to distances approx. 1.5/T in SU(3) gauge theory lattice simulations for the range of temperatures 0.9<= T/T_c<= 2.2. The vacuum-subtracted correlator exhibits non-monotonic behavior, and is almost conformal by 2T_c. Its broad maximum at r approx. 0.6/T suggests a dense medium with only weak short-range order, similar to a non-relativistic fluid near the liquid-gas phase transition, where eta/s is minimal.

## Key claim

The vacuum-subtracted energy density spatial correlator in the SU(3) gluon plasma exhibits a single broad maximum at r ≈ 0.6/T, suggesting a dense medium with only weak short-range order, similar to a non-relativistic fluid near the liquid-gas phase transition where η/s is minimal; this behavior is almost conformal by 2T_c.

## Method

The thermal part of the energy density spatial correlator G_ee(T,r) was computed in SU(3) gauge theory using lattice Monte-Carlo simulations with the isotropic Wilson action and a once-HYP-smeared clover discretization of the energy density operators. The correlator was evaluated on (1/T)·L^3 lattices for temperatures 0.9 ≤ T/T_c ≤ 2.2, out to distances ≈ 1.5/T, with the zero-temperature vacuum contribution subtracted to isolate thermal effects.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 0.9 ≤ T/T_c ≤ 2.2
- Maximum of G_ee occurs at r_max ≈ 0.6/T
- At short distances, G_ee diverges as -r^(-4) near the origin
- Screening mass ratio M(T)/M_4 = 0.630(14) at 1.24 T_c (Nτ=8), 0.906(20) at 1.65 T_c (Nτ=6), and 1.276(32) at 2.20 T_c (Nτ=6)
- The temperature T* where thermal screening exceeds the D=4 glueball mass is T* = 1.790(36) T_c
- G_ee exceeds its asymptotic value e^2(T) by about 5-15% at the maximum
- The shear viscosity in the same temperature range satisfies η/s < 1.0

## Context

This work extends the study of the quark-gluon plasma as a "most perfect liquid" by introducing the energy density spatial correlator as a relativistic analogue of the radial distribution function g(r) used in non-relativistic fluids. The author notes that the radial distribution function of monopoles computed in SU(2) gauge theory by D'Alessandro (2007) has a similar shape to G_ee, and that color charge and monopole-antimonopole correlators in models by Gelman (2006) also look alike. The results enable future calculations of full spatial correlations of the energy-momentum tensor in SU(3) gauge theory and full QCD, and suggest a benchmark comparison with strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory where η/s is known to be small (Policastro, 2001; Kovtun, 2006).
