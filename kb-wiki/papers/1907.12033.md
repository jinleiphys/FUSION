# Energy loss of heavy quarks in the isotropic collisional hot QCD medium

> Collisional energy loss of heavy partons (charm and bottom quarks) has been determined within the framework of semi-classical transport theory implying Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel. Hot QCD medium effects have been incorporated while employing a quasi-particle description of the medium in terms of effective gluons, quarks and antiquarks with respective temperature dependent effective fugacities. The momentum dependence of the energy loss for charm and bottom quark has been investigated. It is observed that with the increase in momentum of the heavy quarks, the loss increases sharply for the smaller values and reaches a saturation later. Further, as compared to the charm quark, bottom quark losses less energy at a particular momentum and collisional frequency. The energy loss is seen to increase with increasing collisional frequency. We also provide a comparative study of the results obtained using BGK-kernel than those using relaxation time approximation (RTA)-kernel and found them consistent with each other. The medium effects in all the situations are seen to play quite significant role.

## Key claim

Collisional energy loss of charm and bottom quarks in an isotropic hot QCD medium is computed using a BGK collisional kernel within semi-classical transport theory, and the results are found to be consistent with those obtained using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) kernel, though the BGK kernel yields slightly lower energy loss at the same momentum and collisional frequency.

## Method

The energy loss expression was derived from Wong's equations and the induced chromo-electric field, expressed in terms of the longitudinal and transverse dielectric permittivity of the medium. The gluon selfenergy and dielectric permittivity were obtained from the Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equation with a BGK collisional kernel in the Abelian (high-temperature) limit, using effective quasi-particle distribution functions (EQPM) with temperature-dependent fugacities to incorporate hot QCD medium effects. The resulting energy loss was computed numerically for charm and bottom quarks as a function of momentum at temperature T = 2 T_c, for different collisional frequencies and equations of state (ideal LO, (2+1) lattice EoS, and 3-loop HTL EoS).

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: 1.8 GeV; bottom quark mass: 4.5 GeV.
- Temperature: T = 2 T_c, with T_c = 0.17 GeV.
- Collisional frequencies studied: nu = 0.0, 0.1 m_D, and 0.3 m_D.
- Energy loss increases sharply at low momentum and saturates at higher momentum for both charm and bottom quarks.
- At fixed momentum and collisional frequency, charm quark loses more energy than bottom quark.
- Energy loss increases with increasing collisional frequency.
- Non-ideal EoSs (LB and HTL_pt) suppress energy loss compared to the ideal (LO) case at fixed collisional frequency.
- At nu = 0.1 m_D and same momentum, the RTA kernel gives slightly larger energy loss than the BGK kernel.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quark collisional energy loss in QGP using transport theory, specifically the RTA-based approach of Elias et al. (2014) and the BGK-kernel study of Han et al. (2017), by incorporating hot QCD medium effects via the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) developed by Chandra and collaborators. The results enable future investigations of momentum anisotropy effects and viscous corrections, as well as the calculation of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for comparison with experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
