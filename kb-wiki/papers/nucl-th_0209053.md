# S=--1 Meson-Baryon Scattering in Coupled Channel Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The $s-$wave meson-baryon scattering amplitude is analyzed for the strangeness $S=-1$ and isospin I=0 sector in a Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel formalism incorporating Chiral Symmetry. Four two-body channels have been considered: $\bar K N$, $πΣ$, $ηΛ$, $ K Ξ$. The needed two particle irreducible matrix amplitude is taken from lowest order Chiral Perturbation Theory in a relativistic formalism. Off-shell behaviour is parameterized in terms of low energy constants, which outnumber those assumed in previous works and provide a better fit to the data. The position of the complex poles in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitude determine masses and widths of the $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1670)$ resonances which compare well with accepted numbers.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel formalism with lowest-order chiral perturbation theory as the kernel, and with off-shell behavior parameterized by more low-energy constants than in previous works, provides a better fit to scattering data and yields pole positions for the Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) resonances that compare well with accepted values, though the extracted branching ratios for the Λ(1670) differ significantly from PDG values.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for s-wave meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=-1, isospin I=0 sector, coupling four channels (K̅N, πΣ, ηΛ, KΞ). The two-particle irreducible kernel is taken from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian in a relativistic formalism, and off-shell effects are reparameterized as low-energy constants. A χ² fit with 12 free parameters is performed to three sets of experimental data: the real and imaginary parts of K̅N→K̅N and K̅N→πΣ amplitudes from 1480 to 1750 MeV, the πΣ mass spectrum from 1330 to 1440 MeV, and the K⁻p→ηΛ total cross section from 1662 to 1684 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Three poles found in the second Riemann sheet: first pole mass M_R = 1368 ± 12 MeV with width Γ_R = 250 ± 20 MeV; second pole mass M_R = 1443 ± 3 MeV with width Γ_R = 50 ± 7 MeV; third pole (Λ(1670)) mass M_R = 1677.5 ± 0.8 MeV with width Γ_R = 29.2 ± 1.4 MeV.
* For the Λ(1670) resonance, extracted branching ratios: B(K̅N) = 0.24, B(πΣ) = 0.08, B(ηΛ) = 0.68, compared to PDG values of B(K̅N) = 0.20 ± 0.05, B(πΣ) = 0.40 ± 0.20, B(ηΛ) = 0.25 ± 0.10.
* Fit uses 56 data points each for K̅N→K̅N and K̅N→πΣ amplitudes, 12 independent data points from the πΣ mass spectrum, and 17 data points from the K⁻p→ηΛ cross section.
* Input masses (in MeV): m_K = 493.68, m_π = 139.57, m_η = 547.3, M_p = 938.27, M_Σ = 1189.37, M_Λ = 1115.68, M_Ξ = 1318.0; averaged meson decay constant f = 1.15 × 93.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel approach of earlier studies (Oset and Ramos, 1998; Kaiser, 2001) by reparameterizing off-shell effects as low-energy constants in the spirit of an effective field theory, and by including more free parameters to improve the fit. It also builds on the observation that heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory fails completely in the K̅N channel near threshold due to the nearby Λ(1405) resonance (Kaiser, 2001). The results enable more precise determination of the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) and provide branching ratios for the Λ(1670) that can be compared with future experimental analyses.
