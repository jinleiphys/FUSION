# Bulk Viscous Damping of Density Oscillations in Neutron Star Mergers

> In this paper, we discuss the damping of density oscillations in dense nuclear matter in the temperature range relevant to neutron star mergers. This damping is due to bulk viscosity arising from the weak interaction ``Urca'' processes of neutron decay and electron capture. The nuclear matter is modelled in the relativistic density functional approach. The bulk viscosity reaches a resonant maximum close to the neutrino trapping temperature, then drops rapidly as temperature rises into the range where neutrinos are trapped in neutron stars. We investigate the bulk viscous dissipation timescales in a post-merger object and identify regimes where these timescales are as short as the characteristic timescale $\sim$10 ms, and, therefore, might affect the evolution of the post-merger object. Our analysis indicates that bulk viscous damping would be important at not too high temperatures of the order of a few MeV and densities up to a few times saturation density.

## Key claim
The paper shows that bulk viscous damping from weak-interaction Urca processes can suppress density oscillations in neutron star merger remnants on timescales as short as ~10 ms, but only in a specific window: temperatures of a few MeV and densities up to a few times saturation density. The damping is strongest near the neutrino trapping temperature, where the bulk viscosity reaches a resonant maximum, and becomes ineffective at higher temperatures where neutrinos are trapped.

## Method
The authors model dense nuclear matter using a relativistic density functional approach and compute the bulk viscosity arising from the weak-interaction Urca processes (neutron decay and electron capture). They then calculate the bulk viscous dissipation timescales for density oscillations in a post-merger object, scanning over temperature and density to identify regimes where the damping timescale becomes comparable to the characteristic dynamical timescale of the merger.

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscous dissipation timescales can be as short as ~10 ms in the post-merger object, comparable to the characteristic dynamical timescale.
- The damping is important at temperatures of the order of a few MeV and densities up to a few times saturation density.
- The bulk viscosity reaches a resonant maximum close to the neutrino trapping temperature, then drops rapidly as temperature rises into the neutrino-trapping regime.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of bulk viscosity in neutron star matter (e.g., by Alford, Schmitt, and collaborators) by focusing specifically on the temperature and density conditions relevant to neutron star mergers rather than isolated neutron stars. The results enable more realistic simulations of post-merger evolution by identifying when bulk viscous damping must be included, and when it can be neglected. The paper opens the question of whether this damping mechanism could affect gravitational wave signals from mergers or the stability of the remnant.
