# Lattice simulations of the QCD chiral transition at real $μ_B$

> Most lattice studies of hot and dense QCD matter rely on extrapolation from zero or imaginary chemical potentials. The ill-posedness of numerical analytic continuation puts severe limitations on the reliability of such methods. We studied the QCD chiral transition at finite real baryon density with the more direct sign reweighting approach. We simulate up to a baryochemical potential-temperature ratio of $μ_B/T=2.7$, covering the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range, and penetrating the region where methods based on analytic continuation are unpredictive.This opens up a new window to study QCD matter at finite $μ_B$ from first principles.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that sign reweighting can directly simulate QCD at real baryochemical potential up to μ_B/T = 2.7, penetrating the region where methods based on analytic continuation from zero or imaginary chemical potentials become unpredictive, while noting that the method is ultimately bottlenecked by the sign problem.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using a tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action with 2stout improved staggered fermions on 16³ × 6 lattices. They used sign reweighting, where the simulated ensemble has weights proportional to the absolute value of the real part of the fermion determinant, and the reweighting factors are signs (±1). They scanned in chemical potential at fixed temperature T = 140 MeV and in temperature at fixed μ_B/T = 1.5, computing the renormalized light quark chiral condensate and the light quark number density via numerical differentiation of the determinant using the reduced matrix formalism.

## Key numbers
- Simulations reached up to μ_B/T = 2.7, covering the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range.
- At large chemical potential or volume, sign reweighting requires approximately (π/2)² ≈ 2.5 times less statistics than phase reweighting.
- At T = 140 MeV, the renormalized chiral condensate crosses its μ_B = 0 crossover value at μ_B/T ≈ 2.2.
- A temperature rescaling T → T(1 + κ(μ_B/T)²) with κ ≈ 0.012 for the chiral condensate and κ ≈ 0.016 for the quark number-to-chemical potential ratio collapses data at μ_B/T = 1.5 onto the μ_B = 0 curves.
- At μ_B/T > 2, sign reweighting errors are an order of magnitude smaller than those from analytic continuation.

## Context
This work extends the sign reweighting approach first noted by deForcrand (2002) and earlier reweighting studies by Hasenfratz (1991), Fodor (2001, 2004), and Giordano (2019) that were restricted to coarse N_τ = 4 lattices. It directly addresses the ill-posedness of numerical analytic continuation used in Taylor expansion methods (Bellwied 2015, Bazavov 2017) and imaginary chemical potential methods (HotQCD 2018, Bonati 2018, Borsanyi 2020). The results enable first-principles continuum-extrapolated finite μ_B calculations within the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range for phenomenologically relevant aspect ratios LT ≈ 3.
