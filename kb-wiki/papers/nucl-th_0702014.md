# Break-up stage restoration in multifragmentation reactions

> In the case of Xe+Sn at 32 MeV/nucleon multifragmentation reaction break-up fragments are built-up from the experimentally detected ones using evaluations of light particle evaporation multiplicities which thus settle fragment internal excitation. Freeze-out characteristics are extracted from experimental kinetic energy spectra under the assumption of full decoupling between fragment formation and energy dissipated in different degrees of freedom. Thermal kinetic energy is determined uniquely while for freeze-out volume - collective energy a multiple solution is obtained. Coherence between the solutions of the break-up restoration algorithm and the predictions of a multifragmentation model with identical definition of primary fragments is regarded as a way to select the true value. The broad kinetic energy spectrum of $^3$He is consistent with break-up genesis of this isotope.

# Break-up stage restoration in multifragmentation reactions

## Key claim

The break-up stage of the Xe+Sn reaction at 32 MeV/nucleon can be partially restored from experimental data, but while the thermal kinetic energy is uniquely determined, the freeze-out volume and collective energy yield multiple solutions; coherence with a microcanonical multifragmentation model (MMM) using identical fragment definitions is proposed as a way to select the true physical value, though the result depends on the assumed limiting temperature parameterization.

## Method

The authors reconstructed break-up fragment partitions event-by-event from experimental INDRA detector data for central Xe+Sn collisions at 32 MeV/nucleon, using experimentally evaluated average multiplicities of evaporated light charged particles to assign internal excitation to primary fragments. They then simulated the Coulomb propagation and sequential evaporation of these fragments, tuning the freeze-out volume, thermal kinetic energy, and collective flow parameters to match experimental asymptotic kinetic energy spectra. Finally, they compared the reconstructed break-up stage with predictions of the microcanonical multifragmentation model MMM for the same source (A=198, Z=83) under different assumptions about fragment limiting temperature.

## Key numbers

- Source characterized by average mass <A>=198 and average charge <Z>=83, with cross section for selected isotropic central collisions estimated at 115 ± 20 mb.
- Average excitation energy per mass unit of primary fragments reproducing experimental charge multiplicity within 10% is 2.5 MeV/nucleon, in agreement with the value 2.2 MeV/nucleon proposed by Hudan et al.
- Average total excitation energy at break-up is 396.9 MeV and Q-value equals 126.7 MeV.
- Average total multiplicity at break-up is 30.5 (asymptotic 53.8), charged product multiplicity 13.8 (asymptotic 23.2; experimental 20.3), and fragment multiplicity (Z ≥ 5) 4.2 (asymptotic 4.0; experimental 4.2).
- Thermal kinetic energy uniquely determined as K_th = 396 MeV, corresponding to a kinetic temperature of 8.65 MeV.
- Three representative freeze-out volume/collective energy solutions: (i) V=4V_0, E_flow=0.68 MeV/nucleon, a_flow=1.3; (ii) V=6V_0, E_flow=0.9 MeV/nucleon, a_flow=1.2; (iii) V=12V_0, E_flow=1.24 MeV/nucleon, a_flow=1.2.
- Total available energy of the source is 7.4 MeV/nucleon for all considered situations.
- The average kinetic energy of ^3He exceeds that of tritons by 15 MeV and that of ^4He by 13 MeV.
- Acceptable agreement between MMM and reconstruction algorithm obtained for V=4V_0 with constant limiting temperature τ=6.8 MeV, yielding average excitation energy per mass unit 2.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the line opened by Piantelli et al. (2004) for estimating freeze-out properties in a model-independent way, but differs by using evaluations of evaporated particle multiplicities from Hudan et al. (2006) and treating all energetic degrees of freedom as fully decoupled. The results are compared with predictions of the MMM microcanonical multifragmentation model (Raduta, 2003) and with the Stochastic Mean Field approach (2006). The paradoxical high kinetic energy of ^3He, previously attributed to coalescence from non-equilibrium nucleons (Neubert, 1995) or an expanding source (Bougault, 1994), is here interpreted as consistent with break-up genesis. The study enables future consistency checks between statistical and dynamical model predictions to determine whether full equilibration is attained in heavy ion reactions.
