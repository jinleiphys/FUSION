# Theory of Inclusive Scattering of Polarized Electrons by Polarized $^{3}$He and the Neutron Form Factors

> The theory of inclusive lepton scattering of polarized leptons by polarized J = 1/2 hadrons is presented and the origin of different expressions for the polarized nuclear response function appearing in the literature is explained. The sensitivity of the longitudinal asymmetry upon the neutron form factors is investigated.

# Theory of Inclusive Scattering of Polarized Electrons by Polarized $^{3}$He and the Neutron Form Factors

## Key claim

The longitudinal asymmetry in quasi-elastic inclusive scattering of polarized electrons by polarized $^{3}$He can be made very sensitive to the neutron electric form factor, provided a proper kinematics is chosen (specifically, a polarization angle $\beta \approx 95^\circ$ at the quasi-elastic peak), which minimizes the otherwise sizeable proton contribution. The authors clarify that the differences between their earlier prescription (II) and the correct prescription (I) for the polarized structure functions affect only the response function $R_{TL'}$ and do not undermine the conclusion that neutron form factor information can be extracted.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive derivation of the inclusive cross section for longitudinally polarized leptons scattering from polarized J=1/2 hadrons within the one-photon-exchange approximation, obtaining the polarized structure functions $G_1^A$ and $G_2^A$ via two different extraction procedures (prescription I and prescription II). They evaluate these structure functions in the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) using a spin-dependent spectral function for $^{3}$He derived from variational wave functions with the Reid soft-core interaction, and compute the asymmetry $A = \Delta/\Sigma$ for quasi-elastic kinematics at incident electron energies of 574 MeV and scattering angles of $44^\circ$ and $51.1^\circ$.

## Key numbers

- Experimental asymmetry at the quasi-elastic peak for $\epsilon_1 = 574$ MeV, $\theta_e = 44^\circ$: $A^{exp}_{qe} = 2.41 \mp 1.29 \mp 0.51\%$ (MIT-Caltech collaboration, Woodward et al. 1990) and $A^{exp}_{qe} = 1.75 \mp 1.20 \mp 0.31\%$ (MIT-Harvard collaboration, Thompson et al. 1992); theoretical value $A^{th} = 3.74\%$ with proton contribution $A^{th}_p = 2.20\%$
- Experimental asymmetry at the quasi-elastic peak for $\epsilon_1 = 574$ MeV, $\theta_e = 51.1^\circ$: $A^{exp}_{qe} = -3.79 \mp 1.37 \mp 0.67\%$ (MIT-Caltech) and $A^{exp}_{qe} = -2.60 \mp 0.90 \mp 0.46\%$ (MIT-Harvard); theoretical value $A^{th} = -3.43\%$ with proton contribution $A^{th}_p = -1.30\%$
- The critical polarization angle that minimizes the proton contribution is $\beta_c = 95^\circ$, found to be effective over a large range of incident electron energies (500-2000 MeV)
- The Galster model parameterization uses $B = 0.71$ (GeV/c)$^2$ for the proton electric form factor dipole form

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Blankleider and Woloshyn (1984) and the authors' own previous work (Ciofi degli Atti, Pace, and Salmé, 1992) by correcting the extraction procedure for polarized structure functions in PWIA, resolving discrepancies with the approach of Schultze and Sauer (1993). The paper reconciles the different expressions for polarized nuclear response functions appearing in the literature and demonstrates that, contrary to doubts raised by Schultze and Sauer (1993), reliable information on neutron form factors can be obtained from inclusive asymmetry measurements on polarized $^{3}$He when the proton contribution is minimized by choosing $\beta \approx 95^\circ$ at the quasi-elastic peak. The authors note that calculations of final state effects are in progress.
