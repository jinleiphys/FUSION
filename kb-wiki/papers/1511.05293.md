# Inverse Magnetic/Shear Catalysis

> It is well known that very large magnetic fields are generated when the Quark-Gluon Plasma is formed during peripheral heavy-ion collisions. Lattice, holographic, and other studies strongly suggest that these fields may, for observationally relevant field values, induce "inverse magnetic catalysis", signalled by a lowering of the critical temperature for the chiral/deconfinement transition. The theoretical basis of this effect has recently attracted much attention; yet so far these investigations have not included another, equally dramatic consequence of the peripheral collision geometry: the QGP acquires a large angular momentum vector, parallel to the magnetic field. Here we use holographic techniques to argue that the angular momentum can also, independently, have an effect on transition temperatures, and we obtain a rough estimate of the relative effects of the presence of both a magnetic field and an angular momentum density. We find that the shearing angular momentum reinforces the effect of the magnetic field at low values of the baryonic chemical potential, but that it can actually decrease that effect at high chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shearing angular momentum acquired by the Quark-Gluon Plasma in peripheral heavy-ion collisions can independently affect the chiral/deconfinement transition temperature, reinforcing the effect of the magnetic field at low baryonic chemical potential but potentially decreasing that effect at high chemical potentials.

## Method

The author uses holographic gauge-gravity duality, specifically the Plebański–Demiański family of asymptotically AdS black hole solutions (the ℓdyKMV₀ metrics), to model a boundary field theory with both a magnetic field and a shearing velocity profile. The black hole parameters are mapped to physical quantities: specific angular momentum a, mean magnetic field B_m, specific entropy ς_S, and baryonic chemical potential μ_B. The Hawking temperature of the black hole is then interpreted as the QGP temperature, and the critical temperature is studied as a function of these parameters.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for central collisions (a = B_m = 0) is taken as T_c0 ≈ 155 MeV (0.75 fm⁻¹), based on lattice results from Bhat (2014).
- The maximal magnetic field for peripheral RHIC collisions is estimated at around m_π² ≈ (0.75 fm⁻¹)² ≈ 0.56 fm⁻², following Skokov (2009).
- The specific entropy ς_S is estimated at around 2 fm (about 10 per baryon) for the zero-field case.
- The maximal velocity V of the plasma is taken as approximately 1 (dimensionless velocity of either projectile), fixing K_V ≈ 0.82473 and J_V ≈ 1.22991.
- The paper uses L = 10 fm for the typical transverse size of the collision zone.

## Context

This work extends the study of inverse magnetic catalysis, previously investigated via lattice computations by Bali (2012) and holographic methods by Mamo (2015) and Rougemont (2015), to include the previously neglected effect of angular momentum. The author notes that the angular momentum vector is parallel to the magnetic field in peripheral collisions, as discussed by Becattini (2013) and Liang (2005). The holographic approach using the KMV₀ and ℓQKMV₀ metrics builds on earlier work by Klemm (1998) and the author's own prior work on shearing plasmas (McInnes 2015). The results are intended to provide a test for theories of the underlying mechanisms of inverse catalysis, particularly in the context of future experiments at non-zero baryonic chemical potential near the critical point.
