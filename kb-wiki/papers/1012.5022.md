# Numeric and symbolic evaluation of the pfaffian of general skew-symmetric matrices

> Evaluation of pfaffians arises in a number of physics applications, and for some of them a direct method is preferable to using the determinantal formula. We discuss two methods for the numerical evaluation of pfaffians. The first is tridiagonalization based on Householder transformations. The main advantage of this method is its numerical stability that makes unnecessary the implementation of a pivoting strategy. The second method considered is based on Aitken's block diagonalization formula. It yields to a kind of LU (similar to Cholesky's factorization) decomposition (under congruence) of arbitrary skew-symmetric matrices that is well suited both for the numeric and symbolic evaluations of the pfaffian. Fortran subroutines (FORTRAN 77 and 90) implementing both methods are given. We also provide simple implementations in Python and Mathematica for purpose of testing, or for exploratory studies of methods that make use of pfaffians.

# Numeric and symbolic evaluation of the pfaffian of general skew-symmetric matrices

## Key claim

The paper presents two numerically stable algorithms for computing the pfaffian of a skew-symmetric matrix that scale as N^3, comparable to standard determinant algorithms, and provides implementations in Fortran, Python, and Mathematica; however, the Householder tridiagonalization method is unsuitable for symbolic computation due to square roots and complex arguments, while the Aitken block diagonalization method requires a pivoting strategy to avoid division by small or zero matrix elements.

## Method

The first method reduces a general skew-symmetric matrix to tridiagonal form using complex Householder transformations, after which the pfaffian is simply the product of the odd-indexed off-diagonal elements. The second method uses Aitken's block diagonalization formula to recursively reduce the matrix dimension by two, expressing the pfaffian as a product of selected matrix elements; this method requires full pivoting for numerical stability and is better suited for symbolic evaluation, where zero denominators are handled by introducing a symbolic variable and taking a limit.

## Key numbers

- For matrices of dimension 100, both implementations require a few milliseconds on a modern personal computer under Linux.
- For matrices of dimension 1000, the computation time is of the order of a few seconds.
- In tests with random skew-symmetric matrices of dimension one thousand, the pfaffians computed by both methods coincide up to one part in 10^10.
- The pfaffian of a tridiagonal skew-symmetric matrix of dimension 2N is given by the product of the odd-indexed off-diagonal elements: d_1 * d_3 * ... * d_{2N-1}.

## Context

This work addresses the need for direct pfaffian evaluation in physics applications such as Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave function overlaps in nuclear structure theory (Robledo, 2009), quantum Monte Carlo methods (Bajdich, 2008), and two-dimensional Ising spin glasses (Thomas, 2009). The methods avoid the sign ambiguity of the square-root relation pf(A) = sqrt(det(A)) and improve upon the prohibitive scaling of the minor expansion method (Caianiello, 1973). The provided Fortran, Python, and Mathematica implementations enable both production-level numerical computation and exploratory symbolic studies in fermionic mean-field theories.
