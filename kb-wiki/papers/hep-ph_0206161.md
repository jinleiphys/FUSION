# J^PC Exotic Mesons from the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> Masses for vector 1--, axialvector 1+-, 1++, and exotic-vector 1-+ mesons are calculated within a quantum field theoretic framework based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations using a model form of the quark-antiquark interaction that is separable. The model provides an excellent description of pion and K-meson observables and the flavor-octet ground-state meson spectrum. With no adjustments to model parameters, a numerical solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation yields two exotic 1-+ mesons with masses of 1439 and 1498 MeV.

## Key claim

Using a separable-kernel Bethe-Salpeter equation with no adjustments to model parameters, two exotic \(1^{-+}\) mesons are predicted at 1439 and 1498 MeV; the authors note that the higher state may be artificially low in mass because the separable kernel supports only two bound states in that channel, unlike more sophisticated non-separable models.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous quark-antiquark Bethe-Salpeter equation in the dressed-ladder approximation, using a separable Ansatz for the gluon propagator that is fully determined by quark propagator functions fit to pion and kaon observables. The Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for vector \(1^{--}\), axialvector \(1^{+-}\) and \(1^{++}\), and exotic-vector \(1^{-+}\) mesons are constructed from Lorentz-covariant, odd-time-parity quark bilinear operators; the resulting eigenvalue equations yield bound-state masses and normalized amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- \(1^{--}\) meson mass: 736 MeV (identified with \(\rho(770)\), \(\omega(782)\); experimental masses 770\(\pm\)1 and 782\(\pm\)0 MeV)
- \(1^{+-}\) meson mass: 1244 MeV (identified with \(h_1(1170)\), \(b_1(1235)\); experimental masses 1170\(\pm\)20 and 1230\(\pm\)3 MeV)
- \(1^{++}\) meson mass: 1337 MeV (identified with \(a_1(1260)\), \(f_1(1285)\); experimental masses 1230\(\pm\)40 and 1282\(\pm\)1 MeV)
- First \(1^{-+}\) exotic meson mass: 1439 MeV (identified with \(\pi_1(1400)\); experimental mass 1376\(\pm\)17 MeV)
- Second \(1^{-+}\) exotic meson mass: 1498 MeV (identified with \(\pi_1(1600)\); experimental mass 1593\(\pm\)8 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework of Burden, Qian, Roberts, Tandy, and Thompson (1997), which successfully described the ground-state flavor-octet pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector meson spectrum, to the exotic \(1^{-+}\) channel. The predicted masses (1439 and 1498 MeV) are lower than the range 1700–2000 MeV from quantum mechanical hybrid models and lattice-QCD simulations (McNerney, 1999), but fall within the 1300–1700 MeV range from bag model calculations (Barnes and Close, 1982) and the 1300–2100 MeV range from QCD sum rules (Balitsky, Dyakonov, and Yung, 1986). The results suggest that the two predicted overlapping resonances could complicate experimental extraction and that the odd-time-parity operators used here may allow significant \(\pi\eta\) decay branching, in contrast to the OZI suppression expected for pure hybrid states.
