# Evidence on the absence of critical transition in AMPT for Pb-Pb collisions at $sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> Event-by-event fluctuations in the spatial patterns in charged particles generated in Pb--Pb collisions at the center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{S_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV are studied within A MultiPhase Transport (AMPT) model. The spatial patterns of the particles generated in the ($η, ϕ$) space for $|η| \le 0.8$ are studied using the methodology of intermittency and erraticity analysis. We find negative intermittency for charged particles generated in a range of $p_{\rm{T}}$ windows. This result contrasts sharply from what is expected for a quark-gluon plasma undergoing hadronization by a second-order phase transition. Appropriate scaling behavior is examined, resulting in definitive scaling exponent $ν_{-}$. Event-by-event fluctuations in the spatial patterns quantified by an index, named erraticity index are determined for different $p_{\rm{T}}$ bins $\leq 1$ GeV/c, for AMPT model. This is the first time that the intermittency and erraticity indices are determined for any model at such high energies. The results presented here can be used for comparison with the fluctuation properties of the experimental data and hence can help the development of a wider scope of understanding of validity of the particle production process by AMPT at these energies on the one hand, and of the true nature of the real data on the other.

## Key claim

The AMPT model (both Default and String Melting modes) does not generate the positive intermittency expected for a quark-gluon plasma undergoing hadronization via a second-order phase transition; instead, it exhibits negative intermittency, placing it in the non-critical class of systems.

## Method

Event-by-event factorial moments \(F_q^e(M)\) were computed for charged particles in \((\eta, \phi)\) space (using cumulative variables to flatten the single-particle density) for \(|\eta| \le 0.8\) and five transverse momentum windows with \(\Delta p_T = 0.1\) GeV/c up to 1.0 GeV/c, from 23424 Default and 19669 String Melting AMPT events of Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV. The scaling of the vertically averaged factorial moments \(\langle F_q \rangle\) with bin number \(M\) was examined for intermittency, and the double moments \(C_{p,q}(M)\) were used to extract the erraticity index \(\mu_q\) from the event-by-event fluctuations of the spatial patterns.

## Key numbers

* Negative intermittency exponent \(\varphi_q^- < 0\) found for all \(p_T\) windows in both AMPT modes, meaning \(\langle F_q(M) \rangle \propto M^{\varphi_q^-}\) with \(\varphi_q^- < 0\).
* Scaling exponent \(\nu_-\) for the relation \(\beta_q = (q-1)^{\nu_-}\) ranges from \(1.738 \pm 0.008\) to \(1.824 \pm 0.008\) for Default AMPT and from \(1.753 \pm 0.004\) to \(1.869 \pm 0.016\) for String Melting AMPT across the five \(p_T\) windows.
* Average multiplicities \(\langle N \rangle\) in the five \(p_T\) windows range from 80.5 (DF) and 66.1 (SM) in the highest window (\(0.9 \le p_T \le 1.0\) GeV/c) to 285.2 (DF) and 434.8 (SM) in the lowest window (\(0.2 \le p_T \le 0.3\) GeV/c).

## Context

This work extends the methodology of intermittency and erraticity analysis, previously applied at lower collision energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 100\) GeV), to LHC energies for the first time using a model. The negative intermittency observed here contrasts with the positive intermittency predicted by Ginzburg-Landau theory for second-order phase transitions (Hwa and Nazirov, 1992) and with 2D Ising model simulations (ref. ising, 2004). The results provide a baseline for comparison with experimental LHC data, enabling tests of whether the AMPT model's particle production mechanism is valid at these energies and whether real data exhibit critical behavior.
