# Production and Interactions of Hyperons and Hypernuclei

> The production of strangeness on the nucleon and hyperon and hypernuclear production in heavy ion collisions at relativistic energies and in antiproton annihilation on nuclei is discussed. The reaction process is described by transport theory with focus on $S=-2$ channels and a comparison of different model interactions. The interactions of hyperons in nuclear matter is investigated in a novel SU(3) approach. An outlook to the $S=-3$ sector and $Ω^-$ physics is given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hyperon and hypernuclear production in heavy ion collisions and antiproton annihilation can be described by transport theory, but the quantitative understanding of hypernuclei requires an accurate knowledge of hyperon-hyperon and hyperon-nucleon interactions, which is still far from being solved satisfactorily to the degree of accuracy required for predictive hypernuclear theory.

## Method

The authors use the Giessen resonance model to describe strangeness production in elementary hadronic reactions, employing a coupled-channels K-matrix approach that includes s-channel resonances and t- and u-channel rescattering processes. For heavy ion collisions and antiproton annihilation, they solve the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equation numerically within the GiBUU transport model, incorporating hadronic mean-fields and binary collisions. The interactions of hyperons in nuclear matter are investigated using a novel SU(3) approach where the eight baryons of the ground state octet are treated on equal footing, with coupling constants determined by three parameters (f₈, f₀, α) for each meson type.

## Key numbers

- The spread among octet baryon masses is about 400 MeV, indicating broken SU(3) symmetry
- For kaon production on the nucleon, data are well described up to a total center-of-mass energy of about √s = 2 GeV
- The Λ-Σ⁰ mixing coefficient c_b = -0.0283, derived from the baryon mass matrix element ⟨Σ⁰|δM|Λ⟩ = [M_Σ⁰ - M_Σ⁺ + M_p - M_n]/√3
- In the S = -2 singlet-even channels, the scattering lengths are only about 10% of the values found in the NN-system
- For Ξ-induced reactions at beam energy E_Ξ = 0.3 GeV (momentum p_Ξ = 0.937 GeV/c), the production of double-Λ fragments and Ξ hyperfragments shows strong dependence on the underlying ΞN interaction model (ESC vs FSS)

## Context

This work extends the Giessen resonance model approach previously applied to hypernuclear production in proton- and photon-induced reactions (Shyam 2004, Shyam 2006) and pion-induced kaon production on nuclei (Bender 2009). The SU(3) approach builds on earlier work by the Nijmegen group (Rijken et al.), the Juelich group, and the Kyoto-Niigata group (Fujiwara et al.), but aims to provide a more complete treatment of in-medium interactions. The transport calculations extend previous investigations by the Frankfurt-Mainz collaboration (Botvina 2013, Botvina 2014) and the authors' own work (Gaitanos 2009, 2011, 2012, 2014; Larionov 2012) to include the S = -2 sector and compare different interaction models. The results enable future experimental tests at the PANDA facility to constrain the poorly known YN and YY interactions in the high-strangeness sector.
