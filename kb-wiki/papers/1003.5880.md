# Nuclear bound states of antikaons, or quantized multiskyrmions?

> The spectrum of strange multibaryons is considered within the chiral soliton model using one of several possible SU(3$ quantization models (the bound state rigid oscillator version). The states with energy below that of antikaon and corresponding nucleus can be interpreted as antikaon-nucleus bound states. In the formal limit of small kaon mass the number of such states becomes large, for real value of this mass there are at least several states. For large values of binding energies interpretation of such states just as antikaon-nuclear bound states becomes more ambiguous.

## Key claim

The paper argues that some states in the spectrum of strange multibaryons obtained from the chiral soliton model, with energy below that of a free antikaon plus the corresponding nucleus, can be interpreted as antikaon-nucleus bound states; however, for large binding energies this interpretation becomes more ambiguous, and the states may instead be understood as quantized multiskyrmions.

## Method

The authors use the bound state rigid oscillator version of SU(3) quantization within the chiral soliton approach (CSA), applying the mass formula for multibaryons with strangeness to configurations with baryon numbers B=2 and B=3. Classical characteristics of skyrmion configurations (moments of inertia, Sigma-term, and flavor excitation energies) are taken from previous calculations using the rational map approximation, and the energy differences between strange and nonstrange states are computed using formulas that include hyperfine splitting corrections and axial symmetry contributions.

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton approach to strange multibaryons, building on previous studies of nonstrange nuclei within the CSA (Kopeliovich et al., 2008) and on the bound state model for hypernuclei (Kopeliovich, 2001). It directly addresses the recent theoretical interest in antikaon-nucleus bound states proposed by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002-2005) and others, suggesting that such states may alternatively be described as quantized multiskyrmions. The results enable further investigation of the relationship between the CSA predictions and conventional many-body approaches to antikaon-nuclear systems, particularly for B=2 and B=3 systems where experimental searches for K^-pp and K^-NNN clusters are ongoing.
