# Exotic bound states of two baryons in light of chiral effective field theory

> Baryon-baryon bound states in the strangeness S=-2, S=-3, and S=-4 sectors are considered. In particular, the dependence of the corresponding binding energies on the quark mass (or equivalently the pion mass) is explored in the framework of chiral effective field theory, in order to connect with current lattice QCD calculations. For a bound state in the Xi-Xi 1S0 channel, predicted by our leading-order effective field theory interaction, binding energies are inferred that are roughly in line with a recent lattice QCD result at meson and baryon masses that correspond to those in the lattice simulation. With regard to the so-called H-dibaryon it is shown that the SU(3) breaking effects induced by the differences of the pertinent two-baryon thresholds (Lambda-Lambda, Xi-N, Sigma-Sigma) have a very pronounced impact on its binding energy. A bound H-dibaryon as found in two lattice calculations could be shifted above the Lambda-Lambda or even above the Xi-N threshold for physical masses of the involved baryons.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the binding energy of the H-dibaryon is extremely sensitive to SU(3) breaking effects from the different two-baryon thresholds (ΛΛ, ΞN, ΣΣ), such that a bound state found in lattice QCD at unphysical quark masses could become unbound (shifted above the ΛΛ or even the ΞN threshold) when extrapolated to physical baryon masses. For the ΞΞ 1S0 channel, the leading-order chiral EFT interaction predicts binding energies that are roughly in line with a lattice QCD result, but only when using the same meson and baryon masses as the lattice simulation.

## Method
The authors use a leading-order (LO) chiral effective field theory (EFT) interaction for baryon-baryon (BB) systems, which includes four-baryon contact terms and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. The low-energy constants (LECs) for the S=-3 and S=-4 sectors are fixed by fitting to hyperon-nucleon scattering data, while the additional LEC for the S=-2, I=0 channel (the H-dibaryon) is treated as a free parameter. The binding energies are obtained by solving coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations, and the quark mass dependence is studied by varying the pion mass and, in specific cases, using the exact meson and baryon masses from a lattice QCD simulation.

## Key numbers
- For the ΞΞ 1S0 channel at physical masses, the LO chiral EFT predicts a binding energy in the range of -2.56 MeV (Λ=550 MeV) to -7.28 MeV (Λ=700 MeV).
- Using the meson and baryon masses from the NPLQCD lattice simulation (Mπ=389 MeV, mN=1151.3 MeV, mΛ=1241.9 MeV, mΞ=1349.6 MeV), the predicted Ξ-Ξ- binding energy is -3.92 MeV (Λ=550 MeV) to -10.41 MeV (Λ=700 MeV), compared to the lattice result of -14.0 ± 1.4 ± 6.7 MeV.
- For the Ξ-Σ- 1S0 channel at physical masses, the binding energy is -2.23 MeV (Λ=550 MeV) to -6.18 MeV (Λ=700 MeV).
- For the Σ-Σ- 1S0 channel, no bound state exists at physical masses, but a bound state with a binding energy of -1.23 MeV to -3.93 MeV appears when using the NPLQCD masses.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous LO chiral EFT studies of hyperon-nucleon (Polinder 2006) and hyperon-hyperon (Polinder 2007) interactions to analyze the quark mass dependence of predicted dibaryon states. It directly connects with lattice QCD calculations from the NPLQCD Collaboration (Beane 2011) and the HAL QCD Collaboration (Inoue 2011), which found evidence for bound states at unphysical quark masses. The paper enables a more informed interpretation of lattice results by showing how SU(3) breaking in baryon masses and the specific contributions of different meson exchanges (pion vs. kaon vs. eta) affect the binding energies as they are extrapolated toward the physical point.
