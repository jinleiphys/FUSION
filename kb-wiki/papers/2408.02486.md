# The Fate of Weakly Bound Light Nuclei in Central Collider Experiments: a Challenge in Favor of a Late Continuous Decoupling Mechanism

> Arguments are presented that the reaction products of central high energy nuclear collisions up to collider energies can rigorously be interpreted in terms of a continuous decoupling mechanism based on continuous equations of motion. The various aspects of the collision dynamics are investigate in terms of the individual decoupling processes. Thereby each observed particle decouples during its own temporal decoupling window. This includes a ``very late'' decoupling of the faintly bound Hypertritons observed in recent ALICE experiments. The success of the strategy is based upon 200 years old wisdom and leads to a revised interpretation of the entire decoupling process.

## Key claim
The observed abundances of all reaction products in central high-energy nuclear collisions, including very loosely bound Hypertritons, can be rigorously explained by a continuous decoupling mechanism where each particle type decouples individually along its own temporal window, rather than by instantaneous freeze-out. The key hedge is that the success of the statistical hadronization model fits with only two parameters (temperature and baryon-chemical potential) arises because fugacities remain constant along non-relativistic adiabatic courses, making the final yields independent of the exact timing and width of each particle's decoupling window.

## Method
The paper develops an analytical framework based on the Boltzmann Equation with continuous decoupling probabilities, where each particle's decoupling is governed by a damping rate $\Gamma(\tau)$ depending on total cross-sections with the surrounding medium. The analysis uses adiabatic evolution along fluid-cell trajectories, preserving entropy and particle number, with non-relativistic adiabatic indices $\kappa=5/3$ for baryons after pions decouple around $T\approx 60$ MeV. The formalism is applied to the baryon sector, showing that fugacities $f(m)$ remain constant along NR-adiabatic courses, leading to yields $N_m^{\rm dec} \propto m^{3/2} f(m)$ independent of the specific decoupling window.

## Key numbers
- Hypertriton binding energy: 130 keV, with rms-radius about 10 fm (Juric 1973)
- Pion-nucleon p-wave cross-section: 16 fm² (Delta resonance, 1952)
- Mean transverse collective flow velocity: $\langle v_\perp \rangle \approx 0.7\,c$, deduced from maxima of transverse momentum spectra around $\langle p_\perp \rangle \approx m$ for NR-particles measured in ALICE (2023)
- Decoupling window for deuterons: maximum occurs at around 1/8th of initial density within a temperature range of $[70 \rightarrow 40]$ MeV, with adiabatic indices $\kappa \in [1.45 \rightarrow 5/3]$
- Pion-nucleon-Delta entanglement ceases around $T \approx 60$ MeV
- Hypertriton halo-tail affected for temperatures $T \ge 5$ MeV (geometrical and Beth-Uhlenbeck estimate by Friman)
- Hadronization decoupling process lasted about 5 fm/c or shorter (Barz 1988, 1990)

## Context
This work extends the continuous decoupling concept previously applied in cosmology (Mukhanov 2005) and in the author's own 2008 paper (Knoll 2008), and directly addresses the puzzle raised in the Outlook section of the review by Andronic, Braun-Munzinger, Redlich, and Stachel (2017, arXiv:1710.09425) regarding how very loosely bound nuclei like Hypertritons can survive at the high temperatures ($T_0=160$ MeV) inferred from statistical hadronization fits. The analysis contradicts instantaneous freeze-out schemes (Cooper-Frye, coalescence) as inappropriate theoretical tools, and instead supports the earlier suggestion by Siemens and Kapusta (1979) regarding the $d/p$-ratio for entropy determination. The work enables a model-independent determination of baryon entropy content and calls for further model studies of non-equilibrium aspects of pions and mesons during the entanglement process.
