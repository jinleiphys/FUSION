# On the Accuracy of Hyperspherical Harmonics Approaches to Photonuclear Reactions

> Using the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method we compare the results for the triton total photodisintegration cross section obtained using the Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonics (CHH) and the Effective Interaction Hyperspherical Harmonics (EIHH) techniques. We show that these two approaches, while rather different both conceptually and computationally, lead to results which coincide within high accuracy. The calculations which include two- and three-body forces are of the same high quality in both cases. We also discuss the comparison of the two approaches in terms of computational efficiency. These results are of major importance in view of applications to the much debated case of the four-nucleon photoabsorption.

## Key claim

The Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonics (CHH) and Effective Interaction Hyperspherical Harmonics (EIHH) methods, when combined with the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) technique, produce triton total photodisintegration cross sections that agree within 0.8% in the peak region and within 2.4% up to pion threshold, despite being conceptually and computationally different.

## Method

The authors solved the LIT equation for the triton using both CHH and EIHH expansions of the wave function, employing the AV18 two-body and UIX three-body nuclear potentials. For CHH, the ground state was expanded with 11 hyperradial functions and hyperspherical states up to Kmax=10 with selected states to Kmax=100; for EIHH, 26 hyperradial functions were used up to Kmax=14 for the ground state and Kmax=15 for the LIT equation. The Lorentz integral transforms were then inverted to obtain the total photoabsorption cross section, and the results from the two independent codes were compared.

## Key numbers

- Relative difference between EIHH and CHH Lorentz integral transforms does not exceed 0.4% in the peak region and remains below 2% up to pion threshold (approximately 140 MeV).
- After inversion, the total photoabsorption cross sections from the two methods differ by less than 0.8% in the peak region and by a maximum of 2.4% up to pion threshold.
- The LIT equation was solved with Lorentzian widths of Γ=10 MeV and Γ=20 MeV, for ω0 values in the interval from -40 MeV to 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark between CHH and Faddeev approaches for three-body photodisintegration (Golak et al., 2002) by testing the newer EIHH method against the established CHH method on the triton with realistic forces. The agreement between CHH and EIHH is even better than the previously demonstrated CHH-Faddeev agreement, and the faster convergence of EIHH makes it promising for application to the four-nucleon photoabsorption case, where three-body force effects may be more prominent and where experimental results remain controversial.
