# Five-body resonances in $^8$He and $^8$C using the complex scaling method

> We study many-body resonances in the neuron-rich $^8$He and the mirror proton-rich $^8$C using the $^4$He+$N$+$N$+$N$+$N$ five-body model with the isospin $T=2$ system. Resonances are described with the complex energy eigenvalues as the Gamow states using the complex scaling method. In $^8$He, we obtain five states, in which four states are resonances, and in $^8$C all five states are resonances.We discuss the isospin-symmetry breaking dynamically induced by the Coulomb interaction in the energy spectra and decay widths of the resonances in two mirror nuclei. We predict the resonance energies and decay widths for the future experiments. We also investigate the configurations of valence nucleons above $^4$He in two nuclei with the $jj$ coupling scheme and all the states dominantly have the $p$-shell configurations. From the configuration mixing, $^8$He and $^8$C give the similar results, which indicates the good symmetry in two nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in the five-body system of $^8$He and $^8$C, the level ordering is the same in both mirror nuclei, but the level spacing is smaller in $^8$C than in $^8$He, indicating a dynamical isospin-symmetry breaking induced by the Coulomb interaction for protons, while the direct distance between complex energy eigenvalues of corresponding states remains similar.

## Method

The authors used a $^4$He+$N$+$N$+$N$+$N$ five-body cluster orbital shell model (COSM) to describe the motion of four valence nucleons around a $^4$He core. Resonances were treated as Gamow states with complex energy eigenvalues using the complex scaling method (CSM), which transforms coordinates by a scaling angle $\theta$ to impose outgoing boundary conditions. The Hamiltonian included the microscopic Kanada-Kaneko-Nagata-Nomoto potential for the $^4$He-nucleon interaction and the Minnesota potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the repulsive strength adjusted to 173.7 MeV to fit the two-neutron separation energy of $^6$He.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of $^8$He: relative energy from $^4$He is 3.22 MeV (experimental: 3.11 MeV); matter radius 2.52 fm; charge radius 1.92 fm.
- $2^+_1$ resonance of $^8$He: relative energy from $^4$He is 0.32 MeV, decay width $\Gamma$ = 0.66 MeV (experimental: 0.46 $\pm$ 0.12 MeV, $\Gamma$ = 0.5 $\pm$ 0.35 MeV).
- Ground state of $^8$C ($0^+_1$): relative energy from $^4$He is 3.32 MeV (experimental: 3.449(30) MeV), decay width $\Gamma$ = 0.072 MeV (experimental: 0.130(50) MeV).
- $0^+_2$ resonance of $^8$He: energy 3.07 MeV, decay width 3.19 MeV.
- $0^+_2$ resonance of $^8$C: energy 8.88 MeV, decay width 6.64 MeV.
- $1^+$ resonance of $^8$He: energy 1.65 MeV, decay width 3.57 MeV.
- $1^+$ resonance of $^8$C: energy 7.89 MeV, decay width 7.28 MeV.
- $2^+_2$ resonance of $^8$He: energy 4.52 MeV, decay width 4.39 MeV.
- $2^+_2$ resonance of $^8$C: energy 9.70 MeV, decay width 9.10 MeV.
- Direct distance between complex energy eigenvalues of $0^+_1$ and $2^+_2$: 8.05 MeV for $^8$He, 7.84 MeV for $^8$C.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Myo et al. (2010, 2012, 2014) that only calculated $0^+$ states in $^8$He and $^8$C, now fully calculating $1^+$ and $2^+$ states for a complete resonance spectroscopy. It builds on prior analyses of isospin symmetry in $^7$He and $^7$B using the $^4$He+$N$+$N$+$N$ model by Myo et al. (2011). The results provide predictions for future experiments on $^8$He and $^8$C resonances, and enable further investigation of isospin-symmetry breaking effects near the drip lines, as well as calculations of transition strength functions using the Green's function with complex scaling.
