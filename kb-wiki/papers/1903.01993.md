# Factorization of jet cross sections in heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a new phenomenological approach to establish QCD factorization of jet cross sections in the heavy-ion environment. Starting from a factorization formalism in proton-proton collisions, we introduce medium modified jet functions to capture the leading interaction of jets with the hot and dense QCD medium. A global analysis using a Monte Carlo sampling approach is performed in order to reliably determine the new jet functions from the nuclear modification factor of inclusive jets at the LHC. We find that gluon jets are significantly more suppressed due to the presence of the medium than quark jets. In addition, we observe that the jet radius dependence is directly related to the relative suppression of quark and gluon jets. Our approach may help to improve the extraction of medium properties from data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the leading-power QCD factorization formalism for inclusive jet production in proton-proton collisions can be extended to heavy-ion collisions by introducing medium-modified jet functions, and that this approach can describe the nuclear modification factor data from the LHC. The hedge is that this factorization is an approximation valid only at leading power, with corrections suppressed by inverse powers of the large momentum transfer, and the kinematic regime where it applies may differ for heavy-ion collisions compared to proton-proton collisions.

## Method

The authors start from the factorized expression for inclusive single jet production in proton-proton collisions and replace the vacuum jet functions with medium-modified jet functions that capture the leading interaction of jets with the quark-gluon plasma. They parametrize the medium-modified jet functions as vacuum jet functions convolved with flexible weight functions, keeping the DGLAP evolution kernels unmodified and only changing the initial condition of the evolution at the jet scale. A Monte Carlo sampling approach (similar to NNPDF and JAM global analyses) is used to determine the parameters of the weight functions by fitting to the nuclear modification factor data for inclusive jets from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- The fit yields a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.1 for data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 1.7 for data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
- The analysis includes jet radii in the range R = 0.2 to 0.4 from the anti-k_T algorithm.
- The medium-modified jet functions are evaluated at the scale mu = p_T = 100 GeV for R = 0.4 jets.
- The data sets cover the most central collisions (0-10% centrality) at both sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the leading-power QCD factorization formalism for jet production in proton-proton collisions (Collins 1989, Ellis 1993) to the heavy-ion environment, building on earlier proposals for medium-modified jet functions by Kang (2017) and Li (2018). It is complementary to other approaches such as analytical calculations of parton energy loss (Baier 1996, Gyulassy 2000) and Monte Carlo event generators with in-medium parton showers (Zapp 2008, Schenke 2009). The approach enables future global analyses that could include gamma/Z+jet data and hadron+jet data to test the universality of the medium-modified jet functions and reduce model bias in extracting medium properties from data.
