# $Q^2$--Dependence of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule

> We test the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule numerically by calculating the total photon absorption cross sections $σ_{1/2}$ and $σ_{3/2}$ on the nucleon via photon excitation of baryon resonances in the constituent quark model. A total of seventeen, low-lying, non-strange baryon resonances are included in this calculation. The transverse and longitudinal interference cross section, $σ_{1/2}^{TL}$, is found to play an important role in the study of the $Q^2$ variation of the sum rule. The results show that the GDH sum rule is saturated by these resonances at a confidence level of 94%. In particular, the $P_{33}(1232)$ excitation largely saturates the sum rule at $Q^2 = 0$, and dominates at small $Q^2$. The GDH integral has a strong $Q^2$-dependence below $Q^2= 1.0 {GeV}^2$ and changes its sign around $Q^2= 0.3 {GeV}^2$. It becomes weakly $Q^2$-dependent for $Q^2 > 1.0 {GeV}^2$ because of the quick decline of the resonance contributions. We point out that the $Q^2$ variation of the GDH sum rule is very important for understanding the nucleon spin structure in the non-perturbative QCD region.

## Key claim

The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule is saturated by the contributions of 17 low-lying non-strange baryon resonances at a confidence level of 94%, with the caveat that the calculation uses a constituent quark model and a phenomenological treatment of non-resonant background that ignores possible interference between background and resonances in the same multipole channel.

## Method

The authors calculated the total virtual photon absorption cross sections σ₁/₂ and σ₃/₂ on the nucleon via photoexcitation of 17 baryon resonances using non-relativistic harmonic oscillator wave functions in the constituent quark model. The helicity amplitudes A₁/₂, A₃/₂, and S₁/₂ were computed for each resonance using an electromagnetic Hamiltonian that includes nonrelativistic, spin-orbit, and non-additive terms, and the total cross sections were obtained as incoherent sums of Breit-Wigner parameterized resonance contributions. The transverse-longitudinal interference cross section σ₁/₂^TL was included in the calculation of the generalized GDH integral I(Q²).

## Key numbers

* The predicted GDH integral at Q²=0 is I(0)=0.492 GeV⁻², compared to the sum rule value 2π²ακ²/M²=0.524 GeV⁻² for the proton, representing 94% saturation.
* The GDH integral changes sign around Q²=0.3 GeV².
* The GDH integral has strong Q²-dependence below Q²=1.0 GeV² and becomes weakly Q²-dependent for Q²>1.0 GeV².
* At small momentum transfers, roughly 75% of the GDH sum rule integral is provided by the P₃₃(1232) resonance contribution.
* The P₃₃(1232) contribution is negative at small Q², turns positive around Q²=0.3 GeV², and becomes negligible after Q²~1 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the earlier quantitative investigation by Burkert and Li (1993) of resonance saturation of the GDH sum rule, and builds on the suggestion by Close, Gilman, and Karliner (1972) that low-lying resonances might saturate the sum rule. The calculation includes the transverse-longitudinal interference cross section σ₁/₂^TL, which had often been omitted in previous studies, and demonstrates its importance in the small Q² region. The results provide a bridge between the real-photon GDH sum rule at Q²=0 and the deep inelastic scattering regime described by perturbative QCD, and are relevant for interpreting planned experiments at facilities such as ELSA, GRAAL, LEGS, and MAMI.
