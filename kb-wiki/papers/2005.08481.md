# Precision measurement of $^{\mathbf{210}}$Bi $β$-spectrum

> The precision measurement of the $β-$spectrum shape for $^{210}$Bi (historically RaE) have been performed with a spectrometer based on semiconductor Si(Li) detector. This first forbidden non-unique transition has the transition form-factor strongly deviated from unity and knowledge of its spectrum would play an important role in low-background physics in presence of $^{210}$Pb background. The measured transition form-factor could be approximated as $S(W) = 1 + (-0.4363 \pm 0.0037) W + (0.0523 \pm 0.0010) W^2$, that is in good agreement with previous studies and has significantly increased parameter precision.

## Key claim

The beta-spectrum shape of the first-forbidden non-unique transition in 210Bi was measured with significantly improved precision, yielding a form-factor parametrization that depends on which Fermi function (point-like nucleus or with finite-size and screening corrections) is used in the analysis; the two parametrizations are consistent with previous studies but have uncertainties reduced by more than a factor of five.

## Method

A Si(Li) semiconductor detector with a tungsten collimator and passive shielding was used in a target-detector geometry to measure the beta spectrum of a 210Bi source prepared by thermal oxidation on a stainless steel foil. The detector response function was modeled with a Geant4.10.04 Monte Carlo simulation using the G4EmStandardPhysics_option4 package, and the experimental spectrum was fitted with a parabolic form-factor C(W) = 1 + C1 W + C2 W2 using a chi-squared likelihood, with six free parameters including the form-factor coefficients and two response-function correction parameters.

## Key numbers

- End-point energy of the 210Bi beta spectrum: 1162 keV.
- Total number of registered 210Bi decays: 1.0 × 10^8 over 634 hours of data-taking.
- Energy resolution for 59.6 keV gamma line of 241Am: FWHM = 900 eV; for 480 keV conversion electrons: FWHM = 1.8 keV; for 30 keV electron conversion line: FWHM = 1.0 keV.
- Form-factor parameters using point-like-nucleus Fermi function: C1 = -0.4523 ± 0.0031, C2 = 0.0560 ± 0.0008 (chi-squared/NDF = 1775.3/1705, P-value = 0.12).
- Form-factor parameters using Fermi function with finite-size and screening corrections: C1 = -0.4470 ± 0.0013, C2 = 0.0552 ± 0.0004 (chi-squared/NDF = 1806.1/1738, P-value = 0.12).
- Correlation coefficient between C1 and C2: 0.96 for the final fit.
- Background level near the 210Bi endpoint: 0.18 counts/h/keV.

## Context

This work extends the measurements of the 210Bi beta spectrum performed by Daniel (1962) using a magnetic spectrometer and by Grau Carles and Malonda (1996) using a solid-state spectrometer, achieving more than five times smaller uncertainties on the form-factor parameters. The improved precision is important for background modeling in low-background experiments such as dark matter searches and solar CNO neutrino detection, where the 210Bi spectrum shape is similar to the recoil electron spectrum from CNO neutrino scattering (Agostini, 2019). The results enable more accurate subtraction of the 210Pb background chain in large neutrino detectors.
