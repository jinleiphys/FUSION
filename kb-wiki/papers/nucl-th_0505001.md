# NN Final-State Interaction in the Helicity Dependence of Inclusive $π^-$ Photoproduction from the Deuteron

> The helicity dependence of the inclusive $π^-$ photoproduction reaction from the deuteron in the $Δ$(1232)-resonance region is investigated with inclusion of final-state nucleon-nucleon rescattering ($NN$-FSI). For the elementary $π$-production operator an effective Lagrangian model which includes the standard pseudovector Born terms and a contribution from the $Δ$-resonance is used. The half-off-shell $NN$-scattering matrix is obtained from a separable representation of a realistic $NN$-interaction. The differential polarized cross-section difference for parallel and antiparallel helicity states is predicted and compared with experiment. We find that the effect of $NN$-FSI is much less important in the helicity difference than in the previously studied unpolarized differential cross section. Furthermore, the contribution of $\vec d(\vecγ,π^-)pp$ to the deuteron spin asymmetry is explicitly evaluated with inclusion of $NN$-FSI. It has been found that the effect of $NN$-FSI is much larger in the asymmetry than in the total cross section, and this leads to an appreciable reduction of the spin asymmetry in the $Δ$-region. Inclusion of such effect also leads to improved and quite satisfactory agreement with existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The effect of nucleon-nucleon final-state interaction (NN-FSI) is much less important in the helicity difference of the differential cross section than in the previously studied unpolarized differential cross section, but it is much larger in the spin asymmetry of the total cross section, leading to an appreciable reduction of the spin asymmetry in the Δ(1232)-resonance region.

## Method

The helicity dependence of the inclusive π⁻ photoproduction reaction from the deuteron was calculated using an effective Lagrangian model for the elementary γN→πN operator, including pseudovector Born terms and a Δ(1232)-resonance contribution. The half-off-shell NN-scattering matrix was obtained from a separable representation of the Paris potential using the EST method, including all partial waves with total angular momentum J ≤ 3. The total transition matrix was computed as the sum of the impulse approximation (IA) term and the NN-rescattering term, and the results were compared with experimental data from the GDH collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The NN-FSI reduces the unpolarized differential cross section by about 15% at θ_π = 0° (forward pion angles).
- The reduction of the spin asymmetry (σ^P - σ^A) due to NN-FSI reaches about 35 μb at its maximum in the Δ(1232)-resonance region.
- For the unpolarized total cross section, NN-rescattering reduces the cross section in the Δ(1232)-resonance region by about 5 percent.
- The calculations were performed for photon lab energies in the Δ(1232)-resonance region, with the elementary operator model validated against experimental data for the free nucleon process.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of unpolarized cross sections for γd→π⁻pp by Darwish (2003) and Levchuk (2001) to the helicity-dependent observables, and complements earlier polarization studies by Darwish (2004) and Logothetidou (2000) that did not include NN-FSI effects. The results enable more precise tests of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule for the deuteron and provide a benchmark for future three-body treatments of the final πNN system and for measurements of the deuteron spin asymmetry.
