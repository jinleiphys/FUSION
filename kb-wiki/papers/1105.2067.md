# Parton showers as sources of energy-momentum deposition in the QGP and their implication for shockwave formation at RHIC and at the LHC

> We derive the distribution of energy and momentum transmitted from a primary fast parton and its medium-induced bremsstrahlung gluons to a thermalized quark-gluon plasma. Our calculation takes into account the important and thus far neglected effects of quantum interference between the resulting color currents. We use our result to obtain the rate at which energy is absorbed by the medium as a function of time and find that the rate is modified by the quantum interference between the primary parton and secondary gluons. This Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal type interference persists for time scales relevant to heavy ion phenomenology. We further couple the newly derived source of energy and momentum deposition to linearized hydrodynamics to obtain the bulk medium response to realistic parton propagation and splitting in the quark-gluon plasma. We find that because of the characteristic large angle in-medium gluon emission and the multiple sources of energy deposition in a parton shower, formation of well defined Mach cones by energetic jets in heavy ion reactions is not likely.

## Key claim

The paper derives the first calculation of the energy-momentum source term for a parton shower including quantum color interference between the primary parton and its medium-induced bremsstrahlung gluons, and finds that because of large-angle in-medium gluon emission and multiple sources of energy deposition, the formation of well-defined Mach cones by energetic jets in heavy ion reactions is not likely.

## Method

The authors start from a thermal field theory result for the source term induced by a single fast parton in a medium of thermal quarks, then extend it to include a primary parton undergoing medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung using Feynman diagrams with color structure. They evaluate the resulting expressions in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation using bare propagators, obtaining a closed analytic form for the source term that accounts for the spatial extent and quantum interference between the parent parton and radiated gluon. The source term is then coupled to linearized hydrodynamics to obtain the bulk medium response.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass: \(m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 N_F/6\) for a quark-only medium, modified to \(m_D^2 \rightarrow g^2 T^2 (1 + N_F/6)\) when including medium gluons
- Coupling constant \(g = 2\), temperature \(T = 0.35\) GeV, and \(m_D = g T\) used for numerical results based on average values for LHC collision energies in a Bjorken expanding plasma
- Color factors: \(C_F = 4/3\) for a quark, \(C_A = 3\) for a gluon, interference terms yield \(-C_A/2 = -3/2\) each
- Interference term plotted at times \(t = 0.5\) fm and \(t = 2.5\) fm for a primary quark with energy 5 GeV, radiated gluon with energy 1 GeV, and emission angle \(\theta = 0.6\) radians
- Energy transfer rate calculated for gluon emission angles \(\theta = 0.1, 0.3, 0.6\) radians for \(q \rightarrow q+g\) systems, and for average angle \(\theta = 0.7\) for \(q \rightarrow q + 4\times g\) and \(g \rightarrow g + 8 \times g\) systems

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the source term that focused on a single primary parton (Neufeld, 2008) and earlier attempts to include medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung that neglected spatial extent and color quantum interference (Neufeld, 2009). The results enable more realistic modeling of how parton showers deposit energy and momentum into the quark-gluon plasma, with implications for interpreting experimental measurements of azimuthal particle correlations associated with high \(p_T\) triggers at RHIC and the LHC, including the proposed Mach cone shockwave explanations.
