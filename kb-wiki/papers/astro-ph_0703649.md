# Equation of State for supernova explosion simulations

> In this work we present a detailed explanation of the construction of an appropriate equation of state (EoS) for nuclear astrophysics. We use a relativistic model in order to obtain an EoS for neutrally charged matter that extends from very low to high densities, from zero temperature to 100 MeV with proton fractions ranging from 0 (no protons) to 0.6 (asymmetric matter with proton excess). For the achievement of complete convergence, the Sommerfeld approximation is used at low temperatures and the Boltzman distribution for relativistic particles is used in the calculation of the electron properties at very low densities. Photons are also incorporated as blackbody radiation. An extension of this EoS is also presented with the inclusion of strangeness by taking into account the sigma minus hyperon only. Strangeness fractions range from 0.02 to 0.3.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a relativistic equation of state (EoS) for supernova simulations covering a wide range of densities, temperatures, and proton fractions, but the numerical calculations are performed only with nucleons (protons and neutrons) despite presenting the full formalism for the baryonic octet. The inclusion of strangeness is limited to the Σ⁻ hyperon only, with the authors noting that Λ hyperons and the full octet should be included in future work.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) in the mean-field approximation to compute the EoS for neutrally charged hadronic matter. The GM3 parametrization (Glendenning and Moszkowski) is employed, with electrons treated as a free Fermi gas and photons as blackbody radiation. At low temperatures (log T between -0.4 and -0.1 MeV), the Sommerfeld approximation is used for baryons to achieve convergence; at very low densities, a Boltzmann distribution for relativistic electrons and positrons is applied. Strangeness is introduced by including the Σ⁻ hyperon only, with threshold densities determined from β-equilibrium conditions for given strangeness fractions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range: from 0 MeV to 100 MeV
- Proton fraction range: 0 (no protons) to 0.6 (asymmetric matter with proton excess)
- Strangeness fraction range: 0.02 to 0.3
- Baryonic density grid: 3 ≤ log(ρ) ≤ 15.7 (g/cm³) with mesh intervals of 0.1
- Temperature grid: -0.4 ≤ log(T) ≤ 2 (MeV) with mesh intervals of 0.1
- Proton fraction grid: 0 ≤ y_p ≤ 0.6 with mesh intervals of 0.02
- GM3 parametrization: effective mass M* = 0.78 M, incompressibility K = 240 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³
- GM3 coupling constants: (g_s/m_s)² = 9.927, (g_v/m_v)² = 4.82, (g_ρ/m_ρ)² = 4.79, κ = 0.017318 g_s³, λ = -0.014526 g_s⁴
- Hyperon coupling constants: x_{sΣ} = 0.7, x_{vΣ} = x_{ρΣ} = 0.783

## Context

This work extends the approach of Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991) by constructing a finite-temperature EoS suitable for supernova simulations, covering a wider parameter space than previous zero-temperature neutron star EoS. The results are compared with the EoS of Shen et al., which uses the TM1 parametrization and includes non-homogeneous phases; the authors note that TM1 breaks down at densities approximately 6 times nuclear saturation density when hyperons are included, giving negative baryon effective masses. The paper enables future inclusion of the pasta phase, α-particles, and the full baryonic octet, with the authors noting that the critical temperature for uniform matter may be lower than the ~15 MeV reported by Shen et al., citing results from pasta-phase studies that found critical temperatures of ~5 MeV for y_p=0.3 matter and ~6 MeV for y_p=0.5 matter.
