# Signatures of dense hadronic matter in ultrarelativistic heavy ion reactions

> The behavior of hadronic matter at high baryon densities is studied within Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD). Baryonic stopping is observed for Au+Au collisions from SIS up to SPS energies. The excitation function of flow shows strong sensitivities to the underlying equation of state (EOS), allowing for systematic studies of the EOS. Dilepton spectra are calculated with and without shifting the $ρ$ pole. Except for S+Au collisions our calculations reproduce the CERES data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD) model can reproduce experimental data on baryonic stopping, collective flow, and dilepton production across a wide range of collision energies, but the default calculation misses two data points in S+Au dilepton spectra by about two standard deviations, and in-medium modifications of the rho meson pole alone do not suffice to explain this discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used the URQMD transport model, which includes 50 baryon species (nucleon, delta, hyperon and their resonances up to 2.11 GeV) and 25 meson species with all isospin-projected states, supplemented by a string model for high-mass excitations. They simulated Au+Au collisions from SIS (1 AGeV) to SPS (160 AGeV) energies, Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV, and S+S and S+Au at 200 AGeV, calculating rapidity distributions, directed transverse momentum, and dilepton mass spectra with and without a density-dependent shift of the rho meson pole.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic stopping produces gaussian rapidity distributions peaked at midrapidity for Au+Au at SIS (1 AGeV), AGS (10.6 AGeV), and Pb+Pb at CERN/SPS (160 AGeV)
- The directed transverse momentum per nucleon with a hard equation of state is more than twice as high as for the cascade calculation for Au+Au from 0.1 to 4 AGeV
- At T=150 MeV, the URQMD equation of state yields about 4-5 times less energy density than a Hagedorn gas or QGP (which give epsilon ~ 1 GeV/fm^3)
- Around T=300 MeV, the URQMD energy density approaches the QGP value of epsilon ~ 10 GeV/fm^3
- Pion annihilation contributes only 40% of the rho peak in S+Au dilepton spectra
- The rho meson pole shift uses lambda=0.18 with the formula m_rho(rho/rho_0) = m_rho(0) / (1 + lambda * rho/rho_0)
- For S+Au, two data points around M ~ 400 MeV are missed by the default calculation by about two standard deviations
- The average decay density for rho mesons in S+Au is <rho_b> < 0.5 rho_0 around M ~ 0.4 GeV, and as low as 0.2 rho_0

## Context

This work extends the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD) model to study dense hadronic matter across SIS, AGS, and SPS energies, building on earlier transport approaches such as those by Stoecker (1986) and Doss et al. (1986). The flow excitation function analysis follows the suggestion by Rischke et al. that phase transitions should produce abnormal behavior in collective motion. The dilepton calculations with density-dependent rho meson mass shifts test predictions from chiral lagrangians by G.E. Brown et al. (1991) and T. Hatsuda et al. (1992), and the results enable systematic comparisons with CERES data to constrain in-medium modifications of vector meson properties.
