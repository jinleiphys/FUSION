# Second-order renormalized Hamiltonian of Yukawa theory

> Using the renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) we calculate the effective Hamiltonians in the theory of a fermion field coupled to a scalar field via the Yukawa interaction. The theory is renormalized by the addition of counterterms. Necessary counterterms are determined by computing matrix elements of the effective Hamiltonian. All calculations are performed up to the second order in the expansion in powers of the coupling constant. Renormalized effective Hamiltonians are well-defined symmetric forms acting in the Fock space as opposed to the renormalized bare Hamiltonian, which is not well-defined without regularization. We introduce computational techniques that should streamline higher-order calculations and may be of independent interest.

## Key claim

Using the renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP), the authors compute renormalized effective Hamiltonians for Yukawa theory up to second order in the coupling constant, but the calculation is perturbative and the resulting effective Hamiltonians are only approximations that break exact longitudinal boost invariance due to the choice of generator in the RGPEP equation.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical front-form Hamiltonian of Yukawa theory, regularize it, and then solve the RGPEP flow equation perturbatively up to second order in the coupling constant. They compute matrix elements of the effective Hamiltonian to identify divergent terms and determine the necessary counterterms, using Wick's theorem and diagrammatic techniques to organize the calculation of tree and loop contributions to the effective Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant \(g\) is dimensionless and the expansion is performed in powers of \(g\), with all calculations carried out to order \(g^2\)
* The RGPEP form factor is \(e^{-t(P_j^- - P_k^-)^2}\), where \(t \ge 0\) is the RGPEP scale parameter and \(P_j^-\) are free Hamiltonian eigenvalues
* The front-form dispersion relation is \(p^- = (m^2 + (p^\perp)^2)/p^+\), where \(p^+\) is strictly positive for massive particles
* The invariant mass of two particles is \(\mathcal{M}_{12}^2 = (m_1^2 + k^2)/x + (m_2^2 + k^2)/(1-x)\), where \(x = p_1^+/(p_1^+ + p_2^+)\) and \(k = (1-x)p_1^\perp - x p_2^\perp\)

## Context

This work extends the RGPEP approach, previously applied to \(\phi^3\) theory in 1+5 dimensions (Allen, 1998; Kylin, 1998; Allen, 1999) and 1+3 dimensions (Glazek, 2001), to Yukawa theory as a simpler setting before tackling QCD. The calculation provides a complete example of a relativistic field theory renormalized via RGPEP, enabling future nonperturbative diagonalization using methods such as discretized light-cone quantization (Pauli, 1985) or basis light-front quantization (Vary, 2009), as well as simulations on quantum computers.
