# Chiral Perturbation Theory: a Primer

> Recently methods have been developed which exploit the chiral symmetry of QCD in order to make rigorous contact with low energy particle physics phenomenology. In these lectures we present a pedagogical introduction to these techniques.

## Key claim

Chiral Perturbation Theory provides a rigorous, low-energy method to make contact between QCD and particle phenomenology, but only for energies much less than 1 GeV, and only when the light quark masses (u, d, s) are treated as small perturbations that explicitly break the underlying chiral symmetry.

## Method

The paper constructs an effective field theory for pseudoscalar mesons (pions, kaons, eta) by defining a nonlinear field U = exp(i·τ·π/v) that transforms under chiral SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R rotations. The lowest-order effective Lagrangian is written at two-derivative order (or one power of chiral symmetry breaking), containing a kinetic term Tr(∂_μ U ∂^μ U^†) and a mass term proportional to m_π^2 Tr(U + U^†). The meson masses are then expressed in terms of the quark mass matrix and a constant B_0, yielding an overdetermined system of equations relating π, K, and η masses.

## Key numbers

- The QCD scale is Λ_QCD ∼ 300 MeV, below which chiral methods apply
- The fine structure constant α = e^2/4π ≈ 1/137
- The running coupling g^2/4π ∼ 1 at low energies, preventing perturbative treatment
- The neutron-proton mass difference from electromagnetic effects is estimated as m_p - m_n ∼ α/(3⟨r⟩) ∼ 0.5 MeV, suggesting m_d - m_u ∼ 2 MeV
- The pseudoscalar meson masses satisfy: m_π^2 = (m_u + m_d) B_0 = 2m̂ B_0, m_K^2 = (m̂ + m_s) B_0, m_η^2 = (1/3)(m_u + m_d + 4m_s) B_0 = (2/3)(m̂ + 2m_s) B_0

## Context

This pedagogical review extends the earlier work on SU(2) and SU(3) flavor symmetries (Gell-Mann's Eightfold Way, 1961) and the concept of spontaneous symmetry breaking in QCD. The paper explains how Goldstone's theorem (Goldstone, 1961) predicts massless pseudoscalar bosons when chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken, but the explicit breaking from nonzero quark masses gives these bosons small but nonzero masses. The effective field theory approach parallels the treatment of superconductivity, where lattice degrees of freedom are integrated out to yield an effective Lagrangian for electron pairs. This work enables systematic calculations of low-energy hadronic processes using chiral perturbation theory as a controlled expansion in momenta and quark masses.
