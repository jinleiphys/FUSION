# The Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid functional with a realistic effective mass

> The Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid (BCPM) functional recently proposed to describe nuclear structure properties of finite nuclei is generalized as to include a realistic effective mass. The resulting functional is as good as the previous one in describing binding energies, radii, deformation properties, etc and, in addition, the description of Giant Quadrupole Resonance energies is greatly improved.

## Key claim

The BCPM functional is generalized to include a density-dependent effective mass extracted from the same Brueckner Hartree-Fock calculations used for the ground-state energy, preserving the nuclear matter equation of state while improving the description of Giant Quadrupole Resonance energies; the rms deviation for binding energies increases slightly to 1.68 MeV (from 1.58 MeV in the original BCPM) but charge radii improve to 0.024 fm (from 0.027 fm).

## Method

The authors replace the bare nucleon mass in the BCPM energy density functional with a density-dependent effective mass obtained from Brueckner Hartree-Fock calculations of symmetric and neutron matter, fitted by polynomials in density and a linear interpolation in isospin asymmetry beta. The kinetic energy term is modified by adding and subtracting a semiclassical kinetic energy contribution to preserve the nuclear matter equation of state, and the spin-orbit strength and Gaussian range parameters are refitted to binding energies of 620 even-even nuclei from the AME 2012 compilation. Finite-nuclei calculations are performed using the HFBaxial code with harmonic oscillator basis, including a density-dependent zero-range pairing force and rotational energy corrections via projection after variation.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation for binding energies: sigma_E = 1.68 MeV for 620 even-even nuclei (AME 2012 compilation)
- RMS deviation for charge radii: sigma_R = 0.024 fm for 315 even-even nuclei (Angeli compilation)
- Fitted parameters: r_{0U} = r_{0L} = 0.752 fm, W_LS = 112 MeV
- Effective mass splitting at saturation density: (m*_n - m*_p)/m = 0.2 beta
- GQR excitation energy for 208Pb: E_3(Q) = 11.18 MeV (theoretical) vs 10.89 +/- 0.30 MeV (experimental)
- GMR excitation energy for 208Pb: E_3(M) = 14.53 MeV (theoretical) vs 13.96 +/- 0.20 MeV (experimental)
- First fission barrier for 240Pu: 6.2 MeV (BCPM*) vs 6.0 MeV (experimental)
- Second fission barrier for 240Pu: 5.5 MeV (BCPM*) vs 5.15 MeV (experimental)
- Fission isomer excitation energy for 240Pu: 1.7 MeV (BCPM*) vs 2.8 MeV (experimental)
- Scaling law coefficients: C_M = 86 MeV for monopole, C_Q = 67 MeV for quadrupole resonances

## Context

This work extends the BCPM functional (Baldo et al., 2015) by incorporating a realistic effective mass, addressing a known deficiency where the original BCPM systematically underestimated GQR excitation energies by about 1 MeV. The approach follows the strategy of Fayans (1998) in constructing functionals from microscopic nuclear matter calculations, and contrasts with Skyrme functionals that typically have effective masses around 0.7m. The new functional enables improved description of giant resonances beyond monopole and dipole modes, while maintaining the good performance of BCPM for binding energies, radii, deformation properties, and fission barriers.
