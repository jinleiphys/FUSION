# Comment on N. Rijal et al. "Measurement of d + 7Be Cross Sections for Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis"

> Rijal, et al. in their recent publication [Phys. Rev. Lett {\bf 122}, 182701 (2019), arXiv:1808.07893], on "Measurement of d + $^7$Be Cross Sections for Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN)", misrepresent their result, they misrepresent previous work of Parker (72) and of Caughlan and Fowler (88), and quite possibly, contradicts the very BBN theory that has been established over the last few decades. This comment is intended to correct these misrepresentations and critically review their claims on BBN.

# Comment on N. Rijal et al. "Measurement of d + 7Be Cross Sections for Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis"

## Key claim
The new FSU19 reaction rate for d + 7Be → 2α + p is identical over the BBN temperature region of interest (0.5–0.9 GK) to the CF88 rate published by Caughlan and Fowler (1988), which has been used in BBN calculations for over thirty years. Therefore, the claim that this measurement reduces the predicted primordial 7Li abundance is incorrect, as no reduction beyond what was already calculated by BBN practitioners can be deduced from the FSU19 rate.

## Method
The comment compares the FSU19 reaction rate (published by Rijal et al. in 2019) to the CF88 rate from Caughlan and Fowler (1988) and to Parker's (1972) s-factor estimate of ~100 MeV·b, plotting ratios over the BBN temperature range. It also examines the s-factor measured in the Gamow window of BBN against Parker's educated guess, and reviews the historical use of the d + 7Be rate in BBN compilations including the twelve canonical BBN reactions.

## Key numbers
- Over the BBN region of interest (0.5–0.9 GK), the central values of the FSU19 rate are identical to the CF88 rate.
- Parker's (1972) s-factor estimate for d + 7Be was ~100 MeV·b.
- The 7Li abundance predicted by Rijal et al. is (7Li/H)p = 4.24–4.61 × 10−10.
- Using the PRIMAT code, the d + 7Be rate would need to be ~30 times larger than the CF88 rate to play a significant role in BBN.
- Without the Parker rate, BBN practitioners found a change in 7Li/H abundance of no more than 1%.

## Context
This comment directly contradicts the claims of Rijal et al. (2019) that their measurement of the d + 7Be cross section reduces the predicted primordial 7Li abundance. It extends the established work of Caughlan and Fowler (1988) and Parker (1972), showing that the FSU19 rate merely confirms the CF88 rate that has been used for decades. The comment argues that the d + 7Be reaction has long been recognized as playing no significant role in BBN and is not included in the twelve canonical BBN reactions listed by Smith, Kawano, and Malaney (1993). The work enables clarification that no new BBN information has been gained since Parker (1972), and suggests a possible mistake in the unspecified BBN code used by Rijal et al.
