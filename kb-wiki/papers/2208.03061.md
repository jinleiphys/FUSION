# The reaction $πN \to ωN$ in a dynamical coupled-channel approach

> A refined investigation on light flavor meson-baryon scatterings is performed using a dynamical coupled-channel approach, the Jülich-Bonn model, that respects unitartiy and analyticity constraints. The channel space of $πN$, $πΔ$, $σN$, $ρN$, $ηN$, $K Λ$ and $K Σ$ is extended by adding the $ωN$ final state. The spectra of $N^*$ and $Δ$ resonances are extracted in terms of complex poles of the scattering amplitudes, based on the result of a global fit to a worldwide collection of data, in the energy region from the $πN$ threshold to center-of-mass energy $z=2.3$ GeV. A negative value of the $ωN$ elastic spin-averaged scattering length is extracted, questioning the existence of bound states of the $ω$ meson in the nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel model by adding the ωN final state, and based on a global fit to data up to 2.3 GeV center-of-mass energy, extracts a negative ωN elastic spin-averaged scattering length, which questions the existence of bound states of the ω meson in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors extended the existing Jülich-Bonn model channel space (πN, πΔ, σN, ρN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ) by adding the ωN final state. They performed a global fit to approximately 9000 data points, including 178 new data points for πN → ωN reactions, using a Lippmann-Schwinger-like scattering equation with tree-level diagrams and correlated two-pion exchange as the kernel, and extracted N* and Δ resonance spectra as complex poles of the scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The fit covers the energy region from the πN threshold to center-of-mass energy z = 2.3 GeV
- The database for ωN includes 178 data points from π⁺n → ωp and π⁻p → ωn reactions
- The total number of fit parameters is 304 (79 for T^NP and 225 for T^P)
- The ωN elastic spin-averaged scattering length is extracted as a negative value (exact numerical value not provided in the truncated text)
- Couplings of N* and Δ states to effective three-body channels are given in units of 10⁻³ √MeV, for example the Δ(1232) 3/2⁺ has a coupling to πΔ(1) of 54.8 + 20.0i (fit A) or 59.5 + 23.9i (fit B)

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn model, which has been developed over three decades from early studies on meson-meson and K⁻N interactions (Mueller 1990, Lohse 1990, Pearce 1991) through applications to πN elastic scattering (Schutz 1994) and coupled-channel extensions including ππN and ηN (Schutz 1998, Krehl 1999, Gasparyan 2003). The addition of ωN enables investigation of the ωN scattering length, which is essential for understanding in-medium ω meson properties and neutron star equations of state, and addresses the open question of whether the ω can form bound states in nuclear matter, with previous QCD sum rule results (Koike 1996, Klingl 1997) supporting attraction while other analyses (Lutz 2001, Penner 2001-2002, Shklyar 2004) contradict this.
