# Exploring central opacity and asymptotic scenarios in elastic hadron scattering

> In the absence of a global description of the experimental data on elastic and soft diffractive scattering from the first principles of QCD, model-independent analyses may provide useful phenomenological insights for the development of the theory in the soft sector. With that in mind, we present an empirical study on the energy dependence of the ratio $X$ between the elastic and total cross sections; a quantity related to the evolution of the hadronic central opacity. The dataset comprises all the experimental information available on proton-proton and antiproton-proton scattering in the c.m. energy interval 5 GeV - 8 TeV. Generalizing previous works, we discuss four model-independent analytical parameterizations for $X$, consisting of sigmoid functions composed with elementary functions of the energy and three distinct asymptotic scenarios: either the standard black disk limit or scenarios above or below that limit. Our two main conclusions are the following: (1) although consistent with the experimental data, the black disk does not represent an unique solution; (2) the data reductions favor a semi-transparent scenario, with asymptotic average value for the ratio $\bar{X}$ = 0.30 $\pm$ 0.12. In this case, within the uncertainty, the asymptotic regime may already be reached around 1000 TeV. We present a comparative study of the two scenarios, including predictions for the inelastic channel (diffraction dissociation) and the ratio associated with the total cross-section and the elastic slope. Details on the selection of our empirical ansatz for $X$ and physical aspects related to a change of curvature in this quantity at 80 - 100 GeV, indicating the beginning of a saturation effect, are also presented and discussed.

## Key claim

Although the black disk limit (X = 0.5) is consistent with the experimental data on the ratio of elastic to total cross sections, it does not represent a unique solution; the data reductions favor a semi-transparent asymptotic scenario with an average value of X = 0.30 ± 0.12, which may already be reached around 1000 TeV within the uncertainty.

## Method

The authors performed an empirical study of the energy dependence of the ratio X = σ_el/σ_tot using all available proton-proton and antiproton-proton scattering data in the center-of-mass energy interval 5 GeV to 8 TeV (42 data points). They constructed four model-independent analytical parameterizations for X(s) by composing two sigmoid functions (logistic and hyperbolic tangent) with two elementary functions of the energy (a power-law and a logarithmic-law), resulting in fits with only three to five free parameters. Both constrained fits (with the asymptotic value A fixed to specific scenarios) and unconstrained fits (with A as a free parameter) were performed, and the results were extended to the inelastic channel and to the ratio Y = σ_tot/(16πB) associated with the total cross section and elastic slope.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic average value of the ratio X = σ_el/σ_tot from unconstrained fits is A = 0.292 ± 0.033 (logistic with power-law variant) and A = 0.292 ± 0.032 (logistic with logarithmic-law variant), with the overall average quoted as 0.30 ± 0.12.
- The asymptotic regime may be reached around 10^3 TeV (1000 TeV) within the uncertainty.
- A change from positive to negative curvature in X(s) is predicted at 80–100 GeV, indicating the beginning of a saturation effect.
- The TOTEM Collaboration data at 7 TeV give X values ranging from 0.252 ± 0.014 to 0.258 ± 0.013; at 8 TeV, X = 0.266 ± 0.016; the ATLAS Collaboration datum at 7 TeV gives X = 0.252 ± 0.004.
- An estimation from the Pierre Auger Collaboration at 57 TeV gives X = 0.31 (+0.17, -0.19), not used in the fits.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Fagundes, Menon, and Silva (2012–2015) that used smaller datasets and only the hyperbolic tangent sigmoid with particular power-law variants. The study generalizes those efforts by introducing four parameterizations (logistic and hyperbolic tangent combined with power-law and logarithmic-law variants) and investigating all three asymptotic scenarios: the standard black disk limit (A = 0.5), scenarios above the black disk (A = 0.75 or 1.0), and scenarios below the black disk (A = 0.436 from COMPETE and TOTEM parameterizations, or A = 0.3). The results enable predictions for inelastic diffraction dissociation and the ratio Y relevant to cosmic-ray extensive air shower studies, and they provide empirical constraints for nonperturbative QCD developments in the soft scattering sector.
