# Precision measurement and modelling of the threshold-free 210Pb β spectrum

> Beta decay is a fundamental process that governs nuclear stability and serves as a sensitive probe of the weak interaction and possible physics beyond the Standard Model of particle physics. However, precise measurements of complete $β$ decay spectra, particularly at low energies, remain experimentally and theoretically challenging. Here we report a high-precision, threshold-free measurement of the full $β$ decay spectrum of 210Pb to excited states of 210Bi, using a transition-edge sensor (TES)-based micro-calorimeter. This approach enables the detection of $β$ particle energies from 0 keV up to their endpoint by coincidence summing with subsequent de-excitation energy, thereby eliminating reconstruction artifacts near zero energy that have traditionally limited low-energy spectral accuracy. To our knowledge, this is the first complete, high-precision $β$ decay spectrum from 0 keV. The data resolve theoretical uncertainties associated with the atomic quantum exchange (AQE) effect. An accompanying ab initio theoretical framework, incorporating atomic, leptonic, and nuclear components, predicts a statistically significant (7.2 {$σ$}) enhancement in $β$ emission probability near zero energy, in agreement with the measurement and in contrast to models that omit AQE corrections. These results provide a new benchmark for $β$ decay theory at low energies, deepen our understanding of the weak interaction, and establish a critical foundation for searches for new physics, including dark matter interactions and precision studies of neutrinos.

# Precision measurement and modelling of the threshold-free 210Pb β spectrum

## Key claim
The authors report the first complete, high-precision β decay spectrum measured from 0 keV, using a transition-edge sensor micro-calorimeter to measure 210Pb decay to excited states of 210Bi. The data resolve theoretical uncertainties in the atomic quantum exchange (AQE) effect, and an accompanying ab initio theoretical framework predicts a 7.2σ enhancement in β emission probability near zero energy that agrees with measurement, but only when AQE corrections are included.

## Method
A transition-edge sensor (TES) micro-calorimeter with a 760-μm-diameter lead-tin spherical absorber (63% Sn, 37% Pb) was operated at 20 mK base temperature with the TES heated to approximately 80 mK. The 210Pb source was embedded naturally in the absorber, and the β decay to the first excited state of 210Bi (excitation energy 46.539 keV) was measured via coincidence summing of the β particle and subsequent de-excitation energy, shifting the β spectrum from [0, 17.0] keV to [46.5, 63.5] keV to eliminate threshold artifacts. The theoretical framework used relativistic local density approximation (RLDA) atomic wave functions, shell-model nuclear structure calculations with the NushellX@MSU code and khpe effective interaction, and a newly developed methodology for consistent bound and continuum electron wave functions.

## Key numbers
- The β-ES spectrum is shifted by ΔE = 46.539 (1) keV, enabling threshold-free measurement from 0 keV to the endpoint of 17.0 keV.
- Energy resolution of 142 eV at the 59.54 keV calibration peak of 241Am.
- Goodness-of-fit χ² = 831 with AQE and 883 without AQE, with 748 degrees of freedom, giving a statistical significance of 7.2σ for the AQE enhancement.
- Branching ratios determined as B_GS = 13.7 (5)% and B_ES = 86.3 (5)%, improving uncertainty by a factor of six over the previous evaluation.
- Signal-to-background ratio of 10.2 in the β-ES region.
- The 210Pb half-life is 22.20 (22) years; the 210Bi daughter has T₁/₂ = 5.012 (5) days and Q_β = 1161.2 (8) keV.
- The ES transition is dominated at ≥95% by the n(2g₉/₂) → p(1h₉/₂) nucleon-nucleon transition.

## Context
This work extends prior measurements of β spectra at low energies, which were limited to thresholds of 0.3 keV for 63Ni (Loidl, 2014) and 0.5 keV for 241Pu (Loidl, 2010), and addresses discrepancies observed in 99Tc (Paul, 2024) and 151Sm (Karsten, 2022) where emission probability exceeded AQE predictions below 5 keV. The results provide a new benchmark for β decay theory at low energies and directly constrain predictions for 212,214Pb β spectra, which are critical background sources in dark matter experiments such as LZ (2022), PandaX (2024), and XENON (2023). The increased low-energy β emission probability also implies identical enhancement for antineutrinos at high energy, potentially explaining reactor antineutrino anomalies (Boser, 2019).
