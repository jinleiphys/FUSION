# QCD Sum Rules for the Isospin-Breaking Axial Correlator with Correct Chiral Behavior

> We revisit the QCD sum-rule treatment of the isospin-breaking axial correlator in light of the recent claim that a previous treatment produced results incompatible with known chiral constraints. The source of the error in the previous analysis is identified, and a corrected version of the sum-rule treatment obtained. It is then shown that, using input from chiral perturbation theory, one may use the resulting sum rule to extract information on the leading chiral behavior of isospin-breaking parameters associated with the coupling of excited pseudoscalar resonances to the axial currents. A rather accurate extraction is possible for the case of the eta'. Demanding stability of the sum-rule analysis also allows us to improve the upper bound on the fourth-order low-energy constant, L_7.

# QCD Sum Rules for the Isospin-Breaking Axial Correlator with Correct Chiral Behavior

## Key claim

The previous QCD sum-rule treatment of the isospin-breaking axial correlator by CHM produced results incompatible with chiral constraints because it omitted a kinematic pole at q^2=0 in the dispersion relation; once this pole is included, the corrected sum rule restores the correct chiral behavior and allows extraction of isospin-breaking couplings of excited pseudoscalar resonances, with a reliable extraction possible for the eta-prime.

## Method

The authors identify that the scalar correlator Pi_2(q^2) has a kinematic pole at q^2=0, which was omitted in the previous dispersion relation for Pi_P(q^2) = Pi_1(q^2) - Pi_2(q^2). They write the corrected dispersion relation including this pole term, whose value is calculable from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). Using 1-loop ChPT results for the pion and eta contributions, they Borel-transform the corrected sum rule and expand to order O(p^4), obtaining a sum rule for the leading chiral behavior of the isospin-breaking parameters g_P of higher pseudoscalar resonances. They then solve the sum rule and its derivative simultaneously for g_eta-prime and an effective parameter representing the eta(1295), pi-prime(1300), eta(1440) region.

## Key numbers

* The corrected sum rule yields g_eta-prime / (theta_0 F^2) = 0.42 +/- 0.15, where theta_0 is the leading-order pi0-eta mixing angle and F is the pion decay constant in the chiral limit.
* The effective parameter for the eta(1295)/pi-prime(1300)/eta(1440) region gives g_pi-prime / (theta_0 F^2) = -0.13 +/- 0.07.
* The absolute values are g_eta-prime = (3.6 +/- 1.9) x 10^{-5} GeV^2 and g_pi-prime = (-1.1 +/- 0.8) x 10^{-5} GeV^2, using the isospin-breaking mass ratio r = (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) = 0.3 +/- 0.05.
* The sum rule improves the upper bound on the fourth-order low-energy constant L_7: stability is lost for |L_7| greater than about 0.48 x 10^{-3}, compared to the ChPT value L_7 = (-0.4 +/- 0.15) x 10^{-3}.
* The previous CHM sum-rule result for (g_eta - g_pi) was more than an order of magnitude smaller than the ChPT value.

## Context

This work corrects the earlier sum-rule analysis of the isospin-breaking axial correlator by CHM (C. H. M. et al.), which was shown by Maltman (1995) to produce results incompatible with chiral constraints. The corrected sum rule uses input from 1-loop ChPT by Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) to fix the low-lying pion and eta contributions, enabling extraction of isospin-breaking couplings of excited pseudoscalar resonances. The analysis demonstrates that the LEC L_7, which receives contributions only from flavor-singlet pseudoscalar states, dominates the sum rule and allows an improved upper bound on its magnitude. This work enables future studies of isospin-breaking effects in the pseudoscalar meson sector using QCD sum rules with correct chiral behavior.
