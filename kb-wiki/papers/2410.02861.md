# QCD critical point: recent developments

> Recent developments aimed at mapping QCD phase diagram and the search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions are briefly reviewed.

## Key claim

Recent theoretical estimates using diverse methods (Padé resummations, conformal maps, hybrid lattice/gauge-gravity, and functional renormalization group) all point to the existence of the QCD critical point in the same region of the phase diagram, but the quantitative properties of the critical point singularity remain unknown and must be constrained by experimental data.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to locating the QCD critical point on the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter, including Padé resummations of Taylor expansion in baryon chemical potential (μ_B) from lattice calculations, conformal mapping techniques, hybrid lattice plus gauge-gravity correspondence, and functional renormalization group methods. It then compares the qualitative features of recent STAR BES-II experimental data on factorial cumulants of proton multiplicity fluctuations to equilibrium expectations for critical fluctuations, and discusses developments in parametric equations of state, hydrodynamic trajectories, non-equilibrium fluctuation evolution, and freezeout via the maximum entropy method.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical estimates place the critical point at T_c ~ 100-110 MeV and μ_c ~ 420-650 MeV, consistent across multiple methods
- The BES-II experiment explored collision energies down to √s ~ 7.7 GeV, corresponding to freezeout μ_B up to approximately 420 MeV
- The parametric equation of state from Kahangirwe (2024) can be used for hydrodynamic calculations up to μ_B = 700 MeV
- The n=3 factorial cumulant shows a peak at around √s = 11 GeV
- The n=4 factorial cumulant shows a dip at √s ≈ 19 GeV
- The monotonic decrease of the n=2 cumulant is interrupted at √s ≲ 11 GeV

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for the QCD critical point search discussed in Bzdak (2019) and Du (2024), and interprets the latest STAR BES-II results presented at CPOD 2024 by Pandav. The work builds on parametric equations of state introduced by Parotto (2018) and improved by Kahangirwe (2024) using the T' expansion scheme from Borsanyi (2021), and on the maximum entropy method for freezeout developed by Pradeep (2022). The results enable future quantitative comparisons between dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions incorporating critical fluctuations and experimental data, which could determine or tightly constrain the non-universal parameters of the QCD equation of state near the critical point.
