# Asymmetric nuclear matter:the role of the isovector scalar channel

> We try to single out some qualitative new effects of the coupling to the $δ$-isovector-scalar meson introduced in a minimal way in a phenomenological hadronic field theory. Results for the equation of state ($EOS$) and the phase diagram of asymmetric nuclear matter ($ANM$) are discussed. We stress the consistency of the $δ$-coupling introduction in a relativistic approach. New contributions to the slope and curvature of the symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting appear particularly interesting. A more repulsive $EOS$ for neutron matter at high baryon densities is expected. Effects on new critical properties of warm $ANM$, mixing of mechanical and chemical instabilities and isospin distillation, are also presented. The $δ$ influence is mostly on the {\it isovectorlike} collective response. The results are largely analytical and this makes the physical meaning quite transparent. Implications for nuclear structure properties of drip-line nuclei and for reaction dynamics with Radioactive Beams are finally pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including the isovector scalar $\delta$-meson in a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model introduces qualitatively new effects on the symmetry energy, neutron-proton effective mass splitting, and phase diagram of asymmetric nuclear matter, but the authors stress that the results are largely analytical and the $\delta$ influence is mostly on the *isovectorlike* collective response, with the caveat that the chosen $\delta$ coupling constant ($f_\delta = 2.5\ \text{fm}^2$) is "relatively well justified" but the aim is "just to show the main qualitative new effects."

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic field theory with minimal self-interacting terms only in the isoscalar scalar $\sigma$-channel, including isoscalar ($\sigma$, $\omega$) and isovector ($\delta$, $\rho$) mesons. They solve the field equations in the mean-field approximation at zero and finite temperature to compute the equation of state, symmetry energy, effective masses, and instability conditions for asymmetric nuclear matter, using two parameter sets: Set I (only $\rho$ meson) and Set II ($\rho$ plus $\delta$ meson), both fitted to the same bulk symmetry energy $a_4 = 30.5\ \text{MeV}$ at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$, binding energy $E/A = -16\ \text{MeV}$, nucleon effective mass $M^\star = 0.75 M_N$ ($M_N = 939\ \text{MeV}$), incompressibility $K_V = 240\ \text{MeV}$ at $\rho_0$.
- Critical temperature of symmetric matter: $T_c = 15.86\ \text{MeV}$.
- Bulk symmetry energy: $a_4 = 30.5\ \text{MeV}$.
- Slope parameter $L$: $L(\rho) = +84\ \text{MeV}$ (only $\rho$) to $L(\rho+\delta) = +103\ \text{MeV}$ (with $\delta$).
- Curvature parameter $K_{sym}$: $K_{sym}(\rho) = +7\ \text{MeV}$ (only kinetic) to $K_{sym}(\rho+\delta) = +120\ \text{MeV}$ (with $\delta$).
- Combination $(K_{sym} - 6L)$: $-497\ \text{MeV}$ (only $\rho$) and $-504\ \text{MeV}$ (with $\delta$).
- Neutron-proton effective mass splitting at normal density $\rho_0$ for $\alpha = 0.5$ ($N = 3Z$): approximately $15\%$.
- $\delta$ coupling constant in Set II: $f_\delta = 2.5\ \text{fm}^2$; $\rho$ coupling constant in Set II: $f_\rho = 3.15\ \text{fm}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the standard RMF approach, which previously included only the isovector vector $\rho$-meson (leading to a linear increase of symmetry energy with density and no neutron-proton effective mass splitting, as noted in Yst (1998) and Mueller and Serot (1995)), by adding the isovector scalar $\delta$-meson exchange. The $\delta$-meson is an essential ingredient of realistic NN potentials and has been suggested in earlier work by Kubis and Kutschera (1997) and in relativistic Brueckner theory by Schiller et al. (1997) and by Hofmann et al. (2001). The results enable further studies of neutron skin thickness in neutron-rich nuclei (related to the symmetry pressure, as discussed by Brown (2000), Typel and Baur (2001), and Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001)), the stability of drip-line nuclei, and the isospin distillation effect in heavy-ion collisions, where the chemical instability can be probed experimentally via the N/Z ratio of fragments.
