# Multiplicative renormalizability of gluon and ghost propagators in QCD

> We reformulate the coupled set of continuum equations for the renormalized gluon and ghost propagators in QCD, such that the multiplicative renormalizability of the solutions is manifest, independently of the specific form of full vertices and renormalization constants. In the Landau gauge, the equations are free of renormalization constants, and the renormalization point dependence enters only through the renormalized coupling and the renormalized propagator functions. The structure of the equations enables us to devise novel truncations with solutions that are multiplicatively renormalizable and agree with the leading order perturbative results. We show that, for infrared power law behaved propagators, the leading infrared behavior of the gluon equation is not solely determined by the ghost loop, as concluded in previous studies, but that the gluon loop, the three-gluon loop, the four-gluon loop, and even massless quarks also contribute to the infrared analysis. In our new Landau gauge truncation, the combination of gluon and ghost loop contributions seems to reject infrared power law solutions, but massless quark loops illustrate how additional contributions to the gluon vacuum polarization could reinstate these solutions. Moreover, a schematic study of the three-gluon and four-gluon loops shows that they too need to be considered in more detail before a definite conclusion about the existence of infrared power behaved gluon and ghost propagators can be reached.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for renormalized gluon and ghost propagators in QCD so that multiplicative renormalizability is manifest, and shows that, contrary to previous studies, the leading infrared behavior of the gluon equation is not determined solely by the ghost loop; the gluon loop, three-gluon loop, four-gluon loop, and even massless quark loops also contribute, and in the new truncation the combination of gluon and ghost loops seems to reject infrared power-law solutions unless additional contributions (e.g., from massless quarks) are included.

## Method

The author rewrites the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the renormalized gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge, eliminating renormalization constants via subtraction at two momenta and using the Taylor identity (tilde Z_1 = 1) to make multiplicative renormalizability explicit. A bare-vertex truncation is then applied to the reformulated equations, and the infrared behavior is analyzed by assuming power-law forms for the propagators and solving the resulting consistency conditions for the exponent kappa and the infrared fixed-point coupling alpha_0. The analysis is extended to include massless quark loops and a schematic discussion of three-gluon and four-gluon loops.

## Key numbers

* The leading ultraviolet logarithmic behavior yields anomalous dimensions: gluon form factor exponent gamma = -13/22, ghost form factor exponent delta = -9/44, and beta_0 = 11N_c/3 for the pure gauge case (N_c = 3).
* For the pure gauge theory (no quarks) in the bare approximation, the gluon equation gives chi_gl(kappa) < 0 for all convergent kappa, while the ghost equation gives chi_gh(kappa) > 0, so no consistent infrared power-law solution exists.
* With massless quarks, a consistent infrared power-law solution first appears at a critical number of flavors N_f approximately 8.9, yielding kappa approximately 0.76.
* For N_f = 9, two solutions exist: kappa = 0.69 and 0.82; for N_f = 10: kappa = 0.49 and 0.90; for N_f = 11: kappa = 0.37 and 0.93; for N_f = 12: kappa = 0.28 and 0.94; as N_f goes to infinity, the two kappa solutions approach 0 and 1, with corresponding alpha_0 values 0 and 4pi/3.

## Context

This work extends the coupled Dyson-Schwinger studies of von Smekal, Hauck, and Alkofer (1998) and Atkinson and Bloch (1998), which found infrared power-law solutions for gluon and ghost propagators with the ghost loop dominating the gluon equation. The paper shows that a multiplicatively renormalizable treatment of the gluon loop (neglected or approximated in those earlier works) changes the infrared analysis, and that additional diagrams (three-gluon, four-gluon, and quark loops) must be considered before concluding whether infrared power-law solutions exist. This enables future work to construct truncations that respect both multiplicative renormalizability and the correct perturbative limit, and to study the role of quark masses and higher-loop contributions in determining the infrared behavior of QCD propagators.
