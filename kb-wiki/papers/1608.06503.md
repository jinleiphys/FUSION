# Hadronic Lorentz Violation in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Any possible Lorentz violation in the hadron sector must be tied to Lorentz violation at the underlying quark level. The relationships between the theories at these two levels are studied using chiral perturbation theory. Starting from a two-flavor quark theory that includes dimension-four Lorentz-violation operators, the effective Lagrangians are derived for both pions and nucleons, with novel terms appearing in both sectors. Since the Lorentz violation coefficients for nucleons and pions are all related to a single set of underlying quark coefficients, it is possible to place approximate bounds on pion Lorentz violation using only proton and neutron observations. The resulting bounds on four pion parameters are at the $10^{-23}$ level, representing improvements of ten orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper derives the relationships between quark-level and hadron-level Lorentz-violating parameters in the Standard-Model Extension (SME) using chiral perturbation theory, showing that the Lorentz violation coefficients for pions and nucleons are all related to a single set of underlying quark coefficients. This allows approximate bounds on pion Lorentz violation at the $10^{-23}$ level to be inferred from proton and neutron observations, representing improvements of ten orders of magnitude over direct pion constraints, though the bounds are only order-of-magnitude estimates because the low-energy constants (LECs) are not precisely known.

## Method

Starting from a two-flavor quark theory with dimension-four CPT-even Lorentz-violating operators, the authors construct the effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons at leading order in chiral perturbation theory, matching the symmetry properties of the quark-level operators onto hadronic degrees of freedom. They identify all chirally invariant terms consistent with the discrete symmetries C, P, and T, and reduce the number of independent terms using integration by parts, symmetry properties of the $C_{\mu\nu}$ tensors, and the equations of motion for nucleons. The resulting effective Lagrangians contain novel Lorentz-violating multi-pion interaction terms and nucleon operators with independent low-energy constants $\beta^{(1)}$ and $\alpha^{(n)}$, which encode short-distance physics.

## Key numbers

- The pion Lorentz-violation tensor is $k_{\pi}^{\mu\nu} = \beta^{(1)}(c_{u_L}^{\mu\nu} + c_{u_R}^{\mu\nu} + c_{d_L}^{\mu\nu} + c_{d_R}^{\mu\nu})$, where $\beta^{(1)}$ is a dimensionless low-energy constant expected to be $\mathcal{O}(1)$ by naive dimensional analysis.
- The pion decay constant in the $SU(2)$ chiral limit is $F \approx 92.4$ MeV.
- Bounds on four pion Lorentz-violation parameters are at the $10^{-23}$ level, representing improvements of ten orders of magnitude over direct astrophysical bounds which are at the $10^{-10}$ to $10^{-13}$ levels.
- Direct astrophysical bounds on combinations of $k_{\pi}^{\mu\nu}$ are at the $\sim m_{\pi}^2/E^2$ level, where $E$ is the energy of observed quanta (e.g., TeV gamma rays).

## Context

This work extends the Standard-Model Extension (SME) framework of Kostelecky and collaborators by using chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg, 1979; Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984, 1985) to relate quark-level and hadron-level Lorentz violation, building on earlier work by Noordmans (2015) that examined a different set of terms. The results enable translation of precise bounds on proton and neutron Lorentz violation from atomic clock experiments (Prestage, 1985; Lamoreaux, 1989; Chupp, 1989; Wolf, 2006; Smiciklas, 2011) into constraints on pion Lorentz violation, and also predict new Lorentz-violating multi-pion interactions that have not been previously studied. Future work could extend the analysis to dimension-three operators, include gluon-sector Lorentz violation, and incorporate couplings to external electromagnetic fields.
