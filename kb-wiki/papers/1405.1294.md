# Proton polarizabilities from polarized Compton scattering: low-energy expansion

> We reexamine the low-energy expansion of polarized Compton scattering off the proton and show that the leading non-Born contribution to the beam asymmetry of low-energy Compton scattering is given by the magnetic polarizability alone, the electric polarizability cancels out. Based on this fact we propose to determine the magnetic dipole polarizability of the proton from the beam asymmetry. We also present the low-energy expansion of doubly-polarized observables, from which the spin polarizabilities can be extracted.

## Key claim

The leading non-Born contribution to the beam asymmetry of low-energy Compton scattering is given by the magnetic polarizability alone, because the electric polarizability cancels out at leading order in the low-energy expansion; this allows a model-independent determination of the magnetic dipole polarizability of the proton from beam asymmetry measurements, provided the photon energy is below about 100 MeV and scattering angles are forward.

## Method

The author reexamines the low-energy expansion (LEX) of polarized Compton scattering off the proton, deriving the invariant amplitudes for the non-Born contribution from an effective Lagrangian that includes the scalar polarizabilities α_E1 and β_M1. The beam asymmetry Σ_3 and the doubly-polarized observables Σ_2x and Σ_2z are expanded in photon energy, and the leading-order LEX results are compared with next-next-to-leading order (NNLO) baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) calculations from Lensky (2009) to assess the region of applicability.

## Key numbers

- PDG values for proton scalar polarizabilities: α_E1^(p) = (12.0 ± 0.6) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β_M1^(p) = (2.5 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Forward spin polarizability: γ_0 = (−1.0 ± 0.08 ± 0.1) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Backward spin polarizability: γ_π = (8.0 ± 1.8) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- The leading LEX result for beam asymmetry is valid at photon beam energy of 100 MeV (good agreement with BChPT, especially at forward angles) but fails at 135 MeV
- The LEX for beam-target asymmetries Σ_2x and Σ_2z is applicable only for photon energies below 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the low-energy expansion formalism for Compton scattering, building on the effective Lagrangian approach and the invariant amplitude decomposition used in chiral perturbation theory calculations by Lensky (2009) and the review by Griesshammer (2012). The key finding that the electric polarizability cancels in the beam asymmetry at leading order resolves a long-standing difficulty in extracting β_M1 independently, which had been problematic because the unpolarized cross section is dominated by α_E1. The results enable a model-independent extraction of β_M1 from planned measurements at the MESA facility in Mainz, and provide low-energy tests for chiral perturbation theory and dispersion relation frameworks used to extract spin polarizabilities at higher energies around the Delta resonance region.
