# Microscopic determination of the interacting boson-fermion model Hamiltonian from the nuclear energy density functional

> A microscopic formulation of the interacting boson-fermion model for odd-$A$ nuclei is made using the nuclear energy density functional framework. Strength parameters for the bosonic Hamiltonian and boson-fermion interactions are shown to be determined completely so that energy surfaces and deformed single-particle energies of the Bose-Fermi systems should match the corresponding self-consistent mean-field solutions for fermionic systems. In an illustrative application to axially symmetric odd-$A$ Eu, this procedure is shown to be valid in describing spherical-to-deformed shape phase transitions in odd-$A$ and even-even systems.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to determine all strength parameters of the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) Hamiltonian microscopically from nuclear energy density functional (EDF) calculations, without any fitting to experimental data. The method is shown to be valid for describing spherical-to-deformed shape phase transitions in odd-\(A\) Eu isotopes, but the mapping of deformed single-particle energies requires a separate scale factor for the boson-fermion interaction in nuclei with equilibrium deformation \(\beta_e \gtrsim 0.2\).

## Method

The authors perform constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Skyrme SkM* force to obtain potential energy curves (PECs) for even-even Sm core nuclei and deformed single-particle energies for odd-\(A\) Eu nuclei. The IBM Hamiltonian parameters \(\{\epsilon_d, \kappa, \chi, c_b\}\) are determined by mapping the HFB PEC onto the bosonic energy surface of the IBM. The boson-fermion interaction strengths \(\{A, \Gamma, \Lambda\}\) are then fixed by matching the IBFM single-particle energies \(\lambda_K(\beta)\) to the HFB single-particle energies \(\epsilon_K(\beta)\) at the equilibrium deformation \(\beta_e\), with an additional scale factor \(c_{bf}\) allowed for deformed nuclei with \(N \geq 90\).

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(R_{4/2} = E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)\) for even-even \(^{148-158}\)Sm is calculated as 2.49, 2.79, 3.13, 3.29, 3.32, and 3.34, compared to experimental values 2.15, 2.32, 3.00, 3.30, 3.29, and 3.29.
- The equilibrium deformation \(\beta_e\) jumps from approximately 0.15 to 0.20 at neutron number \(N=90\) for Eu isotopes, and increases to \(\beta_e \approx 0.25\) for \(^{157}\)Eu and \(^{159}\)Eu.
- The ground-state spin of odd-\(A\) Eu changes from \(I = 11/2^-\) to \(5/2^-\) at \(N=90\), consistent with experiment.
- The depth of the HFB PEC minimum is approximately 1 MeV for \(^{149}\)Eu and \(^{151}\)Eu.
- The derived scale factor \(c_{bf}\) for deformed nuclei with \(N \geq 90\) is approximately equal to unity.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic foundation of the interacting boson model (IBM) for even-even nuclei (Nomura 2008) to the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) for odd-\(A\) nuclei. Previous IBFM calculations (Scholten 1985, Nomura 2016, Nomura 2017) determined boson-fermion interaction strengths by fitting to experimental data, whereas this method derives all parameters from the EDF-SCMF framework. The approach enables spectroscopic predictions for heavy odd-\(A\) nuclei far from stability, including those relevant for \(\beta\) and \(\beta\beta\) decay studies, and can be extended to include intruder states and configuration mixing as recently done for odd-\(A\) Nb nuclei (Gavrielov 2022, Leviatan 2025).
