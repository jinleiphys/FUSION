# Observation of the critical end point in the phase diagram for hot and dense nuclear matter

> Excitation functions for the Gaussian emission source radii difference ($R^2_{\text{out}} - R^2_{\text{side}}$) obtained from two-pion interferometry measurements in Au+Au ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 7.7 - 200$ GeV) and Pb+Pb ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76$ TeV) collisions, are studied for a broad range of collision centralities. The observed non-monotonic excitation functions validate the finite-size scaling patterns expected for the deconfinement phase transition and the critical end point (CEP), in the temperature vs. baryon chemical potential ($T,μ_B$) plane of the nuclear matter phase diagram. A Finite-Size Scaling (FSS) analysis of these data indicate a second order phase transition with the estimates $T^{\text{cep}} \sim 165$~MeV and $μ_B^{\text{cep}} \sim 95$~MeV for the location of the critical end point. The critical exponents ($ν\sim 0.66$ and $γ\sim 1.2$) extracted via the same FSS analysis, places the CEP in the 3D Ising model universality class.

## Key claim

The non-monotonic excitation functions for the Gaussian emission source radii difference (R²_out - R²_side) from two-pion interferometry validate the finite-size scaling patterns expected for the deconfinement phase transition and the critical end point (CEP), but the paper hedges that these are "initial estimates" and that "further detailed studies at RHIC are crucial to make a more precise determination."

## Method

Excitation functions for the Gaussian emission source radii difference (R²_out - R²_side) were constructed from two-pion interferometry measurements in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 - 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, for multiple centrality selections. A Finite-Size Scaling (FSS) analysis was performed by associating the peak positions, heights, and widths of these excitation functions with the characteristic transverse size R-bar obtained from Monte Carlo Glauber calculations, using power-law scaling relations for the susceptibility magnitude, peak position shift, and width as functions of system size.

## Key numbers

- CEP location estimates: T_cep ~ 165 MeV and mu_B_cep ~ 95 MeV
- Critical exponent nu ~ 0.66 extracted from the peak position scaling with R-bar
- Critical exponent gamma ~ 1.2 extracted from the peak magnitude scaling with R-bar
- The asymptotic peak position sqrt(s_NN)(infinity) ~ 47.5 GeV from the finite-size scaling fit
- The data span sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 - 200 GeV for Au+Au and sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- The transverse size R-bar systematic uncertainties are less than 10%

## Context

This work extends prior observations of non-monotonic excitation functions for viscous damping (Lacey 2013, Lacey 2014) and for the radii difference (Lacey 2014, Adare 2014) by providing a quantitative Finite-Size Scaling analysis that attributes these patterns to the deconfinement phase transition and CEP. The extracted critical exponents place the CEP in the 3D Ising model universality class, consistent with expectations from lattice QCD and model calculations (Fodor 2004, Li 2011, Nagata 2014, deForcrand 2014). The results enable future beam energy scan measurements at RHIC to refine the CEP location and test for similar scaling in other collision systems.
