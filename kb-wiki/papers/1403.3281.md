# Electromagnetism in Quark-Antiquark Bound States

> Non-perturbative proof is presented of a unique version of Goldstone theorem, that electromagnetism contributes to the masses of spinless particles of quark-antiquark bound states in the form of a commutator of the quark electric matrix and a coefficient factor which may be expressed simply in terms of a three-body Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, in the case where the Lagrangian conserves an approximate $SU(3)$ chiral symmetry. The coupled Bethe-Salpeter equation and Dyson-Schwinger equation in the ladder-rainbow approximation are layed out with every ingredient renormalized, which are applied to the problem of evaluating the mass differences of some charged and neutral pseudoscalar and vector mesons. The numerical results yield satisfactory agreement with experimental observations for the mass spectrum and meson decay constants. Estimates of the light quark masses are also given in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative proof that electromagnetism contributes to the masses of spinless quark-antiquark bound states via a commutator of the quark electric charge matrix with a coefficient factor expressed in terms of a three-body Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, but only in the case where the Lagrangian conserves an approximate SU(3) chiral symmetry; the numerical results are obtained under the ladder-rainbow approximation with a specific Ansatz for the strong effective interaction.

## Method

The authors derive a generalized axial-vector Ward identity that includes electromagnetic corrections, then extract an exact mass formula for pseudo-Goldstone bosons by equating pole terms in the Green's functions of three currents (axial-vector, pseudoscalar, and electromagnetic pseudoscalar). They solve the coupled Bethe-Salpeter equation and Dyson-Schwinger equation in the ladder-rainbow approximation with renormalization, using the Maris-Tandy effective interaction for the strong sector and one-photon exchange for the electromagnetic sector, with Chebyshev expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass fitted to \(m_{\pi^0}=135\) MeV for the neutral pion, used as input to determine the degenerate u and d quark masses
- Fine structure constant \(\alpha\) taken approximately as \(1/137\)
- Maris-Tandy model parameters: \(\Lambda=234\) MeV, \(m_t=0.5\) GeV, \(n_f=4\), with renormalization scale \(\mu=19\) GeV
- The common electromagnetic correction \(\Delta\) appears in the mass formulae for \(\pi^\pm\) and \(K^\pm\) squared masses: \(m_{\pi^\pm}^2 = 2v(m_u+m_d)/F^2 + \Delta\), \(m_{K^\pm}^2 = 2v(m_u+m_s)/F^2 + \Delta\)

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative Bethe-Salpeter approach to meson structure by incorporating electromagnetic effects alongside strong interactions, building on earlier chiral perturbation theory treatments by Dashen (1969) and Langacker (1973) and on Bethe-Salpeter calculations by Jain (1993). The formalism enables computation of electromagnetic mass splittings between charged and neutral pseudoscalar and vector mesons, and provides a framework for extracting light quark masses from hadron observables, complementing lattice simulations by Blum (2007, 2010).
