# A QCD-like theory with the ZNc symmetry

> We propose a QCD-like theory with the ZNc symmetry. The flavor-dependent twisted boundary condition (TBC) is imposed on Nc degenerate flavor quarksin the SU(Nc) gauge theory. The QCD-like theory is useful to understand the mechanism of color confinement. Dynamics of the QCD-like theory is studied by imposing the TBC on the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The TBC model is applied to two- and three-color cases. The ZNc symmetry is preserved below some temperature Tc, but spontaneously broken above Tc. The color confinement below Tc preserves the flavor symmetry. Above Tc, the flavor symmetry is broken, but the breaking is suppressed by the entanglement between the Polyakov loop and the chiral condensate. Particularly at low temperature, dynamics of the TBC model is similar to that of the PNJL model with the standard fermion boundary condition, indicating that the ZNc symmetry is a good approximate concept in the latter model even if the current quark mass is small. The present prediction can be tested in future by lattice QCD, since the QCD-like theory has no sign problem.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a QCD-like theory in which the Z_{N_c} symmetry is an exact internal symmetry, achieved by imposing a flavor-dependent twisted boundary condition (TBC) on N_c degenerate flavor quarks in SU(N_c) gauge theory; this theory is identical to QCD at zero temperature and preserves Z_{N_c} symmetry below a critical temperature T_c, with the claim that Z_{N_c} is a good approximate concept in standard QCD even for small current quark masses.

## Method

The authors impose a flavor-dependent twisted boundary condition (TBC) on N_c degenerate flavor quarks in SU(N_c) gauge theory, where the temporal boundary condition for each flavor f is q_f(x, beta) = -exp(i theta_f) q_f(x, 0) with theta_f = theta_1 + 2pi(f-1)/N_c. They study the dynamics by implementing this TBC in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, applying the resulting "TBC model" to the cases N_c = N_f = 2 and N_c = N_f = 3, solving the mean-field thermodynamic potential via stationary conditions.

## Key numbers

- For N_c = 2 with (theta_u, theta_d) = (-pi/2, pi/2): the deconfinement transition occurs at T_c approximately 260 MeV, where the Polyakov loop Phi becomes nonzero above this temperature.
- For N_c = 2 with (theta_u, theta_d) = (0, pi): the deconfinement transition occurs at T_c approximately 235 MeV for alpha = 0, and at T_c approximately 265 MeV for alpha = 0.2.
- For N_c = 2: the Polyakov potential parameters are a = 858.1 MeV and b^{1/3} = 210.5 MeV, yielding a second-order deconfinement phase transition at T_c = 270 MeV in the pure gauge theory.
- For N_c = 3: the Polyakov potential parameters are a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_3 = -1.75, with T_0 rescaled to 195 MeV to reproduce T_c = 160 MeV from full lattice QCD.
- For N_c = 2: the NJL parameters are m_0 = 5.4 MeV, Lambda = 657 MeV, G_s = 7.23 GeV^{-2}, yielding -<bar u u>^{1/3} = 218 MeV, f_pi = 75.4 MeV, and m_pi = 140 MeV at vacuum.

## Context

This work extends the study of Z_{N_c} symmetry in QCD, building on the Roberge-Weiss (RW) periodicity established by lattice QCD (FP, D'Elia, Chen, and others) and the extended Z_{N_c} symmetry in QCD at imaginary chemical potential. The TBC model enables a theory where Z_{N_c} is an exact internal symmetry rather than an external one, allowing the authors to investigate the interplay between confinement and flavor symmetry breaking. The predictions can be tested by future lattice QCD simulations since the QCD-like theory has no sign problem, and the results clarify the role of Z_{N_c} as an approximate symmetry in standard PNJL models (Sakai, Kashiwa, and others).
