# Nuclear charge radii of aluminium isotopes at the proton drip line

> Understanding the evolution of nuclear size away from stability remains a central challenge in nuclear physics. In neutron-deficient systems, charge radii can be highly sensitive to the interplay between strong and electromagnetic interactions, and the effects of weak binding, giving rise to exotic nuclear phenomena. However, experimental data on these systems has been limited by short lifetimes and low production rates. Here we report the first laser-spectroscopy measurements of nuclear charge radii along the neutron-deficient aluminium isotopic chain, from $^{25}$Al to the proton-drip-line nucleus $^{22}$Al, using the {Resonance Ionization Spectroscopy Experiment} (RISE) at the {Facility for Rare Isotope Beams} (FRIB). Our measurements reveal a step-like increase in charge radius toward the drip line, with similar radii for $^{22,\,23}$Al. A comparison of our results with those of their mirror partners reveals an almost identical correlation with the calculated proton skins and is consistent with the systematic trend of well-bound nuclei. These results offer insight for understanding the evolution of nuclear size at the proton dripline and place important constraints on modern nuclear theory. They also demonstrate the unique combined capabilities of RISE and FRIB to probe the structures of previously inaccessible nuclei at the limits of existence.

## Key claim

The first laser-spectroscopy measurements of nuclear charge radii from $^{25}$Al down to the proton-drip-line nucleus $^{22}$Al reveal a step-like increase toward the drip line, with $^{22}$Al and $^{23}$Al having similar radii within uncertainties; this trend does not indicate a pronounced proton-halo configuration in $^{22}$Al, consistent with the valence proton predominantly occupying a $d_{5/2}$ orbital ($\ell=2$) where the centrifugal and Coulomb barriers suppress the strong near-threshold enhancement expected for an $s$-wave halo.

## Method

The Resonance Ionization Spectroscopy Experiment (RISE) at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB) performed collinear resonance ionization spectroscopy on bunched, neutralized aluminium atoms produced by fragmentation of a $^{28}$Si primary beam. Hyperfine spectra of the $3s^23p\ ^2P_{1/2} \rightarrow 3s^25s\ ^2S_{1/2}$ transition were measured for $^{22,23,24,25}$Al using a pulsed narrow-linewidth Ti:Sa laser for resonant excitation at 265 nm and a pulsed Nd:YAG laser at 532 nm for non-resonant ionization, with isotope shifts extracted by Doppler tuning the ion bunch velocity via a variable-potential charge-exchange cell. Differential mean-square charge radii were obtained from the measured isotope shifts using calculated field-shift and mass-shift constants, and compared with predictions from nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) and valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Isotope shift for $^{25}$Al relative to $^{27}$Al: $-2.5(17)[39]$ MHz, yielding $\delta\langle r^2_\mathrm{ch}\rangle = -0.01(2)[6]\{9\}$ fm$^2$ and absolute charge radius $3.060(18)$ fm
- Isotope shift for $^{24}$Al (ground state, $I^\pi=4^+$): $-2.6(25)[39]$ MHz, yielding $\delta\langle r^2_\mathrm{ch}\rangle = 0.01(4)[6]\{14\}$ fm$^2$ and absolute charge radius $3.063(25)$ fm
- Isotope shift for $^{24\mathrm{m}}$Al (isomer, $I^\pi=1^+$): $4.0(28)[39]$ MHz, yielding $\delta\langle r^2_\mathrm{ch}\rangle = 0.10(4)[6]\{14\}$ fm$^2$ and absolute charge radius $3.078(25)$ fm; isomer shift $\delta\nu^{24\mathrm{m},24}=6.6(29)$ MHz corresponding to $\delta R_{\mathrm{ch}}=0.015(6)$ fm
- Isotope shift for $^{23}$Al: $5.0(22)[39]$ MHz, yielding $\delta\langle r^2_\mathrm{ch}\rangle = 0.14(3)[6]\{19\}$ fm$^2$ and absolute charge radius $3.083(33)$ fm
- Isotope shift for $^{22}$Al: $2.6(51)[39]$ MHz, yielding $\delta\langle r^2_\mathrm{ch}\rangle = 0.12(7)[6]\{25\}$ fm$^2$ and absolute charge radius $3.081(43)$ fm
- Reference charge radius of $^{27}$Al: $R_{\mathrm{ch}} = 3.0614(29)$ fm based on a recent reevaluation by Ohayon (2025)
- Field-shift constant $F = 70.11(13)$ MHz/fm$^2$ and mass-shift constant $K = -0.7(21)$ GHz$\cdot$u from Skripnikov (2024)
- Proton separation energy of $^{22}$Al: $100.4(8)$ keV from Campbell (2024) and Sun (2024)

## Context

This work provides the first experimental charge radii for neutron-deficient aluminium isotopes, extending laser-spectroscopy measurements to the proton drip line where data were previously unavailable due to short lifetimes and low production rates. The results constrain modern ab initio calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions, including NLEFT and VS-IMSRG approaches, and complement recent measurements confirming $^{22}$Al as the last bound aluminium isotope by Kostyleva (2024). The finding that $^{22}$Al and $^{23}$Al have similar radii, together with the spin assignment of $4^+$ from Jensen (2026) that disfavors a halo configuration, supports the interpretation that the observed radii reflect gradual evolution of the proton distribution rather than a strongly developed halo tail, in contrast to expectations from earlier suggestions of a possible proton halo in $^{22}$Al by Lee (2020) and $^{23}$Al by Cai (2002).
