# Study of the leptonic decays of pseudoscalar $B, D$ and vector $B^*, D^*$ mesons and of the semileptonic $B\to D$ and $B\to D^*$ decays

> We present results for different observables in weak decays of pseudoscalar and vector mesons with a heavy $c$ or $b$ quark. The calculations are done in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model improved at some instances by heavy quark effective theory constraints. We determine pseudoscalar and vector meson decay constants that within a few per cent satisfy $f_V M_V/f_P M_P=1$, a result expected in heavy quark symmetry when the heavy quark masses tend to infinity. We also analyze the semileptonic $B\to D$ and $B\to D^*$ decays for which we evaluate the different form factors. Here we impose heavy quark effective theory constraints among form factors that are not satisfied by a direct quark model calculation. The value of the form factors at zero recoil allows us to determine, by comparison with experimental data, the value of the $|V_{cb}|$ Cabbibo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element. From the $B\to D$ semileptonic decay we get $|V_{cb}|=0.040\pm0.006$ in perfect agreement with our previous determination based on the study of the semileptonic $Λ_b\to Λ_c$ decay and also in excellent agreement with a recent experimental determination by the DELPHI Collaboration. We further make use of the partial conservation of axial current hypothesis to determine the strong coupling constants $g_{B^*Bπ}(0)=60.5\pm 1.1$ and $g_{D^*Dπ}(0)=22.1\pm0.4$. The ratio $R=(g_{B^*Bπ}(0) f_{B^*}\sqrt{M_D})/ (g_{D^*Dπ}(0) f_{D^*}\sqrt{M_B})=1.105\pm0.005$ agrees with the heavy quark symmetry prediction of 1.

## Key claim

The authors determine the CKM matrix element \(|V_{cb}| = 0.040 \pm 0.006\) from the semileptonic \(B \to D\) decay, but this result depends on imposing heavy quark effective theory (HQET) constraints among form factors that are not satisfied by a direct nonrelativistic constituent quark model calculation alone.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with five different interquark potentials (BHAD, AL1, AL2, AP1, AP2) to compute meson wave functions and decay observables. For the semileptonic \(B \to D\) and \(B \to D^*\) decays, they extract the Isgur-Wise function \(\xi(w)\) from the quark model form factor \(h_+(w)\) using HQET relations, then re-evaluate the other form factors from that function. The strong coupling constants \(g_{B^*B\pi}(0)\) and \(g_{D^*D\pi}(0)\) are determined using the partial conservation of axial current (PCAC) hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- \(|V_{cb}| = 0.040 \pm 0.006\) from the \(B \to D\) semileptonic decay, using the authors' calculated \(F_D(1) = 1.04\) and experimental values of \(|V_{cb}|F_D(1)\)
- Pseudoscalar decay constants: \(f_D = 243^{+21}_{-17}\) MeV, \(f_{D_s} = 341^{+7}_{-5}\) MeV, \(f_B = 155^{+15}_{-12}\) MeV, \(f_{B_s} = 239^{+9}_{-7}\) MeV
- Vector decay constants: \(f_{D^*} = 223^{+23}_{-19}\) MeV, \(f_{D_s^*} = 326^{+21}_{-17}\) MeV, \(f_{B^*} = 151^{+15}_{-13}\) MeV, \(f_{B_s^*} = 236^{+14}_{-11}\) MeV
- Strong coupling constants: \(g_{B^*B\pi}(0) = 60.5 \pm 1.1\) and \(g_{D^*D\pi}(0) = 22.1 \pm 0.4\)
- Ratio \(R = (g_{B^*B\pi}(0) f_{B^*} \sqrt{M_D}) / (g_{D^*D\pi}(0) f_{D^*} \sqrt{M_B}) = 1.105 \pm 0.005\)
- Slope of the Isgur-Wise function: \(\rho^2 = 0.35 \pm 0.02\)
- Slope of \(F_D(w)\) at \(w=1\): \(\rho_D^2 = 0.38 \pm 0.02\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonrelativistic quark model study of \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) semileptonic decays (Albertus et al., 2005) to the meson sector, where they again find that direct quark model calculations violate HQET constraints. The results for \(|V_{cb}|\) agree with their earlier baryon determination and with the DELPHI Collaboration experimental value of \(|V_{cb}| = 0.0414 \pm 0.0012 \pm 0.0021 \pm 0.0018\). The pseudoscalar decay constants are compared with lattice QCD results from UKQCD (2001) and Fermilab Lattice (2004), as well as QCD spectral sum rules by Narison, showing good agreement for \(f_{B_s}\) but larger values for \(f_D\) and \(f_{D_s}\). The work enables improved theoretical predictions for semileptonic \(B\) decays and CKM matrix element extraction, while highlighting the need for HQET constraints in nonrelativistic quark model calculations.
