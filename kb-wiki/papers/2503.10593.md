# Chiral Magnetic Effect enhancement at lower collision energies

> We extend previous holographic studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) by incorporating a time-dependent magnetic field. Various magnetic field profiles proposed in the literature are implemented, and their impact on the CME signal is analyzed in both static and expanding backgrounds. Interestingly, the integrated chiral magnetic current can exhibit a non-monotonic dependence on the collision energy. Our results suggest that the CME signal is enhanced at collision energies below $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV. In addition, we derive a quasi-equilibrium formula for the chiral magnetic effect in the expanding background that is valid at late times.

## Key claim

The authors extend previous holographic studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) by incorporating time-dependent magnetic fields, finding that the time-integrated chiral magnetic current can exhibit a non-monotonic dependence on collision energy, with enhancement suggested at energies below \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, contingent on the specific magnetic field profile and the interplay between initial field strength and field lifetime.

## Method

The authors use a holographic \(U(1)_V \times U(1)_A\) model with a Chern-Simons term to study the CME in strongly coupled plasma, treating the magnetic field as a small perturbation (\(B/(\pi T)^2 \ll 1\)) on top of a chirally imbalanced, finite-temperature background. They implement various time-dependent magnetic field profiles from the literature in both static (non-expanding) and Bjorken-expanding backgrounds, solving the linearized Maxwell equations to obtain the time evolution of the chiral magnetic current. For the expanding case, they employ a late-time analytical solution for the metric and derive a quasi-equilibrium formula for the chiral magnetic current valid at late times.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV) can reach \(eB \approx 10 m_\pi^2\) where \(m_\pi \approx 0.14\) GeV; at LHC energies these values are exceeded.
- For RHIC collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the peak magnetic field is \(B \sim m_\pi^2 \sim (140\ \text{MeV})^2\) with temperature \(T \sim 300\ \text{MeV}\), giving \(B/(\pi T)^2 \sim 2\times 10^{-2}\).
- For LHC energies, \(B \sim 10 m_\pi^2\) and \(T \sim 600-1000\ \text{MeV}\), giving a maximum ratio \(B/(\pi T)^2 \sim (2-5)\times 10^{-2}\).
- The holographic model parameters are fixed as \(\kappa^2 = 24\pi^2/19 \simeq 12.5\) and \(\alpha = 6/19 \simeq 0.316\).
- The quasi-equilibrium formula for the chiral magnetic current in the expanding background is \(2\kappa^2 J^{\text{quasi-eq}}_{\text{CME}} = 8\alpha \mu_5(\tau) B_z(\tau)\).
- Deviations between linearized and backreacted solutions become non-negligible when \(B/(\pi T)^2 > 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of the CME that were restricted to constant or Bjorken-expanding magnetic fields (Cartwright 2021, Grieninger 2023) by implementing realistic time-dependent magnetic field profiles. The results address theoretical uncertainties raised by the STAR Collaboration's isobar runs blind analysis (STAR 2021) and subsequent re-examinations (Kharzeev 2022, Lacey 2022), suggesting that the longer lifetime of magnetic fields at lower collision energies may enhance the CME signal even when initial field strengths are weaker. The findings motivate further experimental searches at lower collision energies and provide a framework for incorporating realistic magnetic field dynamics into phenomenological models of heavy-ion collisions.
