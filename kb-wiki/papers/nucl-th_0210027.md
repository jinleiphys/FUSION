# The Bethe-Salpeter equation and the low energy theorems for $πN$ scattering

> The Bethe-Salpeter (BS) amplitude for $πN$ scattering is evaluated at the off mass shell points corresponding to the Low Energy Theorems (LET) based on PCAC and current algebra. The results suggest a way of maintaining consistency between BS equation and LET.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for πN scattering, derived from a chirally invariant Lagrangian, is inconsistent with the Low Energy Theorems (LET) of current algebra and PCAC; the source of the disagreement is the higher-order multiple scattering of t-channel ρ exchange, specifically the ρππ term in the Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors solved the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation for πN scattering using a potential derived from a chirally invariant Lagrangian that includes s- and u-channel nucleon and Δ poles, and t-channel ρ and σ poles. They introduced two classes of vertex form factors (Type I and Type II) with varying cut-off parameters, fitted to s- and p-wave scattering data up to a pion laboratory energy of 300 MeV. The resulting off-mass-shell amplitudes were then evaluated at the Adler, Weinberg, and Cheng-Dashen points and compared to the predictions of the Low Energy Theorems.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-even scattering length a⁺ ranges from -0.025 to -0.05 m_π⁻¹ across the four form factor models (I, II n=2, II n=4, II n=10).
- The σ-term σ_πN(0) varies from 23.8 MeV (Type I) down to 1.62 MeV (Type II n=10).
- The BS amplitude at the Adler point (in units of m_π⁻¹) is 0.366 for Type I, 0.0949 for II n=2, 0.0411 for II n=4, and 0.0180 for II n=10, all non-zero in contrast to the LET prediction of zero.
- The renormalised pion cut-off Λ_π^R ranges from 1.22 GeV (Type I) to 0.822 GeV (Type II n=10), with Δ_π (the form factor difference at the pion pole and at q²=0) ranging from 1.3% to 2.79%.

## Context

This work extends the traditional two-body scattering approach using the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Lahiff 1999) and compares it with the Low Energy Theorems derived from current algebra and PCAC, as formulated by Weinberg (1966), Adler (1965), and Cheng-Dashen (1971). The results show that the BS amplitude fails to satisfy the LET due to chiral symmetry breaking from t-channel ρ exchange, and the authors suggest that introducing a symmetric ρππ Lagrangian (Weinberg and Zumino 1967) could restore consistency, enabling a unified framework that respects both unitarity and chiral symmetry.
