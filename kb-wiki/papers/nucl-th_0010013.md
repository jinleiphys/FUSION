# Nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering as a tool to probe properties of three-nucleon forces

> Faddeev equations for elastic Nd scattering have been solved using modern NN forces combined with the Tucson-Melbourne two-pion exchange three-nucleon force, with a modification thereof closer to chiral symmetry and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. Theoretical predictions for the differential cross section and several spin observables using NN forces only and NN forces combined with three-nucleon force models are compared to each other and to the existing data. A wide range of energies from 3 to 200 MeV is covered. Especially at the higher energies striking three-nucleon force effects are found, some of which are supported by the still rare set of data, some are in conflict with data and thus very likely point to defects in those three-nucleon force models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three-nucleon force (3NF) effects in nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering become large at higher energies (above 65 MeV), but the existing data support some 3NF model predictions while contradicting others, indicating that current 3NF models have defects in their spin structure rather than providing a definitive validation.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering using five modern nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces (AV18, CD-Bonn, NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93) alone and combined with three different three-nucleon force models: the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) two-pion exchange 3NF, a chirally modified version (TM'), and the Urbana IX 3NF. Calculations were performed for incident neutron energies from 3 to 200 MeV, with partial waves up to two-nucleon subsystem total angular momentum j_max=5 (142 channels) and three-nucleon total angular momenta up to J=13/2, with convergence checked against j_max=6 (194 channels).

## Key numbers

- Energy range covered: 3 to 200 MeV incident neutron energy
- Number of partial wave states used: 142 channels (j_max=5) for standard calculations, 194 channels (j_max=6) for convergence checks
- TM 3NF parameters: a = 1.13/m_π, b = -2.58/m_π^3, c = 1.0/m_π^3, d = -0.753/m_π^3
- TM' modified parameter: a' = a - 2m_π^2 c = -0.87/m_π
- TM' cutoff with CD-Bonn: Λ = 4.593 m_π
- Triton binding energy underbinding from NN forces alone: 0.5-1 MeV for three nucleons, 2-4 MeV for ^4He
- AV18 deuteron binding energy used: 2.242 MeV (instead of experimental 2.225 MeV)

## Context

This work extends exact Faddeev calculations for three-nucleon scattering (previously limited to NN forces only) by incorporating three-nucleon force models, building on the authors' prior formalism (Gloeckle et al., 1996). It contrasts with bound-state studies that showed 3NF effects in binding energies (e.g., Pudliner et al., 1997 for A up to 8) by focusing on the richer set of spin observables available in scattering. The results enable future systematic studies using chiral effective field theory (Weinberg, 1990; van Kolck, 1994) to establish the correct spin structure of three-nucleon forces, as current models show contradictory agreement with existing data.
