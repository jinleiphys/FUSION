# Neutrino induced meson productions in forward limit

> We study neutrino-induced meson productions off the nucleon in the forward limit by applying the PCAC hypothesis to our dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model. The DCC model reasonably describes pi N, gamma N -> pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma data in the resonance region. We give a prediction for nu N -> pi N, pi pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma$ reactions cross sections. We compare our results with those from the Rein-Sehgal model, and find a significant difference.

## Key claim

The authors predict neutrino-induced meson production cross sections in the forward limit by applying the PCAC hypothesis to their dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model, but the prediction is strictly valid only at zero momentum transfer (Q^2 = 0) and for forward scattering (theta = 0), relying on the approximation that the off-shell pion T-matrix at q^2 = 0 is similar to the on-shell T-matrix at q^2 = m_pi^2.

## Method

The authors used their previously developed dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model, which solves a coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation with meson-exchange driving terms and bare N* excitation mechanisms, to analyze pi N and gamma N -> pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma data up to W = 2.1 GeV. They then applied the PCAC hypothesis to relate the structure function W_2 (and thus F_2 = omega W_2) for neutrino-induced reactions at Q^2 = 0 to the total cross sections of the corresponding pion-induced reactions pi N -> X from the DCC model.

## Key numbers

- The DCC model analysis includes fitting about 20,000 data points up to W = 2.1 GeV.
- The pi N production dominates up to W = 1.5 GeV; above that energy, pi pi N production becomes comparable to pi N.
- Other meson productions (eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma) contribute about O(10^-1) to O(10^-2) of the pi N and pi pi N contributions.
- The Rein-Sehgal (RS) model underestimates the Delta(1232) peak by about 20% compared to the DCC result.

## Context

This work extends the authors' DCC model, previously tested against pi N and gamma N data (Nakamura et al., 2012), to neutrino-induced reactions for the first time. It provides the first prediction of neutrino-induced pi pi N, eta N, and K Y production rates based on a data-tested model, and it contradicts the widely used Rein-Sehgal model (Rein and Sehgal, 1981) which is shown to significantly overestimate F_2 at higher energies. The results enable improvement of Monte Carlo simulators for neutrino oscillation experiments, and the authors note that full description for non-forward kinematics requires development of a dynamical axial current model, which is underway.
