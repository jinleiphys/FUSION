# Quasifree (e,e'p) reactions on nuclei with neutron excess

> We study the evolution of the (e,e'p) cross section on nuclei with increasing asymmetry between the number of neutrons and protons. The calculations are done within the framework of the distorted-wave impulse approximation, by adopting nonrelativistic and relativistic models. We compare the results obtained with three different approaches based on the mean-field description for the proton bound state wave function. In the nonrelativistic model phenomenological Woods-Saxon and Hartree-Fock wave functions are used, in the relativistic model the wave functions are solutions of Dirac-Hartree equations. The models are first tested against experimental data on $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{48}$Ca nuclei, and then they are applied to calculate \eep cross sections for a set of spherical calcium and oxygen isotopes. From the comparison of the results obtained for the various isotopes we can infer information about the dependence of the various ingredients of the models on the neutron to proton asymmetry.

## Key claim

The evolution of (e,e'p) reduced cross sections with increasing neutron-to-proton asymmetry is studied using three mean-field models (nonrelativistic Woods-Saxon, nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock, and relativistic Dirac-Hartree), and while all models reproduce existing data for stable nuclei, they produce different predictions for neutron-rich isotopes, particularly regarding the magnitude and shape of the cross section as a function of missing momentum.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) and relativistic DWIA (RDWIA) calculations of exclusive (e,e'p) reactions on spherical calcium (40,48,52,60Ca) and oxygen (16,22,24,28O) isotopes. For the nonrelativistic DWIA, proton bound-state wave functions were obtained either from a Woods-Saxon (WS) well adjusted to reproduce experimental separation energies and momentum distributions, or from Hartree-Fock (HF) equations with the Gogny D1M interaction; the outgoing proton wave functions were eigenfunctions of the phenomenological optical potential of Schwandt et al. (1982). For the RDWIA, bound states were solutions of Dirac-Hartree equations from a relativistic Lagrangian with sigma, omega, and rho meson fields, and the scattering states were obtained using the relativistic EDAD1 optical potential of Cooper et al. (1993). The models were first tested against experimental data for 16O, 40Ca, and 48Ca, then applied to predict cross sections for the neutron-rich isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework used to analyze (e,e'p) data from Saclay (Frois et al., 1984; Boffi et al., 1993, 1996), NIKHEF (de Witt Huberts, 1990; Lapikas, 1993; Kramer et al., 1990, 2001), and MAMI (Blomqvist et al., 1995) to neutron-rich isotopes relevant for future facilities like ELISe at FAIR (GSI, 2006) and SCRIT at RIKEN (Suda et al., 2001, 2010). The study compares the traditional phenomenological Woods-Saxon approach used in the DWEEPY code (Giusti et al., 1987, 1988) with parameter-free Hartree-Fock (Gogny D1M from Gorley et al., 2009) and relativistic mean-field (Horowitz et al., 1979, 1981, 1991) descriptions, revealing that while all models agree for stable nuclei, they diverge for neutron-rich systems, enabling tests of nuclear structure models in the exotic regime.
