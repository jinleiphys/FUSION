# Novel approach to investigate ATOMKI anomaly using Coherent CAPTAIN-Mills detectors

> ATOMKI nuclear anomaly has suggested a new BSM (Beyond the Standard Model) boson with mass $\sim17$ MeV emitted from excited nuclei and quickly decays into a pair of $e^+e^-$. In order to search for the new particle, we propose a new approach that utilizes the ongoing Coherent CAPTAIN-Mills (CCM) 10-ton LAr (liquid argon) detectors. The neutrons from the Lujan target can scatter inelastically by the PMT glass in the CCM detector can produce the new boson which solves the ATOMKI anomaly. The new boson can be detected from its decay to a $e^+e^-$ pair. We find that CCM probes a large area of the anomaly-allowable parameter space. We also show the prediction for a 100-ton LAr detector and 5-ton EOS water detector.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ongoing Coherent CAPTAIN-Mills (CCM) 10-ton liquid argon detector can probe the ATOMKI anomaly by detecting a new 17 MeV boson produced via inelastic neutron scattering on oxygen in the PMT glass, but the sensitivity depends on nuclear physics uncertainties and the ability to reconstruct the e+e- invariant mass from Cherenkov light.

## Method

The authors calculate the production rate of a hypothetical 17 MeV boson X from neutron-induced excitation of 16O nuclei in the PMT glass of the CCM detector, using the Gamow shell model with coupled channels (GSM-CC) to compute inelastic neutron-oxygen cross sections. The X boson flux is derived from the neutron flux at the detector, the neutron cross sections, and the ratio of X decay width to photon decay width for each excited nuclear state. The expected number of X boson events is then computed for CCM, a future 100-ton PIP2-BD detector, and a 5-ton EOS water detector, and compared to the parameter space allowed by the ATOMKI 8Be and 4He anomalies.

## Key numbers

- The X boson mass suggested by the ATOMKI anomaly is approximately 17 MeV.
- CCM operates with a 0.8 GeV proton beam producing 5.6 x 10^14 protons-on-target per second, with a 10-ton (7 ton fiducial) LAr detector located 20 meters from the target.
- The PMT glass contains 69.33 kg of oxygen in 130 kg of glass (200 PMTs), with a planned three-year run ending in 2025 (7.5 x 10^21 POT per year).
- PIP2-BD would use a 2 GeV proton beam with a 100-ton LAr detector, 1294 PMTs (841.1 kg glass), 9.9 x 10^22 POT per year, planned for at least 5 years.
- The neutron cross sections for 16O are of order 10^-3 to 10^-2 barn, and total decay widths of excited states range from 34 keV (17.14 MeV state) to 680 keV (22.15 MeV state).
- The spin operator matrix element squared for the 17.14 MeV 1+ state is 0.042008, and for the 18.79 MeV 1+ state is 0.011664.

## Context

This work extends the search for the new boson suggested by the ATOMKI anomaly (Krasznahorkay 2015, 2018, 2019, 2022) beyond dedicated pair-spectrometer experiments, proposing a novel use of existing neutrino detectors. The approach is complementary to direct searches such as MEG II (2024) which found no significant signal, and addresses theoretical concerns raised by Zhang (2020) regarding production mechanisms. The results enable future experiments like PIP2-BD and EOS to cover the remaining parameter space, and the method can be generalized to search for other low-mass bosons from neutron inelastic scattering.
