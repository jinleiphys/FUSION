# Polarization of $Λ(1405)$ in the $γp \rightarrow K^+ πΣ$ reaction

> In this paper, we study the polarization of the $Λ(1405)$ in the $γp \rightarrow K^+ πΣ$ reaction within an effective Lagrangian approach and isobar model. In our model, the $Λ(1405)$ is excited through the t-channel $K/K^*$ exchanges and u-channel hyperon exchange. Compared to previous studies, we also include the contribution from a contact term, which is necessary for our model to interpret the polarization of the $Λ(1405)$. In addition, we also discuss the possibility to verify the proposed two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ using the polarization data. We find that the polarization of the $Λ(1405)$ or the polarization of the final $Σ$ in this reaction is sensitive to the invariant mass $M_{πΣ}$. Thus the measurement of the dependence of the $Λ(1405)$ polarization on the $M_{πΣ}$ can offer valuable information about the pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$.

## Key claim

The polarization of the final Sigma baryon in the gamma p -> K+ pi Sigma reaction, measured as a function of the pi-Sigma invariant mass M_{piSigma}, can distinguish between the one-pole and two-pole pictures of the Lambda(1405) resonance, provided a contact term is included in the theoretical model.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach and isobar model to describe the gamma p -> K+ pi Sigma reaction at center-of-mass energies from 2.3 GeV to 2.8 GeV, where s-channel nucleon resonance contributions are negligible. They include t-channel K and K* exchanges, u-channel hyperon exchange, and a contact term, with the t-channel meson propagators replaced by Regge propagators. The free parameters (cutoff parameter Lambda_B, coupling constants mu_{Lambda*Lambda}, g_{Lambda* K N}, and contact term strength g_c) are fitted to CLAS data on total cross sections, angular distributions, invariant mass spectra, and Sigma polarization.

## Key numbers

- Fitted masses for the two-pole case: M_{Lambda*_L} = 1357.9 +/- 1.1 MeV and M_{Lambda*_H} = 1425.1 +/- 3.4 MeV
- Decay widths for the two-pole case: Gamma_{Lambda*_L} = 47 MeV and Gamma_{Lambda*_H} = 75 MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: 2.41 for the one-pole case, 2.13 for the two-pole case
- Fitted cutoff parameter for baryon exchange: Lambda_B = 2.00 +/- 0.05 GeV (one-pole) and 1.94 +/- 0.09 GeV (two-pole)
- Fitted coupling constant ratio gamma = g_{Lambda* KN}/g_{Lambda* pi Sigma} = 2.36 +/- 0.02 (one-pole case)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Lambda(1405) in photoproduction by the CLAS Collaboration (Moriya 2013, 2014), which measured angular distributions and invariant mass spectra but did not provide enough constraints to distinguish the two-pole structure predicted by Chiral Unitary approaches (Oller 2000, Oset 2001, Jido 2003). The authors show that polarization data, which were previously used only for determining the spin-parity of the Lambda(1405), can serve as a new observable to test the two-pole picture. The measurement of Sigma polarization versus M_{piSigma} in future experiments with high statistics could resolve the long-standing question of whether the Lambda(1405) consists of one or two poles.
