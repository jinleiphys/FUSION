# Are $N\barΩ$ bound states?

> Inspired by the progress of the experimental search of the $NΩ$ dibaryon by the STAR collaboration, we study $N\barΩ$ systems in the framework of quark delocalization color screening model. Our results show that the attraction between $N$ and $\barΩ$ is a little bit larger than that between $N$ and $Ω$, which indicates that it is more possible for the $N\barΩ$ than the $NΩ$ system to form bound states. The dynamic calculations state that both the $J^{P}=1^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ $N\barΩ$ systems are bound states. The binding energy of these two states are deeper than that of $NΩ$ systems with $J^{P}=2^{+}$, and the $NΩ$ system with $J^{P}=1^{+}$ is unbound. The calculation of the low-energy scattering phase shifts, scattering length and the effective range also supports the existence of the $N\barΩ$ bound states with $J^{P}=1^{+}$ and $2^{+}$. So the $N\barΩ$ states are better hexaquark states and stronger signals are expected in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both the \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) and \(J^{P}=2^{+}\) \(N\bar{\Omega}\) systems are bound states, with the attraction between \(N\) and \(\bar{\Omega}\) being slightly larger than that between \(N\) and \(\Omega\). The hedge is that this conclusion is drawn within the specific framework of the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), and the authors note that the \(N\Omega\) system with \(J^{P}=2^{+}\) only becomes bound through multi-channel coupling, whereas the \(N\bar{\Omega}\) bound states are found in a single-channel calculation.

## Method

The authors used the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), a constituent quark model that includes quark delocalization and color screening to generate intermediate-range attraction. They calculated effective potentials for the \(S\)-wave \(N\bar{\Omega}\) systems with isospin \(I=1/2\) and spin parity \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) and \(2^{+}\), and then performed dynamic bound-state calculations using the resonating group method (RGM). Low-energy scattering phase shifts, scattering lengths, and effective ranges were also computed using the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variational method to confirm the existence of bound states.

## Key numbers

* The \(N\bar{\Omega}\) system with \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) has a single-channel binding energy of \(-10.4\) MeV.
* The \(N\bar{\Omega}\) system with \(J^{P}=2^{+}\) has a single-channel binding energy of \(-8.7\) MeV.
* The \(N\Omega\) system with \(J^{P}=2^{+}\) has a multi-channel coupling binding energy of \(-6.4\) MeV; the \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) \(N\Omega\) system is unbound.
* The scattering length for the \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) \(N\bar{\Omega}\) system is \(2.4269\) fm, with an effective range of \(0.47785\) fm and a binding energy \(B'\) of \(-6.9\) MeV.
* The scattering length for the \(J^{P}=2^{+}\) \(N\bar{\Omega}\) system is \(2.7928\) fm, with an effective range of \(0.81106\) fm and a binding energy \(B'\) of \(-6.1\) MeV.
* The scattering length for the \(J^{P}=2^{+}\) \(N\Omega\) system is \(2.8007\) fm, with an effective range of \(0.5770\) fm and a binding energy \(B'\) of \(-5.2\) MeV.
* The scattering length for the \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) \(N\Omega\) system is \(-0.37807\) fm, with an effective range of \(-2.3544\) fm, indicating no bound state.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon-antibaryon systems, following the experimental search for the \(N\Omega\) dibaryon by the STAR collaboration (2019) and earlier theoretical predictions of a narrow \(N\Omega\) resonance by Goldman et al. (1987) and Oka. The authors previously studied the \(N\Omega\) system in the QDCSM (Huang et al., 2015) and found it to be a narrow resonance; here they apply the same model to the \(N\bar{\Omega}\) system, finding stronger binding. The results are consistent with those from the chiral quark model (Zhang et al.), and the authors suggest that the \(N\bar{\Omega}\) state, which cannot annihilate to vacuum due to its quark content, provides a more stable system for experimental searches for baryon-antibaryon bound states.
