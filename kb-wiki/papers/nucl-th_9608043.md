# Influence of spin-rotation measurements on partial-wave analyses of elastic pion-nucleon scattering

> Recent measurements of spin-rotation parameters in elastic $π^+ p$ scattering are in marked disagreement with predictions of the Carnegie-Mellon$-$Berkeley and Karlsruhe-Helsinki analyses. Using the method of Barrelet, we show how this discrepancy can be removed. We then show how this Barrelet transformation alters the partial-wave amplitudes. The effect of unitarity and analyticity constraints is also considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Barrelet transformation applied to a single zero-trajectory in the Karlsruhe-Helsinki and Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley partial-wave analyses can remove the marked disagreement with recent spin-rotation measurements at 1.3 GeV, without altering the fit to the existing cross-section and polarization database, though the resulting amplitudes may require further readjustment to satisfy unitarity and analyticity constraints.

## Method

The authors applied the Barrelet method to the KA84 solution (and similarly to the CMB and KH80 solutions) by identifying one particular zero trajectory that crosses the unit circle at about 700 MeV and remains influential through the resonance region. They performed the transformation \(w_i \to 1/w_i^*\) for this trajectory, which preserves the differential cross section, total cross section, and polarization but changes the spin-rotation parameters. The transformed amplitudes were then compared to the original solutions for their description of the \(A\) parameter in elastic \(\pi^+ p\) scattering at 1.3 GeV, and the effect on partial-wave amplitudes and unitarity was examined.

## Key numbers

- The spin-rotation parameter \(A\) for \(\pi^+ p\) scattering was measured at 1.3 GeV between 120 and 140 degrees, showing an angular dependence very different from the KH and CMB predictions.
- The Barrelet transformation shifted the \(\pi NN\) coupling constant \(f^2\) by less than 1% over a range of t-values, within its uncertainty.
- The zero trajectory crosses the unit circle at about 700 MeV and remains influential through the remainder of the resonance region.
- The D\(_{35}\) partial wave shows a small violation of unitarity (imaginary part becomes negative) at energies where the imaginary part is very small.

## Context

This work extends the KH (Karlsruhe-Helsinki) and CMB (Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley) partial-wave analyses, which were performed without spin-rotation data in the resonance region, by incorporating recent ITEP-PNPI measurements of the \(A\) parameter at 1.3 GeV. The Barrelet method, originally proposed by Barrelet (1970), is used to resolve an ambiguity that cannot be fixed by cross-section and polarization data alone. The results enable future full partial-wave analyses that include spin-rotation data, and the altered partial-wave amplitudes provide guidance for comparing with quark-model predictions for resonance candidates near 1.3 GeV.
