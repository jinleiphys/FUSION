# A Physics Informed Bayesian Neural Network for the Neutron Star Equation of State

> We present a physics-informed Bayesian neural-network framework to infer neutron-star equations of state from theoretical priors and to propagate the associated uncertainties to stellar observables. Trained on a large and representative ensemble of hadronic EoSs, the model learns $P(ε)$ via stochastic variational inference, incorporating soft constraints at saturation density and from perturbative QCD, together with penalties enforcing monotonicity and causality. The accepted core EoSs are matched to an SLy4 crust and evolved through a unified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff-plus-tidal solver to generate posterior predictions in the mass-radius ($M$-$R$) and mass-tidal-deformability ($M$-$Λ$) planes. The inferred posterior is consistent with NICER radius measurements and the observed $2.0\,M_\odot$ maximum-mass constraint, yielding $R_{1.4}=12.1^{+1.4}_{-0.9}\,\mathrm{km}$, $Λ_{1.4}=580^{+520}_{-240}$, and $M_{\mathrm{max}}\simeq 2.11\pm0.05\,M_\odot$ (90\% CI). The resulting canonical tidal deformability can be assessed \emph{a posteriori} against current gravitational-wave constraints. Overall, this framework provides a flexible, non-parametric mapping from microphysical EoS uncertainties to neutron-star observables.

# A Physics Informed Bayesian Neural Network for the Neutron Star Equation of State

## Key claim

The physics-informed Bayesian neural network (PI-BNN) provides a non-parametric functional posterior over the neutron-star equation of state that is consistent with NICER radius measurements and the observed 2.0 solar mass maximum-mass constraint, but the GW170817 tidal-deformability estimate is used only as an external posterior comparison rather than as a likelihood component of the inference, and the learned priors reflect the hadronic training ensemble and should not be interpreted as spontaneously generating first-order phase transitions absent from that distribution.

## Method

The authors train a Bayesian neural network on 65 hadronic equations of state from the CompOSE database, learning the mapping from energy density to pressure via stochastic variational inference with a mean-field Gaussian variational distribution and Softplus activation functions. Soft constraints at nuclear saturation density (150 MeV/fm^3) and perturbative QCD regime (10,000 MeV/fm^3) are embedded directly in the Evidence Lower Bound through pyro.factor statements, together with derivative-level penalties enforcing monotonicity and causality. The accepted core EoS samples are matched to an SLy4 crust and evolved through a unified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff-plus-tidal solver to generate posterior predictions in the mass-radius and mass-tidal-deformability planes.

## Key numbers

- R_{1.4} = 12.1^{+1.4}_{-0.9} km (90% CI) for the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star
- Lambda_{1.4} = 580^{+520}_{-240} (90% CI) for the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star
- M_max approximately 2.11 +/- 0.05 solar masses (90% CI) for the maximum neutron star mass
- Soft constraint anchor at saturation density: epsilon_sat = 150 MeV/fm^3 with target pressure P_sat = 1.5 MeV/fm^3
- Soft constraint anchor at perturbative QCD regime: epsilon_pQCD = 10,000 MeV/fm^3 with target pressure P_pQCD = 3,333 MeV/fm^3
- Training data domain truncated at epsilon = 3000 MeV/fm^3
- Post-training acceptance rate approximately 95% due to soft constraints during training
- Prior scale sigma_w = 0.1 for network weights and biases
- Fixed likelihood noise sigma_obs = 0.01

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian Power Regression with heteroscedastic errors (BPR-HE) framework from Chimanski (2023) by replacing the fixed analytic power-law mean with a fully non-parametric Bayesian neural network, reducing structural bias from predefined parametrizations such as piecewise polytropes from Read (2009) and spectral expansions from Lindblom (2010). The framework enables joint uncertainty quantification for the two principal observable channels of current multi-messenger neutron-star inference (mass-radius and mass-tidal-deformability), and its posterior can be compared a posteriori with gravitational-wave constraints from GW170817 and NICER radius measurements from Riley (2019, 2021) and Choudhury (2024).
