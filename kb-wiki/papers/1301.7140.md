# Ab-initio No-Core Gamow Shell Model calculations with realistic interactions

> No-Core Gamow Shell Model (NCGSM) is applied for the first time to study selected well-bound and unbound states of helium isotopes. This model is formulated on the complex energy plane and, by using a complete Berggren ensemble, treats bound, resonant, and scattering states on equal footing. We use the Density Matrix Renormalization Group method to solve the many-body Schrödinger equation. To test the validity of our approach, we benchmarked the NCGSM results against Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky exact calculations for $^3$H and $^4$He nuclei. We also performed {\textit ab initio} NCGSM calculations for the unstable nucleus $^5$He and determined the ground state energy and decay width, starting from a realistic N$^3$LO chiral interaction.

## Key claim

The No-Core Gamow Shell Model (NCGSM) is applied for the first time to bound and unbound states of helium isotopes, but the method is benchmarked only for the well-bound systems $^3$H and $^4$He against exact Faddeev calculations, while the calculation for the unbound $^5$He nucleus is presented as a demonstration without direct experimental or exact theoretical comparison for the decay width.

## Method

The NCGSM formulates the many-body problem on the complex energy plane using a complete Berggren ensemble of single-particle states, which includes bound, resonant, and scattering states. The intrinsic Hamiltonian with a realistic NN interaction (Argonne $\upsilon_{18}$ or chiral N$^3$LO, both renormalized via $V_{low-k}$ with a cutoff of 1.9 fm$^{-1}$) is solved using the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) method to diagonalize the complex-symmetric Hamiltonian matrix. Two-body matrix elements in the Berggren basis are obtained by expanding the short-range part of the interaction in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis and computing overlaps between Berggren and oscillator states.

## Key numbers

* $^3$He ground state energy converges to -5.352 MeV using the N$^3$LO interaction renormalized at $\Lambda = 1.9$ fm$^{-1}$, with convergence achieved at $N_{max} = 21$ in the HO expansion of the interaction.
* The energy of $^3$He changes by only $\sim 30$ keV when varying $N_{max}$ from 5 to 29, demonstrating rapid convergence of the HO expansion method.
* The basis generating potential (Gamow Hartree-Fock) gives the $0s_{1/2}$ proton and neutron states in $^3$H at energies $-10.417$ MeV and $-11.982$ MeV, respectively, along the imaginary momentum axis.
* The scattering continua for $s_{1/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, and $p_{1/2}$ partial waves are discretized with 18 points each, extending up to $k_{max} \approx 4$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) framework, previously applied with an inert core, to a no-core formulation where all nucleons are active, enabling ab initio treatment of weakly bound and unbound light nuclei. The NCGSM is benchmarked against exact Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for $^3$H and $^4$He (Nogga et al., Yakubovsky), and the DMRG method is adapted from earlier GSM/DMRG applications with schematic interactions (Rotureau et al.) to handle realistic forces. The approach enables future studies of open quantum systems such as $^6$Be and other nuclei near the drip lines, where continuum coupling effects are essential.
