# Lepton flavor (universality) violation in rare kaon decays

> Recent anomalies in the decays of $B$ mesons and the Higgs boson provide hints towards lepton flavor (universality) violating physics beyond the Standard Model. We observe that four-fermion operators which can explain the $B$-physics anomalies have corresponding analogs in the kaon sector, and we analyze their impact on $K\toŌÄ\ell \ell'$ and $K\to\ell \ell'$ decays $(\ell=őľ,e)$. For these processes, we note the corresponding physics opportunities at the NA62 experiment. In particular, assuming minimal flavor violation, we comment on the required improvements in sensitivity necessary to test the $B$-physics anomalies in the kaon sector.

## Key claim

Four-fermion operators that could explain the observed B-meson and Higgs anomalies have analogs in the kaon sector, and the resulting lepton flavor universality violation (LFUV) and lepton flavor violation (LFV) in rare kaon decays could be probed at the NA62 experiment, but only if experimental sensitivity improves by at least an order of magnitude to test the parameter space relevant for the B anomalies.

## Context

This work extends the study of LFUV and LFV anomalies observed in B meson decays by LHCb (2013-2015) and the Higgs decay h ‚Üí őľŌĄ by CMS (2015) to the kaon sector, following the observation that the same four-fermion operators can generate effects in both systems. The analysis builds on the chiral perturbation theory framework for rare kaon decays developed by Ecker, Pich, and de Rafael (1987) and the comprehensive review by Cirigliano et al. (2011). The paper identifies NA62 as the key experiment for testing these correlations, noting that the projected sensitivity for LFV modes could reach the level needed to probe the B-anomaly parameter space, while also highlighting that the KOTO experiment at J-PARC may provide complementary sensitivity for neutral kaon modes.
