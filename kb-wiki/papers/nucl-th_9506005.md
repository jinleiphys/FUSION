# Extraction of the $πNN$ coupling constant from NN scattering data

> We reexamine Chew's method for extracting the $πNN$ coupling constant from np differential cross section measurements. Values for this coupling are extracted below 350 MeV, in the potential model region, and up to 1 GeV. The analyses to 1~GeV have utilized 55 data sets. We compare these results to those obtained via $χ^2$ mapping techniques. We find that these two methods give consistent results which are in agreement with previous Nijmegen determinations.

## Key claim

The Chew-extrapolation method and chi-squared mapping techniques give consistent values for the piNN coupling constant extracted from np differential cross sections, and these values agree with previous Nijmegen determinations near g^2/4pi = 13.7; however, the individual extractions show considerable scatter, and the authors cannot claim to have improved upon results from dispersion-relation analyses of pion-nucleon scattering data.

## Method

The authors applied a modified Chew extrapolation method to np differential cross section data below 1 GeV, using a Legendre-polynomial expansion of the difference between experimental and model values of a kinematic variable y. In the region below 350 MeV, they used a "difference method" with the Nijmegen, Bonn, and Paris potentials as input models, and also employed their own partial-wave solution SM95. As a consistency check, they performed chi-squared mapping of g^2/4pi against chi^2 over different energy regions, analyzing pp and np data separately and in combined fits using the Nijmegen Coulomb distorted-wave Born approximation for the one-pion exchange contribution.

## Key numbers

- Average g^2/4pi = 13.58 +/- 0.04 (unnormalized) and 13.75 +/- 0.04 (normalized) from 55 datasets below 1000 MeV using SM95 as input
- Average g^2/4pi = 13.47 +/- 0.08 (unnormalized) and 13.51 +/- 0.08 (normalized) from 18 datasets below 400 MeV using SM95 as input
- From chi^2 mapping of combined pp and np data below 400 MeV: g^2/4pi = 13.80 +/- 0.07 (solution A), 13.83 +/- 0.07 (solution B), and 14.38 +/- 0.06 (solution C, using plane-wave Born approximation)
- From chi^2 mapping of combined pp and np data below 1600 MeV: g^2/4pi = 13.51 +/- 0.03
- Individual extractions ranged from 11.63 +/- 0.47 (814.9 MeV, Bizard et al. data) to 16.50 +/- 0.57 (96.0 MeV, Roehnnquist et al. data)

## Context

This work extends the Chew extrapolation method originally proposed by Chew (1958) and applies it to a larger set of np differential cross section data than previous studies. The results are compared with the Nijmegen group's determinations from Stoks, Timmermans, and de Swart (1993) and with the Virginia Tech partial-wave analyses from Arndt, Strakovsky, and Workman (1994). The study finds that the extracted coupling constants are consistent across different methods and input models, but the scatter in individual extractions prevents improvement over the precision achieved from dispersion-relation analyses of elastic pion-nucleon scattering data.
