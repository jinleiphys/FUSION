# Application of a semi-microscopic core-particle coupling method to the backbending in odd deformed nuclei

> In two previous papers, the Kerman-Klein-Donau-Frauendorf (KKDF) model was used to study rotational bands of odd deformed nuclei. Here we describe backbending for odd nuclei using the same model. The backbending in the neighboring even nuclei is described by a phenomenological two band model, and this core is then coupled to a large single-particle space, as in our previous work. The results obtained for energies and M1 transition rates are compared with experimental data for 165Lu and for energies alone to the experimental data for 179W. For the case of 165Lu comparison is also made with previous theoretical work.

# Application of a semi-microscopic core-particle coupling method to the backbending in odd deformed nuclei

## Key claim
The Kerman-Klein-Donau-Frauendorf (KKDF) model, previously applied to rotational bands of odd deformed nuclei without backbending, can describe backbending in odd nuclei by coupling a phenomenological two-band model of the even-even core to a large single-particle space; the method reproduces energies and M1 transition rates for 165Lu and energies for 179W, but the phenomenological core description is chosen because it provides a more accurate fit to data than existing microscopic calculations.

## Method
The authors first construct a phenomenological two-band mixing model for the even-even neighboring nuclei (180W for 179W; 164Yb and 166Hf for 165Lu), using a Harris formula expansion to third order for the uncoupled bands and a band-coupling interaction with angular-momentum-dependent strength C(I). The resulting core energies and quadrupole matrix elements are then used as input to the KKDF equations of motion, where a large single-particle space (5 major shells from Woods-Saxon calculations) is coupled to the core states. The quadrupole field strength κ is treated as a free parameter, and single-particle energies are allowed to vary by ±5%; the over-completeness of the basis is handled by a projection operator method that separates physical from unphysical states.

## Key numbers
- For 179W: the yrast band (K=7/2) is reproduced with high accuracy; backbending occurs at J ~ 31/2, consistent with experimental observations interpreted as Fermi alignment (t-band).
- For 165Lu: the calculation reproduces experimental B(M1) values of the yrast band "quite well and clearly better" than the cranking calculation and core-particle coupling model of Chen et al.
- The g-factor formula used for the even core is g = g_R + (g_j - g_R) i/I, with g_R = 0.31 and g_j = -0.20 for the ν_{13/2} level.
- For 180W, the excited band has states with odd angular momentum values, allowing independent fitting of the excited band parameters first.

## Context
This work extends the KKDF model applications of Protopapas, Klein, and Walet (previous papers, 1995-1996) from rotational bands without backbending to include the backbending phenomenon. It contrasts with the semi-microscopic approach of Chen et al. (previous theoretical work on 165Lu), who used a quasiparticle description of the s-band; the present authors argue their phenomenological core fit yields better agreement with experimental spectra above the backbend. The method enables future applications to transitional regions where particle-number conservation becomes essential, as noted in the thesis of Protopapas.
