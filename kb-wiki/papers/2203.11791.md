# Hybrid stars and QCD phase transition with a NJL-like model

> In this paper, we introduce a self-consistent mean field approximation to study the QCD phase transition and the structure of hybrid stars within the framework of NJL model. In our practice, a phenomenological parameter $α$ is introduced, which reflects the weights of "direct" channel and "exchange" channel under a finite chemical potential. The mass-radius relation is obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation using a crossover equation of state (EOS). We calculate the density distribution in a two solar-mass hybrid star to show the effects of different parameters. We also calculate the tidal Love number $k_2$ and the deformability $(Λ)$. It is found that the stiffness of the EOS increases with $α$, which allows us to obtain a hybrid star with a maximum mass of 2.40 solar-mass through our model. The observation of over 2.06 solar-mass neutron stars may indicates that the chiral transition may be a crossover on the whole $T-μ$ plane.

# Hybrid stars and QCD phase transition with a NJL-like model

## Key claim
The chiral phase transition in neutron star conditions is a crossover rather than a first-order transition when the phenomenological parameter α exceeds 0.47, which allows the model to support hybrid stars with masses up to 2.40 solar masses. The observation of neutron stars over 2.06 solar masses may indicate that the chiral transition is a crossover on the entire temperature-chemical potential plane.

## Method
The authors introduce a self-consistent mean field approximation within the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, incorporating a Fierz transformation to include vector channel interactions. A phenomenological parameter α weights the contributions of the "direct" (Hartree) and "exchange" (Fock) channels. The quark matter equation of state is combined with the APR hadronic equation of state using a smooth crossover function, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for hybrid stars.

## Key numbers
- Maximum hybrid star mass reaches 2.40 solar masses for α = 0.9
- For α = 0.5, maximum mass is 2.01 solar masses, with radius 10.81 km
- For α = 0.6, maximum mass is 2.06 solar masses, with radius 10.84 km
- For α = 0.7, maximum mass is 2.17 solar masses, with radius 10.92 km
- For α = 0.8, maximum mass is 2.22 solar masses, with radius 11.03 km
- Tidal deformability Λ for a 1.4 solar mass star ranges from 312 (α = 0.5) to 377 (α = 0.9)
- The critical α for crossover transition in neutron star conditions is 0.47; for heavy ion collision conditions (n_q = 0.4 n_B), the threshold is 0.58
- The crossover connection parameter μ_0 = 923 MeV and μ_1 = 1200 MeV

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent mean field approach of Wang (2019) to study hybrid stars, addressing the long-standing problem that standard NJL models predict chiral restoration at chemical potentials near 330 MeV, contradicting hadron physics expectations that the transition occurs at 4-8 times nuclear saturation density. The model enables comparison with astronomical observations of massive pulsars such as PSR J0740+6620 (2.14 solar masses) and PSR J2215+5135 (2.27 solar masses), and can be further constrained by neutron star cooling rate measurements and gravitational wave observations from LIGO/Virgo.
