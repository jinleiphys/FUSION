# A Poincaré-covariant current operator for interacting systems and deuteron electromagnetic form factors

> In front-form dynamics a current operator for systems of interacting particles, which fulfills Poincaré, parity and time reversal covariance, together with hermiticity, can be defined. The electromagnetic form factors can be extracted without any ambiguity and in the elastic case the continuity equation is automatically satisfied. Applications to the calculation of deuteron form factors are presented, and the effects of different nucleon-nucleon interactions, as well as of different nucleon form factors are investigated.

# A Poincaré-covariant current operator for interacting systems and deuteron electromagnetic form factors

## Key claim

In front-form dynamics, a current operator built from the sum of free one-body currents in a specific Breit frame (where the momentum transfer is directed along the spin-quantization axis) satisfies extended Poincaré covariance, hermiticity, and current conservation for elastic scattering, provided the current is covariant with respect to rotations around the z-axis. The authors claim this approach eliminates the ambiguities that plague standard treatments using the free current in the q⁺ = 0 frame.

## Method

The authors adopt front-form Hamiltonian dynamics with a fixed number of particles, where seven of the ten Poincaré generators are interaction-free and Lorentz boosts are interaction-free. For the two-body deuteron system, the mass operator is defined as M̃² = M₀² + V, yielding a mass equation with the same form as the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation in momentum representation. The current operator in the chosen Breit frame is taken as the sum of free one-body currents Jᵘ(0) = Σᵢ j_free,iᵘ, and the deuteron electromagnetic form factors are extracted from the 2S+1 independent matrix elements of this current.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron magnetic moment μ_d and quadrupole moment Q_d are calculated for eight different N-N interactions (Av14, RSC, Paris, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen1, Nijmegen93, RSC93, and Av18) and compared against experimental values; relativistic effects bring both μ_d and Q_d closer to experimental values except for the CD-Bonn interaction.
- For the deuteron form factor A(Q²), the data from Refs. JLABA and JLABC (open dots and upward triangles in Fig. 2(a)) are well described; the neutron electric form factor G_Eⁿ requires higher values in the range 0.5–1 (GeV/c)² relative to the Gari model.
- The form factor A(Q²) is plotted as A(Q²)/(G_D² × F) with G_D = (1 + Q²/0.71)⁻² and F = (1 + Q²/0.1)⁻²·⁵; the form factor B(Q²) is plotted as B(Q²)/(G_D² × F₁) with F₁ = (1 + Q²/0.1)⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the front-form Hamiltonian dynamics approach previously developed by Lev, Pace, and Salmè (1998) for constructing Poincaré-covariant current operators, which had already been successfully tested in deep inelastic scattering. The authors apply this formalism to deuteron electromagnetic properties, obtaining results for magnetic and quadrupole moments and elastic form factors that improve upon nonrelativistic calculations and avoid the ambiguities present in the standard approach of Grach and Kondratyuk (1984) using the free current in the q⁺ = 0 frame. The approach enables future investigation of many-body currents that must separately satisfy extended Poincaré covariance and hermiticity, and the authors note that a more stringent comparison with new TJNAF data for B(Q²) will become possible.
