# Spin polarization of an expanding and rotating system

> We study the longitudinal spin polarization of a relativistic fluid of massive spin-1/2 particles undergoing a boost-invariant expansion in the longitudinal direction and rotating in the transverse plane. We express the polarization vector in terms of spin moments and derive closed equations of motion for the latter using spin kinetic theory with a nonlocal relaxation time approximation. These equations of motion are valid at any time of the evolution, from the free-streaming regime to the hydrodynamic regime. At late time, the polarization features contributions from gradients of the fluid velocity and of the temperature, that emerge from the nonlocal part of the collision term. Our results can be used to explore polarization phenomena in the context of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed equations of motion for the longitudinal spin polarization of a relativistic fluid of massive spin-1/2 particles undergoing boost-invariant expansion and transverse rotation, using a nonlocal relaxation time approximation. The key hedge is that the polarization at late time includes contributions from gradients of both fluid velocity and temperature that emerge from the nonlocal part of the collision term, and that the spin moments approach asymptotic values rather than local-equilibrium values due to this nonlocality.

## Method

The authors start from spin kinetic theory with a nonlocal relaxation time approximation (NLRTA) collision term, which drives the distribution function to an asymptotic form rather than local equilibrium. They express the longitudinal polarization vector in terms of spin moments (moments of the distribution function weighted by spherical harmonics and powers of momentum), derive exact equations of motion for these moments from the Boltzmann equation, and then close the system by truncating to the spin moments that survive at late time (the asymptotic spin moments). The spin potential is expressed through the total angular momentum via matching conditions, and its dynamics are determined from separate equations of motion.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal spin moments with n+ℓ even decay as τ⁻¹ in the late free-streaming regime (when cosθ = 0), while transverse spin moments with n+ℓ odd decay as τ⁻² in the same regime.
* The asymptotic spin moments that contribute to the longitudinal polarization are G^z_{00r}, G^z_{11r}, G^z_{20r} (from the nonlocal collision term) and G^x_{10r}, G^x_{21r} (from transverse components).
* The relaxation time τ_R sets the time scale for approach to the asymptotic distribution function, with the hydrodynamic regime defined as τ/τ_R → ∞.
* The nonlocal collision term parameter ξ determines the time scale of angular-momentum conversion between orbital and spin contributions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of transverse spin polarization in a boost-invariant expanding system (Weickgenannt 2023) by relaxing translational invariance in the transverse plane and imposing only rotational symmetry, allowing nonzero fluid vorticity around the longitudinal axis. It builds on spin kinetic theory developments (Weickgenannt 2020, 2024) and addresses open questions in the description of longitudinal Lambda polarization as a function of momentum in heavy-ion collisions, where previous works (Liu 2021, Fu 2021, Becattini 2021) included only ideal contributions while neglecting dissipative terms. The results enable exploration of polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions using a tractable set of equations that capture dynamics from free-streaming to hydrodynamic regimes.
