# Improved Nearside-Farside Method for Elastic Scattering Amplitudes

> A simple technique is described that provides an improved nearside-farside (NF) method for elastic scattering amplitudes. The technique, involving the novel resummation of a Legendre partial wave series, reduces the importance of unphysical contributions to NF subamplitudes, which can arise in more conventional NF decompositions. Detailed applications are made to a strong absorption model and to a ^{16}O + ^{16}O optical potential at E_{lab} = 145 MeV. We also discuss ^{16}O + ^{16}O at E_{lab} = 480, 704, 1120 MeV, and alpha + ^{12}C, alpha + ^{40}Ca, both at E_{lab} = 1370 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved nearside-farside (NF) method for elastic scattering amplitudes that uses a novel resummation of the Legendre partial wave series, which reduces unphysical contributions that can appear in the conventional Fuller NF decomposition, particularly in cases where the full cross section is simpler than the NF subamplitudes.

## Method

The authors apply a modified Yennie-Ravenhall-Wilson (YRW) resummation technique to the partial wave series for the elastic scattering amplitude, using two parameters α and β per resummation step, before splitting the Legendre polynomials into traveling angular wave components Q_l^(∓)(x). The resummation parameters are chosen according to a rule that suppresses contributions from low partial waves where the semiclassical interpretation is less valid. The method is tested on a strong absorption model (SAM) and on optical model calculations for ¹⁶O + ¹⁶O at several energies and α + ¹²C, α + ⁴⁰Ca at 1370 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Fuller NF method (Fuller, 1975) by incorporating and improving upon the Hatchell resummation technique (Hatchell, 1989) and the modified YRW resummation procedure proposed by Whiteley (2001) and Noli (2002). The paper demonstrates that the conventional Fuller method produces unphysical contributions in seven collision systems, including a SAM and several optical model cases, and shows that the improved resummation can clean these contributions. The method enables more reliable physical interpretation of elastic scattering angular distributions in heavy-ion and nuclear physics, particularly for cases where the standard NF decomposition produces oscillatory NF cross sections when the full cross section is monotonic.
