# The form factors of the nucleon at small momentum transfer

> We study the low energy expansion of the nucleon's electroweak form factors in the framework of an effective chiral Lagrangian including pions, nucleons and the $Delta (1232)$. We work to third order in the so-called small scale expansion and compare the results with the ones previously obtained in the chiral expansion. In addition, these calculations serve as a first exploratory study of renormalization and decoupling within the small scale expansion.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including the Delta(1232) resonance via the small scale expansion to order epsilon^3 improves the description of the isovector Pauli form factor compared to conventional chiral perturbation theory, but the Delta contributions do not affect the axial form factor's q^2 dependence or the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant at this order.

## Method
The authors constructed the complete pi N Delta gamma effective Lagrangian to order epsilon^3 in the small scale expansion, which treats the pion mass, external momenta, and the N-Delta mass splitting (Delta = 0.294 GeV) as small parameters. They computed one-loop diagrams for the nucleon's isovector and isoscalar electromagnetic form factors and the axial form factors, performed renormalization of the nucleon mass and coupling constants, and analyzed the decoupling of Delta degrees of freedom in the limit Delta to infinity.

## Key numbers
- The isovector Pauli radius squared (r_2^v)^2 = 0.61 fm^2, comprising a chiral contribution of 0.52 fm^2 and a Delta-pi loop contribution of 0.09 fm^2, compared to the empirical value of 0.80 fm^2
- The isovector Dirac radius squared (r_1^v)^2 = 0.80 fm^2 - 12 B_10^(r)(1 GeV)/(4 pi F_pi)^2, where the chiral plus Delta contribution gives 0.80 fm^2 and the counterterm B_10^(r)(1 GeV) = 0.63 reproduces the empirical value of 0.585 fm^2
- The Delta-N mass splitting used is Delta = 0.294 GeV, with the ratio R = Delta/m_pi + sqrt(Delta^2/m_pi^2 - 1) = 3.95
- The nucleon mass M_N = 0.938 GeV, pion mass m_pi = 0.14 GeV, pion decay constant F_pi = 0.0925 GeV, axial coupling g_A = 1.26, and pi N Delta coupling g_{pi N Delta} = 1.05

## Context
This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculations of form factors by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) and Fearing, Lewis, and Scherer (1996) to include the Delta(1232) resonance via the small scale expansion developed by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1997). The results enable comparison with dispersion-theoretical analyses by Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996) and provide a first exploratory study of renormalization and decoupling in the small scale expansion, with implications for understanding the TRIUMF measurement of the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P from radiative muon capture.
