# Transfer probabilities for the reactions $^{14,20}$O+$^{20}$O in terms of multiple time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov trajectories

> The transfer reaction between two nuclei in the superfluid phase is studied with the Time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) theory. In order to restore the symmetry of the relative gauge angle a set of independent TDHFB evolutions is done. Then the transfer probability is computed using a triple projection method. This method is first tested to determine the transfer probabilities on a toy model and compared to the exact solution. It is then applied to the reactions $^{20}$O+$^{20}$O and $^{14}$O+$^{20}$O in a realistic framework with a Gogny interaction.

# Transfer probabilities for the reactions $^{14,20}$O+$^{20}$O in terms of multiple time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov trajectories

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a set of independently evolved Time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) trajectories with different initial relative gauge angles, combined with a triple projection method, can reproduce exact pair transfer probabilities in a toy model and predict large enhancement of pair transfer in realistic reactions, but only after introducing ad-hoc prescriptions (center-of-mass shift and chemical potential corrections) to stabilize the probability after the reaction.

## Method

The authors initialize a set of HFB states with different relative gauge angles between two superfluid nuclei, evolve each independently using the TDHFB equation with a Gogny D1S interaction, and compute the transfer probability via a triple projection method: initial projection on the left particle number, final projection on the total particle number, and projection on the left or right particle number. For the realistic calculations, they introduce a center-of-mass shift correction to compensate for trajectory dispersion and a chemical potential correction to prevent spurious phase rotation. The method is first validated on a toy model with a time-dependent gaussian coupling between two systems of paired particles, comparing to the exact solution of the many-body Schrodinger equation.

## Key numbers

- For the toy model: $\Omega=4$ doubly degenerate levels, $N_L^0=N_R^0=4$ particles, pairing strength $G=-1$ MeV, coupling strength $V_0=-0.03$ MeV, gaussian parameter $a=0.3 \times 10^{44} s^{-2}$, time range $t_i = -15\times 10^{-22}$ s to $t_f = 15\times 10^{-22}$ s.
- For the realistic calculations: the projector is discretized with $M=8$ sampling points (tested with $M=16$ showing no difference).
- For the reaction $^{20}$O+$^{20}$O: the probability to transfer one neutron is smaller than $10^{-4}$ for all calculations; the Q-value for one-neutron transfer is $Q_{1n}=-3.8$ MeV while the pair transfer Q-value is $Q_{2n}=-0.9$ MeV.
- For the reaction $^{14}$O+$^{20}$O: the pair transfer probability shows an enhancement of about two orders of magnitude compared to $P_1^2/4$.

## Context

This work extends the projection method for transfer probabilities in time-dependent mean-field calculations, originally proposed by Simenel (2010) and generalized for superfluid fragments by Scamps (2013), to the case where both fragments are initially superfluid. It builds on the finding by Hashimoto (2016) that fusion dynamics changes with the initial relative gauge angle, and on the observation by Magierski (2016) and Sekizawa (2017) that the nucleus-nucleus potential contains a non-negligible pairing contribution varying with gauge angle. The method enables future calculations of sub-barrier transfer probabilities for reactions where large enhancement factors are observed experimentally, such as those studied by Corradi (2011) and Montanari (2014).
