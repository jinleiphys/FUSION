# Dijets in p+Pb collisions and their quantitative constraints for nuclear PDFs

> We present a perturbative QCD analysis concerning the production of high-pT dijets in p+Pb collisions at the LHC. The next-to-leading order corrections, scale variations and free-proton PDF uncertainties are found to have only a relatively small influence on the normalized dijet rapidity distributions. Interestingly, however, these novel observables prove to retain substantial sensitivity to the nuclear effects in the PDFs. Especially, they serve as a more robust probe of the nuclear gluon densities at $x>0.01$, than e.g. the inclusive hadron production. We confront our calculations with the recent data by the CMS collaboration. These preliminary data lend striking support to the gluon antishadowing similar to that in the EPS09 nuclear PDFs.

## Key claim

Normalized dijet rapidity distributions in p+Pb collisions at the LHC retain substantial sensitivity to nuclear effects in PDFs, especially for gluon densities at x > 0.01, while being robust against next-to-leading order corrections and scale variations. The preliminary CMS data lend striking support to gluon antishadowing similar to that in the EPS09 nuclear PDFs, though the hedge is that the data are preliminary and the reweighting effect is not dramatic because EPS09 already has gluon antishadowing built in.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations using the MEKS Monte-Carlo code, with asymmetric p+Pb beam energies (proton at 4 TeV, lead at 1.58 TeV per nucleon) and CMS kinematic cuts (leading jet pT > 120 GeV, subleading jet pT > 30 GeV, |eta| < 3). They compared normalized dijet rapidity distributions and forward-to-backward asymmetries against CMS data, and applied Hessian PDF reweighting to quantify the impact of the dijet data on the EPS09 nuclear PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Proton beam energy: 4 TeV; lead beam energy per nucleon: approximately 1.58 TeV (82/208 × 4 TeV)
- Rapidity shift between center-of-mass and laboratory frame: approximately -0.465 units
- Leading jet pT threshold: > 120 GeV; subleading jet pT threshold: > 30 GeV
- Pseudorapidity range for jets: |eta| < 3
- EPS09 global fit uses Delta chi^2 = 50 for defining PDF error sets
- Reweighting performed at Q^2 = 1.69 GeV^2 for the gluon modification factor R_G

## Context

This work extends the EPS09 global fit of nuclear PDFs (Eskola 2009) by using LHC p+Pb dijet data from CMS to constrain nuclear gluon densities, which were previously only weakly constrained by inclusive pion production in deuteron+gold collisions at RHIC (Adler 2006, Abelev 2009). The analysis contrasts with the DSSZ fit (de Florian 2011), which attributed RHIC pion data to nuclear effects in fragmentation functions rather than gluon PDFs, and with HKN07 (Hirai 2007), which left gluons very weakly constrained. The results enable improved constraints on nuclear gluon PDFs at large x, where uncertainty is currently large.
