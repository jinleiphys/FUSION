# Melting rho Meson and Thermal Dileptons

> We give a brief survey of theoretical evaluations of light vector mesons in hadronic matter, focusing on results from hadronic many-body theory. We emphasize the importance of imposing model constraints in obtaining reliable results for the in-medium spectral densities. The latter are subsequently applied to the calculation of dilepton spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, with comparisons to recent NA60 data at the CERN-SPS. We discuss aspects of space-time evolution models and the decomposition of the excess spectra into different emission sources.

## Key claim

Hadronic many-body calculations predict a strong broadening and little mass shift of the rho-meson spectral function in hot/dense matter; the good agreement with NA60 dilepton data supports the notion of a "melting" rho meson close to the expected phase boundary to the QGP, while calculations based on a dropping-mass scenario are disfavored.

## Method

The authors calculate in-medium rho-meson spectral functions using hadronic many-body theory with effective Lagrangians, implementing selfenergy insertions into the rho propagator from interactions with surrounding mesons and baryons. They then convolute the resulting thermal dilepton emission rates over a space-time evolution model (an expanding thermal fireball tuned to hydrodynamic simulations) to compute dilepton spectra for In(158 AGeV)+In collisions at the CERN-SPS, comparing to NA60 data. The calculations include contributions from the quark-gluon plasma, in-medium rho, omega and phi mesons, 4-pion-type annihilation, primordial Drell-Yan annihilation, and rho decays after thermal freezeout.

## Key numbers

- Initial QGP phase temperature T0 = 197 MeV for central In(158 AGeV)+In collisions
- Hadrochemical freezeout at (mu_B^c, T_c) = (232, 175) MeV
- Thermal freezeout at T_fo ~ 120 MeV after a total lifetime of ~7 fm/c
- Baryon chemical potential mu_B = 330 MeV for approximate SPS conditions
- Normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Predicted in-medium omega meson width Gamma_omega^med ~ 50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- In-medium phi meson width ~ 50 MeV at rho_0 from nuclear photoproduction
- Approximate weighting of rho:omega:phi contributions to the electromagnetic correlator of 10:1:2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous interpretation of NA60 dilepton spectra (vanHees 2006) and compares to calculations based on a dropping-mass scenario (LKB96) which is disfavored by the data. The results confirm earlier hadronic many-body predictions of a strongly broadened rho spectral function (Rapp 1997) and are compatible with QCD sum rule constraints (Leupold 1997). The findings enable future systematic evaluations of Weinberg and QCD sum rules to establish connections between vector-meson spectral functions and chiral symmetry restoration, using constraints from lattice QCD.
