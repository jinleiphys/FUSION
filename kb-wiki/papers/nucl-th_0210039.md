# Observable Consequences of Crossover-type Deconfinement Phase Transition

> Equation of State (EoS) for hot and dense nuclear matter with a quark-hadron phase transition is constructed within a statistical mixed-phase model assuming coexistence of unbound quarks in nuclear surrounding. This model predicts the deconfinement phase transition of the crossover type. The so-called "softest point" effect of EoS is analyzed and confronted to that for other equations of state which exhibit the first order phase transition (two-phase bag model) or no transition at all (hadron resonance gas). It is demonstrated that the beam energy dependence of the directed flow is a smooth function in the whole range from SIS till SPS energies and allows to disentangle different EoS, being in good agreement with experimental data for the statistical mixed-phase model. In contrast, excitation functions for relative strangeness abundance turn out to be insensitive to the order of phase transition.

# Observable Consequences of Crossover-type Deconfinement Phase Transition

## Key claim
The statistical mixed-phase (MP) model predicts a deconfinement phase transition of the crossover type, and the beam-energy dependence of directed flow is a smooth function from SIS to SPS energies that allows disentangling different equations of state, with the MP model showing good agreement with experimental data. However, the excitation functions for relative strangeness abundance are insensitive to the order of the phase transition.

## Method
The author constructs an equation of state (EoS) for hot and dense nuclear matter using a statistical mixed-phase model where unbound quarks and gluons coexist with hadrons in a homogeneous phase, with density-dependent mean-field interactions that simulate confinement at low density and asymptotic freedom at high density. This EoS is applied to a relativistic two-fluid hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions, where the projectile and target fluids interact through friction terms based on nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The directed flow of baryons and strangeness production are calculated as excitation functions across beam energies from SIS to SPS ranges.

## Key numbers
- Deconfinement temperature in the MP model: T_dec = 153 MeV (for two light flavors at zero baryon density)
- Softest point energy density in the MP model at n_B=0: ε_SP ≈ 0.45 GeV/fm³
- Softest point energy density in the bag model at n_B=0: ε_SP ≈ 1.5 GeV/fm³
- The softest point for baryonic matter vanishes for n_B ≳ 0.3 n_0 (where n_0 is normal nuclear matter density)
- Freeze-out density used in hydrodynamic calculations: n_f = 0.8 n_0
- Freeze-out energy density for strangeness calculations: ε_f = 0.15 GeV/fm³ ≈ m_N n_0
- Critical energy density for deconfinement: ε_c ∼ 1 GeV/fm³
- Critical temperature for deconfinement: T_c ∼ 170 MeV
- MP model parameters: γ = 0.92, A^(1/(3γ+1)) = 250 MeV

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the softest point effect in the EoS by Hung and Shuryak (1995) and Rischke (1996), who used a two-phase bag model with a first-order phase transition. The MP model was previously developed by Nikonov, Shanenko, and Toneev (1998) and Toneev, Nikonov, and Shanenko (1998). The two-fluid hydrodynamic approach follows Maruhn, Rischke, and Stoecker (1991). The directed flow results are compared with transport calculations from RQMD, ARC, and ART codes, as well as RBUU calculations by Cassing (1999). The strangeness production uses the canonical formalism of Hagedorn and Rafelski (1985) and Cleymans, Redlich, and Suhonen (1991). The results enable future investigation of dilepton and hard photon production as more promising signals to distinguish crossover from first-order phase transitions.
