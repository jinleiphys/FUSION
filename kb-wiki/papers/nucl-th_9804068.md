# Nuclear effects in the F3 structure function

> By using a relativistic framework and accurate nuclear spectral functions we evaluate the ratio F_{3A}/AF_{3N} of deep inelastic neutrino scattering. Parametrizations of this ratio for different values of Q^2 are provided. These results should be useful for taking into account the nuclear effects in analyses of experimental data in neutrino reactions in nuclear targets, and test QCD predictions for the nucleon structure functions. In particular, the size of the nuclear corrections is of the same order of magnitude as the size of the QCD corrections to the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects in the neutrino structure function \(F_{3A}\) are sizeable (around 10% reduction of the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule) and differ from those in the charged-lepton structure function \(F_{2A}\), primarily because the virtual meson cloud contributes to \(F_{2A}\) but not to \(F_{3A}\); the paper provides parametrizations of the ratio \(R_3 = F_{3A}/AF_{3N}\) for \(Q^2\) values of 5, 30, and 50 GeV\(^2\) to enable extraction of the free-nucleon \(F_{3N}\) from nuclear data.

## Method

The authors compute the neutrino-nucleus cross section via the neutrino self-energy in nuclear matter using a relativistic many-body approach and the local density approximation. The hadronic tensor for the nucleus is obtained by convoluting the free-nucleon hadronic tensor with hole spectral functions for \({}^{56}\)Fe taken from a relativistic nuclear-matter calculation that reproduces nuclear binding and momentum distributions. The ratio \(R_3(x,Q^2) = F_{3A}(x,Q^2)/A F_{3N}(x,Q^2)\) is then evaluated in the Bjorken limit, and the results are fitted to the functional form \(R_3(x) = A(B-x)^\alpha/(1-x)^\beta\).

## Key numbers

- At \(x \simeq 0.6\), the ratio \(R_3\) is around 0.8 for \({}^{56}\)Fe.
- At low \(x\), the ratio \(R_3\) is around 0.9.
- Nuclear effects can reduce the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule integral by about 10%.
- The factor \((p_0\gamma - p_z)/(p_0 - p_z)\gamma\) in the convolution formula produces changes of around 10% in \(R_3\) for \(x \approx 0.8-1\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\).
- Fit parameters for \(R_3(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\): \(A = 0.691\), \(B = 1.29\), \(\alpha = 1.21\), \(\beta = 0.688\).
- Fit parameters for \(R_3(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 30\) GeV\(^2\): \(A = 0.647\), \(B = 1.06\), \(\alpha = 6.88\), \(\beta = 6.19\).
- Fit parameters for \(R_3(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 50\) GeV\(^2\): \(A = 0.599\), \(B = 1.07\), \(\alpha = 6.43\), \(\beta = 5.64\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic many-body formalism previously applied to charged-lepton scattering (Marco, Oset, and Fernandez de Cordoba, 1996) to the parity-violating neutrino structure function \(F_3\), which has no charged-lepton counterpart. The results differ significantly from the deuteron calculation of Sidorov and Tokarev (1995) and show discrepancies of about 10% at low \(x\) compared to the iron calculation of Kulagin (1998), which uses a non-relativistic formalism with a flux factor to enforce baryon number conservation. The parametrizations enable experimentalists to correct nuclear data for \(F_{3A}\) and thereby test QCD predictions such as the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule and extract the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\).
