# Constraints from the GW170817 merger event on the nuclear matter equation of state

> The detection of the GW170817 neutron star merger event has incited an intense research activity towards the understanding of the nuclear matter equation of state. In this paper we compare in particular the pressure-density relation obtained from heavy-ion collisions with the analysis of the NS merger event. Moreover, we present recent calculations of neutron star's moment of inertia and tidal deformability using various microscopic equations of state for nuclear and hybrid star configurations, and confirm several universal relations. We also discuss the recent constraints for the NS radii determined by GW170817, and find compatible radii between 12 and 13 kilometers, thus identifying the suitable equations of state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several microscopic equations of state (EOS) based on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body theory are fully compatible with the constraints from the GW170817 merger event, but only after noting that the heavy-ion collision data are actually more restrictive than the gravitational-wave data, and that the UIX EOS is the only one compatible with both sets of data over the whole density range.

## Method

The authors computed neutron star properties (mass-radius relations, tidal deformability, moment of inertia) using multiple microscopic EOSs based on the BHF approach with realistic two-body and three-body nucleonic forces (Argonne V18, Bonn B, Nijmegen 93 potentials) and several hybrid star EOSs combining BHF hadronic matter with a Dyson-Schwinger model for quark matter. They compared these theoretical predictions against the pressure-density constraints extracted from heavy-ion collision data (transverse flow and subthreshold kaon production) and from the GW170817 gravitational-wave analysis, and tested universal I-Love-Q relations.

## Key numbers

* Maximum masses for the microscopic EOSs range from 2.04 solar masses (UIX) to 2.51 solar masses (BOB), all exceeding the 2 solar mass observational limit except the hyperonic EOSs V18(N+Y) at 1.65 solar masses and BOB(N+Y) at 1.37 solar masses.
* Radii for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star span from 11.59 km (APR) to 13.10 km (DBHF), with the GW170817-compatible range being 12 to 13 km.
* Tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses ranges from 274 (APR) to 681 (DBHF), with the GW170817 constraint of 400 to 800.
* The universal relation for normalized moment of inertia is given by I/MR^2 = 0.207 + 0.857 beta +/- 0.011, where beta = M/R is the compactness.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear matter EOSs from heavy-ion collision studies (FOPI collaboration flow data and KaoS collaboration kaon production data from 2002) to the new gravitational-wave constraints from the GW170817 event reported by the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2017). The paper confirms the universal I-Love-Q relations proposed by Yagi and Yunes (2013, 2017) for a broad set of microscopic EOSs, and identifies that the tension between large radii from GW170817 (12-13 km) and smaller radii from quiescent LMXB observations (9.9-11.2 km, Ozel 2016) could be resolved in two-families or twin-star scenarios. The results enable future simultaneous measurements of mass, radius, and moment of inertia to potentially detect the presence of hyperons in neutron stars.
