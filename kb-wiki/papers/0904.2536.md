# Shock Treatment: Heavy Quark Drag in a Novel AdS Geometry

> We calculate the drag force on a heavy quark hit by a shock wave, thus generalizing the strongly coupled AdS/CFT heavy quark drag calculations to both hot and cold nuclear matter. The derivation employs the trailing string configuration, similar to that used in the literature for a quark moving through a thermal medium, though in the shock metric the string profile is described by a much simpler analytic function. Our expression for the drag depends on the typical transverse momentum scale of the matter in the shock. For a thermal medium this scale becomes proportional to the temperature, making our drag coefficient and momentum limit of applicability identical to those found previously. As the shock wave can be composed of either thermalized or non-thermalized media, our derivation extends the existing drag calculations to the case of arbitrarily distributed matter.

## Key claim

The drag force on a heavy quark in a shock wave geometry depends only on the typical transverse momentum scale of the matter in the shock, and for a thermal medium this scale becomes proportional to the temperature, making the drag coefficient and momentum limit of applicability identical to those found previously for a thermal medium; the derivation extends existing drag calculations to arbitrarily distributed matter, whether thermalized or not.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a heavy quark as the endpoint of a Nambu-Goto string in an AdS$_5\times S^5$ background, employing a shock wave metric (with a step function $\theta(x^-)$ in the $dx^{-2}$ term) rather than a black hole metric. They solve the string equations of motion in the static gauge to find a trailing string configuration $x(t,z)=x_0 + \sqrt{\mu}\,z^3/3$, which is valid for all $z$ and is analytically simpler than the corresponding black hole solution. The drag force is extracted from the canonical momentum flow down the string, then boosted to the medium rest frame and related to the medium's energy-momentum tensor via holographic renormalization.

## Key numbers

* Drag force in the heavy quark rest frame: $dp/dt = \sqrt{\lambda}\,\sqrt{\mu}/(2\pi)$, where $\lambda$ is the 't Hooft coupling and $\mu$ is the shock parameter with units GeV$^4$.
* Relation between $\mu$ and medium properties: $\mu = \pi^2 \Lambda^4 (p'/M)^2$, where $\Lambda$ is the typical momentum scale of medium particles and $p'/M$ is the Lorentz factor of the heavy quark in the medium rest frame.
* Final drag force in the medium rest frame: $dp'/dt' = -(\sqrt{\lambda}/2)\,(\Lambda^2/M_q)\,p'$.
* For a thermal medium with $\Lambda = \sqrt{\pi}T$, the result reproduces the black hole metric result: $dp'/dt' = -\pi\sqrt{\lambda}\,T^2\,p'/(2M_q)$.
* The heavy quark mass in vacuum is $M_q = T_0 L^2/z_M = \sqrt{\lambda}/(2\pi z_M)$, where $z_M$ is the D7 brane location.

## Context

This work extends previous AdS/CFT heavy quark drag calculations by Herzog et al. (2006), Gubser et al. (2006), and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2007), which assumed a strongly coupled thermal ${\mathcal N}=4$ SYM plasma described by a black hole metric. The shock wave geometry, originally introduced by Janik (2005), allows the calculation to apply to both hot and cold nuclear matter, including non-equilibrium media. The result enables future studies of heavy quark energy loss in proton-nucleus collisions and in the early, non-thermalized stages of heavy ion collisions, and suggests that the form of heavy quark energy loss may be universal across different medium states in strongly coupled theories.
