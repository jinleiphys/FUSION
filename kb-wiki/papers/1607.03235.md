# Compact Star Matter: EoS with New Scaling Law

> In this paper we present a simple discussion on the properties of compact stars using an EoS obtained in effective field theory anchored on scale and hidden-local symmetric Lagrangian endowed with topology change and a unequivocal prediction on the deformation of the compact star, that could be measured in gravitational waves. The objective is not to offer a superior or improved EoS for compact stars but to confront with a forthcoming astrophysical observable the given model formulated in what is considered to be consistent with the premise of QCD. The model so obtained is found to satisfactorily describe the observation of a 2-solar mass neutron star with a minimum number of parameters. Specifically the observable we are considering in this paper is the tidal deformability parameter (equivalently the Love number k_2), which affects gravitational wave forms at the late period of inspiral stage. The forthcoming aLIGO and aVirgo observations of gravitational waves from binary neutron star system will provide a valuable guidance for arriving at a better understanding of highly compressed baryonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper presents a specific equation of state (EoS) for compact stars derived from a new scaling law (new-BR/BLPR) that incorporates a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at high density, and shows that this EoS can accommodate a 2-solar-mass neutron star while making a testable prediction for the tidal deformability parameter. The authors explicitly state that the objective is not to offer a superior or improved EoS, but to confront a specific model with forthcoming astrophysical observations from gravitational waves.

## Method

The authors construct an EoS for neutron star matter (neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in weak equilibrium) using a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach with an effective Lagrangian that incorporates hidden local symmetry and scale symmetry. The density dependence of the Lagrangian parameters is determined by a new scaling law (new-BR/BLPR) that implements a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a threshold density \(n_{1/2}\). The TOV equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and the tidal deformability parameter \(\lambda\) (Love number \(k_2\)) is computed from linearized metric perturbations for a static, spherically symmetric star.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for \(npe\mu\) matter: \(M \sim 2.1 M_\odot\) with radius \(R \sim 11\) km and central density \(n_c \sim 5.7 n_0\).
- For pure neutron matter (from prior work): maximum mass \(M \sim 2.4 M_\odot\) with radius \(R \sim 12\) km and central density \(n \sim 4.7 n_0\).
- Compactness \(C = M/R\) in the mass range \(1.0 - 2.0 M_\odot\): 0.12 - 0.26; for \(1.4 M_\odot\): 0.14.
- Tidal deformability parameter \(\lambda\) for \(1.4 M_\odot\): 2.86 (in units of \(G = c = 1\)).
- Dimensionless deformability \(\Lambda = 634\) for \(M = 1.35 M_\odot\).
- Tidal deformability parameter range over the mass range: \(4.0 - 0.68\).
- Radius for mass range \(1 M_\odot - 2 M_\odot\): 11.2 - 12.2 km.
- Causality limit (maximum valid density): \(n_c \sim 5.7 n_0\).

## Context

This work extends the pure neutron matter calculations of Dong et al. (2016) to a more realistic \(npe\mu\) composition, using the same new scaling law (new-BR/BLPR) that incorporates a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density \(\sim (2-3)n_0\). The approach contrasts with standard nuclear physics EoS such as SLy, AP3, and MPA1 (which give \(\lambda = 1.70, 2.22, 2.79\) respectively for \(1.4 M_\odot\)), and with alternative stiffening mechanisms that implement a smooth changeover to quark matter as discussed by Hatsuda (2016) and Baym (2016). The predicted tidal deformability is within the measurable range (\(\delta \Lambda > 400\)) for the upcoming aLIGO and aVirgo gravitational wave detectors, enabling direct observational tests of the model.
