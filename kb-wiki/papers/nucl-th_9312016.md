# Theory of Coupled Pion--Trinucleon Systems

> We derive the dynamical equations which consistently couple the four--body ($π$NNN) system to the underlying three--nucleon system. Our treatment can be considered the proper generalization of the Afnan--Blankleider equations for the coupled NN--$π$NN system. The resulting connected--kernel equation resembles in structure the Yakubovskii--Grassberger--Sandhas equation for the standard four--body problem, but involves 24 chain--labelled components (rather than the usual 18 ones) and allows for a consistent evaluation of reaction amplitudes involving $π$ absorption/production.

# Theory of Coupled Pion–Trinucleon Systems

## Key claim

The authors derive connected-kernel dynamical equations that consistently couple the four-body (πNNN) system to the underlying three-nucleon system, generalizing the Afnan–Blankleider equations for the NN–πNN system. The resulting equations are formally similar to the Yakubovskii–Grassberger–Sandhas equations for the standard four-body problem but involve 24 chain-labelled components rather than the usual 18, and the kernel is connected after one or two iterations depending on the sector.

## Method

The authors start from the Afnan–Blankleider (AB) equations for the NNN–πNNN system and rewrite them as a super-Lippmann–Schwinger equation in the space of all partitions into three clusters (7 such partitions). They then introduce partitions into two clusters, distinguishing three types (I: (πNN)N, II: (πN)(NN), III: (NNN)π), and derive sub-amplitudes for each type via coupled equations that include the dressed πNN vertex operators. Using a super sum rule for the two-cluster potentials, they obtain a final connected-kernel equation for the unknowns U^{(3)}_{a'b'} that has the same structure as the standard four-body Yakubovskii–Grassberger–Sandhas equation but with 24 components and coupling between the four-body and three-nucleon sectors.

## Key numbers

- The resulting connected-kernel equation involves **24 chain-labelled components**, compared to the **18 components** of the standard Yakubovskii–Grassberger–Sandhas four-body problem.
- The number of partitions into three clusters for the πNNN system is **7**.
- The six extra components (24 vs. 18) arise because **six different 2-cluster partitions** (all type I and type II) have one additional component for the absorption channel.
- Two sectors (out of four) are connected after **one iteration** of the kernel, while the π-scattering and π-production sectors require a **second iteration**.

## Context

This work extends the Afnan–Blankleider formalism (Afnan and Blankleider, 1980) from the coupled NN–πNN system to the NNN–πNNN system, addressing the disconnectedness problem that made previous formulations ill-conditioned. It improves upon the isobar-based approach of Avishai and Mizutani (1983), which the authors note was "extremely involved" and demanded "more complete and aesthetic descriptions." The resulting equations enable consistent evaluation of reaction amplitudes involving pion absorption and production in three-nucleon systems, and can be used to calculate processes such as ³He(π,NN)N.
