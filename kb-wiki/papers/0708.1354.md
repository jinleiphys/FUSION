# Multi-channel algebraic scattering theory and the structure of exotic compound nuclei

> A Multi-Channel Algebraic Scattering (MCAS) theory is presented with which the properties of a compound nucleus are found from a coupled-channel problem. The method defines both the bound states and resonances of the compound nucleus, even if the compound nucleus is particle unstable. All resonances of the system are found no matter how weak and/or narrow. Spectra of mass-7 nuclei and of {}^{15}F, and MCAS results for a radiative capture cross section are presented.

## Key claim

The Multi-Channel Algebraic Scattering (MCAS) method can determine all bound states and resonances of a compound nucleus from a coupled-channel problem, even for particle-unstable nuclei, but the required interaction potentials have so far been constructed only from a simple collective rotational model with deformation taken to second order, and only low-energy scattering from light-mass nuclei has been considered.

## Method

The MCAS method solves coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger integral equations in momentum space by expanding the interaction potential matrix elements in finite sums of energy-independent separable terms, using sturmian functions as the expansion basis. The sturmians are determined from the same coordinate-space model interaction potentials and can be selected to enforce Pauli principle constraints. Bound states are found from zeros of a matrix determinant when all channels are closed, while resonances are identified when complex eigenvalues of a related matrix cross the unit circle in the Gauss plane near the point (1,0).

## Key numbers

- For the $p+^{14}$O system (giving particle-unstable $^{15}$F), MCAS results for elastic cross sections at $\theta_{c.m.}=180^\circ$ compare reasonably with data from Gomez del Campo (2004) and Gulyas (2005).
- For $^{15}$C, MCAS finds two bound states ($\frac{1}{2}^+$ ground state and $\frac{5}{2}^+$) and three narrow negative-parity resonances within the spread of a broad $\frac{3}{2}^+$ resonant state.
- For $^7$Li, MCAS calculations produced 12 states up to 15 MeV excitation, with the lowest 9 matching known spin-parity states; the $\frac{3}{2}^-$ ground state is at $-10.0$ MeV relative to the $p+^6$He threshold.
- For $^7$Be, the $\frac{3}{2}^-$ ground state is at $-10.7$ MeV (experimental) compared to MCAS values of $-11.0$ MeV ($n+^6$Be) and $-10.1$ MeV ($^3$He+$^4$He).
- The calculated astrophysical $S$-factor for $^3$He-$^4$He radiative capture overestimates measured data by only 20%, with no arbitrary scaling or adjusted spectroscopic factor.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS approach previously developed by Amos (2003) and Canton (2006) to treat mirror scattering systems using isospin symmetry, enabling predictions for nuclei beyond the proton drip line such as $^{15}$F. The method provides a systematic determination of sub-threshold bound and resonance states that can be used to evaluate radiative capture cross sections, as demonstrated for the $^3$He-$^4$He system by Canton, Levchuck, and Shebeko (2007), avoiding the orthogonality problem that has affected past radiative capture calculations. The results enable future studies of exotic compound nuclei and astrophysical capture reactions using a unified theoretical framework.
