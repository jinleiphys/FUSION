# Photoproduction of a πρPair and Transversity GPDs

> We demonstrate that the chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon can be accessed through the exclusive photoproduction process gamma + N -> pi + rho + N', in the kinematics where the meson pair has a large invariant mass and the final nucleon has a small transverse momentum, provided the vector meson is produced in a transversely polarized state. We calculate perturbatively the scattering amplitude at leading order in alpha_s: We build a simple model for the dominant transversity GPD HT (x; chi; t) based on the concept of double distribution. Counting rates estimates show that the experiment looks feasible with the real photon beam characteristics expected at JLab@12 GeV, in low Q2 leptoproduction at Jlab@12 GeV and in the COMPASS experiment.

## Key claim

The chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon can be accessed through the exclusive photoproduction process gamma + N -> pi + rho + N', provided the vector meson is produced in a transversely polarized state and the meson pair has a large invariant mass; the feasibility depends on the magnitude of the chiral-odd GPDs, which remain largely unknown.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering amplitude at leading order in alpha_s using collinear factorization, writing the amplitude as a convolution of the hard partonic subprocess with the transversity GPD H_T, the pion distribution amplitude, and the chiral-odd distribution amplitude of the transversely polarized rho meson. They model the dominant transversity GPD H_T(x,xi,t) using a double distribution ansatz based on quark transversity parton distribution functions from Anselmino et al., with a dipole form factor for the t-dependence (C = 0.71 GeV^2). The integrations over the meson momentum fractions v and z are performed analytically, while the integration over the GPD variable x is done numerically.

## Key numbers

- The chiral-odd decay constant for the transversely polarized rho meson is f_rho^perp = 160 MeV.
- The dipole form factor parameter for the t-dependence of the GPD is C = 0.71 GeV^2.
- The differential cross section d sigma / d M_pi_rho^2 is shown at s_gamma_N = 20 GeV^2 for JLab kinematics.
- The low Q^2 window for leptoproduction is 0.02 < Q^2 < 1 GeV^2.
- The skewness parameter xi is given by xi = tau / (2 - tau) with tau = M^2_pi_rho / (S_gamma_N - M^2).

## Context

This work extends the strategy initiated in IPST (2009) for accessing chiral-odd GPDs through multi-meson final states, previously applied to diffractive production of two vector mesons. It contrasts with other attempts to access transversity GPDs (Liuti et al.) by relying on known leading twist factorization theorems rather than alternative approaches. The process is expected to be observable at JLab@12 GeV (probing the large xi region) and at the COMPASS experiment at CERN (probing the small xi region, around xi ~ 0.01), providing complementary information on transversity GPDs.
