# Projection and ground state correlations made simple

> We develop and test efficient approximations to estimate ground state correlations associated with low- and zero-energy modes. The scheme is an extension of the generator-coordinate-method (GCM) within Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA). We show that GOA fails in non-Cartesian topologies and present a topologically correct generalization of GOA (topGOA). An RPA-like correction is derived as the small amplitude limit of topGOA, called topRPA. Using exactly solvable models, the topGOA and topRPA schemes are compared with conventional approaches (GCM-GOA, RPA, Lipkin-Nogami projection) for rotational-vibrational motion and for particle number projection. The results shows that the new schemes perform very well in all regimes of coupling.

## Key claim

The standard Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) fails for collective coordinates with non-Cartesian topologies (such as rotational angles or periodic particle-number gauge angles), and the authors propose a topologically corrected generalization (topGOA) that preserves the correct periodic or spherical structure of the coordinate space, yielding accurate ground-state correlation energies even in regimes where conventional GOA breaks down.

## Method

The authors extend the generator-coordinate method (GCM) within the Gaussian overlap approximation by replacing the Cartesian Gaussian ansatz with a form that respects the topology of the collective coordinate, using sine-squared arguments for periodic coordinates (rotations on a sphere, particle-number gauge angle). They derive the topGOA overlaps and Hamiltonian kernels for a three-level Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (with rotational symmetry) and for particle-number projection, then solve the Griffin-Hill-Wheeler equation directly with these topologically corrected kernels. A small-amplitude limit of topGOA, called topRPA, is also derived, yielding simple second-order expressions for the vibrational correction after variation-after-projection.

- For the three-level model, the vibrational RPA frequency in the deformed regime (χ>1) is ω = ε√(χ²−1).
- The topRPA scheme reproduces the topGOA correlation energy to within graphical accuracy for N=12, N=20, and N=40 across all χ (see Figure 4).
- For particle-number projection, the topGOA norm overlap is exp(−2⟨Ñ²⟩ sin²(η/2)), where ⟨Ñ²⟩ is the variance of the particle number operator in the HFB state.

## Context

This work extends the GCM-GOA framework (reviewed in GCM (1970s)) and the RPA correlation theory (Rowe (1970), Ring & Schuck (1980)) by correcting the topological failure of GOA for rotational and gauge-angle coordinates, a problem previously noted for rotational motion in weakly deformed systems (rot (1990s)). The topGOA and topRPA schemes enable efficient variation-after-projection calculations for angular momentum and particle number, providing a simple second-order alternative to full GCM that works in all coupling regimes, including the transitional region where RPA becomes unstable. The approach substantiates the "rule of thumb" used in large-scale fits by Goriely (2000s) for rotational corrections.
