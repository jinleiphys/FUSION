# Baryon form factors in chiral perturbation theory

> We analyze the electromagnetic form factors of the ground state baryon octet to fourth order in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory. Predictions for the Σ^- charge radius and the Λ-Σ^0 transition moment are found to be in excellent agreement with the available experimental information. Furthermore, the convergence behavior of the hyperon charge radii is shown to be more than satisfactory.

## Key claim
The paper claims that relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory (using infrared regularization) to fourth order provides predictions for hyperon electromagnetic form factors that agree well with available data, particularly for the Σ⁻ charge radius and the Λ-Σ⁰ transition magnetic moment, and that the chiral expansion for hyperon charge radii converges satisfactorily. The hedge is that the convergence is shown to be "more than satisfactory" only for the hyperon charge radii, while the magnetic moments exhibit more problematic convergence behavior that depends sensitively on which loop contributions are counted at which chiral order.

## Method
The authors calculate the electromagnetic form factors of the ground state baryon octet (p, n, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) to fourth order in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory using infrared regularization. They fit low-energy constants (LECs) from the second-, third-, and fourth-order meson-baryon Lagrangians to experimental magnetic moments and to the proton and neutron charge and magnetic radii, then predict the corresponding quantities for hyperons and the Λ-Σ⁰ transition. Vector mesons are included as active degrees of freedom for the full momentum dependence up to Q² = 0.3 GeV².

## Key numbers
- Σ⁻ charge radius: predicted ⟨(r_E^Σ⁻)²⟩ = 0.67 ± 0.03 fm² at fourth order in IR, compared to experimental value 0.60 ± 0.08 (stat.) ± 0.08 (syst.) fm² from SELEX collaboration
- Λ-Σ⁰ transition magnetic moment: predicted μ_ΛΣ⁰ = 1.606 ± 0.008 n.m. at fourth order in IR, compared to experimental |μ_ΛΣ⁰| = 1.61 ± 0.08 n.m.
- Proton charge radius: fitted to 0.717 fm² (experimental value from dispersion relations)
- Neutron charge radius: fitted to -0.113 fm² (experimental value from dispersion relations)
- Proton magnetic radius: fitted to 0.699 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Neutron magnetic radius: fitted to 0.790 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Λ charge radius: predicted 0.11 ± 0.02 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Σ⁺ charge radius: predicted 0.60 ± 0.02 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Σ⁰ charge radius: predicted -0.03 ± 0.01 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Ξ⁰ charge radius: predicted 0.13 ± 0.03 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Ξ⁻ charge radius: predicted 0.49 ± 0.05 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Λ-Σ⁰ transition charge radius: predicted 0.03 ± 0.01 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Λ magnetic radius: predicted 0.48 ± 0.09 fm² at fourth order in IR
- Σ⁺ magnetic radius: predicted 0.80 ± (error not fully given in truncated text) fm² at fourth order in IR

## Context
This work extends the SU(2) calculation of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Kubis and Meißner (2000) to the three-flavor case, and improves upon earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of hyperon form factors by Kubis, Hammel, and Meißner (1999) and by Puglia and Ramsey-Musolf (1999). The results enable predictions for hyperon form factors that can be tested by future experiments at ELSA and TJNAF, and provide input for understanding kaon photo- and electroproduction off nucleons and light nuclei. The paper also addresses the convergence of the chiral expansion for magnetic moments, which had been debated in the literature by Jenkins, Luke, Manohar, and Savage (1993), by Durand and Ha (1998), and by Kubis and Meißner (1999).
