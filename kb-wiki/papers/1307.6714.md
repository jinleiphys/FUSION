# Neutron Star Matter

> In this presentation I discuss two aspects of the neutron-matter equation of state. One relates to the symmetry energy of nuclear matter and empirical constraints on its slope parameter at saturation density. The second deals with spatially inhomogeneous chiral phases of deconfined quark matter in the inner core of a neutron star.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\) at saturation density can be constrained more precisely via the electric dipole polarizability of \(^{208}\)Pb than via direct neutron skin measurements, while the existence of inhomogeneous chiral phases in deconfined quark matter in the inner core remains speculative and depends on the poorly understood high-density equation of state.

## Method

The author reviews constraints on the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy \(S(n)\) by correlating the measured electric dipole polarizability \(\alpha_D\) of \(^{208}\)Pb with the neutron skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np}\) and the slope parameter \(L\), using mean-field model predictions. For the inner core, the author examines spatially modulated chiral phases in QCD-like models (Nambu Jona-Lasinio model, chiral quark-meson model) by numerically minimizing the thermodynamic potential for one-dimensional plate-like and two-dimensional square and hexagonal lattice modulations of the chiral condensate at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb from the PREX experiment is \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.34^{+0.15}_{-0.17}\) fm (limited statistics).
- The measured electric dipole polarizability of \(^{208}\)Pb is \(\alpha_D = 20.1 \pm 0.6\) fm\(^3\)/e\(^2\), which translates to a skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.156 \pm 0.021\) fm.
- Nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The pulsar masses PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+043 are \(1.97 \pm 0.04\) and \(2.01 \pm 0.04\) solar masses, respectively.
- For two-dimensional chiral modulations, a sharp onset of the crystalline phase occurs around \(\mu \approx 310\) MeV at \(T=0\).

## Context

This work extends constraints on the symmetry energy slope \(L\) from nuclear physics, building on correlations established by LaPr (2001), FuWo (2006), and ACVW (2011), and uses the dipole polarizability measurement by Tamii (2011) to improve upon the PREX neutron skin result. The discussion of inhomogeneous chiral phases follows studies by Nickel (2009) and CNB (2010) in QCD-like models, and finds that one-dimensional plate-like modulations are energetically favored over two-dimensional rod-like structures, which may affect transport properties in the inner core if deconfined quark matter exists.
