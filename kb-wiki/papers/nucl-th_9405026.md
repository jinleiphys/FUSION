# Hermitian boson mapping and finite truncation

> Starting from a general, microscopic fermion-to-boson mapping that preserves Hermitian conjugation, we discuss truncations of the boson Fock space basis. We give conditions under which the exact boson images of finite fermion operators are also finite (e.g., a 1+2-body fermion Hamiltonian is mapped to a 1+2-body boson Hamiltonian) in the truncated basis. For the most general case, where the image is not necessarily exactly finite, we discuss how to make practical and controlled approximations.

# Hermitian boson mapping and finite truncation

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Hermitian, finite boson image of a fermion Hamiltonian can be obtained in a truncated boson Fock space only under specific conditions: the truncated set of fermion pair operators must satisfy a completeness relation and, for two-body interactions, an additional factorization condition; otherwise the exact boson image is not necessarily finite and requires a systematic expansion.

## Method

The authors construct boson representations by mapping matrix elements of fermion operators rather than commutation relations, using a vector coherent state method generalized from Rowe (1984). They show that in the full Fock space the boson representation of any Hamiltonian factorizes into a finite boson image times an infinite norm operator, with the image commuting with the norm. For truncated spaces, they derive sufficient conditions for exact factorization to persist, and for cases where it does not, they propose a linked-cluster expansion using the inverse square root of the norm operator in the physical subspace to construct controlled approximations.

## Key numbers

- The norm operator is expressed as a normal-ordered exponential: $\hat{\cal N}_B = \colon \exp \left( -\sum_{k=2}^\infty \frac{(-1)^k}{2k} \hat{C}_k \right) \colon$, where $\hat{C}_k$ are $k$th order Casimir operators of SU($2\Omega$).
- For the pairing interaction in the seniority-0 subspace with a single $s$-boson, the truncated representation gives $\left [\hat{V}^P_B \right ]_T = -G\left( s^\dagger s + \frac{1}{2\Omega^2} s^\dagger s^\dagger ss \right)$, while the correct image from the numerical truncation method is $\bar{V}^P_B = -G\left( s^\dagger s - \frac{1}{\Omega} s^\dagger s^\dagger ss \right)$.
- The renormalization factor $f_{\Omega_a} = 4\Omega_a^2/N_a$ for the two-boson part of the image in truncated spaces ranges from unity (full space) to 2 for a very small subspace, where $N_a = \Omega_a(2\Omega_a+(-1)^p)$.
- For the SO(8) and Sp(6) models, the truncated subspace uses pairs with pseudo orbital angular momentum $K=0$ (SO(8)) or pseudospin $I=0$ (Sp(6)), where the completeness relation holds.

## Context

This work extends the Hermitian boson mapping approach of Marshalek (1987) and Kuchta (1989) by providing explicit conditions under which finite, Hermitian boson images exist in truncated spaces, addressing a long-standing problem in microscopic foundations of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello (1975). The paper contrasts with the non-Hermitian Dyson mapping (1956) and the infinite-expansion Marumori mapping (1964), showing that the Hermitian approach avoids mixing of physical and spurious states. The results enable systematic approximations for realistic nuclear structure calculations, particularly for models like SO(8) and Sp(6) where the completeness condition holds, and provide a practical linked-cluster expansion method for general truncations.
