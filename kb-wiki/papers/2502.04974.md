# Speed of sound in Kaluza-Klein Fermi gas

> A five-dimensional Kaluza-Klein spacetime model is considered, with one extra compactified spatial dimension. The equation of state of an electrically neutral, zero-temperature Fermi gas with a repulsive linear potential is described. From the equation of state, the speed of sound squared is calculated and shown for different model parameters. Its properties are studied from lower energies up to the conformal limit.

## Key claim
The speed of sound squared in a five-dimensional Kaluza-Klein Fermi gas with a repulsive linear potential can reach the conformal limit of 1/4 (rather than the usual 3D limit of 1/3) when an infinite number of Kaluza-Klein excitation levels are allowed, but only at energy densities far above those relevant for realistic neutron stars (central energy density ~1100±150 MeV/fm³ for a maximal-mass pulsar).

## Method
The authors constructed a zero-temperature Fermi gas model in a 5D Kaluza-Klein spacetime with one compactified spatial dimension (radius r_c), using neutrons (mass 939.57 MeV) as ground-state baryons with a Kaluza-Klein mass ladder. A repulsive linear potential U(n) = ξ n was added to model nuclear interactions, and the speed of sound squared c_s² = ∂p/∂ε was computed from the resulting equation of state for various values of the extra dimension size r_c, interaction strength ξ, and excitation number N_exc.

## Key numbers
- Neutron mass used as ground-state baryon: 939.57 MeV
- Central energy density for a realistic maximal-mass stable pulsar (~2 solar masses): ε_c = 1100 ± 150 MeV/fm³
- Speed of sound squared saturation occurs at energy densities around 10⁶–10⁸ MeV/fm³ (BSM regime)
- For N_exc = ∞, the conformal limit becomes c_s² = 1/4 (compared to the 3D conformal limit of 1/3)
- Interaction strengths tested: ξ = 500, 1000, and 1500 MeV/fm³
- Extra dimension sizes tested: r_c = 0.01 fm and 100 fm
- For N_exc = 10 with ξ = 0 and r_c = 0.01 fm, 10 dips are visible corresponding to new energy levels

## Context
This work extends the Kaluza-Klein neutron star model of Horváth et al. (2024) by analyzing the sensitivity of the speed of sound squared to model parameters (r_c, ξ, N_exc). It builds on prior work by Barnaföldi et al. (2007, 2015, 2020) and Karsai et al. (2016) that used simplified equations of state to constrain the compactification radius from pulsar data. The results enable future comparisons with neutron star observations and high-energy collision data (Ozel 2016, Gardim 2019, Tsang 2018, Wei 2020) to test for extra-dimensional signatures, though the relevant energy scales for realistic neutron stars remain well below the BSM regime where Kaluza-Klein effects become prominent.
