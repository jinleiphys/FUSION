# Dropping Sigma-Meson Mass and In-Medium Pion-Pion Correlations

> The influence of a dropping sigma-meson mass on previously calculated in-medium pion-pion correlations in the J=I=0 (sigma-meson) channel is investigated. It is found that the invariant-mass distribution around the vacuum threshold experiences a further strong enhancement over and above the standard many-body effects. The relevance of this result for the theoretical interpretation of this result for the theoretical interpretation of recent A(pi-2 pi)X data is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a dropping sigma-meson mass, linked to partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter, further enhances the previously calculated in-medium pion-pion correlations in the scalar-isoscalar channel, but the authors caution that vertex corrections and Pauli-blocking effects, not fully included, could weaken the results, and that refined reaction calculations are needed to confirm the connection to experimental data.

## Method

The authors use the linear sigma model treated in leading order of the 1/N expansion to compute the in-medium sigma-meson propagator and the T-matrix for pion-pion scattering. The sigma-meson mass is given a simple linear density dependence \( m_{\sigma}(\rho) = m_{\sigma}(1 - \alpha \rho/\rho_0) \) with \(\alpha\) values of 0.2 and 0.3, extracted from the density dependence of the chiral condensate. The pion propagator includes coupling to \(\Delta\)-hole, particle-hole, and 2p-2h excitations in cold nuclear matter, renormalized by repulsive short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density, the peak height of the imaginary part of the in-medium sigma-meson propagator is increased by a factor of 2 for \(\alpha = 0.2\) and by a factor of 4 for \(\alpha = 0.3\), compared to the case without sigma-mass modification (\(\alpha = 0\)).
- The vacuum sigma-meson mass \(m_{\sigma}\) is used as the reference, with the density-dependent ansatz \( m_{\sigma}(\rho) = m_{\sigma}(1 - \alpha \rho/\rho_0) \), where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear saturation density.
- The range of \(\alpha\) values used (0.2 to 0.3) is taken from the quantitative investigation by Hatsuda et al. (1999).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of in-medium pion-pion correlations by Schuck et al. (1997) and Aouissat et al. (1998), which found a strong enhancement near the \(2m_{\pi}\) threshold from p-wave renormalization of pions. It directly addresses the suggestion by Hatsuda et al. (1999) that a dropping sigma-meson mass from chiral symmetry restoration could produce similar effects, and combines both mechanisms. The results are relevant for interpreting the CHAOS collaboration data on the \(A(\pi,2\pi)X\) reaction (Bonutti et al., 1999), and the authors propose that future \(A(\gamma,2\pi)\) experiments at MAMI Mainz could further test the predictions by probing the nuclear interior with varying mass number.
