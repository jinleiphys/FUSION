# QCD sum rule approach for the light scalar mesons as four-quark states

> We study the two point-function for the scalar mesons $σ, κ, f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ as diquak-antidiquark states. We also study the decays of these mesons into $ππ$, $Kπ$ and $K\bar{K}$. We found that the couplings are consistent with existing experimental data, pointing in favor of the four-quark structure for the light scalar mesons.

# QCD sum rule approach for the light scalar mesons as four-quark states

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule calculations for the hadronic couplings of the scalar mesons sigma, kappa, f0(980) and a0(980) treated as diquark-antidiquark states produce results that are consistent with existing experimental data, but the agreement is not exact within error bars, the biggest discrepancy appears for the f0 to pi pi coupling, where alpha_s corrections may play an important role.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules to evaluate two-point correlation functions for the scalar meson decay constants and three-point correlation functions for the hadronic couplings to pseudoscalar pairs. The scalar mesons are described by interpolating currents representing S-wave bound states of a diquark-antidiquark pair, with the diquark taken as a spin-zero colour anti-triplet and flavour anti-triplet. For the three-point functions, the authors work at the pion pole with q^2=0, perform a single Borel transform, and extract the coupling constants by fitting the linear M^2 dependence and extrapolating to M^2=0 to account for pole-continuum transitions.

## Key numbers

- Scalar meson decay constants from two-point sum rules: f_sigma = (7.5 ± 1.0) MeV, f_kappa = (1.6 ± 0.3) MeV, f_f0 = (1.1 ± 0.1) MeV
- Hadronic coupling from three-point sum rules: g_sigma pi+ pi- = (3.1 ± 0.5) GeV
- Hadronic coupling: g_kappa K+ pi- = (3.6 ± 0.3) GeV
- Hadronic coupling: g_f0 K+ K- = g_a0 K+ K- = (1.6 ± 0.1) GeV
- Hadronic coupling: g_f0 pi+ pi- = (0.47 ± 0.05) GeV
- Experimental couplings extracted from decay widths: g_sigma pi+ pi- exp = (2.6 ± 0.2) GeV, g_kappa K+ pi- exp = (4.5 ± 0.4) GeV, g_f0 pi+ pi- exp = (1.6 ± 0.8) GeV
- Input masses used: m_sigma = 0.5 GeV, m_kappa = 0.8 GeV, m_f0 = 0.98 GeV
- Continuum thresholds: s0_sigma = 1.0 GeV^2, s0_kappa = 1.2 GeV^2, s0_f0 = 1.5 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule studies of the f0(980) as a four-quark state by Latorre and Pascual (1985) for the decay constant and by Narison (1986) for the hadronic couplings, now considering the full scalar nonet (sigma, kappa, f0, a0) with different interpolating currents. The four-quark interpretation follows the diquark-antidiquark picture originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) and the specific current construction of Maiani, Piccinini, Polosa and Riquer (2004). The results support the four-quark structure for light scalar mesons below 1 GeV, complementing lattice calculations by Alford and Jaffe (2000) and helping to explain the observed decay patterns in charmed meson decays from the E791 Collaboration (2001-2002).
