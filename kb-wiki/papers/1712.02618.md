# Wounded quark emission function at the top RHIC energy

> The wounded nucleon and quark emission functions are extracted for different centralities in d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200\ \text{GeV}$ using Monte Carlo simulations and experimental data. The shape of the emission function depends on centrality in the wounded nucleon model, whereas it is practically universal (within uncertainties) in the wounded quark model. Predictions for $dN_{ch}/dη$ distributions in p+Au and $^3$He+Au collisions are presented.

# Wounded quark emission function at the top RHIC energy

## Key claim

The shape of the wounded source emission function depends on centrality in the wounded nucleon model, whereas it is practically universal (within uncertainties) for various centralities in the wounded quark model, suggesting that soft particle production in d+Au collisions is controlled by the number of wounded quarks rather than wounded nucleons.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of d+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, drawing nucleon positions in gold from a Woods-Saxon distribution (radius R = 6.38 fm, skin depth a = 0.535 fm) and deuteron nucleon positions from a Hulthen form. For the wounded quark model, three constituent quarks per nucleon were distributed according to an exponential density with proton radius rp = 0.81 fm. The inelastic quark-quark cross section σqq was determined by trial and error to reproduce the nucleon-nucleon cross section σnn = 41 mb, yielding σqq ≈ 7 mb. Using Eq. (2) from the paper, the emission function F(η) was extracted for each centrality class from the PHOBOS dNch/dη data.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: σnn = 41 mb at √s = 200 GeV
- Inelastic quark-quark cross section: σqq ≈ 7 mb, determined by matching the proton-proton collision probability to 41 mb
- Woods-Saxon parameters for gold: nuclear radius R = 6.38 fm, skin depth a = 0.535 fm
- Hulthen parameters for deuteron: A = 0.457 fm⁻¹, B = 2.35 fm⁻¹
- Proton radius for quark distribution: rp = 0.81 fm
- Mean number of wounded nucleons in d+Au min-bias: deuteron = 1.61, gold = 6.69
- Mean number of wounded quarks in d+Au min-bias: deuteron = 3.73, gold = 8.97
- Mean number of wounded quarks in 0-20% central d+Au: deuteron = 5.63, gold = 19.01
- Negative binomial distribution parameters for wounded nucleons: ⟨n⟩ = 5, k = 1
- Negative binomial distribution parameters for wounded quarks: ⟨nq⟩ = 5/1.3, kq = 1/1.3

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bialas (2004) and others that extracted average (over centrality) emission functions, but did not investigate the centrality dependence of F(η). The wounded quark model has been successfully applied to various colliding systems and energies by Adler (2013), Adare (2015), Bozek (2016), Lacey (2016), Loizides (2016), and Mitchell (2016). The results enable predictions for dNch/dη distributions in p+Au and ³He+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, which the authors provide for future experimental verification by PHENIX. The universal wounded quark emission function also offers a foundation for interpreting longitudinal structure in the color glass condensate framework.
