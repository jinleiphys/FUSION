# Medium modified leading hadrons, jets and sub-jets in a single formalism

> Vacuum and medium modified fragmentation functions are used to study the properties of hadrons produced in hard interactions, in $p$-$p$ and $A$-$A$ collisions, respectively. We study the modification of reconstructed jets and high transverse momentum (high-$p_\mathrm{T}$) hadrons as they propagate through a dense medium. Jets and jet modification are studied within the framework of a factorized jet function. The vacuum jet function is found consistent with PYTHIA simulations. Both the modification of the fragmentation function and the jet function are carried out within the identical higher-twist energy loss formalism. This approach is then extended to di-hadron production within a jet. Its further extension to a "di-prong" function and its application to the soft-drop measurements are discussed. The methodology of extending these semi-analytical calculations to full Monte-Carlo simulations is outlined. All calculations are carried out within a (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic simulation and compared to experimental data from a variety of collision energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a single higher-twist energy-loss formalism can simultaneously describe medium-modified leading hadrons, narrow jets, and soft-drop jet substructure, provided the jet radius is small enough that the jet forms outside the QGP medium.

## Method

The authors first compute vacuum quark and gluon jet functions at low energy (E=10 GeV) and large radius (R=1.0) using PYTHIA simulations, then boost them to higher energy (E=100 GeV) where the jet radius shrinks by a factor of 10. They evolve these boosted jet functions to higher virtuality scales using DGLAP evolution equations in vacuum and a medium-modified evolution equation based on the higher-twist formalism with a (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic background. For jet substructure, they define a "di-prong function" (DPF) analogous to the di-hadron fragmentation function and evolve it similarly, then compare results to PYTHIA simulations and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum jet functions were calculated at E=10 GeV and R=1.0, then boosted to E=100 GeV where the radius reduces by a factor of 10
- Jet functions were evolved from Q=10 GeV to Q=100 GeV using DGLAP evolution
- Medium-modified evolution of extremely narrow jets with R=0.01 showed good agreement with CMS single-hadron R_AA data
- Di-prong functions were calculated at Q=10 GeV and Q=100 GeV (with z=z1+z2=0.75) from PYTHIA and compared to evolved results at Q=100 GeV

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism previously used for single-hadron fragmentation functions (Wang 2001, Majumder 2007) to jet functions and di-prong functions, providing a unified description. It differs from the DDSS approach (Dasgupta 2014), which assumed a delta-function jet function at R=1, and the KRV approach (Kang 2016), which used SCET-based evolution. The formalism enables simultaneous predictions for leading hadrons, small-radius jets, and soft-drop jet substructure in heavy-ion collisions, with planned extension to angular evolution for variable jet radii and predictions from RHIC to LHC energies.
