# Three loop correction in the formation of QGP droplet

> Quark-gluon plasma (QGP) droplet formation is re-considered with the addition of three loop correction to the earlier loop factors in the mean field potential. The correction of the three loop factor increases stability in the droplet formations of QGP at different parametrization factors of the QGP fluid and it is in better agreement in comparison to the lattice results of pressure, energy density and other thermodynamic relations. This implies that the contribution of the three loop enhances in showing the characteristic features of the QGP fluid. It shows that increasing the loop increased the strength of parametrization value which we defined earlier as a number parameter of fluid dynamics. It indicates that the model with the loop correction boosts in explaining about the formation of QGP droplet in the expansion of early universe

## Key claim

The addition of a three-loop correction to the mean field potential improves the agreement of the model's thermodynamic predictions (pressure, energy density, entropy, speed of sound) with lattice QCD results, but only after a "necessary modification" of the lower momentum cutoff in the free-energy integration; without this rescaling, the model gives too large values at low temperature.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian for quarks and gluons using a thermal mass that includes one-, two-, and three-loop coefficients (a₁, a₂, a₃) in the strong coupling expansion. They compute the density of states from the confining mean-field potential, then evaluate the free energy for fermions (u, d, s quarks) and bosons (gluons) plus light hadrons, and minimize the total free energy to obtain droplet sizes and surface tension. The lower limit of the momentum integration is rescaled empirically to match lattice data.

## Key numbers

- Quark flow parameter: γ_q = 1/14; gluon flow parameter: γ_g = (60–68) × γ_q (i.e., 60γ_q, 64γ_q, 68γ_q) for stable droplet formation.
- QCD scale parameter: Λ ≈ 0.15 GeV.
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 0.15 GeV; up and down quark masses set to 0 MeV.
- Three-loop coefficient a₃ = 209.884 − 51.4048 n_l + 2.9061 n_l² − 0.0214 n_l³, where n_l is the number of light quark flavors.
- Degeneracy factors: 6 for quarks, 8 for gluons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier one-loop and two-loop models (Jena et al., earlier papers) by adding the three-loop correction to the mean-field potential. It follows the liquid-drop model approach of Ramanathan et al. (Ramanathan, earlier work) for computing surface tension and droplet stability. The improved agreement with lattice data (Kajantie, Iwasaki, Michael, earlier works) suggests the model can describe QCD phase structure and QGP droplet formation relevant to early-universe expansion, though the empirical rescaling of the momentum cutoff remains a limitation.
