# Non-Hermitian Ferromagnetism in an Ultracold Fermi Gas

> We develop a non-Hermitian effective theory for a repulsively interacting Fermi gas in the excited branch. The on-shell $T$-matrix is employed as a complex-valued interaction term, which describes a repulsive interaction between atoms in the excited branch and a two-body inelastic decay to the attractive branch. To see the feature of this model, we have addressed, in the weak coupling regime, the excitation properties of a repulsive Fermi polaron as well as the time-dependent number density. The analytic expressions obtained for these quantities qualitatively show a good agreement with recent experiments. By calculating the dynamical transverse spin susceptibility in the random phase approximation, we show that a ferromagnetic system with nonzero polarization undergoes a dynamical instability and tends towards a heterogeneous phase.

## Key claim

The paper develops a non-Hermitian effective theory for a repulsively interacting Fermi gas in the excited branch, where the complex-valued interaction derived from the on-shell two-body T-matrix captures both the repulsive interaction and the inelastic two-body decay to the attractive branch; the authors show that a ferromagnetic system with nonzero polarization undergoes a dynamical instability and tends towards a heterogeneous phase, but the results are restricted to the weak coupling regime and neglect Pauli-blocking effects, three-body recombination, and the inverse pumping process from molecular dissociation.

## Method

The authors construct a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian using the on-shell two-body T-matrix with an attractive contact interaction, yielding a complex-valued repulsive interaction U_R(k) = (4πa/m)(1-ika)/(1+k²a²) that depends on the incoming relative momentum. They compute the repulsive Fermi polaron properties (energy, decay rate, effective mass) using a Hartree (lowest-order) self-energy approximation, and derive the time-dependent number density from the Lindblad equation for the reduced density matrix in the repulsive branch. The dynamical transverse spin susceptibility is calculated within the random phase approximation (RPA) to analyze the ferromagnetic instability.

## Key numbers

- Repulsive polaron energy: E_r = ε_F,↑ (16/π)[1/(k_F,↑a) - 2 tan⁻¹(k_F,↑a/2)/(k_F,↑a)²]
- Decay rate: Γ = ε_F,↑ (32/π)[(k_F,↑a/2)² - ln(1+(k_F,↑a/2)²)]/(k_F,↑a)²
- Effective mass ratio: m/m* = 1 - (16/3π)(k_F,↑a)³/(4+k_F,↑²a²)²
- Quasiparticle residue: Z = [1 + (8/π)[tan⁻¹(k_F,↑a/2) - 2k_F,↑a/(4+k_F,↑²a²)]]⁻¹
- Time-dependent number density for unpolarized gas shown for k_F,σa = 0.35, 0.78, and 1.53, compared with experimental results from Amico (2020)

## Context

This work extends theoretical efforts on ferromagnetism in repulsive Fermi gases beyond mean-field approximations such as quantum Monte-Carlo simulations by Pilati (2010) and perturbation theories by Duine (2005) and He (2016), by incorporating non-Hermitian effects from two-body inelastic decay. The non-Hermitian approach is analogous to empirical optical potential models used in nuclear physics by Feshbach (1958) and provides a framework to understand recent experiments on out-of-equilibrium dynamics by Amico (2020) and Scazza (2020), which show that metastable ferromagnetic states can evolve into heterogeneous phases rather than simply decaying to thermal equilibrium. The model enables future studies of non-equilibrium ferromagnetism and phase separation in ultracold Fermi gases, though quantitative description requires including Pauli-blocking effects, three-body recombination, and the inverse molecular dissociation process.
