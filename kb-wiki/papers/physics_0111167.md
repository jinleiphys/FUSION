# Majorana solution of the Thomas-Fermi equation

> We report on an original method, due to Majorana, leading to a semi-analytical series solution of the Thomas-Fermi equation, with appropriate boundary conditions, in terms of only one quadrature. We also deduce a general formula for such a solution which avoids numerical integration, but is expressed in terms of the roots of a given polynomial equation.

## Key claim

Majorana’s semi-analytical series solution of the Thomas-Fermi equation avoids numerical integration by expressing the solution in terms of a single quadrature and, in a further elaboration, in terms of the roots of a polynomial equation; the method is presented as an original alternative to Sommerfeld’s approximate solution, which had a divergent first derivative at the origin.

## Method

The Thomas-Fermi equation \(\varphi'' = \varphi^{3/2} / \sqrt{x}\) with boundary conditions \(\varphi(0)=1\) and \(\varphi(\infty)=0\) is transformed via a double change of variables \((x,\varphi) \to (t,u)\) into a first-order differential equation for \(u(t)\). The function \(u(t)\) is expanded as a power series in \(\tau = 1-t\), with coefficients determined iteratively from a recurrence relation. The parametric solution for \(x(t)\) and \(\varphi(t)\) is then expressed in terms of a single quadrature involving \(u(t)\), and an approximate analytic form is obtained by truncating the series and decomposing the integrand into rational functions whose integration yields a result depending on the roots of a polynomial.

## Key numbers

* The first coefficient of the series expansion for \(u(t)\) is \(a_1 = 9 - \sqrt{73} \approx 0.455996\).
* The sum of the series coefficients is \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n \approx 2.7746\), determined from the finite value \(\varphi'_0 \approx 1.588\).
* The series exhibits geometric convergence with \(a_n / a_{n-1} \sim 4/5\) for \(n \to \infty\).
* Truncating the series at \(n=10\) yields a relative error of about \(0.1\%\) compared to the exact numerical solution; truncating at \(n=20\) yields a relative error of about \(0.01\%\).
* Numerical values for the first 20 coefficients are tabulated (e.g., \(a_2 = 0.304455\), \(a_3 = 0.222180\), \(a_4 = 0.168213\), \(a_5 = 0.129804\), \(a_6 = 0.101300\), \(a_7 = 0.0796352\), \(a_8 = 0.0629230\), \(a_9 = 0.0499053\), \(a_{10} = 0.0396962\)).

## Context

This work extends the known exact particular solution of the Thomas-Fermi equation found by Sommerfeld (1932), which satisfied only the boundary condition at infinity and had a divergent first derivative at the origin. Majorana’s method provides a convergent series solution that satisfies both boundary conditions and can be generalized to other ordinary differential equations. The solution is potentially relevant for atomic physics and for nuclear matter problems in neutron stars, as discussed by Shapiro and Teukolsky (1983).
