# Equation of State at High-Baryon Density and Compact Stellar Objects

> In this contribution I review the connection between compact stars and high-baryon density matter, focusing on astrophysical observables for deconfinement to quark matter. I discuss modern ingredients, repositories, and constraints for the neutron-star equations of state. Finally, I draw comparisons between dense and hot matter created in neutron-star mergers and heavy-ion collisions, and the possibility of quantitatively establishing a link between them.

# Equation of State at High-Baryon Density and Compact Stellar Objects

## Key claim
The connection between neutron-star mergers and heavy-ion collisions can now be quantitatively established through self-consistent comparisons of matter at similar densities, temperatures, and entropies, despite an 18-order-of-magnitude difference between the systems. However, the deconfinement of quark matter inside cold beta-equilibrated neutron stars remains an open question, as it is not yet known whether massive neutron-star cores are dense enough for deconfinement to take place.

## Method
The author reviews the equation of state (EoS) for neutron-star matter, covering zero-temperature beta-equilibrated matter in neutron-star cores and finite-temperature matter relevant for proto-neutron stars and mergers. The review draws on constraints from low-energy nuclear experiments around nuclear saturation density, chiral effective field theory, heavy-ion collisions, lattice QCD at low density, and perturbative QCD at asymptotically high energies. The discussion includes a comparison between neutron-star mergers and heavy-ion collisions using the SU(3) CMF model with excluded volume including parity doublets in relativistic hydrodynamics simulations with full general relativity.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-star masses range from 1 to 2 solar masses (M$_\odot$); modeling neutron stars as a free gas of baryons generates errors of approximately 100% in reproducing observed masses.
- Neutron-star densities span from 1 g/cm$^3$ in the atmosphere to about 10$^{15}$ g/cm$^3$ in the center, corresponding to a number density of about 1 baryon per fm$^3$.
- Nuclear saturation density $n_0$ is approximately 0.15 fm$^{-3}$; the crust extends until around this density.
- The conformal limit of massless free quarks requires the speed of sound squared $c_s^2$ to approach 1/3 from below at asymptotically large densities, while 2 M$_\odot$ stars require $c_s^2$ approaching 1 in natural units at intermediate densities.
- The binary neutron-star merger simulation used a total mass of 2.8 M$_\odot$; the heavy-ion collision was Au+Au at laboratory energy E$_{\rm{lab}}$ = 450 MeV.

## Context
This review extends the line of work connecting neutron-star observables to the high-density equation of state, building on constraints from nuclear experiments, chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2013, Tews 2018), and astrophysical observations including NICER and LIGO/VIRGO results. The paper discusses modern EoS repositories such as CompOSE and the developing MUSES framework, and highlights recent work by Most (2022) that provides the first self-consistent comparison between neutron-star mergers and low-energy heavy-ion collisions, enabling quantitative study of astrophysics in the laboratory. Open questions include whether deconfinement to quark matter occurs inside neutron stars and whether it precedes the appearance of hyperons.
