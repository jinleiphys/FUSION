# Fermion Masses, Neutrino Mixing and Higgs-Mediated Flavor Violation in 3HDM with $S_3$ Permutation Symmetry

> The Yukawa and scalar sectors of a general $S_3$-symmetric three-Higgs doublet model (3HDM) are investigated. The Yukawa interactions are constructed in an $S_3$-invariant way, while the scalar potential contains $S_3$ soft-breaking terms. Global fits to the quark/lepton masses and CKM/PMNS matrices are performed. Excellent fits to all fermion mass and mixing parameters are obtained. Both normal ordering and inverted ordering of neutrino masses are found to be admissible within the framework, with a prediction for the CP-violation phase, $δ_{CP} \simeq 120^0$. The fit results in the Yukawa sector are further investigated, together with the scalar sector, imposing constraints from Higgs-mediated neutral meson mixing and neutron electric dipole moment (EDM). We explore the lowest allowed mass of the heavy Higgs bosons, consistent with these constraints, and find it to be about 17 TeV. The corresponding neutron EDM is around $1.7\times10^{-27}$ e-cm, which is within reach of proposed experiments. It is found that the constraints from the $K$-meson system dominate, while those from the $D$ meson system are marginal.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a general $S_3$-symmetric three-Higgs doublet model (3HDM) with soft $S_3$ breaking in the scalar sector can simultaneously fit all quark and lepton masses and mixing parameters, but only if the heavy Higgs bosons have a mass of at least about 17 TeV, with the dominant constraint coming from $K^0$-$\overline{K}^0$ mixing rather than from $D$ or $B$ meson systems.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general $S_3$-invariant Yukawa interactions for quarks and leptons, assigning the three fermion generations to a doublet and a singlet representation of $S_3$, and introduced right-handed neutrinos to generate neutrino masses via the seesaw mechanism. They performed global fits to quark and lepton masses, the CKM matrix, and the PMNS matrix, then imposed constraints from Higgs-mediated neutral meson mixing ($K^0$, $B_d^0$, $B_s^0$, $D^0$) and the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) to determine the lower bound on heavy Higgs masses.

## Key numbers

- The lowest allowed mass of the heavy Higgs bosons consistent with all constraints is about 17 TeV.
- The corresponding neutron EDM is around $1.7 \times 10^{-27}$ e-cm, about one order of magnitude below the current experimental limit of $1.8 \times 10^{-26}$ e-cm.
- The CP-violation phase in the PMNS matrix is predicted to be $\delta_{CP} \simeq 120^\circ$.
- The constraints from the $K$-meson system dominate, with $\Delta m_K \lesssim 3.484 \times 10^{-15}$ GeV and $|\epsilon_K| \lesssim 2.23 \times 10^{-3}$; $D$ meson constraints are marginal with $\Delta m_D \lesssim 6.25 \times 10^{-15}$ GeV.
- The electroweak vacuum expectation value is fixed at $\sqrt{v_1^2+v_2^2+v_3^2}=246$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 3HDM with $S_3$ symmetry by Teshima (2011, 2012), GonzalezCanales (2013), and Das (2015), which focused on simplified cases with additional symmetries or CP conservation in the scalar sector. The authors construct the most general $S_3$-invariant interactions and simultaneously analyze quark and lepton sectors while incorporating constraints from neutral meson mixing and neutron EDM. The results enable future experimental searches for heavy Higgs bosons around 17 TeV and neutron EDM measurements at the $10^{-27}$ e-cm level.
