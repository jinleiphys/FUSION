# Scattering of Antiprotons by Nuclei (Atoms) in the Range of Low Energies $E\lesssim 10^5$ eV. Mirror Reflection, Diffraction, and Channeling of Antiprotons in Crystals

> Studying antiproton scattering by nuclei (atoms) in the range of low energies, we found out that the increase in the antiproton-nucleus scattering amplitude through Coulomb interaction provides the possibility to investigate spin-dependent processes accompanying the interaction of antiprotons with nuclei (polarized or unpolarized) by means of mirror reflection of antiprotons from the vacuum-matter boundary, diffraction and channeling (surface diffraction and channeling) of antiprotons in crystals.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the increase in the antiproton-nucleus scattering amplitude at low energies (E ≲ 10⁵ eV), arising from Coulomb interaction effects on the nuclear scattering amplitude, makes it possible to observe spin-dependent processes, including spin polarization and spin rotation, via mirror reflection, diffraction, and channeling of antiprotons in crystals, but the magnitude of these effects is estimated to be on the order of 10⁻¹ to 10⁻², meaning the effects are small but potentially measurable.

## Method

The author derives the mirror-reflection coefficient for antiprotons from a vacuum-matter boundary using a microscopic summation of scattered waves from planes of scatterers, obtaining an expression involving the coherent elastic scattering amplitude at a nonzero angle equal to twice the grazing angle. The scattering amplitude is decomposed into Coulomb and nuclear contributions, with the nuclear amplitude modified by a Coulomb enhancement factor g = 2παZc/v for negatively charged particles. The author then analyzes how spin-orbit interaction and scattering by polarized nuclei contribute to the reflection amplitude, and derives expressions for the polarization and spin rotation of reflected antiprotons.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton energy range considered: E ≲ 10⁵ eV
- For antiproton energies from 100 to 1000 eV, using a typical nuclear scattering amplitude |f| ∼ 10⁻¹² cm, the mirror-reflection coefficient can be close to unity only at grazing angles φ ≤ 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ radians for pure nuclear scattering
- The Coulomb enhancement factor for negatively charged particles (attractive case) is g_att = 2παZc/v, where α is the fine structure constant
- The ratio |f_N|/|f_Coul| is estimated as ≤ 3 × 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻² for k ≤ 10¹⁰ to 10¹¹ cm⁻¹ and φ ∼ 10⁻¹ radians
- The characteristic energy E_A = ℏ²/(4mR_A²) ≈ 10⁻² eV, below which the Coulomb scattering cross section changes behavior
- The contribution of spin-orbit scattering to the mirror-reflection coefficient is estimated as R_so ∼ 10⁻¹ to 10⁻²
- The degree of polarization acquired by an initially unpolarized beam upon reflection is estimated at 10⁻¹ to 10⁻²
- The spin rotation angle for a polarized beam incident on a polarized target is estimated at 10⁻¹ to 10⁻² radians

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Coulomb effects on antiproton-nucleus scattering previously discussed in Baryshevsky (PHL, year not given in text), which showed that Coulomb scattering increases the real part of the elastic scattering amplitude as antiproton energy decreases. The paper generalizes the treatment of mirror reflection from the standard neutron optics formalism (Gurevich and Tarasov, Landau and Lifshitz) to account for the fact that the mirror-reflection amplitude is determined by scattering at a nonzero angle (twice the grazing angle) rather than zero-angle scattering, following the approach of Heno (year not given). The results enable experimental determination of the amplitudes A₁ and A₂ describing spin-dependent antiproton-nucleus scattering by measuring polarization and spin rotation in mirror reflection, diffraction, and channeling experiments, which could be performed at facilities such as FLAIR.
