# Slowly Rotating Two-Fluid Neutron Stars: Coupled Frame-Dragging, Inertia Splitting, and Universal Relations

> We develop a fully relativistic framework to study the rotational response of gravitationally coupled two-fluid neutron stars within the slow-rotation approximation. Treating the two components as independently conserved perfect fluids interacting only through spacetime curvature, we derive the coupled equilibrium and frame-dragging equations and exploit their linear structure to construct a basis decomposition of the rotational response. This formulation leads to a natural definition of the effective total moment of inertia, which generalizes the single-fluid concept and depends solely on the equilibrium background. It further reveals that the coupled system admits two intrinsic collective rotational eigenmodes, characterized by distinct eigen-moments of inertia, even in the absence of relative rotation between the fluids. Applying this framework to neutron stars containing dark matter, we explore how the presence of an additional gravitationally bound component modifies the global rotational response and its relation to tidal deformability. Our results demonstrate that the persistence or breakdown of rotational-tidal universality in two-fluid neutron stars is governed by dark-sector microphysics rather than by the mere presence of an additional component, and establish a unified framework for interpreting rotational observables, intrinsic mode structure, and universal relations in multi-component relativistic stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in gravitationally coupled two-fluid neutron stars, the system admits two intrinsic collective rotational eigenmodes characterized by distinct eigen-moments of inertia even without relative rotation between the fluids, and that the persistence or breakdown of rotational-tidal universal relations is governed by dark-sector microphysics rather than merely by the presence of an additional component.

## Method

The authors develop a fully relativistic slow-rotation framework for two-fluid neutron stars by generalizing the Hartle-Thorne formalism to two independently conserved perfect fluids interacting only through spacetime curvature. They derive coupled equilibrium and frame-dragging equations, exploit the linear structure of the master equation to construct a basis decomposition of the rotational response, and compute the effective total moment of inertia and eigen-moments of inertia for configurations containing baryonic matter and a dark matter component. The framework is applied to dark-matter-admixed neutron stars using representative dark matter models including a mirror-like dark matter model and a fermionic self-interacting sector.

## Key numbers

- The total angular momentum of the two-fluid star is given by \( J_T = \frac{8\pi}{3} \int_0^{R_Y} [ (\mathcal{E}_X + P_X)(\Omega_X - \omega) + (\mathcal{E}_Y + P_Y)(\Omega_Y - \omega) ] r^4 e^{\Lambda - \Phi} dr \), where \( R_Y \) is the outer radius of fluid Y.
- The effective total moment of inertia is defined as \( I_T^{\rm eff} \equiv I_X^{\rm eff} + I_Y^{\rm eff} \), where \( I_X^{\rm eff} \) and \( I_Y^{\rm eff} \) are coefficients in the linear decomposition \( J_T = I_X^{\rm eff} \Omega_X + I_Y^{\rm eff} \Omega_Y \).
- The observationally relevant moment of inertia is defined as \( I_{\rm obs} \equiv J_T / \Omega_{\rm NM} \), where \( \Omega_{\rm NM} \) is the angular velocity of nuclear matter; in the co-rotating limit \( \Omega_X = \Omega_Y \), this reduces to \( I_{\rm obs} = I_T^{\rm eff} \).
- The frame-dragging function satisfies the boundary condition \( r d\omega/dr + 3\omega = 0 \) at the stellar surface \( r = R_Y \), and regularity requires \( (d\omega/dr)_{r=0} = 0 \) at the center.

## Context

This work extends the Hartle-Thorne formalism (1967, 1968) for single-fluid neutron stars to gravitationally coupled two-fluid systems, building on prior studies of static two-fluid TOV equations for dark-matter-admixed neutron stars (e.g., PhysRevD.77.023006, PhysRevD.111.043016, Kumar2025). The framework enables systematic analysis of rotational observables and universal relations (such as the I-Love-Q relations of PhysRevD.88.023009) in multi-component relativistic stars, and provides a foundation for interpreting gravitational-wave observations from binary neutron star mergers (e.g., GW170817, PhysRevLett.121.161101) and x-ray measurements from NICER (Dittmann 2024, Vinciguerra 2024) to constrain dark matter microphysics.
