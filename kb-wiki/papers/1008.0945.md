# Modeling the transverse momentum dependent parton distributions

> We review quark model calculations of the transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs). For the T-even TMDs, we discuss the physical origin of model relations which hold in a large class of quark models. For the T-odd TMDs we review results in a light-cone constituent quark model (CQM) with the final state interaction effects generated via single-gluon exchange mechanism. As phenomenological application, we show the good agreement between results in the light-cone CQM and available experimental data for the Collins asymmetry.

## Key claim

Quark model relations among T-even TMDs (three linear and one quadratic) can be traced to rotational symmetry assumptions about the quark and nucleon spin degrees of freedom, specifically cylindrical symmetry around the coordinate axes in the instant-form spin basis, rather than being accidental features of individual models.

## Method

The authors review T-even TMDs by expressing them in terms of light-cone Fock operators and then transforming to the canonical (instant-form) spin basis via a Wigner rotation. For T-odd TMDs, they compute the Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions in a light-cone constituent quark model (CQM) where final-state interactions are generated by a single-gluon exchange mechanism, representing the T-odd distributions as overlaps of light-cone amplitudes differing by one unit of orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The Sivers function for both up and down quarks is dominated by interference of S and P wave components, while P-D wave interference contributes at most 20%.
- Model results for the first transverse-momentum moment of the Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions are evolved to Q² = 2.5 GeV² for comparison with parametrizations.
- The Burkardt sum rule (net transverse momentum from final-state interactions sums to zero) is exactly reproduced in the model calculation of the Sivers function.
- Collins asymmetry results for charged pion production on a proton target at Q² = 2.5 GeV² agree with HERMES data, and results on deuterium are compatible with COMPASS data extending to low x.

## Context

This work extends quark model calculations of TMDs by Pasquini and collaborators (2008, 2010) and Lorce (2007), providing a unified explanation for model relations previously observed in multiple quark models (Avakian 2010, She 2009, Efremov 2009, Jakob 1997). The T-odd TMD calculations build on the light-cone CQM framework successfully applied to generalized parton distributions (Boffi 2007), form factors (Pasquini 2007), and distribution amplitudes (Pasquini 2009). The results enable phenomenological tests of the rotational symmetry assumptions underlying the model relations and provide predictions for Collins asymmetries that can be compared with ongoing SIDIS experiments at HERMES and COMPASS.
