# Nuclear physics and astrophysics of the r-process

> Some nuclear and astrophysical aspects of the r-process are discussed. Particular attention is paid to observations of abundances in metal-poor stars and their implications for the astrophysical site and yield patterns of the r-process. The effects of supernova neutrinos and related nuclear processes on the yield patterns are explored. The uncertainties in the theoretical nuclear input for the r-process are discussed and the need for experimental data is emphasized.

## Key claim

Observations of metal-poor stars suggest that supernovae are the major r-process site and that there are two distinct kinds of r-process, with supernovae from progenitors of ~8–10 solar masses producing heavy r-process elements (A>130) and those from progenitors >10 solar masses producing light r-process elements up to the A~130 peak; however, how the required r-process conditions are actually obtained in supernovae remains quite uncertain.

## Method

The paper reviews the neutrino-driven wind model of r-process nucleosynthesis in supernovae, where neutrinos emitted from a neutron star heat material and drive a mass outflow, with neutrino capture reactions determining the neutron-richness of the wind. It also discusses the neutron star merger model, where neutron star matter is ejected during binary mergers. The review compares these models against observations of elemental abundances in metal-poor Galactic halo stars, using abundance patterns to infer the astrophysical site and yield patterns of the r-process.

## Key numbers

- The solar r-process pattern shows prominent peaks at mass numbers A~130 and 195.
- The neutron star has a gravitational binding energy of ~10^53 erg.
- The neutrino-driven wind becomes neutron rich because antineutrinos producing neutrons have a higher luminosity and higher average energy than the neutrinos producing protons.
- Very metal-poor stars show substantial Eu abundances at Fe abundances as low as ~10^-3 times solar, and substantial Ba abundances at Fe abundances as low as ~10^-4 times solar.
- The absolute abundances of heavy r-process elements (A>130) differ by a factor of ~100 between CS 31082–001 and HD 122563.
- Neutrinos have average energies of ~10 MeV (electron neutrinos) and ~20 MeV (muon and tau neutrinos and their antiparticles).
- The calculated r-process pattern in Cowan (1999) shows severe deficiencies below the A~130 and 195 peaks.

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier review (Qian 2003) and builds on foundational work on the neutrino-driven wind model (Duncan 1986; Woosley 1992; Witti 1992; Meyer 1992; Takahashi 1994; Woosley 1994) and neutron star merger models (Lattimer 1974, 1976; Ruffert 1997; Rosswog 1999; Meyer 1989; Freiburghaus 1999). The observational constraints from metal-poor stars follow work by McWilliam (1995), Burris (2000), and Johnson (2001), and the diversity of supernova sources was first suggested by Wasserburg (1996) based on meteoritic data. The paper enables future work on neutrino oscillations, magnetic fields, rotation, and experimental measurements of neutron-rich nuclei far from stability.
