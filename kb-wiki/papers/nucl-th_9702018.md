# First-forbidden beta decay of 17N and 17Ne

> It is shown that differences, due to charge-dependent effects, in the 17N and 17Ne ground-state wave functions account for the fact that the experimentally measured branch for the beta+ decay of 17Ne to the first excited state of 17F is roughly a factor of two larger than expected on the basis of nuclear matrix elements which reproduce the corresponding beta- branch in the decay of 17N.

## Key claim

Differences in the ground-state wave functions of 17N and 17Ne, arising from charge-dependent effects that shift the 1s1/2 single-particle energy relative to the 0d5/2 orbit, account for the experimentally observed factor-of-two enhancement of the beta+ branch of 17Ne to the first excited state of 17F compared to predictions based on matrix elements that reproduce the corresponding beta- branch in 17N decay.

## Method

The author computed first-forbidden beta-decay matrix elements for the 1/2- to 1/2+ transitions using Woods-Saxon radial wave functions bound at physical separation energies, with the initial-state wave functions expressed in a weak-coupling 1-hbar-omega basis of a 0p-shell hole coupled to (1s0d)^2 eigenstates. To estimate charge-dependent effects, the Wildenthal USD interaction was used to obtain (sd)^2 wave functions for 18O, then the s1/2^2 diagonal matrix element was shifted by twice the s1/2 single-particle energy difference between 17O and 17F (752 keV) plus 147 keV for the Coulomb two-body matrix element difference, and the new matrix was diagonalized to obtain wave functions for 18Ne. The resulting increase in s1/2^2 intensity from 15% to 21.7% in the ground state was then applied to the 17Ne initial-state wave function.

## Key numbers

* Experimental beta+ branch of 17Ne to the 1/2+ first-excited state of 17F: 1.65(16)% (Borge et al., 1993) and 1.44(16)% (Ozawa et al., 1996); average 1.55(12)%.
* Experimental beta- branch of 17N to the 1/2+ first-excited state of 17O: 3.0(5)% (Poletti and Pronko, 1973; Alburger and Wilkinson, 1976).
* Decay energy W0 for 17N: 8.32 MeV; for 17Ne: 13.52 MeV.
* Phase-space integral I0 for 17N: 0.3051; for 17Ne: 2.380.
* Separation energy for the 1s1/2 neutron in 17N: 6.00 MeV; for the 1s1/2 proton in 17Ne: 1.48 MeV.
* Experimental f-value for 17N decay: 44.4(74); for 17Ne decay: 873(64).
* With identical nuclear structure and meson-exchange enhancement factor epsilon_mec = 1.5, predicted f(0) for 17N is 45.7 and for 17Ne is 491, giving a predicted beta branch for 17Ne of less than about 0.9%.
* With charge-dependent wave function modification (Ne') and epsilon_mec = 1.5, predicted f(0) for 17Ne is 854, in agreement with the experimental f-value of 873(64).
* s1/2^2 intensity in the 18O ground state: 15.0%; in the 18Ne ground state: 21.7% (an increase of 44%).
* Unique first-forbidden beta branch of 17N to the ground state of 17O: 1.6(5)% (Silbert and Hopkins, 1964), corresponding to f(2) = 24(8).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study by Millener and Warburton (1985) of J+/- to J-/+ first-forbidden decays in light nuclei, and addresses the discrepancy between the experimental 17Ne beta branch measured by Borge et al. (1993) and Ozawa et al. (1996) and earlier theoretical predictions based on charge-independent wave functions. The calculation demonstrates that T_z-dependent differences in s1/2 occupancy, driven by the 376 keV Coulomb energy difference between 0d5/2 and 1s1/2 orbits at A=17, can explain the observed asymmetry, and it enables future consistent shell-model calculations with charge-dependent interactions to treat both first-forbidden and allowed decays of 17N and 17Ne, as well as related systems such as 16C.
