# Landau theory of nuclear level density and its application in description of nuclear level density in the region of discrete and s-wave neutron resonance energies

> In this work, the reliability of the Landau expression for the nuclear level density calculations is tested, for the first-time, to describe nuclear level densities of some light, intermediate mass and heavy nuclei at excitations corresponding to discrete and s-wave neutron resonance energies. The chi-2 minimizing method is used in treatment of the experimental data for the two suggested energy range of discrete energies given by Nuclear Data Sheet [1] and by the systematic for nuclear level density parametrization in [2]. Our comparison with the related data in the discrete energy range has shown that the results obtained by the Landau expression are better than those of back-shifted Fermi-gas model and constant temperature approximation. This result is also valid for some nuclei of interest when the s-wave neutron resonance level density is included to check theoretical prescriptions in the energy range from initial bound states to unbound states near the neutron binding energy.

## Key claim

The Landau expression for nuclear level density is tested for the first time and shown to describe experimental level densities in the discrete energy range better than the back-shifted Fermi-gas model and constant temperature approximation, but this superiority is conditional on the specific data set used (RIPL-3 discrete levels) and does not hold uniformly when s-wave neutron resonance data are included in the fit.

## Method

The authors fit the Landau level density formula (ρ_L = exp(2√(a_L U))/T_L), the back-shifted Fermi-gas model, and the constant temperature approximation to experimental cumulative level numbers from RIPL-3 and from the Koning (2008) systematic, using χ² minimization with adjustable level density parameter a and energy shift Δ. For each nucleus, fits were performed separately for discrete levels alone and for discrete levels combined with s-wave neutron resonance spacings at the neutron binding energy, using both rigid-body and Gilbert-Cameron spin cutoff parameters.

## Context

This work extends the Landau theory of nuclear level density originally proposed by Landau (1937) to modern nuclear data, comparing it against the standard back-shifted Fermi-gas model (Dilg et al., 1973) and the composite Gilbert-Cameron model (Gilbert and Cameron, 1965) as implemented in the RIPL-3 database and the Koning (2008) systematic. The results show that the Landau expression can serve as an alternative phenomenological description of nuclear level densities, particularly for discrete levels, but its performance degrades relative to other models when fitting both discrete and resonance data simultaneously, especially for heavy deformed nuclei. This enables further testing of the Landau approach across a wider range of nuclei and excitation energies.
