# Progress in understanding short-range structure in nuclei: an experimental perspective

> High-energy electron scattering is a clean and precise probe for measurements of hadronic and nuclear structure, with a key role in understanding the role of high-momentum nucleons (and quarks) in nuclei. Jefferson Lab has dramatically expanded our understanding of the high-momentum nucleons generated by short-range correlations, providing sufficient insight to model much of their impact on nuclear structure in neutron stars, and in low- to medium-energy scattering observables including neutrino oscillation measurements. These short-range correlations also appear to be related to the modification of the quark distributions in nuclei, and efforts to improve our understanding of the internal structure of these short-distance and high-momentum configurations in nuclei will provide important input on a wide range of high-energy observables.

# Progress in understanding short-range structure in nuclei: an experimental perspective

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRCs) dominate the high-momentum tail of nuclear momentum distributions and can be isolated via inclusive electron scattering at x>1, but the extracted SRC contribution a₂ requires corrections for center-of-mass motion of the SRC pair (approximately 20% for A≥12) and isospin-dependent effects, meaning the naive plateau ratio does not directly equal the relative number of 2N-SRC pairs.

## Method

The authors review inclusive A(e,e') measurements at x>1 and Q²>1.4 GeV² that isolate scattering from high-momentum nucleons in SRCs, using the plateau in A/D cross-section ratios to extract the SRC strength parameter a₂. Exclusive two-nucleon knockout measurements A(e,e'pp), A(e,e'pn), and A(p,ppn) are used to determine the isospin structure and center-of-mass momentum distributions of SRC pairs, with comparisons to calculations from the Generalized Contact Formalism and realistic NN potentials.

## Key numbers

- The a₂ plateau in A/D cross-section ratios was observed for x>1.4 and Q²>1.4 GeV² for ³He, ⁴He, ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁵⁶Fe, and ¹⁹⁷Au from SLAC data (Frankfurt 1993)
- The ³He/²H ratio used to convert A/³He to A/²H is 2.12±0.06, averaged from Frankfurt (1993) and Fomin (2011)
- Center-of-mass momentum width for pp-SRCs in ¹²C: 143±17 MeV/c from Brookhaven (p,ppn) measurements (Tang 2002)
- Center-of-mass momentum width for pp-SRCs in ¹²C: 136±20 MeV/c from JLab Hall A (e,e'pp) (Jefferson Lab Hall A 2007)
- Center-of-mass momentum width for np-SRCs in ⁴He: 100±20 MeV/c from JLab Hall A (e,e'pn) (Lab Hall A 2014)
- Center-of-mass momentum widths from CLAS: 143±7 MeV/c for ¹²C to 157±14 MeV/c for ²⁰⁸Pb (CLAS 2018)
- Smearing corrections to a₂: approximately 10% for ³He, 20% for A≥12 (Fomin 2011)
- Correlated protons accompanied by back-to-back neutron emission 92⁺⁸₋₁₈% of the time in ¹²C(p,ppn) (Piasetzky 2006)
- ³He(e,e'p)/³H(e,e'p) ratio falls from above 2 at low missing momentum to approximately 1.4 at p_miss=250 MeV/c (Jefferson Lab Hall A Tritium 2019)

## Context

This review extends the foundational SRC framework established by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981, 1988) and the SLAC inclusive measurements (Frankfurt 1993), building on earlier reviews by Arrington (2011), Hen (2016), and Fomin (2017). The work synthesizes recent Jefferson Lab results that map SRC contributions across nuclei, showing that a₂ does not simply scale with average nuclear density (A⁻¹/³) as ⁹Be is an outlier (Arrington 2012). The review enables improved modeling of nuclear structure for neutron star equations of state and neutrino oscillation measurements, while identifying open questions about 3N-SRC contributions at x near 2 and the evolution of isospin structure at momenta above 600 MeV/c.
