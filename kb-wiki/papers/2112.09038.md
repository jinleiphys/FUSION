# Hadron spin structure from lattice QCD

> We present results on the spin carried by quarks and gluons in the nucleon using lattice QCD simulations with physical values of the light, strange and charm quark masses. We also discuss selective results on the x-dependence of parton distribution functions computed in lattice QCD employing the quasi-parton distribution approach and the large momentum effective theory.

# Hadron spin structure from lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations with physical light, strange, and charm quark masses can reproduce the nucleon momentum sum rule (total momentum fraction summing to 100%) and the spin sum rule (total spin summing to approximately 100% of the proton spin), but the results carry statistical uncertainties of order 10-15% and rely on perturbative treatment of the off-diagonal mixing coefficients between quark and gluon operators, which have not yet been computed nonperturbatively.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor using twisted mass fermion ensembles with physical pion mass, extracting generalized form factors from ratios of three-point to two-point correlation functions. Renormalization is performed nonperturbatively for the diagonal elements of the quark-gluon mixing matrix, while off-diagonal mixing coefficients are taken from one-loop perturbation theory. For the x-dependence of parton distribution functions, the quasi-PDF approach within large momentum effective theory (LaMET) is employed, using boosted nucleon states with momenta up to 1.38 GeV and matching the quasi-distributions to light-cone PDFs via perturbative kernels.

## Key numbers

- Up quark average momentum fraction: approximately 35% of the proton momentum
- Down quark average momentum fraction: approximately half that of the up quark (about 17-18%)
- Strange quark average momentum fraction: approximately 5%
- Charm quark average momentum fraction: approximately 2%
- Gluon average momentum fraction: approximately 45%
- Total momentum sum: 104.5(11.8)% in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV, confirming the momentum sum rule within uncertainties
- Total proton spin sum: 94.6(14.2)(2.8)% of the proton spin, confirming the spin sum rule
- Sum of B20 form factors for quarks and gluons: -0.099(91)(28), compatible with zero as required by momentum and spin sum rules
- Up quark intrinsic spin contribution: approximately 85% of the proton intrinsic spin
- Down quark intrinsic spin: approximately half the up quark contribution with opposite sign
- Strange quark intrinsic spin: negative contribution, approximately five times larger in magnitude than the charm contribution
- Charm quark intrinsic spin: approximately 1% contribution
- Quasi-PDF calculations performed at nucleon momenta of 0.83 GeV, 1.11 GeV, and 1.38 GeV (corresponding to 6π/L, 8π/L, and 10π/L respectively)
- Flavor-decomposed helicity PDFs computed using an ensemble with pion mass mπ = 260 MeV

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD program of hadron structure calculations by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration (ETMC), building on decades of experimental measurements from the European Muon Collaboration (1987-1989), the Spin Muon Collaboration (1992-1996), and COMPASS (since 2002), which established that only about 25-30% of the nucleon spin comes from valence quarks. The results confirm the momentum and spin sum rules at the physical point, eliminating the need for chiral extrapolation that was problematic in earlier work by Colangelo (2010) and others. The direct computation of x-dependent PDFs via LaMET, pioneered by Ji (2013), enables comparison with phenomenological analyses such as JAM17 and NNPDFpol1.1, and opens the path toward computing generalized parton distributions, twist-3 PDFs, and transverse momentum distributions for the nucleon and other hadrons.
