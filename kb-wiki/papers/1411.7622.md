# Transport coefficients in superfluid neutron stars

> We study the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients as well as the thermal conductivity as arising from the collisions among phonons in superfluid neutron stars. We use effective field theory techniques to extract the allowed phonon collisional processes, written as a function of the equation of state and the gap of the system. The shear viscosity due to phonon scattering is compared to calculations of that coming from electron collisions. We also comment on the possible consequences for r-mode damping in superfluid neutron stars. Moreover, we find that phonon collisions give the leading contribution to the bulk viscosities in the core of the neutron stars. We finally obtain a temperature-independent thermal conductivity from phonon collisions and compare it with the electron-muon thermal conductivity in superfluid neutron stars.

## Key claim

Phonon collisions in superfluid neutron stars provide the leading contribution to bulk viscosities in the core and a temperature-independent thermal conductivity, but the shear viscosity from phonons only becomes comparable to electron-based damping for r-modes at temperatures above approximately 7 × 10⁸ K, and only when the phonon mean free path is smaller than the stellar radius (hydrodynamic regime).

## Method

The authors used effective field theory (EFT) to derive the phonon self-couplings from the equation of state (EoS) and superfluid gap, then solved the Boltzmann equation for phonon binary collisions using variational methods to compute shear viscosity, bulk viscosities, and thermal conductivity. For bulk viscosities, they employed Khalatnikov's method based on the dynamical evolution of phonon number density, incorporating the next-to-leading-order phonon dispersion law with a negative curvature parameter γ. The calculations used the APR EoS as parametrized by Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen, and two different gap models for neutron pairing in the ¹S₀ and ³P₂ channels.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity from phonon binary collisions scales as 1/T⁵, a universal feature also seen in ⁴He and cold Fermi gases at unitarity.
- The phonon mean free path exceeds the 10 km stellar radius for temperatures below approximately 10⁹ K, making a hydrodynamic description questionable below that temperature.
- For a 1.4 M☉ star, phonon shear viscosity contributes non-negligibly to r-mode damping for T ≳ 7 × 10⁸ K; for a 1.93 M☉ star, this requires T ≳ 10⁹ K.
- The frequency-dependent bulk viscosity ζ₂ at 4n₀ shows deviations >10% from its static value for ω = 10⁵ s⁻¹ at T ≳ 10⁹ K for the ¹S₀(A)+³P₂(i) gap model, and strong frequency dependence across the whole temperature range for the ¹S₀(a)+³P₂(h) model.
- Phonon collisions give the leading contribution to bulk viscosities in the core at T ∼ 10⁹ K for typical radial pulsations, compared to direct and modified Urca processes.
- Thermal conductivity from phonons is temperature-independent well below T_c and scales as κ ∝ 1/Δ⁶, with the proportionality constant depending on density.
- For the ¹S₀(A)+³P₂(i) gap model, the phonon thermal conductivity mean free path stays below 10 km at n = 0.5 n₀, at n = n₀ for T ≳ 6 × 10⁸ K, and at 2n₀ for T ≳ 3 × 10⁹ K.
- The critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal transition is T_c ∼ 0.57 Δ, with values around 3.4 × 10⁹ K at n₀ for the ¹S₀(A)+³P₂(i) model.

## Context

This work extends the EFT-based approach to phonon transport in superfluid neutron matter previously developed by Manuel et al. (2011, 2012, 2013, 2014) and applies it to all three major transport coefficients. It contrasts with earlier calculations of shear viscosity from electron collisions by Shternin and Yakovlev (2008) and of bulk viscosities from Urca processes by Haensel et al. (2000, 2001). The results enable more precise modeling of r-mode instability windows and neutron star cooling, and the authors note that electron-phonon collisions, not yet included, may become important when the phonon and electron-muon contributions to thermal conductivity are comparable.
