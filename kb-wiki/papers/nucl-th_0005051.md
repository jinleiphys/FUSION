# New Solutions to Covariant Non-equilibrium Dynamics

> New solutions of 3+1D covariant kinetic theory are presented for nuclear collisions in the energy domain Ecm ~ 200 AGeV. They are obtained using MPC, a new Monte-Carlo parton transport technique that employs very high parton subdivision that is necessary to preserve covariance. The transport results are compared with ideal hydrodynamics solutions. We show that the transport evolution differs significantly from hydrodynamics. In addition, we compare the transport freeze-out distributions to those obtained from ideal hydrodynamics with the Cooper-Frye isotherm freeze-out prescription. The transport freeze-out four-volume is shown to be sensitive to the reaction rates and deviates from both time-like and space-like freeze-out 3D hypersurfaces commonly assumed. In particular, we find that there does not exist a universal freeze-out temperature. Finally, the transverse momentum distributions are found to deviate by up to an order of magnitude from (Cooper-Frye frozen) hydrodynamics for a wide range of possible initial conditions and reaction rates at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC energies (Ecm ∼ 200 AGeV), the evolution of a massless gluon gas deviates strongly from ideal Euler hydrodynamics throughout the 3+1D evolution, and it is not possible to mimic the nonequilibrium observables by simply applying the Cooper-Frye isotherm freeze-out prescription to ideal hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors solve the 3+1D Lorentz-covariant Boltzmann transport equation with elastic 2→2 scattering for a massless gluon gas (g=16 internal degrees of freedom) using MPC, a new Monte-Carlo parton transport algorithm that employs parton subdivision factors up to l=800 to preserve Lorentz covariance. They compare the transport results to ideal Euler hydrodynamic solutions from the Rischke-Dumitru code using an ideal gas equation of state (e=3p), starting from the same HIJING-based initial conditions at proper time τ0=0.1 fm/c and temperature T(τ0)=500 MeV, with isotropic differential cross sections and various cross section values (σ0=3, 15, 30, 60, 121 mb) and cutoff parameters (μ=0, 0.1, 0.5 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T(τ0)=500 MeV at proper time τ0=0.1 fm/c, with equilibrium initial gluon density nη,0 ≈ 2.65 fm−2
- Transverse momentum distributions deviate by up to an order of magnitude between transport and Cooper-Frye frozen hydrodynamics for realistic gluonic cross sections (∼ few mb)
- Even for a physically extreme 15 mb cross section, the difference between transport and hydrodynamic solutions is still a factor of two to three
- Parton subdivision factors up to l=800 (for R0=2 fm), 200 (R0=4 fm), 150 (R0=6 fm), and 100 (R0=8 fm) were necessary to recover covariance
- Even for subdivision l=100, 13% of collisions correspond to signal propagation velocity larger than 1.5c
- Initial Bjorken cylinder radii R0=2, 4, 6, or 8 fm were tested; increasing radius from 2 to 6 fm does not reduce the discrepancy

## Context

This work extends earlier 1+1D studies by Gyulassy (1997) and Zhang (1999) to full 3+1D Bjorken expansion, focusing on the freeze-out problem. It uses the HIJING multiple mini-jet generator (Gyulassy, 1994) for initial conditions and compares to the OSCAR-compliant relativistic Euler hydrodynamic code by Rischke and Dumitru. The results contradict the common assumption that ideal hydrodynamics with Cooper-Frye freeze-out can describe RHIC collisions, and instead show that microscopic transport techniques such as MPC and ZPC (Zhang, 1998) are necessary to account for finite transition probabilities. The paper enables future work incorporating inelastic 2↔3 partonic processes and more realistic equations of state.
