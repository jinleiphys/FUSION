# Two-particle angular correlations in pp and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies from a multi-phase transport model

> We apply a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to study two-particle angular correlations in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}= 7$ TeV. Besides being able to describe the angular correlation functions of meson-meson pairs, a large improvement for the angular correlations of baryon-baryon and antibaryon-antibaryon is achieved. We further find that the AMPT model with new quark coalescence provides an even better description on the anti-correlation feature of baryon-baryon correlations observed in the experiments. We also extend the study to p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}= 5.02$ TeV and obtained similar results. These results help us better understand the particle production mechanism in $pp$ and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with string melting and a new quark coalescence scheme can qualitatively describe the near-side depression in baryon-baryon and antibaryon-antibaryon angular correlations observed in ALICE data, a feature that previous Monte Carlo models (e.g., PYTHIA) failed to reproduce even qualitatively.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (both default and string-melting versions) to simulate minimum-bias pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV and p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV. They computed two-particle correlation functions C(Delta-eta, Delta-phi) using the same event-mixing and kinematic selection criteria as the ALICE experiment (|eta| < 0.8; pT thresholds: 0.5 GeV/c for p/anti-p, 0.3 GeV/c for K, 0.2 GeV/c for pions, 0.6 GeV/c for Lambda/anti-Lambda). They compared results with hadronic rescattering turned on (t_H = 20 fm/c) and off (t_H = 0 fm/c), and tested different parton cross sections (0, 1.5, 3, 6 mb) and two quark coalescence schemes.

## Key numbers

* pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV: parton cross section set to 1.5 mb in the main AMPT calculations.
* p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV: similar baryon-baryon correlation features observed as in pp.
* Kinematic cuts: |eta| < 0.8; pT > 0.5 GeV/c for p/anti-p, pT > 0.3 GeV/c for K, pT > 0.2 GeV/c for pions, pT > 0.6 GeV/c for Lambda/anti-Lambda.
* Hadronic rescattering time compared: t_H = 0 fm/c (off) versus t_H = 20 fm/c (on).
* Parton cross sections tested: 0 mb, 1.5 mb, 3 mb, and 6 mb for studying parton scattering effects.

## Context

This work extends the ALICE Collaboration (2017) measurement of two-particle correlations in pp collisions, which showed that standard Monte Carlo models (e.g., PYTHIA) could describe meson-meson correlations but failed to reproduce the near-side depression in baryon-baryon correlations. The AMPT model, previously successful for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (Lin 2005, Ma 2016), is here applied to small systems. The new quark coalescence scheme (He 2017) improves the description of baryon production at LHC energies. The results enable further study of particle production mechanisms in small collision systems and suggest that parton-level dynamics and coalescence hadronization are essential for baryon correlations.
