# Structure of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei with chiral hyperon-nucleon potentials

> The structure of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei is studied using the chiral hyperon-nucleon potentials derived at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) by the Jülich--Bonn--Munich group. Results for the separation energies of $Λ$ single-particle states for various hypernuclei from $^5_Λ$He to $^{209}_{\,\,\,\,\,Λ}$Pb are presented for the LO interaction and the 2013 (NLO13) and 2019 (NLO19) versions of the NLO potentials. It is found that the results based on the LO potential show a clear tendency for overbinding while those for the NLO13 interaction underbind most of the considered hypernuclei. A qualitatively good agreement with the data is obtained for the NLO19 interaction over a fairly large range of mass number values when considering the uncertainty due to the regulator dependence. A small spin-orbit splitting of the $p$-, $d$-, $f$-, and $g$-wave states is predicted by all interactions, in line with the rather small values observed in pertinent experiments.

# Structure of single-Λ hypernuclei with chiral hyperon-nucleon potentials

## Key claim
The chiral next-to-leading order (NLO19) hyperon-nucleon potential yields qualitatively good agreement with experimental Λ separation energies over a large mass-number range only when considering the uncertainty due to regulator dependence; the leading-order (LO) potential systematically overbinds while the earlier NLO13 version underbinds most hypernuclei.

## Method
The authors compute Λ single-particle separation energies in finite nuclei using a perturbative many-body approach based on Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory. They first solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Goldstone equation in infinite nuclear matter to obtain YN G-matrices, then approximate the finite-nucleus G-matrices via an expansion to second order in the nuclear-matter G-matrices, and finally solve the Schrödinger equation with the real part of the Λ self-energy to determine bound states.

## Key numbers
- For ⁵_ΛHe, the LO potential (cutoff 550-700 MeV) gives s₁/₂ separation energies of 4.04-3.26 MeV; NLO13 gives 0.73-4.90 MeV; NLO19 gives 2.16-5.63 MeV; experimental value is 3.12(2) MeV.
- For ²⁰⁹_ΛPb, the LO potential gives s₁/₂ separation energies of 40.97-37.32 MeV; NLO13 gives 25.75-39.51 MeV; NLO19 gives 36.28-41.58 MeV; experimental value for ²⁰⁸_ΛPb is 26.9(8) MeV.
- The Λ single-particle potential at zero momentum in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density (ρ₀=0.17 fm⁻³) is -U_Λ(k=0)=38.75-35.62 MeV for LO, 31.47-28.50 MeV for NLO13, and 40.99-32.25 MeV for NLO19; experimental range is 27-30 MeV.
- The spin-orbit splitting of p-states in ¹³_ΛC predicted by NLO13 at cutoff 700 MeV is Δp=0.25 MeV, and by NLO19 at cutoff 700 MeV is Δp=0.36 MeV; experimental value is 0.152±0.054±0.036 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous microscopic hypernuclear structure calculations using meson-exchange YN interactions (Morten et al., 1996; Vidaña et al., 1998; Vidaña, 2017) to the chiral YN potentials developed by the Jülich-Bonn-Munich group at LO (2006) and NLO (2013, 2019). It complements ab initio no-core shell model calculations of light hypernuclei (Wirth and Roth, 2019) and BHF studies of hyperons in nuclear matter (Haidenbauer et al., 2017). The results enable systematic comparison of chiral EFT predictions with hypernuclear data across a wide mass range, and highlight the need for three-body forces (appearing at N²LO) to reduce regulator dependence and improve agreement for heavy hypernuclei.
