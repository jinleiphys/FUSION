# Further explorations of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass formulas. XI: Stabilizing neutron stars against a ferromagnetic collapse

> We construct a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mass model, labeled HFB-18, with a generalized Skyrme force. The additional terms that we have introduced into the force are density-dependent generalizations of the usual $t_1$ and $t_2$ terms, and are chosen in such a way as to avoid the high-density ferromagnetic instability of neutron stars that is a general feature of conventional Skyrme forces, and in particular of the Skyrme forces underlying all the HFB mass models that we have developed in the past. The remaining parameters of the model are then fitted to essentially all the available mass data, an rms deviation $σ$ of 0.585 MeV being obtained. The new model thus gives almost as good a mass fit as our best-fit model HFB-17 ($σ$ = 0.581 MeV), and has the advantage of avoiding the ferromagnetic collapse of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass model (HFB-18) that eliminates the unphysical ferromagnetic collapse of neutron stars predicted by all previous Skyrme forces used in their mass models, while maintaining a mass fit almost as good as their best previous model (HFB-17). The stabilization is achieved by adding density-dependent generalizations of the usual \(t_1\) and \(t_2\) terms to the Skyrme force, with the condition that these new terms cancel exactly in unpolarized homogeneous nuclear matter of any charge asymmetry.

## Method

The authors generalized the conventional Skyrme force by adding two new terms: a \(t_4\) term (density-dependent generalization of \(t_1\)) and a \(t_5\) term (density-dependent generalization of \(t_2\)), with parameters constrained so that they cancel in unpolarized homogeneous matter. They then performed a complete refit of all model parameters (Skyrme force, contact pairing force with density-dependent strength fitted to realistic \(^1S_0\) gaps, Wigner correction, and spurious collective energy correction) to the 2149 measured masses from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation, subject to constraints on both unpolarized and polarized neutron matter and requiring the isoscalar effective mass to be \(0.8M\) at equilibrium density.

## Key numbers

* The new model HFB-18 achieves an rms deviation \(\sigma = 0.585\) MeV from the 2149 measured masses, compared to \(\sigma = 0.581\) MeV for the best-fit model HFB-17.
* The previous force BSk17 (underlying HFB-17) predicted complete ferromagnetic polarization of neutron matter at a density \(\rho_{frmg} = 1.24\,\rho_0\), where \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\) nucleons.fm\(^{-3}\) is the equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter.
* The Skyrme force SLy4 postpones the onset of polarization in neutron matter to \(\rho_{frmg} = 4.4\rho_0\) but shows a transition in neutron-star matter at the much lower density \(\rho_{frmg} = 2.5\rho_0\); SLy4 gives an rms deviation for even-even nuclei of 5.1 MeV.
* The rms difference between the mass predictions of HFB-18 and HFB-17 for all 8389 nuclei is 0.433 MeV, with a mean difference (HFB-18 minus HFB-17) of 0.198 MeV.
* The new \(t_4\) and \(t_5\) terms contribute 38 MeV to the binding energy of \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' series of Skyrme-HFB mass models (HFB-9 onward, Goriely et al. 2005; HFB-17, Goriely et al. 2009) by solving a long-standing problem: all conventional Skyrme forces predict a ferromagnetic collapse of neutron stars at supernuclear densities, contradicting microscopic calculations using realistic forces (Fantoni et al. 2001; Bombaci et al. 2006; Samyn et al. 2007; Bordbar et al. 2008; Vidana et al. 2002) that find no such polarization up to about \(5\rho_0\). The new model enables reliable extrapolations to neutron-rich nuclei in neutron-star crusts and allows a unified treatment of the inner crust and homogeneous core of neutron stars, including the transition region. An alternative approach to this problem was followed by Margueron et al. (2009).
