# Merging ab initio theory and few-body approach for $(d,p)$ reactions

> A new framework for $A(d,p)B$ reactions is introduced by merging the microscopic approach to computing the properties of the nucleon-target systems and the three-body $n+p+A$ reaction formalism, thus providing a consistent link between the reaction cross sections and the underlying microscopic structure. In this first step toward a full microscopic description, we focus on the inclusion of the neutron-target microscopic properties. The properties of the neutron-target subsystem are encapsulated in the Green's function which is computed with the Coupled Cluster theory using a chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions. Subsequently, this many-body information is introduced in the few-body Green's Function Transfer approach to $(d,p)$ reactions. Our benchmarks on stable targets $^{40,48}$Ca show an excellent agreement with the data. We then proceed to make specific predictions for $(d,p)$ on neutron rich $^{52,54}$Ca isotopes. These predictions are directly relevant to testing the new magic numbers $N=32,34$ and are expected to be feasible in the first campaign of the projected FRIB facility.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a framework that merges coupled-cluster (CC) many-body theory with the few-body Green's Function Transfer (GFT) reaction formalism for $(d,p)$ reactions, but only the neutron-target microscopic properties are computed from first principles in this first step; the proton-target and deuteron-target optical potentials are still taken from phenomenological fits.

## Method

The authors compute the neutron-target Green's function and optical potential using coupled-cluster theory with singles and doubles (CCSD) in a Berggren basis, employing the chiral NNLOsat nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interaction. This many-body information is then inserted into the Green's Function Transfer (GFT) few-body reaction formalism, which expresses the $(d,p)$ cross section in terms of the neutron-target Green's function and a breakup density amplitude. The deuteron-target and proton-target optical potentials are taken from phenomenological fits to elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- For the largest model space (N2/N3=14/16), the PA-EOM ground state energies are underbound by approximately 500 keV for 41Ca (experimental value -8.36 MeV) and approximately 600 keV for 49Ca (experimental value -5.14 MeV)
- For 53Ca, the PA-EOM ground state energy is -3.16 MeV compared to the experimental value -3.46 MeV (difference ~300 keV); for 55Ca, the PA-EOM value is -1.76 MeV compared to -2.60 MeV (difference ~820 keV)
- The uncertainty from using different deuteron optical potentials (local fit versus global systematics) is less than 15% at the peak of the angular differential cross section
- The coordinate-frame correction (laboratory vs. center-of-mass) introduces a shift of 190 keV for 40Ca, resulting in a difference of less than 4% in the cross section at the peak

## Context

This work extends the Green's Function Transfer approach of Potel et al. (2015, 2017) by replacing phenomenological neutron-target inputs with microscopic coupled-cluster calculations, and extends prior coupled-cluster Green's function work (gfccpap, gfccpap2) into the reaction domain. Previous GFT calculations for 40Ca(d,p)41Ca used inputs from the Dispersive Optical Model (DOM), while post-form DWBA calculations with self-consistent Green's function overlaps were reported for oxygen isotopes by Flavigny et al. The approach enables predictions for (d,p) reactions on exotic calcium isotopes 52,54Ca, which are expected to be measurable in the first campaign of the FRIB facility and are relevant to testing the new magic numbers N=32,34.
