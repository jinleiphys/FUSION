# Close encounters with attractors of the third kind

> We report on the existence of a hydrodynamic attractor in the Mueller-Israel-Stewart framework of a fluid living in the novel geometry discovered recently by Grozdanov. This geometry, corresponding to a hyperbolic slicing of dS$_3\times\mathbb{R}$, complements previous analyses of attractors in Bjorken (flat slicing) and Gubser (spherical slicing) flows. The fluid behaves like a sharply localized droplet propagating rapidly along the lightcone. Typical solutions approach the hydrodynamic attractor rapidly at late times despite a Knudsen number exceeding unity, suggesting that the inverse Reynolds number captures hydrodynamization more faithfully since the shear stress vanishes at late times. This is in stark contrast to Gubser flow, which has both the Knudsen and inverse Reynolds number becoming small for intermediate times. We close with a comparison to Weyl-transformed Bjorken flow and discuss possible phenomenological applications.

# Close encounters with attractors of the third kind

## Key claim
A hydrodynamic attractor exists in the Mueller-Israel-Stewart framework for a conformal fluid living in the hyperbolic slicing of dS$_3\times\mathbb{R}$ (the "third kind" of flow, complementing Bjorken and Gubser flows). The attractor is approached rapidly at late de Sitter times even though the Knudsen number exceeds unity, because the inverse Reynolds number vanishes, in stark contrast to Gubser flow where both Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers become small at intermediate times.

## Method
The authors solve the conformal Mueller-Israel-Stewart equations for energy-momentum conservation and shear-stress relaxation in the background metric of the hyperbolic slicing of dS$_3\times\mathbb{R}$ discovered by Grozdanov (2025). The fluid four-velocity is taken at rest in this metric, and the dissipative tensor is parameterized by a single dimensionless quantity $\pi\equiv-\Pi/(\varepsilon+p)$. Transport coefficients are taken from holography: $C_\eta = 1/(4\pi)$ and $C_\tau = (2-\log 2)/(2\pi)$. The equations are solved numerically for various initial conditions at fixed initial energy density but varying its time derivative, and the hydrodynamic gradient expansion is computed order by order in the relaxation time.

## Key numbers
- The late-time attractor value for the logarithmic derivative of the energy density is $f_\infty = \frac{4 \left(\sqrt{C_\eta}-2 \sqrt{C_\tau}\right)}{3 \sqrt{C_\tau}}$, where $C_\eta$ and $C_\tau$ are the dimensionless shear viscosity and relaxation time coefficients.
- The repulsor (unstable) solution at late times is $\tilde f_\infty = -4\frac{ 2 \sqrt{C_\tau}+\sqrt{C_\eta}}{3 \sqrt{C_\tau}}$, which is proportional to the value the Bjorken attractor takes for small $w=\tau T$ in Heller (2015).
- The inviscid energy density solution is $\varepsilon(\rho)=\varepsilon_0 \sinh^{-8/3}\rho$, which after Weyl transformation to Bjorken coordinates gives $\varepsilon(\tau)\sim \tau^{-4/3}$ in the limit $q\rightarrow 0$.
- The initial proper time of the system is $\tau = 1/q$ at the radial center $r=0$, where $q$ is the associated energy scale.
- For the flat foliation (uplifted Bjorken flow), the late-time temperature scales as $T(\rho_B)\sim e^{2\rho_B/3}\sim \tau^{2/3}$, which after Weyl transform gives the usual Bjorken result $T(\tau)\sim \tau^{-1/3}$.

## Context
This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors beyond the two canonical flows: Bjorken flow (boost-invariant matter in flat slicing, studied extensively by Heller (2015), Romatschke (2017), and many others) and Gubser flow (spherical slicing of dS$_3$, studied by Denicol (2018) and Chattopadhyay (2019)). The new geometry is the hyperbolic slicing of dS$_3\times\mathbb{R}$ discovered by Grozdanov (2025), denoted $\kappa=-1$. The results show that the inverse Reynolds number is a more faithful measure of hydrodynamization than the Knudsen number in this geometry, unlike in Gubser flow where both become small. The work enables future studies of this geometry in microscopic theories such as kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation (as done for Bjorken by Florkowski (2014) and for Gubser by Denicol (2014)) and holographic duals (as constructed for Bjorken by Janik (2005, 2006) and recently for Gubser by Banerjee (2023) and Mitra (2024)).
