# Point-Form Hamiltonian Dynamics and Applications

> This short review summarizes recent developments and results in connection with point-form dynamics of relativistic quantum systems. We discuss a Poincare invariant multichannel formalism which describes particle production and annihilation via vertex interactions that are derived from field theoretical interaction densities. We sketch how this rather general formalism can be used to derive electromagnetic form factors of confined quark-antiquark systems. As a further application it is explained how the chiral constituent quark model leads to hadronic states that can be considered as bare hadrons dressed by meson loops. Within this approach hadron resonances acquire a finite (non-perturbative) decay width. We will also discuss the point-form dynamics of quantum fields. After recalling basic facts of the free-field case we will address some quantum field theoretical problems for which canonical quantization on a space-time hyperboloid could be advantageous.

## Key claim

Point-form dynamics offers a clean separation of interaction-dependent Poincaré generators (the four-momentum components) from kinematic ones (the Lorentz generators), enabling manifestly Lorentz-covariant equations; however, the Bakamjian-Thomas construction used to implement interactions for finite-degree-of-freedom systems violates cluster separability, which introduces spurious dependencies in derived observables such as form factors.

## Method

The authors review the construction of Poincaré-invariant quantum theories using point-form dynamics, where the four-momentum operator is built from an interacting mass operator and a free velocity operator. They develop a multichannel Bakamjian-Thomas framework that couples N-particle and (N+1)-particle channels via vertex interactions derived from field-theoretical interaction densities, using velocity-state basis states that factor out the overall system velocity. Electromagnetic form factors are extracted from the one-photon-exchange optical potential for electron-hadron scattering, while hadron resonances are treated by dressing bare confinement eigenstates through Goldstone-boson loops in a coupled-channel mass eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- The first excited state in a toy meson model (quark, antiquark, scalar meson) with harmonic-oscillator confinement and meson-quark coupling compatible with the Goldberger-Treiman relation has a mass of about 1.45 GeV and a width of 0.026 GeV, with the ground state at about 0.8 GeV.
- Convergence for the ground and first excited states in the toy model is achieved with 4 bare states after 5 iterations.
- The threshold for complex mass eigenvalues (unstable resonances) is given by \( m_{\mathrm{th}} = m_0 + m_{GB} \), where \( m_0 \) is the lightest bare baryon mass and \( m_{GB} \) the Goldstone-boson mass.

## Context

This review extends Dirac's (1949) classification of forms of relativistic dynamics and the Bakamjian-Thomas (1953) construction for interacting Poincaré generators, addressing the known cluster-separability problem identified by Keister and Polyzou (1991). The coupled-channel approach for hadron form factors builds on earlier point-form work by Klink (1998, 2000) and Biernat et al. (2009), and the dressing of constituent-quark models via meson loops parallels the Sato-Lee model (2009) used at EBAC/JLab, but aims to predict meson-baryon couplings from the quark level rather than extracting bare quantities from data. The formalism enables future calculations of electroweak form factors for spin-1 bound states and non-perturbative decay widths for baryon resonances within the chiral constituent-quark model.
