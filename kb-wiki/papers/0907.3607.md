# Rapidity distribution of photons from an anisotropic Quark-Gluon-Plasma

> We calculate rapidity distribution of photons due to Compton and annihilation processes from {\em Quark Gluon Plasma} (QGP) with pre-equilibrium momentum-space anisotropy. We also include contributions from hadronic matter with late stage transverse expansion. A phenomenological model has been used for the time evolution of hard momentum scale $p_{\rm hard}(τ)$ and anisotropy parameter $ξ(τ)$. As a result of pre-equilibrium momentum-space anisotropy, we find significant modification of photons rapidity distribution. For example, with {\em fixed initial condition} (FIC) {\em free-streaming} ($δ=2$) interpolating model we observe significant enhancement of photon rapidity distribution at fixed $p_T$, where as for FIC {\em collisionally-broadened} ($δ=2/3$) interpolating model the yield increases till $y \sim 1$. Beyond that suppression is observed. With {\em fixed final multiplicity} (FFM) {\em free-streaming} interpolating model we predict enhancement of photon yield which is less than the case of FIC. Suppression is always observed for FFM {\em collisionally-broadened} interpolating model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pre-equilibrium momentum-space anisotropy in the Quark-Gluon Plasma significantly modifies the rapidity distribution of thermal photons, with the direction and magnitude of modification depending on the specific interpolating model used (free-streaming vs. collisionally-broadened) and on whether fixed initial conditions or fixed final multiplicity are enforced.

## Method

The authors calculate the rapidity distribution of photons from Compton and annihilation processes in an anisotropic QGP using a phenomenological model for the time evolution of the hard momentum scale p_hard(τ) and anisotropy parameter ξ(τ), with a smooth transition to ideal (1+2)d hydrodynamics at τ_iso. They include contributions from hadronic matter with late-stage transverse expansion, using rapidity-dependent parton distribution functions and initial temperature T_i(η) derived from a Gaussian multiplicity distribution. The photon modification factor Φ(y,τ_iso) is defined as the ratio of yields with and without pre-equilibrium anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature at η=0 for RHIC: T_i = 446 MeV with formation time τ_i = 0.147 fm/c
- Initial temperature at η=0 for LHC: T_i = 897 MeV with formation time τ_i = 0.073 fm/c
- Critical temperature for phase transition: T_c ~ 170 MeV
- Isotropization time range needed to describe PHENIX data: 0.5 fm/c ≤ τ_iso ≤ 1.5 fm/c
- For FIC free-streaming (δ=2) model at RHIC: enhancement of photon yield observed up to y ~ 5, with marginal suppression at y ≥ 5
- For FIC collisionally-broadened (δ=2/3) model at RHIC: enhancement observed for 0 ≤ y ≤ 1.5, suppression for y ≥ 1.5
- Maximum τ_iso allowing ≤20% entropy generation at RHIC: 1.2 fm/c for collisionally-broadened, 18 fm/c for free-streaming
- Gaussian width σ for multiplicity distribution: σ = 3 for RHIC, σ = 5 for LHC

## Context

This work extends previous studies of photon production from anisotropic QGP by Bhattacharya and Roy (2008) and Mauricio et al. (2007) from transverse momentum distributions to rapidity distributions, incorporating rapidity-dependent initial conditions. The results enable using photon rapidity density as a probe to distinguish between different pre-equilibrium dynamics (free-streaming vs. collisionally-broadened) and to constrain the isotropization time, complementing earlier work by Dumitru et al. on rapidity dependence at SPS energies and by Mauricio et al. on dilepton rapidity distributions.
