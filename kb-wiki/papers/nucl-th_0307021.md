# How does breakup influence the total fusion of $^{6,7}$Li at the Coulomb barrier?

> Total (complete + incomplete) fusion excitation functions of $^{6,7}$Li on $^{59}$Co and $^{209}$Bi targets around the Coulomb barrier are obtained using a new continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method of calculating fusion. The relative importance of breakup and bound-state structure effects on total fusion is particularly investigated. The effect of breakup on fusion can be observed in the total fusion excitation function. The breakup enhances the total fusion at energies just around the barrier, whereas it hardly affects the total fusion at energies well above the barrier. The difference between the experimental total fusion cross sections for $^{6,7}$Li on $^{59}$Co is notably caused by breakup, but this is not the case for the $^{209}$Bi target.

## Key claim

Breakup enhances total fusion of $^{6,7}$Li at energies just around the Coulomb barrier, but has very little effect (enhancement by ~2-3.5%) at energies well above the barrier; the difference between experimental total fusion cross sections for $^{6,7}$Li on $^{59}$Co is notably caused by breakup, but this is not the case for the $^{209}$Bi target.

## Method

Total (complete + incomplete) fusion excitation functions were calculated using a three-body continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method with short-range imaginary fusion potentials for each projectile fragment separately (alpha+deuteron for $^{6}$Li, alpha+triton for $^{7}$Li). Full coupled channels calculations were performed with the code FRESCO, including breakup couplings, continuum-continuum couplings, reorientation couplings, and bound-state structure effects, while target excitations were estimated separately.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co: $V_B = 11.74$ MeV; for $^{7}$Li+$^{59}$Co: $V_B = 11.68$ MeV
- Coulomb barrier for $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi: $V_B = 29.71$ MeV; for $^{7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi: $V_B = 29.57$ MeV
- Breakup enhances total fusion by ~2% for $^{59}$Co target and ~3.5% for $^{209}$Bi target at energies well above the barrier
- Off-diagonal imaginary couplings reduce total fusion cross sections by ~13% around the barrier and ~6% well above the barrier for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co
- Barrier penetration model (BPM) underestimates full coupled channels cross sections by ~40% just around the barrier and ~8% well above the barrier for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co
- Target excitations increase total fusion by ~5% around the barrier for $^{59}$Co and ~3% for $^{209}$Bi

## Context

This work extends the CDCC fusion approach of Diaz-Torres et al. (2003) by using separate short-range fusion potentials for each fragment rather than a single center-of-mass absorption potential, which is necessary for two-cluster projectiles with charged fragments of similar mass. The results contradict the barrier penetration model approach used by Keeley et al. (2002) for predicting total fusion with weakly bound projectiles, showing that BPM underestimates fusion around the barrier. The calculations reproduce experimental data for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co from Beck et al. (2003) and for $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi from Dasgupta et al. (2002), but underestimate $^{7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi data, suggesting that transfer channels not included in the model may be important for that system.
