# Probing the density dependence of the symmetry potential with peripheral heavy-ion collisions

> The peripheral heavy-ion collisions of $^{112, 124}Sn+ ^{86}Kr$ at $E_{b}= 25AMeV$ are studied by means of the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model(ImQMD). It is shown that the slope of the average N/Z ratio of emitted nucleons vs impact parameters for these reactions is very sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Our study also shows that the yields of $^{3}H$ and $^{3}He$ decrease with impact parameters and slope of the yield of $^{3}H$ vs impact parameters as well as the ratio of Y($^{3}H$)/Y($^{3}He$) depend on the symmetry potential strongly for peripheral heavy-ion collisions.

# Probing the density dependence of the symmetry potential with peripheral heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The slope of the average N/Z ratio of emitted nucleons versus impact parameter, as well as the yield of 3H and the ratio Y(3H)/Y(3He) as functions of impact parameter, are sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry potential in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, but only for impact parameters in the range b = 7-10 fm where neck emission dominates.

## Method
The authors performed Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model calculations for peripheral collisions of 112Sn+86Kr and 124Sn+86Kr at 25 AMeV incident energy. They compared two parameterizations of the bulk symmetry potential energy density: a soft case with gamma = 0.5 and a stiff case with gamma = 1.0. Clusters were constructed using a coalescence model with R0 = 3.0 fm and P0 = 250 MeV/c, and light charged particle production was treated via coalescence of free nucleons following the mechanism of Neubert and Botvina (2000).

## Key numbers
- Incident energy: 25 AMeV for both 112Sn+86Kr and 124Sn+86Kr reactions
- For central collisions (b = 2, 4 fm) of 124Sn+86Kr, the Y(3H)/Y(3He) ratio is about 2.5 with soft-sym and 1.9 with stiff-sym; for 112Sn+86Kr, about 1.98 with soft-sym and about 1.54 with stiff-sym
- With the coalescence mechanism included, the Y(3H)/Y(3He) ratio for 124Sn+86Kr central collisions is about 2.33 with soft-sym and 1.86 with stiff-sym; for 112Sn+86Kr about 1.85 with soft-sym and 1.44 with stiff-sym
- The increasing slope of the N/Z ratio of emitted free nucleons versus impact parameters (b = 7-10 fm) with stiff-sym is about 2-3 times larger than with soft-sym
- The average decreasing slope of the yield of 3H vs impact parameters for 112Sn+86Kr with soft-sym is about 1.47 times larger than with stiff-sym; for 124Sn+86Kr it is about 1.59 times larger
- Coalescence parameters: R0 = 3.0 fm, P0 = 250 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the study of isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions to peripheral reactions at Fermi energies, where neck emission dominates, complementing earlier studies of multifragmentation in central collisions by Bao-An Li (1997, 2002) and others. The authors use the ImQMD model previously developed by Wang and collaborators (2002, 2004). The results suggest that the slope of N/Z ratio of emitted nucleons versus impact parameter, along with 3H yields and the Y(3H)/Y(3He) ratio, can serve as probes of the density dependence of the symmetry potential, enabling future experimental tests with neutron-rich beams at facilities such as those producing rare isotope beams.
