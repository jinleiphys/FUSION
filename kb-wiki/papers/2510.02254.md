# Parton distributions in the shockwave formalism

> In this work, we calculate a broad class of parton distributions - including parton distribution functions (PDFs), transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs), generalized parton distributions (GPDs), generalized transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (GTMDs), and diffractive parton distributions - directly from their operator-level definition in the shockwave approximation for the target nucleon. This approximation is valid in the high-energy limit of scattering, corresponding to the small-$x$ regime. The shockwave framework allows us to employ the eikonal approximation and express the parton distributions in terms of Wilson-line correlators, naturally formulated within the color-glass condensate effective field theory. We present a comprehensive set of Feynman rules for evaluating parton distributions in this limit, and demonstrate how they can be systematically applied to calculate all phenomenologically relevant leading-twist parton distributions at leading order. This work establishes a unified starting point for future studies that aim to bridge the color-glass condensate approach with the partonic description of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors calculate a broad class of parton distributions (PDFs, TMDs, GPDs, GTMDs, and diffractive distributions) from operator-level definitions in the shockwave approximation, which is valid in the high-energy limit corresponding to the small-x regime; however, they emphasize that the shockwave approximation and the small-x approximation correspond to power counting in different parametric variables, and only the expansion around the parameter x p^+ L^- (the shockwave limit) is required for the calculations without further expansion around x.

## Method

The authors work in a frame where the target moves with large p^+ (along +z direction) and employ the eikonal approximation, which states that interaction with the target is instantaneous in light-cone time and transverse coordinates are conserved. They express parton distributions in terms of Wilson-line correlators within the color-glass condensate (CGC) effective field theory, deriving a comprehensive set of Feynman rules that combine rules for gauge links (eikonal interactions of gluons) and rules for scattering off the shockwave. These Feynman rules are then systematically applied to evaluate all phenomenologically relevant leading-twist parton distributions at leading order, working in the light-cone gauge A^- = 0 for gluons.

## Key numbers

- For protons, the shockwave approximation requires x << 1/(M_p R_p) approximately 0.2, based on the estimate M_p R_p approximately 5
- For heavy nuclei, the required x value is slightly lower, with (M_A/A) x R_A approximately A^{1/3} x 5, due to the increase in the width of the shockwave from multiple nucleons

## Context

This work extends previous case-by-case results for gluon PDF (Baier 1996, Mueller 1999, 2001), GPDs (Hatta 2016, 2017), TMDs (Dominguez 2011, Marquet 2009), GTMDs (Hatta 2016, Boussarie 2018), and diffractive distributions (Hatta 2022, Hauksson 2024) by providing a unified systematic framework with transparent Feynman rules. It presents for the first time complete leading-order results for the quark PDF and GPD including their finite parts, as well as TMDs and GTMDs with arbitrary gauge-link structures. This framework enables future studies bridging the CGC approach with the partonic description of the nucleon, with direct applicability to interpreting observables at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).
