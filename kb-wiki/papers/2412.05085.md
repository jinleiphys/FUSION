# Born-Oppenheimer Renormalization group for High Energy Scattering: the Setup and the Wave Function

> We develop an approach to QCD evolution based on the sequential Born-Oppenheimer approximations that include higher and higher frequency modes as the evolution parameter is increased. This Born-Oppenheimer renormalization group is a general approach which is valid for the high energy evolution as well as the evolution in transverse resolution scale $Q^2$. In the former case it yields the frequency ordered formulation of high energy evolution, which includes both the eikonal splittings which produce gluons with low longitudinal momentum, and the DGLAP-like splittings which produce partons with high transverse momentum. In this, first paper of the series we lay out the formulation of the approach, and derive the expression for the evolved wave function of a hadronic state. We also discuss the form of the $S$-matrix which is consistent with the frequency ordering.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Born-Oppenheimer renormalization group approach to QCD evolution that uses frequency (k^-) ordering rather than longitudinal momentum (k^+) ordering, claiming this naturally unifies BFKL-type and DGLAP-type splittings within a single framework, but only for dilute projectiles and without quarks in this first paper of the series.

## Method

The authors partition the projectile Hilbert space into slow modes (frequency below a cutoff E) and fast modes (frequency between E and Ee^Δ), then treat the slow modes as a fixed background while calculating the fast-mode wave function perturbatively using the interaction Hamiltonian H_SF derived from the light-front QCD Hamiltonian. The evolved wave function is obtained via the operator Ω = lim_{τ→∞} U(0,τ) acting on the product of the slow-mode wave function and the fast-mode Fock vacuum, where U is the interaction-picture evolution operator. The S-matrix operator is then computed as the time-ordered exponential of the projectile-target interaction Hamiltonian H_PT, with the target having a sharp frequency distribution around k^- ≈ E.

## Key numbers

- The frequency window for fast modes is defined as E < p^- ≡ p^2/(2p^+) < E e^Δ, where Δ is taken infinitesimally small to derive differential evolution equations.
- The total collision energy is s = 2P^+ E, where P^+ is the projectile longitudinal momentum and E is the frequency separation scale.
- The amplitude computed to order g^3 using the Born-Oppenheimer wave function reproduces the large transverse logarithms previously calculated in Lu and Mueller (no specific numerical value given for the logarithms).

## Context

This work extends the frequency-ordered evolution picture discussed by Armesto et al. (2008) and later by Ducloué et al. (2019), which argues that frequency (k^-) is the most appropriate evolution parameter for high-energy scattering. The approach addresses two known problems in NLO JIMWLK and BFKL evolution: the instability from large corrections due to unrestricted emission of high-transverse-momentum gluons (previously addressed via "kinematical constraints" by Kutak, Motyka, Beuf, Vera, Iancu, and Ducloué), and the large transverse logarithms related to DGLAP evolution within JIMWLK (formulated by the same authors in earlier work). The method enables a first-principles unification of low-x and intermediate-x physics, which has been pursued by Balitsky and Tarasov (2015-2016) and others, and is intended for future application to EIC phenomenology once quarks and dense projectile effects are included.
