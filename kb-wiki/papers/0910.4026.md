# Deformation effect on the center-of-mass correction energy in nuclei ranging from Oxygen to Calcium

> The microscopic center-of-mass (c.m.) correction energies for nuclei ranging from Oxygen to Calcium are systematically calculated by both spherical and axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with the effective interaction PK1. The microscopic c.m. correction energies strongly depend on the isospin as well as deformation and deviate from the phenomenological ones. The deformation effect is discussed in detail by comparing the deformed with the spherical RMF calculation. It is found that the direct and exchange terms of the c.m. correction energies are strongly correlated with the density distribution of nuclei and are suppressed in the deformed case.

## Key claim

The microscopic center-of-mass correction energy depends not only on mass number but also on nuclear deformation and isospin, with the deformation effect arising from competition between the direct and exchange terms, which are both suppressed in deformed nuclei compared to spherical ones.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of microscopic center-of-mass correction energies using both spherical and axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with the PK1 effective interaction for nuclei with atomic numbers 8 to 20. The Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations were solved via harmonic-oscillator basis expansion with 14 shells, and pairing correlations were omitted. The microscopic correction was decomposed into direct and exchange terms, and the results were compared with two phenomenological formulas: one from harmonic oscillator states and one fitted to Skyrme interaction calculations.

## Key numbers

- The difference in microscopic c.m. correction energy between deformed and spherical calculations, ΔE_c.m., reaches up to about 0.5 MeV for nuclei with quadrupole deformation |β| > 0.1.
- The direct term difference ΔE_c.m.^dir increases with deformation up to 1 MeV, while the exchange term difference ΔE_c.m.^exc decreases with deformation down to -0.6 MeV.
- The difference in matter rms-radii between deformed and spherical calculations, ΔR, increases monotonically up to approximately 0.1 fm with increasing |β|.
- The phenomenological formula E_c.m.^osc = -(3/4) × 41 × A^(-1/3) MeV and E_c.m.^fit = -17.2 × A^(-0.2) MeV are used as comparisons.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of center-of-mass corrections in relativistic mean-field theory, particularly following the work of Bender (2000) on microscopic correction methods and Long (2004) on PK1 interactions. The systematic comparison of spherical versus deformed calculations for nuclei from Oxygen to Calcium fills a gap in understanding how deformation affects the c.m. correction, which had previously been studied only phenomenologically without accounting for deformation. The results enable improved treatment of translational symmetry restoration in deformed nuclei and suggest that phenomenological formulas should be applied with caution depending on nuclear deformation and isospin.
