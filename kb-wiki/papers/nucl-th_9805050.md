# Collisional Damping of Nuclear Collective Vibrations in a Non-Markovian Transport Approach

> A detailed derivation of the collisional widths of collective vibrations is presented in both quantal and semi-classical frameworks by considering the linearized limits of the extended TDHF and the BUU model with a non-Markovian binary collision term. Damping widths of giant dipole and giant quadrupole excitations are calculated by employing an effective Skyrme force, and the results are compared with GDR measurements in Lead and Tin nuclei at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper derives collisional damping widths for nuclear collective vibrations using a non-Markovian transport approach, showing that the correct energy-conserving factor in the collision term yields a quadratic temperature dependence of the form \(\Gamma = \Gamma_{0}[(\hbar\omega)^{2}+(2\pi T)^{2}]\), but the calculated \(\Gamma_{0}\) using Skyrme forces underestimates experimental GDR data by a factor of 2-3, indicating that the major uncertainty arises from the lack of accurate knowledge of in-medium cross-sections near the Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors linearize the extended time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) and Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equations with a non-Markovian binary collision term, deriving expressions for the collisional damping width of collective modes in both quantal and semi-classical frameworks. They apply these formulas to giant dipole and giant quadrupole resonances by parametrizing the distortion factors of the momentum distribution in terms of Legendre functions (\(\chi_{Q}=p^{2}P_{2}(\cos\theta)\) and \(\chi_{D}=pP_{1}(\cos\theta)\)) and using an effective Skyrme force to compute the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections. The bulk contribution to the damping width is evaluated using standard Fermi liquid theory approximations with the energy integral formula \(\int d\epsilon_{1}d\epsilon_{2}d\epsilon_{3}d\epsilon_{4} \delta(\hbar\omega\pm\Delta\epsilon) \bar{f_{1}}\bar{f_{2}}f_{3}f_{4}\approx \mp \frac{\hbar\omega}{6} \frac{(\hbar\omega)^{2}+(2\pi T)^{2}}{1-\exp(-\hbar\omega/T)}\), while surface contributions are estimated by omitting Pauli blocking factors in the tail region.

## Key numbers

- The collisional width in nuclear matter limit takes the form \(\Gamma = \Gamma_{0}[(\hbar\omega)^{2}+(2\pi T)^{2}]\), where \(\Gamma_{0}\) differs for different resonances and is determined by nuclear matter properties and in-medium cross-sections.
- The bulk contribution to the quadrupole mode collisional width is \(\Gamma_{Q}^{bulk}(r)=\frac {\hbar}{N_{Q}}\frac {4 \pi}{5} {m^{\ast}} ^{2} \rho p_{F}^{2} I_{Q}(r) [(\hbar\omega)^{2}+(2\pi T)^{2}]\).
- The bulk contribution to the dipole mode collisional width is \(\Gamma_{D}^{bulk}(r)=\frac {\hbar}{N_{D}}\frac {2\pi}{3} {m^{\ast}}^{2} \rho I_{D}(r)[(\hbar\omega)^{2}+(2\pi T)^{2}]\).
- The surface contribution for the quadrupole mode is \(\Gamma_{Q}^{surf}(r) \approx \frac {\hbar}{N_{Q}} \frac{2\pi}{3}\rho p_{F}^{3} (m^{\ast} \hbar \omega)^{3/2}I_{Q}(r)\).
- The surface contribution for the dipole mode is \(\Gamma_{D}^{surf}(r) \approx \frac {\hbar}{N_{D}} \frac{\pi}{3}\rho p_{F}^{3} (m^{\ast} \hbar \omega)^{1/2}I_{D}(r)\).
- The normalization factors are \(N_{Q}=(4\pi/5) \int d {\bf r}m^{\ast} p_{F}^{5}\) and \(N_{D}=(4\pi/3) \int d {\bf r}m^{\ast} p_{F}^{3}\).
- The effective Pauli blocking factor is parameterized as \(F_{\lambda}(r)=1+\left (\frac{\epsilon_{F}(r)}{\epsilon_{F}(0)}\right )^{\beta} \left ( F_{\lambda}(0)-1 \right )\).

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by Ayik and collaborators (AyiBoi, AyiBel) on collisional damping of giant resonances within the linearized BUU model with non-Markovian collision terms, and provides a more detailed derivation in both quantal and semi-classical frameworks. The approach addresses the inadequacy of standard Markovian treatments (Bonasera) which lead to incorrect energy conservation factors that severely restrict the available 2p-2h phase space for damping. The results are compared with experimental GDR measurements in Lead and Tin nuclei (Hofmann, Tin, Lead), and the paper notes that the quadratic temperature dependence fits well with measured GDR widths in \(^{120}Sn\) and \(^{208}Pb\) nuclei, though the calculated \(\Gamma_{0}\) using a cross-section of 40 mb underestimates data by a factor of 2-3. The work enables future calculations using improved effective forces that have the correct free cross-section limit, as the Skyrme forces used here have a strongly erroneous behavior at very low densities in the nuclear surface region.
