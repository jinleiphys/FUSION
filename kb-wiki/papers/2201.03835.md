# Bayesian inference of finite-nuclei observables based on the KIDS model

> Bayesian analyses on both isoscalar and isovector nuclear interaction parameters are carried out based on the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) model under the constraints of nuclear structure data of $^{208}$Pb and $^{120}$Sn. Under the constraint of the neutron-skin thickness, it is found that incorporating the curvature parameter $K_{sym}$ of nuclear symmetry energy as an independent variable significantly broadens the posterior probability distribution function (PDF) of the slope parameter $L$, and affects the related correlations. Typically, the anticorrelation between $L$ and the symmetry energy at saturation density disappears, while a positive correlation between $L$ and $K_{sym}$ is observed. Under the constraint of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR), incorporating the skewness parameter as an independent variable also significantly broadens the posterior PDF of the nuclear matter incompressibility $K_0$. Even with the broad uncertainties of higher-order parameters of the equation of state (EOS), robust constraints of $L<90$ MeV and $K_0<270$ MeV are obtained. Our study quantifies the consistency between the constraints on $L$ from the neutron-skin data of PREXII and isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) data, and the constraints on $K_0$ from the data of ISGMR in $^{208}$Pb and $^{120}$Sn.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when the curvature parameter \(K_{sym}\) of the nuclear symmetry energy is treated as an independent variable (rather than being coupled to lower-order parameters as in the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model), the anticorrelation between the slope parameter \(L\) and the symmetry energy at saturation density disappears, and a positive correlation between \(L\) and \(K_{sym}\) emerges. Even with broad uncertainties in higher-order equation-of-state parameters, robust upper limits of \(L < 90\) MeV and \(K_0 < 270\) MeV are obtained.

## Method

Bayesian analyses were performed using the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) energy-density functional, which allows higher-order equation-of-state parameters (e.g., \(K_{sym}\) and the skewness parameter \(Q_0\)) to be varied independently. The posterior probability distribution functions of nuclear interaction parameters were constrained by experimental data on the neutron-skin thickness, isovector giant dipole resonance centroid energy and electric polarizability, and isoscalar giant monopole resonance excitation energy for \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{120}\)Sn. Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm was used to compute the posterior distributions.

## Key numbers

*   Neutron-skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb: \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.283 \pm 0.071\) fm (PREXII data).
*   Neutron-skin thickness of \(^{120}\)Sn: \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.150 \pm 0.017\) fm (from proton elastic scattering experiments).
*   Robust upper limit on the slope parameter of the symmetry energy: \(L < 90\) MeV.
*   Robust upper limit on the nuclear matter incompressibility: \(K_0 < 270\) MeV.
*   Isoscalar nucleon effective mass fixed at \(m_s^\star/m = 0.82\) to reproduce the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance excitation energies in \(^{208}\)Pb (\(10.9 \pm 0.1\) MeV) and \(^{120}\)Sn (\(12.7 \pm 0.4\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses based on the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model (Xu 2020, Xu 2021, Xu 2021b) by employing the more flexible KIDS model, which decouples higher-order equation-of-state parameters. The study addresses the "soft Tin puzzle" (discrepancy between ISGMR data for Sn and Pb) and the "PREXII puzzle" (inconsistency between the stiff symmetry energy favored by PREXII data and constraints from IVGDR data). The results enable quantification of the compatibility between different experimental constraints and provide a framework for future studies incorporating additional observables or nuclear systems.
