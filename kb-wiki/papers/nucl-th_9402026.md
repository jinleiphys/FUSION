# P,T-Violating Nuclear Matrix Elements in the One-Meson Exchange Approximation

> Expressions for the P,T-violating NN potentials are derived for $π$, $ρ$ and $ω$ exchange. The nuclear matrix elements for $ρ$ and $ω$ exchange are shown to be greatly suppressed, so that, under the assumption of comparable coupling constants, $π$ exchange would dominate by two orders of magnitude. The ratio of P,T-violating to P-violating matrix elements is found to remain approximately constant across the nuclear mass table, thus establishing the proportionality between time-reversal-violation and parity-violation matrix elements. The calculated values of this ratio suggest a need to obtain an accuracy of order $ 5 \times 10^{-4}$ for the ratio of the PT-violating to P-violating asymmetries in neutron transmission experiments in order to improve on the present limits on the isovector pion coupling constant.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of comparable coupling constants, the P,T-violating nuclear matrix elements from rho and omega exchange are suppressed by about two orders of magnitude relative to pion exchange, so that the isovector pion-exchange component dominates P,T-violation in nuclei. The ratio of P,T-violating to P-violating matrix elements remains approximately constant across the nuclear mass table, establishing a proportionality between time-reversal-violation and parity-violation matrix elements.

## Method

The authors derived nonrelativistic P,T-violating nucleon-nucleon potentials for pi, rho, and omega exchange from the corresponding Lagrangians, then computed effective one-body matrix elements for closed-shell-plus-one configurations using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions. Short-range correlation functions were applied to the two-body operators to account for the nucleon-nucleon repulsion at short distances. The ratio kappa(1) of P,T-violating to P-violating matrix elements was evaluated exactly (without asymptotic approximations) for sample neutron states across a range of nuclear masses.

## Key numbers

- For the ratio kappa(1) (the proportionality constant linking P,T-violating to P-violating matrix elements), the calculated values range from 2 to 5 across the nuclear mass table.
- The upper limit on the isovector pion coupling constant from existing electric dipole moment experiments is |gbar(1)prime_piNN| less than about 10^{-10}.
- To improve on this limit, neutron transmission experiments would need to measure the ratio of P,T-violating to P-violating asymmetries to an accuracy of order 5 x 10^{-4} for the isovector channel.
- For A=138 (139La), the derived upper limits on the asymmetry ratio lambda are: |lambda(0)| less than about 1.6 x 10^{-5}, |lambda(1)| less than about 5 x 10^{-4}, and |lambda(2)| less than about 2.5 x 10^{-5}.
- The P-violating asymmetry in 139La is about 10^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the one-meson-exchange treatment of P,T-violation previously applied to pion exchange by Griffiths and Vogel (1991) to include rho and omega exchange, and provides exact numerical evaluations rather than asymptotic approximations. The authors find that the strong mass dependence reported by Griffiths and Vogel using asymptotic potentials does not apply to the true potentials. The results enable experimental searches for P,T-violation in neutron transmission and gamma-decay experiments to be interpreted in terms of limits on the isovector pion coupling constant, complementing constraints from neutron electric dipole moment experiments which are less sensitive to the isovector term.
