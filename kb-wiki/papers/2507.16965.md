# Impact of 3D macro physics and nuclear physics on the p nuclei in O-C shell mergers

> O-C shell mergers in massive stars are a site for producing the p nuclei by the $γ$ process, but 1D stellar models rely on mixing length theory, which does not match the radial velocity profiles of 3D hydrodynamic simulations. We investigate how 3D macro physics informed mixing impacts the nucleosynthesis of p nuclei. We post-process the O-shell of the $M_\mathrm{ZAMS} = 15~\mathrm{M}_\odot$, $Z = 0.02$ model from the NuGrid stellar data set. Applying a downturn to velocities at the boundary and increasing velocities across the shell as obtained in previous results, we find non-linear, non-monotonic increase in p-nuclei production with a spread of 0.96 dex, and find that isotopic ratios can change. Reducing C-shell ingestion rates as found in 3D simulations suppresses production, with spreads of 1.22-1.84 dex across MLT and downturn scenarios. Applying dips to the diffusion profile to mimic quenching events also suppresses production, with a 0.51 dex spread. We analyze the impact of varying all photo-disintegration rates of unstable n-deficient isotopes from Se-Po by a factor of 10 up and down. The nuclear physics variations for the MLT and downturn cases have a spread of 0.56-0.78 dex. We also provide which reaction rates are correlated with the p nuclei, and find few correlations shared between mixing scenarios. Our results demonstrate that uncertainties in mixing arising from uncertain 3D macro physics are as significant as nuclear physics and are crucial for understanding p-nuclei production during O-C shell mergers quantitatively.

## Key claim

The uncertainties in mixing arising from 3D macro physics are as significant as nuclear physics uncertainties for understanding p-nuclei production during O-C shell mergers, but the impact is non-linear and non-monotonic, with different mixing scenarios producing spreads of 0.51-1.84 dex in p-nuclei production and few correlations between reaction rates and p-nuclei shared across mixing scenarios.

## Method

The authors post-processed the O-shell of a M_ZAMS = 15 M_sun, Z = 0.02 model from the NuGrid stellar data set using the multi-zone post-processing code mppnp with a 1470-isotope network. They modified the 1D mixing profiles to mimic 3D hydrodynamic results by applying a downturn to velocities at the shell boundary, increasing velocities across the shell, reducing C-shell ingestion rates, and applying dips to the diffusion profile to mimic quenching events. They also varied all photo-disintegration rates of unstable neutron-deficient isotopes from Se to Po by a factor of 10 up and down to compare the impact of nuclear physics versus mixing uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Spread of 0.96 dex in p-nuclei production when applying a downturn to velocities at the boundary and increasing velocities across the shell
- Spreads of 1.22-1.84 dex across MLT and downturn scenarios when reducing C-shell ingestion rates as found in 3D simulations
- Spread of 0.51 dex when applying dips to the diffusion profile to mimic quenching events
- Spread of 0.56-0.78 dex for nuclear physics variations (varying all photo-disintegration rates of unstable neutron-deficient isotopes from Se to Po by a factor of 10 up and down) for the MLT and downturn cases
- The stellar model has M_ZAMS = 15 M_sun and Z = 0.02
- The O-burning shell extends from 1.55 M_sun to 1.95 M_sun, and the first ingested C-burning shell from 1.96 M_sun to 2.11 M_sun
- The merger onsets at log10(t - t_end)/yr approximately -3.85 and reaches full extent at approximately -4
- A timestep of Delta t = 0.01 second and 400 equidistant mass zones were used
- A single simulation costs approximately 8 hours on 40 cores, for a total of 274 core years

## Context

This work extends the study of p-nuclei production via the gamma-process in O-C shell mergers, which was previously investigated by Ritter (2018) and Roberti (2023) using 1D stellar models that rely on mixing length theory. The authors build on 3D hydrodynamic simulations by Andrassy (2020) and Yadav (2020a) that show higher convective velocities than MLT, a gradual downturn in mixing efficiency at shell boundaries, and potentially lower C-shell ingestion rates. The results demonstrate that 3D macro physics uncertainties must be incorporated into nucleosynthesis models to quantitatively understand p-nuclei production, and that future work should focus on identifying which reaction rate correlations are robust across different mixing scenarios.
