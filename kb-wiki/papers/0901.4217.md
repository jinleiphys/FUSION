# Equation of state for nuclear matter in relativistic mean-field theory and Maxwellian phase transition to strange quark matter

> Equation of state for superdense nuclear matter is considered in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory, when the scalar-isovector -meson effective field is taken into account, as well. Assuming that the transition to the strange quark matter is a usual first-order phase transition described by Maxwells construction, the changes of the parameters of phase transition caused by the presence of -meson field are investigated. To describe a quark phase the advanced version of the MIT bag model is used, in which the interactions between quarks are taken into account in the one-gluon exchange approximation. For different values of the bag constant B, some series of the equations of the state of matter with deconfinement phase transition are constructed. Also the upper bound, Bcr, corresponding to the unstable state of the infinitizimal quark core in a neutron star is found.

## Key claim
The inclusion of the scalar-isovector δ-meson field in relativistic mean-field theory modifies the parameters of the first-order deconfinement phase transition from nuclear matter to strange quark matter, specifically lowering the transition pressure and baryon number densities while increasing the density jump parameter; however, for bag constants below a critical value (Bcr = 69.3 MeV/fm³), the infinitesimal quark core in a neutron star becomes unstable.

## Method
The equation of state for superdense nuclear matter was computed using relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with σ, ω, ρ, and δ meson fields, with coupling constants fitted to reproduce symmetric nuclear matter properties at saturation density (n0 = 0.153 fm⁻³, binding energy −16.3 MeV, incompressibility 300 MeV, asymmetry energy 32.5 MeV). The quark phase was described using an improved MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange interactions for u, d, s quarks (masses 5, 7, 150 MeV respectively, αs = 0.5). The deconfinement phase transition was treated as a first-order Maxwell construction, and calculations were performed for bag constants B in the range 60–120 MeV/fm³.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: n0 = 0.153 fm⁻³, binding energy per baryon f0 = −16.3 MeV, incompressibility modulus K = 300 MeV, asymmetry energy E_sym^(0) = 32.5 MeV.
- For B = 60 MeV/fm³ with δ-meson field: transition pressure P0 = 2.11 MeV/fm³, nuclear phase density nN = 0.1207 fm⁻³, quark phase density nQ = 0.2831 fm⁻³, density jump parameter λ = 2.327.
- For B = 69.3 MeV/fm³ with δ-meson field: transition pressure P0 = 13.84 MeV/fm³, nN = 0.2241 fm⁻³, nQ = 0.3504 fm⁻³, λ = 1.504.
- For B = 100 MeV/fm³ with δ-meson field: transition pressure P0 = 93.30 MeV/fm³, nN = 0.4746 fm⁻³, nQ = 0.6497 fm⁻³, λ = 1.213.
- Critical bag constant for stability of infinitesimal quark core: Bcr = 69.3 MeV/fm³, corresponding to λcr = 3/2.

## Context
This work extends prior RMF studies (e.g., Refs. [2-3] which introduced the δ-meson for low-density asymmetric nuclear matter) by applying the same framework to superdense matter and the deconfinement phase transition. It uses the Maxwell construction for first-order phase transitions, contrasting with more complex treatments, and employs the MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange (Ref. [6]) for the quark phase. The results enable future calculations of neutron star structure and stability, particularly identifying the bag constant threshold (Bcr = 69.3 MeV/fm³) below which infinitesimal quark cores are unstable, following the stability criterion from Ref. [7].
