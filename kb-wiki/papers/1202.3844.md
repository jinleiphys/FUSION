# Energy dependence of inclusive photon elliptic flow in heavy-ion collision models

> We present a comparison of inclusive photon elliptic flow parameter (v_{2}) measured at RHIC and SPS high energy heavy-ion collision experiments to calculations done using the AMPT and UrQMD models. The new results discussed includes the comparison of the model calculations of photon v_{2} to corresponding measurements at the forward rapidities. We observe that the AMPT model which includes partonic interactions and quark coalescence as a mechanism of hadronization is in good agreement with the measurements even at forward rapidities (2.3 < η< 3.9) at RHIC as was previously observed for measurements at midrapidity. At the top SPS energy the contribution from partonic effects are smaller than that at RHIC energy, based on the comparison of the measured photon v_{2} with those from the AMPT default and UrQMD model calculations. We find that if the measurements in RHIC beam energy scan (BES) and LHC energies would require an energy dependent partonic cross section in the AMPT models, then the observed longitudinal scaling of v_{2} at top RHIC energies (19.6-200 GeV) will be violated. We also discuss the relation between the inclusive photon v_{2} and those of their parent π^{0}'s for the beam energies of 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV. The model results show that the transverse momentum (p_{\mathrm T}) integrated v_{2} of π^{0} is larger by about 44% relative to those of the inclusive photons. Finally we present the expectations of inclusive photon v_{2} for the RHIC beam energy scan (BES) program and LHC from the transport models, so that they can be compared to corresponding measurements using the data already collected at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with partonic interactions and quark coalescence (AMPT-SM) agrees with inclusive photon elliptic flow measurements at forward rapidities at RHIC, but if RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) and LHC measurements require an energy-dependent partonic cross section, then the observed longitudinal scaling of v₂ at top RHIC energies (19.6–200 GeV) will be violated.

## Method

The authors generated events using the UrQMD (version 2.3) and AMPT (version 1.25t3, with HIJING version 1.35) event generators at various beam energies. For RHIC energies the parton-parton cross section in AMPT-SM was taken as 10 mb, and for LHC energy it was taken as 1.5 mb. The inclusive photon elliptic flow was calculated from the second Fourier coefficient of the azimuthal angle distribution with respect to the reaction plane, and model results were compared to experimental data from WA98 at SPS and STAR and PHENIX at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum (pT) integrated v₂ of π⁰ is larger by about 44% relative to that of inclusive photons, for beam energies from 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV.
- At SPS top energy (Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV), the ordering of model v₂ values is: v₂(UrQMD) < v₂(AMPT-default) < v₂(AMPT-SM), with AMPT-SM closest to WA98 measurements.
- For Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV at forward rapidity (2.3 < η < 3.9), AMPT-SM results are much higher than AMPT default and UrQMD, and comparable to STAR preliminary measurements.
- The maximum pT integrated v₂ at midrapidity for charged hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV is 0.087 ± 0.002 (stat) ± 0.003 (syst).
- Typical photon counting efficiency for a realistic PMD is about 60%, and typical purity is around 60-70%, which could lead to a difference in actual and measured v₂ of the order of 15%.

## Context

This work extends comparisons of transport model calculations to inclusive photon elliptic flow measurements at forward rapidities, building on prior studies at midrapidity. The authors compare to measurements from the WA98 experiment at SPS (Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV) and from the STAR and PHENIX experiments at RHIC (Au+Au at 200 GeV). The results enable predictions for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (7.7–200 GeV) and LHC energies, and test the longitudinal scaling of v₂ previously reported by PHOBOS and BRAHMS for inclusive charged particles.
