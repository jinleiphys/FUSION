# Modeling the nonperturbative contributions to the complex heavy-quark potential

> In this paper, we construct a simple model for the complex heavy quark potential which is defined through the Fourier transform of the static gluon propagator. Besides the hard thermal loop resummed contribution, the gluon propagator also includes a non-perturbative term induced by the dimension two gluon condensate. Within the framework of thermal field theory, the real and imaginary parts of the heavy quark potential are determined in a consistent way without resorting to any extra assumption as long as the exact form of the retarded/advanced gluon propagator is specified. The resulting potential model has the desired asymptotic behaviors and reproduces the data from lattice simulation reasonably well. By presenting a direct comparison with other complex potential models on the market, we find the one proposed in this work shows a significant improvement on the description of the lattice results, especially for the imaginary part of the potential, in a temperature region relevant to quarkonium studies.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a complex heavy-quark potential model that includes nonperturbative contributions from the dimension-two gluon condensate, claiming significant improvement over existing models in describing lattice data for the imaginary part of the potential, but only in the temperature region relevant to quarkonium studies and with the caveat that the extended KMS model (V_I) fails for the imaginary part, requiring an additional string contribution to achieve agreement.

## Method

The authors define the complex heavy-quark potential via the Fourier transform of the static gluon propagator in the Keldysh representation, adding a nonperturbative term proportional to the dimension-two gluon condensate to the hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed propagator. They extract the Debye mass by fitting the real part of the potential to quenched QCD lattice data from Burnier et al. (2016) and then use those values to predict the imaginary part. After finding that the initial extended KMS model (V_I) overshoots the lattice imaginary part, they introduce an additional string contribution to the retarded/advanced propagator with a dimensionless constant a=4, chosen to keep the leading-order medium correction negative in the confined phase.

## Key numbers

- String tension: σ = 0.215 GeV², determined from lattice data at 113 MeV.
- Strong coupling constant: α_s = 0.272, determined from lattice data at 113 MeV.
- Debye mass at T = 406 MeV: m_D = 423 MeV, extracted from fit to Re V lattice data.
- Debye mass at T = 369 MeV: m_D = 258 MeV.
- Debye mass at T = 338 MeV: m_D = 231 MeV.
- Debye mass at T = 312 MeV: m_D = 134 MeV.
- Debye mass at T = 290 MeV: m_D = 87.8 MeV.
- Debye mass at T = 271, 254, 226, 113 MeV: m_D = 0 MeV (taken as zero in confined phase).
- Critical temperature: T_c ≈ 290 MeV, inferred from the T-dependence of m_D.
- Parametrization of Debye mass in deconfined phase: m_D(T) = a T + b/T, with a = 1.719 and b = -0.123 GeV².
- Distance scale where nonperturbative effects become important for Re V: r_s(T) = √(α_s/σ) ≈ 0.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the real-valued Karsch-Mehr-Satz (KMS) potential model (Karsch 1987) to a complex potential by including nonperturbative contributions from the dimension-two gluon condensate, building on the approach of Megias et al. (2005) for the retarded propagator. It directly compares with the complex potential models of Thakur et al. (2013) and Burnier et al. (2015), finding that the proposed model shows significant improvement for the imaginary part when compared to lattice data from Burnier et al. (2016). The resulting potential is intended for use in phenomenological studies of quarkonium binding energies and decay widths in the quark-gluon plasma.
