# Photoproduction of eta meson within a coupled-channels K-matrix approach

> We investigate photoproduction of eta mesons off protons and neutrons within a coupled-channels effective-Lagrangian method which is based on the K-matrix approach. The two-body final channels included are pi-N, eta-N, phi-N, rho-N, gamma-N, K-Lambda, and K-Sigma. Non-resonant meson-baryon interactions are included in the model via nucleon intermediate states in the s- and u-channels, meson exchanges in the t-channel amplitude and the u-channel resonances. The nucleon resonances S_{11}(1535), S_{11}(1650), S_{31}(1620), P_{11}(1440), P_{11}(1710), P_{13}(1720), P_{33}(1232), P_{33}(1600), D_{13}(1520), D_{13}(1700), and D_{33}(1700) are included explicitly in calculations. Our model describes simultaneously the available data as well on total and differential cross sections as on beam and target asymmetries. This holds for the p(gamma,eta)p reaction for photon energies ranging from very close to threshold to up to 3 GeV. The polarization observables show strong sensitivity to resonances that otherwise contribute only weakly to the total cross section. It is found that the pronounced bump-like structure seen in the excitation function of the n(gamma,eta)n cross section at gamma energies around 1 GeV, can be explained by the interference effects of S_{11}, P_{11} and P_{13} resonance contributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a coupled-channels K-matrix approach, using an effective-Lagrangian framework with explicit nucleon resonances up to spin-3/2, can simultaneously describe total and differential cross sections as well as beam and target asymmetries for eta photoproduction on protons from threshold to 3 GeV, and that the bump-like structure in the neutron excitation function near 1 GeV arises from interference among S11, P11, and P13 resonance contributions rather than from a D15(1675) resonance.

## Method

The authors construct a K-matrix model from tree-level diagrams using an effective Lagrangian, including s-channel baryon resonances, u-channel nucleon and resonance terms, t-channel meson exchanges, and contact terms to restore gauge invariance broken by phenomenological dipole form factors. The model space includes two-body final channels pi-N, eta-N, phi-N, rho-N, gamma-N, K-Lambda, and K-Sigma, with eleven nucleon resonances (S11, P11, P13, P33, D13, D33) up to spin-3/2 explicitly included; coupling to states outside the model space is treated via energy-dependent decay widths in resonance propagators. Parameters were adjusted to reproduce Virginia Tech partial wave amplitudes for pion-nucleon scattering and pion photoproduction, with only minor adjustments made for the eta-N channel.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range covered: from threshold to 3 GeV for the gamma p -> eta p reaction
- S11(1535) resonance mass: 1.525 GeV (bare mass); coupling to eta N channel: g = 2.2
- S11(1650) resonance mass: 1.690 GeV (bare mass); coupling to eta N channel: g = -0.8
- P13(1720) resonance mass: 1.750 GeV (bare mass); coupling to eta N channel: g = 0.12
- D13(1520) resonance mass: 1.515 GeV (bare mass); coupling to eta N channel: g = 1.2
- Cut-off parameter for form factors: lambda = 1.2 GeV^2 (except kaon Born channels: 1.0 GeV^2)
- S11(1535) dominates cross sections from near threshold to W = 1.7 GeV (E_gamma ~ 1.1 GeV)
- S11(1650) important for E_gamma between 0.950 and 1.28 GeV
- P13(1720) important for E_gamma between 1.1 and 2.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels effective-Lagrangian approach previously applied to pion and kaon photoproduction by Uso et al. (2005, 2006) to the eta meson channel, supplementing the Giessen model calculations of Shyam et al. (2007) with a different treatment of channels outside the model space (energy-dependent widths versus effective xi-N states) and excluding spin-5/2 resonances as their contributions are negligible. The model enables simultaneous description of the comprehensive CB-ELSA, Mainz-TAPS, CLAS, and GRAAL data sets on eta photoproduction, and provides an alternative explanation for the neutron bump structure near 1 GeV that does not require the D15(1675) resonance invoked by the eta-MAID model of Wen et al. (2002) or the narrow state proposed by Kuznetsov et al. (2007) and Fix et al. (2007).
