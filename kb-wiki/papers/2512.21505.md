# Model-Independent Bound on Neutrino Energy Reconstruction from Nuclear Targets

> Neutrino energy reconstruction on nuclear targets underlies oscillation measurements and precision tests of weak interactions. Inclusive charged--current data have long exhibited degeneracies commonly attributed to axial-mass tuning, multinucleon dynamics, and final-state interactions. This work shows that, even in the idealized limit of perfect detectors and exact nuclear dynamics, inclusive lepton-only reconstruction admits no unique inverse. A strictly positive lower bound on neutrino energy resolution follows from the finite energy-transfer support of the inclusive nuclear axial response. The result identifies an irreducible contribution to reconstruction uncertainty that is independent of modeling assumptions and experimental resolution.

# Model-Independent Bound on Neutrino Energy Reconstruction from Nuclear Targets

## Key claim

Even in the idealized limit of perfect detectors and exact nuclear dynamics, inclusive lepton-only neutrino energy reconstruction admits no unique inverse; a strictly positive lower bound on neutrino energy resolution follows from the finite energy-transfer support of the inclusive nuclear axial response, independent of modeling assumptions and experimental resolution.

## Method

The author derives a formal bound by expressing the inclusive charged-current cross section as a convolution of a leptonic kernel (determined by measured lepton kinematics) with an inclusive axial response functional R_A(Q²,ω) that encodes all hadronic dynamics. Because the energy transfer ω is unobserved in inclusive measurements, a fixed lepton configuration (E_ℓ, Ω_ℓ) is compatible with a set of neutrino energies E_ν = E_ℓ + ω for ω in the support [ω_min, ω_max] of R_A, making the inverse problem set-valued. The minimal achievable resolution is bounded from below by half the width of this support.

## Key numbers

- The support width of the inclusive nuclear response for light and medium nuclei such as C, O, and Ar is ω_max − ω_min ∼ O(100 MeV), as established in inclusive (e,e') measurements and response-function analyses.
- When inserted into the bound ΔE_ν ≥ (1/2)(ω_max − ω_min), this scale implies an irreducible neutrino-energy uncertainty at the level of several tens of MeV.

## Context

This work addresses a logically prior question to studies that quantify reconstruction biases within specific nuclear models, such as those by Ankowski, Martini, and Formaggio, which have documented that distinct nuclear descriptions (including variations of axial mass, multinucleon excitations, and final-state interactions) can reproduce the same inclusive lepton spectra while implying different neutrino energy reconstructions. The result identifies an irreducible contribution to reconstruction uncertainty that persists even in the idealized limit of perfect measurements, and it does not constrain measurements incorporating exclusive hadronic information or additional kinematic tagging.
