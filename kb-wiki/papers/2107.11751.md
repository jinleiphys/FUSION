# Jet shape and redistribution of the lost energy from jets in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC in a multiphase transport model

> Jet-medium interaction involves two important effects: jet energy loss and medium response. The search for jet-induced medium excitations is one of the hot topics in jet quenching study in relativistic nuclear collisions. In this work, we perform a systematic study on how the lost energy from hard jets evolves with the bulk medium and redistributes in the final state of heavy-ion collisions via a multi-phase transport model. In particular, the ($Δη, Δϕ$) distribution of charged particles with respect to the jet axis and jet shape function are studied for various Pb+Pb collision centralities and for different transverse momentum intervals of charged particles. Our numerical result shows a strong enhancement of soft particles at large angles for Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p collisions at the LHC, qualitatively consistent with recent CMS data. This indicates that a significant fraction of the lost energy from hard jets is carried by soft particles at large angles away from the jet axis.

## Key claim

The AMPT model, which includes only elastic parton collisions and no medium-induced radiative processes, produces a strong enhancement of soft particles at large angles relative to the jet axis in Pb+Pb collisions compared to p+p collisions, qualitatively consistent with CMS data; however, the model overestimates this enhancement, suggesting that the missing inelastic radiative processes would improve agreement with data.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting mechanism to simulate jet-medium interactions in Pb+Pb and p+p collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV. They applied the anti-k_T jet reconstruction algorithm with cone size R = 0.4 within the FASTJET framework, used a mixed-event method for acceptance correction, and subtracted background using the side-band method following the CMS Collaboration procedure. The partonic cross section was set to σ = 1.5 mb, the same value used in previous work on dijet asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Jet suppression: nuclear modification factor R_AA(p_T^jet) shown for 0-10% and 70-80% central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, with jet cone size R = 0.4 and |y| < 2.8
- Jet selection: jets with p_T^jet > 120 GeV, R = 0.4, and |η_jet| < 1.6
- Charged particle p_T bins: bounded by 0.7, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 20 and 300 GeV
- Centrality intervals studied: 0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, and 50-100% for Pb+Pb collisions
- Partonic cross section used: σ = 1.5 mb
- Δr bin width for radial distributions: δr = 0.05

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT studies of γ-jet imbalance (Ma, 2013), dijet asymmetry (Gao, 2016), and jet fragmentation functions (Ma, 2013) to the detailed (Δη, Δφ) distributions and jet shape functions measured by CMS (2018). The study directly compares with CMS data at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV and shows that elastic collisions alone transport energy to very large angles, while the inclusion of medium-induced radiative processes (missing in AMPT) would likely improve agreement. The work enables future studies that incorporate both elastic and inelastic jet-medium interactions to better describe the redistribution of lost jet energy.
