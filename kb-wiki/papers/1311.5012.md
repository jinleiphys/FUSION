# Derivation of capture and reaction cross sections from experimental quasi-elastic and elastic backscattering probabilities

> The direct measurement of the reaction or capture (fusion) cross section is a difficult task since it would require the measurement of individual cross sections of many reaction channels, and most of them could be reached only by specific experiments. This would require different experimental set-ups not always available at the same laboratory and, consequently, such direct measurements would demand a large amount of beam time and would take probably some years to be reached. Because of that, the measurements of elastic scattering angular distributions that cover full angular ranges and optical model analysis have been used for the determination of reaction cross sections. This traditional method consists in deriving the parameters of the complex optical potentials which fit the experimental elastic scattering angular distributions and then of deriving the reaction cross sections predicted by these potentials. Even so, both the experimental part and the analysis of this latter method are not so simple. In the present work we present a much simpler methods to determine reaction and capture (fusion) cross sections. They consist of measuring only elastic or quasi-elastic scattering at one backward angle, and from that, the extraction of the reaction or capture cross sections can easily be performed.

## Key claim

The authors propose that reaction and capture (fusion) cross sections can be extracted simply from experimental elastic or quasi-elastic scattering data measured at a single backward angle, rather than requiring full angular distributions or direct measurement of many reaction channels. The method relies on the conservation of total reaction flux and an energy-shifting approximation for the angular momentum dependence, and its accuracy is demonstrated for several systems but shows deviations at sub-barrier energies for some reactions.

## Method

The method uses the experimental elastic backscattering probability \(P_{el}(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}},J=0)\) to derive the reaction cross section \(\sigma_R\), and the quasi-elastic backscattering probability \(P_{qe}(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}},J=0)\) plus breakup probability \(P_{BU}(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}},J=0)\) to derive the capture cross section \(\sigma_{cap}\). The angular momentum dependence of the reaction and capture probabilities is approximated by shifting the center-of-mass energy using an expansion of the Coulomb barrier height up to second order in \(\Lambda = J(J+1)\), with parameters \(R_b\) (barrier position) and \(\omega_b\) (barrier curvature) obtained from a double-folding nucleus-nucleus potential with Skyrme-type density-dependent interaction. The cross sections are then computed by converting the sum over partial waves into an integral over energy.

## Key numbers

* For the reactions \(^{16}\mathrm{O} + ^{120}\mathrm{Sn}\), \(^{18}\mathrm{O} + ^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\), \(^{16}\mathrm{O} + ^{144}\mathrm{Sm}\), and \(^{16}\mathrm{O} + ^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\), the critical angular momenta used are \(J_{cr} = 54\), 56, 57, and 62, respectively.
* The difference between results from the exact and approximate capture cross section formulas is less than 5% at the highest energies for the systems studied.
* For the \(^{6}\mathrm{Li} + ^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) reaction, the extracted mean breakup probability \(<P_{BU}>\) has a maximum of 0.26 at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_b \approx 4\) MeV.
* For the \(^{16}\mathrm{O} + ^{154}\mathrm{Sm}\) and \(^{32}\mathrm{S} + ^{96}\mathrm{Zr}\) reactions, the extracted capture cross sections required energy shifts of 1.7 MeV and 1.9 MeV, respectively, to align with directly measured capture data.
* For the \(^{7}\mathrm{Li} + ^{64}\mathrm{Zn}\) reaction, the maximum absolute value of the transfer cross section \(\sigma_{tr}\) at energies near the Coulomb barrier is about 30 mb.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using elastic scattering angular distributions and optical model analysis for determining reaction cross sections, as traditionally done by fitting complex optical potentials to full angular distributions. The authors cite prior work by Canto (2006) on the conservation of reaction flux and by Timmers and collaborators on quasi-elastic backscattering measurements. The proposed method enables simpler experimental determination of reaction and capture cross sections, as well as extraction of angular momentum moments and transfer plus inelastic cross sections, requiring only a single backward-angle measurement rather than multiple experimental setups or extensive beam time.
