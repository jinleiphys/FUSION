# Nonlocal PNJL models and heavy hybrid stars

> Nonlocal PNJL models allow for a detailed description of chiral quark dynamics with running quark masses and wave function renormalization in accordance with lattice QCD (LQCD) in vacuum. Their generalization to finite temperature T and chemical potential μ allows to reproduce the μ-dependence of the pseudocritical temperature from LQCD when a nonvanishing vector meson coupling is adjusted. This restricts the region for the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram and stiffens the quark matter equation of state (EoS). It is demonstrated that the construction of a hybrid EoS for compact star applications within a two-phase approach employing the nonlocal PNJL EoS and an advanced hadronic EoS leads to the masquerade problem. A density dependence of the vector meson coupling is suggested as a possible solution which can be adjusted in a suitable way to describe hybrid stars with a maximum mass in excess of 2 M_sun with a possible early onset of quark deconfinement even in the cores of typical (M ~ 1.4 M_sun) neutron stars.

# Nonlocal PNJL models and heavy hybrid stars

## Key claim
Nonlocal PNJL models with a constant vector meson coupling cannot produce hybrid stars with quark matter cores that satisfy the 2 solar mass constraint; however, introducing a chemical-potential-dependent vector coupling allows hybrid star configurations with maximum masses above 2 solar masses and possible quark deconfinement even in 1.4 solar mass neutron stars, provided the hadronic equation of state is sufficiently stiff (e.g., DBHF rather than APR).

## Method
The authors construct a nonlocal Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (nl-PNJL) model with wave function renormalization, calibrated to lattice QCD data for the quark propagator in vacuum and the slope of the pseudocritical temperature at small chemical potentials. They compute the quark matter equation of state at zero temperature using the Matsubara formalism, then perform a Maxwell construction for the deconfinement phase transition between the nl-PNJL quark matter and two standard hadronic equations of state (APR and DBHF). To resolve the masquerade problem, they introduce an interpolation ansatz for the vector coupling strength as a function of chemical potential, with a low-density value fixed by lattice QCD and a high-density asymptotic value chosen to allow a phase transition. The resulting hybrid equations of state are used to solve the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations for nonrotating compact star sequences.

## Key numbers
- The slope of the pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD is κ = 0.059, reproduced by the nl-PNJL model with scaled vector coupling η = 0.555 (set C parametrization).
- The critical endpoint (CEP) position depends on η; for η = 0.555 the CEP shifts to lower temperature and higher chemical potential compared to lower vector couplings.
- For the DBHF hadronic EoS, the asymptotic high-density vector coupling must satisfy η_> ≤ 0.3 to allow a phase transition to quark matter.
- The interpolation parameters for the chemical-potential-dependent vector coupling are suggested in the ranges μ̄ ∼ 360–410 MeV and Γ ∼ 30 MeV.
- Hybrid stars based on the DBHF EoS can have quark matter onset at masses as low as 1.2 M⊙, and can reach maximum masses above 2 M⊙, satisfying the PSR J1614-2230 constraint of M = 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙.
- Hybrid stars based on the APR EoS do not fulfill the 2 M⊙ maximum mass constraint and also violate the radius constraint from RX J1856.5-3754.

## Context
This work extends nonlocal PNJL models (Blaschke 2007, Contrera 2007, Hell 2008) with wave function renormalization (Contrera 2010, Horvatic 2010, Hell 2011) to compact star applications, where previous studies had omitted wave function renormalization (Blaschke 2007, Orsaria 2012). The paper addresses the masquerade problem identified by Alford (2004), where quark and hadronic equations of state become nearly indistinguishable in the relevant density range. The proposed density-dependent vector coupling provides a mechanism to reconcile the lattice-QCD-calibrated quark matter model with the 2 solar mass neutron star constraint from Demorest (2010), while also enabling early quark deconfinement in typical neutron stars. The results highlight the tension between compact star constraints and heavy-ion collision flow data (Danielewicz 2002), suggesting that both types of observations together can guide the dense QCD matter equation of state.
