# Metric anisotropies and nonequilibrium attractor for expanding plasma

> We consider the evolution of a system of chargeless and massless particles in an anisotropic space-time given by the Bianchi type I metric. Specializing to the axis-symmetric case, we derive the framework of anisotropic hydrodynamics from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation. We consider the case of the axis-symmetric Kasner metric and study the approach to the emergent attractor in near and far-off-equilibrium regimes. Further, by relaxing the Kasner conditions on metric coefficients, we study the effect of expansion geometries on the far-off-equilibrium attractor and discuss its implications in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the far-off-equilibrium attractor in anisotropic hydrodynamics is governed entirely by the relative expansion rates along the longitudinal and transverse axes, and that this attractor emerges only when the expansion is sufficiently rapid, regardless of the specific metric parameters.

## Method

The authors derive anisotropic hydrodynamics equations from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation for a system of massless, chargeless particles in an axis-symmetric Bianchi type I metric. They specialize to the Kasner metric and its generalizations, obtaining evolution equations for the anisotropy parameter and scale parameter by taking first and second moments of the Boltzmann equation. The resulting ordinary differential equation for the anisotropy parameter is solved numerically for various initial conditions and expansion geometries.

## Key numbers

- For Kasner case I (a=0, c=1), the gradient expansion coefficients give c_0 = 0 and c_1 = -2, with chi = -1
- For Kasner case II (a=2/3, c=-1/3), the gradient expansion coefficients give c_0 = 0 and c_1 = -2, with chi = +1
- The Lyapunov exponent for perturbations around the Navier-Stokes solution is -3/2 for both Kasner cases
- The exponent of tau in the perturbation decay is (1+chi)/chi = 0 for Kasner I and 2 for Kasner II
- The free-streaming evolution follows xi_FS(t) = -1 + (t_in/t)^(2(a-c)) (1+xi_in), where the exponent 2(a-c) determines the stable fixed point

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation of anisotropic hydrodynamics from Bianchi type I metrics by Dash (2017) by including microscopic interactions via the relaxation-time approximation. It builds on the study of nonequilibrium attractors in Bjorken flow by Strickland (2017), Blaizot (2019), and Jaiswal (2022), and generalizes these results to arbitrary expansion geometries. The framework enables systematic study of how different expansion rates affect the approach to equilibrium, with implications for modeling the early-time dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions where large pressure anisotropies are present.
