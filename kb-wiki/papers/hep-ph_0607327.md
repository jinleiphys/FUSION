# Momentum Broadening in an Anisotropic Plasma

> The rates governing momentum broadening in a quark-gluon plasma with a momentum anisotropy are calculated to leading-log order for a heavy quark using kinetic theory. It is shown how the problematic singularity for these rates at leading-oder is lifted by next-to-leading order gluon self-energy corrections to give a finite contribution to the leading-log result. The resulting rates are shown to lead to larger momentum broadening along the beam axis than in the transverse plane, which is consistent with recent STAR results. This might indicate that the quark-gluon-plasma at RHIC is not in equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper calculates momentum-broadening rates for a heavy quark in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma to leading-logarithm accuracy, showing that the problematic singularity in these rates at leading order is lifted by next-to-leading order gluon self-energy corrections. The resulting rates predict larger momentum broadening along the beam axis than in the transverse plane, which the author suggests might indicate that the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC is not in equilibrium, though the calculation is limited to qualitative effects only.

## Method

The author uses kinetic theory to compute the fluctuation coefficients kappa_parallel, kappa_perp, and kappa_z that govern momentum broadening for a heavy quark, working within the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation. The calculation separates the contributions into a "regular" part and an "anomalous" part, where the anomalous contribution arises from resumming next-to-leading order gluon self-energy corrections that cure a non-integrable singularity in the static limit. The gluon propagator for anisotropic systems is taken from Romatschke (2003), and the calculation is performed analytically for small anisotropy parameter xi and numerically for larger values.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of momentum broadening by Moore and Teaney (2004) from isotropic to anisotropic plasmas, using the anisotropic gluon propagator formalism developed by Romatschke (2003). The author applies the results to interpret STAR dihadron correlation data from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, suggesting that the observed broader correlations in rapidity than in azimuth could be explained by a finite system anisotropy with xi approximately 10. The calculation enables future work on full leading-order corrections, light quark and gluon results, and inclusion of radiation effects in anisotropic plasmas, while calling into question the assumption of thermal equilibrium at RHIC.
