# Covariant form factors for spin-1 particles

> Spin-1 particles, is a fundamental bound state for the two quarks, and play a crucial role in elucidating the electromagnetic properties within the realm of hadronic physics. Their intrinsic relativistic nature mandates a quantum field theory (QFT) framework for a comprehensive analysis. In this investigation, we employ both the instant form of QFT and light-front quantum field theory (LFQFT) as our theoretical tools. While conventional LFQFT approaches predominantly focus on the plus component of the electromagnetic current to extract the properties of spin-1 vector particles, our study extends this analysis by systematically incorporating the minus component as well. Our findings demonstrate that achieving a rigorous equivalence between these distinct current components necessitates the inclusion of nonvalence terms within the electromagnetic current operator. This crucial inclusion serves to restore manifest covariance and ensures a robust consistency with the results independently derived from the more conventional instant form of quantum field theory.

# Covariant form factors for spin-1 particles

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that achieving rigorous equivalence between the plus and minus components of the electromagnetic current for spin-1 particles in light-front quantum field theory requires the systematic inclusion of nonvalence (zero-mode) terms; without these terms, the minus component exhibits a substantially larger violation of rotational symmetry than the plus component, but after their inclusion both components yield identical covariant form factors.

## Method
The author calculates the electromagnetic current matrix elements for a spin-1 bound state (rho meson scale) using both instant-form (equal-time) quantum field theory and light-front quantum field theory, employing the Mandelstam formula with a simplified vertex model (Gamma = gamma^mu) and a regularization function to make loop integrations finite. The covariant form factors F1, F2, and F3 are extracted separately from the plus (J^+) and minus (J^-) components of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame under the Drell-Yan condition (q^+ = 0), and nonvalence contributions are computed using the "dislocation pole method" to restore rotational symmetry.

## Key numbers
- The covariant form factors F1, F2, and F3 are related to charge G0, magnetic G1, and quadrupole G2 form factors via: G0 = F1 + (2/3)eta*G2, G1 = F3, G2 = F1 - F3 + (1+eta)F2, where eta = q^2/(4*m_v^2)
- At zero momentum transfer: G0(0) = 1 (units of e), G1(0) = mu_v (units of e/2*m_v), G2(0) = Q_v (units of e/m_v^2)
- The angular condition for both current components is Delta^pm(Q^2) = (1+eta)(J^pm_yy - J^pm_zz) = 0, which is satisfied automatically in equal-time calculations but requires nonvalence terms in light-front calculations

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nonvalence contributions in light-front quantum field theory for scalar particles (Naus 1998, Sales 1998) and pseudoscalar mesons (de Melo 1999, Bakker 2001) to the case of spin-1 vector particles. The author builds on the formalism developed by de Melo (1997) and Frankfurt (1981, 1993) for electromagnetic form factors of spin-1 particles, and applies the Grach (1984) prescription for eliminating the I^+_00 component that generates zero modes. The results enable consistent extraction of electromagnetic form factors for vector mesons (rho, kaon star) and the deuteron using either current component in light-front calculations, provided nonvalence terms are properly included.
