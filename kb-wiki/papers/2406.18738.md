# New Spin Structure Constraints on Hyperfine Splitting and Proton Size

> The 1S hyperfine splitting in hydrogen is measured to an impressive ppt precision and will soon be measured to ppm precision in muonic hydrogen. The latter measurement will rely on theoretical predictions, which are limited by knowledge of the proton polarizability effect $Δ_\text{pol}$. Data-driven evaluations of $Δ_\text{pol}$ have long been in significant tension with baryon chiral perturbation theory. Here we present improved results for $Δ_\text{pol}$ driven by new spin structure data, reducing the long-standing tension between theory and experiment and halving the dominating uncertainty in hyperfine splitting calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents improved data-driven results for the proton polarizability effect Δpol that reduce the long-standing tension between data-driven evaluations and baryon chiral perturbation theory predictions, halving the dominating uncertainty in hyperfine splitting calculations, though the results remain conditional on the specific kinematic coverage of the new spin structure data.

## Method

The authors performed a data-driven dispersive evaluation of the proton polarizability contribution Δpol to the 1S hyperfine splitting using new experimental proton spin structure data (g1 and g2 structure functions) from the g2p and EG4 experiments at Jefferson Lab, which extended the world data coverage particularly in the low-Q² region most relevant to atomic bound states. The polarizability contribution was computed via the two-photon exchange diagram formalism, integrating over the inelastic spin structure functions across the two-dimensional phase space of Bjorken x and momentum transfer Q².

## Key numbers

- The new data-driven evaluation of Δpol reduces the uncertainty of this quantity by a factor of two compared to previous determinations
- The extracted proton Zemach radius from the 1S hyperfine splitting in hydrogen is RZ = 1.036(8) fm
- The 1S hyperfine splitting in hydrogen is currently measured to 12 digits of precision (ppt level)
- The muon-to-electron mass ratio is approximately 200, giving the muon 200³ greater probability of probing the proton substructure due to the wavefunction squared at the origin scaling as 1/(πaB³n³)

## Context

This work extends previous data-driven evaluations of Δpol by Hyperfine2, Tomalak, and Faustov, which were consistent with each other but in significant tension with baryon chiral perturbation theory predictions by Hagelstein (2016, 2023). The improved evaluation provides crucial theoretical guidance for the upcoming first-ever measurements of the muonic hydrogen ground-state hyperfine splitting being pursued by the CREMA collaboration at PSI and the FAMU collaboration at RIKEN-RAL, and enables a more precise extraction of the proton Zemach radius from hydrogen spectroscopy.
