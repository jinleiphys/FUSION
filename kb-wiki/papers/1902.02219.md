# Comparison of AMD calculations with experimental data for peripheral collisions of 93Nb+93Nb,116Sn at 38 MeV/nucleon

> Experimental data concerning binary events in peripheral collisions for the systems 93Nb+93Nb and 93Nb+116Sn at 38 MeV/nucleon, collected with the Fiasco setup, are compared with calculations performed with the dynamic code AMD, coupled with the statistical code Gemini used as an afterburner. The comparison focuses on the properties of the quasiprojectile (QP) and on the total multiplicities of the emitted light charged particles. A good reproduction of the average mass ratio, charge Z and c.m. angle of the QP is obtained in the examined impact parameter range (b~7-12 fm). Concerning the light charged particles, a general agreement is found for the total emitted charge, while some discrepancy remains for the multiplicities of the various species, especially for the protons which are always overestimated by the calculations.

## Key claim
AMD calculations coupled with Gemini as an afterburner reproduce the average mass ratio, charge Z, and center-of-mass angle of the quasiprojectile for peripheral collisions of 93Nb+93Nb and 93Nb+116Sn at 38 MeV/nucleon in the impact parameter range b ≈ 7–12 fm, but systematically overestimate proton multiplicities while total emitted charge shows general agreement.

## Method
The authors compared experimental data from the Fiasco setup with AMD (Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics) transport model calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme-force parametrization with stiff symmetry energy (L = 108 MeV) and a new test-particle method for two-nucleon collisions. The AMD primary fragments were de-excited using the Gemini statistical afterburner (both Fortran90 and C++ versions), and the resulting secondary events were filtered with a software replica of the experimental setup to account for geometrical coverage and detection thresholds. The comparison focused on binary events selected by collinearity conditions, using the variable C_b (derived from secondary velocities of quasiprojectile and quasitarget) as a proxy for impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 38 MeV/nucleon for both systems 93Nb+93Nb and 93Nb+116Sn
- Impact parameter range examined: b ≈ 7–12 fm (from about 0.5 b_graz to b_graz)
- Grazing impact parameters: approximately 12.3 fm for 93Nb+93Nb and 12.6 fm for 93Nb+116Sn
- Switching time t_sw = 500 fm/c used as standard, with tests from 200 to 10000 fm/c
- Screening parameter y = 0.85 (standard) and y = 0.42 (reduced in-medium cross section) tested
- For the asymmetric system 93Nb+116Sn, the ratio ⟨R_A/R_v⟩ deviates from 1 by ≤ 2% across all C_b values
- Comparison restricted to C_b > 700 MeV due to abundance of 3-body events below this threshold
- Total kinetic energy deficit for t_sw = 200 (500) fm/c: about 45 (20) MeV, corresponding to 2.5% (1.1%) for quasi-elastic events

## Context
This work extends previous studies of peripheral heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies (20–50 MeV/nucleon) by Piantelli et al. (2006, 2007, 2008) that focused on light charged particles and intermediate mass fragments, now providing a detailed comparison with AMD model calculations. It complements earlier AMD comparisons that were mainly focused on central collisions (Ono, 2002; Ono, 2013) or inclusive data (Tian, 2018), and addresses the need for more stringent constraints on transport models highlighted by recent model comparisons (Zhang, 2018; Xu et al.). The results enable future refinement of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and cluster correlation treatments in AMD, particularly to resolve the proton overestimation discrepancy.
