# Role of the $N^*(2080)$ in $pp \to pK^+ Λ(1520)$ and $π^- p \to K^0 Λ(1520)$ reactions

> We investigate the $Λ(1520)$ hadronic production in the $p p \to p K^+ Λ(1520)$ and $π^- p \to K^0 Λ(1520)$ reactions within the effective Lagrangian method. For $π^- p \to K^0 Λ(1520)$ reaction, in addition to the "background" contributions from $t-$channel $K^*$ exchange, $u-$channel $Σ^+$ exchange, and $s-$channel nucleon pole terms, we also consider the contribution from the nucleon resonance $N^*(2080)$ (spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$), which has significant coupling to $KΛ(1520)$ channel. We show that the inclusion of the nucleon resonance $N^*(2080)$ leads to a fairly good description of the low energy experimental total cross section data of $π^- p \to K^0 Λ(1520)$ reaction. From fitting to the experimental data, we get the $N^*(2080)Nπ$ coupling constant $g_{N^*(2080)N π} = 0.14 \pm 0.04$. By using this value and with the assumption that the excitation of $N^*(2080)$ is due to the $π^0$-meson exchanges, we calculate the total and differential cross sections of $p p \to p K^+ Λ(1520)$ reaction. We also demonstrate that the invariant mass distribution and the Dalitz Plot provide direct information of the $Λ(1520)$ production, which can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The nucleon resonance \(N^*(2080)\) (spin-parity \(J^P = 3/2^-\)) with a significant coupling to the \(K\Lambda(1520)\) channel is necessary to describe the low-energy total cross section data of the \(\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda(1520)\) reaction, and its inclusion, together with the assumption that the excitation in \(pp\) collisions proceeds via \(\pi^0\)-meson exchange, yields predictions for the \(pp \to pK^+ \Lambda(1520)\) reaction that are consistent with the experimental upper limit at one energy and with one measured data point after adjusting cutoff parameters.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian method combined with an isobar model, computing tree-level Feynman diagrams for both reactions. For \(\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda(1520)\), they included \(s\)-channel nucleon pole and \(N^*(2080)\) resonance terms, \(t\)-channel \(K^*\) exchange, and \(u\)-channel \(\Sigma^+\) exchange; for \(pp \to pK^+ \Lambda(1520)\), they considered only \(\pi^0\)-meson exchange diagrams involving the nucleon pole, \(\Sigma^+\) pole, and \(N^*(2080)\) resonance, neglecting the \(K^*\) exchange. They performed a three-parameter \(\chi^2\)-fit to 12 experimental total cross section data points for the \(\pi^- p\) reaction to determine the \(N^*(2080)N\pi\) coupling constant and cutoff parameters, then used those values to predict cross sections and kinematic distributions for the \(pp\) reaction.

## Key numbers

*   \(N^*(2080)N\pi\) coupling constant: \(g_{N^*(2080)N \pi} = 0.14 \pm 0.04\), obtained from a \(\chi^2\)-fit to \(\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda(1520)\) total cross section data (12 data points below \(\sqrt{s}=3.1\) GeV, \(\chi^2/dof = 1.1\)).
*   Branching ratio of \(N^*(2080) \to N\pi\): \((2.9 \pm 1.6)\%\), derived from the fitted coupling constant using \(M_{N^*} = 2115\) MeV and \(\Gamma_{N^*} = 254\) MeV.
*   Total cross section prediction for \(pp \to pK^+ \Lambda(1520)\) at beam momentum \(p_{\text{lab}} = 3.65\) GeV: \(0.01\ \mu\)b, which is 20 times smaller than the experimental upper limit of \(0.2\ \mu\)b from the COSY-ANKE Collaboration.
*   Total cross section prediction for \(pp \to pK^+ \Lambda(1520)\) at beam momentum \(p_{\text{lab}} = 4.34\) GeV (kinetic beam energy \(T_p = 3.5\) GeV): \(11.5\ \mu\)b, compared to the HADES measurement of \(5.6 \pm 1.1 \pm 0.4^{+1.1}_{-1.6}\ \mu\)b.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the \(N^*(2080)\) resonance in photo- and electro-production of \(\Lambda(1520)\) (Xie et al., 2013; He et al., 2013; Nam et al., 2013) to hadronic production channels, specifically \(\pi^- p\) and \(pp\) collisions. It provides the first determination of the \(N^*(2080)N\pi\) coupling constant from \(\pi^- p\) data and makes predictions for the \(pp\) reaction that can be tested by experiments such as DISTO, COSY-ANKE, and HADES. The results also connect to studies of non-\(\phi\) kaon pair production in \(pp\) collisions, where \(\Lambda(1405)\) (Geng et al., 2007; Xie et al., 2011) and \(\Lambda(1520)\) (Roca et al., 2006) play important roles, and to the broader problem of "missing \(N^*\) states" around 2.0 GeV (Capstick et al., 2000).
