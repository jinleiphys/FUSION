# Violation and persistence of the K-quantum number in warm rotating nuclei

> The validity of the K-quantum number in rapidly rotating warm nuclei is investigated as a function of thermal excitation energy U and angular momentum I, for the rare-earth nucleus 163Er. The quantal eigenstates are described with a shell model which combines a cranked Nilsson mean-field and a residual two-body interaction, together with a term which takes into account the angular momentum carried by the K-quantum number in an approximate way. K-mixing is produced by the interplay of the Coriolis interaction and the residual interaction; it is weak in the region of the discrete rotational bands (U \lesim 1MeV), but it gradually increases until the limit of complete violation of the K-quantum number is approached around U \sim 2 - 2.5 MeV. The calculated matrix elements between bands having different K-quantum numbers decrease exponentially as a function of $ΔK$, in qualitative agreement with recent data.

## Key claim

The K-quantum number in warm rotating nuclei shows a gradual transition from persistence to violation as thermal excitation energy increases, with K-mixing remaining weak below approximately 1.5-1.8 MeV for angular momenta up to 35 hbar, contrary to the complete K-violation predicted by the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble model for neutron resonance states.

## Method

The authors constructed a cranked shell model for 163Er combining a cranked Nilsson mean-field with a surface-delta residual interaction and an additional J_z^2/2J term to account for the K-quantum number's effect on rotational energy. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian using 4000 np-nh basis states for each spin and parity, and quantified K-mixing through a ratio comparing the variance of J_z^2 within individual eigenstates to the total variance of K^2 across states.

## Key numbers

- K-mixing onset (r_Kmix approximately 0.5) occurs around U approximately 1.5 MeV for spins I = 59/2, 61/2, compared to rotational damping onset at U approximately 1.0 MeV
- Complete K-violation (r_Kmix approaching 1) is approached around U approximately 2-2.5 MeV
- Experimental interaction matrix elements at band crossings show exponential scaling with Delta K, with K-correlation interval delta K_0 approximately 1.6
- Calculated matrix elements for low-lying eigenstates give delta K_0 approximately 2.1, while K-basis matrix elements give delta K_0 approximately 1.5
- Total number of regular rotational bands N_band is approximately 30-40 for I greater than 30, with approximately 25 low-K bands (K less than 8) and approximately 15 high-K bands (K greater than 8)
- For very high spins I = 50-58, the K-correlation interval increases to delta K_0 approximately 3-4
- At low angular momenta near band heads, the K-forbiddeness factor translates to delta K_0 approximately 0.4

## Context

This work extends the cranked shell model approach previously developed by Matsuo (1997) for describing rotating compound states and rotational damping, now incorporating the K-quantum number through a J_z^2 term related to the tilted-axis cranking model. The results contradict the complete K-violation predicted by the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble model for neutron resonance states as discussed by Barrett and Mottelson, and provide a theoretical framework for understanding experimental observations by Bosetti (1996) of K-selection rule persistence in quasicontinuum gamma spectra following fusion reactions. The calculations enable future quantitative comparison with new quasicontinuum data and gamma-cascade analyses.
