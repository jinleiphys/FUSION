# Susceptibilities with multi-quark interactions in PNJL model

> We have investigated the fluctuations and the higher order susceptibilities of quark number, isospin number, electric charge and strangeness at vanishing chemical potential for 2+1 flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The calculations are performed for the bound effective potential in the quark sector requiring up to eight quark interaction terms. These have been contrasted to the lattice results which currently have somewhat heavier quarks in the light flavor sector. The results show sufficient qualitative agreement. For comparison we also present the results obtained with the conventional effective potential containing upto six quark interaction terms.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including eight-quark interaction terms in the 2+1 flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model improves the agreement with lattice QCD results for higher-order susceptibilities and thermodynamic quantities, particularly near the crossover transition temperature, though the agreement remains qualitative rather than quantitative due to the lattice data using heavier light quarks.

## Method

The authors computed Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure up to eighth order in quark number, isospin, electric charge, and strangeness chemical potentials at zero chemical potential using two versions of the 2+1 flavor PNJL model: model A with up to six-quark interactions and model B with additional eight-quark interactions. The thermodynamic potential was minimized numerically with respect to the chiral condensates (sigma_u, sigma_d, sigma_s) and the Polyakov loop fields (Phi, anti-Phi), then the pressure was fitted to a polynomial in each chemical potential to extract the susceptibility coefficients. Specific heat and speed of sound were also calculated from the temperature derivatives of the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature T_C is 181 MeV for model A and 169 MeV for model B
- At high temperature, c_2^q (quark number susceptibility) reaches approximately 98% of the Stefan-Boltzmann (SB) limit for model A and 99% for model B
- The softest point of the equation of state (p/epsilon)_min is approximately 0.07 for model A and 0.06 for model B, compared to lattice values of 0.05 (Cheng et al.) and 0.13 (Fodor et al.)
- The expansion range for quark chemical potential was restricted to mu_q approximately 300 MeV, for isospin chemical potential to mu_I less than 70 MeV, and for strangeness chemical potential to mu_S less than 200 MeV below T_C
- The least-squares of all polynomial fits were 10^(-10) or less
- For isospin susceptibility c_2^I at high temperature, model B reaches approximately 99.5% of the SB limit while model A reaches approximately 98%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the PNJL model with eight-quark interactions by Osipov et al. (2006-2008) and the authors' own prior work (Deb et al., 2009) by computing higher-order susceptibilities and comparing them to lattice QCD results from Cheng et al. (2008) and Fodor et al. (2009). The results are also compared with the Polyakov loop extended quark-meson (PQM) model calculations by Schaefer et al. (2007-2010). The inclusion of eight-quark interactions (model B) produces sharper peaks in higher-order susceptibilities near T_C and better agreement with lattice data for the softest point of the equation of state, while model A (with only up to six-quark interactions) shows closer agreement with lattice data at low temperatures for some observables. The work enables more precise modeling of fluctuations in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, LHC, and FAIR, though the authors note that pion, kaon, and diquark condensates are not included and may become important at higher chemical potentials.
