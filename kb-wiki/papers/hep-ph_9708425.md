# Effective Quark Interactions and QCD-Propagators

> We compute the momentum dependence of the effective four quark interaction in QCD after integrating out the gluons. Our method is based on a truncation of exact renormalization group equations which should give reasonable results for momenta above the confinement scale. The difference between the four quark interaction and the heavy quark potential can be minimized for an optimal renormalization scheme in Landau gauge. Within the momentum range relevant for quarkonia our results agree with phenomenological potentials. We also calculate the propagators for gluons and ghosts in Landau-gauge.

## Key claim

The authors compute the momentum-dependent effective four-quark interaction in QCD by integrating out gluons using a truncation of exact renormalization group equations, claiming that this method gives reasonable results for momenta above the confinement scale, but with the hedge that the detailed quantitative value of the string tension depends strongly on the truncation, indicating insufficiency of the present approximations for very low values of \(q^2\).

## Method

The authors solve non-perturbative flow equations for the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge, derived from the exact renormalization group equation for the effective average action, using a truncation that approximates the momentum-dependent three- and four-point vertices by classical ones with an effective running gauge coupling \(g_k\). Starting from perturbative initial conditions at a high scale \(k_0\), they numerically evolve the coupled system of partial differential equations for the gluon two-point function \(\overline{G}_A(q^2)\) and ghost two-point function \(G_{gh}(q^2)\) down to \(k \rightarrow 0\), with the fermionic cutoff \(k_F\) held fixed. The effective four-quark interaction is then extracted from the gauge-invariant kernel of the effective action by solving the gluon field equations as a functional of the quark fields.

## Key numbers

* The compositeness scale for mesonic bound states is found at \(k_\varphi \sim 600-700\) MeV, with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking occurring typically for \(k \sim 450\) MeV and quarks acquiring a constituent mass \(m_q \sim 300-350\) MeV.
* The two-loop confinement scale in the \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme is taken as \(\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}} = 315\) MeV, and the heavy quark potential \(V(q^2)\) almost agrees with the two-loop potential for \(3 \Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}} \leq \sqrt{q^2} \lesssim 10 \Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}\).
* For lower momenta \(\sqrt{q^2} < 2.5 \Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}\) the result for the potential strongly deviates from the two-loop potential and is close to phenomenological potentials in the range \(\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}} \lesssim \sqrt{q^2} \lesssim 2.5 \Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using exact renormalization group equations for QCD propagators and the heavy quark potential by Wetterich and collaborators (1990s), and is closely related to Schwinger-Dyson equation approaches reviewed in the literature. The computed effective four-quark interaction at the scale \(k_\psi\) serves as input for subsequent flow to lower scales where mesonic bound states and chiral symmetry breaking are treated, enabling a systematic connection between perturbative QCD and low-energy effective models such as Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models.
