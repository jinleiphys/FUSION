# Dynamical Study of Multifragmentation and Related Phenomena in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In the first part of thesis, we shall deal with fragment emission in central collisions studied as a function of beam energy and system mass. Central collisions are also important candidate in view of exploring collective expansion and squeeze out phenomena \cite{dan93}. We have simulated the central collisions of $^{20}Ne+^{20}Ne$, $^{40}Ar+^{45}Sc$, $^{58}Ni+^{58}Ni$, $^{86}Kr+^{93}Nb$, $^{129}Xe+^{124}Sn$, and $^{197}Au+^{197}Au$. Peak IMF multiplicity is observed to follow power law of form: $c A^П„_{tot}$, with exponent $П„$ close to unity. Next we try to understand the clusterization mechanism in spectator matter fragmentation using \emph{simulated annealing clusterization algorithm} (SACA) advanced by Puri \emph{et al}. Earlier recognition of fragments structure (around 60 fm/c) also points towards dynamical origin of fragments. We shall also highlight the importance of momentum dependent interactions in probing nuclear EoS via intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions. Estimation of baryonic entropy would also be discussed.

## Key claim

The peak multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) in central heavy-ion collisions follows a power-law scaling with total system mass, with exponent П„ close to unity, but this result is obtained specifically within the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model framework and is compared against experimental data from the MSU 4ПЂ-array setup.

## Context

This work extends the experimental multifragmentation studies performed on the MSU 4ПЂ-array setup by providing QMD model calculations that reproduce observed trends in IMF multiplicity as a function of beam energy and system mass. The SACA clusterization method, advanced by Puri et al., is applied to explain the universality feature in IMF production across the incident energy range 400-1000 AMeV, which was previously observed in ALADiN experimental data. The study enables further investigation of nuclear matter equation of state through momentum-dependent interactions and baryonic entropy estimation via composite particle yield ratios.
