# Anomalous net-baryon-rapidity spectra at RHIC

> Net-baryon rapidity distributions in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon are shown to consist of three components. The nonequilibrium contributions are accounted for in a Relativistic Diffusion Model. Near midrapidity, a third fraction containing approximately 22 protons reaches local statistical equilibrium in a discontinuous transition. It may be associated with a deconfinement of the participant partons and thus, serve as a signature for Quark-Gluon Matter formation.

# Anomalous net-baryon-rapidity spectra at RHIC

## Key claim
The net-baryon rapidity distribution in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon contains a third fraction of approximately 22 protons near midrapidity that reaches local statistical equilibrium in a discontinuous transition, which may serve as a signature for Quark-Gluon Matter formation, but only if the preliminary BRAHMS data hold, and the interpretation depends on the Relativistic Diffusion Model with a linear Fokker-Planck equation and q=1 statistics.

## Method
The author applied a Relativistic Diffusion Model (RDM) based on a Fokker-Planck equation in rapidity space, using linear drift and constant diffusion coefficients with delta-function initial conditions at the beam rapidities. The net-proton rapidity distribution was constructed as a linear superposition of two nonequilibrium Gaussian components (broadened by diffusion from the beam rapidities) plus a third equilibrium component at midrapidity, with the diffusion coefficient enhanced by a factor of approximately 3.7 beyond the weak-coupling dissipation-fluctuation theorem to account for collective and memory effects.

## Key numbers
- BRAHMS net-proton rapidity density at y=0: dN/dy = 7.1 ± 0.7 (stat.) ± 1.1 (sys.) in central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, before Lambda/anti-Lambda feed-down correction of 17.5 per cent.
- The equilibrium fraction contains Z_eq ≈ 22 protons, corresponding to about 14 per cent of the net protons.
- The theoretical weak-coupling rapidity width coefficient from the dissipation-fluctuation theorem is sqrt(D_y tau_y) = 7.6 × 10^{-2} at T = 170 MeV.
- The enhancement factor for the rapidity width coefficient due to collective and memory effects is g(sqrt(s)) ≈ 3.7 at RHIC energies, compared to g ≈ 2.6 at SPS energies.
- Beam rapidity is y_b = ±5.36 at p = 100 GeV/c per nucleon.
- The interaction time parameter tau_int/tau_y = 0.26 for the nonequilibrium components.

## Context
This work extends the Relativistic Diffusion Model previously applied to SIS, AGS, and SPS energies (Wolschin 1999) to RHIC energies, where a central valley in net-proton rapidity spectra appears for the first time. At SPS energies (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV), the data from Appelshauser et al. (1999) were well described by nonequilibrium distributions alone, with no separable equilibrated component. The author contrasts this with the RHIC result, where a discontinuous transition to equilibrium is inferred, and notes that perturbative QCD alone cannot account for the baryon transport over 4-5 units of rapidity, requiring nonperturbative mechanisms such as the gluon junction mechanism (Rossi and Veneziano 1980). The finding enables future investigation of the energy dependence of the midrapidity valley as a potential signature for quark-gluon plasma formation in heavy systems, as opposed to p-pbar collisions where no such signatures were found (Geich-Gimbel 1989).
