# $Υ$ absorption in hadronic matter

> The cross sections of $Υ$ absorption by $π$ and $ρ$ mesons are evaluated in a meson-exchange model. Including form factors with a cutoff parameter of 1 or 2 GeV, we find that due to the large threshold of these reactions the thermal average of their cross sections is only about 0.2 mb at a temperature of 150 MeV. Our results thus suggest that the absorption of directly produced $Υ$ by hadronic comovers in high energy heavy ion collisions is unimportant.

## Key claim

The thermal average of the cross sections for $\Upsilon$ absorption by $\pi$ and $\rho$ mesons is only about 0.2 mb at a temperature of 150 MeV when form factors with a cutoff parameter of 1 or 2 GeV are included, suggesting that absorption of directly produced $\Upsilon$ by hadronic comovers in high energy heavy ion collisions is unimportant.

## Method

The authors evaluated the cross sections for $\Upsilon$ absorption by $\pi$ and $\rho$ mesons using a meson-exchange model based on an SU(5) flavor symmetric Lagrangian. They considered the processes $\pi \Upsilon \rightarrow B^* \bar B$, $\pi \Upsilon \rightarrow B \bar {B^*}$, $\rho \Upsilon \rightarrow B \bar B$, and $\rho \Upsilon \rightarrow B^* \bar {B^*}$, including form factors with a monopole form at three-point vertices and a squared monopole form at four-point vertices, using cutoff parameters of 1 or 2 GeV. Coupling constants were determined from the vector meson dominance model and QCD sum rules rather than from the broken SU(5) symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Thermal average of both $\langle \sigma_{\pi \Upsilon}v \rangle$ and $\langle \sigma_{\rho \Upsilon}v \rangle$ is about 0.2 mb at a temperature of 150 MeV, after including form factors.
- With cutoff parameter between 1 and 2 GeV, the values for $\sigma_{\pi \Upsilon}$ and $\sigma_{\rho \Upsilon}$ are roughly 8 mb and 1 mb, respectively.
- Kinematic thresholds for the three $\Upsilon$ absorption processes (with $m_\rho=770$ MeV) are 1.01 GeV, 0.33 GeV, and 0.42 GeV, compared to 0.64 GeV, -0.14 GeV, and 0.15 GeV for the corresponding $J/\psi$ absorption processes.
- The ratio $\sigma_\Upsilon(\sqrt s=14~{\rm GeV})/\sigma_\psi (\sqrt s=5~{\rm GeV})$ is about 3, 0.5, and 0.4 for the three processes, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model previously applied to $J/\psi$ absorption by Haglin (2000) and others to the case of $\Upsilon$ absorption, motivated by the need to understand hadronic absorption effects before using $\Upsilon$ suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signature at RHIC and LHC. The results show that $\Upsilon$ absorption by hadronic comovers is much weaker than $J/\psi$ absorption (by a factor of 5 to 10 in thermal averages at the same temperature), primarily due to larger kinematic thresholds. This enables future studies to focus on partonic dissociation mechanisms for $\Upsilon$ suppression, and the authors note that extension to heavier $b\bar b$ states such as $\Upsilon (2S)$, $\Upsilon (3S)$, and $\chi_{bi}$ states is needed for a complete understanding of $\Upsilon (1S)$ yields from heavy ion collisions.
