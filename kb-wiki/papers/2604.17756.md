# Polarization, Maximal Concurrence, and Pure States in High-Energy Collisions

> We establish a quantitative relation between local spin polarization and quantum entanglement in two-qubit systems by deriving an upper bound on the concurrence at fixed local polarizations, showing that increasing polarization constrains the maximum achievable entanglement. We further demonstrate that this bound is saturated by pure states in certain cases with identical polarizations. As a concrete physical application, we consider the parity-violating process $e^+e^- \to Z^0 \to q\bar{q}$, which generates final-state spin polarization. We show that the maximal concurrence is attained in specific kinematic regions and is significantly reduced relative to the unpolarized case. These results establish a general, process-independent framework connecting local polarization, maximal entanglement, and the role of pure states.

## Key claim

The paper derives an upper bound on concurrence (a measure of quantum entanglement) for two-qubit systems with fixed local spin polarizations, showing that increasing polarization constrains the maximum achievable entanglement; this bound is saturated by pure states only when the two polarization magnitudes are equal.

## Method

The authors analytically derive the maximal concurrence as a function of the magnitudes of the two local Bloch vectors by restricting to X-state density matrices and then verifying the bound through exhaustive numerical simulation over 2,000,000 randomly generated general two-qubit density matrices. They apply the formalism to the parity-violating process e+e- → Z0 → qqbar in the center-of-mass frame, computing the final-state density matrix from the R-matrix and evaluating the concurrence as a function of quark speed u and scattering angle z = cosθ.

## Key numbers

- Maximal concurrence for up-type quarks in e+e- → Z0 → qqbar: Cup_max ≃ 0.744, achieved at u = 1 and z = 0 (ultra-relativistic limit with perpendicular scattering angle)
- Maximal concurrence for down-type quarks in e+e- → Z0 → qqbar: Cdown_max ≃ 0.353, achieved at u = 1 and z = 0
- The analytic upper bound on concurrence at fixed polarizations a and b: Cmax = sqrt((1 - max{a,b})(1 + min{a,b}))
- The bound is strictly tighter than the previous bound C ≤ sqrt(1 - max{||B+||2, ||B-||2}) from Zhang (2008) and Barr (2024) whenever ||B+|| ≠ ||B-||
- For the e+e- process at the optimal kinematic point, the Bloch vector magnitude is B = 2(cVq cAq)2 / ((cVq)2 + (cAq)2)2, with sin2θW = 0.2312

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum entanglement in high-energy collisions, complementing recent quantum tomography of fermion pairs in e+e- collisions by Guo (2026) and quantum information studies at the Electron-Ion Collider by Cheng (2025). The results provide a process-independent framework connecting single-particle polarization to pairwise maximal entanglement, applicable to any qqbar production mechanism including polarized deep inelastic scattering, parity-violating processes, and heavy-ion collisions. The bound is more restrictive than the earlier result of Zhang (2008) and Barr (2024), and the e+e- application shows that polarization from weak interactions significantly reduces the maximal concurrence from the unpolarized value of 1 obtained by Qi (2025).
