# Axial Anomaly and the Nucleon Spin

> In this letter, we have taken a particular Lagrangian, which was introduced to resolve U(1) problem, as an effective QCD Lagrangian, and have derived a formula of the quark content of the nucleon spin. The difference between quark content of the proton (ΔΣ_p) and that of the neutron (ΔΣ_n) is evaluated by this formula. Neglecting the higher-order isospin corrections, this formula can reduce to Efremov's results in the large N_c limit.

## Key claim

Using a specific effective QCD Lagrangian originally introduced to resolve the U(1) problem, the authors derive a formula for the quark content of the nucleon spin that includes higher-order isospin corrections; neglecting those corrections, the formula reduces to Efremov's results in the large N_c limit, but the full calculation yields a significantly larger difference between proton and neutron quark spin contributions.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian that incorporates the U(1) axial anomaly via a topological current and ghost field, with explicit SU(3) flavor breaking. They derive an expression for the quark spin content ΔΣ by evaluating the forward matrix elements of the axial-vector singlet current and the topological current at q²=0, obtaining ΔΣ = (1/2M_N)[λ + F_s(̃m²I - ̃m g_QNN)], where λ contains meson-nucleon coupling constants and mixing angles, and the ghost-nucleon coupling constant g_QNN is determined from charge symmetry breaking in the pion-nucleon coupling using Nijmegen phase shift analysis data.

## Key numbers

- ΔΣ_p = 0.66 (quark spin content of the proton)
- ΔΣ_n = 0.50 (quark spin content of the neutron)
- ΔΣ_p - ΔΣ_n = 0.16 (difference between proton and neutron quark spin content)
- Δ̃g_p = 0.36 (gluon anomaly contribution for the proton)
- Δ̃g_n = 0.20 (gluon anomaly contribution for the neutron)
- g_QNN = 3.49 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ (ghost-nucleon coupling constant)
- g²_ppπ⁰ = 13.48 (proton-proton-pion coupling constant squared)
- g²_nnπ⁰ = 13.61 (neutron-neutron-pion coupling constant squared)
- Mixing angles: θ₁ = -1.7×10⁻², θ₂ = 0.9×10⁻², θ₃ = -0.31
- F_π = 186.4 MeV (pion decay constant)
- ̃m² = Δm²_η' ≈ 0.726 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cheng and Li (1990) on the U(1) anomaly and nucleon spin, and directly addresses the calculation of Efremov et al. (1990) by including higher-order isospin corrections that were previously neglected. The authors show that the assumption Δm_η' g_QNN << g_η'NN ≈ 6.3 made by Efremov et al. is not justified, as their calculated value Δm_η' g_QNN ≈ 2.97 implies significant suppression of g_η'NN, consistent with the Skyrme model prediction of g_η'NN ≈ 0 in the large N_c limit. The results enable improved treatment of ghost pole exchange in one-boson-exchange potential analyses of nucleon-nucleon scattering.
