# Lattice Study of Dense Matter with Two Colors and Four Flavors

> We present results from a simulation of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with N_f=4 flavors of Wilson fermion and non-zero quark chemical potential mu, using the same 12^3x24 lattice, bare gauge coupling, and pion mass in cut-off units as a previous study with N_f=2. The string tension for N_f=4 is found to be considerably smaller implying smoother gauge field configurations. Thermodynamic observables and order parameters for superfluidity and color deconfinement are studied, and comparisons drawn between the two theories. Results for quark density and pressure as functions of mu are qualitatively similar for N_f=2 and N_f=4; in both cases there is evidence for a phase in which baryonic matter is simultaneously degenerate and confined. Results for the stress-energy tensor, however, suggest that while N_f=2 has a regime where dilute matter is non-relativistic and weakly-interacting, N_f=4 matter is relativistic and strongly-interacting for all values of mu above onset.

# Lattice Study of Dense Matter with Two Colors and Four Flavors

## Key claim

For SU(2) lattice gauge theory with four flavors of Wilson fermion, there is a range of chemical potential where degenerate quark matter remains color-confined, supporting the existence of a quarkyonic-like phase; however, unlike the two-flavor case where dilute matter is non-relativistic and weakly-interacting, four-flavor matter is relativistic and strongly-interacting for all chemical potentials above onset, as indicated by the stress-energy tensor.

## Method

The authors performed hybrid Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with N_f=4 flavors of Wilson fermion on a 12^3x24 lattice at bare gauge coupling beta=1.9, matching the pion mass in lattice units (m_pi a=0.677(14)) to a previous N_f=2 study. They measured the quark density, pressure, energy density, superfluid order parameter, Polyakov line, and gluonic observables as functions of quark chemical potential mu, with a diquark source ja=0.04 to control critical slowing down in the superfluid phase.

## Key numbers

- String tension sqrt(sigma) a = 0.1096(64) for N_f=4, yielding lattice spacing a = 0.052(3) fm assuming sqrt(sigma)=440 MeV, about a factor of three smaller than the N_f=2 value a=0.186(8) fm
- Pion mass m_pi a = 0.677(14) at kappa=0.158, matched to the N_f=2 value m_pi a=0.68(1)
- Onset chemical potential mu_o = 0.5 m_pi = 0.34 a^{-1}
- Quark density ratio n_q/n_SB^latt is close to unity over the range 0.75 < mu/m_pi < 1.0 for N_f=4
- Deconfinement transition occurs at mu_D approx 1.0 m_pi for both N_f=2 and N_f=4
- At the largest chemical potential studied (mu/m_pi=1.76), n_q^{N_f=4}/n^sat = 0.32, where n^sat=2N_f N_c is the saturation density
- Vacuum subtraction for quark energy density epsilon_q^0 a^4 = 0.3724(10) for N_f=4

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of QC_2D (SU(2) gauge theory) with N_f=2 by Hands et al. (2010) using the same lattice action and parameters, enabling a controlled comparison of flavor dependence. The finding of a confined but degenerate phase for mu_Q < mu < mu_D supports the quarkyonic matter concept originally discussed for large-N_c theories by McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The results also connect to chiral perturbation theory predictions by Kogut et al. (2000) for the BEC/BCS crossover at onset, and to earlier studies of the hadron spectrum and superfluid condensate by Hands et al. (2000, 2001, 2006, 2007) and Kogut et al. (2001). The dramatically smaller string tension for N_f=4 enables cleaner identification of topological excitations, to be explored in a follow-up paper by Hands et al. (2011).
