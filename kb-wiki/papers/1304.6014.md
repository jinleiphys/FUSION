# Meson Exchange Current (MEC) Models in Neutrino Interaction Generators

> Understanding of the so-called 2 particle-2 hole (2p-2h) effect is an urgent program in neutrino interaction physics for current and future oscillation experiments. Such processes are believed to be responsible for the event excesses observed by recent neutrino experiments. The 2p-2h effect is dominated by the meson exchange current (MEC), and is accompanied by a 2-nucleon emission from the primary vertex, instead of a single nucleon emission from the charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) interaction. Current and future high resolution experiments can potentially nail down this effect. For this reason, there are world wide efforts to model and implement this process in neutrino interaction simulations. In these proceedings, I would like to describe how this channel is modeled in neutrino interaction generators.

## Key claim

The 2p-2h effect, dominated by meson exchange current (MEC), is believed to be responsible for event excesses observed by recent neutrino experiments, but the paper notes that other approaches appear to work equally well for reproducing the MiniBooNE CCQE and NCEL cross section data, so more kinematic comparisons are needed to confirm MEC as the responsible process.

## Method

The paper describes how the MEC channel is modeled in the GENIE neutrino interaction generator, using the Dytman model for leptonic kinematics (a Gaussian distribution between QE and Delta peaks tuned to electron scattering data) and the nucleon cluster model for hadronic kinematics (two nucleons independently selected from the Fermi sea, forming a cluster that decays isotropically). The model is compared with MiniBooNE CCQE double differential cross section data and predictions for nucleon kinematics are shown, with comparisons to implementations in NuWro and GiBUU generators.

## Key numbers

- The Dytman model MEC strength is chosen to turn off monotonically from 1 GeV to 5 GeV to agree with both MiniBooNE and NOMAD data
- The nucleon cluster model predicts a characteristic peak in the sum of all nucleon kinetic energies around approximately 0.3 GeV at the MiniBooNE neutrino beam energy of about 800 MeV
- The Dytman model uses Sachs magnetic form factor with power 6 (dipole form factor is power 2), motivated by deuteron elastic scattering form factor
- In GENIE, initial nucleon pair ratio is n-p:n-n = 1:4 based on isospin ansatz; in NuWro it is n-p:n-n = 9:1 based on short range correlation; in GiBUU it is n-p:n-n = 12:5 based on statistical average

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the 2p-2h effect first pointed out by Martini et al. (2010) shortly after the MiniBooNE experiment showed their CCQE double differential cross section. The paper describes how MEC is implemented in GENIE, with comparisons to models in NuWro (which offers three leptonic models: np-nh model from Marteau et al., Valencia model from Nieves et al., and Bodek et al.'s transverse enhancement model) and GiBUU (from Mosel et al.). The work enables future high resolution experiments such as MicroBooNE to potentially nail down the MEC effect by precise counting of outgoing nucleons, building on earlier proton counting from NOMAD, K2K, and SciBooNE.
