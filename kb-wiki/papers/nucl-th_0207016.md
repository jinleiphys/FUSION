# The reconstructed final state of Au + Au collisions from PHENIX and STAR data at sqrt{s}=130 AGeV - indication for quark deconfinement at RHIC

> The final state of Au + Au collisions at sqrt{s}=130 AGeV at RHIC has been reconstructed within the framework of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model, by performing a simultaneous fit to final data on two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations of the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations, and final identified single particle spectra as measured by the PHENIX Collaboration. The results indicate a strongly three-dimensional expansion with a four-velocity field that is almost a spherically symmetric Hubble flow. We find large transverse geometrical source sizes, R_G = 9.8 +- 1.2 fm, a relatively short mean freeze-out time of tau0 = 6.1 +- 0.3 fm/c and a short duration of particle emission, Delta_tau = 0.02 +- 1.5 fm/c. Most strikingly, we find an indication for a hot central part or the hydrodynamically evolving core, characterized by a central temperature of T0 = 202 +- 13 MeV, that is close to (or even above) the deconfinement temperature of the quark-hadron phase transition. The best fit indicates a cold surface temperature of T_s = 110 +- 16 MeV. When the possibility of the hot center is excluded, the confidence level of the fit decreases from 28.9 % to 1.2 %. Predictions are made for the rapidity dependence of the slope parameters, for the transverse mass dependence of the rapidity-width of the single-particle spectra, and for the transverse velocity dependence of the non-indentical particle correlations.

## Key claim

The simultaneous fit to final PHENIX and STAR data within the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model indicates a hot central core with temperature T0 = 202 ± 13 MeV, close to or above the deconfinement temperature, but this result depends on the specific model assumptions and the authors note that the central temperature increased significantly (from 142 ± 4 MeV in preliminary analysis to 202 ± 13 MeV) when final rather than preliminary data were used.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous fit of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model to final PHENIX single-particle spectra (negative pions, kaons, and antiprotons) and final two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation (HBT) radius parameters from both the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV. The fit utilized a Gaussian approximation to the oscillating prefactor in the correlation function and included an m_t-dependent core-halo correction, with the absolute normalization of spectra used to extract chemical potentials. The quality of the fit was evaluated by chi-squared per degree of freedom and confidence level, and alternative fits with fixed central temperatures of 140 MeV and 110 MeV were performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Central temperature T0 = 202 ± 13 MeV from the best simultaneous fit (Fit-I)
- Surface temperature Ts = 110 ± 16 MeV from the best fit
- Transverse geometrical source size RG = 9.8 ± 1.2 fm
- Mean freeze-out time tau0 = 6.1 ± 0.3 fm/c
- Duration of particle emission Delta_tau = 0.02 ± 1.5 fm/c
- Average transverse flow <u_t> = 1.08 ± 0.17 from the best fit
- Confidence level of best fit: 28.9% (chi-squared/NDF = 74/68 = 1.09)
- When central temperature fixed to T0 = 140 MeV, confidence level decreased to 1.0%
- When central temperature fixed to T0 = 110 MeV, confidence level decreased to 2.7e-3%
- Central chemical potential for pions: mu0_pi- = 75 ± 19 MeV
- Central chemical potential for kaons: mu0_K- = 107 ± 14 MeV
- Central chemical potential for antiprotons: mu0_pbar = 305 ± 41 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model analysis previously applied to CERN SPS data (Ster et al., 1999) and preliminary RHIC data (Csorgo and Ster, 2001) to final published PHENIX and STAR data at RHIC. The results contradict predictions of a soft, long-lived, evaporative Quark-Gluon Plasma phase that would produce Rout/Rside >> 1, instead supporting predictions by Csorgo and Csernai (1993) of Rout/Rside approximately 1 and a short duration of particle emission. The findings are qualitatively and quantitatively similar to the hydrodynamical analysis of Florkowski and Broniowski (2002), who found T0 = 165 ± 7 MeV and average transverse flow velocity <beta_t> = 0.49 from single-particle spectra alone. The results enable future tests of the predicted rapidity dependence of slope parameters and the 1/m_t scaling of HBT radii at high transverse mass, which would provide independent constraints on the central temperature.
