# Nuclear medium effects in muonic neutrino interaction with energies from 0.2 GeV to 1.5 GeV

> Nuclear reactions induced by muon neutrino with energies in the range from 0.2 to 1.5 GeV in Monte Carlo calculations framework in the intra-nuclear cascade model are studied. This study was done by comparison between the available experimental data and theoretical values of total cross section, and the energy distribution of emitted lepton energy in the reaction muon neutrino-nucleus, using the targets: $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{27}$Al, $^{40}$Ar, $^{56}$Fe and $^{208}$Pb. A phenomenological toy model of primary neutrino-nucleon interaction gives a good agreement of our theoretical inclusive neutrino nucleus cross in comparison with the available experimental data. Some interesting results on the behavior of the cross section as function of 1p-1n and higher contributions are also sketched. The previous results on the percentage of fake events related in available experiments in $^{12}$C were expanded for the set studied nuclei. With the increase of mass target, the nuclear effects in the cross sections were observed along with the importance to take into account fake events in the reactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple phenomenological "Kinematic Model" (KM) for the primary neutrino-nucleon interaction, implemented within the CRISP intranuclear cascade code, gives good agreement with available experimental data for inclusive muon neutrino-nucleus cross sections, but only when like-type events (which include final state interactions) are considered; true-type events systematically underestimate the data, particularly for carbon-12.

## Method

The authors implemented a toy model of the primary neutrino-nucleon interaction (quasi-elastic and Delta resonance channels) into the CRISP Monte Carlo intranuclear cascade code, which treats the target nucleus as a Fermi gas in a square-well potential. The free parameters of the primary interaction model were fitted to neutrino-deuterium cross section data from Brookhaven National Laboratory and Argonne National Laboratory. The code then simulates the full intranuclear cascade including final state interactions, Pauli blocking, and particle evaporation, calculating cross sections for six target nuclei (carbon-12, oxygen-16, aluminum-27, argon-40, iron-56, and lead-208) over the neutrino energy range 0.2 to 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The CCqe cross section per nucleon is parameterized as a 4th-order polynomial in neutrino energy: sigma(CCqe) = sum_{n=0}^4 A_n E_nu^n, with coefficients A_0 = (2.77 +/- 1.30) x 10^-2, A_1 = 1.07 +/- 0.48, A_2 = (-1.01 +/- 0.31) x 10^-1, A_3 = (-2.45 +/- 0.25) x 10^-1, A_4 = (7.32 +/- 1.30) x 10^-2 (units of 10^-38 cm^2 for A_0, others dimensionless in context).
* The CCres channel sigma_0 values (dimensionless) are: channel B (nu_mu + p -> mu- + pi+ + p) = 0.66 +/- 0.12; channel C (nu_mu + n -> mu- + pi+ + n) = 0.26 +/- 0.07; channel D (nu_mu + n -> mu- + pi0 + p) = 0.25 +/- 0.07.
* The interaction threshold for neutrino-nucleon is around 0.45 GeV, while for nuclear targets the threshold is below that energy due to Fermi motion.
* The geometric cross section used is sigma_g = pi r_0^2 A^(2/3) with r_0 = 1.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the CRISP model, previously applied to photon, electron, proton, and neutron induced reactions (Deppman 2004, Andrade 2011, Israel 2014), to neutrino-nucleus interactions for the first time. It contrasts with event generators like GENIE (Andreopoulos 2009) and NUANCE (Casper 2002), which the authors note rely on free-particle cascade simulations with limited applicability at low energies and outdated nuclear physics. The study expands on prior work by Lalakulich and Mosel (2010, 2013) and Ericson (2016) on fake events in quasi-elastic reactions on carbon-12, now analyzing six nuclei and showing that the probability of crossed channel events is roughly proportional to A^(1/3). The results enable improved treatment of nuclear medium effects in neutrino oscillation experiments such as K2K and MiniBooNE.
