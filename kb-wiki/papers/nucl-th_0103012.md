# Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation with an optimal Ground State

> A new Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation approach is presented. The corresponding ground state is variationally determined and exhibits a minimum energy. New solutions for the ground state, some with spontaneously broken symmetry, of a solvable Hamiltonian are found. A non-iterative procedure to solve the non-linear QRPA equations is used and thus all possible solutions are found. These are compared with the exact results as well as with the solutions provided by other approaches.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new QRPA approach where the ground state is variationally determined by minimizing its energy, and claims that this method finds multiple solutions (some with spontaneously broken symmetry) that prevent the QRPA collapse, but the agreement with exact results depends on which solution branch is selected and on the interaction strength.

## Method

The authors consider the proton-neutron monopole Lipkin Hamiltonian, which is exactly solvable, for a system of 4 protons and 6 neutrons in a single j=9/2 shell. They perform a boson mapping of the Hamiltonian following the Marumori recipe, then apply a QRPA treatment with a new ansatz for the ground state that includes both a displacement parameter t and a squeezing parameter z. The non-linear QRPA equations are solved non-iteratively by first inserting the variational solution for r (obtained from minimizing the ground state energy) into the RPA matrices, then determining the ratio Y/X from the QRPA equations and inserting it into the constraint equation to find rho.

## Key numbers

* For kappa' = 0.5, the exact ground state energy is -0.104 MeV; solution a2 gives E_g.s. = -0.104 MeV and E_rpa = 1.906 MeV, while solution a1 gives E_g.s. = 7.001 MeV and E_rpa = 0.046 MeV.
* For kappa' = 1.5, the exact ground state energy is -0.823 MeV; solution a2 gives E_g.s. = -0.679 MeV and E_rpa = 0.908 MeV, while solution c1 gives E_g.s. = 6.950 MeV and E_rpa = 0.024 MeV.
* For kappa' = 2.5, the exact ground state energy is -3.638 MeV; solution c3 gives E_g.s. = -3.609 MeV and E_rpa = 4.580 MeV, while solution a2 gives E_g.s. = -1.841 MeV and E_rpa = 0.675 MeV.
* The particle-hole strength is fixed at chi' = 0.5, and the particle-particle strength kappa' is varied in the interval (0,3) MeV.
* The system has 4 protons and 6 neutrons in a j = 9/2 shell, with epsilon = 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to cure the collapse of standard QRPA, which occurs for a critical value of the attractive particle-particle force. It builds on the EPP QRPA formalism of Simkovic et al. (2000), which implemented the Pauli exclusion principle consistently, and on the semiclassical approach of Raduta et al. (2000), which defined a new static ground state. The new method unifies these complementary approaches and enables future realistic treatments of many-body systems, particularly for double beta decay calculations where the proton-neutron force is expected to be near its critical value.
