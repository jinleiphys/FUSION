# Bremsstrahlung in Alpha-Decay

> We present the first fully quantum mechanical calculation of photon radiation accompanying charged particle decay from a barrier resonance. The soft-photon limit agrees with the classical results, but differences appear at next-to-leading-order. Under the conditions of alpha-decay of heavy nuclei, the main contribution to the photon emission stems from Coulomb acceleration and may be computed analytically. We find only a small contribution from the tunneling wave function under the barrier.

# Bremsstrahlung in Alpha-Decay

## Key claim

The first fully quantum mechanical calculation of photon radiation accompanying alpha-decay from a barrier resonance shows that the dominant contribution to bremsstrahlung comes from Coulomb acceleration (classical motion), with only a small contribution from the tunneling wave function under the barrier; this contradicts the semiclassical theory of Dyakonov and Gornyi (1996) which predicted significant tunneling contributions.

## Method

The authors treat the alpha-particle decay as a single-particle barrier resonance using a Gamow state with complex energy for the initial state and continuum wave functions for the final state, with perturbative coupling of the photon to the current in the dipole approximation. The matrix element is separated into an imaginary part (classical Coulomb acceleration, evaluated analytically using hypergeometric functions) and a real part (tunneling contribution, evaluated numerically). The nuclear potential is a square well plus Coulomb potential, with parameters fixed to reproduce the empirical decay energy and mean lifetime for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For 210Po (E_alpha = 5.3 MeV), the dimensionless parameter Delta = hbar/(tau * E_alpha) = 7.3e-30, indicating an extremely narrow resonance.
- For 214Po (E_alpha = 7.7 MeV), the predicted rate at E_gamma = 0.6 MeV is 65 times higher than for 210Po.
- The classical soft-photon limit gives dP/dE_gamma = (4*alpha*Z_eff^2)/(3*pi) * (E_alpha/(m*c^2)) * E_gamma^{-1}.
- The semiclassical evaluation of the Coulomb integral deviates from the full quantum mechanical result by less than 1% for photon energies up to 1 MeV.
- For 210Po, zeta = 1 corresponds to E_gamma ≈ 0.24 MeV.

## Context

This work provides the first fully quantum mechanical treatment of bremsstrahlung in alpha-decay, extending earlier semiclassical work by Dyakonov and Gornyi (1996) which predicted observable tunneling contributions. The calculation is consistent with experimental data from Kasagi et al. (1997) for 210Po, showing no need for tunneling interference effects. However, the results disagree with measurements by D'Arrigo et al. (1994) for 214Po and 226Ra, which reported rates more than an order of magnitude larger than the quantum mechanical prediction. The formalism enables future calculations for other alpha-emitting nuclei and clarifies the role of tunneling in photon emission during barrier penetration.
