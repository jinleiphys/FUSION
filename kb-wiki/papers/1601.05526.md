# Quadrupole and monopole transition properties of $0^+_2$ in Gd isotopes

> The longstanding problem of characterization of the $0^+_2$ states in Gd isotopes is revisited by adopting the Nilsson$+$BCS mean field and the random-phase approximation. The interband electric quadrupole transition strengths varying almost two orders of magnitude are nicely reproduced at the same time as other observables. These results indicate that the $0^+_2$ states, in particular, those in lighter isotopes are well described as $β$ vibrations excited on top of deformed ground states without recourse to the shape-coexistence picture.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $0^+_2$ states in lighter Gd isotopes (particularly $^{152}$Gd and $^{154}$Gd) are well described as $\beta$ vibrations excited on top of deformed ground states within the random-phase approximation, without requiring the shape-coexistence picture that has been a longstanding alternative interpretation.

## Method

The authors employ a Nilsson + BCS mean field combined with the random-phase approximation (RPA), using a doubly-stretched quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction. They determine ground-state quadrupole deformations via the Nilsson-BCS-Strutinsky method assuming $\epsilon_4=0$, then calculate $B(E2,0^+_2\rightarrow2^+_1)$, $\rho^2(E0,0^+_2\rightarrow0^+_1)$, and their ratio $X(E0/E2)$ for $^{150-160}$Gd isotopes. The rotational effect on interband $E2$ transitions is incorporated using a method based on the generalized intensity relation (GIR) that expresses the angular-momentum dependence of transition matrix elements in terms of intrinsic matrix elements from the mean field and RPA.

## Key numbers

- $B(E2,0^+_2\rightarrow2^+_1)$ varies by almost two orders of magnitude across the Gd isotope chain, from large values in lighter isotopes to small values in heavier ones
- For $^{152}$Gd, the calculated in-band $B(E2,2^+_1\rightarrow0^+_1)=75$ W.u. from the rotational-model expression, consistent with the experimental value $73^{+7}_{-6}$ W.u.
- For $^{152}$Gd, the ratio of interband to in-band $B(E2)$ is 0.12 (without rotational effect) and 0.52 (with GIR rotational effect)
- For $^{152}$Gd, the zero-point amplitude $\beta_0=0.073$ (no rotational effect) and $\beta_0=0.154$ (with GIR) compared to static deformation $\beta_\mathrm{s}=0.207$
- For $^{154}$Gd, the RPA calculation gives $B(E2,0^+_2\rightarrow2^+_1)=22.7$ W.u. (without rotational effect) and the phonon has summed squared backward amplitudes $\sum\phi^2=3.07$
- For $^{158}$Gd, experimental $B(E2,0^+_2\rightarrow2^+_1)=1.1$ W.u. and $B(E2,0^+_3\rightarrow2^+_1)=2.1$ W.u., both smaller than expected for ordinary $\beta$ vibrations
- For $^{158}$Gd, $\rho^2(E0,2^+_2\rightarrow2^+_1)\le0.8\times10^{-3}$ and $\rho^2(E0,2^+_3\rightarrow2^+_1)=(17\pm3)\times10^{-3}$
- Ground-state deformations $\epsilon_2$ range from 0.07 ($^{150}$Gd, $N=86$) to 0.27 ($^{156}$Gd, $N=94$)

## Context

This work revisits the longstanding controversy over the nature of $0^+_2$ states in Gd isotopes, which has been debated since the 1970s when transfer reaction experiments (e.g., Ref. FGH for Gd) found cross sections comparable to ground-state transitions, interpreted via the shape-coexistence picture of Heyde and Wood (Ref. HW) and others. The authors extend their earlier RPA approach (Matsuzaki and Ueno, Ref. SN) and compare with the interacting boson approximation model (Ref. BWC) and geometrical coherent-state model (Ref. RRM) that reproduce $\rho^2(E0)$ data. The results support the $\beta$-vibration interpretation of Ref. Bu for lighter isotopes, while the persistent disagreement for $^{158}$Gd, where $E2$ strengths are strongly fragmented to higher energies, remains an open problem not resolved by this or previous calculations including those of Lo Iudice et al. (Ref. LSS) and Gerceklioglu (Ref. Ge).
