# Theoretical Developments in QCD Jet Energy Loss

> We review the recent developments in the theory of jet-quenching. First, we analyze the coherent vacuum cascade and incoherent medium-induced cascade separately. We then discuss the interplay between the two kinds of cascade and the resulting partial decoherence of the inner jet structure. Finally, we report on recent calculations of higher-order corrections. In particular, the dominant radiative corrections to jet observables that yield the renormalization of the jet-quenching parameter are addressed.

## Key claim

This review argues that a consistent theory of jet energy loss in QCD matter requires treating the vacuum parton cascade (ordered in virtuality/angle) and the medium-induced cascade (evolving in real time) as distinct but interleaved processes, with the key condition that when the medium's transverse resolution scale \(Q_s\) is much smaller than the inverse jet transverse size \(r_\perp^{-1}\), the jet remains coherent and the two cascades factorize.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for jet quenching by first analyzing the vacuum cascade under Modified-Leading-Log-Approximation (MLLA) with angular ordering, then the medium-induced cascade described by a real-time rate equation for the inclusive gluon distribution. It examines the interplay between these cascades through the antenna radiation pattern, where in-medium color randomization suppresses vacuum coherence and opens phase space for additional soft gluon radiation. Finally, it reports on the resummation of double-logarithmic radiative corrections to transverse momentum broadening and energy loss, which are absorbed into a renormalization group evolution of the jet-quenching parameter \(\hat q\).

## Key numbers

* The characteristic frequency for medium-induced radiation is \(\omega_c \equiv \hat q L^2\); gluons with frequencies larger than \(\omega_c\) are strongly suppressed by the LPM effect.
* The minimum angle for the multiple branching regime is \(\theta_s \equiv \theta_c / \bar\alpha^2\), where \(\theta_c \equiv Q_s / \omega_c \sim 1/\sqrt{\hat q L^3}\) and \(\bar\alpha \equiv \alpha_s N_c/\pi\).
* The decoherence parameter \(\Delta_\text{med} = 1 - \exp[-r_\perp^2 Q_s^2 / 12]\) interpolates between 0 (fully coherent, \(r_\perp \ll Q_s^{-1}\)) and 1 (full decoherence, \(r_\perp \gg Q_s^{-1}\)).
* The double-logarithmic radiative correction to transverse momentum broadening is \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle_\text{typ} \simeq \hat q L \left[1 + (\bar\alpha/2) \ln^2(L/\tau_0)\right]\), with cutoff \(\tau_0 \sim 1/m_D\).
* The mean radiative energy loss with resummed double logarithms scales as \(\langle \Delta E \rangle \sim L^{2+\gamma}\) with \(\gamma = 2\sqrt{\bar\alpha}\), lying between the weak-coupling result \(\sim L^2\) and the AdS/CFT strong-coupling result \(\sim L^3\).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on parton energy loss by Gyulassy (1993), Baier (1996), Zakharov (1996), and Wiedemann (2000), which focused on single-gluon radiation spectra, to a full description of in-medium parton showers. It addresses the conceptual gap between leading-order energy loss calculations and the need to describe jet observables measured at the LHC, particularly the missing energy in asymmetric dijet events reported by CMS (2014) and the softening of jet fragmentation functions in Pb-Pb collisions reported by ATLAS (2014). The renormalization group treatment of \(\hat q\) connects lattice calculations of the transport coefficient (Majumder, 2012; Panero, 2013) with next-to-leading-order Hard-Thermal-Loop calculations (Ghiglieri, 2015), and enables future precision phenomenology of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.
