# Critical dynamics near QCD critical point

> In this thesis, we study the critical dynamics near the QCD critical point. Near the critical point, the relevant modes for the critical dynamics are identified as the hydrodynamic modes. Thus, we first study the linear dynamics of them by the relativistic hydrodynamics. We show that the thermal diffusion mode is the most relevant mode, whereas the sound mode is suppressed around the critical point. We also find that the Landau equation, which is believed to be an acausal hydrodynamic equation, has no problem to describe slowly varying fluctuations. Moreover, we find that the Israel-Stewart equation, which is a causal one, gives the same result as the Landau equation gives in the long-wavelength region. Next, we study the nonlinear dynamics of the hydrodynamic modes by the nonlinear Langevin equation and the dynamic renormalization group (RG). In the vicinity of the critical point, the usual hydrodynamics breaks down by large fluctuations. Thus, we must consider the nonlinear Langevin equation. We construct the nonlinear Langevin equation based on the generalized Langevin theory. After the construction, we apply the dynamic RG to the Langevin equation and derive the RG equation for the transport coefficients. We find that the resulting RG equation turns out to be the same as that for the liquid-gas critical point except for an insignificant constant. Consequently, the bulk viscosity and the thermal conductivity strongly diverge at the critical point. Then, a system near the critical point can not be described as a perfect fluid by their strong divergences. We also show that the thermal and viscous diffusion modes exhibit critical-slowing down with the dynamic critical exponents z_{thermal} \sim 3 and z_{viscous} \sim 2, respectively. In contrast, the sound mode shows critical-speeding up with the negative exponent z_{sound} \sim -0.8.

## Key claim

The QCD critical point belongs to the same dynamic universality class as the liquid-gas critical point (model H), despite the relativistic nature of the underlying theory, because the dynamic renormalization group equations for the transport coefficients turn out to be identical except for an insignificant constant.

## Method

The author first studied the linear dynamics of the hydrodynamic modes (baryon number density and energy-momentum fluctuations) using relativistic hydrodynamics with both the Landau and Israel-Stewart equations. Then, a nonlinear Langevin equation was constructed based on the generalized Langevin theory (Mori theory) and relativistic hydrodynamics, using the thermodynamic potential of the 3d Ising model for the static properties. Finally, the dynamic renormalization group was applied to this Langevin equation to derive RG equations for the transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Thermal diffusion mode exhibits critical-slowing down with dynamic critical exponent z_thermal ~ 3
- Viscous diffusion mode exhibits critical-slowing down with dynamic critical exponent z_viscous ~ 2
- Sound mode shows critical-speeding up with negative exponent z_sound ~ -0.8
- Baryon number fluctuation is predicted to scale as <(delta N)^2> ~ xi^2 near the critical point

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Fujii (2004) and Son (2004) who identified the slow modes near the QCD critical point as hydrodynamic modes but did not study their dynamics, particularly the coupling between baryon number and energy-momentum fluctuations. The author also addresses the controversy over bulk viscosity divergence at the QCD critical point raised by Karsch (2008), Romatschke (2009), Moore (2009), and Sasaki (2010), showing that the dynamic RG treatment yields strong divergence of bulk viscosity and thermal conductivity. This enables future beam energy scan experiments at RHIC to search for signatures of the QCD critical point through enhanced baryon number fluctuations and modified transport properties.
