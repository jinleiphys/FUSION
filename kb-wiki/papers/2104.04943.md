# Generating function for nucleus-nucleus scattering amplitudes in Glauber theory

> A new approach to deal with the scattering amplitudes in Glauber theory is proposed. It relies on the use of generating function, that has been explicitly found. The method is applied to the analytical calculation of the nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering amplitudes in the all interaction orders of the Glauber theory.

# Generating function for nucleus-nucleus scattering amplitudes in Glauber theory

## Key claim
The authors propose a novel analytical method using a generating function to compute nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering amplitudes in Glauber theory to all interaction orders, achieving accuracy comparable to Monte Carlo calculations but with significantly less computational time. The method relies on the assumption that the nucleon-nucleon interaction range is small compared to typical nuclear sizes, and that nucleon-nucleon correlations are neglected.

## Method
The authors represent the Glauber scattering amplitude as a functional integral over auxiliary fields, then derive a closed-form generating function by exploiting the short-range nature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction to treat the interaction as point-like. The generating function is evaluated analytically by discretizing the spatial integrals and taking the continuum limit, yielding an expression in terms of overlap integrals of nuclear density profiles. The scattering amplitude is obtained by differentiating the generating function with respect to auxiliary parameters.

## Key numbers
- For 12C-12C scattering at 950 MeV per nucleon with R_rms = 2.49 fm: reaction cross section = 867 mb (exact differentiation) and 857 mb (A>>1 approximation), compared to experimental value 853 ± 6 mb.
- For 12C-12C scattering at 950 MeV per nucleon: total cross section = 1363 mb (exact differentiation) and 1371 mb (A>>1 approximation).
- For 11Li-12C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon: reaction cross section = 1024 mb (Gaussian halo), 1031 mb (O halo), 1033 mb (2S halo), 1021 mb (non-halo), compared to experimental 1040 ± 60 mb.
- For 11Be-12C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon: reaction cross section = 911 mb (Gaussian), 918 mb (O), 916 mb (2S), 914 mb (non-halo), compared to experimental 942 ± 8 mb.
- For 14Be-12C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon: reaction cross section = 1120 mb (Gaussian), 1128 mb (O), 1131 mb (2S), 1103 mb (non-halo), compared to experimental 1139 ± 90 mb.
- Nucleon-nucleon total cross section used: σ_NN_tot = 43 mb; slope parameter β = 0.2 fm².

## Context
This work extends the Glauber theory framework for nucleus-nucleus scattering, which has been applied extensively to hadron-nucleus collisions but is more computationally demanding for nucleus-nucleus systems. The authors derive a generating function that reproduces the well-known optical approximation (Czyz and Maximon, 1969) and the rigid target approximation (Bialas and Bleszynski, 1977; Alkhazov et al., 1977) as limiting cases. The method enables analytical calculations for any pair of colliding nuclei, including halo nuclei such as 11Li, 11Be, and 14Be, and can be extended to compute differential elastic cross sections with Coulomb corrections for heavy nuclei. The results agree with Monte Carlo calculations by Merino et al. (2009) using the same parameters and density parametrization.
