# Validity of Born Approximation for Nuclear Scattering in Path Integral Representation

> The first and second Born approximation are studied with the path integral representation for $ {\cal T} $ matrix. The $ {\cal T}$ matrix is calculated for Woods-Saxon potential scattering. To make corresponding integrals solvable analytically, an approximate function for the Woods-Saxon potential is used. Finally it shown that the Born series is converge at high energies and orders higher than two in Born approximation series can be neglected.

## Key claim

The Born series converges for Woods-Saxon potential scattering at high energies, and terms beyond second order can be neglected; however, this conclusion is demonstrated only for the specific system \(^{16}\text{O}+^{20}\text{Ne}\) using an approximate analytic form of the Woods-Saxon potential that reproduces the true potential to better than 3%.

## Method

The authors derive a path-integral representation of the \(\mathcal{T}\) matrix for potential scattering using velocity path integrals and "phantom" degrees of freedom to cancel divergent phases. They expand the \(\mathcal{T}\) matrix in powers of the potential to obtain the first and second Born approximations, then apply these to an approximate analytic form of the Woods-Saxon potential. The first and second Born \(\mathcal{T}\)-matrix elements are computed for the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{20}\text{Ne}\) system as functions of scattering energy and angle.

## Key numbers

- The approximate analytic function for the Woods-Saxon potential reproduces the true potential with precision better than 3% for any \(r\) value.
- Woods-Saxon parameters for the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{20}\text{Ne}\) system: \(V_0 = 10\ \text{MeV}\), \(r_0 = 1.48\ \text{fm}\), \(a = 0.45\ \text{fm}\).
- The first and second Born approximations are compared at \(E = 24.5\ \text{MeV}\); the second Born term is smaller than the first and can be neglected (shown in figures with scale \(10^{-7}\)).
- The Fourier transform of the Woods-Saxon potential is given as \(\tilde V(\mathbf{q}) = \pi V_0 a^3 \left[ \frac{7 e^{-R_0/a}}{(q^2 a^2 + 1)^2} - \frac{6 e^{-2R_0/a}}{(q^2 a^2 + 4)^2} \right]\).

## Context

This work extends the velocity path-integral formalism for the \(\mathcal{T}\) matrix developed in prior work (Campbell et al., cited as reference [12]) to the Woods-Saxon potential, which is widely used in nuclear shell model and optical model calculations. The study enables the use of Born approximation for elastic scattering analyses with short-range nuclear potentials, showing that higher-order terms can be safely neglected at high energies. The approximate analytic form of the Woods-Saxon potential follows a method used for two-parameter Fermi distribution density functions (reference [21]).
