# Variation after $K$-projection in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for low-energy dipole excitations in $^{10}$Be and $^{16}$O

> For study of dipole excitations, a new method of variation after $K$-projection in the framework of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the deformation $β$ constraint was proposed. The method was applied to $^{10}$Be and $^{16}$O to describe low-energy dipole excitations and found to be a useful and economical approach for dipole excitations. In the application to $^{10}$Be, two dipole states in low-energy region were described. For $^{16}$O, the $1_1^-$ and $1_2^-$ states with remarkable dipole strengths were obtained. The $1_1^-$ state is characterized by significant toroidal dipole (TD) and compressional dipole strengths, whereas the $1_2^-$ has significant TD strength and shows a developed $α+ {}^{16}$O cluster structure. Dipole properties in $^{16}$O were discussed by analysis of current densities of the dipole transitions.

## Key claim

The proposed variation-after-$K$-projection method within antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with a deformation $\beta$ constraint is a useful and economical approach for describing low-energy dipole excitations, but its advantage over the simpler parity-projection-only method is case-dependent: it is essential for obtaining the $1_2^-$ state in $^{10}$Be (which requires triaxial configurations), whereas for $^{16}$O the essential bases can also be obtained by the parity-projection-only method, albeit with less energy gain.

## Method

The authors performed energy variation of AMD wave functions after simultaneous parity and $K$-projection (K-VAP) under a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$, using the $\beta$-constraint AMD framework. For each nucleus, they generated $K^\pi=0^+$, $0^-$, and $1^-$ basis wave functions at various $\beta$ values, then superposed these bases using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with full angular momentum projection to construct the ground and $1^-$ states. They used the Volkov No.2 central force with G3RS spin-orbit force for $^{10}$Be and the MV1 central force with G3RS spin-orbit force for $^{16}$O.

## Key numbers

- $^{10}$Be: $1_1^-$ state at $E_x = 7.96$ MeV with $B(\text{TD}) = 2.39 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$, $B(\text{CD}) = 2.39 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^4$, $B(E1) = 6.92 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^2$ (K-VAP result)
- $^{10}$Be: $1_2^-$ state at $E_x = 14.9$ MeV with $B(\text{TD}) = 2.59 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^4$, $B(\text{CD}) = 2.89 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^4$, $B(E1) = 2.47 \times 10^{-1}$ fm$^2$ (K-VAP result)
- $^{10}$Be: energy-weighted $E1$ strength for $0_1^+ \rightarrow 1_2^-$ accounts for about 10% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule
- $^{16}$O: ground state binding energy $120.3$ MeV (K-VAP), slightly underestimating the experimental value $127.6$ MeV
- $^{16}$O: $1_1^-$ state at $E_x = 9.78$ MeV with $B(\text{TD}) = 3.62 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$, $B(\text{CD}) = 2.87 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$ (K-VAP result)
- $^{16}$O: $1_2^-$ state at $E_x = 12.47$ MeV with $B(\text{TD}) = 3.80 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$, $B(\text{CD}) = 9.35 \times 10^{-5}$ fm$^4$ (K-VAP result)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study of low-energy dipole excitations in $^{10}$Be (Shikata and Kanada-En'yo, 2019) and the earlier AMD study of $^{16}$O by Kanada-En'yo (2017). The method improves upon the standard $\beta$-constraint AMD with parity projection only (P-VAP) by incorporating $K$-projection in the variation, which allows efficient generation of triaxially deformed configurations essential for certain dipole states without requiring the more expensive $\beta\gamma$-constraint AMD. The results enable future systematic studies of low-energy dipole modes (toroidal, compressional, and cluster excitations) across a range of stable and unstable nuclei.
