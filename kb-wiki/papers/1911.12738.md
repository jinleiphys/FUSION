# Gluon Radiation from a classical point particle II: dense gluon fields

> The goal of this paper is to extend the results of Ref. [1], where formulae were derived for gluonic radiation for a high energy nucleus colliding with a classical colored particle. In Ref. [1], we computed the amplitudes for radiation in the fragmentation region of the particle for a dilute gluonic field. In this paper, we compute the radiation by solving the fluctuation equations of the dense background field in a specific gauge which makes it simple to solve the asymptotic radiation from an initial condition immediately after the passage of the nucleus. We identify and compute two components of gluon radiation, a bulk component which extends to the central region and bremsstrahlung, which may give rise to an experimentally observable intensity peak in the target fragmentation region.

## Key claim

The paper extends the dilute-field gluon radiation results of Kajantie, McLerran, and Paatelainen (2019) to the case of a dense gluonic background field, deriving analytic expressions for two distinct radiation components (bulk and bremsstrahlung) that together describe the full gluon spectrum in the target fragmentation region, subject to the condition that the calculation uses a classical point-particle approximation and a specific gauge choice that simplifies the asymptotic radiation problem.

## Method

The authors solve the fluctuation equations for the dense background gluon field in light-cone coordinates, using advanced and retarded Green's functions to compute the radiation field from a classical colored quark that is kicked by the passage of a high-energy nucleus. They evaluate the Fourier-transformed radiation amplitudes at the light-cone boundary \(x^-=0\), then compute the gluon distribution by squaring the amplitudes and averaging over an ensemble of color sources using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with a Gaussian distribution of color charges, including the correlation function of adjoint Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale \(Q_s^2 = \frac{1}{2} \lambda g^2 C_A\) where \(C_A = N\) is the adjoint Casimir, with \(\lambda\) describing the color source density of the nucleus.
- The infrared-regulated dipole function \(D_m(r_T) = \frac{1}{2\pi m_{\rm IR}^2}[1 - m_{\rm IR} r_T K_1(m_{\rm IR} r_T)]\) with \(m_{\rm IR} = 0.2\) used as the infrared regulator.
- The large-\(q_T\) behavior of the Wilson line correlation function: \(S(q_T) = \frac{2(Q_s^A)^2}{q_T^4} + \frac{8(Q_s^A)^4}{\pi q_T^6}\left(\log\frac{q_T}{\Lambda_{\rm QCD}} - 1\right)\) for \(q_T \gg Q_s^A = 2\).
- The bulk gluon spectrum at large \(k_T\) scales as \(\sim 1/k_T^4\) with a logarithmic correction, with the rapidity distribution showing a linear increase for \(y < \log(k_T/m_{\rm IR})\) and a plateau above this boundary.
- The bremsstrahlung component decays as \(\sim e^{-2y}\) when approaching the central region.

## Context

This work extends the dilute-field results of Kajantie, McLerran, and Paatelainen (2019) to dense gluon fields, building on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and subsequent work on gluon production in pA collisions by Blaizot, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2004) and Dumitru, Hayashigaki, and Jalilian-Marian (2006). The results enable computation of energy deposition from gluon radiation in the target fragmentation region as input to initial conditions for hydrodynamic evolution of QCD matter, and could be combined with baryon number initial values from the Dirac equation solutions of McLerran, Schlichting, and Sen (2019).
