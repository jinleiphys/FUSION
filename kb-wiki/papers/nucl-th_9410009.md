# Parity Mixed Doublets in A = 36 Nuclei

> The $γ$-circular polarizations ($P_γ$) and asymmetries ($A_γ$) of the parity forbidden M1 + E2 $γ$-decays: $^{36}Cl^{\ast} (J^π = 2^{-}; T = 1; E_{x} = 1.95 $ MeV) $\rightarrow$ $^{36}Cl (J^π = 2^{+}; T = 1; g.s.)$ and $^{36}Ar^{\ast} (J^π = 2^{-}; T = 0; E_{x} = 4.97 $ MeV) $\rightarrow$ $^{36}Ar^{\ast} (J^π = 2^{+}; T = 0; E_{x} = 1.97 $ MeV) are investigated theoretically. We use the recently proposed Warburton-Becker-Brown shell-model interaction. For the weak forces we discuss comparatively different weak interaction models based on different assumptions for evaluating the weak meson-hadron coupling constants. The results determine a range of $P_γ$ values from which we find the most probable values: $P_γ$ = $1.1 \cdot 10^{-4}$ for $^{36}Cl$ and $P_γ$ = $3.5 \cdot 10^{-4}$ for $^{36}Ar$.

## Key claim

The parity nonconserving (PNC) circular polarizations and asymmetries for two parity-mixed doublets in A=36 nuclei are calculated theoretically, yielding most probable values of \(P_\gamma = 1.1 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(^{36}\text{Cl}\) and \(P_\gamma = 3.5 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(^{36}\text{Ar}\); these values depend on the choice of weak interaction model and are subject to uncertainties in the weak meson-hadron coupling constants, with the \(^{36}\text{Ar}\) result being less reliable due to truncations in the shell-model space.

## Method

The author used the shell-model code OXBASH with the Warburton-Becker-Brown interaction in a \(2s1d\)-\(2p1f\) model space, truncating to \((2s1d)^{20}\) (\(0\hbar\omega\)) for positive parity states and \((2s1d)^{19}(2p1f)^{1}\) (\(1\hbar\omega\)) for negative parity states. The PNC matrix elements were calculated for the transitions \(^{36}\text{Cl}^* (2^-; T=1; E_x=1.95\ \text{MeV}) \rightarrow ^{36}\text{Cl} (2^+; T=1; \text{g.s.})\) and \(^{36}\text{Ar}^* (2^-; T=0; E_x=4.97\ \text{MeV}) \rightarrow ^{36}\text{Ar}^* (2^+; T=0; E_x=1.97\ \text{MeV})\) using weak interaction models from Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (DDH), Dubovik and Zenkin (DZ), Adelberger and Haxton (AH), and Kaiser and Meissner (KM). Short-range correlations were included by multiplying harmonic oscillator wave functions with the Miller and Spencer factor.

## Key numbers

- Most probable circular polarization for \(^{36}\text{Cl}\): \(P_\gamma = 1.1 \times 10^{-4}\) (using \(M_{PNC} = -0.057\ \text{eV}\)).
- Most probable circular polarization for \(^{36}\text{Ar}\): \(P_\gamma = 3.5 \times 10^{-4}\) (using \(M_{PNC} = 0.122\ \text{eV}\)).
- DDH PNC matrix element for \(^{36}\text{Ar}\): \(M_{PNC} = 0.12\ \text{eV}\) (dominant transition \(1d_{3/2} - 2p_{3/2}\)).
- For \(^{36}\text{Cl}\) with DDH couplings: pion contribution \(M_\pi = 0.050\ \text{eV}\) and rho+omega contribution \(M_{\rho+\omega} = -0.069\ \text{eV}\); for KM couplings: \(M_\pi = 0.00207\ \text{eV}\) and \(M_{\rho+\omega} = -0.023\ \text{eV}\).
- For \(^{36}\text{Ar}\) with DDH couplings: \(M_\rho = 0.113\ \text{eV}\) and \(M_\omega = 0.009\ \text{eV}\).
- Experimental upper limit for \(^{18}\text{F}\): \(P_\gamma \leq 3.9 \times 10^{-4}\) (PAGE87).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of parity-mixed doublets (PMD) in light nuclei, specifically a prior investigation of the \(^{36}\text{Cl}\) case by ODGS using a smaller \((1d_{3/2}, 1f_{7/2})\) model space where the one-body PNC matrix element vanished. The present calculation uses a larger model space and finds that the one-body term dominates. The \(^{36}\text{Ar}\) PMD is isoscalar and sensitive to the \(h_\rho^0\) weak coupling constant, analogous to the \(0^+1, 0^-1\) doublet in \(^{14}\text{N}\) (HCBW93), while the \(^{36}\text{Cl}\) PMD is isoscalar+isovector and sensitive to a combination of \(h_\rho^0\) and \(h_\pi^1\), complementary to the \(^{19}\text{F}\) case (Adelberger and Haxton, 1985) where the isoscalar and isovector contributions are in phase. The predicted polarizations are within the accuracy of existing experimental setups, enabling possible measurements via forward-backward asymmetry in reactions such as \(^{39}\text{K}(\vec{p},\alpha)^{36}\text{Ar}\) or \(^{39}\text{K}(\vec{n},\alpha)^{36}\text{Cl}\).
