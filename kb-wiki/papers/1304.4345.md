# Conformal Behavior in QCD

> We give a new perspective on the properties of quarks and gluons at finite temperature T in N_f = 2 ~ 6 QCD. We point out the existence of an IR fixed point for the gauge coupling constant at T>T_c (T_c is the chiral phase transition temperature). Based on this observation we predict theoretically and verify numerically that the correlation functions of a meson G(t) at T/T_c > 1 decay with a power-law corrected Yukawa-type decaying form, G(t)=c exp(-m t)/t^alpha in the "conformal region" defined by m < c Lambda_IR, where Lambda_IR is the IR cutoff, m is the characteristic scale of the spectrum in the meson cannel and c is a constant of order 1. The decaying form is the characteristics of conformal theories with an IR cutoff. We discuss in detail how the resulting hyper scaling relation of physical observables may modify the existing argument about the order of the chiral phase transition in the N_f=2 case.

## Key claim

The authors claim that for QCD with $N_f = 2 \sim 6$ at temperatures above the chiral phase transition temperature $T_c$, the gauge coupling runs to an infrared fixed point at the scale of the temperature $T$, making the system a "conformal field theory with an IR cutoff." This implies that meson correlation functions decay with a power-law corrected Yukawa form $G_H(t) = c \exp(-\tilde{m}_H t)/t^{\alpha_H}$ rather than a simple exponential, but only in a "conformal region" defined by $m_H < c \, \Lambda_{\mathrm{IR}}$ where $\Lambda_{\mathrm{IR}}$ is the IR cutoff.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with $N_f=2$ massless Wilson quarks and the standard one-plaquette gauge action on a $16^3 \times 64$ lattice, with anti-periodic boundary conditions in the time direction. They computed the temporal propagator $G_H(t)$ of local meson operators (pseudo-scalar and vector channels) for several values of the gauge coupling $\beta = 6.5, 7.0, 8.0, 10.0, 15.0$ and various bare quark masses, and fitted the data to either an exponential decay form $c_H \exp(-m_H t)$ or a power-law corrected Yukawa form $\tilde{c}_H \exp(-\tilde{m}_H t)/t^{\alpha_H}$.

## Key numbers

* At $\beta=6.5$ with $K=0.146$ ($m_q = 0.0337(3)$), the pseudo-scalar channel fit gives $\tilde{m}_\pi = 0.371(9)$ and $\alpha_\pi = 0.71(8)$.
* At $\beta=6.5$ with $K=0.147$ ($m_q = 0.0083(2)$), the pseudo-scalar channel fit gives $\tilde{m}_\pi = 0.295(16)$ and $\alpha_\pi = 1.00(16)$.
* At $\beta=10.0$ with $K=0.130$ ($m_q = 0.1626(1)$), the exponential fit gives $m_\pi = 0.3887(5)$ for the state continued from lower $K$, while the state continued from higher $K$ gives $\tilde{m}_\pi = 0.495(11)$ and $\alpha_\pi = 1.40(11)$, indicating a first-order transition.
* At $\beta=5.9$ (below the chiral transition), the effective mass shows a plateau even for very small quark mass $m_q = 0.0046$ ($K=0.153$), consistent with exponential decay.
* The chiral transition is identified around $\beta_c \approx 6.0$ with $K_c \approx 0.151$ on the $16^3 \times 64$ lattice.

## Context

This work extends the concept of "conformal field theories with an IR cutoff" introduced by the same group in studies of many-flavor conformal gauge theories at zero temperature (Ishikawa, Iwasaki, Nakayama, Yoshie, 2012). It directly addresses the long-standing issue of the order of the chiral phase transition in $N_f=2$ QCD, which Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) mapped to a three-dimensional $O(4)$ sigma model. The authors argue that the existence of an IR fixed point and the resulting hyper-scaling relation $m_H = c \, m_q^{1/(1+\gamma^*)}$ violates the analyticity assumption used by Aoki et al. (2012) to argue for a first-order transition, potentially resolving the apparent contradiction with earlier $O(4)$ scaling observations by Karsch (1994) and Iwasaki et al. (1997).
