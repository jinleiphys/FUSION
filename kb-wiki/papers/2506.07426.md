# Tracing the Evolution of Nuclear Excitation at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We investigate the evolution of nuclear excitation in electron-nucleus (e+A) collisions at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) using the BeAGLE event generator. Leveraging the EIC's unique collider kinematics, we demonstrate the remarkable capability to separate distinct nuclear reaction stages, hard scattering, intranuclear cascade, and nuclear de-excitation, in the laboratory frame. Our systematic analysis reveals that event-by-event fluctuations in the mean transverse momentum (k) are highly sensitive to the intranuclear cascade formation time and nuclear geometry, while minimally affected by variations in electron beam energy. These findings establish k as a robust observable for constraining nuclear excitation mechanisms, providing critical benchmarks for future EIC experiments and guiding theoretical advancements in nuclear transport modeling.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that event-by-event fluctuations in the mean transverse momentum (κ) can serve as a robust observable for constraining the intranuclear cascade formation time in electron-nucleus collisions at the EIC, but this sensitivity is conditional on the observable being measured in specific rapidity regions (ePIC-II and ePIC-III) where intranuclear cascade and nuclear de-excitation processes dominate, respectively.

## Method

The study uses version 1.03 of the BeAGLE event generator, a hybrid code that integrates DPMJet for hadron production and intranuclear cascades, PYTHIA6 for partonic interactions, and FLUKA for nuclear remnant de-excitation. The authors generated 50 million events for each configuration and varied the formation time parameter τ_f to examine its effect on the variance of event-by-event transverse momentum fluctuations, defined as κ = C / M(p_T)^2, where C is the second-order momentum correlation and M(p_T) is the mean transverse momentum. The analysis was performed for e+Au collisions at 18×110 GeV and 5×110 GeV, and for e+Au, e+Ru, and e+Cu at 18×110 GeV, with results separated into the ePIC-II (intranuclear cascade-dominated) and ePIC-III (de-excitation-dominated) rapidity regions.

## Key numbers

- κ increases with τ_f in the intranuclear cascade-dominated region (ePIC-II) and decreases with τ_f in the de-excitation-dominated region (ePIC-III) for e+Au collisions at 18×110 GeV
- κ shows minimal dependence on electron beam energy when comparing 5×110 GeV and 18×110 GeV configurations for a fixed nuclear target (Au)
- κ exhibits pronounced sensitivity to target nucleus size when comparing Au, Ru, and Cu at 18×110 GeV, with the magnitude and formation-time dependence changing significantly as system size decreases
- For the ePIC-II selection (intranuclear cascade-dominated region), κ increases with decreasing atomic mass number A
- For the ePIC-III selection (de-excitation-dominated region), κ decreases with increasing atomic mass number A, with weaker system-size dependence for small nuclei

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear excitation in lepton-nucleus collisions that were restricted to fixed-target configurations (ALADIN 2002, HADES 2011, Cerizza 2016), where the three reaction stages (hard scattering, intranuclear cascade, and nuclear de-excitation) overlap substantially in rapidity. The paper builds on the BeAGLE model framework (Chang 2022) and the formation zone formalism (Ferrari 1995, Zheng 2014) to propose κ as a new observable that can disentangle intranuclear cascade and de-excitation contributions at the EIC. The results provide critical benchmarks for future EIC experiments and guide theoretical advancements in nuclear transport modeling, particularly for constraining the formation time parameter in models such as DPMJet and FLUKA.
