# Axial Anomaly, Mismatched Fermi Surfaces and Vector Interaction in Dense Neutral Quark Matter

> We report on effects of the q-$\bar{\rm q}$ vector interaction and/or the U(1)$_A$-anomaly-induced chiral-diquark coupling on the charge-neutral quark matter in $β$-equilibrium. We show that when the vector coupling is absent, there can appear a cross-over region sandwiched by two critical points in the intermediate temperature ($T$) region, while the phase transition in the low-$T$ region including zero temperature keeps being first order until the strength of the anomaly term is increased to have a critical value. On the other hand, when the vector coupling is also present, there appears a crossover region in the low-$T$ area including zero temperature with a new critical point, as was first demonstrated by Kitazawa et al and the present authors without and with the charge-neutral condition, respectively. We remark that the possible chromomagnetic instability is suppressed and can be even completely absent owing to the enhanced diquark coupling due to the anomaly term and/or by the vector interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the U(1)_A-anomaly-induced chiral-diquark coupling can, together with the vector interaction, produce crossover regions and multiple critical points in the phase diagram of charge-neutral quark matter under beta-equilibrium, but the specific phase structure (e.g., whether a crossover window opens at low temperature including zero temperature) depends critically on the relative strengths of the anomaly term (K'/K) and the vector coupling (G_V/G_S).

## Method

The authors use a two-plus-one-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with four-Fermi scalar-pseudoscalar interactions (G_S), U(3)_L × U(3)_R-invariant vector-axial-vector interactions (G_V), the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft anomaly term (strength K), and an additional U(1)_A-anomaly-induced chiral-diquark coupling term (strength K'). They impose electric charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium, then solve the mean-field gap equations for the constituent quark masses M_i and dynamical Majorana masses Δ_i as functions of temperature and chemical potential, mapping the phase boundaries for chiral restoration and color-superconducting (CSC) transitions.

## Key numbers

- Current quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV.
- Without vector interaction (G_V = 0): at K'/K = 2.0, the phase diagram has a single critical point; increasing K'/K to 2.2 opens a crossover window in the intermediate-T region; at K'/K = 2.8, the first-order critical line disappears completely.
- With vector coupling fixed at G_V/G_S = 0.25: a crossover window in the intermediate-T region appears for much smaller K'/K; at K'/K = 0.57, a new crossover window opens in the low-T region including zero temperature; at K'/K = 1.0, the first-order critical line ceases to exist.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Zhang, Fukushima and Kunihiro (2008) who showed a crossover region sandwiched by two critical points in charge-neutral quark matter without the anomaly term, and the study by Kitazawa et al (2002) who demonstrated that the vector interaction enlarges the crossover region and can eliminate the QCD critical point. The paper also addresses the chromomagnetic instability, showing it can be suppressed or eliminated by the enhanced diquark coupling from the anomaly term and/or the vector interaction. The results enable future work on the combined fluctuations of chiral, diquark and baryon densities as the soft mode near the phase boundary.
