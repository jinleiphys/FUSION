# Probing the structure of the initial state of heavy-ion collisions with $p_T$-dependent flow fluctuations

> The connection between initial-state geometry and anisotropic flow can be quantified through a well-established mapping between $p_T$-integrated flow harmonics and cumulants of the initial transverse energy distribution. In this paper we successfully extend this mapping to also include $p_T$-differential flow. In doing so, we find that subleading principal components of anisotropic flow can reveal previously unobserved details of the hydrodynamic response, in both the linear and the nonlinear regimes. Most importantly, we show that they provide novel information on the small-scale structures present in the initial stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the well-established linear mapping between initial-state geometry and \(p_T\)-integrated anisotropic flow to also describe \(p_T\)-differential flow, showing that subleading principal components of flow fluctuations can reveal previously unobserved details of the hydrodynamic response and provide novel information on small-scale structures in the initial state of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, though the mapping remains approximate and the subleading components are much smaller than the leading ones.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event hybrid hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV using the TRENTo initial-state model, Music viscous hydrodynamics, and UrQMD for the hadronic afterburner, with model parameters taken from a Bayesian analysis optimized to LHC data. They characterized the initial transverse energy density using a cumulant expansion to define eccentricities \(\epsilon_{n,m}\) and constructed power-series predictors for the differential flow harmonics \(V_n(p_T)\) that include linear response to higher-order eccentricities and nonlinear terms. The optimal response coefficients \(\kappa(p_T)\) were obtained by minimizing residuals against full hydrodynamic simulations, and the resulting event-by-event predictions were compared to the full hydrodynamic results using Pearson correlation coefficients and principal component analysis.

## Key numbers

* Pearson correlation coefficient \(Q_2\) and \(Q_3\) between full hydrodynamic \(V_n(p_T)\) and predictions from eccentricities are consistently close to 1 for all predictors that include the leading eccentricity \(\epsilon_{n,n}\), with improvements visible when including additional eccentricities and nonlinear terms, especially for noncentral collisions.
* The analysis uses 7-term predictors for \(V_2\) and \(V_3\) that include four linear terms (with eccentricities \(\epsilon_{2,2}\), \(\epsilon_{2,4}\), \(\epsilon_{2,6}\), \(\epsilon_{2,8}\) for \(V_2\) and \(\epsilon_{3,3}\), \(\epsilon_{3,5}\), \(\epsilon_{3,7}\), \(\epsilon_{3,9}\) for \(V_3\)) and three nonlinear terms.
* Events are simulated for 40%-50% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.

## Context

This work extends the established mapping between initial-state geometry and anisotropic flow developed by Gardim et al. (2011, 2014) and Teaney et al. (2010, 2012) from \(p_T\)-integrated to \(p_T\)-differential flow harmonics. It builds on the principal component analysis of flow fluctuations first proposed by Bhalerao et al. (2014) and subsequently explored by Mazeliauskas et al. (2015), Bozek (2017), and others, and experimentally measured by the CMS Collaboration (2017). The results enable experimental extraction of information about small-scale structures in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions through measurements of subleading principal components of anisotropic flow, which are sensitive to corrections beyond the simple linear response to the leading eccentricity.
