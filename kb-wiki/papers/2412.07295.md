# Exploring $ Λ{\text-} $ and $ Ξ{\text -}$triton correlation functions in heavy-ion collisions

> The $ Λ{\text -} $ and $ Ξ{\text -}$triton(t) momentum correlation functions, to be measured in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, are explored. Mainly, STAR detector acquired data provides an opportunity to explore the $ Λt $ correlation function. The $ Λt $ correlation functions are calculated using an isle-type and spin-averaged $ Λt $ potential, also, its sensitivity to changes in potential strength has also been investigated. % Besides, even though there is no experimental data on the $ Ξ{\text -} $triton interaction yet, I constructed $Ξt$ potentials based on the first principles HAL QCD and Nijmegen extended soft-core (ESC08c) model of spin- and isospin averaged $ΞN$ interactions in single-folding potentials (SFP) approach. Then, the $Ξt$ correlation functions are calculated for these two modern potentials as well as for Nijmegen hard-core model D (NHC-D) $ΞN$ potential. The numerical results predict that, with good measurement resolution, it might be possible to recognize different potentials with a correlation function at relatively small source sizes, i.e., $ R = 1-3 $ fm.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that with good measurement resolution, it might be possible to distinguish different hyperon-nucleon interaction models by measuring Lambda-triton and Xi-triton momentum correlation functions at relatively small source sizes of R = 1-3 fm, though the author notes this is an exploratory study on a simple technical level and full-fledged high-precision approaches are needed for actual data analysis.

## Method
The author calculated Lambda-triton correlation functions using Kurihara's isle-type spin-averaged Lambda-triton potential, and constructed Xi-triton potentials via the single-folding potential approach using spin- and isospin averaged Xi-nucleon interactions from HAL QCD lattice calculations, Nijmegen extended soft-core (ESC08c) model, and Nijmegen hard-core model D (NHC-D). The correlation functions were computed using the Koonin-Pratt formula with a spherical static Gaussian source function for source sizes R = 1, 3, and 5 fm.

## Key numbers
- Lambda-triton potential U_Lambda-t gives scattering length a0 = 5.9 fm, effective range r0 = 2.3 fm, and binding energy B_Lambda = 1.27 MeV; the strengthened potential U_Lambda-t+ (20% increase) gives a0 = 4.6 fm, r0 = 2.1 fm, and B_Lambda = 2.57 MeV
- For Xi-triton system: NHC-D potential gives scattering length a0 = -114.0 fm, effective range r0 = 3.1 fm, and a Coulomb-assisted bound state at 0.14 MeV; ESC08c gives a0 = 64.2 fm, r0 = 1.8 fm, and a Coulomb-assisted bound state at 0.30 MeV
- HAL QCD potentials at t/a = 11, 12, 13 give scattering lengths a0 = -2.2, -1.6, -1.0 fm and effective ranges r0 = 6.1, 7.5, 11.5 fm respectively, with no bound states
- The experimental ground state energy of Lambda-4H is E = -2.04(4) MeV for the singlet state and E = -0.99(4) MeV for the triplet state
- New Lambda binding energy measurements for the 0+ state of Lambda-4H: 2.12(1)_stat(9)_syst MeV (Esser 2015), 2.157(5)_stat(77)_syst MeV (A1 Collaboration, Schulz 2016), and 2.22(6)_stat(14)_syst MeV (STAR Collaboration, Abdallah 2022)

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of hadron-deuteron correlation functions (such as pd, K-d, Lambda-d, Xi-d by Bazak 2020, Haidenbauer, Ogata 2021) and recent hyperon-alpha correlation studies (jinno 2024, kamiya 2024, etminan 2024) to hyperon-triton systems. The predictions are motivated by STAR detector measurements of Lambda binding energies in hypernuclei (Abdallah 2022) and femtoscopic evidence for attractive Xi-p interaction (PhysRevLett.123.112002). The results enable future experimental comparison with STAR data from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV, and the author notes that a full four-body scattering treatment of three nucleons and a hyperon is needed beyond this exploratory study.
