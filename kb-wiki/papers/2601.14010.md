# Frame Dependence in Generalized Chiral Kinetic Theory

> We investigate the frame dependence of distribution functions within the framework of generalized chiral kinetic theory. Based on the derived transformation rules governing the choice of frame, we analytically obtain the global equilibrium solution in the presence of vorticity and electromagnetic fields. Our results show that, under the assumption of a varying electromagnetic field, these equilibrium solutions can be uniquely determined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that global equilibrium solutions for distribution functions in generalized chiral kinetic theory can be uniquely determined only under the assumption of a varying electromagnetic field, while the constant-field case leaves the solutions underdetermined.

## Method

The authors derive transformation rules for the distribution functions defined with respect to an auxiliary timelike vector n^mu, which serves as the frame velocity, by requiring that the underlying Wigner functions J_s^mu and S^mu^nu (or their duals) remain independent of the choice of n^mu. Using these frame-dependence transformation rules, they analytically solve the generalized chiral kinetic equations (GCKEs) at zeroth and first order in hbar to obtain the Wigner functions in global equilibrium under vorticity and electromagnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The zeroth-order distribution functions are given by J_sn^(0) = (1/(4 pi^3)) [theta(p_0 - m)/(1 + exp(beta·p - bar mu)) + theta(-p_0 - m)/(1 + exp(-beta·p + bar mu))], with M^(0)mu_perp = 0, where beta^mu = u^mu/T and bar mu = mu/T
- The thermal vorticity Omega_mu_nu = (1/2)(partial_mu beta_nu - partial_nu beta_mu) is a constant tensor satisfying partial_rho Omega_mu_nu = 0 in global equilibrium
- The integrability condition for the electromagnetic field is F_mu_lambda Omega_nu^lambda - F_nu_lambda Omega_mu^lambda = -beta^lambda (partial_lambda F_mu_nu), with the right-hand side vanishing for constant fields

## Context

This work extends the generalized chiral kinetic theory (GCKT) developed by Ma (2022) for fermions of arbitrary mass by examining the frame-dependence transformation rules that were not addressed in that prior work. The paper resolves the ambiguity in global equilibrium solutions that led to differing results between Ma (2022) and Gao (2019), showing that unique determination requires varying electromagnetic fields. The results enable consistent application of GCKT to systems with spacetime-dependent electromagnetic fields, such as those encountered in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
