# An alternative scheme for effective range corrections in pionless EFT

> We discuss an alternative scheme for including effective range corrections in pionless effective field theory. The standard approach treats range terms as perturbative insertions in the T -matrix. In a finite volume this scheme can lead to singular behavior close to the unperturbed energies. We consider an alternative scheme that resums the effective range but expands the spurious pole of the T -matrix created by this resummation. We test this alternative expansion for several model potentials and observe good convergence.

## Key claim

The standard perturbative treatment of effective range corrections in pionless EFT, when applied in a finite volume, produces singular behavior near the unperturbed energies due to an infinite tower of poles in the finite-volume dimer propagator. The authors propose an alternative scheme that resums the effective range but expands only the spurious pole created by this resummation, thereby avoiding high powers of energy denominators while maintaining good convergence.

## Method

The authors split the two-body amplitude into two terms: one containing the physical dimer pole and one containing the spurious deep pole. The spurious pole term is then expanded in a Taylor series in the squared boosted relative momentum \(k^{*2}\) around zero, effectively removing the pole while keeping the physical pole intact. The modified Faddeev equation is rewritten with a new effective potential \(W\) that absorbs the expanded spurious pole contributions, and the authors verify analytically that \(W\) retains the structure of a low-energy polynomial plus one-particle exchange, with the difference from the original kernel \(Z\) absorbable into renormalization of the effective couplings. The method is tested numerically for several model potentials.

## Key numbers

- The spurious pole is located at momentum \(k_2 \simeq 2/r\), where \(r\) is the effective range, while the physical dimer pole is at \(k_1 \simeq 1/a\) where \(a\) is the scattering length.
- For the two-nucleon system, the spurious pole is at roughly 200 MeV, outside the range of validity of pionless EFT.
- The subtraction functions \(f_1(k^*)\), \(f_2(k^*)\), and \(f_3(k^*)\) count at orders \(O(p^2)\), \(O(p^4)\), and higher respectively for small momenta, where \(p\) is a typical low-energy scale.
- Using \(f_2(k^*)\) with one three-body coupling \(H_0\) yields \(\text{Re}\,W^{(2)} = O(p^2)\); using \(f_3(k^*)\) with two couplings \(H_0, H_2\) yields \(\text{Re}\,W^{(2)} = O(p^4)\).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of effective range corrections in pionless EFT, which was developed for three-nucleon systems by Bedaque (1998), Hammer (2001), Ji (2011), and Vanasse (2013). The standard perturbative approach of those works expands the effective range in the two-body amplitude, which works well in infinite volume but becomes numerically problematic in finite volume due to singular denominators near the finite-volume energy poles. The alternative scheme presented here is designed for use with the finite-volume quantization condition developed by Hammer (2017), enabling lattice QCD calculations of three-body systems with range corrections without the singular behavior of the standard expansion.
