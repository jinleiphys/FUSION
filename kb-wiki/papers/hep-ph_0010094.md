# Multiplicity of different hadrons in e+e-, pp, and AA collisions

> Employing the recently developed neXus model, we compare the yields of different hadrons in ultra-relativistic collisions: electron-positron annihilation at 91 GeV, proton-proton scattering at 17 GeV and nucleus-nucleus collisions at 17 GeV (SPS) and 200 GeV (RHIC). Plotting the yields as a function of the hadron masses, we find very surprising results: we observe that the spectra are practically identical for e+e- at 91 GeV and central nucleus-nucleus reactions at SPS and RHIC energies, whereas the spectrum for proton-proton scattering is somewhat steeper. All have the form one expects if the particles were emitted by a canonical system which is characterized by a temperature and chemical potentials. These identical forms have, however, different origins: in e+e- and pp the exponential shape it is due to the statistical behavior of string fragmentation, which has absolutely nothing to do with thermalization, in AA it is caused by phase space. The fact that e+e- and nuclear results agree is pure coincidence. Surprisingly the results for pp and e+e- differ, although here the production mechanism is identical. In pp collisions we see directly that the string energy is very limited and hence the high mass baryons are suppressed. We conclude that it is practically impossible to draw conclusions from hadronic yields about the reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The exponential mass-yield spectra of hadrons are practically identical for e+e- annihilation at 91 GeV and central nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS (17 GeV) and RHIC (200 GeV), but this agreement is pure coincidence because the production mechanisms are fundamentally different: string fragmentation in e+e- and phase-space decay of droplets in AA collisions.

## Method

The authors used the neXus model, a parton-based Gribov-Regge theory, to simulate hadron production in four systems: e+e- annihilation at 91 GeV, pp scattering at 17 GeV, central Pb-Pb collisions at 17 AGeV (SPS), and central Au-Au collisions at 200 AGeV (RHIC). For nucleus-nucleus collisions, primary interactions produced strings via multiple scattering, while secondary interactions were treated with a droplet model where high-energy-density regions decayed microcanonically via phase space when the energy density dropped below a critical value.

## Key numbers

- e+e- annihilation at sqrt(s) = 91 GeV: the decaying string almost always has the full energy of 91 GeV
- pp and AA reactions at sqrt(s) = 17 GeV: the distribution of string energies peaks at around 1 GeV and falls steeply toward the maximum of 17 GeV
- The threshold for multi-strange baryon production (Omega) is around 3 GeV
- The droplet decay used a freeze-out energy density of epsilon = 0.15 GeV
- The neXus results for Pb-Pb at 17 AGeV agree with extrapolated experimental data over four orders of magnitude in multiplicity

## Context

This work extends the neXus model comparisons previously reported by Drescher, Aichelin, and Werner (2000) to address the long-standing question of whether enhanced strange particle yields in heavy-ion collisions can serve as a "smoking gun" for quark-gluon plasma formation, as argued by Rafelski and Muller (1982). The authors show that the identical exponential mass-yield spectra in e+e- and AA collisions make it impossible to distinguish between string fragmentation and thermalized phase-space decay, contradicting the assumption that such spectra necessarily indicate thermalization. This result enables a more cautious interpretation of hadron yield data from RHIC and future heavy-ion experiments, emphasizing that the quest for the QGP requires more differential observables.
