# Many-Body Interactions of Neutrinos with Nuclei - Observables

> Background: The total inclusive cross sections obtained for quasielastic (QE) scattering in the Mini Booster Neutrino Experiment (MiniBooNE) are significantly larger than those calculated by all models based on the impulse approximation and using the world average value for the axial mass of $M_A \approx 1 \GeV$. This discrepancy has led to various, quite different explanations in terms of increased axial masses, changes in the functional form of the axial form factor, increased vector strength in nuclei, and initial two-particle interactions. This is disconcerting since the neutrino energy reconstruction depends on the reaction mechanism. Purpose: We investigate whether exclusive observables, such as nucleon knock-out, can be used to distinguish between the various proposed reaction mechanisms. We determine the influence of 2p-2h excitations on the energy reconstruction. Method: We use the Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model to predict numbers and spectra of knock-out nucleons. The model is extended by incorporating a simple, but realistic treatment of initial 2p-2h excitations. Results: We show numbers and spectra of knock-out nucleons and show their sensitivity to the presence of 2p-2h initial excitations. We also discuss the influence of 2p-2h excitations on the neutrino energy reconstruction. Conclusions: 2p-2h excitations do lead to an increase in the number $n$ of knock-out nucleons for $n \ge 2$ while only the $n=1$ knock-out remains a clean signal of true QE scattering. The spectra of knock-out nucleons do also change, but their qualitative shape remains as before. In the energy reconstruction 2p-2h interactions lead to a downward shift of the reconstructed energy; this effect of 2p-2h excitations disappears at higher energies because the 2p-2h influence is spread out over a wider energy range.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether exclusive observables, such as nucleon knock-out, can distinguish between the various proposed reaction mechanisms that explain the MiniBooNE quasielastic cross section excess, finding that 2p-2h excitations increase the number of knock-out nucleons for n ≥ 2 while only single-nucleon knock-out remains a clean signal of true quasielastic scattering.

## Method

The authors extended the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model by incorporating a simple but realistic treatment of initial 2p-2h excitations, using a physically motivated ansatz for the relevant matrix element that avoids the difficulties of calculating meson exchange currents and correlation effects. They calculated inclusive cross sections and numbers and spectra of knock-out nucleons, and analyzed the impact of 2p-2h processes on neutrino energy reconstruction. The model uses an axial mass of M_A = 1 GeV for quasielastic scattering and treats final-state interactions of all particles.

## Key numbers

- The world average axial mass parameter is M_A = 1.026 GeV, determined from fits to data from electro-pion production and older neutrino data on nucleon or deuterium targets
- MiniBooNE and K2K experiments required an increased axial mass of M_A = 1.23 GeV (or M_A = 1.35 GeV from a shape-only fit to dσ/dQ²) to fit their data in the Fermi-gas model
- Benhar et al. showed that a model based on state-of-the-art nucleon spectral functions required an even larger axial mass of M_A = 1.6 GeV for a fit of the differential data
- The MiniBooNE experiment gives a possible inaccuracy of only about 10% for the flux determination
- Explicit pion cross sections obtained by MiniBooNE are about a factor 1.5-2 larger than those obtained with the NUANCE event generator
- The NOMAD experiment working at significantly higher energies (between about 5 and 100 GeV) observed no such excess of measured over expected quasielastic cross section

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the MiniBooNE quasielastic cross section excess, which has been explained by various mutually exclusive mechanisms including increased axial masses (MiniBooNE Collaboration 2007), changes in the axial form factor (Bhattacharya 2011), increased vector strength in nuclei (Bodek 2011), initial two-particle interactions (Martini 2009, Nieves 2011), and final-state interactions (Meucci 2011). The paper enables future experimental discrimination between these mechanisms through exclusive measurements of knock-out nucleons, and provides a framework for implementing 2p-2h effects in event generators for neutrino oscillation experiments.
