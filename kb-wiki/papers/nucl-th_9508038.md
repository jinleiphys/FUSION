# Exact methods for Campi plots

> We introduce for canonical fragmention models an exact method for computing expectation values which exclude the largest cluster. This method allows for the computation of the reduced multiplicity and other quantities of interest introduced by Campi, and a comparison shows that the percolation model and a recent canonical model differ mostly only in small respects in these ensemble averages.

# Exact methods for Campi plots

## Key claim

The paper introduces an exact computational method for canonical fragmentation models that avoids Monte Carlo sampling, and shows that a Gibbs model with parameter τ = 2.5 produces Campi plots that duplicate percolation model results in many respects, contradicting earlier claims that the two models had distinctly different behavior. The authors hedge that the reduced variance may not reliably distinguish percolation from a simple Gibbs model, and that a different method is needed to distinguish these models.

## Method

The authors exploit recursive properties of the microcanonical partition function Z_A^(m) for Gibbs-weighted fragmentation models, where the weight of a fragmentation outcome is proportional to ∏ x_k^(n_k)/n_k!. They compute expectation values conditioned on a fixed largest cluster size k_max by taking differences of partition functions with truncated parameter sets, specifically ΔZ_A^(m)(k_max) = Z_A^(m)(x_1,...,x_k_max,0,...,0) - Z_A^(m)(x_1,...,x_k_max-1,0,...,0). This allows exact calculation of Pr(k_max), ⟨n_k⟩(k_max), and the reduced variance ⟨γ₂⟩ without statistical errors.

## Key numbers

- The Gibbs model with τ = 2.5 and the percolation model have the same height and location of the peak of the reduced variance ⟨γ₂⟩ when plotted against (m-1)/A.
- The only significant difference between the two models is the width of the peak, which is larger in the Gibbs model than in the percolation model.
- The scaling behavior of the position, width, and height of the peak with changing system size A also agrees between the two models.

## Context

This work extends the Campi plot method introduced by Campi and Krivine (1988, 1992) for distinguishing fragmentation models, who had previously shown that a Gibbs model with τ = 1.0 had distinctly different behavior from percolation. The authors show that a different parameter choice (τ = 2.5) produces results very similar to percolation, contradicting the earlier conclusion. The exact method enables computation without statistical errors and can be applied to other models such as the equipartitioning model of Sobotka and Moretto (1985). The technique opens the possibility of using these exact calculations to further study critical exponents and condensation phenomena in nuclear fragmentation.
