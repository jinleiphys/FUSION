# Coherent pi0-pi0 photoproduction on lightest nuclei

> Coherent photoproduction of two neutral pions on the deuteron and ^3He is calculated. The isoscalar and isovector parts of the elementary photoproduction amplitude was determined by fitting the measured total cross section on protons and neutrons. The dependence of the cross section on the isospin of the target nucleus is discussed.

## Key claim

The cross section for coherent \(\pi^0\pi^0\) photoproduction on light nuclei is predicted to depend strongly on the target isospin, with the deuteron (isospin 0) cross section being extremely small because the isoscalar part of the elementary amplitude is only about 8% of the proton amplitude, while the \(^3\)He (isospin 1/2) cross section is roughly 45 times larger.

## Method

The authors used an impulse approximation, summing single-nucleon photoproduction operators \(\hat{t}_{\gamma N}\) over all nucleons in the target. The elementary amplitude for \(\gamma p \to \pi^0\pi^0 p\) was taken from a phenomenological fit (KaFiPra) that includes a \(D_{13}(1520)\) resonance and other partial waves; the neutron amplitude was derived by fixing the isoscalar/isovector ratio \(\alpha = t_0/t_{\gamma p} = 0.08\) using data for \(\gamma n \to \pi^0\pi^0 n\) from Ajaka (2008). The nuclear cross sections were then computed for the deuteron and \(^3\)He using phenomenological wave functions from Mach (1986) and Nisk (1990), respectively.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar part \(t_0\) of the \(\pi^0\pi^0\) photoproduction amplitude is only 8% of the proton amplitude \(t_{\gamma p}\) (i.e., \(\alpha = 0.08\)).
- The ratio of cross sections \(\sigma_{\gamma ^3\mathrm{He}} / \sigma_{\gamma d}\) is about 46 at \(E_\gamma = 450\) MeV, 31 at \(E_\gamma = 550\) MeV, and 13 at \(E_\gamma = 700\) MeV.
- The cross section for \(\gamma ^3\mathrm{He} \to \pi^0\pi^0\,^3\mathrm{He}\) reaches an appreciable value of about 0.4 \(\mu\)b.
- For \(^7\)Li, the ratio \(R\) of the cross section to that on \(^3\)He is estimated as 0.41 at \(E_\gamma = 400\) MeV, decreasing to 0.25 at \(E_\gamma = 750\) MeV.
- The off-shell ambiguity in fixing the \(\gamma N\) invariant energy leads to a difference of up to 4 MeV in the final nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of coherent \(\pi^0\eta\) photoproduction (Egorov, 2013) to the \(\pi^0\pi^0\) channel. The predicted strong isospin dependence mirrors that seen in \(\eta\) photoproduction in the \(S_{11}(1535)\) region, where the isoscalar part is also small (\(\alpha \approx 0.1\)) according to Krusche (1995) and Fix (1997). The results suggest that coherent \(\pi^0\pi^0\) photoproduction on \(^3\)He could serve as a clean probe of the spin-isospin structure of the elementary \(\gamma N \to \pi^0\pi^0 N\) amplitude, and that the deuteron cross section is so small that inclusive measurements on the deuteron can effectively isolate the neutron cross section. The authors note that better data for \(\pi^0\pi^0\) photoproduction on quasifree neutrons (with improved statistics over Ajaka (2008)) are needed to reduce the uncertainty in the isotopic structure.
