# Interaction between antiprotonic helium ion and He atom: Potential Energy Surface

> Potential Energy Surface for the ($\mathrm{\bar{p}} - \mathrm{He}^{2+} - \mathrm{He}$) system is calculated in the framework of the restricted (singlet spin state) Hartree-Fock method with subsequent account of the electronic correlations within the second order perturbation method (MP2). The geometry of heavy particles is described in variables of distance $r$ from nucleus $a$ to antiproton, distance $R$ from the center of mass of the $(\bar{p} - a)$ pair to $\mathrm{He}$ atom containing nucleus $b$, and an angle $θ$ between $\mathbf{r}$ and $\mathbf{R}$. The potential $V(R,r,\cosθ)$ of the interaction between $\mathrm{He}$ atom and a $\bar{p} - a$ subsystem involves a total energy of two electrons in the field of three heavy particles and Coulomb interactions of the $b$ nucleus with antiproton and the $a$ nucleus. The expansion of this potential in terms of Legendre polynomials $\mathrm{P}_k(\cosθ)$ is obtained. Matrices of the multipole terms $V^k(r,R)$ ($k=0,1,2$) are obtained in the basis of antiprotonic helium ion states. The results are compared with the model potential that was used earlier in the calculations of collisional Stark transitions of $(\mathrm{\bar{p}He}^{2+})$ ion.Total cross sections of collisional Stark transitions obtained with the PES potentials exceed the model results by 15 - 20\% at $E \leq 12$ K.

# Interaction between antiprotonic helium ion and He atom: Potential Energy Surface

## Key claim
The *ab initio* potential energy surface (PES) for the (antiproton - He²⁺ - He) system yields total cross sections of collisional Stark transitions that exceed the earlier model-potential results by 15–20% at collision energies E ≤ 12 K, but the qualitative shape of the PES multipole terms remains similar to the model.

## Method
The PES was calculated using the restricted (singlet spin state) Hartree-Fock method with electronic correlations included via second-order Møller-Plesset perturbation theory (MP2). The geometry was described by Jacobi coordinates: distance r from the He²⁺ nucleus to the antiproton, distance R from the center of mass of the (antiproton - He²⁺) pair to the He atom, and angle θ between r and R. The potential V(R, r, cosθ) was computed on a regular grid of 42,336 configurations (r = 0.1–0.9 a.u., R = 0.5–10 a.u., cosθ = –1 to +1) using the aug-cc-pV6Z basis set, and expanded in Legendre polynomials up to k = 2. Matrix elements of the multipole terms V^k(r,R) were evaluated in the basis of antiprotonic helium ion hydrogen-like wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Total cross sections of collisional Stark transitions from the state n=30, l=29 obtained with the PES potentials exceed the model results by 15–20% at E ≤ 12 K.
- The PES was calculated on a grid of 42,336 configurations: r from 0.1 to 0.9 a.u. (step 0.04 a.u.), R from 0.5 to 10 a.u. (step 0.1 a.u.), cosθ from –1 to +1 (step 0.1).
- The model potential parameters used for comparison are: polarizability α = 1.383 a.u., well depth D₀ = 0.075 a.u., equilibrium distance Rₑ = 1.46 a.u., Morse exponent β = 1.65 a.u., cutoff parameter γ = 0.005 a.u.
- The observed effective quenching cross sections for antiprotonic helium ions in states n=28–32, l=n-1 are σ_q ∼ (4–10) × 10⁻¹⁵ cm² at T ∼ 10 K.
- The calculations used masses: m_antiproton = 1.0073 a.m.u., M_⁴He = 4.0015 a.m.u., M_³He = 3.0149 a.m.u.

## Context
This work extends earlier model-potential calculations of collisional Stark transitions for (antiprotonic He²⁺) ions by Korenman and collaborators (2013–2014), which used a Morse-plus-polarization potential V₀(R) with dipole and quadrupole terms derived from it. The model gave cross sections of order σ_q but insufficient to fully explain the observed annihilation rates from ASACUSA experiments at CERN. The present *ab initio* PES provides deeper potential wells and quantum-number-dependent monopole terms, enabling more accurate close-coupling calculations of Stark transitions and annihilation rates at low temperatures (∼10 K). The results are directly applicable to interpreting the density-dependent lifetimes and isotope effects observed for antiprotonic helium ions with ³He and ⁴He.
