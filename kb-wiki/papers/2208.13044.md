# Addressing energy density functionals in the language of path-integrals I: Comparative study of diagrammatic techniques applied to the (0+0)-D $O(N)$-symmetric $φ^{4}$-theory

> The energy density functional (EDF) method is currently the only microscopic theoretical approach able to tackle the entire nuclear chart. Nevertheless, it suffers from limitations resulting from its empirical character and deteriorating its reliability. This paper is part of a larger program that aims at formulating the EDF approach as an effective field theory (EFT) in order to overcome these limitations. A relevant framework to achieve this is the path-integral (PI) formulation of quantum field theory (QFT). The latter indeed provides a wide variety of treatments of the many-body problem well suited to deal with non-perturbative interactions and to exploit a Lagrangian resulting from an EFT as a starting point. While developing the formalism in a general setting, we present a comparative study of such techniques applied to a toy model, i.e. the (0+0)-D $O(N)$-symmetric $φ^{4}$-theory. More specifically, our focus will be on the following diagrammatic techniques: loop expansion (LE), optimized perturbation theory (OPT) and self-consistent perturbation theory (SCPT). With these methods, we notably address the spontaneous breakdown of the $O(N)$ symmetry with care especially since spontaneous symmetry breakings (SSBs) play a paramount role in current implementations of the EDF approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that path-integral diagrammatic techniques (loop expansion, optimized perturbation theory, and self-consistent perturbation theory) can be systematically applied to a toy (0+0)-dimensional O(N)-symmetric φ⁴ model as a benchmark for developing an effective field theory formulation of nuclear energy density functionals, but the authors caution that the performance of these methods may differ when applied to realistic nuclear interactions with richer symmetry patterns and competing channels.

## Method

The authors applied three diagrammatic techniques, loop expansion (LE), optimized perturbation theory (OPT), and self-consistent perturbation theory (SCPT), to the (0+0)-dimensional O(N)-symmetric φ⁴ theory, using the path-integral formalism. They first rewrote the partition function via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations to obtain original, mixed, and collective representations of the model, then computed the ground-state energy and density for each technique and compared them to the exact analytical solution expressed in terms of Kummer confluent hypergeometric functions. The study also employed resummation methods (Padé-Borel, conformal mapping, and Borel-hypergeometric) to handle asymptotic series arising from LE and SCPT.

## Key numbers

- Exact ground-state energy: E_gs^exact = -ln(Ω_N R_{N-1}) where Ω_N = 2π^(N/2)/Γ(N/2) and R_N is defined by an integral involving the Kummer confluent hypergeometric function _1F_1
- Exact ground-state density: ρ_gs^exact = R_{N+1}/(N R_{N-1})
- For the classical potential minima: when m² < 0 and λ ≠ 0, the degenerate vacua satisfy φ² = -3! m²/λ > 0, with O(N) symmetry spontaneously broken to O(N-1)
- The exact 1-point correlation function φ̅^exact = 0 for all values of λ and m², confirming no spontaneous symmetry breaking in the full quantum theory

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Furnstahl and collaborators (2003-2020) who exploited the path-integral formalism and the 2-particle-point-irreducible effective action framework to connect nuclear EDFs with effective field theories of QCD. The comparative study of diagrammatic techniques on a solvable toy model provides a benchmark for understanding how collective degrees of freedom capture correlations, serving as an intermediary step toward more realistic applications with chiral EFT interactions and three-nucleon forces.
