# $Λ_c N$ bound states revisited

> The one-boson exchange potential model for $Λ_cN$ is constructed and the possibility of $Λ_cN$ bound states is examined. We consider an effective Lagrangian for the charmed baryons, $Λ_c$, $Σ_c$, and $Σ_c^*$, reflecting the heavy quark symmetry, chiral symmetry, and hidden local symmetry. We determine the coupling constants using various methods. With the derived nonrelativistic potentials, we study the bound state problem of the $Λ_cN$ system and relevant coupled channel effects. It is found that molecular bound states of $Λ_cN$ are plausible, for which the channel couplings of $Σ_cN$ and $Σ_c^*N$ are essential.

## Key claim

Molecular bound states of $\Lambda_c N$ are plausible, but only when channel couplings to $\Sigma_c N$ and $\Sigma_c^* N$ are included; the one-pion-exchange interaction alone in the $\Lambda_c N$ channel is forbidden, so scalar and vector meson exchanges are required, and the binding is sensitive to the cutoff parameter.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-boson-exchange potential model for the $\Lambda_c N$ system using an effective Lagrangian for charmed baryons ($\Lambda_c$, $\Sigma_c$, $\Sigma_c^*$) that respects heavy quark symmetry, chiral symmetry, and hidden local symmetry. Coupling constants were determined from strong decay widths, quark model estimates, vector meson dominance, chiral multiplet assumptions, and QCD sum rules. Nonrelativistic potentials were derived and the bound state problem was solved variationally for $J^P=0^+$ and $J^P=1^+$ states, including coupled channels $\Sigma_c N$ and $\Sigma_c^* N$ with a monopole form factor cutoff $\Lambda$ around 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- $|g_2| = 0.598$ from $\Sigma_c \to \Lambda_c \pi$ decay width; $|g_4| = 0.999$ from $\Sigma_c^* \to \Lambda_c \pi$ decay width.
- Quark model gives $g_1 = 1.00$, $g_2 = -0.61$, $g_4 = 1.06$, $g_A = 1.25$ using $g_A^q = 0.75$.
- Scalar meson coupling $\ell_B = -3.1$ from chiral multiplet estimate using $\Delta M_{\bar 3} \approx 573$ MeV and $f_\pi = 92.3$ MeV.
- Vector meson couplings: $(\beta_B g_V) = -6.0$, $(\beta_S g_V) = 12.0$, $(\lambda_S g_V) = 19.2$ GeV$^{-1}$, $(\lambda_I g_V) = -6.8$ GeV$^{-1}$ from quark model with $g_\rho^q = 3.0$, $f_\rho^q = 0.0$, $m_q = 313$ MeV.
- Hadron masses used: $m_{\Lambda_c} = 2286.46$ MeV, $m_{\Sigma_c} = 2453.56$ MeV, $m_{\Sigma_c^*} = 2517.97$ MeV, $m_\sigma = 600$ MeV, $m_\pi = 137.27$ MeV, $m_\rho = 775.49$ MeV, $m_\omega = 782.65$ MeV.

## Context

This work revisits the $\Lambda_c N$ bound state problem first studied by Dover (1977) using SU(4) symmetry, now applying modern heavy quark effective theory and hidden local symmetry. It extends earlier studies of charmed hypernuclei by Tsushima (2003) and heavy-quark deuteronlike states by Fromel (2005) and Diaz (2005), but uses a dedicated hadron-level potential rather than rescaled $NN$ interactions. The results enable experimental searches for charmed hypernuclei at facilities such as GSI-FAIR and J-PARC, and can be extended to bottom baryon systems.
