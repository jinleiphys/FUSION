# Phenomenological model for the Drell-Yan process: Reexamined

> Drell-Yan pair production is investigated. We reexamine a model where the quark momentum fraction is defined as the ratio of the corresponding light cone components of the quark and parent nucleon in a naive parton-model approach. It is shown that results differ from the standard parton model. This is due to unphysical solutions for the momentum fractions within the naive approach which are not present in the standard parton model. In a calculation employing full quark kinematics, i.e. including primordial quark transverse momentum, these solutions also appear. A prescription is given to handle these solutions in order to avoid misleading results. The impact of these solutions in the full kinematical approach is demonstrated and compared to the modified result.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a naive parton-model approach defining quark momentum fractions via light-cone components produces unphysical solutions (where partons move opposite to their parent nucleon) that artificially enhance the Drell-Yan cross section; these solutions must be explicitly removed to recover the standard parton-model result, and the same problem persists when including primordial transverse momentum.

## Method
The authors compare the standard collinear parton model with a "naive" approach that defines momentum fractions as ratios of light-cone components (x₁ = p₁⁻/P₁⁻, x₂ = p₂⁺/P₂⁺), showing that the on-shell conditions admit two solution sets, one physical and one unphysical. They then extend this analysis to full kinematics including transverse momentum distributions, using a Gaussian ansatz for the transverse momentum dependence of parton distribution functions, and derive the cross section by solving the kinematic constraints in terms of q and k variables to identify which solution branch corresponds to the physical standard parton model.

## Key numbers
- The standard parton model result for the double-differential cross section is dσ/(dM² dx_F) = Σ_i q_i² f_i(x₁₊,M²) f_ī(x₂₊,M²) × (4πα²/(9M²)) × (q_z)_max/(S E_coll), where α ≈ 1/137.
- The unphysical collinear solution gives x₁ = 0, x₂ = 0 with x̃₁ = x̃₂ = M/√S for x_F = 0, corresponding to partons moving opposite to their parent nucleons.
- The Gaussian transverse momentum distribution has width parameter D related to average squared transverse momentum by ⟨(p_i⊥)²⟩ = 4D².
- The calculation is performed at hadron c.m. energies √S > 27 GeV, where nucleon mass effects are negligible.

## Context
This work reexamines the phenomenological model of Linnyk et al. (2004, 2005, 2006) for Drell-Yan production with full quark kinematics, showing that those works obtained incorrect results by not removing unphysical solutions for the momentum fractions. The paper extends the standard collinear parton model (Drell and Yan, 1970) and transverse-momentum-dependent approaches (D'Alesio, 2004) by providing a prescription to properly handle kinematic constraints. This enables correct calculations of Drell-Yan observables at moderate energies (e.g., at PANDA/FAIR) where non-perturbative effects are important, and clarifies the connection between transverse-momentum-dependent factorization and the Bjorken limit.
