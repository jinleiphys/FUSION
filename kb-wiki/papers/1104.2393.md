# Photo- and electroproduction of the K^0Lambda near threshold and effects of the K^0 electromagnetic form factor

> By extending our previous isobar model to the K^0Lambda isospin channel we investigate the properties of the K^0Lambda photo- and electroproduction at energies near threshold. It is found that the pseudovector (PV) coupling yields significantly larger cross section. Variation of the K_1 coupling constants has significant effect only on the PV model. The electromagnetic form factor of the neutral kaon K^0 is found to have a sizable effect on the longitudinal cross section of the K^0Lambda electroproduction near the threshold.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous isobar model for $K^+\Lambda$ production to the $K^0\Lambda$ isospin channel, finding that the pseudovector (PV) coupling yields significantly larger cross sections than the pseudoscalar (PS) coupling, and that the $K^0$ electromagnetic form factor has a sizable effect on the longitudinal cross section of $K^0\Lambda$ electroproduction near threshold, with the caveat that the effect is model-dependent and requires experimental verification.

## Method

The authors used SU(3) symmetry to relate hadronic coupling constants of background terms between the $K^+\Lambda$ and $K^0\Lambda$ channels, replacing proton helicity photon couplings with neutron couplings from the Particle Data Group. For electroproduction, the scalar photon coupling of the $S_{11}(1650)$ resonance was taken from the MAID2007 model, and two relativistic quark models (light-cone quark model and quark-meson vertex model) were employed to compute the $K^0$ electromagnetic form factor effects on separated differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Neutron helicity photon coupling $A_{1/2}^n = -0.015 \pm 0.021$ GeV$^{-1/2}$, compared to proton coupling $A_{1/2}^p = 0.053 \pm 0.016$ GeV$^{-1/2}$
- Ratio of neutral to charged $K^*$ transition moments: $g_{K^{*0} K^{0} \gamma}/g_{K^{*+} K^{+} \gamma} = -1.53 \pm 0.20$
- Ratio of neutral to charged $K_1$ couplings: $r_{K_1K\gamma} = -0.45$
- Scalar photon coupling of $S_{11}(1650)$ resonance: $S_{1/2}^n = 0.010$ GeV$^{-1/2}$
- Contribution of $S_{11}(1650)$ resonance to $K^0\Lambda$ total cross section at $W=50$ MeV above threshold is only about 3%
- At $Q^2=0.65$ GeV$^2$, the LCQ model effect on the interference cross section $\sigma_{LT}$ is around 30%
- At forward angle $\theta_K^{\rm c.m.}=25.84^\circ$ and $Q^2\approx 0.5$ GeV$^2$, the LCQ form factor raises the longitudinal cross section up to 50%

## Context

This work extends the author's previous isobar model for $K^+\Lambda$ production (Mart 2010) to the $K^0\Lambda$ channel, addressing a discrepancy where the Kaon-Maid model predicted a total cross section twice as large for $K^0\Lambda$ compared to $K^+\Lambda$, contrary to other isospin channels. The results corroborate an earlier finding (Mart 1997) that the $K^0$ charge form factor significantly affects the longitudinal cross section, but with reduced model dependence by focusing on energies very close to threshold. The work enables experimental tests of the $K^0$ electromagnetic form factor using electroproduction on neutrons, with the authors noting that data with about 10% uncertainties could distinguish between the light-cone quark and quark-meson vertex models.
