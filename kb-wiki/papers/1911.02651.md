# From Parton Saturation to Proton Spin: the Impact of BFKL Equation and Reggeon Evolution

> Lev Lipatov was a giant in the field of strong interactions and a dominant force in high-energy QCD for many decades. His work deeply influenced both how we think about QCD and how we perform calculations in the theory. Below we describe the work in two related research directions: the physics of parton saturation and proton spin at small $x$. Both developments would have been impossible without Lipatov's groundbreaking work. Saturation physics would not have happened without the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) equation. The recent progress in our theoretical understanding of the proton spin contribution coming from small-$x$ partons started with the seminal paper by Kirschner and Lipatov resumming double logarithms of energy in the Reggeon evolution.

# From Parton Saturation to Proton Spin: the Impact of BFKL Equation and Reggeon Evolution

## Key claim

The BFKL equation, which predicts cross sections growing as a positive power of center-of-mass energy squared \(s\) and thus violating the Froissart–Martin unitarity bound, must be unitarized by parton saturation effects; the Gribov–Glauber–Mueller (GGM) formula and the Balitsky–Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation provide a controlled framework in which multiple rescatterings restore unitarity while keeping the coupling weak, provided the saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) is perturbatively large.

## Method

The paper derives the dipole picture of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small Bjorken \(x\), expressing the cross section as a convolution of the virtual photon light-cone wave functions with the dipole–target scattering amplitude. For a large nucleus, multiple rescatterings of the quark–antiquark dipole on nucleons are resummed via the GGM formula, exponentiating the leading-order two-gluon exchange amplitude. To include radiative corrections enhanced by \(\alpha_s \ln(1/x)\), the Mueller dipole model at large \(N_c\) is employed, leading to the BK evolution equation for the dipole \(S\)-matrix, which resums powers of \(\alpha_s \ln(1/x)\) to all orders.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the quark-loop diagram to the handbag diagram in DIS scales as \(\alpha_s / x\); for \(x < 0.01\) the \(1/x\) enhancement overcomes the \(\alpha_s\) suppression.
- The saturation scale scales with atomic number as \(Q_s^2 \sim A^{1/3}\), making it perturbatively large for sufficiently heavy nuclei.
- The black disk limit for the total dipole–nucleus cross section is \(\sigma_{tot}^{q\bar{q}A} \le 2\pi R^2\), leading to the Froissart–Martin bound \(\sigma_{tot}^{q\bar{q}A} \le 2\pi a^2 \ln^2(s/s_0)\) with \(a \sim 1/m_\pi\).
- The leading-order forward dipole amplitude on a single nucleon is \(N_{LO} = (\pi \alpha_s^2 C_F / N_c) \, T(\vec{b}_\perp) \, x_\perp^2 \ln(1/(x_\perp \Lambda))\).
- The GGM resummation parameter is \(\alpha_s^2 A^{1/3}\); when \(\alpha_s^2 A^{1/3} \gtrsim 1\) multiple rescatterings must be resummed.

## Context

This work extends the seminal BFKL equation of Kuraev, Fadin, Balitsky, and Lipatov (1977–1978) and the unitarity-violation problem it raised, which was first addressed by Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1984) who proposed parton saturation. The GGM formula (Mueller, 1989) provides the quasi-classical saturation mechanism for a large nucleus, equivalent to the McLerran–Venugopalan model (1993–1994). The BK equation (Balitsky, 1995–1998; Kovchegov, 1999) then incorporates small-\(x\) evolution to all orders in \(\alpha_s \ln(1/x)\) at large \(N_c\), enabling a systematic study of saturation dynamics and unitarization of QCD cross sections at high energy.
