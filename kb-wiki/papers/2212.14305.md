# Ridge regression for minimizing the couplings of hyperon resonances in the $K^+ Λ$ photoproduction

> We employed the isobar model for investigating the $K^+Λ$ photoproduction process. We paid special attention to the recent CLAS polarization data and enhanced the $χ^2$ minimization by adding a penalty term. Without changing the set of included resonances used by the model, this technique known as Ridge regression leads to reduced couplings that in previous studies acquired unreasonably large values. As a result, we have arrived at a much more robust model with hyperon couplings which are reduced to more physical values. This model serves us to extract valuable information on the background to the $K^+Λ$ photoproduction and particularly on the role of various hyperon resonances. The set of the nucleon resonances is the same with respect to previous fits but their role may have changed due to different couplings which they acquire in the present fit.

## Key claim
Ridge regression (L2 regularization) applied to the isobar model for $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction reduces hyperon resonance couplings from unphysically large values to more physical ones without changing the set of included resonances, but the authors note that the set of nucleon resonances remains the same while their role may change due to different couplings acquired in the regularized fit.

## Method
The authors employed an isobar model with tree-level Feynman diagrams, including Born terms, $t$-channel kaon exchanges ($K^*$ and $K_1$), $u$-channel hyperon resonances, and $s$-channel nucleon resonances up to approximately 2.2 GeV. They added a penalty term $\lambda^4 \sum g_j^2$ (Ridge regression) to the standard $\chi^2$ minimization, applied only to the hyperon resonance couplings, and used 3-fold cross-validation with the "one standard-error rule" to select the optimal regularization parameter $\lambda$. The model was fitted to 4640 datapoints including differential cross sections from CLAS and LEPS, hyperon polarization $P$ data, and new CLAS polarization data on $\Sigma$, $T$, $O_{x^\prime}$, and $O_{z^\prime}$.

## Key numbers
- The optimal regularization parameter for the BS2r fit was $\tilde{\lambda} = 0.27$, determined by 3-fold cross-validation with the one-standard-error rule.
- The optimal regularization parameter for the BS2Mr fit was $\tilde{\lambda} = 0.13$, determined by the same procedure.
- The spin-3/2 hyperon resonance $\Sigma(1940)$ couplings changed from $g_1 = -0.86$ and $g_2 = 0.18$ in the unregularized fit to $g_1 = -0.69$ and $g_2 = 0.079$ after Ridge regularization.
- The $\chi^2$ error in the BS2r regularized fit increased by approximately 9% compared to its unregularized version (BS2r0).
- The experimental data used spanned center-of-mass energies from $W = 1.71$ GeV to $W = 2.355$ GeV, with 4640 total datapoints.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous BS1 and BS2 models (Petrellis and Skoupil, 2016) which suffered from hyperon resonance couplings exceeding 10 in absolute value, a long-standing issue also present in earlier models such as Saclay-Lyon (1990s), Williams-Ji-Cotanch (1990s), and Janssen et al. (2001). The Ridge regression technique provides a less arbitrary alternative to imposing manual constraints on coupling values during fitting. The resulting model enables extraction of information on the background contributions to $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction and the role of various hyperon resonances, which could inform future coupled-channel analyses by groups such as Bonn-Gatchina, Juelich-Bonn, and ANL-Osaka.
