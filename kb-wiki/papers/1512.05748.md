# Measurement of nuclear effects in neutrino interactions with minimal dependence on neutrino energy

> We present a phenomenological study of nuclear effects in neutrino charged-current interactions, using transverse kinematic imbalances in exclusive measurements. Novel observables with minimal dependence on neutrino energy are proposed to study quasielastic scattering, and especially resonance production. They should be able to provide direct constraints on nuclear effects in neutrino- and antineutrino-nucleus interactions.

# Measurement of nuclear effects in neutrino interactions with minimal dependence on neutrino energy

## Key claim
Transverse kinematic imbalance observables, particularly the single-transverse variables δpT and δαT, can provide measurements of nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus charged-current interactions that are largely independent of the incident neutrino energy, provided the neutrino energy is above the saturation scale of approximately 0.5 GeV. The paper proposes these observables for studying both quasielastic scattering and, for the first time, resonance production, but the minimal energy dependence is a property that holds only after kinematic saturation is reached and is subject to second-order effects from polarization, Pauli blocking, and transverse projection.

## Method
The authors performed a phenomenological study using Monte Carlo simulations with the NuWro (version 11q) and GENIE (version 2.10.0 with hA FSI model) event generators to predict distributions of single-transverse kinematic imbalance variables (δpT, δαT, δφT) for neutrino charged-current interactions on carbon targets. They compared predictions across different nuclear state models (relativistic Fermi gas, Bodek-Ritchie modified RFG, local Fermi gas, and spectral function) and examined the effects of final-state interactions by switching them on and off in the simulations, using the NuMI on-axis neutrino flux for energy distributions.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale for minimal neutrino energy dependence is approximately 0.5 GeV (Q²/2mN ∼ O(0.5 GeV)), above which the charged lepton retains most of the increase in neutrino energy.
- Fermi momentum pF for bound nucleons ranges up to approximately 200 MeV/c.
- In the nominal GENIE simulation for quasielastic scattering on carbon at NuMI beam energy, events with protons that undergo elastic final-state interactions amount to about 40% of the sample.
- The low-qtm region where collinear enhancement in δαT appears is below approximately 200 MeV/c at NuMI energy.
- The axial mass MA^QE values compared in the study are 0.8, 1.2 (nominal), and 1.6 GeV, showing that transverse kinematic imbalances are much less sensitive to this variation than the final-state proton momentum.

## Context
This work extends the use of transverse momentum imbalance, previously employed by the NOMAD experiment (Lyubushkin, 2008) to enhance quasielastic event purity, by introducing the transverse boosting angle δαT as a new observable for the first time. The study is motivated by the need to understand nuclear effects in neutrino interactions for oscillation experiments such as T2K (Abe, 2011) and MINERνA (Aliaga, 2013), as well as future experiments like DUNE (Adams, 2013). The technique enables direct comparison of nuclear effects across experiments with different neutrino energy spectra and can be extended to electron-nucleus scattering, where the well-known beam energy allows additional study of energy imbalance. The paper identifies discrepancies between NuWro and GENIE predictions that motivated the GENIE Collaboration to investigate elastic interaction effects in their hA FSI model.
