# Modification of nuclear transitions in stellar plasma by electronic processes: K-isomers in 176Lu and 180Ta under s-process conditions

> The influence of the stellar plasma on the production and destruction of K-isomers is studied for the examples 176Lu and 180Ta. Individual electromagnetic transitions are enhanced predominantly by nuclear excitation by electron capture, whereas the other mechanisms of electron scattering and nuclear excitation by electron transition give only minor contributions. It is found that individual transitions can be enhanced significantly for low transition energies below 100 keV. Transitions with higher energies above 200 keV are practically not affected. Although one low-energy transition in 180Ta is enhanced by up to a factor of 10, the stellar transition rates from low-K to high-K states via so-called intermediate states in 176Lu and 180Ta do not change significantly under s-process conditions. The s-process nucleosynthesis of 176Lu and 180Ta remains essentially unchanged.

## Key claim

Although individual low-energy transitions (below 100 keV) can be enhanced by up to a factor of 10 via nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC), the stellar transition rates from low-K to high-K states through intermediate states in 176Lu and 180Ta do not change significantly under s-process conditions, and the s-process nucleosynthesis of both nuclei remains essentially unchanged.

## Method

The authors calculated temperature-dependent enhancement factors for individual electromagnetic transitions in 176Lu and 180Ta by summing contributions from gamma radiation, internal conversion (IC) modified for partial ionization, nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC), and electron scattering. They applied these calculations to the experimentally confirmed intermediate state (IMS) at 839 keV in 176Lu and its decays to low-K and high-K levels, and to the candidate IMS at 594 keV in 180Ta, using a plasma density of 1000 g/cm³ and temperatures from 1 keV to 1 MeV typical of s-process conditions in thermally pulsing AGB stars.

## Key numbers

- The 72 keV M1 transition in 180Ta from the 5+ IMS at 594 keV to the low-K 4+ level at 520 keV shows NEEC enhancement up to a factor of 10 relative to the radiative rate at temperatures below 10 keV.
- For 176Lu, NEEC from low-K levels to the 5-;4 IMS at 839 keV accounts for a global excitation rate increase by a factor around 1.6 at temperatures below 10 keV.
- The 116 keV transition from the 5-;4 IMS at 839 keV in 176Lu shows NEEC enhancement of only a few percent against radiative excitation.
- The 237 keV E2 transition from the 5+ IMS to the 7+ high-K state at 357 keV in 180Ta shows very small enhancement, with electron inelastic scattering contributing at most 10% of the radiative rate.
- The stellar transition rate enhancement for 180Ta reaches at best 20% at the lowest temperatures, while for 176Lu the enhancement at temperatures below 15 keV does not affect nucleosynthesis because the absolute stellar rate drops below 10⁻¹⁵/s at 15 keV.
- The 72 keV transition in 180Ta has a laboratory half-life of 16.1 ± 1.9 ns for the 5+ IMS level.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of K-isomer production and destruction in 176Lu and 180Ta under s-process conditions (Heil 2008, Mohr 2009, Gintautas 2009) by quantifying the previously neglected role of electronic plasma processes (NEEC, electron scattering, NEET). The results confirm that the s-process nucleosynthesis of 176Lu and 180Ta remains unchanged from earlier calculations that omitted these effects, and they enable future studies to focus on other astrophysical sites (r-process, p-process, nu-process) where higher temperatures or densities might make these electronic enhancements relevant.
