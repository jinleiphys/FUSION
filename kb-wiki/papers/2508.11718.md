# About possibility to observe spin dichroism effect (the effect of tensor polarization acquiring) for the effect of tensor polarization acquiring by a nonpolarized deuteron beam passing through the nonpolarized internal target of Nuclotron

> Deuteron passage through a nonpolarized target is accompanied by birefringence effect which reveals as diverse phenomena, namely: rotation of spin and tensor polarization about the momentum direction, spin oscillations, vector polarization conversion to tensor that and vice versa, spin dichroism. Possibility to study deuteron spin dichroism effect i.e. the effect of tensor polarization acquiring by a nonpolarized deuteron beam moving in NuclotronM and passing through its internal target is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper discusses the possibility of observing spin dichroism (tensor polarization acquisition) for an initially nonpolarized deuteron beam passing through a nonpolarized internal target at the Nuclotron-M accelerator complex, but only provides a theoretical feasibility analysis rather than reporting an actual experimental observation.

## Method

The authors derive the spin-dependent refractive index for deuterons (spin S=1) in nonpolarized matter using the forward scattering amplitude expanded in terms of spin operators, showing that the refractive index differs for spin projections m=±1 versus m=0. They then calculate the evolution of vector and tensor polarization components for a deuteron beam passing through a target, using both exact exponential propagation and first-order thin-target approximations, and combine this with the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation to describe spin precession in the storage ring's electromagnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The tensor polarization acquired by an initially nonpolarized deuteron beam after passing through a nonpolarized target of thickness L is given by p_zz ≈ -(2/3)ρz(σ_±1 - σ_0), where σ_±1 and σ_0 are the cross sections for deuterons with spin projections m=±1 and m=0 respectively
- The tensor polarization can be expressed as p_zz(L) = (2N_a/3M_r)∫_0^L (σ_0(E(L')) - σ_±1(E(L'))) dL', where N_a is Avogadro's number, M_r is the molar mass of the target matter, and L is target thickness in g/cm²
- Previous experiments observed spin dichroism for deuterons in the energy range 5-20 MeV (at Universität zu Köln) and at momentum 5 GeV/c (at JINR Dubna with external target)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of spin birefringence for particles with spin S≥1 developed by Baryshevsky (1992, 1993), which predicted that even a nonpolarized target causes spin dichroism due to different absorption for different spin projections. The paper proposes a new experimental configuration using the internal target of Nuclotron-M at the NICA accelerator complex, building on previous transmission experiments at JINR (Azhgirei, 2007) and Köln (2011), and suggests that the predicted tensor polarization could be measured with the existing CH₂ polarimeter detection system (Kurilkin, 2011).
