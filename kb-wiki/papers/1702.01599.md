# A combined mean-field and three-body model tested on the $^{26}$O-nucleus

> We combine few- and many-body degrees of freedom in a model applicable to both bound and continuum states and adaptable to different subfields of physics. We formulate a self-consistent three-body model for a core-nucleus surrounded by two valence nucleons. We treat the core in the mean-field approximation and use the same effective Skyrme interaction between both core and valence nucleons. We apply the model to $^{26}$O where we reproduce the known experimental data as well as phenomenological models with more parameters. The decay of the ground state is found to proceed directly into the continuum without effect of the virtual sequential decay through the well reproduced $d_{3/2}$-resonance of $^{25}$O.

# Combined mean-field and three-body model for $^{26}$O

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent model that combines mean-field treatment of a core nucleus with a three-body description of two valence neutrons, and reproduces experimental data for $^{26}$O with fewer free parameters than phenomenological models. The ground state decay proceeds directly into the continuum without virtual sequential decay through the $d_{3/2}$ resonance of $^{25}$O, though the authors note that the core structure is maintained for all distances rather than gradually relaxing, which affects the calculated width.

## Method

The authors formulate a Hamiltonian for an $A+2$ system divided into a core (treated with Skyrme SLy4 mean-field Hartree-Fock) and two valence neutrons (treated with the hyperspherical adiabatic Faddeev expansion method). The coupled equations for core and valence wave functions are solved iteratively to self-consistency, with Pauli forbidden states from core-occupied orbitals removed from the three-body calculation. The finite-range nucleon-nucleon interaction in vacuum is used for the valence neutron-neutron interaction, while the Skyrme interaction is used for core-core and core-valence interactions.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of $^{26}$O: $E(^{26}\text{O}) = -172.490$ MeV, which is 62 keV lower than the pure Hartree-Fock energy $E_{HF}(^{26}\text{O}) = -172.428$ MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy: 18 keV (energy difference between $^{26}$O and $^{24}$O with valence neutrons at infinity)
- Distortion energy of $^{24}$O core within $^{26}$O: 285 keV above the Hartree-Fock ground state of $^{24}$O
- Two neutrons bound by 267 keV with respect to the distorted $^{24}$O nucleus
- Lowest adiabatic potential barrier height: 0.8 MeV
- Neutron-core invariant mass peak at 0.85 MeV, compared to experimental $d_{3/2}$ resonance at 0.749(10) MeV (Kohley 2016)
- Lifetime estimates: $10^{-16}$ s (with full potential shift) to $10^{-15}$ s (with tail-only shift), compared to experimental half-life between $10^{-17}$ s and $10^{-15}$ s (Kohley 2016)
- Three-body interaction parameters: $\rho_0 = 6$ fm, $S_0 = -6.45$ MeV
- Outer turning point: 66.3 fm
- Wave function composition: 90% neutron-core $d_{3/2}$-wave, with remaining 10% equally distributed among $p_{3/2}$, $d_{5/2}$, and $f_{7/2}$ waves

## Context

This work extends the combination of few-body and many-body methods to treat both bound and continuum states, addressing a gap between traditional few-body cluster methods (which assume inert constituents) and many-body methods (which focus on small-distance structure). The model is tested on $^{26}$O, a neutron dripline nucleus with a double-magic $^{24}$O core (Hoffman 2009) and two valence neutrons, where previous phenomenological investigations (Hagino 2016) used more parameters. The approach enables future applications to halo formation, Efimov physics (Braaten 2006), and open quantum systems across nuclear physics and cold atomic gases.
