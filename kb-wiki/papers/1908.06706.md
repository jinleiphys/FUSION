# Towards a theory of baryon resonances

> In this talk, I discuss methods that allow for a systematic and model-independent calculation of the hadron spectrum. These are lattice QCD and/or its corresponding Effective Field Theories. Assorted results are shown and I take the opportunity to discuss some misconceptions often found in the literature.

## Key claim

Systematic and model-independent calculations of the hadron spectrum require either lattice QCD or effective field theories (or their combination), but the most distinctive feature of excited states, their decays, are only captured for very few states in lattice QCD, making the combination with unitarized chiral perturbation theory essential for extracting resonance poles from finite-volume data.

## Method

The talk reviews the application of Luscher's finite-volume formalism to relate lattice QCD energy levels to continuum scattering phase shifts, extended to coupled-channel systems using unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UCHPT). For the coupled-channel case, the T-matrix is constructed as T^{-1}(s) = V^{-1}(s) - G(s), where V(s) comes from the SU(3) heavy-light chiral effective Lagrangian at next-to-leading order and G(s) is a regularized two-point scalar loop function, with finite-volume modifications implemented by replacing the continuum loop integration with a discrete sum over lattice momenta. The method is applied to re-analyze lattice data on Dpi, Deta, D_s Kbar scattering and to compute baryon resonance widths using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the complex-mass scheme.

## Key numbers

- The QCD scale Lambda_QCD is approximately 210 MeV (for N_f = 5, MSbar, mu = 2 GeV)
- The Delta(1232) phase shift calculations used pion masses from 160 to 390 MeV on lattices with a = 0.075 fm and a = 0.06 fm
- The Hadron Spectrum Collaboration reported one S-wave pole at (2275.9 +/- 0.9) MeV in the Dpi, Deta, D_s Kbar system with isospin I=1/2 at M_pi approximately 390 MeV
- The charm-strange scalar meson pole was predicted at 2315(+18)(-28) MeV, consistent with the PDG value for D_s0*(2317)
- For the D_0* two-pole structure: lower pole at (2105(+6)(-8), 102(+10)(-11)) MeV and higher pole at (2451(+36)(-26), 134(+7)(-8)) MeV for mass and half-width
- For the D_1 two-pole structure: lower pole at (2247(+5)(-6), 107(+11)(-10)) MeV and higher pole at (2555(+47)(-30), 203(+8)(-9)) MeV
- For the B_0* two-pole structure: lower pole at (5535(+9)(-11), 113(+15)(-17)) MeV and higher pole at (5852(+16)(-19), 36 +/- 5) MeV
- For the B_1 two-pole structure: lower pole at (5584(+9)(-11), 119(+14)(-17)) MeV and higher pole at (5912(+15)(-18), 42(+5)(-4)) MeV
- The deuteron binding energy is about 2.22 MeV, its radius is 2.14 fm, and the proton radius is about 0.85 fm
- The deuteron binding momentum gamma = 45.7 MeV = 0.23 fm^{-1}, with scattering length a = -5.419(7) fm and effective range r = 1.764(8) fm

## Context

This talk extends the program of systematic, model-independent hadron spectroscopy using lattice QCD and effective field theories, building on the foundational work of Luscher (1986) for finite-volume scattering and the development of unitarized chiral perturbation theory by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001). The two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405), first noted by Oller (2000), is shown to have an analog in the D_0*(2400) system, resolving the enigma that the D_0* mass exceeds the D_s0*(2317) mass despite containing a lighter quark. The analysis enables future lattice tests of the two-pole scenario, as the sextet pole becomes a bound state for meson masses above 575 MeV in the SU(3) limit, and predicts similar two-pole structures for B-meson resonances that can be tested experimentally.
