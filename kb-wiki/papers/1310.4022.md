# System size dependence of hadron $p_T$ spectra in p+p and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV

> We make a systematic study of transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra of hadrons produced in $p+p$ and in different centralities of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$\,GeV using phenomenological fit functions. The Tsallis distribution gives a very good description of hadron spectra in $p+p$ collisions with just two parameters but does not produce the same in Au+Au collisions at intermediate $p_{T}$. To explain the hadron spectra in heavy ion collisions in a wider $p_{T}$ range we propose a modified Tsallis function by introducing an additional parameter which accounts for collective flow. The new analytical function gives a very good description of both mesons and baryons spectra at all centralities of Au+Au collisions in terms of parameters having a potential physics interpretation. With this modified Tsallis function we study the spectra of pions, kaons, protons, $Î›(1115)$ and $Îž^{-}$ as a function of system size at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. The parameter representing transverse flow increases with system size and is found to be more far baryons than that for mesons in central Au+Au collisions. The freeze-out temperature for baryons increases with centrality in Au+Au collisions more rapidly than for mesons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified Tsallis distribution that adds a transverse flow parameter to the standard Tsallis function, enabling a good description of hadron spectra in Au+Au collisions across a wider $p_T$ range than the standard two-parameter Tsallis distribution, which works well only for $p+p$ collisions.

## Method

The authors fit the standard Tsallis distribution to hadron spectra in $p+p$ collisions to extract the power parameter $n$ for each particle species (pions, kaons, protons, $\Lambda$, $\Xi^{-}$). They then fix these $n$ values and fit a modified Tsallis function (Eq. 8 in the paper) to Au+Au collision data at four centralities (10-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-80%) to extract the transverse flow velocity $\beta$ and freeze-out temperature $T$ as functions of system size. The fits use published PHENIX and STAR data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- For $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, the power parameter $n$ values are: $\pi$: $9.55 \pm 0.13$, $K$: $8.61 \pm 0.24$, $p$: $11.95 \pm 0.10$, $\Lambda$: $13.24 \pm 5.36$, $\Xi$: $13.24 \pm 5.18$
- For the most central Au+Au collisions, the transverse flow velocity $\beta$ for protons is approximately 0.6 and for pions is approximately 0.4
- The $\chi^2$/ndf for pion fits in $p+p$ is 28.2/27; for Au+Au centralities it ranges from 11.7/35 (10-20%) to 24.1/35 (60-80%)
- The $p_T$ ranges covered include: pions 0.3-6.0 GeV/$c$, kaons 0.4-1.8 GeV/$c$, protons 0.47-6.0 GeV/$c$, $\Lambda$ 0.35-4.75 GeV/$c$, $\Xi^{-}$ 0.67-5.0 GeV/$c$

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to describe hadron spectra using Tsallis or Hagedorn distributions in heavy ion collisions, where the PHENIX collaboration used a modified Hagedorn formula (PPG088, PPG077) and other authors added exponential terms to the Tsallis function (Bylinkin, 2010). The modified Tsallis function enables systematic study of flow and freeze-out parameters across system sizes, showing that baryons and mesons separate clearly in both flow velocity and freeze-out temperature for central Au+Au collisions, with baryons freezing out earlier than mesons.
