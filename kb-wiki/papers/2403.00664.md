# Nonperturbative Collins-Soper Kernel from Chiral Quarks with Physical Masses

> We present a lattice QCD calculation of the rapidity anomalous dimension of quark transverse-momentum-dependent distributions, i.e., the Collins-Soper (CS) kernel, up to transverse separations of about 1 fm. This unitary lattice calculation is conducted, for the first time, employing the chiral-symmetry-preserving domain wall fermion discretization and physical values of light and strange quark masses. The CS kernel is extracted from the ratios of pion quasi-transverse-momentum-dependent wave functions (quasi-TMDWFs) at next-to-leading logarithmic perturbative accuracy. Also for the first time, we utilize the recently proposed Coulomb-gauge-fixed quasi-TMDWF correlator without a Wilson line. We observe significantly slower signal decay with increasing quark separations compared to the established gauge-invariant method with a staple-shaped Wilson line. This enables us to determine the CS kernel at large nonperturbative transverse separations and find its near-linear dependence on the latter. Our result is consistent with the recent lattice calculation using gauge-invariant quasi-TMDWFs, and agrees with various recent phenomenological parametrizations of experimental data.

## Key claim
The Collins-Soper (CS) kernel extracted from Coulomb-gauge-fixed quasi-TMDWFs without Wilson lines shows a near-linear dependence on transverse separation up to about 1 fm, with significantly slower signal decay than the gauge-invariant method, but the result is consistent with both the gauge-invariant lattice calculation and phenomenological parametrizations only when power corrections and higher-order perturbative corrections are well suppressed (i.e., for large enough transverse separation and small enough momentum differences).

## Method
The CS kernel was extracted from ratios of pion quasi-transverse-momentum-dependent wave functions (quasi-TMDWFs) at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) perturbative accuracy, using a 2+1-flavor domain wall fermion ensemble with physical quark masses and lattice spacing a = 0.0836 fm. The quasi-TMDWF correlators were computed in Coulomb gauge without Wilson lines, and the bare matrix elements were obtained from two-state fits to the ratio of quasi-TMDWF and pion-pion two-point correlation functions, with renormalization performed via ratios to the b_z=0, P_z=0 matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 2.3549(49) GeV (a = 0.0836 fm)
- Maximum pion momentum: P_z = 1.85 GeV (n_z = 8)
- Transverse separations accessed: up to about 1 fm (b_perp = 10a)
- CS kernel determined at transverse separations b_perp up to approximately 1 fm
- Perturbative corrections applied at NLL accuracy with MS-bar scale mu = 2 GeV
- CS kernel consistent with N^3LL perturbative prediction for b_perp less than about 0.4 fm
- Gaussian smearing radius: r_G = 0.58 fm
- Number of configurations: 64, with All Mode Averaging (2 exact + 128 sloppy solutions for n_z in [4,8]; 1 exact + 32 sloppy for n_z in [0,3])

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD approach to the CS kernel by using the Coulomb-gauge quasi-TMD method proposed by Zhao (2023), avoiding Wilson-line complications that plagued earlier gauge-invariant calculations such as those by Avkhadiev (2024) and the Lattice Parton Collaboration (2023). The results agree with the gauge-invariant lattice calculation by Avkhadiev (2024) at short distances and with phenomenological parametrizations from MAP22 (Bacchetta 2022), ART23 (Moos 2023), IFY23 (Isaacson 2023), and HSO24 (Aslan 2024), enabling future first-principles studies of TMDs at larger transverse separations in the nonperturbative regime.
