# Bose-Einstein condensation of five $α$ clusters in $^{20}$Ne and supersolidity

> We show that the five $α$ cluster states recently observed in $^{20}$Ne, slightly above the five $α$ threshold energy, are Bose-Einstein condensates of five $α$ clusters. The states are described well using a superfluid cluster model, where the order parameter is defined. We suggest that the the five $α$ states are fragmented. Theory predicts the emergence of a five $α$ rotational roton band characterized by a large moment of inertia. This band is formed through roton excitations of the five $α$ BEC vacuum and possesses dual properties of superfluidity and crystallinity, a property of supersolidity. The persistent existence of such a roton bandis discussed and confirmed for the four $α$ condensate above the four $α$ threshold in $^{16}$O and the three $α$ condensate above the three $α$ threshold in $^{12}$C.

# Bose-Einstein condensation of five α clusters in ²⁰Ne and supersolidity

## Key claim
The five α cluster states recently observed slightly above the five α threshold in ²⁰Ne are Bose-Einstein condensates, but the observed states are fragmented, at least one of the four candidate states must be interpreted as a fragmented component rather than a pure condensate vacuum, and the authors assume that two states at 21.8 and 22.5 MeV are fragmented from a centroid.

## Method
The authors apply a superfluid cluster model (SCM) based on effective field theory, in which the order parameter of the α-cluster BEC is defined and the Nambu-Goldstone mode operators from spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global phase are formulated. The model Hamiltonian for a bosonic field representing the α cluster includes a harmonic trap potential and an α–α interaction; the Gross-Pitaevskii equation determines the order parameter, and the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equation describes collective excitations on the condensate. The two parameters controlling the trap strength and stability are adjusted to reproduce the experimental energies, assuming a condensation rate of 50% and taking the 21.2 MeV 0⁺ state as the vacuum.

## Key numbers
- The calculated rms radius of the 21.2 MeV 0⁺ state assuming no fragmentation is 4.29 fm, which is significantly smaller than the 5.62 fm rms radius of the four α condensate 0⁺ state at 15.1 MeV in ¹⁶O; after assuming fragmentation, the vacuum radius becomes 6.04 fm, comparable to 5.5 fm from Yamada (2004).
- The rotational constant k = ħ²/(2𝒥) for the predicted roton band in ²⁰Ne is 67 keV, compared to 147 keV for the K=0₁⁻ parity-doublet band at 5.79 MeV and 101 keV for the K=0₄⁺ band at about 8.78 MeV; the moment of inertia is about 3.0 times that of the K=0₁⁺ ground band.
- The SCM calculation with Ω=1.06 MeV/ħ and Vr=505 MeV reproduces the experimental centroid data and locates the BdG 2⁺ state at 22.9 MeV and the 4⁺ state at 24.2 MeV, with NG mode 0⁺ states at 22.2 and 23.2 MeV.
- B(E2) values for the roton band transitions in ²⁰Ne at 50% condensation rate include: 786 e²fm⁴ for 2⁺(BdG)→0⁺(NG vacuum), 2182 e²fm⁴ for 2⁺(BdG)→0⁺(NG excited), 1487 e²fm⁴ for 4⁺(BdG)→2⁺(BdG), and 2070 e²fm⁴ for 6⁺(BdG)→4⁺(BdG).
- For ¹⁶O, the rotational constant from the SCM is 80 keV, agreeing with the 86 keV from the crystallinity picture of Ohkubo (2010).
- For ¹²C, the SCM calculation with Ω=1.80 MeV/ħ, Vr=422 MeV and N₀=3 reproduces the experimental band and predicts a 6⁺ state at 15.4 MeV in the roton band.

## Context
This work extends the superfluid cluster model previously applied to three α condensates in ¹²C by Nakamura (2016) and Katsuragi (2018) and to four α condensates in ¹⁶O by Takahashi (2020). It addresses the recent experimental observation of five α cluster candidate states in ²⁰Ne by Adachi (2020) and Swartz (2015), which a five α cluster model calculation by Zhou (2023) could only partially reproduce. The paper unifies the interpretation of α condensate bands in ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ²⁰Ne as exhibiting dual properties of superfluidity and crystallinity, a signature of supersolidity, and predicts a rotational roton band in ²⁰Ne that awaits experimental confirmation. The concept of supersolidity in α cluster structures was previously suggested by Ohkubo (2020, 2022).
