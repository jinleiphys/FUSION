# Alpha-alpha scattering in the Multiverse

> We investigate the phase shifts of low-energy alpha-alpha scattering under variations of the fundamental parameters of the Standard Model, namely the light quark mass, the electromagnetic fine-structure constant as well as the QCD theta-angle. As a first step, we recalculate alpha-alpha scattering in our Universe utilizing various improvements in the adiabatic projection method, which leads to an improved, parameter-free prediction of the S- and D-wave phase shifts for laboratory energies below 10 MeV. We find that positive shifts in the pion mass have a small effect on the S-wave phase shift, whereas lowering the pion mass adds some repulsion in the two-alpha system. The effect on the D-wave phase shift turns out to be more pronounced as signaled by the D-wave resonance parameters. Variations of the fine-structure constant have almost no effect on the low-energy alpha-alpha phase shifts. We further show that up-to-and-including next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, variations of these phase shifts with respect to the QCD theta-angle can be expressed in terms of the theta-dependent pion mass.

## Key claim

The authors compute low-energy alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts under variations of the light quark mass, the electromagnetic fine-structure constant, and the QCD theta-angle, finding that positive shifts in the pion mass have a small effect on the S-wave phase shift while lowering the pion mass adds repulsion, and that variations of the fine-structure constant have almost no effect; the theta-angle dependence is mapped onto the pion mass dependence up to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Method

The authors use Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) with a periodic cubic lattice of spacing 1.97 fm and temporal spacing 1.32 fm, employing the adiabatic projection method (APM) to extract S- and D-wave phase shifts from auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo simulations. The pion mass dependence of the nuclear Hamiltonian is expressed via partial derivatives of the two-alpha cluster energy with respect to the pion mass, the nucleon mass, the one-pion-exchange coupling, and the short-range low-energy constants, with the latter determined from the pion mass dependence of the inverse singlet and triplet NN scattering lengths. The electromagnetic dependence is treated by computing energy shifts from the long-range Coulomb interaction and a proton-proton contact operator, while the theta-angle dependence is inferred from the theta-dependent pion mass using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The spatial lattice spacing is a = 1.97 fm and the temporal lattice spacing is a_t = 1.32 fm.
- The pion mass shift used to compute the partial derivative with respect to the explicit pion mass is Delta M_pi = 4.59 MeV, equal to the empirical mass difference between neutral and charged pions.
- The pion-nucleon sigma term gives x_1 = 0.84(7) for the derivative of the nucleon mass with respect to the pion mass.
- The derivative of the one-pion-exchange coupling with respect to the pion mass is x_2 = -0.053(16) in lattice units.
- The derivatives of the short-range low-energy constants are x_3 = -0.153(96) and x_4 = 0.049(46).
- The relative strength of the proton-proton contact term is x_pp = 0.39(5).
- The inverse singlet and triplet NN scattering length derivatives are bar_A_s = 0.54(24) and bar_A_t = 0.33(16).

## Context

This work extends previous NLEFT studies of the Hoyle state and triple-alpha process (Epelbaum 2012, Epelbaum 2013, Lahde 2019) by providing an ab initio calculation of alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts under variations of fundamental parameters, rather than relying on model-dependent descriptions of nuclear forces. The results enable more elaborate stellar reaction network calculations in the Multiverse and complement earlier investigations of the QCD theta-angle bounds from nuclear binding (Lee 2020) and anthropic considerations (Ubaldi 2008).
