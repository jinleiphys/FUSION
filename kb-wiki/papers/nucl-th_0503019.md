# Dihadron fragmentation: in vacuum and in matter

> Two particle correlations within a single jet produced in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus as well as in heavy-ion collisions are explored. This is performed within the framework of the medium modified dihadron fragmentation functions. The modification occurs due to gluon bremsstrahlung induced by multiple scattering. The modified fragmentation functions for dihadrons are found to follow closely that of single hadrons leading to a weak nuclear suppression of their ratios as measured by HERMES in DIS experiments. Meanwhile, a moderate medium enhancement of the near-side correlation of two high $p_T$ hadrons is found in central heavy-ion collisions, partially due to trigger bias caused by the competition between parton energy loss and the initial Cronin effect.

## Key claim

The medium modification of dihadron fragmentation functions follows closely that of single hadron fragmentation functions, leading to a weak nuclear suppression of their ratios in DIS off nuclei (as measured by HERMES) but a moderate medium enhancement of the near-side correlation of two high \(p_T\) hadrons in central heavy-ion collisions, partially due to trigger bias caused by the competition between parton energy loss and the initial Cronin effect.

## Method

The author computes the medium-modified dihadron fragmentation functions at next-to-leading twist in pQCD, including contributions from multiple scattering and induced gluon bremsstrahlung. The modification is expressed in terms of modified splitting functions that depend on the quark-gluon correlation function in the nucleus \(T^A_{qg}\), with the single free parameter \(\tilde{C}=0.006\) GeV\(^2\) fixed from single hadron fragmentation data from HERMES. For heavy-ion collisions, the calculation is extended by assuming \(\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle \simeq \mu^2\) (the Debye screening mass) and using the average initial jet energy \(\langle E_T \rangle\) and energy loss \(\langle \Delta E \rangle\) determined from the suppression of single inclusive hadron spectra.

## Key numbers

* In DIS off a nitrogen target (\(A=14\)), the double ratio \(\mathcal{R}_2(z_2)\) (associated hadron distribution in nitrogen vs. deuterium) shows a weak suppression at large \(z_2\), with the theoretical prediction agreeing with HERMES data without free parameters.
* In central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the ratio \(I_{AA}\) (associated hadron distribution in \(Au+Au\) vs. \(p+p\)) shows a moderate enhancement that increases with the number of participant nucleons \(N_{part}\), while in peripheral collisions a slight suppression is observed.
* The parameter \(\tilde{C}=0.006\) GeV\(^2\) is determined from the kinematics of the HERMES experiment and is the only parameter in the modified dihadron fragmentation function.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of single hadron fragmentation functions in cold nuclear matter (Guo and Wang, 2000; Majumder, 2004) to dihadron correlations, using the same formalism of higher-twist contributions from multiple scattering and induced gluon radiation. The calculations are compared to HERMES data on DIS off nuclei (Dinezza, 2004) and STAR data on \(Au+Au\) collisions at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2003). The results enable future studies of jet substructure as a probe of the partonic structure of dense matter, including both cold nuclear matter and the hot deconfined medium produced in heavy-ion collisions.
