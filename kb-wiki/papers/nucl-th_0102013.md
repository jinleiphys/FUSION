# Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Nucleon in a Relativistic Quark Pair Creation Model

> We study the effects of the | qqq q\bar{q} > component of the hadronic wave function on the description of the electromagnetic structure of the nucleon. Starting with a qqq baryonic wave function which describes the baryonic and mesonic low energy spectrum, the extra q\bar{q} pair is generated through a relativistic version of the 3P_0 model. It is shown that this model leads to a renormalization of the quark mass that allows one to construct a conserved electromagnetic current. We conclude that these dynamical relativistic corrections play an important role in reproducing the Q2 dependence of the electromagnetic form factors at low Q^2.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including explicit $|qqq\;q\bar{q}\rangle$ components in the nucleon wave function, generated through a relativistic $^3P_0$ model, significantly improves the description of nucleon electromagnetic form factors at low $Q^2$, but only when gauge invariance is restored by a mass renormalization that links the $^3P_0$ strength $\beta$ to the quark mass shift.

## Method

The authors start with a non-relativistic $3q$ baryon wave function from a spectroscopic potential model (linear confinement plus one-gluon exchange terms) that fits the low-energy baryon and meson spectrum. They generate an extra $q\bar{q}$ pair using a relativistic version of the $^3P_0$ pair-creation operator, then compute the single-quark electromagnetic transition operator from three contributions: the elementary emission model (EEM), resonant vector-meson ($\rho$, $\omega$) diagrams, and non-resonant $q\bar{q}$ propagation. Gauge invariance is enforced by requiring a linear relation $m = m_0 + \beta/2$ between the physical quark mass $m$, the bare mass $m_0$, and the $^3P_0$ strength $\beta$, which cancels the breaking terms between the EEM and non-resonant sectors.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment fitted to its experimental value by adjusting $\beta$; neutron magnetic moment then predicted as $\mu_n = -1.89$ (experimental value not quoted in text).
- EEM contribution gives up to 60% of the magnetic moment at $Q^2=0$; non-resonant (NR-EEM) contribution gives almost 40% of $\mu_p$; resonant contribution vanishes at $Q^2=0$.
- Predicted proton charge square mean radius: $\langle r_p^2\rangle = 0.48$ fm$^2$ (experimental: $0.74 \pm 0.02$ fm$^2$ from Brown (1986)).
- Predicted neutron charge square mean radius: $\langle r_n^2\rangle = -0.06$ fm$^2$ (experimental: $-0.1215 \pm 0.0016$ fm$^2$ from Koester (1976)).
- EEM diagram alone gives $\langle r_p^2\rangle = 0.32$ fm$^2$; resonant diagram accounts for 30% of $\langle r_p^2\rangle$.
- Magnetic form factors are reproduced up to $Q^2 = 1.2$ GeV$^2$.
- Quark mass in the spectroscopic model: $m = 0.337$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier treatment of strong pionic decays (Cano (1996)) and photo/electroproduction of the Roper resonance $N(1440)$ (Cano (1998)) to the elastic nucleon form factors, using the same $^3P_0$ mechanism for $q\bar{q}$ pair creation. It contrasts with non-relativistic quark model calculations (Koniuk (1980)) and two-body exchange current approaches (Robson (1993)), and complements Light-Front and Point-Form studies (Konen (1990), Wagenbrunn (2000)). The results can be compared to Helminen (1999), who evaluated exchange currents and quark form factor contributions to charge radii. The model enables a unified description of strong and electromagnetic transitions of baryons with a single parameter $\beta$, though the authors note that a full program including pionic decays and photo/electroproduction of resonances with the same $\beta$ value remains to be carried out.
