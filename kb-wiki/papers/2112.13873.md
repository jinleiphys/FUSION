# An Effective Field Theory of Magneto-Elasticity

> We utilize the coset construction to derive the effective field theory of magnon-phonon interactions in (anti)-ferromagnetic and ferrimagnetic insulating materials. The action is used to calculate the equations of motion which generalize the Landau-Lifshitz and stress equations to allow for magneto-acoustic couplings to all orders in the fields at lowest order in the derivative expansion. We also include the symmetry breaking effects due to Zeeman, and Dzyaloshinsky-Moriya interactions. This effective theory is a toolbox for the study of magneto-elastic phenomena from first principles. As an example we use this theory to calculate the leading order contribution to the magnon decay width due to its the decay into phonons.

## Key claim

The paper uses the coset construction to derive an effective field theory (EFT) of magnon-phonon interactions in insulating (anti-)ferromagnets and ferrimagnets, claiming that the action is completely dictated by the spontaneous symmetry breaking pattern and can describe magneto-acoustic couplings to all orders in the fields at lowest order in the derivative expansion, but only within a chosen power counting scheme that assumes a particular hierarchy of scales.

## Method

The authors applied the coset construction to the symmetry breaking pattern of a homogeneous, isotropic solid with spin order, parametrizing the vacuum manifold with Goldstone fields for broken translations, rotations, boosts, and spin rotations. They imposed inverse Higgs constraints to eliminate redundant fields, leaving phonon fields \(\pi^i\) and magnon fields \(\chi^a\), and then wrote the most general effective Lagrangian containing one derivative on each phonon and the least possible number of derivatives on magnons, including Wess-Zumino-Witten terms. The resulting action was used to derive generalized equations of motion and to calculate the leading contribution to the magnon decay width from decay into phonons.

## Key numbers

*   The magnon decay width due to decay into phonons is calculated at leading order, though the full text is truncated and does not provide the explicit numerical value or expression in the available excerpt.
*   The effective action for ferromagnets includes a WZW term \(\mathcal{L}_{WZW}^{\chi}\) with coefficient \(c_2\), which provides a quadratic term with one time derivative, while for antiferromagnets the leading kinetic term comes from \(\frac{1}{2} F_3 (u) \nabla_t \chi_a \nabla_t \chi^a\) with two time derivatives.
*   The phonon Lagrangian reduces to \(\mathcal{L} \to \frac{c_1}{2} \det (D)\, [ \partial_t \phi^j (D^{-1})_j{}^i ]^2 - F_1 (u)\), where \(c_1\) is an arbitrary coefficient and \(u_{ij}\) is the strain tensor.

## Context

This work extends the coset construction technique, previously applied separately to magnons (Burgess 1998) and relativistic phonons (Nicolis 2013), to the coupled magneto-elastic system for the first time. It generalizes the Landau-Lifshitz and stress equations to include magneto-acoustic couplings to all orders in the fields, and provides a first-principles EFT framework for studying phenomena such as magnon damping and interactions of Skyrmions with magnons and phonons (Pavaskar 2031). The approach enables systematic inclusion of explicit symmetry breaking from Zeeman and Dzyaloshinsky-Moriya interactions, and can be adapted to different power counting schemes depending on the hierarchy of scales in a given material.
