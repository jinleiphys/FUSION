# Meson Systems with Ginsparg-Wilson Valence Quarks

> Unphysical effects associated with finite lattice spacing and partial quenching generally lead to to the presence of unphysical terms in chiral extrapolation formulae, which must be removed to make physical predictions. We use mixed action effective field theory to discuss that through next-to-leading order, simulations of meson systems which employ valence quarks satisfying the Ginsparg-Wilson relation (lattice-chiral symmetry), depend only upon the physical counterterms of interest, provided one uses a lattice-physical (on-shell) renormalization scheme. With this lattice-physical renormalization, the I = 2 pi-pi, I = 3/2 K-pi, I=1 K-K meson scattering lengths combined with f_K / f_pi share only two linearly independent counterterms, both of which are physical. This allows us to make use of existing lattice QCD simulations to make a prediction of the I = 1 K-K scattering length.

# Meson Systems with Ginsparg-Wilson Valence Quarks

## Key claim
Mixed action lattice simulations using Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks yield chiral extrapolation formulae for meson scattering processes that, through next-to-leading order, depend only on physical counterterms, provided one employs a lattice-physical (on-shell) renormalization scheme. This holds despite the presence of unphysical lattice-spacing and partial-quenching effects in the underlying effective theory.

## Method
The author uses mixed action effective field theory to analyze meson scattering processes in lattice QCD simulations where valence quarks satisfy the Ginsparg-Wilson relation (preserving chiral symmetry on the lattice) while sea quarks use a different, computationally cheaper discretization. The analysis shows that at next-to-leading order, every operator in the mixed action Lagrangian that incorporates lattice spacing or partial quenching effects for external valence-valence mesons amounts to a renormalization of one of the two operators in the leading-order Lagrangian. An on-shell renormalization scheme absorbs all unphysical counterterm effects into the lattice-physical meson masses and decay constants.

## Key numbers
- The unphysical contribution to the I=2 pi-pi scattering length, given by the term $\tilde{\D}_{ju}^4 / 6 m_\pi^4$, is approximately 10% of the physical next-to-leading order contribution in the lattice simulation of Beane (2005).
- The I=2 pi-pi scattering length at next-to-leading order, expressed in terms of lattice-physical parameters, is: $m_\pi a_{\pi\pi}^{I=2}= -\frac{m_\pi^2}{8 \pi f_\pi^2} \Bigg\{ 1 + \frac{m_\pi^2}{(4\pi f_\pi)^2} \bigg[ 3\ln \left( \frac{m_\pi^2}{\mu^2} \right) -1 -l_{\pi\pi}^{I=2}(\mu) -\frac{\tilde{\D}_{ju}^4}{6\, m_\pi^4} \bigg] \Bigg\}$.
- The I=2 pi-pi, I=3/2 K-pi, and I=1 K-K meson scattering lengths combined with $f_K / f_\pi$ share only two linearly independent counterterms, both physical.

## Context
This work extends the mixed action lattice QCD approach introduced by Bar (2002) and builds upon the effective field theory framework for partial quenching developed by Bernard (1993) and for lattice-spacing artifacts by Sharpe (1998). The analysis generalizes the finding of Chen (2005) that the I=2 pi-pi scattering length in mixed action theories depends only on physical counterterms when expressed in lattice-physical parameters. The result enables a prediction of the I=1 K-K scattering length using existing lattice QCD simulations from Beane (2006), providing a test of both the mixed action formalism and the convergence of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory.
