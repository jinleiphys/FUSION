# Majorana neutrino mass constraints in the landscape of nuclear matrix elements

> We discuss up-to-date constraints on the Majorana neutrino mass $m_{ββ}$ from neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) searches in experiments using different isotopes: KamLAND-Zen and EXO ($^{136}$Xe), GERDA and MAJORANA ($^{76}$Ge) and CUORE ($^{130}$Te). Best fits and upper bounds on $m_{ββ}$ are explored in the general landscape of nuclear matrix elements (NME), as well as for specific NME values obtained in representative nuclear models. By approximating the likelihood of $0νββ$ signals through quadratic forms, the analysis of separate and combined isotope data becomes exceedingly simple, and allows to clarify various aspects of multi-isotope data combinations. In particular, we analyze the relative impact of different data in setting upper bounds on $m_{ββ}$, as well as the conditions leading to nonzero $m_{ββ}$ at best fit, for variable values of the NMEs. Detailed results on $m_{ββ}$ from various combinations of data are reported in graphical and numerical form. Implications for future $0νββ$ data analyses and NME calculations are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper provides a simplified quadratic approximation for combining neutrinoless double beta decay data from multiple isotopes, showing that the best-fit value for the Majorana neutrino mass can be nonzero depending on the specific values of nuclear matrix elements, particularly when including CUORE data which shows a slight positive fluctuation.

## Method

The authors parametrize experimental likelihood profiles for Xe (KamLAND-Zen and EXO), Ge (GERDA and MAJORANA), and Te (CUORE) as quadratic functions of the signal strength S_i = 1/T_i, then recast these as functions of m^2_ββ using the relation S_i = G_i M^2_i m^2_ββ. They explore constraints across a continuous "landscape" of nuclear matrix elements M_i in the range [0.2, 20], as well as for 15 representative NME values from four nuclear models (shell model, QRPA, EDF, and IBM) taken from a recent compilation.

## Key numbers

- KamLAND-Zen sets a 90% C.L. half-life limit T_90 = 2.30 × 10^26 y for ¹³⁶Xe, with quadratic coefficients a=5.157, b=3.978, c=0.000
- GERDA sets a 90% C.L. half-life limit T_90 = 1.80 × 10^26 y for ⁷⁶Ge, with linear coefficients a=0.000, b=4.867, c=0.000
- CUORE sets a 90% C.L. half-life limit T_90 = 0.216 × 10^26 y for ¹³⁰Te, with quadratic coefficients a=0.245, b=-0.637, c=0.414
- Combined Xe+Ge+Te* (null-signal pseudo-experiment) gives 2σ bounds on m_ββ ranging from 38.5 meV (case 12, EDF model) to 120.4 meV (case 9, QRPA model)
- Phase space factors: G_Xe = 14.78 × 10⁻⁶, G_Ge = 2.40 × 10⁻⁶, G_Te = 14.42 × 10⁻⁶ (in units of 10⁻²⁶ y⁻¹ (meV)⁻²)

## Context

This work extends the quadratic approximation approach first noted by Caldwell (2017) and further developed by Capozzi (2021) for combining 0νββ data, now incorporating the latest KamLAND-Zen results. The analysis enables do-it-yourself global fits of multi-isotope data for arbitrary NME values, clarifying how different isotopes contribute to upper bounds and how positive fluctuations (as seen in CUORE) could lead to nonzero best-fit m_ββ values depending on the NME landscape. The approach anticipates future scenarios where precise multi-isotope data might test specific nuclear models by selecting allowed ratios of matrix elements.
