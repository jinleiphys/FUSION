# Complex Langevin and other approaches to the sign problem in quantum many-body physics

> We review the theory and applications of complex stochastic quantization to the quantum many-body problem. Along the way, we present a brief overview of a number of ideas that either ameliorate or in some cases altogether solve the sign problem, including the classic reweighting method, alternative Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations, dual variables (for bosons and fermions), Majorana fermions, density-of-states methods, imaginary asymmetry approaches, and Lefschetz thimbles. We discuss some aspects of the mathematical underpinnings of conventional stochastic quantization, provide a few pedagogical examples, and summarize open challenges and practical solutions for the complex case. Finally, we review the recent applications of complex Langevin to quantum field theory in relativistic and nonrelativistic quantum matter, with an emphasis on the nonrelativistic case.

## Key claim

This review argues that the sign problem in quantum many-body physics is not an intrinsic property of a given Hamiltonian but rather depends on the chosen Hubbard-Stratonovich decoupling scheme, and that the complex Langevin approach offers a promising path forward for systems where the sign problem cannot be eliminated by reformulation, though mathematical underpinnings remain incomplete.

## Method

The paper reviews the path-integral formulation of quantum many-body systems, showing how the sign problem arises when the Boltzmann weight cannot be interpreted as a probability measure due to complex phases. It surveys multiple approaches to the sign problem: reweighting, alternative Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations, dual variables, Majorana fermion representations, density-of-states methods (including the logarithmic linear regression algorithm and functional fit approach), imaginary asymmetry methods, Lefschetz thimbles, and complex Langevin. The review emphasizes complex Langevin as applied to both relativistic and nonrelativistic quantum field theories, with detailed discussion of the mathematical formalism and open challenges.

## Key numbers

* Reweighting leads to a phase average that decays as exp[-beta V (omega - omega_pq)], where V is spatial volume and beta is inverse temperature, making the required number of samples grow exponentially with spacetime volume.
* For the repulsive Hubbard model, the condition for a sign-problem-free Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation in the density channel requires exp(tau U/2) <= 1, i.e. U <= 0, which is not the repulsive case of interest.
* The discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation for the Hubbard model uses lambda_d set by cosh(lambda_d) = exp(tau U/2), while the continuous version uses lambda_c = sqrt(tau U).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on quantum Monte Carlo methods for the many-body problem, covering approaches from the classic reweighting method of PhysRevLett.61.2635 through more recent developments. It discusses the work of PhysRevB.48.589 on alternative Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations for the Hubbard model, the density-of-states methods building on Wang and Landau (PhysRevLett.86.2050) and the LLR algorithm of PhysRevD.90.094502, and the complex Langevin approach applied to quantum chromodynamics at finite density (Aarts:2015tyj, Gattringer:2016kco) and nonrelativistic systems. The review enables future work by mapping the landscape of sign-problem approaches and identifying open challenges in the mathematical foundations of complex Langevin.
