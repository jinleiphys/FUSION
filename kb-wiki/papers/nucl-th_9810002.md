# Two Pion Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions at Intermediate Energies

> We have developed a model for the N N --> N N pi pi reaction and evaluated cross sections for the different charged channels. The low energy part of those channels where the pions can be in an isospin zero state is dominated by N* excitation, driven by an isoscalar source followed by the decay N* --> N (pi pi, T=0, s-wave). At higher energies, and in channels where the pions are not in T=0, Delta excitation mechanisms become relevant.

# Two Pion Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim
The model shows that at low energies, the dominant mechanism for two-pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is excitation of the Roper resonance N*(1440) via an isoscalar exchange, followed by its decay into N(ππ) in the T=0, s-wave state; at higher energies and in channels where the pions are not in T=0, Delta resonance excitation mechanisms become relevant. The authors note that the free parameters c*1 and c*2 for the N* → N(ππ) decay cannot both be uniquely determined from the partial decay width alone and are instead constrained by fitting to π− p → π+ π− n data.

## Method
The authors constructed a model for the NN → NNππ reaction using tree-level Feynman diagrams derived from chiral Lagrangians, including non-resonant terms, Delta resonance terms (diagrams 9-15), and N*(1440) resonance terms (diagrams 4-8). The N* → N(ππ)T=0 s-wave decay was parameterized with two free constants c*1 and c*2, constrained by fitting to the π− p → π+ π− n reaction from a prior model by the same group. Final state interaction was implemented for the pp → ppπ+π− channel at Tp < 900 MeV using a separable non-local potential fitted to 1S0 np phase shifts.

## Key numbers
- N*(1440) coupling constants: ṽ = 0.477 for N*Nπ, gN*Δπ = 2.07 for N*Δπ, obtained from partial widths assuming branching ratios of 65% and 25% respectively and a total width of 350 MeV
- Isoscalar σNN coupling: g²σNN/4π = 5.69; σNN* coupling from fit to (α,α') data: g²σNN*/4π = 1.33
- Two parameter sets for N* → N(ππ) decay: Set I with c*1 = -7.27 GeV⁻¹ and c*2 = 0; Set II with c*1 = -12.7 GeV⁻¹ and c*2 = 1.98 GeV⁻¹
- ΔNπ coupling constant f* = 2.13 from Δ → Nπ partial width; fΔ = 4/5 fNNπ from SU(6) quark model
- Isoscalar s-wave πN amplitude parameter λ1 = 0.0075
- T=0 exchange contribution is an order of magnitude larger than T=1 exchange for channels where pions can be in T=0
- Diagram (12) becomes relevant at proton kinetic energies Tp > 1 GeV

## Context
This work extends a previous model for the πN → ππN reaction by the same group (Oset and collaborators, 1998) to the NN → NNππ case. The model is directly comparable to experimental data from the WASA/PROMICE collaboration at CELSIUS, and is relevant to the ABC effect observed in np → d(ππ)⁰ at LAMPF (Holas and collaborators, 1980) and Saturne (Plouin and collaborators, 1990), as well as the MOMO experiment at COSY studying pd → ³He π+ π−. The results also have implications for the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction via a mechanism where one π⁰ is emitted and the other absorbed (Oset and collaborators, 1998).
