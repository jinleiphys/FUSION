# Two Pion Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions at Intermediate Energies

> We have developed a model for the N N --> N N pi pi reaction and evaluated cross sections for the different charged channels. The low energy part of those channels where the pions can be in an isospin zero state is dominated by N* excitation, driven by an isoscalar source followed by the decay N* --> N (pi pi, T=0, s-wave). At higher energies, and in channels where the pions are not in T=0, Delta excitation mechanisms become relevant.

## Key claim
The model shows that at low energies, the dominant mechanism for two-pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is excitation of the Roper resonance N*(1440) via an isoscalar exchange, followed by its decay into N(ПҖПҖ) in the T=0, s-wave state; at higher energies and in channels where the pions are not in T=0, Delta resonance excitation mechanisms become relevant. The authors note that the free parameters c*1 and c*2 for the N* вҶ’ N(ПҖПҖ) decay cannot both be uniquely determined from the partial decay width alone and are instead constrained by fitting to ПҖвҲ’ p вҶ’ ПҖ+ ПҖвҲ’ n data.

## Context
This work extends a previous model for the ПҖN вҶ’ ПҖПҖN reaction by the same group (Oset and collaborators, 1998) to the NN вҶ’ NNПҖПҖ case.
