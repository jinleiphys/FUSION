# Extension of continuum time-dependent Hartree-Fock method to proton states

> This paper deals with the solution of the spherically symmetric time-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation applied to nuclear giant monopole resonances in the small amplitude regime. The problem is spatially unbounded as the resonance state is in the continuum. The practical requirement to perform the calculation in a finite-sized spatial region yields an artificial boundary, which is not present physically. The question of how to ensure the boundary does not interfere with the internal solution, while keeping the overall calculation time low is studied. Here we propose an absorbing boundary condition scheme to handle the conflict. The derivation, via a Laplace transform method, and implementation is described. An inverse Laplace transform required by the absorbing boundaries is calculated using a method of non-linear least squares. The accuracy and efficiency of the scheme is tested and results presented to support the case that they are a effective way of handling the artificial boundary.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an absorbing boundary condition (ABC) scheme for the spherically symmetric time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approximation applied to nuclear giant monopole resonances, extending previous work by accounting for the long-ranged Coulomb potential in the exterior region, with the inverse Laplace transform of the ABC kernel approximated via a non-linear least squares method rather than an exact analytic inversion.

## Method

The authors split the spatial domain into an interior region containing the short-range Skyrme potentials and an exterior region containing only the long-range Coulomb and centrifugal potentials, assuming zero density in the exterior. They derive an absorbing boundary condition at the artificial boundary \(r=R\) by applying a Laplace transform to the exterior Schrödinger-like equation, obtaining a Whittaker function kernel whose inverse Laplace transform is approximated using a non-linear least squares bootstrap method that represents the kernel as a sum of poles. The TDHF equations are solved numerically using finite differences with a Crank-Nicholson time-stepping scheme, and the discretized ABC is applied at the boundary to prevent unphysical reflections.

## Key numbers

- Skyrme parameters used: \(t_0 = -1090.0\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(t_3 = 17288.0\) MeV fm\(^6\).
- Coulomb constant: \(\eta = e^2/(4\pi\epsilon_0) \approx 1.44\) e\(^2\) MeV\(^{-1}\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The simplified Skyrme interaction reproduces magic numbers for \(^4\)He, \(^{16}\)O, and \(^{40}\)Ca.
- The non-linear least squares procedure uses 41 discretization points for the integral and iterates \(i_{\text{max}} = 5\) times.

## Context

This work extends the previous ABC implementation for TDHF by Stevenson et al. (2013) (PhysRevC.87.014330) by incorporating the long-ranged Coulomb potential into the exterior boundary condition, which was previously neglected. The method enables more efficient and accurate calculations of giant monopole resonances in doubly magic nuclei without requiring excessively large spatial domains, and is relevant to studies of nuclear incompressibility, the equation of state, neutron stars, supernovae, and heavy-ion collisions.
