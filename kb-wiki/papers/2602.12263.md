# Systematic Operator Construction for Non-relativistic Effective Field Theories: Hilbert Series versus Young Tensor

> This work establishes a systematic framework for operator construction in the non-relativistic effective field theory, incorporating both the three dimensional Euclidean symmetry and the internal symmetries. By employing double cover of the rotation group, we extend the Hilbert series to the non-relativistic systems, and eliminates redundancies introduced by the spin operator. We also generalize the Young tensor method to the non-relativistic cases through the $SU(2)$ semi-standard Young tableaux, which allows for the construction of operator bases with repeated fields at any given mass dimension. Utilizing the Young tensor technique and Hibert series as cross-check, we obtain the complete operator bases for the following cases: heavy particle (and also heavy quark) effective theory operators up to mass dimension 9; pion-less effective theory operators, including nucleon-nucleon contact interactions up to $\mathcal{O}(Q^4)$ and three-nucleon interactions at $\mathcal{O}(Q^2)$; and finally the spin-1/2 dark matter-nucleon operators up to $\mathcal{O}(v^4)$.

## Key claim
This work establishes a systematic framework for constructing complete operator bases in non-relativistic effective field theories by extending both the Hilbert series and Young tensor methods to handle three-dimensional Euclidean symmetry with internal symmetries, while eliminating redundancies from spin operators and Fierz identities that plagued previous approaches.

## Method
The authors employ the double cover of the rotation group (Spin(3) ≅ SU(2)) to extend the Hilbert series method to non-relativistic systems, removing redundancies introduced by explicit spin operators. They generalize the Young tensor method using SU(2) semi-standard Young tableaux, which allows construction of operator bases with repeated fields at any given mass dimension while automatically handling symmetrization and eliminating redundancies from equations of motion, integration by parts, and Fierz identities. The two methods are used as cross-checks to obtain complete operator bases for multiple non-relativistic effective field theories.

## Context
This work extends the Hilbert series method previously applied to HQET by Kobach (2017, 2018) and the Young tensor method developed by Li (2020, 2022) to non-relativistic systems with Euclidean symmetry. It addresses limitations in prior approaches: the Hilbert series method using explicit spin operators failed for multi-fermion sectors, while manual construction methods by Gunawardana (2017) for HQET and Girlanda (2011) for pionless EFT faced growing challenges at higher orders. The framework enables systematic construction of operator bases for heavy particle effective theory, pionless effective theory, and dark matter-nucleon interactions, with automatic handling of discrete symmetries (P and T) that previously required manual imposition.
