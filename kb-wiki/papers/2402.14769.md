# Generalized eikonal identities for charged currents

> We discuss QED radiative corrections to contact operators coupling two heavy fields and one light field. New eikonal identities are derived in the static limit that demonstrate the equivalence of a class of ladder graphs to an equivalent theory with a single heavy-light vertex and a background Coulomb field which communicates exclusively with the light field. We apply these new identities to nuclear beta decays and demonstrates that the "independent particle model" used by Jaus, Rasche, Sirlin \& Zucchini is closely related, though not identical, to a model independent EFT calculation.

## Key claim

The paper derives new eikonal identities for charged-current processes (where the heavy particle changes its electric charge) in the static limit, showing that a class of ladder graphs is equivalent to a theory with a single heavy-light vertex and a background Coulomb field. The "independent particle model" used by Jaus, Rasche, Sirlin and Zucchini is closely related, though not identical, to a model-independent effective field theory calculation.

## Method

The author uses partial fraction identities and combinatorics to sum over all permutations of soft photon attachments to heavy particles in the static limit (neglecting nuclear recoil, setting v_A = v_B = v). The analysis treats heavy particles as point-like with eikonal propagators 1/(v·q + i0) and applies to contact operators coupling two heavy fields (charges Z and Z+z) and one light field. The resulting identities are then applied to nuclear beta decay by constructing a heavy particle effective field theory (HPET) Lagrangian and performing a decoupling transformation using Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear charges in superallowed beta decays range from Z=6 (for ¹⁰C) to Z=37 (for ⁷⁴Rb)
- At three loops (O(Z²α³)) without the eikonal identities, 144 diagrams would need evaluation; with the identities, only 10 graphs are required for the amputated amplitude
- The charge difference z = Z_B - Z_A is the residual charge of the heavy particle after decoupling

## Context

This work extends eikonal identities (Yennie, Frautschi and Suura, 1961; Weinberg, 1965) to charged-current processes where the heavy particle changes its electric charge, which had not been systematically treated before. The identities are applied to superallowed beta decays, which provide the most precise extraction of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element |V_ud| (Hardy, 2020). The paper compares the model-independent EFT approach to the historical independent particle model of Jaus (1970, 1972) and Sirlin and Zucchini (1986), finding that the independent particle model reproduces the correct long-distance behavior but may contain spurious short-range contributions. This enables systematic calculation of O(Z²α³) corrections to superallowed beta decays and simplifies analysis of QED corrections at high loop order for charged-current processes.
