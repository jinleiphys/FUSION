# Electromagnetic structure of A=2 and 3 nuclei in chiral effective field theory

> The objectives of the present work are twofold. The first is to address and resolve some of the differences present in independent, chiral-effective-field-theory (\chiEFT) derivations up to one loop, recently appeared in the literature, of the nuclear charge and current operators. The second objective is to provide a complete set of \chiEFT predictions for the structure functions and tensor polarization of the deuteron, for the charge and magnetic form factors of 3He and 3H, and for the charge and magnetic radii of these few-nucleon systems. The calculations use wave functions derived from high-order chiral two- and three-nucleon potentials and Monte Carlo methods to evaluate the relevant matrix elements. Predictions based on conventional potentials in combination with \chiEFT charge and current operators are also presented. There is excellent agreement between theory and experiment for all these observables for momentum transfers up to q< 2.0-2.5 (1/fm); for a subset of them, this agreement extends to momentum transfers as high as q~5-6 (1/fm). A complete analysis of the results is provided.

## Key claim

The paper resolves differences between independent chiral-effective-field-theory (χEFT) derivations of nuclear charge and current operators up to one loop, and provides predictions for electromagnetic observables of A=2 and 3 nuclei that show excellent agreement with experiment for momentum transfers up to q ≲ 2.0–2.5 fm⁻¹, with agreement extending to q ≃ 5–6 fm⁻¹ for a subset of observables.

## Method

The authors derived nuclear charge and current operators in χEFT up to one loop (order e Q), resolving discrepancies between previous time-ordered perturbation theory derivations by Pastore et al. (2009, 2011) and Kölling et al. (2009, 2011). They computed deuteron structure functions and tensor polarization, plus charge and magnetic form factors and radii of ³He and ³H, using wave functions from high-order chiral two- and three-nucleon potentials and Monte Carlo methods to evaluate matrix elements, with the hyperspherical harmonics expansion for trinucleon wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range for excellent agreement with experiment: q ≲ 2.0–2.5 fm⁻¹ for all observables; for a subset, agreement extends to q ≃ 5–6 fm⁻¹
- Normalization values: G_C(0)=1, G_M(0)=(m_d/m_N) μ_d, G_Q(0)=m_d² Q_d for deuteron; F_C(0)=1, F_M(0)=μ for trinucleons
- Nucleon form factor normalizations: G_E^S(0)=G_E^V(0)=1, G_M^S(0)=0.880 μ_N, G_M^V(0)=4.706 μ_N
- γNΔ electromagnetic form factor parameters: μ_{γNΔ}=3 μ_N, Λ_{Δ,1}=0.84 GeV, Λ_{Δ,2}=1.2 GeV
- Momentum cutoff parameter Λ in range 500–600 MeV for regularizing two-body operators
- Monte Carlo integration required ~100,000 configurations for statistical errors of a few percent at highest q values

## Context

This work extends earlier χEFT studies of deuteron electromagnetic structure by Walzl (2001), Phillips (2003, 2007), and Kölling et al. (2012) (which focused on the B(q) structure function), while providing the first one-loop χEFT predictions for ³He and ³H elastic form factors. The calculations resolve differences between the independent derivations of Pastore et al. (2009, 2011) and Kölling et al. (2009, 2011) for the two-pion-exchange charge and current operators, and enable future applications of these consistent operators to heavier nuclei and electroweak processes.
