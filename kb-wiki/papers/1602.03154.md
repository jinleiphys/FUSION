# Constraints on large-$x$ parton distributions from new weak boson production and deep-inelastic scattering data

> We present a new set of leading twist parton distribution functions, referred to as "CJ15", which take advantage of developments in the theoretical treatment of nuclear corrections as well as new data. The analysis includes for the first time data on the free neutron structure function from Jefferson Lab, and new high-precision charged lepton and W-boson asymmetry data from Fermilab. These significantly reduce the uncertainty on the d/u ratio at large values of x and provide new insights into the partonic structure of bound nucleons.

## Key claim

The CJ15 analysis presents a new set of leading twist parton distribution functions that significantly reduce the uncertainty on the \(d/u\) ratio at large \(x\) by including for the first time data on the free neutron structure function from Jefferson Lab and new high-precision charged lepton and \(W\)-boson asymmetry data from Fermilab, with the caveat that the nuclear corrections in deuterium remain the main source of model dependence and are now treated with a phenomenological fit of off-shell parameters directly to data rather than relying solely on physically-motivated models.

## Method

The analysis performs a global QCD fit at next-to-leading order (NLO) to over 4500 data points from deep-inelastic scattering (BCDMS, SLAC, NMC, HERA, HERMES, Jefferson Lab), Drell-Yan production (E866 at Fermilab), and \(W\)-boson and lepton charge asymmetry data from the CDF and D0 collaborations at the Tevatron. Nuclear corrections in deuterium are implemented via a convolution formalism using several modern deuteron wave functions (AV18, CD-Bonn, WJC-1, WJC-2) and a phenomenological parametrization of nucleon off-shell effects with parameters fitted to data, replacing the earlier approach of using fixed physically-motivated models. The analysis also includes target mass corrections and higher twist corrections parametrized as a polynomial in \(x\), and implements the S-ACOT scheme for heavy quark treatment.

## Key numbers

* The global fit includes over 4500 data points from a variety of high energy scattering processes.
* Cuts on DIS data are applied at \(Q^2 > Q_0^2 = 1.69\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W^2 > 3\) GeV\(^2\).
* The charm and bottom quark masses are taken as \(m_c = 1.3\) GeV and \(m_b = 4.5\) GeV, respectively.
* The strange quark sea is fixed at \(\kappa = (s + \bar s)/(\bar u + \bar d) = 0.4\).
* The BONuS experiment contributed around 200 data points on the ratio of neutron to deuteron \(F_2\) structure functions up to \(x \approx 0.6\).
* The E866 Drell-Yan data are cut at dimuon masses \(M_{\mu^+\mu^-} > 6\) GeV, reducing the data set from 375 to 250 points.
* The new D0 \(W\)-boson asymmetry data have about 10 times larger integrated luminosity than the earlier CDF measurement and extend over a larger rapidity range up to \(\approx 3\).

## Context

This work extends the CJ series of global PDF analyses (CJ10, CJ11, CJ12) by incorporating new Jefferson Lab BONuS data on the free neutron structure function, which avoids the large nuclear uncertainties that afflicted previous neutron extractions from deuterium targets (as discussed in MT96 and KMK09). The analysis also resolves the tension between lepton and \(W\)-boson asymmetry data that was evident in the CJ12 analysis, and enables more precise determination of the \(d/u\) ratio at large \(x\) with reduced model dependence in nuclear corrections, paving the way for future constraints from the SeaQuest experiment (E906) at Fermilab and LHC data on Drell-Yan and \(W\)-boson production.
