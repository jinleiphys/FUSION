# Effective Field Theories of Light Nuclei

> Effective field theories have been developed for the description of light, shallow nuclei. I review results for two- and three-nucleon systems, and discuss their extension to halo nuclei.

## Key claim
Effective field theories (EFTs) can systematically describe light, shallow nuclei by exploiting the separation between a low momentum scale (associated with shallow bound states or resonances) and a high momentum scale (the pion mass or core excitation energy), but the power counting requires fine-tuning and, in the three-nucleon system, a three-body force that exhibits limit-cycle renormalization.

## Method
The author reviews the "pionless" EFT for two- and three-nucleon systems, where the Lagrangian contains only nucleon fields with contact interactions and auxiliary "dimeron" fields to handle fine-tuned shallow states. For halo nuclei, the EFT is generalized to include a core field (e.g., an alpha particle) and dimeron fields for each partial wave, with power counting determined by the relative sizes of the low-energy scale (halo binding or resonance energy) and the high-energy scale (core excitation). The EFT is applied to neutron-alpha scattering, fitting parameters to phase-shift data and comparing cross sections with and without resummation of bubble diagrams near the resonance.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: 1.91 MeV at NLO (experimental: 2.22 MeV).
- Triton binding energy: 8.31 MeV at NLO (experimental: 8.48 MeV).
- Quartet (s3/2) nucleon-deuteron scattering length: 6.33 ± 0.10 fm (experimental: 6.35 ± 0.02 fm).
- Low-energy scales for neutron-alpha scattering: M_lo ~ 30 MeV, M_hi ~ 100 MeV.
- Pionless EFT valid for momenta Q ≤ m_pi (about 140 MeV); neutron-alpha EFT reproduces data up to neutron energies of about 0.5 MeV at LO and 0.8 MeV at NNLO (without resonance resummation).

## Context
This work extends the pionless EFT developed by van Kolck and collaborators (e.g., Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck) for few-nucleon systems to halo nuclei, following earlier studies of two- and three-nucleon systems using effective-range expansion and dimeron fields (Kaplan 1997). It enables systematic calculations of halo nuclei such as 6He (as 4He + n + n) and reactions like p + 7Be → 8B + gamma, providing a QCD-based framework for nuclear astrophysics near the driplines. The approach contrasts with chiral EFTs that include pions explicitly (reviewed in the same paper) and with phenomenological potential models (e.g., Kievsky).
