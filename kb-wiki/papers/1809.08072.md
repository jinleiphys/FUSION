# Pentaquark states with the $QQQq\bar{q}$ configuration in a simple model

> We discuss the mass splittings for the $S$-wave triply heavy pentaquark states with the $QQQq\bar{q}$ $(Q=b,c;q=u,d,s)$ configuration which is a mirror structure of $Q\bar{Q}qqq$. The latter configuration is related with the nature of $P_c(4380)$ observed by the LHCb Collaboration. The considered pentaquark masses are roughly estimated with a simple method. One finds that such states are probably not narrow even if they do exist. This leaves room for molecule interpretation for a state around the low-lying threshold of a doubly heavy baryon and a heavy-light meson, e.g. $Ξ_{cc}D$, if it were observed. As a by product, we conjecture that upper limits for the masses of the conventional triply heavy baryons can be determined by the masses of the conventional doubly heavy baryons.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that triply heavy pentaquark states with the \(QQQq\bar{q}\) configuration are probably not narrow even if they exist, which leaves room for a molecular interpretation for any state observed near the threshold of a doubly heavy baryon and a heavy-light meson, such as \(\Xi_{cc}D\).

## Method

The authors constructed spin-color wave functions for S-wave \(QQQq\bar{q}\) pentaquarks (with \(Q=b,c\) and \(q=u,d,s\)) and calculated chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) matrices for systems with total angular momentum \(J=1/2, 3/2, 5/2\). They extracted effective quark masses and coupling constants \(C_{ij}\) from the mass splittings of conventional hadrons, then estimated pentaquark masses using both a direct sum of effective masses and a reference threshold method based on baryon-meson thresholds with the same quark content.

## Key numbers

* For the \(cccn\bar{n}\) system, the lowest \(J^P = 1/2^-\) state has an estimated mass of 5910 MeV (direct method) or 5638 MeV (using the \(\Xi_{cc}D\) threshold).
* For the \(cccn\bar{n}\) system, the \(J^P = 3/2^-\) state has an estimated mass of 5922 MeV (direct method) or 5650 MeV (using the \(\Xi_{cc}D\) threshold).
* For the \(bbbn\bar{n}\) system, the lowest \(J^P = 1/2^-\) state has an estimated mass of 15891 MeV (direct method) or 15422 MeV (using the \(\Xi_{bb}\bar{B}\) threshold).
* For the \(bbbn\bar{n}\) system, the \(J^P = 3/2^-\) state has an estimated mass of 15888 MeV (direct method) or 15418 MeV (using the \(\Xi_{bb}\bar{B}\) threshold).
* For the \(ccbn\bar{n}\) system, the lowest \(J^P = 1/2^-\) eigenvalue is -95.0 MeV (CMI eigenvalue), corresponding to a mass of 9130 MeV (direct method), 8868 MeV (using the \(\Xi_{cc}\bar{B}\) threshold), or 8736 MeV (using the \(\Xi_{bc}D\) threshold).
* The effective quark masses extracted from conventional hadrons are \(m_n = 361.7\) MeV, \(m_s = 540.3\) MeV, \(m_c = 1724.6\) MeV, and \(m_b = 5052.8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of hidden-charm pentaquarks with the \(qqqc\bar{c}\) configuration (related to the \(P_c(4380)\) state observed by LHCb) to the mirror configuration \(QQQq\bar{q}\) with three heavy quarks. The authors note that previous studies (Wu et al., 2017) found that mixing effects in the \(udsQ\bar{Q}\) system could lower pentaquark masses, but here the \(QQQq\bar{q}\) states are estimated to lie above the relevant baryon-meson thresholds, suggesting they are unlikely to be narrow. The results enable future experimental searches for triply heavy baryons versus pentaquarks, and the authors conjecture that upper limits for conventional triply heavy baryon masses can be determined from doubly heavy baryon masses.
