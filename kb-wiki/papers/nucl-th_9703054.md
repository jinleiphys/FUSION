# Parity-violating quasifree electron-deuteron scattering in a covariant approach

> The role of relativistic corrections associated with lower Dirac components of the deuteron wavefunction, is examined for parity-violating (PV) electron scattering. The relation between these corrections and negative energy components of the struck nucleon in relativistic PWIA is elucidated. The model dependence induced by describing such effects, using different deuteron vertex functions and prescriptions for the half-off-shell nucleon vertices, is compared against the precision required for studies of nucleon strangeness in quasielastic PV $e-d$ scattering.

## Key claim

Relativistic corrections from the lower Dirac components of the deuteron wavefunction, which are not included in the standard semirelativistic (factorized) plane-wave impulse approximation (FPWIA), produce residual effects on the parity-violating asymmetry that are less than 1% for the kinematics of interest, provided the struck nucleon is near its mass shell (i.e., on the quasielastic peak).

## Method

The author calculates the parity-violating (PV) electron-deuteron scattering asymmetry using a covariant relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), where the hadronic tensor is built from a covariant deuteron vertex function and a half-off-shell nucleon vertex. The RPWIA result is then decomposed into contributions from positive-energy (forward-propagating) and negative-energy (backward-propagating) components of the struck nucleon propagator, showing that the standard factorized PWIA (FPWIA) corresponds to a projection onto the positive-energy sector only. The model dependence is tested by using different deuteron vertex functions (from the spectator-equation solutions of Buck and Gross, labeled by a parameter λ that interpolates between pseudovector and pseudoscalar πNN coupling) and two different prescriptions for the half-off-shell nucleon vertex (Γ₁ and Γ₂).

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is |b| ≈ 2.22 MeV, and the deuteron Fermi momentum is k_F ≈ 60 MeV/c.
- At momentum transfer |q| > 0.7 GeV/c, earlier nonrelativistic calculations found relativistic effects of 6% in the PV asymmetry; the present covariant calculation shows that the residual relativistic effects (beyond the factorized PWIA) are less than 1% for the kinematics of interest.
- The off-shellness of the struck nucleon near threshold can be 24%, 63%, and 115% for |q| = 1, 2, and 4 GeV/c, respectively, but on the quasielastic peak the lower integration limit |y| = 0, so small-momentum components dominate.
- The P-state contributions to the positive-energy-projected momentum distribution ρ₊₊ cancel exactly, leaving ρ₊₊ = (u² + w²)/(4π), identical to the nonrelativistic momentum distribution up to normalization.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hadjimichael et al. (1990s), who estimated that relativistic effects (rather than final-state interactions) are the main source of model dependence in the PV asymmetry for quasifree kinematics at |q| > 0.7 GeV/c, but used a nonrelativistic expansion for the deuteron. The present covariant calculation, following the approach of Keister and Tjon (1990s) and Beck et al. (1990s), shows that the residual relativistic effects from a fully relativistic deuteron description are small (below 1%) on the quasielastic peak, validating the use of the simpler factorized PWIA for extracting nucleon strangeness form factors from PV e-d experiments at TJNAF and MIT-Bates. This result is important for planning experiments such as SAMPLE (phase II) and TJNAF E91-017, and it suggests that semirelativistic PWIA may be reliable for heavier nuclei at high momentum transfer, though the effects may be larger due to stronger binding.
