# All-charm tetraquarks at hadron colliders: A high-precision fragmentation perspective

> We present the TQ4Q2.0 fragmentation functions for the production of all-heavy (fully heavy) $S$-wave tetraquarks ($T_{4Q}$) with scalar ($0^{++}$), axial-vector ($1^{+-}$), and tensor ($2^{++}$) quantum numbers in high-energy hadronic collisions. This work extends the previous TQ4Q1.1 framework by incorporating nonconstituent heavy-quark contributions and introducing a replica-based uncertainty-quantification strategy derived from multi-scale variations (MHOUs). The construction follows a nonrelativistic QCD factorization approach, combining gluon- and heavy-quark-initiated fragmentation channels at leading power. Initial-scale inputs are modeled through updated potential-inspired wave functions, while the subsequent DGLAP evolution is performed via the threshold-aware HF-NRevo scheme. A comprehensive systematic analysis of uncertainties is carried out, with contributions from color-composite long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) and perturbative multiscale inputs. The resulting TQ4Q2.0 grids, publicly released in LHAPDF6 format, provide the first complete phenomenological set for all-heavy exotics, enabling precise studies of all-charm tetraquark production and jet-associated observables within the JETHAD environment. This article completes the high-energy resummation-driven generation of the TQ4Q program and establishes a definitive baseline for future collider-oriented analyses of all-heavy multiquark dynamics.

## Key claim

The TQ4Q2.0 fragmentation functions provide the first complete phenomenological set for all-heavy tetraquark production, but their reliability is restricted to the high-transverse-momentum regime (p_T greater than about 20 GeV for all-charm and greater than about 50 GeV for all-bottom tetraquarks), where collinear fragmentation dominates over multiparton interactions and power corrections are suppressed.

## Method

The fragmentation functions are constructed within a nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization approach, combining gluon- and heavy-quark-initiated fragmentation channels at leading power, and extending the previous TQ4Q1.1 framework by incorporating nonconstituent heavy-quark contributions. Initial-scale inputs are modeled through updated potential-inspired wave functions, with color-composite long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) derived from potential models, while the subsequent DGLAP evolution is performed via the threshold-aware HF-NRevo scheme. A replica-based uncertainty-quantification strategy is introduced, derived from multi-scale variations (MHOUs), and the resulting grids are publicly released in LHAPDF6 format.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-quark mass for charm: m_c = 1.5 GeV; for bottom: m_b = 4.9 GeV
- All-charm tetraquark mass: M_T4c approximately 4 m_c, about 6.5 GeV
- All-bottom tetraquark mass: M_T4b approximately 4 m_b, about 18 GeV
- Fragmentation region identified as p_T greater than about 20 GeV for all-charm tetraquarks and p_T greater than about 50 GeV for all-bottom tetraquarks
- Gluon fragmentation dominance for J/psi production sets in at p_T greater than about 10-15 GeV
- Charmed B meson fragmentation thresholds up to about 80 GeV

## Context

This work extends the previous TQ4Q1.1 framework (Celiberto, 2025) by incorporating nonconstituent heavy-quark contributions and introducing a replica-based uncertainty-quantification strategy. The construction follows the NRQCD-based approach developed by Feng (2020) and Bai (2024) for all-heavy tetraquark fragmentation, and runs in parallel with well-established methodologies developed in the study of collinear and multidimensional hadron structure by Kassabov (2022), Harland-Lang (2018), Ball (2021), and the NNPDF collaboration (2024). The resulting TQ4Q2.0 grids enable precise studies of all-charm tetraquark production and jet-associated observables within the JETHAD environment, completing the high-energy resummation-driven generation of the TQ4Q program and establishing a baseline for future collider-oriented analyses at the (HL-)LHC and Future Circular Collider.
