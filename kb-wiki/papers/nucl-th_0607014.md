# Flow Coefficients and Jet Characteristics in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Identifying jets in heavy ion collisions is of significant interest since the properties of jets are expected to get modified because of the formation of quark gluon plasma. The detection of jets is, however, difficult because of large number of non-jet hadrons produced in the collision process. In this work we propose a method of identifying a jet and determining its transverse momentum by means of flow analysis. This has been done an event-by-event basis.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to identify jets and determine their transverse momentum, opening angle, and particle multiplicity on an event-by-event basis using Fourier flow coefficients, but the method's reliability depends on the jet having a sufficiently large number of particles relative to background fluctuations and a small opening angle (roughly \(m \Delta \phi / \sqrt{12} < 1\) for the expansion to hold).

## Method

The authors compute the squared flow coefficients \(v_m^2\) and transverse-momentum-weighted flow coefficients \(v_{m,p_T}^2\) from the azimuthal distribution of charged particles in simulated Pb+Pb collisions at 5.5 TeV/nucleon using the HIJING event generator. They fit a straight line \(b - c m^2\) to the computed \(v_m^2\) versus \(m^2\) and extract the number of jet particles from the intercept (\(N_j^2 = b N^2\)) and the jet opening angle from the slope (\(\Delta \phi^2 = 12c/b\)); the \(p_T\)-weighted coefficients similarly yield the jet transverse momentum. The analysis is performed for events with one embedded jet and for events with two back-to-back jets, using \(p_T\) cuts of 0.5, 0.75, and 1.0 GeV on background particles.

## Key numbers

*   For an input of 10 jet particles with jet \(p_T = 18.26\) GeV and opening angle \(\pi/6\) radians, with a 1.0 GeV \(p_T\) cut, the extracted number of jet particles is \(10.52 \pm 1.37\), the extracted jet \(p_T\) is \(18.17 \pm 1.75\) GeV, and the extracted opening angle is \(0.43 \pm 0.09\) radians (with \(p_T\) weight) and \(0.46 \pm 0.08\) radians (without \(p_T\) weight).
*   For the same input but with a 0.5 GeV \(p_T\) cut, the extracted number of jet particles is \(14.11 \pm 3.46\) (systematically 40% higher than input), while the extracted jet \(p_T\) remains \(18.14 \pm 3.11\) GeV (within 5% of input).
*   For events with only background particles (no jet), the flow coefficients are generally smaller than 0.005 (constant weight) and 0.015 (\(p_T\)-weighted), setting the scale for background.
*   For a single jet with background, \(v_2^2\) is larger than 0.1, which is an order of magnitude larger than the expected dynamical elliptic flow contribution of \(\sim 0.01-0.02\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior method (Sahu 2004) for jet identification using flow coefficients by incorporating transverse-momentum weighting to estimate the jet's transverse momentum, not just its particle multiplicity and opening angle. The method is designed to overcome the difficulty of standard jet reconstruction algorithms (cone or \(k_T\) algorithms) in heavy ion collisions, where a large background of non-jet hadrons obscures the jet. The approach enables event-by-event jet characterization and can distinguish between single-jet events (indicative of complete jet quenching) and two-jet events (no quenching) via the odd-even pattern of flow coefficients, which could be applied to data from RHIC and LHC experiments.
