# $πNN^*(1440)$ and $σNN^*(1440)$ coupling constants from a microscopic $NN \to NN^*(1440)$ potential

> By means of a $NN \to NN^*(1440)$ transition potential derived in a parameter-free way from a quark model, we determined simultaneously the $πNN^*(1440)$ and $σNN^*(1440)$ coupling constants.

## Key claim
The paper determines the πNN*(1440) and σNN*(1440) coupling constants simultaneously from a quark-model-derived NN → NN*(1440) transition potential with no free parameters, but the extracted values depend on the choice of vertex form factor and coupling scheme at the baryon level.

## Method
The authors start from a quark-quark interaction containing confinement, one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange (OPE), and one-sigma exchange (OSE) terms, with parameters previously fixed from NN scattering data. They compute the NN → NN*(1440) transition potential via a Born-Oppenheimer approximation by sandwiching the quark-quark potential between NN and NN*(1440) states written in terms of quarks. The asymptotic (R ≥ 4 fm) behavior of the OPE and OSE parts of this potential is then matched to baryon-level Yukawa forms to extract the coupling constants.

## Key numbers
- |gπNN*(1440) / gπNN| = 0.25
- |gσNN*(1440) / gσNN| = 0.475
- gπNN*(1440) / √(4π) = -0.94 (absolute value quoted due to arbitrary phase choice)
- gπNN² / (4π) = 14.8 (experimental value used to fix the chiral coupling constant αch = 0.027)
- Λ = 4.2 fm⁻¹ (cutoff parameter)
- Λ²/(Λ² - mπ²) = 1.0286 (vertex factor for pion, close to 1)
- The ratio of the two ratios: gπNN*(1440)/gπNN = 0.53 × gσNN*(1440)/gσNN

## Context
This work extends the same quark-model approach previously applied to NN scattering (Fernandez et al., 1993; Valcarce et al., 1994) and to Δ excitation (Juliá-Díaz et al., 2001) to the Roper resonance N*(1440). The ratio |gπNN*(1440)/gπNN| = 0.25 is similar to that obtained by another quark-model calculation (Nue et al., 1998) but a factor 1.5 smaller than values from partial decay width analyses. The ratio |gσNN*(1440)/gσNN| = 0.475 agrees well with the only experimental result available, from fits to p(α,α') cross sections in the 10-15 GeV region (Dieperink et al., 1999). The results enable future dynamical calculations including the N*(1440) width and center-of-mass corrections.
