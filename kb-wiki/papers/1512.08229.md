# Strange matter and strange stars in a thermodynamically self-consistent perturbation model with running coupling and running strange quark mass

> A quark model with running coupling and running strange quark mass, which is thermodynamically self-consistent at both high and lower densities, is presented and applied to study properties of strange quark matter and structure of compact stars. An additional term to the thermodynamic potential density is determined by meeting the fundamental differential equation of thermodynamics. It plays an important role in comparatively lower density and ignorable at extremely high density, acting as a chemical-potential dependent bag constant. In this thermodynamically enhanced perturbative QCD model, strange quark matter still has the possibility of being absolutely stable, while the pure quark star has a sharp surface with a maximum mass as large as about 2 times the solar mass and a maximum radius of about 11 kilometers.

# Strange matter and strange stars in a thermodynamically self-consistent perturbation model with running coupling and running strange quark mass

## Key claim

Strange quark matter can still be absolutely stable in a thermodynamically enhanced perturbative QCD model that adds a chemical-potential-dependent bag-like term to restore thermodynamic consistency at lower densities, but only for a specific range of the two free parameters (C and B0) that excludes the parameter region where two-flavor quark matter would be stable.

## Method

The authors start from the first-order perturbative QCD thermodynamic potential for cold quark matter with massless up and down quarks and massive strange quarks, using running coupling and running strange quark mass from renormalization group equations at one-loop level. They then determine an additional term Omega' to the thermodynamic potential density by requiring that the fundamental differential equation of thermodynamics (dOmega = -sum_i n_i d mu_i at zero temperature) be satisfied, which fixes the relation between the renormalization subtraction point u and the chemical potentials through an implicit equation involving the model parameter C. The equation of state is obtained by numerically solving the chemical equilibrium, charge neutrality, and baryon number conservation conditions together with the implicit equation for u, with the constant B0 as a second free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of pure quark stars: about 2 times the solar mass (approximately 2 M_sun)
- Maximum radius of pure quark stars: about 11 kilometers
- QCD scale parameter for three flavors at one-loop level: Lambda_3 = 146.2 MeV
- Strange quark mass scale parameter for three flavors at one-loop level: hat_m_s,3 = 279.9 MeV
- Three representative parameter sets in the absolutely stable region: (1/C, B0^(1/4)/MeV) = (0.5, 135), (0.4, 140), (0.8, 125)
- Energy per baryon threshold for absolute stability of strange quark matter: less than 930 MeV
- Energy per baryon threshold for metastability: between 930 MeV and 939 MeV
- Starting point for the path integral of Omega': mu_u0 = mu_d0 = mu_s0 = 313 MeV

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamically enhanced perturbative QCD (EPQ) model previously developed for one-flavor (Peng 2005) and massless two-flavor (Xu 2014) cases to the realistic three-flavor case with massive strange quarks. It addresses the thermodynamic inconsistency that arises when naively extrapolating perturbative QCD to lower densities, a problem also encountered in quasiparticle models (Schertler 1997, Gorenstein 1995) and equivparticle models (Peng 2008, Xia 2014). The resulting equation of state enables calculations of strange star structure with maximum masses reaching about 2 solar masses, comparable to the most massive observed neutron stars, and provides a framework for future studies of hybrid stars and the deconfinement phase transition.
