# Hyperon-nuclear interactions from SU(3) chiral effective field theory

> The interaction between hyperons and nucleons has a wide range of applications in strangeness nuclear physics and is a topic of continuing great interest. These interactions are not only important for hyperon-nucleon scattering but also essential as basic input to studies of hyperon-nuclear few- and many-body systems including hypernuclei and neutron star matter. We review the systematic derivation and construction of such baryonic forces from the symmetries of quantum chromodynamics within non-relativistic SU(3) chiral effective field theory. Several applications of the resulting potentials are presented for topics of current interest in strangeness nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic derivation of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions within non-relativistic SU(3) chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order, but the resulting potentials are constructed under the assumption of the SU(3) flavor symmetry limit (equal pion, kaon, and eta masses) for the contact-term relations, with explicit symmetry breaking introduced only linearly in the quark masses via terms proportional to \(m_K^2 - m_\pi^2\).

## Method

The baryon-baryon interaction potentials were constructed from contact terms and one- and two-Goldstone-boson exchange diagrams up to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, using a non-relativistic power counting. The contact Lagrangian was expanded to \(\mathcal{O}(q^2)\) and projected onto partial waves, yielding 6 leading-order and 34 next-to-leading-order low-energy constants organized by irreducible SU(3) representations. The one- and two-meson-exchange contributions were computed analytically in the center-of-mass frame with an expansion in inverse baryon mass, and ultraviolet divergences in loop diagrams were treated by dimensional regularization with the scale \(\lambda\).

## Key numbers

* 6 momentum-independent contact terms contribute at leading order, visible only in \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) partial waves.
* 22 next-to-leading-order contact terms contain only baryon fields and derivatives (SU(3) symmetric), and 12 additional NLO terms include the diagonal matrix \(\chi\) producing explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking.
* The SU(3)-breaking terms in S-waves are proportional to \(m_K^2 - m_\pi^2\) and involve 12 constants \(c_\chi^{1,\dots,12}\).
* For the \(NN\) channel, the formalism recovers the well-known 2 leading and 7 next-to-leading order low-energy constants of Epelbaum (2004).
* The three-baryon contact Lagrangian contains 18 low-energy constants \(\lc_1\) through \(\lc_{18}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Epelbaum 2004, Entem 2003) to the full SU(3) baryon octet, enabling systematic studies of hyperon-nucleon scattering and hypernuclear systems. The derived potentials provide a framework for investigating \(\Sigma\Sigma\), \(\Sigma\Xi\), and \(\Xi\Xi\) bound states (Haidenbauer 2015) and for incorporating three-baryon forces into calculations of light hypernuclei (Nogga 2002, Wirth 2014), potentially addressing the hyperon puzzle in neutron star matter.
