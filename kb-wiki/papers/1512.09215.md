# Exploring the QCD Phase Structure with Beam Energy Scan in Heavy-ion Collisions

> Beam energy scan programs in heavy-ion collisions aim to explore the QCD phase structure at high baryon density. Sensitive observables are applied to probe the signatures of the QCD phase transition and critical point in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and SPS. Intriguing structures, such as dip, peak and oscillation, have been observed in the energy dependence of various observables. In this paper, an overview is given and corresponding physics implications will be discussed for the experimental highlights from the beam energy scan programs at the STAR, PHENIX and NA61/SHINE experiments. Furthermore, the beam energy scan phase II at RHIC (2019-2020) and other future experimental facilities for studying the physics at low energies will be also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental highlights from the Beam Energy Scan (BES) programs at RHIC and SPS, arguing that several non-monotonic structures observed in the energy dependence of various observables (dip, peak, oscillation) around 14-20 GeV center-of-mass energy may indicate signatures of the QCD phase transition or critical point, but cautions that definitive conclusions require more statistics from BES-II and careful theoretical modeling.

## Method

The paper surveys experimental results from the STAR, PHENIX, and NA61/SHINE collaborations for Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV (and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV from ALICE). It examines the energy dependence of freeze-out conditions, nuclear modification factor R_CP, directed flow v1, HBT radii, third flow harmonic v3, and higher-order moments of net-proton distributions (kappa sigma^2 and S sigma). The analysis compares these observables to theoretical predictions from lattice QCD, thermal models, and transport models such as UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature saturates at about sqrt(s_NN)=10 GeV with a limiting temperature T_limit ~ 160 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature is nearly constant over sqrt(s_NN)=7.7-39 GeV
- R_CP for charged hadrons at high pT (pT > 3 GeV/c) shows clear suppression for 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, but is larger than unity for lower energies (7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27 GeV)
- Slope of net-proton directed flow shows a minimum around sqrt(s_NN)=14.5 GeV
- v3^2{2} values at 7.7 and 11.5 GeV for 50-60% centrality become consistent with zero
- v3^2{2}/n_ch,PP shows a local minimum around 20 GeV
- kappa sigma^2 of net-proton distributions shows a minimum around 20 GeV for 0-5% centrality, with values deviating significantly below unity at 19.6 and 27 GeV
- S sigma/Skellam for 0-5% centrality shows a peak at 14.5 GeV
- BES-II aims to collect about 100 million minimum bias events for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV
- Fixed target mode at RHIC allows energy coverage from sqrt(s_NN)=3 GeV (mu_B=720 MeV) up to 7.7 GeV

## Context

This review extends the experimental program described in the RHIC BES white paper (2010) and the NA61/SPS program (2011), building on earlier lattice QCD calculations showing a crossover at small baryon chemical potential (2006) and predictions of a first-order phase transition at high baryon density (2007). The observed structures in the energy range 14-20 GeV motivate the BES-II program at RHIC (2019-2020) and future facilities including CBM at FAIR (sqrt(s_NN)=2-8 GeV), J-PARC (sqrt(s_NN)=2-6.2 GeV), and MPD at NICA (sqrt(s_NN)=4-11 GeV). The paper suggests that finer energy scans between 14.5 and 19.6 GeV, particularly at sqrt(s_NN)=16.5 GeV, could help map out the critical region near the QCD critical point.
