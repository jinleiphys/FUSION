# Correlational latent heat by nonlocal quantum kinetic theory

> The kinetic equation of nonlocal and non-instantaneous character unifies the achievements of the transport in dense quantum gases with the Landau theory of quasiclassical transport in Fermi systems. Large cancellations in the off-shell motion appear which are hidden usually in non-Markovian behaviors. The remaining corrections are expressed in terms of shifts in space and time that characterize the non-locality of the scattering process. In this way quantum transport is possible to recast into a quasi-classical picture. The balance equations for the density, momentum, energy and entropy include besides quasiparticle also the correlated two-particle contributions beyond the Landau theory. The medium effects on binary collisions are shown to mediate the latent heat, i.e., an energy conversion between correlation and thermal energy. For Maxwellian particles with time-dependent s-wave scattering, the correlated parts of the observables are calculated and a sign change of the latent heat is reported at a universal ratio of scattering length to the thermal De Broglie wavelength. This is interpreted as a change from correlational heating to cooling.

## Key claim

The paper reports a sign change of the correlational latent heat at a universal ratio of scattering length to thermal De Broglie wavelength, which is independent of the interaction strength and is interpreted as a transition from correlational heating to correlational cooling for Maxwellian particles with time-dependent s-wave scattering.

## Method

The author develops a nonlocal quantum kinetic theory that extends the Boltzmann equation by including space and time shifts derived from the phase shift of the T-matrix, recasting quantum transport into a quasiclassical picture. The balance equations for density, momentum, energy, and entropy are derived including both quasiparticle and correlated two-particle contributions beyond Landau theory. As an exploratory example, the author calculates all corrections analytically for Maxwellian particles with a time-dependent s-wave scattering length, using the free-space T-matrix and performing the momentum integrations with the help of the error function.

## Key numbers

* The sign change of the energy gain relative to external power occurs at \(x_0 \approx 1.8184\), which corresponds to \(a_\mathrm{sc}/\lambda \approx 0.7254\), where \(\lambda\) is the thermal De Broglie wavelength.
* In the high-temperature limit, the molecular energy behaves as \(E^\mathrm{mol} = n^{\rm mol} T \left( \frac{1}{2} + \frac{4}{\sqrt{\pi} x} + o(x^{-2}) \right)\).
* In the low-temperature limit, the molecular energy behaves as \(E^\mathrm{mol} = n^{\rm mol} T \left( \frac{7}{2} - 6 x^2 + o(x^3) \right)\).
* The low-temperature limit of the correlated density is \(n^{\rm mol} = - n_a n_b \lambda^3 \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{4} x^3 + o(x^4) = -2\pi^2 n_a n_b a_\mathrm{sc}^3 + o(T^2)\).
* The high-temperature limit of the correlated density is \(n^{\rm mol}(x) = - n_a n_b \lambda^3 \frac{\pi}{4} + o(x^{-1})\), vanishing with \(T^{-3/2}\).
* For low temperatures (small \(x\)), the ratio \(I_E^{\rm gain}/w_E = -1 + 3 x^2 + o(x)\), indicating correlational cooling.
* For high temperatures, the ratio \(I_E^{\rm gain}/w_E = 1/2 - 2/\sqrt{\pi} x + o(x^{-2})\), indicating correlational heating.

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal kinetic theory previously developed by the author and collaborators (Morawetz et al., 1997-2001) which unifies transport in dense quantum gases with Landau's quasiclassical transport in Fermi systems. The paper provides the first analytical calculation of all nonlocal corrections for a model of time-dependent s-wave scattering, demonstrating that the energy gain acts as a latent heat rate due to correlations. The results are relevant to nonequilibrium quantum systems in cold atoms near Feshbach resonances (Chin, 2010), plasma physics, and nuclear physics, and the correlated density expression reproduces the Beth-Uhlenbeck equation of state (Beth and Uhlenbeck, 1937).
