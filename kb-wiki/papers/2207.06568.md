# Measurement of sequential $Υ$ suppression in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV with the STAR experiment

> We report on measurements of sequential $Υ$ suppression in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV with the STAR detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) through both the dielectron and dimuon decay channels. In the 0-60% centrality class, the nuclear modification factors ($R_{\mathrm{AA}}$), which quantify the level of yield suppression in heavy-ion collisions compared to $p$+$p$ collisions, for $Υ$(1S) and $Υ$(2S) are $0.40 \pm 0.03~\textrm{(stat.)} \pm 0.03~\textrm{(sys.)} \pm 0.09~\textrm{(norm.)}$ and $0.26 \pm 0.08~\textrm{(stat.)} \pm 0.02~\textrm{(sys.)} \pm 0.06~\textrm{(norm.)}$, respectively, while the upper limit of the $Υ$(3S) $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$ is 0.17 at a 95% confidence level. This provides experimental evidence that the $Υ$(3S) is significantly more suppressed than the $Υ$(1S) at RHIC. The level of suppression for $Υ$(1S) is comparable to that observed at the much higher collision energy at the Large Hadron Collider. These results point to the creation of a medium at RHIC whose temperature is sufficiently high to strongly suppress excited $Υ$ states.

## Key claim

The paper reports that in 0-60% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, the Υ(3S) is significantly more suppressed than the Υ(1S), with the Υ(3S) nuclear modification factor upper limit (0.17 at 95% confidence) lying below the Υ(1S) RAA of 0.40, even when the Υ(3S) limit is evaluated at 99% confidence (0.26). The abstract's claim of "sequential suppression" is hedged by noting that the Υ(2S) RAA of 0.26 lies between the Υ(1S) and Υ(3S) values only as a hint, and that the Υ(1S) suppression magnitude is comparable to that at the LHC despite a factor-of-25 difference in collision energy.

## Method

Υ mesons were reconstructed in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using both the dielectron channel (|y|<1, 2.3 nb⁻¹ integrated luminosity, BEMC trigger) and the dimuon channel (|y|<0.5, 27 nb⁻¹ integrated luminosity, MTD dimuon trigger) with the STAR detector. Raw yields were extracted via unbinned maximum-likelihood fits to invariant mass spectra, with lineshapes from GEANT3 simulations tuned to match J/ψ widths in data, and correlated backgrounds from PYTHIA6. The nuclear modification factor RAA was computed by dividing the per-event yield in Au+Au by the pp reference cross section scaled by the average number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions from a Glauber model, combining the two decay channels using inverse-variance weighting.

## Key numbers

- Υ(1S) RAA in 0-60% centrality: 0.40 ± 0.03 (stat.) ± 0.03 (sys.) ± 0.09 (norm.)
- Υ(2S) RAA in 0-60% centrality: 0.26 ± 0.08 (stat.) ± 0.02 (sys.) ± 0.06 (norm.)
- Υ(3S) RAA upper limit in 0-60% centrality: 0.17 at 95% confidence level; 0.26 at 99% confidence level
- pp reference cross section for Υ(1S+2S+3S) at √s = 200 GeV, |y|<0.5: dσ/dy = 75 ± 15 pb
- Interpolated pT shape parameter T for Υ(1S) at √s = 200 GeV: 1.40 ± 0.06 GeV; for Υ(2S): 1.51 ± 0.10 GeV
- Υ(1S) peak widths: 221 MeV/c² (dimuon), 129 MeV/c² (dielectron)

## Context

This measurement extends previous RHIC results from STAR (2013) and U+U collisions (Adamczyk 2016) that showed only a hint (<1.5σ) of stronger suppression for Υ(2S+3S) relative to Υ(1S), now providing separate Υ(2S) and Υ(3S) measurements with improved precision. The sequential suppression pattern is similar to that observed at the LHC by CMS (2018) in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, but with comparable Υ(1S) suppression despite the factor-of-25 energy difference, suggesting that primordial Υ(1S) are not significantly suppressed in the QGP at either energy. The results enable further constraints on model calculations such as the Open Quantum System plus pNRQCD approach (Brambilla 2021, 2022) and transport models (Du 2017), and improve understanding of the in-medium heavy quark-antiquark potential and QGP thermodynamic properties at RHIC.
