# Two quasiparticle wobbling in the even-even nucleus 130Ba

> Two newly observed bands built on a two-quasiparticle configuration in 130Ba have been investigated for the first time with the microscopic projected shell model. The experimental energy spectra and the available electromagnetic transition probabilities are well reproduced. The wobbling character of the higher band is revealed by the angular momentum projected wavefunctions via the K plot and the azimuthal plot. This provides the first strong microscopic evidence for wobbling motion based on a two-quasiparticle configuration in even-even nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first strong microscopic evidence for wobbling motion based on a two-quasiparticle configuration in an even-even nucleus, specifically in 130Ba. The wobbling character is demonstrated through angular momentum projected wavefunctions via the K plot and azimuthal plot, though the authors note that unlike an ideal wobbler, the B(M1)/B(E2) ratios are comparable due to the involvement of two h11/2 quasiparticles.

## Method

The authors used the projected shell model (PSM), which performs shell-model configuration mixing based on a Nilsson mean field with angular momentum projection, starting from a pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian. They constructed two-quasiparticle states from proton h11/2 orbitals and diagonalized the Hamiltonian in the space of projected states to obtain energy spectra and wavefunctions. The wobbling geometry was then analyzed using K plots (probability distributions of angular momentum components on the three intrinsic axes) and azimuthal plots (probability distributions of the polar and azimuthal angles of the angular momentum in the intrinsic frame).

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters chosen as (β, γ) = (0.24, 30°)
- Monopole pairing strength: GM = 0.15 MeV for neutrons and GM = 0.16 MeV for protons
- Quadrupole pairing strength GQ assumed to be 0.2 × GM
- Bandhead at I = 10ħ for the zero-phonon band
- At I = 10ħ, the angular momentum is mainly along the short axis with K ∼ 10ħ on that axis
- At I = 23ħ, the components of angular momentum on the intermediate and short axes become comparable, suggesting proximity to a critical spin

## Context

This work extends the study of wobbling motion from odd-A nuclei (e.g., 161Lu, 163Lu, 165Lu, 167Lu, 167Ta, 135Pr, 105Pd) to a two-quasiparticle configuration in an even-even nucleus, following the experimental identification of bands S1 and S1' in 130Ba by Petrache (2019). The decreasing wobbling energy with increasing spin confirms the "transverse wobbling" mechanism proposed by Frauendorf (2014). The PSM approach had previously been applied to wobbling in 135Pr by Sensharma (2019), but without the geometric illustration of angular momentum orientation that this work provides through K plots and azimuthal plots, techniques introduced by Chen (2017) for chiral doublet bands.
