# Nonparametric model for the equations of state of neutron star from deep neural network

> It is of great interest to understand the equation of state (EOS) of the neutron star (NS), whose core includes highly dense matter. However, there are large uncertainties in the theoretical predictions for the EOS of NS. It is useful to develop a new framework, which is flexible enough to consider the systematic error in theoretical predictions and to use them as a best guess at the same time. We employ a deep neural network to perform a non-parametric fit of the EOS of NS using currently available data. In this framework, the Gaussian process is applied to represent the EOSs and the training set data required to close physical solutions. Our model is constructed under the assumption that the true EOS of NS is a perturbation of the relativistic mean-field model prediction. We fit the EOSs of NS using two different example datasets, which can satisfy the latest constraints from the massive neutron stars, NICER, and the gravitational wave of the binary neutron stars. Given our assumptions, we find that a maximum neutron star mass is $2.38^{+0.15}_{-0.13} M_\odot$ or $2.41^{+0.15}_{-0.14}$ at $95\%$ confidence level from two different example datasets. It implies that the $1.4 M_\odot$ radius is $12.31^{+0.29}_{-0.31}$ km or $12.30^{+0.35}_{-0.37}$ km. These results are consistent with results from previous studies using similar priors. It has demonstrated the recovery of the EOS of NS using a nonparametric model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a deep neural network combined with Gaussian process regression can reconstruct the equation of state of neutron stars in a nonparametric way, but this reconstruction is explicitly conditioned on the assumption that the true EOS is a perturbation of the relativistic mean-field model prediction, and the results depend on the specific training data generation scheme.

## Method

The authors used Gaussian process regression to generate continuous EOS curves from seven randomly sampled points in the phi-ln p plane, where phi is an intermediate variable that enforces physical causality and stability conditions. They then trained a deep neural network with five layers (56 input neurons, three hidden layers with 60, 40, and 40 neurons using ReLU activation, and 6 output neurons with tanh activation) to map mass-radius observation data points onto the EOS representation. The training dataset consisted of 1,000,000 sets of 14 simulated mass-radius data points generated from nine relativistic mean-field parameter sets (BigApple, DD2, DDLZ1, DDME1, DDME2, DDMEX, NL3, PKDD, and TW99), with the training process repeated 100 times to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: $2.38^{+0.15}_{-0.13} M_\odot$ at 95% confidence level from scheme 1; $2.41^{+0.15}_{-0.14} M_\odot$ at 95% confidence level from scheme 2
- Radius of a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star: $12.31^{+0.29}_{-0.31}$ km from scheme 1; $12.30^{+0.35}_{-0.37}$ km from scheme 2
- Seven pressure points were selected in the range $\ln p \in [1,7]$ for the Gaussian process regression
- The training dataset contained 1,000,000 sets of 14 data points each, drawn from an initial pool of 5,000,000 sets
- The neural network was trained for 100 epochs with a batch size of 1000

## Context

This work extends the data-driven approach of Fujimoto et al. (2021), who used a feed-forward deep neural network with a polytropic parameterization of the EOS, by instead using a nonparametric Gaussian process representation that avoids assumptions about the functional form of the EOS. It also differs from the Bayesian nonparametric inference method of Landry and Essick (2019), who combined Gaussian processes with Bayesian inference using nuclear-theoretic models as priors. The results are consistent with constraints from the GW170817 event, NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the masses of the three known massive neutron stars (PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J0740+6620). The framework enables future incorporation of additional observational data as it becomes available, without requiring reparameterization of the EOS.
