# Overview of Strangeness Nuclear Physics

> Abridged version of talk at SENDAI08, International Symposium on Strangeness Nuclear and Hadronic Systems, Dec. 2008, to be published in the Proceedings, Int. J. Mod. Phys. E. Version 2: added one reference

# Overview of Strangeness Nuclear Physics

## Key claim
Selected topics in strangeness nuclear physics are reviewed, with the claim that the observed doublet splittings in light hypernuclei provide a stringent measure of the smallness of the Lambda spin-orbit term, but the precise magnitude and shape of repulsive components in Sigma and antikaon nuclear potentials remain model dependent.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental data from gamma-ray spectroscopy of Lambda hypernuclei at BNL and KEK, inclusive (K-,K+) and (K-,n) spectra, and Sigma- atomic data, comparing them to theoretical calculations using Nijmegen soft-core meson-exchange models, chiral effective-field theory potentials, and quark-model resonating group method calculations. The review also examines few-body stochastic variational calculations for s-shell hypernuclei and relativistic mean-field calculations for neutron star matter and multi-antikaon nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Lambda well depth in nuclear matter: empirical value 28 MeV from fitting binding energies of Lambda single-particle states
- Lambda spin-orbit splitting in 89-Y: observed f-orbit splitting suggests 1.7 MeV, but consistent with merely 0.2 MeV after shell-model analysis
- Doublet splitting in 9-Be: 43 +/- 5 keV between 5/2+ and 3/2+ levels
- Doublet splitting in 16-O: 26.4 +/- 1.7 keV between 1- and 0- levels
- Effective Lambda N interaction parameters for A=7,9: Delta=430, S_Lambda=-15, S_N=-390, T=30 (all in keV)
- Effective Lambda N interaction parameters for A=15,16: Delta=315, S_Lambda=-15, S_N=-350, T=23.2 (all in keV)
- Sigma+ binding energy in 4-Sigma-He: 4.4 +/- 0.3 +/- 1 MeV with width 7.0 +/- 0.7 +1.2/-0.0 MeV
- Re V_opt^Sigma at nuclear matter density: approximately 40-50 MeV for the preferred potential F
- Xi hyperon potential from inclusive (K-,K+) on 12-C: approximately -14 MeV
- K-pp binding energy predictions: range from 17-23 MeV (AMD) to 60-95 MeV (Faddeev coupled-channel)
- Re V^Kbar at nuclear matter density: -(150-200) MeV from K- atom fits, approximately -100 MeV from chiral models, -(40-60) MeV from self-consistent approaches
- Lambda-Lambda separation energy in 6-Lambda-Lambda-He: approximately 1 MeV

## Context
This review extends the work of the Nijmegen group (Rijken, 2008) and Julich group (Haidenbauer, 2005; Polinder, 2006) on meson-exchange and chiral effective-field theory YN and YY potentials, and quark-model calculations by Fujiwara (2007). It also references lattice QCD calculations by Beane (2007) and Nemura (2009). The paper enables future experiments at J-PARC searching for Xi hypernuclei and K-pp quasibound states, and provides constraints for models of neutron star matter where strangeness appears through hyperons or kaon condensation. The review concludes that multi-strange hypernuclei remain the lowest-energy configuration for multi-strange systems, arguing against antikaon condensation in the laboratory under strong-interaction equilibrium conditions, while kaon condensation in neutron stars remains possible under weak-interaction equilibrium.
