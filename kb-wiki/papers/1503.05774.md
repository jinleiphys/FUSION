# Evidence for $Δ(2200)7/2^-$ from photoproduction and consequence for chiral-symmetry restoration at high mass

> We report a partial-wave analysis of new data on the double-polarization variable $E$ for the reactions $γp\to π^+ n$ and $γp\to π^0 p$ and of further data published earlier. The analysis within the Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa) formalism reveals evidence for a poorly known baryon resonance, the one-star $Δ(2200)7/2^-$. This is the lowest-mass $Δ^*$ resonance with spin-parity $J^P=7/2^-$. Its mass is significantly higher than the mass of its parity partner $Δ(1950)7/2^+$ which is the lowest-mass $Δ^*$ resonance with spin-parity $J^P=7/2^+$. It has been suggested that chiral symmetry might be restored in the high-mass region of hadron excitations, and that these two resonances should be degenerate in mass. Our findings are in conflict with this prediction.

## Key claim

The paper reports strong evidence for the existence of the one-star $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$ resonance from a coupled-channel partial-wave analysis of photoproduction data, finding its mass at $2176 \pm 40$ MeV, which is significantly higher than its parity partner $\Delta(1950)7/2^+$ at $1917 \pm 4$ MeV. This mass splitting contradicts the prediction from chiral-symmetry restoration that these two resonances should be mass-degenerate.

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave analysis within the Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa) formalism, fitting new double-polarization data on the variable $E$ for $\gamma p \to \pi^+ n$ and $\gamma p \to \pi^0 p$ together with previously published data on differential cross sections, beam asymmetries, and other polarization observables ($T$, $P$, $H$, $G$). The data were fitted jointly with data on $N\eta$, $\Lambda K$, $\Sigma K$, $N\pi^0\pi^0$, and $N\pi^0\eta$ from both photo- and pion-induced reactions, using a $K$-matrix parametrization of resonances and background contributions.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$: $2176 \pm 40$ MeV
- Width of $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$: $210 \pm 70$ MeV
- Mass of $\Delta(1950)7/2^+$: $1917 \pm 4$ MeV
- Width of $\Delta(1950)7/2^+$: $251 \pm 8$ MeV
- Helicity amplitudes for $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$: $A_{1/2} = 60 \pm 20$ and $A_{3/2} = -(20 \pm 8)$ (in units of $10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$)
- Helicity amplitudes for $\Delta(1950)7/2^+$: $A_{1/2} = -67 \pm 5$ and $A_{3/2} = -94 \pm 4$ (in units of $10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$)
- $N\pi$ branching ratio of $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$: $3.5 \pm 1.5\%$
- $N\pi$ branching ratio of $\Delta(1950)7/2^+$: $46 \pm 2\%$
- Difference in squared masses: $(1.06 \pm 0.17)$ GeV$^2$, consistent with the slope of the leading Regge trajectory for $\Delta^*$'s of $(1.08 \pm 0.01)$ GeV$^2$
- Fit deteriorated by $\delta\chi^2 = 597$ when $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$ was removed from the fit

## Context

This work extends previous partial-wave analyses by the Bonn-Gatchina group (Anisovich 2011, 2013) and provides the strongest evidence to date for the $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$ resonance, which was previously listed as a one-star resonance in the Review of Particle Properties. The result contradicts the hypothesis of chiral-symmetry restoration in highly excited hadrons proposed by Glozman (1999, 2003), which predicted that the parity partner of $\Delta(1950)7/2^+$ should have a mass around 1900-1950 MeV, and instead supports quark model predictions by Loring (2001) and AdS/QCD calculations by Forkel (2008) that give masses near 2200 MeV. The analysis enables future energy-independent (model-independent) determinations of photoproduction multipoles when further polarization data become available.
