# Quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model for strange quark matter

> It is found that the radius of a stable strangelet decreases as the temperature increases in a quark mass density-dependent model. To overcome this difficulty, we extend this model to a quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model in which the vacuum energy density at zero baryon density limit B depends on temperature. An ansatz is introduced and the regions for the best choice of the parameters are studied.

# Quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model for strange quark matter

## Key claim

The standard quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model produces the unphysical result that the radius of a stable strangelet decreases as temperature increases; this paper proposes a quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model where the vacuum energy density B depends on temperature via an ansatz, and shows that with a suitable choice of the parameter a in the range 0.65 ≤ a ≤ 0.8, the radius instead increases with temperature.

## Method

The authors extend the QMDD model by introducing a temperature-dependent vacuum energy density B(T) = B₀[1 − a(T/T_c) + b(T/T_c)²], with T_c = 170 MeV as the critical deconfinement temperature and the condition 1 − a + b = 0 so that B vanishes at T_c. They compute the thermodynamic potential for u, d, s quarks and electrons in a finite spherical strangelet using the multi-reflection theory for the density of states, including surface and curvature contributions, and solve for the stable radius R by minimizing the total free energy F with respect to R at fixed baryon number A = 20.

## Key numbers

- Stable radius at T = 0: R = 2.27 fm (for A = 20, B = 170 MeV fm⁻³, m_s0 = 150 MeV)
- Stable radius at T = 50 MeV in QMDD model: R = 2.18 fm (decrease from zero-temperature value)
- Stable radius at T = 50 MeV in QMDTD model with a = 0.65: R = 2.31 fm (increase from zero-temperature value)
- Critical temperature for quark deconfinement: T_c = 170 MeV
- Best parameter range for a: 0.65 ≤ a ≤ 0.8, determined by requiring both R(T) and E/A(T) to be monotonically increasing functions for 0 ≤ T ≤ 80 MeV

## Context

This work addresses a failure of the standard QMDD model (Fowler, Raha, Weiner 1981; Chakrabarty 1991; Benrenuto and Lugones 1995) when applied to finite strangelets at finite temperature. The authors note that effective masses of nucleons and mesons are known to decrease with temperature from Thermo Field Dynamics calculations (Zhang, Gao, Su 1997; Gao, Zhang, Su 1996; Wang et al. 1999), motivating a temperature-dependent quark mass. The proposed QMDTD model enables physically reasonable studies of strangelet properties at finite temperature and can also be applied to bulk strange quark matter, potentially providing a more reliable signature for quark-gluon plasma formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as suggested by Greiner, Koch and Stocker (1987).
