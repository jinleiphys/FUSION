# Spin-orbit correlation energy in neutron matter

> We study the relevance of the energy correlation produced by the two-body spin-orbit coupling present in realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. To this purpose, the neutron matter Equation of State (EoS) is calculated with the realistic two-body Argonne $v_8'$ potential. The shift occuring in the EoS when spin-orbit terms are removed is taken as an estimate of the spin-orbit correlation energy. Results obtained within the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone expansion, extended up to three hole-line diagrams, are compared with other many-body calculations recently presented in the literature. In particular, excellent agreement is found with the Green's function Monte-Carlo method. This agreement indicates the present theoretical accuracy in the calculation of the neutron matter EoS.

# Spin-orbit correlation energy in neutron matter

## Key claim

The spin-orbit correlation energy in neutron matter, estimated by the shift in the equation of state when spin-orbit terms are removed from the Argonne $v_8'$ potential, can be as large as 4-5 MeV per nucleon and increases with density; however, this estimate depends on the many-body method used, as the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte-Carlo method shows difficulty in accurately capturing the spin-orbit contribution.

## Method

The neutron matter equation of state was calculated using the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone (BBG) expansion up to three hole-line diagrams, with the Argonne $v_8'$ two-body nucleon-nucleon potential and its spin-orbit-free version $v_6'$. The single-particle auxiliary potential was treated in the continuous choice, and the difference between the EoS obtained with the two potentials was taken as the spin-orbit correlation energy. Results were compared with Green's function Monte-Carlo (GFMC), variational chain summation (VCS), and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte-Carlo (AFDMC) calculations from the literature.

## Key numbers

- At BHF level, the difference between the $v_8'$ and $v_6'$ EoS reaches about 14 MeV at $\rho = 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$.
- When three-body correlations are included, the gap between the two EoS is reduced by about 40% at the highest considered density.
- For the $v_8'$ potential at $\rho = 0.40$ fm$^{-3}$, the BBG energy per particle is 29.044 MeV; the AFDMC value is 35.3 MeV.
- For the $v_6'$ potential at $\rho = 0.40$ fm$^{-3}$, the BBG energy per particle is 37.552 MeV; the AFDMC value is 36.0 MeV.
- The discrepancy between BBG and UC-GFMC correlation energies does not exceed 2% for densities up to 0.24 fm$^{-3}$.
- At $\rho = 0.24$ fm$^{-3}$ with the $v_8'$ potential, BBG gives 15.846 MeV per particle, UC-GFMC gives 16.9 MeV, and VCS gives 14.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BBG calculations of Baldo (2000) for the Argonne $v_{14}$ and $v_{18}$ potentials to the simplified $v_8'$ and $v_6'$ interactions, enabling direct comparison with the GFMC results of Carlson (2003) and the AFDMC results of Fantoni (2001). The excellent agreement between BBG and GFMC (within 2%) indicates that the many-body problem for neutron matter with two-body interactions is well under control in the density range up to 0.24 fm$^{-3}$, while the disagreement with AFDMC highlights the difficulty of accurately treating spin-orbit correlations. The authors identify the inclusion of three-body forces as the necessary next step for extending this comparison to more realistic neutron matter calculations.
