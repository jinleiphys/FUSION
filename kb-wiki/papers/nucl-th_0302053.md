# Intensity Interferometry for a Chaotic Source with a Collective Flow and Multiple Scattering

> We study the effects of a collective flow and multiple scattering on two-particle correlation measurements in Hanbury-Brown-Twiss intensity interferometry. We find that under a collective flow the effective source distribution in a two-particle correlation measurement depends on the initial source distribution. In addition, it depends on a collective flow phase function which consists of terms that tend to cancel each other. As the detected particles traverse from the source point to the freeze-out point, they are subject to multiple scattering with medium particles. We examine the effects of multiple scattering on HBT correlations. By using the Glauber theory of multiple scattering at high energies and the optical model at intermediate energies, we find that multiple scattering leads to an absorption and an effective density distribution that depends on the initial source distribution.

## Key claim

Under a collective flow, the effective source distribution measured in two-particle HBT correlations depends on the initial source distribution and a collective flow phase function whose terms tend to cancel each other; multiple scattering leads to an absorption and an effective density distribution that depends on the initial source distribution rather than the freeze-out density distribution.

## Method

The author derives the two-particle correlation function for a chaotic source by summing probability amplitudes for boson (or fermion) production from source points, propagation along classical trajectories under a collective flow, and detection. Multiple scattering of detected particles with medium particles is treated quantum-mechanically using Glauber theory of multiple scattering at high energies (pion energies 300–1200 MeV) and the optical model at intermediate energies (120–766 MeV), rather than using incoherent collision probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Pion energy range for Glauber theory applicability: 300 to 1200 MeV (citing Ari91, Ose91, Joh92)
- Pion energy range for optical model applicability: 120 MeV to 766 MeV (citing Hon99)
- Experimental HBT ratio R_out/R_side ≈ 0.9–1.1, while hydrodynamical predictions give R_out/R_side significantly larger than 1.0 at freeze-out (citing Ris96, Hei02, Zsc02)

## Context

This work extends the probability-amplitude framework for intensity interferometry from Wong (1994) to include collective flow and multiple scattering effects. It addresses the puzzle that experimental HBT radii show weak collision-energy dependence and that R_out/R_side ≈ 0.9–1.1 contradicts hydrodynamical predictions (Rischke and Gyulassy, 1996; Heinz, 2002; Zschiesche, 2002) and covariant transport results (Molnar, 2002). The analysis suggests that HBT measurements probe the initial source distribution rather than the freeze-out configuration assumed in emission function modeling (Wiedemann and Heinz, 1998, 1999) and hydrodynamic approaches.
