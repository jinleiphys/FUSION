# Correlated fluctuations near the QCD critical point

> In this paper, we introduce a freeze-out scheme for the dynamical models near the QCD critical point through coupling the decoupled classical particles with the order parameter field. With a modified distribution function that satisfies specific static fluctuations, we calculate the correlated fluctuations of net protons on the hydrodynamic freeze-out surface. A comparison with recent STAR data shows that our model calculations could roughly reproduce energy dependent cumulant $C_4$ and $κσ^2$ of net protons through tuning the related parameters. However, the calculated $C_2$ and $C_3$ with both Poisson and Binomial baselines are always above the experimental data due to the positive contributions from the static critical fluctuations. In order to qualitatively and quantitatively describe the experimental data, the dynamical critical fluctuations and more realistic non-critical fluctuation baselines should be investigated in the near future.

# Correlated fluctuations near the QCD critical point

## Key claim

The authors introduce a freeze-out scheme for dynamical models near the QCD critical point that can roughly reproduce the energy-dependent cumulant C₄ and κσ² of net protons measured by STAR through tuning parameters, but the calculated C₂ and C₃ with both Poisson and Binomial baselines are always above experimental data due to positive contributions from static critical fluctuations, indicating that dynamical critical fluctuations and more realistic non-critical fluctuation baselines are needed for full quantitative description.

## Method

The authors couple classical particles decoupled from the hydrodynamic freeze-out surface with a fluctuating order parameter (sigma) field, using a modified distribution function expanded to linear order in the sigma field. They calculate the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th cumulants of correlated fluctuations of net protons by integrating static correlators of the sigma field (derived from a probability distribution with cubic and quartic terms) over a (3+1)-dimensional freeze-out surface obtained from the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 with smooth MC-Glauber initial conditions. The cumulants from critical fluctuations are summed with either Poisson or Binomial distribution baselines for non-critical statistical fluctuations, and parameters (coupling g, correlation length ξ, λ₃, λ₄) are tuned within allowed ranges to compare with STAR data at √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Correlation length ξ is set to maximum values of O(3 fm) near the critical point, decreasing to 0.5-1 fm away from it; specific values used range from 0.5 fm (at 200 GeV) to 4 fm (at 19.6 and 27 GeV in para-II)
- Coupling g is tuned between 0 and 10, with values ranging from 0.1 (at 200 GeV in para-III) to 3.2 (at 7.7 GeV in para-I and para-II)
- Dimensionless parameters: λ̃₃ ranges between (0,8) and λ̃₄ ranges between (4,20) based on lattice simulations; specific values used include λ̃₃ from 0 to 6 and λ̃₄ from 8 to 14
- Hydrodynamic simulations start at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c with specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.08 and specific bulk viscosity ζ/s = 0.0
- Cumulants are calculated within transverse momentum ranges 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV and 0.4 < pT < 2 GeV, with rapidity range |y| < 0.5
- At √sNN = 7.7 GeV, mean values of net protons (C₁) within both pT ranges increase by approximately a factor of 2 compared to √sNN = 19.6 GeV
- For the Poisson baseline, κσ² = 1 for net protons; for Binomial distributions, cumulants depend on an additional parameter εx = C₂ˣ/Mx

## Context

This work extends the framework of static critical fluctuations developed by Stephanov (2008, 2009, 2011) by implementing a freeze-out scheme that allows comparison with experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC's Beam Energy Scan program. The authors build on chiral hydrodynamics frameworks established by Paech (2003) and the Frankfurt group (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013), but note that previous dynamical models lacked proper freeze-out procedures for calculating hadron fluctuations. The approach enables direct comparison with STAR measurements of net-proton cumulants (Adamczyk 2013, Luo 2015), but the sign problem for C₂ and C₃ from static critical fluctuations points to the need for full dynamical evolution of the sigma field, as pioneered by Mukherjee (2015) who found that skewness and kurtosis could change sign during dynamical evolution, though their zero-mode approach cannot be directly applied to the freeze-out surface formalism presented here.
