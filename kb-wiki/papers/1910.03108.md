# Analysis of the one-neutron transfer to $^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni induced by ($^{18}$O, $^{17}$O) reaction at 84 MeV

> Background: Recently, a systematic exploration of two-neutron transfer induced by the ($^{18}$O, $^{16}$O) reaction on different targets has been performed. The high resolution data have been collected at the MAGNEX magnetic spectrometer of the INFN-LNS laboratory in Catania and analyzed with the coupled reaction channel (CRC) approach. The simultaneous and sequential transfers of the two neutrons have been considered under the same theoretical framework without the need of adjustable factors in the calculations. Purpose: A detailed analysis of the one-neutron transfer cross sections is important to study the sequential two-neutron transfer. Here, we examine the ($^{18}$O, $^{17}$O) reaction on $^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni targets. These even-even nuclei allow for investigation of one-neutron transfer in distinct nuclear shell spaces. Method: The MAGNEX spectrometer was used to measure mass spectra of ejectiles and extract differential cross sections of one-neutron transfer to low-lying states. We adopted the same CRC formalism used in the sequential two-neutron transfer, including relevant channels and using spectroscopic amplitudes obtained from shell model calculations. We also compare with one-step distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA). Results: For the $^{18}$O + $^{16}$O and the $^{18}$O + $^{28}$O systems we used two interactions in the shell model. The experimental angular distributions are reasonably well reproduced by the CRC calculations. In the $^{18}$O + $^{64}$Ni system, we considered only one interaction and the theoretical curve describes the shape and order of magnitude observed in the experimental data. Conclusions: Comparisons between experimental, DWBA and CRC angle-integrated cross sections suggest that excitations before or after the transfer of neutron is relevant in the $^{18}$O + $^{16}$O and $^{18}$O + $^{64}$Ni systems.

## Key claim

The coupled reaction channel (CRC) formalism, using spectroscopic amplitudes from shell-model calculations and the Sao Paulo potential as the optical potential without adjustable normalization factors, reasonably reproduces the experimental angular distributions for one-neutron transfer in the (18O,17O) reaction on 16O, 28Si, and 64Ni targets at 84 MeV, though the quality of agreement varies by system and the ground-to-ground transition to 28Si shows poorer agreement, possibly related to target deformation.

## Method

The MAGNEX magnetic spectrometer at INFN-LNS Catania measured mass spectra of ejectiles from 84 MeV 18O beams on WO3, 28Si, and 64Ni targets, extracting differential cross sections for one-neutron transfer to low-lying states of 17O, 29Si, and 65Ni. The authors adopted the CRC formalism using the FRESCO code with the Sao Paulo potential as the optical potential, using spectroscopic amplitudes from shell-model calculations (ZBM and psdmod interactions for the oxygen system; psdmod and psdmwkpn interactions for the silicon system; bjuff model space for the nickel system). They also compared CRC results with one-step distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 84 MeV (laboratory frame) for all three reactions
- Target thicknesses: WO3 at 210 +/- 20 microgram/cm2; 28Si at 140 +/- 10 microgram/cm2; 64Ni at 110 +/- 10 microgram/cm2
- MAGNEX spectrometer acceptance: approximately 50 msr
- Overall energy resolution: about 250 keV (Full Width at Half Maximum)
- Angular resolution: 0.3 degrees for 64Ni; 0.5 degrees for 16O and 28Si
- Systematic uncertainty: 10% from target thickness and beam integration, not included in error bars
- For the 16O and 28Si targets, angular coverage: 4 degrees < theta_lab < 15 degrees (two spectrometer settings at 8 and 10 degrees)
- For the 64Ni target, angular coverage: about 7 degrees < theta_lab < 29 degrees (three spectrometer settings at 12, 18, and 24 degrees)
- Spectroscopic factor for 28Si g.s. to 29Si g.s. transition: 0.51 (psdmod interaction) and 0.32 (psdmwkpn interaction); literature values: 0.53 (Mermaz 1979) and 0.37 (Peterson 1983)
- Spectroscopic factor for 28Si g.s. to 29Si 1.27 MeV (3/2+) transition: 0.68 (psdmod) and 0.65 (psdmwkpn); literature: 0.74 (Mermaz 1979) and 0.53 (Peterson 1983)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for one-neutron wave functions: reduced radii 1.2 fm, diffuseness 0.6 fm for nuclear cores
- Normalization coefficient for imaginary part of Sao Paulo potential: 0.6 in entrance partition; 0.78 in outgoing partition
- Mass diffuseness of 18O projectile: 0.61 fm

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of two-neutron transfer reactions induced by (18O,16O) on various targets (Cappuzzello 2013, Cavallaro 2015, Carbone 2016, Ermamatov 2017, Linares 2018, Paul 2017) by analyzing the one-neutron transfer channel (18O,17O) as an intermediate step in sequential two-neutron transfer. The CRC approach with the Sao Paulo potential and shell-model spectroscopic amplitudes, previously applied to two-neutron transfer without adjustable factors, is here tested on one-neutron transfer to assess the robustness of the sequential transfer calculations. The results enable a more complete understanding of the competition between simultaneous and sequential mechanisms in two-neutron transfer, and the comparison with earlier (d,p) reaction studies (Mermaz 1979, Peterson 1983, Lee 2009) provides a benchmark for spectroscopic factors from heavy-ion induced transfer.
