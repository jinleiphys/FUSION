# The exact discontinuity of a partial wave along the left-hand cut and the exact $N/D$ method in non-relativistic scattering

> We first deduce the analytical continuation in the complex planes of the initial and final three-momenta of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in coupled or uncoupled partial-wave amplitudes. This result allows us to deduce a master equation whose solution is the exact discontinuity of the on-shell partial-wave amplitudes along the left-hand cut. This equation is always a linear non-singular integral equation whose solution is fixed exclusively by the knowledge of the potential, applicable to either regular or singular potentials. The capability of calculating exactly this discontinuity allows one to settle the exact $N/D $ method in two-body non-relativistic scattering for coupled and uncoupled waves. We exemplify this new advance in scattering theory by explicitly checking the agreement between the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the corresponding solutions of the exact $N/D$ method for some examples that involve regular and singular potentials, either attractive or repulsive.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact, linear, non-singular integral equation whose solution gives the discontinuity of on-shell partial-wave amplitudes along the left-hand cut, fixed exclusively by the potential and applicable to both regular and singular potentials; this enables an exact \(N/D\) method for coupled and uncoupled waves in non-relativistic scattering, with the hedge that the method works for either attractive or repulsive singular potentials but requires an extra boundary condition (self-adjoint extension) for the attractive case.

## Method

The authors first perform the analytical continuation of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in the complex planes of the initial and final three-momenta for coupled or uncoupled partial-wave amplitudes. From this continuation they derive a master equation that yields the exact discontinuity of the on-shell partial-wave amplitudes along the left-hand cut, which is a linear non-singular integral equation whose solution depends only on the potential. They then use this discontinuity to settle the exact \(N/D\) method, and verify the agreement between the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and the exact \(N/D\) solutions for examples involving regular and singular potentials, both attractive and repulsive.

## Key numbers

- The classical turning point \(r_c\) is finite for regular and repulsive singular potentials, but for an attractive singular potential \(r_c = 0\) at high enough energy because the potential overcomes the centrifugal barrier as \(r \to 0\).
- For a singular potential diverging as \(-1/r^n\) with \(n > 2\) in the attractive case, the reduced wave function magnitude decreases as \(r^{n/4}\) for \(r \to 0\) and oscillates with a wave number diverging as \(r^{-n/2}\).
- For a repulsive singular potential, the wave function behaves as \(r^{n/4} \exp(-B r^{-n/2+1})\) for \(r \to 0\), with \(B\) a constant.
- The scale \(\beta_n\) for a long-range potential \(C_n r^{-n}\) is given by \(\beta_n = (2 \mu |C_n| / \ell(\ell+1))^{1/(n-2)}\).
- The Rydberg constant is \(R = m_e \alpha^2 / 4\pi\).
- The energy levels of a Hydrogen atom are \(E_n = -\mu \alpha^2 / (2 n^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the theory of non-relativistic scattering with singular potentials, building on the Quantum Defect Theory developed by Hartree (1928) and Seaton, and on the self-adjoint extension approach of Meetz and the orthogonality condition introduced by Case. It also connects to renormalization techniques used for singular potentials in effective field theories, as discussed by van Kolck and others. The exact \(N/D\) method enabled by this work allows for a complete treatment of two-body scattering with singular potentials, which is relevant for nuclear interactions, atomic physics (van der Waals potentials \(-C_6/r^6\)), and electron-atom interactions (potentials \(-1/r^4\)).
