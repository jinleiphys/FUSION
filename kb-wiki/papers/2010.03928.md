# Phenomenological constraints on the transport properties of QCD matter with data-driven model averaging

> Using combined data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion and Large Hadron Colliders, we constrain the shear and bulk viscosities of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at temperatures of ${\sim\,}150{-}350$ MeV. We use Bayesian inference to translate experimental and theoretical uncertainties into probabilistic constraints for the viscosities. With Bayesian Model Averaging we account for the irreducible model ambiguities in the transition from a fluid description of the QGP to hadronic transport in the final evolution stage, providing the most reliable phenomenological constraints to date on the QGP viscosities.

## Key claim

The paper provides the most reliable phenomenological constraints to date on the shear and bulk viscosities of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at temperatures of approximately 150-350 MeV, but only after accounting for irreducible model ambiguities in the transition from fluid dynamics to hadronic transport via Bayesian Model Averaging. The constraints are data-driven, meaning that at temperatures above about 250 MeV the experimental measurements provide little constraining power and the posterior distributions largely reflect the prior assumptions rather than the data.

## Method

The JETSCAPE multistage model was used to simulate heavy-ion collisions, combining the TRENTO initial condition model, free-streaming pre-hydrodynamic evolution, (2+1)D second-order dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics with parametrized temperature-dependent specific shear and bulk viscosities, and three different particlization models (14-moment Grad, relativistic Chapman-Enskog in the relaxation-time approximation, and Pratt-Torrieri-Bernhard) for the transition to hadronic kinetic transport with SMASH. Bayesian inference was applied to constrain model parameters by comparing simulations to experimental data from RHIC (Au+Au at 0.2 TeV) and the LHC (Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV), and Bayesian Model Averaging was used to combine results across the three particlization models weighted by their Bayes evidence.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of Bayes evidences for the three particlization models is approximately 5000:2000:1 for Grad, Pratt-Torrieri-Bernhard, and Chapman-Enskog respectively, strongly disfavoring the Chapman-Enskog model.
- The most likely values for specific shear viscosity (eta/s) in the deconfinement region (approximately 150-250 MeV) are of order 0.1.
- The most likely values for specific bulk viscosity (zeta/s) in the deconfinement region are around 0.05-0.1, though constraints are weaker than for eta/s.
- The experimental data provide significant constraints on viscosities only for temperatures between approximately 150 and 250 MeV; above 250 MeV the posteriors are close to the prior 90% credible ranges.
- Ignoring the proton dN/dy reduces the odds against the Chapman-Enskog particlization model from 5000:1 to 5:1.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian inference studies of QGP viscosities (Bernhard 2019) by using broader priors and explicitly quantifying the irreducible model uncertainty from the particlization stage, which earlier studies did not address. The results are consistent with Bernhard (2019) in showing that constraints at high temperatures originate primarily from priors rather than data, but the current work demonstrates this more clearly by comparing posteriors to priors. The analysis enables future studies to systematically include additional measurements, model uncertainties, error correlations, and more rigorous prior specifications for estimating transport properties of QGP.
