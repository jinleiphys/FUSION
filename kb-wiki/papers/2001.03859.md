# Nucleon effective mass in hot dense matter

> Nucleon effective masses are studied in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body approach at finite temperature. Self-consistent calculations using the Argonne $V_{18}$ interaction including microscopic three-body forces are reported for varying temperature and proton fraction up to several times the nuclear saturation density. Our calculations are based on the exact treatment of the center-of-mass momentum instead of the average-momentum approximation employed in previous works. We discuss in detail the effects of the temperature together with those of the three-body forces, the density, and the isospin asymmetry. We also provide an analytical fit of the effective mass taking these dependencies into account. The temperature effects on the cooling of neutron stars are briefly discussed based on the results for betastable matter.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the nucleon effective mass in hot dense matter depends nontrivially on temperature, density, and isospin asymmetry, with temperature effects generally modest compared to density effects but most pronounced at low and high densities; the authors provide an analytical fit valid for densities 0.1 to 0.8 fm⁻³, temperatures 10 to 50 MeV, and asymmetries -1 to 1, but caution that the fit should only be used in the regime of homogeneous nuclear matter modeled by Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations at finite temperature for asymmetric nuclear matter, using the Argonne V18 two-body nucleon-nucleon potential plus a consistent microscopic three-body force (TBF) derived from meson-exchange currents. They used an exact treatment of the center-of-mass momentum in the Bethe-Goldstone equation, replacing the average-c.m.-momentum approximation used in earlier BHF studies. The effective mass was extracted from the momentum derivative of the single-particle energy at the Fermi surface for varying density, isospin asymmetry, and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter with TBF: 0.186 fm⁻³, with energy per baryon at saturation -14.5 MeV (differing from earlier values of 0.198 fm⁻³ and -15.0 MeV due to exact c.m. momentum treatment).
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition: about 13 MeV with TBF, about 16 MeV without TBF.
- Threshold density for direct Urca process in beta-stable matter at zero temperature: approximately 0.38 fm⁻³.
- Standard deviation of the analytical fit for effective mass: 0.010 (V18 only) and 0.008 (V18+TBF).
- Fit valid for density range 0.1 to 0.8 fm⁻³, temperature range 10 to 50 MeV, asymmetry range -1 to 1.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature BHF calculations of Baldo et al. (2014) and Li et al. (2016) to finite temperature, and improves upon earlier finite-temperature BHF studies by Lejeune et al. (1986), Baldo et al. (1988), and others by using exact c.m. momentum treatment rather than the average-momentum approximation. The results provide microscopic input for modeling proto-neutron star cooling, neutron star mergers, core-collapse supernovae, and heavy-ion collisions; the authors note that at low densities (below about 0.1 fm⁻³) cluster formation becomes important and BHF theory is not applicable, while at high densities (above about 0.8 fm⁻³) quark degrees of freedom should be considered.
