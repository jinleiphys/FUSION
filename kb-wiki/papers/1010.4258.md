# Parton Energy Loss in Strongly Coupled AdS/CFT

> This is a brief review of the theory and phenomenology of parton energy loss in strongly coupled field theories with a gravity dual and its comparison with parton energy loss in QCD at weak coupling.

## Key claim

This review argues that parton energy loss in a strongly coupled gauge theory with a gravity dual (AdS/CFT) differs qualitatively from perturbative QCD predictions, but that strong coupling likely applies only after the parton's virtuality has evolved down to modest values, meaning a hybrid approach combining pQCD for the hard scattering and AdS/CFT for the infrared dynamics may be necessary.

## Method

The paper reviews the holographic AdS/CFT duality, where the dynamics of N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at strong 't Hooft coupling maps onto classical supergravity in a 5-dimensional anti-de Sitter space with a black hole horizon representing the thermal medium. Energy loss of heavy quarks is studied via a trailing string anchored to a D7-brane, while light partons and gluons are modeled as "doubled up" strings emerging from and returning to the black hole horizon, with the stopping distance determined by the string's disappearance into the horizon. The virtuality evolution and splitting functions for partons in the strongly coupled scenario are compared with pQCD calculations, and a hybrid splitting function with a virtuality threshold between the two regimes is proposed.

## Key numbers

* The stopping distance for a gluon in the strongly coupled theory is given by ℓ_stop(E) = (1/πT) (E/(T√λ))^(1/3), while for light quarks the prefactor is approximately 1/2.
* For a 30 GeV gluon with initial virtuality Q0=5 GeV in a medium with T0=300 MeV at t0=1 fm/c (RHIC conditions), the in-medium contribution to parton virtuality dominates after about 0.5 fm/c in the AdS/CFT scenario but significantly later (t>2.5 fm/c) in pQCD.
* For a 300 GeV gluon with Q0=20 GeV and T0=400 MeV (LHC conditions), the gluon virtuality in pQCD exceeds that in AdS/CFT until approximately 3 fm/c, and it takes almost 5 fm/c for the vacuum contribution to fall below the in-medium contribution in pQCD.
* The virtuality evolution in the medium follows dQ/dt ~ -T^2 in the strongly coupled AdS/CFT scenario, compared to Q(t)^2 ~ q̂ t ~ T^3 t in pQCD.

## Context

This review extends the pQCD theory of radiative energy loss due to Zakharov (1996) and Baier et al. (1996) (BDMPS) by considering the strongly coupled regime accessible via the AdS/CFT duality of Maldacena (1997) and Aharony et al. (1999). It builds on prior work by Gubser (2006), Herzog (2006), Chesler (2007), and others on the trailing string description of heavy quark energy loss, and by Gubser (2008) and Chesler (2008) on light parton stopping distances. The review enables future hybrid approaches that combine pQCD for hard scattering with AdS/CFT for the infrared dynamics, and identifies specific measurements needed at RHIC and LHC to distinguish between the two paradigms.
