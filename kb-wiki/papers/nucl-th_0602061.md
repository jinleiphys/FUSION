# Anomalous diffusion and anisotropic nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation

> We analyse a bidimensional nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation by considering an anisotropic case, whose diffusion coefficients are $D_x \propto |x|^{-θ}$ and $D_y \propto |y|^{-γ}$ with $θ, γ\in {\cal{R}}$. In this context, we also investigate two situations with the drift force $\vec{F}(\vec{r},t)=(-k_{x}x, -k_y y)$. The first one is characterized by $k_x/k_y=(2+γ)/(2+θ)$ and the second is given by $k_{x}=k$ and $k_{y}=0$. In these cases, we can verify an anomalous behavior induced in different directions by the drift force applied. The found results are exact and exhibit, in terms of the $q$-exponentials, functions which emerge from the Tsallis formalism. The generalization for the $D$-dimensional case is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents exact solutions for a bidimensional nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation with anisotropic, position-dependent diffusion coefficients, but only under specific conditions: the drift force must satisfy either \(k_x/k_y = (2+\gamma)/(2+\theta)\) or \(k_x = k\) and \(k_y = 0\), and the nonlinearity parameter \(\nu\) is related to the Tsallis \(q\)-exponential via \(\nu = 2-q\).

## Method

The authors analyze the anisotropic nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation \(\partial_t \rho = \mathcal{D}_x \partial_x (|x|^{-\theta} \partial_x \rho^\nu) + \mathcal{D}_y \partial_y (|y|^{-\gamma} \partial_y \rho^\nu) - \nabla \cdot (\vec{F}\rho)\) with linear drift \(\vec{F} = (-k_x x, -k_y y)\). They propose a \(q\)-exponential ansatz \(\rho(\vec{r},t) = \exp_q[-\beta_x |x|^{2+\theta} - \beta_y |y|^{2+\gamma}]/Z\) and derive coupled ordinary differential equations for the time-dependent parameters \(\beta_x(t)\), \(\beta_y(t)\), and \(Z(t)\). The system is solved exactly for the drift-free case and for the two special drift conditions, while the case \(k_x = k, k_y = 0\) yields only implicit solutions.

## Key numbers

- The diffusion coefficients scale as \(D_x \propto |x|^{-\theta}\) and \(D_y \propto |y|^{-\gamma}\) with \(\theta, \gamma \in \mathbb{R}\).
- The relation between drift coefficients for the first solvable case is \(k_x/k_y = (2+\gamma)/(2+\theta)\).
- The nonlinearity parameter satisfies \(\nu = 2-q\), where \(q\) is the Tsallis entropic index.
- The exponent governing the time evolution of \(\beta_y(t)\) is \(\varepsilon = (4+\theta+\gamma)(1-\nu)/[(2+\theta)(2+\gamma)]\).
- The second moment \(\langle x^2 \rangle\) scales as \(1/\beta_x^{2/(2+\theta)}(t)\), with sub-, normal, or super-diffusive behavior determined by the sign of \(\theta - (4+\theta+\gamma)(1-\nu)/(2+\gamma)\).

## Context

This work extends previous one-dimensional analyses of nonlinear Fokker-Planck equations by Plastino and Plastino (1995), Tsallis and Bukman (1996), and Borland (1998) to the anisotropic bidimensional case with position-dependent diffusion coefficients. It also generalizes the spatially-dependent diffusion coefficient approach of Malacarne, Pedron, Mendes, and Lenzi (2001, 2002) to anisotropic media. The results enable the study of anomalous diffusion in systems where the medium properties differ along orthogonal directions, such as diffusion on fractals as studied by Shaughnessy and Procaccia (1985), and connect to the nonextensive Tsallis thermostatistics framework.
