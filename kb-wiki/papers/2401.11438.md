# Tensor force impact on shell evolution in neutron-rich Si and Ni isotopes

> The influence of the tensor interaction of nucleons on the characteristics of neutron-rich silicon and nickel isotopes was studied in this work. Tensor forces are taken into account within the framework of the Hartree-Fock approach with the Skyrme interaction. It is shown that the addition of tensor component of interaction improves the description of the splitting between different single-particle states and leads to a decrease in nucleon-nucleon pairing correlations in silicon and nickel nuclei. Special attention was given to the role of isovector tensor forces relevant for interaction of like nucleons.

## Key claim

The tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, particularly the isovector part responsible for interaction of like nucleons, can produce effects on single-particle splittings comparable to those of the neutron-proton tensor interaction, contrary to the common assumption that the isovector tensor force is significantly weaker.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy5+T and SGII+T parametrizations, which include tensor interaction terms added perturbatively to the central Skyrme forces. Pairing correlations were treated using the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approach with a delta-force pairing potential, and the calculations assumed spherical symmetry. Additional comparisons were made using SLy4+T, SAMi+T, and SGII+T2 interactions to probe the role of different tensor force strengths.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for silicon isotopes: SLy5 parametrization shows best agreement with experimental data from AME2020 across the chain; SGII overestimates binding energy for all isotopes considered.
- Proton 1d3/2-1d5/2 splitting in 34Si: SGII+T gives 8.74 MeV, SLy5+T gives 8.48 MeV, compared to experimental value of 6.9 +/- 1.6 MeV.
- Neutron 1d3/2-1d5/2 splitting in 34Si: SGII+T gives 4.22 MeV, SLy5+T gives 7.23 MeV, compared to experimental value of 4.2 +/- 1.9 MeV.
- Neutron 1f7/2-1d3/2 splitting in 42Si: SGII+T2 gives 3.18 MeV, SGII+T gives 4.56 MeV, SLy5+T gives 4.98 MeV, compared to experimental value of 1.8 +/- 2.2 MeV.
- Proton 1f5/2-1f7/2 splitting in 68Ni: SGII+T gives approximately 6.3 MeV (from figure), SLy5+T gives approximately 7.5 MeV (from figure), compared to experimental data from Bes11_1 and Gr07.
- Tensor parameters for SGII+T: alpha_T = -180 MeV fm^5, beta_T = 120 MeV fm^5; for SLy5+T: alpha_T = -170 MeV fm^5, beta_T = 100 MeV fm^5; for SAMi+T: alpha_T = -39.80 MeV fm^5, beta_T = 66.65 MeV fm^5.

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor force effects on shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei, building on the framework established by Otsuka (2005, 2020) for neutron-proton tensor interactions. It specifically addresses the less-studied isovector tensor component, following earlier work by Chen (2021) on zirconium isotopes. The results for silicon isotopes are compared with previous calculations by Tar08 using SLy5 parametrization, and for nickel isotopes with studies by Les07, Ban22, Ro21, and Ot06. The findings enable better understanding of how tensor forces affect single-particle structure and pairing correlations in neutron-rich nuclei, with implications for shell evolution near N=20, 28, and 50 magic numbers.
