# Extrapolation uncertainties in the importance-truncated No-Core Shell Model

> We report on Li-6 calculations performed with the IT-NCSM and compare them to full NCSM calculations. We employ the Entem and Machleidt chiral two-body N3LO interaction (regulated at 500 MeV/c), which has been modified to a phase-shift equivalent potential by the similarity renormalization group (SRG) procedure. We investigate the dependence of the procedure on the technique employed to extrapolate to the complete Nmax space, the harmonic oscillator energy, and investigate the dependence on the momentum-decoupling scale (λ) used in the SRG. We also investigate the use of one or several reference states from which the truncated basis is constructed. We find that the uncertainties generated from various extrapolating functions used to extrapolate to the complete Nmax space increase as Nmax increases. The extrapolation uncertainties range from a few keV for the smallest Nmax spaces to about 50 keV for the largest Nmax spaces. We note that the difference between extrapolated IT-NCSM and NCSM ground-state energies, however, can be as large as a 100-250 keV depending on the chosen harmonic oscillator energy. We also present the extrapolation of IT-NCSM results to Nmax infinity and compare these to similarly extrapolated full NCSM results.

## Key claim

The IT-NCSM ground-state energies extrapolated to the complete Nmax space agree with full NCSM results to within 100–250 keV, but the extrapolation uncertainty itself (a few keV to about 50 keV) is smaller than this systematic difference, which depends on the chosen harmonic oscillator energy and the SRG momentum-decoupling scale.

## Method

The authors performed IT-NCSM calculations for 6Li using the Entem and Machleidt chiral two-body N3LO interaction (regulated at 500 MeV/c), softened via the similarity renormalization group (SRG) to momentum scales λ = 2.02 fm⁻¹ and λ = 1.50 fm⁻¹. They varied the harmonic oscillator energy ħΩ across 12, 16, 20, and 24 MeV, and extrapolated IT-NCSM ground-state energies to the complete Nmax space (up to Nmax = 14) using several different extrapolation functions applied to a series of calculations with varying importance threshold κ. They compared these extrapolated IT-NCSM results to full NCSM calculations performed with the ANTOINE code, and also extrapolated both IT-NCSM and full NCSM results to Nmax = ∞.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolation uncertainties from various extrapolating functions range from a few keV for the smallest Nmax spaces to about 50 keV for the largest Nmax spaces.
- The difference between extrapolated IT-NCSM and full NCSM ground-state energies can be as large as 100–250 keV, depending on the chosen harmonic oscillator energy (ħΩ).
- For the Nmax = ∞ extrapolation, the difference between IT-NCSM and full NCSM calculations for 6Li is about 100–300 keV.
- SRG momentum-decoupling scales λ = 2.02 fm⁻¹ and λ = 1.50 fm⁻¹ were used; IT-NCSM performs equally well for both.
- Harmonic oscillator energies ħΩ = 12, 16, 20, and 24 MeV were employed.

## Context

This work extends the IT-NCSM approach introduced by Roth (2009) by providing a systematic analysis of extrapolation uncertainties that had not been fully discussed previously. The paper compares IT-NCSM results to full NCSM calculations up to Nmax = 14, using the ANTOINE code for the full-space reference. The findings enable more reliable use of IT-NCSM for p-shell nuclei where full NCSM calculations are computationally prohibitive, and establish a procedure for assigning uncertainties to Nmax = ∞ extrapolations.
