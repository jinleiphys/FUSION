# Interaction of solar neutrinos with $^{98,100}$Mo isotopes and the influence of nuclear resonances

> The process of neutrino interaction with $^{98}$Mo and $^{100}$Mo isotopes is studied taking into consideration the effect of charge-exchange resonances. The experimental data on the strength functions $S(E)$ obtained in charge-exchange reactions $(p; n)$ and $(^3\mathrm{He}, t)$ and the strength functions $S(E)$ calculated within the theory of finite Fermi systems were used. We studied the effect of the resonance structure of $S(E)$ on the cross sections for solar-neutrino. We investigated the contribution of each high-lying resonance to the capture cross section $σ(E_ν)$. The contributions of all components of the solar neutrino spectrum are calculated. We estimate the contribution of different components of solar neutrinos as the background for the double-beta decay experiments on $^{100}$Mo.

## Key claim

The cross sections for solar neutrino capture on $^{98}$Mo and $^{100}$Mo differ by a factor of more than 45, primarily because the Q-value for $^{100}$Tc (172.1 keV) is much lower than for $^{98}$Tc (1684 keV), allowing low-energy pp neutrinos to dominate the capture rate on $^{100}$Mo while hard boron and hep neutrinos dominate for $^{98}$Mo.

## Method

The authors calculated the charge-exchange strength functions S(E) for GT excitations of $^{98,100}$Mo using the theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) in the coordinate representation with a Woods-Saxon basis and pairing, solving the secular equations for the effective field. They compared these calculations with experimental strength functions from $^{98}$Mo(p,n)$^{98}$Tc and $^{100}$Mo($^{3}$He,t)$^{100}$Tc reactions, then computed neutrino capture cross sections $\sigma(E_\nu)$ and capture rates R (in SNU) using the BS05(OP) solar model, including and excluding individual resonances to quantify their contributions.

## Key numbers

- Q-value for $^{98}$Tc: $1684 \pm 3$ keV; for $^{100}$Tc: $172.1 \pm 1.4$ keV
- Calculated GT resonance energy for $^{98}$Tc: $E_{\mathrm{GTR}} = 12.45$ MeV (experimental: $\approx 12.3$ MeV, difference 0.15 MeV)
- Calculated GT resonance energy for $^{100}$Tc: $E_{\mathrm{GTR}} = 13.20$ MeV (experimental: $\approx 13.3$ MeV, difference 0.10 MeV)
- Total capture rate for $^{98}$Mo using experimental strength function: $R_{\mathrm{Total}} = 18.52$ SNU; using TFFS strength function: $R_{\mathrm{Total}} = 19.028$ SNU
- Total capture rate for $^{100}$Mo using experimental strength function: $R_{\mathrm{Total}} = 993.05$ SNU; using TFFS strength function: $R_{\mathrm{Total}} = 860.11$ SNU
- Disregarding GTR and PR1 reduces $R_{\mathrm{Total}}$ for $^{98}$Mo by 55% (experimental) or 43% (TFFS); for $^{100}$Mo the reduction is only 1% (experimental) or 1.8% (TFFS)
- For $^{100}$Mo, a 100 keV shift in ground-state position changes $R_{\mathrm{Total}}$ by about 150 SNU per 50 keV step, primarily from the pp neutrino channel

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino capture on molybdenum isotopes by Lutostansky and collaborators using TFFS, and provides updated cross sections relevant to double-beta decay experiments using $^{100}$Mo such as NEMO-3, SuperNEMO, CUPID-Mo, and AMoRE. The results are compared with prior calculations by Ejiri and Elliott (2014, 2017) who obtained $R_{\mathrm{Total}} = 989$ SNU and 975 SNU respectively for $^{100}$Mo, and with earlier work by Erokhina (1995) who reported $28^{+15}_{-8}$ SNU for $^{98}$Mo. The paper demonstrates that the resonance structure of the strength function has a much smaller effect on the total capture rate for $^{100}$Mo than for $^{98}$Mo, because low-energy neutrinos dominate the $^{100}$Mo capture and are unaffected by high-lying resonances.
