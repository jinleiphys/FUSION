# Emulators for scarce and noisy data: application to auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo for the deuteron

> The validation, verification, and uncertainty quantification of computationally expensive theoretical models of quantum many-body systems require the construction of fast and accurate emulators. In this work, we develop emulators for auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC), a powerful many-body method for nuclear systems. We introduce a reduced-basis method (RBM) emulator for AFDMC and study it in the simple case of the deuteron. Furthermore, we compare our RBM emulator with the recently proposed parametric matrix model (PMM) that combines elements of RBMs with machine learning. We contrast these two approaches with a traditional Gaussian Process emulator. All three emulators constructed here are based on a very limited set of 5 training points, as expected for realistic AFDMC calculations, but validated against $\mathcal{O}(10^3)$ exact solutions. We find that the PMM, with emulator errors of only $\approx 0.1 \%$ and speed-up factors of $\approx 10^7$, outperforms our implementation of the other two emulators when applied to AFDMC.

## Key claim

The parametric matrix model (PMM) emulator achieves average errors of approximately 0.1% and speed-up factors of approximately 10^7 relative to full AFDMC calculations for the deuteron, outperforming both a reduced-basis method (RBM) emulator and a Gaussian process emulator when all are trained on only 5 training points.

## Method

The authors constructed three emulators for AFDMC calculations of the deuteron binding energy using local chiral EFT interactions at leading order (LO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). The RBM emulator uses a Petrov-Galerkin projection method that avoids computing overlaps between exact AFDMC eigenstates by using analytic trial wave functions as projecting functions. The PMM emulator assumes the ground state energy can be approximated by the lowest eigenvalue of a matrix that is affine in the low-energy couplings (LECs), with the matrix elements inferred from training data. A Gaussian process emulator with a combined Matérn and dot-product kernel was implemented for comparison. All emulators were trained on 5 samples drawn from posterior distributions of LECs and validated against O(10^3) exact solutions from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- PMM emulator achieves average percentage error of approximately 0.1% with speed-up factors of approximately 10^7 relative to full AFDMC calculations for the deuteron at LO
- RBM emulator with 5 training points achieves average percentage error of 1.8% for the deuteron at LO
- Kullback-Leibler divergence between PMM-predicted and exact posterior distribution on deuteron binding energy is 5 x 10^-5 at LO
- Kullback-Leibler divergence for RBM (GP) emulator relative to exact solution is 0.008 (0.03) at LO
- PMM with N_dim = 2 and 11 training points achieves average error of 0.2% for N2LO interactions in a 4-dimensional parameter space, with largest outlier error of 2.4%
- PMM with N_dim = 2 and 11 training points yields KL divergence of 3 x 10^-4 relative to exact PDF at N2LO
- Training points at LO range from -2.3 MeV to -0.9 MeV in deuteron binding energy

## Context

This work extends the development of emulators for quantum Monte Carlo methods, which were previously in early stages (Frame 2019, Sarkar 2023). The floating block method was recently proposed by Sarkar (2023) as a solution to the overlap problem in RBM emulators for lattice Monte Carlo. The Petrov-Galerkin projection approach introduced here circumvents the overlap problem differently by using analytic trial wave functions. The PMM was recently proposed by Cook (2024) and is here applied to AFDMC for the first time. These emulators enable the use of chiral EFT interactions and QMC methods in Bayesian inference pipelines for interpreting multi-messenger neutron star observations, which would otherwise be prohibitively expensive due to the O(10^5) CPU-hours required per typical AFDMC simulation.
