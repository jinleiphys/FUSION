# Recent results from parton cascade and microscopic transport

> Parton cascade is a microscopic transport approach for the study of the space-time evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions and its experimental manifestations. In the following, parton cascade calculations on elliptic flow and thermalization will be discussed. Dynamical evolution is shown to be important for the production of elliptic flow including the scaling and the breaking of the scaling of elliptic flow. The degree of thermalization is estimated using both an elastic parton cascade and a radiative transport model. A longitudinal to transverse pressure ratio, $P_L/P_T\approx 0.8$, is shown to be expected in the central cell in central collisions. This provides information on viscous corrections to the ideal hydrodynamical approach.

## Key claim

Parton cascade calculations show that only partial thermalization is achieved in relativistic heavy ion collisions, with a longitudinal-to-transverse pressure ratio of approximately 0.8 in the central cell of central collisions, providing a quantitative measure of viscous corrections to ideal hydrodynamics.

## Method

The study uses the Boltzmann equation solved via Monte Carlo methods in parton cascade models, including both elastic (2-to-2) and inelastic (2-to-3 and 3-to-2) gluon interactions. The AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model is employed with both default and string melting options, using ZPC (Zhang's Parton Cascade) for parton evolution, Lund string fragmentation or coalescence for hadronization, and ART (A Relativistic Transport) model for hadron transport. For controlled thermalization studies, an initial local thermal gluon distribution at proper time 0.5 fm/c with uniform space-time rapidity distribution and transverse radius of 5 fm is evolved with 16 test particles per real particle.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal to transverse pressure ratio $P_L/P_T \approx 0.8$ in the central cell in central collisions, consistent across AMPT results with 10 mb parton cross section and radiative transport with $\alpha_s=0.6$
- For elastic parton cascade with dynamical screening, $P_L/P_T$ evolution scales with the combination $\alpha_s T_0$, demonstrated for $T_0=0.5$ GeV and $T_0=1$ GeV with $\alpha_s T_0=0.3$ GeV
- Initial temperature $T_0=0.5$ GeV and initial proper time $\tau=0.5$ fm/c used for controlled thermalization studies
- Space-time rapidity density of 1000 gluons distributed between $\eta_s=-5$ and +5
- $\sigma_{23}$ fixed to 20% of $\sigma_{22}$ for inelastic calculations
- Freeze-out energy density values of $\epsilon=0.2$ GeV/fm$^3$ and $\epsilon=0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$ used to study elliptic flow scaling

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade approach introduced by Geiger and Muller (1991) and developed through VNI and other codes, building on earlier studies of elliptic flow by Zhang, Gyulassy and Ko (1999) and Molnar and Gyulassy (2001) who found that large elastic cross sections (approximately 45 mb) were needed to describe RHIC elliptic flow data. The results provide quantitative input for viscous hydrodynamical calculations, showing that the $P_L/P_T \approx 0.8$ anisotropy level should be expected in improved viscous hydro studies, and that the breaking of $v_2/\epsilon_x$ scaling observed experimentally by Alver (2006) can be explained by freeze-out dynamics.
