# Scattering Processes of Excited Exotic Atoms: Close-Coupling Approach

> The scattering processes of exotic atoms in excited states from hydrogen such as elastic scattering, Stark transitions and Coulomb de-excitation are studied within a close coupling approach. The vacuum polarization and the strong interaction shifts of $ns$-states (in case of hadronic atoms) are taken into account. The differential and integral cross sections of the above processes are calculated to use them as the input in cascade calculations. The effect of closed channels on the scattering processes is investigated.

## Key claim
The close-coupling (CC) approach provides a unified, self-consistent quantum-mechanical treatment of elastic scattering, Stark transitions, and Coulomb de-excitation (CD) for excited exotic hydrogen-like atoms colliding with hydrogen, free from the additional approximations used in prior semiclassical or adiabatic studies; however, the calculated CD cross sections disagree with earlier semiclassical and adiabatic results, particularly regarding the energy dependence at low energies and the distribution over final states.

## Method
The authors solved the close-coupling Schrödinger equations for the system of an exotic atom (muonic, pionic, or antiprotonic hydrogen) in an excited state \((n,l)\) colliding with a hydrogen atom in the ground state. The total wave function was expanded in a basis of channel states including all open channels with principal quantum number \(n \le n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is the entrance channel value), and the coupled radial equations were solved numerically using the Numerov method with standing-wave boundary conditions. The interaction potential included the full Coulomb interactions between all four particles (exotic particle, proton, electron, and target proton), and for hadronic atoms the energy shifts of \(ns\)-states due to vacuum polarization and strong interaction were incorporated.

## Key numbers
- Cross sections were calculated for muonic, pionic, and antiprotonic hydrogen atoms in excited states with \(n = 2\)–\(14\) and kinetic energies relevant for cascade calculations.
- For hadronic atoms, the \(1s\)-state energy shift used was \(\epsilon_{1s} = 721\) eV, with \(ns\)-state shifts taken as \(\epsilon_{ns} = \epsilon_{1s} / n^3\).
- The \(l\)-averaged CD cross sections for \((\mu p)_n + H\) collisions at low energies (below about 1 eV) follow a \(\sim 1/\sqrt{E}\) dependence (Wigner threshold law), in contrast to the \(1/E\) dependence from the semiclassical model of Bracci and Fiorentini (1982) and the adiabatic hidden crossing theory.
- For muonic atoms with \(n \ge 4\), transitions with \(\Delta n > 1\) make up 16%–37% of the total CD cross section at \(E = 1\) eV.
- For pionic atoms \((\pi p)_4 + H\), the maximal suppression of CD cross sections due to \(ns\)-state energy shifts is about a factor of two at very low energy, while at \(E_{\rm cm} > 1\) eV the suppression does not exceed 15%.
- For antiprotonic atoms \((p\bar{p})_8 + H\), the contribution of transitions with \(\Delta n > 1\) to the total CD cross section is about 50%, comparable to the \(\Delta n = 1\) contribution.

## Context
This work extends the close-coupling approach previously applied by the same authors (Popov and Pomerantsev, 2004–2007) to provide a unified treatment of elastic scattering, Stark transitions, and Coulomb de-excitation for exotic atoms. It contradicts the semiclassical model of Bracci and Fiorentini (1982) and the advanced adiabatic approach (AAA) of other authors, which give too small CD cross sections and a different energy dependence at low energies. The calculated cross sections are intended as input for improved cascade models of exotic atoms, enabling more reliable analysis of experimental data from precision spectroscopy of muonic, pionic, and antiprotonic hydrogen.
