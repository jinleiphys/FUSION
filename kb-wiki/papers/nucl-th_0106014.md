# Nuclei, Superheavy Nuclei and Hypermatter in a chiral SU(3)-Modell

> A model based on chiral SU(3)-symmetry in nonlinear realisation is used for the investigation of nuclei, superheavy nuclei, hypernuclei and multistrange nuclear objects (so called MEMOs). The model works very well in the case of nuclei and hypernuclei with one Lambda-particle and rules out MEMOs. Basic observables which are known for nuclei and hypernuclei are reproduced satisfactorily. The model predicts Z=120 and N=172, 184 and 198 as the next shell closures in the region of superheavy nuclei. The calculations have been performed in self-consistent relativistic mean field approximation assuming spherical symmetry. The parameters were adapted to known nuclei.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) model, when fitted to finite nuclei, predicts Z=120 and N=172, 184, and 198 as the next shell closures in superheavy nuclei, while the often-cited Z=114 appears only as a weak subshell closure; the model rules out the existence of metastable exotic multistrange objects (MEMOs) because all hyperon-hyperon potentials are repulsive.

## Method

The authors constructed a Lagrangian density based on chiral SU(3) symmetry in nonlinear realisation, including scalar (σ, ζ), vector (ω, φ, ρ), and glueball (χ) fields, with explicit symmetry breaking terms. They solved the equations of motion in the relativistic mean-field approximation under spherical symmetry and the no-sea approximation, with pairing treated via the Constant-Gap Model. Parameters were adjusted via a least-squares fit to the binding energies, charge diffraction radii, and surface thicknesses of eight spherical nuclei (¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹¹²Sn, ¹²⁴Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb), with additional fits including ²⁶⁴Hs for superheavy extrapolations.

## Key numbers

- The model predicts the next proton shell closure at Z=120, with N=172, 184, and 198 as the next neutron magic numbers.
- The Λ-particle potential in nuclear matter is fixed at -28 MeV.
- The Ξ-particle potential in nuclear matter is repulsive at +30.3 MeV.
- The Λ-potential in Λ-matter is repulsive; the Ξ-potential in Ξ-matter is about +126 MeV at nuclear density.
- The first null in the form factor of oxygen lies at about 2% higher momentum than experiment, and the first maximum deviates by about 7%.
- For lead, the first null is at slightly (∼1%) too small momenta.
- A change of g_{Nω} by 0.25% leads to a variation of χ² of about 85%; varying χ₀ by 2% changes χ² by many orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean-field studies of nuclei and hypernuclei (e.g., Walecka (1974), Ćwiok et al. (1996), Rutz et al. (1997)) by incorporating chiral SU(3) symmetry more closely aligned with QCD. It contradicts non-relativistic models that predict Z=114 as a strong magic number (e.g., Ćwiok et al. (1996)) and rules out the existence of MEMOs previously suggested in Walecka-type models (Schramm et al. (1992, 1993, 1994a, 1994b)). The results enable further investigations of deformation effects in superheavy nuclei and the search for a more fundamental description of hyperon potentials that could allow bound multistrange objects.
