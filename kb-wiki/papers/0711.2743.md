# Pion--deuteron scattering length in Chiral Perturbation Theory up to order χ^{3/2}

> A complete calculation of the corrections to pion-deuteron scattering length up to order $χ^{3/2}$ with $χ=m_π/M_{N}$ is performed. The calculation includes the dispersive contributions and corrections due to the explicit treatment of the $Δ$ resonance. s-wave pion-nucleon scattering parameters are extracted from a combined analysis of modern experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper performs a complete calculation of isospin-conserving corrections to the pion-deuteron scattering length up to order χ^(3/2) (where χ = m_π/M_N), including dispersive contributions and explicit Δ-resonance effects; although these individual corrections are significant, their net effect is very small, which helps resolve a previously observed inconsistency in the extraction of s-wave πN scattering parameters from combined experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculated the pion-deuteron scattering length using Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) up to order χ^(3/2), including two new classes of diagrams: dispersive corrections from the processes πd → NN → πd and πd → γNN → πd, and diagrams with explicit Δ(1232) isobar degrees of freedom. The dispersive corrections were evaluated using the CCF potential for NN distortions and cross-checked with CD Bonn, Paris, and AV18 potentials. The Δ-resonance contributions were computed using seven different NN potentials (phenomenological models with and without explicit Δ, plus EFT-derived wave functions) with the πNΔ coupling constant h_A = 2.77. The analysis also incorporated leading-order isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) effects by introducing the redefined quantity ᾶ^+ that absorbs the poorly known LEC f_1.

## Key numbers

* Net correction from order χ^(3/2) diagrams: δa_πd^Δ + δa_πd^disp = (-0.6 ± 1.5) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
* Dispersive correction (hadronic): δa_πd^disp = (-2.9 ± 1.4) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), averaged over four NN potentials
* Δ-resonance correction: δa_πd^Δ = (2.38 ± 0.40) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), averaged over seven NN potentials
* Electromagnetic dispersive contribution to the real part: -0.1 × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
* Ratio R = lim_{q→0} σ(πd → NN)/σ(πd → γNN) = 2.83 ± 0.04 (from Highland et al.)
* Pion decay constant: F_π = 92.4 MeV
* LEC c_1 = -0.9^(+0.2)_(-0.5) GeV^(-1)
* LEC f_2 = -(0.97 ± 0.38) GeV^(-1)
* LEC f_1: |f_1| ≤ 1.4 GeV^(-1) (from naive dimensional analysis)

## Context

This work extends the ChPT treatment of pion-deuteron scattering by Beane et al. (2005), who calculated corrections up to order χ and found that the sum of NLO diagrams vanishes, and by Baru et al. (2005) who identified the role of recoil corrections. The calculation addresses a problem that emerged after the improved measurement of the π^-p atom width by Gotta et al. (2006), which eliminated the common intersection region for the three experimental bands (pionic hydrogen shift, pionic hydrogen width, and pionic deuterium shift) that previously allowed extraction of {a^+, a^-}. The results enable a consistent extraction of the redefined parameter ᾶ^+ (which absorbs leading ISB uncertainties) and a^- from combined data, and show that explicit Δ treatment renders the previously model-dependent boost corrections negligible. The paper notes that next-to-leading order ISB corrections for the πd system remain to be calculated to match the accuracy achieved for π^-p by Gasser et al. and by Nadia et al.
