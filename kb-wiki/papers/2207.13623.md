# The two-photon decay of X(6900) from light-by-light scattering at the LHC

> The LHCb Collaboration has recently discovered a structure around 6.9 GeV in the double-$J/ψ$ mass distribution, possibly a first fully-charmed tetraquark state $X(6900)$. Based on vector-meson dominance (VMD) such a state should have a significant branching ratio for decaying into two photons. We show that the recorded LHC data for the light-by-light scattering may indeed accommodate for such a state, with a $γγ$ branching ratio of order of $10^{-4}$, which is larger even than the value inferred by the VMD. The spin-parity assignment $0^{-+}$ is in better agreement with the VMD prediction than $0^{++}$, albeit not significantly at the current precision. Further light-by-light scattering data in this region, clarifying the nature of this state, should be obtained in the Run 3 and probably in the high-luminosity phase of the LHC (Run 4 etc.).

## Key claim

The excess seen in ATLAS light-by-light scattering data between 5–10 GeV diphoton invariant mass can be accommodated by the X(6900) tetraquark state, but the fitted two-photon branching ratio of order 10⁻⁴ exceeds the vector-meson dominance (VMD) expectation by about two orders of magnitude, although the discrepancy is not statistically significant given the large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors extended the SuperChic v3.05 Monte Carlo code, which includes perturbative QCD quark-loop contributions to light-by-light scattering, by adding the X(6900) resonance along with known bottomonium states (ηb(1S), ηb(2S), χb0(1P), χb0(2P)). They performed a fit to the unfolded diphoton invariant mass spectrum from ATLAS ultra-peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, treating the ratio ΓX→γγ/√Γtot as the fitting parameter in the narrow resonance approximation, with the total width assumed to be dominated by the di-J/ψ decay.

## Key numbers

- X(6900) mass: 6886 ± 11 ± 11 MeV (interference scenario) or 6905 ± 11 ± 7 MeV (no-interference scenario) from LHCb
- Di-J/ψ width: 168 ± 33 ± 69 MeV (interference) or 80 ± 19 ± 33 MeV (no-interference) from LHCb
- Fitted two-photon branching ratio: (4.0⁺⁰·⁹₋₁·₁) × 10⁻⁴ (interference scenario) or (5.6⁺¹·³₋₁·⁶) × 10⁻⁴ (no-interference scenario)
- Fitted two-photon width: 67⁺¹⁵₋₁₉ keV (interference) or 45⁺¹¹₋₁₄ keV (no-interference)
- Integrated fiducial cross section with X(6900): σfidX = 121 ± 20 nb; without X: σfid⁰ = 76 nb; experimental value: σfidexp = 120 ± 17 (stat.) ± 13 (syst.) ± 4 (lumi.) nb
- Improvement over Standard Model prediction: about 2.3σ
- VMD estimate for scalar: B_VMD^S(X→γγ) = (2.8 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁶
- VMD estimate for pseudoscalar: B_VMD^PS(X→γγ) = (6.4 ± 0.8) × 10⁻⁶

## Context

This work extends the LHCb discovery of the X(6900) structure in di-J/ψ mass distributions (LHCb, 2020) by connecting it to the excess observed in ATLAS light-by-light scattering data (ATLAS, 2020). The VMD estimates follow the approach of Barger (1975) and Redlich (2000), differing from the non-gauge-invariant treatment of Esposito (2021). The results enable future LHC Run 3 and high-luminosity measurements to clarify whether the X(6900) is a single tetraquark state or multiple resonances, with the Landau-Yang theorem potentially constraining its spin-parity assignments.
