# Nuclear cluster structure effect on elliptic and triangular flows in heavy-ion collisions

> The initial geometry effect on collective flows, which are inherited from initial projectile structure, is studied in relativistic heavy-ion collisions of $^{12}$C + Au by using a multi-phase transport model (AMPT). Elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) which are significantly resulted from the chain and triangle structure of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ with three-$α$ clusters, respectively, in central $^{12}\mathrm{C}$+$^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ collisions are compared with the flow from the Woods-Saxon distribution of nucleons in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$. $v_3/v_2$ is proposed as a probe to distinguish the pattern of $α$-clustered $^{12}\mathrm{C}$. This study demonstrates that the initial geometry of the collision zone inherited from nuclear structure can be explored by collective flow at the final stage in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The initial geometry of the collision zone inherited from the nuclear cluster structure of the projectile can be explored through collective flow at the final stage in heavy-ion collisions; specifically, the ratio of triangular flow to elliptic flow ($v_3/v_2$) is proposed as a probe to distinguish between chain and triangle patterns of $\alpha$-clustered $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, with the caveat that the observable is most effective when compared against a reference system such as a nucleus without exotic structure or an isobar nucleus.

## Method

The study used the multi-phase transport model (AMPT) to simulate $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ + $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and 10 GeV. The initial nucleon distribution in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ was configured in three ways: three $\alpha$ clusters arranged in a chain structure, three $\alpha$ clusters arranged in a triangle structure, and a Woods-Saxon distribution of nucleons (no clustering), with the $\alpha$-cluster configurations obtained from an extended quantum molecular dynamics model (EQMD). The participant plane method was employed event-by-event to calculate elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) from final-state hadrons, and the initial coordinate anisotropies were quantified as participant eccentricity coefficients ($\epsilon_2$ and $\epsilon_3$).

## Key numbers

- For the Triangle structure of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, the average radial center of an $\alpha$ cluster $r_c$ = 1.8 fm with width $\sigma_{r_c}$ = 0.1 fm; for the Chain structure, $r_c$ = 2.5 fm with $\sigma_{r_c}$ = 0.1 fm for two $\alpha$ clusters and the third at the center.
- Most central collisions were defined by impact parameter $b < 2$ fm, corresponding to $\mathrm{N_{track}} > 280$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and $\mathrm{N_{track}} > 90$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 10 GeV, for charged pions, kaons, and protons in rapidity window $-2 < y < 2$ and transverse momentum $0.2 < p_T < 6$ GeV/c.
- The ratio $v_3/v_2$ of final hadrons showed an increasing trend with $\mathrm{N_{track}}$ for the Triangle structure pattern of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, while it remained approximately flat for the Chain and Woods-Saxon patterns at both collision energies.
- The values of $\epsilon_3\{PP\}/\epsilon_2\{PP\}$ dropped in the order of Triangle structure, Woods-Saxon, and Chain structure pattern of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Broniowski and Ruiz Arriola (2014) and others that the intrinsic geometry of light nuclei can be studied in heavy-ion collisions through elliptic and triangular flow, using the AMPT model which has been successful in describing collective flow at RHIC and LHC energies. The study enables experimental searches for $\alpha$-clustering in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ by measuring $v_3/v_2$ in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$+$^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ collisions relative to a reference system, such as collisions with a non-exotic nucleus or an isobar nucleus, to distinguish between chain, triangle, and uniform nucleon distributions.
