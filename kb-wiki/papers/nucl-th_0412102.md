# Nuclear Three-body Force Effect on a Kaon Condensate in Neutron Star Matter

> We explore the effects of a microscopic nuclear three-body force on the threshold baryon density for kaon condensation in chemical equilibrium neutron star matter and on the composition of the kaon condensed phase in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. Our results show that the nuclear three-body force affects strongly the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy and consequently reduces considerably the critical density for kaon condensation provided that the proton strangeness content is not very large. The dependence of the threshold density on the symmetry energy becomes weaker as the proton strangeness content increases. The kaon condensed phase of neutron star matter turns out to be proton-rich instead of neutron-rich. The three-body force has an important influence on the composition of the kaon condensed phase. Inclusion of the three-body force contribution in the nuclear symmetry energy results in a significant reduction of the proton and kaon fractions in the kaon condensed phase which is more proton-rich in the case of no three-body force. Our results are compared to other theoretical predictions by adopting different models for the nuclear symmetry energy. The possible implications of our results for the neutron star structure are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The nuclear three-body force (TBF) reduces the critical baryon density for kaon condensation in neutron star matter by about 8% to 25%, provided the proton strangeness content is not very large; the effect becomes smaller as the strangeness content increases.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne V18 two-body force supplemented by a microscopic three-body force constructed from the meson-exchange current method. The kaon-nucleon interaction was described by the Kaplan-Nelson chiral Lagrangian, and the coupled equations for chemical equilibrium, charge neutrality, and energy minimization were solved to determine the threshold density and composition of the kaon condensed phase.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for kaon condensation with TBF included: range 2.4ρ₀ to 3.8ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the empirical saturation density of nuclear matter).
- Critical density without TBF: range 2.6ρ₀ to 5.0ρ₀.
- For a₃mₛ = -134 MeV (low strangeness), TBF reduces critical density from 5.0ρ₀ to 3.8ρ₀, a reduction of more than 20%.
- For a₃mₛ = -310 MeV (high strangeness), TBF reduces critical density from 2.6ρ₀ to 2.4ρ₀, a reduction of less than 10%.
- Central value adopted for a₃mₛ = -222 MeV, close to the lattice gauge calculation value of -231 MeV with error less than 4%.
- Maximum neutron star mass with TBF and no kaons: about 2.3 M☉; without TBF: about 1.6 M☉ (for a₃mₛ = -222 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation by Thorsson et al. (1994) and Kubis (2003), who used phenomenological parametrizations or variational approaches for the nuclear symmetry energy. The authors' BHF approach with a microscopic three-body force provides a stiffer high-density symmetry energy than the variational UV14+TNI interaction used by Kubis (2003), leading to qualitatively different predictions for the composition of the condensed phase. The results enable more realistic calculations of neutron star structure and maximum mass, and highlight the need to resolve discrepancies between BHF and variational predictions for the high-density symmetry energy.
