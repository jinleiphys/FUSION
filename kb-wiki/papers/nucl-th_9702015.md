# Polarization Observables in Phi Meson Photoproduction and the Strangeness Content of the Proton

> The contributions of direct knockout processes, in addition to the diffractive production and one-pion-exchange processes associated with the phi-rho-pi and phi-gamma-pi couplings, to the polarization observables of the phi photoproduction from proton are calculated. We make use of Pomeron-photon analogy and a relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model. We find that some of the double polarization observables are very sensitive to a possible (s s-bar) admixture in the proton. It arises from the difference in the spin structures of the three different amplitudes. This suggests that such measurements could be very useful to probe the strangeness content in the proton.

# Polarization Observables in Phi Meson Photoproduction and the Strangeness Content of the Proton

## Key claim

Measurements of double polarization observables in phi meson photoproduction could provide a clean signature of a possible s\bar{s} admixture in the proton, even with less than 1% strangeness admixture, because the direct knockout and diffractive production mechanisms have very different spin (helicity) dependence. The claim is conditional on the validity of the relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model and the Pomeron-photon analogy used to describe the diffractive amplitude.

## Method

The authors calculate the contributions of three mechanisms to phi photoproduction from the proton: diffractive production via vector-meson dominance (VDM) with Pomeron exchange, one-pion-exchange (OPE) processes, and direct knockout processes arising from a hypothesized s\bar{s} component in the proton wave function. The knockout amplitudes are evaluated using a relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model for the nucleon wave function, with the VDM amplitude determined by fitting to existing cross section data at W = 2 GeV. The helicity amplitudes are then used to compute single and double polarization observables, with particular focus on the longitudinal beam-target asymmetry L_BT.

## Key numbers

- The VDM diffractive cross section is parameterized as (dσ/dt)_VDM = σ_γ(W) b_φ exp(-b_φ |t - t_max|) with b_φ = 4.01 GeV^{-2} and σ_γ(W) = 0.2 μb around W = 2 GeV.
- The unpolarized cross section calculations assume a strangeness probability B^2 = 1% and |a_0| = |a_1| = 1/√2 for the spin-0 and spin-1 fractions of the s\bar{s} pair.
- The longitudinal beam-target asymmetry L_BT shows significant deviation from the VDM+OPE prediction even with B^2 = 0.25% strangeness admixture at W = 2.155 GeV.
- The optimal range of initial photon energy to measure the s\bar{s} component is expected to be around 2-3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Henley, Krein, and Williams (1992) and Titov, Oh, and Yang (1994, 1997) on phi electroproduction, where the direct knockout mechanism was found to be comparable to VDM at moderately large momentum transfer but difficult to disentangle from cross section measurements alone. The paper proposes that polarization observables, rather than unpolarized cross sections, can distinguish the mechanisms because the VDM, OPE, and knockout amplitudes have distinct helicity structures. The results suggest that new experiments at current electron facilities could probe the strangeness content of the proton, which has been indicated by deep inelastic scattering measurements of nucleon spin structure, BNL elastic νp scattering, and analyses of the πN sigma term, though alternative explanations without strangeness also exist.
