# Convergence in charmonium structure: light-front wave functions from basis light-front quantization and Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We present a systematic comparison of charmonium light-front wave functions obtained through two complementary non-perturbative approaches: Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE). Key observables include the charge form factor, gravitational form factors, light-cone distribution amplitudes, decay constants, and two-photon transition form factors. Despite their distinct theoretical foundations and model parameters, the predictions from BLFQ and DSE exhibit remarkable agreement across all observables. This convergence validates both frameworks for studying charmonium structure and highlights the complementary strengths of Hamiltonian-based (BLFQ) and Lagrangian-based (DSE) methods in addressing non-perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

Despite their distinct theoretical foundations and model parameters, the predictions from Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) exhibit remarkable agreement across all five sets of observables, validating both frameworks for studying charmonium structure; however, the BLFQ light-front wave functions are broader than the DSE ones in both transverse momentum and longitudinal directions, and the DSE results are presented only as central values without uncertainty estimates due to the difficulty of estimating the Rainbow-Ladder truncation error.

## Method

The authors computed charmonium light-front wave functions (LFWFs) using two complementary non-perturbative approaches: BLFQ diagonalizes the light-front QCD Hamiltonian in a symmetry-preserving basis with a truncation parameter N_max, while DSE solves QCD's Green's functions in the continuum using the Maris-Tandy model with Rainbow-Ladder truncation, projecting covariant Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes to the light cone via k_perp-dependent moments. Using the same LFWFs from each approach, they calculated five sets of observables: charge form factor, gravitational form factors A(Q^2) and D(Q^2), light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs), decay constants, and two-photon transition form factors.

## Key numbers

- Decay constant f_P from BLFQ: 0.414(78) GeV; from DSE: 0.396 GeV; both close to the Lattice QCD result 0.395(2) GeV (Davies 2010)
- D-term D(0) for eta_c from BLFQ: -4.6; from DSE: -4.1
- Spin-singlet normalization I_updown-downup from BLFQ: 0.926; from DSE: 0.860
- Spin-triplet normalization I_upup + I_downdown from BLFQ: 0.074; from DSE: 0.140
- BLFQ adopted N_max = 8 for central values, corresponding to UV scale Lambda_UV = 2.8 GeV approximately equal to M_c\bar{c}
- DSE results evolved from mu_0 = 2.6 GeV to mu = 2.8 GeV to match the BLFQ energy scale

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons between non-perturbative approaches for hadron structure, such as those by Leitao (2017) and Lan (2024), by systematically comparing BLFQ and DSE predictions for charmonium across multiple observables. The agreement validates the BSA projection method of Shi (2018, 2020) for heavy quarkonia and demonstrates that the leading Fock sector LFWFs projected from Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are sufficiently accurate for describing hadron structure, while noting that for light mesons like the pion, higher Fock states are more important. This convergence enables future studies of more complex systems such as exotic hadrons, heavy-light mesons, and nuclei, and strengthens the reliability of both methods for interpreting experimental data from Jefferson Lab, the LHC, and future electron-ion colliders.
