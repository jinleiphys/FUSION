# Magnetic Radius of the Deuteron

> The root-mean square radius of the deuteron magnetic moment distribution, $ r_{Md}$, is calculated for several realistic models of the $NN$--interaction. For the Paris potential the result is $r_{Md} = 2.312 \pm 0.010 $ fm. The dependence of $r_{Md} $ on the choice of $NN$ model, relativistic effects and meson exchange currents is investigated. The experimental value of $r_{Md}$ is also considered. The necessity of new precise measurements of the deuteron magnetic form factor at low values of $Q^2$ is stressed.

# Magnetic Radius of the Deuteron

## Key claim
The root-mean-square magnetic radius of the deuteron calculated for the Paris potential is 2.312 ± 0.010 fm, which disagrees significantly with the experimental value of 1.90 ± 0.14 fm extracted from existing low-Q² elastic electron-deuteron scattering data. The authors argue that this discrepancy is real and not resolved by including relativistic effects or meson exchange currents, which contribute only small corrections.

## Method
The magnetic radius r_Md was calculated from the derivative of the deuteron magnetic form factor at Q²=0 using the non-relativistic impulse approximation (NRIA) with several realistic NN potentials. The authors derived an analytic expression for <r²_Md> in terms of the deuteron S- and D-state wave functions, the nucleon magnetic and charge radii, and the D-state probability p_d. Corrections from relativistic effects (Darwin-Foldy and nucleon motion terms) and meson exchange currents (using a simple exponential parametrization from Lomon) were estimated separately.

## Key numbers
- For the Paris potential: r_Md = 2.312 ± 0.010 fm
- Experimental value extracted from polynomial fit to existing data: r_Md = 1.90 ± 0.14 fm
- Deuteron charge radius from best analysis of elastic ed-scattering: r_Cd = 2.128 ± 0.011 fm
- Deuteron charge radius from atomic isotope shift measurements (Hansch et al.): r_Cd = 2.136 ± 0.005 fm
- Input proton charge radius: <r²_Ep>^(1/2) = 0.862 ± 0.012 fm
- Input neutron charge radius: <r²_En> = -0.1194 ± 0.0018 fm² (with a more recent consensus value of -0.1140 ± 0.0026 fm²)
- For the Reid soft core potential, MEC correction parameters: β₁ = 0.0288, α₁ = 0.16 fm²

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of deuteron static properties by calculating the magnetic radius independently from the better-known charge radius, using the same formalism as prior NN potential studies (Paris, Reid, and other realistic potentials). The authors note that unlike the nucleon case, where scaling laws imply equal charge and magnetic radii for the proton, no such relation holds for the deuteron. The paper identifies the need for new precise measurements of the deuteron magnetic form factor at low Q², similar to the high-precision measurements of the longitudinal part A(Q²) used by Platchkov and collaborators for the charge radius. Improved knowledge of r_Md would enable better tests of NN interaction models, more accurate calculations of hyperfine splitting in deuterium atomic levels (following work by Azam), and could serve as a proxy for the neutron magnetic radius until direct neutron-electron scattering measurements become available.
