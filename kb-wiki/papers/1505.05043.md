# Spurious finite-size instabilities in nuclear energy density functionals: spin channel

> It has been recently shown, that some Skyrme functionals can lead to non-converging results in the calculation of some properties of atomic nuclei. A previous study has pointed out a possible link between these convergence problems and the appearance of finite-size instabilities in symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) around saturation density. We show that the finite-size instabilities not only affect the ground state properties of atomic nuclei, but they can also influence the calculations of vibrational excited states in finite nuclei. We perform systematic fully-self consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations in spherical doubly-magic nuclei. We employ several Skyrme functionals and vary the isoscalar and isovector coupling constants of the time-odd term $\mathbf{s}\cdot Δ\mathbf{s}$ . We determine critical values of these coupling constants beyond which the RPA calculations do not converge because RPA the stability matrix becomes non-positive.By comparing the RPA calculations of atomic nuclei with those performed for SNM we establish a correspondence between the critical densities in the infinite system and the critical coupling constants for which the RPA calculations do not converge. We find a quantitative stability criterion to detect finite-size instabilities related to the spin $\mathbf{s}\cdot Δ\mathbf{s}$ term of a functional. This criterion could be easily implemented into the standard fitting protocols to fix the coupling constants of the Skyrme functional.

## Key claim

The paper shows that finite-size instabilities in the spin channel of Skyrme energy density functionals, previously linked to convergence problems in ground-state calculations, also affect the calculation of vibrational excited states in finite nuclei; the authors establish a quantitative stability criterion based on the critical density at which a pole appears in symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) RPA calculations, which can be used to detect such instabilities before they manifest in finite-nucleus calculations.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations in spherical doubly-magic nuclei (⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Ni, ¹³²Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb) using six Skyrme functionals (T44, SAMi, BSk27, SIII, SKO', SLy5). They systematically varied the isoscalar and isovector coupling constants of the time-odd term **s**·Δ**s** through a multiplicative parameter γ, and determined the critical values beyond which the RPA stability matrix becomes non-positive (producing imaginary eigenvalues). The results were compared with analytical RPA calculations in infinite nuclear matter to establish a correspondence between critical densities in the infinite system and critical coupling constants in finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the SIII functional, the critical isoscalar coupling constant (best extrapolation to infinite basis) is 32.37 MeV fm⁵, with lower limit 27.28 MeV fm⁵ and upper limit 34.05 MeV fm⁵; the critical isovector coupling constant is 43.10 MeV fm⁵ (lower 36.55, upper 45.25).
- For the SLy5 functional, the critical isoscalar coupling constant is 47.69 MeV fm⁵ (lower 41.26, upper 49.80); the critical isovector coupling constant is 43.47 MeV fm⁵ (lower 36.90, upper 45.63).
- For the T44 functional, the critical isoscalar coupling constant is 47.28 MeV fm⁵ (lower 39.06, upper 49.96); the critical isovector coupling constant is -2.01 MeV fm⁵ (lower -13.02, upper 1.60), indicating the nominal value is already unstable.
- For the BSk27 functional, the critical isoscalar coupling constant is 28.22 MeV fm⁵ (lower 22.11, upper 30.05); the critical isovector coupling constant is 38.81 MeV fm⁵ (lower 33.16, upper 40.67).
- For the SAMi functional, the critical isoscalar coupling constant is 47.54 MeV fm⁵ (lower 41.30, upper 49.58); the critical isovector coupling constant is 45.15 MeV fm⁵ (lower 38.81, upper 47.23).
- The critical density ρ_c for the isoscalar channel relative to saturation density ρ_sat is 1.13 for SIII, 1.01 for SLy5, 0.96 for T44, 1.11 for BSk27, and 0.97 for SAMi; for the isovector channel it is 1.05 for SIII, 0.97 for SLy5, 0.96 for T44, 1.00 for BSk27, and 1.00 for SAMi.
- The SKO' functional is spin-unstable in the isoscalar channel at very low densities (ρ ≈ 0.14 fm⁻³), making it unsuitable for RPA calculations.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Hellemans et al. (2013), who established a relation between finite-size instabilities in the scalar-isovector channel and zero-energy modes in infinite nuclear matter RPA, to the vector (spin) channel of Skyrme functionals. The study was motivated by convergence problems detected in Time-Dependent-Hartree-Fock calculations by Fracasso et al. (2012) and Cranked-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Hellemans et al. (2012), which were associated with time-odd terms depending on the Laplacian and gradient of the spin density. The stability criterion derived here can be implemented into standard fitting protocols to prevent finite-size instabilities during the optimization of Skyrme functional coupling constants, as previously done for the scalar channel by Pastore et al. (2013).
