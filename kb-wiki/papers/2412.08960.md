# Magnetic polarisability of octet baryons near the physical quark-mass point

> The magnetic polarisabilities of octet baryons are calculated close to the physical quark-mass point using the background field method in lattice QCD. This first calculation draws on the identification and elimination of exceptional configurations that have hindered previous attempts. The origin of the exceptional configuration problem lies in the use of a Wilson-type fermion action on electro-quenched gauge field configurations, where the dynamical-fermion gauge-field generation algorithm the electric charges of the quarks. Changes in the fermion determinant that would suppress some gauge fields in the background magnetic field are neglected, leaving improbable gauge fields that generate large additive mass renormalisations which manifest as significant outliers in correlation-function distributions. An algorithm for the systematic identification and removal of these exceptional configurations is described. We find the light up and down quarks to be problematic, particularly the up quark with its larger electric charge. The heavier mass of the strange quark protects the hyperon correlation functions to some extent. However, these also benefit from the removal of exceptional configurations. In many cases, the magnetic polarisability is calculated with good precision. We find our results to be in accord with the behaviour anticipated by chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The magnetic polarisabilities of octet baryons are calculated close to the physical quark-mass point using lattice QCD, but only after developing and applying a systematic algorithm to identify and remove "exceptional configurations" that had previously prevented such calculations; the results are found to be in accord with the behaviour anticipated by chiral perturbation theory, though the light up and down quarks remain particularly problematic.

## Method

The authors use the uniform background field method in lattice QCD on electro-quenched gauge field configurations generated by the PACS-CS collaboration, employing a Wilson-clover fermion action with background-field-corrected clover coefficient. Baryon correlation functions are calculated at five magnetic field strengths corresponding to \(k_d = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2\) on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice with \(m_\pi = 156\) MeV, using eigenmode projection at the sink and Landau-mode projection at the hadronic level to isolate the ground state. The magnetic polarisability is extracted from a ratio of spin-field aligned and anti-aligned correlation functions that eliminates the magnetic moment contribution, with a weighted average of fit windows using an Akaike information criterion.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass of the lightest ensemble: \(m_\pi = 156\) MeV, with lattice spacing \(a = 0.0933(13)\) fm and 197 gauge configurations.
* Non-zero magnetic field strengths used: \(eB = \pm 0.087\), \(\pm 0.174\), and \(\pm 0.348\) GeV\(^2\).
* The rate of exceptional configurations identified for the worst-case proton (doubly represented up quark at \(k_d = \pm 4\)) is only 1.4%, compared to a quenched occurrence of 6% for \(m_\pi = 306(7)\) MeV reported in Boinepalli (2004) using the FLIC fermion action.
* The spatial cubic lattice at the lightest quark mass has length \(L = 3.0\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of magnetic polarisabilities by Hall (2014), Bignell (2018, 2020), and Kabelitz (octet) that were unable to probe the near-physical quark-mass regime due to large statistical uncertainties from exceptional configurations. The authors identify the origin of this problem in the use of electro-quenched configurations where the fermion determinant does not suppress improbable gauge fields, and they provide a systematic algorithm for removal. The results enable direct comparison with chiral perturbation theory predictions at the physical point and open the way for future calculations that include the background field in the gauge-field generation process.
