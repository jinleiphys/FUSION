# Triplet paring in neutron matter

> The presence of superfluidity in neutron star interiors can affect the cooling of neutron stars in intricate ways, enhancing certain mechanisms and suppressing others. Model calculations employing realistic nuclear potentials in Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory generally suggest the development of a $^3$P$_2$-$^3$F$_2$ pairing gap, and therefore the presence of superfluidity in dense neutron-star matter.Improved models that go beyond conventional mean-field calculations by including polarization effects suggest a suppression of the triplet gap. We have evaluated the pairing interaction by summing the ``parquet'' Feynman diagrams which include both ladder and ring diagrams systematically, {\em plus\} a set of important non-parquet diagrams, making this the most comprehensive diagram-based approach presently available. Our results suggest a radical suppression of the $^3$P$_2$-$^3$F$_2$ triplet pairing gap and an enhancement of $^3$P$_0$ pairing.

## Key claim

The authors find that including the most comprehensive set of many-body diagrams (parquet plus important non-parquet diagrams) leads to a radical suppression of the \(^3\)P\(_2\)-\(^3\)F\(_2\) triplet pairing gap in neutron matter and an enhancement of \(^3\)P\(_0\) pairing, reversing the long-standing expectation that \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing dominates in neutron star cores. The effect is attributed to the strong suppression of the spin-orbit interaction by short-ranged screening, which prevents neutrons from coming close enough to feel the attractive spin-orbit force.

## Method

The authors calculated the pairing interaction in neutron matter by self-consistently summing all ring and ladder Feynman diagrams (the "parquet" class) plus a set of important non-parquet "twisted chain" diagrams, using the operator representation of the Reid \(v_8\) and Argonne AV18 nucleon-nucleon interactions. The pairing gap equation was solved in the \(^1\)S\(_0\), \(^3\)P\(_0\), and coupled \(^3\)P\(_2\)-\(^3\)F\(_2\) channels, using a non-interacting single-particle spectrum \(\epsilon(k) = \hbar^2 k^2/2m\) and the angle-averaged approximation for the quasiparticle energy denominator.

## Key numbers

- The \(^3\)P\(_2\)-\(^3\)F\(_2\) pairing gap becomes "minute" (effectively zero) when many-body effects are included, compared to a gap of order 0.1–1 MeV at the mean-field level for Fermi wave numbers \(k_F \le 1.8\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- A measurable \(^3\)P\(_0\) pairing gap appears when many-body effects are included, whereas with the bare interaction the \(^3\)P\(_0\) gap is only present when the spin-orbit force is turned off.
- The \(^1\)S\(_0\) pairing gap is somewhat reduced by many-body effects, consistent with earlier work; the calculation was limited to densities corresponding to \(k_F \le 1.8\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- The effective mass ratio is expected to be between 1.05 and 0.95, which scales the gap but is negligible compared to the order-of-magnitude changes reported (the effect can change the gap by two orders of magnitude).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic many-body approach of Krotscheck et al. (v3bcs, v4) by including the spin-orbit term necessary for triplet pairing, and contradicts the long-standing narrative initiated by Tamagaki (1970) and others that \(^3\)P\(_2\)-\(^3\)F\(_2\) pairing prevails in neutron star matter. The results agree qualitatively with the suppression of the triplet gap found by Schwenk and Friman (2004) using polarization corrections, but go further by showing a reversal to \(^3\)P\(_0\) pairing. The findings enable more confident consideration of neutron star cooling scenarios that do not involve \(^3\)P\(_2\) superfluidity, while motivating study of the effect of \(^3\)P\(_0\) pairing on cooling curves.
