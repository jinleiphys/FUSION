# Magnetic susceptibility of a strongly interacting thermal medium with 2+1 quark flavors

> Thermodynamics of the three-flavor quark-meson model with axial anomaly is studied in the presence of external magnetic fields. The nonperturbative functional renormalization group is employed in order to incorporate quantum and thermal fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation. We calculate the magnetic susceptibility with proper renormalization and find that the system is diamagnetic in the hadron phase and paramagnetic in the hot plasma phase. The obtained values of the magnetic susceptibility are in reasonable agreement with the data from first-principle lattice QCD. Comparison with the mean-field approximation, the Hadron Resonance Gas model and a free gas with temperature-dependent masses is also made.

# Magnetic susceptibility of a strongly interacting thermal medium with 2+1 quark flavors

## Key claim
The functional renormalization group applied to the three-flavor quark-meson model with axial anomaly can reproduce the qualitative temperature dependence of the magnetic susceptibility seen in lattice QCD, showing diamagnetism in the hadron phase and paramagnetism in the hot plasma phase, but the model fails to reproduce the inverse magnetic catalysis (decrease of the chiral pseudo-critical temperature with increasing magnetic field) observed in lattice simulations.

## Method
The authors employed the functional renormalization group (FRG) within the local-potential approximation to solve the Wetterich equation for the three-flavor quark-meson model coupled to an external magnetic field, using optimized regulator functions and a two-dimensional Taylor expansion of the scale-dependent effective potential around its running minimum. The flow equations were integrated from the ultraviolet cutoff scale k = 1 GeV down to k = 0, with parameters adjusted to reproduce pion and kaon decay constants and meson masses at zero temperature and magnetic field. The magnetic susceptibility was extracted from the subtracted pressure ΔP = [P(T,B) - P(T,0)] - [P(0,B) - P(0,0)] through polynomial fitting, with renormalization performed by subtracting the divergent T = 0 contribution.

## Key numbers
- The chiral pseudo-critical temperature at B = 0 from three-flavor FRG is T_c = 181 MeV, used for temperature normalization in comparisons with lattice data
- The magnetic susceptibility χ(T) from FRG at T > T_c is approximately 30% lower than lattice QCD results from Bali et al. (2014)
- The pion decay constant f_π = 91.8 MeV and kaon decay constant f_K = 112.3 MeV at eB = T = 0 from the FRG calculation
- Light quark constituent mass M_u = M_d = 298.1 MeV and strange quark mass M_s = 430.8 MeV at eB = T = 0
- The sigma meson mass minimum shifts from approximately 180 MeV at B = 0 to approximately 220 MeV at eB = 14 m_π^2, indicating an increase of T_c with magnetic field

## Context
This work extends the FRG analysis of the three-flavor quark-meson model by Mitter and Schaefer (2013) to nonzero magnetic fields, and provides the first FRG calculation of the magnetic susceptibility with 2+1 quark flavors. The results are compared with lattice QCD simulations by Levkova and DeTar (2013), Bonati et al. (2013), and Bali et al. (2014), as well as with mean-field approximation and a non-interacting gas with temperature-dependent masses. The model's failure to reproduce inverse magnetic catalysis (the decrease of T_c with B seen in lattice simulations by Bali et al., 2011) suggests that chiral effective models in their current form cannot capture this phenomenon, though they may still describe weak-field magnetic properties correctly. The calculation enables quantitative comparison between effective model predictions and lattice data for the magnetic susceptibility across the full temperature range.
