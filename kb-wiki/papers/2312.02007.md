# Far-from-equilibrium attractors for massive kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation

> In this proceedings contribution, we summarize recent findings concerning the presence of early- and late-time attractors in non-conformal kinetic theory. We study the effects of varying both the initial momentum-space anisotropy and initialization times using an exact solution of the 0+1D boost-invariant Boltzmann equation with a mass- and temperature-dependent relaxation time. Our findings support the existence of a longitudinal pressure attractor, but they do not support the existence of distinct attractors for the bulk viscous and shear pressures. Considering a large set of integral moments, we show that for moments with greater than one power of longitudinal momentum squared, both early- and late-time attractors are present.

# Far-from-equilibrium attractors for massive kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation

## Key claim

The authors find evidence for a longitudinal pressure attractor in non-conformal kinetic theory, but do not find distinct attractors for the bulk viscous and shear pressures when considered separately; the attractor behavior is present only for moments with greater than one power of longitudinal momentum squared, and the existence of early-time attractors depends on the specific moment considered.

## Method

The authors solve the 0+1D boost-invariant Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) exactly for a massive gas, using a temperature- and mass-dependent relaxation time. They compute all integral moments of the one-particle distribution function and compare the exact time evolution to first-order Navier-Stokes predictions from both the 14-moment approximation and the Chapman-Enskog approximation, scanning over initial momentum-space anisotropy parameters and initialization times.

## Key numbers

- Mass set to m = 1 GeV and initial temperature T₀ = 1 GeV for the attractor study
- Initial anisotropy parameter α₀ = 1/√(1+ξ₀) scanned over values {0.12, 0.25, 0.5, 1, 2} at fixed initialization time τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c
- Initialization times scanned over τ₀ ∈ {0.01, 0.02, 0.04} fm/c with ξ₀ = 0
- The function γ(ˆm) goes to unity in the massless limit and grows linearly at large m/T, with γ(ˆm) ≥ 1 meaning a massive gas always relaxes more slowly than a massless one

## Context

This work extends prior exact solutions for a massive gas by Florkowski et al. (2014) by computing all moments of the distribution function and using a temperature- and mass-dependent relaxation time, going beyond prior works that assumed constant or inversely-proportional-to-temperature relaxation times. The findings are consistent with and expand upon recent research that found late- and early-time attractors in the scaled longitudinal pressure but not in the shear and bulk viscous corrections separately. The authors released their computational code along with the paper, enabling future studies of attractor behavior in heavy-ion collision models.
