# Study of proton-proton Scattering using Phase Function Method

> Background: The study of np and pp scattering, central to understanding nuclear force, remains an optional topic in many undergraduate nuclear physics curriculum. Purpose: The main thrust of this paper is to study pp scattering using the phase function method to obtain the observed S-wave phase shifts and cross-sections at various energies. Methods: The pp interaction has been modeled by choosing the Malfliet-Tjon potential for the nuclear part along with the screened Coulomb potential. The phase equation has been solved to obtain scattering phase shifts using the fourth-order RK method (RK-4). Results: The interaction potential obtained from optimized parameters matches well with the realistic Argonne V18 potential for 1S0 state of pp scattering and the scattering phase shifts as well as the cross-section for energies ranging from 1-350 MeV are in good agreement with expected data. Conclusion: Introducing the phase function method for S-wave (l=0) could bring this interesting study of nucleon-nucleon scattering to the undergraduate classroom.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that proton-proton scattering phase shifts and cross sections for the 1S0 state can be accurately reproduced using the phase function method with a Malfliet-Tjon nuclear potential combined with a screened Coulomb (Hulthén) potential, but only after optimizing the screening parameter to a specific value (a = 5 fm) that minimizes the mean square error to 0.29.

## Method

The authors model the proton-proton interaction using the Malfliet-Tjon potential for the nuclear part (three parameters: attractive strength, repulsive strength, and range) plus a screened Coulomb potential represented by the Hulthén potential. They solve the first-order Riccati-type phase equation for S-wave (l=0) scattering using the fourth-order Runge-Kutta method (RK-4), with the phase equation integrated from the origin to an asymptotic distance where the potential becomes negligible (rf = 30 fm). Model parameters are optimized by minimizing the mean square error between simulated and expected phase shifts from the Granada database using a Variational Monte Carlo technique.

## Key numbers

- Mean square error (MSE) of 0.29 for the optimized model with screening parameter a = 5 fm and final integration distance rf = 30 fm
- Optimized Malfliet-Tjon parameters: Vr = 7171.2823 MeV-fm, Va = 1570.9068 MeV-fm, μA = 2.45 fm^-1
- Interaction potential depth Vd = -99.44 MeV at equilibrium distance rd = 0.85 fm, compared to Argonne AV-18 potential depth of approximately -103.5 MeV at rd = 0.89 fm
- Coulomb barrier height VCB = 0.11 MeV at distance rC = 4.3 fm
- Scattering phase shifts computed for laboratory energies from 1 to 350 MeV, with absolute percentage errors ranging from 0.11% (at 50 MeV) to 5.99% (at 300 MeV)
- Mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) of 2.18% across all energies
- Example phase shifts: at 1 MeV lab energy, expected 32.677 degrees versus obtained 34.285 degrees (4.92% error); at 350 MeV, expected -10.828 degrees versus obtained -10.397 degrees (3.98% error)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of neutron-proton scattering using the phase function method (Awasthi et al., 2024) to the more challenging case of proton-proton scattering, where the long-range Coulomb interaction complicates the scattering formalism. The approach uses the Malfliet-Tjon potential as a simpler alternative to realistic potentials like the Argonne V18 potential (which requires up to 40 parameters) and the Yukawa potential (which fails above 50 MeV). The paper enables undergraduate and graduate students to compute nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and cross sections using a straightforward numerical method, making this topic more accessible in nuclear physics curricula.
