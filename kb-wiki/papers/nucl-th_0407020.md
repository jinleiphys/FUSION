# Alpha matter on a lattice

> We obtain the equation of state of interacting alpha matter and the critical temperature of Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha particles within an effective scalar field theory. We start from a non-relativistic model of uniform alpha matter interacting with attractive two-body and repulsive three-body potentials and reformulate this model as a O(2) symmetric scalar ϕ^6 field theory with negative quartic and positive sextic interactions. Upon restricting the Matsubara sums, near the temperature of Bose-Einstein condensation, to the zeroth order modes we further obtain an effective classical theory in three spatial dimensions. The phase diagram of the alpha matter is obtained from simulations of this effective field theory on a lattice using local Monte-Carlo algorithms.

## Key claim

The paper obtains the equation of state and critical temperature of Bose-Einstein condensation for interacting alpha matter using an effective scalar field theory, but the results are limited to the regime near the critical temperature where only the zeroth-order Matsubara modes contribute, and the approach neglects the internal structure of alpha particles and the Mott effect at higher densities.

## Method

The authors start from a non-relativistic Hamiltonian of uniform alpha matter with attractive two-body and repulsive three-body contact interactions, then reformulate it as an O(2) symmetric scalar phi^6 field theory with negative quartic and positive sextic couplings. They restrict the Matsubara sums to the zeroth-order modes near the Bose-Einstein condensation temperature, obtaining an effective classical theory in three spatial dimensions. The phase diagram is obtained from Monte-Carlo simulations of this effective field theory on a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using a combination of heatbath and local Metropolis algorithms.

- Two-body scattering length from the Van der Spuy and Pienaar (VP) potential: a_sc = -7.33 fm, corresponding to coupling constant g_2 = -954.3 MeV fm^3
- Three-body coupling constant from the Kato potential: g_3 = 5944.8 MeV fm^3
- Core radius of the VP potential: a = 1.7 fm; well depth V_0 = 7.2 MeV; well width 4 fm
- Lattice spacing fixed at a_L = 1 fm; lattice sizes from 8^3 to 64^3 used, with results shown for 32^3
- Critical density for extinction of alpha particles: n_alpha* ~ 0.03 fm^{-3} for effective radius R_alpha ~ 2 fm

## Context

This work extends prior variational calculations of the ground state of uniform alpha matter by Clark and co-workers (Clark 1990s), specifically comparing to results from Johnson and Clark for the VP and Ali-Bodmer (AB) potentials. The approach is complementary to earlier studies of alpha matter ground state properties (Tamagaki, Brink, Turner, Aguilera, Thosaki) and to studies of Bose-Einstein condensation in alpha nuclei (Schuck, Suzuki). The paper is motivated by the scalar phi^4 field theory studies of critical temperature shifts in repulsive atomic Bose gases (Baym, 1990s). The results enable future study of the spectrum of bound states including the Efimov effect for three-body states, and provide a framework that could be applied to dilute Bose gases with long-range attractive and short-range repulsive interactions.
