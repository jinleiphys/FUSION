# Vector mesons at finite temperature and QCD sum rules

> Finite energy sum rules for vector and axial-vector currents are derived in a thermal medium to provide constraints for the spectral behavior of rho and a1 mesons at nonvanishing temperature and hence to study the tendency toward chiral symmetry restoration. The parity-mixing ansatz for the rho and a1 spectra, including finite widths, is investigated as a function of temperature. Characteristic differences between vector and axial-vector channels are discussed with regard to the implementation of the chiral-symmetry-breaking scale, 4pi times the pion decay constant, in the sum rule approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that finite energy sum rules (FESR) for vector and axial-vector currents at finite temperature can constrain the spectral behavior of rho and a1 mesons, but the applicability of this approach is limited to temperatures well below the "melting" temperature of the resonances because the broad a1 distribution prevents separation of scales at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors derive finite energy sum rules (FESR) for the lowest two moments of vector and axial-vector spectral functions at finite temperature, using the operator product expansion (OPE) with temperature-dependent condensates evaluated via soft-pion theorems and chiral perturbation theory. They apply a parity-mixing ansatz for the rho and a1 spectra, first with zero-width (large-Nc limit) spectral functions and then with realistic empirical spectral functions including finite widths, and compare results to a generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) effective field theory calculation.

## Key numbers

- The temperature at which parity mixing parameter epsilon = 0.5 (maximal mixing) is T_d = sqrt(3) * f_pi ≈ 151 MeV in the chiral limit (f_pi = 87 MeV), close to the chiral crossover temperature T_c = 170-190 MeV
- At T=0, the continuum threshold in the vector channel is sqrt(s_V) ≈ 1.1 GeV, close to the chiral scale 4*pi*f_pi ≈ 1 GeV
- At T=0, the continuum threshold in the axial-vector channel is sqrt(s_A) ≈ 1.54 GeV, with a1 mass m_A = 1.26 GeV
- The average vector mass at T=0 is 791 ± 2 MeV, rising to 807 ± 5 MeV at T=120 MeV when using realistic spectral functions
- The average vector mass at T=100 MeV including T^4 corrections is 798 ± 4 MeV
- The GHLS model gives average vector mass 787 ± 2 MeV at T=0, unchanged at T=80 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of FESR at finite baryon density (Kwon, 2008) to finite temperature, and builds on the parity-mixing framework introduced by Dey (1990) and the thermal condensate evaluation method of Hatsuda (1992). The results confirm the earlier finding by Eletsky (1994) that the vector meson pole position remains unchanged at order T^2, while providing a systematic sum-rule framework for studying the approach to chiral symmetry restoration. The analysis enables future studies of how the rho and a1 spectral functions evolve toward degeneracy as temperature increases toward the chiral transition.
