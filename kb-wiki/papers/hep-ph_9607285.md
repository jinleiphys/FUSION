# Suppression of J/psi and Psi' Production in High-Energy Pb on Pb Collisions

## Key claim
The anomalous J/psi suppression observed by NA50 in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV can be explained by a transition to a new phase of strong J/psi absorption, which occurs when the local energy density exceeds approximately 3.4 GeV/fm^3. The author argues that absorption by produced soft particles (comovers) alone cannot explain the Pb-Pb data, contrary to claims by Gavin and Vogt (1996), Capella et al. (1996), and Cassing and Ko (1996).

## Method
The author uses the microscopic absorption model (MAM), previously introduced in Wong (1996), which treats J/psi and psi' absorption via two components: a hard component from collisions with baryons at high relative energies, and a soft component from collisions with produced gluons or hadrons at low relative energies (about 200 MeV). The model is extended to include a new phase of strong absorption that activates when the number of baryon-baryon collisions at a given point reaches a threshold N_c = 4, corresponding to a local energy density threshold. The author fits the model to NA38 and NA50 data for p-A, O-Cu, O-U, S-U, and Pb-Pb collisions, varying the absorption cross sections and rate constants for soft particle absorption.

## Key numbers
- Threshold energy density for the new phase: epsilon_c ~ 3.4 GeV/fm^3, derived from N_c = 4 collisions, dn^{NN}/dy ~ 1.9, pion transverse mass m_t = 0.35 GeV, internucleon spacing d = 2.46 fm, and Lorentz contraction factor gamma = 9.2 at Pb-Pb collision energy.
- Absorption cross section for psi-N interactions: sigma_abs(psi N) = 6.94 mb in Model A (baryons only), and 6.36 mb in Models B and C (including soft particles).
- Rate constant for J/psi absorption by soft gluons: k_{psi g} = 0.0956 c/fm; for psi' absorption by soft gluons: k_{psi' g} = 3 c/fm.
- Rate constant for J/psi absorption by soft hadrons: k_{psi h} = 0.0493 c/fm; for psi' absorption by soft hadrons: k_{psi' h} = 3 c/fm.
- New phase rate constant: k_{psi x} = 1 c/fm; effective absorption cross section in the new phase: sigma_abs^x(psi N) = 14 mb.
- The ratio B sigma_{J/psi}^{pp} / sigma^{pp}(DY)_{2.9-4.5} for pp collisions was determined by NA51 to be 44 +/- 3.
- Lattice gauge result for comparison: epsilon_c/T_c^4 ~ 20 with T_c ~ 0.2 GeV gives quark-gluon plasma energy density ~ 4.2 GeV/fm^3.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic absorption model (MAM) of Wong (1996) to address the anomalous J/psi suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration (Gonin and Lourenco, 1996) in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV. The paper directly contradicts the comover absorption explanations of Gavin and Vogt (1996), Capella et al. (1996), and Cassing and Ko (1996), arguing that their models fail because they incorrectly relate the density of produced soft particles to transverse energy without accounting for the collision volume. The author suggests that the new phase of strong absorption may be the quark-gluon plasma, as the threshold energy density of 3.4 GeV/fm^3 is close to the lattice QCD estimate of 4.2 GeV/fm^3 from Blum et al. (1995). The work enables further study of the new phase through deformation effects in U-U collisions and S-Pb collisions at high transverse energy.
