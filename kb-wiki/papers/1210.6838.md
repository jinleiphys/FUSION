# Thermalization and Bose-Einstein Condensation in Overpopulated Glasma

> We report recent progress on understanding the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma during the early stage in a heavy ion collision. The initially high overpopulation in the far-from-equilibrium gluonic matter ("Glasma") is shown to play a crucial role. The strongly interacting nature (and thus fast evolution) naturally arises as an {\em emergent property} of this pre-equilibrium matter where the intrinsic coupling is weak but the highly occupied gluon states coherently amplify the scattering. A possible transient Bose-Einstein Condensate is argued to form dynamically on a rather general ground. We develop a kinetic approach for describing its evolution toward thermalization, and based on that we find approximate scaling solutions as well as numerically study the onset of condensation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the fast thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions can be explained as an emergent property of a weakly coupled but highly overpopulated gluonic system (the Glasma), where the high occupation number \(f \sim 1/\alpha_{\rm s}\) coherently amplifies scattering rates to order unity despite the small intrinsic coupling \(\alpha_{\rm s} \ll 1\). A transient Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC) is argued to form dynamically on general thermodynamic grounds, provided the system evolution is dominated by elastic processes over a certain time window.

## Method

The authors develop a kinetic approach assuming dominance of \(2 \leftrightarrow 2\) elastic scattering, deriving a transport equation in the small-angle approximation that retains the full nonlinearity of the bosonic \(f(1+f)\) terms. They identify approximate scaling solutions for the distribution function in both a static box and a boost-invariant expanding geometry, and numerically solve the transport equation to study the onset of condensation from overpopulated initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- The initial Glasma distribution has occupation \(f(p \leq Q_{\rm s}) = 1 / \alpha_{\rm s}\) and \(f(p > Q_{\rm s}) = 0\), with saturation scale \(Q_{\rm s}\).
- The overpopulation parameter for the Glasma is \((n\, \epsilon^{-3/4})_G \sim 1 / \alpha_{\rm s}^{1/4}\), compared to the thermal Bose gas value \((n\epsilon^{-3/4})_B \approx 0.28\).
- For the static box scaling solution, the soft scale evolves as \(\Lambda_{\rm s} \sim Q_{\rm s} (t_0/t)^{3/7}\) and the hard scale as \(\Lambda \sim Q_{\rm s} (t_0/t)^{-1/7}\).
- The parametric thermalization time in the static box is \(t_{\rm th} \sim (1/Q_{\rm s}) \, (1/\alpha_{\rm s})^{7/4}\).
- For the expanding case with fixed anisotropy \(\delta \equiv P_L/\epsilon\), the thermalization time is \(t_{\rm th} \sim (1/Q_{\rm s}) \, (1/\alpha_{\rm s})^{7/(3-\delta)}\).
- In the static box, the system reaches the onset of condensation for any initial overpopulation parameter \(n\epsilon^{-3/4} > 0.28\); in the expanding isotropic case, the critical value shifts to about \(n\epsilon^{-3/4} > 0.40\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier kinetic framework (Blaizot 2011, BLM) for understanding thermalization in the Glasma, and it contrasts with classical-statistical lattice simulations of scalar field theory (Epelbaum 2011, Gelis 2011, Berges 2012) that have reported evidence for Bose condensate formation. The paper enables further study of the role of inelastic processes and the extension to non-Abelian gauge theories, which remain under investigation (Berges 2011, Berges 2012, Kurkela 2012, Schlichting 2012).
