# Sum Rules in the CFL Phase of QCD at finite density

> We study the asymmetry between the vector current and axial-vector current correlators in the colour-flavour locking (CFL) phase of QCD at finite density. Using Weinberg's sum rules, we compute the decay constant $f_π$ of the Goldstone modes and find agreement with previous derivations. Using Das's sum rule, we also estimate the contribution of electromagnetic interactions to the mass of the charged modes. Finally, we comment on low temperature corrections to the effective field theory describing the Goldstone bosons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) in the CFL phase can be derived from Weinberg sum rules, but only when the leading non-vanishing contribution comes from a two-loop diagram (with a gluon exchange) rather than a one-loop diagram, because the naive one-loop contribution vanishes identically and the next-to-leading order is infrared-divergent, cancelling the \(g^2\) coupling to yield a \(g^0\) result.

## Method

The authors compute the gluon polarization tensor in the CFL phase using Nambu-Gorkov propagators in a colour-flavour basis, extracting the Debye and Meissner masses. They then evaluate the difference between vector and axial-vector current correlators at zero momentum, showing that the one-loop contribution vanishes and the leading non-zero contribution arises from a two-loop diagram where each quark loop involves off-diagonal (superconducting) Nambu-Gorkov propagators and the gluon propagator is regulated by the Debye or Meissner mass. The Weinberg sum rules are then used to extract \(f_\pi\) and \(v_\pi\). For the electromagnetic mass of charged Goldstone bosons, Das's sum rule is applied, and a rough estimate is given by assuming the integral is dominated by momenta below the gap \(\Delta\).

## Key numbers

* \(f_\pi^2 = \frac{21 - 8 \ln 2}{18} \frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2}\) (pion decay constant squared in the CFL phase, at leading order, with \(\mu\) the quark chemical potential).
* \(v_\pi = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\) (velocity of the Goldstone modes in the CFL phase).
* \(m_D^2 = g^2 \frac{21 - 8 \ln 2}{18} \frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2}\) (Debye mass squared for gluons in the CFL phase).
* \(m_M^2 = \frac{1}{3} m_D^2\) (Meissner mass squared for gluons in the CFL phase).
* \(M^2_{\pi^\pm} = M^2_{K^\pm} \sim \tilde{e}^2 \Delta^2\) (rough estimate of the electromagnetic contribution to the mass of charged Goldstone bosons, where \(\tilde{e}\) is the effective coupling and \(\Delta\) the gap).
* \(f_\pi(T) = f_\pi \left(1 - \frac{1}{8} \frac{T^2}{v_\pi^3 f_\pi^2}\right)\) (low-temperature correction to the pion decay constant, for \(T \ll f_\pi\)).
* \(v_\pi(T) = v_\pi\) (velocity of Goldstone modes unchanged at leading order in temperature).

## Context

This work extends the application of Weinberg sum rules, originally developed for QCD in vacuum (Weinberg 1967), to the colour-flavour locking (CFL) phase of QCD at finite density. It provides an alternative derivation of the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) and the velocity \(v_\pi\) previously computed by Son and Stephanov (2000) using matching of effective theory to microscopic calculations, and confirms their values \(a=b=1\) for the hidden gauge symmetry parameters. The paper also applies Das's sum rule (Das 1967) to estimate electromagnetic contributions to charged Goldstone boson masses in the CFL phase, correcting an earlier attempt by Hong (2000) that used a one-loop diagram. The results enable further studies of low-temperature corrections to the effective field theory and the behaviour of charged meson masses at finite density.
