# Analytic descriptions for transitional nuclei near the critical point

> Exact solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with a five-dimensional square well potential, in isolation or coupled to a fermion by the five-dimensional spin-orbit interaction, are considered as examples of a new class of dynamical symmetry or Bose-Fermi dynamical symmetry. The solutions provide baselines for experimental studies of even-even [E(5)] and odd-mass [E(5|4)] nuclei near the critical point of the spherical to deformed gamma-unstable phase transition.

## Key claim
The paper presents exact solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with a five-dimensional square well potential as examples of a new class of dynamical symmetry (E(5) for even-even nuclei) or Bose-Fermi dynamical symmetry (E(5|4) for odd-mass nuclei), but only when the operators are restricted to a bounded domain defined by β ≤ β_W, making the square well problem a dynamical symmetry only in a qualified sense compared to standard algebraic models.

## Method
The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian for a five-dimensional infinite square well potential V(β)=0 for β≤β_W and V(β)=∞ for β>β_W, separating radial and angular variables. The radial equation reduces to a Bessel equation with solutions f(β) ∝ β^{-3/2}J_ν(ε^{1/2}β), where the boundary condition at β_W yields discrete eigenvalues ε_{ξτ} = (x_{ν(τ),ξ}/β_W)^2. For the odd-mass extension, a fermion with j≤3/2 is coupled to the core via a five-dimensional spin-orbit interaction, leading to the E(5|4) Bose-Fermi dynamical symmetry based on the E(5)⊗U(4) algebraic structure. Electromagnetic transition strengths are computed by factoring radial and angular matrix elements, with the E2 operator expanded to include both first-order (α) and second-order ((α×α)^{(2)}) terms.

## Key numbers
- The separation constant Λ = τ(τ+3) for τ = 0,1,2,...
- Bessel function order ν(τ) = τ + 3/2
- Eigenvalues ε_{ξτ} = (x_{ν(τ),ξ}/β_W)^2, where x_{ν,ξ} is the ξth zero of J_ν
- For the leading-order E2 operator t_{21}α, transitions obey Δτ = ±1; strengths normalized to B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) should be multiplied by (0.07453) t₂₁² β_W² for absolute values
- For the second-order E2 operator t₂₂(α×α)^{(2)}, transitions obey Δτ = 0 or ±2; strengths normalized to B(E2;2⁺₂→0⁺₁) should be multiplied by (0.009754) t₂₂² β_W⁴ for absolute values
- Quadrupole moments from t₂₂(α×α)^{(2)} normalized to Q(2⁺₁) should be multiplied by (0.2124) t₂₂ β_W² for absolute values

## Context
This work extends the E(5) critical-point symmetry introduced by Iachello (2000) for even-even nuclei near the spherical-to-deformed γ-unstable phase transition, providing more complete calculations of electromagnetic matrix elements including higher-order terms. The E(5|4) extension for odd-mass nuclei builds on the Bose-Fermi dynamical symmetries previously formulated within the interacting boson fermion model (IBFM) by Iachello and collaborators, but now applicable to transitional nuclei rather than only structural limits. The results provide baselines for experimental studies of nuclei such as ¹³⁴Ba, ¹⁰⁴Ru, and ¹⁰²Pd (studied by Casten, Arias, Frank, and others) and enable further theoretical developments including the construction of Spin(5)⊃Spin(3) isoscalar factors needed for all problems involving Spin(5) coupling schemes.
