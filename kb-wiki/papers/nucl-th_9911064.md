# Prompt Contributions to the Dilepton Yield in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The mass spectrum is calculated for those dileptons which are produced in the early phase of a heavy ion collisions via the direct production NN -> (l^+)(l^-)X and via the Compton process GN -> (l^+)(l^-)X with prompt gluons radiated in preceding NN interactions. Both mechanisms produce a mass spectrum which decreases steeply with invariant mass of the l^+l^- pair and which is below the CERES data for Pb-Au collisions by about one order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The direct production of dileptons via NN → l⁺l⁻X and the Compton process GN → l⁺l⁻X from prompt gluons radiated in preceding NN interactions both produce mass spectra that decrease steeply with invariant mass and lie below the CERES Pb-Au data by about one order of magnitude, contributing less than 10% of the observed yield.

## Method

The authors calculated the dilepton mass spectrum from two early-stage partonic processes in heavy ion collisions. For direct NN → l⁺l⁻X production, they used a light-cone approach with a phenomenological dipole cross section (saturation model) and compared it to a perturbative QCD parton-model calculation of gluonic Compton scattering. For the prompt gluon mechanism, they computed the spectrum of gluons radiated in NN collisions using perturbative gluon radiation formulas, then calculated the subsequent GN → l⁺l⁻X Compton process, integrating over nuclear densities with Glauber geometry to obtain rates for p-Be, p-Au, and Pb-Au collisions at 158 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- The direct production cross section for Pb-Au collisions is below the CERES data by about one order of magnitude (factor ~10) for invariant masses M = 0.2–0.8 GeV.
- The constituent quark mass was varied between 150 MeV and 300 MeV, producing the upper and lower bounds of the shaded theoretical bands.
- The dipole cross section saturation model uses r₀(s) = 0.88 (s/s₀)⁻⁰·¹⁴ fm with s₀ = 1000 GeV², and σ₀(s) = σ_tot^{πp}(s) (1 + 3r₀²/(8⟨r²_ch⟩_π)) where σ_tot^{πp}(s) = 23.6 (s/s₀)⁰·⁰⁸ mb and ⟨r²_ch⟩_π = 0.44 fm².
- The inelastic NN cross section used is σ^{in}_{NN} ≈ 30 mb.
- The pQCD cutoff parameter Λ ≈ 250 MeV and the mean free path of a quark Δz ≈ 0.6 fm were used in the prompt gluon calculation.
- The gluon distribution function used is f_G(x) = C_G x⁻¹·¹ (1-x)⁵, normalized so gluons carry half the nucleon momentum.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of dilepton production in heavy ion collisions by focusing on the very early partonic phase, in contrast to most theories that locate the origin of observed dileptons in the hot hadronic phase (Brown, 1990s; Peters, 1990s). The prompt gluon mechanism was previously identified as important for charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions (Charm, 1998), which motivated this investigation. The results show that these early-stage partonic processes cannot explain the CERES and HELIOS data for Pb-Au collisions, leaving the observed enhancement (factor ~3 over hadronic decay calculations) to be explained by later-stage hadronic or medium effects.
