# Excitation energy dependence of symmetry energy of finite nuclei

> A finite range density and momentum dependent effective interaction is used to calculate the density and temperature dependence of the symmetry energy coefficient Csym(rho,T) of infinite nuclear matter. This symmetry energy is then used in the local density approximation to evaluate the excitation energy dependence of the symmetry energy coefficient of finite nuclei in a microcanonical formulation that accounts for thermal and expansion effects. The results are in good harmony with the recently reported experimental data from energetic nucleus-nucleus collisions.

# Excitation energy dependence of symmetry energy of finite nuclei

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient of finite nuclei decreases approximately linearly with excitation energy (C_sym(E*) ≈ C_sym(E*=0)(1 − αE*) with α ≈ 0.06), and this dependence arises primarily from the changing equilibrium density of the expanding hot nucleus rather than from any intrinsic temperature dependence of the symmetry energy at fixed density.

## Method

The authors use the modified Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) finite-range, momentum- and density-dependent effective interaction within a finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi formulation to calculate the symmetry energy coefficient of infinite nuclear matter as a function of density and temperature. They then apply the local density approximation to finite nuclei, using a microcanonical framework with self-similar scaling of the density profile to account for thermal expansion, and determine the equilibrium configuration by maximizing entropy at fixed excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy coefficient of infinite nuclear matter at saturation density is C_sym(ρ_0) = 34.0 MeV, with density dependence C_sym(ρ) ∝ (ρ/ρ_0)^γ where γ = 0.65 for the SBM interaction with n=1/6 (nuclear incompressibility K_∞ = 238 MeV)
- The exponent γ = 0.65 agrees closely with the experimentally extracted value γ ≈ 0.69 from Shetty et al.
- For finite nuclei, the excitation energy dependence is C_sym(E*) ≈ C_sym(E*=0)(1 − αE*) with α ≈ 0.06
- The maximum excitation energy a mononucleus can hold is approximately 5 MeV/A with free variation of surface diffuseness

## Context

This work provides the first microscopic calculation of the excitation energy dependence of the symmetry energy for finite nuclei, extending prior infinite-matter calculations. The results agree favorably with experimental data from Shetty et al. (2005) obtained from isoscaling analysis of multifragmentation data, and with density measurements from Natowitz et al. (2002) derived from caloric curve analysis. The calculation uses the SBM interaction, which reproduces ground-state bulk properties for A > 16 and yields an equation of state consistent with variational calculations by Friedman and Wirca (1981). The work enables improved interpretation of isoscaling and isotopic distribution data from nucleus-nucleus collisions, and opens the question of how free variation of surface diffuseness would affect the weakening of symmetry energy with excitation.
