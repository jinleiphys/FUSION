# Entanglement entropy and flow in two dimensional QCD: parton and string duality

> We discuss quantum entanglement between fast and slow degrees of freedom, in a two dimensional (2D) large $N_c$ gauge theory with Dirac quarks, quantized on the light front. Using the 't Hooft wave functions, we construct the reduced density matrix for an interval in the momentum fraction $x$-space, and calculate its von Neumann entropy in terms of structure functions, that are measured by DIS on mesons (hadrons in general). We found that the entropy is bounded by an area law with logarithmic divergences, proportional to the rapidity of the meson. The evolution of the entanglement entropy with rapidity, is fixed by the cumulative singlet PDF, and bounded from above by a Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy of 1. At low-$x$, the entanglement exhibits an asymptotic expansion, similar to the forward meson-meson scattering amplitude in the Regge limit. The evolution of the entanglement entropy in parton-$x$ per unit rapidity, measures the meson singlet PDF. The re-summed entanglement entropy along the single meson Regge trajectory, is string-like. We suggest that its extension to multi-meson states, models DIS scattering on a large 2D $^\prime$nucleus$^\prime$. The result, is a large rate of change of the entanglement entropy with rapidity, that matches the current Bekenstein-Bremermann bound for maximum quantum information flow. This mechanism may be at the origin of the large entropy deposition and rapid thermalization, reported in current heavy ion colliders, and may extend to future electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The entanglement entropy of a single meson in 2D QCD evolves with rapidity and is bounded from above by a Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy of 1, but when resummed over multi-meson states to model deep inelastic scattering on a 2D nucleus, the rate of change of entanglement entropy with rapidity matches the Bekenstein-Bremermann bound for maximum quantum information flow.

## Method

The authors quantize 2D large N_c QCD with Dirac quarks on the light front using discrete light-cone quantization, constructing the reduced density matrix for an interval in momentum fraction x-space from the 't Hooft wave functions. They calculate the von Neumann entropy for a single asymmetric cut in longitudinal momentum, analogous to a DIS experiment where a single parton-x is singled out. They then resum over the full meson Regge trajectory and extend to multi-meson states to model DIS scattering on a 2D nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The Kolmogorov-Sinai bound for the entanglement entropy of an n-meson is S_KS = 1 (sum of positive Lyapunov exponents)
- For the pseudo-Goldstone state, the change in rapidity of entanglement entropy is dS_pi(x_0)/dchi = 2x_0
- For the resummed string density matrix, the entanglement entropy is S(x_0) = 2x_0 ln Lambda^- - (bar x_0 - x_0) ln (bar x_0 - x_0)
- For the multi-meson state with general N_c, the entanglement entropy is S_E = ln(N_c + 1) x_0 Lambda^-
- The rate of change with rapidity of the string entanglement entropy for multi-meson states is dS_E(x_0)/dchi = ln(N_c + 1) x_0 Lambda^-

## Context

This work extends the analysis of entanglement entropy in hadronic processes discussed by Stoffers (2012), Shuryak (2017), and Liu (2018) to the exactly solvable setting of 2D QCD, where the 't Hooft equation (1974) provides the wave functions. The duality between low-x partons and string bits, following from the work of Karliner (1988) and Qian (2014), explains why the entanglement provides an efficient mechanism for scrambling information. The results suggest that the large entropy deposition and rapid thermalization observed at RHIC and LHC may originate from this mechanism, and the framework may extend to future electron-ion collider measurements.
