# $Λ(1405)$ and $\barΛ(1405)$ in $J/ψ$ four-body decays

> We study the structure of the baryon resonances $Λ(1405)$ and $\barΛ(1405)$ in $J/ψ$ four body decays $J/ψ\to Σ\bar Σππ$ in the framework of a coupled channel chiral unitary approach. With still sufficient freedom for model parameters, the $Λ(1405)$ and $\barΛ(1405)$ resonances are generated by simultaneously taking the meson baryon and meson anti-baryon final state interactions into account. The $πΣ$ ($π\barΣ$) invariant mass distributions peak around 1410 MeV, which favors the assertion that the $Λ(1405)$ ($\barΛ(1405)$) is a superposition of the two $Λ(1405)$ ($\barΛ(1405)$) states which dominantly couple to $\bar K N$ ($K\bar N$) and $πΣ$ ($π\barΣ$), respectively. We also calculate the amplitude for isospin I=1 which gives hints on a possible I=1 baryon resonance in the energy region of the $Λ(1405)$, which up to now has not been observed.

# Λ(1405) and \(\bar\Lambda\)(1405) in \(J/\psi\) four-body decays

## Key claim

The \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\bar\Lambda(1405)\) resonances are generated dynamically in \(J/\psi \to \Sigma\bar\Sigma\pi\pi\) decays via coupled-channel meson-baryon and meson-anti-baryon final-state interactions, and the \(\pi\Sigma\) (\(\pi\bar\Sigma\)) invariant mass distributions peak around 1410 MeV, consistent with the interpretation that the observed resonance is a superposition of two states, one coupling dominantly to \(\bar K N\) (\(K\bar N\)) and the other to \(\pi\Sigma\) (\(\pi\bar\Sigma\)), though the model parameters retain sufficient freedom that the precise shape depends on the choice of coupling constants.

## Method

The authors construct amplitudes for \(J/\psi \to B\bar B MM\) using a phenomenological SU(3)-invariant Lagrangian for the primary vertex, then incorporate final-state interactions via the chiral unitary coupled-channel approach of Oset and Ramos (1998). The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved algebraically with on-shell factorization, using a cutoff \(q_{\text{max}} = 630\) MeV for the meson-baryon loop integrals, and both meson-baryon and meson-anti-baryon rescattering are included simultaneously. The free parameters \(r_2 = x_2/x_3\) and \(r_4 = x_4/x_3\) are varied, with \(r_4 = 0.2\) fixed from fits to \(J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^+\pi^-\) data from Li and Oset (2004), and \(r_2 = 0.6\) chosen as a representative value.

## Key numbers

- The \(\pi\Sigma\) and \(\pi\bar\Sigma\) invariant mass distributions peak around **1410 MeV**, with a width of approximately **60 MeV**.
- The two poles of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) from earlier work (Jido et al., 2003) are at **(1390 − i60) MeV** and **(1426 − i16) MeV**; the present results are consistent with a superposition of these two states.
- The cutoff parameter \(q_{\text{max}} = 630\) MeV is used; varying it by ±10 MeV (to 620 or 640 MeV) changes cross sections by about **10%**.
- The coupling constant combination \(g_\alpha = (x_3 + 2x_4)g = 1.1 \times 10^{-6}~\text{MeV}^{-2}\) is taken from Li and Oset (2004) to reproduce the \(\pi\pi\) spectrum and width of \(J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^+\pi^-\).
- The parameter \(r_2\) is varied in the range **0.1 to 2.0**; the shape of distributions is qualitatively independent of the precise value.
- Including meson-meson rescattering adds about **30%** extra strength to the peak region, but does not change the qualitative conclusions.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oset and Ramos (1998) and Jido et al. (2003), which predicted two \(\Lambda(1405)\) poles, to the specific decay \(J/\psi \to \Sigma\bar\Sigma\pi\pi\), following the methodology of Li and Oset (2004) for meson-meson final-state interactions in \(J/\psi\) decays. It also connects to the photoproduction study of Nacher et al. (1999), where different \(\pi\Sigma\) charge-channel shapes were predicted and later confirmed experimentally. The results enable experimental extraction of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\bar\Lambda(1405)\) line shapes from \(J/\psi\) decays at BES or similar facilities, and provide a method to search for a predicted but unobserved \(I=1\) baryon resonance near the \(\bar K N\) threshold, originally discussed by Oller and Meissner (2001) and Jido et al. (2003).
