# Revisiting the lower bound on tidal deformability derived by AT 2017gfo

> We revisit the lower bound on binary tidal deformability tilde{Lambda} imposed by a luminous kilonova/macronova, AT 2017gfo, by numerical-relativity simulations of models that are consistent with gravitational waves from the binary neutron star merger GW170817. Contrary to the claim made in the literature, we find that binaries with tilde{Lambda}<~400 can explain the luminosity of AT 2017gfo, as long as moderate mass ejection from the remnant is assumed as had been done in previous work. The reason is that the maximum mass of a neutron star is not strongly correlated with the tidal deformability of neutron stars with a typical mass of ~1.4M_sun. If the maximum mass is so large that the binary does not collapse into a black hole immediately after merger, the mass of the ejecta can be sufficiently large irrespective of the binary tidal deformability. We present models of binary mergers with tilde{Lambda} down to 242 that satisfy the requirement on the mass of the ejecta from the luminosity of AT 2017gfo. We further find that the luminosity of AT 2017gfo could be explained by models that do not experience bounce after merger. We conclude that the luminosity of AT 2017gfo is not very useful for constraining the binary tidal deformability. Accurate estimation of the mass ratio will be necessary to establish a lower bound using electromagnetic counterparts in the future. We also caution that merger simulations that employ a limited class of tabulated equations of state could be severely biased due to the lack of generality.

# Revisiting the lower bound on tidal deformability derived by AT 2017gfo

## Key claim

Contrary to the claim in Radice et al. (2018) that binary tidal deformability must satisfy tilde{Lambda} greater than about 400 to explain the kilonova AT 2017gfo, binaries with tilde{Lambda} less than about 400 can explain the luminosity as long as the maximum neutron star mass is sufficiently large (at least about 2.1 solar masses) and/or the binary is asymmetric. The luminosity of AT 2017gfo is not very useful for constraining binary tidal deformability.

## Method

The authors performed full general-relativistic numerical simulations of binary neutron star mergers using the SACRA code, adopting piecewise polytropic equations of state with systematically varied maximum masses (2.00, 2.05, and 2.10 solar masses) and pressures at 10^14.7 g/cm^3. They simulated both equal-mass binaries (1.375-1.375 solar masses) and unequal-mass binaries (1.2-1.55 solar masses, mass ratio 0.774) consistent with GW170817, tracking dynamical ejecta mass and disk mass for each model up to 10-20 milliseconds after merger.

## Key numbers

* Binary tidal deformability tilde{Lambda} = 242 is the lowest value among models that can eject 0.05 solar masses and explain the luminosity of AT 2017gfo, achieved by an unequal-mass binary (q=0.774) with a long-lived remnant and maximum mass 2.10 solar masses.
* All asymmetric binaries (q=0.774) with maximum mass 2.10 solar masses can eject 0.05 solar masses, with tilde{Lambda} values down to 242.
* No equal-mass binary with tilde{Lambda} less than 377 can eject 0.05 solar masses; the lowest equal-mass model that succeeds has tilde{Lambda} = 508 with 77% ejection efficiency.
* For the model with tilde{Lambda} = 242, reducing the adiabatic index for thermal effects from 1.8 to 1.7 changes the remnant from long-lived to short-lived, reducing the disk mass from 0.26 solar masses to 0.045 solar masses.

## Context

This work directly challenges the lower bound of tilde{Lambda} greater than about 400 proposed by Radice et al. (2018) based on numerical simulations with mean-field equations of state, and loosens the later Bayesian limits of tilde{Lambda} greater than about 300 from Radice and Dai (2019) and tilde{Lambda} greater than about 279 from Coughlin et al. (2018). The authors build on the argument of Tews et al. (2018) that maximum mass is only weakly correlated with tidal deformability, and demonstrate that asymmetric binaries with moderate maximum masses can produce sufficient ejecta even at low tilde{Lambda}. The results imply that fitting formulas for disk mass versus tilde{Lambda} used in previous Bayesian analyses have severe systematic errors, and that accurate estimation of the mass ratio from gravitational waves is necessary to establish lower bounds using electromagnetic counterparts.
