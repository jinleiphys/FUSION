# Covariant Spectator Theory of np scattering: Deuteron Quadrupole Moment

> The deuteron quadrupole moment is calculated using two CST model wave functions obtained from the 2007 high precision fits to $np$ scattering data. Included in the calculation are a new class of isoscalar $np$ interaction currents automatically generated by the nuclear force model used in these fits. The prediction for model WJC-1, with larger relativistic P-state components, is 2.5% smaller that the experimental result, in common with the inability of models prior to 2014 to predict this important quantity. However, model WJC-2, with very small P-state components, gives agreement to better than 1%, similar to the results obtained recently from $χ$EFT predictions to order N$^3$LO.

## Key claim

The deuteron quadrupole moment is calculated using two covariant spectator theory (CST) wave functions from 2007 high-precision fits to np scattering data, with model WJC-2 (small P-state components) agreeing with experiment to better than 1%, while model WJC-1 (larger P-state components) under-predicts by 2.5%, similar to the failure of pre-2014 models.

## Method

The quadrupole moment was extracted by taking the Q²→0 limit of the difference of two matrix elements of the two-body current in the Breit frame, using covariant wave functions expanded in terms of S-state (u), D-state (w), and P-state (v_t, v_s) components. Eight types of correction terms were computed, including relativistic kinetic energy corrections, contributions from strong nucleon form factors, isoscalar interaction currents generated by momentum-dependent vertex functions, and interference terms between S/D and P-state wave functions. The calculation used the unique determination of interaction currents from current conservation principles developed in prior work (Gross 2014).

## Key numbers

- Model WJC-1 predicts a quadrupole moment 2.5% below the experimental value (δQ_pred = -2.5%)
- Model WJC-2 predicts a quadrupole moment 0.8% below the experimental value (δQ_pred = -0.8%)
- The experimental deuteron quadrupole moment is Q_exp = 0.286 (units of e/m_d²)
- The nonrelativistic contribution Q^Δ_NR is -0.011 for WJC-1 and -0.018 for WJC-2 (normalized by Q_exp)
- The relativistic correction Q_Rc is +0.010 for both models
- The interference term Q_int contributes -0.014 for WJC-1 and +0.002 for WJC-2
- Model WJC-1 has 27 parameters with χ²/datum ≈ 1.06; model WJC-2 has 15 parameters with χ²/datum ≈ 1.12
- The normalization correction N_CST is +0.052 for WJC-1 and +0.023 for WJC-2

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator theory (CST) framework developed by Gross (1969, 1972, 1982) and the high-precision NN scattering fits of Gross and Stadler (2008). It addresses the long-standing "unresolved problem" identified by Machleidt (2000) where calculations of the deuteron quadrupole moment consistently under-predicted the experimental value by several percent, with prior results ranging from -9.0% (Gross 1991) to -2.1% (CD Bonn with MEC estimate). The WJC-2 result (0.8% error) is comparable to recent χEFT predictions at N³LO by the ODU-Pisa group (Piarulli 2012), which gave errors of -0.3% (cutoff 500 MeV) and -1.4% (cutoff 600 MeV). The calculation enables future work on deuteron form factors (planned as the fourth paper in this series) and suggests that the quadrupole moment could potentially be used as a fitting constraint in future NN scattering models.
