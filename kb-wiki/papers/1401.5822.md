# Symmetry energy, neutron skin, and neutron star radius from chiral effective field theory interactions

> We discuss neutron matter calculations based on chiral effective field theory interactions and their predictions for the symmetry energy, the neutron skin of 208 Pb, and for the radius of neutron stars.

# Symmetry energy, neutron skin, and neutron star radius from chiral effective field theory interactions

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions, when combined with renormalization-group evolution and uncertainty estimates from three-nucleon force couplings, provide tight, parameter-free predictions for the symmetry energy, neutron skin of 208Pb, and neutron star radii, but the results are dominated by uncertainties in three-nucleon forces, and the neutron matter calculations are only directly constrained up to about saturation density (0.16 fm⁻³), requiring polytropic extensions for higher densities.

## Method
The authors performed neutron matter calculations using chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions up to N³LO, with and without renormalization-group evolution of the NN potential to a low-momentum scale of 2.0 fm⁻¹. They extracted the energy per particle and pressure of neutron matter, then used an empirical Skyrme-type parametrization to interpolate between neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, from which they derived the symmetry energy and its density derivative. For neutron star applications, they extended the equation of state beyond 1.1 times saturation density using general piecewise polytropic extensions and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations, retaining only those equations of state that remain causal and support a neutron star of a given maximum mass.

## Key numbers
- Neutron matter energy per particle calculated up to saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy S_v = 29.7 – 33.2 MeV
- Density derivative of symmetry energy L = 32.5 – 57.0 MeV
- Neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb predicted to be 0.17 ± 0.03 fm
- Including doubly-magic nuclei constraints gives neutron skins of ²⁰⁸Pb = 0.182 ± 0.010 fm and ⁴⁸Ca = 0.173 ± 0.005 fm
- Proton fraction in beta equilibrium at saturation density: 4% – 5.3%
- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star, predicted radius range R = 9.7 – 13.9 km
- Central density of a 1.4 (1.97) M⊙ neutron star does not exceed 4.4 (7.6) n₀
- Saturation properties: E/A = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 236 MeV

## Context
This work extends chiral EFT neutron matter calculations (Hebeler et al. 2013) to predictions for the symmetry energy, neutron skin, and neutron star radii, providing tighter constraints than previous empirical extractions. The results agree well with constraints from energy-density functionals for nuclear masses, the ²⁰⁸Pb dipole polarizability (Tamii et al. 2011), isobaric analog state analyses (Danielewicz et al. 2013), and variational calculations (Akmal et al. 1998). The neutron matter constraints have been used to explore gravitational wave signals in neutron-star mergers (Bauswein et al. 2014) and enable future tests with rare isotope beam facilities and improved three-nucleon force treatments.
