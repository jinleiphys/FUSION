# Widths of $\bar K$-nuclear deeply bound states in a dynamical model

> The relativistic mean field (RMF) model is applied to a system of nucleons and a $\bar K$ meson, interacting via scalar and vector boson fields. The model incorporates the standard RMF phenomenology for bound nucleons and, for the $\bar K$ meson, it relates to low-energy ${\bar K}N$ and $K^-$ atom phenomenology. Deeply bound $\bar K$ nuclear states are generated dynamically across the periodic table and are exhibited for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O over a wide range of binding energies. Substantial polarization of the core nucleus is found for these light nuclei. Absorption modes are also included dynamically, considering explicitly the reduced phase space for $\bar K$ absorption from deeply bound states. The behavior of the calculated width as function of the $\bar K$ binding energy is studied in order to explore limits on the possible existence of narrow $\bar K$ nuclear states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that deeply bound $\bar K$ nuclear states in light nuclei (¹²C, ¹⁶O) with binding energies in the range 100–200 MeV will have widths of approximately 40 ± 5 MeV, which is considerably larger than what simple phase-space suppression alone would suggest, due to dynamical nuclear polarization effects that compress the nuclear density.

## Method

The authors apply a relativistic mean field (RMF) model to a system of nucleons and a single $\bar K$ meson, interacting via scalar ($\sigma$) and vector ($\omega$) boson fields treated in the mean-field approximation. The coupled equations for nucleons and meson fields are solved self-consistently, allowing the $\bar K$ to polarize the nuclear core and vice versa. Absorption widths are included via a $t\rho$ optical-model approach, with the imaginary potential constrained by $K^-$ atom data and modified by phase-space suppression factors for the two dominant decay channels: $\bar K N \rightarrow \pi \Sigma$ (80%) and $\bar K NN \rightarrow Y N$ (20%). The coupling constants $g_{\sigma K}$ and $g_{\omega K}$ are scanned over to explore a wide range of binding energies.

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, calculated widths in the binding energy range $B_K \sim 100 - 200$ MeV assume values $40 \pm 5$ MeV.
- The phase-space suppression factor $f$ drops to a residual value of $f=0.02$ when both $f_1$ and $f_2$ vanish.
- Replacing $\rho$ by $\rho^2$ for the density dependence of non-pionic decay modes is estimated to increase widths by 10–15 MeV.
- Switching on the $\pi \Lambda$ decay mode would increase the width estimate by 5–10 MeV in the range $B_K \sim 100 - 180$ MeV.
- The effective $\hbar \omega$ spacing between neighboring $\bar K$ states in $^{16}$O is of order 100 MeV for the deepest spectrum, decreasing to 80 MeV and 70 MeV as the $1s$ binding is made lower.
- A lower limit of $\Gamma_K \sim 35 - 40$ MeV is placed on $\bar K$ states in $^{16}$O bound in the range $B_K \sim 100 - 200$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the RMF approach previously applied to $\bar N$ states in nuclei by BMS02 and MSB04, and contrasts with the shallow chirally-motivated $\bar K$-nucleus potentials of BGN00 and CFG01, which cannot yield binding energies greater than about 50 MeV. The results provide guidance for interpreting experimental searches for $\bar K$ nuclear bound states, particularly the KEK-PS E471 and BNL-AGS E930 experiments, and rule out the interpretation by Kishimoto et al. (2003) of a peak at $B_K \sim 90$ MeV in $^{16}$O as a $1p$ state, since the dynamical calculation gives much larger level spacings. The approach is applicable across the periodic table and can be extended to multi-$\bar K$ configurations and the $\bar K$ condensation limit.
