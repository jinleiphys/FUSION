# Nuclear modification of charged hadron production at LHC

> We analyze the recent results for suppressed production of charged hadrons for Pb+Pb collisions at the center of mass energy of 2.76 TeV/nucleon-pair. We closely follow the treatment used recently by us where partons lose energy due to radiation of gluons following multiple scatterings while traversing the quark gluon plasma, before fragmenting into hadrons at the center of mass energy of 200 GeV/nucleon-pair. We obtain an empirical value for the momentum transport coefficient $(\widehat{q})$ and provide predictions for azimuthal anisotropy of hadron momenta for non-central collisions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the complete coherence regime of parton energy loss (where energy loss per unit length is proportional to path length) provides a good description of charged hadron suppression data for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV for transverse momenta above 10 GeV/c, but this description is based on tuning free parameters (k, α, κ) separately for each centrality and each energy-loss regime.

## Method
The authors use next-to-leading order perturbative QCD (NLO pQCD) to generate parton distributions, then model parton energy loss via gluon bremsstrahlung with multiple scatterings in the quark-gluon plasma, using the Wang-Huang-Sarcevic model for multiple scattering. They classify energy loss into three regimes (Bethe-Heitler, LPM, and complete coherence) based on the formation time of radiated gluons relative to the mean free path and path length, and tune the energy-loss-per-collision parameters (k, α, κ) to reproduce the CMS measurements of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadrons at four centrality classes.

## Key numbers
- The momentum transport coefficient q̂ varies from 0.63 GeV²/fm for 0–5% centrality to 0.91 GeV²/fm for 30–50% centrality in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.
- The width of the transverse momentum distribution ⟨p_Tw²⟩ = q̂ ⟨L⟩ is 3.36 GeV² for 0–5% centrality and 2.70 GeV² for 30–50% centrality.
- The magnitude of energy loss per unit length (dE/dx) for a given average path length increases by a factor of 2–3 when going from RHIC (200 GeV) to LHC (2.76 TeV) energies.
- The Bethe-Heitler regime describes suppression only over a small p_T window of 5–8 GeV/c; the LPM regime works for p_T ∼ 6–15 GeV/c; the complete coherence regime works for 10 GeV/c < p_T < 100 GeV/c.
- The q̂ values at LHC are about 2.5 times higher than those obtained for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV using a similar analysis.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous jet-quenching analysis at RHIC energies (200 GeV) to LHC energies (2.76 TeV), following the same formalism used in Srivastava and collaborators (2010). The results confirm the prediction by Baier et al. that total parton energy loss ΔE is proportional to L², now validated at 2.76 TeV. The analysis enables predictions for azimuthal anisotropy (v₂) at high p_T for non-central collisions, though the authors note their results overestimate ALICE data by factors of 1.5–2, likely due to assumptions of uniform nuclear density and a static medium.
