# Beta - and gamma-decay transition rates in the two-group shell model

> In the framework of the two-group configuration model we obtain formulas for the reduced transition rates for beta- and gamma-transitions in even-even, odd-odd, even-odd, and odd-even nuclei. We explored dependencies of the transition rates on the occupancies of the involved subshells, as well as on the spin values of the initial and final states. The obtained formulas are useful for the qualitative spectroscopic analysis of experimental data, particulary in the regions of magicity, including the regions of the "remote" nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper derives closed-form algebraic expressions for beta- and gamma-decay reduced transition rates within the two-group shell model, but these formulas are explicitly limited to cases where the seniorities of the involved nuclear states are not more than two, and they are intended for qualitative spectroscopic analysis rather than precise numerical predictions.

## Method
The author uses the formalism of fractional parentage decompositions in the jj-coupling scheme and neutron-proton representation to calculate reduced matrix elements for single-particle transition operators between two-group configurations. Algebraic expressions for the fractional parentage coefficients are inserted for seniorities s=0, 1, and 2, yielding explicit formulas for B(λ) in terms of subshell occupancies n₁ and n₂, angular momenta, and single-particle transition rates. The treatment covers both beta-decay (where the two orbitals are of different nucleon types) and gamma-decay (where both orbitals refer to the same nucleon type but transitions occur between different groups).

## Key numbers
- For the case n₁ even, n₂ even, with J₁=J₂=s₁=s₂=Iᵢ=0 and J'₁=j₁, J'₂=j₂, s'₁=s'₂=1, I_f=λ: B(λ; 0→λ) = [n₁(2j₂+1-n₂)/(2j₂+1)] B_sp(λ; j₁→j₂)
- For the case n₁ even, n₂ odd, with J₁=s₁=0, J₂=j₂, s₂=1, Iᵢ=j₂, and J'₁=j₁, s'₁=1, J'₂=s'₂=0, I_f=j₁: B(λ; j₂→j₁) = [n₁(n₂+1)/(2j₂+1)²] B_sp(λ; j₁→j₂)
- For the case n₁ odd, n₂ even, with J₁=j₁, s₁=1, J₂=s₂=0, Iᵢ=j₁, and J'₁=s'₁=0, J'₂=j₂, s'₂=1, I_f=j₂: B(λ; j₁→j₂) = [(2j₁+2-n₁)(2j₂+1-n₂)]/[(2j₁+1)(2j₂+1)] B_sp(λ; j₁→j₂)
- For the case n₁ odd, n₂ odd, with J₁=j₁, s₁=1, J₂=j₂, s₂=1, Iᵢ=λ, and J'₁=J'₂=s'₁=s'₂=I_f=0: B(λ; λ→0) = [1/(2λ+1)] [(n₂+1)(2j₁+2-n₁)/(2j₂+1)] B_sp(λ; j₁→j₂)
- For gamma-decay between states with seniority 1 on both groups, the gyromagnetic ratio is: g_I = (g₁+g₂)/2 + [(g₁-g₂)/2]·[j₁(j₁+1)-j₂(j₂+1)]/[I(I+1)]
- The radial matrix element for oscillator potential: ⟨n' ℓ+1|r|nℓ⟩ = (ħ/mω)^(1/2) [δ_{n',n}√(n+ℓ+3/2) - δ_{n',n-1}√n], with ħ/mω ≈ 1.01 A^(1/3) fm² for ħω = 41/A^(1/3) MeV

## Context
This work extends the author's previous calculations in the representation of total isospin (Isakov 2009) by performing the analysis in the neutron-proton representation, which is particularly important for Fermi transitions where the operator is a rotation in isospin space. The formulas build on the fractional parentage coefficient formalism of Talmi and the two-group configuration algebra developed by Neudat, Nemetz, and Hong-zhou. The results enable qualitative spectroscopic analysis of experimental data without complicated numerical calculations, especially for nuclei near magic numbers and in regions of "remote" nuclei.
