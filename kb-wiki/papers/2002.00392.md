# Charm-hadron production in $pp$ and AA collisions

> Recent measurements of various charm-hadron ratios in $pp$, $p$-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC have posed challenges to the theoretical understanding of heavy-quark hadronization. The $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio in $pp$ and $p$-Pb collisions shows larger values than those found in $e^+e^-$ and $ep$ collisions and predicted by Monte-Carlo event generators based on string fragmentation, at both low and intermediate transverse momenta ($p_T$). In AA collisions, the $D_s/D$ ratio is significantly enhanced over its values in $pp$, while the $Λ_c/D^0$ data indicates a further enhancement at intermediate $p_T$. Here, we report on our recent developments for a comprehensive description of the charm hadrochemistry and transport in $pp$ and $AA$ collisions. For $pp$ collisions we find that the discrepancy between the $Λ_c/D^0$ data and model predictions is much reduced by using a statistical hadronization model augmented by a large set of "missing" states in the charm-baryon spectrum, contributing to the $Λ_c$ via decay feeddown. For $AA$ collisions, we develop a 4-momentum conserving resonance recombination model for charm-baryon formation implemented via event-by-event simulations that account for space-momentum correlations (SMCs) in transported charm- and thermal light-quark distributions. The SMCs, together with the augmented charm-baryon states, are found to play an important role in describing the baryon-to-meson enhancement at intermediate momenta. We emphasize the importance of satisfying the correct (relative) chemical equilibrium limit when computing the charm hadrochemistry and its momentum dependence with coalescence models.

## Key claim

The large $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio observed in $pp$ collisions, which exceeds predictions from string-fragmentation Monte-Carlo generators and $e^+e^-$ measurements, can be largely explained by augmenting the statistical hadronization model with a set of "missing" charm-baryon states predicted by relativistic quark models but not yet listed by the Particle Data Group.

## Method

For $pp$ collisions, the authors used a statistical hadronization model (SHM) at a hadronization temperature of 160-170 MeV, augmented by charm-baryon states from the relativistic quark model (RQM) that decay into the ground-state $\Lambda_c$ via feeddown. For $AA$ collisions, they developed a 3-body resonance recombination model (RRM) implemented via event-by-event simulations that incorporate space-momentum correlations (SMCs) between charm quarks from Langevin diffusion and thermal light quarks from the same QGP background medium, ensuring exact charm-number conservation and correct chemical equilibrium limits.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio in $pp$ collisions: SHM with PDG states predicts $\sim 0.22$, while the augmented RQM scenario yields $\sim 0.57$ at $T_H=170$ MeV and $\sim 0.44$ at $T_H=160$ MeV
- Total charm cross section fitted for $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV: $d\sigma^{c\bar{c}}/dy = 0.855$ mb for PDG scenario and $1.0$ mb for RQM scenario
- Low-$p_T$ $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ ratio in $AA$ collisions: $\sim 0.5$, essentially the same as the $pp$ value
- $D_s^+/D^0$ ratio in $AA$ collisions: enhanced relative to $pp$ due to strangeness-equilibrated environment with fugacity $\gamma_s \simeq 1$

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously applied to light- and strange-hadron production by Andronic (2017) and the standard SHM prediction for $\Lambda_c/D^0$ by Andronic (2007), which substantially underestimated the ALICE measurements at $\sqrt{s}=5$ and 7 TeV $pp$ collisions. The augmented charm-baryon spectrum is motivated by relativistic quark model predictions from Ebert (2011) and lattice QCD. The resonance recombination model with space-momentum correlations resolves a long-standing problem of coalescence model fragility for $v_2$ predictions identified by Ravagli (2008), and enables controlled predictions for charm-hadron ratios, $R_{\rm AA}$, and $v_2$ in heavy-ion collisions that can be tested against future LHC and RHIC data.
