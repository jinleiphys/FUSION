# Nuclear medium effects on the $\bar K^*$ meson

> The $\bar K^*$ meson in dense matter is analyzed by means of a unitary approach in coupled channels based on the local hidden gauge formalism. The $\bar K^*$ self-energy and the corresponding $\bar K^*$ spectral function in the nuclear medium are obtained. We observe that the $\bar K^*$ develops a width in matter up to five times bigger than in free space. We also estimate the transparency ratio of the $γA \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ reaction. This ratio is an excellent tool to detect experimentally modifications of the $\bar K^*$ meson in dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\bar K^*$ meson develops a width in nuclear matter up to five times larger than its free-space width, but this result is obtained from a self-consistent coupled-channel calculation that incorporates both the $\bar K^* \to \bar K \pi$ decay in the medium and the $\bar K^* N$ interaction, with the caveat that the $\bar K^* N$ amplitudes mediated by intermediate $\bar K N$ or $\pi Y$ states are not unitarized.

## Method

The $\bar K^* N$ interaction was derived from t-channel vector-meson exchange within the local hidden gauge formalism, and the scattering amplitude was obtained by solving the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for the $S=-1$ sector with isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$. The $\bar K^*$ self-energy in nuclear matter was computed from two sources: the $\bar K^* \to \bar K \pi$ decay in the medium (including pion and antikaon self-energies and vertex corrections) and the $\bar K^* N$ interaction integrated over the Fermi sea of nucleons, with the latter determined self-consistently. The transparency ratio for the $\gamma A \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ reaction was then estimated from the in-medium $\bar K^*$ width.

## Key numbers

- Free $\bar K^*$ width from $\bar K^* \to \bar K \pi$ decay: 42 MeV (calculated), compared to the experimental value of $50.8 \pm 0.9$ MeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density ($\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$), the $\bar K^*$ width from $\bar K \pi$ decay alone (including vertex corrections) is 105 MeV at the $\bar K^*$ mass, about twice the vacuum width.
- The fully self-consistent $\bar K^*$ width at $\rho_0$ is 260 MeV, about five times the free width.
- Transparency ratios were calculated for photon lab energies of 4.3 GeV and 6 GeV (corresponding to center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s}=3$ GeV and 3.5 GeV, respectively) for various nuclei, normalized to $^{12}$C.

## Context

This work extends the study of vector mesons in nuclear matter, previously focused on the $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\phi$ mesons (Rapp, Hayano, Mosel; NA60, Wood; Nanova), to the strange vector meson $\bar K^*$, which had not been discussed in the literature. The theoretical framework builds on the hidden-gauge formalism for vector meson-baryon interactions (Oset 2009) and the self-consistent treatment of meson self-energies in matter (Ramos 1999, Tolos 2006). The predicted strong in-medium broadening of the $\bar K^*$ can be tested experimentally via the transparency ratio in photoproduction reactions, providing a tool to detect modifications of the $\bar K^*$ in dense matter.
