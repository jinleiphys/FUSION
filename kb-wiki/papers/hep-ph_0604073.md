# The sum rules for the spin dependent structure functions $g_1$ in the isovector reaction

> In the isovector reaction, the sum rule for the spin dependent function $g_1$ which is related to the cross section of the photoproduction is derived. In the small $Q^2$ region, the sum rule is dominated by the low energy contribution and it tightly connects the resonance, the elastic, and the non-resonant contributions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a sum rule for the spin-dependent structure function \(g_1\) in the isovector reaction that relates it to photoproduction cross sections, but the sum rule may be divergent depending on the high-energy behavior; the author provides a regularization procedure via analytical continuation from the nonforward direction to give the sum rule physical meaning even in the divergent case.

## Method

The author starts from the null-plane current commutation relation to obtain a \(Q^2\)-independent sum rule for the antisymmetric combination of \(g_1^{[ab]}\). By setting \(Q_0^2 = 0\) and using the relation between the structure function and the photoproduction cross section difference \(\Delta\sigma^{ab}(\nu)\), the sum rule is transformed into an experimentally testable form involving the Born term, resonance contributions, and non-resonant contributions. The regularization of the potentially divergent sum rule is performed by analytical continuation from the nonforward direction, subtracting singular pieces as the momentum transfer \(|t|\) goes to zero.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff energy \(E_c = 2\) (GeV\(^2\)) is chosen such that the high-energy contribution \(K(E_c, Q^2)\) is expected to be small and almost negligible.
- The sign change in the difference between non-resonant contributions occurs near \(Q^2 \sim 0.15\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), where the Born term variation becomes rapid while resonance contributions change slowly.
- The Born term \(B(Q^2)\) is expressed in terms of the anomalous magnetic moments \(\mu_p - \mu_n\) and the Sachs form factors \(G_E^+(Q^2)\) and \(G_M^+(Q^2)\), which are differences between proton and neutron form factors.

## Context

This work extends the null-plane sum rule formalism of Dicus, Jackiw, and Teplitz (1971) and the author's own previous work (Koretune, 2005) on the current anti-commutation relation sum rule, which included a singlet quantity, to the current commutation relation case which yields a nonsinglet sum rule. The sum rule tightly connects resonance, elastic, and non-resonant contributions in the low \(Q^2\) region, motivated by experimental interest in polarized structure functions at low \(Q^2\) (Burkert, 2005). The regularization method follows the analytical continuation approach from nonforward directions (analy, year not specified), and the numerical estimates use the Galster parameterization (Galster, 1971) for the neutron electric form factor and resonance parameters from Simula (year not specified).
