# Jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ in lattice QCD

> We present the first calculation of the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ in quenched and (2+1)-flavor QCD on a 4-D Euclidean lattice. The light-like propagation of an energetic parton is factorized from the mean square gain in momentum transverse to the direction of propagation, which is expressed in terms of the thermal field-strength field-strength correlator. The leading-twist term in its operator product expansion is calculated on the lattice. Continuum extrapolated quenched results, and full QCD estimates based on un-renormalized lattice data, over multiple lattice sizes, are compared with (non) perturbative calculations and phenomenological extractions of $\hat{q}$. The lattice data for $\hat{q}$ show a temperature dependence similar to the entropy density. Within uncertainties, these are consistent with phenomenological extractions, contrary to calculations using perturbation theory.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first 4-D Euclidean lattice QCD calculation of the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$, but the full QCD result is only an estimate due to lack of rigorous control of renormalization factors and mixing with unknown quark operators on the lattice. The quenched results are continuum-extrapolated, while the (2+1)-flavor QCD results are based on un-renormalized lattice data.

## Method
The authors factorize the light-like propagation of an energetic parton from the mean square transverse momentum gain, expressing $\hat{q}$ in terms of a thermal field-strength field-strength correlator. They compute the leading-twist term in the operator product expansion of this correlator on the lattice, using pure SU(3) gauge ensembles and (2+1)-flavor QCD ensembles with the HISQ action and tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action. The calculation is performed on multiple lattice sizes with aspect ratio $n_s/n_\tau = 4$ for $n_\tau = 4, 6, 8$, and the quenched results are continuum extrapolated.

## Key numbers
- Quenched results are continuum extrapolated using pure SU(3) gauge ensembles at temperatures ranging from 135 MeV (for $n_\tau=8$ at $\beta_0=5.70$) to 1146 MeV (for $n_\tau=4$ at $\beta_0=6.60$)
- (2+1)-flavor QCD ensembles use light sea quark masses $m_l = m_s/20$ at temperatures from 147 MeV (for $n_\tau=6$ at $\beta_0=6.0$) to 843 MeV (for $n_\tau=8$ at $\beta_0=8.2$)
- The critical temperature for pure SU(3) is set to $T_c \approx 265$ MeV, with the lattice parameter $\Lambda_L = 5.5$ MeV
- Perturbative radiative corrections to $\hat{q}$ are estimated at $\delta \hat{q} \sim 0.5 \hat{q}$, implying an approximate 50% excess that would bring phenomenological extractions down to about 66% of their current values

## Context
This work extends the framework first proposed by Majumder (2012) for computing $\hat{q}$ from first principles within lattice gauge theory. The lattice results show a temperature dependence similar to the entropy density and are consistent with phenomenological extractions from the JET and JETSCAPE collaborations, contrary to perturbative calculations. The calculation enables direct comparison between first-principles lattice QCD and phenomenological extractions of $\hat{q}$ from heavy-ion collision data, and future work will need to include multiple scattering and emission processes as well as renormalization of quark operators.
