# Strange Goings on in Quark Matter

> We review recent work on how the superfluid state of three flavor quark matter is affected by non-zero quark masses and chemical potentials.

# Strange Goings on in Quark Matter

## Key claim

The superfluid color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of three-flavor quark matter is destabilized by non-zero quark masses, specifically the strange quark mass, leading to kaon condensation as a mechanism to reduce strangeness content; this occurs when the effective chemical potential from the strange quark mass exceeds the kaon mass, but the analysis is limited to the regime where perturbation theory is reliable and the effective chiral theory is valid.

## Method

The author uses the effective chiral theory of the CFL phase (CFLχTh) combined with high-density effective theory (HDET) to compute the masses of Goldstone bosons and the vacuum energy shift due to non-zero quark masses. Matching between the effective chiral theory and perturbative QCD at large baryon density determines the pion decay constant and the coefficients of mass-dependent terms in the chiral Lagrangian. The vacuum energy is then minimized to find the ground state, allowing the identification of the onset of kaon condensation and the properties of the condensed phase.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant in the CFL phase is given by \( f_\pi^2 = \frac{21-8\log(2)}{18} \left(\frac{p_F^2}{2\pi^2}\right) \), where \( p_F \) is the Fermi momentum.
- The coefficients for the mass terms in the chiral Lagrangian are \( A_1 = -A_2 = \frac{3\Delta^2}{4\pi^2} \) and \( A_3 = 0 \), where \( \Delta \) is the superconducting gap.
- The critical strange quark mass for kaon condensation, to leading order in the coupling \( g \), is \( \left. m_s \right|_{crit} = 3.03 \cdot m_d^{1/3} \Delta^{2/3} \).
- The hypercharge density in the kaon condensed phase is \( n_Y = f_\pi^2 \mu_{eff} \left( 1 - \frac{m_K^4}{\mu_{eff}^4} \right) \), with \( \mu_{eff} = m_s^2/(2p_F) \), and satisfies \( n_Y < \Delta p_F^2/(2\pi) \) within the range of validity of the effective theory.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the CFL phase originally proposed by Alford (1999) and Rapp (1998) by incorporating the effects of non-zero quark masses using the effective chiral theory developed by Casalbuoni (1999). The results confirm and agree with the earlier matching calculation by Son and Stephanov (1999) for the coefficients \( A_{1,2,3} \). The kaon condensation mechanism is compared to the earlier proposal by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) for kaon condensation in hadronic matter, and it provides a path from ideal CFL matter (with equal numbers of up, down, and strange quarks) to matter with reduced strangeness content relevant for compact star interiors.
