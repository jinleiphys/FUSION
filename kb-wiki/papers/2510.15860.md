# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of Light Nuclei with Fully Propagated Theoretical Uncertainties

> We report on the first quantum Monte Carlo calculations of helium isotopes with fully propagated theoretical uncertainties from the interaction to the many-body observables. To achieve this, we build emulators for solutions to the Faddeev equations for the binding energy and Gamow-Teller matrix element of $^3\text{H}$, as well as for auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of the $^4\text{He}$ charge radius, employing local two- and three-body interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. We use these emulators to determine the posterior distributions for all low-energy couplings that appear in the interaction up to this order using Bayesian inference while accounting for theoretical uncertainties. We then build emulators for auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo for helium isotopes and propagate the full posterior distributions to these systems. Our approach serves as a framework for $\textit{ab initio}$ studies of atomic nuclei with consistently treated and correlated theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first quantum Monte Carlo calculations of helium isotopes where theoretical uncertainties from the chiral effective field theory interaction are fully propagated to the many-body observables, but this is achieved only at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) and does not include uncertainties on the pion-nucleon couplings that enter the three-nucleon two-pion-exchange term.

## Method

The authors built emulators using eigenvector continuation and the parametric matrix model for solutions to the Faddeev equations for the $^3$H ground-state energy and Gamow-Teller matrix element, and for auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of the $^4$He charge radius. These emulators were used in a Bayesian inference framework to determine posterior distributions for all 11 low-energy couplings (9 nucleon-nucleon and 2 three-nucleon) of a local chiral EFT interaction at N$^2$LO with cutoff $R_0 = 0.6$ fm. The posterior distributions were then propagated through PMM emulators for AFDMC calculations of $^3$He, $^4$He, and $^6$He ground-state energies.

## Key numbers

- The EC emulator reproduces the exact $^3$H energy with an average emulator uncertainty of approximately 0.02%, while the PMM achieves approximately 1%.
- The PMM emulator for the $^4$He charge radius achieves emulator errors of approximately 1%.
- For the $^3$H Gamow-Teller matrix element, both PMM and EC emulators achieve emulator errors of approximately 0.2%.
- The total computational cost of generating the AFDMC training data was approximately 5 million CPU-hours.
- The authors evaluated observables for approximately $2.5 \times 10^5$ posterior samples in minutes on commodity hardware.
- The chiral EFT truncation uncertainty was estimated using the Epelbaum-Krebs-Meissner prescription with a breakdown scale $\Lambda_b = 600$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of uncertainty quantification in nuclear theory by moving beyond the standard approach of estimating EFT truncation uncertainties at the observable level (as done by Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meissner (2015) and Melendez, Furnstahl, Phillips et al. (2019)) to instead propagate uncertainties from the interaction level through to many-body observables. The framework enables future ab initio studies of nuclei with consistently treated and correlated theoretical uncertainties, with planned extensions to halo nuclei such as $^6$He. The results differ from those of Carlsson, Ekstrom, Forssen et al. (2016), who found strong dependence of NN LEC posteriors on whether NN and 3N terms were fit simultaneously or separately, which the authors attribute to their inclusion of EFT truncation uncertainties in the fits.
