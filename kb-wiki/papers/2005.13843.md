# Fock space dualities

> A general theorem due to Howe of dual action of a classical group and a certain non-associative algebra on a space of symmetric or alternating tensors is reformulated in a setting of second quantization, and familiar examples in atomic and nuclear physics are discussed. The special case of orthogonal-orthogonal duality is treated in detail. It is shown that, like it was done by Helmers more than half a century ago in the analogous case of symplectic-symplectic duality, one can base a proof of the orthogonal-orthogonal duality theorem and a precise characterization of the relation between the equivalence classes of the dually related irreducible representations on a calculation of characters by combining it with an analysis of the representation of a reflection. Young diagrams for the description of equivalence classes of irreducible representations of orthogonal Lie algebras are introduced. The properties of a reflection of the number non-conserving part in the dual relationship between orthogonal Lie algebras corroborate a picture of an almost perfect symmetry between the partners.

## Key claim

Howe's general duality theorem for classical groups acting on symmetric or alternating tensor spaces can be reformulated in a second-quantization setting familiar to physicists, and the orthogonal-orthogonal special case can be proven by combining a character calculation (analogous to Helmers's symplectic-symplectic proof from 1961) with an analysis of the representation of a reflection, yielding a precise characterization of the dual irreducible representations that differs from Howe's group-algebra duality because orthogonal groups and their Lie algebras have different reducibilities due to the topological disconnectedness of reflections.

## Method

The author reformulates Howe's duality theorem (Howe 1989) in a second-quantized Fock-space framework, defining classical groups (GL(d), O(d), Sp(d)) acting on a 1-particle state space V and constructing the invariant subalgebra of the graded Lie algebra of bilinear field-operator products. For the orthogonal-orthogonal case, the proof proceeds by calculating characters combined with analyzing the representation of a reflection (the coset disconnected from rotations), building on the author's earlier Lie-algebra duality result (Neergård 2019) and using generalized Young diagrams to describe equivalence classes of irreducible representations of orthogonal Lie algebras.

## Key numbers

- The dimension d of the 1-particle state space V is even for the symplectic group Sp(d)
- For the GL(d)-GL(k) duality example with electrons in an atomic shell: d = 2l + 1 (spatial magnetic quantum numbers) and k = 2 (spin magnetic quantum numbers)
- The number of rows in a GL(d) irrep's Young diagram cannot exceed min(d,k)
- For the O(d)-o(2k) duality, the highest weight w_tau of the lh^G irrep is given by w_tau = -lambda_{k+1-tau} - d/2, where lambda_p are the row lengths of the G irrep's Young diagram

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier Lie-algebra duality result (Neergård 2019) and places it within Howe's general duality framework (Howe 1989), which was published in preprint form in 1976 but remained largely unknown to the atomic and nuclear physics community. The orthogonal-orthogonal case builds on Helmers's symplectic-symplectic duality proof (Helmers 1961) and addresses a gap left by Howe (1995), who did not provide a precise characterization of the dual irreps in the O(d)-o(2k) case. The work also connects to later results by Rowe, Repka, and Carvalho (2011) and earlier proposals by Flowers and Szpikowski (1964) for an o(8) Lie algebra of quasispin in LS coupling.
