# Valence quark-stopping and gluon junction-stopping scenarios in electron-nucleus collisions at the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider: Which one is correct?

> In the current literature, two stopping scenarios are being discussed in the context of high-energy collisions: the valence quark scenario and the gluon or baryon junction scenario. In the valence quark-stopping scenario, three valence quarks each contribute one-third of the baryon number within a baryon. Conversely, in the gluon junction-stopping scenario, the gluon junction is responsible for carrying the entire baryon number. At present, there is no consensus regarding which type of stopping scenario is correct. Based on a multi-source thermal model, our investigation indicates that the experimental data analyzed in both previous and present studies suggest that the valence quark-stopping scenario is more suitable for semi-quantitative discussions in high-energy collisions. It is anticipated that this scenario can be further validated through electron-nucleus ($eA$) collisions at the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Key claim

Based on a multi-source thermal model analysis of net-proton rapidity distributions in nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at GeV energies, the authors argue that the valence quark-stopping scenario is more suitable for semi-quantitative discussions than the gluon junction-stopping scenario, but they acknowledge that neither scenario has been conclusively verified and that future electron-nucleus (eA) collisions at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) are needed for further validation.

## Method

The authors apply a multi-source thermal model with two components: a soft excitation process (involving sea quarks, gluons, and leptons) described by an Erlang distribution for transverse momentum and a Gaussian form for rapidity, and a hard scattering process (involving principal participating parton pairs) described similarly. They fit net-proton rapidity density data from NA49 (Pb-Pb at 17.2 GeV) and BRAHMS (Au-Au at 62.4 and 200 GeV) using both a three-Gaussian distribution and a multi-component distribution with a mutually reflective two-logarithmic Gaussian weight, then extrapolate to predict net-proton distributions in electron-proton (ep) collisions at equivalent energies.

- Predictions for ep collisions at sqrt(s) = 17.2, 62.4, and 200 GeV show net-protons only in backward and central rapidity regions, with zero yield in forward rapidity region

## Context

This work extends the longstanding debate between two baryon number carrier scenarios: the valence quark picture (each quark carries one-third baryon number, forming a triangular topology) and the gluon junction picture (a non-perturbative gluon field configuration carries the entire baryon number, forming a Y-shaped topology), as discussed by previous authors (Kharzeev 1996, Kharzeev and Levin 2001, and others). The analysis uses the multi-source thermal model previously developed by Liu and collaborators (2013, 2017) to fit net-proton data from NA49 and BRAHMS collaborations. The predictions for EIC kinematics enable future experimental discrimination between the two scenarios by measuring whether net-baryons concentrate in the backward rapidity region (valence quark-stopping, due to high penetrability of non-principal valence quarks) or in the central rapidity region (gluon junction-stopping, due to strong stopping power of the gluon junction).
