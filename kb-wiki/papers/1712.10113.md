# Directed flow in Au+Au collisions from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan at the STAR experiment

> We report results of $v_1(y)$ and $dv_1/dy$ near mid-rapidity for $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $K_s^0$, $p$, $\overline{p}$, $Λ$, $\overlineΛ$ and $ϕ$ from Beam Energy Scan Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 7.7 - 200 GeV using the STAR detector at RHIC. The $dv_{1}/dy$ of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$ and $K_s^0$ mesons remains negative over all beam energies. The $dv_1/dy$ of $p$ and $Λ$ baryons shows a sign change around 10 - 15 GeV, while net baryons (net p and net $Λ$) indicate a double sign change. The $dv_1/dy$ of $\overline{p}$, $\overlineΛ$ and $ϕ$ show a similar trend for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>$ 14.5 GeV. For the first time, $v_{1}$ measurements are used to test a quark coalescence hypothesis. Many measurements are found to be consistent with the particles being formed via coalescence of constituent quarks. The observed deviations from that consistency offer a new approach for probing the collision process at the quark level.

# Directed flow in Au+Au collisions from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan at the STAR experiment

## Key claim
The directed flow slope of protons and Lambda baryons changes sign near 11.5 GeV, while net baryons show a double sign change, and these measurements are used for the first time to test a quark coalescence sum rule hypothesis. The coalescence sum rule holds for produced quarks at 11.5–200 GeV but deviates at 7.7 GeV, indicating that assumptions about equal flow for up, down, and strange quarks break down at the lowest beam energy.

## Method
Directed flow v1(y) and its slope dv1/dy at mid-rapidity were measured for π±, K±, Ks0, p, p̅, Λ, Λ̅, and φ mesons in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7–200 GeV using the STAR detector. The first-order reaction plane was determined using Beam Beam Counters (BBC) for 7.7–39 GeV and Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDC) for 62.4 and 200 GeV, with particle identification via the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and Time-of-Flight (ToF) detectors. The slope dv1/dy was extracted from linear fits over |y| < 0.8 (|y| < 0.6 for φ) for 10–40% centrality collisions.

## Key numbers
- Proton and Λ dv1/dy change sign near √sNN = 11.5 GeV
- All mesons (π±, K±, Ks0) have negative dv1/dy at all beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV
- K+ slope lies above K− at and above √sNN = 11.5 GeV, with reversal of positions at 7.7 GeV
- φ meson dv1/dy follows the trend of anti-baryons for √sNN > 14.5 GeV
- Net baryon (net p and net Λ) dv1/dy agree within uncertainties at all energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV
- Net-K dv1/dy follows net p and net Λ for √sNN > 14.5 GeV, but diverges below 14.5 GeV
- Coalescence sum rule for produced quarks (Λ̅ compared with 1/3 p̅ + K−) holds at √sNN = 11.5–200 GeV but deviates at 7.7 GeV

## Context
This work extends the directed flow measurements from STAR BES phase-I (STAR, 2014) to include Λ, Λ̅, K±, Ks0, and φ mesons, complementing earlier proton and pion results. The observed sign change in baryon dv1/dy near 10–15 GeV qualitatively resembles 3-fluid hydrodynamic model calculations with a first-order phase transition (Stoecker, 2005), but current models (hybrid, PHSD, 3FD, JAM) cannot reproduce the basic trend. The quark coalescence sum rule tests provide a new approach to probe partonic dynamics, and future BES-II detector upgrades (inner TPC, eTOF, Event Plane Detector) will improve precision, while heavy flavor measurements with the Heavy Flavor Tracker may probe early transient magnetic fields.
