# Simple solutions of fireball hydrodynamics for self-similar, ellipsoidal flows

> A new family of simple, analytic solutions of self-similarly expanding fireballs is found for systems with ellipsoidal symmetry and a direction dependent, generalized Hubble flow. Gaussian, shell like or oscillating density profiles emerge for simple choices of an arbitrary scaling function. New, cylindrically or spherically symmetric as well as approximately one dimensional hydrodynamical solutions are obtained for various special choices of the initial conditions.

## Key claim

A new family of exact, analytic solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics is found for ellipsoidally symmetric, self-similar expansions, where the density and temperature profiles are determined by an arbitrary positive scaling function of a single scaling variable, generalizing previous results that were restricted to constant temperature profiles or specific equations of state.

## Method

The author solves the non-relativistic continuity, Euler, and energy equations for an ideal gas with equation of state \(p = nT\) and \(\epsilon = \kappa p\), assuming a generalized Hubble flow velocity field \(\mathbf{v} = (\dot{X}/X \, r_x, \dot{Y}/Y \, r_y, \dot{Z}/Z \, r_z)\). The solutions are expressed in terms of a scaling variable \(s = r_x^2/X^2 + r_y^2/Y^2 + r_z^2/Z^2\), where the scale parameters \(X(t), Y(t), Z(t)\) obey Lagrangian equations of motion equivalent to a classical particle in a repulsive potential. The temperature scaling function \(\mathcal{T}(s)\) can be chosen arbitrarily (positive), and the density profile \(\nu(s)\) is then determined by a matching condition derived from the equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The equation of state parameter \(\kappa\) can take any positive value; the non-relativistic ideal gas corresponds to \(\kappa = 3/2\).
- The scale parameters evolve according to \(X \ddot{X} = Y \ddot{Y} = Z \ddot{Z} = (T_i/m) (V_0/V)^{1/\kappa}\), where \(V = XYZ\) and \(T_i\) is a constant of integration.
- For the Gaussian solution (\(\mathcal{T}(s)=1\)), the density profile is \(\nu(s) = \exp(-s/2)\).
- For the Buda-Lund type solutions, the temperature scaling function is \(\mathcal{T}(s) = 1/(1 + b s)\) with \(b = \frac{1}{2}\langle \Delta T/T \rangle\), and the density profile is \(\nu(s) = (1 + b s) \exp[ - (T_i/(2 T_0)) (s + b s^2/2) ]\).
- For oscillating density waves, an example uses \(\mathcal{T}(s) = (1 + \alpha \cos \beta s)^{-1}\) with parameters \(\alpha = 0.2\) and \(\beta = 2.0\) in the figure.

## Context

This work extends earlier spherically symmetric solutions (Zimanyi-Bondorf-Garpman, 1975; Csorgo et al., 1996) and cylindrically symmetric solutions (De-Garpman-Sperber-Bondorf-Zimanyi, 1983) to ellipsoidal symmetry with arbitrary scaling functions and arbitrary \(\kappa > 0\). It also generalizes the Buda-Lund hydro model (Csorgo et al., 1996) to ellipsoidal flows. The solutions provide analytic insight into expanding fireballs with nontrivial morphology, and their late-time limit corresponds to known accelerationless relativistic solutions such as the Hwa-Bjorken flow (Hwa, 1974; Bjorken, 1983) and the Hubble flow. The missing link to relativistic, accelerating, explicit solutions with realistic equations of state is identified as an open problem.
