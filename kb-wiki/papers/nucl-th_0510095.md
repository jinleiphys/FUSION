# Strange quark collectivity of $ϕ$ meson at RHIC

> Based on A Multi-Phase Transport model, the elliptic flow $v_{2}$ of $ϕ$ mesons which is reconstructed from $K^{+}K^{-}$ at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energy has been studied. The results show that reconstructed $v_{2}$ of $ϕ$ meson can keep the earlier information before $ϕ$ decays and it seems to obey the number of constituent quark scaling as other mesons and baryons. This result indicates that the $ϕ$ $v_2$ mostly reflects the parton level collectivity developed during the early stage of the collisions and the strange and light up/down quarks have similar collectivity properties before the hadronization.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with string melting, the reconstructed elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(\phi\) mesons from \(K^+K^-\) pairs retains the pre-decay parton-level collectivity and obeys number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling, indicating that strange quarks and light up/down quarks have similar collectivity properties before hadronization.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting to simulate minimum-bias \(^{197}\)Au + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. The \(\phi\) meson was reconstructed from \(K^+K^-\) pairs via invariant mass distributions, with combinatorial background subtracted using an event-mixing method. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) was extracted from the azimuthal distribution and corrected for reaction-plane resolution, and the in-medium hadronic rescattering effect was studied by turning the ART hadronic cascade on or off.

## Key numbers

- \(\phi\) meson \(v_2\) from minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV was reconstructed from \(\sim 100,000\) events.
- The full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the reconstructed \(\phi\) mass peak was 5.003, 5.073, 5.004, and 4.869 MeV for centralities 0–80%, 0–20%, 20–40%, and 40–80%, respectively, compared to the default AMPT value of 4.43 MeV.
- The parton scattering cross section was set to 10 mb.
- The NCQ scaling was observed in the intermediate \(p_T\) range of 1.2 to 4.0 GeV/c, with scaling behavior for \(p_T/n_q > 0.6\) GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow and NCQ scaling, previously observed for stable baryons and mesons in the AMPT model with string melting (Lin and Ko, 2002; Lin et al., 2005), to the unstable \(\phi\) meson. The results support the idea that \(\phi\) meson \(v_2\) reflects parton-level collectivity from the early stage of the collision, consistent with the weak hadronic interaction of \(\phi\) mesons argued by Shor (1985). This enables future experimental comparisons at RHIC to test whether a partonic state with collective strange quarks is created.
