# Holographic Baryons and Instanton Crystals

> In a wide class of holographic models, like the one proposed by Sakai and Sugimoto, baryons can be approximated by instantons of non-abelian gauge fields that live on the world-volume of flavor D-branes. In the leading order, those are just the Yang-Mills instantons, whose solutions can be obtained from the celebrated ADHM construction. This fact can be used to study various properties of baryons in the holographic limit. In particular, one can attempt to construct a holographic description of the cold dense nuclear matter phase of baryons. It can be argued that holographic baryons in such a regime are necessarily in a solid crystalline phase. In this review we summarize the known results on the construction and phases of crystals of the holographic baryons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in holographic models like Sakai-Sugimoto, cold dense nuclear matter at large $N_c$ and large 't Hooft coupling $\lambda$ is necessarily in a solid crystalline phase, but the authors note that the interaction between baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model is purely repulsive at all separation distances, and even in the generalized non-antipodal version the attractive-to-repulsive force ratio can never exceed $1/9$, so nuclei will not form.

## Method

The authors review the construction of baryons as Yang-Mills instantons in the five-dimensional flavor gauge theory on D8 branes in the Sakai-Sugimoto model and its generalized non-antipodal version. They analyze the energy functional for multi-instanton configurations, using the Dirac-Born-Infeld and Chern-Simons actions reduced to five dimensions, and study the minimization over instanton moduli spaces in the leading-order (Yang-Mills instantons) and next-to-leading order to find ground state configurations for baryon crystals.

## Key numbers

- The baryon size is of order $r \sim 1/M_{\rho}\sqrt{\lambda}$, becoming comparable to the string scale in the $\lambda\to\infty$ limit.
- In the generalized non-antipodal Sakai-Sugimoto model, the ratio of attractive to repulsive forces between baryons is $F_a/F_r = C^2(z) = (1-\zeta^{-3})/9 \times (u_0/u(z))^8 \le 1/9 < 1$, where $\zeta = u_0/u_{\Lambda}$ parameterizes the non-antipodal configuration.
- The Kaluza-Klein scale is set by $M_{\rm KK} \equiv 1/R$, where $2\pi R = (4\pi/3)(R_{D4}^3/u_{\Lambda})^{1/2}$.
- The five-dimensional Yang-Mills coupling near the bottom of the U-shaped flavor branes is $1/(2g^2_{\rm YM}(z)) = (N_c\lambda M_{\rm KK})/(216\pi^3) \times (\zeta + (8\zeta^3-5)/(9\zeta) M_{\rm KK}^2 z^2 + O(M_{\rm KK}^4 z^4))$.
- The net electric charge of an instanton (baryon) is $Q_{\rm el} = N_c/2$.

## Context

This review extends the Skyrme model approach to baryonic matter, specifically the work of Klebanov (1985) on simple cubic skyrmion lattices and Kugler and Shtrikman (1989) on face-centered cubic lattices and half-skyrmion transitions, into the holographic setting of the Sakai-Sugimoto model (2004) and its generalizations by Aharony et al. (2006). The paper summarizes the authors' own program from Kaplunovsky, Melnikov, and Sonnenschein (2012, 2013) on instanton crystals as holographic duals of cold nuclear matter, and relates to prior holographic studies of finite baryon density by Bergman (2007), Rozali et al. (2007), Kim et al. (2007), and Rho et al. (2009). The work enables future study of phase transitions between instanton crystal configurations and the onset of quark liquid phases at high density.
