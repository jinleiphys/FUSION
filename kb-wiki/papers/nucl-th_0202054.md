# Scaling properties of the transverse mass spectra

> Motivated from the formation of an initial state of gluon-saturated matter, we discuss scaling relations for the transverse mass spectra at BNL's Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). We show on linear plots, that the transverse mass spectra for various hadrons can be described by an universal function. The transverse mass spectra for different centralities can be rescaled into each other. Finally, we demonstrate that scaling is also present in proton-antiproton collider data and compare it to scaling at RHIC.

## Key claim
The transverse mass spectra of identified hadrons at RHIC and in proton-antiproton collisions can be described by a single universal function of transverse mass, consistent with scaling relations expected from an initial state of gluon-saturated matter (color glass condensate), provided that constant factors for strangeness and baryon number are applied and an additional vacuum scale is introduced.

## Method
The authors took minimum bias and centrality-binned transverse mass spectra for identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons) from PHENIX at RHIC for gold-gold collisions at √s = 130 GeV, and proton-antiproton data from UA1, UA2, and UA5 at √s = 540 and 630 GeV. They plotted the spectra as a function of transverse mass, applied constant rescaling factors κ_h for kaons and protons to account for strangeness and baryon number, and divided the data by a power law fit to test for deviations from a universal scaling function. For centrality dependence, they rescaled both the normalization and the transverse mass variable by centrality-dependent factors and compared to a reference power law fit to the most central bin.

## Key numbers
- The power law fit to the minimum bias data used parameters p_s = 2.71 GeV and n = 16.3, with the mean transverse momentum given by ⟨p_t⟩ = 2 p_s/(n-3).
- Deviations from scaling in the minimum bias data are only about 30% including systematic errors from the fit.
- For the centrality study, the reference power law fit to the most central bin used p_s = 1.65 GeV and n = 11.8; scaling works within 30% up to m_t ∼ 2 GeV for all centrality classes.
- The saturation scale p_s changes with centrality as p_s²/p²_{s,c} = 0.61 + 0.39(N_{part}/347)^{1/3}.
- The mean transverse momentum measured by UA1 for p̄p collisions at √s = 200 GeV is ⟨p_t⟩ = 392 ± 3 MeV; the STAR collaboration measured ⟨p_t⟩ = 508 ± 12 MeV for central gold-gold collisions at √s = 130 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the scaling relations predicted for the color glass condensate initial state (Gribov, 1978; McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994; Kovchegov, 1996) to hadron transverse mass spectra at RHIC, contradicting the notion of radial flow or thermalized matter. The authors compare their results to the centrality and pseudorapidity dependence of charged multiplicity at RHIC (Kharzeev and Nardi, 2001) and to the constant mean transverse momentum seen at the ISR (about 300 MeV). The findings enable a description of particle production across different collision systems and centralities without invoking thermalization, and suggest that similar scaling should be observable for hyperons in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
