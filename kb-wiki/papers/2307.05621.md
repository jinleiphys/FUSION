# Thermal phonon fluctuations and stability of the magnetic dual chiral density wave phase in dense QCD

> We study the stability against thermal phonon fluctuations of the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase, an inhomogeneous phase arising in cold dense QCD in a magnetic field. Following a recent study that demonstrated the absence of the Landau-Peierls (LP) instability from this phase, we calculate the (threshold) temperature at which the phonon fluctuations wash out the long-range order over a range of magnetic fields and densities relevant to astrophysical applications. Using a high-order Ginzburg-Landau expansion, we find that the threshold temperature is very near the critical temperature for fields of order $10^{18}$ G, and still a sizable fraction of the critical temperature for fields of order $10^{17}$ G. Therefore, at sufficiently large magnetic fields, the long-range order of the MDCDW phase is preserved over most of the parameter space where it is energetically favored; at smaller magnetic fields, the long-range order is still preserved over a considerable region of parameter space relevant to compact stars. We provide general symmetry arguments to explain why a magnetic field alone is not enough to eliminate the LP instability that characterizes single-modulated phases in 3+1 dimensions.

## Key claim

The magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase avoids the Landau-Peierls instability not simply because of the magnetic field, but because of the *coexistence* of a magnetic field (which breaks rotational symmetry) with a ground state that breaks time-reversal symmetry; the threshold temperature at which thermal phonon fluctuations wash out long-range order remains a sizable fraction of the critical temperature for fields of order 10^17 G and approaches the critical temperature for fields of order 10^18 G.

## Method

The authors used a high-order Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the thermodynamic potential for a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model in an external magnetic field, extending the expansion to arbitrarily high order N (with N ≥ 20 needed for reliability). They derived the low-energy effective theory of phonon fluctuations from the GL expansion, computed the average square phonon fluctuation in the infrared limit, and solved the transcendental equation for the threshold temperature T_thr (defined as the temperature where ⟨M⟩ = e^{-1} M_0) over a range of chemical potentials and magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- Threshold temperature T_thr is "very near" the critical temperature T_c for magnetic fields of order 10^18 G.
- Threshold temperature T_thr is "still a sizable fraction" of T_c for magnetic fields of order 10^17 G.
- The study focuses on magnetic field values in the range 10^17–10^18 G, based on astrophysical estimates.
- The condensate is favored over a range of densities 3.5–9 n_s (where n_s is nuclear saturation density).
- The maximum stellar mass constraint used for comparison is M ≳ 2 M_⊙.
- The heat capacity of an MDCDW neutron star core satisfies C_V ≳ 10^36 (T/10^8) erg/K.
- The upper bound for core magnetic fields from neutron star stability is ∼8×10^18 (1.4 M_⊙/M) G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration (Ferrer, Gyory, and de la Incera, 2022) that the MDCDW phase lacks the Landau-Peierls instability at arbitrarily low temperatures, which plagues other single-modulated inhomogeneous phases such as the real kink crystal and the DCDW phase (Hidaka, 2013; Lee, 1992). The results enable assessment of whether the MDCDW phase remains viable as a candidate for the inner phase of neutron stars with strong internal magnetic fields, complementing prior studies of its energetics (Ferrer, Gyory, and de la Incera, 2020), stellar mass compatibility (Inhomogeneous Stars, 2020), and heat capacity (Ferrer, Gyory, and de la Incera, 2020).
