# Dissipation dynamics of a scalar field

> We investigate the dissipation rate of a scalar field in the vicinity of the phase transition and the ordered phase, specifically within the universality class of model A. This dissipation rate holds significant physical relevance, particularly in the context of interpreting effective potentials as inputs for dynamical transport simulations, such as hydrodynamics. To comprehensively understand the use of effective potentials and other calculation inputs, such as the functional renormalization group, we conduct a detailed analysis of field dependencies. We solve the functional renormalization group equations on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour to determine the effective potential and dissipation rate for both finite and infinite volumes. Furthermore, we conduct a finite-size scaling analysis to calculate the dynamic critical exponent z. Our extracted value closely matches existing values from the literature.

## Key claim

The dissipation rate of a scalar field in model A universality class can be computed using the functional renormalization group on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour, and the resulting dynamic critical exponent extracted from finite-size scaling closely matches existing literature values, though the calculation relies on a leading-order derivative expansion truncation.

## Method

The authors solved functional renormalization group equations formulated on the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path contour to obtain the scale-dependent effective potential and dissipation rate for a single-component real scalar field with Z2-symmetric Landau-Ginzburg Hamiltonian. They employed a leading-order derivative expansion truncated at first order in time derivatives and second order in spatial derivatives, retaining full field dependence of the effective potential and dissipation rate. The flow equations were solved numerically for both infinite volume and finite spatial volumes, and a finite-size scaling analysis was performed to extract the dynamic critical exponent z.

## Key numbers

- Critical mass value: m²_c = -0.927814 (used for finite-size scaling analysis)
- Static critical exponent β = 0.36 from fit of field expectation value φ₀(t_r) = a Θ(t_r) |t_r|^β with a = 0.95
- Static critical exponent ν = 0.67 from fit of correlation length ξ = a |t_r|^(-ν) with a = 3.56
- Dynamic critical exponent z = 2.011 from finite-size scaling fit of relaxation time t_c(0, L) versus linear box size L at the critical mass

## Context

This work extends previous functional renormalization group studies of model A by Canet (2006), Canet (2011), and others, and connects the dissipation rate calculation to hydrodynamic transport simulations. The authors demonstrate how coarse-grained inputs from the fRG, specifically the effective potential and dissipation rate, can be used to close the system of equations in hydrodynamic descriptions of critical dynamics. The results enable first-principle input for dynamical transport simulations such as hydrodynamics near phase transitions, particularly relevant for understanding the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions.
