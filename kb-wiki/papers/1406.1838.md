# Applications of Basis Light-Front Quantization to QED

> Hamiltonian light-front quantum field theory provides a framework for calculating both static and dynamic properties of strongly interacting relativistic systems. Invariant masses, correlated parton amplitudes and time-dependent scattering amplitudes, possibly with strong external time-dependent fields, represent a few of the important applications. By choosing the light-front gauge and adopting an orthonormal basis function representation, we obtain a large, sparse, Hamiltonian matrix eigenvalue problem for mass eigenstates that we solve by adapting ab initio no-core methods of nuclear many-body theory. In the continuum limit, the infinite matrix limit, we recover full covariance. Guided by the symmetries of light-front quantized theory, we adopt a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis for transverse modes that corresponds with eigensolutions of the soft-wall anti-de Sitter/quantum chromodynamics (AdS/QCD) model obtained from light-front holography. We outline our approach and present results for non-linear Compton scattering, evaluated non-perturbatively, where a strong and time-dependent laser field accelerates the electron and produces states of higher invariant mass i.e. final states with photon emission.

## Key claim
Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) and its time-dependent extension (tBLFQ) provide a nonperturbative framework for solving quantum field theory in both stationary and time-dependent regimes, demonstrated here for QED with the caveat that the chosen laser background field does not obey Maxwell's equations in vacuum, as the work focuses on a proof-of-concept demonstration rather than phenomenology.

## Method
The authors adopt light-front quantization with a basis consisting of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator states for transverse modes and discretized longitudinal plane waves, producing a sparse Hamiltonian matrix eigenvalue problem for mass eigenstates solved using ab initio no-core nuclear many-body methods. For time-dependent processes, they extend BLFQ to tBLFQ by solving the light-front SchrÃ¶dinger equation non-perturbatively in the interaction picture, using a Fock-space truncation (electron plus electron-photon states) and a classical laser background field modeled as \(e\mathcal{A}^-(x^-)=2 m a_0 \cos{(\omega x^-)}\) to study non-linear Compton scattering.

## Key numbers
- Electron anomalous magnetic moment extrapolation to infinite basis reproduces the Schwinger result \(\sqrt{1/(8\pi^2)}\); BLFQ calculations span \(N_{\text{max}}=18\) to \(258\) (where \(N_{\text{max}}=K-\frac{1}{2}\)).
- Laser parameters used for time evolution: \(a_0=0.5\) and \(\omega=1.0\) MeV; electromagnetic coupling constant \(\alpha = e^2/(4\pi) = 1/137\).
- At light-front time \(x^+=14\) MeV\(^{-1}\), the probability of finding the system in the \(K=5.5\) single electron state is approximately 0.75, while the initial \(K=1.5\) electron state is almost completely depleted.
- For \(a_0=10\) and \(\omega=1.0\) MeV, the average invariant mass \(\langle M\rangle\) of the electron system increases with exposure time, reflecting energy pumped into the system by the laser field.

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ approach (Vary 2009) to time-dependent processes, building on Hamiltonian light-front formalism (Brodsky 1997) and adapting no-core methods from nuclear many-body theory. The transverse harmonic oscillator basis is guided by the soft-wall AdS/QCD model from light-front holography (de Teramond 2008, Brodsky 2013). The results enable future applications to generalized parton distributions, multi-photon emission processes, and ultimately strong scattering problems in QCD, as well as more realistic modeling of next-generation laser facilities and relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
