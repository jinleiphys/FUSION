# Tools for incorporating a D-wave contribution in Skyrme energy density functionals

> The possibility of adding a D-wave term to the standard Skyrme effective interaction has been widely considered in the past. Such a term has been shown to appear in the next-to-next-to-leading order of the Skyrme pseudo-potential. The aim of the present article is to provide the necessary tools to incorporate this term in a fitting procedure: first, a mean-field equation written in spherical symmetry in order to describe spherical nuclei and second, the response function to detect unphysical instabilities. With these tools it will be possible to build a new fitting procedure to determine the coupling constants of the new functional.

## Key claim

The paper provides the formal tools (mean-field equations in spherical symmetry and linear-response functions) needed to incorporate a gauge-invariant fourth-order (D-wave) term into the Skyrme energy density functional, but it does not itself perform a fit or determine the coupling constants; it only prepares the framework for such a future fitting procedure.

## Method

The authors derive the energy density functional from a fourth-order Skyrme pseudo-potential that includes D-wave contributions while respecting Galilean and gauge invariance, extending the standard second-order Skyrme interaction. They then write the Hartree-Fock equations in spherical symmetry, obtaining a fourth-order radial differential equation for the single-particle wave functions, and derive the linear response formalism (inverse-energy-weighted sum rule) to detect unphysical instabilities in infinite nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The standard second-order Skyrme functional has 10 free parameters (2 at 0th order, 4 at 2nd order, 1 spin-orbit, 3 for the density-dependent term); adding the fourth-order terms brings the total to 14 parameters (adding 4 more at 4th order).
- Including a real three-body term and a first-order tensor term would increase the parameter count to 18.
- Carlsson et al. (2011) showed that including fourth-order terms in a Density Matrix Expansion improves agreement with finite-range Gogny calculations from an average difference of approximately 10 MeV at second order to approximately 1 MeV at fourth order.

## Context

This work extends the systematic exploration of higher-order terms in the Skyrme pseudo-potential begun by Raimondi et al. (2011) and Carlsson et al. (2008), and follows the authors' own previous derivation of the fourth-order pseudo-potential in Cartesian basis (Davesne et al., 2013). The tools provided enable future fitting procedures to determine the coupling constants of the new functional, addressing the conclusion of the UNEDF collaboration (Kortelainen et al., 2014) that standard Skyrme functionals cannot be further improved at the single-reference level.
