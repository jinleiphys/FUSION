# The nucleon-nucleon system in chiral effective theory

> I discuss the conditions under which the application of chiral perturbation theory to the NN potential gives reliable results for NN scattering phase shifts. ChiPT also yields a convergent expansion for the deuteron charge operator. For cutoffs < 1 GeV, this produces precise predictions for deuterium's quadrupole and charge form factors in the range Q^2 < 0.25 GeV^2.

## Key claim

Chiral effective theory (χET) predictions for nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts are reliable only when the cutoff Λ is kept below approximately 1 GeV; above this cutoff, partial waves with L > 0 and even S-waves at higher orders show cutoff-dependent instabilities that indicate the theory is not properly renormalized.

## Method

The author applies chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to compute the NN potential up to fixed orders O(P²), O(P³), and O(P⁴), then iterates this potential via the Schrödinger or Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain scattering amplitudes. For the deuteron, wave functions from these potentials are combined with χPT-derived electromagnetic current operators to compute charge and quadrupole form factors, with cutoffs varied between 500 and 800 MeV to assess convergence and theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff Λ ≈ 1 GeV is identified as the point where phase-shift instabilities first appear in partial waves with L > 0 and in S-waves at O(P²) and O(P³).
- For cutoffs between 500 and 800 MeV, the O(P⁴) χET potential describes NN data with "considerable accuracy" and very little residual cutoff dependence.
- The deuteron point-proton charge radius from NNLO wave functions with O(eP⁴) current operator is ⟨r²⟩^{1/2}_{pt} = 1.975(1) fm, compared to the isotope-shift value 1.9748(7) fm.
- The prediction for the deuteron charge form factor G_C is accurate to ±1.5% at Q² = 0.16 GeV².
- The quadrupole moment Q_d varies by 2% when Λ is changed from 500 to 800 MeV, with the experimental value Q_d = 0.2859(3) fm².
- The prediction for G_Q at Q² = 0.16 GeV² has a theoretical uncertainty of ∼3%.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory program initiated by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Epelbaum et al. (1999, 2005) and Entem and Machleidt (2003, 2006), who fitted NN potentials up to O(P⁴) to data. It contradicts the assumption that straightforward χPT potentials can be used over a wide range of cutoffs, showing instead that cutoffs must be kept below ∼1 GeV to avoid renormalization problems identified by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) and others. The deuteron form-factor predictions enable tests against forthcoming JLab data and BLAST measurements, and constrain the short-distance operator at O(eP⁵) via the deuteron charge radius.
