# Unshadowing the constituent quark number scaling of harmonic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> Constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow has been proposed as one key observable to identify the phase transition or the absence of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions. At the fixed target program at RHIC the STAR collaboration has recently reported that NCQ scaling breaks when decreasing the collision energy from $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 4.5$ to $3.0$ GeV. However, the generation of elliptic flow is dominated by a highly intricate interplay of spectator shadowing, squeeze-out and geometry dependent hadron emission governed by their cross sections. Therefore in this article we will disentangle the shadowing contribution from the harmonic flow signal of the particle emitting source, effectively ``unshadowing'' the source. We introduce Fourier coefficients that quantify the azimuthal absorption rate of hadrons decoupling from the system. We benchmark the derived results using a toy model based on a ballistic Glauber description of the penetrating nuclei and calculate how shadowing qualitatively alters the constituent quark number scaling of the hadron emitting source. The results are thus relevant for interpreting recent STAR measurements as well as the upcoming measurements by CBM at FAIR.

## Key claim
The breaking of constituent quark number (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow observed by STAR at collision energies below \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 4.5\) GeV may not indicate the disappearance of partonic collectivity, but instead results from spectator shadowing that distorts the measured harmonic flow coefficients. The paper derives a method to "unshadow" the source by subtracting the shadowing contribution, showing that the underlying hadron-emitting source could still obey NCQ scaling even when the measured data do not.

## Method
The authors derive analytic expressions connecting measurable harmonic flow coefficients \(\mathcal{V}^h_n\) to the flow coefficients of the hadron-emitting source \(v_n\) and the Fourier coefficients of the transverse escape probability \(p_n^h\), which quantifies azimuthal absorption by spectator nucleons. They expand the product of the parton coalescence distribution and the escape probability as Fourier series, obtaining closed-form relations for mesons and baryons. A toy model using a ballistic Glauber description of penetrating nuclei with constant effective cross sections is employed to calculate the shadowing coefficients and demonstrate the qualitative effect on elliptic flow at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 3.0\) and \(7.7\) GeV.

## Key numbers
- STAR reported NCQ scaling breaks when decreasing collision energy from \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 4.5\) to \(3.0\) GeV.
- Toy model calculations use \(v_{2,\mathrm{max}}^q = 0.05\) and \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV for the parton elliptic flow parametrization.
- Effective absorption cross sections used: \(\sigma^\mathrm{eff}_{\pi N} = 50\) mb, \(\sigma^\mathrm{eff}_{K N} = 12\) mb, \(\sigma^\mathrm{eff}_{\overline{K} N} = 50\) mb, \(\sigma^\mathrm{eff}_{\phi N} = 5\) mb, \(\sigma^\mathrm{eff}_{p N} = 40\) mb, \(\sigma^\mathrm{eff}_{\Lambda N} = 30\) mb.
- Toy model calculations are performed for peripheral Au+Au collisions at impact parameter \(b = 7\) fm at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 3.0\) GeV and \(7.7\) GeV.
- The leading-order scaling relations after unshadowing are \(\mathcal{V}^M_n(p_\mathrm{T}) - p^M_n(p_\mathrm{T}) = 2 v_n(p_\mathrm{T} / 2)\) for mesons and \(\mathcal{V}^B_n(p_\mathrm{T}) - p^B_n(p_\mathrm{T}) = 3 v_n(p_\mathrm{T} / 3)\) for baryons.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Kolb (2004) on NCQ scaling from quark coalescence by incorporating spectator shadowing, which is negligible at high energies but dominant at low-GeV collisions. It directly addresses the STAR-FXT measurements (STAR 2021, STAR 2025) that reported breaking of NCQ scaling below \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \approx 4.5\) GeV, which had been interpreted as "disappearance of partonic collectivity." The results provide a method to correct measured flow data for shadowing, enabling future studies by CBM at FAIR and reanalysis of STAR data to test whether the hadron-emitting source itself exhibits NCQ scaling. The \(\phi\) meson, with its small cross section (\(\sigma^\mathrm{eff}_{\phi N} = 5\) mb), is identified as a promising non-shadowed baseline for such investigations.
