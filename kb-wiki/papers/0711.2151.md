# Hadron interactions from lattice QCD

> Studies on hadron interactions from lattice QCD are reviewed. The $S$-wave $ππ$ scattering lengths of the I=0 and I=2 channels are extracted from various lattice determinations of low energy constants in $N_f=2$ chiral perturbation theory. The results agree with each other and agree also with other non-lattice estimates. Recently the $P$-wave $ππ$ scattering phase shift for the I=1 channel has been calculated. A preliminary estimate of the $ρ$ meson decay width from the phase shift is consistent with the experimental value. Two approaches to potentials between hadrons are discussed. One is a method using static quarks to define the distance between two hadrons. The other is a method to define a potential from a wave function of two hadrons. An application of the latter to the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potential turns out to reproduce qualitative features of the phenomenological $NN$ potential such as attraction at long distance and repulsion at short distance. Theoretical issues of this approach are also discussed.

# Hadron interactions from lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can reproduce the qualitative features of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, including attraction at long distance and repulsion at short distance, by defining a potential from the wave function of two nucleons, but this potential is energy-dependent in general and requires further theoretical development to become a unique, physically meaningful local potential.

## Method

The author reviews two approaches to extracting hadron interactions from lattice QCD. The conventional method uses finite-volume techniques (Lüscher's method) to extract scattering lengths and phase shifts from energy shifts in a finite box. The newer method defines a potential by computing the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter-like wave function of two hadrons from four-point correlators and then inverting the Schrödinger equation to obtain a local potential. For the NN potential, quenched QCD simulations were performed on a 32^4 lattice with plaquette gauge action and Wilson quark action at a lattice spacing of approximately 0.137 fm, using 2000 configurations at m_pi ~ 370 and 527 MeV and 1000 configurations at m_pi ~ 732 MeV.

## Key numbers

- S-wave pi-pi scattering lengths from ETM collaboration: M_pi a_0^0 = 0.221(1) and M_pi a_0^2 = -0.0427(1) (with M_pi = 140 MeV and F_pi = 93 MeV)
- Direct result for a_0^2 from NPLQCD collaboration: M_pi a_0^2 = -0.0443(4)
- For the rho meson decay width from CP-PACS: tan(delta) = 0.0791(9) at sqrt(s) = 0.776(8) (continuum dispersion) or tan(delta) = 0.074(7) at sqrt(s) = 0.800(8) (lattice dispersion), with the rho mass m_rho = 0.86(1) in lattice units
- Estimated rho decay width: Gamma^ph = 162(35) MeV (continuum) and Gamma^ph = 140(27) MeV (lattice), consistent with the experimental value of 150 MeV
- Lattice spacing for NN potential calculation: a ~ 0.137 fm from the rho meson mass
- Pion masses used in NN potential: m_pi ~ 370, 527, and 732 MeV
- For the heavy baryon-baryon potential: m_pi ~ 500 MeV at a ~ 0.19 fm on a 20^3 x 24 lattice

## Context

This review extends the program of extracting hadron interactions from lattice QCD, building on the finite-volume method of Lüscher (1986) for scattering phase shifts and the Rummukainen-Gottlieb formula (1995) for moving frames. The work on NN potentials from wave functions, pioneered by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2007), represents a new approach that qualitatively reproduces the phenomenological NN potentials (Bonn, Reid93, AV18) derived from scattering data. The results enable future calculations of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions relevant to neutron star cores and hypernuclei, as well as systematic chiral and continuum extrapolations toward physical quark masses.
