# Shell Model Description of Isotope Shifts in Calcium

> Isotope shifts in the nuclear charge radius of even and odd calcium isotopes are calculated within the nuclear shell model. The model space includes all configurations of nucleons in the $2s, 1d_{3/2}, 1f_{7/2}, {\rm and} ~2p_{3/2}$ orbits. The shell model describes well the energies of the intruder states in Sc and Ca, as well as the energies of the low-lying $2^+$ and $3^-$ states in the even Ca isotopes. The characteristic features of the isotope shifts, the parabolic dependence on $A$ and the prominent odd-even staggering, are well reproduced by the model. These features are related to the partial breakdown of the $Z = 20$ shell closure caused by promotion, due to the neutron-proton interaction, of the $ds$ shell protons into the $fp$ shell.

# Shell Model Description of Isotope Shifts in Calcium

## Key claim

The characteristic parabolic shape and odd-even staggering of calcium isotope shifts can be reproduced by large-scale shell model calculations without introducing many-body forces or adjustable parameters, provided the valence space includes the $2s$, $1d_{3/2}$, $1f_{7/2}$, and $2p_{3/2}$ orbits for both protons and neutrons. However, the calculated magnitude of the isotope shifts is systematically smaller than the experimental values, suggesting that the neglected $d_{5/2}$ and $f_{5/2}$ orbits may be needed for quantitative agreement.

## Method

The authors performed full-configuration shell model calculations for calcium isotopes $^{40}$Ca through $^{48}$Ca using the ANTOINE and NATHAN codes, with a valence space consisting of the $d_{3/2}$, $s_{1/2}$, $f_{7/2}$, and $p_{3/2}$ subshells for both protons and neutrons (treating $^{28}$Si as the inert core). The two-body matrix elements were taken from Retamosa et al. (1997) and adjusted by subtracting a schematic pairing Hamiltonian to avoid double counting, with the cross $sd$-$fp$ monopoles retuned to reproduce the gap at $^{40}$Ca and the spectra of $^{39}$K and $^{41}$Ca. The isotope shifts were then computed from the calculated number of protons promoted to the $fp$ shell, $n_{fp}^{\pi}(A)$, using the relation $\delta r_c^2(A) = (1/Z) n_{fp}^{\pi}(A) \cdot b^2$ with a constant oscillator parameter $b = 1.974$ fm.

## Key numbers

- The number of protons in the $fp$ shell, $n_{fp}^{\pi}(A)$, is 1.10 in $^{40}$Ca, reaches a maximum of 1.97 in $^{44}$Ca, and decreases to 0.78 in $^{48}$Ca.
- The oscillator parameter used is $b = 1.974$ fm, taken from Caurier (1980).
- The largest $m$-scheme basis dimension encountered is 34,274,564 for the ground state of $^{43}$Ca.
- The total Gamow-Teller strength in $^{40}$Ca calculated within the model space (with quenching factor 0.744) is $B(GT) = 0.53$; when allowing $t=1$ excitations involving the $f_{5/2}$ and $d_{5/2}$ orbits, the calculated $B(GT)$ becomes 2.75, compared to the experimental value of $1.6 \pm 0.1$ below 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier configuration-mixing approach of Caurier (1980), which described calcium isotope shifts using a $(f_{7/2}d_{3/2})^n$ space with an isoscalar particle-hole force, by demonstrating that modern large-scale shell model calculations can reproduce the same features without adjustable many-body forces. The results are consistent with the "islands of inversion" phenomenon at $N=20$ identified in neutron-rich nuclei by Caurier (1998, 2000), showing that similar cross-shell proton-neutron correlations are already visible in stable calcium isotopes. The calculation enables quantitative connection between isotope shifts, intruder state energies, and Gamow-Teller strength as complementary signatures of the partial breakdown of the $Z=20$ shell closure.
