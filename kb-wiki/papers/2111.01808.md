# The transversity parton distribution function of the nucleon using the pseudo-distribution approach

> We present a determination of the non-singlet transversity parton distribution function (PDF) of the nucleon, normalized with respect to the tensor charge at $μ^2=2$ GeV$^2$ from lattice quantum chromodynamics. We apply the pseudo-distribution approach, using a gauge ensemble with a lattice spacing of 0.094 fm and the light quark mass tuned to a pion mass of 358 MeV. We extract the transversity PDF from the analysis of the short-distance behavior of the Ioffe-time pseudo-distribution using the leading-twist next-to-leading order (NLO) matching coefficients calculated for transversity. We reconstruct the $x$-dependence of the transversity PDF through an expansion in a basis of Jacobi polynomials in order to reduce the PDF ansatz dependence. Within the limitations imposed by a heavier-than-physical pion mass and a fixed lattice spacing, we present a comparison of our estimate for the valence transversity PDF with the recent global fit results based on single transverse spin asymmetry. We find the intrinsic nucleon sea to be isospin symmetric with respect to transversity.

## Key claim

The authors determine the $x$-dependence of the nucleon transversity parton distribution function (PDF) normalized by the tensor charge at $\mu^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$ using lattice QCD, finding the intrinsic nucleon sea to be isospin symmetric with respect to transversity, but note that this result is obtained under the limitations of a heavier-than-physical pion mass of 358 MeV and a fixed lattice spacing of 0.094 fm.

## Method

The authors applied the pseudo-distribution approach, computing the reduced Ioffe-time pseudo-distribution (rpITD) from equal-time matrix elements of a bilocal quark operator with a straight Wilson line on a $32^3 \times 64$ lattice with 2+1 flavor Wilson-clover fermions at a pion mass of 358 MeV. They extracted the bare nucleon matrix elements using two-state fits to the ratio of three-point to two-point functions and then matched the rpITD to the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ transversity PDF using next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative matching coefficients. The $x$-dependence was reconstructed through an expansion in a basis of Jacobi polynomials to reduce the PDF ansatz dependence.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: $a = 0.094$ fm; pion mass: $M_\pi = 358$ MeV; lattice size: $32^3 \times 64$.
- Nucleon momenta used: $P_3 = 0, 0.41, 0.82, 1.23, 1.64, 2.06, 2.47$ GeV, corresponding to $n_3 = 0$ through $6$ with $\Delta = 0.41$ GeV.
- Nucleon mass from the ensemble: $M_N = 1.115(5)$ GeV at $P_3 = 0$.
- Energy gap between ground and first excited state at $P_3 = 0$: $E_1 - E_0 = 1.3(2)$ GeV.
- Source-sink separations used: $t_s = 4a, 6a, 8a, 10a, 12a, 14a$, corresponding to 0.388 fm to 1.358 fm in physical units.
- The 1-loop effect on the Wilson coefficient $C_1$ at $z_3 \approx 0.4$ fm is about 10%, and on $C_2$ about 20%, at $\mu = \sqrt{2}$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD computations of the transversity PDF using the LaMET approach by Liu et al. (2018) and Alexandrou et al. (2018) by providing an independent determination using the pseudo-distribution approach, which suffers from different systematic effects such as power corrections and renormalization prescriptions. It complements earlier lattice studies of the tensor charge and its moments by Bhattacharya et al. (2016), Green et al. (2012), and others, and enables future comparisons with global fit results from the JAM collaboration (Cammarota et al., 2020) and with upcoming experimental data from the Electron-Ion Collider and JLab 12 GeV.
