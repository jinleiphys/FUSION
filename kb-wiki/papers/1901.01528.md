# Chiral Kinetic Theory from Effective Field Theory Revisited

> We revisit the chiral kinetic equation from high density effective theory approach, finding a chiral kinetic equation differs from counterpart derived from field theory in high order terms in the $O(1/μ)$ expansion, but in agreement with the equation derived in on-shell effective field theory upon identification of cutoff. By using reparametrization transformation properties of the effective theory, we show that the difference in kinetic equations from two approaches are in fact expected. It is simply due to different choices of degree of freedom by effective theory and field theory. We also show that they give equivalent description of the dynamics of chiral fermions.

## Key claim

The chiral kinetic equation derived from high density effective field theory (HDET) differs from the field-theory-derived counterpart in higher-order terms of the \(O(1/\mu)\) expansion, but agrees with the equation from on-shell effective field theory upon identification of the cutoff; the difference is expected and arises from different choices of degrees of freedom, and both descriptions are equivalent for the dynamics of chiral fermions.

## Method

The authors start from the Lagrangian for right-handed chiral fermions at finite density \(\mu\) and zero temperature, decompose the fermion momentum into a large Fermi momentum \(\mu \mathbf{v}\) and a small residual momentum \(\mathbf{l}\), and integrate out the heavy anti-particle modes to obtain an effective Lagrangian valid near the Fermi surface. They then construct the gauge-invariant Wigner function from the two-point function of the effective field, derive its equation of motion using gradient expansion up to \(O(1/\mu^2)\), and extract the dispersion relation and transport equation. The reparametrization transformation properties of the effective theory are analyzed to show that the apparent differences between the HDET-derived kinetic equation and the field-theory-derived one are due to different choices of degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

* The effective Lagrangian is expanded in powers of \(1/\mu\), with terms kept up to \(O(1/\mu^2)\): the leading term is \(i v \cdot D\), the \(O(1/\mu)\) term is \(\frac{\slashed{D}^2_\perp}{2\mu}\), and the \(O(1/\mu^2)\) term is \(\frac{\slashed{D}_\perp (-i \bar{v} \cdot D) \slashed{D}_\perp}{4\mu^2}\).
* The dispersion relation extracted from the Wigner function delta function includes terms up to \(O(1/\mu^2)\): \(l_0 = l_\parallel + \frac{1}{2\mu}[l^2_\perp - \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{v}] - \frac{1}{2\mu^2}[l_\parallel (l^2_\perp - \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{v})] - \frac{1}{4\mu^2}[\mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{l}_\perp + (\mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{l})\cdot \mathbf{v}]\).
* The coefficients in the \(O(1/\mu^2)\) terms of the summed and subtracted equations of motion (\(I^{(2)}_+\) and \(I^{(2)}_-\)) differ from those in Son and Yamamoto (2012) but agree with Carignano, Manuel and Torres-Rincon (2018) upon identification of the cutoff.

## Context

This work revisits the high density effective theory approach of Son and Yamamoto (2012) and compares it with the on-shell effective field theory approach of Carignano, Manuel and Torres-Rincon (2018), finding agreement between the two effective theory derivations. The paper extends the discussion of reparametrization invariance from heavy quark effective theory (Georgi, 1990; Chen, 1993) to the HDET context, showing that the ambiguity in the chiral kinetic equation is a consequence of different choices of degrees of freedom rather than a physical discrepancy. This enables a unified understanding of chiral kinetic theory from effective field theory and field theory approaches, and clarifies the origin of higher-order differences in the transport equation.
