# Is the Turner Window Open? Seeking Closure with Resonant Absorption of Galactic Axions in NaI Dark Matter Detectors

> Motivated by the DAMA/LIBRA annual modulation signal, the dark matter community has invested heavily in ultra-clean underground NaI detectors to search for light WIMPs. We point out a new target of opportunity for these detectors -- axions produced by the carbon-burning stars within our galaxy. These stars synthesize large quantities of $^{23}$Na, keeping it at temperatures $\sim 10^9$K for periods up to tens of thousands of years. Under these conditions, $^{23}$Na radiates 440 keV axions through repeated photo-excitation and axio-deexcitation of its first excited state. Upon reaching a NaI detector, the process is reversed: the axion is resonantly absorbed, producing a 440 keV deexcitation photon. NaI thus serves as both $γ$ source and $γ$ detector. We find that existing NaI detectors can probe axion-nucleon couplings $|g_{aNN}^\mathrm{eff~^{23}Na}| \approx g_{app} \sim 10^{-6}$--$10^{-2}$, including QCD axions with $m_a \gtrsim 10$ eV. While there are several astrophysical constraints on axions with these couplings, our re-examination of these bounds shows that substantial gaps remain, providing strong motivation for the proposed searches.

## Key claim

Existing NaI dark matter detectors can probe axion-nucleon couplings \(|g_{aNN}^{\mathrm{eff~^{23}Na}}| \approx g_{app} \sim 10^{-6}\)–\(10^{-2}\) via resonant absorption of 440 keV axions produced by galactic carbon-burning stars, but the actual sensitivity window is bounded by both detector statistics and stellar reabsorption, yielding a \(3\sigma\) exclusion range of \(8.4 \times 10^{-7} \lesssim |g_{aNN}^{\mathrm{eff~^{23}Na}}| \lesssim 1.5 \times 10^{-2}\) for a 500 kg-yr NaI exposure.

## Method

The authors compute the galactic flux of 440 keV axions from the first excited state of \({}^{23}\)Na, which is thermally populated in carbon-burning stars at temperatures \(\sim 10^9\) K. They use the MESA stellar evolution code to model an ensemble of progenitors (masses \(\gtrsim 7.5 M_\odot\)) and embed them in a galactic model fitted to disk and bulge stellar populations with a Salpeter initial mass function. The resonant absorption cross section in NaI detectors is normalized to the known radiative width of the \({}^{23}\)Na excited state (\(\Gamma_\gamma = 4.01 \times 10^{-4}\) eV) and the E2/M1 mixing ratio (\(\delta = 0.065\)), with the thermal line broadening \(\sigma_\mathrm{TH} \approx 0.88\) keV at \(T_9 = 1\) governing the overlap between the emitted axion line and the detector absorption line. Stellar reabsorption of axions on \({}^{23}\)Na, Compton scattering, and the Primakoff process are included in the escape probability calculation, though resonant reabsorption on \({}^{23}\)Na dominates the opacity.

## Key numbers

* Most probable galactic axion flux: \(\phi_0 = 0.173 \times 10^6 \, |g_{aNN}^{\mathrm{eff~^{23}Na}} / 10^{-7}|^2\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\)
* Median galactic axion flux: \(\langle \phi_a^\mathrm{galactic} \rangle = 0.214 \times 10^6 \, |g_{aNN}^{\mathrm{eff~^{23}Na}} / 10^{-7}|^2\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\)
* Peak galactic flux: \(\phi_a \approx 2.1 \times 10^{10}\) axions/cm\(^2\)/sec at couplings \(\sim 10^{-3}\)–\(10^{-5}\)
* Thermal line width: \(\sigma_\mathrm{TH}(T_9=1) \approx 0.88\) keV for the 440.2 keV transition
* Radiative width of the \({}^{23}\)Na excited state: \(\Gamma_\gamma = 4.01 \times 10^{-4}\) eV
* Detector resolution: \(\Gamma_\mathrm{FWHM} \approx 22\) keV from COSINE (\(\Delta E/E \approx 0.05\) above 100 keV)
* Background rate: \(b = 0.37\) counts/kg/d/keV from COSINE-100 crystals in 430–450 keV bins
* \(3\sigma\) statistical background uncertainty: \(\Delta_\mathrm{bg}^\mathrm{stat} \approx 3660\) events for 500 kg-yr
* \(3\sigma\) exclusion range: \(8.4 \times 10^{-7} \lesssim |g_{aNN}^{\mathrm{eff~^{23}Na}}| \lesssim 1.5 \times 10^{-2}\)
* SNO neutron bound: \(1.9 \times 10^{-5} < |g_{aNN}^3|\) and \(|g^3_{aNN} - g^0_{aNN}| < 0.95 \times 10^{-3}\)
* SN1987A cooling bound: \(1.19 \times 10^{-9} < [(g_{app}+0.435 g_{ann})^2 + 1.45 g_{ann}^2]^{1/2} \lesssim 3 \times 10^{-7}\)

## Context

This work extends the concept of stellar axion production via nuclear "pumping" first proposed by Haxton and Lee (1991) for \({}^{57}\)Fe and \({}^{23}\)Na, but applies it to galactic rather than solar sources. The authors build on their own recent galactic modeling (HLMR, 2025) and detailed nuclear calculations (HLRR2, 2025) to show that NaI detectors built to test the DAMA/LIBRA annual modulation signal (Bernabei et al., 2003) can simultaneously search for 440 keV axions. The paper re-examines three astrophysical constraints: the SNO deuteron breakup limit (PhysRevLett.126.091601, 2021), the SN1987A Kamiokande II gamma-ray limits (PhysRevLett.65.960, 1990), and the SN1987A cooling bound (Caputo et al., 2024), finding that substantial gaps remain in the "Turner window" (Turner, 1990) for axions with masses \(m_a \gtrsim 10\) eV. The proposed searches could probe QCD axion models (DFSZ, KSVZ) and ALPs with dominant proton or neutron couplings, potentially closing the Turner window.
