# Nucleon structure at large x: nuclear effects in deuterium

> I review quark momentum distributions in the nucleon at large momentum fractions x. Particular attention is paid to the impact of nuclear effects in deuterium on the d/u quark distribution ratio as x -> 1. A new global study of parton distributions, using less restrictive kinematic cuts in Q^2 and W^2, finds strong suppression of the d quark distribution once nuclear corrections are accounted for.

## Key claim

Nuclear corrections in deuterium, when properly accounted for via a relativistic off-shell smearing model, lead to a strong suppression of the extracted \(d\) quark distribution at large \(x\), with the effect being up to 40% at \(x \approx 0.8\) relative to fits that neglect such corrections; the \(d/u\) ratio therefore remains consistent with nonperturbative QCD predictions (\(d/u \to 0\) as \(x \to 1\)) rather than with perturbative QCD-inspired models that predict a finite ratio.

## Method

The author reviews the relativistic impulse approximation for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) from deuterium, using the formalism of Melnitchouk, Schreiber, and Thomas (MST) which separates the deuteron structure function into a convolution of the nucleon momentum distribution (smearing function) with the free nucleon structure function, plus an off-shell correction. A new global NLO parton distribution fit (CTEQ6X) is performed with relaxed kinematic cuts (\(Q^2 > 1.69\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W^2 > 3\) GeV\(^2\)), incorporating target mass corrections (TMCs) and phenomenological higher twist (HT) terms, and applying the weak binding approximation (WBA) smearing model with finite-\(Q^2\) kinematics to account for nuclear effects in deuterium.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is \(\varepsilon_d = -2.2\) MeV, approximately 0.1% of its mass, and the typical nucleon momentum in the deuteron is \(|\bm{p}| \sim 130\) MeV, leading to an average nucleon virtuality \(p^2\) about 4% smaller than the free nucleon mass.
- The off-shell correction \(\delta^{\rm (off)} F_2^d\) reduces \(F_2^d\) by approximately 1–2% compared to the on-shell approximation.
- The overall nuclear effect on the ratio \(F_2^d/F_2^N\) is a 2–3% depletion at intermediate \(x\) (\(x \sim 0.5\)), with a steep rise at larger \(x\) (\(x \gtrsim 0.6\)–0.7) due to Fermi motion, shown at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\).
- In the CTEQ6X fit, the \(d\) quark distribution is suppressed by up to 40% for \(x \approx 0.8\) relative to previous fits with no nuclear corrections.
- The CTEQ6X analysis relaxed DIS kinematic cuts from \(Q^2 < 4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W^2 < 12.25\) GeV\(^2\) to \(Q^2 > 1.69\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W^2 > 3\) GeV\(^2\), approximately doubling the number of DIS data points.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on nuclear corrections in deuterium pioneered by Melnitchouk, Schreiber, and Thomas (1994) and by Kulagin, Piller, and collaborators, and directly updates the earlier CTEQ6.1 parton distributions which neglected nuclear effects. The CTEQ6X analysis demonstrates that incorporating deuterium smearing is essential for extracting the neutron structure function at large \(x\), and the results contradict the predictions of perturbative QCD-inspired models that expect a finite \(d/u\) ratio as \(x \to 1\). The findings enable future experimental programs at Jefferson Lab with 12 GeV, including the use of mirror nuclei \(^3\)He/\(^3\)H to cancel nuclear effects up to \(x \approx 0.85\), spectator-tagged DIS on deuterium (BONUS experiment), and parity-violating electron DIS on hydrogen to directly probe the \(d/u\) ratio via weak interactions.
