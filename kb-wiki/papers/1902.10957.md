# Nature of the $Y(4260)$: A light-quark perspective

> The $Y(4260)$ has been one of the most puzzling pieces among the so-called $XYZ$ states. In this paper, we try to gain insights into the structure of the $Y(4260)$ from the light-quark perspective. We study the dipion invariant mass spectrum of the $e^+ e^- \to Y(4260) \to J/ψπ^+π^-$ process and the ratio of the cross sections ${σ(e^+e^- \to J/ψK^+ K^-)}/{σ(e^+e^- \to J/ψπ^+π^-)}$. In particular, we consider the effects of different light-quark SU(3) eigenstates inside the $Y(4260)$. The strong pion-pion final-state interactions as well as the $K\bar{K}$ coupled channel in the $S$-wave are taken into account in a model-independent way using dispersion theory. We find that the SU(3) octet state plays a significant role in these transitions, implying that the $Y(4260)$ contains a large light-quark component. Our findings suggest that the $Y(4260)$ is neither a hybrid nor a conventional charmonium state, and they are consistent with the $Y(4260)$ having a sizeable $\bar D D_1$ component which, however, is not completely dominant.

## Key claim

The $Y(4260)$ contains a large light-quark component, specifically an SU(3) octet component, which implies it is neither a hybrid nor a conventional charmonium state; however, the authors hedge that their findings are "consistent with" a sizeable $\bar D D_1$ component that is "not completely dominant," leaving open the possibility of a mixed interpretation rather than a pure molecular state.

## Method

The authors analyzed the dipion invariant mass spectrum of $e^+ e^- \to Y(4260) \to J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-$ and the ratio $\sigma(e^+e^- \to J/\psi K^+ K^-)/\sigma(e^+e^- \to J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-)$ using a dispersive approach with a modified Omnes representation. They decomposed the $Y(4260)$ into SU(3) flavor singlet and octet light-quark components, constructed chiral Lagrangians for contact terms, and included $Z_c(3900)$-exchange and triangle diagrams ($Y(4260) \to \bar D D_1 \to \bar D D^\ast \pi \to J/\psi \pi\pi$) as left-hand-cut contributions. The strong coupled-channel $\pi\pi/K\bar K$ final-state interactions in the $S$-wave were treated model-independently using two different parametrizations (Dai-Pennington and Bern/Orsay), while $D$-wave FSI were treated with single-channel elastic rescattering.

## Key numbers

- The $Y(4260)$ mass and width were fixed to $4222\ \text{MeV}$ and $44.1\ \text{MeV}$, respectively, taken from the central values of the BESIII fit in Ablikim (2016)
- The dipion invariant mass $m_{\pi\pi}$ reaches above $1.1\ \text{GeV}$, allowing access to the $K\bar K$ threshold region
- The ratio of cross sections was measured at two energy points: $E=4.23\ \text{GeV}$ and $E=4.26\ \text{GeV}$ by BESIII
- The pion decay constant was taken as $F = 92.1\ \text{MeV}$
- The dispersive integrals used three subtractions to ensure convergence, with the asymptotic cutoff at $\sqrt{s_0}=1.3\ \text{GeV}$ below the onset of $4\pi$ inelasticities

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the $Y(4260)$ beyond the charmonium and hybrid interpretations proposed by Zhu (2005) and Close (2005), and beyond the pure $\bar D D_1$ molecular model of Ding (2008) and Cleven (2013). By demonstrating that an SU(3) octet light-quark component is necessary to reproduce the BESIII data, the authors provide evidence against the $Y(4260)$ being a pure conventional $c\bar c$ state or a pure hybrid. The analysis enables future studies to quantify the relative weights of the $\bar D D_1$ molecular component versus the light-quark component, and motivates further experimental measurements of the $J/\psi K^+K^-$ channel to constrain the strange-quark content.
