# Physics-Informed Neural Network for Solving the Diffusion Equation in the Expanding QCD Medium

> We employ Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) to solve the diffusion of heavy quarks within the expanding hot QCD medium generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Due to the strong coupling between heavy quarks and the bulk medium, the evolution of heavy quarks can be effectively characterized by a diffusion equation. This approach assumes the instantaneous kinetic thermalization of heavy quarks following their production in nuclear collisions. The local density of heavy quarks is intrinsically coupled to the velocity profile of the hot QCD medium. By incorporating the fluid velocity profiles provided by a hydrodynamic model directly into the diffusion equation, we utilize the deep neural network (DNN) to efficiently determine the heavy-quark evolution. Furthermore, this work provides a valuable reference for the application of deep learning techniques to the treatment of non-thermalized heavy-quark dynamics. The rapid calculation of heavy-quark diffusion using DNNs further facilitates the study of heavy-quark coalescence within a large ensemble of fluctuating hot media.

# Physics-Informed Neural Network for Solving the Diffusion Equation in the Expanding QCD Medium

## Key claim
Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) can solve the charm-quark diffusion equation in an expanding QCD medium with accuracy comparable to a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method, but only under the assumption of instantaneous kinetic thermalization of heavy quarks following their production in nuclear collisions. The approach enables rapid calculation of charm-quark densities in event-by-event fluctuating media, though the paper does not demonstrate speedup over traditional methods for a single event.

## Method
The authors use the MUSIC hydrodynamic package to simulate the temperature and velocity profiles of the QGP in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with impact parameter b = 6 fm. They then embed these fluid velocity fields directly into a diffusion equation for charm-quark density, assuming full kinetic thermalization. A deep neural network with four hidden layers of 256 neurons each and Tanh activation functions is trained to minimize a composite loss function consisting of the PDE residual, initial condition loss, and boundary condition loss, using a hybrid Adam/L-BFGS optimization strategy with temporal domain decomposition.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions
- Impact parameter: b = 6.0 fm
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Training time intervals: [0, 3], [0, 6], and [0, 10] fm/c
- Total training epochs: 20,000 (14,000 Adam + L-BFGS fine-tuning)
- Network architecture: 4 hidden layers, 256 neurons each
- Total loss function converged to below 10^-3 after 20,000 epochs
- Boundary domain: rectangular transverse plane with vertices at (+/- 20, +/- 20) fm

## Context
This work extends the PINN framework introduced by Raissi et al. (2019) to the specific problem of heavy-quark diffusion in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on hydrodynamic modeling of the QGP using the MUSIC package (Schenke et al., 2010) and prior work on charm-quark diffusion equations (Zhao et al., 2017). The approach enables future study of charmonium regeneration via coalescence in event-by-event fluctuating media, and the authors state their intention to extend the framework to solve the Fokker-Planck equation for simultaneous spatial and momentum-space diffusion.
