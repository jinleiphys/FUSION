# Strong isospin breaking with twisted mass lattice QCD

> In this work we propose a method for including strong isospin breaking in twisted mass lattice calculations, while preserving flavor identification. We utilize a partially quenched construction in which the sea quarks are given by the standard twisted mass lattice action while the valence quarks have an additional strong isospin breaking mass term. This construction allows for a practical use with existing twisted-mass gauge ensembles. Additionally, we construct the relevant partially quenched twisted mass chiral perturbation theory for both mesons and baryons to O(m_q^2, m_q a, a^2). We provide explicit expressions for the pion, nucleon and delta masses, as well as the corresponding mass splittings, and discuss the resulting errors from including the strong isospin breaking in the valence sector only. Finally, we demonstrate how the application of this idea can be used, with mild approximations, to determine the values of both the up and down quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to include strong isospin breaking (m_u ≠ m_d) in twisted mass lattice QCD calculations while preserving flavor identification, using a partially quenched construction where only valence quarks carry the isospin-breaking mass term. The method is presented as practical for use with existing twisted-mass gauge ensembles, but the author notes that including strong isospin breaking only in the valence sector introduces systematic errors that must be quantified.

## Method

The author constructs a partially quenched twisted mass lattice action where sea quarks use the standard twisted mass action with degenerate masses, while valence quarks receive an additional strong isospin breaking mass term proportional to τ₃^v. The corresponding partially quenched twisted mass chiral perturbation theory (χPT) is developed for both mesons and baryons through O(m_q², m_q a, a²), including vacuum alignment and hairpin interaction analysis. Explicit expressions for pion, nucleon, and delta masses and their splittings are derived, with matching relations provided between the partially quenched and unquenched chiral Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

- The chiral Lagrangian is constructed through O(m_q², m_q a, a²) for mesons and through next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) for baryons
- The pion mass splitting between charged and neutral pions is given by m_π⁰² = m_π±² + (4δ̂²/f²)l₇ - (64W′â² sin²(ω))/f², where δ̂ is the isospin breaking parameter and l₇ is a Gasser-Leutwyler coefficient
- The nucleon mass splitting (m_n - m_p) receives no NLO corrections; the NLO contributions exactly cancel, leaving the expansion of the mass splitting on the same footing as the pion mass expansion
- The delta mass splittings also have exact cancellation of NLO contributions, with coefficients c_Δ = (-3, -1, 1, 3) for Δ⁺⁺, Δ⁺, Δ⁰, Δ⁻ respectively
- The partially quenched hairpin contribution to the pion mass yields a constant term Δ_PQ⁴/(2(4πf)²) rather than an enhanced chiral logarithm, because the valence and sea quark masses are equal

## Context

This work extends the twisted mass lattice QCD framework developed by Frezzotti et al. (1999-2000) and the twisted mass χPT for mesons by Münster (2003), Sharpe (2004), and for baryons by Walker-Loud (2005). It addresses the problem of non-perturbative flavor mixing identified in earlier work (Walker-Loud 2005) when including strong isospin breaking in twisted mass actions. The method enables lattice determinations of up and down quark masses as advocated by Wilczek (2002) and computations of isospin violations in the hadron spectrum, similar to the partially quenched approach used by Beane (2006) for the neutron-proton mass splitting. The construction also connects to Osterwalder-Seiler valence quarks (Osterwalder 1977) with non-degenerate masses.
