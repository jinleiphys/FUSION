# Time-Evolution of the Coherent and the Squeezed States of Many-Body Systems Based on the Basic Idea of the Boson Mapping and the TDHF Method

> Various works performed by the present authors in the 1990s are reviewed. The topics discussed in this paper are mainly related to the time-evolution of the coherent and the squeezed states of the systems obeying the su(2)- and the su(1,1)-algebra. The formulations are based on the basic idea of the boson mapping and the TDHF theory in canonical form. Under the time-dependent variational procedure for trial states appropriately chosen, the time-evolution of the systems under investigation is described. Further, it is shown that this method enables us to obtain the classical counterparts of the original quantal systems.

## Key claim

This paper reviews a body of work by the authors from the 1990s, showing that the time-dependent variational method, when combined with the boson mapping technique and the canonicity condition from TDHF theory, can describe the time-evolution of coherent and squeezed states for systems obeying the su(2)- and su(1,1)-algebra, and that this procedure yields classical counterparts of the original quantum systems under appropriate choices of trial states.

## Method

The authors employ the Schwinger boson representation for the su(2)- and su(1,1)-algebra, then derive the Holstein-Primakoff boson representation using the MYT boson mapping method (Marumori, Yamamura, Tokunaga, 1964). They construct coherent states for each algebra and impose the canonicity condition (Marumori, Maskawa, Sakata, Kuriyama, 1980) on the trial state parameters, which allows the parameters to be treated as canonical variables in classical mechanics. The time-dependent variational principle then yields Hamilton's equations of motion for these variables, with the Hamiltonian given by the expectation value of the quantum Hamiltonian in the trial state.

## Key numbers

- For the su(2)-algebra coherent state, the expectation value of the spin magnitude operator is \( \langle \gamma | \hat{S} | \gamma \rangle = |w|^2 \), where \( w \) is a complex parameter.
- For the su(2)-algebra, the classical counterpart of the spin operators are given by \( (S_+)_c = \sqrt{\hbar} \mathbf{X}^* \sqrt{2\mathbf{S} - \hbar \mathbf{X}^* \mathbf{X}} \), \( (S_-)_c = \sqrt{2\mathbf{S} - \hbar \mathbf{X}^* \mathbf{X}} \sqrt{\hbar} \mathbf{X} \), and \( (S_0)_c = \hbar \mathbf{X}^* \mathbf{X} - \mathbf{S} \), where \( \mathbf{S} \) and \( \mathbf{X} \) are canonical variables.
- For the su(1,1)-algebra coherent state, the expectation value of the magnitude operator is \( \langle c | \hat{T} | c \rangle = |W|^2 + \hbar/2 \), where \( W \) is a complex parameter.
- For the su(1,1)-algebra, the Holstein-Primakoff representation gives \( \widetilde{T}_+ = \sqrt{\hbar} \hat{X}^* \sqrt{2T + \hbar \hat{X}^* \hat{X}} \), \( \widetilde{T}_- = \sqrt{2T + \hbar \hat{X}^* \hat{X}} \sqrt{\hbar} \hat{X} \), and \( \widetilde{T}_0 = \hbar \hat{X}^* \hat{X} + T \).

## Context

This review extends the authors' earlier work on the canonical form of TDHF theory (Marumori, Maskawa, Sakata, Kuriyama, 1980) and their previous investigations of many-fermion systems using Grassmann variables (Kuriyama and Yamamura, 1987). The paper builds on the boson expansion theories of Belyaev and Zelevinsky (1962) and Marumori, Yamamura, and Tokunaga (1964), as well as the work of Marshalek and Holzwarth on the time-dependent Hartree-Bogoliubov theory. The results enable the classical description of quantum systems obeying su(2)- and su(1,1)-algebra, with applications to damped and amplified oscillators and thermal effects via the phase space doubling of thermo field dynamics (Takahashi and Umezawa).
