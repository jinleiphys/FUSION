# Elastic Differential Cross Sections for Space Radiation Applications

> The eikonal, partial wave (PW) Lippmann-Schwinger, and three-dimensional Lippmann- Schwinger (LS3D) methods are compared for nuclear reactions that are relevant for space radiation applications. Numerical convergence of the eikonal method is readily achieved when exact formulas of the optical potential are used for light nuclei (A $\le$ 16), and the momentum-space representation of the optical potential is used for heavier nuclei. The PW solution method is known to be numerically unstable for systems that require a large number of partial waves, and, as a result, the LS3D method is employed. The effect of relativistic kinematics is studied with the PW and LS3D methods and is compared to eikonal results. It is recommended that the LS3D method be used for high energy nucleon-nucleus reactions and nucleus-nucleus reactions at all energies because of its rapid numerical convergence and stability.

## Key claim

The paper recommends that the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger (LS3D) method be used for high energy nucleon-nucleus reactions and nucleus-nucleus reactions at all energies because of its rapid numerical convergence and stability, but this recommendation is conditional on the method's demonstrated agreement with partial wave (PW) and eikonal methods only in appropriate energy regimes, and the paper notes that extensive comparisons to experimental data will be communicated in subsequent manuscripts.

## Method

The authors compared three solution methods for the Lippmann-Schwinger equation: the eikonal approximation (using exact formulas of the optical potential for light nuclei with A ≤ 16 and momentum-space representation for heavier nuclei), the partial wave (PW) decomposition method, and the full three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger (LS3D) method. All methods used the same parameterizations for nuclear matter densities (harmonic well for light nuclei, Wood-Saxon for heavier nuclei) and the nucleon-nucleon transition amplitude, with the optical potential computed in the first-order single scattering approximation. The methods were applied to nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering at lab projectile kinetic energies from 150 MeV/n to 20 GeV/n, with both non-relativistic and relativistic kinematics.

## Key numbers

- p + ¹⁶O, p + ⁵⁶Fe, ⁴He + ¹⁶O, and ¹²C + ⁵⁶Fe reactions were computed at lab projectile kinetic energies of 150, 500, 1000, and 20,000 MeV/n
- The LS3D method used a maximum of 44 Gaussian quadrature points such that total elastic cross sections changed less than 1% for all reactions with energies up to 100 GeV/n
- The eikonal method used a maximum of 100 Gaussian quadrature points such that total elastic cross sections changed less than 1% for all reactions with energies up to 100 GeV/n
- The PW method used a convergence tolerance of |T_l - U_l| ≤ 10⁻⁴ % for the partial wave summation
- Experimental comparisons included p + ³²S at T_Lab = 1 GeV, p + ⁴⁰Ca at T_Lab = 500 MeV, p + ⁵⁸Ni at T_Lab = 1 GeV, and ⁴He + ⁴⁰Ca at T_Lab = 347 MeV/n

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger method, which had previously been applied mainly to nucleon-nucleon interactions (LS3D_1, LS3D_2, LS3D_4) and to low-energy nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions by Rodriguez-Gallardo et al. (LS3D_3). The paper demonstrates that the LS3D method can be applied to high-energy reactions relevant to space radiation transport codes such as NASA's HZETRN, where the PW method becomes numerically unstable due to the large number of partial waves required. The results enable future work on equal-mass kinematics effects and extensive experimental data comparisons, which the authors state will be communicated in subsequent manuscripts.
