# Probing the transversity spin structure of a nucleon in neutrino-production of a charmed meson

## Key claim

Including $O(m_c)$ terms in the coefficient functions and/or $O(m_D)$ twist-3 contributions in the heavy meson distribution amplitudes leads to a non-zero transverse amplitude for exclusive neutrino production of a $D$ pseudoscalar charmed meson on an unpolarized target, working in the collinear QCD framework where chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions.

## Method

The authors compute the hard scattering amplitude for the exclusive reaction $\nu_l N \to l^- D^+ N'$ in the generalized Bjorken regime, using collinear QCD factorization. The amplitude is expressed in terms of nucleon transversity GPDs and the $D$-meson distribution amplitude, with the hard subprocess $W^+ d \to D^+ d$ evaluated including $O(m_c)$ terms from the heavy quark propagator and $O(m_D)$ twist-3 contributions from the meson distribution amplitude. The azimuthal dependence of the cross section is derived to separate the transverse amplitude contributions from chiral-even and chiral-odd GPDs.

## Key numbers

- The transverse amplitude prefactor contains $(m_c - 2M_D)$ where $m_c$ is the charmed quark mass and $M_D$ is the $D$-meson mass, showing the two sources of the transverse amplitude.
- The differential cross section $\sigma_{--}$ is proportional to $\frac{4\xi^2 C^2 (m_c+2M_D)^2}{(Q^2+M_D^2)^2}$, with $C = \frac{2\pi}{3}C_F \alpha_s V_{dc}$.
- The modified Bjorken variable is defined as $x_B^D \equiv \frac{Q^2+M_D^2}{2p_1.q}$, with skewness parameters $\xi \approx \frac{x_B^D}{2-x_B^D}$ and $\xi' \approx \frac{x_B}{2-x_B^D}$.
- Numerical estimates are shown for $s = 20$ GeV$^2$, $y = 0.7$, and $t = t_0$ as a function of $Q^2$, with cross sections of the same order of magnitude as those for neutrino production of $\pi$ or $D_s$ mesons estimated in earlier work.

## Context

This work extends the collinear QCD factorization framework for exclusive reactions, previously applied to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DGPR) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS), to the weak interaction sector using neutrino beams. The flavor-changing character of the electroweak current allows charmed quark production from light quark partonic distributions, building on earlier work on weak GPDs (weakGPD) and transversity GPD definitions (transGPDdef, transGPDno, transGPDacc). The results enable experimental access to transversity GPDs through azimuthal angular observables in neutrino-production at planned high-energy neutrino facilities such as NOvA, potentially without requiring additional dedicated equipment.
