# Baryon anticorrelations and the Pauli principle in PYTHIA

> We present a computational investigation of a problem of hadron collisions from recent years, that of baryon anticorrelations. This is an experimental dearth of baryons near other baryons in phase space, not seen upon examining numerical Monte Carlo simulations. We have addressed one of the best known Monte Carlo codes, PYTHIA, to see what baryon (anti)correlations it produces, where they are originated at the string-fragmentation level in the underlying Lund model, and what simple modifications could lead to better agreement with data. We propose two ad-hoc alterations of the fragmentation code, a "one-baryon" and an "always-baryon" policies that qualitatively reproduce the data behaviour, i.e anticorrelation, and suggest that lacking Pauli-principle induced corrections at the quark level could be the culprit behind the current disagreement between computations and experiment.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the experimentally observed baryon anticorrelation (a deficit of baryons near other baryons in phase space) can be qualitatively reproduced by ad-hoc modifications to the PYTHIA event generator's string fragmentation algorithm, and suggest that the absence of Pauli-principle-induced corrections at the quark level is the likely cause of the current disagreement between simulations and data, though the modifications are not derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors analyzed baryon and antibaryon correlations produced by the PYTHIA Monte Carlo event generator (using the Monash tune) at the level of individual string fragmentation, distinguishing between meson strings (baryon number 0) and baryon strings (baryon number 1). They implemented two ad-hoc modifications to the fragmentation code: a "one-baryon" policy that rejects any string fragmentation producing more than one baryon (or antibaryon), and an "always-baryon" policy, both applied via a Von Neumann rejection step during the Lund string fragmentation loop.

## Key numbers

- The forward peak in baryon-baryon correlations at Δφ ≈ 0, present in PYTHIA simulations, is identified as the feature that must be suppressed to match experimental data; the peak is already present at the level of individual string fragmentation.
- String-string correlations show a peak at Δφ = π for baryon-baryon strings, with twin peaks at large relative pseudorapidity of order 10 or more, which are outside the ALICE detector acceptance.
- The afterburners correction method, which applies a weight based on the square modulus of a wavefunction involving Coulomb and nuclear final-state interactions, was tested but did not fully suppress the Δφ = 0 ridge in proton-proton correlations.

## Context

This work addresses the baryon anticorrelation problem first exposed by the ALICE collaboration (Graczykowski 2014, ALICE 2016) and corroborated by the STAR collaboration (STAR 2019), where experimental data show a negative correlation for baryon pairs at small phase-space separation that Monte Carlo simulations fail to reproduce. The authors show that the afterburners method proposed by Graczykowski (2021) does not fully correct the issue, and instead propose modifications at the string fragmentation level, suggesting that the Pauli principle at the quark level (not implemented in PYTHIA 8.3) is the missing physical ingredient. This work enables future implementation of Fermi-Dirac statistics in event generators and provides a testbed for understanding baryon production mechanisms in high-energy collisions.
