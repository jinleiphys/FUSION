# Production of Resonances in Partial Chemical Equilibrium

> Within the model of partial chemical equilibrium (PCE) we calculate the multiplicity ratios of selected unstable resonances to given stable species. We focus on those ratios that have been measured either in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC or in Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy. The model provides an interpretation how in an expanding hadronic fireball with decreasing temperature the final numbers of stable hadrons after decays of all resonances remain unchanged. Each stable species acquires its own chemical potential and the resonances are kept in equilibrium with them. Multiplicities of unstable resonances provide a test of this scenario. ## Key claim

The paper tests whether the Partial Chemical Equilibrium (PCE) model can reproduce measured resonance-to-stable-hadron ratios in heavy-ion collisions; it finds that while the model works for \(K^{*}/K\) and \(\rho^{0}/\pi\) ratios, it fails for \(\phi\)-meson and hyperon resonances, indicating that those species do not equilibrate with their decay products in the expanding hadronic fireball.

## Method

The authors apply the PCE model, which assumes that after chemical freeze-out the effective numbers of stable hadrons remain constant as the system cools, while resonances stay in equilibrium with their decay products via chemical potentials derived from the stable species. They solve evolution equations for number densities under isentropic expansion, starting from equilibrium chemical potentials at the chemical freeze-out temperature and evolving to lower temperatures. The resulting resonance yields are compared to experimental data from Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy, with temperatures extracted by projecting measured ratios onto theoretical curves.

## Key numbers

- The chemical freeze-out temperature for nuclear collisions at LHC and top RHIC energies is above 150 MeV (from prior work cited in the paper).
- Kinetic freeze-out temperatures from single-particle \(p_t\) spectra range from about 150 MeV down to as low as 80 MeV (from prior work cited in the paper).
- For \(\rho^{0}/\pi\) ratios from Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, more central collisions indicate lower freeze-out temperatures, consistent with a larger system that stays together longer.
- For \(K^{*0}/K^{-}\) ratios, temperatures extracted from the PCE model coincide with temperatures obtained from blast-wave fits to single-particle \(p_t\) spectra.
- For \(\phi/K^{-}\) ratios, most measured values lie above the PCE model predictions, except for the most central collisions at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends the PCE model originally introduced by Bebie (1991) to interpret resonance production in heavy-ion collisions, building on the Statistical Hadron Resonance Gas Model of Andronic (2017, 2016) which successfully reproduces stable hadron abundances. The results contradict the assumption that all resonances remain in equilibrium with their decay products throughout the hadronic phase, as the \(\phi\)-meson and hyperon resonances are overproduced relative to the model. The findings enable future refinements of the PCE model by including interactions via scattering phase shifts and revisiting the assumption of isentropic expansion.
