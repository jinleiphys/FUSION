# Comparison of optical potential for nucleons and $Δ$ resonances

> Precise modeling of neutrino interactions on nuclear targets is essential for neutrino oscillations experiments. The modeling of the energy of final state particles in quasielastic (QE) scattering and resonance production on bound nucleons requires knowledge of both the removal energy of the initial state bound nucleon as well as the average Coulomb and nuclear optical potentials for final state leptons and hadrons. We extract the average values of the real part of the nuclear optical potential for final state nucleons ($U_{opt}^{QE}$) as a function of the nucleon kinetic energy from inclusive electron scattering data on nuclear targets ($\bf_{6}^{12}C$+$\bf_{8}^{16}O$, $\bf_{20}^{40}Ca$+$\bf_{18}^{40}Ar$, $\bf_{3}^{6}Li$, $\bf_{18}^{27}Al$, $\bf_{26}^{56}Fe$, $\bf_{82}^{208}Pb$) in the QE region and compare to calculations. We also extract values of the average of the real part of the nuclear optical potential for a $Δ(1232)$ resonance in the final state ($U^Δ_{opt}$) within the impulse approximation. We find that $U^Δ_{opt}$ is more negative than $U_{opt}^{QE}$ with $U^Δ_{opt}\approx$1.5~$U_{opt}^{QE}$ for $\bf_{6}^{12}C$.

## Key claim

The average real part of the nuclear optical potential for a final-state Δ(1232) resonance is more negative than that for final-state nucleons in quasielastic scattering, with the ratio approximately U^Δ_opt ≈ 1.5 × U^QE_opt for carbon-12, but this result is extracted within the impulse approximation and relies on modeling the QE peak with an effective spectral function and smearing free-nucleon structure functions with a relativistic Fermi gas.

## Method

The authors extracted the average real part of the nuclear optical potential for final-state nucleons (U^QE_opt) by fitting a relativistic Fermi gas model to inclusive electron-nucleus quasielastic differential cross sections on targets including ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁰Ar, ⁶Li, ²⁷Al, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb, fitting only the top third of the QE distribution. For the Δ(1232) resonance, they extracted U^Δ_opt by fitting a sum of the QE contribution (modeled with an effective spectral function including 2p2h) and Fermi-smeared Bosted-Christy fits to free-nucleon inelastic structure functions to a subset of the same data where the Δ peak was clearly identifiable, varying the normalization of QE and resonance cross sections and U^Δ_opt as free parameters.

## Key numbers

* For ¹²C+¹⁶O, the intercept of U^QE_opt at p_f3²=0 is -0.029±0.004 GeV and the slope versus p_f3² is 0.040±0.010 GeV/GeV²; for U^Δ_opt on ¹²C, the intercept is -0.049±0.001 GeV and the slope is 0.059±0.001 GeV/GeV².
* For ⁵⁶Fe, the intercept of U^QE_opt at p_f3²=0 is -0.033±0.001 GeV and the slope versus p_f3² is 0.055±0.003 GeV/GeV²; for U^Δ_opt on ⁵⁶Fe, the intercept is -0.083±0.005 GeV and the slope is 0.135±0.008 GeV/GeV².
* For ²⁰⁸Pb, the intercept of U^QE_opt at p_f3²=0 is -0.041±0.002 GeV and the slope versus p_f3² is 0.110±0.011 GeV/GeV²; no U^Δ_opt values were extracted for ²⁰⁸Pb due to large Fermi smearing.
* For ¹²C, U^Δ_opt ≈ 1.5 × U^QE_opt.
* The average Coulomb potential |V_eff| used for ¹²C is 3.1 MeV; for ²⁰⁸Pb it is 18.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous extraction of removal energies and average nuclear optical potentials for final-state nucleons from inclusive QE electron scattering data (Bodek and Cai, 2019) by adding a comparison to calculations using the optical potential formalisms of Cooper, Hama, Clark and Mercer (1993, 2009) and by extracting the analogous potential for Δ(1232) resonances. The results provide input parameters for neutrino event generators such as GENIE and GiBUU, which currently do not include the real part of the nuclear optical potential for final-state hadrons; the extracted U^Δ_opt values enable improved modeling of resonance production in neutrino-nucleus scattering for experiments like MINERvA, T2K, and DUNE.
