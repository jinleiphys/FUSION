# Comparison of optical potential for nucleons and $őĒ$ resonances

> Precise modeling of neutrino interactions on nuclear targets is essential for neutrino oscillations experiments. The modeling of the energy of final state particles in quasielastic (QE) scattering and resonance production on bound nucleons requires knowledge of both the removal energy of the initial state bound nucleon as well as the average Coulomb and nuclear optical potentials for final state leptons and hadrons. We extract the average values of the real part of the nuclear optical potential for final state nucleons ($U_{opt}^{QE}$) as a function of the nucleon kinetic energy from inclusive electron scattering data on nuclear targets ($\bf_{6}^{12}C$+$\bf_{8}^{16}O$, $\bf_{20}^{40}Ca$+$\bf_{18}^{40}Ar$, $\bf_{3}^{6}Li$, $\bf_{18}^{27}Al$, $\bf_{26}^{56}Fe$, $\bf_{82}^{208}Pb$) in the QE region and compare to calculations. We also extract values of the average of the real part of the nuclear optical potential for a $őĒ(1232)$ resonance in the final state ($U^őĒ_{opt}$) within the impulse approximation. We find that $U^őĒ_{opt}$ is more negative than $U_{opt}^{QE}$ with $U^őĒ_{opt}\approx$1.5~$U_{opt}^{QE}$ for $\bf_{6}^{12}C$.

## Key claim

The average real part of the nuclear optical potential for a final-state őĒ(1232) resonance is more negative than that for final-state nucleons in quasielastic scattering, with the ratio approximately U^őĒ_opt ‚Čą 1.5 √ó U^QE_opt for carbon-12, but this result is extracted within the impulse approximation and relies on modeling the QE peak with an effective spectral function and smearing free-nucleon structure functions with a relativistic Fermi gas.

## Method

The authors extracted the average real part of the nuclear optical potential for final-state nucleons (U^QE_opt) by fitting a relativistic Fermi gas model to inclusive electron-nucleus quasielastic differential cross sections on targets including ¬Ļ¬≤C, ¬Ļ‚Ā∂O, ‚Āī‚ĀįCa, ‚Āī‚ĀįAr, ‚Ā∂Li, ¬≤‚Ā∑Al, ‚ĀĶ‚Ā∂Fe, and ¬≤‚Āį‚ĀłPb, fitting only the top third of the QE distribution. For the őĒ(1232) resonance, they extracted U^őĒ_opt by fitting a sum of the QE contribution (modeled with an effective spectral function including 2p2h) and Fermi-smeared Bosted-Christy fits to free-nucleon inelastic structure functions to a subset of the same data where the őĒ peak was clearly identifiable, varying the normalization of QE and resonance cross sections and U^őĒ_opt as free parameters.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous extraction of removal energies and average nuclear optical potentials for final-state nucleons from inclusive QE electron scattering data (Bodek and Cai, 2019) by adding a comparison to calculations using the optical potential formalisms of Cooper, Hama, Clark and Mercer (1993, 2009) and by extracting the analogous potential for őĒ(1232) resonances. The results provide input parameters for neutrino event generators such as GENIE and GiBUU, which currently do not include the real part of the nuclear optical potential for final-state hadrons; the extracted U^őĒ_opt values enable improved modeling of resonance production in neutrino-nucleus scattering for experiments like MINERvA, T2K, and DUNE.
