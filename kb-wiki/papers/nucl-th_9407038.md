# A gauge invariant unitary theory for pion photoproduction

> The Ward-Takahashi identities are central to the gauge invariance of the photoproduction amplitude. Here we demonstrate that unitarity and in particular the inclusion of both the $πN$ and $γπN$ thresholds on equal footing yields a photoproduction amplitude that satisfies both two-body unitarity and the generalized Ward-Takahashi identities. The final amplitude is a solution of a set of coupled channel integral equations for the reactions $πN\rightarrowπN$ and $γN\rightarrowπN$. % Figures available from the authors upon request, % e-mail: phchv@pippin.cc.flinders.edu.au % Submitted to Physical Review C

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a pion photoproduction amplitude which satisfies both two-body unitarity and the generalized Ward-Takahashi identities can be obtained by including the πN and γπN thresholds on equal footing, provided the amplitude is derived from a set of coupled channel integral equations for the reactions πN → πN and γN → πN.

## Method

The authors employ Ohta's minimal electromagnetic coupling procedure to gauge a Lagrangian with form factors representing the internal quark-gluon structure of hadrons, generating gauge-invariant currents for the nucleon, pion, and πNN vertex. They then derive coupled integral equations for πN elastic scattering and pion photoproduction using Taylor's last-cut lemma to expose the πN and γπN unitary cuts, classifying Feynman diagrams according to their irreducibility. The resulting photoproduction amplitude is shown to be identical to that obtained from applying the Ward-Takahashi identities to the one-pion-irreducible πNN three-point function, thereby establishing that the coupled channel solution satisfies both unitarity and gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon propagator is required to have a simple pole at the nucleon mass m with unit residue, imposing the conditions r(m²) = h(m²) and r(m²) + 2m²{r'(m²) − h'(m²)} = 1, where r and h are form factors in the Lagrangian.
- The pion propagator is required to have a pole at q² = μ² with unit residue, where μ is the pion mass.
- The electromagnetic coupling constant e appears in the charge operator Q = e(1+τ₃)/2 for the nucleon.

## Context

This work extends earlier approaches to pion photoproduction that imposed unitarity via the Watson Theorem (1954) by using on-shell πN phase shifts independently of the Born amplitude, as well as more recent extensions by Naus and Koch (1990) and Lee and Pearce (1991) that employed off-shell πN amplitudes from separable potentials or chiral Lagrangians. The present formulation achieves consistency between the πN dynamics and the electromagnetic currents by requiring that the πN dynamics determine the charge and current distribution, rather than treating the Born amplitude and final-state distortions independently. This enables future calculations that can incorporate quark-gluon degrees of freedom from models such as the Cloudy Bag Model (Thomas, 1980; Theberge, 1984) or the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian, while maintaining both unitarity and gauge invariance.
