# Cooling of Color Superconducting Compact Stars

> We review the status of research on the cooling of compact stars, with emphasis on the influence of color superconducting quark matter phases. Although a consistent microscopic approach is not yet available, severe constraints on the phase structure of matter at high densities come from recent mass and cooling observations of compact stars.

## Key claim

Observations of cooling compact stars can provide severe constraints on the phase structure of dense QCD matter, even though a consistent microscopic approach to color superconducting quark matter phases is not yet available.

## Method

The paper reviews the interplay between hadronic equations of state (EoS) of the relativistic mean-field type and hybrid EoS incorporating a deconfinement phase transition to quark matter within Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type models. Cooling curves for hybrid star configurations are computed and compared to observational data on surface temperature versus age, using the direct Urca process threshold and mass constraints from pulsar observations to test admissible phase patterns of color superconductivity.

## Key numbers

- Pulsar PSR J0751+1807 mass determined to \(2.1 \pm 0.2 M_\odot\).
- Innermost stable circular orbit frequency measurement in 4U 1636-536 corresponds to a mass of \(1.9-2.1 M_\odot\).
- Maximum masses for stiff EoS are above \(2.3 M_\odot\) with radii \(R\sim 12-13\) km.
- The direct Urca process is initiated at a proton fraction of \(\sim 14\%\) (including muons).
- Typical neutron star mass range from population synthesis is \(1.0 M_\odot \le M_{\rm typ} \le 1.5 M_\odot\).
- For the 2SC+X pairing pattern, the X-gap ansatz uses \(\mu_c = 330\) MeV, \(\alpha = 25\), and \(\Delta_c = 5\) MeV.
- Stable hybrid star configurations with masses even below \(1.3 M_\odot\) have been obtained for the 2SC phase.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on compact star cooling (Blaschke 2004, Grigorian 2005) by incorporating modern mass constraints from PSR J0751+1807 (Nice 2005) and RXTE data (Barret 2005). It shows that purely hadronic EoS from a set of relativistic mean-field models (Klahn 2006) cannot simultaneously satisfy all observational constraints, motivating hybrid EoS with a deconfinement transition to quark matter. The results enable future microscopic calculations of pairing gaps and transport properties for color-spin-locking and other spin-1 phases, and call for improved approaches using Dyson-Schwinger equations and Polyakov loop augmented models to connect to lattice QCD.
