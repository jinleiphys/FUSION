# Observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering with two-photon exchange

> The general form of the cross section as well as the polarizations of electron-deuteron elastic scattering are given. Two-photon exchange effects are analyzed. Possible signatures of the two-photon exchange effects in the electron-deuteron elastic scattering are discussed.

# Observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering with two-photon exchange

## Key claim
Two-photon exchange (TPE) effects in elastic electron-deuteron scattering can be identified through deviations from two theta-independent combinations of observables that hold exactly in the one-photon-exchange (OPE) approximation, and through a nonzero vector polarization P_y which vanishes in OPE. The authors derive general expressions for all observables but do not compute numerical TPE corrections; they instead provide the formal framework and identify kinematic conditions where TPE signatures would be most accessible.

## Method
The authors construct the general scattering amplitude for elastic eD scattering using six independent Lorentz structures (G_i, i=1-6) allowed by parity, charge-conjugation, and helicity conservation, where G_4, G_5, G_6 vanish in OPE. They derive analytic expressions for the unpolarized differential cross section, three vector polarizations (P_x, P_y, P_z), and three tensor polarizations (T_20, T_21, T_22) in terms of bilinear products of the OPE form factors G_1, G_2, G_3 and the TPE-induced form factors G_i^(2). The expressions are then examined in the forward and backward scattering angle limits to identify which observables are most sensitive to TPE contributions.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron charge form factor normalization: G_c(0) = 1
- Deuteron quadrupole form factor normalization: G_Q(0) = M_D^2 Q_D = 25.83
- Deuteron magnetic form factor normalization: G_M(0) = 1.714
- The ratio R = mu_p G_E/G_M from polarization transfer experiments at Jefferson Lab follows R ≈ 1 - 0.135(Q^2 - 0.24) over the same Q^2 range where Rosenbluth separation gives R ≈ 1 for Q^2 < 6 GeV^2
- Two theta-independent combinations are identified: C_1 = I_0(1 + 2P_zz) and C_2 = (I_0 P_xz)(I_0 P_x)/(I_0 P_z), which depend only on Q^2 in OPE
- At small scattering angles (theta ≤ 15 degrees), cot^2(theta/2) ≥ 55 and tan^2(theta/2) ≈ 1.9 × 10^(-2)
- At backward angles (theta ≥ 165 degrees), cot^2(theta/2) ≤ 1.9 × 10^(-2) and tan^2(theta/2) > 55

## Context
This work extends the study of two-photon exchange effects from the nucleon case (Blunden 2003, Guichon 2003, Chen 2004) to elastic electron-deuteron scattering, where earlier studies (Gunion 1973, Franco 1973, Boitsov 1973, Lev 1975, Rekalo 1999) focused only on cross section signatures. The paper provides a model-independent theoretical framework for analyzing polarization observables beyond the OPE approximation used in previous deuteron form factor extractions (Arnold 1980, Garcon 1990, Alexa 1999, Garcon 1994). The derived theta-independent constraints enable experimental searches for TPE effects without requiring model calculations, and the authors note that a model calculation is underway (Zhou 2006).
