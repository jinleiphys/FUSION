# Baryon resonances and hadronic interactions in a finite volume

> In a finite volume, resonances and multi-hadron states are identified by discrete energy levels. When comparing the results of lattice QCD calculations to scattering experiments, it is important to have a way of associating the energy spectrum of the finite-volume lattice with the asymptotic behaviour of the S-matrix. A new technique for comparing energy eigenvalues with scattering phase shifts is introduced, which involves the construction of an exactly solvable matrix Hamiltonian model. The model framework is applied to the case of $Δ\rightarrow Nπ$ decay, but is easily generalized to include multi-channel scattering. Extracting resonance parameters involves matching the energy spectrum of the model to that of a lattice QCD calculation. The resulting fit parameters are then used to generate phase shifts. Using a sample set of pseudodata, it is found that the extraction of the resonance position is stable with respect to volume for a variety of regularization schemes, and compares favorably with the well-known Luescher method. The model-dependence of the result is briefly investigated.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new matrix Hamiltonian method for extracting resonance parameters from finite-volume lattice QCD energy spectra that is easily generalizable to multi-channel scattering, and demonstrates that this method yields resonance positions comparable to the well-known Lüscher method, with the extraction being stable with respect to volume for a variety of regularization schemes.

## Method

An exactly solvable matrix Hamiltonian model is constructed for the Δ → Nπ decay in a finite volume, where the Hamiltonian includes a bare Δ baryon state coupled to discrete pion-nucleon momentum states available in a cubic box of size L³. The coupling strengths are derived from chiral effective field theory with a regulator function, and the eigenvalue equation yields a discrete energy spectrum that matches the one-loop Nπ contribution to the Δ baryon self-energy near the pole position. The model parameters (χ_Δ and Δ_0) are then fit to the energy levels of a lattice QCD calculation (or pseudodata), and the fitted parameters are used to generate phase shifts via the on-shell t-matrix formula.

## Key numbers

- The physical Δ-N mass splitting used as input is E_Δ ≡ 292 MeV.
- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- The SU(6) coupling value used is C = -1.52.
- A dipole regulator mass of Λ = 0.8 GeV is chosen for generating pseudodata, being well-matched to phenomenology in the nucleon-pion sector.
- When varying the regularization scale Λ, a systematic uncertainty of only a few MeV is observed in the extracted resonance energy.
- A Gaussian regulator parameter of Λ ≃ 0.6 GeV provides the best matching with the pseudodata when Λ is treated as an additional fit parameter.

## Context

This work extends the standard Lüscher method (Lüscher 1991) for relating finite-volume energy spectra to scattering phase shifts, which becomes increasingly difficult for multi-channel scattering problems. The proposed matrix Hamiltonian approach is designed to be easily generalized to more complicated interactions and additional channels, unlike the geometric equation derived from Lüscher's method which is valid only for two well-separated particles. The method enables future lattice QCD calculations to extract resonance parameters for systems such as multi-channel scattering where Lüscher's method becomes challenging, and the authors note that ongoing research in this direction includes work by He (2005), Bernard (2012), Döring (2012), and Hansen (2012).
