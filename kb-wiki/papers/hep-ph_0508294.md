# Neutrino Oscillations in the Early Universe: A Real Time Formulation

> Neutrino oscillations in the early Universe prior to the epoch of primordial nucleosynthesis is studied by implementing real time non-equilibrium field theory methods. We focus on two flavors of Dirac neutrinos, however, the formulation is general. We obtain the equations of motion for neutrino wavepackets of either chirality and helicity in the plasma allowing for CP asymmetry. Contributions non-local in space-time to the self-energy dominate over the asymmetry for $T \gtrsim 3-5 \mathrm{MeV}$ if the lepton and neutrino asymmetries are of the same order as the baryon asymmetry. We find a new contribution which cannot be interpreted as the usual effective potential. The mixing angles and dispersion relations in the medium depend on \emph{helicity}. We find that resonant transitions are possible in the temperature range $ 10 \lesssim T \ll 100 \mathrm{MeV} $. Near a resonance in the mixing angle, the oscillation time scale in the medium as compared to the vacuum is \emph{slowed-down} substantially for small vacuum mixing angle. The time scale of oscillations \emph{speeds-up} for off resonance high energy neutrinos for which the mixing angle becomes vanishingly small. The equations of motion reduce to the familiar oscillation formulae for negative helicity ultrarelativistic neutrinos, but include consistently both the \emph{mixing angle and the oscillation frequencies in the medium}. These equations of motion also allow to study the dynamics of right handed as well as positive helicity neutrinos.

## Key claim

Neutrino oscillations in the early Universe prior to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis are studied using real-time non-equilibrium field theory, revealing that non-local (in space-time) contributions to the self-energy dominate over lepton and neutrino asymmetry contributions for temperatures \(T \gtrsim 3-5\,\mathrm{MeV}\) when asymmetries are of the same order as the baryon asymmetry, and that the mixing angles and dispersion relations depend on helicity.

## Method

The authors implement real-time non-equilibrium field theory methods combined with linear response to obtain effective Dirac equations for neutrino wavepackets of either chirality and helicity in a thermal medium, allowing for CP asymmetry. The one-loop self-energy from charged and neutral currents is expanded to lowest order in frequency and momentum (up to order \(g^2/M_W^4\)), and the retarded self-energy is used to derive equations of motion for the expectation value of the neutrino field. Two temperature regimes are studied: \(m_e \ll T \ll m_\mu\) (just prior to BBN) and \(m_e, m_\mu \ll T \ll M_W\) (above the QCD phase transition), with lepton, neutrino, and quark asymmetries included.

## Key numbers

- The non-local contributions to the self-energy dominate over asymmetry contributions for \(T \gtrsim 3-5\,\mathrm{MeV}\) when lepton and neutrino asymmetries are of order \(10^{-9}\) (baryon asymmetry scale).
- Resonant transitions are possible in the temperature range \(10 \lesssim T \ll 100\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(T \sim 10\,\mathrm{MeV}\), resonant flavor oscillations occur for neutrino energies in the few MeV range.
- The vacuum mixing parameters used are: \(\overline{M} \approx 0.25\,\mathrm{eV}\), \(|\delta M^2| \approx 7.9\times 10^{-5}\,\mathrm{(eV)^2}\), \(\tan^2\theta \approx 0.40\).
- The ratio \(|\delta M^2|/\overline{M}^{\,2} \ll 1\) in the nearly degenerate case.
- For \(T \gtrsim 10\,\mathrm{MeV}\), the weak interaction relaxation rate \(\Gamma \sim G_F^2 T^5\) times the vacuum oscillation time \(\tau_{osc} \sim E/\delta M^2\) gives \(\Gamma \tau_{osc} \sim 10\,(0.1\,T/\mathrm{MeV})^6\).

## Context

This work extends previous quantum field theory studies of neutrino dispersion relations in hot and dense media by Notzold and Raffelt (1988) and by J.C. D'Olivo et al. (1992), which addressed either dispersion relations or mixing under restrictive approximations to lowest order in \(g^2/M_W^2\). It also builds on the authors' own free-field study of oscillations in a neutrino gas (DanMan) that revealed quantum interference and many-body time scales not captured by single-particle descriptions. The results enable a consistent treatment of neutrino mixing and oscillations in the early Universe that includes both the mixing angle and oscillation frequencies in the medium, and can be generalized to study the dynamics of right-handed and positive helicity neutrinos, as well as the interplay between oscillations and relaxation in future work.
