# Nuclear aspects of neutral current non-standard $ν$-nucleus reactions and the role of the exotic $μ^-\to e^{-}$ transitions experimental limits

> The nuclear aspects of flavour changing neutral current (FCNC) processes, predicted by various new-physics models to occur in the presence of nuclei, are examined by computing the relevant nuclear matrix elements within the context of the quasi-particle RPA using realistic strong two-body forces. One of our aims is to explore the role of the non-standard interactions (NSI) in the leptonic sector and specifically: (i) in lepton flavour violating (LFV) processes involving the neutral particles $ν_\ell$ and $\tildeν_\ell$, $\ell = e,μ,τ$ and (ii) in charged lepton flavour violating (cLFV) processes involving the charged leptons $\ell^-$ or $\ell^+$. As concrete nuclear systems we have chosen the stopping targets of $μ^-\rightarrow e^-$ conversion experiments, i.e. the $^{48}\mathrm{Ti}$ nucleus of the PRIME/PRISM experiment at J-PARC and the $^{27}\mathrm{Al}$ of the COMET at J-PARC as well as of the Mu2e at Fermilab. These experiments have been designed to reduce the single event sensitivity down to $10^{-16}$--$10^{-18}$ in searching for charged lepton mixing events. Our goal is, by taking advantage of our detailed nuclear structure calculations and using the present limits or the sensitivity of the aforementioned exotic $μ^- \rightarrow e^-$ experiments, to put stringent constraints on the parameters of NSI Lagrangians.

## Key claim

The paper uses realistic nuclear structure calculations within the quasi-particle RPA framework to show that the non-standard interaction (NSI) contributions to neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering are significantly smaller than the Standard Model contributions, and that the most severely constrained channel is the $\nu_{\mu} \rightarrow \nu_{e}$ transition due to the experimental limit $\epsilon_{\mu e}^{f P} = 2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ derived from $\mu^{-}\rightarrow e^{-}$ conversion experiments.

## Method

The authors computed coherent neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for $^{48}\mathrm{Ti}$ and $^{27}\mathrm{Al}$ targets using the quasi-particle RPA with realistic Bonn C-D two-body forces, solving BCS equations for the even-even $^{48}\mathrm{Ti}$ ground state and using experimental charge densities from electron scattering for the odd-$A$ $^{27}\mathrm{Al}$ nucleus. They evaluated the nuclear matrix elements for both Standard Model and non-standard interactions, including non-universal (NU) and flavour-changing (FC) terms, and used the experimental upper limits from $\mu^{-}\rightarrow e^{-}$ conversion experiments to constrain the NSI parameters in the effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

* The SINDRUM-II experiment set the limit $R^{\mathrm{Ti}}_{\mu e} < 6.1 \times 10^{-13}$ for $\mu^{-} + ^{48}\mathrm{Ti} \rightarrow e^{-} + ^{48}\mathrm{Ti}$ conversion branching ratio
* The COMET experiment expects to reach sensitivity $R^{\mathrm{Al}}_{\mu e} < 10^{-16}$ using $^{27}\mathrm{Al}$ target
* The Mu2e experiment aims for target sensitivity $R^{\mathrm{Al}}_{\mu e} < 6\times 10^{-17}$ with single event sensitivity $2\times 10^{-17}$
* The PRIME experiment on $^{48}\mathrm{Ti}$ may reach sensitivity $R^{\mathrm{Ti}}_{\mu e} < 10^{-18}$
* The MEG experiment set limit $Br(\mu^+ \rightarrow e^+ \gamma) < 5.7 \times 10^{-13}$
* The SINDRUM II collaboration set limit $Br(\mu^+ \rightarrow e^+ e^+ e^-) < 1.0 \times 10^{-12}$
* The NSI parameter $\epsilon_{\mu e}^{f P} = 2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ was derived from nuclear $\mu^{-}\rightarrow e^{-}$ conversion limits
* For $^{48}\mathrm{Ti}$, the ratio $R_{\mathrm{tot},\nu_{e}} = 1.037$ and $R_{\mathrm{tot},\nu_{\mu}} = 1.293$ at asymptotic energies $E_{\nu} \approx 120$ MeV
* Flux-averaged cross sections for $^{48}\mathrm{Ti}$: $\langle\sigma_{\mathrm{SM}}\rangle = 5.15 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ for $\nu_e$ and $15.2 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ for $\nu_{\mu}$
* Flux-averaged cross sections for $^{27}\mathrm{Al}$: $\langle\sigma_{\mathrm{SM}}\rangle = 1.50 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ for $\nu_e$ and $4.61 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ for $\nu_{\mu}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-standard neutrino interactions by Barranco et al. and Amanik et al. by incorporating realistic nuclear structure calculations with momentum-dependent form factors, which the authors note can lead to differences of even an order of magnitude for supernova neutrino energies compared to approximations that neglect the momentum dependence. The results enable more accurate predictions for supernova neutrino detection signals in future experiments like COMET, Mu2e, and PRIME/PRISM, and provide constraints on NSI parameters that can be used in studies of neutrino propagation through supernova envelopes and core-collapse physics.
