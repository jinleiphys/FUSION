# Ejectile Polarization for $^2 H(e,e'\vec p)n$ at GeV energies

> We perform a fully relativistic calculation of the $^2 H(e,e'\vec p)n$ reaction in the impulse approximation employing the Gross equation to describe the deuteron ground state, and we use the SAID parametrization of the full NN scattering amplitude to describe the final state interactions (FSIs). The formalism for treating the ejectile polarization with a spin projection on an arbitrary axes is discussed. We show results for the six relevant asymmetries and discuss the role of spin-dependent FSI contributions.

## Key claim

The authors perform a fully relativistic calculation of the $^2 H(e,e'\vec p)n$ reaction in the impulse approximation, showing that the six ejectile polarization asymmetries are highly sensitive to spin-dependent final state interactions (FSIs), but the abstract does not emphasize that three of these asymmetries are zero in plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and that off-shell FSI contributions are generally small except for one specific asymmetry.

## Method

The authors used the Gross equation to describe the deuteron ground state relativistically and employed the SAID parametrization of the full NN scattering amplitude to compute spin-dependent final state interactions. They calculated the hadronic tensor for polarized proton production, defined six asymmetries in a coordinate system aligned with the experimental spectrometer setup, and evaluated momentum distributions of these asymmetries for beam energy 5.5 GeV and transferred four-momentum $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$. The NN scattering amplitude was decomposed into central, single spin-flip (spin-orbit), and double spin-flip terms to isolate their contributions to the asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 5.5 GeV; transferred four-momentum: $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$; used for all numerical results shown in figures.
- For $x = 1.3$ and $\phi_p = 35^o$, the off-shell FSI contribution to $A^{s'}_p$ reduces the asymmetry significantly for missing momenta around $p_m = 0.7$ GeV.
- The SAID parametrization of NN scattering data is available only for lab kinetic energies of 1.3 GeV or less, limiting the accessible kinematics.
- The cutoff parameter for the off-shell nucleon form factor was set to $\Lambda_N = 1$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fully relativistic calculation of the unpolarized $D(e,e'p)n$ reaction (Jeschonnek and Van Orden, 2008) and their study of target polarization in $\vec D(e,e'p)n$ (Jeschonnek and Van Orden, 2009). It differs from calculations using the generalized eikonal approximation (Misak, Ciofi degli Atti, and others) or the diagrammatic approach (Laget) by including all spin-dependent pieces in the nucleon-nucleon amplitude. The results enable clean comparisons with future Jefferson Lab experiments measuring recoil proton polarization, as the small off-shell FSI contributions reduce theoretical uncertainty.
