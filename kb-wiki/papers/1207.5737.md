# Specific shear viscosity in hot rotating systems of paired fermions

> The specific shear viscosity $\barη$ of a classically rotating system of nucleons that interact via a monopole pairing interaction is calculated including the effects of thermal fluctuations and coupling to pair vibrations within the selfconsistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation. It is found that $\barη$ increases with angular momentum $M$ at a given temperature $T$. In medium and heavy systems, $\barη$ decreases with increasing $T$ at $T\geq$ 2 MeV and this feature is not affected much by angular momentum. But in lighter systems (with the mass number $A\leq$ 20), $\barη$ increases with $T$ at a value of $M$ close to the maximal value $M_{max}$, which is defined as the limiting angular momentum for each system. The values of $\barη$ obtained within the schematic model as well as for systems with realistic single-particle energies are always larger than the universal lower-bound conjecture $\hbar/(4πk_B)$ up to $T$=5 MeV.

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity of a classically rotating system of paired fermions is always larger than the conjectured universal lower bound of ℏ/(4πk_B) up to T=5 MeV, but this result is obtained only within a monopole pairing interaction model and does not account for residual interactions of nonzero multipolarities such as dipole, quadrupole, or octupole.

## Method

The authors solve the pairing Hamiltonian for a classically rotating spherical system using the finite-temperature BCS with quasiparticle-number fluctuations (FTBCS1) and include coupling to pair vibrations via the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (SCQRPA). The shear viscosity is calculated from the Green-Kubo relation using the retarded Green's function for quasiparticle propagation, normalized to a reference value η₀ at T=0 and M=0. The specific shear viscosity is obtained as the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, where entropy includes both quasiparticle and phonon contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the schematic model with N=10, the largest difference between FTBCS1 and FTBCS1+SCQRPA shear viscosity is almost 2 orders of magnitude at T∼2.7 MeV
- The pairing interaction parameter G is set to 1.04 MeV, 0.53 MeV, and 0.14 MeV for ²⁰O, ⁴⁴Ca, and ¹²⁰Sn respectively, yielding Δ_N(0) ≈ 3.0 MeV, 2.0 MeV, and 1.42 MeV
- The nuclear density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is adopted for all calculations
- The reference shear viscosity η₀ = 1.0×10⁻²³ MeV s fm⁻³ is used for both schematic and realistic calculations
- The limiting angular momentum M_max is 10ℏ for N=10, 13ℏ for N=20, 43ℏ for N=100, 3ℏ for ²⁰O, and 4ℏ for ¹²⁰Sn
- At T=5 MeV and M=0, the specific shear viscosity in realistic systems ranges from twice to ten times its value at T=0 and M=0

## Context

This work extends the Green-Kubo approach for calculating specific shear viscosity from giant dipole resonance parameters, previously applied to non-rotating nuclei by Auerbach and collaborators and by Dang (2011), to include finite angular momentum. The results contradict the behavior found in Ref. [GDRJ] where specific shear viscosity extracted from GDR parameters in hot rotating nuclei shows a tendency of decreasing with increasing M at high T. The formalism enables future calculations of shear viscosity in any finite hot rotating Fermi systems using spectral intensities or response functions, beyond the monopole pairing model considered here.
