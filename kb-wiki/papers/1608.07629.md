# Baryons in the unquenched quark model

> In this contribution, we present the unquenched quark model as an extension of the constituent quark model that includes the effects of sea quarks via a $^{3}P_{0}$ quark-antiquark pair-creation mechanism. Particular attention is paid to the spin and flavor content of the proton, magnetic moments and $β$ decays of octet baryons.

## Key claim

The unquenched quark model (UQM), which adds sea-quark effects via a \(^{3}P_{0}\) pair-creation mechanism, can qualitatively reproduce the spin and flavor content of the proton and maintain the good agreement of the constituent quark model (CQM) with magnetic moments, but only when the \(^{3}P_{0}\) coupling strength is normalized to the observed value of the proton flavor asymmetry.

## Method

The authors extend the constituent quark model by adding quark-antiquark pairs as a perturbation using a \(^{3}P_{0}\) model for pair creation, summing over intermediate baryon-meson states in one-loop diagrams. They employ a simplified version of the UQM that includes only the pion cloud (i.e., \(N\pi\) and \(\Delta\pi\) components) and compute the spin and flavor content of the proton, magnetic moments of octet baryons, and pion fluctuation probabilities, normalizing the results to two different experimental values of the proton flavor asymmetry (E866/NuSea and NMC).

## Key numbers

- Proton flavor asymmetry \(\mathcal{A}(p) = \Delta L\): UQM1 = 0.118 (normalized to E866/NuSea value 0.118 ± 0.012), UQM2 = 0.158 (normalized to NMC value 0.158 ± 0.010).
- \(\Delta u\): CQM = 4/3, UQM1 = 1.132, UQM2 = 1.064, experiment = 0.842 ± 0.013.
- \(\Delta d\): CQM = −1/3, UQM1 = −0.368, UQM2 = −0.380, experiment = −0.427 ± 0.013.
- \(\Delta \Sigma = \Delta u + \Delta d + \Delta s\): CQM = 1, UQM1 = 0.764, UQM2 = 0.684, experiment = 0.330 ± 0.039.
- Axial coupling \(g_A = \Delta u - \Delta d\): CQM = 5/3, UQM1 = 1.500, UQM2 = 1.444, experiment = 1.2723 ± 0.0023.
- Magnetic moment of \(\Sigma^+\): CQM = 2.673, UQM1 = 2.589, UQM2 = 2.509, experiment = 2.458 ± 0.010 (in nuclear magnetons).
- Probability of \(p \rightarrow n\pi^+\) fluctuation: UQM1 = 0.180, UQM2 = 0.241, experiment = 0.17 ± 0.01.
- Total probability of pion fluctuation of the proton: UQM1 = 0.455, UQM2 = 0.609, experiment = 0.470.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model (CQM) by incorporating sea-quark effects via the \(^{3}P_{0}\) mechanism, motivated by earlier studies by Tornqvist (1990s) and others on quark-model extensions with \(q\bar{q}\) pairs. It directly addresses the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) asymmetry in the nucleon sea (measured by the E866/NuSea and NMC collaborations) and the proton spin crisis (measured by HERMES and COMPASS). The results enable further studies of baryon structure by showing that the pion cloud can simultaneously explain the flavor asymmetry, reduce the quark-model values of \(\Delta u\) and \(g_A\), and produce a 25–30% contribution of orbital angular momentum to the proton spin, while preserving the successful CQM predictions for magnetic moments.
