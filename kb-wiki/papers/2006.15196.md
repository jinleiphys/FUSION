# A shell-model study of calcium isotopes towards their drip line

> We report in this paper a study in terms of the nuclear shell model about the location of the calcium isotopes drip line. The starting point is considering the realistic two-body potential derived by Entem and Machleidt within chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), as well as a chiral three-body force at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) whose structure and low-energy constants are consistent with the two-body potential. Then we construct the effective single-particle energies and residual interaction needed to diagonalize the shell-model Hamiltonian. The calculated two-neutron separation energies agree nicely with experiment until 56Ca, which is the heaviest isotope whose mass has been measured, and do not show any sign of two-neutron emission until 70Ca. We discuss the role of the choice of the model space in determining the neutron drip line, and also the dependence of the results on the parameters of the shell-model Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The shell-model calculations predict that the calcium isotopic chain remains bound against two-neutron emission at least up to **⁷⁰Ca**, but this result depends critically on including three-body correlations and on the choice of model space; without these correlations the drip line would be placed at N=40.

## Method

The authors start from chiral two-body (N³LO) and three-body (N²LO) potentials by Entem and Machleidt, with consistent low-energy constants (c_D = -1, c_E = -0.34). They construct an effective shell-model Hamiltonian via the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion, including one- and two-body Goldstone diagrams through third order in the NN potential and first order in the NNN potential, with a model space of four 0f1p orbitals plus the neutron 0g₉/₂ orbital outside a ⁴⁰Ca core. Density-dependent two-body terms are derived from three-body correlation diagrams to account for many-body effects in systems with more than two valence neutrons.

## Key numbers

* Calculated two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ for ⁵⁶Ca is 3.377 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 4.492 MeV (from Audi 2003, Wienholtz 2013, Michimasa 2018).
* Calculated S₂ₙ for ⁶⁰Ca is 3.017 MeV, for ⁶⁸Ca is 3.048 MeV, and for ⁷⁰Ca is 3.605 MeV; all remain positive, indicating bound nuclei.
* The neutron 0g₉/₂ single-particle energy is calculated at 9.9 MeV relative to the 0f₇/₂ orbital (set to 0.0 MeV).
* The odd-even mass difference around ⁶⁸Ni is calculated as 4.2 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 3.2 MeV (Audi 2003).
* The oscillator parameter ℏω used is 11 MeV, derived from the formula ℏω = 45 A⁻¹/³ − 25 A⁻²/³ for A=40.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model study of 0f1p-shell nuclei (Ma 2019) by adding the neutron 0g₉/₂ orbital to reach heavier calcium isotopes. The prediction that calcium isotopes remain bound up to ⁷⁰Ca is consistent with the Bayesian model-averaging analysis of density-functional calculations by Neufcourt (2019), which found ⁶⁸Ca has about 76% posterior probability of being bound. The results contrast with earlier coupled-cluster calculations by Hagen (2012) that predicted ⁶⁰Ca as loosely bound, and with the authors' own calculations without the 0g₉/₂ orbital, which placed the drip line at N=38. The work enables future experimental searches for bound calcium isotopes beyond ⁶⁰Ca and motivates including continuum effects, as the authors note they are working to implement Berggren-basis calculations for fp-shell nuclei.
