# Shell plus pairing effect arguments for cluster preformation at the nuclear surface in cold fission

> In 1928 G. Gamow as well as Condon and Gurney gave the first explanation of alpha decay as a quantum tunnelling of a preformed particle at the nuclear surface. Soon after experimental discovery in 1984 by Rose and Jones of cluster radioactivity, confirming earlier (1980) predictions by Sandulescu, Poenaru and W. Greiner, a microscopic theory also explained the phenomenon in a similar way. Here we show for the first time that in a spontaneous cold fission process the shell plus pairing corrections calculated with Strutinsky's procedure may give a strong argument for preformation of a light fission fragment near the nuclear surface. It is obtained when the radius of the light fragment, $R_2$, is increased linearly with the separation distance, $R$, of the two fragments, while for $R_2=$~constant one gets the well known two hump potential barrier.

# Shell plus pairing effect arguments for cluster preformation at the nuclear surface in cold fission

## Key claim
The shell plus pairing corrections calculated with Strutinsky's procedure provide a strong argument for preformation of a light fission fragment near the nuclear surface in spontaneous cold fission, but only when the radius of the light fragment is increased linearly with the separation distance of the two fragments; the well-known two-hump potential barrier is obtained when the light fragment radius is held constant.

## Method
The authors applied the macroscopic-microscopic method using the Yukawa-plus-exponential model for the macroscopic deformation energy and Strutinsky's procedure for shell plus pairing corrections, based on the asymmetric two-center shell model (ATCSM). They calculated the total deformation energy as a function of separation distance R and mass asymmetry η, using cranking inertia for the dynamical part, and solved the BCS equations for pairing gaps. The calculations were performed on a grid of up to 412 values of separation distance and 24 values of mass asymmetry to find the least action trajectory.

## Key numbers
- For the parent nucleus ²⁸²Cn, the deep minimum of shell plus pairing correction energy occurs around (R−R_i)/(R_t−R_i) = 0.82 for symmetrical fission with linearly increasing R₂
- The nuclear radius constant used was r₀ = 1.16 fm
- The spontaneous fission half-life of ²⁸²Cn was reproduced with log₁₀ T_f^exp (s) = −3.086
- The grid for real calculation used 412 values of (R−R_i)/(R_t−R_i) and 24 values of η = (A₁−A₂)/A
- Similar results were obtained for ²⁵²Cf and ²⁴⁰Pu

## Context
This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic approach originally developed by Strutinsky (1967) for explaining fission barriers and shape isomers, and builds on the authors' previous calculations (Poenaru and Gherghescu, 2016) using the two-center shell model. The results provide a microscopic argument for cluster preformation at the nuclear surface, analogous to the explanation of alpha decay by Gamow (1928) and Condon and Gurney (1928), and of cluster radioactivity by Rose and Jones (1984) following predictions by Sandulescu, Poenaru, and Greiner (1980). The method enables calculation of spontaneous fission half-lives for superheavy nuclei where cluster decay may compete with alpha decay and spontaneous fission.
