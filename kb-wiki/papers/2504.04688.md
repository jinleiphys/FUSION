# Identifying $α$-cluster configurations in $^{20}$Ne via ultracentral Ne+Ne Collisions

> The initial-state geometry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions provides a novel probe to nuclear cluster structure. For $^{20}$Ne, a novel approach is proposed to distinguish between the cluster configurations (5$α$ versus $α+ ^{16}$O) in order to gain insight into nuclear structure transitions governed by many-body quantum correlations. Through analytical calculations with the microscopic Brink model and event-by-event simulations using the hydrodynamic framework, we establish the normalized symmetric cumulant NSC (3, 2) and the Pearson coefficient $ρ_2 (v_{2}^{2},\ δ[p_{\mathrm{T}}])$ as quantitative discriminators to reveal enhanced cluster degrees of freedom in the ground state of $^{20}$Ne. The ultracentral Ne+Ne collisions at the LHC can experimentally identify these two competing configurations via these flow correlation observables, opening a new paradigm for probing clustering in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(3, 2) and the Pearson coefficient ρ₂(v₂², δ[pT]) can distinguish between the 5α and α+¹⁶O cluster configurations in ²⁰Ne via ultracentral Ne+Ne collisions at the LHC, but only if the sign of these observables is used as the discriminator; the authors explicitly note that ρ₃ fails as a reliable discriminator in the final state due to fluctuation effects, and that the initial-state ρ₂ for the diffuse 5α configuration exhibits a sign inconsistent with analytical results, yet this very discrepancy enables clean distinction.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic Brink cluster wave function to generate nucleon density distributions for ²⁰Ne in both the 5α bi-pyramidal configuration (parameterized by distances D₁ and D₂) and the α+¹⁶O limit (D₁→0). They performed analytical calculations of eccentricity-based observables from these densities, then carried out event-by-event simulations using the TᵣENTo+VISHNU+UrQMD (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics framework for Ne+Ne collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV, with centrality determined by initial entropy density S and kinematic cuts 0.2 GeV ≤ pT ≤ 3 GeV and |η| ≤ 0.8.

## Key numbers

- The experimental rms charge radius of ²⁰Ne is approximately 3.00 fm (Angeli 2013), used to constrain the structural parameters D₁ and D₂ within a 10% error margin (2.7 < √⟨r²⟩ < 3.3 fm).
- The harmonic oscillator size parameter b is varied within 1.46 < b < 1.76 fm to cover systematic uncertainty of the cluster model.
- The Woods-Saxon distribution parameters used for comparison are R₀ = 2.791 fm, a = 0.698 fm, and w = -0.168 (De Vries 1987).
- The structure phase boundary where NSC(3, 2) = 0 occurs at k ≈ 3, where k = D₂/D₁.
- The symmetric bi-pyramidal 5α configuration corresponds to k = 5/3.
- The α+¹⁶O configuration yields positive NSC(3, 2); the 5α configuration yields negative NSC(3, 2).
- The 0-5% centrality range preserves the sign distinction for NSC(3, 2) in both initial and final states.
- The 0-10% centrality range preserves the sign distinction for ρ₂(v₂², δ[pT]) in both initial and final states.

## Context

This work extends the use of heavy-ion collision flow observables from deformation studies in heavy nuclei (U+U collisions at STAR, Xe+Xe collisions at LHC) to cluster structure in light nuclei, where the deformation framework fails. It contrasts with the approach of Giacalone (2024) which characterized shape deformation but lacked resolving power between competing cluster configurations. The paper enables experimental tests using Ne+Ne collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV and fixed-target Pb+Ne collisions at √sNN = 70.9 GeV at the LHC, and builds on the Brink cluster model (Brink 1966) and the nonlocalized THSR model (Zhou 2013) for nuclear structure description.
