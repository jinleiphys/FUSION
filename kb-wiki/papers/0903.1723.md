# The Nucleon Distribution Amplitudes and their application to nucleon form factors and the $N \to Δ$ transition at intermediate values of $Q^2$

> We compare a recent lattice determination of the nucleon distribution amplitudes with other approaches and models. We study the nucleon distribution amplitudes up to twist 6 in next-to leading conformal spin and we also investigate conformal d-wave contributions to the leading twist distribution amplitude. With the help of light-cone sum rules one can relate the distribution amplitudes to the form factors of the nucleon or the $ N \to Δ$ transition at intermediate values of the momentum transfer. We compare our results with experimental data in the range 1 GeV$^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10$ GeV$^2$. Keeping in mind that we are working only in LO QCD and NLO-QCD corrections might be sizeable we already obtain a surprisingly good agreement for the nucleon form factors $G_M^n$, $G_M^p$, $G_A^p$ and $G_T^p$ and for the $N \to Δ$ transition form factor ratios $R_{EM}$ and $R_{SM}$.

# The Nucleon Distribution Amplitudes and their application to nucleon form factors and the $N \to \Delta$ transition at intermediate values of $Q^2$

## Key claim

The authors compare a recent lattice determination of nucleon distribution amplitudes with QCD sum rule estimates and phenomenological models, and use light-cone sum rules (LCSR) to relate these distribution amplitudes to nucleon form factors and $N \to \Delta$ transition form factors. They obtain surprisingly good agreement with experimental data in the range $1 \text{ GeV}^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10 \text{ GeV}^2$ for several form factors, but explicitly caution that they are working only at leading order (LO) in QCD and that next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD corrections might be sizeable.

## Method

The authors expand nucleon distribution amplitudes up to twist 6 in next-to-leading conformal spin, including conformal d-wave contributions to the leading twist distribution amplitude. They compare non-perturbative parameters extracted from lattice QCD simulations (using two dynamical quark flavours with non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions) with QCD sum rule estimates from Chernyak-Ogloblin-Zhitnitsky (1987) and Braun-Lenz-Wittmann (2006), and with the phenomenological BK model of Bolz (1996). Using light-cone sum rules, they compute nucleon electromagnetic, axial, and pseudoscalar form factors, as well as $N \to \Delta$ transition form factor ratios, and compare these predictions with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Lattice value of $f_N$: $3.234(63)(86) \times 10^{-3} \text{ GeV}^2$ at renormalization scale $1 \text{ GeV}^2$, about 35% smaller than the QCD sum rule estimate of $5.0(5) \times 10^{-3} \text{ GeV}^2$
- Lattice value of $\lambda_1$: $-35.57(65)(136) \times 10^{-3} \text{ GeV}^2$ at $1 \text{ GeV}^2$, compared to QCD sum rule estimate $-27(9) \times 10^{-3} \text{ GeV}^2$
- Lattice value of $\lambda_2$: $70.02(128)(268) \times 10^{-3} \text{ GeV}^2$ at $1 \text{ GeV}^2$, compared to QCD sum rule estimate $54(19) \times 10^{-3} \text{ GeV}^2$
- Ratio $f_N/\lambda_1$ from lattice: $-0.0909 \pm 0.0054 \pm 0.0095$, compared to QCD sum rule estimate $-0.185 \pm 0.064$ (a factor of two difference)
- Non-relativistic relation $2\lambda_1 + \lambda_2 = 0$ satisfied with $\left|(2\lambda_1 + \lambda_2)/(2\lambda_1 - \lambda_2)\right|_{\text{LAT}} = 0.008 \pm 0.013$
- Leading twist moment $\varphi^{100}$ from lattice: $0.3999(37)(139)$ at $1 \text{ GeV}^2$, compared to asymptotic value $1/3 \approx 0.333$ and QCD sum rule value $0.560(60)$
- Leading twist moment $\varphi^{010}$ from lattice: $0.2986(11)(52)$ at $1 \text{ GeV}^2$, compared to asymptotic value $1/3 \approx 0.333$ and QCD sum rule value $0.192(12)$
- Leading twist moment $\varphi^{001}$ from lattice: $0.3015(32)(106)$ at $1 \text{ GeV}^2$, compared to asymptotic value $1/3 \approx 0.333$ and QCD sum rule value $0.229(29)$
- Momentum transfer range studied: $1 \text{ GeV}^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10 \text{ GeV}^2$

## Context

This work extends the light-cone sum rule approach to nucleon form factors developed by Braun-Lenz-Mueller-Stein (2001) and Lenz-Wittmann-Stein (2003), and the QCD sum rule determination of distribution amplitudes by Braun-Fries-Maul-Stein (2000) and Braun-Lenz-Wittmann (2006). The lattice results from the QCDSF collaboration (2007, 2008) provide a non-perturbative determination of the distribution amplitude parameters without model assumptions, and the comparison reveals that the QCD sum rule estimates tend to overestimate deviations from the asymptotic form. The BLW model, which interpolates between asymptotic and sum rule values, and the lattice results both give distribution amplitude moments close to the asymptotic values. The work enables more reliable predictions for nucleon form factors at intermediate $Q^2$ once NLO QCD corrections to the light-cone sum rules are computed, and provides lattice input for the $N \to \Delta$ transition form factors which depend on the individual values of $f_N$ and $\lambda_1$ (unlike the electromagnetic form factors which depend only on their ratio when using the Ioffe current).
