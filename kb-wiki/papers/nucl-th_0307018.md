# Genuine collective flow from Lee-Yang zeroes

> We propose to use the theory of phase transitions of Lee and Yang as a practical tool to analyze long-range correlations in a finite-size system. We apply it to the analysis of anisotropic flow in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and show that this method is more reliable than any other used so far.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Lee-Yang theory of phase transitions, specifically the analysis of zeroes of a generating function, can be used as a practical and more reliable method to extract anisotropic flow coefficients from experimental data in nucleus-nucleus collisions, provided the flow signal is larger than a statistical fluctuation threshold.

## Method

The method constructs for each event the global observable \(Q^\theta = \sum_{j=1}^M \cos n(\phi_j - \theta)\), where \(n\) is the Fourier harmonic of interest, \(M\) is the multiplicity, and \(\theta\) is an arbitrary reference angle. The moment generating function \(G(z) \equiv \langle e^{z Q^\theta} \rangle\) is then evaluated on the imaginary axis for real positive \(r\) (i.e., \(G(ir)\)) by averaging over many events; the first minimum \(r_0\) of \(|G(ir)|\) is located, and the flow coefficient is obtained as \(v_n = j_{01} / (M r_0)\), where \(j_{01} \approx 2.405\) is the first zero of the Bessel function \(J_0\).

## Key numbers

- For simulated data with \(M = 300\) particles per event, \(v_2 = 6\%\), and \(N_{\rm evts} = 20000\) events, the method reconstructed \(v_2 = 5.95\%\), in excellent agreement with the input value.
- The spurious flow from statistical fluctuations in the no-flow case is bounded by \(v_n^\theta \lesssim j_{01} / \sqrt{2 M \ln N_{\rm evts}}\), which for the simulated parameters (\(M=300\), \(N_{\rm evts}=20000\)) gives about \(3.1\%\).
- The first zero of the theoretical generating function under the central-limit approximation lies at \(z_0 = i j_{01} / (M v_n)\), where \(j_{01} \simeq 2.405\).

## Context

This work extends the cumulant-based methods for flow analysis developed by Borghini (2000) by providing a formally simpler and more systematic approach based on Lee-Yang zeroes, which is argued to be perfectly stable against nonflow correlations such as those from resonance decays or minijet production. The method enables a cleaner separation of collective flow from few-body correlations, and the authors note that it can be extended to differential flow as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity, as detailed in a companion paper (Bhalerao, Borghini, and Ollitrault, 2003).
