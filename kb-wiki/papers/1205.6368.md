# Systematic analysis of symmetry energy effects in the neutron star crust properties

> The functional form of the nuclear symmetry energy in the whole range of densities relevant for the neutron stars is still unknown. Discrepancies concern both the low as well as the high density behaviour of this function. By use of Bezier curves three different families of the symmetry energy shapes, relevant for different density range were introduced. Their consequences for the crustal properties of neutron stars are presented.

## Key claim

The paper systematically explores how different density regimes of the nuclear symmetry energy affect neutron star crust properties, finding that large values of the symmetry energy at very low densities (near zero) are in contradiction with neutron star observations, even after correcting for the pathological neutron clusterization problem that plagued earlier models.

## Method

The authors constructed three families of symmetry energy shapes using Bezier curves, which allow independent variation of the symmetry energy at low, intermediate, and high density ranges while preserving known saturation properties. For each family, they computed the crust-core transition density using three different stability criteria (the compressibility condition K_mu, the finite-size stability condition v(Q), and the two-phase Gibbs coexistence condition), then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass, radius, crust thickness, and moment of inertia profiles.

## Key numbers

- For the Delta E-k models, the crust-core transition density n_c(K_mu) decreases from 0.093 fm^-3 for k=0 MeV to 0.020 fm^-3 for k=14 MeV, where k = E_s(0)
- The Delta E-k models produce maximum neutron star masses of about 2 solar masses and radii between 12-13 km
- For the L-high-c and L-high-b families, the slope L takes values between 40 and 120 MeV, with corresponding n_c(K_mu) ranging from 0.100 fm^-3 (L=60) to 0.139 fm^-3 (L=120)
- The critical masses satisfying the Direct Urca constraint are 1.2 solar masses for Delta E-k models, 0.5-1.0 solar masses for L-high-c models, and 0.5-1.3 solar masses for high-E_s models

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Kubis (2007) and Kubis et al. (2010) on symmetry energy effects in neutron star crusts, using Bezier curves to overcome limitations of polynomial parametrizations that could produce unphysical behavior. The authors incorporate experimental constraints from neutron skin thickness measurements (Chen et al., 2010), isospin diffusion (Tsang et al., 2009), and low-density symmetry energy measurements (Natowitz et al., 2010). The results enable systematic testing of symmetry energy models against astrophysical constraints including the 2 solar mass measurement (Demorest et al., 2010), the Vela pulsar crustal moment of inertia bound (Link et al., 1999), and the Direct Urca cooling constraint (Popov et al., 2006).
