# Chiral three-nucleon forces and the evolution of correlations along the oxygen isotopic chain

> The impact of three-nucleon forces (3NFs) along the oxygen chain is investigated for the spectral distribution for attachment and removal of a nucleon, spectroscopic factors and matter radii. We employ self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory which allows a comprehensive calculation of the single particle spectral function. For the closed subshell isotopes, $^{14}$O, $^{16}$O, $^{22}$O, $^{24}$O and $^{28}$O, we perform calculations with the Dyson-ADC(3) method. The remaining open shell isotopes are studied using the newly developed Gorkov-SCGF formalism up to second order. We produce plots for the full-fledged spectral distributions. The spectroscopic factors for the dominant quasiparticle peaks are found to depend very little on the leading order (NNLO) chiral 3NFs. The latters have small impact on the calculated matter radii, which, however are consistently obtained smaller than experiment. Similarly, single particle spectra tend to be diluted with respect to experiment. This effect might hinder, to some extent, the onset of correlations and screen the quenching of calculated spectroscopic factors. The most important effects of 3NFs is thus the fine tuning of the energies for the dominant quasiparticle states, which govern the shell evolution and the position of driplines. Although present chiral NNLO 3NFs interactions do reproduce the binding energies correctly in this mass region, the details of the nuclear wave function remain at odd with the experiment showing too small radii and a too dilute single particle spectrum, similar to what already pointed out for larger masses. This suggests a lack of repulsion in the present model of NN+3N interactions which is mildly apparent already for masses in the A=14--28 range.

## Key claim

Although chiral NNLO three-nucleon forces (3NFs) reproduce binding energies correctly in the A=14–28 mass region, the details of the nuclear wave function remain at odds with experiment, showing too small radii and a too dilute single-particle spectrum, suggesting a lack of repulsion in the present model of NN+3N interactions that is mildly apparent already for these masses.

## Method

The authors employed self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory to calculate the single-particle spectral function for oxygen isotopes. For closed subshell isotopes (14O, 16O, 22O, 24O, 28O), they used the Dyson-ADC(3) method, which is fully non-perturbative and retains all self-energy diagrams up to third order. For open shell isotopes, they used the newly developed Gorkov-SCGF formalism truncated at second order, which allows addressing truly open shell nuclei through a Nambu-Gorkov formulation that breaks particle-number conservation to describe pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 3H calculated with the chosen 3NF low-energy constants (cD = -0.2, cE = 0.098) is -8.32 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -8.48 MeV
- Varying the oscillator frequency between 20 and 24 MeV in Dyson-ADC(3) calculations produced up to 450 keV variations in the binding energy of 24O
- Varying both the oscillator frequency (20–24 MeV) and the SRG cutoff (1.88–2.0 fm−1) gave a maximum variation of 310 keV for the neutron 1/2− quasihole in 24O, corresponding to 2% of its value of -14.22 MeV
- The largest variation for proton quasiparticle energies was 550 keV for a 5/2+ quasiparticle, mostly due to variations in the oscillator frequency
- Increasing the 3NF model space from Nmax = 14 to 16 changed Gorkov total binding energies by approximately 500 keV
- The contribution of the two-body correction term to rms radii is ≤ 0.03 fm and decreases with mass number
- Errors in the approximation of the three-nucleon interaction expectation value using non-interacting three-body density matrices were found not to exceed 250 keV on the total binding energy for 16O and 24O

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio calculations by Cipollone, Barbieri, and Navratil (2013) that showed 3NFs are crucial for understanding the oxygen dripline at 24O and affect N and F isotopes up to at least 29F. The study provides the first fully microscopic calculations of the evolution of single-particle spectral functions along a full isotopic chain, enabling a more thorough test of modern chiral interactions. The results suggest a lack of repulsion in the present model of NN+3N interactions, similar to what was previously pointed out for larger masses, and indicate that while present chiral NNLO 3NFs reproduce binding energies correctly, the details of the nuclear spectral function remain inconsistent with experiment.
