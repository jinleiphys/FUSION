# Study of continuum nuclear structure of 12C via (p,p'X) at intermediate energies

> The inclusive 12C(p,p') and exclusive 12C(p,p'X) reactions have been studied with a beam energy of 156 MeV and for X = p and alpha. The study focuses on the (p,p'X) reaction mechanism and on the structure of 12C just above the particle-emission threshold, 14 < E_x < 28 MeV. Cross sections were simultaneously measured for all three reactions. The exclusive data were analyzed by making multiple-peak fits of the spectra and by Legendre-polynomial fits of the angular correlations. Multiple-peak fits were also made of the inclusive spectra. The resultant cross sections were compared to theoretical calculations. An analysis of the results shows that this region of E_x consists predominantly of resonant excitations, in contradiction to the findings of previous analyses.

## Key claim

The low-energy continuum of 12C (14–24 MeV excitation) consists predominantly of resonant excitations with very little nonresonant background, contradicting earlier analyses that assumed large nonresonant contributions to the inclusive (p,p') spectrum.

## Method

Inclusive 12C(p,p') and exclusive 12C(p,p'X) reactions were measured simultaneously at 156 MeV beam energy using the IUCF K600 spectrometer for scattered protons and an array of silicon detectors for coincident charged particles (p and α). Multiple-peak fits of the spectra and Legendre-polynomial fits of angular correlation functions were performed, and the resulting cross sections were compared to DWIA calculations using shell-model one-body density-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 156 MeV
- Excitation energy range studied: 14 ≤ E_x ≤ 28 MeV
- Momentum transfers: q_cm = 0.71, 0.93, and 1.16 fm^-1
- Energy resolution: 140 keV FWHM
- Residual-nucleus mass resolution: better than 250 keV FWHM
- Angle-integrated cross sections for α_1 and p_0 account for respectively 24% and 36% of the inclusive cross section for 17 ≤ E_x ≤ 24 MeV
- For the 19.4 MeV peak, p_0 and α_1 emission account for 48% and 20% of the inclusive cross section at low q
- Calculated nucleon decay width for the 19.4 MeV state: 468 keV (Γ_p0(d) = 86 keV, Γ_p0(s) = 168 keV, Γ_n0(s) = 214 keV)
- Measured α_1 width for the 19.4 MeV state: ~100 keV
- Normalization factor of 0.55 for the sum of T=0 and T=1 4^- states at 19.7 MeV
- Ground-state spectroscopic factor of 0.56 (mainly d_3/2) for the J^π = 3^-_4, T=0 state at 20.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends coincidence studies of continuum nuclear structure previously performed with (e,e'X) (Knopfler 1986) and (α,α'X) (Zwart 1985) reactions to intermediate-energy (p,p'X) on a complex nucleus for the first time. It contradicts earlier inclusive (p,p') analyses by Buenerd (1977), Comfort (1982), and Jones (1983) that assumed large nonresonant backgrounds, and demonstrates that proper treatment of broad resonance tails eliminates the need for such backgrounds. The results enable reanalysis of inclusive (p,p') data and provide constraints for shell-model calculations of 12C, particularly for states such as the 20.6 MeV level whose nature remains unclear.
