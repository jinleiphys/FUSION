# Spatial structure of neutron Cooper pair in low density uniform matter

> We analyze spatial structure of the neutron Cooper pair in superfluid low-density uniform matter by means of BCS calculations employing a bare force and the effective Gogny interaction. It is shown that the Cooper pair exhibits a strong spatial di-neutron correlation in a wide range of neutron density $ρ/ρ_0\approx 10^{-4}-0.5$. This feature is related to the crossover behavior between the pairing of the weak coupling BCS type and the Bose-Einstein condensation of bound neutron pairs. We also show that the zero-range delta interaction can describe the spatial structure of the neutron Cooper pair if the density dependent interaction strength and the cut-off energy are appropriately chosen. Parameterizations of the density-dependent delta interaction satisfying this condition are discussed.

## Key claim

The neutron Cooper pair in superfluid low-density uniform matter exhibits a strong spatial di-neutron correlation in a wide range of neutron density (ρ/ρ₀ ≈ 10⁻⁴–0.5), characterized by the Cooper pair's r.m.s. radius becoming smaller than the average inter-neutron distance, and this feature is related to a crossover between weak-coupling BCS pairing and Bose-Einstein condensation of bound neutron pairs, though the realization of the crossover may be marginal and density-dependent.

## Method

BCS calculations were performed for uniform neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter using the G3RS bare nucleon-nucleon force (a local potential with three Gaussians reproducing the ¹S scattering length a = −17.6 fm) and the effective Gogny D1 interaction, with Hartree-Fock single-particle spectra derived from the Gogny force or an effective-mass approximation for the bare force. The gap equation and number equation were solved without any momentum cut-off, and the Cooper pair wave function Ψ_pair(r) was computed from the u,v-factors to analyze the spatial structure as a function of neutron density.

## Key numbers

* The pairing gap Δ_F reaches a maximum around ρ/ρ₀ ∼ 0.1–0.3, with Δ_F ≈ 2.5 MeV at ρ/ρ₀ ∼ 0.2 for neutron matter using the G3RS force.
* The r.m.s. radius ξ_rms of the Cooper pair decreases from ∼50 fm at ρ/ρ₀ ≈ 1 to ξ_rms ≈ 4.5–6 fm at ρ/ρ₀ ∼ 0.1, and stays at ξ_rms ≈ 5–6 fm in the density region ρ/ρ₀ ∼ 10⁻²–0.1.
* At ρ/ρ₀ = 1/8, ξ_rms = 4.81 fm (symmetric matter, Gogny D1) and 4.94 fm (neutron matter, G3RS), both smaller than the average inter-neutron distance d = 4.55 fm.
* The probability P(d) for the Cooper pair partners to be correlated within the average inter-neutron distance d exceeds 0.8 at ρ/ρ₀ = 1/8 and reaches 0.92 at ρ/ρ₀ = 1/64 and 1/512.
* At ρ/ρ₀ = 1/512, the gap-to-Fermi-energy ratio Δ_F/e_F ≈ 0.4, larger than the value Δ_F/e_F ≈ 0.25 at ρ/ρ₀ = 1/8 where the gap is near its maximum.

## Context

This work extends previous BCS calculations of neutron pairing in low-density matter (Takatsuka 1972, 1984; Baldo 1990; Lombardo 2001) by systematically analyzing the spatial structure of the Cooper pair wave function and its density dependence, and by connecting the strong di-neutron correlation to the BCS-BEC crossover framework discussed for fermion systems with large scattering length (Leggett 1980; Nozieres 1985). The results provide a reference for understanding di-neutron correlation in neutron-rich nuclei and neutron star crusts, and enable parameterization of density-dependent delta interactions (DDDI) constrained by the spatial structure of the Cooper pair rather than only by the pairing gap.
