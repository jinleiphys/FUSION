# Estimating transport coefficients of interacting pion gas with K-matrix cross sections

> We estimate the transport coefficients, $viz.$, shear and bulk viscosities as well as thermal and electrical conductivities, of hot pionic matter using relativistic Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation. We use K-matrix parametrization of pion-pion cross sections to estimate the transport coefficients which incorporate multiple heavy resonances while simultaneously preserving the unitarity of S-matrix. We compare transport coefficients estimated using K-matrix parametrization with existing literature on pionic transport coefficients. We find that the K-matrix scheme estimations are in reasonable agreement with previous results.

## Key claim

The K-matrix parametrization of pion-pion cross sections, which incorporates multiple heavy resonances while preserving S-matrix unitarity, yields transport coefficients for hot pionic matter that are in reasonable agreement with previous results from chiral perturbation theory and other models, though the shear viscosity estimates are smaller than those from chiral perturbation theory and larger than those from the Chapman-Enskog approximation using the same K-matrix cross sections.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation to derive expressions for shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, thermal conductivity, and electrical conductivity of a hot pion gas. They computed the thermal-averaged relaxation time using K-matrix parametrization of pion-pion elastic scattering cross sections, which incorporates multiple heavy resonances while preserving unitarity, and compared these results with those from phase shift parametrization. Thermodynamic quantities were estimated using the S-matrix formulation of statistical mechanics, and the study also examined the effect of including pi-K, pi-eta, and pi-N interactions on the transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity of pure pion gas at T = 150 MeV using K-matrix parametrization is approximately 0.002 GeV^3 (estimated from Fig. [shear_pion(a)]), which is smaller than chiral perturbation theory results from Lang (2012) and Fernandez-Fraile (2009).
* The ratio eta/s for pure pion gas decreases with increasing temperature in the K-matrix formalism, with values around 0.3-0.4 at T = 100-150 MeV (estimated from Fig. [shear_pion(b)]).
* Bulk viscosity estimated using K-matrix formalism is of the same order of magnitude as chiral perturbation theory results from Fernandez-Fraile (2009) and Dobado (2011) that consider only elastic pi-pi scattering, while results from Lu (2011) that include inelastic processes are two orders of magnitude higher.
* The ratio zeta/s for pure pion gas decreases with increasing temperature, with higher values at low temperature (T ~ m_pi) due to strong conformal symmetry breaking.
* Including pi-K, pi-eta, and pi-N interactions reduces shear viscosity and significantly reduces the eta/s ratio compared to pure pion gas.
* Bulk viscosity is larger for the pi-K-eta-N mixture compared to pure pion gas due to additional conformal symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in hadronic matter, particularly the shear viscosity calculations using K-matrix cross sections in the Chapman-Enskog approximation by Wiranata (2013), and compares with results from chiral perturbation theory by Lang (2012), Fernandez-Fraile (2009), and Dobado (2011). The study also builds on calculations of thermal and electrical conductivities for pion gas by Ghosh (2015), Mitra (2014), Greif (2016), and others. The K-matrix approach enables the incorporation of multiple heavy resonances while preserving unitarity, which is important for accurate modeling of the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collision phenomenology, particularly for understanding the temperature dependence of transport coefficients near the hadron-to-quark-gluon plasma phase transition.
