# Enhanced nucleon transfer in tip collisions of $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn

> Multinucleon transfer processes in low-energy heavy ion reactions have attracted increasing interest in recent years aiming at production of new neutron-rich isotopes. Clearly, it is an imperative task to further develop understanding of underlying reaction mechanisms to lead experiments to success. In this paper, from systematic time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for the $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn reaction, it is demonstrated that transfer dynamics depend strongly on the orientations of $^{238}$U, quantum shells, and collision energies. Two important conclusions are obtained: (i) Experimentally observed many-proton transfer from $^{238}$U to $^{124}$Sn can be explained by a multinucleon transfer mechanism governed by enhanced neck evolution in tip collisions; (ii) Novel reaction dynamics are observed in tip collisions at energies substantially above the Coulomb barrier, where a number of nucleons are transferred from $^{124}$Sn to $^{238}$U, producing transuranium nuclei as primary reaction products, that could be a means to synthesize superheavy nuclei. Both results indicate the importance of the neck (shape) evolution dynamics, which are sensitive to the orientations, shell effects and collision energies, for exploring possible pathways to produce new unstable nuclei.

# Enhanced nucleon transfer in tip collisions of $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn

## Key claim

The experimentally observed many-proton transfer from $^{238}$U to $^{124}$Sn, which had been a mystery for over 30 years, can be explained by a multinucleon transfer mechanism governed by enhanced neck evolution in tip collisions of the deformed $^{238}$U nucleus, but only when the symmetry axis of $^{238}$U is aligned parallel to the collision axis; side collisions produce negligible transfer regardless of energy.

## Method

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations were performed for the $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn reaction using the Skyrme SLy5 energy density functional, with the deformed $^{238}$U nucleus (prolate, $\beta\simeq0.27$) placed in three initial orientations relative to the collision axis ($x$, $y$, and $z$ directions). Production cross sections for primary reaction products were computed using the particle-number projection method from TDHF wave functions, and secondary deexcitation was modeled by coupling TDHF with the statistical compound-nucleus code GEMINI++. Head-on and non-central collisions were simulated at various center-of-mass energies to map the energy and orientation dependence of nucleon transfer.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier heights from frozen Hartree-Fock: $V_{\rm B}^{\rm tip}\simeq410$ MeV for tip collisions and $V_{\rm B}^{\rm side}\simeq448$ MeV for side collisions, giving $E_{\rm c.m.}/V_{\rm B}^{\rm tip}\simeq1.13$ and $E_{\rm c.m.}/V_{\rm B}^{\rm side}\simeq1.04$ at the experimental energy $E_{\rm c.m.}\simeq465$ MeV.
- At $E_{\rm c.m.}\simeq465$ MeV in tip collisions, up to around 15 protons are transferred from $^{238}$U to $^{124}$Sn, consistent with the experimental data of Mayer (1985).
- In the plateau region ($442$ MeV $\lesssim E_{\rm c.m.}\lesssim 552$ MeV), tip collisions transfer about 10 neutrons and 6 protons on average from $^{238}$U to $^{124}$Sn, yielding average fragment numbers around ($N_{\rm H}\simeq136$, $Z_{\rm H}\simeq86$) and ($N_{\rm L}\simeq84$, $Z_{\rm L}\simeq56$).
- At $E_{\rm c.m.}\simeq736$ MeV in tip collisions, the direction of transfer reverses: up to 16 neutrons and 11 protons are transferred from $^{124}$Sn to $^{238}$U, producing primary fragments roughly corresponding to $^{93}_{38}$Sr$_{55}$ and $^{265}_{103}$Lr$_{162}$.
- The static Hartree-Fock ground state of $^{124}$Sn is oblate with $\beta\simeq0.11$, while $^{238}$U is prolate with $\beta\simeq0.27$.
- TDHF calculations used a 3D box of $56$ fm $\times$ $56$ fm $\times$ $24$ fm for non-central collisions ($b\le10$ fm) and $72$ fm $\times$ $32$ fm $\times$ $24$ fm for head-on collisions, with an 0.8-fm mesh spacing.

## Context

This work provides the first microscopic explanation for the many-proton transfer observed by Mayer (1985) at GSI in $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn dissipative collisions, which had lacked a clear theoretical explanation for over three decades. The results extend earlier TDHF studies of orientation effects in $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U by the same author and demonstrate that neck evolution dynamics, rather than simple nucleon exchange, govern multinucleon transfer. The finding of inverse quasifission at higher energies, where nucleons flow from the lighter to the heavier nucleus, differs from the shell-effect-driven transfer reported by Zagrebaev (2013) for $^{238}$U+$^{248}$Cm and by Kedziora (2010) for tip-on-side configurations in $^{232}$Th+$^{250}$Cf. The work enables future systematic TDHF surveys of projectile-target combinations and collision energies to identify pathways to neutron-rich heavy and superheavy nuclei, potentially accessing the island of stability through systems such as $^{186}$W+$^{248}$Cm.
