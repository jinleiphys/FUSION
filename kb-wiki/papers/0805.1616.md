# X(3872) and the bound state problem of $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ ($\bar{D}^0{D}^{\ast0}$) in a chiral quark model

> The bound state problem of $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ ($\bar{D}^0{D}^{\ast0}$) is relevant to the molecular interpretation of the X(3872). We investigated this problem in a chiral quark model by solving the resonating group method equation. We found the system is unbound through S-wave $蟺$ and $蟽$ interactions. The inclusion of $蟻$ and $蠅$ meson exchanges is helpful to the formation of a molecule. Because the binding energy relies on the coupling constants, we cannot draw a definite conclusion whether a molecular state exists in $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ ($\bar{D}^0{D}^{\ast0}$) system. When moving on to the bottom counterpart, we obtained an S-wave $B\bar{B}^\ast$ state.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ system is unbound when only S-wave $\pi$ and $\sigma$ exchange interactions are considered in a chiral quark model, but the inclusion of $\rho$ and $\omega$ meson exchanges can make a bound state possible; however, because the binding energy depends on the coupling constants, the authors cannot draw a definite conclusion whether a molecular state exists in this system.

## Method

The authors studied the $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ system in a chiral constituent quark model by solving the resonating group method (RGM) equation. They considered one-meson exchange interactions between light quarks in the two meson clusters, including $\pi$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ exchanges, and calculated the binding energy for S-wave interactions. The same framework was also applied to the bottom analog $B\bar{B}^\ast$ system.

## Key numbers

- For the $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ system with only $\pi$ and $\sigma$ exchange, no bound state was found (system is unbound) across all parameter combinations explored.
- For the $B\bar{B}^\ast$ system with $\pi$ and $\sigma$ exchange only, binding energies range from 0.5 MeV to 9.3 MeV depending on parameters (e.g., $m_b=4720$ MeV, $b_u=0.45$ fm, $m_\sigma=595$ MeV, $\Lambda=1000$ MeV gives $E_0=3.3$ MeV; $m_b=5259$ MeV, $b_u=0.45$ fm, $m_\sigma=535$ MeV, $\Lambda=1500$ MeV gives $E_0=9.3$ MeV).
- For the $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ system with vector meson exchanges included (extended chiral quark model, $f_{chv}/g_{chv}=0$, $b_u=0.45$ fm, $m_\sigma=535$ MeV), a bound state appears with $E_0=1.7$ MeV for $m_c=1870$ MeV and $\Lambda=1500$ MeV; other parameter combinations yield unbound results.
- For the $B\bar{B}^\ast$ system with vector meson exchanges included ($f_{chv}/g_{chv}=0$, $b_u=0.45$ fm, $m_\sigma=535$ MeV), binding energies range from 17.2 MeV to 23.1 MeV depending on $m_b$ and $\Lambda$ (e.g., $m_b=4720$ MeV, $\Lambda=1100$ MeV gives $E_0=17.2$ MeV; $m_b=5259$ MeV, $\Lambda=1500$ MeV gives $E_0=23.1$ MeV).
- For the symmetric wave function case (equal neutral and charged components) in the $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ system with vector meson exchanges, binding energies range from 4.5 MeV to 24.5 MeV (e.g., $m_c=1430$ MeV, $\Lambda=1100$ MeV, $f_{chv}/g_{chv}=0$ gives $E_0=12.1$ MeV; $m_c=1870$ MeV, $\Lambda=1500$ MeV, $f_{chv}/g_{chv}=0$ gives $E_0=24.5$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic-level studies by Liu (2008) that found $\pi$ and $\sigma$ exchange alone could not bind the $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ system, and contrasts with dynamical calculations by Swanson and Wong where binding was possible when short-range quark-gluon force was considered. The results enable future experimental searches for a $B\bar{B}^\ast$ molecular state at Tevatron or LHCb, and suggest that if the X(3872) is not a pure molecule, a mixing scheme of charmonium and molecular components may be needed to resolve its puzzles.
