# Axial $N\to Δ(1232)$ and $N \to N^{\star}(1440)$ transition form factors

> We calculate the axial $N\to Δ(1232)$ and $N\to N^{\star}(1440)$ transition form factors in a chiral constituent quark model. As required by the partial conservation of axial current ($PCAC$) condition, we include one- and two-body axial exchange currents. For the axial $N\to Δ(1232)$ form factors we compare with previous quark model calculations that use only one-body axial currents, and with experimental analyses. The paper provides the first calculation of all weak axial $N\to N^{\star}(1440)$ form factors. Our main result is that exchange currents are very important for certain axial transition form factors. In addition to improving our understanding of nucleon structure, the present results are relevant for neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section predictions needed in the analysis of neutrino mixing experiments.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first calculation of all weak axial \(N \to N^{\star}(1440)\) form factors, and demonstrates that two-body axial exchange currents (gluon, pion, and confinement currents) are essential for certain axial transition form factors, particularly \(C_3^A(0)\) and \(C_6^{A,\text{non-pole}}(0)\), which are otherwise near zero in impulse approximation; however, the model yields a \(C_5^A(0)\) value (0.93) that is smaller than the PCAC estimate of \(1.15 \pm 0.01\), implying a \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant only about 4/5 of the empirical value.

## Method

The authors calculate the axial \(N \to \Delta(1232)\) and \(N \to N^{\star}(1440)\) transition form factors within a chiral constituent quark model that includes a confinement potential, one-gluon exchange, and one-pion exchange potentials. The axial current operator is constructed to satisfy the partial conservation of axial current (PCAC) condition by including both one-body (impulse approximation) and two-body exchange currents (gluon, pion, and confinement). Wave functions for the nucleon, \(\Delta(1232)\), and \(N^{\star}(1440)\) are expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis up to \(N=2\) excitation quanta, with mixing coefficients determined by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian; two confinement models (color-screened and quadratic with anharmonic terms) are used to check sensitivity.

## Key numbers

*   \(C_5^A(0) = 0.93\) for the \(N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition (model A, total), compared to the PCAC estimate of \(1.15 \pm 0.01\) from the empirical \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant \(g_{\pi N\Delta}(q^2=m_\pi^2) = 28.6 \pm 0.3\) and \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.
*   \(C_3^A(0) = 0.035\) for the \(N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition (model A, total), dominated by the pion exchange current contribution of 0.049, while the impulse approximation gives \(-0.0068\).
*   \(C_6^{A,\text{non-pole}}(0) = -0.72\) for the \(N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition (model A, total), dominated by the confinement current contribution of \(-0.73\), while the impulse approximation gives 0.033.
*   The axial mass parameter from experimental fits is \(M_A = 1.28^{+0.08}_{-0.10}\) GeV, and the axial \(N \to \Delta\) transition radius \(r_A^2\) lies in the range \(0.394 \sim 0.477\) fm\(^2\).
*   From the model's \(C_5^A(0)\), the extracted \(g_{\pi N\Delta}(0) = 22.2\), implying a \(\Delta(1232)\) width \(\Gamma_\Delta \approx 70\) MeV, about 60% of the experimental width of \(\approx 120\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model calculations of elastic axial nucleon form factors (David and Hernandez, 2005) and axial couplings related to nucleon spin (David and Hernandez, 2006) to inelastic transitions to the \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N^{\star}(1440)\) resonances. It contrasts with earlier quark model calculations (Isgur and Karl, 1980; and others) that used only one-body axial currents and violated PCAC, and with the D-mixing model (Isgur and Karl, 1980) that employed large D-state admixtures to mimic exchange current effects. The results are relevant for neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section predictions needed in neutrino oscillation experiments, and the first calculation of all three axial \(N \to N^{\star}(1440)\) form factors enables future experimental searches for these weak transitions.
