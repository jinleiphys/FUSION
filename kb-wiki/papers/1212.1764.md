# The black disk and the dip in the differential elastic cross section at asymptotic energy

> We test the validity of the black disk limit in elastic scattering by studying the evolution of the dip in the scaling variable $τ=-t_{D}σ^{tot}$, where $t_{D}$ is the transverse momentum squared at the dip and $σ_{tot}$ the total cross section. As $s\rightarrow \infty $ and $-t_{D} \rightarrow 0 $, $τ$ may consistently be approaching the black disc value, $τ\xrightarrow[ \sqrt{s}\rightarrow \infty ]{} τ_{BD}=35.92$ GeV$^{2}$ mb.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the dip in the differential elastic cross section, when expressed as the scaling variable τ = -t_D σ_tot, approaches the black disk value τ_BD = 35.92 GeV² mb as energy increases; the authors find that τ may consistently be approaching this limit as s → ∞ and -t_D → 0, but the approach is slow and asymptotic energies have not yet been reached.

## Method

The authors introduce a grey disk approximation with an energy-dependent function f(s) describing unitarity saturation, where f(s) → 1 in the black disk limit. They use a parametrization for the opacity Ω̄(s) = 2(γ₁ + γ₂ ln(s) + γ₃ ln²(s)) with specific coefficients, and model the dip position -t_D as a function of energy using σ_tot(s) = 2πR²(s)f(s) and a parameter α. They fit the parameter α to experimental data from LHC (pp at 7 TeV) and Auger Observatory (pAir at 57 TeV) to test whether geometric scaling holds at all energies or only asymptotically.

## Key numbers

- The black disk value of the scaling variable is τ_BD = 35.92 GeV² mb.
- The opacity parametrization uses γ₁ = 0.29, γ₂ = 0.0191, γ₃ = 0.0013352.
- The fitted parameter α = 1.47, indicating geometric scaling is only asymptotic.
- The expected value of τ at √s = 14 TeV is τ_* = 44.9 GeV² mb.
- The ratio σ_inel/σ_tot at asymptotic energies is estimated as 0.509 ± 0.011.
- The radius parametrization uses R₀ = 0.0936 mb^(1/2) and √s₀ = 2.216 × 10⁻⁹ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the geometric scaling approach of Dias de Deus (1973) and others, which predicted a constant σ_el/σ_tot ratio that disagrees with observed growth at LHC energies. The authors introduce an energy-dependent opacity function f(s) to describe unitarity saturation, following earlier work by Brogueira and Dias de Deus (2011). The results enable tests of whether LHC and cosmic-ray data are approaching the black disk limits σ_el/σ_tot → 1/2, σ_tot/B(s) → 8π, and τ_DIP → 35.92 GeV² mb, while showing that asymptopia has not yet been reached.
