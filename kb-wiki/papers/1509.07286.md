# Double scattering production of two $ρ^0$ mesons in UPC

> We review our recent results for double-scattering mechanism in the exclusive $AA \to AA ρ^0ρ^0$ reaction in ultrarelativistic ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. The cross section for single and double-$ρ^0$ production is calculated in the impact parameter space equivalent photon approximation. We compare the results of our calculation with the STAR and ALICE Collaboration results on one $ρ^0(770)$ meson and four-charged-pion production.

# Double scattering production of two $\rho^0$ mesons in UPC

## Key claim
The double-scattering (photon-Pomeron) mechanism dominates over the two-photon fusion mechanism for exclusive $AA \to AA \rho^0\rho^0$ production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies, but this mechanism accounts for only about 20% of the four-charged-pion cross section measured by the STAR Collaboration, suggesting that other processes (such as decays of excited $\rho^0$ resonances) are the dominant contribution.

## Method
The authors calculate cross sections for single and double $\rho^0(770)$ meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions using the impact parameter space equivalent photon approximation. For single $\rho^0$ production, the cross section is expressed as a convolution of the photon flux (using nuclear form factors) with the $\gamma A \to \rho^0 A$ cross section, where the $\rho^0$–nucleus total cross section is computed using either classical or quantum mechanical Glauber formulas with parameters fitted to HERA data. The double $\rho^0$ production cross section via double scattering is obtained by integrating the product of single-production probability densities over impact parameter, with a factor of 1/2 for identical mesons, and the $\rho^0$ mass is smeared using the ALICE parametrization.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV), the double-scattering contribution to four-pion production accounts for only 20% of the cross section measured by the STAR Collaboration (with acceptance $|\eta_\pi| < 1$).
- At the LHC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV), the VDM-Regge component of the $\gamma\gamma$ fusion elementary cross section is about three orders of magnitude larger than the low-energy component, opposite to the case at RHIC energy.
- The $\gamma\gamma$ fusion contribution is one order of magnitude smaller than the double-scattering mechanism at both RHIC and LHC energies.
- The rapidity distribution for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV is much broader than that for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations of double photoproduction (DS_2014) and photon-photon fusion (gamgam_rho0rho0) for exclusive vector meson pair production in ultraperipheral collisions. The single $\rho^0$ production cross sections are compared with experimental results from the STAR Collaboration (STAR) and ALICE Collaboration (rho_smearing), using theoretical approaches from KN1999, GM, and FSZ. The results show that while the double-scattering mechanism is the dominant production channel for $\rho^0\rho^0$ pairs, it cannot explain the full four-pion yield observed by STAR, pointing to the need for future models of $\rho^0(1450)$ and $\rho^0(1700)$ resonance production and their decay into four charged pions.
