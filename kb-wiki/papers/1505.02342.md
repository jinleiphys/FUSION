# Studying re-scattering effect in heavy-ion collision through $K^*$ Production

> We have studied the $K^*$ production within A Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV to understand the hadronic re-scattering effect on the measured yields of the resonance. The hadronic re-scattering of the $K^*$ decay daughter particles ($π$ and K) will alter their momentum distribution thereby making it difficult to reconstruct the $K^*$ signal through the invariant mass method. An increased hadronic re-scattering effect thus leads to a decrease in the reconstructed yield of $K^*$ in heavy-ion collisions. Through this simulation study we argue that a decrease in $K^*$/K ratio with increase in collision centrality necessarily reflects the hadronic re-scattering effect. Since the re-scattering occurs in the hadronic phase and $K^*$ has a lifetime of 4 fm/c, we present a toy model based discussion on using measured $K^*$/K to put a lower limit on the hadronic phase lifetime in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a decrease in the measured $K^{*}/K$ ratio with increasing collision centrality necessarily reflects hadronic re-scattering effects, and that this ratio can be used to set a lower limit on the hadronic phase lifetime, assuming no regeneration occurs between chemical and kinetic freeze-out.

## Method
The authors used the default version of the A Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT, version 1.25t9b) to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. They varied the hadronic cascade termination time from 0.6 fm/$c$ to 30 fm/$c$ to control the amount of hadronic re-scattering, tracking $K^{*}$ production, decay, and the momentum distributions of daughter $\pi$ and $K$ mesons. Approximately 50,000 events were generated for each configuration for minimum bias (0-80% centrality) collisions, with results presented for rapidity $\mid y \mid < 0.5$.

## Key numbers
- $K^{*}$ lifetime used in the analysis: 4 fm/$c$
- Hadronic cascade termination times varied from 0.6 fm/$c$ to 30 fm/$c$
- For central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, the lower limit on the time between chemical and kinetic freeze-out is about 3.7 fm/$c$
- The $[K^{*}/K^{-}]_{chemical}$ value taken from p+p data is 0.36
- Lorentz boost factors for $\Delta t$ estimates range from 1.38 to 1.57

## Context
This work extends transport model studies of resonance production in heavy-ion collisions, specifically following earlier AMPT calculations by Lin (2001) that predicted both re-scattering and regeneration effects. The study provides a simulation-based interpretation of experimental $K^{*0}/K^{-}$ data from STAR (Aggarwal 2010) and other collaborations, showing that the observed centrality-dependent suppression relative to p+p collisions indicates dominance of re-scattering over regeneration. The toy model for extracting hadronic phase lifetime builds on the framework of Rapp (2000) and Broniowski (2003), and the resulting lower limit of 3.7 fm/$c$ for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is consistent with other estimates from Adams (2003).
