# Flavor decomposition of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors at low $Q^2$

> The spatial distribution of charge and magnetization within the proton is encoded in the elastic form factors. These have been precisely measured in elastic electron scattering, and the combination of proton and neutron form factors allows for the separation of the up- and down-quark contributions. In this work, we extract the proton and neutron form factors from world's data with an emphasis on precise new data covering the low-momentum region, which is sensitive to the large-scale structure of the nucleon. From these, we separate the up- and down-quark contributions to the proton form factors. We combine cross section and polarization measurements of elastic electron-proton scattering to separate the proton form factors and two-photon exchange (TPE) contributions. We combine the proton form factors with parameterization of the neutron form factor data and uncertainties to separate the up- and down-quark contributions to the proton's charge and magnetic form factors. The extracted TPE corrections are compared to previous phenomenological extractions, TPE calculations, and direct measurements from the comparison of electron and positron scattering. The flavor-separated form factors are extracted and compared to models of the nucleon structure. With the inclusion of the precise new data, the extracted TPE contributions show a clear change ofsign at low $Q^2$, necessary to explain the high-$Q^2$ form factor discrepancy while being consistent with the known $Q^2 \to 0$ limit. We find that the new Mainz data yield a significantly different result for the proton magnetic form factor and its flavor-separated contributions. We also observe that the RMS radius of both the up- and down-quark distributions are smaller than the RMS charge radius of the proton.

## Key claim

The extracted two-photon exchange (TPE) contributions show a clear change of sign at low $Q^2$, which is necessary to explain the high-$Q^2$ form factor discrepancy while remaining consistent with the known $Q^2 \to 0$ limit; this result depends on the inclusion of precise new low-$Q^2$ data from Mainz and an improved parameterization of the polarization-transfer ratio.

## Method

The authors combined unpolarized cross section (Rosenbluth separation) and recoil polarization measurements of elastic electron-proton scattering to simultaneously extract the proton electric and magnetic form factors and the two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution, using the Borisyuk-Kobushkin parametrization. They then combined the extracted proton form factors with a parametrization of neutron form factor data and uncertainties to separate the up- and down-quark contributions to the proton's charge and magnetic form factors via isospin relations, assuming charge symmetry and neglecting strange quarks.

## Key numbers

- The new fit to the recoil-polarization ratio $\mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p$ is given by $R = 1/(1 + 0.1430 Q^2 - 0.0086 Q^4 + 0.0072 Q^6)$, with $Q^2$ in (GeV/c)$^2$, and absolute uncertainty $\delta^2_R(Q^2) = (0.006)^2 + (0.015\ln(1+Q^2))^2$.
- The extracted TPE term is parametrized as $a(Q^2) = 0.016 - 0.030 \sqrt{Q^2}$, with $Q^2$ in (GeV/c)$^2$.
- At low $Q^2$, the Mainz data yield values of $G_M^p$ that are systematically 2--5% larger than previous world data.
- The proton RMS charge radius from electron scattering is $r_E \approx 0.88$ fm; the dipole form corresponds to an RMS radius of 0.811 fm, suggesting an RMS radius of approximately 0.81 fm for the up-quark distribution and below 0.81 fm for the down-quark distribution.
- In the $Q^2 = 0$ limit, the flavor-separated form factors yield $G_E^u = 2$, $G_E^d = 1$, $G_M^u = (2\mu_p + \mu_n) = 3.67 \mu_N$, and $G_M^d = (\mu_p + 2\mu_n) = -1.03 \mu_N$, where $\mu_N$ is the nuclear magneton.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Qattan and Arrington (2012) to lower $Q^2$ values by including new high-precision cross section data from Mainz (Bernauer, 2010) and an improved parametrization of polarization data from multiple experiments including Gayou (2001), Puckett (2012), and Zhan (2011). The extracted TPE contributions are compared to hadronic TPE calculations by Blunden (2005) and Arrington (2007), as well as to direct measurements from electron-positron scattering comparisons by Adikaram (2014) and Rachek (2014). The flavor-separated form factors are compared to the earlier CJRW analysis by Cates (2011), which neglected TPE effects, and to models including Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations by Cloet (2009), pion-cloud relativistic constituent quark models by Cloet (2012), and generalized parton distribution models by Gonzalez (2013). The results enable more precise tests of nucleon structure models at large distances and provide constraints on the spatial distributions of up and down quarks within the proton.
