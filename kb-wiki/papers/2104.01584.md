# Scaling with deformation in probable $p$-wave halo $^{34}$Na

> We investigate the electric dipole response of $^{34}$Na, a probable $p$-wave one-neutron halo nucleus, lying in the "island of inversion" and having a deformed structure. We use a semi-analytic approach to probe the dipole response and further compare the results obtained from a post form finite-range distorted wave Born approximation theory of Coulomb breakup. The effects of deformation are figured out on the peak positions of the electric dipole strength distribution which further constraint the one-neutron separation energy of the deformed projectile and it leads to a two-dimensional scaling of total $B(E1)$ strength with parameters: separation energy and deformation.

# Scaling with deformation in probable $p$-wave halo $^{34}$Na

## Key claim
The electric dipole response of the deformed one-neutron halo candidate $^{34}$Na shows a two-dimensional scaling with both one-neutron separation energy and quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$, but the semi-analytic approach yields dipole strength distributions that are only weakly dependent on deformation, whereas the finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory shows a clear sensitivity to $\beta_2$, especially near the peak positions.

## Method
The authors used two theoretical approaches to compute the electric dipole response of $^{34}$Na. The first is a semi-analytic (SA) method in which the bound state wave function is obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with an axially symmetric quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potential, and the continuum wave function is replaced by its asymptotic form (spherical Bessel function). The second is a post-form finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory of Coulomb breakup, where deformation enters via the deformed Woods-Saxon potential $V_{dn}$ between the core ($^{33}$Na) and the valence neutron. Both methods were applied to the reaction $^{34}$Na + $^{208}$Pb at 100 MeV/u beam energy, with the valence neutron assumed to occupy the $2p_{3/2}$ orbital and a one-neutron separation energy $S_n = 0.17$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- One-neutron separation energy of $^{34}$Na: $S_n = 0.17$ MeV (taken from Gaud12).
- Beam energy for Coulomb breakup on $^{208}$Pb target: 100 MeV/u.
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: radius $r_0 = 1.24$ fm, diffuseness $a_0 = 0.62$ fm, spin-orbit strength $V_s = 15$ MeV.
- Total integrated $B(E1)$ values from the SA approach: 1.00 $e^2$fm$^2$ for $\beta_2 = 0.1$, 0.3, and 0.5 (no variation with deformation).
- Total integrated $B(E1)$ values from the FRDWBA approach: 1.25 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $\beta_2 = 0.1$, 1.42 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $\beta_2 = 0.3$, and 1.73 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $\beta_2 = 0.5$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Manju, 2019) of electric dipole responses of medium-mass $p$-wave halos $^{31}$Ne, $^{34}$Na, and $^{37}$Mg, which assumed spherical projectiles. The present paper focuses specifically on $^{34}$Na, a nucleus in the "island of inversion" around $N=20$ with known deformed structure (Singh, 2016). The results enable future investigations of other deformed $p$-wave halo systems such as $^{31}$Ne and $^{37}$Mg, as well as additional observables including asymptotic normalization coefficients, root-mean-square radii, and sum rules at finite deformation. The discrepancy between the SA and FRDWBA results regarding deformation sensitivity requires further investigation.
