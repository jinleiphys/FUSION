# Three-dimensional correlated-fermion phase separation from analysis of the geometric mean of the individual susceptibilities

> A quasi-Gaussian approximation scheme is formulated to study the strongly correlated imbalanced fermions thermodynamics, where the mean-field theory is not applicable. The non-Gaussian correlation effects are understood to be captured by the statistical geometric mean of the individual susceptibilities. In the three-dimensional unitary fermions ground state, an {\em universal} non-linear scaling transformation relates the physical chemical potentials with the individual Fermi kinetic energies. For the partial polarization phase separation to full polarization, the calculated critical polarization ratio is $P_C={[1-(1-ξ)^{6/5}]}/{[1+(1-ξ)^{6/5}]}\doteq 0.34$. The $ξ=4/9$ defines the ratio of the symmetric ground state energy density to that of the ideal fermion gas.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the non-Gaussian correlation effects in strongly interacting imbalanced fermions can be captured by the statistical geometric mean of the individual susceptibilities, and that this approach yields a critical polarization ratio of approximately 0.34 for the transition from partial to full polarization at unitarity, which agrees with Monte Carlo calculations (0.39) and experimental measurements (0.36) but differs significantly from the mean-field value (0.93).

## Method

The authors formulate a quasi-Gaussian approximation scheme using a medium-scaling Hamiltonian with a contact interaction renormalized by the geometric mean of the individual spin-component susceptibilities. The thermodynamic potential is derived from coupled parametric equations involving effective chemical potentials and the in-medium scattering length, with the non-linear correlation effects encoded in the geometric mean of the Lindhard response functions. At zero temperature and unitarity (infinite scattering length), the formalism reduces to a compact set of equations relating physical chemical potentials to Fermi kinetic energies through a universal non-linear scaling transformation.

## Key numbers

- Critical polarization ratio for partial-to-full polarization phase separation: \(P_C = 0.34\), calculated from \(P_C = [1-(1-\xi)^{6/5}]/[1+(1-\xi)^{6/5}]\) with \(\xi = 4/9\)
- The universal constant \(\xi = 4/9\) defines the ratio of the symmetric ground state energy density to that of the ideal noninteracting fermion gas at zero temperature
- Mean-field theory gives \(P_C \approx 0.93\), while the experimental value is \(P_C \approx 0.36\) and Monte Carlo results give \(P_C \approx 0.39\)
- The critical concentration \(x_C \doteq 0.49\) corresponds to the minimum of the scaling function \(g(x)\)

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Chen (2007, 2008) for strongly correlated fermions and addresses the unresolved problem of the three-dimensional asymmetric fermions ground state at unitarity, where mean-field theory fails and Monte Carlo methods suffer from the fermion sign problem as noted by Braaten (2008). The calculated relation between the critical polarization ratio \(P_C\) and the universal constant \(\xi\) agrees with Monte Carlo calculations by Lobo (2006) and Pilati (2008), as well as experimental measurements by Yong (2007), and provides an analytical framework for understanding the quantum phase separation dynamics in trapped fermion systems.
