# Extraction of P11 Resonance from pi-N Data and Its Stability

> An important question about resonance extraction is how much resonance poles and residues extracted from data depend on a model used for the extraction, and on the precision of data. We address this question with the dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) model developed in Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at JLab. We focus on the P11 pi-N scattering. We examine the model-dependence of the poles by varying parameters to a large extent within the EBAC-DCC model. We find that two poles associated with the Roper resonance are fairly stable against the variation. We also develop a model with a bare nucleon, thereby examining the stability of the Roper poles against different analytic structure of the P11 amplitude below pi-N threshold. We again find a good stability of the Roper poles.

## Key claim

The two poles associated with the Roper resonance in the P11 pi-N partial wave are fairly stable against large variations of parameters within the EBAC-DCC model, and also against using a model with a bare nucleon that has a different analytic structure below the pi-N threshold, provided the data below W = 1.5 GeV are well reproduced.

## Method

The authors used the dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) model developed at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at JLab, which includes pi-N, eta-N, and pi-pi-N channels (with pi-Delta, rho-N, and sigma-N components). They varied the parameters of the meson-exchange mechanisms and bare N* states to a large extent, and also developed a separate model with a bare nucleon state, fitting each to the SAID single-energy solution (SAID-SES) and in one case to the CMB solution. Resonance poles were then extracted via analytic continuation to the unphysical sheets of the Riemann surface.

## Key numbers

* The two Roper poles from the JLMS fit are at (1357, 76) MeV and (1364, 105) MeV (Re M_R, -Im M_R) on the (upuupp) and (upuppp) sheets, respectively.
* The 2N*-3p fit gave Roper poles at (1368, 82) MeV and (1375, 110) MeV, with a chi-squared per data point of 3.28.
* The 2N*-4p fit gave Roper poles at (1372, 80) MeV and (1385, 114) MeV, plus an additional pole at (1636, 67) MeV, with chi-squared per data point of 3.36.
* The bare nucleon model (1N0 1N*-3p) gave Roper poles at (1363, 81) MeV and (1377, 128) MeV, with chi-squared per data point of 2.51.
* The fit to the CMB solution (2N*-4p-CMB) gave Roper poles at (1379, 89) MeV and (1386, 109) MeV, with chi-squared per data point of 4.91.

## Context

This work extends the EBAC group's prior extraction of P11 poles from the JLMS fit (Nakamura et al., 2010) by systematically testing the stability of those poles against model variations. It addresses a long-standing question about how much extracted resonance parameters depend on the reaction model used, and it contrasts with other analyses that reported only a single pole in the Roper region. The results enable more confident use of the two-pole structure of the Roper resonance for comparisons with lattice QCD and other theoretical approaches, while also highlighting the need for more precise data above W = 1.5 GeV to resolve higher-mass pole structures.
