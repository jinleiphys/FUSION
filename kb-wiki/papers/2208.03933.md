# Reconciling Multi-messenger Constraints with Chiral Symmetry Restoration

> We consider the parity doublet model for nucleonic and delta matter to investigate the structure of neutron stars. We show that it is possible to reconcile the multi-messenger astronomy constraints within a purely hadronic equation of state (EOS), which accounts for the self-consistent treatment of the chiral symmetry restoration in the baryonic sector. We demonstrate that the characteristics of the EOS required by the astrophysical constraints do not necessarily imply the existence of a hadron-quark phase transition in the stellar core.

## Key claim

The paper shows that multi-messenger constraints on neutron star properties can be satisfied by a purely hadronic equation of state that includes chiral symmetry restoration in the baryonic sector, without requiring a hadron-quark phase transition. The key hedge is that the characteristic softening and subsequent stiffening of the equation of state, which resembles signals often attributed to deconfinement, can instead arise from the in-medium mass reduction of negative-parity baryon states within the parity doublet model.

## Method

The authors employ the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation for nucleonic and delta matter, including both positive-parity states (N(938), Δ(1232)) and their negative-parity chiral partners (N(1535), Δ(1700)). They solve the thermodynamic potential under neutron star conditions for varying chirally invariant mass parameters m₀^N and m₀^Δ, then compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities to compare against constraints from the GW170817 event and the 2 M⊙ pulsar measurements.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass constraint: M_max = (2.08 ± 0.07) M⊙ from Fonseca (2021)
- Tidal deformability constraint for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: Λ₁.₄ = 190^{+390}_{-120} from the LIGO-VIRGO Collaboration (2018)
- Chirally invariant mass parameters explored: m₀^N = 550, 600, 650, 700 MeV
- Energy density range for the deconfinement transition estimate from lattice QCD: ε_QGP ≈ 400–700 MeV/fm³ from Bazavov (2014)
- Polytropic index threshold for quark matter onset from Annala (2019): γ < 1.75

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model studies of Marczenko (2021, 2022) and Takeda (2017) to simultaneously satisfy modern multi-messenger constraints. It directly contradicts the interpretation of Annala (2019) that a drop in the polytropic index below 1.75 signals deconfined quark matter, showing instead that chiral symmetry restoration in purely hadronic matter produces the same signature. The results enable future work linking neutron star observations to fundamental QCD properties such as chiral symmetry restoration and the emergence of conformal matter, as noted by Marczenko (2022).
