# New Results in the CBF theory for medium-heavy nuclei

> Momentum distributions, spectroscopic factors and quasi-hole wave functions of medium-heavy doubly closed shell nuclei have been calculated in the framework of the Correlated Basis Function theory, by using the Fermi hypernetted chain resummation techniques. The calculations have been done by using microscopic two-body nucleon-nucleon potentials of Argonne type, together with three-body interactions. Operator dependent correlations, up to the tensor channels, have been used.

## Key claim

The Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory, using the Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain (FHNC/SOC) approximation with operator-dependent correlations up to tensor channels, can reproduce experimental spectroscopic factors for medium-heavy doubly closed shell nuclei, though the agreement is better for deeply bound shells than for levels near the Fermi surface, likely due to long-range correlations not treated in this approach.

## Method

The authors solved the many-body Schrödinger equation variationally using correlated wave functions of the form Ψ = FΦ, where F is a many-body correlation operator and Φ is a Slater determinant in a jj coupling scheme. They used the Argonne v'8 two-nucleon potential together with the Urbana XI three-body force, and solved the FHNC equations in the SOC approximation to compute the one-body density matrix, momentum distributions, quasi-hole wave functions, and spectroscopic factors for five doubly closed shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Proton momentum distributions for ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb were calculated; correlated distributions show high-momentum components "orders of magnitude larger" than independent particle model (IPM) predictions
- Spectroscopic factors for proton bound states of ²⁰⁸Pb were compared with experimental data from van01t; agreement is better for deeply bound shells than for levels closer to the Fermi surface
- The squared quasi-hole wave function for the 3s₁/₂ proton state of ²⁰⁸Pb shows progressive depletion at the nuclear center as correlations are added: IPM (uncorrelated), scalar-only (Jastrow), four-channel operatorial (f⁴), and full six-channel including tensor (f⁶)

## Context

This work extends the CBF approach previously applied to nuclear and neutron matter (Wir88, Akm98) to finite medium-heavy nuclei, building on earlier calculations of binding energies and charge distributions for the same nuclei (Bis06). The FHNC/SOC computational scheme was extended to treat different proton and neutron numbers and to include antiparallel spin terms. The results enable future studies of long-range correlations (coupling to low-lying surface vibrations) which are expected to improve agreement for states near the Fermi surface, and provide microscopic input for analyses of electron scattering and knockout reactions.
