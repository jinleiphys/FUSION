# Examining the $N$ = 28 shell closure through high-precision mass measurements of $^{46-48}$Ar

> The strength of the $N$ = 28 magic number in neutron-rich argon isotopes is examined through high-precision mass measurements of $^{46-48}$Ar, performed with the ISOLTRAP mass spectrometer at ISOLDE/CERN. The new mass values are up to 90 times more precise than previous measurements. While they suggest the persistence of the $N$ = 28 shell closure for argon, we show that this conclusion has to be nuanced in light of the wealth of spectroscopic data and theoretical investigations performed with the \emph{SDPF-U} phenomenological shell model interaction. Our results are also compared with \emph{ab initio} calculations using the Valence Space In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group and the Self-Consistent Green's Function approaches. Both calculations provide a very good account of mass systematics at and around $Z$ = 18 and, generally, a consistent description of the physics in this region. This combined analysis indicates that $^{46}$Ar is the transition between the closed-shell $^{48}$Ca and collective $^{44}$S.

## Key claim

High-precision mass measurements of $^{46-48}$Ar suggest the persistence of the $N=28$ shell closure for argon, but this conclusion must be nuanced when combined with spectroscopic data and theoretical calculations; the combined analysis indicates that $^{46}$Ar is the transition between the closed-shell $^{48}$Ca and collective $^{44}$S.

## Method

The masses of $^{46-48}$Ar were measured using the ISOLTRAP mass spectrometer at ISOLDE/CERN. For $^{46}$Ar and $^{47}$Ar, the Time-of-Flight Ion-Cyclotron-Resonance (ToF-ICR) technique in a Penning trap was used, including Ramsey-type excitation for $^{47}$Ar; for $^{48}$Ar, the Multi-Reflection Time-of-Flight Mass Separator (MR-ToF MS) was employed as a mass spectrometer due to low yield. The new mass values were compared with phenomenological shell-model calculations using the SDPF-U interaction and with ab initio calculations using the Valence Space In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (VS-IMSRG) and Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) approaches.

## Key numbers

- $^{46}$Ar mass excess: -29771.3(23) keV, 20 times more precise than the AME2012 value of -29730(40) keV
- $^{47}$Ar mass excess: -25367.3(12) keV, approximately 90-fold improvement in precision over the AME2012 value of -25210(90) keV, and 157 keV more bound
- $^{48}$Ar mass excess: -22355(17) keV, compared to the AME2016 value of -22280(310) keV
- Charge-exchange half-life of $^{38}$Ar$^{+}$ in the RFQ cooler-buncher: initially 23(2) ms, improving to 50(13) ms after liquid-nitrogen cold trap installation
- Charge-exchange half-life of $^{36}$Ar$^{+}$ in the RFQ cooler-buncher during 2015 run: 33(5) ms

## Context

This work extends previous mass measurements of neutron-rich argon isotopes performed using the S800 spectrometer at NSCL (Meisel 2015) and earlier Q-value measurements from transfer reactions, providing dramatically improved precision. The results are compared with SDPF-U shell-model calculations (Caurier 2014) and ab initio VS-IMSRG and SCGF calculations, testing their ability to describe the evolution of the $N=28$ shell closure below calcium. The combined analysis enables a refined understanding of the transition from spherical to collective behavior in the $N=28$ region, positioning $^{46}$Ar as the transitional nucleus between the closed-shell $^{48}$Ca and the collective $^{44}$S.
