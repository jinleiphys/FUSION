# Analysis of $π$d elastic scattering data to 500 MeV

> An energy-dependent and a set of single-energy partial-wave analyses of $π$d elastic scattering data have been completed. Amplitudes are presented for pion laboratory kinetic energies up to 500~MeV. These results are compared with those found in other recent analyses. We comment on the present database and make suggestions for future work. ( Figures are available from the authors upon request. )

## Key claim

The authors present energy-dependent and single-energy partial-wave analyses of πd elastic scattering data up to 500 MeV pion laboratory kinetic energy, but caution that results above 300 MeV should not be taken too seriously because only about 200 measurements cover the 300–500 MeV region.

## Method

The energy-dependent fit used a coupled-channel K-matrix form to maintain unitarity, with 21 searched partial waves (J ≤ 5) and 66 varied parameters, expanding matrix elements as polynomials in pion laboratory energy with appropriate phase-space factors. Single-energy solutions were produced up to 300 MeV using a binning width of 10 ± 5 MeV, with starting values and fixed energy derivatives taken from the energy-dependent fit and constraint errors of 0.02 added in quadrature to 5% of each amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Total database: 1362 experimental data points, including 57 π⁺d and 67 π⁻d total cross sections, 516 π⁺d and 236 π⁻d differential cross sections, 280 π⁺d and 5 π⁻d deuteron vector analyzing-power (iT₁₁) measurements, 42 π⁺d deuteron tensor analyzing-power (T₂₀) measurements, 123 combined tensor analyzing-powers (τ₂₁ and τ₂₂) for π⁺d, 30 laboratory deuteron tensor analyzing-power (t₂₀^lab) for π⁺d, and 6 unpolarized total elastic cross sections.
- The energy-dependent solution gave χ² = 2743 for 1362 data and 333 experiments below 500 MeV.
- 9% of the total dataset was removed (134 points: 2 σ_T, 88 dσ/dΩ, 3 σ_T^el, 6 iT₁₁, and 35 t₂₀^lab) due to serious conflicts within the database.
- χ²/datum was 1.86 for π⁺d versus 2.55 for π⁻d.
- The analysis covers √s from 2.015 GeV to 2.437 GeV, corresponding to pion laboratory kinetic energies from 287.5 MeV to 1287 MeV in the pp system.

## Context

This work extends previous partial-wave analyses of πd elastic scattering by the Grenoble-Rehovot group (Arvieux and Rinat, 1980), the Osaka group (Hiroshige et al., 1982, 1984, 1990), the Saskatoon group (Stevenson and Shin, 1987), and the Mexico-Karlsruhe group (Garcilazo et al., 1989), using a significantly larger database covering a broader energy interval up to 500 MeV. The results enable future coupled-channel analyses combining pp elastic scattering, πd elastic scattering, and the πd → pp reaction to investigate the dibaryon resonance question, and the amplitudes are now incorporated into the SAID program for interactive use.
