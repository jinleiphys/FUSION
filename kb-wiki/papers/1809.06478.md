# Weak radius of the proton

> The weak charge of the proton determines its coupling to the $Z^0$ boson. The distribution of weak charge is found to be dramatically different from the distribution of electric charge. The proton's weak radius $R_W= 1.545\pm 0.017$ fm is 80\% larger than its charge radius $R_{ch}\approx 0.84$ fm because of a very large pion cloud contribution. This large weak radius can be measured with parity violating electron scattering and may provide insight into the structure of the proton, various radiative corrections, and possible strange quark contributions.

# Weak radius of the proton

## Key claim

The weak charge distribution of the proton is dramatically different from its electric charge distribution, with the weak radius \(R_W = 1.545 \pm 0.017\) fm being approximately 80% larger than the charge radius \(R_{ch} \approx 0.84\) fm, primarily due to a very large pion cloud contribution. This result depends on using the smaller charge radius from muonic hydrogen (0.8418 fm) and the lattice QCD value for the strangeness radius; using the CODATA charge radius (0.877 fm) yields \(R_W = 1.564 \pm 0.017\) fm instead.

## Method

The weak radius \(R_W\) is derived from the relation \(R_W^2 = R_{ch}^2 + (Q_n/Q_p) R_n^2 - (1/Q_p) R_s^2\), where \(Q_p\) and \(Q_n\) are the proton and neutron weak charges, \(R_n^2\) is the neutron charge radius squared, and \(R_s^2\) is the strangeness radius. The calculation uses standard model values for the weak charges (\(Q_p = 0.0710\), \(Q_n = -0.9902\)), the lattice QCD value for \(R_s^2 = -0.0054 \pm 0.0016\) fm\(^2\), and the neutron charge radius squared \(R_n^2 = -0.1148 \pm 0.0035\) fm\(^2\). The weak charge density is also examined via three-dimensional Fourier transforms of the weak form factor and via transverse densities.

## Key numbers

- Proton weak radius \(R_W = 1.545 \pm 0.017\) fm (using muonic hydrogen charge radius \(R_{ch} = 0.8418 \pm 0.0007\) fm)
- Proton weak radius \(R_W = 1.564 \pm 0.017\) fm (using CODATA charge radius \(R_{ch} = 0.877 \pm 0.007\) fm)
- Weak skin \(\Delta R = R_W - R_{ch} = 0.703 \pm 0.017\) fm (muonic hydrogen case) or \(0.687 \pm 0.017\) fm (CODATA case)
- Neutron weak radius \(R_W^n = 0.8434 \pm 0.0012\) fm
- Neutron charge radius squared \(R_n^2 = -0.1148 \pm 0.0035\) fm\(^2\)
- Strangeness radius from lattice QCD: \(R_s^2 = -0.0054 \pm 0.0016\) fm\(^2\)
- Strangeness radius from Qweak collaboration: \(R_s^2 = -0.013 \pm 0.007\) fm\(^2\)
- Proton weak charge measured by Qweak: \(Q_p = 0.0719 \pm 0.0045\)
- Proton weak charge using lattice QCD constraint: \(Q_p = 0.0685 \pm 0.0038\)
- Weak radius of \(^{208}\)Pb: \(5.826 \pm 0.181\) fm (from PREX experiment)

## Context

This work extends the understanding of proton structure beyond the traditional electric and magnetic form factors studied since Hofstadter (1950s) and the Jefferson Lab experiments on magnetization distributions. It directly addresses the proton radius puzzle arising from the muonic hydrogen measurement by Pohl et al. (2010) that gave a smaller charge radius than CODATA values. The calculation shows that the weak radius is dominated by the neutron charge radius and the large ratio of neutron to proton weak charges, and it enables future parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as P2 at Mainz to measure \(R_W\) directly, providing insight into up and down quark distributions, radiative corrections, and strange quark contributions. The weak skin of the proton is contrasted with the neutron skin in heavy nuclei measured by the PREX experiment on \(^{208}\)Pb, and with expected measurements from CREX on \(^{48}\)Ca.
