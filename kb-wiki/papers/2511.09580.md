# Fermi-Dirac Wigner function for massive spin-1/2 particles in local equilibrium

> A recently proposed Boltzmann local equilibrium Wigner function for massive spin-1/2 particles is generalized to the case of Fermi-Dirac statistics. The resulting formula ensures the correct normalization of the mean polarization vector and reproduces the generalized thermodynamic relations with spin that were obtained in earlier studies. Moreover, we show that the macroscopic currents constructed from the Fermi-Dirac Wigner function can be obtained as derivatives of a suitably defined generating function with respect to the Lagrange multipliers (temperature, hydrodynamic flow, and chemical potentials). The identified generating function also indicates that the underlying framework can be classified as a divergence-type theory.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes a previously proposed Boltzmann local equilibrium Wigner function for massive spin-1/2 particles to the case of Fermi-Dirac statistics, showing that the resulting formula ensures correct normalization of the mean polarization vector and reproduces generalized thermodynamic relations with spin, but only under the condition that the same convergence criterion derived for the Boltzmann case applies because the Fermi-Dirac distribution reduces to the Boltzmann one at high momentum.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann local equilibrium Wigner function for spin-1/2 particles and replace the Boltzmann distribution functions in the spin-extended phase space with Fermi-Dirac distribution functions of the form g_FD^{±±}(x,p) = [exp(∓ξ(x) + β(x)·p ∓ √(-a²(x,p))) + 1]^{-1}. They then compute the two-by-two spin density matrices, macroscopic currents (baryon number current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor), and a generating function that allows the framework to be classified as a divergence-type theory.

## Key numbers

- The mean spin polarization vector is given by P = (1/2)(a_*/√(a_*²)) × (Σ_{i,j=±} j g_FD^{ij})/(Σ_{i,j=±} g_FD^{ij}), where a_* is the spin polarization vector in the particle rest frame
- For a rigidly rotating fluid in global equilibrium with constant parameters Ω₀ and T₀, the averaged polarization vector ⟨P⟩ ∝ (Ω₀ n_z)/(T₀ m) (⟨E_p⟩ - ⟨p_z²/(E_p+m)⟩), where n_z is the unit vector along the rotation axis

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann local equilibrium Wigner function proposed by Bhadury (2025) to Fermi-Dirac statistics, which is necessary for analyzing systems at relatively lower temperatures and high baryon densities where spin polarization effects are most pronounced, as shown in studies by Deng (2021) and others. The framework reproduces generalized thermodynamic relations for particles with spin previously obtained by Florkowski (2024) and Drogosz (2024), and enables future analysis of nonlinear causality and equilibrium stability of perfect spin hydrodynamics within a divergence-type framework.
