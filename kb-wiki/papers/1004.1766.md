# Sensitivity of deexcitation energies of superdeformed secondary minima to the density dependence of symmetry energy with the relativistic mean-field theory

> The relationship between deexcitation energies of superdeformed secondary minima relative to ground states and the density dependence of the symmetry energy is investigated for heavy nuclei using the relativistic mean field (RMF) model. It is shown that the deexcitation energies of superdeformed secondary minima are sensitive to differences in the symmetry energy that are mimicked by the isoscalar-isovector coupling included in the model. With deliberate investigations on a few Hg isotopes that have data of deexcitation energies, we find that the description for the deexcitation energies can be improved due to the softening of the symmetry energy. Further, we have investigated deexcitation energies of odd-odd heavy nuclei that are nearly independent of pairing correlations, and have discussed the possible extraction of the constraint on the density dependence of the symmetry energy with the measurement of deexcitation energies of these nuclei.

## Key claim

The deexcitation energies of superdeformed secondary minima (SSM) relative to ground states in heavy nuclei are sensitive to differences in the symmetry energy, and this sensitivity originates almost uniquely from the modification to the isovector potential induced by the isoscalar-isovector coupling, with the isoscalar potential variations between the two states nearly canceling out.

## Method

The authors used the deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the NL3* parameter set, including an isoscalar-isovector coupling term (parameterized by Lambda_v) to modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy. They calculated the binding energies of ground states and superdeformed secondary minima for Hg and Au isotopes in the mass region A~190, adjusting the rho-NN coupling constant g_rho to keep the symmetry energy fixed at k_F = 1.15 fm^-1 for each Lambda_v value. The deexcitation energy was obtained as the difference between the SSM and ground-state binding energies, and the BCS pairing interaction with constant pairing gaps from Moller and Nix was included.

## Key numbers

- For $^{194}$Hg, the deexcitation energy increases from 5.52 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.000) to 6.60 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.030), compared to the experimental value of 6.0 MeV.
- For $^{192}$Hg, the deexcitation energy increases from 4.03 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.000) to 5.03 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.030), compared to the experimental value of 5.3 MeV.
- For $^{191}$Hg, the deexcitation energy increases from 3.59 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.000) to 4.44 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.030), compared to the experimental value of 4.6 MeV.
- The neutron skin thickness in $^{194}$Hg ground state decreases from 0.213 fm (Lambda_v = 0.000) to 0.141 fm (Lambda_v = 0.030), while the SSM neutron skin thickness decreases from 0.182 fm to 0.115 fm.
- The ground-state binding energy per nucleon of $^{194}$Hg changes from 7.913 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.000) to 7.948 MeV (Lambda_v = 0.030), a variation of about 7 MeV total.
- With slight readjustment of the sigma meson mass (by up to 0.07 MeV), the ground-state binding energy variation is nearly eliminated while the deexcitation energy variation remains almost unchanged.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Horowitz et al. (2001) and Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005) that used the isoscalar-isovector coupling to modify the neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei, and by Lalazissis et al. (2000) who predicted diverse deexcitation energies for nuclei in the A~190 region using various models. The paper establishes a direct relationship between deexcitation energies of superdeformed secondary minima and the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which was previously unavailable in the literature. It enables the use of measured deexcitation energies of odd-odd nuclei (such as Au isotopes) that are nearly independent of pairing correlations to constrain the isovector content of nuclear models and the density dependence of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities.
