# Symplectic symmetry and clustering in atomic nuclei

> A new symplectic-based shell-model approach to clustering in atomic nuclei is proposed by considering the simple system $^{20}$Ne. Its relation to the collective excitations of this system is mentioned as well. The construction of the Pauli allowed Hilbert space of the cluster states with maximal permutational symmetry is given for the $^{16}$O+$^{4}$He $\rightarrow$ $^{20}$Ne channel in the case of one-component many-particle nuclear system. The equivalence of the obtained cluster model space to that of the semi-microscopic algebraic cluster model is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that cluster excitations in atomic nuclei can be described within a symplectic-based shell-model framework by isolating cluster degrees of freedom through an appropriate reduction of the full dynamical symmetry group \(Sp(6m,R)\), and demonstrates that for the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{4}\text{He} \rightarrow ^{20}\text{Ne}\) channel, the resulting cluster model space is equivalent to that of the semi-microscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM), but only when one restricts to fully symmetric \(SU(3)\) irreducible representations.

## Method

The author constructs the Pauli-allowed Hilbert space for the two-cluster system \(^{20}\text{Ne}\) by reducing the full dynamical group \(Sp(6(A-1),R)\) through a chain that isolates a single Jacobi vector \(q_0\) describing the relative motion of the two clusters, while the remaining Jacobi vectors describe the intrinsic states of the \(^{16}\text{O}\) and \(^{4}\text{He}\) clusters. The permutational symmetry is ensured via the orthogonal groups \(O(A_i-1)\) and their reductions to the symmetric groups \(S_{A_i}\). The resulting cluster states are classified by \(SU(3)\) irreps of the type \((n_0+2n,0)\) for even parity and \((n_0+2n+1,0)\) for odd parity, which together reproduce the SACM space of \((n_0+n,0)\) irreps with \(n_0=8\).

## Key numbers

- The \(O(m)\) symmetry of the intrinsic motion in \(^{20}\text{Ne}\) is \((12,4,4)\), where \(m = A-1 = 19\).
- The \(Sp(6,R)\) bandhead for collective excitations in \(^{20}\text{Ne}\) has \(SU(3)\) symmetry \((8,0)\).
- The \(Sp(6,R)\) irreducible representation for \(^{20}\text{Ne}\) is \(\langle \sigma \rangle = \langle 12+\frac{19}{2}, 4+\frac{19}{2}, 4+\frac{19}{2} \rangle\).
- The Pauli principle requires \(n_0 = 8\) for the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{4}\text{He}\) channel.
- The cluster model space is spanned by \(SU(3)\) irreps \((8,0), (10,0), (12,0), \ldots\) (even parity) and \((9,0), (11,0), (13,0), \ldots\) (odd parity).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing connection between symplectic symmetry and nuclear collective motion, first explored by Goshen and Lipkin, Rosensteel and Rowe (1977, 1980), and Filippov and collaborators (1981), by explicitly linking the \(Sp(6,R)\) framework to cluster degrees of freedom. The proposed approach unifies the shell model, the symplectic \(Sp(6,R)\) model, and the cluster model within a single algebraic scheme, building on earlier \(SU(3)\)-based connections by Bayman and Bohr (1958) and Hecht, as well as recent no-core symplectic model studies of clustering in \(^{12}\text{C}\) by Dreyfuss (2013) and Launey (2016). The equivalence demonstrated here enables the use of symplectic computational methods for calculating excitation spectra and transition probabilities in light nuclei where clustering is important, without requiring effective charges to reproduce quadrupole collectivity.
