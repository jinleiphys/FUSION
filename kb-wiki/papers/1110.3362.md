# How well do we know the neutron structure function?

> We present a detailed analysis of the uncertainty in the neutron F2n structure function extracted from inclusive deuteron and proton deep-inelastic scattering data. The analysis includes experimental uncertainties as well as uncertainties associated with the deuteron wave function, nuclear smearing, and nucleon off-shell corrections. Consistently accounting for the Q^2 dependence of the data and calculations, and restricting the nuclear corrections to microscopic models of the deuteron, we find significantly smaller uncertainty in the extracted F2n/F2p ratio than in previous analyses. In addition to yielding an improved extraction of the neutron structure function, this analysis also provides an important baseline that will allow future, model-independent extractions of neutron structure to be used to examine nuclear medium effects in the the deuteron.

## Key claim

By restricting the analysis to microscopic models of the deuteron and consistently accounting for the Q² dependence of the data, the uncertainty in the extracted F₂n/F₂p ratio is significantly smaller than in previous analyses, though the result remains model-dependent and the quoted uncertainty bands represent the RMS spread across a chosen set of nuclear models rather than a rigorous statistical bound.

## Method

The authors extracted the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio F₂n/F₂p from inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data on deuteron and proton targets from SLAC, BCDMS, and NMC, using F₂d/F₂p data compiled by Whitlow (1991) for 3 < Q² < 230 GeV². They interpolated all data to a common Q² = 16 GeV² and applied a modified smearing formalism that includes a convolution of smeared nucleon structure functions plus an off-shell correction term δF₂d, using five different deuteron wave functions (N3LO, CD-Bonn, AV18, WJC-1, WJC-2) and multiple smearing models (WBA, relativistic MST, light-front ACHL, RT) with and without nucleon off-shell corrections.

## Key numbers

- The extraction of F₂n/F₂p is limited to data in the range 8 < Q² < 32 GeV², interpolated to a fixed Q² = 16 GeV².
- The interpolation to Q₀² differs from a simple average over the limited Q² range by at most 0.7%, with a typical correction of 0.3%.
- At x = 0.85, the present analysis gives a 1σ range of 0.18 < F₂n/F₂p < 0.32, compared to the previous range of 0.2 < F₂n/F₂p < 0.7.
- For on-shell extractions only, the range at x = 0.85 is 0.16 < F₂n/F₂p < 0.28.
- For extractions including off-shell effects, the range at x = 0.85 is 0.25 < F₂n/F₂p < 0.36.
- The CJ Collaboration (Accardi 2011) found a range of 0.32 < F₂n/F₂p < 0.50 at x = 0.85 and Q² = 16 GeV².
- Taking a linear sum of all uncertainties yields the range 0.04 < F₂n/F₂p < 0.42 at x = 0.85.
- The smearing factor calculation changes F₂n/F₂p by less than 0.01 at x = 0.85 when using the updated F₂n value.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Arrington (2009) by systematically assessing uncertainties from the deuteron wave function, nucleon off-shell effects, and different nuclear smearing models, while excluding models that rely on extrapolations from heavier nuclei as used by Whitlow (1991), Bodek (1991), and Melnitchouk (1996). The results provide a reliable baseline for the neutron structure function assuming only traditional nuclear effects, enabling future model-independent extractions from experiments such as those proposed by the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV program to search for more exotic nuclear medium effects.
