# On convergence properties of GPD expansion through Mellin/conformal moments and orthogonal polynomials

> We examine convergence properties of reconstructing the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) through the universal moment parameterization (GUMP). We provide a heuristic explanation for the connection between the formal summation/expansion and the Mellin-Barnes integral in the literature, and specify the exact convergence condition. We derive an asymptotic condition on the conformal moments of GPDs to satisfy the boundary condition at $x=1$ and subsequently develop an approximate formula for GPDs when $x>ξ$. Since experimental observables constraining GPDs can be expressed in terms of double or even triple summations involving their moments, scale evolution factors, and Wilson coefficients, etc., we propose a method to handle the ordering of the multiple summations and convert them into multiple Mellin-Barnes integrals via analytical continuations of integer summation indices.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the formal summation expansion of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in terms of conformal moments and partial wave functions is only conditionally convergent, and that the Mellin-Barnes integral representation provides the correct resummation; the authors derive an asymptotic condition on conformal moments that ensures GPDs vanish at \(|x| = 1\), a property that was violated in prior parameterizations.

## Method

The authors establish the connection between the formal GPD expansion and the Mellin-Barnes integral by analytically continuing the conformal partial wave functions \(p_n(x,\xi)\) to complex \(j\) via the Schläfli integral representation. They then apply the Sommerfeld-Watson transformation to convert the divergent formal summation into a convergent Mellin-Barnes integral. For handling multiple summations that arise at next-to-leading order evolution, they develop a method to convert double or triple summations into multiple Mellin-Barnes integrals through analytical continuation of integer summation indices.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic condition for conformal moments to ensure GPDs vanish at \(|x| = 1\) is \(\mathcal{F}_j = \left(\frac{2}{1+\sqrt{1-\xi^2}}\right)^{-j} \smallO\left(\frac{1}{j}\right)\) as \(|j| \to \infty\) with \(|\arg j| < \pi/2\).
- For PDFs, the asymptotic condition for the Mellin moment \(f_j\) to ensure support on \([0,1]\) is \(f_j = \smallO\left(\frac{1}{j}\right)\) as \(j \to \infty\).
- The hypergeometric function in the GPD Mellin-Barnes integral asymptotically behaves as \({}_2F_1 \sim 2\sqrt{2} C(z) \left[\frac{2}{1+\sqrt{1-z}}\right]^j\) as \(|j| \to \infty\) for \(z = \xi^2/x^2 < 1\), where \(C(z) = \frac{(\sqrt{1-z})^{1/2}}{(1+\sqrt{1-z})^{3/2}}\).

## Context

This work extends the universal moment parameterization (GUMP) framework developed by Guo et al. (2022, 2023) by resolving mathematical convergence issues in the GPD reconstruction from conformal moments. It builds on the complex conformal spin formalism of Mueller (2006) and Muller (2014) by providing explicit asymptotic conditions and a method to handle multiple summations needed for next-to-leading order evolution. The results enable future global analyses that combine lattice calculations, parton distribution functions, and experimental form factor data to extract GPDs from deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production measurements.
