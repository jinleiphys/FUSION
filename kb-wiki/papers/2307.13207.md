# Comparative Study of alpha-alpha interaction potentials constructed using various phenomenological models

> In this paper, we have made a comparative study of alpha-alpha scattering using different phenomenological models like Morse, double Gaussian, double Hulthen, Malfliet-Tjon and double exponential for the nuclear interaction and atomic Hulthen as screened coulomb potential. The phase equations for S, D and G channels have been numerically solved using 5th order Runge-Kutta Method to compute scattering phase shifts for elastic scattering region consisting of energies up to 25.5 MeV. The model parameters in each of the chosen potentials were varied in an iterative fashion to minimize the mean absolute percentage error between simulated and expected scattering phase shifts. A comparative analysis revealed that, all the phenomenological models result in exactly similar inverse potentials with closely matching mean absolute percentage error values for S, D and G state. One can conclude that any mathematical function that can capture the basic features of two body interaction would always guide correctly in construction of inverse potentials.

# Comparative Study of alpha-alpha Interaction Potentials Constructed Using Various Phenomenological Models

## Key claim

All five phenomenological models (Morse, double Gaussian, double Hulthen, Malfliet-Tjon, and double exponential) produce essentially identical inverse potentials for alpha-alpha scattering when parameters are optimized against experimental phase shift data, with mean absolute percentage errors converging to within about 1 percent. However, this equivalence holds only after imposing physical constraints on the screening radius and ensuring that the Coulomb barrier heights match observed resonance energies; without these constraints, the optimized potentials are not physically realistic.

## Method

The authors solved the phase equations for S (l=0), D (l=2), and G (l=4) partial waves using a 5th-order Runge-Kutta method within the Phase Function Method, treating the nuclear interaction with five different phenomenological potentials (Morse, double Gaussian, double Hulthen, Malfliet-Tjon, and double exponential) and the Coulomb interaction with the atomic Hulthen screened potential. Model parameters for each potential were optimized by minimizing the mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) between simulated and experimental scattering phase shifts for energies up to 25.5 MeV, first with the screening radius as a free parameter and then with the screening radius fixed to satisfy physical constraints on Coulomb barrier heights.

## Key numbers

- Energies of observed resonances in alpha-alpha scattering: 0.09184 MeV (S-state, l=0), 3.03 MeV (D-state, l=2), and 11.35 MeV (G-state, l=4)
- For l=0 with screening radius a=15-25 fm, Coulomb barrier heights ranged from 0.06 to 0.17 MeV across all models; MAPE values were 0.8-2.1 percent
- For l=2 with screening radius a=6-9 fm, Coulomb barrier heights ranged from 2.49 to 2.96 MeV; MAPE values were 3.7-5.2 percent
- For l=4 with screening radius a=3-5 fm, Coulomb barrier heights ranged from 9.32 to 11.59 MeV; MAPE values were 0.1-4.6 percent
- The double exponential potential achieved the best MAPE for l=4 at 0.1 percent with a=4 fm
- The value of hbar^2/(2mu) for the alpha-alpha system is 10.44217 MeV fm^2
- The product V_o * a for the atomic Hulthen potential is fixed at 0.2758 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the inverse scattering approach of Anil et al. (2022), who used Morse potential with erf() Coulomb screening, by replacing the erf() function with the atomic Hulthen screened potential and by testing four additional phenomenological forms. The study also re-optimizes the double Hulthen potential parameters from Laha et al. (various years) because previous parameter sets did not produce Coulomb barrier heights matching the known resonance energies. The authors introduce the Malfliet-Tjon potential for alpha-alpha scattering for the first time, previously used only for n-p, n-d, and p-d systems. The finding that any mathematical function capturing basic two-body interaction features yields equivalent inverse potentials suggests that the specific functional form is less important than the optimization procedure and physical constraints, which may simplify future construction of alpha-nucleus interactions.
