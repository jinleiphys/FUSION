# A Many-Body Density Energy Functional

> The Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and the Kohn-Sham equations, which are at the basis of the Density Functional Theory, are reformulated in terms of a particular many-body density, which is translational invariant and therefore is relevant for self-bound systems. In a similar way that there is a unique relation between the one-body density and the external potential that gives rise to it, we demonstrate that there is a unique relation between that particular many-body density and a definite many-body potential. The energy is then a functional of this density and its minimization leads to the ground-state energy of the system. As a proof of principle, the analogous of the Kohn-Sham equation is solved in the specific case of $^4$He atomic clusters, to put in evidence the advantages of this new formulation in terms of physical insights.

# A Many-Body Density Energy Functional

## Key claim

The authors reformulate the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and Kohn-Sham equations in terms of a translation-invariant many-body density \(\nu(\rho)\) (depending on the hyperradius \(\rho\)) rather than the usual one-body density, demonstrating a unique relation between this many-body density and a hypercentral potential \(W(\rho)\). The claim is conditional on the existence of such a \(W(\rho)\) that reproduces the same density as the full interaction; the proof shows uniqueness once existence is assumed.

## Method

The authors replace the standard one-body density functional \(E[n(\vec r)]\) with a functional \(E[\nu(\rho)]\) of the hyperradial density \(\nu(\rho)\), defined from the hyperspherical harmonics expansion of the many-body wave function. They prove a one-to-one mapping between \(\nu(\rho)\) and a hypercentral potential \(W(\rho)\) via a reductio ad absurdum argument analogous to the original Hohenberg-Kohn proof. For \(^4\)He atomic clusters, they construct \(W(\rho)\) by averaging Gaussian two-body and three-body contact interactions over the hyperangular part of the ground state, fitting the parameters to the dimer binding energy and tetramer binding energy from the Aziz HFD-HE2 potential, then solving the resulting one-dimensional hyperradial Kohn-Sham-like equation for clusters of varying size \(N\).

## Key numbers

- Dimer binding energy of \(^4\)He from the Aziz HFD-HE2 potential: \(\epsilon_2 = 0.83012\) mK, with scattering length \(a = 235.547\,a_0\) and effective range \(r_e = 13.978\,a_0\).
- Gaussian two-body parameters fitted to the dimer: \(\alpha = 10.0485\,a_0\) and \(A = -1.208018\) K.
- Tetramer binding energy used for fitting three-body parameters: \(0.5332\) K (HFD-HE2 value).
- Optimal three-body parameters: \(\beta = 8.33\,a_0\) and \(B = 7.211\) K, with the acceptable interval \(7.5\,a_0 < \beta < 9.0\,a_0\).
- Binding energy per particle for large \(N\) extrapolates to \(E_N/N \rightarrow 6.4 \pm 0.7\) K, compared to the experimental homogeneous system value of \(7.1\) K.
- Unit radius \(r_0\) from the best parametrization reproduces GFMC results to better than \(5\%\), with large-\(N\) limit \(r_0 \rightarrow 4.1 \pm 0.2\,a_0\).

## Context

This work extends the density functional theory (DFT) framework, originally formulated by Hohenberg and Kohn (1964) and Kohn and Sham (1965) for systems with external potentials, to self-bound systems where translational invariance is required. It addresses a known difficulty in nuclear DFT, previously tackled by Engel (2007), Giraud et al. (2008), Barnea (2007), and others, by replacing the one-body density with a many-body density that is naturally translation-invariant. The application to \(^4\)He clusters uses an effective field theory approach inspired by Bira and Bazak, with results comparable to Green Function Monte Carlo calculations by Pandharipande et al. and to soft Gaussian potential results by Gattobigio et al. The formalism enables straightforward calculation of binding energies for any particle number via a single hyperradial equation, and the authors suggest future application to nuclear systems and trapped systems.
