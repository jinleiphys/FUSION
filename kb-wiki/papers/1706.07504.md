# Estimation of M1 scissors mode strength for deformed nuclei in the medium to heavy mass region by statistical Hauser-Feshbach model calculations

> Radiative neutron capture is an important nuclear reaction whose accurate description is needed for many applications ranging from nuclear technology to nuclear astrophysics. The description of such a process relies on the Hauser-Feshbach theory which requires the nuclear optical potential, level density and $γ$-strength function as model inputs. It has recently been suggested that the M1 scissors mode may explain discrepancies between theoretical calculations and evaluated data. We explore statistical model calculations with the strength of the M1 scissors mode estimated to be dependent on the nuclear deformation of the compound system. We show that the form of the M1 scissors mode improves the theoretical description of evaluated data and the match to experiment in both the fission product and actinide regions. Since the scissors mode occurs in the range of a few keV $\sim$ a few MeV, it may also impact the neutron capture cross sections of neutron-rich nuclei that participate in the rapid neutron capture process of nucleosynthesis. We comment on the possible impact to nucleosynthesis by evaluating neutron capture rates for neutron-rich nuclei with the M1 scissors mode active.

# Estimation of M1 scissors mode strength for deformed nuclei in the medium to heavy mass region by statistical Hauser-Feshbach model calculations

## Key claim
The M1 scissors mode strength, assumed to follow a Lorentzian form with strength proportional to the square of nuclear deformation and centroid proportional to the absolute value of deformation, improves Hauser-Feshbach calculations of neutron capture cross sections in the fission product region and, by extrapolation, in the actinide region. However, the improvement is clearest for strongly deformed nuclei in the fission product region, while in the actinide region the lack of experimental data limits firm conclusions, and the impact on r-process abundance yields is relatively small compared to other uncertain nuclear physics inputs such as nuclear masses.

## Method
The authors performed statistical Hauser-Feshbach model calculations of neutron radiative capture cross sections, adding a Lorentzian-shaped M1 scissors mode to the conventional generalized Lorentzian gamma-ray strength function. The M1 scissors mode strength was extracted by fitting to evaluated nuclear data libraries at 200 keV for selected nuclei in the fission product region, yielding a quadratic dependence on the nuclear deformation parameter beta_2. The resulting parameterization was then applied to calculate capture cross sections and average gamma-ray widths across the chart of nuclides, and the impact on r-process nucleosynthesis was evaluated using the PRISM reaction network with three different astrophysical trajectories.

## Key numbers
- M1 scissors mode strength parameterization: sigma_M1 * Gamma_M1 = (42.4 +/- 5.0) * beta_2^2 mb MeV, with assumed Gamma_M1 = 1.5 MeV
- For Gd-152 and Gd-154, the fitted sigma_M1 * Gamma_M1 values were 4.1 and 5.5 mb MeV respectively, which are 2.2 times larger than the systematic formula prediction
- The Maxwellian average cross section (MACS) for Gd-153 at 30 keV from the KADoNiS database is 4550 +/- 700 mb, while the systematic formula gives 3322 mb; with an adjusted sigma_M1 * Gamma_M1 = 4.3 mb MeV the MACS becomes 3836 mb
- Neutron capture rates of neutron-rich nuclei at T = 1.0 GK increase by up to a maximum factor of roughly 5 when the M1 scissors mode is included
- The calculated average gamma-ray widths in the actinide region are overestimated by 15-25% for Np-237 and Am-241,243 compared to compiled values

## Context
This work extends the findings of Ullmann (2014) on the M1 scissors mode in U-238 and Guttormsen (2014) on actinides, applying a deformation-dependent parameterization to the fission product region where Watanabe (2010) had demonstrated deficiencies in Hauser-Feshbach calculations. The study builds on earlier experimental work by Ziegler (1990), Margraf (1993), and the review by Heyde, von Neumann-Cosel, and Richter (2010) establishing the correlation between M1 strength and nuclear deformation. The results enable improved predictions of neutron capture cross sections for deformed nuclei without artificial re-normalization of the gamma-ray strength function, and provide a systematic treatment of the M1 scissors mode for large-scale nuclear data applications and r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
