# Spin polarisabilities of the nucleon at NLO in the chiral expansion

> We present a calculation of the fourth-order (NLO) contribution to spin-dependent Compton scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, and we give results for the four spin polarisabilities. These results have been presented before, in hep-ph/0002133. Here we address the issue of whether one-particle reducible graphs in the heavy baryon theory contribute to the polarisabilities.

# Spin polarisabilities of the nucleon at NLO in the chiral expansion

## Key claim
The fourth-order (NLO) contributions to the four spin polarisabilities of the nucleon in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory are as large or larger than the leading-order pieces, making comparison with experimental determinations questionable. No low-energy constants except the anomalous magnetic moments enter at this order, but the convergence of the chiral expansion for spin polarisabilities has not yet been reached even in the absence of the Delta resonance.

## Method
The authors calculated the fourth-order (NLO) contribution to spin-dependent Compton scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT) using the epsilon dot v = 0 gauge. They evaluated the one-loop diagrams contributing at NLO, Taylor expanded the amplitudes in photon energy omega, and extracted the omega^3 terms that define the four spin polarisabilities gamma_1 through gamma_4, along with the derived quantities gamma_0 (forward spin polarisability) and gamma_pi (backward spin polarisability). They also addressed the treatment of one-particle reducible graphs and their effect on the definition of polarisabilities.

## Key numbers
- Leading-order (third-order) forward spin polarisability gamma_0 = 4.51 x 10^(-4) fm^4 for both proton and neutron, from pi N loops
- Delta pole contribution to gamma_0 estimated at -2.4 x 10^(-4) fm^4, with pi Delta loops contributing -0.2 x 10^(-4) fm^4 (Hemmert et al.)
- NLO results for the proton (tau_3 = +1): gamma_1 = 4.5 - (2.1 + 1.3) = 1.1 x 10^(-4) fm^4; gamma_2 = 2.3 - (3.1 + 0.7) = -1.5 x 10^(-4) fm^4; gamma_3 = 1.1 - (0.8 + 0.1) = 0.2 x 10^(-4) fm^4; gamma_4 = -1.1 + (3.9 + 0.5) = 3.3 x 10^(-4) fm^4
- NLO forward spin polarisability gamma_0 = 4.5 - (6.9 + 1.5) = -3.9 x 10^(-4) fm^4 for the proton
- NLO backward spin polarisability gamma_pi = 4.5 + (2.7 - 1.1) = 6.1 x 10^(-4) fm^4 for the proton (plus third-order t-channel pion exchange contribution of -42.7 x 10^(-4) fm^4)
- Anomalous magnetic moments used: kappa_s = -0.12, kappa_v = 3.71
- Experimental forward spin polarisability gamma_0 for the proton ranges from -0.6 to -1.5 x 10^(-4) fm^4 from dispersion analyses
- LEGS group extracted gamma_pi = -27 x 10^(-4) fm^4 for the proton, compared to the previously accepted value of -37 x 10^(-4) fm^4

## Context
This work extends earlier leading-order calculations of nucleon spin polarisabilities in HBCPT by Bernard et al. (1992) and Meissner (1995), which gave gamma_0 = 4.51 from pi N loops alone. The calculation agrees with the fourth-order result for gamma_0 by Ji et al. (Osborne, 1999) and with the full set of polarisabilities by Gellas et al. (1999), though the authors disagree with Gellas et al. on the definition of polarisabilities regarding the treatment of one-particle reducible graphs. The results show that convergence of HBCPT for spin polarisabilities is poor even without the Delta, which at fifth order has been estimated to change the sign of gamma_0 (Bernard et al., 1992). The work enables comparison with dispersion-relation extractions and motivates inclusion of explicit Delta degrees of freedom as done by Hemmert et al.
