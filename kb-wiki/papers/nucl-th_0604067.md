# $\bar K$-Nuclear Deeply Bound States?

> Following the prediction by Akaishi and Yamazaki of relatively narrow $\bar K$-nuclear states, deeply bound by over 100 MeV where the main decay channel $\bar K N \to πΣ$ is closed, several experimental signals in stopped $K^-$ reactions on light nuclei have been interpreted recently as due to such states. In this talk I review (i) the evidence from $K^-$-atom data for a {\it deep} $\bar K$-nucleus potential, as attractive as $V_{\bar K}(ρ_0) \sim -(150 - 200)$ MeV at nuclear matter density, that could support such states; and (ii) the theoretical arguments for a {\it shallow} potential, $V_{\bar K}(ρ_0) \sim -(40 - 60)$ MeV. I then review a recent work by Mareš, Friedman and Gal in which $\bar K$-nuclear bound states are generated dynamically across the periodic table, using a RMF Lagrangian that couples the $\bar K$ to the scalar and vector meson fields mediating the nuclear interactions. Substantial polarization of the core nucleus is found for light nuclei, with central nuclear densities enhanced by almost a factor of two. The binding energies and widths calculated in this dynamical model differ appreciably from those calculated for a static nucleus. These calculations provide a lower limit of $Γ_{\bar K} \sim 50 \pm 10$ MeV on the width of nuclear bound states for $\bar K$ binding energy in the range $B_{\bar K} = 100 - 200$ MeV.

# $\bar K$-Nuclear Deeply Bound States?

## Key claim

The paper argues that deeply bound $\bar K$-nuclear states with binding energies of 100–200 MeV are theoretically possible, but their widths are likely at least 50 ± 10 MeV, which is substantially larger than the narrow widths originally predicted by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) for states where the main $\bar K N \to \pi \Sigma$ decay channel is closed.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian that couples the $\bar K$ meson to scalar ($\sigma$) and vector ($\omega$) meson fields, solving the coupled equations for nucleons and the $\bar K$ self-consistently across the periodic table from $^{12}$C to $^{208}$Pb. The imaginary part of the $\bar K$-nucleus potential is taken from a phenomenological $t\rho$ form fitted to $K^-$-atom data, with phase-space suppression factors applied to account for the binding energy dependence of the $\bar K N \to \pi Y$ and $\bar K N \to Y N$ decay channels. The coupling constants $g_{\sigma K}$ and $g_{\omega K}$ are scaled to produce different binding energies while the nuclear core is allowed to polarize dynamically.

## Key numbers

- The depth of the $\bar K$-nucleus potential at nuclear matter density from $K^-$-atom fits is $V_{\bar K}(\rho_0) \sim -(150 - 200)$ MeV for the deep potential, versus $V_{\bar K}(\rho_0) \sim -(40 - 60)$ MeV for the shallow potential from chirally motivated models.
- The lower limit on the width of $\bar K$ nuclear bound states for binding energy $B_{\bar K} = 100 - 200$ MeV is $\Gamma_{\bar K} \sim 50 \pm 10$ MeV.
- For the $1s$ state in $^{12}$C, the static calculation gave $B_{K^-} = 132$ MeV while the dynamical calculation gave $B_{K^-} = 172$ MeV using the same coupling constants.
- The central nuclear density in $^{12}_{K^-}$C increases by up to 75% in the range of binding energies studied, and by almost a factor of two in $^{208}_{K^-}$Pb for $B_{K^-}$ above 150 MeV.
- The $t\rho$ potential depth for medium-weight to heavy nuclei is about 80 MeV, while density-dependent potentials reach 150–200 MeV with lower $\chi^2$ values (84–103 versus 129 for $t\rho$).

## Context

This work extends the debate between the deep $\bar K$-nucleus potential derived from global fits to $K^-$-atom data (Friedman, Gal, and Mareš, 1999, 2005) and the shallow potential from chirally motivated coupled-channel calculations (Ramos and Oset, 2000; Cieplý, Friedman, and Gal, 2001). The RMF dynamical calculations go beyond static nuclear approximations used in earlier work, showing that nuclear polarization substantially increases binding energies and widths in light nuclei. The results provide guidance for interpreting experimental signals from KEK-PS E471, BNL-AGS E930, and DA$\Phi$NE-FINUDA, and suggest that searches for narrow $\bar K$ deeply bound states face widths of at least 50 MeV, making experimental identification challenging.
