# Uncertainties in extracting $S_{17}$ from transfers

> The transfer reaction $^7{\rm Be(d,n)}^8{\rm B}$ is modelled for the recent low experimental energies using the DWBA. Tests of the validity of the ANC method for the extraction of the $S_{17}$ astrophysical factor are performed. We show that a peripheral assumption is no longer accurate for center of mass energies greater than approximately 15 MeV. In all cases we found that core deformation and/or excitation effects to be small as long as the optical potential for the entrance channel is not strongly surface peaked. Multistep corrections to the DWBA were found to be small. We also emphasize that the lack of knowledge of the optical potentials for the entrance and outgoing channels induces severe uncertainties in the extracted $S_{17}$ astrophysical factor.

## Key claim

The asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) method for extracting the astrophysical S-factor S17 from the 7Be(d,n)8B transfer reaction is reliable only when the reaction is peripheral, which holds for center-of-mass energies below approximately 15 MeV; at higher energies the interior contribution invalidates the peripheral assumption, and the method also suffers from severe uncertainties due to poorly known optical potentials for the entrance and exit channels.

## Method

The transfer reaction 7Be(d,n)8B was modeled using the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) for center-of-mass energies of 5.8 MeV, 15.6 MeV, and 38.9 MeV. The authors tested the peripheral assumption by evaluating the radial integral of the transition amplitude with varying cutoff radii and by examining the partial-wave decomposition of the cross section. They systematically varied the optical potentials for the entrance and exit channels, using potentials extracted from elastic scattering on different targets (6Li, 7Li, 9Be) at different energies, and studied the sensitivity of the extracted S17 to the choice of 8B ground-state wave function models, including those with core deformation and excitation. Multistep corrections from couplings to excited states of 7Be were also evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The transfer reaction at Ecm = 5.8 MeV peaks at partial wave L = 3, corresponding to an impact parameter of 4.5 fm, which is significantly larger than the 8B interaction radius of 2.95 fm, confirming a peripheral reaction.
- At Ecm = 38.9 MeV the cross section peaks at L = 4, corresponding to an impact parameter of 2.3 fm, indicating significant interior contribution.
- For the low-energy data at 5.8 MeV, using only optical potentials taken from elastic scattering at the correct energy, the extracted S17 is 23.5 ± 3.7 eV b.
- Using the full range of optical potentials for entrance and exit channels, the extracted S17 is 26.2 ± 8.4 eV b.
- The uncertainty in S17 from using different entrance-channel optical potentials taken from higher-energy elastic scattering (Ecm ≈ 9.3 MeV) is up to 16%.
- The uncertainty from using entrance-channel potentials from different targets (6Li and 9Be) at the correct energy is 20%.
- The uncertainty from the exit-channel optical potentials is of order 12%.
- For one-channel single-particle models of 8B (Kim, Tombrello, Robertson, Barker, Esbensen), the ratio b2/S17cap is constant at approximately 0.0267 eV-1 b-1 fm-1, and the extracted S17 factors differ by less than 5%.
- For the deformed model reo1 (β2 = +0.5) with a strongly surface-peaked optical potential (Surface II), the extracted S17 differs from the Kim model by 16%.
- Multistep corrections to the DWBA reduce the differential cross section peak by approximately 8%, giving an 8% effect on the extracted S17.

## Context

This work extends the ANC method proposed by Xu et al. (1994) for extracting S17 from transfer reactions, and directly addresses the analysis of the 7Be(d,n)8B data from Liu et al. (1996) at 5.8 MeV, as well as new data from MSU at 38.9 MeV and RIKEN at 15.6 MeV. The authors show that the peripheral assumption fails above approximately 15 MeV, contradicting the simple application of the ANC method at higher energies. The study also builds on earlier work by Nunes et al. (1997) on core deformation effects in 8B, finding that deformation/excitation effects matter only when the optical potential is strongly surface peaked. The results enable more reliable extraction of S17 from future low-energy transfer measurements, provided that elastic scattering data for the entrance and exit channels are also measured to constrain the optical potentials.
