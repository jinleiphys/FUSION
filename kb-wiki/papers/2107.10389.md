# Impact of clustering on the $^8$Li $β$ decay and recoil form factors

> We place unprecedented constraints on recoil corrections in the $β$ decay of $^8$Li, by identifying a strong correlation between them and the $^8$Li ground state quadrupole moment in large-scale ab initio calculations. The results are essential for improving the sensitivity of high-precision experiments that probe the weak interaction theory and test physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM). In addition, our calculations predict a $2^+$ state of the $α+α$ system that is energetically accessible to $β$ decay but has not been observed in the experimental $^8$Be energy spectrum, and has an important effect on the recoil corrections and $β$ decay for the $A=8$ systems. This state and an associated $0^+$ state are notoriously difficult to model due to their cluster structure and collective correlations, but become feasible for calculations in the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell-model framework.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a strong linear correlation between the recoil-order form factors \(j_2/A^2c_0\) and \(j_3/A^2c_0\) in the \(\beta\) decay of \(^8\)Li and the ground-state quadrupole moment of \(^8\)Li, enabling the authors to predict these recoil corrections with significantly reduced uncertainties compared to previous experimental extractions, provided the calculations are restricted to the lowest \(2^+\) state in \(^8\)Be.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale ab initio calculations using the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with several chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials (NNLO\(_\mathrm{opt}\), N\(^3\)LO-EM, NNLO\(_\mathrm{sat}\), and JISP16) in model spaces up to \(N_\mathrm{max}=16\). They computed the recoil-order form factors \(j_2\), \(j_3\), \(d\), and \(b\) for the \(^8\)Li \(\beta\) decay to individual \(2^+\) states in \(^8\)Be, and established a linear regression between the calculated \(j_{2,3}/A^2c_0\) values and the calculated \(^8\)Li ground-state quadrupole moment, using the experimental quadrupole moment to predict the recoil terms.

## Key numbers

*   Predicted \(j_2/A^2c_0 = -966 \pm 13 \pm 33\) and \(j_3/A^2c_0 = -1546 \pm 19 \pm 40\) for the decay to the lowest \(2^+\) state in \(^8\)Be, where the first uncertainty is from the experimental quadrupole moment and the second from the regression at 99% confidence.
*   The experimental \(^8\)Li ground-state quadrupole moment used for the correlation is \(3.14(2) \, e\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) from Borremans et al. (2005).
*   The predicted \(d/Ac_0\) for the lowest \(2^+\) state is \(10.0 \pm 1.0\), and the predicted \(b/Ac_0\) is \(6.0 \pm 0.4\).
*   The calculated Gamow-Teller matrix element \(|M_{\mathrm{GT}}|\) for the decay to the lowest \(2^+\) state is \(0.16(1)\) with NNLO\(_\mathrm{opt}\), compared to an experimental value of \(0.190\) from Barker (1989) and \(0.284\) from Warburton (1986) without an intruder state.
*   The extrapolated energies of the predicted intruder \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states in \(^8\)Be lie between 5 and 15 MeV above the ground state.

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations to recoil-order corrections in \(A=8\) \(\beta\) decay, which were previously extracted experimentally with large uncertainties by Sumikama et al. (2011) and De Braeckeleer et al. (1995). The results directly reduce the systematic uncertainty on tensor-current limits from the high-precision experiment of Burkey et al. (2021). The paper also supports the existence of low-lying intruder \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states in \(^8\)Be originally proposed by Barker (1968, 1969) from R-matrix fits, and shows that including such a state resolves a discrepancy in the Gamow-Teller matrix element noted by Warburton (1986). This enables improved sensitivity for future tests of the weak interaction's V\(-\)A structure, the conserved vector current hypothesis, and searches for second-class currents.
