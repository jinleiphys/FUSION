# On the anomalous superfluid hydrodynamics

> It has been shown by Son and Surówka that the presence of anomaly in hydrodynamics with global U(1) symmetry can induce vortical and magnetic currents. The induced current is uniquely determined by anomaly from the existence of an entropy current with non-negative divergence. In this work, we extended the analysis to hydrodynamics with U(1) symmetry spontaneously broken, i.e. U(1) superfluid hydrodynamics. We found that all possible first order gradient corrections are determined up to five arbitrary functions, with the entropy current containing one arbitrary function. Furthermore, the stress tensor does not receive correction from terms proportional to the magnetic field.

## Key claim

This work extends the analysis of anomalous hydrodynamics from normal fluids to superfluids with spontaneously broken U(1) symmetry, finding that first-order gradient corrections in the parity-odd sector are determined up to five arbitrary functions, in contrast to the nearly complete determination found by Son and Surówka for normal fluids.

## Method

The author enumerates all possible parity-odd first-order gradient terms for the stress tensor, charge current, and Josephson equation in relativistic U(1) superfluid hydrodynamics, using the method of independent structures following Minwalla (2009). The constraint of non-negative entropy production is then applied to determine the transport coefficients, with the entropy current itself allowed to contain first-order gradient corrections.

## Key numbers

- 24 total parity-odd first-order gradient corrections are enumerated: 13 for the stress tensor, 9 for the charge current, and 2 for the Josephson equation
- The transport coefficients are determined up to 5 arbitrary functions: s_B, Π_V, Π_ω, V_Ω, and T_σ
- The entropy current is parametrized by a single arbitrary function s_B
- The stress tensor receives no correction from terms proportional to the magnetic field (P_B = N_B = Λ_B = 0)
- The anomalous current coefficient C appears in the solution for the entropy current coefficients, e.g., s_ω = 2μ s_B - (2Cμ³)/(3T) and s_Ω = -(2Cμ²)/T + 2(1 - (sT/f²)∂_X)s_B

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Son and Surówka (2009) on anomalous hydrodynamics with global U(1) symmetry to the case where the U(1) symmetry is spontaneously broken, i.e., superfluid hydrodynamics. It builds upon the formulation of relativistic superfluid hydrodynamics by Son (2001) and the enumeration of transport coefficients for U(1) superfluids by Minwalla (2009) and Herzog and Yarom (2009). The results contrast with normal fluid hydrodynamics where the entropy production criterion almost completely fixes the first-order gradient corrections. The work enables future studies of anomaly-induced transport in superfluid systems relevant to QCD phenomenology and heavy ion collisions.
