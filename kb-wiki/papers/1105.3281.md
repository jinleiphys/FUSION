# Spectroscopy and intruder configurations of $^{33}$Mg and $^{31}$Ne studied with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> Excitation spectra and neutron single particle configurations of $^{33}$Mg and $^{31}$Ne are investigated by using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with generator coordinate method. It is shown that both nuclei have strongly deformed $3/2^-$ ground state with a $3p2h$ configuration. The excitation spectra are qualitatively understood in terms of the Nilsson model and the calculation has shown the coexistence of different intruder configurations within small excitation energy. The calculated one neutron separation energy of $^{31}$Ne is rather small ($S_n=250$ keV) and implies a p-wave one neutron halo with a strongly deformed core.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both $^{33}$Mg and $^{31}$Ne have strongly deformed $3/2^-$ ground states with a $3p2h$ (three-particle two-hole) intruder configuration, but the authors acknowledge that an alternative $1/2^+$ assignment for $^{33}$Mg cannot be ruled out and that the calculated $\beta$ decay strengths are incompatible with observations under either interpretation.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective nuclear force, combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) and angular momentum projection. Variational calculations were performed with constraints on the matter quadrupole deformation $\beta$, and the neutron single-particle orbits were analyzed to identify particle-hole configurations. The final wave functions were obtained by superposing thousands of Slater determinants with different deformations, $K$ quantum numbers, and single-particle configurations via the Hill-Wheeler equation.

## Key numbers

- One neutron separation energy of $^{31}$Ne: $S_n = 250$ keV, supporting a $p$-wave neutron halo structure.
- Calculated binding energy of $^{33}$Mg: 251.1 MeV, underestimating the observed value by about 1 MeV.
- One neutron separation energy of $^{33}$Mg: 1.9 MeV, compared to the observed value of 2.22 MeV.
- Ground state of $^{33}$Mg is dominated by the $3p2h$ configuration at approximately 87% (estimated from overlap with the $3/2^-$ minimum at $\beta=0.45$).
- First excited state of $^{33}$Mg ($5/2^-_1$) calculated at 0.6 MeV, possibly corresponding to the observed 0.48 MeV state.
- Calculated magnetic moment of the $3/2^-_1$ state in $^{33}$Mg: $-0.70\ \mu_N$, compared to the experimental value of $-0.7456\ \mu_N$.
- Matter root mean square radius of $^{31}$Ne: 3.42 fm, larger than $^{30}$Ne (3.31 fm), $^{32}$Ne (3.40 fm), and $^{33}$Mg (3.34 fm).
- Intra-band $E2$ transition probability for the ground band of $^{33}$Mg: $B(E2; 3/2^-_1 \rightarrow 5/2^-_1) = 282\ e^2\text{fm}^4$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study of $^{31}$Mg (Kimura 2007) to the $N=21$ isotones $^{33}$Mg and $^{31}$Ne, which lie in the "island of inversion" region around $N=20$ first identified by Thibault (1975) and Warner (1990). The calculations support the $3/2^-$ ground state assignment for $^{33}$Mg suggested by magnetic moment measurements (Yordanov 2007) and one-neutron knockout reactions (Kanungo 2010), rather than the $3/2^+$ assignment from $\beta$ decay studies (Nummela 2001, Tripathi 2008). For $^{31}$Ne, the small calculated separation energy and $3/2^-$ ground state support the $p$-wave halo interpretation suggested by Coulomb breakup measurements (Nakamura 2010) and large reaction cross sections (Takechi 2010). The results enable further theoretical work combining AMD with the resonating group method to better describe halo tails, and call for more detailed experimental and theoretical study of the $A=33$ isobars to resolve the $\beta$ decay discrepancies.
