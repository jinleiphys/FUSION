# A Collective Model of Baryons

> We propose an algebraic description of the geometric structure of baryons in terms of the algebra $U(7)$. We construct a mass operator that preserves the threefold permutational symmetry and discuss a collective model of baryons with the geometry of an oblate top.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an algebraic description of baryon structure using a U(7) algebra, where baryons are treated as collective rotational and vibrational excitations of an oblate symmetric top, but the authors hedge that this model produces mass predictions of comparable quality to quark potential models despite having very different wave functions, meaning masses alone cannot distinguish between the two approaches.

## Method

The authors introduce a U(7) algebraic framework by quantizing the Jacobi coordinates of a three-body system using two vector bosons (L^π=1^-) and one scalar boson (L^π=0^+), constructing an S_3-invariant mass operator up to quadratic terms in the U(7) generators. They then specialize to a collective model with the geometry of an oblate symmetric top by selecting equilibrium conditions r_ρ=r_λ and θ=π/2, decomposing the mass operator into vibrational and rotational parts, and adding a spin-flavor part using the SU(6) ⊃ SU(3) ⊗ SU(2) dynamic symmetry of Gürsey and Radicati.

## Key numbers

- The mass formula contains 7 parameters: M_0^2=0.260 GeV^2, λ_1 N=1.192 GeV^2, λ_2 N=1.538 GeV^2, α=1.056 GeV^2, a=-0.042 GeV^2, b=0.029 GeV^2, c=0.125 GeV^2
- The inverse slope of Regge trajectories α=1.056 GeV^2 is consistent with QCD estimates and nearly identical to the value extracted from meson masses (α=1.092 GeV^2)
- The N(1440) Roper resonance is classified as a one-phonon excitation (n_u=1, n_v+n_w=0) in the oblate top model, whereas in a harmonic oscillator quark model it is a two-phonon excitation
- The ground state wave function for the nucleon N(939) is shown for R^2 values of 0.2, 1.0, and 5.0, demonstrating spread over many oscillator shells

## Context

This work extends the algebraic models of a generation ago (Dothan and collaborators, 1970s) by incorporating the threefold permutational symmetry essential for baryon structure, and contrasts with the single-particle picture of the nonrelativistic quark potential model (Isgur and Karl, 1978) and its relativized form (Capstick and Isgur, 1986). The U(7) framework enables future studies of transition form factors for photocouplings (N* → N+γ) and can be extended to strange baryons where the S_3 symmetry is lowered to S_2.
