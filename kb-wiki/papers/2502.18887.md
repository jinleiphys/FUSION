# A hybrid model for multi-particle production and multi-fragment emission in electron-nucleus collisions at the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider

> To present a prediction of the multi-particle production and multi-fragment emission in electron-nucleus ($eA$) collisions at the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), a simple hybrid model which is based on the multi-source thermal model and the ideal gas model is proposed in this article. According to the hybrid model, some statistical laws such as the two-component Erlang distribution and others are presented, which means a two-source production. These statistical laws are hopeful to describe the bulk properties of multiple particles produced in the scattering of electron-nucleon ($eN$) and multiple fragments emitted in the fragmentation of excited residual nucleus. Although both the scattering and fragmentation can occur in $eA$ collisions at the EIC, their two-sources are different. In $eN$ scattering, the multiple particles come from the soft excitation and hard scattering processes respectively, which are classified as two different types of events. In nuclear fragmentation, the multiple fragments come from the cold and hot sources which exist in the same excited nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid model combining a multi-source thermal model and an ideal gas model to predict statistical distributions of both particles and fragments produced in electron-nucleus collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider, but the model is presented as a predictive framework rather than a validated one, with the authors noting that the two-component distributions for particles and fragments arise from physically distinct sources (soft/hard processes for particles versus cold/hot sources for fragments) and that the model's parameters would need to be constrained by future EIC data.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid model where multi-particle production in electron-nucleon scattering is described by a two-component Erlang distribution, with the soft process (electron interacting with sea quarks/gluons) and hard process (electron interacting with valence quarks) each contributing an Erlang distribution that is a fold of exponential distributions from individual participants. For multi-fragment emission from the excited residual nucleus, the model uses a two-component Erlang distribution for fragment multiplicity and a two-component Rayleigh distribution (from the classical ideal gas model) for fragment transverse momentum and angular distributions, with cold and hot sources at different temperatures. The pseudorapidity distributions of charged particles are described using a two-cylinder model with projectile and target strings characterized by rapidity ranges and a rapidity gap parameter.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy for electron-nucleon collisions at the EIC is estimated as \(\sqrt{s} \approx 20-140\) GeV
- For charged particle multiplicity in \(e^+p\) collisions at DESY-HERA (\(\sqrt{s} \approx 300\) GeV), the single-component Erlang fit parameter \(\langle n_{i1}\rangle\) ranges from 0.38 (for \(2E_B^{\rm cr}=1.5-4\) GeV) to 1.45 (for \(2E_B^{\rm cr}=45-100\) GeV) in the Breit frame, with \(m_1=6\) fixed
- In the hadronic center-of-mass frame at DESY-HERA, \(\langle n_{i1}\rangle\) ranges from 0.44 (for \(M_{eff}=1.5-4\) GeV) to 2.23 (for \(W=150-225\) GeV), with \(m_1\) between 6 and 11
- For light meson production at Jefferson Laboratory energies, the parameter \(M_1\) ranges from 3 to 5, lower than the \(m_1=6-11\) observed at \(\sqrt{s}=300\) GeV
- Average transverse momenta for those low-energy measurements are estimated as \(\langle p_T\rangle \approx (0.4-1.2)\) GeV/\(c\)
- In \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=24-63\) GeV, string lengths for low multiplicity events (\(n_{ch}=2-4\)) are \(L_{y_P}=L_{y_T}=3.20-3.40\) with rapidity gap \(\Delta y=0.8-1.2\); for high multiplicity events (\(n_{ch}=20-24\)), string lengths are \(L_{y_P}=L_{y_T}=2.00-2.80\) with \(\Delta y=0\)
- In \(e^+e^-\) annihilations at \(\sqrt{s}=14-34\) GeV, string lengths satisfy \(L_{y_P}=L_{y_T}=1.72-2.20\) with gap \(\Delta y=0.4-0.6\)

## Context

This work extends the multi-source thermal model previously applied by the authors to \(e^+p\) collisions at DESY-HERA (Liu et al., earlier work) and to light meson production at Jefferson Laboratory, now proposing a unified framework for both particle production and nuclear fragmentation in electron-nucleus collisions at the future EIC. The model contrasts with quantum chromodynamics-based approaches by using statistical physics methods, and the authors note that at EIC energies the small Bjorken-\(x\) regime and gluon saturation physics will enhance the soft process contribution. The framework enables predictions for multiplicity, transverse momentum, pseudorapidity, and angular distributions of both charged particles and nuclear fragments in \(eA\) collisions, with parameters that can be refined once EIC data become available.
