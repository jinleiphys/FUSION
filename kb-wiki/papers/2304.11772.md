# Relativistic effects in Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Microscopic calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections are critical for the success of the neutrino-oscillation program. In addition to retaining nuclear correlations in the initial and final state of the reaction, they are based on consistent nuclear interactions and transition current operators, thereby enabling robust uncertainty quantification. In this work, we address a significant limitation of these microscopic methods, which arises from their nonrelativistic nature. By performing the calculations in a reference frame that minimizes nucleon momenta and utilizing the so-called ``two-fragment'' model, we extend the applicability of Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering to higher momenta than currently possible. To validate this approach, we compare our theoretical predictions against inclusive data measured by the MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINER$ν$A experiments.

# Relativistic effects in Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
By performing Green's function Monte Carlo calculations in the active-nucleon Breit frame and applying the two-fragment model, the authors extend the applicability of nonrelativistic microscopic calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering to higher momentum transfers than previously possible, though the method assumes single-nucleon knockout remains the dominant reaction mechanism even when two-body currents are included.

## Method
The authors compute charged-current electroweak response functions of 12C using Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) with one- and two-body current operators, evaluating them in the active-nucleon Breit (ANB) frame where nucleon momenta are minimized. They then Lorentz-boost these responses back to the laboratory frame, applying the two-fragment model to incorporate relativistic corrections to the kinematics of quasielastic scattering. The resulting cross sections are flux-averaged and compared against inclusive data from MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA experiments.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum for 12C taken as k_F = 225 MeV
- Energy shift epsilon_nr ~ 40 MeV used to center nonrelativistic scaling variable peaks at psi_nr = 0
- Energy shift epsilon_r ~ 30 MeV used to align ANB response peaks at relativistic scaling variable psi = 0
- Momentum transfer values analyzed: q = 500, 700, and 900 MeV for scaling comparisons
- Average neutrino energies range from 700 MeV (T2K) up to 6 GeV (MINERvA medium-energy NuMI beam)
- MINERvA kinematic cuts: scattering angle theta_mu < 17 degrees, muon momentum 2 GeV < |p_mu| < 20 GeV, neutrino energy E_nu < 20 GeV, momentum transfer |q| < 2 GeV
- Dipole axial form factor cutoff M_A = 1 GeV used in calculations
- GFMC one-body contribution quenched by approximately 10% relative to other theoretical approaches in MINERvA comparisons

## Context
This work extends the method developed by Efros (2005) and Rocco (2018) for electromagnetic responses of light nuclei (A=3,4) to charged-current neutrino scattering on 12C, addressing the nonrelativistic limitation of GFMC approaches previously applied by Lovato (2016, 2017, 2020). The results are consistent with correlated basis function theory (Fabrocini, 1997) and relativistic mean field (Franco-Munoz, 2022) findings that transverse enhancement arises from one- and two-body current interference in single-nucleon knockout. The approach enables meaningful comparisons between ab initio calculations and neutrino oscillation experiment data, though the authors note that MiniBooNE data tensions with T2K and MINERvA hamper firm conclusions about axial form factor parameterizations.
