# Non-Fermi Liquid Behavior Induced by Resonant Diquark-pair Scattering in Heated Quark Matter

> We show how the quasiparticle picture of quarks changes near but above the critical temperature T_c of the color-superconducting phase transition in the heated quark matter. We demonstrate that a non-Fermi liquid behavior of the matter develops drastically when the diquark coupling constant is increased owing to the coupling of the quark with the pairing soft mode: We clarify that the depression and eventually the appearance of a gap structure in the spectral function as well as the anomalous quark dispersion relation of the quark can be understood in terms of the resonant scattering between the incident quark and a particle near the Fermi surface to make the pairing soft mode.

## Key claim

The paper shows that near but above the critical temperature T_c of the color-superconducting phase transition, the quasiparticle picture of quarks is altered from Fermi-liquid to non-Fermi liquid behavior, with the effect becoming more drastic when the diquark coupling constant G_C is increased. The authors clarify that the depression and eventual gap-like structure in the spectral function, as well as the anomalous dispersion relation, can be understood in terms of resonant scattering between the incident quark and a particle near the Fermi surface to form the pairing soft mode.

## Method

The authors calculate the quark spectral function and dispersion relation in the T-matrix approximation, incorporating diquark-pair fluctuations in the quark self-energy. They vary the diquark coupling constant G_C in the range 3.11 GeV^{-2} to 4.35 GeV^{-2} for a fixed reduced temperature epsilon = (T - T_c)/T_c = 0.01, with a three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 650 MeV and quark chemical potential mu = 400 MeV. The dynamical structure factor S(k,omega) of the pair field is used to analyze the resonant scattering mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Diquark coupling constant G_C varied in the range 3.11 GeV^{-2} < G_C < 4.35 GeV^{-2}
- Three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 650 MeV
- Quark chemical potential mu = 400 MeV
- Reduced temperature epsilon = (T - T_c)/T_c = 0.01
- For G_C = 3.11 GeV^{-2}, T_c = 40.04 MeV
- For G_C = 3.73 GeV^{-2} (1.2 times larger), T_c = 59.82 MeV
- For G_C = 4.35 GeV^{-2} (1.4 times larger), T_c = 80.11 MeV
- The diquark gap at zero temperature may become as large as Delta ~ 100 MeV at densities corresponding to mu ~ 400 MeV
- The ratio r_xi = Delta/mu may become as large as 0.2 to 0.3, compared to r_xi ~ 0.001 in metal superconductors

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kitazawa, Kunihiro, Nemoto, 2004, 2005) which showed that precursory soft modes composed of diquark-pair fields exist even well above T_c and cause a depression of the quark spectral function. The present paper clarifies the mechanism of resonant scattering, which is also known in condensed matter physics (Janko, Maly, Levin, 1997; high-T_c superconductivity literature). The results contrast with another mechanism for non-Fermi liquid behavior in ungapped quark matter, where resummed perturbation theory (Rebhan, 2002) shows that coupling with unscreened long-range gluonic fields gives vanishing group velocity at the Fermi surface, whereas the present mechanism produces pseudogap formation as a precursory phenomenon of color superconductivity at finite temperature.
