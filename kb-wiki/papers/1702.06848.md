# Analysis of recent CLAS data on $f_{1}(1285)$ photoproduction

> Based on the experimental data released recently by the CLAS Collaboration, the $f_{1}(1285)$ photoproduction off a proton target is investigated in an effective Lagrangian approach. In our model, $s$-channel, $u$-channel, and $t$-channel Born terms are included to calculate the differential cross sections, which are compared with the recent CLAS experiment. An interpolating Reggeized treatment is applied to the $t$ channel, and it is found that the $t$-channel contribution is dominant in the $f_1(1285)$ photoproduction and the $u$-channel contribution is responsible for the enhancement at backward angles. The present calculation does not take into account any explicit $s$-channel nucleon-resonance contribution; nevertheless, our model provides a good description of the main features of the data. It suggests small couplings of the $f_1(1285)$ and the nucleon resonances in this energy region.

## Key claim

The authors show that the CLAS differential cross section data for $f_1(1285)$ photoproduction can be reproduced without including any explicit $s$-channel nucleon-resonance contributions, using only $t$-channel (with an interpolating Reggeized treatment), $u$-channel, and $s$-channel Born terms. This suggests that couplings of the $f_1(1285)$ to nucleon resonances are small in the energy region studied, though the authors acknowledge that an $s$-channel-like effect may arise from the interpolating function itself.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach including $t$-channel $\rho$ and $\omega$ meson exchanges, $u$-channel nucleon exchange, and $s$-channel nucleon exchange. An interpolating Reggeized treatment is applied to the $t$ channel, which smoothly transitions between the Feynman and Regge regimes using a sigmoid function. The seven free parameters (four for the Regge interpolation, the anomalous magnetic moment $\kappa_{f_1}$, and cutoffs $\Lambda_t$ and $\Lambda_u$) are fitted to 45 CLAS data points across five center-of-mass energy bins (2.35 to 2.75 GeV) using the MINUIT code, minimizing $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Best fit yields $\chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.27$ for the full model with seven free parameters.
- Fitted anomalous magnetic moment of the $f_1(1285)$: $\kappa_{f_1} = 1.94 \pm 0.39$.
- Fitted cutoff for $t$ channel: $\Lambda_t = 0.92 \pm 0.04$ GeV; for $s$ and $u$ channels: $\Lambda_s = \Lambda_u = 0.68 \pm 0.06$ GeV.
- When $g_{\rho f_1\gamma}$ is treated as a free parameter, the fit yields $\chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.21$ and a corresponding decay width $\Gamma_{f_1 \rightarrow \rho\gamma} \simeq 259 \pm 12$ keV, consistent with the CLAS experimental value of $453 \pm 177$ keV.
- The Feynman model (without Regge interpolation) gives $\chi^2/d.o.f. = 6.31$, indicating poor agreement with data.
- Data cover center-of-mass energies $W = 2.35, 2.45, 2.55, 2.65, 2.75$ GeV at nine angle bins.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Kochelev et al. (2009) and Domokos et al. (2009), which only considered $t$-channel contributions and failed to reproduce the new CLAS data (Dickson et al., 2016). The CLAS data showed a rapid falloff at forward angles and an enhancement at backward angles, which the authors attribute to the interpolating Reggeized $t$ channel and the $u$-channel nucleon exchange, respectively. The results contradict the CLAS helicity analysis suggestion that an $s$-channel $J^P=3/2^+$ nucleon resonance dominates; instead, the authors argue that the $t$ channel is dominant. This work enables future studies with more precise data from the CLAS12 experiment at JLab to search for possible small $s$-channel resonance contributions.
