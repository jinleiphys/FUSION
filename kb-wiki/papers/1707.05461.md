# Pion transverse-momentum spectrum and elliptic anisotropy of partially coherent source

> In this letter, we study the pion momentum distribution of a coherent source and investigate the influences of coherent emission on the pion transverse-momentum ($p_T$) spectrum and elliptic anisotropy. With a partially coherent source, constructed by a conventional viscous hydrodynamics model (chaotic part) and a parameterized expanding coherent source model, we reproduce the pion $p_T$ spectrum and elliptic anisotropy coefficient $v_2(p_T)$ in the peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. It is found that the influences of coherent emission on the pion $p_T$ spectrum and $v_2(p_T)$ are related to the initial size and shape of the coherent source, largely due to the interference effect. However, the effect of source dynamical evolution on coherent emission is relatively small. The results of the partially coherent source with 33% coherent emission and 67% chaotic emission are consistent with the experimental measurements of the pion $p_T$ spectrum, $v_2(p_T)$, and especially four-pion Bose-Einstein correlations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a partially coherent pion source, with 33% coherent emission and 67% chaotic emission, can reproduce experimental measurements of the pion transverse-momentum spectrum and elliptic anisotropy in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions, but only if the specific shear viscosity of the hydrodynamical chaotic part is taken to be larger (approximately 0.4-0.43) than the conventional value of 0.2 used in pure chaotic source models.

## Method

The authors constructed a partially coherent source by combining a conventional (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics model (the iEBE-VISHNU code package with MC-KLN initial conditions) for the chaotic part with a parameterized expanding coherent source model having a Gaussian initial spatial distribution and parameterized expansion velocities. They computed the pion transverse-momentum spectrum and elliptic anisotropy coefficient v2(pT) for peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and compared the results with experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration, also checking consistency with four-pion Bose-Einstein correlation measurements.

## Key numbers

- The partially coherent source with 33% coherent emission and 67% chaotic emission reproduced experimental data for the pion pT spectrum and v2(pT) in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV with 40-50% centrality.
- The specific shear viscosity of the hydrodynamical chaotic source was taken to be approximately 0.4-0.43, compared to the conventional value of 0.20 used in pure chaotic source models.
- The initial transverse size parameter of the coherent source was R_T=0.25 fm, consistent with the experimental observation that suppression of four-pion correlations extends at least up to pT approximately 340 MeV/c (giving an estimated minimum transverse size of 0.29 fm from the uncertainty relation).
- For a static coherent source with initial geometric parameters R_T=R_z=1 fm and S_T=2, the width sqrt(pT^2) for pT<2 GeV increased by approximately 3.7% due to expansion, compared to approximately 16.2% for the chaotic source.
- The coherent fraction of the partially coherent source was consistent with experimental measurements in two K_T4 regions: 0.16 < K_T4 < 0.3 GeV/c and 0.3 < K_T4 < 1.0 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of coherent pion emission in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, motivated by the ALICE Collaboration's observation of significant suppression of three- and four-pion Bose-Einstein correlations in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV (ALICE, 2014; ALICE, 2016). The partially coherent source model builds upon conventional viscous hydrodynamics approaches such as those of Shen (2011) and Song (2007, 2008), and the parameterized coherent source model follows earlier work on coherent emission by Gyulassy (1979) and Wong (2007). The results enable future systematic studies of the interplay between source coherence and viscosity in heavy-ion collisions.
