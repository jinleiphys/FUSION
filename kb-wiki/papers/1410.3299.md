# Quarkonia suppression in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> We estimate the modification of quarkonia yields due to different processes in the medium produced in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy. The quarkonia and heavy flavour cross sections calculated up to next-to-leading order (NLO) are used in the study. Shadowing corrections are obtained with the NLO EPS09 parametrization. A kinetic model is employed which incorporates quarkonia suppression inside a QGP, suppression due to hadronic comovers, and regeneration from charm pairs. The quarkonia dissociation cross section due to gluon collisions has been considered and the regeneration rate has been obtained using the principle of detailed balance. The modification in quarkonia yields due to collisions with hadronic comovers has been estimated assuming that the comovers are pions. The manifestations of these effects on the nuclear modification factors for both $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ in different kinematic regions has been demonstrated for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV in comparison with the measurements. Both the suppression and regeneration due to a deconfined medium strongly affect the low and intermediate $p_T$ range. The large observed suppression of $J/ψ$ at $p_T~>$ 10 GeV/$c$ exceeds the estimates of suppression by gluon dissociation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a kinetic model incorporating gluon dissociation, hadronic comover suppression, and regeneration via detailed balance can reproduce the measured nuclear modification factors for J/ψ and Υ in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, but the large observed suppression of J/ψ at pT > 10 GeV/c exceeds the estimates from gluon dissociation alone, suggesting additional mechanisms such as energy loss may be required.

## Method

The authors calculate quarkonia and heavy flavor cross sections up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in pQCD using CT10 parton densities and the color evaporation model, with shadowing corrections from the NLO EPS09 parametrization. A kinetic rate equation is solved for the proper-time evolution of the quarkonia population, incorporating dissociation by thermal gluons, dissociation by hadronic comovers (assumed to be pions), and regeneration from thermal heavy quark pairs using detailed balance. The QGP evolution is modeled with isentropic cylindrical expansion using a lattice QCD equation of state, with initial temperatures determined from measured charged particle multiplicities.

- Initial temperature T0 = 0.484 GeV at midrapidity for 0-5% most central collisions, and T0 = 0.427 GeV at forward rapidity (2.5 ≤ y ≤ 4)
- Charm quark mass mc = 1.27 ± 0.09 GeV; bottom quark mass mb = 4.65 ± 0.09 GeV
- pp cross section for cc̅: 4.11^{+2.69}_{-2.50} mb; for J/ψ: 21.6^{+10.6}_{-10.4} μb; for bb̅: 110.5^{+15.1}_{-14.2} μb; for Υ: 0.22^{+0.07}_{-0.06} μb

- Initial proper time τ0 = 0.3 fm/c; freeze-out temperature Tf = 0.140 GeV
- Transverse acceleration aT = 0.1 c² fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the kinetic model approach of Thews (2000) and Zhao (2011) to LHC energies, incorporating both gluon dissociation and regeneration via detailed balance, along with hadronic comover suppression following Vogt (1988). The calculations are compared to CMS and ALICE measurements at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, showing that regeneration dominates at low pT (explaining the flat centrality dependence observed by ALICE) while gluon dissociation dominates at intermediate pT. The discrepancy at high pT (pT > 10 GeV/c) suggests that energy loss mechanisms, similar to those for open charm, may be needed to fully explain the CMS data. The Υ(1S) results indicate that feed-down from excited states (Υ(2S) and Υ(3S)) is important for understanding the measured suppression.
