# Quark-meson coupling model with constituent quarks: Exchange and pionic effects

> The binding energy of nuclear matter including exchange and pionic effects is calculated in a quark-meson coupling model with massive constituent quarks. As in the case with elementary nucleons in QHD, exchange effects are repulsive. However, the coupling of the mesons directly to the quarks in the nucleons introduces a new effect on the exchange energies that provides an extra repulsive contribution to the binding energy. Pionic effects are not small. Implications of such effects on observables are discussed.

## Key claim

Exchange effects in the quark-meson coupling model are repulsive, as in quantum hadrodynamics, but the direct coupling of mesons to constituent quarks introduces an extra repulsive contribution to the binding energy from the internal kinetic energy of the quarks; pionic effects are not small and require a sizable readjustment of the scalar meson-quark coupling constant (approximately +40%).

## Method

The authors calculate the energy density of symmetric nuclear matter using a quark-meson coupling model with massive constituent quarks (m_q = 350 MeV) confined by a harmonic oscillator potential. They include the σ, ω, π, and ρ mesons, treat the mean-field (Hartree) and exchange (Fock) contributions, and determine the effective quark mass m*_q and the nucleon size parameter b* variationally. The Fock energy is evaluated using a Gaussian approximation for the nucleon wavefunction and a nonrelativistic reduction for qualitative insight, with numerical integration for the full relativistic calculation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy per nucleon: E/A – M_N ≈ –15.75 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F ≈ 1.36 fm⁻¹
- Quark mass: m_q = 350 MeV; σ meson mass: m_σ = 550 MeV; ω meson mass: m_ω = 783 MeV
- Nuclear compressibility K in Hartree approximation: 545 MeV for QHD mean-field theory; for b = 0.6 fm, K is considerably smaller than 545 MeV
- Readjustment of coupling constants when including Fock terms: g_ω changes by about –10%, g_σ changes by about +40%
- Pion-quark coupling constant: f²_qπ = (9/25) f²_Nπ exp(–m²_π b²/2), with f²_Nπ = 0.98, m_π = 138 MeV, and g²_Nπ/4π = 14.4
- Net attraction from π and ρ mesons (with zero-range component removed): about 24 MeV per nucleon
- With quark-exchange effects included: g²_σ = 173.45, g²_ω = 56.2, compressibility K = 375 MeV, b*/b = 1.07

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Saito and Thomas (1995) and Guichon (1988) by including exchange (Fock) terms and pionic effects, which had not been investigated in that model previously. The results show that the internal quark structure provides an extra repulsion compared to quantum hadrodynamics (Walecka model), and the readjustment of coupling constants is important for describing spin-orbit splittings in finite nuclei. The paper enables future studies of chiral symmetry, short-range correlations, and the replacement of ω exchange by quark-exchange effects at short distances, as discussed in the context of the resonating group method and the work of Banerjee and Tjon (1997).
