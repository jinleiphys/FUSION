# Aspects of model dependence of eta'-eta complex treated by going beyond the isospin limit

> Exploring the extent of model dependence, we study effects of certain Ansaetze for the T-dependence of the correction term in the QCD topological susceptibility. The one producing unwanted effects on results at T > 0 in the eta'-eta complex in the usual limit of isospin symmetry, is largely cured from its peculiar behavior and brought into agreement with the other, when breaking of isospin symmetry is allowed and the realistic quark mass ratio m_u/m_d = 0.50 is adopted.

# Aspects of model dependence of eta'-eta complex treated by going beyond the isospin limit

## Key claim

The paper shows that certain parameterizations of the temperature-dependent correction term in the QCD topological susceptibility produce qualitatively different (and initially problematic) behavior for the eta' and eta masses at T > 0, but this model dependence is largely cured and the results from different Ansätze are brought into agreement when isospin symmetry is broken by adopting the realistic quark mass ratio m_u/m_d = 0.50.

## Method

The authors use a Dyson-Schwinger (DS) effective model of non-perturbative low-energy QCD to compute the temperature dependence of the eta', eta, and pi^0 masses. They extend their previous isospin-symmetric analysis by allowing unequal u- and d-quark masses (m_u = 0.67 m_d and m_u = 0.5 m_d) and diagonalizing the full 3x3 mass matrix in the hidden-flavor basis, which includes both non-anomalous contributions from DS equations and anomalous contributions from the QCD topological susceptibility via Shore's generalization of the Witten-Veneziano relation. Two different Ansätze for the temperature dependence of the correction term C_m(T) are tested: one using the strange quark condensate with exponent delta=1, and one using the up quark condensate with exponent delta=1/5.

## Key numbers

- The realistic quark mass ratio adopted is m_u/m_d = 0.50, consistent with the QCD Lagrangian ratio 0.493 ± 0.019 (ZylaPDG2020).
- For variant (b) with m_u = 0.5 m_d, the quark mass parameters are m_u = 3.40 MeV, m_d = 6.80 MeV, m_s = 115.61 MeV.
- For variant (a) with m_u = 0.67 m_d, the quark mass parameters are m_u = 4.37 MeV, m_d = 6.52 MeV, m_s = 115.34 MeV.
- The Yang-Mills topological susceptibility at T=0 is taken as chi_YM = (191 MeV)^4 from lattice results (DelDebbio 2004).
- The eta' mass drop at T ~ T_Ch is reported as greater than or approximately 200 MeV, consistent with experimental indications (Csorgo 2009).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study Horvatic (2018) which obtained a smooth crossover U_A(1) transition with significant eta' mass drop but no eta mass drop in the isospin-symmetric limit. It also builds on Benic (2011) which introduced the extension of the eta'-eta approach to T > 0 using chiral-limit condensates, and on Shore (2006, 2007) who generalized the Witten-Veneziano relation. The present paper demonstrates that introducing realistic isospin breaking resolves discrepancies between different parameterizations of the correction term C_m(T), and brings the results into agreement with the earlier isosymmetric work Horvatic (2018). This enables more robust predictions for the temperature dependence of U_A(1) restoration and the eta' mass behavior near the chiral transition temperature.
