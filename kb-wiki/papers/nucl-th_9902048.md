# A next-to-next-to-leading-order $pp \to ppπ^0$ transition operator in chiral perturbation theory

> We present a systematic analysis of next-to-next-to-leading-order diagrams that contribute to the $pp \to ppπ^0$ production at threshold. Analytic expressions are given for the effective transition operators, and the relative importance of various types of diagrams is discussed. The vertex-correction-type graphs are found to give only small corrections to lower order graphs in conformity with expectations. By contrast, we find very large contributions from the two-pion graphs which can be interpreted as a part of effective $σ$-meson exchange diagrams. The recoil correction to the pion rescattering diagram also turns out to be large.

## Key claim

The authors present a next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) calculation of the $pp \to pp\pi^0$ transition operator in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT), finding that two-pion exchange diagrams give very large contributions (sometimes an order of magnitude larger than nominally leading terms), while vertex-correction-type graphs give only small corrections as expected. However, these results are obtained without full distorted-wave (DW) calculations of initial- and final-state interactions, and the authors caution that a full DW calculation could significantly modify the numerical estimates.

## Method

The authors systematically evaluated all irreducible Feynman diagrams up to chiral order $\nu = 2$ in Weinberg's counting scheme that contribute to $pp \to pp\pi^0$ production at threshold, using the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian expanded to order $\bar{\nu}=2$. From 19 topologically distinct new diagram types, isospin selection rules and the s-wave character of the outgoing pion reduced the relevant set to six types (Types I-VI) plus a recoil correction to the pion rescattering diagram (Type VII). The authors derived analytic expressions for the effective transition operators containing one-loop integrals, regularized with a cutoff $\lambda = 1$ GeV, and compared the magnitudes of each NNLO operator relative to the leading-order pion rescattering operator at threshold kinematics.

## Key numbers

* The two-pion exchange diagram Type IV gives a ratio $R_{IV} = 9.5$ (parameter set A), $5.1$ (set B), and $7.2$ (set C) relative to the leading pion rescattering operator.
* The two-pion exchange diagrams Type II and Type III individually give ratios of $R_{II} = 6.7$ and $R_{III} = -6.7$ (set A), which cancel each other at threshold.
* The recoil correction to the pion rescattering diagram (Type VII) gives $R_{VII} = 2.6$ (set A), $1.9$ (set B), and $2.0$ (set C).
* Vertex correction diagrams Type V and Type VI give small contributions: $R_V = 0.18$ and $R_{VI} = 0.14$ (set A).
* The enhancement factor for two-pion exchange diagrams is estimated as $k^2 f_\pi^{-2} = - m_\pi m_N f_\pi^{-2} \simeq -15$.
* The low-energy constants used are: $c_1 = -0.87$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_2 = 3.34$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_3 = -5.25$ GeV$^{-1}$ (set A).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculation (Park et al., 1996) and the calculations of Cohen et al. (1996) to the next chiral order. The results are consistent with the phenomenological model of Lee and Riska (1993), who found that shorter-range isoscalar meson-exchange processes like $\sigma$- and $\omega$-exchanges are important for this reaction. The large two-pion exchange contributions can be interpreted as part of an effective $\sigma$-meson exchange, following the soft-pion arguments of Brown and Durso (1971). The paper enables future full distorted-wave calculations and comparisons with experimental data from Meyer et al. (1990) and Bondar et al. (1995), though the authors note that the convergence of the chiral expansion for this reaction remains an open question requiring calculations at yet higher orders.
