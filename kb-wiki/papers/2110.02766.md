# Generalization of Weinberg's Compositeness Relations

> We generalize the time-honored Weinberg's compositeness relations by including the range corrections through considering a general form factor. In Weinberg's derivation, he considered the effective range expansion up to $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ and made two additional approximations: neglecting the non-pole term in the Low equation; approximating the form factor by a constant. We lift the second approximation, and work out an analytic expression for the form factor. For a positive effective range, the form factor is of a single-pole form. An integral representation of the compositeness is obtained and is expected to have a smaller uncertainty than that derived from Weinberg's relations. We also establish an exact relation between the wave function of a bound state and the phase of the scattering amplitude neglecting the non-pole term. The deuteron is analyzed as an example, and the formalism can be applied to other cases where range corrections are important.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Weinberg's compositeness relations by lifting the constant-form-factor approximation while still neglecting the non-pole term in the Low equation; the resulting compositeness is strictly within [0,1] and has a smaller uncertainty of order (RΛ)^{-2} compared to the order (RΛ)^{-1} uncertainty in the original Weinberg relations.

## Method

The authors start from the Low equation for the half-shell T-matrix, adopt a separable ansatz, and neglect the non-pole term while keeping a general momentum-dependent form factor g(p). They derive a dispersive representation of the function F(W) and obtain an integral expression for the compositeness X in terms of the phase shift δ_B, which is the phase of the on-shell T-matrix under the approximation of neglecting the non-pole term. For the case of the effective range expansion (ERE) up to O(p^2), they work out an analytic expression for the form factor g^2(p) and apply the formalism to the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: E_B = -2.224575(9) MeV
- Deuteron scattering length: a = -5.419(7) fm
- Deuteron effective range: r = 1.766(8) fm
- Weinberg's compositeness for deuteron: X_W = 1.68 (exceeds the probability range [0,1])
- Compositeness from the generalized relation for deuteron: X = 1 (100% composite)
- For the deuteron, Weinberg's relations give uncertainty of order (R m_π)^{-1} ≈ 0.3, while the generalized relation gives uncertainty of order 0.3^2 = 0.09
- Using a pole-approximation parametrization with cutoff Λ = 416 MeV, the lower bound on compositeness is X(Λ) = 92.8%
- Even with a much smaller cutoff Λ = m_π, the lower bound is X(m_π) = 62%
- For the D_s0*(2317) and D_s1(2460), the systematic uncertainty from using Weinberg's relations is estimated as (RΛ)^{-2} ∈ [0.03, 0.07] with Λ ∼ m_ρ

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's original compositeness relations (Weinberg, 1965) by removing the constant-form-factor approximation, which was the second of two approximations in the original derivation (the first being neglect of the non-pole term). The paper builds on prior work on compositeness by Baru (2010), Hanhart (2011), Hyodo (2011, 2013), and others, and on discussions of range corrections by Matuschek (2020). The formalism enables more reliable extraction of compositeness for near-threshold hadrons such as the deuteron, D_s0*(2317), and D_s1(2460), and can be applied to other composite systems with short-range interactions beyond hadron physics.
