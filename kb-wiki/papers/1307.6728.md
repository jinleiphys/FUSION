# Influence of pions on the hadron-quark phase transition

> In this work we present the features of the hadron-quark phase transition diagrams in which the pions are included in the system. To construct such diagrams we use two different models in the description of the hadronic and quark sectors. At the quark level, we consider two distinct parametrizations of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models. In the hadronic side, we use a well known relativistic mean-field (RMF) nonlinear Walecka model. We show that the effect of the pions on the hadron-quark phase diagrams is to move the critical end point (CEP) of the transitions lines. Such an effect also depends on the value of the critical temperature (T_0) in the pure gauge sector used to parametrize the PNJL models. Here we treat the phase transitions using two values for T_0, namely, T_0 = 270 MeV and T_0 = 190 MeV. The last value is used to reproduce lattice QCD data for the transition temperature at zero chemical potential.

## Key claim

The inclusion of pions in the hadronic sector of the RMF-PNJL approach shifts the critical end point (CEP) of the hadron-quark phase transition curves, and in one specific parametrization (NL3-FUKU08 with the original value of the b parameter) the CEP is completely removed, making the transition first-order along all baryonic chemical potential values.

## Method

The authors matched a relativistic mean-field (RMF) NL3 model, supplemented with a free degenerate pion gas, to two distinct parametrizations of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model (RRW06 and FUKU08) using the Gibbs criterion of equal pressures and chemical potentials at the same temperature. The pion mass was fixed at 139 MeV, and the phase transitions were computed for two values of the pure-gauge critical temperature T0 (270 MeV and 190 MeV) to compare with lattice QCD data.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass used in the calculation: m_pi = 139 MeV
- Two values of the critical temperature T0 in the pure gauge sector: T0 = 270 MeV and T0 = 190 MeV (the latter used to reproduce lattice QCD transition temperature at zero chemical potential)
- For the NL3-FUKU08 parametrization with the original b parameter, the CEP is completely removed from the transition curve, meaning the transition is first-order for all baryonic chemical potential values
- The effect of pions on the phase transition is only noticeable at temperatures around 139 MeV (consistent with the pion mass)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the hadron-quark phase transition using effective models (PNJL and RMF approaches) by explicitly including pion degrees of freedom in the hadronic sector, which had been neglected in earlier matching calculations. The results show that pion contributions can qualitatively change the phase diagram by shifting or eliminating the critical end point, which is relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments where pions are among the most abundant particles produced. The approach follows the same RMF-PNJL matching method used in previous work by the same group (Lourenco et al., 2012) but now incorporates the pion gas contribution.
