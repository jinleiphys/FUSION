# Finite temperature calculations for the spin polarized asymmetric nuclear matter with the LOCV method

> The lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) technique has been used to investigate some of the thermodynamic properties of spin polarized hot asymmetric nuclear matter, such as the free energy, symmetry energy, susceptibility and equation of state. We have shown that the symmetry energy of the nuclear matter is substantially sensitive to the value of spin polarization. Our calculations show that the equation of state of the polarized hot asymmetric nuclear matter is stiffer for the higher values of the polarization as well as the isospin asymmetry parameter. Our results for the free energy and susceptibility show that the spontaneous ferromagnetic phase transition cannot occur for hot asymmetric matter.

## Key claim

The authors use the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the AV18 potential to show that for hot asymmetric nuclear matter, the free energy curves for different polarizations never cross and the magnetic susceptibility remains positive at all densities and temperatures, meaning no spontaneous ferromagnetic phase transition occurs. This conclusion is conditional on the LOCV approximation and the specific AV18 interaction employed, and contrasts with some earlier calculations using Skyrme-like forces or the D1P Gogny force that did find such a transition.

## Method

The authors apply the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to spin-polarized hot asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, using the AV18 two-nucleon potential. They compute the free energy per nucleon via a cluster expansion truncated at the two-body level, with correlation functions determined by minimizing the two-body energy subject to a normalization constraint. The single-particle energies are treated with a quadratic approximation using effective masses as variational parameters, and the free energy is minimized with respect to these effective masses to obtain the chemical potentials and thermodynamic properties.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density for unpolarized symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature: approximately 0.31 fm⁻³, which is greater than the empirical value of 0.16 fm⁻³.
* Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at the calculated saturation density (0.31 fm⁻³) and zero temperature: approximately -18 MeV, which is smaller (more bound) than the empirical value of -16 MeV.
* Symmetry energy for unpolarized nuclear matter at the calculated saturation density (0.31 fm⁻³) and zero temperature: about 39 MeV, which is greater than the empirical range of 28-32 MeV.
* Symmetry energy for unpolarized nuclear matter at the empirical saturation density (0.16 fm⁻³) and zero temperature: about 26 MeV, which is less than the empirical value.
* The ratio det(1/χ)/det(1/χ_F) for hot asymmetric nuclear matter is always positive at all densities and temperatures examined, including at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ for various isospin asymmetry parameters and temperatures up to 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LOCV calculations at zero temperature for spin-polarized neutron matter (Bigdeli and Bordbar, 2009), symmetric nuclear matter (Bigdeli and Bordbar, 2009), and asymmetric nuclear matter (Bigdeli and Bordbar, 2009) to finite temperature. It also extends the finite-temperature LOCV calculations of Modarres (1993, 1995, 1997, 1998) for unpolarized systems to the spin-polarized case. The results agree with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Bombaci et al. (2006) using the AV18 potential and with Monte Carlo calculations by Fantoni et al. (2001), all finding no ferromagnetic transition, but contradict calculations using Skyrme-like interactions (Rios et al., 2005) and the D1P Gogny force (Lopez-Val et al., 2005) that did find such a transition. The work enables further studies of neutrino mean free paths in protoneutron stars and the cooling of neutron stars.
