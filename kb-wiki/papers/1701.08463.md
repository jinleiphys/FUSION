# Strangeon and Strangeon Star

> The nature of pulsar-like compact stars is essentially a central question of the fundamental strong interaction (explained in quantum chromo-dynamics) at low energy scale, the solution of which still remains a challenge though tremendous efforts have been tried. This kind of compact objects could actually be strange quark stars if strange quark matter in bulk may constitute the true ground state of the strong-interaction matter rather than Fe^56 (the so-called Witten's conjecture). From astrophysical points of view, however, it is proposed that strange cluster matter could be absolutely stable and thus those compact stars could be strange cluster stars in fact. This proposal could be regarded as a general Witten's conjecture: strange matter in bulk could be absolutely stable, in which quarks are either free (for strange quark matter) or localized (for strange cluster matter). Strange cluster with three-light-flavor symmetry is renamed strangeon, being coined by combining "strange nucleon" for the sake of simplicity. A strangeon star can then be thought as a 3-flavored gigantic nucleus, and strangeons are its constituent as an analogy of nucleons which are the constituent of a normal (micro) nucleus. The observational consequences of strangeon stars show that different manifestations of pulsar-like compact stars could be understood in the regime of strangeon stars, and we are expecting more evidence for strangeon star by advanced facilities (e.g., FAST, SKA, and eXTP).

# Strangeon and Strangeon Star

## Key claim

The authors propose that pulsar-like compact stars may be "strangeon stars", 3-flavored gigantic nuclei composed of strangeons (clusters of roughly equal numbers of up, down, and strange quarks), rather than conventional neutron stars or strange quark stars, but this remains a conjecture pending observational confirmation, as the non-perturbative nature of QCD at realistic stellar densities prevents a first-principles proof.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical motivation for strangeon matter by comparing energy scales: at baryon density ~0.16 fm⁻³, the typical momentum scale ~400 MeV exceeds the strange quark mass (~100 MeV), making three-flavor symmetry energetically possible for macroscopic nuclei. The authors estimate the QCD coupling constant using a Dyson-Schwinger-Equation parameterization, finding α_s > 2 at quark number density ~3 n₀ (momentum squared ~0.16 GeV²), which implies strongly coupled (non-perturbative) conditions. They then survey observational consequences of a solid strangeon star model, surface properties (drifting sub-pulses, clean fireballs for supernovae/GRBs, non-atomic spectra, absorption lines, strangeness barrier) and global properties (stiff equation of state, self-bound low masses, anisotropic pressure and starquakes, rigidity), comparing these to existing pulsar, magnetar, and X-ray source data.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark current mass: ~100 MeV; up/down quark current masses: a few MeV
- Baryon density of nuclear matter: n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³; quark separation at this density: Δx ~ 0.5 fm; corresponding energy scale: E_nucl ~ 400 MeV (from Heisenberg relation Δx·pc ~ ħc ≈ 200 MeV·fm)
- Electron Compton wavelength: λ_e ~ 10³ fm; threshold for three-flavor symmetry in a gigantic nucleus: nucleon number A > 10⁹
- QCD coupling constant at momentum squared p² ≈ 0.16 GeV²: α_s > 2 (from Dyson-Schwinger-Equation parameterization with α_s(0) = 2.972)
- Fermi energy of electrons in two-flavor macro-nucleus: E_F ~ 100 MeV; in three-flavor case: E_F ~ 10 MeV (charge per baryon ~10⁻⁵)
- Maximum strangeon star mass: > 2 M_⊙; minimum mass: < 10⁻² M_⊙
- Observed mass of PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙; PSR J2317+1439: > 2.04 M_⊙ (90% confidence)
- Energy released in SGR superflares: 10⁴⁴–10⁴⁷ erg (attributed to type II giant starquakes)
- Melting temperature for liquid-solid phase transition in proto-strangeon star: ~1 MeV
- Absorption features in CCO 1E 1207.4-5209: ~0.7 keV and ~1.4 keV; absorption lines in SGR 1806-20 burst spectrum: ~17.5, 11.2, 7.5, 5.0 keV
- Redshifted O VIII Ly-α emission line: z = 0.009 (from 4U 1700+24)

## Context

This work extends the original "Witten's conjecture" (Witten 1984), that strange quark matter in bulk could be the true ground state of strong-interaction matter, to a generalized form where quarks may be localized in clusters (strangeons) rather than free. It builds on the authors' prior concept of "strange quark-cluster" matter (Xu 2003) and earlier work on H-dibaryon clusters (LGX 2013). The paper contrasts strangeon stars with conventional neutron star models (hadron stars and hybrid/mixed stars, which are gravity-bound) and with MIT bag model strange quark stars (Alcock 1986) and color-superconductivity models (Alford 2008), arguing that perturbative QCD treatments are inadequate at realistic stellar densities. The observational program enabled includes tests with FAST, SKA, and eXTP, and gravitational wave detection of kilo-Hz signals from mergers or starquakes of solid strangeon stars.
