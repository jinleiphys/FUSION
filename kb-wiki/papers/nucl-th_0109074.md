# Charge-Symmetry Violation in Pion Scattering from Three-Body Nuclei

> We discuss the experimental and theoretical status of charge-symmetry violation (CSV) in the elastic scattering of pi+ and pi- on 3H and 3He. Analysis of the experimental data for the ratios r1, r2, and R at Tpi = 142, 180, 220, and 256 MeV provides evidence for the presence of CSV. We describe pion scattering from the three-nucleon system in terms of single- and double-scattering amplitudes. External and internal Coulomb interactions as well as the Delta-mass splitting are taken into account as sources of CSV. Reasonable agreement between our theoretical calculations and the experimental data is obtained for Tpi = 180, 220, and 256 MeV. For these energies, it is found that the Delta-mass splitting and the internal Coulomb interaction are the most important contributions for CSV in the three-nucleon system. The CSV effects are rather sensitive to the choice of pion-nuclear scattering mechanisms, but at the same time, our theoretical predictions are much less sensitive to the choice of the nuclear wave function. It is found, however, that data for r2 and R at Tpi = 142 MeV do not agree with the predictions of our model, which may indicate that there are additional mechanisms for CSV which are important only at lower energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charge-symmetry violation (CSV) in elastic pion scattering from three-body nuclei is experimentally significant and can be largely explained by the combined effects of the Delta-mass splitting and internal Coulomb interactions, but the model fails to reproduce the data for the ratios r2 and R at Tpi = 142 MeV, suggesting additional lower-energy CSV mechanisms.

## Method

The authors model pion elastic scattering from 3H and 3He using single- and double-scattering amplitudes in the Delta33 resonance region, with the elementary piN amplitude taken as the P33 partial wave. They incorporate three CSV sources: external Coulomb interactions between charged pions and nuclei, the mass splitting of different charge states of the Delta33 isobar, and internal Coulomb effects from the difference between 3H and 3He wave functions due to proton-proton repulsion in 3He. The theoretical ratios r1, r2, and R are computed and compared with experimental data at four incident pion energies.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ratios r2 and R reach approximately 1.2 at 180 MeV near 80 degrees in the center-of-mass frame, showing up to about 20% deviation from unity.
- The Delta33 isobar mass splitting parameters used are b = 1.38 MeV and c = 0.13 MeV, with average resonance mass 1232 MeV and width Gamma_Delta = 120 MeV.
- The Gaussian wave function uses a slope parameter b = 1.65 fm, chosen to fit the 3H charge form factor below momentum transfer Q = 400 MeV/c.
- The two-component Gaussian wave function uses parameters D1 = 1, D2 = -1.9, alpha1 = 0.70 fm^-2, alpha2 = 2.24 fm^-2, reproducing the 3He charge form factor minimum at Q = 670 MeV/c.
- The deuteron asymmetry A_pi is only about 2 percent, compared to the much larger CSV effects in the three-nucleon system.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of CSV in pion-deuteron scattering by Masterson et al. (1982) and Baru et al., where only small differences were found, to the three-nucleon system where CSV effects are much more pronounced. It builds on prior analyses of pion-3H/3He scattering by Kim, Kamalov et al., and Gmitro and Gashi, which used first-order optical potentials or isospin-symmetric wave functions without achieving a reasonable description of CSV. The results enable future investigation of additional CSV mechanisms that may become important at lower energies near 142 MeV, where the model fails to reproduce the data.
