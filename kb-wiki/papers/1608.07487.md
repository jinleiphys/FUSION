# The Algebraic Cluster Model: Structure of 16O

> We discuss an algebraic treatment of four-body clusters which includes both continuous and discrete symmetries. In particular, tetrahedral configurations with T(d) symmetry are analyzed with respect to the energy spectrum, transition form factors and B(EL) values. It is concluded that the low-lying spectrum of 16O can be described by four alpha-particles at the vertices of a regular tetrahedron, not as a rigid structure but rather a more floppy structure with relatively large rotation-vibration interactions and Coriolis forces.

## Key claim

The low-lying spectrum of ¹⁶O can be described by four alpha particles at the vertices of a regular tetrahedron, but not as a rigid structure; rather, the description requires a floppy structure with relatively large rotation-vibration interactions and Coriolis forces.

## Method

The authors develop a U(10) algebraic cluster model (ACM) for four-body systems, quantizing the three Jacobi coordinates (ρ, λ, η) with vector boson operators plus an additional scalar boson, under conservation of total boson number N. The Hamiltonian is constructed to be invariant under the tetrahedral group T_d ∼ S_4, and eigenvalues and eigenfunctions are obtained by matrix diagonalization in a coupled harmonic oscillator basis rather than by solving coupled differential equations. Transition form factors and B(EL) values are calculated by expressing the transition operators in terms of the algebraic operators, using the dipole operators D̂_ρ, D̂_λ, D̂_η as algebraic counterparts of the Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium configuration of the tetrahedral top has coordinates of equal length q_0 = √(2R²/(1+R²)), with ζ_0 = arctan √2 and χ_0 = π/4, and mutual angles θ_{ρλ,0} = θ_{λη,0} = θ_{ηρ,0} = π/4.
- The vibrational energy spectrum of the spherical top with tetrahedral symmetry is E_{3,vib} = ω₁(v₁ + 1/2) + ω₂(v₂ + 1) + ω₃(v₃ + 3/2), where v₁ is a symmetric stretching A₁ vibration, v₂ a doubly degenerate E vibration, and v₃ a three-fold degenerate F₂ vibration.
- The vibrational frequencies are ω₁ = 4NR²ξ₁, ω₂ = 8NR²/(1+R²) ξ₂, ω₃ = 8NR²/(1+R²) ξ₃; for rigid configurations R²=1 and ω_i = 4Nξ_i.
- The rotational energies of the ground state band are given by E_{3,rot} = κ₁ L(L+1), with states characterized by L=I.
- The ground state band rotational levels have angular momentum and parity L^P = 0⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 6±, … for symmetric states with t=A₁.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic cluster model (ACM) previously applied to three-alpha systems in ¹²C (ACM1, ACM2) to four-body cluster systems, specifically ¹⁶O as a 4α configuration. The U(10) ACM builds on the earlier U(7) model for three-body systems and the U(4) vibron model for two-body systems, and is related to the interacting boson model (IBM) for collective nuclear states. The results show evidence for T_d symmetry in ¹⁶O and open the way for applications to other four-body cluster systems with generic geometric configurations.
