# Meson-Meson Interactions and Resonances in the 't Hooft Model

> We studied meson-meson interactions using the 't Hooft model, which represents QCD in 1+1 dimensions and assumes a large number of colors ($N_c$). The dominant interactions in this large $N_c$ limit are generated by quark exchange. Our results show that QCD in 1+1 dimensions allows the realization of a constituent-type quark model for the mesons, and generates a scalar ``$σ$''-like meson-meson resonance, whose effective coupling and mass are determined by the underlying QCD dynamics. These results suggest an interpretation of the lightest scalar mesons as $q\bar{q} q \bar{q}$ systems.

# Meson-Meson Interactions and Resonances in the 't Hooft Model

## Key claim

The 't Hooft model in 1+1 dimensions with large $N_c$ generates a scalar "sigma"-like meson-meson resonance from quark-exchange diagrams alone, but only within a limited range of coupling constants where dressed quark masses remain real; for couplings exceeding $g = 81.64$ MeV the constituent quark interpretation breaks down and the resonance moves away from threshold.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark bound states in the 't Hooft model using light-cone gauge, expanding the wave function in cubic splines and solving the resulting linear matrix equation with standard eigenvalue routines. They then computed meson-meson scattering amplitudes from the leading-order quark-exchange box and crossed-box diagrams (eight diagrams total when accounting for flavor interchange), performing the $k_+$ integration analytically and the $k_-$ integration numerically with convergence criteria of less than 1% relative change under parameter doubling.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass was fixed to 140 MeV by tuning bare quark masses and the coupling $g$, with the bare mass ratio fixed to $m_{01}/m_{02} = 3/4$.
- The maximum coupling for physical (real) dressed masses is $g = 81.64$ MeV for the first flavor and $g = 100.8$ MeV for the second flavor.
- For Model I ($g = 1$ MeV): resonance energy $E_R = 280.0$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 0.07$ MeV.
- For Model II ($g = 20.1$ MeV): resonance energy $E_R = 282.4$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 16.4$ MeV.
- For Model III ($g = 80$ MeV): resonance energy $E_R = 280.7$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 23.2$ MeV.
- For Model IV ($g = 2500$ MeV): resonance energy $E_R = 300.0$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 64.7$ MeV; dressed masses become imaginary in this model.
- The bare mass of the first quark as a function of coupling is $m_{01} = 60.57 - 0.178\,g$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft model (1974) to meson-meson scattering, using the large $N_c$ limit where quark exchange dominates over gluon exchange. It contrasts with the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach of Cotanch (2002) which uses Euclidean metric and an effective $q\bar q$ interaction in 3+1 dimensions; the present calculation uses Minkowski metric throughout. A lattice calculation by Jaffe (2003) indicated a possible $q\bar q q\bar q$ bound state just below threshold in pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar s-wave scattering, and the authors interpret their near-threshold resonance as supporting the relevance of $q\bar q q\bar q$ structure for light scalar mesons, while noting that inclusion of gluon exchange and higher-order $1/N_c$ terms would be needed for a more realistic description.
