# Poincare' covariant light-front spectral function and transverse momentum distribution

> In valence approximation the fermion correlator is simply related to the light-front spectral function. Then the leading twist time-reversal even transverse momentum distributions can be explicitly obtained from the light-front wave function of the system and the twist-three distributions are linear combinations of the transverse distributions at leading twist.

# Poincaré covariant light-front spectral function and transverse momentum distributions

## Key claim
The authors show that, in valence approximation, the six time-reversal even (T-even) leading-twist transverse momentum distributions (TMDs) can be expressed as linear combinations of six scalar functions derived from the light-front wave function, and that previously proposed linear relations among TMDs hold exactly only when the contribution from orbital angular momentum L=2 in the one-body off-diagonal density matrix is absent, while a quadratic relation from prior work does not hold even in that case.

## Method
The authors construct a Poincaré covariant light-front spin-dependent spectral function for a spin-1/2 system composed of three fermions (such as 3He or a nucleon in valence approximation), using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of Poincaré generators. They integrate the spectral function over the intrinsic energy of the (A-1) subsystem to obtain the spin-dependent momentum distribution, expressed through six scalar functions b_i,M(x,k_perp). By evaluating traces of the fermion correlator and relating them to traces of the spectral function, they derive explicit relations between the leading-twist TMDs and these scalar functions, and similarly obtain twist-three TMDs in terms of the same functions.

## Key numbers
- The spin-dependent momentum distribution is expressed through six scalar functions b_i,M(x,k_perp) for i=0,1,...,5, obtained from the light-front wave function in momentum space.
- Only orbital angular momentum values L=0 or L=2 are allowed in the decomposition of the one-body off-diagonal density matrix.
- The linear relation Delta f = Delta'_T f + (|p_perp|^2 / (2 M^2)) * h^perp_1T and the relation g_1T = -h^perp_1L hold exactly when the L=2 contribution is absent.
- The twist-three TMDs are related to twist-two TMDs through the same linear relations found in Bacchetta (2007), once gluon contributions are disregarded.

## Context
This work extends the Poincaré covariant light-front framework previously developed by the authors (Pace, Salmè, Scopetta, 2015) for three-body systems, embedding successful few-nucleon phenomenology while satisfying normalization and momentum sum rules. It builds upon the light-cone model studies of TMDs by Barone (2002) and the linear relations among transverse parton distributions discussed by JMR (2008) and BP (2009). The approach enables evaluation of TMDs for a nucleon in 3He, which could be extracted from spin asymmetry measurements in 3He(e,e'p) experiments at high momentum transfer, and preliminary results for the EMC effect in 3He show improvements over a convolution approach with a momentum distribution.
