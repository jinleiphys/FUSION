# $Ξ(1690)^-$ production in the $K^-p\to K^+K^-Λ$ reaction process near threshold

> We investigate $Ξ(1690)^-$ production from the $K^-p\to K^+K^-Λ$ reaction within the effective Lagrangian approach at the tree-level Born approximation. We consider the $s$- and $u$-channel $Σ/Λ$ ground states and resonances for the $Ξ$-pole contributions, in addition to the $s$-channel $Λ$, $u$-channel nucleon pole, and $t$-channel $K^-$-exchange for the $ϕ$-pole contributions. The $Ξ$-pole includes $Ξ(1320)$, $Ξ(1535)$, $Ξ(1690)(J^p=1/2^-)$,and $Ξ(1820)(J^p=3/2^-)$. We calculate the Dalitz plot density of $(d^2σ/dM_{K^+K^-}dM_{K^-Λ}$ at 4.2 GeV$/c$) and the total cross sections for the $K^-p\to K^+K^-Λ$ reaction near the threshold. The calculation results are in good agreement with previously acquired experimental data. Using the parameters from the fit, we present the total and differential cross sections for the two-body $K^-p\to K^+Ξ(1690)^-$ reaction near the threshold. In our calculation, a strong enhancement at backward $K^+$ angles is predicted because of the dominant $u$-channel contribution. We also demonstrate that the Dalitz plot analysis for $p_{K^-}=1.915 -- 2.065$ GeV/c enables us to access direct information regarding $Ξ(1690)^-$ production, which can be tested by future $K^-$ beam experiments. The possible spin-parity states of $Ξ(1690)^-$ are briefly discussed as well.

## Key claim

The authors model the $K^-p\to K^+K^-\Lambda$ reaction using an effective Lagrangian approach at tree-level Born approximation, finding that the Dalitz plot analysis for beam momenta between 1.915 and 2.065 GeV/c can provide direct information on $\Xi(1690)^-$ production, but the calculation assumes $J^P=1/2^-$ for $\Xi(1690)^-$ as favored by BaBar and theoretical works, while the spin-parity remains experimentally undetermined.

## Method

The authors computed tree-level Feynman diagrams for the $K^-p\to K^+K^-\Lambda$ reaction, including $s$- and $u$-channel $\Sigma/\Lambda$ ground states and resonances for $\Xi$-pole contributions ($\Xi(1320)$, $\Xi(1535)$, $\Xi(1690)$, $\Xi(1820)$), and $s$-channel $\Lambda$, $u$-channel nucleon pole, and $t$-channel $K^-$-exchange for $\phi(1020)$-pole contributions. They used effective Lagrangian densities with coupling constants taken from the Nijmegen soft-core potential model, chiral unitary model, and SU(6) relativistic quark model, and introduced phenomenological form factors with cutoff parameters fitted to reproduce experimental data from Gay et al. (1976) at 4.2 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for $K^-p\to K^+\Xi(1690)^-$ peaks at $p_{K^-}=2.6$ GeV/c ($E_{\rm cm}=2.47$ GeV) with a value of $1.5~\mu$b.
- The threshold beam momentum for $K^-p\to K^+K^-\Lambda$ is 1.687 GeV/c; for $K^-p\to K^+\Xi(1690)^-$ it is 1.878 GeV/c.
- Cutoff parameters for form factors: $\Lambda_{\Xi(1322)}=1.3$ GeV, $\Lambda_{\Xi(1532)}=1.3$ GeV, $\Lambda_{\Xi(1690)}=0.75$ GeV, $\Lambda_{\Xi(1820)}=1.1$ GeV, $\Lambda_\phi=0.44$ GeV.
- Full decay widths: $\Gamma_{\Xi(1532)}=9.1$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\Xi(1690)}=6$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\Xi(1820)}=24$ MeV.
- Branching ratios from chiral unitary model: $\Gamma_{\Xi(1690)\to\overline{K}\Lambda}/\Gamma_{\Xi(1690)}=0.271$, $\Gamma_{\Xi(1690)\to\overline{K}\Sigma}/\Gamma_{\Xi(1690)}=0.533$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\Xi(1690)^-$ production, which has been observed by Dionisi et al. (1982), Biagi et al. (1981, 1983), and Belle, with BaBar favoring $J=1/2$ assignment. The authors build on the effective Lagrangian approach used by Shyam et al. (2011) for $K^-p\to K^+\Xi^-$ and by Nam et al. (2004) for similar reactions, and they note that bubble chamber data from Schlein et al. (1969), Badier et al. (1973), Bellefon et al. (1975), and Gay et al. (1976) provide the only existing experimental constraints. The calculation enables future experiments at J-PARC using the Hyperon Spectrometer at the K1.8 beam line to determine the spin-parity of $\Xi(1690)^-$ via double-polarization asymmetry measurements.
