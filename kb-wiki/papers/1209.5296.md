# Chiral Fermi liquid approach to neutron matter

> We present a microscopic calculation of the complete quasiparticle interaction, including central as well as noncentral components, in neutron matter from high-precision two- and three-body forces derived within the framework of chiral effective field theory. The contributions from two-nucleon forces are computed in many-body perturbation theory to first and second order (without any simplifying approximations). In addition we include the leading-order one-loop diagrams from the N2LO chiral three-nucleon force, which contribute to all Fermi liquid parameters except those associated with the center-of-mass tensor interaction. The relative-momentum dependence of the quasiparticle interaction is expanded in Legendre polynomials up to L=2. Second-order Pauli blocking and medium polarization effects act coherently in specific channels, namely for the Landau parameters f_1, h_0 and g_0, which results in a dramatic increase in the quasiparticle effective mass as well as a decrease in both the effective tensor force and the neutron matter spin susceptibility. For densities greater than about half nuclear matter saturation density ρ_0, the contributions to the Fermi liquid parameters from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force scale in all cases approximately linearly with the nucleon density. The largest effect of the three-nucleon force is to generate a strongly repulsive effective interaction in the isotropic spin-independent channel. We show that the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force leads to an increase in the spin susceptibility of neutron matter, but we observe no evidence for a ferromagnetic spin instability in the vicinity of the saturation density ρ_0. This work sets the foundation for future studies of neutron matter response to weak and electromagnetic probes with applications to neutron star structure and evolution.

## Key claim

The authors present a microscopic calculation of the complete quasiparticle interaction (including central and noncentral components) in neutron matter from chiral two- and three-body forces, finding that second-order Pauli blocking and medium polarization effects act coherently in specific channels to produce a dramatic increase in the quasiparticle effective mass and a decrease in the spin susceptibility, but they observe no evidence for a ferromagnetic spin instability in the vicinity of nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The quasiparticle interaction was computed in many-body perturbation theory to first and second order from high-precision chiral two-nucleon forces (without simplifying approximations), plus leading-order one-loop diagrams from the N2LO chiral three-nucleon force. The relative-momentum dependence of the interaction was expanded in Legendre polynomials up to L=2, and the scalar functions multiplying the central, spin-spin, exchange tensor, center-of-mass tensor, and cross-vector operators were extracted from spin-space matrix elements using a partial-wave decomposition. The two-nucleon potential was evolved via renormalization-group methods to a cutoff scale of approximately 2.0 fm^-1 to improve convergence.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle effective mass M* is given by M*/M_n = 1 + F_1/3, where M_n = 939.6 MeV is the free neutron mass.
- The neutron matter spin susceptibility is given by chi = mu_n^2 * N_0 / (1 + G_0), where mu_n = -1.913 (free-space neutron magnetic moment in nuclear magnetons) and N_0 = k_f M*/pi^2.
- For the chiral three-nucleon force combined with the bare chiral N3LO potential, the low-energy constants are c_1 = -0.81 GeV^-1 and c_3 = -3.2 GeV^-1.
- For the chiral three-nucleon force combined with the low-momentum potential V_low-k, the low-energy constants are c_1 = -0.76 GeV^-1 and c_3 = -4.78 GeV^-1.
- The compression modulus of neutron matter is given by K = (3k_F^2/M*) (1 + F_0), where F_0 = N_0 f_0.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2011, 2012) from symmetric nuclear matter to pure neutron matter, and it generalizes earlier Fermi liquid studies of neutron matter that used microscopic two-body forces or phenomenological Skyrme and Gogny interactions (e.g., Fantoni et al. 2001, Rios et al. 2005, Garcia et al. 2009). The results set the foundation for future studies of neutron matter response to weak and electromagnetic probes, with applications to neutron star structure and evolution, particularly regarding neutrino emissivity and the spin response to strong magnetic fields.
