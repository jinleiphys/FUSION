# The properties of the three-nucleon system with the dressed-bag model for $NN$ interaction. II: Coulomb and CSB effects

> Coulomb and charge-symmetry breaking effects in the $^3$He ground state within the dressed dibaryon model developed recently for $2N$ and $3N$ forces are examined in detail. Particular attention has been paid to the Coulomb displacement energy $ΔE_C=E_B(^3{\rm H})- E_B(^3{\rm He})$ and rms charge radii of $^3$H and $^3$He. A new scalar $3N$ force between the third nucleon a$ dibaryon is found to be very important for a correct description of the Coulomb energy and rms charge radius in $^3$He. In view of the new results for $ΔE_C$ obtained here, the role of the effects of charge symmetry breaking in the nuclear force is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the long-standing puzzle of the Coulomb displacement energy ΔE_C (the difference between the binding energies of ³H and ³He) can be explained without free parameters by a new scalar three-nucleon force arising from sigma-meson exchange between a dressed dibaryon and the third nucleon, but this explanation depends on the specific model assumptions and the accepted value of the nn scattering length, which remains uncertain.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations of the ³He and ³H ground states using a multicomponent dressed-bag model (DBM) that includes both a three-nucleon channel and three additional channels involving a dressed dibaryon (six-quark bag) interacting with the third nucleon. They incorporated Coulomb effects via a "smeared" Coulomb interaction that accounts for finite charge radii of the nucleon (0.87 fm) and dibaryon (0.6 fm), and they included a new three-body Coulomb force between the charged bag and the third nucleon. The calculations used a Gaussian basis with nonlinear parameters chosen on a Chebyshev grid, and the matrix elements for the three-body Coulomb interaction were reduced to analytical formulas.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb displacement energy ΔE_C = E_B(³H) - E_B(³He) is calculated as 710 keV for DBM(I) and 692 keV for DBM(II), compared to the experimental value of approximately 760 keV and the conventional theoretical value of about 640 keV (which gives a gap of ca. 120 keV).
- The total weight of all 6qN components P_6qN in ³H is 10.99% for DBM(I) and 7.39% for DBM(II); in ³He it is 10.80% for DBM(I) and 7.26% for DBM(II).
- The three-body force contribution V^(3N) to the total Hamiltonian expectation is -7.15 MeV for ³H in DBM(I) and -4.69 MeV in DBM(II), compared to -1.19 MeV for the conventional AV18+UIX model.
- The kinetic energy T is 112.8 MeV for ³H in DBM(I) and 112.4 MeV in DBM(II), compared to 51.4 MeV for AV18+UIX.
- The rms charge radius of the proton is taken as 0.87 fm, and the rms charge radius of the dibaryon is taken as 0.6 fm.
- The smeared Coulomb interaction parameters are α_NN^(-1/2) = 1.005 fm and α_6qN^(-1/2) = 0.863 fm.

## Context

This work extends the dressed-bag model developed by the Moscow-Tübingen group (Kukulin et al., 2002; 2003) to include Coulomb and charge-symmetry breaking effects in the three-nucleon system, addressing the long-standing ΔE_C puzzle first identified in Faddeev calculations by Gignoux (mid-1960s). The authors contrast their approach with the conventional explanation by Nogga et al. (2000), who attributed the 120 keV gap to differences in pp and nn scattering lengths, noting that this explanation depends on the uncertain nn scattering length value. The new scalar three-nucleon force, which is much stronger than conventional three-nucleon forces associated with intermediate Δ-isobar formation and two-pion exchange (reviewed by the 3NF community), provides an alternative explanation and enables further tests of the dressed-bag model in other nuclear systems.
