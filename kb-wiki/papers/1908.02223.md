# Neutron valence structure from nuclear deep inelastic scattering

> Mechanisms of spin-flavor SU(6) symmetry breaking in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) are studied via an extraction of the free neutron structure function from a global analysis of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) data on the proton and on nuclei from $A = 2$ (deuterium) to 208 (lead). Modification of the structure function of nucleons bound in atomic nuclei (known as the EMC effect) are consistently accounted for within the framework of a universal modification of nucleons in short-range correlated (SRC) pairs. Our extracted neutron-to-proton structure function ratio $F_2^n/F_2^p$ becomes constant for $x_B \ge 0.6$, equalling $0.47 \pm 0.04$ as $x_B \rightarrow 1$, in agreement with theoretical predictions of perturbative QCD and the Dyson Schwinger equation, and in disagreement with predictions of the Scalar Diquark dominance model. We also predict $F_2^{^3\mathrm{He}}/F_2^{^3\mathrm{H}}$, recently measured, yet unpublished, by the MARATHON collaboration, the nuclear correction function that is needed to extract $F_2^n/F_2^p$ from $F_2^{^3\mathrm{He}}/F_2^{^3\mathrm{H}}$, and the theoretical uncertainty associated with this extraction.

## Key claim

The extracted free neutron-to-proton structure function ratio \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) becomes constant for \(x_B \ge 0.6\), equalling \(0.47 \pm 0.04\) as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\), in agreement with perturbative QCD and Dyson-Schwinger Equation predictions, but in disagreement with the Scalar Diquark dominance model. This result depends on consistently accounting for nuclear medium effects (the EMC effect) via a universal modification of nucleons in short-range correlated pairs, using data from nuclei ranging from deuterium to lead.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) data on the proton and on nuclei from \(A = 2\) (deuterium) to 208 (lead), modeling the nuclear structure function as a sum of contributions from unmodified uncorrelated nucleons and from modified nucleons in proton-neutron short-range correlated (SRC) pairs. They extracted a nucleus-independent universal modification function (UMF) using Bayesian inference via a Hamiltonian Markov Chain Monte Carlo (HMCMC) from \(F_2^A/F_2^d\) data for \(0.08 \le x_B \le 0.95\) in eight nuclei (\({}^{3}\)He, \({}^{4}\)He, \({}^{9}\)Be, \({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{27}\)Al, \({}^{56}\)Fe, \({}^{197}\)Au, and \({}^{208}\)Pb). This UMF was then used to correct deuterium DIS data for bound-nucleon structure modification effects and to extract \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) up to \(x_B \approx 0.9\).

## Key numbers

- The extracted \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) ratio as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\) equals \(0.47 \pm 0.04\).
- The ratio becomes approximately constant starting at \(x_B \approx 0.6\).
- The analysis used DIS data for \(0.08 \le x_B \le 0.95\) from eight nuclei: \({}^{3}\)He, \({}^{4}\)He, \({}^{9}\)Be, \({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{27}\)Al, \({}^{56}\)Fe, \({}^{197}\)Au, and \({}^{208}\)Pb.
- The model uncertainty in extracting \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) from the MARATHON \(F_2^{^{3}\mathrm{He}}/F_2^{^{3}\mathrm{H}}\) measurement can be as high as \(\sim 25\%\) for current realistic models.
- The predicted \(F_2^{^{3}\mathrm{He}}/F_2^{^{3}\mathrm{H}}\) ratio is compared with models by Kulagin and Petti (KP) and Tropiano et al. (TEMS), with differences at \(x_B > 0.7\) creating a \(\sim 10\%\) difference in the extracted \(F_2^n/F_2^p\).

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) that only used deuterium DIS data, such as those by CTEQ (CT14), CTEQ-JLab (CJ15), and Arrington et al. (2011), which either could not discriminate among theoretical predictions or preferred the scalar diquark prediction. By including DIS data from nuclei heavier than deuterium and consistently accounting for the EMC effect via a universal modification function for SRC pairs, the authors obtain a result that agrees with perturbative QCD and Dyson-Schwinger Equation predictions. The analysis also provides predictions for the MARATHON experiment's measurement of \(F_2^{^{3}\mathrm{He}}/F_2^{^{3}\mathrm{H}}\) and quantifies the model uncertainty in extracting \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) from that data, enabling future independent verification.
