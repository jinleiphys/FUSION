# WKB expansion for the angular momentum and the Kepler problem: from the torus quantization to the exact one

> We calculate the WKB series for the angular momentum and the non--relativistic 3-dim Kepler problem. This is the first semiclassical treatment of the angular momentum for terms beyond the leading WKB approximation. We explain why the torus quantization (the leading WKB term) of the full problem is exact, even if the individual torus quantization of the angular momentum and of the radial Kepler problem separately is not exact. PACS numbers: 03.65.-w, 03.65.Ge, 03.65.Sq

## Key claim

The paper shows that the torus quantization (leading-order WKB) of the full three-dimensional Kepler problem yields the exact energy spectrum, even though the individual torus quantizations of the angular momentum and the radial Kepler problem separately are not exact; this exactness arises because the higher-order quantum corrections from the two separated problems compensate each other term by term when combined.

## Method

The authors perform a systematic WKB expansion for the angular momentum eigenvalue problem and for the radial Kepler problem, calculating the first few higher-order terms beyond the leading Bohr-Sommerfeld (torus) quantization. For the angular momentum, they introduce a small parameter epsilon to organize the WKB series and compute the phase integrals up to second order, then conjecture the general form for all even-order terms. For the radial Kepler problem, they compute the WKB phase integrals up to fourth order in hbar, using the exact quantized angular momentum, and again conjecture the general formula for all higher-order terms.

## Key numbers

*   For the angular momentum, the quantization condition up to second order is lambda + 1/(8 lambda) = l + 1/2, where lambda = L/hbar and l = n_theta + n_phi.
*   The second-order WKB correction to the angular momentum quantization is given by the contour integral oint dS_2 = pi i / (4 lambda).
*   For the radial Kepler problem, the quantization condition up to fourth order in hbar is (alpha/sqrt(-2E) - L) - hbar^2/(8L) + hbar^4/(128 L^3) = (n_r + 1/2) hbar.
*   The second-order WKB correction for the radial problem is (hbar/i)^2 oint dsigma_2 = - hbar^2 pi/(4L).
*   The fourth-order WKB correction for the radial problem is (hbar/i)^4 oint dsigma_4 = hbar^4 pi/(64 L^3).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of higher-order WKB corrections beyond the leading torus quantization, which was previously known to be necessary for accurate energy level predictions in integrable systems (Prosen and Robnik 1993, Graffi, Manfredi and Salasnich 1994). The paper provides the first semiclassical treatment of the angular momentum beyond the leading WKB approximation and explains the curious exactness of the torus quantization for the full Kepler problem. This analysis enables future work on assessing the accuracy of semiclassical methods for more general integrable systems with two or more degrees of freedom, and for nonintegrable systems using methods like the Gutzwiller trace formula (Gutzwiller 1990).
