# J/Psi Suppression in an Equilibrating Parton Plasma

> Short-distance QCD is employed to calculate the $J/ψ$ survival probability in an equilibrating parton gas, whose evolution is governed by a set of master rate equations. Partons in the early stage of high-energy nuclear collisions may initially not be in equilibrium, but their average transverse momentum is sufficiently high to break up a $Q\bar{Q}$ bound state. Such a break-up during the evolution of the parton gas is shown to cause a substantial $J/ψ$ suppression at both RHIC and LHC energies, using realistic estimates of the initial parton densities. The transverse momentum dependence of the suppression is also shown to be sensitive to the initial conditions and the evolution history of the parton plasma.

# J/Psi Suppression in an Equilibrating Parton Plasma

## Key claim

The paper shows that substantial J/ψ suppression occurs in a deconfined but chemically non-equilibrating parton gas at both RHIC and LHC energies, but this suppression is smaller than what would occur in a fully equilibrated parton plasma. The transverse momentum dependence of the suppression is sensitive to the initial conditions and evolution history of the parton system, with the suppression decreasing at high transverse momentum due to the behavior of the gluon-J/ψ cross section at high gluon momenta.

## Method

The authors calculate the gluon-J/ψ dissociation cross section using short-distance QCD and an operator product expansion method, then thermally average this cross section over a Bose-Einstein gluon distribution with a fugacity parameter to account for chemical non-equilibrium. They model the time evolution of the parton gas using master rate equations for gluon and quark fugacities coupled to one-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion, with initial conditions from HIJING Monte Carlo simulations. The J/ψ survival probability is then computed by integrating the dissociation rate over the time the J/ψ spends inside the deconfined parton gas, accounting for its transverse motion and the system's cooling.

## Key numbers

- The gluon-J/ψ dissociation cross section peaks at gluon energy q⁰ = 10ε₀/7 = 0.92 GeV in the J/ψ rest frame, with a maximum value of about 3 mb.
- The binding energy ε₀ = 2M_D - M_J/ψ, with M_J/ψ and M_D the J/ψ and D meson masses respectively.
- The time to reach local momentum isotropy (kinetic thermalization) is estimated as τ₀ ~ 0.5-0.7 fm/c.
- At RHIC energy, the effective temperature drops below T_c ≈ 200 MeV in about 1-2 fm/c, and the parton system hardly reaches chemical equilibrium.
- At LHC energy, the deconfined state may exist for 4-5 fm/c, and the parton gas comes very close to chemical equilibrium.
- Using a Boltzmann distribution instead of Bose-Einstein changes the thermal cross section by about 20%.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Matsui and Satz (1986) that J/ψ suppression due to color screening can probe deconfinement, by calculating the suppression in a more realistic non-equilibrium parton gas rather than an ideal equilibrated plasma. The gluon-J/ψ dissociation cross section is based on the operator product expansion method of Kharzeev and Satz (1994), and the master rate equations for chemical equilibration follow the approach of Biro, Mueller, and Wang (1993). The results enable using the transverse momentum dependence of J/ψ suppression as a probe of the initial temperature and evolution history of the parton system produced in heavy-ion collisions, and motivate further studies that include pre-resonance suppression, nuclear effects on production, and possible J/ψ production via gluon fusion during the evolution.
