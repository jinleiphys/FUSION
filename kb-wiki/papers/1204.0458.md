# Solution of the Schrödinger equation containing a Perey-Buck nonlocality

> The solution of a radial Schrödinger equation for ψ(r) containing a nonlocal potential of the form \int{K(r,r') ψ(r') dr'} is obtained to high accuracy by means of two methods. An application to the Perey-Buck nonlocality is presented, without using a local equivalent representation. The first method consists in expanding ψ in a set of Chebyshev polynomials, and solving the matrix equation for the expansion coefficients numerically. An accuracy of between 1:10^{6} to 1:10^{14} is obtained, depending on the number of polynomials employed. The second method consists in expanding ψ into a set of N Sturmian functions of positive energy, supplemented by an iteration procedure. For N=15 an accuracy of 1:10^{4} is obtained without iterations. After one iteration the accuracy is increased to 1:10^{6}. The method is applicable to a general nonlocality K.

## Key claim

The paper presents two high-accuracy methods for solving the radial Schrödinger equation with a general nonlocal potential, applied specifically to the Perey-Buck nonlocality without recourse to a local equivalent potential; the spectral Chebyshev method achieves accuracies between 1:10^5 and 1:10^11 depending on the number of polynomials, while the Sturmian expansion method with 15 basis functions and one iteration reaches 1:10^6 accuracy.

## Method

The radial Schrödinger equation containing a nonlocal kernel K(r,r') is transformed into a Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation. The first method expands the wave function in Chebyshev polynomials over a single radial partition and uses singular value decomposition (SVD) to represent the nonlocal kernel, solving the resulting matrix equation for the expansion coefficients. The second method expands the wave function in a set of positive-energy Sturmian functions generated by an auxiliary Woods-Saxon potential, solves a matrix equation for the expansion coefficients, and optionally applies an iterative correction for the truncation error.

## Key numbers

- Spectral Chebyshev method with N=51 polynomials yields accuracy of 2×10^−5; with N=71 yields 10^−7; with N=301 yields 4×10^−11 (radial interval [0,20 fm], k=0.5 fm^−1)
- Computing time for spectral method: 0.4 s for N=51, 0.7 s for N=71, 13 s for N=301 (MATLAB on Intel TM2 Quad 2.83 GHz, 8 GB RAM)
- Sturmian expansion with N_S=15 basis functions and no iteration yields accuracy of 9×10^−5; after one iteration accuracy improves to 2×10^−6
- Sturmian expansion with N_S=20 basis functions and no iteration yields accuracy of 3×10^−6; after one iteration accuracy improves to 3×10^−7
- Computing time for Sturmian method with N_S=15: 11 s for the base solution, 0.04 s for one iteration
- Singular values of the Perey-Buck kernel (L=0) drop below 10^−5 for s>30, allowing truncation of the SVD expansion at s=30 for 1:10^5 accuracy
- Chebyshev expansion coefficients of the wave function reach magnitude ~10^−14 for index j=100 and beyond

## Context

This work extends prior methods for solving nonlocal Schrödinger equations, including the iterative Lanczos approach of Kim and Udagawa (1990), the perturbative SVD method of Essaid, and earlier Chebyshev spectral methods that were limited to semi-separable rank-one nonlocalities. The spectral method presented is less complex and requires less computing time than the Sturmian method, but the Sturmian approach is useful for applications such as describing resonances in low-energy nucleon-nucleus scattering and implementing the Pauli exclusion principle, as demonstrated by Canton. The methods enable accurate solutions for general nonlocal kernels without constructing local equivalent potentials, which have been widely used since Perey and Buck.
