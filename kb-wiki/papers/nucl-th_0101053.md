# Predicting total reaction cross sections for nucleon-nucleus scattering

> Nucleon total reaction and neutron total cross sections to 300 MeV for 12C and 208Pb, and for 65 MeV spanning the mass range, are predicted using coordinate space optical potentials formed by full folding of effective nucleon-nucleon interactions with realistic nuclear ground state densities. Good to excellent agreement is found with existing data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that total reaction cross sections for nucleon-nucleus scattering up to 300 MeV can be predicted using complex, nonlocal, coordinate-space optical potentials formed by full folding of effective nucleon-nucleon interactions with realistic nuclear ground state densities, without any *a posteriori* adjustment of results. The hedge is that the predictions show "good to excellent agreement" with existing data, but the authors note specific discrepancies: the model overestimates neutron total reaction cross section data for both 12C and 208Pb by 10-20%, and fails below 20 MeV for proton scattering on 12C due to discrete nuclear structure effects not captured by the first-order folding model.

## Method

The authors constructed coordinate-space optical potentials by full folding of effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, derived from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) g-matrices of the BonnB NN interaction, with ground state densities from nuclear structure models. For 12C they used a complete (0+2)hw shell model with the WBT interaction; for 208Pb they used both a simple packed shell model and a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) specification. They solved the resulting nonlocal Schrodinger equations to obtain phase shifts and then computed total reaction cross sections from the S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Proton total reaction cross section predictions at 65.5 MeV for a diverse set of nuclei from 9 to 208 mass number: g-folding results agree with experiment, while t-folding results consistently overestimate the data.
- For neutron total reaction cross sections from 12C, the g-folding predictions overestimate extracted experimental values by 10-20%.
- For proton scattering from 208Pb, the SHF-based predictions slightly underestimate data in the 10 to 40 MeV energy range.
- For proton scattering from 208Pb above 100 MeV, the SHF and 0hw model density predictions differ by about 100 mb.
- The g-folding predictions for neutron total cross sections from 208Pb reproduce the Ramsauer diffraction structure in the 30 to 200 MeV region better than the recent semi-microscopic optical model result.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic folding model approach, previously successful for differential cross sections and spin observables, to the prediction of integral observables (total reaction and total cross sections) for both proton and neutron scattering. It provides a predictive alternative to phenomenological Woods-Saxon optical potentials, which are subject to uncertainties and ambiguities that can yield different total reaction cross sections while fitting elastic angular distributions. The results enable more reliable inputs for accelerator-driven system (ADS) design, astrophysical nucleosynthesis calculations, and studies of neutron density distributions in nuclei, complementing parity-violating electron scattering experiments.
