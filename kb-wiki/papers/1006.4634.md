# An elementary stringy estimate of transport coefficients of large temperature QCD

> Modeling QCD at large temperature with a simple holographic five dimensional theory encoding minimal breaking of conformality, allows for the calculation of all the transport coefficients, up to second order, in terms of a single parameter. In particular, the shear and bulk relaxation times are provided. The result follows by deforming the AdS background with a scalar dual to a marginally relevant operator, at leading order in the deformation parameter.

## Key claim

Modeling QCD at large temperature with a simple holographic five-dimensional theory where conformality is broken at leading order by a marginally relevant operator allows all transport coefficients up to second order to be expressed in terms of a single parameter, the deviation from conformality \(\delta \equiv (1-3c_s^2)\). The results are valid only at first order in \(\delta\) and for theories where the dual scalar has the simplest possible exponential potential.

## Method

The authors consider a Chamblin-Reall model: a five-dimensional gravity theory with a single scalar field \(\phi\) and potential \(V(\phi) = V_0 e^{\epsilon \phi/V_0}\), dual to a marginally relevant operator. They exploit the fact that for specific semi-integer values of a parameter \(\sigma\) (related to the equation of state \(\varepsilon = (2\sigma-1)p\)), such models arise from toroidal dimensional reduction of pure gravity in higher dimensions. By analytically continuing the known hydrodynamic energy-momentum tensor from \(AdS_6\) (the nearest semi-integer case) back to the desired \(\sigma\), they obtain all transport coefficients at leading order in the deformation parameter \(\delta\).

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\), unchanged from the conformal value.
* Shear relaxation time: \(T\tau_\pi = (2-\log 2)/(2\pi) + [3(16-\pi^2)/(64\pi)]\delta\).
* Bulk viscosity to shear viscosity ratio: \(\zeta/\eta = (2/3)\delta\).
* Bulk relaxation time: \(T\tau_\Pi = (2-\log 2)/(2\pi)\), independent of \(\delta\) at leading order.
* At \(T \sim 1.5 T_c\) with \(c_s^2 \sim 0.283\) (from lattice data of Katz (2005)): \(T\tau_\pi = 0.222\), \(T\tau_\Pi = 0.208\), \(\zeta/\eta = 0.101\), \(T\kappa/s = 0.022\), \(T\lambda_1/s = 0.014\), \(T\lambda_2/s = -0.021\), \(T\kappa^*/s = -0.006\), \(T\tau_\pi^* = -0.031\), \(T\xi_1/s = 0.001\), \(T\xi_2/s = 0.001\), \(T\xi_5/s = 0.001\), \(T\xi_6/s = 0.004\), and \(\lambda_3 = \lambda_4 = \xi_3 = \xi_4 = 0\).
* At \(T \sim 3 T_c\), corrections to conformal values are below \(10\%\).

## Context

This work extends the conformal results for second-order transport coefficients from \({\cal N}=4\) SYM (Baier 2007) to a non-conformal setting, providing a holographic estimate for QCD where lattice results (Meyer 2007) have large uncertainties. The relations are derived from the general framework of Kanitscheider (2009) and Romatschke (2009), and apply to any theory with a marginally relevant deformation, including cascading plasmas (Gubser 2001) and D3D7 plasmas (Bigazzi 2009). The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC energies, particularly by providing the shear and bulk relaxation times which influence the plasma evolution.
