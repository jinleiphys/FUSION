# The role of the Pauli principle in three-cluster systems composed of identical clusters

> Within the microscopic model based on the algebraic version of the resonating group method the role of the Pauli principle in the formation of continuum wave function of nuclear systems composed of three identical $s$-clusters has been investigated. Emphasis is placed upon the study of the exchange effects contained in the genuine three-cluster norm kernel. Three-fermion, three-boson, three-dineutron ($3d'$) and $3α$ systems are considered in detail. Simple analytical method of constructing the norm kernel for $3α$ system is suggested. The Pauli-allowed basis functions for the $3α$ and $3d'$ systems are given in an explicit form and asymptotic behavior of these functions is established. Complete classification of the eigenfunctions and the eigenvalues of the $^{12}$C norm kernel by the $^8$Be$=α+α$ eigenvalues has been given for the first time. Spectrum of the $^{12}$C norm kernel is compared to that of the $^{5}$H system.

## Key claim

The Pauli principle in three-cluster systems composed of identical s-clusters can be fully characterized through the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the genuine three-cluster norm kernel, with the Pauli-allowed states for the 3α and 3d' systems being explicitly constructible and classifiable by an additional quantum number k that resolves the SU(3)-degeneracy problem, contrary to earlier approaches that lacked such classification.

## Method

The authors employed the algebraic version of the resonating group method (RGM) within a microscopic model, using a Fock-Bargmann space representation to construct the norm kernel for three-cluster systems. They derived explicit analytical expressions for the norm kernels of three-fermion, three-boson, three-dineutron (3d'), and 3α systems by expanding the overlap integrals of Slater determinants composed of modified Bloch-Brink orbitals, and solved the eigenvalue problem for the norm kernel using a transformation to complex vectors A and B that simplifies the SU(3) basis construction.

## Key numbers

- For the three-fermion system, at each number of oscillator quanta ν there are [ (ν − 2μ)/3 ] Pauli-allowed basis functions and one Pauli-forbidden state belonging to the same SU(3) representation (2ν − 4μ, 2μ), where [x] denotes the integer part.
- For the three-boson system, at each number of oscillator quanta there are [ (ν − 2μ)/3 ] + 1 basis functions belonging to the SU(3) representation (2ν − 4μ, 2μ) with zero orbital momentum, all of which are Pauli-allowed.
- The partial norm kernels i_{(λ̄,μ̄)} for three fermions are normalized to the dimensionality of the (λ̄,μ̄) SU(3)-representation: (λ̄+1)(μ̄+1)(λ̄+μ̄+2)/2.
- For the three-fermion system, the Pauli-allowed states have unit eigenvalues (Λ = 1) while the Pauli-forbidden states correspond to zero eigenvalues (Λ = 0).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach for three-cluster systems composed of an s-cluster and two neutrons (Lashko, 2008) to systems of three identical s-clusters, providing a complete classification of Pauli-allowed states that resolves the SU(3)-degeneracy problem left open by earlier studies such as Kamimura (1981) and Uegaki (1979). The method improves upon the Orthogonality Condition Model (OCM) of Saito (1977) and Horiuchi (1974, 1975), which lacked the ability to uniquely determine eigenfunctions in cases of SU(3)-degeneration, and upon the three-cluster equation of Fujiwara (2002) which omitted some exchange effects contained in the genuine three-cluster norm kernel. The results enable systematic truncation of model space consistent with the Pauli principle and provide insight into possible decay channels of three-cluster nuclei.
