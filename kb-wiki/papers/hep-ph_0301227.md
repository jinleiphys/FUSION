# Update on pion weak decay constants in nuclear matter

> The QCD sum rule calculation of the in-medium pion decay constants using pseudoscalar-axial vector correlation function, $i \int d^4x e^{ip\cdot x} < ρ| T[{\bar d}(x) i γ_5 u (x) {\bar u}(0) γ_μγ_5 d (0)] | ρ>$ is revisited. In particular, we argue that the dimension 5 condensate, $<{\bar q} (i D_0)^2 q >_N + {1\over 8} < {\bar q} g_s σ\cdot {\cal G} q >_N$, which is crucial for splitting the time ($f_t$) and space ($f_s$) components of the decay constant, is not necessarily restricted to be positive. Its positive value is found to yield a tachyonic pion mass. Using the in-medium pion mass as an input, we fix the dimension 5 condensate to be around $-0.025 {\rm GeV}^2 \sim -0.019$ GeV$^2$. The role of the $N$ and $Δ$ intermediate states in the correlation function is also investigated. The $N$ intermediate state is found not to contribute to the sum rules. For the $Δ$ intermediate state, we either treat it as a part of the continuum or propose a way to subtract explicitly from the sum rules. With (and without) explicit $Δ$ subtraction while allowing the in-medium pion mass to vary within 139 MeV $ \le m_π^* \le$ 159 MeV, we obtain $f_s/f_π= 0.37 \sim 0.78$ and $f_t / f_π= 0.63 \sim 0.79$.

# Update on pion weak decay constants in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The dimension 5 condensate that splits the time and space components of the in-medium pion decay constant is not necessarily positive; a positive value yields a tachyonic (imaginary-mass) pion, contradicting causality. By using the in-medium pion mass as an input, the dimension 5 condensate is fixed to a negative value around -0.025 to -0.019 GeV², and the Δ intermediate state contribution strongly suppresses the space component f_s.

## Method

The authors revisit the QCD sum rule calculation of the in-medium pion decay constants using the pseudoscalar-axial vector correlation function in nuclear matter. They evaluate the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 5, apply Borel-weighted sum rules, and constrain the dimension 5 condensate by requiring the sum rules to reproduce a physically acceptable in-medium pion mass (139–159 MeV). They also compute contributions from N and Δ intermediate states to the correlation function, finding the N state does not contribute, while the Δ contribution is either treated as part of the continuum or explicitly subtracted using an additional weight in the sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The dimension 5 condensate D₅ is fixed to be negative, in the range -0.025 GeV² to -0.019 GeV², using the in-medium pion mass as input.
- With the in-medium pion mass m_π* varying between 139 MeV and 159 MeV, the ratio f_s/f_π ranges from 0.37 to 0.78, and f_t/f_π ranges from 0.63 to 0.79.
- At m_π* = 139 MeV without Δ subtraction, f_t/f_π = 0.79 and f_s/f_π = 0.78; with Δ subtraction, f_t/f_π = 0.77 and f_s/f_π = 0.57.
- At m_π* = 159 MeV without Δ subtraction, f_t/f_π = 0.69 and f_s/f_π = 0.68; with Δ subtraction, f_t/f_π = 0.63 and f_s/f_π = 0.37.
- The Δ subtraction changes f_s/f_π by 27% at m_π* = 139 MeV and by 45% at m_π* = 159 MeV.
- The vacuum pion decay constant f_π is taken as 131 MeV; nuclear saturation density is ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule calculation of Kim (2001) for in-medium pion decay constants, correcting the earlier assumption that the dimension 5 condensate must be positive. The results contradict the earlier finding of f_s/f_t ≥ 1 and instead agree with the causal constraint from Pisarski (1996) that f_s/f_t ≤ 1, as well as with in-medium chiral perturbation theory results from Kirchbach (1997) and Meissner (2001) which give f_s/f_t ∼ 0.28 or smaller. The method enables future application to kaonic channels to study in-medium f_K, where gluonic dimension 5 operators may become important due to the larger strange quark mass.
