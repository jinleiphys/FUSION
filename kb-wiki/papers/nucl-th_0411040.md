# Gapless phases of color-superconducting matter

> We discuss gapless color superconductivity for neutral quark matter in beta equilibrium at zero as well as at nonzero temperature. Basic properties of gapless superconductors are reviewed. The current progress and the remaining problems in the understanding of the phase diagram of strange quark matter are discussed.

## Key claim

Gapless color-superconducting phases (g2SC and gCFL) can appear in neutral quark matter under beta-equilibrium conditions when the mismatch between quark Fermi momenta (due to the strange quark mass or electric charge neutrality) exceeds the pairing gap, but these phases suffer from a chromomagnetic instability that leaves the true ground state unknown.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model with a local current-current interaction for three-flavor quark matter, incorporating electric and color charge neutrality conditions and beta equilibrium. They solve the gap equations for the diquark condensates \(\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3\) as functions of the strange quark mass \(m_s\) and temperature, properly treating the strange quark mass in the Dirac equation rather than using a simple shift of the chemical potential. The phase diagram is mapped in the plane of temperature versus \(m_s^2/\mu\) at a fixed quark chemical potential \(\mu = 500\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density is \(\rho_0 \approx 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\); the highest baryon densities in stars are less than about \(10\rho_0\).
- The strange quark mass \(m_s\) in dense medium is estimated to lie between about 100 MeV and 500 MeV.
- The quark chemical potential \(\mu\) in the center of compact stars is of order 500 MeV.
- The transition from the CFL phase to the gCFL phase occurs when \(m_s^2/2\mu \approx \Delta\) (where \(\Delta \equiv \phi_1\)), or equivalently when \(m_s^2/\mu \approx 2\phi_1\).
- The phase diagram is computed at a fixed quark chemical potential \(\mu = 500\) MeV.
- The NJL model cutoff used in this paper is \(\Lambda = 653\) MeV; Ref. [FKR] uses \(\Lambda = 800\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of gapless two-flavor color superconductivity (g2SC) by Shovkovy (2004) to three-flavor matter, and confirms the phase diagram of Ruester (2004) using a more rigorous treatment of the strange quark mass. The results differ from those of dSC (2004) regarding the existence of the dSC phase, which is found to be sensitive to the NJL cutoff parameter. The paper highlights that both the g2SC and gCFL phases develop chromomagnetic instabilities (as shown in pi (2004) and pi-gCFL (2004)), leaving the true ground state of neutral dense quark matter an open problem, with mixed phases proposed as alternatives by SHH (2004) and RR (2004).
