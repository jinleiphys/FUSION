# KNO scaling in quark and gluon jets at the LHC

> The Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling of hadron multiplicity distributions, empirically confirmed to hold approximately in $e^+e^-$ collisions and Deep Inelastic Scattering, has been observed to be violated in hadron-hadron collisions. In this work, we show that the universality of KNO scaling can be extended to hadron-hadron collisions when restricted to QCD jets. We present a comprehensive study of KNO scaling in QCD jets produced in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. Using perturbative QCD calculations in the double logarithmic approximation and PYTHIA simulations, we find that KNO scaling approximately holds for both quark and gluon jets across a broad jet $p_T$ range, from $0.1$ TeV to $2.5$ TeV, at both the parton and hadron levels. Especially, we highlight characteristic differences between the KNO scaling functions of quark and gluon jets, with the quark-jet scaling function lying above that of gluon jets at both low and high multiplicities. This distinction is essential for interpreting inclusive jet data at the LHC. Furthermore, we propose direct experimental tests of KNO scaling in QCD jets at the LHC through quark-gluon discrimination using jet substructure techniques, as demonstrated by applying energy correlation functions to PYTHIA-generated data.

## Key claim

KNO scaling, which is violated in inclusive hadron-hadron collisions, approximately holds for both quark and gluon jets individually in proton-proton collisions at the LHC, but only when the analysis is restricted to QCD jets rather than inclusive charged particle multiplicities; the scaling functions for quark and gluon jets are distinct, with the quark-jet function lying above the gluon-jet function at both low and high multiplicities.

## Method

The authors performed perturbative QCD calculations in the double logarithmic approximation (DLA) to compute parton multiplicity distributions up to n = 1000 for quark and gluon jets across jet pT from 0.1 TeV to 2.5 TeV, using an iterative relation for generating functions with color coherence effects. They supplemented these analytic calculations with PYTHIA simulations of proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, selecting leading dijets reconstructed with the anti-kt algorithm (R = 0.4) and requiring |eta| < 2.1 and pT_lead/pT_sublead < 1.5, following the ATLAS (2019) event selection. They also demonstrated experimental feasibility by applying energy correlation functions (C1^(beta) with beta = 0.2) to discriminate quark-rich and gluon-rich jets in PYTHIA-generated data.

## Key numbers

- Jet pT range studied: 0.1 TeV to 2.5 TeV, covering LHC-relevant energies
- Parton multiplicity distributions computed up to n = 1000 in DLA
- Infrared cutoff Q0 = 0.5 GeV used in DLA calculations; jet radius R = 0.4
- For DLA asymptotic scaling functions: integration constant beta_0 ≈ 2.553
- Quark-jet color factor ratio: c_q = C_F/N_c = 4/9; gluon-jet color factor ratio: c_g = 1
- In PYTHIA inclusive charged hadron distributions: peak position shifts from n_ch/bar{n}_ch ≈ 0.92 at lowest pT bin to n_ch/bar{n}_ch ≈ 0.63 at highest pT bin
- For quark-gluon discrimination with C1^(beta): quark-rich jets defined as C1^(beta) < 0.17; gluon-rich jets defined as C1^(beta) > 0.28
- Quark fraction among quark-rich jets increases from 76% at lowest pT bin to 94% at highest pT bin; gluon fraction among gluon-rich jets decreases from 88% to 52%, remaining above 75% for pT ≤ 0.7 TeV
- At pT = 0.1 TeV in DLA: r_g ≈ 0.78 (gluon jet fraction); at pT = 2.5 TeV: r_q ≈ 0.81 (quark jet fraction)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies showing that KNO scaling should emerge in QCD jets in the asymptotic limit of high virtuality (Konishi 1979, Dokshitzer 1982, Dokshitzer 1991), and builds on PYTHIA simulations suggesting approximate KNO scaling within jets with deviations from multi-parton interactions and hadronization (Vertesi 2020). It provides an alternative interpretation of ATLAS (2019) inclusive charged particle multiplicity distributions in leading dijets compared to Germano (2024), who did not distinguish quark and gluon jet contributions. The results enable direct experimental tests of KNO scaling at the LHC using jet substructure techniques such as energy correlation functions (Larkoski 2013), and motivate future theoretical work on complete perturbative QCD calculations beyond DLA and extensions to heavy-ion collisions.
