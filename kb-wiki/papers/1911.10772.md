# Neutron pairing with medium polarization beyond the Landau approximation

> We revisit the long-standing problem of the superfluid transition temperature $T_c$ in dilute neutron matter. It is well known that $T_c$ is strongly affected by medium polarization effects (screening) which modify the pairing interaction in the medium. We study these effects within the random-phase approximation (RPA). It turns out that the widely used Landau approximation is sufficient only at densities below about 0.002 fm$^{-3}$. At higher densities, the full RPA leads to stronger screening than the Landau approximation.

## Key claim

The widely used Landau approximation for medium polarization effects in dilute neutron matter is sufficient only at densities below about 0.002 fm⁻³; at higher densities, the full random-phase approximation (RPA) leads to stronger screening than the Landau approximation, and the previously reported anti-screening (enhancement of the critical temperature) is either weakened or eliminated.

## Method

The authors computed the superfluid transition temperature T_c in dilute neutron matter by solving the BCS gap equation with the low-momentum interaction V_lowk derived from the AV₁₈ potential. Medium polarization effects were included via diagrams (a) and (b): diagram (a) is a single particle-hole bubble exchange, and diagram (b) is the RPA resummation of the bubble series, evaluated exactly (beyond the Landau approximation) using Skyrme-type particle-hole interactions from five different parameterizations (SLy4, SLy5, BSk19, BSk20, BSk21). The RPA vertex was obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the full momentum-dependent Skyrme interaction, and the resulting screened pairing interaction was used to compute T_c.

## Key numbers

- The Landau approximation is sufficient only at densities below about 0.002 fm⁻³ (corresponding to k_F ≈ 0.4 fm⁻¹).
- At low density (ρ = 0.01 fm⁻³, k_F = 0.67 fm⁻¹), the full RPA and Landau approximation agree well; at higher density (ρ = 0.08 fm⁻³, k_F = 1.33 fm⁻¹), the full RPA density response falls below the Lindhard function at large q/k_F, while the Landau approximation does not capture this.
- With the SLy5 interaction, the full RPA never gives anti-screening (enhancement of T_c compared to bare V_lowk), whereas the Landau approximation had shown anti-screening for k_F ≳ 0.7 fm⁻¹.
- With the SLy4 interaction, anti-screening survives only in a range around k_F ∼ 1 fm⁻¹ and is much weaker than within the Landau approximation.
- The BSk19, BSk20, and BSk21 parameterizations all give no anti-screening when using full RPA.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ramanan 2018) which used the Landau approximation for the RPA series and found anti-screening at higher densities due to cancellations between density and spin fluctuations. The present results show that the Landau approximation, used in many prior calculations (Shen 2005, Cao 2006, Ding 2016, Zhang 2016), overestimates screening effects at higher densities. The full RPA with Skyrme interactions produces stronger screening than the Landau approximation, reducing or eliminating anti-screening. This enables more reliable predictions of neutron superfluidity in neutron-star crusts and highlights the need for finite-range interactions or quantum Monte Carlo benchmarks to resolve the model dependence of the results.
