# Photon production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies

> Single photon spectra in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies are studied in the relativistic transport model that incorporates self-consistently the change of hadron masses in dense matter. We separate the total photon spectrum into `background' arising from the radiative decays of $π^0$ and $η$ mesons, and the `themal' one from other sources. For the latter we include contributions from radiative decays of $ρ$, $ω$, $η'$, and $a_1$, radiative decays of baryon resonances, as well as two-body processes such as $ππ\to ργ$ and $πρ\to πγ$. It is found that more than 95% of all photons come from the decays of $π^0$ and $η$ mesons, while the thermal photons account for less than 5% of the total photon yield. The thermal photon spectra in our calculations with either free or in-medium meson masses do not exceed the upper bound set by the experimental measurment of the WA80 Collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors show that the WA80 single-photon data from central S+Au collisions at 200 AGeV do not necessarily imply quark-gluon plasma formation, because a hadronic transport model with a sufficient number of hadron degrees of freedom can reproduce the observed lack of direct photon excess without invoking a phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model (based on the Walecka-type mean-field model) with initial hadron abundances from the RQMD string-fragmentation model. They calculated single-photon spectra by separating contributions into "background" from π⁰ and η radiative decays and "thermal" photons from radiative decays of ρ, ω, η′, a₁, baryon resonances, and two-body processes (ππ → ργ and πρ → πγ). Calculations were performed both with free meson masses and with in-medium dropping meson masses determined self-consistently from the scalar mean field.

## Key numbers

- More than 95% of all single photons come from π⁰ and η decays; thermal photons account for less than 5% of the total photon yield.
- The ω radiative decay is the most important thermal photon source for transverse momenta above about 0.5 GeV.
- The a₁ radiative decay width used is Γ(a₁ → πγ) = 1 MeV at the a₁ pole mass.
- The ω radiative decay width is parameterized as Γ(ω → π⁰γ)(M) = 0.01316 |p_π|³, with |p_π| = (M² − m_π²)/(2M).
- The a₁ radiative decay width is parameterized as Γ(a₁ → πγ)(M) = 0.01214 |p_π|⁵.
- The branching ratios used are B(π⁰ → γγ) = 0.988 and B(η → γγ) = 0.3925.
- The thermal photon spectra in both free-mass and in-medium-mass cases do not exceed the WA80 upper bound for transverse momenta below 1 GeV; at higher transverse momenta the results touch the experimental upper bound.

## Context

This work extends the same relativistic transport model previously used by Li, Ko, and Brown (1995-1996) to explain the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES and Helios-3 collaborations at CERN-SPS. It directly addresses the WA80 single-photon data, which had been interpreted by Sinha et al. (1994), Dumitru et al. (1995), and Hirao et al. (1997) as evidence for QGP formation based on hydrodynamical models with limited hadron degrees of freedom. The results agree with the hydrodynamical calculations of Sollfrank et al. (1996) and Cleymans et al. (1997), who found that including a sufficient number of hadron resonances can explain the data without QGP. The paper enables a consistent simultaneous explanation of both dilepton enhancement and the absence of direct photon excess within a single hadronic transport framework.
