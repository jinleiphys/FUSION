# Production of neutron-rich isotopes around N=126 in multinucleon transfer reactions

> The multinucleon transfer reactions has been investigated within the dinuclear system model. The nucleon transfer is coupled to the dissipation of relative motion energy and angular momentum by solving a set of microscopically derived master equations. A barrier distribution approach is implemented in the model in order to including the contributions of different orientations and dynamical effects. The available data of fragment production via multinucleon transfer reactions could be understood with the model for the reactions of $^{136}$Xe + $^{198}$Pt/$^{208}$Pb near Coulomb barrier energies. It is found that the production of heavy neutron-rich nuclei weakly depends on the incident energy. However, the yields of proton-rich nuclei increase with the incident energy.

## Key claim

The production of heavy neutron-rich nuclei (N>126) in multinucleon transfer reactions weakly depends on the incident energy, while the yields of proton-rich nuclei increase with incident energy; this conclusion is drawn from calculations using the dinuclear system model with a barrier distribution approach that includes orientation and dynamical effects.

## Method

The dynamics of multinucleon transfer reactions in collisions of heavy nuclei (136Xe + 198Pt/208Pb) was investigated using the dinuclear system (DNS) model, where nucleon transfer is coupled to dissipation of relative motion energy and angular momentum by solving microscopically derived master equations for protons and neutrons separately. A barrier distribution approach was implemented to account for contributions from different collision orientations and dynamical effects, and the survival probabilities of primary fragments after particle evaporation and fission competition were calculated using a statistical approach based on Weisskopf evaporation theory.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 136Xe + 208Pb at center-of-mass energy 450 MeV, calculations reproduce available experimental data from Argonne National Laboratory (Bai 2015).
- The Coulomb barrier at touching distance for 136Xe + 208Pb is V_C = 427 MeV.
- Neutron-rich nuclei 204Pt, 203Ir, and 202Os could be produced with cross sections above 1 nb in the 136Xe + 208Pb reaction.
- The relaxation time for radial motion is tau_r = 5 x 10^-22 s, and for angular momentum is tau_J = 15 x 10^-22 s.
- Calculations were performed at center-of-mass energies of 450 MeV and 526 MeV for 136Xe + 208Pb, and at multiple bombarding energies for 136Xe + 198Pt.

## Context

This work extends the dinuclear system model approach previously applied to superheavy nucleus formation (Feng 2009, Adamian 2010) to the production of neutron-rich isotopes around N=126, a region difficult to reach via projectile fragmentation or asymmetric fission. The calculations are compared with experimental data from Argonne National Laboratory (Bai 2015) and Dubna (Kozulin 2012) for the reactions 136Xe + 208Pb and 136Xe + 198Pt, and the results support the proposal by Zagrebaev and Greiner (2007, 2008) that multinucleon transfer reactions are a viable route to neutron-rich heavy nuclei. The model enables predictions for future experiments at facilities such as HIRFL in Lanzhou.
