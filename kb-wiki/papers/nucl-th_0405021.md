# Some Aspects of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> We consider different aspects of the virtual Compton amplitude in QCD on two examples: small-x physics accessible in the Regge regime and twist-3 approximation in the description of DVCS through the general parton distributions. Using this model, we give an estimate for the cross section of deeply virtual Compton scattering for the kinematics of CEBAF at Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The paper provides a model-dependent estimate for the deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) cross section at Jefferson Lab kinematics using a twist-2 approximation with a factorized ansatz for double distributions, and it also examines twist-3 corrections to restore gauge invariance; the results are conditional on the specific choice of the profile function \(h_{as}(x,y)=6y(1-x-y)\) and the use of forward parton distributions from the Musatov-Radyushkin model (Musatov and Radyushkin, 2000).

## Method

The author calculates the DVCS amplitude at twist-2 accuracy using the handbag diagram and a factorized ansatz for double distributions, where the forward quark-parton distributions are combined with an asymptotic profile function. The Bethe-Heitler (BH) cross section is computed exactly using the Dirac and Pauli form factors of the proton, and the interference term between BH and DVCS is isolated by considering the difference in cross sections for electrons with opposite helicities. Twist-3 corrections are then incorporated via a gauge-invariant extension of the twist-2 amplitude, following the approach of Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (1998) and Vanderhaeghen et al. (1999).

## Key numbers

*   The Bethe-Heitler cross section is calculated in units of nb/GeV\(^4\) for initial electron energy \(E_0 = 5.75\) GeV and final electron energy \(E_e = 3.75\) GeV, with the azimuthal angle of the emitted photon set to \(20^\circ\).
*   The proton mass is taken as \(M = 0.938\) GeV.
*   The Dirac form factor of the proton at \(t=0\) is \(F_1^p(0)=1\), and the Pauli form factor is \(F_2^p(0)=1.79\) (anomalous magnetic moment).
*   Cross sections are presented for electron beam energies of \(E_e = 4.25\) GeV, \(6\) GeV, and \(11\) GeV, with Bjorken \(x_B\) values of \(0.15\), \(0.2\), and \(0.3\), and \(Q^2\) values of \(1.3\), \(1.5\), \(2.0\), and \(2.5\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The momentum transfer \(t\) ranges from \(-0.1\) to \(-0.5\) GeV\(^2\), limited by \(t_{min} = -4 M^2 \xi^2/(1-\xi^2)\).
*   The interference term contributes approximately 40% to 60% of the total cross section across the kinematics studied.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for DVCS developed by Muller et al. (1994) and the model for non-forward parton distributions by Musatov and Radyushkin (2000), providing numerical cross-section estimates for the CEBAF facility at Jefferson Lab. It addresses the need for higher-twist corrections, as discussed by Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (1998) and Vanderhaeghen et al. (1999), and compares with twist-3 calculations by Anikin et al. (2000), Penttinen et al. (2000), Belitsky and Muller (2000), and Radyushkin and Weiss (2000). The results enable experimental planning for DVCS measurements at Jefferson Lab and provide a baseline for extracting generalized parton distributions from future data.
