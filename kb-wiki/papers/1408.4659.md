# LHC data challenges the contemporary parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions

> We discuss the inclusive high-pT charged-particle production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. The experimental data are compared to the NLO perturbative QCD calculations employing various sets of parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions. Most of the theoretical predictions are found to disastrously overpredict the measured cross sections, even if the scale variations and PDF errors are accounted for. The problem appears to arise from the presently too hard gluon-to-hadron fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that none of the current sets of parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions (FFs) can optimally describe LHC data for inclusive charged-hadron production at transverse momentum above 10 GeV, with most predictions disastrously overpredicting the measured cross sections. The problem appears to arise specifically from the presently too hard gluon-to-hadron fragmentation functions, rather than from parton distribution functions or scale uncertainties.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive high-pT charged-hadron production in proton-proton collisions at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD using the public INCNLO code, with CT10NLO parton distribution functions and seven different sets of fragmentation functions (Kretzer, KKP, BFGW, HKNS, AKK05, DSS, and AKK08). They compared the theoretical predictions to published CMS and ALICE data at LHC energies, varying the renormalization, factorization, and fragmentation scales independently by factors of two (excluding extreme ratios that produce artificially large logarithms) to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The PDF-originating uncertainty is around 5% at high pT.
- The Kretzer FFs give the best description of the data, being around 20% above the data beyond pT ~ 10 GeV.
- The data-to-theory ratio for Kretzer FFs remains remarkably flat despite the absolute cross section dropping around eleven orders of magnitude.
- The scale uncertainty is huge at small pT but levels off beyond pT ~ 10 GeV.
- The analysis focuses on LHC data at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, with similar findings at other LHC energies and Tevatron data from CDF.

## Context

This work extends the observation that NLO pQCD calculations for inclusive charged-hadron production at CMS (2012) and ALICE (2013) systematically overshoot measurements, while related processes like inclusive jet production (Chatrchyan 2012, Aad 2011) and isolated photon production (Chatrchyan 2011, Aad 2011) at the LHC agree with NLO calculations. The study identifies that the gluon fragmentation functions from DSS and AKK08, which included hadroproduction data from RHIC, SPS, and Tevatron at low pT, are poorly constrained because e+e- annihilation data predominantly constrain quark fragmentation. The results enable future FF fits to use only the region pT > 10 GeV, discarding lower-pT data where scale uncertainty is enormous and baryon production shows evidence of non-fragmentation physics.
