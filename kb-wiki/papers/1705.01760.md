# "Sloppy" nuclear energy density functionals (II): Finite nuclei

> A study of parameter sensitivity of nuclear energy density functionals, initiated in the first part of this work \cite{NV.16}, is extended by the inclusion of data on ground-state properties of finite nuclei in the application of the manifold boundary approximation method (MBAM). Density functionals used in self-consistent mean-field calculations, and nuclear structure models based on them, are generally "sloppy" and exhibit an exponential range of sensitivity to parameter variations. Concepts of information geometry are used to identify the presence of effective functionals of lower dimension in parameter space associated with parameter combinations that can be tightly constrained by data. The MBAM is used in an iterative procedure that systematically reduces the complexity and the dimension of parameter space of a sloppy functional, with properties of nuclear matter and data on finite nuclei determining not only the values of model parameters, but also the optimal functional form of the density dependence.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Manifold Boundary Approximation Method (MBAM) can systematically reduce the parameter space dimension of a "sloppy" nuclear energy density functional (EDF) when constrained by both nuclear matter equations of state and ground-state properties of finite nuclei, but the specific reduced functional form obtained depends on the particular data set used.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic functional DD-PC1 with ten parameters and apply the MBAM iteratively: at each step they compute the Hessian matrix of the chi-squared penalty function at the best-fit point, identify the eigenvector corresponding to the smallest eigenvalue (the "softest" direction), integrate the geodesic equation along that direction until the model manifold boundary is reached, and construct a new model with one fewer parameter by Taylor-expanding the coupling function at the boundary. The data set includes seven points of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state and six points of the neutron matter equation of state from Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (1998), plus binding energies, charge radii, and neutron skin values for eight spherical nuclei from O-16 to Pb-214.

## Key numbers

* The eigenvalues of the 10x10 Hessian matrix for the initial functional span ten orders of magnitude, characteristic of sloppy models.
* The data set contains N = 29 observables: 7 symmetric nuclear matter energies, 6 neutron matter energies, and ground-state properties of 8 nuclei (binding energies, charge radii, and neutron-proton radius differences where available).
* The adopted errors for the fit are 10% for nuclear matter energies, 0.1% for binding energies, 0.2% for charge radii, and 5% for neutron skin values.
* After the first MBAM iteration, the nine-parameter functional has eigenvalues spanning nine orders of magnitude.
* The first iteration reduces the isoscalar-vector coupling from three parameters (a_v, b_v, d_v) to two parameters (tilde-a_v, tilde-b_v) by linearizing the exponential density dependence.
* The second iteration reduces the isoscalar-scalar coupling from four parameters (a_s, b_s, c_s, d_s) to three parameters (tilde-a_s, tilde-b_s, tilde-c_s) by converting the exponential to a quadratic polynomial in density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Nikšić and Vretenar, 2016) which applied information geometry and MBAM to nuclear matter only, by now including finite-nucleus data that are essential for realistic EDF parameter determination. The approach builds on the MBAM framework developed by Transtrum and collaborators (2010-2015) for sloppy models in other fields, and demonstrates that the DD-PC1 functional, like other nuclear EDFs, contains effective models of lower dimension that can be systematically extracted. The resulting reduced functionals could enable more efficient global fits across the nuclear chart and provide guidance for constructing next-generation EDFs with minimal parameter sets.
