# Fusion reactions in plasmas as probe of the high-momentum tail of particle distributions

> In fusion reactions, the Coulomb barrier selects particles from the high-momentum part of the distribution. Therefore, small variations of the high-momentum tail of the velocity distribution can produce strong effects on fusion rates. In plasmas several potential mechanisms exist that can produce deviations from the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution. Quantum broadening of the energy-momentum dispersion relation of the plasma quasi-particles modifies the high-momentum tail and could explain the fusion-rate enhancement observed in low-energy nuclear reaction experiments.

## Key claim

Quantum broadening of the energy-momentum dispersion relation of plasma quasi-particles can produce a high-momentum tail that enhances fusion rates, but this mechanism only produces significant enhancement below about 2 keV beam energy, whereas the experimentally observed enhancement in deuterated metal targets begins at 6-8 keV, so the quantum-tail effect alone cannot reproduce the experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the momentum distribution of target particles in a plasma using a Lorentzian broadening of the energy-momentum dispersion relation with a width determined by Coulomb collisional cross sections. They compute the d(d,p)t fusion reaction rate by integrating the broadened target momentum distribution (with a Maxwellian energy distribution) against a monoenergetic projectile distribution, using the astrophysical S-factor approximation S(E_CM) ≈ 43 keV·b. They separate the rate contributions from three regions of the target momentum distribution: low-momentum (below 0.54 (T E_S^5)^(1/6)), central peak (0.54 to 1.15 (T E_S^5)^(1/6)), and high-momentum tail (above 1.15 (T E_S^5)^(1/6)).

## Key numbers

- The energy scale E_S for deuterons at density n = 4.38 × 10^23 cm^(-3) is approximately 2.03 eV.
- At temperature T_1 = 2.44 × 10^(-2) eV, the modified distribution scale (T E_S^5)^(1/6) = 0.770245 eV; at T_2 = 0.109 eV, it is 0.988481 eV.
- The high-momentum tail of the broadened distribution decreases as Ep^(-7/2) rather than exponentially.
- The astrophysical S-factor used is S_0 = 43 keV·b, with error ≤ 6% for beam energies ≤ 10 keV.
- The reference density n_0 = 0.843542 × 10^24 cm^(-3) and the Rydberg energy E_0 = 3.02649 eV are used in defining the energy scale.

## Context

This work extends the quantum-tail mechanism proposed by Starostin (2003) as a possible explanation for the enhanced d(d,p)t reaction rates measured in deuterated metal targets by Raiola (2002, 2004, 2005) and Bonomo (2003). The authors show that while the quantum broadening shifts the momentum distribution to higher energies and produces a power-law tail, the enhancement only appears below 2 keV, whereas the experimental excess begins at 6-8 keV, leaving the puzzle unresolved. The paper also critiques the Debye screening explanation of Raiola (2004, 2005) by noting that the Debye number N_Deb ≈ 3 × 10^(-5) is far too small for a cooperative screening effect, and that a non-degenerate screening formula was used in a regime where electrons are degenerate.
