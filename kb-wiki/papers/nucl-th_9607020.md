# Dimensional Power Counting in Nuclei

> Weinberg's dimensional power counting for nuclei is reprised in this primer. The role of QCD and chiral symmetry in constructing effective Lagrangians is discussed. The two-scale hypothesis of Manohar and Georgi is combined with power counting in amplitudes to shed light on the scales of nuclear kinetic and potential energies, the size of strong-interaction coupling constants, the size of meson-exchange currents, and the relative sizes of $N$-nucleon forces. Numerous examples are worked out and compared to conventional nuclear models.

## Key claim

Weinberg's dimensional power counting provides an organizing principle for nuclear physics by showing that nuclear amplitudes can be expanded as a series in \(Q/\Lambda\), where \(Q \sim m_\pi\) is a typical nuclear momentum and \(\Lambda \sim 4\pi f_\pi \sim 1\) GeV is the chiral symmetry breaking scale, but the counting only works straightforwardly for irreducible graphs while reducible graphs (iterations of the potential) must be treated separately by solving the Schrödinger equation.

## Method

The paper reprises Weinberg's dimensional power counting for nuclei by combining the two-scale hypothesis of Manohar and Georgi (scales \(f_\pi\) and \(\Lambda\)) with dimensional analysis of effective Lagrangians built from pion and nucleon fields. The author constructs effective Lagrangians by "freezing out" short-range degrees of freedom (heavy mesons, resonances, \(N\bar{N}\) pairs) into zero-range interactions, then applies power counting to Feynman diagrams to determine the scaling of nuclear energies with \(Q\) and \(\Lambda\). The counting is validated by comparing with numerical results from realistic potentials for few-nucleon systems.

## Key numbers

- Typical nuclear momentum: \(p c \sim \hbar c/R \sim 100\text{--}150\) MeV \(\sim m_\pi c^2\), giving \(v/c \sim m_\pi/M_N \sim 10\text{--}20\%\)
- Pion-decay constant: \(f_\pi \simeq 92.4\) MeV; axial-vector coupling: \(g_A \simeq 1.26\)
- One-pion-exchange potential scale per pair: \(\langle OPEP \rangle \sim m_\pi^3 g_A^2 / 4\pi f_\pi^2 \sim 20\) MeV
- For the triton: \(\langle V_\pi \rangle \sim -15\) MeV/pair and \(\langle T \rangle \sim 15\) MeV/nucleon
- Two-pion-exchange potential scale per pair: \(\langle TPEP \rangle \sim m_\pi^5 / (4\pi f_\pi)^3 f_\pi \sim 0.5\) MeV/pair
- Two-pion-exchange three-nucleon force scale per triplet: \(\langle 2\pi E\,3NF \rangle \sim m_\pi^6 / M_N (4\pi f_\pi)^2 f_\pi^2 \sim 0.75\) MeV/triplet
- Natural coupling constant for \(\pi NN\) vertex: \(c_\pi \sim g_A \sim 1.26\)
- Natural coupling constant for \(\omega\)-meson zero-range interaction: \(c_\omega \sim f_\pi^2 g_\omega^2 / 2 m_\omega^2 \sim 1.75\)
- KSFR relation gives \(\rho\)-meson zero-range coupling: \(c_\rho \sim 0.25\)
- Strong-interaction coupling constants scale as \(g_x \sim \Lambda/f_\pi \sim 10\)

## Context

This primer extends Weinberg's seminal work (Weinberg, 1979, 1990-1992) on applying chiral perturbation theory to nuclei, and builds on the two-scale hypothesis of Manohar and Georgi (1984). The paper also presents an older alternative "rules of scale" approach developed for meson-exchange currents (Friar, 1970s-1980s) but notes its limitations for loop calculations. The work enables systematic understanding of why \(N\)-nucleon forces decrease in strength as \(N\) increases, authenticating decades of nuclear phenomenology, and provides a framework for interpreting few-nucleon system calculations such as those by Wiringa and collaborators (1995) that achieved \(\lesssim 1\%\) uncertainty for \(A \leq 6\) nuclei.
