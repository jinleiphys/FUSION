# Finite temperature effects on g-modes of inviscid neutron stars

> We study the effect of temperature on secular, compositional $g$-modes in the core of inviscid neutron stars. Using a chiral $SU(2)_f$ sigma model, we construct isentropic temperature profiles for hot and dense matter and find that the frequency of the global core $g$-mode's dependence on temperature is governed by the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter $L$. As a result, the $g$-mode frequency of a warm neutron star can be either higher or lower than that of its cold counterpart, depending on $L$. Our results highlight the interplay of thermal effects and composition gradients, and demonstrate the potential of neutron star $g$-mode observations to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the frequency of composition-driven g-modes in warm neutron stars can be either higher or lower than in cold stars, depending on the value of the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter L, with the minimum physically allowed L in their model being approximately 49 MeV.

## Method

The authors use a chiral SU(2)f sigma model extended to finite temperature via thermal occupation factors for nucleons, constructing isentropic temperature profiles for hot dense matter. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain equilibrium stellar structures, then compute g-mode frequencies under the relativistic Cowling approximation by integrating the linearized perturbation equations with boundary conditions at the center and surface. The Brunt-Vaisala frequency is calculated from the difference between equilibrium and adiabatic sound speeds, with the latter evaluated at fixed entropy per baryon and fixed proton fraction.

## Key numbers

* The model uses saturation density n0 = 0.153 fm^-3, nuclear incompressibility K0 = 250 MeV, and symmetry energy S0 = 32.0 MeV
* The minimum physically allowed symmetry energy slope in the model is L_min approximately 49 MeV
* The CREX result favors 0 less than or approximately L less than or approximately 51 MeV, while PREX-II suggests 76 MeV less than or approximately L less than or approximately 165 MeV
* Astrophysical constraints give L approximately 57.7 plus or minus 19 MeV from a meta-analysis of 24 studies
* The model is consistent with observational constraints for L less than or approximately 80 MeV
* The Cowling approximation reproduces full general relativistic results within approximately 5 to 10 percent

## Context

This work extends studies of g-modes in neutron stars by Reisenegger and Goldreich (1992) and later authors, who established that composition-driven g-modes probe internal stratification. It contrasts with findings by Lozano (2022) that composition-driven g-modes are not supported above T approximately 10 MeV under uniform temperature profiles, and with Zhao (2025) who found suppression already at T approximately 5 MeV when finite reaction rates are included. The results demonstrate that g-mode observations could help resolve the tension between CREX and PREX-II measurements of the symmetry energy slope, complementing constraints from neutron-star mass-radius measurements and tidal deformabilities.
