# Fusion of light exotic nuclei at near-barrier energies : effect of inelastic excitation

> The effect of inelastic excitation of exotic light projectiles (proton- as well as neutron-rich) $^{17}$F and $^{11}$Be on fusion with heavy target has been studied at near-barrier energies. The calculations have been performed in the coupled channels approach where, in addition to the normal coupling of the ground state of the projectile to the continuum, inelastic excitation of the projectile to the bound excited state and its coupling to the continuum have also been taken into consideration. The inclusion of these additional couplings has been found to have significant effect on the fusion excitation function of neutron-rich $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb whereas the effect has been observed to be nominal for the case of proton-rich $^{17}$F on the same target. The pronounced effect of the channel coupling on the fusion process in case of $^{11}$Be is attributed to its well-developed halo structure.

## Key claim

The inclusion of inelastic excitation of the projectile to its bound excited state and its coupling to the continuum has a significant effect on the fusion excitation function of neutron-rich \({}^{11}\)Be on \({}^{208}\)Pb, but only a nominal effect for proton-rich \({}^{17}\)F on the same target, with the difference attributed to the well-developed halo structure of \({}^{11}\)Be.

## Method

Coupled channels calculations were performed for \({}^{17}\)F + Pb and \({}^{11}\)Be + Pb systems at near-barrier energies, including couplings from the projectile ground state to the continuum and, additionally, inelastic excitation to the bound first excited state and its coupling to the continuum. The continuum was discretized into 10 bins of 200 keV each (up to 2 MeV for \({}^{17}\)F and up to 8 MeV for \({}^{11}\)Be), and both Coulomb and nuclear effects were included. For \({}^{11}\)Be, the proper rms radius of 2.91 fm was used, dipole transitions were considered with \(s\)-, \(p\)-, and \(d\)-waves in the continuum, and continuum-continuum couplings were included.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{11}\)Be, the rms radius is 2.91 fm, which is about 20% larger than the value of 2.45 fm obtained from the \(r_0 A^{1/3}\) systematics with \(r_0 = 1.1\) fm.
* The Coulomb barrier for \({}^{11}\)Be on Pb is about 1.6 MeV lower due to the larger rms radius.
* The first excited state of \({}^{11}\)Be is situated at 320 keV above the ground state, with a \(B(E1)\) value of \(0.36 \pm 0.03\) W.u. for the transition.
* Including the first excited state of \({}^{11}\)Be in the calculation increases both complete and total fusion cross sections below the barrier by almost a factor of two at smaller energies, and decreases complete fusion cross sections at above-barrier energies by about 21%.
* The one-nucleon separation energy is 0.504 MeV in \({}^{11}\)Be and 0.6 MeV in \({}^{17}\)F.
* The first excited state of \({}^{17}\)F is situated 495 keV above the ground state, and the ground state is 600 keV below the breakup threshold.

## Context

This work extends the coupled channels formalism of Hagino et al. (2000) by including continuum-continuum couplings, the proper rms size of \({}^{11}\)Be, and the first excited state of both projectiles. The results for \({}^{11}\)Be are compared with total fusion data on \({}^{209}\)Bi from Signiorini et al. (1998) and for \({}^{17}\)F with data from Rehm et al. (1998). The paper suggests that future work should investigate a more exotic proton-rich nucleus such as \({}^{8}\)B, which has a one-proton separation energy of only 0.137 MeV and may show larger breakup effects.
