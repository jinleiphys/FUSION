# The Core of $^{25}$F studied by the $^{25}$F(-1p)$^{24}$O reaction

> The $^{25}$F($5/2^+) (-1p) ^{24}$O reaction was studied at the NSCL using the S800 spectrometer. The experimental spectroscopic factor for the ground-state to ground-state transition indicates a substantial depletion of the proton $d_{5/2}$ strength compared to shell-model expectations. Our result supports the findings reported by Tang \textit{et al.}, from their study of the $(p,2p)$ reaction at RIBF. The overlap between the $^{25}$F and $^{24}$O ground-states is considerably less than anticipated if $^{24}$O acted as a robust and rigid doubly-magic core in $^{25}$F. We interpret the results within the framework of the Particle-Vibration Coupling (PVC) of a $d_{5/2}$ proton coupled to a quadrupole phonon of an effective core. This approach provides a good description of the experimental data by requiring an effective $^{24}$O* core with a phonon energy of $\hbarω_2$= 3.2 MeV, and a $B(E2) ~ 2.7$ W.u., softer and more collective than a bare $^{24}$O. Both the Nilsson deformed mean field and the PVC models appear to capture the properties of the effective core of $^{25}$F, suggesting that the additional proton tends to polarize the free, doubly magic $^{24}$O in such a way that it becomes either slightly deformed or a quadrupole vibrator.

# The Core of $^{25}$F studied by the $^{25}$F(-1p)$^{24}$O reaction

## Key claim

The ground-state to ground-state spectroscopic factor for proton removal from $^{25}$F is substantially suppressed (C$^2$S$_{exp}=0.27(4)$) compared to shell-model predictions near unity, indicating that the $^{24}$O core within $^{25}$F is not a rigid doubly-magic nucleus but rather a softer, more collective effective core. The suppression is consistent with particle-vibration coupling of a $d_{5/2}$ proton to a quadrupole phonon, but the authors note that systematic reaction-mechanism effects (the $R_S$ suppression factor) could account for part of the reduction, implying a theoretical spectroscopic factor of C$^2$S$_{th}=0.56(15)$ after such corrections.

## Method

A secondary beam of $^{25}$F at approximately 77 MeV/nucleon, produced by fragmentation of a 140-MeV/nucleon $^{48}$Ca primary beam at the NSCL, impinged on a 188-mg/cm$^2$ $^{9}$Be target at the target position of the S800 spectrograph. The $^{24}$O reaction residues were identified event-by-event in the S800 focal plane detectors via time-of-flight and energy loss, and the parallel momentum distribution was measured using the Cathode-Readout Drift Chambers. The experimental cross section was compared to a calculated single-particle cross section ($\sigma_{sp}=15.7$ mb) from an eikonal reaction model to extract the spectroscopic factor.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross section for $^{25}$F($5/2^+$) to $^{24}$O($0^+$) ground-state proton removal: $\sigma_{exp}=4.3(6)$ mb
- Calculated single-particle cross section (unit spectroscopic factor): $\sigma_{sp}=15.7$ mb
- Experimental spectroscopic factor: C$^2$S$_{exp}=0.27(4)$
- Spectroscopic factor from the earlier NSCL measurement (50 MeV/nucleon, carbon target, reanalyzed): 0.29(5)
- Spectroscopic factor from the RIKEN $(p,2p)$ measurement (Tang et al., 2020): 0.36(13)
- Theoretical spectroscopic factor required by the data after accounting for $R_S$ systematics: C$^2$S$_{th}=0.56(15)$
- $R_S$ suppression factor expected from systematics at $\Delta S=10.17$ MeV: 0.45(10)
- Fitted phonon energy of the effective $^{24}$O* core: $\hbar\omega_2=3.2$ MeV
- $B(E2)$ of the effective $^{24}$O* core: approximately 2.7 W.u. (0.0055 $e^2b^2$)
- PVC model spectroscopic factor: C$^2$S$_{th,PVC}=0.6$
- Overlap between free $^{24}$O and effective $^{24}$O* cores: approximately 0.87

## Context

This work extends and confirms the findings of Tang et al. (2020) from a $(p,2p)$ knockout reaction at RIBF, which reported a spectroscopic factor of 0.36(13) and concluded that the $^{25}$F ground state differs significantly from a $^{24}$O$_{gs}+p$ configuration. The present result also agrees with a reanalysis of the earlier lower-energy proton-removal measurement by Thoennessen et al. (2003) on a carbon target. The authors interpret the suppression within the Particle-Vibration Coupling (PVC) framework of Bohr and Mottelson, as well as the Nilsson plus Particle Rotor Model (PRM) from their earlier works (Macchiavelli et al., 2017, 2020), both of which capture the polarization of the $^{24}$O core by the additional $d_{5/2}$ proton. The results enable further tests of shell-model effective interactions and ab-initio calculations in the neutron-rich oxygen-fluorine region, and suggest similar core-polarization effects may be present in the $^{28}$O-$^{29}$F system, though experimental confirmation is needed.
