# Electric dipole moment of $^{199}$Hg atom from P, CP-odd electron-nucleon interaction

> We calculate the effect of the P, CP-odd electron-nucleon interaction on the electric dipole moment of the $^{199}$Hg atom by evaluating the nuclear spin matrix elements in terms of the nuclear shell model. It is found that the neutron spin matrix element of the $^{199}$Hg nucleus is $\langle Ψ|\, σ_{nz} | Ψ\rangle \approx -0.4$ with a dominant configuration of $p_{1/2}$ orbital neutron. We also derive constraints on the CP phases of Higgs-doublet models, supersymmetric models, and leptoquark models from the latest experimental limit $|d_{\rm Hg}| < 7.4 \times 10^{-30}e$ cm.

## Key claim
The nuclear spin matrix element of the neutron in $^{199}$Hg is calculated to be approximately -0.3765, which is close to the simple estimate of -1/3 from a single valence neutron in the $p_{1/2}$ orbital, indicating that configuration mixing has only a limited effect on this quantity.

## Method
The nuclear spin matrix elements of $^{199}$Hg were evaluated using the pair-truncated shell model (PTSM), where the full shell-model space is truncated into subspaces composed of collective pairs of nucleons. Neutrons were treated as holes in the six orbitals between magic numbers 82 and 126, and protons as holes in the five orbitals between magic numbers 50 and 82. The effective Hamiltonian employed a monopole plus quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, with strength parameters optimized by a chi-squared fit to experimental spectra of $^{200}$Hg, $^{198}$Hg, $^{198}$Pt, and $^{196}$Pt.

## Key numbers
- Neutron spin matrix element of $^{199}$Hg: $\langle \sigma_{nz} \rangle = -0.3765$
- Proton spin matrix element of $^{199}$Hg: $\langle \sigma_{pz} \rangle = 0.0088$
- Atomic coefficient for tensor-type interaction: ${\cal R}_T \approx -3.3 \times 10^{-20} e$ cm (from relativistic normal coupled-cluster method, about 2% uncertainty)
- Atomic coefficient for pseudoscalar-type interaction: ${\cal R}_{\rm PS} \approx -1.2 \times 10^{-22} e$ cm
- Atomic coefficient for scalar-pseudoscalar-type interaction: ${\cal R}_{\rm SP} \approx -2.8 \times 10^{-22} e$ cm (about 20% uncertainty)
- Experimental upper limit on $^{199}$Hg EDM: $|d_{\rm Hg}| < 7.4 \times 10^{-30} e$ cm

## Context
This work extends the nuclear shell-model approach previously applied to $^{129}$Xe by Yoshinaga et al. (2013) and Teruya et al. (2015) to the $^{199}$Hg nucleus, which currently provides the most stringent experimental EDM limit. The calculated nuclear spin matrix elements enable quantitative constraints on CP-violating new physics models including the two-Higgs doublet model, supersymmetric models, and leptoquark models, going beyond the simple single-valence-nucleon estimate of Flambaum et al. (1997).
