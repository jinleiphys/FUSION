# Proton-neutron pairing correlations in the nuclear shell model

> A shell-model study of proton-neutron pairing in f - p shell nuclei using a parametrized hamiltonian that includes deformation and spin-orbit effects as well as isoscalar and isovector pairing is reported. By working in a shell-model framework we are able to assess the role of the various modes of proton-neutron pairing in the presence of nuclear deformation without violating symmetries. Results are presented for $^{44}$Ti, $^{46}$Ti and $^{48}$Cr.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a parametrized shell-model Hamiltonian including quadrupole-quadrupole, isoscalar pairing, isovector pairing, and spin-orbit terms can reproduce key features of f-p shell nuclei near N=Z, but the abstract's claim of assessing "the role of the various modes of proton-neutron pairing" is hedged by the finding that isoscalar and isovector pairing have identical effects only in the absence of spin-orbit coupling and only for the specific case of N=Z=2 (⁴⁴Ti).

## Method
The authors performed full shell-model calculations for neutrons and protons in the 1f-2p shell outside a ⁴⁰Ca core, using a schematic Hamiltonian with a mass quadrupole operator, an isoscalar (T=0, J=1) pairing term, an isovector (T=1, J=0) pairing term, and a one-body spin-orbit term. They systematically varied the strength parameters of the pairing and spin-orbit interactions, starting from pure SU(3) rotational motion, and computed ground-state band energies, B(E2) values, and pair numbers for ⁴⁴Ti, ⁴⁶Ti, and ⁴⁸Cr.

## Key numbers
- Optimal Hamiltonian parameters: χ = -0.05 MeV, a = b = 12, α = 20, giving an acceptable fit to spectra of ⁴⁴Ti, ⁴⁶Ti, and ⁴⁸Cr.
- In ⁴⁴Ti (N=Z=2) with no spin-orbit term, isoscalar and isovector pairing have precisely the same effect on ground-state band energy splittings.
- In ⁴⁶Ti (two excess neutrons) with no spin-orbit term, isoscalar pairing is strongly suppressed relative to isovector pairing.
- In ⁴⁸Cr, the backbend near I=12 cannot be reproduced with pure isoscalar pairing but requires isovector pairing; there is no significant difference between pure isovector and SU(4) (equal strength) pairing results.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies using exactly-solvable SO(8) models (e.g., by Dobaczewski and collaborators) and non-degenerate orbital models (Errea et al., Satula, Sandulescu) that could not simultaneously restore rotational and isospin symmetries. By using the full shell model, the authors confirm that isoscalar pairing is strongly focused on N=Z nuclei and is suppressed by both neutron excess and spin-orbit coupling, and they corroborate the earlier shell-model finding by Poves that the backbend in ⁴⁸Cr originates from isovector pairing. The results enable future systematic studies of competing pairing modes in deformed N≈Z nuclei without symmetry violations.
