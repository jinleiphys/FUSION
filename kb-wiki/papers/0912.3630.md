# A relativistic model for the non-mesonic weak decay of the ${_Λ^{12} C}$ hypernucleus

> A fully relativistic finite nucleus wave-function approach to the non-mesonic weak decay of the ${_Λ^{12} C}$ hypernucleus is presented. The model is based on the calculation of the amplitudes of the tree-level Feynman diagrams for the $ΛN\to NN$ process and includes one-pion exchange and one-kaon exchange diagrams. The pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector choices for the vertex structure are compared. Final-state interactions between each one of the outgoing nucleons and the residual nucleus are accounted for by a complex phenomenological optical potential. Initial $ΛN$ and final $NN$ short-range correlations are included by means of phenomenological correlation functions. Numerical results are presented and discussed for the total non-mesonic decay width $Γ_{nm}=Γ_n+Γ_p$, the $Γ_n/Γ_p$ ratio, the $a_Λ$ intrinsic asymmetry parameter, and the kinetic energy and angular spectra.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully relativistic finite-nucleus wave-function model for the non-mesonic weak decay of the ${_\Lambda^{12}C}$ hypernucleus, but limits its scope to one-pion-exchange and one-kaon-exchange diagrams for one-nucleon induced decay only, acknowledging that neglected contributions (such as two-nucleon induced decay and heavier meson exchanges) could play an important role in the decay dynamics.

## Method

The model calculates tree-level Feynman diagrams for the $\Lambda N\to NN$ process using covariant amplitudes with Dirac phenomenology, including one-pion-exchange and one-kaon-exchange diagrams. Initial $\Lambda N$ and final $NN$ short-range correlations are included via phenomenological correlation functions, and final-state interactions of the outgoing nucleons with the residual nucleus are accounted for by a complex phenomenological optical potential. The pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector choices for the vertex structure are compared, with a modified derivative pseudo-vector coupling preferred for consistency with short-range correlation treatment.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic one-meson-exchange models that have been the standard approach for hypernuclear non-mesonic decay calculations, such as those by the Pavia group and others using the wave-function method. The model aims to provide a fully relativistic treatment where previously only a few calculations (cited as relPS, rel1, rel2, Cmes2) had explored relativistic effects. The results enable comparison with recent KEK experimental data for ${_\Lambda^{12}C}$ and can test whether a conceptually simple relativistic model with few free parameters can reproduce the main observables, potentially clarifying the role of relativity in the decay dynamics.
