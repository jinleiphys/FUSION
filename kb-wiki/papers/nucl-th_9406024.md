# RPA calculation of $K^+$-nucleus cross-sections with a density-dependent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock NN interaction

> In the calculation of the $K^+$-nucleus cross sections, the coupling of the mesons exchanged between the $K^+$ and the target nucleons to the polarization of the Fermi and Dirac seas has been taken into account. This polarization has been calculated in the one-loop approximation but summed up to all orders (RPA approximation). For this calculation a density-dependent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock NN interaction providing a good description of both nuclear matter and finite nuclei has been used. The agreement with experiment is considerably improved.

# RPA calculation of $K^+$-nucleus cross-sections with a density-dependent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock NN interaction

## Key claim

The coupling of $\sigma$ and $\omega$ mesons exchanged between the $K^+$ and target nucleons to the polarization of the Fermi and Dirac seas, summed to all orders in the RPA approximation using a relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock NN interaction, considerably improves agreement with experimental $K^+$-nucleus total cross-section ratios without any free parameters. The authors claim this is the first fully microscopic (hadronic-level) calculation to achieve such agreement, but note that the polarization is calculated only in the small energy-momentum space-like region dominant for forward scattering.

## Method

The authors calculated $K^+$-nucleus total cross sections from the forward scattering amplitude using the optical theorem, with an optical potential built by folding the in-medium KN amplitude by the nuclear density. The in-medium KN amplitude was modified to include the coupling of $\sigma$ and $\omega$ mesons to particle-hole and nucleon-antinucleon excitations, calculated in the one-loop approximation and summed to all orders (RPA) using the density-dependent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock NN interaction of Brockmann and Machleidt. Pauli blocking for nucleon intermediate states was introduced, and the free-space KN interaction was used for densities below half saturation density.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $R_T = \sigma_{tot}(K^+-^{12}C) / [6 \cdot \sigma_{tot}(K^+-d)]$ was calculated from 400 MeV/c to 900 MeV/c.
- Including the polarization effect raised the $R_T$ ratio by approximately 5% on average compared to the free-space KN interaction calculation.
- The point-like proton distribution for $^{12}C$ was taken from electron-scattering charge density data of Sick and McCarthy.
- The free-space KN interaction used was the full Bonn boson exchange model.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of medium effects in $K^+$-nucleus scattering, including nucleon swelling (Siegel, Kaufmann, and Gibbs), scaling of meson effective masses (Brown, Rho; Caillon and Labarsouque), and scattering on exchanged mesons (Akulinichev; Johnson and Kabir). It uses the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock NN interaction of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990), which previously reproduced nuclear matter saturation and finite nuclei properties in the work of Brockmann and Toki. The result enables further microscopic calculations of $K^+$-nucleus interactions without phenomenological adjustments, and suggests that the dominant physics of forward $K^+$-nucleus scattering has been captured.
