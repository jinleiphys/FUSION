# Universal relations for anisotropic interacting quark stars

> Interacting quark stars, which are entirely composed of interacting quark matter including perturbative QCD corrections and color superconductivity, can meet constraints from various pulsar observations. In realistic scenarios, pressure anisotropies are expected in the star's interior. Recently, the stellar structural properties of anisotropic interacting quark stars have been investigated. In this study, we further explore the universal relations (URs) related to the moment of inertia $I$, tidal deformability $Λ$, compactness $C$, and the $f$-mode nonradial pulsation frequency for such stars. Our results reveal that these approximate URs generally hold, being insensitive to both the EOS variations as well as to the presence of anisotropy. In contrast to previous studies on anisotropic neutron stars, we find that more positive anisotropy tends to enhance the $I-Λ$ and $I-C$ URs, but weakens the $C-Λ$ UR. For all the URs involving $f$-mode frequency, we find that they are enhanced by the inclusion of anisotropy (whether positive or negative). Utilizing these URs and the tidal deformability constraint from the GW170817 event, we put limits on the structural properties of isotropic and anisotropic quark stars, such as the moment of inertia $I_{1.4}$, the canonical radius $R_{1.4}$ and the canonical $f$-mode frequency $f_{f,1.4}$, all of which are very different compared to those of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal relations (URs) connecting moment of inertia, tidal deformability, compactness, and f-mode frequency for interacting quark stars remain approximately insensitive to both equation-of-state variations and the presence of pressure anisotropy, but with the hedge that the degree of universality depends on the sign and magnitude of the anisotropy parameter, with positive anisotropy enhancing some URs and weakening others.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for anisotropic interacting quark stars using the Quasi-Local anisotropy profile, where the anisotropic factor is proportional to the product of the local compactness and radial pressure, controlled by a dimensionless parameter beta_QL. They compute the moment of inertia via the slowly rotating approximation, tidal deformability from the Love number formalism, and f-mode frequencies under the Cowling approximation, then fit the resulting relations with polynomial expansions to quantify their universality across different values of the interaction parameter lambda and anisotropy parameter beta_QL.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropy parameter beta_QL is varied in the range [-0.6, 0.6] for which appreciable changes in mass-radius diagrams are observed while remaining consistent with observations.
- For the I-Lambda UR, maximum deviations between numerical data and fits are at the ~1% level across the range of adopted lambda values.
- For the C-Lambda UR, maximum deviations are at the ~8% level, much larger than for the I-Lambda UR.
- For the I-C UR, maximum fractional differences are at least 5%.
- Using the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint Lambda_1.4 <= 800, the canonical radius R_1.4 is bounded to <= 11.793 km for beta_QL = -0.6, <= 11.770 km for isotropic case, and <= 11.728 km for beta_QL = 0.6.
- The canonical moment of inertia I_1.4 is bounded to <= 1.763 x 10^45 g cm^2 for beta_QL = -0.6, <= 1.783 x 10^45 g cm^2 for isotropic case, and <= 1.795 x 10^45 g cm^2 for beta_QL = 0.6.
- The canonical f-mode frequency f_f,1.4 is bounded to >= 2.004 kHz for beta_QL = -0.6, >= 2.108 kHz for isotropic case, and >= 2.213 kHz for beta_QL = 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the study of universal relations for anisotropic neutron stars, previously investigated by Das (2022) using the Bowers-Liang model and Mohanty (2023) using the Quasi-Local model, to the case of interacting quark stars. The authors find that the I-C and C-Lambda relations for quark stars are quite different from those for neutron stars derived by Yagi and Yunes (2017), but are consistent with the analytic expressions for quark stars derived by Chan (2016). The results enable using gravitational-wave constraints from events like GW170817 to place limits on the structural properties of quark stars, which are very different from those of neutron stars.
