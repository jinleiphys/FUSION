# Isentropic evolution of the matter in heavy-ion collisions and the search for the critical endpoint

> We study the isentropic evolution of the matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions for various values of the entropy-per-baryon ratio of interest for the ongoing and future experimental searches for the critical endpoint (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram: these includes the current Beam-Energy-Scan (BES) program at RHIC and the fixed-target collisions foreseen for the near future at various facilities. We describe the hot-dense matter through two different effective Lagrangians: the PNJL (Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio) and the PQM (Polyakov-quark-meson) models. We focus on quantities expected to have a direct experimental relevance: the speed of sound, responsible for the collective acceleration of the fireball, and the generalized susceptibilities, connected to the cumulants of the distributions of conserved charges. In principle, they should affect the momentum spectra and the event-by-event fluctuations of the yields of identified particles. Taking realistic values for the initial temperature and the entropy-per-baryon ratio we study the temporal evolution of the above quantities looking for differences along isentropic trajectories covering different regions of the QCD phase diagram, passing far or close to the CEP or even intersecting the first-order critical line.

## Key claim
The paper investigates whether isentropic trajectories (constant entropy-per-baryon ratio) in heavy-ion collisions can reveal signatures of the QCD critical endpoint (CEP) or first-order phase transition, but finds that for all currently accessible Beam-Energy-Scan (BES) energies at RHIC (center-of-mass energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV), the system evolves through a smooth crossover region rather than near the CEP or first-order line.

## Method
The authors model hot dense QCD matter using two effective Lagrangians in the mean-field approximation: the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model. They compute the phase diagram, speed of sound, and generalized quark-number susceptibilities along isentropic trajectories with entropy-per-baryon ratios estimated from experimental data at various BES energies, tracking the evolution from initial entropy density down to kinetic freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers
- CEP location in PNJL model: \((\mu_B^{\rm CEP}, T^{\rm CEP}) = (875, 121)\) MeV
- CEP location in PQM model: \((\mu_B^{\rm CEP}, T^{\rm CEP}) = (903, 118)\) MeV
- Critical isentrope passing through CEP: \(s/n_B = 7.02\) in PNJL model; \(s/n_B = 6.16\) in PQM model
- Initial entropy density at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV: \(s_0 = 84\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Entropy-per-baryon ratio at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV: \(S/B = 331.6\)
- Entropy-per-baryon ratio at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV: \(S/B = 17.5\)
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV: \(T_{\rm kin}^{\rm fo} = 89\) MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV: \(T_{\rm kin}^{\rm fo} = 116\) MeV

## Context
This work extends previous effective-model studies of the QCD phase diagram (e.g., Ratti (2005) for PNJL, Mocsy (2003) for PQM) by focusing specifically on isentropic evolution with experimentally realistic entropy-per-baryon ratios from the RHIC BES program (Adamczyk (2017), Abelev (2008)). The results suggest that current BES energies probe only the crossover region, motivating searches at lower energies (e.g., at FAIR and NICA) where trajectories might approach the CEP or first-order line; the paper also provides a framework for interpreting future measurements of speed of sound and conserved-charge fluctuations.
