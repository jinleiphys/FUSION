# Quadrupole deformation in $Λ$-hypernuclei

> Shapes of light normal nuclei and $Λ$-hypernuclei are investigated using relativistic mean field approach. The FSUGold parametrization is used for this purpose. The addition of a $Λ$ is found to change the shape of the energy surface towards prolate. The deformation in a $Λ$-hypernucleus, when the hyperon is in the first excited state, is also discussed. The effect of the inclusion of the hyperon on the nuclear radius is generally small with one exception.

## Key claim

The addition of a Λ hyperon generally changes the energy surface of light nuclei towards prolate deformation, but the effect on the quadrupole deformation parameter β is usually small; the paper's central hedge is that previous calculations reported a decrease in deformation upon adding a Λ, whereas the present work finds that deformation may increase in some cases, and that oblate minima disappear entirely in the hypernuclei studied.

## Method

The authors performed constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the FSUGold parametrization, with the ΛN interaction parameters fitted to experimental separation energies of 12 hypernuclei in the mass region 16 to 208. They restricted deformation to axially symmetric, reflection-symmetric shapes (prolate and oblate ellipsoids), expanded boson fields in Legendre polynomials up to angular momentum L=6, and solved the field equations on a 0.1 fm grid. Pairing was neglected because it was found unimportant for light nuclei in this mass range, and the energy surface as a function of quadrupole deformation β was obtained using Lagrange's undetermined multiplier method.

## Key numbers

- For ⁸Be core: β = 0.38 (prolate); for ⁹_ΛBe (Λ in s-state): β = 0.34, Λ separation energy E_Λ = 7.189 MeV (experimental value 6.71 ± 0.04 MeV).
- For ¹²C core: β = -0.21 (oblate); for ¹³_ΛC (Λ in s-state): β = 0.13 (prolate), E_Λ = 11.842 MeV (experimental value 11.69 ± 0.12 MeV).
- For ¹³_ΛC (Λ in p-state): β = 0.14, E_Λ = 0.779 MeV, with proton radius r_p = 2.61 fm, neutron radius r_n = 3.00 fm, and total radius r = 2.92 fm (the largest radius increase observed).
- For ²⁸Si core: β = 0.00 (spherical); for ²⁹_ΛSi (Λ in s-state): β = 0.06 (prolate), E_Λ = 18.897 MeV.
- For ²⁴Mg core: β = 0.25 (prolate); for ²⁵_ΛMg (Λ in s-state): β = 0.23, E_Λ = 16.014 MeV.
- For ²⁵_ΛMg (Λ in p-state): β = 0.24, E_Λ = 8.039 MeV, with radii nearly unchanged from the core.

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean field studies of hypernuclei, contrasting with earlier Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by Zhou et al. (2010) and Win et al. (2010) that reported similar deformations for ¹²C and ¹³_ΛC, and with RMF calculations by Hagino et al. (2009) that found deformation completely disappears in ¹³_ΛC and ²⁹_ΛSi. The results also differ from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations by Isaka et al. (2011), which found that a Λ in p-wave enhances deformation while s-wave reduces it. The paper enables future work on the mechanism driving the disappearance of oblate minima and the systematic study of shape polarization by hyperons in light nuclei.
