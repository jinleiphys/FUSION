# Systematic Matter and Binding-Energy Distributions from a Dispersive Optical Model Analysis

> We present the first systematic nonlocal dispersive optical model analysis using both bound-state and scattering data of $^{16,18}$O, $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{58,64}$Ni, $^{112,124}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb. In all systems, roughly half the total nuclear binding energy is associated with the most-bound 10% of the total nucleon density. The extracted neutron skins reveal the interplay of asymmetry, Coulomb, and shell effects on the skin thickness. Our results indicate that simultaneous optical model fits of inelastic scattering and structural data on isotopic pairs are effective for constraining asymmetry-dependent nuclear structural quantities otherwise difficult to observe experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic nonlocal dispersive optical model (DOM) analysis across multiple nuclei from A=16 to A=208, finding that roughly half the total nuclear binding energy is associated with the most-bound 10% of the total nucleon density in all systems studied, with the exact fraction depending weakly on nuclear size and being independent of asymmetry.

## Method

The authors performed a nonlocal dispersive optical model analysis using both bound-state and scattering data for the isotope pairs 16,18O, 40,48Ca, 58,64Ni, 112,124Sn, and 208Pb. They simultaneously fit nine sectors of experimental data (differential elastic-scattering cross sections, analyzing powers, reaction cross sections, total cross sections, binding energies, charge radii, charge densities, single-nucleon separation energies, and particle numbers) using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling for parameter optimization and uncertainty characterization. The self-energy was restricted to -300 MeV to 200 MeV with respect to the Fermi energy, and the hole spectral function was extracted from the propagator via the Dyson equation to calculate nucleon point densities and binding energies per the Migdal-Galitsky rule.

## Key numbers

- For all nine isotopes studied, the fraction of total binding energy possessed by the most-bound 10% of the total nucleon density (BF10%) exceeds 40%
- For 40Ca, a single-particle calculation placing all spectral density for s1/2 nucleons at their lowest eigenvalue yields BF10% of 31%, compared to the median value of 48% from the full DOM analysis
- The linear model BF10% = x0 + A^(1/3) xA + ((N-Z)/A) xα gives parameter posteriors: x0 = 36^(44)_(30), xA = 4.1^(6.1)_(1.5), xα = 3^(31)_(-26) (16th, 50th, and 84th percentile values)
- Neutron skin for 16O: -0.025^(-0.023)_(-0.027) fm; for 18O: 0.06^(0.11)_(0.02) fm; for 40Ca: -0.051^(-0.048)_(-0.055) fm; for 48Ca: 0.22^(0.24)_(0.19) fm; for 58Ni: -0.03^(-0.02)_(-0.05) fm; for 64Ni: -0.01^(0.03)_(-0.04) fm; for 112Sn: 0.05^(0.08)_(0.02) fm; for 124Sn: 0.17^(0.23)_(0.12) fm; for 208Pb: 0.18^(0.25)_(0.12) fm
- Extrapolation from 58,64Ni gives a 56Ni skin thickness of -0.04 ± 0.03 fm; from 112,124Sn gives a 100Sn skin thickness of -0.07 ± 0.06 fm
- Extrapolation from 16O and 40Ca gives neutron skins of -0.07 ± 0.02 fm for 56Ni and -0.12 ± 0.04 fm for 100Sn
- The 18O neutron skin of 0.06^(0.11)_(0.02) fm is compatible with the mirror-nucleus upper limit of 0.17 fm from the 18Ne-18O charge radius difference of 0.20 ± 0.01 fm

## Context

This work extends previous DOM case studies that examined only single calcium isotopes (Mahzoon 2014, Mahzoon 2017, Atkinson 2018) or included almost no bound-state information (Mueller 2011), by performing the first multi-nucleus treatment of nucleon matter and binding energy distributions in an optical-model framework. The binding energy distribution for 16O agrees with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock treatment of Muther (1995) and with general features of ab initio many-body calculations for nuclear matter (Vonderfecht 1993). The results for 48Ca (0.22 fm) and 208Pb (0.18 fm) are close to those from recent experimental studies (Zenihiro 2018, Tarbert 2014, Zenihiro 2010) and the relativistic density functional model FSUGold, but differ from the coupled-cluster-based prediction of 0.12-0.15 fm for 48Ca from Hagen (2016), which the proposed CREX experiment is expected to resolve. The median skins for 112,124Sn (0.05 and 0.17 fm) are almost identical to those extracted by Terashima (2008) from 295-MeV proton elastic scattering on Sn isotopes.
