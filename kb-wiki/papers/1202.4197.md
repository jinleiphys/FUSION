# Final State Interactions Effects in Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions

> Final State Interactions effects are discussed in the context of Monte Carlo simulations of neutrino-nucleus interactions. A role of Formation Time is explained and several models describing this effect are compared. Various observables which are sensitive to FSI effects are reviewed including pion-nucleus interaction and hadron yields in backward hemisphere. NuWro Monte Carlo neutrino event generator is described and its ability to understand neutral current $π^0$ production data in $\sim 1$ GeV neutrino flux experiments is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Final State Interactions (FSI) effects, particularly Formation Time (FT) models, significantly impact neutrino-nucleus interaction simulations, and that the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator can reproduce neutral current π⁰ production data at ~1 GeV only when FT effects are included, with the caveat that the FT parameter τ must be tuned to ~8 fm/c based on NOMAD backward pion data.

## Method

The authors implemented the Oset model of intra-nuclear cascade (INC) within the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator, using effective pion-nucleon cross sections and a step length of 0.2 fm for hadron propagation through the nucleus. They compared several FT models (coherence length for quasielastic scattering, Δ propagation for resonance interactions, and the Ranft model for deep inelastic scattering) and tuned the Ranft parameter τ to reproduce NOMAD data on backward-moving pions, then applied the model to neutral current π⁰ production data.

## Key numbers

- The Ranft FT parameter τ was tuned to ~8 fm/c based on NOMAD backward π⁻ data with momentum between 350 and 800 MeV/c
- For π⁺ on ¹²C at T_π = 85 MeV, the probability of macroscopic quasi-elastic process proceeding through n=1,2,3 collisions: NuWro gives 0.89, 0.10, 0.01 (Oset model: 0.90, 0.09, 0.01)
- For π⁺ on ¹²C at T_π = 245 MeV, the probability of macroscopic quasi-elastic process proceeding through n=1,2,3,4 collisions: NuWro gives 0.67, 0.24, 0.07, 0.02 (Oset model: 0.69, 0.25, 0.05, 0.01)
- For π⁺ on ¹²C at T_π = 85 MeV, the probability of pion absorption after n=0,1,2 quasi-elastic scatterings: NuWro gives 0.87, 0.12, 0.01 (Oset model: 0.81, 0.17, 0.02)
- For π⁺ on ¹²C at T_π = 245 MeV, the probability of pion absorption after n=0,1,2,3 quasi-elastic scatterings: NuWro gives 0.41, 0.37, 0.16, 0.05 (Oset model: 0.37, 0.41, 0.17, 0.04)
- The SKAT parameterization gives μ² = 0.08 ± 0.04 GeV² based on low-momentum proton data (300 MeV/c < p < 600 MeV/c)
- The GENIE FT parameter τ₀ = 0.342 fm/c
- The Δ resonance width Γ ≈ 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the intra-nuclear cascade approach of Oset (1982) and the formation time concepts from Landau and Pomeranchuk, Stodolsky, and Ranft, applying them specifically to neutrino-nucleus interactions in the 1 GeV energy region relevant to oscillation experiments (MINOS, T2K, MiniBooNE, NOvA). The paper compares NuWro predictions with NOMAD backward pion data and demonstrates that FT effects are crucial for understanding NC π⁰ production, which is a background to ν_μ → ν_e oscillation searches in water Cherenkov detectors. The results enable improved systematic error estimates for neutrino oscillation measurements by providing a validated FSI model within a Monte Carlo framework.
