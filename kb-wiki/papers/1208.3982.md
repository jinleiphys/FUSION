# Contribution of the "hanged" diagrams into the reaction $np \to np π^+ π^-$

> The contribution of "hanged" diagrams into the reaction $np \rightarrow np π^+ π^-$ was considered. It was shown that taking into account of these diagrams permits to get better description of the effective mass spectrum of $π^+π^-$-combinations.

## Key claim

The inclusion of so-called "hanged" diagrams (π-meson and pomeron exchange) improves the description of the π⁺π⁻ effective mass spectrum in the reaction np → np π⁺π⁻, particularly for masses near 300 MeV/c², but the authors note that at P₀ = 5.20 GeV/c the contribution of these diagrams did not exceed 2%, and interference between diagrams was neglected.

## Method

The matrix elements for "hanged" diagrams involving π-meson exchange and pomeron (P) exchange were constructed, using formfactors from prior work (Jerusalimov, OPERnp2pi), off-shell elastic ππ-scattering amplitudes, and vertex functions with parameters from Nikitin. The squared matrix element was written as the sum of the squared amplitudes for π⁰ exchange, π± exchange, and pomeron exchange, neglecting interference terms, and calculations were performed for the reaction np → np π⁺π⁻ at two kinematic conditions.

## Key numbers

- At P₀ = 5.20 GeV/c, the contribution of "hanged" diagrams did not exceed 2%.
- The constant of strong interaction used was G²/4π = 14.6.
- Calculations were performed for the reaction at P₀ = 1.73 GeV/c and at T_kin = 1.25 GeV (the latter using ~10⁷ events from the HADES setup).
- The improved description of the π⁺π⁻ effective mass spectrum was noted for masses close to 300 MeV/c².

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of the reaction np → np π⁺π⁻ at intermediate energies (1.73 < P₀ < 5.2 GeV/c) by Jerusalimov (OPERnp2pi), which considered reggeized π-meson exchange and one-baryon exchange diagrams but omitted "hanged" diagrams. The present analysis was motivated by statistically significant bumps observed in HADES data at T_kin = 1.25 GeV (np2piH, HADES), which were previously attributed to fluctuations at low statistics. The inclusion of "hanged" diagrams enables a better description of the low-mass π⁺π⁻ region and suggests a dynamical origin for the observed structure.
