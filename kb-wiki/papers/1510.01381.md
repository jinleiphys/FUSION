# Liquid-gas phase transition in strange hadronic matter with relativistic models

> Background: The advent of new dedicated experimental programs on hyperon physics is rapidly boosting the field, and the possibility of synthetizing multiple strange hypernuclei requires the addition of the strangeness degree of freedom to the models dedicated to nuclear structure and nuclear matter studies at low energy. Purpose: We want to settle the influence of strangeness on the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. Because of the large uncertainties concerning the hyperon sector, we do not aim at a quantitative estimation of the phase diagram but rather at a qualitative description of the phenomenology, as model independent as possible. Method: We analyze the phase diagram of low density matter composed of neutrons, protons and $Λ$ hyperons using a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model. We largely explore the parameter space to pin down generic features of the phase transition, and compare the results to ab-initio quantum Monte Carlo calculations. Results: We show that the liquid-gas phase transition is only slightly quenched by the addition of hyperons. Strangeness is seen to be an order parameter of the phase transition, meaning that dilute strange matter is expected to be unstable with respect to the formation of hyper-clusters. Conclusions: More quantitative results within the RMF model need improved functionals at low density, possibly fitted to ab-initio calculations of nuclear and $Λ$ matter.

## Key claim

The liquid-gas phase transition in low-density hadronic matter is only slightly quenched by the addition of \(\Lambda\) hyperons, and strangeness acts as an order parameter of the transition; however, the authors emphasize that due to large uncertainties in the hyperon sector, their results are qualitative and model-independent rather than quantitative predictions of the phase diagram.

## Method

The authors analyzed the phase diagram of zero-temperature matter composed of neutrons, protons, and \(\Lambda\) hyperons using both the Linear Walecka Model (LWM) and the Nonlinear Walecka Model (NLWM) within a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) framework. They systematically varied the hyperon-meson coupling constants over a wide parameter space constrained by single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclear data (\(U_{\Lambda}^{N}(n_0) = -28\) MeV) and double-\(\Lambda\) bond energy data (\(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.67\) MeV), and compared the RMF results to ab-initio Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of n\(\Lambda\) matter.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\) (NLWM) and \(0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) (LWM).
* Single-\(\Lambda\) potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: \(U_{\Lambda}^{N}(n_0) = -28\) MeV, used to constrain the \(\chi_{\sigma\Lambda}\) and \(\chi_{\omega\Lambda}\) coupling ratios.
* Double-\(\Lambda\) bond energy: \(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.67\) MeV, interpreted as \(U_{\Lambda}^{\Lambda}(n_0/5) = -0.67\) MeV, used to constrain the strange meson couplings \(\chi_{\sigma^*\Lambda}\) and \(\chi_{\phi\Lambda}\).
* Nuclear matter incompressibility: \(K = 300\) MeV (NLWM) and \(554\) MeV (LWM); symmetry energy: \(\mathcal{E}_{\text{sym}} = 32.5\) MeV (NLWM) and \(39.22\) MeV (LWM); symmetry energy slope: \(L = 94\) MeV (NLWM) and \(127.22\) MeV (LWM).

## Context

This work extends the study of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, previously explored for isospin asymmetry by Muller (1995), Chomaz (2004), and Ducoin (2006), into the strangeness dimension. It contrasts with earlier studies by Schaffner (2002) that predicted a thermodynamic phase transition in strange compressed baryonic matter leading to exotic neutron star families, and with more recent analyses by Aoki (2009) and Ahn (2013) suggesting very weak \(\Lambda\)-\(\Lambda\) attraction. The results enable future work on the formation of hyperclusters in low-density matter, with possible implications for relativistic heavy-ion collisions as discussed by Mallik (2015) and Khan (2015).
