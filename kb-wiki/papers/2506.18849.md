# Nuclear $β$-decay half-lives within the subtracted second random-phase approximation

> We employ, within the framework of Skyrme energy-density functional theory, the subtracted second random-phase approximation, recently developed for charge-exchange excitations, to compute $β$-decay half-lives in four nuclei, $^{24}$O, $^{34}$Si, $^{78}$Ni, and $^{132}$Sn. Following our recent results on the description of the Gamow-Teller strength, we proceed coherently in the present work by computing $β$-decay half-lives using the bare value of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$. Half-lives are thus obtained, within the allowed Gamow-Teller approximation, without the use of any ad hoc quenching factors. A genuine quenching is indeed microscopically introduced in our model owing to the correlations induced by the coupling of one-particle one-hole configurations with two-particle two-hole ones. The role of the so-called $J^2$ terms is also studied. By comparing our results with experimental data, we show a general improvement of $β$-decay half-lives with respect to results obtained within the commonly used Random Phase Approximation (RPA). The inclusion of the two-particle two-hole configurations produces a more fragmented and richer spectrum within the $β$-window, resulting in lower $β$ half-lives with respect to the RPA ones.

## Key claim

The subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA), applied with the bare axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A = 1.26\) and without any ad hoc quenching factors, yields \(\beta\)-decay half-lives that are systematically lower than those from the standard Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and generally in better agreement with experimental data for the four spherical, non-superfluid nuclei studied. The improvement is conditional on the inclusion of two-particle two-hole (\(2p-2h\)) configurations, which microscopically introduce a genuine quenching of the Gamow-Teller strength.

## Method

The authors employ the subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA) within the Skyrme energy-density functional framework, using the Hartree-Fock approximation for the ground state and both RPA and SSRPA for excited states. The \(\beta\)-decay half-lives are computed in the allowed Gamow-Teller approximation using the bare value \(g_A = 1.26\), with the GT strength function calculated from the one-body transition operator \(\hat{O}^{-} = \sum_i \sigma(i) \tau^{-}(i)\). The role of the \(J^2\) terms in the Skyrme interaction is also investigated by switching them on and off for the SGII and SLy5 parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{78}\)Ni, the SSRPA with the SGII interaction yields a half-life in excellent agreement with the experimental value \(T_{1/2} = 122.2 \pm 5.1\) ms (Xu et al.).
- For \(^{132}\)Sn, the RPA half-life is formally infinite (zero total GT strength within the \(\beta\)-window), while the SSRPA provides a finite half-life.
- The convergence of SSRPA results is achieved with energy cutoffs on \(2p-2h\) configurations of 50–55 MeV for \(^{24}\)O, 40–45 MeV for \(^{34}\)Si and \(^{78}\)Ni, and 30–35 MeV for \(^{132}\)Sn, with relative differences between successive evaluations below 1\%.
- The constant \(D\) in the half-life formula is \(D = 6163.4 \pm 3.8\) s.
- The mass difference between neutron and hydrogen is \(\Delta_{nH} = 0.78227\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SSRPA studies of Gamow-Teller strength (Gambacurta and Grasso, 2020, 2022) to the computation of \(\beta\)-decay half-lives, demonstrating that explicit inclusion of \(2p-2h\) configurations reduces or eliminates the need for ad hoc quenching factors. The results are consistent with those of Sagawa et al. (2023) and with the particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model of Niu et al. (2015, 2018), though the SSRPA shows force-dependent behavior, particularly for the SLy5 parametrization. The work enables future applications to neutrino-less double \(\beta\)-decay matrix elements using unquenched quantities, though the authors note that extension to deformed or open-shell systems would require further development.
