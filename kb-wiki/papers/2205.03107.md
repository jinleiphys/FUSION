# Wave Phenomena In General Relativistic Magnetohydrodynamics

> Here we study the wave propagation and stability of general relativistic non-resistive dissipative second-order magnetohydrodynamic equations in curved space-time. We solve the Boltzmann equation for a system of particles and antiparticles using the relaxation time approximation and the Chapman-Enskog-like gradient expansion for the off-equilibrium distribution function, truncating beyond second-order in curved space-time in electromagnetic fields. Unlike holographic calculation~\cite{Baier:2007ix}, we show that the viscous evolution equations do not explicitly depend on the curvature of space-time. Also, we have tested the causality and stability of the second-order theory in curved space-time in the presence of linearised metric perturbation and derived dispersion relations for various modes. Interestingly, we found the coupling of gravitational modes with the usual magneto-sonic modes in the small wave-number limit. Also, we show additional non-hydrodynamical modes arise due to gravity for a bulk-viscous fluid.

## Key claim

The authors derive second-order general relativistic magnetohydrodynamic equations from kinetic theory and show that, unlike holographic calculations (Baier 2007), the viscous evolution equations do not explicitly depend on the curvature of space-time, though the derivation assumes a weak gravitational field limit where the Christoffel symbol contribution is much smaller than the cube of the temperature.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation with a relaxation time approximation collision kernel in curved space-time, using a Chapman-Enskog-like gradient expansion for the off-equilibrium distribution function truncated beyond second order in the presence of electromagnetic fields. They then linearize the resulting conservation equations, Maxwell's equations, and the Einstein field equations around a flat background with small metric perturbations to derive dispersion relations and test causality and stability.

## Key numbers

- The smallness condition for the gravitational force is given by $\tau_c \Gamma^{\mu}_{\alpha\beta} \ll 1$, corresponding to a weak gravitational force limit $\mathcal{F}_g^{\mu} \ll T^3$
- The first-order bulk viscosity coefficient is $\beta_{\Pi} = \frac{5\beta}{3}J_{42}^{(1)+} + \mathcal{X} J_{31}^{(0)+} - \mathcal{Y} J_{21}^{(0)-}$ where $\mathcal{X} = \frac{J_{10}^{(0)+}(\epsilon+P) - J_{20}^{(0)-}n_f}{D_{20}}$ and $\mathcal{Y} = \frac{J_{20}^{(0)-}(\epsilon+P) - J_{30}^{(0)+}n_f}{D_{20}}$
- The first-order shear viscosity coefficient is $\beta_{\pi} = \beta J_{42}^{(1)+}$
- The first-order diffusion coefficient is $\beta_V = \frac{1}{h}J_{21}^{(0)-} - J_{21}^{(1)-}$
- For non-viscous fluid with gravity, sound modes have dispersion $\omega = \pm c_s \sqrt{k_x^2 + k_z^2}$ and gravitational modes have dispersion $\omega = \pm \sqrt{k^2 - 2\Lambda + 16\pi G P_0}$

## Context

This work extends the second-order causal dissipative magnetohydrodynamic formulations previously derived for flat space-time by Denicol (2018), Panda (2020, 2021) and others to curved space-time, lifting the restriction of Minkowski metric. It enables numerical simulations of dissipative GRMHD in astrophysical contexts such as binary neutron star mergers and heavy-ion collisions where strong magnetic fields and curved space-time are relevant, while confirming that the viscous evolution equations take the same form as in flat space-time, consistent with the approach taken by Most (2021).
