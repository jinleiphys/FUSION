# Study of chirally motivated low-energy $K^-$ optical potentials

> The $K^-$ optical potential in the nuclear medium is evaluated self consistently from a free-space $K^-N$ $t$ matrix constructed within a coupled-channel chiral approach to the low-energy $\bar K N$ data. The chiral-model parameters are fitted to a select subset of the low-energy data {\it plus} the $K^-$ atomic data throughout the periodic table. The resulting attractive $K^-$ optical potentials are relatively `shallow', with central depth of the real part about 55 MeV, for a fairly reasonable reproduction of the atomic data with $χ^2 / N \approx 2.2$. Relatively `deep' attractive potentials of depth about 180 MeV, which result in other phenomenological approaches with $χ^2 / N \approx 1.5$, are ruled out within chirally motivated models. Different physical data input is required to distinguish between shallow and deep $K^-$ optical potentials. The ($K^{-}_{\rm stop},π$) reaction could provide such a test, with exclusive rates differing by over a factor of three for the two classes of potentials. Finally, forward ($K^-,p$) differential cross sections for the production of relatively narrow deeply bound $K^-$ {\it nuclear} states are evaluated for deep $K^-$ optical potentials, yielding values considerably lower than those estimated before.

## Key claim

Chirally motivated models of the $K^-$ optical potential, fitted simultaneously to low-energy $\bar K N$ data and $K^-$ atomic data throughout the periodic table, produce relatively shallow potentials (real central depth about 55 MeV) that rule out the deep potentials (depth about 180 MeV) favored by phenomenological approaches, but the atomic data alone cannot unambiguously distinguish between the two classes because the quality of fit for the shallow potential ($\chi^2/N \approx 2.2$) is inferior to that of the deep potential ($\chi^2/N \approx 1.5$).

## Method

The authors constructed a chirally motivated coupled-channel model of the $\bar K N$ interaction with six meson-baryon channels, using separable potentials with parameters fitted to threshold branching ratios and cross sections at 110 MeV/c, plus 65 $K^-$ atomic data points across the periodic table. The in-medium $K^-$ optical potential was evaluated self consistently by including $\bar K$ and nucleon self energies in the meson-baryon propagator, with the nucleon optical potential taken as $V_0 = (-60 - i10)$ MeV at nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Key numbers

- Real central depth of the chirally motivated $K^-$ optical potential: about 55 MeV at the center of Ni nucleus
- Imaginary depth of the chirally motivated $K^-$ optical potential: about 60 MeV at the center of Ni nucleus
- $\chi^2/N = 2.2$ for the best chirally motivated fit to 65 $K^-$ atomic data points
- $\chi^2/N = 1.5$ for the best phenomenological deep potential fits to the same atomic data
- Depth of the deep phenomenological potential: about 180 MeV (real part) and 71 MeV (imaginary part) at the center of Ni
- $\chi^2 = 300$ ($\chi^2/N = 4.62$) for the unmodified Ramos and Oset (2000) amplitudes applied to atomic data without any free parameters
- Inverse range parameters for the chiral model: $\alpha_{K^-p} = \alpha_{\bar K^0 n} = 757.8$ MeV, $\alpha_{\pi^0\Lambda} = 300$ MeV, $\alpha_{\pi^+\Sigma^-} = \alpha_{\pi^0\Sigma^0} = \alpha_{\pi^-\Sigma^+} = 448.1$ MeV
- Pseudoscalar meson decay constant: $f_\pi = 94.5$ MeV
- Measured threshold branching ratios used in fits: $\gamma = 2.36 \pm 0.04$, $R_c = 0.664 \pm 0.011$, $R_n = 0.189 \pm 0.015$
- Calculated ($K^-_{\rm stop},\pi$) capture rates per stopped $K^-$ for $^{12}$C ground state production: $0.24 \times 10^{-3}$ for the chiral potential, compared to $0.98 \pm 0.12 \times 10^{-3}$ measured by Tamura et al. (1994)

## Context

This work extends the chirally motivated coupled-channel approach of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Waas, Kaiser, and Weise (1996) by including $\bar K$ and nucleon self energies self consistently, following the suggestion of Lutz (1998) and the calculations of Ramos and Oset (2000) and Schaffner-Bielich et al. (2000). It contradicts the deep phenomenological potentials of Friedman, Gal, and Batty (1993, 1994) and the relativistic mean field calculations of Friedman et al. (1999), which fit atomic data with $\chi^2/N \approx 1.5$ but produce potentials about 180 MeV deep. The paper enables future experimental tests using ($K^-_{\rm stop},\pi$) reactions, where exclusive rates differ by over a factor of three between shallow and deep potentials, and forward ($K^-,p$) reactions for producing deeply bound $K^-$ nuclear states, though the calculated cross sections are considerably lower than estimates by Kishimoto (1999).
