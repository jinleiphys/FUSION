# Isospin symmetry breaking in atomic nuclei

> The importance of the isospin symmetry and its breaking in elucidating the properties of atomic nuclei is reviewed. The quark mass splitting and the electromagnetic origin of the isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) for nuclear many-body problem is discussed. The experimental data on isobaric analogue states cannot be described only with the Coulomb interaction, and ISB terms in the nucleon-nucleon interaction are needed to discern the observed properties. In the present work, the ISB terms are explicitly considered in nuclear energy density functional and spherical shell model approaches, and a detailed investigation of the analogue states and other properties of nuclei is performed. It is observed that isospin mixing is largest for the $N=Z$ system in the density functional approach.

## Key claim

The paper reviews and extends the understanding that isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) in atomic nuclei cannot be explained solely by the Coulomb interaction; explicit ISB terms in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, originating from quark mass splitting and electromagnetic effects, are required to describe experimental data on isobaric analogue states, and the authors demonstrate that isospin mixing is largest for N=Z systems in the density functional approach.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive review combined with new calculations using two complementary theoretical frameworks: nuclear energy density functional (DFT) and spherical shell model approaches. They explicitly incorporated ISB terms into the nucleon-nucleon interaction within these frameworks and systematically investigated isobaric analogue states, mirror nuclei mass differences, and isospin mixing across the nuclear chart.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton mass difference: 1.29 MeV, a fundamental manifestation of ISB critical for the existence of the universe.
- Up-quark mass: m_u = 2.19(15) MeV; down-quark mass: m_d = 4.67(20) MeV; strange-quark mass: m_s = 94(3) MeV at the 2 GeV scale in the MS scheme from lattice QCD with QED corrections.
- Charge independence breaking (CIB) scattering length difference: Δa_CIB = 5.7 ± 0.3 fm, defined as (a^N_pp + a^N_nn)/2 - a^N_np.
- Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) scattering length difference: Δa_CSB = a^N_pp - a^N_nn = 1.5 ± 0.5 fm.
- Mass differences for light mirror nuclei: n-p = +1.29 MeV; ³H-³He = +0.52 MeV; ⁵He-⁵Li = +0.21 MeV; ⁷Li-⁷Be = -0.35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the foundational understanding of isospin symmetry from Wigner (1937) and the isobaric mass multiplet equation (IMME) framework, building on the classification scheme of charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions by Henley and Miller (1969, 1979). It addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and incorporates modern lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2015) that reproduced the neutron-proton mass difference. The paper enables future systematic studies of ISB effects across the nuclear chart using both DFT and shell model approaches, particularly for N=Z nuclei where isospin mixing is predicted to be largest.
