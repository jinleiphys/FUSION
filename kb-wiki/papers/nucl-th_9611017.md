# A functional approach to nuclear electromagnetic response functions

> The separated electromagnetic responses $R_L(q,ω)$ and $R_T(q,ω)$ for inclusive electron scattering off nuclei are studied within a functional scheme.

# A functional approach to nuclear electromagnetic response functions

## Key claim

The paper claims that a one-loop calculation within a boson loop expansion (BLE) using a carefully chosen effective interaction can explain the long-standing discrepancy between the longitudinal response $R_L$ and transverse response $R_T$ and the predictions of the Fermi gas model, but only for momenta where the loop expansion converges quickly; at the lowest momentum examined ($q=300$ MeV) some problems remain, and the approach cannot produce any shift in the quasielastic peak position for $R_T$.

## Method

The authors apply the stationary phase approximation (SPA) to a generating functional describing a relativistic system of nucleons and pions, then restrict to a nonrelativistic potential theory with interactions in the particle-hole channels carrying the quantum numbers of $\pi$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons, including $N\Delta$ and $\Delta\Delta$ transitions. The effective interaction is parametrized with momentum-dependent Landau parameters $g'_{L,T}(q)$ and vertex cutoffs, with the many-body cutoffs set to 800 MeV in the pion channel and 1300 MeV in the rho channel; the scalar omega coupling is treated as a free parameter fixed by fitting the longitudinal response of $^{12}C$ at $q=300$ MeV. The calculation is performed at one-loop order in the boson loop expansion, which prevents summation of the Dyson series for nucleonic self-energies.

## Key numbers

- Many-body cutoff in the pion channel: 800 MeV; in the rho channel: 1300 MeV
- Mass of the transverse rho set to 600 MeV, intended to account for medium modifications
- Landau parameter $g'(0)$ set to 0.35; at $q \sim 2k_F$, $g'_L(q) \simeq 0.75$
- Scalar omega coupling fixed to approximately 0.15 by fitting $R_L$ for $^{12}C$ at $q=300$ MeV
- Vertex cutoffs $\Lambda_{NN}$ for the pion longitudinal channel: 1300 MeV; for the pion transverse channel: 1750 MeV; for all $N\Delta$ and $\Delta\Delta$ transitions: 1000 MeV
- The Coulomb sum rule failure is washed out at $q \sim 600$ MeV/c according to Jourdan's reanalysis

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous functional approach (Cenni and Saracco, 1994; Cenni and Saracco, 1995) by including interactions in all particle-hole channels with $T=0,1$ and scalar, spin-longitudinal, and spin-transverse quantum numbers, as well as $\Delta_{33}$ resonance contributions needed for $R_T$. It addresses the longstanding puzzle, highlighted by Jourdan (1995, 1996), that the longitudinal response is quenched relative to the independent particle model while the transverse response is enhanced, with the effect depending on target nucleus. The approach enables a unified description of both response functions, but the authors note that world data for $R_T$ on $^{40}Ca$ appear too low compared to original Saclay data, a feature also seen in other calculations (Fabrocini, 1996), and call for new experiments at TJNAF to resolve the experimental situation.
