# A New Expansion for Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

> We introduce a new and well defined power counting for the effective field theory describing nucleon-nucleon interactions. Because of the large NN scattering lengths it differs from other applications of chiral perturbation theory and is facilitated by introducing an unusual subtraction scheme and renormalization group analysis. Calculation to subleading order in the expansion can be done analytically, and we present the results for both the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels.

# A New Expansion for Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

## Key claim
The paper introduces a well-defined power counting for effective field theory (EFT) describing nucleon-nucleon interactions, enabled by a novel subtraction scheme (power divergence subtraction, or PDS) in dimensional regularization. The claim is conditional on the large NN scattering lengths making the expansion differ from standard chiral perturbation theory, and the expansion is demonstrated to subleading order for the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels.

## Method
The authors construct a nonrelativistic effective field theory with nucleon fields and, for momenta comparable to the pion mass, include explicit pion fields via the chiral Lagrangian. They use dimensional regularization with a new subtraction scheme (PDS) that subtracts poles at both D=4 and D=3, which removes power-law divergences and allows a consistent expansion where the large scattering lengths are treated nonperturbatively. The leading-order amplitude sums all loop graphs with the contact interaction C0 to all orders, while subleading contributions (including one-pion exchange and momentum-dependent contact terms) are treated perturbatively. The free parameters are fit to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers
- 1S0 channel scattering length: a = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm
- 1S0 channel effective range: r0 = 2.73 ± 0.03 fm
- 3S1 channel scattering length: a = 5.423 ± 0.005 fm
- Leading-order value of C0 at μ = mπ: C0(mπ) = -4π/(M mπ) = -3.7 fm²
- Fitted parameters at μ = mπ: C0(mπ) = -3.34 fm², C2(mπ) = 3.24 fm⁴, D2(mπ) = -0.42 fm⁴
- Expansion scale: ΛNN = (8π f²)/(gA² M) ≈ 300 MeV
- Expected natural size of |C2(mπ)| and |D2(mπ)|: ~ 4π/(M ΛNN mπ²) ≈ 3.5 fm⁴
- The theory reproduces data well up to center-of-mass momentum p ≈ 150 MeV, with fits optimized over p ≤ 200 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts to apply chiral perturbation theory to nuclear physics (Weinberg 1990, Kaplan 1996, and others) which encountered difficulties due to large NN scattering lengths. The authors demonstrate that Weinberg's power counting is not consistent for these systems. The PDS subtraction scheme provides a systematic expansion that enables analytic calculations of NN scattering amplitudes, and the authors note it is also applicable to other finely tuned theories with power-law divergences, such as the standard model with a light Higgs and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. A future publication (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise 1998) will present applications to other partial waves, inelastic processes, deuteron properties, and the N-body problem.
