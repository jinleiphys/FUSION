# Modified Debye-Huckel Electron Shielding and Penetration Factor

> Screened potential, modified by non standard electron cloud distributions responsible for the shielding effect on fusion of reacting nuclei in astrophysical plasmas, is derived. The case of clouds with depleted tails in space coordinates is discussed. The modified screened potential is obtained both from statistical mechanics arguments based on fluctuations of the inverse of the Debye-Huckel radius and from the solution of a Bernoulli equation used in generalized statistical mechanics. Plots and tables useful in evaluating penetration probability at any energy are provided.

## Key claim

The paper derives a modified Debye-Huckel screened potential for nuclear fusion in astrophysical plasmas by assuming that the electron cloud follows a generalized \(q\)-type spatial distribution with depleted tails (for \(0<q<1\)), rather than the standard Boltzmann exponential distribution; this modification enhances the penetration factor at low energies compared to the standard Debye-Huckel result, especially when the number of particles inside the Debye sphere is small and fluctuations of the inverse Debye radius are non-negligible.

## Method

The authors obtain the modified screened potential \(V_q(r)\) using two complementary approaches: (1) a super-statistics framework where the inverse Debye-Huckel radius \(1/R_{\rm DH}\) is treated as a fluctuating parameter distributed according to a gamma distribution, leading to a power-law form for \(r\,V_q(r)\); and (2) a generalized Poisson equation (Bernoulli equation) from Tsallis statistical mechanics, whose solution yields the same \(q\)-exponential potential. The penetration factor \(\Gamma_q(E)\) through the modified barrier is then calculated numerically via the WKB approximation, and the equivalent screening energy \(U_q\) is tabulated and fitted with an analytic interpolation formula.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^{12}{\rm C} - ^{12}{\rm C}\) fusion in laboratory experiments (graphite target, \(k T \simeq 6\times10^{-8}\) MeV, \(\rho \sim 1.7\) g/cm\(^3\), \(D \simeq 0.0168\) MeV), the penetration factor at \(E = 0.0059\) MeV is \(\Gamma_{q=0.50}(E) = 5.20\times10^{-285}\) compared to \(\Gamma_{\rm DH}(E) = 2.16\times10^{-349}\) and \(\Gamma_{\rm C}(E) = 8.16\times10^{-494}\).
* For \(^{12}{\rm C} - ^{12}{\rm C}\) fusion in massive stars (\(k T = 30\times10^{-3}\) MeV, \(\rho \simeq 10^9\) g/cm\(^3\), \(D \simeq 1.04\) MeV), at \(E = 2.2\) MeV the enhancement factor of \(\Gamma_{q=0.25}(E)\) over \(\Gamma_{q=0.99}(E)\) is about 2.5, with \(\Gamma_{q=0.25}(2.2\ {\rm MeV}) = 6.64\times10^{-22}\) and \(\Gamma_{q=0.99}(2.2\ {\rm MeV}) = 2.62\times10^{-22}\).
* For the \(^{14}{\rm N(p,}\gamma)^{15}{\rm O}\) reaction at solar core conditions, at \(E = 0.047\) MeV and \(q = 0.5\), the enhancement factor \(f_{q,\,{\rm DH}}(E) = 3.33\).
* The interpolating formula for \(U_q/D\) (valid for \(0<q<0.8\) and \(0<D/E \leq 10^2\)) uses parameters \(a(q)\), \(b(q)\), \(c(q)\) given by explicit polynomial expressions in \(q\) (e.g., \(a(q) = 0.484012 + 1.984961\,q + 42.933001\,q^8\)).

## Context

This work extends the standard Debye-Huckel screening theory (Salpeter 1954) by incorporating non-Maxwellian electron distributions, motivated by observations of depleted high-energy tails in astrophysical plasmas (Oxenius 1986, Collins 1989, Chevallier 1996) and by generalized statistical mechanics (Tsallis 1988, 1995). It differs from approaches using \(k\)-Lorentz distributions (Bryant 1996, Rubab 2006) by adopting a \(q\)-type spatial distribution derived from super-statistics (Beck 2001, Wilk 2000). The results enable more accurate evaluation of nuclear fusion rates in stellar cores, dense plasmas, and supernova environments, particularly at low energies where standard Debye-Huckel screening underestimates the penetration probability.
