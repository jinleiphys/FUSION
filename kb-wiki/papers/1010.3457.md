# Dynamical Polarizabilities of SU(3) Octet of Baryons

> We present calculations and an analysis of the spin-independent dipole electric and magnetic dynamical polarizabilities for the lowest in mass SU(3) octet of baryons. These extensive calculations are made possible by the recent implementation of semi-automatized calculations in Chiral Perturbation Theory which allows evaluating dynamical spin-independent electromagnetic polarizabilities from Compton scattering up to next-to-the-leading order. Our results are in good agreement with calculations performed for nucleons found in the literature. The dependencies for the range of photon energies up to 1 GeV, covering the majority of the meson photo production channels, are analyzed. The separate contributions into polarizabilities from the various baryon meson clouds are studied.

## Key claim

The authors present next-to-leading-order (NLO) Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations of spin-independent dipole electric and magnetic dynamical polarizabilities for the entire SU(3) octet of baryons, but explicitly exclude resonance excitations (such as the Δ(1232)) and note that the power-counting inconsistency of ChPT makes next-to-NLO calculations desirable for convergence.

## Method

The authors used the Computational Hadronic Model (CHM), which extends the FeynArts package into the hadronic sector, to semi-automatize the calculation of Compton scattering amplitudes up to NLO in SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory. They extracted the energy-dependent structure functions A₁(ω,θ) and A₂(ω,θ) from the spin-independent Compton amplitude, then solved for the dynamical electric polarizability α_E1(ω) and magnetic polarizability β_M1(ω) using the zero-momentum-transfer approximation. Renormalization was performed using the Modified Minimal Subtraction scheme, and separate contributions from neutral pions (including eta), charged pions, and kaons were isolated by selectively including diagram topologies.

- Neutron static electric polarizability: α_E1^(n) = (12.0 ± 1.7) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Proton static magnetic polarizability: β_M1^(p) = (0.8 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron static magnetic polarizability: β_M1^(n) = (8.8 ± 1.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Σ⁺ static electric polarizability: α(0) = 17.1 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Σ⁻ static electric polarizability: α(0) = 17.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Σ⁰ static electric polarizability: α(0) = 8.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Λ static electric polarizability: α(0) = 3.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Ξ⁰ static electric polarizability: α(0) = 2.1 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Ξ⁻ static electric polarizability: α(0) = 0.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Σ⁺ static magnetic polarizability: β(0) = 0.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Σ⁻ static magnetic polarizability: β(0) = 1.9 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Σ⁰ static magnetic polarizability: β(0) = 5.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³

- The charged pion cloud flip from diamagnetic to paramagnetic behavior occurs in the (40-80) MeV range

## Context

This work extends previous Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations of nucleon polarizabilities (such as those by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner using SU(2) Heavy Baryon χPT to O(p⁴)) to the full SU(3) octet of baryons, providing the first systematic NLO results for hyperon dynamical polarizabilities. The results enable future experimental studies of baryon structure through Virtual Compton Scattering and polarized-target experiments, and the authors identify the need for next-to-NLO calculations and inclusion of resonance excitations (particularly the Δ(1232)) as the next steps. The ordering of neutral baryon electric polarizabilities (α_n > α_Σ⁰ > α_Λ > α_Ξ⁰) agrees with the Heavy Baryon ChPT results of Meissner, but the charged baryon ordering (α_Σ⁻ > α_Σ⁺ > α_p > α_Ξ⁻) differs from those predictions.
