# Majorana Neutrino Magnetic Moment and Neutrino Decoupling in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We examine the physics of the early universe when Majorana neutrinos (electron neutrino, muon neutrino, tau neutrino) possess transition magnetic moments. These extra couplings beyond the usual weak interaction couplings alter the way neutrinos decouple from the plasma of electrons/positrons and photons. We calculate how transition magnetic moment couplings modify neutrino decoupling temperatures, and then use a full weak, strong, and electromagnetic reaction network to compute corresponding changes in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis abundance yields. We find that light element abundances and other cosmological parameters are sensitive to magnetic couplings on the order of 10^{-10} Bohr magnetons. Given the recent analysis of sub-MeV Borexino data which constrains Majorana moments to the order of 10^{-11} Bohr magnetons or less, we find that changes in cosmological parameters from magnetic contributions to neutrino decoupling temperatures are below the level of upcoming precision observations.

# Majorana Neutrino Magnetic Moment and Neutrino Decoupling in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

## Key claim
Majorana neutrino transition magnetic moments on the order of 10^{-10} Bohr magnetons can alter neutrino decoupling temperatures and BBN abundance yields, but given current Borexino constraints limiting Majorana moments to 10^{-11} Bohr magnetons or less, the resulting changes in cosmological parameters from magnetic contributions to neutrino decoupling temperatures are below the level of upcoming precision observations.

## Method
The authors calculated neutrino interaction rates including both weak and magnetic scattering and annihilation channels for Majorana neutrinos with transition magnetic moments, using thermally averaged cross sections with Fermi-Dirac statistics and Debye screening to handle infrared divergences. They then modified the Wagoner-Kawano BBN reaction network to incorporate altered neutrino decoupling temperatures for each flavor, computing the resulting changes in primordial abundance yields of 4He, D, 3He, and 7Li as functions of the transition magnetic moments mu_{e mu}, mu_{e tau}, and mu_{mu tau}.

## Key numbers
- Weak decoupling temperatures: electron neutrinos decouple at approximately 1.27 MeV, muon and tau neutrinos decouple at approximately 1.48 MeV
- Magnetic scattering rate dominates over weak scattering rate starting at a temperature of about 0.4 MeV for a magnetic moment of 10^{-10} Bohr magnetons
- For magnetic moments in the range (10^{-11} to 6.3 x 10^{-10}) Bohr magnetons, the largest found 4He mass fraction is Y_P approximately 0.2465, within the observationally inferred range of 0.2465 plus or minus 0.0097
- Relatively high magnetic moments of approximately 6.3 x 10^{-10} Bohr magnetons can only reduce lithium abundance to approximately 3.94 x 10^{-10}, far from the observationally inferred value of (1.6 plus or minus 0.3) x 10^{-10}
- For small neutrino magnetic moment, the modified WK code gives D/H = 2.61 x 10^{-5}, agreeing with the PArthENoPE value of (2.65 plus or minus 0.07) x 10^{-5}
- The turn-around temperature for muon/tau neutrino decoupling effects on Y_P is approximately 0.4 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutrino magnetic moments in BBN that focused on Dirac neutrinos and right-handed state production, such as Morgan, Fukugita et al., Elmfors et al., and Ayala et al., who obtained limits on Dirac neutrino magnetic moments of order 10^{-11} to 10^{-10} Bohr magnetons. Unlike those works, this paper examines Majorana neutrinos with transition magnetic moments, which do not produce right-handed states. The analysis uses recent Borexino constraints on Majorana transition moments from Canas et al. (2015) and finds that BBN observables are insensitive to moments at that level. This enables future work with more precise observational constraints on primordial abundances and the effective number of relativistic species N_eff to potentially probe Majorana transition moments, and motivates development of codes like BURST that can self-consistently treat flavor-changing currents in BBN.
