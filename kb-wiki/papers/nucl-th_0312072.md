# Shape-Invariance and Many-Body Physics

> Recent developments in the study of shape-invariant Hamiltonians are briefly summarized. Relations between certain exactly solvable problems in many-body physics and shape-invariance are explored. Connection between Gaudin algebras and supersymmetric quantum mechanics is pointed out.

## Key claim

Shape-invariance, a condition from supersymmetric quantum mechanics, can be used to derive exactly solvable many-body Hamiltonians, and there is a direct mapping between Gaudin algebras and factorized supersymmetric Hamiltonians, provided the superpotential satisfies Bethe-ansatz-type conditions.

## Method

The paper first reviews the algebraic formulation of shape-invariance, introducing operators \(\hat{B}_\pm\) and a parameter-shifting operator \(\hat{T}\). It then derives multiple commutators of the Hamiltonian with \(\hat{B}_+\) and shows that for a generalized pairing problem with only one- and two-body terms, the third nested commutator vanishes, imposing constraints on the remainder functions \(R(a_n)\). Finally, it connects this formalism to Gaudin algebras by defining a function \(\Psi(\lambda)\) and requiring its residues to vanish, yielding Bethe-ansatz conditions that link the superpotential \(W(\lambda)\) to the Gaudin algebra generators.

## Key numbers

- The energy eigenvalue for the generalized pairing problem is given by \(n R(a_1) + \frac{1}{2} W n(n-1)\), where \(W = R(a_2) - R(a_1)\).
- The Bethe-ansatz condition for the Gaudin-type problem is \(W(\xi_i) = \sum_{i \neq j} \frac{1}{\xi_i - \xi_j}\).
- The Gaudin Hamiltonian eigenvalue is \(E(\lambda) = W^2(\lambda) + W'(\lambda) + 2 \sum_i \left( \frac{W(\lambda)-W(\xi_i)}{\lambda - \xi_i} \right)\).
- For the \(s\) and \(p\) boson example, as \(\alpha_s \rightarrow \alpha_p\), the operator \(B_+(\lambda)B_-(\lambda)\) reduces to \(\frac{1}{\lambda - \alpha_p} \hat{P}_4\), where \(\hat{P}_4\) is the O(4) pairing operator.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic formulation of shape-invariance from one-dimensional potentials (Gendenshtein, 1983; Cooper et al., 1995) to many-body systems, building on the Calogero-Sutherland model solved by Ghosh (1997). It connects supersymmetric quantum mechanics to Gaudin algebras (Gaudin, 1976, 1983) and Richardson's exact solution of the pairing model (Richardson, 1963), enabling the study of exactly solvable pairing and bosonic systems. The paper suggests that shape-invariant potentials with parameters related by linear-fractional transformations remain an unexplored direction, and that treating Hamiltonian parameters as auxiliary dynamical variables is reminiscent of the path leading to the Interacting Boson Model (Arima and Iachello, 1976).
