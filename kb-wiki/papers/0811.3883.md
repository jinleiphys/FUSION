# A self-confined Fermi-gas model for nuclear collective motion

> The main result of this study consists in working out a microscopic description of collective motion in hot nuclei, allowing to express the parameters of a phenomenological collective model in terms of nucleonic quantities.

## Key claim

The paper derives the phenomenological dynamic equation for nuclear shape parameters from first principles, expressing the inertia \(B\), deformation force \(f\), and friction coefficient \(\gamma\) in terms of nucleonic quantities under the assumption that collective variables change much more slowly than the number and momentum densities from which they are built.

## Method

The author constructs a quasiequilibrium density matrix \(\rho_{\mathrm{q}}\) constrained by expectation values of the Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{H}\), number operator \(\mathcal{N}\), and collective coordinate, momentum, and inertia operators (\(Q\), \(P\), \(\mathcal{M}\)). A canonical transformation of the nucleon field operators \(\psi_{\mathbf{x}}\), \(\psi_{\mathbf{x}}^{\ast}\) is used to incorporate a non-zero mass flow, and the continuity equation together with the adiabaticity hypothesis yields closed-form solutions for the collective variables. The general many-body expressions for \(B\), \(f\), and \(\gamma\) are then adapted to a self-confined Fermi gas with a phenomenological effective force.

## Key numbers

* The paper derives the equation of motion \(B\ddot{q} + \frac{1}{2}\dot{q}^2 \partial_q B = f - \gamma \dot{q}\) for the nuclear shape parameter \(q(T)\), where \(B\), \(f\), and \(\gamma\) are functions of \(q\).
* The inertia parameter from the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach is given as \(B = m^2 \langle q | i[i[\mathcal{H},\widetilde{\phi}],\widetilde{\phi}] | q \rangle = m \int d\mathbf{x} \langle q | n_{\mathbf{x}} | q \rangle (\nabla \widetilde{\phi}_{\mathbf{x}})^2\), with nucleon mass \(m\) and number density operator \(n_{\mathbf{x}}\).
* The mass flow in the quasiequilibrium ensemble is \(\mathbf{j}_{\mathbf{x}} = m \rho_{A,\beta}(\mathbf{x},q) \mathbf{u}_{\mathbf{x}}\), where \(\rho_{A,\beta}(\mathbf{x},q)\) is the number density and \(\mathbf{u}_{\mathbf{x}} = m^{-1} \nabla \chi_{\mathbf{x}}\) is the fluid velocity field.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic interpretation of collective motion in hot nuclei beyond earlier approaches such as the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory (Villars 1977, Brink and Weiguny 1975) and the linear-response-based friction models (Hofmann 1978, Aleshin 1999, 2003, 2005, 2007). The author resolves inconsistencies in prior derivations of the deformation force \(f\) (identified as internal energy by Strutinsky and Tyapin 1970 versus free energy by others) and provides a unified framework that enables critical analysis of previous microscopic models for \(B\), \(f\), and \(\gamma\). The self-confined Fermi gas model constructed here is a natural extension of self-consistent mean-field models for ground-state nuclei (Skyrme 1959, Vautherin and Brink 1972, Gogny 1975) to hot nuclei with collective motion.
