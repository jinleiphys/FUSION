# Algebraic treatment of three-body problems

> We discuss an algebraic treatment of three-body systems in terms of a U(7) spectrum generating algebra. In particular, we develop the formalism for nonlinear configurations and present an algebraic description of vibrational and rotational excitations of symmetric (X_3) and asymmetric tops (XY_2 and XYZ). The relevant point group symmetry is incorporated exactly.

# Algebraic treatment of three-body problems

## Key claim

The paper develops a U(7) spectrum generating algebra for three-body systems that can describe vibrational and rotational excitations of both symmetric (X₃) and asymmetric tops (XY₂ and XYZ), but the formalism is specifically limited to nonlinear configurations where the equilibrium shape has a nonzero hyperradius (R > 0) and, for asymmetric tops, a nonzero relative angle between the Jacobi vectors (γ ≠ 0, π).

## Method

The authors treat the six degrees of freedom of two relative Jacobi vectors by introducing dipole bosons (p†_ρ, p†_λ) for each relative coordinate plus an auxiliary scalar boson (s†), all under a conserved total boson number N, generating a U(7) algebra. They construct intrinsic and collective Hamiltonians using coherent (condensate) states to obtain energy surfaces, then perform a mean-field (Bogoliubov) analysis to extract normal modes and rotational excitations for each configuration, incorporating point group symmetries (D₃ for X₃, C₂ᵥ for XY₂, Cₛ for XYZ) exactly through permutation operators P(12) and P(123).

## Key numbers

- The model space is spanned by the symmetric irreducible representation [N] of U(7), containing harmonic oscillator shells with n = n_ρ + n_λ = 0, 1, …, N.
- For nonlinear X₃ configurations, the equilibrium shape is characterized by β = π/4 and γ = π/2 (equilateral triangle with orthogonal Jacobi coordinates of equal length).
- For nonlinear XY₂ configurations, the equilibrium shape has γ = π/2 (perpendicular Jacobi coordinates).
- The intrinsic Hamiltonian for X₃ yields a symmetric stretching mode u and a degenerate doublet (ε_v = ε_w) of antisymmetric stretching and bending modes.
- The mean-field intrinsic Hamiltonian for XYZ configurations contains six coupling coefficients (ε_u, ε_v, ε_w, ε_uv, ε_uw, ε_vw) expressed in terms of N, R, β, γ, and parameters A–F.

## Context

This work extends the U(7) algebraic approach previously introduced for three identical objects (X₃ configurations) in Bijker, Leviatan (1995) and Bijker, Dieperink, Leviatan (1994) to the general case of asymmetric tops (XY₂ and XYZ). The formalism provides a unified algebraic description of vibrational and rotational excitations applicable to molecular physics (X₃, XY₂, XYZ molecules) and hadronic physics (qqq baryons), enabling systematic classification of states using point group symmetries. The normal mode analysis for XY₂ configurations is the algebraic analogue of the orthogonal transformation of Delves coordinates to normal coordinates in the small displacement limit as described by Bowman (1985).
