# Estimating the relevance of predictions from nuclear mean-field models

> This contribution reviews the present status of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach as one of the leading self-consistent mean-field models in the physics of atomic nuclei. It starts with a brief summary of the formalism and strategy for proper calibration of the SHF functional. The main emphasis lies on an exploration of the reliability of predictions, particularly in the regime of extrapolations. Various strategies are discussed to explore the statistical and systematic errors of SHF. The strategies are illustrated on examples from actual applications. Variations of model and fit data are used to get an idea about systematic errors. The statistical error is evaluated in straightforward manner by statistical analysis based on $χ^2$ fits. This also allows also to evaluate the correlations (covariances) between observables which provides useful insights into the structure of the model and of the fitting strategy.

## Key claim

This review argues that the predictive power of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) models can be estimated through a combination of statistical error analysis (based on chi-squared fits) and systematic error exploration (via variations of model and fit data), but emphasizes that these uncertainty estimates are themselves model-dependent and require careful interpretation, particularly for extrapolations beyond the pool of fit data.

## Method

The paper reviews the SHF energy-density functional formalism, including its composition from kinetic, Skyrme, Coulomb, pairing, and correlation energy terms, and describes the calibration strategy using least-squares (chi-squared) fits to nuclear ground-state properties. It then discusses methods for evaluating statistical uncertainties through covariance analysis of the fit parameters, and systematic uncertainties through variations of the functional form and the set of fit data, illustrating these approaches with applications to nuclear matter properties and finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter: rho_eq, defined at the point where d/d(rho_0)(E/A) = 0
- Pairing functional parameter rho_{0,pair} approximately 0.2 fm^{-3} when treated as a free parameter (Kluepfel 2009)
- Coulomb constant e^2 = 1.44 MeV fm
- Typical cutoff for pairing space: epsilon_cut = 5 MeV with Delta_epsilon = epsilon_cut/10
- Surface pairing concentrates pairing at density rho_{0,pair} = 0.16 fm^{-3} (near matter equilibrium density)

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of self-consistent mean-field models dating to the early 1970s, following the Skyrme force proposal by Skyrme (1959) and the first high-quality parametrizations by Vautherin and Brink (1972). It contrasts with electronic density functional theory (Hohenberg 1964, Kohn 1965) where functionals derive from first-principles calculations, while nuclear functionals rely on empirical calibration. The review enables systematic uncertainty quantification for SHF predictions, which is transferable to competing models such as the Gogny force (Gogny 1970, Decharge 1980) and relativistic mean-field models (Serot 1986, Reinhard 1989).
