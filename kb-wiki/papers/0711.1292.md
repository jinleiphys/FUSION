# Microscopic Derivation of Collective Hamiltonian by Means of the Adiabatic Self-Consistent Collective Coordinate Method

> Microscopic dynamics of the oblate-prolate shape coexistence/mixing phenomena in 68Se and 72Kr are studied by means of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method in conjunction with the pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) Hamiltonian including the quadrupole pairing interaction. Quantum collective Hamiltonian is constructed, and excitation spectra, spectroscopic quadrupole moments and quadrupole transition properties are evaluated. The effect of the time-odd pair field on the collective mass (inertia function) of the large-amplitude vibration and the rotational moments of inertia about three principal axes is evaluated. Basic properties of the shape coexistence/mixing are well reproduced. The calculation indicates that the oblate-prolate shape mixing decreases as the angular momentum increases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, with a gauge-invariant formulation that properly accounts for particle number fluctuation degrees of freedom, can successfully describe oblate-prolate shape coexistence and mixing in 68Se and 72Kr, and that the time-odd pair field increases the collective mass and affects the rotational moments of inertia, though the calculation indicates that oblate-prolate shape mixing decreases as angular momentum increases.

## Method

The authors applied the ASCC method in conjunction with a pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) Hamiltonian including quadrupole pairing interaction to construct a quantum collective Hamiltonian describing coupled large-amplitude vibration and three-dimensional rotation of triaxially deformed nuclei. They solved the moving-frame Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation and moving-frame quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) equations iteratively along a collective path connecting oblate and prolate minima, and extended the Thouless-Valatin equations for rotational moments of inertia to non-equilibrium HFB states along the collective path.

## Key numbers

- For 68Se and 72Kr, the collective path connecting oblate and prolate minima runs approximately along the valley of the potential energy surface lying in the triaxial deformed region, with the step size δq set to 0.0157 in the calculation
- The mixing parameter ε for constructing the initial trial generator from the two lowest moving-frame QRPA solutions was set to 0.1
- The model space consists of two major oscillator shells (lower shell quantum number NL and upper shell NL+1), with a reduction factor ζ = (NL+3/2)/(NL+5/2) applied to quadrupole matrix elements of the upper shell
- For N=Z nuclei like 68Se and 72Kr, the factors α_n^2 = (2N/A)^(2/3) and α_p^2 = (2Z/A)^(2/3) are unity

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent collective coordinate (SCC) method developed by Marumori (1980) and Matsuo (1986) by using the adiabatic approximation to treat large-amplitude collective motions non-perturbatively in the collective coordinate while expanding only in the collective momentum. It builds on previous applications of the ASCC method to oblate-prolate shape coexistence in 68Se and 72Kr by Kobayasi (2004) that did not include requantization or evaluation of excitation spectra and transition probabilities. The results enable future detailed comparison with experimental data and with other theoretical approaches such as large-scale shell model calculations by Kaneko (2004), shell model Monte Carlo by Langanke (2003), configuration mixing calculations by Bender (2006), and Excited Vampir calculations by Petrovici (2002, 1996, 2000).
