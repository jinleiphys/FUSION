# Random Matrix Theory with U(N) Racah Algebra for Transition Strengths

> For finite quantum many-particle systems, a given system, induced by a transition operator, makes transitions from its states to the states of the same system or to those of another system. Examples are electromagnetic transitions (then the initial and final systems are same), nuclear beta and double beta decay (then the initial and final systems are different), particle addition to or removal from a given system and so on. Working towards developing a complete statistical theory for transition strength densities (transition strengths multiplied by the density of states at the initial and final energies), we have started a program to derive formulas for the lower order bivariate moments of the strength densities generated by a variety of transition operators. In this paper results are presented for a transition operator that removes $k_0$ number of particle by considering $m$ spinless fermions in $N$ single particle states. The Hamiltonian that is $k$-body is represented by EGUE($k$) [embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble of $k$-body interactions] and similarly the transition operator by an appropriate independent EGUE. Employing the embedding $U(N)$ algebra, finite-$N$ formulas for moments up to order four are derived and they show that in general the smoothed (with respect to energy) bivariate transition strength densities take bivariate Gaussian form. Extension of these results to particle addition operator and beta decay type operators are discussed.

## Key claim

For a system of \(m\) spinless fermions in \(N\) single-particle states with a \(k\)-body Hamiltonian represented by EGUE(\(k\)) and a particle-removal transition operator of rank \(k_0\) represented by an independent EGUE, the smoothed bivariate transition strength density is close to a bivariate Gaussian form, as indicated by fourth-order cumulants with magnitude \(|k_{PQ}| \lesssim 0.3\) for typical parameter values; the asymptotic limit (\(N \to \infty\), \(k, k_0 \ll m\)) confirms that cumulants vanish as \(-k^2/m\) and the correlation coefficient approaches \(1 - (k k_0)/(2m)\).

## Method

The author derives finite-\(N\) formulas for the bivariate moments \(M_{PQ} = \overline{\langle \mathcal{O}^\dagger H^Q \mathcal{O} H^P \rangle^m}\) up to order \(P+Q=4\) for a particle-removal operator \(\mathcal{O}\) that removes \(k_0\) fermions, using the \(U(N)\) Wigner-Racah algebra and the tensorial decomposition of the Hamiltonian \(H(k)\) into irreducible parts \(B^{\nu,\omega_\nu}(k)\). The ensemble averages are computed by applying the Wigner-Eckart theorem, properties of \(U(N)\) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, and Racah \(U\)-coefficients, with the Hamiltonian and transition operator represented by independent embedded Gaussian unitary ensembles (EGUE). Asymptotic formulas for the cumulants \(k_{PQ}\) are obtained in the limit \(N \to \infty\) with \(m\), \(k\), \(k_0\) fixed and \(k, k_0 \ll m\), and extensions to particle-addition and beta-decay-type operators are discussed.

## Context

This work extends the program for statistical theory of transition strength densities in finite many-particle quantum systems, following results for particle-number-conserving transition operators in Kota (2015, Group 30). It provides the first analytical formulas for bivariate moments of strength densities generated by particle-removal operators within the embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble (EGUE) framework, using the \(U(N)\) Racah algebra developed by Kota (2005). The results enable future studies of beta-decay and double-beta-decay strength distributions (with \(k_0=1\) and \(k_0=2\)) and apply to nuclear physics experiments involving one- and two-nucleon removal reactions, as discussed in Schiffer (2012) and Fröhlich (2007).
