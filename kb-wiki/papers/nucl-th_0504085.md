# Generalized mass formula for non-strange and hyper nuclei with SU(6) symmetry breaking

> A simultaneous description of non-strange nuclei and hypernuclei is provided by a single mass formula inspired by the spin-flavour SU(6) symmetry breaking. This formula is used to estimate the hyperon binding energies of Lambda, double Lambda, Sigma, Cascade and Theta hypernuclei. The results are found to be in good agreement with the available experimental data on 'bound' nuclei and relativistic as well as quark mean field calculations. This mass formula is useful to estimate binding energies over a wide range of masses including the light mass nuclei. It is not applicable for repulsive potential.

## Key claim

The authors propose a single generalized mass formula (BWMH) that simultaneously describes binding energies of non-strange nuclei and hypernuclei (Lambda, double Lambda, Sigma, Cascade, and Theta) by incorporating hyperon mass and strangeness-dependent SU(6) symmetry breaking terms, but the formula is explicitly not applicable for repulsive potentials and therefore cannot describe heavier Sigma hypernuclei where the Sigma-nucleus potential is known to be repulsive.

## Method

The authors extended the modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula (BWM) by adding a hyperon term containing the hyperon mass, a constant, and a strangeness-dependent term proportional to |S|/A^(2/3). The three parameters c0, c1, and c2 in the hyperon term were determined by empirical fits to experimental hyperon separation energies of 35 Lambda, 3 double-Lambda, and 6 Cascade hypernuclei, yielding final values c0=0.0335, c1=26.7, and c2=48.7 with an overall root-mean-square deviation of approximately 1.4 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Volume coefficient a_v = 15.777 MeV, surface coefficient a_s = 18.34 MeV, Coulomb coefficient a_c = 0.71 MeV, symmetry coefficient a_sym = 23.21 MeV, and k = 17 in the base BWM formula for non-strange nuclei
- Pairing term delta_new uses c = 30, with delta = +/- 12 A^(-1/2) for even-even or odd-odd nuclei, and 0 for odd-A nuclei
- Final hyperon term parameters: c0 = 0.0335, c1 = 26.7, c2 = 48.7, with overall r.m.s. deviation of about 1.4 MeV for the fit to all hypernuclei data
- BWMH predicts S_Lambda for ^10_Lambda Li = 11.4 MeV, consistent with the experimental range of 10-12 MeV
- BWMH predicts Sigma^0 binding energy in ^4_Sigma^0 He = 2.69 MeV and Sigma^+ binding energy in ^4_Sigma^+ He = 1.6 MeV, compared to experimental values of 2.8 +/- 0.7 MeV to 4.4 +/- 0.3 +/- 1 MeV
- BWMH predicts Theta^+ separation energy for ^10_Theta+ Li = 26.4 MeV and for ^11_Theta+ Be = 25.1 MeV
- BWMH predicts Theta^+ + ^12 C binding energy = 23.24 MeV, compared to Lambda + ^12 C binding energy = 12.65 MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility obtained from the volume coefficient is about 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula (BWM) previously developed by Samanta and Adhikari (2002, 2004) for non-strange nuclei to include hypernuclei, building upon earlier separate mass formulas for Lambda and Cascade hypernuclei by Dover and Gal (1993) and the SU(6)-inspired formula by Levai, Cseh, Van Isacker and Juillet (1998). Unlike the Dover-Gal formula, BWMH preserves normal nuclear matter properties and reproduces the line of stability; unlike the Levai formula, it avoids divergence of binding energy per nucleon at large A and reproduces experimental neutron separation energy oscillations. The formula enables estimation of binding energies for a wide range of hypernuclei including Theta hypernuclei, providing guidance for experimental searches, and is suitable for extension to astrophysical problems such as neutron star equations of state.
