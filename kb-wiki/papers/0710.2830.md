# Precision extraction of a_nn from pi- d --> n n gamma using chiral perturbation theory

> The neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn provides a sensitive probe of charge-symmetry breaking in the strong interaction. Here we summarize our recent efforts to use chiral perturbation theory in order to systematically relate a_nn to the shape of the neutron spectrum in the reaction pi- d --> n n gamma. In particular we show how the chiral symmetry of QCD relates this process to low-energy electroweak reactions such as p p --> d e+ nu_e. This allows us to reduce the uncertainty in the extracted a_nn (mainly due to short-distance physics in the two-nucleon system) by a factor of more than three, to <0.05 fm. We also report first results on the impact that two-nucleon mechanisms of chiral order P^4 have on the pi- d --> n n gamma neutron spectrum.

## Key claim

By using chiral perturbation theory to relate the short-distance physics in the reaction π⁻ d → nn γ to that in the well-constrained solar pp fusion process (pp → d e⁺ ν_e), the theoretical uncertainty in the extracted neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn can be reduced to <0.05 fm, but only if the fit is restricted to the final-state-interaction (FSI) peak region of the neutron spectrum.

## Method

The authors performed a chiral perturbation theory (χPT) calculation of the π⁻ d → nn γ reaction, including one-body amplitudes up to O(P³) and two-body amplitudes up to O(P⁴) (N3LO). They used chiral NN wave functions derived from one- and two-pion exchange potentials with a square-well regulator at short distances (cutoff R between 1–2 fm) to parameterize unknown short-distance physics. The unknown low-energy constant d̂ appearing in the two-body current was constrained by requiring that the calculation reproduce the accurately known rate of pp → d e⁺ ν_e (solar pp fusion), which is related to π⁻ d → nn γ via chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical uncertainty in a_nn extracted from the full neutron spectrum at N3LO is ±0.3 fm.
- The theoretical uncertainty in a_nn extracted from only the FSI peak region at N3LO is ±0.05 fm, a reduction by at least a factor of three compared to previous calculations.
- The accepted value of a_nn from prior experiments is −18.59 ± 0.4 fm, with previous theoretical uncertainty of approximately ±0.3 fm.
- The electromagnetic correction to the nn scattering length from the magnetic-moment interaction is approximately −0.3 fm.
- The dimensionless parameters governing charge-symmetry breaking are (m_d − m_u)/Λ and α_em/π, both less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of π⁻ d → nn γ by Gari, Gmitro, and Sotona (GGS) and by de Teramond, which had theoretical uncertainties of approximately ±0.3 fm dominated by short-distance NN physics. The chiral perturbation theory approach provides a systematic connection between this reaction and low-energy electroweak processes such as pp → d e⁺ ν_e (solar pp fusion), as previously explored by Park et al. (2003) for tritium beta decay. This enables a much more precise extraction of a_nn, which is a sensitive probe of charge-symmetry breaking in the strong interaction, and the framework can be extended to other reactions such as μ⁻ d → nn ν_μ (to be measured at PSI) and neutrino-deuteron breakup relevant for the SNO detector.
