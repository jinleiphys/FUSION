# The maximum mass of dark matter existing in compact stars based on the self-interacting fermionic model

> By assuming that only gravitation acts between dark matter (DM) and normal matter (NM), we studied DM admixed neutron stars (DANSs) using the two-fluid TOV equations. The NM and DM of compact stars are simulated by the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory and non-self-annihilating self-interacting fermionic model, respectively. The effects of the particle mass of fermionic DM $m_f$ and the interaction strength parameter $y$ on the properties of DANSs are investigated in detail. $m_f$ and $y$ are considered as the free parameters due to the lack of information about the particle nature of DM so far. For a DANS, we suggest a simple universal relationship $M_D^{\max}=(0.267 y +0.627-3.21\frac{M_N}{\M_{\odot}})( \frac{1\GeV}{m_f})^2 \M_{\odot}$ for $y>100$, where $M_D^{\max}$ is the maximum mass of DM existing in DANSs and $M_N$ is the mass of the neutron star without DM. For free fermion DM model ($y$=0), the relationship becomes $ M_D^{\max}=(0.627-0.027\frac{M_N^2}{\M_{\odot}^2}) ( \frac{1\GeV}{m_f})^2 \M_{\odot}$. The radius of DM $R_D$ shows a linear relationship with $M_D^{\max}$ in DANSs, namely $R_D=(7.02 \frac{M_D^{\max}}{ \M_{\odot}}+1.36)$~km. These conclusions are independent of the different NM EOSs from RMF theory. Such a kind of universal relationship connecting the nature of DM particle and mass of stars might shed light on the constraining the nature of the DM by indirect method.

# Wiki Page: Maximum Mass of Dark Matter in Compact Stars (Self-Interacting Fermionic Model)

## Key claim

The paper proposes universal relationships connecting the maximum mass of dark matter that can exist inside a neutron star to the dark matter particle mass and interaction strength, but only under the assumption that dark matter and normal matter interact solely through gravity and that the dark matter is non-self-annihilating. The relationships are claimed to be independent of the nuclear matter equation of state, but they are derived for a specific range of interaction strengths (y > 100 for the main formula) and are explicitly nonlinear for weaker interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for compact stars containing both normal matter and dark matter, where the two fluids couple only through gravity. Normal matter is described by the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory using four different parameter sets (DDLN, DDME1, DDME2, PKDD), while dark matter is modeled as a non-self-annihilating self-interacting fermionic gas with a dimensionless interaction strength parameter y and fermion mass m_f. The maximum mass of dark matter in the admixed star is computed by varying the central energy density of dark matter while keeping the central energy density of normal matter fixed at values from 100 to 1000 MeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers

- For pure dark matter stars, the maximum mass follows M_D^max = (0.269 y + 0.627) (1 GeV / m_f)^2 M_sun, consistent with Narain (2006).
- For dark matter admixed neutron stars with y > 100: M_D^max = (0.267 y + 0.627 - 3.21 M_N / M_sun) (1 GeV / m_f)^2 M_sun, where M_N is the neutron star mass without dark matter.
- For the free fermion dark matter model (y = 0): M_D^max = (0.627 - 0.027 M_N^2 / M_sun^2) (1 GeV / m_f)^2 M_sun.
- The radius of the dark matter component relates linearly to its maximum mass: R_D = (7.02 M_D^max / M_sun + 1.36) km.
- The neutron star without dark matter has masses of 0.188, 1.957, and 2.390 M_sun for central energy densities of 100, 500, and 1000 MeV/fm^3 respectively (using DDME2 equation of state).
- For m_f = 1 GeV and y = 50, the dark matter mass can reach 9.0 M_sun, with total star mass up to 9.5 M_sun.

## Context

This work extends the pure dark matter star relationship derived by Narain (2006) to the case of dark matter admixed neutron stars, where both normal and dark matter coexist. It builds on prior two-fluid TOV studies by Leung (2011), Li (2012), Xiang (2014), and Tolos (2015), who explored compact stars with non-self-annihilating fermionic dark matter. The universal relationships found here are independent of the nuclear matter equation of state, which could enable indirect constraints on dark matter particle properties through astronomical observations of neutron star masses and radii. The paper notes that for y < 100 the relationship becomes nonlinear, and for y < 1 the pure dark matter star relationship is also nonlinear as previously noted by Narain (2006).
