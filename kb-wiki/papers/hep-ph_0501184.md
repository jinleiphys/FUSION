# Supernova neutrinos can tell us the neutrino mass hierarchy independently of flux models

> We demonstrate that the detection of shock modulations of the neutrino spectra from a galactic core-collapse supernova is sufficient to obtain a high significance determination of the neutrino mass hierarchy if the supernova event is observed in both a Mton-class water Cherenkov detector and a 100 kton-class liquid argon detector. Neither detailed supernova neutrino flux modelling nor observation of Earth matter effects is needed for this determination. As a corollary, a nonzero value of θ_x will be established.

# Supernova neutrinos can tell us the neutrino mass hierarchy independently of flux models

## Key claim

The detection of shock-induced modulations in the neutrino spectra from a galactic core-collapse supernova, observed in both a Mton-class water Cherenkov detector and a 100 kton-class liquid argon detector, is sufficient to determine the neutrino mass hierarchy at high significance without requiring detailed supernova neutrino flux modeling or observation of Earth matter effects, provided that tan²θ_x ≫ 10⁻⁵.

## Method

The authors simulated neutrino spectra from a galactic supernova at 10 kpc using a parameterized time-dependent shock density profile from Fuller et al. (1992) and the analytic hopping probability formalism of Fogli et al. (2003). They generated 10,000 Monte Carlo realizations of ν_e spectra in a 100 kton liquid argon detector (via ν_e + ⁴⁰Ar absorption) and \bar{ν}_e spectra in a 1 Mton water Cherenkov detector (via inverse β-decay), randomly sampling supernova model parameters over wide ranges of average energies and luminosities. The mass hierarchy was inferred from the ratio R of mean event energy in the late time bin (4-10 seconds) to the early time bin (0-4 seconds) in each detector channel.

## Key numbers

- For the normal hierarchy with tan²θ_x = 0.01, 88% of simulated spectra have R(ν_e,Ar) < 0.98, and the normal hierarchy is identified with 99.67% probability at that threshold.
- For the inverted hierarchy with tan²θ_x = 0.01, 99.5% of simulated spectra have R(\bar{ν}_e,H₂O) < 0.99, indicating the inverted hierarchy at 99.99% confidence level.
- The Garching model (with \bar{ν}_e and ν_x having identical average energies of 15 MeV and ν_e at 12 MeV) still allows 87% of normal-hierarchy spectra to have R(ν_e,Ar) < 0.98, with 99% confidence.
- The supernova is assumed at distance 10 kpc with binding energy 3 × 10⁵³ ergs, yielding O(10⁵) events in liquid argon and O(10⁶) events in water Cherenkov.
- The analysis covers initial average energies: 14 ≤ ⟨E_{\bar{ν}_e}⁰⟩ ≤ 22 MeV, ⟨E_{ν_e}⁰⟩ = (0.5-0.9)⟨E_{\bar{ν}_e}⁰⟩, ⟨E_{ν_x}⁰⟩ = (1.0-1.6)⟨E_{\bar{ν}_e}⁰⟩, with luminosity ratios 0.5 ≤ Φ_{ν_e}⁰/Φ_{ν_x}⁰ ≤ 2.0 and 0.5 ≤ Φ_{\bar{ν}_e}⁰/Φ_{ν_x}⁰ ≤ 1.6.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of supernova neutrino oscillations with static density profiles by Dighe et al. (1999) and others, which found that mass hierarchy determination depended sensitively on the uncertain ratio ⟨E_{ν_x}⟩/⟨E_{\bar{ν}_e}⟩. The key advance is exploiting the shock-induced dip in the time evolution of average neutrino energy, first noted by Fuller et al. (1992) and later studied by Tomas et al. (2004), to determine the hierarchy independently of flux model assumptions. The method enables a definitive test of the mass hierarchy from a single galactic supernova event, and as a corollary establishes that tan²θ_x ≫ 10⁻⁵ (i.e., a nonzero θ₁₃), complementing long-baseline oscillation experiments that suffer from eight-fold parameter degeneracies involving the CP phase δ.
