# Phenomenological Schwinger-Dyson approach to eta-eta' mass matrix

> It is reviewed pedagogically how a very successful description of the eta-eta' mass matrix can be achieved in the consistently coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach in spite of the limitations of the ladder approximation. This description is in agreement with both phenomenology and lattice results.

## Key claim

The consistently coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter (SD-BS) approach, despite the limitations of the ladder approximation, can provide a surprisingly satisfactory description of the eta-eta' mass matrix when the U_A(1) anomaly effect is parameterized by a single additional parameter beta, which can alternatively be fixed from lattice results on topological susceptibility.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for dressed light quark propagators (u, d, s) using a phenomenologically successful interaction, then employ these propagators in consistent Bethe-Salpeter equations for quark-antiquark bound states, using the same ladder approximation and effective dressed gluon exchange in both equations. The non-anomalous part of the squared-mass matrix is constructed from the calculated masses of the u-ubar, d-dbar, and s-sbar pseudoscalar bound states, and the U_A(1) anomaly is incorporated by adding an anomalous contribution that shifts only the singlet eta_0 mass by an amount 3 beta, with a flavor-symmetry-breaking factor X allowing for different transition amplitudes between nonstrange and strange sectors. The resulting 2x2 mass matrix in the nonstrange-strange (NS-S) basis is then diagonalized to obtain the physical eta and eta' masses and the mixing angle.

## Key numbers

* Model-calculated u-ubar mass M_{u-ubar} = 0.1373 GeV (fitted to empirical pion mass)
* Model-calculated s-sbar mass M_{s-sbar} = 0.7007 GeV
* Flavor-symmetry-breaking factor X = f_pi / f_{s-sbar} = 0.689 (from model calculation)
* Anomalous mass shift parameter beta = 0.286 GeV^2 (determined from trace of empirical mass matrix)
* Calculated eta_NS mass = 769 MeV (0.592 GeV^2)
* Calculated eta_S mass = 792 MeV (0.627 GeV^2)
* NS-S mixing angle phi = 43.2 degrees (equivalent to octet-singlet mixing angle theta = -11.5 degrees)
* Model prediction for eta mass: M_eta = 575 MeV (empirical: 547 MeV)
* Model prediction for eta' mass: M_eta' = 943 MeV (empirical: 958 MeV)
* Topological susceptibility from model: chi = (178 MeV)^4, in excellent agreement with lattice result (175 +/- 5 MeV)^4 from Alles et al. (1996)

## Context

This work extends the SD-BS approach previously applied to light pseudoscalar mesons by Kekez, Klabucar, and collaborators (1996-2001) to the specific problem of eta-eta' mixing, showing that the approach reproduces both the Gell-Mann-Okubo relation and the Witten-Veneziano formula. The results agree well with the two-mixing-angle scheme reviewed by Feldmann (1999), with lattice results from the UKQCD collaboration (McNeile, 2000), and with the authors' own calculations of eta, eta' to gamma-gamma processes. The approach enables future work where the anomalous parameter beta can be eliminated entirely by using lattice results for topological susceptibility, making the description fully predictive.
