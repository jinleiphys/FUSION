# Method of multiple internal reflections in description of nuclear alpha-decay

> A spatial-time method of description of alpha-decay of a nucleus is presented. The method is based on the quantum mechanical model of the alpha-decay of a compound nuclear system with the barrier. alpha-decay dynamics is described on the basis of multiple internal reflections of wave packets from boundaries of the decay barrier. The method allows to study this process in details at any time moment or point of space. According to the method, some time before the time moment of the nuclear decay the nucleus makes oscilations. In the calculations of the time duration of the alpha-decay of the compound nucleus we propose to take into account the time durations of these oscillations. The method of the multiple internal reflections allows to calculate the time of such oscillations before the alpha-decay and the total alpha-decay time.

# Method of multiple internal reflections in description of nuclear alpha-decay

## Key claim
The paper presents a non-stationary method for describing alpha-decay that incorporates multiple internal reflections of wave packets at the barrier boundaries, revealing that the compound nucleus undergoes oscillations for some time before the actual decay event. The authors propose that these oscillation durations should be included when calculating the total alpha-decay time, rather than treating the decay as a single instantaneous tunneling event.

## Method
The authors generalize a prior one-dimensional approach for particle tunneling through a rectangular barrier (using multiple internal reflections of non-stationary wave packets) to a spherically symmetric three-region potential representing a compound nucleus: an inner well (region I, r < R₁), a barrier region (region II, R₁ < r < R₂), and an external region (region III, r > R₂). They construct incident, transmitted, and reflected wave packets step-by-step at each boundary, derive recurrence relations for the constant factors at each reflection/transmission step, and sum the series to obtain total transmitted and reflected wave functions. Time analysis is performed using the stationary phase method applied to the total transmitted wave packet, yielding the decay time as the energy derivative of the phase of the transmission coefficient C_tr.

## Key numbers
- The barrier potential is defined with three regions: region I at potential -V₀ for r < R₁, region II at potential V₁ for R₁ < r < R₂, and region III at potential 0 for r > R₂ (no numerical values for V₀, V₁, R₁, or R₂ are given in the text).
- The wave numbers are defined as k₁ = (1/ħ)√(2m(E+V₀)) for region I, ξ = (1/ħ)√(2m(V₁-E)) for region II, and k = (1/ħ)√(2mE) for region III (no numerical energies or masses are specified).
- The total alpha-decay time is given by τ_tr = ħ ∂/∂E arg C_tr, where C_tr is the transmission coefficient defined in equation (13) (no numerical values for τ_tr are computed in the paper).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior non-stationary method of multiple internal reflections, previously applied to one-dimensional tunneling problems (Maydanyuk 2000, Maydanyuk 2002) and to elastic scattering of particles upon spherical nuclei (Maydanyuk 2000). It builds on earlier stationary treatments of multiple internal reflections for plane waves above a barrier (McVoy 1967, Anderson 1989, Fermor 1966) and on the time analysis of nuclear processes using the stationary phase method (Olkhovsky 1992, Olkhovsky 1997, Maydanyuk 1999). The method enables detailed space-time analysis of alpha-decay dynamics at any point in space or time, and opens the possibility of calculating oscillation times of the compound nucleus before decay, which the authors propose should be included in future decay-time calculations.
