# Moments of inertia of rare-earth nuclei and the nuclear time-odd mean fields within exact solutions of the adiabatic theory

> We systematically analyse the nuclear moments of inertia determined within the Skyrme and Gogny density functional theories. The time-odd mean fields generated by collective rotation are self-consistently determined by a novel exact iterative solution of the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) equations. Although details of the results depend on the functional used, the calculated moments of inertia are in good overall agreement with the experimental data, with no adjustable parameters. To show the essential importance of the time-odd mean fields, we compared the ATDHFB moments of inertia with those obtained from the Inglis-Belyaev formula. For Skyrme density functionals, we find strong correlations between the effective mass and the impact of the time-odd mean fields on the rotational and vibrational collective inertia.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the time-odd mean fields, self-consistently determined by an exact iterative solution of the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) equations, significantly affect nuclear moments of inertia, but the magnitude of this effect depends strongly on the chosen density functional, neutron and proton numbers, and deformation, so no single enhancement factor can replace the full ATDHFB calculation.

## Method

The authors developed a novel iterative method to solve the ATDHFB equations exactly without inverting the two-body stability matrix, transforming the problem into a fixed-point equation solved in the quasiparticle basis. They implemented this method within the non-relativistic density functional solver HFODD using a three-dimensional Cartesian harmonic-oscillator basis, and performed systematic calculations for even-even rare-earth nuclei (Gd, Dy, Er, Yb, Hf, W, Os isotopes with neutron numbers 82-126) using two Skyrme functionals (SkM* and UNEDF1) and one Gogny functional (D1S). The ATDHFB moments of inertia were compared with those from the Inglis-Belyaev (IB) formula and with experimental values extracted from measured first 2+ excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- For 166Er with SkM*: IB moment of inertia = 27.584 hbar^2/MeV, ATDHFB moment of inertia = 34.950 hbar^2/MeV, quadrupole deformation beta_20 = 0.320
- For 166Er with UNEDF1: IB moment of inertia = 36.401 hbar^2/MeV, ATDHFB moment of inertia = 41.342 hbar^2/MeV, quadrupole deformation beta_20 = 0.324
- For 166Er with D1S: IB moment of inertia = 27.865 hbar^2/MeV, ATDHFB moment of inertia = 41.147 hbar^2/MeV, quadrupole deformation beta_20 = 0.322
- For SkM*: mean residual between IB moment of inertia and experiment = -2.58 hbar^2/MeV (underestimation), mean residual for ATDHFB = +1.89 hbar^2/MeV (overestimation), with standard deviations of 4.06 hbar^2/MeV (IB) and 3.62 hbar^2/MeV (ATDHFB)
- For SkM*, ATDHFB/IB ratios mostly lie between 1.2 and 1.3, with exceptions in 186-192Yb, 186Gd, and 188Dy
- For D1S in osmium isotopes with neutron numbers 84-94, ATDHFB/IB ratios reach 1.5 or 1.6
- For seven Skyrme functionals in 166Er, the ratio ATDHFB/IB for rotational inertia correlates with isoscalar effective mass with slope -0.582 and R^2 = 0.9027; for vibrational inertia, slope -0.323 and R^2 = 0.9352

## Context

This work extends the ATDHFB method beyond the cranking (Inglis-Belyaev) approximation, which neglects time-odd mean fields and has been known since early studies to underestimate moments of inertia, requiring ad hoc enhancement factors of about 1.3 (as used in Giuliani (2018), Vretenar (2005), and Ryssens (2022)). The iterative method avoids the prohibitive computational cost of inverting the two-body stability matrix that limited earlier ATDHFB applications. The results confirm the findings of Petrik (2018) using the finite-amplitude method that large and mass-dependent deviations from the cranking approximation exist, and show that no single enhancement factor is justified. The strong correlation between effective mass and the ATDHFB/IB ratio for Skyrme functionals suggests that including rotational moments of inertia in future functional adjustments could improve predictions of nuclear collective dynamics.
