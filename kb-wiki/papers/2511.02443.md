# General relativistic study of $f$-mode oscillations in neutron stars with gravitationally bound dark matter

> A comprehensive investigation of nonradial oscillations in neutron star (NS) admixed with gravitationally bounded dark matter (DM) is carried out within the framework of full general relativity. The relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism is employed to illustrate the hadronic equation of state (EOS), while a physically motivated, gravitationally captured, non-uniform fermionic Higgs-portal DM component is incorporated to model DM-admixed NS. The DM distribution is characterized by two free parameters: $αM_χ$, an effective scaling factor that combines the DM concentration and the DM candidate mass, and $β$, a steepness index controlling the DM density distribution. The quasi normal mode (QNM) characteristics such as fundamental ($f$) mode frequency and its corresponding gravitational-wave (GW) damping time ($τ$) is calculated for DM-admixed NS by solving the general relativistic perturbed equations involving axial as well as polar modes. The study demonstrates how the inclusion of DM distribution modifies the $f$-mode frequency and enhances the damping rate, reflecting a stronger coupling between matter and spacetime perturbations. Considering DM effects, the correlation analysis among DM model parameters, NS observables and QNM characteristics also carried out. Analytic fits for the $f-C-τ$ and $f-Λ-τ$ relations are constructed and calibrated for DM-admixed NS models. Building upon asteroseismic universal relations (URs), multimessenger constraint from the GW170817 event is employed by mapping the tidal deformability $Λ_{1.4}$ into the $(f_{1.4},τ_{1.4})$ space, thereby providing observational bounds on the oscillation properties of canonical DM-admixed NS model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a physically motivated, non-uniform dark matter (DM) distribution in neutron stars (NS) modifies the fundamental ($f$)-mode oscillation frequency and enhances the gravitational-wave damping rate, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of the effective scaling parameter $\alpha M_\chi$ and the steepness index $\beta$ that control the DM density profile; the effect becomes pronounced only beyond a critical value of $\alpha M_\chi$.

## Method

The authors model DM-admixed NS using a single-fluid approximation within the relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism, employing the Hornick4 equation of state (EOS) for hadronic matter and a Higgs-portal fermionic DM component with a non-uniform density distribution described by two free parameters: $\alpha M_\chi$ (effective scaling factor combining DM concentration and candidate mass) and $\beta$ (steepness index). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for hydrostatic equilibrium and then compute the complex quasinormal mode (QNM) frequencies by solving the full general relativistic perturbation equations for axial and polar modes, integrating Zerilli's wave equation to obtain the $f$-mode frequency and damping time $\tau$.

## Key numbers

* For a $1 \, M_\odot$ NS model with $\alpha M_\chi = 0.05$, the $f$-mode frequencies are approximately $1.57$ kHz, $1.60$ kHz, and $1.77$ kHz for $\beta = 1, 2,$ and $4$, respectively.
* For the same $1 \, M_\odot$ NS model with $\alpha M_\chi = 0.05$, the damping times are $0.42$ s, $0.41$ s, and $0.32$ s for $\beta = 1, 2,$ and $4$, respectively.
* The pure hadronic Hornick4 EOS yields a maximum mass of $2.32 \, M_\odot$ and a canonical radius $R_{1.4} \approx 12.85$ km.
* The best-fit coefficients for the $f$-mode frequency scaling relation $f({\rm kHz}) = a_r + b_r\sqrt{M/R^3}$ are $a_r = 0.686$ kHz and $b_r = 40.535$ kHz km.
* The best-fit coefficients for the normalized damping time relation $R^4/(M^3\tau_f) = a_i + b_i (M/R)$ are $a_i = 0.086$ and $b_i = -0.273$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of NS asteroseismology that assumed a uniform DM density distribution, such as those by Panotopoulos and Lopes (2017) and Flores et al. (2024), by adopting the physically motivated non-uniform DM profile of Kumar and Sotani (2025). It also builds on the general relativistic treatment of $f$-mode oscillations by Sotani (2021) and Pradhan and Chatterjee (2022), who showed that the Cowling approximation overestimates $f$-mode frequencies by $10-30\%$. The derived universal relations for DM-admixed NS, combined with the multimessenger constraint from the GW170817 event mapping tidal deformability $\Lambda_{1.4}$ into the $(f_{1.4}, \tau_{1.4})$ space, enable observational bounds on oscillation properties of canonical DM-admixed NS models.
