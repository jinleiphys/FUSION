# Tilted-axis-cranking covariant density functional theory for the high-spin spectroscopy in $^{69}$Ga

> The tilted-axis-cranking covariant density functional theory is applied to investigate the three newly-observed positive-parity bands SI, SII, and SIII in $^{69}$Ga. The energy spectra and angular momenta are calculated and compared with the experimental data. For the yrast band SI, pairing correlations play a crucial role for the states with spin $I\leq 23/2\hbar$. The bands SII and SIII are suggested to be signature partner bands with positive and negative signatures, respectively. The transition probabilities $B(E2)$ for these bands are predicted, and await further experimental verification. By analyzing the angular momentum alignments microscopicly, it is revealed that the $g_{9/2}$ protons and neutrons play an important role in the description of the collective structure of $^{69}$Ga.

## Key claim

The tilted-axis-cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) can describe the three newly-observed positive-parity bands in $^{69}$Ga, but only when pairing correlations are included for states with spin $I \leq 23/2\hbar$ in the yrast band SI; for higher spins and for bands SII and SIII, pairing correlations are negligible.

## Method

The TAC-CDFT was applied using the PC-PK1 relativistic density functional, solving the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional harmonic-oscillator basis with ten major shells. For band SI, pairing correlations were treated with the shell-model-like approach (SLAP) using monopole pairing forces with strengths of 0.7 MeV for neutrons and 0.75 MeV for protons, determined by reproducing experimental odd-even mass differences. Configuration-constrained cranking calculations without pairing were first performed to identify the valence-nucleon configurations for each band.

## Key numbers

- Band SI has a deformation $\beta = 0.41$ and $\gamma = 0.0^\circ$, with configuration $\nu(g_{9/2})^4(pf)^6\otimes \pi(g_{9/2})^1(pf)^2$ and energy $-596.2$ MeV at $\hbar\omega = 0$ MeV.
- Bands SII and SIII have a deformation $\beta = 0.57$ and $\gamma = 0.0^\circ$, with configuration $\nu(g_{9/2})^4(pf)^6\otimes\pi(g_{9/2})^3$ and energy $-595.6$ MeV at $\hbar\omega = 0$ MeV.
- Pairing correlations are crucial for states with spin $I \leq 23/2\hbar$ in band SI; for bands SII and SIII, the band head spins are larger than $10\hbar$, making pairing correlations negligible.
- The effective neutron and proton pairing strengths used in the SLAP are 0.7 MeV and 0.75 MeV, respectively, with the many-particle configuration space truncated at an excitation energy cutoff of $E_c = 10$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of TAC-CDFT, previously used for even-even nuclei in the $A\approx 60-70$ mass region (Zhu 2006, Ljungvall 2010, Hoteling 2010, Crawford 2013, Albers 2016, Sensharma 2022, Ayangeakaa 2022), to odd-mass nuclei, specifically $^{69}$Ga. The three positive-parity bands were recently observed by Idoko (2025) through a $^{26}\text{Mg}(^{48}\text{Ca},p4n\gamma)$ multi-nucleon transfer reaction. The calculations provide predictions for $B(E2)$ transition probabilities that await experimental verification, and reveal that $g_{9/2}$ protons and neutrons are the primary drivers of the collective structure.
