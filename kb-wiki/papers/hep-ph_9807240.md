# Spin-Orbit Force of Instanton-Induced Interaction in Strange and Charmed Systems

> Effects of the spin orbit-force on hadronic systems with strangeness or charm are investigated by a valence quark model with the instanton-induced interaction. By introducing this interaction, the spin-orbit splittings in the negative-parity hyperons becomes 0.14 -- 0.37 times smaller. The flavor-octet baryon mass spectrum and the splittings in the charmed baryons become consistent with the experiments. Though the splitting is also reduced in the flavor-singlet baryons, it still gives two third of the experimental value. The reduction comes from the cancellation between the one-gluon exchange and the instanton-induced interaction, which is channel-specific. In most of the two-baryon channels, the symmetric and antisymmetric spin-orbit force of the YN interaction remains strong after introducing this instanton effect. A few exceptional channels, however, are found where the cancellation affects strongly and the spin-orbit force becomes small.

## Key claim

The instanton-induced interaction (III) produces a channel-specific cancellation with the one-gluon exchange (OGE) spin-orbit force, reducing spin-orbit splittings in negative-parity hyperons to 0.14--0.37 times their OGE-only values, while leaving the spin-orbit force in most two-baryon channels strong enough to remain consistent with experimental data.

## Method

A nonrelativistic valence quark model was employed with a harmonic oscillator (0s)²(0p) orbital wave function for single baryons and a six-quark cluster wave function for two-baryon systems. The Hamiltonian included kinetic, confinement, one-gluon exchange (OGE), and instanton-induced interaction (III) terms; the III strength was set to give 40% of the N-Δ mass difference (p_III = 0.4), consistent with the η-η' mass difference. Matrix elements of the spin-orbit parts of OGE and III were computed for negative-parity hyperons (strangeness 0, -1, -2) and for two-baryon channels with strangeness, using the adiabatic potential at relative distance R=0.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit splittings in negative-parity hyperons become 0.14 to 0.37 times smaller than in the OGE-only model (p_III = 0.4).
- The N-Δ mass difference used to fix the combined OGE+III strength is 293 MeV.
- At p_III = 0.4, the calculated splitting between the octet Λ(1670) and Λ(1690) is 31 MeV (compared to 224 MeV at p_III = 0).
- The flavor-singlet Λ splitting (Λ(1405) and Λ(1520)) is 80 MeV at p_III = 0.4 (experimental value: 115 MeV).
- The S-wave Σ* octet-decuplet mass difference is 176 MeV (experimental: 192 MeV).
- The charmed Λ_c(2594) and Λ_c(2627) splitting is 32.7 MeV experimentally.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on nonstrange P-wave systems (Takeuchi 1994, 1996), where the OGE-III cancellation was first demonstrated, to strange and charmed baryons and hyperon-nucleon interactions. It addresses the long-standing spin-orbit problem in valence quark models: OGE alone gives strong spin-orbit force that fits two-nucleon data but overpredicts baryon resonance splittings, a difficulty noted by Isgur and Karl (1978) and others. The results enable a more fundamental understanding of the weak ΛN spin-orbit force observed in hypernuclei and provide a framework for future calculations coupling three-quark states to meson-baryon channels such as N-anti-K.
