# Goldstone Bosons in a Finite Volume: The Partition Function to Three Loops

> A system of Goldstone bosons - stemming from a symmetry breaking $O(N) \to O(N-1)$ - in a finite volume at finite temperature is considered. In the framework of dimensional regularization, the partition function is calculated to 3 loops for 3 and 4 dimensions, where Polyakov's measure for the functional integration is applied. Although the underlying theory is the non-linear $σ$ model, the 3 loop result turns out to be renormalizable in the sense that all the singularities can be absorbed by the couplings occuring so far. In finite volume, this property is highly non trivial and confirms the method for the measure. We also show that the result coincides with the one obtained using the Faddeev- Popov measure. This is also true for the maximal generalization of Polyakov's measure: none of the additional invariant terms that can be added contributes to the dimensionally regularized system. Our phenomenological Lagrangian describes e.g. 2 flavor chiral QCD as well as the classical Heisenberg model, but there are also points of contact with the Higgs model, superconductors etc. Moreover the finite size corrections to the susceptibility might improve the inerpretation of Monte Carlo results on the lattice.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the partition function for Goldstone bosons from spontaneous symmetry breaking O(N) → O(N-1) in a finite volume at finite temperature is perturbatively renormalizable to three loops in dimensional regularization, despite the underlying non-linear sigma model being non-renormalizable in more than two dimensions; this property is highly non-trivial in finite volume and confirms the Polyakov method for the functional measure.

## Method

The authors consider the non-linear sigma model in d=3 and d=4 dimensions with spontaneous symmetry breaking O(N) → O(N-1), using a finite rectangular box with periodic boundary conditions and a small external magnetic field of order O(V^{-1}). They decompose the field into collective variables (global rotations for zero modes) and fluctuation fields, expand the action in powers of L^{2-d} (where L is the linear box size), and compute the partition function to three loops using dimensional regularization with Polyakov's measure for the path integral.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field magnitude is taken as H = O(V^{-1}), where V is the volume of the box
- The Goldstone boson mass is given by m² = ΣH/F², with mL << 1 (the epsilon-expansion regime)
- The expansion parameter is the dimensionless quantity L^{2-d}/F², and the calculation extends to order (L^{2-d})³
- The calculation is performed for dimensions d=3 and d=4

## Context

This work extends the results of Gasser and Leutwyler (1987, 1988) and Hasenfratz and Leutwyler (1990) by computing the large volume expansion of the partition function to three loops (order (L^{2-d})³), whereas previous work had considered lower orders. The results enable more precise analytical calculations of finite size effects for comparison with lattice Monte Carlo simulations, particularly for the susceptibility, and apply to systems including two-flavor chiral QCD, the classical Heisenberg model, and the Higgs model.
