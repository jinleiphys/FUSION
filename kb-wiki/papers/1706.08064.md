# $ϕ$ meson transparency in nuclei from $ϕN$ resonant interactions

> We investigate the $ϕ$ meson nuclear transparency using some recent theoretical developments on the $ϕ$ in medium self-energy. The inclusion of direct resonant $ϕN$-scattering and the kaon decay mechanisms leads to a $ϕ$ width much larger than in most previous theoretical approaches. The model has been confronted with photoproduction data from CLAS and LEPS and the recent proton induced $ϕ$ production from COSY finding an overall good agreement. The results support the need of a quite large direct $ϕN$-scattering contribution to the self-energy.

# ϕ meson transparency in nuclei from ϕN resonant interactions

## Key claim

The inclusion of direct resonant ϕN-scattering mechanisms (via K*Λ loops) in the ϕ meson self-energy produces a much larger in-medium ϕ width than most previous theoretical approaches, and this improved model reproduces photoproduction transparency data from CLAS and COSY, though the LEPS photoproduction data would require even stronger absorption and the two photoproduction datasets appear hardly reconcilable.

## Method

The authors calculate the ϕ meson self-energy in nuclear matter from two sources: (1) kaon cloud mechanisms from ϕ→K⁺K⁻ decay with dressed kaon/antikaon propagators, and (2) direct ϕN elastic scattering via K*-hyperon loops, evaluated using two different unitarized coupled-channel approaches, the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model and an SU(6) spin-flavor extension of chiral perturbation theory. The nuclear transparency ratio (σ_A / A σ_N) is computed in an eikonal approximation for both photoproduction and proton-induced production, including Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and initial-state proton absorption where relevant.

## Key numbers

- In vacuum, the ϕ meson decays 83% via ϕ→K⁺K⁻, with a free width of 4 MeV.
- At normal nuclear density, the kaon-cloud mechanisms alone give Γ_ϕ ≈ 30 MeV (from Ref. Cabrera (2002)).
- The additional ϕN-scattering mechanisms produce an extra broadening of 40–50 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- The antikaon optical potential shows an attraction of −60 MeV at normal nuclear matter density for antikaons at rest.
- For proton-induced production at COSY/ANKE, the proton kinetic energy is 2.83 GeV; both σ_pn and σ_pp are approximately 42 mb.
- The average ϕ momentum used for LEPS comparison is p_ϕ = 1.8 GeV; for CLAS comparison, p_ϕ = 2.0 GeV; for COSY comparison, p_ϕ = 1.3 GeV.
- The isospin asymmetry correction from neutron-rich nuclei is of order 10% for lead at the COSY energy.

## Context

This work extends the ϕ self-energy model of Cabrera (2016) by testing it against experimental transparency data, improving upon earlier calculations that only included kaon-cloud mechanisms (Cabrera (2002)). The results are compared with photoproduction data from LEPS (Ishikawa (2004)) and CLAS (Wood (2010)), and proton-induced production data from COSY (Hartmann (2012), Polyanskiy (2010)). The study supports the need for large direct ϕN-scattering contributions but highlights discrepancies between the two photoproduction datasets, calling for new, more precise experiments and suggesting that measurements of ϕ nuclear bound states could help constrain both the real and imaginary parts of the ϕ self-energy.
