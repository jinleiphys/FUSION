# Level Statistics of Near-Yrast States in Rapidly Rotating Nuclei

> The nearest neighbour level spacing distribution and the $Δ_3$ statistics of level fluctuations associated with very high spin states ($I \gesim 30$) in rare-earth deformed nuclei are analysed by means of a cranked shell model. The many particle-many hole configurations created in the rotating Nilsson potential are mixed by the surface-delta two-body residual interaction. The levels in the near-yrast region show a Poisson-like level spacing distribution. As the intrinsic excitation energy $U$ increases, the level statistics shows a gradual transition from order to chaos, reaching at $U \gesim 2$ MeV the Wigner distribution typical of the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble of random matrices. This transition is caused by the residual two-body interaction. On the other hand, the level spacings between the yrast and the first excited state show a peculiar behaviour, displaying a Wigner-like distribution instead of the Poisson-like distribution seen for the other near-yrast rotational states. The lowest spacings reflect the properties of the single-particle orbits in the mean-field, and are only weakly affected by the residual two-body interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that near-yrast states in rapidly rotating rare-earth nuclei exhibit a Poisson-like level spacing distribution, but as intrinsic excitation energy increases above approximately 2 MeV, the distribution transitions to the Wigner distribution characteristic of chaotic quantum systems. However, the spacing between the yrast band and the first excited state with the same spin and parity shows a Wigner-like distribution even at the lowest excitation energies, a behavior that originates from the mean-field single-particle level structure rather than from the residual two-body interaction.

## Method

The authors used a cranked Nilsson model without static pairing, generating many particle-many hole (np-nh) configurations in a diabatic single-particle basis for 40 rare-earth nuclei in the A=160-174 region at spins I ≥ 30. They introduced a surface-delta two-body residual interaction (SDI) with strength V₀=27.5/A MeV to mix these configurations, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian for each spin and parity separately using the lowest 1000 basis states to obtain the lowest 300 energy levels. The nearest-neighbor level spacing distribution (NND) and Δ₃ spectral rigidity were calculated after unfolding the spectra using a constant-temperature level density formula, with the NND fitted to a Brody distribution parametrized by the Brody parameter w (w=0 for Poisson, w=1 for Wigner).

## Key numbers

- The Brody parameter for the lowest 5 levels (near-yrast region, U ≤ 0.7 MeV) is w = 0.301 ± 0.012 for spin interval I₀ = 32-50
- For levels 291-300 (U ∼ 2.3 MeV), the Brody parameter reaches w = 0.888 ± 0.012, close to the Wigner limit (w = 1.0)
- The transition to chaos occurs at intrinsic excitation energy U ≥ 2 MeV
- The average level spacing associated with the N=1 yrast rotational band is D ∼ 350 keV
- The average spacing between single-particle hole and particle orbitals with same parity and signature is ⟨D⟩ ∼ 400 keV
- The SDI strength used is V₀ = 27.5/A MeV
- The level density reaches ∼ 10²/MeV at U ∼ 1 MeV above yrast in rare-earth nuclei
- For the Δ₃ statistics at U ∼ 2.3 MeV, the GOE limit holds up to L_max ∼ 6

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Åberg (1990) that used a schematic residual interaction with random-sign matrix elements, finding that the onset of chaos occurred at lower excitation energy (U = 1.5-2.0 MeV) than in the present calculation due to the selectivity of the SDI matrix elements. The results are consistent with experimental analyses by Garrett et al. (1990s) showing Poisson-like distributions for high-spin rotational levels near yrast in deformed nuclei, and with the phenomenon of rotational damping (Lauritzen et al., 1980s) which sets in at U ∼ 0.8 MeV. The study enables future experimental tests of the predicted order-to-chaos transition in rapidly rotating nuclei, particularly for the near-yrast states that may become resolvable in upcoming experiments.
