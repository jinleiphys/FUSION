# Muonic Lithium atoms: nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift

> In view of the future plans to measure the Lamb shift in muonic Lithium atoms we address the microscopic theory of the $μ$-$^6$Li$^{2+}$ and $μ$-$^7$Li$^{2+}$ systems. The goal of the CREMA collaboration is to measure the Lamb shift to extract the charge radius with high precision and compare it to electron scattering data or atomic spectroscopy to see if interesting puzzles, such as the proton and deuteron radius puzzles, arise. For this experiment to be successful, theoretical information on the nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift is needed. For $μ$-$^6$Li$^{2+}$ and $μ$-$^7$Li$^{2+}$ there exist only estimates of nuclear structure corrections based on experimental data that suffer from very large uncertainties. We present the first steps towards an ab initio computation of these quantities using few-body techniques.

## Key claim

The authors present the first steps toward an ab initio computation of nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic Lithium atoms, but the results are preliminary: several terms are still missing, the dipole response functions are only computed up to 100 MeV, and the potential model dependence remains the largest unquantified source of uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the Lamb shift for muonic-6Li2+ and muonic-7Li2+ by separating it into dipole terms (derived from photo-absorption cross sections calculated with the AV4' potential using hyperspherical harmonics expansions) and Zemach terms (obtained from variational Monte Carlo charge density distributions using the AV18+UX interaction). The dipole integrals are evaluated with an energy cut-off at 100 MeV, with truncation errors estimated from the difference between 80 MeV and 100 MeV cut-offs. The Zemach integrals are computed via Monte Carlo statistical simulation to propagate uncertainties from the density data points.

## Key numbers

* For muonic-6Li2+, the dipole term δ(0)D1 = -41.0(2) meV and the Coulomb term δ(0)C = 7.85(3) meV, with uncertainties from the energy cut-off.
* For muonic-7Li2+, the dipole term δ(0)D1 = -51.0(3) meV and the Coulomb term δ(0)C = 9.89(4) meV.
* The Zemach term δ(1)Z1 = 23.47(1) meV for muonic-6Li2+ and 22.03(1) meV for muonic-7Li2+, with statistical uncertainties from Monte Carlo analysis.
* The Zemach term δ(1)Z3 = 165.4(2) meV for muonic-6Li2+ and 126.5(2) meV for muonic-7Li2+.
* The preliminary total TPE correction for muonic-6Li2+ is -11.8 meV with a lower bound uncertainty of 0.3 meV, compared to the earlier estimate of -15 ± 4 meV from Drake et al. (1988).
* For muonic-7Li2+, the preliminary total is -22.2 meV with a lower bound uncertainty of 0.4 meV, compared to the earlier estimate of -21 ± 4 meV from Drake et al. (1988).

## Context

This work extends the program of ab initio few-body calculations of nuclear structure corrections that previously succeeded for muonic deuterium, muonic-3He+, and muonic-4He+, where calculations using realistic nuclear potentials reduced uncertainties by factors of 2-3 compared to earlier estimates based on experimental data. The CREMA collaboration plans to measure the Lamb shift in muonic Lithium atoms to extract charge radii and test whether puzzles similar to the proton radius puzzle (Pohl et al., 2010; Antognini et al., 2013) and deuteron radius puzzle (Pohl et al., 2016) appear in systems with more nucleons. The present results are consistent with the earlier estimates of Drake et al. (1988) but with smaller numerical uncertainties, though a complete calculation including monopole, quadrupole, magnetic-dipole, and off-diagonal density terms is still needed.
