# The Physics and Prospects of Super-Tau Charm Factories

> The proposed Super tau-charm factories are a powerful new class of high-luminosity electron-positron colliders operating in the center-of-mass energy range between 2 and 7 GeV, a region that spans thresholds for tau leptons, open-charm hadrons, charmonium and charmonium-like states, hyperons, and light hadrons. With unprecedented data samples, threshold kinematics, and quantum-coherent production, these facilities offer unique opportunities to advance precision tests of the Standard Model and to search for physics beyond it. In this review, we examine the physics prospects of the Super Tau-Charm Facility, focusing on precision charm measurements, CP violation in mesons and baryons, tau lepton properties and rare decays, and nonperturbative QCD phenomena such as hadronization, spectroscopy, and time-like form factors. We also discuss the experimental landscape, technological challenges, and complementarity with existing and planned facilities. Together, these capabilities position super tau-charm factories at the forefront of the precision frontier in particle physics.

## Key claim

The Super Tau-Charm Facility (STCF) can address foundational questions about quark matter formation, fundamental symmetry violation, and new physics through precision observables, but its full potential depends on achieving a design luminosity exceeding \(0.5 \times 10^{35} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\) and operating at center-of-mass energies from 2 to 7 GeV, a regime where threshold kinematics and quantum coherence provide unique experimental advantages that complement both high-energy colliders and low-energy precision experiments.

## Method

The paper reviews the proposed STCF as a next-generation electron-positron collider operating in the 2–7 GeV center-of-mass energy range, with a design luminosity goal exceeding \(0.5 \times 10^{35} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\). It examines the physics program across precision charm measurements, CP violation in mesons and baryons, tau lepton properties and rare decays, and nonperturbative QCD phenomena, while also surveying the experimental landscape including BESIII, LHCb, and Belle II, and discussing accelerator technology advancements such as the large Piwinski angle with Crab Waist scheme.

## Key numbers

- Design luminosity goal for STCF: exceed \(0.5 \times 10^{35} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\) at optimized energy near 4 GeV, with upgrade potential to \(10^{35} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\)
- BEPCII/BESIII peak luminosity: \(1.1 \times 10^{33} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\) (current operating tau-charm facility)
- SuperKEKB achieved luminosity in 2024: \(4.47 \times 10^{34} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\), with design goal of \(6 \times 10^{35} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\)
- Projected annual integrated luminosities at STCF: inverse-attobarn level, yielding samples of order \(10^{10}\) tau pairs and comparably large yields of charmed mesons, charmed baryons, and hyperons
- Current experimental limits on rare charm decays: \(\mathcal{B}(D^0 \to \mu^+\mu^-) < 3.1 \times 10^{-9}\), \(\mathcal{B}(D^0 \to e^+e^-) < 7.9 \times 10^{-8}\), \(\mathcal{B}(D^0 \to \mu^+e^-) < 1.3 \times 10^{-8}\)
- Upper limit on \(D^{0*}(2007) \to e^+e^-\) from CMD-3: \(< 1.7 \times 10^{-6}\); from LHCb for \(D^{0*}(2007) \to \mu^+\mu^-\): \(< 2.6 \times 10^{-8}\)
- Projected statistical sensitivities for \(D^0\)-\(\bar D^0\) mixing parameters at STCF with 1 ab\(^{-1}\) at 4030 MeV: \(\delta x = 4.0 \times 10^{-4}\), \(\delta y = 1.6 \times 10^{-4}\) for the \(K^-\pi^+\pi^0\) mode
- Jarlskog invariant for quark-sector CP violation: \(J \sim 3 \times 10^{-5}\)

## Context

This review extends the physics case for tau-charm factories beyond the current BEPCII/BESIII program (operating since 2009 with luminosity \(1.1 \times 10^{33} \, \mathrm{cm}^{-2} \, \mathrm{s}^{-1}\)) and complements the B factory program at Belle II and the hadron-collider program at LHCb. The proposed STCF in China and the Super Charm-Tau factory (SCTF) in Russia represent a third generation of circular electron-positron colliders, building on the large Piwinski angle with Crab Waist scheme validated at DA\(\Phi\)NE in 2009 and now adopted by SuperKEKB. The facility enables precision tests of CKM unitarity, searches for lepton flavor violation in tau and charm decays at the \(10^{-9}\)–\(10^{-10}\) level, and studies of exotic XYZ states and glueball candidates, while also providing crucial inputs for the hadronic contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
