# High Pt hadron-hadron correlations

> We propose the formulation of a dihadron fragmentation function in terms of parton matrix elements. Under the collinear factorization approximation and facilitated by the cut-vertex technique, the two hadron inclusive cross section at leading order (LO) in e+ e- annihilation is shown to factorize into a short distance parton cross section and the long distance dihadron fragmentation function. We also derive the DGLAP evolution equation of this function at leading log. The evolution equation for the non-singlet and singlet quark fragmentation function and the gluon fragmentation function are solved numerically with the initial condition taken from event generators. Modifications to the dihadron fragmentation function from higher twist corrections in DIS off nuclei are computed. Results are presented for cases of physical interest.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ratio of dihadron to single-hadron fragmentation functions shows minimal variation as the energy scale \(Q^2\) is varied over a wide range, and that this ratio is also only weakly modified in cold nuclear matter, consistent with experimental observations from HERMES and RHIC.

## Method

The authors formulate a dihadron fragmentation function \(D_q^{h_1,h_2}(z_1,z_2,Q^2)\) using parton matrix elements and the cut-vertex technique, demonstrating factorization of the two-hadron inclusive cross section in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation at leading order. They derive DGLAP evolution equations for the non-singlet, singlet quark, and gluon dihadron fragmentation functions at leading-log accuracy, and solve them numerically using initial conditions extracted from the JETSET event generator at \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\). Medium modifications in deep-inelastic scattering off nuclei are computed using the nuclear-enhanced higher-twist formalism, including multiple scattering and induced gluon bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

* The DGLAP evolution is solved from \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) up to \(Q^2 = 109\) GeV\(^2\) (i.e., \(\log(Q^2) = 4.693\)).
* For the non-singlet quark dihadron fragmentation function, results are presented for fixed ratios \(z_1 = 2z_2\) and \(z_1 = 3z_2\).
* The quark and gluon dihadron fragmentation functions are extracted from JETSET at \(\mu^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) with the leading hadron momentum fraction fixed at \(z_1 = 0.5\).
* The ratio \(D(z_1,z_2,Q^2)/D(z_1,Q^2)\) for gluons shows almost no change as the energy scale is varied over two orders of magnitude.
* Medium modification results for a Nitrogen nucleus are presented as the double ratio \(R_{A/D} = R_A^{h_1 h_2}/R_D^{h_1 h_2}\), compared to preliminary HERMES data.

## Context

This work extends the standard DGLAP formalism for single-hadron fragmentation functions (Gribov and Lipatov, 1972; Dokshitzer, 1977; Altarelli and Parisi, 1977) to the dihadron case, enabling theoretical predictions for two-hadron correlations within jets. The study was motivated by experimental observations at RHIC (Adler et al., 2003) that same-side high-\(p_T\) hadron correlations in central \(Au+Au\) collisions remain similar to those in \(p+p\) collisions, and by similar findings in DIS experiments at HERMES (Diefenthaler, 2004). The medium modification calculations build on the higher-twist formalism of Guo and Wang (2000). The results enable future calculations of dihadron fragmentation modifications in a hot quark-gluon plasma.
