# Nuclear Structure Aspects of Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We decompose the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements into sums of products over the intermediate nucleus with two less nucleons. We find that the sum is dominated by the J^pi=0^+ ground state of this intermediate nucleus for both the light and heavy neutrino decay processes. This provides a new theoretical tool for comparing and improving nuclear structure models. It also provides the connection to two-nucleon transfer experiments.

## Key claim

The neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NME) can be decomposed into sums over intermediate states of the nucleus with two fewer nucleons, and this sum is dominated by the \(J^\pi = 0^+\) ground state of that intermediate nucleus for both light and heavy neutrino exchange processes, with cancellations from higher-spin states reducing the total.

## Method

The authors used the nuclear shell model with the JUN45 Hamiltonian in the \(jj44\) model space for \(^{76}\)Ge and \(^{82}\)Se, and the GXPF1A Hamiltonian in the \(pf\) model space for \(^{48}\)Ca. They expressed the NME as a sum over intermediate states of the \((A-2)\) nucleus by rewriting the two-body transition densities in terms of two-nucleon transfer amplitudes, and then evaluated the running sum as a function of excitation energy in the intermediate nucleus. The closure approximation was used for light-neutrino exchange with optimal closure energies of 0.5 MeV, 3.5 MeV, and 3.5 MeV for \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{76}\)Ge, and \(^{82}\)Se, respectively.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{76}\)Ge, the total NME is reduced by about a factor of two for light neutrinos and about 20% for heavy neutrinos due to cancellations from intermediate states with \(J_m > 0\) up to about 6 MeV excitation.
* For \(^{48}\)Ca, 80% of the total matrix elements come from just the \(0^+\) ground state and the first excited \(2^+\) state of the \((A-2)\) nucleus.
* The tensor contribution to the total NME is less than 2%.
* The Gamow-Teller contribution alone is only about 10% smaller than the total from Gamow-Teller plus Fermi plus Tensor contributions.
* The effective strength of the Fermi \(0\nu\beta\beta\) operator is about a factor of ten smaller than the Gamow-Teller operator.
* Many-body perturbation theory calculations show an enhancement for the light-neutrino NME of 20% for \(^{76}\)Ge and 30% for \(^{82}\)Se relative to the shell model.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements beyond the traditional charge-exchange decomposition to intermediate states in the \((A-2)\) nucleus, providing a new theoretical tool for comparing models such as the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA), the interacting shell model, the interacting boson model, the generator coordinate method, and the projected Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov model. The finding that the NME is dominated by the ground state of the \((A-2)\) nucleus connects directly to two-nucleon transfer experiments, which are enhanced by pairing interactions as discussed in earlier work by Broglia (1978) and others. This decomposition enables systematic comparisons of how different models treat pairing and quenching, and opens the way to using knockout and transfer reaction data to constrain the NME.
