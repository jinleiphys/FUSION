# A Constituent Picture of Hadrons from Light-Front QCD

> It may be possible to derive a constituent approximation for bound states in QCD using hamiltonian light-front field theory. Cutoffs that violate explicit gauge invariance and Lorentz covariance must be employed. A similarity renormalization group and coupling coherence are used to compute the effective hamiltonian as an expansion in powers of the canonical QCD running coupling constant. At second order the QCD hamiltonian contains a confining interaction, which is being studied using bound state perturbation theory. Explicit constituent masses appear because of symmetry violations, and confinement also produces mass gaps, leading to the possibility of an accurate non-perturbative constituent approximation emerging in light-front QCD.

# A Constituent Picture of Hadrons from Light-Front QCD

## Key claim

It may be possible to derive a constituent approximation for hadrons from QCD using light-front hamiltonian field theory, but only if cutoffs that violate manifest gauge invariance and Lorentz covariance are employed, and if symmetry violations are treated approximately. The constituent picture is not forced by truncating Fock space; rather, it emerges dynamically from renormalization group transformations that replace high-energy many-body states with effective few-body interactions.

## Method

The author uses a similarity renormalization group transformation that lowers a cutoff on the change in free energy between states, combined with coupling coherence to fix the effective hamiltonian as an expansion in powers of the running QCD coupling constant. The effective hamiltonian is computed to second order in the coupling, and the resulting interactions are analyzed in the quark-antiquark sector to study confinement. A second infrared cutoff on longitudinal momentum fractions is introduced to regulate divergences, with the requirement that all such divergences cancel exactly for color singlet states.

## Key numbers

- Quark self-energy at second order: $\Sigma_{\Lambda}(p) = \frac{g_\Lambda^2 C_F \Lambda^2}{4 \pi^2 {\cal P}^+} \left\{ \ln\left( \frac{p^+}{\epsilon {\cal P}^+} \right) - \frac{3}{4} \right\} + {\cal O}(\epsilon)$, where $\Lambda$ is the energy cutoff, $\epsilon$ is the longitudinal momentum cutoff parameter, and ${\cal P}^+$ is the longitudinal momentum scale
- The singular quark-antiquark interaction contains a factor $\left( \frac{1}{q^+} \right)^2$ with theta functions $\theta(\Lambda^2 / {\cal P}^+ - |q^-|)$ and $\theta(|q^+| - \epsilon {\cal P}^+)$
- The expectation value of the singular interaction for identical states yields $- \frac{g_\Lambda^2 C_F \Lambda^2}{2 \pi^2 {\cal P}^+} \log(1/\epsilon) \int \frac{dQ^+ d^2Q_{\perp}}{16\pi^3} \phi_2^*(Q) \phi_1(Q)$, which exactly cancels the divergent quark and antiquark mass contributions for color singlets
- The residual confining potential diverges logarithmically: $V(r)-V(0) \rightarrow \frac{g_\Lambda^2 C_F \Lambda^2}{4 \pi^2 {\cal P}^+} \log(|x^-|)$ as $|x^-| \rightarrow \infty$ with $x_\perp=0$, and $V(r)-V(0) \rightarrow \frac{g_\Lambda^2 C_F \Lambda^2}{2 \pi^2 {\cal P}^+} \log(|x_\perp|)$ as $|x_\perp| \rightarrow \infty$ with $x^-=0$

## Context

This work extends the light-front renormalization group approach developed by Wilson, G{\l}azek, and collaborators (Wilson et al., 1994; G{\l}azek and Wilson, 1993, 1994) and the author's own earlier work on coupling coherence (Perry, 1994; Perry and Wilson, 1993). It contrasts with earlier Tamm-Dancoff truncation approaches (Perry, Harindranath, and Wilson, 1990) that lacked a dynamical mechanism to limit particle number. The derived confining interaction enables bound state perturbation theory calculations for hadrons, with initial applications to heavy mesons reported by Brisudov{\'a} and Perry (1996). The approach opens the possibility of systematic few-body calculations in QCD, though open questions remain about rotational symmetry restoration, the role of chiral symmetry breaking operators, and whether higher-order corrections convert the logarithmic potential into a linear one.
