# Physics of Strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> This review cover our current understanding of strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP), especially theoretical progress in (i) explaining the RHIC data by hydrodynamics, (ii) describing lattice data using electric-magnetic duality; (iii) understanding of gauge-string duality known as AdS/CFT and its application for "conformal" plasma. In view of interdisciplinary nature of the subject, we include brief introduction into several topics "for pedestrians". Some fundamental questions addressed are: Why is sQGP such a good liquid? What is the nature of (de)confinement and what do we know about ''magnetic'' objects creating it? Do they play any important role in sQGP physics? Can we understand the AdS/CFT predictions, from the gauge theory side? Can they be tested experimentally? Can AdS/CFT duality help us understand rapid equilibration/entropy production? Can we work out a complete dynamical "gravity dual" to heavy ion collisions?

## Key claim

This review argues that the Quark-Gluon Plasma created at RHIC is not a weakly coupled gas as previously assumed from perturbative QCD, but rather a strongly coupled liquid whose properties can be understood through analogies with classical strongly coupled plasmas, ultracold atomic gases, and the AdS/CFT correspondence, though many of these connections remain qualitative rather than proven.

## Method

The paper is a comprehensive review that synthesizes theoretical progress across three main approaches: (i) relativistic hydrodynamics applied to RHIC heavy ion collision data, including ideal and viscous hydrodynamics with equations of state; (ii) electric-magnetic duality descriptions of lattice QCD data; and (iii) the gauge-string duality (AdS/CFT) applied to conformal plasma. The review also includes pedagogical introductions to classical strongly coupled plasmas and ultracold Fermi gases, using molecular dynamics simulations and experimental atomic trap data as comparative tools.

## Key numbers

- The plasma parameter Gamma for molten salt (NaCl) at T ~ 10^3 K is Gamma ~ 60, placing it in the glassy liquid regime
- At RHIC, the initial temperature is T_i ~ 400 MeV ~ 1/(0.5 fm), giving RT ~ 10 >> 1 for Au nuclei with radius R ~ 6 fm
- The chemical freezeout temperature at RHIC is T ~ 170 MeV, while rescattering continues to T_f ~ 90 MeV for central collisions
- The maximal radial flow velocity at RHIC is about 0.7 c
- For strongly coupled ultracold fermionic atoms, the universal constant beta for the ground state energy density is defined as epsilon(n) = beta * (hbar^2 n^(2/3) / m)
- The ratio eta/(hbar s) for strongly coupled fermionic atoms lies between the sQGP value (light blue band) and liquid He-4 (dark blue bar), making it the "second best liquid"
- The critical temperature for superfluidity in strongly coupled fermionic atoms is T_c/E_F = 0.35, and the pair melting temperature is T_2/T_F ~ 0.7-0.8

## Context

This review extends the paradigm shift from perturbative QCD descriptions of QGP (initiated by Collins and Perry (1974) and Shuryak (1977)) to the strongly coupled regime discovered at RHIC starting in 2000. It connects heavy ion physics to classical plasma physics (reviewed by Mrowczynski and Thoma (2007)), ultracold atomic gas experiments (O'Hara et al., 2002), and the AdS/CFT correspondence from string theory. The work enables future development of complete dynamical gravity duals for heavy ion collisions and quantitative understanding of transport properties in strongly coupled matter, including the possible application of AdS/CFT ideas to ultracold atomic gases as proposed by Son (2008) and Balasubramanian and McGreevy (2008).
