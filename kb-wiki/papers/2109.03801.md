# Bosonic Dark Matter in Neutron Stars and its Effect on Gravitational Wave Signal

> We study an impact of self-interacting bosonic dark matter (DM) on various observable properties of neutron stars (NSs). The analysis is performed for asymmetric DM with masses from few MeV to GeV, the self-coupling constant of order $\mathcal{O}(1)$ and various DM fractions. Allowing a mixture between DM and baryonic matter, the formation of a dense DM core or an extended dark halo have been explored. We find that both distribution regimes crucially depend on the mass and fraction of DM for sub-GeV boson masses in the strong coupling regime. From the combined analysis of the mass-radius relation and the tidal deformability of compact stars including bosonic DM, we set a stringent constraint on DM fraction. We conclude that observations of 2$M_{\odot}$ NSs together with $Λ_{1.4}\leq580$ constraint, set by LIGO/Virgo Collaboration, favour sub-GeV DM particles with low fractions below $\sim 5 \%$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that observations of 2 solar mass neutron stars together with the tidal deformability constraint of Lambda_1.4 <= 580 from LIGO/Virgo favor sub-GeV bosonic dark matter particles with low fractions below approximately 5%, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of self-coupling constant of order O(1) and applies only to asymmetric dark matter that does not self-annihilate.

## Method

The authors model neutron stars as two-fluid systems with baryonic matter described by the induced surface tension (IST) equation of state and bosonic dark matter described by a self-interacting complex scalar field with repulsive self-interaction. They solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for various dark matter particle masses (from a few MeV to GeV), self-coupling constants (0.5 pi to 2 pi), and dark matter fractions (up to 50%), then compute the mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities to compare with observational constraints.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of pure boson stars: M_max^BS approximately 10 solar masses times lambda^(1/2) times (100 MeV / m_chi)^2
- For m_chi = 400 MeV and lambda = pi, a dark matter core of radius approximately 5 km forms inside the neutron star
- Transition from dark matter core to dark matter halo occurs at m_chi approximately 175 MeV for lambda = pi and F_chi = 10%
- For m_chi <= 105 MeV and lambda = pi, the 2 solar mass constraint is satisfied for all dark matter fractions
- The limiting masses for different self-coupling constants are: (lambda = 0.5 pi, m_chi = 88.4 MeV), (lambda = 1.5 pi, m_chi = 116 MeV), (lambda = 2 pi, m_chi = 125 MeV)
- The tidal deformability constraint Lambda_1.4 <= 580 from LIGO/Virgo favors sub-GeV dark matter fractions below approximately 5%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter in neutron stars by Nelson (2019), Ellis (2018), and others, who examined fermionic and non-interacting bosonic dark matter, by incorporating repulsive self-interactions among bosonic dark matter particles. The paper introduces a new phenomenon called "dark matter core-halo transition" not previously reported, where the outermost radius of the star can interchange between the baryonic and dark matter components along the mass-radius profile. The results enable future gravitational wave observations from LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA and x-ray observations from NICER to constrain dark matter properties in neutron stars.
