# Measuring spin correlation between quarks during QCD confinement

> The vacuum is now understood to possess a rich and complex structure, characterized by fluctuating energy fields and a condensate of virtual quark-antiquark pairs. The spontaneous breaking of the approximate chiral symmetry, signaled by the nonvanishing quark condensate $\langle q\bar{q}\rangle$, is dynamically generated through topologically nontrivial gauge configurations such as instantons. The precise mechanism linking the chiral symmetry breaking to the mass generation associated with quark confinement remains a profound open question in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) - the fundamental theory of strong interaction. High energy proton-proton collisions could liberate virtual quark-antiquark pairs from the vacuum that subsequently undergo confinement to form hadrons, whose properties could serve as probes into QCD confinement and the quark condensate. Here, we report evidence of spin correlations in $Λ\barΛ$ hyperon pairs inherited from spin-correlated strange quark-antiquark virtual pairs. Measurements by the STAR experiment at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory reveal a relative polarization signal of $(18 \pm 4)\%$ that links the virtual spin-correlated quark pairs from the QCD vacuum to their final-state hadron counterparts. Crucially, this correlation vanishes when the hyperon pairs are widely separated in angle, consistent with the decoherence of the quantum system. Our findings provide a new experimental paradigm for exploring the dynamics and interplay of quark confinement and entanglement.

## Key claim
The STAR Collaboration reports evidence that spin correlations observed in Lambda-antilambda hyperon pairs produced in high-energy proton-proton collisions are inherited from spin-correlated strange quark-antiquark virtual pairs liberated from the QCD vacuum, with the correlation vanishing when the hyperon pairs are widely separated in angle, consistent with quantum decoherence.

## Method
The STAR experiment at RHIC measured the relative polarization of Lambda-antilambda hyperon pairs produced in proton-proton collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV per nucleon pair. The spin correlation between the hyperons was extracted from the angular distribution of their decay products, using the weak decay asymmetry to determine the hyperon polarization. The measurement was performed as a function of the angular separation between the two hyperons to test for decoherence effects.

## Key numbers
* Relative polarization signal of (18 +/- 4)% measured for Lambda-antilambda hyperon pairs from proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV at RHIC.
* The correlation vanishes when hyperon pairs are widely separated in angle, consistent with decoherence of the quantum system.

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD vacuum structure by providing experimental access to spin correlations in virtual quark-antiquark pairs, which are related to the chiral condensate and instanton-induced effects. The measurement enables a new experimental paradigm for exploring quark confinement and entanglement, building on theoretical frameworks that link the QCD vacuum structure to hadronization processes. The results open the possibility of using hyperon pair spin correlations as a probe of the QCD vacuum and the mechanism of chiral symmetry breaking.
