# Quenching factor of Gamow-Teller and spin dipole giant resonances

> Gamow-Teller (GT) and spin-dipole (SD) strength distributions of four doubly magic nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb are studied by the self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (RPA) method. The Skyrme forces SAMi and SAMi-T without/with tensor interactions are adopted in our calculations. The calculated strengths are compared with available experimental data. The RPA results of GT and SD strengths of all four nuclei show fine agreement with observed GT and SD resonances in energy. A small GT peak below the main GT resonance is better described by the Skyrme interaction SAMi-T with the tensor terms. The quenching factors for GT and SD are extracted from the comparisons between RPA results and experimental strengths. It is pointed out that the quenching effect on experimental SD peaks is somewhat modest compared with that on GT peaks in the four nuclei.

## Key claim

The quenching factor for spin-dipole (SD) resonances is systematically larger (qf ~ 0.8) than that for Gamow-Teller (GT) resonances (qf ~ 0.55-0.69) across four doubly magic nuclei, indicating that the quenching effect on SD peaks is "somewhat modest" compared with GT peaks, contrary to the common assumption that both modes quench similarly.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (HF+RPA) calculations in coordinate space with a radial mesh of 0.1 fm and box boundary conditions, using the Skyrme energy density functionals SAMi (without tensor terms) and SAMi-T (with tensor terms determined from ab initio Brueckner HF calculations with the AV18 interaction). GT and SD strength distributions were computed for the t- channel of 48Ca, 90Zr, 132Sn, and 208Pb, and for the t+ channel of 90Zr, with the lowest ten unoccupied states for each l and j included as particle states. Quenching factors were extracted by comparing the integrated RPA strengths with experimental strengths integrated up to the same maximum excitation energy (25 MeV for 132Sn, 30 MeV for the other nuclei).

## Key numbers

- 48Ca GT: experimental integrated strength 15.3 ± 2.2, RPA sum rule 23.96 (SAMi) / 23.74 (SAMi-T), quenching factor qf = 0.64 for both interactions
- 90Zr GT: experimental integrated strength 20.75, RPA sum rule 29.89 (SAMi) / 29.59 (SAMi-T), qf = 0.69 (SAMi) / 0.70 (SAMi-T)
- 132Sn GT: experimental integrated strength 53 ± 15, RPA sum rule 95.61 (SAMi) / 93.86 (SAMi-T), qf = 0.55 (SAMi) / 0.56 (SAMi-T)
- 208Pb GT: experimental integrated strength 85.00, RPA sum rule 130.65 (SAMi) / 128.05 (SAMi-T), qf = 0.65 (SAMi) / 0.66 (SAMi-T)
- 48Ca SD total: experimental sum rule 97 ± 11 fm^2, RPA sum rule 148.69 (SAMi) / 133.43 (SAMi-T), qf = 0.66 (SAMi) / 0.73 (SAMi-T)
- 90Zr SD t-: experimental sum rule 247 ± 20 fm^2, RPA sum rule 314.27 (SAMi) / 305.05 (SAMi-T), qf = 0.78 (SAMi) / 0.81 (SAMi-T)
- 90Zr SD t+: experimental sum rule 98 ± 9 fm^2, RPA sum rule 169.49 (SAMi) / 168.72 (SAMi-T), qf = 0.58 for both interactions
- 208Pb SD total: experimental sum rule 1004 ± 23 fm^2, RPA sum rule 1279.6 (SAMi) / 1256.2 (SAMi-T), qf = 0.78 (SAMi) / 0.80 (SAMi-T)
- 208Pb SD 0- component: experimental sum rule 107 ± 7 fm^2, qf = 0.63 (SAMi) / 0.67 (SAMi-T)
- 208Pb SD 1- component: experimental sum rule 450 ± 15 fm^2, qf = 1.02 for both interactions
- 208Pb SD 2- component: experimental sum rule 447 ± 15 fm^2, qf = 0.67 (SAMi) / 0.68 (SAMi-T)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of GT quenching in lighter nuclei, where shell model calculations for p-shell (A < 16) required an effective GT operator multiplied by 0.82 ± 0.015, for sd-shell (16 < A < 40) required 0.77 ± 0.02, and for pf-shell (A between 41 and 50) required 0.744 ± 0.015 (qf = 0.554 ± 0.022). The authors note that SD quenching has been much less discussed than GT quenching, despite its importance for double beta decay calculations. The results provide quenching factors for both GT and SD modes that should be implemented in future theoretical studies of double beta decay probabilities, and motivate further work with microscopic models beyond RPA and with two-body currents.
