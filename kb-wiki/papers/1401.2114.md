# The beta decay of 38Ca: Sensitive test of isospin symmetry-breaking corrections from mirror superallowed 0+-to-0+ transitions

> We report the first branching-ratio measurement of the superallowed 0+-to-0+ beta transition from 38Ca. The result, 0.7728(16), leads to an ft value of 3062.3(68)s with a relative precision of +/-0.2%. This makes possible a high-precision comparison of the ft values for the mirror superallowed transitions, 38Ca-to-38mK and 38mK-to-38Ar, which sensitively tests the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections required to extract Vud, the up-down quark-mixing element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix, from superallowed beta decay. The result supports the corrections currently used, and points the way to even tighter constraints on CKM unitarity.

## Context

This work adds the first new well-characterized superallowed transition in nearly a decade to the set of 13 previously known cases surveyed by Hardy and Towner (2009). The mirror ft-value ratio provides a sensitive test of the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections (ОҙC) required to extract Vud from superallowed ОІ decay, with the advantage that theoretical uncertainties on the difference term (ОҙCбөҮвҲ’ОҙCбөғ) are significantly smaller than on the individual corrections. The result points toward a potential reduction of the uncertainty on Vud and the CKM unitarity sum by discriminating between Woods-Saxon and Hartree-Fock radial wave functions used in ОҙC calculations, and the authors report being well advanced in making a similar measurement for ВівҒҙAr, where a 40-year-old measurement by Hagberg et al. (1974) currently provides insufficient precision.
