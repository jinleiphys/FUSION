# The beta decay of 38Ca: Sensitive test of isospin symmetry-breaking corrections from mirror superallowed 0+-to-0+ transitions

> We report the first branching-ratio measurement of the superallowed 0+-to-0+ beta transition from 38Ca. The result, 0.7728(16), leads to an ft value of 3062.3(68)s with a relative precision of +/-0.2%. This makes possible a high-precision comparison of the ft values for the mirror superallowed transitions, 38Ca-to-38mK and 38mK-to-38Ar, which sensitively tests the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections required to extract Vud, the up-down quark-mixing element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix, from superallowed beta decay. The result supports the corrections currently used, and points the way to even tighter constraints on CKM unitarity.

## Key claim

The first measurement of the superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ β-branching ratio from ³⁸Ca yields an ft value of 3062.3(68) s with ±0.2% relative precision, and the resulting mirror ft-value ratio ftᵃ/ftᵇ = 1.0036(22) favors Woods-Saxon over Hartree-Fock calculations for isospin symmetry-breaking corrections, though the result is not yet definitive.

## Method

The authors produced ³⁸Ca via the ¹H(³⁹K,2n)³⁸Ca reaction using a 30A-MeV ³⁹K beam from the Texas A&M K500 superconducting cyclotron, separated the ejectiles by charge-to-mass ratio in the MARS recoil separator, and implanted 99.7% pure samples in a fast tape-transport system. They measured β-γ coincidences with a plastic scintillator and a calibrated HPGe detector, determined the β-branching ratio to the 1698-keV 1⁺ state in ³⁸K from the 1568-keV γ-ray intensity, summed all Gamow-Teller branches from observed γ-ray peaks, and subtracted from 100% to obtain the superallowed branching ratio.

## Key numbers

- Superallowed branching ratio of ³⁸Ca: 0.7728(16) (statistical uncertainty ±0.0014, systematic uncertainty ±0.0009, combined in quadrature)
- ft value for ³⁸Ca superallowed transition: 3062.3(68) s
- ft value for mirror transition ³⁸ᵐK→³⁸Ar: 3051.5(9) s (from 2009 survey updated with Eronen et al. QEC measurement)
- Mirror ft-value ratio ftᵃ/ftᵇ for A=38: 1.0036(22)
- Half-life of ³⁸Ca: 443.77(35) ms
- QEC value for ³⁸Ca superallowed branch: 6612.12(7) keV
- HPGe detector efficiency at 1568 keV: 0.1777(4)%
- β-detector efficiency ratio εβ/εβᵢ for the 1568-keV transition: 1.0038(4)
- Summing correction factor for 1568-keV γ ray: 1.0263(26)
- Dead-time/pile-up correction factor: 1.01366(11)
- Calculated ftᵃ/ftᵇ ratios for A=38: 1.00196(39) with Woods-Saxon, 0.99976(43) with Hartree-Fock

## Context

This work adds the first new well-characterized superallowed transition in nearly a decade to the set of 13 previously known cases surveyed by Hardy and Towner (2009). The mirror ft-value ratio provides a sensitive test of the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections (δC) required to extract Vud from superallowed β decay, with the advantage that theoretical uncertainties on the difference term (δCᵇ−δCᵃ) are significantly smaller than on the individual corrections. The result points toward a potential reduction of the uncertainty on Vud and the CKM unitarity sum by discriminating between Woods-Saxon and Hartree-Fock radial wave functions used in δC calculations, and the authors report being well advanced in making a similar measurement for ³⁴Ar, where a 40-year-old measurement by Hagberg et al. (1974) currently provides insufficient precision.
