# Chiral phase transition in an extended linear sigma model: initial results

> We investigate the scalar meson mass dependence on the chiral phase transition in the framework of an SU(3), (axial)vector meson extended linear sigma model with additional constituent quarks and Polyakov loops. We determine the parameters of the Lagrangian at zero temperature in a hybrid approach, where we treat the mesons at tree-level, while the constituent quarks at 1-loop level. We assume two nonzero scalar condensates and together with the Polyakov-loop variables we determine their temperature dependence according to the 1-loop level field equations.

## Key claim

The pseudocritical temperature of the chiral phase transition in this extended linear sigma model depends strongly on the mass of the scalar-isoscalar \(f_0^L\) (sigma) meson: a high sigma mass of 1.3 GeV yields a pseudocritical temperature \(T_c \approx 550\) MeV, while tuning the sigma mass down to 400 MeV reduces \(T_c\) to 150–200 MeV, in line with lattice QCD results.

## Method

The authors construct an SU(3) linear sigma model extended with (axial)vector mesons, constituent quarks, and Polyakov loops. They determine the 14 Lagrangian parameters at zero temperature via a \(\chi^2\) fit to experimental masses and decay widths from the PDG, using a hybrid approach where mesons are treated at tree level and constituent quarks at one-loop level (including fermion vacuum fluctuations). At finite temperature, they solve four coupled field equations for the non-strange condensate \(\phi_N\), strange condensate \(\phi_S\), and the Polyakov-loop variables \(\Phi\) and \(\bar{\Phi}\), using a mean-field Polyakov-loop potential of polynomial form from Ratti (2005) and including only fermionic thermal fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Non-strange condensate at zero temperature: \(\phi_N = 0.1622\) GeV
- Strange condensate at zero temperature: \(\phi_S = 0.1262\) GeV
- Yukawa coupling: \(g_F = 4.9429\)
- With sigma mass \(m_{f_0^L} = 1.3\) GeV (obtained by setting \(\lambda_1 = 0\)), the pseudocritical temperature for \(\phi_N\) is \(T_c \approx 550\) MeV
- With sigma mass tuned to \(m_{f_0^L} = 400\) MeV (corresponding to the physical \(f_0(500)\)), the pseudocritical temperature drops to \(T_c \approx 150\)–\(200\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the (axial)vector meson extended linear sigma model previously analyzed at zero temperature by Parganlija (2013) and Kovacs and Wolf (2013) by adding constituent quarks and Polyakov loops to study finite-temperature behavior. The finding that a low-mass sigma (\(\sim 400\) MeV) is needed to reproduce a realistic pseudocritical temperature (\(\sim 150\) MeV from lattice QCD by Aoki (2009)) suggests that the scalar-isoscalar state driving the transition is likely not a pure \(q\bar{q}\) state, consistent with the conclusions of Parganlija (2013). This result aligns with similar work by Schaefer and Wagner using a model without vector mesons, and enables further study of the QCD phase diagram at finite density for comparison with heavy-ion experiments at RHIC, LHC, and FAIR.
