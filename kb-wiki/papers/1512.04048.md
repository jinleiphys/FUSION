# $B^0 \to D^0 \bar D^0 K^0$, $B^+ \to D^0 \bar D^0 K^+$ and the scalar $D \bar D$ bound state

> We study the $B^0$ decay to $D^0 \bar D^0 K^0$ based on the chiral unitary model that generates the X(3720) resonance, and make predictions for the $D^0 \bar D^0$ invariant mass distribution. From the shape of the distribution, the existence of the resonance below threshold could be induced. We also predict the rate of production of the X(3720) resonance to the $D^0 \bar D^0$ mass distribution with no free parameters.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the shape of the \(D^0 \bar D^0\) invariant mass distribution in \(B^0 \to D^0 \bar D^0 K^0\) decay can reveal the existence of the scalar X(3720) resonance below the \(D \bar D\) threshold, but the absolute normalization of the distribution is not predicted; only the ratio of the resonance production rate to the mass distribution is parameter-free under the assumption that X(3720) is dynamically generated.

## Method

The authors use a chiral unitary model that generates the X(3720) resonance as a bound state of \(D \bar D\) in isospin \(I=0\), solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels (\(D^+ D^-\), \(D^0 \bar D^0\), \(D_s^+ D_s^-\)). They compute the decay amplitudes for \(B^0 \to \bar K^0 D^0 \bar D^0\) and \(B^- \to K^- D^0 \bar D^0\) via hadronization of the weak decay quarks followed by final-state rescattering, and evaluate the differential decay width \(d\Gamma/dM_{\rm inv}\) for the \(D^0 \bar D^0\) invariant mass distribution. The effect of a finite width for X(3720) (36 MeV from \(\eta\eta\) decay) is also studied by adding an \(\eta\eta\) channel.

## Key numbers

- The X(3720) resonance pole is found at \(\sqrt{s_R} = 3719.4 + i0\) MeV (zero width) using parameters \(\alpha = -1.3\), \(\mu = 1500\) MeV from the chiral unitary model.
- When including the \(\eta\eta\) channel, the width of X(3720) is set to \(\Gamma = 36\) MeV, tuned with a coupling strength \(a = 42\).
- The \(D^0 \bar D^0\) mass distribution for \(\bar B^0 \to \bar K^0 D^0 \bar D^0\) is shown from threshold (approx. 3730 MeV) up to about 3920 MeV, with a clear enhancement over phase space.
- For \(B^- \to K^- D^0 \bar D^0\), the comparison with experimental data from Lees (2014) is made in bins of 40 MeV: [threshold, 3750], [3750, 3790], [3790, 3830], [3830, 3870], [3870, 3910] MeV, after subtracting \(\psi(3770)\) and \(D_{s1}(2700)\) contributions.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to light mesons (e.g., \(f_0(980)\) as a \(K \bar K\) bound state) to the heavy quark sector, specifically the \(D \bar D\) interaction that generates the X(3720) resonance, as studied in Daniel (2008). It is motivated by the experimental observation of \(B^+ \to D^0 \bar D^0 K^+\) by Lees (2014) and ongoing LHCb studies. The method is analogous to earlier work on \(B^0_s \to D_s^- (K D)^+\) by Miguel Mari (2013) and on \(\bar B^0 \to \bar K^{*0} X(YZ)\) by Liang and Raquel (2014), where resonances below threshold were inferred from invariant mass spectra. The paper enables future experimental searches for the X(3720) state, particularly in \(B^0 \to D^0 \bar D^0 K^0\) where the absence of tree-level production makes the resonance signal more prominent.
