# On the bulk viscosity of anisotropically expanding hot QCD plasma

> The bulk viscosity, $ζ$ and its ratio with the shear viscosity, $ζ/η$ have been studied in an anisotropically expanding pure glue plasma in the presence of turbulent color fields. It has been shown that the anisotropy in the momentum distribution function of gluons, which has been determined from a linearized transport equation eventually leads to the bulk viscosity. For the isotropic (equilibrium) state, a recently proposed quasi-particle model of pure SU(3) lattice QCD equation of state has been employed where the interactions are encoded in the effective fugacity. It has been argued that the interactions present in the equation of state, significantly contribute to the bulk viscosity. Its ratio with the shear viscosity is significant even at $1.5 T_c$. Thus, one needs to take in account the effects of the bulk viscosity while studying the hydrodynamic expansion of QGP in RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity of hot QCD plasma, driven by chromo-Weibel instabilities from anisotropic gluon distributions, is significant relative to shear viscosity even at 1.5 T_c, contrary to the near-conformal expectation; the ratio ζ/η is computed unambiguously within a quasi-particle model of pure SU(3) lattice QCD, but the absolute values of ζ and η depend on the unknown temperature dependence of the gluon quenching parameter q̂.

## Method
The author solves a linearized transport equation for gluons in an anisotropically expanding pure glue plasma, retaining only the Vlasov (force) term from turbulent chromo-magnetic fields and neglecting collision and source terms. The equilibrium distribution is taken from a quasi-particle model that maps the lattice QCD equation of state onto non-interacting gluons with a temperature-dependent fugacity z_g. The perturbation function Δ₂(p) is obtained by inverting the angular operator L², leading to an expression for ζ in terms of the gluon quenching parameter q̂; the ratio ζ/η is then computed exactly by canceling q̂.

## Key numbers
- ζ/η ≈ 2.3 at T = 1.3 T_c
- ζ/η ≈ 1.0 at T = 1.5 T_c
- ζ/η ≈ 0.2 at T = 2.0 T_c
- T_c is taken as 0.27 GeV
- ζ/η becomes negligible for T ≥ 2.5 T_c

## Context
This work extends the transport-theory approach of Müller (2007) and Chandra (2011) for shear viscosity to the bulk viscosity of QGP, using the same quasi-particle model for the pure SU(3) lattice QCD equation of state from Karsch (2007). It contrasts with leading-order perturbative QCD results of Chen (2006), which give much smaller ζ/η and a near-conformal picture even at lower temperatures. The findings enable inclusion of temperature-dependent ζ/η in hydrodynamic simulations of QGP at RHIC and LHC, and motivate future extensions to full QCD with baryon chemical potential.
