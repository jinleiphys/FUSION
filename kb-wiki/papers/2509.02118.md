# Theory Summary

> This is the write-up of the summary talk on theory activities in the heavy-ion community as presented at Quark Matter 2025. It contains a (biased) selection of results from the parallel program of the conference. The progress is reported for three different areas: Bulk dynamics and initial state; jets, heavy flavor and electromagnetic probes and QCD phase diagram and observables. The idea is to convey the major achievements in the field and give a perspective for future directions.

## Key claim

The summary talk presents a biased selection of approximately 2% of the theory contributions from the Quark Matter 2025 parallel program, focusing on three areas of heavy-ion theory; the author explicitly acknowledges that the selection is subjective and cannot cover the full breadth of the conference.

## Method

The author surveyed 100 theory contributions from the parallel program of Quark Matter 2025, selecting representative results in bulk dynamics and initial state, jets and heavy flavor, and the QCD phase diagram. The summary organizes these results into the three thematic sections, highlighting connections between theory and experiment, and noting the increasing use of Bayesian analyses, machine learning, and large-scale collaborations in the field.

## Key numbers

- Close to 1000 participants attended Quark Matter 2025, with 100 theory contributions in the parallel program.
- The summary talk covered approximately 2% of the 1500 minutes of parallel program content.
- The JETSCAPE collaboration's Bayesian analysis of PbPb and XeXe data revealed clear evidence of the triaxial structure of xenon.
- Lattice QCD calculations of the heavy quark diffusion constant have been performed over a broad range of temperatures very close to the physical pion mass (HotQCD, 2025).
- The STAR collaboration released final results on net proton cumulants from the beam energy scan program at RHIC (STAR, 2025).
- The deconfinement transition was determined to be slightly above the chiral transition (Borsanyi et al., 2024).

## Context

This summary extends the tradition of conference summary talks at the Quark Matter series, which has become the largest in its history. It reports progress in refining the "standard model" of heavy-ion collisions based on non-equilibrium initial conditions, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, and hadronic transport, while noting that interfaces between stages still imply considerable uncertainties. The work enables future comparisons with upcoming experimental campaigns including CBM at FAIR (2029), EIC at BNL, ALICE3, and NA60+ at CERN (2030s), and calls for meta-analysis combining results from different Bayesian analyses such as those by the JETSCAPE collaboration (Mankolli).
