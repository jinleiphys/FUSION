# Microscopic description of nuclei in the middle of the pf-shell by a shell model calculation with G-matrix interaction

> Energy levels and electromagnetic properties of with $N=28\sim 30$ nuclides are studied in terms of a large-scale shell model calculation, which contains no newly adjusted parameters. The Kuo-Brown $G$-matrix interaction is shown to reproduce energy levels of 205 low-lying states of these nuclei. We evaluate effective charges by incorporating the core-polarization effects caused by the coupling to GQR's. We then compute E2 moments and transition probabilities. The M1 moments and transition rates are calculated by quoting the effective $g$-factors of Towner, which are obtained by taking into account the meson-exchange and the core-polarization mechanisms. By this microscopic calculation most of the E2 properties and the magnetic moments are reproduced. Although there are agreements and disagreements in the M1 transition rates, the general tendency is reproduced. The $(e,e')$ and $(p,p')$ excitation from the ground state to some low-lying $2^+$ states is also discussed.

## Key claim

The Kuo-Brown G-matrix interaction, without any phenomenological adjustment, reproduces the energy levels of 205 low-lying states across 24 nuclides with N=28-30, provided a sufficiently large configuration space (including up to two-particle excitations from the 0f7/2 orbit) is used; the authors note that k-convergence is worst for 56Ni and that higher k configurations are needed for some states in 56Ni, 55Co, and 57Ni.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations for nuclei with 20 < Z ≤ 28 and 28 ≤ N ≤ 30, assuming a 40Ca inert core and including configurations with up to two particles (k ≤ 2) excited from the 0f7/2 orbit to the 0f5/2, 1p3/2, and 1p1/2 orbits, using an M-scheme code with Lanczos diagonalization. The effective Hamiltonian was the Kuo-Brown G-matrix interaction derived from the Hamada-Johnston potential with 3p-1h corrections, using experimental single-particle energies (0f7/2 = 0.0 MeV, 0f5/2 = 6.5 MeV, 1p3/2 = 2.1 MeV, 1p1/2 = 3.9 MeV). For E2 properties, effective charges were evaluated by incorporating core-polarization effects from coupling to giant quadrupole resonances, while M1 properties used effective g-factors from Towner including meson-exchange and core-polarization mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- 205 low-lying states of 24 nuclides with N=28-30 were reproduced by the Kuo-Brown interaction
- For 56Ni, the k ≤ 2 calculation gave Ex(2_1^+) = 5.792 MeV, while the k ≤ 4 calculation gave 2.724 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.701 MeV
- For 56Ni, B(E2; 2_1^+ → 0_1^+) = 80.4 e^2 fm^4 (k ≤ 2) and 80.6 e^2 fm^4 (k ≤ 4), compared to experimental 74 ± 36/28 e^2 fm^4
- The ground-state k=0 probability for 56Ni was 63.4% in the k ≤ 2 calculation, reducing to 28% in the k ≤ 4 calculation, with the ground-state energy lowered by 3.3 MeV
- For 50Ti and 54Fe, the k=0 calculation gave Ex(2_1^+) = 1.02 MeV, while experimental values are 1.55 MeV (50Ti) and 1.40 MeV (54Fe)
- Single-particle energies used: 0f7/2 = 0.0 MeV, 0f5/2 = 6.5 MeV, 1p3/2 = 2.1 MeV, 1p1/2 = 3.9 MeV
- The largest M-scheme dimension among calculated nuclei was 211,833 (for 55Mn, N=30)

## Context

This work extends the application of the Kuo-Brown G-matrix interaction (Kuo and Brown, 1968) from lighter pf-shell nuclei (A ≤ 44) to the middle of the pf-shell (A=49-58), where sd-shell excitations are expected to be minimal. It improves upon earlier calculations by Horie and Ogawa (which used only k=0 configurations and could not reproduce states like the 2_3^+ and 2_4^+ in 56Fe) and by Oberlechner and Richert (who used a different realistic interaction with limited success). The results enable systematic testing of microscopic effective interactions without phenomenological adjustment and provide wavefunctions for interpreting (e,e') and (p,p') excitation data to low-lying 2^+ states.
