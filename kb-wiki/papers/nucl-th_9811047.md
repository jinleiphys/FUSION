# Effective Theory for the non-relativistic three-body System

> We discuss renormalization of the non-relativistic three-body problem with short-range forces. The problem becomes non-perturbative at momenta of the order of the inverse of the two-body scattering length, and an infinite number of graphs must be summed. This summation leads to a cutoff dependence that does not appear in any order in perturbation theory. We argue that this cutoff dependence can be absorbed in a single three-body counterterm and compute the running of the three-body force with the cutoff.

# Effective Theory for the non-relativistic three-body System

## Key claim
The non-relativistic three-body problem with short-range forces and a large two-body scattering length requires a single three-body counterterm at leading order to absorb the cutoff dependence that arises from summing an infinite number of diagrams, even though each individual leading-order three-body diagram is ultraviolet finite.

## Method
The authors construct an effective field theory for non-relativistic bosons using a Lagrangian with a dummy field for two-boson states, then derive an integral equation for particle/bound-state scattering that sums all leading-order diagrams with resummed two-body interactions. They analyze the asymptotic solution of this integral equation analytically and numerically, and determine the running of the three-body force with the cutoff by requiring cutoff independence of the on-shell scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The two-body scattering length \(a_2\) is much larger than the range \(R\) of the interaction, making the expansion non-perturbative at momenta of order \(1/a_2\).
- For the numerical illustration, the three-body scattering length is set to \(a_3 = 1.56 a_2\).
- The effective range for particle/bound-state scattering is predicted as \(r_3 = 0.57 a_2\).
- For the \(^4\)He system with \(a_2 = 124.7\) Å and \(a_3 = 195\) Å from a phenomenological potential, the effective range is \(r_3 = 71\) Å.
- The shallowest bound state has a cutoff-independent binding energy of \(B_3 = 1.5 / M a_2^2\); for the \(^4\)He system this gives an excited trimer state at \(B_3 = 1.2\) mK.
- The three-body force \(H(\Lambda)\) is given analytically by \(H(\Lambda)= -\frac{\sin(s_0\ln({\Lambda}/{\Lambda_\star})-{\rm arctg}(1/s_0))}{\sin(s_0 \ln({\Lambda}/{\Lambda_\star})+{\rm arctg}(1/s_0))}\) and agrees with numerical solutions to high accuracy.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to three-body systems with large scattering lengths, building on prior developments in the two-body case (the "edict" references) and on the known Thomas and Efimov effects that show cutoff sensitivity in bosonic three-body systems. The results enable systematic EFT calculations for three-nucleon systems and for \(^4\)He trimers, and the authors note that similar integral equations make the arguments relevant for three-fermion systems with internal quantum numbers as well (referencing work by Bedaque, van Kolck, and others). The specific numerical values for \(^4\)He are taken from a phenomenological helium-helium potential that gives the correct dimer binding energy.
