# Structure of the proton drip line nucleus $^{17}$F

> The odd $A$ proton drip line nucleus $^{17}$F has been studied in a semi-microscopic model which couples proton quasiparticle motion to the vibrational motion of the neighbouring even-even $^{18}$Ne core. The experimentally observed low lying excitation spectrum, electric quadrupole moment, magnetic dipole moment, $B(E1)$ and $B(E2)$ values have been fairly well reproducd. The calculated rms radius of the first excited state is well reproduced and is found to be larger than that of the ground state which agrees with the experimental observation.

# Structure of the proton drip line nucleus \(^{17}\)F

## Key claim

The quasiparticle-core coupling model, using Woods-Saxon wave functions expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis (WSHO), can reproduce the experimental observables of \(^{17}\)F without sophisticated calculations, but the calculated root-mean-square (rms) radius of the ground state (4.83 fm) overestimates the experimental value (3.78 fm), even though the rms radius of the first excited state (5.34 fm) agrees well with experiment (5.33 fm).

## Method

The authors coupled the motion of a proton quasiparticle to the collective vibrational motion of the even-even \(^{18}\)Ne core, restricting the calculation to two quadrupole phonon space and setting the octupole interaction strength to zero. Single-particle wave functions were generated from a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters adjusted to reproduce the experimental binding energies of the \(1d_{5/2}\) (0.600 MeV) and \(2s_{1/2}\) (0.105 MeV) states, and the unbound \(1d_{3/2}\) state was treated by expanding the Woods-Saxon wave function in a harmonic oscillator basis with 10 nodal quantum numbers. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a basis of core phonon states coupled to quasiparticle orbitals (\(1d_{5/2}\), \(2s_{1/2}\), \(1d_{3/2}\) for positive parity; \(1p_{3/2}\), \(1p_{1/2}\) for negative parity), with the quadrupole interaction strength \(X_2 = 0.5\) and quasiparticle energies treated as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Ground state (\(5/2^+\)) rms radius: calculated 4.83 fm, experimental 3.78 fm
- First excited state (\(1/2^+\)) rms radius: calculated 5.34 fm, experimental 5.33 fm
- Ground state electric quadrupole moment: calculated 0.108 eb, experimental 0.10 \(\pm\) 0.02 eb
- Ground state magnetic dipole moment: calculated 4.7080 \(\mu_N\), experimental 4.72130 \(\pm\) 0.00025 \(\mu_N\)
- \(B(E2)\) for \(1/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+\) transition: calculated 21.9 W.u., experimental 25.0 \(\pm\) 0.5 W.u.
- \(B(E1)\) for \(1/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^+\) transition: calculated 0.2 \(\times\) 10\(^{-3}\) W.u., experimental (1.5 \(\pm\) 0.3) \(\times\) 10\(^{-3}\) W.u.
- \(B(E1)\) for \(5/2^- \rightarrow 5/2^+\) transition: calculated 6.1 \(\times\) 10\(^{-3}\) W.u., experimental (4.3 \(\pm\) 0.8) \(\times\) 10\(^{-3}\) W.u.
- Binding energy of last proton in ground state: 0.600 MeV; in first excited state: 0.105 MeV
- First \(2^+\) state of \(^{18}\)Ne core at 1.887 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle-core coupling model previously applied by the same authors to one-neutron halo nuclei \(^{11}\)Be and \(^{19}\)O (Bhattacharya and Krishan, 1997) to the proton-rich nucleus \(^{17}\)F, which has a last proton binding energy of only 0.60 MeV and is a candidate for a one-proton halo. The study contrasts with earlier calculations by Furnstahl and Price (1989) using relativistic Hartree theory (which gave rms charge radius 2.76 fm and magnetic moment 4.87 \(\mu_N\)) and by Lombard (1990) using density functional methods (which gave total binding energy 126.6 MeV), neither of which provided detailed spectroscopic information. The results enable further exploration of proton halo phenomena and suggest that simple core-coupling models can describe drip-line nuclei without requiring more sophisticated many-body calculations.
