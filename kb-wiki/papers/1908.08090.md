# Skyrme Functional with Tensor Terms from ab initio Calculations: Results for the Spin-Orbit Splittings

> A new Skyrme functional including tensor terms is presented. The tensor terms have been determined by fitting the results of relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) studies on neutron-proton drops. Unlike all previous studies, where the tensor terms were usually determined by fitting to experimental data of single-particle levels, the pseudodata calculated by RBHF does not contain beyond mean-field effect such as the particle-vibration coupling and, therefore, can provide information on the tensor term without ambiguities. The obtained new functional, named SAMi-T, can describe well ground-state properties such as binding energies, radii, spin-orbit splittings and, at the same time, the excited state properties such as those of the Giant Monopole Resonance (GMR), Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR), Gamow-Teller Resonance (GTR), and Spin-Dipole Resonance (SDR).

## Key claim

The tensor terms in the new Skyrme functional SAMi-T are determined by fitting to the *relative change* of spin-orbit splittings in neutron-proton drops from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations, rather than to absolute values; this avoids ambiguities from beyond-mean-field effects like particle-vibration coupling that contaminate fits to experimental single-particle levels.

## Method

The tensor parameters α and β in the spin-orbit potential were constrained by fitting the evolution of spin-orbit splittings in neutron-proton drops calculated with RBHF theory using the Bonn A interaction, which contains no beyond-mean-field effects. The remaining parameters of the functional followed the fitting protocol of the existing SAMi functional, with ground-state properties (binding energies, charge radii) and excited-state properties (GMR, GDR, GTR, SDR) also included in the fit. The resulting functional, SAMi-T, was then tested against experimental spin-orbit splittings in finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies and charge radii for several selected doubly magic nuclei are reproduced with an accuracy of 1%.
- For 16O and 40Ca, SAMi-T gives proton spin-orbit splittings that agree well with experimental data, whereas SAMi gives slightly smaller values.
- For 90Zr and 208Pb, SAMi-T and SAMi give similar spin-orbit splittings.
- For 48Ca, 56Ni, 100Sn, and 132Sn, both SAMi-T and SAMi give spin-orbit splittings smaller than experimental data; for 90Zr both are larger.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Shen (2016, 2017, 2018) who used RBHF calculations on neutron drops to isolate tensor effects, and further develops the neutron-proton drop pseudodata of Shen (2018c). It contrasts with earlier studies such as Otsuka (2005) and Brown (2006) that fitted tensor terms to experimental single-particle levels, where particle-vibration coupling (Colo 1994, Niu 2012, Litvinova 2011) obscures the tensor signal. The SAMi-T functional enables future studies of spin-orbit splittings and collective excitations across the nuclear chart with tensor terms constrained by ab initio theory rather than phenomenology.
