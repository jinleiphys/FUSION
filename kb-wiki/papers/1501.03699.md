# Modeling the pion Generalized Parton Distribution

> We compute the pion Generalized Parton Distribution (GPD) in a valence dressed quarks approach. We model the Mellin moments of the GPD using Ansätze for Green functions inspired by the numerical solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger Equations (DSE) and the Bethe-Salpeter Equation (BSE). Then, the GPD is reconstructed from its Mellin moment using the Double Distribution (DD) formalism. The agreement with available experimental data is very good.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model of the pion Generalized Parton Distribution (GPD) that, while agreeing well with available experimental data, is based on an algebraic approximation that does not fully satisfy the Axial-Vector Ward Takahashi Identities, and therefore fails to reproduce the soft pion theorem linking the GPD to the pion Parton Distribution Amplitude.

## Method

The author computes the Mellin moments of the pion GPD using a triangle diagram approximation with quark propagators and pion vertices modeled by algebraic Ansätze inspired by numerical solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger Equations (DSE) and Bethe-Salpeter Equation (BSE). The GPD is then reconstructed from its Mellin moments using the Double Distribution (DD) formalism via a Radon transform. The model parameters include an effective quark mass M and a parameter nu fixed to 1 to recover the asymptotic pion distribution amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The pion form factor (Mellin moment of order 0 of the GPD) is compared with experimental data from Amendolia (1986) and Huber (2008); the agreement is described as "very encouraging" for the algebraic model.
- The Parton Distribution Function (PDF) from the model is compared with a PDF fit to data, both before and after evolution at low scale (shown in right panel of Fig. 2).
- The parameter nu is fixed to 1, a value which allows recovery of the pion asymptotic distribution amplitude as in Chang (2013).
- The model reproduces the perturbative behavior of the PDF at large x (shown in right panel of Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to the pion Parton Distribution Amplitude by Chang (2013) to the case of the pion GPD. The model uses the Double Distribution formalism introduced by Mueller (1998), Radyushkin (1998), and others to ensure polynomiality of the Mellin moments. The author notes that the triangle diagram approximation slightly breaks the expected symmetry of the PDF with respect to x=1/2, and that upgrading the model with full numerical solutions of the DSE-BSE would satisfy the Axial-Vector Ward Takahashi Identities and thus the soft pion theorem from Polyakov (1998). This work enables future development of a proton GPD based on a quark-diquark model.
