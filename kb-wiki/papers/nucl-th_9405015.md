# Large Angle K+-Deuteron Scattering as a Probe for Meson Exchange Effects

> Meson exchange contributions have been suggested as one class of medium effects which might explain the continuing discrepancy between experimental results and theoretical predictions for K+-nucleus scattering. These exchange effects are negligible for near-forward K+-deuteron scattering because of the large size of the deuteron, but at large angles they might be expected to be significant compared to the weak double scattering. Detailed calculations, however, show that the quadrupole and magnetic contributions from single scattering are large enough to dominate both double scattering and exchange contributions even at large momentum transfers. The exchange contributions become significant only if the target deuteron is highly aligned so that the single scattering contributions are greatly reduced.

## Key claim

Meson exchange contributions to K+-deuteron scattering become significant only if the target deuteron is highly aligned so that the single scattering contributions are greatly reduced; for unpolarized deuterons, quadrupole and magnetic contributions from single scattering dominate both double scattering and exchange contributions even at large momentum transfers.

## Method

The author used Glauber theory to calculate single scattering, elastic double scattering, and pion exchange contributions to the K+-deuteron scattering amplitude at a laboratory momentum of 970 MeV/c. The calculations incorporated the D-wave component of the deuteron wave function, spin-dependent K+-nucleon amplitudes from Hashimoto (1984), and deuteron form factors from the Paris potential fits of Lacombe et al. (1981). The pion exchange contribution was estimated using the prescription of Jiang and Koltun (1992), which includes both soft-pion and K*(892) resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory momentum: 970 MeV/c for all calculations
- Exchange contribution is smaller than double scattering by a factor of roughly five at large momentum transfers
- Exchange contribution adds only about 40% to the differential cross section at the largest momentum transfers shown for unpolarized deuterons
- For aligned deuterons, cancellation between spherical and quadrupole contributions occurs near -t = 8.5 fm^-2
- Exchange contributions become significant above -t = 8 fm^-2, where the differential cross section has decreased by more than two orders of magnitude from its forward value
- For alignment -2 along the normal to the scattering plane, beyond the dip region the cross section is increased by a factor of about 2 due to exchange contributions

## Context

This work extends the ongoing investigation of medium effects in K+-nucleus scattering, where discrepancies between experimental results and theoretical predictions have been observed by Mardor et al. (1990) and Krauss et al. (1992). The paper builds on prior work by Siegel, Kaufmann and Gibbs (1984) on K+-nucleus multiple scattering, and on the pion-deuteron scattering formalism of Michael and Wilkin (1969). The results suggest that testing meson exchange contributions requires extremely challenging experimental conditions: momentum transfers above 8 fm^-2, nearly perfect deuteron alignment, and high angular resolution to resolve the narrow dip in the differential cross section where sensitivity is greatest.
