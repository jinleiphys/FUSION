# Directed flow induced by electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions

> Strong electromagnetic fields are expected to be generated in off-central relativistic heavy ion collisions, which can induce a splitting of the directed flow of charged particles and anti-particles ($Δv_1$). Such a splitting manifests even for neutral charmed mesons pairs ($D^0,\bar{D}^0$), hence being a direct probe of the formation of deconfined phase with charm quarks as degree of freedom. In the limit of large $p_T$ and weak interaction with the QGP, a formula of $Δv_1(p_T,y_z)$ of charged particles and anti-particles as a function of $p_T$ and rapidity $y_z$ can be obtained, which is found to be related to the spectra of charged particles and the integrated effect of the Lorentz force. This formula is expected to be valid to heavy quarks and leptons at high $p_T$, where the modification to their equations of motion due to the interaction with both QGP and electromagnetic fields is small, and should have a general application. We also proposed a measurement of $Δv_1(p_T,y_z)$ of leptons from $Z^0$ decay and its correlation to that of $D$ mesons, which would be a strong probe determining whether the large splitting measured in experiments has the electromagnetic origin.

# Directed flow induced by electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The authors derive a compact formula for the splitting of directed flow (Δv₁) of charged particles and anti-particles induced by electromagnetic fields in off-central heavy ion collisions, which they argue is robust for heavy quarks and leptons at high transverse momentum (p_T) where interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) are weak. The formula is expected to be valid only in the limit of large p_T and weak interaction with the QGP, with the approximation holding when momentum shifts from electromagnetic fields (much smaller than 1 GeV) are small compared to initial momenta.

## Method
The authors analytically derive the directed flow v₁ from a distribution function modified by electromagnetic fields, using a shift in transverse momentum (Δ_x, Δ_y) and a transition function T that represents the integrated effect of the Lorentz force. They then obtain a simplified expression for Δv₁ proportional to -|q| (∂ ln f / ∂ p_T) times an integral over rapidity involving the time evolution of the magnetic field B(τ). To test the effect of QGP interactions, they numerically solve the Fokker-Planck equation with interaction strength tuned to reproduce experimental R_AA and v₂ of D mesons at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- The momentum shifts from electromagnetic fields are "much smaller than 1 GeV in most configurations of e.m. fields"
- For charged leptons from Z⁰ decay, Δv₁(l) shows a jump around 45 GeV/c, and its magnitude is always smaller than Δv₁(D⁰)
- For case EM1 (which reproduces experimental measurements), dΔv₁^D/dη is positive with magnitude "order two larger" than case EM2 (calculated using constant medium conductivity in the lower limit of LQCD calculations)
- The interaction with QGP damps dΔv₁^D/dη only at p_T < 2 GeV, with negligible suppression at high p_T

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analytical study (Sun, 2020) on directed flow induced by electromagnetic fields, building on numerical studies by Gursoy (2014), Das (2016), and Chatterjee (2018). The paper provides a general formula applicable to heavy quarks and leptons, and proposes a correlation measurement between Δv₁ of leptons from Z⁰ decay and that of D mesons as a test for whether the large splitting measured in experiments at 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions (Acharya, 2019) has an electromagnetic origin. The work enables experimental discrimination between electromagnetic and other origins of directed flow splitting in heavy ion collisions.
