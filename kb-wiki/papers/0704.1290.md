# Isospin diffusion in thermal AdS/CFT with flavor

> We study the gauge/gravity dual of a finite temperature field theory at finite isospin chemical potential by considering a probe of two coincident D7-branes embedded in the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background. The isospin chemical potential is obtained by giving a vev to the time component of the non-Abelian gauge field on the brane. The fluctuations of the non-Abelian gauge field on the brane are dual to the SU(2) flavor current in the field theory. For the embedding corresponding to vanishing quark mass, we calculate all Green functions corresponding to the components of the flavor current correlator. We discuss the physical properties of these Green functions, which go beyond linear response theory. In particular, we show that the isospin chemical potential leads to a frequency-dependent isospin diffusion coefficient.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a finite isospin chemical potential in a thermal gauge theory dual to a probe D7-brane system leads to a frequency-dependent isospin diffusion coefficient \(D(\omega) \propto \frac{1}{T} \sqrt{\omega/\mu}\), a nonlinear effect beyond linear response theory, valid under the approximation \(\omega < \mu < T\).

## Method

The authors embed two coincident D7-brane probes in the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background (Lorentzian signature) and introduce a constant isospin chemical potential \(\mu\) via a non-Abelian gauge field background \(A_0^3 = \mu\) on the branes, for the massless quark embedding. They derive the Dirac-Born-Infeld action to second order in \(\alpha'\), obtain the linearized equations of motion for fluctuations of the SU(2) gauge field, decouple the flavor components using the combinations \(X_i = A_i^1 + i A_i^2\) and \(\widetilde X_i = A_i^1 - i A_i^2\), and solve them analytically in the hydrodynamic limit using a power series ansatz in \(\omega^{1/2}\) and \(q^2\) with incoming wave boundary conditions at the horizon.

## Key numbers

* The frequency-dependent diffusion coefficient is found as \(D(\omega) \propto \frac{1}{T} \sqrt{\omega/\mu}\) for small frequencies, with \(\omega < \mu < T\).
* The dimensionless quantities are defined as \(\wn = \omega/(2\pi T)\), \(\qn = q/(2\pi T)\), \(\mn = \mu/(2\pi T)\).
* The indices for the near-horizon solutions are \(\beta = \pm \frac{i\wn}{2}\sqrt{1 - \frac{2\mn}{\wn}}\) and \(\widetilde\beta = \pm \frac{i\wn}{2}\sqrt{1 + \frac{2\mn}{\wn}}\), which in the approximation \(\wn < \mn\) become \(\beta \approx \mp \sqrt{\wn\mn/2}\) and \(\widetilde\beta \approx \pm i\sqrt{\wn\mn/2}\).
* The approximation used requires \(\qn, \wn < 1\) (hydrodynamic limit).

## Context

This work extends the gauge/gravity dual approach to finite-temperature field theories with flavor by combining the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background (used by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2002) for R-current correlators) with the non-Abelian probe brane method of Apreda, Erdmenger, Evans, and Guralnik (2005) for introducing an isospin chemical potential. The frequency-dependent diffusion coefficient qualitatively agrees with results from quantum liquid theory (PhysRevLett and Rabani for liquid para-hydrogen) and is relevant for isospin diffusion measured in heavy-ion reactions (Liu (2006), Tsang (2003)). This work enables future studies of phase diagrams with isospin chemical potential in the gauge/gravity dual context.
