## Key claim
The paper claims that the $\Xi(2030)$ baryon can be interpreted as a $P$-wave $\bar{K}^{*}\Sigma$ molecular state with quantum numbers $I(J^{P})=1/2(5/2^{+})$, and that the $\Xi(2120)$ may correspond to one of three other bound states ($1/2(1/2^{-})$, $1/2(3/2^{-})$, or $1/2(3/2^{+})$) produced from the same interaction, but the assignment for $\Xi(2120)$ remains ambiguous due to scarce experimental information on its spin and parity.

## Method
The authors studied the $\bar{K}^{*}\Sigma$ interaction using a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach with one-boson-exchange potentials. They considered exchanges of vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$) and pseudoscalar mesons ($\pi$, $\eta$), with coupling constants determined by SU(3) flavor symmetry. Poles of the scattering amplitude were searched for in the complex energy plane to identify dynamically generated molecular states for spin parities up to $J=5/2$.

## Key numbers
- The $\Xi(2030)$ has an experimental mass of $2025\pm5$ MeV and a width of $20^{+15}_{-5}$ MeV (PDG).
- A bound state with $I(J^{P})=1/2(5/2^{+})$ is produced at a cutoff $\Lambda=1.3$ GeV, with a mass that can reach 2030 MeV as the cutoff increases.
- Bound states with $I(J^{P})=1/2(1/2^{-})$, $1/2(3/2^{-})$, and $1/2(3/2^{+})$ are found with cutoffs around 1.0 to 1.3 GeV, producing masses in the range 2.03 to 2.08 GeV.
- Two bound states with isospin $3/2$ and spin parities $1/2^{-}$ and $1/2^{+}$ are found with cutoffs about 1.0 GeV, producing masses around 2.04 to 2.08 GeV.
- The $\bar{K}^{*}\Sigma$ threshold is approximately 2.09 GeV (implied by the bound state masses being below this value).

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of exotic baryons from the heavy flavor sector (e.g., the LHCb pentaquark states $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ interpreted as $\bar{D}^{*}\Sigma_c$ molecules by He (2016)) to the light flavor sector. It builds on prior studies of $\bar{K}N$ molecular states for the $\Lambda(1405)$ (Dalitz, 1959) and of $\Sigma^{*}K$ and $\Sigma K^{*}$ molecules for nucleon resonances $N(1875)$ and $N(2100)$ (He, 2015, 2017). The results are consistent with chiral unitary approach calculations by Oset (2009) and Khemchandani (2016), which also found $\bar{K}^{*}\Sigma$ molecular states near 2.05 GeV. The study enables future experimental searches for double-strangeness baryons at JLab, J-PARC, and PANDA, and suggests a pattern analogous to the $P_c$ states where a $P$-wave $5/2^{+}$ state and an $S$-wave $3/2^{-}$ state arise from the same vector-meson-baryon interaction.
