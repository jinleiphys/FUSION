# Constraining Low-Energy Proton Capture on Beryllium-7 through Charge Radius Measurements

> In this paper, we point out that a measurement of the charge radius of Boron-8 provides indirect access to the S-factor for radiative proton capture on Beryllium-7 at low energies. We use leading-order halo effective field theory to explore this correlation and we give a relation between the charge radius and the S-factor. Furthermore, we present important technical aspects relevant to the renormalization of pointlike P-wave interactions in the presence of a repulsive Coulomb interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the charge radius of Boron-8 and the astrophysical S-factor for radiative proton capture on Beryllium-7 are correlated at leading order in halo effective field theory, meaning a precise measurement of the Boron-8 charge radius can provide indirect access to the S-factor at low energies without requiring a short-distance counterterm at this order.

## Method

The authors use leading-order halo effective field theory with explicit inclusion of the proton, the Beryllium-7 ground-state core, and its first excited state as degrees of freedom, treating the Coulomb interaction nonperturbatively via the Coulomb Green's function. They match the low-energy coupling constants to the proton separation energy of Boron-8 and to asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from three sources: variational Monte Carlo calculations by Nollett and Wiringa, NCSM/RGM calculations by Navratil et al., and a proton-transfer experiment by Tabacaru et al. The charge form factor is then calculated from tree-level and loop diagrams, and the charge radius is extracted from its expansion in momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The one-proton separation energy of Boron-8 is B = 0.1375 MeV, and the first excited state energy of the Beryllium-7 core is E* = 0.4291 MeV.
- The currently accepted threshold S-factor is S(0) = (20.8 ± 0.7 (expt.) ± 1.4 (theor.)) eV b.
- The effective range r1 obtained from Nollett ANCs is -(60 ± 4) MeV; from Navratil ANCs, -63 MeV; from Tabacaru ANCs, -(69 ± 13) MeV.
- The charge radius of Boron-8 predicted using Nollett ANCs is (2.56 ± 0.08 fm)^2; using Navratil ANCs, (2.50 fm)^2; using Tabacaru ANCs, (2.41 ± 0.18 fm)^2.

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT treatment of the Beryllium-7-proton system previously performed by Zhang, Nollett and Phillips (2014), who calculated the S-factor at leading order and obtained S(0) = (18.2 ± 1.2 (ANC only)) eV b. The present paper differs by focusing on the correlation between the charge radius and the S-factor, showing that a measurement of the Boron-8 charge radius via modern isotope shift techniques can provide independent constraints on the S-factor. This enables a new indirect approach to determining the low-energy proton capture cross section on Beryllium-7, which is critical for understanding the high-energy solar neutrino flux.
