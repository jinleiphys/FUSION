# Non-nucleonic degrees of freedom and the spin structure of the deuteron

> Electro-disintegration of the deuteron at large $Q^2$ currently represents on of the most promising reactions which allows to probe the bound nuclear state at internal momenta comparable to the rest mass of the nucleon. Large internal momentum in this case makes non-nuncleonic states energetically more feasible and the question that we address is what are the signatures that will indicate the existence of such states in the ground state of the nuclear wave function. To probe such states we developed a light-front formalism for relativistic description of a composite pseudo-vector system in which emerging proton and neutron are observed in electro-disintegration reaction. In leading high energy approximation our calculations show the possibility of the existence of a new ``incomplete" P-state-like structure in the deuteron at extremely large internal momenta. The incompleteness of the observed P-state violates the angular condition for the momentum distribution, which can happen only if the deuteron contains non-nucleonic structures, such as $ΔΔ$, $N^*N$ or hidden color components. Because such states have distinctive angular momentum ($l=1$) they significantly modify the polarization properties of the deuteron wave function. As a result in addition to angular anisotropy of the LF momentum distribution of the nucleon in the deuteron one predicts strong modification of the tensor polarization asymmetry of the deuteron beyond the S- and D- wave predictions at large internal momenta in the deuteron.

## Key claim
The paper claims that at extremely large internal momenta (comparable to the nucleon rest mass), the deuteron may exhibit a new "incomplete" P-state-like structure in its light-front wave function, which violates the angular condition for the momentum distribution. This violation can only occur if the deuteron contains non-nucleonic components such as ΔΔ, N*N, or hidden color states, and the claim is conditional on the leading high-energy approximation used in the calculations.

## Method
The author developed a light-front (LF) formalism for the relativistic description of the deuteron as a composite pseudo-vector system, applied to the electro-disintegration reaction e + d → e' + p + n at large momentum transfer Q². The vertex function for the d → pn transition was constructed with six scalar functions (Γ₁ through Γ₆), and a high-energy approximation was used to identify leading-order terms in the small parameter Δ⁻/(2m_N). The resulting LF wave function was expressed through two-component spinors, revealing S-wave, D-wave, and a new P-wave-like structure arising from the Γ₅ term, with the P-wave contributing only for deuteron helicities λ_d = ±1.

## Key numbers
- The P-wave radial function P(k) is proportional to k³/m_N³, giving an extra k²/m_N² factor beyond the standard k^(l=1)/m_N term that characterizes radial P-waves.
- The new P-wave contribution to the LF momentum distribution is proportional to k_⊥²/k² times P²(k), introducing explicit dependence on the transverse momentum component.
- The non-nucleonic component was modeled assuming 1% contribution to total normalization from the P-wave, consistent with an experimental estimate of 0.7% from Ref. [deltadelta].
- Measurable angular dependence in the LF momentum distribution is expected at k ≳ 1 GeV/c, with inelastic transitions corresponding to non-nucleonic components taking place at k ≳ 800 MeV/c.
- The tensor polarization asymmetry shows qualitative differences from S- and D-wave predictions already at k ≈ 800 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the light-front approach to deuteron structure previously developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981) and others, and builds on the author's own earlier work (Sargsian, 2022). The predictions are motivated by recent Jefferson Lab measurements (Hall C, 2020) that reached k ∼ 1 GeV/c and qualitatively disagreed with conventional deuteron wave functions for k ≳ 800 MeV/c. If confirmed, the results would motivate theoretical modeling of non-nucleonic components such as ΔΔ, N*N, or hidden-color N_cN_c states, and would advance understanding of high-density nuclear matter and quark-hadron transitions.
