# Emergence of $^4$H $J^π=1^-$ resonance in contact theories

> We obtain the $s$- and $p$-wave low-energy scattering parameters for n$^3$H elastic scattering and the position of the $^4$H $J^π=1^-$ resonance using the pionless effective field theory at leading order. Results are extracted with three numerical techniques: confining the system in a harmonic oscillator trap, solving the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space, and using an effective two-body cluster approach. The renormalization of the theory for the relevant amplitudes is assessed in a cutoff-regulator range between $1\,\text{fm}^{-1}$ and $10\,\text{fm}^{-1}$. Most remarkably, we find a cutoff-stable/RG-invariant resonance in the $^4$H $J^π=1^-$ system. This $p$-wave resonance is a universal consequence of a shallow two-body state and the introduction of a three-body $s$-wave scale set by the triton binding energy. The stabilization of a resonant state in a few-fermion system through pure contact interactions has a significant consequence for the powercounting of the pionless theory. Specifically, it suggests the appearance of similar resonant states also in larger nuclei, like 16-oxygen, in which the theory's leading order does not predict stable states. Those resonances would provide a starting state to be moved to the correct physical position by the perturbative insertion of sub-leading orders, possibly resolving the discrepancy between data and contact EFT.

# Emergence of $^4$H $J^\pi=1^-$ resonance in contact theories

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a cutoff-stable $p$-wave resonance in the $^4$H $J^\pi=1^-$ system emerges from purely $s$-wave contact interactions in leading-order pionless effective field theory, provided a three-body $s$-wave scale (set by the triton binding energy) is introduced. This resonance is a universal consequence of a shallow two-body state combined with that three-body scale, and its existence suggests that similar resonant states may appear in larger $p$-shell nuclei like $^{16}$O, potentially resolving the discrepancy between contact EFT predictions and experimental data for those systems.

## Method

The authors used leading-order pionless effective field theory (EFT($\not{\pi}$)) with SU(4)-symmetric contact two-body and three-body interactions, regularized with a Gaussian cutoff. They employed three numerical techniques: solving the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space, confining the system in a harmonic oscillator trap with the stochastic variational method, and using a resonating group method (RGM) effective two-body cluster approach. The $s$- and $p$-wave low-energy scattering parameters for n$^3$H elastic scattering were extracted via effective range expansion, and the $^4$H $J^\pi=1^-$ resonance pole was obtained directly from the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with purely outgoing wave boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- $s$-wave scattering length in the contact limit ($\Lambda \to \infty$) for the "nuclear" case: $a_0(\infty) = 3.05(1)$ fm
- $s$-wave effective range in the contact limit for the "nuclear" case: $r_0(\infty) = 1.59(7)$ fm
- $p$-wave scattering volume in the contact limit for the "nuclear" case: $a_1(\infty) = -9.9(6)$ fm$^3$
- $p$-wave effective range in the contact limit for the "nuclear" case: $r_1(\infty) = 1.3(1)$ fm$^{-1}$
- $s$-wave scattering length in the contact limit for the "unitary" case: $a_0(\infty) = 2.27(1)$ fm
- $p$-wave scattering volume in the contact limit for the "unitary" case: $a_1(\infty) = -5.1(1)$ fm$^3$
- Resonance pole position from Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for $\Lambda \gtrsim 6$ fm$^{-1}$: $E_{\rm res} = -8.7 - i3.1$ MeV (relative to the four-body energy scale; the complex n$^3$H momentum stabilizes at $|k_{\rm res}| \approx 0.35$ fm$^{-1} \simeq 0.5 m_\pi$)
- The cutoff-regulator range studied: $1$ fm$^{-1}$ to $10$ fm$^{-1}$
- Triton binding energy used to fit the three-body LEC: $B_3 = 8.48$ MeV
- Deuteron binding energy used for the "nuclear" two-body parametrization: $B_2 = 2.22$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of resonances in few-body systems with contact interactions, including Efimov resonances in bosonic systems (Deltuva, 2012) and $s$-shell resonances with negative effective range (Habashi, 2020). It directly addresses the open question of whether leading-order pionless EFT supports poles in $p$-shell nuclei, following earlier findings that $^6$Li and other $p$-shell nuclei clusterize into unbound $s$-shell subcomponents at LO (Contessi, 2017; Schafer, 2020). The existence of a cutoff-stable $p$-wave resonance in $^4$H provides a starting state that could be moved to the correct physical position by perturbative insertion of sub-leading orders, potentially resolving the discrepancy between contact EFT and experimental data for larger nuclei. The results also contrast with a study of $^3$n (Dietz, 2021) which found no $p$-wave resonances, consistent with the absence of a finite scale to fix the resonance position.
