# Pion Compton scattering and bremsstrahlung

> The pion-polarizability functions are structure functions of pion-Compton scattering. They can be assessed in high-energy pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung reactions, $π^- +A\toπ^- +γ+A$. We present numerical expectations for pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung cross sections in the Coulomb region, i.e. the small-angle region where the nuclear scattering is dominated by the Coulomb interaction. We investigate the prospects of measuring the polarizability functions for pion-Compton c.m. energies from threshold up to 1 GeV. A meson-exchange model is used for the pion-Compton amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper presents numerical expectations for pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung cross sections in the Coulomb region, showing that pion polarizabilities can be extracted from such measurements, but only if the pion-Compton c.m. energy is kept well below the rho-meson mass for threshold polarizabilities, or if specific phase-space regions are isolated for higher energies.

## Method

The authors use a meson-exchange model for the pion-Compton amplitude, including contributions from sigma, rho, and a1 meson exchanges, to compute the bremsstrahlung cross section for the reaction pi- + A -> pi- + gamma + A. They integrate the cross-section distribution over the transverse momenta of the final-state photon and over the Coulomb peak factor, deriving analytic expressions for the distribution functions in both the low-mass-Compton region (near threshold) and the rho-mass-Compton region.

## Key numbers

- The threshold values of the polarizability functions in the meson-exchange model are lambda_1 = 0.0006 and lambda_2 = -0.0131, where lambda_1 is proportional to (alpha_pi + beta_pi) and lambda_2 is proportional to beta_pi.
- For the COMPASS experiment, the cut on transverse momenta is q_bot_cut = 15 MeV/c.
- At a Compton mass equal to the rho mass and an incident pion energy E_1 = 190 GeV, the minimum longitudinal momentum transfer at the kinematic endpoint is q_min = 1.5 MeV/c.
- The maximal x-value for a cut at sqrt(s_max) = 3.75 m_pi is x_max = 0.93.
- The maximal photon transverse momentum for sqrt(s) = 2 m_pi is q_2_bot_max = 3 m_pi / 4.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Gal'perin et al. and the experimental work of Antipov et al., who showed that pion polarizabilities could be determined from bremsstrahlung reactions. The COMPASS experiment at CERN is a refinement of the Antipov experiment. The paper provides the theoretical basis for extracting the generalized polarizability functions lambda_1 and lambda_2 from pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung measurements, enabling future experiments to probe pion polarizabilities for Compton c.m. energies from threshold up to 1 GeV.
