# Compact stars in Rastall gravity: hydrostatic equilibrium and radial pulsations

> Within the context of Rastall gravity, we investigate the hydrostatic equilibrium and dynamical stability against radial pulsations of compact stars, where a free parameter $β$ measures the deviations from General Relativity (GR). We derive both the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and the Sturm-Liouville differential equation governing the adiabatic radial oscillations. Such equations are solved numerically in order to obtain the compact-star properties for two realistic equations of state (EoSs). For hadronic matter, the fundamental mode frequency $ω_0$ becomes unstable almost at the critical central energy density corresponding to the maximum gravitational mass. However, for quark matter, where larger values of $\vertβ\vert$ are required to observe appreciable changes in the mass-radius diagram, there exist stable stars after the maximum-mass configuration for negative values of $β$. Using an independent analysis, our results reveal that the emergence of a cusp can be used as a criterion to indicate the onset of instability when the binding energy is plotted as a function of the proper mass. Specifically, we find that the central-density value where the binding energy is a minimum corresponds precisely to $ω_0^2 =0$.

## Key claim

In Rastall gravity, the standard criterion that the maximum gravitational mass on the mass-central density curve coincides with the onset of radial instability (zero fundamental mode frequency) fails for quark matter; instead, for negative values of the Rastall parameter β, stable configurations exist beyond the maximum-mass point, and the binding energy minimum (cusp) provides a reliable independent indicator of instability onset.

## Method

The authors derived the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for hydrostatic equilibrium in Rastall gravity, where a free parameter β measures deviations from General Relativity (GR). They then derived the Sturm-Liouville equation governing adiabatic radial oscillations by perturbing the field equations and fluid variables to first order. These equations were solved numerically for two realistic equations of state: the MIT bag model for quark matter (bag constant B^(1/4) = 148 MeV) and the IU-FSU parameterization of the nonlinear Walecka model for hadronic matter, with the BPS equation of state for the outer crust.

## Key numbers

- For hadronic matter (IU-FSU EoS), the fundamental mode frequency ω₀ becomes unstable almost at the critical central energy density corresponding to the maximum gravitational mass, consistent with GR-like behavior.
- For quark matter (MIT bag model), larger values of |β| are required to observe appreciable changes in the mass-radius diagram compared to hadronic matter.
- The central-density value where the binding energy is a minimum corresponds precisely to ω₀² = 0, establishing the cusp in the binding energy versus proper mass curve as a stability indicator.
- The gravitational mass in Rastall gravity includes an extra effective mass term: m(r) = 4π∫ r̄² ρ₀(r̄) dr̄ - 4πβ∫ r̄² [ρ₀(r̄) - 3p₀(r̄)] dr̄, where β = (1-α)/(2(1-2α)) and α is the Rastall parameter (α=1 recovers GR).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of compact stars in Rastall gravity (Oliveira 2015, Xi 2020, Mota 2022) by providing the first dynamical stability analysis via radial pulsations in this theory, filling a gap identified by the authors. It builds on Chandrasekhar's foundational work on radial oscillations in GR (Chandrasekhar 1964) and parallels similar stability analyses in other modified gravity theories, such as scalar-tensor theories (Doneva 2013, Pretel 2024), 4D Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity (Doneva 2021), and f(R,T) theories (Pretel 2021). The finding that the binding energy cusp method works where the standard M(ρ_c) method fails aligns with results in anisotropic stars (Pretel 2020), hybrid stars (Pereira 2018), and charged strange quark stars (Arbanil 2015).
