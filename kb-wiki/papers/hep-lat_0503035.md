# Color confinement and dual superconductivity of the vacuum. IV

## Key claim

A finite size scaling analysis of the monopole condensation order parameter for N_f=2 QCD around the chiral point is consistent with a first order deconfining phase transition, in agreement with scaling properties of the specific heat studied in a previous paper. The analysis excludes an O(4) second order transition, supporting dual superconductivity as the mechanism for confinement even in the presence of dynamical quarks.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations with two degenerate flavors of Kogut-Susskind quarks using the standard gauge and fermion actions, with temporal size N_t=4 and spatial sizes L=12,16,20,24,32 at various quark masses. They measured the quantity rho = d/d beta ln(mu), where mu is the monopole creation operator, and performed a finite size scaling analysis comparing the scaling of rho with the critical exponents expected for a first order transition (nu=1/3) versus an O(4) second order transition (nu=0.75). Configuration updating used the standard Hybrid R algorithm.

## Key numbers

- Temporal lattice size fixed at N_t=4; spatial sizes L=12, 16, 20, 24, and 32 were used
- For the first order scaling test, the critical exponent nu=1/3 was assumed; for the O(4) test, nu=0.75 was assumed
- The quark mass value used corresponds to m_pi/m_rho approximately 0.505, as reported in Carmona (2002)
- The deconfining transition in the quenched theory occurs at beta approximately 1.01 for U(1) lattice gauge theory

## Context

This paper is the fourth in a series (Papers I, II, III by the same authors) developing and testing the hypothesis that confinement in QCD is produced by dual superconductivity of the vacuum via monopole condensation. The work extends previous numerical studies by Carmona (2002) that showed mu is nonzero below the transition line and zero above it for N_f=2 QCD. The finite size scaling analysis confirms results from a companion paper (CHIPAPER) that studied the specific heat and chiral susceptibility, finding disagreement with O(4) behavior and indications of a first order transition. This result is significant because it supports the existence of a genuine order parameter for confinement in full QCD, where no Z_3 symmetry exists, and enables further study of the deconfinement transition with improved actions and algorithms.
