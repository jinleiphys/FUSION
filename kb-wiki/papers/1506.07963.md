# A Universal Damping Mechanism of Quantum Vibrations in Deep Sub-Barrier Fusion Reactions

> We demonstrate the damping of quantum octupole vibrations near the touching point when two colliding nuclei approach each other in the mass-asymmetric $^{208}$Pb + $^{16}$O system, for which the strong fusion hindrance was clearly observed. We, for the first time, apply the random-phase approximation method to the heavy-mass asymmetric di-nuclear system to calculate the transition strength $B$(E3) as a function of the center-of-mass distance. The obtained $B$(E3) strengths are substantially damped near the touching point, because the single-particle wave functions of the two nuclei strongly mix with each other and a neck is formed. The energy-weighted sums of $B$(E3) are also strongly correlated with the damping factor which is phenomenologically introduced in the standard coupled-channel calculations to reproduce the fusion hindrance. This strongly indicates that the damping of the quantum vibrations universally occurs in the deep sub-barrier fusion reactions.

## Key claim
The damping of quantum octupole vibrations near the touching point of colliding nuclei is demonstrated to be a universal mechanism responsible for fusion hindrance in deep sub-barrier reactions, specifically shown for the mass-asymmetric 208Pb + 16O system where the damping factor from RPA calculations strongly correlates with the phenomenological damping factor that reproduces experimental fusion cross sections.

## Method
The authors applied the random-phase approximation (RPA) method to the heavy-mass asymmetric di-nuclear system 208Pb + 16O, calculating the mean-field potential using a folding procedure with a single Yukawa function and describing nuclear shapes with reflection-asymmetric lemniscatoids parametrization after the touching point. They solved the axially-symmetric Schrodinger equation with spin-orbit force, expanded single-particle wave functions in deformed harmonic-oscillator bases, and calculated B(E3) transition strengths for octupole vibrational states as a function of the center-of-mass distance R. The RPA equation was solved at each R using density-dependent residual interactions, taking the sum of B(E3) strengths for octupole excitations with energies below 4 MeV for 208Pb and below 6 MeV for 16O.

## Key numbers
- For 208Pb at R=20 fm: first 3- excited state energy = 2.86 MeV, B(E3, 3_1- -> 0_1+) = 7.13 x 10^4 e^2·fm^6
- For 16O at R=20 fm: first 3- excited state energy = 4.64 MeV, B(E3, 3_1- -> 0_1+) = 124 e^2·fm^6
- Single-particle p1/2 and p3/2 state energies in 16O at infinite separation: -5.88 MeV and -10.7 MeV, respectively
- The p1/2 state in 16O becomes positive (resonance) around R=18 fm
- Damping factor parameters used: r_d = 1.298 fm, a_d = 1.05 fm, largest eigenvalue of lambda_alpha = 1.46 fm
- Basis functions with energies lower than 26 hbar-omega were included
- Smoothing parameter mu = 0.8 fm, radius R_0 = 1.27A^(1/3) fm

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous RPA study of light mass-symmetric 16O + 16O and 40Ca + 40Ca systems (Ichikawa et al., 2013) to a heavy mass-asymmetric system, providing microscopic evidence for the damping factor phenomenologically introduced in the adiabatic approach by Ichikawa et al. (2009). The results support the adiabatic model over the sudden approach proposed by Misicu and Esbensen (2006, 2007), and the strong correlation between the calculated B(E3) strengths and the phenomenological damping factor that reproduces experimental data for 208Pb + 16O (Das et al., 2007) indicates this damping mechanism should be incorporated into standard coupled-channel models to describe fusion hindrance.
