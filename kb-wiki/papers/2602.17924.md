# Higher order quantization conditions for two-body scattering with spin

> We examine the Lüscher quantization condition to high order for the scattering of a spinless particle and a spin-1/2 particle in a periodic box. First, we derive the quantization conditions in a non-relativistic framework up to total angular momentum $J=11/2$ in both cubic and elongated geometries, and for both rest and moving frames. Then, we introduce a method to transparently cross-check their convergence, using both quantized energy levels in the box and infinite-volume phase shifts for the same potential. We clarify how to incorporate spin-orbit coupling into the formalism and show in detail how the quantization conditions converge order by order in the various irreducible representations. In all, we validated 19 quantization conditions (12 in cubic box, 7 in elongated box). This is a necessary step in applying the method in precision studies of systems in finite volume with half-integer spin, such as meson-baryon scattering.

## Key claim

The authors derive and validate higher-order Luscher quantization conditions for spinless-spin-1/2 scattering up to total angular momentum J=11/2, but the convergence of these conditions is explicitly checked order-by-order using a non-relativistic framework with a specific potential, meaning the results are demonstrated for a particular interaction model rather than proven generally.

## Method

The authors derive quantization conditions in a non-relativistic framework for scattering of a spinless particle and a spin-1/2 particle in a periodic box, working up to J=11/2 for both cubic and elongated geometries, and for rest and moving frames. They construct basis vectors for the irreducible representations of the double groups (²O_h, ²D_4h, ²C_2v, ²C_3v) in terms of spin spherical harmonics Y(J,M) up to J=13/2. The convergence of the quantization conditions is cross-checked by comparing quantized energy levels in the box against infinite-volume phase shifts computed from the same potential, and the incorporation of spin-orbit coupling into the formalism is clarified.

## Key numbers

- Quantization conditions derived up to total angular momentum J=11/2 for both cubic and elongated geometries.
- A total of 19 quantization conditions were validated: 12 in the cubic box and 7 in the elongated box.
- Basis vectors tabulated for spin spherical harmonics Y(J,M) up to J=13/2 for the irreducible representations of the double groups.
- For the cubic box (²O_h), basis vectors are provided for G1 (J=1/2, 7/2, 9/2, 11/2, 13/2), G2 (J=5/2, 7/2, 11/2, 13/2), and H (J=3/2, 5/2, 7/2, 9/2, 11/2, 13/2) representations.
- For the elongated box (²D_4h), basis vectors are provided for G1 and G2 representations up to J=13/2.

## Context

This work extends the Luscher quantization condition formalism (Luscher, 1991) to higher partial waves for systems with half-integer total angular momentum, specifically spinless-spin-1/2 scattering. It provides the necessary group-theoretical machinery for precision studies of meson-baryon scattering in finite-volume lattice QCD calculations, enabling future applications to systems such as pion-nucleon or kaon-nucleon scattering where spin-orbit coupling plays a role.
