# Possible signal for critical point in hadronization process

> We argue that recent data on fluctuations observed in heavy ion collisions show non-monotonic behaviour as function of number of participants (or "wounded nucleons") N_W. When interpreted in thermodynamical approach this result can be associated with a possible structure occuring in the corresponding equation of state (EoS). This in turn could be further interpreted as due to the occurence of some characteristic points (like "softest point" or "critical point") of EoS discussed in the literature and therefore be regarded as a possible signal of the QGP formation in such collisions. We show, however, that the actual situation is still far from being clear and calls for more investigations of fluctuation phenomena in multiparticle production processes to be performed.

## Key claim

The authors argue that non-monotonic behavior in fluctuation data from heavy ion collisions, when plotted as a function of the number of participants (wounded nucleons) rather than collision energy, can be interpreted as a possible signal of a critical point or softest point in the equation of state of hadronizing matter; however, they emphasize that the situation remains far from clear and that multiple interpretations (including percolation or spinodal decomposition) are possible.

## Method

The authors transform experimental fluctuation measures (PHENIX's \(F_T\) and NA49/CERES's \(\Phi\)) into the ratio \(R = (\delta S/S)^2/(\delta E/E)^2\) using a derived analytical formula that introduces a correlation coefficient \(\rho\) between particle multiplicity \(N\) and total transverse momentum \(\sum p_T\). They then connect \(R\) to the equation of state parameter \(\alpha = p/\varepsilon\) via thermodynamic identities, and separately analyze interferometric volume data to extract the energy dependence of the reaction volume \(V\) and its derivative \(\partial \ln V / \partial \ln E\).

## Key numbers

- The correlation coefficient \(\rho\) is set to 0.85, based on the constraint \(\rho > 0.83\) derived from the condition that multiplicity fluctuations are broader than Poissonian and that \(\omega = Var(p_T)/\langle p_T\rangle^2 \simeq 0.43\) (from CERES data).
- The derivative \(\partial \ln V / \partial \ln E\) changes sign at a critical energy: \(c_1 = -0.73\) for \(\sqrt{s} < \sqrt{s_c}\) and \(c_2 = +0.15\) for \(\sqrt{s} > \sqrt{s_c}\).
- The critical energy from volume data is \(\sqrt{s_c} \simeq 6\) GeV, obtained from the formula \(\sqrt{s_c} = (\alpha T/(q-1))(2/N_W)(c_1 c_2/(c_1 + c_2))\) using \(\alpha = 1/3\), \(T = 0.14\) GeV, and \(q-1 \approx 0.003\).
- The lower bound on \(R\) from Poissonian-type fluctuations is \(R > 0.699\) (for \(\omega = 0.43\)).
- A second possible critical point is observed at \(\sqrt{s} \sim 100\) GeV from PHENIX \(F_T\) data.

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Gazdzicki, Gorenstein, and Mrowczynski (1999) for investigating the equation of state via energy dependence of fluctuations, by applying it to centrality (participant number) dependence instead. It directly uses data from PHENIX (2002), NA49 (2002), and CERES (2002) collaborations, and compares with percolation-based interpretations from other authors. The results enable future experimental tests to distinguish between the softest point, critical point, and spinodal decomposition scenarios, and suggest that direct measurement of \(Var(N)/\langle N\rangle\) and \(Var(p_T)/\langle p_T\rangle^2\) would allow model-independent access to the equation of state.
