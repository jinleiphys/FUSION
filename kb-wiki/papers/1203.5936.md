# High multiplicity pp events and J/ψ production at LHC

> We discuss the dependence of J/ψ production on the charged particle multiplicity in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. We show that, in the framework of parton saturation or string interaction models, the hard J/ψ production exhibits a significant growth with the multiplicity, which is stronger than linear in the high density domain. This departure from linearity, that should affect any hard observable, applies for high multiplicity proton-proton collisions in the central rapidity region and is a consequence of the parton saturation or the strong interaction among colour ropes that take place at LHC energies. Our assumption, the existence of coherence effects present in proton-proton collisions at high energy, can also be checked by studying the particular shape of the probability distribution associated to the J/ψ production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed rise of J/ψ production with charged particle multiplicity in high-multiplicity pp events at LHC can be explained by string interaction or parton saturation effects, and that this rise becomes stronger than linear (specifically quadratic) in the high-density domain, contrary to the linear dependence expected at low multiplicities.

## Method

The authors model J/ψ production in the string percolation framework, where the number of produced strings N_s is proportional to the number of parton-parton collisions and thus to J/ψ yield, while the charged particle multiplicity is damped by a factor F(ρ) = sqrt((1-e^{-ρ})/ρ) that depends on the string density ρ = N_s σ_0 / σ. They derive an analytic relation between normalized J/ψ yield and normalized charged particle multiplicity, and compare it to ALICE data for central (|y|<0.9) and forward (2.5<y<4) rapidity regions. They also compute the probability distribution for J/ψ production using the minimum bias distribution and their derived relation.

## Key numbers

- The transverse size of a single string is r_0 ~ 0.25 fm, giving σ_0 = π r_0^2
- The inelastic pp cross section used is σ = σ_inel^{pp} = 70 mb (from Antchev 2011)
- For central rapidity, the average number of strings is <N_s> = 16; for forward rapidity, <N_s> = 8
- The uncertainty in <N_s> induces an uncertainty in <ρ> of 20%, comparable to the error of the last experimental point
- The authors suggest measuring at multiplicities dN/dη / <dN/dη> ~ 8 to 10 to check the behavior

## Context

This work extends the string percolation and parton saturation approaches previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (RHIC data from Back 2004, Adcox 2004, Adams 2005, Arsene 2004; LHC data from Schukraft 2011, Steinberg 2011, Wyslouch 2011) to high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC. It directly addresses the ALICE collaboration measurement (Abelev 2012) of J/ψ production in pp collisions. The authors note that the string densities reached in high-multiplicity pp events are comparable to those in CuCu central collisions or AuAu peripheral collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, where J/ψ melting is observed. The work enables using pp data as a baseline to disentangle J/ψ suppression from sequential dissociation and J/ψ enhancement from c-char recombination in heavy-ion collisions.
