# Interplay between chiral symmetry breaking and the QCD Kondo effect

> We investigate the interplay between the (light-light quark) chiral condensate and heavy-light quark condensate induced by the QCD Kondo effect, which is described by an effective Lagrangian with four-point interactions and the mean-field approximation. We find that the appearance of Kondo condensates decreases the critical chemical potential of the chiral condensate. In the region near the critical chemical potential, a coexistence phase with two kinds of condensates can appear. The behavior of such an interplay at finite temperature and with nonzero current light-quark mass is also discussed.

## Key claim

The appearance of the Kondo condensate (heavy-light quark condensate) decreases the critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry breaking, and a coexistence phase with both condensates can appear near the critical chemical potential, but only when the heavy-light coupling is sufficiently strong relative to the light-light coupling.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian with four-point interactions for both light-light (NJL-type) and heavy-light (color-singlet) sectors, applying the mean-field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential. They introduce both the chiral condensate (constituent quark mass M) and the Kondo condensate (gap Δ) as order parameters, then minimize the thermodynamic potential to obtain the phase structure for Nf=1 and Nf=2 light flavors at finite temperature, chemical potential, and with nonzero heavy-quark residual energy λ.

## Key numbers

- For G_Qq = 0 (no Kondo condensate), the critical chemical potential for the chiral condensate is μ_c^χ = 0.2765 GeV
- At G_Qq = 2G_qq, μ_c^χ decreases by a few tens of MeV relative to the G_Qq = 0 case
- The cutoff parameter is set to Λ = 0.65 GeV, with G_qq = 2(9/2)/Λ^2
- Finite temperature results are shown at T = 5 MeV
- Finite λ results are shown at λ = 1 MeV
- The color-superconducting gap is noted as Δ_CS = 140 MeV at μ = 500 MeV for comparison

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the QCD Kondo effect (Yasui 2016, Yasui 2017) by including the chiral condensate, which is the dominant feature of low-energy QCD (Nambu 1961). It complements the work of Kanazawa (2016) on competition with the color-superconducting phase at high density. The results are relevant for understanding how heavy mesons (D, D_s, B) can serve as probes of chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter created at FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, and for evaluating the nonperturbative aspect of the QCD Kondo effect near the critical density.
