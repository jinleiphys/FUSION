# Spin Polarizabilities of the Nucleon from Polarized Low Energy Compton Scattering

> As guideline for forthcoming experiments, we present predictions from Chiral Effective Field Theory for polarized cross sections in low energy Compton scattering for photon energies below 170 MeV, both on the proton and on the neutron. Special interest is put on the role of the nucleon spin polarizabilities which can be examined especially well in polarized Compton scattering. We present a model-independent way to extract their energy dependence and static values from experiment, interpreting our findings also in terms of the low energy effective degrees of freedom inside the nucleon: The polarizabilities are dominated by chiral dynamics from the pion cloud, except for resonant multipoles, where contributions of the Delta(1232) resonance turn out to be crucial. We therefore include it as an explicit degree of freedom. We also identify some experimental settings which are particularly sensitive to the spin polarizabilities.

## Key claim

Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChEFT) predictions for polarized Compton scattering cross sections, including explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, can guide forthcoming experiments to extract the four static dipole spin polarizabilities of the nucleon, but the authors caution that while unpolarized cross sections are well described up to 170 MeV, the polarized asymmetries are much more sensitive to fine details and their predictions above the pion production threshold are only qualitatively correct.

## Method

The authors calculate Compton scattering amplitudes in Chiral Effective Field Theory at leading-one-loop order (O(epsilon^3)) using both Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (with only pions and nucleons) and the Small Scale Expansion (which adds explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom). They expand the structure amplitudes in terms of six dynamical dipole polarizabilities (two spin-independent: alpha_E1(omega), beta_M1(omega); four spin: gamma_E1E1(omega), gamma_M1M1(omega), gamma_E1M2(omega), gamma_M1E2(omega)) and compute four asymmetries for circularly polarized photons scattering off polarized protons and neutrons, covering photon energies up to approximately 170 MeV in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: below 170 MeV in the center-of-mass frame
- Static forward spin polarizability of the proton from GDH experiment: gamma_0 = (-1.01 +/- 0.13) x 10^(-4) fm^4
- Static backward spin polarizability of the proton from LEGS group: gamma_pi = (-27.1 +/- 3.6) x 10^(-4) fm^4
- Static backward spin polarizability of the proton from MAMI low-energy data: gamma_pi = (-36.1 +/- 2.2) x 10^(-4) fm^4
- Static backward spin polarizability of the proton from MAMI Delta-region data: gamma_pi = (-37.9 +/- 3.6) x 10^(-4) fm^4
- ChEFT prediction for static backward spin polarizability: gamma_pi = -36.7 x 10^(-4) fm^4 (from HHKK)
- Neutral pion mass used: 135.0 MeV; charged pion mass: 139.6 MeV
- Isoscalar nucleon mass: 938.9 MeV; pion decay constant: 92.4 MeV
- Anomalous magnetic moment of proton: 1.793; of neutron: -1.913

## Context

This work extends the calculation of dynamical nucleon polarizabilities from Hildebrandt, Griesshammer, and Hemmert (2003) for unpolarized Compton scattering to the polarized case, providing predictions for double-polarized experiments. It contrasts with the low-energy expansion approach of Babusci et al., which uses static polarizabilities and breaks down near the pion production threshold and Delta resonance, by employing energy-dependent dynamical polarizabilities that capture cusps and resonance structures. The results enable experimental extraction of all four dipole spin polarizabilities (not just the two linear combinations gamma_0 and gamma_pi) from forthcoming measurements at facilities like HIgammaS at TUNL, and provide a comparison between chiral frameworks with and without explicit Delta degrees of freedom, building on earlier work by BKM and HHKK.
