# GPDs at an EIC

> The feasibility for a measurement of the exclusive production of a real photon, a process although known as Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) at an Electron Ion Collider (EIC) has been explored. DVCS is universally believed to be a golden measurement toward the determination of the Generalized Parton Distribution (GPDs) functions. The high luminosity of the machine, expected in the order of 10^34 cm^-2 s^-1 at the highest center-of-mass energy, together with the large resolution and rapidity acceptance of a newly designed dedicated detector, will open a opportunity for very high precision measurements of DVCS, and thus for the determination of GPDs, providing an important tool toward a 2+1 dimensional picture of the internal structure of the proton and nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an Electron Ion Collider (EIC) will enable high-precision measurements of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) and thus determination of Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs), but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that systematic uncertainties can be controlled to 5% based on experience at HERA and expected detector improvements, and that the Bethe-Heitler (BH) background can be sufficiently suppressed through detector capabilities.

## Method

The study used the MILOU Monte Carlo generator to simulate both DVCS and BH processes for two EIC beam-energy configurations: 5x100 GeV (stage-1) and 20x250 GeV (stage-2). Simulated data samples corresponding to 100 fb^-1 (stage-2) and 10 fb^-1 (stage-1) were smeared according to expected detector resolutions, and mock data were used to measure the |t|-differential cross section and charge- and spin-asymmetries. The BH contamination was investigated as a function of y for each Q^2, x_Bj, and |t| bin, and a systematic uncertainty of 5% was assumed based on HERA experience and expected EIC detector improvements.

## Key numbers

- Luminosity expected at the highest center-of-mass energy: 10^34 cm^-2 s^-1
- BH background uncertainty from proton form factor: approximately 3%
- Stage-2 beam energies: 20x250 GeV; stage-1 beam energies: 5x100 GeV
- Simulated data samples: 100 fb^-1 for stage-2 and 10 fb^-1 for stage-1, each corresponding to approximately 1 year of data taking at 50% operational efficiency
- For stage-2, BH contamination grows from negligible at low-y to about 70% at y ~ 0.6
- For stage-1, most statistics are contained in the safe region y < 0.3
- Roman Pot spectrometer can measure proton momentum in the range 0.03 < |t| < 0.88 GeV^2
- For |t| > 1.0 GeV^2, the proton can be measured in the main detector

## Context

This work extends the feasibility studies for DVCS measurements from previous facilities like HERA (experiments H1 and ZEUS) and ongoing programs at COMPASS II and JLAB@12 GeV, which provide limited information on GPDs in a narrower phase space. The paper demonstrates that an EIC would cover a much larger phase space, particularly at small x_Bj, and would have a great impact on constraining GPDs, especially the GPD E which remains unconstrained from current data. The results enable future tomographical imaging of sea-quark distributions in the impact parameter space for both unpolarized and polarized proton beams.
