# Properties of the Boltzmann equation in the classical approximation

> We study the Boltzmann equation with elastic point-like scalar interactions in two different versions of the the classical approximation. Although solving numerically the Boltzmann equation with the unapproximated collision term poses no problem, this allows one to study the effect of the ultraviolet cutoff in these approximations. This cutoff dependence in the classical approximations of the Boltzmann equation is closely related to the non-renormalizability of the classical statistical approximation of the underlying quantum field theory. The kinetic theory setup that we consider here allows one to study in a much simpler way the dependence on the ultraviolet cutoff, since one has also access to the non-approximated result for comparison.

## Key claim

The classical statistical approximation (CSA) of the underlying quantum field theory, when applied to the Boltzmann equation, introduces a dependence on the ultraviolet cutoff that is a direct consequence of the non-renormalizability of the CSA; this cutoff dependence appears even when the particle distribution has compact support and does not extend to the cutoff, and it manifests differently in the two versions of the classical approximation considered.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann equation with elastic 2-to-2 point-like scalar interactions and derive two classical approximations (labeled C0 and C1) by truncating the collision term in the retarded-advanced Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. The C0 approximation retains only the terms cubic in the distribution function f, while the C1 approximation retains the leading and subleading powers in f by substituting f → f + 1/2. They then analyze the ultraviolet cutoff dependence analytically by computing the linear-in-f terms in the C1 collision term, and numerically by solving the Boltzmann equation for homogeneous systems with a large UV cutoff, comparing the classical approximations to the unapproximated result.

- The linear-in-f term in the C1 collision term contains a contribution proportional to Λ_UV², specifically: (∂_t + v_p·∇) f(p) = - (g⁴ Λ_UV²)/(1024π³) · (1/ω_p) f(p) + ...
- The characteristic timescale t_* at which spurious cutoff effects become order-one corrections is given by: Q t_* ~ (1024π³/g⁴) · (Q/Λ_UV)², where Q is the typical physical momentum scale.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Epelbaum, Gelis, and Wu (2014), who showed that the CSA is a non-renormalizable approximation of the underlying quantum field theory, with the problem starting at 2-loops. The present paper investigates the same non-renormalizability in the simpler setting of kinetic theory, where the unapproximated Boltzmann equation can be solved numerically as a reference, something impossible in the full quantum field theory. The results are consistent with large-scale lattice CSA simulations by Berges et al. (2013), which observed strong UV cutoff dependence. The kinetic theory setup enables a much simpler study of the UV cutoff dependence and provides insight into the limitations of the CSA for applications to early-stage heavy ion collisions, where the glasma evolves under classical Yang-Mills equations.
