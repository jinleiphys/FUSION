# Three-body scattering from nonperturbative flow equations

> We consider fermion-dimer scattering in the presence of a large positive scattering length in the frame of functional renormalization group equations. A flow equation for the momentum dependent fermion-dimer scattering amplitude is derived from first principles in a systematic vertex expansion of the exact flow equation for the effective action. The resummation obtained from the nonperturbative flow is shown to be equivalent to the one performed by the integral equation by Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (STM). The flow equation approach allows to integrate out fermions and bosons simultaneously, in line with the fact that the bosons are not fundamental but build up gradually as fluctuation induced bound states of fermions. In particular, the STM result for atom-dimer scattering is obtained by choosing the relative cutoff scales of fermions and bosons such that the fermion fluctuations are integrated out already at the initial stage of the RG evolution.

## Key claim

The functional renormalization group (FRG) approach to fermion-dimer scattering reproduces the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation result for the atom-dimer scattering amplitude, but only when the relative cutoff scales for fermions and bosons are chosen such that fermion fluctuations are integrated out already at the initial stage of the RG evolution.

## Method

The authors derive a flow equation for the momentum-dependent fermion-dimer scattering amplitude from the exact FRG equation for the effective action, using a systematic vertex expansion truncated at fourth order in the fields. They work in the vacuum limit (zero density and temperature) with a two-channel model describing fermions and composite bosons coupled via a Feshbach coupling, taking the broad resonance limit where the Feshbach coupling goes to infinity. A momentum-independent mass-like cutoff function is used for both fermions and bosons, with a dimensionless parameter \(c\) setting the relative scale for eliminating the two species during the RG flow.

## Key numbers

- The universal ratio of fermion-dimer to fermion scattering length is \(a_3/a = 1.18\), obtained from the STM equation.
- The binding energy of a dimer is \(\hat \epsilon_{\text{M}} = -1/(M\hat a^2)\), derived from the flow equation in the broad resonance limit.
- The relation between scattering length and the fermion gap is \(a = 1/\sqrt{-\sigma_{\text{A}}}\), where \(-\sigma_{\text{A}}\) is half the molecular binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group technique, previously applied to critical phenomena and many-body systems, to the few-body scattering problem of fermion-dimer interactions. It directly connects to the classic STM result from Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (1956) and the effective field theory reformulation by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1997, 1999). The method enables simultaneous integration of fermion and boson fluctuations, which is relevant for coupled fermion-boson theories at quantum critical points, and can be extended to four-particle (dimer-dimer) scattering as noted by Kagan (2005) and Gurarie (2006).
