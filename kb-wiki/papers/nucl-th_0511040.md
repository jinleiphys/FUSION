# Momentum, Density, and Isospin dependence of the Symmetric and Asymmetric Nuclear Matter Properties

> Properties of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter have been investigated in the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach based on projection techniques using the Bonn A potential. The momentum, density, and isospin dependence of the optical potentials and nucleon effective masses are studied. It turns out that the isovector optical potential depends sensitively on density and momentum, but is almost insensitive to the isospin asymmetry. Furthermore, the Dirac mass $m^*_D$ and the nonrelativistic mass $m^*_{NR}$ which parametrizes the energy dependence of the single particle spectrum, are both determined from relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. The nonrelativistic mass shows a characteristic peak structure at momenta slightly above the Fermi momentum $\kf$. The relativistic Dirac mass shows a proton-neutron mass splitting of $m^*_{D,n} <m^*_{D,p}$ in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. However, the nonrelativistic mass has a reversed mass splitting $m^*_{NR,n} >m^*_{NR,p}$ which is in agreement with the results from nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key claim

The isovector optical potential in asymmetric nuclear matter depends sensitively on density and momentum, but is almost insensitive to the isospin asymmetry parameter \(\beta\), a nuance not captured in the abstract's general statement about isospin dependence.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach using projection techniques to extract the Lorentz structure of the in-medium \(T\)-matrix, with the Bonn A potential as the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. The self-consistent set of Bethe-Salpeter and Dyson equations was solved iteratively to determine the scalar and vector components of the nucleon self-energy, from which the optical potentials, Dirac mass, and nonrelativistic effective mass were computed for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at various densities and momenta.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density \(n_0 = 0.166\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the isovector optical potential at \(k=0\) is in good agreement with the empirical value of 22–34 MeV.
- The nonrelativistic effective mass shows a pronounced peak slightly above the Fermi momentum \(k_F\) at densities \(0.5 n_0\), \(n_0\), and \(2 n_0\), with the peak shifting to higher momenta and broadening with increasing density.
- The Dirac mass decreases continuously with increasing Fermi momentum, while the Landau mass (nonrelativistic mass at \(k=k_F\)) initially decreases but starts to rise again at high Fermi momenta.
- In isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, the relativistic Dirac mass exhibits a proton-neutron mass splitting of \(m^*_{D,n} < m^*_{D,p}\), while the nonrelativistic mass shows a reversed splitting \(m^*_{NR,n} > m^*_{NR,p}\).

## Context

This work extends previous DBHF studies by van Dalen et al. (2005) using projection techniques to resolve the momentum dependence of effective masses and optical potentials, which less precise fit methods could not capture. The results contradict relativistic mean-field (RMF) predictions (e.g., Baran et al., 2005) that yield \(m^*_{NR,n} < m^*_{NR,p}\), but agree with nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations by Zuo et al. (2005) and others. The findings enable more reliable input for transport models of radioactive nuclear collisions and for neutron star cooling scenarios involving direct Urca processes.
