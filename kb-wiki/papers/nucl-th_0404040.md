# Constraining the neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich matter

> Within Bombaci's phenomenological single-nucleon potential model we study the neutron-proton effective mass splitting $m_n^*- m_p^*$ in neutron-rich matter. It is shown that an effective mass splitting of $m_n^*<m_p^*$ leads to a symmetry potential that is inconsistent with the energy dependence of the Lane potential constrained by the nucleon-nucleus scattering experimental data.

## Key claim
Within Bombaci's phenomenological single-nucleon potential model, a neutron-proton effective mass splitting of \(m_n^* < m_p^*\) is ruled out because it produces a symmetry potential whose energy dependence is inconsistent with the Lane potential constrained by nucleon-nucleus scattering data; the opposite splitting \(m_n^* > m_p^*\) is consistent with the data.

## Method
The author used Bombaci's phenomenological formalism for the momentum-dependent single-nucleon potential in asymmetric nuclear matter, which extends the Gale-Bertsch-Das Gupta formalism. Two parameter sets were chosen to give opposite signs of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting (\(m_n^* > m_p^*\) and \(m_n^* < m_p^*\)) while keeping the symmetry energy nearly identical. The isoscalar potential \((U_n+U_p)/2\) and the isovector (Lane) potential \((U_n-U_p)/(2\delta)\) were computed at normal density \(\rho_0\) and compared with variational many-body predictions and empirical constraints from nucleon-nucleus scattering experiments.

## Key numbers
- The isoscalar potential from the Bombaci formalism agrees with variational many-body predictions up to wave number \(k = 2.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The empirical Lane potential decreases linearly with beam energy as \(U_{\text{Lane}} = a - b E_{\text{kin}}\), where \(a \simeq 22-34\ \text{MeV}\) and \(b \simeq 0.1-0.2\).
- The parameter set for case 1 (\(m_n^* > m_p^*\)) uses \(z_1 = -36.75\ \text{MeV}\), \(x_0 = -1.477\), \(x_3 = -1.01\); the set for case 2 (\(m_n^* < m_p^*\)) uses \(z_1 = 50\ \text{MeV}\), \(x_0 = 1.589\), \(x_3 = -0.195\).
- The Bombaci formalism uses parameters \(A = -144\ \text{MeV}\), \(B = 203.3\ \text{MeV}\), \(C = -75\ \text{MeV}\), \(\sigma = 7/6\), and reproduces an incompressibility \(K_0 = 210\ \text{MeV}\) for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical debate on the sign of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich matter, where microscopic many-body theories (e.g., Landau-Fermi liquid theory by Sjoberg (1976) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approaches) predict \(m_n^* > m_p^*\), while some phenomenological effective interactions predict the opposite. The analysis rules out \(m_n^* < m_p^*\) using existing experimental data, complementing a proposal by Rizzo et al. (2004) to use radioactive-beam reactions for the same purpose. This constraint is important for understanding neutron star properties, isospin diffusion in nuclear collisions, and shell effects in nuclei far from stability.
