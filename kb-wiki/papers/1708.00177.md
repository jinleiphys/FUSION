# Introduction of the one-body correlation operator in the unitary-model-operator approach

> In the earlier unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA), one-body correlations have been taken into account approximately by the diagonalization of unitary-transformed Hamiltonians in the $0p0h$ and $1p1h$ space. With this prescription, the dependence of the harmonic-oscillator energy ($\hbarω$) on calculated observables is not negligible even at larger model spaces. In the present work, we explicitly introduce the one-body correlation operator so that it optimizes the single-particle basis states and then reduces the $\hbarω$-dependence. For an actual demonstration, we calculate the energy and radius for the $^{4}$He ground state with the softened nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions from Argonne v18 (AV18) and chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to the next-to-next-to-next leading order (N$^{3}$LO). As a result, we obtain practically $\hbarω$-free results at sufficiently large model spaces. The present results are reasonably close to those by the other ab initio calculations with the same $NN$ interactions. This methodological development enables us more systematic analysis of calculation results in the UMOA. We also discuss qualitatively the origin of the $\hbarω$-dependence on calculated observables in a somewhat simplified way.

# Introduction of the one-body correlation operator in the unitary-model-operator approach

## Key claim

By explicitly introducing the one-body correlation operator into the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA), the authors obtain practically $\hbar\omega$-free results for the $^{4}$He ground-state energy and radius at sufficiently large model spaces, whereas earlier UMOA calculations that treated one-body correlations only approximately via diagonalization in the $0p0h$ and $1p1h$ space showed non-negligible $\hbar\omega$-dependence even at larger model spaces.

## Method

The authors extend the UMOA by including a one-body correlation operator $S^{(1)}$ in addition to the previously used two-body correlation operator $S^{(2)}$, with the unitary transformation $U = e^{S^{(1)}}e^{S^{(2)}}$. They solve the one-body Schrödinger equation with an auxiliary potential $w_1$ to determine $s_1$, then transform the two-body interaction with $e^{s_1+s_2}$, solve the two-body Schrödinger equation to determine $s_{12}$, and iterate until self-consistency of the one-body field is achieved. The ground-state energy and point-nucleon radius are evaluated from the normal-ordered zero-body term of the transformed Hamiltonian and transformed radius operator, respectively, using softened nucleon-nucleon interactions (low-momentum $V_{\text{low }k}$ from AV18 and SRG-transformed chiral EFT N$^3$LO) instead of the earlier two-step decoupling method with angle-average approximation.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{4}$He with $V_{\text{low }k}$ (AV18, $\Lambda = 1.9$ fm$^{-1}$): approximately $-24$ MeV at $e_{\text{max}} = 12$ with $\hbar\omega = 28$ MeV when including $S^{(1)}$, compared to approximately $-22$ MeV without $S^{(1)}$ at the same model space and $\hbar\omega$
- Ground-state energy of $^{4}$He with $V_{\text{low }k}$ (AV18): the $\hbar\omega$-dependence is reduced from about 4 MeV variation (without $S^{(1)}$) to less than 1 MeV variation (with $S^{(1)}$) over $\hbar\omega = 20$–36 MeV at $e_{\text{max}} = 12$
- Point-nucleon root-mean-square matter radius of $^{4}$He with $V_{\text{low }k}$ (AV18): approximately 1.45 fm at $e_{\text{max}} = 12$ with $\hbar\omega = 28$ MeV when including $S^{(1)}$, compared to approximately 1.55 fm without $S^{(1)}$ at the same model space and $\hbar\omega$
- The squared overlap between the ground state and reference state increases from about 0.85 (without $S^{(1)}$) to about 0.95 (with $S^{(1)}$) at $\hbar\omega = 28$ MeV and $e_{\text{max}} = 12$
- Convergence criterion for the self-consistent iteration: $\max(|\widetilde{w}^{({\rm new})}_{1} - \widetilde{w}^{({\rm old})}_{1}|) < 10^{-5}$

## Context

This work extends the UMOA framework (Suzuki 1994, Fujii 2004, 2009) by following the approach of Kohno (2012) in the coupled-cluster method (CCM), where including one-body correlations drastically reduced $\hbar\omega$-dependence. The authors compare their results for $^{4}$He with Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations (Nogga 2004) and CCSD calculations (Hagen 2007) using the same $NN$ interactions, finding reasonable agreement. This methodological development enables more systematic analysis of UMOA results by removing the $\hbar\omega$-dependence that previously hindered reliable comparisons with other ab initio methods and with experimental data.
