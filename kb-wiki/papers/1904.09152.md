# A microscopic description for polarization in particle scatterings

> We propose a microscopic description for the polarization from the first principle through the spin-orbit coupling in particle collisions. The model is different from previous ones based on local equilibrium assumptions for the spin degree of freedom. It is based on scatterings of particles as wave packets, an effective method to deal with particle scatterings at specified impact parameters. The polarization is then the consequence of particle collisions in a non-equilibrium state of spins. The spin-vorticity coupling naturally emerges from the spin-orbit one encoded in polarized scattering amplitudes of collisional integrals when one assumes local equilibrium in momentum but not in spin.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a microscopic description of global polarization in particle collisions based on spin-orbit coupling in scatterings of wave packets, showing that spin-vorticity coupling emerges naturally from spin-orbit coupling when one assumes local equilibrium in momentum but not in spin, without requiring the spin degree of freedom to be thermalized.

## Method

The authors model particle scatterings as wave packets with specified impact parameters, deriving collision rates for spin-0 particles in a multi-particle system and then generalizing to spin-1/2 particles. They work in the center-of-mass system of each collision, imposing constraints that the two incident wave packets have zero time difference and zero longitudinal spatial separation in that frame. The polarization rate is obtained by convolving phase space distributions with polarized scattering amplitudes, summing over spin states with the quantization direction along the reaction plane normal.

## Key numbers

- The STAR collaboration measured global polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7-200 GeV, with magnitude of order a few percent decreasing with collision energy
- The wave packet amplitude takes a Gaussian form with width parameter alpha, where the authors set equal width for both incident particles: alpha_A = alpha_B = alpha
- The polarization is defined in the range [-1,1] (not [-1/2,1/2]) due to a factor of 2 in the normalization convention

## Context

This work extends previous statistical-hydrodynamic models (Becattini 2013, Florkowski 2017) and Wigner function formalisms (Fang 2016, Weickgenannt 2019) that assumed local equilibrium for the spin degree of freedom, which the authors note is not justified. The recent disagreement between some theoretical models and STAR data on longitudinal polarization (Niida 2018, Becattini 2017, Xia 2018) indicates spins may not be in local equilibrium. The paper provides a first-principles microscopic mechanism for global polarization through particle collisions in non-equilibrium, enabling calculations of quark polarization rates in a quark-gluon plasma where momentum is in local equilibrium but spin is not.
