# Unique Access to u-Channel Physics: Exclusive Backward-Angle Omega Meson Electroproduction

> Backward-angle meson electroproduction above the resonance region, which was previously ignored, is anticipated to offer unique access to the three quark plus sea component of the nucleon wave function. In this letter, we present the first complete separation of the four electromagnetic structure functions above the resonance region in exclusive omega electroproduction off the proton, e + p -> e' + p + omega, at central Q^2 values of 1.60, 2.45 GeV^2 , at W = 2.21 GeV. The results of our pioneering -u ~ -u min study demonstrate the existence of a unanticipated backward-angle cross section peak and the feasibility of full L/T/LT/TT separations in this never explored kinematic territory. At Q^2 =2.45 GeV^2 , the observed dominance of sigma_T over sigma_L, is qualitatively consistent with the collinear QCD description in the near-backward regime, in which the scattering amplitude factorizes into a hard subprocess amplitude and baryon to meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs): universal non-perturbative objects only accessible through backward angle kinematics.

## Key claim

The first complete separation of the four electromagnetic structure functions in exclusive omega meson electroproduction above the resonance region is presented, demonstrating an unanticipated backward-angle cross section peak and the feasibility of full L/T/LT/TT separations in this previously unexplored kinematic territory. At Q^2 = 2.45 GeV^2, the observed dominance of sigma_T over sigma_L is qualitatively consistent with the collinear QCD description in the near-backward regime, but the paper does not claim definitive confirmation of the TDA framework due to limited statistics and the need for higher Q^2 data.

## Method

The experiment used 2.6-5.2 GeV electron beams on a liquid hydrogen target in Jefferson Lab Hall C, detecting recoil protons in the High Momentum Spectrometer (HMS) and scattered electrons in the Short Orbit Spectrometer (SOS). Data were taken at two central Q^2 values of 1.60 and 2.45 GeV^2 at common central W = 2.21 GeV, with two epsilon settings at each Q^2 (epsilon = 0.32, 0.59 at Q^2 = 1.6 GeV^2; epsilon = 0.27, 0.55 at Q^2 = 2.45 GeV^2). The omega yield was extracted from missing-mass spectra by subtracting simulated rho and two-pion backgrounds, and the four structure functions (sigma_T, sigma_L, sigma_LT, sigma_TT) were obtained by fitting the phi-dependent cross sections at both epsilon settings simultaneously using the Rosenbluth separation technique.

## Key numbers

- Central Q^2 values: 1.60 and 2.45 GeV^2, at central W = 2.21 GeV
- Two epsilon settings per Q^2: epsilon = 0.32 and 0.59 at Q^2 = 1.6 GeV^2; epsilon = 0.27 and 0.55 at Q^2 = 2.45 GeV^2
- Background from aluminum target cell and random coincidences: less than 5% contribution to total yield
- Proton loss due to multiple scattering inside HMS: 7-10%
- Correlated scale error of unseparated cross section: 2.6%
- Epsilon uncorrelated u correlated systematic error: 1.7-2.0%
- Bins with simulated omega tail contribution greater than 60% were excluded from analysis (9% of bins)
- Chi^2 per degree of freedom for omega yield quality control: center value 0.94, width 0.77
- Chi^2 per degree of freedom for background yield quality control: center value 1.3, width 0.97

## Context

This work extends the study of backward-angle meson electroproduction, which was previously ignored, into a kinematic regime accessible only through u-channel physics. It builds on the theoretical framework of baryon-to-meson Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) developed by Pire (2004, 2005), Lansberg (2011), and others, and follows the first measurement of near-backward pi+ electroproduction by the CLAS collaboration reported by Park (2018), which showed promising signs of predicted 1/Q^8 scaling but lacked critical evidence for sigma_T dominance. The results enable future tests of TDA predictions at higher Q^2 and provide the first experimental access to the three-quark-plus-sea component of the nucleon wave function through backward-angle omega production.
