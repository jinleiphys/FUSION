# Quasiparticle RPA with finite rank approximation for Skyrme interactions

> A finite rank separable approximation for the particle-hole RPA calculations with Skyrme interactions is extended to take into account the pairing. As an illustration of the method energies and transition probabilities for the quadrupole and octupole excitations in some O, Ar, Sn and Pb isotopes are calculated. The values obtained within our approach are very close to those that were calculated within QRPA with the full Skyrme interaction. They are in reasonable agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The finite rank separable approximation for particle-hole RPA with Skyrme interactions is extended to include pairing correlations, producing results for quadrupole and octupole excitations that are "very close" to full QRPA calculations and in "reasonable agreement" with experimental data, though the method is presented as an illustration rather than a definitive benchmark.

## Method

The authors start from the Skyrme interaction SIII and approximate the residual particle-hole interaction by its Landau-Migdal form, keeping only the l=0 terms. The radial integrals are evaluated using an N-point Gauss integration formula, which allows the residual interaction to be expressed as a sum of N separable terms. The Hamiltonian is then treated in the quasiparticle representation via the Bogoliubov transformation, and the QRPA equations are solved using the linearized equation-of-motion approach, with the matrix dimensions never exceeding 4N x 4N regardless of configuration space size.

## Key numbers

- For the giant dipole resonance (GDR) in 36Ar, the calculated energy centroid is Ec = 19.9 MeV, compared to the empirical systematics value of Ec = (31.2 A^{-1/3} + 20.6 A^{-1/6}) MeV.
- For the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) in 36Ar, the calculated energy centroid is Ec = 18.8 MeV, compared to the empirical value Ec = 63 A^{-1/3} = 19.1 MeV.
- For the isovector GQR in 36Ar, the calculated energy centroid is Ec = 30.5 MeV, about 10% less than predicted by empirical systematics.
- The finite rank approximation uses N = 45 for multipolarities lambda <= 3 in nuclei with A <= 208; increasing N to 60 in 208Pb changes results for energies and transition probabilities by not more than 1%.
- For octupole excitations in 206Pb, the method requires inverting a matrix of dimension 2N = 90 instead of diagonalizing a 1376 x 1376 matrix without the finite rank approximation.
- The BCS subspace includes all subshells lying below 5 MeV, and the single-particle spectra are cut off at 190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite rank separable approximation for particle-hole RPA previously developed by Severyukhin et al. (1998) for Ar isotopes, now incorporating pairing correlations to treat open-shell nuclei. The approach produces results close to full QRPA calculations with Skyrme forces by Khan et al. (2000, 2001) and is similar in structure to the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) of Soloviev et al., but with all parameters derived from Skyrme forces rather than being phenomenological. The method enables future studies of two-phonon coupling effects, which the authors note are in progress.
