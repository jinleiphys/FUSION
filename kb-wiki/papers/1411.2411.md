# Three-body calculation of the $1s$ level shift in kaonic deuterium

> The first calculation of kaonic deuterium $1s$ level shift using Faddeev-type equations was performed. The obtained results were compared with commonly used approximate approaches.

# Three-body calculation of the 1s level shift in kaonic deuterium

## Key claim
The paper presents the first exact three-body Faddeev-type calculation of kaonic deuterium's 1s level shift and width, treating all interactions (strong and Coulomb) simultaneously without reduction to an effective two-body problem. The exact results show that the commonly used corrected Deser formula gives a reasonable real part but a quite large error for the width, while the effective optical K⁻-d potential approximation is the most accurate among the approximate methods.

## Method
The authors solved the Noble form of Faddeev equations for the K⁻pn three-body system using a discrete basis of Coulomb Sturmian functions, which yields a tridiagonal matrix for the pure Coulomb Hamiltonian. The strong interactions were modeled with four different one-term separable complex potentials with Yamaguchi form factors, fitted to reproduce either chirally motivated scattering lengths or experimental kaonic hydrogen data from SIDDHARTA. The eigenvalue of the three-body Hamiltonian was found by searching for the zero of the determinant Det(A(z))=0 in the vicinity of the reference energy corresponding to a deuteron and a kaon in the Coulomb field of the deuteron center of mass.

## Key numbers
- The exact Faddeev results for the 1s level shift and width (ΔE - iΓ/2) for the four potentials range from (−641 − i428) eV to (−736 − i413) eV for kaonic deuterium.
- The polarization potential correction (Coulomb acting between K⁻ and proton rather than K⁻ and deuteron) causes a real shift of approximately 10 eV from the reference energy.
- The calculation achieved an accuracy of ∼0.5% (∼1–2 eV) using 30–40 Sturmian functions per variable, resulting in a final matrix dimension of about 5000.
- The original Deser formula gives values such as (−723 − i596) eV for potential V_I, while the corrected Deser formula gives (−675 − i351) eV for the same potential.
- The effective optical K⁻-d potential approximation yields (−650 − i434) eV for potential V_I, compared to the exact Faddeev result of (−641 − i428) eV.
- Kaonic hydrogen shifts for the four potentials are (−280 − i268) eV, (−217 − i292) eV, (−219 − i293) eV, and (−280 − i266) eV.

## Context
This work extends the method proposed by Papp (year not specified) for simultaneous treatment of short-range and Coulomb forces in three-body problems, which had previously been applied only to repulsive Coulomb cases or purely Coulomb systems. It directly compares with the approximate approaches of Deser (year not specified) and the corrected Deser formula, as well as with the effective optical K⁻-d potential method used by Shevchenko (2009) and repeated with new potentials. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from the SIDDHARTA-2 experiment and provide a benchmark for testing energy-dependent chiral interaction models, which require extension of the formalism to treat energy-dependent potentials and the πΣ channel.
