# Towards understanding the probability of $0^+$ ground states in even-even many-body systems

> For single-$j$ shells with $j={7/2}, {9/2}$ and 11/2, we relate the large probability of $I^+$ ground states to the largest (smallest) coefficients $α^J_{I(v β)} = <nv βI |$ $A^{J \dagger} \cdot A^J | n vβI>$, where $n$ is the particle number, $v$ is the seniority, $β$ is an additional quantum number, and $I$ is the angular momentum of the state. Interesting regularities of the probabilities of $I^+$ ground states are noticed and discussed for 4-particle systems. Several counter examples of the $0^+$ ground state (0GS) predominance are noticed for the first time.

# Towards understanding the probability of $0^+$ ground states in even-even many-body systems

## Key claim

The large probability of $0^+$ ground states in single-$j$ shells arises from the largest or smallest coefficients $\alpha^J_{I(v\beta)}$ in the linear expansion of eigenvalues in terms of two-body interaction parameters $G_J$, but this is not a universal rule: counterexamples exist where $0^+$ ground states are not predominant, such as for $j=7/2$ and $j=13/2$ shells with 4 particles.

## Method

The authors analyze identical nucleons in single-$j$ shells ($j=7/2, 9/2, 11/2$) by expressing eigenvalues $E_{I(v)} = \sum_J \alpha^J_{I(v)} G_J$ as linear combinations of random two-body interaction parameters $G_J$ distributed as Gaussian random numbers. They relate the probability of a given $I^+$ state being the ground state to whether its coefficients $\alpha^J_{I(v)}$ are the largest or smallest among all states for a given $J$, using a "shift argument" where the difference between coefficients produces a systematic bias in the energy ordering. For $j=9/2$ and higher shells where seniority mixing occurs, they approximate eigenvalues by diagonal matrix elements in the seniority basis, noting this underestimates the $0^+$ ground state probability.

## Key numbers

- For $j=7/2$ with 4 particles: the states with largest or smallest $\alpha^J_{I(v)}$ are $I(v)=0(0)$, $2(4)$, $4(4)$, and $8(4)$; these are predicted to have large ground-state probabilities.
- For $j=9/2$ with 4 particles: the $0^+$ ground state probability is 66.4%, the $4^+$ probability is 12.2%, the $12^+$ probability is 17.4%, and the $2^+$ probability is 3.4% (from TBRE calculations).
- For $j=9/2$ with 4 particles: the predicted $0^+$ ground state probability using the no-mixing integral (analogous to Eq. 6) is 45.11%, which is smaller than the full TBRE result of 66.4%.
- For a two-$j$ shell ($j=7/2$ plus $j=5/2$) with 4 particles: the $0^+$ ground state probability is 24% while the $2^+$ ground state probability is 33%.
- As $j$ increases, the $0^+$ ground state probability saturates around 35%, and the ground state probabilities of $I=2$, $4$, and $I_{max}$ states converge around 9%.

## Context

This work extends the study of $0^+$ ground state predominance in random two-body interactions (TBRE) first investigated by Johnson et al. (1998-2000), who showed that even-even many-body systems with random interactions preferentially have $0^+$ ground states. It contrasts with earlier explanations by Bijker and Frank (2000) that attributed the effect to larger distribution widths of $0^+$ eigenvalues, and by Mulhall et al. (2000) using geometric chaoticity. The authors provide a new mechanistic explanation based on the extremal coefficients $\alpha^J_{I(v)}$ in the eigenvalue expansion, and for the first time identify counterexamples where $0^+$ ground states are not dominant. This enables future work to understand why certain coefficients $\alpha^J_{0(v)}$ are systematically largest or smallest, and to extend the analysis to more general multi-$j$ shells and boson systems.
