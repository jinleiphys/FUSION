# Electron removal energies in noble gas atoms up to 100 keV: ab initio GW vs XPS

> X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) measures electron removal energies, providing direct access to core and valence electron binding energies, hence probing the electronic structure. In this work, we benchmark for the first time the ab initio many-body GW approximation on the complete electron binding energies of noble gas atoms (He-Rn), which spans 100~keV. Our results demonstrate that GW achieves an accuracy within 1.2% in XPS binding energies, by systematically restoring the underestimation from density-functional theory (DFT, error of 14%) or the overestimation from Hartree-Fock (HF, error of 4.7%). Such results also imply the correlations of $d$ electrons are very well described by GW.

## Key claim

The GW approximation achieves a mean absolute percentage error of 1.2% in XPS binding energies across all noble gas atoms (He to Rn), systematically correcting both the underestimation from DFT-PBE (14% error) and the overestimation from Hartree-Fock (4.7% error), but this accuracy is contingent on using self-consistency on quasiparticle energies and the ZORA relativistic approximation.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio GW calculations starting from either DFT-PBE or Hartree-Fock mean-field solutions, using Gaussian basis sets (x2c-TZVPPall-2c) with Coulomb-fitting resolution of identity. Relativistic effects were treated with the zero-order regular approximation (ZORA) and compared to the third-order Douglas-Kroll (DK3) approximation. Self-consistency was applied only to quasiparticle energies while keeping wavefunctions fixed at the starting point, using the codes NWCHEM for HF/DFT and Fiesta for GW.

## Key numbers

- GW achieves a mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) of 1.2% on XPS binding energies across all noble gas atoms (He to Rn), compared to 14.0% for DFT-PBE and 4.7% for Hartree-Fock (ZORA relativistic approximation)
- The mean absolute error (MAE) for GW is 6.0 eV (GW@PBE) and 6.3 eV (GW@HF) with ZORA, versus 44.5 eV for PBE and 16.2 eV for HF
- For the deepest core level (Rn 1s at approximately 98404 eV), GW achieves a relative error of -0.1% (GW@PBE) and -0.1% (GW@HF)
- The GW quasiparticle renormalization factor for d and f electrons is Z = 0.87 ± 0.03, except for Xe 4d where Z = 0.61
- ZORA spin-orbit splits show MAE of 0.35 eV (PBE) and 1.18 eV (HF), while DK3 shows MAE of 17.6 eV (PBE) and 17.1 eV (HF)

## Context

This work extends the application of the GW approximation from band gaps of solids and HOMO-LUMO gaps of molecules (as demonstrated by Hybertsen and Louie (1985) and Godby and Sham (1987)) to core electron binding energies up to 100 keV for the first time. The results contradict the common assumption that GW may fail at high energies, and they enable future benchmarking of GW on molecular core levels and solid-state XPS spectra. The study also validates that self-consistent GW on energies alone is sufficient to achieve starting-point independence, as PBE and HF represent the two extremes in hybrid theories as noted by Atalla and Scheffler (2013).
