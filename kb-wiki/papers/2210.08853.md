# Hidden bottom pentaquark in the SU(5) version of the flavor-spin model

> We generalize to five distinct flavors the flavor-spin hyperfine interaction introduced previously for four flavors and used in the study of $uudc\overline{c}$ pentaquark. As a particular case here we study the lowest states of the pentaquark $uudb\overline{b}$, of either positive or negative parity, in a constituent quark model with linear confinement and the presently extended hyperfine interaction. The positive parity states have one unit of angular momentum located in the subsystem of four quarks and are described by translationally invariant states of orbital permutation symmetry $[31]_O$ which requires the configuration $s^3 p$. The negative parity states are described by the configuration $s^4$ of permutation symmetry $[4]_O$. We show that the lowest state has the quantum numbers $J^P$ = $1/2^+$ or $3/2^+$ and I = $1/2$ and is located below the $Σ_b B$ threshold by - 132 MeV. We present a comparison between the spectra of $uudc\overline{c}$ and $uudb\overline{b}$ pentaquarks.

## Key claim

The lowest state of the $uudb\overline{b}$ pentaquark has positive parity with quantum numbers $J^P = 1/2^+$ or $3/2^+$ and isospin $I = 1/2$, lying 132 MeV below the $\Sigma_b B$ threshold, contrary to chromomagnetic models where the lowest state typically has negative parity.

## Method

The author generalizes the SU(4) flavor-spin hyperfine interaction to SU(5) within a constituent quark model that includes linear confinement and meson-exchange potentials (pion, eta, B meson, eta_c, eta_b, and others). The four-quark subsystem $uudb$ is treated with translationally invariant orbital wave functions of permutation symmetry $[31]_O$ for positive parity states (configuration $s^3 p$) and $[4]_O$ for negative parity states (configuration $s^4$), and the total Hamiltonian is solved variationally with two parameters $a$ and $b$ describing the internal motion of the four-quark subsystem and its relative motion to the $\overline{b}$ antiquark.

## Key numbers

- The lowest positive parity state $|1\rangle|\overline{b}\rangle$ has a calculated mass of 10961 MeV, with variational parameters $a = 1.900$ fm$^{-2}$ and $b = 1.387$ fm$^{-2}$.
- This state lies 132 MeV below the $\Sigma_b B$ threshold.
- The mean distance between quarks in the four-quark subsystem for the lowest state is $\langle r_{12} \rangle = 0.910$ fm, and the mean distance between the four-quark cluster and the antiquark is $\langle r_{45} \rangle = 0.950$ fm.
- The negative parity state $|3\rangle|\overline{b}\rangle$ has a mass of 11112 MeV, and the state $|4\rangle|\overline{b}\rangle$ has a mass of 11334 MeV.
- The quark masses used are $m_{u,d} = 340$ MeV and $m_b = 4660$ MeV, with meson masses $\mu_\pi = 139$ MeV, $\mu_\eta = 547$ MeV, and $\mu_B = 5279$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SU(4) flavor-spin model previously applied to the $uudc\overline{c}$ pentaquark by Stancu (2019) to the SU(5) case for hidden bottom pentaquarks. It contrasts with chromomagnetic (one-gluon-exchange) models such as those of Ali (2019) and Weng (2019), which predict the lowest state to have negative parity, and with molecular interpretations of the LHCb pentaquarks observed in 2015 and 2019. The results enable future experimental searches for bottom analogues of the hidden charm pentaquarks, for which the naming convention $P^N_{\Upsilon}(mass)$ has already been proposed by Gershon (2022).
