# Nuclear medium effects in lepton-nucleus DIS in the region of $x \gtrsim 1$

> The nuclear medium effects in the nuclear structure functions and differential cross sections in the deep inelastic scattering (DIS) of charged lepton and neutrino from nuclear targets are studied in the region of large $x$ including $x\ge 1$. The nuclear medium effects due to the Fermi motion and the binding energy of nucleons and the nucleon correlations are included using nucleon spectral function calculated in a microscopic field theoretical model. The numerical results for the nuclear structure functions and the cross sections are obtained using the nucleon structure function evaluated at the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with the Martin-Motylinski-Harland Lang-Thorne (MMHT) parameterization of the nucleonic parton distribution functions (PDFs) and are compared with the available experimental data on electron scattering from the Jefferson Lab (JLab) and SLAC Nuclear Physics Facility (NPAS). In the case of neutrino scattering the results are relevant for understanding the DIS contributions to the recent inclusive cross sections measured by the Main Injector Neutrino ExpeRiment to study v-A interactions (MINERvA) as well as theoretical predictions are made for Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE). The importance of isoscalarity corrections in heavier nuclear targets as well as the effect of the kinematic cut on the CM energy $W$ in defining the DIS region have also been discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear medium effects from Fermi motion, binding energy, and nucleon correlations, modeled via a microscopic spectral function, are essential to describe lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering structure functions and cross sections in the region \(x \gtrsim 1\), but the agreement with experimental data depends on the specific kinematic cuts applied (e.g., on the center-of-mass energy \(W\)) and on whether isoscalarity corrections are included for non-isoscalar nuclear targets.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear structure functions \(F_{1A}, F_{2A}, F_{3A}\) and differential cross sections for charged lepton and neutrino scattering off carbon, argon, iron, and lead targets by convolving nucleon structure functions (evaluated at NNLO using MMHT parton distribution functions, with target mass corrections) over a nucleon hole spectral function derived from a microscopic field-theoretical model of nuclear matter, using the local density approximation to extend results to finite nuclei. The formalism accounts for Fermi motion, binding energy, and nucleon-nucleon correlations, and includes separate treatment of proton and neutron densities for non-isoscalar nuclei. Results are compared with electron scattering data from JLab (CLAS, E89-008) and SLAC (NE18, NPAS), and predictions are made for neutrino scattering relevant to MINERvA and DUNE.

## Key numbers

- For carbon, the Fermi momentum is taken as \(p_{F_N} = 221\) MeV; for iron, \(p_{F_N} = 251\) MeV (global Fermi gas model values).
- The binding energy per nucleon used is 7.6 MeV for \(^{12}\)C, 8.6 MeV for \(^{40}\)Ar, 8.8 MeV for \(^{56}\)Fe, and 7.8 MeV for \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The kinetic energy per nucleon used is 20.0 MeV for \(^{12}\)C, 29.0 MeV for \(^{40}\)Ar, 30.0 MeV for \(^{56}\)Fe, and 32.6 MeV for \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The kinematic region considered has \(Q^2 \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x \gtrsim 0.8\), with results shown at specific \(Q^2\) values such as 3 GeV\(^2\) and 6.8 GeV\(^2\) for iron.
- For neutrino scattering, the MINERvA experiment uses a low-energy beam peaking at \(E_{\nu_l} = 3\) GeV in the energy range \(2 \le E_{\nu_l} \le 20\) GeV, and the DUNE experiment spans energies up to tens of GeV.
- The kinematic cut defining the pure DIS region used in MINERvA is \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W > 2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of nuclear medium effects in DIS at \(x \le 0.8\) by the same group (Sajjad Athar et al., 2007-2021) into the region \(x \gtrsim 1\), where Fermi motion and nucleon correlations dominate and the EMC effect gives way to the "superfast quark" regime. It directly addresses the lack of theoretical predictions for neutrino-nucleus DIS at high \(x\) noted by the MINERvA collaboration (Tice et al., 2014), whose data for \(\nu_\mu\) scattering off carbon, iron, and lead at \(x \le 1.5\) were not well described by existing models. The results enable improved interpretation of current and future neutrino oscillation experiments (DUNE, ICARUS, SBND, MicroBooNE) by providing a framework to reduce the 25-30% systematic uncertainty from nuclear cross section modeling, and complement prior spectral function approaches applied to electromagnetic scattering (Fernandez de Cordoba et al., 1995; Frankfurt et al., 1988).
