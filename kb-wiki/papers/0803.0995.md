# Cross sections for low-energy $π^-γ$ reactions

> We review the cross sections for low-energy $π^- γ$ reactions in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. Charged pion Compton scattering, $π^- γ\to π^-γ$, is considered up to one-loop order where the pion's internal structure enters through the difference of the electric and magnetic pion polarizability, $α_π- β_π$. The ongoing COMPASS experiment aims at measuring this important structure constant with high statistics using the Primakoff effect. In the same way, the reaction $π^- γ\to π^- π^0$ serves as a test of the QCD chiral anomaly (via the $γ3π$-coupling constant $F_{\gamma3π}$). Furthermore, we calculate the total cross sections for neutral and charged pion-pair production, $π^- γ\to π^- π^0π^0$ and $π^- γ\to π^-π^+π^-$, which are governed by the chiral $ππ$-interaction. Finally, we investigate the radiative (correction) process $π^- γ\to π^- γγ$ and calculate the corresponding two-photon mass spectrum. This information will be useful for analyzing the $π^-$ Primakoff scattering events with photons in the final state.

# Cross sections for low-energy π⁻γ reactions

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory at one-loop order provides sufficiently accurate predictions for low-energy π⁻γ cross sections (up to √s < 4mπ) to guide the COMPASS experiment's Primakoff measurements, but the effects of pion structure on observables are small, at most an 11% reduction of the differential cross section in backward directions, meaning that experimental extraction of the polarizability difference απ − βπ requires both high statistics and careful inclusion of loop corrections to avoid underestimation.

## Method

The authors calculate total and differential cross sections for several π⁻γ reactions using chiral perturbation theory at one-loop order, including charged pion Compton scattering (π⁻γ → π⁻γ), neutral pion production (π⁻γ → π⁻π⁰), double pion production (π⁻γ → π⁻π⁰π⁰ and π⁻γ → π⁻π⁺π⁻), and the radiative process π⁻γ → π⁻γγ. For the double pion production channels, tree-level diagrams from the chiral Lagrangian are evaluated with three-body phase space integration; for Compton scattering, the one-loop amplitudes incorporate pion-loop corrections and the polarizability difference απ − βπ via low-energy constants extracted from the PIBETA experiment's measurement of the axialvector-to-vector form factor ratio.

## Key numbers

- Chiral perturbation theory two-loop prediction for the pion polarizability difference: απ − βπ = (5.7 ± 1.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Gasser, 2006)
- Current-algebra value from PIBETA data: απ − βπ ≈ 6.0 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, derived from l̄₆ − l̄₅ = 3.0 ± 0.3
- Existing experimental results from Serpukhov: απ − βπ = (15.6 ± 7.8) × 10⁻⁴ fm³; from MAMI: απ − βπ = (11.6 ± 3.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- MARK II data extraction: απ − βπ = (4.4 ± 3.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Two-loop prediction for polarizability sum: απ + βπ = 0.16 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Chiral anomaly coupling constant: Fγ3π = e/(4π²fπ³) = 9.72 GeV⁻³
- Serpukhov measurement of Fγ3π: (12.9 ± 1.4) GeV⁻³; after electromagnetic corrections: (10.7 ± 1.2) GeV⁻³
- Total cross section for π⁻γ → π⁻π⁰π⁰ near threshold: σtot(s)thr = α√3 (√s − 3mπ)³/[π mπ (8fπ)⁴]
- Total cross section for π⁻γ → π⁻π⁺π⁻ near threshold: σtot(s)thr = α√3 (√s − 3mπ)³/[9π mπ (4fπ)⁴]
- Charged pion-pair production cross section reaches 2.6 μb at √s = 6mπ
- Infrared coefficient σir(s) reaches a maximum of 7.45 nb at √s = 3mπ

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework previously applied to pion polarizabilities by Gasser (2006) and Burgi (2004), and to the γ3π anomaly by Bijnens (1998) and Hannah (1999). It provides cross section formulas needed for the COMPASS experiment (ongoing at CERN), which aims to measure απ − βπ via the Primakoff effect with higher statistics than earlier experiments at Serpukhov (Antipov, 1986) and MAMI (Mainz, 2005). The calculations enable extraction of the chiral anomaly constant Fγ3π from π⁻γ → π⁻π⁰ data and provide background estimates for the radiative process π⁻γ → π⁻γγ, which will be essential for analyzing Primakoff scattering events with photons in the final state.
