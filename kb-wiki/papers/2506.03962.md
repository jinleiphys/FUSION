# The influence of nuclear short range correlations on sub-threshold particle production in proton-nucleus collisions

> The apparent production of (multi-)strange baryons and mesons at sub-threshold energies in proton-heavy ion collisions is a consequence of short range correlations (SRC), which have recently been observed in lepton-nucleus scattering. They may enhance the available center of mass energy of individual nucleon-nucleon collisions and allow, therefore, for sub-threshold particle production in proton-nucleus collisions. Calculations demonstrate that SRC enhance the probability for particle production at nominal sub-threshold energies up to a factor of $\times 10^3$ as compared to a simple Fermi gas model. We benchmark the idea by calculating the $Ξ^-$ multiplicity in nominal sub-threshold p+Nb collisions which compare well with the data measured by the HADES collaboration. These findings are of prime relevance for upcoming experiments at the FAIR facility, especially for the study of charmed hadrons.

## Key claim

Short range correlations (SRC) in nuclei, which produce a deuteron-like tail in the nucleon momentum distribution, can enhance the probability for sub-threshold hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions by up to a factor of 10^3 compared to a simple Fermi gas model, but the enhancement is largest only for energies far below the elementary production threshold; for the single existing data point (HADES p+Nb at 61 MeV below threshold) the difference between the SRC and Fermi predictions is comparable to the experimental uncertainty.

## Method

The authors use the parametrization of the nucleon momentum distribution from Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1995), which separates the distribution into a Fermi-gas part and a deuteron-like SRC tail, for nuclei from ^4He to ^208Pb. They compute the center-of-mass energy spectrum dN/d√s for nucleon-nucleon collisions in a proton-nucleus system by convolving the projectile momentum with the target momentum distribution, then integrate this spectrum above the elementary production thresholds for various hadrons. For the Ξ^- multiplicity in p+Nb, they multiply the √s spectrum by the pp→Ξ^-X cross section and normalize to a Glauber calculation of binary collisions.

## Key numbers

- SRC enhance the probability for particle production at nominal sub-threshold energies up to a factor of ×10^3 compared to a simple Fermi gas model (lower panel of Fig. 2, for deeply sub-threshold energies)
- The Ξ^- production threshold in pp collisions is √s_thr = 3.241 GeV; the HADES measurement in p+Nb at 3.5 GeV beam kinetic energy corresponds to √s_NN = 3.18 GeV, which is 61 MeV below threshold
- For Λ production in p+Pb, the relative number of collisions above threshold drops to 0.1% at √s_NN - √s_thr ≈ 0.24 GeV for Fermi gas and ≈ 0.47 GeV including SRC
- For Λ_c production in p+Pb, the 0.1% level is reached at √s_NN - √s_thr ≈ 0.67 GeV for Fermi gas and ≈ 1.40 GeV including SRC
- The deepest sub-threshold production (at fixed 0.1% fraction) is achieved with Fe nuclei, gaining approximately 100 MeV compared to He and approximately 50 MeV compared to Pb

## Context

This work extends the study of short range correlations observed in lepton-nucleus scattering by the CLAS collaboration (2018, 2019) to hadronic collisions, providing a direct link between nuclear structure and intermediate-energy heavy ion physics. It offers an alternative to multi-step processes proposed by Steinheimer (2015) for sub-threshold φ and Ξ production, which rely on poorly constrained decay channels of high-mass nucleon resonances. The results are of prime relevance for upcoming experiments at the FAIR facility, especially for studying charmed hadrons like J/ψ and Λ_c at SIS100 energies.
