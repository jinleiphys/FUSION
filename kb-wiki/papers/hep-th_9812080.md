# Systematic Renormalization in Hamiltonian Light-Front Field Theory: The Massive Generalization

> Hamiltonian light-front field theory can be used to solve for hadron states in QCD. To this end, a method has been developed for systematic renormalization of Hamiltonian light-front field theories, with the hope of applying the method to QCD. It assumed massless particles, so its immediate application to QCD is limited to gluon states or states where quark masses can be neglected. This paper builds on the previous work by including particle masses non-perturbatively, which is necessary for a full treatment of QCD. We show that several subtle new issues are encountered when including masses non-perturbatively. The method with masses is algebraically and conceptually more difficult; however, we focus on how the methods differ. We demonstrate the method using massive phi^3 theory in 5+1 dimensions, which has important similarities to QCD.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed systematic renormalization method for Hamiltonian light-front field theory from massless particles to massive particles non-perturbatively, showing that several subtle new issues arise that make the method algebraically and conceptually more difficult; the method is demonstrated using massive phi^3 theory in 5+1 dimensions as a testbed for eventual application to QCD.

## Method

The authors regulate the theory by placing a smooth cutoff on change in free mass, then use a unitary transformation to relate the invariant-mass operator at different cutoff scales. They split the reduced interaction into cutoff-dependent and cutoff-independent parts, and derive expressions for both using an expansion in powers of the running coupling and in powers and logarithms of the mass. For the massive theory, they redefine the coupling with an exponential factor exp(9 m^4 / Lambda^4) to cancel cutoff factors, and they fix even-order cutoff-independent two-point interactions by requiring the completely disconnected parts of forward T-matrix elements to vanish.

## Key numbers

- The coupling definition includes a factor exp(9 m^4 / Lambda^4) that differs from the massless theory.
- The scale dependence of any term in the expansion has the form Lambda^(6-2N_int) (m/Lambda)^alpha [log(m/Lambda)]^beta (p_perp/Lambda)^gamma, where N_int is the total number of particles in final and initial states that participate in the interaction, and alpha, beta, gamma are non-negative integers.
- Cutoff-independent contributions occur when the condition 6 - 2N_int - alpha - gamma = 0 is satisfied, which only allows two-point and three-point interactions.
- Figure 1 shows C_3(m, Lambda, Lambda') as a function of m/Lambda for Lambda=2.0 with Lambda'=2.2 and Lambda'=3.0, demonstrating exponential damping of the running coupling with increasing mass.
- Figure 2 shows the non-canonical part of the invariant-mass operator matrix element for phi_1 -> phi_2 phi_3 versus |k_perp|/Lambda at fixed g_Lambda^2 = 512 pi^3 / 3 and y=1/2, for m/Lambda values of 0.005, 0.335, and 0.545.

## Context

This work builds directly on the massless renormalization method developed by Allen and Perry (1998) and extends it to include particle masses non-perturbatively, which is necessary for a full treatment of QCD. The method is inspired by earlier work by Dyson, Wilson, Glazek and Wilson, and Wegner on similarity renormalization. The demonstration using massive phi^3 theory in 5+1 dimensions is chosen because it is asymptotically free and has diagram structure similar to QCD, making it a good perturbative development ground; the authors note that the massless version can already be applied to QCD quantities where masses are unimportant, such as the low-lying glueball spectrum.
