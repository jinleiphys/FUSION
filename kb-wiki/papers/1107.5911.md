# Resolutions of Identity for Some Non-Hermitian Hamiltonians. I. Exceptional Point in Continuous Spectrum

> Resolutions of identity for certain non-Hermitian Hamiltonians constructed from biorthogonal sets of their eigen- and associated functions are given for the spectral problem defined on entire axis. Non-Hermitian Hamiltonians under consideration possess the continuous spectrum and the following peculiarities are investigated: (1) the case when there is an exceptional point of arbitrary multiplicity situated on a boundary of continuous spectrum; (2) the case when there is an exceptional point situated inside of continuous spectrum. The reductions of the derived resolutions of identity under narrowing of the classes of employed test functions are revealed. It is shown that in the case (1) some of associated functions included into the resolution of identity are normalizable and some of them may be not and in the case (2) the bounded associated function corresponding to the exceptional point does not belong to the physical state space. Spectral properties of a SUSY partner Hamiltonian for the Hamiltonian with an exceptional point are examined.

## Key claim

The paper constructs resolutions of identity for non-Hermitian Hamiltonians with continuous spectrum that contain an exceptional point, showing that when the exceptional point lies on the boundary of the continuous spectrum some associated functions are normalizable and some are not, while when it lies inside the continuous spectrum the bounded associated function does not belong to the physical state space.

## Method

The authors study a sequence of PT-symmetric Hamiltonians \(h_n = -\partial^2 + n(n+1)/(x-z)^2\) with \(\operatorname{Im} z \neq 0\), which are intertwined by ladder operators and have a free-particle Hamiltonian \(h_0\) as their base. They construct explicit eigenfunctions for the continuous spectrum and chains of eigen- and associated functions at the exceptional point \(E=0\), then derive biorthogonality relations and build resolutions of identity by deforming the integration contour in the complex \(k\)-plane around \(k=0\). The reductions of these resolutions under narrowing of the test function classes are examined, and spectral properties of a SUSY partner Hamiltonian are analyzed.

## Key numbers

* The exceptional point at \(E=0\) for Hamiltonian \(h_n\) has algebraic multiplicity \(n\), and the Green function as a function of \(k=\sqrt{E}\) has a pole of order \(2n+1\) at \(k=0\).
* For the case \(n=2\), the Hamiltonian is \(h_2 = -\partial^2 + 6/(x-z)^2\); the normalizable eigenfunction is \(\psi_{20}(x) = -3/[\sqrt{2\pi}(x-z)^2]\) and the bounded associated function is \(\psi_{21}(x) = -1/(2\sqrt{2\pi})\).
* The resolution of identity for \(n=2\) reduces to different forms depending on test function class: for test functions from \(CL_\gamma\) with \(\gamma > -1\) the full form (Equation res7) holds; for \(\gamma > 1\) a reduced form (Equation res10) holds; for \(\gamma > 3\) only the integral plus the two \(\psi_{20}\psi_{21}\) terms remain (Equation res6).
* The coefficients \(\beta_j\) for the \(\varepsilon\)-dependent associated functions are given: \(\beta_0 = 1\), \(\beta_1 = 1/6\), \(\beta_2 = 31/360\), \(\beta_3 = 863/15120\), \(\beta_4 = 76813/1814400\).

## Context

This work extends the study of exceptional points in non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, previously examined by Berry (2004), Heiss (2000), and others, from the discrete spectrum case to the continuous spectrum. The authors build on their own earlier work (Andrianov and Sokolov, 2006; Andrianov and Cannata, 2007) and provide the foundation for a companion paper (Sokolov, Part II) containing detailed proofs. The results enable the construction of complete biorthogonal bases in rigged Hilbert spaces for non-Hermitian Hamiltonians with continuous spectra, and the SUSY partner construction offers a tool for regulating algebraic multiplicities at exceptional points.
