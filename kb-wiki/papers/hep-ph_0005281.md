# Chiral Symmetry and the Parity-Violating $NNπ$ Yukawa Coupling

> We construct the complete SU(2) parity-violating (PV) $π, N, Δ$ interaction Lagrangian with one derivative, and calculate the chiral corrections to the PV Yukawa $NNπ$ coupling constant $h_π$ through ${\cal O}(1/Λ_χ^3)$ in the leading order of heavy baryon expansion. We discuss the relationship between the renormalized $\hpi$, the measured value of $\hpi$, and the corresponding quantity calculated microscopically from the Standard Model four-quark PV interaction.

## Key claim

The renormalized parity-violating (PV) $NN\pi$ Yukawa coupling $h_\pi^{\rm EFF}$ extracted from experiment differs significantly from the bare coupling $h_\pi^{\rm BARE}$ computed in microscopic models, because chiral loop corrections and contributions from other PV low-energy constants (such as $h_A^1$, $h_\Delta$, and $h_A^\Delta$) are substantial and have been omitted in previous analyses.

## Method

The authors constructed the complete SU(2) parity-violating $\pi, N, \Delta$ interaction Lagrangian at one-derivative order, then computed all chiral loop corrections to the PV Yukawa $NN\pi$ coupling through ${\cal O}(1/\Lambda_\chi^3)$ using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) with dimensional regularization. They summed non-vanishing contributions from nucleon and pion wavefunction renormalization, vertex corrections from PV Yukawa and axial $NN\pi\pi$ interactions, and $\Delta$ intermediate state contributions, then expressed the renormalized coupling $h_\pi^{\rm EFF}$ in terms of the bare coupling and other PV low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

* The renormalized coupling is given by $h_\pi^{\rm EFF} = 0.5 h_\pi + 0.25 h_A^1 - 0.24 h_\Delta + 0.079 h_A^\Delta$, using $\delta = 0.3$ GeV, $g_A = 1.267$, $g_{\pi N\Delta} = 1.05$, and $\mu = \Lambda_\chi = 1.16$ GeV.
* The experimental value from $^{18}$F PV $\gamma$-decay is $h_\pi = (0.73 \pm 2.3) g_\pi$, where $g_\pi = 3.8 \times 10^{-8}$.
* The DDH/FCDH "best value" for $h_\pi$ is $7 g_\pi$, with a "reasonable range" of $(0 \to 30) g_\pi$.
* The DDH/FCDH "best value" for $h_\Delta$ is $-20 g_\pi$, with a "reasonable range" of $(-51 \to 0) g_\pi$.
* The wavefunction renormalization reduces the dependence on the bare coupling by roughly 50%.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear parity violation pioneered by Kaplan and Savage (1993) and the meson-exchange framework of DDH (1980) and FCDH (1991), which used SU(6)$_w$ symmetry and the quark model to compute PV couplings. The authors show that the discrepancy between the $^{18}$F experimental result and the DDH "best value" may be less troubling than previously thought, because the renormalized coupling $h_\pi^{\rm EFF}$ receives large contributions from chiral loops and other PV constants that are not included in microscopic calculations. This work enables a more complete analysis of hadronic PV data that includes the full chiral structure of the PV interaction, and motivates future unquenched lattice QCD calculations of $h_\pi$ using light quarks.
