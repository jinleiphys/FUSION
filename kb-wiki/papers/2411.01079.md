# Adiabatic state preparation for digital quantum simulations of QED in 1 + 1D

> Quantum electrodynamics in 1 + 1D (QED2) shares intriguing properties with QCD, including confinement, string breaking, and interesting phase diagram when the non-trivial topological $θ$-term is considered. Its lattice regularization is a commonly used toy model for quantum simulations of gauge theories on near-term quantum devices. In this work, we address algorithms for adiabatic state preparation in digital quantum simulations of QED2. We demonstrate that, for specific choices of parameters, the existing adiabatic procedure leads to level crossing between states of different charge sectors, preventing the correct preparation of the ground state. We further propose a new adiabatic Hamiltonian and verify its efficiency in targeting systems with a nonzero topological $θ$-term and in studying string breaking phenomena.

## Key claim

The existing adiabatic state preparation procedure for QED in 1+1D, which conserves total charge throughout the evolution, fails to prepare the correct ground state when the true ground state lies in a nonzero charge sector or when level crossing occurs between charge sectors; the authors propose a new adiabatic Hamiltonian that mixes charge sectors and successfully prepares the ground state across the full parameter space of topological angle and fermion mass.

## Method

The authors compare two adiabatic state preparation protocols for the lattice Schwinger model with open boundary conditions, using a first-order Suzuki-Trotter decomposition for the time evolution. The standard approach (H_A1) linearly interpolates the mass, hopping, and theta parameters while preserving total charge, starting from a charge-zero product state. The new approach (H_A2) uses an initial Hamiltonian proportional to a staggered magnetic field term that does not commute with the charge operator, allowing transitions between charge sectors during the adiabatic evolution. The prepared states are compared with exact diagonalization results for ground state energy and total charge expectation values across the m-theta parameter space, both with and without external static charges.

## Key numbers

- For a lattice with N=16 sites, L=8.19, g=1.8, using M=1000 Trotter steps and total evolution time T=60, the new adiabatic procedure (H_A2) with beta*N=30 reproduces the ground state energy with a relative error at worst of order 10^{-2}
- The standard procedure (H_A1) with m_0=0.5, theta_0=0 never leaves the charge 0 sector, failing to prepare the correct ground state for the region where theta/pi is between approximately 1 and 1.5, where the true ground state lies in the charge 1 sector
- For the new procedure, the optimal beta parameter lies in the interval beta*N in [20, 40] for the system sizes studied
- The charge expectation value from H_A2 becomes non-integer near boundaries between charge sectors, indicating leakage due to a closing gap; this occurs for specific static charge separations where the gap vanishes

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic state preparation approach for the lattice Schwinger model developed by Chakraborty et al. (2022) and Honda et al. (2022), which used a charge-conserving adiabatic path. The authors demonstrate that the charge-conserving approach fails for nonzero topological theta angles and when static charges are present, because the ground state can belong to a nonzero charge sector. The new protocol enables study of the phase diagram with a topological theta term (a regime with a sign problem for classical Monte Carlo) and investigation of string breaking phenomena, complementing recent experimental realizations by Gonzalez et al. (2024) and De et al. (2024). The method is particularly relevant for open boundary conditions and could be extended to C* boundary conditions used in state-of-the-art QCD+QED simulations.
