# Critical point and Bose-Einstein condensation in pion matter

> The Bose-Einstein condensation and the liquid-gas first order phase transition are studied in the interacting pion matter. Two phenomenological models are used: the mean-field model and the hybrid model. Free model parameters are fixed by fitting the lattice QCD data on the pion Bose condensate density at zero temperature. In spite of some minor differences the two model demonstrate an identical qualitative and very close quantitative behavior for the thermodynamic functions and electric charge fluctuations. A peculiar property of the considered models is an intersection of the Bose-Einstein condensation line and the line of the first order phase transition at the critical end point.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in interacting pion matter, the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) line and the first-order phase transition (FOPT) line intersect at the critical endpoint (CP), a peculiar property that depends on the smallness of attractive interactions; the authors hedge that this result holds for two specific phenomenological models (mean-field and hybrid) whose parameters are fixed by fitting lattice QCD data at zero temperature, and that the presence or absence of the FOPT remains an open question because the lattice data cannot give indisputable estimates of the attractive parameter \(A\).

## Method

The authors constructed two phenomenological models of interacting pion matter: a mean-field model with a potential \(U(n) = -An + Bn^2\) (attractive and repulsive terms) and a hybrid model combining a repulsive mean field \(Bn^2\) with an effective pion mass \(m^* = m - A n_s\) for attractive interactions. Free parameters \(A\) and \(B\) were fixed by fitting lattice QCD data on the pion Bose condensate density at zero temperature. Thermodynamic functions and electric charge fluctuations (scaled variance, skewness, kurtosis) were then calculated across the \((\mu, T)\) phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- Fitting parameters: \(A = 0.05\;m^{-2}\), \(B = 1.30\;m^{-5}\), where \(m = 140\) MeV is the pion mass.
- Ground state of the pion Bose condensate at \(T=0\): number density \(n_0 \cong 0.029\;m^3 \cong 0.01\;\text{fm}^{-3}\), binding energy per pion \(W \cong -0.00036\;m \cong -0.05\;\text{MeV}\).
- Critical temperature of the CP: \(T_c \cong 0.21\;m \cong 28\;\text{MeV}\).
- At the CP, the electric charge density \(n_Q^c \cong n_c = \frac{2}{3} n_0\).
- The scaled variance \(\omega_Q\) becomes infinite at the CP and finite on the BEC line (except at the CP) due to repulsive interactions; on the BEC line, \(\omega_Q \cong \frac{T}{n_+} \left[ \frac{dU}{dn_+} \right]^{-1}\).
- At \(T=124\) MeV, the model's \(n_Q\) as a function of \(\mu\) was compared with lattice QCD data, with an additional contribution from \(\rho^\pm\) mesons (mass \(m_\rho \cong 775\) MeV, degeneracy \(g_\rho=3\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of Bose-Einstein condensation and first-order phase transitions in pion matter, following earlier investigations using an effective mass model with \(\phi^4\) and \(\phi^6\) interactions (PhysRevC.103.065201, 2021) which also found the CP lying on the BEC line. The authors compare their mean-field and hybrid models to lattice QCD simulations by Brandt et al. (2017, 2018) and to leading-order chiral perturbation theory by Son and Stephanov (2000). The results enable future studies of resonance contributions (e.g., \(\rho\) mesons) at higher temperatures and further exploration of whether the FOPT is present or absent in pion matter.
