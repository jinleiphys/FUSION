# Covariant density functional theory for antimagnetic rotation

> Following the previous letter on the first microscopic description of the antimagnetic rotation (AMR) in 105Cd, a systematic investigation and detailed analysis for the AMR band in the frame-work of tilted axis cranking (TAC) model based on covariant density functional theory are carried out. After performing the microscopic and self-consistentTAC calculations with an given density functional, the configuration for the observed AMR band in 105Cd is obtained from the single-particle Routhians. With the configuration thus obtained, the tilt angle for a given rotational frequency is determined self-consistently by minimizing the total Routhian with respect to the tilt angle. In such a way, the energy spectrum, total angular momenta, kinetic and dynamic moments of inertia, and the B(E2) values for the AMR band in 105Cd are calculated. Good agreement with the data is found. By investigating microscopically the contributions from neutrons and protons to the total angular momentum, the "two-shears-like" mechanism in the AMR band is clearly illus-trated. Finally, the currents leading to time-odd mean fields in the Dirac equation are presented and discussed in detail. It is found that they are essentially determined by the valence particles and/or holes. Their spatial distribution and size depend onthe specific single-particle orbitals and the rotational frequency.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the tilted axis cranking model based on covariant density functional theory can reproduce the experimental antimagnetic rotation band in 105Cd without adjustable parameters, but the calculated dynamic moments of inertia underestimate the data even though the overall trend of constant values is reproduced.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent tilted axis cranking calculations using a relativistic point-coupling Lagrangian (interaction PC-PK1) with no additional parameters, solving the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells. The configuration for the AMR band in 105Cd was fixed at the bandhead with the odd neutron in the lowest h11/2 level and the remaining nucleons filled self-consistently, while the tilt angle was determined by minimizing the total Routhian for each rotational frequency. The rotational frequency was increased in steps of 0.1 MeV, and the occupation of levels was tracked by maximizing wavefunction overlaps between successive frequency steps.

## Key numbers

* The energy splitting of single-particle Routhians in 105Cd ranges from 0 to 2.3 MeV due to the Coriolis term breaking time reversal invariance.
* The rotational frequency was increased in steps of δ(ħΩ) = 0.1 MeV for the configuration tracking procedure.
* The kinetic energy increases monotonously up to roughly 6 MeV along the band, while the interaction energy declines monotonously up to roughly 2 MeV.
* The Coulomb energy and center-of-mass correction energy change by less than 0.5 MeV along the band.
* The total angular momentum increases almost linearly with rotational frequency and agrees well with experimental data.
* The calculated dynamic moments of inertia underestimate the experimental data, though both kinetic and dynamic moments of inertia stay roughly constant.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous letter (Zhao 2011) that provided the first microscopic description of antimagnetic rotation in 105Cd, offering a more detailed analysis including Routhians, energies, angular momenta, moments of inertia, electromagnetic transitions, and nuclear currents. The calculations are based on covariant density functional theory, which treats time-odd fields (nuclear magnetism) self-consistently through Lorentz invariance, unlike previous non-relativistic approaches using the pairing plus quadrupole model (Frauendorf 2001) or microscopic-macroscopic models (Simons 2003, Simons 2005) that either neglected polarization effects or treated them only partially. The results enable future microscopic investigations of antimagnetic rotation in other mass regions and provide a foundation for understanding the "two-shears-like" mechanism through self-consistent current distributions.
