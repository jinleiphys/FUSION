# Pionless Effective Field Theory Evaluation of Nuclear Polarizability in Muonic Deuterium

> We calculate the longitudinal structure function of the deuteron up through next-to-next-to-leading order in the framework of pionless effective field theory. We use these results to compute the two-photon polarizability contribution to Lamb shift in muonic deuterium, which can be utilized to extract the nuclear charge radius of the deuteron. We present analytical expressions order-by-order for the relevant transition matrix elements and the longitudinal structure function, and we give numerical results for the corresponding contributions to the Lamb shift. We also discuss the impact of relativistic and other higher-order effects. We find agreement with previous calculations and explain the accuracy of our calculation.

## Key claim
The authors calculate the two-photon polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic deuterium using pionless effective field theory up through next-to-next-to-leading order, finding agreement with previous calculations that used chiral effective field theory or the Argonne V18 potential, but with the advantage of a systematic, model-independent uncertainty estimate.

## Method
The longitudinal structure function of the deuteron was computed from transition matrix elements for the electric density operator, evaluated diagrammatically in pionless effective field theory at leading order, next-to-leading order, and next-to-next-to-leading order. These results were then integrated with the longitudinal kernel to obtain the inelastic two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift, while the elastic contribution (third Zemach moment) was computed from the deuteron electric form factor previously derived in the same framework.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: B_d = 2.2246 MeV
- Effective range in the triplet channel: ρ_d = 1.764 fm
- Expansion parameter: γ ρ_d ≈ 0.4, where γ = √(m_N B_d) is the deuteron binding momentum
- Nucleon mass used: m_N = 938.92 MeV (two times the proton-neutron reduced mass)
- The longitudinal kernel in the non-relativistic limit: K_L^(NR) = 1/[q^2(ω + q^2/2m_μ)]
- Deuteron electric form factor at NNLO: F_E(q^2) = [1/(1-ρ_d γ)] × [4γ/q × arctan(q/(4γ)) - ρ_d γ]

## Context
This work extends the approach of Rosenfelder (1983) and Leidemann (1995) for two-photon exchange in muonic deuterium, and is consistent with calculations using the Argonne V18 potential (Pachucki 2011), chiral effective field theory (Hernandez 2014, 2018), and zero-range approximated interactions. The pionless EFT framework provides a systematic expansion with transparent regulator dependence, enabling rigorous uncertainty estimation that is complementary to the dispersion relation analysis of Carlson (2014). This work enables improved extraction of the deuteron charge radius from muonic deuterium spectroscopy, which is relevant to the proton radius puzzle.
