# k-dependent SU(4) model of high-temperature superconductivity and its coherent-state solutions

> We extend the SU(4) model [1-5] for high-Tc superconductivity to an SU(4)k model that permits explicit momentum (k) dependence in predicted observables. We derive and solve gap equations that depend on k, temperature, and doping from the SU(4)k coherent states, and show that the new SU(4)k model reduces to the original SU(4) model for observables that do not depend explicitly on momentum. The results of the SU(4)k model are relevant for experiments such as ARPES that detect explicitly k-dependent properties. The present SU(4)k model describes quantitatively the pseudogap temperature scale and may explain why the ARPES-measured T* along the anti-nodal direction is larger than other measurements that do not resolve momentum. It also provides an immediate microscopic explanation for Fermi arcs observed in the pseudogap region. In addition, the model leads to a prediction that even in the underdoped regime, there exist doping-dependent windows around nodal points in the k-space, where antiferromagnetism may be completely suppressed for all doping fractions, permitting pure superconducting states to exist.

## Key claim

The SU(4)_k model extends the earlier SU(4) model to include explicit momentum (k) dependence in predicted observables, and its coherent-state solutions yield k-dependent gap equations that reduce to the original k-independent SU(4) results when averaged over momentum. The model claims to describe quantitatively the pseudogap temperature scale and may explain why the ARPES-measured T* along the anti-nodal direction is larger than other measurements that do not resolve momentum, but these results depend on the specific approximations used to obtain analytical solutions, including the replacement of summation quantities with mean values and the assumption of proportionality between average and k-dependent gaps.

## Method

The authors construct a k-dependent SU(4)_k Hamiltonian by restoring momentum dependence to the single-particle energy and interaction strengths while preserving the SU(4) symmetry through the sign of the d-wave formfactor. They derive coupled k-dependent gap equations for the superconducting gaps (Δ_d, Δ_π) and the antiferromagnetic pseudogap (Δ_q) using a coherent-state (generalized Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) approximation. To obtain analytical solutions, they replace quantities in summations with mean values, evaluate gaps at the Fermi surface, and assume proportionality between average and k-dependent gaps, yielding simplified equations with the same structure as the original SU(4) model but with k-anisotropic interaction strengths.

## Key numbers

- The maximum value of |g(k)| is g_{0k} = |1 - cos(ñk)|, which changes from 0 to 2 across the first Brillouin zone.
- The factor 1/γ̄ (relating maximum to averaged T*) takes a maximum value of 1.6 at very small dopings, falls rapidly between P=0.05 and 0.08, and reaches unity at the critical doping P=0.18.
- The critical hole-doping fraction x_{qθ} is given by x_{qθ} = sqrt[(χ γ(θ_{k_f})/γ̄ - G_0 δ(θ_{k_f})) / (χ γ(θ_{k_f})/γ̄ - G_1 δ(θ_{k_f}))].
- The k-dependent PG closure temperature is T*(k) = χ (γ(θ_{k_f})/γ̄) Ω R (1-x²) / (4k_B).
- The superconducting transition temperature is T_c(k) = G_0 δ(θ_{k_f}) Ω R x / (4k_B atanh(x)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier SU(4) model (Sun 2006, Sun 2007) for high-temperature superconductivity, which described SC gaps, pseudogaps, and transition temperatures T_c and T* for data not resolving explicit k-dependence. The new SU(4)_k model addresses experimental observations from ARPES (Norman 1998, Kanigel 2006) showing explicit momentum dependence, including Fermi arcs in the pseudogap region. The model predicts that even in the underdoped regime, doping-dependent windows around nodal points in k-space may exist where antiferromagnetism is completely suppressed, permitting pure superconducting states.
