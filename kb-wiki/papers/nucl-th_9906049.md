# Measuring longitudinal amplitudes for electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons using recoil polarization in parallel kinematics

> We propose a new method for measuring longitudinal amplitudes for electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons that exploits a symmetry relation for polarization observables in parallel kinematics. This polarization technique does not require variation of electron scattering kinematics and avoids the major sources of systematic errors in Rosenbluth separation.

# Measuring longitudinal amplitudes for electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons using recoil polarization in parallel kinematics

## Key claim

The paper proposes that for electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons in parallel kinematics (where nucleon momentum and spin are both parallel to the momentum transfer), the ratio between longitudinal and transverse cross sections can be extracted from a single recoil polarization measurement at fixed electron kinematics, without requiring the traditional Rosenbluth separation method. This claim holds under the assumptions of one-photon exchange and parity conservation, but does not require dominance of any particular resonance.

## Method

The author derives symmetry relations for helicity amplitudes in parallel and antiparallel kinematics, showing that only two independent amplitudes (one transverse, one longitudinal) contribute in each case. Using these relations, the longitudinal component of recoil polarization is expressed in terms of the longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratio, yielding an algebraic formula that extracts this ratio from a single polarization measurement at fixed kinematics. The method is developed for the reaction p(e,e'N)x with an unpolarized target and either recoil or target polarization, and the formalism is connected to the standard CGLN multipole expansion.

## Key numbers

- The optimum kinematics for measuring the ratio R = sigma_L / sigma_T when systematic errors dominate is achieved at epsilon = 1/sqrt(2), where epsilon is the transverse polarization of the virtual photon.
- The longitudinal polarization Pi_z satisfies the bound |Pi_z| <= |h| sqrt(1 - epsilon^2), where h is the beam helicity, with the limiting value realized for a purely transverse electroproduction amplitude.
- For the N -> Delta transition near the P33(1232) resonance, the combination Re(S_{1-} + 4 S_{1+} -/+ S_{0+}) M_{1+}* can be extracted from the x-component of recoil polarization in parallel versus antiparallel kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the polarization technique demonstrated by Arnold, Carlson, and Gross (1981) for elastic nucleon form factor separation to the case of inelastic electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons. It builds upon earlier discussions of the symmetry in parallel kinematics by Boffi (1988), Giusti (1989), and Raskin and Donnelly (1989), and corrects a sign error in the multipole expansions used by Raskin and Donnelly and subsequently by Schmieden (1998). The method enables model-independent extraction of longitudinal amplitudes for transitions such as gamma_v N -> P33(1232) -> N pi or gamma_v N -> S11(1535) -> N eta, and offers an alternative to the traditional R_LT response function approach that Mertz et al. (1999) showed current models fail to reproduce reliably.
