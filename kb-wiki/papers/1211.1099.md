# Halos in a deformed Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> In this contribution we present some recent results about neutron halos in deformed nuclei. A deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum has been developed and the halo phenomenon in deformed weakly bound nuclei is investigated. These weakly bound quantum systems present interesting examples for the study of the interdependence between the deformation of the core and the particles in the halo. Magnesium and neon isotopes are studied and detailed results are presented for the deformed neutron-rich and weakly bound nuclei 42Mg. The core of this nucleus is prolate, but the halo has a slightly oblate shape. This indicates a decoupling of the halo orbitals from the deformation of the core. The generic conditions for the existence of halos in deformed nuclei and for the occurrence of this decoupling effect are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the deformed neutron-rich nucleus 42Mg, the neutron halo has a slightly oblate shape while the core is prolate, indicating a decoupling of the halo orbitals from the deformation of the core; this result is conditional on the specific parameter set PK1 used in the calculations, with 42Mg being the last bound nucleus in Mg isotopes under that parameter set.

## Method

The authors developed a deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DefRHBC) by expanding the quasiparticle wave functions in a Woods-Saxon basis and solving the RHB equation in blocks characterized by conserved quantum numbers m (z-component of angular momentum) and parity. For the particle-particle channel, a density-dependent zero-range pairing force was used, and the scalar and vector potentials were expanded in Legendre polynomials for axially deformed nuclei with spatial reflection symmetry. Magnesium and neon isotopes were studied, with detailed results presented for 42Mg using the parameter sets NL3 and PK1.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation of 42Mg ground state: beta ≈ 0.41
- Two-neutron separation energy of 42Mg: S2n ≈ 0.22 MeV
- Deeply bound single neutron levels in 42Mg: ε_can < -2 MeV (core)
- Weakly bound single neutron levels in 42Mg: ε_can > -0.3 MeV (halo)
- 42Mg is the last bound nucleus in Mg isotopes when using the PK1 parameter set

## Context

This work extends the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory developed by Meng (1996, 1998) for spherical nuclei to deformed systems, building on the Woods-Saxon basis approach proposed by Zhou (2003). The results for 42Mg predict a decoupling of halo deformation from core deformation, which had not been seen in earlier spherical halo studies. This enables future experimental investigations of deformed halos through longitudinal momentum distributions in single-particle removal reactions, as proposed by Navin (1998) and Sakharuk (1999), and may influence understanding of sub-barrier capture processes in heavy ion collisions.
