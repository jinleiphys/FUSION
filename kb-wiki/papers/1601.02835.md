# Hidden-charm Pentaquark States in Heavy Ion Collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> In the framework of the quark combination, we derive the yield formulas and study the yield ratios of the hidden-charm pentaquark states in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We propose some interesting yield ratios which clearly exhibit the production relationships between different hidden-charm pentaquark states. We show how to employ a specific quark combination model to evaluate the yields of exotic $P_c^+(4380)$, $P_c^+(4450)$ and their partners on the basis of reproducing the yields of normal identified hadrons, and execute the calculations in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76$ TeV as an example.

## Key claim

The authors derive yield formulas for hidden-charm pentaquark states using the quark combination mechanism, but the results depend on three explicit assumptions: flavor independence of the normalized joint momentum distribution, factorization of the kernel function, and identical internal configurations for all hidden-charm pentaquark species. The yield ratios are claimed to be independent of collision energy, colliding nuclei, and centrality only under these assumptions.

## Method

The authors start from a general quark-antiquark system and derive the average number of hidden-charm pentaquark states using the quark combination mechanism, introducing a factorized kernel function with spin and parity weighting factors. They then apply a specific quark combination model (SDQCM) extended to include multiquark states, using a one-dimensional rapidity axis with a uniform joint distribution, and calculate yields for central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The model reproduces yields of normal identified hadrons first, then estimates pentaquark yields using the same framework.

## Key numbers

- The relative production ratio of J=3/2 to J=1/2 pentaquark states (R_{J31}) is taken as 1.6, and that of J=5/2 to J=1/2 (R_{J51}) as 0.6, based on simple spin counting.
- The relative production ratio of L=1 to L=0 pentaquark states (R_{P10}) is taken as 0.258, determined by the Wigner function method from ExHIC2011PRL.
- The calculations are performed for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_{NN}) = 2.76 TeV as an example.
- The strangeness suppression parameter lambda_s in heavy ion collisions is stated to lie in the range 0.4-0.5, based on RQWang2012PRC.

## Context

This work extends the quark combination model (SDQCM) previously applied to normal hadrons by the Shandong group (Shao 2005, Xie 1988, Shao 2009) to include exotic multiquark states, specifically hidden-charm pentaquarks. It builds on the LHCb discovery of P_c+(4380) and P_c+(4450) resonances (LHCb 2015) and prior applications of the coalescence model to exotic states such as X(3872) and Theta_cs (Lee 2008, ExHIC2011). The yield ratios proposed are intended to test the combination production mechanism and guide experimental searches for additional pentaquark states in heavy ion collisions at the LHC.
