# Dynamics of strangeness production in the near threshold nucleon-nucleon collisions

> We investigate the associated strangeness Lambda K+ and Sigma0 K+ productions in proton-proton collisions within an effective Lagrangian model. The initial interaction between the two nucleons is modeled by the exchange of pi, rho, omega, and sigma mesons and the strangeness production proceeds via excitations of N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) baryonic resonances. The parameters of the model at the nucleon-nucleon-meson vertices are determined by fitting the elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering with an effective interaction based on the exchange of these four mesons, while those at the resonance vertices are calculated from the known decay widths of the resonances and from the vector meson dominance model. Experimental data taken recently by the COSY-11 collaboration are described well by this approach. The one-pion-exchange diagram dominates the production process at both higher and lower beam energies. The excitation of the N*(1650) resonance dominates both the production channels at near threshold energies. Our model with final state interaction effects among the outgoing particles included within the Watson-Migdal approximation, is able to explain the observed beam energy dependence of the ratio of the total cross sections of these two reactions.

# Dynamics of strangeness production in the near threshold nucleon-nucleon collisions

## Key claim

The effective Lagrangian model, with parameters fixed by elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering data and resonance decay widths (not freely varied), describes the COSY-11 data for both $pp \to p\Lambda K^+$ and $pp \to p\Sigma^0 K^+$ total cross sections and their ratio without requiring arbitrary normalization constants, provided final state interaction effects are included via the Watson-Migdal approximation.

## Method

The initial nucleon-nucleon interaction is modeled by exchange of $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\sigma$ mesons, with coupling constants determined by fitting elastic $pp$ and $pn$ scattering T-matrices at beam energies from 400 MeV to 4.0 GeV. Strangeness production proceeds via excitation of $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1710)$, and $N^*(1720)$ baryonic resonances; coupling constants at resonance vertices are calculated from known decay branching ratios and the vector meson dominance model. Final state interactions among the outgoing particles are included using the Watson-Migdal approximation with a Coulomb-modified effective range expansion of the phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Ratio $R$ of total cross sections $\sigma(pp \to p\Lambda K^+)/\sigma(pp \to p\Sigma^0 K^+)$ is about $28^{+6}_{-9}$ for excess energy $\epsilon < 13$ MeV (COSY-11 data, Seweryniak et al. 1999).
- At higher beam energies ($\epsilon \approx 1000$ MeV), the ratio $R$ is only about 2.5.
- Final state interaction effects account for about 60%–80% of the observed ratio $R$ for $\epsilon < 30$ MeV, and about 50% beyond this energy.
- The $N^*(1650)$ resonance dominates both production channels for $\epsilon < 30$ MeV.
- For the $pp \to pK^+\Sigma^0$ reaction, the $Q$ values (excess energy threshold for significant resonance contribution) are 36 MeV for $N^*(1650)$, 72 MeV for $N^*(1710)$, and 105 MeV for $N^*(1720)$.
- For the $pp \to pK^+\Lambda$ reaction, the $Q$ values are 115 MeV for $N^*(1650)$, 150 MeV for $N^*(1710)$, and 185 MeV for $N^*(1720)$.

## Context

This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian studies of $pp \to pK^+Y$ reactions (Shyam 1999, 2001) and applies the same framework previously used for $pp \to pp\pi^0$, $pp \to pn\pi^+$, and $pp \to ppe^+e^-$ reactions. It addresses the puzzle raised by the COSY-11 collaboration (Seweryniak et al. 1999) that the ratio $R$ is much larger near threshold than at higher energies. Prior calculations by Gasparian et al. (2000) and Sibirtsev et al. (2000) considered only $\pi$- and $K$-exchange mechanisms without baryonic resonance excitations, while this work shows that the $N^*(1650)$ resonance is essential for explaining the near-threshold data. The results enable further studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions and the properties of the $S_{11}$ $N^*(1650)$ resonance, and provide input for transport models of kaon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions.
