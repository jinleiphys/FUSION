# Freeze-out temperature from net-Kaon fluctuations at RHIC

> We compare the mean-over-variance ratio of the net-kaon distribution calculated within a state-of-the-art hadron resonance gas model to the latest experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC by the STAR collaboration. Our analysis indicates that it is not possible to reproduce the experimental results using the freeze-out parameters from the existing combined fit of net-proton and net-electric charge mean-over-variance. The strange mesons need about 10-15 MeV higher temperatures than the light hadrons at the highest collision energies. In view of the future $Λ$ fluctuation measurements, we predict the $Λ$ variance-over-mean and skewness-times-variance at the light and strange chemical freeze-out parameters. We observe that the $Λ$ fluctuations are sensitive to the difference in the freeze-out temperatures established in this analysis. Our results have implications for other phenomenological models in the field of relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that net-kaon fluctuation data from the STAR collaboration cannot be reproduced using the freeze-out parameters obtained from a combined fit of net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations; instead, strange mesons require freeze-out temperatures approximately 10-15 MeV higher than light hadrons at the highest collision energies, though this conclusion relies on assuming the system evolution follows lattice QCD isentropic trajectories.

## Method

The authors used a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with the 2016 Particle Data Group list including all one-star to four-star resonances and their full decay channels, applying the same acceptance cuts in rapidity and transverse momentum as the STAR experiment. They calculated the mean-over-variance ratio of the net-kaon distribution (χ₁^K/χ₂^K) along lattice QCD isentropic trajectories, which provide a relationship between temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B, and compared these calculations to STAR data from the Beam Energy Scan. The freeze-out parameters for kaons were extracted from the overlap regions between the theoretical bands and experimental data, while light hadron freeze-out parameters were taken from a previous combined fit of net-proton and net-charge fluctuations by Alba et al. (2014).

## Key numbers

- At √s_NN = 200 GeV, the overlap between theoretical band and experimental data corresponds to a temperature range between T ~ 163-190 MeV, which is above the light chemical freeze-out temperature T^f = 148 ± 6 MeV.
- Strange mesons need about 10-15 MeV higher temperatures than light hadrons at the highest collision energies.
- At lower collision energies, the overlap region is located around T ~ 160 MeV.
- The analysis covers baryon chemical potentials up to μ_B ~ 0-200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of freeze-out parameters beyond light hadrons (protons and electric charge) to strange mesons (kaons), building on the combined fit of net-proton and net-charge fluctuations performed by Alba et al. (2014). It addresses the long-standing tension between light and strange particle yields observed in thermal fits at both RHIC and LHC, previously noted by Floris (2014) and Adamczyk et al. (2017). The results enable future tests of the flavor hierarchy scenario through upcoming Λ fluctuation measurements from STAR, which the authors predict will show a clear separation between the light and strange freeze-out scenarios.
