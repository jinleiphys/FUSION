# Scaling approach to rigid and soft nuclear deformation through flow fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The nature of octupole deformation, whether static or vibrational, remains an open question in nuclear physics. Here, we propose a scaling approach to probe this ambiguity by triangular flow fluctuations using multi-particle cumulants, $c_{3,\varepsilon}\{4\}$, in relativistic $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions. We demonstrate that both $|c_{3,\varepsilon}\{4\}|$ and the ratio $|c_{3,\varepsilon}\{4\}/c^2_{3,\varepsilon}\{2\}|$ scale linearly with the fourth-order moment of octupole deformation, $\langle β^4_{3,\mathrm{U}} \rangle$. Combined with the known linear relation of $c_{3,\varepsilon}\{2\}$ to $\langle β^2_{3,\mathrm{U}} \rangle$, this new relation provides a direct extraction of both the mean and variance of the octupole deformation fluctuations, finally discriminating between static and dynamic origins. This work establishes a new tool to probe the static and dynamic collective modes in high-energy nuclear collisions, advancing a significant step toward refining the initial conditions of quark-gluon plasma.

# Scaling approach to rigid and soft nuclear deformation through flow fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the fourth-order cumulant of triangular flow, $|c_{3,\varepsilon}\{4\}|$, and its ratio to the squared second-order cumulant, $|c_{3,\varepsilon}\{4\}/c^2_{3,\varepsilon}\{2\}|$, scale linearly with the fourth-order moment of octupole deformation $\langle \beta^4_{3,\mathrm{U}} \rangle$ in ultra-central $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions, enabling discrimination between static (rigid) and vibrational (soft) octupole deformation, but only under the assumption that the deformation parameters follow Gaussian fluctuations.

## Method

The authors derive analytical relations connecting the second- and fourth-order cumulants of the initial-state eccentricity ($c_{3,\varepsilon}\{2\}$ and $c_{3,\varepsilon}\{4\}$) to the second- and fourth-order moments of the octupole deformation parameter $\beta_3$ ($\langle \beta_3^2 \rangle$ and $\langle \beta_3^4 \rangle$). They validate these relations using the \texttt{T\raisebox{-.5ex}{R}ENTo} initial condition model for $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions at 193 GeV, systematically varying the mean deformation $\bar{\beta}_3$ and its variance $\sigma_{\beta_3}$ while keeping $\langle \beta_3^2 \rangle$ fixed at 0.01, and comparing results to $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The second-order moment of octupole deformation for $^{238}$U is constrained to $\langle \beta_\mathrm{3,U}^2 \rangle = \bar{\beta}_\mathrm{3,U}^2 + \sigma_{\beta_\mathrm{3,U}}^2 \approx 0.01$ from prior two-particle triangular flow measurements.
- The \texttt{T\raisebox{-.5ex}{R}ENTo} simulations use seven cases (U$_{\text{case 0}}$ through U$_{\text{case 6}}$) with $\langle \beta_3^2 \rangle = 0.01$ fixed, while $\langle \beta_3^4 \rangle$ ranges from 0.000100 (rigid, $\bar{\beta}_3=0.10$, $\sigma_{\beta_3}=0.00$) to 0.000300 (soft, $\bar{\beta}_3=0.00$, $\sigma_{\beta_3}=0.100$).
- The nuclear radius for $^{238}$U is $R_{\rm 0,U}=6.81$ fm with surface diffuseness $a_{\rm U}=0.55$ fm, quadrupole deformation $\beta_{\rm 2, U}=0.28$, and hexadecapole deformation $\beta_{\rm 4, U}=0.09$.
- For $^{197}$Au, the parameters are $R_{\rm 0,Au}=6.62$ fm, $a_{\rm AU}=0.52$ fm, $\beta_{\rm 2, Au}=0.14$, $\gamma_{\rm Au}=45^{\circ}$.
- The \texttt{T\raisebox{-.5ex}{R}ENTo} model uses reduced thickness parameter $p=0$, nucleon width $w=0.5$ fm, number of constituents per nucleon $n_c=1$, and minimum nucleon-nucleon distance $d_\text{min}=0.9$ fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies that established the sensitivity of two-particle triangular flow $\langle v_3^2 \rangle$ to the mean square octupole deformation $\langle \beta_3^2 \rangle$ in $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2025; Zhang et al., 2025). Those measurements found $\beta_{\rm 3,U} \sim 0.08-0.1$ but could not distinguish static from vibrational deformation. The present scaling approach using four-particle cumulants provides the missing sensitivity to the fourth-order moment $\langle \beta_3^4 \rangle$, enabling extraction of both the mean $\bar{\beta}_3$ and variance $\sigma_{\beta_3}^2$ of the octupole deformation distribution. This method can be applied to existing RHIC data and future LHC measurements, and the authors note that higher-order cumulants ($c_{n,\varepsilon}\{6\}$ and $c_{n,\varepsilon}\{8\}$) could further probe non-Gaussian fluctuations such as skewness and kurtosis of the deformation parameters.
