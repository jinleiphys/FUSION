# Searching for entanglement in final polarization states of the neutron-proton scattering

> We investigate polarization states of the outgoing neutron-proton ($np$) pair in elastic polarized neutron and proton scattering, aiming to find unambiguous evidence for entanglement of their spin states. To obtain complete information about these states, we calculate, using the high precision nucleon-nucleon potential AV18, the final polarizations of the neutron and proton as well as their spin correlation coefficients, which unequivocally define the corresponding spin density matrix. We compute all terms contributing to polarizations and spin correlations, e.g. not only induced polarizations and correlations resulting from unpolarized $np$ scattering, but also contributions from single polarization and correlation transfers from individual polarized incoming nucleons, and, for the first time, allotment to both quantities stemming from a doubly spin polarized initial state. We find that for the most part the final spin states are statistical mixture of states.The only pure states occur for highly polarized incoming neutrons and protons with maximal polarizations. By quantifying the degree of entanglement through entanglement power and concurrence, we observed that the entanglement of impure final states increases with energy. Among the pure spin states resulting from incoming states with maximal neutron and proton polarizations, we found, at $E_{lab}=100$~MeV, cases of strongly entangled Bell-type states with only a small admixture of entanglement-spoiling contributions.

## Key claim

The authors find that final spin states of the outgoing neutron-proton pair in elastic scattering are predominantly statistical mixtures, with pure states occurring only when both incoming nucleons have maximal polarizations. The abstract's claim of "strongly entangled Bell-type states" is qualified by the condition that these appear only at specific energies and scattering angles, and even then contain "only a small admixture of entanglement-spoiling contributions."

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using the high-precision AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential in momentum-space partial-wave basis, including all states with total angular momenta up to j_max=5. They computed all contributions to final neutron and proton polarizations and spin correlation coefficients: induced terms from unpolarized scattering, single polarization transfers from each polarized incoming nucleon, and for the first time, double polarization transfers from a doubly spin-polarized initial state. The degree of entanglement was quantified using entanglement power and concurrence measures applied to the resulting spin density matrices.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed at three incoming neutron laboratory energies: E_lab = 10, 50, and 100 MeV
- At E_lab = 100 MeV, cases of strongly entangled Bell-type states were found among pure spin states resulting from incoming states with maximal neutron and proton polarizations
- Entanglement power reaches maximum value of 0.5 for maximally entangled Bell states
- Entanglement power for general entangled states follows epsilon = 2|alpha_0|^2(1 - |alpha_0|^2), vanishing at |alpha_0|^2 = 0 or 1 and reaching maximum 0.5 at alpha_0 = 1/sqrt(2)
- Incoming neutron and proton polarizations were restricted to y-components with values p_y^n, p_y^p in the range [-1.0, 1.0]

## Context

This work extends previous studies of entanglement generation in neutron-proton scattering by Beane (2019), Bai (2022, 2023, 2024), and Liu (2023), which used the S-matrix and entanglement power to study entanglement induced by the NN interaction. The present study differs by using the transition operator T instead of the S matrix, and by computing the full spin density matrix of the outgoing np pair including double spin-polarization transfers for the first time. This enables future experimental searches for entanglement signatures in np scattering by providing predictions for specific polarization observables that could be measured in experiments with polarized beams and targets.
