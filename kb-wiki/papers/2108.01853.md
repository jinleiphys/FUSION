# New form of kernel in equation for Nakanishi function

> The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude $Φ(k,p)$ is expressed, by means of the Nakanishi integral representation, via a smooth function $g(γ,z)$. This function satisfies a canonical equation $g=Ng$. However, calculations of the kernel $N$ in this equation, presented previously, were restricted to one-boson exchange and, depending on method, dealt with complex multivalued functions. Although these difficulties are surmountable, but in practice, they complicate finding the unambiguous result. In the present work, an unambiguous expression for the kernel $N$ in terms of real functions is derived. For the one-boson scalar exchange, the explicit formula for $N$ is found. With this equation and kernel, the binding energies, calculated previously, are reproduced. Their finding, as well as calculation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in the Minkowski space, become not more difficult than in the Euclidean one. The method can be generalized to any kernel given by irreducible Feynman graph. This generalization is illustrated by example of the cross-ladder kernel.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new, unambiguous form of the kernel \(N\) in the canonical equation \(g=Ng\) for the Nakanishi function, which avoids the complex multivalued functions that plagued previous formulations; the new kernel is expressed entirely in terms of real functions and requires no delicate branch choices, making numerical solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space no more difficult than in Euclidean space.

## Method

The author starts from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude expressed via the Nakanishi integral representation and the previously derived kernel \(V\) for the ladder (one-boson scalar exchange) case. Instead of first integrating over the Feynman parameter \(v\) and then over the complex angle \(\phi\) (which introduced multivalued logarithms and square roots), the order of integration is reversed: the \(\phi\)-integration is performed first on an integrand containing no multivalued functions, yielding real functions, and the subsequent \(v\)-integration is carried out analytically. The resulting kernel \(N\) is separated into a regular part (from the residue at \(y=0\) when the pole lies outside the unit circle) and a singular part (from the delta-function contribution when the denominator vanishes), and explicit formulas are given for both contributions depending on the roots of a quadratic equation.

## Key numbers

* For test parameters \(\alpha = 1\), \(m = 1\), \(\mu = 0.15\), \(M = 1.9\), with variables \(\gamma = 0.1\), \(\gamma' = 1\), \(z = 0.2\), \(z' = 0.35\), the kernel value is \(N = 0.1153980591510\), matching the result from Nak2 (2020) to all digits.
* For the same parameters but \(\gamma = 0.5\), \(\gamma' = 0.1\), \(z = 0.2\), \(z' = 0.35\), the kernel value is \(N = -0.0531890858160\), again matching Nak2 (2020) to all digits.
* In the massless exchange limit \(\mu \to 0\), the kernel reduces to the Wick-Cutkosky kernel, reproducing Eq. (15) from Cutkosky (1954) and Eq. (12) from Carbonell, Karmanov, and Sargsyan (2017) for the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the program of solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation via the Nakanishi representation, which began with the ladder kernel in Kusaka and Williams (1995) and was advanced by Frederico, Salmè, and Viviani (2014) and Nak2 (2020). The previous formulations of the kernel \(N\) involved complex multivalued functions requiring careful branch selection, which complicated practical calculations. The new real-function kernel enables straightforward numerical solution of the canonical equation for \(g(\gamma,z)\) and can be generalized to any kernel given by an irreducible Feynman graph, as illustrated for the cross-ladder kernel. This makes the Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter approach a routine tool for computing binding energies and electromagnetic form factors.
