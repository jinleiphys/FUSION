# Neutron-proton scattering at next-to-next-to-leading order in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory

> We present a systematic study of neutron-proton scattering in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT), in terms of the computationally efficient radial Hamiltonian method. Our leading-order (LO) interaction consists of smeared, local contact terms and static one-pion exchange. We show results for a fully non-perturbative analysis up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), followed by a perturbative treatment of contributions beyond LO. The latter analysis anticipates practical Monte Carlo simulations of heavier nuclei. We explore how our results depend on the lattice spacing a, and estimate sources of uncertainty in the determination of the low-energy constants of the next-to-leading-order (NLO) two-nucleon force. We give results for lattice spacings ranging from a = 1.97 fm down to a = 0.98 fm, and discuss the effects of lattice artifacts on the scattering observables. At a = 0.98 fm, lattice artifacts appear small, and our NNLO results agree well with the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis for S-wave and P-wave channels. We expect the peripheral partial waves to be equally well described once the lattice momenta in the pion-nucleon coupling are taken to coincide with the continuum dispersion relation, and higher-order (N3LO) contributions are included. We stress that for center-of-mass momenta below 100 MeV, the physics of the two-nucleon system is independent of the lattice spacing.

## Key claim

The authors present a systematic study of neutron-proton scattering in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), showing that at a lattice spacing of 0.98 fm, lattice artifacts appear small and NNLO results agree well with the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis for S-wave and P-wave channels, while peripheral partial waves are expected to require further improvements including N3LO contributions and matching lattice momenta to the continuum dispersion relation.

## Method

The authors used the radial Hamiltonian method, which groups lattice points with the same radial coordinate weighted by spherical harmonics, to compute neutron-proton scattering phase shifts and mixing angles on a cubic lattice. The leading-order (LO) interaction consisted of smeared local contact terms and static one-pion exchange, with two-pion exchange potentials explicitly included at NLO and NNLO for the first time in NLEFT. The low-energy constants (LECs) of the NLO two-nucleon force were determined by chi-square minimization against neutron-proton phase shifts and mixing angles, and the analysis was performed for lattice spacings ranging from 1.97 fm down to 0.98 fm.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings studied: a = 1.97 fm down to a = 0.98 fm
- At a = 0.98 fm, lattice artifacts appear small and NNLO results agree well with the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis for S-wave and P-wave channels
- For center-of-mass momenta below 100 MeV, the physics of the two-nucleon system is independent of the lattice spacing
- The coarse lattice spacing of a = 1.97 fm corresponds to a momentum cutoff of pi / a = 314 MeV
- Typical radial parameters used: R_in ~ 24 fm, R_out ~ 28 fm, R_wall ~ 30 fm

## Context

This work extends prior NLEFT calculations of neutron-proton scattering that were performed at LO by Borasoy (2006) and at NLO by Borasoy (2007) using the spherical wall method, which were limited to a coarse lattice spacing of 1.97 fm. The radial Hamiltonian formalism was pioneered by Elhatisari (2015) for alpha-alpha scattering and applied to two-nucleon systems with a simplified model potential by Lu (2015). This study enables future NLEFT calculations of heavier nuclei with improved lattice actions and smaller lattice spacings, and provides quantitative uncertainty estimates for the LECs that propagate into predictions of nuclear ground-state energies.
