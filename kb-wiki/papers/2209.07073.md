# Analyses of the collective properties of hadronic matter in Au-Au collisions at 54.4 GeV

> We investigated the strange hadrons transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra in Au-Au collision at $\sqrt {s_{NN}}$ = 54.4 GeV in the framework of modified Hagedorn function with embedded flow. We extracted the kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_0$, transverse flow velocity $β_T$, kinetic freeze-out volume $V$, mean transverse momentum $<p_T>$, the entropy parameter $n$ and the multiplicity parameter $N_0$. We reported that all these parameters increase towards the central collisions. The larger kinetic freeze-out temperature , transverse flow velocity, kinetic freeze-out volume and the entropy parameter (n) in central collisions compared to peripheral collisions show the early decoupling of the particles in central collisions. In addition, all the above parameters are mass dependent. The kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_0$), the entropy parameter $n$ and mean transverse momentum ($<p_T>$) are larger for massive particles, while the transverse flow velocity ($β_T$), kinetic freeze-out volume ($V$) and the multiplicity parameter ($N_0$) show the opposite behavior. Larger $T_0$, $n$ and smaller $β_T$ as well as $V$ of the heavier particles indicates the early freeze-out of the heavier particles, while larger $<p_T>$ for the heavier particles evince that the effect of radial flow is stronger in heavier particles. The separate set of parameters for each particle shows the multiple kinetic freeze-out scenario, where the mass dependent kinetic freeze-out volume shows the volume differential freeze-out scenario. We also checked the correlation among different parameters, which include the correlation of $T_0$ and $β_T$, $T_0$ and $V$, $β_T$ and $V$, $<p_T>$ and $T_0$, $<p_T>$ and $β_T$, $<p_T>$ and $V$, $n$ and $T_0$, $n$ and $β_T$, and $n$ and $V$, and they all are observed to have positive correlations with each other which validates our results.

# Analyses of the collective properties of hadronic matter in Au-Au collisions at 54.4 GeV

## Key claim
The modified Hagedorn function with embedded flow describes the transverse momentum spectra of strange hadrons in Au-Au collisions at 54.4 GeV, and the extracted kinetic freeze-out parameters show a mass-dependent multiple kinetic freeze-out scenario where heavier particles freeze out earlier than lighter ones. The claim is conditional on the specific choice of the modified Hagedorn function (Eq. 6) and the restriction of parameter ranges during fitting (e.g., T0 for Ks0 was allowed to vary between 0.034 and 0.050 GeV, n restricted to 11–15.2).

## Method
The authors fitted the transverse momentum spectra of Ks0, Lambda, anti-Lambda, Xi- and anti-Xi+ measured by the STAR Collaboration in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 54.4 GeV across seven centrality intervals (0–5% to 60–80%) using a modified Hagedorn function with embedded transverse flow (Eq. 6). The fit extracted kinetic freeze-out temperature T0, transverse flow velocity beta_T, kinetic freeze-out volume V, entropy parameter n, multiplicity parameter N0, and mean transverse momentum <pT> for each particle species and centrality bin. The fitting procedure was repeated three times with different initial parameter values to verify stability.

## Key numbers
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T0 for Ks0 ranges from 0.034 ± 0.005 GeV (60–80% centrality) to 0.050 ± 0.005 GeV (0–5% centrality)
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T0 for Xi- ranges from 0.079 ± 0.005 GeV (60–80% centrality) to 0.104 ± 0.005 GeV (0–5% centrality)
- Transverse flow velocity beta_T for Ks0 ranges from 0.482 ± 0.011 (60–80% centrality) to 0.620 ± 0.008 (0–5% centrality)
- Transverse flow velocity beta_T for Xi- ranges from 0.214 ± 0.008 (60–80% centrality) to 0.300 ± 0.007 (0–5% centrality)
- Kinetic freeze-out volume V for Ks0 ranges from 4530 ± 163 fm^3 (60–80% centrality) to 5568 ± 185 fm^3 (0–5% centrality)
- Kinetic freeze-out volume V for Xi- ranges from 2800 ± 120 fm^3 (60–80% centrality) to 3700 ± 115 fm^3 (0–5% centrality)
- Entropy parameter n for Ks0 ranges from 11.0 ± 1.2 (60–80% centrality) to 15.2 ± 0.5 (0–5% centrality)
- Entropy parameter n for Xi- ranges from 16.5 ± 1.1 (60–80% centrality) to 21.1 ± 2.1 (0–5% centrality)

## Context
This work extends the study of kinetic freeze-out scenarios in heavy-ion collisions to sqrt(s_NN) = 54.4 GeV, a beam energy scan energy where STAR collected high statistics data in 2017. The results support a multiple kinetic freeze-out scenario (as previously reported by the authors in Waqas et al. (2021) for non-strange, strange and multi-strange particles) and a volume differential freeze-out scenario, in contrast to the single freeze-out scenario reported by some earlier works (e.g., ref. [44] in the paper). The positive correlations among all extracted parameters validate the consistency of the modified Hagedorn approach. The work enables future comparisons with charm particle freeze-out, which the authors expect to occur earlier than multi-strange particles.
