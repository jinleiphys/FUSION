# Nuclear information entropy, gravitational form factor, and glueballs in AdS/QCD

> The nuclear configurational entropy (NCE) is employed to derive two parameters encoding the Pomeron Regge trajectory and the spectrum of closed strings, which are also related to the glueball mass, precisely matching data of the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC. In the Regge regime of holographic QCD, the (Reggeized) glueball propagator and the gravitational form factor of the proton occupy a relevant spot in AdS/QCD. They are used for deriving the cross-sections for the TeV scale proton-proton scattering process, which are the main ingredient for computing the NCE. The nuclear configurational stability is also addressed.

## Key claim

The nuclear configurational entropy (NCE) can be used to extract the Pomeron Regge trajectory parameters and closed-string spectrum parameters that match TOTEM experimental data, with the global minimum of the NCE corresponding to the most configurationally stable and experimentally dominant values of these parameters.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross-section for proton-proton scattering in the Regge regime using a holographic AdS/QCD model that incorporates the $2^{++}$ glueball exchange as the Pomeron, the Reggeized glueball propagator, and the proton gravitational form factor derived from a soft-wall AdS/QCD framework with a quadratic dilaton field. The nuclear configurational entropy is then calculated from the normalized differential cross-section as a function of the Mandelstam variables $s$ and $t$, and minimized over the parameter space of the closed-string trajectory intercept $\alpha_c(0)$ and slope $\alpha'_c$.

## Key numbers

* Global minimum of the NCE: NCE$_{\text{min}}$($\alpha^{\text{min}}_c(0),\alpha^{\prime\text{min}}_c$) = 5.4482 nat
* Optimal Pomeron intercept: $\alpha^{\text{min}}_c(0) = 1.0889$, matching TOTEM data with 0.26% accuracy
* Optimal Pomeron slope: $\alpha^{\prime\text{min}}_c = 0.3921$ GeV${}^{-2}$, matching TOTEM data with 0.74% accuracy
* Coupling constant between the glueball field and the QCD stress-energy-momentum tensor: $\lambda = 9.95 \pm 0.12$ GeV${}^{-1}$, obtained from TOTEM data
* Quadratic dilaton parameter: $\kappa = 350$ MeV, depending on the proton mass and the $\rho$ meson mass
* The differential cross-section and total cross-section were fitted in the range $0.546\ \text{TeV} \lesssim \sqrt{s} \lesssim 13$ TeV at TOTEM

## Context

This work extends the application of configurational entropy techniques to high-energy hadron scattering within holographic QCD, building on prior AdS/QCD treatments of the Pomeron and proton gravitational form factor by Xie (2019) and Domokos (2009). The NCE-derived parameters $\alpha_c(0) = 1.0889$ and $\alpha'_c = 0.3921$ GeV${}^{-2}$ corroborate the fitting results of Xie (2019), who obtained $\alpha_c(0) = 1.086 \pm 0.002$ and $\alpha'_c = 0.395 \pm 0.002$ GeV${}^{-2}$ from direct fits to TOTEM data. The approach enables the determination of Pomeron parameters from information-theoretic principles rather than direct cross-section fitting, and connects the glueball mass to the closed-string spectrum through the relation $\frac{a_c(0)}{a_c^\prime} = -\sqrt{m_{\text{g}}}$.
