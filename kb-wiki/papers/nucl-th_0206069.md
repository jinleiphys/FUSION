# Quadrupole resonances in unstable oxygen isotopes in time-dependent density-matrix formalism

> The strength functions of quadrupole modes in the unstable oxygen isotopes 22O and 24O are calculated using an extended version of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory known as the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM). It is found that TDDM gives the lowest quadrupole states which are energetically shifted upward and become significantly collective due to the coupling to two-body configurations. It is pointed out that these features of the lowest quadrupole states are similar to those obtained in the quasi-particle random phase approximation.

## Key claim

The time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) predicts that the lowest quadrupole states in the unstable oxygen isotopes 22O and 24O are shifted upward in energy and become significantly collective due to coupling to two-body configurations, with properties similar to those obtained in quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations, but the quantitative application is restricted to these two isotopes where a first-order approximation of sub-shell closure may be justified.

## Method

The authors used an extended version of time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory called time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), which determines the time evolution of one-body and two-body density matrices in a self-consistent manner. They performed static Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme III force with spin-orbit coupling to obtain initial ground states, then evolved the system using TDDM equations with a density-dependent delta-function residual interaction to obtain correlated ground states. Finally, they excited quadrupole modes by boosting single-particle wavefunctions with a quadrupole velocity field and obtained strength functions from Fourier transformation of the time-dependent quadrupole moment, using three different truncation schemes (TDDM1, TDDM2, TDDM3) that included progressively larger single-particle spaces.

## Key numbers

- In 22O, the neutron 1d5/2, 2s1/2, and 1d3/2 single-particle energies are -6.8 MeV, -2.3 MeV, and 1.3 MeV, with occupation probabilities 0.92, 0.14, and 0.05 respectively (TDDM1 calculation).
- In 24O, the neutron 1d5/2, 2s1/2, and 1d3/2 single-particle energies are -7.3 MeV, -2.3 MeV, and 0.8 MeV, with occupation probabilities 0.97, 0.89, and 0.10 respectively (TDDM1 calculation).
- The correlation energy is -4.4 MeV in 22O and -3.5 MeV in 24O (TDDM1).
- The average pairing gap is -3.1 MeV for 22O and -2.7 MeV for 24O (TDDM1), increasing to -3.6 MeV and -3.5 MeV respectively in TDDM2.
- The excitation energy of the lowest isoscalar quadrupole state in 22O increases from 3.8 MeV (TDHF) to 4.7 MeV (TDDM1) to 5.0 MeV (TDDM2).
- The excitation energy of the lowest isoscalar quadrupole state in 24O increases from 2.3 MeV (TDHF) to 3.5 MeV (TDDM1) to 4.4 MeV (TDDM2).
- Quadrupole strength below 6 MeV in 22O: 214 fm4 (TDHF), 271 fm4 (TDDM1), 300 fm4 (TDDM2).
- Quadrupole strength below 6 MeV in 24O: 163 fm4 (TDHF), 206 fm4 (TDDM1), 249 fm4 (TDDM2).
- B(E2) value for the first 2+ state in 22O: 11.0 e2fm4 (TDHF), 14.2 e2fm4 (TDDM1), 16.4 e2fm4 (TDDM2), comparable to the observed value of 21 +/- 8 e2fm4.
- B(E2) value for the first 2+ state in 24O: 3.5 e2fm4 (TDHF), 5.4 e2fm4 (TDDM1), 7.2 e2fm4 (TDDM2).
- The fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) depleted below 60 MeV is about 98% for isoscalar modes and about 93% for proton modes in all calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDDM calculations by Tohyama that did not include spin-orbit splitting, and now allows quantitative treatment of low-lying quadrupole states in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. The results are compared with QRPA calculations by Khan, Matsuo, and others, which also show that pairing correlations are essential to reproduce large B(E2) values for first 2+ states. The approach enables future studies of how two-body correlations, particularly pairing correlations, affect collective excitations in unstable nuclei, and the authors note that the modest damping of the giant quadrupole resonance in 24O compared to 16O is due to hindered coupling to two-body configurations from the spatial extension of neutron wavefunctions.
