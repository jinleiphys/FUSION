# Z+jet correlation with NLO-matched parton-shower and jet-medium interaction in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The impact of jet quenching on $Z^0$-tagged jets in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) is investigated. We employ Sharpa Monte Carlo program that combines next-to-leading order matrix elements with matched resummation of parton shower to compute the initial $Z^0$+jet production. The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model is then used to simulate the propagation, energy attenuation of and medium response induced by jet partons in the quark-gluon plasma. With both higher-order corrections and matched soft/collinear radiation as well as a sophisticated treatment of parton energy loss and medium response in LBT, our numerical calculations can provide the best description so far of all available observables of $Z^0$+jet simultaneously in both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions, in particular, the shift of the distribution in transverse momentum asymmetry $x_{\rm jZ}=p_T^{\rm jet}/p_T^Z$, the modification of azimuthal angle correlation in $Δϕ_{\rm{j}Z}=|ϕ_{\rm jet}-ϕ_Z|$ and the overall suppression of average number of $Z^0$-tagged jets per boson $R_{\rm jZ}$ at $\sqrt s =5.02$ TeV as measured by the CMS experiment. We also show that higher-order corrections to $Z^0$+jet production play an indispensable role in understanding $Z^0$+jet azimuthal angle correlation at small and intermediate $Δϕ_{\rm jZ}$, and momentum imbalance at small $x_{\rm jZ}$. Jet quenching of the sub-leading jets is shown to lead to suppression of $Z^0$+jet correlation at small azimuthal angle difference $Δϕ_{\rm jZ}$ and at small $x_{\rm jZ}$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that combining next-to-leading-order (NLO) matrix elements with matched parton-shower resummation (via Sherpa) for the initial Z0+jet production, followed by the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model for jet-medium interaction, provides the best description so far of all available Z0+jet observables simultaneously in both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. The hedge is that this agreement is achieved only with a specific choice of the strong coupling constant (αs = 0.20) in the LBT model, and that the description of the azimuthal correlation at small ΔφjZ still requires future experimental data with better statistics to be confirmed unambiguously.

## Method

The authors used the Sherpa Monte Carlo event generator, which combines NLO matrix elements with matched Collins-Soper-Sterman dipole parton shower resummation, to compute initial Z0+jet production in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV. The propagation and energy loss of jet partons through the quark-gluon plasma was simulated using the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes both elastic scattering via the linear Boltzmann equation and inelastic scattering via the higher-twist formalism for induced gluon radiation. The bulk medium evolution was provided by the 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamical model with initial conditions from the AMPT model, and jets were reconstructed from final partons using the anti-kT algorithm with cone size R = 0.3.

## Key numbers

- CMS kinematic cuts: electron pT > 20 GeV, |ηe| < 2.5 (excluding 1.44 < |ηe| < 2.47); muon pT > 10 GeV, |ημ| < 2.4; Z0 reconstructed from opposite-charge lepton pairs with 70 < Mll < 110 GeV and pTZ > 40 GeV; jet pT > 30 GeV, |ηjet| < 1.6, jet cone size R = 0.3.
- Strong coupling constant in LBT model set to αs = 0.20 for best fit to experimental data.
- In p+p collisions, approximately 75% of Z0 bosons with pTZ > 80 GeV are paired with jets, while only about 65% find tagged jets in Pb+Pb collisions due to parton energy loss.
- The asymmetry distribution xjZ = pTjet/pTZ is shifted toward smaller values in 0-30% central Pb+Pb collisions compared to p+p, with the mean value ⟨xjZ⟩ showing a difference Δ⟨xjZ⟩ ranging from 0.075 (40-50 GeV pTZ bin) to 0.143 (≥80 GeV pTZ bin).
- Pythia 6.4 underestimates the azimuthal distribution at small ΔφjZ by a factor of approximately 2 compared to CMS data in p+p collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of Z0-tagged jet production in heavy-ion collisions that used leading-order (LO) matrix elements matched to parton showers, such as the GLV model (Neufeld 2010, 2012), the Hybrid model (Casalderrey-Solana 2015), and JEWEL (Kunnawalkam Elayavalli 2016), which could only qualitatively describe CMS data. The authors demonstrate that NLO corrections and resummed soft/collinear radiation are indispensable for understanding the Z0+jet azimuthal correlation at small and intermediate ΔφjZ and the momentum imbalance at small xjZ. The LBT model had previously been used to describe photon-tagged hadron/jet production and light/heavy flavor meson suppression (Wang 2013, He 2015, Cao 2016). This work enables future studies of jet-medium interactions with higher precision, particularly for sub-leading jet suppression effects that require better experimental statistics to be confirmed.
