# The Electromagnetic Mass Difference of Pions at Low Temperature

> We compute low temperature corrections to the electromagnetic mass difference of pions in the chiral limit. The computation is done in a model independent way in the framework of chiral perturbation theory, using the background field method and the hard thermal loop approximation. We also generalize at low temperature the sum rule of Das et al. We find that the mass difference between the charged and neutral pions decreases at low temperature $T$ with respect to the T=0 value. This is so in spite of the fact that charged particles always get a thermal correction to their masses of order $\sim eT$, where $e$ is the gauge coupling constant. Our result can be understood as a consequence of the tendency towards chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic mass difference between charged and neutral pions decreases at low temperature, despite the fact that charged particles acquire a thermal mass correction of order \(eT\), because the effect is dominated by the tendency toward chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature. The result is valid only in the chiral limit (massless quarks) and for low temperatures \(T \ll f_\pi\), with corrections of order \(T^2/f_\pi^2\) being the leading thermal contributions.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop thermal effective action for soft Goldstone bosons using chiral perturbation theory extended to include electromagnetism, employing the background field method and the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation in the imaginary time formalism. They also generalized the zero-temperature sum rule of Das et al. (1967) to low temperature by using thermal correlators of vector and axial vector currents computed by Dey et al., applying soft pion theorems and current algebra at finite temperature. Both approaches were used to extract the temperature-dependent squared masses of charged pions in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature electromagnetic mass difference of pions is \(\Delta M_\pi = 4.8\) MeV (theoretical estimate using \(M_\pi = 135\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(M_\rho = 770\) MeV, \(F_\rho = 153\) MeV, \(\alpha = 1/137\)), compared to the experimental value \((\Delta M_\pi)_{\text{exp}} = 4.6\) MeV.
- The temperature-dependent squared mass difference is given by \(\Delta M_\pi^2(T) = \left(1 - \frac{T^2}{6 f_\pi^2}\right) \Delta M_\pi^2 + \pi \alpha T^2\), where \(\Delta M_\pi^2 = M^2_{\pi^\pm} - M^2_{\pi^0}\).
- The pion decay constant at low temperature is \(f_\pi(T) = f_\pi \left(1 - \frac{N}{24} \frac{T^2}{f_\pi^2}\right)\) for \(N=2\) or \(N=3\).
- The temperature-dependent coefficient \(C(T)\) is \(C(T) = C \left(1 - \frac{N}{6} \frac{T^2}{f_\pi^2}\right) + \frac{T^2 f_\pi^2}{8}\).
- The charged pion mass squared at low temperature is \(M^2_{\pi^\pm}(T) \approx M^2_{\pi^\pm} \left(1 - \frac{N}{12} \frac{T^2}{f_\pi^2}\right) + \frac{e^2 T^2}{4}\) for \(N=2\) or \(N=3\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature chiral perturbation theory computation of the pion electromagnetic mass difference by Das et al. (1967) and the later chiral Lagrangian treatment by de Rafael (1994) to finite temperature. It also generalizes the sum rule approach of Das et al. (1967) to low temperature, using thermal correlators from Dey et al. The results agree with Kapusta (1993) except that Kapusta did not include the \(T^2/f_\pi^2\) corrections. The work enables future studies of thermal effects on pseudoscalar meson masses and chiral symmetry restoration at higher temperatures, and confirms that Dashen's theorem remains valid at finite temperature in the chiral limit.
