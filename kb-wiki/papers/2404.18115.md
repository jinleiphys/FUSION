# Exploring system size dependence of jet modification in heavy-ion collisions

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, jet quenching in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) has been extensively studied, revealing important insights into the properties of the color deconfined nuclear matter. Over the past decade, there has been a surge of interest in the exploration of QGP droplets in small collision systems like $p$+$p$ or $p$+A collisions driven by the observation of collective flow phenomena. However, the absence of jet quenching, a key QGP signature, in these systems poses a puzzle. Understanding how jet quenching evolves with system size is crucial for uncovering the underlying physics. In this study, we employ the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model to investigate jet modification in $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru, $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. Our findings highlight the system size sensitivity exhibited by jet nuclear modification factor ($R_\mathrm{AA}$) and jet shape ($ρ$), contrasting to the relatively weak responses of jet mass ($M$), girth ($g$) and momentum dispersion ($p_\mathrm{T}{D}$) to system size variations. These results offer invaluable insights into the system size dependence of the QGP properties and await experimental validation at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors use the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model to show that jet nuclear modification factor ($R_\mathrm{AA}$) and jet shape ($\rho$) exhibit clear system size dependence across $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru, $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, while jet mass ($M$), girth ($g$), and momentum dispersion ($p_\mathrm{T}D$) show relatively weak sensitivity to system size variations, though the abstract's claim of "awaiting experimental validation" hedges that these are theoretical predictions not yet confirmed by data.

## Method

The authors simulate jet production using PYTHIA 8 for initial hard scatterings, model QGP evolution with the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc, and describe jet-medium interactions using the LBT model which includes both elastic and inelastic scatterings of jet partons via the Boltzmann equation. Jet observables are computed at the partonic level using the anti-$k_\mathrm{T}$ algorithm in FastJet, with medium response included through recoil and negative partons to ensure energy-momentum conservation. The strong coupling constant is fixed at $\alpha_s=0.3$, tuned to reproduce inclusive jet suppression in Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Jet $R_\mathrm{AA}$ in central Au+Au, Zr+Zr, and Ru+Ru collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV shows significant suppression, with Au+Au exhibiting stronger suppression than the smaller systems across jet cone sizes $R=0.2$, $0.3$, and $0.4$
- Jet shape ratio between A+A and $p$+$p$ collisions at $R=0.4$ shows enhancement at large $r$ with clear system size ordering: Au+Au > Zr+Zr $\approx$ Ru+Ru
- Jet mass distributions for $R=0.4$ jets with $25 < p_\mathrm{T} < 30$ GeV/$c$ show no clear difference between Au+Au and Zr+Zr (Ru+Ru) collisions in either average values or distributions
- Jet girth distributions for $R=0.3$ jets under "HardCore" selection (parton $p_\mathrm{T} > 2$ GeV/$c$) shift toward lower values in A+A compared to $p$+$p$, but do not exhibit clear system size dependence between Au+Au and Zr+Zr/Ru+Ru
- The QGP hydrodynamic evolution begins at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$, and the QGP boundary is defined by the hypersurface at $T_\mathrm{pc}=165$ MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on system size dependence of jet energy loss (Zigic 2018, Shi 2018, Katz 2019, Huss 2020, Liu 2021, Li 2021) by investigating fully reconstructed jet observables rather than only energetic hadrons, and by comparing Zr+Zr and Ru+Ru collisions to Au+Au at RHIC energy. The study is motivated by recent measurements on full jets in $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr and $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru collisions at RHIC (He 2024), and aims to provide theoretical reference for ongoing jet measurements. The results suggest that $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and jet shape are the most promising observables for bridging small and large collision systems, while jet mass, girth, and momentum dispersion may distinguish scenarios with or without QGP but are not sensitive to system size variations.
