# Variational approach for pair optimization in the nucleon pair approximation

> We propose a pair-condensate variational approach (PCV) to determine a set of the most important collective pairs in the description of low-lying states in atomic nuclei. Having available the precise details on these key collective pairs -- their spin, parity, and structure -- can be particularly useful in calculations based on the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA), helping to reduce their uncertainties. In trial calculations for the transitional Ba isotopes, our variational approach describes the evolution of quadrupole-deformation properties similar to Hartree-Fock treatments, while at the same time highlighting the $γ$ softness of $^{132}$Ba. Our approach can conclusively determine which collective pairs are critical for obtaining the lowest possible yrast, quasi-beta, quasi-gamma bands, producing both the level structure of these bands and related B(E2) values in reasonable consistency with experiment. These trial calculations suggest that with our PCV approach the NPA can be meaningfully applied to transitional nuclei with a wide spectrum of shapes. We also show that while neutron negative-parity pairs could in principle have an important impact on backbending in $^{132}$Ba, they are not favored for this nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a pair-condensate variational (PCV) approach to determine the most important collective pairs for nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) calculations, but the method is tested only on transitional Ba isotopes and the results depend on which of two phenomenological Hamiltonians (PAR-1 or PAR-2) is used, meaning the approach does not yet provide a unique or Hamiltonian-independent prescription for pair selection.

## Method

The authors construct a trial wave function as a condensate of uncoupled collective pairs (without fixed angular momentum or parity) and minimize the Hamiltonian expectation value using the Broyden-Fletcher-Goldfarb-Shanno algorithm. After variation, they decompose the optimized uncoupled pair into collective pairs with definite angular momentum and parity by orthogonalizing the structure coefficients, and compute quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ from the quadrupole operator expectation values. For high-spin states, they introduce cranking to isolate the backbending region.

## Key numbers

- For 132Ba with PAR-1 Hamiltonian: minimum energy -12.334 MeV, β = 0.106, γ < 1°; SD pairs contribute 0.463 (neutron S), 0.453 (neutron D), 0.447 (proton S), 0.489 (proton D) weights.
- For 132Ba with PAR-2 Hamiltonian: minimum energy -15.418 MeV, β = 0.102, γ = 16°; SD pairs contribute 0.428 (neutron S), 0.449 (neutron D), 0.520 (proton S), 0.444 (proton D) weights.
- For 136Ba with PAR-1: minimum energy -6.242 MeV, β = 0.040, γ = 20°; neutron S weight 0.642, neutron D weight 0.349, proton S weight 0.786, proton D weight 0.210.
- For 136Ba with PAR-2: minimum energy -7.806 MeV, β = 0.031, γ > 59°; neutron S weight 0.701, neutron D weight 0.296, proton S weight 0.911.
- At the backbend (ω_B) for 132Ba with PAR-1: neutron Lπ=10+ (H) pair weight > 0.999; with PAR-2: neutron Lπ=10+ (H) pair weight 0.945.
- PCV minimum energy is ~1.5 MeV lower than Hartree-Fock for the same Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) by providing a variational method to select collective pairs a priori, rather than relying on intuition or trial-and-error as in earlier NPA studies (Yoshinaga 2004, Lei 2011, Cheng 2016). The approach is inspired by Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov treatments from the 1980s (Refs. hfb-1, hfb-2) that demonstrated the importance of SD pairs, and by the collective-pair condensate formalism used for O(6) nuclei and Monte Carlo shell model calculations (Refs. pair-o6, mcsm). The method enables systematic application of NPA to transitional nuclei with various shapes, and can clarify which pairs (e.g., negative-parity versus (νh11/2)-2 pairs) are responsible for backbending in 132Ba.
