# Yukawa's Pion, Low-Energy QCD and Nuclear Chiral Dynamics

> A survey is given of the evolution from Yukawa's early work, via the understanding of the pion as a Nambu-Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry in QCD, to modern developments in the theory of the nucleus based on the chiral effective field theory representing QCD in its low-energy limit.

## Key claim

This survey argues that Yukawa's pion is understood as a Nambu-Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry in QCD, and that chiral effective field theory provides a systematic framework for low-energy nuclear physics, but the applicability of this framework is limited to energy-momentum scales small compared to the Delta-nucleon mass difference (about 300 MeV) unless the Delta isobar is included as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Method

The paper traces the historical evolution from Yukawa's 1935 meson theory through the development of chiral effective field theory, using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to illustrate spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the emergence of pions as Goldstone bosons. It then presents the systematic construction of chiral Lagrangians for both the pure meson sector (pion-pion interactions) and the pion-nucleon sector, expanding in powers of derivatives (external momenta) and quark masses, with low-energy constants fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 0.14 GeV, much less than the chiral symmetry breaking scale 4*pi*f_pi ~ 1 GeV
- Pion decay constant: f_pi ~ 0.09 GeV
- Average light quark mass: (1/2)(m_u + m_d) ~ 7 MeV at renormalization scale ~1 GeV
- Chiral condensate magnitude: |<psi-bar psi>| ~ (0.3 GeV)^3 at renormalization scale ~1 GeV
- Nucleon sigma term: sigma_N ~ 45-55 MeV, deduced from low-energy pion-nucleon data

## Context

This review extends the conceptual framework of Taketani et al. (1951), who proposed the hierarchy of scales governing the nucleon-nucleon interaction with one-pion exchange at long distances and two-pion exchange at intermediate distances. It connects Yukawa's original work to modern chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg, 1979; Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984) and the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961), enabling systematic calculations of low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, threshold pion photo- and electroproduction, and Compton scattering on nucleons, as well as providing a framework for understanding in-medium modifications of pions in nuclear matter and pionic atoms.
