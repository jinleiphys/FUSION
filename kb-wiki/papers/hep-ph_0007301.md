# Scenario for Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions: III. Gluons in the expanding geometry

> We derive expressions for various correlators of the gauge field and find the propagators in Hamiltonian dynamics which employs a new gauge $A^τ=0$. This gauge is a part of the wedge form of relativistic dynamics suggested earlier as a tool for the study of quantum dynamics in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We prove that the gauge is completely fixed. The gauge field is quantized and the field of radiation and the longitudinal fields are unambiguously separated. The new gauge puts the quark and gluon fields of the colliding hadrons in one Hilbert space and thus allows one to avoid factorization.

## Key claim

The paper derives the propagators and quantizes the gluon field in the gauge \(A^\tau=0\) within the wedge form of relativistic dynamics, claiming that this gauge is completely fixed provided the physical charge density \(\tau j_\tau\) vanishes at \(\tau=0\) (the moment of first nuclear contact), which is expected from the colorlessness of the nuclei.

## Method

The author linearizes the classical equations of motion for the gauge field in the \(A^\tau=0\) gauge on constant proper-time hypersurfaces, solves the inhomogeneous system using the method of variation of parameters, and separates the transverse, longitudinal, and instantaneous parts of the retarded propagator. The gauge field is then quantized by constructing Wightman functions and canonical commutation relations, with the Gauss law used to eliminate unphysical degrees of freedom. The propagator is expressed as a bilinear expansion over the free-field modes \(V^{(1)}\) and \(V^{(2)}\) for the transverse part, while the longitudinal and instantaneous parts are derived from the response to external currents and static charge densities.

## Key numbers

- The transverse modes \(V^{(1)}_{{\vec k},\nu}(x)\) and \(V^{(2)}_{{\vec k},\nu}(x)\) are normalized with the prefactor \(e^{-\pi\nu/2}/(2^{5/2}\pi k_{\bot})\) and involve Hankel functions \(H^{(2)}_{-i\nu}(k_{\bot}\tau)\) and related functions \(R^{(2)}_{\mu,-i\nu}(k_{\bot}\tau)\).
- The scalar product for vector-field states is defined as \((V,W)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}d\eta\int d^2{\vec r}\,\tau\,{\sf g}^{ik} V^{*}_{i} i{\stackrel{\leftrightarrow}{\partial}}_{\tau} W_k\).
- The instantaneous kernel for the static potential is given by \(K_i(\tau;{\vec r},\eta)= -\theta(\tau - r_{\bot})/(4\pi)\) times derivatives of \(1/R_{12}\), where \(R_{12}=(r_{\bot}^2+\tau^2\sinh^2\eta)^{1/2}\).
- The retarded propagator for the transverse field is expressed as \(\Delta^{(tr)}_{lm}(x,y)= -i\int d\nu\int d^2{\vec k}\sum_{\lambda=1,2}[V^{(\lambda)}_{\nu{\vec k};l}(x)V^{(\lambda)*}_{\nu{\vec k};m}(y)- \text{c.c.}]\).

## Context

This paper is the third in a series (preceded by papers [I] and [II] by the same author) that develops the wedge form of relativistic dynamics for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. It extends the earlier treatment of fermion fields to the gluon sector, providing a boost-invariant gauge that connects the two light-front gauges used in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and allows a smooth transition to the Bjorken expansion regime. The results enable subsequent calculation of the quark self-energy in an expanding quark-gluon system (presented in the next paper) and establish a framework for treating screening effects at early collision stages without the spurious poles that plague homogeneous axial gauges.
