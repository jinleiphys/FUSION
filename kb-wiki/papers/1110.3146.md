# Renormalization of Polyakov loops in different representations and the large-N limit

> We study the renormalization of Polyakov loops in different irreducible representations of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories at finite temperature, and investigate their behavior in the large-N limit.

# Renormalization of Polyakov loops in different representations and the large-N limit

## Key claim
The renormalized Polyakov loop in SU(4) Yang-Mills theory jumps to a value close to 1/2 at T_c and grows to near one by 2T_c, but the authors caution that these preliminary results at finite lattice spacing, finite volume, and limited temperature range do not allow conclusions about whether the loop ever exceeds unity, nor do they contradict prior SU(3) findings.

## Method
The authors perform lattice Monte Carlo simulations of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories with N from 2 to 8, using both the standard Wilson gauge action and the tree-level Symanzik-improved action on isotropic hypercubic lattices. They compute Polyakov loops in the twelve lowest non-trivial independent irreducible representations for each gauge group using Young calculus and the Weyl formula, and determine the renormalization factor Z from the constant term in the zero-temperature static quark-antiquark potential via Cornell fits to smeared Wilson loops.

## Key numbers
- For SU(4) with the tree-level improved action, the renormalized Polyakov loop in the fundamental representation jumps to a value close to 1/2 at T = T_c and reaches values near one at temperatures around 2T_c.
- The largest representation computed for SU(8) is the 336-dimensional representation.
- The coefficient of the 1/r term in the SU(4) static potential (gamma) is approximately compatible with the effective Bosonic string model prediction gamma = -pi/12 over the range of beta values investigated.
- Simulations use lattices with N_t = 5 sites in the Euclidean time direction and N_s = 20 sites in each spacelike direction for the SU(4) results shown.

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of the large-N limit for finite-temperature Yang-Mills theories, particularly the equation of state studied by the same community (Teper 2009 review, large-N pressure papers). It tests Casimir scaling of Polyakov loop free energies in different representations, following earlier tests (Casimir scaling tests), and investigates whether the large-N limit clarifies the non-monotonic temperature dependence predicted by perturbative expansions (perturbative Polyakov loop) versus the positive slope from holographic arguments (Noronha). The results enable systematic comparison of renormalized Polyakov loops across gauge groups and representations, with implications for effective models of the deconfined plasma near T_c (Pisarski 2000) and for Extended Technicolor models with dynamical fermions in different representations.
