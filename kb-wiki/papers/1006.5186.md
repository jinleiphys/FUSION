# Heavy-Light Few Fermion Clusters at Unitarity

> We examine the physics of two, three, and four heavy fermions interacting with a single light fermion via short-range interactions. Four-particle bosonic Efimov states have proven important experimentally and also been the subject of significant theoretical effort. Similar fermionic systems are just now being investigated. We find that with some simple interactions the four- and five-particle states collapse to the interaction range at smaller mass ratios than the three-body state, and also before larger clusters can collapse. These states and their excitations can be studied in cold atom experiments, providing unique insights into the role of few-body systems in many-body physics.

## Key claim
For heavy-light fermion clusters at unitarity, four- and five-particle states collapse to the interaction range at smaller mass ratios than the three-body state, and also before larger clusters can collapse. This behavior is non-universal and depends on the specific form of the two-body and three-body interactions.

## Method
The authors used variational Quantum Monte Carlo techniques to calculate ground-state energies of systems with one light fermion and N heavy fermions (N=2,3,4,9,10) interacting via short-range Poschl-Teller and Gaussian two-body potentials tuned to unitarity. They also included a three-body interaction term and used trial wave functions with anti-symmetric heavy-particle coordinates and a positive-definite light-particle function, with all coordinates measured from the system center-of-mass.

## Key numbers
- For the Poschl-Teller potential at mass ratio M/m = 13: 2H1L binding energy = -0.001(1) E₀, 3H1L = -0.088(1) E₀, 4H1L = -0.340(1) E₀ (where E₀ ≡ ħ²/(2μ r_eff²))
- For the Poschl-Teller potential at mass ratio M/m = 14: 2H1L binding energy = -0.0057(6) E₀, 3H1L = -0.135(1) E₀, 4H1L = -0.454(1) E₀
- RMS radii for 2H1L at M/m = 14.5: light particle = 1.14 r_eff, heavy particle = 0.30 r_eff (QMC results)
- RMS radii for 3H1L at M/m = 11.75: light particle = 0.42 r_eff, heavy particle = 0.24 r_eff (QMC results)
- RMS radii for 4H1L at M/m = 10.25: light particle = 0.30 r_eff, heavy particle = 0.24 r_eff (QMC results)

## Context
This work extends the study of heavy-light fermion clusters by Nishida (2008), who found three-body resonances for mass ratios 8.6 < M/m < 13.6 and assumed that four- and five-body states would not be favored. The present results contradict that assumption by showing that 4H1L and 3H1L clusters can bind at lower mass ratios than the 2H1L system. This enables future studies of strongly-interacting gases of multi-particle clusters in cold atom experiments, and suggests that a richer phase structure may exist in heterogeneous Fermi mixtures than previously considered.
