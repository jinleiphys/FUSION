# Heart-Shaped Nuclei: Condensation of Rotational-Aligned Octupole Phonons

## Key claim

The strong octupole correlations in the mass region A ≈ 226 are interpreted as rotation-induced condensation of octupole phonons whose angular momentum is aligned with the rotational axis, rather than as a static pear shape with inversion tunneling. The discrete phonon energy and parity conservation generate oscillations of the energy difference between the lowest rotational bands with positive and negative parity, and anharmonicities tend to synchronize the rotation of the condensate and the quadrupole shape of the nucleus forming a rotating heart shape.

## Method

The paper develops a theoretical model treating the quadrupole-deformed nucleus as a rigid rotor with moment of inertia J, the octupole vibration as harmonic with frequency Ω₃, and no interaction between octupole phonons and the quadrupole deformed potential. The energy of n-boson states rotating with angular velocity ω is calculated as E_n = ħΩ₃(n + 1/2) + ω²J/2, with total angular momentum I = ni + ωJ for aligned phonons each carrying i ≈ 3ħ. The model is applied to analyze experimental data from the ENSDF database for ²²⁰Ra and Th isotopes, examining energy differences ΔE(I) between positive and negative parity sequences and angular momentum I(ω) as functions of rotational frequency.

## Key numbers

- In ²²⁰Ra, the π = − (negative parity) sequence starts with about 3ħ more angular momentum than the π = + (positive parity) sequence
- At the one-two-phonon band crossing near I = 24 in ²²⁰Ra, the angular momentum difference is −3ħ at ħω = 0.26 MeV
- For N = 134 Th isotopes, the π = − sequence starts with only ≈ 2ħ of extra angular momentum
- For N = 136 Th isotopes, the two sequences start with an angular momentum difference of only ≈ 1.5ħ
- The critical angular frequency for condensation is ω_c = Ω₃/i
- For N = 130 isotones, the critical frequency is ħω_c ≈ 0.21 MeV
- The average elongation of the two-phonon state is √⟨2|β₃²|2⟩ ∼ 0.1, comparable with the static deformation from mean field calculations around N = 134

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of alternating parity rotational bands in the A ≈ 226 mass region, previously attributed to intrinsic pear shapes generated by combining axial octupole (Y₃₀) and axial quadrupole (Y₂₀) shapes as described by Butler and Nazarewicz. The conventional interpretation involving rapid tunneling between two pear shapes related by parity inversion, as developed by Butler and Nazarewicz and by Jolos and collaborators, does not account for the observed crossing of positive and negative parity sequences in ²²⁰Ra. The phonon condensation model provides an alternative explanation and enables predictions of oscillatory behavior in ΔE and I(ω) that can be tested experimentally. The progressive synchronization of angular velocities with increasing neutron number N toward N = 136 suggests a transition from phonon condensation to a static heart shape, which should be confirmed by appropriate mean field calculations.
