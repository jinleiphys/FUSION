# Local Duality Predictions for x ~ 1 Structure Functions

> Recent data on the proton F_2 structure function in the resonance region suggest that local quark-hadron duality works remarkably well for each of the low-lying resonances, including the elastic, to rather low values of Q^2. We derive model-independent relations between structure functions at x ~ 1 and elastic electromagnetic form factors, and predict the x -> 1 behavior of nucleon polarization asymmetries and the neutron to proton structure function ratios from available data on nucleon electric and magnetic form factors.

## Key claim
Local quark-hadron duality can be used to derive model-independent relations between structure functions at x ~ 1 and elastic electromagnetic form factors, allowing predictions for the x → 1 behavior of nucleon polarization asymmetries and structure function ratios from available form factor data, with the caveat that the reliability of these predictions depends on the quality of empirical form factor data and improves with increasing Q².

## Method
The author derives finite-energy sum rule relations connecting elastic form factors to scaling structure functions by integrating over the region between the pion production threshold and the elastic point, then differentiates these relations with respect to Q² to express scaling functions at threshold in terms of Q² derivatives of elastic form factors. Using parameterizations of global nucleon electric and magnetic form factor data from Mergell, Meißner and Drechsel (1996), the author computes the x → 1 behavior of the polarization asymmetry A₁ and the neutron-to-proton structure function ratios F₂ⁿ/F₂ᵖ, F₁ⁿ/F₁ᵖ, and g₁ⁿ/g₁ᵖ.

## Key numbers
- The proton polarization asymmetry A₁ᵖ approaches unity as x → 1, in contrast to the SU(6) prediction of 5/9.
- The neutron polarization asymmetry A₁ⁿ approaches unity as x → 1, in contrast to the SU(6) prediction of 0.
- The F₂ⁿ/F₂ᵖ ratio approaches the asymptotic value (dG_M^{n 2}/dQ²)/(dG_M^{p 2}/dQ²), with the helicity retention model prediction of 3/7 being close to the empirical ratio μ_n²/μ_p² ≈ 4/9.
- The g₂ⁿ/g₂ᵖ ratio approaches μ_n²/(μ_p(μ_p-1)) ≈ 0.73 as Q² → ∞.
- Proton polarization asymmetries vary by approximately 18%, 7%, and 2% at Q² = 1, 10, and 50 GeV² respectively when using different form factor parameterizations.
- Neutron polarization asymmetries vary by approximately 100%, 14%, and 3% at the same Q² values.
- The F₂ and g₁ neutron-to-proton ratios differ from dipole parameterizations by approximately 6% and 25% respectively at Q² = 5 GeV², decreasing to approximately 4% and 10% at 20 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the original observations of Bloom and Gilman (1970-1971) on quark-hadron duality and the inclusive-exclusive connection, building on the finite-energy sum rule formalism of Dolen, Horn and Schmid (1968) and the work of de Rújula, Georgi and Politzer (1975). The predictions are relevant for interpreting Jefferson Lab data on resonance region structure functions (Niculescu et al., 2000) and for testing mechanisms of spin-flavor SU(6) breaking in the nucleon, including the d-quark suppression scenario (Feynman, 1972; Close, 1973; Carlitz, 1975; Close and Thomas, 1988) and the helicity retention model (Farrar and Jackson, 1975). Future measurements at the energy-upgraded CEBAF accelerator at Jefferson Lab will enable more complete tests of local duality between elastic form factors and x ~ 1 structure functions.
