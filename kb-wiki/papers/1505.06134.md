# The $K \bar K π$ decay of the $f_1(1285)$ and its nature as a $K^* \bar K -cc$ molecule

> We investigate the decay of $f_1(1285) \to πK \bar K$ with the assumption that the $f_1(1285)$ is dynamically generated from the $K^* \bar{K} - cc$ interaction. In addition to the tree level diagrams that proceed via $f_1(1285) \to K^* \bar{K} - cc \to πK \bar K$, we take into account also the final state interactions of $K \bar K \to K \bar K$ and $πK \to πK$. The partial decay width and mass distributions of $f_1(1285) \to πK \bar K$ are evaluated. We get a value for the partial decay width which, within errors, is in fair agreement with the experimental result. The contribution from the tree level diagrams is dominant, but the final state interactions have effects in the mass distributions. The predicted mass distributions are significantly different from phase space and tied to the $K^* \bar{K} - cc$ nature of the $f_1(1285)$ state.

## Key claim

The $f_1(1285)$ resonance is assumed to be dynamically generated from the $K^* \bar{K} - cc$ interaction (a molecular state), and its decay to $\pi K \bar K$ is evaluated under this assumption. The calculated branching ratio of $(7.2 - 8.3)\%$ is in fair agreement with the experimental value of $(9.0 \pm 0.4)\%$, but the authors note that the $f_1(1285)$ may have additional non-molecular components, with the $K \bar K^*$ molecular component accounting for only about $50\%$ of the wave function probability according to the compositeness sum rule.

## Method

The decay $f_1(1285) \to \pi K \bar K$ is modeled via tree-level diagrams where the $f_1(1285)$ decays to $K^* \bar{K} - cc$ and the off-shell $K^*$ subsequently decays to $K\pi$. The tree-level amplitude uses the $f_1 K^* \bar{K}$ coupling $g_{f_1} = 7555$ MeV (obtained from the pole residue in the scattering amplitude) and the $K^* K \pi$ vertex from the hidden gauge Lagrangian. Final state interactions (FSI) of $K \bar K \to K \bar K$ (in $I=1$) and $\pi K \to \pi K$ (in $I=1/2$) are included via triangular loop diagrams, with scattering amplitudes obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation using a chiral unitary approach with a cutoff $q_{\rm max} = 900$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio $B.R.[f_1(1285) \to \pi K \bar K] = (7.2 - 7.8)\%$ using $g_{f1} = 7555$ MeV and $g_{f1} = 7230$ MeV, respectively; the range expands to $(7.2 - 8.3)\%$ when varying cutoffs.
- Experimental branching ratio: $(9.0 \pm 0.4)\%$ (Particle Data Group, Barberis 1997, Barberis 1998).
- Partial decay width $\Gamma = 1.9$ MeV for $g_{f1} = 7555$ MeV; $\Gamma = 1.74$ MeV for $g_{f1} = 7230$ MeV.
- Coupling constant $g_{f_1} = 7555$ MeV (this work) compared to $7230$ MeV from Roca (2005) and $9687$ MeV from Jorgifran (2011, based on an incorrect method).
- Mass of $f_1(1285)$: $M_{f_1} = 1281.9$ MeV.
- $K^*$ total decay width: $\Gamma_{\rm on} = 49.1$ MeV.
- Pion decay constant: $f = 93$ MeV.
- Cutoff for $G$ function of $\bar{K} K^*$: approximately $1000$ MeV; cutoff for $K\bar{K}$ and $\pi K$: $900$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $f_1(1285)$ previously developed by Roca (2005), where the resonance appears as dynamically generated from the single channel $K^* \bar K -cc$ interaction. It follows earlier studies of the $f_1(1285)$ decays to $\pi a_0(980)$ and $\pi f_0(980)$ by Jorgifran (2011), which were confirmed by BESIII (Ablikim 2015). The present calculation contrasts with the conventional $q \bar q$ interpretation of the $f_1(1285)$ advocated by Klempt (2003), Gavillet (1984), and Sheldon (2014), and the authors note that the $K \bar K^*$ molecular component may account for only about $50\%$ of the wave function (Gengfinite 2015, using the compositeness sum rule). The predicted mass distributions in $K \bar K$ and $\pi K$ are significantly different from phase space and have not yet been measured experimentally; their observation would provide a test of the molecular nature of the $f_1(1285)$.
