# Quantum canonical ensemble and correlation femtoscopy at fixed multiplicities

> Identical particle correlations at fixed multiplicity are considered by means of quantum canonical ensemble of finite systems. We calculate one-particle momentum spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions in the ideal gas by using a recurrence relation for the partition function. Within such a model we investigate the validity of the thermal Wick's theorem and its applicability for decomposition of the two-particle distribution function. The dependence of the Bose-Einstein correlation parameters on the average momentum of the particle pair is also investigated. Specifically, we present the analytical formulas that allow one to estimate the effect of suppressing the correlation functions in a finite canonical system. The results can be used for the femtoscopy analysis of the A+A and p+p collisions with selected (fixed) multiplicity.

## Key claim

The thermal Wick's theorem, which allows the decomposition of two-particle distribution functions into products of single-particle distributions, is violated in a canonical ensemble with fixed particle number, leading to suppression of Bose-Einstein correlation functions; this suppression is a finite-volume effect that disappears only in the thermodynamic limit where the canonical and grand-canonical ensembles become equivalent.

## Method

The authors calculate one-particle momentum spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions for an ideal relativistic boson gas using a recurrence relation for the canonical partition function with fixed particle number. They derive analytical expressions for the expectation values of creation and annihilation operators in a finite-volume canonical ensemble with periodic boundary conditions, then extend the results to continuous-mode momentum representation appropriate for femtoscopy analysis. The validity of the thermal Wick's theorem is tested by comparing the canonical-ensemble results with the standard grand-canonical decomposition.

## Key numbers

* For a two-pion system (N=2) with R=3 fm, T=0.06 GeV, and average momentum p=0.2 GeV/c, the correlation function intercept is suppressed and the function loses its Gaussian form, becoming less than unity at intermediate q values.
* In the low particle number density approximation, the correlation strength parameter λ(p) ≈ 1 − 4 e^(−βE(p)+βm) (2π)³ λ_T³ / V, where λ_T = (2π m T)^(−1/2) is the thermal wavelength and V = (2π)^(3/2) R³.
* The ratio of canonical partition functions in the low-density approximation is Z_(N−1)/Z_N ≈ (2π)³ (N/V) I^(−1)(β), where I(β) = ∫ d³p exp(−β E(p)) = 4π β^(−1) m² K₂(β m).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bose-Einstein correlations in finite systems beyond the Kopylov-Podgoretsky model of independent incoherent sources (Kopylov and Podgoretsky, 1970s) and the results of Sinyukov (1990s) and Lednicky (2000s) on symmetrization in ensembles of thermal particles. The paper provides analytical formulas for the suppression of correlation functions in fixed-multiplicity events, which can be used for femtoscopy analysis of A+A and p+p collisions at the LHC where events are selected by charged-particle multiplicity bins. The results enable quantitative estimates of how finite-system effects distort the standard Gaussian parametrization C(p,q) = 1 + λ(p) exp(−R_G² q²) commonly used in experimental analyses.
