# On the magic of transforming antikaonic nuclear broad quasibound states into narrow intrinsic decaying states

> Comments are made on Akaishi, Myint and Yamazaki's interpretation of antikaonic quasibound nuclear states as generalized Kapur-Peierls decaying states [arXiv:0805.4382]. We argue that these `intrinsic decaying states' have little to do with low-energy antikaon-nucleon dynamics.

# On the magic of transforming antikaonic nuclear broad quasibound states into narrow intrinsic decaying states

## Key claim

The authors argue that the "Intrinsic Decaying States" (IDS) proposed by Akaishi, Myint and Yamazaki (AMY) to replace broad $S$-matrix pole states for antikaon-nuclear quasibound states are not eigenstates of the full coupled-channel Hamiltonian and therefore have little to do with low-energy $\bar K N$ dynamics; the IDS concept cannot be extended satisfactorily across all relevant channels.

## Method

The authors analyze the formal definitions of Gamow states (poles of the $S$-matrix) versus Kapur-Peierls states, showing that IDS satisfy an eigenstate equation only in the $\bar K N$ ($P$) channel but not in the $\pi\Sigma$ ($Q$) channel. They then demonstrate using a realistic 10-channel chirally motivated coupled-channel model (parameter set giving $\sigma_{\pi N}=40$ MeV from Cieply and Smejkal (2007)) that two Gamow poles naturally arise, and that the lower pole becomes narrow and approaches a bound state below the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold when interaction strength is increased, contrary to the behavior AMY claimed required IDS.

## Key numbers

- The chirally motivated model yields two Gamow poles on the [$+,-$] Riemann sheet at $(E_R, -i\Gamma_R/2) = (1391, -i51)$ MeV and $(1450, -i69)$ MeV, corresponding respectively to the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance and a higher pole above the $\bar K N$ threshold.
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance corresponds to the lower pole at $(1391, -i51)$ MeV.
- The $\bar K N$ and $\pi\Sigma$ thresholds are separated by about 100 MeV.

## Context

This work directly critiques the IDS concept introduced by Akaishi, Myint and Yamazaki (2008) as an alternative to Gamow states for describing antikaonic nuclear quasibound states. The authors argue that chirally motivated coupled-channel models (reviewed by Hyodo and Weise, 2008) naturally produce two dynamical poles with behavior that does not require IDS, and that the AMY motivation was apparently to discredit the large widths (order 100 MeV) found for $K^-pp$ quasibound states in three-body Faddeev calculations by Shevchenko et al. (2008), compared to the smaller width (about 60 MeV) found in the single-channel calculation by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002). The work enables proper use of Gamow states for describing $\bar K$ nuclear quasibound states within coupled-channel dynamics.
