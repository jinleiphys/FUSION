# The generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for deuteron electrodisintegration

> The generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule $I^{GDH}_{γ^*d}(Q^2)$ for deuteron electrodisintegration $d(e,e')np$ as function of the squared four-momentum transfer $Q^2$ is evaluated by explicit integration. The calculation is based on a conventional nonrelativistic framework using a realistic $NN$-potential and including contributions from meson exchange currents, isobar configurations and leading order relativistic terms. Good convergence is achieved. The prominent feature is a deep negative minimum, $I_{γ^* d}^{GDH}=-9.5$ mb, at low $Q^2\approx 0.2$ fm$^{-2}$ which is almost exclusively driven by the nucleon isovector anomalous magnetic moment contribution to the magnetic dipole transition to the $^1S_0$-state. Above $Q^2=20$ fm$^{-2}$ the integral $I^{GDH}_{γ^*}(Q^2)$ approaches zero rapidly.

## Key claim

The generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral for deuteron electrodisintegration exhibits a deep negative minimum of -9.5 mb at low four-momentum transfer \(Q^2 \approx 0.2\) fm\(^{-2}\), which is almost exclusively driven by the nucleon isovector anomalous magnetic moment contribution to the magnetic dipole transition to the \(^1S_0\)-state, with the calculation based on a conventional nonrelativistic framework using a realistic \(NN\)-potential.

## Method

The generalized GDH integral \(I^{GDH}_{\gamma^* d}(Q^2)\) was evaluated by explicit integration of the transverse spin asymmetry \(\sigma_{T,\gamma^*}^P - \sigma_{T,\gamma^*}^A\) over the final-state excitation energy \(E_{np}\) up to 1 GeV, using a nonrelativistic framework with the Bonn r-space, Bonn q-space, and Argonne \(V_{18}\) potentials. The calculation included one-body currents with Siegert operators, explicit meson exchange currents from \(\pi\)- and \(\rho\)-exchange, isobar configurations (including a coupled-channel approach for the dominant \(N\Delta\)-configuration), and leading-order relativistic contributions, with final-state interactions included for all multipoles up to \(L=6\).

## Key numbers

- The generalized GDH integral reaches a minimum value of \(I_{\gamma^* d}^{GDH} = -9.5\) mb at \(Q^2 \approx 0.2\) fm\(^{-2}\).
- Above \(Q^2 = 20\) fm\(^{-2}\), the integral \(I^{GDH}_{\gamma^*}(Q^2)\) approaches zero rapidly.
- The resonance-like structure in the spin asymmetry appears at \(E_{np} \approx 70\) keV, corresponding to the anti-bound \(^1S_0\)-state.
- For real photons (\(Q^2 = 0\)), the sum rule prediction for the deuteron is \(I^{GDH}_{\gamma,d} = 0.65\) \(\mu\)b from the deuteron's anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_d = -0.143\).
- The photodisintegration contribution alone yields \(-413\) \(\mu\)b.
- The quasi-free peak in the spin asymmetry is located at \(E_{np}/\text{MeV} \approx 10\,Q^2/\text{fm}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends previous evaluations of the GDH sum rule for the deuteron by Arenhoevel and collaborators (1997) and Deltuva and Arenhoevel (2003), which considered photodisintegration and single pion production channels but found that the incoherent pion production contributions had not converged. The present calculation provides the first evaluation of the electrodisintegration channel contribution to the generalized GDH integral, and the results enable experimental tests of low-energy few-body nuclear dynamics. The author notes that an independent evaluation in the framework of effective field theory would be valuable, and future theoretical work should address the spin asymmetry and GDH integral for coherent and incoherent single pion as well as two-pion electroproduction channels.
