# The Lambda(1405) state in a chiral unitary approach with off-shell corrections to dimensional regularized loop functions

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in the framework of unitary coupled-channel approximation by using the pseudoscalar meson-baryon octet interaction. The loop function of the intermediate meson and baryon is deduced in a dimensional regularization scheme, where the relativistic kinetic effect and off-shell corrections are taken into account. According to the experimental data at the $K^- p$ threshold, the subtraction constants in the loop function are determined. The squared amplitude is suppressed strongly and only one $Λ(1405)$ state is generated dynamically in the strangeness $S=-1$ and isospin $I=0$ sector.

## Key claim
When off-shell corrections and relativistic kinetic effects are included in the dimensional regularized loop function of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, the squared amplitude is suppressed strongly and only one Lambda(1405) state is generated dynamically in the strangeness S=-1, isospin I=0 sector, in contrast to the two-pole picture found in earlier unitary coupled-channel calculations such as Oller (2001) and Jido (2003).

## Method
The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in the unitary coupled-channel approximation using the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian for pseudoscalar meson and baryon octet interactions. The loop function of the intermediate meson and baryon is derived in a dimensional regularization scheme without the on-shell approximation, yielding a revised form that includes off-shell corrections. The subtraction constants in the loop function are determined by fitting to experimental data at the K^- p threshold from the SIDDHARTA experiment, including the energy shift and width of kaonic hydrogen and branching ratios.

## Key numbers
- The Lambda(1405) pole is found at 1383+81i MeV on the complex sqrt(s) plane in the isospin I=0 sector, above the pi Sigma threshold and in the second Riemann sheet.
- A second I=0 pole is found at 1653+12i MeV, interpreted as a candidate for the Lambda(1670)1/2- resonance, coupling strongly to the K Xi channel.
- In the isospin I=1 sector, one resonance is found at 1570+244i MeV, tentatively associated with the Sigma(1620)1/2- state.
- The subtraction constants for the off-shell loop function (with mu=630 MeV) are: a_{bar K N} = -1.1, a_{pi Lambda} = -1.6, a_{pi Sigma} = -1.9, a_{eta Lambda} = -2.7, a_{eta Sigma} = -2.6, a_{K Xi} = -2.8.
- The fitted model reproduces the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data: Delta E = 283.13 eV, Gamma = 541.06 eV, branching ratios Rn = 0.3 and Rc = 0.61.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller (2001) and Jido (2003), which found two poles for the Lambda(1405) using on-shell factorization of the loop function. The authors argue that the on-shell approximation eliminates important off-shell elements, and their revised loop function suppresses the second pole near the bar K N threshold. The results are constrained by the precise SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data (2011), and the single-pole finding contrasts with the two-pole picture supported by the CLAS photoproduction experiment of Moriya (2013). This enables further study of whether the single-pole or two-pole interpretation of the Lambda(1405) is correct.
