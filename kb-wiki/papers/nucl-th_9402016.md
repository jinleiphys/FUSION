# Theoretical Interpretation of the NE18 Experiment on Nuclear Transparency in A(e,e'p) Scattering

> The spectral function, measured in $A(e,e'p)$ reactions, is distorted by the final-state interaction of the struck proton with the residual nucleus. This causes a broadening of the observed transverse-momentum distribution which is large even in the $d(e,e'p)$ reaction. We discuss the effects of this $p_{\perp}$-broadening on the nuclear transparency measured in the recent NE18 experiment. Within conventional Glauber theory we can describe the measurements. Transparency effects are thus small in agreement with our earlier predictions.

## Key claim

Within conventional Glauber theory, the final-state interaction (FSI) of the struck proton with the residual nucleus can describe the NE18 measurements of nuclear transparency, and the color transparency (CT) signal is still negligibly small at momentum transfers up to Q² ≤ 7 (GeV/c)², consistent with the authors' earlier predictions.

## Method

The authors calculated the distorted spectral function for A(e,e'p) reactions using the Glauber multiple-scattering approximation, accounting for both elastic and inelastic rescattering of the struck proton. For the deuteron, they used a realistic Bonn wave function and computed the transparency factor as a function of the transverse-momentum acceptance. For heavier nuclei, they employed a local-density approximation for the one-body density matrix and derived expressions for the p⊥-broadening and the longitudinal momentum shift Δp_{m,z} caused by the real part of the pN scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron, the estimated attenuation effect is 1 - T_d ∼ σ_{tot}(pn) / (2π R_d²) ∼ 0.07, about twice as large as the Glauber shadowing effect in σ_{tot}(pd).
- The longitudinal momentum shift due to the real part of the pN amplitude is Δp_{m,z} ≈ σ_{tot}(pN) α_{pN} n_A ∼ α_{pN} · 70 MeV/c; with α_{pN} ∼ -0.5 at NE18 kinematics, this gives Δp_{m,z} ∼ -35 MeV/c.
- The NE18 experiment used a transverse-momentum cutoff of p⊥^{max} = 170 MeV/c for the deuteron and p⊥ ≤ 250 MeV/c for heavier targets.
- For ¹²C(e,e'p) scattering, the threshold for color transparency is estimated at Q² ∼ 5 (GeV/c)², and CT effects increase the transparency by ∼ 5% at the largest Q² of the NE18 experiment.

## Context

This work provides a quantitative Glauber-theory description of the NE18 experiment at SLAC (1994), which had concluded there was no conclusive evidence for color transparency up to Q² ≤ 7 (GeV/c)². The authors extend their earlier sum-rule approach (Nikolaev et al., 1993) by accounting for the finite spectrometer acceptance in missing energy and missing momentum, which prevents factorization of the spectral function into a PWIA term times an energy-independent attenuation factor. The analysis enables future searches for color transparency signals in dedicated ⁴He(e,e'p) experiments at CEBAF, and corrects a point missed in earlier discussions of FSI effects (prior work from 1993).
