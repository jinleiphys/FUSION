# Reaction processes of muon-catalyzed fusion in the muonic molecule $ddμ$ studied with the tractable $T$-matrix model

> Muon-catalyzed fusion has recently regained significant attention due to experimental and theoretical developments being performed. The present authors [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 109} 054625 (2024)] proposed the tractable $T$-matrix model based on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to approximate the elaborate two- and three-body coupled-channel (CC) calculations [Kamimura, Kino, and Yamashita, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 107}, 034607 (2023)] for the nuclear reaction processes in the muonic molecule $dtμ$, $(dtμ)_{J=0} \to\!^4{\rm He} + n + μ+ 17.6 \, {\rm MeV}$. % or $(^4{\rm He}μ)_{nl} + n + 17.6 \,{\rm MeV}$. The $T$-matrix model well reproduced almost all of the results generated by the CC work. In the present paper, we apply this model to the nuclear reaction processes in the $ddμ$ molecule, $(ddμ)_{J=1} \to\!^3{\rm He} + n + μ+3.27 \,$ MeV or $t + p + μ+ 4.03 \,$ MeV, in which the fusion takes place via the $p$-wave $d$-$d$ relative motion. Recently, significantly different $p$-wave astrophysical $S(E)$ factors of the reaction $d + d \to\!^3{\rm He} + n$ or $t + p$ at $E \! \simeq \! 1$ keV to 1 MeV have been reported experimentally and theoretically by five groups. Employing many sets of nuclear interactions that can reproduce those five cases of $p$-wave $S(E)$ factors, we calculate the fusion rate of the $(ddμ)_{J=1}$ molecule using three kinds of methods where results are consistent with each other. We also derive the $^3{\rm He}$-$μ$ sticking probability and the absolute values of the energy and momentum spectra of the emitted muon. The violation of charge symmetry in the $p$-wave $d$-$d$ reaction and the $ddμ$ fusion reaction is discussed. Information on the emitted 2.45-MeV neutrons and \mbox{1 keV-dominant} muons should be useful for the application of $ddμ$ fusion.

## Key claim

The tractable $T$-matrix model, previously validated for $dt\mu$ fusion, is applied to $dd\mu$ fusion and yields consistent fusion rates across three different methods, but the calculated rates depend strongly on which of five significantly different published $p$-wave astrophysical $S(E)$ factors for the $d+d$ reactions is used as input.

## Method

The authors employ three methods to calculate the fusion rate of the $(dd\mu)_{J=1}$ molecule: (i) an optical-potential model using a complex $d$-$d$ potential fitted to reproduce the summed $p$-wave $S(E)$ factors of the $d+d \to ^3\mathrm{He}+n$ and $d+d \to t+p$ reactions; (ii) a $T$-matrix model with nonlocal coupling potentials between the $d$-$d$ and outgoing channels, fitted to the same $S(E)$ factors; and (iii) a variant of the $T$-matrix model using different Jacobi coordinates for the outgoing waves. The three-body bound state of $dd\mu$ is solved using the Gaussian Expansion Method with 2,600 basis functions across three rearrangement channels.

## Key numbers

- Fusion rate $\lambda^{\rm (opt)}_{11}$ for the $(dd\mu)_{J=1,v=1}$ state ranges from $1.75(5) \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (using Angulo+ 1998 $S(E)$ factor) to $5.05(5) \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (using Arai+ 2011 $S(E)$ factor), averaged over five optical-potential sets.
- Effective fusion rate $\widetilde{\lambda}^{\rm (opt)}_{\rm f}$ ranges from $2.0 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (Angulo+ 1998) to $5.3 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (Arai+ 2011).
- The $J=1,v=0$ state fusion rate $\lambda^{\rm (opt)}_{10}$ ranges from $5.5(2) \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (Angulo+ 1998) to $15.7(1) \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (Arai+ 2011).
- The latest experimental effective fusion rate from Balin et al. (2011) is $\widetilde{\lambda}_{\rm f} = (3.81 \pm 0.15) \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$.
- The calculated binding energy of the $(dd\mu)_{J=1,v=1}$ state relative to the $(d\mu)_{1s}+d$ threshold is $\varepsilon_{11} = -1.974$ eV (with $l_{\rm max}=4$) or $-1.961$ eV (with $l_{\rm max}=2$).
- The nuclear interaction contributes $-1.44 \times 10^{-6}$ eV to the real part and $-1.39 \times 10^{-7}$ eV to the imaginary part of the eigenenergy.
- The deexcitation rate from $J=v=1$ to $v=0$ is $\Gamma_{\rm dex} = 0.2 \times 10^8\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ from Bakalov (1988).

## Context

This work extends the tractable $T$-matrix model previously developed by the same authors for $dt\mu$ fusion (Wu et al., Phys. Rev. C 109, 054625, 2024) to the $dd\mu$ system, which was studied earlier using a three-body coupled-channel method by Kamimura, Kino, and Yamashita (Phys. Rev. C 107, 034607, 2023). The paper addresses the significant discrepancies among five published $p$-wave $S(E)$ factors for $d+d$ reactions reported by Angulo and Descouvemont (1998), Nebia et al. (2002), Arai et al. (2011), Tumino et al. (2014), and Solovyev (2024). The results enable evaluation of the $^3\mathrm{He}$-$\mu$ sticking probability and muon energy/momentum spectra, which are relevant for proposed applications of $dd\mu$ fusion such as the thorium subcritical reactor concept by Iiyoshi et al. (2023).
