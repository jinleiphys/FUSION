# The nuclear symmetry energy from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model

> The microscopic mechanisms of the symmetry energy in nuclear matter are investigated in the framework of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) model with a high-precision realistic nuclear potential, pvCDBonn A. The kinetic energy and potential contributions to symmetry energy are decomposed. They are explicitly expressed by the nucleon self-energies, which are obtained through projecting the $G$-matrices from the RBHF model into the terms of Lorentz covariants. The nuclear medium effects on the nucleon self-energy and nucleon-nucleon interaction in symmetry energy are discussed by comparing the results from the RBHF model and those from Hartree-Fock and relativistic Hartree-Fock models. It is found that the nucleon self-energy including the nuclear medium effect on the single-nucleon wave function provides a largely positive contribution to the symmetry energy, while {the nuclear medium effect on the nucleon-nucleon interaction, i.e., the effective $G$-matrices generates the negative contribution}. The tensor force plays an essential role in the symmetry energy around the density. The scalar and vector covariant amplitudes of nucleon-nucleon interaction dominate the potential component of the symmetry energy. Furthermore, the isoscalar and isovector terms in the optical potential are extracted from the RBHF model. The isoscalar part is consistent with the results from the analysis of global optical potential, while the isovector one has obvious differences at higher incident energy due to the relativistic effect.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy from the RBHF model satisfies all recent experimental constraints across the whole density region, but this agreement depends on the inclusion of nuclear medium effects that reduce the kinetic energy part and on the tensor force playing an essential role around saturation density; the relativistic effect alone (RHF model) provides too large a symmetry energy without high-momentum correlations.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone equation with the high-precision pvCDBonn A potential, using a subtracted representation scheme that projects the G-matrix into Lorentz covariant amplitudes (scalar, vector, pseudovector). They decomposed the symmetry energy into kinetic and potential components expressed via nucleon self-energies, and compared results from Hartree-Fock (HF), relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF), and RBHF models to isolate nuclear medium effects on the wave function and on the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density from RBHF with pvCDBonn A: \(n_\mathrm{sat}=0.19\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), binding energy per nucleon \(E_\mathrm{B}/A(n_\mathrm{sat})=-16.89\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Symmetry energy at saturation density from RBHF: \(E_\mathrm{sym}(n_\mathrm{sat})=34.48\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Empirical constraint on symmetry energy at saturation density cited from literature: \(E_\mathrm{sym}(n_0)=31.6\pm2.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Empirical density slope of symmetry energy cited from literature: \(L=58.9\pm16\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- D-state probability of deuteron for pvCDBonn A potential: \(P_D=4.2\%\).
- Saturation properties from scheme (3) with Bonn A: \(\rho_0=0.179\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(E/A=-16.18\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(K=250\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic decomposition of symmetry energy previously studied in non-relativistic BHF models (Li 2006, Zuo 2014) and in relativistic Hartree-Fock models (Zhao 2015, Sun 2016, Liu 2018, Miyatsu 2020) by using the RBHF framework with a high-precision charge-dependent potential. It enables future relativistic optical potential analyses for nucleon-nucleus scattering, as the isovector optical potential from RBHF shows differences from nonrelativistic models (Xu 2010, Li 2015) at higher incident energies.
