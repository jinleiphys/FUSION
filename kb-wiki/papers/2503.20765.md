# Feasibility of measuring the speed of sound of the quark-gluon plasma from the multiplicity and mean $p_T$ of ultracentral heavy-ion collisions

> The mean transverse momentum $\langle p_T \rangle$ of hadrons has been observed experimentally and in numerical simulations to have a power-law dependence on the hadronic multiplicity $N$ in ultracentral relativistic heavy-ion collisions: $\langle p_{T} \rangle \propto N^{b_{\rm UC}}$. It has been put forward that this exponent $b_{\rm UC}$ is the speed of sound of quark-gluon plasma measured at a temperature determined from $\langle p_T \rangle$. We study step by step the connection between (i) the energy and entropy of hydrodynamic simulations and (ii) experimentally measurable observables. We show that an argument based on energy and entropy should yield an exponent equal to the pressure over energy density $P/\varepsilon$, rather than the speed of sound $c_s^2$; however, we also observe that $\langle p_T \rangle$ and $N$ are not sufficiently accurate proxies for the energy and entropy to make this possible in practice. From simulations, we find that the exponent $b_{\rm UC}$ is significantly different whether the ''effective volume'' is strictly constant or not, a condition that cannot be enforced experimentally. Additional tests using a modified equation of state find that the exponent $b_{\rm UC}$ exhibits a variable degree of correlations with the speed of sound and with $P/\varepsilon$, but is not an accurate measurement of either quantity in general.

## Key claim

The exponent \(b_{\rm UC}\) extracted from the power-law relation \(\langle p_{T} \rangle \propto N^{b_{\rm UC}}\) in ultracentral heavy-ion collisions is not a reliable measurement of the speed of sound squared \(c_s^2\) of the quark-gluon plasma; instead, a thermodynamic argument based on energy and entropy would yield the pressure over energy density \(P/\varepsilon\), and in practice the observable is not an accurate proxy for either quantity due to the failure of key assumptions about the effective volume and the proportionality between measured observables and thermodynamic variables.

## Method

The authors performed boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with a Gaussian initial temperature profile in the transverse plane, varying the initial peak temperature \(T_0\) and width \(\sigma\). They used the HotQCD lattice equation of state and a modified equation of state with an artificial bump in \(c_s^2\) above 150 MeV. For each combination of initial parameters and hypersurface temperature \(T_f\), they computed the total energy \(E\) and entropy \(S\) on constant-temperature hypersurfaces, then applied the Cooper-Frye procedure with the iSS particle sampler to obtain charged hadron spectra at midrapidity, from which they calculated \(\langle p_T \rangle\) and multiplicity \(N\). They evaluated the derivative \(b_{\rm UC} = d\ln\langle p_T \rangle / d\ln N\) under two different variation schemes: one enforcing constant effective volume \(V_{\rm eff}\) and one varying only \(T_0\) with fixed \(\sigma\).

## Key numbers

- The exponent \(b_{\rm UC}\) computed from simulations with the QCD equation of state, under the assumption of constant effective volume, agrees with \(P/\varepsilon\) exactly (by construction) and not with \(c_s^2\); for example, at effective temperatures between 200 and 250 MeV, the points fall on the \(P/\varepsilon\) curve.
- When the effective volume is not held constant (varying only \(T_0\) with fixed \(\sigma\)), the observable \(b_{\rm UC}\) for the QCD equation of state falls close to \(c_s^2\) for temperatures between 180 MeV and 230 MeV, but this agreement is described as coincidental.
- For the modified equation of state, with the same variation scheme (varying only \(T_0\)), \(b_{\rm UC}\) shows no correlation with \(c_s^2\) in the 180-230 MeV temperature range.
- The ratio \(\langle p_T \rangle / T_{\rm eff}\) ranges from approximately 2.7 to 3.9 depending on the hypersurface temperature; for the QCD equation of state, at high multiplicities the ratio is about 3.2 for \(T_f = 150\) MeV, 3.5 for \(T_f = 130\) MeV, and 3.8 for \(T_f = 110\) MeV.

## Context

This work directly tests the claim made by Gardim et al. (2019, 2024) that the exponent \(b_{\rm UC}\) measured in ultracentral collisions equals the speed of sound of the quark-gluon plasma, a claim that has been compared to lattice QCD calculations by the HotQCD Collaboration (2014) and measured by the CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE collaborations. The authors show that the original argument relies on three assumptions (proportionality of \(\langle p_T \rangle\) to \(T_{\rm eff}\), proportionality of \(N\) to entropy \(S\), and constant effective volume) that are not simultaneously valid, and that the apparent agreement between \(b_{\rm UC}\) and \(c_s^2\) in a narrow temperature window arises from an accidental cancellation of effects. This work extends the discussion by Nijs et al. (2023) on centrality-determination dependence and by Soares Rocha et al. (2024) on the late-time limit of hydrodynamic evolution, and it implies that extracting the QCD equation of state from \(b_{\rm UC}\) requires additional experimental constraints beyond the exponent itself.
