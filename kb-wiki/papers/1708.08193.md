# Alpha Clustering with a Hollow Structure --- Geometrical Structure of Alpha Clusters from Platonic Solids to Fullerene Shape

> We study $α$-cluster structure based on the geometric configurations with a microscopic framework, which takes full account of the Pauli principle, and which also employs an effective inter-nucleon force including finite-range three-body terms suitable for microscopic alpha-cluster models. Here, special attention is focused upon the $α$ clustering with a hollow structure; all the $α$ clusters are put on the surface of a sphere. All the Platonic solids (five regular polyhedra) and the fullerene-shaped polyhedron coming from icosahedral structure are considered. Furthermore, two configurations with dual polyhedra, hexahedron-octahedron and dodecahedron-icosahedron, are also scrutinized. As a consequence, we insist on the possible existence of stable $α$-clustering with a hollow structure for all the configurations. Especially, two configurations, that is, dual polyhedra of dodecahedron-icosahedron and fullerene, have a prominent hollow structure compared with other six configurations.

# Alpha Clustering with a Hollow Structure

## Key claim
All eight geometric configurations of alpha clusters placed on the surface of a sphere (five Platonic solids, two dual polyhedra, and a fullerene shape) exhibit stable energy pockets in microscopic calculations with the F1 effective force, but the dodecahedron-icosahedron and fullerene configurations show the most prominent hollow structure; the authors qualify that the adiabatic energy curves include spurious center-of-mass energy of each alpha particle that has not been removed.

## Method
The authors employed a Brink-Bloch type microscopic wave function with full antisymmetrization and the Tohsaki F1 effective inter-nucleon force, which includes finite-range three-body terms. Alpha clusters were placed at the vertices of eight polyhedral configurations on a common sphere of radius rho, and the adiabatic energy per alpha was calculated as a function of this single variational parameter. The Pauli index p_i(rho) was computed to quantify the antisymmetrization effect at each radius.

## Key numbers
- Tetrahedron (4 alphas, 16O): energy pocket at rho = 1.4 fm with depth -3.039 MeV per alpha, shortest alpha-alpha distance 2.29 fm
- Hexahedron (8 alphas, 32S): energy pocket at rho = 2.1 fm with depth -1.643 MeV per alpha, shortest distance 2.97 fm
- Octahedron (6 alphas, 24Mg): energy pocket at rho = 2.5 fm with depth 1.228 MeV per alpha (unbound), shortest distance 2.89 fm
- Dodecahedron (20 alphas, 80Zr): energy pocket at rho = 2.9 fm with depth -0.367 MeV per alpha, shortest distance 3.05 fm
- Icosahedron (12 alphas, 48Cr): energy pocket at rho = 3.9 fm with depth 6.700 MeV per alpha (unbound), shortest distance 2.78 fm
- Hexahedron-octahedron (14 alphas, 56Ni): energy pocket at rho = 3.2 fm with depth 8.838 MeV per alpha (unbound), shortest distance 2.94 fm
- Dodecahedron-icosahedron (32 alphas, 128Gd): energy pocket at rho = 4.9 fm with depth 11.694 MeV per alpha (unbound), shortest distance 3.14 fm
- Fullerene (60 alphas, 240 nucleus with Z=120): energy pocket at rho = 7.7 fm with depth 18.868 MeV per alpha (unbound), shortest distance 3.11 fm
- Single nucleon size parameter b = 1.415 fm gives alpha binding energy 27.500 MeV (experimental 28.294 MeV)

## Context
This work extends microscopic alpha-cluster studies beyond the (0p)-shell 4N nuclei that had been the focus of prior work using the Brink-Bloch model (Brink, 1966) and the Fujiwara review (1980). The authors use the Tohsaki F1 force (Tohsaki, 1994), which reproduces saturation properties across a wide mass range unlike the Volkov force (Volkov, 1965) or the Brink-Boeker force (Brink and Boeker, 1967). The results suggest that balloon-shaped nuclei with a central void could exist even in the ultra-super heavy region, which would require revision of the empirical nuclear radius formula, and the authors propose that the THSR wave function (Tohsaki et al., 2001) used for alpha condensation is a candidate for more quantitative study of these configurations.
