# Subtracted Dispersion Relations for In-Medium Meson Correlators in QCD Sum Rules

> We analyze subtracted dispersion relations for meson correlators at finite baryon density and temperature. Such relations are needed for QCD sum rules. We point out the importance of scattering terms, as well as finite, well-defined subtraction constants. Both are necessary for consistency, in particular for the equality of the longitudinal and transverse correlators in the limit of the vanishing three-momentum of mesons relative to the medium. We present detailed calculations in various mesonic channels for the case of the Fermi gas of nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that previous QCD sum rule calculations for in-medium meson correlators (such as those by Jin, Hatsuda-Lee, and others in the late 1990s) have been incomplete because they included scattering terms but overlooked a finite, well-defined subtraction constant in the dispersion relation; both the scattering term and the subtraction constant are necessary for consistency, particularly to ensure the equality of longitudinal and transverse correlators in the limit of vanishing three-momentum relative to the medium.

## Method

The authors evaluate in-medium meson correlators at the 1-particle-1-hole (1p-1h) level using the imaginary-time formalism for a Fermi gas of nucleons. They compute the retarded correlator for various mesonic channels (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, tensor, and vector-tensor mixing) by performing the angular integration over the nucleon momentum and then analytically continuing to real energies. The subtraction constant \(\Pi(|{\bf q}|,{\bf q})\) and the scattering term strength \(R\) are extracted in the long-wavelength limit (\({\bf q}\rightarrow 0\)) for each channel.

## Key numbers

- For the longitudinal vector channel at zero temperature, the scattering term strength is \(R_{V_L} = -k_F^3/(3\pi E_F)\), which at low density approximates to \(-k_F^3/(3\pi M)\).
- For the transverse vector channel at zero temperature, the scattering term strength is \(R_{V_T} = \frac{1}{6\pi}\left[ k_F^3/E_F + 3M^2 \Phi(v_F) \right]\), where \(\Phi(v_F) = v_F + \frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{1-v_F}{1+v_F}\) and \(v_F = k_F/E_F\); at low density this becomes \(k_F^5/(15\pi M^3)\).
- The subtraction constant coefficient for the transverse vector channel is \(W^{(V_T)} = 1/2\), while for the longitudinal vector channel it is \(W^{(V_L)} = 0\).
- For the scalar channel at zero temperature, the scattering term strength is \(R^{(S)} = (M^2/\pi)\Phi(v_F)\), which at low density becomes \(-k_F^3/(3\pi M)\), and the subtraction constant coefficient is \(W^{(S)} = -1/2\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of in-medium QCD sum rules for vector mesons previously performed by Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov (1991) for a pion gas, and by Jin, Hatsuda, Lee, and others for nuclear matter, by clarifying the role of subtraction constants that were neglected in those earlier treatments. The results enable future applications of QCD sum rules to other mesonic channels (scalar, pseudoscalar, axial-vector, tensor) at finite baryon density, providing the necessary scattering terms and subtraction constants for constructing consistent sum rules. The authors note that their Fermi gas calculation is a model calculation and that other effects (meson production, rescattering, meson exchange) will modify both the scattering terms and subtraction constants.
