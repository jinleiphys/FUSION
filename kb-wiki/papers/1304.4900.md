# Fermion Bag Approach to Fermion Sign Problems

> The fermion bag approach is a new method to tackle fermion sign problems in lattice field theories. Using this approach it is possible to solve a class of sign problems that seem unsolvable by traditional methods. The new solutions emerge when partition functions are written in terms of fermion bags and bosonic worldlines. In these new variables it is possible to identify hidden pairing mechanisms which lead to the solutions. The new solutions allow us for the first time to use Monte Carlo methods to solve a variety of interesting lattice field theories, thus creating new opportunities for understanding strongly correlated fermion systems.

## Key claim

The fermion bag approach can solve a class of fermion sign problems that appear unsolvable by traditional methods, specifically when the fermion matrix contains a complex diagonal term that makes the determinant complex, but the bosonic action can be expressed in worldline variables with positive Boltzmann weights.

## Method

The method groups Grassmann variables into separate "fermion bags" rather than integrating over all fermion degrees of freedom uniformly as in the traditional determinant approach. For each configuration of monomer fields (representing interaction sites), the Grassmann integral factorizes into contributions from interaction-site bags (computed trivially) and a free fermion bag (computed as a determinant of a reduced matrix). The bosonic fields are simultaneously rewritten in worldline variables using an expansion in modified Bessel functions, yielding positive Boltzmann weights that combine with the non-negative fermion determinant.

## Key numbers

- The fermion bag partition function for the plaquette model takes the form \( Z = \sum_{[n_P]} U^{N_P} \mathrm{Det}(W(n_P)) \), where \( N_P \) is the total number of plaquettes with \( n_P = 1 \) and \( W(n_P) \) is obtained from the matrix \( M \) by dropping rows and columns associated with sites belonging to non-zero plaquettes.
- For the general solvable class, the partition function becomes \( Z = \int [d\sigma d\rho] \sum_{[n,b]} g^k \ \Omega(b,\sigma,\rho,n) \ \mathrm{Det}(W(\sigma,n)) \), where \( k \) is the number of monomer sites and \( \Omega \) is a non-negative Boltzmann weight.
- The bosonic action \( S_b(\sigma,\phi) = s(\sigma,\rho) - \sum_{x,y} \beta_{xy} \cos(\varepsilon_x\theta_x + \varepsilon_y\theta_y) \) is solvable when \( \beta_{xy} \geq 0 \), with the \( k \)-point correlation function expressed using modified Bessel functions \( I_{b_{xy}}(\beta_{xy}) \).

## Context

This work extends the meron-cluster approach of Chandrasekharan (1999) to a wider class of lattice field theories, and builds on worldline representations for bosonic field theories developed by Chandrasekharan (2008), Wolff (2009), and Gattringer (2012). The fermion bag approach enables Monte Carlo studies of strongly correlated fermion systems that were previously intractable, including three-dimensional staggered fermion models with Yukawa couplings and Hamiltonian models with dynamical pairing interactions, which could not be studied using traditional determinant-based methods due to sign problems.
