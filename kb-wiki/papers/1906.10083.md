# Relativistic invariance in Euclidean formulations of quantum mechanics

> Relativistic invariance in Euclidean formulations of quantum mechanics is discussed. Relativistic treatments of quantum theory are needed to study hadronic systems at sub-hadronic distance scales. Euclidean formulations of relativistic quantum mechanics have some computational advantages. In the Euclidean representation the physical Hilbert space inner product is expressed in terms of Euclidean space-time variables with no need for any analytic continuation. The identification of the complex Euclidean group with the complex Poincaré group relates the infinitesimal generators of both groups. In this work explicit representations of the Poincaré generators in Euclidean space-time variables for all positive-mass positive-energy irreducible representations of the Poincaré group are derived. The commutation relations are checked, both hermiticity and self-adjointness are established, and reflection positivity of the kernels is verified.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that explicit representations of the Poincaré generators in Euclidean space-time variables can be constructed for all positive-mass positive-energy irreducible representations of the Poincaré group, with the caveat that the construction relies on the identification of the complex Euclidean group with the complex Poincaré group, and the resulting generators are self-adjoint only after applying the Osterwalder-Schrader reflection positivity construction, which restricts test functions to those with support for positive absolute and relative Euclidean times.

## Method

The authors start with Minkowski-space irreducible representations of the Poincaré group in a basis of simultaneous eigenstates of mass, spin, linear momentum, and z-component of canonical spin, then transform these to Lorentz-covariant representations using SL(2,C) matrices. They relate these to Euclidean covariant kernels by analytically continuing the time variable and constructing Euclidean Green's functions of the form S^s_e(x_e,μ;y_e,ν) = ∫ (2/(2π)^4) [e^{i p_e·(x_e-y_e)}/(p_e^2+m^2)] D^s_{μν}(p_e·σ_e) d^4p, which are shown to be reflection positive. The Poincaré generators are then expressed in terms of Euclidean space-time variables by identifying Euclidean rotations involving space and Euclidean time coordinates with Lorentz boosts at complex rapidity, and Euclidean time translations with Minkowski time translations at t = -iτ.

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean approach to relativistic quantum mechanics originally developed by Schwinger (1950s), Symanzik (1966), and formalized by Osterwalder and Schrader (1973-1974), who showed that an explicit analytic continuation is not necessary to construct a relativistic quantum theory and that the reconstruction is not limited to local field theories. The paper provides explicit representations of Poincaré generators for irreducible representations, which enables the construction of interacting models by decomposing general Euclidean covariant distributions into direct integrals of irreducible kernels, analogous to diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. This addresses the difficulty of satisfying cluster properties in other relativistic quantum mechanics approaches, such as those of Sokolov (1977), Coester (1982), and Keister (1991).
