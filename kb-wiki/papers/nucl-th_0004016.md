# Color superconductivity in cold, dense quark matter

> We review what is different and what is similar in a color superconductor as compared to an ordinary BCS superconductor. The parametric dependence of the zero-temperature gap on the coupling constant differs in QCD from that in BCS theory. On the other hand, the transition temperature to the superconducting phase is related to the zero-temperature gap in the same way in QCD as in BCS theory.

## Key claim

The zero-temperature color-superconducting gap in QCD depends parametrically on the coupling constant as exp(-constant/g) rather than the BCS-like exp(-constant/g²), but the ratio of the critical temperature to the zero-temperature gap remains the same as in BCS theory, T_c/φ₀ ≃ 0.567, at least to leading order in the coupling.

## Method

The authors compare the gap equations for point-like four-fermion interactions, massive scalar boson exchange, and single-gluon exchange in QCD. For QCD, they analyze the gap equation including the long-range magnetic gluon exchange, retaining the frequency dependence of the gluon propagator and the energy dependence of the gap function. They then extend the analysis to nonzero temperature by inserting a tanh factor into the gap equation and dividing the integration range into near-Fermi-surface and far-from-Fermi-surface regions.

## Key numbers

- For point-like four-fermion interactions: φ₀ ≃ 2 μ exp(-1/G²)
- For massive scalar boson exchange (M ∼ g μ): φ₀ ≃ 2 μ exp[-1/(g² ln(1 + 2μ²/M²))]
- For magnetic gluon exchange in QCD: φ₀ ≃ 2 μ exp(-1/g)
- Refined QCD result: φ₀ = b μ g⁻⁵ exp(-c/g) with c = 3π²/√2
- The constant b = 512 π⁴ (2/N_f)^(5/2) b', where b' is undetermined; Brown, Liu, and Ren obtained b' = exp[-(π²+4)/8] ≃ 0.176
- T_c/φ₀ = e^γ/π ≃ 0.567, where γ ≃ 0.577 is the Euler–Mascheroni constant
- For 2-flavor QCD without wavefunction corrections, the gap can reach ∼100 MeV; with wavefunction renormalization (3 flavors), the gap reduces to a few MeV and the transition temperature is at most ∼6 MeV

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Bailin and Love (1984), who estimated the gap at ∼1 MeV using one-gluon exchange with screening masses, and the more recent work of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schäfer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998), who used point-like four-fermion interactions and found gaps up to ∼100 MeV. The authors show that the correct treatment of long-range magnetic gluon exchange, first noted by Barrois (1979) but not published, changes the parametric dependence of the gap. The result enables calculation of the color-superconducting transition temperature in the T-μ phase diagram of nuclear matter, with implications for heavy-ion collisions and neutron star cores.
