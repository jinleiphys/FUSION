# Compact star deformation and universal relationship for magnetized white dwarfs

> Recently super-Chandrasekhar mass limit has been derived theoretically in presence of strong magnetic field to complement experimental observations. In the framework of Newtonian physics, we have studied the equilibrium configurations of such magnetized white dwarfs by using the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation of state for magnetized white-dwarfs. Hartle formalism, for slowly rotating stars, has been employed to obtain the equations of equilibrium. Various physical quantities of uniformly rotating and non-rotating white dwarfs have been calculated within this formalism. Consequently, the universality relationship between the moment of inertia(I), rotational love number($λ$) and spin induced quadrupole moment(Q), namely the I-Love-Q relationship, has been investigated for such magnetized white dwarfs. The relationship between I, eccentricity and Q i.e. I-eccentricity-Q relationship has also been derived. Further, we have found that, the I-eccentricity-Q relationship is more universal in comparison to I-Love-Q relationship.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for magnetized white dwarfs, the I-eccentricity-Q relationship is more universal than the I-Love-Q relationship, but this universality is demonstrated only within the framework of Newtonian physics and the slow-rotation approximation, using a specific relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation of state for magnetized matter.

## Method

The authors employed the Hartle formalism for slowly rotating stars within Newtonian gravity to compute equilibrium configurations of magnetized white dwarfs. They used a relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation of state that incorporates Landau quantization of electrons in strong magnetic fields, treating rotation as a perturbation up to first order in Omega^2. The equilibrium equations were solved numerically for Helium, Carbon, Oxygen, and Iron white dwarfs at various magnetic field strengths, from which the moment of inertia (I), rotational Love number (lambda), spin-induced quadrupole moment (Q), and eccentricity were extracted.

## Key numbers

- The Keplerian angular velocity Omega_K is defined as sqrt(G M_tot / r_e^3), used as the upper limit for the slow-rotation approximation.
- The rotational apsidal constant k_2 is given by k_2 = [3 - eta_2(a)] / [2(2 + eta_2(a))], where eta_2(a) is the surface value of the logarithmic derivative of ellipticity.
- The tidal Love number lambda is defined as lambda = (2/3G) a^5 k_2, where a is the radius of the unperturbed star.
- The quadrupole moment Q is defined as Q = K_1 / G, where K_1 is determined from continuity conditions at the stellar surface.
- The eccentricity e is defined as e = sqrt(1 - (r_p / r_e)^2), where r_p and r_e are polar and equatorial radii respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of I-Love-Q universal relationships, previously established for cold white dwarfs by Boshkayev et al. (2017) and shown to break down at high temperatures by Boshkayev et al. (2018), to the case of magnetized white dwarfs. It also builds on the authors' own prior development of the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation of state for magnetized white dwarfs (Roy et al., 2019). The results enable future gravitational wave studies of white dwarf binaries by providing EoS-independent relationships that may hold even in the presence of strong magnetic fields, though the authors note that the I-Love-Q relationship loses universality under realistic magnetic field configurations, similar to findings for neutron stars by Haskell et al. (2014).
