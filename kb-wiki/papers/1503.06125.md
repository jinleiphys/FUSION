# The Non-Mesonic Weak Decay of Double-Lambda Hypernuclei: A Microscopic Approach

> The non--mesonic weak decay of double--$Λ$ hypernuclei is studied within a microscopic diagrammatic approach. Besides the nucleon--induced mechanism, $ΛN\to nN$, widely studied in single--$Λ$ hypernuclei, additional hyperon--induced mechanisms, $ΛΛ\to Λn$, $ΛΛ\to Σ^0 n$ and $ΛΛ\to Σ^-p$, are accessible in double--$Λ$ hypernuclei and are investigated here. As in previous works on single--$Λ$ hypernuclei, we adopt a nuclear matter formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation and a one--meson exchange weak transition potential (including the ground state pseudoscalar and vector octets mesons) supplemented by correlated and uncorrelated two--pion--exchange contributions. The weak decay rates are evaluated for hypernuclei in the region of the experimentally accessible light hypernuclei $^{10}_{ΛΛ}$Be and $^{13}_{ΛΛ}$B. Our predictions are compared with a few previous evaluations. The rate for the $ΛΛ\to Λn$ decay is dominated by $K$--, $K^*$-- and $η$--exchange and turns out to be about 2.5\% of the free $Λ$ decay rate, $Γ_Λ^{\rm free}$, while the total rate for the $ΛΛ\to Σ^0 n$ and $ΛΛ\to Σ^- p$ decays, dominated by $π$--exchange, amounts to about 0.25\% of $Γ_Λ^{\rm free}$. The experimental measurement of these decays would be essential for the beginning of a systematic study of the non--mesonic decay of strangeness $-2$ hypernuclei. This field of research could also shed light on the possible existence and nature of the $H$--dibaryon.

## Key claim

The non-mesonic weak decay of double-Λ hypernuclei includes hyperon-induced mechanisms (ΛΛ → Λn, ΛΛ → Σ⁰n, ΛΛ → Σ⁻p) that are not accessible in single-Λ hypernuclei; the ΛΛ → Λn decay rate is about 2.5% of the free Λ decay rate, while the combined Σ⁰n and Σ⁻p channels amount to about 0.25%, with the caveat that these predictions depend on the specific meson-exchange model and the local density approximation used to extrapolate from nuclear matter to finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic diagrammatic approach using infinite nuclear matter formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, with a one-meson exchange weak transition potential (including pseudoscalar and vector octet mesons: π, η, K, ρ, ω, K*) supplemented by correlated and uncorrelated two-pion-exchange contributions. The decay rates were evaluated for double-Λ hypernuclei in the mass range A ∼ 12, corresponding to the experimentally accessible systems ¹⁰_ΛΛBe and ¹³_ΛΛB, using a 1s₁/₂ harmonic oscillator wavefunction for the Λ particles with ℏω = 13.6 MeV and a Λ binding energy V_Λ = −13.6 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The ΛΛ → Λn decay rate is 2.48 × 10⁻² Γ_Λ^free (about 2.5% of the free Λ decay rate) for A ∼ 12 double-Λ hypernuclei
- The ΛΛ → Σ⁰n decay rate is 0.08 × 10⁻² Γ_Λ^free and the ΛΛ → Σ⁻p decay rate is 0.17 × 10⁻² Γ_Λ^free (combined about 0.25% of Γ_Λ^free)
- The nucleon-induced decay rates for A ∼ 12 are Γ_n = 0.48 Γ_Λ^free and Γ_p = 1.12 Γ_Λ^free, giving Γ_N = 1.60 Γ_Λ^free with Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.43
- The ratio Γ_Λn/Γ_Σ⁰n is approximately 30 in this work
- The ratio Γ_n/Γ_Λn is approximately 19.4 for the A ∼ 12 calculation

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-mesonic weak decay from single-Λ hypernuclei to double-Λ hypernuclei, where additional hyperon-induced decay channels become accessible. The results are compared with earlier finite nucleus calculations by Parreño (2001) for ⁶_ΛΛHe, which gave Γ_Λn = 3.6 × 10⁻² Γ_Λ^free, and by Itonaga (2001) for ⁶_ΛΛHe and ¹⁰_ΛΛBe, which gave values ranging from 3.4 to 5.3 × 10⁻² Γ_Λ^free. The present calculation is the first to include a two-pion-exchange mechanism derived from chiral unitary approach, though this contribution was found to be negligible (0.002 × 10⁻² Γ_Λ^free). The experimental measurement of these decays at facilities like J-PARC and FAIR could provide information on the ΛΛ strong interaction and potentially shed light on the existence of the H-dibaryon.
