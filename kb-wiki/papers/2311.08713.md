# Four-body bound states in momentum space: the Yakubovsky approach without two-body $t-$matrices

> This study presents a solution to the Yakubovsky equations for four-body bound states in momentum space, bypassing the common use of two-body $t-$matrices. Typically, such solutions are dependent on the fully-off-shell two-body $t-$matrices, which are obtained from the Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for two-body subsystem energies controlled by the second and third Jacobi momenta. Instead, we use a version of the Yakubovsky equations that doesn't require $t-$matrices, facilitating the direct use of two-body interactions. This approach streamlines the programming and reduces computational time. Numerically, we found that this direct approach to the Yakubovsky equations, using 2B interactions, produces four-body binding energy results consistent with those obtained from the conventional $t-$matrix dependent Yakubovsky equations, for both separable (Yamaguchi and Gaussian) and non-separable (Malfliet-Tjon) interactions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a reformulation of the Yakubovsky equations for four-body bound states in momentum space, which directly uses two-body interactions instead of two-body t-matrices, produces binding energies consistent with the conventional t-matrix-dependent approach for both separable and non-separable potentials, while simplifying programming and reducing computational time.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Yakubovsky integral equations in momentum space for four identical particles, using a version that replaces the two-body t-matrix with the two-body interaction directly. They discretize the continuous momentum and angular variables using Gauss-Legendre quadratures with hyperbolic mapping for Jacobi momenta and linear mapping for angle variables, and employ the Lanczos iterative method to solve the eigenvalue problem where the physical binding energy corresponds to an eigenvalue equal to one. The calculations are performed for s-wave projected separable potentials (Yamaguchi and Gaussian form factors) and the non-separable Malfliet-Tjon potential, with multi-dimensional interpolations on shifted momentum arguments handled by Cubic-Hermite spline interpolation.

## Key numbers

* Four-body binding energy for the Malfliet-Tjon V potential: -30.08 MeV from the t-matrix-free approach, compared to -30.07 MeV from conventional t-matrix-dependent calculations (Kamada, 1992).
* Four-body binding energy for the Yamaguchi-IV potential: -36.27 MeV from the t-matrix-free approach, compared to -36.3 MeV from conventional t-matrix-dependent calculations (PhysRevC.15.2257.2).
* Four-body binding energy for the Gaussian potential: -31.27 MeV from both the t-matrix-free and conventional approaches (converged to 0.01 MeV precision).
* All calculations used hbar^2/m = 41.47 MeV·fm^2, with 40 mesh points for angle variables and up to 150 mesh points for Jacobi momenta magnitudes.

## Context

This work extends the t-matrix-free Yakubovsky approach previously solved in configuration space by Lazauskas et al. (2004, 2018, 2019, 2020) to momentum space, providing an alternative to the conventional t-matrix-dependent Yakubovsky equations used by Kamada (1992), Nogga (2002), and Hadizadeh (2007). The approach enables more straightforward numerical implementation for four-body bound state calculations and is related to a similar t-matrix-free method recently applied to relativistic three-body bound states by Mohammadzadeh (submitted). The extension to include interactions beyond s-wave is reported as work in progress.
