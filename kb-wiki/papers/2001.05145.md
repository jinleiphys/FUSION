# Low-lying level structure of $Λ$ hypernuclei and spin dependence of $ΛN$ interaction with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> $ΛN$ spin-spin and spin-orbit splittings in low-lying excitation spectra are investigated for $p$-shell $Λ$ hypernuclei on the basis of the microscopic structure calculation within the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, where the $ΛN$ $G$-matrix interaction derived from the baryon-baryon interaction model ESC (extended soft core) is used. It is found that the ground-state spin-parity is systematically reproduced in the $p$-shell $Λ$ hypernuclei by tuning the $ΛN$ spin-spin and spin-orbit interactions so as to reproduce the experimental data of $^{4}_Λ$H, $^{7}_Λ$Li and $^9_Λ$Be. Furthermore, we also focus on the excitation energies of the excited doublets as well as the energy shifts of them by the addition of a $Λ$ particle.

## Key claim

By tuning the ΛN spin-spin and spin-orbit interactions within the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics framework to reproduce experimental data of ⁴_ΛH, ⁷_ΛLi, and ⁹_ΛBe, the ground-state spin-parity of p-shell Λ hypernuclei can be systematically reproduced, though the authors note that fine tuning of the YNG interactions is still necessary to describe level ordering of ground-state spin doublet partners properly.

## Method

The authors applied an extended version of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei (HyperAMD) to several p-shell Λ hypernuclei, using the Gogny D1S force as the effective nuclear interaction and the G-matrix interaction derived from the Nijmegen ESC14 potential (with many-body effects, ESC14+MBE) as the ΛN effective interaction. The ΛN interaction was decomposed into central (spin-spin) and spin-orbit (symmetric LS and antisymmetric LS) components, and the Fermi momentum dependence of the G-matrix was treated by the averaged density approximation. Wave functions were obtained by variational minimization under constraints on nuclear quadrupole deformation, followed by angular momentum projection and generator coordinate method calculations.

## Key numbers

- The experimental spin-doublet splitting for ¹²_ΛC (2⁻ – 1⁻_g) is 0.162 MeV.
- The experimental spin-doublet splitting for ¹¹_ΛB (7/2⁺ – 5/2⁺_g) is 0.263 MeV.
- The theoretical ratio ΔE₁₁/ΔE₁₂ ≈ 1.67 from the schematic model agrees well with the experimental ratio of 1.62.
- A recent ¹²C(e,e'K⁺)¹²_ΛB experiment suggests ΔE(¹²_ΛB) ≈ 0.18 MeV.
- In ⁶Li, the excitation energy of the 3⁺ state calculated with modified Gogny D1S (α^NN = 1.2) is 2.21 MeV, close to the observed value of 2.19 MeV.
- The excitation energy of the 3⁺ state in ⁶Li calculated with the original Gogny D1S is lower than experiment by about 700 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HyperAMD calculations (Isaka and Yamamoto, 2016, 2017) that successfully reproduced the mass dependence of Λ binding energies B_Λ but gave incorrect level ordering for spin doublet partners in several p-shell hypernuclei. The paper addresses the long-standing problem of spin-orbit splittings in hypernuclei, where earlier shell-model analyses using NSC97f (Hiyama, 2010) showed insufficient cancellation between symmetric LS and antisymmetric LS contributions, while the ESC model (Rijken, 2010) was designed to improve this cancellation. The results enable systematic application of the revised YNG interactions to hypernuclei across the full mass region.
