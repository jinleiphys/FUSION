# QCD Sum Rules: Isospin Symmetry Breakings in Pion-Nucleon Couplings

> We use the method of QCD sum rules in the presence of an external pion field to investigate isospin symmetry breakings in pion-nucleon couplings. Possible manifestations of such isospin symmetry breaking are examined in the context of low-energy nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) scattering. We discuss numerical results in relation to both the existing data and other theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules with an external pion field, the authors find that the magnitude of the pion-neutron coupling \(|g_{\pi nn}|\) is larger than \(|g_{\pi pp}|\) by 0.80%, a result that differs in sign from earlier quark-model calculations (Cao) and a three-point correlation function approach (Meissner), but which accounts for the observed charge-symmetry breaking scattering length difference \(\delta a_{\text{CSB}}\) both in sign and roughly in magnitude.

## Method

The authors evaluate correlation functions of nucleon interpolating currents in the presence of an external pion field, expanding quark propagators up to dimension-eight operators including quark condensates, gluon condensates, and mixed condensates. Isospin symmetry breaking is introduced through the differences \(m_u \neq m_d\), \(\langle \bar{u}u \rangle \neq \langle \bar{d}d \rangle\), non-universal pion-quark couplings from electromagnetic vertex renormalizations, and the neutron-proton mass difference. Borel-transformed QCD sum rules are then matched to hadronic parametrizations to extract the couplings \(g_{\pi^0 pp}\), \(g_{\pi^0 nn}\), and \(g_{\pi^+ np}\).

## Key numbers

* The relative difference between the \(\pi^0 nn\) and \(\pi^0 pp\) couplings is 0.58% from the sum rule alone; combined with the pion-quark coupling difference, \(|g_{\pi^0 nn}|\) exceeds \(|g_{\pi^0 pp}|\) by 0.80%.
* The relative difference between the \(\pi^+ np\) and \(\pi^0 pp\) couplings is 0.35% from the sum rule; combined with vertex renormalizations, \(|g_{\pi^+ np}|\) exceeds \(|g_{\pi^0 pp}|\) by 0.15%.
* The charge-symmetry breaking scattering length difference \(\delta a_{\text{CSB}} \equiv |a_{pp}| - |a_{nn}|\) is experimentally \(-(1.5 \pm 0.5)\) fm; the predicted coupling difference contributes about \(-1.0\) fm to this quantity.
* The charge-dependent scattering length difference \(\delta a_{\text{CD}} \equiv |a_{np}| - |a_{nn}|\) is experimentally \((5.0 \pm 0.3)\) fm, dominated by the pion mass difference \(m_{\pi^+} \neq m_{\pi^0}\) rather than coupling differences.
* Input parameters include: \(a_q = 0.546\ \text{GeV}^3\), \(m_u = 5.1\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_d = 8.9\ \text{MeV}\), \(b = 0.474\ \text{GeV}^4\), \(m_0^2 = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\chi a = -3.14\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\sigma = -10^{-3}\), \(\epsilon = 1.3 \times 10^{-3}\).
* The Borel window used is \(0.8\ \text{GeV}^2 \leq M_B^2 \leq 1.5\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to pion-nucleon couplings (previously applied by Ioffe, and more recently by Meissner) by including isospin breaking effects from quark mass differences, condensate differences, and electromagnetic vertex renormalizations. It contradicts the sign of the charge-symmetry breaking found in quark-model calculations (Cao) and in the three-point correlation function approach (Meissner). The results enable a quantitative understanding of both the charge-symmetry breaking (\(\delta a_{\text{CSB}}\)) and charge-dependent (\(\delta a_{\text{CD}}\)) scattering length differences in low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering, which had previously been attributed primarily to the pion mass difference and \(\rho-\omega\) mixing (Henley, Miller).
