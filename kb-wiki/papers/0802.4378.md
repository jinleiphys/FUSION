# Superscaling and Charge-Changing Neutrino Scattering from Nuclei in the $\boldsymbol Δ$-Region beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model

> The superscaling analysis using the scaling function obtained within the coherent density fluctuation model is extended to calculate charge-changing neutrino and antineutrino scattering on $^{12}$C at energies from 1 to 2 GeV not only in the quasielastic but also in the delta excitation region. The results are compared with those obtained using the scaling functions from the relativistic Fermi gas model and from the superscaling analysis of inclusive scattering of electrons from nuclei.

# Superscaling and Charge-Changing Neutrino Scattering from Nuclei in the Δ-Region beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model

## Key claim

The coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) scaling functions, previously applied to quasielastic (QE) electron and neutrino scattering, are extended to the Δ-resonance region for charge-changing neutrino and antineutrino scattering on ¹²C at incident energies from 1 to 2 GeV, with the caveat that the CDFM Δ-region scaling function requires effective parameters (R_Δ = 1.565 fm, b_Δ = 0.420 fm, and a reduced normalization coefficient of 0.54 instead of 3/4) that differ from the QE case, and the results depend on whether a symmetric (c₁ = 0.72) or asymmetric (c₁ = 0.63) QE scaling function is used depending on whether the momentum transfer lies in the scaling domain (q ≥ 450 MeV/c ≈ 2k_F) or not.

## Method

The authors construct CDFM scaling functions for both the QE and Δ-regions using realistic nucleon density and momentum distributions, with the Δ-region scaling function obtained by integrating the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) Δ-scaling function weighted by a density-derived weight function |F_Δ(R)|². These scaling functions are then inserted into the standard formalism for charge-changing neutrino-nucleus cross sections, using the Hohler parametrization 8.2 for vector nucleon form factors and the axial-vector form factors from the SuSA framework, to compute differential cross sections for ν_μ and anti-ν_μ scattering on ¹²C at muon angles of 30°, 45°, and 60° and incident energies of 1, 1.5, and 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The CDFM Δ-region scaling function for ¹²C uses effective parameters: R_Δ = 1.565 fm, b_Δ = 0.420 fm, and a coefficient of 0.54 (instead of 3/4) in the RFG scaling function, with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.20 fm⁻¹ ensuring normalization to unity.
- The QE CDFM scaling function uses c₁ = 0.72 (symmetric, for q ≥ 450 MeV/c ≈ 2k_F) or c₁ = 0.63 (asymmetric, for q ≤ 450 MeV/c), compared to the RFG value c₁ = 0.75.
- At ε_ν = 1 GeV and θ_μ = 30°, the relative height of the Δ-peak to QE peak (Δ/QE) is approximately 0.5; at θ_μ = 60°, this ratio becomes ≥ 1.
- At θ_μ = 45°, the Δ/QE ratio increases from ≤ 1 at ε_ν = 1 GeV to approximately 4/3 at ε_ν = 2 GeV.
- The antineutrino scattering cross section at ε = 1 GeV and θ_μ = 45° is about 5 times smaller than the neutrino cross section.

## Context

This work extends the CDFM superscaling analysis from the QE region (previously applied to electron scattering by Ivanov et al. (2006) and to neutrino scattering by the same group) into the Δ-region, completing a unified description of both inclusive electron and neutrino scattering from nuclei on the same footing. The results are compared with the RFG model and the phenomenological superscaling analysis (SuSA) of Barbaro et al. (2004), and they show that the CDFM with asymmetric scaling function (c₁ = 0.63) yields QE cross sections close to SuSA, while the symmetric version (c₁ = 0.72) resembles the RFG model. This enables reliable predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the 1-2 GeV energy range relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments, where both QE and Δ-excitation contributions must be accounted for simultaneously.
