# Self-Consistent Approximations to Non-Equilibrium Many-Body Theory

> Within the non-equilibrium Green's function technique on the real time contour, the Phi-functional method of Baym is reviewed and generalized to arbitrary non-equilibrium many-particle systems. The scheme may be closed at any desired order in the number of loops or vertices of the generating functional. It defines effective theories, which provide a closed set of coupled classical field and Dyson equations, which are self-consistent, conserving and thermodynamically consistent. The approach permits to include unstable particles and therefore unifies the description of resonances with all other particles, which obtain a mass width by collisions, decays or creation processes in dense matter. The inclusion of classical fields enables the treatment of soft modes and phase instabilities. The method can be taken as a starting point for adequate and consistent quantum improvements of the in-medium rates in transport theories.

## Key claim

The paper reviews and generalizes Baym's Phi-functional method to arbitrary non-equilibrium many-particle systems, claiming that the scheme can be closed at any desired order in the number of loops or vertices of the generating functional, producing self-consistent, conserving, and thermodynamically consistent approximations. The hedge is that this framework is presented as a starting point for consistent quantum improvements of in-medium rates in transport theories, rather than as a complete solution.

## Method

The authors employ the non-equilibrium Green's function technique on the real time contour, extending Baym's Phi-functional approach to arbitrary non-equilibrium systems. The method defines effective theories through a generating functional that can be truncated at any desired order in loops or vertices, yielding a closed set of coupled classical field and Dyson equations. The approach incorporates unstable particles and classical fields, enabling treatment of resonances, soft modes, and phase instabilities.

## Key numbers

- The paper is a formal/methodological review with no original numerical results presented.

## Context

This work extends Baym's Phi-functional method (1962) from equilibrium to arbitrary non-equilibrium many-particle systems, building on the non-equilibrium Green's function technique developed by Kadanoff and Baym (1962) and Keldysh (1965). It enables the consistent inclusion of unstable particles and classical fields in transport theories, providing a foundation for quantum improvements of in-medium rates that can be applied to dense matter systems such as those studied in heavy-ion collisions and nuclear astrophysics.
