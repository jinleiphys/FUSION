# Measurement of the 92,93,94,100Mo(g,n) reactions by Coulomb Dissociation

> The Coulomb Dissociation (CD) cross sections of the stable isotopes 92,94,100Mo and of the unstable isotope 93Mo were measured at the LAND/R3B setup at GSI Helmholtzzentrum für Schwerionenforschung in Darmstadt, Germany. Experimental data on these isotopes may help to explain the problem of the underproduction of 92,94Mo and 96,98Ru in the models of p-process nucleosynthesis. The CD cross sections obtained for the stable Mo isotopes are in good agreement with experiments performed with real photons, thus validating the method of Coulomb Dissociation. The result for the reaction 93Mo(g,n) is especially important since the corresponding cross section has not been measured before. A preliminary integral Coulomb Dissociation cross section of the 94Mo(g,n) reaction is presented. Further analysis will complete the experimental database for the (g,n) production chain of the p-isotopes of molybdenum.

## Key claim

The Coulomb Dissociation method is validated for measuring (gamma,n) cross sections on stable molybdenum isotopes by agreement with real-photon experiments, and a first-ever measurement of the 93Mo(gamma,n) cross section is reported; however, the 94Mo(gamma,n) result is preliminary, with an integrated cross section that is 0.74 times the value derived from earlier photoabsorption data by Beil et al. (1974), pending further efficiency corrections and systematic error studies.

## Method

The (gamma,n) reactions were studied in inverse kinematics using the LAND/R3B setup at GSI. A beam of each molybdenum isotope impinged on a lead target, and the time-varying Coulomb field of the target was treated as a flux of virtual photons whose absorption probability was converted to a (gamma,n) cross section. Background contributions from nuclear interactions were subtracted using measurements with a carbon target and an empty target, with a nuclear scaling factor alpha calculated from the black disc model (alpha = 1.53 for 94Mo). The neutron kinetic energy in the fragment rest frame was reconstructed event-by-event, and the spectrum was corrected for LAND efficiency and acceptance using simulations.

## Key numbers

- Preliminary integrated Coulomb Dissociation cross section for the reaction Pb(94Mo,93Mo+n)Pb at a beam energy of 500 MeV: 675 mb.
- Coulomb Dissociation cross section calculated from the photoabsorption data of Beil et al. (1974) for 94Mo(gamma,n): 909 mb.
- Ratio of the experimental integrated cross section to that derived from photoabsorption data for 94Mo: 0.74.
- Nuclear scaling factor alpha for the 94Mo projectile in the black disc model: 1.53.

## Context

This work extends the experimental validation of the Coulomb Dissociation method for (gamma,n) reactions on stable molybdenum isotopes, previously tested for 92Mo and 100Mo by Ershova (2012), and provides the first data for the unstable isotope 93Mo. The results address the underproduction of 92,94Mo and 96,98Ru in p-process nucleosynthesis models (Arnould 2003; Travaglio 2011). The ongoing analysis of 94Mo(gamma,n) will complete the experimental database for the photoneutron production chain of the molybdenum p-isotopes, enabling improved constraints on statistical model calculations used in stellar reaction networks.
