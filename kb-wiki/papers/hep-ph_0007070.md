# Finite pion width effects on the rho--meson

> We study the influence of the finite damping width of pions on the in-medium properties of the rho-meson in an interacting meson gas model at finite temperature. Using vector dominance also implications on the resulting dilepton spectra from the decay of the rho-meson are presented. A set of coupled Dyson equations with self energies up to the sunset diagram level is solved self consistently. Following a Phi-derivable scheme the self energies are dynamically determined by the self consistent propagators. Some problems concerning the self consistent treatment of vector or gauge bosons on the propagator level, in particular, if coupled to currents arising from particles with a sizable damping width, are discussed.

## Key claim

The finite damping width of pions, generated by a four-pion self-interaction to simulate the width pions would obtain in a baryon-rich environment, significantly enhances the low-mass dilepton spectrum from rho-meson decay below 500 MeV, with the low-mass region dominated by pion bremsstrahlung contributions that vanish in lowest-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors solved a set of coupled Dyson equations with self-energies up to the sunset diagram level self-consistently, using a Phi-derivable scheme where the self-energies are dynamically determined by the self-consistent propagators. The model considered a purely mesonic system of charged pions, neutral rho-mesons, and a1-mesons at a temperature of 110 MeV, with the four-pion coupling adjusted to produce pion damping widths of 50 MeV and 125 MeV. For the vector meson polarisation tensor, the authors projected onto the transverse part of the propagators to work around the violation of Ward-Takahashi identities inherent in the self-consistent scheme.

## Key numbers

- Pion damping widths of about 50 MeV (moderate case) and 125 MeV (strong case) were generated around the peak of the pion spectral function at a temperature of T = 110 MeV
- The rho-meson vacuum width was set to Gamma_rho = 150 MeV at a nominal mass of 770 MeV
- The a1-meson vacuum width was set to Gamma_a1 = 400 MeV at a nominal mass of 1200 MeV
- The transverse rho-meson width Gamma_T was approximately 150 MeV, almost constant over the displayed invariant mass range in the self-consistent result
- The pion spectral function and width were shown at a fixed momentum of 150 MeV/c
- The rho-meson spectral function was shown at a fixed three-momentum of 150 MeV/c
- The low-mass region below 500 MeV showed significant enhancement from finite pion width effects
- The threshold gap between sqrt(s) in [0, 2m_pi] was completely filled in the self-consistent results
- The longitudinal and transverse components deviated from one another towards low invariant masses below sqrt(s) < 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical investigations of vector meson in-medium properties by including the damping width of pions within a self-consistent scheme, whereas most prior studies either ignored the pion width or treated it within extended perturbation theory (ubw99). The calculations are motivated by experimental observations from the CERES and DLS collaborations showing enhanced low lepton pair mass spectra between 300 MeV and 600 MeV in heavy ion collisions compared to pp-collisions. The paper discusses the fundamental problem that self-consistent truncation schemes for vector or gauge bosons violate gauge invariance on the correlator level, and outlines a background field method to construct a gauge-covariant external polarisation tensor via ladder resummation, though this remains numerically impractical for full off-shell calculations.
