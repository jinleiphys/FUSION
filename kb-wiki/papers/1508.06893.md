# Nuclear forces and their impact on neutron-rich nuclei and neutron-rich matter

> We review the impact of nuclear forces on matter at neutron-rich extremes. Recent results have shown that neutron-rich nuclei become increasingly sensitive to three-nucleon forces, which are at the forefront of theoretical developments based on effective field theories of quantum chromodynamics. This includes the formation of shell structure, the spectroscopy of exotic nuclei, and the location of the neutron dripline. Nuclear forces also constrain the properties of neutron-rich matter, including the neutron skin, the symmetry energy, and the structure of neutron stars. We first review our understanding of three-nucleon forces and show how chiral effective field theory makes unique predictions for many-body forces. Then, we survey results with three-nucleon forces in neutron-rich oxygen and calcium isotopes and neutron-rich matter, which have been explored with a range of many-body methods. Three-nucleon forces therefore provide an exciting link between theoretical, experimental and observational nuclear physics frontiers.

## Key claim

Three-nucleon forces provide a crucial link between neutron-rich nuclei and neutron-rich matter, but the theoretical uncertainties in the Hamiltonian, particularly from the truncation in chiral effective field theory and from uncertainties in the low-energy couplings, have been less explored than the uncertainties from many-body calculations.

## Method

The authors review the impact of nuclear forces on neutron-rich systems by surveying results from multiple many-body methods (many-body perturbation theory, coupled-cluster theory, self-consistent Green's function, and multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group) applied to chiral effective field theory interactions. They examine how three-nucleon forces affect shell structure, spectroscopy, and ground-state energies in oxygen and calcium isotopes, and how the same forces constrain the equation of state of neutron-rich matter and neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- The mass measurement of $^{52}$Ca at TITAN/TRIUMF found it to be 1.74 MeV more bound, representing the largest change in the atomic mass evaluation in the last ten years.
- The experimental peak in magnetic dipole $B(M1)$ strength in $^{48}$Ca at 10.2 MeV is reproduced in MBPT calculations, but with too much strength, suggesting a modest quenching of $q=0.9$ in the spin $g$-factor.
- The breakdown scale of chiral EFT is $\Lambda_{\rm b} \sim 500$ MeV, leading to a typical expansion parameter $Q/\Lambda_{\rm b} \sim 1/3$.
- Saturation density of nuclear matter is $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- For the blue band in neutron matter results, the NN potential was RG-evolved to a low-momentum scale $\Lambda = 2.0$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This review extends the understanding of three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory, building on earlier work by Epelbaum (2009) and Machleidt (2011) on chiral EFT for nuclear forces, and by Bogner (2005) on nuclear matter saturation. It surveys results that demonstrate three-nucleon forces are essential for the $N=28$ shell closure in calcium, which was not reproduced with NN forces only in calculations by Holt (2012) and Hagen (2012). The work enables future studies of order-by-order convergence in chiral EFT, inclusion of N$^3$LO three-nucleon forces in nuclei beyond $A=3$, and improved constraints on neutron star properties from nuclear forces.
