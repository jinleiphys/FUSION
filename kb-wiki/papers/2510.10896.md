# Laser-assisted α decay of actinide nuclei in bichromatic fields

> Actinide nuclei provide a suitable platform for studying the laser-assisted nuclear $α$ decay, with potential applications in nuclear transmutation, nuclear radiotherapy, and nuclear battery regulation. In the present work, we develop a deformed one-parameter model to quantitatively study the influence of ultra-intense laser fields on the $α$ decay of actinide nuclei. Our calculations show that the $α$-decay half-lives of these nuclei can be altered to some finite extent under laser intensities anticipated at near-future laser facilities. Furthermore, we found that, from the perspective of the nucleus, the laser field's effect on $α$ decay is governed by the nuclear shell structure and decay energy. The $α$-emitting nuclei with lower decay energies and located farther from neutron shell closures are more susceptible to the laser fields. From the perspective of the laser driver, we proposed a bichromatic laser scheme to enhance the effects of laser fields on $α$ tunneling of actinide nuclei. With appropriate phase conditions and amplitude ratios, it is shown that a fundamental-second-harmonic ($ω$-$2ω$) bichromatic field can increase the time-averaged modification by one to two orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The authors develop a deformed one-parameter model to show that the alpha-decay half-lives of actinide nuclei can be altered by 0.01% to 0.1% under laser intensities anticipated at near-future facilities (around 10^25 W/cm^2), and that a fundamental-second-harmonic bichromatic laser scheme can increase the time-averaged modification by one to two orders of magnitude compared to monochromatic fields.

## Method

The authors extend the one-parameter model of alpha decay by incorporating nuclear deformation effects through quadrupole, hexadecapole, and hexacontatetrapole deformation parameters, using a double-folding approach for the deformed Coulomb potential. They introduce an electric-dipole interaction term to model the laser field effect on the alpha particle and daughter nucleus, treating the tunneling process under the quasistatic approximation since the tunneling time (~10^-21 s) is much shorter than the optical cycle. The model is calibrated by fitting the single adjustable parameter g to experimental half-lives of 118 favored alpha emitters using least-squares minimization, achieving a standard deviation of 0.435.

## Key numbers

- The standard deviation between calculated and experimental half-lives for 118 actinide nuclei is sigma_min = 0.435 with g = 0.168, corresponding to agreement within a factor of 2.7.
- For the recently synthesized nucleus 210Pa (E_alpha = 8.258 MeV, experimental half-life 6.0 ms), the model predicts a half-life of 5.047 ms.
- At laser intensity I_0 = 10^25 W/cm^2 and wavelength 800 nm, the instantaneous alpha-decay half-lives of actinide nuclei are altered by 0.01% to 0.1%.
- The time-averaged modification of tunneling probability Delta P_t^avg scales linearly with laser intensity I_0 (slope k_i = 1 on a log-log plot).
- Adjusting the carrier-envelope phase can increase or decrease the time-averaged modification by up to a factor of approximately 35 (maximum |chi| reaching about 35).
- A bichromatic field with appropriate phase conditions and amplitude ratios can increase the time-averaged modification by one to two orders of magnitude compared to monochromatic fields.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies on laser-assisted nuclear decay, including the elliptically polarized scheme of Qi et al. (2019), the short rectangular pulse approach of Misicu and Rizea (2019), and the frequency-chirped asymmetric pulse method of Cheng et al. (2022). The authors specifically address the role of nuclear deformation in actinide nuclei, which was not fully incorporated in prior one-parameter models. The results enable future experimental tests at next-generation petawatt-class laser facilities and suggest potential applications in nuclear transmutation of isotopes like 239Pu and 237Np, nuclear radiotherapy using 225Ac and 227Th, and nuclear battery regulation with 238Pu and 244Cm.
