# Scalar Pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone Boson in Nuclei and Dense Nuclear Matter

> The notion that the scalar listed as $f_0 (500)$ in the particle data booklet is a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone (NG) boson of spontaneously broken scale symmetry, explicitly broken by a small departure from an infrared fixed point, is explored in nuclear dynamics. That notion which puts the scalar -- that we shall identify as a "dilaton" -- on the same footing as the pseudo-scalar pseudo-NG bosons, i.e., octet $π$, while providing a simple explanation for the $ΔI=1/2$ rule for kaon decay, generalizes the standard chiral perturbation theory (S$χ$PT) to "scale chiral perturbation theory," denoted $χ$PT$_σ$, with {\it one infrared mass scale for both symmetries}, with the $σ$ figuring as a chiral singlet NG mode in non-strange sector. Applied to nuclear dynamics, it is seen to provide possible answers to various hitherto unclarified nuclear phenomena such as the success of one-boson-exchange potentials (OBEP), the large cancellation of strongly attractive scalar potential by strongly repulsive vector potential in relativistic mean field theory of nuclear systems and in-medium QCD sum rules, the interplay of the dilaton and the vector meson $ω$ in dense skyrmion matter, the BPS skyrmion structure of nuclei accounting for small binding energies of medium-heavy nuclei, and the suppression of hyperon degrees of freedom in compact-star matter.

## Key claim

The paper explores the hypothesis that the scalar meson $f_0(500)$ is a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken scale symmetry, with explicit breaking from a small departure from an infrared fixed point and current quark masses; this assumption is taken as potentially valid in medium even if not rigorously proven in the QCD vacuum, and the authors proceed "until we are hit by a torpedo."

## Method

The authors formulate a "scale chiral perturbation theory" ($\chi$PT$_\sigma$) that combines chiral symmetry and scale symmetry by introducing a dilaton field $\sigma$ as a conformal compensator, with the counting rule ${\cal O}(m_q) \sim {\cal O}(p^2) \sim {\cal O}(\Delta\alpha)$. They construct a hidden-local-symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian including pions, vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$), nucleons, and the dilaton, then apply it to nuclear matter by allowing the dilaton condensate $f_\sigma^*$ to vary with density, which generates density-dependent nucleon and vector meson masses.

## Key numbers

- The scalar $f_0(500)$ has a mass around 600 MeV and a large width of order the mass.
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation for the dilaton gives $m_N = f_\sigma g_{\sigma NN}$; using $f_\sigma \simeq f_\pi$ and $g_{\sigma NN} \simeq 9-10$ yields $m_N \simeq 0.84-0.94$ GeV.
- The vector meson mass scales as $m_V^* = (f_\sigma^*/f_{0\sigma}) m_V$ and the nucleon mass as $m_N^* = (f_\sigma^*/f_{0\sigma}) m_N$.
- The pion decay constant scales as $f_\pi^* = (f_\sigma^*/f_{0\sigma}) f_{0\pi}$ and the pion mass as $m_\pi^* = \sqrt{f_\sigma^*/f_{0\sigma}} \, m_\pi$.
- The $\sigma NN$ coupling constant $g_{\sigma NN}$ remains unscaling at the order considered.

## Context

This work extends the Crewther-Tunstall (2015) proposal that the $f_0(500)$ is a dilaton pseudo-NG boson into nuclear dynamics, generalizing standard chiral perturbation theory (S$\chi$PT) to include scale symmetry. It provides a framework to understand the success of one-boson-exchange potentials (OBEP) and relativistic mean field (RMF) theories of Walecka (1974), the cancellation of scalar and vector potentials in nuclear matter, and the behavior of dense skyrmion matter. The approach enables systematic error estimates in nuclear potentials and offers a path to study dense neutron-star matter, including the suppression of hyperon degrees of freedom.
