# Momentum distributions of $α$-particles from decaying low-lying $^{12}$C-resonances

> The complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method is used to compute momentum and energy distributions of the three $α$-particles emerging from the decay of low-lying $^{12}$C-resonances. The large distance continuum properties of the wave functions are crucial and must be accurately calculated. We discuss separately decays of natural parity states: two $0^+$, one $1^{-}$, three $2^+$, one $3^-$, two $4^+$, one $6^+$, and one of each of unnatural parity, $1^{+}$, $2^-$, $3^+$, $4^-$. The lowest natural parity state of each $J^π$ decays predominantly sequentially via the $^{8}$Be ground state whereas other states including unnatural parity states predominantly decay directly to the continuum. We present Dalitz plots and systematic detailed momentum correlations of the emerging $α$-particles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the lowest natural parity state of each spin-parity in 12C decays predominantly sequentially via the 8Be ground state, while other states including unnatural parity states predominantly decay directly to the three-body continuum, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of three-body interaction parameters and the complex scaling angle used to separate resonances from background.

## Method

The authors used the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to solve Faddeev equations for the 3-alpha system in coordinate space, computing resonance wave functions for 12C states below 15.96 MeV excitation energy. They employed an Ali-Bodmer alpha-alpha potential modified to reproduce the 8Be s-wave resonance, with a three-body Gaussian short-range potential adjusted to match observed resonance energies. Momentum and energy distributions of the three alpha particles were obtained by Fourier transforming the resonance wave functions at large hyperradius (80 fm), using Zeldovic regularization for direct decays and Breit-Wigner distributions for sequential decays through 8Be(0+).

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state (0+1 at 7.66 MeV excitation) decays 95% sequentially via 8Be(0+) and 5% directly
- The 2+1 state at 8.66 MeV decays 97% sequentially and 3% directly
- The 3- state at 9.60 MeV decays 96% sequentially and 4% directly
- The 4+1 state at 10.52 MeV decays 92% sequentially and 8% directly
- The 1- state at 10.88 MeV decays 70% sequentially and 30% directly
- The 0+2 state at 11.22 MeV decays 59% sequentially and 41% directly
- The 2+2 state at 11.76 MeV decays 15% sequentially and 85% directly
- The 2+3 state at 13.76 MeV decays 4% sequentially and 96% directly
- The 4+2 state at 14.10 MeV decays 20% sequentially and 80% directly
- The 6+ state at 14.40 MeV decays 5% sequentially and 95% directly
- The three-body short-range potential range was fixed at b = 6 fm
- Asymptotic behavior of distributions was reached for hyperradii larger than about 60 fm, with computations performed at rho = 80 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of 12C resonance structures (Alvarez-Rodriguez 2007) by computing the momentum distributions that result from decay of those resonances, providing predictions that can be compared with experimental data. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of the 2+ resonance positions and widths, where experiments by Johansson (2003), Itoh (2004), and Diget (2005) have not reached agreement, and where earlier theoretical approaches include Faddeev calculations by Taksaka, R-matrix computations by Barker, and Kurchatov fitting by Korsheninnikov. The computed distributions enable experimental determination of unknown spins and parities, such as the tentative assignment of the 13.26 MeV state as 4- rather than 2-, also suggested by Freer (2007).
