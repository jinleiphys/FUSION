# Excitation energy shift and size difference of low-energy levels in $p$-shell $Λ$ hypernuclei

> We investigated structures of low-lying $0s$-orbit $Λ$ states in $p$-shell $Λ$ hypernuclei ($^A_ΛZ$) by applying microscopic cluster models for nuclear structure and a single-channel folding potential model for a $Λ$ particle. For $A>10$ systems, the size reduction of core nuclei is small, and the core polarization effect is regarded as a higher-order perturbation in the $Λ$ binding. The present calculation qualitatively describes the systematic trend of experimental data for excitation energy change from $^{A-1} Z$ to $^A_ΛZ$, in $A>10$ systems. The energy change shows a clear correlation with the nuclear size difference between the ground and excited states. In $^7_Λ\textrm{Li}$ and $^9_Λ\textrm{Be}$, the significant shrinkage of cluster structures occurs consistently with the prediction of other calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for $p$-shell $\Lambda$ hypernuclei with mass number $A>10$, the core polarization effect (size reduction of the core nucleus due to the $\Lambda$ particle) is small and can be treated as a higher-order perturbation, while for lighter systems like $^7_\Lambda\textrm{Li}$ and $^9_\Lambda\textrm{Be}$, significant shrinkage of cluster structures occurs. The excitation energy shift from the core nucleus $^{A-1}Z$ to the hypernucleus $^A_\Lambda Z$ shows a clear correlation with the nuclear size difference between the ground and excited states of the core.

## Method

The author applied microscopic cluster models (generator coordinate method with Brink-Bloch cluster wave functions) to describe the nuclear structures of core nuclei ($^6\textrm{Li}$, $^8\textrm{Be}$, $^{10}\textrm{B}$, $^{11}\textrm{B}$, $^{11}\textrm{C}$, $^{12}\textrm{C}$), using $\alpha+d$, $2\alpha$, $2\alpha+d$, $2\alpha+t(h)$, and $3\alpha$ cluster configurations with cluster breaking components included for $^{11}\textrm{B}$, $^{11}\textrm{C}$, and $^{12}\textrm{C}$. For the $\Lambda$ particle, a single $S$-wave channel calculation with a folding potential model was performed, where $\Lambda$-nucleus potentials were constructed by folding effective $\Lambda$-$N$ interactions (the $\Lambda NG$ interactions with ESC08a parametrization) with the nuclear density from the cluster models. Core polarization was taken into account by artificially enhancing the central nuclear interactions to simulate the $\Lambda$ impurity effect, then optimizing the enhancement factor to minimize the total energy of the system.

## Key numbers

- For $A>10$ systems, the nuclear size change $\delta_\Lambda(R_N)$ (the rms nuclear matter radius change caused by the $\Lambda$) is less than 5% in the ground state.
- The calculated $\Lambda$ binding energies for $^7_\Lambda\textrm{Li}$, $^9_\Lambda\textrm{Be}$, $^{11}_\Lambda\textrm{B}$, $^{12}_\Lambda\textrm{B}$, $^{12}_\Lambda\textrm{C}$, and $^{13}_\Lambda\textrm{C}$ are reasonably reproduced by both ESC08a(DD) and ESC08a(DI) interactions (specific numerical values for each system are not given in the truncated text).
- The calculated $B(E2)$ for $^6\textrm{Li}(3^+_1)$ is 11.3 e$^2$fm$^4$ (experimental: 10.7(8) e$^2$fm$^4$); for $^{12}\textrm{C}(2^+_1)$ it is 7.3 e$^2$fm$^4$ (experimental: 7.6(4) e$^2$fm$^4$).
- The calculated rms matter radius $R_N$ for $^{12}\textrm{C}(0^+_1)$ is 2.35 fm (experimental point-proton radius: 2.298 fm); without the $p_{3/2}$ cluster breaking component, the $3\alpha$ model gives 2.52 fm.
- The calculated Coulomb shift $\Delta_\textrm{mir}(E_x)$ for $^{11}\textrm{B}(3/2^-_2)$ is 0.22 MeV (experimental: 0.22 MeV), and for $^{11}\textrm{B}(1/2^-_1)$ it is 0.17 MeV (experimental: 0.13 MeV).

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of $p$-shell $\Lambda$ hypernuclei using cluster models, building on prior calculations by Motoba (1984), Hiyama (1996-2010), and Isaka (2011-2017) for specific hypernuclei like $^7_\Lambda\textrm{Li}$ and $^9_\Lambda\textrm{Be}$. The paper proposes a handy treatment of the $\Lambda$ particle using a folding potential with microscopic cluster model densities, which can be applied to general structure models for ordinary nuclei. This enables systematic investigation of excitation energy shifts across a wide mass-number region, linking the shifts to the size difference between ground and excited states of the core nucleus, and testing whether the $\Lambda$ can serve as a probe of original core nuclear properties.
