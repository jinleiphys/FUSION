# Irreducible tensor approach to spin observables in photo production of mesons with arbitrary spin-parity $s^π$

> A theoretical formalism leading to elegant derivation of formulae for all spin observables is outlined for photo production of mesons with arbitrary spin parity $s^π$. The salient features of this formalism based on irreducible tensor techniques are i) the number of independent irreducible tensor amplitudes is $4(2s+1)$, ii) a single compact formula is sufficient to express these amplitudes in terms of allowed electric and magnetic multipole amplitudes and iii) all the spin observables including beam analyzing powers as well as the differential cross section are expressible in terms of bilinear irreducible tensors of rank 0 to $2(s+1)$. The relationship between the irreducible tensor amplitudes and the helicity amplitudes is elucidated in general and explicit expressions for the helicity amplitudes are given in terms of the irreducible tensor amplitudes in the particular cases of pseudoscalar and vector meson photo production. The connection between the irreducible tensor amplitudes introduced here and the well known CGLN amplitudes for photo production of pseudoscalar mesons is also established.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general irreducible tensor formalism for photo production of mesons with arbitrary spin-parity \(s^{\pi}\), claiming that all spin observables can be expressed in terms of bilinear irreducible tensors of rank 0 to \(2(s+1)\), with the number of independent irreducible tensor amplitudes being exactly \(4(2s+1)\), a result that generalizes the known numbers for pseudoscalar (\(s=0\)) and vector (\(s=1\)) meson cases, but the formalism is purely theoretical and does not provide numerical fits to data.

## Method

The authors employ irreducible tensor operator techniques to expand the reaction amplitude for photo production of mesons with arbitrary spin \(s\) on a nucleon, expressing it in terms of channel spin \(s_f\) and irreducible tensor amplitudes of rank \(\lambda\). They derive a compact formula for these amplitudes in terms of electric and magnetic multipole amplitudes \({\mathcal E}^j_{ls_f;L}\) and \({\mathcal M}^j_{ls_f;L}\) using partial wave expansions, Racah algebra, and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, and then construct bilinear irreducible tensors \(B^{\Lambda}_{m_{\Lambda}}\) of rank \(\Lambda\) from which all spin observables, including beam analyzing powers and differential cross-section, are obtained.

## Key numbers

* The number of independent irreducible tensor amplitudes is \(4(2s+1)\); for pseudoscalar mesons (\(s=0\)) this gives 4 amplitudes, and for vector mesons (\(s=1\)) it gives 12 amplitudes.
* For electro production, the total number of independent amplitudes is \(6(2s+1)\).
* The total number of irreducible tensor amplitudes before applying symmetry relations is \(N_{tot} = 8(2s+1)\).
* The widths of mesons mentioned: \(\rho\) has width 150.2 MeV, \(\omega\) has width 8.44 MeV, \(\varphi\) has width 4.458 MeV, \(\eta\) has width \(1.18 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV, and \(\eta'\) has width 0.2 MeV.
* The exotic baryon state mass is \(M = 1555 \pm 10\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CGLN formalism of Chew, Goldberger, Low, and Nambu (1957) for pseudoscalar meson photo production and the helicity amplitude formalism of Schilling, Seyboth, and Wolf (1970) for vector meson photo production to mesons of arbitrary spin-parity. It provides a unified theoretical framework that can be applied to photo production of higher-spin mesons such as \(f_2(1270)\), \(a_2(1320)\), \(\pi_2(1670)\), \(\omega_3(1670)\), \(\rho_3(1690)\), \(a_4(2040)\), and \(f_4(2050)\), and enables future analysis of spin observables from experiments at facilities like JLab, ELSA, ESRF, MAMI, and Spring8.
