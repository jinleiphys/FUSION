# Dynamical Screening Effects on Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> A moving ion in plasma creates a deformed electric potential depending on the ion velocity, which leads to the distinct screening effect compared to the standard static Salpeter formula. In this paper, adopting the test charge method, we explore the dynamical screening effects on big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN). We find that the high temperature in the early universe causes the ion velocity to be faster than the solar condition so that the electric potential is effectively polarized. However, the low density of background plasma components significantly suppresses the dynamical screening effects on thermonuclear reaction rates during the BBN epoch. We compare our results with several thermonuclear reaction rates for solar fusion considering the dynamical screening effects. Also, we discuss the additional plasma properties in other astrophysical sites for the possible expansion from the present calculation in the future.

## Key claim

The authors find that while the high temperature in the early universe causes ion velocities to be faster than in solar conditions, the low density of background plasma components significantly suppresses dynamical screening effects on thermonuclear reaction rates during the BBN epoch, contrary to the expectation that high temperature alone would produce large effects.

## Method

The authors adopt the test charge method to derive the dielectric permittivity for a moving ion in the BBN plasma, considering electrons, positrons, protons, and helium-4 as plasma components with Fermi-Dirac distributions for electrons and positrons. They calculate the deformed Coulomb potential for moving ions and then compute the enhancement factor for thermonuclear reaction rates by integrating over the center-of-mass energy and angle between relative velocity and center-of-mass velocity. The final primordial abundances are estimated using a linear approximation with sensitivity coefficients from Coc (2010).

## Key numbers

- The dynamical screening effect changes the thermonuclear reaction rate of $^2$H(p,$\gamma$)$^3$He by the order of $\lesssim 10^{-7}$, which is within the uncertainty from LUNA experiments.
- The fractional difference in D/H between dynamical screening and standard BBN is $-4.29 \times 10^{-8}$.
- The fractional difference in $^7$Li/H between dynamical screening and standard BBN is $+11.7 \times 10^{-8}$.
- The fractional difference in $^3$He/H between dynamical screening and standard BBN is $-3.94 \times 10^{-8}$.
- For the solar condition ($\rho=1.6 \times 10^2$ g/cm$^3$, $T=1.25 \times 10^7$ K), the dynamical enhancement factor $f_s(E_G)$ for the $p$-$p$ reaction is 1.0262, which is 0.9889 times the static Salpeter enhancement factor.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical screening formalism of Carraro et al. (1988) from solar fusion to the BBN environment, where previous studies using the static Salpeter formula (Wagoner, 2011) and relativistic Thomas-Fermi corrections (Famiano, 2016) found negligible screening effects. The authors show that dynamical screening produces even smaller effects than static screening in BBN, contrary to the solar case where dynamical screening can produce noticeable corrections. The results enable future studies of dynamical screening in other astrophysical environments such as presupernova stars, collapsar jets, and Type Ia supernovae, where the plasma conditions may yield more significant dynamical screening effects.
