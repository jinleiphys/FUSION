# Alternative sets of hyperspherical harmonics: Satisfying cusp conditions through frame transformations

> By extending the concept of Euler-angle rotations to more than three dimensions, we develop the systematics under rotations in higher-dimensional space for a novel set of hyperspherical harmonics. Applying this formalism, we determine all pairwise Coulomb interactions in a few-body system without recourse to multipole expansions. Our approach combines the advantages of relative coordinates with those of the hyperspherical description. In the present method, each Coulomb matrix element reduces to the ``1/r'' form familiar from the two-body problem. Consequently, our calculation accounts for all the cusps in the wave function whenever an interparticle separation vanishes. Unlike a truncated multipole expansion, the calculation presented here is exact. Following the systematic development of the procedure for an arbitrary number of particles, we demonstrate it explicitly with the simplest nontrivial example, the three-body system.

## Key claim

By extending Euler-angle rotations to more than three dimensions, the authors develop a novel set of hyperspherical harmonics that allow exact calculation of all pairwise Coulomb interactions in a few-body system without multipole expansions, with the caveat that the method is demonstrated explicitly only for the three-body system, while the systematic development is presented for an arbitrary number of particles.

## Method

The authors construct hyperspherical harmonics defined through their behavior under rotation operators in (3N-3)-dimensional space, using ladder operators and a vector of quantum numbers to label degenerate harmonics. They transform between different Jacobi trees (coordinate systems) by applying sequences of elementary two-dimensional kinematic rotations, where each Coulomb interaction term is calculated in the Jacobi tree where the corresponding inter-particle separation appears as a primary coordinate. The rotation matrix elements for these transformations are expressed in terms of generalized Wigner d-symbols, with the rotation angle determined by the masses of the particle complexes involved.

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical harmonics formalism developed by Feshbach (1950s), Delves, Smirnov, Macek, Lin, and others, providing a more flexible set of basis functions that are frame-independent and suitable for transformation between different coordinate systems. The approach addresses the limitation of conventional multipole expansions (used in independent-particle models) which can only account for cusps arising from vanishing separations involving a reference particle, not those between other particle pairs. The method enables exact calculation of Coulomb matrix elements in few-body systems without the convergence problems associated with truncated multipole expansions, and the authors note that the resulting harmonics have applicability beyond the specific context of charged-particle Hamiltonians.
