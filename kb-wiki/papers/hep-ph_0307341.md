# Diquarks and Exotic Spectroscopy

> We propose that the recently discovered Θbaryon is a bound state of four quarks and an antiquark, containing two highly correlated ud-pairs. If so, the Θbaryon has positive parity, and it lies in an near-ideally mixed SU(3)_{f} \mathbf{\bar{10}}_{f} oplus \mathbf{8}_{f}. The Roper resonance and the P_{11}(1710) fit naturally into this classification. We predict an isospin 3/2 multiplet of Ξ's (S=-2) with J^Π=\half^{+} around 1750 MeV. A search for manifestly exotic Ξ^{+} and Ξ^{--} in this mass range could provide a sharp test of our proposal. We predict that charm and bottom analogues of the Θbaryon are stable against strong decays.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the recently discovered Θ⁺ baryon is a bound state of four quarks and an antiquark containing two highly correlated spin-zero ud-diquarks, which gives it positive parity and places it in a near-ideally mixed SU(3)ₓ **10**ₓ ⊕ **8**ₓ multiplet; this interpretation is conditional on the assumption that the two diquarks occupy a spatially antisymmetric wavefunction, which is required for identical diquarks like [ud]².

## Method

The authors construct a correlated quark model where four quarks bind into two spin-zero, color and flavor **3̄** diquarks (treated as composite bosons), which then combine with an antiquark into a color singlet. They use a schematic Hamiltonian with SU(3)ₓ breaking from strange quark mass and an additional binding energy difference between diquarks containing strange quarks, parameterized by α = ¾(M_Λ − M_Σ) ≈ 60 MeV per strange quark, to estimate masses of the resulting **8**ₓ ⊕ **10**ₓ states. They compare their mass hierarchy to the chiral soliton model and identify specific known resonances (Roper N(1440), N(1710), Λ(1600), Σ(1660)) as candidates for the predicted states.

## Key numbers

- The Θ⁺(1540) mass fixes M₀ + m_s ≈ 1540 MeV.
- The Roper resonance N(1440) P₁₁ is identified with the lightest state in the multiplet, fixing M₀ ≈ 1440 MeV.
- The predicted Σ and Λ hyperon resonances have mass ≈ M₀ + m_s + α ≈ 1600 MeV.
- The N_s state with hidden strangeness has estimated mass ≈ M₀ + 2m_s + α ≈ 1700 MeV, identified with N(1710) P₁₁.
- The Σ_s state with hidden strangeness has estimated mass ≈ M₀ + 3m_s + 2α ≈ 1850 MeV.
- The Ξ cascade resonances (including exotic Ξ⁺ and Ξ⁻⁻) have estimated mass ≈ M₀ + 2m_s + 2α ≈ 1750 MeV.
- The Θ⁰_c (charm analog) is estimated at 2710 MeV, which is 100 MeV below threshold for strong decay to pD⁻.
- The Θ⁺_b (bottom analog) is estimated at 6050 ± 10 MeV, which is 165 ± 10 MeV below threshold for strong decays to pB⁰ and nB⁺.
- The Θ⁺ lies about 100 MeV above K⁺n threshold at center-of-mass momentum k = 270 MeV.
- For a simple square well potential of range 1 fermi, a P-wave resonance 100 MeV above threshold has width above 175 MeV; producing a width of order 10 MeV requires a range of about 0.05 fermi.

## Context

This work extends the diquark correlation concept previously applied to light scalar mesons by Jaffe (1976) and to baryon spectroscopy by Anselmino et al. (1993), and contrasts with the chiral soliton model predictions of a Θ⁺ near 1540 MeV by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and Praszalowicz (1987). The paper provides a framework for understanding the narrow width of the Θ⁺ and predicts light charge-exotic Ξ states that distinguish the correlated diquark picture from the chiral soliton model; it also enables lattice gauge theory tests and suggests that charm and bottom analogs of the Θ⁺ may be stable against strong decays, with the NA49 Collaboration reporting evidence for an exotic Ξ⁻⁻ at a mass about 100 MeV above the authors' rough estimate after submission.
