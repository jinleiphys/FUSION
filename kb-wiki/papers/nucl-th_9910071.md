# Electromagnetic response of light nuclei

> We show examples of neutron capture, photo dissociation and Coulomb dissociation processes, relevant for studying nuclear structure properties of some light nuclei. In the case of the neutron capture, we show how interference effects among the direct and resonance processes can be accounted for. The same interference effect is shown to play an important role in the photo dissociation of 9Be, for excitation energies just above the neutron emission threshold. Finally, the Coulomb dissociation of radioactive projectiles is shown to provides basic structure information on neutron rich exotic systems. The example of the 19C halo structure is shown.

# Wiki Page: Electromagnetic Response of Light Nuclei

## Key claim
Interference effects between direct radiative capture (DRC) and resonance processes can be accounted for in neutron capture, photodissociation, and Coulomb dissociation reactions, and these interference effects play an important role in the photodissociation of 9Be for excitation energies just above the neutron emission threshold. The Coulomb dissociation of radioactive projectiles provides basic structure information on neutron-rich exotic systems, as demonstrated for the 19C halo structure.

## Method
The authors formulate the DRC model using wave functions for continuum and bound states, with radial wave functions obtained by solving scattering and bound-state problems in a Woods-Saxon potential. For neutron capture, the cross section is expressed as a sum of DRC, Breit-Wigner resonance, and interference terms, with the interference term containing a cosine factor dependent on the resonance phase shift. For Coulomb dissociation, the cross section distribution is related to the photo-nuclear cross section via virtual-photon numbers in the semiclassical limit of Coulomb trajectories.

## Key numbers
- 16O(n,gamma)17O reaction: p-wave resonance at 434 KeV with neutron width Gamma_n = 45 KeV; partial gamma-ray widths Gamma_gamma0 = Gamma_gamma1 = 0.5 eV; total radiative width Gamma_gamma = 1.0 eV (40% smaller than the BNL compiled value)
- 9Be(gamma,n)8Be reaction: resonance parameters E_r = 73.4 KeV, Gamma_n = 255 KeV, Gamma_gamma = 0.53 eV; spectroscopic factor S = 0.5 from shell-model calculation using Cohen-Kurath residual interaction
- 19C Coulomb dissociation: incident energy E_i = 67 A MeV; neutron separation energy S_n = 0.53 +/- 0.13 MeV; spectroscopic factor S = 0.67 for the |18C(0+) x nu(2s1/2);1/2+> configuration
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: r0 = 1.236 fm, d = 0.62 fm, V_so = 7.5 MeV; well depths V0 = 52.9 MeV (ground state) and V0 = 54.5 MeV (first excited state) for 17O

## Context
This work extends the DRC model with interference effects previously formulated by Mengoni, Otsuka, and Ishihara (1995) to include resonance contributions, and applies it to neutron capture in 16O, photodissociation of 9Be, and Coulomb dissociation of 19C. The 9Be photodissociation study is motivated by its time-reversal process (neutron capture by 8Be), which plays a crucial role in alpha-induced recombination during Type II supernovae as discussed by Goerres et al. (1995). The Coulomb dissociation results for 19C, based on a RIKEN experiment by Nakamura et al. (1999), establish the ground state spin/parity as 1/2+ and determine the neutron separation energy, enabling further studies of halo structure in neutron-rich systems.
