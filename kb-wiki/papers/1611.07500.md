# On the Origin of the Elliptic Flow and its Dependence on the Equation of State in Heavy Ion Reactions at Intermediate Energies

> Recently it has been discovered that the elliptic flow, v2, of composite charged particles emitted at midrapidity in Heavy-Ion collisions at intermediate energies shows the strongest sensitivity to the Nuclear Equation of State (EoS) which has been observed up to now within a microscopic model. This dependence on the nuclear EoS is predicted by Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) calculations [1] which show as well that the absorption or rescattering of in-plane emitted particles by the spectator matter is not the main reason for the EoS dependence of the elliptic flow at mid-rapidity but different density gradients (and therefore different forces) in the direction of the impact parameter (x-direction) as compared to the direction perpendicular to the reaction plan (y-direction), caused by the presence of the spectator matter. The stronger density gradient in y-direction accelerates the particles more and creates therefore a negative v2. When using a soft momentum dependent EoS, the QMD calculations reproduce the experimental results.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of composite charged particles at midrapidity in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies shows the strongest sensitivity to the nuclear Equation of State (EoS) observed so far within a microscopic model, but this dependence originates from different density gradients in the impact-parameter direction versus the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane, not from absorption or rescattering of in-plane emitted particles by spectator matter as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate semi-central Au+Au collisions at 0.6 and 1.5 AGeV with an impact parameter of 6 fm. They separately tracked momentum transfers due to nucleon-nucleon collisions and due to the mean-field potential, and decomposed the elliptic flow into contributions from protons at different transverse distances from the collision center at the passing time.

## Key numbers

- The passing time for Au+Au collisions is 22.9 fm/c at 0.6 AGeV and 16.9 fm/c at 1.5 AGeV.
- The compression modulus \(K\) is 200 MeV for the soft EoS (SM) and 376 MeV for the hard EoS (HM).
- At 0.6 AGeV with the soft EoS, the mean-field contribution to the out-of-plane momentum excess \(\Delta P_y^o - \Delta P_x^o\) is only about 30% of the total; at 1.5 AGeV with the hard EoS, the mean-field contribution dominates.
- The mean-field force for the hard EoS is nearly double that for the soft EoS.
- The number of collisions suffered by selected protons is almost unchanged by the choice of EoS.

## Context

This work extends the findings of the FOPI collaboration (FOPI, 2011) and earlier QMD calculations (Fevre, 2015) that showed elliptic flow at intermediate energies has a larger dependence on the nuclear EoS than kaon production (Hartnack, 2005). The paper contradicts the earlier interpretation by Danielewicz (2002) that negative \(v_2\) at low energies is primarily due to spectator shadowing blocking in-plane emission. The results enable using elliptic flow measurements at beam energies between 0.2 and 2.0 AGeV as a quantitative probe of the nuclear EoS, particularly through the mean-field contribution.
