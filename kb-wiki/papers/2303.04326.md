# A possibility of existence of a pseudovector-type quark-antiquark condensate in the quark matter and Nambu-Goldstone modes on that condensate in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> A possibility of a pseudovector-type quark-antiquark condensed phase, which leads to a quark spin polarized phase, in the quark matter is investigated taking account of the vacuum effects leading to the chiral symmetry breaking by using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Also, possible Nambu-Goldstone modes on the pseudovector-type quark-antiquark condensate and the tensortype quark-antiquark condensate, which also leads to the quark spin polarized phase, are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a pseudovector-type quark-antiquark condensate (leading to quark spin polarization) can exist in quark matter at finite density, including the previously neglected vacuum effects that drive chiral symmetry breaking. The authors find that such a condensate can appear, but only when the pseudovector coupling strength exceeds a threshold (around \(G_{pv} \gtrsim 1.2 G_S\)), and that a first-order phase transition from the chiral condensed phase to the pseudovector condensed phase occurs at a critical quark chemical potential.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and pseudovector interactions, introducing auxiliary fields via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. They derive gap equations for the chiral condensate \(\sigma_s\) and the pseudovector condensate \(\sigma_{pv}\) (which is proportional to the quark spin polarization \(\langle \psi^\dagger \Sigma_3 \tau_3 \psi \rangle\)), including both vacuum and finite-density contributions via the quark chemical potential \(\mu\). The effective potential is evaluated numerically with a three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 643\) MeV and scalar coupling \(G_S = 5.20\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (fixed to reproduce \(f_\pi = 92\) MeV and the constituent quark mass \(M = 313\) MeV), treating the pseudovector coupling \(G_{pv}\) as a free parameter. The Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes on the condensates are then analyzed by computing the two-point vertex functions (inverse meson propagators) at zero momentum.

## Key numbers

* The three-momentum cutoff is set to \(\Lambda = 643\) MeV and the scalar coupling constant to \(G_S = 5.20\) GeV\(^{-2}\), reproducing the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92\) MeV and the quark mass \(M = 313\) MeV.
* For pseudovector coupling \(G_{pv} = 1.1 G_S\), the effective potential has a minimum at \(\sigma_{pv} = 0\) for all chemical potentials; for \(G_{pv} = 1.2 G_S\) and above, a nontrivial pseudovector condensate \(\sigma_{pv} \neq 0\) appears at finite \(\mu\).
* At \(G_{pv} = 1.2 G_S\), the first-order phase transition from the chiral condensed phase (\(\sigma_s \neq 0, \sigma_{pv} = 0\)) to the pseudovector condensed phase (\(\sigma_s = 0, \sigma_{pv} \neq 0\)) occurs around \(\mu \approx 312\) MeV.
* At \(G_{pv} = 1.3 G_S\), the absolute minimum of the effective potential gives nonzero \(\sigma_{pv}\) even at \(\mu = 0\), which the authors deem unphysical because it would imply quark spin polarization in vacuum.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin-polarized quark matter in the NJL model (Maedan, Morimoto et al.) by including the vacuum contributions to the gap equations, which were often neglected. It also builds on earlier work by Tsue (2015) on tensor-type condensates and by Matsuoka (2018) on magnetic moments in hybrid stars. The results indicate that a pseudovector condensate can coexist with the chiral condensate in a certain parameter region, leading to three NG modes (one pion and two axial-vector mesons) plus two additional massless modes arising from mixing between pions and rho mesons. This analysis enables further study of the QCD phase diagram at high density and low temperature, particularly regarding spin-polarized phases in compact stars.
