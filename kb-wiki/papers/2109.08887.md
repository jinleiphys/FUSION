# Kinetic theory of overpopulated gluon systems with inelastic processes

> In this work, the role of inelastic processes in the formation of a transient Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) is investigated based on kinetic theory. We calculate the condensation rate for an overpopulated gluon system which is assumed to be in thermal equilibrium and with the presence of a BEC. The matrix elements of the inelastic processes are chosen as the isotropic one and the gluons are considered to have a finite mass. Our calculations indicate that the inelastic processes can hinder the formation of a BEC since the negatively infinite net condensation rate can destroy any BEC instantly.

## Key claim

Inelastic processes hinder the formation of a transient Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) in an overpopulated gluon system because the net condensation rate is negatively infinite when using isotropic matrix elements and a finite gluon mass, which would destroy any BEC instantly; this result is derived for a system assumed to be in thermal equilibrium with a BEC already present, not for a dynamical formation scenario.

## Method

The net condensation rate for an overpopulated gluon system in thermal equilibrium and with a BEC present was calculated analytically using kinetic theory. The Boltzmann equation was separated into contributions from elastic processes (2-to-2) and three types of inelastic processes (2-to-3 and 3-to-2) involving condensate particles, with isotropic matrix elements and a finite gluon mass \(m\). The rate equations were derived by integrating the Boltzmann equation for condensate particles over momenta, and the integrals were evaluated analytically for the isotropic matrix element case.

## Key numbers

- For a massive gluon system with isotropic matrix elements, the net condensation rate from the process \(g+c+c \longleftrightarrow g+g\) is negatively infinite, scaling as \(A \ln^2(0) + B \ln(0) + C\) where the coefficient \(A\) is negative.
- The process \(g+c \longleftrightarrow g+g+g\) gives a positively infinite condensation rate, scaling as \(B_1 \ln(0) + C_1\) with \(B_1\) negative.
- The process \(g+g+c \longleftrightarrow g+g\) gives a negatively finite condensation rate, scaling as \(C_2\) with \(C_2\) negative.
- For a massless gluon system (\(m=0\)), the net condensation rates for all three inelastic processes are zero, meaning inelastic processes cannot destroy a massless gluon BEC.
- The elastic processes give a net condensation rate of zero at equilibrium for both massive and massless cases.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of BEC formation in overpopulated gluon systems beyond purely elastic collisions (Blaizot 2011, Berges 2012, Zhou 2017, Chen 2018) by including inelastic processes. It confirms the numerical results of Lenkiewicz (2019) through an analytical calculation using the same isotropic matrix elements and massive gluons. The finding that inelastic processes hinder BEC formation contradicts the earlier suggestion by Huang (2013) that inelastic processes can catalyze BEC onset, but agrees with the analysis of Blaizot (2016) and the 2PI study of Tsutsui (2017). The work enables future studies of longitudinally expanding systems more relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
