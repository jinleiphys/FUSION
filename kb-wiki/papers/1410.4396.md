# Blurred path-spin entanglement in Stern-Gerlach apparatus: interplay between magnetic inhomogeneity and Larmor precession

> We argue that the non-adiabatic evolution of spin states in Stern-Gerlach apparatus can blur the manifestation of path spin entanglement. This fact introduces a contradiction to the usual perception of spin measurement even in a formally ideal situation. Through quantitative calculation, we have pointed out the specific reason behind the breakdown of adiabatic evolution to be the spatial inhomogeneity of applied magnetic field. The angle $θ$ between the $z$ component of magnetic moment of the particle and applied magnetic field is also a determining factor for the category of evolution of spin states. Adiabaticity always can be restored by choosing a sufficiently small value of $θ$. Considering azimuthal inhomogeneity, we have examined the nature of path spin entanglement in the context of Stern-Gerlach experiment.

# Blurred path-spin entanglement in Stern-Gerlach apparatus: interplay between magnetic inhomogeneity and Larmor precession

## Key claim

The authors argue that non-adiabatic evolution of spin states in a Stern-Gerlach apparatus can blur path-spin entanglement even under formally and operationally ideal conditions (where spatial wavefunction overlap is zero), contradicting the usual assumption that a specific path corresponds to a particular spin. The breakdown of adiabaticity arises from spatial inhomogeneity of the magnetic field and depends critically on the angle θ between the magnetic moment and the applied field; adiabaticity can always be restored by choosing a sufficiently small θ.

## Method

The authors analyze the time evolution of spin-1/2 particles in an azimuthally inhomogeneous magnetic field by working in the particle rest frame, where the spatial variation of the static field becomes an explicit time dependence in the interaction Hamiltonian. They solve the Schrödinger equation for the spin states using a first-order Taylor expansion of the azimuthal angle Φ(τ), obtaining the coefficients for transition and survival probabilities in the instantaneous eigenbasis. The final path-spin entangled state is constructed by combining the evolved spin states with the spatial wavefunctions φ₊ and φ₋, assuming formal and operational idealness (I=0, E=0) is maintained.

## Key numbers

- The adiabatic condition is given by |(ω/ω₀) sin θ| << 1, where ω = dΦ/dτ|_(τ=0) is the rate of change of the azimuthal angle and ω₀ is the Larmor frequency.
- The transition probability from up spin to down spin in the upward direction (and vice versa in the downward direction) is P_tr = |α₋|² = |β₊|² = (ω² sin²θ / ω̄²) sin²(ω̄t/2), where ω̄ = √(ω₀² + ω² − 2ω₀ω cosθ).
- At time t = (2n+1)π/ω̄ with cosθ = ω₀/ω, the survival probabilities vanish (α₊ = β₋ = 0), meaning up spins appear only in the downward direction and down spins only in the upward direction.
- In the strong-field limit ω₀ >> ω, the conventional result is recovered: α₊ = e^(−iω₀t/2), α₋ = 0, β₋ = e^(iω₀t/2), β₊ = 0.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Stern-Gerlach experiments beyond the standard adiabatic treatment found in textbooks and in earlier work by Platt (1992) and Oliveira (2006), which assumed a time-averaged magnetic field with fixed direction. The authors show that the azimuthal inhomogeneity of the magnetic field, neglected in those studies, can cause non-adiabatic spin evolution even when the spatial wavefunctions are perfectly separated. This finding has implications for quantum information devices that use Stern-Gerlach apparatuses as beam splitters, as the effect could serve as a spin-flipping tool. The authors note that a more general treatment beyond the azimuthally inhomogeneous case is left for future work (Dutta 2014).
