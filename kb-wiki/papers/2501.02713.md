# Axial-Vector and Tensor Spin Polarization and Chiral Restoration in Quark Matter

> We study spontaneous spin-polarizations of quark matter with flavour $SU(2)$ symmetry at zero temperature in the NJL model. In a relativistic framework, there are two types of the spin-spin interactions: axial-vector (AV) and tensor (T), which accordingly give rise to different types of spin-polarized materials. When the spin-spin interaction is sufficiently strong, the spin-polarized phase emerges within a specific density region. As the spin-spin interaction becomes stronger, this phase extends over higher density region beyond the critical density of chiral restoration in normal quark matter. We show that the spin-polarized phase leads to another kind of the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking phase.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in quark matter described by the NJL model, tensor-type spin polarization can persist even when the dynamical quark mass vanishes (chirally restored phase), whereas axial-vector-type spin polarization cannot exist in that regime; this means the spin-polarized phase can lead to a distinct form of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking beyond the usual chiral restoration transition.

## Method
The authors employ the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with flavor SU(2) symmetry at zero temperature, treating axial-vector (AV) and tensor (T) spin-spin interactions separately under the mean-field approximation. They compute the quark propagator, thermodynamic potential, and the self-consistent equations for the dynamical quark mass and the spin mean fields using proper time regularization, then solve for the spin-polarized phases as functions of baryon density.

## Key numbers
- The critical density of chiral restoration in normal quark matter, denoted as \(\rho_c^{\rm NQ}\), is given by \(\rho_c^{\rm NQ} = N_d p_c^3 / 3\pi^2\) with \(N_d = 6\) and \(p_c\) determined from \(1 - (N_d/4\pi^2) G_s (\Lambda^2 - 2p_c^2) = 0\).
- The vacuum dynamical quark mass \(M_0\) satisfies \(1 - (N_d/4\pi^2) G_s \Lambda^2 F_2(M_0^2/\Lambda^2) = 0\), where \(F_2(x) = x \int_x^\infty d\tau \, \tau^{-2} e^{-\tau}\).
- The maximum surface magnetic field strength for a compact star is estimated as \(B_{\rm max} = (8\pi/3) f_Q \mu_q \rho_0\), yielding \(B_{\rm max} \sim O(10^{15-17})\) G for \(f_Q = 1\).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Maruyama and Tatsumi (2000) on relativistic spin operators and by Tsue et al. (2012) who showed tensor-type spin polarization can appear beyond the chiral restoration density. It contrasts with the axial-vector spin polarization studied by Maedan (2007) and by Nakano et al. (2003), which requires non-zero dynamical quark mass. The results enable further investigation of the coexistence of spin polarization with color superconductivity, as explored by Tsue et al. (2014) and Matsuoka et al. (2016), and with inhomogeneous chiral condensates such as the dual chiral density wave studied by Nickerson and Tatsumi (2005).
