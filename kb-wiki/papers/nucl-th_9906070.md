# Truncation of Large Shell-Model Eigenproblems by Model Space Partitioning

> A method for solving the shell-model eigenproblem in a severely truncated space, spanned by properly selected correlated states obtained by partitioning the full configuration space, is proposed. The method describes in a practically exact way the low energy spectroscopic properties of nuclei, as exemplified in schematic models. The applicability of the method to heavy nuclei as well in contexts different from the nuclear shell model is stressed

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method for solving the nuclear shell-model eigenproblem in a severely truncated space that is free of the overcompleteness and spurious admixture problems common to other correlated-basis approaches, and claims that an energy truncation criterion works well because a significant part of the interaction effect is already included in the correlated basis states.

## Method

The full configuration space is partitioned into two orthogonal subspaces (P1 and P2), and the eigenvalue problems for all allowed particle numbers are solved separately within each partition to obtain correlated eigenstates. A product basis is then formed from these correlated subsystem eigenstates, and the full Hamiltonian (including the inter-partition interaction H12) is diagonalized in this basis after truncating by an energy cutoff E* that retains only basis states whose summed subsystem energies lie below a chosen threshold.

## Key numbers

- For a model space of ten single-particle levels with N = 30 nucleons and constant pairing strength G = 0.16 MeV, practically exact ground-state energies were obtained with matrices of order 150, while the full space basis dimension was 59,702.
- For a model space of 32 equispaced doubly-degenerate levels with N = 32 particles, level spacing 1 MeV, and pairing constant G = 0.345 MeV, the best computed energies differed from exact values by less than 0.1%, with excitation energies 3.060 and 4.900 MeV being practically exact, while the full basis dimension was 6.01 × 10^8.
- The ground-state energy converged to 90.241 MeV (truncation to 150 states) from a full-space value of 90.241 MeV for the ten-level model.
- The ground-state energy converged to 263.356 MeV (truncation to 3999 states) compared to the exact value of 263.171 MeV for the 32-level model.

## Context

This work extends the chain-calculation method (Covello et al., 1995) and addresses the overcompleteness problems found in the multi-step shell model (Liotta, 1982) and the broken-pair approximation (Allaart et al., 1988). The method enables almost exact shell-model calculations for heavy nuclei where full diagonalization is prohibitive, and the authors note it can be applied to systems of neutrons and protons separately and to excitations outside a major shell, as well as to ultra-small superconducting grains studied by Mastellone et al. (1998) and Braun and von Delft (1998).
