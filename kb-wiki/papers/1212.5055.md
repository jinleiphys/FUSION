# Scattering in the pion-nucleon negative parity channel in lattice QCD

> We study the coupled pion-nucleon system (negative parity, isospin 1/2) based on a lattice QCD simulation for nf=2 mass degenerate light quarks. Both, standard 3-quarks baryon operators as well as meson-baryon (4+1)-quark operators are included. This is an exploratory study for just one lattice size and lattice spacing and at a pion mass of 266 MeV. Using the distillation method and variational analysis we determine energy levels of the lowest eigenstates. Comparison with the results of simple 3-quark correlation studies exhibits drastic differences and a new level appears. A clearer picture of the negative parity nucleon spectrum emerges. For the parameters of the simulation we may assume elastic s-wave scattering and can derive values of the phase shift.

# Scattering in the pion-nucleon negative parity channel in lattice QCD

## Key claim

Including explicit meson-baryon (4+1)-quark operators alongside standard 3-quark baryon operators in lattice QCD calculations of the negative parity nucleon channel reveals a new energy level and significantly shifts the spectrum compared to analyses using only 3-quark interpolators, but this is an exploratory study at a single lattice size, lattice spacing, and pion mass of 266 MeV, so systematic uncertainties from volume, spacing, and quark mass are not yet controlled.

## Method

The authors performed a lattice QCD simulation with nf=2 mass-degenerate dynamical quarks (improved Wilson-Clover action) on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with spatial extent 1.98 fm and pion mass 266 MeV. They constructed a 9x9 correlation matrix using three 3-quark nucleon interpolators (each with two smearing levels, Nv=32 and Nv=64) and three pion-nucleon interpolators (with Nv=32), applied the distillation method to compute all required Wick contractions including disconnected diagrams, and extracted energy levels via the variational method with correlated exponential fits.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 266(3)(3) MeV, with lattice value a*m_pi = 0.1673(2)
- Nucleon (positive parity) ground state mass: m_N = 1068(6) MeV, with lattice value a*m_N = 0.672(4)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1239(13) fm, spatial extent L = 1.98 fm, L*m_pi ≈ 2.68
- With only 3-quark interpolators: lowest two energy levels at 1.359(43) GeV and 1.709(29) GeV
- With pion-nucleon interpolators included: three lowest levels at 1.272(8) GeV, 1.662(30) GeV, and 1.792(29) GeV
- Phase shift values derived from Luscher analysis: delta = 68(59)i degrees (level 1, below threshold), 89(9) degrees (level 2), 47(10) degrees (level 3)
- Estimated resonance mass from linear fit to rho_0: m_R = 1.678(99) GeV
- Estimated scattering length: a_0 ≈ 5.3(±1.4) GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of baryon resonances that previously used only 3-quark interpolators (Bulava 2010, Engel 2010, Engel 2012, Mahbub 2012), which found two low-lying negative parity levels but with the lower one tending below the experimental N*(1535). The approach follows successful meson resonance studies that included explicit hadron-hadron operators (Lang 2011, Lang 2012, Mohler 2012). The results enable future work with moving frames, different volumes, and inclusion of inelastic channels (N-eta, Lambda-K) to map the full resonance region and compare with continuum models and unitarized chiral perturbation theory.
