# Status of experimental knowledge on the unbound nucleus $^{13}$Be

> The structure of the unbound nucleus $^{13}$Be is important to understanding the Borromean, two-neutron halo nucleus $^{14}$Be. The experimental studies conducted over the last four decades are reviewed in the context of the beryllium chain of isotopes and some significant theoretical studies. One focus of this paper is the comparison of new data from a $^{12}$Be(d,p) reaction in inverse kinematics, which was analyzed using GEANT4 simulations and a Bayesian fitting procedure, with previous measurements. Two possible scenarios to explain the strength below 1~MeV above the neutron separation energy were proposed in that study: a single $p$-wave resonance, or a mixture of an $s$-wave virtual state with a weaker either $p$-wave or $d$-wave resonance. Comparisons of recent invariant mass and the (d,p) experiments show good agreement between the transfer measurement and the two most recent high-energy nucleon removal measurements.

## Key claim

The paper reviews four decades of experimental studies on the unbound nucleus $^{13}$Be and presents a new analysis of a $^{12}$Be(d,p) transfer reaction, concluding that the low-lying structure remains disputed. The best fit to the new data favors a single narrow $p$-wave resonance at 0.55 MeV above the neutron threshold, but a mixture dominated by an $s$-wave virtual state with a weaker $p$- or $d$-wave resonance cannot be ruled out.

## Method

The authors review experimental studies of $^{13}$Be using both missing-mass techniques (transfer reactions) and invariant-mass techniques (knockout, breakup, and Coulomb dissociation). They focus on a new $^{12}$Be(d,p) experiment performed at 9.5 AMeV using the IRIS solid deuterium target system at ISAC-II, analyzed with GEANT4 simulations and a Bayesian fitting procedure. The new data are compared with resonance parameters from recent invariant-mass measurements by feeding those parameters into the same GEANT4 simulation and fitting the relative intensities.

## Key numbers

- The $^{12}$Be(d,p) experiment used a beam energy of 9.5 AMeV.
- The best single-resonance fit gave a $p$-wave resonance at 0.55 MeV above the neutron threshold with width $\Gamma = 0.11$ MeV, and a $d$-wave ($5/2^+$) resonance at 2.22 MeV.
- The mixed-case fit gave $s$-wave contribution $70^{+8}_{-6}\%$ with $p$-wave $30^{+6}_{-8}\%$ ($\chi^2$/NDF = 3.32), or $s$-wave $93^{+2}_{-2}\%$ with $d$-wave $7^{+2}_{-2}\%$ ($\chi^2$/NDF = 3.87).
- Using literature resonance parameters from Cor19 gave $\chi^2$/NDF = 2.62; from Rib18 gave $\chi^2$/NDF = 2.76; from Ran14 gave $\chi^2$/NDF = 3.47; from Kon10 gave $\chi^2$/NDF = 5.51.
- The $^{12}$Be(d,p) data with a single $p$-wave plus the $d$-wave resonance gave $\chi^2$/NDF = 2.02.
- The $5/2^+$ resonance is reported at energies ranging from 1.8 MeV (Ale83) to 2.56 MeV (Mar15) above the neutron threshold across different experiments.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing experimental effort on $^{13}$Be, which began with the first resonance discovery by Aleksandrov (1983) and includes key prior work by Thoennessen (2000), Simon (2007), and others. The new transfer measurement by Kovoor (2023) provides the first low-energy (d,p) data on $^{13}$Be, enabling direct comparison with invariant-mass results from Kon10, Ran14, Rib18, and Cor19. The work enables improved three-body models of the Borromean halo nucleus $^{14}$Be, where $^{13}$Be serves as the crucial $^{12}$Be+n subsystem, and tests theoretical predictions from Descouvemont (1994), Fortune (2019), and the transfer-to-continuum calculations of Casal (2020).
