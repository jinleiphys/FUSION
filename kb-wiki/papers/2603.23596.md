# A Breath of Fresh Air for Molière: Detecting Molière Scattering using Jet Substructure Observables in Oxygen Collisions

> Ultra-relativistic oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions are a promising arena in which to probe rare, large-angle, high momentum-transfer $2\rightarrow2$ Molière scatterings between energetic jet partons and quasiparticles in quark-gluon plasma (QGP). As a jet propagates through the droplet of QGP formed in the same collision, its constituents lose energy to and excite wakes in the medium, and may scatter off quark- and gluon-like quasiparticles in QGP. Using the hybrid strong/weak coupling model, we show that including Molière scatterings between jet partons and medium quasiparticles is essential to reproduce recent CMS measurements of charged-particle suppression in OO collisions with this model. We then present the first theoretical study of how jet-medium interactions modify the internal structure of jets in OO collisions. We find that Molière scatterings broaden the Soft Drop splitting angle $R_g$, enhancing the population of $R=0.4$ and $R=0.8$ jets with $R_g\gtrsim0.2$ in OO collisions relative to pp collisions. Energy-energy correlators (EECs) provide a complementary probe, exhibiting enhanced large-angle correlations within jets due to jet-induced wakes and Molière scattering. In both cases, we propose an experimental measurement where the relevant OO/pp ratio can, if enhanced above unity in future data as in our calculations, be a distinctive, model-independent, detection of hard scattering off QGP quasiparticles. We furthermore use our calculations of EECs to show how the angular scale corresponding to the deflection of jet or medium partons by Molière scattering is imprinted in the EEC for jets with radius $R_{\rm jet}\sim0.8$ in OO collisions. These results demonstrate that jet substructure measurements in OO collisions are promising avenues to probe the quasiparticles that emerge at short distances within an otherwise strongly coupled medium.

## Key claim

The authors show that including Moliere scatterings between jet partons and medium quasiparticles in the hybrid strong/weak coupling model is essential to reproduce recent CMS measurements of charged-particle suppression in oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions, but they acknowledge this evidence is "far from model-independent" because it depends on their specific modeling of nuclear parton distribution functions, parton energy loss, jet wakes, and Moliere scattering itself.

## Method

The authors use the hybrid strong/weak coupling model (Hybrid Model), which describes parton shower evolution via Pythia 8, incorporates holographically derived energy loss with a fitted parameter kappa_sc, and includes hydrodynamic wakes excited in the quark-gluon plasma. They implement elastic 2-to-2 Moliere scatterings between jet partons and thermal quark or gluon quasiparticles, requiring Mandelstam variables |t|, |u| > a m_D^2 with threshold parameter a=10, using coupling g_s=2.25, and model soft transverse momentum kicks below this threshold as Gaussian broadening with parameter K=15.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron R_AA in OO collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV: Hybrid Model calculations with Moliere scatterings included (kappa_sc = 0.335) reproduce CMS data, while calculations without Moliere scatterings (kappa_sc = 0.37) fail to describe the measurements
- Soft Drop splitting angle R_g: Moliere scatterings enhance the population of R=0.4 and R=0.8 jets with R_g greater than or approximately 0.2 in OO collisions relative to pp collisions, for jets with 40 < p_T^jet < 60 GeV using charged particles with p_T^ch track > 150 MeV and grooming parameters z_cut = 0.1, beta = 0
- Energy-energy correlator (EEC) calculations for R_jet = 0.8 jets with 40 < p_T < 80 GeV show a local maximum in the OO/pp ratio at R_L approximately 0.5 when using charged hadrons with p_T > 2 GeV, corresponding to the typical deflection angle from Moliere scattering

## Context

This work extends the hybrid strong/weak coupling model studies of Moliere scattering previously applied to PbPb collisions by Hulcher (2026) and others, and provides the first theoretical analysis of jet substructure observables in OO collisions. The authors argue that because OO collisions produce smaller QGP droplets than PbPb collisions, the contribution of Moliere scatterings to suppressing charged hadron production is larger relative to strongly coupled energy loss, making OO collisions the best arena to detect and study these scatterings. The results demonstrate that measurements of Soft Drop R_g and energy-energy correlators in OO collisions can provide model-independent signatures of hard scattering off QGP quasiparticles, a goal identified by Aprahamian (2015) as central to the field.
