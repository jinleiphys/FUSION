# Global Anomalies, Discrete Symmetries, and Hydrodynamic Effective Actions

> We derive effective actions for parity-violating fluids in both $(3+1)$ and $(2+1)$ dimensions, including those with anomalies. As a corollary we confirm the most general constitutive relations for such systems derived previously using other methods. We discuss in detail connections between parity-odd transport and underlying discrete symmetries. In (3+1) dimensions we elucidate connections between anomalous transport coefficients and global anomalies, and clarify a previous puzzle concerning transports and local gravitational anomalies.

## Key claim

The paper derives hydrodynamic effective actions for parity-violating fluids in (3+1) and (2+1) dimensions, confirming that the transport coefficients a1, a2, and a3 in the constitutive relations are associated with global anomalies (pure gauge, mixed gauge, and pure gravitational) rather than local anomalies, and that the previously puzzling relation between a2 and local mixed gravitational anomalies should be understood as a combination of a universal low-energy relation to global anomalies and a UV-dependent relation between local and global anomaly coefficients.

## Method

The authors construct closed time path (CTP) generating functionals for systems with a U(1) symmetry, using Stuckelberg fields X^mu and phi for diffeomorphisms and gauge transformations, and a scalar field beta for inverse temperature. They expand the hydrodynamic action to leading order in hbar, formulate it in physical spacetime using r-variables and a-variables, and impose invariance under spacetime diffeomorphisms, gauge transformations, and fluid spacetime diffeomorphisms to derive the most general parity-odd constitutive relations. The analysis is performed separately for (3+1)-dimensional and (2+1)-dimensional systems, with explicit computation of partition functions on S^1 x S^2 spatial manifolds to expose global anomaly transformations.

## Key numbers

* In (3+1) dimensions, the parity-odd current is J^mu_o = xi_omega omega^mu + xi_B B^mu, with xi_omega = -3 c hbar mu^2 (1 - 2/3 alpha) + 2 a1 mu T (1 - alpha) + a2 T^2 (1 - 2 alpha) - 2 a3 T^3 n0/(epsilon0 + p0) and xi_B = -3 c hbar mu (2 - alpha) + 2 a1 T (1 - alpha) - a2 T^2 n0/(epsilon0 + p0), where alpha = mu n0/(epsilon0 + p0).
* Under a large gauge transformation on S^1 x S^2, the partition function transforms as Z -> exp[8 pi^2 m a1 / q^2 + i 2 pi n a2 / q] Z for integers m, n, where q is the minimal U(1) charge.
* Under a large gauge transformation of the Kaluza-Klein U(1) when only v_i has magnetic flux on S^2, Z -> exp(-2 a3 r) Z for integer r.
* The relation a2 = -32 pi^2 lambda connects the transport coefficient a2 to the local mixed gravitational anomaly coefficient lambda, but this is not a low-energy relation.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic effective action formalism developed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015-2017) to parity-violating and anomalous fluids, confirming the constitutive relations previously derived using entropy current methods by Son and Surowka (2009) and Neiman and Oz (2010), and equilibrium partition function approaches by Banerjee, Bhattacharya, Jain, and Sharma (2012) and Jensen, Loganayagam, and Yarom (2012). The paper resolves a puzzle regarding the relation between the transport coefficient a2 and local gravitational anomalies raised by Landsteiner, Megias, and Pena-Benitez (2011-2012) and others, showing that a2 is directly tied to global mixed gravitational anomalies as suggested by Golkar, Jain, and Son (2015) and Chowdhury and David (2016). This enables systematic incorporation of hydrodynamic fluctuations in parity-violating systems and provides a framework for probing microscopic discrete symmetries and global anomalies through transport measurements.
