# Quark-Gluon Plasma Freeze-Out from a Supercooled State?

> The quark-gluon plasma, formed in the first 3 fm/c of the heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, supercooles due to nucleation and develops soon a negative pressure in the bag model. The negative pressure yields mechanical instability which may lead to a sudden timelike deflagration to a (super)heated hadron gas. The resulting freeze-out times are shorter than those of the standard nucleation scenario.

# Quark-Gluon Plasma Freeze-Out from a Supercooled State?

## Key claim
The freeze-out times estimated from HBT interferometry data at RHIC and LHC energies (8-13 fm/c and 11-16 fm/c respectively) are shorter than the characteristic nucleation time of approximately 100 fm/c, implying that the quark-gluon plasma cannot rehadronize through bubble nucleation alone and must instead undergo a sudden timelike deflagration from a mechanically unstable, supercooled state with negative pressure.

## Method
The authors extrapolate freeze-out proper-times from existing HBT data using the scaling relation R_L = t_f Δη and the charged particle rapidity density scaling with projectile mass and energy. They then compare these timescales to the homogeneous nucleation scenario of Csernai and Kapusta (1992), which predicts a bottom temperature of approximately 0.8 T_c reached at about 7 fm/c and a completion time of 50 fm/c. Using the bag model equation of state and the conservation laws across a timelike deflagration front (Taub adiabat, Rayleigh line, and Poisson adiabat), they derive conditions under which a sudden transition from supercooled QGP to (super)heated hadron gas becomes possible.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out time at CERN SPS for S + U: t_f = 4.5 - 6.5 fm/c (from R_L = 4.5 ± 0.5 fm and Δη = sqrt(T_f/m_T))
- Extrapolated freeze-out times: 6-10 fm/c at CERN SPS for Pb+Pb, 8-13 fm/c at RHIC for Au+Au, and 11-16 fm/c at LHC for Pb+Pb
- Critical temperature: T_c = 169 MeV (from nucleation calculations)
- Pressure of supercooled QGP vanishes at T = 0.98 T_c
- Supercooled QGP reaches T = 0.7-0.9 T_c ≈ 120-150 MeV
- Characteristic nucleation time: approximately 100 fm/c for a longitudinally expanding gas of gluons and massless quarks
- Completion time in nucleation scenario: t_com = 50 fm/c
- Bottom temperature in nucleation: T_b ≈ 0.8 T_c reached at t_b ≈ 7 fm/c
- Ratio of degrees of freedom r = a_Q/a_H decreases from 37/3 (n_F = 2) to 16/3 (n_F = 0) in a hot glue scenario
- For d=1 Bjorken expansion: t_c(1) = 14.3 fm/c; for d=3 expansion: t_c(3) = 5.6 fm/c (starting from t_I = 3.5 fm/c, T_I = 1.6 T_c)
- Timelike deflagration becomes possible at t = 7.3 fm/c in a model with d=3 expansion and n_F = 0
- Charged particle rapidity density at CERN SPS for central S + U: approximately 133 at midrapidity
- Scaling exponent α for rapidity density: 1.1 ≤ α ≤ 4/3, with conservative value α = 1.3 used

## Context
This work extends the nucleation calculations of Csernai and Kapusta (1992) and the bubble kinetics studies of Deka (1984) by incorporating the mechanical instability of supercooled QGP at negative pressure. The authors connect their proposed sudden rehadronization mechanism to experimental observations: the short freeze-out times inferred from HBT data (following the scaling relation of Sinyukov (1994) and the analysis of Stock (1993)), the strange antibaryon enhancement observed by the WA85 collaboration at CERN SPS, the high effective slope temperatures of approximately 210 ± 20 MeV for strange particles reported by NA35, and the observation of free hadronic masses in dilepton spectra. The timelike deflagration mechanism builds on earlier work by Gong (1993), Holme (1993), and Aram (1993) on sudden hadronization from supercooled QGP. This scenario enables a "pion-flash" with simultaneous hadronization and freeze-out, which would be signaled by vanishing difference between side and out HBT radii, free resonance masses in dilepton spectra, and a clean strangeness signature of QGP formation.
