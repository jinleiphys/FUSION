# Boundary conditions for star matter and other periodic fermionic systems

> Bulk fermionic matter, as it can be notably found in supernova matter and neutrons stars, is subject to correlations of infinite range due to the antisymmetrisation of the N-body wave function, which cannot be explicitly accounted for in a practical simulation. This problem is usually addressed in condensed matter physics by means of the so-called Twist Averaged Boundary Condition method. A different ansatz based on the localized Wannier representation has been proposed in the context of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. In this paper we work out the formal relation between the two approaches. We show that, while the two coincide when working with exact eigenstates of the N-body Hamiltonian, differences appear in the case of variational approaches, which are currently used for the description of stellar matter. Some model applications with Fermionic Molecular Dynamics are shown.

## Key claim

The Twist Averaged Boundary Condition (TABC) method and the localized Wannier representation approach, while formally equivalent when applied to exact eigenstates of the N-body Hamiltonian, yield different results when used within variational approaches such as Fermionic Molecular Dynamics, which are the methods currently employed for describing stellar matter.

## Method

The authors derive the formal relationship between the TABC method, standard in condensed matter physics, and the Wannier-based ansatz previously proposed in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. They then perform model applications using Fermionic Molecular Dynamics to compare the two boundary condition treatments for periodic fermionic systems, demonstrating where and why differences arise in variational calculations.

## Key numbers

- The paper presents figures showing energy per nucleon E/N in MeV as a function of system size parameters, with values ranging from approximately 11.5 MeV to 20 MeV for different configurations.
- For the free Fermi gas reference, the energy per nucleon E_FG/N is shown to vary with system size L in Fm, with values around 10.1 to 10.8 MeV depending on the boundary condition treatment.
- The density rho in fm^{-1} is plotted against position x in fm, with density values ranging from 0 to approximately 0.5 fm^{-1} for the spatial density profiles.
- The kinetic energy expectation values <K>/k_F are shown to range from approximately -1 to 1 in the scaled units used, depending on the twist parameter k_0 N/k_F.

## Context

This work extends the methodological comparison between condensed matter boundary condition techniques (TABC, developed by Lin, Zong, and Ceperley and by Kolo) and nuclear physics approaches (Wannier representation in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics) to the specific context of stellar matter simulations. The paper enables practitioners modeling supernova matter and neutron stars to understand the systematic uncertainties introduced by their choice of boundary conditions in variational calculations, and provides a formal framework for assessing when the two methods can be expected to agree or disagree.
