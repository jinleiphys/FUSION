# Hydrodynamic Predictions for Mixed Harmonic Correlations in 200 GeV Au+Au Collisions

> Recent measurements at the LHC involve the correlation of different azimuthal flow harmonics $v_n$. These new observables add constraints to theoretical models and probe aspects of the system that are independent of the traditional single-harmonic measurements such as 2- and multi-particle cumulants $v_n\{m\}$. Many of these new observables have not yet been measured at RHIC, leaving an opportunity to make predictions as a test of models across energies. We make predictions using NeXSPheRIO, a hydrodynamical model which has accurately reproduced a large set of single-harmonic correlations in a large range of transverse momenta and centralities at RHIC. Our predictions thus provide an important baseline for comparison to correlations of flow harmonics, which contain non-trivial information about the initial state as well as QGP transport properties. We also point out significant biases that can appear when using wide centrality bins and non-trivial event weighting, necessitating care in performing experimental analyses and in comparing theoretical calculations to these measurements.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that mixed-harmonic flow correlations at RHIC will be qualitatively similar to LHC measurements but typically smaller in magnitude, and that these observables provide a robust test of the hydrodynamic paradigm because the normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(3,2) shows little dependence on initial conditions, viscosity, or collision energy. However, the authors emphasize that experimental analysis details such as centrality bin width and event weighting can introduce significant biases that must be accounted for in any comparison between theory and experiment.

## Method

The authors used the NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic model, which combines NeXus initial conditions (a parton-based Gribov-Regge picture) with 3+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution and hadronic decays, to simulate 200 GeV Au+Au collisions. They calculated symmetric cumulants NSC(n,m) and event-plane correlations for momentum-integrated measurements with STAR kinematic cuts (p_T > 200 MeV, |η| < 1), using 1% centrality bins recombined into 10% bins with multiplicity-dependent event weights matching the ALICE collaboration's procedure. For exploratory studies of NSC(3,2) dependence, they also used initial-state eccentricity calculations from MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, rcBK-MC, and IP-Glasma models, exploiting the approximate proportionality v_n ∝ ε_n for n ≤ 3.

## Key numbers

- Predictions are made for 200 GeV Au+Au collisions with kinematic cuts p_T > 200 MeV and |η| < 1, matching STAR TPC acceptance
- 2000 events are used for each 10% centrality bin in the NeXSPheRIO calculations
- The normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(3,2) shows negligible difference from the eccentricity-based εSC(3,2) when using 1% bins with multiplicity weights, but a statistically significant difference appears when using the correct experimental weighting procedure
- Large centrality bins (e.g., 10% vs 1%) produce a spurious positive correlation in NSC(n,m) due to intra-bin centrality fluctuations, with the effect strongest in central collisions
- Multiplicity-dependent event weights (W = M(M-1) for 2-particle and W = M(M-1)(M-2)(M-3) for 4-particle correlations) bias measurements toward lower v_n values in wide centrality bins
- Viscosity dependence of NSC(4,2) is statistically significant with 10% bins and unit weights, but becomes statistically insignificant when using 1% recombined bins with multiplicity weights

## Context

This work extends the study of mixed-harmonic flow correlations, which have been measured at the LHC by ATLAS (Aad, 2014) and ALICE (2016), to RHIC energies where they have not yet been measured. The NeXSPheRIO model used here has previously shown good agreement with single-harmonic measurements at RHIC including v_2, v_3, and v_4 across a wide range of centrality and transverse momentum (Gardim, 2012). The paper demonstrates that these new observables can discriminate between models that fit traditional observables equally well, as noted by Niemi (2015) and Zhou (2015), and that the normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(3,2) is particularly robust as a test of the hydrodynamic paradigm because it depends little on initial conditions, viscosity, or collision energy. The authors also show that analysis details can affect even investigations of viscosity dependence, as demonstrated by the vanishing of statistically significant viscosity effects in NSC(4,2) when using proper centrality binning and event weighting.
