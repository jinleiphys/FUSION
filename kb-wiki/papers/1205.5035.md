# Gluon saturation in dijet production in p-Pb collisions at Large Hadron Collider

> We study saturation effects in the production of dijets in p-p and p-Pb collisions using the framework of high energy factorization. We focus on central-forward jet configurations, which allow for probing gluon density at low longitudinal momentum fraction. We find significant suppression of the central-forward jet decorrelations in p-Pb compared to p-p, which we attribute to saturation of gluon density in nuclei.

# Gluon saturation in dijet production in p-Pb collisions at Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim
The authors find significant suppression of central-forward jet decorrelations in p-Pb compared to p-p collisions, which they attribute to saturation of gluon density in nuclei. However, the nuclear gluon density is modeled via a simple A^{1/3} enhancement of the nonlinear term in the evolution equation, which the authors acknowledge is "not sufficient to fully model the nuclear target and is a somewhat crude approximation."

## Method
The authors use high energy factorization (also known as k_t-factorization) to compute dijet production cross sections, focusing on asymmetric central-forward jet configurations where one jet probes gluon density at low longitudinal momentum fraction x. They solve a unified BK/DGLAP evolution equation for the unintegrated gluon density, fitted to combined HERA F_2 data, and extend the framework to nuclear targets by enhancing the nonlinear saturation term by A^{1/3} (with A=207 for Pb). The cross section is computed using 2-to-2 partonic matrix elements with one off-shell initial-state gluon, with collinear parton distributions (CTEQ6mE) for the on-shell side.

## Key numbers
- The unintegrated gluon density is fitted to HERA data in the kinematic range x < 0.01 and the full Q^2 range, achieving chi^2/ndof = 1.73 with parameters N=0.994, beta=18.6, D=-82.1, and R=2.40 GeV^{-1}.
- The initial scale for evolution is set to k_0^2 = 1 GeV^2, with the strong coupling one-loop Lambda_QCD = 350 MeV.
- For p-Pb collisions at the LHC, the azimuthal decorrelation cross section near the peak (Delta-phi near pi) is suppressed by a factor of about two compared to p-p, with the effect extending to lower Delta-phi as the p_t threshold is lowered from 35 GeV to 15 GeV.
- The ratio of coincidence probabilities for d-Au/p-p at RHIC at sqrt(s)=200 GeV with p_t1 > 2.5 GeV and p_t2 > 1.5 GeV shows suppression of the away-side peak correctly reproduced by the framework.
- The linear fit (without saturation) to HERA data restricted to Q^2 above 4.5 GeV^2 gives chi^2=1.51, but fitting the full Q^2 range yields chi^2=3.86, indicating saturation is needed.

## Context
This work extends the study of saturation effects in forward dijet production from earlier work by Deak et al. (2010, 2011) using the CASCADE Monte Carlo generator, and from Marquet (2003) and Iancu (2006) on dijet production with large rapidity separation. The authors compare their results to CMS measurements of central-forward jet p_t spectra and to STAR d-Au dihadron data from Braidot (2010, 2011). The framework enables future studies of saturation in p-Pb collisions at the LHC, particularly through azimuthal decorrelation observables that probe the unintegrated gluon density at low k_t, and opens the possibility to test the validity of high energy factorization in the region near Delta-phi = pi.
