# Single transverse-spin asymmetry for $D$-meson production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering

> We study the single-transverse spin asymmetry for open charm production in the semi-inclusive lepton-hadron deep inelastic scattering. We calculate the asymmetry in terms of the QCD collinear factorization approach for $D$ mesons at high enough $P_{h\perp}$, and find that the asymmetry is proportional to the twist-three tri-gluon correlation function in the proton. With a simple model for the tri-gluon correlation function, we estimate the asymmetry for both COMPASS and eRHIC kinematics, and discuss the possibilities of extracting the tri-gluon correlation function in these experiments.

## Key claim

The single-transverse-spin asymmetry (SSA) for $D$-meson production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) is directly proportional to the diagonal part of the twist-three tri-gluon correlation function $T_G(x,x)$ in the proton, because the photon-gluon fusion subprocess at this order has no "hard-pole" contribution; this makes the measurement a clean extraction of $T_G(x,x)$, provided the observed transverse momentum $P_{h\perp}$ is large enough for collinear factorization to apply.

## Method

The authors calculate the SSA using QCD collinear factorization at leading order for the photon-gluon fusion subprocess $\gamma^*+g\to c+\bar{c}$, with the charm quark fragmenting into a $D$ meson. They derive the twist-three polarized cross section by applying the master formula for the tri-gluon correlation function, evaluating the imaginary part from unpinched poles in diagrams where an extra gluon is attached to the initial state. The asymmetry is obtained by taking the ratio of the polarized and unpolarized cross sections, both integrated over the azimuthal angle $\phi$ between the hadron and lepton planes.

## Key numbers

- The tri-gluon correlation function is modeled as $T_G(x,x)=\lambda_g\, G(x)$ with $\lambda_g=0.07$ GeV, using the same sign and size as the quark-gluon correlation function $T_F(x,x)$.
- For COMPASS kinematics ($S_{ep}=300$ GeV$^2$, $x_B=0.01$, $Q=1$ GeV), the unpolarized $D^0$ production cross section at $P_{h\perp}\sim 1$ GeV is shown to be potentially measurable.
- For eRHIC kinematics ($S_{ep}=2500$ GeV$^2$, $x_B=0.01$, $Q=4$ GeV), the production rate is smaller but may still yield enough events with high luminosity.
- The SSA $\langle 1 \rangle$ (the $\phi$-integrated asymmetry) shows a minimum at $z_h\sim 0.5$ and increases as $z_h$ moves away from this value, a prediction of the twist-three formalism.

## Context

This work extends the twist-three formalism for single-transverse-spin asymmetries, previously applied to quark-gluon correlation functions by Qiu and Sterman (1991) and to direct photon production by Ji (1992), to the gluonic sector via the tri-gluon correlation function $T_G(x,x)$. It enables the extraction of $T_G(x,x)$ from SIDIS experiments at COMPASS and the planned eRHIC, complementing studies of the gluon Sivers function in the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) approach by Anselmino et al. (2004) and Boer et al. (2003). The same approach can be applied to open charm production in hadronic collisions, dominated by gluon-gluon fusion, as noted by Yuan (2008) and Vitev (2006).
