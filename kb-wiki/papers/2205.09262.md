# Nuclear Weak Rates and Nuclear Weak Processes in Stars

> Nuclear weak rates in stellar environments are obtained by shell-model calculations including Gamow-Teller (GT) and spin-dipole transitions, and applied to nuclear weak processes in stars. The important roles of accurate weak rates for the study of astrophysical processes are pointed out. The weak rates in $sd$-shell are used to study the evolution of ONeMg cores in stars with 8-10 M$_{\odot}$. Cooling of the core by nuclear Urca processes, and the heating by double e-captures on $^{20}$Ne are studied. Especially, the e-capture rates for a second-forbidden transition in $^{20}$Ne are evaluated with the multipole expansion method of Walecka and Behrens-B$\ddot{\mbox{u}}$hring, and the final fate of the cores, core-collapse or thermonuclear explosion, are discussed. The weak rates in $pf$-shell are applied to nucleosynthesis of iron-group elements in Type Ia supernovae. The over-production problem of neutron-rich iron isotopes compared with the solar abundances is now reduced to be within a factor of two. The weak rates for nuclear Urca pair with $A$=31 in the island of inversion are evaluated with the effective interaction obtained by the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa method. The transition strengths and e-capture rates in $^{78}$Ni, important for core-collapse processes, are evaluated with the $pf$-$sdg$ shell, and compared with those obtained by the random-phase-approximation and an effective rate formula. $β$-decay rates of $N$ =126 isotones are evaluated with both the GT and first-forbidden transitions. The half-lives are found to be shorter than those obtained by standard models. Neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections on $^{13}$C, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ar are obtained with new shell-model Hamiltonians. Implications on nucleosynthesis, neutrino detection, neutrino oscillations and neutrino mass hierarchy are discussed.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations of nuclear weak rates, including Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole transitions, are refined and applied to stellar processes; the abstract's claim of "important roles" is hedged by the fact that the final fate of O-Ne-Mg cores (core-collapse or thermonuclear explosion) remains dependent on the competition between contraction and heating, and the over-production of neutron-rich iron isotopes is only reduced to within a factor of two, not eliminated.

## Method

Electron-capture and beta-decay rates for sd-shell nuclei were evaluated using the USDB shell-model Hamiltonian, with Gamow-Teller strengths and experimental data incorporated, and Coulomb corrections applied to the chemical potential and threshold energies. For forbidden transitions in 20Ne, the multipole expansion method of Walecka and the Behrens-Bühring method were used to treat the lepton-energy dependence of transition strengths. Rates for pf-shell nuclei were computed with the GXPF1J Hamiltonian, and for cross-shell nuclei (sd-pf and pf-sdg) with effective interactions from the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa method.

## Key numbers

- Beta-decay Q-values for the A=23, 25, and 27 Urca pairs are 4.376 MeV, 3.835 MeV, and 2.610 MeV, respectively.
- The Urca density for the (23Na, 23Ne) pair is found at log10(rho Ye) = 8.96 with Coulomb effects and 8.92 without; for (25Mg, 25Na) it is 8.81 with and 8.77 without Coulomb effects.
- The quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant is taken as g_A^eff/g_A = 0.764.
- The new log ft value for the beta-decay of 20F (2+_g.s.) to 20Ne (0+_g.s.) is 10.89 +/- 0.11.
- The over-production of neutron-rich iron isotopes in Type Ia supernova nucleosynthesis is reduced to within a factor of two of solar abundances.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering weak-rate tabulations of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1980-1982) and the shell-model-based rates of Oda (1994) for sd-shell nuclei and Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000) for pf-shell nuclei. It also builds on the shell model Monte Carlo and random-phase-approximation approach of Hix and Langanke (1999) for heavier nuclei. The refined rates enable more accurate modeling of O-Ne-Mg core evolution in 8-10 solar mass stars, including Urca cooling and double electron-capture heating, and improve nucleosynthesis predictions for Type Ia supernovae and r-process waiting-point nuclei at N=126.
