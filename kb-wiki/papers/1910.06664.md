# Chemical equilibration of QGP in hadronic collisions

> We performed state-of-the-art QCD effective kinetic theory simulations of chemically equilibrating QGP in longitudinally expanding systems. We find that chemical equilibration takes place after hydrodynamization, but well before local thermalization. By relating the transport properties of QGP and the system size we estimate that hadronic collisions with final state multiplicities $dN_\text{ch}/dη> 10^2$ live long enough to reach approximate chemical equilibrium for all collision systems. Therefore we expect the saturation of strangeness enhancement to occur at the same multiplicity in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

Chemical equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma occurs after hydrodynamization but well before local thermalization, and hadronic collisions with final-state charged-particle multiplicities above approximately 100 are expected to live long enough to reach approximate chemical equilibrium for all collision systems (proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus).

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Boltzmann equations for quark and gluon distribution functions undergoing homogeneous boost-invariant expansion, using leading-order QCD effective kinetic theory with elastic 2-to-2 and collinear 1-to-2 processes. They initialized the system with zero fermion density and a highly anisotropic gluon distribution (anisotropy parameter ξ=10), then evolved the distributions to equilibrium for coupling constants λ = 1, 5, 10, and 20, corresponding to shear viscosity-to-entropy ratios η/s ranging from approximately 1900 down to 0.4.

## Key numbers

- Chemical equilibration (fermions reaching 90% of equilibrium energy density) occurs at τ/τ_R ≈ 1.2, with this scaled time being nearly independent of the coupling constant λ across the range λ = 1 to 20.
- For phenomenological parameters η/s = 0.16, ⟨sτ⟩ = 4.1 GeV², and ν_eff = 40, the chemical equilibration time converts to τ_chem ≈ 1.5 fm, with fermion and gluon energy densities becoming equal at τ ≈ 0.6 fm.
- The minimum charged-particle multiplicity required to achieve chemical equilibrium before freeze-out is dN_ch/dη ≳ 110, assuming τ_chem/τ_R = 1.2, η/s = 0.16, and that the system lifetime equals its transverse size.

## Context

This work extends the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) and the gluonic kinetic theory simulations of Kurkela (2015) by including full leading-order QCD kinetic theory with both quarks and gluons. The results provide a theoretical foundation for the observed strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity collisions measured by ALICE (2017) and Abelev (2013), and enable quantitative predictions connecting QGP transport properties to experimental hadron chemistry across proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus systems.
