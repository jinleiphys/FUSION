# TeV Lepton Number Violation: From Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay to the LHC

> We analyze the sensitivity of next-generation tonne-scale neutrinoless double $β$-decay ($0νββ$) experiments and searches for same-sign di-electrons plus jets at the Large Hadron Collider to TeV scale lepton number violating interactions. Taking into account previously unaccounted for physics and detector backgrounds at the LHC, renormalization group evolution, and long-range contributions to $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements, we find that the reach of tonne-scale $0νββ$ generally exceeds that of the LHC. However, for a range of heavy particle masses near the TeV scale, the high luminosity LHC and tonne-scale $0νββ$ may provide complementary probes.

# TeV Lepton Number Violation: From Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay to the LHC

## Key claim
The reach of tonne-scale neutrinoless double beta decay experiments generally exceeds that of the LHC for TeV-scale lepton number violating interactions, contrary to earlier claims by Helo et al. (2013) that the LHC would achieve substantially greater reach. However, for a range of heavy particle masses near the TeV scale, the high luminosity LHC and tonne-scale neutrinoless double beta decay may provide complementary probes.

## Method
The authors analyzed a simplified model containing a scalar doublet S and a Majorana fermion F that generates same-sign di-electron plus di-jet final states at the LHC and contributes to neutrinoless double beta decay. They implemented the model in Madgraph with Pythia showering and PGS detector simulation for 14 TeV proton-proton collisions, including previously unaccounted detector backgrounds from charge flip and jet fake events. They also incorporated renormalization group evolution of the effective operators from the TeV scale down to 1 GeV and long-range contributions to the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements via pion-exchange diagrams.

## Key numbers
- Present GERDA upper limit on neutrinoless double beta decay half-life for 76Ge: T_{1/2} < 3 x 10^{25} years
- Future tonne-scale 76Ge sensitivity: T_{1/2} = 6 x 10^{27} years
- For M_S = M_F = 1 TeV and g_1 = g_2 = 0.176, the signal cross section before cuts is 0.142 fb
- After all cuts, the signal cross section is 0.033 fb with significance S/sqrt(S+B) = 0.0738 per sqrt(fb)
- Non-observation with approximately 735 fb^{-1} (or 70 fb^{-1}) would imply exclusion at the level of the present GERDA limit, depending on the nuclear matrix element value
- Discovery (S/sqrt(S+B) >= 5) requires greater than 4.6 ab^{-1} (or greater than 435 fb^{-1}), depending on nuclear matrix element value
- The QCD evolution suppresses the Wilson coefficient C_1 at 1 GeV to 0.203 times its value at the high scale, with effective coefficient C_eff = 0.092 C_1(Lambda)
- The hadronic matching scale Lambda_H = 2.74 GeV, derived from m_pi^2/(m_u+m_d) with m_{pi+} = 139 MeV and m_u+m_d = 7 MeV
- The nuclear matrix element M_0 for 76Ge from QRPA is -1.99

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Helo et al. (2013) on simplified models for TeV-scale lepton number violation, but finds their conclusions about LHC reach were overly optimistic. The authors incorporate three factors omitted in the earlier work: realistic LHC detector backgrounds (charge flip and jet fake), renormalization group evolution of effective operators, and long-range contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements via the pion-exchange mechanism of Prezeau et al. (2003). The results enable comparison of the complementary discovery potential of tonne-scale neutrinoless double beta decay experiments and the high-luminosity LHC, and motivate reducing uncertainties in nuclear and hadronic matrix elements as well as refining LHC background estimates.
