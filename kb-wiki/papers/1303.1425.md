# Laser-assisted nuclear photoeffect reexamined

> The S-matrix element and the cross section of the laser-assisted nuclear photoeffect are recalculated in a gauge invariant manner taking into account the effect of the Coulomb field of the remainder nucleus. The γ-photon energy dependence of the laser free cross section obtained in the plane wave and long-wavelength Coulomb-Volkov approximations are compared. Numerically the laser-assisted partial cross sections with laser photon energy 2 keV and some different polarization states of γ-photon of energy 3 MeV are investigated.

## Key claim

The laser-assisted nuclear photoeffect cross section is recalculated in a gauge invariant manner including the Coulomb repulsion between the ejected proton and the remainder nucleus, yielding a total cross section that is 0.0161 times smaller than the prior result of Dadi (2013) at a gamma photon energy of 3 MeV, contrary to the much larger cross section previously reported.

## Method

The S-matrix element and cross section are derived in the radiation (pA) gauge using the long-wavelength approximation (LWA) for both the laser field (circularly polarized, vector potential A_L) and the gamma photon field. The initial bound proton state is taken as a 1s-like wavefunction for a proton in ^8B (binding energy 0.137 MeV), and the final state is a Coulomb-Volkov wavefunction in the LWA, incorporating the Coulomb field of the residual ^7Be nucleus (Z=4). The gauge invariant S-matrix element is obtained via Eq. (27) of Kalman and Kiss (1990), and the partial cross sections for channels with n laser photon absorptions or emissions are computed using Bessel function expansions and integrated over proton emission angles.

## Key numbers

- Laser-free averaged total cross section at gamma photon energy 3 MeV: sigma_T = 1.02 mb (with Coulomb correction included).
- Laser-free cross section in the plane wave approximation (without Coulomb correction) at 3 MeV: sigma_T / chi_C^2(Q_0) = 4.11 mb.
- The total cross section from the present gauge-invariant, Coulomb-corrected calculation is 0.0161 times the result of Dadi (2013) at gamma photon energy 3 MeV; this factor decomposes into a factor of 1/4.03 from the Coulomb repulsion and a factor of 1/15.4 from gauge invariance.
- Binding energy of the proton in ^8B: E_b = 0.137 MeV; nuclear parameter nu = 1.84.
- Laser photon energy used in numerical partial cross section calculations: 2 keV; gamma photon energy: 3 MeV.
- Laser intensities investigated: 4.0 x 10^21, 1.0 x 10^23, and 2.5 x 10^24 W/cm^2.
- Parameter delta (ratio of A_L term to Q term) is less than or equal to 0.004 for gamma energies >= 3 MeV, and increases to about 0.05 at gamma energy 0.189 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the gauge-invariant treatment of the laser-assisted x-ray photoeffect by Kalman and Kiss (1990) to the nuclear photoeffect, correcting the prior calculation by Dadi (2013) which neglected both gauge invariance and the Coulomb repulsion between the outgoing proton and the residual nucleus. The results show that the Coulomb factor (Fermi correction factor from beta decay theory) strongly suppresses the cross section near threshold and remains significant even at MeV gamma energies. This enables more accurate modeling of photonuclear reactions in intense laser fields, particularly for astrophysically relevant nuclei like ^8B, and clarifies the proper treatment of gauge invariance in such calculations.
