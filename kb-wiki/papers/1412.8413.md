# Electromagnetic baryon form factors in the Poincare-covariant Faddeev approach

> Baryons are treated as three-quark systems using QCD degrees of freedom in Poincare-covariant bound-state equations. The quark self-energy as well as the interaction between quarks are approximated by a vector-vector interaction via a single dressed-gluon exchange (rainbow-ladder truncation), thereby allowing a unified study of quark, meson and baryon properties. Here we will focus on the calculation of electromagnetic properties of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 ground state baryons.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev approach yields electromagnetic form factors for nucleons, Delta baryons, and N-to-Delta transitions that agree reasonably with experimental and lattice data, but the authors note that the absence of structure at low momentum transfer, particularly in magnetic form factors, indicates missing meson-cloud effects that the truncation cannot capture.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the three-body Faddeev equation for baryon bound states, both in the rainbow-ladder truncation where the quark-gluon vertex is replaced by its bare form and the three-body kernel vanishes. The electromagnetic current is constructed from the conserved current for a three-quark system, using the nonperturbative quark-photon vertex obtained from its own Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the Faddeev amplitudes calculated self-consistently from the interaction kernel.

## Key numbers

- Proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) are compared to experimental data (see Fig. 1 in the paper for references).
- Delta electromagnetic form factors \(G_{E0}\), \(G_{M1}\), \(G_{E2}\), and \(G_{M3}\) are compared to lattice results (see Fig. 2 for references).
- N-to-Delta transition form factors \(G_M^\star\), \(R_{EM}\), and \(R_{SM}\) are compared to experimental data up to 6 GeV\(^2\) (the highest data point for \(R_{EM}\) from Aznauryan (2009)).
- The Delta Faddeev amplitude contains 128 Dirac tensors; the nucleon amplitude contains 64 Dirac structures.
- The ratio \(R_{EM}\) remains negative up to 6 GeV\(^2\) in experimental data, while an s-wave-only calculation would predict \(R_{EM} \rightarrow +100\%\) asymptotically.

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev approach for baryons, previously applied to nucleon and Delta properties by Eichmann (2011) and Sanchis-Alepuz (2013), to include N-to-Delta transition form factors. The results support the quark-diquark interpretation of lowest-lying baryons found in earlier quark-diquark models by Cloet (2008) and Segovia (2013), while the missing pion-cloud effects point to the need for going beyond rainbow-ladder truncation, as discussed by Fischer (2009) and Williams (2014).
