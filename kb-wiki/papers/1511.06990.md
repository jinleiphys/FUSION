# Vector Meson Masses from Hidden Local Symmetry in Constant Magnetic Field

> We discuss the magnetic responses of vector meson masses based on the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model in constant magnetic field, described by the lightest two-flavor system including the pion, rho and omega mesons in the spectrum. The effective masses influenced under the magnetic field are evaluated in a way of the derivative/chiral expansion established in the HLS model. At the leading order O(p^2) the g-factor of the charged rho meson is fixed to be 2, implying that the rho meson at this order is treated just like a point-like spin-1 particle. Beyond the leading order, one finds anomalous magnetic interactions of the charged rho meson, involving the anomalous magnetic moment, which give corrections to the effective mass. It is then suggested that up to O(p^4) the charged rho meson tends to become massless. Of interest is that nontrivial magnetic-dependence of neutral mesons emerges to give rise to the significant mixing among neutral mesons. Consequently, it leads to the dramatic enhancement of the omega meson mass, which is testable in future lattice simulations. Corrections from terms beyond O(p^4) are also addressed.

## Key claim
The authors argue that up to ${\cal O}(p^4)$ in the derivative/chiral expansion of the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model, the charged rho meson tends to become massless in a constant magnetic field, but they hedge that this conclusion may be modified by higher-order corrections. The neutral omega meson mass is dramatically enhanced through intrinsic-parity odd terms at ${\cal O}(p^4)$, a prediction they state is testable in future lattice simulations.

## Method
The authors formulate the HLS model for the lightest two-flavor system (pion, rho, and omega mesons) in a constant magnetic field using a background field method. They expand the Lagrangian to ${\cal O}(p^2)$ and ${\cal O}(p^4)$ in the derivative/chiral expansion, compute the effective masses of charged and neutral vector mesons by diagonalizing the mass matrices, and fix the ${\cal O}(p^4)$ couplings using lattice QCD results for the anomalous magnetic moment and experimental decay widths for the intrinsic-parity odd sector.

## Key numbers
- The charged rho meson g-factor at leading order ${\cal O}(p^2)$ is fixed to 2, giving an effective mass $m_\rho^2(eB) = m_\rho^2 - eB$ for the lowest Landau level.
- The anomalous magnetic moment correction from ${\cal O}(p^4)$ terms is fixed to $-g^2 z_3 + g^2 z_7/2 = 0.4 \pm 0.2$ from lattice QCD (Luschevskaya 2015).
- At $eB = 0.3$ GeV$^2$, the charged rho effective mass decreases from about 0.55 GeV (leading order) to about 0.43 GeV when including the anomalous magnetic moment.
- The omega meson mass increases by about 37% when $eB$ reaches 0.3 GeV$^2$, using experimental decay widths $\Gamma(\rho^0 \to \pi^0 \gamma) \simeq 8.9 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV and $\Gamma(\omega \to \pi^0 \gamma) \simeq 7.0 \times 10^{-4}$ GeV to fix couplings $|g_{\rho\pi\gamma}| \simeq 8.3 \times 10^{-1}$ GeV$^{-1}$ and $|g_{\omega\pi\gamma}| \simeq 2.3 \times 10^{0}$ GeV$^{-1}$.

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD in strong magnetic fields, relevant for magnetars and heavy ion collisions, using the HLS model developed by Bando (1984, 1987) and systematically expanded by Tanabashi (1993) and Harada (2003). It contrasts with earlier effective model studies that predicted rho meson condensation (Chernodub 2010, Callebaut 2011) and with lattice QCD results (Hidaka 2012) that argued against such condensation. The predicted omega mass enhancement provides a testable target for future lattice simulations, and the framework enables extension to finite temperature for applications to magnetic catalysis and heavy ion physics.
