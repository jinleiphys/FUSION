# Dual quark condensate in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> The dual quark condensate $Σ^{(n)}$ proposed recently as a new order parameter of the spontaneous breaking of the ${\mathbb Z}_3$ symmetry are evaluated by the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, where $n$ are winding numbers. The Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model well reproduces lattice QCD data on $Σ^{(1)}$ measured very lately. The dual quark condensate $Σ^{(n)}$ at higher temperatures is sensitive to the strength of the vector-type four-quark interaction in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and hence a good quantity to determine the strength.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can reproduce lattice QCD data on the dual quark condensate (dressed Polyakov loop) \(\Sigma^{(1)}\), but only when the Polyakov-loop potential of the logarithm form (RRW06) is used; the polynomial form (RTW05) fails. The dual quark condensate at higher temperatures is sensitive to the strength of the vector-type four-quark interaction, making it a good quantity to determine that strength, but this sensitivity is conditional on the temperature being above approximately \(1.5\,T_{\rm c}^{\Sigma^{(1)}}\).

## Method

The dual quark condensate \(\Sigma^{(n)}\) was evaluated within the mean-field approximation to the two-flavor PNJL model, which includes scalar-type four-quark, scalar-type eight-quark, and vector-type four-quark interactions. The chiral condensate \(\sigma(\varphi)\) was computed as a function of a twisted angle \(\varphi\) in the temporal boundary condition for quarks, with the Polyakov loop \(\Phi\) and its conjugate fixed to their values under the anti-periodic boundary condition (\(\varphi=\pi\)). The dual quark condensate was then obtained by Fourier transforming \(\sigma(\varphi)\) with respect to \(\varphi\). Four variants of the PNJL model (PNJL-I through PNJL-IV) and one NJL model were compared, using two different forms of the Polyakov-loop potential (RRW06 and RTW05).

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature for the dressed Polyakov loop \(T_{\rm c}^{\Sigma^{(1)}}\) equals \(T_{\rm c}=173\) MeV in PNJL-II and PNJL-III with the RRW06 potential, and equals \(0.97\,T_{\rm c}\) in the NJL model.
- In PNJL-II, the pseudo-critical temperatures are \(T_{\rm c}^{\Phi}=T_{\rm c}\) and \(T_{\rm c}^{\sigma}=1.14\,T_{\rm c}\) (with \(T_{\rm c}=173\) MeV).
- The vector-type four-quark interaction strength ratio used in PNJL-III is \(G_{\rm v}/G_{\rm s}=0.667\).
- The effect of the vector-type four-quark interaction on \(\Sigma^{(0)}\) and \(\Sigma^{(1)}\) is sizable at temperatures \(T > 1.5\,T_{\rm c}^{\Sigma^{(1)}}\).
- The scalar-type eight-quark interaction affects \(\Sigma^{(0)}\) and \(\Sigma^{(1)}\) appreciably only in the range \(T_{\rm c}^{\Sigma^{(1)}} < T < 2\,T_{\rm c}^{\Sigma^{(1)}}\).
- If \(\Lambda\) and \(G_{\rm s}\) are varied by 1%, \(\Sigma^{(1)}\) changes by about 10%, but the normalized quantity \(\Sigma^{(1)}/\Sigma^{(1)}_{T_{\rm c}}\) near \(T_{\rm c}\) is hardly changed.

## Context

This work extends the study of the dual quark condensate, originally proposed by Bilgici et al. (2008, 2009) as an order parameter for \(\mathbb{Z}_3\) center symmetry, from quenched and full lattice QCD to the PNJL model. It builds on the PNJL model framework of Fukushima (2004) and the Polyakov-loop potentials of Ratti et al. (2006) (RTW05) and Roessner et al. (2007) (RRW06). The results enable the determination of the vector-type four-quark interaction strength in the PNJL model, which is otherwise a free parameter, by comparing the calculated dual quark condensate with lattice QCD data at high temperature.
