# Vector mesons in the Extended Chiral Quark Model

> We extend our previous formulation of low-energy QCD in terms of an effective lagrangian containing operators of dimensionality $d\le 6$ constructed with pseudoscalars and quark fields, describing physics below the scale of chiral symmetry breaking. We include in this paper the vector and axial-vector channels. We follow closely the Extended Chiral Quark Model approach and consistently work in the large-$N_c$ and leading log approximation and take into account the constraints from chiral symmetry and chiral symmetry restoration. The optimal fit of all parameters gives further support to a heavy scalar meson with a mass $\sim 1$ GeV and a value of the axial pion-quark coupling constant $g_A \simeq 0.55$ to 0.66, depending on some assumptions concerning the Weinberg sum rules.

## Key claim

The Extended Chiral Quark Model (ECQM), when extended to include vector and axial-vector mesons, provides a "not bad" but "not brilliant" overall fit to experimental data, with the axial meson mass systematically underestimated when the second Weinberg sum rule is assumed to be saturated by a single resonance in each channel.

## Method

The authors extend their previous ECQM formulation by adding four-fermion operators for vector and axial-vector channels, then bosonize the theory using auxiliary fields for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons. They integrate out quark fields in the large-Nc and leading-log approximation, retaining only logarithmically enhanced terms from the quark loop, and impose chiral symmetry restoration constraints via sum rules for the scalar-pseudoscalar and vector-axial-vector two-point correlators.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass Σ₀ ranges from approximately 150 MeV (for gA ≈ 0.71) to a maximum of about 190 MeV (for mσ ~ 1.1 GeV)
- Scalar meson mass mσ ≈ (900 ± 300) MeV, derived from the chiral constant L8 = (0.9 ± 0.4) × 10⁻³
- Axial pion-quark coupling constant gA ≈ 0.55 to 0.66, depending on assumptions concerning the Weinberg sum rules
- For mσ = 900 MeV, the lower bound on gA is approximately 0.60, with gA ≈ 0.66 giving L5 ≈ 0.9 × 10⁻³ and gA ≈ 0.71 giving L5 ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻³
- For a heavier scalar meson with mσ = 1060 MeV, the minimum gA ≈ 0.48, with gA ≈ 0.62 predicting mA ≈ 1 GeV
- Vector meson mass mV = mρ = 770 MeV; axial meson mass mA = ma1 ≈ 1.1 to 1.2 GeV (phenomenological input)
- Pion decay constant F₀ = 90 MeV; B₀ (1 GeV) ≈ 1.5 GeV; current quark mass m̂q (1 GeV) ≈ 6 MeV
- Heavy pseudoscalar mass mΠ ≈ 1.3 GeV
- The ratio ΔSP/(Zπ mΠ²) ≈ 0.03, justifying the approximation in the scalar sum rules

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ECQM formulation (Andrianov and Espriu, 1998) by adding vector and axial-vector channels, building on the Chiral Quark Model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model approaches. The paper shows that the NJL model fails to satisfy chiral symmetry restoration constraints in the scalar channel (requiring gA ≈ 2.5, which is unrealistic), while the ECQM provides a more consistent framework. The results enable further study of how higher-mass excited vector and axial-vector resonances are needed to fully satisfy the fourth Weinberg sum rule, and constrain the chiral constants L5, L8, and L10.
