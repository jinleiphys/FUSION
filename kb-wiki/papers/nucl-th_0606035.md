# The nuclear AC-Stark shift in super-intense laser fields

> The direct interaction of super-intense laser fields in the optical frequency domain with nuclei is studied. As main observable, we consider the nuclear AC-Stark shift of low-lying nuclear states due to the off-resonant excitation by the laser field. We include the case of accelerated nuclei to be able to control the frequency and the intensity of the laser field in the nuclear rest frame over a wide range of parameters. We find that AC-Stark shifts of the same order as in typical quantum optical systems relative to the respective transition frequencies are feasible with state-of-the-art or near-future laser field intensities and moderate acceleration of the target nuclei. Along with this shift, we find laser-induced modifications to the proton root-mean-square radii and to the proton density distribution. We thus expect direct laser-nucleus interaction to become of relevance together with other super-intense light-matter interaction processes such as pair creation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that direct laser-nucleus interactions via the nuclear AC-Stark shift can become observable with state-of-the-art or near-future laser intensities, but only under the condition that the laser field is far off-resonant with nuclear transitions and that the nuclei are accelerated to achieve Doppler-shifted frequencies and intensities in the nuclear rest frame.

## Method

The authors calculated the AC-Stark shift of proton single-particle states using second-order perturbation theory in the electric dipole approximation, with the interaction Hamiltonian \(H_I = -e \vec{E}(t) \cdot \vec{r}\). The required proton single-particle wave-functions were obtained from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using the NL3 parameterization, solved on a coordinate-space grid in axial symmetry. The calculations assumed the adiabatic limit where the laser photon energy is much smaller than nuclear transition energies, and considered head-on collisions of accelerated nuclei with optical laser fields to boost the effective frequency and intensity in the nuclear rest frame.

## Key numbers

- AC-Stark shifts of approximately 1 keV are reached at nuclear rest-frame intensities of roughly \(10^{34}\) W/cm\(^2\) for \(^{16}\)O and \(10^{32}\) W/cm\(^2\) for \(^{168}\)Er and \(^{240}\)Pu.
- For \(\gamma\) factors of about 1000 to 4000, the Doppler shift amplifies the laser intensity by factors of \(4.0 \times 10^6\) to \(6.4 \times 10^7\), allowing lab-frame intensities of \(10^{22-24}\) W/cm\(^2\) to reach nuclear rest-frame intensities of \(10^{28-31}\) W/cm\(^2\).
- The lowest measured E1 excitations for the studied nuclei are 7.117 MeV (\(^{16}\)O), 1.359 MeV (\(^{168}\)Er), and 0.555 MeV (\(^{240}\)Pu), ensuring off-resonant conditions for laser photon energies of order keV in the nuclear rest frame.
- For \(^{168}\)Er at \(E_N = 1.0 \times 10^{16}\) V/cm, the classical oscillation extent of the nuclear center of mass is \(\Delta x_{pol} \approx 3 \times 10^{10}\) fm for \(\hbar\omega = 1\) eV and \(\Delta x_{pol} \approx 3 \times 10^{4}\) fm for \(\hbar\omega = 1\) keV, compared to the nuclear size of about 6 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of direct laser-nucleus interactions beyond the near-resonant case considered in the authors' earlier letter (Burvenich, Evers, Keitel, 2006) for x-ray lasers, to the off-resonant regime using super-intense optical lasers. It contrasts with previous work that dismissed direct laser-nucleus interactions due to small matrix elements (Matinyan, 1998) and with indirect approaches such as laser-induced pair creation (Burke et al., 1997) or plasma-mediated nuclear reactions (Schwoerer et al., 2001; Karsch et al., 1998). The results suggest that nuclear AC-Stark shifts comparable in relative magnitude to those in atomic quantum optics (Beauvoir et al., 1998; Haeffner et al., 2003) could be achieved, potentially enabling nuclear quantum optics experiments and new diagnostic tools for nuclear structure.
