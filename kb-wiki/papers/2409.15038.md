# Simulating Charm Quarks in IP-Glasma Initial Stage and Quark-Gluon Plasma: A Hybrid Approach for charm quark phenomenology

> We present phenomenological findings on charm quark transport while including its energy loss in both pre-equilibrium and hydrodynamic stages of the evolution. We employed the MARTINI event generator for the production and evolution of heavy quarks in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The sensitivity of the heavy meson nuclear modification factor and flow coefficient to the early stage of heavy-ion collisions and bulk medium evolution is analyzed for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. Our study provides insights into the interaction strength of charm quarks during the early phase and within the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Charm quark energy loss in the pre-equilibrium IP-Glasma stage has a non-negligible effect on D-meson observables, even though the initial phase has a short lifetime and a fluctuating energy density profile; the authors approximate the pre-equilibrium phase as a thermalized gluonic medium with a defined temperature as a first step, rather than using a full non-Abelian field treatment.

## Method

The authors use the MARTINI event generator to evolve charm quarks through both the IP-Glasma initial stage and the hydrodynamically expanding QGP phase described by the MUSIC viscous hydrodynamic code. Charm quarks are initially generated with PYTHIA8.2, with spatial distributions sampled from the fluctuating IP-Glasma background. The pre-equilibrium stage is approximated as a thermalized gluon medium with a conformal equation of state, and charm quark transport is modeled via Langevin equations using a temperature-dependent lattice spatial diffusion coefficient with 2+1 flavors. Hadronization into D-mesons uses either fragmentation alone or a combination of fragmentation and heavy-light coalescence.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV are simulated
- Switching time from Glasma to plasma phase is fixed at τ_w = 0.4 fm
- The spatial diffusion coefficient 2πDT is taken from N_f=2+1 lattice QCD estimates (Altenkort 2023)
- D-meson R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 are computed with and without pre-equilibrium energy loss; the pre-equilibrium contribution modifies both observables
- Comparison of D-meson R_AA with experimental data from ALICE (2021) is shown for Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends heavy-flavor phenomenology by including charm quark energy loss in the pre-equilibrium IP-Glasma stage, which is typically ignored in studies that only consider the QGP phase. The hybrid approach builds on the IP-Glasma initial state model (Schenke 2012), the MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics framework (Schenke 2010), and the MARTINI event generator (Young 2011). The study enables future work on full non-Abelian field treatments of charm quark interactions in the Glasma and more precise tomographic probes of the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions.
