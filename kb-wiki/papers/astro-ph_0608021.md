# Hybrid protoneutron stars with the MIT bag model

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of protoneutron stars. For the hadronic sector, we use a microscopic equation of state involving nucleons and hyperons derived within the finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory, with realistic two-body and three-body forces. For the description of quark matter, we employ the MIT bag model both with a constant and a density-dependent bag parameter. We calculate the structure of protostars with the equation of state comprising both phases and find maximum masses below 1.6 solar masses. Metastable heavy hybrid protostars are not found.

## Key claim

The authors find that hybrid protoneutron stars (PNS) containing both hadronic and quark matter have maximum masses below 1.6 solar masses, and that metastable heavy hybrid protostars do not exist, in contrast to purely hyperonic stars where metastability can occur during cooling.

## Method

The hadronic phase was described using a microscopic equation of state derived from finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with nucleons, hyperons, and realistic two-body (Argonne V18) and three-body (Urbana model) forces. The quark matter phase was described using the MIT bag model with both a constant bag parameter (B = 90 MeV/fm³) and a density-dependent bag parameter with a gaussian parametrization. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated via the Maxwell construction in beta-stable matter at finite temperature, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved for stellar structure with an isothermal core and isentropic envelope.

## Key numbers

- Maximum PNS masses: approximately 1.55 solar masses for the constant bag model and 1.48 solar masses for the density-dependent bag model
- For the density-dependent bag model, the heaviest PNS has baryonic mass M_B = 1.28 solar masses and gravitational mass M_G = 1.48 solar masses, transitioning to a neutron star with M_G = 1.26 solar masses
- The maximum stable neutron star mass after possible accretion is M_G = 1.53 solar masses for the density-dependent bag model
- For purely nucleonic PNS (no hyperons), maximum mass is in the range 1.77–1.87 solar masses
- The transition density from hadronic to quark matter is of the order of nuclear matter saturation density (rho_0 ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³)
- Core temperatures studied range from T = 0 to 50 MeV; above 40–50 MeV all stellar configurations become unstable

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cold baryonic neutron stars (Nicotra et al., 2006) and cold hybrid neutron stars (Burgio et al., 2002) to the hot, neutrino-trapped protoneutron star regime. It also extends earlier work on baryonic protoneutron stars (Nicotra et al., 2006) by including a hadron-quark phase transition. The results contrast with relativistic mean field models, where adding quark matter typically reduces the maximum neutron star mass, whereas in this microscopic approach the maximum mass increases with quark matter inclusion. The findings enable more realistic modeling of protoneutron star evolution and cooling, and suggest that heavy hybrid protoneutron stars will not exhibit metastable behavior during their transition to cold neutron stars.
