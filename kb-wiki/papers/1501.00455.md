# Hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen constrains new pseudoscalar interactions

> We constrain the possibility of a new pseudoscalar coupling between the muon and proton using a recent measurement of the 2S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen.

# Hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen constrains new pseudoscalar interactions

## Key claim
A new pseudoscalar coupling between the muon and proton is constrained by the measured 2S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen, but only at the 2-sigma level and only for pseudoscalar masses below 100 MeV where the product of couplings remains perturbative. The new interaction has negligible effect on the Lamb shift used to extract the proton radius, so it does not resolve the proton radius discrepancy.

## Method
The authors compute the contribution of a light pseudoscalar particle exchange to the hyperfine splitting of the 2S state in muonic hydrogen, using a nonrelativistic reduction of the pseudoscalar vertex and the known 2S wavefunction. They then compare the allowed range for the pseudoscalar contribution delta E_a, extracted from the difference between the measured hyperfine splitting and the theoretical prediction (which includes the Zemach radius from electron-proton scattering), to the expression for delta E_a as a function of the pseudoscalar mass m_a and the product of couplings f_mu f_p.

## Key numbers
- Measured 2S hyperfine splitting: 22.8089 ± 0.0051 meV
- Theoretical prediction for 2S hyperfine splitting: (22.9843 ± 0.0030) - (0.1621 ± 0.0010) r_Z + delta E_a meV, with Zemach radius r_Z = 1.045 ± 0.004 fm from e-p scattering
- Best-fit pseudoscalar contribution to hyperfine splitting: delta E_a = -0.006 meV
- 2-sigma allowed range for delta E_a: [-0.018, 0.006] meV
- Allowed product of couplings f_mu f_p at 2-sigma: [-0.00040, 0.00013] for m_a = 0; [-0.00173, 0.00058] for m_a = 10 MeV; [-0.015, 0.005] for m_a = 100 MeV
- CLEO constraint on proton pseudoscalar coupling: |f_p| < 0.029 at 90% C.L. for m_a < 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Barger (2010) which noted that a pseudoscalar interaction has negligible effect on the Lamb shift but significant effect on hyperfine splitting. The constraint is complementary to limits from the muon anomalous magnetic moment (which gives a negative contribution from pseudoscalar couplings while the measured value is higher than the Standard Model expectation) and from collider searches by CMS in the dimuon channel at higher masses. The result enables future searches for light pseudoscalars coupling to muons and protons, and clarifies that such particles cannot explain the proton radius discrepancy from Pohl (2010) and Antognini (2013).
