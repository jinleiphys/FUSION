# Nuclear Structure at the Crossroads

## Key claim
The paper summarizes progress, priorities, and open questions for nuclear effective field theory (EFT) developments based on the 2019 INT program "Nuclear Structure at the Crossroads," but it is not an exhaustive treatment; it is a summary of main points from presentations and discussions, with selective updates, and the authors refer readers to recent reviews for more extensive background.

## Method
The authors organized a five-week Institute for Nuclear Theory (INT) program in 2019 that brought together different approaches to nuclear structure and reactions. Workshop participants confronted an extended list of intertwined issues including degrees of freedom, Hamiltonians and currents for ab initio calculations, uncertainty quantification, ab initio methods and tools, lattice QCD, energy density functionals as EFTs, reactions and structure, and experimental impact. The paper summarizes the ideas for proceeding that emerged from presentations and discussions at this program.

## Key numbers
- Three decades: the time since Weinberg's groundbreaking papers on chiral EFT (1990-1992) to the 2019 INT program
- Five weeks: duration of the INT program "Nuclear Structure at the Crossroads"
- A ~ 100: the mass number reach of ab initio methods applicable from light to medium-mass nuclei
- A = 4: the mass number up to which the role of the unitary limit in nuclei is well established
- A = 16: the mass number for which pionless EFT seems to fail binding at leading order (Contessi 2017, Yang 2020)
- N^2LO and N^3LO: the orders in chiral EFT to which most calculations using chiral NN and 3N interactions have been performed

## Context
This paper extends the work initiated by Weinberg (1990-1992) on nuclear forces from chiral Lagrangians and summarizes discussions from the 2019 INT program. It builds on recent reviews by Machleidt (2016), Epelbaum (2019), Hammer (2019), Hergert (2020), Hebeler (2020), and Tews (2020). The paper identifies priorities for moving forward in low-energy nuclear theory, particularly regarding the role of EFT, and points to the 2021 INT program "Nuclear Forces for Precision Nuclear Physics" as a follow-up.
