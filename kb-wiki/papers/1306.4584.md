# Topology Change and Tensor Forces for the EoS of Dense Baryonic Matter

> When skyrmions representing nucleons are put on crystal lattice and compressed to simulate high density, there is a transition above the normal nuclear matter density $n_0$ from a matter consisting of skyrmions with integer baryon charge to a state of half-skyrmions with half-integer baryon charge. We exploit this observation in an effective field theory formalism to access dense baryonic system. We find that the topology change involved implies a changeover from a Fermi liquid structure to a non-Fermi liquid with the chiral condensate in the nucleon "melted off." The $\sim 80%$ of the nucleon mass that remains, invariant under chiral transformation, points to the origin of the (bulk of) proton mass that is not encoded in the standard mechanism of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. The topology change engenders a drastic modification of the nuclear tensor forces, thereby nontrivially affecting the EoS, in particular, the symmetry energy, for compact star matter. It brings in stiffening of the EoS needed to accommodate a neutron star of $\sim 2$ solar mass. The strong effect on the EoS in general and in the tensor force structure in particular will also have impact on processes that could be measured at RIB-type accelerators.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density above normal nuclear matter density (n₀) signals a transition from Fermi liquid to non-Fermi liquid behavior, which drastically modifies nuclear tensor forces and stiffens the equation of state (EoS) for compact stars. The hedge is that this topology change is inferred from skyrmion crystal calculations and translated into parameter changes in an effective field theory, but the precise scaling behavior and the breakdown of mean-field theory in the half-skyrmion phase remain conjectural.

## Method

The authors use a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian derived from holographic QCD, with parameters fixed by the pion decay constant fπ and the ρ-meson mass mρ, to describe baryons as skyrmions on a crystal lattice. They compress the lattice to simulate increasing density and identify a transition density n₁/₂ where skyrmions with integer baryon charge fractionize into half-skyrmions with half-integer baryon charge. The topology change is then "translated" into density-dependent scaling of the in-medium pion decay constant, nucleon mass, and vector meson couplings (the "BLPR scaling"), which are input into a Vlowk-EFT many-body calculation to compute the EoS and symmetry energy for compact-star matter.

## Key numbers

- The skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition density is in the range 1.5 ≤ n₁/₂/n₀ ≤ 2.0, where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For n < n₁/₂, the in-medium pion decay constant scales as fπ*/fπ ≈ 1/(1 + c n/n₀) with c ≈ 0.2; for n ≥ n₁/₂, it stays constant at fπ*/fπ ≈ 0.8.
- The in-medium nucleon mass scales identically: mN*/mN ≈ 1/(1 + c n/n₀) with c ≈ 0.2 for n < n₁/₂, and mN*/mN ≈ 0.8 for n ≥ n₁/₂, implying about 80% of the nucleon mass remains "unmelted" as the chiral condensate goes to zero.
- The symmetry energy S shows a cusp at n₁/₂, decreasing below n₁/₂ and then increasing as the pion tensor force dominates above n₁/₂.
- Neutron star masses up to ~2 solar masses are accommodated, with central densities reaching up to ~5.5 n₀ when topology change is included.

## Context

This work extends the earlier "Brown-Rho scaling" (BR91) by incorporating a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions, which was not accounted for in the original scaling. The new scaling, termed "BLPR scaling," is supported by skyrmion crystal calculations (Manton and Sutcliffe, 2004; Park and Vento, 2009) and by RG analyses of HLS theory (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003). The predicted stiffening of the EoS and the cusp in the symmetry energy are consistent with the skyrmion crystal results of Lee, Park, and Rho (2013). The approach enables a description of massive neutron stars (~2 M⊙) and suggests that mean-field approximations break down in the half-skyrmion phase, requiring higher-order many-body calculations. The finding that ~80% of the nucleon mass remains chiral-invariant points to an emergent mass not captured by the standard chiral symmetry breaking mechanism, reminiscent of the parity-doublet model (DeTar and Kunihiro, 1989).
