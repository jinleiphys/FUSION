# Ground-state properties and symmetry energy of neutron-rich and neutron-deficient Mg isotopes

> A comprehensive study of various ground-state properties of neutron-rich and neutron-deficient Mg isotopes with $A$=20-36 is performed in the framework of the self-consistent deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS method. The correlation between the skin thickness and the characteristics related with the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy is investigated for this isotopic chain following the theoretical approach based on the coherent density fluctuation model and using the Brueckner energy-density functional. The results of the calculations show that the behavior of the nuclear charge radii and the nuclear symmetry energy in the Mg isotopic chain is closely related to the nuclear deformation. We also study, within our theoretical scheme, the emergence of an "island of inversion" at neutron-rich $^{32}$Mg nucleus, that was recently proposed from the analyses of spectroscopic measurements of $^{32}$Mg low-lying energy spectrum and the charge rms radii of all magnesium isotopes in the $sd$ shell.

# Ground-state properties and symmetry energy of neutron-rich and neutron-deficient Mg isotopes

## Key claim
The deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS method with standard Skyrme forces (SLy4, SGII, Sk3) predicts spherical ground states for $^{32}$Mg, contradicting the experimentally observed "island of inversion" at N=20; only by artificially increasing the spin-orbit strength of the SLy4 interaction by 20% does the calculation produce a strongly deformed prolate intruder configuration ($\beta$=0.38) that reproduces the expected shape coexistence.

## Method
Self-consistent deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with density-dependent Skyrme interactions (SLy4, SGII, Sk3) and BCS pairing correlations were performed for Mg isotopes with A=20-36. The coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) was applied using the Brueckner energy-density functional to compute the nuclear symmetry energy in finite nuclei from the monopole term of the deformed density distributions. Additional calculations for $^{32}$Mg and $^{31}$Mg modified the spin-orbit strength of SLy4 by 20% to test the emergence of the "island of inversion."

## Key numbers
- Neutron skin thickness $\Delta R$ for $^{20}$Mg with SLy4 force: 0.33 fm (proton skin), nearly coinciding with the value of 0.34 fm from Lenske et al. (1998)
- Neutron skin thickness for $^{36}$Mg with SLy4 force: 0.39 fm (neutron skin)
- Quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ = 0.38 for the intruder configuration of $^{32}$Mg when spin-orbit strength of SLy4 is increased by 20%, close to $\beta$ = 0.32 from Yordanov et al. (2012)
- Charge rms radius of $^{31}$Mg increases from 3.117 fm to 3.154 fm when spin-orbit strength is increased by 20%
- Excited $0^{+}_{2}$ state in $^{32}$Mg found at 1.058 MeV from Wimmer et al. (2010)
- Empirical shell gap for $^{32}$Mg: 1.10(3) MeV from Chaudhuri et al. (2013)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of neutron skin-symmetry energy correlations in spherical Ni, Sn, Pb nuclei (Gaidarov et al., 2011) and deformed Kr, Sm isotopes (Gaidarov et al., 2012) to the lighter Mg isotopic chain. The calculations are compared with experimental charge radii from Yordanov et al. (2012) at ISOLDE-CERN, two-neutron separation energies from Audi et al. (2003), and matter radii from Suzuki et al. (1998). The results confirm that standard mean-field approaches fail to reproduce the "island of inversion" at N=20 without ad-hoc modifications, consistent with shell-model treatments by Warburton et al. (1990) and Caurier et al. (2014) showing that the N=20 and N=28 islands merge in the magnesium chain. The analysis enables future constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from light neutron-rich nuclei and provides a baseline for beyond-mean-field treatments incorporating shape mixing.
