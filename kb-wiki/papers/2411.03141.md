# Lattice QCD calculation of the subtraction function in forward Compton amplitude

> The subtraction function plays a pivotal role in calculations involving the forward Compton amplitude, which is crucial for predicting the Lamb shift in muonic atom, as well as the proton-neutron mass difference. In this work, we present a lattice QCD calculation of the subtraction function using two domain wall fermion gauge ensembles at the physical pion mass. We utilize a recently proposed subtraction point, demonstrating its advantage in mitigating statistical and systematic uncertainties by eliminating the need for ground-state subtraction. Our results reveal significant contributions from $Nπ$ intermediate states to the subtraction function. Incorporating these contributions, we compute the proton, neutron and nucleon isovector subtraction functions at photon momentum transfer $Q^2\in[0,2]$ GeV$^2$. For the proton subtraction function, we compare our lattice results with chiral perturbation theory prediction at low $Q^2$ and with the results from the perturbative operator-product expansion at high $Q^2$. Finally, using these subtraction functions as input, we determine their contribution to two-photon exchange effects in the Lamb shift and isovector nucleon electromagnetic self-energy.

# Lattice QCD calculation of the subtraction function in forward Compton amplitude

## Key claim

The authors present a lattice QCD calculation of the subtraction function for the proton, neutron, and isovector nucleon using a recently proposed subtraction point at ν₀ = i|Q|, which they demonstrate reduces statistical and systematic uncertainties by eliminating the need for ground-state subtraction. The results show significant contributions from Nπ intermediate states and provide new constraints in the intermediate momentum transfer region Q² ∈ [0,2] GeV² that is not well-covered by either chiral perturbation theory or perturbative operator-product expansion.

## Method

The calculation uses two 2+1-flavor domain wall fermion gauge ensembles from the RBC-UKQCD Collaboration at near-physical pion mass (142.6-142.9 MeV) with spatial volume L = 4.6 fm and lattice spacings a⁻¹ = 1.023(2) GeV and 1.378(5) GeV. The hadronic functions H_{μν}(x) are derived from four-point correlation functions using 1024 point-source and 1024 smeared-source propagators per configuration with the random sparsening-field technique. The subtraction function T₁(i|Q|,Q²) is computed using a hybrid method that combines lattice QCD input for the hadronic function H(t) with experimental input for the electric polarizability, anomalous magnetic moment, and charge radius, with optimal weighting parameters c₁ and c₂ determined by variance minimization.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD calculation of the forward Compton amplitude (Fu 2022) and provides the first full lattice QCD determination of the subtraction function at physical pion mass. It addresses a long-standing challenge in dispersive analyses of two-photon exchange effects, which previously relied on models leading to significant variation depending on the chosen approach (Chew 1957, Carlson 2011, Hill 2016). The results are compared with chiral perturbation theory predictions at low Q² (Biloshytskyi 2023) and perturbative operator-product expansion at high Q² (Hill 2016), while providing new constraints in the intermediate momentum region. The calculation enables improved precision for the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift determination of the proton charge radius and for the isovector nucleon electromagnetic self-energy, where the subtraction function previously introduced the largest uncertainty (Walker-Loud 2012, Gasser 2020).
