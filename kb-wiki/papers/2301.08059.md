# Relations for low-energy constants in baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit $Δ(1232)$ derived from the chiral quark model

> We study the relations between low-energy constants (LECs) in the chiral Lagrangians with $Δ(1232)$ and those in the quark-level description model up to the third chiral order. Ten structure correspondences are involved in getting the relations. This situation is more complicated than the spin-1/2 baryon case. The obtained results may help to further investigations involving the $Δ(1232)$ baryons.

## Key claim

The paper derives relations between low-energy constants (LECs) in baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom and those in the chiral quark model, up to third chiral order, but only under the assumption that two of the three quarks in the baryon act as spectators, with corrections from one-quark spectator and no-spectator cases deferred to future studies.

## Method

The authors compare hadron-level coupling matrix elements in Delta chiral perturbation theory (Delta chi PT) with quark-level matrix elements in the chiral quark model in the nonrelativistic limit, using the Rarita-Schwinger field for the spin-3/2 Delta baryon. They reorganize the Delta interaction terms from previous work (Jiang 2017, Jiang 2018) into forms that allow setting ten structure correspondences between hadron-level and quark-level operators, introducing multiplication factors k1 through k5 to account for possible inequivalence between the two descriptions. The LEC relations are then obtained by equating the coupling constants from the hadron-level Lagrangians (pi NN, pi Delta Delta, pi N Delta) to those from the quark-level Lagrangian (pi qq) through these correspondences.

## Key numbers

* The mass difference between Delta and nucleon is Delta = m_Delta - m_N approximately 294 MeV.
* The leading-order coupling constants are g_A (pi NN), g_A^q (pi qq), e_1^(1) (pi Delta Delta), and f_1^(1) (pi N Delta), with the relation g_A^q = (3/5) g_A in usual chiral quark models.
* The Delta fields in flavor space are represented by T^{abc} with T^{111} = Delta^{++}, T^{112} = T^{121} = T^{211} = Delta^+ / sqrt(3), T^{122} = T^{212} = T^{221} = Delta^0 / sqrt(3), T^{222} = Delta^-.

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to spin-1/2 baryons in Jiang (2022) to the more complicated case of spin-3/2 Delta baryons, where the Rarita-Schwinger field introduces redundant degrees of freedom and the structure correspondences are not one-to-one as in the spin-1/2 case. The results may help constrain the LECs in Delta chi PT, where experimental data for the unstable Delta(1232) are scarce, by relating them to better-known LECs in the pi NN sector through the chiral quark model. The approach complements resonance saturation estimates (Pich 2008, Ecker 1988, Bernard 1993, Liu 2010, Liu 2011, Du 2016) and large N_c expansion methods for determining LEC values.
