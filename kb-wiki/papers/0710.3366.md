# From Quark-Gluon Plasma to the Perfect Liquid

> After reviewing some basic concepts of the theory of strongly interacting matter above nuclear energy density and reviewing some salient results of the experimental program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), these lectures explain why the quark-gluon plasma observed in the RHIC experiments has been called a ``perfect liquid.'' They then give an introduction to some recent ideas concerning the possible origin of the nearly inviscid nature of the quark-gluon plasma and discuss the connection between low viscosity and strong parton energy loss of hot QCD matter.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC behaves as a nearly "perfect" liquid with almost undetectable viscosity, contradicting the perturbative QCD expectation that it should behave like a dilute gas; however, the claim is hedged by the observation that this behavior is observed only in the temperature domain accessible at RHIC (T ≤ 2Tc), and that the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio may be as large as a factor of four above the conjectured quantum lower bound.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD simulations of the equation of state and flavor susceptibilities for strongly interacting matter at zero baryon density, then surveys experimental results from the four RHIC experiments (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR) for Au+Au, d+Au, Cu+Cu, and p+p collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies from 19.6 to 200 GeV. The analysis focuses on three key observables: elliptic flow coefficients v2 as a function of transverse momentum and hadron mass, baryon-to-meson ratios at intermediate pT interpreted via quark recombination models, and the nuclear modification factor RAA for pions, eta mesons, and direct photons.

## Key numbers

- Energy density of ground state nuclear matter: ε0 ≈ 0.14 GeV/fm³
- Critical temperature for the QCD cross-over: Tc ≈ 160 MeV
- Energy density at Tc: approximately 1 GeV/fm³, roughly 7.5 times normal nuclear matter density
- Energy density achieved at RHIC: (30–100)ε0
- Temperature of the early universe exceeding 200 MeV for the first 10 μs after the Big Bang
- Thermalization time-scale at RHIC: less than 1 fm/c
- Baryon chemical potential at the conjectured critical endpoint: μB ≈ 300 MeV
- Shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio lower bound: 4πη/s ≥ 1 (Kovtun, Son, Starinets 2004)
- Perturbative QCD prediction for shear viscosity (leading logarithmic order, Nf=3): ηC ≈ 9s/(100π αs² ln αs⁻¹)
- Energy loss parameter q̂ extracted from RHIC data: 0.5–20 GeV²/fm (normalized to τ=1 fm/c)
- Baryon-to-meson ratios (p/π⁺ and p̄/π⁻) near unity at pT = 2–3 GeV/c in Au+Au collisions
- Elliptic flow scaling: v2(M)(pT) ≈ 2 v2(q)(pT/2) for mesons; v2(B)(pT) ≈ 3 v2(q)(pT/3) for baryons
- Color screening length: μG/Q⁻¹ < 0.5 fm
- Saturation field intensity from plasma instabilities: ⟨E²⟩ ≈ ⟨B²⟩ ∼ g²T⁴
- Correlation length of dynamically generated color fields: order (gT)⁻¹

## Context

This lecture paper reviews the transition from the perturbative expectation of a weakly interacting quark-gluon plasma to the discovery at RHIC of a strongly coupled, nearly inviscid fluid. It extends the lattice QCD equation-of-state calculations by the MILC and HotQCD collaborations (Aoki 2006, Cheng 2006) and the flavor susceptibility studies by Gavai (2005) and Majumder (2005, 2006). The work connects the low viscosity of the sQGP to strong parton energy loss via the transport parameter q̂, following the formalism of Baier (1996) and Majumder (2007). The paper also discusses the AdS/CFT duality result for N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (Policastro 2001, Buchel 2004) as a theoretical benchmark for the viscosity bound, and compares with the dilute Fermi gas of ⁶Li atoms at sub-μK temperatures as a real system approaching the same bound. The anomalous viscosity mechanism via color-field domains builds on the Weibel instability work of Mrowczynski (1993, 1994) and the saturation simulations by Arnold (2005) and Rebhan (2005).
