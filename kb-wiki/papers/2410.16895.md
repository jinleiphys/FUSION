# Interactions of omega mesons in nuclear matter and with nuclei

> In-medium interactions of omega-mesons in infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei are investigated in a microscopic approach with nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-resonance particle-hole polarization modes. The nuclear polarization tensor formalism is used. Covariant mean-field self-energies are taken into account. Longitudinal and transversal self-energies are derived. The theoretical results are applied to finite nuclei in local density approximation. Applications to recent data on the in-medium width of omega mesons in 93-Nb are presented. The data are well described by self-energies containing S-wave and P-wave resonances. Low-energy parameters are determined. For the first time a spectrum of omega-nucleus bound states is obtained, albeit with reduced lifetimes.

## Key claim
For the first time, a spectrum of omega-nucleus bound states is obtained, albeit with reduced lifetimes; the data on the in-medium width of omega mesons in 93-Nb are well described by self-energies containing S-wave and P-wave resonances, but the theoretical error band reveals large uncertainties mainly due to large errors of the data in the threshold region.

## Method
The nuclear polarization tensor formalism was used to describe omega-meson self-energies in infinite asymmetric nuclear matter and finite nuclei, with nucleon-nucleon (NN^{-1}) and nucleon-resonance (N*N^{-1}) particle-hole polarization modes. Covariant mean-field self-energies were taken into account, and longitudinal and transversal self-energies were derived. The theoretical results were applied to finite nuclei in local density approximation, and coupling constants to medium excitations were determined by fit to photo-production data for omega + 93-Nb from Friedrich et al. (2016).

## Key numbers
- In-medium width of omega meson at central density rho_A = 0.140 fm^{-3} of 93-Nb: compared to experimentally deduced values from Friedrich et al. (2016) as a function of omega momentum p_Lab.
- Scattering length: a_s = 5.6542 - i0.9041 fm; effective range: r_s = 3.7669 - i0.5759 fm.
- Bound state found at energy epsilon_B = -0.488 MeV with dissipative width Gamma^{(A)}_B = 4.445 MeV.
- Total widths of bound states: Gamma_{1s} = 28.28 MeV, Gamma_{1p} = 27.16 MeV, Gamma_{2s} = 24.06 MeV.
- Free omega decay width: Gamma_free = 8.68 MeV.
- In-medium contributions to bound state widths: Gamma^{(A)}_{1s} = 19.60 MeV, Gamma^{(A)}_{1p} = 18.48 MeV, Gamma^{(A)}_{2s} = 15.38 MeV.
- Lifetimes of bound states: c tau_tot ~ 5 to 6 fm, compared to c tau_free ~ 22 fm.

## Context
This work extends the nucleon-nucleon and resonance-nucleon particle-hole approach discussed in detail by Lenske (2023) to omega-meson interactions, following early theoretical studies such as Celenza (1991) for the omega-meson and Peters (1997) for the rho-meson. It contrasts with later studies by Klingl (1997), Lutz (2001), Muehlich (2006), Rapp (2009), and Ramos (2013) that focused on in-medium meson loop self-energies, which the author notes may partially overlap with the particle-hole self-energies and risk overcounting. The results enable further exploration of meson-nucleus bound states and in-medium studies of vector meson properties.
