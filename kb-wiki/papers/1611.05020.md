# Overview of electromagnetic probes in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> An introductory overview of electromagnetic probes in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is provided. Experimental evidence supporting the production of thermal photons and dileptons in heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are reviewed. Thermal electromagnetic emission evaluated from hydrodynamical models of collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

Thermal photons and dileptons emitted from the deconfined plasma in heavy-ion collisions can provide valuable information about the properties of hot nuclear matter, but the thermal signal is often outshone by other sources of electromagnetic radiation, making it difficult to isolate experimentally.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental evidence for thermal photons and dileptons at RHIC and the LHC, comparing measurements to predictions from hydrodynamical models of the collision evolution. Thermal electromagnetic emission is computed by integrating theoretical emission rates over the spacetime profile provided by hydrodynamics, using local values of temperature, flow velocity, and viscous corrections. The contributions from "cold" sources (hadronic decays, prompt photons, Drell-Yan, charm decays) are subtracted statistically or estimated from proton-proton collisions to isolate the thermal signal.

## Key numbers

- The deconfined plasma lifetime is approximately 10 fm, with an equilibration time for photons and dileptons of about 100-500 fm (for soft photons of 1-3 GeV energy and plasma temperatures of 150-400 MeV), making the plasma optically thin.
- The initial approach to local equilibrium occurs at proper time tau ~ 0.1-1 fm, and the hydrodynamical expansion lasts until tau ~ 10 fm.
- In Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, an excess of dileptons is observed in the invariant mass regions 0.3-0.6 GeV and 0.8-1.0 GeV, not seen in proton-proton collisions.
- Direct photon measurements in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV show a significant excess above prompt photons for p_T^gamma below about 1.5 GeV.
- The measured direct photon v_2 in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is of the same order as the pion v_2, while prompt photons are expected to have a v_2 about an order of magnitude lower than pions.

## Context

This overview extends the line of work using electromagnetic probes as direct signatures of the deconfined plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, building on hydrodynamical modeling approaches summarized by Denicol (2016) and de Souza (2015). The paper discusses how current hydrodynamical calculations generally underestimate the direct photon spectra and v_2 at RHIC and the LHC (Shen 2015, Shen 2016), while a recent hydrodynamic calculation by Vujanovic (2013) found good agreement with the low-invariant-mass dilepton spectra from STAR. The work enables more systematic comparisons of hydrodynamical models with both electromagnetic and hadronic observables to determine whether agreement requires modifying the spacetime description of the plasma, including new radiation sources, or improving thermal emission rates.
