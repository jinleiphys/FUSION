# Abundance Uncertainties Obtained With the PizBuin Framework For Monte Carlo Reaction Rate Variations

> Uncertainties in nucleosynthesis models originating from uncertainties in astrophysical reaction rates were estimated in a Monte Carlo variation procedure. Thousands of rates were simultaneously varied within individual, temperature-dependent errors to calculate their combined effect on final abundances. After a presentation of the method, results from application to three different nucleosynthesis processes are shown: the $γ$-process and the s-process in massive stars, and the main s-process in AGB stars (preliminary results). Thermal excitation of nuclei in the stellar plasma and the combined action of several reactions increase the final uncertainties above the level of the experimental errors. The total uncertainty, on the other hand, remains within a factor of two even in processes involving a large number of unmeasured rates, with some notable exceptions for nuclides whose production is spread over several stellar layers and for s-process branchings.

## Key claim
The paper claims that total abundance uncertainties from reaction rate variations remain within a factor of two even for processes involving many unmeasured rates, but this result carries the qualifier that notable exceptions occur for nuclides whose production is spread over several stellar layers and for s-process branchings, where uncertainties can be larger.

## Method
The authors implemented a Monte Carlo procedure in the PizBuin code suite that simultaneously varies thousands of reaction rates within individual, temperature-dependent uncertainty factors, using uniform distributions within the uncertainty limits. For each of 10000 MC iterations per trajectory, the code supplies a random variation factor for each rate, and a fast reaction network computes the resulting abundances. Key reactions are identified by calculating correlation factors between the variation factors of all reactions and the corresponding variations in final abundances, rather than by simple sensitivity analysis.

## Key numbers
- 10000 MC iterations per trajectory were confirmed sufficient to sample the space of possible reaction rate combinations
- For the gamma-process in 15 and 25 solar mass stars, overall uncertainties are "much smaller than a factor of two" despite most contributing reactions being unmeasured
- For weak reactions on excited states, the theory uncertainty factor was set to U_th^weak = 10
- For measured weak rates, U_g.s.^weak was taken as either the uncertainty of a measured half-life or a factor of 1.3
- The gamma-process study used trajectories from 15 solar mass and 25 solar mass KEPLER stellar evolution models
- The weak s-process study used a 25 solar mass star model
- The main s-process study used a 3 solar mass AGB model calculated with the MESA code

## Context
This work extends previous nucleosynthesis uncertainty studies that varied individual rates separately (e.g., Rapp, Reifarth, and Marco for the gamma-process) by introducing simultaneous Monte Carlo variation of all rates with temperature-dependent uncertainties. The method supersedes earlier approaches that relied on sensitivity slopes dY/dR to identify important reactions. The results enable improved planning of experimental efforts to constrain key reactions and provide a sound basis for further investigations in Galactic Chemical Evolution. The paper notes that previous work by Rauscher (2014, 2016) established the framework for combining experimental and theoretical uncertainties, and that the gamma-process study used trajectories from the KEPLER stellar evolution code as described by Rauscher and collaborators (2002) and Heger and Woosley (2007).
