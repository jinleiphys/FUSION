# Onset of deconfinement in nucleus-nucleus collisions: Review for pedestrians and experts

> Evidence for the energy threshold of creating the quark-gluon plasma in nucleus-nucleus collisions, the so-called onset of deconfinement, has been found by the energy scan program of the NA49 experiment at the CERN SPS. In this paper we review the experimental and theoretical status of this phenomenon. First, the basic, qualitative ideas are presented for non-experts. Next, the latest experimental results are compared to a statistical model within which the onset of deconfinement and its signals had been predicted. Finally, alternative interpretations and open questions are discussed.

## Key claim

The NA49 experiment at the CERN SPS has found evidence for the energy threshold of quark-gluon plasma creation in nucleus-nucleus collisions, the so-called onset of deconfinement, based on rapid changes in hadron production properties within a narrow energy range of \(\sqrt{s}_{NN}=\) 7–12 GeV. The authors hedge that the evidence is based on qualitative expectations and quantitative predictions of a specific statistical model, and that alternative interpretations and open questions remain.

## Method

The authors review the experimental energy scan program of the NA49 experiment at the CERN SPS, which measured central Pb+Pb collisions at several lower SPS energies (\(\sqrt{s}_{NN} =\) 6.3, 7.6, 8.7, and 12.3 GeV). They compare the measured energy dependence of hadron production properties, such as the \(\langle K^+ \rangle/\langle \pi^+ \rangle\) ratio and the apparent temperature of \(K^+\) mesons, to predictions of the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES), which assumes a first-order phase transition between a confined hadronic state (W-state) and a deconfined quark-gluon plasma state (Q-state) with an ideal gas equation of state modified by a bag constant.

## Key numbers

* The sharp maximum in the \(\langle K^+ \rangle/\langle \pi^+ \rangle\) ratio and the onset of rapid changes in hadron production occur within a narrow energy range of \(\sqrt{s}_{NN}=\) 7–12 GeV in central Pb+Pb collisions.
* The critical temperature of the deconfinement phase transition is estimated to be \(T \approx 2\cdot 10^{12}\) K (170 MeV), as suggested by Hagedorn.
* The bag constant \(B\) is adjusted to 600 MeV/fm\(^3\) to obtain a critical temperature \(T_c = 200\) MeV in the analytical calculations.
* The strange (anti-)quark mass in the Q-state is taken as 175 MeV, and the mass of strange degrees of freedom in the W-state is assumed to be 500 MeV (equal to the kaon mass).
* The internal number of degrees of freedom for the QGP is 48 (16 for gluons plus 7/8 of 36 for quarks), and for the W-state it is taken as 48/3 = 16 for non-strange degrees of freedom.

## Context

This work extends the search for the quark-gluon plasma that began at the CERN SPS and BNL AGS in the mid-1980s, following earlier proposals by Rafelski and Satz for QGP signatures. It directly tests predictions of the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES) proposed by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (mid-1990s), which predicted a non-monotonic energy dependence of strange hadron production, particularly a sharp maximum in the \(\langle K^+ \rangle/\langle \pi^+ \rangle\) ratio at about \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} \approx 7.5\) GeV. The results motivate ongoing and future experimental programs at the CERN SPS, BNL RHIC, FAIR SIS, and JINR NICA to study the onset of deconfinement in more detail.
