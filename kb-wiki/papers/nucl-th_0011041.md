# The inclusive reaction d(γ,π)NN in the first resonance region

> Inclusive single pion photoproduction on the deuteron is studied in the first resonance region. The calculation is based on the use of the diagrammatic approach. Pole diagrams and one-loop diagrams with NN rescattering in the final state are taken into account. The elementary operator for pion photoproduction from the nucleon is taken in on-shell form and calculated using the SAID and MAID multipole analyses. Our predictions for total and differential cross section show good agreement with the available experimental data. Invoking some information on the reactions $gamma d\toπ^0 d and $γd\to np$ we predict the total photoabsorption cross section for deuterium. We find that our results overestimate the experimental data in the center of the Δ-peak (\sim 320 MeV) by about 10%.

## Key claim

The authors' diagrammatic model for inclusive single pion photoproduction on the deuteron, using on-shell elementary amplitudes from SAID and MAID multipole analyses and including nucleon-nucleon final-state interaction, achieves good agreement with available differential and total cross section data, but overestimates the experimental total photoabsorption cross section for deuterium at the center of the Δ-peak (∼320 MeV) by about 10%.

## Method

The calculation employs a diagrammatic approach retaining pole diagrams and one-loop diagrams with NN rescattering in the final state, using the non-relativistic Bonn OBEPR deuteron wave function and solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the NN scattering amplitude in partial waves with total angular momentum J=0 and 1. The elementary γN→πN amplitude is taken in on-shell CGLN form with partial amplitudes F_i(ω,Θ_π) from the SAID and MAID multipole analyses, and the inclusive cross section is obtained by integrating the five-fold differential cross section over pion momentum and nucleon solid angle.

## Key numbers

- The model overestimates the experimental total photoabsorption cross section per nucleon for deuterium at the Δ-peak center (∼320 MeV) by 33 μb (SAID) and 48 μb (MAID) relative to the experimental value of (452±5) μb.
- For the d(γ,π^0)np channel total cross section at the peak, inclusion of FSI reduces the overestimate to about 25 μb above the measured data.
- The reconstructed total photoabsorption cross section per nucleon at 320 MeV from separate channel data is (487±17) μb, compared to the authors' predictions of 485 μb (SAID) and 500 μb (MAID).
- The numerical integration accuracy for differential cross sections is better than 2%.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Laget (1981) and Schmidt (1996) for incoherent π^0 photoproduction on the deuteron, which significantly overestimated data in the Δ-region, by replacing the Blomqvist-Laget parametrization with more realistic SAID and MAID multipole analyses for the elementary amplitude. The results enable improved predictions for all inclusive d(γ,π)NN channels and, combined with coherent π^0 production calculations by Kamalov (1997) and a phenomenological fit for γd→np by Rossi (1989), allow a critical comparison of the total photoabsorption cross section for deuterium with direct measurements, highlighting an unresolved 10% discrepancy at the Δ-peak that suggests possible inconsistencies among experimental datasets.
