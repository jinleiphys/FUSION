# Microscopic description of the twist mode in normal and superfluid trapped Fermi gases

> We investigate the "twist" mode (rotation of the upper against the lower hemisphere) of a dilute atomic Fermi gas in a spherical trap. The normal and superfluid phases are considered. The linear response to this external perturbation is calculated within the microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach. In the normal phase the excitation spectrum is concentrated in a rather narrow peak very close to the trapping frequency. In the superfluid phase the strength starts to be damped and fragmented and the collectivity of the mode is progressively lost when the temperature decreases. In the weak-pairing regime some reminiscence of the collective motion still exists, whereas in the strong-pairing regime the twist mode is completely washed out. The disappearance of the twist mode in the strong-pairing regime with decreasing temperature is interpreted in the framework of the two-fluid model.

## Key claim

The twist mode in a trapped superfluid Fermi gas progressively loses collectivity as temperature decreases below the critical temperature, and in the strong-pairing regime (where the pairing gap exceeds the trap frequency) the mode completely disappears at zero temperature, which the authors interpret via the two-fluid model as arising because only the normal-fluid component participates in the twist motion.

## Method

The authors solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Bogoliubov-de Gennes) equations self-consistently for a dilute atomic Fermi gas in a spherical harmonic trap, using a zero-range interaction parametrized by the s-wave scattering length. They then compute the linear response (free quasiparticle response function) to the twist operator \(z L_z\), which generates a counterrotation of the upper and lower hemispheres. Because the zero-range interaction does not couple to the twist operator, the QRPA response reduces to the free quasiparticle response, and the strength function \(S(\omega) = -\Im \Pi(\omega)/\pi\) is evaluated for both normal and superfluid phases at various temperatures and particle numbers.

## Key numbers

* For \(10^5\) atoms of \(^6\)Li (scattering length \(a = -2160 a_0\), trap frequency \(\Omega = 2\pi \times 144\) Hz, coupling \(g = -0.4\)) in the normal phase at zero temperature, the average twist frequency is \(\omega_\text{av} = 1.088\) in units of \(\hbar\Omega\), in agreement with the fluid-dynamical prediction \(\omega_\text{fd} = 1.087\).
* For \(2\times 10^5\) atoms under the same conditions, \(\omega_\text{av} = 1.100\) and \(\omega_\text{fd} = 1.101\).
* In the weak-pairing regime (about 1800 atoms, \(\mu = 16\), coupling \(g = -1\)), the pairing field at the trap center is \(\Delta(r=0) = 0.67\) at \(T=0\), \(0.26\) at \(T=0.13\), and \(0\) at \(T=0.15\) (all in units of \(\hbar\Omega\)); the strength function peak shifts from \(\omega \approx 1.12\) in the normal phase to higher energies as temperature decreases.
* In the strong-pairing regime (about \(3.6\times 10^4\) atoms, \(\mu = 40\), \(g = -1\)), the central pairing field values are \(\Delta(r=0) = 12.7\) at \(T=0\), \(12.5\) at \(T=2\), \(9.6\) at \(T=5\), and \(0\) at \(T=6.5\); the collective peak near \(\omega = 1.2\) in the normal phase disappears completely at \(T=0\), leaving only a fragmented low-strength structure between \(\omega = 2\) and \(\omega = 10\).

## Context

This work extends prior fluid-dynamical calculations of the twist mode in trapped Fermi gases by Vinas and Roth (2003), which treated only the normal phase, and complements studies of the spin-dipole mode in superfluid gases by Bruun and Mottelson (2001). The authors also draw on nuclear-physics studies of the twist mode in magic nuclei by Schwesinger (1980) and Ponomarev (1998), where pairing was neglected. The results enable experimental detection of the superfluid transition: the presence of the twist mode in the normal phase signals the collisionless regime, and its disappearance at lower temperatures would indicate the onset of superfluidity, particularly in the strong-pairing regime where the irrotational flow limit is reached.
