# Towards an optical potential for rare-earths through coupled channels

> The coupled-channel theory is a natural way of treating nonelastic channels, in particular those arising from collective excitations, defined by nuclear deformations. Proper treatment of such excitations is often essential to the accurate description of reaction experimental data. Previous works have applied different models to specific nuclei with the purpose of determining angular-integrated cross sections. In this work, we present an extensive study of the effects of collective couplings and nuclear deformations on integrated cross sections as well as on angular distributions in a consistent manner for neutron-induced reactions on nuclei in the rare-earth region. This specific subset of the nuclide chart was chosen precisely because of a clear static deformation pattern. We analyze the convergence of the coupled-channel calculations regarding the number of states being explicitly coupled. Inspired by the work done by Dietrich \emph{et al.}, a model for deforming the spherical Koning-Delaroche optical potential as function of quadrupole and hexadecupole deformations is also proposed. We demonstrate that the obtained results of calculations for total, elastic and inelastic cross sections, as well as elastic and inelastic angular distributions correspond to a remarkably good agreement with experimental data for scattering energies above around a few MeV.

## Key claim

Deforming the spherical Koning-Delaroche optical potential by replacing the radius parameter with an angle-dependent expression that includes quadrupole and hexadecupole deformations, and using it in coupled-channel calculations without any further parameter adjustment, yields remarkably good agreement with experimental data for neutron-induced reactions on rare-earth nuclei for scattering energies above around a few MeV, consistent with the adiabatic (frozen nucleus) limit.

## Method

The authors deformed the spherical Koning-Delaroche (KD) global optical potential by replacing the radius parameter in each Woods-Saxon form factor with an angle-dependent expression containing quadrupole (\(\beta_2\)) and hexadecupole (\(\beta_4\)) deformation parameters, with a correction applied to the reduced radius to ensure volume conservation. Coupled-channel calculations coupling the ground state rotational band were performed using the EMPIRE reaction code (with ECIS for direct reactions) for neutron-induced reactions on 38 rare-earth nuclides, and the results were compared with experimental data for total, elastic, and inelastic cross sections as well as elastic and inelastic angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for neutron-induced reactions on \(^{184}\)W showed "dramatic improvement" over spherical model calculations, with the coupled-channel results (black curve) closely following experimental data while spherical calculations (green curve) deviated significantly.
- For \(^{174}\)Yb total cross sections, convergence of coupled-channel calculations required coupling to 5-6 rotational states; coupling to a sixth state was necessary in the incident energy region between 10 keV and approximately 5 MeV.
- For \(^{158}\)Gd, deformation parameters used were \(\beta_2 = 0.348\) (from Raman et al.) and \(\beta_4 = 0.056\) (from Smith, 2004); the first 2\(^+\) and 4\(^+\) states have excitation energies of 79.5 keV and 261.5 keV, respectively.
- For \(^{184}\)W, deformation parameters used were \(\beta_2 = 0.236\) (from Raman et al.) and \(\beta_4 = -0.080\) (from Hendrie, 1968); the 2\(^+\), 4\(^+\), and 6\(^+\) states have excitation energies of 111.2 keV, 364.1 keV, and 748.3 keV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dietrich et al. (2012), who showed that scattering from rare-earth and actinide nuclei is very near the adiabatic (frozen nucleus) limit, suggesting that loss of flux to rotational excitations might be unimportant. It contrasts with the alternative approach of Kunieda et al. (2007), who attempted to unify scattering from spherical and deformed nuclei by treating all nuclei as statically deformed. The results demonstrate that deforming the spherical KD potential without further adjustment provides a consistent first step toward an optical potential for the rare-earth region, filling a current lack of optical model potentials in this important region, and enabling future work on angular distributions for additional nuclei such as Gd, Ho, and W.
