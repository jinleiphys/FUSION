# Lambda(1520) photoproduciton off the proton target with Regge contributions

> We investigate the Lambda(1520,3/2^-)=Lambda* photoproduction off the proton target with the Regge contributions to go beyond the resonance region, in addition to the tree-level Born terms consisting of the s, t, and u channels, and contact term. For this purpose, the Reggeized propagators for the K and K* exchanges in the t channel are employed in a gauge-invariant manner. We also take into account the contribution from a nucleon resonance D_{13}(2030). We compute the angular and energy dependences of the production process, including polarization observables, such as the photon-beam asymmetry and the polarization-transfer coefficients, resulting in good qualitative agreement with current experimental data. It turns out that the Regge contributions are necessary to explain the high-energy data beyond E_{gamma}~4 GeV especially for the angular dependences in the forward-scattering region. On the contrary, the polarization observables are insensitive to the Regge contributions due to the contact-term dominance and gauge invariance. We also compute the K^{-}-angle distribution function in the Gottfried-Jackson frame, using the polarization-transfer coefficients in the z direction. It shows nontrivial theta and E_{gamma} dependences in the forward-scattering region.

## Key claim

The Regge contributions are necessary to explain the high-energy data for Lambda(1520) photoproduction beyond E_gamma ~ 4 GeV, especially for angular dependences in the forward-scattering region, but the polarization observables are insensitive to these Regge contributions due to contact-term dominance and gauge invariance.

## Method

The authors computed the gamma p -> K+ Lambda(1520) reaction using a model that includes tree-level Born terms (s, t, and u channels), a contact term, a nucleon resonance D13(2030) contribution, and Reggeized propagators for K and K* exchanges in the t channel. The Reggeized propagators were implemented with a smooth interpolation between the resonance and high-energy regions using hyperbolic tangent functions, and gauge invariance was maintained by redefining the relevant amplitudes. The model parameters were fixed using experimental data from CLAS, LEPS, and earlier experiments.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g_{KNLambda*} extracted from decay widths is approximately 11.
- The gamma K*+ K- coupling constant g_{gamma K*+ K-} = 0.254/GeV.
- The D13(2030) resonance full decay width was set to Gamma_{D13} ~ 500 MeV.
- The Regge trajectory slopes are alpha_K = 0.70 GeV^{-2}(t - M_K^2) and alpha_{K*} = 1 + 0.85 GeV^{-2}(t - M_{K*}^2).
- The cutoff parameter s_0 for the Regge propagators was chosen as 1 GeV.
- The interpolation parameters were s_Regge = 9 GeV^2 (corresponding to sqrt(s) > 3 GeV), t_Regge = 0.1 GeV^2, s_0 = 1.0 GeV^2, t_0 = 0.08 GeV^2, and cutoff mass Lambda = 675 GeV.
- The photon-beam asymmetry Sigma was measured to be -0.01 +/- 0.07 for theta less than about 60 degrees, and between -0.1 and 0.1 for E_gamma = 1.75 to 2.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic model of Nam (2005) and Nam (2006) for Lambda(1520) photoproduction beyond the resonance region by incorporating Regge theory, following the hybrid approach of Corthals (2005) and Corthals (2006) for other hyperon photoproduction reactions. The results contradict the simple Born-term-only model at high energies (E_gamma ~ 11 GeV) where Boyarski (1970) data show a bump structure near |t| ~ 0.2 GeV^2 that only the Regge-included model reproduces. This enables future studies of Lambda(1520) production at higher energies and provides a framework for analyzing polarization observables in the forward-scattering region.
