# Presupernova neutrinos: realistic emissivities from stellar evolution

> We present a new calculation of neutrino emissivities and energy spectra from a massive star going through the advanced stages of nuclear burning (presupernova) in the months before becoming a supernova. The contributions from beta decay and electron capture, pair annihilation, plasmon decay, and the photoneutrino process are modeled in detail, using updated tabulated nuclear rates. We also use realistic conditions of temperature, density, electron fraction and nuclear isotopic composition of the star from the state of the art stellar evolution code MESA. Results are presented for a set of progenitor stars with mass between 15 $M_\odot$ and 30 $M_\odot$. It is found that beta processes contribute substantially to the neutrino emissivity above realistic detection thresholds of few MeV, at selected positions and times in the evolution of the star.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first comprehensive calculation of presupernova neutrino emissivities that combines detailed beta-process modeling (using updated nuclear rate tables) with realistic, time-evolving stellar profiles from the MESA code, finding that beta processes contribute substantially to the neutrino emissivity above realistic detection thresholds of a few MeV at selected positions and times, rather than being universally dominant.

## Method

The authors used the stellar evolution code MESA (version r7624) to simulate progenitor stars of 15, 20, 25, and 30 solar masses from pre-main-sequence to core collapse, with a 204-isotope reaction network. They calculated neutrino emissivities and energy spectra from beta decay and electron capture using the rate tables of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1980-1985), Oda et al. (1994), and Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2001), and from thermal processes (pair annihilation, plasmon decay, photoneutrino) using analytic and semi-analytic formalisms from the literature, with the differential rates computed via Monte Carlo integration where necessary.

## Key numbers

- Progenitor masses considered: 15, 20, 25, and 30 solar masses
- The MESA simulation used a reaction network of 204 isotopes up to zinc-66
- The star's evolution was simulated from pre-main-sequence to core collapse, defined as when infall velocity exceeds 1 × 10^8 cm/s
- The spatial resolution at core collapse was approximately 1000-2000 zones
- The detection threshold considered for neutrino spectra is 2 MeV
- Central temperature immediately before collapse is approximately 4-5 × 10^9 K
- Central density immediately before collapse is approximately 10^7 g/cm^3
- The suppression of muon and tau neutrino flavors in plasmon decay is by a factor of (C_V^x/C_V^e)^2 ≈ 3 × 10^-3
- The maximum plasmon energy for longitudinal plasmons is less than 1 MeV for typical presupernova conditions

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Odrzywolek and Heger (2009, 2010) that used nuclear statistical equilibrium or alpha-networks to determine isotopic composition, which those authors explicitly stated were inadequate. It also goes beyond the thermal-process-only calculations of Kato et al. (2015) and the pair-annihilation-focused work of Odrzywolek et al. (2003, 2004) by including a full, self-consistent stellar evolution simulation with a large nuclear reaction network. The results enable realistic predictions for presupernova neutrino detection, particularly for the closest supernova candidate Betelgeuse, and provide a direct probe of nuclear fusion beyond hydrogen and helium in the months before core collapse.
