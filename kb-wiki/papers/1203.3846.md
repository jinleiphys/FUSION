# Liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter with density-dependent relativistic mean-field models

> The liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter is studied within density-dependent relativistic mean-field models where the density dependence is introduced according to the Brown-Rho scaling and constrained by available data at low densities and empirical properties of nuclear matter. The critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition is obtained to be 15.7 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter falling on the lower edge of the small experimental error bars. In hot asymmetric matter, the boundary of the phase-coexistence region is found to be sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The critical pressure and the area of phase-coexistence region increases clearly with the softening of the symmetry energy. The critical temperature of hot asymmetric matter separating the gas phase from the LG coexistence phase is found to be higher for the softer symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter is 15.7 MeV, which falls on the lower edge of the experimental error bars (16.6 ± 0.9 MeV); in hot asymmetric matter, the critical temperature separating the single-phase region from the two-phase region is higher for models with a softer symmetry energy, but the sensitivity is only moderate.

## Method

The authors used density-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (SLC and SLCd) where the density dependence follows the Brown-Rho scaling, with parameters constrained by low-density data and empirical nuclear matter properties. They solved the Gibbs conditions for phase coexistence in hot asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, using a geometrical construction method on proton and neutron chemical potential isobars to determine the binodal surface and critical points.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter: 15.7 MeV, for both SLC and SLCd models.
- Experimental critical temperature for symmetric matter: 16.6 ± 0.9 MeV (Jagannan et al., 2002).
- At T = 10 MeV, example pressures used in the geometrical construction: p = 0.235, 0.175, 0.12, 0.098, and 0.08 MeV fm⁻³.
- The symmetry energy expansion was performed at a reference density ρ_h = 0.08 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter using non-relativistic models (Xu et al., 2007) and nonlinear RMF models (Bao et al., 2009), by employing density-dependent RMF models with Brown-Rho scaling that successfully describe finite nuclei and neutron stars (Jiang et al., 2007a, 2007b). The results show that the binodal surface is very sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, while the critical temperature shows only moderate sensitivity, suggesting that the phase coexistence boundary may help constrain the symmetry energy.
