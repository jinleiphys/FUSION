# Second-Order Dissociation and Transition of Heavy Quarkonia in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We revisit the dissociation of heavy quarkonia by thermal partons at the next-to-leading order (NLO, also known as inelastic parton scattering dissociation) in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). Utilizing the chromo-electric dipole coupling from QCD multipole expansion as an effective Hamiltonian, this has been conducted in the approach of second-order quantum mechanical perturbation theory, which allows us to systematically incorporate the bound state wave functions. Employing the quarkonium wave functions and binding energies obtained from an in-medium potential model, we then numerically evaluate the dissociation cross sections and rates for various charmonia and bottomonia, where the infrared and collinear divergences are regularized by the thermal masses of medium partons. We demonstrate that distinct from the leading order (LO, also known as gluo-dissociation) counterparts peaking at relatively low gluon energy and falling off thereafter, the NLO cross sections first grow and then nearly saturate as the incident parton energy increases, as a result of the outgoing parton carrying away the excess energy. The resulting NLO dissociation rates increase with temperature and take over from the LO counterparts toward high temperatures, similar to pertinent findings from previous studies. We also evaluate the in-medium second-order transition between different bound states, which may contribute to the total thermal decay widths of heavy quarkonia in the QGP.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that next-to-leading order (NLO) dissociation cross sections for heavy quarkonia by thermal partons in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) first grow and then nearly saturate as incident parton energy increases, unlike leading order (LO) gluo-dissociation which peaks at low gluon energy and falls off; this behavior results from the outgoing parton carrying away excess energy, and the NLO rates take over from LO counterparts toward high temperatures.

## Method

The authors employed second-order quantum mechanical perturbation theory using the chromo-electric dipole coupling from QCD multipole expansion as an effective Hamiltonian, constructing Feynman diagrams from four coupling vertices (V_SO, V_OO, V_3g, and V_q\bar{q}g). They derived transition amplitudes for NLO dissociation of heavy quarkonia by thermal gluons and quarks/antiquarks, using bound state wave functions and binding energies from an in-medium potential model, with infrared and collinear divergences regularized by thermal masses of medium partons. The calculations were performed in the rest frame of the quarkonium, neglecting three-momentum transfer from the incident parton.

## Key numbers

- The NLO dissociation cross sections for various charmonia and bottomonia were numerically evaluated using wave functions and binding energies from an in-medium potential model, with thermal masses of medium partons used to regularize divergences.
- The NLO dissociation rates increase with temperature and take over from LO counterparts toward high temperatures, similar to findings from previous studies.
- The in-medium second-order transition between different bound states was also evaluated, which may contribute to the total thermal decay widths of heavy quarkonia in the QGP.

## Context

This work extends the approach first introduced in Chen (2018) for studying NLO break-up of heavy quarkonium by thermal gluons, generalizing it to include thermal quarks and antiquarks. It differs from the "quasifree" treatment of Grandchamp (2001) and Riek (2010) by systematically incorporating bound state wave functions, and from the imaginary potential method of Laine (2006) and Brambilla (2013) by directly calculating cross sections from dynamical scattering processes. The results provide improved dissociation rates for use in semiclassical and quantum transport descriptions of heavy quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions, addressing discrepancies noted in Andronic (2024).
