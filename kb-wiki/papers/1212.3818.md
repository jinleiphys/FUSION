# The shear channel spectral function in hot Yang-Mills theory

> We determine a next-to-leading order result for the shear channel thermal spectral function in SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, working in the limit of vanishing external three-momentum. The result is subsequently applied to the evaluation of the corresponding imaginary time correlator, and its use in the context of sum rules is discussed. Our hope is that the calculation will eventually find use in the nonperturbative determination of the shear viscosity of the theory.

## Key claim

The paper determines a next-to-leading order (NLO) result for the shear channel thermal spectral function in SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, but only in the limit of vanishing external three-momentum and for frequencies ω ≳ T, because the perturbative expansion breaks down at ω = O(gT) and requires Hard Thermal Loop resummation that the authors do not perform.

## Method

The authors compute the Euclidean momentum-space shear correlator from one- and two-loop Feynman diagrams in dimensionally regularized SU(N) Yang-Mills theory at nonzero temperature, then analytically continue to Minkowski space to obtain the spectral function. They generalize the master-integral techniques developed for the bulk channel by Laine et al. (2011), introducing a fictitious mass parameter to regulate infrared divergences and using projection-operator averaging over the direction of the external three-momentum (set to zero). The most complicated finite parts are evaluated numerically, while divergent terms and logarithms of the renormalization scale are handled analytically.

## Key numbers

* The NLO shear spectral function is given by ρ_η(ω)/(4d_A) = (ω^4/(4π))(1 + 2n_(ω/2)){−1/10 + (g^2 N_c/(4π)^2)(2/9 + φ_T^η(ω/T))}, where n_x = 1/(e^(βx)−1) and d_A = N_c^2−1.
* In the UV limit ω → ∞, the temperature-dependent function φ_T^η(ω/T) vanishes as 41π^6 T^6/(3 ω^6) + O(T^8/ω^8), confirming the expectation of Caron-Huot (2009).
* In the IR limit ω → 0, φ_T^η(ω/T) behaves as 7π^2 T^3/(6 ω^3) + O(T^2/ω^2), causing the NLO spectral function to approach a positive constant of O(g^2).
* The deformed imaginary time correlator G_η^def(τ), integrated from ω_0 ≈ 0.6 T, shows exceptionally good agreement between LO and NLO results, with the NLO correction being highly suppressed.

## Context

This work extends the NLO bulk-channel spectral function calculation of Laine et al. (2011) to the technically more complicated shear channel, building on the operator product expansion analysis of the shear correlator by Schroder et al. (2011). The result is intended for eventual use in nonperturbative determinations of the shear viscosity from lattice data, following the analytic continuation method of Burnier et al. (2011), and can be compared to existing lattice and AdS/CFT calculations of Euclidean correlators by Meyer (2007), Huebner et al. (2008), and Kajantie et al. (2011).
