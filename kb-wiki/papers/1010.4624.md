# Role of center vortices in chiral symmetry breaking in SU(3) gauge theory

> We study the behavior of the AsqTad quark propagator in Landau gauge on SU(3) Yang-Mills gauge configurations under the removal of center vortices. In SU(2) gauge theory, center vortices have been observed to generate chiral symmetry breaking and dominate the infrared behavior of the quark propagator. In contrast, we report a weak dependence on the vortex content of the gauge configurations, including the survival of dynamical mass generation on configurations with vanishing string tension.

## Key claim

In SU(3) gauge theory, removing center vortices (defined via mesonic Maximal Center Gauge) eliminates the confining string tension but leaves dynamical chiral symmetry breaking largely intact, with the quark mass function at the smallest momentum suppressed by only about 15% compared to full configurations, a sharp contrast to SU(2) where vortex removal restores chiral symmetry.

## Method

The authors generated SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice configurations using the Lüscher-Weisz mean-field improved action, then fixed them to Direct Maximal Center Gauge (mesonic version) and projected the links onto the nearest Z₃ center element. They decomposed each configuration into vortex-only (center-projected) and vortex-removed (original links times the conjugate of the center projection) parts, rotated both to Landau gauge, and computed the AsqTad quark propagator and the gluon propagator on each ensemble.

## Key numbers

- The infrared value of the mass function on full configurations is around 350 MeV, consistent with the constituent quark model.
- After vortex removal, the mass function at the smallest nontrivial momentum (q²_min = 0.10 GeV²) is suppressed by about 15% compared to full configurations, with dynamical mass generation still clearly present.
- The vortex area density ρ/σ is approximately 0.38–0.40 across lattice spacings from 0.093 fm to 0.272 fm, independent of the lattice spacing.
- Vortex-only configurations recover roughly 60% of the full string tension, while vortex-removed configurations show no sign of a confining potential.
- The gluon dressing function shows strong suppression of infrared enhancement upon vortex removal, similar to the SU(2) case.

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) center vortex studies of Bowman (2008) and deForcrand (1999) to SU(3), where earlier work by Langfeld (2003) and Cais (2007, 2008) had shown that vortex-only SU(3) configurations recover only about 2/3 of the string tension. The finding that chiral symmetry breaking survives vortex removal in SU(3), unlike in SU(2), raises the possibility of separately studying confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in SU(3) gauge theory, and suggests that either an improved definition of SU(3) vortices is needed or that these phenomena genuinely decouple for the SU(3) gauge group.
