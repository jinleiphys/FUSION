# Lasers as a Bridge between Atomic and Nuclear Physics

> This paper reviews the application of optical and UV laser radiation to several topics in low-energy nuclear physics. We consider the laser-induced nuclear anti-Stokes transitions, the laser-assisted and the laser-induced internal conversion, and the Electron Bridge and Inverse Electron Bridge mechanisms as tools for deexciting and exciting of low-lying nuclear isomeric states. A study of the anomalous, by low-lying, nuclear isomeric states (on an example of the $^{229}$Th nucleus) is presented in detail.

## Key claim

This review argues that direct optical and UV laser radiation (photon energies ~1-10 eV) can mediate low-energy nuclear transitions through the atomic electron shell, but the effectiveness is severely limited: laser-induced electromagnetic anti-Stokes transitions require extremely high intensities (I ≳ 10^19 W/cm^2) to dominate over spontaneous decay, and the factor (λ_ca/λ_c)^3 in the enhancement ratio makes the effect noticeable only for nuclear level spacings ≲ 10-10^-2 eV.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical frameworks for several laser-nuclear coupling mechanisms: laser-induced nuclear anti-Stokes transitions (using Bethe-Lamb equations for a three-level nuclear system), laser-assisted internal conversion (where laser photons compensate the energy deficit between nuclear transition energy and electron binding energy), and the Electron Bridge and Inverse Electron Bridge mechanisms (where bound electrons mediate nuclear excitation or deexcitation via virtual photon exchange). The review applies these methods specifically to the anomalously low-lying isomeric state of ^229Th, using non-covariant perturbation theory with one-resonance-level approximations and dipole interaction Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- Direct laser-nucleus coupling matrix element magnitude: M ≈ (10^-12 - 10^-13) I^1/2 eV, giving coupling strength M/ΔE_A ≲ (10^-15 - 10^-16) I^1/2 for nuclear level spacing ΔE_A ≳ 1 keV
- Compton excitation cross section for ^161Dy (E_FI = 43.8 keV, bound-bound transition): σ_γ = 110 μb
- Compton excitation cross section for ^185Tm (E_FI = 8.42 keV, bound-bound transition): σ_γ = 196 μb
- Compton excitation cross section for ^193Pt (E_FI = 12.7 keV, bound-bound transition): σ_γ = 62 μb
- For bound-free transitions, cross sections range from 5×10^-2 μb (^57Fe) to 1 μb (^129Xe)
- For laser-induced anti-Stokes transitions, η ~ 1 (equal rates of two-step and one-step processes) requires I ≳ 10^14 W/cm^2 under resonant conditions; domination of anti-Stokes process requires I ≳ 10^19 W/cm^2
- For ^111mPd isomeric state: γ_c^-1 ≈ 1.5×10^-11 s, Γ_ca = 2.3×10^-6 eV (E1), Γ_cb = 4.3×10^-5 eV (E2), Γ_c ≈ 4.5×10^-5 eV; condition |V_ac| ≳ Γ_c corresponds to I ≳ 1.3×10^19 W/cm^2
- Direct Compton excitation cross section for γ-rays (E_F - E_I - ħω_L ≈ 10^5 eV): σ_γγ^A ≈ 10^-39 - 10^-40 cm^2

## Context

This review consolidates theoretical work spanning from Einstein's 1925 speculation about optically induced radioactivity to Soviet and American studies from the 1970s-1990s, including the controversial claims (1977-1983) about laser modification of β-decay total rates that were later refuted by investigations showing no field-dependent enhancement in the long-wavelength limit (JLF, IMT). The paper extends earlier work on Compton excitation of nuclei (ISB) and the Bethe-Lamb treatment of laser-induced transitions (Bec84, Sar74), and it enables future experimental searches for laser-induced deexcitation of nuclear isomers, particularly the anomalously low-lying ^229mTh isomer, using the Duke University Free Electron Laser facility (E_L ≲ 12 eV).
