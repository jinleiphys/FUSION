# Flavor Oscillations in Core-Collapse Supernovae

> Core collapse supernovae are unique laboratories to study many aspects of neutrino physics. The vicinity of the proto-neutron star in a core-collapse supernova is characterized by large matter and neutrino densities. A salient feature of this region is the impact of neutrino-neutrino interactions. Properties of the ensuing non-linear many-neutrino system are examined with a particular emphasis on its collective behavior and its symmetries. The impact of neutrino properties and interactions on the r-process nucleosynthesis that may take place in the supernova environment is discussed.

## Key claim

Collective neutrino oscillations in core-collapse supernovae, driven by neutrino-neutrino interactions, can be understood through symmetries and integrability of the many-neutrino system, but the single-angle approximation used in many calculations may be inadequate in realistic supernova environments where neutrino angular distributions are not half-isotropic.

## Method

The paper derives the Hamiltonian for a dense neutrino gas using SU(2) flavor isospin algebras for two flavors, including one-body terms (mixing and matter interactions) and two-body neutrino-neutrino interaction terms. It then applies a random phase approximation (RPA) to reduce the two-body Hamiltonian to a one-body form, and exploits an analogy with the reduced BCS Hamiltonian to identify conserved quantities (Gaudin invariants) for both the full and reduced Hamiltonians. The diagonalization of the one-body Hamiltonian with a Lagrange multiplier is used to demonstrate the spectral split or swapping phenomenon.

## Key numbers

- Neutrinos carry about 10% of the progenitor star's rest mass, approximately 10^53 ergs, in contrast to the total optical and kinetic energy which is only about one percent of this amount.
- The diffusion time of neutrinos from the proto-neutron star is slightly less than ten seconds.
- The reactor neutrino flux deficiency could be explained with a single additional sterile neutrino state with mass splitting of the order of ~1 eV^2 and a small mixing with active states.
- The electron neutrino survival probability P(nu_e -> nu_e) is independent of the value of the CP-violating phase delta when only the tree-level matter potential is used.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae, building on the MSW effect (Wolfenstein 1978) and earlier studies of neutrino-neutrino interactions (Pantaleone 1992). It connects to the BCS theory analogy for neutrino oscillations (Richardson 1963, Gaudin 1976) and discusses recent numerical work by Cherry (2010) showing that multi-angle formulations yield significant differences from single-angle approximations. The paper enables future work on multi-angle instabilities, neutrino halos, and the role of symmetries in numerical calculations of the supernova neutrino system.
