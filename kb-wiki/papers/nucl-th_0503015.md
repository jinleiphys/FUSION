# Benchmark calculation for proton-deuteron elastic scattering observables including Coulomb

> Two independent calculations of proton-deuteron elastic scattering observables including Coulomb repulsion between the two protons are compared in the proton lab energy region between 3 MeV and 65 MeV. The hadron dynamics is based on the purely nucleonic charge-dependent AV18 potential. Calculations are done both in coordinate space and momentum space. The coordinate-space calculations are based on a variational solution of the three-body Schrödinger equation using a correlated hyperspherical expansion for the wave function. The momentum-space calculations proceed via the solution of the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equation using the screened Coulomb potential and the renormalization approach. Both methods agree within 1% on all observables, showing the reliability of both numerical techniques in that energy domain. At energies below three-body breakup threshold the coordinate-space method remains favored whereas at energies higher than 65 MeV the momentum-space approach seems to be more efficient.

## Key claim

Two independent calculations of proton-deuteron elastic scattering observables, one in coordinate space using the Kohn variational principle and one in momentum space using the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equation with screened Coulomb and renormalization, agree within 1% on all observables for the AV18 potential in the proton lab energy range 3–65 MeV, but the abstract's claim of reliability is qualified by the observation that the coordinate-space method is favored below the three-body breakup threshold while the momentum-space approach is more efficient above 65 MeV.

## Method

The coordinate-space calculation solves the three-body Schrödinger equation variationally using the Kohn variational principle, expanding the wave function in a pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis with appropriate Coulomb-distorted asymptotic boundary conditions. The momentum-space calculation solves the symmetrized Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equation using a screened Coulomb potential with a smooth cutoff (exponent n=4) and corrects for the unscreened limit via a renormalization factor, treating high two-nucleon partial waves perturbatively. Both calculations use the charge-dependent AV18 two-nucleon potential with no three-nucleon force.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section, vector analyzing power, and tensor analyzing powers for pd elastic scattering at proton lab energies of 3, 10, and 65 MeV agree between the two methods to better than 1%.
- Screening radius needed for convergence in the momentum-space approach: 20 fm at 3 MeV, 10 fm at 10 MeV, and 7 fm at 65 MeV.
- Corresponding on-shell wavelengths: 24.8 fm at 3 MeV, 13.6 fm at 10 MeV, and 5.3 fm at 65 MeV.
- For the strong interaction partial waves, the momentum-space calculation uses maximum two-body angular momentum j_max=5 at 3 and 10 MeV, and j_max=6 at 65 MeV; screened Coulomb is included up to j_max=25.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark comparison of Kievsky et al. (2001) for the AV14 potential below breakup threshold to the AV18 potential and to energies above the three-body breakup threshold (up to 65 MeV). It resolves a disagreement reported by Suslov et al. (2004) for the Malfliet-Tjon I-III potential, confirming the Kohn variational principle results of Kievsky et al. (2001) via the momentum-space AGS method. The results enable reliable comparison of pd elastic scattering data with calculations including Coulomb, and allow identification of observables where Coulomb effects (sizable at 3 and 10 MeV, small at 65 MeV except at forward angles) can be neglected.
