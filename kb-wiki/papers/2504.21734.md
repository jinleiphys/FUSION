# Thermoelectric Thomson coefficient of quark-gluon plasma in the presence of a time-varying magnetic field

> Heavy-ion collision experiments such as the Large Hadron Collider and the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider offer a unique platform to study several key properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), a deconfined state of strongly interacting matter. Quarks, being the electrically charged particles, can induce an electric current in the medium in response to the temperature gradients. Hence, the QGP medium can behave like a thermoelectric medium. The thermoelectric coefficients, such as the Seebeck and Thomson coefficients, can help us to understand the intricate transport phenomenon of the medium. In peripheral collisions, the intense, transient, and time-dependent magnetic field created due to spectator protons significantly influences the thermoelectric properties of the QGP medium, affecting the charge and heat transport. This work uses the quasi-particle model to calculate the Thomson coefficient in QGP. The Thomson effect, describing the continuous heating or cooling of the charge-carrying medium in the presence of temperature gradients, remains largely unexplored in QGP. The Seebeck effect, which relates temperature gradients to induced electric fields, has been widely studied in the literature. For the first time, we calculate the magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson coefficients. We have studied their dependence on temperature, baryon chemical potential, center of mass energy, and time-dependent magnetic field with different decay parameters. The transverse Thomson effect originates due to the presence of the Nernst effect in the presence of a magnetic field. Our results provide new insights into the higher-order thermoelectric transport properties of the QGP medium in the context of heavy-ion collisions.

# Thermoelectric Thomson coefficient of quark-gluon plasma in the presence of a time-varying magnetic field

## Key claim

For the first time, the magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma are calculated using a quasi-particle model, showing that these higher-order thermoelectric transport coefficients depend on temperature, baryon chemical potential, center-of-mass energy, and the decay parameters of the time-dependent magnetic field. The Thomson coefficient is non-zero only when the Seebeck coefficient has a temperature dependence, and the transverse Thomson effect originates from the Nernst effect in the presence of a magnetic field.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann transport equation under relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a QGP medium composed of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, using a quasi-particle model (Gorenstein-Yang) that attributes effective thermal masses to partons to reproduce lattice QCD results. For the zero magnetic field case, the Thomson coefficient is obtained from the first Thomson relation Th = T dS/dT, where S is the Seebeck coefficient derived by setting the electric current to zero. For the non-zero magnetic field case, a time-varying magnetic field of the form B = B0 exp(-tau/tau_B) is introduced, and the magneto-Thomson coefficient Th_B and transverse Thomson coefficient Th_N are calculated from the magneto-Seebeck and Nernst coefficients, respectively, using momentum-independent relaxation times for quarks.

## Key numbers

- The Seebeck coefficient S is negative throughout the temperature range for mu_B = 0.10 GeV and 0.30 GeV, but slightly positive at lower temperatures for mu_B = 0.60 GeV before turning negative.
- For mu_B = 0.10 GeV, the Thomson coefficient Th is positive and increases with temperature T; for mu_B = 0.30 and 0.60 GeV, Th is negative over the whole temperature range.
- The magnetic field magnitude can reach 10-15 m_pi^2 (approximately 10^18 Gauss), where m_pi is about 140 MeV, which is much higher than the QCD scale (Lambda_QCD approximately 1.5 m_pi).
- The QCD scale-fixing parameter Lambda_T is taken as 0.115 GeV in the calculations.
- The strong coupling constant alpha_s is fixed at 0.5 for numerical estimation of the relaxation time.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of thermoelectric transport in QGP, where the Seebeck, magneto-Seebeck, and Nernst coefficients have been extensively investigated in the presence of a magnetic field by Abhishek (2020), Kurian (2021), K (2022), and Zhang (2020). The Thomson effect, describing continuous heating or cooling of the charge-carrying medium under temperature gradients, had remained largely unexplored in QGP until this study. The results provide new insights into higher-order thermoelectric transport properties of the QGP medium in the context of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where intense transient magnetic fields from spectator protons significantly influence charge and heat transport.
