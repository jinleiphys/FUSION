# The limits of the nuclear landscape explored by the relativistic continuum Hatree-Bogoliubov theory

> The ground-state properties of nuclei with 8 $\le$ Z $\le$ 120 from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line have been investigated using the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory with the relativistic density functional PC-PK1. With the effects of the continuum included, there are totally 9035 nuclei predicted to be bound, which largely extends the existing nuclear landscapes predicted with other methods. The calculated binding energies, separation energies, neutron and proton Fermi surfaces, root-mean-square (rms) radii of neutron, proton, matter, and charge distributions, ground-state spins and parities are tabulated. The extension of the nuclear landscape obtained with RCHB is discussed in detail, in particular for the neutron-rich side, in comparison with the relativistic mean field calculations without pairing correlations and also other predicted landscapes. It is found that the coupling between the bound states and the continuum due to the pairing correlations plays an essential role in extending the nuclear landscape. The systematics of the separation energies, radii, densities, potentials and pairing energies of the RCHB calculations are also discussed. In addition, the alpha-decay energies and proton emitters based on the RCHB calculations are investigated.

## Key claim

The relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory with the PC-PK1 functional predicts 9035 bound nuclei for 8 ≤ Z ≤ 120, which is a larger number than previous theoretical landscapes, but this result depends on the inclusion of pairing correlations that couple bound states to the continuum; without such coupling, some neutron-rich nuclei predicted as unbound become bound.

## Method

The authors solved the RCHB equations in coordinate space using the shooting method with a box size of 20 fm, mesh size of 0.1 fm, and angular momentum cutoff of Jmax = 19/2 ħ. They used the relativistic density functional PC-PK1 for the particle-hole channel and a surface pairing force (η = 1) with strength V0 = −342.5 MeV fm³ fitted to experimental odd-even mass differences. For odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, blocking effects were treated by selecting the lowest-energy configuration from candidate single-particle levels near the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

* 9035 bound nuclei predicted for 8 ≤ Z ≤ 120 from proton drip line to neutron drip line
* Pairing strength V0 = −342.5 MeV fm³ for both neutrons and protons, fitted with a cutoff energy of 100 MeV
* Saturation density ρ0 = 0.152 fm⁻³ used in the pairing force
* Box size Rbox = 20 fm yields relative accuracy of 0.002% in total energy for ³⁰⁴120 compared to Rbox = 27.5 fm
* Energy difference between Δr = 0.1 fm and Δr = 0.05 fm is smaller than 0.0035 MeV for ³⁰⁴120 (about 0.0002% of total energy)
* PC-PK1 reproduces experimental masses with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.859 MeV for elements from Z = 50 to 91

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean field calculations without pairing correlations by Hirata (1997) and earlier RHB studies by Afanasjev (2013, 2014, 2015) by including continuum effects self-consistently via the RCHB framework. The predicted landscape of 9035 bound nuclei exceeds the approximately 7000 bound nuclei predicted by Skyrme-DFT calculations with six parametrizations by Erler (2012). The results provide a systematic tabulation of ground-state properties for all nuclei from oxygen to element 120, enabling future studies of alpha-decay energies, proton emitters, and astrophysical nucleosynthesis pathways.
