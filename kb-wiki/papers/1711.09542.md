# Hybridization of tensor-optimized and high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for light nuclei with bare interaction

> Many-body correlations play an essential role in the ab initio description of nuclei with nuclear bare interactions. We propose a new framework to describe light nuclei by the hybridization of the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) and the high-momentum AMD (HM-AMD), which we call "HM-TOAMD". In this framework, we describe the many-body correlations in terms of not only the correlation functions in TOAMD, but also the high-momentum pairs in the AMD wave function. With the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction AV8', we sufficiently reproduce the energy and radius of the 3H nucleus in HM-TOAMD. The effects of tensor force and short-range repulsion in the bare interaction are nicely described in this new framework. We also discuss the convergence in calculation and flexibility of the model space for this new method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the new hybrid framework HM-TOAMD can describe the many-body correlations in light nuclei using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction AV8' by combining correlation functions from TOAMD with high-momentum pairs from HM-AMD, and that this combination provides a more flexible and computationally simpler path to ab initio calculations of p-shell and heavier nuclei than extending TOAMD alone.

## Method

The authors construct the HM-TOAMD wave function as a linear combination of basis states, each consisting of an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) Slater determinant that includes high-momentum nucleon pairs (via imaginary shifts D in the Gaussian centroids) multiplied by correlation functions for short-range repulsion (F_S) and tensor force (F_D). For the 3H nucleus, they superpose basis states with three kinds of high-momentum pairs (p↑n↑, p↑n↓, n↑n↓) using various shift magnitudes D_z from 1 fm to 12 fm, and expand the correlation functions in Gaussian functions with range parameters from 0.01 fm^-2 to 5 fm^-2. They solve the eigenvalue problem for the superposition coefficients and compare the resulting energy, Hamiltonian components, and matter radius to Double-F TOAMD and few-body calculations.

## Key numbers

- The total energy of 3H obtained with HM-TOAMD is -7.64 MeV, compared to -7.68 MeV from Double-F TOAMD and -7.76 MeV from GFMC.
- The kinetic energy component is 47.29 MeV, the central component is -22.47 MeV, the tensor component is -30.60 MeV, and the LS component is -1.87 MeV.
- The matter radius of 3H from HM-TOAMD is 1.73 fm, compared to 1.75 fm from both Double-F TOAMD and few-body calculations.
- The energy curves show two minima for the (p↑, n↓) high-momentum pair: one at D_z = 4 fm (corresponding to momentum k_z = 1.12 fm^-1) from tensor effects, and one at D_z = 10 fm (k_z = 2.8 fm^-1) from short-range repulsion.
- The Single-F TOAMD energy (no high-momentum pairs) is -4.67 MeV; adding all six kinds of high-momentum pairs with n_D=7 yields -7.64 MeV, an improvement of about 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TOAMD framework (Myo et al., 2015-2017) and HM-AMD framework (Myo et al., 2017; Itagaki et al., 2017) for s-shell nuclei by hybridizing the two approaches. The HM-TOAMD results for 3H agree well with Double-F TOAMD and Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations (Wiringa et al., 2001; Suzuki et al., 2008). The method is designed to enable future ab initio studies of p-shell nuclei and cluster nuclei, where the triple and higher products of correlation functions required in TOAMD become computationally prohibitive, by instead using high-momentum pairs which are easier to implement within the AMD framework.
