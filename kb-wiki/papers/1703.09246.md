# Light baryons below and above the deconfinement transition: medium effects and parity doubling

> We study what happens to the Nucleon, Delta and Omega baryons in the hadronic gas and the quark-gluon plasma, with particular interest in parity doubling and its emergence as the plasma is heated. This is done using simulations of lattice QCD, employing the FASTSUM anisotropic Nf=2+1 ensembles, with four temperatures below and four above the deconfinement transition temperature. Below Tc we find that the positive-parity groundstate masses are largely temperature independent, whereas the negative-parity ones are reduced considerably as the temperature increases. This may be of interest for heavy-ion phenomenology. Close to the transition, the masses are nearly degenerate, in line with the expectation from chiral symmetry restoration. Above Tc we find a clear signal of parity doubling in all three channels, with the effect of the heavier s quark visible.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f=2+1\) flavors, positive-parity baryon groundstate masses remain largely temperature-independent below the deconfinement transition, while negative-parity masses decrease considerably with temperature; above \(T_c\), clear parity doubling is observed in all three baryon channels, though the heavier strange quark in the \(\Omega\) channel makes the effect less pronounced than in the nucleon and \(\Delta\) channels.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the FASTSUM anisotropic \(N_f=2+1\) ensembles with a fixed-scale approach, varying the temperature by changing the temporal lattice extent \(N_\tau\). They computed baryon two-point correlation functions at zero spatial momentum for the nucleon (\(N\)), \(\Delta\), and \(\Omega\) channels, using Gaussian smearing on both source and sink to enhance groundstate overlap, and extracted masses by fitting the correlators to a two-exponential ansatz that accounts for both positive- and negative-parity contributions.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: \(M_\pi = 384(4)\) MeV (heavier than in nature due to the unphysical light-quark masses used)
- Pseudo-critical temperature: \(T_c = 185(4)\) MeV, determined from the renormalized Polyakov loop
- Eight temperatures studied: four below \(T_c\) (\(T/T_c = 0.24, 0.76, 0.84, 0.95\)) and four above \(T_c\) (\(T/T_c = 1.09, 1.27, 1.52, 1.90\))
- Spatial lattice spacing: \(a_s = 0.1227(8)\) fm; inverse temporal lattice spacing: \(a_\tau^{-1} = 5.63(4)\) GeV; renormalized anisotropy \(\xi = a_s/a_\tau = 3.5\)

## Context
This work extends earlier quenched lattice studies of baryon screening masses by DeTar (1987) and Datta (2012) to full QCD with dynamical \(N_f=2+1\) flavors, and provides the first systematic lattice investigation of light baryon in-medium modifications across the deconfinement transition. The results enable quantitative comparisons with heavy-ion phenomenology, particularly regarding the temperature dependence of negative-parity baryon masses and the emergence of parity doubling as a signature of chiral symmetry restoration. The findings also motivate future studies of baryon spectral functions and the extension to nonzero baryon chemical potential.
