# 8Li electron spectrum versus 8B neutrino spectrum: implications for the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory

> The sensitivity of the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) to measure the shape of the recoil electron spectrum in the charged-current reaction of $^{8}$B solar neutrinos interacting with deuterium can be improved if the results of a $^{8}$Li beta-decay calibration experiment are included in the test. We calculate an improvement in sensitivity, under certain idealistic assumptions, of about a factor of 2, sufficient to resolve different neutrino-oscillation solutions to the solar-neutrino problem. We further examine the role of recoil and radiative corrections on both the $^{8}$B neutrino spectrum and the $^{8}$Li electron spectrum and conclude that the influence of these effects on the ratio of the two spectra as measured by SNO is very small.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of SNO to measure the shape of the recoil electron spectrum from charged-current interactions of 8B solar neutrinos can be improved by about a factor of 2 when including a 8Li beta-decay calibration experiment, but this improvement assumes certain idealistic conditions regarding detector response and systematic uncertainties that may not be fully achievable in practice.

## Method

The authors calculated the average kinetic energy of recoil electrons from 8B neutrino absorption on deuterium, ⟨Te⟩ν, and from 8Li beta decay, ⟨Te⟩e, using R-matrix theory to model the intermediate 8Be* states populated in both decays. They incorporated recoil (forbidden) corrections using the elementary-particle treatment of Holstein (1974) and radiative corrections accounting for internal bremsstrahlung detection in a calorimetric detector, then constructed the ratio ⟨Te⟩ν/⟨Te⟩e to evaluate cancellation of common-mode uncertainties from the 8B neutrino spectrum, detector energy resolution, and absolute energy calibration.

## Key numbers

- The 3σ uncertainty in ⟨Te⟩ν alone is 2.8%, dominated by absolute energy calibration uncertainty.
- The 3σ uncertainty in the ratio ⟨Te⟩ν/⟨Te⟩e is reduced to 1.2% when including a 8Li calibration experiment.
- The uncertainty from the 8B neutrino spectrum (characterized by an offset parameter b) is reduced by a factor of 7.6 in the ratio.
- The uncertainty from detector energy resolution is reduced by a factor of 8.5 in the ratio.
- The uncertainty from absolute energy calibration is reduced by a factor of 5.0 in the ratio.
- The statistical error (3σ) on 5000 charged-current events is 0.98%.
- The threshold kinetic energy for event acceptance is Tmin = 5 MeV.
- The detector resolution width at 10 MeV is σ10 = 1.1 ± 0.11 MeV (1σ errors).
- The absolute energy calibration error is assumed to be 1% at 10 MeV, corresponding to δ = ±100 keV (1σ error).
- The effective 3σ uncertainty of the alpha-particle energy offset is b = ±0.104 MeV, following Bahcall et al. (1996).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bahcall and Lisi (1996) on the SNO charged-current spectral shape test by proposing a ratio method using a 8Li calibration source, which was planned as part of the SNO calibration strategy described by Sur (1994). The authors show that the ratio ⟨Te⟩ν/⟨Te⟩e can distinguish between the small-angle MSW and large-angle MSW neutrino oscillation solutions, which Bahcall and Lisi (1996) found was unlikely using ⟨Te⟩ν alone. The approach enables improved discrimination among neutrino oscillation scenarios (SMA, LMA, and vacuum oscillations) in the SNO detector.
