# Matrix product states for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions

> We provide an efficient and accurate method for converting Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions into matrix product states (MPSs). These wave functions, also known as Bogoliubov vacua, exhibit a peculiar entanglement structure that the eigenvectors of the reduced density matrix are also Bogoliubov vacua. We exploit this important feature to obtain their optimal MPS approximation and derive an explicit formula for corresponding MPS matrices. The performance of our method is benchmarked with the Kitaev chain and the Majorana-Hubbard model on the honeycomb lattice. The approach facilitates the applications of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions and is ideally suited for combining with the density-matrix renormalization group method.

# Matrix product states for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions

## Key claim
The paper provides an efficient and accurate method for converting Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions into matrix product states (MPSs) by exploiting the property that eigenvectors of the reduced density matrix are themselves Bogoliubov vacua, but the method's performance depends on the pairing strength and system gap, with the MPO-MPS method performing comparably for large pairing strengths.

## Method
The authors utilize the correlation matrix technique to diagonalize the reduced density matrix of HFB wave functions (Bogoliubov vacua). They derive a Pfaffian formula for the MPS matrices by expressing both the Schmidt vectors and the original state as Bogoliubov vacua via Bloch-Messiah decomposition, then computing overlaps using Wick's theorem. The method is benchmarked on the Kitaev chain and the Majorana-Hubbard model on the honeycomb lattice, with truncation performed by keeping only Bogoliubov modes whose eigenvalues are close to 0 or 1.

## Key numbers
- For the Kitaev chain with length N=60, chemical potential μ=0.5, and bond dimension χ=256 (for Δ<0.2) or χ=64 (for Δ≥0.2), the per-site energy deviation δϵ_K is less than 10^{-5} for relatively large pairing strength Δ.
- For the Majorana-Hubbard model on a 20×6 lattice at g=0 (noninteracting limit), the energy deviation of the resulting MPS is about 5×10^{-5} with bond dimension up to 1024.
- For the mean-field Hamiltonian on a 12×6 cylinder with t'=-0.2, the ground state |Ψ_M⟩ is approximated as an MPS with bond dimension χ_M=600.
- The relative energy difference between the mean-field state |Ψ_M⟩ and the DMRG-optimized state |Ψ_D⟩ (with bond dimension χ_D=3000) at coupling strength g=0.5 is approximately 6.5%, and the fidelity is |⟨Ψ_D|Ψ_M⟩|≈0.499.
- For the DMRG ground-state search with random MPS initial ansatz, the relative difference between two lowest entanglement energy levels is approximately 10^{-4}, and the variational energy relative difference compared to |Ψ_D⟩ is also approximately 10^{-4}.

## Context
This work extends previous methods for converting Slater determinants into MPSs (Fishman 2015, Tu 2020, Petrica 2021) to the more general case of HFB wave functions with fermion pairing. It improves upon the MPO-MPS method (Jin 2020), which requires a well-localized basis for Bogoliubov quasiparticles that cannot always be guaranteed, and upon the method of Petrica (2021), which doubles the entanglement by requiring an extra copy of the HFB state. The approach enables using physically motivated HFB wave functions as initial input in DMRG calculations to circumvent local minima and accelerate ground-state searches, as demonstrated on the Majorana-Hubbard model where the HFB state captures essential topological physics even when quantitatively differing from the true ground state.
