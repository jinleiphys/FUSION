# Possible $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ molecular states and their productions in nulceon-antinulceon collision

> In this work, a study of possible molecular states from the $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ interaction and their productions in nucleon-antinucleon collision is performed in a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach. Two bound states with quantum numbers $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$ and $1^{--}$ are produced with almost the same binding energy from the $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ interaction which is described by the light meson exchanges. However, the result does not support the assignment of experimentally observed $Y(4630)$ as a $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ molecular state because it is hard to obtain a peak near experimental mass of the $Y(4630)$ which is far above the $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ threshold. The possibility to search these states in nucleon-antinucleon collision is studied by including couplings to $N\bar{N}$ and $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ channels. The peaks can be found obviously near the $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ threshold in the $D^*\bar{D}^*$ channel at an order of amplitude of 10 $μ$b. Too small width of state with $0^{-+}$ may lead to the difficulty to be observed in experiment. Based on the results in the current work, search for the $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ molecular state with $1^{--}$ is suggested in process $N\bar{N}\to D^*\bar{D}^*$, which is accessible at $\rm \bar{P}ANDA$.

## Key claim
Two bound states with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=0^{-+}\) and \(1^{--}\) are produced from the \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) interaction with almost the same binding energy, but the result does not support assigning the experimentally observed \(Y(4630)\) as a \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) molecular state because the \(Y(4630)\) mass is about 60 MeV above the \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) threshold, while the calculated bound states lie below threshold.

## Method
The \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) interaction was described by light meson exchanges (\(\omega\) and \(\sigma\)) using effective Lagrangians with heavy quark and chiral symmetries. Bound states were searched by solving a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation (qBSE) in the complex energy plane. Couplings to \(N\bar{N}\) and \(D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}\) channels were included to study production cross sections in nucleon-antinucleon collisions, with the potential kernel constructed via one-boson-exchange and form factors parameterized by a cutoff \(\Lambda_e = m_e + \alpha \times 0.22\) GeV.

## Key numbers
- Two bound states with \(J^{PC}=0^{-+}\) and \(1^{--}\) have binding energies of 3.0 MeV at cutoff parameter \(\alpha = 0.0\), increasing to 26.8 MeV at \(\alpha = 0.4\) (Table 1).
- The molecular state with \(J^{PC}=1^{--}\) has a width of order 0.1 MeV after coupled-channel effects.
- The molecular state with \(J^{PC}=0^{-+}\) has a width of about 0.1 keV after coupled-channel effects, three orders of magnitude smaller than the \(1^{--}\) state.
- The cross section for \(N\bar{N} \to D^*\bar{D}^*\) near the \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) threshold is at an order of magnitude of 10 \(\mu\)b for \(\alpha\) values from 0.1 to 0.4.
- The cross section for \(N\bar{N} \to N\bar{N}\) is at an order of magnitude of 0.1 \(\mu\)b, and for \(D\bar{D}\) and \(D\bar{D}^*\) channels at an order of magnitude of 0.01 \(\mu\)b.

## Context
This work extends studies of \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) molecular states by Lee (2011) and Simonov (2011), who predicted strong attraction via \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) exchanges, and addresses the long-standing question of whether \(Y(4630)\) observed by Belle (2008) and BESIII (2017) is such a molecule. The results contradict the molecular interpretation of \(Y(4630)\) and instead suggest searching for the \(1^{--}\) state in \(N\bar{N} \to D^*\bar{D}^*\) at the \(\bar{P}\)ANDA facility, enabling experimental tests of baryonium near the \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) threshold.
