# Collective Flow signals the Quark Gluon Plasma

> A critical discussion of the present status of the CERN experiments on charm dynamics and hadron collective flow is given. We emphasize the importance of the flow excitation function from 1 to 50 A$\cdot$GeV: here the hydrodynamic model has predicted the collapse of the $v_1$-flow and of the $v_2$-flow at $\sim 10$ A$\cdot$GeV; at 40 A$\cdot$GeV it has been recently observed by the NA49 collaboration. Since hadronic rescattering models predict much larger flow than observed at this energy we interpret this observation as potential evidence for a first order phase transition at high baryon density $ρ_B$. A detailed discussion of the collective flow as a barometer for the equation of state (EoS) of hot dense matter at RHIC follows. Additionally, detailed transport studies show that the away-side jet suppression can only partially ($<$ 50%) be due to hadronic rescattering. We, finally, propose upgrades and second generation experiments at RHIC which inspect the first order phase transition in the fragmentation region, i.e. at $μ_B \approx 400$ MeV ($y \approx 4-5$), where the collapse of the proton flow should be seen in analogy to the 40 A$\cdot$GeV data. The study of Jet-Wake-riding potentials and Bow shocks -- caused by jets in the QGP formed at RHIC -- can give further information on the equation of state (EoS) and transport coefficients of the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP).

## Key claim

The collapse of directed flow ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) observed by NA49 at 40 A$\cdot$GeV in Pb+Pb collisions is interpreted as potential evidence for a first order phase transition at high baryon density, because hadronic rescattering models without a phase transition predict much larger flow than observed at this energy.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares experimental flow data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC with predictions from ideal hydrodynamic models (with and without a QGP phase transition) and microscopic hadronic transport models (UrQMD, HSD). The flow excitation functions from 1 to 50 A$\cdot$GeV are examined, focusing on the collapse of $v_1$ and $v_2$ as a barometer for the equation of state. Additionally, parton cascade simulations with inelastic $2 \leftrightarrow 3$ processes are used to study early thermalization at RHIC.

## Key numbers

* The critical baryochemical potential for the first order phase transition is predicted by lattice QCD to be $\mu_B^c \approx 400 \pm 50$ MeV with critical temperature $T_c \approx 150-160$ MeV.
* At 40 A$\cdot$GeV, NA49 observes a collapse of proton $v_1$ and $v_2$ flow, while UrQMD calculations without a phase transition predict approximately 3% elliptic flow for midcentral and peripheral protons.
* At RHIC, hadronic rescattering models can explain less than 30% of the observed elliptic flow $v_2$ for $p_T > 2$ GeV/c, interpreted as evidence for initial pressure above 1 GeV/fm$^3$.
* The away-side jet suppression can only partially (less than 50%) be due to hadronic rescattering.
* NexSpherio ideal hydro simulations with NEXUS initial conditions predict greater than 50% event-by-event fluctuations of proton $v_2$ values.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic predictions of flow collapse as a signature of a first order phase transition originally proposed by Hofmann (1974, 1976) and Stocker (1979-1986). The observed collapse at 40 A$\cdot$GeV by NA49 contradicts the smooth increase predicted by hadronic transport models such as UrQMD (Bass 1998) and HSD (Cassing 1999). The paper enables future searches for the first order phase transition in the RHIC fragmentation region at $y \approx 4-5$ and at the planned FAIR facility at GSI, and motivates the study of jet-wake-riding potentials and bow shocks in QGP to extract transport coefficients.
