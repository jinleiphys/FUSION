# Nuclear suppression of J/Psi: from RHIC to the LHC

> A parameter-free calculation for J/Psi suppression in pA collisions, based on the dipole description, is confronted with the new data from the PHENIX experiment. Achieving good agreement, we employed this model predicting the contribution of initial state interactions (ISI) to J/Psi suppression in AA collisions. Such a transition from pA to AA is not straightforward, since involves specific effects of double color filtering and boosting of the saturation scale. Relying on this refined ISI contribution, we updated the previous analysis of RHIC data on J/Psi production in Cu-Cu and Au-Au collisions at \sqrt{s}=200 GeV, and determined the transport coefficient of the created dense medium at q_0=0.6 GeV^2/fm. Nuclear effects for J/Psi production at the LHC are predicted using the transport coefficient q_0=0.8 GeV^2/fm, extracted from data on suppression of high-p_T hadrons in central lead-lead collisions at \sqrt{s}=2.76 TeV. Our analysis covers only direct J/Psi production, while data may also include the feed-down from decay of heavier states and B-mesons.

## Key claim

A parameter-free dipole-model calculation of J/Psi suppression in pA collisions agrees with PHENIX data, enabling a refined extraction of the initial-state interaction (ISI) contribution to AA collisions. The transport coefficient of the dense medium created in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is determined to be q_0=0.6 GeV^2/fm, but the analysis covers only direct J/Psi production, while data may include feed-down from heavier charmonia and B-mesons.

## Method

The authors use a dipole description of the charmonium production process, where the transverse size of the cc-bar pair is frozen during propagation through the nucleus because the formation time exceeds the nuclear radius. The dipole breakup cross section is taken from a global fit to HERA data on the proton structure function F_2. For AA collisions, the ISI contribution is extended using a double-color-filtering formalism that accounts for the different attenuation in the two nuclei, and the final-state interaction (FSI) suppression is expressed in terms of the medium transport coefficient q_hat via the relation between dipole cross section and broadening.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: q_0 = 0.6 GeV^2/fm
- Transport coefficient for Cu-Cu collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: (q_0)_Cu = 0.38 GeV^2/fm, scaled from the Au-Au value by participant number ratio
- Transport coefficient for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: q_0 = 0.8 GeV^2/fm, extracted from high-p_T hadron suppression data
- p_T-integrated nuclear modification factor R_AA at y=0 for central Cu-Cu at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: 0.54
- p_T-integrated R_AA at y=0 for central Au-Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: 0.34
- p_T-integrated R_AA at y=0 for central Pb-Pb at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: 0.18
- p_T-integrated R_AA at y=0 for central Pb-Pb at sqrt(s)=5.50 TeV: 0.16
- Mean J/Psi radius: sqrt(<r_J^2>) = 0.42 fm
- Mean cc-bar dipole separation squared: <r_T^2> = 0.045 fm^2
- Coherence length at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and y=0: l_c = 12 fm

## Context

This work extends the dipole-based approach of Kopeliovich et al. (previous papers) for J/Psi suppression from pA to AA collisions, updating the earlier analysis of RHIC data from the same group. The model is tested against PHENIX data on d-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. The extracted transport coefficient for LHC energies (q_0=0.8 GeV^2/fm) is taken from an independent analysis of high-p_T hadron suppression in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV. The results enable separation of cold nuclear matter effects from medium-induced suppression in heavy-ion collisions, but the authors caution that feed-down from Psi', chi_c, and B-meson decays may significantly affect comparisons with data.
