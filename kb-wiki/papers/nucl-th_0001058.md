# Electroproduction of the d* dibaryon

> The unpolarized cross section for the electroproduction of the isoscalar $J^π= 3^+$ di-delta dibaryon $d^*$ is calculated for deuteron target using a simple picture of elastic electron-baryon scattering from the $ΔΔ(^7D_1)$ and the $NN (^3S_1)$ components of the deuteron. The calculated differential cross section at the electron lab energy of 1 GeV has the value of about 0.24 (0.05) nb/sr at the lab angle of 10$^\circ$ (30$^\circ$) for the Bonn B potential when the dibaryon mass is taken to be 2.1 GeV. The cross section decreases rapidly with increasing dibaryon mass. A large calculated width of 40 MeV for $d^*(ΔΔ^7S_3)$ combined with a small experimental upper bound of 0.08 MeV for the $d^*$ decay width appears to have excluded any low-mass $d^*$ model containing a significant admixture of the $ΔΔ(^7S_3)$ configuration.

## Key claim

The unpolarized cross section for electroproduction of the isoscalar \(J^\pi = 3^+\) di-delta dibaryon \(d^*\) from a deuteron target is calculated to be about 0.24 nb/sr at \(10^\circ\) and 0.05 nb/sr at \(30^\circ\) for a 1 GeV electron beam and dibaryon mass of 2.1 GeV using the Bonn B potential, but these estimates are necessarily rough because of the neglect of many components and processes, and the cross section decreases rapidly with increasing dibaryon mass.

## Method

The calculation uses a simple picture of elastic electron-baryon scattering from the \(\Delta\Delta(^7D_1)\) and \(NN(^3S_1)\) components of the deuteron, treating the \(d^*\) as a pure \(\Delta\Delta(^7S_3)\) state with a two-centered Gaussian wave function. The electroproduction cross section is evaluated from the longitudinal Coulomb and transverse electric and magnetic multipole operators, including contributions from both convective and magnetization currents. For the \(NN(^3D_3)\) component of \(d^*\), a perturbative treatment is used with a principal-value Green function, and the decay width is calculated self-consistently for each input potential at each dibaryon mass.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section at electron lab energy of 1 GeV: about 0.24 nb/sr at lab angle \(10^\circ\) and 0.05 nb/sr at \(30^\circ\) for the Bonn B potential with dibaryon mass 2.1 GeV
- Calculated decay width of \(d^*(\Delta\Delta\,^7S_3)\): about 40 MeV at \(m^* = 2.1\) GeV for a realistic potential treated perturbatively without rescattering corrections
- Experimental upper bound on the width of any undetected \(I=0, J=3\) np resonance: 0.08 MeV, derived from a 5 mb-MeV bound on the integrated area of the total np cross section at c.m. energies 2.00 to 2.23 GeV
- Three-momentum transfer minimal value: about 238 MeV/\(c\) at \(m^* = 2100\) MeV
- Perturbative normalization of the \(NN(^3D_3)\) component: 0.038 (3.8%) for potential A28 with \(m^* = 2100\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 100\) MeV
- Deuteron \(\Delta\Delta(^7D_1)\) probability in perturbative approximation: 0.65% compared to exact value of 0.419% for potential A28
- Theoretical estimates of total \(P_{\Delta\Delta}\) in the deuteron: 0.4-0.5% from Dymarz and Khanna (1990), 0.08% from Ivanov et al. (1999), experimental upper bound of 0.4% at 90% CL from Allasia et al. (1986)

## Context

This work extends earlier preliminary studies by Qing (1997) and Sun (1997) of \(d^*\) electroproduction from deuteron targets, which found very small cross sections at small momentum transfers using a different mechanism based on electroexcitation of \(N^*(1520)\). The calculated cross sections are smaller by two orders of magnitude than the preliminary value of about 10 nb/sr obtained by the Nanjing group (Qing, Sun, and Wong, 1999), with part of the discrepancy attributed to the inclusion of only \(\pi\) exchange versus \(\pi+\rho\) exchange. The large calculated width of 40 MeV for the \(\Delta\Delta(^7S_3)\) configuration, combined with the experimental upper bound of 0.08 MeV from Lisowski et al. (1982), appears to exclude any low-mass \(d^*\) model containing a significant admixture of this configuration, though the \(d^*\) could exist at higher masses if the dynamics differs from the delocalization and color-screening model.
