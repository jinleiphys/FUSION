# A moving mirror in AdS space as a toy model for holographic thermalization

> It is expected that thermalization may be described within gauge/gravity duality by considering time-dependent configurations on the gravity side of the correspondence, for instance a gravitational collapse of a matter configuration in Anti-de Sitter space. As a step towards the ambitious goal of describing such a configuration, we investigate a simple time-dependent toy model in which a mirror moves in the radial direction of Anti-de Sitter space. For this configuration, we establish a procedure for calculating two-point functions of scalar fluctuations, based on a WKB approximation. We test our method on two sample trajectories for the mirror, and find that the singularity structure of the two-point functions is in agreement with geometric optics.

## Key claim

The paper uses a moving mirror in Anti-de Sitter space as a toy model for holographic thermalization, establishing a procedure for calculating two-point functions of scalar fluctuations based on a WKB approximation, but explicitly notes this is only "a step towards the ambitious goal" of describing gravitational collapse, and the results are limited to the geometric optics (UV) limit of the correlator.

## Method

The authors place a mirror moving in the radial direction of AdS_{d+1} with Dirichlet boundary conditions and solve the scalar wave equation for two specific mirror trajectories. For a mirror moving with constant velocity (preserving scaling symmetry), they perform an eigenmode decomposition using scaling variables and compute the two-point correlator as a Mellin transform. For general trajectories, they develop a WKB approximation that reduces the partial differential equation to an ordinary differential equation, expressing the correlator as a Mellin transform involving the ratio of incoming and outgoing waves.

## Key numbers

- The mirror trajectory preserving scaling symmetry is given by t/z = u_0, corresponding to constant velocity 1/u_0
- The correlator diverges when b/a = n, where b = ln(t/t') and a = ln((u_0+1)/(u_0-1)), with n an integer
- The function g(πb/a) in the correlator has a period of 4π, with singularities appearing every 2π
- For the retarded correlator in the B=0 limit: δφ^d(t)/δφ^0(t') = θ(t-t') × 2^d/(t-t')^{d+1} × Γ((1+d)/2)/(Γ((1-d)/2)Γ(d))
- For the advanced correlator in the A=0 limit: δφ^d(t)/δφ^0(t') = θ(t'-t) × 2^d/(t'-t)^{d+1} × Γ((1+d)/2)/(Γ((1-d)/2)Γ(d))

## Context

This work extends the static mirror case considered by Amado and Hoyos (2010) to time-dependent mirror trajectories, and generalizes the quasi-static approximation for collapsing shells studied by Giddings (2006), Danielsson (2009), and others. The results are consistent with the bulk-cone singularities conjecture proposed by Hubeny, Liu, and Rangamani (2007), which states that two-point correlators become singular when boundary points are connected by bulk null geodesics. The approach enables future study of gravitational collapse of thin matter shells beyond the quasi-static approximation, and the scaling variable used is reminiscent of the late-time scaling variable of Janik and Peschanski (2006).
