# Back-reaction in the presence of thermalizing collisions

> Preequilibrium parton production following an ultrarelativistic nucleus--nucleus collision is studied in terms of the decay of a strong chromoelectric field which generates pairs through the Schwinger mechanism. Back-reaction of the partons with the field is included and a model transport equation containing a collision term is solved for the central rapidity region based on an approximation in which the partons relax to a thermal distribution.

# Back-reaction in the presence of thermalizing collisions

## Key claim
The interplay between back-reaction and thermalizing collisions in preequilibrium parton production can be modeled by combining a transport formalism with a relaxation-approximation collision term, but the qualitative transition between collision-dominated damping and back-reaction-driven plasma oscillations occurs only when the collision time is about an order of magnitude larger than the reciprocal effective parton mass.

## Method
The Boltzmann–Vlasov transport equation in 1+1 dimensions was solved for the central rapidity region, including a Schwinger source term for pair production and a collision term that relaxes the distribution to a thermal equilibrium distribution with a temperature determined self-consistently at each proper-time step. The Maxwell equation for the chromoelectric field was solved simultaneously to include back-reaction from the conduction and polarization currents. Numerical solutions were obtained using a method of characteristics for three values of the collision time (0.2, 1, and 10 in units of inverse effective parton mass), with initial conditions of electric field strength 4 and no partons present at proper time corresponding to u = -2.

## Key numbers
- Initial electric field strength: E = 4 (in units of parton effective mass m), with charge e = 1.
- For collision time tauc = 0.2 and tauc = 1 (in units of 1/m), temperature peaks at around 1.5 m for bosons and near 2 m for fermions.
- For collision time tauc = 10, the temperature levels off around u ~ 5 at T ~ 0.38 m for bosons and 0.39 m for fermions.
- The qualitative difference between collision-dominated and back-reaction-dominated regimes occurs for collision times about an order of magnitude larger than the reciprocal effective parton mass.

## Context
This work combines the transport formalism for back-reaction developed by Bialas and Czyz (1988) with the relaxation-approximation collision term used by Kajantie and Matsui (1985), and parallels the hydrodynamic calculation of Gatoff, Kerman, and Matsui (1987) but at the transport level without appeal to hydrodynamics. It extends earlier field-theory comparisons by Cooper, Eisenberg, Kluger, Mottola, and Svetitsky (1992-1993) that found close agreement between classical transport and quantum field theory results but neglected parton-parton scattering. The study enables exploration of the transition between rapid thermalization dominated by collisions and plasma oscillations driven by back-reaction, and identifies the collision time scale at which this transition occurs.
