# Collisional energy loss of a heavy quark in a semiquark-gluon plasma

> By utilizing a background field effective theory, we compute the collisional energy loss of a heavy quark moving through a semiquark-gluon plasma characterized by nontrivial holonomy for Polyakov loops. We consider the elastic scatterings between the incident heavy quark and the thermal partons with both hard and soft momentum transfers. As compared to the energy loss obtained from the perturbation theory, the hard processes get modified through the thermal distribution functions that depend on the background field, while the proper treatment of the soft processes strongly relies on the use of the hard-thermal-loop resummed gluon propagator derived from the background field effective theory. Our results show that the heavy quark energy loss is significantly suppressed in the semiquark-gluon plasma due to a background field that is self-consistently generated in the effective theory. On the other hand, the suppression has a strong dependence on the temperature of the plasma which becomes negligible above $2 - 3 $ times the critical temperature. For a realistic coupling constant, ignoring a relatively weak dependence on the heavy quark velocity, the suppression on the collisional energy loss can be approximated by an overall factor determined solely by the background field. This simple conclusion is expected to be useful for phenomenological applications in the heavy flavor physics.

## Key claim

The collisional energy loss of a heavy quark is significantly suppressed in a semiquark-gluon plasma due to a self-consistently generated background field, but this suppression becomes negligible above 2-3 times the critical temperature and, for a realistic coupling constant, can be approximated by an overall factor determined solely by the background field when ignoring a relatively weak dependence on heavy quark velocity.

## Method

The authors computed the collisional energy loss of a heavy quark in a semiquark-gluon plasma using a background field effective theory with nontrivial Polyakov loop holonomy, considering elastic scatterings between the heavy quark and thermal partons. Hard momentum transfer processes were modified through background-field-dependent thermal distribution functions, while soft processes were treated using hard-thermal-loop resummed gluon propagators derived from the background field effective theory, with the Dyson-Schwinger equation solved in the temporal axial gauge. The calculation was performed for a gluonic plasma (no dynamical quarks) in SU(3) gauge theory, separating contributions into hard and soft parts using an arbitrary cutoff scale q*.

## Key numbers

* The background field for SU(3) is parametrized as q = (1/36)(9 - sqrt(81 - 80 (T_d/T)^2)), where T_d is the critical temperature and M/T_d = 2*sqrt(10)*pi/9
* The suppression becomes negligible above 2-3 times the critical temperature
* For the s- and u-channel scatterings, the background field modification factor equals 4(1-3q)^2/3 for SU(3), which reduces to 4/3 for vanishing background field
* The Debye screening mass m_D^2 = N g^2 T^2/3 with N=3 for SU(3)
* The Casimir invariant C_F = 4/3 for SU(3)

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy quark collisional energy loss in QCD plasma, first detailed by Braaten and Thoma (1991), by incorporating a background field effective theory that describes the semiquark-gluon plasma region between the critical temperature and a few times that temperature. A previous study by Lin (2013) addressed the same problem using perturbation theory, but the current work introduces HTL resummed gluon propagators for the first time to provide quantitative predictions in a semi-QGP, which is necessary because elastic scatterings are infrared-sensitive quantities. The effective theory used here is based on matrix models developed by Meisinger (2001) and Hidaka (2020) that can qualitatively reproduce lattice thermodynamics for SU(N) gauge theories, and the results are expected to be useful for phenomenological applications in heavy flavor physics at RHIC and LHC.
