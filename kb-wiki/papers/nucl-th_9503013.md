# Repulsive Short Range Three-Nucleon Interaction

> The three nucleon interaction that arises from pion and the effective scalar and vector meson exchange components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is constructed and shown to be repulsive. Using several wavefunction models we show that this interaction reduces the calculated binding energy of the trinucleons by about 200 keV. The contributions of intermediate $N$(1440) resonances to this three nucleon interaction and of the $D$-state component in the trinucleon model are estimated, and shown to be small.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a repulsive short-range three-nucleon interaction (TNI) from pion and effective scalar/vector meson exchange components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and shows that this interaction reduces the calculated binding energy of trinucleons by about 200 keV, but only when the TNI is constructed consistently with the Bonn OBEPQ potential model; using the Paris potential model instead yields an attractive net contribution of about −101 keV, revealing a strong dependence on the underlying NN interaction model.

## Method
The authors derived TNI potentials from Feynman diagrams involving pion exchange combined with scalar (σ, a₀) and vector (ω, ρ) meson exchanges, mediated by intermediate nucleon-antinucleon (N\(\bar N\)) pair excitation and N(1440) Roper resonance excitation. They replaced the bare meson-exchange terms with the corresponding effective scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector exchange components extracted from realistic NN interaction models (Bonn OBEPQ and Paris potentials) using the method of Blunden (1988). Numerical matrix elements were computed using harmonic oscillator, Malfliet-Tjon I-III, Paris, and Bonn OBEPQ trinucleon wavefunctions, with partial wave decomposition described in an appendix.

## Key numbers
- The net repulsive contribution from the π-short range TNI (N\(\bar N\) pair excitation) with the Bonn OBEPQ model is 194 keV for the Bonn wavefunction, 134 keV for the Paris wavefunction, 126 keV for the Malfliet-Tjon wavefunction, and 99 keV for the harmonic oscillator wavefunction.
- When constructed from the Paris potential, the net matrix element becomes attractive: −101 keV for the Paris wavefunction and −200 keV for the oscillator wavefunction.
- The N(1440) intermediate state contributes a net repulsion of about 50 keV with the oscillator model (200 keV from πσ* and −150 keV from πω*), and about 20 keV with the Malfliet-Tjon wavefunction (65 keV from πσ* and −45 keV from πω*).
- The πNN* coupling constant is \(f_\pi^{*2}/4\pi \simeq 0.031\), derived from the N(1440) → Nπ decay width (60% branching ratio of 350 MeV total width).
- The σNN* coupling constant is \(g_\sigma^{*2}/4\pi \simeq 0.1\), with an uncertainty factor of about 2.
- The ωNN* coupling constant is estimated as \(g_\omega^{*2}/4\pi = 0.24\).
- Form factor cutoff parameters used: Λ_π = 1.3 GeV/c², Λ_σ = Λ_ω = 2.0 GeV/c².

## Context
This work extends the Tucson-Melbourne TNI model (Coon et al., 1979) and the Brazil TNI (Robilotta, 1985) by constructing short-range TNI components directly from the same meson-exchange mechanisms that define the NN interaction, rather than from independent hadronic phenomenology. It addresses the overbinding problem found when the Tucson-Melbourne π-exchange TNI is used with realistic NN potentials (Chen et al., 1986; overbinding of a few hundred keV in trinucleons and 2–4 MeV in alpha particles), which previously required a phenomenological repulsive short-range TNI (phTNI). The results enable consistent inclusion of short-range TNI in three- and four-nucleon calculations, but reveal that the sign and magnitude depend critically on the choice of NN potential model and the associated chiral representation parameter \(\tilde{\mu}\).
