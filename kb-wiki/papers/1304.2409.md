# Comparison of GiBUU calculations with MiniBooNE pion production data

> Background. Neutrino-induced pion production can give important informationon the axial coupling to nucleon resonances. Furthermore, pion production represents a major background to quasielastic-like events. Single pion production data from the MiniBooNE in charged current neutrino scattering in mineral oil appeared higher than expected within conventional theoretical approaches. Purpose. We aim to investigate which model parameters affect the calculated cross section and how they do this. Method. The Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model is used for an investigation of neutrino-nucleus reactions. Results. Presented are integrated and differential cross sections for 1π^+ and 1π^0 production before and after final state interactions in comparison with the MiniBooNE data. Conclusions. For the MiniBooNE flux all processes (QE, 1π-background, Δ, higher resonance production, DIS) contribute to the observed final state with one pion of a given charge. The uncertainty in elementary pion production cross sections leads to a corresponding uncertainty in the nuclear cross sections. Final state interactions change the shape of the muon-related observables only slightly, but they significantly change the shape of pion distributions.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE data for charged-current single pion production on mineral oil (CH₂) can be described within the GiBUU model only when all reaction mechanisms (quasielastic, Δ resonance, higher resonances, deep inelastic scattering) are included, but the calculated cross sections remain systematically lower than the data by 20% to 60% depending on the choice of elementary input (ANL-tuned lower bound vs. BNL-tuned upper bound).

## Method

The Giessen Boltzmann–Uehling–Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model was used to simulate neutrino-nucleus reactions on CH₂ targets, incorporating initial-state nuclear effects (Fermi motion, binding potential, Pauli blocking) and full final-state interactions (FSI) including pion absorption, charge exchange, and elastic scattering. The elementary pion production cross sections on free nucleons were taken from separate fits to the ANL and BNL bubble-chamber data, treated as lower and upper systematic boundaries, and the Oset–Salcedo (OS) collisional broadening of the Δ resonance in the nuclear medium was included.

## Key numbers

- The BNL elementary cross sections are about 30% larger than the ANL elementary cross sections (see Fig. 1, comparing data from Kitagaki (1986) and Radecky (1981)).
- For a 1 GeV neutrino on carbon, the OS medium modification of the Δ resonance decreases the Δ-production cross section by an additional 5–8% beyond the 5% reduction from Fermi motion, binding, and Pauli blocking alone.
- The single-pion production cross section from Δ decay on carbon is decreased by 15–20% when OS medium modifications are included, and after FSI the reduction at the peak of the pion kinetic energy distribution exceeds 20%.
- For 1π⁺ production on CH₂, the calculated cross section after FSI is about 30% lower than the corresponding cross section before FSI for both ANL and BNL input.
- The calculated muon kinetic energy distribution for 1π⁺ production is consistently 20% (upper boundary, BNL-tuned) to 60% (lower boundary, ANL-tuned) lower than the MiniBooNE data.
- For 1π⁰ production, the Δ channel is about 5 times less important than for π⁺, and the shape of the calculated muon kinetic energy distribution differs from the data.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU-based studies of Leitner (2008, 2009) and Lalakulich (2011) by systematically comparing both ANL and BNL elementary input to the MiniBooNE charged-current pion production data (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010). The results highlight that the ambiguity in elementary pion production cross sections (ANL vs. BNL) propagates directly into nuclear cross sections, and that the characteristic shape of pion distributions after FSI, previously observed in photon-induced pion production (Krusche, 2004) and in independent calculations by Hernandez (2012), is not seen in the MiniBooNE data, raising questions about the consistency of the treatment. The paper calls for new, improved elementary-target neutrino pion production data to resolve the ambiguity and enable a better determination of the axial coupling to nucleon resonances.
