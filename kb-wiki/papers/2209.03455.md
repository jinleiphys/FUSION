# Cold dense quark matter with phenomenological medium effects: a self-consistent formulation of the quark-mass density-dependent model

> We revisit the quark-mass density-dependent model -- a phenomenological equation of state for deconfined quark matter in the high-density low-temperature regime -- and show that thermodynamic inconsistencies that have plagued the model for decades, can be solved if the model is formulated in the canonical ensemble instead of the grand canonical one. Within the new formulation, the minimum of the energy per baryon occurs at zero pressure, and the Euler's relation is verified. Adopting a typical mass-formula, we first analyze in detail a simple model with one particle species. We show that a ``bag'' term that produces quark confinement naturally appears in the pressure (and not in the energy density) due to density dependence of the quark masses. Additionally, the chemical potential gains a new term as in other models with quark repulsive interactions. Then, we extend the formalism to the astrophysically realistic case of charge-neutral three-flavor quark matter in equilibrium under weak interactions, focusing on two different mass formulae: a flavor dependent and a flavor blind one. For these two models, we derive the equation of state and analyze its behavior for several parameter choices. We systematically analyze the parameter space and identify the regions corresponding to self-bound 2-flavor and 3-flavor quark matter, hybrid matter and causal behavior.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the long-standing thermodynamic inconsistencies in the quark-mass density-dependent model (QMDDM) can be resolved by formulating the model in the canonical ensemble rather than the grand canonical ensemble, provided that the effective quark mass is treated as a function of particle number density rather than chemical potential.

## Method

The authors reformulate the QMDDM in the canonical ensemble at zero temperature, starting from the Helmholtz free energy of a free Fermi gas of quasiparticles with density-dependent masses. They derive analytic expressions for the pressure, chemical potential, and speed of sound for a one-flavor system, then extend the formalism to charge-neutral three-flavor quark matter in weak equilibrium using two mass formulae: a flavor-dependent formula where each quark mass depends on its own number density, and a flavor-blind formula where all quark masses depend on the baryon number density.

## Key numbers

- Effective quark mass formula: \(M(n) = m + C/n^{a/3}\), where \(m\) is the current quark mass (e.g., 5 MeV for the one-flavor example), \(a\) and \(C\) are positive free parameters
- The bag term that produces confinement is given by \(B(n) = -g M^3 n (\partial M/\partial n) \beta(x) > 0\), where \(\beta(x) = [x\sqrt{x^2+1} - \arcsinh(x)]/(4\pi^2)\)
- At asymptotically large densities, the speed of sound tends to the conformal limit \(1/\sqrt{3}\)
- The chemical potential gains a new term \(-B/n\) from the density dependence of the quark mass, in addition to the free-gas contribution \(\mu^{\text{FG}} = M\sqrt{x^2+1}\)

## Context

This work resolves thermodynamic inconsistencies that have plagued the QMDDM since its introduction by Fowler, Raha, and Weiner (1981) and subsequent reformulations by Benvenuto and Lugones (1989, 1995) and Peng et al. (2000). Previous attempts to fix the model within the grand canonical ensemble, such as those by Yin and Su (2008), introduced ad hoc bag terms and lost the natural emergence of confinement. The canonical formulation preserves the key feature that a confining bag term arises naturally from the density dependence of quark masses, while ensuring that the minimum of the energy per baryon occurs at zero pressure and Euler's relation is verified. This enables consistent applications to neutron star interiors and systematic exploration of parameter space for self-bound quark matter, hybrid matter, and causal behavior.
