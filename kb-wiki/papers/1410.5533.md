# Effect of tensor force on dissipation dynamics in time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory

> The role of tensor force on the collision dynamics of $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O is investigated in the framework of a fully three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory. The calculations are performed with modern Skyrme energy functional plus tensor terms. Particular attention is given on the analysis of dissipation dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. The energy dissipation is found to decrease as an initial bombarding energy increases in deep-inelastic collisions for all the Skyrme parameter sets studied here because of the competition between the collective motion and the single-particle degrees of freedom. We reveal that the tensor forces may either enhance or reduce the energy dissipation depending on the different parameter sets. The fusion cross section without tensor force overestimates the experimental value by about 25\%, while the calculation with tensor force T11 has good agreement with experimental cross section.

## Key claim

The tensor force in Skyrme energy functionals can either enhance or reduce energy dissipation in heavy-ion collisions of \({}^{16}\)O+\({}^{16}\)O, depending on the specific parameter set; the fusion cross section without tensor force overestimates the experimental value by about 25%, while the calculation with the T11 tensor parameter set agrees well with experiment.

## Method

Fully three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations were performed for head-on collisions of \({}^{16}\)O+\({}^{16}\)O on a Cartesian grid (32×24×24 points, spacing 1 fm, time step 0.2 fm/c) using modern Skyrme energy functionals with added tensor terms. Six parameter sets were compared: SLy5 (no tensor), SLy5+T, and four T\(IJ\) parametrizations (T11, T13, T31, T33). Fusion cross sections were obtained via the sharp-cutoff formula from the maximum and minimum impact parameters for which fusion occurs.

## Key numbers

- Energy dissipation (defined as \(P_{\rm dis}=1-E_{\rm fin}/E_{\rm c.m.}\)) decreases as initial center-of-mass energy increases for all six Skyrme parameter sets studied, over an energy range from the inelastic threshold up to 200 MeV.
- Fusion cross section without tensor force (SLy5) at a center-of-mass energy of 70.5 MeV overestimates the experimental value by about 25%.
- For the T11 parameter set at 70.5 MeV, the fusion window has \(b_{\rm min}=2.3\) fm and \(b_{\rm max}=6.5\) fm, yielding the smallest fusion cross section among the sets and good agreement with experiment.
- The nuclei start at an initial distance \(d_0=16\) fm along the \(x\) axis; grid spacing \(\Delta r=1\) fm and time step \(\Delta t=0.2\) fm/c were used.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies of \({}^{16}\)O+\({}^{16}\)O collisions (Bonche 1978, Maruhn 1985, Umar 1986, Reinhard 1988, Tohyama 2002, Maruhn 2006, Loebl 2011, Simenel 2013) by including the full tensor force in a fully three-dimensional calculation without symmetry restrictions. The authors note that earlier TDHF calculations systematically underestimated one-body energy dissipation due to approximations on the effective interaction and geometric symmetry. The results enable systematic testing of tensor-force parametrizations (such as those from Colo 2007 and Lesinski 2007) against experimental fusion data, and suggest that the T11 parameter set is a good choice for studying heavy-ion collision dynamics.
