# Sub-leading correction of two-gluon rapidity correlations of strong colour field

> In the framework of Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory (EFT), we calculate two-gluon rapidity correlations in the leading and sub-leading orders of $p_\perp/Q_s$. In the leading order, both short- and long-range rapidity correlations are enhanced. In contrast, the contribution of sub-leading order is mainly short range quantum correlations. It is much smaller than that of the leading one, but is not negligible. Transverse momentum dependence of rapidity correlation shows that the leading order is sensitive to the saturation momentum of two incident particles, but the sub-leading one is not.

## Key claim

In the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory framework, the sub-leading order correction in \(p_\perp/Q_s\) to two-gluon rapidity correlations is much smaller than the leading order contribution but is not negligible, contributing mainly short-range quantum correlations; the leading order is sensitive to the saturation momentum of the two incident particles while the sub-leading order is not.

## Method

The authors calculated two-gluon inclusive production in the CGC effective field theory under the leading-log approximation, expanding the background gauge field in powers of \(\tilde{\rho}/\mathbf{k}^2_\perp\) to obtain leading and sub-leading orders in \(p_\perp/Q_s\). They evaluated the average over color charge configurations using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with local Gaussian correlators, and performed numerical integrations for \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV using running coupling constant \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

* The two-gluon rapidity correlation is peaked around \(p_\perp \sim Q_{sA}+Q_{sB}\), which for \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV corresponds to approximately 2 GeV.
* The sub-leading order contribution is less than 10% for a rapidity gap of \(\Delta y=3\), and larger than 10% for rapidity gaps \(\Delta y \le 1.5\).
* For transverse momenta \(\mathbf{p}_\perp=\mathbf{q}_\perp=1\), 2, and 3 GeV, the correlation strength varies with the sub-leading order being strongest at the smallest transverse momentum.
* When the rapidity gap \(|\Delta y| \le 2\), the sub-leading order contribution exceeds 10% of the total (leading plus sub-leading) correlation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous leading-order calculation (Zhao 2015) of two-gluon rapidity correlations in \(pp\), \(pA\), and \(AA\) collisions under fixed coupling, now incorporating sub-leading order corrections and running coupling. The results are qualitatively consistent with dihadron correlation data from the ALICE Collaboration (2016) for \(pPb\) collisions. The calculations enable more complete and accurate descriptions of the CGC EFT on two-gluon correlation patterns, particularly for short-range rapidity correlations where the sub-leading order is non-negligible.
