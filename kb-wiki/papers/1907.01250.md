# 1+1 dimensional relativistic magnetohydrodynamics with longitudinal acceleration

> Non-central heavy-ion collisions generate the strongest magnetic field of the order of $10^{18}-10^{19}$ Gauss due to the electric current produced by the positively charged spectators that travel at nearly the speed of light. Such transient electromagnetic fields may induce various novel effects in the hydrodynamic description of the quark gluon plasma for non-central heavy-ion collisions. We investigate the longitudinal acceleration effects on the 1+1 dimensional relativistic magnetohydrodynamics with transverse magnetic fields. We analyze the proper time evolution of the system energy density. We find that the longitudinal acceleration parameter $λ^*$, magnetic field decay parameter $a$, equation of state $κ$, and initial magnetization $σ_0$ have nontrivial effects on the evolutions of the system energy density and temperature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the longitudinal acceleration parameter $\lambda^*$, magnetic field decay parameter $a$, equation of state $\kappa$, and initial magnetization $\sigma_0$ have nontrivial effects on the evolution of the system energy density and temperature in 1+1 dimensional relativistic magnetohydrodynamics with transverse magnetic fields. However, the analysis is restricted to the limit of infinite electrical conductivity and a constant speed of sound, with the authors noting that a fully realistic solution would require results from lattice QCD with a temperature-dependent speed of sound.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions as a perfectly conducting, non-viscous fluid coupled to a transverse magnetic field that decays as a power law in proper time, $B(\tau) = B_0 (\tau_0/\tau)^a$. They use Rindler coordinates and parametrize the fluid rapidity as $\Omega = (1+\lambda^*)\eta_s$ with a small constant acceleration parameter $\lambda^*$. The energy conservation and Euler equations are solved both analytically under the CNC approximation (superhard equation of state with $\kappa=1$) and numerically for more realistic equations of state with constant speed of sound.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength in non-central heavy-ion collisions: $10^{18}-10^{19}$ Gauss.
- Initial magnetization parameter $\sigma_0 \equiv B_0^2/e_0$ is varied in numerical solutions, with values including $\sigma_0 = 0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0$ for $a=2/3$ and $\sigma_0 = 0.01, 1.0, 10.0$ for $a=2$.
- Magnetic field decay exponent $a$ is varied: $a=2/3$, $a=1$ (ideal-MHD limit), and $a=2$ are compared.
- Equation of state parameter $\kappa$ (where $p = e/\kappa$) is varied: $\kappa = 1, 3, 7, 10$ are compared.
- Longitudinal acceleration parameter $\lambda^*$ is varied: $\lambda^* = 0, 0.03, 0.06, 0.1$ are compared.
- For the CNC approximation ($\kappa=1$), the analytical solution for the normalized energy density is $\widetilde{e}(\tau,\eta_s) = (\tau_0/\tau)^{2\lambda} + (\sigma_0/2)[(\tau_0/\tau)^{2\lambda} - (\tau_0/\tau)^{2a}]$, where $\lambda = 1+\lambda^*$.

## Context

This work extends the longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken flow (Bjorken, 1983) and the Bjorken-Victor type flow (Roy, 2015) by including longitudinal acceleration effects, which are important for finite expanding systems. It also generalizes the CNC solution (Csorgo, 2006; Csanad, 2016; Jiang, 2017, 2018) to include magnetic fields. The results enable more realistic modeling of the energy density evolution in non-central heavy-ion collisions, with the authors noting that future work should include dissipative effects (shear and bulk viscosity, finite electrical conductivity) and temperature-dependent equations of state from lattice QCD simulations (Gupta, 2004; Qin, 2015).
