# Universality in Hadronic and Nuclear Collisions at High Energy

> Recent experimental results in proton-proton and in proton-nucleus collisions at Large Hadron Collider energies show a strong similarity to those observed in nucleus-nucleus collisions, where the formation of a quark-gluon plasma is expected. We discuss the comparison between small colliding systems and nucleus-nucleus collisions, for: a)~the strangeness suppression factor $γ_s$ and yields of multi-strange hadrons; b)~the average transverse momentum, $p_t$, with particular attention to the low $p_t$ region where soft, non-perturbative effects are important; c)~the elliptic flow scaled by the participant eccentricity. The universal behavior in hadronic and nuclear high energy collisions emerges for all these observables in terms of a specific dynamical variable which corresponds to the entropy density of initial system in the collision and which takes into account the transverse size of the initial configuration and its fluctuations.

# Universality in Hadronic and Nuclear Collisions at High Energy

## Key claim
The universal behavior observed across proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions in strangeness suppression, average transverse momentum, and elliptic flow is driven by the initial entropy density of the system, which depends on both the transverse size of the initial configuration and its fluctuations. The claim is conditional on the use of a specific dynamical variable (initial entropy density) that must be evaluated differently for different collision systems: Glauber Monte Carlo for nucleus-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions, and a Color Glass Condensate parameterization for proton-proton collisions.

## Method
The authors compare three observables across proton-proton (pp), proton-lead (pPb), and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions as a function of initial entropy density, defined via the one-dimensional hydrodynamic formulation as s0 * tau0 = (1.5/AT) * (dNch/dy). For strangeness suppression, they use the statistical hadronization model parameter gamma_s and yields of multi-strange hadrons (K, Lambda, Xi, Omega) relative to pions. For average transverse momentum, they restrict the analysis to the low-pT region (0.15 < pT < 1.15 GeV) where soft non-perturbative effects dominate. For elliptic flow, they scale v2 by the participant eccentricity epsilon_part, which accounts for event-by-event fluctuations in the initial geometry.

## Key numbers
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_s increases with initial entropy density up to the fixed point gamma_s = 1, where suppression disappears, for pp at sqrt(s) = 26 GeV to 7 TeV, pPb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, PbPb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, AuAu at sqrt(s) = 19.6, 27, 39, and 200 GeV, and CuCu at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.
- Average pT evaluated in the region 0.15 < pT < 1.15 GeV shows a slowly increasing trend across all colliding systems as a function of initial entropy density.
- For PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the initial entropy density value of 20.1 +/- 0.8 corresponds to pp multiplicity (dNch/deta) = 100 +/- 4 and PbPb multiplicity = 1943 +/- 56 at 0-5% centrality.
- For PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the initial entropy density value of 3.1 +/- 1.1 corresponds to pp multiplicity = 12.4 +/- 3.0 and PbPb multiplicity = 17.5 +/- 1.8 at 80-90% centrality.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of strangeness enhancement in small systems by Castorina et al. (2015-2016) and scaling of average transverse momentum by the Color Glass Condensate framework (Larry et al., 2015-2017). It also builds on observations of elliptic flow scaling with participant eccentricity in AuAu, CuCu, and PbPb collisions by Alver et al. (2007), Chatrchyan et al. (2012), and Manly et al. (2005). The paper enables future experimental checks of universality by providing a table of equivalent multiplicities between pp and PbPb collisions at LHC energies, and suggests that measurements at a future 100 TeV pp collider could significantly extend the reach of high-multiplicity pp studies.
