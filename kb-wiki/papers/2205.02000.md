# Perturbative Calculations of Deuteron Form Factors

> We calculate the deuteron charge, electric quadrupole, and magnetic form factors up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, treating subleading corrections, especially that of chiral nuclear forces, in perturbation theory. We examine the power counting based on naive dimensional analysis by investigating the ultraviolet cutoff variation of these form factors. We find that the N${}^2$LO magnetic form factor shows significant cutoff dependence, suggesting the contact current operator responsible be enhanced. After promotion to N${}^2$LO, it indeed renormalizes the magnetic form factor. This is in agreement with a previous work based on renormalization-group analysis. For the charge and quadrupole moments, perturbative calculation allows us to study how they scale with multiple low-energy parameters such as the pion mass, deuteron binding momentum, and momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the N\(^2\)LO magnetic form factor of the deuteron shows significant cutoff dependence, suggesting that the contact current operator responsible must be promoted to N\(^2\)LO to achieve renormalization, in agreement with a previous renormalization-group analysis by Pavon Valderrama (2014). For the charge and quadrupole form factors, no such enhancement is needed.

## Method

The authors calculate the deuteron charge (G_C), electric quadrupole (G_Q), and magnetic (G_M) form factors up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO) in chiral effective field theory, treating subleading chiral nuclear forces in perturbation theory. They use the LO chiral potential (one-pion exchange plus a contact term) to generate the deuteron wave function, then add N\(^2\)LO corrections from relativistic, nucleon-structure, boost, and potential contributions, examining the ultraviolet cutoff variation of the form factors to test naive dimensional analysis power counting.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy: \(B_d = 2.225\) MeV, used to fix the LO contact coupling \(C^{(0)}\).
* Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 138.0\) MeV, used as a representative kinematic point for cutoff variation studies.
* At \(q = m_\pi\), the N\(^2\)LO magnetic form factor shows about 15% variation relative to its LO value over cutoff values from 1600 MeV upward.
* The isoscalar charge radius of the nucleon: \(\langle r_{ES}^2 \rangle = (0.777\ \text{fm})^2\), used in the structure correction to charge and quadrupole form factors.
* The isoscalar magnetic radius of the nucleon: \(\langle r_{MS}^2 \rangle = (0.707\ \text{fm})^2\), used in the structure correction to the magnetic form factor.
* Experimental deuteron quadrupole moment: \(Q_d = 0.2859(3)\) fm\(^2\); magnetic moment: \(\mu_d = 0.8574\); charge radius squared: \(r_d^2 = 4.537(51)\) fm\(^2\).
* The N\(^2\)LO correction to the magnetic moment \(\mu_d\) shows a ten-percent level variation with cutoff, much larger than the expected 0.4% uncertainty from \((\gamma_d/\delta)^3\).
* After renormalization with the contact current operator, the N\(^2\)LO magnetic form factor achieves cutoff independence for \(q = m_\pi\), \(2m_\pi\), and \(3m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends previous chiral effective field theory calculations of deuteron form factors (Phillips 1999, Walzl 2001, Phillips 2003, Phillips 2006, Valderrama 2007, Epelbaum 2013, Filin 2020) by treating subleading chiral potentials in perturbation theory rather than nonperturbatively. The results confirm the renormalization-group analysis of Pavon Valderrama (2014) that the contact magnetic current operator is enhanced relative to naive dimensional analysis, and enable systematic study of how deuteron form factors scale with multiple low-energy parameters such as the pion mass, deuteron binding momentum, and momentum transfer.
