# Azimuthal collimation of long range rapidity correlations by strong color fields in high multiplicity hadron-hadron collisions

> The azimuthal collimation of di-hadrons with large rapidity separations in high multiplicity p+p collisions at the LHC is described in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory [1] by N_c^2 suppressed multi-ladder QCD diagrams that are enhanced α_S^(-8) due to gluon saturation in hadron wavefunctions. We show that quantitative computations in the CGC framework are in good agreement with data from the CMS experiment on per trigger di-hadron yields and predict further systematics of these yields with varying trigger pT and charged hadron multiplicity. Radial flow generated by re-scattering is strongly limited by the structure of the p+p di-hadron correlations. In contrast, radial flow explains the systematics of identical measurements in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The azimuthal collimation of di-hadrons with large rapidity separations in high multiplicity p+p collisions can be explained by "Glasma graphs" (multi-ladder QCD diagrams) that are suppressed by 1/N_c^2 but enhanced by α_S^(-8) due to gluon saturation, rather than by radial flow from re-scattering; the authors show quantitative agreement with CMS data only after applying a constant K-factor of 2.3 and using a Zero-Yield-at-Minimum (ZYAM) procedure that isolates collimated contributions above a Δφ-independent background.

## Method

The authors computed the correlated two-gluon Glasma distribution using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, expressed in terms of unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) derived from solutions of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation. The centrality dependence was modeled by varying the initial saturation scale Q_0^2 (0.15, 0.3, 0.45, and 0.6 GeV^2) at the initial rapidity while keeping the transverse area S_⊥ fixed, and the di-hadron yields were computed using the ZYAM procedure to extract the associated per-trigger yield for different trigger and associated particle p_T windows. The authors also tested a radial boost model to compare the role of flow in p+p versus Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- The Glasma graphs are enhanced by α_S^(-8) compared to the α_S^(-4) enhancement of back-to-back graphs at high parton densities.
- The constant ζ (accounting for multiple scattering effects) was set to the empirical value ζ=1/6, based on fits to p+p and A+A multiplicity distributions.
- The overall normalization required a constant K-factor of K=2.3 to match CMS data.
- For p+p collisions, the radial boost model suggests an upper bound of β=0.25 for transverse flow velocity.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at √s=2.76 TeV, the authors used Q_0^2=0.9 GeV^2 in the fundamental representation and tested transverse boosts of β=0, 0.65, and 0.85.
- The initial saturation scales Q_0^2 values used were 0.15, 0.3, 0.45, and 0.6 GeV^2 for different centralities in p+p collisions.
- The saturation scales at the probed x values (x∼10^{-4} to 10^{-5}) span Q_s^2∼1-4 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the qualitative CGC description of long-range rapidity correlations by Dumitru et al. (2010) to quantitative comparisons with CMS data on di-hadron yields in high multiplicity p+p collisions at √s=7 TeV. The authors contrast their CGC-based mechanism with alternative explanations involving radial flow from re-scattering, as proposed by Bozek (2010) and Werner (2010), showing that flow is strongly limited in p+p (β<0.25) but dominates in Pb+Pb collisions. The results enable further predictions for the systematics of di-hadron yields with varying trigger p_T and charged hadron multiplicity, and provide a framework for distinguishing initial-state saturation effects from final-state collective flow in small collision systems.
