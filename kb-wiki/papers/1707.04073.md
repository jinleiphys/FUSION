# Lightly Bound Skyrmions, Tetrahedra and Magic Numbers

> In the lightly bound Skyrme model, several Skyrmions having particularly strong binding are clusters of unit baryon number Skyrmions arranged as truncated tetrahedra. Their baryon numbers form the sequence B = 4, 16, 40, 80, 140, 224. This is the standard sequence of tetrahedral numbers multiplied by four, and therefore agrees with the sequence of magic proton and neutron numbers 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112 that occurs in the nuclear shell model in the absence of strong spin-orbit coupling. This sequence includes several of the magic numbers that are predicted for tetrahedrally deformed nuclei, and also appears in the context of the FCC lattice geometry investigated long ago by Wigner and revived more recently.

# Lightly Bound Skyrmions, Tetrahedra and Magic Numbers

## Key claim

In the lightly bound Skyrme model, the most strongly bound Skyrmion clusters are truncated tetrahedra whose baryon numbers follow the sequence B = 4, 16, 40, 80, 140, 224, which matches the sequence of magic proton and neutron numbers 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112 from the nuclear shell model in the absence of strong spin-orbit coupling, but only for nuclei with equal numbers of protons and neutrons.

## Method

The author classifies finite clusters of unit baryon number Skyrmions arranged as subsets of a face-centred cubic (FCC) lattice, using the approximation that binding is dominated by nearest-neighbour pair interactions (bonds) of equal strength. Clusters with tetrahedral symmetry are identified by selecting vertices of the FCC lattice with coordinates (a,b,c) where a, b, c are odd integers and a+b+c = 3 mod 4, and the total binding energy is computed as the number of bonds between nearest neighbours. The truncated tetrahedral clusters are constructed by starting with a complete tetrahedron having an even number of baryons on an edge (2N) and removing four equal tetrahedra with N-1 baryons on an edge.

## Key numbers

* The sequence of magic baryon numbers for truncated tetrahedral clusters is B = 4, 16, 40, 80, 140, 224, corresponding to magic proton/neutron numbers 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112.
* The B=4 tetrahedron has 6 bonds and binding energy per baryon E/B = 1.5.
* The B=16 cluster has 48 bonds and E/B = 3.
* The B=40 cluster has 150 bonds and E/B = 3.75.
* The binding energy per baryon formula is E/B = 6 - 9/(N+1), approaching 6 for large N.
* The ratio of surface to volume coefficients from the liquid drop formula is predicted as a_S/a_V = (3/2)^(2/3) approximately 1.3.
* The bond unit is estimated as 2.6 MeV, giving a one-baryon separation energy of 13 MeV and a one-baryon excitation energy of 5.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the lightly bound Skyrme model developed by the Leeds group (Har, GHS, GHK) to higher baryon numbers, where the standard Skyrme model had reached an impasse with cubic B=4 clusters failing to produce a B=40 solution for Calcium-40. The truncated tetrahedral clusters connect to Wigner's SU(4) model of nuclei (Wig) and to the FCC lattice geometry investigated by Kugler and Shtrikman (KSh) and Castillejo et al. (CJJ). The results enable modelling of tetrahedrally deformed magic nuclei including Helium-4, Oxygen-16, and Calcium-40, and suggest that the B=40 Skyrmion can be decomposed into ten alpha-particle-like B=4 tetrahedra, which was previously not possible in any Skyrmion model.
