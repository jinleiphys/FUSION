# Nuclear Structure Studies of Neutron-Rich Nuclei : Astrophysical Implications

> It is proposed here to investigate three major properties of the nuclear force that influence the amplitude of shell gaps, the nuclear binding energies as well as the nuclear $β$-decay properties far from stability, that are all key ingredients for modeling the r-process nucleosynthesis. These properties are derived from experiments performed in different facilities worldwide, using several various state-of-the-art experimental techniques including transfer and knockout reactions. Expected consequences on the r process nucleosynthesis as well as on the stability of super heavy elements are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that three specific, poorly constrained properties of the nuclear force, the density and isospin dependence of the spin-orbit force, the hierarchy of shell-evolution forces, and the reduction of proton-neutron interaction strength at the drip line, can be experimentally constrained using selected neutron-rich nuclei, and that these constraints are essential for improving r-process nucleosynthesis models and predictions for super-heavy element stability.

## Method
The study reviews experimental results from transfer and knockout reactions performed at GANIL, NSCL/MSU, and GSI facilities. The density dependence of the spin-orbit force was probed by measuring the neutron \(2p_{3/2} - 2p_{1/2}\) splitting in \(^{35}\)Si via the \(^{34}\)Si\((d,p)^{35}\)Si reaction, exploiting the central proton depletion in \(^{34}\)Si. The hierarchy of nuclear forces was inferred from the evolution of first-excited-state energies (\(2^+_1\)) in even-even nuclei across isotopic chains. The reduction of proton-neutron interaction at the drip line was quantified by determining the binding energies of the \(J=1^+\)–\(4^+\) multiplet in \(^{26}\)F, using atomic mass measurements, in-beam \(\gamma\)-ray spectroscopy, isomer decay, and invariant-mass spectroscopy of the unbound \(3^+\) state.

## Key numbers
- The neutron \(2p_{3/2} - 2p_{1/2}\) splitting in \(^{35}\)Si is reduced by a factor of about two compared to the neighboring \(N=21\) isotones \(^{41}\)Ca and \(^{37}\)S.
- The unbound \(J=3^+\) state in \(^{26}\)F lies at 323(33) keV above the neutron emission threshold \(S_n\) of 1.04(12) MeV.
- The excitation energy of the \(J=2^+\) state in \(^{26}\)F is 0.657(7) MeV.
- The \(J=4^+\) isomeric state in \(^{26}\)F lies at 0.643 MeV with a 2 ms lifetime.
- The effective proton-neutron interaction at the drip line is reduced by about 30% compared to shell-model calculations (USDA interaction) that do not account for the continuum.

## Context
This work extends the experimental study of shell evolution far from stability, following the observation that magic numbers weaken or disappear in neutron-rich nuclei, as previously shown for \(N=8\), 20, and 40 by Hannawald (1999), Sorlin (2002), and Crawford (2010). The results on the spin-orbit force in \(^{34}\)Si provide the first experimental demonstration of its density dependence, which had been debated between relativistic and non-relativistic models (Sharma 1995, Lalazissis 1998, Bender 1999). The finding of a reduced proton-neutron interaction in \(^{26}\)F quantifies an effect that must be incorporated into models to improve predictions for the location of drip lines, \(\beta\)-decay lifetimes, and neutron-emission probabilities relevant to the r-process and neutron-star crust physics.
