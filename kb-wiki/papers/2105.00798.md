# The I-Love-Q Relations for Superfluid Neutron Stars

> The I-Love-Q relations are approximate equation-of-state independent relations that connect the moment of inertia, the spin-induced quadrupole moment, and the tidal deformability of neutron stars. In this paper, we study the I-Love-Q relations for superfluid neutron stars for a general relativistic two-fluid model: one fluid being the neutron superfluid and the other a conglomerate of all charged components. We study to what extent the two-fluid dynamics might affect the robustness of the I-Love-Q relations by using a simple two-component polytropic model and a relativistic mean field model with entrainment for the equation-of-state. Our results depend crucially on the spin ratio $Ω_{\rm n}/Ω_{\rm p}$ between the angular velocities of the neutron superfluid and the normal component. We find that the I-Love-Q relations can still be satisfied to high accuracy for superfluid neutron stars as long as the two fluids are nearly co-rotating $Ω_{\rm n}/Ω_{\rm p} \approx 1$. However, the deviations from the I-Love-Q relations increase as the spin ratio deviates from unity. In particular, the deviation of the Q-Love relation can be as large as $O(10\%)$ if $Ω_{\rm n}/Ω_{\rm p}$ differ from unity by a few tens of percent. As $Ω_{\rm n}/Ω_{\rm p} \approx 1$ is expected for realistic neutron stars, our results suggest that the two-fluid dynamics should not affect the accuracy of any gravitational waveform models for neutron star binaries that employ the relation to connect the spin-induced quadrupole moment and the tidal deformability.

## Key claim

The I-Love-Q relations remain accurate for superfluid neutron stars only when the two fluids are nearly co-rotating (spin ratio Omega_n/Omega_p approximately 1); deviations from the relations can reach order 10% for the Q-Love relation if the spin ratio differs from unity by a few tens of percent, but since realistic neutron stars are expected to have Omega_n/Omega_p approximately 1, the two-fluid dynamics should not affect gravitational waveform models that use these relations.

## Method

The authors model superfluid neutron stars using a general relativistic two-fluid framework, where one fluid represents the neutron superfluid and the other represents the conglomerate of charged components (protons, electrons, etc.). They compute the moment of inertia, spin-induced quadrupole moment, and tidal deformability for two different equation-of-state models: a simple two-component polytropic model and a relativistic mean field model that includes entrainment between the fluids. The calculations are performed for various values of the spin ratio Omega_n/Omega_p between the angular velocities of the two fluids to test the robustness of the I-Love-Q relations.

## Key numbers

- The deviation of the Q-Love relation can be as large as O(10%) when the spin ratio Omega_n/Omega_p differs from unity by a few tens of percent.
- The I-Love-Q relations are satisfied to high accuracy when the two fluids are nearly co-rotating, i.e., Omega_n/Omega_p approximately 1.

## Context

This work extends the original I-Love-Q relations, which were established for single-fluid neutron stars and are approximately independent of the equation of state, to the case of superfluid neutron stars described by a two-fluid model. The results confirm that for the nearly co-rotating conditions expected in realistic neutron stars, the two-fluid dynamics do not compromise the utility of these relations for gravitational waveform modeling of neutron star binaries, as previously employed in studies such as those by Yagi and Yunes (2013). The findings also enable future work to incorporate superfluid effects into waveform models with confidence, provided the co-rotation assumption holds.
