# Nucleon-nucleon correlations inside atomic nuclei: synergies, observations and theoretical models

> While the main features of atomic nuclei are well described by nuclear mean-field models, there is a large and growing body of evidence which indicates an important additional role played by spatially-correlated nucleon-nucleon structures. The role of nucleonic structures was first suggested by Heidmann in 1950 to explain pick-up reactions of energetic nucleons. Since then a steady flux of new experimental evidence has confirmed the presence of similar structures inside atomic nuclei, dominated by correlations between pairs of nucleons. The role of these internal nucleon-nucleon correlations has been established using various energetic probes like photons, pions, leptons and hadrons. These correlated structures are essential for understanding the interaction of particles with nuclei and their presence provides an explanation of many specific nuclear phenomena including backscattered protons, copious deuteron production, sub-threshold particle production, neutrino interactions with nuclei and the EMC effect. On the theoretical side, these measurements have stimulated a large number of phenomenological models specifically devised to address these enigmatic observations. While reviews exist for specific interactions, there is currently no published commentary which systematically encompasses the wide range of experimental signatures and theoretical frameworks developed thus far. The present review draws together the synergies between a wide range of different experimental and theoretical studies, summarises progress in this area and highlights outstanding issues for further study.

# Nucleon-nucleon correlations inside atomic nuclei: synergies, observations and theoretical models

## Key claim

While nuclear mean-field models successfully describe the main features of atomic nuclei, a large and growing body of evidence indicates that spatially-correlated nucleon-nucleon structures, dominated by neutron-proton pairs, play an important additional role; the review systematically encompasses the wide range of experimental signatures and theoretical frameworks for these correlations, which has not been done previously despite the existence of reviews for specific interactions.

## Method

The authors conducted a comprehensive literature review drawing together experimental and theoretical studies of nucleon-nucleon correlations inside nuclei, covering seven categories of experimental indicators: photon interactions via the quasi-deuteron mechanism, inclusive and exclusive electron/proton reactions, pion absorption reactions, quasi-elastic knockout of deuterons, backscattering of hadrons and sub-threshold particle production, neutrino interactions, and the EMC effect. On the theoretical side, the review covers the independent pair model (IPAM), the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM), and the contact formalism, among other approaches.

## Key numbers

- Levinger's proportionality constant L = 6.4 is considered in the present work, though L values from 4 to 10 have been reported in the literature.
- For ejected nucleons with momenta above 250 MeV/c, approximately 92% of the time protons ejected from 12C targets are accompanied by correlated neutrons.
- At Jefferson Lab, 96 ± 22% of (e,e'p) events with missing momentum above 300 MeV/c had a recoiling neutron.
- At least 90% of two-body SRCs are np pairs; after considering FSI and single-charge exchange, almost 94% of SRC pairs are composed of np interactions while the remaining 6% are due to nn and pp interactions.
- The (γ,pp)/(γ,np) ratio for 12C was about 2% at low missing energy (up to 40 MeV), increasing with missing energy.
- The CM momentum of SRCs is found to be in the range of 100-160 MeV/c for different nuclei.
- The fraction of (e,e'pp)/(e,e'pn) events increased from approximately 5% to approximately 12% for missing momentum increments from 500 MeV/c to 750 MeV/c.
- For pion absorption on 3He, the ratio σ(π+,pp)/σ(π-,np) was approximately 20, indicating 2N absorption is mainly due to pion absorption by quasi-deuteron pairs.
- Pion absorption reactions have led to copious deuteron ejection up to 25% compared to the yield of ejected protons or neutrons.
- A damping factor exp(-D/Eγ) with D = 60 MeV was introduced by Levinger to account for lower (γ,np) experimental cross sections at lower photon energies.

## Context

This review extends the work of Heidmann (1950), who first suggested nucleonic structures to explain pick-up reactions, and Levinger's quasi-deuteron model (QDM) formulated to explain photonuclear reactions above 40 MeV. It draws together experimental evidence from photon, pion, electron, proton, and neutrino probes that has been accumulating since the 1950s, including recent SRC measurements at BNL and Jefferson Lab by Piasetzky (2006), Subedi (2008), and Duer (2019). The review enables a unified understanding of correlated nucleon structures across different energy regimes and probes, and highlights outstanding issues such as the detailed mechanisms of 3N- and multi-nucleon pion absorption processes, which are still not well understood.
