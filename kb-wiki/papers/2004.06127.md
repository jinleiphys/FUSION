# Asymptotic behavior of meson transition form factors

> One of the open issues in evaluations of the contribution from hadronic light-by-light scattering to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $(g-2)_μ$ concerns the role of heavier scalar, axial-vector, and tensor-meson intermediate states. The coupling of axial vectors to virtual photons is suppressed for small virtualities by the Landau-Yang theorem, but otherwise there are few rigorous constraints on the corresponding form factors. In this paper, we first derive the Lorentz decomposition of the two-photon matrix elements into scalar functions following the general recipe by Bardeen, Tung, and Tarrach. Based on this decomposition, we then calculate the asymptotic behavior of the meson transition form factors from a light-cone expansion in analogy to the asymptotic limits for the pseudoscalar transition form factor derived by Brodsky and Lepage. Finally, we compare our results to existing data as well as previous models employed in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper derives the asymptotic (Brodsky-Lepage) scaling behavior of transition form factors for scalar, axial-vector, and tensor mesons, showing that these form factors follow specific power laws in the average photon virtuality \(Q^2\), but only under the condition that the light-cone expansion is applied at leading order, and for axial-vector and tensor mesons the results are sensitive to the first non-vanishing Gegenbauer coefficient, with some form factors exhibiting singularities in singly-virtual limits that cancel in physical helicity amplitudes.

## Method

The authors first construct the Lorentz decomposition of the two-photon matrix elements for scalar, axial-vector, and tensor mesons following the Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach (BTT) recipe to obtain scalar form factors free of kinematic singularities. They then calculate the asymptotic behavior of these form factors using a light-cone expansion in analogy to the Brodsky-Lepage (BL) limit for pseudoscalar mesons, employing distribution amplitudes for each meson type and performing the leading-order perturbative QCD calculation with free quark propagators.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar transition form factor scales as \(1/Q^2\) asymptotically, with asymmetry function \(f^P(w)\) giving \(-3/2\) at \(w = \pm 1\) and \(-1\) at \(w = 0\)
- Scalar form factor \(\mathcal{F}_1^S\) scales as \(1/Q^2\) and \(\mathcal{F}_2^S\) scales as \(1/Q^4\), with asymmetry function \(f^S(w)\) giving \(3/2\) at \(w = \pm 1\) and \(3/5\) at \(w = 0\)
- Axial-vector form factors \(\mathcal{F}_2^A\) and \(\mathcal{F}_3^A\) scale as \(1/Q^4\), while \(\mathcal{F}_1^A\) scales as \(\mathcal{O}(Q^{-6})\); asymmetry function \(f_2^A(w)\) gives \(3/4\) at \(w = +1\) and \(1/2\) at \(w = 0\)
- Tensor form factor \(\mathcal{F}_1^T\) scales as \(1/Q^4\) and \(\mathcal{F}_2^T,\mathcal{F}_3^T,\mathcal{F}_4^T,\mathcal{F}_5^T\) scale as \(1/Q^6\); asymmetry function \(f_1^T(w)\) gives \(0\) at \(w = \pm 1\) and \(-3/14\) at \(w = 0\)
- The ratio \(\mathcal{F}_2^S(q_1^2,q_2^2)/\mathcal{F}_1^S(q_1^2,q_2^2) \sim m_S^2/Q^2\) asymptotically

## Context

This work extends the Brodsky-Lepage asymptotic analysis (Lepage 1979, 1980), previously applied only to pseudoscalar mesons, to scalar, axial-vector, and tensor meson transition form factors, which are needed for evaluating the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon \((g-2)_\mu\). The results provide rigorous asymptotic constraints that can be compared to existing data and previous models such as the quark-model approach of Schuler (1997), and they enable improved modeling of heavier intermediate states in \((g-2)_\mu\) calculations, particularly for axial-vector mesons where the Landau-Yang theorem suppresses couplings at small virtualities.
