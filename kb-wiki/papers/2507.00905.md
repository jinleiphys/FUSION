# Interplay of prompt and non-prompt photons in photon-triggered jet observables

> Prompt photons are important yet challenging to observe in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as they are produced in the early stages and traverse almost the entire QGP medium without interaction. Experimental analyses typically employ isolation cuts, in the hope to identify prompt photons. Most theoretical studies consider only events with actual prompt photons, assuming no contribution from isolated non-prompt photons to reduce computational cost. For the first time, we present a study that compares simulation results generated using inclusive (bremsstrahlung) and prompt-photon events with multiple experimental observables for both $p-p$ and $Pb-Pb$ collisions at $5.02$ TeV. Simulations are carried out using the multi-stage JETSCAPE framework tuned to describe the quenching of jets and hadrons. Isolated non-prompt photons are generated in hard photon bremsstrahlung, where the photon is radiated at a sufficient angle to the jet. Several photon triggered jet and jet substructure observables show significant contributions from inclusive photons, yielding an improvement in comparison with experimental data. Novel photon triggered jet substructure observables are also expected to show new structures, yet to be detected in experiment. This effort examines the significance of isolated non-prompt photons using parameters tuned for a simultaneous description of the leading hadron and jet spectrum, and thus provides an independent verification of the multistage evolution framework.

## Key claim

For the first time, this study compares simulations of inclusive (bremsstrahlung) and prompt-photon-only events for photon-triggered jet observables in both p-p and Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, finding that isolated non-prompt photons significantly contribute to several observables and improve agreement with experimental data, particularly at large x_Jγ values and small Δφ values.

## Method

The authors used the multi-stage JETSCAPE framework with PYTHIA for initial hard scattering, MATTER vacuum shower for p-p collisions, and MATTER+LBT module combination for Pb-Pb collisions, with 2+1D VISHNU hydrodynamic profiles and TRENTo initial conditions for the bulk medium. They generated two event samples: total inclusive events (including bremsstrahlung photons radiated at sufficient angle to the jet) and prompt-photon-only events (photons from initial hard scattering only), then compared simulated photon-triggered jet asymmetry (x_Jγ) and azimuthal correlations with ATLAS and CMS experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for both p-p and Pb-Pb collisions
- Photon-jet asymmetry defined as x_Jγ = p_T^jet / p_T^γ, with distributions peaking near x_Jγ = 1 for p-p and shifting to lower values for Pb-Pb
- Azimuthal correlation analysis shows difference between full and prompt photon events becomes small when Δφ > 7π/8, explaining why x_Jγ distributions (which apply this cut) show less difference between the two event types
- Statistical errors for full events are significantly larger than for prompt photon events due to the prompt photon production cross-section being suppressed by the fine-structure constant α ~ 1/137

## Context

This work extends prior JETSCAPE framework studies (JETSCAPE Collaboration, 2019, 2022, 2024, 2025) that previously considered only prompt-photon events to reduce computational cost, while experimental analyses by ATLAS (2018, 2023) and CMS (2017) use isolation cuts to identify prompt photons. The study provides independent verification of the multistage evolution framework by using parameters tuned for simultaneous description of leading hadron and jet spectra, and enables future experimental searches for novel photon-triggered jet substructure observables predicted to show new structures not yet detected.
