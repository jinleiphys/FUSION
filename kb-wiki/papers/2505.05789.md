# Measurement of medium-induced acoplanarity in central Au-Au and pp collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV using direct-photon+jet and $π^{0}$+jet correlations

> The STAR Collaboration reports measurements of acoplanarity using semi--inclusive distributions of charged--particle jets recoiling from direct photon and $π^{0}$ triggers, in central Au+Au and $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV. Significant medium--induced acoplanarity broadening is observed for large but not small recoil jet resolution parameter, corresponding to recoil jet yield enhancement up to a factor of $\approx20$ for trigger--recoil azimuthal separation far from $π$. This phenomenology is indicative of the response of the Quark--Gluon Plasma to excitation, but not the scattering of jets off of its quasiparticles. The measurements are not well--described by current theoretical models which incorporate jet quenching.

# Measurement of medium-induced acoplanarity in central Au-Au and pp collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV using direct-photon+jet and $\pi^{0}$+jet correlations

## Key claim
Significant medium-induced acoplanarity broadening is observed for large (R=0.5) but not small (R=0.2) recoil jet resolution parameter, corresponding to recoil jet yield enhancement up to a factor of approximately 20 for trigger-recoil azimuthal separation far from pi. This phenomenology is indicative of the response of the Quark-Gluon Plasma to excitation (the "wake"), but not the scattering of jets off of its quasiparticles, and the measurements are not well-described by current theoretical models which incorporate jet quenching.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration measured semi-inclusive distributions of charged-particle jets recoiling from direct photon and pi0 triggers with 11 < ETtrig < 15 GeV in pp and central (0-15% highest multiplicity) Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameters R=0.2 and 0.5 from charged tracks with pTtrack > 0.2 GeV and |etatrack| < 1, and the recoil jet yield was reported in 10 < pTjetch < 20 GeV. Uncorrelated combinatorial jet background in central Au-Au collisions was corrected using mixed events, and the resulting distributions were unfolded for instrumental effects and corrected for jet-finding efficiency.

## Key numbers
- Recoil jet yield enhancement up to a factor of approximately 20 for trigger-recoil azimuthal separation far from pi: the value of IAA(dphi) at dphi approximately 2.65 differs for R=0.2 and 0.5 by a factor 20 +/- 2 (sys) for pi0 triggers and recoil jet 15 < pTjetch < 20 GeV
- For gamma_direct triggers and recoil jet 10 < pTjetch < 15 GeV, the factor is 3.8 +/- 1.7 (sys) at dphi approximately 2.65
- Trigger particles have 11 < ETtrig < 15 GeV
- Recoil jets reported in 10 < pTjetch < 20 GeV for R=0.2 and 0.5
- Integrated luminosity: 23 pb^-1 for pp and 3.9 nb^-1 for central Au-Au collisions
- The gamma_direct fraction of the gamma-rich population is in the range approximately 40-80%
- Pi0-tagged population purity is greater than 95%
- The Gaussian broadening width parameter qhatL is constrained to 3 < qhatL < 13 GeV^2

## Context
This measurement extends the STAR analysis reported in STAR (2023) and STAR (2023) to measure acoplanarity, complementing ALICE measurements at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV (ALICE, 2023) which observed similar R-dependent broadening in h+jet correlations at the LHC. The results are compared to JEWEL with medium-recoil effects, an analytic QCD calculation with Gaussian in-medium broadening (Chen, 2016), and the Hybrid Monte Carlo model with hydrodynamic wake (Casalderrey-Solana, 2014, 2016, 2018); none of these calculations describe the data well, indicating that current theoretical models require modification of their underlying physics description. The measurement provides new insight into the nature of the interaction between jets and the QGP, and enables systematic comparison of gamma_direct and pi0 triggers to probe color charge and pathlength dependence of jet quenching effects.
