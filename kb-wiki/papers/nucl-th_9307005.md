# Mean Field Approximation to two-subsystems: Examples

> Using the Funtional Integrals Formulation is developes a self-consistent mean field expansion to evolution operators of a system composed by two subsystems. This is a general expansion and can be generalized for more of two subsystems, which can be as system composed by fermions and bosons with an interaction between their ($σ$-model , $σ-ω$ model or maser model) as a fermionic system with two or more different interactions between fermions particles, (NJL model ).

## Key claim

This paper develops a general self-consistent mean-field expansion for the evolution operator of a system composed of two interacting subsystems (such as fermions and bosons, or fermions with two different interactions), using functional integral methods; the expansion is formal and illustrative rather than providing specific numerical predictions, with the key condition that the final state is chosen as the time-evolved initial state to the desired order of approximation.

## Method

The author starts from a Hamiltonian describing two subsystems (labeled 1 and 2) with kinetic terms and an interaction term, and represents the infinitesimal evolution operator using Gaussian functional integrals with auxiliary fields σ and σ*. A stationary phase approximation (SPA) is performed by expanding the auxiliary fields around their stationary values, leading to mean-field Hamiltonians for each subsystem that contain self-consistent expectation values of operators from both subsystems. The expansion is carried out to zeroth and second order, with the second order introducing correlation effects through the determinant of the second-derivative matrix of the action, and the trial interactions W₁, W₂, and W₁₂ are chosen to enforce unitarity.

## Key numbers

- No specific numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal development of the mean-field expansion method.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous mean-field expansion for the many-fermion initial condition problem (Cruz-Barrios, 1993) to systems with two or more interacting subsystems. The formalism is intended to be applicable to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the σ-model, the σ-ω model, and the maser (Dicke) model, enabling future analysis of dynamical properties and correlations (such as RPA and beyond-RPA effects) in both nonrelativistic and relativistic systems with multiple subsystems.
