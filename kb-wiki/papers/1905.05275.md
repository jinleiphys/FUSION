# Extracting free-space observables from trapped interacting clusters

> The energy spectrum of two short-range interacting particles in a harmonic potential trap has previously been related to free-space scattering phase shifts. But the existing formula for this purpose is exact only in the limit of an infinitely shallow trap. Here we provide a systematically improved formula---describing the low-energy dynamics---that enables the use of finite traps. This paves the way for extracting nuclear scattering phase shifts from {\it ab initio} nuclear many-body structure calculations, a long-sought goal in nuclear physics. The derivation establishes effective field theory as a powerful framework for studying the connection between structure information of a trapped system (with two or more sub-clusters) and continuum physics in the fields of both nuclear and condensed-matter physics.

# Extracting free-space observables from trapped interacting clusters

## Key claim
The paper provides a systematically improved formula that relates the energy spectrum of two short-range interacting particles in a harmonic trap to free-space scattering phase shifts, which is exact at finite trap frequencies (not only in the infinitely-shallow-trap limit). The derivation uses pionless effective field theory to account for the external potential's modifications to the interaction at short distances, enabling extraction of nuclear scattering phase shifts from *ab initio* many-body structure calculations.

## Method
The author constructs a pionless effective field theory Lagrangian for two spin-zero particles in a given partial wave, using a dimer field to represent the compound system and including new coupling terms between the harmonic trap potential and particles. The fully dressed dimer propagator is computed in both free space and in the trap by summing self-energy insertions, and the quantization condition is derived by setting the denominator of the trapped propagator to zero. Renormalization is performed by relating the momentum cutoff in free space to the cutoff on radial excitation number in the trap, with specific relations given for s-wave, p-wave, and d-wave.

## Key numbers
- For p-wave neutron-alpha scattering, the original BERW formula fails at trap frequencies omega = 3, 4, 6, 9, and 16 MeV (typical values in *ab initio* calculations), producing extracted phase shifts that systematically deviate from the exact curve.
- The improved formula's series expansion breaks down when the relative energy E exceeds approximately 20 to 40 MeV, as shown by truncation errors reaching 100% of the right-hand-side value.
- For s-wave renormalization, the relation between the momentum cutoff Lambda and the radial cutoff n_Lambda is: T_bar_Lambda = 2 n_Lambda (1 + O(n_Lambda^{-1})), where T_bar_Lambda = Lambda^2/(2 m_r omega).
- For p-wave renormalization: T_bar_Lambda = 2 n_Lambda (1 + (7/4) n_Lambda^{-1} + O(n_Lambda^{-2})).
- For d-wave renormalization: T_bar_Lambda = 2 n_Lambda (1 + (9/4) n_Lambda^{-1} - (37/32) n_Lambda^{-2} + O(n_Lambda^{-3})).

## Context
This work extends the original formula of Busch et al. (1998) and its generalizations by Stetcu et al. (2007, 2010), Rotureau et al. (2010), and others (collectively called the BERW formula), which were exact only in the limit of an infinitely shallow trap. The improved formula enables the use of finite traps, which is crucial because *ab initio* nuclear structure calculations have uncontrolled errors when the trap frequency approaches zero. The approach is analogous to the Luscher formula (Luscher 1990, Beane 2003) used in lattice quantum chromodynamics, and it paves the way for extracting nuclear scattering phase shifts from *ab initio* calculations of trapped nuclear clusters, a long-sought goal in nuclear physics. The framework is also applicable to cold atom physics and could be extended to study exotic atoms and quantum dots.
