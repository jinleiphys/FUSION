# Constraints on spin-dependent parton distributions at large x from global QCD analysis

> We investigate the behavior of spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) at large parton momentum fractions x in the context of global QCD analysis. We explore the constraints from existing deep-inelastic scattering data, and from theoretical expectations for the leading x -> 1 behavior based on hard gluon exchange in perturbative QCD. Systematic uncertainties from the dependence of the PDFs on the choice of parametrization are studied by considering functional forms motivated by orbital angular momentum arguments. Finally, we quantify the reduction in the PDF uncertainties that may be expected from future high-x data from Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV.

# Constraints on spin-dependent parton distributions at large x from global QCD analysis

## Key claim

Existing inclusive polarized deep-inelastic scattering data can accommodate the pQCD expectation that polarized-to-unpolarized PDF ratios approach unity as x→1, but the data do not require it; the Δd⁺ distribution remains essentially unconstrained for x ≳ 0.5–0.6, and different parametrizations (including those motivated by orbital angular momentum) yield equally good fits with qualitatively different large-x behavior.

## Method

The authors performed global next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD fits to all available inclusive polarized DIS asymmetry data with Q² > 1 GeV² and W > 1.87 GeV, using the JAM Collaboration baseline fit that includes target mass corrections, higher twist contributions (twist-3 and twist-4) to g₁ and g₂, and nuclear smearing for deuterium and ³He targets. They explored systematic uncertainties by comparing the standard four-parameter JAM parametrization with a simplified three-parameter form and with an OAM-inspired parametrization containing an additional (1−x)⁵ log²(1−x) term, both with and without imposing the pQCD constraint Δq⁺/q⁺ → 1 at x=1. To quantify future constraints, they generated pseudodata using projected statistical and systematic uncertainties from planned Jefferson Lab 12 GeV experiments and repeated the global analysis.

## Key numbers

- The JAM baseline fit yields an overall χ² per data point of 0.98 for over 1,000 data points.
- For the JAM fit, the leading (1−x) exponents at the input scale Q₀² are β_u = 3.3 for Δu⁺ and β_d = 4.0 for Δd⁺; for the simplified SIMP fit these become β_u = 2.5 and β_d = 3.4.
- Imposing the x→1 constraint (JAM+ fit) increases χ²/N_dat to 1.01; forcing Δd⁺ to cross zero at x=0.75 gives χ²/N_dat = 1.02, while forcing a crossing at x=0.5 gives χ²/N_dat = 1.06.
- The OAM-inspired fit without the x→1 constraint achieves χ²/N_dat = 0.98; with the constraint (OAM+) it gives χ²/N_dat = 1.02.
- Fits including only leading twist contributions (no higher twist corrections) yield χ²/N_dat values of 1.07 (JAM), 1.09 (OAM), and 1.12 (SIMP) without the x→1 constraint, and 1.11 (JAM) and 1.16 (OAM) with the constraint.
- Including pseudodata from planned Jefferson Lab 12 GeV experiments reduces the relative error on both Δu⁺ and Δd⁺ by approximately 70% for x = 0.6–0.8 at Q² = 1 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the JAM Collaboration global analysis (JAM-DIS, 2013) which found significant effects from higher twist corrections on polarized PDFs, particularly a larger Δd⁺ magnitude at x ≳ 0.2. It directly tests the pQCD prediction of Farrar and Jackson (1975) that Δq/q → 1 as x→1, and the earlier global analysis by Leader, Sidorov and Stamenov (1998) that imposed this constraint and found a steep rise in Δd/d at intermediate x. The study also evaluates the OAM-inspired parametrization of Avakian et al. (2007), which introduced (1−x)⁵ log²(1−x) terms from L_z=1 Fock state components. The results demonstrate that current data cannot discriminate between these scenarios, and quantify the dramatic improvement expected from Jefferson Lab 12 GeV experiments, which will measure polarization asymmetries up to x ≈ 0.8 in DIS kinematics and enable critical tests of competing theoretical models for large-x PDF behavior.
