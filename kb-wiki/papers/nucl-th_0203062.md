# Aspects of Coulomb Dissociation and Interference in Peripheral Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Coherent vector meson production in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions is discussed. These interactions may occur for impact parameters much larger than the sum of the nuclear radii. Since the vector meson production is always localized to one of the nuclei, the system acts as a two-source interferometer in the transverse plane. By tagging the outgoing nuclei for Coulomb dissociation it is possible to obtain a measure of the impact parameter and thus the source separation in the interferometer. This is of particular interest since the life-time of the vector mesons are generally much shorter than the impact parameters of the collisions.

## Key claim

Coherent vector meson production in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions acts as a two-source interferometer in the transverse plane, and by tagging outgoing nuclei for Coulomb dissociation one can obtain a measure of the impact parameter and thus the source separation; this is of particular interest because the lifetimes of the vector mesons are generally much shorter than the impact parameters of the collisions.

## Method

The authors calculate cross sections for exclusive vector meson production (ρ⁰, ω, φ, J/Ψ) in Au+Au at RHIC and Pb+Pb at the LHC using the Weizsäcker-Williams method for the photon spectrum and a Glauber model for photonuclear cross sections, with experimentally determined photon-proton cross sections as input. They then compute the interference pattern from the two-source amplitude, accounting for the relative phase from the two nuclei acting as photon emitters, and modify the impact-parameter integration by weighting with probabilities for mutual Coulomb dissociation (general Xn,Xn or single-neutron 1n,1n emission) to show how breakup tagging alters the transverse momentum and rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for exclusive ρ⁰ production in Au+Au at RHIC: 590 mb
- Cross section for exclusive ρ⁰ production in Pb+Pb at LHC: 5200 mb
- Cross section for exclusive J/Ψ production in Au+Au at RHIC: 0.29 mb
- Cross section for exclusive J/Ψ production in Pb+Pb at LHC: 32 mb
- Cross section for ρ⁰ production in coincidence with mutual single-neutron emission (1n,1n) at RHIC: 3.7 mb
- Cross section for ρ⁰ production in coincidence with mutual general photonuclear breakup (Xn,Xn) at RHIC: 42 mb
- Cross section for ρ⁰ production in coincidence with mutual single-neutron emission (1n,1n) at LHC: 12 mb
- Cross section for ρ⁰ production in coincidence with mutual general photonuclear breakup (Xn,Xn) at LHC: 210 mb
- Median impact parameters for light vector mesons: approximately 40 fm at RHIC and 200 fm at LHC
- Interference expected to be significant for pT < 5 MeV/c at RHIC and pT < 1 MeV/c at LHC
- Total cross section for mutual Coulomb excitation in Au+Au at RHIC: 11 barns; mutual GDR excitation with single neutron emission in both directions: 0.4 barn
- Lifetime of ρ⁰: 1.3 fm/c; ω: 23 fm/c; φ: 44 fm/c; J/Ψ: 2300 fm/c

## Context

This work extends calculations of coherent vector meson production in peripheral heavy-ion collisions (previously reported by the authors in PRC) by incorporating the two-source interference effect first identified in their PRL paper, and by adding the possibility of tagging the impact parameter via Coulomb dissociation. The use of Zero-Degree Calorimeters at RHIC, which detect forward neutrons from nuclear breakup, provides experimental access to this tagging; data from the first year of RHIC running (reported by the STAR collaboration) already show vector meson production in coincidence with mutual dissociation. The interference phenomenon is particularly interesting because the short lifetimes of the vector mesons mean the interference involves the decay products in an entangled state, providing a test of the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox.
