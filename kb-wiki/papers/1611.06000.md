# Description of the $^{11}$Li$(p,d){^{10}}$Li transfer reaction using structure overlaps from a full three-body model

> Recent data on the differential angular distribution for the transfer reaction $^{11}$Li(p,d)$^{10}$Li at $E/A=5.7$ MeV in inverse kinematics are analysed within the DWBA reaction framework, using the overlap functions calculated within a three-body model of $^{11}$Li. The weight of the different $^{10}$Li configurations in the system's ground state is obtained from the structure calculations unambiguously. The effect of the $^{9}$Li spin in the calculated observables is also investigated. We find that, although all the considered models succeed in reproducing the shape of the data, the magnitude is very sensitive to the content of $p_{1/2}$ wave in the $^{11}$Li ground-state wave function. Among the considered models, the best agreement with the data is obtained when the $^{11}$Li ground state contains a $\sim$31\% of $p_{1/2}$ wave in the $n$-$^9$Li subsystem. Although this model takes into account explicitly the splitting of the $1^+$ and $2^+$ resonances due to the coupling of the $p_{1/2}$ wave to the $3/2^-$ spin of the core, a similar degree of agreement can be achieved with a model in which the $^{9}$Li spin is ignored, provided that it contains a similar p-wave content.

# Description of the $^{11}$Li$(p,d){^{10}}$Li transfer reaction using structure overlaps from a full three-body model

## Key claim

The magnitude of the calculated differential cross section is very sensitive to the $p_{1/2}$ wave content in the $^{11}$Li ground-state wave function, and the best agreement with the data is obtained when the ground state contains approximately 31% $p_{1/2}$ wave in the $n$-$^9$Li subsystem; however, a similar degree of agreement can be achieved with a model that ignores the $^{9}$Li spin, provided it contains a similar p-wave content.

## Method

The authors analyze the $^{11}$Li(p,d)$^{10}$Li transfer reaction at $E/A=5.7$ MeV in inverse kinematics using the DWBA reaction framework, replacing the simple Woods-Saxon overlap functions used in prior work with overlap functions calculated from a full three-body model of $^{11}$Li. The $^{11}$Li ground state is described as a three-body system ($^9$Li + $n$ + $n$) using an expansion in hyperspherical harmonics, while the $^{10}$Li continuum is described using $n$+$^9$Li scattering states generated with the same neutron-core interaction. Four different $n$-$^9$Li potential models are considered, two including the $^{9}$Li spin ($I=3/2^-$) and two ignoring it, to study the influence of structure properties on the calculated cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The $^{11}$Li(p,d)$^{10}$Li reaction was measured at $E/A = 5.7$ MeV in inverse kinematics.
- The best agreement with experimental data is obtained when the $^{11}$Li ground state contains approximately 31% $p_{1/2}$ wave in the $n$-$^9$Li subsystem.
- For model P1I (including $^{9}$Li spin), the $^{10}$Li $p_{1/2}$ resonances are located at 0.37 MeV ($1^+$) and 0.61 MeV ($2^+$), with a centroid at 0.52 MeV.
- The $^{10}$Li $2^-$ ground state (virtual state) from model P1I has a scattering length of $a_s = -37.9$ fm.
- The $^{11}$Li ground state from model P1I has matter radius $r_{mat} = 3.2$ fm and charge radius $r_{ch} = 2.41$ fm.
- The $^{11}$Li two-neutron separation energy adopted is $S_{2n} = 369.15(65)$ keV from Smith (2008).
- The experimental $^{10}$Li excitation spectrum from Sanetullaev (2016) shows a resonant-like structure at $E_r = 0.62(4)$ MeV, with a spectroscopic factor of $0.67 \pm 0.12$ for an $\ell=1$ configuration.

## Context

This work reexamines the $^{11}$Li(p,d)$^{10}$Li transfer reaction data from Sanetullaev (2016), which were originally analyzed using a simple Woods-Saxon model for the neutron overlap. The present analysis uses a more sophisticated three-body model of $^{11}$Li, providing absolute cross sections without renormalization, unlike the original analysis. The extracted $p$-wave content of approximately 30% is close to the value from Sanetullaev (2016) but smaller than the 45% extracted from fragmentation experiments by Simon (1999) and the approximately 40% from Garrido (2002), while being consistent with the 27.6% from Vinh Mau and Pacheco (1996). The disparity of values across different theoretical works calls for further experimental and theoretical investigation.
