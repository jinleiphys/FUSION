# Hypertriton lifetime

> Over the last decade, conflicting values of the hypertriton ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$ lifetime $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ were extracted from relativistic heavy-ion (RHI) collision experiments, ranging from values compatible with the free-$Λ$ lifetime $τ_Λ$-as expected naively for a very weakly bound $Λ$ in ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$-to lifetimes as short as $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}) \approx (0.4-0.7)\,τ_Λ$. In a recent work [Phys. Lett. B 811, 135916 (2020)] we studied this ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$ lifetime puzzle theoretically using realistic three-body ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$ and ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ wave functions computed within the ab initio no-core shell model approach with interactions derived from chiral effective field theory. In particular, $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ was found to be strongly correlated with the $Λ$ separation energy $B_Λ$ in ${}_Λ^3\mathrm{H}$, the value of which is rather poorly known experimentally and, in addition, is known to suffer from sizable theoretical uncertainties inherent in the employed nuclear and hypernuclear interaction models. In the present work we find that these uncertainties propagate into $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$, and thus limit considerably the theoretical precision of its computed value. Although none of the conflicting RHI measured $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})$ values can be excluded, but rather can be attributed to a poor knowledge of $B_Λ$, we note the good agreement between the lifetime value $τ({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})=238(27)$ ps computed at the lowest value $B_Λ=66$ keV reached by us and the very recent ALICE measured lifetime value $τ^{\mathrm{ALICE}}({}_Λ^3\mathrm{H})=253(11)(6)$ ps associated with the ALICE measured $B_Λ$ value $B^{\mathrm{ALICE}}_Λ=102(63)(67)$ keV [Phys. Rev. Lett. 131, 102302 (2023)].

## Key claim

The theoretical precision of the hypertriton lifetime is fundamentally limited by uncertainties in the \(\Lambda\) separation energy \(B_\Lambda\) and by systematic uncertainties in the nuclear and hypernuclear interaction models; while none of the conflicting experimental lifetime values can be excluded, the computed lifetime \(\tau({}_\Lambda^3\mathrm{H}) = 242(28)\) ps at \(B_\Lambda = 66\) keV agrees well with the ALICE measurement of \(253(11)(6)\) ps.

## Method

The authors computed the two-body \(\pi^-\) decay rate \(\Gamma({}_\Lambda^3\mathrm{H} \to {}^3\mathrm{He} + \pi^-)\) using realistic three-body wave functions for both hypernucleus and final nucleus, generated within the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) with chiral effective field theory interactions. They employed 42 parametrizations of the NN+NNN interaction (NNLOsim family) and four versions of the leading-order YN interaction with cutoffs from 550 to 700 MeV, incorporated distorted-wave pion wave functions from a \(\pi^-\)-\(^3\)He optical potential, and included \(\Sigma NN\) admixtures in the hypertriton wave function. The inclusive \(\pi^-\) decay rate was obtained using the measured branching ratio \(R_3 = 0.35(4)\) for the two-body channel, and the total lifetime was derived by applying the \(\Delta I = 1/2\) rule and adding nonmesonic and pion-absorption corrections.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton lifetime computed at \(B_\Lambda = 66\) keV: \(\tau({}_\Lambda^3\mathrm{H}) = 242(28)\) ps
- ALICE measured lifetime: \(\tau^{\mathrm{ALICE}}({}_\Lambda^3\mathrm{H}) = 253(11)(6)\) ps, with \(B_\Lambda^{\mathrm{ALICE}} = 102(63)(67)\) keV
- Free-\(\Lambda\) lifetime: \(\tau_\Lambda = 263(2)\) ps
- Pion momentum in the two-body decay: \(q_\pi = 114.4\) MeV, corresponding to pion energy \(E_\pi = 179.3\) MeV
- Branching ratio for the two-body \(\pi^-\) decay channel: \(R_3 = 0.35(4)\)
- Nonmesonic and pion-absorption corrections increase the decay rate by 1.5% and 0.8%, respectively, giving a total factor of 0.978 applied to the pionic lifetime

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Gazda et al., Phys. Lett. B 811, 135916, 2020) by systematically exploring theoretical uncertainties from chiral EFT interactions, and addresses the long-standing puzzle of conflicting hypertriton lifetime measurements from STAR, HypHI, and ALICE collaborations. The results show that the spread in experimental lifetimes can be attributed to poor knowledge of the \(\Lambda\) separation energy \(B_\Lambda\), and the calculation enables future work to constrain hypernuclear interactions by combining precise lifetime measurements with improved determinations of \(B_\Lambda\).
