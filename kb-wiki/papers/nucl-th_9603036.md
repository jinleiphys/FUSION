# Is it Possible to Describe Short-range NN Correlations in Nuclei on the Basis of Nucleonic Degrees of Freedom Only?

## Key claim

By constructing a phenomenological effective broad core (BC) NN potential with a repulsive core about 1.5–2 times broader than realistic potentials, it is possible to describe the Coulomb form factors of few-body nuclei (²H, ³H, ³He, ⁴He) using only nucleonic degrees of freedom, provided the low-energy NN phase shifts remain unchanged. The approach reduces the relative and absolute contribution of meson exchange currents (MEC) to charge form factors, making MEC a correction rather than a dominant effect.

## Method

The author constructed a central NN potential parameterized as a sum of five Gaussians, fitted to low-energy S-wave n-p phase shifts, the deuteron binding energy, and the ³H charge form factor, binding energy, and root mean squared charge radius. The repulsive core width was treated as the primary free parameter, yielding a core radius of approximately 1 fm (versus 0.5–0.6 fm for realistic potentials). Wave functions for A=2–4 systems were calculated using a variational method with a multidimensional Gaussian non-orthogonal basis, with odd partial waves (³P and ¹P) set to zero.

## Key numbers

- Repulsive core width of BC potential: approximately 1 fm, compared to 0.5–0.6 fm for realistic potentials (Nijm.II).
- BC potential describes NN phase shifts up to E_Lab ≈ 100 MeV.
- Weight of mixed-symmetry S' component in three-body wave functions: P ≈ 1.4%.
- Deuteron form factor described well up to q² ≈ 10 fm⁻².
- MEC contribution to the second maximum of three-body charge form factors reduces by a factor of about 1.5 relative to calculations with realistic NN potentials.
- Cutoff masses for πNN form factor: Λ_πNN = 0.6–0.8 GeV (soft) versus Λ_πNN ≈ 1.2 GeV commonly used.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kolesnikov (prior work), who first suggested that a very wide repulsive core could explain the position of the second maximum in ³He and ⁴He form factors. It contrasts with calculations using realistic NN forces plus MEC (e.g., by the groups cited as SR_2, SR_34, 3mec), which require high cutoff masses (Λ ≈ 1.2 GeV) that contradict modern nucleon structure calculations (Machleidt, vertex, skyrme). The BC potential reproduces the same charge densities as realistic potentials with MEC, and its additional repulsive bump at r ≈ 2.5 fm agrees qualitatively with the density-dependent NN potential from the σ-ω model of Walecka (sig_om). The approach enables the use of this effective potential in microscopic methods like the Resonating Group Method (RGM) for heavier nuclei, and suggests that soft meson-nucleon form factors (Λ_πNN = 0.6–0.8 GeV) are consistent with the reduced MEC contribution.
