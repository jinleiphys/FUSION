# The fusion dynamics for a positive Q-value system: $^{27}$Al+$^{45}$Sc using SEDF and role of spin-orbit interaction potential

> The fusion dynamics for a positive Q-value systems: $^{27}$Al+$^{45}$Sc, at near and deep sub-barrier energies has been investigated using the proximity potentials of Skyrme energy density formalism in semi classical extended Thomas Fermi approach for arbitrarily chosen Skyrme forces: SLy4, SIV, SGII and Proximity77 of Blocki and co-workers. The calculated fusion excitation functions for the proximity potentials obtained for Skyrme forces mentioned above and for the Proximity77 have been compared with experimental data. The proximity potential for Skyrme force SIV is found to be the best and is used in the calculations of the quantities like logarithmic derivative, barriers distributions and $S$-factor. Further, the role of spin-orbit interaction potential in the fusion dynamics of this system has been investigated.

## Key claim

The proximity potential derived from the Skyrme energy density formalism (SEDF) using the Skyrme force SIV reproduces the measured fusion excitation function for the positive Q-value system $^{27}$Al+$^{45}$Sc better than potentials from SLy4, SGII, or Proximity77, but only when the repulsive spin-orbit interaction potential is included in the total nuclear potential.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear interaction potential using the SEDF in the semi-classical extended Thomas-Fermi approach for three Skyrme forces (SLy4, SIV, SGII) and also used the Proximity77 pocket formula. The total interaction potential was obtained by adding the Coulomb potential, and the fusion cross-section as a function of center-of-mass energy was computed using Wong's formula. The spin-orbit interaction potential was then either included or excluded from the total potential to study its effect on the fusion excitation function, logarithmic derivative, barrier distribution, and astrophysical S-factor.

## Key numbers

- The Q-value for the $^{27}$Al+$^{45}$Sc system is 9.63 MeV.
- Including the spin-orbit interaction potential shifts the barrier position from 9.801 fm to 9.671 fm, changes the barrier height from 36.661 MeV to 36.987 MeV, and changes the barrier curvature from 2.91 MeV to 2.71 MeV.
- The fusion cross-section was measured down to about 300 nb at deep sub-barrier energies.
- The interaction barrier height including the spin-orbit term is 36.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of fusion hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies, a phenomenon first observed by Jiang (2002) for systems such as $^{60}$Ni+$^{89}$Y and later shown to be a general behavior for both positive and negative Q-value systems by Jiang (2006). The authors apply the SEDF approach, previously used by Verma (2009), to the $^{27}$Al+$^{45}$Sc system where standard coupled-channel calculations failed to reproduce experimental data from CL (2010). The inclusion of the repulsive spin-orbit term is analogous to adding repulsion to the proximity potential, similar to the shallow potential model used by Misicu and Esbensen (2007) and others to explain fusion hindrance. This work enables further investigation of whether SEDF potentials can describe fusion dynamics for other systems, particularly at deep sub-barrier energies where more experimental data are needed.
