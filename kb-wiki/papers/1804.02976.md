# Baryon number diffusion with critical fluctuations

> The description of dynamical fluctuations near the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions is crucial for understanding the existing and upcoming experimental data from the beam energy scan programs. In this talk we discuss the evolution of fluctuations of the net-baryon density as given by a stochastic diffusion equation. We study equilibrium as well as dynamical systems for which we can show the impact of nonequilibrium effects on the second-order moment.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a Gaussian stochastic diffusion equation for net-baryon density can capture critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point up to second-order moments, but only in a linear approximation that neglects non-Gaussian couplings and surface tension effects.

## Method
The authors numerically solved a one-dimensional stochastic diffusion equation for net-baryon density with periodic boundary conditions, using an implicit scheme. The free energy functional contained only a Gaussian term with criticality encoded via the temperature-dependent mass parameter \(m^2 \propto 1/\xi(T)^2\) motivated by the 3d Ising model equation of state. They studied both static equilibrium systems and dynamical systems with a time-dependent temperature following \(T(\tau)=T_0(\tau_0/\tau)^{dc_s^2}\) with \(d=3\) and \(c_s^2=1/3\).

## Key numbers
- Diffusion coefficient fixed at \(D=1\) fm, with \(\kappa = D n_c/T\) and \(n_c=1/(3\text{ fm}^3)\)
- Critical temperature \(T_c=0.15\) GeV and correlation length scale \(\xi_0=0.48\) fm
- System length \(L=10\) fm with lattice sizes \(N_x=64, 128, 256, 512\) (resolution \(\Delta x=0.078125\) fm)
- Initial temperature \(T_0=0.5\) GeV, cooling begins at \(\tau_0=1\) fm/c, critical temperature reached at \(\tau-\tau_0=2.33\) fm/c
- Three initial diffusion coefficients tested: \(D(\tau_0)=1, 0.1, 0.01\) fm

## Context
This work extends the study of dynamical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions from previous chiral-order-parameter models (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013, Herold 2016) to the net-baryon density as the critical mode, following the theoretical framework of Hohenberg (1977), Son (2004), and Fujii (2004). The results provide a benchmark for future inclusion of non-Gaussian couplings motivated by the 3d Ising universality class, and are relevant for interpreting beam energy scan data from experiments at RHIC.
