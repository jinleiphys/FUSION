# Longitudinal flow decorrelation in heavy-ion collision at RHIC energies using a multi-phase transport model

> We present a study on the longitudinal flow decorrelation in heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 to 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions) using the AMPT model. We measure the second and third order factorization ratios ($r_{2}$ and $r_{3}$) across BES energies, finding $r_{2}$ weakly dependent on collision energy while $r_{3}$ shows a more prominent collision energy dependent. The effect of parton-parton scattering cross section on $r_{2}$ and $r_{3}$ is studied which suggests that longitudinal decorrelation could be a potential observable to constrain transport properties of the medium. We also analyze the contributions of flow-plane and flow magnitude decorrelation, with flow-plane decorrelation being dominant. we measure a recently proposed observable, the four particle cumulant ($T_{2}$), which remains resilient to non-flow effects and exhibits sensitivity to different decorrelation patterns. Through the measurement of $T_{2}$, we consistently observe a hint of S-shaped or torqued decorrelation across all energy ranges in peripheral collisions (40-80\%). But in central and mid-central collisions, presence of any specific pattern of decorrelation is missing in AMPT model.

## Key claim
The AMPT model predicts that the second-order factorization ratio $r_2$ is weakly dependent on collision energy across RHIC BES energies (11.5 to 200 GeV), while the third-order ratio $r_3$ shows a more prominent energy dependence; however, the four-particle cumulant $T_2$ measurement only provides a "hint" of S-shaped (torqued) decorrelation in peripheral collisions (40-80%) and is consistent with zero in central and mid-central collisions, meaning no specific decorrelation pattern is conclusively established.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model (version v1.26t9b) in string-melting mode to simulate Au+Au collisions at six beam energies from 11.5 to 200 GeV. They measured the factorization ratios $r_2$ and $r_3$ using pseudorapidity windows matching STAR detector acceptance (TPC: $-1.5 < \eta_a < 1.5$, EPD: $2.1 < \eta_b < 5.1$) and extracted slope parameters $F_2$ and $F_3$ by fitting $r_n = e^{-2F_n \eta_a / y_{beam}}$. They separately decomposed decorrelation into flow-plane and flow-magnitude contributions and computed the four-particle cumulant $T_2$ to distinguish S-shaped from C-shaped decorrelation patterns.

## Key numbers
- $r_2$ and $r_3$ measured at six energies: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4$, and $200$ GeV in Au+Au collisions
- $p_T$ range for flow measurements: $0.2 - 4.0$ GeV/c
- Parton-parton cross sections used: 3 mb and 10 mb, corresponding to $\eta_s/s$ values of 0.229 (200 GeV, 3 mb) and 0.086 (200 GeV, 10 mb); at 19.6 GeV: 0.355 (3 mb) and 0.126 (10 mb)
- Average ratio $F_3/F_2 = 5.2 \pm 0.3$ in 10-40% centrality
- $T_2$ measured with transverse momentum cut $0.15 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/c and pseudorapidity windows $2.1 < |\eta_{f(b)}| < 5.1$

## Context
This work extends previous longitudinal flow decorrelation studies at LHC energies (CMS Collaboration, 2015) and at RHIC top energy (200 GeV) to the lower RHIC BES energy range (11.5-200 GeV). It uses the AMPT model, which has been successful in describing flow measurements at both RHIC and LHC energies. The study enables future comparisons with upcoming experimental data from the RHIC BES Phase II program and suggests that $r_2$ and $r_3$ measurements could constrain the shear viscosity to entropy ratio ($\eta_s/s$) of the quark-gluon plasma, building on prior work by Jun Xu (2016) on AMPT transport properties.
