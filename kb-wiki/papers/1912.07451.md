# Dirac spectrum and the BEC-BCS crossover in QCD at nonzero isospin asymmetry

> For large isospin asymmetries, perturbation theory predicts the QCD ground state to be a superfluid phase of $u$ and $\bar{d}$ Cooper pairs. This phase, which is denoted as the BCS phase, is expected to be smoothly connected to the standard phase with Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of charged pions at $μ_I\ge m_π/2$ by an analytic crossover. A first hint for the existence of the BCS phase, which is likely characterised by the presence of both, deconfinement and charged pion condensation, is coming from the lattice observation that the deconfinement crossover smoothly penetrates into the BEC phase. To further scrutinize the existence of the BCS phase, in this proceedings article we investigate the complex spectrum of the massive Dirac operator in 2+1-flavor QCD at nonzero temperature and isospin chemical potential. The spectral density near the origin is related to the BCS gap via a generalization of the Banks-Casher relation to the case of complex Dirac eigenvalues (derived for the zero-temperature, high-density limits of QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential).

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the BCS phase of QCD at large isospin asymmetry exists by studying the complex spectrum of the massive Dirac operator, using a generalization of the Banks-Casher relation to connect the spectral density near the origin to the BCS gap; this approach is derived for the zero-temperature, high-density limits and is applied to lattice QCD at nonzero temperature, so the connection between spectral observables and the BCS gap is only approximate at the finite temperatures simulated.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of quarks at nonzero temperature and isospin chemical potential, computing the complex eigenvalues of the massive Dirac operator. They then analyzed the spectral density near the origin of the complex eigenvalue plane, relating it to the BCS gap via a generalized Banks-Casher relation originally derived for zero temperature and high density. The study used configurations from the same ensembles that previously showed the deconfinement crossover smoothly penetrating into the BEC phase.

## Key numbers

* The BEC phase is expected to set in at isospin chemical potential mu_I >= m_pi/2, where m_pi is the pion mass.
* The study uses 2+1-flavor QCD (two light quarks and one strange quark) at nonzero temperature, though the specific temperature and chemical potential values are not given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies that observed the deconfinement crossover smoothly penetrating into the BEC phase of charged pion condensation, which provided a first hint for the existence of the BCS phase. The paper builds on the generalized Banks-Casher relation derived by earlier authors for the zero-temperature, high-density limits of QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential. If successful, this approach would enable direct lattice determination of the BCS gap and help confirm whether the BEC and BCS phases are connected by an analytic crossover as predicted by perturbation theory.
