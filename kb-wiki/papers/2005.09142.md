# The thermal conductivity of "pastas" in neutron star matter

> This investigation explores the phononic thermal conductivity of nuclear star matter as it undergoes the "topological" transition to the "pasta" regime, and further down to the solid-liquid phase transition. The study was carried out using molecular dynamics simulations with nuclear potentials embedded in an effective (i.e. Thomas-Fermi) Coulomb potential. The thermal conductivity experiences a dramatic change within a narrow temperature interval around T=1 MeV. This change accomplishes the "pasta" breakdown during a heating process. The thermal conductivity by flipping protons' or neutrons' velocity further shows a decoupling for asymmetric nuclear star matter.

## Key claim

The phononic thermal conductivity of neutron star matter in the "pasta" regime undergoes a dramatic change within a narrow temperature interval around T=1 MeV, with the "decoupling" between parallel and transverse conductivity being associated with the topological breakdown of pasta structures rather than a simple phase transition.

## Method

Classical molecular dynamics simulations were performed using Pandharipande nuclear potentials for neutron-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-proton interactions, with electron screening introduced through a Thomas-Fermi potential with an effective screening length of 20 fm. The Muller-Plathe non-equilibrium method was used to compute thermal conductivity by generating a heat flux through periodic velocity exchanges between particles in "hot" and "cold" bins, with systems of 4,000 nucleons in cubic cells at densities from 0.03 fm^-3 to 0.05 fm^-3. The thermal conductivity was measured separately for heat flux parallel and transverse to the pasta structures, and computed for all nucleons together or for protons and neutrons separately.

## Key numbers

- The "pasta" breakdown (topological transition) occurs near T=1.25 MeV for symmetric matter (x=0.5), as seen in the internal energy jump
- The thermal conductivity was measured over a temperature range from T=0.1 MeV to T=2.1 MeV
- System densities ranged from ρ=0.03 fm^-3 to ρ=0.05 fm^-3
- The screening length λ for the Thomas-Fermi potential was set to 10 fm (not 20 fm as stated in the text; the text gives both values)
- The cut-off distance for cluster recognition was set to rc=4 fm for symmetric matter and rc=5 fm for asymmetric matter
- The Muller-Plathe procedure attains a precision of 10% on a system of 2600 Lennard-Jones particles
- For asymmetric matter with proton ratio x=0.3, the cluster connecting process appears fulfilled near T=1 MeV

## Context

This work extends molecular dynamics studies of pasta phases in neutron star crusts, previously investigated for the equation of state by Dorso (2012) and collaborators, to the thermal transport properties. It addresses the controversy noted by Horowitz (2016) and Nandi (2018) regarding the role of nucleon thermal conductivity in late-time cooling of neutron stars like MXB 1659-29, where variations of an order of magnitude may arise from pasta alignment. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star thermal evolution by providing nucleonic conductivity values that account for the topological transition from solid pasta structures to homogeneous matter, complementing earlier work by Dunn (2016) on the role of pasta structures in energy transport.
