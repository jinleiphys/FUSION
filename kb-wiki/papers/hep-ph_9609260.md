# Quarkonium Production and Color Deconfinement in Nuclear Collisions

> The suppression of quarkonium production in nucleus-nucleus collisions was originally proposed as a signal of colour deconfinement. Strong ``anomalous" J/ψsuppression in Pb-Pb collisions has been reported at this Conference by the NA50 Collaboration. Is this suppression really anomalous? Can we conclude that the quark-gluon plasma is already discovered? What has to be done next? I address these questions basing on the current theoretical understanding of quarkonium production and new precise experimental information.

## Key claim

The anomalous J/ψ suppression observed in Pb-Pb collisions by the NA50 Collaboration goes beyond what can be explained by conventional nuclear absorption of a pre-resonance charmonium state, but the author cautions that this does not yet constitute definitive proof of quark-gluon plasma formation, as a unified microscopic approach combining screening and dynamical gluon dissociation still needs to be developed.

## Method

The author applies the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) to compute quarkonium dissociation cross sections from gluon structure functions, and uses Glauber theory with a pre-resonance absorption model to calculate J/ψ and ψ' survival probabilities in p-A and A-B collisions. The pre-resonance state is treated as a color-octet [c̄c]₈g state with transverse size ~0.20-0.25 fm, and its absorption cross section on nucleons is estimated by rescaling the high-energy J/ψ break-up cross section by the color factor 9/4. The centrality dependence is modeled via a wounded-nucleon approach relating transverse energy E_T to impact parameter, with Gaussian fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Absorption cross section for the pre-resonance charmonium state in nuclear matter: σ_abs = 6.3 ± 0.6 mb (95% C.L.) from NA38/51 p-A data at 200 and 450 GeV
- Absorption cross section from E772 data at 800 GeV: σ_abs = 7.4 ± 0.7 mb
- Estimated break-up cross section for the |[c̄c]₈g⟩ state on nucleons: σ_(c̄c g)N ≃ 6-7 mb
- Estimated break-up cross section for bottomonium pre-resonance states: σ_(b̄b g)N ≃ 1.5-2 mb
- J/ψ break-up cross section on nucleons at fixed-target energies: σ_(J/ψ N) ≃ 2.5-3 mb
- ψ'/(J/ψ) ratio in p-A collisions: α = 0.0 ± 0.02 (95% C.L.) from fit to A^α form
- 8 ± 2% of observed J/ψ's originate from ψ' decay in pp and pA collisions
- Transverse size of the |[c̄c]₈g⟩ state: r_⊥ ≃ (2m_c Λ_QCD)^{-1/2} ≃ 0.20-0.25 fm
- Central Pb-Pb collisions produce energy density about 25% higher than the maximum attainable in S-U collisions

## Context

This work extends the pre-resonance absorption model developed by Kharzeev and collaborators (1996) to explain J/ψ and ψ' suppression in p-A and light-ion collisions, building on the OPE-based calculations of quarkonium dissociation by Peskin (1979) and Kharzeev and Satz (1994). The analysis contradicts earlier claims that S-U data required deconfinement, showing instead that conventional nuclear absorption with σ_abs = 6.3 mb describes those data without anomalous suppression. The failure of this same approach for Pb-Pb collisions enables the interpretation that a deconfined medium may have been created, motivating future experiments at RHIC and the LHC to confirm the result and distinguish between screening and dynamical dissociation mechanisms.
