# Inelastic shadowing of secondary pi+-, K+-, p, and bar(p) produced in p+Pb collisions at LHC energy

> The inclusive spectra of secondaries produced in soft (minimum-bias) p+Pb collisions at LHC energy are calculated in the frame of the Quark-Gluon String Model, by including the inelastic screening corrections (percolation effects). These effects are expected to be quite large at the very high energies, and they should drive down the spectra in the midrapidity region more than 2 times, at sqrt(sNN)=5 TeV.

## Key claim

Inelastic nuclear screening corrections (percolation effects) in p+Pb collisions at LHC energy reduce the midrapidity inclusive spectra of secondary hadrons by a factor slightly larger than 2, and this suppression factor is practically the same for all produced particle species (π±, K±, p, and p̅), consistent with the interpretation that the screening originates from Pomeron interactions rather than from final-state interactions of the produced secondaries.

## Method

The authors used the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), based on Reggeon calculus and the 1/Nc expansion in QCD, to calculate inclusive rapidity spectra of π±, K±, p, and p̅ in minimum-bias p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5 TeV. Inelastic screening (percolation) effects were incorporated by limiting the maximum number of cut Pomerons per nucleon to nmax = 21, a value fixed by normalizing the total charged-particle multiplicity to ALICE data; the calculations also included baryon charge transfer via the string junction mechanism. The resulting spectra were compared to CMS experimental data for p+Pb and to QGSM calculations for pp collisions at the same energy.

## Key numbers

- The average number of inelastic collisions of the incident proton in the Pb target is ⟨ν⟩p+Pb ≈ 7.9, using σpp_inel ≈ 72 mb and σpPb_prod ≈ 1900 mb at √sNN = 5 TeV.
- The ratio r = (dn/dy in p+Pb) / (dn/dy in pp) in the midrapidity region (|y| ≤ 1) is 3.62–3.70 for all measured secondaries: π+ (3.70), π− (3.69), K+ (3.69), K− (3.66), p (3.63), p̅ (3.62).
- The QGSM calculation for total charged-particle multiplicity in p+Pb at midrapidity gives dnch/dη = 16.28, normalized to the ALICE experimental value of 16.81 ± 0.71 at √sNN = 5 TeV.
- The maximum number of Pomerons used in the percolation treatment is nmax = 21 at √sNN = 5 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the QGSM treatment of inelastic screening corrections previously applied to d+Au collisions at RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV) by C. Merino, C. Pajares, and Yu. M. Shabelski (2010) and by A. Capella, A. Kaidalov, and J. Tran Thanh Van (2000), where saturation effects were found to suppress inclusive densities by about a factor of 2. The present calculation shows that at LHC energies the suppression factor is slightly larger than 2 and is independent of the hadron species, which, if confirmed, would indicate that final-state interactions of secondaries are negligible. The results enable further tests of the Pomeron-interaction origin of nuclear screening and can be extended to hyperon production, where similar suppression factors are predicted with ∼5% accuracy.
