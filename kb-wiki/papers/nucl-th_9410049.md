# Where the Nuclear Pions are

> Three recent experiments, which looked at pionic effects in nuclei have concluded that there are no excess pions. This puts into serious question the conventional meson-exchange picture of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Based on arguments of partial restoration of chiral symmetry with density we propose a resolution to this problem.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent absence of excess pions in three recent experiments can be explained by partial restoration of chiral symmetry with density, which causes hadron masses (especially the nucleon and rho meson) to drop in the nuclear medium, rather than by a failure of the meson-exchange picture of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors employ a model based on Brown-Rho scaling, where hadron masses decrease with density according to the scaling relation m*/m ≈ fπ*/fπ, with the pion mass exempted. They incorporate the Holinde-Thomas interaction (which includes an elementary pion and a π' meson representing a correlated ρπ system) and calculate in-medium modifications of the spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse response functions using linear response theory within the Random-Phase-Approximation. For deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes, they use a two-phase model of the nucleon (bare quark core plus virtual meson-baryon pairs) with proper wave function renormalization constants.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon effective mass at saturation density is taken as mN*/mN = 0.7, with a linear density dependence mN*(ρ)/mN = 1 - 0.3(ρ/ρ0)
- The rho meson mass at saturation density is mρ*(ρ0)/mρ = 0.79 from the scaling relations
- The in-medium axial coupling constant at saturation density is gA*/gA = 0.99, giving gA* ≈ 1
- The pion mass increases by approximately 5 MeV at saturation density ρ0
- For the (p,n) polarization transfer experiment at q = 1.72 fm-1, the condition for zero net tensor force requires mρ*/mρ = 0.84, while the average density probed is 0.35 ρ0 where mρ*/mρ = 0.93
- The first moment of the pion distribution function for Fe (average density 0.87 ρ0) is M1π/A = 0.70 without scaling and M1π/A = 0.39 with Brown-Rho scaling, compared to the free nucleon value M1π/N = 0.41
- The cutoff parameter qc = 3.94 fm-1 for NN correlations and qc = 8.66 fm-1 for NΔ and ΔΔ transition potentials

## Context

This work addresses the puzzle raised by Bertsch et al. (1994) regarding three experiments (LAMPF polarization transfer, deep inelastic muon scattering, and Fermilab Drell-Yan) that found no evidence for excess pions in nuclei, contradicting conventional meson-exchange models. The authors extend the Brown-Rho scaling framework (Brown and Rho, 1991) to explain these results, building on earlier work by Holinde and Thomas (who introduced a π' meson to reconcile sea quark distributions with NN data) and by Janssen et al. (who identified the π' as a correlated ρπ system). The approach enables a unified explanation of all three experiments through dropping hadron masses, with the polarization transfer data most sensitive to the nucleon effective mass and the deep inelastic experiments more sensitive to the softening of the πNN vertex via the dropping rho meson mass.
