# Towards an effective field theory for vector mesons

> The assumption that vector mesons dominate the interactions of hadrons with electromagnetism (vector-meson dominance - VMD) provides an important phenomenological concept. On the other hand, a clear microscopic derivation is still missing and there are cases where VMD drastically fails, e.g. for the omega transition form factor. In principle, effective field theories with their systematic expansion and power counting could provide a tool to assess the validity of VMD and more generally to describe the interactions of vector mesons at low energies. Though the systematic development is still in an infant stage we present here a Lagrangian for light pseudoscalar and vector mesons which is inspired by ideas from effective field theories. The Lagrangian is used to calculate electromagnetic meson form factors. It turns out that one can reproduce both the successes of VMD concerning the pion form factors and the deviations from VMD concerning the omega transition form factor.

## Key claim

The authors present a Lagrangian for light pseudoscalar and vector mesons inspired by effective field theory ideas, but they acknowledge that a systematic low-energy expansion with a proven power counting has not yet been established for the energy region where vector mesons are important; the approach remains at present a phenomenological tree-level model, though one-loop calculations are underway to test its validity as a systematic effective field theory.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian for pions and vector mesons (rho, omega) using antisymmetric tensor fields for the vector mesons, combined with the leading-order chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian and the Wess-Zumino-Witten term. They determine the three free parameters (h_P, e_V, h_A) from two-body decays: rho to two pions, rho to e+e-, and omega to pi0 gamma. The Lagrangian is then used at tree level, with rescattering effects resummed via a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion form factor, to calculate electromagnetic meson form factors without imposing vector-meson dominance by hand.

## Key numbers

- |e_V| ≈ 0.22, determined from rho → e+e- decay
- h_P ≈ 0.30, determined from rho → 2π decay
- |h_A| ≈ 2.1, determined from omega → π0 γ decay
- The intrinsic hard scale of chiral perturbation theory is 4π f ≈ 1 GeV, where f ≈ 90 MeV is the pion decay constant
- The VMD prediction for the slope of the pion transition form factor is Λ_VMD = m_V ≈ 0.77 GeV, which agrees with the experimental result Λ_PDG = (0.76 ± 0.05) GeV

## Context

This work extends the framework of chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1978, Gasser and Leutwyler 1984) by including vector mesons as explicit degrees of freedom, motivated by the failure of vector-meson dominance for the omega transition form factor observed by NA60 and Lepton G experiments (Arnaldi 2009, Landsberg 1986). The approach builds on the hadrogenesis conjecture (Lutz 2001, 2003, 2008) and earlier work on vector mesons in effective field theories (Ecker 1988, 1989; Jenkins 1995). The results enable systematic calculation of electromagnetic form factors including the double-virtual pion transition form factor, which is important for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon gyromagnetic ratio (Jegerlehner 2009).
