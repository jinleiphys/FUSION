# Quark structure of pseudoscalar mesons

> I review to which extent the properties of pseudoscalar mesons can be understood in terms of the underlying quark (and eventually gluon) structure. Special emphasis is put on the progress in our understanding of eta-eta' mixing. Process-independent mixing parameters are defined, and relations between different bases and conventions are studied. Both, the low-energy description in the framework of Chiral Perturbation Theory and the high-energy application in terms of light-cone wave functions for partonic Fock states, are considered. A thorough discussion of theoretical and phenomenological consequences of the mixing approach will be given. Finally, I will discuss mixing with other states pi^0, eta(c), ...).

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing discrepancies in the value of the eta-eta' mixing angle (ranging from -10 degrees to -20 degrees in the literature) can be largely resolved by recognizing that a single mixing angle is insufficient: the decay constants connecting physical states to octet/singlet fields cannot be described by a simple rotation, requiring a more general parametrization scheme.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical foundations of pseudoscalar meson structure, starting from QCD chiral symmetry and the U(1)_A anomaly, then develops a mixing scheme based on the chiral effective Lagrangian for the pseudoscalar nonet including the eta' meson. The scheme uses the quark-flavor basis with consequent application of the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule, leading to a parametrization with a single mixing angle phi, and relates this to octet/singlet decay constants of eta and eta' mesons. The approach is applied to both low-energy chiral perturbation theory and high-energy exclusive reactions using light-cone wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The eta' mass was originally predicted by Schwinger (1964) as 1600 MeV, which is 50% too high due to neglect of SU(3)_F corrections
- Early mixing angle estimates include Isgur at theta_P approximately -10 degrees, Kramer et al. and Fritzsch/Jackson at theta_P approximately -11 degrees, and Diakonov/Eides (1981) at theta_P approximately -9 degrees
- Later phenomenological analyses found values around -20 degrees (Ball, Frere, Tytgat) and -15 degrees (Bramon et al.)
- Schechter et al. (1993) recovered a mixing scenario with theta_P approximately -13 degrees
- The eta' mass squared should behave as O(1/N_C) in the large-N_C limit of QCD

## Context

This review extends the long tradition of eta-eta' mixing studies dating back to Schwinger (1964) and builds on the work of Isgur, Kramer et al., Fritzsch and Jackson, and Diakonov and Eides (1981). It directly addresses the discrepancies between different analyses by Leutwyler and Kaiser (1997) and Kroll, Stech, and Feldmann (1997-1998), who showed that a single mixing angle is inadequate. The new scheme enables consistent description of eta and eta' properties across different phenomenological situations and energy scales, from low-energy chiral perturbation theory to hard exclusive reactions.
