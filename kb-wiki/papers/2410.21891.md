# Constraining the hidden-charm pentaquark predictions and discriminating the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ spins through the effective range expansion

> The Weinberg compositeness criterion dictates that a pure shallow bound state is characterized by a large scattering length $a_0\gg\mathcal{O}(1/β)$ and a positive effective range $r_0$ that naturally scales to the size of $\mathcal{O}(1/β)$, where $1/β$ signifies the interaction range. In constructing the contact-range effective field theory (EFT) up to the next-to-leading order to describe the pentaquarks $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$ observed by the LHCb collaboration in 2019, we match the effective range $r_0$ at single-channel situation for these pentaquarks with the low-energy couplings within the EFT framework. Three different schemes are used to connect the couplings with the effective range. We find positive effective ranges $r_0$ of the natural size of $\mathcal{O}(1/β)$ for the spin configurations $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^-$ for $P_c(4440)$ and $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$ for $P_c(4457)$ within the molecular $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ description. Additionally, predictions from the power counting for low-energy couplings or Wilsonian coefficients suggest that, under heavy quark spin symmetry, the broad $P_c(4380)$ resonance, discovered by the LHCb collaboration in 2015, when considered as part of the single-channel $\bar{D}^{(*)} Σ_c^{(*)}$ molecular system alongside $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$, has a mass of approximately $4376$ $\rm{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The authors use the Weinberg compositeness criterion to show that, within a contact-range effective field theory (EFT) description of the pentaquarks as molecular \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\Sigma_c\) bound states, the spin assignment where \(P_c(4440)\) has \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-\) and \(P_c(4457)\) has \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\) yields positive effective ranges of natural size, consistent with pure molecular states, while the opposite assignment does not.

## Method

The authors construct a contact-range effective field theory up to next-to-leading order (NLO) for the \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\Sigma_c\) systems, matching the low-energy couplings to the masses of \(P_c(4312)\), \(P_c(4440)\), and \(P_c(4457)\) via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Gaussian regulator. They extract the scattering length \(a_0\) and effective range \(r_0\) from the NLO potential using three different matching schemes: (I) neglecting the NLO spin-spin coupling \(D_b\); (II) including the broad \(P_c(4380)\) as an additional input; (III) varying \(D_b\) within a range dictated by EFT power counting.

## Key numbers

* For scenario B (\(P_c(4440)\) as \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-\), \(P_c(4457)\) as \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\)) in Scheme I with \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV, the effective ranges are \(r_0 = 0.61\) fm for \(P_c(4312)\), \(0.53\) fm for \(P_c(4440)\), and \(0.75\) fm for \(P_c(4457)\); with \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV, they are \(0.50\) fm, \(0.44\) fm, and \(0.58\) fm respectively.
* For scenario A in Scheme I with \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV, the effective ranges are negative: \(r_0 = -0.04\) fm for \(P_c(4312)\), \(-0.02\) fm for \(P_c(4440)\), and \(-0.06\) fm for \(P_c(4457)\).
* The predicted mass of the broad \(P_c(4380)\) resonance, when treated as part of the single-channel \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\Sigma_c^{(*)}\) molecular system under heavy quark spin symmetry, is approximately \(4376\) MeV.
* The binding momenta \(\gamma = \sqrt{2\mu E_B}\) for the \(P_c\) states in the molecular picture are about \(100\)–\(200\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the LHCb pentaquarks \(P_c(4312)\), \(P_c(4440)\), and \(P_c(4457)\) (LHCb, 2019) by applying the Weinberg compositeness criterion, originally developed for the deuteron (Weinberg, 1965), to discriminate between the spin assignments of \(P_c(4440)\) and \(P_c(4457)\). It builds on prior contact-range EFT studies of these states (Liu, 2019; Peng, 2020) and on heavy quark spin symmetry predictions of a seven-state pentaquark multiplet (Liu, 2019; Du, 2019). The results enable future experimental searches to distinguish the spin configurations and suggest that the broad \(P_c(4380)\) (LHCb, 2015) may not be a molecular partner of the other three pentaquarks, though its mass is consistent with the predicted value of about 4376 MeV.
