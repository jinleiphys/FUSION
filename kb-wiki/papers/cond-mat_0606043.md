# Zero Temperature Thermodynamics of Asymmetric Fermi Gases at Unitarity

> The equation of state of a dilute two-component asymmetric Fermi gas at unitarity is subject to strong constraints, which affect the spatial density profiles in atomic traps. These constraints require the existence of at least one non-trivial partially polarized (asymmetric) phase. We determine the relation between the structure of the spatial density profiles and the T=0 equation of state, based on the most accurate theoretical predictions available. We also show how the equation of state can be determined from experimental observations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that thermodynamic constraints, Monte Carlo simulations, analytic estimates, and experimental data together imply the existence of at least one nontrivial partially polarized phase in the zero-temperature equation of state of an asymmetric two-component Fermi gas at unitarity, but the authors note that within statistical errors the possibility of an empty partially polarized region cannot yet be ruled out by the value of the lower bound on the transition parameter Y1.

## Method

The authors analyze the grand-canonical phase diagram of a dilute two-component Fermi gas with s-wave interactions at unitarity, using the local density approximation (LDA) to relate the equation of state to spatial density profiles in atomic traps. They derive constraints on the dimensionless functions g(x) and h(y) that describe the energy density and pressure, using known forms for the fully polarized free Fermi gas and the fully paired superfluid phase characterized by the parameter ξ. They estimate the upper bound Y0 on the transition parameter y0 by solving the zero-range interaction boundary conditions for a single impurity fermion in a background of majority fermions, using lattice configurations to correct the continuum approximation.

## Key numbers

- ξ = 0.42(1) for the symmetric superfluid phase, from Monte Carlo data (Carlson 2005, ABCG 2004)
- Δ/εF = 0.504(24) for the pairing gap, where εF is the Fermi energy of the free gas at the same density (Carlson 2005)
- yc ≈ -0.099(15) for the critical transition parameter if no partially polarized phase exists
- Y1 = -0.09(3) for the lower bound on y1, derived from the constraint μ- ≤ Δ
- Y0 ≈ -0.54(4) for the upper bound on y0, estimated from the single-impurity calculation at unitarity
- γ = (1-y1)/(1-y0) = 0.70(5) from MIT experimental data (Zwierlein 2006), relating the transition radii in harmonic traps
- Pc = 0.70(5) for the critical polarization where the innermost phase transition reaches the trap center (MIT experiments, Zwierlein 2005, 2006)
- y1 ≈ 0.05 and y0 ≈ -0.4 extracted from experimental data using the relation γ = 0.70(5)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Cohen (2005) on the equation of state constraints for asymmetric Fermi gases at unitarity, and provides model-independent bounds that complement model-dependent analyses such as mean-field approaches and the work of Chevy (2006) and Heiselberg (2006). The results enable experimental extraction of the full equation of state from measured density profiles in atomic traps, and the tight constraints on g(x) and h(y) will help locate novel phases such as LOFF states, deformed Fermi surface states, or p-wave superfluid states. The analysis is strictly valid only at T=0, and the authors caution that finite temperature effects may alter the extracted values, particularly for y0.
