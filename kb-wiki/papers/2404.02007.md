# Uncovering the mechanism of chiral three-nucleon force in driving spin-orbit splitting

> The three-nucleon force (3NF) is crucial in shaping the shell structure of atomic nuclei, particularly impacting the enhancement of spin-orbit (SO) splitting, especially in nuclei with significant deviations from stability. Despite its importance, the specific mechanisms driving this enhancement remain unclear. In this study, we introduce a decomposition scheme based on the rank of irreducible tensors forming the 3NF, derived from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order, to elucidate their influence on SO splitting. Within the shell-model framework, our analysis reveals that the rank-1 component of the 3NF is the primary factor enlarging the energy gap between the $0p_{3/2}$ and $0p_{1/2}$ single-particle levels in $p$-shell nuclei, while the rank-2 component makes a subdominant contribution. Since the rank-1 component originates exclusively from the $2π$-exchange 3NF, our finding will not depend on the choice of the low-energy constants of contact terms. We also remark on the antisymmetry of the rank-1 3NF, which can affect the quantum entanglement of spin states. This study lays the groundwork for further exploration into this field toward a microscopic understanding of the 3NF impact on the nuclear shell structure.

## Key claim

The rank-1 irreducible tensor component of the chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) at next-to-next-to-leading order, which originates exclusively from the two-pion-exchange process, is the primary driver of spin-orbit splitting enhancement in p-shell nuclei, while the rank-2 component makes a subdominant contribution and the rank-3 component is negligible. This finding is independent of the choice of low-energy constants for contact terms because the rank-1 component arises solely from the two-pion-exchange 3NF.

## Method

The authors decomposed the chiral 3NF at next-to-next-to-leading order into irreducible tensor components of rank lambda (0, 1, 2, 3) based on the spin-momentum structure of each term (contact, one-pion exchange plus contact, and two-pion exchange with its c1, c3, c4 constituents). They then implemented this decomposition within the shell-model framework for p-shell nuclei, deriving an effective Hamiltonian for the 0p-shell valence space outside doubly magic He-4 using many-body perturbation theory with the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion, including normal-ordered one-body and normal-ordered two-body terms of the 3NF.

## Key numbers

* The rank-1 component of the 3NF accounts for approximately 75% of the total 3NF-induced enhancement of the effective single-particle energy gap between 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 in Be-8 and C-12.
* The rank-2 component contributes approximately 20% to the gap enhancement in Be-8 and C-12, while the rank-3 component contributes only about 2% to the splitting outside He-4.
* In C-12, the full 3NF approximately doubles the spin-orbit splitting computed with only the two-nucleon force, from 4.29 MeV to 8.81 MeV for protons and from 4.36 MeV to 10.02 MeV for neutrons.
* The low-energy constants were set to c_D = -1.00 and c_E = -0.34, with a nonlocal regulator cutoff of 500 MeV for both two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering tensor decomposition study by Ando and Bando (1981), who reported that the rank-1 tensor of the Fujita-Miyazawa and Tucson-Melbourne 3NFs enhances spin-orbit splitting in O-16 and Ca-40, but did not explore other tensor ranks. The present study provides a systematic decomposition of the modern chiral 3NF and demonstrates the dominance of the rank-1 component from two-pion exchange, which is independent of contact-term low-energy constants. This enables future investigations into whether the rank-1 3NF can account for specific observables in proton-deuteron scattering, such as linear combinations of vector analyzing powers, and motivates extension of the analysis to heavier pf-shell nuclei where global 3NF effects have already been evidenced by previous work.
