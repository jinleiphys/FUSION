# Anisotropic flow far from equilibrium

> We compute analytically the anisotropic flow in an expanding mixture of several species of relativistic massive particles. We find that a single collision per particle in average already leads to sizable elliptic flow, with mass ordering between the species.

## Key claim

The authors compute analytically that a single collision per particle on average already produces sizable elliptic flow with mass ordering between particle species, even far from equilibrium, a regime where the system has not undergone enough rescatterings to reach local thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The expanding system is modeled as a two-dimensional mixture of several relativistic massive particle species governed by the Boltzmann equation, solved analytically in the limit of a small number of collisions per particle by expanding the distribution function around the free-streaming solution. The flow harmonics \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) are derived from the loss term of the collision integral, integrated over time, position, and azimuthal angles, with the cross section fixed so that the average number of collisions per particle is at most one.

## Key numbers

* Elliptic flow in the massless gas limit (both \(\beta_i = \beta_k = 1\)), with cross section set so particles rescatter at most once in average: \(v_2/\epsilon \simeq 0.083\), with less than 5% deviation up to \(\epsilon = 0.75\).
* Lorentz gas limit (massless diffusing particles on infinitely massive fixed centers, \(\beta_i = 1\), \(\beta_k = 0\)), with a single rescattering per particle in average for \(\epsilon = 0\): \(v_2 \simeq 0.25\,\epsilon\), \(v_4 \simeq -0.094\,\epsilon^2\), giving \(v_4/v_2^2 \simeq -1.5\) across a wide \(\epsilon\) range.
* For small \(\beta_i\) and non-vanishing \(\beta_k\), the velocity-dependent functions scale as \({\cal F}_2 \sim \beta_i^2/8\) and \({\cal F}_4 \sim -\beta_i^4/128\), leading to \(v_n(p_i) \propto p_i^n\) in the non-relativistic regime.
* The eccentricity dependence: \(v_2\) scales linearly with \(\epsilon\) for small eccentricities (less than 5% deviation up to \(\epsilon = 0.75\)); \(v_4\) scales quadratically with \(\epsilon\) (less than 5% deviation up to \(\epsilon = 0.45\)).

## Context

This work extends the only previous analytical attempt by Heiselberg (1998), which treated non-relativistic particles in the low-eccentricity limit, to relativistic massive particles across all centralities and momenta. The results show that the mass ordering of elliptic flow, a signature usually associated with hydrodynamic behavior in the many-collision regime, already appears after a single collision per particle, challenging the interpretation of mass ordering as evidence of local thermal equilibrium. The analytical expressions enable future comparisons with experimental data from peripheral collisions and with kinetic-theory simulations in the few-collision regime.
