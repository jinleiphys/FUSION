# Multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions using canonical and grand-canonical ensemble

> We report the higher order cumulants and their ratios for baryon, charge and strangeness multiplicity in canonical and grand-canonical ensembles in ideal thermal model including all the resonances. When the number of conserved quanta is small, an explicit treatment of these conserved charges is required, which leads to a canonical description of the system and the fluctuations are significantly different from the grand canonical ensemble. Cumulant ratios of total charge and net-charge multiplicity as a function of collision energies are also compared in grand canonical ensemble.

## Key claim
The paper shows that higher-order cumulant ratios (C3/C2 and C4/C2) for total charge multiplicity in the canonical ensemble (CE) approach the grand-canonical ensemble (GCE) values faster than the lower-order ratio C2/C1 as the system size (parameter z) increases, but only when the explicit net charge of the system is zero; for non-zero net charge the convergence is slower.

## Method
The authors calculate cumulants of total baryon, electric charge, and strangeness multiplicity distributions using the hadron resonance gas model in both the canonical ensemble (CE) and grand-canonical ensemble (GCE), including all resonances. For CE, they use partition functions with explicit conservation of net charge (ΔQ = 0, 1, 2) and evaluate cumulants via modified Bessel functions. For GCE, they use Boltzmann approximation and parametrized freeze-out conditions (temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB as functions of collision energy √sNN) from Cleymans (2005).

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature parametrization: T(μB) = a − bμB² − cμB⁴ with a = 0.166 ± 0.002 GeV, b = 0.139 ± 0.016 GeV⁻¹, c = 0.053 ± 0.021 GeV⁻³.
- Energy dependence of baryon chemical potential: μB(√sNN) = d/(1 + e√sNN) with d = 1.308 ± 0.028 GeV and e = 0.273 ± 0.008 GeV⁻¹.
- For total baryons in GCE, all cumulant ratios (C2/C1, C3/C2, C4/C2) are unity at all collision energies, following Poisson expectation.
- For total strangeness in GCE, C2/C1 decreases with increasing collision energy, unlike total baryon and total charge which remain constant.
- For net-charge and total charge in GCE, C4/C2 ratios are identical as a function of √sNN, while C3/C2 ratios differ strongly.

## Context
This work extends the study of multiplicity fluctuations in thermal models from earlier work by Begun (2004) and Hauer (2007, 2009), who showed that C2/C1 in CE and GCE become equivalent only in the large-volume limit. The paper provides the first systematic comparison of higher-order cumulant ratios (C3/C2 and C4/C2) between CE and GCE for baryon, charge, and strangeness. The results enable experimental searches for the QCD critical point at RHIC Beam Energy Scan by providing a thermal baseline: the authors argue that total charge fluctuations (which have the same C4/C2 as net-charge) should be measured experimentally and compared to these thermal model predictions to look for non-monotonic behavior.
