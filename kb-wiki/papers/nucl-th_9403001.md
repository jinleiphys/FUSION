# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations and Final State Interaction in Inclusive Quasi-Elastic Electron Scattering off Nuclei at x>1

> Inclusive quasi-elastic electron scattering off nuclei is investigated at high momentum transfer (Q^2>1 (GeV/c)^2) and x>1 adopting a consistent treatment of nucleon-nucleon correlations in initial and final states. It is shown that in case of light as well as complex nuclei the inclusive cross section at 1.3<x<2 is dominated by the absorption of the virtual photon on a pair of correlated nucleons and by their elastic rescattering in the continuum, whereas at x>2 it is governed by the rescattering of the outgoing off-mass-shell nucleon in the complex optical potential generated by the ground state of the residual (A-1)-nucleon system.

# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations and Final State Interaction in Inclusive Quasi-Elastic Electron Scattering off Nuclei at x>1

## Key claim

The inclusive cross section at 1.3 < x < 2 is dominated by virtual photon absorption on a pair of correlated nucleons followed by their elastic rescattering in the continuum, while at x > 2 it is governed by rescattering of the outgoing off-mass-shell nucleon in a complex optical potential, but only if off-shell suppression factors are included in the final state interaction, without which on-shell optical potentials grossly overestimate the cross section and disagree with experiment.

## Method

The authors separate the inclusive (e,e') cross section into two contributions: σ₀ from transitions to ground and one-hole states of the (A-1) system, and σ₁ from transitions to more complex excited configurations (mainly 2p-2h states from ground-state NN correlations). For σ₁, they replace the plane-wave final state of the correlated pair with the exact NN scattering wave function (two-nucleon rescattering), using the same two-nucleon amplitudes computed for the deuteron with the RSC potential. For σ₀, they treat single-nucleon rescattering via an eikonal optical potential that includes an exponential off-shell suppression factor exp(-δ|M² - p'²|) to account for the virtuality of the struck nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer: Q² > 1 (GeV/c)² throughout the study
- Kinematic region of interest: x > 1.3, where x = Q² / 2Mν and 1.3 corresponds approximately to 1 + k_F/M
- The correlated part of the spectral function P₁ is evaluated for nucleon momenta k > 1.5 fm⁻¹ and removal energies E ~ k²/2M
- Calculations performed for ²H, ⁴He, and ⁵⁶Fe at Q² ~ 2 (GeV/c)²
- The two-nucleon rescattering mechanism brings theory into agreement with data in the range 1.3 < x < 2
- At x > 2, the single-nucleon rescattering with off-shell suppression is required to match experimental data

## Context

This work extends earlier plane-wave Impulse Approximation (IA) calculations by several authors (1990-1993) that showed NN correlations affect the x > 1 region, but contradicts the finding of a 1993 study that on-shell optical potential FSI completely overwhelms correlation effects. The authors reconcile these conflicting results by showing that on-shell optical potentials are unjustified at x > 1 because the struck nucleon is highly virtual, and they introduce a consistent treatment where both initial-state correlations and final-state interactions are treated on the same footing. The approach enables a unified description of inclusive scattering from deuterons to complex nuclei like ⁵⁶Fe, and provides a framework for extracting information about short-range correlations from inclusive data.
