# T-matrix in discrete oscillator representation

> We investigate T-matrix for bound and continuous-spectrum states in the discrete oscillator representation. The investigation is carried out for a model problem - the particle in the field of a central potential. A system of linear equations is derived to determine the coefficients of the T-matrix expansion in the oscillator functions. We selected four potentials (Gaussian, exponential, Yukawa, and square-well ones) to demonstrate peculiarities of the T-matrix and its dependence on the potential shape. We also study how the T-matrix expansion coefficients depend on the parameters of the oscillator basis such as the oscillator length and the number of basis functions involved in calculations.

## Key claim

The T-matrix expansion in a discrete oscillator basis converges for bound and scattering states of a particle in a central potential, but the rate of convergence depends strongly on the shape of the potential, the oscillator length, and the number of basis functions; the Gaussian potential converges much faster than the exponential, Yukawa, or square-well potentials.

## Method

The authors transform the Schrödinger equation and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the half-off-shell T-matrix into a discrete oscillator representation using a complete set of oscillator functions in coordinate and momentum space. They solve the resulting systems of linear equations for the T-matrix expansion coefficients (both as a vector and as a matrix) for four central potentials: Gaussian, exponential, Yukawa, and square-well. Calculations are performed for the s-wave (L=0) only, using up to 500 oscillator functions and four values of the oscillator length (b = 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0 fm), with the constant ħ²/m = 41.47 MeV·fm².

## Key numbers

- Bound state energies for four potentials (a = 1 fm, V₀ = -85 MeV): Gaussian -3.564 MeV, exponential -7.006 MeV, Yukawa -26.744 MeV, square-well -9.388 MeV (obtained with N=300 functions and b = 1.0 fm).
- For the square-well potential, the T-matrix calculated with N=50 and N=75 oscillator functions is sufficiently close to the exact T-matrix, while N=25 gives noticeable deviations.
- For the Yukawa potential, at least N=125 oscillator functions are needed to be close to the "exact" T-matrix.
- The upper limit of momenta covered by N oscillator functions is given by p ≤ √(4N+2L+3)/b; with b = 2.0 fm, nonzero T-matrix values are obtained for p ≤ 17 fm⁻¹.
- Phase shifts are calculated for energy E = 5.0 MeV to study convergence with N.

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix method (also known as the algebraic version of the resonating group method), formulated by Filippov and Okhrimenko (1980) and by Heller and Yamani (1975), by providing the first systematic investigation of the T-matrix itself in the discrete oscillator representation rather than just wave functions and phase shifts. Prior work by Rakityansky et al. (2001) used the oscillator basis to calculate phase shifts and the T-matrix for a Gaussian potential, but only in momentum space. The results enable more efficient selection of oscillator basis parameters (size and oscillator length) for scattering calculations in nuclear, atomic, and molecular systems, and they identify that the Gaussian potential converges much faster than potentials with a tail (exponential, Yukawa) or a sharp cutoff (square-well).
