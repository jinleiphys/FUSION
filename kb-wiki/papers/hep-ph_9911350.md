# High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Highlights of the results from ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS are reviewed. In particular, I discuss how the experimental results indicate that a collective strongly interacting system has been produced, and what are the implications towards the Quark Gluon Plasma. The physical ideas behind measuring certain observables are introduced. The future program of high energy nuclear collisions at BNL-RHIC and CERN-LHC/ALICE is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The experimental results from ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS indicate that a collective strongly interacting system has been produced, but the implications for Quark Gluon Plasma formation remain indirect; the observed signals are consistent with a thermalized system but do not constitute definitive proof of deconfinement.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental highlights from fixed-target Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS with beam energy 158 A GeV (nucleon-nucleon cms energy √s = 17.3 GeV), analyzing transverse momentum spectra, azimuthal flow asymmetries, particle ratios, two-pion interferometry (HBT), event-by-event fluctuations, and strangeness enhancement measurements from the NA44, NA49, NA50, WA97, and WA98 collaborations. Thermal model fits to particle ratios and comparisons with hadron-hadron collision data are used to extract freeze-out parameters and quantify collective behavior.

## Key numbers

- Critical deconfinement temperature T_c at μ_B=0: about 265 MeV for pure SU(3) glue, with quark estimates in the range T_c = 140–200 MeV
- Best thermal model fit to SPS particle ratios gives T_fo = 170 MeV and μ_B^fo = 270 MeV
- HBT analysis combined with m_T-distributions yields freeze-out temperature range 80 MeV < T_fo < 120 MeV, transverse flow velocity 0.35 < v_T < 0.5, transverse extension R_fo ~ 2R_Pb, and lifetime ~ 10 fm/c
- Global strangeness enhancement factor λ_s^{AA} ≈ 2 λ_s^{pp} in A+A collisions relative to p+p and e⁺+e⁻ collisions
- Dynamical event-by-event fluctuations in Pb+Pb at SPS: <1% in single-event ⟨p_T⟩ and <4.9% in K/π ratio (at 90% confidence level)
- SPS beam energies: E_b = 158 A GeV for Pb-beam (√s = 17.3 GeV), with lower energy run at E_b = 40 A GeV planned; future RHIC Au+Au at √s = 200 A GeV and LHC Pb+Pb at √s = 5500 A GeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS, building on earlier reviews by Heinz (1999), Blaizot (1999), and Bass (1999). The observed collective flow, thermal particle ratios, and strangeness enhancement support the interpretation of a thermalized strongly interacting system, but definitive observation of the QCD phase transition requires the higher energies and luminosities of the upcoming RHIC and LHC/ALICE programs. The results enable quantitative tests of hydrodynamic models and thermal freeze-out scenarios, while open questions remain regarding the behavior of multistrange baryons (Ω, Ξ) and the search for critical fluctuations at the QCD phase boundary.
