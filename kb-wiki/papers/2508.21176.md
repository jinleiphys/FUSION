# Probing the onset of collectivity via scaled particle spectra in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> We identify a novel scaling in the transverse momentum spectra of produced particles, obtained by removing the global scales of multiplicity and mean transverse momentum. Hydrodynamic simulations and experimental data reveal an almost universal scaled spectrum across centralities, systems, and even small systems, pointing to its origin in the collective, fluid-like dynamics of the QGP. Comparing this observable with Bayesian a priori distributions shows its independent constraining power on QCD transport properties, while also exposing limitations of current models. A detailed posterior analysis will be pursued in future work, opening a new avenue to refine our understanding of collectivity in heavy-ion collisions.

# Probing the onset of collectivity via scaled particle spectra in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The paper identifies a novel scaling in transverse momentum spectra after removing global scales of multiplicity and mean transverse momentum, which collapses onto a nearly universal curve across centralities and systems (Pb-Pb, Xe-Xe, p-Pb) in both hydrodynamic simulations and experimental data. However, the scaling breaks down at high transverse momentum and in very small systems (p-p), and the authors note that current state-of-the-art models may not capture the observed universal shape when compared to experimental data.

## Method

The authors define a scaled spectrum U(x_T) = (⟨p_T⟩/N) dN/dp_T = (1/N) dN/dx_T, where x_T = p_T/⟨p_T⟩, and analyze this observable in event-by-event hybrid simulations combining initial conditions, pre-equilibrium stage, 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics, particlization, and hadronic scattering. They compare simulation results for pions, kaons, and protons across seven centrality bins using parameters from Bayesian analyses of Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and Au-Au at 0.2 TeV, and then compare with experimental data from ALICE for Pb-Pb, Xe-Xe, p-Pb, and p-p collisions. Gaussian emulators trained on JETSCAPE simulation data are used to compare prior distribution predictions with experimental U(x_T).

## Key numbers

- Hybrid simulations used 5,000 events across seven centrality bins for Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV and Au-Au at 0.2 TeV
- Experimental data analyzed include Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, Xe-Xe at 5.44 TeV, p-Pb at 5.02 TeV, and p-p at 7 TeV
- 99% of pions are produced to the left of the vertical gray lines shown in the experimental data figures
- The 90% credible intervals from prior predictions are shown as light blue bands for Pb-Pb collisions at 0-5%, 10-20%, and 50-60% centrality

## Context

This work extends the finding of Muncinelli (2024) that scaled spectra become independent of centrality in event-by-event hybrid simulations, now demonstrating the effect across particle species (pions, kaons, protons) and in experimental data from multiple collision systems. The scaling provides evidence for hydrodynamic behavior in p-Pb collisions and possibly in high-multiplicity p-p collisions, complementing traditional anisotropic flow analyses from the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020). The results motivate future Bayesian posterior analyses to refine constraints on QCD transport properties, as current models calibrated on p_T-integrated observables (such as the JETSCAPE MAP parameter set) are inconsistent with the observed universal shape.
