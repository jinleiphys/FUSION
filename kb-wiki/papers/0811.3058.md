# Three-dimensional angular momentum projected relativistic point-coupling approach for the low-lying excited states in $^{24}$Mg

> A full three-dimensional angular momentum projection on top of a triaxial relativistic mean-Geld calculation is implemented for the first time. The underlying Lagrangian is a point coupling model and pairing correlations are taken into account by a monopole force. This method is applied for the low-lying excited states in 24Mg. Good agreement with the experimental data is found for the ground state properties. A minimum in the potential energy surface for the 2+ state, with beta = 0.55, gamma = 10 deg, is used as the basis to investigate the rotational energy spectrum as well as the corresponding B(E2) transition probabilities as compared to the available data.

## Key claim

A full three-dimensional angular momentum projection on top of a triaxial relativistic mean-field calculation with point-coupling interactions is implemented for the first time, and applied to the low-lying excited states of $^{24}$Mg. The approach qualitatively reproduces the observed excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities, but the predicted level spacing is overestimated, likely because the variation is not carried out after projection.

## Method

The intrinsic mean-field state is obtained by minimizing an energy functional derived from a relativistic point-coupling Lagrangian (parameter set PC-F1) with pairing correlations treated via a monopole BCS approach. A full three-dimensional angular momentum projection is then performed on this triaxial intrinsic state to restore rotational symmetry, and the projected states are used to calculate the rotational energy spectrum and B(E2) transition probabilities for $^{24}$Mg. The calculations are carried out by expanding the wavefunctions in an isotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 8 major shells, and the overlap kernels are evaluated using a Gauss-Legendre quadrature over the Euler angles.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field potential energy surface minimum for $^{24}$Mg is at $E = -193.57$ MeV with $\beta_p = 0.48$, compared to the experimental binding energy of $E = -198.26$ MeV (Audi 2003).
- The energy gain from restoring rotational symmetry in the ground state of $^{24}$Mg is about 5 MeV.
- The minimum on the projected $0^+$ state potential energy surface has $\beta_p = 0.56$, close to the experimental value $\beta = 0.57$ (Fewell 1979).
- The minimum on the projected $2^+$ state potential energy surface is at $\beta \approx 0.55$, $\gamma \approx 10^\circ$.
- The numerical integration over Euler angles uses $N_\theta = 14$, $N_\phi = N_\psi = 24$, achieving a precision of $0.001\%$ in the energy of a projected state with angular momentum up to $J=6$.
- The pairing strength constants are $G_n = 34.60/A$ for neutrons and $G_p = 33.75/A$ for protons.

## Context

This work extends previous angular momentum projection studies on $^{24}$Mg that were restricted to axially symmetric mean-fields, such as those using the Skyrme SLy4 force (Valor 2000), the Gogny D1S force (Guzman 2002), and the relativistic point-coupling model with PC-F1 force (Niksic 2006). By introducing triaxial deformation at the mean-field level and performing full three-dimensional angular momentum projection, the approach can now describe the $K=2$ band, which was inaccessible in axially symmetric calculations. The results indicate that a full Generator Coordinate Method approach is needed for a quantitative understanding of the low-lying states in $^{24}$Mg, and work toward this goal is in progress.
