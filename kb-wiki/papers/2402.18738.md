# QCD at Finite Temperature and Density -- Equation of State

> As an important set of thermodynamic quantities, knowledge of the equation of state over a broad range of temperatures and chemical potentials in the QCD phase diagram is crucial for our understanding of strongly-interacting matter. There is a good understanding from first-principles results in lattice QCD, perturbative QCD and chiral effective field theory about the equation of state. However, these approaches are valid in different regimes of the phase diagram, and therefore, a method of providing an equation of state that covers a full range of the phase diagram involves matching together these results with appropriate models in order to fill in the gaps between these regions. Furthermore, with such equations of state, important questions about QCD phase structure can begin to be addressed, such as whether there is a critical point in the QCD phase diagram. In this contribution to the proceedings, equations of state from first-principles and effective theories will be discussed in order to understand how QCD thermodynamics is affected by the presence of a critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that equilibrium proton fluctuations near a putative QCD critical point can be estimated using universal properties of the 3D Ising model, but the magnitude of these fluctuations is highly sensitive to the chosen non-universal mapping parameters and the path of the freeze-out curve through the phase diagram, meaning that direct comparison to experimental data requires further out-of-equilibrium treatment.

## Method

The author maps the 3D Ising model onto the QCD phase diagram using a non-universal parameterization (Eqs. 1-2) and computes the 3rd and 4th order proton fluctuations (Eq. 7) by incorporating the critical correlation length from universality to second order in the epsilon-expansion (Eq. 5) and mu_B-dependent higher-order couplings lambda_3 and lambda_4. The mapping parameters are chosen to be consistent with STAR experimental results and constraints from universality, causality, and stability, moving away from the orthogonal Ising axes used in the BEST equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Critical point chemical potential: mu_B,c = 420 MeV
- Angle difference between Ising axes: Delta alpha = alpha_1 - alpha_2 = 10 degrees
- Scaling parameters: w = 8, rho = 0.2
- Proton coupling to sigma field: g_p ~ 7 (from Athanasiou (2010))
- Fourth order proton fluctuations along the experimentally fitted freeze-out curve at Delta T = 5 MeV are of order 10^2
- Freeze-out curves considered at 3, 5, and 7 MeV below the critical point

## Context

This work extends the approach of Athanasiou (2010) for calculating particle fluctuations near a QCD critical point by incorporating updated universal inputs for the correlation length and mu_B-dependent couplings, and builds on the BEST equation of state framework developed by Parotto (2018) and Karthein (2021). The results are intended to serve as equilibrium input for out-of-equilibrium fluctuation calculations following the Gaussian fluctuation treatment of Pradeep, Rajagopal, Stephanov, and Yin (2022), enabling future comparison with data from the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and constraints on the non-universal mapping between the 3D Ising model and QCD.
