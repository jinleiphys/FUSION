# Cross-shell states in $^{15}$C: a test for p-sd interactions

> The low-lying structure of $^{15}$C has been investigated via the neutron-removal $^{16}$C$(d,t)$ reaction. Along with bound neutron sd-shell hole states, unbound p-shell hole states have been firmly confirmed. The excitation energies and the deduced spectroscopic factors of the cross-shell states are an important measure of the $[(p)^{-1}(sd)^{2}]$ neutron configurations in $^{15}$C. Our results show a very good agreement with shell-model calculations using the SFO-tls interaction for $^{15}$C. However, a modification of the $p$-$sd$ and $sd$-$sd$ monopole terms was applied in order to reproduce the $N=9$ isotone $^{17}$O. In addition, the excitation energies and spectroscopic factors have been compared to the first calculations of $^{15}$C with the $ab~ initio$ self-consistent Green's function method employing the NNLO$_{sat}$ interaction. The results show the sensitivity to the size of the $N=8$ shell gap and highlight the need of going beyond the current truncation scheme in the theory.

## Key claim

The low-lying structure of 15C has been investigated via the neutron-removal 16C(d,t) reaction, and the results show very good agreement with shell-model calculations using the SFO-tls interaction, but only after a modification of the p-sd and sd-sd monopole terms was applied to reproduce the N=9 isotone 17O. The excitation energies and spectroscopic factors compared to ab initio self-consistent Green's function calculations using the NNLOsat interaction show sensitivity to the size of the N=8 shell gap and highlight the need to go beyond the current truncation scheme in the theory.

## Method

The experiment was performed at GANIL using a secondary 16C beam at 17.2 MeV/nucleon incident on a CD2 target, with tritons from the (d,t) reaction detected by three MUST2 telescopes in the forward hemisphere and beam-like fragments detected in a zero-degree telescope. Excitation energies were reconstructed using the missing-mass technique, and angular distributions were compared to finite-range Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations using the code FRESCO to deduce spectroscopic factors, normalized to a total sd-shell occupancy of 2. Asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) were also extracted by varying the Woods-Saxon potential radius from 1.15 to 1.35 fm.

## Key numbers

* Ground state (1/2+): excitation energy -0.30(+0.18/-0.22) MeV, spectroscopic factor C2S = 0.65(16), transferred angular momentum l = 0
* First excited state (5/2+): excitation energy 1.10(+0.28/-0.16) MeV, spectroscopic factor C2S = 1.35(32), transferred angular momentum l = 2
* Resonance at 2.76(7) MeV (1/2-): spectroscopic factor C2S = 1.74(23), transferred angular momentum l = 1, ANC squared = 15.78(31) fm-1
* Resonance at 5.41(11) MeV (3/2-): spectroscopic factor C2S = 1.25(24), transferred angular momentum l = 1, ANC squared = 26.85(54) fm-1
* The summed spectroscopic factor for the 1/2- state represents 87% of the total 0p1/2 occupancy assuming independent particle filling
* The maximum single-particle strength for the 0d5/2 orbital accounts for 84.5(88)% of the total based on Macfarlane-French sum rule

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 15C using single-neutron adding reactions such as 14C(d,p) by Cecil, Goss, Murillo, and Kay, two-neutron transfer reactions by Truong and Cappuzzello, and knockout reactions by Sauvan, Maddalena, and Yamaguchi, which provided extensive information on positive-parity states but little on negative-parity cross-shell states. The results provide the first firm confirmation of unbound p-shell hole states in 15C via a single-neutron removal reaction, enabling stringent tests of both phenomenological shell-model interactions like SFO-tls and ab initio calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions such as NNLOsat. The data constrain the N=8 shell gap and the p-sd monopole terms in effective interactions, and highlight the need for improved truncation schemes in ab initio methods to capture 2p-1h configurations.
