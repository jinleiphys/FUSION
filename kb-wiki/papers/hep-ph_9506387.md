# Thermal effects on dilepton production from $π-π$ annihilation

> We study finite temperature effects on dilepton production from pion-pion annihilation in hot hadronic matter. The softening of the pion dispersion relation in a medium is found to enhance the production rate of dileptons with invariant masses in the region of $2m^*_π(T)<M<m_ρ$. On the other hand, the reduction of the pion electromagnetic form factor at finite temperature leads to a suppression of the dilepton production rate. Including both effects, we have found that the dilepton yield is slightly enhanced in the invariant mass region $M= 270\sim 600$ MeV but is suppressed around the vector meson resonance. We further discuss the relevance of our results to recent experimental data from the S+Au collisions at CERN/SPS energies by the CERES collaboration.

# Thermal effects on dilepton production from π-π annihilation

## Key claim
Including both the softening of the pion dispersion relation and the reduction of the pion electromagnetic form factor at finite temperature, the dilepton yield from pion-pion annihilation is slightly enhanced in the invariant mass region M = 270–600 MeV but suppressed around the vector meson resonance; however, these thermal effects may not be enough to explain the excess dileptons observed in the low invariant mass region by the CERES collaboration.

## Method
The authors used an effective chiral Lagrangian with hidden gauge symmetry (including vector mesons) to compute one-loop thermal corrections to the pion self-energy and the pion electromagnetic form factor at finite temperature. The dilepton production rate from π⁺π⁻ → e⁺e⁻ was then calculated in the rest frame of the virtual photon using the modified pion dispersion relation and the temperature-dependent form factor, with the momentum dependence factorized for finite three-momentum dileptons.

## Key numbers
- The effective pion mass decreases to mπ* ≈ 133 MeV at T = 160 MeV.
- The combined medium effects yield a factor of 4 enhancement in the ratio between the yield at the two-pion threshold and that at the rho meson mass.
- The enhancement region is specified as M = 270–600 MeV, with a peak at M ∼ 350 MeV from the softening of the dispersion relation.
- The rho meson width used is Γρ = 152 MeV; the pion decay constant is fπ = 93 MeV.
- The estimated dilepton yield with free vector meson masses is about a factor 4–6 less than the measured one in the low invariant mass region M = 200–600 MeV, and about a factor 2 more near the vector meson resonance.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of dilepton production from pion-pion annihilation in hot hadronic matter, which had previously used free-space parameters and could not explain the excess observed by the CERES collaboration in S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon. The authors build on the hidden gauge approach (Bando et al.) and the massive Yang-Mills approach, and they reference earlier calculations by Gale and Kapusta, as well as transport model results by Li et al. and hydrodynamic model calculations by Denisenko et al. The results indicate that while thermal effects modify the dilepton spectrum shape, a full explanation of the CERES data likely requires including baryon density effects and proper expansion dynamics.
