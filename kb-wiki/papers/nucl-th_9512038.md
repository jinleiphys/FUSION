# Classical and Quantal Irregular Scatterings with Complex Target

> One-dimensional scattering by a target with two internal degrees of freedom is investigated. The damping of resonance peaks and the associated appearance of the fluctuating background in the quantum inelastic scattering amplitudes are found. Examination of the analog classical system reveals a disorderly reaction function, which is then related to the quantum amplitude through a semiclassical argument.

# Classical and Quantal Irregular Scatterings with Complex Target

## Key claim

The damping of resonance peaks and appearance of fluctuating background in quantum inelastic scattering amplitudes, which occurs when the target undergoes complex motion, can be related to a disorderly classical reaction function through a semiclassical argument; however, the paper notes that no quantitative agreement is observed between classical and quantal results at the value of ℏ = 1, only qualitative similarities.

## Method

The author constructs a one-dimensional scattering model where a projectile interacts with a target having two internal degrees of freedom (angle-action variables ϑ, I and φ, J). Quantum scattering amplitudes are computed via the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the T-matrix, with the target internal coupling strength s varied to produce regular (s=0), weakly chaotic (s=1), and strongly chaotic (s=10) target dynamics. Classical scattering is analyzed by computing the reaction function p_out(x_in) from trajectories of the full Hamiltonian, and a semiclassical S-matrix is constructed from the classical probability density using the stationary phase approximation.

## Key numbers

- Incident momentum set to p_α = 6.5 (kinetic energy p_α²/2m = 21.125), capable of exciting about 60 target levels
- Particle-target interaction strength λ = 50, chosen to be comparable to the incoming kinetic energy
- Target masses fixed to m = 1, M = √2, N = √3
- Target internal coupling strengths examined: s = 0 (no mixing), s = 1 (weak chaotic mixing), s = 10 (strong chaotic mixing)
- 200 lowest target levels (between ε = 0 and 75) included in the nearest neighbor spacing distribution calculation
- Target energy for classical Poincaré sections taken as ε = 10
- Initial target variables for classical scattering: ϑ_in = φ_in = 0, I_in = J_in = 1.0

## Context

This work extends the theory of non-integrable scatterings and Ericson fluctuations (Ericson, 1960s) to a model with two internal target degrees of freedom, building on the irregular scattering studies of Blummel and Smilansky (1990s). The paper connects the damping of resonance peaks in quantum scattering to the disorderly structure of the classical reaction function, and extends Smilansky's semiclassical argument to systems with multiple target degrees of freedom. The semiclassical S-matrix construction follows the method of Miller (1970s) incorporating action integrals and Maslov indices, and the results suggest that the partitioning of excitation strength from simple states to complex configurations, frequently encountered in atomic and nuclear physics, can be understood through classical chaotic dynamics.
