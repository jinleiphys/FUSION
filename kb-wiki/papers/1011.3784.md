# Density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction from three-nucleon forces

> Microscopic calculations based on realistic nuclear hamiltonians, while yielding accurate results for the energies of the ground and low-lying excited states of nuclei with $A \leq 12$, fail to reproduce the empirical equilibrium properties of nuclear matter, that are known to be significantly affected by three-nucleon forces. We discuss a scheme suitable to construct a density-dependent two-nucleon potential, in which the effects of $n$-particle interactions can be included by integrating out the degrees of freedom of $(n-2)$-nucleons. Our approach, based on the formalism of correlated basis function and state-of-the-art models of the two- and three-nucleon potentials, leads to an effective interactionthat can be easily employed in nuclear matter calculations, yielding results in good agreement with those obtained from the underlying three-body potential.

## Key claim

The authors present a scheme to construct a density-dependent two-nucleon potential that effectively incorporates three-nucleon forces by averaging over the degrees of freedom of the third nucleon, but the work is limited to the linear-in-density term arising from the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential and is tested only against FHNC and AFDMC calculations using truncated Argonne potentials, not against full empirical nuclear matter saturation.

## Method

The authors use the Correlated Basis Function (CBF) formalism and the Fantoni-Rosati cluster expansion to compute the expectation value of the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential in symmetric nuclear matter, then integrate out the degrees of freedom of one nucleon to obtain an effective density-dependent two-nucleon potential. The calculation is performed at the level of the linear-in-density term, employing the FHNC/Single Operator Chain (SOC) approximation to sum the cluster diagrams that include both dynamical correlations (from the correlation operator) and statistical correlations (from the Fermi gas exchange operators). The resulting effective potential is then used in FHNC variational calculations of the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter with the Argonne \(v_6^\prime\) and \(v_8^\prime\) two-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- The ratio between the potential energies associated with two- and three-body interactions is \(\sim 20\%\) at density \(\rho \sim 2 \rho_0\), where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the nuclear matter saturation density.
- The cutoff parameter in the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential is fixed at \(c = 2.1\) fm\(^{-2}\).
- The strength parameter \(A_{2\pi}\) for the two-pion exchange term of the Urbana IX potential is \(-0.0293\) MeV when used with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential.
- The strength parameter \(U_0\) for the repulsive term of the Urbana IX potential is \(0.0048\) MeV when used with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological TNI (Three-Nucleon-Interaction) model of Lagaris and Pandharipande (1980s) and Friedman and Pandharipande, which introduced density-dependent two-nucleon potentials with free parameters fitted to nuclear matter saturation properties, by instead deriving the density-dependent potential from the microscopic Urbana IX three-nucleon force. The approach enables the inclusion of three-nucleon force effects in Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter, which cannot currently handle the full commutator term of the Urbana IX potential, and may also be applied to compute nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections in the nuclear medium for astrophysical applications such as transport coefficients and neutrino emission rates.
