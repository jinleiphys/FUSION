# The effect of isoscalar-isovector coupling in infinite nuclear matter

> Working on the framework of Relativistic Mean Field theory, we exposed the effect of nonlinear isoscalar-isovector coupling on G2 parameter set on the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy in infinite nuclear matter. The observables like symmetric energy and few related coefficients are studied systematically. We presented the results of stiff symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities and a soft variation at normal densities. Correlation between the symmetric energy and the isoscalar-isovector coupling parameter fully demonstrated for wide range of density. The work further extended to the octet system and showed the effect of coupling over the equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that adding a nonlinear isoscalar-isovector coupling term (with coupling constant \(\Lambda_v\)) to the G2 parameter set of the extended relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model softens the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation densities without significantly altering other saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, but only for a limited range of \(\Lambda_v\) values (0.01–0.04).

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean-field equations self-consistently using the G2 parameter set of the E-RMF model, augmented by an explicit \(\omega\)-\(\rho\) meson coupling term proportional to \(\Lambda_v\). They computed the energy density, pressure, symmetry energy \(E_{sym}\), and its slope parameter \(L_{sym}\) for infinite nuclear matter as functions of baryon density, and extended the calculation to octet baryon matter (including \(n\), \(p\), \(\Lambda^0\), \(\Sigma^{0,\pm1}\), \(\Xi^{-,0}\)) under \(\beta\)-equilibrium and charge neutrality for neutron star applications.

## Key numbers

* Saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter with the G2 set: Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.00\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), binding energy \(E \sim 16.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), compressibility \(K_0 = 215\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(\Lambda_v = 0.00\): \(E_{sym} = 36.40\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(L_{sym} = 105.67\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at saturation density.
* For \(\Lambda_v = 0.02\): \(E_{sym} = 38.87\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(L_{sym} = 113.09\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(\Lambda_v = 0.04\): \(E_{sym} = 38.02\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(L_{sym} = 110.52\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(\Lambda_v = 0.10\): \(E_{sym} = 33.39\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(L_{sym} = 96.65\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The authors suggest the limiting range of \(\Lambda_v\) is 0.01–0.04 to keep observables within experimental constraints from heavy-ion collisions (HIC), pygmy dipole resonance (PDR), isobaric analog states (IAS), and the finite-range droplet model (FRDM).

## Context

This work extends the E-RMF model with the G2 parameter set (Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1996–1997) by adding an isoscalar-isovector coupling term previously discussed in the context of FSU parametrizations (Fattoyev and others, 2010). The study systematically shows how varying \(\Lambda_v\) softens the symmetry energy and its slope parameter, enabling the model to satisfy experimental constraints from HIC (2009), PDR (2007, 2010), IAS, and FRDM (2012) that the bare G2 set fails to meet. The results enable future calculations of neutron star properties using the constrained parameter range.
