# Hadron Structure using Continuum Schwinger Function Methods

> The vast bulk of visible mass emerges from nonperturbative dynamics within quantum chromodynamics (QCD) -- the strong interaction sector of the Standard Model. The past decade has revealed the three pillars that support this emergent hadron mass (EHM); namely, a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge, and dressed-quarks with constituent-like masses. Theory is now working to expose their manifold and diverse expressions in hadron observables and highlighting the types of measurements that can be made in order to validate the paradigm. In sketching some of these developments, this discussion stresses the role of EHM in forming nucleon electroweak structure and the wave functions of excited baryons through the generation of dynamical diquark correlations; producing and constraining the dilation of the leading-twist pion distribution amplitude; shaping pion and nucleon parton distribution functions -- valence, glue and sea, including the antisymmetry of antimatter; and moulding pion and proton charge and mass distributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that emergent hadron mass (EHM), responsible for 94% of the nucleon mass, is supported by three pillars (a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge, and dressed-quarks with constituent-like masses) and that continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) can now expose the manifold expressions of EHM in hadron observables, but the discussion is a sketch of developments rather than a comprehensive proof, and the paradigm requires experimental validation through specific measurements.

## Method

The author reviews results from Poincaré-covariant analyses of QCD's gauge sector, combining continuum studies with lattice-QCD configurations generated with three domain-wall fermions at the physical pion mass to obtain parameter-free predictions for the gluon mass function and the process-independent effective charge. A quark+diquark approximation to the Faddeev equation is used to compute nucleon axial form factors and the wave functions of excited baryons, with the kernel formed from the running gluon mass and effective charge. Flavour-separated axial form factors are obtained and used to calculate light-front transverse axial charge spatial density profiles.

## Key numbers

* Gluon mass-scale: \(m_0 = 0.43(1)\,{\rm GeV}\), the renormalisation-group-invariant value characterising the infrared gluon mass function.
* Process-independent effective charge at zero momentum: \(\hat\alpha(k=0)/\pi = 0.97(4)\).
* Proton mass budget: EHM contributes 94%, Higgs boson contributes 1%, and EHM+Higgs interference contributes 5% (separation at renormalisation scale \(\zeta = 2\,\)GeV).
* Ratio of flavour-separated axial charges for the proton: \(g_A^d/g_A^u = -0.32(2)\) using the complete nucleon amplitude with axialvector diquarks, compared to \(-0.054(13)\) for a scalar-diquark-only picture.
* Flavour-singlet combination of valence quark contributions to the proton spin at the hadron scale: \(g_A^u+g_A^d = 0.65(2)\), indicating quarks carry roughly 65% of the proton spin.
* Diquark masses used in the Faddeev equation: \(m_{[ud]} = 0.80\,\mbox{GeV}\), \(m_{\{uu\}} = m_{\{ud\}} = 0.89\,\)GeV.
* At \(x = Q^2/m_N^2 = 10\), the dipole fit overestimates the true axial form factor result by 56%.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on emergent hadron mass and continuum Schwinger function methods developed by Roberts and collaborators over the past decade (Roberts 2020, 2021; Binosi 2022; Ding 2022). It builds upon the Faddeev equation framework introduced by Cahill (1988) and Reinhardt (1989), and the symmetry-preserving electromagnetic current developed by Oettel (1999). The results enable predictions for neutrino-nucleus interaction analyses that depend on the nucleon axial form factor, and provide a framework for interpreting data from Jefferson Lab on baryon electrocouplings, including the \(\gamma+p \to \Delta(1232)\) and \(\gamma+p \to \Delta(1600)\) transitions. The work contradicts simple quark model assignments by showing that baryon wave functions contain significant orbital angular momentum components and that no baryon is simply a radial excitation of another.
