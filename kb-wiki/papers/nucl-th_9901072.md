# Photon Absorption on a Neutron-Proton Pair in 3He and 4He

> We have developed a phenomenological quasi-deuteron model to describe photodisintegration reactions of $^3$He and $^4$He at intermediate photon energies, and compared the results with experimental data obtained by TAGX group in which contributions from photon absorption by $pn$ pair were selectively observed. The data can be reproduced reasonably well, however there still remain certain discrepancies between calculated results and data.

## Key claim

A phenomenological quasi-deuteron model can reproduce the gross features of exclusive photodisintegration data for both ³He and ⁴He, but the model fails to reproduce the observed energy dependence of the total cross section for ³He and the step-like change near 150 MeV for ⁴He, indicating essential differences from deuteron photodisintegration.

## Method

The authors constructed a quasi-deuteron model in which the photon absorption amplitude on a neutron-proton pair in the target nucleus is taken from the experimental differential cross section of the deuteron photodisintegration reaction d(γ,p)n, fitted over the energy range 20–440 MeV. The relative wavefunction between the absorbing pn pair and the spectator particle (a proton for ³He, a deuteron for ⁴He) was assumed to be a Gaussian with Fermi momentum p_f estimated from charge radii (146 MeV for ³He, 205 MeV for ⁴He). Final-state three-body phase space was integrated exactly, and a constant normalization factor was introduced for the ⁴He case only.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum p_f = 150 MeV used for ³He (consistent with charge radius); p_f = 200 MeV used for ⁴He (consistent with charge radius).
- For ³He(γ,pn)p, the total cross section data show a peak at E_γ = 225 MeV, while the calculated peak appears at 275 MeV.
- For ⁴He(γ,pn)d, the calculated total cross section with p_f = 200 MeV is larger than data at lower energies and smaller at higher energies, and does not reproduce the strong energy dependence around E_γ = 150 MeV.
- The singlet pn pair contribution to the ³He total cross section was evaluated in prior work (Wilhelm et al., 1994; Niskanen, 1995) to be around 50 μb at E_γ = 100 MeV.
- Estimated absolute cross section for the ³-body decay channel of ⁴He at E_γ ~ 40 MeV is about 1 mb, based on work by Efros et al. (1997).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-deuteron model originally proposed by Levinger (1951) and later by Futami and Miyazima (1971), applying it to exclusive TAGX data for ³He(γ,pn)p and ⁴He(γ,pn)d. It contrasts with microscopic calculations by Wilhelm et al. (1994) and Niskanen (1995) for ³He, which included singlet configurations and Δ currents but did not produce angular-dependent observables directly comparable to data. The results indicate that a fully microscopic model with accurate wavefunctions and hadronic currents is needed to resolve the discrepancies, particularly the step-like behavior in ⁴He near 150 MeV and the energy dependence of the ³He total cross section.
