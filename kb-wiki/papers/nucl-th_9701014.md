# Confinement and scaling in deep inelastic processes

> We show that parton confinement in the final state generates large $1/Q^2$ (higher twist) corrections to Bjorken scaling. For treatment of these non-perturbative effects, we derive a new expansion in powers of $1/Q^2$ for the structure function that is free of infra-red singularities and which reduces corrections to the leading term. The leading term represents scattering from an off-mass-shell parton, which keeps the same virtual mass in the final state. This term appears as a function of a new variable $\bar x(x,Q^2)$, which coincides with the Bjorken variable x for $Q^2\to\infty$. Analysis of the data shows excellent scaling at large x, where power corrections are dominant. The approach accommodates also the QCD logarithmic corrections and the behavior of the structure functions is well reproduced within a wide kinematical range. Since the new scaling variable $\bar x(x,Q^2)$ incorporates the higher twist effects, it allows us to extract the ratio $F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)$ for $x\to 1$ from the available data at moderate Q^2.

# Confinement and scaling in deep inelastic processes

## Key claim

The paper argues that parton confinement in the final state generates large $1/Q^2$ (higher twist) corrections to Bjorken scaling, and that these non-perturbative effects can be effectively absorbed into a modified scaling variable $\bar x(x,Q^2)$, which reduces the corrections to the leading term. The claim is conditional on the choice of spectator mass parameters: excellent scaling is achieved only when the spectator is treated as composed of current quarks ($m_0=0$) rather than constituent quarks ($m_0 > 600$ MeV).

## Method

The author derives a new expansion in powers of $1/Q^2$ for the nucleon structure function using a Bethe-Salpeter equation framework, where the struck quark propagator is modified so that the quark retains the same off-shell mass before and after virtual photon absorption. The resulting scaling variable $\bar x(x,Q^2)$ depends on the target mass $M$ and the invariant mass of spectator partons $m_s$, which is approximated as $m_s^2 \simeq m_0^2 + C(x,Q^2)(1-x)$ with $C$ treated as a phenomenological parameter. The approach is tested against SLAC data at large $x$ ($x \geq 0.7$, $5 < Q^2 < 30$ (GeV/c)$^2$) and BCDMS/NMC data at moderate $x$ ($0.35 < x < 0.75$, up to $Q^2 = 230$ (GeV/c)$^2$), with QCD logarithmic corrections included at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The SLAC data used cover $Q^2 < 30$ (GeV/c)$^2$ for $x \geq 0.7$, and BCDMS data extend to $Q^2 \leq 230$ (GeV/c)$^2$ for $x \leq 0.75$.
- Data points with invariant mass of the final state less than $(M+\Delta)^2$ where $\Delta = 300$ MeV were excluded to avoid resonance regions.
- Excellent scaling in $\bar x$ is obtained with $m_0 = 0$ and $C = 3$ (GeV)$^2$ for the spectator mass parameter.
- Poor scaling results for $m_0 > 600$ MeV, corresponding to constituent quark masses for the spectator.
- The QCD fits use $\Lambda = 100$ MeV and $\Lambda = 200$ MeV at next-to-leading order, with slightly better agreement for $\Lambda = 100$ MeV.
- The extracted ratio $F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)$ at large $x$ approaches approximately $2/3$, not the lower bound of $1/4$ expected from some earlier analyses.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic treatment of final-state confinement by Gurvitz (1990s) and the harmonic oscillator model of Greenberg (1970s) to the relativistic domain via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. It contradicts the expectation from analyses of BCDMS and NMC data (1990s) that $F_2^n/F_2^p \to 1/4$ for $x\to 1$, instead finding a ratio near $2/3$ consistent with the simple quark-parton model prediction of equal valence quark distributions. The approach enables extraction of asymptotic structure functions from moderate-$Q^2$ data and can be applied to sum rules and small-$x$ regions where higher-twist effects remain an open problem.
