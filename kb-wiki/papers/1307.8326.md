# Approximate relativistic bound states of a particle in Yukawa field with Coulomb tensor interaction

> We obtain the approximate relativistic bound state of a spin-1/2 particle in the field of the Yukawa potential and a Coulomb-like tensor interaction with arbitrary spin-orbit coupling number k under the spin and pseudospin (p-spin) symmetries. The asymptotic iteration method is used to obtain energy eigenvalues and corresponding wave functions in their closed forms. Our numerical results show that the tensor interaction removes degeneracies between the spin and p-spin doublets and creates new degenerate doublets for various strengths of tensor coupling.

## Key claim
The paper obtains approximate relativistic bound-state energies and wave functions for a spin-1/2 particle in a Yukawa potential plus a Coulomb-like tensor interaction, under spin and pseudospin symmetries, using the asymptotic iteration method. The abstract's claim that the tensor interaction "removes degeneracies" is qualified by the fact that the results are approximate (valid only for small screening parameter, ar << 1) and that the tensor interaction creates new degenerate doublets for specific strengths of tensor coupling.

## Method
The Dirac equation with scalar and vector Yukawa potentials and a Coulomb-like tensor potential U(r) = -A/r is solved under the conditions of pseudospin symmetry (Σ(r) = constant) and spin symmetry (Δ(r) = constant). The centrifugal term 1/r² is approximated by 4a²e^{-2ar}/(1-e^{-2ar})², valid for ar << 1, and the resulting second-order differential equations are transformed using z = (e^{-2ar} - 1)^{-1}. The asymptotic iteration method (AIM) is then applied to obtain closed-form energy eigenvalue equations and wave functions expressed in terms of hypergeometric functions.

## Key numbers
- For pseudospin symmetry with C_ps = -5.0 fm⁻¹, V₀ = 1, a = 0.1 fm⁻¹, M = 0.5 fm⁻¹, and no tensor interaction (A = 0), the doublet (1s₁/₂, 2p₃/₂) has energy -4.500000000 fm⁻¹.
- For the same parameters with tensor strength A = 0.5, the new doublet (1s₁/₂, 1p₃/₂, 2p₃/₂, 2d₅/₂) has energy -4.499390062 fm⁻¹.
- For pseudospin symmetry with C_ps = 0 and A = 0, the doublet (1s₁/₂, 2p₃/₂) has energy -4.500000000 fm⁻¹, while (1p₃/₂, 2s₁/₂, 2d₅/₂) has energy -0.208711915 fm⁻¹.
- For spin symmetry with C_s = 5.0 fm⁻¹ and A = 0, the state 1s₁/₂ has energy 4.489714770 fm⁻¹ and 2f₇/₂ has energy 4.500000000 fm⁻¹.
- For pseudospin symmetry with C_ps = -40 fm⁻¹ and A = 0, the states 1s₁/₂ and 2d₅/₂ both have energy -39.49974424 fm⁻¹, while 1p₃/₂ and 0f₅/₂ both have energy -39.500000000 fm⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the Dirac equation with Yukawa potential using methods such as the Nikiforov-Uvarov method (Setare and Haidari) and the asymptotic iteration method (Ikhdair). It specifically incorporates a Coulomb-like tensor interaction, which was previously introduced into the Dirac equation by other authors (referenced as BJ10-BJ15) to study spin-orbit coupling effects in nuclear physics. The results enable calculation of bound-state energies and wave functions for relativistic particles in screened Coulomb potentials with tensor coupling, relevant for nuclear shell-model studies and meson spectroscopy. The authors note that their approximation is valid only for the lowest orbital states, and that the spin symmetry case with A = 0 and C_s = 0 reduces to the non-relativistic Yukawa solution.
