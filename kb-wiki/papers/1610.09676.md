# Limits on hadron spectrum from bulk medium properties

## Key claim
The bulk thermal properties of hadron gas measured on the lattice preclude a very fast rising of the number of resonance states in the QCD spectrum as assumed by the Hagedorn hypothesis, unless a substantial repulsion between hadronic resonances is present. If the Hagedorn growth continued above masses ~1.8 GeV, the thermodynamic functions would noticeably depart from measured lattice values at temperatures above 140 MeV, just below the transition temperature to quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The author compared two hadron resonance gas models: one including all known resonances from the Particle Data Group tables up to 1.8 GeV with Breit-Wigner widths taken into account (HRG(Gamma)), and the same model amended with additional states above 1.8 GeV modeled using the Hagedorn formula fitted to the lower-mass spectrum. The resulting thermodynamic quantities (trace anomaly, entropy density, sound velocity) were compared to lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and Hot QCD collaborations.

## Key numbers
- The Hagedorn temperature used in the fit to the density of states was T_H = 260 MeV.
- The established QCD spectrum reaches about 2 GeV, with the Hagedorn hypothesis working well up to about 1.8 GeV.
- The inclusion of extra non-interacting states above M = 1.8 GeV placed the trace anomaly way above lattice data at temperatures T > 140 MeV.
- The hadron resonance gas model is credible below T ~ 170 MeV, where the cross-over to quark-gluon plasma occurs.

## Context
This work extends earlier investigations by Karsch (2003), Huovinen (2009), Andronic (2012), Arriola (2014), and Lo (2015) on the sensitivity of thermal bulk properties to the hadron resonance spectrum. The author shows that including resonance widths improves agreement with lattice data, as previously noted by Arriola (2012). The paper demonstrates that repulsive interactions between resonances, such as excluded volume corrections or cancellations between channels (e.g., the sigma meson contribution canceled by the isospin-2 channel as shown by Broniowski (2015)), could allow additional high-mass states to exist without violating lattice constraints. This enables future work to constrain the high-lying QCD spectrum by properly accounting for both the number of states and their interactions.
