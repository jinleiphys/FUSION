# Nucleon Tomography and Generalized Parton Distribution at Physical Pion Mass from Lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice calculation of the nucleon isovector unpolarized generalized parton distribution (GPD) at the physical pion mass using a lattice ensemble with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) generated by MILC Collaboration, with lattice spacing $a\approx 0.09$~fm and volume $64^3\times 96$. We use momentum-smeared sources to improve the signal at nucleon boost momentum $P_z \approx 2.2$ GeV, and report results at nonzero momentum transfers in $[0.2,1.0]\text{ GeV}^2$. Nonperturbative renormalization in RI/MOM scheme is used to obtain the quasi-distribution before matching to the lightcone GPDs. The three-dimensional distributions $H(x,Q^2)$ and $E(x,Q^2)$ at $ξ=0$ are presented, along with the three-dimensional nucleon tomography and impact-parameter--dependent distribution for selected Bjorken $x$ at $μ=3$ GeV in $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme.

# Nucleon Tomography and Generalized Parton Distribution at Physical Pion Mass from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon isovector unpolarized generalized parton distribution (GPD) at the physical pion mass, but the results are reliable only for Bjorken x > 0.05 because the small-x and antiquark regions are dominated by Fourier-transform artifacts and lack of precision lattice data to constrain them.

## Method

The authors use a 2+1+1 flavor HISQ ensemble from MILC Collaboration with lattice spacing a ≈ 0.09 fm and volume 64³ × 96 at physical pion mass Mπ ≈ 135 MeV, employing clover valence fermions with Gaussian momentum smearing to achieve nucleon boost momentum Pz ≈ 2.2 GeV. They compute quasi-GPD matrix elements with Wilson-line displacements up to z = ±12 lattice units, apply nonperturbative renormalization in the RI/MOM scheme, and match to lightcone GPDs using one-loop matching conditions, extracting H(x,Q²) and E(x,Q²) at ξ = 0 for momentum transfers Q² in [0.2, 1.0] GeV².

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a ≈ 0.09 fm, spatial extent ≈ 5.8 fm, temporal extent ≈ 8.6 fm, with physical pion mass Mπ ≈ 135 MeV
- Nucleon boost momentum Pz ≈ 2.2 GeV (corresponding to 2π/L × {0,0,10})
- Total statistics: 501,760 measurements over 1,960 configurations
- Momentum transfer squared values: Q² = {0, 0.19, 0.39, 0.77, 0.97} GeV²
- Renormalization scale: μ = 3 GeV in MSbar scheme, with RI/MOM scale μR = 3.8 GeV and pzR = 2.2 GeV
- Spatial momentum transfer configurations: nx² + ny² ∈ {0, 4, 8, 16, 20} with integer nx, ny

## Context

This work extends the LaMET (quasi-PDF) method from one-dimensional parton distributions to three-dimensional GPDs, following the first lattice GPD study for pions by Chen et al. (2019) and contemporaneous work by the ETM Collaboration (Alexandrou et al., 2019, 2020) at pion mass Mπ ≈ 260 MeV with boost momentum 1.67 GeV. The results enable the first lattice-QCD-based nucleon tomography via impact-parameter-dependent distributions q(x,b), and provide a benchmark for future calculations with smaller lattice spacings and higher boost momenta to access the small-x and antiquark regions.
