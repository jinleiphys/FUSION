# The importance of few-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory

> We study the importance of few-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory for describing many-nucleon systems. A combinatorial argument suggests that three-nucleon forces -- which are conventionally regarded as next-to-next-to-leading order -- should accompany the two-nucleon force already at leading order (LO) starting with mass number $A\approx 10-20$. We find that this promotion enables the first realistic description of the $^{16}$O ground state based on a renormalization-group-invariant LO interaction. We also performed coupled-cluster calculations of the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter and our results indicate that LO four-nucleon forces could play a crucial role for describing heavy-mass nuclei. The enhancement mechanism we found is very general and could be important also in other many-body problems.

## Key claim

The authors argue that three-nucleon forces, conventionally treated as next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, should be promoted to leading order (LO) for nuclei with mass number A ≈ 10–20 based on a combinatorial enhancement mechanism, and that this promotion enables the first realistic description of the ¹⁶O ground state using a renormalization-group-invariant LO interaction.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-cluster (CC) calculations at the Λ-CCSD(T) level for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca, and CCD(T) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter, using LO chiral interactions with non-local super-Gaussian regulators. They started from two previously constructed RG-invariant NN interactions (MWPC40 and SEP40) and added three-nucleon forces with low-energy constants c_D and c_E, constrained by requiring that their perturbative contributions to ³H and ⁴He binding energies do not exceed 1/6 of the corresponding binding energies, and then treated these forces nonperturbatively in heavier systems.

## Key numbers

- The combinatorial estimate suggests three-nucleon forces become as important as the two-nucleon force for mass number A in the range 10–20, using a breakdown scale M_hi between 0.5 and 1 GeV.
- The ¹⁶O ground-state energy obtained with NN+NNN interactions falls within 10% of the experimental value E(¹⁶O) ≈ −128 MeV for the cutoff range Λ = 450–550 MeV.
- The ¹⁶O charge radius varies by about 10% around 2.1 fm across cutoffs Λ = 450–550 MeV, which is about 20% of the experimental value.
- The ⁴⁰Ca ground-state energy predictions fall within 15% of the experimental value for cutoffs up to Λ = 500 MeV.
- Binding energy per nucleon B_A/A for NN+NNN interactions at LO with MWPC40 and Λ = 450 MeV: 3.3 MeV for ³H, 8 MeV for ⁴He, 8.2 MeV for ¹⁶O, and 9.4 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca.
- The NN-only binding energy per nucleon for the same interaction and cutoff: 3.3 MeV for ³H, 8 MeV for ⁴He, 17.5 MeV for ¹⁶O, and 31.6 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca.

## Context

This work extends the modified Weinberg power counting (MWPC) approach of Yang (2020) and the separable dibaryon formulation (SEP) by promoting few-nucleon forces to LO based on a combinatorial argument, addressing the failure of previous RG-invariant LO interactions to produce stable ground states in nuclei heavier than ⁴He. The results suggest that LO four-nucleon forces may be needed for heavy-mass nuclei, consistent with findings from N²LO lattice calculations under Weinberg power counting by Lähde (2013) that an SU(4)-symmetric four-nucleon force is required for accurate binding in α-particle nuclei. The enhancement mechanism is argued to be general and applicable to other many-body problems where density and interaction range interplay nontrivially.
