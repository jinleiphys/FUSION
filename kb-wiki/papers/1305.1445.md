# Spin observables in 71A MeV 6,8He-hydrogen scattering

> Predictions of cross sections and analyzing powers using g-folding optical potentials for the scattering of 71A MeV 6,8He ions from (polarized) hydrogen are compared with data. A g-folding model in which exchange amplitudes are evaluated explicitly was used with wave functions of 6,8He specified from a no-core shell model that used a complete (0+2+4)hw basis. The analyzing powers reveal some sensitivities to the details of the wave functions, especially in the case of halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The g-folding model predictions for analyzing powers in 71A MeV 6,8He-hydrogen scattering show sensitivity to the details of the nuclear wave functions, especially for the halo nucleus 6He, but the existing data are binned over 10-degree intervals and have a 19% scale uncertainty, making definitive conclusions about new physics premature.

## Method

The authors used a g-folding optical potential model with exchange amplitudes evaluated explicitly, employing no-core shell model wave functions from a complete (0+2+4)ℏω basis for 6He and 8He. The effective NN interaction was derived from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone solutions of the Bonn-B potential, and single-particle wave functions were taken as either Woods-Saxon (with binding energies constrained by separation energies) or harmonic oscillator functions. Elastic scattering differential cross sections and analyzing powers were calculated using the DWBA98 code and compared with experimental data from Sakaguchi et al. (2011, 2013) and Korshennikov et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 71A MeV for both 6He and 8He beams on hydrogen targets
- For 6He: the Woods-Saxon calculation used a neutron binding energy of 1.8 MeV (the single neutron separation energy to the lowest resonance in 5He); the "nohalo" calculation used a larger binding energy of approximately 7 MeV for neutrons in the 0p-shell
- For 6He: the oscillator calculation used oscillator parameter b = 1.7 fm
- The oscillator energy used in the shell model was ℏω = 14 MeV
- The analyzing power data have an additional 19% scale uncertainty (Sakaguchi et al., 2011)
- The differential cross section calculations are considered reliable out to 100-110 degrees in the center of mass (where cross sections exceed a few tenths of a mb/sr)

## Context

This work extends the g-folding model approach previously applied successfully to stable nuclei from 3He to 238U across 25-300 MeV (Amos, 2000) and to exotic nuclei at lower energies (Karataglidis, 2000; Lagoyannis, 2001; Stiliaris, 2002). The present analysis of analyzing power data at 71A MeV tests the model's sensitivity to nuclear structure details, particularly the halo character of 6He versus the neutron skin of 8He. The results enable future experiments with better angular resolution to distinguish between competing structure models and potentially reveal new physics in the projectile-target interaction.
