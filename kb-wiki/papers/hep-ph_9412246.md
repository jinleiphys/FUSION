# Constraining Spectral Functions at Finite Temperature and Chemical Potential with Exact Sum Rules in Asymptotically Free Theories

> Within the framework of the operator product expansion (OPE) and the renormalization group equation (RGE), we show that the temperature and chemical potential dependence of the zeroth moment of a spectral function (SF) is completely determined by the one-loop structure in an asymptotically free theory, and in particular in QCD. Logarithmic corrections are found to play an essential role in the derivation. This exact result constrains the shape of SF's, and implies striking effects near phase transitions. Phenomenological parameterizations of the SF, often used in applications such as the analysis of lattice QCD data or QCD sum rule calculations at finite temperature and baryon density must satisfy these constraints. We also explicitly illustrate in detail the exact sum rule in the Gross-Neveu model.

## Key claim
The temperature and chemical potential dependence of the zeroth moment of a spectral function is completely determined by the one-loop structure of an asymptotically free theory, but only when logarithmic corrections are properly accounted for; naive expansions that ignore these logarithms yield incorrect sum rules or fail to recognize that the moment integral is divergent.

## Method
The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) and the renormalization group equation (RGE) to derive the asymptotic behavior of the Euclidean correlator at large momentum. They match this to an asymptotic expansion of the spectral function's dispersion integral, using a repeated partial integration method to handle logarithmic corrections. The derivation is explicitly illustrated in the soluble Gross-Neveu model in the large-N limit, where the exact spectral function, condensates, and Wilson coefficients can be calculated.

## Key numbers
- The critical line for the second-order phase transition in the Gross-Neveu model is given by the equation $\ln(2) = \frac{1}{2}\int_0^M \frac{dk}{k} \frac{\sinh(k/T)}{\cosh[(k+\mu)/2T]\cosh[(k-\mu)/2T]} - \int_M^\infty \frac{dk}{k} f(k;T,\mu)$, where $M$ is the dynamical fermion mass at zero temperature and chemical potential.
- The dynamical fermion mass at $(T,\mu)=0$ is defined as $M = \kappa \exp(-\pi/g^2)$, where $\kappa$ is the subtraction point and $g^2$ is the coupling constant.
- The renormalized effective potential for the Gross-Neveu model is $V(\sigma^2) = \frac{N}{4\pi} \left\{ \sigma^2\left(\ln\frac{\sigma^2}{M^2} - 1\right) + 4T \int_0^\infty dk \ln \left[ \frac{(1+e^{-(k+\mu)/T})(1+e^{-(k-\mu)/T})}{(1+e^{-(\sqrt{k^2+\sigma^2}+\mu)/T})(1+e^{-(\sqrt{k^2+\sigma^2}-\mu)/T})} \right] \right\}$.

## Context
This work extends the application of sum rules from zero temperature and density, as pioneered by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), to finite temperature and chemical potential. It provides an exact constraint that must be satisfied by phenomenological parameterizations of spectral functions used in the analysis of lattice QCD data (e.g., Chu et al., 1993) and QCD sum rule calculations at finite temperature (e.g., Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov, 1986; Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1985). The results enable a rigorous check of spectral function models near phase transitions, showing that the shape of the spectral function can be drastically modified compared to its zero-temperature form.
