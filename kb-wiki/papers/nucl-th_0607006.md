# Detecting Strangeness -4 Dibaryon States

> Recent experiments at Jefferson Laboratory and potential new facilities at the Japan Proton Accelerator Research Complex (J-PARC) make it evident that the discovery of a spin-singlet S-wave di-cascade bound state of two cascade (Xi) particles is feasible. We state the simple arguments, based on SU(3) flavor symmetry, for the existence of this bound state, review the previous predictions and comment on the experimental conditions necessary for detection.

## Key claim

SU(3) flavor symmetry predicts that the $^1S_0$ strong interaction between two cascade ($\Xi$) particles is identical to the $^1S_0$ nucleon-nucleon interaction; because the $NN$ system has a quasibound state and the $\Xi\Xi$ reduced mass is about 40% larger, the $\Xi\Xi$ system should form a true bound state, though the author notes that models cannot provide definitive proof that the state exists.

## Method

The author uses SU(3) flavor symmetry to equate the $^1S_0$ $\Xi\Xi$ and $NN$ interactions, employing a four-baryon Lagrangian without derivative terms. Three simple potential models (square well, non-local separable, and delta-shell) are parameterized to reproduce the $^1S_0$ $NN$ scattering length ($a = -18$ fm or $-24$ fm) and effective range ($r_e = 2.8$ fm), then the reduced mass is changed from the $NN$ value to the $\Xi\Xi$ value to compute binding energies. The results are compared with six realistic soft-core baryon-baryon potentials from Stoks and Rijken (1999) that include one-boson exchange and SU(3) breaking effects.

## Key numbers

- Square well potential: binding energies of 7.48 MeV (set I, $a=-18$ fm) and 6.83 MeV (set II, $a=-24$ fm), with scattering lengths of 10.6 fm and 9.81 fm respectively
- Separable potential: binding energies of 0.66 MeV (set I) and 0.73 MeV (set II), with scattering lengths of 8.0 fm and 7.6 fm respectively
- Delta-shell potential: binding energies of 0.549 MeV (set I) and 0.606 MeV (set II), with scattering lengths of 8.75 fm and 8.34 fm respectively
- The six realistic potentials from Stoks and Rijken (1999) predict binding energies ranging from 0.1 to 15.8 MeV
- Photon threshold energy for production on deuterium is about 4.8 GeV; kaon threshold energy is 3.8 GeV
- The di-cascade lifetime would be roughly that of a free $\Xi$, about $2 \times 10^{-10}$ s

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) flavor symmetry arguments used in the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula to the two-baryon system, building on the baryon-baryon interaction formalism of Savage and Wise (1995). The predictions are consistent with the six realistic potential models of Stoks and Rijken (1999) and with the existence of $\Lambda\Lambda$ hypernuclei discussed by Schaffner (1993). The paper calls for experimental searches at Jefferson Laboratory using the missing-mass technique in $\gamma D$ reactions and at J-PARC using $K^- D$ reactions, enabled by the recent observation of $\Xi^-$ production in $\gamma p$ reactions at Jefferson Laboratory reported by Price (2004).
