# Triple collisions (e+p+Be7) in solar plasma

> Several nuclear reactions involving the Be7 nucleus, not included into the standard model of the pp-chain, are discussed. A qualitative analysis of their possible influence on the fate of the Be7 in solar plasma and of their role in the interpretation of the solar neutrino experiments is given. As an example, the reaction rate of the nonradiative production of B8 in the triple collision p + e^- + Be7 ---> B8 + e^- is estimated in the framework of the adiabatic approximation. For the solar interior conditions the triple collision reaction rate is approximately 10^{-4} of that for the binary process p + Be7 ---> B8 + gamma .

## Key claim

The triple collision reaction p + e⁻ + ⁷Be → ⁸B + e⁻, which is not included in the standard pp-chain, has a reaction rate approximately 10⁻⁴ of the binary process p + ⁷Be → ⁸B + γ under solar interior conditions, suggesting that while this nonradiative channel plays a minor role in the present Sun, it may have been significant at earlier stages of the universe when electron densities were much higher.

## Method

The authors estimated the reaction rate for the nonradiative production of ⁸B via the triple collision p + e⁻ + ⁷Be → ⁸B + e⁻ using the adiabatic approximation, treating the ⁸B nucleus as a bound state of a proton and ⁷Be with a binding energy of 0.138 MeV. The electron's Coulomb interaction with the heavy particles was treated in the Born approximation, while the p-⁷Be scattering and bound-state wave functions were obtained from radial Schrödinger equations with a Woods-Saxon strong potential and a Coulomb potential from a uniformly charged sphere of radius 2.95 fm. The reaction rate was averaged over Maxwellian momentum distributions at solar temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of ⁸B relative to the p+⁷Be threshold is 0.138 MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon strong potential parameters are V₀ = 34 MeV for the scattering state and V'₀ = 32.62 MeV for the bound state, with diffuseness a_N = 0.52 fm and nuclear radius R = 2.95 fm.
- At θ = 14 × 10⁶ °K, the binary reaction rate <σ₂v>N_A = 1.73 × 10⁻¹² cm³ mol⁻¹ sec⁻¹ and the triple collision rate <Σ(θ)>N_A² = 1.83 × 10⁻¹⁶ cm³ mol⁻¹ sec⁻¹.
- The ratio <R₃(θ)>/<R₂(θ)> ranges from 1.06 × 10⁻⁴ at θ = 14 × 10⁶ °K to 0.89 × 10⁻⁴ at θ = 20 × 10⁶ °K, for an electron density n_e = 100 N_A cm⁻³.
- The average kinetic energy of particles in the plasma is ~1 keV, while the average electron potential energy is ~10 eV.

## Context

This work extends the standard pp-chain model of the Sun, as described in Bahcall (1989), by considering triple collisions involving ⁷Be that are normally neglected. The authors argue that the mutual dependence of the weak capture e⁻+⁷Be and the radiative capture p+⁷Be, when both occur from the same three-body initial state, may affect the interpretation of solar neutrino experiments such as Homestake and Kamiokande. The nonradiative triple collision studied here is analogous to the reaction e⁻+p+²H → ³He+e⁻ previously investigated by Rakityansky, Sofianos, Howell, Braun, and Belyaev (1997). The results enable future studies of other triple collisions, such as p+p+⁷Be → ⁸B+p, which may require a more rigorous Faddeev treatment.
