# Analytical forms of the deuteron wave function for Nijmegen group potentials and polarization characteristics of A(d,d')X reactions

> Polarization observables of the A(d,d')X reactions have been calculated according to the received coeffcients of the analytical form for deuteron wave function in coordinate space for the nucleon-nucleon Nijmegen group potentials (NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93). The obtained values of tensor Ayy and vector Ay analyzing powers have been compared with the published experimental data at t- scaling for the inelastic scattering of deuterons on hydrogen, carbon and beryllium. Theoretical values of tensor-tensor Kyy and vector-vector Ky polarization transfers have been also evaluated in the plane-wave impulse approximation.

# Analytical forms of the deuteron wave function for Nijmegen group potentials and polarization characteristics of A(d,d')X reactions

## Key claim
The analytical form of the deuteron wave function (DWF) for Nijmegen group potentials (NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93) yields theoretical values of tensor Ayy and vector Ay analyzing powers that, within the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), show approximate t-scaling but strongly differ from experimental data in the region |t| = 0-1.0 (GeV/c)^2, with the vector component Ay being strongly dependent on the parameter P (ratio of spin-dependent to spin-independent amplitudes) and better matching experiment at a=0.4.

## Method
The author used previously obtained coefficients of an analytical DWF form in coordinate space (u(r) = r^(3/2) sum A_i exp(-a_i r^3), w(r) = r sum B_i exp(-b_i r^3)) for three Nijmegen group potentials (NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93). Within the PWIA model, tensor Ayy and vector Ay analyzing powers were calculated using formulas involving spherical and quadrupole deuteron form factors and a parameter P = a*p characterizing the ratio of spin-dependent to spin-independent amplitudes for the NN -> NN*(2190) process. Theoretical values of tensor-tensor Kyy and vector-vector Ky polarization transfers were also evaluated.

## Key numbers
- Tensor analyzing power Ayy calculated at parameter a=0.4 for the NN -> NN*(2190) amplitude ratio
- Vector analyzing power Ay calculated at a=0.4, a=1.0, and a=1.1, with best agreement to experiment at a=0.4
- t-scaling range for experimental data on beryllium: up to approximately -0.9 (GeV/c)^2 (from prior work at 4.5 GeV/c)
- t-scaling range for experimental data on hydrogen and carbon: up to -1.5 (GeV/c)^2 (from prior work at 9.0 GeV/c)
- Region where Ayy from PWIA calculations intersects zero: |t| = 1.0-1.2 (GeV/c)^2 (for Paris and Bonn potentials in prior work)
- Region where polarization transfers Kyy and Ky are sensitive to the NN -> NN*(2190) amplitude: up to |t| = 1.5 (GeV/c)^2
- Initial deuteron momenta for experimental comparisons: 4.5 GeV/c, 5.0 GeV/c, 9.0 GeV/c
- Detection angles for experimental data: 80 mrad, 85 mrad, 178 mrad

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of polarization observables for A(d,d')X reactions that were performed using Paris and Bonn potentials (Ladygin 2002, Ladygin 2006) by applying the same PWIA framework to Nijmegen group potentials (NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93). The analytical DWF form used was previously developed for Reid93 and Argonne v18 potentials (Zhaba 2016). The theoretical results are compared with experimental data from multiple experiments on hydrogen (Ladygin 2006), carbon (Ladygin 2006, Afanasiev 1998, Azhgirey 1998), and beryllium (Ladygin 2000, Azhgirey 2005, Azhgirey 2001). The calculated polarization transfers Kyy and Ky, for which no experimental data exist, could enable future experimental tests and can be used to determine partial cross-sections and spin-flip cross-sections for the spin structure of vector + A -> vector + B processes.
