# Isospin violation in mixing and decays of rho- and omega-mesons

> Influence of the isospin-violating (ρ^0, ω)-mixing is discussed for any pair of decays of ρ^0, ωinto the same final state. It is demonstrated, in analogy to the CP-violation in neutral kaon decays, that isospin violation can manifest itself in various forms: direct violation in amplitudes and/or violation due to mixing. In addition to the known decays (ρ^0, ω)\toπ^+π^- and (ρ^0, ω)\toπ^0γ, the pair of decays to e^+e^- and the whole set of radiative decays with participation of ρ^0, ω(in initial or final states) are shown to be also useful and perspective for studies. Existing data on these decays agree with the universal character of the mixing parameter and indirectly support enhancement of ρ^0\toπ^0γin respect to ρ^{\pm}\toπ^{\pm}γ. Future precise measurements will allow to separate different forms of isospin violation and elucidate their mechanisms.

# Isospin violation in mixing and decays of rho- and omega-mesons

## Key claim

Isospin violation in the (rho^0, omega)-meson system can manifest in two distinct forms analogous to CP violation in neutral kaon decays: direct violation in production or decay amplitudes, and violation due to mixing; the mixing parameter is universal across all decay pairs, but existing data cannot yet fully separate the two forms, and the apparent enhancement of rho^0 to pi^0 gamma relative to rho^pm to pi^pm gamma is consistent with mixing having a negative real part.

## Method

The paper formulates a 2x2 matrix propagator for the bare rho^0 and omega states, diagonalizing the mass-squared matrix to obtain physical states and propagators, analogous to the Lee-Oehme-Yang treatment of neutral kaon mixing. Ratios of decay widths for pairs of decays (omega, rho^0) to the same final state are expressed in terms of a single dimensionless mixing parameter wtG and a direct isospin-violating parameter a^(0) for each decay channel, with T-invariance assumed to set wtG_rhoomega = wtG_omegarho. The formalism is applied to decays to pi+pi-, pi0gamma, etagamma, e+e-, and eta' to (rho^0, omega) gamma, using the additive quark model to relate the direct isospin structure of photonic couplings.

## Key numbers

* Mixing parameter |wtG| = (6.2 +/- 0.5) x 10^-2 extracted from the ratio Gamma(omega to 2pi)/Gamma(rho to 2pi) using Particle Data Tables 2002, with the caveat that the realistic error should be at least twice higher due to variation between editions.
* Ratio r_eta'rho0/omega = Gamma(eta' to rho^0 gamma)/Gamma(eta' to omega gamma) = 9.74 +/- 1.05 from Particle Data Tables 2002.
* Ratio r_rho0/omega_eta = Gamma(rho^0 to eta gamma)/Gamma(omega to eta gamma) = 10.3 +/- 2.6 from Particle Data Tables 2002.
* Ratio r_rho0/omega(ee) = Gamma(rho^0 to e+e-)/Gamma(omega to e+e-) = 11.42 +/- 0.42 from Particle Data Tables 2002.
* Ratio r_omega/rho_pm_pi = Gamma(omega to pi^0 gamma)/Gamma(rho^pm to pi^pm gamma) = 10.9 +/- 1.3 from Particle Data Tables 2002, which exceeds the prediction based on mixing alone with Re wtG < 0.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of isospin violation in vector meson mixing, previously studied for omega to pi+pi- and rho^0 to pi^0 gamma, to all pairs of decays of rho^0 and omega into the same final state, including e+e- and radiative decays. The analogy to CP violation in neutral kaon decays, as formulated by Lee, Oehme, and Yang (1957), is used to separate direct and mixing-induced isospin violation. The paper builds on earlier work by the author (Azimov, 2002, in European Physical Journal A) and references the treatment of rho to pi gamma by BOC (likely Bijnens, Oset, and collaborators). Future precise measurements of these decay pairs could discriminate between QED and QCD sources of isospin violation and determine whether the mixing parameter is truly universal.
