# Interaction induced deformation in momentum distribution of spin polarized nuclear matter

> Effects of spin polarization on the structure of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are investigated. We show that the spin polarization induces a deformation of the Fermi spheres for nucleons with spin parallel and opposite to the polarization axes. This feature can be related to the structure of the one pion exchange contribution to a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. While the anisotropies in the momentum distribution lower the energy of the system by small amount, the associated variations of the single particle energies with the angle between the polarization axis and the particle momentum are significant.

# Interaction induced deformation in momentum distribution of spin polarized nuclear matter

## Key claim
Spin polarization in nuclear matter induces a deformation of the Fermi spheres for nucleons with spin parallel and opposite to the polarization axis, but the energy gain from this deformation is too small to alter conclusions about the equation of state or the possible existence of a ferromagnetic state. The effect is significant only for the angle-dependence of single-particle energies, which may affect response functions and neutrino propagation.

## Method
The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations using the charge-dependent CDBonn nucleon-nucleon interaction, parameterizing the Fermi momentum as a function of angle relative to the spin-polarization axis via \(k_F(s,\vartheta) = k_{F0}(s)(1 + \delta(s)\cos^2\vartheta)\). They solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently, allowing the momentum distribution to deform, and evaluated the single-particle potential using partial wave expansion of the G-matrix. The spin asymmetry and angle-dependence were ignored in the Pauli operator and starting energy used to solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density \(\rho_0\) with 25% spin polarization: the single-particle potential for nucleons with spin parallel to the polarization axis at momentum 1.46 fm\(^{-1}\) is about 2.7 MeV more attractive in the direction perpendicular to the polarization axis (\(\theta = \pi/2\)) than parallel or antiparallel, yielding a deformation parameter \(\delta(\uparrow) \approx -0.03\) (oblate).
- For completely polarized nuclear matter at \(\rho_0\): the energy difference between single-particle energies for momentum parallel versus perpendicular to the polarization axis is around 20 MeV at momentum 1.72 fm\(^{-1}\), with deformation parameter \(\delta(\uparrow) \approx -0.15\).
- For completely polarized pure neutron matter at \(\rho_0\): the gain in single-particle energy is around 6 MeV for momentum perpendicular versus parallel to the polarization axis at momentum 2.16 fm\(^{-1}\), with deformation parameter \(\delta(\uparrow) \approx -0.03\).
- The gain in energy per nucleon due to deformation in completely polarized nuclear matter is 0.53 MeV smaller than the energy calculated for the spherical momentum distribution.

## Context
This work extends BHF calculations of spin-polarized nuclear matter by Vidaña et al. (2000), who evaluated single-particle energies assuming spherical momentum distributions, by introducing deformed Fermi surfaces. The deformation is analogous to the dumbbell shape of the deuteron density distribution for spin projection \(M_s = \pm 1\) as discussed by Forest (1999). The results suggest that while deformation does not alter the equation of state or the possibility of ferromagnetic transitions, the angle-dependent single-particle spectrum could significantly affect nuclear response functions and neutrino mean free paths in neutron stars with strong magnetic fields, building on the response function framework of Reddy et al. (1998) and Sawyer (1999).
