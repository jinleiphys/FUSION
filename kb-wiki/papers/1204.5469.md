# N-Jettiness as a probe of nuclear dynamics

> We propose the use of N-jettiness (τ_N), a global event shape variable, as a probe of nuclear dynamics in lepton-nucleus collisions. It characterizes the amount of soft radiation between the jet and nuclear beam directions. We write down the factorization for the 1-jettiness ($τ_1$) distribution for the production of a single hard jet (J) in lepton-nucleus collisions: \ell+A(P) \to J(P_{J})+X. Each nuclear target gives rise to a unique pattern radiation, determined by nuclear dynamics, that can be quantified by the τ_1-distribution. Up to power corrections, the τ_1-distribution allows for a direct measurement of the nuclear PDFs. Additional nuclear-dependent effects will be dominated through power corrections of size $\sim Q_s^2(A)/(P_{JT}τ_1)$ where $Q_s(A)$ is a dynamical scale sensitive to nuclear medium effects. Such nuclear-dependent effects and the dependence of Q_s(A) on the nuclear atomic number $A$ can be probed through a dedicated program of precision measurements of τ_1-distributions for various nuclei and kinematics. We give numerical results for the 1-jettiness distribution for the simplest case of a proton target at next-to-leading-log accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the 1-jettiness (τ₁) distribution in lepton-nucleus collisions can probe nuclear dynamics, but the direct measurement of nuclear PDFs via τ₁ is valid only up to power corrections; the dominant nuclear-dependent effects appear through power corrections of size ∼ Q_s²(A)/(P_JT τ₁), where Q_s(A) is a dynamical nuclear scale sensitive to medium effects.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization formula for the 1-jettiness distribution in single-jet production in lepton-nucleus collisions using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), separating the cross section into a hard function, a nuclear beam function, a jet function, and a soft function. The beam function is matched onto nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) via perturbative coefficients, and large logarithms of P_JT/τ₁ are resummed using renormalization group evolution between the scales μ_H ∼ P_JT, μ_J ∼ √(τ₁ P_JT), and μ_S ∼ τ₁. Numerical results are presented for a proton target at next-to-leading-log (NLL) accuracy, including a model for non-perturbative soft function effects at τ₁ ∼ Λ_QCD.

## Key numbers

- For a proton target at √s = 90 GeV, P_JT = 20 GeV, y = 0 (Stage I EIC kinematics), the NLL τ₁-distribution is shown with scale variation bands for r = {1/2, 2} around the central scale r = 1.
- For a proton target at √s = 300 GeV, P_JT = 20 GeV, y = 0 (HERA kinematics), the NLL τ₁-distribution is shown with the same scale variation, and the singular part of the NLO cross-section is also plotted.
- The τ₁-distribution is cutoff at 1 GeV to keep the soft function perturbative.
- The non-perturbative soft function model uses parameters (a, b, Λ) in units of GeV, with example sets including (2.0, -0.2, 0.2), (1.2, -0.1, 0.3), (2.2, -0.4, 0.5), and (1.8, -0.05, 0.4).

## Context

This work extends the N-jettiness formalism, originally developed for proton-proton collisions by Stewart et al. (2010) and Jouttenus et al. (2011), to lepton-nucleus collisions as a new diagnostic tool for nuclear medium effects. It complements existing jet tomography approaches used in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, where jet quenching has been observed. The proposed measurements of τ₁-distributions for various nuclei at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) could enable extraction of the power-law exponent α describing the atomic number dependence of the saturation scale Q_s(A), testing the expectation from Luo et al. (1994) that α ∼ 1/3.
