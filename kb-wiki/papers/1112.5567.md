# Associated hyperon-kaon production via neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We present the investigation of the neutrino-induced strangeness associated production on nuclei in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) framework at the intermediate neutrino energies. In this study, the elementary hadronic weak amplitudes are embedded inside the nuclear medium for the description of the exclusive channels of neutrino-nucleus interactions. These amplitudes are extracted using a model-dependent evaluation of the hadronic vertex using the Born term approximation in which the application of the Cabibbo V-A theory and SU(3) symmetry are assumed to be valid. The nuclear effects are included via the bound state wavefunctions of the nucleon obtained from the relativistic mean field (RMF) models. Two kinematics settings are used to examine various distributions of the differential cross section in the rest frame of the target nuclei. The numerical results are obtained for the neutrino-induced charged-current (CC) \,$\rm K^{^+}Λ$-production on bound neutrons in $1s^{1/2}$ and $1p^{3/2}$ orbitals of $^{12}$C. The angular distributions are forward peaked under both kinematic settings, whereas under the quasifree setting the cross sections tend mimic the missing momentum distribution of the bound nucleon inside the nucleus.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first theoretical description of neutrino-induced associated strangeness production (K⁺Λ) on nuclei within a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) framework, but the results are explicitly baseline calculations that neglect final-state interactions, and the elementary amplitudes rely on the Born term approximation assuming no strong resonances near threshold.

## Method
The authors embed elementary hadronic weak amplitudes (extracted via Born term approximation using Cabibbo V-A theory and SU(3) symmetry) into the nuclear medium by invoking the impulse approximation, where the incident neutrino interacts with a single bound nucleon inside the target nucleus via exchange of a single weak gauge boson. Bound nucleon wavefunctions are obtained from the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with NL3 parametrization for ¹²C, while the outgoing kaon and hyperon are described as plane waves. The differential cross section is calculated for charged-current νμn → μ⁻K⁺Λ on neutrons in the 1s¹/² and 1p³/² orbitals of ¹²C under two kinematic settings.

## Key numbers
- Fermi constant G_F = 1.166 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Cabibbo angle: cos θ_C ≈ 0.97
- The momentum-space radial wavefunctions indicate significantly larger cross sections can be obtained by tuning the missing momentum magnitude in the range ≤ 0.3 GeV
- The bound nucleon binding energy E_b is determined from the RMF model with NL3 parameter set (specific numerical values for 1s¹/² and 1p³/² orbitals are not given in the truncated text)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous free-nucleon formulation (Adera and Van der Ventel, 2010) to include nuclear medium effects via bound state wavefunctions, representing the first theoretical treatment of this process on nuclei. It contrasts with earlier theoretical estimations by Shrock (1974), Mecklenburg (1978), Amer (1978), and Dewan (1981) that assumed free nucleon targets, and complements the work of Rafi Alam et al. (2010) on ΔS = 1 single-strange-particle production using chiral symmetry. The formalism provides baseline RPWIA calculations against which future relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations and experimental data from MINERνA, K2K, and MiniBooNE can be compared.
