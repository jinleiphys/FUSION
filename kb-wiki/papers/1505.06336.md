# Four-point functions and the permutation group S4

> Four-point functions are at the heart of many interesting physical processes. A prime example is the light-by-light scattering amplitude, which plays an important role in the calculation of hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. In the calculation of such quantities one faces the challenge of finding a suitable and well-behaved basis of tensor structures in coordinate and/or momentum space. Provided all (or many) of the external legs represent similar particle content, a powerful tool to construct and organize such bases is the permutation group S4. We introduce an efficient notation for dealing with the irreducible multiplets of S4, and we highlight the merits of this treatment by exemplifying four-point functions with gauge-boson legs such as the four-gluon vertex and the light-by-light scattering amplitude. The multiplet analysis is also useful for isolating the important kinematic regions and the dynamical singularity content of such amplitudes. Our analysis serves as a basis for future efficient calculations of these and similar objects.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic method for constructing tensor bases for four-point functions using the permutation group S4, which is particularly useful for organizing the large number of tensor structures (136 for the four-gluon vertex and light-by-light amplitude) into irreducible multiplets, thereby isolating the most relevant momentum dependence and simplifying the implementation of gauge invariance and analyticity constraints.

## Method

The authors introduce an efficient notation for irreducible multiplets of the permutation group S4, generalizing their earlier S3 treatment for three-point functions. They apply this multiplet analysis to decompose the Lorentz-invariant momentum variables and tensor structures of four-point functions into S4 singlets, then use transversality constraints to derive a "type-II" basis separating gauge parts from transverse parts that are free of kinematic singularities. The method is demonstrated for the scalar two-photon current as a template, then extended to vector four-point functions such as the four-gluon vertex and light-by-light scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The four-gluon vertex and light-by-light amplitude have (N,M) = (136,6) tensor structures and independent Lorentz invariants, respectively, without implementing gauge invariance
* The three-gluon vertex has (N,M) = (14,3) tensor structures and invariants
* A nucleon Bethe-Salpeter amplitude has (N,M) = (64,5) and a Delta-baryon has (128,5)
* A tetraquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitude has (N,M) = (256,9) tensor structures and invariants
* The scalar two-photon current is decomposed into 5 tensor structures (type I) which reduce to 2 transverse structures (type II) after imposing gauge invariance

## Context

This work extends the permutation-group multiplet approach previously applied to the three-gluon vertex and baryon Faddeev amplitudes by Eichmann (2014) and others. It provides a technical foundation for efficient calculations of the light-by-light scattering amplitude relevant to the muon (g-2) puzzle, as well as the four-gluon vertex and tetraquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The method enables future calculations that properly separate gauge-invariant transverse parts from gauge parts, avoiding unphysical kinematic singularities that would otherwise arise in incomplete dynamical calculations.
