# Enhanced yield ratio of light nuclei in heavy ion collisions with a first-order QCD phase transition

> Using a transport model that includes a first-order chiral phase transition between the partonic and the hadronic matter, we study the development of density fluctuations in the matter produced in heavy ion collisions as it undergoes the phase transition, and their time evolution in later hadronic stage of the collisions. Using the coalescence model to describe the production of deuterons and tritons from nucleons at the kinetic freeze out, we find that the yield ratio $ N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/ N_\text{d}^2$, where $N_\text{p}$, $N_\text{d}$, and $N_\text{t}$ are, respectively, the proton, deuteron, and triton numbers, is enhanced if the evolution trajectory of the produced matter in the QCD phase diagram passes through the spinodal region of a first-order chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

Using a transport model that includes a first-order chiral phase transition, the yield ratio \( N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/ N_\text{d}^2 \) is enhanced if the evolution trajectory of the produced matter in the QCD phase diagram passes through the spinodal region of a first-order chiral phase transition; the enhancement is conditional on the density fluctuations developed during the phase transition surviving later hadronic scatterings.

## Method

The authors extended a partonic transport model based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model with a first-order chiral phase transition (vector coupling \( g_V = 0 \)) to include hadronic scatterings via a relativistic transport (ART) model. They initialized quark matter with a Woods-Saxon density distribution and evolved it using the test particle method, then converted quarks to hadrons via spatial coalescence. Light nuclei (deuterons and tritons) were produced from kinetically freeze-out nucleons using a coalescence model with Gaussian Wigner functions, and the yield ratio was computed from the nucleon density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The yield ratio \( N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/ N_\text{d}^2 \) for the first-order phase transition case (\( g_V = 0 \)) is around 0.5, compared to around 0.4 from a smooth crossover transition (AMPT model with no first-order transition), regardless of collision energy.
- The critical temperature and net-quark density for the first-order transition in the NJL model with \( g_V = 0 \) are around 70 MeV and 0.85 fm\(^{-3}\), respectively.
- The initial central net-quark density was set to \( \rho_0 = 1.5 \) fm\(^{-3}\) with a Woods-Saxon radius \( R = 6 \) fm and surface thickness \( a = 0.6 \) fm.
- The deuteron Wigner function parameter \( \sigma \) is related to the deuteron root-mean-square radius \( r_\text{d} \) via \( \sigma = \sqrt{4/3} \, r_\text{d} \approx 2.26 \) fm; the triton root-mean-square radius is \( r_\text{t} = 1.59 \) fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Sun et al. (2017, 2018) that first proposed the yield ratio \( N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/ N_\text{d}^2 \) as a probe of density fluctuations from a first-order phase transition, but those studies did not model the survival of fluctuations through hadronic scatterings. The present results contradict predictions from the AMPT model with a smooth crossover transition (Sun et al., 2020), which gave a constant ratio of about 0.4. The findings suggest that the non-monotonic enhancement observed in experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and SPS energies could be due to a first-order QCD phase transition, and they enable future comparisons with stochastic fluid dynamics and nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics approaches.
