# Onset of Deconfinement in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions - Past, Present and Future -

> In 2007 Mark I. Gorenstein celebrated his 60th birthday. This report is dedicated to Mark and it sketches the results obtained during the past ten years of our collaboration and friendship. They concern search for and study of the onset of deconfinement in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The onset of deconfinement in nucleus-nucleus collisions is indicated by a change in the energy dependence of pion multiplicity, strangeness-to-entropy ratio, and temperature at a collision energy of about 30A GeV, as predicted by the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES). The claim is hedged by the model assumption that the early-stage system is in equilibrium and that the transition temperature T_C lies between 170 and 200 MeV, placing the transition region from 15A to 60A GeV.

## Method

The authors analyzed final NA49 results from the CERN SPS energy scan program (20A, 30A, 40A, 80A, and 158A GeV Pb+Pb collisions) together with AGS and RHIC data, comparing them to predictions of the SMES model. The SMES assumes fast equilibration of matter at the early stage of collisions and uses the generalized Fermi-Landau conditions to relate pion multiplicity to early-stage entropy, with the slope parameter proportional to g^(1/4) where g is the effective number of internal degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The transition region extends from 15A to 60A GeV for T_C values of 170–200 MeV
- The steepening of pion energy dependence starts at about 20A GeV in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions
- The increase of the slope in the pion multiplicity per wounded nucleon versus F (Fermi collision energy measure) is a factor of about 1.3, corresponding to an increase of effective internal degrees of freedom by a factor of 1.3^4 ≈ 3
- The onset of deconfinement is indicated at about 30A GeV based on the non-monotonic energy dependence of the ⟨K+⟩/⟨π+⟩ ratio
- The strangeness carrier mass in confined matter is about 500 MeV (kaon mass), while in QGP it is reduced to the strange quark mass of about 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SMES model proposed by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1997) and confirmed by the NA49 Collaboration's energy scan program (Afanasiev 2002). It contradicts the earlier paradigm that strangeness enhancement and J/ψ suppression were specific QGP signals, as argued by Rafelski (1980s) and Satz (1980s). The results enable future experimental searches at CERN SPS (NA61), BNL RHIC (STAR, PHENIX), JINR NICA (MPD), and FAIR SIS-300 (CBM), as well as theoretical studies of the transition line structure, critical point, and fluctuations in relativistic gases.
