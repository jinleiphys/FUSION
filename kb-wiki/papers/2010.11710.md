# Dyson-Schwinger approach to pion-nucleon scattering using time-ordered perturbation theory

> We present a simple description of pion-nucleon ($πN$) scattering taking into account the full complexity of pion absorption and creation on the nucleon. To do this we solve Dyson-Schwinger equations within the framework of Time-Ordered Perturbation Theory. This enables us to construct partial wave separable $ πN$ t matrices that can be useful in models of nuclear processes involving fully dressed nucleons. At the same time, our approach demonstrates features of Quantum Field Theory, like particle dressing, renormalisation, and the use of Dyson-Schwinger equations, in a non-relativistic context that is maximally close to that of Quantum Mechanics. For this reason, this article may also be of pedagogical interest.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple, non-relativistic model of pion-nucleon scattering that solves Dyson-Schwinger equations within Time-Ordered Perturbation Theory, achieving full dressing of all nucleon propagators (including those in intermediate states) without the one-pion approximation used in prior work; this is presented as a pedagogical demonstration of quantum field theory concepts in a quantum-mechanical setting, but the numerical results are limited to fitting phase shifts below the onset of inelasticity (center-of-mass energies below approximately \(m+3m_\pi\)).

## Method

The authors model pion-nucleon scattering using a Hamiltonian with non-relativistic nucleon kinematics and relativistic pion kinematics, including a bare \(\pi NN\) vertex and a separable background potential. They solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations (for the dressed nucleon propagator, dressed vertex, and background t-matrix) iteratively in the \(P_{11}\) partial wave, using Gaussian quadrature with contour rotation to handle singularities, and employing a dispersion relation for the dressed nucleon propagator to manage the energy dependence during iteration. The form factors for the bare vertex and separable potential are taken as analytic functions of momentum with adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

* The model is designed to describe \(\pi N\) scattering for center-of-mass energies below approximately \(m+3m_\pi\), which is the experimental onset of inelasticity.
* The bare nucleon mass \(m_0\) is set to \(m_0 = m - \Sigma(m)\) to ensure the dressed propagator has a pole at the physical nucleon mass \(m\).
* The wave function renormalization constant is given by \(Z = 1/[1 - \Sigma'(m)]\).
* The separable potential in the \(P_{11}\) partial wave uses \(\lambda = -1\) (repulsive sign).
* The form factors are parameterized as \(f_0(k) = (k C_0 / \sqrt{\omega_\pi(k)}) \cdot 1/(k^2 + \Lambda^2)^{n_0}\) and \(h(k) = (k C_1 / \sqrt{\omega_\pi(k)}) [1/(k^2 + \beta_1^2)^{n_1} + C_2 k^{2n_2}/(k^2 + \beta_2^2)^{n_3}]\), with free parameters \(C_0, C_1, C_2, \beta_1, \beta_2, \Lambda\) and integer powers \(n_0, n_1, n_2, n_3\).

## Context

This work extends the coupled \(\pi NN\)-\(NN\) few-body equations developed by Mizutani and Koltun (1977), Avishai (1979), Afnan (1980), Blankleider (1981), and Rinat (1982), which all used the one-pion approximation (neglecting states with two or more pions) and thus contained partially dressed nucleons. The authors aim to provide the \(\pi N\) input (separable t-matrices with fully dressed nucleons) needed for the consistent convolution formulation of Kvinikhidze (1992) and Blankleider (1993), which resolved the dressing inconsistency but had not been numerically implemented. The paper is also intended as a pedagogical demonstration of Dyson-Schwinger equations, renormalization, and particle dressing in a non-relativistic context.
