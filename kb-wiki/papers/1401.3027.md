# SAID Analysis of Meson Photoproduction : Determination of Neutron and Proton EM Couplings

> We present an overview of the GW SAID group effort to analyze on new pion photoproduction on both proton- and neutron-targets. The main database contribution came from the recent CLAS and MAMI unpolarized and polarized measurements. The differential cross section for the processes gamma n --> pi- p was extracted from new measurements accounting for Fermi motion effects in the impulse approximation (IA) as well as NN and piN effects beyond the IA. The EM coupling results are compared to other recent studies.

# SAID Analysis of Meson Photoproduction: Determination of Neutron and Proton EM Couplings

## Key claim

The GW SAID group has extracted differential cross sections for $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ from deuteron-target measurements by applying final-state interaction (FSI) corrections beyond the impulse approximation, yielding new neutron helicity amplitudes that sometimes deviate significantly from previous SAID and PDG values; however, the neutron-target data constitute only about 15% of the present SAID database, and the extracted cross sections remain model-dependent due to the required nuclear corrections.

## Method

The analysis used the SAID partial-wave analysis framework to fit pion photoproduction data on both proton and neutron targets, incorporating new CLAS and MAMI-B measurements. For neutron-target reactions, the $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ cross section was extracted from $\gamma d \to \pi^- pp$ data in the quasi-free kinematic region using the impulse approximation, with Feynman diagrams for NN- and $\pi$N-FSI effects included, using the Bonn potential for the deuteron description and SAID phenomenological amplitudes for $\gamma N \to \pi N$, NN-elastic, and $\pi$N-elastic processes as inputs.

## Key numbers

- The overall SAID $\chi^2$/data for proton-target fits remained stable at 2.1 as the database grew from 13.4k to 27.3k data points since 1995
- New CLAS beam asymmetry $\Sigma$ measurements for $\gamma p \to \pi^0 p$ and $\gamma p \to \pi^+ n$ covered E$_\gamma$ = 1.100 to 1.860 GeV, corresponding to CM energy W = 1.7 to 2.1 GeV, at $\theta$ = 30 to 150$^\circ$
- The FSI correction factor for CLAS $\gamma d \to \pi^- pp$ data (E$_\gamma$ = 1050 to 2700 MeV, $\theta$ = 30 to 160$^\circ$) was $\Delta\sigma/\sigma < 10\%$
- MAMI-B data covered E$_\gamma$ = 300 to 455 MeV and $\theta$ = 60 to 140$^\circ$ in the $\Delta$-isobar region
- Neutron-target data constitute only about 15% of the present SAID database
- For the N(1675)5/2$^-$ state, the SAID GB12 neutron helicity amplitudes are $nA_{1/2} = -58 \pm 2$ and $nA_{3/2} = -80 \pm 5$ (in units of $10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$), compared to PDG12 values of $-43 \pm 12$ and $-58 \pm 13$
- For the N(1650)1/2$^-$ state, SAID GB12 gives $nA_{1/2} = -40 \pm 10$, while PDG12 gives $-15 \pm 21$ (in $10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$)
- The Fermi-motion smearing energy scale in deuteron targets is approximately 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the GW SAID program established by Dick Arndt in 1997, which includes $\pi N \to \pi N$, $\gamma N \to \pi N$, and $\gamma^* p \to \pi N$ components. The analysis incorporates new CLAS and MAMI-B measurements to improve neutron-target couplings, which are less precise than proton couplings due to the need for model-dependent nuclear corrections from deuteron targets. The results are compared to other recent studies including BnGa12/BnGa13 (2012-2013), Kent12 (2012), MAID07 (2007), and PDG12 (2012) determinations, with significant deviations found for states such as N(1650)1/2$^-$, N(1675)5/2$^-$, and N(1680)5/2$^+$. Future experiments at MAMI-C (threshold to 800 MeV and up to 1500 MeV) are planned to measure $\gamma d \to \pi^- pp$ and $\gamma d \to \pi^0 np$ with improved FSI corrections.
