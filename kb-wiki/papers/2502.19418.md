# Work and heat exchanged during sudden quenches of strongly coupled quantum systems

> How should one define thermodynamic quantities (internal energy, work, heat, etc.) for quantum systems coupled to their environments strongly? We examine three (classically equivalent) definitions of a quantum system's internal energy under strong-coupling conditions. Each internal-energy definition implies a definition of work and a definition of heat. Our study focuses on quenches, common processes in which the Hamiltonian changes abruptly. In these processes, the first law of thermodynamics holds for each set of definitions by construction. However, we prove that only two sets obey the second law. We illustrate our findings using a simple spin model. Our results guide studies of thermodynamic quantities in strongly coupled quantum systems.

## Key claim

The paper proves that, for sudden quenches of strongly coupled quantum systems, only two of three classically equivalent definitions of internal energy (and their associated work and heat definitions) obey the second law of thermodynamics; the third definition can violate it, despite all three satisfying the first law by construction.

## Method

The authors consider a composite system-reservoir setup with a finite quantum system S and reservoir R coupled via an interaction term V. They examine three candidate definitions for the system's internal energy: the total energy minus the isolated reservoir's energy (U_tot), the expectation value of the Hamiltonian of mean force (U_H*), and the expectation value of an effective Hamiltonian derived from the Hamiltonian of mean force (U_E*). For quench processes (abrupt Hamiltonian changes at t=0 followed by unitary evolution under a fixed Hamiltonian), they derive work and heat from each internal-energy definition and test whether the inequality W ≥ ΔF_S (the second law) holds analytically and numerically using a two-spin model.

## Key numbers

* For the two-spin model with parameters α=0.8, γ=1.2, χ=1.8, and β=1, during a system quench where ε changes from ε_A=0 to ε_B, W_E* violates the second law at certain ε_B values while W_tot and W_H* satisfy it (Figure 1a).
* For the two-spin model with parameters ε=1.0, α=5.0, χ_B=1.2, and β=1, during an interaction quench where coupling constants change from γ_A=χ_A=0 to γ_B and χ_B, W_E* violates the second law at some γ_B values while W_tot and W_H* satisfy it (Figure 1b).
* When χ=0 in the two-spin model (satisfying commutation relations [H_S^A, V] = [H_S^B, V] = 0), all three work definitions coincide and obey the second law during a system quench (Figure 1c).

## Context

This work extends classical strong-coupling thermodynamics results from Seifert (2016) and Jarzynski (2017) into the quantum regime, where the three internal-energy definitions that are equivalent classically can diverge. The paper builds on prior approaches to quantum strong-coupling thermodynamics by Rivas (2019, 2020), Miller (2018), and Campisi (2009), and it enables future studies of thermodynamic quantities in strongly coupled quantum systems, including lattice gauge theories as proposed by Davoudi (2024).
