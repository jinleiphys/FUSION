# The anapole moment and nucleon weak interactions

> From the recent measurement of parity nonconservation (PNC) in the Cs atom we have extracted the constant of the nuclear spin dependent electron-nucleon PNC interaction, $κ= 0.442 (63)$; the anapole moment constant, $κ_a = 0.364 (62)$; the strength of the PNC proton-nucleus potential, $g_p = 7.3 \pm 1.2 (exp.) \pm 1.5 (theor.)$; the $π$-meson-nucleon interaction constant, $f_π\equiv h_π^{1} = [9.5 \pm 2.1 (exp.) \pm 3.5 (theor.)] \times 10^{-7}$; and the strength of the neutron-nucleus potential, $g_n = -1.7 \pm 0.8 (exp.) \pm 1.3 (theor.)$.

## Key claim

From the recent measurement of parity nonconservation (PNC) in the Cs atom, the authors extract the anapole moment constant and related weak interaction parameters, but the extracted values carry significant theoretical uncertainties (up to 30% from nuclear structure calculations) and depend on the choice of nuclear model and the treatment of finite-nuclear-size corrections.

## Method

The authors used the measured parity-nonconserving transition amplitudes between the 6s and 7s states of 133Cs, together with many-body atomic calculations of the ratio of nuclear-spin-dependent to spin-independent PNC amplitudes, to extract the constant κ of the nuclear-spin-dependent electron-nucleon PNC interaction. They then separated κ into three contributions (anapole moment, weak electron-nucleus spin-dependent term, and combined weak-hyperfine term) to isolate the anapole moment constant κa, and related κa to the proton-nucleus weak potential strength gp via nuclear shell-model calculations. Finally, they used the DDH meson-nucleon coupling framework to extract the π-meson-nucleon interaction constant fπ and the neutron-nucleus potential strength gn.

## Key numbers

- κ (nuclear-spin-dependent electron-nucleon PNC interaction constant) = 0.442 (63) for 133Cs
- κa (anapole moment constant) = 0.364 (62) for 133Cs, after finite-nuclear-size correction of 1.6%
- gp (strength of PNC proton-nucleus potential) = 7.3 ± 1.2 (exp.) ± 1.5 (theor.)
- fπ ≡ hπ1 (π-meson-nucleon interaction constant) = [9.5 ± 2.1 (exp.) ± 3.5 (theor.)] × 10−7
- gn (strength of neutron-nucleus potential) = −1.7 ± 0.8 (exp.) ± 1.3 (theor.)
- Measured PNC transition amplitude E = 1.5935 (56) mV/cm for the 6s to 7s transition in 133Cs
- Measured nuclear-spin-dependent contribution Im(E1a)/β = 0.077 (11) mV/cm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of nuclear anapole moments introduced by Zel'dovich (1957) and developed by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1980, 1984), providing the first unambiguous extraction of the anapole moment constant from experimental data. The results enable comparison with the DDH "best" values of meson-nucleon weak interaction constants (Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein, 1980) and with QCD calculations of fπ (Khatsimovskii, 1985; Kaplan, 1993), while also highlighting the tension with smaller estimates of fπ derived from 18F PNC measurements. The analysis enables future measurements of anapole moments in other nuclei such as 207Pb to further constrain the neutron-nucleus weak interaction.
