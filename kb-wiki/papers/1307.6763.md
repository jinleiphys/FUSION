# Electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma across the deconfinement transition

> A lattice calculation is presented for the electrical conductivity of the QCD plasma with 2+1 dynamical flavours at nonzero temperature. We employ the conserved lattice current on anisotropic lattices using a tadpole-improved clover action and study the behaviour of the conductivity over a wide range of temperatures, both below and above the deconfining transition. The conductivity is extracted from a spectral-function analysis using the Maximal Entropy Method and a discussion of its systematics is provided.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD observation of an increase in the electrical conductivity divided by temperature, sigma/T, across the deconfinement transition in a 2+1 flavour plasma, with the rise beginning already below T_c. The result is extracted via the Maximum Entropy Method and carries systematic uncertainties from the default model parameter b, with the conductivity robust for b values between 0.4 and 1.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 dynamical quark flavours using a tadpole-improved clover action on anisotropic lattices with renormalised anisotropy xi = a_s / a_tau = 3.5. They computed the electromagnetic current-current correlator in the zero-momentum limit using the exactly conserved lattice vector current, neglecting disconnected diagrams. The electrical conductivity was extracted from the spectral function via the Kubo formula using the Maximum Entropy Method with Bryan's algorithm, employing a default model m(omega) = m_0 (b + a_tau omega) and varying the parameter b to assess systematic uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a_s = 0.1227(8) fm, a_tau = 0.03506(23) fm, corresponding to a_tau^{-1} = 5.63(4) GeV
- Renormalised anisotropy: xi = a_s / a_tau = 3.5
- Light quark mass tuned to M_pi / M_rho = 0.446(3)
- Temperatures studied: 117, 141, 156, 176, 201, 235, 281, and 352 MeV, corresponding to T/T_c from 0.63 to 1.90
- Critical temperature estimated from renormalised Polyakov loop inflection point
- Conductivity results shown in Fig. 5 as C_em^{-1} sigma/T versus temperature, with C_em = 5/9 e^2 for two light flavours; systematic uncertainty from varying b between 0.4 and 1 is indicated by vertical size of rectangles, statistical jackknife error combines all b values in that range

## Context

This work extends previous lattice calculations of the electrical conductivity in quenched QCD (N_f=0) by Gupta, Aarts (2007), Ding (2010), and a two-flavour study at a single temperature by Brandt. It is the first lattice study with 2+1 dynamical flavours across a wide temperature range, enabling observation of the temperature dependence of sigma/T. The results enable comparison with effective QCD models and semi-analytical approaches such as those by Fernandez-Fraile (2005), Nam (2012), Cassing (2013), and Qin, and may inform phenomenological modelling of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions as discussed by Tuchin (2013) and McLerran (2013).
