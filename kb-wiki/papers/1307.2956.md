# Dark matter effect on the mass measurement of neutron stars

> Newly-determined mass of 1.97 $\pm$ 0.04 $M_{\odot}$ for PSR J1614-2230 has been a challenge for the neutron star with a hyperon core (namely hyperon star), since hyperons usually reduce the theoretical maximum mass of the star. In this article, we consider dark matter as another possible constituent in hyperon stars' interior to loose this mass constrain. We take dark matter as self-interacting Fermi gas with certain repulsive interaction among the dark matter particles and non-interaction between dark matter and ordinary matter as is generally assumed. We find that the star maximum mass is sensitive to the particle mass of dark matter, and a high enough star mass larger than 2 $M_{\odot}$ could be achieved when the particle mass is small enough. In this particular model, a strong upper limit 0.64 GeV for dark matter mass is obtained in strongly-interacting dark matter and 0.16 GeV for dark matter mass in weakly-interacting dark matter. Dark matter accumulated around the star could also contribute to the mass measurement, however, such contribution could be safely ignored when the generally used dark matter density is assumed.

# Dark matter effect on the mass measurement of neutron stars

## Key claim

The presence of non-self-annihilating dark matter as a Fermi gas with repulsive self-interaction inside hyperon stars can raise the theoretical maximum mass above 2 solar masses, but only if the dark matter particle mass is below a model-dependent upper limit of 0.64 GeV for strongly-interacting dark matter or 0.16 GeV for weakly-interacting dark matter.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for a two-fluid model where the total pressure and energy density are simple sums of ordinary hypernuclear matter and dark matter contributions. The ordinary matter equation of state uses the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with microscopic three-body forces and Nijmegen ESC08b hyperon-nucleon potentials, while the dark matter is modeled as a self-interacting Fermi gas with a single interaction scale parameter m_I set to 100 MeV for strongly-interacting or 300 GeV for weakly-interacting candidates. The gravitational contribution of a dark matter halo around the star is estimated using the Roche lobe size of the PSR J1614-2230 binary system and an average local dark matter density of 0.474 GeV/cm^3.

## Key numbers

- The measured mass of PSR J1614-2230 is 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses, which motivates the hyperon puzzle addressed in this work.
- For strongly-interacting dark matter (m_I = 100 MeV), the dark matter particle mass must obey m_chi < 0.64 GeV to allow a hyperon star maximum mass above 2 solar masses.
- For weakly-interacting dark matter (m_I = 300 GeV), the dark matter particle mass must obey m_chi < 0.16 GeV to allow a hyperon star maximum mass above 2 solar masses.
- The extra mass contribution from a gravitationally captured dark matter halo around PSR J1614-2230 is approximately 10^{-24} solar masses, which is negligible for Shapiro delay analysis.
- The Roche lobe radius of the binary system is estimated as 3 × 10^{11} cm, and the average local dark matter density is taken as 0.474 GeV/cm^3.

## Context

This work addresses the "hyperon puzzle" raised by the Demorest (2010) measurement of PSR J1614-2230, where hyperonic equations of state from Brueckner theory (Burrows and collaborators, 2010-2011) predict maximum masses below 1.4 solar masses. It extends previous dark matter-admixed neutron star models by Leung et al. (2011) and Ciarcelluti and Sandin (2011) by systematically varying the dark matter particle mass and interaction strength, and by considering the gravitational capture of a dark matter halo. The results place upper limits on non-self-annihilating dark matter particle masses that could be tested by future observations of high-mass neutron stars.
