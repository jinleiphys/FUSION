# Approximate Treatment of Hermitian Effective Interactions and a Bound on the Error

> The Hermitian effective interaction can be well-approximated by (R+R^dagger)/2 if the eigenvalues of omega^dagger omega are small or state-independent(degenerate), where R is the standard non-Hermitian effective interaction and omega maps the model-space states onto the excluded space. An error bound on this approximation is given.

## Key claim
The Hermitian effective interaction \(W\) can be well approximated by \(W_{app} = (R+R^{\dagger})/2\) (where \(R\) is the standard non-Hermitian effective interaction) if the eigenvalues \(\mu_{\alpha}^2\) of \(\omega^{\dagger}\omega\) are either small or state-independent (degenerate), and an explicit error bound on this approximation is derived.

## Method
The authors derive an analytical bound \(\Delta W \leq W_0 Z_w\) for the deviation \(\Delta W = \sum_{ij} |\langle i|W-W_{app}|j\rangle|\), where \(W_0\) is a constant bounded by \(d^2 V_0\) (with \(d\) the model-space dimension and \(V_0\) the maximum matrix element of \(PHP\) and \(PVQ\)), and \(Z_w\) is a function of the eigenvalues \(\mu_{\alpha}^2\) of \(\omega^{\dagger}\omega\). They then test this bound numerically using a 4x4 exactly solvable model Hamiltonian with a strength parameter \(x\), taking the lowest two eigenstates of \(H_0\) as the model space and computing the effective interactions via the Krenciglowa-Kuo iteration scheme.

## Key numbers
- For \(x=0.04\), \(\Delta W = 0.5\times 10^{-3}\) and \(\delta E = 0.3\times 10^{-3}\); the eigenvalues \(\mu_{\alpha}^2\) are 0.054 and \(2.8\times 10^{-5}\).
- For \(x=0.22\), \(\Delta W = 0.7\times 10^{-5}\) and \(\delta E = 0.1\times 10^{-5}\); the eigenvalues \(\mu_{\alpha}^2\) are 0.461 and 0.453 (nearly degenerate).
- For \(x=0.34\), \(\Delta W = 0.965\) and \(\delta E = 0.653\); the eigenvalues \(\mu_{\alpha}^2\) are 1.269 and 0.172 (one large, one small).
- The bound \(W_0 Z_w\) is shown to always exceed \(\Delta W\) for all values of \(x\) tested, with \(W_0\) taken as \(0.6+15x\) (3/20 of \(d^2 V_0\)).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study of non-Hermiticity in effective interactions (Suzuki et al., 1993) by providing a formal criterion and error bound for the Hermitian approximation. It builds on the formal theory of Hermitian effective interactions developed by des Cloizeaux (1960) and Brandow (1967), and on the improved approach of Suzuki (1982). The results enable practitioners to judge when the computationally simpler approximation \(W_{app}\) can be safely used in shell-model calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, as previously explored by Kuo et al. (1993).
