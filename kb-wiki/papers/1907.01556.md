# Three-pion contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization

> We address the contribution of the $3π$ channel to hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) using a dispersive representation of the $e^+e^-\to 3π$ amplitude. This channel gives the second-largest individual contribution to the total HVP integral in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $(g-2)_μ$, both to its absolute value and uncertainty. It is largely dominated by the narrow resonances $ω$ and $ϕ$, but not to the extent that the off-peak regions were negligible, so that at the level of accuracy relevant for $(g-2)_μ$ an analysis of the available data as model independent as possible becomes critical. Here, we provide such an analysis based on a global fit function using analyticity and unitarity of the underlying $γ^*\to3π$ amplitude and its normalization from a chiral low-energy theorem, which, in particular, allows us to check the internal consistency of the various $e^+e^-\to 3π$ data sets. Overall, we obtain $a_μ^{3π}|_{\leq 1.8\,\text{GeV}}=46.2(6)(6)\times 10^{-10}$ as our best estimate for the total $3π$ contribution consistent with all (low-energy) constraints from QCD. In combination with a recent dispersive analysis imposing the same constraints on the $2π$ channel below $1\,\text{GeV}$, this covers nearly $80\%$ of the total HVP contribution, leading to $a_μ^\text{HVP}=692.3(3.3)\times 10^{-10}$ when the remainder is taken from the literature, and thus reaffirming the $(g-2)_μ$ anomaly at the level of at least $3.4σ$. As side products, we find for the vacuum-polarization-subtracted masses $M_ω=782.63(3)(1)\,\text{MeV}$ and $M_ϕ=1019.20(2)(1)\,\text{MeV}$, confirming the tension to the $ω$ mass as extracted from the $2π$ channel.

## Key claim

The three-pion channel's contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization is determined using a dispersive representation of the $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ amplitude, yielding a result that is consistent with all low-energy QCD constraints, but the analysis reveals internal tensions among different experimental data sets that require careful treatment of correlations and energy-calibration uncertainties.

## Method

The authors constructed a global fit function for the $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ cross section based on Khuri-Treiman equations, which encode analyticity and unitarity of the $\gamma^*\to 3\pi$ amplitude, with the normalization fixed at $q^2=0$ by the chiral anomaly (Wess-Zumino-Witten term) and at the $\omega$ and $\phi$ resonance peaks by the corresponding decay widths. The fit included a conformal polynomial to account for non-resonant contributions and was applied to data from SND, CMD-2, DM1, DM2, ND, and BaBar, using an iterative covariance matrix method to avoid the D'Agostini bias from normalization uncertainties.

## Key numbers

* Total $3\pi$ contribution to $a_\mu$ up to $1.8\,\text{GeV}$: $a_\mu^{3\pi}|_{\leq 1.8\,\text{GeV}} = 46.2(6)(6) \times 10^{-10}$ (best estimate consistent with all QCD constraints)
* Combined HVP contribution covering nearly $80\%$ of the total: $a_\mu^\text{HVP} = 692.3(3.3) \times 10^{-10}$ when remainder taken from literature
* Vacuum-polarization-subtracted $\omega$ mass: $M_\omega = 782.63(3)(1)\,\text{MeV}$
* Vacuum-polarization-subtracted $\phi$ mass: $M_\phi = 1019.20(2)(1)\,\text{MeV}$
* The $(g-2)_\mu$ anomaly is reaffirmed at the level of at least $3.4\sigma$

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach previously applied to the $2\pi$ channel below $1\,\text{GeV}$ by Colangelo et al. (2018) to the $3\pi$ channel, which gives the second-largest individual contribution to HVP. The analysis enables a data-driven, model-independent determination of the $3\pi$ contribution that can be combined with the $2\pi$ dispersive result to cover nearly $80\%$ of the total HVP integral, with the remainder taken from literature compilations such as Davier et al. (2017) and Keshavarzi et al. (2018). The results also confirm a tension in the $\omega$ mass extracted from the $2\pi$ channel.
