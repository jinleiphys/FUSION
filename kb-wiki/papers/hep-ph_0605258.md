# Nuclear Medium Effects in $J/Ψ$ Production at HERA-B

> In this paper we estimate the influence of the shadowing effect and initial state parton energy loss in the quarkonium production at HERA-B. We analyze the $x_F$ behavior of the effective exponent $α(x_F)$ and present a comparison with the preliminary HERA-B data for $J/Ψ$ production. Moreover, we estimate the magnitude of these effects in the $J/Ψ$ production at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that the combination of nuclear shadowing and initial-state parton energy loss, as modeled by the EKS or DS parameterizations and the LQS or BDMPS energy-loss models, cannot describe the preliminary HERA-B data for J/Ψ production at negative x_F, suggesting that the magnitude of antishadowing may be overestimated or that the energy loss in cold nuclear matter is smaller than predicted.

## Method

The authors used the color evaporation model (CEM) at leading order in perturbative QCD to compute J/Ψ production cross sections in proton-nucleus collisions. They incorporated nuclear shadowing via two parameterizations (EKS and DS) and initial-state parton energy loss via two estimates (LQS and BDMPS), then calculated the effective exponent α(x_F) from the ratio of cross sections on heavy and light targets. The results were compared with preliminary HERA-B data for tungsten and carbon targets at √s = 41.6 GeV, and predictions were also made for RHIC at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- HERA-B center-of-mass energy: √s = 41.6 GeV.
- For the LQS energy-loss estimate with α_s ~ 0.3 and A = 184: −dE/dz ≃ 1.28 GeV/fm.
- For the BDMPS energy-loss estimate with α_s ~ 0.3 and A = 184: −dE_q/dz ≃ 0.12 GeV/fm and −dE_g/dz ≃ 0.28 GeV/fm.
- The quark mean energy loss per unit length from Drell-Yan data is constrained to −dE_q/dz ≃ 0.2 ± 0.15 GeV/fm (Arleo, 2002).
- The D̅D threshold used in the CEM: 2m_D = 3.74 GeV.
- The EKS parameterization is valid for Q² ≥ Q₀² = 2.25 GeV².
- For nuclear absorption alone in the CEM at HERA-B energy: α(x_F = −0.5) ≈ 0.97 and α(x_F > 0) ≈ 1 (color singlet); α(x_F = −0.5) ≈ 0.96 and α(x_F > 0) ≈ 0.95 (color octet).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear medium effects in quarkonium production, particularly the analyses of shadowing and energy loss by Eskola, Kolhinen, and Salgado (EKS, 1999) and by De Florian and Sassot (DS, 2004), as well as the energy-loss models of Baier et al. (BDMPS, 1997) and of Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (LQS, 1994). The paper compares its predictions with preliminary HERA-B data for J/Ψ production at negative x_F, a kinematic region not well constrained by earlier experiments such as E866/NuSea and NA3. The results indicate that current models overpredict suppression at negative x_F, motivating further measurements at RHIC to disentangle shadowing, energy loss, and nuclear absorption effects.
