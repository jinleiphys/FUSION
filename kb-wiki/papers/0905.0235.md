# Half-Skyrmion Hadronic Matter at High Density

> The hadronic matter described as a skyrmion matter embedded in an FCC crystal is found to turn into a half-skyrmion matter with vanishing (in the chiral limit) quark condensate and {\em non-vanishing} pion decay constant at a density $n_{1/2S}$ lower than or at the critical density $n_c^{χSR}$ at which hadronic matter changes over to a chiral symmetry restored phase with deconfined quarks. When hidden local gauge fields and dilaton scalars -- one "soft" and one "hard" -- are incorporated, this phase is characterized by $a=1$, $f_π\neq 0$ with the hidden gauge coupling $g\neq 0$ but $\ll 1$. While chiral symmetry is restored in this region in the sense that $\la\bar{q}q\ra=0$, quarks are still confined in massive hadrons and massless pions. This phase seems to correspond to the "quarkyonic phase" predicted in large $N_c$ QCD. It also represents the "hadronic freedom" regime relevant to kaon condensation at compact-star density. As $g\to 0$ (in the chiral limit), the symmetry "swells" -- as an emergent symmetry due to medium -- to $SU(N_f)^4$ as proposed by Georgi for the "vector limit." The fractionization of skyrmion matter into half-skyrmion matter is analogous to what appears to happen in condensed matter in (2+1) dimensions where half-skyrmions or "merons" enter as relevant degrees of freedom at the interface. Finally the transition from baryonic matter to color-flavor-locked quark matter can be bridged by a half-skyrmion matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at a density \(n_{1/2S}\) lower than or equal to the chiral restoration density \(n_c^{\chi SR}\), skyrmion matter in an FCC crystal undergoes a topology change into a half-skyrmion phase where the quark condensate vanishes (in the chiral limit) but the pion decay constant remains nonzero, with quarks still confined in massive hadrons and massless pions present.

## Method

The authors simulate dense hadronic matter by placing skyrmions on a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal lattice and squeezing the crystal to increase density, using a Skyrme-dilaton Lagrangian that incorporates a soft dilaton scalar associated with the trace anomaly of QCD. They then extend this to a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian that includes the lowest-lying vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\omega\)) and two dilatons (one soft, one hard), and minimize the energy density with respect to variational parameters in a Fourier expansion of the mean fields. The calculation is performed for two dilaton mass scenarios (light at 720 MeV and heavy at 3000 MeV) to study the phase structure.

## Key numbers

- The transition density \(n_{1/2S}\) at which skyrmions fractionize into half-skyrmions occurs at or below the chiral restoration density \(n_c^{\chi SR}\).
- For a heavy dilaton with mass \(m_\chi = 3000\) MeV, there is a distinctive half-skyrmion phase where \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle^* = 0\) but \(f_\pi^* \sim \langle \chi \rangle^* \neq 0\).
- For a light dilaton with mass \(m_\chi = 720\) MeV, the half-skyrmion phase shrinks to a point.
- The "flash temperature" in hot medium is given as \(T_{flash} \sim 120\) MeV, and the chiral transition temperature as \(T_{\chi SR} \sim 200\) MeV.
- Normal nuclear matter density is \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Kaon condensation is predicted at a density \(\sim 3 n_0\).

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach of Klebanov (1985) and the FCC skyrmion matter studies, and builds on the hidden local symmetry framework of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and the dilaton scaling ideas of Brown and Rho (1991). The half-skyrmion phase is identified with the "quarkyonic phase" predicted in large \(N_c\) QCD by McLerran and Pisarski (2007), and with the "hadronic freedom" regime relevant for kaon condensation in compact stars as discussed by Brown, Lee, and Rho (2007). The paper enables a unified description of the transition from baryonic matter to color-flavor-locked quark matter via a half-skyrmion bridge, and has implications for the maximum mass of neutron stars.
