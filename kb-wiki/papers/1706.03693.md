# Description of Charged Particle Pseudorapidity Distributions in Pb+Pb Collisions with Tsallis Thermodynamics

> The centrality dependence of pseudorapidity distributions for charged particles produced in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV and 200 GeV at RHIC, and in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at LHC are investigated in the fireball model, assuming that the rapidity axis is populated with fireballs following one distribution function. We assume that the particles in the fireball fulfill the Tsallis distribution. The theoretical results are compared with the experimental measurements and a good agreement is found. Using these results, the pseudorapidity distributions of charged particles produced in Pb+Pb central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV and 10 TeV are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Tsallis non-extensive thermodynamics, previously applied only to proton-proton collisions, can successfully describe charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions in heavy-ion collisions across a wide range of centralities and beam energies, but only after applying a transverse momentum cut at 7.5 GeV/c because a single Tsallis distribution fails to fit the full particle spectra in Pb+Pb collisions.

## Method

The authors model the rapidity axis as populated with fireballs following a q-Gaussian distribution function, where each fireball emits particles according to the Tsallis distribution. They first fit the Tsallis parameters (temperature T and entropic factor q) to experimental transverse momentum spectra with a pT cut at 7.5 GeV/c, then use those parameters together with fitted fireball distribution parameters (y0 and sigma) to compute the pseudorapidity distributions via numerical integration over pT and fireball rapidity.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, the pT cut is applied at 7.5 GeV/c to allow single Tsallis distribution fits to particle spectra
- The total charged particle multiplicity uncertainty is 4.5% at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 6.1% at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV in Pb+Pb central collisions
- Predictions are made for Pb+Pb central collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and 10 TeV, with 6% uncertainty added to the extrapolated total charged particle number
- The fireball distribution parameters y0 and sigma from Au+Au and Pb+Pb central collisions follow a linear relation with ln(sqrt(s_NN))

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive approach of Cleymans (2015) from proton-proton collisions to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (Au+Au at 130 and 200 GeV) and LHC (Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV). It provides an alternative to multi-source thermal models (Liu 2013, 2014) and hydrodynamic models (Jiang 2016) that also describe pseudorapidity distributions, and enables predictions for future LHC runs at 5.02 and 10 TeV. The paper notes that sophisticated models like HIJING, AMPT, EPOS-LHC, and UrQMD cannot successfully describe the measured distributions except the CGC-based model which only works near mid-pseudorapidity.
