# Spreading Width for Decay out of a Superdeformed Band

> The attenuation factor F responsible for the decay out of a superdeformed (SD) band is calculated with the help of a statistical model. This factor is given by 1/F = (1 + Gamma(down) / Gamma(S)). Here, Gamma(S) is the width for the collective E2 transition within the superdeformed band, and Gamma(down) is the spreading width which describes the mixing between a state in the SD band and the normally deformed (ND) states of equal spin. The attenuation factor F is independent of the statistical E1 decay widths Gamma(N) of the ND states provided that the Gamma(N) are much larger than both Gamma(down) and Gamma(S). This condition is generically met. Previously measured values of F are used to determine Gamma(down).

# Spreading Width for Decay out of a Superdeformed Band

## Key claim

The attenuation factor F for decay out of a superdeformed (SD) band is given by F = (1 + Gamma_down / Gamma_S)^{-1}, where Gamma_down is the spreading width for mixing between the SD state and normally deformed (ND) states, and this factor is independent of the statistical E1 decay widths of the ND states provided that those widths are much larger than both Gamma_down and Gamma_S, a condition the authors argue is generically met. This allows model-independent extraction of Gamma_down directly from measured intensity reductions, without performing a full calculation of the decay process.

## Method

The authors model the system with a Hamiltonian coupling a single SD state to a set of ND states described by the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) of random matrices, adding a diagonal width matrix containing the E2 width Gamma_S for the SD state and the statistical E1 width Gamma_N for the ND states. They calculate the ensemble-averaged E2 transition intensity feeding the next lower SD state using stochastic scattering theory, averaging over the GOE distribution of ND energies and coupling matrix elements. The key step is showing that for Gamma_N much larger than Gamma_S and Gamma_down, the fluctuating part of the scattering matrix can be neglected, yielding the simple attenuation factor F.

## Key numbers

- For 192Hg at spin 12: Gamma_down = 0.18 x 10^{-4} eV, Gamma_S = 1.16 x 10^{-4} eV, D = 34 eV, Gamma_N = 103 x 10^{-4} eV
- For 192Hg at spin 10: Gamma_down = 5.44 x 10^{-4} eV (estimated lifetime), Gamma_S = 0.54 x 10^{-4} eV, D = 30 eV, Gamma_N = 103 x 10^{-4} eV
- For 194Hg at spin 12: Gamma_down = 0.97 x 10^{-4} eV, Gamma_S = 1.44 x 10^{-4} eV, D = 92 eV, Gamma_N = 181 x 10^{-4} eV
- For 194Hg at spin 10: Gamma_down >= 8.9 x 10^{-4} eV (estimated lifetime), Gamma_S >= 0.47 x 10^{-4} eV, D = 79 eV, Gamma_N = 184 x 10^{-4} eV
- For 194Pb at spin 10: Gamma_down = 0.11 x 10^{-4} eV, Gamma_S = 0.66 x 10^{-4} eV, D = 1699 eV, Gamma_N = 16 x 10^{-4} eV
- For 194Pb at spin 8: Gamma_down = 0.09 x 10^{-4} eV (estimated lifetime), Gamma_S = 0.28 x 10^{-4} eV, D = 1549 eV, Gamma_N = 17 x 10^{-4} eV
- The spreading width is extracted via Gamma_down = hbar (1 - F_exp) / tau, where F_exp is the measured intensity reduction and tau is the lifetime of the decaying SD state
- The uncertainty in Gamma_down from statistical fluctuations is estimated at a factor of 2 or 3

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Schiffer, Herskind, and Gascon (1989) and the calculations of Vigezzi, Broglia, and Dossing (1990) and Shimizu et al. (1992, 1993) for decay out of superdeformed bands, but differs by deriving a direct, parameter-free relation between the measured attenuation factor and the spreading width. The authors apply their result to experimental data from Kruecken et al. (1994, 1996, 1997) and Kuehn et al. (1997) for 192Hg, 194Hg, and 194Pb. The extracted spreading widths provide direct information on the barrier separating the first and second minima in the nuclear potential, analogous to the doorway-state treatment of isobaric analog resonances.
