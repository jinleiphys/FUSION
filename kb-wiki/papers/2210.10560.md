# Dilepton production in the photodisintegration of the deuteron

> We study the lepton pair production in the photodisintegration of the deuteron process. The complete seven-fold differential cross section is calculated via the Bethe-Heitler mechanism with final state interactions taken into account. The deuteron bound state is described by a relativistic covariant deuteron-nucleon vertex. With numerical results, we find that the differential cross section has strong dependence on the lepton azimuthal angle in the small polar angle region and sharp peaks appear in the dependence on the invariant mass of the produced lepton pair or the two nucleons in the final state. We demonstrate that such nearly singular feature originates from the collinearity between the produced lepton or antilepton and the incident photon, and it is physically regularized by the lepton mass in our calculation. The final state interaction between the knocked-out nucleon and the recoil nucleon redistributes the differential cross section over the missing momentum, with a significant enhancement at large missing momentum and a suppression in the intermediate region. With a further decomposition of the final state interaction contribution, it is found that the on-shell term dominates the near quasi-elastic region while the off-shell term dominates the other end. In addition, we examine the contribution from the interference between the proton amplitude and the neutron amplitude, which as expected is found negligible even if the proton-neutron rescattering is included. The result in this work can serve as an input for the analysis and background estimation of multiple exclusive measurements at Jefferson Lab and future electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Heitler mechanism for dilepton production in deuteron photodisintegration exhibits nearly singular features in the differential cross section that are physically regularized by the lepton mass, and the final state interaction between the knocked-out nucleon and the recoil nucleon redistributes the cross section over missing momentum with a significant enhancement at large missing momentum and suppression in the intermediate region.

## Method

The complete seven-fold differential cross section was calculated via the Bethe-Heitler mechanism using a relativistic covariant deuteron-nucleon vertex within the covariant spectator theory. The final state interaction was included via nucleon-nucleon rescattering parametrized in Lorentz covariant form with all spin-dependent contributions, using phase shift analysis from the SAID program. The amplitude was decomposed into on-shell and off-shell positive energy contributions, with the negative energy contribution neglected.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy set at 8.5 GeV, just above the J/psi production threshold from free nucleons and accessible at Jefferson Lab
- For the angular distribution study: s_ll = 9.6 GeV^2 (mass square of J/psi), s_pn = 5 GeV^2, t = -1.5 GeV^2, p_1 = 1.4 GeV, phi_1 = 180 degrees
- The differential cross section for e+e- production sharply peaks around s_ll = 9.15 GeV^2, where the electron is collinear to the incident photon
- The off-shell form factor parameter Lambda_N = 1.2 GeV was used for the nucleon-nucleon scattering

## Context

This work extends the study of Bethe-Heitler processes on deuteron targets, which are the dominant background for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) measurements as discussed by Berger (2001) and Boer (2015). The covariant spectator theory approach follows the formalism of Gross (1972, 1991, 2010) and Jeschonnek (2008), and the results can serve as input for background estimation at Jefferson Lab and future electron-ion colliders. The calculation enables more precise extraction of generalized parton distributions and J/psi production cross sections near threshold.
