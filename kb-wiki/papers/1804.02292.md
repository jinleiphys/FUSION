# Study of hot thermally fissile nuclei using relativistic mean field theory

> We have studied the properties of hot $^{234,236}$U and $^{240}$Pu nuclei in the framework of relativistic mean field formalism. The recently developed FSUGarnet and IOPB-I parameter sets are implemented for the first time to deform nuclei at finite temperature. The results are compared with the well-known NL3 set. The said isotopes are structurally important because of the thermally fissile nature of $^{233,235}$U and $^{239}$Pu as these nuclei ($^{234,236}$U and $^{240}$Pu) are formed after the absorption of a thermal neutron, which undergoes fission. Here, we have evaluated the nuclear properties, such as shell correction energy, neutron-skin thickness, quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation parameters and asymmetry energy coefficient for these nuclei as a function of temperature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the recently developed FSUGarnet and IOPB-I relativistic mean field parameter sets, applied for the first time to deformed nuclei at finite temperature, reproduce ground-state properties of thermally fissile nuclei comparably to the well-established NL3 set, but the authors note that the NL3 set systematically overestimates excitation energy and predicts different shell melting behavior compared to the newer parameter sets.

## Method

The authors performed temperature-dependent relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the effective field theory motivated RMF formalism with three parameter sets (FSUGarnet, IOPB-I, and NL3) for the nuclei 208Pb, 234U, 236U, and 240Pu. The coupled Dirac and meson field equations were solved in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with BCS pairing, where temperature was introduced through the occupation numbers via the Fermi-Dirac distribution for quasiparticle energies. Nuclear properties including binding energy, excitation energy, shell correction energy, neutron-skin thickness, quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation parameters, and asymmetry energy coefficient were evaluated as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy per nucleon for 234U: 7.60 MeV (FSUGarnet and NL3), 7.61 MeV (IOPB-I), compared to experimental 7.60 MeV
- Ground-state binding energy per nucleon for 240Pu: 7.56 MeV (FSUGarnet), 7.57 MeV (IOPB-I), 7.55 MeV (NL3), compared to experimental 7.56 MeV
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 for 234U: 0.20 (FSUGarnet and IOPB-I), 0.24 (NL3), compared to experimental 0.27
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 for 240Pu: 0.24 (FSUGarnet), 0.25 (IOPB-I), 0.27 (NL3), compared to experimental 0.29
- Charge radius for 234U: 5.84 fm (FSUGarnet and NL3), 5.88 fm (IOPB-I), compared to experimental 5.83 fm
- Shell correction energies extracted from S² versus E* plots: approximately -17.79 MeV for 208Pb, -7.57 MeV for 234U, -8.58 MeV for 236U, and -10.08 MeV for 240Pu (values read from figure intercepts)
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K∞: 271.38 MeV (NL3), 229.5 MeV (FSUGarnet), 222.65 MeV (IOPB-I)

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature RMF studies by applying the FSUGarnet and IOPB-I parameter sets, originally fitted to nuclear matter and neutron star properties, to deformed actinide nuclei for the first time. The study is motivated by the thermally fissile nature of 233U, 235U, and 239Pu, whose compound nuclei (234U, 236U, and 240Pu) are formed after thermal neutron absorption; the authors build on prior fission studies by Bharat (2017) and Seinthil (2017) that showed fission fragment yields depend on temperature. The results enable future microscopic calculations of fission dynamics for these reactor-relevant nuclei, particularly the temperature dependence of shell effects and the equation of state parameters that connect to neutron star physics.
