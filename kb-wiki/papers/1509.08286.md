# Holographic entropy and real-time dynamics of quarkonium dissociation in non-Abelian plasma

> The peak of the heavy quark pair entropy at the deconfinement transition, observed in lattice QCD, suggests that the transition is effectively driven by the increase of the entropy of bound states. The growth of the entropy with the inter-quark distance leads to the emergent entropic force that induces dissociation of quarkonium states. Since the quark-gluon plasma around the transition point is a strongly coupled system, we use the gauge-gravity duality to study the entropy of heavy quarkonium and the real-time dynamics of its dissociation. In particular, we employ the Improved Holographic QCD model as a dual description of large $N_c$ Yang Mills theory. Studying the dynamics of the fundamental string between the quarks placed on the boundary, we find that the entropy peaks at the transition point. We also study the real-time dynamics of the system by considering the holographic string falling in the black hole horizon where it equilibrates. In the vicinity the deconfinement transition, the dissociation time is found to be less than a fermi, suggesting that the entropic destruction is the dominant dissociation mechanism in this temperature region.

## Key claim

The peak of heavy quark-antiquark entropy at the deconfinement transition, previously observed in lattice QCD, is confirmed as a generic feature of Einstein-dilaton holographic models; however, the peak height in the Improved Holographic QCD (IHQCD) model is lower than the lattice result, and the high-temperature asymptotic behavior of the entropy differs from perturbative QCD, indicating that the classical Nambu-Goto action may need modification near the transition.

## Method

The authors use the IHQCD model, an Einstein-dilaton holographic dual of large \(N_c\) Yang-Mills theory, to compute the entropy of a heavy quark-antiquark pair by evaluating the on-shell Nambu-Goto action of a fundamental string stretched between the quarks in the deconfined (black hole) background. They also study real-time dissociation dynamics by solving the string equation of motion as the string falls from the boundary toward the black hole horizon, extracting the dissociation time from the trajectory.

## Key numbers

- The Nambu-Goto string tension is fixed as \(T_f \ell^2 = 6.5\), where \(\ell\) is the AdS radius, determined by matching to the lattice confining string tension.
- The five-dimensional Planck mass is fixed by the Stefan-Boltzmann law as \((M_p \ell)^3 = 1/(45 \pi^2)\).
- For temperatures \(T > 1.1 T_c\), the computed entropy of the heavy quark-antiquark pair agrees quantitatively with lattice QCD data from Kaczmarek (2005).
- The dissociation time of quarkonium near \(T = T_c\) is less than one fermi (1 fm), indicating a fast dissociation process.
- The string approaches the black hole horizon exponentially as \(r(t) - r_H \simeq e^{-4 \pi T t}\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic studies of Hashimoto (2014) by using the IHQCD model, which reproduces the hadron spectrum and thermodynamics of Yang-Mills theory, rather than simpler AdS backgrounds. The entropy peak at deconfinement supports the proposal by Kharzeev (2014) and Hashimoto (2014) that an emergent entropic force drives quarkonium dissociation. The fast dissociation time (less than 1 fm at \(T_c\)) suggests that entropic destruction is the dominant dissociation mechanism near the transition, and the results enable future studies of dissociation for finite quark-antiquark distances and comparisons with lattice QCD on the role of long strings.
