# Iterative Monte Carlo analysis of spin-dependent parton distributions

> We present a comprehensive new global QCD analysis of polarized inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, including the latest high-precision data on longitudinal and transverse polarization asymmetries from Jefferson Lab and elsewhere. The analysis is performed using a new iterative Monte Carlo fitting technique which generates stable fits to polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) with statistically rigorous uncertainties. Inclusion of the Jefferson Lab data leads to a reduction in the PDF errors for the valence and sea quarks, as well as in the gluon polarization uncertainty at $x \gtrsim 0.1$. The study also provides the first determination of the flavor-separated twist-3 PDFs and the $d_2$ moment of the nucleon within a global PDF analysis.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new global QCD analysis of polarized inclusive deep-inelastic scattering that uses an iterative Monte Carlo fitting technique to produce stable fits with statistically rigorous uncertainties, but the analysis is restricted to inclusive DIS data only, with a full analysis including semi-inclusive DIS and hadron production data deferred to a future publication.

## Method

The analysis uses an iterative Monte Carlo (IMC) fitting technique that transforms flat Monte Carlo priors into posteriors consistent with data, combined with data resampling and cross-validation, while performing all calculations in Mellin space for computational efficiency. The fit includes target mass corrections to all orders in M^2/Q^2, nuclear smearing corrections for deuteron and ^3He targets, and for the first time separates twist-3 parton distribution functions into individual u and d flavors within a global PDF analysis. The analysis uses measured A_parallel and A_perpendicular asymmetries directly rather than derived quantities, and includes new high-precision data from Jefferson Lab experiments eg1b, eg1-dvcs, and E06-014 on ^3He, along with the most recent A_1 measurements on the proton from COMPASS.

## Key numbers

- The analysis includes data down to Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 and W^2 = 3.5 GeV^2, almost doubling the number of DIS data points compared to analyses with more stringent cuts
- The gluon polarization uncertainty is reduced at x greater than or approximately equal to 0.1 due to inclusion of Jefferson Lab data
- The d_2 moment of the nucleon is defined as d_2(Q^2) = 2 * Mellin_g_1(3,Q^2) + 3 * Mellin_g_2(3,Q^2), with leading contributions arising at the twist-3 level
- The twist-3 evolution uses the large-N_c approximation with anomalous dimension given by 1/(11 - 2/3 N_f) * (psi(0,N) + gamma_E - 1/4 + 1/(2N))

## Context

This work extends the previous JAM13 analysis by the Jefferson Lab Angular Momentum Collaboration, which performed a first analysis of inclusive longitudinal and transverse polarization data down to low Q^2 and W^2 values. The new iterative Monte Carlo approach differs from standard PDF fitting methods used by groups such as DSSV (2009), LSS (2010), BB (2010), AAC (2009), and AKS (2013), which use single fits with Hessian or Lagrange multiplier error methods that can suffer from overfitting and multiple local minima. The study enables future work including semi-inclusive DIS and inclusive jet and pion production in polarized pp collisions, and provides the first flavor-separated determination of twist-3 PDFs and the d_2 moment within a global PDF analysis.
