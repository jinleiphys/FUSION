# Energy functional based on natural orbitals and occupancies for static properties of nuclei

> The possibility to use functionals of occupation numbers and natural orbitals for interacting fermions is discussed as an alternative to multi-reference energy density functional method. An illustration based on the two-level Lipkin model is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a functional of occupation numbers and natural orbitals (Density Matrix Functional Theory, DMFT) can serve as an alternative to multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) methods for describing static nuclear properties, but only illustrates this possibility for the schematic two-level Lipkin model, not for realistic nuclei.

## Method

The author constructs an energy functional for the two-level Lipkin model consisting of a mean-field part depending on natural orbitals and occupation numbers, plus a correlation energy term. The correlation functional is derived analytically for the N=2 case and then extended to larger particle numbers by introducing a semi-empirical scaling factor eta(N) = 1.5 N^{-2/3} to reproduce the expected N^{4/3} scaling behavior in the large N limit. The total energy is minimized with respect to the mixing angle alpha and occupation number n_0.

## Key numbers

* For the N=2 case, the correlation energy is given analytically as: E_Cor^(N=2)(alpha, n_0) = -2 V { sin^2(2alpha) n_0(1-n_0) + (sin^4(alpha) + cos^4(alpha)) sqrt(n_0(1-n_0)) }
* For N >= 3, the correlation energy uses a scaling factor eta(N) = c N^{-2/3} with c = 1.5, giving E_Cor^(N>=3) = eta(N) * [N(N-1)/2] * E_Cor^(N=2)
* The mean-field functional is: E_MF(alpha, n_0) = -(epsilon/2) N { cos(2alpha)(2n_0-1) + (chi/2) sin^2(2alpha)(2n_0-1)^2 }, where chi = V(N-1)/epsilon
* Results are shown for N = 5 to 20 particles as a function of chi = V(N-1)/epsilon

## Context

This work extends the Density Matrix Functional Theory originally proposed by Gilbert (1975) for electronic systems to nuclear physics, as an alternative to the multi-reference EDF approach that has encountered theoretical difficulties (Lacroix 2009, Bender 2009, Duguet 2009). The Lipkin model (Lipkin 1965) is used because Hartree-Fock fails for it while the Generator Coordinate Method (Rin80, Sev06) works well. The paper concludes that while DMFT shows promise for the schematic model, designing an accurate functional for realistic nuclei remains an open challenge.
