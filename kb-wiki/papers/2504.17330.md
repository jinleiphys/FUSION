# Exploring the giant monopole resonance in superheavy nuclei: A theoretical perspective

> Within the relativistic mean field framework, in an extended Thomas-Fermi approximation, we calculate the binding energy and charge distribution radius for the latest superheavy nuclei, synthesised in various laboratories, with atomic numbers $Z = 110-118$. The binding energy and radii are compared with the results obtained from relativistic Hartree calculations along with the experimental data, wherever available, to check the reliability of the methods. The calculations are extended to estimate the giant monopole resonances to understand the collective vibration of the nucleons for such superheavy nuclei. The giant monopole resonances obtained from scaling calculations are compared with the constraint computations. Furthermore, the results are compared with other known methods, such as the relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and time-dependent mean field calculations, along with some known lighter nuclei, specifically Zr isotopes (N = 42-86) and O isotopes (N = 10-36). Finally, the nuclear compressibility of the superheavy nuclei is predicted from the energy obtained in the breathing mode.

## Key claim

The relativistic extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) approximation, using the NL3 parameter set, can reliably predict the giant monopole resonance (GMR) energies and nuclear compressibility for superheavy nuclei (Z = 110–118), but the binding energies from RETF show a systematic overbinding of about 50 MeV (approximately 2%) compared to experimental data, which the authors attribute to the omission of shell structure in the semi-classical approach.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) approximation within the nonlinear σ–ω model (Boguta and Bodmer 1977) using the NL3 parameter set. They calculated ground-state binding energies and charge radii for superheavy isotopes with Z = 110–118, comparing results with relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) calculations and available experimental data. The giant monopole resonance (GMR) excitation energies were computed using both the scaling method (giving the upper bound ES) and the constrained method (giving the lower bound EC), from which the nuclear compressibility KA of finite nuclei was derived.

## Context

This work extends the study of isoscalar giant monopole resonances (ISGMR) into the superheavy region (Z = 110–118), which had not been previously explored with the RETF method. The authors compare their results with relativistic RPA calculations (Lalazissis 1997) and time-dependent mean-field calculations for lighter nuclei (O and Zr isotopes, following Biswal 2015). The NL3 parameter set was chosen because it reproduces a stiffer equation of state consistent with recent neutron star observations (GW190814) and gives good agreement with experimental data for stable and unstable nuclei. The calculations enable predictions of nuclear compressibility for superheavy nuclei, which can guide future experimental measurements of the breathing mode in this mass region.
