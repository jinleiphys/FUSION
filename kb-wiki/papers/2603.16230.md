# Lattice QCD at finite temperature and density

> I review recent lattice results on strongly interacting matter under extreme conditions, with emphasis on the finite-temperature QCD transition at $μ_B=0$, its approach toward the chiral limit and the fate of the $U_A(1)$ anomaly, as well as recent constraints on the QCD phase boundary and the possible critical endpoint at $μ_B>0$. I also discuss selected advances in lattice methods and in QCD thermodynamics under external conditions, in particular strong magnetic fields, isospin chemical potential, rotation, acceleration, and quark spin polarization.

# Lattice QCD at finite temperature and density

## Key claim
The finite-temperature QCD transition at zero baryon chemical potential is a smooth crossover with a pseudocritical temperature around 156 MeV from continuum-extrapolated staggered fermion calculations, but this picture requires cross-checking with chirally symmetric lattice fermions that treat chiral symmetry and the axial anomaly more faithfully at finite lattice spacing. At nonzero baryon chemical potential, recent lattice constraints exclude a critical endpoint for mu_B < 450 MeV at the 2-sigma level, though this bound depends on modeling assumptions and extrapolation from the temperature window directly constrained by lattice data.

## Method
The author reviews lattice QCD results for strongly interacting matter under extreme conditions, covering calculations using multiple fermion discretizations including staggered (HISQ, stout), Möbius domain-wall, overlap, and Wilson fermions. The review synthesizes results from simulations at zero and imaginary baryon chemical potential, employing Taylor expansion, analytic continuation, Lee-Yang edge singularity analysis, and constant-entropy constructions to circumvent the sign problem at real nonzero baryon density.

## Key numbers
- Pseudocritical temperature at physical quark masses and mu_B=0: T_pc = 156 MeV from continuum-extrapolated staggered fermions (HotQCD 2018, Borsanyi 2020)
- Pseudocritical temperature from Möbius domain-wall fermions at N_tau=8: T_pc = 158.7^{+2.6}_{-2.3} MeV (Gavai 2024, Bhattacharya 2014)
- Chiral-limit critical temperature in N_f=2 QCD from MDWF: T_c = 153 MeV (Aoki 2024)
- Chiral-limit critical temperature in N_f=2+1 QCD: T_c = 132-135 MeV (HotQCD 2019, Kotov 2021)
- Curvature of chiral critical line: kappa_2 = 0.015(1) at N_tau=8 (Ding 2024)
- Lee-Yang edge extrapolation for CEP: (T_CEP, mu_B,CEP) = (105^{+8}_{-18}, 422^{+80}_{-35}) MeV from Bielefeld-Parma on N_tau=6 (Clarke 2024)
- Constant-entropy CEP estimate: (T_CEP, mu_B,CEP) = (114.3 +/- 6.9, 602.1 +/- 62.1) MeV (Shah 2024)
- Lee-Yang-zero analysis best-fit CEP temperature: T_CEP = 55.8^{+6.3}_{-7.9} MeV, with 84% probability that T_CEP < 103 MeV or CEP does not exist (Adam 2025)
- Wuppertal-Budapest exclusion: no CEP for mu_B < 450 MeV at 2-sigma level (Borsanyi 2025)
- Phase boundary curvature from FASTSUM Wilson fermions at M_pi = 391 MeV: kappa = 0.0131(23)(23) (Smecca 2024)
- Phase boundary curvature from FASTSUM at M_pi = 239 MeV: kappa = 0.034(14) (Smecca 2024)
- Topological susceptibility nonzero up to T = 600 MeV in N_f=2+1+1 Wilson twisted-mass QCD (Kotov 2025)

## Context
This review extends the established lattice QCD picture of the finite-temperature transition at mu_B=0, originally established by Aoki (2006) as a crossover, and updates constraints on the QCD phase diagram at nonzero baryon density. The work builds on prior lattice calculations by HotQCD (2018, 2019), Borsanyi (2020), and the JLQCD collaboration, and provides context for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and the LHC. The results enable improved comparisons between first-principles QCD thermodynamics and experimental observables such as conserved-charge fluctuations from STAR BES-II, and constrain the search for a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
