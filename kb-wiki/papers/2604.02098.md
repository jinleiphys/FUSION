# Chiral-scale effective field theory for dense and thermal systems

> In this contribution, I will present some properties of nuclear matter (NM) by using the chiral-scale effective field theory that is anchored on the chiral, scale and hidden local flavor symmetries of QCD. We show that the sound velocity (SV) of the compact star matter can saturate the conformal limit, the SV exhibits a peak configuration in the intermediate density. To extend the chiral-scale effective field theory to both dense and tnermal systems, we setup a chiral-scale density counting (CSDC) rule and explore the contributions up to $\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})$.

## Key claim

The chiral-scale effective field theory predicts that the sound velocity of compact star matter can saturate the conformal limit after a topology change density \(n_{1/2}\), but this does not imply scale invariance because the trace of the energy-momentum tensor remains a nonzero density-independent constant that does not contribute to the sound velocity.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral-scale effective field theory Lagrangian that includes nucleons, pions as Nambu-Goldstone bosons of chiral symmetry breaking, the lightest scalar meson as the dilaton of scale symmetry breaking, and the lowest-lying vector mesons as gauge bosons of hidden local flavor symmetry. They apply a mean-field approximation to nuclear matter and introduce a chiral-scale density counting (CSDC) rule to systematically expand the Lagrangian up to \(\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})\) for application to both dense and thermal systems. The density dependence of medium-modified parameters is guided by the skyrmion crystal approach, where parameters first decrease with density and then become constant after a topology change density \(n_{1/2}\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(n_0 = 0.158\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})\), compared to empirical \(0.155 \pm 0.050\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Binding energy at saturation \(E(n_0) = -15.4\) MeV at \(\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})\), compared to empirical \(-15.0 \pm 1.0\) MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{\text{sym}}(n_0) = 32.2\) MeV at \(\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})\), compared to empirical \(30.9 \pm 1.9\) MeV
- Symmetry energy slope \(L(n_0) = 77.8\) MeV at \(\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})\), compared to empirical \(52.5 \pm 17.5\) MeV
- Incompressibility \(K(n_0) = 391\) MeV at \(\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})\), compared to empirical \(230 \pm 30\) MeV
- Liquid-gas phase transition critical temperature \(T_c = 22.5\) MeV at \(\mathcal{O}(k_c^{12})\), compared to empirical \(20.0 \pm 3.0\) MeV
- Topology change density constrained to \(2.0 n_0 \lesssim n_{1/2} \lesssim 4 n_0\) from nuclear physics and heavy-ion collision data
- Characteristic momentum for cores of massive neutron stars up to \(k_c \sim 700\) MeV \(\sim 10 n_0\)

## Context

This work extends the chiral-scale effective field theory approach systematized by Li (2016) and builds on the Brown-Rho scaling idea from Brown (1991) for medium-modified parameters. The pseudoconformal structure where sound velocity saturates the conformal limit at densities below perturbative QCD applicability contradicts the earlier belief from Tews (2018) that such behavior would prevent obtaining massive neutron stars around two solar masses, though similar results have since been observed in various models as noted by Kapusta (2021). The chiral-scale density counting rule enables systematic extension to thermal systems up to approximately 100 MeV, with future work planned for full parameter space scans, higher loop corrections, and inclusion of strange degrees of freedom.
