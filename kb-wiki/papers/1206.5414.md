# Eta-meson production in the resonance energy region

> We perform an updated coupled-channel analysis of eta-meson production including all recent photoproduction data on the proton. The dip observed in the differential cross sections at c.m. energies W=1.68 GeV is explained by destructive interference between the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1560)$ states. The effect from $P_{11}(1710)$ is found to be small but still important to reproduce the correct shape of the differential cross section. For the $π^- N \to ηN$ scattering we suggest a reaction mechanism in terms of the $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1560)$, and $P_{11}(1710)$ states. Our conclusion on the importance of the $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1560)$, and $P_{11}(1710)$ resonances in the eta-production reactions is in line with our previous results. No strong indication for a narrow state with a width of 15 MeV and the mass of 1680 MeV is found in the analysis. $ηN$ scattering length is extracted and discussed.

## Key claim

The dip observed in the differential cross section for eta-meson photoproduction on the proton at center-of-mass energy W=1.68 GeV is explained by destructive interference between the S11(1535) and S11(1650) resonances, with a small but important contribution from the P11(1710) state; no strong indication is found for a narrow exotic state with a width of 15 MeV and mass of 1680 MeV.

## Method

A coupled-channel partial-wave analysis was performed using the K-matrix approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, simultaneously fitting data for the (gamma/pi) p to eta p, pi N, 2pi N, omega N, and K Lambda channels. The interaction kernel included s-, u-, and t-channel contributions from tree-level Feynman diagrams, with resonance and background contributions generated from effective Lagrangians respecting chiral symmetry in the low-energy regime. Photoproduction reactions were treated perturbatively, neglecting the gamma N channel in the sum over intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- The dip in the differential cross section is observed at center-of-mass energy W = 1.68 GeV
- The S11(1535) resonance has a mass of 1526(2) MeV, total width 131(12) MeV, and eta N branching ratio of 58(4) percent
- The S11(1650) resonance has a pole width of 89 MeV, comparable to the GWU result of 80 MeV
- The P11(1710) resonance has a pole width of 159 MeV
- The eta N scattering length was extracted and discussed
- The g_eta NN coupling constant was found to be 0.31, consistent with previous results
- The cutoff parameter for spin-1/2 hadronic vertices is 3.0 GeV, for spin-3/2 hadronic vertices is 0.97 GeV, and for spin-5/2 hadronic vertices is 1.13 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel analysis (Shklyar, 2006) by including high-precision Crystal Ball data from MAMI (McNicoll, 2010) for gamma p to eta p, confirming that the dip structure can be explained by established resonances without invoking exotic states. The results contradict the interpretation of the structure at W=1.68 GeV as a narrow pentaquark-like state as suggested by Azimov (2005), and differ from the Bonn-Gatchina group's explanation (Anisovich, 2008) involving the S11(1650) state for the neutron channel. The analysis enables future studies of eta-meson production on the neutron and highlights the need for new measurements of the pi N to eta N reaction to further constrain the reaction mechanism.
