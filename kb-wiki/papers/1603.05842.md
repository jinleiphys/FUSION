# Highly-anisotropic hydrodynamics for central collisions

> The framework of leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics is supplemented with realistic equation of state and self-consistent freeze-out prescription. The model is applied to central proton-nucleus collisions. The results are compared to those obtained within standard Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics. It is shown that the resulting hadron spectra are highly-sensitive to the hydrodynamic approach that has been used.

## Key claim
Leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics, when supplemented with a realistic lattice QCD equation of state and a self-consistent freeze-out prescription, predicts significantly harder hadron transverse-momentum spectra for central p-Pb collisions compared to standard Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics, indicating that the pressure anisotropy is overestimated in the Israel-Stewart approach.

## Method
The authors solved the leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics equations for a boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric (1+1)-dimensional system, using an ellipsoidal form of the distribution function. They replaced the ideal-gas equation of state with lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration and applied the Cooper-Frye formula on an isothermal freeze-out hypersurface to obtain hadron spectra. The initial energy density profile was set using the standard optical Glauber model.

## Key numbers
- The transverse-momentum spectra of $\pi^0$ from central p-Pb collisions are compared between leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics (dashed blue lines) and Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics (solid black lines), showing that anisotropic hydrodynamics predicts much harder spectra.
- The logarithmic slope of the $\pi^0$ $p_T$-spectra is shown for both approaches, with the anisotropic hydrodynamics result (dashed blue lines) differing significantly from the viscous hydrodynamics result (solid black lines).
- The self-consistent inclusion of anisotropy corrections at freeze-out (dashed blue lines) leads to large corrections to the final spectra compared to the case where corrections are neglected (dashed-dotted green lines).

## Context
This work extends the leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics framework of Nopoush (2015) by applying it to central p-Pb collisions and comparing with standard Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics. It builds on prior studies showing that anisotropic hydrodynamics agrees well with exact solutions of relaxation-type kinetic theory for Bjorken flow (Florkowski 2013, 2014) and Gubser flow (Denicol 2014, Nopoush 2014). The results demonstrate that for small systems like p-Pb, off-equilibrium corrections at freeze-out are significant and must be included properly, enabling more accurate descriptions of matter evolution in asymmetric heavy-ion reactions.
