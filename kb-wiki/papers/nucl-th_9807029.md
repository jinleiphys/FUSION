# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov description of the deformed N = 28 region

> Ground-state properties of neutron-rich $N \approx 28$ nuclei are described in the framework of Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory. The model uses the NL3 effective interaction in the mean-field Lagrangian, and describes pairing correlations by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. Two-neutron separation energies and ground-state quadrupole deformations that result from fully self-consistent RHB solutions, are compared with available experimental data. The model predicts a strong suppression of the spherical N=28 shell gap for neutron-rich nuclei: the $1f7/2 \to fp$ core breaking results in deformed ground states. Shape coexistence is expected for neutron-rich Si, S and Ar isotopes.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with finite-range pairing interaction predicts a strong suppression of the spherical N=28 shell gap for neutron-rich nuclei with Z ≤ 18, leading to deformed ground states; however, this suppression is conditional on the proton number, as the spherical gap remains approximately 4 MeV for the Z=18 isotone ⁴⁶Ar and the N=28 nuclei become spherical starting from ⁴⁸Ca (Z=20).

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations self-consistently by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential, using 12 oscillator shells for fermion wave functions and 20 for meson fields. The NL3 effective interaction was used for the mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations were described by the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the pairing channel. Constrained RHB calculations with a quadratic constraint on the quadrupole moment were performed to construct binding energy curves as a function of deformation.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁴S, the RHB model predicts a prolate ground state with quadrupole deformation β₂ = 0.31, in agreement with experimental data giving |β₂| = 0.258(36)
- For ⁴⁶Ar, the ground-state oblate minimum deformation agrees with experimental data giving |β₂| = 0.176(17)
- For ⁴²Si, the binding energy displays a deep oblate minimum at β₂ ≈ -0.4, with a prolate minimum at approximately 1.5 MeV excitation energy
- For ⁴⁴S, the oblate minimum is found approximately 200 keV above the prolate ground state
- The spherical N=28 gap for the Z=18 isotone ⁴⁶Ar is approximately 4 MeV
- Average neutron pairing gaps ⟨Δ_N⟩ ≈ 2 MeV are calculated for open-shell nuclei in this region
- For ⁴⁴S, the neutron canonical pairing gaps are around 2.2 MeV in the volume, with an average value at the Fermi surface around 1.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the N≈28 region by Werner et al. (1994, 1996) using Skyrme Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean-field (RMF) with BCS pairing, and by Lalazissis et al. (1998) using RMF with BCS and the NL-SH interaction. The key advance is the first application of fully self-consistent RHB theory with finite-range pairing to deformed nuclei, which provides a more realistic description of pairing correlations than the constant-gap BCS approximation used in prior work. The results enable future studies of shape coexistence and shell quenching in neutron-rich nuclei, and the comparison between RHB and RMF+BCS demonstrates that the treatment of pairing significantly affects predictions for radii and deformation in γ-soft nuclei.
