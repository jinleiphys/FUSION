# Nucleon and Pion Form Factors in Different Forms of Relativistic Quantum Mechanics

> Calculations of form factors in different forms of relativistic kinematics are presented. They involve the instant, front and point forms. In the two first cases, different kinematical conditions are considered while in the latter case, both a Dirac-inspired approach and a hyperplane-based one are incorporated in our study. Numerical results are presented for the pion form factors with emphasis on both the low and high Q**2 range. A new argument is presented, explaining why some approaches do considerably much better than other ones whenonly a single-particle current is considered.

## Key claim

Calculations of the pion charge form factor using a single-particle current in different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) fall into "good" and "bad" sets, with the discrepancy reaching huge factors; the success of the point-form description of nucleon form factors is mostly due to a factor that a more complete calculation is expected to remove, recovering a situation similar to the standard front-form calculation.

## Method

The author calculates form factors for a two-body pion system using instant, front, and point forms of relativistic kinematics, employing a mass operator with a confining potential (string tension 1 GeV/fm) and a gluon exchange interaction (strength alpha_s = 0.35). For the instant and front forms, different kinematical conditions are considered including the standard Breit frame and q^+ = 0 configurations, as well as a parallel kinematics limit where both coincide. For the point form, both a Dirac-inspired approach and a hyperplane-based one are incorporated, and two-body currents (pair-type term) are calculated in the instant form to test the QCD asymptotic behavior.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass: m_q = 0.3 GeV; pion mass: M_pi = 0.14 GeV
- String tension: sigma_{s.t.} = 1 GeV/fm; gluon exchange strength: alpha_s = 0.35
- The charge form factor in the "bad" approaches scales with momentum transfer Q through the quantity (2*bar{e_k}/M_pi)*Q, giving a charge radius that scales like the inverse of the pion mass
- At high Q^2, the charge form factor in the single-particle approximation shows a Q^{-4} asymptotic behavior, while the scalar form factor F_0(Q^2) shows the QCD-expected Q^{-2} asymptotic behavior
- The two-body current contribution in the instant form provides the right Q^{-2} asymptotic behavior for the charge form factor

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on the spinless case (Desplanques 2004) and is motivated by the successful description of nucleon form factors in the point-form approach (Wagenbrunn 2000) while a standard front-form calculation (Cardarelli 1995) fails under the same conditions. The paper argues that Poincare space-time translation invariance, rather than Lorentz invariance, is the relevant criterion for evaluating RQM approaches, and that the difficulty in reconciling point-form descriptions of nucleon and pion form factors vanishes when this is properly accounted for.
