# Cross sections for neutron-induced reactions from surrogate data: revisiting the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation for $(n,n^{\prime})$ and $(n,2n)$ reactions

> Earlier work has demonstrated that cross sections for neutron-induced fission and radiative neutron capture can be determined from a combination of surrogate reaction data and theory. For the fission case, it was shown that Weisskopf-Ewing approximation, which significantly simplifies the implementation of the surrogate method, can be employed. Capture cross sections cannot be obtained, and require a detailed description of the surrogate reaction process. In this paper we examine the validity of the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation for determining unknown $(n,n^{\prime})$ and $(n,2n)$ cross sections from surrogate data. We find that peak cross sections can be estimated using the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation, but the shape of the $(n,n^{\prime})$ and $(n,2n)$ cross sections, especially for low neutron energies, cannot be reliably determined without accounting for the angular-momentum differences between the neutron-induced and surrogate reaction. To obtain reliable $(n,n^{\prime})$ and $(n,2n)$ cross sections from surrogate reaction data, a detailed description of the surrogate reaction mechanisms is required. To do so for the compound-nucleus energies and decay channels relevant to these reactions, it becomes necessary to extend current modeling capabilities.

## Key claim

Peak cross sections for (n,n') and (n,2n) reactions can be estimated using the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation from surrogate data, but the shape of these cross sections, particularly at low neutron energies, cannot be reliably determined without accounting for angular-momentum differences between the neutron-induced and surrogate reactions.

## Method

The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach statistical reaction calculations with realistic parametrizations for n+⁹⁰Zr, n+¹⁵⁷Gd, and n+²³⁸U as representative spherical and deformed nuclei. They first extracted the Jπ-dependent decay probabilities G_χ^CN(E_ex,Jπ) for one- and two-neutron emission from well-calibrated calculations, then simulated surrogate measurements by multiplying these decay probabilities with schematic spin-parity distributions F_δ^CN(E_ex,Jπ) to assess the impact of applying the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation in situations where its assumptions are not strictly valid.

## Key numbers

* For n+⁹⁰Zr at E_n ≈ 1 MeV, p-wave capture dominates and produces a distribution favoring negative-parity states within a narrow range of spins
* For (n,n') reactions, excitation energies between approximately 10 and 20 MeV are relevant; for (n,2n) reactions, E_ex = 20-30 MeV are important
* The neutron separation energy S_n(⁹¹Zr) = 7.195 MeV marks the energy region of interest for neutron capture
* For the ⁹²Zr(p,d) surrogate experiment with E_p = 28.5 MeV, the Jπ distribution was found to vary little over several MeV around E_ex = S_n(⁹¹Zr) = 7.195 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier surrogate method studies that demonstrated the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation works for (n,f) reactions (Escher and Dietrich, 2006) but fails for (n,γ) reactions (Escher and Dietrich, 2010; Chiba, 2010). The paper investigates the intermediate case of (n,n') and (n,2n) reactions, finding that peak cross sections can be estimated but full Hauser-Feshbach modeling is needed for reliable shapes. This enables future surrogate experiments to determine multiple neutron-induced cross sections simultaneously, as illustrated by the ⁹⁰Zr benchmark experiment (Semkova, 2010), but requires extending current modeling capabilities to handle compound-nucleus energies and decay channels relevant to these reactions.
