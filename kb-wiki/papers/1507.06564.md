# Forward Heavy Quarkonium Productions at the LHC

> We investigate the low transverse momentum heavy quarkonium ($J/ψ$, $Υ$) productions in the forward rapidity region of $pp$ and $pA$ collisions at the LHC as an important probe to the transverse momentum tomography of the gluons in hadrons in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework. By implementing the Sudakov resummation consistently in the CGC formalism, we achieve an excellent agreement between the improved CGC calculations and the LHC data. We show that both the small-$x$ and the Sudakov effects are essential for a complete description of heavy quarkonium productions in the low transverse momentum region. This provides a solid foundation to study the small-$x$ gluon saturation in a big nucleus with the future $pA$ programs at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that implementing Sudakov resummation consistently within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism is essential to describe the transverse momentum broadening of heavy quarkonium (especially Upsilon) production at the LHC, resolving a puzzle where earlier CGC calculations without Sudakov effects underestimated the mean transverse momentum of Upsilon relative to J/psi.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for forward heavy quarkonium (J/psi and Upsilon) production in pp and pA collisions at LHC energies using the CGC framework with the color evaporation model. They implement Sudakov resummation by introducing a Sudakov factor in momentum space, which adds double logarithms of the form alpha_s C_A ln^2(M^2/p_perp^2) to the standard CGC calculation that includes small-x evolution via the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation. The Sudakov factor is separated into perturbative and non-perturbative parts following the Collins-Soper-Sterman formalism, with non-perturbative parameters taken from fits to color octet quarkonium production.

## Key numbers

- For J/psi production at sqrt(s)=7 TeV in pp collisions, the normalization factor F_J/psi is 0.0975 without Sudakov and 0.1495 with Sudakov resummation
- For Upsilon(1S) production at sqrt(s)=7 TeV in pp collisions, the normalization factor F_Upsilon(1S) is 0.488 without Sudakov and 0.390 with Sudakov resummation
- The peak of the Upsilon p_perp spectrum shifts from around 1 GeV (without Sudakov) to around 3 GeV (with Sudakov) at sqrt(s)=7 TeV
- For J/psi production, the Sudakov effect is negligible because the saturation momentum Q_s ~ 1-2 GeV is not much smaller than the J/psi mass
- For Upsilon production, the Sudakov logarithms are important because the bottom quark mass is around 4 GeV
- In pPb collisions, the initial saturation scale for the nucleus is set as Q_sA,0^2 = 3 Q_s,0^2 with c=0.5 in the relation Q_sA,0^2 = c A^(1/3) Q_s,0^2
- The non-perturbative Sudakov parameters are g1=0.03, g2=0.87, and g1*g3=-0.17 with Q0=1.6 GeV and b_max=0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier CGC calculations of forward J/psi production by Fujii et al. (2013) and Ducloue et al. (2015) that provided decent descriptions of J/psi data but failed to reproduce the larger mean transverse momentum observed for Upsilon by the LHCb collaboration. The authors resolve this puzzle by implementing Sudakov resummation, following the theoretical framework developed by Mueller et al. (2012, 2013) and Qiu et al. (2013) for combining Sudakov and small-x resummation in the CGC hybrid formalism. This enables future quantitative studies of gluon saturation in the LHC pA program and provides a foundation for extracting k_perp-dependent gluon distributions in protons and nuclei.
