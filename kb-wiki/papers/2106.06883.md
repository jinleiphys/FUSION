# Axial meson contribution to proton form factors

> The effect of axial meson exchange on extraction of proton electromagnetic form factors in $ep$ scattering is calculated. Taking as an example $f_1(1285)$ meson exchange, it is quantitatively shown how it affects Rosenbluth and polarization transfer methods. The contribution of an axial meson is negligible in comparison with the traditional box-type two photon exchange and could not be responsible for difference of form factors extracted by different techniques. However, results support the idea that meson exchange could explain deviation of individual polarizations.

# Axial meson contribution to proton form factors

## Key claim

The contribution of axial meson (specifically $f_1(1285)$) exchange to proton electromagnetic form factors extracted from $ep$ scattering is negligible compared to traditional box-type two-photon exchange and cannot explain the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer methods; however, the results support the idea that meson exchange could explain the deviation of individual polarizations (specifically longitudinal recoil polarization $P_L$) from one-photon-exchange predictions.

## Method

The authors calculated the interference amplitude between one-photon exchange and axial meson exchange in elastic electron-proton scattering, using the Breit frame formalism. They derived analytic expressions for the corrections to the unpolarized cross section (Rosenbluth method) and to the polarization transfer observables ($P_x$, $P_z$, and the ratio $R = \mu_p G_E/G_M$). The effective electron-meson coupling $g_{eA}(Q^2)$ was computed via a triangle loop diagram using the $f_1(1285)$ meson as an example, with form factors parameterized from L3 Collaboration data and the nonrelativistic quark model relation $F^{(0)} = -F^{(1)}$. The proton-meson coupling $g_{pA}$ was taken from the literature estimate of Kirchbach (1995) as $g_{f_1 N} = -(14.6 \div 17.2)$.

## Key numbers

- The effective coupling $F^{(0)}_{f_1(1285)}(M_{f_1}^2,0,0) = (0.266 \pm 0.043)~\text{GeV}^{-2}$ at the meson mass shell, fixed from L3 data using the nonrelativistic quark model
- The slope parameter for the $f_1(1285)$ transition form factor is $\Lambda_{f_1(1285)} = 1.04$~GeV
- The correction to the OPE cross section from $f_1(1285)$ exchange is of order $0.1\%$, with maximum at $Q^2 = 3~\text{GeV}^2$
- The correction to the ratio $R = \mu_p G_E/G_M$ from polarization transfer is of order $0.05\%$
- The proton-meson coupling is $g_{f_1 N} = -(14.6 \div 17.2)$ from Kirchbach (1995)

## Context

This work extends the study of meson exchange contributions to proton form factors, following earlier proposals by Drell and Sullivan (1965) for axial-vector mesons in hyperfine splitting and more recent calculations by Dorokhov et al. (2017) for muonic hydrogen spectroscopy. It contrasts with the standard box-type two-photon exchange calculations of Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2005), which give corrections of a few percent but fail to describe polarization observables such as the longitudinal recoil polarization $P_L$ measured by the GEp-2gamma Collaboration (Meziane, 2010) and Puckett et al. (2017). The results suggest that while axial meson exchange alone cannot resolve the Rosenbluth-polarization transfer discrepancy, the sum of contributions from multiple mesons (including $f_1(1420)$ and $a_1(1260)$) could potentially explain the observed deviation in individual polarizations, though a more detailed analysis is required.
