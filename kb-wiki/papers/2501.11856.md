# Nucleon electric dipole form factor in QCD vacuum

> In the QCD vacuum, the nucleon form factors receive contributions from the underlying ensemble of topological pseudoparticles, which are sensitive to a finite vacuum angle $θ$. We use this observation to derive a novel relationship between the Pauli and electric dipole form factors, for light quark flavors. This relationship allows for an explicit derivation of the proton and neutron electric dipole moments induced by a small CP violating $θ$ angle, in terms of the vacuum topological susceptibity times pertinent magnetic moments. The results compare well with some recent lattice estimates.

## Key claim

The authors derive a novel relationship between the CP-even Pauli form factor and the CP-odd electric dipole form factor for light quarks in the QCD instanton liquid model (ILM), showing that the nucleon electric dipole moment induced by a small CP-violating vacuum angle θ is proportional to the topological susceptibility times the nucleon magnetic moment, with results that "compare well with some recent lattice estimates" but are subject to corrections from omitted instanton-anti-instanton molecules and the quark-diquark approximation used for the nucleon.

## Method

The authors model the QCD vacuum as an ensemble of instantons and anti-instantons (pseudoparticles) with density n_{I+A} = 1 fm^{-4} and average size ρ = 0.313 fm, and compute the electromagnetic vertex for a light quark scattering off a single pseudoparticle using zero-mode and non-zero-mode propagators. They average the resulting form factors over the pseudoparticle number distributions, using the topological susceptibility χ_t to capture the variance of the topological charge, and relate the CP-odd form factor F_3 to the Pauli form factor F_2 through the ratio χ_t / n_{I+A}. The nucleon is approximated as a quark-scalar diquark compound, and the nucleon electric dipole moments are obtained by combining the derived relation with empirical magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Instanton plus anti-instanton density: n_{I+A} = 1 fm^{-4}
- Average instanton size: ρ = 0.313 fm
- Topological susceptibility in the ILM: χ_t = (73.19 MeV)^4
- Determinantal mass for light quarks: m* = 103.6 MeV (with current mass m = 6 MeV)
- Light chiral condensate in ILM: |⟨ψ̄ψ⟩| = (223.5 MeV)^3
- Neutron electric dipole moment in ILM: d_n = -3.81 × 10^{-3} eθ·fm
- Proton electric dipole moment in ILM: d_p = 3.57 × 10^{-3} eθ·fm
- Ratio |d_n/d_p| in ILM: 1.067

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model approach previously applied to the Pauli form factor by Kochelev (2003) and to the neutron electric dipole moment by Faccioli et al. (2004), who obtained |d_n| = (6-14) × 10^{-3} eθ·fm using a numerical ensemble of pseudoparticles. The derived relationship between F_2 and F_3 provides a "model-independent" connection that the authors suggest should prove useful for more accurate lattice estimates of the nucleon dipole form factor, comparing their results to recent lattice calculations from the χQCD collaboration (Liang et al., 2023) and the ETMC collaboration (Alexandrou et al., 2020), as well as chiral perturbation theory calculations by Mereghetti et al. (2010).
