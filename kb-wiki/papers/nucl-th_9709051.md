# Landau Ginzburg Theory and Nuclear Matter at Finite Temperature

> Based on recent studies of the temperature dependence of the energy and specific heat of liquid nuclear matter, a phase transition is suggested at a temperature $\sim .8$ MeV. We apply Landau Ginzburg theory to this transition and determine the behaviour of the energy and specific heat close to the critical temperature in the condensed phase.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that liquid nuclear matter undergoes a second-order phase transition at a critical temperature of approximately 0.8 MeV, and that Landau Ginzburg theory can describe the thermodynamic properties of the condensed phase near this temperature, with the caveat that sharp features in the energy and specific heat will appear smoothed out when extracted from finite nuclei data.

## Method

The authors apply Landau Ginzburg theory to model the phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter, using a quadratic temperature dependence for the energy per nucleon in the uncondensed phase derived from a Fermi gas approximation. They determine the free energy in the condensed phase by expanding in powers of an order parameter (related to the pairing gap) to fourth order, and extract the temperature dependence of the energy per nucleon and specific heat near the critical temperature. The uncondensed phase parameters are taken from fits to the finite-temperature semi-empirical mass formula of Davidson et al. (1993), and the normal phase is modeled as a Fermi gas of quasiparticles with an effective mass of approximately twice the free nucleon mass, following an analogy with liquid helium-3.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the phase transition: approximately 0.8 MeV.
- Effective mass of quasiparticles in the normal phase: approximately 2 times the free nucleon mass (m* ~ 2 m), deduced from the specific heat data of Davidson et al. (1993).
- Quadratic coefficient for the uncondensed phase energy per nucleon: k2 = 1/6.7 MeV^-1, with a2 = -16 MeV.
- Discontinuity in specific heat at the transition: approximately 0.6 MeV, using the relation ΔcV ≈ 2.43 cV and assuming Tc ~ 0.8 MeV.
- Estimated transition temperature from Fermi liquid to Fermi gas: approximately 10 MeV, compared to 15-20 MeV in Jaqaman et al. (1983-1984) and approximately 5 MeV for finite nuclei in Pochodzalla et al. (1995).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature studies of nuclear matter by Davidson et al. (1993), who observed a structure in the energy and specific heat of symmetric nuclear matter between 0.5 and 1.3 MeV using a finite-temperature semi-empirical mass formula. The paper applies Landau Ginzburg theory, previously used for liquid helium and superconductors, to provide a theoretical framework for this proposed pairing transition, and draws an analogy with normal liquid helium-3 to justify a larger effective mass in the normal phase. The results suggest that the stiffer energy per nucleon above 1.3 MeV, compared to a free Fermi gas, could affect astrophysical calculations such as neutrino production rates in stars, which currently use the free-nucleon Fermi gas model of Lattimer and Ravenhall (1978).
