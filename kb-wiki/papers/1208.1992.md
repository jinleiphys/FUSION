# Nuclear structure and double beta decay

> Study of the neutrinoless double beta decay, $0νββ$, includes a variety of problems of nuclear structure theory. They are reviewed here. The problems range from the mechanism of the decay, i.e. exchange of the light Majorana neutrino neutrino versus the exchange of some heavy, so far unobserved particle. Next, the proper expressions for the corresponding operator are described that should include the effects of the nucleon size and of the recoil order terms in the hadronic current. The issue of proper treatment of the short range correlations, in particular for the case of the heavy particle exchange, is discussed also. The variety of methods employed these days in the theoretical evaluation of the nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ is briefly described and the difficulties causing the spread and hence uncertainty in the values of $M^{0ν}$ are discussed. Finally, the issue of the axial current quenching, and of the resonance enhancement in the case of double electron capture are described.

## Key claim

The paper reviews nuclear structure theory problems relevant to neutrinoless double beta decay, emphasizing that the relationship between the decay rate and the effective Majorana neutrino mass is "to some extent problematic" because heavy particle exchange mechanisms at the TeV scale can produce comparable decay rates without involving light Majorana neutrino exchange.

## Method

The review examines the theoretical framework for both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double beta decay modes, deriving the neutrino potential from the weak interaction Hamiltonian and integrating over virtual neutrino momentum. It describes the operator formalism including finite nucleon size corrections via dipole form factors, higher-order hadronic current terms (weak magnetism, induced pseudoscalar), and the closure approximation for summing over intermediate nuclear states. The paper also surveys the variety of nuclear structure methods (with emphasis on QRPA) used to evaluate nuclear matrix elements and discusses the treatment of short-range correlations for heavy particle exchange mechanisms.

## Context

This review extends the long line of work on double beta decay theory dating back to Goeppert-Meyer (1937) and the first laboratory observation by Moe (1987), building on earlier reviews by Suhonen and Civitarese (1998), Elliott and Vogel (2002), and Avignone, Elliott, and Engel (2007). The paper discusses the Schechter-Valle theorem (1982) linking any lepton number violation to Majorana neutrino mass, and addresses the Ikeda sum rule for Gamow-Teller strength as applied to double beta decay by Magda (reference in text). The work enables improved interpretation of ongoing and future 0νββ experiments by clarifying the uncertainties in nuclear matrix elements from different theoretical methods, the role of axial current quenching, and the distinction between light and heavy particle exchange mechanisms.
