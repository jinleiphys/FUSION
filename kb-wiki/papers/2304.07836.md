# Tabulated Equations of State From Models Informed by Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We construct four equation of state (EoS) tables, tabulated over a range of temperatures, densities, and charge fractions, relevant for neutron star applications such as simulations of neutron star mergers. The EoS are computed from a relativistic mean-field theory constrained by the pure neutron matter EoS from chiral effective field theory, inferred properties of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, and astrophysical observations of neutron star structure. To model nuclear matter at low densities, we attach an EoS that models inhomogeneous nuclear matter at arbitrary temperatures and charge fractions. The four EoS tables we develop are available from the CompOSE EoS repository compose.obspm.fr/eos/297 and gitlab.com/ahaber/qmc-rmf-tables.

## Key claim

The paper constructs four tabulated equations of state (EoS) for neutron star merger simulations by combining a relativistic mean-field theory (QMC-RMF) constrained by chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) at zero temperature with a low-density inhomogeneous nuclear matter model (HS(IUF)), but the consistency with finite-temperature chiEFT is only demonstrated for three of the four models (QMC-RMF1,2,3) at T=20 MeV, while QMC-RMF4 is explicitly inconsistent with that finite-temperature calculation.

## Method

The authors developed four relativistic mean-field theories (QMC-RMF1-4) whose coupling constants were fitted to reproduce chiEFT calculations of pure neutron matter from approximately 0.5 to 2 times nuclear saturation density, properties of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, and astrophysical neutron star observations. They attached the HS(IUF) inhomogeneous nuclear matter EoS at low densities via a thermodynamically consistent phase transition, using a pressure offset B to ensure the transition occurs below about 0.5 nuclear saturation density for low charge fractions. The resulting EoS tables were tabulated over temperatures from 0.1 to 158.48 MeV, baryon densities from 10^-12 to 1.58 fm^-3, and charge fractions from 0.01 to 0.6, including anti-nucleons which become significant at high temperature and low charge fraction.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 (used as reference density throughout)
- Temperature range for EoS tables: T from 0.1 to 158.48 MeV
- Baryon density range: nB from 10^-12 to 1.58 fm^-3
- Charge fraction range: yq from 0.01 to 0.6
- Bare nucleon mass: mN = 939 MeV (used for both neutrons and protons)
- Electron mass: me = 0.511 MeV
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 491.5 MeV, m_omega = 782.5 MeV, m_rho = 763.0 MeV
- Nuclear saturation properties (same for all four EoS tables): n0 = 0.1546 fm^-3, B_sat = -16.39 MeV, kappa = 231.3 MeV, J = 31.29 MeV, L = 47.20 MeV
- QMC-RMF coupling constants provided for all four models (Table 1), with g_sigma ranging from 7.54 to 8.32, g_omega from 8.43 to 9.94, g_rho from 10.88 to 12.18
- Pressure offset B values: QMC-RMF1 = -577307 MeV^4, QMC-RMF2 = -460210 MeV^4, QMC-RMF3 = -723934 MeV^4, QMC-RMF4 = -150000 MeV^4

## Context

This work extends the QMC-RMF models developed in Alford (2022) by tabulating them over the thermodynamic ranges relevant for neutron star merger simulations, including finite temperatures and arbitrary charge fractions. The EoS tables are made available on the CompOSE repository (compose.obspm.fr/eos/297) and GitLab, enabling direct use by simulation groups studying neutron star mergers or core-collapse supernovae. The approach contrasts with commonly used RMFTs such as GM1, NL3, IU-FSU, and SFHo, which are not informed by pure neutron matter data from chiEFT, and the authors show that the IUF RMFT (used for the low-density HS model) violates chiEFT constraints on pure neutron matter binding energy above approximately n0.
