# Ab initio computations of the fourth-order charge density moments of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb

> Neutron skins of neutron-rich nuclei connect nuclei with the matter in neutron stars. High-precision measurements of nuclear charge densities to extract higher-order moments are proposed to be sensitive to neutron radii and skin thicknesses. We investigate the charge density of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, leading candidates for such studies, with ab initio nuclear structure calculations. We find strong correlations between the fourth-order charge density moment $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ and the charge and neutron radii, allowing us to predict $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ for $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. We find a substantially weaker correlation between the fourth-order charge density moment and the neutron skin, limiting the ability of high-precision electron scattering to determine the neutron skin in a model-independent manner.

# Ab initio computations of the fourth-order charge density moments of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb

## Key claim
The authors find a strong correlation between the fourth-order charge density moment $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ and both the charge radius and the neutron radius in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, but a substantially weaker correlation between $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ and the neutron skin thickness $R_\mathrm{skin}$. This weak correlation limits the ability of high-precision electron scattering to determine the neutron skin in a model-independent manner, contrary to earlier proposals that suggested such measurements could directly constrain the neutron skin.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) calculations at the IMSRG(2) level for $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb using two selected chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (the $\Delta$NNLO$_\mathrm{GO}$ and 1.8/2.0 (EM7.5) Hamiltonians) plus an ensemble of 34 nonimplausible Hamiltonians. They computed charge form factors $F_\mathrm{ch}(q^2)$ and extracted the second- and fourth-order charge density moments by evaluating the form factor at 11 equally spaced points from $q^2=0$ to $1$ fm$^{-2}$, constructing a Gaussian process interpolator, and taking derivatives as $q^2\to0$.

## Key numbers
- Predicted $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ for $^{48}$Ca: $204.4 \pm 3.2$ fm$^4$, obtained from the correlation with experimental $R_\mathrm{ch}^2$ from Noel (2024) ($12.076 \pm 0.070$ fm$^2$)
- Predicted $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ for $^{208}$Pb: $1172.5 \pm 16.7$ fm$^4$, obtained from the correlation with experimental $R_\mathrm{ch}^2$ from Kurasawa (2021)
- For $^{48}$Ca, the extracted value from Kurasawa (2021) of $R_\mathrm{ch}^4 = 194.734 \pm 2.544$ fm$^4$ disagrees with the prediction because that work used a smaller $R_\mathrm{ch}^2$ value ($11.910 \pm 0.061$ fm$^2$ versus $12.076 \pm 0.070$ fm$^2$)
- For $^{208}$Pb, the extracted value from Kurasawa (2021) of $R_\mathrm{ch}^4 = 1171.981 \pm 17.627$ fm$^4$ agrees with the prediction
- Using the $R_\mathrm{n}^2$-$R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ correlation and a theoretical prediction for $R_\mathrm{n}^2$ from Heinz (2024), the predicted $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ for $^{48}$Ca is $205.8 \pm 10.7$ fm$^4$
- Uncertainty from Gaussian process interpolation on $R_\mathrm{ch}^2$ and $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ is less than 0.001 fm$^2$ and 0.3 fm$^4$ for $^{48}$Ca (0.005 fm$^2$ and 1.8 fm$^4$ for $^{208}$Pb), respectively
- For the 1.8/2.0 (EM7.5) Hamiltonian, $R_\mathrm{ch}^2$ and $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ of $^{208}$Pb change by 0.7% and 1.2% when varying model-space truncation from 15 to 13 major shells; for the $\Delta$NNLO$_\mathrm{GO}$ Hamiltonian, the changes are 0.8% and 1.7%

## Context
This work extends ab initio calculations to the fourth-order charge density moment of heavy nuclei, building on previous IMSRG calculations of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb by Hu (2022) and Heinz (2024). The study directly addresses proposals by Kurasawa (2019, 2021, 2022) that high-precision electron scattering measurements of $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ could be used to infer neutron radii and skin thicknesses, which had previously relied on phenomenological mean-field calculations with model-dependent results. The findings contradict the assumption of a strong $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$-$R_\mathrm{skin}$ correlation and instead show that such inferences carry inherent model dependence, while confirming that $R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ can constrain neutron radii through the stronger $R_\mathrm{n}^2$-$R_\mathrm{ch}^4$ correlation. This work enables future combined analyses of electron scattering and parity-violating experiments (CREX, PREX, MREX) with ab initio theory to reduce uncertainties in neutron skin determinations.
