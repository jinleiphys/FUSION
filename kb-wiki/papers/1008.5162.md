# Initial Conditions and Global Event Properties from Color Glass Condensate

> Perturbative unitarization from non-linear effects is thought to deplete the gluon density for transverse momenta below the saturation scale. Such effects also modify the distribution of gluons produced in heavy-ion collisions in transverse impact parameter space. I discuss some of the consequences for the initial conditions for hydrodynamic models of heavy-ion collisions and for hard ``tomographic'' probes. Also, I stress the importance of realistic modelling of the fluctuations of the valence sources for the small-x fields in the impact parameter plane. Such models can now be combined with solutions of running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution to obtain controlled predictions for initial conditions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Perturbative unitarization from non-linear effects in the Color Glass Condensate framework predicts a modified distribution of produced gluons in transverse impact parameter space, leading to a higher eccentricity of the initial density profile for intermediate impact parameters compared to Glauber-like models; however, specific numerical estimates at the limited available RHIC energy depend on the model for the unintegrated gluon distribution.

## Method

The paper discusses the theoretical framework of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) for modeling initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions, focusing on how non-linear gluon saturation modifies the transverse spatial distribution of produced particles. It describes the Monte-Carlo sampling of nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution to model fluctuations of valence sources, and the construction of unintegrated gluon distributions using the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) model, where the saturation scale Q_s^2(x;r_perp) = Q_0^2(r_perp) (x_0/x)^lambda exhibits energy-dependent growth. The paper also discusses how these initial conditions are combined with hydrodynamic evolution to compute elliptic flow v_2.

## Key numbers

- The minimal distance enforced by hard core repulsion between nucleons is approximately 0.4 fm.
- The eccentricity epsilon is defined as epsilon = <y^2 - x^2> / <y^2 + x^2>, where the average is taken with respect to the distribution of produced gluons in the transverse plane.
- A (5-20)% contribution of hard particles needs to be added to the soft particle production to fit the centrality dependence of dN/dy.
- The correlation length of valence charge density fluctuations is of order 1/Q_s, which is much smaller than the size of a fluid cell (linear dimension ~ 1/T).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model of gluon saturation and the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) model for particle production, building on earlier work by Hirano (2005) and Drescher (2006) on initial conditions for hydrodynamics. It enables controlled predictions for initial conditions at the LHC by combining solutions of the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation with Monte-Carlo codes that generate configurations of large-x valence sources. The paper also connects to studies by Alver (2010) on triangular flow and by Jia (2010) on the azimuthal asymmetry of high-p_T jets, and discusses how fluctuations in the initial state can give rise to structures observed in two-particle angular correlations such as the near-side ridge.
