# Magnetic rotations in 198Pb and 199Pb within covariant density functional theory with pairing correlations

> The observed shears bands in 198Pb and 199Pb are investigated within the framework of tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory, and the separable pairing force is adopted to consider the pairing correlations. The energy spectra, total angular momenta, and transition probabilities agree well with the experimental data. The bandhead energy differences are reproduced satisfactorily without any ad hoc renormalizations when the pairing correlations are taken into account. The angular momentum vectors are discussed in detail to analyze the pairing effects on magnetic rotations.

## Key claim

The observed shears bands in 198Pb and 199Pb are reproduced by tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) with a separable pairing force, and the bandhead energy differences are reproduced without ad hoc renormalizations only when pairing correlations are included.

## Method

The tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) with a separable pairing force was applied to four magnetic rotational bands in 198Pb and 199Pb. The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation was solved iteratively using the PC-PK1 density functional in the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing force with parameters G = -738 MeV fm³ and a = 0.636 fm in the particle-particle channel, with three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis of 12 major shells. Specific multi-quasiparticle configurations were traced and blocked, corresponding to proton configuration π(s⁻²₁/₂ h₉/₂ i₁₃/₂) with various combinations of neutron holes (i₁₃/₂ and fp-shell).

## Key numbers

- Pairing force parameters: G = -738 MeV fm³ and a = 0.636 fm, taken from Tian (2009)
- For band 1 in 198Pb, the spin region around 19–21 ħ shows deviations between calculated and experimental total angular momenta near back-bending
- The calculated B(M1) values decrease with total angular momentum for all bands, a typical feature of magnetic rotation
- The Dirac effective mass was scaled by a factor of 0.58 to approximate the nucleon mass in calculating magnetic moments for B(M1) values

## Context

This work extends the TAC-CDFT calculations of Yu (2012) on magnetic rotations in 198Pb and 199Pb, which neglected pairing correlations and required ad hoc renormalization of bandhead energies and produced too low rotational frequencies for band crossings. By including pairing correlations via a separable pairing force, following the approach of Zhao (2015) for monopole pairing and Wang (2017) for separable pairing in 109Ag, these problems are resolved. The results confirm the conclusions of Zhao (2015) that pairing correlations have two competitive consequences: reducing the magnitude of proton and neutron angular momenta while expediting the merging of their directions.
