# New constraints on equation of state of hot QCD matter

> The longitudinal structure of the quark-gluon plasma(QGP) remains a key challenge in heavy-ion physics. In this Letter, we propose a novel observable, event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations Var$_{\langle p_{T} \rangle}$, which is sensitive to the local pressure gradients and serves as a probe of longitudinal dynamics in the initial state of QGP. We demonstrate that the covariance of averaged transverse momentum at two rapidities $\mathrm{Cov}_{\langle p_T \rangle}(η_1, η_2)$ and its associated decorrelation measures, $R_{p_T}(η_1, η_2)$ and $r_{p_T}(η, η_{\mathrm{ref}})$, exhibit strong sensitivity to the stiffness of equation of state (EoS) of QGP, while showing negligible dependence on the QGP transport coefficients. This distinctive behavior, revealed through state-of-the-art (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations, establishes a powerful approach for constraining the EoS of QCD matter. In the meantime, our results provide new insights into the longitudinal structure of the QGP and its properties under high baryon density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations and their decorrelation across pseudorapidity are sensitive to the stiffness of the equation of state of hot QCD matter while showing negligible dependence on QGP transport coefficients, but this sensitivity is demonstrated only for 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at 19.6 GeV and is not observable at higher collision energies such as 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC framework coupled with a 3D-Glauber dynamical initialization model and UrQMD hadronic transport for 0-10% central 197Au+197Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV. They introduced a parametric modification of the NEOS-BQS equation of state via a parameter alpha that controls stiffness, generating a family of equations of state with squared speed of sound values ranging from 0.15 to 0.225, and computed the covariance of mean transverse momentum at two pseudorapidities, the Pearson coefficient R_pT, and the three-bin decorrelation r_pT.

## Key numbers

- Squared speed of sound for the baseline NEOS-BQS equation of state: c_s^2 = 0.19 at T = 0.18 GeV and s/n_B = 51
- Squared speed of sound for the stiffer equation of state (alpha = -0.3): c_s^2 = 0.225 at T = 0.18 GeV and s/n_B = 51, which is about 15% larger than the baseline
- Squared speed of sound for the softer equation of state (alpha = 0.5): c_s^2 = 0.15 at T = 0.18 GeV and s/n_B = 51
- The stiffer equation of state yields enhanced mean transverse momentum by approximately 0.02 GeV compared to the baseline
- The decorrelation coefficient R_pT shows approximately 50% deviation from unity at pseudorapidity gap Delta_eta ~ 2
- The decorrelation exhibits approximately 30% variation between different equations of state at eta_1 = -1 and eta_2 = 1.5
- Transport coefficients contribute only about 5-10% variation to the decorrelation

## Context

This work extends prior studies of longitudinal decorrelations in heavy-ion collisions, including pseudorapidity dependence of anisotropic flow (Petersen 2011, Pang 2012, Jia 2017) and forward-backward decorrelations of transverse momentum (Bozek 2016, Chatterjee 2017). The results establish that mean transverse momentum decorrelation provides a cleaner probe of the equation of state than anisotropic flow decorrelation, which shows minimal dependence on both the equation of state and viscosity. The observables introduced are directly measurable with current high-statistics data from the BES-II program at RHIC using the iTPC detector, and could be applied at future facilities such as FAIR-CBM, NICA, and HIAF.
