# Hadronization in Nuclear Environment and Electroproduction of Leading Hadrons

> Radiative energy loss of a highly virtual quark originating from a deep-inelastic electron scattering plays a crucial role in production of leading hadrons off nuclei. The density of energy loss for gluon radiation turns out to be time- and energy--dependent in inclusive hadron production. Important phenomena involved are Sudakov's suppression of no radiation of that part of gluon spectrum, which is forbidden by energy conservation, and color transparency, which suppresses the final state interaction of the produced colorless wave packet. We model the soft part of hadronization, which usually is supposed to be due to the color strings, using also the gluon radiation and nicely reproduce the string parameters. Our parameter-free calculations provide a good agreement with available data on $z_h$-dependence of the quark fragmentation function in vacuum, as well as $ν$-, $z_h$- and $Q^2$-dependence of nuclear effects. We come to the conclusion that the energy range of ELFE - HERMES is most sensitive to the underlying dynamics of hadronization provided that nuclear targets are used.

## Key claim

The paper argues that radiative energy loss of a highly virtual quark, including Sudakov suppression of forbidden gluon radiation and color transparency of the produced colorless wave packet, can fully describe hadronization in both vacuum and nuclear matter, without invoking the traditional string model for the soft part of hadronization. The authors claim that the energy range of the ELFE and HERMES experiments is most sensitive to the underlying dynamics of hadronization when nuclear targets are used.

## Method

The authors model hadronization by treating both hard and soft gluon radiation perturbatively, using a running QCD coupling fixed at low transverse momentum to reproduce the string tension of 1 GeV/fm. They derive the time-dependent radiative energy loss of a quark, including a Sudakov form factor to enforce energy conservation, and then compute the distribution of hadron production times by projecting the resulting quark-gluon system onto hadron light-cone wave functions. For nuclear targets, they incorporate the probability that the produced colorless wave packet escapes the nucleus without inelastic interaction, including color transparency effects from its small transverse size.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(k_0 \approx 0.7\) GeV is chosen to reproduce the energy loss density corresponding to the string tension, \(dE/dt \approx 1\) GeV/fm.
- The mean production time \(t_{pr}\) approximately scales in \((1-z_h)\nu\) and weakly depends on \(Q^2\); it vanishes at \(z_h \approx 1\).
- The time \(t_1\) at which the energy loss density changes is given by \(t_1 = (1-z_h)/x_{Bj}m_N\), and \(t_2 = (Q^2/\lambda^2) t_1\).
- The density of energy loss is constant at \(dE/dt = -\epsilon Q^2/2\) up to time \(t=t_1\), then slows to \(dE/dt = -\epsilon\nu (1-z_h)/t\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD approach to radiative energy loss developed by the same authors and others (Kopeliovich, Nemchik, Predazzi, 1995) by modeling the soft part of hadronization via gluon radiation rather than the traditional string model. It provides parameter-free calculations that agree with EMC and SLAC data on the \(z_h\)-dependence of the quark fragmentation function in vacuum and the \(\nu\)-, \(z_h\)-, and \(Q^2\)-dependence of nuclear effects. The approach enables predictions for future experiments at ELFE and HERMES, where nuclear effects are expected to be most sensitive to the dynamics of hadronization.
