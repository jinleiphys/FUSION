# Implications of GRB 130603B and its macronova for r-process nucleosynthesis

> The tentative identification of a Li-Paczynski macronova following the short GRB 130603B indicated that a few hundredths of a solar mass of neutron star matter were ejected and that this ejected mass has radioactively decayed into heavy r-process elements. If correct, this confirms long standing predictions (Eichler, Livio, Piran and Schramm 1989) that on the one hand, sGRBs are produced in compact binary mergers (CBMs) and on the other hand that these events are significant and possibly dominant sources of the heavy (A>130) r-process nuclei. Assuming that this interpretation is correct we obtain a lower limit of 0.02 m_sun on the ejected mass. Using the current estimates of the rate of sGRBs and with a beaming factor of 50, mergers associated with sGRBs can produce all the observed heavy r-process material in the Universe. We confront this conclusion with cosmochemistry and show that even though such events are rare, mixing is sufficient to account for the current homogeneous distribution of r-process material in the Galaxy. However, the appearance of significant amounts of Eu in some very low metallicity stars requires that some mergers took place very early on, namely with a very short time delay after the earliest star formation episodes. Alternatively, an additional early r-processsource may have contributed at that early stage. Finally, we note that evidence for short lived {244}^Pu in the very early solar system suggests that a merger of this kind took place within the vicinity of the solar system shortly (a few hundred million years) before its formation.

## Key claim

If the tentative identification of a Li-Paczynski macronova following GRB 130603B is correct, then compact binary mergers (CBMs) are confirmed as the engines of short gamma-ray bursts and are significant, possibly dominant, sources of heavy (A>130) r-process nuclei, though the appearance of europium in very low metallicity stars requires either very early mergers with short time delays or an additional early r-process source.

## Method

The authors derived a lower limit on the ejected mass from the observed nIR luminosity of the macronova and the late-time radioactive heating rate, assuming a fraction of radioactive energy is converted locally to heat and radiated as nIR. They compared the implied merger rate from r-process nucleosynthesis requirements with the observed short GRB rate and a beaming factor of 50, and confronted these results with cosmochemical data on europium abundances in low-metallicity stars and evidence for plutonium-244 in the early solar system.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on ejected mass: m_ej > 0.02 m_sun (assuming epsilon = 0.5 for the fraction of radioactive energy in electrons/positrons and gamma-rays)
- Estimated ejected mass used for further calculations: 0.04 m_sun
- Milky Way contains M^(A>130) = 1.6 x 10^4 m_sun in heavy r-process material
- Required number of mergers in the Galaxy: N = 4 x 10^5 (assuming m_ej = 0.04 m_sun)
- Implied CBM rate: R_CBM = 300 Gpc^-3 yr^-1 (for m_ej = 0.04 m_sun)
- Observed sGRB rate: R_SGRB = 6 +/- 2 Gpc^-3 yr^-1
- Implied beaming factor: approximately 50 (1 out of 50 mergers produces an observable sGRB)
- Half-life of plutonium-244: t_1/2 = 81 Myr (tau = 117 Myr)
- Observed nIR magnitude: H_160,AB = 25.73 +/- 0.2 (M_J,AB approximately -15.35), corresponding to intrinsic luminosity approximately 10^41 erg/sec
- GRB 130603B duration: 0.18 +/- 0.02 s at redshift 0.356

## Context

This work extends the long-standing prediction by Eichler, Livio, Piran, and Schramm (1989) that compact binary mergers produce short GRBs and are major r-process sources, and builds on the macronova/kilonova concept from Li and Paczynski (1998). The paper addresses previous arguments against the neutron star merger scenario from Qian (2000) and Argast et al. (2004) regarding chemical evolution, and suggests that turbulent mixing, differential rotation, and binary kicks can resolve the apparent inconsistency with observed europium scatter in low-metallicity stars. The results enable future searches for radio signals from ejecta interacting with circum-merger material using the Extended Very Large Array, and predict that another short GRB at comparable distance with an accompanying IR macronova should be detected within a year or two.
