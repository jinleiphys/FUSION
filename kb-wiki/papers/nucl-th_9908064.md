# Delayed Collapse of Protoneutron Stars with Kaon Condensate

> Equation of state with kaon condensate is derived for isentropic and neutrino-trapped matter. Both are important ingredients to study the delayed collapse of protoneutron stars. Solving the TOV equation, we discuss the static properties of protoneutron stars and implications for their delayed collapse.

## Key claim

The paper argues that kaon condensation is a plausible mechanism for the delayed collapse of protoneutron stars into low-mass black holes, but only under specific conditions: the collapse occurs during deleptonization when a star initially with lepton fraction \(Y_{le}=0.4\) and entropy per baryon \(S=2\) evolves to a neutrino-free state with the same entropy, and the star's baryon number exceeds the terminal point on the target curve.

## Method

The authors derive an equation of state for isentropic and neutrino-trapped matter with kaon condensate using a nonlinear sigma model based on chiral symmetry. Fluctuations around the kaon condensate are treated via successive chiral rotations to avoid the Lee-Yang term, and thermodynamic functions are computed to one-loop order in the imaginary-time path integral with Hartree approximation for kaon-nucleon interactions. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation is then solved to obtain static properties of protoneutron stars, and the total baryon number is used as a conserved quantity to evaluate evolutionary paths.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon number for a neutrino-free, cold (\(S=0\)) star that can be reached from a \(S=2\) initial state: \(N_B \le 2.14 \times 10^{57}\)
- Maximum mass of cold neutron stars determined from this evolutionary constraint: \(1.54 M_\odot\)
- Lepton fraction used for neutrino-trapped matter: \(Y_{le}=0.4\) (where \(Y_{le}=Y_e+Y_{\nu_e}\))
- Isothermal equation of state shown for temperatures \(T=0\) and \(T=50\) MeV
- Entropy per baryon values considered: \(S=0, 1, 2\)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of kaon condensation at zero temperature, notably by Kaplan and Nelson, and provides a consistent finite-temperature treatment based on chiral symmetry. The authors build on their own prior formulation (Yasuhira and Tatsumi, 1998) to include thermal and neutrino-trapping effects. The results enable future dynamical simulations of protoneutron star evolution and suggest that the maximum mass of cold neutron stars should be revised downward from the static \(T=0\) value when evolutionary effects are considered, as previously argued by Takatsuka.
