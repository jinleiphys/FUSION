# The role of hyperfine mixing in $b\to c$ semileptonic and electromagnetic decays of doubly-heavy baryons

> We analyze the effects of hyperfine mixing in $b\to c\, $ semileptonic and electromagnetic decays of doubly heavy baryons.

## Key claim

Hyperfine mixing between $S_h=0$ and $S_h=1$ spin components in doubly-heavy $bc$ baryons has a significant impact on both semileptonic and electromagnetic decay widths, though the masses themselves change only slightly when mixing is included; the effect is negligible for $cc$ and $bb$ baryons due to wave-function antisymmetry constraints.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model to compute masses and decay widths of ground-state doubly-heavy baryons, first in the unmixed $S_h$-basis (where the heavy-quark subsystem spin is well defined) and then for mixed states obtained by diagonalizing the mass matrices in the $bc$ sector. Semileptonic $b\to c$ decay widths are calculated with $|V_{cb}|=0.0413$ for $l=e,\mu$, and electromagnetic decay widths are computed for photon transitions between $bc$ baryons.

## Key numbers

- Mixed-state masses: $M_{\Xi_{bc}^{(1)}} = 6967$ MeV, $M_{\Xi_{bc}^{(2)}} = 6919$ MeV, $M_{\Omega_{bc}^{(1)}} = 7046$ MeV, $M_{\Omega_{bc}^{(2)}} = 7005$ MeV
- Mixing coefficients: $\Xi_{bc}^{(1)} = 0.902\,\Xi'_{bc} + 0.431\,\Xi_{bc}$; $\Omega_{bc}^{(1)} = 0.899\,\Omega'_{bc} + 0.437\,\Omega_{bc}$
- Semileptonic unmixed widths (in $10^{-14}$ GeV): $\Gamma(\Xi_{bb}\to\Xi_{bc}\,l\bar\nu_l) = 2.00$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc}\to\Xi_{cc}\,l\bar\nu_l) = 2.57$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc}'\to\Xi_{cc}^*\,l\bar\nu_l) = 2.35$
- Semileptonic mixed widths (in $10^{-14}$ GeV): $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc}^{(2)}\to\Xi_{cc}\,l\bar\nu_l) = 3.50$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc}^{(1)}\to\Xi_{cc}^*\,l\bar\nu_l) = 3.14$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc}^{(2)}\to\Xi_{cc}^*\,l\bar\nu_l) = 0.017$ (a factor of 44 reduction from the unmixed case)
- Electromagnetic unmixed widths (in $10^{-8}$ GeV): $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^*\to\Xi_{bcu}\gamma) = 105$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcd}^*\to\Xi_{bcd}\gamma) = 50.5$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^*\to\Xi'_{bcu}\gamma) = 4.04$
- Electromagnetic mixed widths (in $10^{-8}$ GeV): $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^*\to\Xi_{bcu}^{(2)}\gamma) = 73.9$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcd}^*\to\Xi_{bcd}^{(2)}\gamma) = 103$, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^{(1)}\to\Xi_{bcu}^{(2)}\gamma) = 12.4$

## Context

This work extends the earlier findings of Roberts and Pervin (2008, 2009) on hyperfine mixing in semileptonic decays of doubly-heavy baryons, qualitatively confirming their conclusion that mixing has an enormous impact but finding results that differ by roughly a factor of two. The authors also provide the first calculation of hyperfine mixing effects on electromagnetic decays of $bc$ baryons, comparing with the relativistic constituent quark model results of Branz et al. (2009). The study enables future work requiring accurate predictions for doubly-heavy baryon decay widths, particularly electromagnetic transitions, which are shown to be highly sensitive to the precise baryon masses and thus demand improved mass determinations.
