# $Ξ_c$ and $Ξ_b$ excited states within a ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm lsf}\times$HQSS model

> We study odd parity $J=1/2$ and $J=3/2$ $Ξ_c$ resonances using a unitarized coupled-channel framework based on a ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm lsf}\times$HQSS-extended Weinberg-Tomozawa baryon-meson interaction, while paying a special attention to the renormalization procedure. We predict a large molecular $Λ_c \bar K$ component for the $Ξ_c(2790)$ with a dominant $0^-$ light-degree-of-freedom spin configuration. We discuss the differences between the $3/2^-$ $Λ_c(2625)$ and $Ξ_c(2815)$ states, and conclude that they cannot be SU(3) siblings, whereas we predict the existence of other $Ξ_c-$states, two of them related to the two-pole structure of the $Λ_c(2595)$. It is of particular interest a pair of $J=1/2$ and $J=3/2$ poles, which form a HQSS doublet and that we tentatively assign to the $Ξ_c(2930)$ and $Ξ_c(2970)$, respectively. Within this picture, the $Ξ_c(2930)$ would be part of a SU(3) sextet, containing either the $Ω_c(3090)$ or the $Ω_c(3119)$, and that would be completed by the $Σ_c(2800)$. Moreover, we identify a $J=1/2$ sextet with the $Ξ_b(6227)$ state and the recently discovered $Σ_b(6097)$. Assuming the equal spacing rule and to complete this multiplet, we predict the existence of a $J=1/2$ $Ω_b$ odd parity state, with a mass of 6360 MeV and that should be seen in the $Ξ_b \bar K$ channel.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the $\Xi_c(2790)$ and $\Xi_c(2815)$ cannot be SU(3) siblings, contrary to some previous assignments, and that the $\Xi_c(2790)$ has a dominant molecular $\Lambda_c \bar K$ component with a $0^-$ light-degree-of-freedom spin configuration rather than the $1^-$ configuration expected from a constituent quark model.

## Method
The authors use a unitarized coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation framework based on a ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm lsf}\times$HQSS-extended Weinberg-Tomozawa baryon-meson interaction. They solve for $T$-matrix poles in the $C=1$, $S=-1$, $I=1/2$ sector (for $\Xi_c$) and $B=-1$, $S=-1$, $I=1/2$ sector (for $\Xi_b$), and systematically compare two renormalization schemes: a one-subtraction (dimensional regularization) scheme and a common UV cutoff scheme, varying the cutoff from 1 GeV to 1.2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- $\Xi_c(2790)$: mass $2792.4 \pm 0.5$ MeV (charged) / $2794.1 \pm 0.5$ MeV (neutral), width $8.9 \pm 1.0$ MeV / $10.0 \pm 1.1$ MeV, assigned $J^P = 1/2^-$
- $\Xi_c(2815)$: mass $2816.73 \pm 0.21$ MeV (charged) / $2820.26 \pm 0.27$ MeV (neutral), width $2.43 \pm 0.26$ MeV / $2.54 \pm 0.25$ MeV, assigned $J^P = 3/2^-$
- $\Xi_c(2930)$: mass $2942 \pm 5$ MeV (charged) / $2929.7^{+2.8}_{-5.0}$ MeV (neutral), width $15 \pm 9$ MeV / $26 \pm 8$ MeV, $J^P$ unknown
- $\Xi_c(2970)$: mass $2969.4 \pm 0.8$ MeV (charged) / $2967.8^{+0.9}_{-0.7}$ MeV (neutral), width $20.9^{+2.4}_{-3.5}$ MeV / $28.1^{+3.4}_{-4.0}$ MeV, $J^P$ unknown
- $\Xi_b(6227)$: mass $6226.9 \pm 2$ MeV, width $18 \pm 6$ MeV, $J^P$ unknown
- Predicted $J=1/2$ $\Omega_b$ odd parity state: mass 6360 MeV, expected to be seen in $\Xi_b \bar K$ channel
- For $\Lambda = 1150$ MeV, the $c_1$ pole (identified with $\Xi_c(2790)$) has mass 2773.59 MeV and width 10.52 MeV, with dominant couplings $g_{\Lambda D^*} = 2.9$, $g_{\Lambda D} = 1.6$, $g_{\Sigma D} = 1.5$, $g_{\Lambda_c \bar K} = 1.3$

## Context
This work extends the unitarized coupled-channel approach previously applied to $\Omega_c$ states by Nieves et al. (2017) and to $\Xi_c$ and $\Xi_b$ sectors by Romanets et al. (2012) and Garcia-Recio et al. (2012), with special attention to renormalization scheme dependence. It revises earlier assignments from Romanets et al. (2012) where the $\Xi_c(2790)$ was identified with a different pole ($c_7$). The results enable predictions for a $J=1/2$ $\Omega_b$ state at 6360 MeV to complete a sextet containing $\Xi_b(6227)$ and $\Sigma_b(6097)$, and clarify the SU(3) multiplet structure of observed $\Xi_c$ resonances.
