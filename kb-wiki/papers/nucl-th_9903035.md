# Interaction of Ultra-Cold Neutrons with Condensed Matter

> General theory of neutron scattering (elastic and inelastic) is presented. It is applicable for the whole domain of slow neutrons and includes as limiting cases existing theories for thermal and cold neutrons and for elastic scattering of UCN. New expression for inelastic scattering cross section for UCN is proposed. It differs from the usually used by proper account of re-scattering processes. Evidence for small heating and cooling of UCN is given.

# Interaction of Ultra-Cold Neutrons with Condensed Matter

## Key claim
The paper presents a general theory of neutron scattering that is applicable across the full slow-neutron domain, including ultra-cold neutrons (UCN), and proposes a new expression for the inelastic scattering cross section that properly accounts for re-scattering processes. The authors claim that this theory qualitatively explains small heating and cooling of UCN, but note that the magnitude of the effect in the phonon model is low and that other collective excitations may be needed to explain observed probabilities.

## Method
The authors derive a general expression for the double differential cross section using a multiple scattering wave approach that avoids the Born approximation and the Fermi pseudopotential, relying instead on the scattering length approximation justified by the short range and large depth of the neutron-nucleus interaction. They expand the correlation functions and neutron amplitudes to second order in the displacement of nuclei from equilibrium (k*u expansion), obtaining four distinct terms in the inelastic cross section that describe re-scattering of both incident and outgoing neutron waves. The theory is applied to a simple model of UCN incident normally on a thick layer of uniform matter, using a phonon model for the correlation function to compute inelastic scattering probabilities per bounce.

## Key numbers
- UCN wavelength: λ ≥ 10 nm (definition of ultra-cold neutron regime)
- Thermal and cold neutron wavelength range: 0.03 nm ≤ λ ≤ 1 nm
- Inelastic scattering probability per bounce for scattered neutrons with energy ε' ≤ U: dw^{(2)}_{ie}/dε' = (2kβ/πU) * (T/Ms²) * (v'/s), where T is target temperature, M is nuclear mass, s is speed of sound, v' = √(2ε'/m)
- Inelastic scattering probability per bounce for scattered neutrons with energy ε' > U: dw^{(2)}_{ie}/dε' = (kβ/πε') * (T/Ms²) * (v'/s) / [1 - (v'/2s)²]
- Magnitude estimates: kβ ∼ 10⁻⁶ and v'/s ∼ 10⁻³ for the phonon model
- Born approximation probability: dw^{B}_{ie}/dε' ∼ (kβ/ms²) * (T/Ms²) * (v'/s), which is additionally suppressed by factor ∼ U/ms² compared to the new expression

## Context
This work extends the standard theory of thermal and cold neutron scattering (based on the Fermi pseudopotential and Born approximation, as in standard references cited as [1,2]) to the ultra-cold neutron regime where re-scattering dominates. It builds upon the multiple scattering wave approach used for elastic UCN scattering by fixed nuclei (references [3,4]) and addresses the observed phenomena of UCN escape from vessels and small heating and cooling reported in reference [5]. The authors criticize an earlier attempt by reference [6] to improve the Born approximation by replacing plane waves with damping functions, showing that such replacement must be made before differentiation with respect to nuclear positions. The theory enables future investigation of UCN interaction with other collective excitations (diffusion and thermal wave modes) that may provide larger probabilities for the observed heating and cooling effects.
