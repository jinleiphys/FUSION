# Nuclear Reactions: A Challenge for Few- and Many-Body Theory

> A current interest in nuclear reactions, specifically with rare isotopes concentrates on their reaction with neutrons, in particular neutron capture. In order to facilitate reactions with neutrons one must use indirect methods using deuterons as beam or target of choice. For adding neutrons, the most common reaction is the (d,p) reaction, in which the deuteron breaks up and the neutron is captured by the nucleus. Those (d,p) reactions may be viewed as a three-body problem in a many-body context. This contribution reports on a feasibility study for describing phenomenological nucleon-nucleus optical potentials in momentum space in a separable form, so that they may be used for Faddeev calculations of (d,p) reactions.

# Nuclear Reactions: A Challenge for Few- and Many-Body Theory

## Key claim
The authors report a feasibility study showing that phenomenological nucleon-nucleus optical potentials can be represented in separable form in momentum space using the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) scheme, enabling their use in Faddeev calculations of (d,p) reactions. The study is limited to the central part of the CH89 optical potential for neutron scattering off ⁴⁸Ca, neglecting spin-orbit terms, and demonstrates that rank-4 separable representations reproduce the s-matrix well for center-of-mass energies below 50 MeV.

## Method
The authors apply the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) separabilization scheme to the partial-wave projected t-matrices derived from the central part of the CH89 phenomenological global optical potential for neutron-⁴⁸Ca scattering. They construct separable representations of rank 3 and rank 4 by selecting support energies at which the exact half-shell t-matrix is used to build form factors, and compare the resulting phase shifts, s-matrices, and half-shell potentials to the exact calculations. For partial waves supporting bound states (l=0,1), they also test including bound-state wave functions as form factors in the separable expansion.

## Key numbers
- The l=4 phase shift from the CH89 optical potential for n+⁴⁸Ca shows its most prominent structure between roughly 5 and 15 MeV center-of-mass energy.
- For the l=1 partial wave, the Wood-Saxon well supports two bound states: a ground state at E_B1 = 28.3 MeV and an excited state at E_B2 = 5.1 MeV.
- Half-shell potentials are compared at fixed on-shell momenta corresponding to E_c.m. = 4.897 MeV and E_c.m. = 48.97 MeV.
- A rank-4 separable expansion describes the l=1 s-matrix essentially perfectly up to 50 MeV.
- For partial waves beyond l=4, no bound states are supported by the central CH89 potential.

## Context
This work extends the EST separabilization technique, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon t-matrices (Haidenbauer, 1984; Haidenbauer, 1986), to phenomenological nucleon-nucleus optical potentials for the first time. It addresses a gap identified in formulations for (d,p) reactions including target excitations (Mukhamedzhanov, 2012; Alt, 2007), which require separable representations of the neutron-nucleus and proton-nucleus subsystem transition amplitudes. The study enables future Faddeev calculations of (d,p) reactions with realistic optical potentials, though the authors note that the fully off-shell behavior of the separable representations in momentum regions relevant to Faddeev calculations requires further investigation, as does extension to proton-nucleus optical potentials.
