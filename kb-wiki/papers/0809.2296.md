# Recent developments in the constituent quark model including quark-antiquark pairs

> We present the formalism for a new generation of unquenched quark models for baryons in which the effects of quark-antiquark pairs (u anti-u, d anti-d and s anti-s) are taken into account in an explicit form via a microscopic, QCD-inspired, quark-antiquark creation mechaniscm. The present approach is an extension of the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur in which now the contributions of quark-antiquark pairs an be studied for any initial baryon and for any flavor of the q anti-q pair. It is shown that the inclusion of q anti-q paris leads to a large contribution of orbital angular momentum to the proton spin.

## Key claim
The authors present a generalization of the Geiger-Isgur flux-tube breaking model that allows inclusion of quark-antiquark pairs (u anti-u, d anti-d, and s anti-s) for any initial baryon and any baryon/meson model, showing that in the closure limit the relative flavor contributions from sea quarks mirror those of valence quarks, and that orbital angular momentum from q anti-q pairs contributes about 40% of the proton spin.

## Method
The authors extend the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur by developing group-theoretical techniques to handle permutation symmetry between identical quarks and constructing an algorithm to generate a complete set of intermediate states for any baryon/meson model expressed in harmonic oscillator wave functions. The quark-antiquark pair creation is inserted via a microscopic 3P0 pair-creation operator with vacuum quantum numbers, and one-loop diagrams are summed over a complete set of intermediate states up to saturation. The formalism is applied in the closure limit (where all intermediate states are degenerate) to compute the flavor decomposition of baryon spin, and then numerically to the proton spin problem.

## Key numbers
- The most recent experimental value for the contribution of quark spins to the proton spin is 33.0 ± 3.9% (from HERMES).
- In the unquenched quark model calculation, q anti-q pairs contribute about half of the proton spin, of which one quarter is due to the spin of the sea quarks and three quarters to orbital angular momentum (about 40% of the proton spin from orbital angular momentum).
- For the ground state decuplet baryons in the closure limit, the relative contributions of Δu:Δd:Δs for the Δ+ (uud) are 6:3:0; for the Ω- (sss) are 0:0:9.
- For the ground state octet baryons in the closure limit, the relative contributions of Δu:Δd:Δs for the proton (uud) are 4:-1:0; for the Λ (uds) are 0:0:3.
- The total spin contribution from sea quarks for decuplet baryons is three times that for octet baryons: (Δu+Δd+Δs)_decuplet = 3(Δu+Δd+Δs)_octet.

## Context
This work extends the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur (1990s), which previously only included s anti-s loops in the proton, to now include u anti-u and d anti-d loops for any baryon resonance. The approach addresses the "spin crisis" of the proton first identified by the European Muon Collaboration at CERN, and complements studies with meson-cloud models and axial exchange currents that also found significant orbital angular momentum contributions. The formalism enables systematic study of flavor asymmetry in the nucleon sea, electromagnetic couplings, and transition form factors of baryon resonances.
