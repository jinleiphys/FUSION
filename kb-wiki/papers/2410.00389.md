# Precise Mass Measurement of the Longest Odd-Odd Chain of \boldmath $1^+$ Ground States

> Precise mass measurements of the ground and isomeric states of the odd-odd $^{108, 110, 112, 114, 116}$Rh were performed using the Canadian Penning Trap at Argonne National Laboratory, showing good agreement with recent JYFLTRAP measurements. A new possible isomeric state of $^{114}$Rh was also observed. These isotopes are part of the longest odd-odd chain of identical ground-state spin-parity assignment of 1$^+$, spanning $^{104-118}$Rh, despite being in a region of deformation. Realistic phenomenological mean-field calculations using ``universal'' Wood-Saxon Hamiltonian were performed, which explained this phenomenon for the first time. In addition, multi-quasiparticle blocking calculations were performed to study the configuration of low-lying states in the odd-odd Rh nuclei, elucidating anomalous isomeric yield ratio observed for $^{114}$Rh.

## Key claim

The paper reports precise mass measurements of the ground and isomeric states of five neutron-rich odd-odd rhodium isotopes, confirming that the chain of 1+ ground states from 104Rh to 118Rh is the longest such chain in the nuclear chart, and provides the first theoretical explanation for this phenomenon using deformed Woods-Saxon mean-field calculations.

## Method

The masses of 108,110,112,114,116Rh in their 1+ charge state were measured using the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique with the Canadian Penning Trap mass spectrometer at the CARIBU facility at Argonne National Laboratory. The measurements used accumulation times of 430-450 ms for most isotopes, with 114Rh measured up to 700 ms to resolve three separate states. Realistic phenomenological mean-field calculations using a universal Woods-Saxon Hamiltonian were performed to explain the persistent 1+ ground-state spin-parity assignment, and multi-quasiparticle blocking calculations were performed to study the configuration of low-lying states.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 108Rh ground state: -84972.9(16) keV, which is 58(14) keV higher than the AME2020 value
- Excitation energy of 108Rh isomeric state: 65.7(23) keV, improving precision by an order of magnitude over AME2020
- Excitation energy of 110Rh isomeric state: 39.9(11) keV
- Excitation energy of 114Rh isomeric state (first): 93.0(11) keV, a newly observed state not in AME2020
- Excitation energy of 114Rh isomeric state (second): 114.2(11) keV
- Excitation energy of 116Rh isomeric state: 121.2(65) keV
- Mass excess of 112Rh observed state: -79562.64(73) keV, consistent with the isomeric state from JYFLTRAP measurements

## Context

This work extends the precision mass measurements of neutron-rich rhodium isotopes previously performed at JYFLTRAP (Hager 2007, Kankainen 2003) and resolves discrepancies with older beta-endpoint measurements (Jokinen 1991, private communication 2000). The measurements confirm the recent JYFLTRAP results (2024) and provide the first theoretical explanation for the longest chain of identical 1+ ground-state spin-parity assignments in odd-odd nuclei using deformed Woods-Saxon calculations. The discovery of a new isomeric state in 114Rh and the observation of anomalous isomeric yield ratios enable future decay spectroscopy studies to confirm the spin assignments and understand the inversion of ground-state and isomeric-state production in fission.
