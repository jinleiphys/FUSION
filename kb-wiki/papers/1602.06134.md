# Spin-isospin transitions in chromium isotopes within the quasiparticle random phase approximation

> Beta decay and electron capture on isotopes of chromium are advocated to play a key role in the stellar evolution process. In this paper we endeavor to study charge-changing transitions for 24 isotopes of chromium ($^{42-65}$Cr). These include neutron-rich and neutron-deficient isotopes of chromium. Three different models from the QRPA genre, namely the pn-QRPA, the Pyatov method (PM) and the Schematic model (SM), were selected to calculate and study the Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions in chromium isotopes. The SM was employed separately in the particle-particle ($pp$) and $pp$ + particle-hole ($ph$) channels. To study the effect of deformation, the SM was first used assuming the nuclei to be spherical and later to be deformed. The PM was used both in $pp$ and $pp$ + $ph$ channels but only for the case of spherical nuclei. The pn-QRPA calculation was done by considering both $pp$ and $ph$ forces and taking deformation of nucleus into consideration. A recent study proved this version of pn-QRPA to be the best for calculation of GT strength distributions amongst the QRPA models. The pn-QRPA model calculated GT distributions had low-lying centroids and small widths as compared to other QRPA models. Our calculation results were also compared with other theoretical models and measurements wherever available. Our results are in decent agreement with previous measurements and shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pn-QRPA model (incorporating both particle-particle and particle-hole forces along with nuclear deformation) is the best among six QRPA-type models for calculating Gamow-Teller strength distributions in chromium isotopes, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that all calculations used a quenching factor of (0.6)² and that the comparison with experimental data was limited to only a few isotopes (⁴⁶Cr, ⁵⁰Cr, ⁵²Cr, ⁵⁴Cr) where measurements existed.

## Method

The authors calculated Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for 24 chromium isotopes (⁴²⁻⁶⁵Cr) using six variants of quasiparticle random phase approximation models: three versions of the Schematic Model (SM), spherical with only particle-hole channel, spherical with both particle-particle and particle-hole channels, and deformed with both channels; two versions of the Pyatov Method (PM), spherical with only particle-hole and with both channels; and one pn-QRPA model incorporating both interaction channels and nuclear deformation. Single-particle energies and wave functions were computed using the Nilsson model with BCS pairing, and all results were multiplied by a quenching factor of (0.6)² to enable comparison with experimental data and prior calculations.

## Key numbers

- Total B(GT)₊ for ⁴⁶Cr to the 1⁺₁ state: pn-QRPA calculated 0.49, compared to measured value and shell model FPD6 value (both close to this number); SM(C) gave 0.19
- pn-QRPA calculated total B(GT)₋ strength for ⁴⁸Cr: 3.33 with centroid at 4.78 MeV in ⁴⁸Mn; SM(C) calculated 3.37 with centroid at 15.4 MeV; PM(B) calculated 0.16 with centroid at 11.5 MeV
- For ⁵⁰Cr, pn-QRPA placed the B(GT)₋ centroid at 4.81 MeV in daughter; SM(C) at 19.16 MeV; PM(B) at 2.79 MeV
- pn-QRPA calculated centroid values for all 24 chromium isotopes in the β-decay direction lay within the range 4–10 MeV
- pn-QRPA calculated centroid values in the electron capture direction lay within the range 2–9 MeV
- pn-QRPA calculated widths between 2 and 5 MeV in both directions; PM(B) and SM(C) calculated widths between 8 and 10 MeV for even-even isotopes in the β-decay direction
- For ⁵⁴Cr, total B(GT)₋ strength: pn-QRPA calculated 8.45, SM(C) calculated 11.19, PM(B) calculated 6.68; pn-QRPA centroid at 6.10 MeV in ⁵⁴Mn

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA studies of GT transitions in titanium isotopes (Cakmak, 2014) to chromium isotopes, which were identified as key weak-interaction nuclei in presupernova evolution by Aufderheide (1994) and Heger (2001). The calculations are compared with shell model results using KB3G, FPD6, and GXPF2 interactions (Onishi, 2005; Caurier, 1994; Petermann, 2007), shell model Monte Carlo calculations (Langanke, 1995), and experimental measurements for ⁴⁶Cr (Onishi, 2005) and ⁵⁰Cr (Fujita, 2011; Adachi, 2007). The results enable future calculation of weak interaction rates for astrophysical simulations, particularly for neutron-rich and neutron-deficient chromium isotopes where no experimental data currently exist.
