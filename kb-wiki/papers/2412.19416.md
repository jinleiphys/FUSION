# Vector and Tensor Spin Polarization for Vector Bosons at Local Equilibrium

> We derive expressions for the vector and tensor components of the spin polarization of massive vector bosons at local thermodynamic equilibrium up to second order in the space-time gradients of the thermodynamic fields pertaining to the canonical stress-energy tensor and spin tensor of the free Proca field. A set of Feynman rules is devised to calculate the Wigner function and the matrix-valued spin-dependent distribution (MVSD) functions order by order in space-time gradients. Due to constraints imposed by time-reversal symmetry, the leading contribution to spin alignment - defined as the 00-component of the tensor polarization - arises from second-order terms in MVSD, for which we provide an analytic formula. We discuss the physical meaning of different contributions to vector and tensor polarization. These formulae provide a prediction of a contribution to the spin alignment which can be compared with the observations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

For massive vector bosons at local thermodynamic equilibrium, the leading contribution to spin alignment (the 00-component of tensor polarization) arises from second-order terms in space-time gradients of thermodynamic fields, because time-reversal symmetry forces the first-order contribution to vanish; this provides a prediction that can be compared with observations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors use the Local Equilibrium Density Operator (LEDO) formalism to compute the matrix-valued spin-dependent distribution (MVSD) for free neutral massive vector bosons (Proca field) up to second order in space-time gradients of the thermodynamic fields. They devise a set of Feynman rules to calculate the Wigner function and MVSD order by order in gradients, and extract the vector and tensor components of spin polarization from the spin density matrix expressed as a Cooper-Frye type integral over the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- The spin alignment parameter \(\H_{00}-1/3\) is related to the irreducible tensor polarization by \(\H_{00}-1/3 = -\sqrt{2/3}\,\fT_{2,0}\).
- For \(\phi\) mesons at RHIC, the observed spin alignment is of order \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-2})\), which is two orders of magnitude larger than the naive expectation from quark coalescence models (\(\mathcal{O}(10^{-4})\) assuming quark polarization comparable to \(\Lambda\) hyperon global polarization).
- The contribution to \(\H_{00}-1/3\) from the B-part (E-part) of fluid vorticity in a quark coalescence estimate is around \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-5})\) (\(\mathcal{O}(10^{-4})\)).

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach previously applied to spin-1/2 fermions (Becattini 2021, Liu 2021, Sheng 2024) to the case of massive vector bosons, providing the first complete second-order gradient calculation of vector and tensor spin polarization at local equilibrium. The results enable quantitative comparison with STAR (2022) and ALICE (2019, 2020, 2022) measurements of spin alignment for \(\phi(1020)\), \(K^{*0}(892)\), and \(J/\psi(3097)\) mesons in heavy-ion collisions, and can be implemented in hydrodynamic simulations to test whether second-order gradient contributions explain the observed large spin alignment of \(\phi\) mesons.
