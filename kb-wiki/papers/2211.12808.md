# Radial Oscillations of Dark Matter admixed Neutron Stars

> Within the relativistic mean-field model, we investigate the properties of dark matter (DM) admixed neutron stars, considering non-rotating objects made of isotropic matter. We adopt the IOPB-I hadronic equation of state (EOS) by assuming that the fermionic DM within super-symmetric models has already been accreted inside the neutron star (NS). The impact of DM on the mass-radius relationships and the radial oscillations of pulsating DM admixed neutron stars (with and without the crust) are explored. It is observed that the presence of DM softens the EOS, which in turn lowers the maximum mass and its corresponding radius. Moreover, adding DM results in higher frequencies of pulsating objects and hence we show the linearity of fundamental mode frequency of canonical NS with DM Fermi momentum. We also investigate the profile of eigenfunctions solving the Sturm-Liouville boundary value problem, and verify its validity. Further, we study the stability of NSs considering the fundamental mode frequency variation with the mass of the star, and verify the stability criterion $\partial M/\partialρ_c > 0$. Finally, the effect of the crust on the large frequency separation for different DM Fermi momenta is shown as well.

## Key claim
The presence of fermionic dark matter (DM) inside neutron stars softens the equation of state, lowering the maximum mass and increasing radial oscillation frequencies; however, the single-fluid approximation used assumes DM and baryonic matter are sufficiently coupled, which may not hold for all DM models, and the DM Fermi momentum is the only free parameter varied.

## Method
The authors adopt the IOPB-I hadronic equation of state within the extended relativistic mean-field model and add fermionic DM (neutralino with mass 200 GeV) interacting via Higgs exchange, with coupling y = 0.07 and nucleon-Higgs form factor f = 0.35. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium and then solve the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue problem for radial oscillations, computing frequencies for the fundamental f-mode and 19 excited p-modes for DM Fermi momenta from 0 to 0.05 GeV. The unified EOS includes the SLY4 crust, and the analysis compares results with and without the crust.

## Key numbers
- DM particle mass: 200 GeV; Higgs mass: 125 GeV; DM-Higgs coupling y = 0.07; nucleon-Higgs form factor f = 0.35; Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV.
- Spin-independent cross section computed: 9.70 × 10⁻⁴⁶ cm², consistent with XENON-1T, PandaX-II, and LUX limits at 90% confidence level.
- DM Fermi momentum varied from 0 to 0.05 GeV; DM density assumed ~1000 times less than baryon density.
- For k_f^DM = 0.00 GeV, critical central density ρ_crit = 2.0022 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³; for 0.03 GeV, ρ_crit = 2.2022 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³; for 0.05 GeV, ρ_crit = 2.9177 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³.
- Fundamental f-mode frequency at 1.4 M_⊙: 2.8450 kHz (k_f^DM = 0.00 GeV), 3.1841 kHz (0.03 GeV), 3.7404 kHz (0.05 GeV).
- First p-mode (n=1) frequency at 1.4 M_⊙: 5.8594 kHz (0.00 GeV), 7.1023 kHz (0.03 GeV), 9.3196 kHz (0.05 GeV).
- Maximum mass decreases with increasing DM Fermi momentum; for k_f^DM ≈ 0.03 GeV, the mass-radius curves reproduce observational data well.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of radial oscillations in neutron stars (Chandrasekhar 1964, Chanmugam 1977, Kokkotas and Ruoff 2001) by incorporating fermionic DM within the IOPB-I EOS (Kumar 2018). It builds on models of DM-admixed neutron stars (Grigorious 2017, Harish et al. 2020, 2021) and uses the single-fluid approximation justified by comparing relaxation time (~2 ms) to oscillation periods. The results enable asteroseismology to constrain DM content in neutron stars, as the linear relationship between canonical f-mode frequency and DM Fermi momentum provides a potential observational signature, and the avoided crossings in higher modes become more prominent with DM.
