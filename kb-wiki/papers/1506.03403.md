# Dynamical coupled-channels model for neutrino-induced meson productions in resonance region

> A dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model for neutrino-nucleon reactions in the resonance region is developed. Starting from the DCC model that we have previously developed through an analysis of $πN, γN\to πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ$ reaction data for $W\le 2.1$ GeV, we extend the model of the vector current to $Q^2\le$ 3.0 (GeV/$c$)$^2$ by analyzing electron-induced reaction data for both proton and neutron targets. We derive axial-current matrix elements that are related to the $πN$ interactions of the DCC model through the Partially Conserved Axial Current (PCAC) relation. Consequently, the interference pattern between resonant and non-resonant amplitudes is uniquely determined. We calculate cross sections for neutrino-induced meson productions, and compare them with available data. Our result for the single-pion production reasonably agrees with the data. We also make a comparison with the double-pion production data. Our model is the first DCC model that can give the double-pion production cross sections in the resonance region. We also make comparison of our result with other existing models to reveal an importance of testing the models in the light of PCAC and electron reaction data. The DCC model developed here will be a useful input for constructing a neutrino-nucleus reaction model and a neutrino event generator for analyses of neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The authors develop a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model for neutrino-nucleon reactions in the resonance region that is the first to give double-pion production cross sections in this region, but the axial-current matrix elements at nonzero \(Q^2\) still require a simple ansatz due to the lack of experimental information, a limitation shared by all existing models.

## Method

Starting from a previously developed DCC model that fits \(\pi N, \gamma N\to \pi N, \eta N, K\Lambda, K\Sigma\) reaction data for invariant mass \(W\le 2.1\) GeV, the authors extend the vector current to \(Q^2\le 3.0\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\) by analyzing electron-induced reaction data for both proton and neutron targets. They derive axial-current matrix elements related to the \(\pi N\) interactions through the Partially Conserved Axial Current (PCAC) relation, which uniquely determines the interference pattern between resonant and non-resonant amplitudes. The model then calculates cross sections for neutrino-induced meson productions and compares them with available bubble chamber data from Argonne National Laboratory (ANL) and Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL).

## Key numbers

- The model covers the resonance region for total hadronic energy \(W\) from \(m_N+m_\pi\) up to about 2 GeV, and momentum transfer \(Q^2\le 3.0\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\).
- The weak interaction uses \(G_F=1.16637\times 10^{-5}\) (GeV)\(^{-2}\) and \(V_{ud}=0.974\).
- The neutral-current weak mixing angle is \(\sin^2\theta_W=0.23\).
- The DCC model previously fit approximately 23,000 data points for \(\pi N, \gamma N\to \pi N, \eta N, K\Lambda, K\Sigma\) reactions.
- The single-pion production data from ANL and BNL differ by about 10%, leading to uncertainty in the axial \(N\Delta\) coupling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DCC model (Nakamura, Kamano, Sato, 2013) from hadronic and electromagnetic reactions to neutrino-induced reactions, addressing three problems in existing models: the neglect of multi-channel couplings required by unitarity, the lack of serious study of neutrino-induced double-pion production over the entire resonance region, and poor control of interference between resonant and non-resonant amplitudes for the axial current. The model enables construction of a neutrino-nucleus reaction model and neutrino event generator for analyses of experiments such as T2K, MiniBooNE, Miner\(\nu\)a, and DUNE, and provides a framework to test models against PCAC and electron reaction data.
