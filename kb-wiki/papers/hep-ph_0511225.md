# Quasiparticle model for deconfined matter and the nucleation of hadrons

> Assuming a first-order chiral transition scenario, we study the process of phase conversion driven by homogeneous nucleation. We adopt a quasiparticle model whose parameters are fit to lattice QCD data to describe the pressure at high temperature in the deconfined sector, and a bag model equation of state for pions in the low-temperature sector. We compute the critical radius and nucleation rate in the thin-wall approximation, and compare the results to the ones obtained using the bag model and the linear $σ$ model.

# Quasiparticle model for deconfined matter and the nucleation of hadrons

## Key claim

Assuming a first-order chiral transition scenario, the authors compute the critical radius and nucleation rate for hadron bubble formation using a quasiparticle model equation of state fit to lattice QCD data, finding that the nucleation process is qualitatively different from previous bag model and chiral model calculations. The results are consistent with a weakly first-order phase transition, but the analysis relies on the thin-wall approximation and requires introducing a bag constant of 156 MeV to make the pressure curves cross at the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors adopt a quasiparticle model for the deconfined phase, with thermally generated masses for quarks and gluons from hard thermal loop calculations and a running coupling, fit to reproduce lattice QCD pressure data. For the hadronic phase they use a gas of massive pions with a bag model equation of state. They construct a coarse-grained free energy and compute the critical radius and nucleation rate in the thin-wall approximation, using a surface tension of 50 MeV/fm² and a correlation length of 0.7 fm, then compare results to those from the bag model and the linear sigma model coupled to quarks.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature set to 178 MeV (with a bag constant of 156 MeV introduced to make pressure curves cross at this temperature)
- Spinodal temperature estimated at 164 MeV (0.92 times the critical temperature)
- Surface tension used in thin-wall approximation: 50 MeV/fm²
- Correlation length used in thin-wall approximation: 0.7 fm
- Critical radius for quasiparticle model is always greater than for the bag model at the same supercooling
- Nucleation rate for quasiparticle model is much smaller than for the bag model (proportional to exp(-ΔF/T) with ΔF proportional to R_c²)

## Context

This work extends the study of first-order QCD phase transition nucleation by replacing the simple bag model equation of state with a quasiparticle model that reproduces lattice data, following the approach of Peshier, Kampfer, and Soff (2002). The results are compared to the bag model calculations of Csernai and Kapusta (1992) and to chiral model results from Scavenius, Dumitru, Fraga, Lenaghan, and Jackson (2001). The finding that the quasiparticle model yields larger critical radii and smaller nucleation rates than these previous approaches suggests that the transition is more weakly first-order than earlier models indicated, which has implications for phase conversion dynamics in the early universe and heavy-ion collisions.
