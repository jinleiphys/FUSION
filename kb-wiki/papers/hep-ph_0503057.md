# Iso-curvature fluctuations through axion trapping by cosmic string wakes

> We consider wake-like density fluctuations produced by cosmic strings at the quark-hadron transition in the early universe. We show that low momentum axions which are produced through the radiation from the axionic string at an earlier stage, may get trapped inside these wakes due to delayed hadronization in these overdense regions. As the interfaces, bordering the wakes, collapse, the axions pick-up momentum from the walls and finally leave the wake regions. These axions thus can produce large scale iso-curvature fluctuations. We have calculated the detailed profile of these axionic density fluctuations and discuss its astrophysical consequences.

## Key claim

Low-momentum axions produced by axionic string radiation at earlier epochs can become trapped inside cosmic string wakes at the quark-hadron transition due to delayed hadronization in these overdense regions, and subsequently escape as the wake interfaces collapse, thereby generating large-scale iso-curvature fluctuations. The paper explicitly hedges that the analysis assumes a first-order QCD phase transition and that the detailed geometry of the collapsing interfaces is planar (sheet-like) rather than spherical, which differs from the conventional bubble-nucleation scenario studied by Hindmarsh (2003).

## Method

The author models wake-like density fluctuations produced by a straight cosmic string moving through relativistic plasma at the QCD epoch, using the shock-wave formalism of Sornborger et al. (1997) to compute the overdensity and opening angle. The evolution of the quark-gluon plasma volume fraction inside a single wake is tracked via the Friedmann equations and energy-momentum conservation, treating the wake as bounded by two parallel planar interfaces. The axion number density distribution from axionic string decay (flat in comoving momentum between scales \(k^*\) and \(\tilde{k}\)) is integrated over the wake volume, and the momentum imparted to axions by the collapsing walls is computed to determine the resulting axionic density fluctuation profile.

## Key numbers

- Axion mass at the onset of QCD transition: \(m_{aq}(T_c) = 5.3 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{eV} \times (10^{12} \, \text{GeV} / \eta_a)\)
- Axion mass in the hadron phase: \(m_{ah} = 6.04 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{eV} \times (10^{12} \, \text{GeV} / \eta_a)\)
- Time when axionic strings move freely: \(t_* \simeq 1.8 \times 10^{-21} \, \text{sec} \times (10^{12} \, \text{GeV} / \eta_a)^4\)
- Time when axions become dynamically significant: \(\tilde{t} \simeq 1.6 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{sec} \times (\eta_a / 10^{12} \, \text{GeV})^{1/3} (t_c / \text{sec})^{2/3}\)
- Density fluctuation from straight string wake: \(\delta \rho / \rho \simeq 3 \times 10^{-5}\) for string velocity \(0.9\), sound speed \(1/\sqrt{3}\), and \(G\mu \sim 10^{-6}\)
- Opening angle of straight string wake: \(\theta_w \simeq 20^\circ\)
- Density fluctuation from wiggly string segment: \(\delta \rho / \rho \simeq 4 \times 10^{-5}\) for string velocity \(0.6\)
- Opening angle of wiggly string segment wake: \(\theta_w \simeq 70^\circ\)
- Supercooling required for bubble nucleation: \(\Delta T_{sc} \equiv 1 - T_{sc}/T_c \simeq 10^{-4}\) at \(T_c = 150 \, \text{MeV}\)
- Duration of nucleation: \(\Delta t_n \simeq 10^{-5} t_H\)
- Typical wake thickness: \(d_{shk} \simeq 3 \, \text{km}\) (for horizon size \(d_H \simeq 10 \, \text{km}\) at \(T_c = 150 \, \text{MeV}\))
- Time scale for wake evolution: \(t_{shk} \simeq 20 \sin\theta_w \, t_H\)
- Number of long strings per horizon: about 15

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Layek 2004a, 2004b) on baryon inhomogeneities from cosmic string wakes at the quark-hadron transition, now applying the same planar interface geometry to axion trapping. It contrasts with the conventional spherical bubble-nucleation scenario of Witten (1984) and the axion trapping analysis of Hindmarsh (2003), who considered spherical QGP bubbles. The paper enables future study of the evolution and astrophysical consequences of large, sheet-like iso-curvature axion fluctuations, which differ geometrically from the spherical clumps previously considered.
