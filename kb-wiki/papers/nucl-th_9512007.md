# Nuclear correlation and finite interaction-range effects in high-energy $(e,e'p)$ nuclear transparency

> Nuclear transparency is calculated for high-energy, semi-inclusive $(e,e'p)$ reactions, by accounting for all orders of Glauber multiple-scattering and by using realistic finite-range $p N$ interaction and (dynamically and statistically) correlated nuclear wave functions. The nuclear correlation effect is reduced due to the $p N$ finite-range effect. The net effect is small, and depends sensitively on details of the nuclear correlations in finite nuclei, which are poorly known at present.

## Key claim

The net effect of nuclear correlations on nuclear transparency in high-energy $(e,e'p)$ reactions is small (only a few percent above the eikonal value with $\sigma_{inel}$) and becomes smaller in heavier nuclei, but the precise value depends sensitively on details of dynamical correlations in finite nuclei, which are poorly known.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear transparency $T$ for semi-inclusive $(e,e'p)$ reactions using all orders of Glauber multiple-scattering theory with realistic finite-range $pN$ interactions, incorporating both statistical (Pauli) and dynamical (Jastrow-type) nuclear correlations. They constructed correlated $A$-body nuclear densities using a product of antisymmetrized Hartree-Fock single-particle wave functions and pair correlation functions from nuclear matter in a local density approximation, then evaluated $T$ via Monte Carlo integration over all nucleon coordinates and spins for ${}^{16}$O and ${}^{40}$Ca at a proton momentum of 4.49 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For ${}^{16}$O at $p_1 = 4.49$ GeV/c, the full calculation (including both finite-range $pN$ interaction and nuclear correlations) yields $T = 0.577 \pm 0.002$.
- For ${}^{16}$O, the eikonal approximation using $\sigma_{inel}$ (zero-range, no correlation) gives $T = 0.548 \pm 0.002$ (from Table I, inferred as the baseline for comparison).
- When the Jastrow correlation functions were artificially enhanced to match variational Monte Carlo two-particle distributions, $T$ for ${}^{16}$O decreased to $0.548 \pm 0.003$, smaller than the full calculation value.
- Using a schematic short-range repulsive correlation $f_c = 1 - \exp(-(r/a)^2)$ with $a = 0.7$ fm, $T$ increased to $0.605 \pm 0.003$ for ${}^{16}$O.
- The net effect of both nuclear correlation and finite-range interaction is several percent above the eikonal $T$ with $\sigma_{inel}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of nuclear transparency that used zero-range $pN$ interactions and simple correlation models, such as those by Nikolaev (1994) and Benhar (1991), which gave widely varying correlation effects (from a few percent to 20-30 percent increase). The authors show that the finite-range $pN$ interaction reduces the correlation effect, and that the net effect is smaller than previously estimated. The results are relevant for interpreting the NE18 experiment at SLAC, which did not observe color transparency in $(e,e'p)$ reactions, and enable more reliable separation of nuclear medium effects from possible pQCD signatures.
