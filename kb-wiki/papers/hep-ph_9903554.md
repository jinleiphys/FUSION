# The angular distribution of the reaction $\barν_e + p \to e^+ + n$

> The reaction $\barν_e + p \to e^+ + n$ is very important for low-energy ($E_ν\lesssim 60$ MeV) antineutrino experiments. In this paper we calculate the positron angular distribution, which at low energies is slightly backward. We show that weak magnetism and recoil corrections have a large effect on the angular distribution, making it isotropic at about 15 MeV and slightly forward at higher energies. We also show that the behavior of the cross section and the angular distribution can be well-understood analytically for $E_ν\lesssim 60$ MeV by calculating to ${\cal O}(1/M)$, where $M$ is the nucleon mass. The correct angular distribution is useful for separating $\barν_e + p \to e^+ + n$ events from other reactions and detector backgrounds, as well as for possible localization of the source (e.g., a supernova) direction. We comment on how similar corrections appear for the lepton angular distributions in the deuteron breakup reactions $\barν_e + d \to e^+ + n + n$ and $ν_e + d \to e^- + p + p$. Finally, in the reaction $\barν_e + p \to e^+ + n$, the angular distribution of the outgoing neutrons is strongly forward-peaked, leading to a measurable separation in positron and neutron detection points, also potentially useful for rejecting backgrounds or locating the source direction.

## Key claim
The positron angular distribution in inverse neutron beta decay, which at low energies is slightly backward, becomes isotropic at about 15 MeV and slightly forward at higher energies due to weak magnetism and recoil corrections of order 1/M (where M is the nucleon mass), contrary to the naive infinite-nucleon-mass expectation of a constant backward asymmetry.

## Method
The authors calculated the differential cross section for the reaction \(\bar{\nu}_e + p \to e^+ + n\) to first order in 1/M, including weak magnetism (via the anomalous nucleon isovector magnetic moment \(f_2 = 3.706\)) and recoil corrections. They expanded the matrix element using standard V-A theory with vector coupling \(f = 1\), axial-vector coupling \(g = 1.26\), and the inner radiative correction \(\Delta^R_{inner} \simeq 0.024\), then integrated over the positron angle to obtain the average cosine \(\langle \cos\theta \rangle\) as a function of antineutrino energy. They also modified the high-energy formula of Llewellyn Smith (1972) to account for threshold effects from the neutron-proton mass difference \(\Delta = M_n - M_p\).

## Key numbers
- The zeroth-order asymmetry coefficient \(a^{(0)} = (f^2 - g^2)/(f^2 + 3g^2) \simeq -0.10\), giving \(\langle \cos\theta \rangle^{(0)} \simeq -0.034 v_e^{(0)}\) at infinite nucleon mass.
- The first-order result \(\langle \cos\theta \rangle^{(1)} \simeq -0.034 v_e^{(0)} + 2.4 (E_\nu/M)\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass; the angular distribution becomes isotropic near \(E_\nu \simeq 15\) MeV.
- The total cross section at zeroth order is \(\sigma^{(0)}_{tot} = 0.0952 \times (E_e^{(0)} p_e^{(0)} / 1\ \mathrm{MeV}^2) \times 10^{-42}\ \mathrm{cm}^2\).
- For supernova antineutrinos with a Fermi-Dirac spectrum, \(\langle \cos\theta \rangle \simeq 0.015\), 0.025, and 0.034 for temperatures \(T = 4\), 5, and 6 MeV respectively; for positrons above 30 MeV at \(T = 5\) MeV, \(\langle \cos\theta \rangle = 0.056\).
- For the LSND experiment, the expected \(\langle \cos\theta \rangle\) after experimental corrections is 0.16, compared to the measured value \(0.20 \pm 0.13\).
- For SN1987A, the expected \(\langle \cos\theta \rangle\) is \(\simeq 0\) for Kamiokande II and \(\simeq 0.08\) for IMB, while the observed values were 0.34 and 0.48 respectively.

## Context
This work extends the earlier cross-section calculations of Vogel (1984) and Fayans (1993) by providing the first detailed analytic calculation of the positron angular distribution to order 1/M, including weak magnetism and recoil. It corrects the naive infinite-mass expectation and modifies the high-energy formula of Llewellyn Smith (1972) to account for threshold effects. The results are directly applicable to reactor antineutrino experiments, supernova neutrino detection (e.g., SuperKamiokande), and oscillation searches at LSND and KARMEN. The paper also enables improved background rejection and source-direction localization for future supernova neutrino observations, and provides a potential test of the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis via measurement of the weak magnetism parameter \(f + f_2\).
