# The Coulomb interaction in Helium-3: Interplay of strong short-range and weak long-range potentials

> Quantum chromodynamics and the electroweak theory at low energies are prominent instances of the combination of a short-range and a long-range interaction. For the description of light nuclei, the large nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths produced by the strong interaction, and the reduction of the weak interaction to the Coulomb potential, play a crucial role. Helium-3 is the first bound nucleus comprised of more than one proton in which this combination of forces can be studied. We demonstrate a proper renormalization of Helium-3 using the pionless effective field theory as the formal representation of the nuclear regime as strongly interacting fermions. The theory is found consistent at leading and next-to-leading order without isospin-symmetry-breaking 3-nucleon interactions and a non-perturbative treatment of the Coulomb interaction. The conclusion highlights the significance of the regularization method since a comparison to previous work is contradictory if the difference in those methods is not considered. With a perturbative Coulomb interaction, as suggested by dimensional analysis, we find the Helium-3 system properly renormalized, too. For both treatments, renormalization-scheme independence of the effective field theory is demonstrated by regulating the potential and a variation of the associated cutoff.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Helium-3 can be properly renormalized in pionless effective field theory at leading and next-to-leading order without isospin-symmetry-breaking three-nucleon interactions, contradicting previous work that inferred such an enhancement. The authors find that both perturbative and non-perturbative treatments of the Coulomb interaction yield consistent renormalization, provided the difference in regularization methods is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors formulate the nuclear bound-state problem using the Schrodinger equation in coordinate representation with a cutoff regularization scheme, solving for two-body scattering amplitudes and three-body bound states. They implement pionless effective field theory at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), calibrating low-energy constants to neutron-proton scattering lengths and effective ranges, the proton-proton scattering length, and the triton binding energy. The Coulomb interaction is included both non-perturbatively (iterated at LO) and perturbatively (treated at NLO via distorted-wave Born approximation), with three-body wave functions obtained using the refined resonating-group method.

## Key numbers

- The proton-proton scattering length used for calibration: a^C_pp = -7.81 fm
- The critical cutoff where an additional three-body bound state enters the spectrum: Lambda_crit ~ 1230 MeV
- The cutoff range explored: from 660 MeV to 2.4 GeV
- The typical momentum scale associated with the Helium-3 bound-state pole: k_typ = sqrt(2 m_N B(^3He)/3) ~ 70 MeV
- The momentum scale below which Coulomb becomes non-perturbative: Q less than alpha m_N ~ 10 MeV
- The numerical uncertainty in the Helium-3 binding energy from the variational method: less than 0.1 MeV
- The universal ratio of consecutive three-body bound-state energies found to converge to approximately 1/515

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory analysis of Helium-3 first accomplished in 2010 by Ando et al. (2010) and others, and directly addresses the recent analysis by Vanasse (2014) which inferred an unnatural promotion of an iso-asymmetric three-nucleon operator to next-to-leading order based on cutoff dependence. The authors show that this conclusion depends on the regularization method, and that with their coordinate-space cutoff scheme, no such enhancement is needed. The results enable consistent treatment of Coulomb interactions in few-nucleon systems within pionless effective field theory and provide a framework for studying the interplay of short-range and long-range forces in systems near the unitary limit.
