# Symmetry violations in few-body reactions: old and new approaches

> As an attempt to find a way to resolve the discrepancy between calculated by traditional methods PV effects and the available set of experimental data, we analyze the methods of calculations of symmetry violating effects in few-body nuclear systems. The overview of methods of calculations of PV and TRIV effects in few-body neutron induced reactions is presented with the analysis of values of calculated parameters and their accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the traditional Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) method for calculating parity-violating (PV) effects in few-body nuclear systems cannot reliably describe available experimental data, and proposes that a "true" effective field theory (EFT) approach using AGS-type Faddeev equations may resolve this discrepancy, though this method has not yet been applied to few-body systems.

## Method

The authors review and classify four approaches for calculating PV and time-reversal invariance violating (TRIV) effects in few-body neutron-induced reactions: (1) the "true" EFT approach using AGS-type Faddeev equations with two-body weak amplitudes from EFT; (2) Faddeev equations with DDH meson-exchange potentials; (3) the "hybrid" EFT approach using potentials derived from EFT Lagrangians; and (4) a nuclear reaction approach using Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) with wave functions from the Schrodinger equation. For the nuclear reaction approach, they estimate PV asymmetry in the n + 3He -> 3H + p reaction using known resonance parameters of 4He levels and a phenomenological weak matrix element formula.

## Key numbers

* PV neutron spin rotation angles for n-d scattering: 7.68, -6.82, and -8.91 in units of 10^-9 rad-cm^-1 for three choices of DDH coupling constants (DDH "best", 4-parameter fit, and 3-parameter fit)
* Neutron spin asymmetry for n-d scattering: 8.99, -2.09, and -2.36 in units of 10^-9 for the same three DDH coupling constant choices
* Estimated PV asymmetry for thermal neutrons in n + 3He -> 3H + p reaction: alpha_PV = -(1-4) x 10^-7 using resonance parameter Set 1, and alpha_PV = -(4-8) x 10^-7 using resonance parameter Set 2
* Weak matrix element estimate for the n + 3He system: w = 0.5 eV, derived from the formula w = 2 x 10^-4 eV sqrt(D_bar) with average level spacing D_bar = 6 MeV
* For the n + 3He reaction at thermal energy: neutron wave vector k_n = 2.19 x 10^-5 fm^-1, proton wave vector k_p = 0.19 fm^-1, 3He radius R = 1.97 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of PV effects beyond the DDH approach (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1980), which has shown discrepancies with experimental data as noted by Zhu (2004), Holstein, Desplanques, and Ramsey-Musolf (2006). The paper builds on recent EFT parameterizations of PV effects by Zhu (2004) and Ramsey-Musolf (2006), and on Faddeev equation methods (Faddeev, 1960; AGS). The nuclear reaction approach enables preliminary estimates for new PV effects in few-body systems without heavy calculations, and the authors note that their estimated PV asymmetry for thermal neutrons agrees well with exact calculations by Viviani (2010).
