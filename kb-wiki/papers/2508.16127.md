# Causality and Stability in Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> The causality and stability of a relativistic hydrodynamic theory is shown to require a consensus between, either (i) newer degrees of freedom apart from the fundamental fluid fields, or (ii) a general hydrodynamic frame other than the Landau or Eckart compromising the field's first principle definition, unless the non-equilibrium derivative correction goes to infinity. Any finitely truncated derivative correction (no matter how high it is) is shown to lead to an acausal theory, unless the corrections are infinitely summed up to include all orders. From an underlying microscopic theory, an exact form of relativistic hydrodynamics has been derived which establishes that the resummation of all order temporal derivatives is essential for causality, which finally 'integrated in' as newer degrees of freedom.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a relativistic hydrodynamic theory cannot simultaneously satisfy three desirable features (causality and stability, definition solely in terms of fluid variables, and a finite number of derivative corrections) unless one of these features is abandoned; any finitely truncated derivative expansion, regardless of its order, inevitably produces acausal propagation, and only an all-order resummation of temporal derivatives (equivalent to introducing new degrees of freedom) restores causality.

## Method
The author begins by expressing the BDNK energy-momentum tensor in terms of Landau-frame variables via a field redefinition, which generates an infinite series of gradient corrections. This infinite series is then recast as a nested set of finitely truncated equations by introducing new non-fluid variables, analogous to the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) approach. The acausality of any finite-order truncation is proven by rewriting the MIS shear viscosity equation as a sum of temporal derivatives and showing that the causality condition Im(ω(k)) ≤ |Im(k)| is violated for any finite N, holding only in the N→∞ limit. Finally, a microscopic derivation from kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation yields an all-order differential equation for the shear stress, which is then expanded into an infinite hierarchy of new degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers
- The causality condition used is Im(ω(k)) ≤ |Im(k)|, and it is satisfied only for the all-order theory (N→∞) with the condition τ_π > η/(ε+P).
- In the kinetic theory derivation, the relaxation time τ_R appears in the all-order differential equation for the shear stress π^{αβ}, with the first equation given by (1+τ_R D)π^{αβ} = 2ησ^{αβ} + ρ_1^{αβ}.
- The second-level equation for ρ_1^{αβ} contains coefficients 2/7 and 12/35 multiplying gradient terms, and the third-level equation for ρ_2^{αβ} contains coefficients 2/21 and 32/105.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate about causality and stability in relativistic hydrodynamics, directly addressing the Navier-Stokes theory (Landau and Eckart, 1950s) which is acausal and unstable, the Muller-Israel-Stewart theory (Muller, 1967; Israel and Stewart, 1976) which introduces new degrees of freedom to restore causality, and the BDNK theory (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun, 2018) which achieves causality by abandoning the Landau or Eckart frame definition. The paper bridges these approaches by showing that the BDNK field redefinition generates an infinite gradient series equivalent to the MIS hierarchy, and that the MIS hierarchy itself arises naturally from kinetic theory. This enables a unified understanding of how new degrees of freedom emerge from microscopic theory and clarifies why finite-order truncations are fundamentally insufficient for causal relativistic hydrodynamics.
