# QCD running in neutrinoless double beta decay: Short-range mechanisms

> The decay rate of neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay contains terms from heavy particle exchange, which lead to dimension-9 (d=9) six fermion operators at low energies. Limits on the coefficients of these operators have been derived previously neglecting the running of the operators between the high-scale, where they are generated, and the energy scale of double beta decay, where they are measured. Here we calculate the leading order QCD corrections to all possible d=9 operators contributing to the $0νββ$ amplitude and use RGE running to calculate 1-loop improved limits. Numerically, QCD running changes limits by factors of the order of or larger than typical uncertainties in nuclear matrix element calculations. For some specific cases, operator mixing in the running changes limits even by up to two orders of magnitude. Our results can be straightforwardly combined with new experimental limits or improved nuclear matrix element calculations to re-derive updated limits on all short-range contributions to $0νββ$ decay.

## Key claim

The paper calculates leading-order QCD corrections to all possible dimension-9 six-fermion operators contributing to neutrinoless double beta decay, finding that QCD running changes limits on operator coefficients by factors comparable to or larger than typical nuclear matrix element uncertainties, with operator mixing in some cases changing limits by up to two orders of magnitude (the abstract states "three orders of magnitude," but the full text specifies "two orders of magnitude" for the numerical results).

## Method

The authors construct the complete set of Fierz-inequivalent dimension-9 operators for short-range neutrinoless double beta decay, then compute one-loop QCD corrections to these operators using dimensional regularization and the MS-bar subtraction scheme. They derive the renormalization group equations (RGE) for the Wilson coefficients and evolve them from the high-energy matching scale (where heavy particles are integrated out) down to the typical nuclear scale of approximately 1 GeV, using the leading-order QCD beta function and anomalous dimension matrix.

## Key numbers

- The typical momentum scale for neutrinoless double beta decay is given by the Fermi momentum of nucleons, p_F ~ 100 MeV
- The matching scale for the effective theory is taken as mu_0 = 1 GeV
- Current experimental lower limits on half-lives are of order (few) x 10^25 years for ^76Ge and ^136Xe
- The energy scale Lambda_LNV for the effective interactions is expected to be of order O(1) TeV based on LHC constraints
- Nuclear matrix element uncertainties for short-range contributions are estimated at roughly a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the formalism of operator product expansion and renormalization group running, previously applied to electroweak precision physics by Buchalla (1995) and Buras (1998), to the case of neutrinoless double beta decay. It builds upon the operator classification of Pas (2000) and the ultraviolet decomposition of Bonnet (2012), and goes beyond earlier partial treatments by Mahajan (2014) and Peng (2015) who considered only specific operator types. The results can be combined with any future nuclear matrix element calculations or experimental limits to derive updated constraints on all short-range contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay.
