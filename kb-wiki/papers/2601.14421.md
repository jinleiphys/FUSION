# Thermodynamics of ideal spin fluids and pseudo-gauge ambiguity

> Conserved currents of relativistic spin fluids derived from microscopic models are known to violate local thermodynamic relations. We present a systematic analysis of pseudo-gauge improvements in ideal spin hydrodynamics and identify a family of pseudo-gauges where standard thermodynamic relations are satisfied. We quantify pseudo-gauge ambiguities in the spin equation of state and derive universal thermodynamic relations that apply to conserved currents in any pseudo-gauge. As an application, we extract the thermodynamic variables and equations of state for free Dirac fermions and scalar fields.

## Key claim
Conserved currents of relativistic spin fluids derived from microscopic models can be made to satisfy standard local thermodynamic relations by choosing an appropriate pseudo-gauge, but the resulting thermodynamic variables (including the spin equation of state) generically inherit pseudo-gauge ambiguities themselves; a notable exception occurs in conformal (scale-invariant) theories where symmetry constraints eliminate this ambiguity.

## Method
The authors perform a systematic perturbative analysis of pseudo-gauge transformations in ideal spin hydrodynamics, working to quartic order in spin. They identify a family of "thermodynamic pseudo-gauges" defined by linear partial differential equations that enforce standard thermodynamic relations on the conserved currents. They then apply this framework to extract thermodynamic variables and equations of state for free Dirac fermions and scalar fields, using previously computed microscopic currents from Palermo (2021), Palermo (2023), Ambrus (2014), Ambrus (2019), and Buzzegoli (2017).

## Context
This work extends the spin hydrodynamics framework developed by Gallegos (2021), Florkowski (2017), and Hattori (2019), and addresses the apparent contradiction between microscopic calculations of conserved currents and the coarse-grained spin hydrodynamics perspective raised by Becattini (2023, 2025). The authors resolve the mismatch for free Dirac fermions and scalar fields, and identify pseudo-gauge-independent thermodynamic invariants that can be used to compare different representations of the spin equation of state. The results enable future work on constraining the equation of state ambiguity in non-conformal spin fluids through stability and causality considerations, and suggest exploring action-based approaches to spin hydrodynamics following Grozdanov (2013) and Crossley (2015).
