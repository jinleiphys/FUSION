# Isospin mixing in the nucleon and 4He and the nucleon strange electric form factor

> In order to isolate the contribution of the nucleon strange electric form factor to the parity-violating asymmetry measured in 4He(\vec e,e')4He experiments, it is crucial to have a reliable estimate of the magnitude of isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) corrections in both the nucleon and 4He. We examine this issue in the present letter. Isospin admixtures in the nucleon are determined in chiral perturbation theory, while those in 4He are derived from nuclear interactions, including explicit ISB terms. A careful analysis of the model dependence in the resulting predictions for the nucleon and nuclear ISB contributions to the asymmetry is carried out. We conclude that, at the low momentum transfers of interest in recent measurements reported by the HAPPEX collaboration at Jefferson Lab, these contributions are of comparable magnitude to those associated with strangeness components in the nucleon electric form factor.

## Key claim

At the low momentum transfers of interest in the HAPPEX measurements (Q² = 0.077 (GeV/c)²), the isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) corrections in both the nucleon and ⁴He are of comparable magnitude to the contributions from strange-quark components in the nucleon electric form factor, meaning that the parity-violating asymmetry cannot be interpreted as a clean extraction of strangeness without accounting for these ISB effects.

## Method

The authors computed the isospin-violating nucleon form factor G_E^{slashed 1}(Q²) using a leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculation combined with resonance saturation estimates for low-energy constants. For the nuclear ISB corrections, they used the Hyperspherical Harmonic expansion method to compute ⁴He wave functions from four different Hamiltonian models (AV18, AV18/UIX, CDB/UIXb, and N3LO) that include explicit ISB terms in the nucleon-nucleon potentials, and then evaluated the ratio of the isovector to isoscalar nuclear form factors F^(1)(q)/F^(0)(q).

## Key numbers

- G_E^{slashed 1}(Q² = 0.077 (GeV/c)²) = -0.0017 ± 0.0006, determined from chiral perturbation theory with resonance saturation
- The nucleon ISB contribution to the asymmetry: -2 G_E^{slashed 1} / [(G_E^p + G_E^n)/2] = 0.008 ± 0.003 at Q² = 0.077 (GeV/c)², using G_E^p = 0.799 and G_E^n = 0.027
- The ratio F^(1)(q)/F^(0)(q) at Q² = 0.077 (GeV/c)² is approximately -0.002, with the T=1 probability in the ⁴He ground state ranging from 0.0020% to 0.0035% across the four Hamiltonian models
- The measured PV asymmetry from HAPPEX: A_PV = [+6.40 ± 0.23 (stat) ± 0.12 (syst)] ppm at Q² = 0.077 (GeV/c)², leading to Γ = 0.010 ± 0.038
- Extracted strange electric form factor: G_E^s[Q² = 0.077 (GeV/c)²] = -0.001 ± 0.016, using the nuclear ISB value F^(1)/F^(0) ≈ -0.00157 from models including three-nucleon potentials

## Context

This work extends the earlier approximate calculation of nuclear ISB corrections by Ramavataran (1994), which used first-order perturbation theory with only Coulomb-induced isospin mixing and found |F^(1)/F^(0)| ≈ 0.0014. The present calculation uses modern realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, chiral N3LO) with explicit ISB terms and non-perturbative wave functions, finding that the Coulomb interaction accounts for only about 50% of the T=1 probability. The results enable future experiments to extract the nucleon strange electric form factor G_E^s from ⁴He parity-violating asymmetry measurements, provided experimental accuracy improves by about an order of magnitude; a lattice QCD estimate by Leinweber (2006) gives G_E^s[0.1 (GeV/c)²] = +0.001 ± 0.004 ± 0.003.
