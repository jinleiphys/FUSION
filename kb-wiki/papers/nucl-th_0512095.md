# Relativistic Mean Field Models at High Densities

> The effects of vector-isovector terms predicted by relativistic mean field models based on effective field theory on the equation of state and the neutrino mean free path in neutron stars have been studied. Using a procedure similar to Ref. [1] in treating the isovector sector, a parameter set (G2*) that predicts a much smaller proton fraction than the standard one (G2) can be obtained. We found that the G2* has a softer equation of state compared to the G2 at higher densities and the disappearance of the anomalous behavior of the neutrino mean free path in neutron star does not depend on the proton fraction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adjusting the vector-isovector channel of the ERMF model (producing parameter set G2*) yields a much smaller proton fraction in neutron stars than the standard G2 set, but this adjustment does not affect the disappearance of the anomalous behavior of the neutrino mean free path, which instead depends on the nucleon effective mass at high densities.

## Method

The authors modified the isovector-vector coupling constants of the ERMF model following the prescription of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001), producing a new parameter set G2* with a larger coupling constant \( \eta_{\rho} = 4.490 \) compared to G2's \( \eta_{\rho} = 0.390 \). They then calculated the symmetry energy, proton fraction, equation of state, and neutrino mean free path for neutron star matter (composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) over baryon densities from \( 1\rho_0 \) to \( 5\rho_0 \), using chemical potential equilibrium and charge neutrality at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- G2* has a threshold density for the direct URCA process of \( \rho_B \approx 2.5 \rho_0 \), higher than that of G2.
- Both G2 and G2* have nucleon effective mass \( M^* \sim 0.6 M \) at nuclear saturation density \( \rho_0 \).
- The coupling constant \( \eta_{\rho} \) is 0.390 for G2 and 4.490 for G2*.
- The scalar meson mass ratio \( m_S/M \) is 0.554 for both G2 and G2*.

## Context

This work extends the ERMF model of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1996), which accurately predicts finite nuclei properties and yields a soft equation of state at high densities consistent with calculations by Akmal et al. (1998), Li (2000), and Baldo (2000), but still predicts too large a proton fraction. The authors follow the isovector adjustment procedure of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) to achieve a softer symmetry energy at high densities, enabling better agreement with prior calculations of proton fraction and direct URCA thresholds, while clarifying that the anomalous neutrino mean free path behavior is controlled by the effective mass, not the proton fraction.
