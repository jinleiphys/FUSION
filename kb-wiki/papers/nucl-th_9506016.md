# Strangeness in Hadronic Stellar Matter

> We examine the presence of strangeness-bearing components, hyperons and kaons, in dense neutron star matter. Calculations are performed using relativistic mean field models, in which both the baryon-baryon and kaon-baryon interactions are mediated by meson exchange. Results of kaon condensation are found to be qualitatively similar to previous work with chiral models, if compatibility of the kaon optical potentials is required. The presence of strangeness, be it in the form of hyperons or kaons, implies a reduction in the maximum mass and a relatively large number of protons, sufficient to allow rapid cooling to take place. The need to improve upon the poorly-known couplings of the strange particles, which determine the composition and structure of neutron stars, is stressed. We also discuss generic problems with effective masses in mean field theories.

## Key claim

The presence of hyperons in dense neutron star matter raises the threshold density for kaon condensation and decisively reduces the maximum stellar mass, such that the additional softening from a kaon condensate becomes a minor effect (mass reduction of only about 0.01 solar masses) when hyperons are already present.

## Method

The authors use relativistic mean field (Walecka-type) models for baryon-baryon interactions, including all charge states of the baryon octet, and a meson-exchange Lagrangian for kaon-baryon interactions mediated by sigma, omega, and rho mesons. They solve the mean field equations at zero temperature for cold catalyzed neutron star matter in beta-equilibrium, computing particle fractions, equation of state, and neutron star masses for cases with and without hyperons and kaon condensation.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for kaon condensation in nucleons-only matter: 2.6 times nuclear saturation density (n0) for parameter set GM2
- Maximum condensate amplitude in nucleons-only matter: approximately 40 degrees
- Maximum condensate amplitude in matter with hyperons: approximately 20 degrees
- Reduction in maximum neutron star mass from kaons in nucleons-only matter: 4-10%
- Reduction in maximum neutron star mass from kaons in hyperonic matter: about 0.01 solar masses
- Reduction in maximum neutron star mass from hyperons alone: approximately 0.5 solar masses
- Kaon optical potential depth from phase shift analysis: -200 ± 20 MeV
- Strangeness-to-baryon ratio in hyperonic matter at highest density: approximately 0.6

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Glendenning (1985) and Ellis et al. (1991) on hyperons in neutron stars, and by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) on kaon condensation, using a meson-exchange framework that is more consistent with the Walecka-type baryon model than the chiral Kaplan-Nelson Lagrangian used in prior work by the same authors (Ellis et al., 1995). The results enable improved modeling of neutron star cooling via direct Urca processes and the possibility of metastable protoneutron stars collapsing to black holes, while stressing the need for better experimental constraints on hyperon and kaon coupling constants.
