# Neutron Fermi Liquids under the presence of a strong magnetic field with effective nuclear forces

> Landau's Fermi Liquid parameters are calculated for non-superfluid pure neutron matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field at zero temperature. The particle-hole interactions in the system, where a net magnetization may be present, are characterized by these parameters in the framework of a multipolar formalism. We use either zero- or finite-range effective nuclear forces to describe the nuclear interaction. Using the obtained Fermi Liquid parameters, the effect of a strong magnetic field on some bulk magnitudes such as isothermal compressibility and spin susceptibility is also investigated.

# Neutron Fermi Liquids under the presence of a strong magnetic field with effective nuclear forces

## Key claim

The variation of Landau Fermi Liquid parameters for pure neutron matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field remains very mild (below approximately 20% for the maximum field strength studied) because the polarization induced by even the strongest astrophysically relevant magnetic fields is small in the density range considered (0.5 to 3.3 times nuclear saturation density). The paper explicitly notes that for magnetic field strengths below about 10^16 G, the change in the Landau ratios is negligible due to the tiny value of the neutron magnetic moment.

## Method

The authors extend Landau Fermi Liquid Theory to a two-component system with spin-up and spin-down neutron populations, using a multipolar expansion of particle-hole interaction matrix elements up to dipolar order (l=0,1). They derive analytical expressions for the monopolar and dipolar Landau parameters using two effective nuclear interactions: the zero-range Skyrme SLy7 force and the finite-range Gogny D1P force, with the induced magnetization at each thermodynamic condition obtained by minimizing the Helmholtz free energy within a Hartree-Fock calculation.

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field strength considered: B = 10^18 G
- Density range considered: 0.5 ρ₀ to 3.3 ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.1737 fm⁻³ (for the Gogny D1P parametrization)
- For the Skyrme SLy7 interaction at saturation density ρ₀, the relative variation of spin up and down compressibility components at maximum field strength is 20%
- For the Gogny D1P interaction at saturation density ρ₀, the relative variation of spin up and down compressibility components at maximum field strength is 28%
- The ferromagnetic transition for the Skyrme SLy7 parametrization occurs at a density around ρ = 3.3 ρ₀
- For the maximum field B = 10^18 G, a polarization Δ_min < 40% is allowed for densities 0.5 < ρ/ρ₀ < 3.3 at zero temperature
- The variation ratios of Landau coefficients remain below |R| ≈ 20% for the maximum magnetic field strength B ≈ 10^18 G
- Magnetar surface magnetic field strengths are approximately B_magnetar ≈ 10^14 - 10^15 G

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Hartree-Fock study of magnetized neutron matter (Perez-Garcia, Navarro, Polls, 2008) by calculating the Landau parameters that characterize quasiparticle interactions. It builds on prior Fermi Liquid Theory calculations for nuclear and neutron matter using realistic interactions by Di (1987), Baldo (1994), and Lombardo (2003), as well as effective interaction calculations for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter by Ventura and collaborators and Navarro and collaborators. The results enable future calculation of response functions for magnetized neutron matter using random phase approximation methods, which are relevant for understanding neutrino opacities in neutron star interiors and the properties of pasta phases in the outer crust.
