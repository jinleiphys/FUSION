# Dissociation of a Heavy Quarkonium at High Temperatures

> We examine three different ways a heavy quarkonium can dissociate at high temperatures. The heavy quarkonium can dissociate spontaneously when it becomes unbound at a temperature above its dissociation temperature. Following the recent work of Digal, Petreczky, and Satz, we calculate the dissociation temperatures of heavy quarkonia taking into account the angular momentum selection rules and using a temperature-dependent potential inferred from lattice gauge calculations. We find that the selection rules change the dissociation temperatures substantially for charmonia but only slightly for bottomia. A quarkonium system in thermal equilibrium with the medium can dissociate by thermalization. The fraction of quarkonium lying above the dissociation threshold increases as temperature increases. A quarkonium can also dissociate by colliding with light hadrons. We evaluate the cross sections for the dissociation of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ in collision with $π$ as a function of the temperature of the hadron medium, using the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson. We find that as the temperature increases, the threshold energy decreases and the dissociation cross section increases.

## Key claim

The dissociation temperatures of heavy quarkonia depend substantially on angular momentum selection rules for charmonia (shifting them upward) but only slightly for bottomia, and the specific values are sensitive to the choice of potential and inclusion of spin-dependent interactions, unlike the simpler treatment of Digal, Petreczky, and Satz (2001).

## Method

The author solves the Schrodinger equation for heavy quarkonium states using a temperature-dependent Q-Qbar potential extracted from lattice gauge calculations of Karsch et al. (2000), represented as a Yukawa plus exponential potential with a temperature-dependent string tension. Spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions are included, and the dissociation thresholds are computed by applying parity and angular momentum selection rules for the allowed final-state meson pairs (D/D* for charmonia, B/B* for bottomia). The dissociation temperatures are found from the intercepts of the state energies with the threshold energies as functions of temperature.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Digal, Petreczky, and Satz (2001) on heavy quarkonium dissociation temperatures by incorporating angular momentum selection rules and a different temperature-dependent potential, finding that selection rules raise dissociation temperatures substantially for charmonia (e.g., psi' from 0.1-0.2 to 0.50 T_c) but only slightly for bottomia. The paper also introduces two additional dissociation mechanisms beyond spontaneous dissociation: dissociation by thermalization (following the approach of Kharzeev, McLerran, and Satz (1995) but with temperature-dependent thresholds) and dissociation by collision with light hadrons using the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson (1992). These results enable more precise predictions for quarkonium suppression as a signal of quark-gluon plasma formation in heavy-ion collisions.
