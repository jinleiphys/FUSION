# Ultrarelativistic transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis statistics

> The analytical expressions for the ultrarelativistic transverse momentum distributions of the Tsallis and the Tsallis-$2$ statistics were obtained. We found that the transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis-factorized statistics, which is now largely used to describe the experimental transverse momentum spectra of hadrons measured in $pp$ collisions at LHC and RHIC energies, in the ultrarelativistic case is not equivalent to the transverse momentum distributions of the Tsallis and the Tsallis-$2$ statistics. However, we revealed that this distribution exactly coincides with the transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis-$2$ statistics in the zeroth term approximation and is transformed to the transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis statistics in the zeroth term approximation by changing the parameter $q$ to $1/q_{c}$. We demonstrated analytically on the basis of the ultrarelativistic ideal gas that the Tsallis-factorized statistics is not equivalent to the Tsallis and the Tsallis-$2$ statistics. In the present paper the Tsallis statistics corresponds to the standard expectation values.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the widely used Tsallis-factorized transverse momentum distribution for hadrons in high-energy collisions is not equivalent to the full Tsallis statistics distribution, but coincides exactly with the zeroth term approximation of the Tsallis-2 statistics and can be transformed into the zeroth term approximation of the Tsallis statistics by changing the parameter \(q\) to \(1/q_c\).

## Method

The author derived analytical expressions for the ultrarelativistic transverse momentum distributions of the Tsallis statistics (using standard expectation values) and the Tsallis-2 statistics (using generalized expectation values) for Maxwell-Boltzmann particles in the grand canonical ensemble. The sums over occupation numbers were evaluated using integral representations of the Gamma function, and a cut-off prescription was introduced for the case \(q<1\) to handle divergences. The resulting distributions were compared analytically to the Tsallis-factorized statistics distribution from Cleymans (2012).

## Key numbers

* For \(\pi^{-}\) pions at \(T=100\) MeV, radius \(R=4\) fm, and \(\mu=0\), the mean number of particles \(\langle N \rangle\) for the Tsallis statistics is 7.2 for \(q=0.99\), 4.9 for \(q=0.995\), 3.5 for \(q=1.0\), 2.9 for \(q=1.005\), and 2.6 for \(q=1.01\).
* The cut-off parameter \(N_0\) in the sum for the Tsallis statistics at \(q<1\) is 0 for \(q=0.9\), 1 for \(q=0.95\), 7 for \(q=0.99\), 16 for \(q=0.995\), and 82 for \(q=0.999\), at \(T=100\) MeV, \(R=4\) fm, and \(\mu=0\).
* The Tsallis statistics in the zeroth term approximation requires the condition \(q>3/4\) for the energy to have a finite value.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation of Tsallis statistics in high-energy physics by clarifying the relationship between the full Tsallis statistics (Parvan 2006a, 2006b, 2015, 2015a) and the Tsallis-factorized statistics used by Cleymans (2012) and others to fit LHC and RHIC data. The paper shows that the commonly used factorized form corresponds only to the zeroth term approximation of the full Tsallis statistics, which is not thermodynamically consistent in the Gibbs limit. This enables future work to use the full Tsallis distribution (Parvan 2016) for more accurate descriptions of hadron spectra, particularly for \(q<1\) where the factorized form may be insufficient.
