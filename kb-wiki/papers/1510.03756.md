# Surface corrections to the moment of inertia and shell structure in finite Fermi systems

> The moment of inertia for nuclear collective rotations is derived within a semiclassical approach based on the Inglis cranking and Strutinsky shell-correction methods, improved by surface corrections within the nonperturbative periodic-orbit theory. For adiabatic (statistical-equilibrium) rotations it was approximated by the generalized rigid-body moment of inertia accounting for the shell corrections of the particle density. An improved phase-space trace formula allows to express the shell components of the moment of inertia more accurately in terms of the free-energy shell correction. Evaluating their ratio within the extended Thomas-Fermi effective-surface approximation, one finds good agreement with the quantum calculations.

## Key claim

The paper derives a semiclassical expression for the nuclear moment of inertia shell correction in terms of the free-energy shell correction, improved by including \(\hbar^2\) surface corrections within the extended Thomas-Fermi effective-surface approximation; the authors find good agreement with quantum calculations for the spheroidal cavity, but note that the results have restricted value for real nuclei because spin-orbit splitting and pairing correlations are neglected.

## Method

The authors start from the Inglis cranking model and Strutinsky shell-correction method, then apply the nonperturbative Gutzwiller periodic-orbit theory to obtain a phase-space trace formula for the moment of inertia shell component \(\delta\Theta\) in terms of the free-energy shell correction \(\delta F\). They evaluate the phase-space average \(\langle r_\perp^2/\varepsilon\rangle\) using the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method up to order \(\hbar^2\), expressing surface corrections through gradients of the Thomas-Fermi particle density in the effective-surface (ES) approximation. The resulting analytical expressions are applied to a spheroidal-cavity mean field and compared with quantum-mechanical calculations.

## Key numbers

- Surface energy constant \(b_{\rm S} \approx 20\) MeV used in the ETF average for \(\langle r_\perp^2/\varepsilon\rangle\).
- Surface corrections to the moment of inertia scale as \(N^{-1/3}\) from Eq. (Estf).
- The classical rigid-body (Thomas-Fermi) moment of inertia is \(\Theta_{\rm TF} = m (a^2+b^2) N/5\).
- The surface energy is \(E_{\rm S} = \sigma \mathcal{S} = b_{\rm S} N^{2/3}\).
- The surface-tension coefficient is \(\sigma = (\hbar \rho_\infty / 36) \sqrt{K/m}\).
- The diffuseness parameter is \(a = \sqrt{\hbar^2/(4mK)}\).
- The solution for the dimensionless particle density is \(w(x) = [1 + \exp(\zeta x)]^{-1}\) with \(w_0 = 1/2\) at the effective surface.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical periodic-orbit theory approach to the moment of inertia developed by Magner et al. (2010) and Gorpinchenko et al. (2015) by including \(\hbar^2\) surface corrections within the ETF effective-surface approximation, following the method of Brack et al. (1985) and the leptodermous expansion of Strutinsky and Magner. The results enable more accurate semiclassical calculations of nuclear moment of inertia shell corrections for larger deformations (\(\eta \sim 1.5-2.0\)) where bifurcations of periodic orbits dominate, and can be directly applied to magnetic susceptibility in metallic clusters and quantum dots. The authors note that future work should include neutron-proton asymmetry, spin-orbit splitting, and pairing correlations for realistic nuclear applications.
