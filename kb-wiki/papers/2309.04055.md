# Artificial first-order phase transition in a magnetized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with a quark anomalous magnetic moment

> Recently, first-order phase transitions have been predicted as an effect of the inclusion of quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) in the hot and magnetized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model (NJL). These transitions appear in the chiral condensate for different combinations of AMM and magnetic fields and could lead to inverse magnetic catalysis. However, in this work, we show that the predicted first-order phase transitions are related to regularization-dependent issues. To show this, we explore, in the context of the vacuum magnetic regularization (VMR) scheme, two different scenarios: when mass-dependent (MD) and mass-independent (MI) terms are present in the subtraction of the divergences. In the MD case, as we increase the AMM value, it is observed the appearance of a nonmassive minimum in the thermodynamical potential, which induces a first-order phase transition from the massive minimum. We argue that the MD terms must be avoided in order to satisfy the predictions of Lattice QCD, and we propose a MI solution that is valid in the limit which the magnetic fields are smaller than the squared of vacuum effective quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that previously reported first-order phase transitions in the chiral condensate of the magnetized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) are artifacts of regularization, specifically arising from mass-dependent divergent terms in the thermodynamic potential that must be subtracted. The authors argue that a mass-independent regularization scheme eliminates these artificial transitions and restores consistency with Lattice QCD predictions.

## Method

The authors apply the vacuum magnetic regularization (VMR) scheme to the mean-field thermodynamic potential of the two-flavor NJL model with a constant quark AMM at zero temperature. They identify mass-dependent (MD) terms proportional to O(eB²) in the Taylor expansion of the ultraviolet regime of the thermodynamic potential, and compare two regularization scenarios: one retaining these MD terms and one where they are eliminated by taking the limit |q_feB|/M₀² → 0, which yields the mass-independent (MI) Schwinger-Weisskopf form used by Dittrich (1977) and Farias (2021).

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters used are: cutoff Λ = 886.62 MeV, bare quark mass m₀ = 7.383 MeV, coupling G = 4.001/Λ², reproducing ⟨ūu⟩¹/³ = -220 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV, and f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- For a fixed magnetic field eB = 0.3 GeV², increasing the quark AMM parameter κ_f causes the thermodynamic potential minimum at M = 0 to become lower than the massive minimum at M ≈ 0.3 GeV, producing an artificial first-order phase transition in the MD regularization.
- The fine-structure constant used is α_e = 1/137, and the Bohr magneton is μ_B = e/(2M₀) where M₀ is the vacuum effective quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of regularization artifacts in the NJL model previously studied by Avancini (2019, 2020) for the zero-AMM case, and directly contradicts predictions of first-order phase transitions reported by Fayazbakhsh (2014) and Kawaguchi (2022) using non-MFIR methods. The authors show that the mass-independent regularization scheme, which follows the approach of Dittrich (1977) and Farias (2021), eliminates these transitions and brings the model into agreement with Lattice QCD results from Bali (2012). This enables reliable studies of the QCD phase diagram with quark AMM in the regime where magnetic fields are smaller than the squared vacuum effective quark mass.
