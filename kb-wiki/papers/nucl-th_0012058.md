# Single photons from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS, QGP vs. hadronic gas

> In a hydrodynamic model, we have analysed the direct photon data obtained by the WA98 collaboration in 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS. The transverse expansion of the system was taken into account. Two scenarios (i) formation of quark-gluon plasma and (ii) formation of hot hadronic gas were considered. Equally well description to the data were obtained in both the scenarios. However, hadronic gas scenario require very high initial temperature ($\sim$ 300 MeV) and it is difficult to conceive existence of hadron gas at that high temperature. If the hadronic fluid has small radial velocity (0.2c-0.3c) initially, the data are well explained in the hadronic gas scenario with reasonable initial temperatures.

## Key claim

Both a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) scenario and a hot hadronic gas scenario describe the WA98 single photon data equally well, but the hadronic gas scenario requires an unphysically high initial temperature of about 300 MeV unless the hadronic fluid is given a small initial radial velocity of 0.2c-0.3c, in which case the data can be explained with reasonable initial temperatures.

## Method

The author solved 3+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations with cylindrical symmetry and boost-invariance, using a Woods-Saxon initial energy density profile. Two equations of state were used: one for a QGP with a bag constant and one for a hadronic gas comprising hadrons with mass less than 2 GeV. Photon production rates included one-loop and two-loop QGP rates (the latter corrected for an overestimate by a factor of 4) and hadronic processes such as pi pi -> rho gamma and pi rho -> pi gamma. The initial temperature was fixed from the observed pion multiplicity (dN/dY = 750) via entropy conservation.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature of QGP for thermalisation time tau_i = 0.2 fm: 341 MeV; for tau_i = 0.6 fm: 237 MeV.
- Initial temperature of hadronic gas for tau_i = 0.2 fm: 304 MeV; for tau_i = 0.6 fm: 211 MeV.
- Hadron density at tau_i = 0.2 fm: 10.41 fm^{-3}; at tau_i = 0.6 fm: 1.32 fm^{-3}.
- Initial radial velocity range that allows a good fit in the hadronic gas scenario: 0.2c to 0.3c.
- The two-loop QGP photon rate was overestimated by a factor of 4 in earlier work (Aurenche 1998); after correction, the yield is reduced by a factor of 2 compared to the uncorrected rate.
- For tau_i = 0.6 fm and T_i = 211 MeV, a good fit to data is obtained with v_r^{ini} = 0.3c.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of WA80 and WA98 single photon data by Srivastava and Sinha (2001), Alam et al (2001), and Peressounko et al (2000), who had reached varying conclusions about whether QGP formation was required. The author shows that the WA98 data cannot discriminate between QGP and hadronic gas scenarios, contradicting claims that the data indicate QGP formation only. The analysis enables future work at RHIC and LHC energies where data may become sensitive to the details of the equation of state and initial conditions.
