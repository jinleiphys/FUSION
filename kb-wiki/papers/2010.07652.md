# Proton and neutron skins and symmetry energy of mirror nuclei

> The neutron skin of nuclei is an important fundamental property, but its accurate measurement faces many challenges. Inspired by charge symmetry of nuclear forces, the neutron skin of a neutron-rich nucleus is related to the difference between the charge radii of the corresponding mirror nuclei. We investigate this relation within the framework of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with Skyrme interactions. Predictions for proton skins are also made for several mirror pairs in the middle mass range. For the first time the correlation between the thickness of the neutron skin and the characteristics related with the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy is investigated simultaneously for nuclei and their corresponding mirror partners. As an example, the Ni isotopic chain with mass number $A=48-60$ is considered. These quantities are calculated within the coherent density fluctuation model using Brueckner and Skyrme energy-density functionals for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter with two Skyrme-type effective interactions, SkM* and SLy4. Results are also presented for the symmetry energy as a function of $A$ for a family of mirror pairs from selected chains of nuclei with $Z=20$, $N=14$, and $N=50$. The evolution curves show a similar behavior crossing at the $N=Z$ nucleus in each chain and a smooth growing deviation when $N\neq Z$ starts. Comparison of our results for the radii and skins with those from the calculations based on high-precision chiral forces is made.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of a neutron-rich nucleus can be obtained from the difference between the proton radii of the corresponding mirror nuclei, provided Coulomb effects are properly accounted for; this relation is approximately linear across families of mirror pairs, with a slope less than unity due to Coulomb repulsion.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the HFBTHO code with Skyrme-type effective interactions SkM* and SLy4 to obtain nuclear densities and radii for mirror nuclei. The symmetry energy and related equation-of-state parameters (pressure, asymmetric compressibility) for finite nuclei were then computed within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), using both Brueckner and Skyrme energy-density functionals for infinite nuclear matter weighted by the HFB density distributions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca: 0.155 fm (SkM*) and 0.154 fm (SLy4), consistent with the predicted value of 0.159 fm using the Gogny-D1S HFB method and with ab initio chiral effective field theory values between 0.14 fm and 0.20 fm.
- Linear fit parameters for the relation $\Delta R_n = c(\Delta R_{mirr}) + d$ across four chains: for SkM* force, $c = 0.866 \pm 0.037$, $d = -0.0633 \pm 0.0041$; for SLy4 force, $c = 0.862 \pm 0.042$, $d = -0.0575 \pm 0.0041$.
- Proton skin of $^{16}$Ne: 0.378 fm (SLy4) and 0.366 fm (SkM*), compared to $0.422 \pm 0.022$ fm from chiral-force-based calculations (Sammarruca 2018).
- Proton skin of $^{20}$Ne: 0.029 fm (SLy4) and 0.024 fm (SkM*), compared to $0.032 \pm 0.006$ fm from Sammarruca (2018).
- Proton skin of $^{36}$Ar: 0.046 fm (SLy4) and 0.044 fm (SkM*), compared to $0.046 \pm 0.007$ fm from Sammarruca (2018).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Brown (2017), who showed that for a particular mirror pair ($^{52}$Ni--$^{52}$Cr) the neutron skin correlates with the proton radius difference using 48 Skyrme functionals, and of Sammarruca (2018), who studied such correlations for families of mirror pairs using chiral interactions. The present paper is the first to simultaneously investigate the correlation between neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy characteristics for both nuclei and their mirror partners, using the Ni isotopic chain ($A=48-60$) as an example. The results enable future measurements of mirror charge radii (e.g., at RIKEN-SCRIT or JLAB CREX/PREX-II) to serve as an alternative route to determine neutron skins and constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy.
