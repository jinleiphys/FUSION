# Semileptonic decay constants of octet baryons in the chiral quark-soliton model

> Based on the recent study of the magnetic moments and axial constants within the framework of the chiral quark-soliton model, we investigate the baryon semileptonic decay constants $(f_1,f_2)$ and $(g_1, g_2)$. Employing the relations between the diagonal transition matrix elements and off-diagonal ones in the vector and axial-vector channels, we obtain the ratios of baryon semileptonic decay constants $f_2/f_1$ and $g_1/f_1$. The $F/D$ ratio is also discussed and found that the value predicted by the present model naturally lies between that of the Skyrme model and that of the nonrelativistic quark model. The singlet axial constant $g^{(0)}_A$ can be expressed in terms of the $F/D$ ratio and $g^{(3)}_A$ in the present model and turns out to be small. The results are compared with available experimental data and found to be in good agreement with them. In addition, the induced pseudotensor coupling constants $g_2/f_1$ are calculated, the SU(3) symmetry breaking being considered. The results indicate that the effect of SU(3) symmetry breaking might play an important role for some decay modes in hyperon semileptonic decay.

## Key claim

The chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM) predicts semileptonic decay constants that agree with experimental data within about 15%, with the effect of SU(3) symmetry breaking being strong for the vector coupling ratio \(f_2/f_1\) but modest (5–10%) for the axial ratio \(g_1/f_1\), contrary to some earlier analyses that suggested larger SU(3) breaking effects.

## Method

The authors compute the semileptonic decay form factors \(f_1(0)\), \(f_2(0)\), \(g_1(0)\), and \(g_2(0)\) for octet baryon transitions using the chiral quark-soliton model, which is based on a low-momentum QCD partition function with dynamical quarks and pseudoscalar meson fields. They expand the quark propagator to first order in the rotational angular velocity \(\Omega\) (1/\(N_c\) corrections) and linear order in the strange quark mass \(m_s\) to incorporate SU(3) symmetry breaking, and they also include wave-function mixing of the octet with anti-decuplet and 27-plet representations. The vector form factors \(f_2(0)\) are related to experimental anomalous magnetic moments via relations that include baryon mass differences, while the axial form factors \(g_1(0)\) are expressed in terms of the model parameters \(a_i\) and the baryon axial constants.

## Key numbers

- The \(F/D\) ratio predicted by the χQSM is 0.61, lying between the Skyrme model value (5/9 ≈ 0.556) and the nonrelativistic quark model value (2/3 ≈ 0.667).
- The singlet axial constant \(g^{(0)}_A\) is 0.36 in the χQSM; using \(F/D = 0.582\) and \(g^{(3)}_A = 1.26\) gives \(g^{(0)}_A = 0.19\).
- For \(\Sigma^- \rightarrow n e^- \bar{\nu}\), the ratio \(g_1/f_1\) from the χQSM with SU(3) breaking is 0.31, compared to the experimental value \(0.34 \pm 0.017\).
- The ratio of \(g_1/f_1\) between \(\Lambda \rightarrow p e^- \bar{\nu}\) and \(\Sigma^- \rightarrow n e^- \\bar{\nu}\) is predicted to be \(-2.28\), compared to the experimental value \(-2.11 \pm 0.15\).
- For \(\Sigma^- \rightarrow n e^- \bar{\nu}\), the experimental values are \(g_1(0) = 0.20 \pm 0.08\) and \(g_2(0) = -0.56 \pm 0.37\) when SU(3) breaking is allowed, versus \(g_1(0) = 0.328 \pm 0.019\) with \(g_2 = 0\).
- The induced pseudotensor coupling \(g_2/f_1\) is calculated for several modes; the \(\Xi^- \rightarrow \Sigma^0\) mode shows the largest value due to the dominance of \(\beta_2\) over \(\beta_1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of magnetic moments (Kim et al., 1996) and axial constants (Blotz et al., 1996) within the χQSM to the off-diagonal semileptonic decay form factors. It addresses the ongoing debate about SU(3) symmetry breaking in hyperon decays, following analyses by Donoghue et al. (1985), Roos (1996), Avenarius (1996), Ehrnsperger and Schäfer (1996), and Ratcliffe (1996), who reached conflicting conclusions about the size of SU(3) breaking effects. The results enable improved extraction of CKM matrix elements and provide predictions for \(g_2\) that can be tested by future experiments on hyperon semileptonic decays.
