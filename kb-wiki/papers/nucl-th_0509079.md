# Shape mixing in the A 70 region

> We investigate the mixing of different shapes in the A~70 region using the adiabatic self-consistent collective method in a rotating nuclei. The calculation is done for 68^Se and 72-78^Kr which are known to show oblate-prolate shape coexistence at low angular momentum. A pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian, which is known to reproduce reproduce the structure nuclei in this mass region, is used. We calculate the collective path between the oblate and prolate minima and discuss how the collective behaviour of the system changes with increasing angular momentum.

## Key claim

The adiabatic self-consistent collective method, applied with a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian, can describe oblate-prolate shape mixing in the A≈70 region, but the reliability of a single collective coordinate breaks down when avoided crossings between RPA modes occur, as indicated by a large decoupling measure D.

## Method

The authors used the local harmonic approximation (LHA) to the adiabatic self-consistent collective method, solving a constrained mean-field problem together with a local random phase approximation (RPA) to determine the constraining operator along the collective path. The RPA was solved in a limited basis of state-dependent one-body operators, with spurious admixtures from particle number and angular momentum operators removed by enforcing orthogonality. The collective Schrodinger equation was then solved on a grid with boundary conditions U(Q_min)=U(Q_max)=0 to obtain excitation energies and wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Energy difference between oblate and prolate minima in 68Se at I=0 is less than 200 keV.
- For 68Se at I=0, the ground state average deformation is ε2=0.211 with γ=25.6 degrees; the first excited 0+ state has ε2=0.216 with γ=27.5 degrees.
- For 68Se at I=2, the ground state average deformation is ε2=0.250 with γ=48.1 degrees; the first excited 2+ state has ε2=0.229 with γ=46.5 degrees.
- For 72Kr at I=0, the ground state average deformation is ε2=0.282 with γ=60.0 degrees; the first excited 0+ state has ε2=0.368 with γ=0.0 degrees.
- For 72Kr at I=2, the ground state average deformation is ε2=0.302 with γ=42.4 degrees; the first excited 2+ state has ε2=0.403 with γ=9.8 degrees.
- For 74Kr at I=0, the ground state average deformation is ε2=0.282 with γ=60.0 degrees; the first excited 0+ state has ε2=0.368 with γ=0.0 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic self-consistent collective method (described in detail in the review by Døssing and Kerman (2000)) to rotating nuclei in the A≈70 region, building on the authors' previous work (Almehed and Walet, 2004) that applied the method without rotation. The calculations for 68Se are compared to similar work by Matsuo et al. (2003, 2004) using related techniques, and the results for 72Kr are compared to experimental data from Bouchez et al. (2003). The study enables future investigations of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient nuclei, but the authors note that for 72Kr a single collective coordinate is insufficient to reliably describe the spectrum, indicating the need for multi-dimensional collective models.
