# The phase structure of the Polyakov--quark-meson model beyond mean field

> The Polyakov-extended quark-meson model (PQM) is investigated beyond mean-field. This represents an important step towards a fully dynamical QCD computation. Both the quantum fluctuations to the matter sector and the back-reaction of the matter fluctuations to the QCD Yang-Mills sector are included. Results on the chiral and confinement-deconfinement crossover/phase transition lines and the location of a possible critical endpoint are presented. Moreover, thermodynamic quantities such as the pressure and the quark density are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first computation of the Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model beyond mean field that includes both quantum fluctuations in the matter sector and the back-reaction of matter fluctuations to the Yang-Mills sector via a flavor- and chemical-potential-dependent Polyakov-loop transition temperature \(T_0(N_f,\mu)\), with the hedge that the back-reaction of the Polyakov loop beyond mean field to the matter fluctuations is suppressed and can be treated as a perturbation across the whole phase diagram.

## Method
The authors use a functional renormalization group (FRG) approach to compute the full effective potential \(\Omega_k[\sigma,\vec\pi,\Phi,\bar\Phi]\) for the PQM model with \(N_f=2\) light quarks, solving the flow equation numerically without Taylor expansion. The back-reaction of the matter sector to the glue sector is encoded by adjusting the Polyakov-loop transition temperature \(T_0\) as a function of flavor number and chemical potential using one-loop perturbative beta-function relations, with the pure Yang-Mills value \(T_0=270\) MeV as baseline. The flow equation includes Polyakov-loop-enhanced quark and antiquark occupation numbers, and the infrared effective potential is evaluated on the mean-field solutions for \(\Phi(\sigma),\bar\Phi(\sigma)\) as a quantitatively reliable approximation.

## Key numbers
* For \(N_f=2\) massless flavors, the Polyakov-loop transition temperature is \(T_0=208\) MeV, down from 270 MeV in pure Yang-Mills.
* For \(N_f=2+1\) flavors with a strange quark mass \(m_s\approx 150\) MeV, \(T_0=187\) MeV.
* The chiral condensate and Polyakov-loop order parameters are computed at \(\mu=0\) for \(T_0=208\) MeV and \(T_0=270\) MeV, with corresponding temperature derivatives shown.
* The systematic error on \(T_0(N_f)\) is estimated as \({}^{+15}_{-20}\) MeV based on comparison with the QCD computation in the chiral limit by Braun (2009).
* The curvature parameter \(\hat\gamma\) governing the \(\mu\)-dependence of \(T_0\) is estimated to lie in the range \(0.7 \lesssim \hat\gamma \lesssim 1\).

## Context
This work extends the mean-field PQM study by Schaefer (2007) by including quantum and thermal fluctuations of the matter sector via FRG, and it builds on the full two-flavor QCD computation at vanishing and imaginary chemical potential by Braun (2009). The approach enables a systematic improvement of effective models toward full dynamical QCD and provides access to the phase boundaries for chiral and confinement-deconfinement transitions as well as thermodynamic quantities such as pressure and quark number density, with the possibility of locating a critical endpoint.
