# Transport Coefficient to Trace Anomaly in the Clustering of Color Sources Approach

> From our previously obtained shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($η/s$) in the framework of clustering of color sources (Color String Percolation Model: CSPM), we calculate the jet quenching parameter $\hat {q}$ and trace anomaly $Δ= (\varepsilon -3\it p)/T^{4}$ as a function of temperature. It is shown that the scaled $\hat {q}/T^{3}$ is in agreement with the recent JET Collaboration estimates. The inverse of $η/s$ is found to represent $Δ$. The results for $Δ$ are in excellent agreement with Lattice Quantum Chromo Dynamics (LQCD) simulations. From the trace anomaly and energy density $ε$, the equation of state is obtained as a function of temperature and compared with LQCD simulations. It is possible that there is a direct connection between the $η/s$ and $Δ$. Thus the estimate of transport coefficient $η/s$ provides $\hat {q}$ and $Δ$ as a function of temperature. Both $Δ$ and $η/s$ describe the transition from a strongly coupled QGP to a weakly coupled QGP.

## Key claim
The inverse of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) is found to represent the trace anomaly Δ = (ε − 3p)/T⁴, enabling the calculation of both the jet quenching parameter and the equation of state from a single transport coefficient, with the caveat that this representation is an ansatz rather than a derived relation.

## Method
The authors use the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) framework, where the transverse string density ξ determines the temperature via the Schwinger mechanism and the color suppression factor F(ξ). From previously obtained η/s values using kinetic theory and string percolation, they compute the scaled jet quenching parameter q̂/T³ using the relation η/s ≈ (3/2)(T³/q̂). The trace anomaly Δ is obtained as the inverse of η/s, and the sound velocity C_s² is derived from Bjorken 1D hydrodynamics using the trace anomaly and energy density.

## Key numbers
- η/s values are 2.5 times the KSS bound for Au+Au at 200 GeV and 3.3 times the KSS bound for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV
- The critical percolation density ξ_c = 1.2 corresponds to a chemical freeze-out temperature T_f = 167.7 ± 2.6 MeV
- √⟨p_t²⟩₁ = 207.2 ± 3.3 MeV at ξ_c = 1.2
- The minimum in η/s ≈ 0.20 determines the peak of the interaction measure Δ ≈ 5 at T_c ≈ 175 MeV
- The peak value of Δ = 4.88 increases to 5.61 when η/s is lowered by 15% at high densities
- JET Collaboration values for q̂/T³: 4.5 ± 1.3 at RHIC (T = 370 MeV) and 3.7 ± 1.4 at LHC (T = 470 MeV)
- Absolute q̂ values: 1.2 ± 0.3 GeV²/fm at T = 370 MeV and 1.9 ± 0.7 GeV²/fm at T = 470 MeV, at initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous CSPM calculations of η/s (de Deus et al., 2015) to connect transport coefficients with thermodynamic quantities. The results for q̂/T³ agree with the JET Collaboration estimates, and the trace anomaly Δ shows excellent agreement with lattice QCD simulations from the HotQCD Collaboration (2012) and Wuppertal Collaboration. The approach enables a unified description of the transition from strongly coupled QGP to weakly coupled QGP through both Δ and η/s, and provides an equation of state that matches lattice QCD calculations of the sound velocity.
