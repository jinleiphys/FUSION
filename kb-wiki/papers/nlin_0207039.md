# Fluctuations of wave functions about their classical average

> Quantum-classical correspondence for the average shape of eigenfunctions and the local spectral density of states are well-known facts. In this paper, the fluctuations that quantum mechanical wave functions present around the classical value are discussed. A simple random matrix model leads to a Gaussian distribution of the amplitudes. We compare this prediction with numerical calculations in chaotic models of coupled quartic oscillators. The expectation is broadly confirmed, but deviations due to scars are observed.

# Fluctuations of wave functions about their classical average

## Key claim
Quantum mechanical wave function amplitudes around their classical average follow a Gaussian distribution, as predicted by a simple random matrix model, but this expectation is only broadly confirmed in numerical calculations of chaotic coupled quartic oscillators, with deviations due to scars observed when expanding in an integrable basis.

## Method
The authors constructed a random matrix model for pairs of Hamiltonians (H₀, H) where the average squared overlaps ⟨|⟨φᵢ|ψⱼ⟩|²⟩ are prescribed by classical phase-space integrals, and showed that in the large-N limit the matrix elements become independent Gaussian variables with variances given by those average intensities. They then tested this prediction numerically by computing eigenfunctions of two coupled quartic oscillator Hamiltonians (H₁ and H₂) expanded in the basis of an integrable uncoupled oscillator (H₀), and also by expanding eigenfunctions of H₂ in terms of H₁ eigenfunctions, analyzing amplitude distributions in the wings of the wave functions far from the classical peak.

## Key numbers
- The classical eigenfunction integral g(ε, E) = ∫ dp dq δ(H₀(p,q)−ε) δ(H(p,q)−E) defines the average squared overlap via equation (1).
- For the numerical calculations, the Hamiltonian parameters were α=10, β=−5.5, γ=5.6 for H₁ and α=10, β=γ=−4.15 for H₂.
- The mean level spacing Δ was 0.826 for H₁ and 0.729 for H₂.
- A window of 4 mean level spacings Δ was dropped around the eigenfunction center for H₁, and 80Δ for H₂; the end of region C was 400Δ away from the center for both Hamiltonians.
- For tail windows of 20 mean level spacings, the χ² test per bin gave χ²₁₆=12.289 for state 900 of H₁ (energies 1000–1020) and χ²₁₆=14.483 for state 500 of H₂ (energies 640–660).

## Context
This work extends the quantum-classical correspondence for average eigenfunction shapes established in a prior study by the same group (Benet et al., 2001, referred to as [2body] in the text), which showed that suitably averaged matrix elements between eigenfunctions of two Hamiltonians are given by a classical phase-space integral. The present paper addresses the fluctuations around that average, which had received little systematic study in dynamical systems. The results enable statistical tests of wave function properties in chaotic systems and provide a framework for identifying scar effects (Heller, 1984) as deviations from the Gaussian prediction, particularly when one Hamiltonian is integrable. The approach also connects to Berry's (1977) picture of chaotic eigenfunctions covering phase space uniformly.
