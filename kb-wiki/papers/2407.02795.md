# Nuclear shell model study of neutrinoless double beta decay under Left-Right symmetric model

> We use the large scale nuclear shell model to calculate the nuclear matrix elements for the neutrino mediated neutrinoless double beta decay within the Left-Right symmetric model for four nuclei: $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{130}$Te and $^{136}$Xe. We perform a systematic analysis on the general magnitude of different terms for related mechanisms. For the $η$ mechanism, we find that the weak magnetism $R$ term dominates the decay rate while the $p$-wave effect is suppressed. While for the $λ$ mechanism, the $ω$ and the $q$ terms are with equal importance. For the latter $q$ term, important contributions from weak-magnetism MM part are observed. Finally, we give the constraints on the new physics parameters $m_{ββ}$, $λ$ and $η$ from current experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents large-scale nuclear shell model calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay within the Left-Right symmetric model, finding that for the \(\eta\) mechanism the weak magnetism \(R\) term dominates the decay rate while the \(p\)-wave effect is suppressed, and for the \(\lambda\) mechanism the \(\omega\) and \(q\) terms are equally important, with the \(q\) term receiving significant contributions from the weak-magnetism MM part, a component omitted in many prior calculations.

## Method

The authors used the large-scale shell model (LSSM) with the NuShellX code to compute two-body transition densities for four nuclei: \(^{76}\)Ge, \(^{82}\)Se, \(^{130}\)Te, and \(^{136}\)Xe. For \(^{76}\)Ge and \(^{82}\)Se, the \(jj44\) model space (four orbitals) was employed with two Hamiltonians (jj44b and jun45); for \(^{130}\)Te and \(^{136}\)Xe, the \(jj55\) model space (five orbitals) was used with two Hamiltonians (jj55pn and GCN50:82). The nuclear matrix elements were evaluated for the mass, \(\omega\), \(q\), \(R\), and \(P\) terms of the Left-Right symmetric model, including all leading-order and sub-leading weak-magnetism components, with CD-Bonn short-range correlations and a closure energy of 7 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{76}\)Ge with the jun45 Hamiltonian, the total mass-term NME \(M_m\) is 5.62 (sum of Fermi, GT, and tensor parts), while the \(M_{RGT}\) value is 4.256.
- For \(^{82}\)Se with jun45, the total mass-term NME is 5.26, and the \(M_{qGT+}\) value is 7.983, about 2–3 times larger than the mass-term GT part.
- For \(^{130}\)Te with jj55a, the total mass-term NME is 5.04, and the \(M_{RGT}\) value is 4.686, larger than \(M_{qGT-}\) for the same nucleus.
- For \(^{136}\)Xe with jj55a, the total mass-term NME is 4.25, and the \(M_P\) value is \(-0.289\).
- The uncertainty from the choice of Hamiltonian for the mass-term NME is about 10% for most nuclei, but reaches about 15–20% for \(^{82}\)Se (Fermi part 20%, GT part 15%).
- The typical exchange momentum for the \(q\) term is around 400 MeV, shifting the strength distribution compared to the mass term where low \(q \ll m_\pi\) dominates.

## Context

This work extends prior LSSM calculations for the standard neutrino mass mechanism (e.g., Senkov (2015), Coraggio (2020), Horoi (2013), Caurier (2011), Menendez (2017)) to the Left-Right symmetric model, addressing the lack of systematic studies for the \(\lambda\) and \(\eta\) mechanisms that include all relevant nuclear current components. Earlier LSSM calculations for these mechanisms (Caurier (1996), Sarkar (2020), Iwata (2021)) omitted the weak-magnetism MM component, which the present work shows is essential for the \(q\) term. The results enable improved constraints on the new physics parameters \(m_{\beta\beta}\), \(\lambda\), and \(\eta\) from current experimental limits, and provide a basis for comparing decay half-lives across isotopes to distinguish mechanisms.
