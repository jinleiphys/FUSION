# Charge-Symmetry-Breaking Potentials from Isospin-Violating Meson-Baryon Coupling Constants

> We consider charge-symmetry violations in the nucleon-nucleon force which result from isospin-violating meson-baryon coupling constants. The vector mesons are assumed to couple to the nucleon's electromagnetic current, which we decompose into isoscalar and isovector quark components. We compute these currents in the context of a constituent quark model. The isospin violations in the meson-baryon couplings arise from the difference in the up and down constituent quark masses. We show that class IV charge-symmetry-breaking potentials arise in the resulting $ω$ and $ρ$ exchange contributions to the $NN$ force. The magnitude of these contributions is consistent with that phenomenologically required by the measured difference of $n$ and $p$ analyzing powers in elastic $\vec{n}-\vec{p}$ scattering at 183 MeV.

# Charge-Symmetry-Breaking Potentials from Isospin-Violating Meson-Baryon Coupling Constants

## Key claim

Isospin violation in the vector-meson-nucleon coupling constants, arising from the up-down constituent quark mass difference, generates class IV charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) potentials in omega and rho exchange that are identical in structure to those from rho-omega mixing and are commensurate in magnitude with the phenomenologically required strength to explain the measured difference of neutron and proton analyzing powers in elastic n-p scattering at 183 MeV, provided the rho-omega mixing amplitude is momentum-transfer dependent and thus small at space-like momenta.

## Method

The authors assume vector mesons couple to the nucleon's electromagnetic current, decomposed into isoscalar and isovector quark components appropriate for omega and rho coupling respectively. They compute these currents in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with SU(6) nucleon wave functions, evaluating the vector and tensor coupling constants at q^2=0 from the spin and flavor structure of the nucleon. The isospin-violating corrections arise exclusively in the tensor couplings, producing isovector corrections for the omega and isoscalar corrections for the rho, which are then used to construct one-boson-exchange CSB potentials.

## Key numbers

- The up-down constituent quark mass difference is taken as Δm = 4.1 MeV (lower bound from Lichtenberg (1989)), with the average quark mass m = M/3 = 313 MeV, giving Δm/m ≈ 0.0131.
- The strength of the class IV CSB potential from coupling-constant isospin violation at q^2=0 is C(q^2=0) = 1.77 × 10^2 (Δm/m) GeV^{-2} ≈ 2.32 GeV^{-2}, using Bonn B potential parameters (g_ω^2/4π = 11.13, g_ρ^2/4π = 0.42).
- For comparison, the on-shell rho-omega mixing potential strength at q^2=0 is C_{ρ-ω}^{on-shell}(q^2=0) ≈ 2.07 GeV^{-2}, using the on-shell mixing matrix element ⟨ρ|H|ω⟩ = -4520 ± 600 MeV^2 from Barkov et al. (1985) and the Bonn B value f_{(1)}^ρ = 6.1 ḡ^ρ.
- The isospin-symmetric nonrelativistic quark model gives f^ρ_{(1)}/g^ρ_N = 4, compared to the Bonn B potential value of f^ρ_N/g^ρ_N = 6.1.

## Context

This work extends studies of charge-symmetry breaking in the nucleon-nucleon force by focusing on mechanism (ii), isospin violation in meson-nucleon coupling constants, rather than the more commonly studied mechanism (i) of rho-omega mixing (Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1992); Piekarewicz and Williams (1993); Hatsuda et al. (1994); Krein, Thomas, and Williams (1993); Mitchell et al. (1994); O'Connell et al. (1994); Maltman (1995)) or mechanism (iii) of nucleon mass differences. The results are very similar to those of Henley and Zhang (1987) from a more complicated quark model. If the rho-omega mixing amplitude is indeed momentum-transfer dependent and small at space-like momenta, this new source of isospin violation can fill the role demanded by the IUCF ΔA measurement (Knutson et al. (1990)); if not, the total CSB potential is probably too large to fit the data. The approach enables future study of the q^2 dependence of these coupling-constant isospin violations.
