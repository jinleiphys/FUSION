# Partonic structure of neutral pseudoscalars via two photon transition form factors

> The $γγ^\ast \to η_{c,b}$ transition form factors are computed using a continuum approach to the two valence-body bound-state problem in relativistic quantum field theory, and thereby unified with equivalent calculations of electromagnetic pion elastic and transition form factors. The resulting $γγ^\ast \to η_c$ form factor, $G_{η_c}(Q^2)$, is consistent with available data: significantly, at accessible momentum transfers, $Q^2 G_{η_c}(Q^2)$ lies well below its conformal limit. These observations confirm that the leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) of heavy-heavy bound-states are compressed relative to the conformal limit. A clear understanding of the distribution of valence-quarks within mesons thus emerges; a picture which connects Goldstone modes, built from the lightest-quarks in Nature, with systems containing the heaviest valence-quarks that can now be studied experimentally, and highlights basic facts about manifestations of mass within the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper computes the $\gamma\gamma^\ast \to \eta_{c,b}$ transition form factors using a continuum Dyson-Schwinger equations framework, finding that at accessible momentum transfers $Q^2 G_{\eta_c}(Q^2)$ lies well below its conformal limit, confirming that the leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes of heavy-heavy bound-states are compressed relative to the conformal limit.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, using perturbation theory integral representations for dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes of $\eta_{c,b}$ mesons computed previously in Ding (2015). The transition form factor $G_{M_5}(Q^2)$ is obtained by evaluating the triangle diagram integral with one highly-virtual photon ($k_1^2=Q^2$) and one quasi-real photon ($k_2^2=0$), supplemented by leading-order ERBL QCD evolution of the meson's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Key numbers

- $\Gamma[\eta_c\to \gamma\gamma] = 6.1$ keV (computed from $f_{\eta_c}=0.26$ GeV), compared with world average $5.1\pm 0.4$ keV
- $\Gamma[\eta_b\to \gamma\gamma] = 0.52$ keV (computed from $f_{\eta_b}=0.54$ GeV), not yet observed experimentally
- $r_{\eta_c} = 0.16$ fm $= 0.23 r_{\pi^0}$ (interaction radius from slope of transition form factor), compared with experimental $r_{\eta_c} = 0.17 \pm 0.01$ fm
- $r_{\eta_b} = 0.041$ fm $= 0.26 r_{\eta_c}$
- Ratio $R(Q^2=35$ GeV$^2) = G_{\eta_c}(Q^2)/G_\pi(Q^2) = 2.4$, which is 64% of the conformal limit value
- At $Q^2=35$ GeV$^2$, $m_{\eta_c}^2/Q^2 = 0.4$ and $m_{\eta_b}^2/Q^2 = 3.5$

## Context

This work extends the unified treatment of pseudoscalar meson transition form factors previously applied to the pion in Raya (2015) and the pion elastic form factor in Chang (2013), now encompassing heavy-heavy systems $\eta_c$ and $\eta_b$. The results confirm the prediction from Ding (2015) that heavy-heavy meson PDAs are compressed relative to the conformal limit, while light-quark meson PDAs are dilated due to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The agreement with BaBar data for $\eta_c$ supports the reliability of the Belle data for $\pi^0$ at large $Q^2$ and casts doubt on the applicability of non-relativistic QCD to exclusive charmonium processes, as argued by Feng (2015).
