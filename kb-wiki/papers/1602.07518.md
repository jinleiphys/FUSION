# Application of an Extended Random Phase Approximation on Giant Resonances in Light, Medium and Heavy Mass Nuclei

> We present results of the time blocking approximation (TBA) on giant resonances in light, medium and heavy mass nuclei. The TBA is an extension of the widely used random-phase approximation (RPA) adding complex configurations by coupling to phonon excitations. A new method for handling the single-particle continuum is developed and applied in the present calculations. We investigate in detail the dependence of the numerical results on the size of the single particle space and the number of phonons as well as on nuclear matter properties. Our approach is self-consistent, based on an energy-density functional of Skyrme type where we used seven different parameter sets. The numerical results are compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The Time Blocking Approximation (TBA), which extends RPA by coupling to phonon excitations, can simultaneously reproduce the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR), isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR), and isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) in light, medium, and heavy nuclei using properly chosen Skyrme parametrizations, but only if the single-particle continuum is treated exactly; the abstract omits that the theory fails to reproduce experimental isoscalar cross sections in light nuclei like 16O because important decay channels (alpha decay) are missing.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the response function within both RPA and TBA using a discrete basis representation with box boundary conditions, with a new method to include the single-particle continuum exactly. The TBA adds 1p1h⊗phonon configurations to the conventional 1p1h RPA space, with the induced interaction W̃(ω) obtained by subtracting the static part W(0) to avoid double counting and ensure stability. Seven different Skyrme energy-density functional parametrizations were used, and the residual interaction V was derived self-consistently from the functional according to the second derivative of the energy density with respect to the density matrix.

## Key numbers

- Box sizes: 15 fm for 16O and 40,48Ca, 18 fm for 208Pb
- Single-particle basis cutoff: ε_max = 100 MeV
- Smearing parameter Δ = 200 keV used in continuum RPA comparison calculations
- For 16O: classical photo-absorption cross section σ_class = 378.5 mb; for 48Ca: 654.5 mb; for 132Sn: 1204.0 mb; for 208Pb: 1605.1 mb (all calculated from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock ground-state mean-square radii)
- In 16O, CRPA and DRPA results differ significantly at Δ ≲ 400 keV, with difference still noticeable at Δ = 1 MeV, disappearing only at Δ = 2 MeV
- In 48Ca, CRPA vs DRPA difference becomes small at Δ ≳ 1 MeV
- In 208Pb, continuum effect is small and manifested only at Δ ≲ 200 keV
- For the GQR in 16O, between 18-23 MeV the alpha-decay width is 90% of the total decay width; between 23-27 MeV it is 70%

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RPA framework of Kluepfel (2009) and the TBA approach of Tselyaev (2007, 2013) by implementing a new exact continuum treatment and applying it systematically to giant resonances across light, medium, and heavy nuclei. The authors previously showed in Lyutorovich (2012) that phonon coupling could resolve the long-standing problem of reproducing GDR centroid energies in both light and heavy nuclei with the same effective interaction, a difficulty documented in systematic RPA surveys by Kluepfel (2009) and Erler (2010, 2011). The present work enables future studies to use the TBA with exact continuum for reliable predictions of giant resonance properties, though the failure for isoscalar resonances in 16O indicates that alpha-decay channels (noted by Wagner) must be included for light nuclei.
