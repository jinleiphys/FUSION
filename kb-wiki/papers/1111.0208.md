# Feshbach resonances and their interaction in light scattering off photonic crystal slabs

> The concept of Feshbach resonances developed for quantum mechanical scattering is applied in the analysis of classical light scattering off photonic crystal slabs. It is shown that this concept can be realized almost perfectly in these systems. As an application guided-mode resonances in the grating waveguide structure (GWS) are studied in detail. Using simple resonance dominance approximation the characteristic properties of isolated Feshbach resonances in light scattering are exhibited. Formation and interaction of overlapping resonances are investigated. The relevant parameters of the GWS are identified which control the shape of the reflectivity of interacting resonances as well as the enhancement of the electromagnetic field. The differences in the properties of TE and TM resonances is emphasized for both isolated and interacting resonances.

# Feshbach resonances and their interaction in light scattering off photonic crystal slabs

## Key claim

The concept of Feshbach resonances, originally developed for quantum mechanical scattering, can be realized "almost perfectly" in classical light scattering off photonic crystal slabs, specifically in grating waveguide structures (GWS), provided the coupling between guided and extended modes is sufficiently weak and the resonance condition $\eta_i + (k_x + nK_g)^2 \approx 0$ is satisfied.

## Method

The authors reformulate Maxwell's equations for TE and TM polarized light scattering off a grating waveguide structure as a system of coupled channel equations analogous to the Feshbach formalism in nuclear and atomic physics. They introduce a "resonance dominance approximation" that neglects continuum contributions to the Green's functions for non-zero diffraction orders, retaining only guided mode contributions, and truncate the system to two coupled modes (the incident channel and one guided mode) to obtain analytic expressions for reflection and transmission amplitudes, resonance widths, and field enhancement coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Resonance width expression: $\tilde{\Gamma}/2 = -|\epsilon_\nu|^2 \omega^4 \text{Im}\Sigma = (\omega^4 / 2 k_z^-) |\epsilon_\nu \mathcal{C}^{(+)}|^2$, where $\epsilon_\nu$ is the Fourier component of the dielectric function, $\omega$ is the frequency, $k_z^-$ is the z-component of the wave vector in the incident medium, and $\mathcal{C}^{(+)}$ is the coupling integral between the guided mode and the incident field.
- The resonance condition for isolated Feshbach resonances: $\eta_i + (k_x + nK_g)^2 \approx 0$, where $\eta_i$ is the eigenvalue of the guided mode equation and $K_g = 2\pi/\Lambda$ is the grating wave number.
- Power conservation identity: $|r(\omega,k_x)|^2 + (k_z^+ / k_z^-) |t(\omega,k_x)|^2 = 1$, valid irrespective of the number of guided modes.

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach resonance formalism from quantum mechanical scattering (Feshbach 1958, 1962) to classical light scattering, building on prior theoretical approaches to guided-mode resonances including the scattering matrix approach (Fan et al. 2002), temporal coupled mode theory (Fan and Joannopoulos 2003), and the coupled mode approach of Rosenblatt et al. (1997) based on Kazarinov et al. (1985) for distributed feedback lasers. Unlike previous treatments that required parameter fitting (Miroshnichenko 2009; Kivshar et al. 2011), the authors derive fully analytic expressions for resonance properties without fitting, and uniquely treat both TE and TM polarized resonances on equal footing. The work enables systematic design of photonic crystal slabs with tailored resonance properties by adjusting GWS parameters, and provides a framework for studying interactions between overlapping Feshbach resonances.
