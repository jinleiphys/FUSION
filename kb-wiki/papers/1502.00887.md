# Contribution of two particle-two hole final states to the nuclear response

> The excitation of two particle-two hole final states in neutrino-nucleus scattering has been advocated by many authors as the source of the excess cross section observed by the MiniBooNE Collaboration in the quasi elastic sector. We analyse the mechanisms leading to the appearance of these final states, and illustrate their significance through the results of accurate calculations of the nuclear electromagnetic response in the transverse channel. A novel approach, allowing for a consistent treatment of the amplitudes involving one- and two-nucleon currents in the kinematical region in which the non relativistic approximation breaks down, is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the excess cross section observed by MiniBooNE in the quasi-elastic sector can be explained by two particle-two hole (2p2h) final states, and that the interference between amplitudes involving one- and two-nucleon currents plays an important role, a feature that emerges from calculations of the transverse electromagnetic response of ⁴He and the sum rule of ¹²C, but only within a non-relativistic framework that must be extended to higher momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors analyze the mechanisms leading to 2p2h final states, initial state correlations (ISC), final state correlations (FSC), and meson-exchange currents (MEC), using Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of the Euclidean (Laplace-transformed) electromagnetic response of ⁴He and the sum rule of ¹²C in the transverse channel. They then outline a novel approach based on a factorisation ansatz that extends the spectral function formalism to consistently treat one- and two-nucleon current contributions in the relativistic regime, expressing the nuclear matrix elements in terms of one- and two-nucleon spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- The transverse response of ⁴He at momentum transfer |**q**| = 500 MeV shows that including two-nucleon currents leads to a sizeable enhancement, with the interference term peaking at energy loss ω < ω_QE (where ω_QE = √(|**q**|² + m²) – m).
- For carbon, the transverse sum rule S_T(|**q**|) at momentum transfer |**q**| ≥ 300 MeV shows that interference contributions are comparable to, and even larger than, the contribution from squaring the two-nucleon current matrix element.
- At beam energy E_e = 961 MeV and electron scattering angle θ_e = 37.5°, the 2p2h contribution from ISC provides approximately 10% of the total quasi-elastic cross section for carbon.
- In the same kinematic setup, the momentum transfer at the quasi-elastic peak is |**q**| ~ 585 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of 2p2h final states beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM) used in event generators, which was shown by Martini (2011), Coletti (2013), and Nieves (2012) to be inadequate for explaining the MiniBooNE excess. The paper builds on the Green's Function Monte Carlo approach of Carlson (2002) and the spectral function formalism of Benhar (1994) and the Local Density Approximation (LDA) spectral function of Benhar (2005). It enables future calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections that consistently include correlations and meson-exchange currents in the relativistic regime, validated against electron scattering data.
