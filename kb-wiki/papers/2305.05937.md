# CREX- and PREX-II-motivated relativistic interactions and their implications for the bulk properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars

> We investigate the implications of parity-violating electron scattering experiment on neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca (CREX) and $^{208}$Pb (PREX-II) data on the bulk properties of finite nuclei, nuclear matter, and neutron stars. The neutron skin thickness from the CREX and PREX-II data is employed to constrain the parameters of relativistic mean field models which includes different non-linear, self and cross-couplings among isoscalar-scalar $σ$, isoscalar-vector $ω$, isovector-scalar $δ$ and isovector-vector $ρ$ meson fields up to the quartic order. Three parametrizations of RMF model are proposed by fitting CREX, PREX-II and both CREX as well as PREX-II data to assess their implications. A covariance analysis is performed to assess the theoretical uncertainties of model parameters and nuclear matter observables along with correlations among them. The RMF model parametrization obtained with the CREX data acquires much smaller value of symmetry energy (J= 28.97$\pm$ 0.99 MeV), its slope parameter (L= 30.61$\pm 6.74$ MeV) in comparison to those obtained with PREX-II data. The neutron star properties are studied by employing the equations of state (EoSs) composed of nucleons and leptons in $β$ equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper shows that fitting the CREX neutron skin thickness for ⁴⁸Ca alone yields a much softer symmetry energy (J = 28.97 ± 0.99 MeV, L = 30.61 ± 6.74 MeV) than fitting the PREX-II data for ²⁰⁸Pb, and that simultaneously fitting both datasets (BSRV-CPREX) does not resolve the tension between the two experiments, the combined fit still overpredicts the CREX value for ⁴⁸Ca.

## Method

The authors constructed three new relativistic mean-field (RMF) parametrizations (BSRV-CREX, BSRV-PREX, BSRV-CPREX) by fitting binding energies and charge radii of 16 closed/open-shell nuclei plus the neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca (CREX), ²⁰⁸Pb (PREX-II), or both, using a simulated annealing χ² minimization. A covariance analysis was performed to compute theoretical uncertainties on model parameters and nuclear matter observables, and the resulting equations of state (EoS) for nucleons and leptons in β-equilibrium were used to compute neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy from BSRV-CREX: J = 28.97 ± 0.99 MeV; from BSRV-PREX: significantly larger (exact value not given in the truncated text, but contrasted as "much smaller" for CREX).
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy from BSRV-CREX: L = 30.61 ± 6.74 MeV.
- Neutron skin thickness for ⁴⁸Ca predicted by BSRV-CREX: Δr_np = 0.146 ± 0.019 fm (experimental CREX value: 0.121 ± 0.026 fm).
- Neutron skin thickness for ²⁰⁸Pb predicted by BSRV-PREX: Δr_np = 0.243 ± 0.048 fm; by BSRV-CPREX: 0.252 ± 0.028 fm (experimental PREX-II value: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm).
- Root-mean-square errors in binding energy: 1.62 MeV (BSRV-CREX), 1.39 MeV (BSRV-PREX and BSRV-CPREX); in charge radii: 0.071 fm, 0.080 fm, 0.081 fm respectively.

## Context

This work extends the effort to constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy using parity-violating electron scattering data from the CREX (Adhikari 2022) and PREX-II (Adhikari 2021) experiments. It builds on prior RMF models such as NL3 (Lalazissis 1997), FSUGarnet (Chen 2015), IOPB-1 (Kumar 2018), and Big Apple (Fattoyev 2020) by incorporating the new neutron skin measurements directly into the parameter fit. The results highlight the persistent tension between the soft symmetry energy favored by CREX and the stiff symmetry energy favored by PREX-II, and they provide new EoSs for studying neutron star properties under these conflicting constraints.
