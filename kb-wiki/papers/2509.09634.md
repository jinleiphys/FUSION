# Algebraic treatment of alpha-cluster nuclei

> In this contribution we review the algebraic cluster model (ACM) for $α$-cluster nuclei with $A=4k$ nucleons and its extension to the cluster shell model (CSM) for $A=4k+x$ nuclei. Particular attention is paid to the question to what extent the $α$-cluster structure survives under the addition of extra nucleons. As an example, we discuss the properties of $^{12}$C and $^{13}$C.

## Key claim

The Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) and its extension, the Cluster Shell Model (CSM), provide a unified algebraic description of alpha-cluster nuclei, with the ACM successfully describing the rotation-vibration spectrum and electromagnetic transitions of 12C in terms of three alpha particles in a triangular configuration, while the CSM extends this framework to odd-cluster nuclei like 13C by coupling single-particle degrees of freedom to the cluster core, with the caveat that the extent to which the alpha-cluster structure survives under the addition of extra nucleons remains an open question being investigated.

## Method

The ACM describes the relative motion of n-body clusters using a spectrum generating algebra U(ν+1) where ν=3(n-1) represents the relative spatial degrees of freedom, with the Hamiltonian constrained by S_n permutation symmetry for identical alpha particles. For 12C (n=3), the rotation-vibration spectrum is approximated by E = ε₁(v₁+1/2) + ε₂(v₂+1) + κL(L+1), with two fundamental vibrations: a symmetric breathing vibration v₁(A'₁) and a degenerate two-dimensional vibration v₂(E'). The CSM extends this by adding a single-particle Hamiltonian with central, spin-orbit, and Coulomb potentials to describe odd-cluster nuclei, with the single-particle levels labeled by irreducible representations of the double point group D'₃ₕ.

## Key numbers

- 12C ground-state rotational band sequence: 0⁺, 2⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 4⁻, 5⁻ (parity doublet 4⁺ and 4⁻ predicted by ACM)
- Beta parameter determined from elastic form factor minimum: β = 1.82 fm
- B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) in 12C: theoretical 7.8 e²fm⁴, experimental 7.63 ± 0.19 e²fm⁴
- B(E3;3₁⁻→0₁⁺) in 12C: theoretical 65.0 e²fm⁶, experimental 104 ± 14 e²fm⁶
- Quadrupole moment Q(2₁⁺) in 12C: theoretical 5.7 e fm², experimental 5.97 ± 0.30 e fm²
- B(E2;3/2₁⁻→1/2₁⁻) in 13C: theoretical 7.8 e²fm⁴, experimental 6.4 ± 1.5 e²fm⁴
- B(E2;5/2₁⁻→1/2₁⁻) in 13C: theoretical 7.8 e²fm⁴, experimental 5.6 ± 0.4 e²fm⁴
- B(E3;5/2₁⁺→1/2₁⁻) in 13C: theoretical 65.0 e²fm⁶, experimental 100 ± 40 e²fm⁶
- Jᴾ=1/2⁺ state at 3.089 MeV in 13C assigned as bandhead of E₁/₂ symmetry rotational band
- Jᴾ=1/2⁻ state at 8.860 MeV in 13C assigned as analogue of the Hoyle state

## Context

This work reviews and extends the Algebraic Cluster Model originally introduced for 12C (Bijker, 2000) and the Cluster Shell Model developed for odd-cluster nuclei (Bijker and collaborators, 2014-2020). The ACM builds on early alpha-cluster models by Wheeler (1930s), Hafstad and Teller (1930s), Brink, and Robson, while the CSM is similar in spirit to the Nilsson model but with the odd nucleon moving in the deformed field generated by the cluster degrees of freedom. The results enable systematic studies of alpha-clustering in nuclei with A=4k+x nucleons, with current work extending to x=2 systems such as 10Be, and open questions remain about whether the predicted negative parity states of the Hoyle band (3⁻ and 4⁻) will be observed experimentally.
