# Nonequilibrium dynamics of a hadronizing quark-gluon plasma

> We investigate the hadronic cooling of a quark droplet within a microscopic model. The color flux tube approach is used to describe the hadronization of the quark phase. The model reproduces experimental particle ratios equally well compared to a static thermal hadronic source. Furthermore, the dynamics of the decomposition of a quark-gluon plasma is investigated and time dependent particle ratios are found.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that experimental particle ratios at SPS energies are compatible with a scenario in which a quark-gluon plasma consecutively evaporates hadrons, provided the plasma contains thermally distributed bound diquark states; without diquarks the model fails to describe observed baryon abundances.

## Method

The authors apply the color flux tube model to describe hadronization of a quark droplet, where a quark crossing the plasma surface is pulled back by a linear confining potential (string tension κ ≈ 0.9 GeV/fm) and the flux tube decays via Schwinger-like qq̄ pair production. Diquarks are included as constituents of the plasma to enhance baryon production, and the model is extended to a quark molecular dynamics (qMD) approach with a two-body Cornell potential for semi-classical quarks, using a Metropolis algorithm to generate thermal initial conditions with a Bjorken-like longitudinal velocity profile.

## Key numbers

- String tension κ ≈ 0.9 GeV/fm, extracted from fits to Regge trajectories or lattice calculations.
- Quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 5 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV.
- Diquark masses from LUND model: (qq)_0 = 420 MeV, (qq)_1 = 490 MeV, (qs)_0 = 590 MeV, (qs)_1 = 640 MeV, (ss)_1 = 790 MeV.
- Diquark masses from NJL model: (qq)_0 = 234 MeV, (qq)_1 = 822 MeV, (qs)_0 = 537 MeV, (qs)_1 = 962 MeV, (ss)_1 = 1087 MeV.
- Plasma bag radius assumed R ∼ 2–6 fm, temperatures T ∼ 100–250 MeV.
- Initial conditions for particle ratio calculations: A_B^init = 100, S/A^init = 45, f_s^init = 0, B^{1/4} = 235 MeV.
- In qMD calculations, initial quark thermal distribution set to T = 200 MeV for u, d, and s quarks.
- Hadronization completes within about 12 fm/c.
- A string radius of 0.5 fm reduces the pair creation rate by about 40%.

## Context

This work extends the color flux tube model of Banerjee et al. (1983) by including diquarks as plasma constituents and allowing dynamical non-equilibrium evolution, in contrast to earlier static approaches by Danos and Rafelski (1983) and Muller and Eisenberg (1984). The model reproduces experimental particle ratios comparably to static thermal fits (e.g., those compiled by Braun-Munzinger et al.) and to dynamical models such as UrQMD (Bass et al.), while predicting time-dependent particle ratios and strangeness distillation. The qMD extension enables future studies of event-by-event fluctuations and transverse mass spectra without requiring an imposed transverse velocity profile, unlike the fireball model of Lee et al. (1990).
