# Dominance of Pion-exchange in R-parity Violating Supersymmetry Contributions to Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We present a new contribution of the R-parity violating supersymmetry (SUSY) to neutrinoless double beta decay via the pion exchange between decaying neutrons. The pion coupling to the final state electrons is induced by the R-parity violating SUSY interactions. We have found this pion-exchange mechanism to dominate over the conventional two-nucleon one. The latter corresponds to direct interaction between quarks from two decaying neutrons without any light hadronic mediator like pion. The constraints on the certain R-parity violating SUSY parameters are extracted from the current experimental neutrinoless double beta decay half-life limit. These constraints are significantly stronger than those previously known or expected from the ongoing accelerator experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pion-exchange mechanism for R-parity violating supersymmetry contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay dominates over the conventional two-nucleon mechanism, but this dominance depends on the hadronization stage and the specific values of the pion matrix element parameter c_P, which are estimated using either the Vacuum Insertion Approximation (VIA) or the non-relativistic Quark Model (QM).

## Method

The authors calculated the contribution of R-parity violating supersymmetry to neutrinoless double beta decay via double-pion exchange between decaying neutrons, using the effective quark-lepton Lagrangian derived from the λ' term of the superpotential. They employed the renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation with proton-neutron pairing (full-RQRPA) to compute both the two-nucleon and pion-exchange nuclear matrix elements for ⁷⁶Ge, and extracted constraints on the R-parity violating SUSY parameters from the experimental half-life limit.

## Key numbers

- Experimental lower bound on ⁷⁶Ge 0νββ-decay half-life: T_{1/2}^{0νββ-exp}(0⁺ → 0⁺) ≥ 9.1 × 10²⁴ years at 90% confidence level
- Two-nucleon mode nuclear matrix elements for ⁷⁶Ge: M̃_q^{2N} = -61, M̃_f^{2N} = 0.85
- Pion-exchange nuclear matrix element for ⁷⁶Ge: M^{πN} = -1800 (QM) and -600 (VIA)
- Constraint on sum of effective MSSM parameters from pion-exchange: η̃_q + η̃_f ≤ 2.1 × 10⁻⁹
- Constraint on λ'_{111} from gluino contribution (VIA): λ'_{111} ≤ 2.0 × 10⁻⁴ (m̃_q/100 GeV)² (m̃_g/100 GeV)^{1/2}
- Constraint on λ'_{111} from neutralino contribution (VIA): λ'_{111} ≤ 5.2 × 10⁻⁴ (m̃_e/100 GeV)² (m_χ/100 GeV)^{1/2}
- Conservative bound on λ'_{111} using SUSY naturalness upper bound (1 TeV): λ'_{111} ≤ 6.3 × 10⁻² (VIA)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of R-parity violating SUSY contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay previously studied by Mohapatra (1986), Vergados (1987), and Hirsch, Klapdor-Kleingrothaus, and Kovalenko (1995-1996), who considered only the conventional two-nucleon mode. The pion-exchange idea was first pointed out by Pontecorvo (1968) and quantitatively realized by Vergados (1982) and Simkovic et al. (1992) for heavy Majorana neutrino exchange. The new constraints on the R-parity violating coupling λ'_{111} are significantly stronger than those previously known or expected from ongoing accelerator experiments at HERA, reducing the accessible parameter space for resonant single squark production and cascade decay mechanisms.
