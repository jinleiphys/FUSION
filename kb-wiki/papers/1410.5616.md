# Thermalization of gluons with Bose-Einstein condensation

> We study the thermalization of gluons far from thermal equilibrium in relativistic kinetic theory. The initial distribution of gluons is assumed to resemble that in the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Only elastic scatterings in static, nonexpanding gluonic matter are considered. At first we show that the occurrence of condensation in the limit of vanishing particle mass requires a general constraint for the scattering matrix element. Then the thermalization of gluons with Bose-Einstein condensation is demonstrated in a transport calculation. We see a continuously increasing overpopulation of low energy gluons, followed by a decrease to the equilibrium distribution, when the condensation occurs. The times of the completion of the gluon condensation and of the entropy production are calculated. These times scale inversely with the energy density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time a complete Bose-Einstein condensation of gluons within kinetic theory, showing that the times for condensation onset, completion, and full thermalization scale inversely with the energy density. However, this result is conditional on assuming gluon number conservation and a static, nonexpanding system with only elastic scatterings.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation for gluons using the BAMPS (Boltzmann approach of multiparton scatterings) transport model, modified to include Bose enhancement factors. They consider a static, homogeneous box of gluons with an initial overoccupied step-function distribution resembling the color glass condensate in heavy ion collisions, using only elastic gg → gg scatterings with a Hard-Thermal-Loop improved matrix element. The evolution is tracked until the system reaches thermal equilibrium with a Bose-Einstein condensate at zero momentum.

## Key numbers

- Critical initial occupation below which no condensation occurs: f₀^c = 0.154
- For f₀ = 2.0 (energy density 53 GeV/fm³): condensation starts at τ_c = 0.376 fm/c, full thermalization reached at 1.5 fm/c
- Equilibrium temperature for f₀ = 2.0: T_BE = 0.53 GeV
- Effective temperature at 0.5 fm/c for f₀ = 2.0: T_eff = 10 GeV
- Cutoff energy for condensate definition: ε = 0.0025 GeV
- Coupling constant used: α_s = 0.3
- Saturation scale: Q_s = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior kinetic theory studies by Blaizot (2013) and Scardina (2014) that could only address evolution up to the onset of Bose-Einstein condensation, by evolving the system beyond onset to full thermalization with a complete condensate. It provides a numerical demonstration that the parametric scaling t_th ∼ Q_s⁻¹ α_s⁻⁷/⁴ from Blaizot (2011) holds in a full transport calculation. The results enable future studies of whether gluon condensation survives when including number-changing processes (gluon bremsstrahlung, quark-antiquark annihilations) and expansion, as discussed by Huang (2013), Blaizot (2014), and York (2014).
