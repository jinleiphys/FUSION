# Energy dependent nuclear suppression from gluon saturation in exclusive vector meson production

> We calculate the exclusive $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ photoproduction cross section at high energies from the Color Glass Condensate approach. The results are compared to the center-of-mass energy dependent $γ+A\to\mathrm{J}/ψ+A$ cross sections extracted from measurements in ultra peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. We predict strong saturation-driven nuclear suppression at high energies, while LHC data prefers even stronger suppression. We explore the effect of nucleon shape fluctuations on the nuclear suppression in the coherent and incoherent cross sections, and show that the most recent measurement of the $|t|$-differential incoherent $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ cross section prefers large event-by-event fluctuations of the nucleon substructure in heavy nuclei, comparable to that found for a free proton.

# Energy dependent nuclear suppression from gluon saturation in exclusive vector meson production

## Key claim
The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) calculation reproduces the energy dependence of coherent J/ψ photoproduction on lead at high photon-nucleon center-of-mass energies (W ≳ 100 GeV) but overestimates the overall normalization by up to 40%, suggesting that LHC data prefers even stronger saturation-driven nuclear suppression than the CGC framework predicts when constrained by HERA γ+p data.

## Method
The authors compute exclusive J/ψ photoproduction cross sections using the CGC effective theory, where the scattering amplitude factorizes into a photon fluctuating into a quark-antiquark dipole followed by eikonal propagation through the target color field. Wilson lines at initial xℙ = 0.01 are obtained from the McLerran-Venugopalan model, and the energy (xℙ) dependence is generated by solving JIMWLK evolution equations. Free parameters describing the proton saturation scale, running coupling scale, and event-by-event fluctuating proton geometry are determined by fitting γ+p → J/ψ + p data from H1, ZEUS, ALICE, and LHCb, with separate fits for protons with and without substructure fluctuations (three hot spots).

## Key numbers
- Coherent γ+Pb cross section at W = 125 GeV is overestimated by 50% in the calculation with nucleon shape fluctuations
- At low W ≲ 100 GeV, the measured γ+Pb cross section is well reproduced within uncertainties
- Nuclear suppression factor S_coh for coherent production at W ∼ 45 GeV is approximately 0.9 in data, while the CGC calculation gives slightly more suppression (S_coh ≈ 0.89–0.90)
- For γ+Au collisions at xℙ = 0.01, S_coh = 0.89 (with shape fluctuations) and 0.90 (without), compared to STAR measurement 0.846 ± 0.063 at xℙ = 0.015
- For incoherent γ+Au production at xℙ = 0.01, S_incoh = 0.58 (with shape fluctuations) and 0.32 (without), compared to STAR measurement 0.36^{+0.06}_{-0.07} at xℙ = 0.015
- The incoherent cross section at W = 125 GeV requires a normalization factor of 0.81 to match ALICE data, while the coherent cross section at the same energy requires a factor of 0.71

## Context
This work extends the UPC results of Mantysaari (2022) to the high-energy photon-nucleus collisions covered by recent photoproduction measurements from ALICE (2023), CMS (2023), and STAR (2023). The authors compare CGC predictions to center-of-mass-energy dependent γ+A → J/ψ + A cross sections extracted from ultra peripheral heavy ion collisions using the method proposed by Guzey (2013). The results show that while the CGC framework captures the energy dependence at high W, the data demands even stronger saturation effects than predicted. The t-differential incoherent cross section from ALICE (2023) prefers large event-by-event fluctuations of nucleon substructure in heavy nuclei, comparable to that found for a free proton. Future measurements at the EIC and LHeC/FCC-he will enable further tests of saturation effects, and next-to-leading order calculations remain an open question for the nuclear modification factor.
