# Pairing properties from random distributions of single-particle energy levels

> Exploiting the similarity between the bunched single-particle energy levels of nuclei and of random distributions around the Fermi surface, pairing properties of the latter are calculated to establish statistically-based bounds on the basic characteristics of the pairing phenomenon. When the most probable values for the pairing gaps germane to the BCS formalism are used to calculate thermodynamic quantities, we find that while the ratio of the critical temperature Tc to the zero-temperature pairing gap is close to its BCS Fermi gas value, the ratio of the superfluid to the normal phase specific heats at Tc differs significantly from its Fermi gas counterpart. The largest deviations occur when a few levels lie closely on either side of the Fermi energy but other levels are far away from it. The influence of thermal fluctuations, expected to be large for systems of finite number of particles, were also investigated using a semiclassical treatment of fluctuations. When the average pairing gaps along with those differing by one standard deviations are used, the characteristic discontinuity of the specific heat at Tc in the BCS formalism was transformed to a shoulder-like structure indicating the suppression of a second order phase transition as experimentally observed in nano-particles and several nuclei. Contrasting semiclassical and quantum treatments of fluctuations for the random spacing model is currently underway.

## Key claim

The paper establishes statistically-based bounds on pairing properties by showing that while the ratio of critical temperature to zero-temperature pairing gap in the random spacing model remains close to the BCS Fermi gas value of approximately 0.57, the ratio of superfluid to normal phase specific heats at the critical temperature can deviate significantly from the Fermi gas value of 2.43, with the largest deviations occurring when a few single-particle levels lie closely on either side of the Fermi energy but other levels are far away from it.

## Method

The authors introduced a random spacing (RS) model in which single-particle energy levels are randomly distributed around the Fermi surface to mimic the bunched shell-model orbitals in nuclei generated through different energy density functionals. They solved the BCS equations generalized to include angular momentum for a large number (at least 500) of independent random realizations of single-particle energy levels for a given particle number, and then examined the influence of thermal fluctuations using a semiclassical treatment of fluctuations following the approach of Refs. [LLI] and [Morettoplb].

## Key numbers

- Ratio Tc/Delta0 for the RS model with spin-doublet levels: 0.57 ± 0.05 (compared to approximately 0.57 for the Fermi gas and constant spacing models)
- Ratio of superfluid to normal specific heats at Tc for RS model with spin-doublet levels: 2.71 ± 0.73 (compared to approximately 2.43 for the Fermi gas and constant spacing models)
- Q = -(1/Tc)(dDelta^2/dT) at Tc for RS model with spin-doublet levels: 9.51 ± 0.81 (compared to approximately 9.4 for the Fermi gas and constant spacing models)
- For RS model with d=2j+1 degeneracies: Tc/Delta0 = 0.57 ± 0.04, C_V^(s)/C_V^(n) at Tc = 3.05 ± 1.53, Q = 9.55 ± 0.98
- For N=30 in RS model: Tc/Delta0 = 0.56 ± 0.04, C_V^(s)/C_V^(n) = 3.23 ± 1.47, Q = 9.81 ± 1.07
- For N=119 in RS model: Tc/Delta0 = 0.57 ± 0.04, C_V^(s)/C_V^(n) = 3.46 ± 2.15, Q = 9.71 ± 1.17
- Mmax/Mc ratio in RS model: 1.22 ± 0.12, in accord with approximately 1.22 for the constant spacing model

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing properties from the Fermi gas and constant spacing models to a random spacing model that better captures the bunched single-particle level structure observed in nuclei from HFB calculations using energy density functionals such as SkO'. The results provide statistically-based bounds for pairing characteristics that can be compared with HF+BCS calculations for specific nuclei like 57Co, 126Sn, and 197Pt, and the semiclassical treatment of fluctuations follows the approach of Morettoplb and LLI. The findings enable future comparisons with a full quantum treatment of fluctuations for the random spacing model, which the authors state is currently underway.
