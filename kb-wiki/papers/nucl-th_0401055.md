# Raynal's use of the word "aberrant" appears more appropriate for his ECIS formulation

> Recently, we published a paper (Nucl. Phys. A 728 (2003) 65; see nucl-th/0306068) presenting a new calculational method for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering at low energies. That method is particularly appropriate for analyses in the region of narrow resonances. The method is based upon the sturmian representation of the S-matrix, and allows inclusion of nonlocality effects due to Pauli principle. It also provides a systematic identification of narrow-resonance spectra and subthreshold bound states. A phenomenological test calculation for low-energy (below 4 MeV) neutrons on 12-C (including the first two excitations of the target) was presented to illustrate the validity of the approach. The model calculation received a violent criticism (see nucl-th/0312038) by the developer of a method (ECIS) which to date cannot handle nonlocality effects and cannot be used easily to identify all narrow resonances. We demonstrate that Raynal's opposition to our development is not well founded by the arguments he presents. Indeed the work we published shows, on rewording the title of nucl-th/0312038, that it is ``aberrant'' phenomenologically to analyze resonant low-energy nucleon-nucleus processes with coupled-channel methods without taking into account the nonlocalities due to the Pauli principle; problems typically encountered in the ECIS formulation.

# Raynal's use of the word "aberrant" appears more appropriate for his ECIS formulation

## Key claim

The authors defend their sturmian-based method for low-energy nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering against criticism by Raynal, arguing that his ECIS formulation is itself "aberrant" because it cannot handle nonlocality effects due to the Pauli principle and cannot easily identify all narrow resonances. The claim is conditional on the low-energy regime (below 4 MeV) where only the elastic channel is accessible and the main issue is the structure of the resonant spectrum.

## Method

The authors use a sturmian representation of the S-matrix to algebrize the multichannel scattering problem through a finite-rank expansion of the interaction, allowing systematic identification of narrow and super-narrow resonances via sturmian trajectories without requiring an extremely fine energy grid. They incorporate a phenomenological deformed optical potential including central, spin-orbit, spin-spin, and orbit-orbit terms carried to second order in the deformation parameter, with symmetrization to ensure hermiticity. Pauli-forbidden states are eliminated from the channel couplings using orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials.

## Key numbers

- The test calculation was performed for neutrons on 12-C at energies below 4 MeV, including the first two excitations of the target.
- The spin-spin potential was necessary to achieve the correct separation between J^π = 3/2+ and J^π = 5/2+ resonances in the spectrum.
- The deformation parameter expansion uses L=2 (quadrupole) tensor operators of type Y2(r̂)·Y2(ŝ) and Y4(r̂)·Y4(ŝ).
- The Sherif and Blair calculations referenced by Raynal were at energies above 20 MeV, with differences found at forward angles for inelastic analyzing powers; the same reference warns that at 18.6 MeV incident protons the claim is not fully consistent.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier method (Amos et al., Nucl. Phys. A 728 (2003) 65) for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering at low energies, and directly responds to criticism by Raynal (nucl-th/0312038) regarding the spin-orbit potential form. The approach contrasts with the ECIS coupled-channel method, which cannot incorporate Pauli principle nonlocalities; the authors note that this violation of the exclusion principle in collective-model calculations has been documented in textbooks by Mahaux and by Greiner. The method enables future analyses of narrow-resonance spectra and subthreshold bound states in low-energy nucleon-nucleus scattering, particularly where Pauli blocking significantly affects the spectra.
