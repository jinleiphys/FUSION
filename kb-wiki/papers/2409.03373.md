# Doubly heavy tetraquark bound and resonant states

> We calculate the energy spectrum of the S-wave doubly heavy tetraquark systems, including the $ QQ^{(\prime)}\bar q\bar q$, $QQ^{(\prime)}\bar s\bar q$, and $ QQ^{(\prime)}\bar s\bar s$ ($Q^{(\prime)}=b,c$ and $q=u,d$) systems within the constituent quark model. We use the complex scaling method to obtain bound states and resonant states simultaneously, and the Gaussian expansion method to solve the complex-scaled four-body Schrödinger equation. With a novel definition of the root-mean-square radii, we are able to distinguish between meson molecules and compact tetraquark states. The compact tetraquarks are further classified into three different types with distinct spatial configurations: compact even tetraquarks, compact diquark-antidiquark tetraquarks and compact diquark-centered tetraquarks. In the $ I(J^P)=0(1^+) $ $QQ\bar q\bar q$ system, there exists the $ D^*D $ molecular bound state with a binding energy of $ -14 $ MeV, which is the candidate for $ T_{cc}(3875)^+ $. The shallow $\bar B^*\bar B$ molecular bound state is the bottom analog of $T_{cc}(3875)^+$. Moreover, we identify two resonant states near the $D^*D^*$ and $\bar B^*\bar B^*$ thresholds. In the $ J^P=1^+ $ $bb\bar q\bar q\,(I=0)$ and $bb\bar s\bar q$ systems, we obtain deeply bound states with a compact diquark-centered tetraquark configuration and a dominant $χ_{\bar 3_c\otimes 3_c}$ component, along with resonant states with similar configurations as their radial excitations. These states are the QCD analog of the helium atom. We also obtain some other bound states and resonant states with ``QCD hydrogen molecule" configurations. Moreover, we investigate the heavy quark mass dependence of the $ I(J^P)=0(1^+) $ $ QQ\bar q\bar q $ bound states. We strongly urge the experimental search for the predicted states.

## Key claim

The authors claim that within a constituent quark model, the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ candidate appears as a $D^*D$ molecular bound state with a binding energy of $-14$ MeV, which is significantly deeper than the experimentally observed binding energy of around $-300$ keV; they note that quark model uncertainties of tens of MeV allow this interpretation, but acknowledge that improvements are needed to describe long-range hadronic interactions.

## Method

The authors solved the complex-scaled four-body Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method within a nonrelativistic constituent quark model employing the AL1 potential, which includes one-gluon-exchange and linear confinement interactions. They applied the complex scaling method to obtain bound states and resonant states simultaneously, and used a novel definition of root-mean-square radii computed from a decomposed non-antisymmetric wave function to distinguish between meson molecules and compact tetraquark states. The compact tetraquarks were further classified into compact even, compact diquark-antidiquark, and compact diquark-centered configurations based on spatial structure analysis.

## Key numbers

- $T_{cc,0(1)}(3864)$: bound state in $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ $cc\bar q\bar q$ system with binding energy $-14$ MeV relative to $D^*D$ threshold; candidate for $T_{cc}(3875)^+$
- $T_{bb,0(1)}(10491)$: deeply bound state in $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ $bb\bar q\bar q$ system with binding energy $-153$ MeV; compact diquark-centered tetraquark with $r_{b_1b_2}^{\rm rms}=0.33$ fm and $97\%$ $\chi_{\bar 3_c\otimes 3_c}$ color component
- $T_{bb,0(1)}(10642)$: shallow bound state in $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ $bb\bar q\bar q$ system with binding energy $-1$ MeV; $\bar B^*\bar B$ molecular state
- $T_{cc,0(1)}(4031)$: resonant state in $cc\bar q\bar q$ system with mass $4031$ MeV and width $\Gamma=54$ MeV, located $1$ MeV below $D^*D^*$ threshold
- $T_{bb,0(1)}(10700)$: resonant state in $bb\bar q\bar q$ system with mass $10700$ MeV and width $\Gamma=2$ MeV, identified as $\bar B^*\bar B^*$ molecular state
- $T_{bb,0(0)}(11195)$: bound state in $I(J^P)=0(0^+)$ $bb\bar q\bar q$ system with binding energy $-111$ MeV
- $T_{bb,0(2)}(11370)$: bound state in $I(J^P)=0(2^+)$ $bb\bar q\bar q$ system with binding energy $-20$ MeV
- $T_{bc,0(2)}(7363)$: molecular bound state in $I(J^P)=0(2^+)$ $bc\bar q\bar q$ system with binding energy $-3$ MeV; $\bar B^*D^*$ molecular state

## Context

This work extends previous studies of doubly heavy tetraquarks using constituent quark models, particularly addressing the discrepancies found in earlier predictions such as those by Yang (2019) who predicted a deeply bound $cc\bar q\bar q$ state with binding energy around $-150$ MeV that contradicted experimental results. The authors build on their previous work using the Gaussian expansion method for tetraquark bound states (Meng, 2023) and resonant states (Chen, 2023; Wu, 2024; Ma, 2024). The results enable experimental searches for predicted bound and resonant states in the $bb\bar q\bar q$, $bc\bar q\bar q$, and systems with strange quarks, and provide a classification scheme for tetraquark spatial configurations that distinguishes QCD helium atom states (compact diquark-centered) from QCD hydrogen molecule states (compact even tetraquarks).
