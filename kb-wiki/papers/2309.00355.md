# $^{16}$O spectral function from coupled-cluster theory: applications to lepton-nucleus scattering

> We calculate the $^{16}$O spectral function by combining coupled-cluster theory with a Gaussian integral transform and by expanding the integral kernel in terms of Chebyshev polynomials to allow for a quantification of the theoretical uncertainties. We perform an analysis of the spectral function and employ it to predict lepton-nucleus scattering. Our results well describe the $^{16}$O electron scattering data in the quasi-elastic peak for momentum transfers $|\mathbf{q}|\gtrapprox500$ MeV and electron energies up to 1.2 GeV, extending therefore the so-called first principles approach to lepton-nucleus cross sections well into the relativistic regime. To prove the applicability of this method to neutrino-nucleus cross sections, we implement our $^{16}$O spectral functions in the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator and provide a comparison with recently published T2K neutrino data.

## Key claim
The authors calculate the $^{16}$O spectral function from coupled-cluster theory using a Chebyshev polynomial expansion of the Gaussian integral kernel, enabling quantification of theoretical uncertainties. Their results describe electron scattering data well only for momentum transfers above approximately 500 MeV, and they note that the imaginary part of the optical potential is omitted, meaning the predictions may be too high and require cancellation from omitted meson-exchange current and pion production contributions.

## Method
The authors combine coupled-cluster theory at the singles-and-doubles (CCSD) level with the Chebyshev expansion of the integral kernel (ChEK method) to reconstruct the spectral function as a histogram with controlled uncertainty bounds. They use the NNLO$_\mathrm{sat}$ chiral Hamiltonian with both nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, approximate three-nucleon interactions at the normal-ordered two-body level, and work in a model space of 15 oscillator shells with harmonic oscillator frequencies from 12 to 20 MeV. The spectral function is then employed within the impulse approximation to compute lepton-nucleus cross sections, including final-state interactions via the real part of an optical potential from Cooper (1993).

## Key numbers
- Momentum transfer threshold: $|\mathbf{q}| \gtrapprox 500$ MeV for good agreement with electron scattering data
- Electron energies: up to 1.2 GeV
- Histogram bin width: $2\Delta = 3.5$ MeV
- Kernel resolution parameter: $\Lambda = 0.66$ MeV
- Number of Chebyshev moments: $N = 4000$
- Hamiltonian energy cutoff for Chebyshev scaling: $E = 250$ MeV
- Dominant spectral function peaks: approximately 13 and 21 MeV for neutrons, 10 and 18 MeV for protons (corresponding to $1p_{1/2}$ and $1p_{3/2}$ states)
- Optimal harmonic oscillator frequency: $\hbar\Omega = 14$ MeV (minimizes non-physical negative contributions to below per-mil level)
- Uncertainty at the quasi-elastic peak: approximately 10%
- Optical potential reliability threshold: kinetic energy $t_\mathrm{kin} > 25$ MeV (momentum $|\mathbf{p}'| \approx 215$ MeV)

## Context
This work extends the first-principles approach to lepton-nucleus scattering into the relativistic regime for $^{16}$O, building on prior coupled-cluster calculations for $^4$He by Sobczyk (2022) and the Lorentz integral transform combined with coupled-cluster theory (LIT-CC) by Sobczyk (2020, 2021). The results are benchmarked against self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations by Rocco (2018) and Barbieri (2019), showing good agreement in charge distributions and momentum distributions. The spectral functions are implemented in the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator, enabling comparison with T2K neutrino data and providing a pathway for quantifying nuclear uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE and T2HK.
