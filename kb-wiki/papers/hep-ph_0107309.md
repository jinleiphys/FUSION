# QCD Factorized Drell-Yan Cross Section at Large Transverse Momentum

> We derive a new factorization formula in perturbative quantum chromodynamics for the Drell-Yan massive lepton-pair cross section as a function of the transverse momentum $Q_T$ of the pair. When $Q_T$ is much larger than the pair's invariant mass $Q$, this factorization formula systematically resums the logarithmic contributions of the type $α_s^m \ln^m(Q_T^2/Q^2)$ to all orders in the strong coupling $α_s$. When $Q_T\sim Q$, our formula yields the same Drell-Yan cross section as conventional fixed order QCD perturbation theory. We show that resummation is important when the collision energy $\sqrt{S}$ is large enough and $Q_T\gg Q$, and we argue that perturbative expansions are more stable and reliable in terms of the modified factorization formula.

## Key claim

The paper derives a modified QCD factorization formula for the Drell-Yan cross section that systematically resums large logarithmic contributions of the type α_s^m ln^m(Q_T^2/Q^2) to all orders when the transverse momentum Q_T is much larger than the pair's invariant mass Q, while reducing to conventional fixed-order perturbation theory when Q_T ∼ Q. The authors argue that this reorganization makes perturbative expansions more stable and reliable, but note that the resummation is important only when the collision energy √S is large enough and Q_T ≫ Q.

## Method

The authors identify that when Q_T^2 ≫ Q^2, the Drell-Yan cross section receives large logarithmic contributions from partonic processes where the virtual photon becomes nearly collinear to a final-state parton, following the same two-stage pattern as fragmentation contributions in prompt photon production. They reorganize the conventional single perturbative expansion into two perturbative expansions plus perturbatively calculable parton-to-virtual-photon fragmentation functions, where the short-distance parton production is evaluated at a single hard scale (~Q_T) and the fragmentation functions absorb the logarithmic dependence on Q. The modified factorization formula includes both the resummed logarithmic contributions (fragmentation) and the non-logarithmic direct contributions, with the difference between modified and conventional factorization at next-to-leading order determined by QCD evolution of the virtual photon fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The Drell-Yan factor α_em/(3πQ^2) < 10^(-3)/Q^2 renders production rates for massive lepton pairs small at large Q and Q_T
- Cross sections are presented for Tevatron energy √S = 2.0 TeV, LHC energy √S = 14 TeV, and RHIC proton-proton energy √S = 500 GeV at rapidity y = 0
- Mass intervals considered: 2 ≤ Q ≤ 3 GeV and 4 ≤ Q ≤ 5 GeV
- The UA1 Collaboration measured Drell-Yan production for virtual photon mass Q ∈ [2m_μ, 2.5] GeV
- The ratio R_z is shown at Tevatron energy √S = 2 TeV, y = 0, and Q = 2 GeV for transverse momenta Q_T = 5 and 50 GeV

## Context

This work extends the conventional QCD factorization formula for Drell-Yan production (Drell and Yan, 1970; Politzer, 1977) to handle the two-scale problem when Q_T ≫ Q, building on earlier work by Berger, Gordon, and Klasen (1998) who identified the Drell-Yan transverse momentum distribution as a source of constraints on the gluon distribution. The approach parallels the Collins-Soper-Sterman resummation formalism (1982-1984) for the case Q_T^2 ≪ Q^2, and uses perturbatively calculable parton-to-virtual-photon fragmentation functions developed by Qiu and Zhang (2001). The modified factorization enables reliable predictions for Drell-Yan production at low invariant mass and high transverse momentum at the Tevatron, LHC, and RHIC, where the gluon-initiated Compton subprocess dominates and can provide constraints on gluon parton densities.
