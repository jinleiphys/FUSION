# $πNN$ coupling determined beyond the chiral limit

> Within the conventional QCD sum rules, we calculate the $πNN$ coupling constant, $g_{πN}$, beyond the chiral limit using two-point correlation function with a pion. We consider the Dirac structure, $iγ_5$, at $m_π^2$ order, which has clear dependence on the PS and PV coupling schemes for the pion-nucleon interactions. For a consistent treatment of the sum rule, we include the linear terms in quark mass as they constitute the same chiral order as $m_π^2$. Using the PS coupling scheme for the pion-nucleon interaction, we obtain $g_{πN}=13.3\pm 1.2$, which is very close to the empirical $πNN$ coupling. This demonstrates that going beyond the chiral limit is crucial in determining the coupling and the pseudoscalar coupling scheme is preferable from the QCD point of view.

# πNN coupling determined beyond the chiral limit

## Key claim

Using conventional QCD sum rules with the $i\gamma_5$ Dirac structure at $m_\pi^2$ order, the pseudoscalar (PS) coupling scheme yields $g_{\pi N}=13.3\pm 1.2$, remarkably close to the empirical value of 13.4, but only when linear quark-mass terms are consistently included as they constitute the same chiral order as $m_\pi^2$; the pseudovector (PV) scheme would give a value twice as large and is therefore disfavored from a QCD perspective.

## Method

The author computes the two-point correlation function of nucleon interpolating fields with an external pion state, Eq. (1), using Ioffe's nucleon current. The OPE side is evaluated at $m_\pi^2$ order by keeping the quark-antiquark component of the pion wave function, including three-particle gluonic contributions, and retaining linear quark-mass terms (which are converted via the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation). The phenomenological side is constructed separately for PS and PV coupling Lagrangians, and the sum rule is matched after Borel transformation, then divided by the nucleon chiral-odd sum rule to eliminate the unknown nucleon coupling $\lambda$.

## Key numbers

- $g_{\pi N} = 13.3 \pm 1.2$ from the PS coupling scheme, compared to the empirical value of 13.4.
- The continuum threshold is taken as $S_\pi = 2.07$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to the Roper resonance; varying to $S_\pi = 2.57$ GeV$^2$ changes the result by only 2% at $M^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$.
- The Borel window used for the fit is $0.65 \le M^2 \le 1.24$ GeV$^2$.
- Standard QCD parameters: $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.23\ \text{GeV})^3$, $\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \mathcal{G}^2 \rangle = (0.33\ \text{GeV})^4$, $m_0^2 = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2$, $f_\pi = 0.093$ GeV, $f_{3\pi} = 0.003$ GeV$^2$.
- The PV coupling scheme would yield $g_{\pi N}$ twice the PS value, i.e., approximately 26.6.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum-rule calculations of $g_{\pi N}$ that used the $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu}$ structure (Kim, Lee, and Oka, 1999) and obtained $g_{\pi N} \sim 10$, which was close to the chiral-limit value rather than the empirical one. Earlier calculations by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1995) used the $i\gamma_5$ structure in the soft-pion limit, while Birse and Krippa (1996) used the $\gamma_5 \not{p}$ structure. By going beyond the chiral limit and consistently including quark-mass terms, the present work achieves agreement with the empirical coupling and provides a QCD-based criterion favoring the pseudoscalar coupling scheme over the pseudovector scheme. This enables more reliable QCD sum-rule calculations of other meson-baryon couplings, such as the $\eta NN$ coupling (Kim, in preparation).
