# Exclusive Electro-Disintegration of 3He at High Q2: I. Generalized Eikonal Approximation

> We develop a theoretical framework for calculation of high $Q^2$ exclusive electro-disintegration of A=3 systems. The main result of this work is the calculation of the final state interaction of the struck energetic nucleon with recoil nucleons within the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) which allows us to account for the finite and relatively large momenta of the bound nucleons in the nucleus. The important advantage of this approach is the possibility to study in a self-consistent way the short range correlations in nuclei. This is due to the fact that GEA does not require a stationary condition for recoil nucleons as conventional Glauber approximation does. As a result GEA treats explicitly the Fermi motion of recoil nucleons in the nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper develops a theoretical framework for calculating high \(Q^2\) exclusive electro-disintegration of \(A=3\) systems using the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), which accounts for finite and relatively large momenta of bound nucleons in the nucleus. The key advantage over the conventional Glauber approximation is that GEA does not require a stationary condition for recoil nucleons, allowing self-consistent study of short-range correlations in nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering amplitude for the \(^3He(e,e'NN)N\) reaction within the generalized eikonal approximation, expressing the amplitude as a sum of diagrams corresponding to \(n\)'th order rescattering of the knocked-out nucleon with residual nucleons. They evaluate each diagram using effective Feynman diagram rules derived within GEA, reducing covariant nuclear vertices to nonrelativistic nuclear wave functions in the limit of small internal momenta. The residual interaction between the two recoil nucleons is described using a scattering representation of the two-nucleon continuum state wave function, evaluated through NN scattering amplitudes in the low-to-intermediate energy region.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic region studied is \(4 \ge Q^2 \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\), with the lower limit providing high-momentum transfer and the upper limit ensuring color coherence effects are small.
- Missing momentum and recoil nucleon momenta are restricted to \(|{\bf p_m}|,|{\bf p_{r2}}|, |{\bf p_{r3}}| \le 400-500\) MeV/c, allowing nucleons to be treated as basic degrees of freedom.
- The approximation of helicity conservation in soft NN scattering is accurate at the level of 2-3% starting at \(Q^2 \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The effective longitudinal momentum transfer in NN rescattering is defined as \(\Delta^0 = (q_0/q)(T_{r2}+T_{r3}+|\epsilon_A|)\), which becomes important at large recoil nucleon energies \(T_{r2}, T_{r3} \sim m\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized eikonal approximation previously applied to deuteron electro-disintegration (Sargsian et al., earlier works) to the \(A=3\) system, specifically \(^3He\). It addresses the need for systematic theoretical studies of high-energy exclusive break-up of few-nucleon systems motivated by experiments at Jefferson Lab with upgraded CEBAF energies. The framework enables future numerical calculations (Part II of this work) to study short-range two- and three-nucleon correlations in nuclei, using realistic ground state wave functions of \(^3He\) based on NN interaction potentials and three-nucleon forces (Bochum group).
