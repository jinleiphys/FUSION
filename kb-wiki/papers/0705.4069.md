# Ab Initio Study of 40Ca with an Importance Truncated No-Core Shell Model

> We propose an importance truncation scheme for the no-core shell model, which enables converged calculations for nuclei well beyond the p-shell. It is based on an a priori measure for the importance of individual basis states constructed by means of many-body perturbation theory. Only the physically relevant states of the no-core model space are considered, which leads to a dramatic reduction of the basis dimension. We analyze the validity and efficiency of this truncation scheme using different realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and compare to conventional no-core shell model calculations for 4He and 16O. Then, we present the first converged calculations for the ground state of 40Ca within no-core model spaces including up to 16\hbarΩ-excitations using realistic low-momentum interactions. The scheme is universal and can be easily applied to other quantum many-body problems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an importance truncation scheme for the no-core shell model that uses a first-order many-body perturbation theory measure to select only physically relevant basis states, enabling converged calculations for nuclei like 40Ca that were previously intractable. The scheme is presented as a variational method that converges from above to the exact eigenvalue for a given interaction, but the authors note that for 40Ca they restrict to the 3p3h level and for 16O to the 4p4h level, meaning the results are upper bounds that may miss higher-order correlations.

## Method

The authors start with a reference state and generate all possible n-particle-n-hole (npnh) excitations up to a maximum excitation energy NmaxℏΩ. They compute an importance weight κν for each basis state using first-order many-body perturbation theory, then retain only states with κν ≥ κmin. The eigenvalue problem is solved in this truncated space, and the process is iterated: dominant components of the resulting ground state serve as a new reference, generating higher-order npnh states in subsequent iterations. For 40Ca the authors restrict to up to 3p3h states, while for 16O they include up to 4p4h states, using either the UCOM correlated interaction or the Vlowk low-momentum interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca with the UCOM interaction at ℏΩ=17 MeV, exponential extrapolation of the 3p3h energies to infinite model-space size gives E∞ ≈ −316 MeV, compared to the experimental binding energy of −342.05 MeV.
- For 40Ca with the Vlowk interaction at ℏΩ=24 MeV, the ground-state energy at Nmax=16 (3p3h) is −461.83 MeV, with an extrapolated E∞ of −462.7 MeV.
- For 16O with the Vlowk interaction at ℏΩ=22 MeV, the ground-state energy at Nmax=14 (4p4h) is −137.75 MeV, with an extrapolated E∞ of −138.0 MeV.
- For 40Ca with Vlowk at ℏΩ=24 MeV, the point mass radius is 2.27 fm and the charge radius is 2.43 fm.
- For 16O with Vlowk at ℏΩ=22 MeV, the point mass radius is 2.03 fm and the charge radius is 2.20 fm.
- The dimension of the importance truncated space for 40Ca around Nmax=16 is of order 10^7, which is smaller than the full NCSM space at Nmax=4.
- For 4He at Nmax=20, the full NCSM model space dimension is beyond 2×10^7, while the truncated basis dimension is of order 10^5.

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell method (NCSM) of Navratil and Ormand (2002) beyond the p-shell, where full NCSM calculations become computationally intractable due to exponential growth of basis dimensions. The importance truncation scheme is benchmarked against full NCSM calculations for 4He and 16O using the Antoine code of Caurier and Nowacki (1999), and the results for 40Ca are compared with coupled-cluster calculations using the same Vlowk interaction (Hagen, private communication). The scheme enables future converged ab initio studies of medium-mass nuclei and is presented as universally applicable to other quantum many-body problems treated by diagonalization techniques.
