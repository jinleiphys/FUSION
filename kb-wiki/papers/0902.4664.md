# Resolving the J/ψRHIC puzzles at LHC

> Experiments with gold-gold collisions at RHIC have revealed (i) stronger suppression of charmonium production at forward rapidity than at midrapidity and (ii) the similarity between the suppression degrees at RHIC and SPS energies. To describe these findings we employ the model that includes nuclear shadowing effects, calculated within the Glauber-Gribov theory, rapidity-dependent absorptive mechanism, caused by energy-momentum conservation, and dissociation and recombination of the charmonium due to interaction with co-moving matter. The free parameters of the model are tuned and fixed by comparison with experimental data at lower energies. A good agreement with the RHIC results concerning the rapidity and centrality distributions is obtained for both heavy Au+Au and light Cu+Cu colliding system. For pA and A+A collisions at LHC the model predicts stronger suppression of the charmonium and bottomonium yields in stark contrast to thermal model predictions.

# Resolving the J/ψ RHIC puzzles at LHC

## Key claim

The model, which includes nuclear shadowing, rapidity-dependent absorption from energy-momentum conservation, and charmonium dissociation and recombination with co-moving matter, can simultaneously explain both the stronger suppression at forward rapidity than at midrapidity at RHIC and the similarity of suppression between RHIC and SPS energies; however, for LHC the model predicts stronger suppression of charmonium and bottomonium yields than at RHIC, in stark contrast to thermal model predictions that predict enhancement.

## Method

The authors employ a model that combines three suppression factors within the nuclear modification factor: nuclear shadowing calculated via the Glauber-Gribov theory using the generalized Schwimmer model, a rapidity-dependent absorptive mechanism incorporating energy-momentum conservation with parameters γ=2 and ε=0.75, and a co-movers interaction model (CIM) that includes both dissociation and recombination of c-cbar pairs via a rate equation assuming longitudinal expansion and boost invariance. The free parameters are tuned and fixed by comparison with experimental data at lower energies (SPS), then applied to RHIC Au+Au and Cu+Cu data at √s = 200 AGeV and extrapolated to LHC Pb+Pb at √s = 5.5 TeV.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, the nuclear absorption cross section is σ_abs = 5 mb at mid-rapidity, derived from σ_QQ = 20 mb with γ=2 and ε=0.75
- For LHC Pb+Pb at √s = 5.5 TeV, the parameter C (controlling recombination) is estimated as C = 2.5 using σ_pp non-diffractive = 59 mb and σ_cc ∝ s^α with α = 0.3, about four times larger than the corresponding value at RHIC
- The realistic range for C at LHC is estimated to be 2 to 3
- Bottomonium (Υ) suppression due to gluon shadowing in Pb+Pb at LHC is about 50% from mid-central to central collisions, and is the same for all members of the Υ family

## Context

This work extends the co-movers interaction model (CIM) of Capella (2005) by incorporating recombination of c-cbar pairs into J/ψ, as modified in a 2008 publication. It directly addresses the PHENIX measurements at RHIC (Adare, 2007) that showed unexpected features: similar suppression at RHIC and SPS energies despite an order-of-magnitude difference in center-of-mass energy, and increasing suppression with rapidity. The predictions for LHC contrast sharply with thermal model predictions of Andronic (2007) that predicted strong enhancement of J/ψ yield with increasing centrality; the present model predicts continued suppression due to large comover density and strong initial-state shadowing. The results establish a baseline for future calculations of bottomonium dissociation and recombination at LHC.
