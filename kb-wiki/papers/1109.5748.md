# Is there evidence for sterile neutrinos in IceCube data?

> Data from the LSND and MiniBooNE experiments, and revised expectations of the antineutrino flux from nuclear reactors suggest the existence of eV-mass sterile neutrinos. 3+2 and 1+3+1 scenarios accommodate all relevant short-baseline neutrino data except for the low-energy MiniBooNE anomaly. We analyze the angular distribution of upward going atmospheric neutrino events in the IceCube-40 dataset for evidence of sterile neutrinos within these scenarios. Depending on how systematic uncertainties are handled, we find strong evidence for, or weak evidence against sterile neutrinos. We show that future IceCube data will definitively settle the issue.

## Key claim

Depending on how systematic uncertainties in the angular distribution are handled, the IceCube-40 data provide either strong evidence for, or weak evidence against, eV-mass sterile neutrinos in the 3+2 and 1+3+1 scenarios that fit short-baseline data.

## Method

The authors analyzed the zenith-angle distribution of 12877 atmospheric muon neutrino events from the IceCube-40 dataset in the energy range 332 GeV to 84 TeV. They compared the observed angular distribution to predictions from three-neutrino oscillations and from 3+2 and 1+3+1 sterile neutrino scenarios using the global best-fit parameters from Kopp (2011), allowing an overall normalization factor and a systematic tilt parameter to float in a chi-squared fit to 9 non-horizontal angular bins.

## Key numbers

* 12877 atmospheric muon neutrino events observed by IceCube-40 in the energy range 332 GeV to 84 TeV (Abbasi 2011).
* The 3+2 scenario best-fit parameters: delta-m-squared_41 = 0.47 eV^2, delta-m-squared_51 = 0.87 eV^2, |U_e4| = 0.128, |U_mu4| = 0.165, |U_e5| = 0.138, |U_mu5| = 0.148, delta/pi = 1.64 (Kopp 2011).
* The 1+3+1(a) scenario best-fit parameters: delta-m-squared_41 = -0.47 eV^2, delta-m-squared_51 = 0.87 eV^2, |U_e4| = 0.129, |U_mu4| = 0.154, |U_e5| = 0.142, |U_mu5| = 0.163, delta/pi = 0.35 (Kopp 2011).
* Without a systematic tilt (b=0), chi-squared values: 3-nu = 35.2, 3+2 = 18.5, 1+3+1(a) = 18.1, 1+3+1(b) = 16.4.
* With a systematic tilt allowed, chi-squared values: 3-nu = 13.0 (b=0.17), 3+2 = 15.2 (b=0.066), 1+3+1(a) = 15.6 (b=-0.058), 1+3+1(b) = 16.0 (b=-0.024).
* The muon neutrino flux is suppressed by approximately 10% above 100 GeV in the sterile neutrino scenarios.
* With 6.5x10^4 future up-going events and no deviation from 3-nu, chi-squared values would be: 3+2 = 111, 1+3+1(a) = 386, 1+3+1(b) = 331.

## Context

This work extends the search for eV-mass sterile neutrinos motivated by the LSND (Aguilar 2001) and MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010) anomalies and the revised reactor antineutrino flux calculation (Mueller 2011). The analysis uses the global best-fit 3+2 and 1+3+1 scenarios from Kopp (2011) that accommodate short-baseline data. The paper demonstrates that the IceCube-40 dataset, despite systematic uncertainties, can already discriminate between sterile and standard three-neutrino scenarios, and that future IceCube data with 1.3x10^5 events above 332 GeV will definitively confirm or exclude these sterile neutrino models.
