# Two pion bound states in a hot pion gas

> The occurrence of poles in the $2π$ propagator in a hot pion medium is studied. The domain of non--trivial solutions to the corresponding bosonic gap equation is investigated as a function of the chemical potential and temperature of the gas.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a hot pion gas can support bound two-pion states (poles in the $2\pi$ propagator) even when no such bound state exists in free space, but only within a specific temperature window between a lower critical temperature $T_0$ and an upper critical temperature $T_c$ for a given chemical potential; this behavior is opposite to the fermionic case where increasing temperature destroys pairing.

## Method

The authors use a separable model for the free-space $\pi$–$\pi$ interaction in which the $2\pi$ state couples to an intermediate $\sigma$ meson ($\ell=0, I=0$) or $\rho$ meson ($\ell=1, I=1$), with coupling constants and form factors chosen to reproduce free-space $\pi$–$\pi$ phase shifts. They derive the in-medium $2\pi$ propagator and the corresponding bosonic gap equation from an effective Hamiltonian that includes a chemical potential for pions, then solve the pole condition and gap equation numerically to map the domain of non-trivial solutions in the $\mu$–$T$ plane.

## Key numbers

- For a fixed pion chemical potential of $\mu = 100$ MeV, the lower critical temperature for the existence of a bound state pole is $T_0 \approx 80$ MeV (pole at $E = 2(m_\pi - \mu)$) and the upper critical temperature is $T_c \approx 137$ MeV (pole at $E = 0$).
- The coupling constant squared $1/g_\sigma^2 \approx 486$ MeV is the value at which the pole condition is satisfied.
- The maximum gap strength $\delta_{\rm max}$ equals $m_\pi - \mu$, and the gap vanishes at the same critical temperature $T_c$ where the bound state pole disappears.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Chanfray, Schuck, and Welke, 1993) to study $\pi$–$\pi$ interactions in a dense and hot pion gas, and is motivated by hadronic cascade simulations (RQMD, ARC, Welke and Bertsch, 1993; Greiner and Rischke, 1993) that use free-space interactions. The finding that finite temperature can induce pairing in bosons, analogous to the Cooper pole in fermion systems, contrasts with the $T=0$ case discussed by Saint James and Nozieres (1964) where a nontrivial gap solution requires a two-boson bound state in free space. The results are preliminary steps toward a Brueckner–Hartree–Fock–Bogoliubov equation of state for a hot pion gas, with open questions about avoiding collapse due to purely attractive $\pi$–$\pi$ interactions and distinguishing single-boson from pair condensation.
