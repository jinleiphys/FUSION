# The Electron-Ion Collider: Assessing the Energy Dependence of Key Measurements

> We provide an assessment of the energy dependence of key measurements within the scope of the machine parameters for a U.S. based Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) outlined in the EIC White Paper. We first examine the importance of the physics underlying these measurements in the context of the outstanding questions in nuclear science. We then demonstrate, through detailed simulations of the measurements, that the likelihood of transformational scientific insights is greatly enhanced by making the energy range and reach of the EIC as large as practically feasible.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates through detailed simulations that the likelihood of transformational scientific insights at a U.S.-based Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) is greatly enhanced by making the energy range and reach as large as practically feasible, specifically comparing a maximum center-of-mass energy of 40 GeV versus 90 GeV for electron-nucleus collisions.

## Method

The authors performed detailed simulations of key measurements across the proposed EIC energy range, including charged current DIS cross-sections, diffractive structure functions, dihadron correlations, and exclusive vector meson production. They employed the dipole model of deep inelastic scattering to compute observables such as the dipole forward scattering amplitude and its dependence on the saturation scale Q_s^2 and virtuality Q^2, comparing the kinematic reach at different center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- The maximal saturation scale Q_s^2 in electron-nucleus collisions at the EIC is approximately 50% larger for sqrt(s)_max = 90 GeV compared to sqrt(s)_max = 40 GeV
- The dipole scattering amplitude N varies by only approximately 20% within the Q^2 reach at sqrt(s) = 40 GeV, but reaches up to a factor of 6 within the Q^2 reach at sqrt(s) = 90 GeV for fixed x = 10^{-3}
- For diffractive events, the square of the dipole amplitude changes by a factor of 25 over the Q^2 range at sqrt(s) = 90 GeV, but only by a factor of 1.7 at the lower center-of-mass energy
- Approximately 15% of electron-proton scattering events at HERA were diffractive
- For charged current DIS with Q^2_min = 100 GeV^2, the cross-section drop at lower sqrt(s) values is relatively modest, but becomes dramatic as Q^2_min increases

## Context

This report extends the physics case for the EIC outlined in the EIC White Paper (Accardi et al., 2012) and builds on lessons from deep inelastic scattering experiments at HERA and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. The study demonstrates that the higher energy option (sqrt(s) = 90 GeV) enables access to the gluon saturation regime through measurements such as the sign change in the diffractive structure function ratio between nuclei and protons, which is out of reach at sqrt(s) = 40 GeV. The work enables future experimental design decisions for the EIC facility parameters.
