# Novel charmonium-like structures in the $J/ψϕ$ and $J/ψω$ invariant mass spectra

> Stimulated by the new evidence of Y(4274) observed in the $J/ψϕ$ invariant mass spectrum, we first propose the charmonium-like state Y(4274) as the S-wave $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)+h.c.$ molecular state with $J^P=0^-$, which is supported well by dynamics study of the system composed of the pseudoscalar and scalar charmed mesons. The S-wave $D\bar{D}_{0}(2400)+h.c.$ molecular charmonium appears as the molecular partner of Y(4274), which is in accord with the enhancement structure appearing at 4.2 GeV in the $J/ψω$ invariant mass spectrum from B decays. Our study shows that the enhancement structures, $i.e.$, the newly observed Y(4274) and the previously announced $Y(4140)/Y(3930)$ in the $J/ψϕ$ and $J/ψω$ invariant mass spectra, can be understood well under the uniform framework of the molecular charmonium, which can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The newly observed Y(4274) enhancement in the J/ψφ invariant mass spectrum is interpreted as an S-wave D_s D̅_s0(2317)+h.c. molecular state with J^P=0^-, supported by a dynamical study of the pseudoscalar-scalar charmed meson system; this assignment depends on the coupling strength h of the D_s0(2317)D_sη interaction, which cannot be extracted experimentally.

## Method

The authors constructed effective potentials for the Y^{s\bar{s}} (D_s D̅_s0(2317)+h.c.) and Y^{u\bar{u}/d\bar{d}} (D D̅_0(2400)+h.c.) systems using heavy meson chiral perturbation theory (HMχPT), including pseudoscalar (π, η), scalar (σ), and vector (ρ, ω, φ) meson exchanges. They solved the Schrödinger equation for these potentials with a cutoff Λ to regularize singularities, varying the coupling parameter h and Λ to find bound state solutions. The binding energies were compared to the mass difference between Y(4274) and the D_s D̅_s0(2317) threshold, and to the enhancement near 4.2 GeV in the J/ψω invariant mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Y(4274) mass: M = 4274.4^{+8.4}_{-6.7} (stat) MeV, width: Γ = 32.3^{+21.9}_{-15.3} (stat) MeV, observed with 3.1σ significance by CDF in the J/ψφ invariant mass spectrum from B → J/ψφK decays with 6 fb^{-1} integrated luminosity.
- The binding energy of the Y^{s\bar{s}} system relative to the D_s0(2317)D_s threshold is approximately -11 MeV.
- For the Y^{u\bar{u}/d\bar{d}} system, binding energies range from -9.85 MeV (Λ=0.9 GeV) to -10.42 MeV (Λ=1.5 GeV), with the bound state solution insensitive to Λ.
- The extracted |h| value for the D_s0(2317)D_sη coupling, corresponding to the -11 MeV binding energy, lies in the range 1.2 to 1.5 with reasonable Λ values around 1 GeV.
- The D_0(2400)Dπ coupling is constrained by the D_0(2400) → Dπ decay width to h = -0.56 ± 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the molecular charmonium framework previously applied to Y(4140) and Y(3930) by Liu et al. (2009) and Liu et al. (2008), which interpreted those states as D_s* D̅_s* and D* D̅* molecules respectively. The paper proposes Y(4274) as the S-wave pseudoscalar-scalar molecular partner of Y(4140), and predicts a corresponding S-wave D D̅_0(2400)+h.c. molecular state decaying to J/ψω, consistent with an enhancement near 4.2 GeV seen by Belle (2004) and BaBar (2007). This uniform molecular picture can be tested by future high-statistics measurements of the J/ψφ and J/ψω invariant mass spectra in B decays, and also suggests that two additional event clusters in the CDF data near ΔM ~ 1.27 GeV and 1.4-1.5 GeV may correspond to other molecular charmonium states.
