# Fundamental Symmetries and Conservation Laws

> I discuss recent progress in low-energy tests of symmetries and conservation laws, including parity nonconservation in atoms and nuclei, electric dipole moment tests of time-reversal invariance, beta-decay correlation studies, and decays violating separate (family) and total lepton number.

## Key claim

Low-energy tests of fundamental symmetries and conservation laws provide a complementary window to high-energy colliders for probing physics both within and beyond the Standard Model, with the caveat that several key results (such as the cesium anapole moment and the isovector hadronic PNC constraint from fluorine-18) are in tension, suggesting either unresolved theoretical uncertainties or the need for new experiments.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical progress across four areas: atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) measurements of weak charges and anapole moments; nucleon-nucleon PNC studies using pseudoscalar observables like gamma-ray circular polarization and analyzing powers; electric dipole moment (EDM) searches in neutrons, paramagnetic atoms, and diamagnetic atoms; and precision beta-decay correlation measurements plus lepton flavor and total lepton number violation searches in muon conversion and neutrinoless double beta decay. The analysis connects experimental limits to effective low-energy couplings, nuclear matrix elements, and fundamental Lagrangian parameters.

## Key numbers

- Weak charge of cesium-133: \(Q_{weak}(^{133}\text{Cs}) = -73.16 \pm 0.29 \text{ (exp)} \pm 0.20 \text{ (theor)}\), providing a 0.3% precision test of the Standard Model
- Bound on extra neutral boson mass from atomic PNC: \(M(Z_0^\prime) \gsim 1.3\) TeV
- Nuclear anapole moment coupling from cesium: \(\kappa = 0.112 \pm 0.016\), dominated by \(\kappa_A \sim 0.090 \pm 0.016\)
- Proton-proton longitudinal analyzing power at 45 MeV: \(A_L(\vec{p}+p, 45\text{ MeV}) = (-1.57 \pm 0.23) \times 10^{-7}\)
- Proton-alpha longitudinal analyzing power at 46 MeV: \(A_L(\vec{p}+\alpha, 46\text{ MeV}) = (-3.34 \pm 0.93) \times 10^{-7}\)
- Gamma-ray asymmetry in fluorine-19: \(A_\gamma(^{19}\text{F}) = (-7.4 \pm 1.9) \times 10^{-5}\)
- Gamma-ray circular polarization in fluorine-18: \(P_\gamma(^{18}\text{F}) = (12 \pm 38) \times 10^{-5}\)
- Neutron EDM limit: \(d_n < 2.9 \times 10^{-26}\) e cm
- Mercury-199 atomic EDM limit: \(|d(^{199}\text{Hg})| \lsim 3.1 \times 10^{-29}\) e cm
- Electron EDM limit from thallium: \(d_e < 1.9 \times 10^{-27}\) e cm
- Muon-to-electron conversion limit on gold: \(\mu^- \text{Au} \leftrightarrow e^- \text{Au} < 7.0 \times 10^{-13}\) (90% c.l.)
- Muon-to-electron conversion limit on titanium: \(\mu^- \text{Ti} \leftrightarrow e^- \text{Ti} < 6.1 \times 10^{-13}\) (90% c.l.)
- Muon-to-electron-gamma branching ratio limit: \(\mu^+ \rightarrow e^+ \gamma < 1.2 \times 10^{-11}\) (90% c.l.)
- Future muon conversion goals: Fermilab goal \(4 \times 10^{-17}\), J-PARC goal \(5 \times 10^{-19}\)
- Seesaw scale from oscillation results: \(M_R \sim 3 \times 10^{15}\) GeV
- Atmospheric neutrino mass scale: \(m_\nu \gsim 0.05\) eV

## Context

This review extends the program of symmetry tests discussed in the DDH framework (Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein 1980) for hadronic PNC and the atomic PNC work of the JILA group (1997) and Derevianko (theoretical corrections). It highlights tensions between the cesium anapole moment and direct hadronic PNC measurements from fluorine-18 and fluorine-19, motivating new neutron-beam experiments at the SNS and next-generation muon conversion searches at J-PARC and Fermilab. The EDM section builds on the Henley (nuclear parity doublets) and Dobaczewski (octupole-deformed nuclei) proposals for enhanced sensitivity in radioactive isotopes like radium-225, while the lepton number section connects to the seesaw mechanism and the Gonzalez-Garcia (2008) discussion of neutrino mass.
