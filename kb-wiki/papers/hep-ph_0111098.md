# Relativistic Massive Vector Condensation

> At sufficiently high chemical potential massive relativistic spin one fields condense. This phenomenon leads to the spontaneous breaking of rotational invariance while linking it to the breaking of internal symmetries. We study the relevant features of the phase transition and the properties of the generated Goldstone excitations. The interplay between the internal symmetry of the vector fields and their Lorentz properties is studied. We predict that new phases set in when vectors condense in Quantum Chromodynamics like theories.

## Key claim

At sufficiently high chemical potential, massive relativistic spin-one fields condense, leading to spontaneous breaking of rotational invariance linked to the breaking of internal symmetries; the number of gapless Goldstone modes is insensitive to the choice of effective potential coefficients, but the momentum dependence of their dispersion relations (linear versus quadratic) depends crucially on whether the potential possesses an enhanced SO(6) symmetry (lambda' = 0) or not (lambda > lambda').

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for an SU(2) adjoint vector field with a tree-level potential truncated at fourth order in the fields, including all terms allowed by Lorentz and SU(2) symmetry. They introduce a chemical potential along the T^3 generator direction, which explicitly breaks SU(2) to U(1) x Z_2 and Lorentz invariance to the rotational SO(3) subgroup. Using a mean-field approach, they identify the vector field itself as the order parameter, compute the curvatures of the potential on the vacuum, and derive the full dispersion relations for all physical states in both the symmetric phase (mu <= m) and the broken phase (mu > m).

## Key numbers

- The symmetric phase becomes unstable when the chemical potential equals the zero-density mass: mu = m, at which point the three physical components associated with E_{phi^-} become massless.
- In the broken phase, the vacuum expectation value is <phi_X^1> = sqrt((mu^2 - m^2)/(lambda - lambda')).
- The scalar Goldstone mode has velocity v_{phi^-_S}^2 = (mu^2 - m^2)/(3 mu^2 - m^2), which vanishes at the transition point mu = m.
- The vector Goldstone mode velocity is v_{phi^-_V}^2 = (lambda'/(2 Delta^2)) * ((mu^2 - m^2)/(lambda - lambda')), where Delta^2 = 2 mu^2 + (lambda'/2)*((mu^2 - m^2)/(lambda - lambda')).
- For the special case lambda' = 0, the potential has 5 null curvatures (flat directions) instead of 3, and one vector Goldstone mode acquires quadratic dispersion relations (E propto |p|^2) instead of linear.

## Context

This work extends the prediction of vector condensation in two-color QCD at nonzero chemical potential made by Lenaghan, Sannino, and Splittorff (2001), which was supported by lattice studies by Lombardo (2001). The authors provide a more detailed analysis of the phase transition and Goldstone spectrum, showing that the interplay between the symmetries of the potential and the kinetic term determines whether Goldstone modes have linear or quadratic dispersion relations. The results enable predictions for a novel superfluid phase transition in two-color QCD with one flavor, where vector condensation can be the leading mechanism, and can be extended to three-color QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential as studied by Splittorff, Son, and Stephanov (2001).
