# The pp -> p Lambda K+ and pp -> p Sigma0 K+ Reactions in the Chiral Unitary Approach

> We study the pp -> p Lambda K+ and pp -> p Sigma0 K+ reactions near threshold by using a chiral unitary approach. We consider the single-pion and single-kaon exchange as well as the final state interactions of nucleon-hyperon, K-hyperon and K-nucleon systems. Our results on the total cross section of the pp -> p Lambda K+ reaction is consistent with the experimental data, and the experimental observed strong suppression of Sigma0 production compared to Lambda production at the same excess energy can also be explained in our model.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary model reproduces the experimental total cross section for the $pp \to p \Lambda K^+$ reaction and explains the strong suppression of $\Sigma^0$ production relative to $\Lambda$ production at the same excess energy, but only when the final state interaction of the $p \Lambda$ system is included; without it, the calculated cross section is about two and a half times smaller than data at threshold.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical model for $pp \to p \Lambda K^+$ and $pp \to p \Sigma^0 K^+$ reactions near threshold, using single-pion and single-kaon exchange mechanisms. They included final state interactions for all pairs in the final state: nucleon-hyperon, kaon-hyperon, and kaon-nucleon systems, with the meson-baryon amplitudes obtained from chiral unitary theory and the baryon-baryon amplitudes fitted to experimental data. The total production amplitude was built from elementary production amplitudes combined with loop functions and final state interaction $T$-matrices, and cross sections were computed via Monte Carlo phase space integration.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for $pp \to p \Lambda K^+$ is calculated for excess energies $\varepsilon$ from 0 MeV to 14 MeV, using a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_{\pi} = 1300$ MeV.
- The dashed line (without $p \Lambda$ final state interaction) is about two and a half times smaller than experimental data at threshold, and less than a factor of two smaller than data at $\varepsilon \sim 14$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gasparian (1999) by allowing all final-state pairs to undergo final state interactions, and by using chiral unitary amplitudes that naturally generate the $N^*(1535)$ resonance. It differs from earlier Juelich group calculations in how final state interactions are implemented, following the method of Junko-Juan. The model explains the experimentally observed strong suppression of $\Sigma^0$ production relative to $\Lambda$ production at the same excess energy, which was previously attributed to destructive interference between pion and kaon exchange in Gasparian (1999). The results enable quantitative determination of the $\Lambda N$ interaction from the energy dependence of the final state interaction.
