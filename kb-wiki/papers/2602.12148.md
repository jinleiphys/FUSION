# Resonating group method for baryon-baryon interactions with unequal oscillator frequencies and its application to the $NΔ$ system in a chiral quark model

> The resonating group method (RGM) is widely used to investigate baryon-baryon interactions at the quark level, typically under the assumption that the two baryons involved share an identical harmonic-oscillator frequency. In reality, however, when a specific interaction Hamiltonian is given, different baryons should have unequal oscillator frequencies due to distinct interaction potentials induced by their different quantum numbers. In this work, we develop a new quark-level RGM formalism for baryon-baryon systems with unequal oscillator frequencies, with the aim of describing both single baryons and baryon-baryon interactions in a consistent framework. We present the formalism for solving bound-state and scattering problems, with particular emphasis on constructing the wave functions of two-baryon systems with unequal oscillator frequencies. The proposed formalism is then applied to the $NΔ$ system within a chiral SU(3) quark model, where the quark-quark interaction includes, in addition to the one-gluon exchange (OGE) and a phenomenological confinement potential, the nonet scalar and pseudoscalar meson exchanges arising from the spontaneous breaking of chiral SU(3) symmetry. The distinctive features of the newly developed formalism are elucidated by comparing the results from the new formulation with those from traditional calculations.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new quark-level resonating group method (RGM) formalism for baryon-baryon systems where the two baryons have unequal harmonic-oscillator frequencies, rather than the identical frequency assumed in all previous quark-model RGM studies. They apply this formalism to the NΔ system within a chiral SU(3) quark model, and claim that the new approach provides a consistent description of both single-baryon properties and baryon-baryon interactions, unlike traditional calculations where the oscillator frequency of Δ is set equal to that of N.

## Method

The authors construct the spatial wave functions of two baryons as products of single-quark Gaussian wave functions with distinct oscillator frequencies ω_A and ω_B for each baryon, determined variationally by matching single-baryon Hamiltonian matrix elements to experimental masses. They introduce generator coordinates to expand the relative-motion wave function between the two clusters, and use integrations over the center-of-mass coordinate and generator coordinate angles to express the total six-quark wave function as a product of single-quark wave functions free from spurious center-of-mass motion. The bound-state and scattering problems are solved by projecting the relative-motion wave function into partial waves and solving the variational RGM equations, with the scattering wave function matched to spherical Hankel functions beyond a cutoff radius R_c where strong interactions vanish.

## Key numbers

- The size parameter b_u for each baryon is related to the oscillator frequency ω via b_u² = 1/(m_u ω), with m_u being the constituent mass of the u(d) quark; specific numerical values of b_u for octet and decuplet baryons were reported in the authors' prior work Huang (2018).
- The oscillator frequencies for octet baryons are "much larger" than those for decuplet baryons, as demonstrated in Huang (2018).
- The relative-motion coordinate between clusters A and B uses a width parameter b_AB² = 1/(M_A ω_A) + 1/(M_B ω_B), where M_A and M_B are the total masses of clusters A and B respectively.
- The center-of-mass width parameter is b_cm² = 1/(M_A ω_A + M_B ω_B).
- The cutoff radius R_c is defined as the distance beyond which all strong interactions between the two clusters can be neglected.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior consistent description of single-baryon energies, deuteron binding energy, and NN scattering phase shifts in Huang (2018), where oscillator frequencies were determined variationally for each baryon rather than treated as identical predetermined parameters. The new formalism enables study of systems composed of two different baryons with unequal oscillator frequencies, which was not possible with previous quark-level RGM approaches that assumed identical frequencies for both clusters (Oka 1980, Harvey 1981, Faessler 1982, and many others). The approach provides a foundation for future studies of baryon-baryon interactions, dibaryons, hadronic molecules, and multiquark states with more physically grounded model parameters.
