# Optical-Model Description of Time-Reversal Violation

> A time-reversal-violating spin-correlation coefficient in the total cross section for polarized neutrons incident on a tensor rank-2 polarized target is calculated by assuming a time-reversal-noninvariant, parity-conserving ``five-fold" interaction in the neutron-nucleus optical potential. Results are presented for the system $n + {^{165}{\rm Ho}}$ for neutron incident energies covering the range 1--20 MeV. From existing experimental bounds, a strength of $2 \pm 10$ keV is deduced for the real and imaginary parts of the five-fold term, which implies an upper bound of order $10^{-4}$ on the relative $T$-odd strength when compared to the central real optical potential.

# Optical-Model Description of Time-Reversal Violation in Neutron-Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim

A time-reversal-violating (T-odd) spin-correlation coefficient in neutron-nucleus total cross sections can be described by a parity-conserving "five-fold" term in the optical potential, and existing experimental bounds on this coefficient for the n+165Ho system at 2 MeV imply an upper bound of order 10^{-4} on the relative T-odd strength compared to the central real optical potential, though the deduced strength of 2 ± 10 keV for the five-fold term is consistent with zero within uncertainty.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations using the code CHUCK for the n+165Ho system over neutron incident energies from 1 to 20 MeV, adding a time-reversal-noninvariant, parity-conserving five-fold interaction term (proportional to the operator s·(I×r̂)(I·r̂)) to a standard deformed Woods-Saxon optical potential. The calculations included ground-state reorientation couplings with angular momentum transfers λ = 2, 4, and 6 assuming a rotational model, and the T-odd spin-correlation coefficient A5 was computed separately for pure real and pure imaginary five-fold terms of strength 0.1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The amplitude of oscillation in A5 is about 4 × 10^{-3} at the low-energy end of the 1–20 MeV range for a five-fold term strength of 0.1 MeV.
- From the experimental value A5 = (0.7 ± 4.1) × 10^{-4} for n+165Ho at 2 MeV, the real and imaginary strengths of the five-fold term are estimated as 2 ± 10 keV.
- The ratio of the five-fold strength to the central real optical potential (≈50 MeV) gives an upper bound αT < 2 × 10^{-4} on the relative T-odd, parity-even strength in the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Context

This work extends optical-model analyses previously applied to parity-nonconserving longitudinal analyzing powers (Koonin and Johnson 1992; Carlson and Hussein 1993) to the case of time-reversal violation, using a five-fold interaction term. The calculation directly interprets the null result of a neutron-transmission test of time-reversal invariance performed by Koster et al. (1991, 1992) using polarized 2-MeV neutrons on an aligned 165Ho target. The derived bound on αT is of the same order as the best sensitivities obtained from detailed-balance experiments (Harney et al. 1990) and from analyses of energy shifts in neutron p-wave resonances (Barabanov et al. 1993), and the approach enables future connection to underlying T-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions via folding-model techniques.
