# Event-by-Event Jet Quenching and Higher Fourier Moments of Hard Probes

> We investigate the effect of event-by-event fluctuations of the fireball created in high energy nuclear collisions on hard probe observables. We show that spatial inhomogeneities lead to changes in the nuclear suppression factor of high momentum hadrons which can be absorbed in the quenching strength q. This can increase the theoretical uncertainty on extracted values of q by up to 50%. We also investigate effects on azimuthal asymmetries v2 and dihadron correlation functions. The latter show a promising residual signal of event-by-event quenching that might allow us to estimate the size of spatial inhomogeneities in the fireball from experimental data.

## Key claim

Spatial inhomogeneities in the fireball created in high energy nuclear collisions, when accounted for event-by-event, change the nuclear suppression factor of high momentum hadrons in a way that can be absorbed by redefining the quenching strength \(\hat q\), introducing an additional theoretical uncertainty on extracted values of \(\hat q\) of up to 50%.

## Method

The authors used the GLISSANDO Glauber Monte Carlo to generate fluctuating density distributions of nucleon-nucleon collisions in the transverse plane for Au+Au collisions. They then simulated jet quenching event-by-event using two energy loss models: a simple LPM-inspired model (sLPM) and the non-deterministic ASW/BDMPS model, comparing results for the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\), azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2\), and dihadron correlation function \(I_{AA}\) against PHENIX data.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical uncertainty on extracted values of \(\hat q\) due to spatial inhomogeneities is at least 50%.
- In the ASW model, the quenching parameter \(c_{\rm ASW}\) had to be adjusted from 1.6 GeV to 2.8 GeV when switching from averaged events to event-by-event calculations to refit the \(R_{AA}\) data.
- Deviations in \(v_2\) between averaged and event-by-event calculations after refitting \(R_{AA}\) are typically less than 20%.
- Fourier coefficients \(v_n\) for \(n=2, 3, 4\) scale linearly with spatial eccentricities \(\epsilon_n\) in both energy loss models; coefficients for \(n=3, 5, 6\) are generally below 1%.

## Context

This work extends studies of jet quenching in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where previous calculations (e.g., Baier 1996, Wiedemann 2000, Gyulassy 2000, Arnold 2002) assumed smooth, averaged fireball geometries. The paper shows that event-by-event fluctuations, as modeled by the GLISSANDO Glauber Monte Carlo (Broniowski 2007), introduce significant uncertainty in extracted \(\hat q\) values, and that dihadron correlations (\(I_{AA}\)) may provide a way to experimentally constrain the correlation function \(R\) and thus the spatial granularity of the fireball. This enables future tomographic studies of the quark gluon plasma structure.
