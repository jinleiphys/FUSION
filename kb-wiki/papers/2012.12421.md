# Evolution of Primordial Neutrino Helicities in Astrophysical Magnetic Fields and Implications for their Detection

> Since decoupling in the early universe in helicity states, primordial neutrinos propagating in astrophysical magnetic fields precess and undergo helicity changes. In view of the XENON1T experiment possibly finding a large magnetic moment of solar neutrinos, we estimate the helicity flipping for relic neutrinos in both cosmic and galactic magnetic fields. The flipping probability is sensitive both to the neutrino magnetic moment and the structure of the magnetic fields, thus potentially a probe of the fields. As we find, even a magnetic moment well below that suggested by XENON1T could significantly affect relic neutrino helicities and their detection rate via inverse tritium beta decay.

# Evolution of Primordial Neutrino Helicities in Astrophysical Magnetic Fields and Implications for their Detection

## Key claim
Even a neutrino magnetic moment several orders of magnitude smaller than the ~1.4–2.9 × 10⁻¹¹ μ_B suggested by the XENON1T excess could produce significant helicity changes in Dirac relic neutrinos propagating through cosmic and galactic magnetic fields, thereby affecting their detection rate via inverse tritium beta decay. The effect depends critically on whether neutrinos are Dirac or Majorana particles, as Majorana neutrinos have no diagonal magnetic moments and thus no helicity flipping in slowly varying magnetic fields.

## Method
The authors calculate the spin precession of relic neutrinos in magnetic fields using the Bargmann–Michel–Telegdi equation in the neutrino rest frame, relating lab-frame magnetic fields to rest-frame fields via Lorentz transformations. They model the cumulative spin rotation as a random walk through galactic magnetic fields with coherence length ~1 kpc, and compute the integrated helicity evolution through cosmic magnetic fields from neutrino decoupling to the present using a correlation function formalism for the cosmic magnetic field power spectrum, assuming flux conservation (Ba² constant) and a power-law spectral form.

## Key numbers
- Present relic neutrino temperature: T_ν0 = 1.945 ± 0.001 K = (1.676 ± 0.001) × 10⁻⁴ eV
- Total relic neutrino number density: n = 56.25 cm⁻³
- Standard Model Dirac neutrino magnetic moment: μ_ν^SM ≃ 3 × 10⁻²¹ m_{-2} μ_B (where m_{-2} is neutrino mass in units of 10⁻² eV)
- XENON1T excess magnetic moment scale: μ_{1T} ~ 1.4–2.9 × 10⁻¹¹ μ_B
- GEMMA upper limit: μ_ν < 2.9 × 10⁻¹¹ μ_B
- Borexino upper bound: μ_{ν_e} < 2.8 × 10⁻¹¹ μ_B
- Milky Way spin rotation estimate: ⟨θ²⟩_{MW} ~ 4 × 10²⁹ m_{-2}² (Λ_g/1 kpc) (B_g/10 μG)² (μ_ν/μ_B)²
- Cosmic spin rotation estimate: ⟨θ²⟩_c ≃ 2 × 10²⁷ (Λ_0/1 Mpc) (B_0/10⁻¹² G)² (μ_ν/μ_B)²
- A magnetic moment ~1.5 × 10⁻¹⁵ μ_B (10⁻⁴ of μ_{1T}) yields helicity flip probability of order unity for m_{-2} (B_g/10 μG) (Λ_g/1 kpc)^{1/2} ~ 1
- For Dirac neutrinos with μ_ν = 5 × 10⁻¹⁴ μ_B (two orders below XENON1T scale), the effective detection coefficient A_eff,D shows measurable deviation from the no-flip case

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of neutrino helicity evolution in gravitational fields (Duda 2001, Silenko 2006, Dvornikov 2006) and magnetic fields (Fujikawa 1973, Semikoz 1993, 1997) by providing quantitative estimates for relic neutrinos in both galactic and cosmic magnetic fields. The paper is motivated by the XENON1T excess (2020) and connects to the PTOLEMY experiment concept for relic neutrino detection via inverse tritium beta decay (Weinberg 1962, Long 2018). The results enable distinguishing Dirac from Majorana neutrinos through detection rate measurements, and suggest that magnetic moments below current experimental bounds could still produce observable helicity effects, while also offering a potential probe of cosmic and galactic magnetic field structure.
