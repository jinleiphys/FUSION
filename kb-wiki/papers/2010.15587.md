# Important Influence of Entrance Channel Reorientation Coupling on Proton Stripping

> While it is well established that the ground state reorientation coupling can have a significant influence on the elastic scattering of deformed nuclei, the effect of such couplings on transfer channels has been much less well investigated. In this letter we demonstrate that the 208Pb(7Li,6He)209Bi proton stripping reaction at an incident energy of 52 MeV can be well described by the inclusion of the 7Li ground state reorientation coupling within the coupled channels Born approximation formalism. Full finite-range distorted wave Born approximation calculations were previously found to be unable to describe these data. Addition of coupling to the 0.478-MeV 1/2- excited state of 7Li, together with the associated two-step transfer path, has little or no influence on the shape of the angular distributions (except that for stripping leading to the 1.61-MeV 13/2+ level of 209Bi which is significantly improved) but does affect appreciably the values of the 209Bi -> 208Pb + p spectroscopic factors. Implications for experiments with weakly-bound light radioactive beams are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including only the ground state reorientation coupling of the deformed \(^7\)Li projectile within the coupled channels Born approximation (CCBA) can describe the \(^{208}\)Pb(\(^7\)Li,\(^6\)He)\(^{209}\)Bi proton stripping reaction at 52 MeV, whereas full finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FR-DWBA) calculations previously failed. The abstract glosses over the condition that the entrance channel elastic scattering data must be refitted when the coupling is included.

## Method

The authors performed CCBA calculations using the code FRESCO, including the \(^7\)Li ground state reorientation coupling via standard rotational model form factors with nuclear multipoles calculated by numerically deforming the radii of the diagonal potential. Two sets of CCBA calculations were carried out: one with ground state reorientation coupling only, and a second adding excitation of the 0.478-MeV \(1/2^-\) state of \(^7\)Li together with the associated two-step transfer path, with optical potential parameters readjusted in each case to recover the fit to the \(^7\)Li + \(^{208}\)Pb elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 52 MeV for the \(^{208}\)Pb(\(^7\)Li,\(^6\)He)\(^{209}\)Bi reaction.
- Coulomb coupling strength: \(B(E2; 3/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^-) = 8.3 \pm 0.5\) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) for the \(^7\)Li transition.
- Nuclear deformation length: \(\delta_2 = 2.0\) fm for \(^7\)Li.
- Spectroscopic factors for the \(\langle^{209}\mathrm{Bi} | ^{208}\mathrm{Pb} + p\rangle\) overlaps extracted from FR-DWBA (set A) range from 0.64 (3.64-MeV \(1/2^-\) level) to 1.40 (0.0-MeV \(9/2^-\) level); from CCBA with ground state reorientation only (set B) range from 0.73 to 1.51; from CCBA with reorientation plus excitation (set C) range from 0.90 to 1.68.
- Quadrupole moment of \(^7\)Li: \(Q = -40.6 \pm 0.8\) mb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Keeley 2020) showing that CCBA calculations including two-step transfer via the 4.44-MeV \(2^+_1\) excited state of \(^{12}\)C could describe the \(^{208}\)Pb(\(^{12}\)C,\(^{11}\)B)\(^{209}\)Bi reaction that FR-DWBA failed to explain. It contradicts the long-standing assumption that ground state reorientation coupling only affects elastic scattering (Hnizdo 1981) and inelastic scattering (Vidabaek 1976), demonstrating for the first time a significant effect on transfer channels from this coupling alone. The results enable improved analysis of transfer reactions with weakly-bound light radioactive beams such as \(^8\)Li, \(^9\)Li, \(^{11}\)Li, and \(^8\)B, which have similar or larger quadrupole moments, and underline the need to measure elastic scattering alongside transfer data.
