# Electromagnetic radii of the nucleon in soft-wall holographic QCD

> We revisit the electromagnetic form factors of the proton and neutron in the original-minimal soft-wall holographic QCD, which has only two parameters, i.e., the mass scale $κ$ and the twist parameter of the nucleon $τ$. We first fix $τ=3$ by the hard scattering rule, and extract $κ=0.402~\text{GeV}$ from the world data (including the Mainz A1 data) of the Sachs magnetic form factor of the proton $G_M^p$. We then predict among others, the charge radius of the proton to be $\bf{r_{p}=0.831\,\pm\,0.008\,\,\text{fm}}$, in perfect agreement with the recent charge radius of the proton measured by the PRad collaboration at Jefferson Lab, and in agreement with the muonic hydrogen experiments. Our prediction for the proton elastic form factor ratio $μ_pG_E^p/G_M^p$ is also in very good agreement with the recent high precision Jefferson Lab recoil polarization experiment E08-007 for $Q^2=0.3\,-\,0.6~\text{GeV}^2$, and with the recent high precision Mainz A1 experiment for $Q^2< 0.13~\text{GeV}^2$.

## Key claim
The authors show that the original-minimal soft-wall holographic QCD, with only two parameters (mass scale κ and nucleon twist τ), can predict a proton charge radius of 0.831 ± 0.008 fm that matches the recent PRad measurement, provided κ is fixed to 0.402 GeV by fitting the world data of the Sachs magnetic form factor of the proton rather than by fitting meson masses.

## Method
The authors fixed the nucleon twist parameter τ = 3 using the hard scattering rule for large momentum transfer, then extracted κ = 0.402 GeV by fitting the holographic Sachs magnetic form factor of the proton G_M^p to world experimental data including the Mainz A1 data. They also considered an alternative parameter choice where κ = m_ρ/2 = 0.388 GeV and τ = m_N^2/m_ρ^2 + 1 = 2.465, using the experimental masses of the rho meson and nucleon, to estimate theoretical uncertainties. The electromagnetic form factors were computed using the Euler beta function expressions derived from the soft-wall holographic QCD framework of Karch (2006) and Abidin and Carlson (2009).

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius: r_p = 0.831 ± 0.008 fm (predicted with τ=3, κ=0.402 GeV)
- Alternative proton charge radius: r_p = 0.839 fm (with τ=2.465, κ=0.388 GeV)
- Mass scale κ = 0.402 GeV (extracted from world G_M^p data including Mainz A1)
- Alternative κ = m_ρ/2 = 0.388 GeV (from rho meson mass m_ρ = 0.775 GeV)
- Proton magnetic radius: sqrt(⟨r_M^2⟩_p) = 0.803 ± 0.012 fm
- Neutron magnetic radius: sqrt(⟨r_M^2⟩_n) = 0.817 ± 0.007 fm
- Neutron charge radius squared: ⟨(r_C^n)^2⟩ = -0.094 ± 0.048 fm^2
- Proton elastic form factor ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p agrees with JLab E08-007 data for Q^2 = 0.3–0.6 GeV^2 and with Mainz A1 data for Q^2 < 0.13 GeV^2

## Context
This work extends the soft-wall holographic QCD approach of Karch (2006) and the earlier form factor calculations of Abidin and Carlson (2009), who used κ = 0.350 GeV and obtained a larger proton charge radius of 0.960 fm. By refitting κ to the magnetic form factor data rather than to meson masses, the authors resolve the proton radius puzzle within the same minimal framework, achieving agreement with the PRad collaboration measurement (Xiong 2019), muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Antognini 2013), and the Jefferson Lab recoil polarization experiment E08-007 (Zhan 2011). The results enable further holographic studies of nucleon structure and demonstrate that the soft-wall model can simultaneously describe electromagnetic form factors across a wide range of momentum transfers.
