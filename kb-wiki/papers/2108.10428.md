# Coherent energy loss effects in dihadron azimuthal angular correlations in Deep Inelastic Scattering at small $x$

> We perform an exploratory study of the role of coherent, medium-induced energy loss in azimuthal angular correlations in dihadron production in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at small $x$ where the target proton/nucleus is modeled as a Color Glass Condensate. In this approach coherent radiative energy loss is part of the higher order corrections to the leading order dihadron production cross section. We include the effects of both gluon saturation and coherent radiative energy loss and show that radiative cold-matter energy loss has a significant effect on the so-called coincidence probability for the back to back production of dihadrons in DIS. We also define a double ratio of coincidence probabilities for a nucleus and proton targets and show that it is very robust against higher order radiative corrections.

## Key claim

Coherent medium-induced energy loss, defined as the difference in soft gluon radiation spectra between a nucleus and a proton target, has a significant effect on the away-side peak of dihadron azimuthal angular correlations in Deep Inelastic Scattering at small x, but a double ratio of coincidence probabilities for nucleus and proton targets is very robust against these higher-order radiative corrections.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for quark-antiquark-gluon production in DIS at small x using the Color Glass Condensate formalism, taking the soft gluon limit and integrating over the final-state gluon transverse momentum. They evaluate the medium-induced radiation spectrum as the difference between the normalized soft gluon radiation spectra for a nucleus and a proton target, using the Gaussian approximation for Wilson line correlation functions (dipoles and quadrupoles) with the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff model. The back-to-back kinematic limit is taken by defining total and relative momenta and expanding to lowest nonzero order in the small dipole size variables.

## Key numbers

- The induced radiation spectrum is largest at the exact back-to-back limit (Δφ = π) and drops off sharply away from this limit, with the effect independent of photon virtuality Q at the exact back-to-back limit when quark and antiquark momenta are set equal to Q.
- For a proton target, the saturation scale is taken as Q²_s,p = 1 GeV²; for a nucleus, Q²_s,A = A^(1/3) Q²_s,p, with results shown for A=2 and A=200.
- The coincidence probability CP(Δφ) is calculated with momentum windows p_min = 2 GeV, p_max = 10 GeV, q_min = 1 GeV, q_max = p, with fixed momentum fractions z₁ = 0.55 and z₂ = 0.45.
- The ratio of CP(Δφ) at next-to-leading order versus leading order shows an enhancement factor of approximately 3 with weak angular dependence.
- The double ratio of CP(Δφ) for a large nucleus over a proton shows a clear increase in magnitude with increasing photon virtuality Q.

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism studies of dihadron azimuthal angular correlations (Marquet 2007, Albacete 2010) by including coherent cold-matter energy loss as part of NLO corrections, following the approach of Munier, Peigné and Petreska (2016) for defining medium-induced radiation spectra. It contrasts with the work of Kang (2011) who showed that combining phenomenological cold-matter energy loss models with nuclear shadowing can also describe experimental data from RHIC (Braidot 2010, Adare 2011). The results enable future full NLO calculations of dihadron production in DIS at small x and motivate going beyond the strict back-to-back limit using improved TMD distributions as advocated by Altinoluk (2019) and Boussarie (2021).
