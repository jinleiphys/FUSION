# The Los Alamos evaluation of $^{239}$Pu neutron-induced reactions in the fast energy range

> A major revision of the evaluation of $^{239}$Pu neutron-induced reaction cross sections is reported in the fast energy range. The evaluation starts at 2.5 keV incident neutron energy and has been extended up to 30 MeV. Several other notable changes are included in this evaluation since the release of ENDF/B-VIII.0 including the adoption of the Standards fission cross section, inclusion of new radiative capture data of Mosby et al., inclusion of the (n,2n) data of Meot et al., in addition to advances in the treatment of reaction modeling. In contrast to previous evaluation efforts, this evaluation is reproducible with detailed information stored chronologically utilizing a Git repository. The final evaluation results have been compiled into an ENDF-formatted file, which has been processed successfully through NJOY, checked for internal consistency, benchmarked versus older evaluations and validated against a suite of critical assemblies and pulsed-spheres.

## Key claim

This evaluation of $^{239}$Pu neutron-induced reaction cross sections in the fast energy range (2.5 keV to 30 MeV) claims to be a major revision over ENDF/B-VIII.0, but the actual improvement is conditional on the adoption of the Standards fission cross section, new radiative capture data from Mosby et al., and (n,2n) data from Meot et al., combined with advances in reaction modeling such as the M1 scissors mode and Engelbrecht-Weidenmueller transformation.

## Method

The evaluation combines differential experimental datasets from EXFOR with theoretical modeling using the Los Alamos statistical Hauser-Feshbach code CoH, employing a coupled-channels scheme for the deformed $^{239}$Pu nucleus. A Bayesian approach with hyperparameter optimization is used to fit model cross sections to experimental data, following an incremental procedure: first fitting total cross section data to fix optical model parameters, then setting fission parameters to match the Standards fission cross section, and subsequently fitting remaining channels. The entire evaluation is recorded incrementally using a Git repository and tied together using the NEXUS computational nuclear data platform.

## Key numbers

- Evaluation covers incident neutron energies from 2.5 keV up to 30 MeV
- Total cross section calculated with coupling between ground state and first six excited states (excitation energies from 7.861 keV to 318.5 keV)
- Static quadrupole deformation set to $\beta_2 = 0.202$, slightly lower than the FRDM-calculated value of 0.236
- Fission barrier parameters for $^{240}$Pu: inner barrier height 5.76 MeV with curvature 0.44, outer barrier height 5.03 MeV with curvature 0.77
- Fission barrier parameters for $^{239}$Pu: inner barrier height 5.75 MeV with curvature 0.52, outer barrier height 5.45 MeV with curvature 0.68
- Radiative capture data from Mosby et al. extends up to 1.33 MeV incident energy
- M1 scissors mode parameters: $E_{\rm M1} = 80 |\beta_2| A^{-1/3}$ MeV, with M1 strength function parameters $E_{\rm M1} = 2.71$ MeV, width 1.5, sigma 1.250
- (n,2n) data from Meot et al. provides three new data points in the ~7-9 MeV region, with the highest point normalized to ENDF/B-VIII.0 at 9.52 MeV carrying $\pm 15\%$ uncertainty
- Collective enhancement parameter for pre-equilibrium level density set to 10 based on pulsed spheres integral data

## Context

This work extends the ENDF/B-VIII.0 evaluation (Brown et al., 2018) by incorporating new experimental data including fissionTPC data (Snyder et al., 2019), radiative capture data from Mosby et al. (2018), and (n,2n) data from Meot et al. (2021), along with theoretical advances such as the M1 scissors mode enhancement previously studied by Ullmann et al. (2014) for uranium isotopes. The evaluation enables improved nuclear data for reactor design, fuel-cycle analysis, and stockpile stewardship, and provides a reproducible framework that can be extended to other plutonium isotopes where experimental data are scarce.
