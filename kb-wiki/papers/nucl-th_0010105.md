# Few-body resonances in light nuclei

> We have localized several few-body resonances in light nuclei, using methods which can properly handle two- or three-body resonant states. Among other results, we predict the existence of a three-neutron resonance, small spin-orbit splittings between the low-lying states in He-5 and Li-5, the nonexistence of the soft dipole resonance in He-6, new 1+ states in Li-8 and B-8, and the presence of a nonlinear amplification phenomenon in the 0+_2 state of C-12.

## Key claim

The paper predicts several few-body resonances in light nuclei using methods that properly handle two- or three-body resonant states, but the abstract's claim of a three-neutron resonance is hedged by the observation that recent experiments did not see evidence of this structure and that calculations using fully realistic forces are needed.

## Method

The author uses a microscopic cluster model (resonating group method, RGM) where nuclear wave functions contain two- or three-cluster configurations with large weight. For two-body scattering states, the relative motion functions are expanded in Gaussian functions matched with correct asymptotics, and the S-matrix poles are found via direct analytic continuation to complex energies. For three-body systems, the complex scaling method is applied to the many-body Hamiltonian, transforming each dynamical coordinate to obtain resonant-state poles as isolated complex eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- n+n virtual state pole energy: E_nn = -0.121 MeV (phenomenological value: -0.123 MeV)
- p+p virtual state pole energy: E_pp = (-0.143 ± i0.466) MeV (phenomenological: (-0.140 ± i0.467) MeV)
- Three-neutron (3n) resonance: J^π = 3/2^+, E_r = 14 MeV, Γ = 13 MeV (using Minnesota interaction)
- Three-proton (3p) resonance: J^π = 3/2^+, E_r = 15 MeV, Γ = 14 MeV (using Minnesota interaction)
- 1/2^+ virtual state in ^3H: E_V = -1.62 MeV (using EH force)
- 1/2^+ virtual state in ^3He: E_V = (-0.43 ± i0.56) MeV (using EH force)
- 0^+_2 state in ^4He: E_r = 93 keV above ^3H+p threshold, Γ = 390 keV (RGM); extended R-matrix gives E_r = 114 keV, Γ = 392 keV
- New 1^+ state in ^8B: E_r = 1.28 MeV, Γ = 0.56 MeV (relative to ^7Be+p)
- New 1^+ state in ^8Li: lies right at the ^7Li+n threshold
- Nonlinear amplification in ^12C 0^+_2 state: a 0.1% change in N-N interaction strength changes the resonance energy by almost 10%

## Context

This work extends prior cluster-model and R-matrix analyses of light-nuclei resonances, correcting earlier results from shell-model approaches (Navratil) that predicted spurious low-lying positive parity states in ^5He and ^5Li. The paper contradicts claims by Cobis (2000) of many narrow α+n+n resonances in ^6He, arguing those results are unphysical. The finding that no soft dipole (1^-) resonance exists in ^6He confirms calculations by Kato (1998) using different methods. The nonlinear amplification phenomenon in ^12C has implications for carbon synthesis in astrophysics, building on work by Oberhummer and collaborators showing that a 0.5% change in N-N force strength would eliminate carbon and oxygen production.
