# Nonlocality effects on Color Spin Locking condensates

> We consider the color spin locking (CSL) phase of two-flavor quark matter at zero temperature for nonlocal instantaneous, separable interactions. We employ a Lorentzian-type form factor allowing a parametric interpolation between the sharp (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model) and very smooth (e.g. Gaussian) cut-off models for systematic studies of the influence on the CSL condensate the deviation from the NJL model entails. This smoothing of the NJL model form factor shows advantageous features for the phenomenology of compact stars: (i) a lowering of the critical chemical potential for the onset of the chiral phase transition as a prerequisite for stability of hybrid stars with extended quark matter cores and (ii) a reduction of the smallest pairing gap to the order of 100 keV, being in the range of values interesting for phenomenological studies of hybrid star cooling evolution.

# Nonlocality effects on Color Spin Locking condensates

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that introducing nonlocality (smoothing the sharp cutoff of the NJL model) systematically lowers both the critical chemical potential for the onset of the CSL phase and the minimal excitation energy (effective pairing gap), with the smallest gap reduced to the order of 100 keV, a range relevant for hybrid star cooling phenomenology, but only for form factors that do not produce unrealistically large chiral condensates in vacuum.

## Method

The authors employ a nonlocal instantaneous separable interaction model with a Lorentzian-type form factor that allows parametric interpolation between the sharp cutoff NJL model and smooth form factors such as Gaussian. They solve the gap equations for the chiral condensate and the CSL pairing gap at zero temperature as functions of chemical potential, using parameter sets fitted to vacuum pion properties and constituent quark masses of 330 MeV and 400 MeV. The diquark coupling constant is fixed to the Fierz-transformed one-gluon exchange value (H_v/G = 8/3) and also tested at twice that value.

## Key numbers

- For NJL-like form factors (Lorentzian with alpha >= 3), the minimal excitation energies E_{1,min} lie in the range of 50–500 keV; for smooth form factors (Lorentzian alpha=2 and Gaussian), they lie between 1 and 100 keV.
- The shift in critical chemical potential for the onset of the chiral phase transition relative to the NJL case is less than 20 MeV for the NJL-like group, but larger than 30 MeV for the smooth form factor group.
- For M=330 MeV parameterization, the slope parameters for the linear scaling with 1/alpha are xi = 1.8 (for E_{1,min}) and xi' = 0.13 (for mu_c); for M=400 MeV, xi = 1.6 and xi' = 0.17.
- The CSL gaps at onset for the NJL-like models are reduced by up to a factor of three relative to the NJL case (e.g., Lorentzian with alpha=3).
- The critical chemical potential for the L10 model is mu_c = 338 MeV (M=330 MeV) and mu_c = 375 MeV (M=400 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the CSL phase by Aguilera et al. (2005) and of nonlocal chiral quark models by Schmidt et al. (1994), Gocke et al. (2001), and Grigorian et al. (2006). It systematically investigates how smoothing the sharp cutoff of the NJL model, motivated by lattice QCD results from Parappilly et al. (2005) showing smooth momentum dependence of quark propagators, affects the CSL pairing gaps. The results enable future detailed cooling simulations of hybrid stars with CSL quark matter cores, since the smallest gap (corresponding to the S_z=0 mode) is inert to strong magnetic fields and the pairing pattern is a flavor singlet insensitive to flavor asymmetry under beta-equilibrium.
