# Spin-dependent part of $\bar{p}d$ interaction cross section and Nijmegen potential

> Low energy $\bar{p}d$ interaction is considered taking into account the polarization of both particles. The corresponding cross sections are obtained using the Nijmegen nucleon-antinucleon optical potential with shadowing effects taken into account. Double-scattering effects are calculated within the Glauber approach and found to be about 10-20%. The cross sections are applied to the analysis of the polarization buildup which is due to the interaction of stored antiprotons with a polarized target. It is shown that, at realistic parameters of a storage ring and a target, the filtering mechanism may provide a noticeable polarization in a time comparable with the beam lifetime. The energy dependence of the polarization rate for deuterium target is similar to that for hydrogen one. However, the time of polarization for deuterium is much smaller than that for hydrogen.

## Key claim

Using the Nijmegen nucleon-antinucleon optical potential and Glauber theory, the spin-dependent parts of the antiproton-deuteron cross section are calculated, and the results indicate that a polarized deuterium target could be used instead of hydrogen with similar or higher efficiency for polarizing stored antiprotons, though the predictions exhibit fairly strong model dependence.

## Method

The antiproton-nucleon scattering amplitudes were calculated using the Nijmegen nucleon-antinucleon optical potential. The antiproton-deuteron cross sections were then obtained within the Glauber theory, including double-scattering effects (shadowing) and accounting for the D-wave contribution to the deuteron wave function for elastic differential cross sections. The spin-dependent cross sections were expressed in terms of matrix elements of the elastic scattering amplitude between deuteron states with definite spin projections, and the resulting beam polarization degree was evaluated at a time equal to twice the beam lifetime.

## Key numbers

* Double-scattering (shadowing) effects in antiproton-deuteron scattering are about 10-20% of the total cross section.
* Shadowing effects decrease the total unpolarized antiproton-deuteron cross section by about 15% across the whole energy region studied.
* Shadowing effects decrease the absolute value of the spin-dependent cross sections sigma1 and sigma2 by about 20-25%.
* The Glauber theory is applicable for antiproton-deuteron scattering down to energies of about 50 MeV.
* The polarization degree predicted by the Nijmegen model is about two to three times larger than that predicted by the Julich models for antiproton-deuteron scattering.
* The time of polarization for deuterium is much smaller than that for hydrogen at the same target areal density of 10^14 cm^-2 and beam revolving frequency of 10^6 s^-1.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of spin-dependent antiproton-proton cross sections using the Nijmegen potential (DmitMilSal10) and the Paris potential (DmitMilStr08) to the antiproton-deuteron system. It also provides a comparison with predictions from the Julich A and Julich D models reported by Uzikov and Haidenbauer (2011). The results enable evaluation of the spin-filtering method for polarizing stored antiprotons using a polarized deuterium target, which is relevant to the PAX Collaboration program.
