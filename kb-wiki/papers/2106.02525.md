# Impact of Multiplicity Fluctuations on Entropy Scaling Across System Size

> The initial state is one of the greatest uncertainties in heavy-ion collisions. A model-agnostic approach is taken in the phenomenological Trento framework which constrains parameters using Bayesian analysis. However, the color-glass condensate (CGC) effective theory predicts initial energy densities that lie outside the recent Bayesian analyses due, in part, to the assumption in Trento of event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations following a $Γ$ distribution. We compare the Trento-preferred $\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}$ scaling to CGC-like $T_{A}T_{B}$ scaling coupled with log-normal fluctuations in $AuAu$ and $dAu$ collisions and find there is a significant impact on the multiplicity distributions and on the eccentricities, which may affect the extraction of viscosity in small systems.

## Key claim

The Trento framework's assumption of event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations following a Gamma distribution may bias Bayesian analyses toward the \(\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}\) entropy scaling, excluding viable CGC-like \(T_{A}T_{B}\) scaling with log-normal fluctuations that can also match experimental multiplicity distributions and produce different eccentricities that could affect viscosity extraction in small systems.

## Method

The authors modified the Trento framework to implement linear \(T_{A}T_{B}\) entropy scaling (converted to entropy via a conformal equation of state as \(s \propto (T_{A}T_{B})^{3/4}\)) and log-normal multiplicity fluctuations, then compared these to the standard \(\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}\) scaling with Gamma fluctuations. They tuned the fluctuation parameter \(k\) for each combination to best match STAR multiplicity data for AuAu and dAu collisions at 200 GeV, then computed two-particle eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\{2\}\) and four-to-two-particle eccentricity ratios \(\varepsilon_n\{4\}/\varepsilon_n\{2\}\) for \(n=2,3\) across centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- For dAu multiplicity distributions, all model combinations are nearly identical below \(N_{ch}/\langle N_{ch}\rangle < 4\), with differences only appearing in ultracentral events.
- For AuAu multiplicity distributions, both scaling models predict wider fluctuations than STAR data at high multiplicity; \(\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}\) is somewhat closer to data than \(T_{A}T_{B}\).
- In AuAu \(\varepsilon_{2}\{2\}\), the two scaling models agree below 60% centrality; in \(\varepsilon_{3}\{2\}\), linear scaling has larger magnitude than \(\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}\) above 30% centrality.
- In dAu, \(T_{A}T_{B}\) scaling produces larger \(\varepsilon_{2}\{2\}\) and \(\varepsilon_{3}\{2\}\) magnitudes than \(\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}\) across all centralities.
- The ratio \(\varepsilon_{2}\{4\}/\varepsilon_{2}\{2\}\) in dAu shows a sign change at different points for \(\sqrt{T_{A}T_{B}}\) versus \(T_{A}T_{B}\) scaling, potentially distinguishing between models.

## Context

This work extends the Trento framework (Moreland, 2018) and its Bayesian analyses (Everett, 2020; Nijs, 2020) that preferred \(p=0\) scaling with Gamma fluctuations, by testing CGC-motivated linear scaling previously studied by Nagle (2018). The results show that multiplicity fluctuations and entropy scaling choices significantly affect eccentricities, especially in small systems like dAu, implying that Trento's Bayesian extraction of QGP viscosities may contain systematic uncertainties from the restricted functional space. The findings enable future work to incorporate broader initial-state models into Bayesian analyses and to use \(\varepsilon_{3}\{4\}/\varepsilon_{3}\{2\}\) from eccentricities alone in ultracentral collisions (Carzon, 2020) as a cheaper constraint.
