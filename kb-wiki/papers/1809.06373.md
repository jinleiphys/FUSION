# Proton charge radius extraction from electron scattering data using dispersively improved chiral effective field theory

> We extract the proton charge radius from the elastic form factor (FF) data using a novel theoretical framework combining chiral effective field theory and dispersion analysis. Complex analyticity in the momentum transfer correlates the behavior of the spacelike FF at finite $Q^2$ with the derivative at $Q^2 = 0$. The FF calculated in the predictive theory contains the radius as a free parameter. We determine its value by comparing the predictions with a descriptive global fit of the spacelike FF data, taking into account the theoretical and experimental uncertainties. Our method allows us to use the finite-$Q^2$ FF data for constraining the radius (up to $Q^2\sim$ 0.5 GeV$^2$ and larger) and avoids the difficulties arising in methods relying on the $Q^2 \rightarrow 0$ extrapolation. We obtain a radius of 0.844(7) fm, consistent with the high-precision muonic hydrogen results.

# Proton charge radius extraction from electron scattering data using dispersively improved chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
The authors extract a proton charge radius of 0.844(7) fm from electron scattering data using dispersively improved chiral effective field theory (DIχEFT), which reconciles the electron scattering data with the muonic hydrogen value. The method relies on comparing theoretical predictions with a descriptive global fit of the spacelike form factor data up to Q² ∼ 0.5 GeV², rather than extrapolating to Q² → 0.

## Method
The authors combine chiral effective field theory with dispersion analysis to compute the proton electric form factor as a function of Q², with the proton charge radius as a free parameter. They generate a family of theoretical predictions for different radius values and compare these to a descriptive global fit of elastic electron-proton scattering data using a bounded polynomial z-expansion, computing a reduced χ² figure of merit over the Q² range 0.01–0.5 GeV². The theoretical uncertainties from high-mass states in the spectral function are estimated by varying the effective pole position, while experimental uncertainties come from the global fit covariance matrix.

## Key numbers
- Extracted proton charge radius: r_E^p = 0.844(7) fm
- Muonic hydrogen value for comparison: r_E^p = 0.84087(39) fm (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013)
- CODATA value for comparison: 0.875 fm (Mohr 2015)
- Upper limit of Q² range for extraction: Q²_max ∼ 0.5 GeV²
- Lower limit of Q² range: Q²_min ∼ 0.01 GeV²
- Number of bins used in χ² calculation: N = 50
- For a restricted fit with Q²_max = 0.1 GeV², the extracted radius would be r_E^p = 0.849(10) fm

## Context
This work addresses the "proton radius puzzle", the discrepancy between the smaller radius from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) and the larger CODATA value from electronic hydrogen and electron scattering (Mohr 2015). The method extends prior dispersive fits by Belushkin (2006) and Lorenz (2012), which also obtained radii ∼0.84 fm, but uses DIχEFT to compute the two-pion spectral functions rather than analytic continuation of empirical πN amplitudes. The approach improves upon low-Q² fits using truncated power series (Horbatsch 2016) by incorporating data at higher Q² where theoretical uncertainties remain under control, and it enables future validation with very low-Q² data from the Jefferson Lab PRad experiment (Gasparian 2014).
