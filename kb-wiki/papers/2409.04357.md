# Seven years of the proxy-SU(3) shell model symmetry

> The proxy-SU(3) symmetry was first presented in HINPw4 in Ioannina in May2017, justified within the Nilsson model and applied to parameter-free predictions of the collective variables beta and gamma in medium-mass and heavy nuclei. Major steps forward, including the connection of the proxy-SU(3) symmetry to the shell model, the justification of the dominance of highest weight states in terms of the short range nature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, as well as the first proposal of appearance of islands of shape coexistence on the nuclear chart, have been presented in HINPw6 in Athens in May 2021. The recently hot topic of the prevalence of triaxial shapes in heavy nuclei will also be briefly outlined in the proxy-SU(3) framework.

# Seven years of the proxy-SU(3) shell model symmetry

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) symmetry provides parameter-free predictions of the collective variables β and γ in medium-mass and heavy nuclei, with the key condition that the highest weight irreducible representation (hw irrep), rather than the irrep with the highest Casimir eigenvalue, determines the ground state band, a distinction that matters primarily in the upper half of each nuclear shell.

## Method

The authors review the proxy-SU(3) framework, which restores SU(3) symmetry in nuclear shells beyond the sd shell by replacing intruder orbitals (e.g., 1h11/2) with their 0[110] proxy counterparts from the orbital below (e.g., 1g9/2), justified within the Nilsson deformed shell model and the spherical shell model via a unitary transformation. They then identify the highest weight SU(3) irreps for each shell (U(6), U(10), U(15)) and map the Elliott labels (λ, μ) to the Bohr-Mottelson collective variables β and γ using established algebraic relations. The dual shell mechanism, comparing shells based on 3D harmonic oscillator magic numbers versus spin-orbit magic numbers, is used to predict regions of shape coexistence and triaxiality on the nuclear chart.

## Key numbers

- For U(6) (sd shell), the hw irrep and highest C₂ irrep differ at particle number M=7: hw gives (4,2) while highest C₂ gives (1,5); for U(10) (pf shell), differences appear at M=11 through M=15; for U(15) (sdg shell), differences appear at M=16 through M=25.
- The variable γ is predicted by γ = arctan(√3(μ+1)/(2λ+μ+3)), with λ > μ corresponding to prolate shapes (γ < 30°), λ < μ to oblate shapes (γ > 30°), and λ = μ to maximal triaxiality (γ = 30°).
- The variance of the triaxiality variable γ from consistent modeling of observables is centered around 8°.
- Oblate irreps occur within the even-even intervals 8-10, 14-18, 24-28, 34-40, 46-54 across the sd, pf, sdg, pfh, sdgi shells.
- Within spin-orbit shells (14-28, 28-50, 50-82, 82-126, 126-184), oblate irreps occur for 22-24, 42-46, 74-78, 116-122, 172-180 particles respectively.
- Within 3D-HO shells (8-20, 20-40, 40-70, 70-112, 112-168), oblate irreps occur for 16-18, 34-38, 64-68, 104-110, 158-166 particles respectively.
- Shape coexistence is predicted to occur only within the intervals 7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112, 146-168 on the nuclear chart.

## Context

This review extends the proxy-SU(3) work first presented in 2017 (Bonatsos 2017a, 2017b) and builds on Elliott's SU(3) classification of the sd shell (Elliott 1958a, 1958b, 1963). It contrasts with the earlier pseudo-SU(3) symmetry (Arima 1969, RatnaRaju 1973, Draayer 1982), which treats intruder orbitals separately and uses a different unitary transformation. The paper enables future applications including the semimicroscopic algebraic quartet model (Cseh 2020, Hess 2021), the Sp(12,R) collective model (Ganev 2022, 2024), and microscopically derived IBM-1 Hamiltonians (Vasileiou 2024). The predictions for shape coexistence have been corroborated by covariant density functional theory calculations (Bonatsos 2022a, 2022b).
