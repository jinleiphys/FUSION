# Analytic crystals of solitons in the four dimensional gauged non-linear sigma model

> The first analytic topologically non-trivial solutions in the (3+1)-dimensional gauged non-linear sigma model representing multi-solitons at finite volume with manifest ordered structures generating their own electromagnetic field are presented. The complete set of seven coupled non-linear field equations of the gauged non-linear sigma model together with the corresponding Maxwell equations are reduced in a self-consistent way to just one linear Schrodinger-like equation in two dimensions. The corresponding two dimensional periodic potential can be computed explicitly in terms of the solitons profile. The present construction keeps alive the topological charge of the gauged solitons. Both the energy density and the topological charge density are periodic and the positions of their peaks show a crystalline order. These solitons describe configurations in which (most of) the topological charge and total energy are concentrated within three-dimensional tube-shaped regions. The electric and magnetic fields vanish in the center of the tubes and take their maximum values on their surface while the electromagnetic current is contained within these tube-shaped regions. Electromagnetic perturbations of these families of gauged solitons are shortly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first analytic topologically non-trivial solutions in the (3+1)-dimensional gauged non-linear sigma model that represent multi-solitons at finite volume with crystalline order, but only under the condition that the Derrick scaling argument is avoided through a combination of a time-periodic ansatz, minimal coupling to a U(1) gauge field, and finite-volume boundary conditions.

## Method

The authors use a generalized hedgehog ansatz for the SU(2)-valued scalar field with a time-periodic dependence and a specific ansatz for the U(1) gauge potential, reducing the complete set of seven coupled non-linear field equations (three from the gauged non-linear sigma model and four from Maxwell equations) to a single integrable equation for the soliton profile and one linear Schrodinger-like equation in two dimensions with a periodic potential. The profile equation is solved in terms of inverse elliptic functions, and the Maxwell equations are then solved with the soliton acting as an effective potential.

## Key numbers

* Topological charge W = -np when q is an odd integer (q = 2v+1 with v in natural numbers), and W = 0 when q is even.
* The boundary condition for the profile requires alpha(2pi) - alpha(0) = n pi, with n an integer.
* The adimensional coordinates have ranges 0 <= r <= 2pi, 0 <= theta <= pi, 0 <= phi <= 2pi, with the physical volume being 4 pi^3 L^3.
* The parameters p and q are integers with p not equal to 0, and q = 2v+1 with v in natural numbers.

## Context

This work extends the generalized hedgehog ansatz approach previously applied to the non-linear sigma model and Skyrme model (Canfora et al., 2017-2018) to construct analytic multi-soliton crystals with electromagnetic fields. It provides the first analytic examples of gauged solitonic tubes in (3+1) dimensions, which resemble the nuclear spaghetti phases studied numerically in nuclear pasta literature (Ravenhall et al., 1983; Hashimoto et al., 1984; Oyamatsu et al., 1993). The results enable analytic study of electromagnetic response functions of these solitonic crystals as an effective periodic medium.
