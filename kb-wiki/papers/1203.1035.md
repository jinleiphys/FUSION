# Magnetic anomaly in UCN trapping: signal for neutron oscillations to parallel world?

> Present experiments do not exclude that the neutron transforms into some invisible degenerate twin, so called mirror neutron, with an appreciable probability. These transitions are actively studied by monitoring neutron losses in ultra-cold neutron traps, where they can be revealed by their magnetic field dependence. In this work we reanalyze the experimental data acquired by the group of A.P. Serebrov at Institute Laue-Langevin, and find a dependence at more than 5σ away from the null hypothesis. This anomaly can be interpreted as oscillation to mirror neutrons with a timescale of few seconds, in the presence of a mirror magnetic field B'~0.1G at the Earth. If confirmed by future experiments, this will have a number of deepest consequences in particle physics and astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper reanalyzes experimental data from the Serebrov group at ILL and finds a 5.2σ deviation from the null hypothesis in the magnetic-field-direction dependence of ultra-cold neutron (UCN) losses, which the authors interpret as possible evidence for neutron–mirror-neutron oscillations in the presence of a mirror magnetic field B' ~ 0.1 G at Earth, but note that the anomaly requires confirmation by future experiments.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed raw data from UCN trap experiments at ILL (Serebrov et al., 2012) where neutrons were stored for 300 s in a 190 L trap under controlled vertical magnetic fields (B ≈ 0.2 G and 2B ≈ 0.4 G, plus small fields b < 1 mG), with field direction switched between up and down. They computed the asymmetry A_B = (N_{-B} – N_{+B})/(N_{-B} + N_{+B}) in detector counts for each field configuration, and performed global fits to extract the mirror magnetic field B' and the n–n' oscillation time τ, marginalizing over the 25% uncertainty in the experimental B field.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry A_B^det in the {B} series (400 measurements) shows a 5.2σ deviation from zero: A_B^det = (6.96 ± 1.34) × 10⁻⁴ (χ²/dof = 0.87).
- The best-fit point for the n–n' oscillation parameters gives B' = 0.11 G and τ_β = 3 s (where τ_β = τ|cosβ|⁻¹/², with β the angle between B and B').
- At 90% CL, the mirror magnetic field is constrained to B' = 0.09–0.12 G, with oscillation time τ in the range 2–10 s.
- At 99% CL, the allowed range extends to 0.08 G < B' < 0.3 G.
- The mean free-flight time between wall collisions is t_ff = 0.094 s with variance σ_ff² = 0.0036 s².
- The effective exposure time including filling and emptying is t_* ≈ 370 s, giving n_* ≈ 4000 wall scatterings.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of neutron–mirror-neutron oscillations proposed by Berezhiani and Bento (2006) and the experimental searches by Serebrov et al. (2008, 2010), Ban et al. (2007), Altarev et al. (2009), and Bodek et al. (2009). The anomaly, if confirmed, would imply the existence of a mirror magnetic field at Earth, with implications for mirror matter as dark matter, and would motivate dedicated resonance-search experiments at ILL and potentially at DUSEL.
