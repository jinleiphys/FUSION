# A Wilson Renormalization Group Approach to Light-Front Tamm-Dancoff Scalar Field Theory

> A program to utilize the Tamm-Dancoff approximation, on the light-front, to solve relativistic quantum field theories, is presented. We present a well defined renormalization program for the Tamm-Dancoff approximation. This renormalization program utilizes a Minkowski space version of Wilson's renormalization group. We studied light-front $ϕ^{4}$ field theory in 3+1 dimensions, within a two-particle truncation of Fock space. We further simplified our calculations by considering only one marginal operator and one irrelevant operator. The renormalization procedure required no more marginal or relevant operators. We derived an effective, renormalized, Hamiltonian. These techniques may be germane to the effort to find an effective, low energy, light-front Hamiltonian for quantum chromodynamics. PACS number(s): 11.10Gh, 11.10Ef

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a renormalization program for light-front Tamm-Dancoff truncated scalar field theory using a Minkowski-space version of Wilson's renormalization group, but the effective Hamiltonian for this two-particle truncation of \(\phi^4\) theory in 3+1 dimensions is found to be trivial, the couplings go to zero as the cutoff is taken to infinity, though the decay is logarithmically slow.

## Method

The authors study light-front \(\phi^4\) field theory in 3+1 dimensions within a two-particle truncation of Fock space, keeping only one marginal operator and one irrelevant operator. They apply a Bloch transformation (the Minkowski-space counterpart of a Kadanoff transformation) to eliminate high-energy eigenstates of a Gaussian massless fixed-point Hamiltonian, then rescale momenta and fields to restore the original cutoff. The running of the marginal coupling \(g\) and irrelevant coupling \(w\) is computed to second order in perturbation theory, and the two-particle to two-particle scattering amplitude is calculated to test cutoff independence.

## Key numbers

- The marginal coupling runs as \(g_{n+1} = g_n - \frac{3g_n^2}{2\pi^2}\ln(\Lambda_0/\Lambda_1)\) when only the marginal operator is retained, with additional terms when the irrelevant operator is included.
- For a starting marginal coupling \(g = 0.1\), a 50% decay in its value occurs only after a cutoff decrease by a factor of \(10^{30}\), or after 100 iterations of reducing the cutoff by a factor of 2.
- The irrelevant coupling tracks the marginal coupling as \(w = -\frac{3g^2}{8\pi^2}\) after initial transience, for small \(g\).
- The real part of the two-boson to two-boson scattering amplitude shows reduced cutoff dependence when both marginal and irrelevant couplings are allowed to run, compared to fixed couplings.

## Context

This work extends Perry's (1994) Wilson renormalization group approach to light-front field theory by adding an explicit Tamm-Dancoff truncation of Fock space from the outset, in contrast to Perry's study where truncation was not adopted at the beginning. The paper shows that for non-asymptotically free theories like \(\phi^4\) and QED, the method yields trivial effective Hamiltonians, suggesting that strictly non-trivial results require asymptotically free theories such as QCD. The techniques are intended to enable non-perturbative numerical solutions of relativistic quantum field theories on present-day computers.
