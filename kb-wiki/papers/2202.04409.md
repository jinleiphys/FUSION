# Radiative corrections to elastic muon-proton scattering at low momentum transfers

> We systematically calculate the radiative corrections of order $α/π$ to elastic muon-proton scattering at low momentum transfers. These include vacuum polarization, photon-loop form factors of the muon and the proton, two-photon exchange corrections and soft photon radiation. In particular, we discuss these corrections for the kinematics of the upcomimg AMBER experiment with a $100\,$GeV muon beam energy. It is found that for the ratio to the Born cross section, only the minor terms from the photon-loop form factors of the proton and two-photon exchange depend on the proton structure predetermined by the strong interactions. Since a prominent role among the radiative corrections is played by soft photon radiation, the calculation of the bremsstrahlung process $μp \toμp γ$ should be extended beyond the soft photon approximation and tailored to the specific experimental conditions.

## Key claim
The radiative corrections of order α/π to elastic muon-proton scattering at low momentum transfers are systematically calculated; for the ratio to the Born cross section, only the minor terms from the photon-loop form factors of the proton and two-photon exchange depend on the proton structure, while the dominant corrections (vacuum polarization, soft photon radiation, and muon vertex corrections) are universal and structure-independent.

## Method
The authors computed the differential cross section for elastic muon-proton scattering including vacuum polarization, photon-loop form factors of the muon and proton, two-photon exchange box diagrams, and soft photon bremsstrahlung. The proton structure was modeled using both a simple dipole parametrization and the most recent dispersion-theory form factors from Lin (2022), with inelastic contributions treated via single and double excitation of the Δ⁺(1232) resonance. The calculations were performed specifically for the kinematics of the upcoming AMBER experiment with a 100 GeV muon beam energy and momentum transfers 32 MeV < Q < 141 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum polarization at Q=300 MeV: Π_vp = 0.93% (0.86% from electrons, 0.07% from muons); at Q=100 MeV: Π_vp = 0.70% (0.69% from electrons, 0.01% from muons)
- The leading correction to the muon anomalous magnetic moment from the photon-loop form factor: F₂^γ(0) = α/2π = 1.1614 × 10⁻³
- For a structureless proton at AMBER kinematics (E₁=100 GeV, s≈214), the two-photon exchange ratio H₂/H₀ reaches about 0.016% at Q=469 MeV; for the physical proton it does not exceed 0.003% for the low momentum transfers considered
- The photon-loop form factor contribution from the muon (2F₁^{γ-fin}) reaches about 2% at the upper end of the momentum transfers considered
- The negative photon-loop form factor contribution from the proton stays below 0.4% in magnitude
- The two-photon exchange correction is of maximal size 0.3 × 10⁻⁴

## Context
This work extends the radiative correction calculations of Kaiser (2010) and Kaiser (2016) to the specific kinematics of the AMBER experiment at CERN, which aims to measure the proton charge radius with an accuracy better than 0.01 fm. The calculations use the best phenomenological proton form factors from Lin (2021) and complement related work on muon-proton scattering radiative corrections by Tomalak (2015, 2018) and Peset (2021). The results enable precise extraction of the proton charge radius from upcoming AMBER and MUSE experiments, potentially resolving the proton radius puzzle that arose from the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement by Pohl (2010).
