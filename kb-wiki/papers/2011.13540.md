# Shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei: the single particle perspective

> The shell evolution has been studied extensively within the framework of interacting shell model, while the studies from the single particle viewpoint is relatively lacking or neglected. In particular, the isospin dependence of spin-orbit splitting has become increasingly important as $N/Z$ increases in neutron-rich nuclei. Following the initial independent-particle strategy towards explaining the occurrence of magic numbers, we have systematically investigated the isospin effect on the shell evolution of neutron-rich nuclei within the Woods-Saxon (WS) mean-field potential plus the spin-orbit term. It is found that new magic numbers $N = 14$ and $N =16$ may emerge in neutron-rich nuclei if one changes the sign of the isospin-dependent term in the spin-orbit coupling while the traditional magic number $N = 20$ may disappear. The magic number $N = 28$ is expected to be destroyed despite the sign choice of the isospin part in spin-orbit splitting, while $N = 50$ may disappear and $N = 82$ persists within the single particle scheme. Besides, an appreciable amount of energy gap appears at $N = 32$ and 34 in neutron-rich Ca isotopes. All these results are more consistent with those of the interacting shell model, when the sign of the isospin term of the WS potential is different from that of the corresponding spin-orbit coupling part. The present study may provide a more reasonable starting point for not only the interacting shell but also other nuclear many-body calculations towards the neutron-dripline.

## Key claim
New magic numbers N=14 and N=16 may emerge in neutron-rich nuclei, and N=20 may disappear, but only if the sign of the isospin-dependent term in the spin-orbit coupling is chosen opposite to that in the central Woods-Saxon potential (κ_so = -κ); the results are more consistent with the interacting shell model under that specific sign choice.

## Method
The authors constructed a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential plus spin-orbit term with isospin-dependent parameters κ (central potential) and κ_so (spin-orbit potential). They performed three sets of calculations (κ_so = +κ, κ_so = -κ, and κ_so = 0) using the GAMOW code, fitting all parameters to single-particle and single-hole states around doubly magic nuclei ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ¹⁰⁰Sn, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb. Single-particle energies were then computed for neutron orbits in oxygen isotopes, N=20 isotones, calcium isotopes, and N=28, 50, 82 isotones as functions of neutron or proton number.

## Key numbers
- In oxygen isotopes with κ_so = -κ, the gap between 1d₅/₂ and 2s₁/₂ orbits increases from 1.6 MeV in ¹⁶O to 2.9 MeV in ³⁰O.
- For N=20 isotones with κ_so = -κ, the N=20 gap is 4.2 MeV for ²⁸O.
- The N=16 gap in N=20 isotones increases from 0.9 MeV in ⁴⁰Ca to 2.0 MeV in ²⁸O with κ_so = +κ, and to 3.0 MeV in ²⁸O with κ_so = -κ.
- In calcium isotopes with κ_so = -κ, the N=34 shell gap is about 1.6 MeV in ⁴⁸Ca.
- The N=32 gaps of ⁴⁸Ca and ⁵⁴Ca are both 1.5 MeV with κ_so = -κ.
- For the extreme neutron-rich nucleus ⁷⁰Ca, the N=50 energy gaps between 1g₉/₂ and 1d₅/₂ orbits are 0.7 MeV (κ_so = +κ) and 2.1 MeV (κ_so = -κ).

## Context
This work extends the single-particle approach of Blomqvist (1960) and Dudek (1981) by systematically varying the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit term, whereas prior studies (e.g., Ma and Qian, 2015) had limited such analysis. The results are compared with interacting shell model calculations (Otsuka, 2001; 2010) that attribute shell evolution to monopole nucleon-nucleon interactions and tensor forces. The single-particle spectra obtained here may serve as a baseline for shell model calculations in the continuum and for studies of pairing and deformation near the neutron dripline.
