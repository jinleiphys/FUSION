# The Roper Puzzle

> We carried out a calculation of the Roper state with the Sequential Empirical Bayesian (SEB) method with overlap valence fermion on 2+1-flavor domain-wall fermion configurations on the 24^3 x 64 lattice with a^{-1} = 1.73 GeV. The light sea quark mass corresponds to a pion mass of 330 MeV. The mass of the Roper, chirally extrapolated to the physical pion mass, is 1404(112) MeV which is consistent with the experimental value at 1440 MeV. When compared to the Roper state calculation with variational method for Clover and twisted mass fermions, it is found that the Roper states from SEB with overlap fermion are systematically lower by 400 - 800 MeV for all the quark masses ranging from light to the strange mass region. We study the origin of the difference by exploring the size of the interpolation field in relation to the radial wavefunction of the Roper and also the dynamical influence of the higher Fock space.

## Key claim
The Roper resonance mass extracted from lattice QCD using the Sequential Empirical Bayesian (SEB) method with overlap fermions is systematically lower (by 400–800 MeV) than results from variational calculations with Clover, chirally improved, and twisted-mass fermions, across all quark masses from light to strange. The authors propose that this discrepancy may arise from the size of the interpolation field relative to the radial node of the Roper wavefunction, and from differing dynamical couplings between three-quark and five-quark Fock components in different fermion actions.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using overlap valence fermions on 2+1-flavor domain-wall fermion configurations (24³ × 64 lattice, a⁻¹ = 1.73 GeV, pion mass 330 MeV). The Roper state was extracted using the Sequential Empirical Bayesian (SEB) method with a Coulomb wall source and a standard three-quark interpolation field. For comparison, a variational calculation with small smeared sources (r = 0.34 fm) was also performed on the same lattice, and SEB was applied to Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (HSC) Clover fermion configurations to isolate algorithmic from fermion-action effects.

## Key numbers
- Chirally extrapolated Roper mass at physical pion mass: 1404(112) MeV, consistent with the experimental value of 1440 MeV.
- Nucleon mass at physical pion mass from the same extrapolation: 999(46) MeV.
- Roper states from SEB with overlap fermion are systematically lower by 400–800 MeV compared to variational calculations with Clover and twisted-mass fermions, across all quark masses.
- On HSC Clover configurations, the SEB method yields a Roper mass ~300 MeV lower than the HSC variational result, with ~3σ significance.
- The Roper radial wavefunction node occurs at ~0.9 fm for mπ = 438 MeV; the variational smearing radius used (0.34 fm) is substantially smaller than this node.
- Overlap integral between nucleon and Roper wavefunctions: ORN = 0.30 for mπ = 633 MeV and 0.59 for mπ = 438 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier quenched calculations (McDowell et al., 2005) that found the positive-parity excited nucleon crossing below the P-wave state at low pion mass, in contrast to Wilson and chirally-improved fermion calculations (Lutz et al., 2007) which gave Roper masses above 2 GeV. The results contradict variational calculations from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Edwards et al., 2011), CSSM (Mahbub et al., 2012), and others using Clover, twisted-mass, and chirally-improved fermions. The authors suggest the discrepancy may be an O(a²) effect related to different couplings between three-quark and five-quark (πN, ηN) Fock components in different fermion actions, consistent with phenomenological meson-baryon coupled-channel models (Suzuki et al., 2009) that show a ~400 MeV downward shift from bare to physical Roper mass. The work enables future checks by calculating the transition matrix element ⟨0|χN,3q|πN⟩ and the overlap integral ORN across different fermion actions.
