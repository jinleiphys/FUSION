# $χ^{\vphantom\dagger}_{c0}(3915)$ As the Lightest $c\bar c s \bar s$ State

> The state $χ^{\vphantom\dagger}_{c0}(3915)$ has recently been demoted by the Particle Data Group from its previous status as the conventional $c\bar c$ $2 {}^3P_0$ state, largely due to the absence of expected $D\bar D$ decays. We propose that $χ^{\vphantom\dagger}_{c0}(3915)$ is actually the lightest $c\bar c s \bar s$ state, and calculate the spectrum of such states using the diquark model, identifying many of the observed charmoniumlike states that lack open-charm decay modes as $c\bar c s \bar s$. Among other results, we argue that $Y(4140)$ is a $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$ $c\bar c s \bar s$ state that has been not been seen in two-photon fusion largely as a consequence of the Landau-Yang theorem.

# χ_{c0}(3915) As the Lightest c\bar c s \bar s State

## Key claim

The authors propose that χ_{c0}(3915) is not the conventional c\bar c 2^3P_0 charmonium state as previously thought, but rather the lightest hidden-charm, hidden-strangeness (c\bar c s\bar s) tetraquark state, which naturally explains its lack of observed open-charm decays and its isosinglet nature.

## Method

The authors employ a Type-II diquark-antidiquark model Hamiltonian with local spin-spin couplings, spin-orbit terms, and orbital excitations between the diquark and antidiquark, neglecting all spin-spin couplings between a quark of the diquark and an antiquark of the antidiquark. They compute the masses of all s-wave and p-wave c\bar c s\bar s states using a four-parameter fit (base mass M_0, orbital excitation coefficient \tilde B, spin-orbit coefficient \alpha, and diquark spin-spin coefficient k), fixing M_0 from the χ_{c0}(3915) mass and k from the Y(4140) mass, then comparing the predicted spectrum to nine observed exotic charmoniumlike candidates that lack open-charm decays.

## Key numbers

- χ_{c0}(3915) mass: M = 3918.4 ± 1.2 MeV, width: Γ = 20 ± 5 MeV (from Particle Data Group)
- χ_{c0}(3915) identified as the ground state X_0 = |0^{++}⟩_1, fixing M_0 ≃ 3920 MeV
- D_s^+ D_s^- threshold lies at 3937 MeV
- J/ψ φ threshold lies at 4116 MeV
- η_c η threshold lies at 3531 MeV
- Y(4140) identified as X_1 = |1^{++}⟩ state, fixing k ≃ 220 MeV
- Predicted masses from the fit (in MeV): M_0 = 3920, k = 220, \tilde B = α = 90
- Predicted s-wave states: X_0 at 3920 MeV, X'_0 at 4360 MeV, X_1 at 4140 MeV, Z at 4140 MeV, Z' at 4360 MeV, X_2 at 4360 MeV
- Predicted p-wave states include Y_1 at 3920 MeV, Y_2 at 4230 MeV, Y_3 at 4360 MeV, Y_4 at 4630 MeV
- Y(4274) reported by CDF as 4274.4^{+8.4}_{-6.7} ± 1.9 MeV and by CMS as 4313.8 ± 5.3 ± 7.3 MeV
- Lattice QCD predicts the 1^{--} charmonium hybrid at 4216 ± 7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the diquark-antidiquark model first proposed by Maiani (2004) and applied to c\bar c s\bar s states by Drenska (2009), using an improved version from Maiani (2014). The paper addresses the PDG's demotion of χ_{c0}(3915) from conventional charmonium status, following objections raised by Guo (2012), Wang (2014), and Olsen (2014) regarding the absence of expected D\bar D decays and the small mass splitting with χ_{c2}(2P). The identification enables a unified description of nine exotic states lacking open-charm decays, including Y(4140), Y(4274), X(4350), and the ISR states Y(4008), Y(4260), Y(4360), and Y(4660), and predicts that Y(4140) as a 1^{++} state is forbidden from two-photon production by the Landau-Yang theorem, explaining its absence in γγ fusion experiments.
