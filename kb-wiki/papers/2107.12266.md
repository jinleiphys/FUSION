# Spinless mesons and glueballs mixing patterns in SU(3) flavor limit

> We present a detailed study of the interactions among scalar and pseudoscalar mesons and glueballs within the framework of the generalized linear sigma model in the SU(3) flavor limit. The basis of our approach is to develop a global understanding of light scalar and pseudoscalar mesons (up to around 2 GeV) and thereby explore the underlying mixings among composite quark matter fields and glueballs. The chiral sector of the Lagrangian is formulated in terms of two chiral nonets representing quark-antiquarks and tetraquarks (in the leading order, this sector contains terms with eight or fewer number of quak or antiquark lines). The Lagrangian also contains a sector that represents scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs and their interactions with the matter chiral fields, in a manner that the axial and trace anomalies of QCD are exactly realized. In this construct, the model has two scalar octets and two pdeudoscalar octets (each a linear combination of two- and four-quark components), as well as three scalar SU(3) singlets and three pseudoscalar SU(3) singlets (each a linear combination of two- and four-quark components as well as a glueball component). With the inputs of the experimental masses of $a_0(980)$, $a_0(1450)$, and the masses of $π(137)$, $π(1300)$ and their decay constants, we perform an extensive numerical simulation to determine the boundaries of the parameter space of the model. We further incorporate experimental data on the mass spectrum of eta states as well as on several decay widths and decay ratios of $f_0$ states to zoom in on the parameter space and make predictions for the substructure of pseudoscalar and scalar SU(3) octets and singlets as well as for the pseudoscalar and scalar glueball masses. We find the scalar and pseudoscalar glueball masses around 1.6 and 2.0 GeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the generalized linear sigma model in the SU(3) flavor limit, the scalar and pseudoscalar glueball masses are found around 1.6 and 2.0 GeV, respectively, but this result depends on a broad numerical scan of the glueball condensate parameter \(h_0\) over a range from 0.20 to 0.87 GeV, without taking inputs from QCD sum-rules or lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian with two chiral nonet fields (representing quark-antiquark and tetraquark mesons) plus scalar and pseudoscalar glueball fields, incorporating chiral symmetry, axial anomaly, and trace anomaly of QCD. They use experimental inputs for the masses of \(a_0(980)\), \(a_0(1450)\), \(\pi(137)\), \(\pi(1300)\) and their decay constants, plus data on eta state masses and \(f_0\) decay widths and ratios, to perform a numerical simulation that scans the parameter space and determines acceptable parameter sets. The model is first solved in a decoupling limit where the scalar glueball does not mix with quark composites, then the decoupling condition is relaxed while maintaining SU(3) flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

* Scalar glueball mass predicted around 1.6 GeV, based on the numerical simulation within the SU(3) flavor limit.
* Pseudoscalar glueball mass predicted around 2.0 GeV, based on the same simulation.
* The glueball condensate parameter \(h_0\) is probed over a range from 0.20 to 0.87 GeV, with no external input from QCD sum-rules or lattice QCD.
* In the decoupling limit, the pure scalar glueball mass is given by \(m_h = 2 h_0\), with \(h_0\) estimated in the range 0.887 to 0.974 GeV from QCD sum-rules, yielding a pure scalar glueball mass range of 1.77 to 1.95 GeV.
* The model has 14 free parameters: \(u_1\) through \(u_6\), \(\gamma_1\), \(\lambda_2\), \(\lambda_3\), \(\Lambda\), \(A\), \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), and \(h_0\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized linear sigma model framework previously developed by Fariborz and collaborators (Jora et al., 2010-2019) for scalar and pseudoscalar mesons up to around 2 GeV, and the extension to include glueballs by Jora (2020) and Fariborz, Jora, and Lyukova (2021). It goes beyond the decoupling limit studied in Jora (2020) by allowing the scalar glueball to mix with quark composites, and it determines the glueball condensate \(h_0\) purely within the model rather than from QCD sum-rules or lattice QCD. The results enable future studies of SU(3) breaking effects to construct a more realistic picture of scalar and pseudoscalar meson substructure and their interactions with glueballs.
