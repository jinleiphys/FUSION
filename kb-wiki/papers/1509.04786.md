# ${\bar p}p$ annihilation into ${\bar D}D$ meson pair within an effective Lagrangian model

> We study the charmed meson pair (${\bar D}^0 D^0$ and $D^- D^+$) production in ${\bar p}p$ annihilation within an effective Lagrangian model that has only the baryon-meson degrees of freedom and involves the physical hadron masses. The reaction amplitudes include terms corresponding to the t-channel $Λ_c^+$, $Σ_c^+$ and $Σ_c^{++}$ baryon exchanges and the s-channel excitation, propagation and decay of the $Ψ$(3770) resonance into the charmed mesons. The initial- and final-state distortion effects have been accounted for by using a simple eikonal approximation-based procedure in the same way as was done in our previous study of the ${\bar p}p \to {\bar Λ}_c^-Λ_c^+$ reaction within a similar model. The ${\bar D}^0 D^0$ production reaction is dominated by the $Λ_c^+$ baryon exchange process, and the corresponding total cross sections are predicted to be in the range of 0.18 - 0.7 $μ$b for antiproton beam momenta varying between threshold and 20 $GeV/c$. The $Ψ$(3770) resonance contributions have a large influence on the differential cross sections of the $D^- D^+$ production reaction.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the total cross section for the ${\bar p}p \to {\bar D}^0 D^0$ reaction is dominated by the $t$-channel $\Lambda_c^+$ baryon exchange process and lies in the range of 0.18-0.7 $\mu$b for antiproton beam momenta from threshold to 20 GeV/c, but this result depends on the specific eikonal approximation used for initial-state distortion effects and the chosen cutoff parameter of 3.0 GeV for the monopole form factors.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian model with baryon-meson degrees of freedom, calculating reaction amplitudes that include $t$-channel exchanges of $\Lambda_c^+$, $\Sigma_c^+$, and $\Sigma_c^{++}$ baryons, as well as $s$-channel excitation, propagation, and decay of the $\Psi(3770)$ resonance into charmed meson pairs. Initial- and final-state distortion effects were accounted for using an eikonal approximation-based procedure with a Gaussian imaginary potential, using the same parameters as in their previous study of the ${\bar p}p \to {\bar \Lambda}_c^- \Lambda_c^+$ reaction (Shyam 2014). The coupling constants were adopted from prior work (Hai11a, Hob14), and the $\kappa_\Psi$ parameter was fixed by comparing the effective Lagrangian model cross sections to a Breit-Wigner resonance model.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for ${\bar p}p \to {\bar D}^0 D^0$ predicted in the range 0.18-0.7 $\mu$b for antiproton beam momenta from threshold to 20 GeV/c.
- At the beam momentum of interest to the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment (15 GeV/c), the total cross section for ${\bar D}^0 D^0$ production is about 570 nb.
- The $\Psi(3770)$ resonance peak cross section for ${\bar p}p \to \Psi(3770) \to {\bar D}^0 D^0$ is about 12 nb.
- Coupling constants used: $g_{N \Lambda_c^+ D} = 13.50$, $g_{N \Sigma_c^+ D} = 2.69$, $g_{N \Sigma_c^{++} D} = 2.69$, $g_{\Psi {\bar D}^0 D^0} = 17.90$, $g_{\Psi D^- D^+} = 14.10$, $g_{\Psi {\bar p} p} = 5.12 \times 10^{-3}$.
- The $\Psi(3770)$ resonance mass is 3773.15 MeV with a total width of 27.2 MeV.
- The $D^- D^+$ production cross section is suppressed relative to ${\bar D}^0 D^0$ by nearly a factor of 650 due to the ratio of coupling constants $(g_{N\Lambda_c^+D}/g_{N\Sigma_c^{++}D})^4$.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied to ${\bar p}p \to {\bar \Lambda}_c^- \Lambda_c^+$ (Shyam 2014) to the charmed meson pair production channels relevant for the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment at FAIR. The results differ from the coupled-channels meson-exchange model of Haidenbauer (2014), which predicts larger $\Psi(3770)$ resonance effects for ${\bar D}^0 D^0$ and larger $D^- D^+$ cross sections due to two-step transition mechanisms not included in this model. The calculations also contrast with the double handbag model of Gorchtein (2013), which predicts total cross sections below 10 nb, and with Regge model results from Kaidalov (1994) and Kholodenko (2012) that give cross sections around 70-100 nb at 15 GeV/c. The work enables predictions for charmonium detection above the open charm threshold and for studies of charmed hypernuclei and $D$-mesic nuclei at FAIR.
