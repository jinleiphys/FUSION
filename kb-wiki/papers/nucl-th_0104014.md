# Coulomb excitation at ultra-relativistic energies

> In previous studies of the theory of Coulomb excitation, the term in the electromagnetic interaction which is quadratic in the vector potential has been ignored. In this paper, we use qualitative arguments and detailed numerical calculations to show that this quadratic term must be included when the bombarding energy is in excess of about 20 GeV per nucleon.

## Key claim

The term in the electromagnetic interaction quadratic in the vector potential (the **A·A** term), previously ignored in all Coulomb excitation theory, must be included for bombarding energies above about 20 GeV per nucleon; at 80 GeV per nucleon the full interaction yields about twice the cross-section of the linear term alone, and at higher energies the quadratic term dominates.

## Method

The authors use the semi-classical theory of Coulomb excitation, treating projectile motion as straight-line at constant speed and the target nucleus quantum-mechanically. They derive the Fourier-transformed matrix elements of both the linear term \(V_1\) and the quadratic term \(V_2\) from the Lienard-Wiechert potentials, then apply the exactly-solvable vibrational model (Poisson distribution for excitation probabilities) to a hypothetical giant quadrupole oscillator band in \(^{40}\)Ca excited by a \(^{208}\)Pb projectile, integrating over impact parameters from a minimum of 12 fm.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(V_2/V_1\) scales as \(\gamma Z_P e^2/(2mc^2 b)\); for \(Z_P=82\) and \(b=10\) fm, this ratio is \(\sim 0.0063\gamma(1-1/\gamma^2)\), giving \(\sim 0.01\) at \(T_P/A=1\) GeV (\(\gamma=2.066\)) and \(\sim 0.34\) at \(T_P/A=50\) GeV (\(\gamma=54.3\)).
- For bombarding energy per nucleon below about 20 GeV, the \(V_2\) contribution is relatively small; at 80 GeV per nucleon, the full \(V_1+V_2\) interaction yields about twice the cross-section of \(V_1\) alone.
- The model uses a B(E2, \(0^+\rightarrow 2^+\)) value of 450 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (exhausting the energy-weighted sum rule) for a giant quadrupole resonance in \(^{40}\)Ca with \(\hbar\omega=20\) MeV, and a minimum impact parameter \(b_{\text{min}}=12\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the classic semi-classical Coulomb excitation theory of Alder and Winther (1975) and the relativistic treatments of Winther and Alder (1979) and Bertulani and Baur (1988), which all neglected the quadratic **A·A** term. The authors show that the Fermi-Weizsäcker-Williams method of equivalent photons does not automatically include this term. The results are relevant to practical questions of beam stability for relativistic heavy ions, as studied by Baur, Bertulani, and collaborators (1990s), and open the need for gauge-invariant coupled-channel calculations and for a more complete relativistic treatment when the scalar potential exceeds the proton rest energy (above about 50 GeV per nucleon).
