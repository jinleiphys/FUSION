# Building up the elliptic flow: analytical insights

> In this paper, we present a fully analytical description of the early-stage formation of elliptic flow in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. We first construct an elliptic deformation of Gubser flow which is a boost invariant solution of the Navier-Stokes equation with a nontrivial transverse profile. We then analytically calculate the momentum anisotropy of the flow as a function of time and discuss the connection with the empirical formula by Bhalerao {\it et al.} regarding the viscosity dependence of elliptic flow.

## Key claim
The paper provides a fully analytical description of early-stage elliptic flow formation in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, but the analysis is valid only under restrictive conditions: the elliptic deformation parameter \(a^2/x_\perp^2 \ll 1\), early times \(\tau \ll x_\perp\), and a window where \(\left(\frac{\eta_0}{C}\right)^{3/4} \gg \frac{\tau}{L\ \text{or}\ x_\perp} \gg \left(\frac{\eta_0}{C}\right)^{3/2}\) for viscous corrections.

## Method
The authors constructed an approximate elliptic solution of the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation by applying a Zhukovsky (Joukowski) conformal transformation to the cylindrically symmetric Gubser flow, which is an exact boost-invariant solution with nontrivial radial flow. They then analytically computed the momentum anisotropy \(\epsilon_p\) from the energy-momentum tensor, separating contributions from spatial anisotropy of the energy density and anisotropy of the flow velocity, and compared the viscous dependence to the empirical formula of Bhalerao et al. (2005).

## Key numbers
- The spatial eccentricity at early times for the ideal solution is \(\epsilon^{ideal}(\tau \approx 0) \approx \frac{14a^2}{9L^2}\).
- The denominator integral \(\int dxdy (T_{xx}+T_{yy}) \approx \frac{8\pi C^4}{5\tau^{4/3}}\left(\frac{2}{L}\right)^{2/3} \left(1-\frac{15\eta_0^2}{2C^2}\left(\frac{L}{2\tau}\right)^{4/3}\right)\).
- The total momentum anisotropy is \(\epsilon_p(\tau)= \frac{20 a^2\tau^2}{3L^4}\left[-\frac{2}{11} -\frac{177\eta_0^2}{154C^2}\left(\frac{L}{2\tau}\right)^{4/3}\right]\), which is negative at leading order.
- The flow anisotropy contribution alone gives \(\left.\frac{\int dx dy (T_{xx}-T_{yy}) }{\int dx dy ( T_{xx}+T_{yy})}\right|_{\delta u} =\frac{20 a^2\tau^2}{3L^4} \left[ \frac{6}{7}- \frac{3\eta_0}{2C}\left(\frac{L}{2\tau}\right)^{2/3} +\frac{513\eta_0^2}{70C^2}\left(\frac{L}{2\tau}\right)^{4/3} \right]\).

## Context
This work extends the exact Gubser flow solutions (Gubser, 2010) by introducing elliptic deformation, enabling analytical study of elliptic flow formation that previously relied on numerical simulations (Kolb et al., 1999; Luzum and Romatschke, 2008). The results provide an explicit analytical justification for the empirical formula of Bhalerao et al. (2005) relating elliptic flow suppression to shear viscosity, and enable future analytical studies of non-flow effects and the role of initial velocity in elliptic flow development.
