# Isobar correlations bearing information on the properties of hot disassembling nuclear sources

> Two-particle correlations based on the multiplicity of selected isobars are found to be sensitive to the parameterization of the fragments' binding energies and the breakup volume assumed in the model calculations. The properties of these correlations have been examined in the framework of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model as a function of the breakup temperature. The model calculations suggest that the maxima of these correlation functions occur at well separated temperatures as the breakup volumes used in the model vary from 3 to 6 times that at normal density. These volumes are within the range assumed in most statistical calculations and supported by experiments. Besides their position, the height and width of the maxima are also found to be sensitive to the parameterization of the fragments' binding energy. The magnitude of all these effects also depends on the isobars considered in the correlations. We found that, due to an interplay between the symmetry energy and the volume dependent terms of the Helmholtz free energy, in the case of nearly symmetric sources, correlations involving light mirror nuclei seem to enhance these effects. We suggest that the proposed correlation functions could be used to extract information on the fragments' energies and on the breakup volume of nuclear sources.

## Key claim

Two-particle correlations based on the multiplicity of selected isobars are sensitive to both the parameterization of fragments' binding energies and the breakup volume assumed in model calculations, with the maxima of these correlation functions occurring at well separated temperatures as the breakup volume varies from 3 to 6 times normal density.

## Method

The authors employed the canonical Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to calculate two-particle correlation functions \(C_{i,j}\) for selected isobar pairs, using recurrence relations derived by Das Gupta and Mekjian (1995) to evaluate statistical weights exactly. They examined sources of mass number \(A_0=189\) with atomic numbers \(Z_0=77\) (neutron rich) and \(Z_0=89\) (nearly symmetric), as well as smaller sources of \(A_0=122\) with \(Z_0=50\) and \(Z_0=77\), using two different parameterizations for fragments' binding energies.

## Key numbers

- For the larger source (\(A_0=189\)), the peak of \(C_{i,j}\) occurs at \(T\approx 5.3\) MeV for breakup volume \(V=6V_0\) and at \(T\approx 6.3\) MeV for \(V=3V_0\) using the binding energy parameterization from ISMMlong.
- Using the mass formula from Bondorf (1995), the peak positions shift to \(T\approx 6.1\) MeV for \(V=6V_0\) and \(T\approx 7.1\) MeV for \(V=3V_0\), approximately 1 MeV higher than with the ISMMlong parameterization.
- For the smaller source (\(A_0=122\)), peaks occur at \(T\approx 5.0\) MeV for \(V=6V_0\) and \(T\approx 5.8\) MeV for \(V=3V_0\) using the ISMMlong binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the study of freeze-out properties in heavy-ion collisions, building on experimental efforts to determine breakup density by groups such as Borderie (2019) and others, which suggest freeze-out densities ranging from 1/3 to 1/10 of normal nuclear matter density. The proposed correlation functions could help narrow the uncertainty on breakup density and provide information on fragments' binding energies, including the symmetry energy coefficient, which may be affected by finite temperature effects as studied by Li (2006), Natowitz (2007), and others.
