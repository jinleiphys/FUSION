# Possible non-prompt photons in $pp$ collisions and their effects in $AA$ analyses

> Direct photons are a powerful tool for elucidating the properties of the hot QCD matter in heavy-ion collisions. They are conventionally estimated by assuming prompt photon contributions in proton-proton collisions and thermal and prompt photon contributions in heavy-ion collisions. On the other hand, there could also be other sources of photons such as pre-equilibrium photons. I investigate prompt, pre-equilibrium and thermal photons and their effects on the direct photon $p_T$ spectra at CERN Large Hadron Collider energies.

# Possible non-prompt photons in $pp$ collisions and their effects in $AA$ analyses

## Key claim
Direct photons in proton-proton collisions may not be purely prompt photons; pre-equilibrium photon contributions could be comparable to total photon yields near the saturation momentum scale $Q_s$, implying that the conventional baseline for heavy-ion analyses using scaled $pp$ direct photon data may require modification.

## Method
The author models direct photon production in Pb-Pb and $pp$ collisions at LHC energies by combining three components: prompt photons from hard processes, thermal photons from a (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model with a lattice QCD-matched equation of state, and pre-equilibrium photons from the turbulent thermalization approach to glasma using the Berges-Reygers-Tanji-Venugopalan emission rate. The pre-equilibrium photon calculation divides the glasma phase into three stages according to the bottom-up thermalization scenario, with auxiliary coefficients scaling the processes into the pre-hydrodynamic phase.

## Key numbers
- Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV: impact parameter $b = 4.6$ fm (average of $0$-$20\%$ centrality events); nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section $\sigma_{pp}^\mathrm{in} = 65$ mb; thermalization time $\tau_\mathrm{th} = 0.4$ fm/$c$
- Critical temperature $T_c = 0.17$ GeV with smoothing width $\Delta T = 0.1 T_c$; kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_f = 0.14$ GeV; QCD coupling $\alpha_s = 0.2$
- Saturation momentum $Q_s = 3$ GeV for Pb-Pb collisions and $Q_s = 0.9$ GeV for $pp$ collisions
- Prompt photon parametrization: $E dR^\gamma/d^3p = 6745 \sqrt{s} / (p_T)^5 \cdot N_\mathrm{coll} / \sigma_{pp}^\mathrm{in}$ with $\sigma_{pp}^\mathrm{in}$ in pb

## Context
This work extends the conventional picture of direct photon production in nuclear collisions, where direct photons in $pp$ collisions are assumed to be purely prompt and thermal photons are only considered in $AA$ collisions. Recent hydrodynamic analyses hinting at primordial collective dynamics in small systems (Weller 2017, PHENIX 2018) and the possibility of thermal photon radiation in high-multiplicity $pp$ events (Shen 2016) motivate the inclusion of pre-equilibrium photons. The results suggest that pre-equilibrium photons could modify the $pp$ baseline used for scaling in $AA$ analyses, and that direct photon measurements may experimentally constrain pre-equilibrium physics through the saturation momentum scale $Q_s$.
