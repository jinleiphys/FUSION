# Quantum van der Waals and Walecka models of nuclear matter

> A comparable study of the quantum van der Waals and Walecka models of nuclear matter is presented. Each model contains two parameters which characterize the repulsive and attractive interactions between nucleons. These parameters are fixed in order to reproduce the known properties of the nuclear ground state. Both models predict a first-order liquid-gas phase transition and a very similar behavior in the vicinity of the critical point. Critical exponents of the quantum van der Waals model are studied both analytically and numerically. There are important differences in the behavior of the thermodynamical functions of the considered models at large values of the nucleon number density. At the same time both models fall into the universality class of mean-field theory.

## Key claim

Both the quantum van der Waals (QvdW) and Walecka models of nuclear matter fall into the mean-field universality class with identical critical exponents, despite important differences in their thermodynamic behavior at high nucleon density; the paper's abstract does not highlight that the critical amplitudes, which characterize the critical behavior, are different in the quantum and classical van der Waals models.

## Method

The authors compare three models of symmetric nuclear matter: the Walecka mean-field model (with scalar σ-meson attraction and vector ω-meson repulsion), the quantum van der Waals (QvdW) model (with excluded-volume repulsion and density-proportional attractive mean field), and a Hybrid model (scalar-meson attraction with excluded-volume repulsion). Each model's two interaction parameters are fixed by reproducing the nuclear ground state properties at T=0: zero pressure, binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV, and saturation density n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³. The critical exponents of the QvdW model are studied both analytically (via Taylor expansion of the scaled variance near the critical point) and numerically (by evaluating γ(τ) and δ(ñ) as functions of proximity to the critical point).

## Key numbers

- Ground state saturation density: n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at ground state: E_B ≈ -16 MeV, giving ε/n ≈ 922 MeV
- QvdW model parameters: b ≈ 3.42 fm³, a ≈ 329 MeV·fm³, μ*_GS ≈ 998 MeV
- Walecka model parameters: c_v² ≈ 11.0 fm², c_s² ≈ 14.6 fm², μ*_GS ≈ 573 MeV, m*_GS ≈ 510 MeV
- Hybrid model parameters: b ≈ 3.12 fm³, c_s² ≈ 3.46 fm², μ*_GS ≈ 897 MeV, m*_GS ≈ 834 MeV
- Critical temperature T_c: Walecka 18.9 MeV, Hybrid 19.2 MeV, QvdW 19.7 MeV (experimental estimate 17.9 ± 0.4 MeV)
- Critical density n_c: Walecka 0.070 fm⁻³, Hybrid 0.071 fm⁻³, QvdW 0.072 fm⁻³ (experimental estimate 0.06 ± 0.01 fm⁻³)
- Critical pressure p_c: Walecka 0.48 MeV·fm⁻³, Hybrid 0.50 MeV·fm⁻³, QvdW 0.52 MeV·fm⁻³ (experimental estimate 0.31 ± 0.07 MeV·fm⁻³)
- Ground state incompressibility K₀: Walecka 553 MeV, Hybrid 674 MeV, QvdW 763 MeV (experimental range 250-315 MeV)
- QvdW critical exponent γ = 1, with critical amplitude G ≈ 0.257
- QvdW critical exponent δ = 3, with critical amplitude D ≈ 1.4
- QvdW limiting density from excluded volume: n_lim = 1/b

## Context

This work extends the quantum van der Waals approach introduced by Vovchenko (2015) and compares it systematically with the classic Walecka mean-field model (1974), which is the simplest model from the relativistic mean-field framework. The study shows that both models, despite their different physical mechanisms for repulsion and attraction, belong to the same mean-field universality class with identical critical exponents (α=0, β=1/2, γ=1, δ=3), though their critical amplitudes differ due to Fermi statistics. The results enable future work using more sophisticated models (such as the Boguta-Bodmer RMF model or the Carnahan-Starling real gas model) to better reproduce empirical incompressibility values while preserving the mean-field critical behavior.
