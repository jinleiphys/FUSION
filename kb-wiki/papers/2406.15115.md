# Electromagnetic isovector form factors of the transition from the $N^*(1520)$ to the nucleon

> Dispersion theory is used to provide a model-independent low-energy representation of the three electromagnetic isovector transition form factors $N^{*}(1520)\to N$. At low energies the virtual photon couples dominantly to a pion pair. Taking the very well understood pion vector form factor and pion re-scattering into consideration, the determination of the transition form factors is traced back to the determination of pion-baryon scattering amplitudes. Their low-energy aspects are parametrized by baryon exchange, accounting for the main decay channels of the $N^*(1520)$. Short-distance physics is encoded in subtraction constants that are fitted to data on space-like form factors and hadronic decays. It is shown that a limitation in the determination of the subtraction constants lies in the fact that isovector form factors require sufficient information about the differences between protons and neutrons. In particular, this calls for improvements in the form factor extraction from the electroproduction of the $N^*(1520)$ on the neutron. Via the dispersion relations, space- and time-like regions are naturally connected from first principles. This allows to predict the time-like form factors that enter the Dalitz decays $N^*(1520)\to N e^- e^+$ and $N^*(1520)\to N μ^- μ^+$. Under the assumption of the dominance of the isovector over the isoscalar channel, the Dalitz decay distributions are predicted.

## Key claim

Dispersion theory provides a model-independent low-energy representation of the three electromagnetic isovector transition form factors for $N^*(1520) \to N$, but the determination of subtraction constants is limited by the need for sufficient information about differences between protons and neutrons, calling for improved form factor extraction from electroproduction on the neutron.

## Method

The authors use once-subtracted dispersion relations to relate the isovector transition form factors to the well-known pion vector form factor and pion-baryon scattering amplitudes, with the two-pion intermediate state dominating at low energies. The pion-baryon amplitudes are constructed from one-baryon exchange processes (accounting for the main decay channels of the $N^*(1520)$) plus pion re-scattering, with short-distance physics encoded in subtraction constants fitted to space-like form factor data and hadronic decays. The formalism connects space-like and time-like regions from first principles, enabling predictions for Dalitz decays $N^*(1520) \to N e^- e^+$ and $N^*(1520) \to N \mu^- \mu^+$.

## Key numbers

- The helicity amplitudes scale as $H_+ \sim 1/Q^4$, $H_0 \sim 1/Q^6$, $H_- \sim 1/Q^6$ at high $Q^2$ (space-like region), while the constraint-free form factors scale as $F_1 \sim 1/Q^6$, $F_2 \sim 1/Q^8$, $F_3 \sim 1/Q^8$
- The dispersive integral cutoff $\Lambda$ is varied between 1 GeV and 2 GeV, with results insensitive to this variation
- The differential decay width for $N^*(1520) \to N \ell^+ \ell^-$ depends on the lepton velocity $\beta_\ell = \sqrt{1 - 4 m_\ell^2 / q^2}$ and the momentum $p_z = \lambda^{1/2}(m_{N^*}^2, m_N^2, q^2) / (2\sqrt{q^2})$
- The real photon decay width is $\Gamma_{N\gamma} = e^2 (m_{N^*} - m_N) (m_N + m_{N^*})^3 (3|H_-(0)|^2 + |H_+(0)|^2) / (96 \pi m_{N^*}^3)$

## Context

This work extends the dispersive formalism previously developed by the Uppsala group for hyperon transition form factors (Granados 2017, Junker 2019), nucleon form factors (Leupold 2017, Alvarado 2023), and nucleon-to-Delta transition form factors (Aung 2024) to the first negative-parity spin-3/2 nucleon excitation. It provides a model-independent alternative to quark models (Ramalho 2013, 2016) and vector meson dominance models (Krivoruchenko 2001, Zetenyi 2001, 2012) for the $N^*(1520) \to N$ transition. The results enable predictions for Dalitz decay distributions measurable by HADES and for time-like form factors accessible at BESIII and Belle II, while also identifying the need for improved neutron electroproduction data from JLab.
