# Projected shell model description for nuclear isomers

> Nuclear isomer is a current research focus. To describe isomers, we present a method based on the Projected Shell Model. Two kinds of isomers, K-isomers and shape isomers, are discussed. For the K-isomer treatment, K-mixing is properly implemented in the model. It is found however that in order to describe the strong K-violation more efficiently, it may be necessary to further introduce triaxiality into the shell model basis. To treat shape isomers, a scheme is outlined which allows mixing those configurations belonging to different shapes.

## Key claim

The Projected Shell Model (PSM) can describe K-isomers and shape isomers, but for K-isomers with strong K-violation (enhanced decay to the ground state), the current axially-symmetric PSM basis provides too weak mixing; a triaxial PSM basis is required to reproduce the observed large inter-band B(E2) values in nuclei such as ¹⁷⁶Hf and ¹⁷⁸W.

## Method

The PSM constructs a shell-model basis from a few quasiparticle (qp) orbitals near the Fermi surfaces, built on axially-deformed Nilsson single-particle states with BCS pairing, then performs angular momentum projection on each multi-qp configuration. For K-isomers, the Hamiltonian (pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole and quadrupole-pairing terms) is diagonalized in the projected basis, mixing states of different K quantum numbers. For shape isomers, a scheme is outlined to compute overlapping matrix elements between configurations belonging to different deformations (e.g., prolate and oblate) by evaluating rotated multi-qp states using the Thouless theorem and HFB transformations.

## Key numbers

- In ¹⁷⁸Hf, the 16⁺ isomer has excitation energy 2.45 MeV and a 31-year lifetime; the PSM calculation used deformation parameters ε₂=0.251 and ε₄=0.056.
- For ¹⁷⁴Yb, the PSM calculation with ε₂=0.275 and ε₄=0.042 reproduced the inter-band B(E2) from the 6⁺ isomer to the 4⁺ ground-band state as 8.49×10⁻⁸ e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value 4.3(8)×10⁻⁹ e²fm⁴.
- For ¹⁷⁶Hf, the experimental inter-band B(E2) from the 6⁺ isomer to ground band is 1.8×10⁻⁵ e²fm⁴; for ¹⁷⁸W it is 2.6×10⁻² e²fm⁴.
- In ⁷²Kr, a shape isomer at 671 keV was observed with half-life τ = 38±3 ns; the PSM calculation finds the prolate minimum 0.7 MeV above the oblate ground state.
- In ⁶⁸Se, the PSM calculation predicts a prolate minimum 1.1 MeV above the oblate ground state.

## Context

This work extends the PSM framework (previously applied to rotational bands) to the specific problem of nuclear isomers, addressing both K-isomers and shape isomers. For K-isomers, the paper contrasts the PSM approach with the γ-tunneling model of Narimatsu, Shimizu, and Shizuma (2001), which successfully described enhanced B(E2) in ¹⁷⁶Hf and ¹⁷⁸W but could not reproduce the small inter-band B(E2) in ¹⁷⁴Yb; the author proposes a unified description via a triaxial PSM, which has been developed for odd-odd nuclei by Gao (2006) and even-even nuclei by Javid (2008). For shape isomers, the work connects to the rp-process in X-ray bursts, where isomers in ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr may affect nucleosynthesis paths, and outlines a general scheme for mixing configurations of different shapes that could be applied to transitional nuclei or superdeformed states.
