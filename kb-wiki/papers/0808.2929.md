# In medium T-matrix for nuclear matter with three-body forces - binding energy and single particle properties

> We present spectral calculations of nuclear matter properties including three-body forces. Within the in-medium T-matrix approach, implemented with the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen potentials plus the three-nucleon Urbana interaction, we compute the energy per particle in symmetric and neutron matter. The three-body forces are included via an effective density dependent two-body force in the in-medium T-matrix equations. After fine tuning the parameters of the three-body force to reproduce the phenomenological saturation point in symmetric nuclear matter, we calculate the incompressibility and the energy per particle in neutron matter. We find a soft equation of state in symmetric nuclear matter but a relatively large value of the symmetry energy. We study the the influence of the three-body forces on the single-particle properties. For symmetric matter the spectral function is broadened at all momenta and all densities, while an opposite effect is found for the case of neutrons only. Noticeable modification of the spectral functions are realized only for densities above the saturation density. The modifications of the self-energy and the effective mass are not very large and appear to be strongly suppressed above the Fermi momentum.

## Key claim

The inclusion of three-body forces via an effective density-dependent two-body force within the in-medium T-matrix approach improves the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, yielding a soft equation of state with incompressibility around 150 MeV (lower than typical experimental estimates of 210 ± 30 MeV) and a relatively large symmetry energy at saturation (37–40 MeV compared to the common estimate of 32 ± 6 MeV).

## Method

The authors solved the self-consistent in-medium T-matrix equations at zero temperature using the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen nucleon-nucleon potentials plus the Urbana three-nucleon interaction. The three-body force was converted into an effective density-dependent two-body potential by averaging over the third nucleon's momentum distribution, then added to the two-body potential in the T-matrix iteration. After tuning the three-body force parameters to reproduce the phenomenological saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter, they computed the energy per particle, spectral functions, self-energies, and effective masses for both symmetric and pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for CD-Bonn + TBF: 0.171 fm⁻³ (compared to experimental 0.16 ± 0.01 fm⁻³)
- Binding energy at saturation for CD-Bonn + TBF: -16.3 MeV (compared to experimental -16 ± 1 MeV)
- Incompressibility at saturation for CD-Bonn + TBF: 148 MeV; for Nijmegen + TBF: 158 MeV (experimental estimate: 210 ± 30 MeV)
- Symmetry energy at saturation for CD-Bonn + TBF: 39.7 MeV; for Nijmegen + TBF: 37.1 MeV (experimental estimate: 32 ± 6 MeV)
- Effective mass at Fermi momentum in symmetric matter at ρ₀ for CD-Bonn + TBF: 0.87 m (m = 939 MeV)
- Effective mass at Fermi momentum in symmetric matter at 3 ρ₀ for CD-Bonn + TBF: approximately 0.6 m
- Effective mass at Fermi momentum in neutron matter at ρ₀ for CD-Bonn + TBF: approximately 0.85 m
- Effective mass at Fermi momentum in neutron matter at 3 ρ₀ for CD-Bonn + TBF: approximately 0.75 m
- Self-energy modification from TBF in symmetric matter: less than 5% at ρ₀, up to 10% at 3 ρ₀
- Self-energy shift from TBF in neutron matter: 5–10 MeV more attractive

## Context

This work extends prior self-consistent T-matrix calculations that included only two-body forces (Bozek 2002, Dewulf 2003, Rios 2007) by incorporating the Urbana three-nucleon interaction, following an earlier attempt using a phenomenological Lagaris-Pandharipande TBF (Soma 2007). The results are compared to variational calculations by Akmal et al. (1998) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Baldo and Maieron (2007), both using the Argonne v18 potential with TBF. The approach enables future finite-temperature calculations of nuclear matter thermodynamics including three-body forces, which are necessary for realistic equations of state for neutron star modeling and heavy-ion collision simulations.
