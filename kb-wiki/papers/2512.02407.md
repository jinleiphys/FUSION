# Non-diagonal DVCS with spinless hadron-to-two-hadron transition $γ^* π\to γππ$

> We present the formalism of hadron-to-two-hadron transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for spinless hadron case. Definitions of the twist-2 unpolarized and polarized $π\toππ$ transition GPDs are introduced with a particular choice of kinematic variables that characterize the produced two-pion system. In the vicinity of $ρ(770)$, we work out the two-pion decay angular distributions of the $e^-π\to e^-γππ$ cross section, incorporating both the Bethe-Heitler and deeply virtual Compton scattering processes. Each cross section exhibits a distinctive angular distribution, which is sensitive to the polarization states of the produced $ρ(770)$ resonance. In addition, we construct the double partial-wave expansion of $π\toππ$ transition GPDs in the two-pion decay angles as a generalization of GPDs for transition from a pion to a spin-$\ell$ resonance state. With the help of the Omnès representation, we constrain the $π\toππ$ transition GPDs in terms of the low energy $ππ$ scattering phase shift and build a phenomenological model for these GPDs.

# Non-diagonal DVCS with spinless hadron-to-two-hadron transition γ*π → γππ

## Key claim

The formalism of hadron-to-two-hadron transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for spinless hadrons is presented, with the π → ππ transition GPDs defined using a particular choice of kinematic variables for the produced two-pion system; the two-pion decay angular distributions of the e⁻π → e⁻γππ cross section in the vicinity of ρ(770) are shown to be sensitive to the polarization states of the intermediate ρ(770) resonance, but only under the assumption that collinear factorization theorems are valid for non-diagonal hard exclusive processes involving transitions from a hadron to a two-hadron system with low invariant mass.

## Method

The twist-2 unpolarized and polarized π → ππ transition GPDs are defined through the Fourier transform of non-diagonal hadronic matrix elements of non-local light cone quark operators, with the GPDs depending on the usual variables (x, ξ, t) plus three additional variables characterizing the final two-pion system: the 2π invariant mass Wππ² and the polar and azimuthal decay angles (θπ*, φπ*) in the helicity frame. The double partial-wave expansion of the π → ππ transition GPDs in the two-pion decay angles is constructed as a generalization of GPDs for transition from a pion to a spin-ℓ resonance state, and the Omnès representation is used to constrain the Wππ² dependence of the partial-wave GPDs using low energy ππ scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- fπ ≃ 92.4 MeV: pion decay constant used in the definition of the π → ππ transition GPDs
- mρ = 770 MeV: ρ-meson mass used in the isolated ρ(770) resonance contribution to the amplitude
- Γρ ≃ 149.1 MeV: ρ → ππ decay width (ParticleDataGroup:2022pth)
- gρππ ≃ 6.01: ρππ effective coupling constant
- Ciso = 1/√2: isospin factor for ρ⁺ → π⁺π⁰
- xB = 0.25, Q² = 2.0 GeV², y = 0.8, Φ = π/2: typical kinematic conditions of the JLab@12GeV facility used for cross section estimations
- -tmin ≃ 0.2 GeV²: minimum momentum transfer value at which the total cross section is dominated by longitudinally polarized ρ(770)
- x = ξ ≃ 0.15, t = tmin: kinematic point on the crossover line used for the Omnès-based partial-wave GPD model

## Context

This work extends the framework of transition GPDs for N → πN (Polyakov 1998, Polyakov 2006) to the spinless hadron case of π → ππ, providing a simplified example for developing the formalism. The dispersive analysis follows the approach formulated in Polyakov (1998), Lehmann-Dronke (1999), and Lehmann-Dronke (2000) for applying dispersive methods to GPDs, and uses the Watson-Migdal final-state interaction theorem (Watson 1954) and the Muskhelishvili-Omnès type dispersion relation (Omnès 1958). The x- and ξ-dependencies of the GPD model are constructed using the Radyushkin double distribution Ansätz (Radyushkin 1998). This formalism enables future generalization to N → πN transition GPDs across the πN resonance region for studying baryon resonances through non-diagonal hard exclusive reactions, as reviewed in a recent white paper (Diehl 2024).
