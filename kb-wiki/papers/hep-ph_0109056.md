# Meson-meson scattering within one loop Chiral Perturbation Theory and its unitarization

> We present the complete one-loop calculation of all the two meson scattering amplitudes within the framework of SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory, which includes pions, kaons and the eta. In addition, we have unitarized these amplitudes with the coupled channel Inverse Amplitude Method, which ensures simultaneously the good low energy properties of Chiral Perturbation Theory and unitarity. We show how this method provides a remarkable description of meson-meson scattering data up to 1.2 GeV including the scattering lengths and the generation of seven light resonances, which is consistent with previous determination of the chiral parameters. Particular attention is paid to discuss the differences and similarities of this work with previous analysis in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper presents the complete one-loop calculation of all two-meson scattering amplitudes in SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory, including three amplitudes that had never appeared published before (Kη→Kη, ηη→ηη, and Kπ→Kη), and unitarizes them with the coupled channel Inverse Amplitude Method to describe meson-meson scattering data up to 1.2 GeV, though the authors note that the η is treated only as the pseudoscalar octet component with η-η' mixing effects absorbed into the L7 parameter, which they acknowledge is "somewhat crude" but sufficient given the present status of experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculated all eight independent meson-meson scattering amplitudes at O(p⁴) in SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory using dimensional regularization and the MS-1 scheme, expressing all results in terms of a single pion decay constant fπ and ensuring exact perturbative unitarity. They then applied the coupled channel Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) using the formula T ≃ T₂(T₂ − T₄)⁻¹ T₂ to unitarize the amplitudes, using the highest possible dimensionality of the I,J channel for all energies rather than switching dimensionality at each threshold.

## Key numbers

- The description covers meson-meson scattering data up to 1.2 GeV, including the generation of seven light resonances
- The IAM reproduces scattering data including scattering lengths and phase shifts, with unitarity violations of "the order of a few percent only" for the ππ-KK̅ channels
- The tree level masses satisfy the Gell-Mann-Okubo relation: 4M₀K² − M₀π² − 3M₀η² = 0
- The chiral expansion is typically limited to energies below 500 MeV without unitarization

## Context

This work extends previous IAM applications by Oller and collaborators (1998-2000) that had only been applied rigorously to ππ and KK̅ final states, and by J.A. Oller (2001) who calculated the full O(p⁴) K⁺K⁻→K⁺K⁻ and K⁺K⁻→K⁰K̅⁰ amplitudes. The new complete calculation allows direct comparison with standard ChPT low energy constants in dimensional regularization and the MS-1 scheme, which was not possible with earlier cutoff-regularized approaches. This enables future work to fit the chiral parameters Lᵢ to the full meson-meson scattering data set while maintaining consistency with standard ChPT determinations from low-energy data.
