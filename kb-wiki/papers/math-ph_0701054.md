# Nonlinear Classical Fields

## Key claim

The paper proposes that classical fields should be treated as a medium with a local four-velocity parameter, and that by regarding this four-velocity as a potential of an additional "w-field," the infinite self-energies of Coulomb and Yukawa fields can be eliminated. The claim is hedged by the statement that this is a "toy model" and that the quantitative results are only qualitative indications.

## Method

The author extends classical field theory by introducing a four-velocity field U(x) as an additional local parameter for any physical field, treating it as a potential of a new "w-field." Using Clifford algebra, a Lagrangian is constructed for the electromagnetic field interacting with its w-field shadow, discarding self-interaction terms in favor of interaction between the field and its shadow field. The resulting nonlinear Maxwell equations are solved for spherically symmetric electrostatic fields, with coherence conditions used to close the system of differential equations.

## Key numbers

- For the nonlinear Coulomb field with the w-field in vacuum state, the scalar velocity parameter is u₀ = -1 for a system with positive electric charge, with s₀ = -2 and q = -1.
- The electric charges of coherent states for even N are: q₀ = 1, q₂ = 1, q₄ = 9, q₆ = 225.
- With interaction constant g = 0.99122686 and electron mass mₑ = 0.511 MeV, the calculated lepton masses are: muon = 101.48 MeV (observed 105.658 MeV), tau = 1712.7 MeV (observed 1777 MeV), and a predicted fourth lepton at 56970.5 MeV.
- For the nonlinear Coulomb field of a light nucleus with radius b, the electrostatic tension is E = eZ/R for R > b, and E = d/[R(R+b)] for R < b, where d is the polarization of internal nucleus medium.

## Context

This work extends the classical field theory tradition originating with Mie (1912), who considered the electron as a state of the electromagnetic field to eliminate infinite self-energy, and contrasts with the Born-Infeld model which violated analyticity. The paper aims to address the electron-muon mass difference puzzle, referencing Tannenbaum (2016) who noted this problem remains unsolved, and suggests that the nonlinear Coulomb field model can explain lepton mass hierarchies while also predicting a fourth lepton. The approach enables a classical framework for finite self-energies and charge quantization without requiring quantum field theory.
