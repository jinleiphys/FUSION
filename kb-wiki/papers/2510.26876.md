# Trapping-potential dependence of the unitary Fermi gas at the BCS-BEC crossover

> Cold-atom experiments which measure Fermi-gas properties near unitarity confine fermionic atoms to a region of space using trapping potentials of various shapes. The presence of a trapping potential introduces a new characteristic physical scale in the superfluid EFT which, inter alia, describes the acoustic branch of excitations in the far infrared well below the scale of the superfluid gap. In this EFT there is a clear hierarchy of scales, and corrections to the homogeneous system due to the trapping potential may be organized into three regions with distinct power counting that relies on both the EFT derivative expansion, and the WKB approximation, which is an expansion in gradients of the trapping potential. The energy spectrum of the superfluid system is obtained in each of the regions by explicit computation of the phonon-field fluctuations, and by the modifications to the dynamic structure factor due to the corresponding density fluctuations. The most significant deviations from linear dispersion due to the trapping potential are found in the far infrared region of the superfluid EFT.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic effective field theory (EFT) framework to compute how a trapping potential modifies the phonon dispersion relation and dynamic structure factor of the unitary Fermi gas at zero temperature, finding that the most significant deviations from linear dispersion occur in the far infrared region of the superfluid EFT, with the sign and magnitude of these corrections depending on the trap shape and the energy regime of the fluctuations.

## Method

The authors construct a superfluid EFT for the Goldstone phonon mode of the unitary Fermi gas, including the trapping potential \(V(r)\) as a spatially dependent shift of the chemical potential. They identify three energy scales, the trap scale \(\varpi\), the fluctuation energy \(q_0\), and the chemical potential \(\mu\), and organize corrections into a double expansion controlled by \(\eta = q_0/\mu\) (EFT derivative expansion) and \(\delta = \varpi/q_0\) (WKB gradient expansion of the potential). The phonon equation of motion is solved using a WKB ansatz for the radial wavefunction, and the dynamic structure factor is computed from the response function in each of the three distinct energy regimes.

## Key numbers

* Bertsch parameter from experiment: \(\xi = 0.376(4)\), giving \(c_0 = 0.168(3)\); from Monte Carlo simulations: \(\xi = 0.372(5)\), giving \(c_0 = 0.166(3)\)
* Fit to DMC simulation data over \(Q = 10-80\) yields \(c_1 = -0.011(1)\) and \(\xi = 0.416(7)\) (\(c_0 = 0.143(3)\))
* Mean-field values: \(\xi = 0.5906\) (\(c_0 = 0.0842\)), \(c_1 = -0.0153\), \(c_2 = -0.0035\)
* Epsilon expansion gives \(c_1 = -0.0209\), \(c_2 = \mathcal{O}(\epsilon^2)\)
* Experimental value of the dispersion curvature parameter: \(\gamma_{\scriptstyle EXP} = -0.085(8)\) (concave branch)
* Speed of sound at unitarity: \(c_s = \sqrt{2\mu/3} = \sqrt{\xi/3}\,k_F\)

## Context

This work extends the superfluid EFT approach of Son (2005) and Ma and collaborators (2009) by systematically incorporating the trapping potential that is always present in cold-atom experiments, going beyond the local density approximation (LDA) used by Ho and Zhou (2010). The analysis provides a framework for interpreting Bragg spectroscopy measurements such as those by PhysRevLett.128.100401 (2022), which found a negative \(\gamma\), and addresses concerns raised by Castin (2024, 2025) about finite-temperature effects and fitting biases in extracting dispersion parameters. The results enable quantitative separation of trap-induced effects from intrinsic homogeneous-system properties in future experiments across the BCS-BEC crossover.
