# Chiral Plasma under Strong Magnetic Fields: A Holographic Analysis of Transport Phenomena

> Chiral plasma appears in several areas of physics, historically starting from primordial plasma in the early Universe, then in quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions, and, more recently, in Dirac and Weyl semimetals. The major signature of the plasma is the non-conservation of the axial current due to the chiral anomaly and the emergence of new, anomaly-induced transport phenomena. In this paper, we study the plasma exposed to arbitrarily strong constant magnetic and weak electric fields. Employing all-order gradient resummation, we write down constitutive relations for electric and axial currents parameterized by thirteen momentum- and magnetic- field-dependent transport coefficient functions. The latter are computed utilizing a theoretical lab for a realistic plasma, namely a holographic $U(1)_V \times U(1)_A$ Maxwell--Chern--Simons theory in Schwarzschild--AdS$_5$, in the probe limit. As an application, we revisit the phenomena of negative magnetoresistance and chiral magnetic waves, beyond the naive hydrodynamic limit.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous holographic studies of dissipationless chiral magnetic waves (CMW) by incorporating a dynamical electric field (scaled as order-epsilon) alongside a strong constant magnetic field (order-one), finding that underdamped collective excitations can still exist at finite momenta beyond the hydrodynamic limit, contrary to the expectation that Ohmic conductivity would overdamp the mode entirely.

## Method

The authors employ a holographic \(U(1)_V \times U(1)_A\) Maxwell–Chern–Simons theory in a Schwarzschild–AdS\(_5\) background in the probe limit, with a constant magnetic field of arbitrary strength and a weak electric field. Using all-order gradient resummation, they promote transport coefficients to frequency-, momentum-, and magnetic-field-dependent transport coefficient functions (TCFs), and derive the most general linearized constitutive relations for the vector and axial currents, parameterized by thirteen distinct TCFs. The TCFs are computed from the holographic model, and the dispersion relation for the CMW is obtained by substituting the constitutive relations into the continuity equations and imposing Gauss's law for the dynamical electric field.

## Key numbers

* Thirteen distinct magnetic-field-dependent transport coefficient functions (TCFs) parameterize the constitutive relations for vector and axial currents (Eqs. 11 and 12).
* The dispersion relation for the CMW with dynamical electric field is given by \(\omega_\pm = -\frac{1}{2} i\sigma_e^0 - iD^0 q^2 \pm (\frac{i}{2}\sigma_e^0)\sqrt{1 - \frac{48\kappa^2 B^2 \sigma_\chi^0}{(\sigma_e^0)^2}(1 + \frac{q^2 \sigma_\chi^0}{12})}\) in the hydrodynamic limit (Eq. 17).
* The modified dispersion relation beyond the hydrodynamic limit is \(\omega_\pm = \pm ({\sigma}_{\chi} - q^{2} D_{\chi})\kappa\, \vec{B}\!\cdot\!\vec{q} - i \left( q^{2} D - D_{B} (\kappa\, \vec{B}\!\cdot\!\vec{q})^{2} \right)\) (Eq. 18), where all TCFs depend on \(\omega\), \(q^2\), \(B^2\), and \((\vec{q}\cdot\vec{B})^2\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic analysis of chiral magnetic waves by Lublinsky and collaborators (P5, P6), who found dissipationless CMW modes at strong magnetic fields in the absence of electric fields, and by SHOV, who included a dynamical electric field but only in the hydrodynamic limit with weak magnetic fields. The present paper bridges these lines of work by incorporating both strong magnetic fields and dynamical electric fields beyond the hydrodynamic limit, using the same holographic model as P5 and P6. It enables future studies of whether long-lived propagating CMW modes survive in realistic chiral plasmas where electric fields cannot be neglected, and complements recent holographic work on underdamped CMW in Weyl semimetal models by Ahn (2024).
