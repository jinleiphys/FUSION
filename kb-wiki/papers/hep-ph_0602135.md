# The medium in heavy-ion collisions

> The nuclear index of refraction, the density of partons, their free path length and energy loss in the matter created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC are estimated within the suggestion that the emission of Cherenkov gluons is responsible for the observed two-bump structure of the angular distribution of hadrons belonging to the companion (away-side) jet.

# The medium in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The two-bump structure observed in the angular distribution of away-side hadrons in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by Cherenkov gluon emission, provided that the nuclear index of refraction exceeds unity, which occurs only for gluon energies within the upper wings of hadronic resonances such as the rho meson.

## Method
The author estimates the nuclear index of refraction using the forward scattering amplitude for gluons, relating it to the density of partonic scatterers via a Breit-Wigner resonance formalism. The observed angular separation of the two peaks in the away-side hadron distribution is used to extract the Cherenkov angle and thus the average index of refraction. The height of the peaks above the minimum is used to estimate the width of the Cherenkov ring, and the standard formula for energy loss by Cherenkov radiation is applied.

## Key numbers
- Cherenkov angle: theta_c approximately 70 degrees, giving average index of refraction <n> approximately 3.
- Lower bound on effective number of partons within a nucleon volume: nu > 40 from rho-meson parameters alone; about 20 when all meson resonances are included.
- Average number of partons from Breit-Wigner weighted integration: <nu> = 60 for rho-meson alone; approximately 30 when all mesons are included.
- Width of the Cherenkov ring: delta approximately 0.1 radians.
- Lower bound on parton free path length: R_f > 20/E_R approximately 4.5/mu approximately 7 x 10^-13 cm.
- Energy loss: dE/dx approximately 1 GeV/fm.

## Context
This work extends the Cherenkov gluon mechanism proposed by Dremin and others (2000-2005) to interpret RHIC data from Wang (2005) showing a two-bump structure in away-side hadron distributions that is absent in pp collisions. The parton density estimates are consistent with those obtained from hydrodynamical analysis of collective flow and from jet quenching analysis by Heinz (2003), Wang (2004), Gyulassy (2004), and others. The results support the hypothesis of a dense partonic state in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that the ring regions should be enriched by pions from rho-meson decays, with an average mass somewhat above 840 MeV.
