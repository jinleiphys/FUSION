# Regularization Methods for Nucleon-Nucleon Effective Field Theory

> Attempts to apply effective field theory (EFT) methods to nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering have raised questions about the nature and limitations of an EFT expansion when used nonperturbatively. We discuss the characteristics of a meaningful EFT analysis and compare them with traditional approaches to NN scattering. A key feature of an EFT treatment is a systematic expansion in powers of momentum, which we demonstrate using an error analysis introduced by Lepage. A clear graphical determination of the radius of convergence for the momentum expansion is also obtained. We use these techniques to compare cutoff regularization, two forms of dimensional regularization, and the dibaryon approach, using a simple model for illustration. The naturalness of the parameters and predictions for bound-state energies are also shown.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the success of an effective field theory (EFT) expansion for nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon scattering depends critically on the regularization scheme used, with dimensional regularization using modified minimal subtraction (DR[MS]) failing for large scattering lengths unless a dibaryon degree of freedom is introduced, while cutoff regularization and dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (DR[PDS]) maintain proper convergence regardless of scattering length.

## Method

The authors used a delta-shell potential as a model for the underlying short-range physics, with parameters chosen to simulate both small and large scattering lengths (including the near-bound-state case relevant to NN scattering). They compared four regularization schemes, cutoff regularization with Gaussian weighting (CR[G]), dimensional regularization with modified minimal subtraction (DR[MS]), dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (DR[PDS]), and the dibaryon approach, by fitting effective potential coefficients order-by-order and plotting the error in p cot δ(p) on log-log scales following Lepage's error analysis method. The coefficients were determined by minimizing the difference between the effective theory and exact results for p cot δ(p) using a weighted polynomial fit.

## Key numbers

- For the delta-shell potential with g = -10 (small scattering length), DR[MS] shows a radius of convergence at p/Λ ∼ 1, while for g = 1.01 (large scattering length with bound state) the radius of convergence drops to p/Λ ∼ 0.1
- The scattering length for the delta-shell potential is a_s = g/(g-1) × 1/Λ, giving a_s = 100/Λ for g = 0.99 (modeling the 1S0 channel) and a_s = 1/Λ for g = -10
- The effective range for the delta-shell potential is r_e = 2(g+1)/(3g) × 1/Λ
- For DR[MS] with g = 1.01, the fitted coefficients become unnaturally large: c = 84.2, d = -5.64 × 10^3, e = 3.78 × 10^5 at third order
- For CR[G] with g = 1.01, the fitted coefficients remain natural: c = -1.42, d = 0.842, e = -0.181 at third order (with Λa = 1)
- For DR[PDS] with g = 1.01 and μ = Λ, the fitted coefficients are also natural: c = -0.842, d = -0.564, e = -0.152 at third order

## Context

This work extends Lepage's (1997) error analysis method for EFT to higher orders and applies it to compare regularization schemes, addressing disagreements in the literature between groups such as Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) who advocated DR[PDS], and those who found problems with DR[MS] for large scattering lengths. The paper shows that cutoff regularization and the dibaryon approach of Kaplan (1997) both produce proper EFT behavior with natural coefficients and clear radius of convergence, while naive DR[MS] fails for large scattering lengths. This enables systematic EFT treatments of NN scattering that can incorporate pions as long-range physics in future work, as the authors note they will address in a subsequent publication.
