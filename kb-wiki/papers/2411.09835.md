# Proton removal from $^{73,75}$Br to $^{72,74}$Se at intermediate energies

> We report new experimental data for excited states of $^{72,74}$Se obtained from proton removal from $^{73,75}$Br secondary beams on a proton target. The experiments were performed with the Ursinus-NSCL Liquid Hydrogen Target and the combined GRETINA+S800 setup at the Coupled Cyclotron Facility of the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory at Michigan State University. Within uncertainties, the inclusive cross sections for proton removal from $^{73,75}$Br on a proton target are identical suggesting that the same single-particle orbitals contribute to the proton-removal reaction. In addition, details of the partial cross section fragmentation are discussed. The data might suggest that $l = 1, 2, 3$, and 4 angular momentum transfers are important to understand the population of excited states of $^{72,74}$Se in proton removal. Available data for excited states of $^{74}$Ge populated through the $^{75}$As$(d,{}^{3}{\mathrm{He}}){}^{74}$Ge proton-removal reaction in normal kinematics suggest indeed that the $fp$ and $sd$ shell as well as the $1g_{9/2}$ orbital contribute. A comparison to data available for odd-$A$ nuclei supports that the bulk of the spectroscopic strengths could be found at lower energies in the even-even Se isotopes than in, for instance, the even-even Ge isotopes. In addition, the population of high-$J$ states seems to indicate that multi-step processes contribute to proton-removal reactions at intermediate energies in these collective nuclei.

## Key claim

Within uncertainties, the inclusive cross sections for proton removal from 73Br and 75Br on a proton target are identical, suggesting that the same single-particle orbitals contribute to the reaction; however, the data only "might suggest" that l = 1, 2, 3, and 4 angular momentum transfers are important, and the population of high-J states "seems to indicate" that multi-step processes contribute in these collective nuclei.

## Method

The experiment used secondary beams of 73Br (36% purity, ~2160 pps) and 75Br (5% purity, ~300 pps) produced by projectile fragmentation of a 150 MeV/u 78Kr primary beam on a 9Be target, selected by the A1900 fragment separator. The beams impinged on the Ursinus-NSCL Liquid Hydrogen Target at the S800 spectrograph target position, with mid-target beam energies of ~95 MeV/u for 73Br and ~89 MeV/u for 75Br. Gamma rays from the reaction residues 72,74Se were detected by the GRETINA array, and partial cross sections were extracted by fitting simulated gamma-ray spectra to the measured ones using known decay branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section for 73Br(p,2p)72Se: 68(4) mb
- Inclusive cross section for 75Br(p,2p)74Se: 66(4) mb
- Partial cross section for the 2+1 state of 72Se at 862 keV: 8.9(8) mb
- Partial cross section for the 2+1 state of 74Se at 635 keV: 9(3) mb
- Partial cross section for the 6+1 state of 72Se at 2467 keV: 5.8(5) mb
- Partial cross section for the 6+1 state of 74Se at 2231 keV: 8.3(9) mb
- Partial cross section for the 8+1 state of 72Se at 3425 keV: 1.8(2) mb
- Summed partial cross section for resolved excited states of 72Se: 51(7) mb
- Summed partial cross section for resolved excited states of 74Se: 48(13) mb
- Mid-target beam energy for 75Br: ~89 MeV/u; for 73Br: ~95 MeV/u
- Areal target density of liquid hydrogen: 69(3) mg/cm2

## Context

This work extends the study of proton-removal reactions on neutron-deficient Se isotopes, adding data for 72,74Se (N=38,40) to the previously published proton-knockout data for 70Se (N=36) from Wimmer et al. (2018) obtained on a 9Be target at RIKEN. The results are compared to available (d,3He) data for stable nuclei in this mass region, specifically the 75As(d,3He)74Ge reaction in normal kinematics, which suggests that fp and sd shell orbitals as well as the 1g9/2 orbital contribute. The data enable future comparisons with shell-model calculations using interactions such as JUN45, GXPF1A, and spdf UMIX, and may motivate theoretical studies of multi-step processes in nucleon-removal reactions on collective nuclei.
