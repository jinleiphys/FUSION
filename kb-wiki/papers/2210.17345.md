# Inclusive production of $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, and $Υ$ states in pNRQCD

> Under some assumptions on the hierarchy of relevant energy scales, we compute the nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) for inclusive production of $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, and $Υ$ states based on the potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) effective field theory. Based on the pNRQCD formalism, we obtain expressions for the LDMEs in terms of the quarkonium wavefunctions at the origin and universal gluonic correlators, which do not depend on the heavy quark flavor or the radial excitation. This greatly reduces the number of nonperturbative unknowns and substantially enhances the predictive power of the nonrelativistic effective field theory formalism. We obtain improved determinations of the LDMEs for $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, and $Υ$ states thanks to the universality of the gluonic correlators, and obtain phenomenological results for cross sections and polarizations at large transverse momentum that agree well with measurements at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper computes NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) for inclusive production of $J/\psi$, $\psi(2S)$, and $\Upsilon$ states using the pNRQCD effective field theory, under the assumption that the hierarchy of relevant energy scales allows the pNRQCD formalism to be applied to non-Coulombic, strongly coupled quarkonia including charmonium and excited bottomonium states.

## Method

The authors use quantum-mechanical perturbation theory on the NRQCD Hamiltonian expanded in powers of $1/m$ to express color-singlet and color-octet LDMEs in terms of quarkonium wavefunctions at the origin and universal gluonic correlators. They compute the contact terms for the $^3S_1^{[1]}$, $^3P_J^{[8]}$, $^1S_0^{[8]}$, and $^3S_1^{[8]}$ LDMEs by expanding the eigenstates of the NRQCD Hamiltonian and integrating out heavy quark fields using Wick contractions, with the gluonic correlators expressed as vacuum expectation values of chromoelectric field operators connected by Wilson lines. The resulting expressions are then used to obtain improved phenomenological determinations of the LDMEs and predictions for cross sections and polarizations at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- $\langle {\cal O}^V(^3S_1^{[1]}) \rangle = 3 \times 2 N_c |\phi_V^{(0)} (\bm{0})|^2$, where $N_c = 3$ is the number of colors and $\phi_V^{(0)} (\bm{0})$ is the quarkonium wavefunction at the origin at leading order in $v$
- $\langle {\cal O}^V(^3P_J^{[8]}) \rangle = 3 \times \frac{2 J+1}{18 N_c} \, {\cal E}_{00} \, |\phi_V^{(0)} (\bm{0})|^2$, where ${\cal E}_{00}$ is a universal gluonic correlator defined in terms of chromoelectric fields
- The color-singlet LDME result reproduces the known vacuum-saturation approximation result from Bodwin (1994)
- The pNRQCD expressions are valid up to corrections of relative order $1/N_c^2$ and relative order $v^2$ for the heavy-quark spin symmetry relations

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD approach previously applied to $\chi_c$ and $\chi_b$ production in Brambilla (2020) and Brambilla (2021) to $S$-wave heavy quarkonia including $J/\psi$, $\psi(2S)$, and excited $\Upsilon$ states. The pNRQCD formalism reduces the number of nonperturbative unknowns compared to the standard four-LDME phenomenology used in NRQCD since Bodwin (1994), which has led to inconsistent LDME determinations and unsatisfactory descriptions of quarkonium production. The universality of the gluonic correlators across different heavy quark flavors and radial excitations enables improved phenomenological determinations and predictions for cross sections and polarizations at the LHC, as well as for $J/\psi$ photoproduction, $\eta_c$ production, associated production of $J/\psi + Z$ and $J/\psi + W$, and $J/\psi$ production at the Electron-Ion Collider.
