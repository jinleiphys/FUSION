# Renormalization group analysis of nuclear force

> We study a relation between nuclear forces based on phenomenological approach (V_ph) and nuclear effective field theory (V_EFT) from a viewpoint of renormalization group. We find the relation between these two types of nuclear force using Wilsonian renormalization group equation. Considering the fact that V_EFT is defined in a certain small model space, we show that a simple contact interaction accurately simulates short-distance physics, and that V_EFT is free from dependence on modelling the details of the short-distance physics. Based on our result, we discuss some features of nuclear effective field theory.

## Key claim
The paper shows that phenomenological nuclear forces (V_ph) and nuclear effective field theory (V_EFT) are related through a Wilsonian renormalization group evolution, but only when the model space is reduced to a sufficiently small cutoff (Λ = 200 MeV). The relation holds under the condition that the short-distance physics is accurately simulated by simple contact interactions, and that the two-pion exchange potential plays essentially no role in the reduced model space.

## Method
The author starts with several high-precision phenomenological NN potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Reid93) and reduces their model space by integrating out high-momentum states using the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation for the two-nucleon system in the center-of-mass frame. The resulting low-momentum effective interaction V_M is then simulated by a nuclear effective field theory potential consisting of the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) plus a few contact interactions with zero, two, and four derivatives. The accuracy of the simulation is checked for both diagonal and off-diagonal partial-wave matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- Model-space reduction is performed down to a cutoff Λ = 200 MeV, at which the model dependence among phenomenological potentials almost disappears.
- The WRG equation is solved for partial waves including the ¹S₀ channel and the ³S₁–³D₁ coupled channel.
- The relation is expected to hold safely up to Λ ∼ 400 MeV if the two-pion exchange potential and higher-order OPEP are properly included in V_EFT.
- For the ³D₁–³S₁ off-diagonal matrix element at Λ = 200 MeV, simulation using the OPEP plus one contact interaction (dashed curve) and plus two contact interactions (dotted curve) is shown to be very accurate.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Birse et al. (2000s) who first derived the Wilsonian renormalization group equation for nuclear forces in a different manner. It directly addresses the questions raised about nuclear effective field theory: whether contact interactions appropriately describe short-distance physics, whether V_EFT is just another phase-equivalent potential, and whether there is a relation between model-dependent phenomenological potentials and model-independent effective field theory. The result enables a quantitative understanding of why nuclear effective field theory is model independent and suggests that similar RG relations likely exist for other nuclear operators such as electroweak currents and pion production operators.
