# Covariant meson-baryon scattering with chiral and large N_c constraints

> We give a review of recent progress on the application of the relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian to meson-baryon scattering. It is shown that a combined chiral and 1/N_c expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter interaction kernel leads to a good description of the kaon-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering data typically up to laboratory momenta of p_lab about 500 MeV. We solve the covariant coupled channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with the interaction kernel truncated to chiral order Q^3 where we include only those terms which are leading in the large N_c limit of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a combined chiral and large \(N_c\) expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter interaction kernel, truncated to chiral order \(Q^3\) with only terms leading in the large \(N_c\) limit, provides a good description of kaon-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon, and pion-nucleon scattering data up to laboratory momenta of about 500 MeV, but the authors note that this is a review of recent progress and the precise value of the decay constant \(f\) is subject to large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors solve the covariant coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson-baryon scattering, using an interaction kernel derived from the relativistic chiral \(SU(3)\) Lagrangian truncated to chiral order \(Q^3\). They include only those terms that are leading in the large \(N_c\) limit of QCD, which suppresses loop corrections to the kernel and reduces the number of free parameters. The baryon decuplet field with \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^+\) is included as an essential ingredient because it is part of the baryon ground state multiplet in the large \(N_c\) limit.

## Key numbers

* The description of scattering data is valid up to laboratory momenta \(p_{\rm lab} \simeq 500\) MeV for kaon-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon, and pion-nucleon systems.
* The pion mass is \(m_\pi \simeq 140\) MeV and the nucleon mass is \(m_N \simeq 940\) MeV, with up and down quark masses \(m_{u,d} \simeq 10\) MeV.
* The empirical decay constants are \(f_\pi = 92.42 \pm 0.33\) MeV and \(f_K \simeq 113.0 \pm 1.3\) MeV, giving a naive estimate \(f \simeq 104\) MeV.
* The parameters for the baryon octet axial-vector couplings are \(F_{[8]} \simeq 0.45\) and \(D_{[8]} \simeq 0.80\), with \(C_{[10]} \simeq 1.6\) for the decuplet.
* The baryon mass splitting parameters are estimated as \(b_D \simeq 0.06\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(b_F \simeq -0.21\) GeV\(^{-1}\), and \(d_D \simeq -0.49\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The typical expansion parameter in the \(\pi N\) sector is \(m_\pi^2/(8\pi f^2) \sim 0.1\), while in the \(\bar K N\) sector it is \(m_K^2/(8\pi f^2) \sim 1\).

## Context

This work extends the successful chiral \(SU(2)\) perturbation theory approach to pion-nucleon scattering (Gasser, Bernard, Meissner) to the \(SU(3)\) flavor group, where standard perturbative chiral perturbation theory fails dramatically for the Weinberg-Tomozawa theorem prediction of the \(K^-\)-proton scattering length. The approach builds on earlier work by Weinberg (1990) and Lutz (1996) showing that reducible diagrams require resummation, and follows the strategy of Kaiser, Ramos, and Oller-Meissner in applying coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations with chiral constraints. The inclusion of large \(N_c\) constraints from QCD (Hooft, Witten, Dashen, Jenkins, Carone, Luty) provides a systematic parameter reduction, enabling a simultaneous description of pion-nucleon, kaon-nucleon, and antikaon-nucleon scattering data.
