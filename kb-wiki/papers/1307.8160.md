# Three-cluster dynamics within an ab initio framework

> We introduce a fully antisymmetrized treatment of three-cluster dynamics within the ab initio framework of the no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM). Energy-independent non-local interactions among the three nuclear fragments are obtained from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and consistent ab initio many-body wave functions of the clusters. The three-cluster Schrödinger equation is solved with bound-state boundary conditions by means of the hyperspherical-harmonic method on a Lagrange mesh. We discuss the formalism in detail and give algebraic expressions for systems of two single nucleons plus a nucleus. Using a soft similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon potential, we apply the method to an $^4$He+$n+n$ description of $^6$He and compare the results to experiment and to a six-body diagonalization of the Hamiltonian performed within the harmonic-oscillator expansions of the NCSM. Differences between the two calculations provide a measure of core ($^4$He) polarization effects.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a fully antisymmetrized treatment of three-cluster dynamics within the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) framework, but the first application to the ground state of ⁶He using a ⁴He+n+n description includes only the ground state of the ⁴He cluster and omits three-nucleon forces, meaning the results provide a measure of core polarization effects rather than a complete ab initio description.

## Method

The authors extend the NCSM/RGM formalism to treat three-cluster dynamics by deriving energy-independent non-local interactions among three nuclear fragments from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and consistent ab initio many-body wave functions of the clusters. The three-cluster Schrödinger equation is solved with bound-state boundary conditions using the hyperspherical-harmonic method on a Lagrange mesh, with the relative motion wave functions expanded in hyperspherical harmonics and the internal region treated via the R-matrix method. Algebraic expressions are given specifically for systems of two single nucleons plus a nucleus (the A-2,1,1 mass partition), and the method is applied to a ⁴He+n+n description of ⁶He using a similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of ⁶He is investigated using a ⁴He(g.s.)+n+n NCSM/RGM calculation based on a SRG-evolved chiral N³LO NN potential
- The hypermomentum quantum number K is defined as K = 2n + ℓ_x + ℓ_y with n = 0,1,2,...
- The relative kinetic energy operator for the three-cluster system includes a term with eigenvalue K(K+4) for the grand-angular kinetic operator
- The external region solution for bound states of neutral systems uses modified Bessel functions of the second kind K_{K+2}(kρ) with k² = -2mE/ħ²

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM binary-cluster formalism (previously applied to nucleon-nucleus scattering, deuteron scattering, and reactions such as ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B and ³H(d,n)⁴He) to three-cluster dynamics, enabling ab initio treatment of Borromean nuclei like ⁶He and reactions with three-body final states such as ³H(³H,2n)⁴He. Prior microscopic three-cluster models (Filippov 1996, Korennov 2004) used central effective forces with state-dependent parameters and simplified s-shell cluster wave functions, whereas this work employs realistic NN potentials and ab initio cluster wave functions. The inclusion of three-nucleon forces and embedding within the NCSMC framework (Quaglioni 2013) are identified as necessary next steps to account for core polarization effects and excited states of the ⁴He cluster.
