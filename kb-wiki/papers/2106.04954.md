# Light mesons with one dynamical gluon on the light front

> We obtain the light meson mass spectroscopy from the light-front quantum chromodynamics (QCD) Hamiltonian, determined for their constituent quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock components, together with a three-dimensional confinement. The eigenvectors of the light-front effective Hamiltonian provide a good quality description of the pion electromagnetic form factor, decay constant, and the valence quark distribution functions following QCD scale evolution. We also show that the pion's gluon densities can be probed through the pion-nucleus induced $J/ψ$ production data. Our pion parton distribution functions provide excellent agreement with $J/ψ$ production data from widely different experimental conditions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including a dynamical gluon Fock component (quark-antiquark-gluon) at the initial scale, together with a three-dimensional confinement, yields a good description of light meson mass spectroscopy and pion properties, but the agreement with experimental data is strongest at low momentum transfer (Q²) and for states included in the fit, while deviations appear for states not used in the fit (e.g., b₁, a₂, ρ′).

## Method
The authors solve the light-front QCD Hamiltonian in Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) for mesons described by quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock components, with a three-dimensional confining potential in the leading Fock sector. They diagonalize the Hamiltonian matrix in a truncated basis defined by longitudinal momentum cutoff K and transverse basis truncation Nmax, fit model parameters (quark mass, gluon mass, confinement strength, vertex mass, coupling constant) to known masses of π, ρ, a₀, a₁, π′, and π₁, then use the resulting light-front wave functions to compute the pion electromagnetic form factor, decay constant, and parton distribution functions evolved via NNLO DGLAP equations.

## Key numbers
- Model parameters: m_q = 0.39 GeV, m_g = 0.60 GeV, κ = 0.65 GeV, m_f = 5.69 GeV, g_s = 1.92; basis truncation {Nmax, K} = {14, 15}; HO scale parameter b = 0.29 GeV
- Pion decay constant from |q\bar{q}⟩ Fock sector: f_π^Th. = 138 MeV, compared to experimental f_π^Exp. = 130.0 ± 0.2 MeV
- Initial scale for PDF evolution: μ₀² = 0.34 ± 0.03 GeV², determined by requiring evolved valence quark momentum fraction ⟨x⟩_valence = 0.48 ± 0.01 at μ² = 5 GeV²
- Pion valence PDF falls off as (1−x)^{1.77} at high x
- Gluons carry {39.5, 42.1, 43.9, 44.6, 45.1}% of pion momentum at {1.69, 4, 10, 16, 27} GeV², respectively
- For J/ψ production calculations: charm quark mass m_c = 1.5 GeV, factorization scale μ_F = 2m_c, renormalization scale μ_R = m_c

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ framework previously applied to mesons with only valence Fock components (Li et al., 2015; Lan et al., 2019) by including a dynamical gluon in the initial state, which produces a larger gluon distribution at large x (>0.2) after QCD evolution compared to the BLFQ-NJL model (Lan et al., 2019) and global fits such as JAM (Barry et al., 2018) and xFitter (Novikov et al., 2020). The results enable studies of pion-induced J/ψ production within the color evaporation model, confirming sensitivity to the pion's gluon density as noted by Chang et al. (2020), and can be extended to generalized parton distributions, transverse momentum dependent parton distributions, and higher Fock sectors.
