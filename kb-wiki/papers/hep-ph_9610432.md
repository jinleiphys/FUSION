# Transverse Momentum of Psi and Dimuon Production in Pb+Pb Collisions

> CERN collaboration NA50 has measured charmonium and Drell-Yan dimuon production in Pb+Pb collisions. Parton scattering broadens the transverse momentum, p_T, distributions for these processes. We predict that the average p_T^{2} will flatten in Pb+Pb collisions as a function of the neutral transverse energy of hadrons, E_T, in contrast to the almost-linear rise seen in S+U - psi +X. If seen, such a flattening will support hadronic explanations of charmonium suppression. Figures 2 and 5 are improved and numerous typographical errors are corrected.

# Transverse Momentum of Psi and Dimuon Production in Pb+Pb Collisions

## Key claim

The authors predict that the average transverse momentum squared of charmonium and Drell-Yan dimuons will flatten in Pb+Pb collisions as a function of neutral transverse energy, in contrast to the almost-linear rise seen in S+U collisions. If observed, this flattening would support hadronic (comover) explanations of charmonium suppression rather than quark-gluon plasma formation, but the authors acknowledge that the plasma explanation cannot be excluded and that the flattening is a geometric consequence of the symmetric Pb+Pb system.

## Method

The authors model initial-state parton scattering as a random walk in momentum space, where each nucleon-nucleon subcollision contributes a fixed increment lambda-squared to the transverse momentum squared of the produced dimuon or psi. They compute the average number of subcollisions as a function of impact parameter using realistic Woods-Saxon nuclear densities from deJager, deVries and deVries (JVV), then fold these with a phenomenological probability distribution for neutral transverse energy production to obtain the E_T dependence of the path length L and the average p_T-squared.

## Key numbers

- Lambda for Drell-Yan dimuons: lambda_mu+mu- = 0.18 +/- 0.01 GeV, chosen to agree with FNAL E772 data
- Lambda for psi production: lambda_psi = 0.36 +/- 0.03 GeV, chosen to agree with NA3 data
- Ratio (lambda_psi / lambda_mu+mu-)^2 ~ 3.9, approximately 70% larger than the perturbative estimate of 9/4
- For Pb+Pb collisions, the authors predict a 12.3% increase in <p_T^2> for psi as E_T increases from 50 to 150 GeV, compared to an 18.5% increase in S+U as E_T varies from 30 to 90 GeV
- With the NA50 sharp-surface approximation, the predicted increase is only 6.8% for psi and 2.7% for dimuons
- The elastic psi N cross section from photoproduction data is 0.079 +/- 0.012 mb, accounting for only 2-4% of the total cross section

## Context

This work extends the parton scattering model of Gavin and Gyulassy (1988) using updated data from the FNAL E772 experiment on pA collisions. The predictions are directly relevant to the NA50 collaboration's observation of psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions, which had been interpreted as evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation via a threshold effect. The authors argue that measurements of the transverse momentum of Drell-Yan dimuons can provide an experimental determination of the path length L, removing the model dependence in the NA50 comparison of psi suppression versus L. This approach builds on the suggestion by Gerschel and Hufner (1992) that measured <p_T^2> could replace the calculated L in such comparisons. The results enable experimental tests that can distinguish between hadronic comover models and plasma-based explanations of charmonium suppression.
