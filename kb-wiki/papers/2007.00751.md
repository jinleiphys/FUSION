# Next-to-leading order Balitsky-Kovchegov equation beyond large $N_\mathrm{c}$

> We calculate finite-$N_\mathrm{c}$ corrections to the next-to-leading order (NLO) Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation. We find analytical expressions for the necessary correlators of six Wilson lines in terms of the two-point function using the Gaussian approximation. In a suitable basis, the problem reduces from the diagonalization of a six-by-six matrix to the diagonalization of a three-by-three matrix, which can easily be done analytically. We study numerically the effects of these finite-$N_\mathrm{c}$ corrections on the NLO BK equation. In general, we find that the finite-$N_\mathrm{c}$ corrections are smaller than the expected $1/N_\mathrm{c}^2 \sim 10\%$. The corrections may be large for individual correlators, but have less of an influence on the shape of the amplitude as a function of the dipole size. They have an even smaller effect on the evolution speed as a function of rapidity.

## Key claim

The finite-\(N_\mathrm{c}\) corrections to the NLO BK equation are smaller than the naive expectation of \(1/N_\mathrm{c}^2 \sim 10\%\); although individual correlators can receive large corrections, the overall effect on the dipole amplitude shape and the evolution speed in rapidity is small.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for the six-Wilson-line correlators appearing in the NLO BK equation using the Gaussian approximation, which expresses all higher-point functions in terms of the two-point dipole correlator. By choosing an orthonormal basis that exploits the coordinate structure of the specific six-point functions needed, the problem reduced from diagonalizing a six-by-six matrix to diagonalizing a three-by-three matrix analytically. The resulting closed-form NLO BK equation was then solved numerically with running coupling and double transverse logarithm resummation, using an MV-model initial condition with \(\qso^2 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\Lambda_\mathrm{QCD} = 0.241\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Key numbers

* The finite-\(N_\mathrm{c}\) corrections are smaller than the expected \(1/N_\mathrm{c}^2 \sim 10\%\) for the overall evolution.
* The initial condition uses \(\qso^2 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\Lambda_\mathrm{QCD} = 0.241\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* The infrared coupling freezes at \(\alpha_\mathrm{s}(r\to\infty) = 0.762\).
* The running coupling uses parameters \(C^2 = 1\), \(\mu_0/\Lambda_\mathrm{QCD} = 2.5\), and \(c = 0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the NLO BK equation beyond the large-\(N_\mathrm{c}\) limit previously studied numerically by Lappi and Mäntysaari (2015, 2016), where the equation was solved in the large-\(N_\mathrm{c}\) approximation with resummations. The finite-\(N_\mathrm{c}\) corrections computed here are analogous to those studied at LO by Rummukainen and Weigert (2003) and Kovchegov and collaborators (2008), where they were also found to be smaller than naive expectations. The results enable more precise phenomenological applications of the CGC framework to future DIS experiments at the EIC, LHeC, and EicC, where NLO accuracy is required.
