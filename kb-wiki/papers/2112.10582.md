# Measurement of the α-particle monopole transition form factor challenges theory: a low-energy puzzle for nuclear forces?

> We perform a systematic study of the $α$-particle excitation from its ground state $0_1^+$ to the $0_2^+$ resonance. The so-called monopole transition form factor is investigated via an electron scattering experiment in a broad $Q^2$-range (from $0.5$ to $5.0$ fm$^{-2}$). The precision of the new data dramatically superseeds that of older sets of data, each covering only a portion of the $Q^2$-range. The new data allow the determination of two coefficients in a low-momentum expansion leading to a new puzzle. By confronting experiment to state-of-the-art theoretical calculations we observe that modern nuclear forces, including those derived within chiral effective field theory which are well tested on a variety of observables, fail to reproduce the excitation of the $α$-particle.

## Key claim

The new high-precision electron scattering data for the α-particle monopole transition form factor, covering a broad Q² range from 0.5 to 5.0 fm⁻², reveal that modern nuclear forces including those from chiral effective field theory fail to reproduce the excitation of the α-particle; this failure persists even at low momentum transfer where chiral effective field theory is expected to work well, and the discrepancy is not due to differences in few-body solvers but rather to the modeling of the nuclear Hamiltonian.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the Mainz Microtron MAMI using continuous-wave electron beams at energies of 450, 690, and 795 MeV scattered on a cryogenic helium-4 target. The two high-resolution magnetic spectrometers A and B detected electrons at forward scattering angles, covering the Q² range from 0.5 to 5.0 fm⁻², with the elastic peak and monopole resonance detected simultaneously. The monopole transition form factor was extracted relative to the simultaneously measured elastic peak of helium-4, with background contributions from the aluminum target cell, radiative tails, and the helium-4 continuum subtracted using dedicated simulations and reduced-density target runs.

## Key numbers

- The Q² range covered is from 0.5 to 5.0 fm⁻²
- The monopole resonance is centered at 20.21 MeV, with the proton-breakup threshold at 19.815 MeV
- The extracted intrinsic width Γ₀ of the monopole resonance ranges from 262 ± 47 keV to 288 ± 39 keV depending on the parameterization and background model
- The chiral effective field theory result is 100% too high at Q² = 1.5 fm⁻² compared to the new experimental data
- The systematic uncertainty from the background model choice is ±3.2%, from the resonance parameterization is −5.8%, and from the uncertainty in Γ₀ is ±4%
- The helium-4 elastic form factor uncertainty used for normalization is 0.5%

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by Walcher (1970), Kobschall (1983), and Frosch (1968), which each covered only portions of the Q² range with larger uncertainties, and provides the first systematic high-precision data across the full range. The results confirm and strengthen the tension with ab-initio calculations using chiral effective field theory potentials from Bacca (2013), while a simplified potential used by Hiyama (2004) shows agreement with the low-momentum data. This puzzle may resemble other unresolved low-energy puzzles in few-body systems such as the Ay puzzle and the space-star configuration puzzle, and further theoretical work including order-by-order chiral effective field theory calculations and variation of three-body force low-energy constants is needed to resolve it.
