# Quasiboson representations of sl(n+1) and generalized quantum statistics

> Generalized quantum statistics will be presented in the context of representation theory of Lie (super)algebras. This approach provides a natural mathematical framework, as is illustrated by the relation between para-Bose and para-Fermi operators and Lie (super)algebras of type B. Inspired by this relation, A-statistics is introduced, arising from representation theory of the Lie algebra A_n. The Fock representations for A_n=sl(n+1) provide microscopic descriptions of particular kinds of exclusion statistics, which may be called quasi-Bose statistics. It is indicated that A-statistics appears to be the natural statistics for certain lattice models in condensed matter physics.

## Key claim

The paper introduces "A-statistics" (also called quasi-Bose statistics) as a generalized quantum statistics arising from the representation theory of the Lie algebra sl(n+1), and claims that this statistics is a special case of Haldane exclusion statistics with the same parameter g=0 as ordinary Bose statistics, but with the additional condition that the total number of particles in any configuration cannot exceed a fixed integer p (the order of the statistics).

## Method

The authors define creation and annihilation operators (CAOs) as root vectors of the Lie algebra sl(n+1), specifically a_i^+ = e_{i,0} and a_i^- = e_{0,i} for i=1,...,n, and derive the triple commutation relations that these operators satisfy. They classify all irreducible Fock representations of this algebra, showing that each representation is uniquely characterized by a positive integer p (the order), and construct explicit orthonormal bases and action formulas for the CAOs on these Fock spaces. The quasi-Bose operators B(p)_i^± are then defined by scaling the CAOs by 1/√p, and their relation to ordinary Bose operators is established via a Holstein-Primakoff-type realization.

## Key numbers

- The Fock space W_p is finite-dimensional, with basis vectors (a_1^+)^{l_1} ... (a_n^+)^{l_n}|0⟩ that are nonzero if and only if l_1 + l_2 + ... + l_n ≤ p.
- The action of the CAOs on the orthonormal basis is given by: a_i^+|p;l_1,...,l_i,...,l_n⟩ = √[(l_i+1)(p - Σ_j l_j)] |p;l_1,...,l_i+1,...,l_n⟩.
- The action of the quasi-Bose operators is: B(p)_i^+|p;l_1,...,l_n⟩ = √[(l_i+1)(1 - (Σ_k l_k)/p)] |p;l_1,...,l_i+1,...,l_n⟩.
- In the limit p→∞, the quasi-Bose operators converge to ordinary Bose operators in a strong topology.
- The quasi-Bose operators of order p=1 are used in the bond operator representation of spin operators for a two-leg spin-1/2 quantum Heisenberg ladder.

## Context

This work extends the known relation between para-Bose statistics and the Lie superalgebra B(0,n)=osp(1/2n) (Ganchev and Palev) and para-Fermi statistics and the Lie algebra B_n=so(2n+1) (Kamefuchi and Takahashi; Ryan and Sudarshan) to the case of the Lie algebra A_n=sl(n+1). The paper builds on Haldane's (1991) concept of exclusion statistics and shows that A-statistics provides a finite-dimensional approximation to ordinary Bose statistics. The quasi-Bose operators are shown to be directly applicable to condensed matter models, specifically the two-leg spin-1/2 Heisenberg ladder studied by Gopalan and collaborators, where they replace the bond operator representation of Chubukov and Sachdev to obtain the correct finite-dimensional local state space.
