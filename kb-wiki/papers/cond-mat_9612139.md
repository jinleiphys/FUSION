# A Plaquette Basis for the Study of Heisenberg Ladders

> We employ a plaquette basis-generated by coupling the four spins in a $2\times2$ lattice to a well-defined total angular momentum-for the study of Heisenberg ladders with antiferromagnetic coupling. Matrix elements of the Hamiltonian in this basis are evaluated using standard techniques in angular-momentum (Racah) algebra. We show by exact diagonalization of small ($2\times4$ and $2\times6$) systems that in excess of 90% of the ground-state probability is contained in a very small number of basis states. These few basis states can be used to define a severely truncated basis which we use to approximate low-lying exact eigenstates. We show how, in this low-energy basis, the isotropic spin-1/2 Heisenberg ladder can be mapped onto an anisotropic spin-1 ladder for which the coupling along the rungs is much stronger than the coupling between the rungs. The mapping thereby generates two distinct energy scales which greatly facilitates understanding the dynamics of the original spin-1/2 ladder. Moreover, we use these insights to define an effective low-energy Hamiltonian in accordance to the newly developed COntractor REnormalization group (CORE) method. We show how a simple range-2 CORE approximation to the effective Hamiltonian to be used with our truncated basis reproduces the low-energy spectrum of the exact $2\times6$ theory at the $\alt 1%$ level.

## Key claim

The authors show that for isotropic spin-1/2 Heisenberg ladders, a basis constructed from the eigenstates of a single 2x2 plaquette concentrates over 90% of the ground-state probability in a very small number of basis states, enabling a severely truncated basis that reproduces the exact low-energy spectrum to within 1% when combined with a range-2 CORE effective Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors construct a "plaquette basis" by coupling the four spins in a 2x2 lattice to a well-defined total angular momentum using standard Racah algebra, then compute Hamiltonian matrix elements in this basis. They perform exact diagonalization of small 2x4 and 2x6 Heisenberg ladders with antiferromagnetic coupling and open boundary conditions, truncate the basis to the four lowest-energy plaquette states (out of 16), and apply the COntractor REnormalization group (CORE) method of Morningstar and Weinstein (1994) to construct a range-2 effective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For the 2x4 system, a single plaquette basis state carries 85% of the ground-state probability; with two states one accounts for nearly the full probability.
- For the 2x6 system, one basis state carries over 70% of the ground-state probability; with three states one accounts for almost 90%.
- The range-2 CORE approximation reproduces the low-energy spectrum of the exact 2x6 theory at the less than 1% level.
- The truncated basis reduces the Hilbert space from 16 states per plaquette to 4 states per plaquette (a factor of four reduction).
- Exact diagonalization of the 2x6 system in the Sz basis requires a 924x924 matrix; the CORE calculation uses at most 20x20 matrices.
- The effective spin-1 ladder has rung couplings four times stronger than the couplings between rungs.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of alternative bases for ladder materials by Martins, Dagotto, and Riera (1996) and builds on the CORE method developed by Morningstar and Weinstein (1994). The plaquette basis approach contrasts with the traditional Sz basis used in most prior calculations, including exact and Lanczos diagonalization techniques (Lanczos 1950), quantum Monte-Carlo methods (Binder and Heermann 1992), and the density-matrix renormalization group approach (White 1992). The mapping to an anisotropic spin-1 ladder provides a natural explanation for the two energy scales previously introduced phenomenologically by Dagotto, Riera, and Scalapino (1992) for doped ladders, and enables future renormalization group studies of infinite ladders.
