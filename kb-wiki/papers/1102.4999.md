# Proceedings of the International PrimeNet Workshop

> These are the proceedings of the PrimeNet Workshop, held in September 16-18, 2010, at the campus of Instituto Superior Técnico in Lisbon, Portugal. This workshop is part of the activities in the project "Study of Strongly Interacting Matter" (acronym HadronPhysics2), which is an integrating activity of the Seventh Framework Program of EU. This HP2 project contains several activities, one of them being the network PrimeNet having the focus on Meson Physics in Low-Energy QCD. This network is created to exchange information on experimental and theoretical ongoing activities on mainly eta and eta-prime physics at different European accelerator facilities and institutes.

## Key claim
These proceedings compile reports from the PrimeNet network, which focuses on meson physics in low-energy QCD, specifically on experimental and theoretical activities concerning eta and eta-prime mesons at European accelerator facilities. The workshop was part of the HadronPhysics2 project, an integrating activity of the EU's Seventh Framework Program.

## Method
The workshop gathered researchers to exchange information on ongoing experimental and theoretical work in eta and eta-prime physics. The proceedings contain individual contributions covering topics such as the decay of eta mesons, the determination of quark masses, measurements of meson transition form factors, searches for eta-mesic nuclei, and studies of the eta-nucleon interaction.

## Context
This workshop proceedings summarizes the activities of the PrimeNet network within the HadronPhysics2 project. It extends the collaborative framework for low-energy QCD studies in Europe, particularly for eta and eta-prime mesons, by bringing together experimental groups from facilities like COSY, J-PARC, and BaBar with theoretical efforts in chiral perturbation theory and lattice QCD. The collection enables a coordinated approach to open questions such as the precise determination of light quark mass ratios, the nature of the ABC effect, and the properties of meson transition form factors.
