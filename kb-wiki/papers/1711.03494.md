# Electromagnetic transition form factors of the Roper resonance in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We consider the electromagnetic transition form factors of the Roper resonance in the framework of an effective field theory of pions, nucleons and delta and Roper resonances as explicit degrees of freedom. Due to the lack experimental data in the region of applicability of low energy effective field theory we fit available free coupling constants and compare obtained results compare to the predictions of a theoretical model.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic transition form factors of the Roper resonance are calculated in baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order, but due to the lack of experimental data at low momentum transfer, the free coupling constants are fitted to reproduce the predictions of a theoretical parametrization rather than being determined from direct measurement.

## Method

The calculation uses baryon chiral perturbation theory with pions, nucleons, and the delta and Roper resonances as explicit dynamical degrees of freedom. Tree and one-loop diagrams contributing to the transition form factors are identified and evaluated up to next-to-next-to-leading order, with power-counting-violating pieces absorbed into renormalization of coupling constants. The unknown parameters contributing at tree order are fixed by reproducing the \(F_2^*\) form factor of the proton at low \(q^2\) using the parametrization of Ramalho (2017).

## Key numbers

- Roper resonance mass: \(m_R = 1365 \pm 15\) MeV
- Delta resonance mass: \(m_\Delta = 1210 \pm 1\) MeV
- Delta resonance width: \(\Gamma_\Delta = 100 \pm 2\) MeV
- Pion mass: \(M_\pi = 139\) MeV
- Nucleon mass: \(m_N = 939\) MeV
- Pion decay constant: \(F_\pi = 92.2\) MeV
- Axial coupling: \(g_A = 1.27\)
- Pion-nucleon-delta coupling: \(h = 1.42 \pm 0.02\)
- Pion-nucleon-Roper coupling: \(g_{\pi NR} = \pm (0.47 \pm 0.05)\)

## Context

This work extends the application of baryon chiral perturbation theory to the Roper resonance, following earlier calculations of its pole mass and width at one-loop order by Borasoy (2006) and Djukanovic (2009) and at two-loop order by Gegelia (2016), as well as a study of its magnetic moment by Bauer (2012). Unlike the approach of Bauer (2014) which included vector mesons, this work restricts to small virtual photon momentum square \(q^2 \sim M_\pi^2\) and uses only pions, nucleons, and the delta and Roper resonances as degrees of freedom. The results enable comparison with future experimental data at low momentum transfer and provide a systematic EFT framework for further studies of nucleon resonance structure.
