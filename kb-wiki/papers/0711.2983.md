# Dijet and photon-jet correlations in proton-proton collisions at RHIC

> We discuss correlations in azimuthal angle as well as correlations in two-dimensional space of transverse momenta of two jets as well as photon and jet. Some $k_t$-factorization subprocesses are included for the first time in the literature. Different unintegrated gluon/parton distributions are used in the $k_t$-factorization approach. The results depend on UGDF/UPDF used. The collinear NLO $2 \to 3$ contributions dominate over $k_t$-factorization cross section at small relative azimuthal angles as well as for asymmetric transverse momentum configurations.

# Dijet and photon-jet correlations in proton-proton collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

The $k_t$-factorization approach, including for the first time $gq \to gq$ and $qg \to qg$ subprocesses, can describe dijet and photon-jet correlations in the semi-hard region (transverse momenta ~5–20 GeV) where the standard NLO collinear approach gives vanishing cross sections for leading-jet configurations; however, the results depend strongly on which unintegrated gluon/parton distribution function (UGDF/UPDF) is used, and the collinear NLO $2 \to 3$ contributions dominate over $k_t$-factorization at small relative azimuthal angles and for asymmetric transverse momentum configurations when considering any jets rather than only leading jets.

## Method

The authors compute dijet and photon-jet cross sections in proton-proton collisions using the $k_t$-factorization formalism, where the transverse momenta of incoming partons are included explicitly via unintegrated parton distribution functions. They evaluate matrix elements for the $2 \to 2$ subprocesses $gg \to gg$, $gg \to q\bar{q}$, $gq \to gq$, and $qg \to qg$, and compare results obtained with different UGDFs/UPDFs from the literature (Kwieciński, BFKL, KL) as well as with standard NLO collinear $2 \to 3$ calculations. For photon-jet correlations, they consider the same $k_t$-factorization framework and compare with NLO collinear results, examining the effects of different cuts on the transverse momentum of the third (unobserved) parton.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV for RHIC calculations and $\sqrt{s} = 1960$ GeV for Tevatron calculations
- Transverse momentum integration ranges: 5 GeV $< p_{1,t}, p_{2,t} <$ 15 GeV for dijet azimuthal correlations; 5 GeV $< p_{1,t}, p_{2,t} <$ 20 GeV for photon-jet correlations
- Rapidity integration range: $-4 < y_1, y_2 < 4$ for dijet; $-5 < y_1, y_2 < 5$ for photon-jet
- Kwieciński UPDFs used with nonperturbative parameter $b_0 = 1$ GeV$^{-1}$ and evolution scale $\mu^2 = 100$ GeV$^2$ as default; also tested $b_0 = 0.5$ and $2.0$ GeV$^{-1}$ and scales $\mu^2 = 0.25$, $10$, and $100$ GeV$^2$
- In the NLO collinear approach for leading jets, $\frac{d\sigma}{d\phi_{-}} = 0$ for $\phi_{-} < \frac{2}{3}\pi$ and $\frac{d\sigma}{dp_{1,t}dp_{2,t}} = 0$ for $p_{1,t} > 2p_{2,t}$ or $p_{2,t} > 2p_{1,t}$ due to kinematical constraints

## Context

This work extends previous $k_t$-factorization studies of dijet correlations in photoproduction (SNSS01) and hadroproduction (LO00) by including $gq \to gq$ and $qg \to qg$ subprocesses for the first time, and presents the first theoretical calculation of photon-jet correlations in elementary collisions. The analysis is motivated by hadron-hadron correlation measurements at RHIC (RHIC_correlations_nucleus_nucleus, RHIC_correlations_proton_proton) and photon-hadron correlations being analyzed by the PHENIX collaboration (JanRak). The results enable future comparisons with experimental jet and photon-jet correlation data at RHIC, and the authors note that inclusion of fragmentation would be needed to connect to measured hadron-hadron correlations.
