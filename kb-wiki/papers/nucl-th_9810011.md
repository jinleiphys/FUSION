# Relativistic mean-field description of nuclei at the drip lines

> We present a review of recent applications of the relativistic mean-field theory to the structure of nuclei close to the drip-lines. For systems with extreme isospin values, the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model provides a unified and self-consistent description of mean-field and pairing correlations. The model has been applied in studies of structure phenomena that include: formation of neutron skin and of neutron halos in light nuclei in the mass region above the s-d neutron shell, the strong isospin dependence of the effective spin-orbit interaction and the resulting modification of surface properties, the suppression of the spherical N=28 shell gap for neutron-rich nuclei and the related phenomenon of deformation and shape coexistence, the proton drip-line in the spherical nuclei $14\leq Z \leq 28$, and ground-state proton radioactivity in the region of deformed nuclei $59 \leq Z \leq 69$.

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, combining the NL3 effective interaction for the mean-field Lagrangian with the Gogny D1S force in the pairing channel, provides a unified and self-consistent description of mean-field and pairing correlations for nuclei at the drip lines, but the present accuracy of model predictions is not comparable with the precision of modern experimental research facilities.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations self-consistently, coupling the Dirac equation for nucleons with Klein-Gordon equations for the sigma, omega, rho meson and photon fields in the mean-field approximation. For spherical nuclei, finite element methods in coordinate space or expansions in spherical harmonic oscillator bases are used; for deformed nuclei, the nucleon spinors and meson fields are expanded in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential. Pairing correlations are included using the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction, and the self-consistent quasi-particle eigenspectrum is transformed into the canonical basis to determine single-particle energies and occupation probabilities.

## Context

This review extends the relativistic mean-field framework, previously successful for β-stable nuclei as described by Ring (1996), to exotic nuclei far from stability, building on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory introduced by Kucharek and Ring (1991). The work enables predictions for neutron halo formation, the suppression of spherical shell gaps, and shape coexistence in neutron-rich regions, and provides a foundation for studying hypernuclei with large neutron excess as well as nuclei relevant to the r-process in nucleosynthesis.
