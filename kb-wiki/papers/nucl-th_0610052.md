# Electromagnetic Probes of Strongly Interacting Matter

> Photons and dileptons are being used to probe the properties of nuclear and quark-gluon matter at high energy densities. This is an area where theory and experiment are driving each other to obtain solid results. However, it is important to clearly separate the assumptions and conclusions concerning the correlation and response functions of a system in thermal equilibrium from the space-time dynamics used to model the evolution of the matter created in the nuclear collision.

## Key claim

Photons and dileptons can probe the properties of nuclear and quark-gluon matter at high energy densities, but one must clearly separate the assumptions about the correlation and response functions of a system in thermal equilibrium from the space-time dynamics used to model the evolution of the matter created in the nuclear collision.

## Method

The paper reviews the formal expressions for electromagnetic emission rates in relativistic quantum field theory, where the photon rate is expressed in terms of the imaginary part of the retarded photon self-energy in the thermal medium. For dileptons in the mass range from a few hundred MeV to just above 1 GeV, the approach uses vector-meson dominance to relate the electromagnetic current to vector-meson fields, with the rho-meson propagator expressed in terms of two scalar self-energies. The theoretical rates are then folded with dynamical evolution models (relativistic hydrodynamics and UrQMD coarse-grained models) and compared to experimental data from CERES, NA60, WA98, and PHENIX.

## Key numbers

- The mean free path for real or virtual photons in hot and dense matter is typically more than 10^2 to 10^4 fm.
- CERES observed an enhancement above the hadronic decay cocktail in the mass range 300 to 700 MeV in Pb+Au collisions at 158 A GeV.
- NA60 measured semi-central In-In collisions at 160 GeV per nucleon with sufficient statistics to allow binning in transverse momentum of the pair.
- WA98 measured direct photons in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV over transverse momenta from 1 to 4 GeV.
- PHENIX measured photons in p-p collisions at 200 GeV and Au+Au at 200 A GeV.

## Context

This review extends the pioneering work of Feinberg and Shuryak on soft quark and gluon interactions producing photons and dileptons, and the DLS Collaboration's first dilepton measurements from nuclear collisions. The paper discusses competing explanations for the CERES dilepton enhancement: the rho-meson broadening approach by Rapp, Chanfray and Wambach versus the dropping rho-mass scaling of Brown and Rho, with subsequent calculations by Eletsky et al. using vacuum scattering amplitudes. The work enables future separation of jet-photon conversion signals from thermal and prompt photons at RHIC, and highlights open questions about the relationship between in-medium spectral densities and dynamical evolution models.
