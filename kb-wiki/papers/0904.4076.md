# The structure of accreted neutron star crust

> Using molecular dynamics simulations, we determine the structure of neutron star crust made of rapid proton capture nucleosynthesis material. We find a regular body centered cubic lattice, even with the large number of impurities that are present. Low charge $Z$ impurities tend to occupy interstitial positions, while high $Z$ impurities tend to occupy substitutional lattice sites. We find strong attractive correlations between low $Z$ impurities that could significantly increase the rate of pycnonuclear (density driven) nuclear reactions. The thermal conductivity is significantly reduced by electron impurity scattering. Our results will be used in future work to study the effects of impurities on mechanical properties such as the shear modulus and breaking strain.

# The structure of accreted neutron star crust

## Key claim

Using molecular dynamics simulations, the authors find that accreted neutron star crust formed from rapid proton capture nucleosynthesis ash forms a regular body-centered cubic lattice even with many impurities, but only when using a composition that is depleted in low-Z elements compared to the original ash composition, the composition used is the solid phase that results from chemical separation during freezing, not the bulk ash composition itself.

## Method

Classical molecular dynamics simulations were performed on a 27648 ion system with a screened Coulomb (Yukawa) interaction potential, using a composition derived from the solid phase of rapid proton capture ash that had undergone chemical separation during freezing. The system was annealed from near the melting point to near zero temperature over 46 days using a special-purpose MDGRAPE-2 board, and radial distribution functions, static structure factors, and thermal conductivities were calculated from 2500 configurations separated by 250 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Impurity parameter Q = 22.54 for the composition used (compared to Q = 38.9 for the original Gupta et al. composition)
- Reference density for simulations: n = 7.18 × 10⁻⁵ fm⁻³
- System size: 27648 ions (assembled from 432-ion and 3456-ion intermediate systems)
- Thermal conductivity at T = 0.1 MeV and density 8.0 × 10¹¹ g/cm³: κ = 1.57 × 10¹⁹ erg/K cm s (compared to κ_imp = 2.08 × 10¹⁹ erg/K cm s for uncorrelated impurities)
- Thermal conductivity at T = 0.2 MeV and density 9.9 × 10¹⁰ g/cm³: κ = 4.20 × 10¹⁸ erg/K cm s
- Thermal conductivity at T = 0.3 MeV and density 2.9 × 10¹⁰ g/cm³: κ = 2.00 × 10¹⁸ erg/K cm s
- Coulomb logarithm Λ at T = 0.1 MeV: 0.160 (compared to Λ_imp = 0.121)
- Melting parameter Γ = 247 for the multi-component plasma (compared to Γ = 175 for a one-component plasma)
- Composition includes 0.93% Oxygen (Z=8, A=24), 0.23% Neon (Z=10, A=28), and 43.06% Selenium (Z=34, A=102)

## Context

This work extends previous molecular dynamics studies of neutron star crust structure (Horowitz 2008) by using a composition that is depleted in low-Z impurities compared to the original rapid proton capture ash composition from Gupta et al., avoiding the phase separation issues encountered in earlier thermal conductivity calculations (Horowitz 2008). The results contradict simple models that assume impurities are randomly distributed (impurity parameter approach), finding instead strong attractive correlations between low-Z impurities that grow with decreasing temperature. The findings enable future calculations of the shear modulus and breaking strain of the crust, which are important for interpreting quasiperiodic oscillations in magnetar giant flares and gravitational wave emission from neutron star mountains. The reduced thermal conductivity from impurity scattering may conflict with interpretations of observed rapid crust cooling in neutron stars KS 1731-260 and MXB 1659-29 (Cackett 2006, Rutledge 2007, Shternin 2007, Brown 2008), suggesting either that the initial rp ash composition is incorrect or that impurity concentrations are reduced by nuclear reactions at greater depths.
