# Correlation between particle spectra and elliptic flow

> We introduce a new observable to probe the collective nature of the radial expansion of the quark-gluon plasma. This observable, dubbed $v_{02}(p_T)$, represents the correlation of the spectrum with elliptic flow, in the same way as the recently measured $v_0(p_T)$ represents the correlation of the spectrum with the transverse momentum per particle. The advantage of $v_{02}(p_T)$ over $v_0(p_T)$ is that it is measured using a three-particle cumulant, as opposed to a pair correlation, which significantly reduces the sensitivity to nonflow effects. We predict non-trivial differences between $v_{02}(p_T)$ and $v_0(p_T)$ in semi-central Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) on the basis of hydrodynamic simulations. A hint of these differences can be seen in the modification of $p_T$ spectra observed by ALICE in event-shape-engineered events.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new observable, \(v_{02}(p_T)\), which measures the correlation between the particle spectrum and elliptic flow squared using a three-particle cumulant; the authors predict that its \(p_T\) dependence differs significantly from the existing \(v_0(p_T)\) observable in semi-central Pb+Pb collisions, with an inverted mass ordering at low \(p_T\), but note that their hydrodynamic simulations are not state-of-the-art and are intended to illustrate qualitative trends rather than make precise quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors performed hydrodynamic simulations of 3000 Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV in the 30%–40% centrality window using the TRENTO model for initial conditions and the MUSIC code for hydrodynamic evolution with a shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s=0.08\) and zero bulk viscosity, followed by Cooper-Frye particlization at a freezeout temperature of 130 MeV. They then computed \(v_{02}(p_T)\) from the simulated events using its definition as a three-particle cumulant and compared it to \(v_0(p_T)\) from the same simulations, also developing a two-component model where the spectrum fluctuation is expressed as a linear combination of response functions to variations in mean transverse momentum \([p_T]\) and elliptic flow squared \(v_2^2\).

## Key numbers

* The integrated observable \(v_{02}\) is estimated to be approximately 0.3% in 30%–40% central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, based on \(\rho_2 \approx 0.2\), \(v_0 \approx 0.02\), and \(\sigma_{v_2^2}/\langle v_2^2\rangle \approx 0.7\).
* The hydrodynamic simulations used a nucleon width \(w = 0.5\) fm, a starting proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/\(c\), and a freezeout temperature \(T = 130\) MeV (corresponding to an energy density of 0.18 GeV/fm\(^3\)).
* The mean transverse momentum \(\langle p_T\rangle\) from the full hydrodynamic simulation was 696 MeV/\(c\).
* The fit to ALICE event-shape-engineering data using Eq. (linearfit) yielded dimensionless constants \(a = 1.5\) and \(b = 0.007\).

## Context

This work extends the line of research on event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, specifically the recently measured \(v_0(p_T)\) observable by ATLAS (2025) and ALICE (2025), which correlates the spectrum with the mean transverse momentum per particle. The new \(v_{02}(p_T)\) observable is a differential version of the Bozek correlator \(\rho_2\) (Bozek, 2016) between \([p_T]\) and \(v_2^2\), which has been studied at RHIC and LHC by ATLAS (2019), ALICE (2021), CMS (2024), and STAR (2024). The paper enables future measurements that can probe pressure gradients in the quark-gluon plasma more directly than existing observables, with reduced sensitivity to nonflow effects compared to pair correlations, and is expected to bring new constraints on nuclear structure and transport coefficients.
