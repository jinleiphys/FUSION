# Covariant propagator and chiral power counting

## Key claim

The authors show that for one-loop diagrams with one or two external baryons/nucleons in chiral effective theory, it is sufficient to separate and subtract all local terms that violate chiral power counting in order to recover standard power counting, without needing extra operations such as the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) prescription. The leading chiral corrections and infrared enhancement or threshold effects remain "stable" as long as covariant propagators are employed for all particles, regardless of whether covariant or heavy-baryon (HB) vertices are used.

## Method

The authors recalculate one-loop diagrams for matrix elements of twist-2 operators in nucleon states (Fig. 1, diagrams a-g) and for NN scattering (Fig. 2, triangle and box diagrams) using fully covariant baryon propagators throughout, first with covariant vertices and then with HB vertices and composite operators. They decompose each amplitude into four categories: local non-chiral terms, local chiral terms, nonlocal relativistic corrections that vanish as ρ → ∞ (where ρ = M_N²/m_π²), and nonlocal "anomalous" terms from infrared enhancement or threshold effects. They then subtract all local non-chiral terms to examine whether the remaining chiral components are independent of the vertex choice.

## Key numbers

- ρ ≡ M_N²/m_π², where M_N is the nucleon mass and m_π is the pion mass; σ ≡ √(4ρ − 1)
- The anomalous term in the NN box diagram (Fig. 2(c)) is −12ρ/σ arctan σ, which becomes IR singular when HB propagators are used
- The suppressed relativistic corrections (R terms) vanish as o(ρ^{−1/2}) in the limit ρ → ∞
- For the mixed calculation with HB vertices, the sum of diagrams for the vector current operator yields a nonzero result: −(5g_A² m_π²)/(16(4π f_π)²) × { (3/2)ρ(Γ_ε + ℓ_N + 13/30) + (Γ_ε + ℓ_N + 3/5) + (4/5)(R_{1a}^{HBv} − R_{1b}^{HBv}) } ≠ 0
- After subtracting local non-chiral terms, the same sum becomes o(ρ^{−1/2}), restoring vector current conservation in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the line of research on chiral power counting in baryonic chiral perturbation theory, which has been problematic since the seminal papers by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) and Weinberg (1990). The heavy-baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) preserves power counting but loses correct kinematics, as shown by Becher and Leutwyler (1999). Recent work by Geng et al. (2016) and others has advocated covariant formulations with EOMS subtraction. The authors propose a unified extended MS (EMS) subtraction scheme that removes all local non-chiral terms while preserving the "anomalous" nonlocal terms from infrared enhancement, which are definite only with covariant propagators. This enables a consistent covariant treatment of nuclear forces that avoids spurious power divergences from nonrelativistic treatments.
