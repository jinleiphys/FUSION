# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Pole term of the Axial-vector Current and the Neutron $β$-decay

> The effect of the pion propagator on the $β$-decay of neutron is investigated to account for the ratio of the axial-vector to the vector part $g_A/g_V$ by including the pole term. A suggestion is made that the sign and the size of the vertex correction is appropriate to improve the discrepancy between the result of the model hamiltonian and the experiment.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that including the pole term of the axial-vector current, with a vertex correction mediated by the pion propagator, can improve the discrepancy between the model Hamiltonian prediction and the experimental value of the axial-vector to vector coupling ratio \(g_A/g_V\), but only when the self-energy parameter \(c\) is chosen appropriately (specifically \(c=2\)).

## Method

The author derives a non-perturbative relation for the vertex function of the axial-vector current using the equation of motion for a massive vector boson field. The pole term of the vertex is then evaluated perturbatively by including the pion propagator in the second-order correction, using the pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling. The integral for the coefficient \(a_1\) of the vertex correction is computed via dimensional regularization, and the divergent part is subtracted using a form-factor relation. The resulting correction is applied to the neutron \(\beta\)-decay by relating the pseudoscalar and axial-vector matrix elements through the asymmetry parameter of the proton.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient of the second-order vertex correction from the pion propagator is \(a_1 = -3.56085\) (after subtracting the divergent part, not including terms of order \(m_\pi^2/M^2\)).
- The pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling constant is \(G_\pi = 13.4544\) (using \(f=1\)).
- The charged pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 0.12825\ \mathrm{GeV}\), derived from the experimental pion lifetime \(\Gamma_\pi^{-1} = 2.6033 \times 10^{-8}\ \mathrm{s}\) and the Fermi coupling constant \(G_F = 1.16637 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The uncorrected ratio from the Goldberger-Treiman relation gives \(-\lambda = 1.300\), while the experimental value is \(-\lambda_{exp} = 1.2695 \pm 0.0029\).
- Using the self-energy parameter \(c = 2\), the corrected ratio becomes \(-(\lambda + \alpha) = 1.2734\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous approach to the pion-nucleon vertex (Kinpara, recent work) by applying the non-perturbative self-energy formalism to neutron \(\beta\)-decay. The calculation uses the partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC) relation and the Goldberger-Treiman relation (Lurie, textbook) to connect the pion decay constant to \(g_A/g_V\). The result suggests that the self-energy parameter \(c\) determined from electromagnetic properties (magnetic moment) may be more relevant for the weak interaction than for low-energy pion-nucleon elastic scattering, pointing toward universality of electromagnetic and weak interactions.
