# Production of high-spin $Žē_J/Žü_J$ ($J=2,3,4,5$) mesons in $ŽĆ^{-}p$ reactions

> In this work, we perform a comprehensive investigation of the production of high-spin $Žē_J$ and $Žü_J$ mesons ($J=2,3,4,5$) in $ŽĆ^- p$ reactions using an effective Lagrangian approach. By constructing the relevant $t$-channel processes and calibrating the model with a single adjustable parameter fitted to existing data, we successfully reproduce the measured total and differential cross sections for the $J=3$ states $Žē_3(1670)$ and $Žü_3(1690)$. Within the same framework, we predict the production cross sections for their lower- and higher-spin partners: $Žē_2(1975)$, $Žü_2(1940)$, $Žē_4(2250)$, $Žü_4(2230)$, $Žē_5(2350)$, and $Žü_5(2350)$. Our results show that these states exhibit measurable cross sections with characteristically forward-peaked angular distributions, underscoring their strong potential for observation in future $ŽĆp$ meson-beam experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that high-spin $\omega_J$ and $\rho_J$ mesons ($J=2,4,5$) can be produced in $\pi^- p$ reactions with measurable cross sections and characteristically forward-peaked angular distributions, but this claim is conditional on the validity of a single universal cutoff parameter $\Lambda_t = 3.5 \pm 0.5$ GeV, which was fitted only to the $J=3$ states $\omega_3(1670)$ and $\rho_3(1690)$.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with $t$-channel meson exchange (Reggeized $\rho$ and $\pi$ propagators) to compute production amplitudes for $\pi^- p \to \omega_J/\rho_J + N$ reactions. They calibrated the model by fitting a single adjustable cutoff parameter $\Lambda_t$ to existing total and differential cross-section data for the $J=3$ states $\omega_3(1670)$ and $\rho_3(1690)$, then applied the same framework without further adjustment to predict cross sections for the $J=2,4,5$ partners. The coupling constants for the high-spin vertices were fixed using partial decay widths calculated within the quark pair creation (QPC) model.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda_t = 3.5 \pm 0.5$ GeV, obtained from fitting data for $\pi^- p \to \omega_3(1670)n$ with $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} \leq 1.3$.
- Coupling constant $g_{\omega_3 \rho \pi} = 38.94\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3}$, derived from the partial width $\Gamma(\omega_3(1670) \to \rho\pi) = 168\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- Coupling constant $g_{\rho_3 \pi \pi} = 12.02\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$, derived from the partial width $\Gamma(\rho_3(1690) \to \pi\pi) = 32\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- Masses and widths from PDG: $\omega_3(1670)$ at $1677 \pm 4$ MeV with width $168 \pm 10$ MeV; $\rho_3(1690)$ at $1686 \pm 4$ MeV with width $186 \pm 14$ MeV.
- Predicted states: $\omega_2(1975)$ at $1975 \pm 20$ MeV, $\rho_2(1940)$ at $1940 \pm 40$ MeV, $\omega_4(2250)$ at $2250 \pm 30$ MeV, $\rho_4(2230)$ at $2230 \pm 25$ MeV, $\omega_5(2250)$ at $2250 \pm 70$ MeV, $\rho_5(2350)$ at $2330 \pm 35$ MeV.
- Regge trajectory slopes: $\alpha_\rho^\prime = 0.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ and $\alpha_\pi^\prime = 0.7\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of high-spin light meson production, which has been relatively scarce compared to spectroscopy-focused studies using Regge trajectory analyses (Anisovich, 2000) and potential models (Bai, 2025; Li, 2022). The authors build on previous QPC model calculations of decay widths (Wang, 2022; Pang, 2015) and apply a consistent production framework to predict cross sections for states that have only been observed in $p\bar{p}$ annihilation (Anisovich, 2002) or are listed as "further states" by the Particle Data Group (2024). The results provide concrete guidance for future $\pi p$ meson-beam experiments to confirm these high-spin states.
