# Light-quark contributions to the magnetic form factor of the Lambda(1405)

> In a recent study of the Lambda(1405), the suppression of the strange-quark contribution to the magnetic form factor was interpreted as the discovery of a dominant antikaon-nucleon composition for this low-lying state. We confirm this result by calculating the light u- and d-quark contributions to the Lambda(1405) magnetic form factor in lattice QCD in order to determine the extent to which their contributions support this exotic molecular description. Drawing on the recent graded-symmetry approach for the flavor-singlet components of the Lambda(1405), the separation of connected and disconnected contributions is performed in both the flavor-octet and singlet representations. The relationship between light-quark contributions to the Lambda(1405) magnetic form factor and the connected contributions of the nucleon magnetic form factors is established and compared with lattice calculations of the same quantities, confirming the KN molecular structure of the Lambda(1405) in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the internal structure of the Lambda(1405) is dominated by a molecular antikaon-nucleon (Kbar N) bound state, by showing that the light-quark contribution to its magnetic form factor, calculated in lattice QCD, agrees with a connected Kbar N model prediction only after the omission of disconnected sea-quark loop contributions is accounted for. The agreement is achieved at the lightest pion mass, indicating that the molecular description holds near the physical quark mass.

## Method

The authors calculate the light u- and d-quark contributions to the magnetic form factor of the Lambda(1405) using lattice QCD on 32^3 x 64 full-QCD ensembles from the PACS-CS collaboration, with five different light quark masses and a constant strange quark mass. They separate connected and disconnected quark-flow contributions using a graded-symmetry approach from partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory, which allows them to determine that the connected diagram carries weight 1/3 and the disconnected diagram carries weight 2/3 of the full QCD process for both flavor-singlet and flavor-octet representations. The connected Kbar N model prediction is then constructed from the nucleon magnetic form factors, with the disconnected contributions removed, and compared to the direct lattice QCD calculation of the Lambda(1405) light-quark magnetic form factor.

## Key numbers

- At the lightest pion mass (m_pi = 156 MeV) and momentum transfer Q^2 ~ 0.16 GeV^2/c^2, the light-quark magnetic form factor of the Lambda(1405) from direct lattice QCD calculation is 0.58(5) mu_N.
- The connected Kbar N model predicts a light-quark magnetic form factor of 0.63(2) mu_N at the same kinematics.
- The nucleon magnetic form factors used in the model are u_p = 1.216(17) mu_N and u_n = -0.366(19) mu_N at the lightest pion mass.
- If disconnected sea-quark loop contributions were included, the Kbar N model prediction would be significantly larger at (2 u_p + u_n)/2 = 1.03(2) mu_N.

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice QCD study of the Lambda(1405) by Hall (2014), which found suppression of the strange-quark contribution to the magnetic form factor and interpreted it as evidence for a dominant Kbar N molecular structure. The present paper provides complementary confirmation by analyzing the light-quark sector, which had received little attention previously. The graded-symmetry approach developed by Hall (2015) for flavor-singlet baryons is used to separate connected and disconnected contributions. Future work will focus on isolating nearby multi-particle scattering states in finite lattice volume and using the formalism of Briceno (2015) to connect lattice QCD results with the full resonance structure.
