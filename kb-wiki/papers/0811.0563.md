# Light scalar mesons in QCD

> I present a mini-review of the masses and couplings of the bare (unmixed) light scalar mesons : $\bar qq, (\overline{qq})(qq), (\bar qq)(\bar qq), gg$ from QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) and low-energy theorems (LET) which we compare with recent lattice calculations when available. Some unbiased comments on the different scenarios are given. The possiblity for the $σ(0.6)$ to be mostly a gluonium/glueball with a huge violation of the OZI rule in its decay is discussed. This review complements and updates the ones presented earlier [1]. Despite some progresses, the internal structure of the light scalar mesons remain puzzling, and some further efforts are required. It will be more fun at LHC if the Higgs of the Standard Model is a $σ$-like resonance.

# Light scalar mesons in QCD

## Key claim
This mini-review argues that the light scalar mesons' internal structure remains puzzling despite progress, but presents evidence that the σ(600) is likely predominantly a gluonium/glueball state with a large OZI-rule violation in its decay, rather than a conventional quark-antiquark or four-quark state.

## Method
The paper reviews masses and couplings of bare (unmixed) light scalar mesons (quark-antiquark, four-quark, diquark-antidiquark, and gluonium configurations) using QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) and low-energy theorems (LET), comparing predictions with experimental data and lattice calculations where available. For the isoscalar channel, the analysis uses the trace of the energy-momentum tensor as the interpolating operator, with Laplace/Borel sum rules for the two-point correlator and vertex sum rules for hadronic couplings.

## Key numbers
- Mass of the a₀(980) from QSSR: M_a₀ ≈ 930 MeV (about 10% error)
- Mass of the κ(840) from QSSR: M_κ ≈ 920 MeV (about 10% error)
- Decay constant of the a₀: f_a₀ ≈ (1.6 ± 0.5) MeV
- Ratio f_κ/f_a₀ ≈ 40, expected from chiral perturbation theory
- Hadronic width Γ(a₀ → ηπ) ≈ 84 MeV, agreeing with the PDG range of 50–100 MeV
- Hadronic width Γ(κ → Kπ) ≈ 104 MeV, about a factor 4 smaller than PDG data
- γγ width of a₀ in the quark-antiquark scheme: Γ(a₀(bar qq) → γγ) ≈ (1.6–2.6) keV
- γγ width of a₀ in the four-quark scheme: Γ(a₀(4q) → γγ) ≈ (2–5) × 10⁻⁴ keV
- Mass of the unmixed scalar gluonium from the subtracted sum rule: M_G ≈ (1.5–1.6) GeV
- Mass of the low-mass gluonium σ_B from the unsubtracted sum rule: M_σ_B ≈ (0.9–1.1) GeV
- Hadronic coupling of σ_B to ππ: |g_σ_Bπ⁺π⁻| ≈ (4–5) GeV, giving Γ(σ_B → ππ) ≈ 0.7 GeV
- γγ width of σ_B: Γ(σ_B → γγ) ≈ (0.2–0.6) keV
- Direct γγ width of σ(600) at the complex pole: Γ_σ→γγ^(dir) ≈ (0.13 ± 0.05) keV
- Total γγ width of σ(600) (direct + rescattering): Γ_σ→γγ^(tot) ≈ (3.9 ± 0.6) keV
- On-shell direct γγ width of σ: Γ_σ→γγ^(os,dir) ≈ (1.0 ± 0.4) keV
- Mass of the unmixed I=0 scalar bar qq meson S₂: M_S₂ ≈ 930 MeV (degenerate with a₀)
- Hadronic width Γ(S₂ → π⁺π⁻) ≈ 120 MeV
- Mass of the unmixed strange scalar bar qq meson S₃: M_S₃ ≈ 948 MeV
- Gluon condensate used: ⟨α_s G²⟩ = (0.07 ± 0.01) GeV⁴

## Context
This review complements and updates earlier reviews by Narison (2006) on light scalar mesons from QCD spectral sum rules. It extends the QSSR approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and the low-energy theorem framework of Novikov, Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1981). The paper compares predictions with lattice calculations from quenched and unquenched approaches, and with experimental data from the Particle Data Group. The analysis of the σ(600) as a gluonium candidate builds on the U(1)_V anomaly framework, analogous to the η' in the U(1)_A channel as discussed by Witten (1979). The results enable further experimental tests of the gluonium interpretation, including the proposed measurement of C-odd asymmetry in e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ at KLOE, and motivate revision of meson-gluonium mixing schemes in light of unquenched lattice results showing the glueball mass shifting down to 1 GeV.
