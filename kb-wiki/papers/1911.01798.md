# Variational formulation of compressible hydrodynamics in curved spacetime and symmetry of stress tensor

> Hydrodynamics of the non-relativistic compressible fluid in the curved spacetime is derived using the generalized framework of the stochastic variational method (SVM) for continuum medium. The fluid-stress tensor of the resultant equation becomes asymmetric for the exchange of the indices, differently from the standard Euclidean one. Its incompressible limit suggests that the viscous term should be represented with the Bochner Laplacian. Moreover the modified Navier-Stokes-Fourier (NSF) equation proposed by Brenner can be considered even in the curved spacetime. To confirm the compatibility with the symmetry principle, SVM is applied to the gauge-invariant Lagrangian of a charged compressible fluid and then the Lorentz force is reproduced as the interaction between the Abelian gauge fields and the viscous charged fluid.

## Key claim

The authors derive a compressible hydrodynamical equation for non-relativistic fluids in curved spacetime using the stochastic variational method (SVM), finding that the fluid-stress tensor becomes asymmetric under index exchange due to the Ricci tensor, contrary to the standard Euclidean case. The result is conditional on the metric satisfying \(g_{0i}=0\) and \(\partial_i g_{00}=0\), and on the assumption of barotropic fluid with local thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors generalize the stochastic variational method (SVM) for continuum media, previously formulated for Euclidean spacetime, to curved spacetime by introducing tetrads and stochastic parallel transport via the Levi-Civita-Ito connection. They define forward and backward stochastic differential equations for fluid element trajectories, derive the corresponding Fokker-Planck equations and a consistency condition relating the forward and backward velocity fields, then apply a stochastic variational principle to a Lagrangian quadratic in the mean forward and backward derivatives to obtain the hydrodynamical equation. The gauge interaction of a charged compressible fluid is treated by adding a gauge-invariant coupling term to the stochastic Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The viscosity coefficient is defined as \(\eta = 2\xi \rho\) and the bulk viscosity as \(\zeta = \eta/D\), where \(\xi = 2\alpha_1 (1/2 + \alpha_2)\nu\) with \(\nu\) the noise intensity parameter and \(\alpha_1, \alpha_2\) arbitrary real constants.
- The parameter \(\kappa = 2\alpha_2 \nu^2\) controls the term analogous to Brenner's bivelocity modification and the quantum potential.
- In the incompressible limit (\(\nabla_i v^i = 0\)) with static geometry and \(\kappa=0\), the viscous term reduces to \(\frac{\eta}{2\rho} g^{jk}\nabla_j \nabla_k v^{i}\), identified as the Bochner Laplacian.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SVM derivation of the compressible Navier-Stokes-Fourier equation in Euclidean spacetime (Koide 2012, 2018) and their separate generalization of SVM to curved spacetime (Koide 2019) to obtain hydrodynamics on spatial Riemannian manifolds. It contrasts with prior incompressible-fluid SVM results on Riemannian manifolds by Yasue (1981) and Cruzeiro (2004), where the former yields the Bochner Laplacian (consistent with the present work) and the latter the Hodge Laplacian; the authors attribute the difference to the choice of stochastic parallel transport. The derivation enables the study of Brenner's bivelocity hydrodynamics in curved spacetime and provides a variational framework for incorporating gauge interactions of charged viscous fluids.
