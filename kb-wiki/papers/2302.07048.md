# $^{113}$Cd $β$-decay spectrum and $g_{\rm A}$ quenching using spectral moments

> We present an alternative analysis of the $^{113}$Cd $β$-decay electron energy spectrum in terms of spectral moments $μ_n$, corresponding to the averaged values of $n^{\rm th}$ powers of the $β$ particle energy. The zeroth moment $μ_0$ is related to the decay rate, while higher moments $μ_n$ are related to the spectrum shape. The here advocated spectral-moment method (SMM) allows for a complementary understanding of previous results, obtained using the so-called spectrum-shape method (SSM) and its revised version, in terms of two free parameters: $r=g_{\rm A}/g_{\rm V}$ (the ratio of axial-vector to vector couplings) and $s$ (the small vector-like relativistic nuclear matrix element, $s$-NME). We present numerical results for three different nuclear models with the conserved vector current hypothesis (CVC) assumption of $g_{\rm V}=1$. We show that most of the spectral information can be captured by the first few moments which are simple quadratic forms (conic sections) in the $(r,\,s)$ plane: an ellipse for $n=0$ and hyperbolae for $n\geq 1$, all being nearly degenerate as a result of cancellations among nuclear matrix elements. The intersections of these curves, as obtained by equating theoretical and experimental values of $μ_n$, identify the favored values of $(r,\,s)$ at a glance, without performing detailed fits. In particular, we find that values around $r\sim 1$ and $s\sim 1.6$ are consistently favored in each nuclear model, confirming the evidence for $g_{\rm A}$ quenching in $^{113}$Cd, and shedding light on the role of the $s$-NME. We briefly discuss future applications of the SMM to other forbidden $β$-decay spectra sensitive to $g_{\rm A}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the spectral-moment method (SMM) can capture most information from the $^{113}$Cd beta-decay spectrum using just the first few moments, and that values around $r \sim 1$ and $s \sim 1.6$ are consistently favored across three nuclear models, confirming evidence for $g_{\rm A}$ quenching. The authors hedge that these results hold under the assumption of the conserved vector current hypothesis with $g_{\rm V}=1$, and that the method avoids detailed fits by instead identifying intersections of conic sections in the $(r,s)$ plane.

## Method

The authors analyzed the $^{113}$Cd beta-decay electron energy spectrum using spectral moments $\mu_n$, defined as the averaged values of $n$th powers of the beta particle energy over the measured energy range above a threshold of 26 keV. They computed theoretical spectra in three nuclear models (IBFM-2, MQPM, and ISM) and expressed the moments as quadratic forms in two free parameters: $r = g_{\rm A}/g_{\rm V}$ (the ratio of axial-vector to vector couplings) and $s$ (the small vector-like relativistic nuclear matrix element, $s$-NME). By equating theoretical and experimental moments, they identified favored $(r,s)$ values from intersections of conic sections (an ellipse for $\mu_0$ and hyperbolae for higher moments) without performing detailed fits.

## Key numbers

- Experimental zeroth moment: $\mu_0^e = 2.526 \times 10^{-24}$ s$^{-1}$, derived from $N_e = 2.222 \times 10^6$ events for $N_d = 8.858 \times 10^{22}$ decaying nuclei over $t = 9.929 \times 10^6$ s (Belli 2007, Belli 2019)
- Experimental energy range: $w_e \in [1.051, 1.634]$, corresponding to threshold $T_{\rm thr} = 26$ keV and endpoint $Q_\beta = 323.83$ keV
- Experimental moments: $\mu_1^e = 1.291$, $\mu_2^e = 1.686$, $\mu_3^e = 2.226$, $\mu_4^e = 2.970$, $\mu_5^e = 4.004$, $\mu_6^e = 5.452$
- Intersection points of $\mu_0$ and $\mu_1$ curves: IBFM-2 model gives $(r=1.125, s=+1.617)$ and $(r=1.029, s=-1.675)$; MQPM model gives $(r=1.068, s=+1.557)$ and $(r=1.020, s=-1.662)$; ISM model gives $(r=0.994, s=+1.635)$ and $(r=0.960, s=-1.729)$
- Nuclear radius used: $R = 1.2 A^{1/3}$ fm $= 5.8$ fm

## Context

This work extends the revised spectrum-shape method (SSM) previously applied to $^{113}$Cd by Kostensalo (2020), which treated both $g_{\rm A}$ and the $s$-NME as free parameters to reconcile half-life and spectrum-shape data. The spectral-moment method (SMM) unifies the half-life method (connected to $\mu_0$) and the SSM (connected to higher moments) into a single framework. The results confirm earlier findings by Kostensalo (2020) using an independent data set from Belli (2007) and Belli (2019), and the authors suggest future applications of the SMM to other forbidden beta-decay spectra sensitive to $g_{\rm A}$ quenching, such as those studied by Kumar (2020), Kostensalo (2017), and Leder (2022).
