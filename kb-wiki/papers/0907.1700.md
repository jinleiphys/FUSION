# Search For the Origin of the Away-side Double-humped Structure of jets in Heavy Ion Collisions

> A model is constructed for the origin of the double-humped structure found in the di-hahron azimuthal correlation on the away-side in heavy ion collisions. The parameters in the model are determined by fitting $Δϕ$ azimuthal distribution in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC given by STAR collaboration when the trigger momentum is in $3-4\rm {GeV/c}$ and the associated particle momentum is in $1.3-1.8\rm{GeV/c}$. Then we apply our model to the semi-central and peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC and the responding $\triangle ϕ$ azimuthal distribution are reproduced respectively. Our results show that the transverse flow effect plays an essential role in the experimentally observed di-hadron azimuthal distribution structure. The spread of the hard parton creation point and initial moving direction is also necessary for the double-humped structure.

# Search For the Origin of the Away-side Double-humped Structure of jets in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The transverse flow effect is the main origin of the double-humped structure on the away-side in heavy ion collisions, but distributions for the hard parton creation point and/or its initial moving direction are also necessary; the model reproduces the experimental data only when all three effects are included simultaneously.

## Method
The authors constructed a Monte Carlo simulation of an away-side jet traversing a thermalized medium, where the hard parton undergoes successive collisions with medium partons characterized by a mean free path lambda. The medium parton momentum is the sum of a thermal component (two-dimensional Boltzmann distribution with parameter omega) and a flow component proportional to the position vector (Hubble-like law with parameter f_a). The hard parton loses momentum in each collision according to a simple momentum transfer formula, and the process continues until the hard parton momentum falls below the mean thermal momentum.

## Key numbers
- The best fit to STAR central Au+Au data (0-12% centrality, trigger momentum 3-4 GeV/c, associated momentum 1.3-1.8 GeV/c) uses parameters: omega = 40, f_a = 0.1, lambda = 0.05 (in units of system radius R=1), alpha = 0.1.
- The dip in the away-side distribution is at Delta phi = pi, and the double humps are at Delta phi approximately pi +/- 1.1 radians.
- The mean magnitude of medium parton momentum is 2/omega = 1/20 of the hard parton momentum; this corresponds to hard parton momentum k = 20 x sqrt(2) x T_c approximately 5 GeV for T_c = 0.17 GeV, consistent with P_trig approximately 3-4 GeV.
- The model reproduces the transition from double-humped to single peak structure when going from semi-central (30-40% centrality) to peripheral (60-92% centrality) Au+Au collisions at PHENIX.

## Context
This work extends the momentum kick model of Wong (2009) for ridge formation to the away-side double-humped structure, and is related to other medium response models such as Mach cone, Cerenkov gluon radiation, and jet broadening. The model enables further study of how collective flow and initial parton geometry together produce the observed di-hadron azimuthal correlations, and can be applied to different collision centralities at RHIC. The authors note that more realistic jet-medium interactions may be needed for quantitative agreement with experimental data.
