# Probing deconfinement in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at imaginary chemical potential

> The phase structure of Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model is explored at imaginary chemical potential, with particular emphasis on the deconfinement transition. We point out that the statistical confinement nature of the model naturally leads to characteristic dependence of the chiral condensate $<\bar{q}q>$ on $θ=μ_I/T$. We introduce a dual parameter for the deconfinement transition by making use of this dependence. By changing a four-fermion coupling constant, we tune the location of the critical endpoint of the deconfinement transition.

## Key claim

The deconfinement transition in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model at imaginary chemical potential can exhibit a critical endpoint (CEP) whose location depends on the four-fermion coupling constant and the choice of Polyakov loop potential; this CEP can be tuned into the real chemical potential regime only when the Polyakov loop potential has a strong first-order transition and the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is large.

## Method

The authors studied the two-flavor PNJL model in the mean-field approximation at imaginary chemical potential \(\mu = i\mu_I\) with \(\theta = \mu_I/T\), using both a polynomial and a logarithmic form of the Polyakov loop effective potential. They solved the gap equations for the chiral condensate \(\sigma\), the modulus of the Polyakov loop \(|\Phi|\), and its phase \(\phi\) as functions of temperature and \(\theta\), and varied the four-fermion coupling constant \(G_s\) to map the location of the deconfinement critical endpoint. They also introduced a modified dual parameter \(\Sigma_\theta^{(n)}\) based on the \(\theta\)-dependence of the chiral condensate and compared it with the dressed Polyakov loop \(\Sigma^{(1)}\).

## Key numbers

- The cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.6315\) GeV and coupling \(G_s = 5.498\) GeV\(^{-2}\) are fixed to reproduce vacuum pion mass and decay constant with \(m_0 = 5.5\) MeV.
- The deconfinement CEP reaches \(\mu_{\rm CEP}^2 = 0\) around \(G_s \simeq 6.3\) GeV\(^{-2}\) for the logarithmic potential.
- For the logarithmic potential, the gap of the Polyakov loop at \(T = T_0\) is \(\Delta \Phi = 0.47\); for the polynomial potential it is \(\Delta \Phi = 0.072\).
- At \(G_s = 25\) GeV\(^{-2}\) with the polynomial potential, the dynamical quark mass at \(T=0\) is around 2.5 GeV, and the first-order deconfinement transition is recovered.
- The chiral condensate shows a cusp at \(\theta = \pi/3\) for \(T = 280\) MeV, indicating a Roberge-Weiss transition.

## Context

This work extends studies of the PNJL model at imaginary chemical potential (Sakai 2008) and builds on lattice QCD results for the Roberge-Weiss transition (Roberge 1986, Forcrand 2002, D'Elia 2007). The results are compared with the large \(N_c\) limit of the PNJL model (McLerran 2009) and with lattice calculations of the Roberge-Weiss endpoint for \(N_f=2\) (Bonati 2011) and \(N_f=3\) (Forcrand 2010). The dual parameter approach follows the dressed Polyakov loop introduced by Bilgici (2008) and its PNJL calculation by Kashiwa (2009). The findings enable future model studies to constrain the QCD phase diagram by matching the order of the Roberge-Weiss endpoint and the location of the deconfinement critical endpoint.
