# Structure of excited states of Be-11 studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> The structures of the ground and excited states of Be-11 were studied with a microscopic method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The theoretical results reproduce the abnormal parity of the ground state and predict various kinds of excited states. We suggest a new negative-parity band with a well-developed clustering structure which reaches high-spin states. Focusing on a $2α$ clustering structure, we investigated structure of the ground and excited states. We point out that molecular orbits play important roles for the intruder ground state and the low-lying $2\hbar ω$ states. The features of the breaking of $α$ clusters were also studied with the help of data for Gamow-Teller transitions.

## Key claim

The authors use antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) to show that the abnormal parity (1/2+) ground state of Be-11 and its excited states can be reproduced without assuming inert cores or clusters, but the reproduction of parity inversion depends on the strength of the spin-orbit interaction: the case (1) interaction reproduces the inversion while the case (2) interaction with weaker spin-orbit forces does not, giving the 1/2+ state 1.4 MeV above the 1/2- state.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations after spin-parity projection (VAP) using AMD wave functions, where single-particle states are Gaussian wave packets with complex parameters for spatial centers and spin directions, and the energy is minimized by frictional cooling. They used the MV1 central force (case 3) with a zero-range three-body term and the G3RS spin-orbit force, adopting two parameter sets differing in spin-orbit strength (3700 MeV vs 2500 MeV). Final wave functions for each Jπ state were obtained by superposing and diagonalizing the optimized AMD wave functions from VAP calculations for the lowest Kπ=1/2+ and Kπ=1/2- bands and a second Kπ=3/2- band.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical binding energy of Be-11: 58.2 MeV with case (1) interaction and 54.4 MeV with case (2), compared to experimental 65.48 MeV
- With case (2) interaction (weaker spin-orbit), the 1/2+ state is 1.4 MeV higher than the 1/2- state, failing to reproduce parity inversion
- The Kπ=3/2- band reaches highest spin 15/2- in case (1) and 13/2- in case (2); with a modified Majorana parameter m=0.60, a 17/2- state is obtained as the highest spin
- Log(ft) values for β- decay from Li-11 to Be-11(1/2-1): 5.0 (theory case 1) vs experimental 5.67(4)
- Log(ft) for β- decay from Li-11 to Be-11(3/2-3) at about 10 MeV: 3.9 (theory case 1), corresponding to the experimentally observed state at 8.04 MeV with log(ft)=4.43(8)
- B(E2; 5/2-2 → 3/2-2) = 37 e2 fm4 in the Kπ=3/2- band
- B(E1; 1/2-1 → 1/2+1) = 0.02 e·fm (theoretical), much smaller than experimental 0.116 ± 0.011 e·fm

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of Be isotopes by Dote et al. (earlier work) and the authors' own VAP calculations on C-12, Be-10, Be-12, and Be-14, providing the first microscopic prediction of a Kπ=3/2- rotational band with well-developed clustering structure in Be-11. The calculations reproduce the abnormal parity ground state and systematically account for β-decay strengths from Li-11 and B-11, including the newly discovered state at 8.04 MeV with strong β- transition from Li-11 reported by Aoi et al. (2002). The results enable future studies of cluster breaking and halo effects in neutron-rich Be isotopes, though the authors note that continuum effects and the halo structure of Li-11 are not fully captured in the present bound-state approximation.
