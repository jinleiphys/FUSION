# Effective Temperature of Non-equilibrium Dense Matter in Holography

> We study properties of effective temperature of non-equilibrium steady states by using the anti-de Sitter spacetime/conformal field theory (AdS/CFT) correspondence. We consider non-equilibrium systems with a constant flow of current along an electric field, in which the current is carried by both the doped charges and those pair created by the electric field. We find that the effective temperature agrees with that of the Langevin systems if we take the limit where the pair creation is negligible. The effect of pair creation raises the effective temperature whereas the current by the doped charges contributes to lower the effective temperature in a wide range of the holographic models.

## Key claim

The effective temperature of non-equilibrium steady states in holographic conductor systems agrees with that of Langevin systems only in the limit where pair creation is negligible; more generally, pair creation raises the effective temperature while the current carried by doped charges lowers it, and at finite charge densities the effective temperature can be lower than the heat bath temperature even in models that previously showed higher effective temperatures at zero density.

## Method

The authors study the D3-D7 holographic model of supersymmetric QCD, where the gluon sector acts as a heat bath (mapped to an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole) and the quark sector is mapped to a probe D7-brane. They introduce a chemical potential for quark charge and apply an external electric field, then compute the effective temperature from the Hawking temperature of the "effective horizon" that appears in the open-string metric governing fluctuations of the probe brane fields, extending the zero-mass and zero-density analysis of Nakamura (2013) to finite mass and finite charge density.

## Key numbers

* The effective temperature is defined as \( T_* = \frac{1}{4\pi} \sqrt{a/b} \), where \(a\) and \(b\) are coefficients extracted from the diagonalized open-string metric at the effective horizon \(z=z_*\).
* The effective horizon location is given by \( z_*^2 = \left( \sqrt{e^2+1} - e \right) z_H^2 \), where \( e \equiv |E|/(\frac{\pi}{2}\sqrt{\lambda} T^2) \) is a dimensionless electric field parameter.
* The nonlinear conductivity is \( \sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{N_f^2 N_c^2 T^2}{16\pi^2} \sqrt{e^2+1} \cos^6\theta(z_*) + \frac{d^2}{e^2+1} } \), where \( d \equiv \langle J^t \rangle/(\frac{\pi}{2}\sqrt{\lambda} T^2) \) is the dimensionless charge density.
* The quark mass is related to the boundary value of the scalar field as \( M_q = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\lambda} T \theta_0 \), and the quark condensate is \( \langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle = -\frac{1}{8}\sqrt{\lambda} N_f N_c T^3 \theta_2 \).

## Context

This work extends the holographic analysis of effective temperature in non-equilibrium steady states by Nakamura (2013), which considered the zero-mass and zero-density limits, to include finite mass of charge carriers and finite charge density. The results show that the effective temperature in conductor systems (where current is carried by many charged particles) can differ from that in Langevin systems (where a single particle is dragged), contrary to naive expectations. The findings enable systematic study of how pair creation and doped charge currents separately affect the effective temperature in holographic models, and provide a framework for comparing effective temperatures across different non-equilibrium systems.
