# Wave functions of the Q.Q interaction in terms of unitary 9-j coefficients

> We obtain wave functions for 2 protons and 2 neutrons in the g_{9/2} shell expressed as column vectors with amplitudes D(J_{p},J_{n}). When we use a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction (Q.Q) we get, in many cases, a very strong overlap with wave functions given by a single set of unitary 9j coefficients --U9j= ((jj)^{2j} (jj^{J_{B}} \ (jj)^{J_{p}} (jj)^{J_{n}})^{I}. Here J_{B}=9 for even I T =0 states. For both even and odd T=1 states we take JB equal to 8 whilst for odd I, T=0 we take JB to be 7. We compare the Q.Q results with those of a more realistic interaction.

# Wave functions of the Q.Q interaction in terms of unitary 9-j coefficients

## Key claim

For a system of two protons and two neutrons in the g_{9/2} shell, the wave functions obtained from a quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q) interaction have very strong overlap with wave functions given by a single set of unitary 9j coefficients, but only for low angular momentum states; for higher spins the overlap drops sharply or requires identifying the unitary 9j wave function with excited states rather than ground states.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction for two protons and two neutrons in the g_{9/2} shell (nucleus 96Cd) and expressed the resulting wave functions as column vectors with amplitudes D(J_p, J_n). They computed overlaps between these wave functions and single sets of unitary 9j coefficients, where the parameter J_B was chosen as 9 for even I T=0 states, 8 for both even and odd T=1 states, and 7 for odd I T=0 states. They compared these overlaps with those obtained using a more realistic interaction (CCGI).

## Key numbers

- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=0 T=0: 0.9996
- Overlap of CCGI with unitary 9j for I=0 T=0: 0.9451
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=2 T=0: 0.9999
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=4 T=0: 0.9986
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=6 T=0: 0.9871
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=8 T=0 (yrast): 0.0481; for the first excited I=8 T=0 state: 0.9505
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=1 T=1: 0.9990
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=3 T=1: 0.9974
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=3 T=0: 0.9939
- Overlap of Q.Q with unitary 9j for I=5 T=0: 0.9125
- Overlap of CCGI with unitary 9j for I=12 T=0: 0.9806 (compared to 0.6590 for Q.Q)
- Overlap of CCGI with unitary 9j for I=14 T=0: 0.9722 (compared to 0.8374 for Q.Q)
- Two-body matrix elements for Q.Q (J=0 to J=9, in arbitrary units): 0, 0.1222, 0.3485, 0.6515, 0.9848, 1.2879, 1.4849, 1.4849, 1.1818, 0.4546

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Zamick, Escuderos, Kleszyk, and Hertz (2010-2013) on maximum-J T=0 pairing, where it was found that some wave functions of the maximum-J pairing Hamiltonian were very close to single sets of unitary 9j coefficients. The present paper shows that this result holds for the more physically reasonable quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, not just the artificial E(J_max) interaction (which gives an unphysical I=16 ground state). The authors compare with the realistic CCGI interaction from Coraggio (2012) and find that while overlaps are generally lower for CCGI, they remain above 0.9 in many cases. This work enables the use of simple unitary 9j coefficients as approximate wave functions for low-spin states of two-proton-two-neutron systems in a single j shell, potentially simplifying calculations for N=Z nuclei near 96Cd.
