# Point-Coupling Models from Mesonic Hypermassive Limit and Mean-Field Approaches

> In this work we show how nonlinear point-coupling models, described by a Lagrangian density that presents only terms up to fourth order in the fermion condensate $(\barψψ)$, are derived from a modified meson-exchange nonlinear Walecka model. The derivation can be done through two distinct methods, namely, the hypermassive meson limit within a functional integral approach, and the mean-field approximation in which equations of state at zero temperature of the nonlinear point-coupling models are directly obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonlinear point-coupling (NLPC) models with terms up to fourth order in the fermion condensate can be derived from a modified nonlinear Walecka (MNLW) model, but not from the standard nonlinear Walecka (NLW) model, through either the hypermassive meson limit or the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The authors first use a functional integral approach to show that the standard Walecka model reduces to a linear point-coupling model in the hypermassive meson limit (zero-order expansion in meson kinetic terms). They then construct a modified nonlinear Walecka (MNLW) Lagrangian that includes cubic and quartic scalar meson self-couplings along with cross-terms coupling the scalar field to powers of the fermion scalar density, which allows exact decoupling of the scalar meson field in the hypermassive limit. Finally, they apply the mean-field approximation with the no-sea approximation to the MNLW model and derive equations of state that are shown to be identical to those of the NLPC model.

## Key numbers

- The NLPC Lagrangian density contains terms up to fourth order in the fermion condensate: $\frac{1}{2}{G'_s}^2(\bar{\psi}\psi)^2$, $\frac{A'}{3}(\bar{\psi}\psi)^3$, and $\frac{B'}{4}(\bar{\psi}\psi)^4$
- The effective nucleon mass in the NLPC model is $M^* = M - {G'_s}^2\rho_s - A'\rho_s^2 - B'\rho_s^3$
- For symmetric nuclear matter, the degeneracy factor is $\gamma=4$; for neutron matter, $\gamma=2$
- The vector and scalar densities are defined as $\rho = \frac{\gamma}{2\pi^2}\int_0^{k_F}k^2dk$ and $\rho_s = \frac{\gamma}{2\pi^2}\int_0^{k_F}\frac{M^*}{(k^2+{M^*}^2)^{1/2}}k^2dk$

## Context

This work extends the study of point-coupling models previously presented by the same authors in Lourenco et al. (2011) and Lourenco et al. (2012), where NLPC models were compared with standard nonlinear Walecka models and used to derive generalized Skyrme energy density functionals. The paper addresses the theoretical challenge of constructing universal nuclear effective density functionals, as discussed by Vretenar et al. (2005), by establishing an analytical connection between finite-range meson-exchange models and zero-range point-coupling models. The derivation enables a rigorous understanding of how the NLPC models, which have been successfully applied to finite nuclei by Nikolaus et al. (1992), emerge from meson-exchange theories in the limit where meson degrees of freedom are frozen.
