# Variational Approximations in a Path-Integral Description of Potential Scattering

> Using a recent path integral representation for the T-matrix in nonrelativistic potential scattering we investigate new variational approximations in this framework. By means of the Feynman-Jensen variational principle and the most general ansatz quadratic in the velocity variables -- over which one has to integrate functionally -- we obtain variational equations which contain classical elements (trajectories) as well as quantum-mechanical ones (wave spreading).We analyse these equations and solve them numerically by iteration, a procedure best suited at high energy. The first correction to the variational result arising from a cumulant expansion is also evaluated. Comparison is made with exact partial-wave results for scattering from a Gaussian potential and better agreement is found at large scattering angles where the standard eikonal-type approximations fail.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a variational approximation using a quadratic-plus-linear trial action in a path-integral representation of the T-matrix yields better agreement with exact scattering results at large angles than standard eikonal-type approximations, though the method is best suited at high energy where the iterative numerical solution converges.

## Method

The authors apply the Feynman-Jensen variational principle to a real-time path-integral representation of the nonrelativistic T-matrix for potential scattering, using the most general trial action quadratic in velocity and anti-velocity variables. They derive coupled variational equations for the trajectory and a Green function matrix, which are solved numerically by iteration. The first correction from a cumulant expansion (the second cumulant) is also evaluated to improve the variational result.

## Key numbers

* For scattering from a Gaussian potential at high energy, the variational approximation with quadratic ansatz shows better agreement with exact partial-wave results at large scattering angles where standard eikonal approximations fail (no specific numerical values for the comparison are given in the truncated text).
* The high-energy expansion of the variational solution reproduces the eikonal phase of Abarbanel and Itzykson (1975) at leading order, with first-order correction chi_AI^(1) = -1/2 J^T Sigma_0 J and second-order correction chi_AI^(2) = -1/2 J^T Sigma_0 H Sigma_0 J, where Sigma_0 = -1/(2K) |z-z'| in the "aikonal" representation.
* The imaginary part from the fluctuation term Omega contributes omega_AI^(2) = -1/4 Tr(Sigma_0 H)^2, which combines with Im X_0 to give Im X_0 + Omega = omega_AI^(2) + O(1/K^3), consistent with unitarity requirements.

## Context

This work extends the linear variational ansatz of Carron (2008) to include quadratic terms that describe wave spreading, improving upon the earlier approach where only linear terms in the trial action were varied. The path-integral representation of the T-matrix was previously derived by Rosenfelder (2008), and the method connects to the eikonal expansion of Wallace (1973) and the Glauber theory of high-energy scattering from composite targets. The variational approach enables systematic corrections via cumulant expansion, similar to treatments of the polaron problem by Feynman (1955) and later authors, and could be extended to many-body scattering and field theory applications.
