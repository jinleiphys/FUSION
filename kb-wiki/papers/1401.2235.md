# In-medium η' mass and η'N interaction in vacuum in the linear sigma model

> We investigate the η'N two-body interaction in the context of the η' meson mass modification in the nuclear medium. It has been argued in several articles that the masses of η' and the other pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η) should degenerate in the chiral-symmetric phase. It is expected that the reduction of the mass difference between eta and eta' would take place in the nuclear matter if one assumes that the decrease of the quark condensate at the normal nuclear density occurs with partial restoration of chiral symmetry. At low density, the in-medium self-energy giving the mass modification by the medium effect can be obtained by the η'N two-body T matrix. Thus, we also estimate the η'N interaction strength in vacuum with the linear sigma model which involves the effect of partial restoration of chiral symmetry. In the view of the linear sigma model, we find that the η'N interaction is attractive and generated through the sigma meson exchange.We expect that the interaction is enough strong and for the existence of a bound state of the η'N system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the η'N two-body interaction in vacuum is attractive and strong enough to potentially form a bound state, based on calculations using the linear sigma model that assume a 35% reduction of the quark condensate at normal nuclear density as an input condition.

## Method

The authors use the SU(3) linear sigma model as a chiral effective model, introducing nucleon degrees of freedom explicitly based on chiral symmetry. They fix the model's six free parameters using observed meson masses, meson decay constants, and the assumed 35% reduction of the quark condensate at normal nuclear density. The in-medium meson masses are calculated by introducing symmetric nuclear matter effects through the mean field approximation of the nucleon, and the η'N T-matrix is obtained by solving the single-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation with dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The η' mass reduces by about 80 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- The η mass enhances by about 50 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- The mass difference between η and η' reduces by about 130 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- The η'N bound state binding energy is 6.2 MeV.
- The η'N scattering length is -2.7 fm.
- The η'N effective range is 0.25 fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of η' meson properties in nuclear matter, following earlier suggestions that η' mass reduction in the nuclear medium could be interpreted as an attractive potential (Saito 2007) and that η'-mesic nuclei might exist (Itahashi 2012). The results contradict the weaker interaction strength suggested by some experimental data (Moskal 2009) and provide a theoretical basis for interpreting ongoing experimental searches for η'-mesic nuclei, using the analogy with the Λ(1405) bound state in the K̄N system.
