# Mirror versus naive assignment in chiral models for the nucleon

> In the framework of chiral model(s) based on the linear realization of chiral symmetry, we investigate the low-energy phenomenological properties of the nucleon and its chiral partner (identified with either $N(1535)$ or $N(1650)$) in the naive assignment and compare the results with the ones obtained in the mirror assignment. We find that, within the naive assignment, we are not able to reproduce the experimental value of the isospin-odd scattering length, while the mirror assignment is in good agreement with it for both choices $N(1535)$ and $N(1650)$. The isospin-even scattering length is not yet conclusive in either assignment because it depends crucially on the poorly known scalar mesonic sector. The decay with $Γ_{N(1535)\rightarrow Nη}$ turns out to be far too small in both the naive and mirror assignments, while $Γ_{N(1650)\rightarrow Nη}$ is described correctly by both of them. In conclusions, the mirror assignment with $N(1650)$ as the chiral partner of the nucleon is the favored configuration.

## Key claim

Within the extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) including (axial-)vector mesons, the mirror assignment with N(1650) as the chiral partner of the nucleon is favored over the naive assignment because the naive assignment cannot reproduce the experimental isospin-odd pion-nucleon scattering length, while the mirror assignment agrees with it for both N(1535) and N(1650) candidates.

## Method

The authors constructed the eLSM for both the mirror and naive assignments of baryon chiral partners, including (pseudo)scalar and (axial-)vector meson degrees of freedom. They performed fits of the model parameters to experimental and lattice data including axial coupling constants, decay widths of N* into N pi, and electromagnetic and strong decays of the a1(1260) meson, then computed the isospin-odd and isospin-even pion-nucleon scattering lengths and the decay widths of N* into N eta for both assignments.

## Key numbers

- Isospin-odd scattering length in mirror assignment with N(1535): a0(-) = (6.41 ± 0.17) x 10^-4 MeV^-1, in agreement with experimental value (6.4 ± 0.1) x 10^-4 MeV^-1
- Isospin-odd scattering length in mirror assignment with N(1650): a0(-) = (6.25 ± 0.19) x 10^-4 MeV^-1, also in agreement with experiment
- Isospin-odd scattering length in naive assignment with N(1535): a0(-) = (4.32 ± 0.34) x 10^-4 MeV^-1, not in agreement with experiment
- Decay width Gamma(N(1535) -> N eta) in mirror assignment: 4.9 ± 0.8 MeV, far below experimental value 63.0 ± 10.5 MeV
- Decay width Gamma(N(1650) -> N eta) in mirror assignment: 12.5 ± 2.5 MeV, in agreement with experimental value 15.0 ± 3.0 MeV
- Mirror assignment mass parameter m0 for N(1535): 459 ± 117 MeV; for N(1650): 659 ± 146 MeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term in mirror assignment: sigma_piN ≈ 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the mirror assignment in the eLSM (Gallas 2009) by constructing for the first time the eLSM version for the naive assignment, and by including the a1 meson decay into rho pi as an additional fit constraint. The results contradict the naive assignment as a viable description of baryon chiral partners, while favoring the mirror assignment with N(1650) over N(1535). The approach is complementary to chiral perturbation theory calculations of pion-nucleon scattering (Baru 2010) and can be extended to nonzero temperature and density for studying chiral phase transitions in hadronic matter.
