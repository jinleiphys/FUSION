# Dynamics of one-dimensional correlated nuclear systems within non-equilibrium Green's function theory

> Theory of non-equilibrium Green's function (NGF) provides a practical framework for studying quantum many-body systems out of equilibrium. Extending the previous mean field approach developed for nuclear systems in one dimension with NGF, we introduce isospin degrees of freedom to the Green's functions and incorporate short-range two-body interactions in the second-order self-consistent approximation to correlations, which represents the scattering of momentum orbitals in the Born approximation. We discuss the preparation of a finite nuclear system and examine the impact of correlations on the ground state. We also excite a finite symmetric nuclear system to oscillate in an isovector dipole mode and explore the dissipation effects in the oscillation. Finally, we demonstrate how to boost a slab to a constant and stable motion in a box, based on Galilean covariance of the theory. The studies in this paper lay the ground for the future exploration of collisions of correlated nuclear systems in one dimension.

## Key claim

Extending a previous mean-field non-equilibrium Green's function (NGF) approach for one-dimensional nuclear systems, the authors introduce isospin degrees of freedom and incorporate short-range two-body interactions in the second-order self-consistent Born approximation, but the auxiliary field parameters must be adjusted to recover the mean-field equation of state because the residual interactions alter the total energy.

## Method

The authors solve the Kadanoff-Baym equations for one-dimensional nuclear systems with separate neutron and proton Green's functions, using a second-order Born approximation for the self-energy to include two-body correlations. They prepare finite correlated nuclear systems via adiabatic switching from a harmonic oscillator ground state to the nuclear Hamiltonian, employing a cooling friction potential during the switching period. The two-body potential is chosen to match one-dimensional collision rates to three-dimensional rates in symmetric nuclear matter in the semi-classical limit.

## Key numbers

- The mapping from 1D to 3D densities follows $\rho^{3D}_{n/p} = \xi \, \rho^{1D}_{n/p}$ with $\xi \approx 0.1217\,$fm$^{-2}$
- Mean-field parameters: $t_0 = -2150.1 \,\text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^3$, $t_3 =14562 \,\text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{3(1+d)}$, $d = 0.257$, fitted to symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density
- Symmetry energy parameters: $S_0=20.1\,$MeV, $\rho_0=0.16\,$fm$^{-3}$, $\sigma=0.35$
- Two-body potential parameters: $V_0 = 205.491 \, \text{MeV}$, $\eta = 0.57 \, \text{fm}$
- Discretization interval $\Delta x = 0.57 \, \text{fm}$ and $\Delta x = \eta$
- Cooling friction strength $f_0 = 4-6$ fm$^4/$c
- Variation less than $1 \, \text{MeV}$ in $E/A$ for uniform matter at fixed $\rho$, $\delta$ and $\Delta x$ across box sizes from about $5$ fm to $25$ fm
- Spectral functions obtained by evolving the stationary system over $300 \, \text{fm}/c$

## Context

This work extends the previous mean-field NGF approach for one-dimensional nuclear systems developed by Rios et al. (2011), adding isospin degrees of freedom and two-body correlations beyond the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. The approach contrasts with semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport models used in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, which cannot capture all quantum correlation effects. The results lay the groundwork for future simulations of collisions of correlated nuclear systems in one dimension, and the introduction of isospin degrees of freedom enables future study of the nuclear symmetry energy and isospin-dependent effects.
