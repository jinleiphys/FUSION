# Quasielastic electron-deuteron scattering in the weak binding approximation

> We perform a global analysis of all available electron-deuteron quasielastic scattering data using Q^2-dependent smearing functions that describe inclusive inelastic e-d scattering within the weak binding approximation. We study the dependence of the cross sections on the deuteron wave function and the off-shell extrapolation of the elastic electron-nucleon cross sections, which show particular sensitivity at x >> 1. The excellent overall agreement with data over a large range of Q^2 and x suggests a limited need for effects beyond the impulse approximation, with the possible exception of the very high-x or very low-Q^2 regions, where short-distance effects or scattering from non-nucleonic constituents in the deuteron become more relevant.

# Quasielastic electron-deuteron scattering in the weak binding approximation

## Key claim
The weak binding approximation (WBA) with Q²-dependent smearing functions provides excellent overall agreement with quasielastic electron-deuteron scattering data across a large range of Q² and x, suggesting limited need for effects beyond the impulse approximation, except possibly at very high x or very low Q² where short-distance effects or scattering from non-nucleonic constituents may become relevant.

## Method
The authors performed a global analysis of all available electron-deuteron quasielastic scattering data using Q²-dependent smearing functions derived within the weak binding approximation framework. They computed deuteron structure functions F₁^d and F₂^d as convolutions of nucleon smearing functions with elastic nucleon form factors, using several deuteron wave functions (Paris, WJC-1, CD-Bonn) and two prescriptions (cc1 and cc2) for off-shell extrapolation of the elastic electron-nucleon cross sections. The calculated cross sections were compared with over 2,000 data points from SLAC, MIT-Bates, and Jefferson Lab experiments covering Q² from approximately 0.1 to 10 GeV² and incident energies from 0.2 to 20 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The complete QE data set amounts to over 2,000 data points covering Q² between approximately 0.1 and 10 GeV² for energies between approximately 0.2 and 20 GeV, from x less than 1 to x approximately 2.
- Using smearing functions computed in the high-Q² (γ→1) limit, the peak in the QE cross section would be a factor of approximately 2 too large in the Q² range around 2 GeV² compared to the full finite-Q² results.
- At x=1, the value of γ is approximately 4.3, 2.1, and 1.2 at Q² = 0.2, 1, and 10 GeV², respectively, covering most of the Q² range of the available QE data.
- The average nucleon momentum in the deuteron is approximately 130 MeV, and the typical nucleon virtuality (p²)^(1/2) is only approximately 2% less than the free nucleon mass.
- Results using the Kelly parametrization of elastic nucleon form factors differ from those using the Arrington et al. and Bosted parametrizations by less than 2% for all kinematics.

## Context
This work extends the weak binding approximation formalism previously developed by Kulagin et al. (1994, 2004, 2006) and applied to unpolarized and polarized scattering from the deuteron, as well as to ³He and heavier nuclei. The same smearing functions had been used in extractions of the free neutron structure function F₂^n by Malace et al. (2009, 2010) and in global QCD analyses of parton distribution functions at large x by the CJ collaboration (2010, 2011, 2012) and others. Despite extensive work on smearing functions for deep-inelastic scattering, this is the first direct test of the WBA formalism against the entire set of available QE scattering data, enabling future identification of regions where rescattering effects, meson exchange currents, or non-nucleonic components may need to be incorporated.
