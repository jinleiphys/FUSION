# Energy-weighted sum rule for nuclear density functional theory

> The expressions for the energy-weighted sum rule of the isoscalar and isovector coordinate operators are derived based on the second-order fluctuation of the local densities. Conventional derivation of the Thouless theorem for the energy-weighted sum rule is based on the double commutator of the Hamiltonian, while the present derivation does not assume a Hamiltonian operator and is applicable to nuclear energy density functionals. The expressions include the contribution of the local gauge symmetry breaking of the energy density functional. It is shown that the local gauge invariance of the kinetic and current densities and kinetic pair density is important, while all the other local densities do not contribute to the energy-weighted sum rule of the coordinate operators. The finite-amplitude method calculations are performed and the expressions for the energy-weighted sum rule are numerically examined for the isoscalar and isovector multipole operators up to $L=3$ for selected spherical and axially deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The energy-weighted sum rule for isoscalar and isovector coordinate operators can be derived for nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs) without assuming a Hamiltonian operator, using only the second-order fluctuation of local densities and the condition of local gauge invariance; the derivation shows that only the kinetic and current densities and kinetic pair density contribute, while all other local densities do not affect the sum rule.

## Method

The author derives the energy-weighted sum rule by considering a small fluctuation to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) state generated by a Hermitian operator, expanding the total energy to second order in the fluctuation parameter, and equating the quadratic term to the sum rule expression. For the isoscalar operator, the fluctuation corresponds to a local gauge transformation, and the sum rule is obtained from the transformation properties of the kinetic energy and local densities under local gauge invariance. For the isovector operator, the transformation is not a local gauge transformation, and the sum rule includes contributions from both the kinetic term and the particle-hole EDF terms involving \(\rho_k\tau_k\) and \(\bm{j}_k^2\). The derived expressions are numerically tested using the finite-amplitude method for isoscalar and isovector multipole operators up to \(L=3\) in selected spherical and axially deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the isoscalar operator, the energy-weighted sum rule is \(m_1(\hat{F}^{\rm IS}) = \alpha^2 \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \int d\rb [\nabla f(\rb)]^2 \rho_0(\rb)\) for a local gauge invariant EDF.
- For the isoscalar operator in a general EDF, the sum rule includes additional terms: \(m_1(\hat{F}^{\rm IS}) = \alpha^2 \int d\rb [\nabla f(\rb)]^2 \left\{ \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \rho_0(\rb) + \sum_{k=0}^1 (C^{\tau}_k + C^j_k) \rho_k(\rb)^2 - \sum_{t=n,p} (4 \tilde{C}_t^{\Delta\rho} + \tilde{C}_t^\tau) |\rhot_t(\rb)|^2 \right\}\).
- For the isovector operator, the kinetic contribution is \(m_1^{\rm kin}(\hat{F}^{\rm IV}) = \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \int d\rb [\nabla f(\rb)]^2 [\alpha_n^2\rho_n(\rb) + \alpha_p^2\rho_p(\rb)]\).
- The enhancement factor for the isovector sum rule from the interaction is \(\kappa(\hat{F}^{\rm IV}) = \frac{t_1(2+x_1)+t_2(2+x_2)}{8}(\alpha_n+\alpha_p)^2 \int d\rb [\nabla f(\rb)]^2 \rho_n(\rb)\rho_p(\rb) / m_1^{\rm kin}(\hat{F}^{\rm IV})\).
- Numerical calculations using the finite-amplitude method are performed for multipole operators up to \(L=3\) for selected spherical and axially deformed nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the Thouless theorem, originally proved for Hamiltonians (Thouless, 1961) and later for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus quasiparticle RPA (PhysRevC.66.024309, 2002), to nuclear density functional theory where no Hamiltonian operator exists. The derivation relies on local gauge invariance rather than double commutators, generalizing previous numerical observations for the SLy4 EDF (PhysRevC.91.044323, 2015) that time-odd current terms are necessary to recover sum-rule values. The results enable verification of sum rules for modern EDFs such as UNEDF functionals (PhysRevC.82.024313, 2010; PhysRevC.85.024304, 2012; PhysRevC.89.054314, 2014) and provide a framework for testing local gauge invariance in future EDF developments.
