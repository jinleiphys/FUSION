# Chiral instabilities & the fate of chirality imbalance in non-Abelian plasmas

> We present a first principles study of chiral plasma instabilities and axial charge transfer in non-Abelian plasmas with a strong gauge-matter coupling $g^2N_f=64$, by performing $3+1$ D real-time classical-statistical lattice simulation with dynamical fermions. We explicitly demonstrate for the first time that -- unlike in an Abelian plasma -- the transfer of chirality from the matter sector to the gauge fields occurs predominantly due to topological sphaleron transitions. We elaborate on the similarities and differences of the axial charge dynamics in cold Abelian $U(1)$ and non-Abelian $SU(2)$ plasmas, and comment on the implications of our findings for the study of anomalous transport phenomena, such as the chiral magnetic effect in QCD matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that in non-Abelian plasmas, unlike in Abelian plasmas, the transfer of chirality from fermions to gauge fields occurs predominantly via topological sphaleron transitions rather than through an inverse turbulent cascade, and that this process completely erases the initial chirality imbalance without generating long-range coherent magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional real-time classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge theory coupled to dynamical fermions, solving the Dirac equation for fermion fields and the Yang-Mills equations for non-Abelian gauge fields with strong gauge-matter coupling g²N_f=64. They initialized the system with a large helicity chemical potential μ_h at low temperature T/μ_h=1/8, and tracked the non-equilibrium evolution of chirality from the fermion sector to the gauge sector. To isolate topological contributions to the Chern-Simons number, they performed gauge covariant cooling of the SU(2) gauge field configurations to remove finite-energy excitations while preserving topology.

## Key numbers

- Coupling strength: g²N_f = 64, implementing strong back-reaction of matter fields to gauge fields
- Initial temperature to helicity chemical potential ratio: T/μ_h = 1/8
- Lattice sizes: N_s = 24 to 40 with lattice spacings μ_h a_s = 0.8 to 1.0
- Fermion mass: m a_s = 5×10⁻⁴, satisfying m ≪ μ_h
- Primary unstable modes appear at momenta |p|/μ_h ∼ 0.5 with largest growth rates
- Unstable growth saturates when occupation numbers reach n_B ∼ 1/g² at μ_h t ∼ 300
- Residual violations of the anomaly relation below ∼5% level for μ_h a_s = 0.8 at late times μ_h t ≳ 500
- Time interval for topological charge measurement: Δt/a_s = 7.5

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral plasma instabilities in Abelian U(1) plasmas by Mace (2019) to non-Abelian SU(2) plasmas, and contrasts the fundamentally different chirality transfer mechanisms between the two cases. While Abelian plasmas exhibit an inverse turbulent cascade generating large-scale helical magnetic fields as shown by Hirono (2015) and Rogachevskii (2017), the authors demonstrate that non-Abelian plasmas instead erase chirality through correlated sphaleron transitions, with implications for understanding the Chiral Magnetic Effect in QCD matter and baryogenesis scenarios in the early universe.
