# Extracting the photoproduction cross section off the neutron gn-->pi-p from deuteron data with FSI effects

> The incoherent pion photoproduction reaction gd-->pi-pp is considered theoretically in a wide energy region Eth =< Eg =< 2700 MeV. The model applied contains the impulse approximation as well as the NN- and piN-FSI amplitudes. The aim of the paper is to study a reliable way for getting the information on elementary gn-->pi-p reaction cross section beyond the impulse approximation for gd-->pi-pp. For the elementary gN-->piN, NN-->NN, and piN-->piN amplitudes, the results of the GW DAC are used. There are no additional theoretical constraints. The calculated cross section dSigma/dOmega(gd->pi-pp) are compared with existing data. The procedure used to extract information on the differential cross section dSigma/dOmega(gn-->pi-p) on the neutron from the deuteron data using the FSI correction factor R is discussed. The calculations for R versus pi-p CM angle θ_1 of the outgoing pion are performed at different photon-beam energies with kinematical cuts for "quasi-free" process gn-->pi-p. The results show a sizeable FSI effect R \neq 1 from S-wave part of pp-FSI at small angles close to θ_1 ~ 0: this region narrows as the photon energy increases. At larger angles, the effect is small (|R-1|<<1) and agrees with estimations of FSI in the Glauber approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the final-state interaction (FSI) correction factor \(R\) for extracting the \(\gamma n \to \pi^- p\) cross section from deuteron data is significantly different from unity only at small pion angles near \(\theta_1 \sim 0\) due to the \(S\)-wave part of \(pp\)-FSI, while at larger angles the FSI effect is small (\(|R-1| \ll 1\)) and consistent with Glauber approach estimates, provided kinematical cuts for the quasi-free process are applied.

## Method

The authors model the \(\gamma d \to \pi^- pp\) reaction using three leading amplitudes: impulse approximation (IA), \(pp\)-FSI, and \(\pi N\)-FSI, with elementary \(\gamma N \to \pi N\), \(NN \to NN\), and \(\pi N \to \pi N\) amplitudes taken from the GW DAC (SAID) partial-wave analyses. The deuteron wave function from the CD-Bonn potential (including S- and D-wave components) is used, and the FSI correction factor \(R\) is defined as the ratio of the full differential cross section to the IA-only differential cross section, computed as a function of the \(\pi^- p\) center-of-mass angle \(\theta_1\) at various photon energies with quasi-free kinematical cuts.

## Key numbers

* Photon energy range studied: from threshold \(E_{th}\) up to \(E_\gamma = 2700\) MeV.
* At \(E_\gamma = 500\) MeV, the \(S\)-wave part of \(pp\)-FSI dominates the FSI contribution at small angles (Fig. 4b).
* The FSI effect is sizeable (\(R \neq 1\)) at small angles \(\theta_1 \sim 0\), and this region narrows as the photon energy increases; at larger angles \(|R-1| \ll 1\).
* The \(\pi N\)-FSI contribution is very small even in the \(\Delta(1232)3/2^+\) region (e.g., at \(E_\gamma = 370\) and 500 MeV).
* The IA cross section at zero angle (\(\theta = 0\)) includes a Pauli correction factor that approaches \(2/3\) when the momentum transfer \(\Delta \to 0\) and \(F_S(\Delta) \to 1\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of FSI in \(\gamma d\) reactions by Laget (1978, 1981) and more recent studies by Fix, Levchuk, and Schwamb, which used MAID or SAID amplitudes for the elementary \(\gamma N\) interaction. The present analysis uses the GW DAC (SAID) amplitudes consistently for all elementary processes and focuses on the specific \(\gamma d \to \pi^- pp\) channel to extract the neutron cross section. The results enable improved extraction of \(\gamma n \to \pi^- p\) data from JLab CLAS experiments, which in turn will constrain partial-wave analyses of nucleon resonance photo-decay amplitudes on the neutron, particularly for states like \(N(1675)5/2^-\) that couple more strongly to neutrons than protons.
