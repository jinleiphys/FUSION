# Phi meson production cross sections in pion-baryon and baryon-baryon interactions

> Phi meson production in pion-baryon and baryon-baryon interactions near the production threshold is studied in a boson-exchange model. Parameters in this model are either taken from the Bonn model for the nucleon-nucleon potential or determined from the measured phi meson decay width and the experimental data for the reactions $π^-p\arr ϕn$ and $pp\arr ppϕ$. The isospin-averaged cross sections for the reactions $πN\arr ϕN$, $πΔ\arr ϕN$, $NN\arr NNϕ$, which are needed in transport models to study phi meson production in heavy-ion collisions at subthreshold energies, have been evaluated. For the reactions $NΔ\arr NNϕ$ and $ΔΔ\arr NNϕ$, which become singular when the exchanged pion is on-shell, the Peierls method is used to calculate their cross sections. We find that the cross section for the reaction $πN\arrϕN$ is much larger than the other cross sections.

# Phi meson production cross sections in pion-baryon and baryon-baryon interactions

## Key claim

The boson-exchange model, with parameters taken from the Bonn potential and fitted to existing data for π⁻p → φn and pp → ppφ, provides isospin-averaged cross sections for φ meson production in πN → φN, πΔ → φN, NN → NNφ, NΔ → NNφ, and ΔΔ → NNφ reactions near threshold; the cross section for πN → φN is found to be much larger than the other cross sections, but the results for NΔ → NNφ and ΔΔ → NNφ rely on the Peierls method to remove singularities from on-shell pion exchange and are therefore model-dependent.

## Method

The authors used a boson-exchange model with Feynman diagrams involving ρ-meson exchange for πN → φN and πΔ → φN, and combined π and ρ exchange for NN → NNφ, NΔ → NNφ, and ΔΔ → NNφ. Coupling constants and cut-off parameters at the πNN, ρNN, and ρNΔ vertices were taken from the Bonn one-boson-exchange model (Model II), while the πφρ coupling constant was determined from the measured φ → πρ decay width (≈ 0.6 MeV) and two additional cut-off parameters at the πρφ vertex were fitted to experimental data for π⁻p → φn and pp → ppφ. For the reactions NΔ → NNφ and ΔΔ → NNφ, where the exchanged pion can go on-shell and produce a singularity, the Peierls method was used to regularize the cross section by introducing a complex four-momentum for the initial-state Δ resonance via its finite width (Γ_Δ ≈ 120 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant f_{πρφ} ≈ 1.04 was determined from the measured φ → πρ decay width of ≈ 0.6 MeV.
- The cut-off parameter Λ_{πρφ}^ρ = 1.2 GeV was obtained by fitting to experimental data for π⁻p → φn.
- The cut-off parameter Λ_{πρφ}^π = 0.95 GeV was obtained by fitting to experimental data for pp → ppφ at p_lab ≈ 10 GeV.
- The cross section for πN → φN is about a factor of five larger than that for πΔ → φN.
- Near threshold, the NΔ → NNφ cross section is about a factor of five larger than the NN → NNφ cross section, while the ΔΔ → NNφ cross section is only slightly larger than the latter.
- The Δ resonance width used in the Peierls method is Γ_Δ ≈ 120 MeV.

## Context

This work extends boson-exchange models previously applied to pion (Engel 1990), kaon (Wu and Ko 1995; Laget 1990; Li and Ko 1995), eta meson (Peters 1995), and dilepton (Grein 1975) production in nucleon-nucleon interactions to the case of φ meson production. The calculated cross sections are intended as inputs for transport models of heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies, where subthreshold φ production is being studied experimentally by the FOPI collaboration; the authors note that a previous study (Li and Ko 1995a) using only kaon-antikaon annihilation gave a φ/K⁻ ratio of about 1%, much below the preliminary FOPI value of about 10%, motivating the inclusion of baryon-baryon and pion-baryon channels. The results also enable future transport-model studies of dilepton spectra from φ decays, relevant for the HADES collaboration experiments at SIS energies.
