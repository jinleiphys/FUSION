# Low Energy Proton-Deuteron Scattering with a Coulomb-Modified Faddeev Equation

> A modified version of the Faddeev three-body equation to accommodate the Coulomb interaction, which was used in the study of three-nucleon bound states, is applied to the proton-deuteron scattering problem at energies below the three-body breakup threshold. A formal derivation of the equation in a time-independent scattering theory is given. Numerical results for phase shift parameters are presented to be compared with those of another methods and results of the phase shift analysis. Differential cross sections and nucleon analyzing powers are calculated with the effects of three-nucleon forces, and these results are compared with recent experimental data. The difference between the nucleon analyzing power in proton-deuteron scattering and that in neutron-deuteron scattering is discussed.

## Key claim

The Coulomb-modified Faddeev equation, previously used for three-nucleon bound states, is applied to proton-deuteron scattering below the three-body breakup threshold by introducing an auxiliary Coulomb potential that cancels the long-range behavior of the proton-proton Coulomb interaction, making the kernel short-range and tractable with iterative methods.

## Method

The author derives a Coulomb-modified Faddeev equation in a time-independent scattering formalism by adding an auxiliary Coulomb potential \(u^c_{\beta,\gamma}(y_\beta)\) that acts between the spectator and the center-of-mass of the pair, such that the difference \(v^c_\alpha(x_\alpha) - u^c_{\beta,\gamma}(y_\beta)\) falls off as \(O(x_\alpha^{-2})\) when combined with a Faddeev component. The resulting equation is solved iteratively using the Method of Continued Fractions, with the channel Coulomb Green's function expressed analytically via regular and irregular Coulomb functions, and the short-range part handled by solving a Schrödinger-like differential equation in the two-body subsystem coordinate.

## Key numbers

- For the Malfliet-Tjon I-III potential, the \(S\)-wave doublet phase shift for \(nd\) scattering at \(E_n^\mathrm{Lab} = 1.5\) MeV is \(-20.6^\circ\) (this work) versus \(-20.7^\circ\) (ref. Ch89); for \(pd\) scattering at \(E_p^\mathrm{Lab} = 1.5\) MeV, the doublet phase shift is \(-16.2^\circ\) (both this work and ref. Ch89).
- For the AV18 potential at \(E_N^\mathrm{Lab} = 1\) MeV, the \(nd\) quartet \(S\)-wave phase shift (\({}^{4}S_{3/2}\)) is \(-46.9^\circ\) (this work, \(J_\mathrm{max}=3\)) versus \(-46.7^\circ\) (PHH method, ref. Ki96); the corresponding \(pd\) value is \(-36.9^\circ\) (this work) versus \(-37.0^\circ\) (PHH).
- For the AV18 potential at \(E_N^\mathrm{Lab} = 3\) MeV, the \(nd\) quartet \(S\)-wave phase shift is \(-70.1^\circ\) (this work, \(J_\mathrm{max}=3\)) versus \(-69.9^\circ\) (PHH); the \(pd\) value is \(-63.0^\circ\) (this work) versus \(-63.1^\circ\) (PHH).
- Differences between this work and the configuration-space Faddeev calculations of Ch89 for MT I-III are on the order of 1% at all energies tabulated.

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb-modified Faddeev approach of Sasakawa and Sawada (1979), previously applied to the \({}^{3}\)He bound state, to the proton-deuteron scattering continuum below breakup. The method is contrasted with alternative approaches: the screening and renormalization method in momentum space (Alt et al., 1978; Berthold et al., 1990; Alt et al., 1996, 1998), the configuration-space partial differential equation approach (Chen et al., 1989; Friar et al., 1996; Chen et al., 2001), the three-potential formalism with Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion (Papp et al., 1997), and the Kohn variational method with Pair-correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic basis (Kievsky et al., 1995, 1996). The results enable accurate calculation of differential cross sections and nucleon analyzing powers with three-nucleon forces for comparison with recent experimental data, and allow study of the difference between analyzing powers in proton-deuteron versus neutron-deuteron scattering.
