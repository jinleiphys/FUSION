# Mean-field nuclear structure calculations on a Basis-Spline Galerkin lattice

> Our goal is to carry out high-precision nuclear structure calculations in connection with Radioactive Ion Beam Facilities. The main challenge for the theory of drip line nuclei is that the outermost nucleons are weakly bound (implying a large spatial distribution) and that these states are strongly coupled to the particle continuum. For these reasons, the traditional basis expansion methods fail to converge. We overcome these problems by representing the nuclear Hamiltonian on a lattice utilizing the Galerkin method with Basis-Spline test functions. We discuss tests of the numerical method and applications to the deformed shell model, HF+BCS and HFB mean field theories.

## Key claim

The paper claims that representing the nuclear Hamiltonian on a coordinate-space lattice using the Galerkin method with Basis-Spline test functions overcomes the convergence failures of traditional harmonic oscillator basis expansion methods for drip-line nuclei, where outermost nucleons are weakly bound and strongly coupled to the particle continuum.

## Method

The authors represent the nuclear Hamiltonian on a two-dimensional cylindrical-coordinate lattice using the Galerkin method with fifth-order Basis-Spline test functions, transforming differential operator equations into matrix-vector equations. They test this numerical method on the deformed Woods-Saxon shell model, on self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme-M* interaction and BCS pairing, and outline the extension to full Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory.

## Key numbers

- For $^{16}$O in Hartree-Fock + BCS with the SkM* force, the 2D Spline-Galerkin code with lattice spacing $\Delta=0.625$ fm yields a total binding energy of -127.48 MeV, compared to -127.73 MeV from a 1D radial finite difference code with $\Delta=0.025$ fm.
- For $^{16}$O, the 2D code reproduces the neutron s1/2 single-particle energy at -33.29 MeV (1D: -33.31 MeV), neutron p3/2 at -19.86 MeV (1D: -19.88 MeV), and neutron p1/2 at -13.53 MeV (1D: -13.55 MeV).
- For $^{16}$O, the 2D code gives proton s1/2 at -29.72 MeV (1D: -29.74 MeV), proton p3/2 at -16.45 MeV (1D: -16.48 MeV), and proton p1/2 at -10.26 MeV (1D: -10.27 MeV).
- The 2D Spline-Galerkin code achieves this accuracy despite a lattice spacing 25 times larger than the 1D code.
- In a test for $^{11}$Li, the last bound neutron separation energy was set to 10 keV to demonstrate the method's ability to represent a weakly bound state with large spatial extent.
- Single-particle neutron levels for $^{40}$Ca were calculated for quadrupole deformations from $\beta_2=-1.25$ (strong oblate) to $\beta_2=+2.25$ (extreme prolate), covering configurations up to a symmetric fission shape.

## Context

This work extends earlier three-dimensional Hartree-Fock codes using Basis-Spline discretization developed by Umar et al. (1994) and addresses the failure of harmonic oscillator basis expansions for drip-line nuclei noted by Nazarewicz et al. (1994). The method enables high-precision mean-field calculations for exotic nuclei near the proton and neutron drip lines, including neutron halos, neutron skins, and proton radioactivity, and is intended to support interpretation of experiments at Radioactive Ion Beam Facilities.
