# Estimating nuclear equation of state parameters away from saturation density

> We explore the density variation of the correlation coefficient of the key parameters of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) with the bulk and crustal properties of neutron stars. The analysis was performed using two diverse sets of nuclear effective interaction theories based on nonrelativistic Skyrme-Hartree Fock model and relativistic mean field model. We find that the commonly studied EoS parameters, namely the isoscalar incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter $K(ρ)$ and the isovector slope of symmetry energy $L(ρ)$, reveal consistently maximum correlation with the radius, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia all around twice the saturation density. We find even more tighter and robust correlations beyond the saturation density for constructed parameter $η= [KL^2]^{1/3}$ allowing the possibility to impose stringent constraints on high-density $K(ρ)$ and $L(ρ)$. Extensive correlation analysis of the EoS parameters with the radius and tidal deformability bounds from the gravitational wave events and recent pulsar observations allow us to provide reliable constraints on the central values of $K(ρ_0) \approx 240$ MeV and $L(ρ_0) \approx 48$ MeV at saturation density and $K(1.6ρ_0) \approx 332^{+88}_{-50}$ MeV and $L(1.6ρ_0) \approx 122^{+26}_{-18}$ MeV at 1.6 times the saturation density. The crust-core transition density and the crustal fraction of moment of inertia are shown to correlate moderately with $L(ρ)$ and $η(ρ)$ near the subsaturation density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the strongest correlations between neutron star observables (radius, tidal deformability, moment of inertia) and the nuclear equation of state parameters, the isoscalar incompressibility \(K(\rho)\) and the isovector slope of symmetry energy \(L(\rho)\), occur consistently around twice the saturation density (\(2\rho_0\)), not at saturation density itself, and that a constructed parameter \(\eta = [K L^2]^{1/3}\) yields even tighter and more robust correlations at supra-saturation densities.

## Method

The authors performed a Pearson correlation analysis between neutron star bulk properties (radius, tidal deformability, moment of inertia) and crustal properties (core-crust transition density, crustal fraction of moment of inertia) and the density-dependent EoS parameters \(K(\rho)\), \(L(\rho)\), and \(\eta(\rho) = [K(\rho)L^2(\rho)]^{1/3}\). They used 28 diverse nuclear effective interaction models: a set of nonrelativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) models and a set of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models. Neutron star structural properties were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the slow-rotation approximation for moment of inertia, while the tidal deformability was computed from the tidal Love number.

## Key numbers

* The strongest correlation between radius and \(L(\rho)\) is \({\cal C}[R_{1.0},L(\rho)] = 0.93\) at a density \(\rho = 0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\) (about \(1.6\rho_0\)) for a \(1.0 M_\odot\) neutron star.
* The strongest correlation between radius and \(K(\rho)\) is \({\cal C}[R_{1.8},K(\rho)] = 0.91\) at about \(\rho = 1.6\rho_0\) for a \(1.8 M_\odot\) neutron star.
* The strongest correlation between radius and \(\eta(\rho)\) is \({\cal C}[R,\eta(\rho)] \approx 0.97\) over a wider density range around \(\rho = 0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The central values of the EoS parameters at saturation density are constrained to \(K(\rho_0) \approx 240\) MeV and \(L(\rho_0) \approx 48\) MeV.
* At \(1.6\rho_0\), the constrained values are \(K(1.6\rho_0) \approx 332^{+88}_{-50}\) MeV and \(L(1.6\rho_0) \approx 122^{+26}_{-18}\) MeV.
* The radii for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star predicted by the EoS models range from \(R_{1.4} \approx 11.6\) to \(14.6\) km.

## Context

This work extends prior correlation studies that were mostly confined to saturation density or below, such as those by Alam (2016), Burgio (2021), and Ducoin (2011), by systematically scanning the density dependence of correlations up to supra-saturation densities. The use of the combined parameter \(\eta = [K L^2]^{1/3}\) builds on earlier work by Sotani (2013, 2015) and Silva (2016) who found strong correlations at saturation density. The results enable more reliable extraction of high-density EoS parameters by linking them to neutron star radius and tidal deformability measurements from gravitational wave events (GW170817, GW190814) and NICER pulsar observations, and provide a pathway to constrain the incompressibility and symmetry energy slope at densities beyond saturation.
