# Extraction of Transition Form Factors for Nucleon Resonances within a Coupled-Channels Model

> We explain how an analytic continuation we have developed recently is applied to determine the residues of the nucleon resonance poles within a dynamical coupled-channel model of meson-baron reactions. A procedure for evaluating the electromagnetic N-N^* transition form factors at resonance poles is developed. Illustrative results of the obtained N^* -> pi N, gamma N transition form factors for P_{11}, P_{33}, and D_{13} nucleon resonances are presented and compared with previous results.

# Extraction of Transition Form Factors for Nucleon Resonances within a Coupled-Channels Model

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their analytic continuation method, applied within the dynamical coupled-channel (MSL) model, can extract resonance pole residues and electromagnetic N-N* transition form factors at the pole positions, but the extracted helicity amplitudes for P11 resonances have large imaginary parts that make direct comparison with previous Breit-Wigner-based analyses ambiguous.

## Method

The authors apply an analytic continuation technique to the MSL dynamical coupled-channel model of meson-baryon reactions, deforming integration contours in the complex energy plane to access unphysical sheets where resonance poles reside. The residues of the N* propagator poles are determined from the zeros of the determinant of the dressed N* propagator, and the electromagnetic transition form factors are evaluated by combining these residues with the dressed vertex functions at the pole positions. The method is illustrated for the P33, D13, and P11 partial waves using the JLMS model parameters previously fitted to πN and γN reaction data.

## Context

This work extends the analytic continuation method developed by Suzuki, Sato, and Lee (2009) for finding resonance poles in the MSL coupled-channel model, which was previously applied to extract 14 nucleon resonances from πN reactions. The results for P33 and D13 residues agree well with Particle Data Group values and with analyses by the GWU/VPI group, Cutkosky and Wang, and the Juelich group, while the P11 channel shows significant model-dependent differences. The method enables future extraction of residues for unstable channels (πΔ, ρN, σN) and provides a framework for comparing pole-extracted form factors with those from Breit-Wigner parametrizations of single-channel K-matrix amplitudes.
