# Emergence of Classicality in Quantum Phase Transitions

> We show that classicality emerges during quantum phase transitions due to parametric interactions without coupling to environments. The Wigner functions are explicitly calculated for the Gaussian vacuum, number, and thermal states of a free scalar field that describes the spinodal instability regime. The Wigner functions are sharply peaked around their classical trajectories during the phase transition and exhibit classical correlation only for unstable long wavelength modes but retain quantum coherence for short wavelength modes. Thus classicality emerges from the quantum evolution of phase transitions without a classical order parameter. We define a quantal ordering parameter that is linear in the field variable and satisfies the classical field equation.

## Key claim

Classicality emerges during quantum phase transitions through parametric interactions without coupling to an environment, but only for unstable long wavelength modes; short wavelength modes retain quantum coherence, and full classicality (including decoherence) requires mode-mixing between long and short wavelength modes.

## Method

The authors model a second-order phase transition using a massive scalar field whose mass changes sign (quenched transition), decomposing the field into decoupled Fourier modes described by time-dependent harmonic oscillators. They construct exact Fock space states (vacuum, number, and thermal states) using the Liouville-von Neumann approach, compute the corresponding density matrices and Wigner functions, and apply quantitative measures of classical correlation and quantum decoherence from Anderson (1995). They then introduce a linearly coupled two-mode model (long wavelength plus short wavelength) to study mode-mixing effects analytically.

## Key numbers

- The measure of classical correlation for long wavelength modes decreases exponentially to zero as the spinodal instability continues, with the amplitude square of the long wavelength mode growing as \(\varphi_{\alpha, U_f}^* \varphi_{\alpha, U_f} = \frac{1}{2 \tilde{\omega}_{\alpha, f}} [\cosh (2 r_{\alpha}) \cosh (2 \tilde{\omega}_{\alpha, f} t) + \sin \delta_{\alpha} \sinh (2 r_{\alpha}) \sinh (2 \tilde{\omega}_{\alpha, f}t)]\).
- The measure of quantum decoherence for the massive scalar field remains constant at \(\delta_{QD} = \frac{1}{2} \tanh(\beta \omega_{\alpha,i}/2)\) throughout the phase transition, with \(\delta_{QD} = 1/2\) for the vacuum state.
- For the linearly coupled two-mode model after the phase transition, the measure of quantum decoherence to order \(\lambda^4\) is \(\delta_{QD} = \frac{\lambda}{2} \sqrt{\frac{5\omega_{H, f}^2(\tilde{\omega}_{S, f} - \omega_{H, f})}{2 \tilde{\omega}_{S, f} (\tilde{\omega}_{S, f}^2 + \omega_{H, f}^2)^2}}\).
- The constant area of the Wigner function ellipse for each mode is \(A_{\alpha} = \coth(\beta \omega_{\alpha,i}/2)\), implying isentropic evolution with entropy \(S_{\alpha} = \ln \coth(\beta \omega_{\alpha,i}/2)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quantum-to-classical transitions in phase transitions previously studied by Lombardo (1997) and Boyanovsky et al. (1995), who used a classical order parameter approach. The paper builds on the authors' prior work (Kim and Lee, 1999) introducing a quantum phase transition model without a classical order parameter, and applies the quantitative measures of classical correlation and quantum decoherence developed by Anderson (1995). The results show that while parametric amplification alone gives classical correlation for unstable modes, full decoherence requires mode-mixing as in the self-interacting \(\Phi^4\) theory, enabling a quantal ordering parameter that satisfies the classical field equation.
