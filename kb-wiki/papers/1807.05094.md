# Forward rapidity isolated photon production in proton-nucleus collisions

> We calculate isolated photon production at forward rapidities in proton-nucleus collisions in the Color Glass Condensate framework. Our calculation uses dipole cross sections solved from the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with an initial condition fit to deep inelastic scattering data and extended to nuclei with an optical Glauber procedure that introduces no additional parameters beyond the basic nuclear geometry. We present predictions for future forward RHIC and LHC measurements. The predictions are also compared to updated results for the nuclear modification factors for pion production, Drell-Yan dileptons and $J/ψ$ mesons in the same forward kinematics, consistently calculated in the same theoretical framework. We find that leading order, running coupling high energy evolution in the CGC picture leads to a significant nuclear suppression at forward rapidities. This nuclear suppression is stronger for photons than for pions. We also discuss how this might change with next-to-leading order high energy evolution.

## Key claim
The authors find that leading-order, running-coupling high-energy evolution in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework predicts a significant nuclear suppression for forward isolated photon production in proton-nucleus collisions, and that this suppression is stronger for photons than for pions. However, they note that next-to-leading order corrections to the high-energy evolution might substantially alter this conclusion, potentially freezing the anomalous dimension and reducing the suppression.

## Method
The calculation uses dipole cross sections obtained from the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation, with an initial condition (the "MV^e" parametrization) fit to inclusive HERA deep inelastic scattering data at momentum fraction x0=0.01. The dipole amplitude is extended from protons to nuclear targets using an optical Glauber procedure with standard Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry, introducing no additional free parameters. Photon production is computed via a quark (described by collinear parton distributions) passing through the target color field and emitting a real photon, with the cross section regulated by an isolation cut of R=0.4 or R=0.1 in the rapidity-azimuth plane.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear modification factor R_pA for isolated photons at RHIC energy sqrt(s)=200 GeV shows suppression at transverse momenta small compared to the saturation scale of the target.
- Nuclear modification factor R_pA for isolated photons at LHC energy sqrt(s)=8 TeV shows suppression extending to much higher photon transverse momenta due to high-energy evolution.
- The anomalous dimension gamma in the small-distance behavior of the dipole amplitude N(r) ~ r^{2gamma} evolves from gamma=1 in the initial condition to gamma approximately 0.6-0.8 under leading-order BK evolution.
- Collinearly resummed NLO BK evolution practically freezes the anomalous dimension to its initial value of gamma=1.

## Context
This work extends the CGC framework to isolated photon production at forward rapidities, building on the dipole formalism of Gelis (2002) and the MV^e parametrization of Lappi (2013). It provides a consistent set of predictions for multiple probes (photons, pions, J/psi mesons, Drell-Yan dileptons, D-mesons) within the same theoretical framework, enabling comparisons that can distinguish saturation effects from cold nuclear matter energy loss models such as that of Arleo (2015). The results enable future tests at RHIC and LHC, and highlight the need for full NLO calculations to resolve the current leading-order ambiguities in the interpretation of nuclear suppression.
