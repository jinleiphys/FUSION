# The relation between cross-section, decay width and imaginary potential of heavy quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma

> Heavy quarkonium suppression was proposed long ago as a signal of the formation of a deconfined phase in heavy ion collisions. Originally the mechanism responsible for this suppression was thought to be color screening. However perturbative computations and recent lattice studies suggest the existence of an imaginary part of the potential which could have a more important role for suppression than screening. In this work we review some general aspects of effective field theories for heavy quarkonium in the medium and discuss the physical phenomena behind the imaginary part of the potential and the decay width of heavy quarkonium and their corresponding cross sections.

# The relation between cross-section, decay width and imaginary potential of heavy quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The paper argues that effective field theory (EFT) methods can systematically relate the imaginary part of the heavy quarkonium potential, the decay width, and the cross-section for dissociation in a quark-gluon plasma, but the dominant dissociation mechanism depends on the relative size of the Debye mass \(m_D\) and the binding energy \(E\): gluo-dissociation dominates when \(E \gg m_D\), while inelastic parton scattering dominates when \(m_D \gg E\).

## Method
The author reviews the application of non-relativistic effective field theories (NRQCD and pNRQCD) extended to finite temperature to describe heavy quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma. The decay width is computed from the imaginary part of the singlet self-energy in pNRQCD, and the corresponding cross-sections are extracted using thermal cutting rules. Two regimes are treated separately: gluo-dissociation (for \(E \gg m_D\)) and inelastic parton scattering (for \(m_D \gg E\)), with explicit cross-section formulas derived for the 1S state.

## Key numbers
- The Bohr radius of \(\Upsilon(1S)\) is given as \(1/a_0 \sim 1355\ \text{MeV}\) and of \(J/\Psi\) as \(1/a_0 \sim 770\ \text{MeV}\) (both from Pineda thesis).
- The binding energy \(E\) for \(\Upsilon(1S)\) and \(J/\Psi\) is \(\sim 350\ \text{MeV}\) (from Pineda thesis).
- The Debye mass squared is \(m_D^2 = \frac{N_c g^2 T^2}{3} + \frac{g^2 T_F N_F}{3}\left(T^2 + \frac{3\mu^2}{\pi^2}\right)\).
- For gluo-dissociation at \(T \gg E\) and zero chemical potential, the decay width correction is \(\delta\Gamma_n = \frac{1}{3}N_C^2 C_F \alpha_s^3 T - \frac{16}{3m} C_F \alpha_s T E_n + \frac{4}{3} N_C C_F \alpha_s^2 T \frac{2}{m n^2 a_0}\).
- For inelastic parton scattering of the 1S state, the cross-section is \(\sigma_{1S}(k,m_D) = 8\pi C_F \alpha_s^2 N_F a_0^2 \left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2\log\left(\frac{2}{x}\right) + \log\left(\frac{y^2}{1+y^2}\right) - \frac{1}{y^2}\log(1+y^2)\right)\), where \(x = m_D a_0\) and \(y = k a_0\).

## Context
This work extends the program of applying effective field theories to heavy quarkonium in medium, building on the foundational proposal of quarkonium suppression as a deconfinement signal by Matsui and Satz (1986) and the perturbative computation of an imaginary potential by Laine et al. (2006). The EFT approach was initiated for finite temperature by Escobedo (2008) and Brambilla (2008), and the specific results for gluo-dissociation and inelastic parton scattering cross-sections were developed in Brambilla (2011) and Brambilla (2013). The key advance is that the cross-sections derived from EFT are independent of the medium's nature as long as it is isotropic and homogeneous, making them applicable to plasmas with finite chemical potential such as those expected in the CBM experiment.
