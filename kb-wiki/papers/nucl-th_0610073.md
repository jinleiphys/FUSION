# Semi-Classical Approach to the Two-Level Pairing Model --Various Aspects of Phase Change--

> Aspects of the phase change of the two-level pairing model are investigated in the semi-classical treatment by using the variational approch with the mixed-mode coherent state. In the classical limit, $hbar \to 0$, the sharp phase transition appears and the two phases exist in the region where the force strength is larger than a certain critical value. However, it is shown that, in the semi-classical treatment, the above-mentioned behavior of the phase change disappears in the analytical and the numerical treatments. As a result, new understanding about aspects of the phase change in this model is given.

## Key claim

In the classical limit (ℏ → 0), the two-level pairing model exhibits a sharp phase transition with two distinct phases when the force strength exceeds a critical value; however, in the semi-classical treatment with finite ℏ, this sharp phase change disappears in both analytical and numerical treatments, giving a new understanding of phase change in this model.

## Method

The authors use a variational approach with a mixed-mode coherent state constructed via the Schwinger boson representation of the su(2)⊗su(2) algebra, which provides a semi-classical approximation that retains finite ℏ. The classical Hamiltonian is derived from the expectation value of the pairing Hamiltonian in this coherent state, and the energy minimum point is determined by solving the stationary conditions for the canonical variables (ψ, σ) in the case of a closed-shell system with S(+) = S(-) and S₀ = 0.

## Key numbers

- The parameter κ̄ = ℏ/2λ satisfies 0 < κ̄ ≤ 1/2 for the original fermion system (minimum Ω = 1 gives L = ℏ/2), with κ̄ → 0 as L → ∞ and κ̄ = 1/2 for the minimum case.
- In the classical limit (ℏ = 0), the solution for the order parameter ρ = σ/2λ is ρ = 0 for any x ≥ 0 and ρ = √(x²−1)/x for x ≥ 1, where x = 4Gλ/ε.
- The critical point in the classical limit occurs at x = 1, where the superconducting phase emerges from the normal phase.
- For finite ℏ, the relation between x and ρ is given by x = (1/√(1−ρ²)) · (ρ³−κ̄)/(ρ²(ρ−κ̄/2)), with the physically relevant region κ̄ ≤ ρ ≤ 1.
- The cross point ρ_P of the two solution branches is given by ρ_P = (κ̄/2)^(1/3) times a complicated expression involving (κ̄/2)^(2/3) terms.

## Context

This work extends the study of phase transitions in exactly-solvable many-particle models such as the Lipkin model and the pairing model, where mean-field approximations (BCS theory) produce fictitious sharp phase transitions. The authors build on their previous formulation (Providencia, Yamamura, and Kuriyama, referred to as (A) in the text) of the su(2)⊗su(2) algebra in the Schwinger boson representation. The results show that the semi-classical treatment with finite ℏ eliminates the sharp phase transition seen in the classical limit, providing a more realistic description for finite quantum systems like nuclei.
