# Fragmentation of Nuclei at Intermediate and High Energies in Modified Cascade Model

> The process of nuclear multifragmentation has been implemented, together with evaporation and fission channels of the disintegration of excited remnants in nucleus-nucleus collisions using percolation theory and the intranuclear cascade model. Colliding nuclei are treated as face--centered--cubic lattices with nucleons occupying the nodes of the lattice. The site--bond percolation model is used. The code can be applied for calculation of the fragmentation of nuclei in spallation and multifragmentation reactions.

## Key claim

The authors implement nuclear multifragmentation into a modified intranuclear cascade model using site-bond percolation theory on a face-centered-cubic lattice, claiming the model can reproduce both spallation and multifragmentation processes reasonably well, but only after tuning the bond-breaking probability \(p_{bond}(0)\) as an energy- and reaction-dependent input parameter.

## Method

Colliding nuclei are represented as face-centered-cubic lattices with nucleons at lattice sites; the intranuclear cascade stage uses a formation-time concept for secondary particles and a black-disk interaction probability. After the cascade, excited remnants are disintegrated via evaporation, fission, or multifragmentation, where multifragmentation is treated by a site-bond percolation model that counts clusters of first- and second-nearest neighbors. The bond-breaking probability is parameterized as a function of impact parameter, and fragment kinetic energies are generated from a Fermi-Dirac-like distribution with an effective temperature proportional to excitation energy divided by number of broken sites.

## Key numbers

* Nearest-neighbor distance in the FCC lattice is 2.0262 fm, reproducing a core density of 0.17 nucleons/fm\(^3\).
* Second-nearest-neighbor distance in the FCC lattice is 2.8655 fm.
* For proton-induced reactions, \(p_{bond}(0)\) varies from 0 at a few tens of MeV to 0.77 at the limiting fragmentation energy (3–4 GeV).
* The adjustable parameter \(c\) in the effective temperature formula is set to 0.7, energy independent, for all reaction types.
* The level density parameter for evaporation and fission is taken as 0.1A MeV\(^{-1}\), the same for both and independent of reaction type and collision energy.
* For the reaction \(^{197}\)Au + p at 0.5 GeV, \(p_{bond}=0.5\) is used.
* For the reaction \(^{197}\)Au + p at 800 A MeV, \(p_{bond}=0.57\) is used.
* For the reaction \(^{208}\)Pb + p at 1 A GeV, \(p_{bond}=0.6\) is used.
* For the reaction \(^{208}\)Pb + d at 1 A GeV, \(p_{bond}=0.62\) is used.
* For the reaction p + \(^{108}\)Ag at 11.5 GeV, \(p_{bond}=0.77\) is used.
* For the reaction p + \(^{197}\)Au at 11.5 GeV, \(p_{bond}=0.77\) is used.
* For the reaction \(^{12}\)C + \(^{108}\)Ag at 4.5 A GeV, \(p_{bond}=0.72\) is used.
* The Fermi-gas and FCC lattice models agree on reaction cross sections to within 5 percent.

## Context

This work extends the modified intranuclear cascade code MCAS (Musulmanbekov, prior work) by adding multifragmentation channels via percolation theory, building on earlier percolation studies that used simple cubic lattices (Bauer, prior work) and FCC lattices (Chao, Santiago, prior work). The FCC lattice model itself follows the nuclear structure approach of Cook and Dallacasa (prior work), which reproduces shell-model quantum numbers. The model enables simultaneous calculation of spallation and multifragmentation residues, and comparisons with GSI inverse-kinematics data for p+Au, p+Pb, and d+Pb reactions show reasonable agreement, though the authors note an overestimation of neutron-rich isotopes that could be corrected by better proton-neutron competition in fission-evaporation channels.
