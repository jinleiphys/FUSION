# QCD and Quark/Hadron Matter

> A brief review on the recent theoretical progress in hot/dense QCD is given. Special emphasis is put on the non-perturbative aspects of QCD plasma and the modification of hadron proporties near the critical temperature of chiral transition.

## Key claim

This review argues that non-perturbative physics persists in QCD even at temperatures much higher than the critical temperature, and that hadron properties (masses) are modified near the chiral transition, but the order of the phase transition for realistic quark masses remains an open question.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical progress in hot/dense QCD by discussing three topics: the multi-scale structure of the QCD plasma (perturbative scale \(T\), electric screening scale \(gT\), and magnetic screening scale \(g^2T\)), the order of the phase transition as studied by lattice QCD and effective models, and dynamical critical phenomena where QCD sum rules at finite temperature are used to relate meson mass shifts to the 4-quark condensate \(\langle (\bar{q}q)^2 \rangle_T\).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature estimated from pion overlap: \(T_c \sim 200\) MeV.
- Critical density estimated from nucleon overlap: \(\rho_c \simeq 3 \rho_0\), with \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) (normal nuclear matter density).
- Light quark condensate at low temperature (chiral limit): \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_T / \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_0 = 1 - [(N_f^2-1)/N_f] \, T^2/12f_\pi^2 + \cdots\).
- Light quark condensate at low density: \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_\rho / \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_0 = 1 - \rho \cdot \Sigma_{\pi N}/f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2 + \cdots\), with \(\Sigma_{\pi N} = (45 \pm 10)\) MeV.
- Extrapolating the low-density formula to \(\rho_0\) gives a condensate decrease of 20–30%.
- Energy density and entropy show a rapid growth in a narrow temperature range of \(\sim 10\) MeV near \(T_c\).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the QCD phase transition by summarizing lattice QCD simulations (e.g., those by Karsch and others in the early 1990s) and effective theory approaches. It builds on the prediction by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1990s) of non-perturbative hadron spectra above \(T_c\) in scalar and pseudoscalar channels, and on the QCD sum-rule calculations by Hatsuda, Lee, and Koike (1990s) for vector meson mass shifts. The work enables future experimental searches for modified hadron masses in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and in fixed-target experiments at KEK-PS and INS-ES.
