# $J/Ψ$ mass shift and $J/Ψ$-nuclear bound state

> We calculate mass shift of the $J/Ψ$ meson in nuclear matter arising from the modification of $DD, DD^*$ and $D^*D^*$ meson loop contributions to the $J/Ψ$ self-energy. The estimate includes the in-medium $D$ and $D^*$ meson masses consistently. The $J/Ψ$ mass shift (scalar potential) calculated is negative (attractive), and complementary to the attractive potential obtained from the QCD color van der Waals forces. Some results for the $J/Ψ$-nuclear bound state energies are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper calculates a negative (attractive) mass shift for the J/Ψ meson in nuclear matter arising from modified DD, DD*, and D*D* meson loop contributions to the J/Ψ self-energy, with the caveat that the magnitude of the shift depends on the cutoff values used in the phenomenological form factors to regularize the loop integrals.

## Method

The authors computed the J/Ψ self-energy in symmetric nuclear matter from DD, DD*, and D*D* meson loop diagrams using effective Lagrangian densities with a common coupling constant g = 7.64. The in-medium masses of the D and D* mesons were calculated consistently within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, a quark-based relativistic mean field model. The J/Ψ-nuclear potentials were then obtained via a local density approximation using nuclear density distributions also calculated within the QMC model, and bound state energies were found by solving the Klein-Gordon equation reduced from the Proca equation.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear matter density (ρ0 = 0.15 fm⁻³), the total J/Ψ mass shift Δm is -18 MeV for a cutoff ΛD = 1500 MeV and -20 MeV for ΛD = 2000 MeV.
* The individual loop contributions at ρ0 for ΛD = 1500 MeV are: DD loop -3.5 MeV, DD* loop -2.5 MeV, and D*D* loop -12 MeV.
* The in-medium J/Ψ mass m*J/Ψ at ρ0 is 3079 MeV for ΛD = 1500 MeV and 3077 MeV for ΛD = 2000 MeV.
* For ²⁰⁸Pb, the 1s bound state energy is -16.80 MeV (ΛD = 1500 MeV) and -19.06 MeV (ΛD = 2000 MeV).
* For ⁴⁰Ca, the 1s bound state energy is -15.15 MeV (ΛD = 1500 MeV) and -17.42 MeV (ΛD = 2000 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of J/Ψ binding in nuclei that used QCD color van der Waals forces, which estimated binding energies ranging from 400 MeV in an A=9 nucleus (Brodsky 1989) down to 8 MeV in nuclear matter (Ko 2000). The present approach is complementary to those based on chromo-electric polarizability (Luke 1992) and QCD sum rules, which estimated mass decreases of 4-7 MeV (Klingl 1998, Hayashigaki 1998, Kim 2000). The results suggest that J/Ψ-nuclear bound states should be observable in experiments at the upgraded CEBAF facility at Jefferson Lab and at FAIR, with the prediction that such states would produce narrow peaks in cross sections due to the absence of Pauli blocking and negligible quark interchange.
