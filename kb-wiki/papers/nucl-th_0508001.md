# Quantitative Relativistic Effects in the Three-Nucleon Problem

> The quantitative impact of the requirement of relativistic invariance in the three-nucleon problem is examined within the framework of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics. In the case of the bound state, and for a wide variety of model implementations and reasonable interactions, most of the quantitative effects come from kinematic factors that can easily be incorporated within a non-relativistic momentum-space three-body code.

## Key claim

For the three-nucleon bound state, most quantitative relativistic effects arise from kinematic factors that can be incorporated into a non-relativistic momentum-space three-body code, rather than from dynamical modifications, though the separation of relativistic, off-shell, and three-body interaction effects is not unique.

## Method

The authors examined Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics for three-nucleon systems, using three distinct methods (Coester-Pieper-Serduke, Glöckle-Lee-Coester, and Glöckle-Kamada) to relate relativistic two-body transition matrix elements to non-relativistic ones fitted to nucleon-nucleon phase shift data. They constructed the kernel of the relativistic Faddeev-Lovelace equations by embedding half-shell two-body transition operators into the three-body Hilbert space, with the most nontrivial step being the construction of fully off-shell input from half-shell operators via spectral expansion or the first resolvent equation.

## Key numbers

- The energy mismatch between non-relativistic and relativistic center-of-momentum energy for a nucleon is a 7% effect at 1 GeV.
- An inconsistent treatment of phase equivalence leads to a 15% error in the total cross section at 1 GeV/c for the Malfliet-Tjon V potential in the Born approximation.
- The relation between relativistic and non-relativistic half-off-shell transition matrix elements in the CPS method involves the factor 4m/(e(k) + e(k')), where e(k) = 2 sqrt(k^2 + m^2).

## Context

This work extends the studies of Glöckle, Kamada, and collaborators (2000s) on Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics in two- and three-body problems, and builds on the methods of Coester, Pieper, and Serduke (1970s) and Glöckle, Lee, and Coester (1980s) for utilizing realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions in relativistic calculations. The paper identifies that the dominant relativistic effects are multiplicative kinematic factors, enabling simpler incorporation into existing non-relativistic momentum-space three-body codes without requiring full relativistic dynamical calculations.
