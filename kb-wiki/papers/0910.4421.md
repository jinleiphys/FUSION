# Strangeness production from pp collisions

> The study of the strangeness production from pp collisions plays important roles in two aspects: exploring the properties of baryon resonances involved and understanding the strangeness production from heavy ion collisions to explore the properties of high energy and high density nuclear matter. Here we review our recent studies on several most important channels for the strangeness production from pp collisions. The previously ignored contributions from Delta*(1620) and N*(1535) resonances are found to play dominant role for the pp --> n K+ Sigma+, pp --> pK+ Lambda and pp --> pp phi reactions near-thresholds. These contributions should be included for further studies on the strangeness production from both pp collisions and heavy ion collisions.

# Strangeness production from pp collisions

## Key claim
The previously ignored contributions from the sub-threshold resonances Δ*(1620) and N*(1535) are found to play a dominant role for the pp → n K+ Σ+, pp → p K+ Λ, and pp → pp φ reactions near threshold, but the authors note that a recent ANKE measurement gives a much smaller cross section for pp → n K+ Σ+ than the COSY-11 data, indicating unresolved experimental discrepancies that require a 4π solid angle detector measurement.

## Method
The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach to calculate total cross sections for strangeness production channels in pp collisions, incorporating meson-exchange mechanisms (π and ρ exchange) and including contributions from sub-threshold baryon resonances Δ*(1620) and N*(1535) that were omitted in previous calculations. For the pp → n K+ Σ+ reaction, both π+ and ρ+ exchange contributions from Δ*(1620) are evaluated separately, while for pp → p K+ Λ and pp → pp φ, the N*(1535) resonance is included based on its newly determined strong coupling to KΛ and φN channels.

## Key numbers
- For pp → n K+ Σ+: previous theoretical calculations fail by an order of magnitude compared with COSY-11 data at energies close to threshold; the inclusion of Δ*(1620) via ρ+ exchange reproduces the COSY-11 data, but the ANKE collaboration reports a much smaller cross section than COSY-11.
- For pp → p K+ Λ: the N*(1535) resonance contribution is calculated using a large g_{N*(1535)KΛ} coupling constant derived from BES data on J/ψ → p p̄ η and J/ψ → p̄ K+ Λ + c.c. reactions.
- For pp → pp φ: the calculation assumes the φ meson is produced predominantly through excitation and decay of the sub-φN-threshold N*(1535) resonance, with the solid curve including the ¹S₀ pp final-state interaction.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies by Tsushima (1999) and Gaspar (2000) on pp → n K+ Σ+, which reproduced higher-energy data but failed near threshold by neglecting either Δ*(1620) or ρ+ exchange contributions. The authors also build on their own prior work (Liu, 2009; Xie, 2009) establishing strong N*(1535) couplings to KΛ and φN from BES data. The results enable improved modeling of strangeness production in heavy ion collisions and suggest that sub-threshold baryon resonances must be included in future transport models for nuclear matter studies.
