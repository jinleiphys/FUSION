# A theoretical description of energy spectra and two-neutron separation energies for neutron-rich zirconium isotopes

> Very recently the atomic masses of neutron-rich Zr isotopes, from $^{96}$Zr to $^{104}$Zr, have been measured with high precision. Using a schematic Interacting Boson Model (IBM) Hamiltonian, the evolution from spherical to deformed shapes along the chain of Zr isotopes, describing at the same time the excitation energies as well as the two-neutron separation energies, can be rather well reproduced. The interplay between phase transitions and configuration mixing of intruder excitations in this mass region is succinctly addressed.

# A theoretical description of energy spectra and two-neutron separation energies for neutron-rich zirconium isotopes

## Key claim
Using a schematic Interacting Boson Model (IBM) Hamiltonian with identical bosons and a global linear term for binding energies, the evolution from spherical to deformed shapes along the Zr isotopic chain can be rather well reproduced, but the description relies on absorbing intruder excitations into effectively changing Hamiltonian parameters rather than treating them explicitly.

## Method
The authors employed an IBM Hamiltonian with a single-boson energy term, a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and an angular momentum term (the Consistent-Q Formalism), using a $^{90}$Zr core with $Z=40, N=50$. They fitted the local Hamiltonian parameters to reproduce low-lying energy spectra of even-even $^{94-104}$Zr isotopes, then derived a global linear contribution to the two-neutron separation energies ($S_{2n}^{gl} = \mathcal{A} + \mathcal{B} N$) by subtracting the calculated local binding energy differences from experimental $S_{2n}$ values.

## Key numbers
- The global part of $S_{2n}$ corresponds to $\mathcal{A} = 67.4$ MeV and $\mathcal{B} = -0.946$ MeV (using atomic number $A$ as variable).
- The parameter $\chi$ in the quadrupole operator was fixed at $-0.8$.
- The quadrupole strength $\kappa$ was set to $0.032$ MeV for spherical and transitional Zr nuclei, and to $0.046$ MeV for rotational-like Zr nuclei.
- The effective charge $e_{eff} = 0.159$ e·b was fixed by reproducing the $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value in $^{100}$Zr.
- In $^{98}$Zr, the $0_3^+$ state at $1.436$ MeV is identified as the head of an intruder band; in $^{100}$Zr, the $0_2^+$ state at $0.331$ MeV is considered an intruder state while the $0_3^+$ state at $0.829$ MeV is the regular state.

## Context
This work extends the technique developed by Fossion et al. (2002) for simultaneously describing energy spectra and two-neutron separation energies using the IBM, applied to the neutron-rich Zr isotopes following high-precision mass measurements from Rintala et al. (2004) and Audi et al. (2003). The study addresses the rapid shape transition observed at mass A=98-100, previously discussed by Moller and Nix (1981), Heyde et al. (1984), and Wood et al. (1999), and contrasts with explicit configuration-mixing approaches (to be shown elsewhere) by using a single-N-boson space with smoothly varying parameters. The results enable future comparisons with relativistic mean-field calculations by Hema et al. (2004) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Blazkiewicz et al. (2005), and motivate further work on whether phase transitions can be induced by low-lying intruder configurations.
