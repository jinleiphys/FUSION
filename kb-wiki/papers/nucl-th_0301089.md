# The $g_{Vσγ}$-coupling constants in hadron electrodynamic

> Recent measurements of the branching ratios for the decays $ω\toπ^0π^0γ$ and $ρ^0\toπ^0π^0γ$ lead to coupling constants of the $Vσγ$-interaction ($V$ is a vector meson) one order of magnitude smaller than previously assumed to describe the threshold cross section of $γ+p\to p+ρ^0$ and the HERMES effect. The new $g^2_{Vσγ}$ couplings are in contradiction with the predictions of the QCD sum rules, but are in good agreement with VDM-estimation of the $σ\to γγ$-width.

# The \(g_{V\sigma\gamma}\)-coupling constants in hadron electrodynamics

## Key claim

The coupling constants \(g_{V\sigma\gamma}\) (where \(V\) is a vector meson) are one order of magnitude smaller than previously assumed, based on new branching ratio measurements for \(\omega\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\) and \(\rho^0\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\). This contradicts QCD sum rule predictions but agrees with vector meson dominance (VDM) estimates of the \(\sigma\to\gamma\gamma\) width, though the result depends strongly on the assumed mass and width of the \(\sigma\)-meson.

## Method

The authors extracted \(g_{\rho\sigma\gamma}\) from the measured branching ratio of \(\rho^0\to\sigma\gamma\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\) using an effective Lagrangian approach, accounting for the finite width of the \(\sigma\)-meson and the possibility that \(m_\sigma > M_\rho\). They also derived an independent estimate from the \(\sigma\to 2\gamma\) decay width using VDM, assuming SU(3) flavor symmetry (\(g_{\rho\sigma\gamma}/g_{\omega\sigma\gamma}\simeq 3\)) and known leptonic decay constants \(g_\rho\) and \(g_\omega\).

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for \(\rho\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\): \((4.1^{+1.0}_{-0.9}\pm 0.3)\times 10^{-5}\) (Novosibirsk, Ac02)
- Branching ratio for \(\rho\to\sigma\gamma\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\): \((1.9^{+0.9}_{-0.8}\pm 0.4)\times 10^{-5}\) (Ac02)
- Partial width \(\Gamma(\rho^0\to\sigma\gamma\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma) = (2.85^{+1.4}_{-1.2}\pm 0.6)\) keV (derived from branching ratio)
- For \(m_\sigma = 0.5\) GeV and \(\Gamma_\sigma = 0.6\) GeV: \(g^2_{\rho\sigma\gamma} = 0.196\)
- For standard parameters \(m_\sigma = 0.5\) GeV, \(\Gamma_\sigma = 0.6\)–1 GeV: \(0.19 \le g^2_{\rho\sigma\gamma} \le 0.30\)
- From \(\sigma\to 2\gamma\) data: \(0.28 \le g^2_{\rho\sigma\gamma} (m_\sigma/1\text{ GeV})^3 \le 0.78\)
- Previous large value from Friman (1996): \(g_{\rho\sigma\gamma}=3\) (for \(m_\sigma=0.5\) GeV), giving \(\Gamma(\rho^0\to\sigma\gamma)=379\) keV and \(\Gamma(\rho^0\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma)=126\) keV
- Previous large value from Miskimen (2000): \(g_{\rho\sigma\gamma}=5\)–6 (for \(m_\sigma=0.6\) GeV), giving \(\Gamma(\rho^0\to\sigma\gamma)=308\)–444 keV and \(\Gamma(\rho^0\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma)=103\)–148 keV
- VDM estimate for \(\Gamma(\sigma\to 2\gamma)\) using \(g_{\rho\sigma\gamma}=3\) and \(m_\sigma=1\) GeV: 123 keV
- Experimental \(\Gamma(\sigma\to 2\gamma)\): 10.6 keV (Particle Data Group) and \((3.8\pm 1.5)\) keV (Boglione 1999)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of radiative vector meson decays, specifically \(\rho^0\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\) and \(\omega\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\), measured at Novosibirsk (Ac02). It contradicts the large coupling constants previously used by Friman (1996) to describe \(\gamma+p\to p+\rho^0\) near threshold, and by Miskimen (2000) to explain the HERMES effect. The new small couplings also disagree with QCD sum rule predictions by Gokalp (2001). The results enable a revision of \(\sigma\)-exchange models for vector meson photoproduction and meson exchange current calculations for deuteron form factors, and suggest that other mechanisms (Pomeron, \(f_2\)-exchange) must be considered for \(\gamma+p\to p+\rho^0\) near threshold.
