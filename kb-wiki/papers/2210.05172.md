# Density profiles near nuclear surface of $^{44,52}$Ti: An indication of $α$ clustering

> We investigate the degree of $α$ ($^4$He nucleus) clustering in the ground-state density profiles of $^{44}$Ti and $^{52}$Ti. Two types of density distributions, shell- and cluster-model configurations, are generated fully microscopically with the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model, which can describe both the j-j coupling shell and $α$-cluster configurations in a single scheme. Despite both the models reproducing measured charge radius data, we found that the $α$ clustering significantly diffuses the density profiles near the nuclear surface compared to the ideal j-j coupling shell model configuration. The effect is most significant for $^{44}$Ti, while it is less for $^{52}$Ti due to the occupation of the $0f_{7/2}$ orbits in the $^{48}$Ca core. This difference can be detected by measuring proton-nucleus elastic scattering or the total reaction cross section on a carbon target at intermediate energies.

# Density profiles near nuclear surface of $^{44,52}$Ti: An indication of $\alpha$ clustering

## Key claim

Despite both shell-model-like (S-type) and alpha-cluster-model-like (C-type) configurations reproducing measured charge radius data for $^{44}$Ti and the neighboring $^{50}$Ti charge radius for $^{52}$Ti, the alpha clustering significantly diffuses the density profiles near the nuclear surface compared to the ideal j-j coupling shell model configuration, with the effect being most significant for $^{44}$Ti and less for $^{52}$Ti due to the occupation of the $0f_{7/2}$ orbits in the $^{48}$Ca core.

## Method

The authors employed the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM) to generate fully microscopic wave functions for $^{44}$Ti and $^{52}$Ti in both shell-model-like (S-type) and alpha-cluster-like (C-type) configurations within a single theoretical scheme. For the S-type configuration, the core-alpha distance $R$ was taken small with $\Lambda_p=\Lambda_n=1$ (for $^{44}$Ti) to produce the $(0f_{7/2})^4$ j-j coupling shell configuration; for the C-type configuration, $\Lambda_p=\Lambda_n=0$ was used with the core-alpha distance $R$ adjusted to reproduce the experimental charge radius. The resulting density distributions were then used as input to the Glauber model to calculate proton-nucleus elastic scattering differential cross sections and total reaction cross sections on a carbon target at intermediate energies.

## Key numbers

- For $^{44}$Ti (S-type): oscillator parameter $\nu = 0.1270$ fm$^{-2}$, core-alpha distance $R = 0.20$ fm, charge radius $r_{\rm ch} = 3.611$ fm, diffuseness $a_p = 0.557$ fm
- For $^{44}$Ti (C-type): oscillator parameter $\nu = 0.1315$ fm$^{-2}$, core-alpha distance $R = 2.85$ fm, charge radius $r_{\rm ch} = 3.611$ fm, diffuseness $a_p = 0.625$ fm
- For $^{52}$Ti (S-type): oscillator parameter $\nu = 0.1297$ fm$^{-2}$, core-alpha distance $R = 0.20$ fm, charge radius $r_{\rm ch} = 3.569$ fm, proton diffuseness $a_p = 0.552$ fm, neutron diffuseness $a_n = 0.574$ fm
- For $^{52}$Ti (C-type): oscillator parameter $\nu = 0.1311$ fm$^{-2}$, core-alpha distance $R = 1.60$ fm, charge radius $r_{\rm ch} = 3.569$ fm, proton diffuseness $a_p = 0.608$ fm, neutron diffuseness $a_n = 0.566$ fm
- For $^{44}$Ti, the difference in total reaction cross sections on a carbon target between S-type and C-type configurations is at most about 2%, larger than typical experimental precision of less than 1%
- For $^{52}$Ti with extended charge radius (3.619 fm), the C-type core-alpha distance becomes $R = 3.01$ fm

## Context

This work extends the investigation of alpha clustering in medium-mass nuclei, building on the predicted $^{40}$Ca+alpha structure of $^{44}$Ti by Michel (1986) and experimental confirmation of inversion doublet structure by Yamaya (1990, 1998). The study uses the AQCM framework developed in a series of papers (AQCM01 through AQCM14) to treat shell and cluster configurations on equal footing. The results enable experimental discrimination between shell and cluster configurations through proton-nucleus elastic scattering measurements up to the first diffraction peak, as prescribed by Hatakeyama (2018), or through total reaction cross section measurements on carbon targets, offering a complementary tool to alpha-knockout reactions such as those used by Tanaka (2021) for quantifying alpha clustering near the nuclear surface.
