# Symplectic group structure of the 48Cr, 88Ru, and 92Pd ground states

> The ground states of 48Cr, 88Ru, and 92Pd are studied in the 1f7/2 or 1g9/2 shell model with effective interactions from the literature. They are found to be composed, quite independently of the shell and the interaction, roughly of 75% of (s,t)=(0,0) and 25% of (s,t)=(4,0), where s is the seniority and t the reduced isospin. Other irreps of the symplectic group Sp(2j+1), where j is the single-nucleon angular momentum, make only very small contributions. The state chi obtained by antisymmetriziation and normalization of the ground state in the stretch scheme of Danos and Gillet [M. Danos and V. Gillet, Phys. Rev. 161, 1034 (1967)] has a very different structure where the Sp(2j+1) irreps other than (s,t)=(0,0) and (4,0) contribute 20% and 41% for j=7/2 and 9/2, respectively. The contributions of chi and the s=0 state to the calculated ground states state are about equal for 48Cr. For 88Ru and 92Pd the s=0 state is unambiguously a better approximation to the calculated states than chi. A state chi' obtained by antisymmetrization and normalization of the product of two stretch-scheme ground states of the system with two valence nucleons or nucleon holes of each type has much larger overlaps with the calculated ground states than chi but a deviating Sp(2j+1) decomposition.

## Key claim

The ground states of 48Cr, 88Ru, and 92Pd in the single-j shell model are composed, quite independently of the shell and the effective interaction, of roughly 75% seniority-zero (s,t)=(0,0) and 25% (s,t)=(4,0), with other symplectic group irreps making only very small contributions; this composition is similar to the n=4 case studied in Neergård (2013), where the corresponding parts are about 80% and 20%.

## Method

The author constructed the interaction matrix in the space of total angular momentum I=0 and isospin T=0 for eight quasinucleons (four quasineutrons and four equivalent quasiprotons) in a single j shell, using a basis of antisymmetrized neutron and proton states. The subspaces of definite seniority s and reduced isospin t were obtained by diagonalizing Racah's seniority operator generalized to jj coupling, and the calculated ground states were decomposed into irreps of the symplectic group Sp(2j+1). The stretch-scheme ground state of Danos and Gillet (1967) was antisymmetrized and normalized to produce the state chi, and a second state chi' was constructed by antisymmetrizing and normalizing the product of two stretch-scheme ground states of the n=4 system.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ground states are composed of roughly 75% (s,t)=(0,0) and 25% (s,t)=(4,0) for both the 1f7/2 and 1g9/2 shells, across all interactions tested.
- For the stretch-scheme state chi, the Sp(2j+1) irreps other than (s,t)=(0,0) and (4,0) contribute 20% for j=7/2 and 41% for j=9/2.
- The squared norm of the antisymmetrized stretch-scheme state P|sigma> is 1.5% for both j=7/2 and j=9/2.
- The squared overlap |<chi|psi>|^2 for 48Cr is about 78% and 77% on average over interactions; for 92Pd with the QLW interaction it is 66%, confirming Qi et al. (2011).
- The squared overlap |<chi'|psi>|^2 is about 93% in the 1f7/2 shell and 85% in the 1g9/2 shell.
- For the n=4 system studied in Neergård (2013), the seniority-zero state makes up about 80% of the calculated ground states, compared to about 75% for n=8.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Neergård (2013) from the n=4 system (two quasineutrons and two equivalent quasiprotons) to the n=8 system (four quasineutrons and four equivalent quasiprotons), which corresponds to the single-j-shell configurations of 48Cr, 88Ru, and 92Pd. The results contradict the interpretation of Cederwall et al. (2011) that the stretch scheme replaces seniority coupling in these nuclei, showing instead that the seniority-zero state is a better approximation than the antisymmetrized stretch-scheme state for 88Ru and 92Pd. The findings enable further study of the relationship between isovector pairing (seniority zero) and the stretch scheme in N=Z nuclei, and support the observation of Qi (2012) that the 92Pd ground state can be well described as an antisymmetrized product of two 96Gd ground states.
