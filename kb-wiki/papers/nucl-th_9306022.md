# Iterative Solution for Effective Interactions in a System with Non-degenerate Unperturbed Energies

> We generalize the Lee-Suzuki iteration method for summing the folded diagram series to the case where the unperturbed model-space energies are non-degenerate. A condition is derived for the convergence of the iteration scheme and this depends on the choice of the model space projection operators. Two choices are examined, in the first the projection operators are defined in terms of the unperturbed states and in the second they are defined in terms of the eigenfunctions obtained at each stage of the iteration. As is illustrated by calculations with a simple model, the second procedure gives the better convergence and, by suitable choice of the starting energies, allows the reproduction of any subset of the exact eigenvalues.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Lee-Suzuki iteration method for summing folded diagram series to systems where the unperturbed model-space energies are non-degenerate, and shows that by choosing projection operators self-consistently at each iteration step (SCGLS method), convergence depends only on energy ratios, allowing any subset of exact eigenvalues to be reproduced by tuning the unperturbed energies.

## Method

The authors derive iterative equations for the effective interaction \(R\) and the operator \(\omega\) in the non-degenerate case, using projection operators \(P_\alpha\) that partition the model space. Two choices for these projection operators are examined: fixed operators defined from the unperturbed basis states (GLS method), and self-consistent operators defined from the eigenfunctions obtained at each iteration (SCGLS method). A convergence condition is derived in terms of a matrix \(\mathbf{Z}\) whose norm must be less than unity, generalizing the degenerate-case condition \(|\epsilon_0 - E_p|/|\epsilon_0 - E_q| < 1\). The methods are tested on a 4×4 model Hamiltonian with a 2-dimensional model space.

## Key numbers

- For the model with parameter \(x=0.05\), the SCGLS method with various choices of unperturbed energies \(\epsilon_\alpha\) converges to different pairs of exact eigenvalues, with accuracy to four decimal places obtained in at most 12 iterations.
- For \(x=0.20\), the SCGLS method reproduces the first and third eigenvalues (maximum \(P\)-space overlap) with markedly better convergence than GLS, which shows oscillatory behavior.
- The convergence condition for SCGLS is \(\parallel \mathbf{Z} \parallel = \max |(\epsilon_p - E_p)/(\epsilon_p - E_q)| < 1\) for \(p=1,\ldots,d\) and all \(q\).
- Divergence occurs at points where \(\epsilon_2 = \frac{1}{2}(E_2 + E_3) = 4.11\) and \(\epsilon_2 = \frac{1}{2}(E_3 + E_4) = 7.29\) for \(x=0.20\), where \(\parallel \mathbf{Z} \parallel = 1\).
- The minimum number of iterations \(n_{\text{min}}\) required for \(\Delta_n < 10^{-4}\) follows \(n_{\text{min}} = c / \log_{10} \parallel \mathbf{Z} \parallel\) with \(c = -5\), matching the exact results.

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Suzuki iteration method (Lee and Suzuki, 1980) and the Krenciglowa-Kuo technique (Krenciglowa and Kuo, 1974) from the degenerate case to non-degenerate unperturbed energies, addressing a limitation of prior approaches that required artificial energy shifts to treat one-body terms. The formalism builds on the \(\hat{Q}\)-box method of Kuo and collaborators (1980s) and is tested against the model of Hoffmann et al. (1970s) used to study intruder states. The SCGLS method enables the reproduction of any subset of exact eigenvalues by tuning unperturbed energies, which is not possible with the standard LS or KK methods that yield only certain eigenvalues.
