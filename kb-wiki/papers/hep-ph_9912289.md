# Quark Orbital Angular Momentum from Lattice QCD

> We calculate the quark orbital angular momentum of the nucleon from the quark energy-momentum tensor form factors on the lattice. The disconnected insertion is estimated stochastically which employs the $Z_2$ noise with an unbiased subtraction. This reduced the error by a factor of 4 with negligible overhead. The total quark contribution to the proton spin is found to be $0.30 \pm 0.07$. From this and the quark spin content we deduce the quark orbital angular momentum to be $0.17 \pm 0.06$ which is $\sim 34$% of the proton spin. We further predict that the gluon angular momentum to be $0.20 \pm 0.07$, i. e. $\sim$ 40% of the proton spin is due to the glue.

## Key claim

The total quark contribution to the proton spin is found to be 0.30 ± 0.07, from which the quark orbital angular momentum is deduced to be 0.17 ± 0.06 (about 34% of the proton spin), and the gluon angular momentum is predicted to be 0.20 ± 0.07 (about 40% of the proton spin), but these results are obtained on a quenched lattice at a single lattice spacing and with chiral extrapolation, so they are subject to systematic uncertainties from the quenched approximation, finite volume, and finite lattice spacing.

## Method

The authors performed a quenched lattice QCD calculation on a 16³ × 24 lattice at β = 6.0 with Wilson fermions at four κ values (0.148, 0.152, 0.154, 0.155), corresponding to quark masses from about 376 MeV down to 80 MeV. They computed the quark energy-momentum tensor form factors T₁(q²) and T₂(q²) from three-point functions, separating the connected insertion (valence and cloud quarks) and disconnected insertion (sea quarks). The disconnected insertion was estimated stochastically using Z₂ noise with an unbiased subtraction method that reduced the error by a factor of 4 with negligible overhead. The total quark angular momentum J_q was obtained from the q² → 0 limit of ½[T₁(q²) + T₂(q²)], and the quark orbital angular momentum L_q was extracted by subtracting the previously calculated quark spin content ½Σ from J_q.

## Key numbers

- Total quark angular momentum J_q = 0.30 ± 0.07 (in units of ℏ/2, i.e., fraction of proton spin)
- Quark orbital angular momentum L_q = 0.17 ± 0.06 (about 34% of the proton spin)
- Quark spin content ½Σ = 0.13 ± 0.06 (from previous lattice calculation by Dong et al. (1995) on the same lattices)
- Gluon angular momentum J_g = 0.20 ± 0.07 (about 40% of the proton spin, predicted from the spin sum rule)
- Connected insertion part J_q,CI = 0.44 ± 0.07 (about 90% of the proton spin)
- Disconnected insertion total J_q,DI = −0.14 ± 0.04
- Disconnected insertion quark spin ½Σ_DI = −0.18 ± 0.03
- Disconnected insertion orbital angular momentum L_q,DI = 0.041 ± 0.036 (consistent with zero)
- Dipole mass from connected insertion fitting: 0.88 ± 0.07 GeV
- Lattice spacing inverse: 1/a = 1.74 GeV (scale used for renormalization)

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon spin structure beyond the quark spin content, which had been previously computed by Dong et al. (1995) and confirmed the small quark spin fraction seen in polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments (Ashman et al., 1988). It provides the first lattice determination of the quark orbital angular momentum via the energy-momentum tensor form factors, following the theoretical framework of Ji (1997) who showed that total angular momentum can be extracted from these form factors. The results enable a complete decomposition of the proton spin into quark spin, quark orbital angular momentum, and gluon angular momentum, and suggest that the gluon angular momentum and sea quark spin largely cancel each other, which the authors relate to the axial anomaly and anomalous chiral Ward identity.
