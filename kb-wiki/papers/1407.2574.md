# Gold-plated moments of nucleon structure functions in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We obtain leading- and next-to-leading order predictions of chiral perturbation theory for several prominent moments of nucleon structure functions. These free-parameter free results turn out to be in overall agreement with the available empirical information on nearly all of the considered moments, in the region of low-momentum transfer ($Q^2 < 0.3$ GeV$^2$). Especially surprising is the situation for the spin polarizability $δ_{LT}$, which thus far was not reproducible in chiral perturbation theory for proton and neutron simultaneously. This problem, known as the "$δ_{LT}$ puzzle," is not seen in the present calculation.

## Key claim

The authors obtain leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) predictions from baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) for several moments of nucleon structure functions, finding overall agreement with empirical data for momentum transfers below 0.3 GeV². Crucially, the spin polarizability δLT is reproduced simultaneously for both proton and neutron without free parameters, resolving the "δLT puzzle" that had persisted in previous chiral perturbation theory calculations.

## Method

The authors computed the doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) amplitude to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the δ-expansion scheme of BχPT, which includes pion, nucleon, and Δ(1232) isobar degrees of freedom with the Δ-nucleon mass difference treated as an intermediate small scale. They evaluated the LO [O(p³)] and NLO [O(p⁴/Δ)] contributions to seven moments of nucleon structure functions, including generalized polarizabilities, the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) integral, the d₂ moment, and the Bjorken integral Γ₁. The calculation contains no free parameters at these orders, making it fully predictive.

## Key numbers

- Proton αE₁+βM₁ at Q²=0: 15.12(82) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (NLO BχPT), compared to empirical 13.8(4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from Olmos de Leon (2001)
- Neutron αE₁+βM₁ at Q²=0: 18.30(99) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (NLO BχPT), compared to empirical 14.40(66) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from Babusci (1997)
- Proton αL at Q²=0: 2.31(12) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵ (NLO BχPT), compared to MAID2007 value of 2.32 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
- Neutron αL at Q²=0: 3.21(17) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵ (NLO BχPT), compared to MAID2007 value of 3.32 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
- Proton γ₀ at Q²=0: -0.93(5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (NLO BχPT), compared to empirical -1.00(8)(12) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ from Dutz (2003)
- Neutron γ₀ at Q²=0: 0.05(1) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (NLO BχPT), compared to MAID2007 value of -0.005 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Proton δLT at Q²=0: 1.35(7) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (NLO BχPT), compared to MAID2007 value of 1.34 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Neutron δLT at Q²=0: 2.20(12) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (NLO BχPT), compared to MAID2007 value of 2.03 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the BχPT framework previously applied to real Compton scattering at Q²=0 by Lensky (2009) to finite momentum transfer, and directly addresses the "δLT puzzle" identified in heavy-baryon (HB) χPT calculations by Hemmert (1996), Kao (2002), and Kao (2003), where δLT could not be reproduced for both proton and neutron simultaneously. The results differ substantially from earlier HBχPT calculations and from the covariant BχPT calculation of Bernard (2012), which resolved the puzzle for the neutron but introduced it for the proton. The present calculation enables improved predictions for two-photon exchange contributions to muonic hydrogen spectroscopy, relevant to the proton charge radius puzzle, and provides testable predictions for the upcoming Jefferson Laboratory measurement of the proton δLT.
