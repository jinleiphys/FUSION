# Isoscalar-vector interaction and hybrid quark core in massive neutron stars

> The hadron-quark phase transition in the core of massive neutron stars is studied with a newly constructed two-phase model. For nuclear matter, a nonlinear Walecka type model with general nucleon-meson and meson-meson couplings, recently calibrated by Steiner, Hemper and Fischer, is taken. For quark matter, a modified Polyakov-Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (mPNJL) model, which gives consistent results with lattice QCD data, is used. Most importantly, we introduce an isoscalar-vector interaction in the description of quark matter, and we study its influence on the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of massive neutron stars. With the constraints of neutron star observations, our calculation shows that the isoscalar-vector interaction between quarks is indispensable if massive hybrids star exist in the universe, and its strength determines the onset density of quark matter, as well as the mass-radius relations of hybrid stars. Furthermore, as a connection with heavy-ion-collision experiments we give some discussions about the strength of isoscalar-vector interaction and its effect on the signals of hadron-quark phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, in the energy range of the NICA at JINR-Dubna and FAIR at GSI-Darmstadt facilities.

# Isoscalar-vector interaction and hybrid quark core in massive neutron stars

## Key claim

The isoscalar-vector interaction between quarks is indispensable if massive hybrid stars (with a quark core) exist in the universe, and its strength determines the onset density of quark matter as well as the mass-radius relations of hybrid stars; however, the coupling strength \(R_V = G_V/G\) is treated as a free parameter because it cannot be determined from experiments or lattice QCD simulations, and the paper shows that \(R_V \geq 0.055\) is required for hybrid stars to reach the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230, while no quarks appear for \(R_V > 0.484\).

## Method

The authors construct a two-phase model: for nuclear matter they use the nonlinear Walecka-type model (SFHO parameter set) with general nucleon-meson and meson-meson couplings calibrated by Steiner, Hemper and Fischer (2013); for quark matter they use a modified three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (mPNJL) model that includes an isoscalar-vector interaction term with strength \(G_V\). The hadron-quark phase transition is computed under Gibbs conditions with thermal, chemical, and mechanical equilibrium and global charge neutrality, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation is solved to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of a hybrid star without the isoscalar-vector interaction (\(R_V = 0\)) is \(1.88\,\mathrm{M}_\odot\), which is less than the known maximum neutron star mass.
- With \(R_V = 0.055\), the maximum mass of a hybrid star reaches the lower mass limit of pulsar J1614-2230 (\(1.97 \pm 0.04\,\mathrm{M}_\odot\)).
- For \(R_V > 0.484\), quark matter does not appear in the neutron star core.
- The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star lies between 10.4 and 12.9 km, independent of assumptions about core composition (from Steiner 2012, Steiner 2010).
- The mPNJL model parameters used: \(\Lambda = 603.2\) MeV, \(G\Lambda^2 = 1.835\), \(K\Lambda^5 = 12.36\), \(m_{u,d} = 5.5\) MeV, \(m_s = 140.7\) MeV.
- The Polyakov-loop potential parameters: \(a_0 = -1.85\), \(a_1 = -1.44 \times 10^{-3}\), \(a_2 = -0.08\), \(a_3 = -0.4\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid neutron stars that had difficulty describing large-mass neutron stars due to a lack of repulsion at high baryon densities in quark matter effective interactions (Burgio 2013). The paper builds on the SFHO nuclear equation of state (Steiner, Hemper and Fischer 2013) and the mPNJL quark model (Dexheimer 2010, Blaschke 2010, Shao 2011), and it connects to heavy-ion collision experiments at NICA (JINR-Dubna) and FAIR (GSI-Darmstadt) where the isoscalar-vector interaction strength may be probed through phase-transition signals (Shao 2012). The results enable future combined constraints on \(R_V\) from neutron star observations and heavy-ion collision data.
