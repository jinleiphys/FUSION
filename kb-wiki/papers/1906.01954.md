# Chemical freeze-out conditions and fluctuations of conserved charges in heavy-ion collisions within quantum van der Waals model

> The chemical freeze-out parameters in central nucleus-nucleus collisions are extracted consistently from hadron yield data within the quantum van der Waals (QvdW) hadron resonance gas model. The beam energy dependences for skewness and kurtosis of net baryon, net electric, and net strangeness charges are predicted. The QvdW interactions in asymmetric matter, $Q/B \neq 0.5$, between (anti)baryons yield a non-congruent liquid-gas phase transition, together with a nuclear critical point (CP) with critical temperature of $T_c=19.5$ MeV. The nuclear CP yields the collision energy dependence of the skewness and the kurtosis to both deviate significantly from the ideal hadron resonance gas baseline predictions even far away, in $(T,μ_B)$-plane, from the CP. These predictions can readily be tested by STAR and NA61/SHINE Collaborations at the RHIC BNL and the SPS CERN, respectively, and by HADES at GSI. The results presented here offer a broad opportunity for the search for signals of phase transition in dense hadronic matter at the future NICA and FAIR high intensity facilities.

## Key claim

The quantum van der Waals hadron resonance gas (QvdW-HRG) model predicts that the nuclear critical point (CP) at T_c = 19.5 MeV causes the skewness and kurtosis of net baryon, net electric, and net strangeness charge fluctuations to deviate significantly from the ideal hadron resonance gas (IHRG) baseline even far away in the (T, μ_B) plane from the CP, not only in its immediate vicinity.

## Method

The authors used the QvdW-HRG model, which incorporates attractive and repulsive interactions between baryons and between antibaryons via two parameters (a = 329 MeV fm^3 and b = 3.42 fm^3) fixed by nuclear ground state properties, while mesons are treated as non-interacting. They fitted the chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_B, volume V, and strangeness under-saturation factor γ_S) to experimental hadron yield data from SIS, AGS, SPS, and LHC for central nucleus-nucleus collisions, using both the IHRG and QvdW-HRG models. The skewness (Sσ) and kurtosis (κσ^2) of conserved charge fluctuations were then calculated as ratios of susceptibilities along the extracted freeze-out lines.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear critical point in the QvdW-HRG model with asymmetry Q/B = 0.4: T_c = 19.48 MeV, μ_B^c = 914.5 MeV
- QvdW interaction parameters: a = 329 MeV fm^3 (attraction), b = 3.42 fm^3 (repulsion), fixed from nuclear ground state saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} and binding energy E_b = -16 MeV
- At the lowest collision energy sqrt(s_NN) = 1.9 GeV, freeze-out baryon density n_B = 2.17 × 10^{-4} fm^{-3} in both models
- For LHC at sqrt(s_NN) = 2760 GeV, QvdW-HRG freeze-out: T = 156 ± 5 MeV, μ_B = 1.5 ± 10.9 MeV, V = 4148 ± 613 fm^3, γ_s = 1.10 ± 0.03
- For top SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV, QvdW-HRG freeze-out: T = 160 ± 7 MeV, μ_B = 314 ± 39 MeV, V = 3503 ± 543 fm^3, γ_s = 0.81 ± 0.04
- For SIS at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.3 GeV, QvdW-HRG freeze-out: T = 48.3 ± 2 MeV, μ_B = 802 ± 23 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous QvdW-HRG studies by Vovchenko et al. (2015-2017) that calculated baryonic charge fluctuations along the freeze-out line, now adding consistent freeze-out extraction within the same model and including electric charge fluctuations, which are more experimentally accessible since nearly all electric charges can be detected. The results provide predictions testable by the STAR collaboration at RHIC BNL, NA61/SHINE at SPS CERN, and HADES at GSI, and offer a baseline for future searches for the QCD critical point at NICA and FAIR facilities, showing that the nuclear liquid-gas CP can produce signals in higher-order fluctuations even at beam energies far from the CP location.
