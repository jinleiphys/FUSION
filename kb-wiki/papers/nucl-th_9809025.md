# Renormalization of the Three-Body System with Short-Range Interactions

> We discuss renormalization of the non-relativistic three-body problem with short-range forces. The problem becomes non-perturbative at momenta of the order of the inverse of the two-body scattering length, and an infinite number of graphs must be summed. This summation leads to a cutoff dependence that does not appear in any order in perturbation theory. We argue that this cutoff dependence can be absorbed in a single three-body counterterm and compute the running of the three-body force with the cutoff. We comment on relevance of this result for the effective field theory program in nuclear and molecular physics.

## Key claim

The non-relativistic three-body problem with short-range forces and a large two-body scattering length requires a single three-body counterterm at leading order to absorb the cutoff dependence that arises from summing an infinite number of diagrams, even though each individual leading-order three-body diagram is ultraviolet finite.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory (EFT) with a non-relativistic boson field, rewritten using a dummy "dibaryon" field to simplify the resummation of two-body interactions. They derive an integral equation for particle/bound-state scattering (Eq. 4) and analyze its asymptotic behavior for momenta much larger than the inverse scattering length but much smaller than the cutoff, finding that the solution has an oscillatory form with a phase that depends on the cutoff. They then introduce a three-body force term and show analytically and numerically that its coupling can be tuned as a function of the cutoff to render physical observables cutoff-independent.

## Key numbers

- The critical value of the parameter lambda below which Eq. (6) has only real roots is lambda_c = 3*sqrt(3)/(4*pi) ≈ 0.4135; for the boson case (lambda=1) there are two imaginary solutions s = ± i s_0 with s_0 ≈ 1.0064.
- For the numerical example with a_3 = 1.56 a_2, the effective range for particle/bound-state scattering is predicted as r_3 = 0.57 a_2.
- For the shallowest bound state, the cutoff-independent binding energy is B_3 = 1.5/(M a_2^2).
- Using phenomenological values for helium-4 (a_2 = 124.7 Å, a_3 = 195 Å), the effective range becomes r_3 = 71 Å and the excited trimer binding energy is B_3 = 1.2 mK.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory program for two-body systems with large scattering lengths (as developed in effective range theory and earlier EFT work) to the three-body case, resolving a puzzle where three-body amplitudes showed sensitivity to the ultraviolet cutoff even though individual diagrams were finite. The authors show that a single three-body counterterm at leading order is necessary and sufficient for renormalization, enabling systematic EFT calculations for systems such as nucleons and helium-4 atoms; the results are relevant to the approach of Ref. 3musketeers where pions are treated perturbatively, and the integral equations are similar to those for three-fermion systems with internal quantum numbers.
