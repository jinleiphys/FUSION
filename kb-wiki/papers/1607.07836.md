# Manifestations of Isospin in Nearest Neighbor Spacing Distributions for the f-p Model Space

> The strong interactions are charge independent. If we limit ourselves to the strong interactions, we have the isospin $T$ as a good quantum number. Here we consider the lack of level repulsion of states of different isospin and how this effect manifests in nearest neighbor spacing (NNS) histograms, which provide a visual and statistical context in which to study distributions of energy level spacings. In particular, we study nucleons in the f-p model space for the nucleus $^{44}$Ti. We also study the effect of the Coulomb interaction on the level spacing distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the f-p model space for ⁴⁴Ti, nearest neighbor spacing (NNS) distributions become more Wigner-like (exhibiting level repulsion) when states are restricted to a single isospin T, but become more Poisson-like when states of different isospin are mixed, because charge-independent interactions cause no level repulsion between states of different isospin. The authors note that even in single-isospin cases, there remain deviations from an ideal Wigner distribution, with entries near zero spacing and a better fit to Poisson at large spacings.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed using the Nushellx program with the GXFP1 interaction in the f-p model space for the nucleus ⁴⁴Ti. NNS histograms were generated for J=4⁺ and J=5⁺ states, separately for T=0, T=1, and T=2 isospin values, then for mixed isospin combinations, and finally including the Coulomb interaction. The ground state region (lowest 5-10% of spacings) was omitted from each configuration to avoid distortion of variance calculations.

## Key numbers

- For J=4⁺, T=0 only: mean spacing = 0.109, variance = 0.611 (with mean normalized to 1)
- For J=4⁺, T=1 only: mean spacing = 0.092, variance = 0.975
- For J=4⁺, T=2 only: mean spacing = 0.237, variance = 0.548
- For J=4⁺, mixed T=0 and T=1: mean spacing = 0.052, variance = 1.272
- For J=4⁺, mixed T=0, T=1, and T=2: mean spacing = 0.043, variance = 1.188
- For J=4⁺ with Coulomb interaction included: mean spacing = 0.038, variance = 0.672
- For J=5⁺, T=0 only: mean spacing = 0.127, variance = 0.884
- For J=5⁺, T=1 only: mean spacing = 0.105, variance = 0.892
- For J=5⁺, T=2 only: mean spacing = 0.266, variance = 0.535
- For J=5⁺, mixed T=0, T=1, and T=2: mean spacing = 0.049, variance = 1.037
- For J=5⁺ with Coulomb interaction included: mean spacing = 0.045, variance = 0.536
- The Wigner distribution has variance 4/π - 1 ≈ 0.273, while the Poisson distribution has variance 1

## Context

This work extends Wigner's (1950s) original insight that symmetries affect level repulsion in NNS distributions, applying it specifically to isospin symmetry in the f-p model space. The authors compare their results to experimental work by Huizenga and Kasanov (showing Poisson-like behavior from mixed parity states) and by Garg et al. (showing Wigner-like behavior from dominantly J=1/2⁺ resonant states in n + ²³²Th up to 3.9 keV), noting that the Garg et al. results match the pattern seen in their single-isospin calculations. The work enables further investigation into why single-isospin calculations still show deviations from ideal Wigner distributions, and demonstrates that including the Coulomb interaction (which breaks isospin symmetry) restores more Wigner-like behavior.
