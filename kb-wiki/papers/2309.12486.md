# Revealing the Origin of Mass through Studies of Hadron Spectra and Structure

> The Higgs boson is responsible for roughly 1% of the visible mass in the Universe. Obviously, therefore, Nature has another, very effective way of generating mass. In working toward identifying the mechanism, contemporary strong interaction theory has arrived at a body of basic predictions, viz. the emergence of a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge, and dressed-quarks with constituent-like masses. These three phenomena - the pillars of emergent hadron mass (EHM) - explain the origin of the vast bulk of visible mass in the Universe. Their expressions in hadron observables are manifold. This contribution highlights a few; namely, some of the roles of EHM in building the meson spectrum, producing the leading-twist pion distribution amplitude, and moulding hadron charge and mass distributions.

# Revealing the Origin of Mass through Studies of Hadron Spectra and Structure

## Key claim
Emergent hadron mass (EHM), not the Higgs boson, is the source of 94% of the visible mass in the Universe, with the Higgs responsible for only roughly 1% of the proton mass. The paper argues that three pillars of EHM (a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge that saturates at infrared momenta, and dressed-quarks with constituent-like masses) are expressed in every strong interaction observable, and that these predictions can be tested at current and planned experimental facilities.

## Method
The author uses continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) methods with symmetry-preserving truncation schemes to compute meson spectra, distribution amplitudes, and form factors. A key improvement over rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation is the inclusion of a dynamically generated dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment (ACM) in the gluon+quark interaction kernel, which expresses EHM effects. For the pion mass distribution, the author reconstructs the pion generalized parton distribution (GPD) from available experimental data on the pion electromagnetic form factor and parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers
- The Higgs boson is responsible for roughly 1% of the visible mass in the Universe; EHM is the source of 94% of the proton mass, with the remaining 5% generated by constructive EHM+Higgs interference.
- The mean absolute relative difference between RL truncation meson masses and central experimental values is 13(8)%; the EHM (ACM) corrected kernel delivers 2.9(2.7)%, a 4.6-fold improvement.
- The pion charge radius is r_pi = 0.64(2) fm; the data-driven prediction for the pion mass distribution radius ratio is r_pi^{theta2}/r_pi = 0.79(3), corresponding to a spacetime volume ratio of 0.40(6).
- The kaon distribution amplitude peak is shifted to x ≈ 0.4, not the naive Higgs-driven expectation of x ≈ 0.02, because the asymmetry is controlled by the ratio of dressed-quark constituent-like masses M_u(0)/M_s-bar(0).
- The phi-prime to phi mass splitting matches experiment to within 2% in the EHM-corrected kernel.

## Context
This work extends the continuum bound-state approach pioneered by Munczek (1994) and Bender (1996), and the rainbow-ladder truncation scheme of Maris (1997, 2003). It directly challenges the quark model paradigm cited in the PDG (Workman, 2022) and the claim by Godfrey (1985) that all mesons can be described in a unified quark model, noting that quark model orbital angular momentum and spin are not Poincaré-invariant quantum numbers. The paper enables future experimental tests of EHM at new facilities such as those described by Brodsky (2020), Chen (2020), Anderle (2021), Arrington (2021), Wang (2022), Accardi (2023), Carman (2023), and Quintans (2022).
