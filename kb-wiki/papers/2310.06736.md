# The 1 $\rightarrow$ 3 Massive Splitting Functions from QCD Factorization and SCET

> Splitting functions are universal functions describing the collinear dynamics of gauge theories, and as such are crucial ingredients for a wide variety of calculations in perturbative QCD. We present analytic results for the triple collinear splitting functions in QCD with a single massive parton. We derive the splitting functions using two distinct methods; first by expanding the squared matrix elements in the collinear limit, and secondly by using soft-collinear effective theory with massive quarks. We find agreement between these two approaches, providing a strong check of our results. Additionally, we also check all iterated and soft limits of our results, finding agreement with predictions from factorization. Our results provide an important ingredient for higher order perturbative calculations involving massive partons, and for the description of the collinear dynamics of heavy flavor jets.

## Key claim

The paper presents analytic results for the triple collinear splitting functions in QCD with a single massive parton, derived using two independent methods (collinear expansion of squared matrix elements and soft-collinear effective theory with massive quarks), with the caveat that the results are at leading order in the strong coupling constant and apply specifically to the case where exactly one of the three collinear partons carries a mass.

## Method

The authors computed the 1 to 3 splitting functions by first expanding the relevant squared QCD matrix elements in the triple collinear limit and comparing with the predictions of QCD factorization, and independently by using soft-collinear effective theory with massive partons (SCET_M). The splitting functions were organized into mass-independent terms (identical to known massless triple collinear splitting functions but with modified Mandelstam variables) plus explicit mass-dependent corrections, and all iterated and soft limits were checked against predictions from factorization.

## Key numbers

- The mass-dependent correction for the Q to q-bar q Q splitting channel is M = - C_F T_R * (2 m^2 / s_tilde_12), where m is the heavy quark mass and s_tilde_12 is the modified Mandelstam variable for the light parton pair
- The splitting functions are computed in d = 4 - 2 epsilon dimensions, with the most singular terms in the collinear limit scaling as O(1/lambda^4) where lambda is the collinear expansion parameter
- The massive parton mass scales like m ~ p_perp_j ~ lambda * p^- in the collinear limit, where p^- is the large light-cone momentum component

## Context

This work extends the known massless triple collinear splitting functions computed at tree level by Catani (1998) and Campbell (1997) to include a single massive parton, and complements the only previously available compact massive splitting functions for the 1 to 2 case by Catani (2000). The results provide an important ingredient for higher-order perturbative calculations involving heavy quarks, for describing the collinear dynamics of heavy flavor jets, and for calculating jet substructure observables such as the three-point energy correlator on heavy quark jets.
