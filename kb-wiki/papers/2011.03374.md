# Covariant formulation of gluon self-energy in presence of ellipsoidal anisotropy

> In this work, a covariant formulation of the gluon self-energy in presence of ellipsoidal anisotropy is considered. It is shown that the general structure of the gluon self-energy can be written in terms of six linearly independent projection tensors. Similar to the spheroidal anisotropy, mass scales can be introduced for each of the collective modes considering the static limits. With a simplified ellipsoidal generalization of the Romatschke-Strickland form, the angular dependencies of the mass scales are studied. It is observed that, compared to the spheroidal case, additional unstable mode may appear in presence of ellipsoidal anisotropy depending upon the choice of the parameters.

# Covariant formulation of gluon self-energy in presence of ellipsoidal anisotropy

## Key claim

The general structure of the gluon self-energy in a medium with ellipsoidal momentum-space anisotropy can be expressed in terms of six linearly independent projection tensors, and the resulting collective modes exhibit an additional unstable mode compared to the spheroidal case, but only for certain choices of the anisotropy parameters (specifically, when both anisotropy parameters are negative).

## Method

The authors construct a covariant tensor basis for the gluon polarization tensor using two anisotropy four-vectors (a^μ and b^μ) together with the heat-bath velocity (u^μ) and the gluon momentum (P^μ), imposing transversality and symmetry to reduce the basis to six independent tensors via a Gram-Schmidt-like orthogonalization procedure. The form factors are extracted numerically from the hard-loop gluon polarization tensor using an ellipsoidal generalization of the Romatschke-Strickland distribution, and the collective modes are obtained from the poles of the effective gluon propagator derived from the Dyson-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The QCD Debye mass scale is defined as m_D^2 = (N_c + N_f/2) g_s^2 Λ^2 / 3, where Λ is a temperature-like scale that reduces to the temperature in equilibrium.
- For the isotropic case (ξ_a = 0, ξ_b = 0), the transverse form factor is Π_T = (m_D^2/2)(ω^2/p^2)[1 - (ω^2 - p^2)/(2ωp) ln((ω+p)/(ω-p))] and the longitudinal form factor is Π_L = (m_D^2/ũ^2)[1 - ω/(2p) ln((ω+p)/(ω-p))], with ũ^2 = -p^2/P^2.
- For the ellipsoidal case with (ξ_a, ξ_b) = (5, 10) and propagation angles (θ_p, φ_p) = (π/4, π/3), the form factors λ and σ are an order of magnitude smaller than the other form factors.
- For (ξ_a, ξ_b) = (-0.5, -0.9), the mass scale corresponding to the Ω_- mode becomes negative (indicating instability) for certain θ_p values, unlike the spheroidal case with (ξ_a, ξ_b) = (0, -0.9) which remains stable throughout the θ_p range.
- The growth rate Γ_{Ω_-} for the unstable mode with (ξ_a, ξ_b) = (-0.5, -0.9) and (θ_p, φ_p) = (5π/12, 5π/12) has amplitudes similar to the spheroidal case.

## Context

This work extends the Romatschke-Strickland (2003) formalism for spheroidal anisotropy to the ellipsoidal case, providing a covariant tensor decomposition that was absent in the previous study of ellipsoidal anisotropy by Kasmaei (2018). The formulation enables the definition of mass scales in the static limit, analogous to the spheroidal case, which is essential for studying the heavy quark potential in anisotropic media as done by Nopoush (2017). The results are consistent with Kasmaei (2018) regarding the appearance of direction-dependent unstable modes, but the covariant approach allows systematic identification of instabilities through negative squared mass scales and provides a framework for future calculations of the hard-thermal loop resummed heavy quark potential in ellipsoidally anisotropic QGP.
