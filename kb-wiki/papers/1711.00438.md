# Signatures of $α$ clustering in ultra-relativistic collisions with light nuclei

> We explore possible observable signatures of $α$ clustering of light nuclei in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions involving ${}^{7,9}$Be, ${}^{12}$C, and ${}^{16}$O. The clustering leads to specific spatial correlations of the nucleon distributions in the ground state, which are manifest in the earliest stage of the ultra-high energy reaction. The formed initial state of the fireball is sensitive to these correlations, and the effect influences, after the collective evolution of the system, the hadron production in the final stage. Specifically, we study effects on the harmonic flow in collisions of light clustered nuclei with a heavy target (${}^{208}$Pb), showing that measures of the elliptic flow are sensitive to clusterization in ${}^{7,9}$Be, whereas triangular flow is sensitive to clusterization in ${}^{12}$C and ${}^{16}$O. Specific predictions are made for model collisions at the CERN SPS energies. In another exploratory development we also examine the proton-beryllium collisions, where the $3/2^-$ ground state of ${}^{7,9}$Be nucleus is polarized by an external magnetic field. Clusterization leads to multiplicity distributions of participant nucleons which depend on the orientation of the polarization with respect to the collision axis, as well as on the magnetic number of the state. The obtained effects on multiplicities reach a factor of a few for collisions with a large number of participant nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that alpha clustering in light nuclei (7,9Be, 12C, 16O) produces observable signatures in harmonic flow coefficients of ultra-relativistic collisions with a heavy target, but only for the highest-multiplicity events where random fluctuations from nucleon distributions are reduced; the effects are also conditional on the specific flow measure used (elliptic for beryllium, triangular for carbon and oxygen).

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model (implemented in GLISSANDO) to simulate collisions of clustered light nuclei with 208Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=17 GeV, corresponding to CERN SPS energies. Nucleon positions were generated within clusters arranged in specific geometric configurations (dumbbell for beryllium, triangle for carbon, tetrahedron for oxygen), with cluster sizes and separations tuned to reproduce one-body densities from Variational Monte Carlo calculations. For proton-beryllium collisions, the Peierls-Yoccoz projection method was applied to generate polarized states with definite magnetic quantum numbers m=1/2 and m=3/2.

## Key numbers

- Cluster separation distances: l=3.2 fm for 7Be, l=3.6 fm for 9Be, l=2.8 fm for 12C, l=3.2 fm for 16O
- Alpha cluster rms radii: r_alpha=1.2 fm for 7Be, r_alpha=1.1 fm for 9Be, 12C, and 16O
- 3He cluster rms radius in 7Be: r_3He=1.4 fm
- Extra neutron distribution parameter in 9Be: r_n=1.9 fm
- Nucleon-nucleon expulsion distance: 0.9 fm
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section at SPS energies: sigma_inel=32 mb
- Binary collision weight parameter: alpha=0.12
- Gaussian smearing width for entropy sources: 0.4 fm
- For proton-beryllium collisions, the probability of wounding N_w=5 nucleons differs by about a factor of 5 between m=1/2 and m=3/2 states when the magnetic field is parallel to the beam axis

## Context

This work extends the approach proposed by Broniowski (2013) and Bozek (2014) for 12C to additional light nuclei (7Be, 9Be, 16O), and builds on the successful description of harmonic flow in d+Au collisions (Bozek 2011) and 3He+Au collisions (Nagle 2013, Bozek 2014), which were later confirmed experimentally by Adare (2013, 2015). The results enable experimental tests at the NA61/SHINE experiment at CERN SPS using secondary beams of light nuclei, and the novel proposal for polarized beryllium-proton collisions opens a potential new direction for future experiments with polarized targets or beams.
