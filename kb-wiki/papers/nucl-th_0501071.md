# Full-coupled channel approach to S=-2 s-shell hypernuclear systems

> We describe full-coupled-channel ab initio calculations among the octet baryons for S=-2 s-shell hypernuclei,_{LambdaLambda}^4H,_{LambdaLambda}^5H and_{LambdaLambda}^6He. The wave function includes Lambda Lambda, Lambda Sigma, N Xi, and Sigma Sigma channels. Minnesota NN, D2^\prime YN and Nijmegen model D simulated YY interactions are used. This is the first attempt to explore the few-body problem of the full-coupled channel scheme for A=4-6, S=-2 multistrangeness hypernuclear systems. Bound state solutions of the Lambda Lambda hypernuclei,_{LambdaLambda}^4H,_{LambdaLambda}^5H and _{LambdaLambda}^6He, are obtained.

# Full-coupled channel approach to S=-2 s-shell hypernuclear systems

## Key claim

The paper presents the first full-coupled-channel ab initio calculations for S=-2 s-shell hypernuclei, obtaining bound state solutions for _ΛΛ_^4H, _ΛΛ_^5H, and _ΛΛ_^6He. However, the results depend on the choice of the YY potential, and the calculated _B_ΛΛ(_ΛΛ_^6He) is slightly larger than the experimental value, with the calculated _B_ΛΛ(_ΛΛ_^4H) being very close to but lower than the _Λ_^3H + Λ threshold.

## Method

The wave function includes ΛΛ, ΛΣ, NΞ, and ΣΣ channels for systems of A=4-6 octet baryons with strangeness S=-2, using a 4x4 Hamiltonian matrix. The Minnesota potential is used for NN interactions, D2' for YN interactions (with the long-range part in the ^3S_1 ΛN-ΛN channel reduced by a factor 0.954 to reproduce the experimental _B_Λ(_Λ_^5He) value), and the Nijmegen model D simulated (ND(S)) potential for YY interactions with hard core radii of 0.56 fm (^1S_0) and 0.45 fm (^3S_1). The stochastic variational method is employed to solve the few-body problem.

## Key numbers

- _B_Λ(_Λ_^3H) = 0.056 MeV (calculated) vs 0.13(5) MeV (experimental)
- _B_Λ(_Λ_^4H) = 2.23 MeV (calculated) vs 2.04(4) MeV (experimental)
- _B_Λ(_Λ_^4H*) = 0.91 MeV (calculated) vs 1.00(4) MeV (experimental)
- _B_Λ(_Λ_^5He) = 3.18 MeV (calculated) vs 3.12(2) MeV (experimental)
- _B_ΛΛ(_ΛΛ_^4H) = 0.107 MeV (calculated)
- _B_ΛΛ(_ΛΛ_^5H) = 4.04 MeV (calculated)
- _B_ΛΛ(_ΛΛ_^6He) = 7.93 MeV (calculated) vs 7.3(3) MeV (experimental)
- Δ_B_ΛΛ(calc)(_ΛΛ_^6He) = 1.58 MeV vs Δ_B_ΛΛ(exp)(_ΛΛ_^6He) = 1.01 ± 0.20 +0.18/-0.11 MeV
- Probability of NΞ component in _ΛΛ_^5H: 4.34%
- Probability of ΛΣ component in _ΛΛ_^5H: 2.52%
- Probability of ΣΣ component in _ΛΛ_^5H: 0.05%
- Mass difference between ΛΛ and ΣΣ channels: approximately 155 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Nemura (2003) on S=-2 hypernuclei and addresses the long-standing _Λ_^5He anomaly identified by Dalitz et al. (1972), where a single-channel ΛN potential could not reproduce the experimental _B_Λ(_Λ_^5He) value. Akaishi et al. recently showed that explicit Σ degrees of freedom resolve this anomaly, motivating the full-coupled-channel approach. The calculations use the D2' YN potential (modified from the original D2 potential by Akaishi et al.) and the Nijmegen model D simulated YY potential with hard core, avoiding unrealistic bound states found in soft core models such as NSC97e or NSC97f. The results enable future comprehensive studies using various YY interaction models.
