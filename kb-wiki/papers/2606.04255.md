# Contact interaction treatment of π and ρ elastic and transition tensor form factors

> Predictions for tensor charges and form factors, elastic and transition, involving $π$- and $ρ$-mesons and their scalar and axialvector diquark partners, are delivered using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector*vector contact interaction (SCI). Two distinct SCI regularisation schemes are employed, with the results showing little sensitivity. Although, as typical in SCI analyses, the form factors are stiff; their infrared behaviour may be considered physically reliable. Notable amongst related quantities are the following: the pion tensor charge is approximately $0.36$ and the associated tensor form factor radius is practically the same as the pion charge radius; the $ρ$-meson tensor charge is roughly 80% of that for the proton; and diquark tensor charges and form factors are semiquantitatively alike with those of their $π$, $ρ$ partners. In addition to being interesting in themselves, the SCI predictions can serve as baselines for future studies with a closer connection to QCD.

# Contact interaction treatment of π and ρ elastic and transition tensor form factors

## Key claim
The symmetry-preserving contact interaction (SCI) framework predicts that the pion tensor charge is approximately 0.36 and that the pion tensor form factor radius is practically the same as the pion charge radius, but these results are only considered physically reliable in the infrared domain because SCI analyses typically produce stiff form factors.

## Method
The authors employed a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector*vector contact interaction (SCI) within the rainbow-ladder truncation of continuum Schwinger function methods, solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-tensor vertex. Two distinct SCI regularisation schemes were used to provide an indication of systematic uncertainty. All results were interpreted as valid at the hadron scale ζ_H = 0.33 GeV, with all-orders evolution applied to obtain predictions at higher resolving scales.

## Key numbers
- Pion tensor charge at ζ_2 = 2 GeV: κ_T^π ≈ 0.36(3), with SCI-I giving 0.37 and SCI-II giving 0.32
- Pion tensor charge radius: r_T^π = 0.57 fm (SCI-I) and 0.60 fm (SCI-II), compared to lQCD result 0.67(10) fm
- ρ-meson tensor charge is roughly 80% of that for the proton
- Dressed light-quark tensor charge at hadron scale: δ_T^{ζ_H} u = 1 = δ_T^{ζ_H} d
- Evolved light-quark tensor charge at ζ_2 = 2 GeV: δ_T^{ζ_2} u = 0.76(11)
- Isoscalar-scalar diquark tensor charge: κ_T^{0+} = 1.72 (SCI-I) and 1.76 (SCI-II)
- Isoscalar-scalar diquark tensor radius: r_T^{0+} = 0.60 fm (SCI-I) and 0.62 fm (SCI-II)

## Context
This work extends previous SCI analyses of meson properties, including those by Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010) who introduced the SCI framework, and complements lattice QCD simulations by Alexandrou (2021) on pion tensor form factors. The results provide baselines for future studies using QCD-connected interactions, such as those by Qin (2011) and Binosi (2014), and can reveal sensitivity to the pointwise behavior of the quark-quark interaction and emergent hadron mass phenomena. The diquark tensor form factors are relevant for baryon structure studies following the work of Barabanov (2020) and Ding (2022).
