# Lambda hyperon polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions from the chiral kinetic approach

> Based on the chiral kinetic approach using initial conditions from a multiphase transport model, we study the spin polarizations of quarks and antiquarks in non-central heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Because of the non-vanishing vorticity field in these collisions, quarks and antiquarks are found to acquire appreciable spin polarizations in the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane. Converting quarks and antiquarks to hadrons via the coalescence model, we further calculate the spin polarizations of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons and find their values comparable to those measured in experiments by the STAR Collaboration.

# Lambda hyperon polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions from the chiral kinetic approach

## Key claim
Using the chiral kinetic approach with initial conditions from a multiphase transport model (AMPT), the authors find that Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperon spin polarizations in non-central heavy ion collisions are comparable to STAR Collaboration measurements, but only when the numerical factor in the chiral kinetic equations is set to 6 (rather than 2) to match the quantum kinetic approach result, and when including vorticity-modified scattering cross sections.

## Method
The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT model to generate initial phase-space distributions of quarks and antiquarks with random helicities. They evolved these partons using chiral kinetic equations of motion that include a Coriolis force from the vorticity field, with a modified phase-space measure factor sqrt(G) = 1 + a*lambda*|p|*(omega dot b) where a=6. Parton scatterings were modified by weighting collision probabilities with sqrt(G(p3)*G(p4)). After the partonic phase, polarized quarks and antiquarks were converted to Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons using a coalescence model that explicitly accounts for spin degrees of freedom via the Wigner function of the hyperon.

## Key numbers
- The vorticity field in the produced quark-gluon plasma can reach a very large value of 10^21 s^-1.
- Impact parameter b = 8.87 fm was used to correspond to the 20-50% centrality bin in Au+Au collisions, obtained from sigma_in = 705 fm^2.
- The root-mean-squared radius of Lambda was taken as 0.877 fm, leading to sigma_rho = 0.89 fm and sigma_lambda = 0.51 fm with quark masses of 3 MeV (u), 6 MeV (d), and 100 MeV (s).
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the total spin polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons in the y-direction (along total orbital angular momentum) at midrapidity |y| <= 1 has a value of 2.44%.
- The spin polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons decreases with increasing collision energy from 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV.
- Including feed-down corrections from resonance decays would reduce the polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons by 15% to 20%.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Lambda hyperon polarization using statistical-hydrodynamic approaches (Becattini et al., 2013; Karpenko et al., 2017) and transport models (Li et al., 2017) by incorporating non-equilibrium effects through the chiral kinetic approach. Unlike earlier studies that calculated polarization from equilibrium distributions in the final state, this work explicitly evolves quark and antiquark spin polarizations during the partonic phase using chiral kinetic equations of motion. The approach enables future study of local structure in spin polarization and non-equilibrium effects that cannot be addressed by hydrodynamic models alone. The results are compared with experimental data from the STAR Collaboration (2017) and show similar trends to other theoretical approaches, though the authors note they cannot explain the seemingly larger anti-Lambda than Lambda polarization seen in data.
