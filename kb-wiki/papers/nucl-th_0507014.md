# Relativistic Transport Approach to Collective Nuclear Dynamics

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) and isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in finite nuclei are studied in the framework of a relativistic transport approach. The kinetic equations are derived within an effective nucleon-meson field theory in the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) scheme, even extended to density dependent vertices. Small amplitude oscillations are analysed using the Relativistic Vlasov (RV) approach, i.e. neglecting nucleon collision terms. The time evolution of the isoscalar monopole moment and isovector dipole moment and the corresponding Fourier power spectra are discussed. In the case of ^{208}Pb we study in detail the dependence of the monopole response on the effective mass and symmetry energy at saturation given by the used covariant effective interaction. We show that a reduced m^* and a larger a_4 can compensate the effect on the ISGMR energy centroid of a much larger compressibility modulus K_{nm}. This result is important in order to overcome the conflicting determination of the nuclear compressibility between non-relativistic and relativistic effective interactions. For the symmetry energy dynamical effects, we carefully analyze the influence of the inclusion of an effective isovector scalar channel, δ-meson field, with constant and density dependent couplings. We show the relevance of the $slope$ (or pressure) of the symmetry energy at saturation on the ISGMR and IVGDR modes for neutron-rich systems. Density dependent vertices are not much affecting our conclusions. Following as a guidance some extended dispersion relations in nuclear matter, we see two main reasons for that, the smoothness of the density dependences around saturation and the presence of compensation effects coming from rearrangement terms.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a reduced effective mass (m*) and a larger symmetry energy at saturation (a4) can compensate the effect on the ISGMR energy centroid of a much larger compressibility modulus (Knm), potentially resolving the conflicting determination of nuclear compressibility between non-relativistic and relativistic effective interactions.

## Method

The authors used a Relativistic Vlasov (RV) transport approach derived from an effective nucleon-meson field theory in the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) scheme, neglecting nucleon collision terms. They simulated small amplitude oscillations of the isoscalar monopole moment and isovector dipole moment in 208Pb using the Relativistic Landau-Vlasov (RLV) test particle method with 100 test particles per nucleon, analyzing the Fourier power spectra to extract excitation energies for various RMF parametrizations with different coupling parameters.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ISGMR in 208Pb: 13.7 ± 0.3 MeV
- NL3 model ISGMR centroid: 13.39 (hbar omega) with Knm = 272 MeV and m*/m = 0.60
- NL2-G model ISGMR centroid: 11.78 (hbar omega) with Knm = 210 MeV and m*/m = 0.83
- NL1-G compressibility modulus: 380 MeV
- NL2-G compressibility modulus: 210 MeV
- NL3 compressibility modulus: 272 MeV
- NL3 symmetry energy a4: 37.40 MeV
- NL1-G and NL2-G symmetry energy a4: 30.62 MeV
- 208Pb asymmetry parameter I = 0.21

## Context

This work extends relativistic transport studies of giant resonances in finite nuclei, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between non-relativistic RPA calculations (Blaizot 1980, Blaizot 1995) supporting Knm around 210-220 MeV and relativistic RPA calculations (Vretenar 1997, Ma 2001) pointing to 250-270 MeV. The results enable a unified interpretation of nuclear compressibility by showing that effective mass and symmetry energy effects can reconcile the two approaches, and demonstrate the relevance of the slope (pressure) of the symmetry energy at saturation for both ISGMR and IVGDR modes in neutron-rich systems.
