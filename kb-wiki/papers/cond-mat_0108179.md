# Effective action and density functional theory

> The effective action for the charge density and the photon field is proposed as a generalization of the density functional. A simple definition is given for the density functional, as the functional Legendre transform of the generator functional of connected Green functions for the density and the photon field, offering systematic approximation schemes. The leading order of the perturbation expansion reproduces the Hartree-Fock equation. A renormalization group motivated method is introduced to turn on the Coulomb interaction gradually and to find corrections to the Hartree-Fock and the Kohn-Sham schemes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the density functional for electronic systems can be rigorously defined as the functional Legendre transform of the generator functional for connected Green functions of the charge density and photon field, and that a renormalization-group motivated evolution equation can systematically improve the Hartree-Fock and Kohn-Sham schemes beyond perturbation theory, provided the ground state can be reached smoothly by turning on the Coulomb interaction from zero.

## Method

The authors introduce a control parameter λ that scales the Coulomb interaction (e → λe) in the Euclidean path integral action for electrons coupled to a photon field. They derive an exact functional differential equation for the λ-dependent effective action Γ_λ[ρ] (the density functional) by differentiating the generator functional W_λ[σ,j] with respect to λ and using the Legendre transform relations. The evolution equation is then integrated from λ₀ ≈ 0 (non-interacting system) to λ = 1 (physical system), with the initial condition given by the one-loop (non-interacting) effective action. The method is applied at various truncation levels (local, bi-local, tri-local) of the density functional, where the bi-local truncation reproduces the Hartree-Fock scheme plus an infinite sum of radiative corrections to the exchange interaction.

## Key numbers

- The leading order of the perturbation expansion reproduces the Hartree-Fock equation; no explicit numerical results for energies or densities are given.
- The evolution equation (Eq. 5.9, labeled \eq{seveq}) is: ∂_λ Γ_λ[ρ] = -λ e² { ρ·Δ⁻¹·ρ + tr[ (δ²Γ_λ[ρ]/δρδρ)⁻¹ Δ⁻¹ ] }, where Δ is the Laplacian and e is the elementary charge.
- The ground state energy for non-interacting electrons is given as E_gr = -lim_{β→∞} (1/β) n_s tr log G⁻¹, where n_s is the number of degenerate spin states and G is the free electron propagator.
- The density functional at the bi-local truncation level reproduces the Hartree-Fock solution together with an infinite sum of radiative corrections to the exchange interaction and the ground state energy; no specific numerical values are provided.

## Context

This work extends the formal connection between density functional theory and the effective action formalism previously explored by the same authors and others (Hohenberg-Kohn 1964, Kohn-Sham 1965) by introducing a renormalization group method to turn on the Coulomb interaction gradually. The approach generalizes the adiabatic approximation of density functional theory (Pines 1963, Langreth-Perdew 1975) and the coordinate scaling method (Levy-Perdew 1985) by using an evolution equation that avoids the need to readjust the external potential. The paper enables systematic improvement of the Kohn-Sham scheme beyond the local density approximation and gradient expansion (Hohenberg-Kohn 1964, Kohn-Sham 1965) by providing a multi-local cluster expansion that can be truncated at the bi-local level to include exchange and radiative corrections.
