# $ϕ$ meson mass and decay width in nuclear matter and nuclei

> The mass and decay width of the $ϕ$ meson in cold nuclear matter are computed in an effective Lagrangian approach. The medium dependence of these properties are obtained by evaluating kaon-antikaon loop contributions to the $ϕ$ self-energy, employing the medium-modified kaon masses, calculated using the quark-meson coupling model. The loop integral is regularized with a dipole form factor, and the sensitivity of the results to the choice of cutoff mass in the form factor is investigated. At normal nuclear matter density we find a downward shift of the $ϕ$ mass by a few percent, while the decay width is enhanced by an order of magnitude. For a large variation of the cutoff mass parameter, the results for the $ϕ$ mass and the decay width turn out to vary very little. Our results support results in the literature which suggest that one should observe a small downward mass shift and a large broadening of the decay width. In order to explore the possibility of studying the binding and absorption of $ϕ$ mesons in nuclei, we also present the single-particle binding energies and half-widths of $ϕ$-nucleus bound states for some selected nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors find that at normal nuclear matter density, the downward shift of the phi meson mass is only a few percent (1.8% on average), while the decay width is enhanced by an order of magnitude, with the results showing very little sensitivity to a large variation of the cutoff mass parameter in the dipole form factor used for regularization.

## Method

The phi meson self-energy in nuclear matter was computed using an effective Lagrangian with kaon-antikaon loop contributions, where the medium-modified kaon masses were calculated within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The loop integral was regularized with a dipole form factor, and the sensitivity to the cutoff mass parameter was investigated over a range of 1000-3000 MeV. For finite nuclei, the Schrodinger equation was solved using a complex phi-nucleus scalar potential determined by a local-density approximation based on the nuclear matter results.

## Key numbers

* At normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3), the in-medium kaon mass decreases by about 13%.
* For cutoff Lambda_K = 1000 MeV: m_phi* = 1009.3 MeV, Gamma_phi* = 37.7 MeV.
* For cutoff Lambda_K = 2000 MeV: m_phi* = 1000.9 MeV, Gamma_phi* = 34.8 MeV.
* For cutoff Lambda_K = 3000 MeV: m_phi* = 994.9 MeV, Gamma_phi* = 32.8 MeV.
* The average downward mass shift of the phi at normal nuclear matter density is 1.8% with a 0.7% standard deviation.
* The phi decay width broadens in average by a factor of 10 with a 0.7 standard deviation from the average at normal nuclear matter density.
* For the largest density treated (rho_B = 3 rho_0), the phi width enhancement reaches a factor of about 20.
* For phi-208Pb bound states with Lambda_K = 3000 MeV: 1s state binding energy E = -21.22 MeV (without imaginary part) and E = -21.06 MeV with half-width 16.25 MeV (with imaginary part).
* For phi-12C bound states with Lambda_K = 3000 MeV: 1s state binding energy E = -7.70 MeV (without imaginary part) and E = -6.47 MeV with half-width 11.00 MeV (with imaginary part).
* No bound state was found for phi-4He when the imaginary part of the potential was included.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of phi meson in-medium properties, including those by Ko et al. (1992) using density-dependent kaon masses from chiral perturbation theory, Hatsuda and Lee (1991, 1996) using QCD sum rules, and more recent calculations by Cabrera et al. (2016) reporting a 3.4% mass shift and 70 MeV width. The results support the experimental findings from KEK-E325 (2005), SPring8 (2004), and CLAS at JLab (2010) that suggest a small downward mass shift and large broadening, while the predictions for phi-nucleus bound states provide guidance for proposed experiments at J-PARC E16 and E29 collaborations and the JLab 12 GeV upgrade program.
