# Heavy quark dynamics within a Boltzmann transport model: radiative vs collisional energy loss

> We discuss the propagation of heavy quarks (charm and bottom) through the QGP by means of a relativistic Boltzmann transport approach including both collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms. In particular we investigate the impact of induced gluon radiation by dynamical QCD medium implementing in our transport model a formula for the emitted gluon spectrum calculated in a higher-twist scheme. We notice that in the region of high transverse momentum ($p_T > 10$ GeV) radiative processes play an essential role giving a dominant contribution to the generation of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ at momentum values for which the energy loss by collisions is in the perturbative regime.

# Heavy quark dynamics within a Boltzmann transport model: radiative vs collisional energy loss

## Key claim
Radiative processes (induced gluon radiation) dominate the generation of both the nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ for heavy quarks at high transverse momentum ($p_T > 10$ GeV), where collisional energy loss is in the perturbative regime and less effective; however, the authors note that non-perturbative effects modeled via a $p_T$-dependent $k$-factor are still required to describe the low-$p_T$ region.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation for heavy quarks (charm and bottom) propagating through a quark-gluon plasma, using a stochastic algorithm for the collision integral. Elastic two-body scatterings ($g+HQ \rightarrow g+HQ$) are included at pQCD leading order with a Debye screening mass and running coupling, while inelastic processes are implemented via a medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum derived from a higher-twist scheme, incorporating both the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) interference effect and the dead-cone effect due to the heavy quark mass.

## Key numbers
- The $p_T$-dependent $k$-factor enhancement is parameterized as $k(p_T) = [1 + (k_0 - 1)\exp(-p_T^2/(2\sigma_p^2))]$, with $k_0 = 4$ and $\sigma_p = 5$ GeV used in the simulations.
- The condition $k(p_T) \rightarrow 1$ is reached at $p_T > 10$ GeV, defining the transition to the perturbative regime.
- Simulations are performed for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ AGeV with impact parameter $b = 7.5$ fm (RHIC conditions).
- The charm quark mass is $M = 1.3$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends prior Boltzmann transport approaches for heavy quarks (e.g., the Catania group's earlier work in ctQGP_PRC90 and ctQGP_PRC89) by adding a radiative energy loss mechanism based on the higher-twist formalism previously applied in Langevin models (Cao, Qin, Bass, Muller, 2013) and linearized Boltzmann approaches (Cao, Luo, Qin, Wang, 2016). The study enables future inclusion of multiple-emission algorithms, coupling of emitted gluons to the bulk dynamics, and more differential observables such as azimuthal correlations, aiming to resolve the $R_{AA}$-$v_2$ puzzle at both RHIC and LHC energies.
