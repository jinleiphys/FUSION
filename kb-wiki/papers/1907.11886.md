# Removing the Wigner bound in non-perturbative effective field theory

> The Wigner bound, setting an upper limit on the scattering effective range, is examined at different orders of contact effective field theory. Using cutoff regulator we show that the bound loosens when higher orders of the theory are considered. For a sharp and a Gaussian regulators, we conjecture an analytic formula for the dependence of the Wigner bound on the theory's order. It follows that the bound vanishes in the limit of infinite order. Using a concrete numerical example we demonstrate that the above surmise still holds after renormalization at finite cutoff. Studying the 3-body system with this example, we have found that limiting the permissible range of cutoffs by the Wigner bound, we avoid the Thomas collapse, and don't need to promote the 3-body force to leading order.

## Key claim

The Wigner bound, which limits the scattering effective range from above, loosens as the order of contact effective field theory (EFT) increases, and the authors conjecture that the bound vanishes entirely in the limit of infinite EFT order, thereby restoring renormalization group invariance for a complete theory.

## Method

The authors studied a contact EFT for scalar bosons, iterating the potential in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain the T-matrix. They derived analytic expressions for the Wigner bound parameter W^(n) up to order N^9LO (Gaussian regulator) and N^10LO (sharp cutoff) by taking the high-cutoff limit and maximizing the effective range over the low-energy constants. They then verified their findings with a concrete numerical example using the Volkov potential, fitting the LECs to reproduce the scattering length, effective range, and shape parameter at N^2LO, and calculating the triton binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Phillips, Beane, and Cohen (1998), who showed that at NLO the effective range is necessarily negative in the infinite-cutoff limit due to the Wigner bound. The authors demonstrate that including higher EFT orders relaxes this bound, contradicting the earlier conclusion that non-perturbative contact EFT cannot be renormalized. This finding enables the possibility of using non-perturbative EFT with positive effective range without promoting the 3-body force to leading order, which has practical computational advantages for many-body calculations.
