# Double-beta decay of $^{150}$Nd to excited levels of $^{150}$Sm

> The $2\nu2β$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to the first excited 740.5 keV $0^{+}_{1}$ level of $^{150}$Sm was measured over 5.845 yr with the help of a four-crystal low-background HPGe $γ$ spectrometry system in the underground low-background laboratory STELLA of LNGS-INFN. A 2.381 kg highly purified Nd-containing sample was employed as the decay source. The expected de-excitation gamma-quanta of the $0^{+}_{1}$ level with energies 334.0 keV and 406.5 keV were observed both in one-dimensional spectrum and in coincidence data resulting in the half-life $T_{1/2}=[0.83^{+0.18}_{-0.13}\mathrm{(stat)}^{+0.16}_{-0.19}\mathrm{(syst)}]\times 10^{20}$ yr. Interpreting an excess of the 334.0-keV peak area as an indication of the $2β$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to the 334.0 keV $2^+_1$ excited level of $^{150}$Sm with a half-life of $T_{1/2}=[1.5^{+2.3}_{-0.6}\mathrm{(stat)}\pm 0.4\mathrm{(syst)}]\times10^{20}$ yr, the $2\nu2β$ half-life of $^{150}$Nd for the transition to the 0$^{+}_{1}$ level is $T_{1/2}=[1.03^{+0.35}_{-0.22}\mathrm{(stat)}^{+0.16}_{-0.19}\mathrm{(syst)}]\times 10^{20}$ yr, in agreement with the previous experiments. Both half-life values reasonably agree with the theoretical calculations in the framework of proton-neutron QRPA with isospin restoration combined with like nucleon QRPA for description of excited states in the final nuclei. For $2\nu2β$ and $0\nu2β$ transitions of $^{150}$Nd and $^{148}$Nd to several excited levels of $^{150}$Sm and $^{148}$Sm, limits were set at level of $T_{1/2}>10^{20}-10^{21}$ yr.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of the $2\nu2\beta$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to the first excited $0^+_1$ level of $^{150}$Sm with a half-life of $T_{1/2}=[1.03^{+0.35}_{-0.22}\mathrm{(stat)}^{+0.16}_{-0.19}\mathrm{(syst)}]\times 10^{20}$ yr, but this value depends on interpreting an excess in the 334.0-keV peak as an indication of a competing decay to the $2^+_1$ excited level with half-life $T_{1/2}=[1.5^{+2.3}_{-0.6}\mathrm{(stat)}\pm 0.4\mathrm{(syst)}]\times10^{20}$ yr, which introduces a systematic ambiguity.

## Method

A 2.381 kg sample of highly purified Nd-containing material (natural neodymium abundance) was placed inside a four-crystal low-background HPGe gamma spectrometry system (GeMulti) located in the STELLA underground laboratory at LNGS-INFN, and data were collected over 5.845 yr. The de-excitation gamma rays from the $0^+_1$ level of $^{150}$Sm at 334.0 keV and 406.5 keV were searched for in both one-dimensional spectra and coincidence spectra, and the half-life was extracted from the observed peak areas after accounting for detector efficiency, sample composition, and background.

## Key numbers

* Half-life for $2\nu2\beta$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to the 740.5 keV $0^+_1$ level of $^{150}$Sm: $T_{1/2}=[0.83^{+0.18}_{-0.13}\mathrm{(stat)}^{+0.16}_{-0.19}\mathrm{(syst)}]\times 10^{20}$ yr (from direct observation of 334.0 keV and 406.5 keV gamma rays).
* Half-life for $2\nu2\beta$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to the 334.0 keV $2^+_1$ level of $^{150}$Sm: $T_{1/2}=[1.5^{+2.3}_{-0.6}\mathrm{(stat)}\pm 0.4\mathrm{(syst)}]\times10^{20}$ yr (interpreted from excess in the 334.0-keV peak area).
* Corrected half-life for $2\nu2\beta$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to the $0^+_1$ level, accounting for the $2^+_1$ contribution: $T_{1/2}=[1.03^{+0.35}_{-0.22}\mathrm{(stat)}^{+0.16}_{-0.19}\mathrm{(syst)}]\times 10^{20}$ yr.
* The sample contained $4.10(7)\times 10^{23}$ nuclei of $^{150}$Nd and $4.18(7)\times 10^{23}$ nuclei of $^{148}$Nd.
* Limits for $2\nu2\beta$ and $0\nu2\beta$ transitions of $^{150}$Nd and $^{148}$Nd to several excited levels were set at $T_{1/2}>10^{20}-10^{21}$ yr.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of the $2\nu2\beta$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to the $0^+_1$ excited level of $^{150}$Sm, which were reported by Barabash (2004) with $T_{1/2}=1.4^{+0.5}_{-0.4}\times 10^{20}$ yr, by Kidd (2014) with $T_{1/2}=1.07^{+0.46}_{-0.26}\times 10^{20}$ yr, and by the NEMO-3 collaboration (Aguerre, 2023) with $T_{1/2}=1.11^{+0.26}_{-0.21}\times 10^{20}$ yr. The present result agrees with these prior experiments and with theoretical calculations using proton-neutron QRPA with isospin restoration combined with like-nucleon QRPA. The improved precision and the first indication of decay to the $2^+_1$ level provide new constraints for nuclear matrix element calculations relevant to $0\nu2\beta$ decay searches.
