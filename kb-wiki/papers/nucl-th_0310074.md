# Hadrons in Nuclei

> Changes of hadronic properties in dense nuclear matter as predicted by theory have usually been investigated by means of relativistic heavy-ion reactions. In this talk I show that observable consequences of such changes can also be seen in more elementary reactions on nuclei. Particular emphasis is put on a discussion of photonuclear reactions; examples are the dilepton production at $\approx 1$ GeV and the hadron production in nuclei at 10 - 20 GeV photon energies. The observable effects are expected to be as large as in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and can be more directly related to the underlying hadronic changes.

# Hadrons in Nuclei

## Key claim

Observable consequences of in-medium changes of hadronic properties can be seen in elementary photonuclear reactions on normal nuclei, not only in relativistic heavy-ion collisions; the expected sensitivity in such reactions is as large as in heavy-ion collisions, but the results can be more directly related to the underlying hadronic changes because the spectator matter remains close to equilibrium.

## Method

The author performed coupled-channel transport calculations for photon-induced reactions on nuclear targets, treating coherent initial-state interactions (shadowing) and incoherent final-state interactions including elastic and inelastic scattering, particle production, and absorption of vector mesons. For dilepton production at 1–2 GeV photon energies, vector mesons were produced and transported with their correct spectral functions rather than with pole masses. For hadron production at 10–20 GeV photon energies, a novel expression for incoherent particle production was derived to separate coherent initial-state and incoherent final-state interactions.

## Key numbers

- In CERES Pb+Au collisions at 40 AGeV, about 1/2 of all dileptons come from densities lower than 2 ρ₀, based on transport simulations.
- For photon-induced dilepton production on ²⁰⁸Pb with a dilepton-momentum cut of 300 MeV, the ω-peak completely disappears from the spectrum when both collisional broadening and mass shift are included.
- For hadron production in high-energy electroproduction, observed hadron multiplicities can be described only with formation times > ≈ 0.3 fm; curves with larger formation times lie close together due to finite target nucleus size.
- Leading hadron cross section during formation is taken as 0.33 σ_h, where σ_h is the normal hadronic interaction cross section.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium hadron changes from the Delta-hole model (Ericson and Weise) and from heavy-ion experiments such as CERES at CERN SPS and DLS at BEVALAC. It provides a cleaner testing ground for assumptions used in analyzing heavy-ion reactions, where the signal integrates over nonequilibrium and equilibrium stages at various densities and temperatures. The photonuclear approach enables direct comparison with predictions from QCD sum rules (Leupold and Mosel) and with models of vector meson spectral functions (Peters, Post, Wambach, Rapp, Cassing). An experiment at JLAB is underway to verify the predicted disappearance of the ω-peak in dilepton spectra.
