# Projection method and new formulation of leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics

> The introduced earlier projection method for boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric systems is used to introduce a new formulation of anisotropic hydrodynamics that allows for three substantially different values of pressure acting locally in three different directions. Our considerations are based on the Boltzmann kinetic equation with the collision term treated in the relaxation time approximation and the momentum anisotropy is included explicitly in the leading term of the distribution function. A novel feature of our work is the complete analysis of the second moment of the Boltzmann equation, in addition to the zeroth and first moments that have been analyzed in earlier studies. We define the final equations of anisotropic hydrodynamics in the leading order as a subset of the analyzed moment equations (and their linear combinations) which agree with the Israel-Stewart theory in the case of small pressure anisotropies.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new formulation of leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics that allows for three substantially different pressure components acting locally in three different directions, by generalizing the Romatschke-Strickland form of the distribution function and analyzing the second moment of the Boltzmann equation in addition to the zeroth and first moments; the final equations are defined as a subset of the analyzed moment equations that agree with the Israel-Stewart theory in the case of small pressure anisotropies.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation and introduce an anisotropic distribution function characterized by three anisotropy parameters (ξ_X, ξ_Y, ξ_Z) that satisfy ξ_X + ξ_Y + ξ_Z = 0, generalizing the Romatschke-Strickland form. For boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric systems, they decompose all tensors using a basis of four orthogonal four-vectors (U, X, Y, Z) and analyze the zeroth, first, and second moments of the Boltzmann equation. From the complete set of second-moment equations, they select two equations (rather than taking one equation from the zeroth moment and another from the second moment) to construct the final leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics equations.

## Key numbers

- The particle number density for the anisotropic distribution function is n(λ,ξ) = 8π k λ³ / (√(1+ξ_X) √(1+ξ_Y) √(1+ξ_Z)), where λ is the momentum scale and k is a normalization constant.
- The equilibrium particle number density is n_eq(T) = 8π k T³.
- The energy density for the anisotropic distribution is ε(λ,ξ) = 24π k λ⁴ R(ξ), where R(ξ) is defined by an integral over angles.
- The equilibrium energy density is ε_eq(T) = 24π k T⁴.
- In the close-to-equilibrium limit, the pressure corrections are π_I ≈ -(32π k T⁴/5) ξ_I for I = X, Y, Z.
- The expansion tensor components are θ_X = -∂θ_⊥/∂r cosh θ_⊥ - ∂θ_⊥/∂τ sinh θ_⊥, θ_Y = -sinh θ_⊥/r, and θ_Z = -cosh θ_⊥/τ, where θ_⊥ is the transverse fluid rapidity, τ is proper time, and r is radial distance.

## Context

This work extends the projection method introduced by Florkowski (2011) for boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric systems, and generalizes earlier anisotropic hydrodynamics frameworks (Florkowski 2010, Martinez 2010, Ryblewski 2010) that used the Romatschke-Strickland form (Romatschke 2003) allowing only two distinct pressure components. The new formulation enables description of systems where all three pressure components differ already at leading order, which is relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where transverse expansion induces differences between transverse pressure components. The analysis of the second moment of the Boltzmann equation provides a foundation for extending the second-order anisotropic hydrodynamics developed by Bazow, Heinz, and Strickland (2013) to cases with arbitrary transverse expansion.
