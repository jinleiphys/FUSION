# Threshold pion production in proton-proton collisions at NNLO in chiral EFT

> The reaction $NN \to NN π$ offers a good testing ground for chiral effective field theory at intermediate energies. It challenges our understanding of the first inelastic channel in nucleon-nucleon scattering and of the charge-symmetry breaking pattern in hadronic reactions. In our previous studies, we presented a complete calculation of the pion-production operator for s-wave pions up-to-and-including next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the formulation of chiral effective field theory, which includes pions, nucleons and $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom. In this paper we calculate the near threshold cross section for the $pp \to d π^{+}$ reaction by performing the convolution of the obtained operators with nuclear wave functions based on modern phenomenological and chiral potentials. The available chiral $NN$ wave functions are constructed with a cutoff comparable with the momentum transfer scale inherent in pion production reactions. Hence, a significant portion of the dynamical intermediate-range physics is thereby cut off by them. On the other hand, the NNLO amplitudes evaluated with phenomenological wave functions appear to be largely independent of the $NN$ model used and give corrections to the dominant leading order contributions as expected from dimensional analysis. The result gives support to the counting scheme used to classify the pion production operators, which is a precondition for a reliable investigation of the chirally suppressed neutral pion production. The explicit inclusion of the $Δ(1232)$ is found to be important but smaller than expected due to cancellations.

## Key claim

The NNLO chiral EFT calculation of the near-threshold $pp \to d \pi^{+}$ cross section, when evaluated with phenomenological nucleon-nucleon wave functions, yields corrections to the leading-order contributions that are consistent with dimensional analysis, supporting the momentum counting scheme used to classify pion production operators. However, the available chiral $NN$ wave functions are constructed with a cutoff comparable to the momentum transfer scale inherent in pion production, so a significant portion of the dynamical intermediate-range physics is cut off by them, making the phenomenological wave functions the more reliable test.

## Method

The authors performed a convolution of the previously derived irreducible pion production operator for s-wave pions up to NNLO in chiral EFT (including pions, nucleons, and $\Delta(1232)$ degrees of freedom) with nuclear wave functions from modern phenomenological potentials (CD Bonn, Nijmegen, AV18) and chiral potentials. The threshold amplitude $M_{3P1}$ for the $pp \to d \pi^{+}$ reaction was computed in momentum space with a sharp ultraviolet cutoff $\Lambda$ varied between 600 and 1000 MeV, and the resulting cross section parameter $\alpha$ was compared to the experimental value extracted from pionic deuterium lifetime measurements.

## Key numbers

* The experimental threshold amplitude extracted from pionic deuterium data is $|M_{3P1}|^\text{exp.} = 21.5^{+0.2}_{-0.5}$ (dimensionless), corresponding to $\sigma(nn \to d \pi^{-}) = (252^{+5}_{-11}) \eta \, \mu b$.
* The total LO amplitude (Weinberg-Tomozawa operator plus direct term) is close to but smaller than the experimental value for all phenomenological $NN$ potentials used.
* The NNLO amplitude contributes up to 10% to the total amplitude $|M_{3P1}|$, and the required NNLO contact term contribution is of the order of 5-15% of the LO contribution, consistent with the power counting estimate $\chi_{\rm MCS} \simeq \sqrt{m_\pi/m_N}$.
* The typical momentum transfer in pion production is about 360 MeV, and cutoff values below 600 MeV yield cutoff-dependent results.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous complete calculation of the s-wave pion production operator at NNLO (Filin 2012, Filin 2013) by performing the full convolution with $NN$ wave functions and comparing to precise experimental data from pionic deuterium lifetime measurements at PSI (Strauch 2010). It tests the convergence of the momentum counting scheme (MCS) proposed by Cohen (1995) and da Rocha (1999), which treats the soft scale $p \sim \sqrt{m_\pi m_N}$ explicitly. The result is a precondition for a reliable investigation of the chirally suppressed neutral pion production channel $pp \to pp \pi^0$, where the leading-order amplitude is numerically very small and higher-order corrections are essential.
