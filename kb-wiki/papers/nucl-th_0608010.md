# J/psi Production in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study J/psi production at RHIC and LHC energies with both initial production and regeneration. We solve the coupled set of transport equation for the J/psi distribution in phase space and the hydrodynamic equation for evolution of quark-gluon plasma. At RHIC, continuous regeneration is crucial for the J/psi momentum distribution while the elliptic flow is still dominated by initial production. At LHC energy, almost all the initially created J\psis are dissociated in the medium and regeneration dominates the J/psi properties.

## Key claim

At RHIC, both initial production and continuous regeneration are important for J/psi production, with regeneration crucial for the momentum distribution but initial production still dominating the elliptic flow; at LHC, regeneration dominates almost all J/psi properties because nearly all initially created J/psis are dissociated in the medium.

## Method

The authors solved a coupled set of equations: a classical Boltzmann-type transport equation for the J/psi phase-space distribution (including loss from gluon dissociation and gain from c-char recombination via detailed balance) together with hydrodynamic equations for the QGP evolution under boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and transverse flow. The transport equation was solved analytically, with the initial J/psi distribution set at tau_0 = 0.6 fm and charm quark distributions treated in two extreme scenarios (pQCD momentum distribution or fully thermalized). The gluon dissociation and its inverse recombination were the only microscopic processes considered, with the step function Theta(T - T_c) restricting them to the QGP phase.

## Key numbers

- Initial J/psi production cross section in p+p: sigma_pp^Psi = 2.61 +/- 0.20 microbarn at RHIC
- Charm production cross section at RHIC: sigma_pp^c-cbar = 622 +/- 57 microbarn
- Charm quark mass used: m_c = 1.87 GeV (including in-medium effects)
- Critical temperature: T_c = 165 MeV
- Initial time for QGP evolution at RHIC: tau_0 = 0.6 fm
- Nuclear absorption cross section: sigma_abs = 3 mb
- Initial p_t broadening parameters: a_gN = 0.076 GeV^2/fm, <p_t^2>_pp = 4.31 GeV^2
- At LHC: sigma_pp^Psi = 18.9 microbarn, sigma_pp^c-cbar = 5740 microbarn, tau_0 = 0.3 fm, initial temperature 680 MeV
- For continuously regenerated J/psis in the thermal scenario at impact parameter b = 7.8 fm, the elliptic flow v_2 is only one half of the suddenly regenerated J/psi v_2 in the coalescence model of Greco (2004)
- The average squared transverse momentum of regenerated J/psis is about 2 GeV^2 in the thermal scenario and about 2.5 GeV^2 in the pQCD scenario

## Context

This work extends the kinetic model approach of Grandchamp (2004) and Thews (2001) by treating J/psi regeneration continuously throughout the QGP volume rather than only at hadronization, and by coupling the transport equation to hydrodynamic evolution of the medium. It contrasts with the sudden regeneration at hadronization assumed in the coalescence model of Greco (2004), finding that continuous regeneration yields a much smaller J/psi elliptic flow at RHIC. The results enable predictions for J/psi observables (R_AA, <p_t^2>, v_2) at LHC energies where regeneration is expected to dominate, and highlight the need for improved treatment of charm quark thermalization in the medium.
