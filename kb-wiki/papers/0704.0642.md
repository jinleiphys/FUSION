# Direct photons and dileptons via color dipoles

> Drell-Yan dilepton pair production and inclusive direct photon production can be described within a unified framework in the color dipole approach. The inclusion of non-perturbative primordial transverse momenta and DGLAP evolution is studied. We successfully describe data for dilepton spectra from 800-GeV pp collisions, inclusive direct photon spectra for pp collisions at RHIC energies $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, and for $p\bar{p}$ collisions at Tevatron energies $\sqrt{s}=1.8$ TeV, in a formalism that is free from any extra parameters.

## Key claim
The color dipole formalism can describe both Drell-Yan dilepton production and inclusive direct photon production in a unified, parameter-free framework, provided the target-side kinematics are in the small-\(x_2\) regime (\(x_2 < 0.1\)) where Pomeron exchange dominates and Reggeon contributions can be ignored.

## Method
The authors compute the transverse-momentum distribution of photon bremsstrahlung from a quark-nucleon interaction using light-cone wavefunctions for the \(\gamma q\) fluctuation, expressed in terms of the dipole cross section \(\sigma_{q\bar{q}}\). The hadronic cross section is obtained by convoluting the elementary partonic cross section with the proton structure function \(F_2^p\) (which encodes the projectile parton distributions). Two parametrizations of the dipole cross section are employed: the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff (GBW) saturation model and a modified GBW model coupled to leading-order DGLAP evolution of the gluon density (GBW-DGLAP). A Gaussian primordial transverse momentum distribution with \(\langle k_T^2\rangle_N = 0.4\ \text{GeV}^2\) is optionally included to improve the low-\(p_T\) dilepton data.

## Key numbers
- Dilepton spectra in 800-GeV \(pp\) collisions at \(\langle x_F\rangle = 0.63\) and \(\langle M_{\mu^+\mu^-}\rangle = 4.80\ \text{GeV}\) and \(5.70\ \text{GeV}\) are described without free parameters; the GBW-DGLAP model improves the high-\(p_T\) tail over the plain GBW model.
- For direct photons at RHIC \(\sqrt{s}=200\ \text{GeV}\) (midrapidity), the dipole models reproduce the PHENIX data; the GBW-DGLAP model gives a better description at higher \(p_T\).
- For direct photons at Tevatron \(\sqrt{s}=1.8\ \text{TeV}\) (\(|\eta|<0.9\)), the GBW model describes data for \(p_T < 30\ \text{GeV}\); DGLAP evolution significantly improves the result at higher \(p_T\).
- The primordial transverse momentum needed for the low-\(p_T\) dilepton data is \(\langle k_T^2\rangle_N = 0.4\ \text{GeV}^2\), smaller than the \(\sim 1\ \text{GeV}\) typically used in parton-model fits.
- The effective quark mass is fixed to \(m_q = 0.2\ \text{GeV}\) for both dilepton and direct photon calculations.

## Context
This work extends the color dipole formalism, previously applied to total and diffractive cross sections (Kopeliovich et al., 1981) and to radiation (Hirano et al., 1996), to a unified description of Drell-Yan and direct photon production. It improves upon earlier dipole-model attempts for Drell-Yan (Kopeliovich et al., 2002) by using data in the kinematic region where the model is valid (\(x_2 < 0.1\)). The approach contrasts with perturbative QCD calculations, which require a \(K\)-factor and a large intrinsic transverse momentum (Wang, 2001) and still struggle to describe all inclusive direct-photon data (Aurenche et al., 2006). The results enable a parameter-free baseline for interpreting direct-photon measurements in heavy-ion collisions and for testing saturation and DGLAP evolution in hadronic collisions.
