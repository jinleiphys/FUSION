# Onset of $η$-meson binding in the He isotopes

> The onset of binding $η$(548) mesons in nuclei is studied in the He isotopes by doing precise $ηNNN$ and $ηNNNN$ few-body stochastic variational method calculations for two semi-realistic $NN$ potentials and two energy dependent $ηN$ potentials derived from coupled-channel models of the $N^{\ast}(1535)$ nucleon resonance. The energy dependence of the $ηN$ subthreshold input is treated self consistently. It is found that a minimal value of the real part of the $ηN$ scattering length $a_{ηN}$ close to 1 fm is required to bind $η$ mesons in $^3$He, yielding then a few MeV $η$ binding in $^4$He. The onset of $η$-meson binding in $^4$He requires that Re$\,a_{ηN}$ exceeds 0.7 fm approximately. These results compare well with results of recent $ηNNN$ and $ηNNNN$ pionless effective field theory calculations. Related optical-model calculations are also discussed.

## Key claim

The onset of η-meson binding in ³He requires a minimal value of the real part of the ηN scattering length Re aηN close to 1 fm, while the onset in ⁴He requires Re aηN to exceed approximately 0.7 fm; these thresholds are conditional on the choice of NN potential, the ηN interaction model, and the momentum scale parameter Λ used in the Gaussian form of the ηN potential.

## Method

The authors performed stochastic variational method (SVM) calculations for the ηNNN (η-³He) and ηNNNN (η-⁴He) few-body systems using two semi-realistic NN potentials (Minnesota MNC and Argonne AV4') and two energy-dependent ηN potentials derived from the GW and CS coupled-channel models of the N*(1535) resonance. The subthreshold energy dependence of the ηN interaction was treated self-consistently by requiring that the output expectation value ⟨δ√s⟩ from the few-body Schrödinger equation matches the input energy argument of the ηN potential.

## Key numbers

- For η-³He with (MNC, GW) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: Bη = 0.990 MeV, Γη = 3.252 MeV
- For η-³He with (AV4p, GW) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: Bη = 0.686 MeV, Γη = 2.438 MeV
- For η-³He with (MNC, CS) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: Bη = −0.161 MeV (unbound)
- For η-⁴He with (MNC, GW) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: Bη = 4.69 MeV, Γη = 4.500 MeV
- For η-⁴He with (AV4p, GW) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: Bη = 3.51 MeV, Γη = 3.615 MeV
- For η-⁴He with (MNC, CS) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: Bη = 0.47 MeV, Γη = 0.901 MeV
- The GW ηN scattering length: aηN = (0.96 + i0.26) fm
- The CS ηN scattering length: aηN = (0.67 + i0.20) fm
- Self-consistent subthreshold energy for η-³He with (AV4p, GW) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: δ√s_sc = −14.881 MeV
- Self-consistent subthreshold energy for η-⁴He with (AV4p, GW) and Λ=4 fm⁻¹: δ√s_sc = −32.411 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hyperspherical-basis ηNN and ηNNN calculations (Barnea, Friedman, Gal, 2015) and their recent pionless EFT SVM calculations (Barnea et al., 2017) by replacing contact interactions with semi-realistic NN potentials. The results confirm that the GW ηN model (Green and Wycech, 2005) can bind both η-³He and η-⁴He, while the weaker CS model (Cieply and Smejkal, 2013) binds only η-⁴He marginally. The findings are compared with optical-model calculations and with the recent experimental upper limits from the WASA-at-COSY facility (Adlarson et al., 2016), which suggest that Re aηN may be less than about 0.7 fm if no η-⁴He bound state is confirmed.
