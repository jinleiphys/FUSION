# Phenomenology of nonperturbative charm in the nucleon

> We perform a comprehensive analysis of the role of nonperturbative (or intrinsic) charm in the nucleon, generated through Fock state expansions of the nucleon wave function involving five-quark virtual states represented by charmed mesons and baryons. We consider contributions from a variety of charmed meson-baryon states and find surprisingly dominant effects from the (Dbar^{*0} Lambda_c+) configuration. Particular attention is paid to the existence and persistence of high-x structure for intrinsic charm, and the x dependence of the c-cbar asymmetry predicted in meson-baryon models. We discuss how studies of charmed baryons and mesons in hadronic reactions can be used to constrain models, and outline future measurements that could further illuminate the intrinsic charm component of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nonperturbative (intrinsic) charm in the nucleon can be described through meson-baryon Fock state fluctuations, with the surprising finding that the \(\bar{D}^{*0} \Lambda_c^+\) configuration produces dominant effects; however, the magnitude and shape of the intrinsic charm distribution remain model-dependent and constrained by limited data, with no definitive confirmation of a specific intrinsic charm probability.

## Method

The authors compute intrinsic charm and anticharm distributions in the nucleon using meson-baryon models, where the proton fluctuates into virtual charmed meson (pseudoscalar \(D\) and vector \(D^*\)) plus charmed baryon (\(\Lambda_c^+\), \(\Sigma_c\), \(\Sigma_c^*\)) states. Splitting functions for these fluctuations are derived from effective hadronic Lagrangians in the infinite momentum frame, with coupling constants taken from \(DN\) and \(\bar{D}N\) scattering analyses using SU(4) symmetry arguments. The resulting \(c\) and \(\bar{c}\) distributions are obtained via convolution integrals over the splitting functions and the parton distributions inside the charmed hadrons, and are compared with data on the charm structure function \(F_2^c(x)\) from EMC and HERA, as well as inclusive \(\Lambda_c\) production data from the R608 and SELEX Collaborations.

## Key numbers

* The \(\bar{D}^{*0} \Lambda_c^+\) configuration is found to be the dominant contributor among all charmed meson-baryon states considered.
* The EMC charm production data at large energy transfers \(\nu \sim 170\) GeV required an intrinsic charm component normalized to \(0.86 \pm 0.60\%\) in the analysis of Harris, Smith and Vogt.
* The analysis by Steffens et al. indicated a slight preference for intrinsic charm in a meson-baryon model at a level of about \(0.4\%\), though the evidence was not conclusive.
* Global QCD analyses by Pumplin, Lai and Tung found that high-energy data could accommodate up to about \(2\%\) intrinsic charm without serious disagreement.
* The coupling constants for the \(p \to \bar{D}^0 \Lambda_c^+\) vertex are \(g_{MBN}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 3.943\), and for the \(p \to \bar{D}^{*0} \Lambda_c^+\) vertex are \(g_{MBN}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 1.590\) and \(f_{MBN}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 5.183\).

## Context

This work extends previous intrinsic charm studies, including the original BHPS model (Brodsky, Hoyer, Peterson, Sakai, 1980) and the scalar five-quark model of Pumplin (2005), by using a more dynamical meson-baryon approach constrained by coupled-channel analyses of baryon-baryon scattering from Haidenbauer et al. (2007, 2008, 2011). It contrasts with global PDF analyses that omit the EMC data and fit only HERA data using perturbative charm alone, as done by MSTW (2008) and JR (2009). The results enable future experimental tests at facilities such as J-PARC, GSI-FAIR, Jefferson Lab after its 12 GeV upgrade, and a future Electron-Ion Collider, where measurements of charmed baryon production and the charm structure function at large \(x\) could distinguish between models.
