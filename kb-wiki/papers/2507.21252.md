# QCD-Gravity double copy in Regge asymptotics: from $2\rightarrow n$ amplitudes to radiation in shockwave collisions

> These lectures discuss multi-particle production in QCD and in gravity at ultrarelativistic energies, their double copy relations, and strong parallels in emergent shockwave dynamics. Dispersive techniques are applied to derive the BFKL equation for multi-gluon production in Regge asymptotics. Identical methods apply in gravity and are captured by a gravitational Lipatov equation. The building blocks in both cases are Lipatov vertices and reggeized propagators satisfying double copy relations; in gravity, Weinberg's soft theorem is recovered as a limit of the Lipatov framework. BFKL evolution in QCD generates wee parton states of maximal occupancy characterized by an emergent semi-hard saturation scale. Renormalization group equations in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) EFT describe wee parton correlations and their rapidity evolution. A shockwave picture of deeply inelastic scattering and hadron-hadron collisions follows, with multi-particle production described by Cutkosky's rules in strong time-dependent fields. Gluon radiation in the CGC EFT has a double copy in gravitational shockwave collisions, with a similar correspondence applicable between gluon and graviton shockwave propagators. Possible extensions of this semi-classical double copy are outlined for computing multi-particle production in gravitational shockwave collisions, self-force and tidal contributions, and classical and quantum noise in the focusing of geodesics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the double copy between QCD and gravity, previously established for tree-level amplitudes, extends to the Regge asymptotics of multi-particle production, where the BFKL framework in QCD has a direct gravitational counterpart governed by a gravitational Lipatov equation; however, this gravitational double copy has a much smaller window of applicability than in QCD, with some elements of the Lipatov construction becoming less relevant in the emergent classical framework for black hole formation.

## Method

The authors apply dispersive techniques and Cutkosky's rules to derive the BFKL equation for multi-gluon production in QCD's Regge limit, computing the imaginary parts of scattering amplitudes from s-channel discontinuities and reconstructing the full amplitudes via logarithmic dispersion relations. Identical dispersive methods are applied to gravity in the ultrarelativistic regime, yielding a gravitational Lipatov equation whose building blocks (Lipatov vertices and reggeized propagators) satisfy double copy relations with their QCD counterparts. The resulting dynamics is recast as an effective field theory of shockwave scattering, where gluon radiation in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) EFT has a double copy in gravitational shockwave collisions, with a dilute-dense power counting scheme enabling analytic computation of rescattering contributions including self-force and tidal effects.

## Context

This work extends Lipatov's original 40-year-old observation of a double copy between QCD and gravity effective vertices in the Regge regime, predating the KLT relations (Kawai, Lewellen, Tye, 1985) and the BCJ double copy (Bern, Carrasco, Johansson). The paper connects this framework to the Color Glass Condensate EFT for QCD and to gravitational shockwave collisions relevant for black hole formation, building on prior work by Amati, Ciafaloni and Veneziano (ACV) on trans-Planckian scattering. The approach enables future computation of multi-particle production in gravitational shockwave collisions, self-force and tidal contributions, and classical and quantum noise in geodesic focusing, with potential connections to the Raychaudhuri equation and quantum imprints on gravitational radiation.
