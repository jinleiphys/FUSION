# Correlations and Fluctuations in the Initial State of high energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> Initial states of high energy heavy ion collisions are studied using a dipole model through the DIPSY event generator that dynamically includes saturation together with the fluctuations and correlations of the BFKL cascade. The eccentricities of first up to fourth moment are calculated at RHIC and LHC. Predictions are made for correlations and fluctuations in rapidity of the eccentricities, and conventional theoretical approximations are tested. A large set of initial state Au-Au, Cu-Au and Pb-Pb collisions have been generated and are published online.

## Key claim

The DIPSY event generator, based on a dipole model that includes saturation and BFKL cascade fluctuations, predicts eccentricities for heavy ion collisions that are generally slightly larger than other models, and reveals that the approximation of independent binary collisions breaks down in dense regions due to gluon chain merging.

## Method

The authors used the DIPSY event generator, which implements a dipole model based on leading-logarithm BFKL evolution in impact parameter space, including running coupling, energy conservation, confinement via a gluon mass, and saturation through unitarisation and the dipole swing. Heavy ions were described by a Wood-Saxon distribution of nucleons with a hard core, each nucleon starting as a triangle of dipoles that evolves in rapidity; the backbone gluons at the interaction rapidity define the t=0 state. Eccentricities epsilon_1 through epsilon_4 were calculated from the gluon positions in a central rapidity slice using standard moment definitions, and correlations between forward and backward rapidity regions were studied.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the correlation coefficients rho_n between eccentricities in forward (eta in 1 to 3) and backward (eta in -3 to -1) regions at N_part > 100 are: rho_1 = 0.49, rho_2 = 0.81, rho_3 = 0.57, rho_4 = 0.58.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the corresponding correlation coefficients are: rho_1 = 0.62, rho_2 = 0.88, rho_3 = 0.71, rho_4 = 0.69.
- In central (b=0) Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the number of correlated gluons n_corr in the peripheral region is approximately 0.4, similar to pp collisions, while in the center the correlation is weaker by a factor of 2.
- The transverse range of the correlation is shortened by a factor of 2 in the center of the collision compared to the peripheral region.
- Converting from <epsilon_n> to sqrt(<epsilon_n^2>) adds approximately 15%, 7%, 13%, and 10% to epsilon_1, epsilon_2, epsilon_3, and epsilon_4 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the dipole model approach previously developed for pp and gamma*p collisions (Avsar 2005, 2006; Flensburg 2008, 2011) to heavy ion systems, providing a framework that includes saturation effects missing in binary collision models such as NeXus, UrQMD, and HIJING. The results enable tests of the initial-state geometry picture of anisotropic flow by providing predictions for rapidity correlations of eccentricities that can be compared to experimental flow data, and the published event sets allow other researchers to use DIPSY initial conditions for final-state evolution studies.
