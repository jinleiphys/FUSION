# Study of the partial wave structure of $π^0η$ photoproduction on protons

> Analysis of the partial wave structure of gamma p --> pi0 eta p reaction is presented in the energy region from threshold up to the total center of mass energy W = 1.9 GeV. Angular distributions measured with the Crystal Ball/TAPS hermetic detector system at the Mainz Microtron MAMI are expanded in terms of spherical harmonics. The relation of the extracted moments to the partial wave structure of the reaction amplitude is discussed and compared with predictions from model calculations.

## Key claim

The partial wave structure of the reaction is dominated by the Δ(1700)3/2− resonance, with the helicity amplitudes A1/2 and A3/2 being nearly equal, but the analysis is limited to unpolarized cross sections and therefore cannot uniquely determine the contributing partial waves.

## Method

The authors analyzed angular distributions of the γp → π0ηp reaction measured with the Crystal Ball/TAPS detector at MAMI C, covering photon energies from threshold to 1450 MeV. They expanded the normalized differential cross section in terms of spherical harmonics YLM(Θ,Φ) in a coordinate system where the quantization axis is taken along the normal to the plane of the final three particles. The extracted moments WLM were compared with predictions from the isobar model of Fix, Kaptari, and Lee (2011).

## Key numbers

- The photon beam energy range covered from the reaction threshold to 1450 MeV with an energy bin of about 4 MeV.
- The spectrometer covers 97% of the full solid angle.
- The systematic uncertainty was estimated to be 5%, including uncertainties in photon flux, target density, and detection efficiency.
- The d-wave contribution to the decay mode Δ(1700)3/2− → ηΔ is about 0.1% at the resonance position W = 1700 MeV.
- The ratio of helicity couplings A1/2 and A3/2 for γp → Δ(1700)3/2− varies between 0.95 and 1.3 according to the model of Fix, Kaptari, and Lee (2011).

## Context

This work extends the model-independent partial wave analysis method previously applied to γp → π0π0p by Kashevarov et al. (2012) and Fix and Ahrens (2011) to the π0η final state. The results confirm the dominance of the Δ(1700)3/2− resonance and the importance of positive parity states such as Δ(1750)1/2+ and Δ(1920)3/2+, as predicted by the isobar models of Horn et al. (2008), Doring et al. (2011), and Fix, Kaptari, and Lee (2011). The analysis enables future studies with polarized beams and targets to uniquely determine the partial wave amplitudes.
