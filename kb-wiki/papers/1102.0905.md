# What densities can be effectively probed in quasifree single-nucleon knockout reactions?

> We address the issue whether quasifree single-nucleon knockout measurements carry sufficient information about the nuclear interior. To this end, we present comparisons of the reaction probability densities for $A(e,e'p)$ and $A(p,2p)$ in quasifree kinematics for the target nuclei $^{4}$He, $^{12}$C, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{208}$Pb. We adopt a comprehensive framework based on the impulse approximation and on a relativized extension of Glauber multiple-scattering reaction theory in which the medium effects related to short-range correlations (SRC) are implemented. It is demonstrated that SRC weaken the effect of attenuation. For light target nuclei, both the quasifree $(p,2p)$ and $(e,e'p)$ can probe average densities of the same order as nuclear saturation density $ρ_{0}$. For heavy nuclei like $^{208}$Pb, the probed average densities are smaller than $0.1ρ_{0}$ and the $(e,e'p)$ reaction is far more efficient in probing the bulk regions than $(p,2p)$.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) weaken the effect of nuclear attenuation in quasifree single-nucleon knockout reactions, but the average densities probed depend strongly on target mass: for light nuclei like ⁴He both (e,e'p) and (p,2p) can probe densities near nuclear saturation density ρ₀, while for heavy nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb the probed average densities are smaller than 0.1ρ₀, with (e,e'p) being far more efficient at probing the bulk than (p,2p).

## Method

The authors computed reaction probability densities for A(e,e'p) and A(p,2p) in quasifree kinematics using a factorized cross-section framework based on the impulse approximation and a relativized extension of Glauber multiple-scattering reaction theory (RMSGA). Short-range correlations were incorporated by replacing the mean-field one-body density with an effective density derived from a correlated two-body density using a Jastrow correlation function with a hard core of 0.8 fm and a bump peaking at about 1.3 fm. Calculations were performed for ⁴He, ¹²C, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb at ejected nucleon kinetic energies of several hundred MeV to 1.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He at ejected proton kinetic energies of 250–1800 MeV, the radial reaction probability densities for both (e,e'p) and (p,2p) peak at radii comparable to the nuclear density distribution.
- For ¹²C, the average density probed in knockout from the deep-lying 1s₁/₂ shell is of the same order as nuclear saturation density ρ₀ for both reactions.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the average density probed in knockout from the 1s₁/₂ shell is smaller than 0.1ρ₀ for both reactions.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the average radius probed in (e,e'p) knockout from the 1s₁/₂ shell is approximately 1.2 A^(1/3) fm, while for (p,2p) it is significantly larger (more peripheral).
- The measured proton root-mean-square radius in ⁵⁶Fe is about 3.75 fm (Peterson 1970).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear attenuation in quasifree knockout reactions, particularly the systematic observation by Lapikas (1993) that extracted spectroscopic factors from A(e,e'p) are substantially smaller than mean-field predictions. The paper provides a quantitative comparison of the density sensitivity of (e,e'p) and (p,2p) reactions, which is directly relevant to interpreting recent (p,2p) measurements on carbon isotopes by Kobayashi (2008) and to planning future experiments at FAIR (Aumann 2007). The results enable more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors from (p,2p) data on unstable nuclei, where the reaction is often used in inverse kinematics.
