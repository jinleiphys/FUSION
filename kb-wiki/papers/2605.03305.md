# Non-radial pulsations of gravitationally coupled two-fluid neutron stars in general relativity

> Non-radial oscillations of neutron stars provide a powerful probe of stellar structure and relativistic gravity, but a fully general relativistic treatment for gravitationally coupled two-fluid stars with independently conserved currents has so far been lacking. In this work, we develop a fully relativistic framework for polar perturbations of gravitationally coupled two-fluid neutron stars, assuming that the two fluids interact only through the common spacetime and are not coupled by entrainment or direct microphysical interactions. We derive the coupled linear perturbation equations governing the metric and both fluid components, and complete the formulation by establishing the regularity, surface, and exterior matching conditions required for a well-posed oscillation eigenvalue problem. We then implement the resulting system numerically and compute representative polar mode spectra for gravitationally coupled two-fluid stellar models. This implementation provides a practical way to address mode identification in gravitationally coupled two-fluid stars, allowing the fundamental ($\mathsf{f}$) and pressure ($\mathsf{p}$) mode branches of the spectrum to be classified according to their dominant inner- or outer-fluid character through the associated eigenfunctions and their node structure. The formalism developed here provides a foundation for extending relativistic asteroseismology to multi-fluid compact stars and for exploring their potential gravitational-wave signatures in a fully general relativistic setting.

## Key claim

The paper develops a fully general relativistic framework for polar (even-parity) non-radial perturbations of neutron stars containing two gravitationally coupled fluids that interact only through the common spacetime geometry, with no entrainment or direct microphysical coupling, and demonstrates that the additional fluid degree of freedom produces distinct mode branches and modifies the equation of state-insensitive universal relations known from single-fluid stars.

## Method

The authors start from a static, spherically symmetric background described by the multi-fluid generalization of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, where each fluid has its own conserved current, four-velocity, and barotropic equation of state. They then derive the complete set of coupled linear perturbation equations for the metric and both fluid components in the Regge-Wheeler gauge, obtaining a system of 2N+2 first-order differential equations (for N=2 fluids) supplemented by two algebraic relations, and establish the regularity conditions at the center, surface boundary conditions at each fluid's radius, and matching conditions to the exterior Schwarzschild spacetime. The resulting eigenvalue problem is implemented numerically to compute polar mode spectra for representative two-fluid stellar models.

## Key numbers

- The outer boundary of the matter distribution is located at R_out = max_X {R_X}, where each fluid component X has its own radius R_X defined by p_X(R_X)=0.
- The total gravitational mass of the configuration is M = m(R_out), where the mass function m(r) satisfies dm/dr = 4π r^2 Σ_X ε_X.
- The analysis is restricted to even-parity modes with l ≥ 2.
- The perturbation equations are derived for a harmonic time dependence e^{iωt} and spherical harmonic decomposition with indices (l,m).

## Context

This work extends the single-fluid relativistic perturbation framework (Thorne 1967, Lindblom 1983, Detweiler 1985) and the Cowling-approximation two-fluid treatment (Sotani 2025) to a fully general relativistic formulation for gravitationally coupled two-fluid stars. It provides the foundation for extending relativistic asteroseismology to multi-fluid compact stars such as dark matter admixed neutron stars (Goldman 2013, Kumar 2025, Caballero 2024) and enables consistent computation of gravitational-wave signatures from such systems, going beyond earlier studies that treated the perturbation sector with only a single displacement field or neglected metric perturbations entirely.
