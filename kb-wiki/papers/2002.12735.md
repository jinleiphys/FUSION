# System response to the initial energy-momentum tensor in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The evolution of a relativistic heavy-ion collision is typically understood as a process that transmutes the initial geometry of the system into the final momentum distribution of observed hadrons, which can be described via a cumulant expansion of the initial distribution of energy density and is represented at leading order as the well-known eccentricity scaling of anisotropic flow. We summarize a proposed extension of this framework to include the contribution from initial momentum-space properties, as encoded in other components of the energy-momentum tensor. Numerical tests validate this proposal.

## Key claim

The paper proposes and validates an extension of the standard cumulant expansion framework for relativistic heavy-ion collisions that includes contributions from initial momentum density and stress (other components of the energy-momentum tensor) in addition to the usual energy density, with the claim that only two additional response coefficients (alpha and beta) are needed to capture these effects for all harmonic orders.

## Method

The authors define a modified energy density rho(x) = T^{tau tau}(x) - alpha partial_i T^{tau i}(x) + beta partial_i partial_j T^{ij}(x), then perform the standard cumulant expansion on this quantity to construct estimators for anisotropic flow coefficients V_n. They test these estimators using state-of-the-art hydrodynamic simulations with IP-Glasma initial conditions, viscous hydrodynamics with Bayesian-inferred transport coefficients, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner, computing the Pearson correlation coefficient between the true V_n and the estimator for each event.

## Key numbers

- The Pearson correlation coefficient Q_2 for elliptic flow improves from approximately 0.97 (using only energy density, alpha=beta=0) to approximately 0.99 when including momentum and stress contributions with optimized alpha and beta values
- The Pearson correlation coefficient Q_3 for triangular flow improves from approximately 0.85 (using only energy density) to approximately 0.95 when including momentum and stress contributions
- The same values of alpha and beta that maximize Q_2 also give near-maximal Q_3, and vice versa, validating the framework's prediction that these response coefficients are independent of harmonic order

## Context

This work extends the standard eccentricity scaling framework (Gardim 2011, Teaney 2010) that relates initial geometry to final anisotropic flow via cumulant expansions of the energy density alone. The authors use IP-Glasma initial conditions (Schenke 2012) and hydrodynamic parameters from a Bayesian analysis (Bernhard 2018). This framework enables more precise separation of early-time dynamics from medium properties in heavy-ion collisions, and may be particularly important for small collision systems where momentum-space degrees of freedom are expected to play a larger role.
