# Investigation of the rotation effects on high--density matter in hybrid stars

> The equation of state (EOS) of high-density matter is still not clear and several recent observations indicate restrictions to EOSs. Theoretical studies should thus elucidate EOSs at high density and/or high temperature. Many theoretical studies have attempted to account for the effect of rotation of rapidly rotating neutron stars (pulsars), which are commonly observed astronomical objects having high-density interiors. Furthermore, neutron stars generate a strong magnetic field. Several recent studies indicate that this magnetic field exerts some restrictions on the EOS. Theoretical studies should thus incorporate these effects. In this paper, we focus on the effect of rotation. We find that one of our EOSs is consistent with these observations, and another is inconsistent. We also find an important relation between radius and rotation.

## Key claim

The paper finds that one of its equations of state (EOS) for hybrid stars, which includes charge screening, is consistent with observational mass-frequency data for rotating neutron stars, while another EOS without screening is inconsistent and therefore inappropriate. The abstract's claim of "an important relation between radius and rotation" is qualified by the specific condition that the equatorial and polar radii become measurably different only when the rotation rate is 400 Hz or higher.

## Method

The author constructs an EOS for the hadron-quark mixed phase using the MIT bag model for quark matter (with u, d, and s quarks, s quark mass 150 MeV, and one-gluon-exchange interaction) and an effective potential for hadron matter (protons, neutrons, electrons). The phase transition is treated as first-order, with a surface contribution parameterized by a surface tension parameter. The EOS is then applied to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for non-rotating stars and subsequently to a stationary, rigidly rotating star in general relativity under assumptions of axial symmetry and perfect fluid matter, following the formalism reviewed by Stergioulas (2003) and Kurkela et al. (2010).

## Key numbers

- The s quark mass is set to 150 MeV in the MIT bag model.
- The equatorial radius (R_eq) and polar radius (R_p) of the rotating star become different when the rotation rate is 400 Hz or higher.
- The Kepler frequency (mass-shedding limit) is shown as a blue curve in the mass-frequency relation, defining the boundary beyond which the centrifugal force exceeds gravity and the configuration is physically invalid.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of hybrid star structure (Endo 2012, 2013) by adding the effect of rotation, following theoretical approaches to rotating neutron stars by Stergioulas (2003), Kurkela et al. (2010), Weber (1999), and Belvedere et al. (2012). The results show that the charge-screened EOS is consistent with observational constraints from rapidly rotating pulsars, while the unscreened EOS is ruled out. The paper enables future studies that could incorporate both rotation and magnetic field effects, as investigated by Dexheimer et al. (2012), Chirenti et al. (2013), Aguirre et al. (2013), and Mallick et al. (2013), and suggests that more realistic models should include color superconductivity and relativistic mean field theory.
