# Elliptic flow from color-dipole orientation in pp and pA collisions

> For ultrarelativistic proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, we perform an exploratory study of the contribution to the elliptic flow $v_2$ coming from the orientation of the momentum of the produced particles with respect to the reaction plane. Via the CGC factorization valid at high energies, this contribution is related to the orientation of a color dipole with respect to its impact parameter, which in turn probes the transverse inhomogeneity in the target. Using the McLerran-Venugopalan model (with impact-parameter dependence) as an effective description for the soft gluon distribution in the (proton or nuclear) target, we present a semi-analytic calculation of the dipole scattering amplitude, including its angular dependence. We find that the angular dependence is controlled by soft gluon exchanges and hence is genuinely non-perturbative. The effects of multiple scattering turn out to be essential (in particular, they change the sign of $v_2$). We find that sizable values for $v_2$, comparable to those observed in the LHC data and having a similar dependence upon the transverse momenta of the produced particles, can be easily generated via peripheral collisions. In particular, $v_2$ develops a peak at a transverse momentum which scales with the saturation momentum in the target.

## Key claim

The authors show that elliptic flow $v_2$ in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions can arise from the orientation of a color dipole with respect to its impact parameter, without requiring final-state hydrodynamic interactions, but they emphasize that this mechanism is genuinely non-perturbative because the angular dependence is controlled by soft gluon exchanges and the effects of multiple scattering are essential (including changing the sign of $v_2$).

## Method

Using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model extended to include impact-parameter dependence, the authors perform a semi-analytic calculation of the dipole scattering amplitude $S(\mathbf{b},\mathbf{r},x_g)$ including its angular dependence, for both a smooth proton target and a lumpy nuclear target. They then compute the elliptic flow coefficient $v_2(p,b)$ via Fourier analysis of the single-inclusive quark production cross-section in dilute-dense collisions, using the CGC factorization formula that relates the cross-section to the Fourier transform of the dipole $S$-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The proton size parameter $R \simeq 0.3$ fm, taken from fits to HERA data using the IP-Sat model.
- The elliptic flow $v_2$ can be quite large, $v_2 \gtrsim 0.1$, in peripheral collisions.
- The eccentricity $\varepsilon_2(B)$ approaches 1 when the impact parameter $B \to \infty$ and vanishes for $B = 0$.
- The dipole scattering amplitude $S(b,r,\theta)$ satisfies the sum-rule $\int \frac{d^2\mathbf{p}}{(2\pi)^2} \tilde{S}(\mathbf{b},\mathbf{p}) = 1$, expressing probability conservation.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposals of Kopeliovich (2007) and Levin (2011) for generating azimuthal asymmetries from impact-parameter inhomogeneity, but uses the MV model with impact-parameter dependence (inspired by saturation models like IP-Sat and bCGC that were calibrated to HERA data) rather than the phenomenological parametrizations used previously. The mechanism is distinct from the "color field domain" model of Kovner (2011) and Dumitru (2014) because it does not require non-planar gluon exchanges and survives in the large-$N_c$ limit, and it differs from hydrodynamic flow explanations by attributing the asymmetry to initial-state scattering rather than final-state collective effects. The results enable future studies of how fluctuations in the gluon distribution (from high-energy evolution or nucleon distribution in a lumpy nucleus) can generate azimuthal asymmetries in more central collisions, measurable via multi-particle correlations.
