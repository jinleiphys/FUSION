# Nuclear parton density modifications from low-mass lepton pair production at the LHC

> In this article, we investigate the potential of low-mass lepton pair production in proton-ion collisions at the LHC to constrain nuclear modifications of parton densities. Similarly to prompt photon production, the transverse momentum spectrum is shown to be dominated by the QCD Compton process, but has virtually no fragmentation or isolation uncertainties. Depending on the orientation of the proton and ion beams and on the use of central or forward detector components, all interesting regions of nuclear effects (shadowing, antishadowing, isospin and EMC effects) can be probed. Ratios of cross sections allow to eliminate theoretical scale and bare-proton parton density errors as well as many experimental systematic uncertainties.

## Key claim

Low-mass lepton pair production in proton-ion collisions at the LHC can constrain nuclear modifications of parton densities via the QCD Compton process, which dominates the transverse momentum spectrum similarly to prompt photon production but without fragmentation or isolation uncertainties; the sensitivity depends on beam orientation and detector acceptance to probe shadowing, antishadowing, isospin, and EMC effects.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD calculations of lepton pair production in proton-lead collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, using the EPS09 nuclear PDFs as baseline with comparisons to HKN07, nCTEQ, and DSSZ parametrizations. They computed fractional contributions of partonic subprocesses (quark-gluon vs. quark-antiquark) and ratios of differential cross sections for central (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS) and forward (ALICE muon spectrometer) detector acceptances, varying beam orientation to access different kinematic regions.

## Key numbers

- QCD Compton process contributes more than 80% for low-mass pairs (2m_mu < Q < 2.5 GeV) and more than 95% for intermediate mass (5 GeV < Q < 6 GeV) at central rapidity with pT < 100 GeV
- EPS09 uncertainty band for central ALICE detector is ±6% at small pT and 2.5% at larger pT for low-mass electron pairs
- nCTEQ prediction differs from central EPS09 by up to 16% at low pT for central low-mass production
- For forward muon pairs in proton-lead collisions, EPS09 uncertainty reaches ±9% at low pT, with nCTEQ predicting 32% shadowing versus 14% for EPS09, 12% for HKN07, and 6% for DSSZ
- For lead-proton collisions with forward muons, EPS09 uncertainty band is ±2% over almost the entire pT range, with other parametrizations differing by up to 8%
- Transition from antishadowing to EMC effect occurs at 300 GeV for ATLAS/CMS kinematics according to EPS09 and DSSZ predictions

## Context

This work extends the idea of using virtual photons (lepton pairs) to constrain nuclear PDFs, previously applied to unpolarized and polarized pp collisions by Berger (1998, 1999) and to weak boson production by Brandt (2013). It contrasts with prompt photon studies by Arleo (2007, 2011) that suffer from fragmentation and isolation uncertainties, and with Drell-Yan measurements that are dominated by quark-antiquark annihilation at low pT. The results enable new global fits of nuclear PDFs using LHC data, addressing the large disagreements between existing parametrizations from EPS09, HKN07, nCTEQ, and DSSZ collaborations, particularly for gluon densities at small x and for all partons at large x.
