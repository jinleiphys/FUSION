# Estimate of the $S$-wave $D^*K$ scattering length in the isospin-0 channel from Belle and LHCb data

> It is shown that the Belle and LHCb data on the interference of the amplitudes of the $S$ and $D$ partial waves in the decays $D_{s1}(2536 )^+\to D^{*+}K^0_S$ and $D_{s1}(2536)^-\to\bar D^{*0}K^-$ allow us to obtain an estimate of the $S$-wave $D^*K$ scattering length in the channel with isospin $I=0$: $|a^{(0)}_{D^*K}|=(0.94\pm0.06)$ fm. The possibility of explaining the found value by the contribution of the $D_{s1}(2460)$ resonance is discussed. The decay of $B_{s1}(5830)\to B^*\bar K$ is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The Belle and LHCb data on the interference of S- and D-partial waves in the decays \(D_{s1}(2536)^+\to D^{*+}K^0_S\) and \(D_{s1}(2536)^-\to \bar D^{*0}K^-\) allow an estimate of the S-wave \(D^*K\) scattering length in the isospin-0 channel, under the assumption that near the \(D^*K\) threshold the measured phase differences are almost completely determined by the phase of the S-wave amplitude of the background \(D^*K\) scattering.

## Method

The authors extracted the absolute value of the phase difference between the S- and D-wave amplitudes from the Belle (2008) and LHCb (2023) three-dimensional angular distribution data, averaging the results from both experiments. They assumed that near the \(D^*K\) threshold the background S-wave phase \(\bar\delta_0\) follows the scattering length approximation \(\bar\delta_0 = a q\), where \(q\) is the center-of-mass momentum, and that the D-wave and mixing contributions are subleading. Using the average phase difference \(|\varphi_0-\varphi_2| = 0.75\pm0.03\) rad at \(\sqrt{s}=m_R\) (the \(D_{s1}(2536)\) mass) and the average momentum \(q=0.158\) GeV, they derived the scattering length magnitude, including a small correction for the \(D_{s1}(2536)\) resonance contribution.

## Key numbers

*   \(|a^{(0)}_{D^*K}| = (0.94 \pm 0.06)\) fm, the S-wave \(D^*K\) scattering length in the isospin-0 channel, obtained from the average phase difference \(|\varphi_0-\varphi_2| = 0.75\pm0.03\) rad at \(q = 0.158\) GeV.
*   \(|\varphi_0-\varphi_2| = (43 \pm 1.7)^\circ\), the average absolute phase difference between S- and D-wave amplitudes from Belle and LHCb data.
*   \(q = 0.158\) GeV, the average center-of-mass momentum at the \(D_{s1}(2536)\) mass, computed using average \(D^*\) and \(K\) meson masses.
*   For the \(D_{s1}(2536)\) decay: \(|S| = 0.792\) GeV, \(\Gamma_S = 0.41\) MeV, \(\Gamma_D = 0.25\) MeV, derived from Belle and LHCb amplitude ratios and the BESIII branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(D_{s1}(2536)^+\to(D^*K)^+) = (71.8\pm9.6\pm7.0)\%\).
*   For the \(D_{s1}(2460)\) resonance contribution: \(G^2 = 3.6\) (coupling to \(D^*K\)), leading to \(\tilde{\Gamma}_S(m_R) \approx 570\) MeV at the \(D_{s1}(2536)\) mass.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical calculations of charmed meson scattering lengths from lattice QCD (Li 2013, Gu 2019), effective chiral theory (Gu 2009, Ge 2010, Li 2011, Gu 2018, Gu 2019, Hu 2022), and other methods (Al 2023, Ik 2023, To 2023, Kh 2024, Li 2024), which were previously inaccessible to direct experiment due to the short lifetimes of charmed mesons. The result is close in magnitude to the value 0.76 fm calculated by Li (2011) using heavy-meson chiral perturbation theory. The analysis enables future experimental checks via femtoscopic correlation functions of \(D^{*0}K^+\) and \(D^{*+}K^0\) pairs, as recently pioneered by the ALICE Collaboration (2024), and provides a method to extract the \(D_{s1}(2460)\) resonance coupling to \(D^*K\) if the scattering length is negative.
