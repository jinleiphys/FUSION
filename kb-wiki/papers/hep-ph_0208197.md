# Time Delay Plots of Unflavoured Baryons

> We explore the usefulness of the existing relations between the $S$-matrix and time delay in characterizing baryon resonances in pion-nucleon scattering. We draw attention to the fact that the existence of a positive maximum in time delay is a necessary criterion for the existence of a resonance and should be used as a constraint in conventional analyses which locate resonances from poles of the $S$-matrix and Argand diagrams. The usefulness of the time delay plots of resonances is demonstrated through a detailed analysis of the time delay in several partial waves of $πN$ elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a positive maximum in time delay is a necessary criterion for the existence of a baryon resonance and should be used as a constraint in conventional analyses that locate resonances from poles of the S-matrix and Argand diagrams, though the authors acknowledge that non-resonant background can deform the positive resonant structure.

## Method

The authors calculated time delay in πN elastic scattering using both the energy derivative of phase shifts and the T-matrix formulation, applying these to single-energy phase shift values from cross-section data and to energy-dependent T-matrix solutions from the SAID program (FA02 and SM95 solutions). They extracted resonance parameters by locating the positions of local maxima in time delay distributions and reading off the widths, then compared these with Particle Data Group Summary Table values and pole positions.

## Key numbers

- The P33 (Δ(1232)) resonance shows a time delay peak at 1216 MeV with a width of approximately 116 MeV, compared to the PDG average mass of 1232 MeV and width of 120 MeV.
- The D13 (N(1520)) resonance shows a time delay peak at 1512 MeV with a width of approximately 50 MeV, compared to the PDG average mass of 1520 MeV and full width of 120 MeV.
- From single-energy fits, the S11 partial wave shows time delay peaks at 1510 MeV (width 37.8 MeV), 1630 MeV (width ~40 MeV), and 1680 MeV (width ~24 MeV), associated with S11(1535), S11(1650), and a possible new resonance.
- From the FA02 energy-dependent solution, the P11 partial wave shows time delay peaks at 1370 MeV (width 298 MeV) and 1745 MeV (width 50 MeV), corresponding to P11(1440) and P11(1710).
- The G17 partial wave shows a time delay peak at 1900 MeV (width 310 MeV), while the FA02 pole position is at 2084 MeV, a shift of nearly 200 MeV.
- The H19 partial wave shows a time delay peak at 2050 MeV (width 276 MeV), while the FA02 pole position is at 2230 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the time delay method, previously applied to meson resonances by Neels et al., to baryon resonances in πN scattering, providing a practical test of the method directly on data. The authors note that the time delay criterion offers a complementary constraint to standard pole-finding and Argand diagram analyses, which have been questioned by authors such as Calucci (who constructed examples of sharp resonances without poles) and Dalitz (who discussed various resonance criteria). The results suggest that time delay plots can reveal resonances with small branching fractions to the πN channel and may indicate new structures, though the authors caution that the quality of single-energy data limits definitive conclusions.
