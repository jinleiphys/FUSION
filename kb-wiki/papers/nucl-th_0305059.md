# Simple solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for longitudinally and cylindrically expanding systems

> Simple, self-similar, analytic solutions of 1 + 1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics are presented, generalizing the Hwa - Bjorken boost-invariant solution to inhomogeneous rapidity distributions. These solutions are generalized also to 1 + 3 dimensional, cylindrically symmetric firetubes, corresponding to central collisions of heavy ions at relativistic bombarding energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents new families of exact analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics that generalize the Hwa-Bjorken boost-invariant solution to allow inhomogeneous rapidity distributions and transverse flow, but only under the condition that the flow velocity field takes the specific scaling form \(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{r}/t\) (a Hubble-type flow) and the product of the density and temperature scaling functions satisfies a constraint, leaving one of them arbitrary.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic continuity equation, the Euler equation, and the temperature equation for a perfect fluid with equations of state \(\varepsilon = m n + \kappa p\) and \(p = n T\), where \(\kappa = 1/c_s^2\). They assume self-similar, Hubble-type velocity profiles of the form \(v_i = (\dot{X}_i / X_i) r_i\) and search for thermodynamic quantities that factor into a time-dependent part and an arbitrary function of the scaling variable \(s = r_x^2/X^2 + r_y^2/Y^2 + r_z^2/Z^2\). For the 1+1 dimensional case, they impose longitudinal scaling; for the 1+3 dimensional cylindrically symmetric case, they impose boost-invariant longitudinal flow and transverse Hubble flow, then derive constraints from the Euler equation that fix the scale parameters and relate the density and temperature scaling functions.

## Key numbers

- For the 1+1 dimensional solution, the effective temperature is given by \(T_{\mathrm{eff}}(y) = T_0 (t_0/\tau_f)^{1/\kappa} / \mathcal{V}(\tanh^2 y / \dot{Z}_0^2)\), with example calculations using \(T_0 (t_0/\tau_f)^{1/\kappa} = 200\) MeV and \(n_0 t_0 = 900\) for a single-peak rapidity distribution.
- For the 1+1 dimensional solution with a two-peaked rapidity distribution, the example uses \(n_0 t_0 = 800\) and \(T_0 (t_0/\tau_f)^{1/\kappa} = 200\) MeV with \(\dot{Z}_0^2 = 1\).
- The 1+3 dimensional solution yields a pressure profile \(p(t,\mathbf{r}) = p_0 (\tau_{z0}/\tau)^{3 + 3/\kappa}\) and a temperature profile \(T(t,\mathbf{r}) = T_0 (\tau_{z0}/\tau)^{3/\kappa} / \NN(s)\), where \(\tau\) is the proper time.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic self-similar solutions of Zimányi, Bondorf, and Garpman (1978) and the Gaussian solutions of Csörgő et al. (2000) to the relativistic regime, and generalizes the Hwa-Bjorken boost-invariant solution (Hwa, 1974; Bjorken, 1983) to include inhomogeneous rapidity distributions and transverse flow. It also provides an analytic foundation for the Buda-Lund parameterization (Csörgő et al., 1996) and the Cracow hydrodynamic parameterization used to describe Au+Au collisions at RHIC at \(\sqrt{s}=130\) and 200 AGeV. The solutions enable analytic calculation of single-particle spectra and two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions, and can serve as test cases for numerical hydrodynamic codes.
