# Collective phenomena in chirally imbalanced medium

> We calculate the gluon polarization tensor for a chirally imbalanced plasma using hard thermal loop approximation in the real time formulation of thermal field theory. The dispersion relations obtained from the poles of the effective gluon propagator are solved numerically as well as analytically in appropriate limiting cases. It is seen that the degenerate transverse modes split into left and right handed circularly polarized modes. We also compute imaginary poles of the propagator which signal the presence of instability in the plasma. Relevant time scales for development of such instabilities are discussed in detail. Furthermore, we compute both the real and imaginary parts of the static heavy-quark potential in the chirally imbalanced plasma and argue that quarkonium suppression is enhanced due to the combined effects of a reduced debye screening length and an increased decay width. In addition, we calculate the gluon spectral density, sum rules and residues for various cases, providing a comprehensive understanding of the collective behaviour of the medium.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that in a chirally imbalanced quark-gluon plasma, the degenerate transverse gluonic modes split into left- and right-handed circularly polarized modes, and that the presence of a chiral chemical potential enhances quarkonium suppression through a combined reduction of the Debye screening length and an increase in the decay width, but these results are derived within the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation and the real-time formulation of thermal field theory, which imposes a weak-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop gluon polarization tensor for a chirally imbalanced plasma using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation within the real-time formulation of thermal field theory. They evaluated the 11-component of the gluon self-energy from the fermionic loop diagram, extracted the real part of the analytic self-energy, and decomposed the polarization tensor into longitudinal, transverse, and anomalous (chirality-induced) structure functions. Dispersion relations were obtained from the poles of the effective gluon propagator via the Dyson-Schwinger equation, and the static heavy-quark potential was derived from the Fourier transform of the gluon propagator in the static limit.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass: \( M_D^2 = g^2 \frac{T^2}{6} (N_f + 2C_A) + \frac{N_f g^2}{2\pi^2} (\mu^2 + \mu_5^2) \), where \(T\) is temperature, \(\mu\) is the quark chemical potential, \(\mu_5\) is the chiral chemical potential, \(N_f\) is the number of quark flavors, and \(C_A\) is the Casimir invariant for the adjoint representation.
* Longitudinal structure function: \( \Pi_L = M_D^2 \frac{q_0^2 - q^2}{q^2} \left(1 - \frac{q_0}{2q} \log{\frac{q_0+q}{q_0-q}}\right) \).
* Transverse structure function: \( \Pi_T = -\frac{1}{2} M_D^2 \frac{q_0^2}{q^2} \left(1 + \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{q}{q_0} - \frac{q_0}{q}\right) \log{\frac{q_0+q}{q_0-q}}\right) \).
* Anomalous structure function: \( \Pi_A = -\frac{g^2}{2\pi^2} \frac{N_f}{2} \mu_5 \frac{q_0^2 - q^2}{q} \left(1 - \frac{q_0}{2q} \log{\frac{q_0+q}{q_0-q}}\right) \).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic-theory calculation of the gluon polarization tensor in a chirally asymmetric system by Son and Surowka (2012) and the demonstration of instabilities in isotropic chiral plasma by Akamatsu and Yamamoto (2013) to a full diagrammatic calculation using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory. The authors also extend the study of heavy-quark potentials in a thermal medium, previously addressed via lattice QCD by Rothkopf (2011) and Burnier and Rothkopf (2014), to the case of a chirally imbalanced plasma. The results enable further investigation of quarkonium suppression mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions and the role of chiral asymmetry in plasma instabilities and thermalization timescales.
