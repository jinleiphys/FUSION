# Fusion dynamics of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C reaction: An astrophysical interest within the relativistic mean-field approach

> The $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion reaction plays a significant role in the later phases of stellar evolution. For a better understanding of the evolution involved, one must understand the corresponding fusion-fission dynamics and reaction characteristics. In the present analysis, we have studied the fusion cross-section along with the S-factor for this reaction using the well-known M3Y and recently developed R3Y nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential along with the relativistic mean-field densities in double folding approach. The density distributions and the microscopic R3Y NN potential are calculated using the NL3$^*$ parameter set. The $\ell$- summed Wong model is employed to investigate the fusion cross-section, with $\ell_{max}$-values from the sharp cut-off model. The calculated results are also then compared with the experimental data. It is found that the R3Y interaction gives a reasonable agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential derived from the relativistic mean-field approach with the NL3* parameter set gives a reasonable fit to the experimental fusion cross-section and S-factor for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C reaction, but only at energies around and above the Coulomb barrier; at far below-barrier energies (below 4.0 MeV), both the M3Y and R3Y potentials overestimate the extrapolated data.

## Method

The authors used the double folding approach to compute the nuclear interaction potential, folding relativistic mean-field (RMF) density distributions of the projectile and target with either the phenomenological M3Y or the microscopic R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential. The RMF densities and the R3Y potential were calculated using the NL3* parameter set. The fusion cross-section was then obtained using the $\ell$-summed Wong model, with the maximum angular momentum $\ell_{max}$ determined from the sharp cut-off model. The astrophysical S-factor was derived from the calculated cross-section using the standard formula for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system.

## Key numbers

- Barrier height for the M3Y NN interaction: 6.264 MeV; for the R3Y NN interaction: 5.955 MeV.
- The R3Y interaction gives a higher fusion cross-section than the M3Y interaction at all center-of-mass energies studied, from 0.5 MeV upward.
- At far below-barrier energies ($E_{c.m.} < 4.0$ MeV), both M3Y and R3Y potentials yield higher fusion cross-sections and S-factors than the extrapolated data of Patterson (1969).
- The astrophysically significant energy range for this reaction is 1-3 MeV, corresponding to stellar temperatures of 0.8 to 1.2 GK.

## Context

This work extends the application of the relativistic mean-field approach, previously used for proton and cluster radioactivity, to the fusion dynamics of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C reaction, which is critical for carbon burning in massive stars. It compares the recently developed R3Y potential with the well-established M3Y potential, and finds the R3Y interaction provides a better fit to experimental data from Patterson (1969) and Aguilera (2006) than the M3Y interaction. The results enable future theoretical predictions of fusion cross-sections at astrophysically relevant sub-barrier energies, where experimental data are unavailable, and suggest extending this microscopic approach to other carbon and oxygen burning reactions such as $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O.
