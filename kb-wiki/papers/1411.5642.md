# Jet transport and photon bremsstrahlung via longitudinal and transverse scattering

> We study the effect of multiple scatterings on the propagation of hard partons and the production of jet-bremsstrahlung photons inside a dense medium in the framework of deep-inelastic scattering off a large nucleus. We include the momentum exchanges in both longitudinal and transverse directions between the hard partons and the constituents of the medium. Keeping up to the second order in a momentum gradient expansion, we derive the spectrum for the photon emission from a hard quark jet when traversing dense nuclear matter. Our calculation demonstrates that the photon bremsstrahlung process is influenced not only by the transverse momentum diffusion of the propagating hard parton, but also by the longitudinal drag and diffusion of the parton momentum. A notable outcome is that the longitudinal drag tends to reduce the amount of stimulated emission from the hard parton.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the photon bremsstrahlung spectrum from a hard quark jet traversing dense nuclear matter is influenced not only by transverse momentum diffusion but also by longitudinal drag and diffusion of the parton momentum, with the notable condition that longitudinal drag tends to reduce the amount of stimulated emission from the hard parton.

## Method

The authors calculate the medium-induced photon bremsstrahlung spectrum from a hard quark produced in deep-inelastic scattering off a large nucleus, using the higher twist (HT) approach with a momentum gradient expansion kept to second order. They include momentum exchanges in both longitudinal and transverse directions between the hard parton and the medium constituents, allowing one component of the longitudinal momentum to be of the order of the transverse momentum exchanged (a "longitudinal-Glauber" scaling). The calculation resums length-enhanced higher twist contributions from multiple scatterings and derives the final expression for the photon bremsstrahlung spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The photon carries a fraction \(y = l^-/q^-\) of the quark forward momentum \(q^-\), where \(l^-\) is the photon's minus-component and \(q^-\) is the virtual photon's minus-component.
- The photon formation time is related to \(x_L = l_\perp^2/[2p^+q^-y(1-y)]\) as \(\tau_{\rm form} = 1/(x_L p^+)\).
- The quark-to-photon splitting function is \(P(y)=[1+(1-y)^2]/y\).
- The exchanged gluons in the longitudinal-Glauber scaling have momenta: \(k^{+} \sim \lambda^{2} Q\), \(k^{-} \sim \lambda Q\), \(k_{\perp} = \lambda Q\), where \(Q\) is the hardest scale and \(\lambda\) is a small dimensionless parameter.

## Context

This work extends the higher twist (HT) approach for medium-induced gluon radiation (Guo and Wang, 2000; Wang, 2001; Majumder, 2009) by incorporating longitudinal momentum exchange into the stimulated radiation vertex, which previous calculations of radiative energy loss had only considered for purely collisional energy loss (Bjorken, 1982; Braaten and Thoma, 1991). The photon calculation serves as an intermediate step toward a full treatment of medium-induced gluon emission with longitudinal scattering, and the results are relevant for photon-jet correlations (Renk, 2006; Zhang, 2009; Qin, 2009) as an electromagnetic probe of jet transport properties in dense media.
