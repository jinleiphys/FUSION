# Compton Scattering and Generalized Polarizabilities

> In recent years, real and virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon have attracted considerable interest from both the experimental and theoretical sides. Real Compton scattering gives access to the so-called electromagnetic polarizabilities containing the structure information beyond the global properties of the nucleon such as its charge, mass, and magnetic moment. These polarizabilities have an intuitive interpretation in terms of induced dipole moments and thus characterize the response of the constituents of the nucleon to a soft external stimulus. The virtual Compton scattering reaction $e^-p\to e^-pγ$ allows one to map out the {\em local} response to external fields and can be described in terms of generalized electromagnetic polarizabilities. A simple classical interpretation in terms of the induced electric and magnetic polarization densities is proposed. We will discuss experimental results for the polarizabilities of the proton and compare them with theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

Real and virtual Compton scattering provide access to nucleon structure beyond global properties like charge, mass, and magnetic moment, but the extraction of polarizabilities depends on model-dependent assumptions and the specific theoretical framework used for analysis, such as chiral perturbation theory or dispersion relations.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results for real Compton scattering (RCS) off the proton and neutron, and virtual Compton scattering (VCS) via the reaction e⁻p → e⁻pγ. For RCS, the low-energy scattering amplitude is expanded in terms of photon momentum, with second-order terms parameterized by electric and magnetic polarizabilities α and β. For VCS, the amplitude is separated into a gauge-invariant pole piece and a residual piece, which is analyzed using electromagnetic multipole decomposition to extract generalized polarizabilities (GPs) that describe the local response of the nucleon to external fields.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability: α_p = 12.1 ± 0.3_stat. ∓ 0.4_syst. ± 0.3_mod. (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Proton magnetic polarizability: β_p = 1.6 ± 0.4_stat. ± 0.4_syst. ± 0.4_mod. (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Baldin sum rule for proton: α_p + β_p = (13.8 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron electric polarizability from quasi-free Compton scattering: α_n = 12.5 ± 1.8_stat. +1.1/‑0.6_syst. ± 1.1_mod. (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Neutron magnetic polarizability from quasi-free Compton scattering: β_n = 2.7 ∓ 1.8_stat. +0.6/‑1.1_syst. ∓ 1.1_mod. (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Baldin sum rule for neutron: α_n + β_n = (15.2 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- VCS structure functions at Q² = 0.33 GeV² and ε = 0.62: P_LL − P_TT/ε = 23.7 ± 2.2_stat. ± 4.3_syst. ± 0.6_syst.norm. GeV⁻²; P_LT = −5.0 ± 0.8_stat. ± 1.4_syst. ± 1.1_syst.norm. GeV⁻²

## Context

This review extends the long history of Compton scattering studies, building on the low-energy theorem of Low (1954) and Gell-Mann and Goldberger (1954), and the Baldin sum rule (1960). It summarizes experimental results from dedicated experiments at MAMI (Roche 2000), MIT/Bates (Miskimen 2004), and JLab (Fonvieille 2002), and compares them with theoretical predictions from chiral perturbation theory (Hemmert 1997), the linear sigma model (Metz 1996), and the nonrelativistic constituent quark model (Pasquini 2000). The work enables future experiments to map out generalized polarizabilities at higher momentum transfers and to test the microscopic origin of the diamagnetic contribution to the magnetic polarizability.
