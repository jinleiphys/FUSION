# Energy loss of quarks in deconfined matter at RHIC: photon-tagged jets, single electron and dilepton spectra from open charm

> We report a first attempt (i) to derive constraints on the energy loss of charm quarks in a deconfined medium from the recent PHENIX data of single-electron transverse momentum spectra and (ii) to estimate the resulting suppression of dileptons from correlated semi-leptonic decays of open charmed mesons. The momentum imbalance of photon-tagged light-quark jets is also considered.

## Key claim

The paper reports a first attempt to derive constraints on charm quark energy loss in deconfined matter from PHENIX single-electron data, but finds that the data do not significantly constrain the energy loss because both zero and substantial energy loss are compatible with the measured spectra.

## Method

The authors use PYTHIA 6.206 with a charm quark mass of 1.5 GeV, intrinsic parton transverse momentum of 2.5 GeV, and a K factor of 5.7 to generate charm cross sections and decay spectra. They implement a simplified energy loss model based on the BDMS formalism, using Monte Carlo averaging over traversed path lengths in a Bjorken-expanding medium with initial temperature 550 MeV and initial time 0.2 fm/c, parameterizing the energy loss strength by a factor zeta. The resulting single-electron spectra are compared to PHENIX data for minimum bias and central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Charm cross section: sigma_{c cbar}^{NN} = 404 microbarns at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV
- Thickness functions: T_AA = 6.2 mb^{-1} for minimum bias collisions, 22.6 mb^{-1} for central collisions
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for single-electron spectra: 0.27 for minimum bias, 0.39 for central collisions without energy loss
- Optimum energy loss strength: zeta = 0.2 to 0.5, with chi^2_{d.o.f.} = 0.32 to 0.31
- Larger energy losses also compatible: chi^2_{d.o.f.} = 0.43 for zeta = 1.0, 0.76 for zeta = 2.0
- No energy loss gives chi^2_{d.o.f.} = 0.39 for central collisions
- Initial temperature: T_i = 550 MeV
- Initial time: tau_i = 0.2 fm/c
- Transverse radius: R_A = 7 fm
- Chiral transition temperature: 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Gallmeister et al. (2001) and Vogt (2001) on charm energy loss and dilepton suppression, and uses the BDMS energy loss formalism from Baier et al. (2001). The paper shows that PHENIX single-electron data at 130 GeV cannot distinguish between zero and moderate charm quark energy loss, which complicates the identification of thermal dilepton contributions in heavy-ion collisions as discussed by Shuryak (2002) and Gale (2002). The authors suggest that better statistics from 200 GeV Au+Au data and pp reference data will be needed for quantitative conclusions, and propose photon-tagged jets as a complementary probe for jet tomography.
