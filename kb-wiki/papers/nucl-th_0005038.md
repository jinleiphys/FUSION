# Nucleon structure effect on the longitudinal response function

> Using the quark-meson coupling model, we study the longitudinal response function for quasielastic electron scattering from nuclear matter within relativistic RPA. In QMC the coupling constant between the scalar meson and the nucleon is expected to decrease with increasing nuclear density. Furthermore, since the electromagnetic form factors of the in-medium nucleon are modified at the same time, the longitudinal response function and the Coulomb sum are reduced by a total of about 20% in comparison with the Hartree contribution.

## Key claim
Using the quark-meson coupling model, the longitudinal response function and Coulomb sum for quasielastic electron scattering from nuclear matter are reduced by about 20% in total compared to the Hartree contribution, with roughly equal contributions from relativistic RPA correlations and from in-medium modification of the proton electric form factor.

## Method
The author calculates the longitudinal response function for nuclear matter within relativistic RPA using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which extends Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD-I) by treating nucleons as MIT bags with quarks coupling to scalar and vector mesons. Three modifications to QHD-I are incorporated: a monopole meson-nucleon vertex form factor with cutoff 1.5 GeV, a density-dependent scalar-meson coupling constant that decreases by about 9% at normal nuclear density, and an in-medium proton electric form factor whose ratio to the free-space form factor is parametrized as \(1 - 0.26 \times Q^2\) at normal density. The nuclear ground state is solved in relativistic Hartree approximation with renormalized vacuum polarization.

## Key numbers
- The scalar-meson coupling constant \(g_s^2\) decreases from 61.85 at zero density by about 9% at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\).
- The effective nucleon mass \(M^*/M = 0.81\) at \(\rho_0\), with incompressibility \(K = 281\) MeV.
- The ratio of the in-medium to free-space proton electric form factor at \(\rho_0\) is parametrized as \(R_{pE}(\rho_0, Q^2) \simeq 1 - 0.26 \times Q^2\) (with \(Q^2\) in GeV\(^2\)).
- The longitudinal response function and Coulomb sum are reduced by about 20% in total compared to the Hartree contribution, with RRPA correlations and in-medium nucleon structure each contributing roughly half.

## Context
This work extends the classic QHD-I approach of Serot and Walecka (1980s) and the relativistic RPA calculations of Horowitz and collaborators, which treated nucleons as point-like particles. By using the QMC model developed by Guichon and collaborators, the author incorporates the expected density-dependent reduction of the scalar-meson coupling and the modification of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the nuclear medium. The results suggest that future calculations for finite nuclei could be compared directly with new experimental data anticipated from electron scattering experiments.
