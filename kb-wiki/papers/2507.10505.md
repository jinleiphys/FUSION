# Signatures of Odd-Parity $s$-wave $Ξ^*$ States in Femtoscopic Correlation Functions

> We investigate the $Ξ^*$ resonances within the molecular picture, where these states are dynamically generated as poles in the unitarized scattering amplitudes arising from the coupled-channel interactions of $K^{*-} Λ$, $K^{*-} Σ^0$, $ρ^- Ξ^0$, $\overline{K}{}^{*0} Σ^-$, $ρ^0 Ξ^-$, $ωΞ^-$, and $ϕΞ^-$. The interaction kernel is derived from the local hidden gauge formalism, while the unitarization procedure employs a hybrid method that combines cutoff and dimensional regularizations in the evaluation of the loop function. From a detailed spectroscopic analysis, we identify two $S = -2$ baryon states whose properties are compatible with some of the $Ξ^*$ resonances listed in the Review of Particle Physics. To explore their possible experimental signatures, we compute the femtoscopic correlation functions for all the vector-baryon pairs considered in the present study, using realistic estimates of production weights and varying source sizes $R = 1, 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.5$ fm.

## Key claim
The paper claims that two odd-parity $s$-wave $\Xi^*$ resonances, identified with the $\Xi(1950)$ and a second state near 2000 MeV, can be dynamically generated as vector-baryon molecular states from the hidden gauge formalism, and that their signatures can be observed in femtoscopic correlation functions. The hedge is that the identification relies on matching the real part of the lowest pole to the PDG mass of the $\Xi(1950)$ within its uncertainty, and the second state is a prediction whose existence depends on the chosen cutoff regularization.

## Method
The authors derive the vector-baryon interaction kernel from the local hidden gauge formalism via $t$-channel vector-meson exchange, reducing it to a Weinberg-Tomozawa-type contact interaction. They unitarize the coupled-channel scattering amplitudes by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation using a hybrid loop function that combines cutoff and dimensional regularization, and incorporate the finite widths of the $\rho$ and $\overline{K}{}^*$ mesons by folding the loop functions with their spectral functions. They then compute femtoscopic correlation functions for all seven coupled channels ($K^{*-}\Lambda$, $K^{*-}\Sigma^0$, $\rho^-\Xi^0$, $\overline{K}{}^{*0}\Sigma^-$, $\rho^0\Xi^-$, $\omega\Xi^-$, $\phi\Xi^-$) using a Gaussian source and production weights obtained from the Thermal-FIST package via the VLC method.

## Key numbers
- Pole positions for the $\Xi(1950)$ state (pole 1) without vector-meson widths: $M_R = 1950.05$ MeV (central cutoff $\Lambda = 795$ MeV), with zero imaginary part; with widths included: $M_R = 1949.79$ MeV, $\Gamma/2 = 7.06$ MeV (central cutoff $\Lambda = 775$ MeV).
- Pole positions for the second $\Xi^*$ state (pole 2) without widths: $M_R = 1994.12$ MeV, $\Gamma/2 = 0$ MeV ($\Lambda = 795$ MeV); with widths: $M_R = 2000.91$ MeV, $\Gamma/2 = 0.84$ MeV ($\Lambda = 775$ MeV).
- Cutoff values varied to match the PDG $\Xi(1950)$ mass uncertainty ($1950 \pm 15$ MeV): $\Lambda = 755, 795, 830$ MeV (without widths) and $\Lambda = 733, 775, 813$ MeV (with widths).
- Source sizes used in correlation function calculations: $R = 1, 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.5$ fm.
- Production weights for the $K^{*-}\Lambda$ correlation function: $\omega_j = 1$ for the $K^{*-}\Lambda$ channel itself, $0.043(3)$ for $K^{*-}\Sigma^0$, $0.062(5)$ for $\rho^-\Xi^0$, $0.036(3)$ for $\overline{K}{}^{*0}\Sigma^-$, $0.032(3)$ for $\rho^0\Xi^-$, and $0$ for $\omega\Xi^-$ and $\phi\Xi^-$.

## Context
This work extends the hidden gauge symmetry studies of Oset and Ramos (2010) and Garzon and Oset (2012) in the $S=-2$ sector, where two degenerate $J^P = 1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ poles were previously found and identified with the $\Xi(1950)$ and $\Xi(2120)$. The present paper refines those predictions by using a hybrid regularization scheme and including vector-meson widths, finding the second pole closer to 2000 MeV rather than 2120 MeV. It also builds on recent femtoscopy developments, particularly the analysis of the $\phi p$ correlation function by Feijoo et al. (2024) and the $S=-1$ vector-baryon femtoscopy study by Encarnacion et al. (2025), enabling future experimental tests of the predicted $\Xi^*$ states via correlation function measurements at facilities such as ALICE.
