# Temperature dependence of the volume and surface contributions to the nuclear symmetry energy within the coherent density fluctuation model

> The temperature dependence of the volume and surface components of the nuclear symmetry energy (NSE) and their ratio is investigated in the framework of the local density approximation (LDA). The results of these quantities for finite nuclei are obtained within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). The CDFM weight function is obtained using the temperature-dependent proton and neutron densities calculated through the HFBTHO code that solves the nuclear Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov problem by using the cylindrical transformed deformed harmonic-oscillator basis. We present and discuss the values of the volume and surface contributions to the NSE and their ratio obtained for the Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains around double-magic $^{78}$Ni, $^{132}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb nuclei. The results for the $T$-dependence of the considered quantities are compared with estimations made previously for zero temperature showing the behavior of the NSE components and their ratio, as well as with the available experimental data. The sensitivity of the results on various forms of the density dependence of the symmetry energy is studied. We confirm the existence of `kinks' of these quantities as functions of the mass number at $T=0$ MeV for the double closed-shell nuclei $^{78}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn and the lack of `kinks' for the Pb isotopes, as well as the disappearance of these kinks as the temperature increases.

# Temperature dependence of the volume and surface contributions to the nuclear symmetry energy within the coherent density fluctuation model

## Key claim
The volume and surface components of the nuclear symmetry energy and their ratio are calculated for finite nuclei at finite temperature using the coherent density fluctuation model, with the finding that the "kinks" observed at T=0 MeV for double closed-shell nuclei 78Ni and 132Sn disappear as temperature increases, while the Pb isotopes show no such kinks at any temperature.

## Method
The authors use the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to transition from nuclear matter to finite nuclei, employing temperature-dependent proton and neutron density distributions calculated with the HFBTHO code (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with cylindrical transformed deformed harmonic-oscillator basis) using SkM* and SLy4 Skyrme forces. The CDFM weight function is obtained from the temperature-dependent total density distributions, and the symmetry energy for asymmetric nuclear matter is incorporated through a power-law parametrization with parameter gamma, as well as alternative density dependences from the literature. The volume and surface components of the nuclear symmetry energy are extracted using the droplet model expansion relating the finite-nucleus symmetry energy S(T) to its volume component S^V(T) and surface component S^S(T) via the ratio kappa(T) = S^V(T)/S^S(T).

## Key numbers
- For the 78Ni nucleus at T=0 MeV with the power-law parametrization [Eq. (19)], kappa = 1.66 for gamma=0.3 and kappa = 2.64 for gamma=0.4; S(T) = 21.35 MeV for gamma=0.3 and S(T) = 19.08 MeV for gamma=0.4.
- For the 78Ni nucleus at T=0 MeV with the alternative parametrization S4(rho) [Eq. (23)], kappa = 1.90 for gamma=0.2 and kappa = 2.69 for gamma=0.3; S(T) = 19.00 MeV for gamma=0.2 and S(T) = 17.48 MeV for gamma=0.3.
- For the 78Ni nucleus at T=0 MeV with the density-dependent M3Y-type parametrization [Eq. (22)], kappa values are 10.2 (S1), 11.9 (S2), and 13.82 (S3), with corresponding S(T) values of 14.62 MeV, 14.22 MeV, and 13.85 MeV.
- The ratio kappa at T=0 MeV for gamma=0.4 is approximately 2.6, consistent with the range 2.10 to 2.90 from previous Brueckner EDF results and with published values from IAS and skin data (2.6 to 3.0) and from masses and skins (2.0 to 2.8).
- For gamma=0.3 at T=0 MeV, kappa = 1.65, consistent with the range 1.6 to 2.0 from data analyses and with previous Skyrme EDF results (1.5 to 1.7 for Ni, 1.52 to 2.1 for Sn, 1.65 to 1.75 for Pb).
- The calculations cover temperatures from T=0 MeV to T=3 MeV for isotopic chains of Ni, Sn, and Pb nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature analysis of volume and surface symmetry energy components (Antonov 2016) and their temperature-dependent study of the nuclear symmetry energy S(T) (Antonov 2017) by now decomposing the finite-temperature symmetry energy into its volume and surface contributions. The results are compared with the subtracted finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi framework of De (2012a), which found substantial temperature dependence of the surface symmetry energy but less sensitivity of the volume term. The study also builds on the pioneering work of Feenberg (1947) on surface symmetry energy contributions and the droplet model analyses of Danielewicz (2003, 2004, 2006) relating the volume-to-surface ratio to neutron-skin thickness. The findings enable future investigations of how thermal effects modify the nuclear equation of state for astrophysical applications such as supernova explosions and neutron star properties.
