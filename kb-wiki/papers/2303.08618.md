# Probing universal dynamics with topological data analysis in a gluonic plasma

> We study nonequilibrium dynamics of SU(2) lattice gauge theory in Minkowski space-time in a classical-statistical regime, where characteristic gluon occupancies are much larger than unity. In this strongly correlated system far from equilibrium, the correlations of energy and topological densities show self-similar behavior related to a turbulent cascade towards higher momentum scales. We employ persistent homology to infer topological features of the gluonic plasma via a hierarchy of simplicial and cubical complexes. All topological observables under investigation are also manifestly gauge invariant and are shown to exhibit self-similar evolution, which relate the spatial and temporal properties of the plasma in terms of universal scaling exponents and functions. The findings may help to understand the early stages of heavy-ion collisions in the limit of high energies, and our methods can also facilitate the topological analysis of other complex systems such as encountered in experiments with ultracold quantum gases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that topological data analysis via persistent homology can reveal universal self-similar scaling behavior in a far-from-equilibrium gluonic plasma, with the same scaling exponents governing both energy density and topological density correlations, provided the system is in a classical-statistical regime where gluon occupancies are much larger than unity.

## Method

The authors perform classical-statistical simulations of SU(2) lattice gauge theory in 3+1 Minkowski space-time with Gaussian over-occupied initial conditions characterized by a momentum scale Q. They compute connected two-point correlation functions of energy density and topological density, and construct alpha complexes from point clouds derived from these densities to extract persistent homology observables including Betti numbers and persistence ratio distributions. The time evolution is obtained by solving the classical equations of motion for link variables and electric fields on a 512^3 lattice with temporal lattice spacing dt/as = 0.05 and Qas = 0.125.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponent beta = -1/7 governs the momentum-space scaling of both energy density and topological density correlations, with alpha_T00 = -1/7 for the amplitude exponent
- The gluon occupancy scales as (t/t')^{-4/7} with momentum scaling exponent -1/7, as previously established
- The volume-averaged energy density is (0.106 +/- 8.4e-8)/Q^4, while the volume-averaged topological density is (2.2e-5 +/- 3.3e-5)/Q^4
- The Betti number scaling exponents are eta_1 = -1/7 and eta_2 = 5eta_1 = -5/7, with limiting values nu_T00 = -0.081/Q^4 and nu_q = -0.13/Q^4
- The self-similar scaling holds for the time range from Qt = 100 to Qt = 1800

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonthermal fixed points in gluonic plasmas (Berges 2013, Kurkela 2012, Schlichting 2012) by using gauge-invariant observables rather than gauge-dependent occupation numbers. It builds on persistent homology methods first applied to scalar field theory near nonthermal fixed points (Spitz 2020) and to confinement in non-Abelian gauge theory (Sale 2022, Spitz 2022). The findings enable topological analysis of other complex systems such as ultracold quantum gases and may help understand the early stages of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
