# Resonance Contributions to $η$ Photoproduction on Protons Found Using Dispersion Relations and an Isobar Model

> The contributions of the resonances $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{15}(1675)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, $D_{13}(1700)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1720)$ to $γp\to ηp$ are found from the data on cross sections, beam and target asymmetries using two approaches: fixed-t dispersion relations and an isobar model. Utilization of the two approaches and comparison of the results obtained with different parametrizations of the resonance contributions allowed us to make conclusions about the model-dependence of these contributions. We conclude that the results for the contributions of the resonances $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $F_{15}(1680)$ to corresponding multipole amplitudes are stable. With this the results for $D_{13}(1520)$ and $F_{15}(1680)$, combined with their PDG photoexcitation helicity amplitudes, allowed us to find the branching ratios $Br (D_{13}(1520)\to ηN)=0.05\pm 0.02%$, $Br (F_{15}(1680)\to ηN)=0.16\pm0.04%$ which have significantly better accuracy than the PDG data. The total Breit-Wigner width of the $S_{11}(1535)$ is model-dependent, we have obtained $Γ(S_{11}(1520))=142 MeV$ and $195 MeV$ using dispersion relations and the isobar model, respectively. The results for the $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{15}(1675)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1720)$ are model dependent, only the signs and orders of magnitude of their contributions to multipole amplitudes are determined. The results for the $D_{13}(1700)$ are strongly model-dependent.

## Key claim

The contributions of the resonances $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$ to the $\gamma p\to\eta p$ multipole amplitudes are stable and model-independent, while the results for $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{15}(1675)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1720)$ are model-dependent with only signs and orders of magnitude determined, and the results for $D_{13}(1700)$ are strongly model-dependent.

## Method

The analysis used fixed-t dispersion relations for invariant amplitudes and an isobar model with Breit-Wigner parametrizations of resonance contributions to multipole amplitudes, fitting data on differential cross sections from TAPS, GRAAL, and CLAS, polarized beam asymmetry from GRAAL, and polarized target asymmetry from ELSA in the energy range $W=1.49-1.73$ GeV. The dispersion relations approach included nucleon pole terms and dispersion integrals over resonance imaginary parts, while the isobar model included Born terms and $t$-channel $\rho$ and $\omega$ exchanges; both approaches included the Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio $Br(D_{13}(1520)\to\eta N) = 0.05 \pm 0.02\%$ (significantly better accuracy than PDG data)
- Branching ratio $Br(F_{15}(1680)\to\eta N) = 0.16 \pm 0.04\%$ (significantly better accuracy than PDG data)
- Total Breit-Wigner width of $S_{11}(1535)$: $\Gamma = 142$ MeV using dispersion relations and $\Gamma = 195$ MeV using the isobar model
- Coupling constants: $g_{\eta NN}/(4\pi)^{1/2} = 0.26$ (dispersion relations) and $0.11$ (isobar model); $g_{\eta NR}/(4\pi)^{1/2} = -1.12$ (dispersion relations) and $-0.9$ (isobar model)
- Overall $\chi^2/\text{datapoint} = 1.24$ in the dispersion relations approach and $1.22$ in the isobar model
- Magnetic moment of $P_{11}(1710)\to\gamma p$ transition: $\kappa_{pp^*} = 0.05$ to $0.06$

## Context

This work extends the isobar model analysis of $\eta$ photoproduction by Chiang et al. (2002) by adding target asymmetry data from ELSA and the Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$, and introduces a complementary fixed-t dispersion relations approach to assess model dependence. The results enable improved determination of $\eta N$ branching ratios for $D_{13}(1520)$ and $F_{15}(1680)$, and provide constraints on the possible identification of $P_{11}(1710)$ as a member of the baryon anti-decuplet predicted by the chiral soliton picture, as discussed by Diakonov et al. (1997).
