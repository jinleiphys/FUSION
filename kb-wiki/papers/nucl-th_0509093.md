# Direct Urca neutrino rate in colour superconducting quark matter

> If deconfined quark matter exists inside compact stars, the primary cooling mechanism is neutrino radiation via the direct Urca processes d->u+e+antinu_e and u+e->d+nu_e. Below a critical temperature, T_c, quark matter forms a colour superconductor, one possible manifestation of which is a condensate of <ud> quark Cooper pairs in an electric-charge neutralising background of electrons. We compute the neutrino emission rate from such a phase, including charged pair-breaking and recombination effects, and find that on a material temperature domain below T_c the pairing-induced suppression of the neutrino emission rate is not uniformly exponential. If gapless modes are present in the condensed phase, the emissivity at low temperatures is moderately enhanced above that of completely unpaired matter. The importance of charged current pair-breaking processes for neutrino emission both in the fully gapped and partially gapped regimes is emphasised.

# Direct Urca neutrino rate in colour superconducting quark matter

## Key claim

The suppression of the direct Urca neutrino emission rate in colour superconducting quark matter below the critical temperature \(T_c\) is not uniformly exponential across the full temperature domain; if gapless modes are present (when the pairing gap \(\Delta\) is smaller than the chemical potential mismatch \(\delta\mu\)), the emissivity at low temperatures can be moderately enhanced above that of completely unpaired matter.

## Method

The authors compute the neutrino emissivity from two-flavour (\(u\), \(d\)) colour superconducting quark matter in \(\beta\)-equilibrium with a charge-neutralising electron background, using the Kadanoff-Baym formalism for neutrino transport. The retarded \(W\)-boson polarisation tensor is evaluated at one-loop level, including both the normal contribution (present above \(T_c\)) and the anomalous contribution (proportional to \(\Delta^2(T)\), arising from Cooper pair-breaking and recombination). The calculation incorporates leading-order perturbative corrections to the quark dispersion relation (linear in \(\alpha_s\)) and uses a BCS-type parameterisation of the temperature-dependent gap.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature is anticipated to be \(T_c \approx 0.6 \Delta_0 \sim 50\ \text{MeV}\).
- The chemical potentials used in the numerical calculation are \(\mu_d = 400\ \text{MeV}\), \(\mu_u = 300\ \text{MeV}\), and \(\mu_e = \mu_d - \mu_u = 100\ \text{MeV}\).
- The emissivity at \(T = T_c\) matches the unpaired result \(\epsilon_0 = (914/315) \tilde{G}_F^2 \alpha_s \mu_d \mu_u \mu_e T^6\) to within a few percent.
- In the fully gapped regime (\(\zeta = \Delta/\delta\mu > 1\)), the suppression is approximately linear on the domain \(0.4 \leq T/T_c \leq 1\).
- In the partially gapped regime (\(\zeta < 1\)), the normalised emissivity tends to a constant at low temperatures rather than being exponentially suppressed.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino emission from unpaired quark matter (Iwamoto, 1982) and from colour superconducting phases, including three-flavour pairing (Jotwani, 2004) and neutral-current pair-breaking processes (Jaikumar and Prakash, 2001). It differs from prior treatments of isospin-triplet pairing (Duncan, Shapiro, and Wasserman, 1983; Yakovlev, 1984) by including coherence factors and pair-breaking processes with a BCS dispersion relation, and from the nuclear Urca case (Sedrakian, 2005) by applying the charged-current pair-breaking mechanism to quark matter. The results are relevant for numerical simulations of the cooling history of neutron stars that may undergo a phase transition to superconducting quark matter, particularly during the protoneutron star phase where temperatures are near \(T_c\) and neutrinos are trapped.
