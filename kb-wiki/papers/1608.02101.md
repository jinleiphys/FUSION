# The energy density representation of the strangeness enhancement from p+p to Pb+Pb

> The energy density is the prime parameter to define the deconfinement of quarks and gluons occurring in collisions of heavy ions. Recently, there is mounting evidence that many observables in proton-proton collisions behave in a manner very similar to the one observed in heavy ions. We present as an additional piece of evidence, a scaling of the strange particle yields as a function of the energy density of the three collision systems: p+p p+Pb and Pb+Pb, using the latest results of the ALICE collaboration.

## Key claim

The relative production of strange and multi-strange baryons (relative to pions) scales with the initial energy density (specifically the Bjorken energy density times hadronization time, ετ) across p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions, with the caveat that the Omega baryon results are not fully conclusive due to large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compute the Bjorken energy density (ετ) for each multiplicity bin in p+p at 7 TeV, p+Pb at 5.02 TeV, and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV using the formula ετ = (3/2)⟨pT⟩(dN/dη)/A, where A is the transverse area of the thermalized system. The radii for p+p and p+Pb are taken from a common parametrization R = 0.405(dN/dη)^(1/3) + 0.332, while for Pb+Pb they use R = 0.772(dN/dη)^(1/3) + 0.049, both derived from ALICE femtoscopy measurements. They then plot the ratios of Ξ/π, Λ/π, and Ω/π as functions of ετ for all three systems.

## Key numbers

- Ξ/π ratios for p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb show a "nearly perfect scaling" when plotted against ετ, with data taken at 7, 5.02, and 2.76 TeV respectively.
- Λ/π ratios also show the same scaling trend across the three systems at the same energies.
- Ω/π ratios show possible higher production in Pb+Pb compared to p+p and p+Pb, but the authors state this is "not completely conclusive due to the large errors."
- The radii parametrization for p+p and p+Pb is R = 0.405(dN/dη)^(1/3) + 0.332, and for Pb+Pb is R = 0.772(dN/dη)^(1/3) + 0.049.
- The pseudorapidity range used is |η| < 0.5 for the particle ratios and |η| < 0.3 for the charged particle densities used in the energy density calculation.

## Context

This work extends the scaling idea previously applied to kaon/pion ratios by Wang (2002) and to minimum-bias strangeness suppression by Castorina (2014), now applied to multiplicity-binned data from the ALICE collaboration. The results support the applicability of hydrodynamics to small systems at high initial temperatures, as suggested by holographic duality studies (Chesler, 2015), and contradict the expectation from thermal models of a limiting temperature. The scaling enables the use of energy density as a unifying parameter to describe strangeness production across all collision systems, raising questions about the underlying processes that produce this universality.
