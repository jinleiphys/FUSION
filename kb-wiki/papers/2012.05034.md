# Insights into $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ from dipion transitions from $Υ(10860)$

> The dipion transitions $Υ(10860)\toπ^+π^-Υ(nS)$ ($n=1,2,3$) are studied in the framework of a unitary and analytic coupled-channel formalism previously developed for analysing experimental data on the bottomoniumlike states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ [Phys. Rev. D 98, 074023 (2018)] and predicting the properties of their spin partners [Phys. Rev. D 99, 094013 (2019)]. In this work we use a relatively simple but realistic version of this approach, where the scattering and production amplitudes are constructed employing only short-ranged interactions between the open- and hidden-flavour channels consistent with the constraints from heavy quark spin symmetry, for an extended analysis of the experimental line shapes. In particular, the transitions from the $Υ(10860)$ to the final states $ππh_b(mP)$ ($m=1,2$) and $πB^{(*)}\bar B^* $ already studied before, are now augmented by the $Υ(10860)\toπ^+π^-Υ(nS)$ final states ($n=1,2,3$). This is achieved by employing dispersion theory to account for the final state interaction of the $ππ$ subsystem including its coupling to the $K\bar K$ channel. Fits to the two-dimensional Dalitz plots for the $π^+π^-Υ$ final states were performed. Two real subtraction constants are adjusted to achieve the best description of the Dalitz plot for each $Υ(nS)$ $(n=1,2,3)$ while all the parameters related to the properties of the $Z_b$'s are kept fixed from the previous study. A good overall description of the data for all $Υ(10860)\toπ^+π^-Υ(nS)$ channels achieved in this work provides additional strong support for the molecular interpretation of the $Z_b$ states.

## Key claim

The paper provides additional strong support for the molecular interpretation of the Zb(10610) and Zb(10650) states by demonstrating that a coupled-channel formalism with only short-ranged interactions, previously used to describe other decay channels, can also achieve a good overall description of the two-dimensional Dalitz plots for the Υ(10860)→π+π−Υ(nS) transitions (n=1,2,3) when dispersive treatment of the ππ final state interaction is included.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed unitary and analytic coupled-channel formalism (Phys. Rev. D 98, 074023 (2018)) by incorporating dispersion theory to account for the final state interaction of the ππ subsystem, including its coupling to the KK̅ channel. They performed maximum likelihood fits to the two-dimensional Dalitz plots for the π+π−Υ final states, adjusting only two real subtraction constants for each Υ(nS) channel while keeping all parameters related to the Zb properties fixed from the prior analysis of the ππhb(mP) and πB(*)B̅* channels.

## Key numbers

- The contact interaction parameters from the prior fit are Cd = −3.30 ± 0.11 GeV−2 and Cf = −0.06 ± 0.13 GeV−2, with a cutoff Λ set to 1 GeV.
- The inelastic coupling constants (absolute values) for the B B̅* channel are: |gΥ(1S)| = 0.30 ± 0.07 GeV−2, |gΥ(2S)| = 1.01 ± 0.20 GeV−2, |gΥ(3S)| = 1.28 ± 0.34 GeV−2, |ghb(1P)| = 3.29 ± 0.38 GeV−3, and |ghb(2P)| = 11.38 ± 1.46 GeV−3.
- The two Zb states are split by about 40 MeV, with the Zb(10610) and Zb(10650) both having JPC = 1+−.
- The ππ invariant mass in the Υ(10860)→ππΥ(1S) channel extends up to 1.4 GeV, well beyond the KK̅ threshold.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analysis of Wang et al. (2018) and Baru et al. (2019), which previously studied the Zb states through the ππhb(mP) and πB(*)B̅* channels but only used total branching fractions for the ππΥ(nS) channels. By now including a full dispersive treatment of the ππ final state interaction with coupling to KK̅, the authors demonstrate consistency with the molecular interpretation across all available decay modes. The approach enables future studies to incorporate one-pion exchange effects and to search for the predicted spin partner states (WbJ with J=0,1,2) in experimental data.
