# Screening properties of quark-gluon plasma obtained from distribution and correlation functions of the constituent quasiparticle model

> Based on the constituent quasiparticle model of quark-gluon plasma (QGP), the matrix elements of the density operator and the Wigner function in the color phase space are presented in form of color path integrals over Wiener and SU(3) group Haar measures. Monte Carlo calculations of quark and gluon momentum distributions and spatial pair distribution functions have been carried out for the strongly coupled QGP plasma in thermal equilibrium at zero baryon chemical potential. The Debye screening mass and the running coupling constant have been obtained from the spatial pair distribution function and are in agreement with the available lattice QCD data. At densities related to the average interparticle distance more than 0.4 fm the gluon bound states in the form of glueballs have been found. Comparison with the Maxwell Boltzmann distribution shows significant influence of interparticle interaction on high energy asymptotics of the momentum distribution functions, resulting in appearance of quantum tails. The new color pair correlation function has been introduced, and related new color screening mass has been discussed.

## Key claim

The Debye screening mass and running coupling constant extracted from spatial pair distribution functions in the constituent quasiparticle model agree with available lattice QCD data, but this agreement is conditional on the model's specific assumptions about quasiparticle masses and the use of a one-loop analytic coupling constant; gluon bound states (glueballs) appear only at densities where the average interparticle distance exceeds 0.4 fm.

## Method

The authors constructed color path integrals over Wiener and SU(3) group Haar measures to represent the density operator matrix elements and Wigner functions for a multi-component QGP consisting of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons with color-electric Coulomb interactions. Monte Carlo simulations in the grand canonical ensemble first determined average particle numbers at zero baryon chemical potential, followed by fixed-particle-number canonical ensemble calculations to obtain momentum distributions and spatial pair distribution functions. The Debye screening mass and running coupling constant were extracted by fitting the spatial pair distribution functions to a screened Coulomb potential form, while a new color pair correlation function was introduced to define a related color screening mass.

## Key numbers

- Gluon bound states (glueballs) appear at average interparticle distances \(r_s \ge 0.4\) fm, where \(r_s = \sqrt[3]{3/4\pi n}\) and \(n\) is the density of all quasiparticles
- The QCD transition temperature is of order 175 MeV
- The QCD scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 206\) MeV is used in the one-loop analytic coupling constant with \(N_f = 3\) flavors
- Quasiparticle masses are given by \(m_g^2 = \frac{1}{12}\left((2N_c+N_f)T^2 + \frac{3}{\pi^2}\sum_{q=u,d,s} \mu_q^2\right)g^2\) for gluons and \(m_q^2 = \frac{N_g}{16N_c}\left(T^2 + \frac{\mu_q^2}{\pi^2}\right)g^2\) for quarks, with \(N_c = 3\)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quasiparticle model previously developed by the same group (Filinov et al., 2014, 2015; Ebel et al., 2017) that reproduced the lattice equation of state near the critical temperature and at nonzero baryon chemical potential. The approach differs from classical treatments by Shuryak (2004, 2017) and Litim and Manuel (2002) by incorporating a quantum treatment of quasiparticles rather than a classical one. The results enable further investigation of QGP kinetic properties such as diffusion coefficient and shear viscosity, and provide a framework for studying screening properties at large quark chemical potentials and out of thermodynamic equilibrium where lattice QCD fails.
