# One-loop off-shell quark-gluon vertex in arbitrary gauge and dimensions: a streamlined approach through the second-order formalism of QCD

> The standard Feynman rules used for perturbative calculations in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) are derived from a Lagrangian that is first-order in derivatives. It includes a three-point quark-gluon vertex which obscures the precise disentangled manner in which spin and momentum are interchanged during these interactions. An unambiguous understanding of this interchange is insightful for efficiently extracting physically relevant information from various Green's functions. To separate the scalar and spin degrees of freedom and gain physical insight from the outset, we examine the quark-gluon vertex using the less commonly employed second-order formalism of QCD. We compute this off-shell vertex in arbitrary space-time dimensions and covariant gauges by using scalar integrals with shifted dimensions, which include higher powers of the propagators, within a combined first- and second-order formalism. This approach naturally identifies the transverse components of the quark-gluon vertex, even before evaluating the tensor Feynman integrals. We also compute the on-shell version of this vertex using exclusively the second-order formalism, facilitating a precise identification of spin and momentum interchange. Through analyzing the Pauli form factor at $k^2=0$ (where $k$ represents the momentum of the external gluon), we find that only a specific set of second-order Feynman diagrams are relevant for calculating the electromagnetic and chromomagnetic dipole moments. These diagrams represent quantum processes in which the spin of the incoming quark changes only once due to interactions with the virtual gluons that form the quark-gluon vertex. All other interactions involve only momentum interchange (scalar interactions). Our results are in complete agreement with those obtained from the first-order formalism.

## Key claim

The second-order formalism of QCD provides a streamlined approach to compute the one-loop quark-gluon vertex that naturally separates scalar and spin degrees of freedom, but the off-shell vertex requires a combined first- and second-order formalism because there are not enough pairs of propagators and first-order vertices to fully rewrite them in terms of scalar propagators and second-order vertices.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop off-shell quark-gluon vertex in arbitrary space-time dimensions and covariant gauges by combining the first- and second-order formalisms of QCD, rewriting all possible pairs of fermion propagators and first-order vertices using the identity that decomposes the product into a second-order contribution and a leftover first-order term. They employed tensor reduction identities in Schwinger parametrization to transform tensor Feynman integrals into scalar integrals with shifted dimensions and higher powers of propagators, then used dimensional reduction identities and integration by parts to reduce all scalar integrals to a set of master integrals. For the on-shell vertex, they used exclusively the second-order formalism in Feynman gauge.

## Key numbers

* The tree-level on-shell quark-gluon vertex in the second-order formalism is given by $\left[\Gamma^{\text{OS}}_{\text{tree}}\right]^{a\mu} = \dfrac{igT^a}{2m}\left[ (p+p^\prime)^\mu - \sigma^{\mu\alpha} k_\alpha \right]$, separating scalar and spin degrees of freedom.
* The one-loop anomalous magnetic dipole moment of the electron in QED at one-loop level is determined to be equal to ${\alpha}/{2\pi}$, where $\alpha={e^2}/{4\pi}$ is the fine-structure constant.
* The abelian contribution to the vertex function involves the color factor $\overline{C} = C_F - C_A/2$, where $C_F = \dfrac{N^2-1}{2N}$ and $C_A = N$ for $SU(N)$.
* The Pauli form factor was analyzed at $k^2=0$, where $k$ represents the momentum of the external gluon, to identify diagrams relevant for electromagnetic and chromomagnetic dipole moments.

## Context

This work extends the second-order formalism for QCD developed by Hostler and Morgan to one-loop calculations of the quark-gluon vertex, building on prior studies of the electromagnetic vertex in standard and reduced QED by the same authors (QEDVer, RQED). The approach provides a transparent separation of scalar and spin degrees of freedom that is obscured in the standard first-order formalism, and it confirms existing results in the literature suggesting that defining the chromomagnetic dipole moment in the limit $k^2 \rightarrow 0$ leads to infrared divergences. The method enables future calculations where only a specific set of second-order Feynman diagrams are relevant for computing electromagnetic and chromomagnetic dipole moments, representing quantum processes where the spin of the incoming quark changes only once due to interactions with virtual gluons.
