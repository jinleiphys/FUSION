# High-resolution spectroscopy of neutron-rich Br isotopes and signatures for a prolate-to-oblate shape transition at N=56

> The first systematic experimental study of the neutron-rich Br isotopes with two complementary state-of-the-art techniques is presented. These isotopes have been populated in the fission process at two different facilities, GANIL and ILL. New spectroscopic information has been obtained for odd-even $^{87-93}$Br isotopes and the experimental results have been compared with state-of-the-art Large-Scale Shell-Model and DNO Shell-Model calculations. As a result of such theoretical approaches, a transition from prolate ($^{87,89}$Br) to oblate ($^{91,93}$Br) shapes is obtained from the subtle balance between proton and neutron quadrupole deformations, as a clear signature of pseudo-SU3 quadrupole regime.

## Key claim
The paper claims a prolate-to-oblate shape transition in neutron-rich Br isotopes at N=56, based on Large-Scale Shell-Model and DNO Shell-Model calculations that reproduce the newly measured level schemes. The abstract's phrase "clear signature of pseudo-SU3 quadrupole regime" hedges that the transition is inferred from theoretical interpretation of the experimental data, not directly measured.

## Method
Excited states in odd-even 87-93Br were populated via fission reactions at two facilities: GANIL using a 238U beam on a 9Be target with the AGATA gamma-ray tracking array and VAMOS++ spectrometer for isotopic identification, and ILL using thermal neutron-induced fission of 235U with the FIPPS HPGe array. Gamma-gamma and gamma-gamma-gamma coincidence techniques were employed to identify transitions and build level schemes, with Doppler correction applied for the AGATA data. The experimental results were compared to Large-Scale Shell-Model (LSSM) and DNO Shell-Model calculations.

## Key numbers
- Gamma-ray energy resolution of 5 keV (FWHM) at 1.2 MeV achieved with AGATA-VAMOS++ at a fragment velocity of v/c ≈ 0.1
- Gamma-ray energy resolution of 2.4 keV (FWHM) at 1.4 MeV achieved with FIPPS at ILL
- 15 new transitions attributed to 87Br, with 4 placed in the level scheme; 12 new transitions attributed to 89Br, with 10 placed; 21 new transitions attributed to 91Br, with 13 placed
- Systematic error of 0.2 keV on gamma-ray energies from AGATA data, deduced from comparison of approximately 50 intense transitions with literature values
- Relative intensity uncertainties include a systematic 5% contribution from detector efficiency correction based on GEANT4 simulations

## Context
This work extends the spectroscopic knowledge of neutron-rich Br isotopes beyond previous studies of 87Br from beta-decay (Wisniewski 2019) and 235U neutron-induced fission (Nyako 2021), and provides the first spectroscopic information for 91Br. The results are compared to LSSM and DNO Shell-Model calculations, which previously described a prolate-to-oblate shape transition in neutron-rich Kr isotopes at N=58 using Symmetry Conserving Configuration Mixing calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction (Rodriguez 2014). The findings enable systematic studies of shape evolution in the A≈90 region and tests of pseudo-SU3 symmetry predictions for quadrupole deformation.
