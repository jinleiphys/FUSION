# Constraints on the momentum dependence of rho-omega mixing

> Within a broad class of models we show that the amplitude for rho^0-omega mixing must vanish at the transition from timelike to spacelike four momentum. Hence in such models the mixing is either zero everywhere or is necessarily momentum-dependent. This lends support to the conclusions of other studies of rho-omega mixing and calls into question standard assumptions about the role of rho-omega mixing in the theoretical understanding of charge-symmetry breaking in nuclear systems.

# Constraints on the momentum dependence of rho-omega mixing

## Key claim
Within a broad class of effective Lagrangian models where vector mesons couple locally to conserved currents satisfying QCD-like commutation relations and no explicit mass-mixing term appears in the bare Lagrangian, the rho-omega mixing amplitude must vanish at four-momentum transfer $q^2=0$, implying the mixing is either zero everywhere or necessarily momentum-dependent.

## Method
The authors prove, using general field-theoretic arguments, that the mixing tensor $C^{\mu\nu}(q)$ for rho-omega mixing is transverse due to current conservation and commutation relations. By comparing the dressed propagator derived from the Dyson equation with the spectral representation of the propagator (the Renard form), they show that the scalar mixing function $\Pi_{\rho\omega}(q^2)$ must satisfy $\Pi_{\rho\omega}(0)=0$, and similarly for the diagonal self-energies $\Pi_{\rho\rho}(0)$ and $\Pi_{\omega\omega}(0)$. The argument is extended to the two-channel case of coupled rho and omega mesons.

## Key numbers
- The on-shell strong-interaction rho-omega mixing amplitude is $-(5130 \pm 600)\ \mathrm{MeV}^2$ (from Hatsuda et al.), after subtracting an electromagnetic contribution of approximately $610\ \mathrm{MeV}^2$ from the measured value of $-(4520 \pm 600)\ \mathrm{MeV}^2$.
- The mixing amplitude $\Pi(q^2)$ must vanish at $q^2=0$ at least as fast as $q^2$, i.e., $\Pi(0)=0$.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (GHT) who first questioned the constant-mixing assumption and found a node near $q^2=0$ in a simple model. It supports qualitatively similar results from Piekarewicz and Williams (who used an $N\bar{N}$ loop with the proton-neutron mass difference and guaranteed local current conservation), several $q\bar{q}$ calculations, and QCD sum-rule approaches by Hatsuda et al. The paper provides a general proof that the node at $q^2=0$ is a rigorous consequence of current conservation in a broad class of effective theories, calling into question the standard assumption of a constant, non-zero rho-omega mixing amplitude in calculations of charge-symmetry breaking in nuclear systems. It enables future work to focus on alternate mechanisms for charge-symmetry violation in the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
