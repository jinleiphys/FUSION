# Two-neutron transfer in nuclei close to the dripline

> We investigate the two-neutron transfer modes induced by (t,p) reactions in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. The nuclear response to the pair transfer is calculated in the framework of continuum-Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (cQRPA). The cQRPA allows a consistent determination of the residual interaction and an exact treatment of the continuum coupling. The (t,p) cross sections are calculated within the DWBA approach and the form factors are evaluated by different methods : macroscopically, following the Bayman and Kallio method, and fully microscopically. The largest cross section corresponds to a high-lying collective mode built entirely upon continuum quasiparticle states.

## Key claim

The largest cross section for two-neutron transfer in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes corresponds to a high-lying collective mode built entirely upon continuum quasiparticle states, which is similar to the long-predicted giant pairing vibration mode; however, the absolute cross section for this mode requires better estimation, and the zero-range DWBA approximation used may underestimate magnitudes.

## Method

The nuclear response to pair transfer was calculated using continuum-Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (cQRPA), which treats the continuum exactly and derives the residual interaction from the same Skyrme SLy4 force used in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground state calculations. The (t,p) cross sections for the reaction 22O(t,p) at 15 MeV/A were calculated within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) using three different form factor methods: macroscopic (from optical potential variation), the Bayman and Kallio method (semi-microscopic with Woods-Saxon single-particle states), and fully microscopic (from cQRPA pair transition densities).

## Key numbers

- For 22O(t,p) at 15 MeV/A using the macroscopic form factor with continuum boundary conditions, total cross sections are: 0.052 mb for the state at 1.6 MeV, 0.122 mb for the state at 9.8 MeV, and 0.270 mb for the giant pairing vibration (gpv) mode around 18 MeV
- The cross section for the gpv mode is about 5 times larger than the cross section for the state at 1.6 MeV
- The continuum treatment affects all cross sections by 20% when comparing box versus continuum boundary conditions
- For the high-energy gpv mode around 18 MeV, the continuum effect on the global cross section is negligible, about 3%
- The residual interaction was renormalized by a factor of 0.80 to put the spurious center-of-mass mode at zero energy for all calculated isotopes
- The unperturbed Green's function used an averaging interval of 0.15 MeV for excitation energies below 11 MeV and 1.0 MeV above 11 MeV
- The Bayman and Kallio form factor used a Woods-Saxon potential with radius 1.25 fm and diffuseness 0.65 fm, with spectroscopic factor 1
- The zero-range approximation used D0 = 2.43 x 10^4 MeV^2 fm^3 for the (t,p) reaction

## Context

This work extends the cQRPA approach developed by Khan et al. (2002) to two-neutron transfer reactions, providing a consistent description of pair transfer, ground state properties, and continuum coupling in nuclei near the dripline. The study builds on earlier predictions of giant pairing vibrations by Broglia and Bes (1977) and Herzog et al. (1985), and on recent work by Fortunato et al. (2002) discussing the possibility of exciting this mode with exotic nuclei. The results enable future calculations using finite-range DWBA instead of the zero-range approximation to make more quantitative cross section predictions, and suggest that pair transfer reactions with loosely bound exotic nuclei may offer a better chance to detect the long-predicted giant pairing vibration mode.
