# Ab initio calculation of the 4He(e,e'd)d reaction

> The two-body knock-out reaction 4He(e,e'd)d is calculated at various momentum transfers. The full four-nucleon dynamics is taken into account microscopically both in the initial and the final states. As NN interaction the central MT-I/III potential is used. The calculation shows a strong reduction of the coincidence cross section due to the final state interaction. Nonetheless the theoretical results exhibit a considerable overestimation of the experimental cross section at lower momentum transfer. Comparisons with other, less complete, calculations suggest that consideration of a more realistic ground state might not be sufficient for a good agreement with experiment, rather a more realistic final state interaction could play an essential role.

## Key claim

The full four-nucleon dynamics in both initial and final states, calculated with the central MT-I/III potential, shows that final state interaction (FSI) causes a strong reduction of the coincidence cross section, but the theoretical results still considerably overestimate experimental data at lower momentum transfers; a more realistic final state interaction, rather than a more realistic ground state, is likely needed for agreement with experiment.

## Method

The longitudinal structure function for the 4He(e,e'd)d reaction was calculated using the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method, which avoids explicit treatment of the continuum final state by solving bound-state-like equations. The four-nucleon ground state and the LIT solutions were expanded over a basis of correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) with hyperradial functions, using the semi-realistic MT-I/III NN potential plus Coulomb interaction. The transition matrix elements were separated into a Born term and an FSI-dependent term, with the latter obtained by inverting the Lorentz integral transform of the function containing all eigenstates of the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Relative energy of the two final deuterons: epsilon_{d,d} = 35 MeV
- Four-momentum transfers considered: q_mu^2 = 1.75, 2.49, 3.36, and 4.79 fm^{-2}
- For the two lower q-values, FSI reduces the strength by about a factor of 5
- At q_mu^2 = 4.79 fm^{-2}, the PWIA overestimates the experimental cross section at theta_d ~ 15 degrees by about a factor of 2.6, while the full calculation underestimates it by about a factor of 1.7
- For the lowest q-value, the experimental cross section is overestimated by almost an order of magnitude
- Theoretical results show deviations up to about 50% in the range 3.3 fm^{-2} <= q_mu^2 <= 4.8 fm^{-2}

## Context

This work extends microscopic calculations of two-body knockout reactions beyond the two- and three-nucleon systems (e.g., Alt (year) and BoKr (year)) to the four-nucleon system 4He, which has binding energy per nucleon and density similar to heavier nuclei. The results are compared to the NIKHEF experiment (NIKHEF, year) and to earlier cluster-model calculations by LOTT1 (year), which used a harmonic oscillator ground state and a phenomenological central potential for FSI between the two outgoing deuterons. The findings suggest that future work should incorporate more realistic NN interactions, including tensor forces, in the final state to achieve agreement with experiment.
