# Gamow-Teller strength distributions at finite temperatures and electron capture in stellar environments

> We propose a new method to calculate stellar weak-interaction rates. It is based on the Thermo-Field-Dynamics formalism and allows the calculation of the weak-interaction response of nuclei at finite temperatures. The thermal evolution of the GT$_+$ distributions is presented for the sample nuclei $^{54, 56}$Fe and ~$^{76,78,80}$Ge. For Ge we also calculate the strength distribution of first-forbidden transitions. We show that thermal effects shift the GT$_+$ centroid to lower excitation energies and make possible negative- and low-energy transitions. In our model we demonstrate that the unblocking effect for GT$_+$ transitions in neutron-rich nuclei is sensitive to increasing temperature. The results are used to calculate electron capture rates and are compared to those obtained from the shell model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method based on Thermo-Field-Dynamics (TFD) to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions at finite temperatures, which does not rely on the Brink hypothesis (the assumption that GT strength distributions on excited states are the same as for the ground state, only shifted by excitation energy). The authors demonstrate that thermal effects shift the GT+ centroid to lower excitation energies and enable negative- and low-energy transitions, with the unblocking effect for GT+ transitions in neutron-rich nuclei being sensitive to increasing temperature.

## Method

The authors extended the proton-neutron quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) to finite temperatures using the Thermo-Field-Dynamics (TFD) formalism, which treats temperature effects through a doubled Hilbert space with tilde-conjugate operators. They applied this finite-temperature QRPA (TQRPA) to calculate GT+ strength distributions for ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Fe and ⁷⁶,⁷⁸,⁸⁰Ge, and also computed first-forbidden transition strengths for the Ge isotopes. The resulting strength distributions were used to calculate electron capture rates in stellar environments, which were compared to results from the shell-model diagonalization approach and the hybrid SMMC+RPA model.

## Key numbers

- Total GT+ strengths at zero temperature with bare operators: S+ = 6.6 for ⁵⁴Fe and S+ = 5.1 for ⁵⁶Fe
- Total GT- strengths at zero temperature with bare operators: S- = 12.7 for ⁵⁴Fe and S- = 17.0 for ⁵⁶Fe
- Zero-temperature thresholds: Q(⁵⁴Fe) = 1.21 MeV and Q(⁵⁶Fe) = 4.20 MeV
- Proton and neutron BCS energy gaps at T=0: Δp(n) = 1.52 (0.0) MeV for ⁵⁴Fe; Δp(n) = 1.57 (1.36) MeV for ⁵⁶Fe
- Effective axial coupling constant used: g*A = 0.74 gA (with gA = -1.25)
- Critical temperature for pairing: Tcr ≈ ½ Δτ(0)

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) approach, previously used for charge-exchange excitations at zero temperature by Kuz'min and collaborators (1984, 1985), to finite temperatures using TFD. It addresses limitations of prior methods: the shell-model diagonalization approach (Langanke et al., 2000, 2001) partially employs the Brink hypothesis for highly excited states, while the Shell-Model Monte-Carlo (SMMC) method (Koonin et al., 1997) cannot provide detailed strength distributions. The new TFD-based method enables calculations for heavier and more neutron-rich nuclei beyond the pf-shell, and can be extended to include higher phonon admixtures for more detailed strength distributions.
