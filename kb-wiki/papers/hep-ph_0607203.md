# Heavy Flavor Hadrons in Statistical Hadronization of Strangeness-rich QGP

> We study b, c quark hadronization from QGP. We obtain the yields of charm and bottom flavored hadrons within the statistical hadronization model. The important novel feature of this study is that we take into account the high strangeness and entropy content of QGP, conserving strangeness and entropy yields at hadronization.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in a fast hadronization of a strangeness-rich QGP, the yields of heavy flavor hadrons (charm and bottom) are significantly influenced by the chemical non-equilibrium of light and strange quarks, and that the relative yields of strange to non-strange heavy mesons (e.g., \(D_s/D\)) provide a direct measure of the strangeness occupancy factor \(\gamma_s/\gamma_q\) at hadronization, rather than being determined solely by temperature.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical hadronization model (SHM) to compute yields of charm and bottom hadrons, enforcing conservation of entropy, strangeness, and heavy flavor quark numbers across the QGP-to-hadron gas phase transition. They assume a fast, sudden hadronization where the QGP breaks up into free-streaming hadrons, and they treat the light quarks (\(u,d\)) as chemically equilibrated in the QGP (\(\gamma_q=1\)) while allowing the strange and heavy quark occupancy factors (\(\gamma_s, \gamma_c, \gamma_b\)) to be determined by the pre-existing yields from initial parton collisions and thermal production. The yields are evaluated using Boltzmann statistics with explicit sums over known hadron states (including excited states) for \(D\), \(D_s\), \(B\), \(B_s\), \(J/\Psi\), and \(\phi\) mesons, and the analysis covers a temperature range of 140–260 MeV.

## Key numbers

*   Assumed charm quark multiplicity at LHC: \(dN_c/dy = 10\) per unit rapidity.
*   Assumed bottom quark multiplicity at LHC: \(dN_b/dy = 1\) per unit rapidity.
*   Assumed fireball volume per unit rapidity at hadronization: \(dV/dy = 800\) fm\(^3\) at \(T = 200\) MeV.
*   Quark masses used in QGP phase: \(m_c = 1.2\) GeV, \(m_b = 4.2\) GeV.
*   Predicted value for the ratio \(J/\Psi \phi / D_s \overline{D_s}\) is approximately 0.35, and this ratio is nearly constant over a wide temperature range (140–280 MeV).
*   The effective number of degrees of freedom in QGP, \(g_{\rm eff}^Q\), varies between 22 and 26 for a 2-flavor system (\(s/S=0\)) and between 30 and 35.5 for a chemically equilibrated 2+1-flavor system (\(\gamma_s=1\)), over the temperature range 140–260 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model (SHM) by incorporating the high strangeness and entropy content of the QGP, building on prior studies of heavy flavor hadronization such as Schroedter (2000) and Becattini (2005). It contrasts with models that assume chemical equilibrium in the hadron phase (e.g., Andronic 2003) by enforcing conservation of strangeness and entropy across the phase transition, which leads to chemical non-equilibrium for light quarks. The results enable experimental tests of the hadronization mechanism at LHC, particularly through the ratio \(J/\Psi \phi / D_s \overline{D_s}\), which is proposed as a robust thermometer of the hadronization temperature, and through the \(D_s/D\) ratio as a probe of the strangeness occupancy factor.
