# Regular and chaotic dynamics in time-dependent relativistic mean-field theory

> Isoscalar and isovector monopole oscillations that correspond to giant resonances in spherical nuclei are described in the framework of time-dependent relativistic mean-field theory. Time-dependent and self-consistent calculations that reproduce experimental data on monopole resonances in 208Pb, show that the motion of the collective coordinate is regular for isoscalar oscillations, and that it becomes chaotic when initial conditions correspond to the isovector mode. Regular collective dynamics coexists with chaotic oscillations on the microscopic level. Time histories, Fourier spectra, state-space plots, Poincare sections, autocorrelation functions, and Lyapunov exponents are used to characterize the nonlinear system and to identify chaotic oscillations. Analogous considerations apply to higher multipolarities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in time-dependent relativistic mean-field theory, the collective coordinate for isoscalar monopole oscillations in spherical nuclei is regular, while for isovector monopole oscillations it becomes chaotic; however, this distinction is conditional on the fact that for isoscalar modes only one type of particle (protons or neutrons) is considered, whereas for isovector modes protons and neutrons move out of phase, and the authors note that the concept of chaos is not well-defined for quantum systems and their description is semi-classical.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent relativistic mean-field (TDRMF) calculations for monopole oscillations in spherical nuclei, specifically 208Pb and 16O. The ground state was first computed using the NL1 parameter set, then monopole oscillations were excited by scaling the radial coordinate of the Dirac spinors: for isoscalar oscillations, proton and neutron densities were both radially expanded by the same factor (a=0.2), while for isovector oscillations the proton density was initially compressed and the neutron density expanded by the same amount. The time evolution of the single-particle Dirac spinors was then propagated self-consistently in the time-dependent mean fields, and the collective dynamics were characterized using time histories, Fourier power spectra, state-space plots, Poincare sections, autocorrelation functions, and Lyapunov exponents.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the experimental isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) energy is 13.7 ± 0.3 MeV; the calculated isoscalar mode in the Fourier spectrum shows a single dominant peak at 11 MeV.
- For 208Pb, the experimental isovector GMR energy is 26 ± 3 MeV; the calculated Fourier spectrum for the isovector mode shows main peaks in the energy region 25-30 MeV.
- The numerical resolution in the frequency domain for the 208Pb calculation was ΔE ≈ 0.4 MeV, with numerical accuracy estimated at ≈ 0.5 MeV, based on a total evolution time T_final = 3000 fm/c.
- For 16O, the isoscalar Fourier spectrum shows two strong peaks at approximately 20 MeV excitation energy.
- For the 1s 1/2 neutron level in 16O, the largest Lyapunov exponent for isoscalar oscillations is consistent with zero, while for isovector oscillations it is positive and large.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of regular and chaotic dynamics in nuclear collective motion, particularly the findings of Blocki (1992, 1993) who analyzed a classical gas of particles in a hard-wall container, and Bauer (1994) who used a self-consistent model with separable forces to show that chaotic single-particle dynamics can coexist with regular collective motion for isoscalar modes. The present paper is the first to systematically compare isoscalar and isovector modes, showing that the coexistence of regular collective dynamics with chaotic microscopic motion holds only for isoscalar modes, while isovector modes produce chaotic collective behavior. The results enable future investigations of higher multipolarities, such as isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonances, which the authors note are in progress.
