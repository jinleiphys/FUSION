# Dynamical properties and secondary decay effects of projectile fragmentation in 107,124Sn + 120Sn collisions at 600 MeV/nucleon

> The formation of the projectile spectator and the fragmentation processes in 107,124Sn + 120Sn collisions at 600 MeV/nucleon are studied with the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. The minimum spanning tree algorithm and the ratio of parallel to transverse kinetic quantities are applied to identify the equilibrated projectile spectator during the dynamical evolution. The influence of secondary decay on fragmentation observables is investigated by performing calculations with and without the statistical code GEMINI. The validity of the theoretical approach is examined by comparing the calculated product yields and correlations with the experimental results of the ALADIN Collaboration for the studied reactions.

## Key claim

The IQMD model with a standard minimum-spanning-tree (MST) algorithm can reproduce the "rise and fall" of fragment production in projectile fragmentation at 600 MeV/nucleon, contrary to previous claims that such reproduction was not possible, provided that the phase-space density constraint (PSDC) method is applied to account for the Pauli principle.

## Method

The isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model was used to simulate 107,124Sn + 120Sn collisions at 600 MeV/nucleon. The minimum spanning tree algorithm with parameters R0 = 3.5 fm and P0 = 250 MeV/c, together with the ratio of parallel to transverse kinetic quantities (RE), was applied to identify the equilibrated projectile spectator during dynamical evolution. Secondary decay effects were investigated by comparing calculations performed with and without the statistical code GEMINI, using three different values of Estop between 2 and 4 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility of 200 MeV at saturation density (without momentum-dependent interactions) for symmetric nuclear matter was used in the IQMD description.
- The potential part of the symmetry energy has a value of 19.0 MeV at saturation density with density dependence modeled as a power law with exponent γi = 0.75.
- Equilibrated projectile spectators were identified with RE values in the range 0.9 < RE < 1.2.
- Equilibration times for the projectile spectator in 124Sn + 120Sn collisions at 600 MeV/nucleon were located in the interval 20 < t < 150 fm/c for impact parameters b = 0, 5, and 10 fm.
- The ALADIN forward spectrometer acceptance for projectile fragments increases with Z from about 90% for Z = 3 to values exceeding 95% for Z ≥ 6.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing puzzle identified by Begemann-Blaich et al. (1993), who concluded that QMD models could not reproduce fragment distributions in projectile fragmentation measured by the ALADIN Collaboration. Previous attempts to circumvent this problem used early fragment recognition algorithms such as SACA (Puri, 1996) or FRIGA (Lefevre, 2016). The present study shows that the MST method with PSDC can reproduce the ALADIN data for 107,124Sn + 120Sn reactions, enabling future investigations of the nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy using standard MST-based fragment recognition.
