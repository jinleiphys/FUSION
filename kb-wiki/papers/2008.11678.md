# Sensitivity of the Polyakov loop and related observables to chiral symmetry restoration

> While the Polyakov loop is an order parameter of the deconfinement transition in the heavy quark mass regime of QCD, its sensitivity to the deconfinement of light, dynamical quarks in QCD is not apparent. On the other hand, the quark mass dependence of the Polyakov loop is sensitive to the appearance of a chiral phase transition. Using lattice QCD calculations in the staggered fermion discretization scheme at finite values of the lattice spacing, $aT = 1/8$, we show here, for the first time, that the Polyakov loop expectation value, and the heavy quark free energy extracted from it, behave like energy-like observables in the vicinity of the chiral phase transition temperature $T_c$. Consistent with scaling behavior of energy-like observables in the 3-$d$, O(2) universality class, the quark mass derivatives diverge in the chiral limit at $T_c$ while the temperature derivatives stay finite. The latter will develop a characteristic spike at $T_c$. This, however, may be resolved only in calculations with quark masses being two orders of magnitude smaller than those currently accessible in lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The Polyakov loop and the heavy quark free energy behave as energy-like observables in the vicinity of the chiral phase transition, meaning their quark mass derivatives diverge at the chiral transition temperature in the chiral limit while their temperature derivatives stay finite; however, the resulting narrow spike in the temperature derivatives may only be resolved with quark masses two orders of magnitude smaller than those currently accessible in lattice QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=8 (lattice spacing aT=1/8) for (2+1)-flavor QCD, with the strange quark mass fixed to its physical value and light quark masses varied over the range H=ml/ms=1/20 to 1/160 (corresponding to pion masses from 160 MeV down to 58 MeV). They analyzed the temperature and quark mass dependence of the renormalized Polyakov loop expectation value and the heavy quark free energy Fq=-T ln⟨P⟩, fitting the quark mass derivative of Fq/T to a scaling ansatz based on the 3-d, O(2) universality class, then using the resulting parameters to describe the full temperature and mass dependence of both observables.

## Key numbers

- Light quark mass range: H = ml/ms from 1/20 to 1/160, corresponding to pion masses 160 MeV ≳ mπ ≳ 58 MeV
- Lattice size: 32³×8 (H=1/20, 1/27), 40³×8 (H=1/40), and 56³×8 (H=1/80, 1/160)
- Chiral phase transition temperature on Nτ=8 lattices: Tc = 144(2) MeV (from Ding 2019)
- Fit results for Hmax=1/27: Tc = 145.6(3) MeV, A = 2.48(2), z0 = 2.24(5), a^r_{0,0} = 2.74(1), a^r_{1,0} = -34.4(7), R⁺ = -0.92(1)
- Fit results for Hmax=1/40: Tc = 144.2(6) MeV, A = 2.26(5), z0 = 1.83(9), a^r_{0,0} = 2.81(3), a^r_{1,0} = -27(1), R⁺ = -0.86(1)
- O(2) critical exponents used: β = 0.349, δ = 4.780, α = 2 - β(1+δ)
- Universal ratio R⁺/R⁻ = c⁺₀/c⁻₀ = 1.12(5)
- At |t| = 0.01, the slope of -Fq/T is about a factor 5 smaller than at Tc

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase transition by the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2011, 2014, 2017; Ding 2019) that determined pseudo-critical temperatures and the chiral phase transition temperature, and it provides the first demonstration that the Polyakov loop and heavy quark free energy behave as energy-like observables sensitive to chiral symmetry restoration. The results are consistent with scaling behavior in the 3-d, O(2) universality class and show qualitative similarity to the specific heat in O(2) spin models (Cucchieri 2002). The analysis enables future continuum-extrapolated studies to determine whether the spike in temperature derivatives becomes broader and more prominent in the O(4) universality class expected in the continuum limit, and it motivates effective model calculations to test this behavior.
