# Quark mass and isospin dependence of the deconfining critical temperature

> We describe the deconfining critical temperature dependence on the pion mass and on the isospin chemical potential in remarkably good agreement with lattice data. Our framework incorporates explicit dependence on quark masses, isospin and baryonic chemical potentials for the case of two flavors through ingredients from well-known high- and low-energy theories. In the low-energy sector, the system is described by a minimal chiral perturbation theory effective action, corresponding to a hot gas of pion quasiparticles and heavy nucleons. For the high-temperature sector we adopt a simple extension of the fuzzy bag model. We also briefly discuss the effects of mass asymmetry and baryon chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple effective framework, combining chiral perturbation theory for the low-energy sector with a fuzzy bag model for the high-energy sector, can simultaneously describe the dependence of the deconfining critical temperature on both the pion mass and the isospin chemical potential in remarkably good agreement with lattice data, without any fitting of mass or isospin chemical potential dependence. The hedge is that the framework produces a first-order transition, while recent lattice calculations indicate a crossover, and the absolute value of the critical temperature is 5-10% different from lattice values, which is acceptable for an effective field theory approach.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective theory combining chiral perturbation theory (χPT) for the low-energy sector, describing a hot gas of pion quasiparticles and heavy nucleons, with a simple extension of the fuzzy bag model for the high-temperature sector, which incorporates non-perturbative confinement effects. The low-energy effective action included dressed pion masses and nucleon masses fixed by vacuum QCD properties and lattice data, while the high-energy sector used a perturbative QCD pressure with fuzzy bag coefficients extracted from lattice fits to the trace anomaly. The critical temperature was extracted by maximizing the total pressure from the two sectors.

## Key numbers

- The fuzzy bag coefficients extracted from lattice fits were: B_fuzzy = 0.05 GeV² and B_MIT = 0.006 GeV⁴.
- The nucleon mass in the chiral limit was M₀ = (0.882 ± 0.003) GeV.
- The pion decay constant was f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- The chiral condensate (1-flavor) in the chiral limit was ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(225 MeV)³.
- The critical temperature results were compared to lattice data for pion masses up to m_π ≈ 1 GeV, where the χPT expansion parameter α ≈ 0.45 gives an estimated error of ~30% in effective masses.
- The critical temperature as a function of isospin chemical potential was shown for μ_I up to about 2 T_c, with the treatment valid for μ_I larger but not much larger than the pion mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram by addressing the simultaneous dependence of the deconfining critical temperature on quark masses and isospin chemical potential, which earlier chiral model analyses (Berges 1997, Dumitru 2003, Braun 2005) failed to reproduce for the mass dependence. Polyakov-loop models (Dumitru 2003) could fit the mass dependence but could not address isospin effects. The framework enables predictions for regimes with quark-mass asymmetry and finite baryon chemical potential, where lattice simulations face the sign problem, and opens the way for comparisons with future Monte Carlo studies and experimental data from RHIC-BES and FAIR-GSI.
