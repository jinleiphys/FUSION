# Photo Reactions with Universal Trimers

> Considering one-body and two-body currents, we study the photoassociation and photodissociation of universal bosonic trimers. Analyzing the relative importance of these currents we identify two physical scenarios (i) Normal hierarchy, where naive power counting holds and the one-body current dominates, and (ii) Strong hierarchy, where the one-body current is suppressed. For both scenarios we observe that at the high frequency tail, the response function exhibits log periodic oscillations in transition to or from any continuum state regardless of the reaction partial wave channel. In contrast, near threshold log periodic oscillations appear only in the leading $s$-wave components. These oscillations are the fingerprints of universal Efimov physics. We discuss the relevance of this effect to contemporary experiments in ultracold atoms.

## Key claim

The paper shows that log periodic oscillations, the fingerprints of universal Efimov physics, appear in the photo response function of universal bosonic trimers in two distinct scenarios: a "normal hierarchy" where one-body currents dominate, and a "strong hierarchy" where one-body currents are suppressed and two-body currents dominate; the oscillations appear in all partial waves at high frequency, but only in the leading s-wave component near threshold.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory to construct one-body and two-body magnetization currents for neutral bosons, then employed the zero-range approximation and adiabatic hyperspherical expansion to compute transition matrix elements between bound Efimov trimer states and continuum states in the unitary limit (scattering length a → ∞). They evaluated the hyperradial integrals analytically in terms of hypergeometric functions, deriving asymptotic expressions for the response function both near threshold (q ≪ κ) and in the high-energy tail (q ≫ κ), and applied these results to compute photoassociation rates in a thermal gas.

## Key numbers

- The Efimov parameter s₀ ≈ 1.00624 appears in the log periodic oscillations of the response function.
- The discrete Efimov spectrum follows εₙ/ε₀ = e^{-2πn/s₀} ≈ 515^{-n}.
- For the r² operator near threshold, the normalized amplitude of oscillations B₂ ≈ 8.475%.
- The constant B₁ ≈ 5.6745 and phase φ ≈ 0.78851 appear in the asymptotic expressions for the transition matrix elements.
- The paper presents photoassociation rates for gas temperatures k_BT = 1.5 E_B, k_BT = E_B, k_BT = 0.5 E_B, and k_BT = 0.1 E_B.

## Context

This work extends the prediction of log periodic oscillations in spin-flip rf reactions by Braaten et al. (2011) to frozen-spin photoreactions of universal trimers, showing that such oscillations appear in all partial waves at high frequency. The results are relevant to contemporary ultracold atom experiments, particularly frozen-spin reactions which fall into the strong hierarchy category, and suggest that the oscillations should be observable at temperatures comparable to the trimer binding energy without fine tuning.
