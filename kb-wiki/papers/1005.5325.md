# Systematic study of the system size dependence of global stopping: Role of momentum dependent interactions and symmetry energy

> Using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamical (IQMD) model, we systematically study the role of momentum dependent interactions in global stopping and analyze the effect of symmetry energy in the presence of momentum dependent interactions. For this, we simulate the reactions by varying the total mass of the system from 80 to 394 at different beam energies from 30 to 1000 MeV/nucleon over central and semi-central geometries. The study is carried in the presence of momentum dependent interactions and symmetry energy by taking into account hard equation of state. The nuclear stopping is found to be sensitive towards the momentum dependent interactions and symmetry energy at low incident energies. The momentum dependent interactions are found to weaken the finite size effects in nuclear stopping.

## Key claim
The paper claims that momentum dependent interactions (MDI) weaken finite-size effects in nuclear stopping, and that the sensitivity of global stopping to symmetry energy is enhanced in the presence of MDI, but only at low incident energies (below approximately 50 MeV/nucleon); at higher energies both effects wash out.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate symmetric heavy-ion collisions for five systems (total mass from 80 to 394) at beam energies from 30 to 1000 MeV/nucleon, at both central and semi-peripheral geometries. They employed a hard equation of state with and without momentum dependent interactions, and with symmetry energy values of 0 and 32 MeV, then quantified global stopping via the anisotropy ratio R and rapidity distributions of free nucleons.

## Key numbers
- Systems simulated: $_{20}Ca^{40} + _{20}Ca^{40}$, $_{28}Ni^{58} + _{28}Ni^{58}$, $_{41}Nb^{93} + _{41}Nb^{93}$, $_{54}Xe^{131} + _{54}Xe^{131}$, and $_{79}Au^{197} + _{79}Au^{197}$ (total mass A_tot from 80 to 394).
- Beam energies: 30, 50, 400, and 1000 MeV/nucleon.
- Symmetry energy values used: 0 and 32 MeV.
- For the $_{79}Au^{197} + _{79}Au^{197}$ reaction, the beam energy above which the anisotropy ratio R starts decreasing from unity (indicating partial transparency) is close to 400 MeV/nucleon.
- At 1000 MeV/nucleon and semi-peripheral geometry, stopping is almost independent of composite mass, with approximately 50% decrease in nuclear stopping compared to central geometry.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of nuclear stopping via rapidity distributions (Khoa 1992) and momentum asymmetry (Bauer and Bertsch 1988) by systematically including isospin degree of freedom via symmetry energy in the presence of momentum dependent interactions. It builds on the finding by Reisdorf (2004) that maximum stopping occurs around 400 MeV/nucleon, and confirms that momentum dependent interactions suppress binary collisions, consistent with earlier work by Puri (1994) and Khoa (1992) on sub-threshold particle production and collective flow disappearance. The results enable future studies of isospin equilibration and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions at radioactive beam facilities such as FAIR/GSI, RIKEN, and FRIB.
