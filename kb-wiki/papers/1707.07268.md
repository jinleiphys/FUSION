# $^1S_0$ pairing in neutron matter

> We report calculations of the superfluid pairing gap in neutron matter for the $^1S_0$ components of the Reid soft-core $V_6$ and the Argonne $V_{4}'$ two-nucleon interactions. Ground-state calculations have been carried out using the central part of the operator-basis representation of these interactions to determine optimal Jastrow-Feenberg correlations and corresponding effective pairing interactions within the correlated-basis formalism (CBF), the required matrix elements in the correlated basis being evaluated by Fermi hypernetted-chain techniques. Different implementations of the Fermi-Hypernetted Chain Euler-Lagrange method (FHNC-EL) agree at the percent level up to nuclear matter saturation density. For the assumed interactions, which are realistic within the low density range involved in $^1S_0$ neutron pairing, we did not find a dimerization instability arising from divergence of the in-medium scattering length, as was reported recently for simple square-well and Lennard-Jones potential models (Phys. Rev. A {\bf 92}, 023640 (2015)).

## Key claim

The authors report that for realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (Reid soft-core \(V_6\) and Argonne \(V_4'\)), they did not find a dimerization instability arising from divergence of the in-medium scattering length, as was recently reported for simple square-well and Lennard-Jones potential models (Phys. Rev. A 92, 023640 (2015)), within the low-density range relevant to \(^1S_0\) neutron pairing.

## Method

The authors performed ground-state calculations for neutron matter using the central parts of the Reid soft-core \(V_6\) and Argonne \(V_4'\) two-nucleon interactions, determining optimal Jastrow-Feenberg correlations via the Fermi-Hypernetted Chain Euler-Lagrange (FHNC-EL) method. They then used these correlations to construct effective pairing interactions within the correlated-basis formalism (CBF) and solved the resulting BCS gap equation for the \(^1S_0\) superfluid pairing gap. Matrix elements in the correlated basis were evaluated using Fermi hypernetted-chain techniques.

## Key numbers

* Different implementations of the FHNC-EL method agree at the percent level up to nuclear matter saturation density.
* The peak of the \(^1S_0\) pairing gap in neutron matter occurs at a Fermi momentum somewhat less than half of \(1.7~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to a density about an order of magnitude below nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0\).
* The neutron-neutron \(S\)-wave scattering length is \(a_0 \approx -18.6~\mathrm{fm}\), implying the diluteness condition \(|a_0|k_F \ll 1\) requires \(k_F \ll 0.05~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), over three orders of magnitude lower in density than the physically relevant neutron-star environment.

## Context

This work extends the correlated BCS (CBCS) approach using Euler-Lagrange FHNC optimization, which provides truly optimal Jastrow-Feenberg correlations without resorting to simple parametrizations, in contrast to earlier work by Pavlou (2017) where different analytic correlation functions produced gap peak values differing by almost a factor of two (1.8 MeV versus 3.3 MeV). The results enable more reliable predictions for \(^1S_0\) pairing in neutron-star crusts and demonstrate that the FHNC-EL method achieves accuracy within better than one percent at low densities, as previously shown for liquid \(^3\)He (Polish, 1990s).
