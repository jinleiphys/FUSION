# Extrapolation of mean-field models to superheavy nuclei

> The extrapolation of self-consistent nuclear mean-field models to the region of superheavy elements is discussed with emphasis on the extrapolating power of the models. The predictions of modern mean-field models are confronted with recent experimental data. It is shown that a final conclusion about the location of the expected island of spherical doubly-magic superheavy nuclei cannot be drawn on the basis of the available data.

# Extrapolation of mean-field models to superheavy nuclei

## Key claim

A final conclusion about the location of the expected island of spherical doubly-magic superheavy nuclei cannot be drawn on the basis of available data, because none of the current mean-field models is able to describe all available data and predictions for spherical shell closures and binding energy systematics are fairly independent of each other.

## Method

The paper compares predictions from self-consistent (SC) mean-field models, both non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, with macroscopic-microscopic (MM) models such as FRDM+FY and YPE+WS. The author analyzes binding energy systematics, single-particle spectra, shell corrections, and Q_alpha values for superheavy nuclei, confronting calculated results with experimental data from recent synthesis experiments at GSI, JINR Dubna, and Berkeley.

## Key numbers

- The shell correction E_shell fluctuates in the range -15 MeV ≤ E_shell ≤ +15 MeV for superheavy nuclei, while the macroscopic energy is of order -2000 MeV.
- For SC models, the absolute error in binding energy can reach up to 5.5 MeV in 208Pb, corresponding to 0.35% relative error.
- Nuclear matter volume coefficients a_vol range from -15.8 MeV (SkM*) to -16.2 MeV (NL-Z, NL3); asymmetry coefficients a_sym range from 29.5 MeV (SkI4) to 41.7 MeV (NL-Z); surface coefficients a_surf range from 17.3 MeV (SkI4) to 18.5 MeV (NL3).
- Empirical values for volume coefficients are a_vol = -16.0 ± 0.2 MeV and a_sym = 32.5 ± 0.5 MeV.
- For the decay chain of 277112, NL-Z2 agrees with data for Z > 104; for the even-even 292116 decay chain, data agree with SkI4, SLy6, and NL3 despite these predicting different spherical magic numbers (Z=114, Z=126, and Z=120 respectively).
- Odd-N nuclei Q_alpha values may differ from even-even systematics by about 500 keV due to blocking effects.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions by Meldner (1960s) and Nilsson that the next spherical doubly-magic nucleus would be 298114, and confronts modern mean-field models with recent experimental data from GSI (Z=110-112) and JINR Dubna (283112, 287-289114, 292116) and Berkeley (293118). The paper shows that while refined MM models confirm the older prediction of 298114, nearly all SC models shift the doubly-magic nucleus to higher charge numbers (either 292120 or 310126). The findings enable future work to improve isovector degrees of freedom in RMF models and to incorporate correlations beyond the mean-field level, which are needed to resolve the conflicting predictions among models.
