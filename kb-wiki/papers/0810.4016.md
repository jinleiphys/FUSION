# Separation of Equilibration Time-Scales in the Gradient Expansion

> We study thermalization by applying gradient expansion to the Kadanoff-Baym equations of the 2PI effective action to two-loop in a theory with Dirac fermions coupled to scalars. In addition to those chemical potentials which equilibrate in the on-shell limit, we identify modes which are conserved in this approximation, but which relax when off-shell effects are taken into account. This implies that chemical equilibration does not require higher loop contributions to the effective action and is compatible with the gradient expansion. We explicitly calculate the damping time-scales of both, on- and off-shell, chemical equilibration rates. It is shown that off-shell equilibration is suppressed by the thermal width of the particles in the plasma, which explains the separation of on- and off-shell chemical equilibration time-scales.

## Key claim

Chemical equilibration in a Yukawa theory does not require higher-loop contributions to the 2PI effective action; off-shell effects within the two-loop gradient expansion are sufficient to relax the chemical potentials that are spuriously conserved in the on-shell (Boltzmann) approximation, and the separation of time-scales between on-shell and off-shell equilibration is controlled by the thermal width of the particles in the plasma.

## Method

The authors apply a gradient expansion to the Kadanoff-Baym equations derived from the two-loop 2PI effective action for a Dirac fermion coupled to a complex scalar via a Yukawa coupling. They linearize the kinetic equations around equilibrium and compute the collision integrals for both on-shell processes (where all three particles satisfy the on-shell condition) and off-shell processes (where one particle is off-shell, with its Breit-Wigner width providing the finite width). The relaxation rates for the chemical potentials are obtained by evaluating the thermally averaged matrix elements in the linear-response regime.

## Key numbers

* On-shell chemical equilibration rate for the combination μ_S + μ_R − μ_L: γ^Q = (6/T³)(k_S⁻¹ + 2k_{L,R}⁻¹) C^on, where C^on is given by eq. (C:on) and scales as y² M^(5/2) T^(3/2) e^(−M/T) for M/T ≫ 1
* For large scalar mass M/T ≫ 1, the on-shell rate asymptotes to γ^Q ∼ M/(16π), independent of the coupling y
* Off-shell equilibration rate for total particle number modes: γ^{Q̄}_1 ≈ (6/T³) k_{L,R}⁻¹ C^off and γ^{Q̄}_2 ≈ (6/T³) k_S⁻¹ C^off, where C^off_φ ∼ 0.126 y⁴ T⁸/M⁴ for M/T ≫ 1
* The off-shell rate is proportional to y⁴, while the on-shell rate is proportional to y², leading to a parametric separation τ_on ≪ τ_off for small coupling

## Context

This work extends the numerical studies of chemical equilibration in scalar λφ⁴ theory by Juchem (2003, 2004) and Berges (2005) to a Yukawa model with Dirac fermions and scalars, and provides analytic expressions for the relaxation rates that were previously only accessible through numerical solutions of the Kadanoff-Baym equations. The authors explicitly address the spuriously conserved charges identified in the Boltzmann-equation treatment of Lindner (2007) and show that off-shell effects within the gradient expansion resolve this issue. The results enable analytic estimates of chemical equilibration time-scales for applications to leptogenesis and electroweak baryogenesis, as discussed by Chung (2008).
