# Stronger $C$-odd color charge correlations in the proton at higher energy

> The non-forward eikonal scattering matrix for dipole-proton scattering at high energy obtains an imaginary part due to a $C$-odd three gluon exchange. We present numerical estimates for the perturbative Odderon amplitude as a function of dipole size, impact parameter, their relative azimuthal angle, and light-cone momentum cutoff $x$. The proton is approximated as $ψ_\mathrm{qqq}|qqq\rangle + ψ_\mathrm{qqqg}|qqqg\rangle$, where $ψ_\mathrm{qqq}$ is a non-perturbative three quark model wave function while the gluon emission is computed in light-cone perturbation theory. We find that the Odderon amplitude increases as $x$ decreases from 0.1 to 0.01. At yet lower $x$, the reversal of this energy dependence would reflect the onset of universal small-$x$ renormalization group evolution.

## Key claim

The Odderon amplitude increases as the light-cone momentum cutoff x decreases from 0.1 to 0.01, but this energy dependence reverses at yet lower x, reflecting the onset of universal small-x renormalization group evolution; however, after correcting symmetry factors, the NLO correction reduces the amplitude at small impact parameter b compared to the original publication, while a stronger Odderon with decreasing x is obtained at larger impact parameters.

## Method

The authors compute the C-odd three-gluon exchange (Odderon) amplitude for dipole-proton scattering in coordinate space using a proton wave function that includes both a non-perturbative three-quark Fock state (from the harmonic oscillator model of Brodsky (1994)) and a perturbative gluon emission computed in light-cone perturbation theory. The Odderon amplitude is evaluated numerically via Monte Carlo integration of the three-gluon correlator, with the angular dependence parametrized as O(r,b) = a1(r,b) cos(theta) + a3(r,b) cos(3theta). The calculation uses alpha_s = 0.2, a collinear regulator m_col = 0.2 GeV, and a low momentum cutoff q_min = 0.03 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At r = b = 0.3 fm, the fitted coefficient a1 (scaled by 100) is -0.063 at x = 0.1, -0.10 at x = 0.03, and -0.16 at x = 0.01; the leading-order value is -0.040
- The a3 coefficient at r = b = 0.3 fm is 0.0035 at x = 0.1, 0.0030 at x = 0.03, and 0.0063 at x = 0.01; the leading-order value is 0.0040
- The Odderon amplitude peaks at impact parameter b slightly less than 0.2 fm for dipole size r = 0.3 fm
- The maximal Odderon amplitude at x >= 0.01 is about 5 x 10^(-3) for alpha_s = 0.2
- Using m_col = 0.3 GeV instead of 0.2 GeV results in 5% larger scattering amplitude at small r and 20% larger at large r
- The Odderon amplitude levels off at about r = 0.7 fm for small impact parameter

## Context

This work extends the perturbative Odderon calculations of Dumitru (2021) by providing corrected numerical results after fixing symmetry factors and a missing sign, and presents the first numerical estimates for the dipole-proton Odderon amplitude including a perturbative gluon Fock state. The results provide initial conditions for small-x QCD evolution of the hard Odderon as formulated by Kovchegov (2003), Hatta (2005), and Lappi (2016), and are relevant for Odderon searches at the Electron-Ion Collider. The obtained Odderon amplitude is about one order of magnitude larger than the quasi-classical result for a proton with Gaussian transverse profile from Kowalski (2006), but significantly smaller than the parameterizations commonly used as initial conditions for small-x evolution in Lappi (2016).
