# Measuring radial flow of partonic and hadronic phases in relativistic heavy ion collision

> It has been shown that the thermal photon and the lepton pair spectra can be used to estimate the radial velocity of different phases of the matter formed in nuclear collisions at ultra-relativistic energies. We observe a non-monotonic variation of the flow velocity with invariant mass of the lepton pair which is indicative of two different sources of thermal dilepton sources at early and late stage of the dynamically evolving system. We also show that the study of radial velocity through electromagnetic probes may shed light on the nature of the phase transition from hadrons to QGP.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the ratio of thermal photon to dilepton transverse momentum spectra can be used to extract the radial flow velocity of different phases of the matter formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and that this radial velocity varies non-monotonically with the invariant mass of the lepton pair, indicating two different thermal sources at early and late stages of the evolving system.

## Method

The authors use ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry to model the space-time evolution of the hot fireball. They compute thermal photon and dilepton production rates from both the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase and the hadronic phase, using hard thermal loop approximations for QGP and hadronic reactions with vector meson decays for the hadronic phase. The ratio of the photon to dilepton spectra is parametrized as a function of transverse momentum, and the slope parameters are related to the average temperature and radial flow velocity through a set of equations that are solved to extract the radial velocity for different invariant mass windows.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature for RHIC: \(T_i = 400\) MeV, with initial thermalization time \(\tau_i = 0.2\) fm/c and freeze-out temperature \(T_F = 120\) MeV.
- Initial temperature for LHC: \(T_i = 700\) MeV, with \(\tau_i = 0.08\) fm/c, giving hadron multiplicity \(dN/dy = 2100\).
- Transition temperature \(T_c = 192\) MeV, taken from lattice QCD calculations (Cheng et al.).
- For the hadronic phase, the radial velocity is extracted using \(p_T \sim 0.5\) GeV and \(M \sim 0.75\) GeV; for the QGP phase, \(M \sim 1.2\) GeV and \(p_T = 0.5\) GeV are used.
- The effective temperature \(T_{\mathrm{eff}}\) from the ratio increases with \(M\) up to the \(\rho\)-peak and then decreases beyond the \(\rho\) mass for RHIC; for LHC, no clear reduction beyond the \(\rho\)-peak is observed.
- The slope of the ratio is insensitive to changes in \(T_F\) from 0.120 GeV to 0.150 GeV and \(T_c\) from 0.192 GeV to 0.175 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of radial flow extraction from hadronic spectra (Heinz, Hung, Hirano) to electromagnetic probes, following the NA60 collaboration's demonstration that dilepton transverse mass spectra can reveal flow information. The authors build on their own previous work (Nayak and Alam, 2009) where the ratio of photon to dilepton spectra was introduced as a tool to cancel model-dependent uncertainties. The non-monotonic variation of radial velocity with invariant mass is interpreted as evidence for two distinct thermal sources (early QGP and late hadronic phase), and the method enables future experimental studies to probe the nature of the phase transition from hadrons to QGP.
