# Leptogenesis and Dark Matter-Nucleon Scattering Cross Section in the SE6SSM

> The E6 inspired extension of the minimal supersymmetric (SUSY) standard model (MSSM) with an extra U(1)_N gauge symmetry, under which right-handed neutrinos have zero charge, involves exotic matter beyond the MSSM to ensure anomaly cancellation. We consider the variant of this extension (SE6SSM) in which the cold dark matter is composed of the lightest neutral exotic fermion and gravitino. The observed baryon asymmetry can be induced in this case via the decays of the lightest right-handed neutrino/sneutrino into exotic states even for relatively low reheating temperatures T_R < 10^{6-7} GeV. We argue that there are some regions of the SE6SSM parameter space, which are safe from all current constraints, and discuss the implications of this model for collider phenomenology.

## Key claim

The observed baryon asymmetry can be generated via decays of the lightest right-handed neutrino and sneutrino into exotic states (rather than ordinary leptons) even for reheating temperatures as low as \(T_R \lesssim 10^{6-7}\,\text{GeV}\), provided the lightest right-handed neutrino mass \(M_1 \lesssim 10^6\,\text{GeV}\) and the sparticle mass scale is below \(10\,\text{TeV}\); this avoids the gravitino overproduction problem that plagues thermal leptogenesis in the MSSM.

## Method

The paper studies the SE6SSM, a variant of the E6-inspired extension of the MSSM with an extra \(U(1)_N\) gauge symmetry and an exact \(\tilde{Z}_2^H\) custodial symmetry that forbids dangerous baryon- and lepton-number-violating operators and tree-level flavor-changing transitions. The cold dark matter is composed of the lightest neutral exotic fermion (from the Higgs-like doublets \(H^d_\alpha, H^u_\alpha\)) and the gravitino. The generation of lepton asymmetry is computed via the out-of-equilibrium decays of the lightest right-handed neutrino \(N_1\) and its scalar partner \(\widetilde{N}_1\) into the exotic lepton doublet \(L_4\) and the extra Higgs-like doublets \(H^u_\alpha\), using one-loop self-energy and vertex corrections to obtain the CP asymmetries \(\varepsilon^\alpha_{1,\ell_4}\) and related quantities. The resulting baryon asymmetry is estimated via an approximate formula \(Y_{\Delta B} \sim 10^{-3} \varepsilon \eta\), where \(\eta\) is an efficiency factor determined by the ratio of the Hubble rate to the decay width of \(N_1\).

## Key numbers

- Reheating temperature bound to avoid gravitino overproduction: \(T_R \lesssim 10^{6-7}\,\text{GeV}\) for gravitino mass \(m_{3/2} \simeq 1\,\text{GeV}\) and gluino mass \(M_{\tilde{g}} \gtrsim 3\,\text{TeV}\).
- Lightest right-handed neutrino mass required for thermal leptogenesis: \(M_1 \lesssim 10^6\,\text{GeV}\).
- Observed baryon asymmetry: \(Y_{\Delta B} = (8.75 \pm 0.23) \times 10^{-11}\).
- Number of relativistic degrees of freedom in the SE6SSM: \(g_* \approx 360\).
- Tree-level partial decay width for \(N_1 \to L_4 + H^u_\alpha\): \(\Gamma^\alpha_{N_1 \ell_4} + \Gamma^\alpha_{N_1 \bar{\ell}_4} = \frac{|h_{1\alpha}|^2}{8\pi} M_1\).
- CP asymmetry expression (for \(|h_{12}| = 0\)): \(\varepsilon = \frac{1}{8\pi} \left[ \sum_{j=2,3} |h_{j1}|^2 f\left(\frac{M_j^2}{M_1^2}\right) \sin 2\Delta\varphi_{j1} \right]\).
- Gravitino relic density scaling: \(\Omega_{3/2} h^2 \sim 0.27 \left(\frac{T_R}{10^8\,\text{GeV}}\right) \left(\frac{1\,\text{GeV}}{m_{3/2}}\right) \left(\frac{M_{\tilde{g}}}{1\,\text{TeV}}\right)^2\).
- Upper bound on gravitino relic density: \(\Omega_{3/2} h^2 \le 0.12\) (from Planck 2015).
- Lifetime constraint for decaying unstable R-parity odd state \(Y\): \(\tau_Y \lesssim 1\,\text{sec}\) for \(m_Y \simeq 1\,\text{TeV}\) and \(m_{3/2} \lesssim 1\,\text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of leptogenesis in the E6-inspired composite Higgs model (Nevzorov 2017, Nevzorov 2022) and in the E6SSM (Hambye 2000, Nevzorov 2018) by focusing on the SE6SSM variant with an exact \(\tilde{Z}_2^H\) symmetry. It addresses the gravitino problem that arises in supergravity models when the reheating temperature exceeds \(10^9\,\text{GeV}\) (as required for thermal leptogenesis in the MSSM), by showing that the SE6SSM can generate sufficient baryon asymmetry at much lower reheating temperatures via decays into exotic states. The work also connects to prior studies of dark matter in the E6SSM (Khalil 2020, Nevzorov 2022) and to the phenomenology of \(Z'\) bosons, Higgs bosons, and exotic matter at colliders. The results enable further exploration of parameter space regions that satisfy all current constraints from cosmology and collider experiments.
