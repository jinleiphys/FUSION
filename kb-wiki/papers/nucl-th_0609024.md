# Roles of isoscalar hyperons in probing the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy

> The role of the isoscalar hyperon Lambda in probing the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy is studied in multi-Lambda hypernuclei, hyperon-rich matter and neutron stars in relativistic models. Relationships between the properties of three types of objects and the neutron thickness in 208Pb are established with respect to the isoscalar-isovector coupling that modifies the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The exotic isotopes far from the neutron drip line can be stabilized by filling in considerable Lambda hyperons. The difference of the binding energy of multi-Lambda hypernuclei from different models is attributed to different symmetry energies. The isovector potential together with the neutron thickness in multi-Lambda hypernuclei investigated is very sensitive to the isoscalar-isovector coupling. The large sensitivity of the Lambda hyperon fraction to the isoscalar-isovector coupling occurs at about 2-3 rho_0 in beta equilibrated hyperon-rich matter. In neutron stars with hyperonization, an on-off effect with respect to the isoscalar-isovector coupling exists for the neutron star radius.

## Key claim

The isoscalar hyperon Lambda can serve as a probe of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, but the sensitivity of hypernuclear properties to the isoscalar-isovector coupling is conditional: it is large only in specific density regimes (around 2-3 times normal nuclear density) and for certain observables, while for neutron star radii with hyperonization an on-off effect exists rather than a continuous variation with the coupling strength.

## Method

The study uses relativistic mean field (RMF) models with the NL3 and S271 parameter sets, incorporating isoscalar-isovector coupling terms that modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Calculations are performed for three systems: multi-Lambda hypernuclei based on Ca isotopes far from the neutron drip line, beta-equilibrated hyperon-rich matter including baryons (N, Lambda, Xi) and leptons (e, mu), and neutron stars using the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The isoscalar-isovector coupling Lambda_v is varied while readjusting the rho-NN coupling constant to keep the average symmetry energy fixed at 25.7 MeV at k_F = 1.15 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers

- The central density in ^{102}Ca+50Lambda is about 1.43 rho_0 in NL3, compared to 1.06 rho_0 in ^{102}Ca alone
- The drip line of Ca isotope can be extended from neutron number 50 to 82 when about 50 Lambda hyperons are filled in
- The total binding energy for ^{102}Ca+50Lambda in NL3 is modified by up to 2 MeV with various Lambda_v values
- The uncertainty of the neutron thickness for ^{102}Ca+40Lambda and ^{102}Ca+50Lambda is about 3 times that for ^{208}Pb
- In NL3, hyperonization reduces the maximum neutron star mass by about 0.9 solar masses from about 2.8 solar masses; in S271, by 0.75 solar masses from about 2.4 solar masses
- The starting density for Lambda appearance is about 1.8 rho_0 in NL3 and 2.2 rho_0 in S271
- The large sensitivity of the Lambda hyperon fraction to the isoscalar-isovector coupling occurs at about 2-3 rho_0

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the density dependence of the symmetry energy through isoscalar-isovector couplings in RMF models by Horowitz and collaborators (2001) and Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005), which focused on neutron skins of heavy nuclei. It also builds on the work of Schaffner and collaborators (1993, 1994) on multi-Lambda hypernuclei and metastable exotic multihypernuclear objects (MEMOs), and on Glendenning's (1985, 1991) findings that hyperonization reduces the maximum mass of neutron stars. The results enable combined constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from measurements of the neutron radius in ^{208}Pb at Jefferson Laboratory, neutron star radii, and Lambda hyperon detection in heavy-ion collisions.
