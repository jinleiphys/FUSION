# Centrality dependence of high $p_T$ suppression in Au+Au collisions suggest quark matter formation

> In a pQCD-based model, we have analyzed the STAR data on the high $p_T$ suppression of charged hadrons, in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV. In the jet quenching or the energy loss picture, $p_T$ spectra of charged hadrons as well as the $p_T$ dependence of nuclear modification factor, in all the centrality ranges, are well explained, with nearly a constant relative energy loss, $ΔE/E=0.56\pm 0.03$. Centrality independence of relative energy loss indicate that the matter produced in central and in peripheral collisions are different, otherwise relative energy loss would have shown strong centrality dependence. Qualitatively, centrality independence of relative energy loss can be understood, if in central Au+Au collisions deconfined matter is produced and the matter remain confined in peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of high-$p_T$ suppression in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV can be explained by partonic energy loss, but after removing the geometric contribution due to increased path length in central collisions, the extracted energy loss indicates that the density of matter produced is only 1.5-2.0 times normal nuclear matter density, precluding the formation of high-density deconfined matter.

## Method

The author used a leading-order pQCD model with CTEQ5L parton distributions and KPP fragmentation functions to compute hadron production in pp collisions, then applied a Glauber model for nuclear geometry in Au+Au collisions. Jet quenching was implemented by replacing the fragmentation function variable $z$ with $z^* = z/(1-\Delta E/E)$, treating the average relative energy loss $<\Delta E/E>$ as a single free parameter fitted to the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ data from STAR (charged hadrons) and PHENIX (charged hadrons and neutral pions) across centrality bins using the CERN MINUIT minimization program. The geometric contribution to energy loss was estimated using a Glauber model calculation of the average number of collisions suffered by partons.

## Key numbers

- In most central collisions (0-5% centrality), the extracted relative energy loss is $<\Delta E/E> \sim 0.2$ (20%) for STAR data.
- In most peripheral collisions (60-80% centrality), STAR data are consistent with zero energy loss, while PHENIX data require finite energy loss of approximately 5%.
- The Glauber model calculation underpredicts the extracted energy loss in central collisions by a factor of 1.5-2.0, indicating the medium density is only 1.5-2.0 times normal nuclear matter density.
- For STAR data, the quadratic fit to energy loss versus path length gives $A = 6.24 \times 10^{-3} \text{ fm}^{-2}$; for PHENIX data, $A = 7.41 \times 10^{-3} \text{ fm}^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the jet quenching analysis of Wang and Wang (2002), who estimated gluon densities 15 times normal nuclear matter density, by explicitly separating geometric and dynamical contributions to energy loss. The author argues that the quadratic dependence of energy loss on in-medium path length in central collisions is characteristic of gluon radiation in a static medium, suggesting partons lose energy before hydrodynamic expansion sets in (time scale 0.2-1.0 fm). The analysis contradicts claims of high-density matter formation and opens the question of whether the observed suppression results from a combination of jet quenching and hadronic interactions in a medium of modest density.
