# Importance of repulsive interactions for the equation of state and other properties of strongly interacting matter

> We illustrate the role of repulsive interactions in a hadron-resonance gas at freeze-out and in a gas of quark-gluon bags. Taking into account non-zero size of particles in hadron gas leads to a significant decrease and shift of the net-baryon density maximum. The transition point from baryon to meson dominated matter depends on the difference between baryon and meson radii. We also show that depending on the properties of the quark-gluon bags one may obtain any type of the phase transition from hadron gas to quark-gluon plasma: the first or second order, as well as four types of the crossover.

# Importance of repulsive interactions for the equation of state and other properties of strongly interacting matter

## Key claim

Repulsive interactions, modeled via excluded volume effects in a hadron-resonance gas, significantly shift the net-baryon density maximum to lower collision energies and alter the baryon-to-meson transition point, but only if baryon and meson radii differ. Additionally, the type of phase transition from hadron gas to quark-gluon plasma (first order, second order, or one of four crossover types) depends entirely on the mass-volume spectrum of quark-gluon bags, not on the presence of the hadron-resonance gas itself.

## Method

The author calculated excluded volume effects along the chemical freeze-out line for central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions using data from SIS, AGS, SPS, and RHIC experiments, applying a Boltzmann approximation with fixed particle radii. For the quark-gluon bag model, the author extended the hadron-resonance gas by adding a spectrum of bags filled with non-interacting massless quarks and gluons, then analyzed the temperature dependence of the average bag size to classify possible transition types.

## Key numbers

- For particle radius r = 0.5 fm, the suppression factor for net-baryon density is about 0.75 at AGS energies and 0.5 at SPS energies.
- With proper radii of 0.5 fm and 1 fm, the net-baryon density maximum moves from 34 AGeV to 17 AGeV and 7 AGeV, respectively.
- For baryon radius r_B = 0.5 fm and meson radius r_M = 0, the baryon-to-meson transition point moves from 46 AGeV to 23 AGeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard hadron-resonance gas model of Becattini (2005) and Cleymans (2005) by incorporating repulsive interactions via the van der Waals excluded volume approach of Gorenstein (1981). The quark-gluon bag analysis builds on earlier phase transition studies by Ferroni and others, and summarizes and extends the classification of crossover types from Begun (2009). The results are directly relevant for planning compressed baryonic matter experiments at GSI and for interpreting heavy-ion collision data across the full energy range from SIS to LHC.
