# Jet radiation in a longitudinally expanding medium

> In a series of previous papers, we have presented a new approach, based on perturbative QCD, for the evolution of a jet in a dense quark-gluon plasma. In the original formulation, the plasma was assumed to be homogeneous and static. In this work, we extend our description and its Monte Carlo implementation to a plasma obeying Bjorken longitudinal expansion. Our key observation is that the factorisation between vacuum-like and medium-induced emissions, derived in the static case, still holds for an expanding medium, albeit with modified rates for medium-induced emissions and transverse momentum broadening, and with a modified phase-space for vacuum-like emissions. We highlight a scaling relation valid for the energy spectrum of medium-induced emissions, through which the case of an expanding medium is mapped onto an effective static medium. We find that scaling violations due to vacuum-like emissions and transverse momentum broadening are numerically small. Our new predictions for the nuclear modification factor for jets $R_{AA}$, the in-medium fragmentation functions, and substructure distributions are very similar to our previous estimates for a static medium, maintaining the overall good qualitative agreement with existing LHC measurements. In the case of $R_{AA}$, we find that the agreement with the data is significantly improved at large transverse momenta $p_T\gtrsim 500$ GeV after including the effects of the nuclear parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The factorisation between vacuum-like emissions and medium-induced emissions, previously established for a static quark-gluon plasma, remains valid for a plasma undergoing Bjorken longitudinal expansion, but only after modifying the rates for medium-induced emissions and transverse momentum broadening and adjusting the phase-space for vacuum-like emissions; a scaling relation maps the expanding-medium energy spectrum onto an effective static medium, though scaling violations from vacuum-like emissions and transverse momentum broadening are numerically small.

## Method

The authors extended their perturbative QCD-based Monte Carlo event generator, originally developed for a static homogeneous plasma, to a medium with Bjorken longitudinal expansion where the jet quenching parameter follows a power-law time dependence \(\hat q(t) = \hat q_0 (t_0/t)^\gamma\) with \(\gamma \le 1\). They derived the modified phase-space boundary for vacuum-like emissions and the time-dependent medium-induced emission rate from the BDMPSZ formalism, then implemented these in Markovian branching processes for both emission types. The scaling relation was tested by comparing parton distributions from the expanding medium to those from an equivalent static medium with \(\hat q_\text{stat} \simeq 4\hat q(L)\), and the full predictions for jet observables were compared to LHC data.

## Key numbers

* The jet quenching parameter for Bjorken expansion takes the form \(\hat q(t) = \hat q_0 (t_0/t)^\gamma\) with \(\gamma = 1\) for an ideal gas (sound velocity \(v_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\)) and \(\gamma\) close to but smaller than 1 in perturbative QCD.
* The initial time is estimated as \(t_0 \simeq 1/Q_s\) with \(Q_s = 1\) to 2 GeV for a Pb nucleus at \(x \sim 10^{-3}\), giving \(t_0 = 0.1\) to 0.2 fm and \(\hat q_0 = Q_s^3 = 5\) to 40 GeV\(^2\)/fm.
* The characteristic medium scale \(\omega_c = \hat q(L) L^2 / 2\) becomes \(\omega_c = Q_s^2 L / 2\) for \(\gamma = 1\), linearly increasing with \(L\) rather than quadratically as in the static case.
* The critical angle for colour decoherence is \(\theta_c = 2 / (Q_s L)\) for \(\gamma = 1\).
* The ratio \(L/t_0 \gtrsim 20\) for typical values, indicating sizeable medium dilution via longitudinal expansion.
* The scaling violations from vacuum-like emissions and transverse momentum broadening are at the level of a few percent on all investigated quantities.
* For the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\), agreement with LHC data is significantly improved at large transverse momenta \(p_T \gtrsim 500\) GeV after including nuclear parton distribution functions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous perturbative QCD approach for jet evolution in a static homogeneous plasma (Caucal 2018, Caucal 2019, Caucal 2020) to a longitudinally expanding medium following the Bjorken picture (Bjorken 1982). The medium-induced emissions build on the BDMPSZ formalism (Baier 1996, Baier 1997, Zakharov 1996, Zakharov 1997) and the multiple-branching resummation of Blaizot (2012, 2013). The scaling relation differs from the one originally observed by Baier (1998) for average energy loss at energies \(\omega \sim \omega_c\), as the present scaling is strictly valid for soft emissions with \(\omega \ll \omega_c\). The results enable improved phenomenological comparisons to LHC jet quenching data by incorporating realistic medium expansion effects while maintaining the same number of free parameters as the static-medium description.
