# Faddeev calculation of 6 He Lambda Lambda using SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interactions

> Quark-model hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions by the Kyoto-Niigata group are applied to the two-Lambda plus alpha system in a new three-cluster Faddeev formalism using two-cluster resonating-group method kernels. The model fss2 gives a reasonable two-Lambda separation energy Delta B_{Lambda Lambda}=1.41 MeV, which is consistent with the recent empirical value, Delta B^{exp}_{Lambda Lambda}=1.01 +/- 0.20 MeV, deduced from the Nagara event. Some important effects that are not taken into account in the present calculation are discussed.

# Faddeev calculation of ⁶_ΛΛHe using SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interactions

## Key claim

The quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2 yields a two-Lambda separation energy ΔB_ΛΛ = 1.41 MeV for ⁶_ΛΛHe, which is consistent with the experimental value of 1.01 ± 0.20 MeV from the Nagara event, but only after accounting for several repulsive corrections (Brueckner rearrangement of the alpha cluster at about 1 MeV and quark Pauli effects at 0.1–0.2 MeV) that are not included in the present calculation.

## Method

The authors performed a three-cluster Faddeev calculation of the ΛΛα system using two-cluster resonating-group method (RGM) kernels derived from the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2. The Λα T-matrix was generated from an effective ΛN force obtained by supersymmetric inversion of the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ ΛN phase shifts of fss2, with a single adjustable parameter f = 0.8923 to reproduce the Λ separation energy of ⁵_ΛHe. The ΛΛ interaction was treated via the ΛΛ component of the redundancy-free ΛΛ-ΞN-ΣΣ coupled-channel T-matrix, with self-consistent treatment of the energy dependence in the RGM kernel.

## Key numbers

- ΔB_ΛΛ = 1.41 MeV for the fss2 model with the SB ΛN force, compared to the experimental value ΔB_exp_ΛΛ = 1.01 ± 0.20 MeV from the Nagara event
- The simple Gaussian ΛΛ potential (SB) reproducing the fss2 ¹S₀ phase shift yields ΔB_ΛΛ = 1.91 MeV, about 0.5 MeV larger than the full fss2 result
- The ΛΛ potential matrix element in fss2 is −2.4 to −2.6 MeV, much smaller than the FSS and Hiyama potential values of −6 to −7 MeV
- The ground-state energy of ⁶_ΛΛHe from fss2 is E = 17.111 − 24.764 = −7.653 MeV, compared to the experimental value E_exp = −7.25 ± 0.19 MeV
- The free-space G-matrix element of fss2 is ⟨(0s)²|G_ΛΛ|(0s)²⟩ = −2.95 MeV at ν = 0.25 fm⁻², reduced to −2.83 MeV at normal nucleon density and −2.63 MeV at normal Lambda density
- The energy gain from switching on the full ΛΛ-ΞN-ΣΣ coupled-channel T-matrix is only 0.27 MeV relative to the ΛΛ-ΞN double-channel calculation

## Context

This work extends the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2, previously applied to the three-nucleon bound state, hypertriton, and ⁹_ΛBe systems, to the double-Lambda hypernucleus ⁶_ΛΛHe. It addresses the discrepancy between earlier large ΔB_ΛΛ values (about 4.3 MeV from the Nijmegen model D) and the Nagara event value of 1.01 ± 0.20 MeV reported by Takahashi et al. (2001). The results contradict the finding that the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC97e gives too weak a ΛΛ interaction (ΔB_ΛΛ ∼ 0.66 MeV from Filikhin, Gal, and Suslov, 2003). The calculation enables future inclusion of the Brueckner rearrangement effect (estimated at about 1 MeV repulsive from the authors' prior work) and the quark Pauli effect between the alpha cluster and Lambda hyperon (0.1–0.2 MeV from Suzuki et al., 1999) to achieve full consistency with experiment.
