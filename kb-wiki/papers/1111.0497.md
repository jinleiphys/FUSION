# Correlation between alpha-decay Energies of Superheavy Nuclei Involving Effect of Symmetry Energy

> A formula for the relationship between the $α$-decay energies ($Q$ values) of superheavy nuclei (SHN) is presented, which is composed of the effects of Coulomb energy and symmetry energy. It can be employed not only to validate the experimental observations and measurements to a large extent, but also to predict the $Q$ values of heaviest SHN with a high accuracy generally which will be very useful for future experiments. Furthermore, the shell closures in superheavy region and the effect of the symmetry energy on the stability of SHN against $α$-decay are discussed with the help of this formula.

## Key claim
The paper presents a formula for the correlation between alpha-decay Q values of neighboring superheavy nuclei that includes contributions from Coulomb energy and symmetry energy, but explicitly excludes shell effects; the formula works well for nuclei not near shell closures, with deviations of no more than 0.5 MeV for nuclei that may cross shell gaps.

## Method
The authors derived a general formula (Eq. EE) relating the Q values of two neighboring superheavy nuclei with the same mass number, proton number, or neutron number, using the isospin asymmetry beta = (N-Z)/A as the variable. The formula contains only the differences in Coulomb energy (parameterized by a_c = 0.71) and symmetry energy (with volume coefficient c_sym = 31.1 and surface-to-volume ratio kappa = 2.31) between the reference and target nuclei. They tested the formula by calculating Q values for recently synthesized superheavy nuclei using experimental Q values of neighboring reference nuclei, and compared the results with experimental measurements.

## Key numbers
- Root-mean-square deviation of 0.077 MeV and average deviation of 0.064 MeV for central values from 380 reference-target combinations using the general formula (Eq. EE), excluding four nuclei (294118, 290115, 282113, 280111)
- Root-mean-square deviation of 0.052 MeV and average deviation of 0.043 MeV from 96 reference-target combinations using the three simpler special cases (Eqs. BB, CC, DD)
- The symmetry energy term contributes about 45% to the Q-value difference in Eq. BB, about 80% in Eq. CC, and about 35% in Eq. DD for the superheavy nuclei studied
- For nuclide 290115, the experimental Q value is 10.14 +/- 0.41 MeV while the formula predicts about 10.4 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous liquid-drop-model formula for alpha-decay Q values of superheavy nuclei (Dong, 2011) by deriving a correlation between neighboring nuclei that cancels shell and pairing corrections. The formula enables reliable prediction of Q values for as-yet-unobserved superheavy nuclei using known neighboring nuclei, which is important because new superheavy nuclei form an isolated island not linked through alpha-decay chains with known nuclei. The results also allow testing of shell closure predictions from various models, including macroscopic-microscopic models predicting Z=114 and N=184 (Patyk, 1990; Aberg, 1994), Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations favoring Z=124,126 and N=184 (Cwiok, 1996; Bender, 1999), and relativistic mean field models favoring Z=120, N=172 (Bender, 1999; Lalazissis, 1996; Rutz, 1997) or Z=120, N=184 (Gambhir, 1990).
