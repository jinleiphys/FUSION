# Covariant spectator quark model description of the $γ^\ast Λ\to Σ^0$ transition

> We study the $γ^\ast Λ\to Σ^0$ transition form factors by applying the covariant spectator quark model. Using the parametrization for the baryon core wave functions as well as for the pion cloud dressing obtained in a previous work, we calculate the dependence on the momentum transfer squared, $Q^2$, of the electromagnetic transition form factors. The magnetic form factor is dominated by the valence quark contributions. The final result for the transition magnetic moment, a combination of the quark core and pion cloud effects, turns out to give a value very close to the data. The pion cloud contribution, although small, pulls the final result towards the experimental value The final result, $μ_{ΛΣ^0}= -1.486 μ_N$, is about one and a half standard deviations from the central value in PDG, $μ_{ΛΣ^0}= -1.61 \pm 0.08 μ_N$. Thus, a modest improvement in the statistics of the experiment would permit the confirmation or rejection of the present result. It is also predicted that small but nonzero values for the electric form factor in the finite $Q^2$ region, as a consequence of the pion cloud dressing.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, including pion cloud dressing, predicts the transition magnetic moment \(\mu_{\Lambda\Sigma^0} = -1.486\,\mu_N\), which is about one and a half standard deviations from the Particle Data Group central value of \(-1.61 \pm 0.08\,\mu_N\); the authors state that a modest improvement in experimental statistics would permit confirmation or rejection of this result.

## Method

The authors applied the covariant spectator quark model, where each baryon is described as a three-constituent-quark system with one quark interacting with the photon and the other two treated as an on-mass-shell spectator diquark. They decomposed the transition current into valence quark core contributions and pion cloud dressing contributions (from one-pion-loop diagrams with intermediate baryon states), using parameters for the baryon wave functions and pion cloud determined in prior work on octet baryon electromagnetic form factors (Ramalho and Tsushima, 2011). The Dirac-type and Pauli-type form factors were calculated as functions of momentum transfer squared \(Q^2\), and the Sachs electric and magnetic form factors were derived from them.

## Key numbers

- Transition magnetic moment at \(Q^2=0\): \(\mu_{\Lambda\Sigma^0} = -1.486\,\mu_N\) (final result including pion cloud), compared to the experimental PDG value \(\mu_{\Lambda\Sigma^0} = -1.61 \pm 0.08\,\mu_N\).
- Bare (valence quark core) transition magnetic moment: \(\mu_{\Lambda\Sigma^0} = -1.53\,\mu_N\) in the equal-mass limit, with bare anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_{0\Lambda\Sigma} = -1.817\).
- Pion cloud contribution to the transition magnetic moment: \(-0.12\,\mu_N\).
- Normalization factor from pion cloud dressing: \(Z_{\Lambda\Sigma} = 0.9246\).
- Decay width of \(\Sigma^0 \to \gamma \Lambda\) calculated from the model: \(\Gamma = 7.9\) keV, compared to the experimental value \(8.9 \pm 0.9\) keV.
- Bare anomalous magnetic moments used: \(\kappa_{0\Sigma^+} = 2.137\), \(\kappa_{0\Sigma^-} = -0.249\), and \(D_2 = 0.0821\) (value of \(\tilde D_2\) at \(Q^2=0\)).

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to octet baryon elastic form factors (Ramalho and Tsushima, 2011), to the inelastic \(\gamma^\ast \Lambda \to \Sigma^0\) transition. It provides predictions for the \(Q^2\) dependence of the transition form factors, where most prior studies were restricted to \(Q^2=0\); exceptions include chiral perturbation theory calculations (Kubis and Meißner, 2001) and other quark model work (Van Cauteren et al., 2004). The results enable future experimental tests of the sign of \(\mu_{\Lambda\Sigma^0}\) and the relative phase between \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^0\) wave functions, which can also be probed in \(\gamma^\ast Y \to \Lambda(1670)\) reactions.
