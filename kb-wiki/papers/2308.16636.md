# Effects of the $α$-cluster structure and the intrinsic momentum component of nuclei on the longitudinal asymmetry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The longitudinal asymmetry in relativistic heavy ion collisions arises from the fluctuation in the number of nucleons involved. This asymmetry causes a rapidity shift in the center of mass of the participating zone. Both the rapidity shift and the longitudinal asymmetry have been found to be significant at the top CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energy for collisions of identical nuclei, and the longitudinal asymmetry is important for reconstructing the colliding vertex and correcting the rapidity shift. However, much discussion of the longitudinal asymmetry has treated the initial condition as a nonzero momentum contributed only by the number of participants, i.e., the asymmetry depends only on the number of participating nucleons. So we naturally raise a physical problem, can other initial conditions, such as two typical initial conditions for nuclei, geometric configuration, and momentum distribution, provide effects on the longitudinal asymmetry? Therefore, in this work we consider other effects on the longitudinal asymmetry other than the fluctuation in the number of participants, e.g., the α clustering structure as well as the intrinsic momentum distribution in the target and projectile nuclei for the collisions in the framework of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. By introducing systems with different α-clustering structure and intrinsic momentum distribution, we calculated the ratio of the rapidity distributions of different systems and extracted expansion coefficients to analyze the difference contributed by these factors. ...

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether initial-state effects beyond the simple fluctuation in participant number, specifically the α-clustering geometric configuration of nuclei and the intrinsic momentum distribution of nucleons (including the high-momentum tail from short-range correlations), can produce measurable contributions to the longitudinal asymmetry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, using the AMPT model framework.

## Method

The authors simulated \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and 6.37 TeV, and \(^{197}\mathrm{Au}+^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) collisions at 200 GeV, using the String Melting version of the AMPT model with 0–10% centrality. They compared systems with Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions against those with α-clustered triangle configurations (for carbon), and compared Free-Fermi-Gas momentum distributions against distributions including a high-momentum tail (HMT) from short-range correlations. The longitudinal asymmetry was characterized by the rapidity shift \(y_0\) and the asymmetry parameter \(\alpha_{ZN}\), and the ratio of rapidity distributions from positive and negative asymmetry events was fitted with a third-order polynomial to extract expansion coefficients \(c_1, c_2, c_3\).

## Key numbers

- \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\) collisions were simulated at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.37\) TeV and 200 GeV; \(^{197}\mathrm{Au}+^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) at 200 GeV.
- The rapidity shift \(y_0\) was found to be of order \(10^{-1}\) for the systems studied, with \(\sigma\) (rapidity width) in the range 2–4.
- For C+C at 200 GeV, the first-order coefficient \(c_1\) in the Woods-Saxon case was smaller than in the FFG and HMT cases, while \(c_2\) and \(c_3\) in the Woods-Saxon case were larger than in the FFG and HMT cases.
- The ratio of simulated rapidity distribution to ideal Gaussian was estimated to be about 1.2 times larger due to variations in \(y_0\) and \(\sigma\) at the \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-1}\) level.
- The \(\langle y_0 \rangle\) values for FFG (200 GeV) and HMT (200 GeV) were smaller than for Woods-Saxon (200 GeV), but their \(c_1\) values were larger.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of longitudinal asymmetry that treated the effect as arising solely from participant number fluctuations (Thakur et al., 2018; ALICE collaboration, 2018). It introduces two additional initial-state effects: α-clustering structure (previously studied in heavy-ion collisions via collective flow by He (2014) and Li (2020)) and the high-momentum tail from short-range correlations (parameterized following Ciofi degli Atti (1991, 1995) and Wang (2017)). The results suggest that intrinsic momentum distributions can modify the rapidity distribution shape and the expansion coefficients, potentially enabling constraints on initial conditions and corrections to colliding vertex reconstruction in LHC and RHIC experiments.
