# Connected-Sea Partons

> According to the path-integral formalism of the hadronic tensor, the nucleon sea contains two distinct components called connected sea (CS) and disconnected sea (DS). We discuss how the CS and DS are accessed in the lattice QCD calculation of the moments of the parton distributions. We show that the CS and DS components for $\bar u(x) + \bar d(x)$ can be extracted by using recent data on the strangeness parton distribution, the CT10 global fit, and the lattice result of the ratio of the strange to $u(d)$ moments in the disconnected insertion. The extracted CS and DS for $\bar u(x) + \bar d(x)$ have distinct Bjorken $x$ dependence in qualitative agreement with expectation. The analysis also shows that the momentum fraction of the $\bar u(x) + \bar d(x)$ is about equally divided between CS and DS at $Q^2 = 2.5 {\rm GeV}^2$. Implications on future global analysis for parton distributions are presented.

# Connected-Sea Partons

## Key claim

The nucleon sea can be separated into two distinct components, connected sea (CS) and disconnected sea (DS), and the CS and DS contributions to \(\bar u(x) + \bar d(x)\) can be extracted using existing experimental data and lattice QCD input, under the ansatz that \(\bar u^{ds}(x) + \bar d^{ds}(x)\) is proportional to \(s(x) + \bar s(x)\) with a proportionality factor \(1/R\) derived from lattice calculations.

## Method

The authors combine the CT10 global fit for \(\bar u(x) + \bar d(x)\) with HERMES semi-inclusive DIS data on \(s(x) + \bar s(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and a lattice QCD result for the ratio \(R = \langle x \rangle_{s+\bar s} / \langle x \rangle_{u+\bar u}(\mathrm{DI}) = 0.857(40)\). Using the ansatz \(\bar u^{ds}(x) + \bar d^{ds}(x) = (1/R)(s(x) + \bar s(x))\), they extract the CS component as \(\bar u^{cs}(x) + \bar d^{cs}(x) = \bar u(x) + \bar d(x) - (1/R)(s(x) + \bar s(x))\). The analysis is performed at \(Q^2 = 2.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and cross-checked with CTEQ6 and MSTW08 PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD ratio \(R = \langle x \rangle_{s+\bar s} / \langle x \rangle_{u+\bar u}(\mathrm{DI}) = 0.857 \pm 0.040\) from 2+1-flavor dynamical fermion configurations with lowest pion mass at 600 MeV.
- Momentum fraction of \(\bar d + \bar u\) from CT10: \(\langle x \rangle_{\bar d + \bar u} = 0.0639(14)\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- CS momentum fraction from CT10: \(\langle x \rangle_{\bar d^{cs} + \bar u^{cs}} = 0.0294(54)\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- DS momentum fraction from CT10: \(\langle x \rangle_{\bar d^{ds} + \bar u^{ds}} = 0.0344(52)\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- Ratio \(\langle x \rangle_{\bar d^{cs} + \bar u^{cs}} / \langle x \rangle_{\bar d^{ds} + \bar u^{ds}} = 0.86(29)\) from CT10, indicating approximately equal division of momentum fraction between CS and DS.
- ATLAS measurement of strange-to-down quark ratio: \(1.00^{+0.25}_{-0.28}\) at \(x = 0.023\) and \(Q^2 = 1.9\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the path-integral formalism of the hadronic tensor developed by Liu and collaborators (1995, 2000), which identified CS and DS as topologically distinct contributions to the nucleon sea. It builds on experimental observations of the \(\bar d - \bar u\) asymmetry from the E866 Drell-Yan experiment (2001) and HERMES semi-inclusive DIS (1998), as well as lattice QCD calculations of sea quark moments (2010, 2012). The extracted CS distribution peaks at medium \(x \sim 0.1\), consistent with the expected \(x^{-1/2}\) small-x behavior of valence-like CS, while the DS distribution follows the \(x^{-1}\) behavior of flavor-singlet pomeron exchange. The results enable future global PDF analyses to treat CS and DS separately in evolution equations, which evolve differently and could improve constraints on gluon distributions and flavor-dependent sea structure.
