# Configuration Space Based Recurrence Relations for Sunset-Type Diagrams

> We derive recurrence relations for the calculation of multiloop sunset-type diagrams with large powers of massive propagators. The technique is formulated in configuration space and exploits the explicit form of the massive propagator raised to a given power. We write down and evaluate a convenient set of basis integrals. The method is well suited for a numerical evaluation of this class of diagrams. We give explicit analytical formulae for the basis integrals in the asymptotic regime.

## Key claim

The paper derives recurrence relations for multiloop sunset-type diagrams with large powers of massive propagators, formulated in configuration space, which reduce the problem to a one-parameter set of basis integrals rather than the three-dimensional tables required by standard momentum-space recurrence relations.

## Method

The authors express three-loop vacuum bubble diagrams of the water melon topology in configuration space as integrals over products of McDonald functions (modified Bessel functions) representing massive propagators raised to arbitrary powers. They use recurrence relations for McDonald functions and partial integration to reduce any such integral to a basis of integrals containing only \(K_0^4(x)\) times powers of \(x\), with or without a logarithmic factor. The method exploits the explicit form of the massive propagator in configuration space and avoids the three-dimensional parameter tables needed in momentum-space approaches.

## Key numbers

- \(I_0(3) = \int_0^\infty K_0^4(x) x^3 dx = -\frac{3}{16} + \frac{7}{32}\zeta(3)\) (zeroth-order term for \(B_N(2,2,2,2)\))
- \(B_4 = -1.7628\ldots\) (numerical value of the master integral, obtained from configuration space integrals)
- \(I_1(2) = \int_0^\infty K_0^3(x) K_1(x) x^2 dx = \frac{7}{16}\zeta(3)\) (zeroth-order term for \(B_N(2,2,2,1)\))
- \(I_{21}(7) = \int_0^\infty K_0(x) K_1^2(x) K_2(x) x^7 dx = \frac{1}{3}\) (exact rational result for \(B_N(2,3,3,4)\))
- \(I(0) = \frac{7}{8}\zeta(3)\) (basis integral for \(q=0\) in the \(D=4\) water melon)

## Context

This work extends the integration-by-parts recurrence technique for three-loop vacuum bubbles developed by Avdeev (1994) and the explicit reduction of the \(B_N\) subclass by Broadhurst (1998). It provides an alternative to the momentum-space RECURSOR package and the MATAD package by Steinhauser (1998), offering a simpler one-parameter reduction that is particularly efficient for large powers of propagators. The method enables direct numerical evaluation of water melon diagrams and can be generalized to any number of loops, potentially allowing higher moments of the \(b\)-quark spectral function beyond the currently available \(n=8\).
