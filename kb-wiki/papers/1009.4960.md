# Current trends in searches for new physics using measurements of parity violation and electric dipole moments in atoms and molecules

> We review current status of the study of parity and time invariance phenomena in atoms, nuclei and molecules. We focus on three most promising areas of research: (i) parity non-conservation in a chain of isotopes, (ii) search for nuclear anapole moments, and (iii) search for permanent electric dipole moments (EDM) of atoms and molecules which are caused by either, electron EDM or nuclear $T,P$-odd moments such as nuclear EDM and nuclear Schiff moment.

# Current trends in searches for new physics using measurements of parity violation and electric dipole moments in atoms and molecules

## Key claim

The paper argues that the most promising low-energy searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model have shifted from single-isotope atomic parity non-conservation (PNC) measurements, where the cesium result was reconciled with the Standard Model after including Breit and QED corrections, to three alternative areas: PNC in isotope chains, nuclear anapole moments, and permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs) of atoms and molecules. The authors note that isotope-chain measurements can be more sensitive to new physics than current parity-violating electron scattering measurements by a factor of 10 for atoms such as Cs, Ba, and Dy, provided that correlated uncertainties in neutron distributions cancel as argued in recent nuclear calculations.

## Method

The paper is a review that surveys theoretical and experimental progress in three areas: (i) PNC measurements across isotope chains, where the ratio of PNC signals for two isotopes eliminates the electron structure factor and is sensitive to new physics couplings; (ii) nuclear anapole moments, which dominate the nuclear-spin-dependent PNC effect in molecules due to mixing of close rotational states of opposite parity; and (iii) permanent EDMs, including electron EDM in paramagnetic systems, nuclear EDM in ions (where screening is reduced), and nuclear Schiff moments in diamagnetic atoms. The authors present atomic and nuclear calculations linking measured EDMs to fundamental T,P-odd interaction constants, and discuss enhancement mechanisms such as octupole deformation for Schiff moments.

## Key numbers

- The PNC effect in heavy alkaline atoms like Fr and Ra+ is 20 times larger than in Cs.
- The estimated contribution to the sensitivity parameter δF from correlated neutron distribution uncertainties is in the range 10^-3 to 10^-2, about an order of magnitude smaller than uncorrelated uncertainties.
- The anapole moment effect in atoms is 50 times smaller than the weak charge effect.
- The interval between opposite-parity rotational-hyperfine states in molecules is five orders of magnitude smaller than in atoms (by factor me/M), leading to PNC effects five orders of magnitude larger.
- The anapole moment effect in molecules grows with nuclear charge as Z^2 A^(2/3) R(Zα), where R(Zα) grows from 1 at low Z to about 10 for Z > 80.
- The best limit on electron EDM from thallium measurements is de = (6.9 ± 7.4) × 10^-28 e cm, using K = -585.
- The limit on electron EDM from mercury measurements is de < 3 × 10^-27 e cm.
- The best limit on the EDM of diamagnetic atoms is |d(199Hg)| < 3.1 × 10^-29 |e| cm.
- The Schiff moment of 199Hg is S = (-1.8 ± 4.6 ± 2.7) × 10^-13 e cm.
- The T,P-odd neutron-proton interaction constant is ηnp = (1 ± 3 ± 2) × 10^-5.
- The Schiff moment of 225Ra is -8.2 × 10^-17 e cm, and for 223Ra it is -8.2 × 10^-17 e cm.
- The Schiff moment of 223Rn is 3.3 × 10^-17 e cm.
- The estimated Schiff moment for an octupole-deformed nucleus is about 700 × 10^-8 η e fm^3, approximately 500 times larger than for a spherical nucleus like Hg.
- The energy interval between opposite-parity rotational states in octupole-deformed nuclei is about 50 keV.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on atomic PNC in cesium (Wood; Dzuba, Flambaum, and Ginges) and addresses the resolution of apparent disagreements with the Standard Model after including Breit and QED corrections. It builds on the proposal by Dzuba, Flambaum, and Kozlov (1986) for isotope-chain PNC measurements and the subsequent analysis by Fortson (1990) of neutron distribution uncertainties, which were later addressed by Brown, Dzuba, and Flambaum (2009) using correlated nuclear calculations. The paper discusses the only measured nuclear anapole moment, that of 133Cs (Wood), and notes inconsistencies with the limit on the Tl anapole moment. It surveys ongoing experiments at Berkeley (Dy, Yb), TRIUMF (Fr, Rb), Los Alamos (Yb+), Groningen KVI (Ra+), Yale (molecules), and Argonne (Ra), and highlights the enhancement of Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei (Auerbach, Flambaum, and Spevak) and soft octupole vibrations, which explains the large predicted Schiff moments for Ra and Rn.
