# Benchmark calculations of pure neutron matter with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions

> We report benchmark calculations of the energy per particle of pure neutron matter as a function of the baryon density using three independent many-body methods: Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone, Fermi hypernetted chain/single-operator chain, and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo. Significant technical improvements are implemented in the latter two methods. The calculations are made for two distinct families of realistic coordinate-space nucleon-nucleon potentials fit to scattering data, including the standard Argonne $v_{18}$ interaction and two of its simplified versions, and four of the new Norfolk $Δ$-full chiral effective field theory potentials. The results up to twice nuclear matter saturation density show some divergence among the methods, but improved agreement compared to earlier work. We find that the potentials fit to higher-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data exhibit a much smaller spread of energies.

## Key claim

The paper reports benchmark calculations of the energy per particle of pure neutron matter using three independent many-body methods, finding improved agreement among methods compared to earlier work, but with some divergence remaining up to twice nuclear saturation density. The authors emphasize that the potentials fit to higher-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data exhibit a much smaller spread of energies, and that the study is limited to two-body forces only, explicitly excluding three-nucleon forces needed for a realistic equation of state.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of the energy per particle of pure neutron matter as a function of baryon density using three independent many-body methods: Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) theory in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation with continuous choice for the single-particle potential, Fermi hypernetted chain/single-operator chain (FHNC/SOC) with inclusion of additional elementary diagrams beyond the FHNC/4 approximation, and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) with improvements in sampling and fermion sign control. The calculations used two families of coordinate-space nucleon-nucleon potentials: the Argonne AV18 interaction and its simplified AV8' and AV6' versions, and four of the Norfolk NV2 chiral effective field theory potentials that include explicit Delta isobar intermediate states, fitted to scattering data up to either 125 MeV or 200 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- Energy per particle of pure neutron matter calculated up to twice nuclear matter saturation density (2ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- AV18 potential fitted up to the pion production threshold (E_lab = 350 MeV) with a hard core cutoff of approximately 1 GeV
- NV2-IIb chiral model fitted at higher energies (E_lab = 200 MeV) with a hard cutoff of approximately 600 MeV in momentum-space
- FHNC/SOC calculations used three healing distances (d_s for singlet, d_p for triplet and spin-orbit, d_t for tensor) and one quenching factor as variational parameters
- AFDMC simulations typically employed 66 neutrons in a periodic box, with finite-size errors of no more than 2% compared to twist averaged boundary conditions
- BBG calculations included partial wave contributions up to total two-body angular momentum J_max = 11
- FHNC/SOC sum rule violation limited to 1% or less at normal density and 3% or less at twice normal density

## Context

This work extends earlier benchmark studies of neutron matter by Baldo (2012) that identified discrepancies among many-body methods as particularly sensitive to spin-orbit components of the nucleon-nucleon force. The paper implements technical improvements in both AFDMC (following Gandolfi (2014) and Lonardoni (2018) for atomic nuclei) and FHNC/SOC (including elementary diagrams previously disregarded), enabling better assessment of systematic errors. The results enable more reliable comparisons between microscopic model Hamiltonians fit to scattering data and astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations such as GW170817, though the authors note that realistic description of the equation of state requires future inclusion of three-nucleon forces.
