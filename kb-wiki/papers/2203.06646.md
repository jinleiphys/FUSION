# Understanding mass hierarchy in collisional energy loss through heavy flavor data

> While experimental observations, such as the mass hierarchy effect, are attributed and analyzed within radiative models, their interpretation crucially depends on collisional energy loss contribution, which is often neglected in such analyses. To our knowledge, neither a (direct) simple relation between collisional energy loss and heavy quark mass is established, nor an observable that quantifies this effect. On the other hand, the upcoming high-luminosity measurements at RHIC and LHC will generate heavy flavor data with unprecedented precision, providing an opportunity to utilize high-pT heavy flavor data to analyze the interaction mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma. To this end, we employ a recently developed DREENA framework based on our dynamical energy loss formalism to study the mass hierarchy in heavy flavor suppression. We present i) Analytical derivation of a direct relation between collisional suppression/energy loss and heavy quark mass. ii) A novel observable sensitive only to the collisional energy loss mechanism to be tested by future high-precision experiments. iii) Analytical and numerical extraction of the mass hierarchy in collisional energy losses through this observable.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the mass hierarchy observed in heavy flavor suppression (the ratio of bottom to charm quark suppression, quantified as \(1-R_{AA}\)) is dominantly driven by collisional energy loss, not radiative energy loss, and that this ratio can be expressed by a simple analytical formula depending only on quark mass and transverse momentum, independent of collision centrality.

## Method

The authors employed the DREENA-C framework, which is based on a dynamical energy loss formalism that consistently includes both collisional and radiative energy loss for heavy quarks traversing a finite-size, finite-temperature QCD medium composed of dynamical (moving) partons. They performed analytical derivations starting from the Thoma-Gyulassy collisional energy loss expression and a parameterized form of the initial parton transverse momentum distribution, and compared these analytical results with full numerical predictions from DREENA-C for charm and bottom quarks at RHIC and LHC energies. The numerical predictions were also compared with existing CMS and ALICE experimental data for non-prompt \(J/\psi\) and \(D\) meson suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass set to \(M_c = 1.2\) GeV and bottom quark mass set to \(M_b = 4.75\) GeV in the DREENA-C framework.
* The initial parton transverse momentum distribution is parameterized as \(\frac{d\sigma^i}{dp^2_\perp} = \frac{C}{(p^2_\perp + M^2)^k}\) with \(\ln(C) \simeq 25.5\) and \(k \simeq 3.5\) for both charm and bottom.
* The analytically derived ratio for the mass hierarchy in collisional energy loss is \(\frac{1-R^b_{AA}}{1-R^c_{AA}} \simeq \frac{1-M_b/p_\perp}{1-M_c/p_\perp}\), which is independent of collision centrality.
* The chromomagnetic to chromoelectric mass ratio was set to \(\frac{\mu_B}{\mu_E} = 0.4\) for both RHIC and LHC conditions.

## Context

This work extends the study of mass hierarchy in parton energy loss, which was previously well-established for radiative energy loss through the dead-cone effect (Dokshitzer and Kharzeev, 2001; Djordjevic and Heinz, 2008), to the collisional energy loss mechanism, which had not been analytically addressed. The authors build on their own DREENA framework (Zigic et al., 2019) and the dynamical energy loss formalism (Djordjevic, 2006; Djordjevic and Djordjevic, 2014) to propose a novel observable that can disentangle collisional from radiative contributions. This work enables future high-precision measurements at RHIC and LHC to directly extract the mass dependence of collisional energy loss from heavy flavor suppression data, particularly at lower transverse momentum where the mass hierarchy is most pronounced.
