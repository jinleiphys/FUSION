# Global Polarization Effect in the Extremely Rapidly Rotating QGP in HIC

> This is prepared for a featured article in Nuclear Physics News. Recently, the global polarization of Λand \barΛ hyperons in heavy-ion collisions (HIC) has been observed by the STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider in Brookhaven National Laboratory. The discovery confirms the theoretical prediction made more than ten years ago and indicates that the quark gluon plasma (QGP) produced in HIC possesses a vorticity as high as 10^21s^-1, much higher than any other fluid observed in nature. This opens a new window to study properties of QGP and a new direction in high energy heavy ion physics. This featured article is aimed to report the basic idea, current status and outlook.

## Key claim

The global polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons observed by STAR confirms the theoretical prediction made more than ten years ago, indicating that the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions possesses a vorticity as high as 10^21 s^-1, though the measured polarization values are small (around 1 percent) and the early measurements were consistent with zero within large errors.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured the global polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC by analyzing the angular distribution of their weak decay products (Lambda to p pi-), using the self-spin analyzing property of the decay. The reaction plane was determined by measuring the sidewards deflection of forward and backward going fragments and particles in the beam-beam counter detectors. The polarization was extracted from the decay angular distribution formula dN/dOmega = (N/4pi)(1 + alpha P_Lambda cos theta), where theta is the decay angle of the proton with respect to the polarization direction in the Lambda rest frame.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization of Lambda hyperons averaged over collision energy: 1.08 +/- 0.15 (stat) +/- 0.11 (sys) percent
- Global polarization of anti-Lambda hyperons averaged over collision energy: 1.38 +/- 0.30 (stat) +/- 0.13 (sys) percent
- Vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma estimated from the data: omega approximately (9 +/- 1) x 10^21 s^-1
- Global orbital angular momentum of the QGP formed in HIC at RHIC can be as large as 10^5 hbar
- Local orbital angular momentum l_0 in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV: l_0 approximately -1.7 for Delta x=1 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction by Liang (2004) that quarks and anti-quarks in QGP created in non-central heavy-ion collisions are polarized in the direction of the global angular momentum, and confirms the existence of a vortical fluid with properties far exceeding any other known fluid in nature. The results open a new window to study properties of QGP through spin-orbital coupling and enable future measurements with higher statistics from STAR BES II and ALICE at LHC, as well as potential studies of the huge magnetic fields expected in heavy-ion collisions that could produce a fine-structure pattern in the polarization of Lambda versus anti-Lambda hyperons.
