# In-plane transverse polarization in heavy-ion collisions

> We give an analytical expression for the in-plane polarization $P^{x}$, in heavy-ion collisions that has, to our knowledge, not been measured in heavy-ion collision experiments. We also carry out a numerical study of $P^{x}$ using a hydrodynamic model simulation as a cross-check for the analytical formula. It is found that if the temperature-gradient contribution is neglected the simulation result for $P^{x}$ qualitatively agrees with the analytical one. The prediction of $P^{x}$ can be tested in experiments and will contribute to provide a complete and consistent picture of spin phenomena in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides an analytical expression for the in-plane spin polarization $P^{x}$ in heavy-ion collisions, which has not been measured experimentally, and shows through hydrodynamic simulation that when the temperature-gradient contribution is neglected, the simulation result for $P^{x}$ qualitatively agrees with the analytical formula.

## Method

The authors use an extended blast-wave model with flow-momentum correspondence to derive analytical expressions for the in-plane polarization $P^{x}$ from thermal vorticity and thermal shear stress tensors. They evaluate the expectation values of polarization vectors by integrating over the freeze-out hypersurface using a perturbation method that treats the directed flow parameter $\alpha_1$, elliptic flow parameter $\rho_2$, and eccentricity $\epsilon$ as small perturbations relative to the mean transverse rapidity $\rho_0$. The analytical results are cross-checked against a hydrodynamic model simulation for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- $P^{x}(\phi_{p})$ is proportional to $\sin(2\phi_{p})$, with the same $\phi_p$ pattern as $P^{z}$ but with a slightly smaller magnitude
- $P^{y} \approx -2P_{\sin2\phi}^{x}$ is an approximate equality confirmed by numerical results
- $P_{\sin2\phi}^{x} = -P_{\cos2\phi}^{y}$ is an exact equality showing symmetry between in-plane and out-of-plane polarization
- The analytical formulas use parameters for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV across different centralities, with freeze-out temperature $T_f$ and transverse rapidity parameter $\rho_0$ extracted from fitting transverse momentum spectra

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of longitudinal and global spin polarizations in the extended blast-wave model (Arslan, 2024), where analytical expressions were found for $P^z$ and $P^y$ under flow-momentum correspondence. The paper addresses the fact that earlier theoretical results for $P^x$ from hydrodynamic or transport model simulations (Karpenko, 2016; Xia, 2018; Sun, 2021) showed very small magnitudes compared to $P^y$, while the authors' analytical formulas show $P^x$ and $P^y$ have the same order of magnitude. The prediction of $P^x$ can be tested in experiments and will contribute to a complete picture of spin phenomena in heavy-ion collisions.
