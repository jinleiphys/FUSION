# Energy Dependence of Strange Particle Yields from a QGP-Fireball

> We explore, as function of the collision energy and stopping in relativistic nuclear collisions, the production yields of strange particles, in particular strange antibaryons,assuming formation of a deconfined thermal QGP-fireball which undergoes a sudden hadronisation. The non-equilibrium freeze-out conditions are established and strange antibaryon excitation functions are shown to have characteristic features that should allow to discriminate between the QGP hypothesis and other reaction scenarios.

## Key claim

The authors explore strange particle yields as a function of collision energy assuming a deconfined thermal QGP-fireball that undergoes sudden hadronisation, but they explicitly note that it is "far from certain today that QGP is formed already in the nuclear collisions below √s = 10A GeV" and that the model assumes thermal equilibrium arises rapidly without resolving the mechanism by which this occurs.

## Method

The authors develop a thermal model of a QGP-fireball at central rapidity, using equations of state for the quark-gluon plasma that incorporate QCD interactions via thermal masses and effective degrees of freedom. They compute strange particle yields as functions of collision energy by evolving the fireball through stages of thermalisation, chemical equilibration of light quarks and gluons, strangeness production, and sudden hadronisation, with four free parameters (two fugacities λ_q and λ_s, strangeness occupancy factor γ_s, and the ratio R^s_c) plus temperature and fireball rapidity. The model is applied to symmetric and asymmetric nuclear collisions at CERN-SPS (200A GeV) and BNL-AGS (10-15A GeV) energies.

## Key numbers

- The inverse slope parameter T⊥ = 232 ± 5 MeV for strange baryons and antibaryons in 200A GeV S–W/Pb interactions, consistent with high m⊥ neutral meson spectra
- Strangeness relaxation time constant τ_s ≤ 6 fm/c in QGP, with τ_s ≈ 2 fm/c for T ≈ 250 MeV from gluon fusion processes
- Experimental results yield γ_s = 0.7–1, implying a QGP lifespan greater than 3 fm according to the relaxation equation
- For 200A GeV S–Au/W/Pb collisions, the central rapidity is y_CM = 2.6 ± 0.1 from the interacting tube model
- The ratio R^s_c = 0.4 is required to maintain baryon enhancement by a factor of 5 relative to thermal equilibrium expectations
- Energy per baryon values explored: E/B = 4.3 GeV (thin lines) and 9.6 GeV (thick lines) for the EoS constraints shown in Figure 1
- Current quark masses used: m_q^0 = 5 MeV, m_s^0 = 160 MeV, m_G^0 = 0
- Critical temperature chosen at T_c = 150 MeV for the QGP transition

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Letessier, Rafelski, Tounsi, 1994) that showed strange particle production results point toward deconfined phase formation at CERN-SPS energies, and contrasts with hadronic gas equilibrium expectations (BNL-AGS thermal analysis, 1994) that describe 10–15A GeV data. The paper enables predictions for strange antibaryon excitation functions across the energy range between SPS and AGS, allowing experimental discrimination between QGP and hadron gas scenarios, and provides a framework for interpreting upcoming experiments at varying collision energies.
