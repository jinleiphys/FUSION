# Superdeformation and hyperdeformation in the $^{108}$Cd nucleus

> The superdeformation and hyperdeformation in $^{108}$Cd have been studied for the first time within the framework of the fully self-consistent cranked mean field theory, namely, cranked relativistic mean field theory. The structure of observed superdeformed bands 1 and 2 have been analyzed in detail. The bumps seen in their dynamic moments of inertia are explained as arising from unpaired band crossings. This is contrary to an explanation given earlier within the framework of projected shell model. It was also concluded that this nucleus is not doubly magic SD nucleus.

# Superdeformation and hyperdeformation in the $^{108}$Cd nucleus

## Key claim
The bumps observed in the dynamic moments of inertia of superdeformed bands 1 and 2 in $^{108}$Cd arise from unpaired band crossings involving the lowest proton $N=6$ orbital and $N=4$ orbitals, contrary to an earlier explanation within the projected shell model that attributed them to paired quasiparticle configurations. The nucleus is not a doubly magic superdeformed nucleus.

## Method
The authors employed fully self-consistent cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory, solving the Dirac equation in an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with deformation parameters $\beta_0=0.65$, $\gamma=0^\circ$ and oscillator frequency $\hbar\omega_0=41A^{-1/3}$ MeV, using the NL1 parameter set for the RMF Lagrangian. Pairing was neglected in the calculations. Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) calculations with standard Nilsson parameters were also performed for comparison. Fermionic states up to $N_F=14$ and bosonic states up to $N_B=16$ were included in the diagonalization.

## Key numbers
- The lowest SD band (band 1) is assigned a spin range from $44\hbar$ up to $66\hbar$, with an alternative possibility of $42\hbar$ to $64\hbar$ given typical 5% accuracy of CRMF calculations.
- The average transition quadrupole moment for the assigned configuration of band 1 is $Q_t \approx 10.8$ $e$b, agreeing with the experimental lower limit of $Q_t = 9.5$ $e$b.
- The band crossing in the calculations occurs approximately 200 keV earlier than in experiment for both bands, suggesting the energy distance between the $\pi[420]1/2$ and $\pi[660]1/2$ orbitals is underestimated by approximately 0.7 MeV.
- For band 2, the transition quadrupole moment is $Q_t \approx 12.2$ $e$b (calculated) versus the experimental value of $Q_t = 8.5$ $e$b (with large errors).
- Hyperdeformed configurations become yrast above $I = 68\hbar$, with transition quadrupole moments increasing from $Q_t = 17.2$ $e$b at $I=44\hbar$ to $Q_t \approx 17.5$ $e$b at $I=84\hbar$, corresponding to a 2.3:1 semiaxis ratio.
- The kinematic moment of inertia for HD configurations drops from $J^{(1)} \approx 70.5$ MeV$^{-1}$ at $I=44\hbar$ to $J^{(1)} \approx 67.5$ MeV$^{-1}$ at $I=84\hbar$.
- The dynamic moments of inertia for HD configurations in the frequency range of the experimental bands are $J^{(2)} \approx 64$ MeV$^{-1}$.
- CNS calculations give $Q_t \sim 10.3$ $e$b for band 1 at $\varepsilon_2 \sim 0.67$, $\gamma \sim 15^\circ$, and $Q_t \approx 18$ $e$b for HD configurations.

## Context
This work extends the study of superdeformation into the $A\sim 110$ mass region, following the experimental observation of two SD bands in $^{108}$Cd by the authors of Refs. [Cd108-1] and [Cd108-2], which were motivated by earlier calculations by Wyss and collaborators (1995) and more recent studies by Chasman (2001). The analysis contradicts the earlier projected shell model interpretation by the authors of Ref. [PSM], who attributed the bump in the dynamic moment of inertia to paired $i_{13/2}$ proton orbitals. The finding that $^{108}$Cd is not doubly magic SD, with a $Z=48$ SD shell gap smaller than those in $^{60}$Zn and $^{152}$Dy, helps explain why searches for SD bands in neighboring nuclei have been unsuccessful so far. The predicted hyperdeformed configurations, expected to be yrast above $I\approx 64\hbar$, provide guidance for future experimental searches.
