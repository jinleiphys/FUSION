# Flavour Distributions in the Nucleons: SU(2) Sea Asymmetry or Isospin Symmetry Breaking?

> The Gottfried sum rule violation reported by the New Muon Collaboration(NMC) was interpreted as indication for a flavour asymmetry of the sea quark in the nucleons. We investigate the alternative possibility that isospin symmetry between the proton and the neutron is breaking. We examine systematically the consequences of this possibility for several processes, namely, neutrino deep inelastic scattering, charged pion Drell-Yan process, proton Drell-Yan process, and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, and conclude that a decision between the two alternative explanations is possible.

## Key claim

The violation of the Gottfried sum rule reported by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC) can be explained either by a flavour asymmetry of the sea quarks in the nucleon (excess of d-anti-d over u-anti-u) or by isospin symmetry breaking between the proton and neutron (excess of sea quarks in the neutron over those in the proton), and the paper systematically examines which experimental processes can distinguish between these two alternatives.

## Method

The authors assume sea flavour symmetry (anti-u = anti-d) in the proton and examine the consequences of isospin symmetry breaking between proton and neutron, where only valence quarks preserve isospin symmetry while sea quarks do not. They derive analytic expressions for several observables, neutrino deep inelastic scattering structure functions, charged pion Drell-Yan cross-section ratios, proton Drell-Yan cross-section asymmetries, and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering hadron yields, and compare the predicted values for the two alternative explanations.

## Key numbers

- The NMC Gottfried sum value is S_G = 0.240 ± 0.016, significantly below the simple quark-parton-model result of 1/3.
- For the SU(2) sea asymmetry explanation: ∫[anti-u(x) − anti-d(x)] dx = −0.140 ± 0.024.
- For the p-n isospin symmetry breaking explanation: ∫[anti-q^p(x) − anti-q^n(x)] dx = −0.084 ± 0.014.
- The new neutrino sum S = ∫[(F2^{νp} + F2^{anti-νp}) − 1/2(F2^{νD} + F2^{anti-νD})] dx is zero for the asymmetric sea explanation and −0.336 ± 0.058 for p-n symmetry breaking.
- The relation between the two explanations is ∫[anti-q^p − anti-q^n] = (3/5) ∫[anti-u − anti-d].

## Context

This work extends the observation by Ma (1992) that isospin symmetry breaking between proton and neutron could explain the NMC Gottfried sum violation, and systematically tests this against the earlier sea asymmetry interpretation proposed by Preparata (1991), Kumano and Londergan (1991), Henley and Miller (1990), and Signal and Thomas (1991). The paper shows that the charged pion Drell-Yan ratio R_sea proposed by Kumano and Londergan (1991) and the proton Drell-Yan asymmetry A_DY discussed by Ellis and Stirling (1991) give approximately the same values for both explanations and thus cannot distinguish them, while the neutrino structure function combination and the semi-inclusive hadron ratio Q^{ch}(z) from Levelt, Mulders and Schreiber (1991) can differentiate between the two possibilities.
