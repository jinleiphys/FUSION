# Triplet Pairing in Neutron Matter

> The separation method developed earlier by us [Nucl. Phys. {\bf A598} 390 (1996)] to calculate and analyze solutions of the BCS gap equation for $^1$S$_0$ pairing is extended and applied to $^3$P$_2$--$^3$F$_2$ pairing in pure neutron matter. The pairing matrix elements are written as a separable part plus a remainder that vanishes when either momentum variable is on the Fermi surface. This decomposition effects a separation of the problem of determining the dependence of the gap components in a spin-angle representation on the magnitude of the momentum (described by a set of functions independent of magnetic quantum number) from the problem of determining the dependence of the gap on angle or magnetic projection. The former problem is solved through a set of nonsingular, quasilinear integral equations, providing inputs for solution of the latter problem through a coupled system of algebraic equations for a set of numerical coefficients. An incisive criterion is given for finding the upper critical density for closure of the triplet gap. The separation method and its development for triplet pairing exploit the existence of a small parameter, given by a gap-amplitude measure divided by the Fermi energy. The revised BCS equations admit analysis revealing universal properties of the full set of solutions for $^3$P$_2$ pairing in the absence of tensor coupling, referring especially to the energy degeneracy and energetic order of these solutions. The angle-average approximation introduced by Baldo et al. is illuminated in terms of the separation-transformed BCS problem and the small parameter expansion...

## Key claim

The separation method for solving BCS gap equations, previously developed for \(^1\)S\(_0\) pairing, is extended to \(^3\)P\(_2\)–\(^3\)F\(_2\) triplet pairing in pure neutron matter, exploiting a small parameter (gap amplitude divided by Fermi energy) to achieve a decomposition that separates the momentum-shape problem from the angular-dependence problem. The authors emphasize that *ab initio* evaluation of the in-medium particle-particle interaction and single-particle energies will be required for a reliable quantitative description, so the numerical results presented use vacuum nucleon-nucleon interactions and free-particle energies as inputs.

## Method

The pairing matrix elements are decomposed into a separable part plus a remainder that vanishes when either momentum variable is on the Fermi surface, reducing the coupled BCS gap equations to a set of nonsingular, quasilinear integral equations for momentum-dependent shape functions (independent of magnetic quantum number) and a coupled system of algebraic equations for numerical coefficients that determine the angular dependence of the gap. The method is applied to pure \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing and to coupled \(^3\)P\(_2\)–\(^3\)F\(_2\) pairing using pairing matrix elements from three vacuum nucleon-nucleon potentials, including the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential. A criterion for the upper critical density for gap closure is derived from the vanishing of a determinant in the linearized system of equations for the coefficients.

## Key numbers

* Critical Fermi momentum for gap closure in coupled \(^3\)P\(_2\)–\(^3\)F\(_2\) pairing with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential and free single-particle energies: \(k_c = 3.6\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* Corrections to the leading order in the small-parameter expansion of the \(^3\)P\(_2\)–\(^3\)F\(_2\) energy gap are down by a factor of \(10^{-5}\) or \(10^{-6}\).
* Spread of condensation energies among different solution types for pure \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing, as reported by Takatsuka and Tamagaki at a typical density using the OPEG potential: only 3%.

## Context

This work extends the separation method of Khodel, Khodel, and Clark (1996) from \(^1\)S\(_0\) pairing to triplet pairing in neutron matter, addressing the additional complexity of angle-dependent gaps in higher angular momentum channels. It provides a framework for analyzing universal properties of \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing that are independent of density and the specific pairing interaction, and it clarifies the angle-average approximation introduced by Baldo et al. The results enable more efficient numerical solution of the triplet pairing problem and establish a foundation for future work incorporating realistic in-medium interactions, which the authors note is necessary for quantitative predictions of nucleonic superfluidity in neutron stars.
