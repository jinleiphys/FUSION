# Wave-function entropy and dynamical-symmetry breaking in the interacting boson model

> The degree of chaos in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-1) is compared with what we call the "dynamical-symmetry content" of the system. The latter is represented by the information entropy of the eigenfunctions with respect to bases associated with dynamical symmetries of the IBM-1, and expresses thus the localization of actual eigenfunctions in these symmetry bases. The wave-function entropy is shown to be a sensitive tool for monitoring the processes of a single dynamical-symmetry breaking or transitions between two and more symmetries. For the IBM-1 hamiltonians studied here, the known features related to chaos, namely the dependence of chaotic measures on the hamiltonian parameters (position in the Casten triangle) and on the angular momentum, turn out to be correlated with the behaviour of the wave-function entropy. PACs 21.60.Fw; 05.45.+b

## Key claim

The paper shows that the wave-function entropy, when averaged over eigenstates and normalized to random-matrix expectations, provides a continuous measure of how close a given IBM-1 Hamiltonian is to any of the five dynamical symmetries (U(5), SU(3), SO(6), SU(3)*, SO(6)*), and that this entropy-based measure correlates with known chaotic properties of the model across the Casten triangle.

## Method

The authors compute the Shannon information entropy of each eigenfunction of the IBM-1 Hamiltonian (Eq. 1, with parameters η and χ) with respect to reference bases associated with each of the five dynamical-symmetry chains. They average this entropy over all eigenstates of fixed angular momentum L and boson number N, then normalize the result using the average entropy of Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) eigenvectors of the same dimension, defining an entropy ratio r^B. The product R of the five ratios (one per symmetry) is compared to standard chaotic measures from prior work by Alhassid, Whelan, and Novoselsky.

## Key numbers

* The U(5) dynamical symmetry is reached at η=1; the SU(3) symmetry at η=0, χ=-√7/2; the SO(6) symmetry at η=0, χ=0.
* A "phase transition" from spherical to deformed shape occurs at η=4/5 in the classical potential.
* The GOE average entropy ⟨W⟩_GOEn is approximately ln(0.482 n) for large dimensions n (Eq. 3).
* The fraction f_deg (Eq. 2) quantifies basis ambiguity due to missing labels; for example, in the SU(3) and SU(3)* limits, f_deg values are shown in Table II (not reproduced here) and are described as "relatively small."
* The entropy ratio r^B (Eq. 4) is defined as (exp(W^B)-1)/(exp(⟨W⟩_GOEn)-1), with a lower bound of 0.
* The product R (Eq. 5) of the five entropy ratios is used to compare with chaotic measures; results are shown for boson numbers N=10 and N=20, and for angular momenta including L=0.

## Context

This work extends the studies of quantum chaos in the Interacting Boson Model by Alhassid, Whelan, and Novoselsky (mid-1990s), who mapped classical and quantum chaotic signatures across the Casten triangle. The authors introduce wave-function entropy as a tool to connect the degree of chaos to the breaking of dynamical symmetries, a link that had been previously suggested but not quantified. The approach enables future studies to identify which dynamical symmetry (or combination of symmetries) a given transitional Hamiltonian is closest to, and to predict regions of regularity or chaos based on entropy measures rather than spectral statistics alone.
