# Transversity Generalized Parton Distributions from gamma N -> pi rhot N' with a Large pi rhot Invariant Mass

> The chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions of the nucleon can be accessed experimentally through the exclusive photoproduction process gamma N -> pi rhoT N', with or without beam and target polarization, provided the vector meson is produced in a transversely polarized state. The kinematical domain of factorization is defined through a large invariant mass of the meson pair and a small transverse momentum of the final nucleon. We calculate perturbatively the scattering amplitude at leading order in alpha_s and build a simple model for the dominant transversity GPD H_T(x, xi, t) based on the concept of double distribution. Counting rate estimates are in progress.

## Key claim

The chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon can be accessed experimentally through the exclusive photoproduction process \(\gamma N \to \pi \rho_T N'\), provided the vector meson is produced in a transversely polarized state and the invariant mass of the meson pair is large enough to justify a leading-twist factorization; the authors calculate the scattering amplitude at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) but note that counting rate estimates are still in progress.

## Method

The authors factorize the scattering amplitude for \(\gamma N \to \pi \rho_T N'\) at large invariant mass \(M_{\pi\rho}^2\) and small transverse momentum of the final nucleon, using the collinear factorization framework for exclusive scattering at fixed angle and large energy. They compute the hard scattering subprocess at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) by evaluating 62 Feynman diagrams (reduced by symmetry), convoluting the hard part with the chiral-odd distribution amplitude of the transversely polarized \(\rho\) meson, the pion distribution amplitude, and a double-distribution model for the dominant transversity GPD \(H_T(x,\xi,t)\). Integrations over the meson momentum fractions \(v\) and \(z\) are performed analytically, while the integration over the GPD variable \(x\) is done numerically.

## Key numbers

- The chiral-odd decay constant for the transversely polarized \(\rho\) meson is \(f_\rho^\bot = 160\) MeV.
- The skewness parameter \(\xi\) is related to the meson pair invariant mass by \(\xi = \tau/(2-\tau)\) with \(\tau = M_{\pi\rho}^2 / (S_{\gamma N} - M^2)\).
- The authors estimate that with the JLab 12 GeV upgrade luminosity of \(\mathcal{L} \sim 10^{35}\) cm\(^2\) s\(^{-1}\), a sufficient number of events per year is expected to extract the dominant transversity GPD.

## Context

This work extends the strategy initiated by Ivanov et al. (2004) for accessing chiral-odd GPDs through hard exclusive production of meson pairs, moving from diffractive two-vector-meson production to photoproduction of a pion and a transversely polarized rho meson. It contrasts with earlier attempts to access transversity GPDs through one-meson electroproduction, which are insensitive at leading twist, and builds on model calculations of chiral-odd GPDs by Scopetta (2004), Pasquini (2005), and others, as well as lattice calculations of their moments. The results enable future experimental extraction of transversity GPDs at facilities like JLab 12 GeV, providing a new probe of the three-dimensional chiral-odd quark content of the nucleon.
