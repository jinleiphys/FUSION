# The Lattice NJL Model at Non-zero Baryon and Isospin Densities

> We present initial results of a numerical investigation of the chiral symmetry restoring transition in the (3+1)-dimensional Nambu -- Jona-Lasinio model with both non-zero baryon chemical potential (mu_B) and isospin chemical potential (mu_I). With non-zero isospin chemical potential, the model suffers from a sign problem. We proceed in two ways: (i) We perform ``partially quenched'' simulations in which mu_I is made non-zero only during the measurement of chiral observables; (ii) We perform full simulations with imaginary isospin chemical potential with the aim to analytically continue results to real mu_I.

## Key claim

The paper presents initial numerical results showing that the chiral symmetry restoring transition in the lattice NJL model can be studied at non-zero isospin chemical potential using either a partially quenched approximation or analytic continuation from imaginary isospin chemical potential, though the results are preliminary and the method for analytic continuation requires further validation across different values of baryon chemical potential and lattice volumes.

## Method

The authors simulate the (3+1)-dimensional lattice NJL model on a 12^4 lattice using two approaches to circumvent the sign problem that arises with real isospin chemical potential: (i) partially quenched simulations where isospin chemical potential is set to zero during gauge field generation and turned on only during measurement of chiral observables; (ii) full simulations with imaginary isospin chemical potential, where the fermion determinant is real, followed by Taylor expansion of observables in powers of the imaginary isospin chemical potential and analytic continuation to real values. The up and down quark condensates, baryon number density, and isospin number density are measured as functions of baryon chemical potential and isospin chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- At mu_B = 400 MeV with weak equilibrium and charge neutrality enforced, the predicted Fermi momenta are: k_F^u = 355.5 MeV, k_F^d = 444.5 MeV, k_F^e = 89 MeV
- Simulations are performed on a 12^4 lattice at mu_B = 0.6 (in lattice units), where the effect of non-zero mu_I is largest
- The real parts of the condensates are fit to constant + quadratic forms in (tilde_mu_I / mu_B), while the imaginary parts are fit to linear-only forms
- The isospin density fit requires inclusion of a cubic term in (tilde_mu_I / mu_B) for good quality

## Context

This work extends prior lattice studies of the NJL model with non-zero baryon chemical potential by Hands (2004) to include separate Fermi surfaces for up and down quarks via isospin chemical potential, motivated by conditions expected in compact star cores where charge neutrality and weak equilibrium force different Fermi momenta (Alford, 1999). The partially quenched results qualitatively agree with mean-field studies by Toublan (2003) and Barducci (2004). The analytic continuation method adapts techniques developed for QCD with small baryon chemical potential by de Forcrand and D'Elia (2002) and Allton (2002). Future work aims to repeat the analysis across multiple baryon chemical potential values in both chirally broken and restored phases on various lattice volumes, and to study the response of the diquark condensate to isospin chemical potential.
