# Proton induced spallation reactions in the energy range 0.1 - 10 GeV

> One of considered nowadays scenarios of proton - nucleus reaction is spallation. Its motivation is based on experimental final-state observations of one heavy nucleus, a small number of light fragments and numerous individual nucleons. It is theoretically described as two step process involving energy deposition in the target nucleus and subsequent evaporation. Several models, based on different assumptions, have been constructed in order to describe the spallation process. The main subject of this work is description of global average properties of proton induced spallation reactions in wide range of projectile energy (0.1 - 10 GeV) and mass of target nuclei. This is investigated within Hadron String Dynamics (HSD) model, based on Boltzmann - Uehling - Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation, supplemented by a statistical evaporation model for the second stage of the reaction. It has been shown that spallation is the most probable scenario of proton - nucleus reaction. Proton induced reactions, in considered here incident energy range, are visibly non-invasive processes. Properties of residual nuclei after the first stage of the reaction are weakly dependent on incident energy, but strongly dependent on mass of target. The second stage of the reaction is a competition of evaporation and fission. Results obtained from the HSD model vary smoothly as function of proton incident energy and mass of target, so interpolation of the results is quite feasible.

## Key claim

Spallation is the most probable scenario for proton-nucleus reactions in the 0.1-10 GeV range, but the HSD model calculations systematically underestimate neutron production at forward angles for heavy targets (W, Pb, Th), indicating that an additional intermediate preequilibrium stage is needed beyond the standard two-stage (fast cascade plus evaporation) description.

## Method

The author used the Hadron String Dynamics (HSD) model, based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation, to simulate the first (intranuclear cascade) stage of proton-induced reactions on targets from Al to Th at incident energies from 0.5 to 5.0 GeV. The second (evaporation) stage was treated with a statistical evaporation model (GEM). For deuteron production, a coalescence mechanism was tested using relative momentum and position conditions on nucleon pairs from the first stage.

## Key numbers

* Neutron multiplicities from the first stage for p(1.2 GeV)+Al: 1.65; for p(1.2 GeV)+Pb: 1.96; for p(1.2 GeV)+Th: 2.00
* Neutron multiplicities from the second stage for p(1.2 GeV)+Al: 1.20; for p(1.2 GeV)+Pb: 21.31; for p(1.2 GeV)+Th: 25.80
* In peripheral (b=5 fm) collisions of 2.5 GeV p+Au, a highly excited group of about 10 nucleons had average total momentum 560(+/-80) MeV/c, while the less excited part of about 184 nucleons had average total momentum 190(+/-60) MeV/c
* The smaller excited source had velocity beta ~ 0.025 c; the larger source had beta ~ 0.0012 c
* Deviations of maximal nuclear density from standard density were of order a few percent for p+Au collisions at 0.5-5.0 GeV
* Deuteron spectra from coalescence of two fast nucleons with relative momentum <= 0.40 GeV/c described the high-energy part of experimental data
* Fermi energy of target nucleons was approximately 35 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of the HSD model to spallation reactions across a wide range of target masses and incident energies, building on the BUU-based approach. The finding that neutron spectra for heavy targets are underestimated at forward angles points to the need for a preequilibrium stage, as also suggested by experimental analyses from the PISA experiment (Bubak, 2007) and the NESSI collaboration (Letourneau, 2002). The parametrization of residual nuclei distributions enables interpolation to other energies and targets not directly simulated.
