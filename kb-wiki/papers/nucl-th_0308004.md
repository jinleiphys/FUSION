# Relativistic Nucleon-Nucleon potentials using Dirac's constraint instant form dynamics

> The formalism of two coupled Dirac equations within constraint instant form dynamics is used to study the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The salient features and the final Schroedinger type equation is given. Explicitly energy dependent coupled channel potentials, for use in partial wave Schroedinger like equations, with nonlinear and complicated derivative terms, result. We developed the necessary numerics and study np and pp scattering phase shifts for energies 0-3 GeV and the deuteron bound state. The interactions are inspired by meson exchange of pi, eta, rho,omega and sigma mesons for which we adjust coupling constants. This yields, in the first instant, high quality fits to the Arndt phase shifts 0-300 MeV. Second, the potentials show a universal, independent from angular momentum, core potential which is generated from the relativistic meson exchange dynamics. Extrapolations towards higher energies, up to 3 GeV, allow to separate a QCD dominated short range zone as well as inelastic nucleon excitation mechanism contributing to meson production. A local and/or nonlocal optical model, in addition to the meson exchange Dirac potential, produces agreement between theoretical and data phase shifts. Third, the 1S0, 3P0 and 3P1 partial waves elicit a fusion/scission, for T-Lab<1GeV, and a fusion/fission, for T-Lab>1 GeV, mechanism for intermediate dibaryon formation.

# Relativistic Nucleon-Nucleon potentials using Dirac's constraint instant form dynamics

## Key claim
The authors develop relativistic nucleon-nucleon potentials from two coupled Dirac equations within constraint instant form dynamics, and show that the resulting energy-dependent coupled-channel potentials produce high-quality fits to Arndt phase shifts from 0-300 MeV, while also revealing a universal, angular-momentum-independent core potential from relativistic meson exchange that extends to 3 GeV. However, the agreement with data up to 3 GeV requires an additional local and/or nonlocal optical model beyond the meson exchange Dirac potential, and the fits are limited to the first instant of adjusting coupling constants.

## Method
The formalism of two coupled Dirac equations within constraint instant form dynamics was used to derive a Schroedinger-type equation with explicitly energy-dependent coupled channel potentials containing nonlinear and derivative terms. The interactions were inspired by meson exchange of pi, eta, rho, omega, and sigma mesons with adjusted coupling constants, and the authors developed numerical methods to study np and pp scattering phase shifts from 0-3 GeV as well as the deuteron bound state.

## Key numbers
- Phase shift fits achieved for energies 0-300 MeV using Arndt phase shift data as reference
- Energy range extended up to 3 GeV for studying QCD-dominated short range zone and inelastic nucleon excitation
- T-Lab < 1 GeV: 1S0, 3P0, and 3P1 partial waves exhibit a fusion/scission mechanism for intermediate dibaryon formation
- T-Lab > 1 GeV: 1S0, 3P0, and 3P1 partial waves exhibit a fusion/fission mechanism for intermediate dibaryon formation

## Context
This work extends the application of Dirac's constraint instant form dynamics to the nucleon-nucleon interaction, building on previous meson exchange potential approaches. The universal core potential generated from relativistic meson exchange dynamics represents a new feature not present in non-relativistic potential models. The separation of a QCD-dominated short range zone at energies up to 3 GeV enables future studies of inelastic nucleon excitation mechanisms and meson production, while the dibaryon formation mechanisms in specific partial waves open new directions for investigating intermediate resonance structures in the nucleon-nucleon system.
