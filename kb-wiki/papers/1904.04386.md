# Neutron star properties: Constraining the nuclear matter EoS

> We examine the influence of the density dependence of the symmetry energy on several properties of neutron stars. In particular, we study the constraints set on the nuclear matter equation of state by the values of the tidal deformability and neutron star radius, using a diverse set of relativistic and non-relativistic mean field models consistent with bulk properties of finite nuclei and the observed lower bound on the maximum mass of neutron star. The tidal deformability and radius show a strong correlation with specific linear combinations of the isoscalar and isovector nuclear matter parameters associated with the EoS. Such correlations suggest that a precise value of the radius or the tidal deformability can put tight bounds on several EoS parameters, in particular, on the slope of the incompressibility and the curvature of the symmetry energy. We show that the density dependence of the symmetry energy has a direct influence on the amount of strangeness inside cold dense matter and, consequently, on the direct Urca process and cooling of neutron stars. We explain the low luminosity of SAX 1808.4-3658 as a result of hyperonic direct Urca processes. Finally, we discuss the strong influence of the density dependence of the symmetry energy on the extension of the crust-core transition region of a magnetized neutron star. The increase of the crust and its of complexity, due to the magnetic field effect, may have a role on the glitch mechanism or on the magnetic field decay.

# Neutron star properties: Constraining the nuclear matter EoS

## Key claim
The density dependence of the symmetry energy strongly influences neutron star radius, tidal deformability, hyperonic content, and crust-core transition properties, but the correlations between individual EoS parameters and neutron star observables are generally only moderate; it is specific linear combinations of parameters (such as M0 + η L0 or M0 + η Ksym,0) that show strong correlations across a wide range of neutron star masses, enabling constraints on the slope of the incompressibility and the curvature of the symmetry energy.

## Method
The study uses a diverse set of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (including NL and DD classes) and Skyrme forces, all consistent with bulk properties of finite nuclei and the observed lower bound on the maximum neutron star mass of 2 solar masses. Pearson correlation coefficients are calculated between neutron star properties (radius, tidal deformability, Love number) and EoS parameters or linear combinations thereof, evaluated at saturation density. For hyperonic stars, the TM1 model with an extra ωρ coupling term is used to vary the symmetry energy slope, with hyperon-meson couplings constrained by hypernuclei properties. The crust-core transition in magnetized matter is analyzed using the dynamical spinodal formalism within the relativistic Vlasov equation.

## Key numbers
- The slope of the symmetry energy L0 is varied in the range 40 < L0 < 80 MeV, as suggested by Tsang (2012), Lattimer (2013), and Oertel (2017).
- The neutron star radius for a 1.4 solar mass star is constrained to 11.09 ≲ R1.4 ≲ 12.86 km.
- Using the linear combination M0 + η Ksym,0 together with bounds on Λ1.4 from GW170817, the slope of the incompressibility M0 is constrained to 1926 < M0 < 3768 MeV and the curvature of the symmetry energy Ksym,0 to -140 < Ksym,0 < 16 MeV.
- For the TM1ωρ family, the symmetry energy slope L varies in the range 55 < L < 110 MeV.
- For a magnetic field of B = 4.4 × 10^17 G and L ~ 90 MeV, the crust-core transition region has a thickness of 0.06 fm^-3 and more than 1 km; for L = 60 MeV, this reduces to 0.03 fm^-3 and 400 m.
- The Λ hyperon onset mass is essentially independent of L and equal to 1.3 solar masses.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of correlations between neutron star properties and EoS parameters previously studied by Alam (2016) and Tuhin (2018), and builds on constraints from the GW170817 gravitational wave event (Abbott 2017, 2018). The hyperonic star analysis addresses the "hyperon puzzle" raised by the two-solar-mass neutron star measurements (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013), showing that hyperons can be present if a hard enough EoS or adequate hyperon-meson couplings are chosen, consistent with Bednarek (2011) and Weissenborn (2012, 2013). The explanation of the low luminosity of SAX J1808.4-3658 via hyperonic direct Urca processes provides an alternative to the earlier explanation by Yakovlev (2004) that required a quite hard EoS and the complete baryonic octet. The magnetized crust-core transition analysis extends the work of Fang (2016, 2017) and has implications for the glitch mechanism (Andersson 2012, Chamel 2013) and magnetic field decay (Pons 2013).
