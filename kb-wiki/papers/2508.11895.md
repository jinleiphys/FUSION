# In-medium heavy quark-antiquark $T$-matrix without partial wave expansion

> The T-matrix of the heavy quark-antiquark ($Q\bar{Q}$) pair interacting through a screened Cornell potential in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is computed without employing the partial wave expansion. This is compared with the results obtained using the conventional method of partial wave expansion. Through a comprehensive survey over a range of screening mass and center-of-mass energy, which, when combined, determine the orbital angular momentum involved in the scattering through the associated force range and incident momentum, it is demonstrated that a substantial number of partial wave terms are necessary to precisely match the full scattering amplitude directly obtained from the method without partial wave expansion. For moderate screening masses (corresponding to one to two times the crossover transition temperature) and center-of-mass energy, the number of partial waves required for satisfactory matching turns out as large as 10-20, substantially larger than what one would expect based on simply measuring the magnitudes of the first few partial wave amplitudes. This highlights the efficiency of the new method in directly obtaining the full scattering amplitude needed for further computation of phenomenological observables. We also employ the new method to calculate the T-matrix at center-of-mass energies below the $Q\bar{Q}$ mass threshold, and demonstrate the presence of bound states of different orbital quantum numbers simultaneously in a single energy scan.

# In-medium heavy quark-antiquark T-matrix without partial wave expansion

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the conventional partial wave expansion (PWE) method for computing the heavy quark-antiquark T-matrix in quark-gluon plasma requires a substantially larger number of partial wave terms (10-20) than one would expect from simply measuring the magnitudes of the first few partial wave amplitudes, particularly for moderate screening masses corresponding to one to two times the crossover transition temperature. The new method without partial wave expansion (no-PWE) directly obtains the full scattering amplitude more efficiently and can simultaneously accommodate bound states of different orbital quantum numbers in a single energy scan.

## Method

The authors solve the three-dimensionally reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation (Thompson form) for the heavy quark-antiquark T-matrix using a screened Cornell potential without employing partial wave expansion, extending the no-PWE method previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering in vacuum (Elster, 1998). They compare these results with the conventional PWE method using the Haftel-Tabakin algorithm, solving the T-matrix equation as a matrix inversion problem after discretizing momentum and angular variables with Gauss-Legendre quadratures. The screening mass is varied over a range corresponding to temperatures from approximately the crossover temperature to three times that temperature, and the center-of-mass energy is varied above and below the heavy quark-antiquark mass threshold.

## Key numbers

- For screening mass m_D = 0.25 GeV, up to l = 30 PWE terms are needed to match the no-PWE result for the real part of the onshell T-matrix at E_CM = 4.5 GeV
- For screening mass m_D = 0.5 GeV, at least l = 18 PWE terms are required for precise matching of the real part at E_CM = 4.5 GeV
- For screening mass m_D = 0.75 GeV, at least l = 10 PWE terms are needed for the real part at E_CM = 4.5 GeV
- For the largest screening mass considered, m_D = 1.5 GeV, only l = 5 PWE terms are needed at E_CM = 4.5 GeV
- The imaginary part requires fewer terms: for m_D = 0.25 GeV, l = 18 terms; for m_D = 0.5 GeV, l = 10 terms; for m_D = 0.75 GeV, l = 6 terms
- The Cornell potential parameters used are alpha = 0.471 and sigma = 0.192 GeV^2, with bare charm quark mass M_c^0 = 1.32 GeV
- The onshell momenta considered are around q_0 ~ 1.5 GeV, typical of near-thermalized charm quark momentum
- The scaling parameter for Gauss-Legendre quadrature momentum integration is b = 0.275 GeV

## Context

This work extends the no-PWE method from nucleon-nucleon scattering in vacuum (Elster, 1998) to the in-medium heavy quark-antiquark system in quark-gluon plasma, where previous T-matrix calculations (Mannarelli, 2005; Cabrera, 2006; van Hees, 2007; Riek, 2010; Liu, 2017) have exclusively employed the partial wave expansion method. The approach enables more efficient computation of the full scattering amplitude needed for phenomenological observables such as the heavy quark diffusion coefficient (He, 2023), and for the first time demonstrates the simultaneous resolution of bound states with different orbital quantum numbers in a single energy scan. Future work will incorporate coupling to the one-body heavy quark self-energy equation for a self-consistent treatment.
