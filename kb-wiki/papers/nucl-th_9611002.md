# Meson-baryon coupling constants from a chiral-invariant SU(3) Lagrangian and application to NN scattering

> We present a chiral-invariant meson-baryon Lagrangian which describes the interactions of the baryon octet with the lowest-mass meson nonets. The nonlinear realization of the chiral symmetry generates pair-meson interaction vertices. The corresponding pair-meson coupling constants can all be expressed in terms of the meson-nucleon-nucleon pseudovector, scalar, and vector coupling constants, and their corresponding $F/(F+D)$ ratios, and for which empirical estimates are given. We show that it is possible to construct an NN potential of reasonable quality satisfying these theoretical and empirical constraints.

## Key claim

The authors claim that it is possible to construct a nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential of reasonable quality that simultaneously satisfies both the constraints of chiral symmetry and empirical estimates for meson-nucleon coupling constants, but only after introducing an extension to the interaction Lagrangian that adds new free parameters without abandoning the empirical constraints on single-meson coupling constants.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral-invariant SU(3) meson-baryon Lagrangian using a nonlinear realization of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, expressing all pair-meson coupling constants in terms of single-meson vertex parameters (pseudovector, scalar, and vector coupling constants and their F/(F+D) ratios). They then applied this Lagrangian to construct an NN potential by evaluating one-boson-exchange contributions together with box, crossed-box, and pair-meson diagrams up to order 1/M² in the nucleon mass, fixing the coupling constants using theoretical and experimental input rather than fitting them to scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is fπ = 92.4 ± 0.3 MeV (from Particle Data Group)
- The weak interaction axial-vector coupling constant is gA = 1.2601 ± 0.0025
- The ρ meson mass is mρ = 770 MeV and the a1 meson mass is ma1 = 1.23 GeV
- The ρNN coupling constant is gV = 5.04 (from ρ⁰ → e⁺e⁻ decay)
- The ρππ coupling constant is gρππ = 6.05 (from ρ → π⁺π⁻ decay)
- The scalar ε(760) meson has a width Γε ≈ 800 MeV
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation holds experimentally within about 2%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Rij96a, Rij96b) where two-meson exchange contributions (box, crossed-box, and pair diagrams) were shown to improve NN scattering descriptions compared to potentials with only one-boson exchanges. The paper builds on the Nijmegen group's earlier hard-core (Nagels 1977) and soft-core (Maessen 1989) hyperon-nucleon one-boson-exchange models, which used SU(3) symmetry to fix coupling constants. The approach contrasts with the chiral perturbation theory method of Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1996), who found it impractical for potential models to compete with more phenomenological approaches. The work enables the future construction of extended hyperon-nucleon potentials with reduced free parameters, which is important for hypernuclei studies where experimental data are scarce and imprecise.
