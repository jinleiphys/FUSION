# Polarized jet fragmentation functions

> We develop the theoretical framework needed to study the distribution of hadrons with general polarization inside jets, with and without transverse momentum measured with respect to the standard jet axis. The key development in this paper, referred to as "polarized jet fragmentation functions", opens up new opportunities to study both collinear and transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions. As two examples of the developed framework, we study longitudinally polarized collinear $Λ$ and transversely polarized TMD $Λ$ production inside jets in both $pp$ and $ep$ collisions. We find that both observables have high potential in constraining spin-dependent fragmentation functions with sizeable asymmetries predicted, in particular, at the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim
The paper develops a theoretical framework for "polarized jet fragmentation functions" that describes the distribution of hadrons with general polarization inside jets, claiming that measuring longitudinally polarized Λ and transversely polarized Λ production inside jets at the future Electron-Ion Collider can discriminate between different scenarios of spin-dependent fragmentation functions that current LEP data cannot distinguish.

## Method
The authors define correlators in Soft Collinear Effective Theory for quark and gluon jets that include the spin of both the initiating parton and the observed hadron, parametrizing them into transverse momentum dependent jet fragmentation functions (TMDJFFs) and their collinear counterparts. They derive the matching relations between these polarized JFFs and standard fragmentation functions, including the next-to-leading order matching coefficients for longitudinally polarized JFFs using the anti-kT jet algorithm. They then apply the framework to compute spin-dependent structure functions for single inclusive jet production in pp and ep collisions.

## Key numbers
- Jet radius parameter R is used throughout, with typical jet scale μJ ~ pJT R
- Longitudinally polarized JFF matching coefficients J^L_ij are given at NLO with terms proportional to αs/(2π) and ln(μ^2/(pJT^2 R^2))
- The anti-kT algorithm term for longitudinally polarized JFFs is Δ_L I_ji^{anti-kT}(zh) = 2 Δ_L P_ji(zh) ln zh
- The longitudinal spin transfer D_LL measurements by STAR at RHIC were binned in two rapidity bins: -1.2 < ηJ < 0 and 0 < ηJ < 1.2 (Adam 2018)

## Context
This work extends the unpolarized jet fragmentation function framework developed by Kang (2016) and Kang (2017) to include spin degrees of freedom, and generalizes the TMDJFF formalism from Kang (2017) to polarized hadrons. It enables discrimination between different helicity fragmentation function scenarios proposed by deFlorian (1997) that equally describe LEP data from Buskulic (1996), by using longitudinal spin transfer measurements at RHIC and the future EIC. The framework also provides a pathway to study transverse Λ polarization via the TMD polarizing fragmentation function D_{1T}^{⊥} in unpolarized collisions at LHC, RHIC, and EIC.
