# Enhanced Dimer Relaxation in an Atomic/Molecular BEC

> We derive a universal formula for the rate constant βfor relaxation of a shallow dimer into deeply-bound diatomic molecules in the case of atoms with a large scattering length a. We show that βis determined by a and by two 3-body parameters that also determine the binding energies and widths of Efimov states. The rate constant βscales like \hbar a/m near the resonance, but the coefficient is a periodic function of ln(a) that may have resonant enhancement at values of a that differ by multiples of 22.7.

## Key claim

The dimer relaxation rate constant β scales like ħa/m near a Feshbach resonance, but the coefficient is a periodic function of ln(a) that can exhibit resonant enhancement at values of a differing by multiples of 22.7, and depends on two 3-body parameters that also determine the binding energies and widths of Efimov states.

## Method

The authors derived a universal expression for the dimer relaxation rate constant β using hyperspherical methods combined with results from effective field theory, exploiting unitarity constraints in both the short-distance region (R ∼ ℓ) and the long-distance region (R ∼ a). They generalized Efimov's radial law to include the effects of deeply-bound diatomic molecules by introducing a complex 3-body parameter η* that describes the loss of probability into high-energy atom-molecule scattering states, and obtained β from the imaginary part of the atom-dimer scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The dimer relaxation rate constant β = [20.3 sinh(2η*)] / [sin²(s₀ ln(aΛ*) - 1.48) + sinh² η*] × (ħ a / m), where s₀ ≈ 1.00624 and the period in ln(a) is π/s₀ ≈ ln(22.7)
- For large η*, β approaches a constant value of approximately 40.5 ħ a / m
- The numerical constants for the atom-dimer scattering length are b₁ = 1.46, b₀ = 2.15, and φ = 0.09, as calculated by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999)
- The 3-body recombination rate constant for a > 0 into shallow dimers is α_shallow = 16.8 (ħ a⁴ / m) × (1 + e⁻⁴η* - 2e⁻²η* cos[2s₀ ln(aΛ*) + 0.38])
- The 3-body recombination rate constant for a > 0 into deep molecules is α_deep = 16.8 (ħ a⁴ / m) × (1 - e⁻⁴η*)
- The 3-body recombination rate constant for a < 0 into deep molecules is α_deep = [4590 sinh(2η*)] / [sin²(s₀ ln(|a| Λ*) - 1.38) + sinh² η*] × (ħ a⁴ / m)

## Context

This work extends Efimov's (1971) radial law for atom-dimer scattering to include the effects of deeply-bound diatomic molecules, which are present in heavy alkali atoms such as Rb, Na, and Cs. The universal expressions for the dimer relaxation rate and 3-body recombination rates (previously studied by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) for the case without deep molecules, and by Braaten and Hammer (2001) for infinitesimal η*) can be used in mean-field descriptions of Bose-Einstein condensates near Feshbach resonances to account for atom loss, and could enable the determination of the 3-body parameters Λ* and η* from experimental data, allowing predictions of Efimov state binding energies and widths.
