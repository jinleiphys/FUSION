# Nonperturbative Effects in Energy Correlators: From Characterizing Confinement Transition to Improving $α_s$ Extraction

> Energy correlators provide a powerful observable to study fragmentation dynamics in QCD. We demonstrate that the leading nonperturbative corrections for projected $N$-point energy correlators are described by the same universal parameter for any $N$, which has already been determined from other event shape fits. Including renormalon-free nonperturbative corrections substantially improves theoretical predictions of energy correlators, notably the transition into the confining region at small angles. Nonperturbative corrections are shown to have a significant impact on $α_s$ extractions.

## Key claim

The leading nonperturbative corrections for projected N-point energy correlators (pENCs) are described by the same universal parameter Omega_{1q} for any N, which has already been determined from other event shape fits; including renormalon-free nonperturbative corrections substantially improves theoretical predictions, notably the transition into the confining region at small angles, and these corrections have a significant impact on alpha_s extractions that is much larger than Monte Carlo estimates suggest.

## Method

The authors derived the leading power correction for pENCs from e+e- collisions, showing it depends on the universal hadronic parameter Omega_{1q} with a factor N/(2^N) and angular dependence 1/(x_L(1-x_L))^{3/2}. They removed the renormalon ambiguity by converting from the MSbar scheme to the R scheme using subtraction scale R, with coefficients d_{kappa n} chosen to eliminate the u=1/2 renormalon pole. They applied this to fixed-order calculations at O(alpha_s) and to the jet functions in the collinear factorization theorem, then compared results with Pythia and Herwig Monte Carlo simulations and OPAL data.

## Key numbers

- Omega_{1q}(R=2 GeV) = 0.658 GeV, obtained from the thrust fit value of 0.323 GeV from Abbate (2010) plus 23% hadron mass corrections following Mateu (2012) and Schindler (2023)
- For the EEC at Q=m_Z, the peak location relation gives 2*Omega_{1q}/(Q*sqrt(x_{L,peak})) approx 2/3, predicting peak locations for higher N via the N/2 relation
- Nonperturbative corrections to the pE3C/EEC ratio slope decrease from ~20% at lower Q to ~5% at higher Q, compared to the ~0-3% effect estimated by Monte Carlo generators
- At Q=1000 GeV, including nonperturbative corrections is a ~10% effect in the direction of decreasing alpha_s

## Context

This work extends the pioneering analysis of power corrections for the energy-energy correlator (EEC) by Korchemsky (1999) and the renormalon analysis by Schindler (2023) to all N-point energy correlators. It builds on the thrust fit results of Abbate (2010) and the R scheme formalism of Hoang (2007, 2008, 2009). The results directly impact the CMS alpha_s extraction from pE3C/EEC ratios (CMS 2024), which achieved alpha_s(m_Z)=0.123^{+0.004}_{-0.005} using Monte Carlo modeling of hadronization; this work shows those Monte Carlo estimates significantly underestimate nonperturbative effects. The findings enable parameter-free predictions for pENCs across all N and motivate revisiting alpha_s determinations using model-independent nonperturbative corrections, particularly for upcoming LEP data reanalyses and measurements at LHC, RHIC, and EIC.
