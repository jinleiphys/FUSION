# Role of the Landau-Migdal Parameters with the Pseudovector and the Tensor Coupling in Relativistic Nuclear Models -- The Quenching of the Gamow-Teller Strength --

> Role of the Landau-Migdal parameters with the pseudovector ($g_a$) and the tensor coupling ($g_t$) is examined for the giant Gamow-Teller (GT) states in the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA). The excitation energy is dominated by both $g_a$ and $g_t$ in a similar way, while the GT strength is independent of $g_a$ and $g_t$ in the RPA of the nucleon space, and is quenched, compared with that in non-relativistic one. The coupling of the particle-hole states with nucleon-antinucleon states is expected to quench the GT strength further through $g_a$.

# Role of the Landau-Migdal Parameters with the Pseudovector and the Tensor Coupling in Relativistic Nuclear Models -- The Quenching of the Gamow-Teller Strength --

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) within the nucleon space is independent of both the pseudovector ($g_a$) and tensor ($g_t$) Landau-Migdal parameters, and is quenched by a factor of $(1-2v_F^2/3)$ compared to the Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita (IFF) sum rule value, regardless of which coupling is used. The excitation energy, however, depends on both parameters in a similar way, and the quenching may be further increased by coupling particle-hole states with nucleon-antinucleon states through the pseudovector coupling.

## Method

The authors constructed relativistic Landau-Migdal parameters with pseudovector ($g_a$) and tensor ($g_t$) contact couplings in the nuclear Lagrangian, chosen to reproduce the non-relativistic Landau-Migdal parameter $g'$ in the non-relativistic limit. They calculated the RPA correlation function for nuclear matter using the no-free-term approximation (NFA), which neglects divergent terms but keeps all nuclear density-dependent terms including Pauli blocking ones, and derived analytic expressions for the excitation energy and strength of giant Gamow-Teller states at zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The non-relativistic Landau-Migdal parameter $g'$ is estimated experimentally to be about 0.6.
- The pion contribution to $g'$ is calculated as $g'_\pi \approx 0.318$ for pion mass $m_\pi = 140$ MeV and Fermi momentum $k_F = 1.36$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The rho-meson contribution $g'_\rho$ is calculated as 0.281 after removing the short-range part of the finite-range interaction.
- The relativistic correction to the GT excitation energy is 14% when using the pseudovector coupling alone.
- The GT strength in the nucleon sector is quenched by 12% in nuclear matter, given by the factor $(1-2v_F^2/3)$, compared to the IFF sum rule value $2(N-Z)$.
- In finite nuclei, the quenched amount is estimated to be about 6%.
- The Tokyo group observed 10% quenching of the IFF sum rule value in $^{90}$Nb through charge-exchange reactions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Maruyama, Kurasawa, Suzuki, 2001-2003) that introduced the pseudovector Landau-Migdal parameter for describing giant GT resonance states in relativistic models, which predicted quenching of the GT strength consistent with recent experiments by Sakai (2000). The present paper examines whether the tensor coupling changes those conclusions, finding that the quenching factor remains the same regardless of which coupling is used. This result distinguishes relativistic models from conventional non-relativistic models where the GT strength is fixed by the IFF sum rule, and it enables future work on understanding the role of nucleon-antinucleon coupling in further quenching, as well as consistent descriptions of pion condensation and charge-exchange reaction response functions.
