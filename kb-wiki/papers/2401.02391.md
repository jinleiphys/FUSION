# Updated numerical study of transverse single-spin asymmetries in single-inclusive pion production from lepton-nucleon collisions

> We revisit the analysis of transverse single-spin asymmetries $A_N$ in lepton-nucleon scattering where only a single pion is detected in the final state, $\ell\,N^\uparrow\to h\, X$. This observable is the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) analogue to $A_N$ in proton-proton collisions, $p^\uparrow p\to h\,X$, that has been studied intensely for decades, especially at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We incorporate new theoretical developments in the collinear twist-3 framework and utilize recent extractions of (Sivers-like and Collins-like) quark-gluon-quark correlators in the numerical computations. We compare our calculations to HERMES measurements as well as make predictions for Jefferson Lab, COMPASS, and EIC kinematics. We further explore the role of next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to the (twist-2) unpolarized cross section (denominator of $A_N$) and consider what can be deduced empirically about the potential numerical significance of the full NLO calculation of $A_N$ in this process. We consider sources of theoretical uncertainty in our predictions, which present potential opportunities then for future measurements to improve our understanding of $A_N$ and multi-parton correlations in hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors revisit the collinear twist-3 (CT3) framework calculation of transverse single-spin asymmetries $A_N$ in lepton-nucleon collisions $\ell N^\uparrow \to \pi X$, incorporating recent extractions of quark-gluon-quark correlators and Lorentz invariance relations, but the central LO predictions generally overshoot the HERMES data, and the full NLO calculation of the polarized numerator remains unavailable, so the agreement with data when NLO unpolarized corrections are included is suggestive but not definitive.

## Method

The authors compute $A_N$ using the CT3 formalism at leading order in $\alpha_s$ and $\alpha_{em}$ for the polarized numerator, which involves Sivers-like ($f_{1T}^{\perp(1)}$) and Collins-like ($H_1^{\perp(1)}$, $\tilde{H}$) twist-3 correlators. For the unpolarized denominator, they use both LO and NLO (in $\alpha_s$) expressions including the Weizsäcker-Williams photon-in-lepton distribution. The non-perturbative inputs are taken from the JAM3D-22 global analysis for the twist-3 functions and from CT18 NLO (unpolarized PDFs) and DSS14 NLO (unpolarized FFs), with uncertainties propagated from the JAM3D-22 extraction.

## Key numbers

- HERMES data at $\sqrt{S}=7.25$ GeV: LO central curves for $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$ generally larger in magnitude than data for $1 < P_T < 2.2$ GeV binning; including NLO unpolarized corrections brings theory into much better agreement.
- JLab 12 GeV predictions ($\sqrt{S}=4.6$ GeV, $\eta=-0.5$): LO $\pi^+$ asymmetry can reach $70-80\%$; with NLO unpolarized corrections it reduces to $\sim 20\%$.
- COMPASS predictions ($\sqrt{S}=17.3$ GeV, $\eta=-1$): LO $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$ asymmetries are $\sim 3\%$ in magnitude and opposite in sign; with NLO unpolarized corrections they become $\sim 1-2\%$.
- EIC predictions: at $\sqrt{S}=29$ GeV, $\eta=0$, LO asymmetries are $\sim 3\%$ for both $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$; at $\sqrt{S}=141$ GeV, $\eta=2$, LO $A_N$ for $\pi^+$ is small up to $P_T \approx 5$ GeV then grows large and positive, while $\pi^-$ trends positive up to $P_T \sim 6$ GeV then turns large and negative.

## Context

This work extends the original CT3 analysis of $\ell N^\uparrow \to \pi X$ by Gamberg et al. (2014) by incorporating Lorentz invariance relations derived by Kanazawa et al. (2015) and using the recent JAM3D-22 global extraction of twist-3 functions from Gamberg et al. (2022). It contrasts with the generalized parton model (GPM) analysis by D'Alesio et al. (2017), which included Weizsäcker-Williams effects and found better agreement with HERMES data. The results enable future measurements at JLab, COMPASS, and the EIC to constrain the poorly-known dynamical twist-3 fragmentation function $\tilde{H}(z)$ and test the applicability of perturbative QCD at lower energies.
