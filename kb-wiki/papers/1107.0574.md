# Coupling vector and pseudoscalar mesons to study baryon resonances

> A study of meson-baryon systems with total strangeness -1 is made within a framework based on the chiral and hidden local symmetries. These systems consist of octet baryons, pseudoscalar and vector mesons. The pseudoscalar meson-baryon (PB) dynamics has been earlier found determinant for the existence of some strangeness -1 resonances, for example, $Λ(1405)$, $Λ(1670)$, etc. The motivation of the present work is to study the effect of coupling the closed vector meson-baryon (VB) channels to these resonances. To do this, we obtain the $PB \rightarrow PB$ and $VB \rightarrow VB$ amplitudes from the t-channel diagrams and the $PB \leftrightarrow VB$ amplitudes are calculated using the Kroll-Ruddermann term where, considering the vector meson dominance phenomena, the photon is replaced by a vector meson. The calculations done within this formalism reveal a very strong coupling of the VB channels to the $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1670)$. In the isospin 1 case, we find an evidence for a double pole structure of the $Σ(1480)$ which, like the isospin 0 resonances, is also found to couple strongly to the VB channels. The strong coupling of these low-lying resonances to the VB channels can have important implications on certain reactions producing them.

## Key claim
The paper claims that coupling closed vector meson-baryon (VB) channels to pseudoscalar meson-baryon (PB) systems produces a very strong coupling of VB channels to the Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) resonances, but this coupling does not significantly alter the pole positions or the PB couplings of these resonances, only changing the amplitude strengths.

## Method
The authors solve coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations for strangeness -1 meson-baryon systems with total isospin 0 and 1, using tree-level amplitudes from chiral and hidden local symmetries. The PB→PB and VB→VB amplitudes are obtained from t-channel diagrams, while PB↔VB transition amplitudes are calculated using a Kroll-Rudermann term where the photon is replaced by a vector meson via vector meson dominance. Loop integrals are regularized using a cutoff method with a Gaussian form factor, with cutoff values of Λ_VB = 545 MeV for VB loops and Λ_PB = 750 MeV for PB loops.

## Key numbers
- The two poles of the Λ(1405) resonance with full PB-VB coupling (g_KR = 6) are found at 1363 - i56 MeV and 1412 - i11 MeV, shifted from 1377 - i63 MeV and 1430 - i15 MeV when PB-VB coupling is zero.
- The coupling constant g_KR is set to 6, derived from the Kawarabayashi-Suzuki-Riazuddin-Fayazuddin relation using f_π = 93 MeV and m_ρ = 770 MeV.
- The cutoff values used are Λ_VB = 545 MeV for vector meson-baryon loops and Λ_PB = 750 MeV for pseudoscalar meson-baryon loops.
- The axial coupling constants used are F = 0.46 and D = 0.8, giving F + D ≈ g_A = 1.26.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of dynamically generated baryon resonances in strangeness -1 systems, particularly the Λ(1405) and Λ(1670), which were previously studied using only PB channels by Kaiser et al. and Jido et al. (2003). It also builds on earlier VB-only studies by the same group (Khemchandani et al., 2011) and by Oset and Ramos (2010). The paper enables future quantitative studies that include more detailed VB interactions, as the simplified t-channel VB model used here is acknowledged as incomplete.
