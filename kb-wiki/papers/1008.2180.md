# Superdeformed Oblate Superheavy Nuclei?

> We study stability of superdeformed oblate (SDO) superheavy $Z\geq 120$ nuclei predicted by systematic macroscopic-microscopic calculations in 12D deformation space and confirmed by the Hartree-Fock calculations with the realistic SLy6 force. We include into consideration high-$K$ isomers that very likely form at the SDO shape. Although half-lives $T_{1/2}\lesssim10^{-5}$ s are calclulated or estimated for even-even spin zero systems, decay hindrances known for high-$K$ isomers suggest that some SDO superheavy nuclei may be detectable by the present experimental technique.

## Key claim
Superdeformed oblate (SDO) minima exist for superheavy nuclei with Z ≥ 120, but even-even spin-zero systems have half-lives ≤ 10⁻⁵ s; however, decay hindrances from high‑K isomers may make some SDO superheavy nuclei detectable with current experimental techniques.

## Method
The authors performed systematic macroscopic‑microscopic calculations in a 12‑dimensional deformation space (including quadrupole, hexadecapole, and higher‑order multipoles) using a deformed Woods‑Saxon potential and the Yukawa‑plus‑exponential macroscopic model. They also repeated minimizations with the LSD liquid‑drop model and with self‑consistent Hartree‑Fock calculations using the Skyrme SLy6 force. Fission half‑lives were computed via the WKB action with cranking mass parameters, and α‑decay half‑lives were estimated using a Viola‑Seaborg type formula.

## Key numbers
- SDO minima occur for quadrupole deformations β₂₀ between −0.4 and −0.5 (axis ratio ≈ 3:2) for Z ≥ 120.
- The largest fission barriers for SDO nuclei are ≈ 3.4 MeV for ²⁸⁶120 and ²⁸⁸122.
- Fission half‑lives along triaxial paths are 10⁻⁶ s for ²⁸⁶120 and 10⁻⁵ s for ²⁸⁸122, with an estimated error of one order of magnitude.
- For the ground‑state to ground‑state α‑decay of ²⁸⁶120, Log(T_α [s]) = −9.1; for the hindered SDO‑to‑SDO transition, Log(T_α [s]) = −5.5.
- The high‑K isomer in ²⁷⁰Ds has a partial α‑decay half‑life of 6.0⁺⁸·²₋₂·₂ ms, compared to the ground‑state half‑life of 100⁺¹⁴⁰₋₄₀ μs.
- β⁺ decay half‑lives for even and odd SDO nuclei are estimated to be of order 0.1–1 s.

## Context
This work extends earlier macroscopic‑microscopic predictions of SDO minima (e.g., the conflicting conclusions in the 2006 paper by Kowal, Skalski, and Sobiczewski) and confirms them with Hartree‑Fock calculations using the SLy6 force. The study also builds on the idea of extra‑stable high‑K shape isomers, advanced by Marinov and collaborators, and on experimental observations of fission hindrance in high‑K isomers in ²⁴⁰–²⁴⁴Cm (Sletten and collaborators). The results suggest that some SDO superheavy nuclei, far from the conventionally expected “island of stability” near N = 174–184, might be detectable if high‑K isomerism provides sufficient decay hindrance.
