# The neutron/proton ratio of squeezed-out nucleons and the high density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy

> Within a transport model it is shown that the neutron/proton ratio of squeezed-out nucleons perpendicular to the reaction plane, especially at high transverse momenta, in heavy-ion reactions induced by high energy neutron-rich nuclei can be a useful tool for studying the high density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

Within a transport model (IBUU04), the neutron/proton ratio of squeezed-out nucleons emitted perpendicular to the reaction plane in heavy-ion collisions of neutron-rich nuclei at high beam energies is shown to be a useful observable for probing the high density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, with the caveat that the effect is most pronounced at high transverse momenta and that the model assumes the MDI interaction with parameter values x=0 and x=-1 at all densities, even though these are only constrained at sub-normal densities.

## Method

The authors used the IBUU04 transport model with a momentum- and isospin-dependent single nucleon potential (MDI) to simulate the reaction of 132Sn + 124Sn at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of 5 fm. They analyzed the azimuthal distribution of midrapidity nucleons and the transverse momentum dependence of the neutron/proton ratio, applying an azimuthal angle cut (80° < φ < 100° and 260° < φ < 280°) to select only squeezed-out nucleons emitted perpendicular to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- The reaction studied was 132Sn + 124Sn at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of 5 fm, reaching a maximal baryon density of about twice normal nuclear matter density (2ρ₀).
- At a transverse momentum of 1 GeV/c, the symmetry energy effect on the n/p ratio of squeezed-out nucleons can be as high as 40%.
- The maximum density reached in the earlier isospin diffusion study of 124Sn + 112Sn at E_beam/A = 50 MeV was about 1.2ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-normal densities, where previous constraints from isospin diffusion (x=0 and x=-1 cases) and neutron-skin thickness measurements only reached densities up to about 1.2ρ₀. The authors compare their proposed observable to other promising probes identified earlier, such as the π⁻/π⁺ ratio (Li 2002, Gaitanos 2004, Li 2005), neutron-proton differential flow (Li 2000, Yong 2006), and the K⁰/K⁺ ratio (Di Toro 2006), noting that the n/p ratio of squeezed-out nucleons carries more direct information about the symmetry energy since it acts directly on nucleons rather than through secondary production mechanisms. The study enables future experimental measurements using existing or planned detector setups, such as coupling the Modular Neutron Array (MoNA) with charged particle detectors at NSCL/MSU or using a new Time-Projection-Chamber with an advanced neutron detector at a radioactive beam facility.
