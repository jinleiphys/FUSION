# Flow effects on multifragmentation in the canonical model

> A prescription to incorporate the effects of nuclear flow on the process of multifragmentation of hot nuclei is proposed in an analytically solvable canonical model. Flow is simulated by the action of an effective negative external pressure. It favors sharpening the signatures of liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei with increased multiplicity and a lowered phase transition temperature.

# Flow effects on multifragmentation in the canonical model

## Key claim
Flow effects on nuclear multifragmentation can be incorporated into an analytically solvable canonical model by simulating flow as an effective negative external pressure, which sharpens signatures of the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei. However, the model's validity is limited to flow energies up to approximately 5 MeV per nucleon, where the thermodynamic equilibration time is small compared to the expansion time scale.

## Method
The authors modify the canonical statistical multifragmentation model of Das et al. (2004) by introducing an effective negative external pressure to simulate collective radial flow. The flow energy per fragment is parametrized as δ i^α T, where δ determines the flow energy per nucleon at temperature T and α (< 1) accounts for the decrease of flow energy per particle with increasing fragment mass. The partition function is calculated using a recursion relation, and observables are computed for ¹⁹⁷Au and ¹⁰⁹Ag systems at constant freeze-out volume (V_f = 6V_0) and at constant flow pressure (P_fl = 0.025 MeV fm⁻³).

## Key numbers
- Flow energy per nucleon at the transition temperature: ~1.6 MeV for δ = 0.5, increasing to ~2.3 MeV for δ = 0.8
- Transition temperature without flow: ~6.7 MeV; with flow (δ = 0.5): ~5.8 MeV
- Constant freeze-out volume used: V_f = 6V_0, where V_0 is the normal volume of the fragmenting system
- Constant flow pressure used: P_fl = 0.025 MeV fm⁻³
- Volume energy term W_0 = 16 MeV; surface tension σ_0 = 18 MeV; critical temperature T_c = 18 MeV; level density parameter a_i = i/16 MeV⁻¹
- Model validity limited to flow energies up to ~5 MeV per nucleon

## Context
This work extends the canonical statistical multifragmentation model of Das et al. (2004) by incorporating collective flow effects that were previously absent. It builds on earlier findings from hydrodynamical models with site-bond percolation (Desbois, 1985), BUU-type formulations (Xu, 1993), and grand canonical thermodynamic calculations (Pal et al., 1997) that showed compression and expansion strongly influence fragment multiplicity. The results enable comparison with experimental multifragmentation data from intermediate energy heavy ion collisions, particularly for caloric curves and IMF multiplicities, and suggest that flow must be accounted for when extracting freeze-out densities from isotopic yield ratios as done by Albergo et al. (1985) and Camaiani et al. (1998).
