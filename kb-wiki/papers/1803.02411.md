# The Deconfinement Phase Transition in Proto-Neutron-Star Matter

> In this work, we study in detail the deconfinement phase transition that takes place in hot/dense nuclear matter in the context of neutron stars and proto-neutron stars (in which lepton fraction is fixed). The possibility of different mixtures of phases with different locally and globally conserved quantities is considered in each case. For this purpose, the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, an effective relativistic model that includes self-consistent chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement to quark matter, is employed. Finally, we compare our results with data provided by PQCD for different temperatures and conditions.

## Key claim
The deconfinement phase transition in proto-neutron-star matter depends critically on whether electric charge and lepton fraction are conserved locally or globally, with the latter allowing mixed-phase regions that are extremely narrow (ranging from about 8 to 30 MeV in baryon chemical potential) compared to the neutron-star case.

## Method
The authors employed the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, an effective relativistic model with SU(3) flavor symmetry that includes self-consistent chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement to quark matter via a scalar field analogous to the Polyakov loop. They solved the grand canonical ensemble equations for baryon octet and three light quark species under five different conservation scenarios: locally or globally conserved electric charge for neutron-star matter, and combinations of locally or globally conserved electric charge and lepton fraction (fixed at 0.4) for proto-neutron-star matter. The model was calibrated to reproduce nuclear saturation properties, lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential, and the location of the critical end-point.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, binding energy B/A = -16 MeV, compressibility K = 300 MeV, asymmetry energy E_sym = 30 MeV with slope L = 88 MeV
- Nuclear liquid-gas critical point: T_c = 16.4 MeV, μ_B,c = 910 MeV
- Critical point for NS LCN (neutron-star locally conserved): T_c = 168.82 MeV, μ̃_c = 230.05 MeV
- Critical point for PNS LCN LY_l (proto-neutron-star locally conserved lepton fraction): T_c = 168.84 MeV, μ̃_c = 241.55 MeV
- Critical point for PNS LCN GY_l (proto-neutron-star globally conserved lepton fraction): T_c = 134.86 MeV, μ_B,c = 900.30 MeV
- Critical point for PNS GCN GY_l (proto-neutron-star globally conserved both): T_c = 150.65 MeV, μ_B,c = 726.65 MeV
- Fixed lepton fraction Y_l = 0.4 for proto-neutron-star matter
- Crossover transition temperature at vanishing chemical potential: 171 MeV
- Critical end-point for symmetric matter: μ_c = 354 MeV, T_c = 167 MeV
- First-order phase transition line terminates at zero temperature at four times saturation density

## Context
This work extends previous studies of deconfinement in neutron-star matter using the CMF model by Dexheimer (2008, 2009) and Hempel (2013) to include the proto-neutron-star regime with fixed lepton fraction. It contrasts with the forced-congruent treatment of lepton fraction (where it is conserved locally despite no long-range force) and instead implements the more physical global conservation scheme. The results enable more realistic equations of state for simulations of proto-neutron-star evolution and cooling, and provide comparisons with perturbative QCD calculations at high temperatures.
