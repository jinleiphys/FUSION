# The unquenched quark model

## Key claim
The unquenched quark model (UQM) introduces explicit quark-antiquark sea pair effects into the constituent quark model via a QCD-inspired 3P0 pair-creation mechanism, and can describe observables like flavor asymmetry and strangeness in the proton as well as self-energy corrections to charmonium and bottomonium spectra. However, the numerical results presented for the proton's strangeness observables are found to be negligible (compatible with zero), and the self-energy corrections to heavy meson masses are small (2-6% for charmonium and 1% for bottomonium).

## Method
The hadron wave function is constructed as a superposition of a zeroth-order valence qqq (for baryons) or qqbar (for mesons) configuration plus a sum over higher Fock components generated by the 3P0 quark-antiquark pair-creation operator T-dagger. The matrix elements of observables are computed on this dressed state, and physical meson masses are obtained by adding self-energy corrections from continuum coupling to the bare potential-model energies.

## Context
This work extends the unitarized quark model approach pioneered by Tornqvist and collaborators and the coupled-channel methods of Van Beveren and Rupp, as well as the Cornell model of Eichten et al. (1974). It also builds on earlier studies of nucleon strangeness content by Geiger (1996) and Bijker (2012), and of baryon-meson loop effects by Capstick and Morel. The UQM enables systematic calculation of observables that depend on sea quark contributions, such as the flavor asymmetry of the proton sea (first observed by NMC at CERN) and the spectroscopy of heavy mesons near open-flavor thresholds like the X(3872) and chi_b(3P).
