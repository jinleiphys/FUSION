# Confinement and the Transverse Lattice

> The status of the transverse lattice formulation of light-front QCD is reviewed. It is explained how confinement arises in this formulation for large lattice spacing. The nonperturbative renormalization procedure is outlined in general and a more detailed discussion is given for the case of $QCD_{2+1}(N_C\rightarrow \infty)$.

## Key claim

The transverse lattice formulation of light-front QCD provides a framework where confinement is "built in" for large lattice spacing, but the continuum limit requires a nonperturbative renormalization procedure that tunes coupling constants to restore rotational invariance; the paper reviews the status of this approach rather than presenting a single new result.

## Method

The transverse lattice discretizes the two transverse spatial coordinates while keeping the longitudinal coordinate and time continuous, using compact SU(N_C) link fields for the transverse gluon components and non-compact gauge fields for the longitudinal direction. The light-front Hamiltonian is derived from this discretized action, and bound states are solved using Monte Carlo techniques based on a random walk in Fock space, combined with ensemble projector methods. For the specific case of QCD_{2+1} in the large-N_C limit, the nonperturbative renormalization procedure involves tuning the coupling constants c_2 and c_4 to approach the critical point where the transverse string tension vanishes in lattice units, while c_g sets the overall mass scale.

## Key numbers

- For QCD_{2+1}(N_C → ∞), the gauge coupling c_g in the 1+1 dimensional sheets is related to the 2+1 dimensional gauge coupling via c_g = g^2/a, carrying dimension L^{-2}.
- Numerical calculations on the transverse lattice for self-interacting scalars were performed on 64 transverse sites using a workstation (Ref. lfepmc).
- Glueball spectra for QCD_{2+1}(N_C → ∞) in the Euclidean formulation are available from extrapolations of numerical results for N_C = 2, 3, 4 (Teper).

## Context

This work extends the transverse lattice approach originally suggested by Bardeen, Pearson, and Rabinovici (BPR) and reviews progress in applying light-front quantization to this hybrid lattice-continuum formulation. The paper describes how Monte Carlo techniques developed for solving transverse lattice Hamiltonians (Burkardt, lfepmc) can be combined with nonperturbative renormalization methods that use coupling constant coherence (ccc) to restore rotational invariance. The approach enables calculation of parton distribution functions and light-like correlation functions from light-front wavefunctions, addressing limitations of Euclidean lattice methods where such quantities are exceedingly difficult to compute.
