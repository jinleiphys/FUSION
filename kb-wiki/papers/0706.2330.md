# Three body resonances in two meson-one baryon systems

> We report four $Σ$'s and three $Λ$'s, in the 1500 - 1800 MeV region, as two meson - one baryon S-wave $(1/2)^+$ resonances. We solve Faddeev equations in the coupled channel approach. The invariant mass of one of the meson-baryon pairs and that of the three particles have been varied and peaks in the squared three body $T$-matrix have been found very close to the existing $S$ = -1, $J^P= 1/2^+$ low lying baryon resonances. The input two-body $t$-matrices for meson-meson and meson-baryon interaction have been calculated by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the potentials obtained in the chiral unitary approach.

## Key claim

The paper reports that four Sigma and three Lambda resonances in the 1500-1800 MeV region can be dynamically generated as S-wave (1/2)+ two meson-one baryon states by solving Faddeev equations with chiral unitary two-body interactions, but the identification relies on peaks in the squared three-body T-matrix that appear "very close to" existing PDG resonances rather than exactly matching their central masses.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in a coupled-channel approach for the total strangeness S=-1, net charge zero configuration, using 22 coupled channels such as π0K-p, π0π0Σ0, and π+π-Λ. The input two-body t-matrices for meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions were calculated by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with potentials from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, regularized with dimensional regularization or a cutoff. The three-body T-matrix was evaluated as a function of the total energy √s and the invariant mass √s23 of one meson-baryon pair, with the squared T-matrix plotted to identify resonance peaks.

## Key numbers

- Σ resonance at √s = 1656 MeV with full width at half maximum ~30 MeV, identified with Σ(1660)
- Σ resonance at √s = 1630 MeV with Γ = 39 MeV, identified with Σ(1620)
- Σ resonance at √s = 1590 MeV with width ~70 MeV, supporting Σ(1560)
- Σ resonance at √s = 1790 MeV with Γ = 24 MeV, supporting Σ(1770)
- Λ resonance at √s = 1568 MeV with width 60 MeV, in the Λ(1600) region
- Λ resonance at √s = 1700 MeV with Γ = 136 MeV, also in the Λ(1600) region
- Λ resonance at √s = 1740 MeV with full width 20 MeV, identified with Λ(1810)
- Theoretical uncertainties: varying the pion decay constant and two-body cutoffs by ~5% changes peak positions by less than 5 MeV from each source, or 7 MeV when summed in quadrature

## Context

This work extends the successful chiral unitary description of low-lying 1/2- hyperon resonances (such as Λ(1405) and Λ(1670), as studied by Jido et al. (2003) and others) to the poorly understood 1/2+ sector, where quark models have faced difficulties (as noted by Riska). The approach builds on earlier applications of chiral dynamics to three-nucleon problems (Epelbaum et al.) and on the finding by Longcola et al. that two-meson clouds give sizable contributions to the 1/2+ baryon antidecuplet spectrum. The results reinforce the PDG suggestion that there may be two P01 states in the Λ(1600) region, and predict a 1/2+ assignment for the Σ(1560) bump. The work enables experimental tests through strong three-body decay channels and radiative decays, as suggested by Michael et al.
