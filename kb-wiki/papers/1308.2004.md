# Scale breaking and fluid dynamics in a dilute two-dimensional Fermi gas

> We study two observables related to the anomalous breaking of scale invariance in a dilute two dimensional Fermi gas, the frequency shift and damping rate of the monopole mode in a harmonic confinement potential. For this purpose we compute the speed of sound and the bulk viscosity of the two dimensional gas in the high temperature limit. We show that the anomaly in the speed of sound scales as $(2P-ρc_s^2)/P\sim z/[\log(T/E_B)]^2$, and that the bulk viscosity $ζ$ scales as $ζ/η\sim z^2/[\log(T/E_B)]^6$. Here, $P$ is the pressure, $c_s^2$ is the speed of sound, $η$ is the shear viscosity, $z$ is the fugacity, and $E_B$ is the two-body binding energy. We show that our results are consistent with the experimental results of Vogt et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 108, 070404 (2012)]. Vogt et al. reported a frequency shift $δω/ω$ of the order of a few percent, and a damping rate smaller than the background rate $Γ/ω_0\sim 5%$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the frequency shift of the monopole mode in a dilute two-dimensional Fermi gas scales as $(2P-\rho c_s^2)/P\sim z/[\log(T/E_B)]^2$ and the bulk viscosity scales as $\zeta/\eta\sim z^2/[\log(T/E_B)]^6$ in the high temperature limit, where $z$ is the fugacity and $E_B$ is the two-body binding energy. These results are consistent with the experimental data of Vogt et al. (2012) only for $\log(k_Fa)\gsim 1.75$, where the virial expansion is reliable, and the predicted damping rate is far smaller than the experimental upper bound.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic properties using a virial expansion to second order in fugacity, obtaining the second virial coefficient from a diagrammatic approach and the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula. They calculate the quasi-particle self energy from the boson propagator at leading order in the fugacity expansion, then use a variational hydrodynamic method to determine the monopole mode frequency shift from the scale-breaking parameter $\gamma_2=2P-\rho c_s^2$. The bulk viscosity is computed via the Chapman-Enskog expansion of the Boltzmann equation, solving the linearized collision integral with a Laguerre polynomial expansion of the off-equilibrium distribution function.

## Key numbers

* Frequency shift $\delta\omega/\omega$ is of order a few percent, consistent with Vogt et al. (2012) who reported deviations from the scale-invariant value $\omega=2\omega_0$ at the few percent level.
* The damping rate from bulk viscosity is $\Gamma/\omega_0 < 10^{-4}$ for $\log(k_Fa)\gsim 2$ under experimental conditions ($N=4\cdot 10^3$, $T/T_F=0.42$), compared to the experimental upper bound $\Gamma/\omega_0 < 5\cdot 10^{-2}$ from Vogt et al. (2012).
* The bulk viscosity scales as $\zeta = (1/2\pi) z^2 \lambda^{-2} / [\log(T/E_B)]^4$, where $\lambda$ is the thermal wavelength.
* The shear viscosity in the same limit is $\eta = \lambda^{-2} / \pi \, [\log(T/E_B)]^2$, from Schafer (2011) and Bruun (2011).
* The second virial coefficient expansion gives $\delta b_2(T) = 1/\log(T/E_B) + \gamma_E/[\log(T/E_B)]^2 + \ldots$, where $\gamma_E$ is Euler's constant.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Taylor (2012) relating monopole frequency shifts to scale breaking in the speed of sound, and the authors' own prior work on bulk viscosity in the three-dimensional Fermi gas (Schaefer, 2013). It provides a quantitative explanation for the experimental observations of Vogt et al. (2012), who measured small frequency shifts and undetectable damping of the monopole mode in a two-dimensional Fermi gas. The results enable future calculations of transport properties in the regime $T\sim E_B$, where bosonic degrees of freedom and three-body collisions become important, and establish that measuring bulk viscosity in the BCS limit will be extremely challenging.
