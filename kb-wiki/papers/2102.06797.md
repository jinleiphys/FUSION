# Hadronic resonance production and interaction in p-Pb collisions at LHC energies in EPOS3

> Using the EPOS3 model with UrQMD to describe the hadronic phase, we study the production of short-lived hadronic resonances and the modification of their yields and $p_{T}$ spectra in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. High-multiplicity p-Pb collisions exhibit similar behavior to mid-peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, and we find indications of a short-lived hadronic phase in p-Pb collisions that can modify resonance yields and $p_{T}$ spectra through scattering processes. The evolution of resonance production is investigated as a function of the system size, which is related to the lifetime of the hadronic phase, in order to study the onset of collective effects in p-Pb collisions. We also study hadron production separately in the core and corona parts of these collisions, and explore how this division affects the total particle yields as the system size increases.

## Key claim

Using the EPOS3 model with UrQMD, the authors find indications of a short-lived hadronic phase in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV that can modify resonance yields and transverse momentum spectra through scattering processes, but the model tends to overestimate the absolute values of resonance-to-long-lived-hadron yield ratios while qualitatively describing their system-size evolution.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS3 model, which treats the initial stage via a multiple-scattering approach based on Pomerons and strings, divides produced string segments into core (evolved via 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics) and corona (hadrons from string decays) contributions, and feeds all hadrons into UrQMD for microscopic description of hadronic interactions. They produced 1.8 million p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with UrQMD turned on and the same number with UrQMD turned off, dividing the event sample into multiplicity classes using charged-particle multiplicity in the pseudorapidity range 2.8 < eta_lab < 5.1. Resonances decaying via specific channels were flagged and their decay products followed; if neither decay product underwent re-scattering, the resonance was flagged as reconstructable.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic-phase lifetime in highest-multiplicity p-Pb collisions (0-5%) is approximately 1.5 fm/c, similar to peripheral Pb-Pb collisions (70-80%)
- Hadronic-phase lifetime in Pb-Pb collisions ranges from 0.5 to 10 fm/c depending on centrality
- The rho(770)0 meson has a lifetime of 1.335 fm/c; K*(892)0 has 4.16 fm/c; phi(1020) has 46.26 fm/c
- The Delta(1232)++ has a lifetime of 1.69 fm/c; Sigma(1385)+ has 5.48 fm/c; Sigma(1385)- has 5.01 fm/c; Lambda(1520) has 12.54 fm/c; Xi(1530)0 has 22 fm/c
- EPOS3 calculations show depletion of resonance yields at low pT below 1-2 GeV/c due to scattering effects, more pronounced for Pb-Pb than p-Pb collisions
- The effect of UrQMD on mean transverse momentum is largest for short-lived resonances (rho and K*), protons, and Lambda, and greatest in central Pb-Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of resonance production in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using EPOS3 (Knospe et al., 2016) to the p-Pb collision system at 5.02 TeV. It builds on experimental observations from ALICE that high-multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions show similar behavior to peripheral Pb-Pb collisions, including multiplicity-dependent suppression of K*(892)0 yields. The results demonstrate that a rigorous description of the hadronic phase is essential even in small collision systems, and enable future comparisons with LHC measurements of resonance production across different system sizes.
