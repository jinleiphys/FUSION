# Thermal relaxation and the complete set of second order transport coefficients for the unitary Fermi gas from kinetic theory

> We compute the complete set of second order transport coefficients of the unitary Fermi gas, a dilute gas of spin 1/2 particles interacting via an $s$-wave interaction tuned to infinite scattering length. The calculation is based on kinetic theory and the Chapman-Enskog method at second order in the Knudsen expansion. We take into account the exact two-body collision integral. We extend previous results on second order coefficients related to shear stress by including terms related to heat flow and gradients of the fugacity. We confirm that the thermal relaxation time is given by the simple estimate $τ_κ= κm/(c_PT)$ even if the full collision kernel is taken into account. Here, $κ$ is the thermal conductivity, $m$ is the mass of the particles, $c_P$ is the specific heat at constant pressure, and $T$ is the temperature.

## Key claim

The authors compute the complete set of second order transport coefficients for the unitary Fermi gas using kinetic theory and the Chapman-Enskog method, extending previous results on shear-related coefficients by including terms related to heat flow and gradients of the fugacity. The calculation is reliable only in the high temperature regime \(T \gtrsim T_F\), where the fugacity expansion is valid and quasi-particles are well-defined.

## Method

The calculation uses the Boltzmann equation with the exact two-body collision integral for s-wave scattering at unitarity, solved via the Chapman-Enskog method at second order in the Knudsen number. The distribution function is expanded around local equilibrium, and the linearized collision operator is inverted using a Laguerre polynomial expansion for both the shear and heat-flow sectors. Second order streaming terms and the second order collision term are computed, and matrix elements are evaluated to extract the transport coefficients from the stress tensor and energy current.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity: \(\eta = \frac{15}{32\sqrt{\pi}} (mT)^{3/2}\) (leading order in fugacity expansion, high temperature limit)
- Thermal conductivity: \(\kappa = \frac{225}{128\sqrt{\pi}} m^{1/2} T^{3/2}\) (leading order in fugacity expansion)
- First order Laguerre coefficient for shear: \(a_0 = -\frac{15\pi}{32\sqrt{2}} \frac{m}{zT}\)
- First order Laguerre coefficient for heat flow: \(b_1 = \frac{45\pi}{32\sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{zT}\)
- Matrix element for thermal conductivity collision integral: \(\langle c_i R_1(x_c) | C_L^1 | R_1(x_c) c^i \rangle = -\frac{8 z^2 \sqrt{m} T^{7/2}}{3 \pi^{5/2}}\)
- At \(T=0.5 T_F\) with \(\eta/n \simeq 0.6\) and wave number \(k=0.14 k_F\), the ratio of first order to ideal sound dispersion is 0.086 and the ratio of second order to ideal is 0.015

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of second order transport coefficients related to shear stress (Schaefer 2014) by including terms involving heat flow and fugacity gradients, completing the set for the unitary Fermi gas. The results are valid in the high temperature regime where kinetic theory applies, and they confirm that the thermal relaxation time satisfies \(\tau_\kappa = \kappa m/(c_P T)\) even with the full collision kernel. The calculation enables quantitative studies of second order hydrodynamic effects in the unitary Fermi gas, which are at the limit of observability in current sound attenuation experiments (Patel 2019).
