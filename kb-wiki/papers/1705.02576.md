# Photon production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV

> We calculate the high energy photon production from the Pb+Pb collisions for different centrality classes at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energy. The jet energy loss in the jet fragmentation, jet-photon conversion and jet bremsstrahlung is considered by using the Wang-Huang-Sarcevic (WHS) and Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS) models. We use the (1+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics to study the collective transverse flow and space-time evolution of the quark gluon plasma (QGP). The numerical results agree well with the ALICE data of the direct photons from the Pb+Pb collisions ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV) for 0-20\%, 20-40\% and 40-80\% centrality classes.

## Key claim
The authors calculate high-energy photon production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies and find that their numerical results agree with ALICE data for direct photons across three centrality classes, but only after including jet energy loss via the WHS and BDMPS models and (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics with transverse flow.

## Method
The calculation combines perturbative QCD for prompt photons (direct production and jet fragmentation), thermal photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronic gas (HG) using hard thermal loop (HTL) rates, and jet-medium interactions (jet-photon conversion and jet bremsstrahlung). Jet energy loss is implemented through the WHS phenomenological model using the BDMPS energy-loss-per-unit-distance formalism, while the space-time evolution of the QGP is described by (1+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with cylindrical symmetry and boost invariance.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperatures: T0 = 762 MeV (0-20% centrality), 680 MeV (20-40%), and 538 MeV (40-80%) at initial time τ0 = 0.15 fm/c.
- Average number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions: ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 1210 (0-20%), 438 (20-40%), and 77 (40-80%).
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: σinelNN = 64 mb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.
- Prompt photons dominate above pT > 9.5 GeV (0-20%), 8.5 GeV (20-40%), and 3.7 GeV (40-80%).
- Freeze-out temperature: Tf = 120 MeV; critical temperature: Tc = 160 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of photon production in heavy-ion collisions by including gluon jet-photon conversion (not considered by Fries et al.) and using the BDMPS energy-loss formalism instead of the average energy loss used in earlier WHS model applications (Wang-Huang-Sarcevic, 1990s). The results enable comparison with ALICE data (2013) for direct photons at LHC energies and test the combined effects of jet energy loss and transverse hydrodynamic flow on photon spectra across different centrality classes.
