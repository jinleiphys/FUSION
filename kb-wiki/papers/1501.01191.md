# $^1S_0$ nucleon-nucleon scattering in the modified Weinberg approach

> Nucleon-nucleon scattering in the $^1S_0$ partial wave is considered in chiral effective field theory within the renormalizable formulation of Ref. [1] beyond the leading-order approximation. By applying subtractive renormalization, the subleading contact interaction in this channel is taken into account non-perturbatively. For a proper choice of renormalization conditions, the predicted energy dependence of the phase shift and the coefficients in the effective range expansion are found to be in a good agreement with the results of the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.

## Key claim

The modified Weinberg approach, using subtractive renormalization with a properly chosen subtraction scale, yields NLO predictions for the $^1S_0$ nucleon-nucleon phase shift that agree well with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis, provided the subtraction scale $\mu$ is chosen of the order of the hard scale (approximately 600-900 MeV) rather than the soft scale.

## Method

The authors solve the Kadyshevsky integral equation for the $^1S_0$ partial wave with a potential consisting of one-pion exchange and contact interactions up to NLO (including a $C_2(p'^2+p^2)$ term). They apply BPHZ subtractive renormalization to remove ultraviolet divergences, expressing the amplitude in closed form using the two-potential formalism. The two renormalized low-energy constants $\tilde C^R(\mu)$ and $C_2^R(\mu)$ are fitted to the Nijmegen PWA phase shifts in the energy range 0-50 MeV for various choices of the subtraction scale $\mu$.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass $M_\pi = 138$ MeV, pion decay constant $F_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, nucleon axial-vector coupling $g_A = 1.267$ (values used for the OPE potential).
- The subtraction scale $\mu$ is varied from 50 to 900 MeV; the appropriate choice for the hard scale is $\mu \sim 600$-$700$ MeV, with acceptable results in the range $\mu = 500\ldots 900$ MeV.
- The NLO phase shift predictions for $\mu$ in the range 500-900 MeV show a spread that matches the estimated theoretical accuracy at NLO in finite-cutoff calculations (referenced as Fig. 9 of Epelbaum (2014)).
- The ratio $R(E_{\rm lab}) = |T^{\rm NLO}|/|T^{\rm LO}|$ at $\mu = 850$ MeV shows that nonperturbative inclusion of the subleading contact interaction becomes advantageous over perturbative inclusion for laboratory energies above approximately 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the modified Weinberg approach of Epelbaum (2012) beyond leading order by including the NLO short-range contact interaction non-perturbatively in the $^1S_0$ channel. It addresses the long-standing criticism of Weinberg's approach regarding non-renormalizability (Savage (1998)) and the convergence problems of the KSW perturbative scheme (Kaplan, Savage, Wise (1998); Gegelia (1998); Cohen (1998)). The results enable future inclusion of two-pion exchange potentials at NLO within this renormalizable framework, and the method provides a systematic way to estimate theoretical uncertainties from the residual $\mu$-dependence.
