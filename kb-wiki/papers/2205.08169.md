# Restriction on the form of quark anomalous magnetic moment from lattice QCD results

> The quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) is dynamically generated through the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. It has been revealed that even though its exact form is still unknown, the quark AMM is essential to explore quark matter properties and QCD phase structure under external magnetic fields. In this study, we take three different forms of the quark AMM and investigate its influence on the chiral phase transition under magnetic field. In general, a negative quark AMM plays the role as magnetic catalyzer and a positive quark AMM plays the role of magnetic inhibition. It is found that a constant quark AMM drives an unexpected 1st order chiral phase transition; a quark AMM proportional to the chiral condensate gives a flip of the sign on the chiral condensate; and a quark AMM proportional to the square of chiral condensate can produce results of chiral condensate as functions of the temperature and the magnetic field in good agreement with the lattice result.

## Key claim
The paper shows that a quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) proportional to the square of the chiral condensate can reproduce lattice QCD results for the chiral condensate as a function of temperature and magnetic field, while a constant AMM or an AMM proportional to the chiral condensate produce unphysical phase transitions or sign flips.

## Method
The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an added quark AMM interaction term, employing a Lorentzian regulator to avoid oscillatory behavior in the chiral condensate. They compute the effective potential and chiral condensate for three assumed forms of the quark AMM: constant, proportional to the chiral condensate, and proportional to the square of the chiral condensate, then compare the resulting temperature and magnetic field dependence with lattice QCD data from Ding (2020) and Ding (2022).

## Key numbers
- Constant AMM values from the constituent quark model: κ_u = 0.29016 GeV⁻¹ and κ_d = 0.35986 GeV⁻¹
- For constant AMM, a first-order chiral phase transition appears at zero temperature at a critical magnetic field eB_c ≈ 0.4 GeV²
- NJL model parameters: Λ = 681.38 GeV, G_S Λ² = 1.860, m_0 = 4.552 MeV, yielding dynamical mass M = 286.19 MeV, pion mass m_π = 138 MeV, and pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Pseudocritical temperature at eB=0: T_pc^(NJL) ≈ 167 MeV, compared to lattice QCD result T_pc^(lat.) ≈ 170 MeV from Ding (2022)
- For constant AMM κ_u=κ_d=0.3 GeV⁻¹ at T=170 MeV, two jumps appear at eB_c ≈ 0.23 GeV² and eB_c ≈ 0.4 GeV²

## Context
This work extends previous NJL model studies of quark AMM effects by Fayazbakhsh (2014), Chaudhuri (2019), and Mei (2020), which found that constant AMM triggers unexpected first-order phase transitions. It also builds on the observation by Chang (2010) that AMM is dynamically generated from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, and on QED results by Lin (2021) showing magnetic-field-dependent electron AMM proportional to mass squared. The paper enables constraining the effective form of quark AMM for low-energy QCD under magnetic fields, with the σ²-dependent form providing a path to reconcile effective models with lattice QCD observations of chiral crossover behavior.
