# How well known is the compressibility of nuclear matter?

> The most accurate approach to determine the compressibility of nuclear matter remains the one based on microscopic Energy Density Functionals (EDFs). Recent analyses yield a value for nuclear incompressibility modulus $K_\sat=240\pm 20$~MeV, defined in nuclear matter as the second derivative of the energy per particle at saturation density. However, we demonstrate that the compressibility modulus can be reduced to values shifted by four times the suggested uncertainty, i.e., $K_\sat\approx 160$~MeV, by providing examples based on models where the second derivative ($K_\sat$) and third derivative ($Q_\sat$) of the energy per particle at saturation density can be independently varied, while the experimental binding energies, charge radii, and ISGMR data in $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb are enforced. The present work suggests a new methodology to access the compressibility of nuclear matter from nuclear experiments, still based on microscopic models, but using EDFs containing more flexibility than the ones employed up to now. Consequences of our results for nuclear matter at supra-saturation density are also discussed by exploring the quarkyonic cross-over. We predict that, for our models with low values for $K_\sat$, the quark onset density has to be low for neutron stars to exist.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the nuclear incompressibility modulus \(K_\sat\) can be as low as approximately 160 MeV, a value shifted by four times the commonly quoted uncertainty of \(\pm 20\) MeV, by using energy density functionals (EDFs) where the second and third derivatives of the energy per particle at saturation density can be independently varied, while still reproducing experimental binding energies, charge radii, and ISGMR data in \(^{120}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Method

The authors construct extended Skyrme EDFs by adding an extra density-dependent term (with parameters \(t_3^\prime\), \(x_3^\prime\), \(\gamma^\prime\)) to the standard Skyrme interaction, breaking the usual linear correlation between \(K_\sat\) and \(Q_\sat\). They compute ISGMR centroid energies using both Constrained-HFB (CHFB) and QRPA approaches for \(^{120}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb, and compare these predictions against experimental ISGMR energies, binding energies, and charge radii via a \(\chi^2\) likelihood analysis to define 95% confidence intervals in the (\(K_\sat\), \(Q_\sat\)) plane.

## Key numbers

- The standard accepted value for the nuclear incompressibility modulus is \(K_\sat = 240 \pm 20\) MeV.
- The authors demonstrate that models with \(K_\sat \approx 160\) MeV can still reproduce experimental data; this is four times the standard uncertainty (i.e., shifted by 80 MeV).
- Experimental ISGMR centroid energies used as constraints: \(E_{\mathrm{ISGMR}}(^{120}\mathrm{Sn}) = 15.5 \pm 0.2\) MeV (from Liga:2010) and \(E_{\mathrm{ISGMR}}(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}) = 13.5 \pm 0.2\) MeV (from Uchida:2003).
- Experimental binding energies: \(B(^{120}\mathrm{Sn}) = -1020 \pm 5\) MeV and \(B(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}) = -1636 \pm 2\) MeV (from Wang:2021).
- Experimental charge radii: \(R_{\mathrm{ch}}(^{120}\mathrm{Sn}) = 4.65 \pm 0.02\) fm and \(R_{\mathrm{ch}}(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}) = 5.50 \pm 0.01\) fm (from Taoli:2021).
- Saturation density is taken as \(\rho_\sat \approx 0.155\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The CHFB and QRPA approaches agree within a theoretical uncertainty of approximately 0.1 to 0.2 MeV for the ISGMR centroid energies.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to determine \(K_\sat\) from ISGMR data, which began with Blaizot (1980) suggesting around 210 MeV, later revised to 260-270 MeV by relativistic mean-field analyses (Lalazissis:2009), and most recently to \(240\pm20\) MeV (Garg:2018). The authors challenge the standard methodology by showing that the tight correlation between \(K_\sat\) and \(Q_\sat\) in conventional EDFs (Skyrme, Gogny, Fayans, and \(\chi\)EFT families) artificially restricts the parameter space. By introducing more flexible EDFs, they enable a broader exploration of the (\(K_\sat\), \(Q_\sat\)) plane, which has implications for the equation of state at supra-saturation density and the possible onset of a quarkyonic phase transition in neutron stars.
