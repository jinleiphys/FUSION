# Projectile structure effects in the Coulomb breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei

> We investigate the Coulomb breakup of neutron rich nuclei 11Be and (19,17,15)C within a theory developed in the framework of Distorted Wave Born Approximation. Finite range effects are included by a local momentum approximation, which allows incorporation of realistic wave functions for these nuclei in our calculations. Energy and angular as well as parallel momentum distributions of the fragments emitted in the breakup of these nuclei on heavy targets have been calculated using several structure models for their ground state. Comparison with the available experimental data shows that the results are selective about the ground state wave function of the projectile. Our investigations confirm that the nuclei 11Be, 19C and 15C have a one-neutron halo structure in their ground states. However, for 17C such a structure appears to be less likely. Calculations performed within our method have also been compared with those from an adiabatic model and the results are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that ¹¹Be, ¹⁹C, and ¹⁵C have a one-neutron halo structure in their ground states, but for ¹⁷C such a structure appears to be less likely. This conclusion is conditional on the selectivity of the calculated energy, angular, and parallel momentum distributions for the ground state wave function of the projectile when compared with available experimental data.

## Method

The authors developed a post-form Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) theory for Coulomb breakup, incorporating finite range effects through a local momentum approximation (LMA) that factorizes the reaction dynamics from the projectile structure. They calculated triple differential cross sections for the breakup of ¹¹Be and ¹⁵,¹⁷,¹⁹C on heavy targets (Au, Pb) using several ground state wave function configurations, including s-wave and d-wave valence neutron couplings and a dynamical core polarization model. The results were compared with experimental data on neutron energy distributions, angular distributions, relative energy spectra, and average momenta of the charged fragment, and also compared with calculations from an adiabatic (AD) model and the Baur-Trautmann (BT) approximation.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy for ¹¹Be: 504 keV (s-wave configuration with spectroscopic factor 0.74)
- Root mean square radius for the valence neutron in ¹¹Be (s-wave): 6.7 fm; rms radius for ¹¹Be: 2.91 fm (core radius 2.28 fm)
- Beam energies used for ¹¹Be breakup on Au: 37 MeV/nucleon, 41 MeV/nucleon, 42 MeV/nucleon, and 44 MeV/nucleon
- Beam energy for ¹¹Be breakup on Pb: 72 MeV/nucleon
- The local momentum in the LMA was evaluated at a distance of 10 fm
- The d-wave configuration for ¹¹Be had a binding energy of 3.872 MeV (sum of 3.368 MeV core excitation and 504 keV separation energy) with spectroscopic factor 0.17

## Context

This work extends earlier post-form DWBA calculations (Shyam 1992) that used a zero-range approximation, which restricted applicability to s-wave projectiles and was questionable at higher beam energies. It also compares with the adiabatic model (Tostevin 1998) and the Baur-Trautmann approximation (Baur and Trautmann), showing that the FRDWBA provides better agreement with experimental data for ¹¹Be breakup, particularly for neutron angular distributions and relative energy spectra. The calculations enable future inclusion of nuclear breakup contributions and application to cases where both fragments are charged.
