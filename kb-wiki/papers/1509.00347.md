# On growth of spinodal instabilities in nuclear matter-II:asymmetric matter

> As an extension of our previous work, the growth of density fluctuations in the spinodal region of charge asymmetric nuclear matter is investigated in the basis of the stochastic mean-field approach in the non-relativistic framework. A complete treatment of density correlation functions are presented by including collective modes and non-collective modes as well.

## Key claim

The growth of density fluctuations in charge-asymmetric nuclear matter within the spinodal region is fully described only when both collective poles and non-collective (cut) contributions are included in the density correlation function; the cut terms slow the exponential growth and cancel divergent behavior near the maximum wave number, producing a regular spectral intensity that satisfies the initial conditions.

## Method

The authors employed the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach in a non-relativistic framework, solving the linearized coupled equations for neutron and proton density fluctuations via one-sided Fourier transformation in the complex frequency plane. They evaluated the equal-time density correlation function by performing contour integration that includes both collective poles (from the dispersion relation) and non-collective cut contributions along the real axis. The spectral intensity was computed in the long-wavelength (semi-classical) limit using an effective Skyrme potential, for initial density \(\rho = 0.3\rho_0\) at two temperatures and three charge asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperatures for the effective Skyrme potential: \(T_c = 15\) MeV for charge asymmetry \(I=0.0\), \(T_c = 14\) MeV for \(I=0.4\), and \(T_c = 10\) MeV for \(I=0.8\), all occurring near initial density \(\rho = 0.3\rho_0\).
- For neutron-rich matter with \(I=0.8\) and \(T=1.0\) MeV, the limiting spinodal boundary occurs at nucleon density around \(\rho = 0.55\rho_0\).
- At temperature \(T=5.0\) MeV and initial charge asymmetry \(I=0.4\), the correlation length is about \(x_C = 3.0\) fm, and the magnitude of the dispersion of density fluctuations at time \(t=30\) fm/\(c\) is about \(\sqrt{\sigma(x_C,t)} = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The number of nucleons in the correlation volume at \(T=5.0\) MeV and \(I=0.4\) fluctuates approximately in the range \(1 \le \Delta A \le 9\) at time \(t=30\) fm/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on charge-symmetric nuclear matter (Acar et al., 2015) by including charge asymmetry and follows the stochastic mean-field approach developed by Ayik and collaborators. It addresses the deficiency noted by Bozek (2014) that collective poles alone do not satisfy initial conditions, and provides a complete treatment of the density correlation function. The results enable future long-time simulations of the liquid-gas phase transition in neutron-rich matter, relevant for the crust of neutron stars.
