# Breakup Reactions of Drip Line Nuclei

> The formal theory of breakup reactions is reviewed. The direct breakup mechanism which is formulated within the framework of the post form distorted wave Born approximation is discussed in detail. In this theory, which requires the information about only the ground state wave function of the projectile, the fragment-target interactions are included to all orders while fragment-fragment interaction is treated only in the first order. The general applicability of this theory to describe the breakup of halo nuclei is demonstrated by comparing the calculations with data for total, as well as energy and angle integrated cross sections and momentum distributions of fragments in reactions induced by a number of halo nuclei. We investigate the role played by the pure Coulomb, pure nuclear and the Coulomb-nuclear interference terms. Postacceleration effects in the Coulomb breakup of neutron halo nuclei is also studied.

## Key claim

The post form distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) theory of direct breakup reactions, which treats fragment-target interactions to all orders but fragment-fragment interaction only in first order, can describe breakup data for halo nuclei provided the full ground-state wave function of the projectile is used as input; the theory's applicability is demonstrated for total and integrated cross sections and momentum distributions, but the authors caution that actual calculations must use full distorted waves rather than the quasi-free limit to explain the data.

## Method

The authors formulate the breakup reaction \(a + t \rightarrow b + c + t\) within the post form DWBA, using the \(T\)-matrix \(T^{(+)[post]}_{fi} = \langle \chi^{(-)}_{q_b} \chi^{(-)}_{q_c} | V_{bc} | \chi^{(+)}_{q_a} \phi_a \rangle\), where \(\phi_a\) is the projectile ground-state wave function and \(V_{bc}\) is the fragment-fragment interaction. For pure Coulomb breakup, they employ a local momentum approximation (LMA) to factorize the six-dimensional reduced amplitude into a product of a structure-dependent form factor and a dynamical term, avoiding the zero-range approximation. The theory includes Coulomb and nuclear breakups and their interference terms consistently, and the authors compare calculations with data for total cross sections, energy- and angle-integrated cross sections, and fragment momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Be: \(S_{n-^{10}\text{Be}} = 504\) keV, with a spectroscopic factor of 0.74 for the \(^{10}\text{Be}(0^+) \otimes 1s_{1/2}\nu\) configuration.
* Woods-Saxon parameters for the valence neutron-\(^{10}\)Be interaction: radius parameter 1.15 fm, diffuseness parameter 0.5 fm.
* The ratio \(|F_r|^2\) between the BT and FRDWBA amplitudes for \(^{11}\)Be breakup on a gold target at 44 MeV/nucleon (forward angles \(\theta_b = 1^\circ\), \(\theta_c = 1^\circ\), \(\phi_c = 1^\circ\)) is close to unity only at sub-Coulomb beam energies, crossing unity twice at higher energies.

## Context

This work reviews and extends the formal theory of breakup reactions for halo nuclei, building on the Serber model (1947) and the post form DWBA framework developed by Baur and collaborators (1984). It contrasts with prior approaches that used zero-range approximations (Satchler), semiclassical coupled channels (Canto 1993), time-dependent Schrödinger equation methods (Kido 1994, Bertsch 1993), and prior form DWBA (Shyam 1991) which treated fragment-target interactions only in first order. The post form DWBA with LMA enables consistent treatment of Coulomb and nuclear breakups and their interference, and allows extraction of projectile ground-state structure from breakup data, including postacceleration effects in neutron halo nuclei.
