# Investigation of a0-f0 mixing

> We investigate the isospin-violating mixing of the light scalar mesons a0(980) and f0(980) within the unitarized chiral approach. Isospin-violating effects are considered to leading order in the quark mass differences and electromagnetism. In this approach both mesons are generated through meson-meson dynamics. Our results provide a description of the mixing phenomenon within a framework consistent with chiral symmetry and unitarity, where these resonances are not predominantly q q-bar states. Amongst the possible experimental signals, we discuss observable consequences for the reaction J/Psi -> phi pi0 eta in detail. In particular we demonstrate that the effect of a0-f0 mixing is by far the most important isospin-breaking effect in the resonance region and can indeed be extracted from experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the isospin-violating mixing of the scalar mesons a0(980) and f0(980) is the dominant isospin-breaking effect in the resonance region near the K̅K thresholds, and can be extracted from experiment, but this dominance relies on the specific kinematic enhancement from the proximity of the kaon thresholds to the resonance poles, which amplifies the mixing amplitude beyond what would be expected from typical isospin-violating scales.

## Method

The authors employ the chiral unitary approach, which unitarizes leading-order chiral perturbation theory amplitudes for pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar scattering in coupled channels, dynamically generating the a0(980) and f0(980) resonances without introducing them explicitly. Isospin violation is included to leading order in quark mass differences and electromagnetism by working in the charge basis with six coupled channels (π+π−, π0π0, ηη, K+K−, K0K̅0, π0η), modifying the loop functions G(s) to account for charged-neutral kaon mass differences and adding one-photon exchange contributions for charged kaon loops. The scalar form factors for J/Ψ → φ π0η are then obtained from the unitarized scattering amplitudes, allowing the mixing amplitude to be decomposed into leading (kaon mass difference) and subleading (isospin-violating vertices and photon exchange) contributions.

## Key numbers

- The leading mixing amplitude ΛL scales as √((mK0² − mK+²)/(mK0² + mK+²)) ~ √((md − mu)/(ms + m̂)), where m̂ = (mu + md)/2, in contrast to common isospin-violating effects which scale as (md − mu)/ms
- The kinematic window between K+K− and K0K̅0 thresholds is only 8 MeV wide, where the mixing amplitude is unusually enhanced
- The ratio of mixing effects to direct isospin violation in the production operator is estimated as mf0Γf0√((md − mu)/ms)/MK², which is of order 4% for the amplitude (using 50 MeV for the f0 width)
- The charged-to-neutral pion mass difference is Δπ = Mπ+² − Mπ0² = 2Ce²/F², and the kaon mass difference is ΔK = MK+² − MK0² = Δπ − (4ε/√3)(MK² − Mπ²), where ε is the leading-order π0η mixing angle

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1997-1998) and the scalar form factor analysis of Meissner and Oller (2000) and Terschlusen and Leupold (2006) by incorporating isospin violation to study a0-f0 mixing. It builds on the model-independent argument by Achasov and coworkers (1979) that the mixing amplitude is enhanced near kaon thresholds, and on the phenomenological calculation of Krehl (2000) which only included kaon mass differences as the source of isospin violation. The results enable quantitative predictions for J/Ψ → φ π0η, which can be tested against BES collaboration data, and provide a framework for analyzing other reactions sensitive to a0-f0 mixing such as γp → pπ0η, π−p → π0ηn, and dd → απ0η.
