# Relating $\hat{q}$, $η/s$ and $ΔE$ in an expanding Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We use linear viscous hydrodynamics to describe the energy and momentum deposited by a fast moving parton in a quark gluon plasma. This energy-momentum is in turn used to compute the probability density for the production of soft partons by means of the Cooper-Frye formula. We use this probability density to render manifest a relation between the average transverse momentum given to the fast moving parton from the medium $\hat{q}$, the entropy density to shear viscosity ratio $η/s$ and the energy lost by the fast moving parton $ΔE$ in an expanding medium under similar conditions to those generated in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC. We find that $\hat{q}$ increases linearly with $ΔE$ for both trigger and away side partons that have been produced throughout the medium. On the other hand, $η/s$ is more stable with $ΔE$. We also study how these transport coefficients vary with the geometrical location of the hard scattering that produces the fast moving partons. The behavior of $\hat{q}$, with $ΔE$ is understood as arising from the length of medium the parton traverses from the point where it is produced. However, since $η/s$ is proportional to the ratio of the length of medium traversed by the fast parton and the average number of scatterings it experiences, it has a milder dependence on the energy it loses. This study represents a tool to obtain a direct connection between transport coefficients and the description of in-medium energy loss within a linear viscous hydrodynamical evolution of the bulk.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in an expanding quark-gluon plasma, the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ increases linearly with the energy lost $\Delta E$, while the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ has a much milder dependence on $\Delta E$; however, the authors caution that the expanding medium cannot be characterized by single values of either $\hat{q}$ or $\eta/s$, and events must be classified by observables such as energy loss before extracting transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors used linear viscous hydrodynamics to compute the energy and momentum deposited by a fast moving parton in a quark-gluon plasma, then applied the Cooper-Frye formula to obtain the probability density for soft parton production. They modeled the expanding medium using a one-dimensional energy loss model tuned to LHC data, with parton events generated via MadGraph 5 for $2\to 2$ processes at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.7$ TeV. The transport coefficients $\hat{q}$ and $\eta/s$ were computed by classifying events according to the energy lost $\Delta E$ or the geometrical location $r$ of the hard scattering.

## Key numbers

* $\hat{q}$ was computed for QGP temperatures $T_0=350$ MeV (with $\epsilon_0=2$ GeV/fm) and $T_0=450$ MeV (with $\epsilon_0=4$ GeV/fm) in the one-dimensional energy loss model.
* For $\epsilon_0=4$ GeV/fm, $\hat{q}$ shows a pronounced maximum around $r=1.4$ fm for both trigger and away-side particles.
* The average transverse momentum of produced partons in the hard scattering at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.7$ TeV is of order 15 GeV.
* For $\epsilon_0$ of order 1-2 GeV/fm, partons lose on average 3-5 GeV; for $\epsilon_0$ of order 4 GeV/fm, partons lose on average 10 GeV.
* $\eta/s$ values cluster around 0.2 (for $T_0=350$ MeV) and around 0.3 (for $T_0=450$ MeV) for most events, except those where $0.5<\Delta E_{\text{trigger}}<2$ GeV.
* The mean free path for constant density was taken as $\lambda_0=0.25$ fm, with $\epsilon_0\lambda_0=0.5$ GeV and $\mu_0=1.5$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ayala et al. (previous work) that used linear viscous hydrodynamics to compute soft-particle production from energy loss, and builds on the model of Wang (2001) for energy loss in an expanding medium tuned to LHC data by Liu et al. The study provides a direct connection between transport coefficients $\hat{q}$ and $\eta/s$ and in-medium energy loss within a linear viscous hydrodynamical evolution of the bulk, enabling future extractions of these coefficients from experimental data on jet quenching at RHIC and LHC energies.
