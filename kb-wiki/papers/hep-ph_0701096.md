# Meson-Baryon Effective Chiral Lagrangians at O(q^3) Revisited

> After our work was published, Frink and Meißner \cite{FM06} pointed out that our {\cal O}(q^3) three-flavour meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian was not minimal. Here, we discuss their findings and revise ours accordingly. We find that eight monomials in our ${\cal O}(q^3)$ Lagrangian are not independent, but in addition, two monomials were wrongly discarded, which, as a result, makes the agreement in the number of independent monomials with \cite{FM06} complete.

## Key claim

The authors revise their previously published O(q^3) three-flavour meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian, finding that eight monomials were not independent and two monomials were wrongly discarded, which brings the total number of independent monomials to 78 in full agreement with the count of Frink and Meißner (2006).

## Method

The authors re-examine their earlier Lagrangian using Cayley-Hamilton relations among flavour matrices, charge conjugation invariance conditions, and equations of motion for baryons to identify redundant monomials. They systematically remove eight monomials (O10, O22, O23, O25, O35, O36, O41, O43), exchange O42 for a different monomial, and add two previously discarded monomials (widehat O32 and widehat O33), producing a complete minimal set of 78 independent monomials presented in a table.

## Key numbers

- The original Lagrangian in Oller et al. (2006) contained 84 monomials; the revised minimal set contains 78 independent monomials.
- Eight monomials from the original Lagrangian are removed as not independent: O10, O22, O23, O25, O35, O36, O41, O43.
- Two monomials (widehat O32 and widehat O33) that were wrongly discarded in Oller et al. (2006) are added back.
- One monomial (O42) is exchanged for widehat O35.

## Context

This erratum corrects the earlier work of Oller et al. (2006) following the critique by Frink and Meißner (2006), who pointed out that the original O(q^3) Lagrangian was not minimal. The corrected Lagrangian now agrees fully with Frink and Meißner (2006) in the count of 78 independent monomials. The authors note that their presentation of monomials is more compact and easier to manipulate than that of Frink and Meißner (2006), and they prefer not to introduce dimensionful parameters to artificially change the dimension of coefficients.
