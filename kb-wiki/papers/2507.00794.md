# Burgers equation for the bulk viscous pressure of quark matter

> The dissipative properties of relativistic strongly interacting nuclear matter significantly influence the damping of stellar oscillations and density fluctuations during compact star mergers. In this work, we derive the evolution equation for the bulk viscous pressure in unpaired quark matter under small deviations from equilibrium. Our analysis reveals that it behaves like a two-component Burgers fluid. We identify four key transport coefficients -- two relaxation times and two bulk viscosity coefficients -- expressed in terms of equilibrium parameters and electroweak nonleptonic and semi-leptonic decay rates. The transport coefficients are evaluated for two distinct equations of state: one based on perturbative quantum chromodynamics and the other on a modified MIT bag model, valid in different density regimes. We also determine the temperature and density region where nonleptonic electroweak processes dominate the dissipation. Our formulation establishes a new way of describing bulk viscous effects in quark matter, applicable for numerical simulations of compact star mergers.

## Key claim

The bulk viscous pressure in unpaired quark matter under small deviations from equilibrium evolves according to a two-component Burgers equation, not the simpler Israel-Stewart equation, and this behavior requires four transport coefficients (two relaxation times and two bulk viscosity coefficients) rather than the two typically assumed.

## Method

The authors derived the evolution equation for the bulk viscous pressure by linearizing the particle fraction evolution equations around equilibrium for a five-species system (u, d, s quarks, electrons, and untrapped neutrinos) with two independent chemical potentials characterizing deviations from equilibrium. They expressed the four transport coefficients in terms of equilibrium parameters and electroweak nonleptonic and semi-leptonic decay rates, then evaluated these coefficients for two equations of state: perturbative QCD (pQCD) at high densities and a modified MIT bag model at intermediate densities.

## Key numbers

- The pQCD equation of state is applicable at baryon densities above approximately 37-38 times nuclear saturation density (n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^-3), corresponding to mu_d ≳ 850 MeV
- The bag model equation of state is applicable at intermediate densities corresponding to mu_d ≈ 400-600 MeV, approximately 3.6 times nuclear saturation density
- The nonleptonic rate lambda_1 scales as mu_d^5 T^2, while semileptonic rates lambda_2 scales as mu_d mu_u mu_e T^4 and lambda_3 scales as mu_s m_s^2 T^4
- The Fermi coupling constant G_F ≈ 1.166 × 10^-5 GeV^-2, the Cabibbo angle Θ_C ≈ 13.02°, and the strange quark mass m_s(Λ=2 GeV) ≈ 94 MeV
- The crossing temperature T_cross where the two Green's functions G_+ and G_- coincide was evaluated over time scales from 10^-4 s to 10^-2 s, with the millisecond scale being relevant for neutron star mergers
- For the bag model, varying a_4 between 0.6-0.8 changes tau_+ by less than 15% and zeta_+ by less than 30%
- In the bag model, zeta_+ scales approximately as (m_s^2)^2, while zeta_- scales as (m_s^2)^5 at low temperatures and as (m_s^2)^(3/2) above the inflection temperature

## Context

This work extends the Burgers-type evolution framework previously applied by Gavassino (2023) to hadronic matter with three independent chemical potentials, showing that unpaired quark matter with five particle species also follows a Burgers equation rather than the Israel-Stewart equation used in recent merger simulations by Chabanov (2023). The formulation enables numerical simulations of compact star mergers to incorporate bulk viscous dissipation through a second-order hydrodynamic framework, and reproduces the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity computed by Hernandez (2024) in the harmonic limit.
