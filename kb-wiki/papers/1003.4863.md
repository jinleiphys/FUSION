# Breathing mode in an improved transport approach

> The nuclear breathing-mode giant monopole resonance is studied within an improved relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport approach. As a new feature, the numerical treatment of ground state nuclei and their phase-space evolution is realized with the same semiclassical energy density functional. With this new method a very good stability of ground state nuclei in BUU simulations is achieved. This is important in extracting clear breathing-mode signals for the excitation energy and, in particular, for the lifetime from transport theoretical studies including mean-field and collisional effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using the same semiclassical energy density functional for both the initialization of ground state nuclei and their dynamical propagation in a relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport approach yields sufficiently stable ground state configurations to extract clear breathing-mode signals for excitation energy and lifetime, including collisional effects.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic BUU equation using the test-particle method, with the mean field described by the nonlinear Walecka model (NL3* parametrization) and the collision integral modeled via a parallel-ensemble Monte-Carlo algorithm with energy- and angular-dependent vacuum nucleon-nucleon cross sections. Ground state nuclei are initialized by solving relativistic Thomas-Fermi (RTF) equations with the same Hamiltonian density functional used for propagation, including gradient terms of meson fields and the isovector-vector rho meson field. The Pauli blocking calculation is improved by introducing a coordinate- and momentum-dependent radius for the momentum space volume element to reduce spurious collisions near the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility modulus of nuclear matter: K_infinity = 258 MeV (from the NL3* parametrization)
- Nucleon Dirac effective mass at saturation: m* = 0.594 m (from NL3*)
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.150 fm^{-3} (from NL3*)
- Time scale of GMR motion: approximately 40-80 fm/c
- Binding energy per nucleon for ground state 100Sn from RTF: E_B/A approximately 8.1 MeV
- Standard initialization produces binding energy smaller by 0.3-0.4 MeV/nucleon relative to the RTF value for 100Sn
- Test particles per nucleon: 10000 for Vlasov calculations, 1000 for full BUU calculations
- Default Pauli blocking parameters: r_p = 80 MeV/c, r_r = 1.86 fm, sigma = 1 fm, r_c = 2.2 fm
- Modified Pauli blocking parameters: r_r = 0.9-1.86 fm, sigma = 0.5 fm, r_c = 1.1 fm, with r_p = max(20 MeV/c, p_F_i(r) - |p*|)

## Context

This work extends previous semiclassical treatments of the giant monopole resonance (GMR) that were restricted to pure Vlasov dynamics (Abrosimov 1998, Morawetz, Serbulent) or used linearized Landau-Vlasov equations (KPS 1996) with approximate finite-size effects. It also contrasts with fully quantum approaches such as nonrelativistic and relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA (Sagawa, Piekarewicz and Centelles 2009), which disagree on the origin of the GMR total width. The improved method enables simultaneous description of the centroid energy and width of the GMR in finite nuclei, including the contribution of two-body collisions to the total width, and can be applied to study the nuclear incompressibility modulus.
