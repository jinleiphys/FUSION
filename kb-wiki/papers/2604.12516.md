# Scattering Faddeev calculations in the double continuum

> We use the configuration-space Faddeev formalism to study scattering of three particles in the double continuum where all particles are free. All scattering processes, starting from and ending in both single and double continua, are collected in a unique matrix. We apply our method to the benchmark system of neutron-deuteron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents a configuration-space Faddeev method that extracts both two-body and three-body breakup contributions from a single calculated wavefunction by using Jacobi coordinates for the single continuum and polar coordinates for the double continuum, applied to neutron-deuteron scattering; the abstract omits that the breakup amplitudes near α ≈ π/2 remain imprecise in finite-box calculations and require additional methods to retrieve the correct asymptotic values.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Faddeev equations in configuration space using polar coordinates (hyperradius ρ and hyperangle α) with a basis of cubic Hermite splines and an orthogonal collocation method. They impose outgoing boundary conditions corresponding to either bound plane waves (for single continuum channels) or cylindrical Hankel functions (for double continuum channels), then extract the elastic and breakup amplitudes by projecting the calculated Faddeev components onto the deuteron bound state and fitting the resulting function to a linear combination of regular and outgoing waves. The method handles all four scattering processes (elastic nd, breakup, three-body recombination, and elastic nnp) within a single scattering matrix.

## Context

This work extends the configuration-space Faddeev formalism to treat the double continuum (all three particles free) alongside the single continuum, building on the boundary condition derivation by Merkuriev (1976) and the benchmark neutron-deuteron calculations compiled by Friar (1995). The method addresses the difficulty that two-body and three-body channels are not orthogonal in the usual sense, contrasting with the orthogonalization approach of Belov (2013). It enables systematic calculation of all scattering processes, elastic, breakup, and recombination, within a unified framework for three-particle systems with short-range potentials.
