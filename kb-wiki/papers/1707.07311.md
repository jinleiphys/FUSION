# Random-interaction study on linear systematics of $I^π=11/2^-$ electromagnetic moments in Cd isotope chain

> In the random-interaction ensembles, electromagnetic moments of Cd $I^π=11/2^-$ isomers predominately present linear systematics as changing the neutron number, which has been reported in realistic nuclear system. Quadrupole-like and $δ$-like $pn$ interaction are responsible for such linear systematics of quadrupole and magnetic moments, respectively.

## Key claim

In the random-interaction ensemble (TBRE), the linear systematics of quadrupole moments ($Q$) and magnetic moments ($\mu$) for $I^\pi=11/2^-$ isomers in Cd isotopes are predominantly reproduced, but the linearity of $\mu$ is weaker than that of $Q$ and requires the additional condition that all even-mass Cd isotopes have $0^+$ ground states.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for $^{112-130}$Cd using a model space limited to $\pi 0g_{9/2}$, $\nu 2s_{1/2}$, $\nu 1d_{3/2}$, and $\nu 0h_{11/2}$ orbits. They generated 3,000,000 sets of two-body interaction elements from the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) with Gaussian distribution, and for each set first verified that all even-mass Cd isotopes had $0^+$ ground states before calculating the $I^\pi=11/2^-$ electromagnetic moments of odd-mass Cd. The linearity of moments with neutron number was quantified using the Pearson correlation coefficient $\rho$.

## Key numbers

* The experimental $Q$ values for Cd $I^\pi=11/2^-$ isomers show $\rho=0.997$ linear systematics with neutron number.
* The experimental $\mu$ values for Cd $I^\pi=11/2^-$ isomers show $\rho=0.862$ linear systematics, weaker than for $Q$.
* After the $0^+$-ground-state sampling, the probability of obtaining $\rho>0.9$ for $Q$ systematics is $P(\rho>0.9)\simeq 15\%$, with a ratio $P(\rho>0.9)/P_{\rm bkg}\simeq 300$ relative to the background distribution.
* Only about 1% of TBRE interaction sets survive the $0^+$-ground-state sampling for all even-mass Cd isotopes.
* For $\mu$ systematics after the $0^+$-ground-state sampling, the probability of $|\rho|>0.9$ is about 1%.
* Best-fit $\delta$-force parameters for the $\pi 0g_{9/2}$-$\nu 0h_{11/2}$ interaction in the $\rho>0.9$ $\mu$ sample: $V^{T=0}=-0.68(7)$ for even $J$, and for odd $J$ $V^{T=0}=-0.6(4)$, $V^{T=1}=0.4(2)$.

## Context

This work extends the random-interaction approach, previously applied mainly to single doubly even nuclei (Johnson, 2002; Lei, 2010), to a nuclear-systematics study of an odd-mass isotope chain. It addresses the linear systematics of $I^\pi=11/2^-$ electromagnetic moments in Cd isotopes reported experimentally (Ref. cd11) and previously studied with BCS (Ref. cd11-bcs), density functional theory (Ref. cd11-zhao), and schematic shell model (Ref. cd11-lei). The results show that quadrupole-like proton-neutron interaction drives the $Q$ linearity, while $\delta$-like $pn$ interaction with attractive $T=0$ and repulsive $T=1$ components drives the $\mu$ linearity, providing a new perspective on how simple regularities emerge from complex nuclear systems.
