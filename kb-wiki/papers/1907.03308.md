# System size scan of D meson $R_\text{AA}$ and $v_n$ using PbPb, XeXe, ArAr, and OO collisions at LHC

> Experimental measurements indicate no suppression (e.g. $R_\text{pPb} \sim 1$) but a surprisingly large D meson $v_2$ was measured in pPb collisions. In order to understand these results we use Trento+v-USPhydro+DAB-MOD to make predictions and propose a system size scan at the LHC involving $^{208}$PbPb, $^{129}$XeXe, $^{40}$ArAr, and $^{16}$OO collisions. We find that the nuclear modification factor approaches unity as the system size is decreased, but nonetheless, in the 0-10% most central collisions $v_2\{2\}$ is roughly equivalent regardless of system size. These results arise from a rather non-trivial interplay between the shrinking path length and the enhancement of eccentricities in small systems at high multiplicity. Finally, we also find a surprising sensitivity of D mesons $v_2\{2\}$ in 0-10% at $p_T = 2-10$ GeV to the slight deformation of $^{129}$Xe recently found at LHC.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that in central (0-10%) collisions, D meson elliptic flow v2{2} is roughly equivalent across PbPb, XeXe, ArAr, and OO systems despite the nuclear modification factor approaching unity as system size decreases, due to a nontrivial interplay between shrinking path length and enhanced eccentricities in small systems at high multiplicity.

## Method
The authors couple Trento initial conditions with event-by-event 2D+1 viscous hydrodynamics (v-USPhydro) and the heavy flavor code DAB-MOD, which uses pQCD FONLL distributions for heavy quark sampling and either a Langevin model (with spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(2πT)=2.23) or a parametrized energy loss model for heavy quark evolution. Hadronization proceeds via hybrid fragmentation/coalescence at decoupling temperature T_d=160 MeV. The same soft sector backgrounds are used as in Alba (2017), Giacalone (2017), and Sievert (2019), with Trento parameters p=0, k=1.6, σ=0.51 fm, and hydro parameters τ_0=0.6 fm, η/s=0.047, T_FO=150 MeV.

## Key numbers
- In 0-10% central OO collisions, the nuclear modification factor R_AA reaches a minimum close to 0.5 at intermediate p_T
- In 30-50% central OO collisions, R_AA reaches approximately 0.7-0.8 at its minimum
- The quantity (1-R_AA) is roughly proportional to the system initial radius ~ A^{1/3} for p_T ≳ 4 GeV
- D meson v2{2} in 0-10% central collisions shows sensitivity to 129Xe deformation for p_T = 2-10 GeV (Langevin up to 5 GeV, energy loss up to 10 GeV)
- The Langevin model uses spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(2πT)=2.23 from Moore (2004)

## Context
This work extends predictions for a system size scan at LHC energies proposed by Citron (2018), providing the first D meson predictions for intermediate systems such as ArAr and OO collisions. It builds on previous PbPb heavy flavor calculations from Katz (2019) and soft sector calculations from Sievert (2019), while addressing the puzzle that experimental pPb measurements show R_pPb ~ 1 (Adam 2015, Acharya 2019) but surprisingly large D meson v2 (CMS 2017). The results enable future comparisons with a proposed RHIC system size scan (Huang 2019) and may help distinguish between hydrodynamic and Color Glass Condensate scenarios for small systems.
