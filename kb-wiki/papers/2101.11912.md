# CP-Symmetry in Scattering of Neutrinos from Nuclei

> The elastic scattering of longitudinal and transversal neutrinos on a spinless nucleus have been discussed taking into account the charge, magnetic, anapole and electric dipole moments of fermions and their weak neutral currents. Compound structure of the neutrino interaction cross section with nuclei have been defined. Invariance of the considered process concerning the C - and P-operations have been investigated in the polarization type dependence.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit formulas for the differential cross sections and charge/right-left asymmetries in elastic electroweak scattering of both longitudinal and transversely polarized neutrinos (and antineutrinos) on spinless nuclei, showing that CP-symmetry properties depend on the polarization type. The authors claim that, regardless of the nature of C-symmetry, nonconservation of P-parity can be explained by interference between vector and axial-vector interaction terms, but this conclusion is conditional on the neutrino possessing a nonzero rest mass and electromagnetic moments.

## Method

The authors start from the standard electroweak Hamiltonian incorporating neutrino electromagnetic form factors (charge, magnetic, anapole, and electric dipole moments) and weak neutral currents. They compute the differential cross sections for elastic scattering of longitudinally and transversely polarized neutrinos on a spinless nucleus, then derive the charge asymmetry (C-odd) and right-left asymmetry (P-odd) for both polarization cases. The analysis uses the nucleus form factors for electromagnetic and weak charges, with the neutrino mass treated as a parameter.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino-nucleus scattering by Kerimov, Aruri, and Safin (1973, 1997) and by Frampton and Vogel (1982), which focused primarily on longitudinal polarization. The paper introduces the treatment of transverse neutrino polarization, which had been largely neglected in prior work, and shows that the right-left asymmetry in C-invariant processes differs between longitudinal and transverse polarization cases. The results enable experimental tests of CP-symmetry in neutrino-nucleus scattering and suggest that measuring right-left asymmetry at two different high energies could test the equality of the neutrino magnetic and electric dipole moments.
