# Finding quark content of neutron stars in light of GW170817

> The detection of gravitational waves from GW170817 has provided a new opportunity to constrain the equation of state (EOS) of neutron stars. In this article, we investigate the possible existence of quarks inside the neutron star core in the context of GW170817. The nucleon phase is treated within the relativistic nuclear mean-field approach where we have employed a fully comprehensive set of available models, and the quark phase is described in the Bag model. We show that the nucleonic EOSs which are inconsistent with the tidal deformability bound become consistent when phase transition to quark matter via Gibbs construction is allowed. We find that several nucleonic EOSs support the presence of pure quark matter core with a small mass not more than $0.17M_\odot$ confined within a radius of 0.9 km. We also find that the strong correlation between tidal deformability and neutron star radii observed for pure nucleonic stars does persist even with a nucleon-quark phase transition and provides an upper limit on the radius of $R_{1.4} \lesssim 12.9$ km for a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star.

## Key claim

The authors show that many nucleonic equations of state (EOSs) which are inconsistent with the tidal deformability bound from GW170817 become consistent when a phase transition to quark matter via Gibbs construction is allowed, but only for a restricted range of quark matter parameters; the tidal deformability constraint severely reduces the allowed quark matter parameter space regardless of the nucleonic EOS.

## Method

The nucleonic phase was treated using 29 relativistic mean-field (RMF) models that satisfy both saturation property constraints and the maximum mass bound of at least 1.97 solar masses. The quark phase was described using the modified MIT Bag model with two free parameters (the bag constant B_eff and the strong interaction correction parameter a_4), and the phase transition between nucleonic and quark matter was constructed via the Gibbs construction allowing a mixed phase. Tidal deformabilities were calculated by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the differential equation for the tidal Love number.

## Key numbers

- Only 3 out of 29 pure nucleonic RMF EOSs (HC, TW99, NLρ) are consistent with the tidal deformability bound Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 from GW170817
- For hybrid stars with quark phase transition, the maximum pure quark matter core mass is 0.17 M⊙ confined within a radius of 0.9 km
- The upper limit on the radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star is R₁.₄ ≤ 12.9 km, obtained from the correlation Λ₁.₄ = 5.22×10⁻⁵ (R₁.₄/km)⁶·³⁵
- For a₄ = 0.6, the maximum allowed bag constant B_eff¹/⁴ ranges from 146 MeV (for NL3σρ3 and NL3v3) to 163 MeV (for FSUGarnet and S271v6) while satisfying Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580
- The maximum mass of pure quark cores (ΔM_Q)max is 0.17 M⊙ for NL3σρ3 and NL3v3, but only 0.00-0.02 M⊙ for most other EOSs

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Nandi 2018) which found only 3 out of 269 RMF EOSs satisfied all constraints, by now systematically exploring the full quark matter parameter space for all viable nucleonic EOSs. The study uses the improved tidal deformability bound Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 from the LIGO-Virgo Collaboration's reanalysis of GW170817 (Abbott 2018), which is more stringent than the earlier bound of Λ₁.₄ ≤ 800. The results enable future searches for hybrid stars in gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers, particularly through the delayed phase transition scenario discussed by Weih (2019).
