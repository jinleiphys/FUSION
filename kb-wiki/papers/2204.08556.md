# Bottomonium-like tetraquarks in a chiral quark model

> The low-lying bottomonium-like tetraquarks $b\bar{b}q\bar{q}$ $(q=u,\,d,\,s)$ with spin-parity $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$, and isospin $I=0,\,1$ or $\frac{1}{2}$, are systematically investigated within the theoretical framework of real- and complex-scaling range of a chiral quark model, which has already been successfully applied in analysis of several multiquark systems. A complete four-body $S$-wave function, which includes meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark and K-type arrangements of quarks, along with all possible color configurations are considered. In the $b\bar{b}q\bar{q}$ $(q=u,\,d)$ tetraquark system, we found resonance states of $B^{(*)} \bar{B}^{(*)}$, $Υω$, $Υρ$ and $η_b ρ$ nature with all possible $I(J^P)$ quantum numbers. Their masses are generally located in the energy range $11.0-11.3$ GeV and their widths are less than $10$ MeV. In addition, extremely narrow resonances, with two-meson strong decay widths less than $1.5$ MeV, are obtained in both $b\bar{b}u\bar{s}$ and $b\bar{b}s\bar{s}$ tetraquark systems. Particularly, four radial excitations of $ΥK^*$ and $η_b K^*$ are found at $\sim11.1$ GeV in $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$ channels of $b\bar{b}u\bar{s}$ system. One $Υ(1S)ϕ(2S)$ resonance state is obtained at $11.28$ GeV in the $J^P=1^+$ sector.

## Key claim
The chiral quark model predicts resonance states in the \(b\bar{b}q\bar{q}\) \((q=u,d,s)\) tetraquark system with masses generally between 11.0 and 11.3 GeV and widths below 10 MeV, but these results depend on the specific configuration and quantum numbers considered; extremely narrow resonances (widths less than 1.5 MeV) are found only in the \(b\bar{b}u\bar{s}\) and \(b\bar{b}s\bar{s}\) systems.

## Method
The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation using a chiral quark model with real- and complex-scaling methods, incorporating a complete \(S\)-wave basis that includes meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and K-type quark arrangements with all possible color configurations. The Hamiltonian includes Goldstone boson exchange, confinement, and one-gluon exchange potentials, with parameters previously fixed from hadron and multiquark phenomenology. The complex-scaling method was used to distinguish bound states, resonances, and continuum states by rotating relative coordinates into the complex plane.

## Key numbers
- Resonance masses in the \(b\bar{b}q\bar{q}\) \((q=u,d)\) system are generally located in the energy range \(11.0-11.3\) GeV.
- Two-meson strong decay widths for these resonances are less than \(10\) MeV.
- Extremely narrow resonances in the \(b\bar{b}u\bar{s}\) and \(b\bar{b}s\bar{s}\) systems have widths less than \(1.5\) MeV.
- Four radial excitations of \(\Upsilon K^*\) and \(\eta_b K^*\) are found at \(\sim 11.1\) GeV in the \(J^P=0^+\), \(1^+\) and \(2^+\) channels of the \(b\bar{b}u\bar{s}\) system.
- One \(\Upsilon(1S)\phi(2S)\) resonance state is obtained at \(11.28\) GeV in the \(J^P=1^+\) sector.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model studies of hidden-charm tetraquarks with strangeness (Yang, 2021) to the bottomonium-like sector, and complements lattice QCD studies (Sadl, 2021) that found no significant attraction in \(b\bar{b}u\bar{d}\) tetraquarks. The results provide predictions for future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb and Belle II, and enable comparisons with effective field theory approaches (Dai, 2022) that suggest attraction in \(B_s^{(*)}B_s^{(*)}\) systems.
