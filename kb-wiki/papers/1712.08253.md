# Core-Collapse Supernova Simulations including Neutrino Interactions from the Virial EOS

> Core-collapse supernova explosions are driven by a central engine that converts a small fraction of the gravitational binding energy released during core collapse to outgoing kinetic energy. The suspected mode for this energy conversion is the neutrino mechanism, where a fraction of the neutrinos emitted from the newly formed protoneutron star are absorbed by and heat the matter behind the supernova shock. Accurate neutrino-matter interaction terms are crucial for simulating these explosions. In this proceedings for IAUS 331, SN 1987A, 30 years later, we explore several corrections to the neutrino-nucleon scattering opacity and demonstrate the effect on the dynamics of the core-collapse supernova central engine via two dimensional neutrino-radiation-hydrodynamics simulations. Our results reveal that the explosion properties are sensitive to corrections to the neutral-current scattering cross section at the 10-20% level, but only for densities at or above $\sim 10^{12}$ g cm$^{-3}$

## Key claim
Explosion properties in core-collapse supernova simulations are sensitive to corrections to the neutral-current scattering cross section at the 10-20% level, but only for densities at or above approximately 10^12 g cm^-3; at the relevant densities of 10^12-10^13 g cm^-3, the model-independent Virial EOS calculation begins to break down and model-dependent RPA calculations must be used.

## Method
The authors performed two-dimensional neutrino-radiation-hydrodynamics simulations using the FLASH code with an energy-dependent M1 neutrino transport scheme and 12 energy groups for three neutrino species (nu_e, nu_bar_e, and nu_x). They implemented corrections to the neutral-current neutrino-nucleon scattering cross section based on the Virial equation of state, which provides model-independent expressions for the axial response function S_A at low densities, transitioning to random phase approximation (RPA) calculations at high densities. Four progenitor models from Woosley & Heger (2007) were simulated with and without these corrections, and for model s20WH07 an additional 15 simulations were run with different random perturbations to the density field to assess statistical variations.

## Key numbers
- Explosion times for model s20WH07 without corrections: 723 +/- 63 ms (defined as shock crossing 400 km)
- Explosion times for model s20WH07 with only high-density (RPA) corrections: 597 +/- 37 ms
- Explosion times for model s20WH07 with full Virial + RPA corrections: 583 +/- 42 ms
- The Virial EOS contributions lower S_A by up to a factor of 2 more than RPA contributions at low densities
- Corrections to S_A at the level of approximately 20% influence dynamics at densities of 10^12-10^13 g cm^-3 and temperatures of approximately 10 MeV
- nu_x luminosity increases by approximately 10% when many-body corrections to the axial response function are included
- Explosion times are reduced by approximately 100-150 ms for models s15WH07, s20WH07, and s25WH07

## Context
This work extends the study of neutrino interaction corrections in core-collapse supernovae, following the model-independent Virial EOS approach of Horowitz et al. (2017) and comparing to the RPA-based work of Burrows et al. (1998). The results show more modest effects than those reported by Burrows et al. (2016), where the same corrections dramatically decreased explosion times. The findings enable future work toward better understanding of the neutrino response at densities of 10^12-10^13 g cm^-3, where model-independent calculations currently break down and improved theoretical input is needed.
