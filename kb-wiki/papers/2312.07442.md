# A new approach to stochastic relativistic fluid dynamics from information flow

> We present a new general formalism for introducing thermal fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics, which incorporates recent developments on the causality and stability of relativistic hydrodynamic theories. Our approach is based on the information current, which measures the net amount of information carried by perturbations around equilibrium in a relativistic many-body system. The resulting noise correlators are guaranteed to be observer-independent for thermodynamically stable models. We obtain an effective action within our formalism and discuss its properties.

# A new approach to stochastic relativistic fluid dynamics from information flow

## Key claim

The authors present a general formalism for introducing thermal fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics that is guaranteed to produce observer-independent noise correlators for thermodynamically stable models, provided the information current satisfies three specific conditions (positivity, uniqueness of equilibrium, and the second law of thermodynamics). The formalism is demonstrated for linearized Israel-Stewart-type theories but the authors suggest the symmetry derived for the effective action should extend to nonlinear systems.

## Context

This work extends the information current framework developed by Gavassino (2021) to construct stochastic fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics, incorporating recent developments on causality and stability. The formalism provides an alternative to approaches such as hydro-kinetics (Akamatsu 2016, Martinez 2018, An 2019) and Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory (Crossley 2015, Glorioso 2017), while being particularly well-suited for Israel-Stewart-type hydrodynamic models (Israel and Stewart 1979). The results enable self-consistent inclusion of thermal fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions, and the symmetry identified in the effective action suggests possible generalization to nonlinear fluctuating hydrodynamics.
