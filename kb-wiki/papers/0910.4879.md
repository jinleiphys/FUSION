# Nuclear medium modification of the F2 structure function

> We study the nuclear effects in the electromagnetic structure function F2(x,Q^2) in nuclei in the deep inelastic lepton nucleus scattering process by taking into account Fermi motion, binding, pion and rho meson cloud contributions. Calculations have been done in a local density approximation using relativistic nuclear spectral functions which include nucleon correlations for nuclear matter. The ratios over deuteron structure function are obtained and compared with the recent JLAB results for light nuclei with special attention to the slope of the x distributions. This magnitude shows a non trivial A dependence and it is insensitive to possible normalization uncertainties. The results have also been compared with some of the older experiments using intermediate mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a model incorporating Fermi motion, binding, pion and rho meson cloud contributions, and shadowing within a local density approximation using relativistic nuclear spectral functions can reproduce the slope of the nuclear structure function ratio \(R_{F2}^A(x,Q^2)\) for light nuclei measured at JLab, even though the slope does not scale with nuclear density as simple models predict. The hedge is that the mesonic cloud contribution is essential for this agreement but carries considerable uncertainties, and the model overestimates data for heavier nuclei by up to 3%.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear structure function \(F_2^A\) for nuclei in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering using a local density approximation. They obtain a relativistic nucleon spectral function from nuclear many-body theory for an interacting Fermi sea in nuclear matter, then apply the local density approximation to finite nuclei. They include contributions from Fermi motion, binding, pion and rho meson clouds (via many-body field theory), target mass corrections following Schienbein et al. (2007), and shadowing following Kulagin and Petti. The deuteron structure function is computed using a derivative expansion and the Paris wave function, and ratios \(R_{F2}^A(x,Q^2) = 2F_2^A/(A F_2^D)\) are compared with JLab data for light nuclei and SLAC data for intermediate-mass nuclei.

## Key numbers

- JLab electron beam energy: 5.767 GeV, with \(Q^2 \sim 3\text{--}6\) GeV\(^2\) for the data compared.
- For beryllium and carbon, the full model (including meson clouds and shadowing) agrees with JLab data in slope and ratio size; shadowing reduces the ratio by about 1% at \(x \sim 0.3\) and less at higher \(x\).
- Target mass corrections affect the ratio by less than 1% at \(x \sim 0.6\) and even less at lower \(x\).
- A 20% variation in the cutoff parameters \(\Lambda\) and \(\Lambda_\rho\) (from 1 GeV to 0.8 or 1.2 GeV) changes the ratio by 2–3%.
- For the deuteron, the derivative expansion and Paris wave function results differ by less than 0.6% up to \(x = 0.6\).
- For calcium and iron at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), the model overestimates SLAC data by about 3%.
- The mesonic cloud carries a light-cone momentum fraction of 3% for \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV in light nuclei; the sum rule prescription of 2% is obtained for \(\Lambda = 0.8\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the model of Marco et al. by including target mass corrections, using deuteron rather than free-nucleon structure functions for ratios, and incorporating shadowing following Kulagin and Petti. It directly confronts recent JLab results from Seely et al. (2009), which showed that the slope of the EMC effect for light nuclei does not scale with nuclear density, contradicting simple fits based on average density or mass number A (e.g., Gomez et al., SLAC). The results enable further tests of nuclear medium effects in deep inelastic scattering, particularly for light nuclei where local density models succeed but simple parametrizations fail, and highlight the need for improved understanding of mesonic cloud contributions and off-shell effects.
