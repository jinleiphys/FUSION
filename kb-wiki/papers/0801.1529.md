# Low excitation structure of $^{10}$B probed by scattering of electron and of 197 MeV polarized protons

> Cross-section and analyzing power data from 197 MeV $(p,p')$ scattering and longitudinal and transverse form factors for electron scattering to low lying states in $^{10}$B have been analyzed as tests of the structure of the nuclear states when they are described using a no-core $(0+2)\hbarω$ shell model. While the results obtained from the shell model clearly show the need of other elements, three-body forces in particular, to explain the observed spectrum, the reasonable level of agreement obtained in the analyses of the scattering data suggest that the wavefunctions from our shell model using only a two-body potential are credible. Any changes to the wavefunctions with the introduction of three-body forces in the shell model Hamiltonian should therefore be relatively minor.

## Key claim
The no-core $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ shell model using only a two-body potential produces wave functions that yield reasonable agreement with electron and proton scattering data for low-lying states in $^{10}$B, suggesting that any changes to these wave functions from introducing three-body forces should be relatively minor, even though the shell model fails to reproduce the correct ground state spin-parity (giving $1^+;0$ instead of the observed $3^+;0$).

## Method
The authors used a complete $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ shell model with the MK3W interaction to generate wave functions and one-body density matrix elements for $^{10}$B states. Electron scattering form factors were calculated in the Born approximation using bare operators with Siegert's theorem for transverse electric form factors and the Darwin correction for longitudinal form factors. Proton scattering at 197 MeV was analyzed using a $g$-folding model for the optical potential and the distorted wave approximation (DWA) with the Melbourne effective $NN$ force, using Woods-Saxon single-nucleon bound-state wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole moment of the $3^+_1$ ground state: calculated value $+6.708$ $e$fm$^2$, compared to experimental $+8.472(56)$ $e$fm$^2$
- $B(E2;1^+_1 \rightarrow 3^+_1)$: calculated 3.185 $e^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental $4.13 \pm 0.06$ $e^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2;1^+_2 \rightarrow 3^+_1)$: calculated 0.270 $e^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental $1.71 \pm 0.26$ $e^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2;1^+_2 \rightarrow 1^+_1)$: calculated 1.172 $e^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental $0.83 \pm 0.40$ $e^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2;3^+_2 \rightarrow 1^+_1)$: calculated 9.057 $e^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental $20.5 \pm 2.6$ $e^2$fm$^4$
- Electron scattering form factor data covered momentum transfer range 0.48 to 2.58 fm$^{-1}$ using six beam energies from 48 to 453 MeV (Cichocki et al., 1995)
- Proton scattering data at 197 MeV from Betker et al. (2005)

## Context
This work extends the approach previously applied to $^{12}$C by Karataglidis et al. (1995), using the same $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ shell model and $g$-folding reaction framework. It addresses deficiencies in earlier analyses by Betker et al. (2005) and Cichocki et al. (1995), who used limited $0\hbar\omega$ shell model spaces requiring ad hoc scaling of effective charges and oscillator lengths. The results enable future tests of three-body forces in shell model Hamiltonians for $^{10}$B, as the correct ground state spin-parity inversion (known from ab initio calculations by Caurier et al., 2002, and quantum Monte Carlo by Pieper, Varga and Wiringa, 2002) requires three-body forces or inclusion of $4\hbar\omega$ components to resolve.
