# The realistic collective nuclear Hamiltonian

> The residual part of the realistic forces ---obtained after extracting the monopole terms responsible for bulk properties--- is strongly dominated by pairing and quadrupole interactions, with important $στ\cdotστ$, octupole and hexadecapole contributions. Their forms differ from the schematic ones through normalizations that lead to a universal $A^{-1/3}$ scaling for all multipoles. Coupling strengths and effective charges are calculated and shown to agree with the observed values.

## Key claim

The residual realistic nuclear Hamiltonian, after removing monopole terms, is dominated by pairing and quadrupole interactions (with additional contributions from sigma-tau, octupole, and hexadecapole terms) that appear in a normalized form, leading to a universal A^{-1/3} scaling for all multipoles; the coupling strengths and effective charges calculated from this approach agree with observed values, but the claim is conditional on the choice of realistic interaction being largely unimportant and on the Hamiltonian being trusted rather than tested.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the residual multipole Hamiltonian H_M in both the normal (W) and multipole (omega) representations using the KLS realistic force for spaces of one and two major oscillator shells (pf and sdg). They identified the dominant eigenstates in the e-representation (multipole form) and compared them with normalized versions of standard pairing and multipole operators (P_01, P_10, sigma-tau, r^2Y_2, sigma, r^3Y_3, r^4Y_4). They then extracted a collective Hamiltonian H_C by eliminating states with |e_gamma| above a cutoff until the E-distribution became symmetric, and estimated renormalizations due to core polarization using the quasiconfiguration method.

## Key numbers

- The normalized pairing operator P_01 has an overlap of 0.992 with the diagonalized eigenvector in the pf shell and 0.998 in the sdg shell; for P_10 the overlaps are 0.928 and 0.910 respectively.
- The normalized quadrupole operator r^2Y_2 has an overlap of 0.996 with the diagonalized eigenvector in the pf shell and 0.997 in the sdg shell.
- The dominant eigenvalues for the two-shell (pf+sdg) space are: E_01 = -5.51 MeV, E_10 = -10.12 MeV, e_20 = -3.88 MeV, e_11 = 3.90 MeV, e_10 = -1.96 MeV, e_30 = 2.69 MeV, e_40 = -2.11 MeV.
- The universal scaling factor is hbar-omega proportional to A^{-1/3}, with hbar-omega_0 = 9 MeV used as reference.
- The pairing renormalization from core polarization gives a correction factor of (1 + 0.32) for the coupling constant E_01.
- The norm of the normalized pairing operator Omega_p is approximately 0.655 A_mp^{2/3}; the norm of the normalized quadrupole operator N_p^2 is approximately 0.085 A_mp^{4/3}.

## Context

This work extends the separation of the nuclear Hamiltonian into a monopole field and a residual multipole part established in the preceding paper by Zuker and Dufour (1995), and builds on realistic interactions from Abzouzi et al. (1991) and Caurier et al. (1994). The results show that the realistic residual interaction can be reduced to a simple normalized form that avoids the space-dependent coupling constants that plagued the traditional pairing-plus-quadrupole model (discussed in Appendix B). This enables the construction of a collective Hamiltonian suitable for large-scale shell model calculations and Monte Carlo techniques (as in Alhassid et al.), and provides a foundation for a mass formula developed in Dufour and Zuker (1995).
