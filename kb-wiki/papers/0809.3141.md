# Holographic Nuclei

> We provide a dual gravity description of heavy atomic nuclei, via AdS/CFT correspondence. In holographic QCD such as Sakai-Sugimoto model, baryons are D-branes wrapping a sphere in 10 dimensional curved spacetime, so any nucleus is a collection of $A$ such D-branes where $A$ is mass number of the nucleus. Quantum theory on the nucleus is ADHM-like U(A) Yang-Mills-Higgs theory on the sphere. Taking a large $A$ limit (corresponding to heavy nuclei) leads to a dual gravity describing collective excitataions of constituent nucleons of the heavy nucleus. This dual gravity computes spectra of the heavy nucleus, and gives discrete states whose gap roughly agrees with experimental nuclear data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a dual gravity description of heavy atomic nuclei via the AdS/CFT correspondence, but the claim is hedged by the need to assume a specific gravitating dyonic instanton solution and a large mass number limit \(A \to \infty\), with the resulting spectrum matching experimental data only in order of magnitude after neglecting all numerical factors.

## Method

The author works within the Sakai-Sugimoto (SS) model of holographic QCD, where baryons are D4-branes wrapping an \(S^4\) sphere in a curved 10-dimensional spacetime. A nucleus with mass number \(A\) is treated as a collection of \(A\) such D4-branes, whose worldvolume theory is a \(U(A)\) Yang-Mills-Higgs theory on \(\mathbf{R} \times S^4\). Taking the large \(A\) limit, the back-reaction of the D4-branes on the geometry is considered, and a near-horizon limit is taken to obtain a dual gravity description; the resulting black 4-brane solution with a 3-ball distribution of singularities is used to compute fluctuation spectra of bulk fields such as the dilaton.

## Key numbers

* The size of a single baryon instanton in the SS model is \(\rho = 3\sqrt{3\pi} \left(\frac65\right)^{1/4} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\lambda}M_{\rm KK}}\), with \(M_{\rm KK} \sim 500\) MeV.
* The nucleus radius scales as \(R_N \sim \frac{A^{1/3}}{\sqrt{\lambda} M_{\rm KK}}\).
* The effective "AdS radius" from the back-reacted geometry is \(R_0 = \frac32 \frac{A^{1/3}}{N_c^{1/3} M_{\rm KK}}\).
* The predicted excitation spectrum for heavy nuclei is \(E_n \sim \frac{N_c^{2/3}}{A^{1/3}\sqrt{\lambda}} M_{\rm KK} \; n\) for \(n=1,2,\dots\).
* With typical SS model parameters (\(M_{\rm KK} \sim 500\) MeV, \(\lambda \sim \mathcal{O}(10)-\mathcal{O}(100)\), \(N_c=3\)), the gap is \(\sim \mathcal{O}(10^2) \times A^{-1/3}\) MeV, compared to the experimental giant resonance formula \(E \sim 80 A^{-1/3}\) MeV for \(A>60\).

## Context

This work extends holographic QCD, previously applied to hadron physics (e.g., glueballs, mesons, baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model), to nuclear physics by providing a dual gravity description of heavy nuclei. It builds on the description of baryons as D-branes in AdS/CFT (Gross and Ooguri) and on the SS model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005), and it draws on analyses of distributed D3-branes in Coulomb branch (Freedman et al., 1999; Brandhuber et al., 1999; Gubser, 1999) to argue for a discrete spectrum. The result enables future comparison with nuclear shell model and liquid drop model, and suggests a possible holographic connection to nuclear hydrodynamics.
