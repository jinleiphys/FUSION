# Bose condensation far from equilibrium

> The formation of Bose condensates far from equilibrium can play an important role in our understanding of collision experiments of heavy nuclei or for the evolution of the early universe. In the relativistic quantum world particle number changing processes can counteract Bose condensation, and there is a considerable debate about the relevance of this phenomenon in this context. We show that the involved question of Bose condensation from initial over-population can be answered for the example of scalar field theories. Condensate formation occurs as a consequence of an inverse particle cascade with a universal power-law spectrum. This particle transport towards low momenta is part of a dual cascade, in which energy is also transfered by weak wave turbulence towards higher momenta. To highlight the importance of number changing processes for the subsequent decay of the condensate, we also compare to non-relativistic theories with exact number conservation. We discuss the relevance of these results for nonabelian gauge theories.

## Key claim

Bose condensation far from equilibrium generically occurs from initial over-population as a consequence of an inverse particle cascade with a universal power-law spectrum, but only for scalar field theories; the generalization to nonabelian gauge theories remains an open question because particle number changing processes in the relativistic quantum world can counteract condensation.

## Method

The authors employed classical-statistical field theory simulations on a space-time lattice for a relativistic N=4 component scalar field theory with quartic self-coupling in d=3 spatial dimensions, sampling initial conditions to generate over-populated distributions up to a characteristic momentum scale Q_s. They also performed analogous classical-statistical simulations of the non-relativistic Gross-Pitaevskii equation for comparison, and used two-particle irreducible (2PI) resummed large-N expansion to next-to-leading order to derive effective kinetic equations describing the cascading regimes.

## Key numbers

- Initial occupation number at characteristic momentum scale Q_s is proportional to the inverse quartic self-coupling: n(Q_s) ~ 1/lambda, with lambda << 1
- For momenta p >= Q_s, the direct energy cascade follows the power-law distribution n(p) ~ (Q_s/p)^(d-3/2) in d spatial dimensions
- For momenta p <= Q_s, the inverse particle cascade follows the power-law distribution n(p) ~ (Q_s/p)^(d+1) in d spatial dimensions
- The effective coupling at low momenta scales as lambda^eff(p <= Q_s) ~ lambda * (p/Q_s)^8
- The dual cascade operates during a long time interval t*Q_s ~ 100 - 10000
- For the non-relativistic theory, the infrared particle cascade exponent is d+2 (giving p^(-5) in d=3)
- For SU(2) gauge theory simulations in d=3, the weak wave turbulence exponent is 3/2, matching the relativistic scalar result

## Context

This work extends the understanding of Bose condensation from close-to-equilibrium scenarios (as in ultracold rubidium atoms, NP) to far-from-equilibrium situations relevant for heavy-ion collisions (Blaizot 2011) and early-universe inflaton dynamics (PH). The dual cascade with directed fluxes had previously been found in non-relativistic systems of cold atoms (Scheppach 2009, BS) and in early-universe inflaton dynamics (Berges 2008). The results enable further investigation of condensation in nonabelian gauge theories, where classical-statistical simulations (Berges 2008) and first non-perturbative studies of coherent gauge field dynamics (Berges 2011) provide starting points, but gauge-invariant signatures of condensation remain to be devised.
