# Pion condensation versus 2SC, speed of sound, and charge neutrality effects in the quark-meson diquark model

> We employ the two-flavor quark-meson diquark model as a low-energy model for QCD at non-zero quark and isospin chemical potentials $μ$ and $μ_I$, and at zero temperature. We map out the phase diagram in the $μ$-$μ_I$ plane, which has four phases: a vacuum phase, a phase with condensed charged pions/Cooper pairs of $u$ and $\bar{d}$ quarks, a normal quark matter phase, and a color superconducting phase (2SC phase). %In the 2SC phase, we study the effects of imposing color and %electric charge neutrality. The global symmetry breaking $SU(3)_c\rightarrow SU(2)_c$ in the 2SC phase gives rise to a number of Nambu-Goldstone bosons. We classify them and briefly discuss their properties. We calculate the speed of sound $c_s$ in the two special cases, finite $μ_I$ and $μ=0$, and finite $μ$ and $μ_I=0$. In both cases, the speed of sound exhibits a maximum and approaches the conformal limit from above as the density increases. For non-zero isospin $μ_I$, this behavior is in agreement with the speed of sound obtained from lattice simulations. In the 2SC phase, the behavior is qualitatively the same if we impose local charge neutrality. Finally, we discuss the possibility of having a mixed phase of negatively charged normal quark matter and positively charged 2SC matter with global color charge neutrality imposed on the latter.

## Key claim

The quark-meson diquark model predicts that the speed of sound in both pure isospin matter and pure baryon matter exhibits a maximum and approaches the conformal limit from above as density increases, with the isospin case agreeing with lattice QCD results; however, this behavior in the 2SC phase is only qualitatively the same when local charge neutrality is imposed.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor quark-meson diquark (QMD) model, a renormalizable low-energy effective model with meson, quark, and diquark degrees of freedom, to compute the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature including one-loop quark fluctuations. They mapped the phase diagram in the plane of quark chemical potential μ and isospin chemical potential μ_I, and calculated the speed of sound for two special cases: finite μ_I with μ=0, and finite μ with μ_I=0, imposing local electric and color charge neutrality conditions in the 2SC phase.

## Key numbers

- The critical isospin chemical potential for the onset of pion condensation is μ_I^c = m_π, with the transition being second order at T=0.
- In the pion-condensed phase, the speed of sound squared is c_s^2 = (μ_I^4 - m_π^4)/(μ_I^4 + 3m_π^4), approaching unity in the ultra-relativistic limit μ_I → ∞.
- Chiral perturbation theory is estimated to be reliable for μ_I up to 200 MeV based on convergence properties.
- In the 2SC phase, the gapless phase (g2SC) occurs when the mismatch parameter δμ ≥ g_ΔΔ_0, where δμ = μ_e/2.
- The 2SC phase contains five Nambu-Goldstone bosons from SU(3)_c → SU(2)_c breaking: one type-A (linear dispersion) and two type-B (quadratic dispersion), with two massive partners of gap 4μ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formulation of the QMD model (Andersen and Nødtvedt, 2024) to include the full phase diagram in the μ-μ_I plane and the effects of charge neutrality. The pion condensation results connect to model-independent predictions from chiral perturbation theory (Son and Stephanov, 2001) and lattice QCD simulations (Abbott et al., 2023, 2024), while the 2SC phase analysis relates to earlier NJL model studies of color superconductivity (Rajagopal and Wilczek, 2001; Rüster et al., 2005; Abuki, 2007). The work enables future comparisons between effective model predictions and lattice data for the equation of state of dense matter relevant to compact star physics.
