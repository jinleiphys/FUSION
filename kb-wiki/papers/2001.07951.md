# Traces of non-equilibrium effects, initial condition, bulk dynamics and elementary collisions in the charm observables

> We study how heavy quark dynamics is affected by the nature of the bulk evolution of the QCD matter, the initial condition of the system, and the treatment of elementary interactions between heavy quarks and the surrounding medium. For the same initial condition and the same QGP expansion scenario we discuss the consequences of the assumption of a local equilibrium by comparing the consequences for the nuclear modification factor and the elliptic flows of charm quarks. For this purpose we employ the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD), which is an off-shell microscopic transport approach, as well as the linearized-Boltzmann (LB) scheme obtained by coarse graining the PHSD bulk and assuming local equilibrium for the interactions of the charm quarks with the bulk. The $R_{\rm AA}$ of charm quarks stemming from the later LB approach is also compared to a genuine fluid dynamics evolution initiated by the coarse grained PHSD, which allows to further assess the consequences of reducing the full n-body dynamics. We then proceed to a systematic comparison of PHSD with MC@HQ, another transport model for heavy flavors which also relies on LB approach. In particular, we investigate the consequences for the nuclear modification factor of charm quarks if we vary separately the initial heavy quark distribution function in matter, the expansion dynamics of the QGP and the elementary interactions of heavy quarks of these models. We find that the results for both models vary significantly depending on the details of the calculation. However, both models achieve very similar predictions for key heavy quark observables for certain combinations of initial condition, bulk evolution and interactions. We conclude that this ambiguity limits our ability to determine the different properties of the system based on the current set of observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of charm quarks vary significantly depending on the details of the calculation (initial condition, bulk dynamics, and elementary interactions), different models can produce very similar predictions for key heavy quark observables for certain combinations of these components, which limits the ability to determine the different properties of the system based on the current set of observables.

## Method

The authors employed the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) model, an off-shell microscopic transport approach, and compared it with a linearized-Boltzmann (LB) scheme obtained by coarse graining the PHSD bulk and assuming local equilibrium for charm quark interactions. They also compared PHSD (in its LB approximation) with MC@HQ, another transport model for heavy flavors that also relies on the LB approach. The study separately varied the initial heavy quark distribution function, the expansion dynamics of the QGP (comparing PHSD with 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics initialized by PHSD), and the elementary interactions of heavy quarks to isolate the influence of each component on charm observables.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear modification factor R_AA of charm quarks at T_c before hadronization in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and b = 2 fm shows that in the LB approach, R_AA is larger at low transverse momentum (p_T < 1 GeV) and a bit smaller around p_T = 2 GeV compared to PHSD.
* The elliptic flow v_2 of D mesons at freeze-out at mid-rapidity (|y| < 1) in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and b = 6 fm is compared with experimental data from the STAR collaboration.
* The transverse pressure in PHSD converges to the isotropic pressure before tau = 1 fm/c in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
* The grid size in the (t,z) coordinate system uses N_z = 38 cells, with cell size dz = 1/gamma_cm initially, growing linearly with time after the nuclei pass through each other.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Song et al. (2019) that examined non-equilibrium effects on transport coefficients of heavy quarks using the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM). The study also builds on comparisons between PHSD and hydrodynamics by Xu et al. (2017) that focused on macroscopic properties of the QGP medium, extending the comparison to heavy quark interactions. The paper enables future work to disentangle the effects of initial conditions, bulk dynamics, and elementary interactions in heavy flavor observables, while highlighting the ambiguity that limits model discrimination based on current measurements.
