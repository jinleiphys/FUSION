# Cumulative pion production via successive collisions in nuclear medium

> Production of pions in proton-nucleus (p+A) reactions outside of a kinematical boundary of proton-nucleon collisions, the so-called cumulative effect, is studied. The kinematical restrictions on pions emitted in backward direction in the target rest frame are analyzed. It is shown that cumulative pion production requires a presence of massive baryonic resonances that are produced during successive collisions of projectile with nuclear nucleons. After each successive collision the mass of created resonance may increase and, simultaneously, its longitudinal velocity decreases. Simulations within Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model reveals that successive collisions of baryonic resonances with nuclear nucleons plays the dominant role in cumulative pion production in p+A reactions.

# Cumulative pion production via successive collisions in nuclear medium

## Key claim
Cumulative pions in proton-nucleus reactions are produced by baryonic resonances with very high masses that are formed through successive inelastic collisions with nuclear nucleons, where each collision can increase the resonance mass and decrease its longitudinal velocity. The probability to reach the maximal kinematic limits in this process is very small, making cumulative pion production a rare process.

## Method
The authors performed a one-dimensional kinematic analysis of successive baryonic resonance collisions with nuclear nucleons (p+N→R₁+N, R₁+N→R₂+N, ..., Rₙ+N→N+N+π(180°)), deriving maximal pion energies E*π,ₙ after n successive collisions under extremum conditions. They then simulated p+²⁰⁸Pb collisions using the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, tracking the number of successive collisions each resonance underwent before decaying to a pion at 180° in the target rest frame.

## Key numbers
- At projectile momentum p₀=6 GeV/c in p+²⁰⁸Pb collisions, the number of UrQMD events was N_ev=5.7×10⁷; at p₀=158 GeV/c, N_ev=4.2×10⁷.
- For n≥4 successive collisions, the resonance velocity vₙ* becomes negative (backward-moving) at finite regions of projectile momentum p₀, meaning heavy resonances may start to move backward after many collisions for not too large p₀.
- The maximal pion energies E*π,ₙ after n successive collisions are shown as functions of p₀ in Fig. 1, with the kinematic boundary for p+N collisions (E*π,₁) being exceeded by contributions from n=2 and n≥3 collisions in UrQMD simulations.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Gorenstein (1977) for cumulative particle production via successive baryonic resonance collisions, contrasting with other theoretical models whose references are given in the authors' recent paper (Motornenko and Gorenstein, 2016). The analysis supports the physical picture that heavy, slow-moving resonances formed during the evolution of p+A reactions are responsible for cumulative pions, and the UrQMD simulations confirm that successive collisions dominate the kinematic region forbidden for proton-nucleon collisions. The results enable further microscopic studies of cumulative effects in heavy-ion collisions and could be tested against experimental data from the Synchrophasotron accelerator at Dubna (Baldin 1974, 1977; Leksin 1977) and other experiments (Frankel 1979; Bayukov 1979).
