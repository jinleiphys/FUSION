# On the nucleons motion in the nucleus as being due to realistic inter-nucleonic forces

> This paper deals with the possible motion of nucleons in the nucleus, which is due to realistic inter-nucleonic forces. This approach provides new or more substantiated conclusions about the nuclear structure than those based on the effective interaction of nucleons, while the shell model of the nucleus may lead to questionable conclusions regarding the nuclear structure and nuclear reaction mechanisms.

# On the nucleons motion in the nucleus as being due to realistic inter-nucleonic forces

## Key claim
The paper argues that realistic inter-nucleonic forces (rather than effective interactions) lead to nucleon pairing and clustering, while non-central forces and three-nucleon forces cause spin-flips that break up those pairs; the shell model may therefore produce questionable conclusions about nuclear structure and reaction mechanisms, particularly regarding the origin of spin S=1 transitions in photonuclear reactions on ⁴He.

## Method
The author compares CD-Bonn NN potential phase shifts (from elastic p,p and n,p scattering up to 350 MeV) with nucleon momentum distributions in ⁴He calculated using realistic NN potentials and three-nucleon forces (UrbanaIX and Tucson-Melbourne) from prior work. Experimental data on the ⁴He(γ,p)³H and ⁴He(γ,n)³He reactions are analyzed to extract the ratio of S=1 transition cross-sections to the dominant electric dipole S=0 cross-section, testing whether these S=1 transitions originate from initial-state spin configurations rather than from mesonic exchange current spin-flip mechanisms.

## Key numbers
- The CD-Bonn NN potential phase analysis covers elastic scattering in the energy range up to 350 MeV for total momentum J ≤ 4 of the NN system.
- The contribution of S=1 transitions in ⁴He(γ,p)³H and ⁴He(γ,n)³He reactions is about ∼10⁻² of the total reaction cross-section.
- In the photon energy range 22 ≤ E_γ ≤ 100 MeV, the ratio ν_p (for proton emission) is 0.01 ± 0.002 and ν_n (for neutron emission) is 0.015 ± 0.003, averaged over that range.
- The total cross-section of the reaction changes by a factor of ∼15 over the photon energy range 22–100 MeV.
- The contribution of D-wave to the deuteron wave function is ∼5%, used as an estimate for spin-flip probability increase with nucleon number A.
- For medium and heavy nuclei, the spin-orbit interaction constant b reaches b ∼ 10 and increases with A.

## Context
This work extends calculations by the author's group (Lyakhno, references [7] and [12]) using realistic NN potentials from Machleidt (2001) and three-nucleon forces from UrbanaIX and Tucson-Melbourne, applied to ⁴He. It contradicts the shell-model interpretation of S=1 transitions in photonuclear reactions, which prior work by Gari and Hebach (reference [9]) and experimental groups (Balestra et al., Jones et al., Arkatov et al., Shima et al.) attributed to mesonic exchange currents causing spin-flip. The paper suggests that future work should consider initial-state spin configurations in nuclei rather than assuming all even-even nuclei have spin zero, and that the shell model's assignment of s- and p-shell knockout may need revision.
