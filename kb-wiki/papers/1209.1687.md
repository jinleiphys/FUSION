# Nucleon Structure from Lattice QCD Using a Nearly Physical Pion Mass

> We report the first Lattice QCD calculation using the almost physical pion mass mpi=149 MeV that agrees with experiment for four fundamental isovector observables characterizing the gross structure of the nucleon: the Dirac and Pauli radii, the magnetic moment, and the quark momentum fraction. The key to this success is the combination of using a nearly physical pion mass and excluding the contributions of excited states. An analogous calculation of the nucleon axial charge governing beta decay has inconsistencies indicating a source of bias at low pion masses not present for the other observables and yields a result that disagrees with experiment.

# Nucleon Structure from Lattice QCD Using a Nearly Physical Pion Mass

## Key claim
Using a nearly physical pion mass of 149 MeV combined with careful removal of excited-state contamination, lattice QCD achieves agreement with experiment for four isovector nucleon observables (Dirac and Pauli radii, magnetic moment, and quark momentum fraction) for the first time; however, the axial charge g_A shows inconsistencies at low pion masses and disagrees with experiment, indicating a source of bias not present for the other observables.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using ten ensembles of gauge fields generated with O(a^2)-improved Symanzik gauge action and tree-level clover-improved Wilson fermions with 2-HEX stout gauge links, simulating two light and one strange quark flavor dynamically. Nucleon matrix elements were extracted from three-point functions using the sequential source method, with excited-state contamination removed by varying the source-sink separation from approximately 0.9 to 1.4 fm and applying the summation method. Chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass were performed using Chiral Perturbation Theory fits over the range 149 to 254 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass of the lightest ensemble: m_pi = 149(1) MeV, only approximately 10% above the physical isospin-limit value of 134.8 MeV
- Dirac radius: (r_1^2)^v = 0.577(29) fm^2, deviating from the mu-p Lamb shift experimental value by -0.07 sigma_lat
- Anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_v = 3.69(38), deviating from experiment by -0.04 sigma_lat
- Pauli radius combination: kappa_v (r_2^2)^v = 2.46(25) fm^2, deviating from experiment by -0.04 sigma_lat
- Quark momentum fraction: <x>_{u-d} = 0.140(21), deviating from the CTEQ6 phenomenological value by -0.71 sigma_lat
- Axial charge: g_A = 0.97(8), deviating from the experimental value 1.2701(25) by -3.75 sigma_lat
- Precision achieved: 5% for (r_1^2)^v, 10% for kappa_v and kappa_v (r_2^2)^v, and 15% for <x>_{u-d}

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations that relied on large chiral extrapolations from pion masses above 250 MeV, where observables like (r_1^2)^v were underestimated by approximately 50% and <x>_{u-d} overestimated by 30-60% compared to experiment (Syritsyn 2009, Yamazaki 2009, Alexandrou 2011, Collins 2011, Hagler 2007, Bali 2012). The key advance is performing calculations at a pion mass so close to physical that chiral extrapolation changes are smaller than statistical uncertainties, eliminating dependence on ChPT convergence. The results enable future lattice calculations to resolve the proton charge radius puzzle by achieving precision comparable to the discrepancy between the CODATA value from hydrogen spectroscopy and the mu-p Lamb shift measurement (Pohl 2010), while the axial charge discrepancy motivates further study of thermal state effects, chiral symmetry breaking, or long-range autocorrelations as possible sources of bias at low pion masses.
