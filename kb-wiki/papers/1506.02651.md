# Correct Definition of the Gluon Distribution Function at High Energy Colliders

> Unlike QED, since F_{μν}^a(x)F^{μνa}(0) in QCD contains cubic and quartic powers of the gluon field the present definition of the gluon distribution function at high energy colliders is not consistent with the number operator interpretation of the gluon. In this paper we derive the correct definition of the gluon distribution function at high energy colliders from first principles which is consistent with the number operator interpretation of the gluon and is gauge invariant and is consistent with the factorization theorem in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard definition of the gluon distribution function at high energy colliders is incorrect because it uses the field strength tensor \(F_{\mu\nu}^a(x)F^{\mu\nu a}(0)\), which contains cubic and quartic powers of the gluon field, making it inconsistent with the number operator interpretation of the gluon. The author derives a new definition based on the quantum gluon field \(Q_\mu^a\) directly, which is gauge invariant and consistent with the factorization theorem in QCD.

## Method

The author uses the path integral formulation of the background field method of QCD, treating the soft-collinear gluon field as an SU(3) pure gauge background field. By analyzing the non-perturbative gluon correlation function in the presence of this background field, the author shows that the correct gluon distribution function must involve the quantum gluon field \(Q_\mu^a\) rather than the field strength tensor \(F_{\mu\nu}^a\). The derivation is performed in covariant gauge, light-cone gauge, general axial gauges, general non-covariant gauges, and general Coulomb gauge, with the final expression including a light-like Wilson line in the adjoint representation.

## Key numbers

- The present definition of the gluon distribution function (eq. \ref{gpdf}) uses \(F^{+\mu a}(0,y^-,0_T) \times F_\mu^{+a}(0)\), which contains cubic and quartic powers of the quantum gluon field \(Q^{\mu a}(x)\).
- The correct definition (eq. \ref{gpdffpi}) is \(f_{g/P}(x) = \frac{P^+}{x2\pi} \int dy^- e^{-ixP^+ y^-} \langle P| Q_\mu^a(0,y^-,0_T) [\mathcal{P}\exp[igT^{(A)b}\int_0^{y^-} dz^- A^{+b}(0,z^-,0_T)]] Q^{\mu a}(0) |P \rangle\).
- The light-like Wilson line in QCD for soft-collinear divergences is given by \(\mathcal{P}\exp[ig \int_0^\infty d\lambda \, l\cdot A^a(l\lambda) T^a]\) (eq. \ref{iiij}).

## Context

This work extends the foundational approach to parton distribution functions by Collins (1989) and the background field method of QCD developed by 't Hooft (1971), Kluberg-Stern and Zuber (1975), and Abbott (1981). The paper corrects the inconsistency in the standard gluon PDF definition, which previously used the field strength tensor and thus violated the number operator interpretation. This enables a consistent definition of the gluon distribution function that can be used in factorization theorems for high-energy collider processes, such as Higgs production via gluon-gluon fusion at the LHC.
