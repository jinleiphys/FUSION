# Self-consistent microscopic calculations for electron captures on nuclei in core-collapse supernovae

> Calculations for electron capture rates on nuclei with atomic numbers between $Z=20$ and $Z=52$ are performed in a self-consistent finite-temperature covariant energy density functional theory within the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation. Electron captures on these nuclei contribute most to reducing the electron fraction during the collapse phase of core-collapse supernovae. The rates include contributions from allowed (Gamow-Teller) and first-forbidden (FF) transitions, and it is shown that the latter become dominant at high stellar densities and temperatures. Temperature-dependent effects such as Pauli unblocking and transitions from thermally excited states are also included. The new rates are implemented in a spherically symmetric 1D simulation of the core-collapse phase. The results indicate that the increase in electron capture rates, due to inclusion of FF transitions, leads to reductions of the electron fraction at nuclear saturation density, the peak neutrino luminosity, and enclosed mass at core bounce. The new rates reaffirm that the most relevant nuclei for the deleptonization situate around the $N = 50$ and $82$ shell closures, but compared to previous simulations, nuclei are less proton rich. The new rates developed in this work are available, and will be of benefit to improve the accuracy of multi-dimensional supernova simulations.

# Self-consistent microscopic calculations for electron captures on nuclei in core-collapse supernovae

## Key claim
The new electron capture rates, calculated self-consistently within finite-temperature covariant energy density functional theory, show that first-forbidden transitions become dominant at high stellar densities and temperatures, leading to a 3% reduction of the electron fraction at saturation density, a 5% reduction in peak neutrino luminosity, and a 7% reduction in enclosed mass at core bounce, but these results depend on the specific nuclear composition model used, and the most relevant nuclei for deleptonization shift toward less proton-rich species near the N=82 shell closure.

## Method
The authors performed finite-temperature relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FT-pnRQRPA) calculations based on a covariant energy density functional with the D3C* interaction, using finite-temperature Hartree-Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (FT-HBCS) theory to determine the nuclear basis for 1652 nuclei from Z=20 to Z=52. Electron capture rates were computed including allowed Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden (Jπ = 0−, 1−, 2−) transitions from ground and thermally excited states, covering temperatures from 0.01 to 50 GK and densities from ρYe = 10¹ to 10¹⁴ g/cm³, then implemented in a 1D spherically symmetric core-collapse supernova simulation using the GR1D code with the s15WW95 progenitor and SHFo equation of state.

## Key numbers
- Electron capture rates were calculated for 1652 individual nuclei from calcium (Z=20) to tellurium (Z=52).
- At ρ = 10¹² g/cm³, the new FT-pnRQRPA rates are up to 4 orders of magnitude larger than the analytic approximation from PhysRevC.95.025805 for neutron-rich nuclei.
- Including first-forbidden transitions results in a 3% reduction of the electron fraction Ye close to nuclear saturation density (nsat ≈ 2.81 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³).
- The peak neutrino luminosity decreases by almost 5% and is delayed by approximately 0.7 ms when first-forbidden transitions are included.
- The enclosed mass at core bounce (proto-neutron star mass) is reduced by about 7% with first-forbidden transitions included.
- The quenching factor applied to Gamow-Teller strength in this work is approximately 0.64, compared to approximately 0.5 in the rates from Juodagalvis et al. (2010).
- At μe ≈ 10 MeV (roughly T ≈ 10 GK and log₁₀ ρYe ≈ 9), the new FT-pnRQRPA rates are larger than the shell-model rates for pf-shell nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the electron capture rate library previously developed by Sullivan et al. (2016) and Juodagalvis et al. (2010), which used hybrid shell-model Monte Carlo and random-phase approximation approaches with Fermi-Dirac parameterizations for occupation numbers. The new rates provide a self-consistent microscopic treatment without the approximations and extrapolations that omitted detailed nuclear structure effects, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei where shell-model calculations are unavailable. The results confirm the finding of Sullivan et al. (2016) that nuclei near the N=50 and N=82 shell closures dominate deleptonization, but show that nuclei near N=82 become less proton rich when first-forbidden transitions are included, for example, deleptonization in ¹³²Sn changes by an order of magnitude. The rates are openly available and are intended to improve the accuracy of future multi-dimensional supernova simulations that cannot yet use full microscopic rate tables for all nuclear species.
