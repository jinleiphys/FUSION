# General analysis and numerical estimations of polarization observables in $\bar N+N\to π+ e^++e^-$ reaction in an exclusive experimental setup

> The dependence of the differential cross section and polarization observables in the $\bar N+N\to π+e^++e^-$ reaction on the polarizations of the proton target and antiproton beam (the produced electron may be unpolarized or longitudinally polarized) have been derived in a general form using hadron electromagnetic current conservation and $P-$ invariance of the hadron electromagnetic interaction. The analysis was done for the case of an exclusive experimental setup where the produced electron and pion are detected in coincidence. The explicit dependence of all polarization observables on two, from five, kinematic variables (the azimuthal angle $φ$ and the virtual-photon parameter $ε$), have been obtained assuming one-photon-exchange. The application to the particular case of a mechanism which contains information on time-like proton form factors in the unphysical region is considered in the Born approximation.

## Key claim
The paper derives a general, model-independent formalism for polarization observables in the reaction \(\bar N+N\to \pi+e^++e^-\) under one-photon-exchange, assuming only hadron electromagnetic current conservation and P-invariance, but the numerical estimates are restricted to the Born approximation using a specific Compton-like diagram model from Ad07 that accesses time-like proton form factors in the unphysical region (below the \(4M^2\) threshold).

## Method
The authors used the hadronic and leptonic tensor formalism in the antiproton-proton center-of-mass system, expressing the five-fold differential cross section in terms of six complex scalar amplitudes \(f_i\) that describe the \(\bar p+p\to\pi+\gamma^*\) subprocess. They explicitly separated the dependence on the azimuthal angle \(\phi\) and the virtual-photon parameter \(\varepsilon\) from the reaction dynamics, then computed the unpolarized cross section, single-spin asymmetries, and double-spin correlation coefficients for various combinations of polarized beam, target, and longitudinally polarized electron. Numerical estimates were performed in the Born approximation using the model of Ad07, which includes \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel exchange diagrams with nucleon form factors.

## Key numbers
- The differential cross section for unpolarized particles is expressed as \(\Sigma = \sigma_T + \varepsilon \cos 2\phi \, \sigma_P + \varepsilon \sigma_L + \sqrt{2\varepsilon(1-\varepsilon)}\cos\phi \, \sigma_I\), where \(\sigma_T = |f_1|^2+|f_2|^2+|f_3|^2+|f_4|^2\), \(\sigma_L = 2(q^2/k_0^2)(|f_5|^2+|f_6|^2)\), \(\sigma_P = |f_3|^2+|f_4|^2-|f_1|^2-|f_2|^2\), and \(\sigma_I = -2(\sqrt{q^2}/k_0)\operatorname{Re}(f_1f_5^*+f_2f_6^*)\eta\).
- The left-right asymmetry for unpolarized particles is \(A_{LT} = \sqrt{2\epsilon(1-\epsilon)}\sigma_I / [\sigma_T + \epsilon(\sigma_L+\sigma_P)]\).
- The single-spin asymmetry for a longitudinally polarized electron is \(A'_{LT} = \sqrt{2\varepsilon(1+\varepsilon)}\sigma'_I \Sigma^{-1}\) with \(\sigma'_I = 2(\sqrt{q^2}/k_0)\operatorname{Im}(f_1f_5^*+f_2f_6^*)\).
- The hadronic tensor for unpolarized initial particles is described by 5 real structure functions \(\alpha_i\) (\(i=1\)–\(5\)); for one polarized hadron by 13 structure functions \(\beta_i\) or \(\bar\beta_i\); for both polarized by 41 structure functions \(\gamma_i\) (\(i=1\)–\(41\)).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Gakh 2010) from inclusive to exclusive measurements where the pion and electron are detected in coincidence, and generalizes the formalism of pion electroproduction (AR77) to the annihilation channel \(\bar N+N\to\pi+e^++e^-\). The reaction is related by crossing symmetry to \(\pi+N\to N+e^-+e^+\) (first suggested by Rekalo, MPR) and to pion electroproduction on nucleons. The numerical application uses the model of Ad07, which allows access to time-like proton form factors in the unphysical region (\(q^2 < 4M^2\)), relevant for the PANDA experiment at FAIR (Wiedner 2011). The formalism enables future experimental extraction of the six scalar amplitudes and the underlying form factors from measured angular distributions and polarization asymmetries.
