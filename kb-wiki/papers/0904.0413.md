# Spontaneous P-violation in QCD in extreme conditions

> We investigate the possibility of parity being spontaneously violated in QCD at finite baryon density and temperature. The analysis is done for an idealized homogeneous and infinite nuclear matter where the influence of density can be examined with the help of constant chemical potential. QCD is approximated by a generalized sigma-model with two isomultiplets of scalars and pseudoscalars. The interaction with the chemical potential is introduced via the coupling to constituent quark fields as nucleons are not considered as point-like degrees of freedom in our approach. This mechanism of parity violation is based on interplay between lightest and heavier degrees of freedom and it cannot be understood in simple models retaining the pion and nucleon sectors solely. We argue that, in the appropriate environment (dense and hot nuclear matter of a few normal densities and moderate temperatures), parity violation may be the rule rather than the exception and its occurrence is well compatible with the existence of stable bound state of normal nuclear matter. We prove that the so called 'chiral collapse' never takes place for the parameter region supporting spontaneous parity violation.

## Key claim

Spontaneous parity violation (SPB) in QCD at finite baryon density and temperature is possible in a generalized sigma model containing two isomultiplets of scalars and pseudoscalars, but only when the heavier degrees of freedom beyond pions and nucleons are included; the authors argue that in dense and hot nuclear matter of a few normal densities and moderate temperatures, parity violation "may be the rule rather than the exception," and they prove that the so-called "chiral collapse" never occurs in the parameter region supporting SPB.

## Method

The authors approximate QCD using a generalized linear sigma model with two chiral multiplets (scalar/pseudoscalar fields H1 and H2), representing the two lowest-lying radial states for given JPC, and couple them to constituent quark fields via a Yukawa interaction. The baryon chemical potential μ and temperature T are introduced through the quark sector using the Matsubara formalism, and the effective potential is computed at one-loop order in the large-Nc limit, with the ω meson field added to provide repulsive vector interactions necessary for nuclear matter saturation. The gap equations for the scalar and pseudoscalar condensates are solved to determine the phase boundaries for spontaneous parity violation in the μ-T plane.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ is approximately 1.2 GeV.
- The constituent quark mass (v.e.v. of H1) is taken to be in the range 0.2 to 0.3 GeV.
- The saturation density of infinite nuclear matter is ρ0 = 0.15 to 0.16 fm⁻³, corresponding to a Fermi momentum pF = 260 to 270 MeV (1.3 to 1.4 fm⁻¹).
- The critical chemical potential μ* for nuclear matter saturation satisfies μ* < σ₁⁰, where σ₁⁰ is the zero-density scalar condensate.
- The range of intermediate nuclear densities where parity breaking is expected is from 3 to 10 times the normal nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Andrianov and Espriu, 2009) to include non-zero temperature, showing that the parity-violating phase persists in a finite domain of the μ-T plane. It builds on the conjecture by Migdal (1970s) and later work by Kharzeev and others on parity violation in dense matter, while contradicting the argument by Witten that parity cannot be spontaneously broken in vector-like theories (the positivity of the measure fails at non-zero chemical potential). The model avoids the "chiral collapse" problem present in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type models (Buballa, 2005) and provides a framework for studying parity violation in environments relevant to compact stars and heavy-ion collisions.
