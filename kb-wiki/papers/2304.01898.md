# Hybrid stars with reactive interfaces: analysis within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> It has been shown recently that quark-hadron conversions at the interface of a hybrid star may have a key role on the dynamic stability of the compact object. In this work we perform a systematic study of hybrid stars with reactive interfaces using a model-agnostic piecewise-polytropic hadronic equation of state and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for three-flavor quark matter. For the hadronic phase we use a soft, an intermediate and a stiff parametrization that match at $1.1 n_0$ {with predictions} based on chiral effective field theory (cEFT) interactions. In the NJL Lagrangian we include scalar, vector and 't Hooft interactions. The vector coupling constant $g_{v}$ is treated as a free parameter. We also consider that there is a split between the deconfinement and the chiral phase transitions which is controlled by changing the conventional value of the vacuum pressure $-Ω_{0}$ in the NJL thermodynamic potential by $-\left(Ω_{0}+δΩ_{0}\right)$, being $δΩ_{0}$ a free parameter. We analyze the mass-radius ($M$-$R$) relation in the case of rapid ($τ\ll 1 \, \mathrm{ms}$) and slow ($τ\gg 1 \, \mathrm{ms}$) conversions, being $τ$ the reaction timescale. In the case of slow interface reactions we find $M$-$R$ curves with a cusp at the maximum mass point where a pure hadronic branch and a slow-stable hybrid star (SSHS) branch coincide. We find that the length of the slow-stable branch grows with the increase of the transition density and the energy density jump at the hadron-quark interface. We calculate the tidal deformabilities of SSHSs and analyse them in the light of the GW170817 event.

## Key claim

The paper systematically studies hybrid stars with reactive quark-hadron interfaces using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, finding that slow interface conversions (reaction timescale much larger than 1 ms) allow dynamically stable hybrid star configurations even where the mass versus central density derivative is negative, which would be unstable under rapid conversions.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining a model-agnostic generalized piecewise-polytropic hadronic equation of state (with soft, intermediate, and stiff parametrizations matching chiral effective field theory predictions at 1.1 times nuclear saturation density) with a three-flavor NJL model for quark matter that includes scalar, vector, and 't Hooft interactions. They treat the vector coupling constant g_v and a vacuum pressure shift parameter δΩ_0 as free parameters, and use the Maxwell construction for a sharp hadron-quark interface. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and assess dynamical stability under both rapid (τ ≪ 1 ms) and slow (τ ≫ 1 ms) conversion scenarios using junction conditions on the radial fluid displacement and Lagrangian pressure perturbation at the interface.

## Key numbers

- Transition pressure P_t ranges from approximately 30 MeV fm^{-3} to above 350 MeV fm^{-3} across the explored parameter space.
- Energy density jump Δε at the hadron-quark interface varies from approximately 50 MeV fm^{-3} to approximately 1700 MeV fm^{-3}.
- Terminal mass M_T of the last stable slow-stable hybrid star configuration ranges from 1.46 M_⊙ to 2.04 M_⊙ depending on the hadronic model and NJL parameters.
- Maximum mass M_max reaches up to 2.91 M_⊙ for the stiff hadronic model with g_v/g_s = 0.2 and δΩ_0 = 182.7 MeV fm^{-3}.
- Terminal radius R_T ranges from 8.6 km to 12.6 km across the models.
- The standard NJL vacuum pressure Ω_0 is 5076 MeV fm^{-3}, with δΩ_0 varied from -18.3 MeV fm^{-3} up to 365.5 MeV fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of slow-stable hybrid stars (SSHSs) by Pereira (2017) and Lugones (2021) from simple phenomenological models like the MIT bag model and constant-speed-of-sound equations of state to the more microphysically detailed NJL model. It revisits the analysis of Lenzi (2012) on hybrid stars within the NJL model but now incorporating reactive interfaces and focusing on SSHSs. The results enable future comparisons with gravitational-wave observations from GW170817 and NICER mass-radius measurements to constrain the quark-hadron transition properties and conversion timescales in hybrid stars.
