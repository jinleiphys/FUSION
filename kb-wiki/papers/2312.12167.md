# Measurements of azimuthal anisotropies in $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O and $γ$+Au collisions from STAR

> In this proceeding, we present the first measurements of azimuthal anisotropies, $v_2$ and $v_3$, in $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at 200 GeV as a function of transverse momentum and multiplicity, by using two- and four-particle correlation methods. We compare our measurements with STAR measurements of $v_n$ in \dau and \heau collisions to provide insight into the impact of system symmetry on initial condition for small systems. We also investigate the ratio $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ as a function of centrality, which is expected to be sensitive to nucleon-nucleon correlation in the $^{16}$O nucleus.

## Key claim

The first measurements of azimuthal anisotropies $v_2$ and $v_3$ in $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at 200 GeV are presented, but the comparison of $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ with theoretical models requires detailed hydrodynamics and transport modeling to determine a response parameter $K$ before definitive conclusions about nucleon-nucleon correlations can be drawn.

## Method

Charged hadrons were detected in the STAR Time Projection Chamber covering $|\eta|\leq 1.5$, and two-particle azimuthal angular correlation yields $Y(\Delta\phi)$ were measured to extract Fourier coefficients $c_n$ for $n=1$ to 4. Nonflow contributions were subtracted using four established methods with the scale factor $f$ estimated from peripheral 60-80% $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions, and systematic uncertainties account for variations among the four methods. The $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ ratio was computed from four-particle correlations and compared with eccentricity ratios $\varepsilon_2\{4\}/\varepsilon_2\{2\}$ from the PHOBOS Glauber model using $^{16}$O configurations from nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT), variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations, and three-parameter Fermi (3pF) distributions.

## Key numbers

- $v_2(p_T)$ in 0-10% $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV is smaller than that in 0-10% $d$+Au and $^{3}$He+Au collisions at the same energy
- $v_3(p_T)$ values are similar among $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O, $d$+Au, and $^{3}$He+Au collisions in the 0-10% centrality class
- The ratio $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ as a function of centrality (defined by charged hadron multiplicity at $|\eta|<1.5$) is closer to the $\varepsilon_2\{4\}/\varepsilon_2\{2\}$ ratio from the VMC model and considerably smaller than that from the NLEFT model or 3pF distributions
- Trigger and associated particles were selected in $0.2 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/c with $1.0 < |\Delta\eta| < 3.0$ for the two-particle correlation analysis

## Context

This work extends the program of small-system collectivity measurements at RHIC, comparing symmetric $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions with previously measured asymmetric systems including $d$+Au, $^{3}$He+Au, and $p$+Au collisions from STAR (2022). The $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ ratio is proposed as a tool to discriminate between different \textit{ab initio} nuclear structure models: the nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) approach of Elhatisari (2017) and the variational Monte Carlo calculations using chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (VMC) of Gezerlis (2013). Future measurements of $\gamma$+Au collisions from Au+Au data taken in 2021 and 2023 will provide further constraints on sub-nucleon fluctuations and nucleon-nucleon correlations.
