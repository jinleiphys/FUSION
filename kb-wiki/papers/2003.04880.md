# Nonparametric constraints on neutron star matter with existing and upcoming gravitational wave and pulsar observations

> Observations of neutron stars, whether in binaries or in isolation, provide information about the internal structure of the most extreme material objects in the Universe. In this work, we combine information from recent observations to place joint constraints on the properties of neutron star matter. We use (i) lower limits on the maximum mass of neutron stars obtained through radio observations of heavy pulsars, (ii) constraints on tidal properties inferred through the gravitational waves neutron star binaries emit as they coalesce, and (iii) information about neutron stars' masses and radii obtained through X-ray emission from surface hot spots. In order to combine information from such distinct messengers while avoiding the kind of modeling systematics intrinsic to parametric inference schemes, we employ a nonparametric representation of the neutron-star equation of state based on Gaussian processes conditioned on nuclear theory models. We find that existing astronomical observations imply $R_{1.4}=12.32^{+1.09}_{-1.47}\,\mathrm{km}$ for the radius of a $1.4\,M_{\odot}$ neutron star and $p(2ρ_\mathrm{nuc})=3.8^{+2.7}_{-2.9}\times10^{34}\,\mathrm{dyn}/\mathrm{cm}^2$ for the pressure at twice nuclear saturation density at the 90% credible level. The upper bounds are driven by the gravitational wave observations, while X-ray and heavy pulsar observations drive the lower bounds. Additionally, we compute expected constraints from potential future astronomical observations and find that they can jointly determine $R_{1.4}$ to ${\cal{O}}(1)\,\mathrm{km}$ and $p(2ρ_\mathrm{nuc})$ to 80% relative uncertainty in the next five years.

## Key claim
Combining gravitational wave, X-ray, and pulsar observations using a nonparametric equation-of-state representation yields joint constraints on neutron star matter that avoid parametric modeling systematics, with the upper bounds driven by gravitational wave data and the lower bounds by X-ray and heavy pulsar observations.

## Method
The authors employed a Gaussian process representation of the neutron star equation of state conditioned on nuclear theory models, which avoids the systematic uncertainties inherent in parametric inference schemes. They combined three observational inputs: lower limits on the maximum neutron star mass from radio observations of heavy pulsars, tidal deformability constraints from gravitational wave detections of binary neutron star mergers, and mass-radius information from X-ray observations of surface hot spots. The analysis was performed using Bayesian inference to produce joint posterior distributions for equation-of-state properties.

## Key numbers
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_{1.4} = 12.32^{+1.09}_{-1.47} km at 90% credible level
- Pressure at twice nuclear saturation density: p(2ρ_nuc) = 3.8^{+2.7}_{-2.9} × 10^34 dyn/cm^2 at 90% credible level
- Maximum neutron star mass from combined data: M_max = 2.22^{+0.30}_{-0.20} solar masses at 90% credible level
- Tidal deformability from combined data: Λ = 451^{+241}_{-279} at 90% credible level
- Future observations can determine R_{1.4} to approximately 1 km and p(2ρ_nuc) to 80% relative uncertainty within five years

## Context
This work extends the nonparametric equation-of-state inference framework previously developed by the authors (Landry et al., 2020) to combine multiple observational messengers simultaneously. It builds upon constraints from the LIGO/Virgo gravitational wave detection GW170817, the NICER X-ray observations of PSR J0030+0451, and radio pulsar mass measurements such as PSR J0740+6620. The results enable future joint analyses incorporating additional gravitational wave detections from upcoming observing runs and further NICER observations of other pulsars.
