# Absence of CP Violation in the Strong Interaction: Vacuum thwarts Axion

> QCD admits a contribution to the action, the $θ$ term, which potentially gives rise to nontrivial phases and violates CP. This is essentially a question of how the vacuum reacts to the $θ$ term. In this talk I will address the problem using new developments on the lattice. The overall solution is contrasted with the axion `solution'.

## Key claim

The strong CP problem is resolved within QCD itself because the electric dipole moment of the neutron vanishes proportionally to the inverse square root of the volume, so that in the infinite-volume limit CP violation is absent even for a vacuum angle θ of order unity; the axion extension is not a viable solution because it forces the theory into the topologically trivial sector Q=0, which eliminates confinement and chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author uses lattice QCD with overlap fermions to define the topological charge Q via the Atiyah-Singer index theorem, counting the difference between left- and right-handed zero modes of the Dirac operator. The electric dipole moment of the neutron is computed from a first-order perturbation in θ, where the topological charge is expressed as an integral over zero-mode eigenvectors, and the probability of finding a zero mode within the nucleon's interaction volume is evaluated from the Gaussian distribution of topological charge. The gluon condensate in the θ vacuum is computed on a 24⁴ lattice at a=0.082 fm for pure SU(3) gauge theory using the gradient flow scheme, and the axion-modified partition function is analyzed by separating the zero-momentum mode of the axion field.

## Key numbers

- Current experimental upper bound on the neutron electric dipole moment: |dₙ| < 1.8 × 10⁻¹³ e·fm (Abel 2020)
- Topological susceptibility at the physical point for N_f=2+1 quark flavors: χ_t = (79 MeV)⁴
- On a (10 fm)⁴ volume, the expected number of zero modes in a (2.5 fm)⁴ reference volume is R = 0.06; on a (20 fm)⁴ volume, R = 0.015 (1.5% probability)
- The dipole moment vanishes as |dₙ| ∝ √(χ_t/V) |θ|
- Screening radius in the θ vacuum: λ_S = 0.31/|θ| [fm] (Schierholz 2022)
- Gluon condensate vanishes outside a small region around θ=0 on the 24⁴ lattice at a=0.082 fm for flow time t/a²=60
- Axion mass suggested for dark matter: m_a = 50(4) μeV (Bonati 2015, Borsanyi 2016)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous lattice studies of the running coupling and confinement (Nakamura 2021, Schierholz 2024) and provides a nonperturbative resolution of the strong CP problem within QCD, contradicting the conventional view that θ must be anomalously small. The result directly challenges the axion solution of Peccei and Quinn (1977), showing that the restriction to Q=0 eliminates the topological susceptibility, invalidating the Witten-Veneziano relation (Witten 1979, Veneziano 1979) and the axion mass formula used for dark matter estimates. The vanishing of the gluon condensate in the topologically trivial sector indicates that the axion extension does not confine, consistent with earlier suggestions by Callan, Dashen and Gross (1977) that chiral symmetry breaking requires the anomaly.
