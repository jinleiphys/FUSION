# An Analytic Solution of Hydrodynamic Equations with Source Terms in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The energy and baryon densities in heavy ion collisions are estimated by analytically solving a 1+1 dimensional hydrodynamical model with source terms. Particularly, a competition between the energy and baryon sources and the expansion of the system is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper provides an analytic solution to 1+1 dimensional hydrodynamic equations with source terms for heavy ion collisions, showing that when the collision time is finite (as at AGS, SIS, and CSR energies), the maximum energy and baryon densities are strongly suppressed by expansion in the source region, contrary to the instantaneous-collision approximations of Landau and Bjorken.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic fluid conservation laws with source terms for energy-momentum and baryon number, using a 3-dimensional homogeneous expansion model in proper time and space-time rapidity coordinates. They consider central Au-Au collisions with full nuclear stopping at incident energies 0.4-14.5 A GeV, dividing the time evolution into three regimes: linear increase before effective expansion begins, competition between source input and expansion, and monotonic decrease after the source ends. The source terms are taken as constant during the collision time, and the equation of state uses sound velocity \(v_s^2 = 1/5\).

## Key numbers

- For a central \(^{197}\)Au-\(^{197}\)Au collision at incident energies \(E_{lab} = 0.4-14.5\) A GeV, the collision time \(d_t\) ranges from 4 fm to 23 fm.
- At \(E_{lab} = 1.8\) A GeV with \(\tau_{exp} = \tau_0\), the maximum scaled energy density is \(\epsilon(\tau_0+d_t)/\epsilon_N = 0.76\), compared to the initial value \(\epsilon(\tau_0)/\epsilon_N = 0.67\).
- In the case of no expansion in the source region, the maximum scaled energy density reaches 17.5 and the maximum scaled baryon density reaches 6 at \(E_{lab} = 14.5\) A GeV.
- Normal nuclear density is defined as \(n_N = 1/(\frac{4}{3}\pi r_N^3)\) with nucleon radius \(r_N = 1.1\) fm and nucleon mass \(m_N = 0.94\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken (1970) scaling solution and Landau (1953) hydrodynamics by incorporating a finite collision time, which is neglected in those instantaneous-collision models. The authors focus on low-energy collisions (AGS, SIS, CSR) where the collision time is comparable to the total evolution time, in contrast to high-energy collisions at RHIC and LHC where the collision time is negligible. The results enable more realistic estimates of energy and baryon densities for studying in-medium effects such as chiral symmetry restoration, and can be compared with experimental data on \(K^-/K^+\) enhancement at SIS (GSI collaboration) and \(J/\psi\) suppression at SPS (Gavin et al., 1994).
