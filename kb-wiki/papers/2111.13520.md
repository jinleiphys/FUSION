# Bottomonium observables in an open quantum system using the quantum trajectories method

> We solve the Lindblad equation describing the Brownian motion of a Coulombic heavy quark-antiquark pair in a strongly coupled quark gluon plasma using the Monte Carlo wave function method. The Lindblad equation has been derived in the framework of pNRQCD and fully accounts for the quantum and non-Abelian nature of the system. The hydrodynamics of the plasma is realistically implemented through a 3+1D dissipative hydrodynamics code. We compute the bottomonium nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow and compare with the most recent LHC data. The computation does not rely on any free parameter, as it depends on two transport coefficients that have been evaluated independently in lattice QCD. Our final results, which include late-time feed down of excited states, agree well with the available data from LHC 5.02 TeV PbPb collisions.

## Key claim

The computation of bottomonium observables (nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow) using the quantum trajectories method shows good agreement with LHC 5.02 TeV PbPb collision data without any free parameters, but only under the condition that the hierarchy of scales \(M \gg 1/a_0 \gg \pi T \sim m_D \gg E\) holds, which restricts the medium temperature to regimes where the Lindblad equation form is valid.

## Method

The authors solve the Lindblad equation derived from pNRQCD and open quantum system formalism for a Coulombic heavy quark-antiquark pair in a strongly coupled quark gluon plasma using the Monte Carlo wave function (quantum trajectories) method implemented in the QTraj code. The medium is modeled through a 3+1D dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics code using the quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) framework tuned to soft hadronic data from 5.02 TeV collisions. For each physical trajectory, the production point is sampled from the nuclear binary collision overlap profile, initial \(p_T\) from an \(E_T^{-4}\) spectrum, and azimuthal angle uniformly; approximately 50-100 quantum trajectories are generated per physical trajectory, with late-time feed down of excited states included via a feed down matrix using branching ratios from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass \(M = m_b = m_{\Upsilon(1S)}/2 = 4.73\) GeV, with \(m_{\Upsilon(1S)}\) from the Particle Data Group (2020)
- Strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.468\) obtained from 1-loop, 3-flavor running with \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}^{N_f=3} = 332\) MeV
- Lattice parameters: radial volume L = 80 GeV\(^{-1}\), NUM = 4096 lattice sites (spacing \(a = 0.0195\) GeV\(^{-1}\)), time step dt = 0.001 GeV\(^{-1}\)
- Wave function initialized at \(t = 0\) fm, evolved in vacuum until \(t = 0.6\) fm, then in medium until local temperature falls below \(T_f = 250\) MeV
- Three parametrizations of \(\hat{\kappa}(T) = \kappa(T)/T^3\) used: \(\hat{\kappa}_U(T)\), \(\hat{\kappa}_C(T)\), \(\hat{\kappa}_L(T)\) (upper, central, lower from Fig. 13 of Brambilla (2020))
- Three values of \(\hat{\gamma} = \gamma/T^3\) used: \(-3.5\), \(-1.75\), and \(0\)
- Experimental cross sections for feed down: \(\vec{\sigma}_{\text{exp}} = \{57.6, 19, 3.72, 13.69, 16.1, 6.8, 3.27, 12.0, 14.15\}\) nb for the nine bottomonium states considered

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD and open quantum system framework developed by Brambilla (2016, 2017) for in-medium bottomonium, where a Lindblad equation was first derived, and builds upon the QTraj code presented by Omar (2021). The results improve upon earlier calculations by Brambilla (2020, 2021) by incorporating realistic 3+1D hydrodynamics and feed down from excited states, enabling direct comparison with LHC data from ALICE (2020), ATLAS (5 TeV), and CMS (2018). The approach enables future parameter-free predictions for bottomonium observables at different collision energies and for other heavy quarkonium systems, provided the hierarchy of scales remains valid.
