# Hyperon Single-Particle Potentials Calculated from SU6 Quark-Model Baryon-Baryon Interactions

> Using the SU6 quark-model baryon-baryon interaction recently developed by the Kyoto-Niigata group, we calculate NN, Lambda N and Sigma N G-matrices in ordinary nuclear matter. This is the first attempt to discuss the Lambda and Sigma single-particle potentials in nuclear medium, based on the realistic quark-model potential. The Lambda potential has the depth of more than 40 MeV, which is more attractive than the value expected from the experimental data of Lambda-hypernuclei. The Sigma potential turns out to be repulsive, the origin of which is traced back to the strong Pauli repulsion in the Sigma N (I=3/2) ^3S_1 state.

## Key claim

The Lambda single-particle potential in nuclear matter calculated from the SU6 quark-model baryon-baryon interaction (FSS version) has a depth of more than 40 MeV, which is more attractive than the value of about 30 MeV expected from experimental data of Lambda-hypernuclei; the Sigma potential turns out to be repulsive, in contrast to several other realistic hyperon-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix in symmetric nuclear matter using the SU6 quark-model baryon-baryon interaction (FSS version) developed by the Kyoto-Niigata group, which is defined through the resonating-group method for (3q)-(3q) clusters. They calculated NN, Lambda N, and Sigma N G-matrices with the continuous choice for intermediate spectra and a self-consistent treatment of the coupled Lambda N - Sigma N (I=1/2) channel. The single-particle potentials were obtained from the diagonal G-matrix elements integrated over the Fermi sea of nucleons.

## Key numbers

* Lambda single-particle potential at zero momentum: U_Lambda(0) = -45.9 MeV at normal nuclear density (k_F = 1.35 fm^-1) for the FSS model.
* Sigma single-particle potential at zero momentum: U_Sigma(0) = +20.7 MeV at normal nuclear density (k_F = 1.35 fm^-1) for the FSS model.
* For comparison, the Nijmegen soft-core potential (NSC89) gives U_Lambda(0) = -29.8 MeV and U_Sigma(0) = -15.3 MeV at the same density.
* The repulsive Sigma potential arises primarily from the I=3/2 ^3S_1+^3D_1 channel, which contributes +48.3 MeV in the FSS model.
* Lambda effective mass ratio m*/m at k ~ 1 fm^-1 is about 0.90 at k_F = 1.35 fm^-1, compared to the nucleon effective mass ratio of 0.66 at the same density.

## Context

This work extends the SU6 quark-model baryon-baryon interactions developed by the Kyoto-Niigata group (Fujiwara et al., 1996; Nakamoto et al., 1995) to the nuclear medium via G-matrix calculations, providing the first quark-model-based predictions for hyperon single-particle potentials. The results contradict the attractive Sigma potentials predicted by the Nijmegen model D and soft-core potentials (Yamamoto and Bando, 1985; Schulze et al., 1995) but agree qualitatively with the repulsive Sigma potential from the Nijmegen model F. The findings enable further studies of hypernuclear structure and the hyperon spin-orbit potential in a separate paper (Kohno et al., 1999).
