# In-Medium $K^+$ Electromagnetic Form Factor with a Symmetric Vertex in a Light Front Approach

> Using the light-front kaon wave function based on a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude model for the quark-antiquark bound state, we study the Electromagnetic Form Factor (EMFF) of the kaon in nuclear medium within the framework of light-front field theory. The kaon model we adopt is well constrained by previous and recent studies to explain its properties in vacuum. The in-medium kaon EMFF is evaluated for the + component of the electromagnetic current, $J^+$, in the Breit frame. In order to consistently incorporate the constituent up and antistrange quarks of the kaon immersed in symmetric nuclear matter, we use the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model, which has been widely applied to various hadronic and nuclear phenomena in a nuclear medium with success. We predict the in-medium modification of the kaon EMFF in symmetric nuclear matter. It is found that, after a fine tuning of the regulator mass, i.e. $m_R = 0.600$ GeV, the model is suitable to fit the available experimental data in vaccum within the theoretical uncertainties, and based on this we predict the in-medium modification of the EMFF.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the in-medium electromagnetic form factor of the K+ meson in symmetric nuclear matter decreases faster with momentum transfer than in vacuum, implying an increase in the K+ charge radius, but this result depends on a fine-tuned regulator mass (m_R = 0.600 GeV) and the specific quark-meson coupling model parameters.

## Method

The authors model the K+ meson as a bound state of up and antistrange quarks using a light-front wave function derived from a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with a symmetric vertex function and pseudoscalar coupling. They compute the plus-component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame, incorporating in-medium effects via the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model, which modifies the up quark mass through a Lorentz-scalar potential and shifts the quark momentum through a vector potential, while the antistrange quark mass remains unchanged. The valence contribution to the form factor is evaluated using the light-front wave function, with the non-valence component set to zero in the adopted framework.

## Key numbers

- Regulator mass m_R = 0.600 GeV used for all calculations (vacuum and in-medium).
- Vacuum up quark mass m_u = 0.220 GeV; antistrange quark mass m_sbar = 0.440 GeV (fixed for all densities).
- In-medium up quark masses: m_u = 0.180 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.25, m_u = 0.143 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.50, m_u = 0.110 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.75.
- In-medium K+ meson masses: m_K = 0.472 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.25, m_K = 0.453 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.50, m_K = 0.435 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.75.
- Vector potential values: V = 0.029 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.25, V = 0.058 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.50, V = 0.088 GeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.75.
- Numerical results compared with experimental data in vacuum up to Q^2 ≈ 0.10 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the light-front approach for meson electromagnetic form factors, previously applied to pions by Frederico (1992) and de Melo (1999), to the K+ meson in nuclear matter using the QMC model developed by the authors (Nucleon, Pion). The symmetric vertex model follows earlier work by Pacheco (2002, 2012) and aims to unify parameter sets for pseudoscalar mesons. The results enable future studies of in-medium modifications of kaon properties and their implications for nuclear physics and QCD in dense matter.
