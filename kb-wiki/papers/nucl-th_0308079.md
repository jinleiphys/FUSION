# Nucleus-nucleus reaction cross-sections calculated for realistic nuclear matter distributions within the Glauber-Sitenko approach

> Basing on the thickness (profile) function, previously obtained for the realistic Fermi type distribution of nucleons in nuclei, calculations are made of the microscopic eikonal phases of the nucleus-nucleus scattering and the total reaction cross- sections. In so doing, the phase is deduced to the one-dimensional integral provided that the Gaussian density distribution for the projectile nucleus and an arbitrary shape of the thickness density for the target nucleus are used. The problems of obtaining parameters of the "point" nucleon density are considered. A possibility of approximating the realistic densities by the "surface-matched" Gaussian functions and the dependence of cross-sections on the nucleon-nucleon interaction radius are discussed. The in-medium effects and the role of the trajectory distortion are studied. Conclusions are made on physics of the process, and comparison with experimental data is made with cross sections calculated by using the developed method where no free parameters are introduced.

## Key claim

The authors develop a method for calculating nucleus-nucleus total reaction cross-sections within the Glauber-Sitenko approach that uses realistic Fermi-type nuclear density distributions rather than Gaussian approximations, and they show that this method can produce results without introducing free parameters, provided the projectile density is taken as Gaussian and the target density as a symmetrized Fermi function.

## Method

The eikonal phase for nucleus-nucleus scattering is expressed as a one-dimensional convolution integral involving the thickness (profile) functions of the projectile and target nuclei, together with a Gaussian form factor for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. For the projectile, a Gaussian density distribution is used; for the target, the symmetrized Fermi (SF) density distribution is employed, whose profile function has an explicit analytic form. The total reaction cross-section is then obtained by integrating the phase over impact parameter, and the results are compared with experimental data and with calculations using modified Gaussian or uniform density approximations.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the shape parameter beta lies in the interval 0.21 to 0.23 fm^-2 at energies around 1 GeV, corresponding to r_N_rms^2 = 0.63 to 0.69 fm^2.
- The symmetrized Fermi density parameters for 208Pb are R = 6.557 fm and a = 0.515 fm, giving R_rms = 5.427 fm.
- For 16O, the SF density parameters are R = 2.562 fm and a = 0.497 fm, giving R_rms = 2.711 fm.
- For 40Ca, the SF density parameters are R = 3.556 fm and a = 0.578 fm, giving R_rms = 3.493 fm.
- The point-like nucleon density parameters for 12C are c = 2.275 fm and d = 0.393 fm.
- The proton rms-radius used in extracting point-like densities is r_0_rms^2 = 0.658 fm^2, while the matter rms-radius used in double-folding potential calculations is r_0_rms^2 = 0.65 fm^2.
- Calculations using the approximate profile with correction factor P_a(R) show a difference of about 2% compared to exact calculations.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic Glauber-Sitenko approach, previously applied to proton-nucleus scattering by Franco and collaborators and generalized to nucleus-nucleus scattering by Czyz and Formanek, by providing analytic expressions for eikonal phases using realistic symmetrized Fermi densities rather than Gaussian approximations. The method enables predictions of total reaction cross-sections for combinations of stable and radioactive nuclei without free parameters, which is relevant for applications such as transmutation of radioactive waste. The authors compare their results with earlier approaches using modified Gaussian functions (Ka) and uniform densities, and they discuss how to extract point-like nucleon densities from electron-nucleus scattering data using the methods of Brown and collaborators or Friedrich and collaborators.
