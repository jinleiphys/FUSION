# Sensitivity of stellar electron-capture rates to parent neutron number: A case study on a continuous chain of twenty Vanadium isotopes

> Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions (B(GT)) in electron-capture (EC) daughters stemming from the parent ground state are computed with the shell-model in the full pf-shell space, with quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) in the formalism of Krumlinde and Möller and with an Approximate Method (AM) for assigning an effective B(GT). These are compared to data available from decay and charge-exchange (CE) experiments across titanium isotopes in the pf-shell from A=43 to A=62, the largest set available for any chain of isotopes in the pf-shell. The present study is the first to examine B(GT) and the associated EC rates across a particular chain of isotopes with the purpose of examining rate sensitivities as neutron number increases. EC rates are also computed for a wide variety of stellar electron densities and temperatures providing concise estimates of the relative size of rate sensitivities for particular astrophysical scenarios. This work underscores the astrophysical motivation for CE experiments in inverse kinematics for nuclei away from stability at the luminosities of future Radioactive Ion Beam Facilities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that electron-capture rate sensitivity increases sharply with neutron number in the Vanadium isotope chain, particularly at the point where the Q-value falls below the reaction threshold of -mₑc², and that this sensitivity is fundamentally different for rates derived from decay data versus charge-exchange data due to the different phase-space dependence on daughter excitation energy.

## Method

Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the electron-capture daughters of twenty Vanadium isotopes (A=43 to A=62) were computed using three approaches: shell-model calculations with the NuShellX code in the full pf-shell space using both GXPF1a and KB3G effective interactions, quasi-particle random-phase approximation in the formalism of Krumlinde and Möller, and an Approximate Method parametrization assigning a single effective GT state. The theoretical B(GT) distributions were compared to available beta-decay data for proton-rich cases and charge-exchange data from (d,²He) reactions on stable isotopes. Electron-capture rates were calculated over a thermodynamic grid spanning electron densities from 10⁷ to 10¹⁴ g/cm³ and temperatures from T₉=0.01 to T₉=100, with rate sensitivities estimated by computing log-ratios relative to the KB3G shell-model rates across three analysis categories defined by neutron number thresholds.

## Key numbers

- The Vanadium-to-Titanium chain spans 20 isotopes from A=43 to A=62, corresponding to electron fractions Yₑ from approximately 0.53 to 0.37
- The Q-value threshold -mₑc² is crossed at N=28 (⁵¹V), defining the boundary between the first two analysis categories
- The onset of g₉/₂ intruder states occurs at N≈34 (⁵⁷V), defining the boundary between the second and third analysis categories
- A quenching factor of (0.744)² was applied to shell-model GT strengths to account for strength lost to excitations outside the model space
- QRPA calculations extended to daughter excitation energies of approximately 25-30 MeV
- The constant K/gᵥ² = 6143 ± 2 s and (gₐ/gᵥ)² = -1.2694 ± 0.0028 were used in the ft-value relation
- EC Q-values range from +13.43(18) MeV for ⁴⁴V to -14.2(11) MeV for ⁶¹V, based on AME2012
- For the three astrophysical scenarios examined, data-based values meeting the threshold criterion numbered seven cases at 10⁷ g/cm³ and 3×10⁹ K, four cases at 10⁹ g/cm³ and 10×10⁹ K, and three cases at 10¹¹ g/cm³ and 0.4×10⁹ K

## Context

This work extends previous electron-capture rate sensitivity studies on individual nuclei (PhysRevC.86.015809, PhysRevLett.112.252501, PhysRevC.92.024312) by examining a continuous chain of isotopes to reveal structural effects on rate sensitivities. The study uses the largest available set of B(GT) data for any chain of isotopes in the pf-shell, including decay data from Hagberg (1997), Hornshoj (1982), and others, as well as charge-exchange data from the ⁵⁰V(d,²He) and ⁵¹V(d,²He) reactions. The results provide motivation for future charge-exchange experiments in inverse kinematics at radioactive ion beam facilities, particularly for nuclei away from stability where even 1-3 additional neutrons beyond stability will have major impacts on electron-capture rate sensitivity for core-collapse supernovae conditions.
