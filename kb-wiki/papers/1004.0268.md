# On Gauge-Invariant Decomposition of Nucleon Spin

> We investigate the relation between the known decompositions of the nucleon spin into its constituents, thereby clarifying in what respect they are common and in what respect they are different essentially. The decomposition recently proposed by Chen et al. can be thought of as a nontrivial generalization of the gauge-variant Jaffe-Manohar decomposition so as to meet the gauge-invariance requirement of each term of the decomposition. We however point out that there is another gauge-invariant decomposition of the nucleon spin, which is closer to the Ji decomposition, while allowing the decomposition of the gluon total angular momentum into the spin and orbital parts. After clarifying the reason why the gauge-invariant decomposition of the nucleon spin is not unique, we discuss which decomposition is more preferable from the experimental viewpoint.

## Key claim

The paper argues that gauge invariance alone does not uniquely determine the decomposition of nucleon spin into quark spin, quark orbital angular momentum, gluon spin, and gluon orbital angular momentum; there exist at least two distinct gauge-invariant decompositions, and the choice between them depends on physical interpretability and experimental observability rather than formal consistency.

## Method

The author systematically compares three decompositions of nucleon spin: the gauge-variant Jaffe-Manohar decomposition, the gauge-invariant Ji decomposition, and the recently proposed Chen et al. decomposition. By first analyzing the simpler QED case of an interacting electron-photon system, the author derives the mathematical relations between the decompositions and identifies the source of non-uniqueness as the ambiguous assignment of a "potential angular momentum" term (arising from the longitudinal electric field) to either the matter or gauge-field sector. The analysis is then extended to QCD using the non-Abelian generalization of the longitudinal-transverse decomposition of the gluon field.

## Key numbers

- Only about 1/3 of the nucleon spin comes from intrinsic quark spin, as established by experimental efforts (EMC88, COMPASS07, HERMES07).
- In the asymptotic limit Q^2 → ∞, the Chen et al. decomposition leads to the conclusion that gluons carry only about 1/5 of the total nucleon momentum, in contrast to the standardly believed value of 1/2 (Chen09).
- The paper shows that the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition can be recovered from the Chen et al. decomposition in a particular gauge (the generalized Coulomb gauge where [A, E] = 0).

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate on nucleon spin decomposition initiated by Jaffe and Manohar (1990) and Ji (1997), and directly addresses the recent proposal by Chen et al. (2008, 2009). The author clarifies that the Chen et al. decomposition is a gauge-invariant generalization of the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition, but presents an alternative gauge-invariant decomposition that preserves the standard dynamical momentum operator p - gA in the quark orbital angular momentum term, similar to the Ji decomposition. The paper enables future experimental and theoretical work to determine which decomposition is more physically meaningful based on observability criteria.
