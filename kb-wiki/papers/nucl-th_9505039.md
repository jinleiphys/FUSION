# Resonant nuclear transition in the d-mu-Li6 muonic molecule

> The reaction rate of the nuclear fusion d+Li6 ----> Be8*(2+,0) is estimated in the case where the nuclei are confined to each other by a muon. For the description of nuclear transitions, a method which is analogous to the Linear Combination of Atomic Orbitals has been used. Using the complex coordinate rotation method, we found that a molecular d-mu-Li6 state exists with energy (-20.3084-i0.0066) eV and |Ψ_{m}(0)|=0.44x10^{-7} fm^-3/2. The nuclear wave functions needed, were constructed in the form of antisymmetrized products of harmonic-oscillator functions for the three-cluster approximation, (ddα), to the five-body (NNNNα) problem. It was found that the reaction rate λis strongly dependent on the energy gap between the d-Li6 threshold and the energy of the final Be8^* resonant state. The value of λobtained by averaging over the width of this resonance, is 0.183x10^10 sec^{-1}.

## Key claim

The reaction rate for the resonant nuclear fusion d + Li6 → Be8*(2+,0) in the d-μ-Li6 muonic molecule is estimated to be 0.183 × 10^10 sec−1, but this value is strongly dependent on the energy gap between the d-Li6 threshold and the energy of the final Be8* resonant state, and the result is obtained by averaging over the width of that resonance.

## Method

The authors employed a method analogous to the Linear Combination of Atomic Orbitals (LCAO) to describe the nuclear transition, writing the total wave function as a linear combination of the molecular state (d-μ-Li6) and the nuclear state (Be8* plus muon). The molecular three-body Coulomb problem was solved using the complex coordinate rotation method with a variational expansion of up to 1400 Slater-type exponential functions, while the nuclear wave functions were constructed as antisymmetrized products of harmonic-oscillator functions in a three-cluster (ddα) approximation to the five-body (NNNNα) problem. The reaction rate was then calculated from the mixing of these two states via matrix elements of the strong nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-alpha potentials.

## Key numbers

- Molecular d-μ-Li6 state energy: (−20.3084 − i0.0066) eV, with the imaginary part corresponding to a predissociation rate of 1.8 × 10^13 sec−1
- Boundary value of the nucleus-nucleus wave function at zero separation: |Ψm(0)| = 0.44 × 10−7 fm−3/2
- Reaction rate λ for the fusion d + Li6 → Be8*(2+,0): 0.183 × 10^10 sec−1
- The d-Li6 threshold lies 22.2798 MeV above the Be8 ground state, and the Be8 excited state is at (22.2 − i0.8) MeV
- The fusion reaction releases 79.78 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of resonant nuclear fusion in muonic molecules, specifically the d-μ-Be7, p-μ-Be10, and t-μ-He3* systems (Rakityansky et al., prior papers), where high reaction rates were also found. The calculation relies on the near-degeneracy of the molecular state energy (−20.3084 eV) with the Be8* resonant state energy (22.2 MeV above ground), enabling mixing between the two states. The results enable further investigation of threshold nuclear reactions at super-low energies inside muonic molecules, which are otherwise inaccessible in direct scattering experiments below the keV region.
