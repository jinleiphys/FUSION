# Initial fluctuations and power spectrum of flow anisotropies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Flow has emerged as a crucial probe for the properties of the thermalized medium produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The evolution of initial state fluctuations leaves imprints on the power spectrum of flow coefficients. Therefore flow coefficients are a crucial probe of initial state fluctuations arising from the parton distributions of the colliding nuclei. This has a very strong correspondence with the physics of power spectrum of cosmic microwave background radiation (CMBR) anisotropies which directly probes initial inflationary fluctuations. Much work has been done to probe these interesting interconnections, in particular, in developing techniques for the measurements of higher flow coefficients. We present a short review of these developments. The effect of initial magnetic field on these features will also be reviewed. All this acquires special importance in view of upcoming electron-ion collider which will directly probe initial parton distribution of the colliding nucleus.

## Key claim

This review argues that initial state fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions should be studied as a primary source of information about initial nucleon/parton distributions, rather than treated as a nuisance for elliptic flow measurements. The authors propose that the power spectrum of flow coefficients, analogous to the CMBR anisotropy power spectrum, can reveal features such as acoustic oscillations and superhorizon mode suppression, though the absence of gravity in heavy-ion collisions only affects the overall scale of the power spectrum without altering its shape.

## Method

The paper presents a short review of developments in studying flow coefficients with a focus on their power spectrum, drawing a detailed correspondence with the CMBR anisotropy power spectrum. It discusses how initial energy density fluctuations from random nucleon and parton distributions evolve hydrodynamically, producing all Fourier coefficients \(v_n\) including odd harmonics, and proposes using the variance \(v_n^{rms}\) (analogous to \(C_l\) for CMBR) plotted against harmonic number \(n\) as the power spectrum. The review also covers magnetohydrodynamical evolution in the presence of strong magnetic fields and discusses qualitative patterns from any superfluid phase of QCD that could be produced in relatively low energy collisions.

## Key numbers

* Thermalization time \(\tau_0\) is estimated to be less than 1 fm for RHIC and LHC energies, with values as short as a tenth of fm for LHC energies.
* The sound velocity for a relativistic ideal plasma is \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\), which defines the sound horizon \(c_s \tau_0\) as the limiting causal distance for hydrodynamics.
* The nucleon size is about 1.6 fm, which sets a scale for superhorizon wavelength inhomogeneities at the equilibration stage.
* For CMBR, the horizon at last scattering corresponds to \(l \sim 200\), with lower \(l\) modes representing superhorizon scales unaltered by causal physics before recombination.

## Context

This work extends the correspondence between flow fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions and CMBR anisotropies originally proposed in a series of papers by some of the authors (Dave, Saumia, Srivastava, 2014; 2015). It builds on earlier recognition that initial state fluctuations affect elliptic flow measurements and techniques developed by Borghini (2001), Bhalerao (2006), and Ollitrault (2009) for multiparticle cumulant expansions. The review enables future studies using the upcoming electron-ion collider to directly probe initial parton distributions, and discusses how isolating initial state fluctuations from hydrodynamical evolution effects can be achieved through magnetohydrodynamical simulations and studies of superfluid QCD phases.
