# Manifestation of 12-Quark Bag State of He-4 Nucleus in Elastic dHe-4 Scattering

> The He-4 d elastic scattering at the momentum of 19.8 GeV/c is analyzed in the framework of the Glauber theory. The scattering amplitude was evaluated using different sets of values of the nucleon-nucleon amplitude parameters and the He-4 density function as a superposition of the Gaussian functions. It is shown that it is impossible to describe simultaneously the pHe-4 and dHe-4 elastic scattering cross sections using the same set of the NN-amplitude parameters. Inclusion of the twelve quark bag admixture to the ground state of the He-4 nucleus in the calculations allows one to reproduce the experimental data quite well. It is shown that the admixture manifests itself in the dHe-4 elastic scattering in all region of the momentum transfer. At small t the effect can be at the level of ~ 10 %. At large t it can be ~ 30 %.

## Key claim

Including a 10.5% admixture of a twelve-quark bag configuration in the ground state wave function of the He-4 nucleus allows the Glauber theory to simultaneously describe both pHe-4 and dHe-4 elastic scattering cross sections using the same set of nucleon-nucleon amplitude parameters, which is otherwise impossible. The effect of this admixture on the dHe-4 differential cross section is approximately 10% at small momentum transfer and approximately 30% at large momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors analyzed dHe-4 elastic scattering at a laboratory momentum of 19.8 GeV/c within the Glauber multiple-scattering formalism, using a graph-theoretic expansion to evaluate all terms of the scattering amplitude. They employed two parametrizations of the He-4 density function (a simple Gaussian and a more complex sum of Gaussians) and several sets of nucleon-nucleon amplitude parameters (sets A, B, C, D) taken from compilations and prior fits. The twelve-quark bag admixture was incorporated by writing the total scattering amplitude as a weighted sum of a deuteron-four-nucleon Glauber amplitude and a deuteron-12q-bag amplitude, with the latter treated as a deuteron-proton-like scattering using a Gaussian parametrization for the nucleon-12q-bag amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory momentum of the dHe-4 scattering: 19.8 GeV/c.
- Weight of the 12q bag admixture in the He-4 ground state: w_12q = 10.5%.
- Total cross section for nucleon-12q bag scattering: sigma_12q^t = 34 mb.
- Slope parameter for nucleon-12q bag scattering: B_12q = 23 (GeV/c)^(-2).
- Effect of the 12q bag admixture on the dHe-4 differential cross section: approximately 10% at small t, approximately 30% at large t.
- For the simple Gaussian parametrization I of the He-4 density, the difference between parametrizations I and II is at the level of 3%.
- The NN-amplitude parameter set A uses sigma_NN^tot = 41.86 mb, B_NN = 7.39 (GeV/c)^(-2), alpha_NN = -0.33; set B uses sigma_NN^tot = 41 mb, B_NN = 7.9 (GeV/c)^(-2), alpha_NN = -0.55.

## Context

This work extends the hypothesis of Dakhno and Nikolaev (1980s) that a 12% admixture of a twelve-quark bag configuration in the He-4 ground state explains irregularities in high-energy pHe-4 elastic scattering. The authors had previously shown (Mosallem and Uzhinskii, 2002) that this hypothesis describes pHe-4 scattering over a wide energy range. The present paper applies the same idea to dHe-4 scattering, demonstrating that it resolves the inconsistency between pHe-4 and dHe-4 data when using consistent NN-amplitude parameters. The results enable future experimental tests requiring absolute normalization accuracy better than 10% to confirm the 12q bag admixture effect.
