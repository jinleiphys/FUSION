# Multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum in $p$-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV

> Semi-inclusive transverse momentum spectra observed in proton-proton and proton-lead nuclear collisions at LHC energies obey a geometric scaling with a scaling variable using multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum. The saturation momentum extracted from the experimental data is proportional to the 1/6 power of the hadron multiplicity in the final state. On the other hand, the system's transverse size is proportional to the 1/3 power of the multiplicity, and the saturation momentum and the transverse size of the system are strongly correlated with the hadron multiplicity in the final state. Since the saturation momentum is proportional to the average transverse momentum of hadrons, one predicts average transverse momentum is also proportional to the 1/6 power of the multiplicity, which is consistent with experimental results at the LHC energy. We found that a nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm pPb}$ calculated by the multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum decreases at $p_{\rm T} \lesssim$ 1GeV/$c$ and that our model can partially explain the $R_{\rm pPb}$'s behavior thought to be caused by nuclear shadowing. On the other hand, Cronin enhancement experimentally observed at $2 \lesssim p_{\rm T} \lesssim $ 6 GeV/$c$ is not reproduced. However, the experimental result, including the Cronin effect, can be reproduced well by introducing $p_{\rm T}$ dependence as a 4$\sim$5\% correction to the multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum.We also discuss a relation between the geometric scaling in the semi-inclusive distributions and the string percolation model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that geometric scaling holds for semi-inclusive transverse momentum spectra in both pp and p-Pb collisions when using a multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum, but the extracted saturation momentum shows a change in its multiplicity dependence at dn/dy around 20 for p-Pb collisions, which is not observed in pp collisions up to the maximum measured multiplicities.

## Method

The author extracted the multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum Q_sat(W*) and effective transverse radius R_T* by fitting a universal Tsallis-type scaling function to semi-inclusive charged pion transverse momentum spectra from pp collisions at 2.76, 7.00, and 13.0 TeV and p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, using data from both ALICE and CMS Collaborations. The fits determined Q_sat(W*) and R_T* for each multiplicity class, and these were then analyzed as functions of the pion multiplicity density dn_pi/dy. The nuclear modification factor R_pPb was calculated using the extracted multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The universal function parameters for the semi-inclusive analysis are q = 1.145 and kappa = 0.1100, scaling 67 semi-inclusive distributions (947 data points) from pp and p-Pb collisions
- The saturation momentum Q_sat(W*) is proportional to the 1/6 power of the hadron multiplicity, and the transverse size R_T* is proportional to the 1/3 power of the multiplicity
- For pp collisions at 7.00 TeV, the saturation momentum from inclusive spectra is Q_sat(W) = 1.213 GeV/c, giving a flux tube size scale of 0.162 fm; at 13.0 TeV, Q_sat(W) = 1.289 GeV/c, giving 0.153 fm
- The average nuclear overlap function for p-Pb is <T_pPb> = 0.0983 mb^-1, and the total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is sigma_INEL_NN = 67.6 mb, giving a constant factor C = 6.645
- The average multiplicity for p-Pb inclusive events is dn_pi/dy = 16.81, and for pp inclusive events is dn_pi/dy = 4.13
- The slope of mean transverse momentum versus [dn/dy]^(1/6) is approximately 0.3 to 0.4 GeV/c
- The nuclear modification factor R_pPb calculated with multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum decreases at p_T less than about 1 GeV/c, partially explaining nuclear shadowing behavior, but Cronin enhancement at 2 to 6 GeV/c is not reproduced without a 4 to 5 percent p_T-dependent correction to the saturation momentum

## Context

This work extends the author's previous geometric scaling analyses for semi-inclusive distributions in pp collisions (Osada 2017, Osada 2019) to p-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The results are consistent with the string percolation model and the Color Glass Condensate framework, and the multiplicity dependence of the mean transverse momentum agrees with experimental data from ALICE (Abelev 2013) and CMS Collaborations. The change in multiplicity dependence of Q_sat at dn/dy around 20 in p-Pb collisions, observed in ALICE data but not in CMS data, suggests a possible rapidity-window dependence that requires further investigation, and the inability to reproduce Cronin enhancement without corrections indicates limitations of the simple geometric scaling approach for nuclear modification factors.
