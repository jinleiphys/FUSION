# Microscopic Model Analysis of the $^{6}$He,$^{6}$Li+$^{28}$Si Total Reaction Cross Sections at the Energy Range 5-50 a Mev

> The existing and some preliminary experimental data on the total cross sections of the $^{4,6}$He, $^{6,7}$Li +$^{28}$Si reactions at energies E=5-50 A MeV are demonstrated. The data on $^{6}$Li,$^{6}$He+$^{28}$Si are analyzed in the framework of the microscopic optical potential with real and imaginary parts obtained with a help of the double-folding procedure and by using the current models of densities of the projectile nuclei. Besides, the microscopic double-folding Coulomb potential is calculated and its effect on cross sections is compared with that when one applies the traditional Coulomb potential of the uniform charge distribution. The semi-microscopic potentials are constructed from both the renormalized microscopic potentials and their derivatives to take into account collective motion effect and to improve an agreement with experimental data.

# Microscopic Model Analysis of the $^{6}$He,$^{6}$Li+$^{28}$Si Total Reaction Cross Sections at the Energy Range 5-50 A MeV

## Key claim

The microscopic double-folding optical potential, without free parameters, overpredicts the total reaction cross sections for $^{6}$He,$^{6}$Li+$^{28}$Si and only follows the data shape at energies above 15 MeV/nucleon; a semi-microscopic potential with four adjustable parameters (strength renormalization plus derivative "surface terms") is required to achieve fair agreement with data across the full 5-50 A MeV range, particularly at lower energies.

## Method

The real part of the nucleus-nucleus optical potential was calculated using the double-folding procedure with the Paris CDM3Y6 effective NN-potential (including exchange terms), while the imaginary part was taken from the high-energy approximation (HEA) theory. Density distributions for $^{6}$He and $^{6}$Li were taken from the Tanihata model, the cluster-orbital shell-model approximation (COSMA), and the large-scale shell-model (LSSM). Semi-microscopic potentials were constructed by adding renormalized volume terms and derivative "surface terms" to account for collective motion effects, with cross sections computed using the DWUCK4 code.

## Key numbers

- Energy range studied: 5-50 A MeV for $^{4,6}$He, $^{6,7}$Li + $^{28}$Si reactions
- For $^{6}$He+$^{28}$Si with LSSM density and renormalization parameters N$_r$=N$_{im}$=0.5, agreement with data was achieved at higher energies but significant discrepancy remained at lower energies
- For the four-parameter fit using Paris CDM3Y6 with LSSM density: N$_r$=0.7, N$_r^{(1)}$=0.4, N$_{im}$=0.5, N$_{im}^{(1)}$=0.03 gave "fairly well agreement" with experimental data
- For the four-parameter fit using Reid BDM3Y2 with FDM density: N$_r$=1, N$_r^{(1)}$=0.212, N$_{im}$=0.3, N$_{im}^{(1)}$=0.038 gave "qualitative description"
- The "bump" in $^{6}$He+$^{28}$Si total reaction cross section occurs at E $\simeq$ 15 MeV
- The Paris CDM3Y6 effective NN-potential parameters include C=0.2658, $\alpha$=3.8033, $\gamma$=4.0, with energy dependence g(E)=1-0.003E/A$_p$

## Context

This work extends the double-folding model approach of Khoa (2000) and the HEA-based microscopic optical potential of Lukyanov, Zemlyanaya, and Lukyanov (2004) to total reaction cross sections for neutron-rich $^{6}$He and stable $^{6}$Li projectiles on $^{28}$Si. The study tests multiple nuclear density models (Tanihata 1992, Zhukov 1993, Karataglidis 2000) and demonstrates that purely microscopic volume potentials fail at low energies, motivating the inclusion of collective surface excitations via derivative terms. The results call for further experimental and theoretical investigation of the cross section enhancement near 10 MeV above the Coulomb barrier, and suggest that angular distribution measurements in the elastic channel could reduce parameter ambiguities in semi-microscopic optical potentials.
