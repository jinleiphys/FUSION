# Interferometric signatures of the temperature dependence of the specific shear viscosity in heavy-ion collisions

> Recent work has shown that a temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio, $η/s$, influences the collective flow pattern in heavy-ion collisions in characteristic ways that can be measured by studying hadron transverse momentum spectra and their anisotropies. Here we point out that it also affects the pair momentum dependence of the Hanbury-Brown$-$Twiss (HBT) radii (the source size parameters extracted from two-particle intensity interferometry) and the variance of their event-by-event fluctuations. This observation establishes interferometric signatures as useful observables to complement the constraining power of single-particle spectra on the temperature dependence of $η/s$.

## Key claim
The temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio, η/s(T), affects not only single-particle spectra and anisotropic flow but also the pair-momentum dependence of Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii and the variance of their event-by-event fluctuations; however, the sensitivity of the sideward radius and the out-side cross term to η/s in the hadronic phase is almost entirely due to the theoretically poorly controlled viscous correction δf at freeze-out, while the outward and longitudinal radii show robust sensitivity only to η/s in the high-temperature QGP phase.

## Method
The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU package to simulate 1000 central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV with MC-Glauber initial conditions and VISH2+1 hydrodynamics with the s95p-PCE165-v0 equation of state. They computed HBT radii from source variances of the pion emission function via the Cooper-Frye integral, using four parametrizations of (η/s)(T) from Molnar (2014): constant low (LH-LQ), low in hadronic phase with high in QGP (LH-HQ), high in hadronic phase with low in QGP (HH-LQ), and high in both phases (HH-HQ). They compared single-shot hydrodynamics (SSH) from ensemble-averaged initial conditions with direct ensemble averaging (DEA) over 1000 fluctuating events, and separated dynamical effects from freeze-out δf corrections by recomputing without δf.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for hydrodynamic evolution
- Transition temperature T_tr = 180 MeV between QGP and HRG phases
- Decoupling temperature T_dec = 120 MeV for freeze-out surface
- Ensemble size N_ev = 1000 events for event-by-event analysis
- Rescaling factors κ_fit for initial entropy profiles: LH-LQ = 0.889, LH-HQ = 0.596, HH-LQ = 0.878, HH-HQ = 0.589
- For the LH-LQ parametrization, (η/s)(T) = 0.08 for all T
- For the HH-HQ parametrization, (η/s)(T) in the QGP phase is given by -0.289 + 0.288(T/T_tr) + 0.0818(T/T_tr)² for T > T_tr
- For the HH-HQ parametrization, (η/s)(T) in the HRG phase is given by 0.681 - 0.0594(T/T_tr) - 0.544(T/T_tr)² for T ≤ T_tr
- High-temperature effects of η/s on R²_o and R²_l can reach 20% for K_T values below 1.5 GeV
- Event-by-event fluctuations boost the Φ_K-averaged means of fluctuating radii by a few percent above single-shot hydrodynamic radii

## Context
This work extends the study by Molnar (2014) on how the temperature dependence of η/s influences collective flow and anisotropic flow coefficients v₂ and v₄, by adding HBT interferometry as a complementary observable. It contrasts with earlier studies by Romatschke (2007) and Bozek (2009) that used constant (T-independent) η/s and extracted HBT radii from Gaussian fits to correlation functions, and with Teaney (2003) whose blast-wave analysis of δf effects on HBT radii is shown here to be fragile due to sensitivity to the velocity shear tensor at freeze-out. The results enable future experimental measurements of both mean HBT radii and their event-by-event variances to constrain the temperature dependence of η/s, particularly in the QGP phase above T_tr.
