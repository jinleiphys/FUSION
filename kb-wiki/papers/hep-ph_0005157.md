# Photoproduction of top in peripheral heavy ion collisions

> In relativistic heavy ion collisions, top quarks can be produced by photon-gluon fusion when a photon from the Weizsäcker-Williams virtual photon field of one nucleus interacts with a gluon in the other nucleus. Photoproduction with heavy ions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) will be the first accessible non-hadronic top production channel. We calculate the $t \bar t$ photoproduction cross sections, pair mass and top quark rapidity distributions in peripheral heavy ion collisions. The cross sections are sensitive to the top quark charge and the large-$Q^2$ gluon distribution in the nucleus. We find a cross section of 94 pb in calcium-calcium collisions, leading to 190 pairs in a one month ($10^6$ sec) LHC run. We also find $p$Pb and $p$Ca cross sections of 5.8 and 3.4 pb respectively, resulting in 6 and 34 $t\bar t$ pairs per month.

# Photoproduction of top in peripheral heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Top quark pairs can be produced via photon-gluon fusion in peripheral heavy ion collisions at the LHC, with cross sections large enough to yield observable event rates, provided that the hadronic rejection factor is only moderate and that statistical separation based on rapidity gaps, nuclear breakup signatures, and multiplicity differences is achievable.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading-order cross section for $t\bar{t}$ production via $\gamma + g \rightarrow t + \bar{t}$ using the Weizsäcker-Williams virtual photon flux from one nucleus and the MRST LO gluon distribution for the target nucleus, integrated over impact parameters where the nuclei do not interact hadronically ($b > 2R_A$). The calculation includes shadowing corrections via a parameterization from Eskola, Kolhinen, and Ruuskanen (1998, 1999), and the photon flux is integrated over the transverse area of the target nucleus outside the nuclear radius. Results are presented for O+O and Pb+Pb collisions, as well as for pO and pPb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Oxygen-oxygen cross section: 15 pb, yielding 210 $t\bar{t}$ pairs in a $10^6$ s LHC run
- Lead-lead cross section: 550 pb, yielding 0.2 $t\bar{t}$ pairs in a $10^6$ s LHC run
- pO cross section: 2.9 pb, yielding 1100 $t\bar{t}$ pairs per month
- pPb cross section: 55 pb, yielding 40 $t\bar{t}$ pairs per month
- Top quark mass used in calculation: $m = 175$ GeV
- Strong coupling constant: $\alpha_s(Q^2) \approx 0.11$ evaluated at $Q^2 = m^2 + p_T^2$
- Top quark charge: $e_t = 2/3$
- Photon cutoff energy in target rest frame: 500 TeV for lead, 1800 TeV for oxygen
- Average gluon momentum fraction at $y=0$, $p_T=0$: $\langle x_2 \rangle \approx 0.06$ for lead, 0.05 for oxygen
- LO $pp \rightarrow t\bar{t}X$ cross section: 41 pb at $\sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV, 80 pb at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV
- Top quark width: $\Gamma_t = 1.5$ GeV, corresponding to decay length over 100 fm in nuclear rest frame

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of heavy quark photoproduction in heavy ion collisions, specifically charm and bottom production by Baron and Baur (1993) and Greiner et al. (1995), to the top quark sector. It corrects an earlier flawed calculation by Schneider, Greiner, and Soff (1992) which used a photon flux integrated over the nuclear interior and overestimated the Pb+Pb cross section by a factor of 10. The results enable future measurements of the top quark charge and mass via a non-hadronic production channel, and a comparison of $pA$ and $AA$ photoproduction rates could provide a measurement of nuclear gluon shadowing at $Q^2$ values far above those currently accessible.
