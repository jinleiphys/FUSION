# Entrance-channel potentials in the synthesis of the heaviest nuclei

> Entrance-channel potentials in nucleus-nucleus collisions, relevant for the synthesis of superheavy elements, are systematically studied within a semi-microscopic approach, where microscopic nuclear densities of the colliding spherical or deformed nuclei are used in semi-classical expressions of the energy-density functional. From experimental data on fusion windows evidence is found that the existence of pockets in the entrance-channel potentials is crucial for fusion. Criteria for the choice of best collision systems for the synthesis of superheavy elements are discussed.

## Key claim

The existence of a pocket in the entrance-channel potential is crucial for fusion in the synthesis of superheavy elements, and the width of the observed fusion window is proportional to the depth of this pocket, with fusion windows lying systematically 5 to 10 MeV below the semi-microscopic potential barrier.

## Method

The authors calculated entrance-channel potentials using a semi-microscopic approach that combines microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov nucleon densities of spherical or deformed nuclei with semi-classical extended Thomas-Fermi energy-density functionals based on Skyrme forces. The interaction potential was obtained by integrating the energy-density functional over the frozen densities of the colliding nuclei, with the Coulomb part calculated from the full charge distribution rather than point-charge approximations. The potentials were evaluated for various projectile-target combinations relevant to cold, hot, and warm fusion pathways for superheavy element synthesis.

## Key numbers

- The collision time for superheavy element production reactions is approximately tau_coll approx 5 x 10^{-22} s, based on a typical pocket oscillator frequency of hbar omega_pocket approx 4 MeV
- The relaxation time of the intrinsic nuclear state is tau_relax approx 2 x 10^{-22} / (epsilon*/MeV) s, which at touching (epsilon* less than about 0.02 MeV per nucleon) gives tau_relax greater than about 10^{-20} s, more than one order of magnitude larger than the collision time
- For the system 58Fe+64Ni, the difference between the SMP barriers obtained with different Skyrme forces and the KNS, Bass, and proximity potentials is around 3 MeV in energy and 1 fm in position
- The binding energy difference between HFB and the semi-classical expression is -0.9 MeV for 48Ca and -0.3 MeV for 208Pb using the SkM* force
- For the synthesis of SHE 118, the authors predict a favorable center-of-mass energy of about 206 MeV for the system 48Ca+252Cf and about 330 MeV for the system 96Zr+198Pt
- The rotational time for a deformed nucleus is tau_rot approx 2 x 10^{-20} s, based on a rotational energy of hbar omega_rot approx 50 keV, while characteristic collision times on the approaching part of the Coulomb trajectory are of order 2 x 10^{-21} s

## Context

This work extends earlier approaches to nucleus-nucleus potentials such as the Bass parametrization (Bass 1974, 1980), the proximity potential (Swiatecki 1977), and the Krappe-Nix-Sierk potential (Krappe, Nix, and Sierk 1979), showing that these simpler models give barriers spread over a large interval compared to the semi-microscopic potential. The study also contrasts with folding procedures using Landau-Migdal interactions (Adamian, Nasirov, Antonenko) and with calculations using only Thomas-Fermi kinetic energy contributions (Denisov and Pilipenko). The results enable more reliable predictions for optimal projectile-target combinations and bombarding energies for synthesizing new superheavy elements, particularly for systems leading to element 118.
