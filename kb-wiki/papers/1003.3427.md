# Emission of charged particles from excited compound nuclei

> The formation of excited compound nucleus (CN) and its statistical decay is investigated within the dinuclear system (DNS) model.The initial DNS is formed in the entrance channel when the projectile is captured by a target, and then the evolution of DNS in mass asymmetry coordinate leads to formation of the hot CN. The emission barriers for complex fragments were calculated within the DNS model by using the double folding procedure for the interaction potential. It is shown that cross sections for complex fragment emission become larger when excited CN is more neutron deficient. This approach gives also an opportunity to calculate the new neutron deficient isotopes production cross sections and can be applied to describe the hot fission of heavy systems.The model was tested by comparison of calculated results with experimental data

## Key claim
The dinuclear system (DNS) model can describe the statistical decay of excited compound nuclei into complex fragments (Z>2), with the key condition that cross sections for complex fragment emission become larger when the excited compound nucleus is more neutron deficient, because neutron evaporation is suppressed in such systems.

## Method
The DNS model treats compound nucleus formation as capture of a projectile by a target, followed by evolution in mass asymmetry coordinate. Emission barriers for complex fragments were calculated using a double folding procedure for the interaction potential. Decay widths were computed via transition state formalism with Fermi-gas level densities, and a Monte-Carlo technique was used to generate cascade decay sequences. The model was tested against experimental data for the reactions 3He+108Ag, 78,86Kr+12C, and 63Cu+12C.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction 3He + 108Ag at Elab=90 MeV leading to CN 111In: Jmax=15 hbar (Jkin_max=17 hbar, Jcr=15 hbar)
- For the reaction 78Kr + 12C at 8.52 MeV/nucleon: Jmax=39 hbar (Jkin_max=39 hbar, Jcr=42 hbar); experimental fitting used Jmax=43 hbar
- For the reaction 86Kr + 12C at 9.31 MeV/nucleon: Jcr=45 hbar, Jkin_max=42 hbar
- For the reaction 63Cu + 12C at 12.96 MeV/nucleon: Jcr=40 hbar, Jkin_max=48 hbar
- The model was tested for asymmetric reactions with A ~ 75-120, where angular momenta up to ~45 hbar are involved

## Context
This work extends the DNS model approach previously developed by the author's group (Kalandarov, 2010) to describe complex fragment emission from excited compound nuclei. It builds on earlier experimental studies by Sobotka (1983) and theoretical work by Lynch and Moretto on complex fragment emission. The model enables calculation of production cross sections for new neutron-deficient isotopes and can be applied to describe hot fission of heavy systems, with further development needed for heavier systems and higher angular momenta.
