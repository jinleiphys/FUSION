# Measurement of Compton scattering at MAMI for the extraction of the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the proton

> A precise measurement of the differential cross-sections $dσ/dΩ$ and the linearly polarized photon beam asymmetry $Σ_3$ for Compton scattering on the proton below pion threshold has been performed with a tagged photon beam and almost $4π$ detector at the Mainz Microtron. The incident photons were produced by the recently upgraded Glasgow-Mainz photon tagging facility and impinged on a cryogenic liquid hydrogen target, with the scattered photons detected in the Crystal Ball/TAPS set-up. Using the highest statistics Compton scattering data ever measured on the proton along with two effective field theories (both covariant baryon and heavy-baryon) and one fixed-$t$ dispersion relation model, constraining the fits with the Baldin sum rule, we have obtained the proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities with unprecedented precision: \begin{align*} &{}α_{E1} = 10.99 \pm 0.16 \pm 0.47 \pm 0.17 \pm 0.34 &{}β_{M1} = 3.14 \pm 0.21 \pm 0.24 \pm 0.20 \pm 0.35 \end{align*} in units of $10^{-4}$\,fm$^3$ where the errors are statistical, systematic, spin polarizability dependent and model dependent.

## Key claim

The paper reports the highest-statistics Compton scattering data ever obtained on the proton, yielding proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities with unprecedented precision, but the quoted central values and errors depend on combining results from three different theoretical models (covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, and fixed-t dispersion relations) and on constraining the fits with the Baldin sum rule, producing a final model-dependent uncertainty.

## Method

The A2 Collaboration at MAMI used a tagged, linearly polarized photon beam from coherent Bremsstrahlung on a diamond radiator, incident on a cryogenic liquid hydrogen target, with scattered photons detected in the Crystal Ball/TAPS nearly-4π calorimeter setup. Unpolarized differential cross-sections were extracted for five photon energy bins from 86 to 140 MeV and twelve polar angular bins from 30° to 150°, while the beam asymmetry Σ₃ was extracted in three energy bins over the same angular range. The scalar polarizabilities αE1 and βM1 were obtained by fitting these data simultaneously with three theoretical frameworks (HDPV dispersion relations, BχPT, and HBχPT), with spin polarizabilities fixed to previous experimental values and the Baldin sum rule included as an additional constraint.

## Key numbers

- αE1 = 10.99 ± 0.16 (stat) ± 0.47 (sys) ± 0.17 (spin pol.) ± 0.34 (model) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- βM1 = 3.14 ± 0.21 (stat) ± 0.24 (sys) ± 0.20 (spin pol.) ± 0.35 (model) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Correlation coefficient between αE1 and βM1: ρ = -0.75
- Baldin sum rule constraint used: αE1 + βM1 = 13.8 ± 0.4 in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Photon energy range for analysis: ωγ = 85 – 140 MeV
- Degree of linear polarization: up to 78%
- Systematic uncertainty on cross-section normalization: 3% (from target density 1%, photon flux 2%, analysis cuts and Monte Carlo 2%)
- Background contamination in cross-section: 10% at low energy and scattering angle down to 0.2% at high energy and central angles
- χ²/DOF for all three fits: 0.89 (82.10/93, 82.96/93, 83.16/93)

## Context

This work extends the previous highest-statistics Compton scattering measurement by the TAPS collaboration (Olmos 2001) by improving statistical precision by approximately a factor of five and providing wider angular coverage with smaller systematic errors. It builds on a pilot experiment by the A2 Collaboration (Sokhoyan 2017) that first measured the photon beam asymmetry below pion threshold. The new data, combined with previously published single and double polarization observables from the A2 Collaboration (Martel 2015, Paudyal 2020), enable a future combined extraction of all six proton polarizabilities from a single facility, resolving ambiguities in the extraction of scalar polarizabilities that motivated the Particle Data Group to adjust their values based solely on improved chiral effective field theory analyses (Lensky 2010, Griesshammer 2012, Lensky 2014, Lensky 2015) without new experimental data.
