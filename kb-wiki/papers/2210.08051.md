# Prospects for $纬^\star 纬^\star \to 蟺蟺$ via lattice QCD

> The $纬^\star 纬^\star \to 蟺蟺$ scattering amplitude plays a key role in a wide range of phenomena, including understanding the inner structure of scalar resonances as well as constraining the hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. In this work, we explain how the infinite-volume Minkowski amplitude can be constrained from finite-volume Euclidean correlation functions. The relationship between the finite-volume Euclidean correlation functions and the desired amplitude holds up to energies where $3蟺$ states can go on shell, and is exact up to exponentially small corrections that scale like $\mathcal{O}(e^{-m_蟺L})$, where $L$ is the spatial extent of the cubic volume and $m_蟺$ is the pion mass. In order to implement this formalism and remove all power-law finite volume errors, it is necessary to first obtain $蟺蟺\to 蟺蟺$, $蟺纬^\star \to 蟺$, $纬^\star \to蟺蟺$, and $蟺蟺纬^\star \to蟺蟺$ amplitudes; all of which can be determined via lattice quantum chromodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact formalism relating the infinite-volume Minkowski amplitude for $\gamma^\star \gamma^\star \to \pi\pi$ to finite-volume Euclidean correlation functions computable via lattice QCD, but this relationship holds only up to energies where $3\pi$ states can go on shell and is exact only up to exponentially small corrections of order $\mathcal{O}(e^{-m_\pi L})$.

## Method

The authors derive a self-consistent equation for the $\gamma^\star \gamma^\star \to \pi\pi$ amplitude in terms of on-shell subprocess amplitudes and short-distance kernels, then relate the divergence-free part of this amplitude to finite-volume Euclidean matrix elements. They separate the Euclidean correlation function into a convergent short-distance part (obtained by subtracting contributions from intermediate states that can go on-shell) and a long-distance part expressed as sums over finite-volume poles, then apply a finite-volume correction factor (the Lellouch-Luscher factor) to remove power-law finite-volume errors from the final $\pi\pi$ state.

## Key numbers

* The finite-volume corrections scale as $\mathcal{O}(e^{-m_\pi L})$, where $L$ is the spatial extent of the cubic volume and $m_\pi$ is the pion mass
* The formalism is valid for energies below the $3\pi$ production threshold
* The lowest-lying glueball candidate is expected to have $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$ quantum numbers and a mass of approximately 1.5 to 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the framework of Briceno (2019) for $1+\mathcal{J} \to 1+\mathcal{J}$ processes to the more complex $\mathcal{J}+\mathcal{J} \to 2$ case, where on-shell two-particle states can appear both between the two currents and in the final state. The formalism builds on Luscher's method for extracting $\pi\pi\to\pi\pi$ scattering amplitudes from finite-volume spectra (Luscher 1986), and on subsequent developments for electromagnetic amplitudes including $\gamma^\star\to\pi\pi$ (Meyer 2011, Briceno 2015) and $\pi\pi\gamma^\star\to\pi\pi$ (Briceno 2015, Baroni 2018). The result enables lattice QCD calculations that can help constrain hadronic light-by-light contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon and provide insight into the glueball content of scalar resonances.
