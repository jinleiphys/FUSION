# Electric dipole polarizability constraints on neutron skin and symmetry energy

> We review the experimental knowledge on the dipole polarizability (DP) of nuclei and its relation to the neutron skin thickness and properties of the neutron-rich matter equation of state (EOS). The discussion focuses on recent experiments using relativistic Coulomb excitation in inelastic proton scattering at extreme forward angles covering a mass range from $^{40}$Ca to $^{208}$Pb. Constraints on the neutron skins and the density dependence of the symmetry energy are derived from systematic comparison to calculations based on density functional theory (DFT) and ab initio methods utilizing interactions derived from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). The results consistently favor a soft EOS around or slightly below the saturation point. An outlook is given on possible improvements of the precision achievable in stable nuclei and studies of exotic neutron-rich unstable nuclei with upcoming experimental facilities.

## Key claim

The review argues that the electric dipole polarizability (DP) of nuclei provides an independent experimental constraint on the neutron skin thickness and the density dependence of the symmetry energy, consistently favoring a soft equation of state around or slightly below saturation density, but the extraction of these quantities from DP measurements requires model-dependent comparisons to density functional theory or ab initio calculations.

## Method

The authors review experimental measurements of the dipole polarizability across a mass range from ⁴⁰Ca to ²⁰⁸Pb, focusing on relativistic Coulomb excitation in inelastic proton scattering at extreme forward angles (including 0°) at energies of several hundred MeV. They compare these experimental results to theoretical predictions from density functional theory (DFT) and ab initio coupled-cluster calculations using interactions derived from chiral effective field theory (χEFT), establishing correlations between the dipole polarizability, neutron skin thickness, and the symmetry energy parameters J and L.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of nuclear matter is n₀ ~ 0.16 fm⁻³
- The symmetry energy parameter J is constrained to a range of about 30 to 35 MeV
- DFT predictions for the neutron skin in ²⁰⁸Pb vary from 0.12 to 0.32 fm
- Ab initio calculations for ⁴⁸Ca predict neutron skin values varying from 0.12 to 0.15 fm
- DFT predictions for ⁴⁸Ca give neutron skin values ranging from 0.16 fm to 0.22 fm

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting the nuclear equation of state to experimentally accessible observables, building on prior studies of neutron skin thickness from elastic proton scattering (Zenihiro 2010), coherent π⁰ production (Tarbert 2014), antiprotonic atoms (Trzcinska 2001, Klos 2007), and parity-violating electron scattering (Adhikari 2021, 2022). The work enables future studies of exotic neutron-rich unstable nuclei at rare isotope beam facilities like RIKEN, FRIB, and GSI/FAIR, where dipole polarizability measurements via relativistic Coulomb excitation in inverse kinematics can probe nuclei with extremely large neutron excess relevant to neutron star physics.
