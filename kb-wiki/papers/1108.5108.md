# On the possibility to measure nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless double beta decay of Ca48

> As shown in Ref. \cite{Rod09}, the Fermi part $M_{F}^{0ν}$ of the total $0νββ$-decay nuclear matrix element $M^{0ν}$ can be related to the single Fermi transition matrix element between the isobaric analog state (IAS) of the ground state of the initial nucleus and the ground state of the final nucleus. The latter matrix element could be measured in charge-exchange reactions. Here we discuss a possibility of such a measurement for $^{48}$Ca and estimate the cross-section of the reaction $^{48}$Ti(n,p)$^{48}$Sc(IAS).

## Key claim

The Fermi part of the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element for Ca48 can be reconstructed from a single isospin-forbidden Fermi transition matrix element measurable in charge-exchange reactions, but only if the isobaric analog state (IAS) is a single, unfragmented state and the perturbative treatment of isospin mixing is justified.

## Method

The author uses the master relation from Rodin (2009) that connects the Fermi matrix element M_F^0ν to an energy-weighted double Fermi transition, which in leading order of Coulomb mixing is dominated by the transition via the IAS. The cross-section for the reaction Ti48(n,p)Sc48(IAS) is estimated by scaling the measured cross-section of Ca48(p,n)Sc48(IAS) using the squared ratio of the suppressed matrix element ⟨IAS|T^+|0_f⟩ to the allowed matrix element ⟨IAS|T^-|0_i⟩, with the suppressed matrix element obtained from QRPA calculations of M_F^0ν via a transformed version of the master relation.

## Key numbers

- The IAS of Ca48 lies at excitation energy E_x = 6.678 MeV in Sc48, with ω_IAS ≈ 8.5 MeV.
- The suppressed matrix element ⟨IAS|T^+|0_f⟩ is estimated as ~0.005, about 1000 times smaller than the first-leg matrix element ⟨IAS|T^-|0_i⟩ ≈ √(N-Z).
- The squared ratio |⟨IAS|T^+|0_f⟩ / ⟨IAS|T^-|0_i⟩|^2 ≈ 2×10^(-6).
- The measured cross-section for Ca48(p,n)Sc48(IAS) at proton energy E_p = 134 MeV is d²σ_pn/dΩdE ≈ 10 mb/(sr MeV).
- The estimated cross-section for Ti48(n,p)Sc48(IAS) is d²σ_np/dΩdE ≈ 20 nb/(sr MeV).
- The Fermi 2νββ nuclear matrix element for Ca48 is estimated as M_F^2ν ≈ -1.4×10^(-3) MeV^(-1).

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Rodin (2009) that relates the Fermi part of the 0νββ-decay matrix element to a single Fermi transition measurable in charge-exchange reactions, applying it specifically to Ca48. The author argues that Ca48 is a favorable candidate because its IAS is a single state without fragmentation, as supported by the measurement of Gr07 showing no fine structure, unlike heavier nuclei where IAS spreading becomes significant. This enables a possible experimental determination of M_F^0ν, which combined with the more reliably calculable ratio M_GT^0ν/M_F^0ν ≈ -2.5, could help constrain the total nuclear matrix element and reduce the factor-of-5 scatter among different theoretical calculations.
