# Concurrent application of ANC and THM to assess the $^{13}{\rm C}(α,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ absolute cross section at astrophysical energies and possible consequences for neutron production in low-mass AGB stars

> The $^{13}{\rm C}(α,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction is considered to be the main neutron source responsible for the production of heavy nuclides (from ${\rm Sr}$ to ${\rm Bi}$) through slow $n$-capture nucleosynthesis ($s$-process) at low temperatures during the asymptotic giant branch (AGB) phase of low mass stars ($\lesssim 3-4\;{\rm M}_{\odot}$, or LMSs). In recent years, several direct and indirect measurements have been carried out to determine the cross section at the energies of astrophysical interest (around $190\pm40\;{\rm keV}$). However, they yield inconsistent results causing a highly uncertain reaction rate and affecting the neutron release in LMSs. In this work we have combined two indirect approaches, the asymptotic normalization coefficient (or ANC) and the Trojan Horse Method (THM), to unambiguously determine the absolute value of the $^{13}{\rm C}(α,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ astrophysical factor. Therefore, we have determined a very accurate reaction rate to be introduced into astrophysical models of $s$-process nucleosynthesis in LMSs. Calculations using such recommended rate have shown limited variations in the production of those neutron-rich nuclei (with $86\leq A\leq 209$) receiving contribution only by slow neutron captures.

# Concurrent application of ANC and THM to assess the $^{13}{\rm C}(\alpha,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ absolute cross section at astrophysical energies and possible consequences for neutron production in low-mass AGB stars

## Key claim

By combining the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) from transfer reactions with Trojan Horse Method (THM) data, the authors determine that the direct measurement dataset of BAI73 (1973) combined with DRO93 (1993) at low energies is the only direct dataset compatible with both the THM S-factor and the ANC of the threshold level, resolving a long-standing discrepancy between indirect approaches that previously yielded inconsistent results for the $^{13}{\rm C}(\alpha,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction rate.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed THM data from La Cognata et al. (2013) using the updated resonance energy and width for the $1/2^{+}$ state of $^{17}{\rm O}$ from Faehlmann et al. (2015) (now a threshold resonance at $4.7\pm3$ keV above the $\alpha$-threshold rather than a sub-threshold resonance) and the most precise ANC value from Avila et al. (2015) of $3.6\pm0.7$ fm$^{-1}$. They performed HOES (half-of-energy-shell) R-matrix fitting of the THM data, using the ANC to constrain the normalization of indirect data, then compared the resulting astrophysical S-factor against three different sets of direct measurements to identify which direct dataset best matches the THM data scaled to match the literature ANC.

## Key numbers

- The $1/2^{+}$ state of $^{17}{\rm O}$ is now centered at $4.7\pm3$ keV above the $\alpha$-threshold, with excitation energy $E^* = 6.3634 \pm 0.0031$ MeV and total width $\Gamma = 0.136 \pm 0.005$ MeV (Faehlmann et al. 2015)
- The squared Coulomb-modified ANC from Avila et al. (2015) is $3.6 \pm 0.7$ fm$^{-1}$, used to constrain the THM normalization
- The best fit to THM data using the BAI73 and DRO93 direct datasets yields a reduced $\tilde{\chi}^2 = 1.65$ over the energy range $-0.3$ to $1.2$ MeV
- The neutron partial width of the $1/2^{+}$ resonance is $\Gamma_n^{1/2^+} = 136 \pm 5$ keV (Faehlmann et al. 2015)
- The Gamow window for astrophysical interest is $190 \pm 40$ keV at a temperature of $10^8$ K

## Context

This work extends the ongoing effort to determine the $^{13}{\rm C}(\alpha,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction rate, which is the main neutron source for the s-process in low-mass AGB stars (less than $3-4$ M$_\odot$). Previous direct measurements (DRO93, HEI08, HAR05, BAI73, KEL89) and indirect approaches (ANC from JOH06, PEL08, GUO12, AVI15; THM from LAC12, LAC13) yielded inconsistent results with uncertainties reaching about $300\%$ in the astrophysically relevant region. The NACRE II compilation (XU13) showed high uncertainties of $+36\%$ and $-28\%$ around $0.09$ GK. This work enables a reliable reaction rate for s-process nucleosynthesis models in low-mass AGB stars, with calculations showing limited variations in the production of neutron-rich nuclei with $86 \leq A \leq 209$.
