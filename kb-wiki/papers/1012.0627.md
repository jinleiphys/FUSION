# The atomic electric dipole moment induced by the nuclear electric dipole moment; the magnetic moment effect

> We have considered a mechanism for inducing a time-reversal violating electric dipole moment (EDM) in atoms through the interaction of a nuclear EDM (d_N) with the hyperfine interaction, the "magnetic moment effect". We have derived the operator for this interaction and presented analytical formulas for the matrix elements between atomic states. Induced EDMs in the diamagnetic atoms 129Xe, 171Yb, 199Hg, 211Rn, and 225Ra have been calculated numerically. From the experimental limits on the atomic EDMs of 129Xe and 199Hg, we have placed the following constraints on the nuclear EDMs, |d_N(129Xe)|< 1.1 * 10^{-21} |e|cm and |d_N(199Hg)|< 2.8 * 10^{-24} |e|cm.

## Key claim

The atomic electric dipole moment (EDM) induced by the nuclear EDM through the hyperfine interaction (the "magnetic moment effect") is generally smaller than the atomic EDM induced by the nuclear Schiff moment, but the nuclear EDM contribution should not be disregarded because many-body corrections to the Schiff moment are large and not yet settled among different theoretical approaches.

## Method

The authors derived the P,T-odd operator for the interaction of a nuclear EDM with the hyperfine interaction and obtained analytical formulas for its matrix elements between atomic states. They performed numerical calculations using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) method and the random phase approximation (RPA) to compute the induced atomic EDMs for the diamagnetic atoms 129Xe, 171Yb, 199Hg, 211Rn, and 225Ra, then used experimental limits on atomic EDMs to constrain the nuclear EDMs.

## Key numbers

- For 129Xe: atomic EDM limit |d(129Xe)| < 6.6 x 10^{-27} |e| cm (from Rosenberry and Chupp, 2001), yielding nuclear EDM constraint |d_N(129Xe)| < 1.1 x 10^{-21} |e| cm
- For 199Hg: atomic EDM limit |d(199Hg)| < 3.1 x 10^{-29} |e| cm (from Griffith et al., 2009), yielding nuclear EDM constraint |d_N(199Hg)| < 2.8 x 10^{-24} |e| cm
- RPA results for atomic EDM in units of 10^{-6} d_N: 129Xe = 5.8, 171Yb = 11, 199Hg = 11, 211Rn = -13, 225Ra = -33
- Relativistic enhancement factors R_M: 129Xe = 1.28, 171Yb = 1.56, 199Hg = 1.83, 211Rn = 2.06, 225Ra = 2.15
- Analytical estimate: d_at^N approximately +/- 10^{-7} mu Z R_M d_N (upper sign for divalent atoms, lower sign for noble gases)

## Context

This work extends the original analysis by Schiff (1963), who first discussed the magnetic moment effect and evaluated EDMs for H and He, and later work by Hinds and Sandars (1980) on TlF. The authors previously calculated atomic EDMs induced by the nuclear Schiff moment, the P,T-odd electron-nucleon interaction, and the electron EDM (Dzuba, Flambaum, Porsev, 2009; Flambaum and Ginges, 2002). The results enable placing limits on nuclear EDMs from neutral atom measurements, which serve as an accuracy benchmark for proposed nuclear EDM measurements in ion storage rings. The contribution from the nuclear EDM is found to be about 1% of the Schiff moment contribution for 199Hg in a simple nuclear model, but the authors caution that many-body calculations of the Schiff moment (Ban, Dobaczewski, Engel, 2010; Dmitriev and Sen'kov, 2003) show large suppression and disagreement between approaches.
