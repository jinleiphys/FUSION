# Recent Results in Charged-Composite Particle Scattering

> A brief overview is given of some recent advances in charged-composite particle scattering. On the theoretical side, I address the three-charged particle wave function asymptotics, the nonperturbative investigation of the long-range behaviour of the optical potential, and the question of the compactness of the kernels of the momentum space integral equations for three charged particles. Among the more practical developments, I report on results of numerical calculations of so-called "triangle" amplitudes, a new, simple and very efficient higher-energy approximation for the latter, and a breakthrough in the quantitative treatment of Coulomb effects in proton-deuteron elastic scattering with realistic nuclear potentials.

# Recent Results in Charged-Composite Particle Scattering

## Key claim
The paper claims that the long-range behavior of the nonstatic optical potential for charged-composite particle scattering retains the same dipole polarisability \(a\) from perturbative approaches even when all higher-order terms are summed nonperturbatively, and that this result holds for all energies on the energy shell, but only under the condition that all three particles have charges of equal sign.

## Method
The author analytically investigates the asymptotic wave function for three free charged particles using Jacobi coordinates and on-shell momenta, deriving the leading singular behavior of the effective potential in momentum-space integral equations. Nonperturbative analysis of the optical potential is performed by inserting the spectral decomposition of the pure Coulomb three-body resolvent with both two- and three-body intermediate states, using the asymptotic three-charged particle wave function in the \(\Omega_{\alpha}\) region. For practical calculations, the author numerically evaluates "triangle" amplitudes (first-order rescattering terms) for arbitrary bound-state wave functions and develops a renormalized Coulomb-Born approximation. Proton-deuteron elastic scattering is treated within the screening and renormalisation approach using a separable representation of the Paris potential (PEST1-6) including \(^3S_1-^3D_1\), \(^1S_0\), and all \(P\) waves.

## Key numbers
- The static potential behaves as \(4\pi e_{\alpha}(e_{\beta}+e_{\gamma})/\Delta_{\alpha}^{2}\) in momentum space for \(\Delta_{\alpha} \to 0\), corresponding to \(e_{\alpha}(e_{\beta}+e_{\gamma})/\rho_{\alpha}\) in coordinate space.
- The nonstatic optical potential in second-order perturbation theory behaves as \(-a/(2\rho_{\alpha}^4)\) in coordinate space for \(\rho_{\alpha} \to \infty\), with \(a\) the static dipole polarisability.
- The nonperturbative result confirms the same \(-a/(2\rho_{\alpha}^4)\) asymptotic form, with no renormalisation of \(a\) from higher-order terms.
- The nondiagonal off-shell effective potential has a branch-point singularity \(\sim 1/D_0(z)^{(1 + i\eta_{\beta} + i\eta_{\alpha})}\) for \(\beta \neq \alpha\).
- On the energy shell, the nondiagonal effective potential behaves as \(1/\bar D_0^{(1- \eta^{(bs)}_{\alpha m}-\eta^{(bs)}_{\beta n})}\) for \(\beta \neq \alpha\).
- The renormalized Coulomb-Born approximation reproduces exact triangle amplitudes to within a few percent at 1 keV incident energy for light projectiles and at 100 keV for heavy projectiles.
- Coulomb-Born approximation magnitudes for light projectiles at 1 keV are off by approximately 40–100 percent.
- Proton-deuteron elastic scattering results at 5 and 10 MeV show good but not perfect agreement with experimental data from Sagara (1994) and Sperisen (1984).

## Context
This work extends the perturbative analysis of the optical potential for charged-composite particle scattering by Merkuriev (1988) and others, providing a nonperturbative confirmation that the dipole polarisability \(a\) is not renormalised by higher-order terms. The compactness analysis of momentum-space integral equations builds on the work of Vesselova (1970) for energies below breakup and extends it to positive energies, showing that kernels remain compact for equal-sign charges but develop nonintegrable singularities for opposite-sign charges. The practical developments enable quantitative treatment of Coulomb effects in proton-deuteron scattering with realistic nuclear potentials, previously restricted to energies below breakup by calculations such as those of Berthold, Stadler, and Zankel (1988) and Kvitsinsky, Roudnev, and Vostrikov (1994). The new renormalized Coulomb-Born approximation provides a simple and efficient alternative to the computationally expensive exact triangle amplitudes, with applications to reactions involving projectiles such as \(e^{\pm}\), \(p\), \(\bar p\) and targets including H, Ps, and \((\bar p,p)\).
