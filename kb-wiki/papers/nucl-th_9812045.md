# Final state interaction effects in mu-capture induced two-body decay of 3He

> The mu-capture process on 3He leading to a neutron, a deuteron and a mu-neutrino in the final state is studied. Three-nucleon Faddeev wave functions for the initial 3He bound and the final neutron-deuteron scattering states are calculated using the BonnB and Paris nucleon-nucleon potentials. The nuclear weak current operator is restricted to impulse approximation. Large effects on the decay rates of the final state interaction are found. The comparison to recent experimental data shows that the inclusion of final state interactions drastically improves the description of the data.

## Key claim

Final state interactions (FSI) between the outgoing neutron and deuteron increase the predicted decay rates for muon capture on ³He (leading to n + d + νμ) by a factor of approximately 200 compared to plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIAS) calculations, and this inclusion is necessary to bring theory into agreement with the only existing experimental data set.

## Method

The authors solved three-nucleon Faddeev equations for the initial ³He bound state and the final neutron-deuteron scattering state, using the Bonn B and Paris nucleon-nucleon potentials. The nuclear weak current operator was treated in the impulse approximation (IA) with standard nucleon weak formfactors, and the final scattering state was decomposed into Faddeev components with a rescattering term computed via an integral equation solved in momentum space with partial wave decomposition (total NN angular momenta up to jmax = 2). Decay rates were obtained by integrating over the unobserved neutrino energy to compare with the experimental deuteron energy spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The inclusion of FSI increases the decay rates by a factor of approximately 200, nearly independent of deuteron and neutrino energies.
- The maximum neutrino energy is given by Eνmax = [(mμ + m³He)² − (3mn − |ED|)²] / [2(mμ + m³He)], where ED is the deuteron binding energy.
- The muon wave function factor includes α = 1/137.036 and the reduced mass m' = (mμ m³He)/(mμ + m³He).
- The pion mass used in the axial formfactor g2A is mπ = 138.13 MeV.
- The nucleon weak formfactors use standard dipole parameterizations: g1V with cutoff 0.71 GeV², g1A with cutoff 1.19 GeV² and coupling −1.262, and 2m g2V with cutoff 0.71 GeV² and value −3.7.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Phillips et al. (1970s) who used the Amado model with separable NN interactions and found large scattering effects, but whose results agreed with some inaccurate experiments while disagreeing with others. It directly addresses the discrepancy reported by Cummings et al. (1990s) and Kuhn et al. (1990s), whose plane-wave impulse approximation calculations underpredicted the measured deuteron production rate by a large factor. The results enable future analyses to use exact three-nucleon continuum states from realistic forces, and the authors note that mesonic exchange currents should be added in future work.
