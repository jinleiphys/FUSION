# On the near-threshold incoherent $ϕ$ photoproduction on the deuteron: any trace of a resonance?

> We study the near-threshold incoherent $ϕ$ photoproduction on the deuteron based on a model of $γN \to ϕN$, consisting of Pomeron, $(π, η)$ exchanges, and a $J^P = 3/2^-$ resonance, which describes the low energy $γp \to ϕp$ LEPS data well, including the peak in the forward differential cross section. The calculation is done up to double rescatterings, with the spin dependence of the elementary $γN \to ϕN$ amplitude retained throughout the calculation. The Fermi motion and final-state interactions (FSI) are all properly treated as prescribed by realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The couplings of the resonance to $γn$ and $ϕn$ channels are estimated with the help of a constituent quark model. The main features of the LEPS and CLAS data are described reasonably well except for some quantitative discrepancies at very low energies and low momentum transfers regions. It is found that contributions of Fermi motion, $pn$ FSI, and resonance are all indispensable in bridging the differences between the single-scattering results and the data. The off-shell rescattering is found to be important as it cancels out a large portion of the on-shell contribution. The discrepancies at low momentum transfer region might be related to the binning size of the data. No peak is found to be associated with the weak resonance as it gets smeared out by the Fermi motion and FSI with deuterium target. The problem at very low energy region hints at the possible contributions from other mechanisms and should be investigated in depth with the use of recent high statistics $γp \to ϕp$ data from CLAS.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a postulated $J^P = 3/2^-$ resonance, previously introduced to explain a peak in forward differential cross sections of $\gamma p \to \phi p$, leaves any observable trace in incoherent $\phi$ photoproduction on the deuteron. The authors find that no peak associated with this weak resonance survives, as it gets smeared out by Fermi motion and final-state interactions with the deuterium target, though the resonance amplitude still interferes constructively with nonresonant contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the $\gamma d \to \phi p n$ reaction using a multiple scattering scheme up to double rescatterings, with the elementary $\gamma N \to \phi N$ amplitude consisting of Pomeron exchange, $(\pi, \eta)$-meson exchanges, and a $J^P = 3/2^-$ resonance contribution. The spin dependence of the elementary amplitude is fully retained, Fermi motion is treated via convolution with the deuteron wave function from the Bonn potential including the $D$ state, and final-state interactions between the outgoing nucleons are evaluated using the realistic Nijmegen $NN$ potential. The resonance couplings to $\gamma n$ and $\phi n$ channels are estimated using a constituent quark model rather than fitted to deuteron data.

## Key numbers

- Resonance mass $M_{N^*} = 2.08$ GeV and width $\Gamma_{N^*} = 0.570$ GeV for both proton and neutron channels
- Coupling constants for the resonance: $g_{\gamma p N^*}^{(1)} = 0.0323$, $g_{\gamma p N^*}^{(2)} = 0.0420$ for proton; $g_{\gamma n N^*}^{(1)} = -0.0441$, $g_{\gamma n N^*}^{(2)} = -0.0193$ for neutron
- $\phi N$ coupling constants: $g_{\phi N N^*}^{(1)} = -20.94$, $g_{\phi N N^*}^{(2)} = -2.61$, $g_{\phi N N^*}^{(3)} = -3.36$ (identical for proton and neutron)
- Total cross sections used for estimating rescattering contributions: $\sigma_{\gamma N \to \phi N} \approx 0.3\ \mu$b, $\sigma_{NN \to NN} \approx 10^6\ \mu$b, $\sigma_{\phi N \to \phi N} \approx 11 \times 10^3\ \mu$b
- The ratio of double-scattering contributions scales as $\sigma(NN):\sigma(\phi N):\sigma(\pi N) = 1 : 1.1 \times 10^{-2} : 5 \times 10^{-4}$
- Differential cross sections are presented at eight energy bins from $1.57 < E_\gamma < 1.67$ GeV up to $2.27 < E_\gamma < 2.37$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model of $\gamma p \to \phi p$ (Kiswandhi et al., 2010, 2012) to the deuteron target, where a $J^P = 3/2^-$ resonance was postulated to explain the nonmonotonic behavior in forward differential cross sections observed by LEPS (2005) and confirmed by CLAS (Dey, 2014; Seraydaryan, 2014). It improves upon the earlier analysis by Sekihara (2012) by including the spin structure of the elementary amplitude, $(\pi, \eta)$ exchanges, and realistic Nijmegen $NN$ interactions for final-state interactions. The results enable future comparisons with high-statistics CLAS data on $\gamma p \to \phi p$ to investigate the very low energy discrepancies, which hint at contributions from other mechanisms beyond the present model.
