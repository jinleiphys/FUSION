# Study of Chemical Equilibration of a Baryon Rich QGP

> Parton equilibration studies for thermally equilibrated but chemically non-equilibrated quark-gluon-plasma (QGP), likely to be formed at the relativistic colliders at BNL and CERN is presented.Parton equilibration is studied enforcing baryon number conservation. Process like quark-flavor interchanging is also taken into consideration.The degree of equilibration is studied comparatively for the various reactions/ constraints that are being considered.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical framework for studying chemical equilibration of a baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma (QGP) that is thermally equilibrated but chemically non-equilibrated, incorporating baryon number conservation and quark-flavor changing processes; however, the author acknowledges that to make the system of equations solvable, an approximation is introduced that expands the exponential terms in the baryon number conservation coefficients, which strictly speaking means baryon number conservation is not exact.

## Method

The author derives coupled evolution equations for parton number densities (gluons, massless light quarks and antiquarks, and massive strange quarks) using Juttner distribution functions for a thermally equilibrated but chemically non-equilibrated QGP with nonzero baryon chemical potential, then later approximates these with modified Fermi-Dirac (MFD) type distributions. The equations incorporate baryon number conservation, gluon multiplication, quark-antiquark pair production, and quark flavor changing processes, with the chemical potential time evolution determined from baryon number conservation and the temperature evolution from Bjorken's energy-momentum conservation equation.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass is taken as approximately 150 MeV.
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, the antiproton-to-proton ratio at mid-rapidity hovers between 0.6 and 0.8 (STAR collaboration).
- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the antiproton-to-proton ratio is a maximum of around 0.8 at zero rapidity (BRAHMS collaboration).
- The Debye screening mass for a multicomponent chemically non-equilibrated parton plasma is given by m_D^2 = (2g^2/pi^2) integral dk k [3f_g + (1/2) sum_{i=u,d,s} (f_i + f_ibar)].
- The inverse mean free path for gluons is lambda_f^{-1} = (9 n_g pi alpha_s^2) / (2 m_D^2 (1 + (2/9)(m_D^2/T^2))).

## Context

This work extends earlier chemical equilibration studies by a group from BARC, Mumbai (2000-2001) that included a chemical potential but did not enforce baryon number conservation and did not distinguish between quarks and antiquarks. It follows the more complete study by He et al. (2004) that included baryon number conservation, but differs by explicitly introducing the strange quark mass, treating quarks and antiquarks separately throughout, and including full phase space calculations for both quark-antiquark pair production and quark flavor changing processes. The framework enables future numerical studies of parton fugacity evolution in baryon-rich QGP at RHIC and LHC energies.
