# Power counting in baryon chiral perturbation theory including vector mesons

> It is demonstrated that using a suitable renormalization condition one obtains a consistent power counting in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory including vector mesons as explicit degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

Using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, a consistent power counting can be obtained in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory even when vector mesons are included as explicit degrees of freedom, provided they appear only as internal lines in diagrams with soft external pions and nucleons.

## Method

The authors apply the EOMS renormalization scheme to three characteristic one-loop diagrams: the nucleon self energy with a vector-meson loop, a vertex diagram involving a pion, nucleon, and rho meson, and the pion self energy. For each diagram, they first apply the modified minimal subtraction scheme of chiral perturbation theory (MS̃), then perform additional finite subtractions by expanding coefficients and integrals in powers of small quantities (p²-m², p̸-m, M², q²) and removing all terms of lower chiral order than the power-counting prediction. The counterterms for these subtractions are generated by expanding bare Lagrangian quantities in terms of renormalized ones.

## Key numbers

- The unrenormalized nucleon self energy diagram is of order Q⁰, violating the power-counting rules; after EOMS renormalization it starts as (p²-m²)³, consistent with power counting as n→4.
- The vertex diagram is predicted by power counting to be of order Q³; after renormalization the difference between the full expression and the subtracted terms is of order Q³ as n→4.
- The pion self energy diagram is renormalized to order Q⁴, with the non-analytic term proportional to (M²/(8π²)) ln(M/M_ρ) cancelling exactly between contributions from different integrals.
- The authors note that ordinary baryon chiral perturbation theory without vector mesons does not show satisfactory agreement with data beyond Q² ≈ 0.1 GeV² for nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Context

This work extends the EOMS renormalization approach of Fuchs (2003) and Gegelia (1999) to include vector mesons, which had previously been identified as an open problem in baryon chiral perturbation theory (Meissner, 2001). The method resolves the power-counting violation that occurs when vector mesons are included as explicit degrees of freedom in the manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation. The next step is to apply full calculations to physical processes such as nucleon electromagnetic form factors, where one-loop calculations in ordinary baryon chiral perturbation theory (Fuchs, 2003; Kubis, 2001) show limited agreement with data.
