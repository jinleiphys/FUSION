# Equation of state of nuclear matter in the first order phase transition

> We investigate the properties of nuclear matter at the first-order phase transitions (FOPT) such as liquid-gas phase transition, kaon condensation, and hadron-quark phase transition. As a general feature of the FOPT of matter consisting of many species of charged particles, there appears a mixed phases with regular structures called "pasta" due to the balance of the Coulomb repulsion and the surface tension between two phases. The equation of state (EOS) of mixed phase is different from those obtained by a bulk application of Gibbs conditions or by the Maxwell construction due to the effects of the non-uniform structure. We show that the charge screening and strong surface tension make the EOS close to that of the Maxwell construction.

## Key claim

The equation of state (EOS) of mixed phase matter at first-order phase transitions in nuclear matter is modified by non-uniform "pasta" structures, and the charge screening effect combined with strong surface tension makes the EOS approach that of the Maxwell construction, rather than the bulk Gibbs conditions.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean field (RMF) model to describe nuclear matter, solving the coupled equations of motion for meson fields and the Coulomb potential self-consistently within Wigner-Seitz cells of spherical (3D), cylindrical (2D), and slab (1D) geometry. They calculated density profiles and binding energies for matter with fixed proton fractions of 0.5 and 0.1, and for beta-equilibrium matter, at zero and finite temperatures, and extended the framework to hadron-quark mixed phase at high density.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density is given as $\rho_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The energy per baryon in non-uniform structures is lowered by up to about 15 MeV per nucleon compared to uniform matter for fixed proton fraction cases.
- For symmetric matter ($Y_p=0.5$) at zero temperature, the most favorable configuration changes from droplet to rod, slab, tube, bubble, and finally uniform matter with increasing density.
- At finite temperature, no pasta structure is obtained at $T=20$ MeV, placing the critical temperature between 10 and 20 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter.
- The saturation properties used in the RMF model include an incompressibility $K(\rho_0)=240$ MeV, effective nucleon mass $m_N^*(\rho_0)=0.78 m_N$, and isospin-asymmetry coefficient $a_{\rm sym}=32.5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of "nuclear pasta" structures in the liquid-gas phase transition, first discussed by Ravenhall (1983), to also cover kaon condensation and hadron-quark phase transitions in compact stars. The self-consistent treatment of Coulomb screening and surface tension within the RMF framework goes beyond previous calculations that used simpler approximations, and the results show that the EOS of mixed phase matter depends critically on the balance between these finite-size effects, with implications for the structure and composition of neutron star crusts and cores.
