# Nuclear decay parameter oscillations as possible signal of quantum-mechanical nonlinearity and emergent gravity

> Several experimental groups reported the evidence of multiple periodic modulations of nuclear decay constants which amplitudes are of the order .1% and periods of one year, 24 hours or about one month. We argue that such deviations from radioactive decay law can be described in nonlinear quantum mechanics framework, in which decay process obeys to nonlinear Shroedinger equation with Doebner-Goldin terms. Proposed corrections to Hamiltonian of quantum system interaction with gravitation field correspond to some emergent gravity theories, in particular, bilocal gravity model. Decay parameter variations under influence of Sun gravity, calculated in our model, agree well with experimental results for alpha-decay life-time oscillations of Polonium isotopes

# Nuclear decay parameter oscillations as possible signal of quantum-mechanical nonlinearity and emergent gravity

## Key claim

Periodic modulations of nuclear decay constants (amplitudes ~0.1%, periods of one year, 24 hours, or one month) can be described by nonlinear quantum mechanics with Doebner-Goldin terms, where the nonlinear parameter depends on the time derivative of the Sun's gravitational potential; the model reproduces observed alpha-decay lifetime oscillations of Polonium isotopes, but only if one assumes that the nonlinear coupling constant is imaginary or real and that the gravity interaction is bilocal rather than local.

## Method

The author modifies the standard Gamow theory of alpha-decay by replacing the linear Schroedinger equation with a nonlinear Schroedinger equation containing Doebner-Goldin (DG) nonlinear terms of the form F = hbar^2 lambda (nabla^2 + |nabla psi|^2/|psi|^2). The nonlinear parameter lambda is taken to depend on the time derivative of the Sun's gravitational potential U at the nucleus position, lambda = g * partial_t U. Using WKB approximation for the nonlinear Hamiltonian, the transmission coefficient D for alpha-particle tunneling through the nuclear potential barrier is derived, yielding an exponential dependence on the nonlinear parameter gamma = m lambda. The resulting lifetime oscillations are compared to experimental data for Po-214 alpha-decay.

## Key numbers

- Observed annual oscillation amplitude for Po-214 lifetime: A_a = 9.8 * 10^(-4) (relative to total decay rate), with phase phi_a = 174 days, period 365 days
- Observed daily oscillation amplitude for Po-214 lifetime: A_d = 8.3 * 10^(-4), with phase phi_d = 12 hours, period 24 hours
- Po-214 mean lifetime: tau_0 = 16.4 * 10^(-6) seconds
- Model estimate of the tunneling exponent for Po-214: phi ≈ 60
- Derived nonlinear coupling constant from annual oscillation fit: g = 0.35 * 10^(-8) sec^3/(m^2 MeV)
- Predicted daily amplitude from model using this g: A_d = 5.5 * 10^(-4) (compared to experimental 8.3 * 10^(-4))
- Annual time derivative of Sun potential amplitude: K^a = 1.5 m^2/sec, with phase phi_u = 185 days
- Daily time derivative of Sun potential amplitude: K^e = 0.9 m^2/sec, with phase phi_e = 12 hours
- Reported annual oscillation amplitude for Si-32 beta-decay: 6 * 10^(-4) relative to total decay rate
- Reported annual oscillation amplitude for Pu-238 alpha-decay: of order 10^(-3)
- Reported annual and daily oscillation amplitude for Po-214 and Po-213: of order 9 * 10^(-4)
- Earth orbit radius variation: maximal/minimal difference about 3 * 10^(-2)

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of periodic nuclear decay modulations reported by several groups (Alburger 1986 for Si-32; Falkenberg 2001; Ellis 2000 for Pu-238; Alimonti 2008 for Po-214 and Po-213) and proposes a theoretical mechanism based on nonlinear quantum mechanics (Doebner and Goldin 1992, 1996) rather than earlier unconvincing explanations such as anomalous neutrino interactions or seasonal variations of fundamental constants. The model connects to emergent gravity theories, particularly bilocal gravity models (Diaz 2015), and predicts that similar temporal variations under solar gravity influence could be observable in other metastable systems involving tunneling, including chemical reactions and possibly biological processes such as seedling bioluminescence (Barlow 2010) and tree stem diameter variations (Morris 2012). The approach enables experimental tests of quantum nonlinearity through precision decay measurements, though the author notes that some beta-decay experiments (Kossert 2006; Bellotti 2009) exclude modulations as large as those reported.
