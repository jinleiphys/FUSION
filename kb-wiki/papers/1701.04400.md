# Quark-Nuclear Hybrid EoS with Excluded Volume Effects

> A two-phase description of the quark-nuclear matter hybrid equation of state that takes into account the effect of excluded volume in both the hadronic and the quark matter phases is introduced. The nuclear phase manifests a reduction of the available volume as density increases, leading to a stiffening of the matter. The quark matter phase displays a reduction of the effective string tension in the confining density functional from available volume contributions. The nuclear equation of state is based upon the relativistic density functional model DD2 with excluded volume. The quark matter is based upon a mean-field modification to the free fermi gas and will be discussed in greater detail. The interactions are decomposed into mean scalar and vector components. The scalar interaction is motivated by a string potential between quarks, whereas the vector interaction potential is motivated by higher order interactions of quarks leading to an increased stiffening at high densities. As an application, we consider matter under compact star constraints of electric neutrality and $β$-equilibrium. We obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars that form a third family, disconnected from the purely hadronic star branch, and fulfill the $2M_\odot$ constraint.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a two-phase hybrid equation of state for quark-nuclear matter that incorporates excluded volume effects in both the hadronic and quark phases, and claims that this model produces mass-radius relations for hybrid stars that form a third family disconnected from the purely hadronic branch while still satisfying the 2 solar mass constraint, but only for specific parameter choices that ensure a sufficiently large latent heat at the phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state by combining a relativistic density functional model for nuclear matter (DD2 with excluded volume) with a quasiparticle model for quark matter derived from a relativistic density-functional approach, then connecting the two phases via a Maxwell construction under conditions of electric neutrality and beta-equilibrium. The quark matter model incorporates a scalar confining interaction motivated by a string-flip model with a density-dependent string tension that is reduced by an available volume fraction factor, plus vector interactions including four-quark and eight-quark repulsive terms. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations for compact star configurations.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum string tension is set to \(D_0^{1/2} = 240\) MeV
- The parameter \(c\) in the eight-quark interaction term is fixed at \(0.036\) fm\(^6\) to maintain causality
- The hadronic excluded volume parameter \(v\) is varied, with values up to \(v = 8.0\) fm\(^3\) (labeled DD2p80)
- The quark matter parameters include \(a = -2.0\) MeV fm\(^3\) for the four-quark coupling and \(b = 2.0\) MeV fm\(^9\) for the eight-quark coupling in the reference parameterization
- The available volume fraction parameter \(\alpha\) is set to \(0.2\) fm\(^6\) in the reference parameterization
- The model satisfies the \(2M_\odot\) constraint from the mass measurement of PSR J0348+0432 at \(M = 2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\)

## Context

This work extends the string-flip model approach of Horowitz (1985) and Ropke (1986) by incorporating a density-dependent reduction of the effective string tension through an available volume fraction, generalizing earlier treatments. It builds on the high-mass twin concept discussed by Gerlach (1968), Kampfer (1981), and more recently by Benic (2014), where a strong first-order phase transition with a large latent heat produces a disconnected third family of hybrid stars. The hadronic DD2 model with excluded volume follows the approach of Typel (2016), and the eight-quark vector interactions follow the scheme introduced by Benic (2014) to provide sufficient stiffening at high densities to satisfy the 2 solar mass constraint. The results enable predictions for radius measurements of high-mass pulsars by the NICER mission, which could observationally confirm the existence of high-mass twin stars.
