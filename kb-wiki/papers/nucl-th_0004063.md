# Goldberger-Treiman Relation and g_piNN from the Three Quark BS/Faddeev Approach in the NJL Model

> A systematic evaluation method of matrix elements of quantum one-body operators between nucleon states in the three-quark Bethe-Salpeter(3qBS)/Faddeev approach in the NJL model is reviewed. We do not confine ourselves to a particular truncation scheme of the 3qBS kernel. One of our main aims is to derive a general condition to be imposed on a given BS kernel in order that the PCAC relation is satisfied correctly. We apply this condition to some particular 3qBS kernels. We numerically calculate g_piNN in the 3qBS/Faddeev approach to estimate the violation of the Goldberger-Treiman/PCAC relation due to the UV-regularization scheme. We finally consider the non-vanishing current quark mass effects on the Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general condition (a chiral Ward identity for the three-quark Bethe-Salpeter kernel) that must be imposed on a given BS kernel in order for the PCAC relation to be satisfied correctly in the NJL model, and shows that even when the truncation scheme is consistent with PCAC, the UV regularization scheme typically spoils the relation, causing violations of the Goldberger-Treiman relation of up to 4%.

## Method

The author introduces space-time dependent external fields (vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar) coupled to the associated one-body operators, then applies functional derivatives with respect to these external fields to the three-quark Bethe-Salpeter equation in the presence of the fields. By analyzing the behavior of the BS kernel under local chiral gauge transformations, a sufficient condition and an infinitesimal chiral Ward identity for the kernel are derived, which guarantee the PCAC relation for nucleon matrix elements. The method is applied to evaluate the nucleon matrix element of the axial current and the pseudoscalar density in the three-quark BS/Faddeev framework, and numerical calculations of \(g_{\pi NN}\) are performed using the Euclidean sharp cut-off regularization scheme.

## Key numbers

* The extracted value of \(g_{\pi NN}\) in the chiral limit is 13.2.
* The violation of the Goldberger-Treiman relation due to the Euclidean sharp cut-off UV regularization scheme is up to 4%.
* The off-shell \(g_{\pi NN}\) (denoted \(\tilde g_{\pi NN}\)) obtained with the single-pole dominance approximation for the form factor \(h_A(q^2)\) is 13.5.
* The axial coupling \(g_A\) is found to be 6% larger than the experimental value.
* The PCAC violation due to the cutoff scheme off the chiral limit is again up to 4%.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic three-quark Bethe-Salpeter/Faddeev approach to baryons in the NJL model, which had previously been limited to the ladder truncation scheme (Reinhardt, Ishii, Asami). The paper provides a systematic method to evaluate matrix elements of one-body operators between nucleon states and a criterion for chiral consistency of BS kernels, enabling future studies of non-trivial \(q\bar{q}\) effects beyond the ladder approximation in a Lorentz covariant manner. The results also show that the mean-field approximation for the vacuum combined with the Euclidean sharp cut-off leads to deviations from the Goldberger-Treiman relation in the opposite direction than expected from experiment, indicating the need to either improve the gap equation beyond mean field or evaluate the on-shell \(g_{\pi NN}\).
