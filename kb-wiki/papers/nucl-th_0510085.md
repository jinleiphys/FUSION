# Comparison between two methods of solution of coupled equations for low-energy scattering

> Cross sections from low-energy neutron-nucleus scattering have been evaluated using a coupled channel theory of scattering. Both a coordinate-space and a momentum-space formalism of that coupled-channel theory are considered.A simple rotational model of the channel interaction potentials is used to find results using two relevant codes, ECIS97 and MCAS, so that they may be compared. The very same model is then used in the MCAS approach to quantify the changes that occur when allowance is made for effects of the Pauli principle.

## Key claim

The coordinate-space code ECIS97 and the momentum-space code MCAS produce essentially equivalent cross sections for low-energy neutron-12C scattering when the same simple rotational model is used, but the MCAS approach reveals that ignoring the Pauli principle introduces numerous spurious states that drastically alter the resonance structure and underlying compound-nucleus spectrum.

## Method

The authors compared coupled-channel calculations for neutron-12C scattering using ECIS97 (coordinate-space) and MCAS (momentum-space with Sturmian expansions), employing a simple rotational model with a fixed Woods-Saxon potential and quadrupole deformation beta2 = -0.52. They considered three target states (0+ ground, 2+ at 4.43 MeV, and 0+2 at 7.67 MeV) and computed cross sections for laboratory energies from 0.1 MeV to 4.0 MeV. The MCAS code was then used with an orthogonalizing pseudo-potential (OPP) method to enforce the Pauli principle by ensuring the Sturmian expansion basis is orthogonal to all occupied single-particle states in the target.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter beta2 = -0.52 used for the rotational model of the neutron-12C system.
- The spherical Woods-Saxon potential has depth 49.92 MeV, radius parameter 2.885 fm, and diffuseness 0.63 fm.
- ECIS97 calculations reveal three resonances near 0.7 MeV, 2.1 MeV, and 3.3 MeV in the laboratory energy range 0.1-4.0 MeV.
- In the zero-deformation limit, the MCAS calculation without Pauli correction gives a spurious (1/2)- state at -5.29 MeV (group 6 in Table Zbt) that is removed by the OPP method, while the allowed (1/2)- state shifts from 1.74 MeV to become the (1/2)-2 state.
- The (5/2)+2 state at 2.08 MeV in the uncorrected calculation becomes the (5/2)+1 state after Pauli correction.

## Context

This work extends the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) theory developed by Amos et al. (2003) and the Pauli-correction method by Canton et al. (2005) by directly comparing with the widely-used ECIS97 code (Raynal, 1988, 1994). The results confirm earlier findings by M. A. G. (1969) and G. R. (1996) that collective model prescriptions violate the Pauli principle, and demonstrate that the MCAS approach can eliminate the resulting spurious states. This enables future work to develop physically meaningful interactions for low-energy neutron scattering that respect the Pauli principle, which is not currently possible with standard coordinate-space codes like ECIS97, CHUCK, GNASH, EMPIRE-II, and TALYS.
