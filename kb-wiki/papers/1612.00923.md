# The nuclear contacts and short range correlations in nuclei

> Atomic nuclei are complex strongly interacting systems and their exact theoretical description is a long-standing challenge. An approximate description of nuclei can be achieved by separating its short and long range structure. This separation of scales stands at the heart of the nuclear shell model and effective field theories that describe the long-range structure of the nucleus using a mean- field approximation. We present here an effective description of the complementary short-range structure using contact terms and stylized two-body asymptotic wave functions. The possibility to extract the nuclear contacts from experimental data is presented. Regions in the two-body momentum distribution dominated by high-momentum, close-proximity, nucleon pairs are identified and compared to experimental data. The amount of short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs is determined and compared to measurements. Non-combinatorial isospin symmetry for SRC pairs is identified. The obtained one-body momentum distributions indicate dominance of SRC pairs above the nuclear Fermi-momentum.

## Key claim

The generalized nuclear contact formalism can describe short-range correlations (SRCs) in nuclei even in the momentum range \(k_F < k < 3k_F\), not only for very high momentum \(k > 3k_F \approx 4\) fm\(^{-1}\) as previously thought, provided one selects pairs with both high relative momentum and low center-of-mass momentum to minimize mean-field contamination.

## Method

The authors extracted nuclear contact values for nuclei up to \(^{40}\)Ca using three independent methods: (1) fitting the factorized two-body momentum-space expressions of the contact formalism to variational Monte Carlo (VMC) two-body momentum densities in the range 4–4.8 fm\(^{-1}\); (2) fitting the same expressions to VMC coordinate-space two-body densities in the range 0.25–1 fm; and (3) using experimental data via the measured \(pp\)-to-\(pn\) SRC pair ratio and the high-momentum scaling factor \(a_2(A/d)\) from inclusive electron scattering. The asymptotic two-body channel wave functions were taken from the zero-energy solution of the AV18 potential, and the contacts were extracted for the deuteron channel (\(\ell=0,2\), \(s=1\), \(j=1\)) and the singlet \(s\)-wave channel (\(\ell=s=j=0\)) for \(pn\), \(pp\), and \(nn\) pairs.

## Key numbers

- For \(^4\)He, the momentum-space contacts (divided by \(A/2\) and multiplied by 100 to give percent of nucleons above \(k_F\)) are: \(C_{pn}^{s=1} = 12.3 \pm 0.1\), \(C_{pn}^{s=0} = 0.69 \pm 0.03\), and \(C_{pp}^{s=0} = C_{nn}^{s=0} = 0.65 \pm 0.03\); experimental extraction gives \(C_{pn}^{s=1} = 14.9 \pm 0.7\) and combined singlet contacts \(0.8 \pm 0.2\).
- For \(^{12}\)C, the momentum-space contacts are: \(C_{pn}^{s=1} = 16.8 \pm 0.8\), \(C_{pn}^{s=0} = 1.4 \pm 0.2\), and \(C_{pp}^{s=0} = C_{nn}^{s=0} = 1.3 \pm 0.2\); experimental extraction gives \(C_{pn}^{s=1} = 18 \pm 2\) and combined singlet contacts \(1.5 \pm 0.5\).
- The contact-formalism prediction reproduces the VMC one-body momentum distribution to within 10%–20% accuracy from \(k_F \sim 255\) MeV/c up to 5 fm\(^{-1}\), where the momentum density varies over 3 orders of magnitude.
- SRC pairs account for 20%–25% of the nucleons in the nucleus and practically all nucleons with momentum above the Fermi momentum \(k_F \sim 255\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the contact theory originally developed for dilute cold atomic gases (Tan 2008; Braaten 2012) to nuclear systems, where the interaction range (0.5–1.5 fm), average interparticle distance (2.5 fm), and scattering lengths (-20 fm for spin-singlet, 5 fm for spin-triplet) do not satisfy strict scale separation. It resolves a tension with prior work (Weiss 2015) that found the contact formalism only valid for \(k > 3k_F\), by demonstrating that selecting pairs with low center-of-mass momentum (\(K < 1-1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\)) extends applicability down to \(k_F\). The results agree with VMC calculations (Wiringa 2014) and experimental data from electron-induced two-nucleon knockout (Korover 2014; Subedi 2008), and enable systematic decomposition of SRC pairs by spin-isospin channel for future studies of neutron-star structure and neutrino-nucleus interactions.
