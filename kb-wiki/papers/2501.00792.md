# First-order phase transition in dynamical 3-flavor QCD at imaginary isospin

> We revisit QCD with three mass-degenerate quark flavors at an imaginary isospin chemical potential set to 4 pi T/3. This choice corresponds to a special point in the parameter space, where the theory possesses an exact Z(3) center symmetry. Through a finite-size scaling analysis, we demonstrate that in this case the finite temperature QCD transition is of first order and entails singular behavior both in the Polyakov loop and in the quark condensate. Our results are based on simulations with stout-smeared staggered quarks and a dedicated multi-histogram analysis.

# First-order phase transition in dynamical 3-flavor QCD at imaginary isospin

## Key claim
The finite-temperature QCD transition for three mass-degenerate quark flavors at an imaginary isospin chemical potential of \(4\pi T/3\) is of first order, as demonstrated through finite-size scaling analysis of the rotated Polyakov loop moments, with the transition persisting for any nonzero quark mass value.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using stout-smeared staggered quarks with the tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on \(N_s^3 \times 6\) lattices with volumes ranging from \(16^3\) to \(32^3\). They set the three degenerate quark masses along a line of constant physics and applied a multi-histogram reweighting method that includes leading-order reweighting in the quark mass to interpolate observables between simulation points. The rotated Polyakov loop and its higher central moments (variance, skewness, kurtosis) were analyzed as functions of the inverse gauge coupling \(\beta\) to characterize the transition order.

## Key numbers
- Critical inverse gauge coupling identified as \(\beta_c \approx 3.835\) from the skewness crossing zero
- For physical strange quark mass \(m = m_s^{\rm phys}\): \(T_c w_0 = 0.248(1)\)
- For physical light quark mass \(m = m_{ud}^{\rm phys}\): \(T_c w_0 = 0.239(1)\)
- For three times physical strange quark mass \(m = 3 \cdot m_s^{\rm phys}\): \(T_c w_0 = 0.2505(5)\)
- Pure gauge theory reference value: \(T_c w_0 = 0.2507(2)\) from Borsanyi (2021)
- Physical \(2+1\)-flavor QCD crossover reference: \(T_c w_0 \approx 0.14\) from BMW (2012)
- The susceptibility peak scales as \(\chi_{\bar P} \propto V\), consistent with a first-order transition

## Context
This work extends the Roberge-Weiss phase structure (Roberge and Weiss, 1986) to three-flavor QCD at imaginary isospin chemical potential, building on earlier lattice studies by Iritani (2015) and Misumi (2015) and effective model analyses by Kouno (2015) and Li (2018). The exact Z(3) center symmetry at this chemical potential, previously discussed by Kouno (2012) and Cherman (2017), enables a direct comparison to the first-order deconfinement transition in pure SU(3) gauge theory. The results connect the first-order transition at imaginary isospin to the first-order region in the standard Columbia plot at high quark masses and zero chemical potential, and enable future mapping of the full three-dimensional phase diagram in temperature, isospin chemical potential, and quark mass space.
