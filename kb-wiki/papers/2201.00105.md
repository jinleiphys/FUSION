# Topological confinement in Skyrme holography

> We study phase transitions in five-dimensional Einstein Gravity with a negative cosmological constant, coupled to a Skyrme matter field. These transitions are topological generalizations of the Hawking-Page transition between thermal Anti de Sitter (AdS) spacetime and an AdS black hole. Phases are characterized by a topological number associated with the Skyrme field configuration. Depending on that topological number and on the Skyrme coupling strength, there occur transitions between those phases at two, one, or no value(s) of the temperature. Through the holographic (AdS/CFT) correspondence, these solutions are dual to topologically non-trivial states in a conformal field theory (CFT) with an SU(2)-symmetry, which support either confined or deconfined (quasi-)particles at strong coupling. We compare to similar known phase transitions, and discuss potential applications to confinement in topological phases of condensed matter and the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Hawking-Page transition between thermal AdS and an AdS black hole can be generalized to include topological Skyrme field configurations, resulting in zero, one, or two phase transitions depending on the Skyrme coupling strength and the topological winding number, rather than the single transition found in the standard case.

## Method

The authors construct an analytic solution to the five-dimensional Einstein-Skyrme equations with a negative cosmological constant, using a Skyrme field ansatz that maps to a topologically non-trivial SU(2) gauge field configuration. They compute the free energy difference between the topological black hole and thermal AdS via the renormalized on-shell action, and analyze the temperature dependence of this free energy to identify phase transitions. The heat capacity and charge susceptibility are evaluated to determine thermodynamic stability.

## Key numbers

- The minimal horizon radius in the limit of infinitely strong Skyrme coupling (e → 0) is r_h = exp(-1) ≈ 0.368 (in units where L=1)
- The standard Hawking-Page critical temperature is T_c = 3/(2πL), recovered in the limit e → ∞ for winding number n=1
- For Skyrme coupling e = √10, the free energy difference vanishes at two horizon radii: r_h = 0.113 and r_h = 1.22 (in units where κ=1)
- The entropy is quantized as S = (2π² n)/(4G₅) r_h³, where n is the integer winding number
- The heat capacity diverges at a critical horizon radius r_h* = (1/2)[L² ± (1/e)√(L²(e²L²+12))]^(1/2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic solution of the Einstein-Skyrme system (Cartwright 2020) by providing a full thermodynamic analysis of the phase transitions. The model generalizes the standard Hawking-Page transition (Hawking 1982) and its holographic interpretation as a confinement/deconfinement transition in N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory (Witten 1998). The results enable future applications to topological phases in condensed matter systems, such as spin chains (Haldane 2016) and spin liquids (PhysRevB.65.165113), as well as to topological effects in heavy ion collisions and the quark-gluon plasma.
