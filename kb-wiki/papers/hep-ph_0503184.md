# The phase diagram of neutral quark matter: Self-consistent treatment of quark masses

> We study the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter within the framework of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. In the analysis, dynamically generated quark masses are taken into account self-consistently. The phase diagram in the plane of temperature and quark chemical potential is presented. The results for two qualitatively different regimes, intermediate and strong diquark coupling strength, are presented. It is shown that the role of gapless phases diminishes with increasing diquark coupling strength.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of neutral three-flavor quark matter is studied within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with dynamically generated quark masses treated self-consistently, showing that the role of gapless phases diminishes with increasing diquark coupling strength, but only for the specific parameter choices of intermediate coupling (G_D = 3/4 G_S) and strong coupling (G_D = G_S).

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor NJL model with local interactions including a scalar diquark channel and the 't Hooft determinant term, solving the mean-field gap equations for chiral condensates (sigma_u, sigma_d, sigma_s) and diquark condensates (Delta_1, Delta_2, Delta_3) together with three charge neutrality conditions (electric, color 3, color 8) at each temperature T and quark chemical potential mu. The ground state is determined by comparing pressures of eight candidate phases (normal, 2SC, 2SC_us, 2SC_ds, uSC, dSC, sSC, and CFL) and then refined by examining quasiparticle spectra for gapless modes.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum constituent quark masses: M_u = M_d = 367.7 MeV, M_s = 549.5 MeV (from parameter set reproducing m_pi = 135.0 MeV, m_K = 497.7 MeV, m_eta' = 957.8 MeV, f_pi = 92.4 MeV)
* For intermediate coupling (G_D = 3/4 G_S) at T=0: first-order chiral transition occurs in mu window 336 MeV to 368 MeV; critical endpoint at (T_cr, mu_cr) ≈ (56, 336) MeV
* For intermediate coupling at T=0: NQ to gCFL transition at mu ≈ 443 MeV; gCFL to CFL transition at mu ≈ 457 MeV
* For intermediate coupling at T=0: zero-temperature gap at mu = 500 MeV is Delta_0^(500) ≈ 76 MeV
* For strong coupling (G_D = G_S) at T=0: zero-temperature gap at mu = 500 MeV is Delta_0^(500) ≈ 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier phase diagram studies of neutral three-flavor quark matter by Ruester et al. (2004) and others (phase-d, phase-d1, phase-d2) that treated quark masses as free parameters or used a shift approximation for the strange quark mass, by instead solving for dynamically generated masses self-consistently. The results differ qualitatively from those of Shovkovy et al. (2003) (SRP) at zero temperature, where the present work finds a gCFL phase rather than a 2SC phase in the mu range 443-457 MeV. The self-consistent treatment enables more reliable predictions for the competition between normal and color-superconducting phases in compact star interiors, though the authors note that fluctuation effects not included in the mean-field approximation could affect the location of the critical endpoint.
