# Role of the isovector spin-orbit potential in mitigating the CREX-PREX dilemma

> Pioneering electroweak measurements of the neutron skin thickness in lead-208 and calcium-48 are challenging our understanding of nuclear dynamics. Many theoretical models suggest that the slope of the symmetry energy controls the development of a neutron skin in neutron-rich nuclei. This led to the expectation that if lead-208 exhibits a large neutron skin, calcium-48 should as well. Given that the PREX collaboration reported a relatively thick neutron skin in lead, we anticipated that calcium would also have a significant neutron skin. Instead, the CREX collaboration reported a thin neutron skin in calcium. Although many suggestions have been proposed, the ``CREX-PREX dilemma" remains unsolved. Recently, an intriguing scenario has emerged, suggesting that an enhanced isovector spin-orbit interaction could simultaneously account for both results. Following this approach, we performed relativistic mean-field calculations with an increased isovector spin-orbit potential. Our findings indicate that while this modification significantly affects the structure of calcium-48, it has only a marginal impact on lead-208, thereby bringing the results into better agreement with experiment. However, the strong enhancement required to mitigate the CREX-PREX dilemma destroys the agreement with a successful spin-orbit phenomenology, primarily by modifying the well-known ordering of spin-orbit partners.

## Key claim

Enhancing the isovector component of the spin-orbit potential in relativistic mean-field calculations can bring the predicted neutron skin thicknesses of both lead-208 and calcium-48 into better agreement with the PREX and CREX experimental results, but the large enhancement required destroys the successful shell-model phenomenology by altering the ordering of spin-orbit partners and eliminating magic numbers.

## Method

The authors start from the Dirac equation with self-consistent scalar, vector, and tensor mean fields and derive an equivalent Schrodinger-like equation without any approximation, from which the spin-orbit potential is explicitly identified. They then artificially enhance the isovector component of the spin-orbit potential by a multiplicative factor beta, applied to the FSUGold2 and FSUGold2(L90) relativistic mean-field models, and compute the resulting neutron skin thicknesses and single-particle spectra for both lead-208 and calcium-48.

## Key numbers

- PREX neutron skin thickness of lead-208: R_skin^208 = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm
- CREX neutron skin thickness of calcium-48: R_skin^48 = (0.121 ± 0.035) fm
- Inferred slope of symmetry energy from PREX: L = (106 ± 37) MeV
- Predicted neutron skin thickness of calcium-48 before CREX measurement: R_skin^48 = (0.229 ± 0.035) fm
- Slope of symmetry energy for FSUGold2 model: L = (112.8 ± 16.1) MeV
- Enhancement factors beta explored: 1 (original), -50, -100, -150, -200, -250, -300
- For beta = -300, both FSUGold2 and FSUGold2(L90) predictions fall within the 67% confidence ellipse of the joint PREX-CREX data

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Yue (2024) and Zhao (2024) that an enhanced isovector spin-orbit interaction could simultaneously account for both the PREX and CREX results, which had created a dilemma because most models predicted a strong correlation between the neutron skins of lead-208 and calcium-48. The study contradicts the expectation from earlier work by Piekarewicz (2021) that calcium-48 would have a large neutron skin if lead-208 did, and it builds on the DINO models introduced by Reed (2023) that improved the description but had objectionable features in ground-state densities. The findings enable future work on a full Bayesian calibration of a new energy density functional that could incorporate a milder enhancement of the isovector spin-orbit potential while preserving shell-model phenomenology.
