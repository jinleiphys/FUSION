# Excitation of multipolar transitions in nuclei by twisted photons

> The explicit expression for the probability of absorption of a twisted photon by an atomic nucleus has been obtained. It is shown that photoabsorption obeys the selection rule $j\geqslant|m_γ|$, where $j$ is the multipolarity of the nuclear transition, when the nuclei lie near the axis along which a twisted photon with a projection of the total angular momentum $m_γ$ propagates. In the long-wave limit, the main contribution to the probability of absorption of a twisted photon comes from the multipole transition with $j=|m_γ|$. The absorption coefficient for twisted photons in a target consisting of many nuclei has been found.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that twisted photons with a definite projection of total angular momentum $m_\gamma$ can selectively excite nuclear multipole transitions with multipolarity $j \geq |m_\gamma|$, and in the long-wave limit the dominant contribution comes from the transition with $j = |m_\gamma|$, provided the nucleus is positioned near the photon propagation axis and the impact parameter satisfies $k_0 b_\perp \ll 1$.

## Method

The authors derived the photoabsorption amplitude using first-order perturbation theory in the coupling constant $e$, expressing the electromagnetic field operator in terms of multipole potentials and representing the nuclear current matrix elements via irreducible tensor operators. They modeled the twisted photon as a Gaussian wave packet with definite total angular momentum projection $m_\gamma$ and helicity $\lambda_0$, and the nucleus center-of-mass as a Gaussian wave packet with impact parameter $\mathbf{b}_\perp$, then integrated over the final nuclear momentum and summed over spin projections using the Wigner-Eckart theorem to obtain the transition probability.

## Key numbers

- The selection rule $j \geq |m_\gamma|$ applies when the nucleus lies near the axis of the twisted photon propagation.
- For a twisted photon with energy $k_0 \approx 10$ keV, the conditions $b_\perp \ll 1$ nm and $\sigma \gg 1$ keV are required for the asymptotic approximation to hold.
- The ratio of the excitation probability for a $j$-th multipole transition by a twisted photon to that by a plane wave photon is $\left[d^j_{m_\gamma\lambda_0}(\theta_k^0)\right]^2 \leq 1$.
- For large target sizes ($k_0 w \gg 1$), the absorption probability reduces to the plane-wave result up to a factor of $1/(2\pi)^{1/2} k_\perp^0 w_{eff}$.

## Context

This work extends the theory of photoabsorption by nuclei beyond the standard plane-wave approximation (Ahiezer and Sitenko) to twisted (vortex) photons, which carry orbital angular momentum. It builds on prior developments in twisted photon sources, including inverse Compton scattering (Jentschura and Serbo, 2011) and channeling in crystals (Bogdanov, Kazinski, and Tukhfatullin, 2022), and on the formalism of Bessel photon states (Jáuregui and Hacyan, 2005). The results enable selective excitation of high-multipolarity nuclear transitions (e.g., giant resonances beyond dipole) that are normally suppressed in plane-wave photoabsorption, and provide the Bouguer law for twisted photon absorption in extended targets.
