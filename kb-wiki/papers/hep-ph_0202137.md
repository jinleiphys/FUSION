# Relativistic dynamics of Qqq systems

> The bound state of constituent quarks forming a $Qqq$ composite baryon is investigated in a QCD-inspired effective light-front model. The light-front Faddeev equations are derived and solved numerically. The masses of the spin 1/2 low-lying states of the nucleon, $Λ^0$, $Λ^+_c$ and $Λ^0_b$ are found and compared to the experimental data. The data is qualitatively described with a flavor independent effective interaction.

# Relativistic dynamics of Qqq systems

## Key claim

The flavor-independent contact interaction previously used to describe mesons in the QCD-inspired light-front model can be extended to baryons, yielding a qualitative description of the binding energies of the nucleon, Λ⁰, Λ⁺c, and Λ⁰b, provided the spin degree of freedom is averaged out and the contact interaction strength is tuned to the nucleon mass.

## Method

The authors derived and numerically solved coupled light-front Faddeev equations for a three-quark system (Qqq) with a pairwise zero-range (contact) interaction, starting from the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equations in ladder approximation and projecting onto the light-front hypersurface via integration over the k⁻ momentum. The diquark mass was fitted to the proton mass (0.938 GeV) using a constituent up-quark mass of 0.386 GeV, and then the binding energies of Λ⁰, Λ⁺c, and Λ⁰b were computed by varying the heavy quark mass (s, c, b) while keeping the bare interaction strength fixed.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses derived from vector meson masses: m_u = 0.384 GeV (from M_ρ/2), m_s = 0.508 GeV (from M_K* - M_ρ/2), m_c = 1.623 GeV (from M_D* - M_ρ/2), m_b = 4.941 GeV (from M_B* - M_ρ/2)
- Up-quark mass used in the baryon calculation: m_u = 0.386 GeV, with fitted diquark mass M_d = 0.695 GeV
- Proton mass: 0.938 GeV
- Saturation binding energy for the Qqq system above baryon mass 2.3 GeV: 0.077 GeV
- Estimated error of 20 MeV on constituent and current quark masses from the meson input values

## Context

This work extends the QCD-inspired light-front "uparrow-downarrow model" of Pauli and collaborators (2000, 2001), previously applied only to mesons, to the baryon sector by studying Qqq systems. The authors build on earlier three-quark light-front calculations by Tobias (1992) and Pacheco et al. (1995), which described the proton mass, charge radius, and electric form factor up to 2 (GeV/c)². The results support extending the flavor-independent effective interaction hypothesis to baryons, while noting that spin effects were averaged out and that future work should include spin-dependent and Coulomb-type interactions.
