# Maximal mass of the neutron star with a deconfined quark core

> The nature of equation of state for the matter in the neutron star plays an important role in determining its maximal mass. In addition, it must comply with the condition of causality. Noting that the central density of a maximally massive neutron star is well above the nuclear saturation density, a deconfined quark core in the central region is motivated in this paper. We analyze this scenario by employing the MIT bag model to represent the core region and one of the unified equations of state for the region outside the core. Such combination is found to solve the problem of causality violation. In each case of the combined equations of state, the radial profile of $ρr^2$ displays a peak and dominant contribution to the total mass of the star comes from the region around the peak value of $ρr^2$, whereas the contribution is small from the regions near the center and the surface. This peak occurs in the region of hadronic matter for the combinations considered in this paper. Importantly, we find that the position of the peak in $ρr^2$ is well-correlated with the maximal mass -- the highest value of $1.98\ M_\odot$ obtains for the case with the peak occurring farthest from the center. This gravitational threshold being obtained for a non-rotating neutron star, we expect the threshold to lie well above 2 $ M_\odot$ for a rapidly rotating neutron star, that may explain the existance of massive pulsars from recent astronomical observations.

# Maximal mass of the neutron star with a deconfined quark core

## Key claim

The maximal mass of a non-rotating neutron star with a deconfined quark core described by the MIT bag model combined with unified hadronic equations of state reaches at most 1.98 solar masses (for the MIT-BSk21 combination), which is below the 2 solar mass threshold; however, the authors argue that rapid rotation could raise this gravitational threshold to 2.38-2.48 solar masses, potentially explaining observed massive pulsars such as PSR J0952-0607.

## Method

The authors construct combined equations of state by using the MIT bag model (with bag constant B = 60 MeV fm^-3 and massless strange quarks) for the deconfined quark core and one of five unified hadronic equations of state (SLy4, FPS, BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) for the outer hadronic region, with the crossover determined by the first intersection point between the two pressure-density curves. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations numerically to obtain stellar structure, mass, radius, and stability for each combined equation of state, verifying causality by checking that the speed of sound does not exceed the speed of light.

## Key numbers

- Maximal mass for MIT-BSk21 combination: 1.98 solar masses, with central density 2.03 x 10^15 g cm^-3 and stellar radius 11.60 km
- Maximal mass for MIT-BSk20 combination: 1.89 solar masses, with central density 2.28 x 10^15 g cm^-3 and stellar radius 10.93 km
- Maximal mass for MIT-SLy combination: 1.85 solar masses, with central density 2.39 x 10^15 g cm^-3 and stellar radius 10.75 km
- Maximal mass for MIT-BSk19 combination: 1.72 solar masses, with central density 2.87 x 10^15 g cm^-3 and stellar radius 9.87 km
- Maximal mass for MIT-FPS combination: 1.71 solar masses, with central density 2.94 x 10^15 g cm^-3 and stellar radius 9.86 km
- Transition densities from MIT bag model to hadronic equations of state: 2.92 times nuclear saturation density for BSk21, 4.10 for BSk20, 4.72 for SLy, 6.33 for BSk19, and 6.78 for FPS
- Nuclear saturation density: 2.67 x 10^14 g cm^-3
- Speed of sound at center for all combined equations of state: 1.73 x 10^10 cm s^-1 (consistent with causality)
- Peak positions in rho r^2 profile: 8.79 km (MIT-BSk21), 8.23 km (MIT-BSk20), 7.89 km (MIT-SLy), 7.10 km (MIT-BSk19), 6.98 km (MIT-FPS)

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron star maximal masses by addressing the causality violation that occurs when purely hadronic equations of state (particularly BSk19 and BSk20) are used throughout the star at ultra-high central densities near 10 times nuclear saturation density, as previously noted by studies using piecewise polytropic fits. The authors build on the unified equations of state developed by the Brussels-Montreal group (BSk19-21) and the SLy and FPS equations of state from Haensel and Potekhin, combining them with the MIT bag model for quark matter. The finding that the peak in the rho r^2 profile correlates with maximal mass provides a new diagnostic for understanding mass distribution in hybrid stars, and the authors suggest that their non-rotating threshold of 1.98 solar masses, when increased by 20-25 percent due to rapid rotation as shown by earlier work, can explain the observed mass of PSR J0952-0607 at 2.35 solar masses.
