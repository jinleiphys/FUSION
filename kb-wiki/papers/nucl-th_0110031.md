# The Hypertriton in Effective Field Theory

> Doublet Lambda d scattering and the hypertriton are studied in the framework of an effective field theory for large scattering lengths. As in the triton case, consistent renormalization requires a one-parameter three-body force at leading order whose renormalization group evolution is governed by a limit cycle. Constraining unknown parameters from symmetry considerations and the measured binding energy of the hypertriton, we calculate the low-energy phase shifts for doublet Lambda d scattering. For the low-energy parameters, we find a_{Lambda d}=(16.8^{+4.4}_{-2.4}) fm and r_{Lambda d}=(2.3 +/- 0.3) fm, where the errors are due to the uncertainty in the hypertriton binding energy. Since the hypertriton is extremely shallow, low-energy three-body observables in this channel are very insensitive to the exact values of the Lambda N low-energy parameters.

## Key claim

The hypertriton binding energy alone cannot determine the ΛN scattering lengths; a three-body force can always compensate for changes in the ΛN low-energy parameters, contrary to claims in potential model studies. The prediction of low-energy doublet Λd scattering parameters is nevertheless robust due to the extreme shallowness of the hypertriton.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective field theory (EFT) for the ΛNN system with large two-body scattering lengths, using a dibaryon formalism with auxiliary fields for the deuteron and the ³S₁ and ¹S₀ ΛN partial waves. They derived coupled integral equations for the J=1/2 Λd scattering amplitudes, expanded around the limit of equal Λ and nucleon masses (y=0), and regulated the equations with a momentum cutoff whose dependence is absorbed by a one-parameter three-body force whose renormalization group evolution follows a limit cycle.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton separation energy into a deuteron and a Λ: B^Λ = 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV
- Hypertriton total binding energy: B₃^Λ = 2.35 MeV
- Deuteron pole momentum: γ_d = 45.68 MeV
- Λd scattering length: a_Λd = (16.8^{+4.4}_{-2.4}) fm
- Λd effective range: r_Λd = (2.3 ± 0.3) fm
- Limit cycle period for the three-body force: exp(π/s₁) ≈ 10.2, where s₁ = 1.35322
- Characteristic momentum of the hypertriton: γ₃^Λ ≈ 0.3 γ_d

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach for three-body systems with large scattering lengths, previously applied to the neutron-deuteron system by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999, 2000), to the strange sector. It contradicts the claim by Cummings, Jibuti, and Fetisov (1997) that the hypertriton binding energy is highly sensitive to the singlet ΛN scattering length; instead, the three-body force can always compensate. The results enable predictions for low-energy Λd scattering and provide a framework for studying other halo nuclei and strange three-body systems.
