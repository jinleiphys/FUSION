# Partial Dynamical Symmetry in Odd-Mass Nuclei

> Spectral features of the odd-mass nucleus $^{195}$Pt are analyzed by means of an interacting boson-fermion Hamiltonian with SO(6) partial dynamical symmetry. For the latter, selected eigenstates are solvable and preserve the symmetry exactly, while other states are mixed. The analysis constitutes a first example of this novel symmetry construction in a mixed Bose-Fermi system.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first example of a partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) in a mixed Bose-Fermi system, applied to the odd-mass nucleus $^{195}$Pt. The hedge is that only selected eigenstates of the Hamiltonian remain solvable and preserve the symmetry exactly, while other states are mixed; the analysis does not claim full symmetry for all states.

## Method

The interacting boson fermion model (IBFM) Hamiltonian was constructed with an SO$^{\rm BF}$(6) dynamical symmetry (DS) limit, then supplemented with two-particle tensor operators (boson-boson and boson-fermion pairs) that annihilate prescribed sets of states. These operators were chosen so that the ground-band states with U$^{\rm BF}$(6) label $[N+1]$ and SO$^{\rm BF}$(6) label $\langle N+1\rangle$ remain solvable eigenstates, while excited-band states with labels $[N,1]\langle N,1\rangle$ and $[N,1]\langle N-1\rangle$ are perturbed. The Hamiltonian parameters were fitted to the experimental spectrum of $^{195}$Pt with $N=6$ bosons and one fermion in the $3p_{1/2}$, $3p_{3/2}$, and $2f_{5/2}$ orbits.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square (rms) deviation for the solvable $[7,0]\langle7,0\rangle$ levels is 12 keV.
- The rms deviation for both the $[7,0]\langle7,0\rangle$ and $[6,1]\langle6,1\rangle$ levels together is 20 keV.
- DS Hamiltonian coefficients (in keV): $a=45.3$, $b=-41.5$, $c=49.1$, $d=1.7$, $d'=5.6$.
- PDS interaction coefficients (in keV): $a'_1=10$, $a_2=-97$, $a_3=112$, $a_0=306$, with final $a=37.7$ keV.
- 25 measured $B$(E2) values in $^{195}$Pt are compared with DS and PDS predictions; the E2 operator uses effective charges $e_{\rm b}=-e_{\rm f}=0.151$ $e$b.
- Example $B$(E2) values: $190(10)\times10^{-3}$ $e^2$b$^2$ for the $3/2^-$ (212 keV) $\rightarrow$ $1/2^-$ (0 keV) transition (DS and PDS both give 179); $38(6)\times10^{-3}$ $e^2$b$^2$ for the mixed $3/2^-$ (99 keV) $\rightarrow$ solvable $1/2^-$ (0 keV) transition (DS gives 35, PDS gives 34).

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry, previously developed for bosonic systems (e.g., IBM in even-even nuclei by Leviatan (2011) and Alhassid (1992)) and fermionic shell models (e.g., Escher (2000, 2002) and Rowe (2001)), to mixed Bose-Fermi systems relevant to odd-mass nuclei. The specific application to $^{195}$Pt builds on the known SO(6) limit of the IBM for even-even platinum isotopes (Arima (1979), Cizewski (1978)) and earlier IBFM studies of odd-mass platinum (Isacker (1984), Bijker (1985)). The analysis enables future exploration of additional PDS types in odd-mass nuclei, such as SU$^{\rm BF}$(3) PDS, and partial supersymmetry via graded Lie algebras.
