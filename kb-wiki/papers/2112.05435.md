# Gluon and valence quark distributions for the pion and kaon in nuclear matter

> In this paper we study the gluon and valence quark distributions in the pion and kaon in nuclear medium for various nuclear densities as well as in vacuum within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the help of the proper-time regularization scheme which simulates a confinement of QCD. The nuclear medium effect is also determined in the same model for the symmetric nuclear matter. We then analyze the gluon and valence quark distributions for the bound pion and kaon in symmetric nuclear matter as well as those in vacuum. We find that the valence quark and gluon distributions in vacuum have relatively good agreements with the experimental data, the lattice QCD simulations, and the JAM Monte-Carlo (MC) global fit QCD analysis. Evolving to the higher factorization scale $Q = $ 4 GeV, the in-medium gluon and valence-quark distributions of the pion for various nuclear densities are turned out to be almost unchanged in comparison to the vacuum cases. On the contrary, for the kaon, they increase significantly with respect to the densities. Finally, we find that the vacuum gluon distribution for the kaon is smaller than that for the pion, which is consistent with other theoretical predictions. This feature holds for the in-medium gluon distribution in the nuclear density up to the saturation density.

# Gluon and valence quark distributions for the pion and kaon in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The valence quark and gluon distributions of the pion in symmetric nuclear matter show almost no change compared to vacuum when evolved to Q = 4 GeV, while for the kaon these distributions increase significantly with nuclear density. The vacuum gluon distribution for the kaon is smaller than that for the pion, and this ordering holds in medium up to saturation density, but the ratio reverses for densities above saturation density.

## Method
The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with proper-time regularization to compute valence quark distributions for pions and kaons in vacuum and in symmetric nuclear matter. Gluon and sea-quark distributions are absent at the initial model scale and are generated purely through next-to-leading order (NLO) Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) QCD evolution from the initial scale Q0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2 to Q^2 = 16 GeV^2. The nuclear medium effect is computed within the same NJL model using a mean-field approach for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: EB/A = -15.7 MeV
- Initial model scale: Q0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2; evolution scale: Q^2 = 16 GeV^2 (Q = 4 GeV)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of in-medium modifications of pion and kaon structures, including the light-front model with quark-meson coupling by deMelo (2014) and the NJL model study of in-medium valence quark distributions by Hutauruk (2019). It is the first calculation of pion and kaon gluon distributions in nuclear medium. The vacuum results are compared with lattice QCD simulations by Fan (2021) and the JAM global fit QCD analysis by Barry (2018). The approach enables future studies of quark-gluon dynamics in bound mesons within symmetric nuclear matter and can inform experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider, Electron-Ion Collider in China, and COMPASS++/AMBER.
