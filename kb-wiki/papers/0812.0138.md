# Analyzing the Effects of Neutron Polarizabilities in Elastic Compton Scattering off ${}^3$He

> Motivated by the fact that a polarized ${}^3$He nucleus behaves as an `effective' neutron target, we examine manifestations of neutron electromagnetic polarizabilities in elastic Compton scattering from the Helium-3 nucleus. We calculate both unpolarized and double-polarization observables using chiral perturbation theory to next-to-leading order (${\mathcal O}(e^2 Q)$) at energies, $ω\leq m_π$, where $m_π$ is the pion mass. Our results show that the unpolarized differential cross section can be used to measure neutron electric and magnetic polarizabilities, while two double-polarization observables are sensitive to different linear combinations of the four neutron spin polarizabilities. [Note added in 2018] The qualitative conclusions and analytic formulae presented in this paper are correct, but several of the numerical results are wrong: see the erratum posted as arXiv:1804.01206 for further details. A full suite of corrected numerical results for cross sections and asymmetries can be found in Margaryan et al., arXiv:1804.00956. They can also be obtained as an interactive Mathematica notebook by emailing hgrie@gwu.edu.

## Key claim

The paper claims that elastic Compton scattering off polarized ${}^3$He can be used to extract neutron electromagnetic polarizabilities, with the unpolarized differential cross section sensitive to the electric and magnetic polarizabilities and double-polarization observables sensitive to linear combinations of the four neutron spin polarizabilities. However, a 2018 note added states that while the qualitative conclusions and analytic formulae are correct, several numerical results are wrong, with corrected results available in a separate erratum and in Margaryan et al. (2018).

## Method

The authors computed the irreducible amplitudes for elastic Compton scattering on ${}^3$He using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT) to next-to-leading order (${\mathcal O}(e^2 Q)$) at photon energies $\omega \leq m_\pi$, where $m_\pi$ is the pion mass. These amplitudes were then sandwiched between ${}^3$He wavefunctions obtained from Faddeev calculations in momentum space, using several nuclear interactions (chiral NLO, chiral Idaho $N^3$LO, and a high-precision model) to assess model dependence. The matrix elements included one-body and two-body currents, with no three-body currents at this order, and were evaluated in a $jj$-coupling basis with Jacobi momenta.

## Key numbers

- The proton electric polarizability: $\alpha_p = (12.0 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$ (Particle Data Group value)
- The proton magnetic polarizability: $\beta_p = (1.9 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$ (Particle Data Group value)
- The Baldin sum rule for the proton: $\alpha_p + \beta_p = (13.8 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$ (Ol01)
- The Baldin sum rule for the neutron (Levchuk and L'vov extraction): $\alpha_n + \beta_n = (15.2 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$
- Neutron electric polarizability from scattering on lead (Schniedmayer et al.): $\alpha_n = (12.6 \pm 1.5 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$
- Neutron electric polarizability from scattering on lead (Koester et al.): $\alpha_n = (0.6 \pm 5.0) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$
- Neutron electric polarizability range from quasi-free Compton scattering on deuteron at SAL: $\alpha_n = (7.6 - 14.0) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$
- Neutron magnetic polarizability range from same experiment: $\beta_n = (1.2 - 7.6) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$
- Neutron electric-magnetic polarizability difference from Mainz experiment: $\alpha_n - \beta_n = (9.8 \pm 3.6 \, {\rm (stat)} \pm 2.2 \, {\rm (model)} {}^{+2.1}_{-1.1} \, {\rm (sys)}) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$
- Neutron backward spin polarizability from quasi-free Compton scattering on deuteron: $\gamma_{\pi n} = (58.6 \pm 4.0) \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^4$ (Ko03)
- Proton backward spin polarizability from Mainz: range from $-36.5$ to $-39.1 \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^4$ (Ca02)
- Chiral perturbation theory prediction for $\gamma_{\pi p}$: $-36.7 \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^4$ (He98, Pa03)
- Chiral perturbation theory prediction for $\gamma_{\pi n}$: $57.4 \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^4$ (Pa03)
- Forward spin polarizability for proton (VPI-FA93 multipole analysis): $\gamma_{0p} \simeq -1.34 \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^4$
- Forward spin polarizability for neutron (VPI-FA93 multipole analysis): $\gamma_{0n} \simeq -0.38 \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^4$
- Chiral perturbation theory prediction for $\gamma_{0p}$: $-2.1 \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^4$ (Pa03)
- Isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities from deuteron Compton scattering (Beane et al.): $\alpha_N = (8.9 \pm 1.5)^{+4.7}_{-0.9} \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$, $\beta_N = (2.2 \pm 1.5)^{+1.2}_{-0.9} \times 10^{-4} \, {\rm fm}^3$
- Binding energy range of ${}^3$He from the nuclear interactions used: 6.89--7.83 MeV (experimental: 7.72 MeV)
- Convergence of one-body matrix elements to within 0.1% using partial waves with $j_{12} \leq 2$
- Convergence of two-body matrix elements to within 0.2% using partial waves with $j_{12} \leq 1$

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of Compton scattering on the deuteron (Beane et al., 2002, 2004; Hildebrandt et al., 2005; Choudhury and Phillips, 2005) to the ${}^3$He nucleus, motivated by the fact that polarized ${}^3$He behaves as an effective neutron target due to the anti-alignment of the two proton spins in the dominant s-state (approximately 90% of the wavefunction). The study is the first calculation of elastic Compton scattering on ${}^3$He in any framework and is intended to provide benchmark results that can guide future experiments and calculations at higher orders in chiral perturbation theory. The results enable parallel extraction of neutron polarizabilities from different nuclear targets, which could build confidence in the extracted values by checking consistency across reactions.
