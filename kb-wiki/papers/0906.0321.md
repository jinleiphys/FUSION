# A New Treatment of 2N and 3N Bound States in Three Dimensions

> The direct treatment of the Faddeev equation for the three-boson system in 3 dimensions is generalized to nucleons. The one Faddeev equation for identical bosons is replaced by a strictly finite set of coupled equations for scalar functions which depend only on 3 variables. The spin-momentum dependence occurring as scalar products in 2N and 3N forces accompanied by scalar functions is supplemented by a corresponding expansion of the Faddeev amplitudes. After removing the spin degrees of freedom by suitable operations only scalar expressions depending on momenta remain. The corresponding steps are performed for the deuteron leading to two coupled equations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new formulation of the Faddeev equation for the three-nucleon (3N) bound state that works directly with vector variables in three dimensions, avoiding the standard partial wave decomposition. The method is presented as an alternative to the approach of Bayegan (2007), and the authors claim that after removing spin degrees of freedom by suitable operations, only scalar expressions depending on momenta remain, leading to a strictly finite set of coupled equations for scalar functions of three variables.

## Method

The authors generalize a previous direct 3D treatment of the Faddeev equation for three identical bosons to the case of nucleons with spin and isospin. They expand the Faddeev components, the NN t-matrix, and the 3N force as products of scalar functions with scalar products of spin operators and momentum vectors, using a complete set of eight spin-momentum operators for the 3N wave function derived from partial wave states in Fach04. By projecting from the left with specific spin states and summing over magnetic quantum numbers, they remove the spin dependence and obtain coupled integral equations for scalar functions of the Jacobi momenta only, with all spin-momentum algebra pre-calculated in scalar kernels.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron wave function is expressed in terms of two scalar functions, phi_1(p) and phi_2(p), which are the s- and d-wave components.
- The NN potential is restricted by rotational, parity, and time-reversal invariance to six terms (w_1 through w_6), with accompanying scalar functions v_j(p,p').
- The Faddeev component for the 3N bound state is expanded in eight scalar functions phi_{tT}^{(i)}(p,q) with eight corresponding spin-momentum operators O_i.
- The 3N system involves three possible isospin states: |gamma_0> = |(0 1/2) 1/2>, |gamma_1> = |(1 1/2) 1/2>, and |gamma_2> = |(1 1/2) 3/2>.
- The 2 pi-exchange 3NF example contains two operator structures: one proportional to the identity and one proportional to sigma_1 dot ((p-p') x (q-q')).

## Context

This work extends the direct 3D treatment of the Faddeev equation previously applied to three-boson systems by Schadow, Liu, and Lin (2008) to the physically relevant case of nucleons. It provides an alternative to the partial wave representation, which becomes increasingly tedious for chiral 3N forces at N3LO as developed by Epelbaum (2002) and others, especially for controlling numerical accuracy at higher angular momenta as noted by Hueber. The formulation enables future numerical calculations of 3N bound states directly in momentum space without partial wave decomposition, and the authors demonstrate the method explicitly for the deuteron as a warm-up exercise, leading to two coupled equations for the s- and d-wave components.
