# Three-body Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas approach to direct nuclear reactions

> Momentum space three-body Faddeev-like equations are used to calculate elastic, transfer and charge exchange reactions resulting from the scattering of deuterons on 12C and 16O or protons on 13C and 17O; 12C and 16O are treated as inert cores. All possible reactions are calculated in the framework of the same model space. Comparison with previous calculations based on approximate methods used in nuclear reaction theory is discussed.

# Three-body Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas approach to direct nuclear reactions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that solving exact Faddeev/Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space with realistic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleus interactions can describe elastic, transfer, and charge-exchange reactions for deuteron- and proton-induced reactions on light nuclei within a single consistent three-body framework, but the quality of agreement with experimental data depends strongly on whether energy-independent or energy-dependent optical potentials are used, and no single dynamical model simultaneously reproduces all observables.

## Method

Momentum-space Faddeev/AGS equations for three-body transition operators were solved after partial-wave decomposition, using the CD-Bonn potential for the neutron-proton interaction and Watson optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus interactions, with the Coulomb interaction included via screening and renormalization. Three dynamical models were compared: Model 1 used energy-independent optical potentials at fixed energies; Model 2 used fully energy-dependent optical potentials; Model 3 used a hybrid approach with energy-dependent potentials in selected partial waves and energy-independent potentials elsewhere. Calculations were performed for deuteron scattering on 12C and 16O, and proton scattering on 13C and 17O, treating 12C and 16O as inert cores.

## Key numbers

- For d + 12C elastic scattering at Ed = 30 MeV, differential cross section divided by Rutherford cross section was calculated and compared to experimental data from Ref. [dC30]
- For p + 13C elastic scattering at Ep = 35 MeV, differential cross section divided by Rutherford cross section was calculated and compared to experimental data from Ref. [pCO35]
- For p + 13C → d + 12C transfer at Ep = 35 MeV, differential cross section was calculated and compared to experimental data from Ref. [pC35d]
- For d + 16O elastic scattering at Ed = 25.4, 36.0, and 63.2 MeV, differential cross section divided by Rutherford cross section was calculated and compared to experimental data from Refs. [dO25-63, dO34]
- For p + 17O elastic scattering at Ep = 35.2 MeV, differential cross section divided by Rutherford cross section was calculated and compared to experimental data from Ref. [pCO35]
- For p + 17O → d + 16O transfer at Ep = 35.2 MeV, differential cross section was calculated and compared to experimental data from Ref. [dO25-63]
- For d + 12C → p + 13C transfer at Ed = 30 MeV, differential cross section was calculated for ground state 1/2- and excited states 1/2+ and 5/2+
- For d + 16O → p + 17O transfer at Ed = 25.4, 36.0, and 63.2 MeV, differential cross section was calculated for ground state 5/2+ and excited state 1/2+
- For p + 13C → n + 13N charge exchange at Ep = 35 MeV, differential cross section was calculated for ground state 1/2-
- For p + 17O → n + 17F charge exchange at Ep = 35 MeV, differential cross section was calculated for ground state 5/2+ and excited state 1/2+
- Binding energies of single-particle states in 13C, 13N, 17O, and 17F were calculated using adjusted optical potential parameters, with values ranging from 0.105 MeV (2s1/2 in 17F) to 38.022 MeV (1s1/2 in 13C)
- The screening radius for Coulomb interactions was R ≈ 10 fm for short-range scattering amplitudes and R ≈ 15 fm for reactions leading to a final (Ap) bound state
- Partial waves included: np with total angular momentum I ≤ 3, nA with orbital angular momentum L ≤ 8, pA with L ≤ 20, and total three-particle angular momentum J ≤ 35

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev/AGS approach previously applied to proton-deuteron scattering (Deltuva 2005a, 2005c) to reactions involving heavier nuclei, building on earlier three-body models of deuteron-induced reactions by Aaron and Shanley (1966) and Alt et al. (2007) that used separable interactions and lacked proper Coulomb treatment. The results are compared with continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations (Austern 1987), which the authors previously benchmarked against exact AGS solutions (Deltuva 2007d), and with standard approximations such as the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) and Glauber approximation (Crespo 2007b). The work enables systematic testing of spectroscopic factors extracted from transfer reactions and provides a framework for future inclusion of three-body optical potentials to account for the energy dependence of nucleon-nucleus interactions.
