# A Self-Consistent Solution to the Nuclear Many-Body Problem at Finite Temperature

> The properties of symmetric nuclear matter are investigated within the Green's functions approach. We have implemented an iterative procedure allowing for a self-consistent evaluation of the single-particle and two-particle propagators. The in-medium scattering equation is solved for a realistic (non-separable) nucleon-nucleon interaction including both particle-particle and hole-hole propagation. The corresponding two-particle propagator is constructed explicitely from the single-particle spectral functions. Results are obtained for finite temperatures and an extrapolation to T=0 is presented.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a fully self-consistent Green's functions approach for symmetric nuclear matter, using a realistic non-separable nucleon-nucleon interaction and including both particle-particle and hole-hole propagation, yields stable numerical solutions at finite temperatures down to T = 5 MeV, with no pairing instability observed in the explored range; the extrapolation to T = 0 is presented but carries estimated uncertainties of up to 3% for interpolated values.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation for the single-particle Green's function and the in-medium T-matrix equation for the two-particle propagator in a self-consistent iterative scheme, using the CDBONN realistic nucleon-nucleon potential. The two-particle propagator is constructed explicitly from the full single-particle spectral functions rather than from quasi-particle approximations, and the T-matrix equation is solved in a partial-wave expansion including all partial waves up to J = 2 for the off-shell self-energy and up to J = 9 for the Hartree-Fock contribution. Calculations are performed for a range of chemical potentials and temperatures, with the self-consistent spectral functions used to compute binding energies via Koltun's sum rule.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon at T = 0 extrapolated to -17.9 MeV
- Density of the system at mu = -15 MeV is about 0.36 fm^{-3}
- Sum rule for the two-particle spectral function satisfied to better than 1% for single particle momenta up to k = 3000 MeV
- Calculations performed for chemical potentials between mu = -24 MeV and mu = 5 MeV at T = 10 MeV
- Calculations performed for temperatures between T = 5 MeV and T = 20 MeV at mu = -15 MeV
- Stable and converging calculations not possible below T = 5 MeV
- Energy and density converged to better than 0.1% by the end of iteration

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Green's functions approach to nuclear matter using realistic interactions, building on earlier attempts that encountered pairing instabilities in the quasi-particle approximation (Alm et al., 1995; Vonderfecht et al., 1993) and on studies using separable interaction models (Bozek and Czerski, 2001-2003). The method enables the calculation of spectral functions and binding energies without the pairing instability that plagued quasi-particle schemes, and provides a framework for studying nuclear matter properties relevant to astrophysical environments at finite temperatures.
