# Heavy-quark effects on cold quark matter and self-bound stars

> Heavy-quark effects on the equation of state for cold and dense quark matter are obtained from perturbative QCD, yielding observables parametrized only by the renormalization scale. In particular, we investigate the thermodynamics of charm quark matter under the constraints of $β$ equilibrium and electric charge neutrality in a region of densities where perturbative QCD is, in principle, much more reliable. Finally, we analyze the stability of charm stars, a possible new branch of ultradense, self-bound compact stars, and find that they are unstable under radial oscillations.

# Heavy-quark effects on cold quark matter and self-bound stars

## Key claim
Using perturbative QCD, the authors find that charm quark stars are dynamically unstable under radial oscillations, contradicting the static stability condition which would suggest stability for some values of the renormalization scale parameter X > 3. The instability holds for all values of X examined, including those where the static condition ∂M/∂εc ≥ 0 is satisfied.

## Method
The authors extend the three-loop perturbative QCD framework of Kurkela (2009) from Nf = Nl + 1 flavors to any number of massive flavors, constructing the thermodynamic potential at O(α²s) for Nl massless and Nm massive quarks. They apply this formalism to charm quark matter under β equilibrium and electric charge neutrality, using the strange and charm quark masses as running parameters with renormalization-group evolution. The equation of state is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure, and radial oscillation stability is analyzed via the method of Gondek (1997).

## Key numbers
- Strange quark mass fixed at ms(2 GeV, Nf = 3,4) = 92.4(1.5) MeV, giving invariant masses m̂²⁺¹s ≈ 246.2 MeV (Nf = 3) and m̂²⁺¹⁺¹s ≈ 243.7 MeV (Nf = 4)
- Charm quark mass fixed at mc(3 GeV, Nf = 4) = 0.9851(63) GeV ≡ m⁰c, giving invariant mass m̂²⁺¹⁺¹c ≈ 3.0895 GeV
- αs(Λ̄ = 1.5 GeV, Nf = 3,4) = 0.336⁺⁰·⁰¹²₋₀·₀₀₈, yielding Λ²⁺¹MS = 343⁺¹⁸₋₁₂ MeV and Λ²⁺¹⁺¹MS = 290⁺¹⁸₋₁₂ MeV
- Renormalization scale parameter X chosen in the range X ≥ 3 to satisfy the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD equation of state framework of Kurkela (2009) to include multiple massive quark flavors, complementing earlier pQCD work by Kapusta (2006) and lattice QCD at high temperatures by Borsanyi (2016). Previous studies of charm stars by Kettner (1994) used only the bag model and found instability against radial oscillations; the present work confirms this result from first-principle pQCD calculations. The findings rule out charm stars as a stable branch of self-bound compact stars, but suggest future investigation of charm quarks in neutron-star mergers.
