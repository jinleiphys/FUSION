# Interpretation of the Neutrino-Nucleus Cross Section

> I discuss the near-degeneracy between models of neutrino-nucleus interactions based on diverse assumptions, and analyze a specific example illustrating how the different reaction mechanisms taken into account, as well as the approximations associated with their implementation, may conspire to make the prediction of two models look similar. I argue that the relevant reaction mechanisms may be unambiguously identified exploiting the availability of electron-nucleus scattering data other than inclusive cross sections, providing detailed information on the nuclear spectral function.

## Key claim

The paper argues that different theoretical models of neutrino-nucleus scattering, based on conceptually different or even contradictory assumptions about reaction mechanisms, can produce nearly identical predictions for inclusive cross sections, meaning that agreement with data may be accidental and the degeneracy must be resolved using exclusive electron scattering data.

## Method

The author compares two specific models of charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) neutrino-carbon scattering: the model of Nieves et al., which combines 1p1h final states with meson-exchange currents (MEC) and long-range RPA correlations, and the hybrid model of Megias et al., which combines a phenomenological scaling analysis of electron data with a theoretical calculation of MEC contributions within the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model. The author then uses the spectral function formalism, constrained by exclusive (e,e'p) data from Mougey et al. (1976) and JLab experiments, to isolate the true 1p1h contribution and compare it against the predictions of both models.

## Key numbers

- The mean neutrino energy of the MiniBooNE flux is ⟨E_ν⟩ = 880 MeV.
- The occupation probability of valence P-state protons in carbon is reduced to Z_P = 0.625 from the independent particle model value of 4, as measured by Mougey et al. (1976).
- The spectral function of Benhar et al. (LDA) yields a spectroscopic factor Z_P = 0.64, within ~2% of the experimental value.
- The continuum strength from nucleon-nucleon correlations integrated over the JLab kinematical region is 0.61 ± 0.06 experimentally, compared to a theoretical value of 0.64.
- The 1p1h cross section from the spectral function formalism is ~20% lower at the peak than the predictions of both the Nieves et al. model and the scaling analysis of Megias et al.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of using electron-nucleus scattering data to constrain models of neutrino-nucleus interactions, following the failure of the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model exposed by MiniBooNE data (CCQE, 2010). The author argues that exclusive (e,e'p) measurements, such as those by Mougey et al. (1976) and Rohe et al. (JLab), provide the essential tool to resolve degeneracies between models like those of Nieves et al. and Megias et al. The spectral function formalism, combined with Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations as benchmarks, is presented as a path toward a comprehensive and consistent description applicable to future experiments using liquid argon technology.
