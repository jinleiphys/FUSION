# Strange magnetism in the nucleon

> Using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to one loop, we derive an analytic and parameter-free expression for the momentum dependence of the strange magnetic form factor of the nucleon $G_M^{(s)} (Q^2)$ and its corresponding radius. This should be considered as a lower bound. We also derive a model-independent relation between the isoscalar magnetic and the strange magnetic form factors of the nucleon based on chiral symmetry and SU(3) only. This gives an upper bound on the strange magnetic form factor. We use these limites to derive bounds on the strange magnetic moment of the proton from the recent measurement of $G_M^{(s)} (Q^2=0.1 GeV^2)$ by the SAMPLE collaboration. We stress the relevance of this result for the on-going and future experimental programs at various electron machines.

## Key claim

Using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to one loop, the authors derive a parameter-free expression for the momentum dependence of the strange magnetic form factor \(G_M^{(s)}(Q^2)\) which should be considered as a lower bound, and a model-independent relation between the isoscalar magnetic and strange magnetic form factors which gives an upper bound.

## Method

The authors compute the strange magnetic form factor of the nucleon to one-loop order in three-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT), considering kaon-loop diagrams (\(K\Lambda\) and \(K\Sigma\) intermediate states) and dimension-two contact terms. They also calculate the isoscalar magnetic form factor in SU(3) to the same order, finding that its momentum dependence is dominated by the kaon cloud with pion contributions canceling exactly. The two results are combined to extract bounds on the strange magnetic moment from the SAMPLE measurement at \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

* The slope of the strange magnetic form factor at \(q^2=0\) is \(\left(\rho_{M}\right)^2 = -0.027\) fm\(^2\), identical for proton and neutron, independent of the strange magnetic moment to this order.
* The isoscalar magnetic radius from the leading chiral contribution is \(\left(r_{M}^{I=0}\right)^2 = 0.18\) fm\(^2\), about 27% of the radius from the empirical dipole parametrization.
* The upper bound on the strange magnetic radius from the dipole parametrization of the isoscalar magnetic form factor is \(\left(\rho_{M}^{(s),\;{\rm dip}}\right)^2 = -0.10\) fm\(^2\).
* From the SAMPLE measurement \(G_M^{(s)}(Q^2=0.1\ \text{GeV}^2) = +0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19\) nuclear magnetons, the extracted strange magnetic moment of the proton is \(\mu_p^{(s)} = 0.03 \ldots 0.18\) n.m. after applying the \(Q^2\)-dependent corrections of \(-0.06\) (lower bound) and \(-0.20\) (upper bound).
* The leading-order kaon-loop contribution to the strange magnetic moment is \(\mu_{N}^{(s)\;{\rm K-loops}} = 2.0\) (in nuclear magnetons), using the average pseudoscalar decay constant \(F_\pi \equiv (F_\pi + F_K)/2 \simeq 102\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (BZ, GSS, BKKM, BFHM) to the three-flavor case for the strange vector current, providing the first parameter-free chiral prediction for the momentum dependence of \(G_M^{(s)}(Q^2)\). The results provide lower and upper bounds that can be used to interpret measurements from the SAMPLE collaboration and guide future experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (G0 collaboration) and other electron machines, addressing the long-standing question of how strange the nucleon is. The analysis contrasts with earlier dispersion-theoretical approaches (Jaffe, 1989; Hohler et al.) and vector meson dominance models that gave a wide range of predictions for the strange magnetic moment (\(-0.8 \le \mu_p^{(s)} \le 0.5\) n.m.).
