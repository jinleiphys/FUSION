# On the current status of OZI violation in $πN$ and pp reactions

> The available data on $ω$ and $ϕ$ production from $πN$ and $pp$ collisions are reanalyzed with respect to an OZI rule violation on the basis of transition matrix elements. The data are found to be compatible with a constant ratio $R$ up to excess energies $ε{\approx}300$ MeV, however, the ratio $R$ deviates substantially from the SU(3) prediction based on the present knowledge of the $ϕ-ω$ mixing angle.

## Key claim

The available data on ω and φ production from πN and pp collisions are compatible with a constant ratio R of transition amplitudes up to excess energies ε ≈ 300 MeV, but this ratio deviates substantially from the SU(3) prediction based on the present knowledge of the φ-ω mixing angle; the authors find that the data are compatible with an average ratio R²(φ/ω) ≈ 1.6×10⁻², which disagrees with the SU(3) estimate of 4.2×10⁻³.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing experimental data on total cross sections for πN → ωN, πN → φN, pp → ppω, and pp → ppφ reactions by extracting transition matrix elements |M_V| from the cross sections using relativistic two-body phase space relations. For πN reactions, they fit the transition amplitudes as a function of excess energy ε with an exponential function |M_V| = M₀ + M₁ exp(-γ ε) and computed the ratio R = |M_ω|/|M_φ|. For pp reactions, they used a three-body phase space formula including final-state interaction corrections between the outgoing nucleons, and compared the extracted amplitudes with the DISTO collaboration data point at 2.85 GeV beam energy.

## Key numbers

- Average ratio of transition amplitudes for πN reactions: ⟨R⟩ = |M_{πN→ωN}| / |M_{πN→φN}| = 8.7 ± 1.8 (95% confidence level), constant within uncertainties up to ε = 10 GeV
- For pp reactions at 2.85 GeV beam energy (DISTO): σ(pp → ppω) = 45 ± 7 μb, σ(pp → ppφ) = 0.17^{+0.07}_{-0.06} μb
- χ² fit to pp data with constant amplitude ratio gives |M_ω|/|M_φ| = 8.5 ± 1.0, with confidence level below 50%
- The DISTO result alone gives |M_ω|/|M_φ| = 5.72^{+1.01}_{-1.17} when using the fitted pp → ppω amplitude
- SU(3) prediction based on mixing angle deviation Δθ_V = 3.7° gives R²(φ/ω) = 4.2 × 10⁻³
- Average ratio from all data (decays and reactions): R²(φ/ω) ≈ 1.6 × 10⁻²
- Ratio of ωρπ to φρπ coupling constants from radiative decays: g_{ωρπ}/g_{φρπ} = 12.9 ± 0.4 (vector dominance model) or 16.8 ± 1.0 (direct coupling model)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of OZI rule violation in hadronic reactions by systematically comparing πN and pp data using transition matrix elements rather than cross sections directly, following the approach of Hanhart (1998). The authors find that the experimental φ/ω ratio deviates substantially from the SU(3) prediction based on the φ-ω mixing angle from the Particle Data Group (1998), consistent with earlier suggestions by Lipkin (1991) that the OZI rule may be violated. The analysis enables future comparisons with two-step production mechanisms involving intermediate K̄K, K*̄K, or K*̄K* states as studied by Locher (1998), Buzatu (1998), and others, and highlights the need for new high-precision data to resolve the discrepancy between the DISTO result and other measurements.
