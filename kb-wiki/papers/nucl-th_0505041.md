# Quasi-inertial ellipsoidal flows in relativistic hydrodynamics

> We search for non-trivial relativistic solutions of the hydrodynamic equations with quasi-inertial flows such as in the Bjorken-like models. The problem is analyzed in general and the known results are reproduced by a method proposed. A new class of 3D anisotropic analytic solutions with quasi-inertial property is found. An ellipsoidal generalization of the spherically symmetric Hubble flow with constant pressure is proposed as a particular case. The relativistic expansion of finite systems into vacuum is also described within this class. A region of applicability and possible utilization of the new solutions for processes of A+A collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims to find a new class of 3D anisotropic analytic solutions to relativistic hydrodynamic equations that exhibit quasi-inertial flow (accelerationless in the rest frame of each fluid element), but this result is conditional on assuming either a gradient-like velocity ansatz or a constant pressure equation of state; the solutions are not fully general and require specific choices of the function \(F(\phi)\) in the d'Alembert equation or the constant pressure condition.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic perfect fluid equations and impose the quasi-inertial condition \(u^{\nu}\partial_{\nu}u^{\mu}=0\), which eliminates acceleration. They then derive consistency conditions and explore two approaches: a gradient-like velocity ansatz \(u^{\mu}=\partial^{\mu}\phi\) that reduces the problem to solving \(\square\phi=F(\phi)\) together with the normalization condition, and a separate construction of an ellipsoidal 4-velocity ansatz \(u^{\mu}=\{t/\tilde{\tau}, a_k(t)x_k/\tilde{\tau}\}\) with time-dependent parameters \(a_i(t)=t/(t+T_i)\). The energy density and particle density are then solved from the continuity equations, yielding general solutions with arbitrary functions fixed by initial conditions.

## Key numbers

* The flow parameters \(a_i(t)\) are given by \(a_i(t)=t/(t+T_i)\), where \(T_i\) are three integration constants with dimension of time (e.g., \(T_i\simeq 1\) fm/c for heavy ion collisions).
* The general solution for enthalpy density is \(\varepsilon+p_0 = F_{\varepsilon}(x_1/(t+T_1), x_2/(t+T_2), x_3/(t+T_3)) / [(t+T_1)(t+T_2)(t+T_3)]\).
* For the generalized Hubble-like solution, the enthalpy density takes the form \(\varepsilon+p_0 = C_{\varepsilon} a_1 a_2 a_3 / \tilde{\tau}^3\) with \(C_{\varepsilon}=T_1 T_2 T_3 (\varepsilon(0,\mathbf{0})+p_0)\).
* The boost-invariant Bjorken solution is recovered by setting \(T_1\rightarrow\infty, T_2\rightarrow\infty\) with finite \(T_3\), giving \(\tilde{\tau}\rightarrow\tau=\sqrt{(t+T_3)^2-x_3^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the known quasi-inertial solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics, the (1+1) boost-invariant Bjorken flow (Bjorken, 1983) and the spherically symmetric Hubble flow (Chiu, 1975), to fully 3D anisotropic ellipsoidal flows. It also generalizes the constant-pressure cylindrical solution of Biro (2000) to asymmetric transverse expansion. The new solutions can describe elliptic flow in non-central heavy ion collisions and serve as test cases for numerical relativistic hydrodynamic codes, particularly during the deconfinement phase transition and the final stage of hadron system evolution.
