# Does Compound Nucleus remember its Isospin- An Evidence from the Fission Widths

> We present an evidence of isospin effects in nuclear fission by comparing the fission widths for reactions involving different isospin states of the same compound nucleus (CN). Yadrovsky suggested this possibility in 1975. Yadrovsky obtained the fission widths for two reaction data sets, namely $^{206}$Pb($α$,f) and $^{209}$Bi(p,f), both leading to same CN, and concluded that "a nucleus remembers the isospin value of the nuclear states leading to fission". We obtain the fission decay widths for both the $T_0+1/2$ and $T_0-1/2$ states of CN by using two appropriate reaction data sets. We then compare the fission widths for the two isospin states of CN. More specifically, we have chosen the combination of $^{206}$Pb($α$,f) and $^{209}$Bi(p,f) same as presented in Yadrovsky's paper and $^{235}$U(d,f) and $^{236}$U(p,f) reaction data sets in this study. A significant difference between the ratios of fission decay widths to total decay widths for different isospin values suggests that isospin plays an important role in fission.

## Key claim

The authors provide evidence that the compound nucleus remembers its isospin during fission by showing a significant difference in fission branching ratios for the \(T_0+1/2\) and \(T_0-1/2\) isospin states, using different experimental data than Yadrovsky (1975) used, but caution that the result depends on the reliability of the chosen fission cross-section datasets.

## Method

The authors used Yadrovsky’s formalism to extract fission branching ratios (\(\Gamma_f/\Gamma_{\text{total}}\)) for the two isospin states of the compound nucleus. For the \(^{209}\)Bi(p,f) and \(^{206}\)Pb(\(\alpha\),f) reactions leading to \(^{210}\)Po, they took fission cross-section data from Prokofiev (for proton-induced) and Huizenga et al. (for alpha-induced), and calculated compound-nucleus formation cross-sections using the PACE4 code. They then applied Eqs. (1) and (2) from Yadrovsky to compute the branching ratios at several excitation energies.

## Key numbers

*   For the \(^{209}\)Bi(p,f) and \(^{206}\)Pb(\(\alpha\),f) dataset at \(E^*_{\text{CN}} = 41.6\) MeV: \(\Gamma_f^{T_0+1/2} / \Gamma_{\text{total}}^{T_0+1/2} = 5.28 \times 10^{-2}\) and \(\Gamma_f^{T_0-1/2} / \Gamma_{\text{total}}^{T_0-1/2} = 1.46 \times 10^{-3}\).
*   For the same dataset at \(E^*_{\text{CN}} = 46.3\) MeV: \(\Gamma_f^{T_0+1/2} / \Gamma_{\text{total}}^{T_0+1/2} = 8.87 \times 10^{-2}\) and \(\Gamma_f^{T_0-1/2} / \Gamma_{\text{total}}^{T_0-1/2} = 1.61 \times 10^{-3}\).
*   The fission branching ratio for the \(T_0+1/2\) state is greater by an order of magnitude than for the \(T_0-1/2\) state across the excitation energies studied (41.6–46.3 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the original suggestion by Yadrovsky (1975) that the compound nucleus remembers its isospin during fission, but uses a different and, according to the authors, more reliable experimental dataset for the \(^{209}\)Bi(p,f) reaction (from Prokofiev) than the one used by Yadrovsky (from Gadioli et al.). The results support the authors’ earlier work (Jain and Swati) on isospin conservation in fission and suggest that isospin remains a relevant quantum number even in heavy, neutron-rich compound nuclei.
