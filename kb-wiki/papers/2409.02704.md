# Calculation of The Abundance of $^{187}$Re-$^{187}$Os Nuclear Clock Nuclides in S-process and Sensitivity Analysis of Maxwellian-Averaged Neutron Capture Cross Sections

> In this paper, the network equations calculation of $^{187}$Re-$^{187}$Os clock-related nuclide abundance in s-process is studied, and the sensitivities of Maxwellian-Averaged neutron capture cross sections for each nuclide are analyzed in detail. Firstly, basing nuclear physical parameters, we give the branching s-process reaction network from $^{184}$W to $^{190}$Os, and establish the corresponding network equations. Using a single path s-process approximation, we obtain an analytical expression of the seed nuclide $^{183}$W abundance of our branching network. Because of the stiffness of the system of network equations, we use the semi-implicit Runge-Kutta method to give the numerical solution of the network equations, and thus obtain the abundance of each nuclide related to the $^{187}$Re-$^{187}$Os nuclear clock in the s-process. Finally, with the numerical solution, the sensitivity analysis of the Maxwellian-Averaged neutron capture cross sections of the nuclear reaction involved in the $^{187}$Re-$^{187}$Os nuclear clock network equations is carried out. Therefore, we find that in s-process, the neutron capture reaction $^{184}$W + n $\to$ $^{185}$W has the greatest influence on the $^{187}$Re-$^{187}$Os nuclear clock reaction network, and the neutron capture reaction $^{186}$W + n $\to$ $^{187}$W has the greatest effect on the particular nuclides $^{187}$Re and $^{187}$Os. So the measurements of these two Maxwellian-Averaged neutron capture cross sections deserve the attention of experimental nuclear physicists.

## Key claim
The authors find that, within the s-process reaction network for the Re-Os nuclear clock, the neutron capture reaction 184W + n → 185W has the greatest overall influence on the network, while the reaction 186W + n → 187W most strongly affects the abundances of the specific clock nuclides 187Re and 187Os. This conclusion is conditional on the specific sensitivity metric defined as the sum of absolute abundance changes across all network nuclides (or the two clock nuclides) when each Maxwellian-averaged cross section is varied from 80% to 120% in 10% steps.

## Method
The authors constructed a branching s-process reaction network from 184W to 190Os and wrote the corresponding system of differential equations for nuclide abundances, using the classical s-process approximation for the seed nuclide 183W. Because the network equations are stiff, they solved them numerically using a fourth-order semi-implicit Runge-Kutta method over a neutron exposure parameter τ from 0.5 to 4.5 with step h = 0.0001. For sensitivity analysis, each of the eight Maxwellian-averaged neutron capture cross sections was individually varied from 80% to 120% in 10% steps, and the sensitivity was defined as the sum over all computational nodes of the absolute abundance differences (1-norm) between the perturbed and original numerical solutions.

## Key numbers
- The half-life of 187Re is 4.35 × 10^10 years (Lindner 1986).
- The half-life of 186Re is 3.72 days, which is much shorter than the typical s-process neutron capture time scale of 10–100 years.
- The half-life of 183W is 1.9 × 10^18 years, treated as stable in the s-process.
- Maxwellian-averaged neutron capture cross sections (in mb) used in the network: 184W (224), 185W (703), 185Re (1535), 186Re (1550), 186W (176), 186Os (422), 187Os (896), 187Re (1160), 188Os (399), 189Os (1168).
- The sensitivity analysis varied each cross section from 80% to 120% in steps of H = 10%.
- The numerical integration used τ from 0.5 to 4.5 with step h = 0.0001, giving 4 × 10^4 computational nodes.

## Context
This work extends the classical s-process abundance calculations of Clayton (1961) and the branching network approach of Pan (2020) and Ward (1976) to the specific 187Re-187Os nuclear clock system. It addresses the difficulty of analytical sensitivity analysis when cross sections of different nuclides are nearly equal (e.g., 185Re and 186Re, 186Os and 188Os), motivating the numerical approach. The results identify the two neutron capture reactions most deserving of improved experimental measurement, following the sensitivity methodology of Gao (2021). This enables better calibration of the 187Re-187Os cosmic nuclear clock for determining the age of the Galaxy, building on the work of Clayton (1964), Takahashi (1983), and Hayakawa (2005).
