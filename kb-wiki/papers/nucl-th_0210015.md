# Determination of S17 from 7Be(d,n)8B reaction: CDCC analyses based on three-body model

> The astrophysical factor $S_{17}$ for $^7$Be($p,γ$)$^8$B reaction is reliably extracted from the transfer reaction $^7$Be($d,n$)$^8$B at $E=7.5$ MeV with the asymptotic normalization coefficient method. The transfer reaction is accurately analyzed with CDCC based on the three-body model. This analysis is free from uncertainties of the optical potentials having been crucial in the previous DWBA analyses.

## Key claim

The astrophysical factor \(S_{17}(0)\) for the \(^7\)Be(\(p,\gamma\))\(^8\)B reaction is reliably extracted from the transfer reaction \(^7\)Be(\(d,n\))\(^8\)B at \(E=7.5\) MeV using the asymptotic normalization coefficient method with a three-body model analysis that is free from the uncertainties of optical potentials that plagued previous DWBA analyses, though the final result still carries a theoretical ambiguity of 6–7% and a 13% experimental error.

## Method

The authors analyzed the \(^7\)Be(\(d,n\))\(^8\)B transfer reaction at 7.5 MeV using a three-body model (\(p+n+^7\)Be) with \(^7\)Be treated as an inert core. The entrance channel dynamics were solved with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, which explicitly includes deuteron breakup states, using a neutron-\(^7\)Li optical potential determined from elastic scattering data at 4.08 MeV and tested against deuteron elastic scattering on \(^7\)Li at 8.0 MeV. The exit channel was treated with the Johnson-Soper adiabatic approximation, and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) was extracted by comparing calculated cross sections to experimental data for four different \(p+^7\)Be potentials describing the \(^8\)B ground state.

## Key numbers

- \(S_{17}(0) = 20.96^{+1.4}_{-1.3}\) (theoretical) \(\pm 2.7\) (experimental) eV b, extracted from the \(^7\)Be(\(d,n\))\(^8\)B reaction at \(E=7.5\) MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor \(S_{\text{exp}} = 0.849\) for the Kim et al. potential yields \(S_{17}(0) = 21.36\) eV b; values for other potentials range from 20.67 to 21.36 eV b.
- The CDCC model space used \(k_{\text{max}} = 1.7\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\ell = 0, 2\), and \(\Delta = 1.7/40\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The adiabatic approximation error is less than 3% at forward angles for \(^8\)B(\(n,n\))\(^8\)B at \(E=4.18\) MeV.
- The finite-range correction for the deuteron ground channel agrees with full finite-range calculation within 2% error at forward angles.

## Context

This work extends the ANC method for \(S_{17}\) from the \(^7\)Be(\(d,n\))\(^8\)B reaction, previously analyzed with DWBA by Liu et al. (2001) which gave \(S_{17}(0) = 27.4 \pm 4.4\) eV b, and addresses the large ambiguities (typically 30%) from \(d\)-\(^7\)Be optical potentials noted by Gagliardi et al. (1999) and Fernandes et al. (1996). The result is consistent with the recommended value \(19^{+4}_{-2}\) eV b from Adelberger et al. (1998) and the direct measurement \(22.3 \pm 1.2\) eV b by Junghans et al. (2002). The analysis enables future reduction of theoretical error to \(\sim 2\%\) with full CDCC calculations in both channels, but calls for new experimental data at higher energies (a few tens of MeV) to improve the 13% experimental uncertainty.
