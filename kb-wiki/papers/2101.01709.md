# Chiral Effective Field Theory and the High-Density Nuclear Equation of State

> Born in the aftermath of core collapse supernovae, neutron stars contain matter under extraordinary conditions of density and temperature that are difficult to reproduce in the laboratory. In recent years, neutron star observations have begun to yield novel insights into the nature of strongly interacting matter in the high-density regime where current theoretical models are challenged. At the same time, chiral effective field theory has developed into a powerful framework to study nuclear matter properties with quantified uncertainties in the moderate-density regime for modeling neutron stars. In this article, we review recent developments in chiral effective field theory and focus on many-body perturbation theory as a computationally efficient tool for calculating the properties of hot and dense nuclear matter. We also demonstrate how effective field theory enables statistically meaningful comparisons between nuclear theory predictions, nuclear experiments, and observational constraints on the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) provides a systematic framework for calculating nuclear matter properties with quantified uncertainties up to about twice nuclear saturation density (2n0), but its applicability is limited by a breakdown scale of order the chiral symmetry breaking scale (~1 GeV), with a Bayesian analysis of free-space NN scattering estimating the actual breakdown scale at approximately 600 MeV.

## Method
The authors review the construction of chiral nuclear interactions using Weinberg power counting, where the most general Lagrangian consistent with low-energy QCD symmetries is expanded in powers of Q = max(p, m_pi)/Lambda_b. These interactions are then implemented in many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) at zero and finite temperature to calculate the nuclear equation of state, with renormalization group methods applied to improve many-body convergence. Bayesian methods are used to quantify and propagate theoretical uncertainties from the chiral expansion truncation.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3, with associated mass density ρ0 ≈ 2.7 × 10^14 g cm^-3
- Central densities in heaviest neutron stars may reach 5-10 n0
- ChEFT breakdown scale estimated at Λ_b ≈ 600 MeV from Bayesian analysis of free-space NN scattering
- ChEFT cutoff scale typically chosen in range 450-600 MeV
- Neutron star masses of about 1-2 solar masses with radii of approximately 10 km
- Expansion parameter Q ≈ m_pi/Λ_b ≈ 1/3

## Context
This review extends the line of work on chiral effective field theory for nuclear interactions, building on the foundational power-counting scheme of Weinberg (1990, 1991, 1992) and recent developments in Bayesian uncertainty quantification by Furnstahl (2015). It enables statistically meaningful comparisons between nuclear theory predictions, nuclear experiments, and neutron star observations in the era of multimessenger astronomy, particularly for constraining the high-density equation of state where ChEFT is no longer directly predictive.
