# Roles of proton-neutron interactions in alpha-like four-nucleon correlations

> An extended pairing plus QQ force model, which has been shown to successfully explain the nuclear binding energy and related quantities such as the symmetry energy, is applied to study the alpha-like four-nucleon correlations in 1f_{7/2} shell nuclei. The double difference of binding energies, which displays a characteristic behavior at $N \approx Z$, is interpreted in terms of the alpha-like correlations. Important roles of proton-neutron interactions forming the alpha-like correlated structure are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the characteristic peak in the double difference of binding energies at N=Z arises from alpha-like four-nucleon correlations, but this peak requires both the P0+P2+QQ force (which drives the formation of alpha-like units) and an additional isoscalar proton-neutron interaction V^{tau=0}_{pi nu} (which provides a very large energy gain to stabilize the T=0 correlated structure). Prior work by Gambhir, Ring and Schuck (1983) could not reproduce sufficient values for the double difference because they did not include such a proton-neutron interaction.

## Method

The authors used a single-j (f7/2)^n shell model with an effective Hamiltonian consisting of isospin-invariant monopole pairing (P0), quadrupole pairing (P2), quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) forces, and a J-independent isoscalar proton-neutron force V^{tau=0}_{pi nu}. They calculated interaction energies separately for the P0+P2+QQ force and V^{tau=0}_{pi nu} for isotopes with np=0, 2, and 4 (Ca, Ti, Cr isotopes) and analyzed the double difference of binding energies S'(np+2,nn+2) - S'(np+2,nn) as a measure of alpha-like correlation energy.

## Key numbers

- The approximate treatment of the (alpha_0)^m model gives energy -32.04 MeV compared to the exact energy -32.20 MeV for the (f7/2)^8 system (48Cr).
- The squared amplitude of the dominant (alpha_0)^2 component in the 48Cr ground state is 0.973.
- The approximate (alpha_0)^3 model gives -52.14 MeV compared to the exact energy -52.39 MeV for the (f7/2)^12 system.
- The overlap of the approximate state with the exact state is 0.983 for the (f7/2)^6 system and 0.987 for the (f7/2)^10 system.
- Force strengths used: g0=0.59, G2=0.9, X=1.2 (in MeV) for the P0+P2+QQ force; k0=1.9 x (48/A) MeV for the proton-neutron force.
- The single-particle base level lambda = -8.364 MeV.
- Coulomb energy correction uses coefficients: 7.279 np + 0.15 np(np-1) - 0.065 np nn (in MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous schematic interaction model (Hasegawa and Kaneko, 1999; Kaneko and Hasegawa, 1999) for N approx Z nuclei in the 1f7/2 shell region, which successfully explained nuclear binding energies and symmetry energy. It directly addresses the double difference of binding energies discussed by Zhang, Casten and Brenner (1989) and Brenner et al. (1990), and the alpha-like four-nucleon correlation interpretation of Gambhir, Ring and Schuck (1983). The results enable future studies of alpha-like correlations in full fp-shell nuclei and suggest that the coupling between alpha-like units and residual neutrons is weak, pointing toward a direct product state description.
