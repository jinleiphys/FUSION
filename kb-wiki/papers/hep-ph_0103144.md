# Watson's theorem and electromagnetism in K -> pi pi decay

> We consider what constraints unitarity and CPT invariance yield on the strong and electromagnetic phases entering K -> pi pi decay. In particular, we show that the relative size of the electromagnetically-induced changes in the I=0 and I=2 phase shifts in the two--pion final state do not depend on the explicit coupling to the pi^+ pi^- gamma channel. This demonstrates that Watson's theorem can be extended to include the presence of electromagnetism. We point out the consequences for the general structure of the K -> pi pi decay amplitudes in the presence of isospin violation.

# Watson's theorem and electromagnetism in \( K \to \pi\pi \) decay

## Key claim
Watson's theorem can be extended to include electromagnetism in \( K \to \pi\pi \) decay: the relative size of the electromagnetically-induced changes in the \( I=0 \) and \( I=2 \) phase shifts does not depend on the explicit coupling to the \( \pi^+\pi^-\gamma \) channel, provided that channel is the only inelastic channel. This result emerges as a consequence of unitarity and CPT invariance, not from numerical analysis.

## Method
The authors construct a general \( 4 \times 4 \) S-matrix for \( K^0 \) decays into three final-state channels: \( (\pi\pi)_0 \), \( (\pi\pi)_2 \), and \( \pi^+\pi^-\gamma \). The \( 3 \times 3 \) submatrix for the pion-photon system is parametrized using a unitary, T-invariant form with three mixing parameters (two angles \( \Theta_1, \Theta_2 \) and one phase \( \delta \)), analogous to bar phase shifts in nucleon-nucleon scattering. Unitarity constraints \( ({\cal S}^\dagger {\cal S})_{21} = ({\cal S}^\dagger {\cal S})_{31} = ({\cal S}^\dagger {\cal S})_{41} = 0 \) are derived to leading order in the Fermi constant \( G_F \), neglecting \( {\cal O}(G_F) \) contributions to \( \pi\pi \) scattering.

## Key numbers
- The S-wave \( \pi\pi \) phase shift difference \( \delta_0 - \delta_2 \) extracted from \( K \to \pi\pi \) decay using physical masses is about \( 57^\circ \), whereas its value from \( \pi\pi \) scattering data is about \( 45^\circ \) with an uncertainty of roughly 10%.
- The branching ratio for \( K_S \to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma \) (with photon momenta above 50 MeV/c) is roughly \( 2 \times 10^{-3} \), while the next largest measured branching ratio, \( K_S \to \gamma\gamma \), is roughly a factor of 1000 smaller.
- The ratio \( A_0/A_2 \sim 22 \) enhances the \( I=2 \) phase shift difference \( \Delta_2 \) relative to \( \Delta_0 \).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Watson's theorem by Gardner and Valencia (2000), who showed the theorem persists through leading order in the up-down quark mass difference. It is triggered by Bernstein's extension of the final-state theorem to three open channels in neutral pion photoproduction. The paper addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the \( \pi\pi \) phase shift difference extracted from \( K \to \pi\pi \) decay (\( \sim 57^\circ \)) and that from \( \pi\pi \) scattering (\( \sim 45^\circ \)), which has been attributed to isospin-violating effects studied by Cirigliano, Donoghue, and Golowich (1999-2000) and others. The framework enables future numerical determination of the \( |\Delta I| = 5/2 \) amplitude in \( K \to \pi\pi \) decay and its impact on the Standard Model prediction for Re\( (\epsilon'/\epsilon) \).
