# Causality and stability in relativistic hydrodynamic theory -- a choice to be endured

> In this work, it has been indicated that the key features requisite for preserving causality and stability of the popularly existing relativistic hydrodynamic theories, can be translated into each other. It has been shown here, that a generic `fluid frame transformation' including all orders of gradient corrections can recast a stable-causal hydrodynamic theory that (i) only includes fundamental fluid variables (velocity and temperature) but requires to be in a general hydrodynamic frame other than the Landau or Eckart, to a theory that is (ii) pathology free in Landau frame but needs newer degrees of freedom. Since frame choice provides the first principle field definitions and degrees of freedom indicate the number of conserved quantities, the causality and stability of a theory seem to require a consensus between the two unless the derivative correction goes to infinity. The key finding of this work is to indicate a connection between these different formalisms, that lead to a causal and stable hydrodynamic theory.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) theory, which is causal and stable in a general hydrodynamic frame with only fundamental fluid variables (temperature and velocity) and a finite number of derivatives, becomes either infinite-derivative or requires new non-fluid degrees of freedom when transformed to the Landau frame, indicating a fundamental tension between the desirable features of hydrodynamics: frame-based variable definitions, finite derivatives, and no extra degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors perform a fluid frame transformation of the BDNK stress tensor from a general hydrodynamic frame to the Landau frame by redefining the fluid velocity and temperature with shift variables that are linear in derivatives but encompass all orders of gradient corrections. They solve the resulting linearized partial differential equations for the shift variables using two approaches: an order-by-order derivative expansion that is summed to infinite order in temporal derivatives, and a "one go" method that sums both spatial and temporal derivatives into a closed form. The resulting nonlocal expressions are then recast into local form by introducing new non-fluid degrees of freedom, analogous to the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory.

## Context

This work extends the BDNK formalism (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, Kovtun 2017-2020) and the MIS theory (Muller 1967, Israel 1976-1979) by showing that the two approaches are connected through fluid frame transformations, contrary to their apparent differences. Previous work by Das and collaborators (2020) noted similar dynamics for temperature evolution in boost-invariant systems, but this paper provides the first mathematical framework attributing the connection to a physical origin. The analysis is agnostic about microscopic descriptions, unlike the work of Dore and collaborators (2021) which connected the formalisms via field redefinition from a microscopic viewpoint. The results enable systematic comparison of predictions between BDNK and MIS-type theories once variables are placed in the same frame, and open the question of extending the analysis to full nonlinear order and higher-derivative corrections.
