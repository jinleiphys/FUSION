# Collision Geometry and Flow in Uranium+Uranium Collisions

> Using event-by-event viscous fluid dynamics to evolve fluctuating initial density profiles from the Monte-Carlo Glauber model for U+U collisions, we report a "knee"-like structure in the elliptic flow as a function of collision centrality, located around the 0.5% most central collisions as measured by the final charged multiplicity. This knee is due to the preferential selection of tip-on-tip collision geometries by a high-multiplicity trigger. Such a knee structure is not seen in the STAR data. This rules out the two-component MC-Glauber model for initial energy and entropy production. Hence an enrichment of tip-tip configurations by triggering solely on high-multiplicity in the U+U collisions does not work. On the other hand, by using the Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDCs) coupled with event-shape engineering such a selection is possible. We identify the selection purity of body-body and tip-tip events in full-overlap U+U collisions. By additionally constraining the asymmetry of the ZDC signals we can further increase the probability of selecting tip-tip events in U+U collisions.

## Key claim

Within the two-component Monte-Carlo Glauber model, a "knee"-like structure in elliptic flow as a function of centrality is predicted for U+U collisions around the 0.5% most central events, but this structure is not seen in STAR data, ruling out that specific model for initial energy and entropy production in ultra-central U+U collisions.

## Method

The authors used event-by-event viscous relativistic fluid dynamics (iEBE-VISHNU code) to evolve fluctuating initial density profiles generated by the two-component Monte-Carlo Glauber model for U+U collisions at 193 A GeV. Initial conditions were computed from deformed Woods-Saxon distributions with quadrupole deformation β20=0.28 and hexadecupole deformation β40=0.093, using a mixing parameter α=0.12 between wounded nucleon and binary collision sources. The specific shear viscosity was set to η/s=0.08, hydrodynamic evolution started at τ0=0.6 fm/c, and decoupling occurred at Tdec=120 MeV, with final observables computed from directly emitted thermal pions.

## Key numbers

- The "knee" structure in elliptic flow v2 versus centrality is located around the 0.5% most central collisions as measured by final charged multiplicity
- Deformation parameters used: β20=0.28 and β40=0.093 for the quadrupole and hexadecupole deformations along the main axis of uranium nuclei
- Nuclear charge radius r0=6.86 fm, surface diffusiveness d=0.44 fm, saturation density ρ0=0.1660 fm−3
- Mixing ratio α=0.12 and κs=17.16 were chosen to reproduce measured charged multiplicities in Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV
- Specific shear viscosity η/s=0.08, starting time τ0=0.6 fm/c, decoupling temperature Tdec=120 MeV
- The relation between thermal pions and charged hadrons: dNch/dη ≃ 4.6 dNπ+/dy
- By cutting on high dS/dy within a 1% ZDC sample, tip-tip events can be enriched to about 50% purity; cutting on low dS/dy enriches body-body events to about 20% purity
- Cutting on low q2 or v2 gives tip-tip sample with about 25% purity, which can be boosted to about 35% by eliminating events with asymmetric ZDC signals
- Cutting on high q2 enriches body-body collisions to about 40% purity

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collision geometry in deformed uranium nuclei (Shuryak 1999, Kolb 2000, Heinz 2004, Kuhlman 2005) and directly tests the two-component MC-Glauber model against STAR data from the 2012 U+U run at RHIC (Wang 2014). The failure of the knee prediction rules out the binary collision component as implemented in the two-component MC-Glauber model, while alternative models such as IP-Glasma (Schenke 2014) and wounded-constituent-quark Glauber (Adler 2013) can potentially accommodate the data. The results enable future experimental selection of tip-tip and body-body collision geometries using combined ZDC and event-shape engineering, which could discriminate between different initial-state models.
