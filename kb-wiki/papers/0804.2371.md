# Constraints on the IR behavior of the ghost propagator in Yang-Mills theories

> We present rigorous upper and lower bounds for the momentum-space ghost propagator G(p) of Yang-Mills theories in terms of the smallest nonzero eigenvalue (and of the corresponding eigenvector) of the Faddeev-Popov matrix. We apply our analysis to data from simulations of SU(2) lattice gauge theory in Landau gauge, using the largest lattice sizes to date. Our results suggest that, in three and in four space-time dimensions, the Landau-gauge ghost propagator is not enhanced as compared to its tree-level behavior. This is also seen in plots and fits of the ghost dressing function. In the two dimensional case, on the other hand, we find that G(p) diverges as p^{-2-2 kappa} with kappa \approx 0.15, in agreement with Ref. [1]. We note that our discussion is general, although we make an application only to pure gauge theory in Landau gauge. Most of our simulations have been performed on the IBM supercomputer at the University of Sao Paulo.

## Key claim

The Landau-gauge ghost propagator in three and four space-time dimensions is likely not enhanced compared to its tree-level behavior (i.e., does not diverge faster than 1/p²), contradicting the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario; this conclusion is based on rigorous upper and lower bounds relating the propagator to the smallest nonzero eigenvalue of the Faddeev-Popov matrix, with the caveat that the analysis applies only to pure SU(2) gauge theory in Landau gauge.

## Method

The authors derived rigorous upper and lower bounds for the momentum-space ghost propagator G(p) in terms of the smallest nonzero eigenvalue λ_min and the corresponding eigenvector of the Faddeev-Popov matrix. They applied these bounds to data from lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge, using volumes up to 320² in 2d, 320³ in 3d, and 128⁴ in 4d, and performed power-law fits of G(p_s) and 1/λ_min as functions of the lattice side L.

## Key numbers

- In 2d, the ghost propagator diverges as p^{-2-2κ} with κ ≈ 0.15, in agreement with Maas (2007); fits of G(p_s) vs. L give 2+2κ = 2.251(9) and α = 2.20(4) for the exponent of 1/λ_min.
- In 3d, fits of G(p_s) vs. L give 2+2κ = 2.02(4); the dressing function p²G(p²) tends to approximately 4.5 as p→0.
- In 4d, fits of G(p_s) vs. L give 2+2κ = 2.043(8) and α = 1.53(2) for 1/λ_min; the dressing function p²G(p²) tends to approximately 4.5 as p→0.
- In 2d, the dressing function fit yields κ = 0.177(2) over p² ∈ [0,0.5] GeV², decreasing to κ = 0.152(7) when restricting to p² ∈ [0,0.1] GeV².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cucchieri (2006) by providing rigorous bounds that constrain the infrared behavior of the ghost propagator, and it directly tests the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario and the Kugo-Ojima criterion, which require an enhanced ghost propagator. The results contradict earlier numerical studies on smaller lattices (Cucchieri 1997) that suggested IR enhancement in 3d and 4d, but agree with recent large-volume studies (Cucchieri 2007, Sternbeck 2007) and analytic approaches (Boucaud) that find a tree-level-like ghost propagator in 3d and 4d. The 2d result agrees with Maas (2007). The findings enable future work to focus on whether the nonperturbative effects observed at intermediate momenta (200-500 MeV) suffice to explain color confinement, rather than requiring extreme infrared enhancement.
