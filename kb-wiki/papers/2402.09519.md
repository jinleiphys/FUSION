# Nonmonotonic specific entropy on the transition line near the QCD critical point

> We investigate the effect of the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) critical point on the isentropic trajectories in the QCD phase diagram. We point out that the universality of the critical equation of state and the third law of thermodynamics require the specific entropy (per baryon) along the coexistence (first-order transition) line to be nonmonotonic at least on one side of that line. Specifically, a maximum must occur. We show how the location of the maximum relative to the QCD critical point depends on the parameters of the critical equation of state commonly used in the literature. We then examine how the isentropic trajectories followed by adiabatically expanding heavy-ion collision fireballs behave near the critical point. We find that a crucial role is played by the sign of the isochoric temperature derivative of pressure at the critical point; this sign determines on which side of the coexistence curve the specific entropy must be nonmonotonic (i.e., has a maximum). We classify different scenarios of the adiabatic expansion that arise depending on the value of the discriminant parameter and the proximity of the trajectory to the critical point.

## Key claim

The specific entropy (entropy per baryon) along the first-order phase transition line must exhibit a maximum on at least one side of the coexistence line, a result that follows from the combination of universal critical scaling and the third law of thermodynamics, with the side of the nonmonotonic behavior determined by the sign of the isochoric temperature derivative of pressure at the critical point.

## Method

The authors use the universal equation of state of the 3D Ising model, mapped onto the QCD phase diagram via a linear transformation with parameters \(w\), \(\rho\), \(\alpha_1\), and \(\alpha_2\) following Parotto (2018), to compute the specific entropy \(\hat s = s/n\) near the QCD critical point. They expand \(\hat s\) along the coexistence line in terms of the leading singular contribution \(\propto (-r)^\beta\) and the subleading singular contribution \(\propto (-r)^{1-\alpha}\), and determine the location of the maximum by balancing these two terms. The analysis is applied to two parameter sets from the BEST Collaboration equation of state, and the isentropic trajectories of adiabatically expanding heavy-ion collision fireballs are classified based on the discriminant parameter and trajectory proximity to the critical point.

## Key numbers

* The critical exponent \(\beta \approx 0.326\) and \(\alpha \approx 0.110\) for the 3D Ising universality class.
* For parameter Set 1 (Parotto 2018 benchmark): \(|r_{\rm max}| = 9.5 \times 10^{-4}\) and \(\hat{s}_{\rm max} - \hat{s}_\crit = 0.076\), with \(\hat s_\crit \approx 20.3\).
* For parameter Set 2 (small \(\alpha_2\) motivated by Pradeep 2019): \(|r_{\rm max}| = 2.5 \times 10^{-2}\) and \(\hat{s}_{\rm max} - \hat{s}_\crit = 0.89\), with \(\hat s_\crit \approx 19.3\).
* The scaling function value \(\tilde\Gr(0) \approx -0.66\).
* The angle \(\alpha_1 \approx 3.85^\circ\) for the benchmark choice, giving \(\cot\alpha_1 \approx 14.9\).

## Context

This work extends the universal critical equation of state framework of Parotto (2018) by demonstrating that the specific entropy along the coexistence line must be nonmonotonic, a feature not previously emphasized in QCD critical point studies. The result contrasts with the simple reheating scenario of Stephanov (1998) and is consistent with observations in some models without confinement, such as Scavenius (2000). The classification of isentropic trajectories, including those that enter the coexistence region between the critical point and its "critical double," enables more precise predictions for adiabatic expansion in heavy-ion collisions and connects to trajectories seen in the \((s, n)\) plane by Akamatsu (2018) and on the \((\mu, T)\) plane by Nonaka (2004) and Dore (2022).
