# Chirality in odd-$A$ nucleus $^{135}$Nd in particle rotor model

> A particle rotor model is developed which couples several valence protons and neutrons to a rigid triaxial rotor core. It is applied to investigating the chirality in odd-$A$ nucleus $^{135}$Nd with $πh_{11/2}^2\otimesνh^{-1}_{11/2}$ configuration for the first time in a fully quantal approach. For the two chiral sister bands, the observed energies and the $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ values for the in-band as well as interband transitions are reproduced excellently. Root mean square values of the angular momentum components and their probability distributions are used for discussing in detail the chiral geometry of the aplanar rotation and its evolution with angular momentum. Chirality is found to change from a soft chiral vibration to nearly static chirality at spin $I=39/2$ and back to another type of chiral vibration at higher spin.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chirality in the odd-\(A\) nucleus \(^{135}\)Nd is a transient phenomenon, changing from a soft chiral vibration to nearly static chirality at spin \(I=39/2\) and back to another type of chiral vibration at higher spin, rather than being a static property across all spins.

## Method

A fully quantal particle rotor model (PRM) was developed that couples several valence protons and neutrons to a rigid triaxial rotor core. The model was applied to the \(\pi h_{11/2}^2\otimes\nu h^{-1}_{11/2}\) configuration in \(^{135}\)Nd, using deformation parameters \(\beta=0.235\) and \(\gamma=22.4^{\circ}\) from a self-consistent triaxial relativistic mean field calculation, with the moment of inertia \({\cal J}_0=29.0\) MeV\(/\hbar^2\) adjusted to experimental energy spectra. The root mean square values of angular momentum components and their probability distributions were used to analyze the chiral geometry and its evolution with angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters: \(\beta=0.235\) and \(\gamma=22.4^{\circ}\) for \(^{135}\)Nd, obtained from microscopic self-consistent triaxial relativistic mean field calculation.
- Single-particle energy coefficients: \(C_p=0.323\) MeV and \(C_n=-0.323\) MeV.
- Moment of inertia: \({\cal J}_0=29.0\) MeV\(/\hbar^2\).
- Empirical intrinsic quadrupole moment: \(Q_0=4.0\) eb.
- Gyromagnetic ratios: \(g_R=Z/A=0.44\), \(g_p=1.21\), \(g_n=-0.21\).
- Maximal chirality (smallest energy difference between doublet bands) occurs at spin \(I=39/2\), with a remaining energy difference of 76 keV between the bands.
- At spin \(I=39/2\), the probability distributions peak at \(K_l=7\), \(K_i=17\), \(K_s=17\).
- The valence neutron hole angular momentum \(\mathbf{J_n} \sim 5\hbar\) aligns mainly along the long axis, and the two valence protons \(\mathbf{J_p} \sim 10\hbar\) lie in the plane spanned by the intermediate and short axes.
- The ratios between moments of inertia for \(\gamma=22.4^{\circ}\): \({\cal J}_i: {\cal J}_s: {\cal J}_l =6.8: 2.6: 1.0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear chirality from odd-odd nuclei to odd-\(A\) nuclei using a fully quantal particle rotor model, going beyond the tilted axis cranking plus random phase approximation (TAC+RPA) approach used by Mukhopadhyay (2007) and Almehed (2008), which could not describe the smooth transition from chiral vibration to quantum tunneling. The PRM reproduces experimental energy spectra and electromagnetic transitions for the doublet bands in \(^{135}\)Nd better than previous TAC+RPA calculations, and the success encourages further application to other nuclei including even-even nuclei, potentially settling disputes on the identification and interpretation of chiral doublet bands based on one-particle-one-hole configurations in odd-odd nuclei.
