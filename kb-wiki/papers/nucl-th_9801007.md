# Parton Cascade Description of Heavy-Ion Collisions at CERN ?

> There seems to be a general consensus now that a first glimpse of a QGP-like effect has become visible in the beautiful NA50 data on J/ψproduction and the `anomalous supression' phenomenon. On the other hand, it is still widely believed that the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energy is predominantly governed by soft, non-perturbative physics. This is ironic: after all, it is unlikely that a QGP could be formed if the underlying dynamics were essentyially soft, rather than that it requires intense quark-gluon production with sufficient cascade-like reinteractions which drive the matter to large density and equilibrium. Therefore, I advocate in this contribution that for ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions a description based on the pQCD interactions and cascade evolution of involved partons can and should be used, owing to the claim that short-range parton interactions play an important role at sufficiently high beam energies, including CERN energy sqrt(s) = 20 A GeV. Here mini-jet production which liberates of quarks and gluons cannot be considered as an isolated rare phenomenon, but can occur quite copiously and may lead to complex multiple cascade-type processes.

# Parton Cascade Description of Heavy-Ion Collisions at CERN

## Key claim

A pQCD-based parton cascade description (the Parton Cascade Model, PCM, implemented in the VNI program) can be applied to heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energies (sqrt(s) ≈ 17–20 A GeV), despite the widespread belief that soft non-perturbative physics dominates at these energies; the author advocates that short-range parton interactions and mini-jet production play an important role even at SPS energies, and that the model provides a good description of experimental data without parameter adjustments.

## Method

The author used the VNI Monte Carlo implementation of the Parton Cascade Model (PCM), which traces nuclear collisions from initial overlap through pQCD parton-cascade evolution, parton conversion into pre-hadronic color-singlet clusters, hadron production via cluster decays, and beam remnant fragmentation. The model parameters were previously fixed by pp (ppbar) cross-section data over sqrt(s) = 10–1800 GeV and e+e- annihilation data, with no fine-tuning to SPS heavy-ion data. The author then compared PCM simulations to CERN SPS data for S+S and Pb+Pb collisions, and extended the analysis to J/psi suppression via comover absorption.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy at CERN SPS is sqrt(s) ≈ 17–20 A GeV.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, hard parton production and cascading provides almost 50% of final particles around mid-rapidity.
- The energy density produced by scattering partons at central rapidity grows by a factor of more than 2 between S+U and Pb+Pb collisions, compatible with scaling epsilon ~ (A1 A2)^(1/3).
- In Pb+Pb collisions, the initial partonic energy density epsilon is predicted to reach 5 GeV/fm^3 at times tau < 1 fm/c in the comoving reference frame.
- For a thermalized gas of free gluons and three light quark flavors, this corresponds to an initial temperature Ti ≈ 230 MeV.
- The critical temperature from lattice-QCD is Tc ≈ 150 MeV.
- The dissociation temperature for J/psi due to color screening is TD ≈ 1.2 Tc = 180–200 MeV.
- For S+U collisions, the predicted parton density epsilon ≈ 2 GeV/fm^3 lies at the upper end of the lattice-QCD transition region.
- The typical Bjorken-x probed at SPS is ⟨x⟩ >~ 0.1, where the gluon density is small.

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model (PCM), previously applied to RHIC and LHC energies, downward to CERN SPS energies, comparing with data from the NA50 and NA49 collaborations. The author argues that the PCM description contradicts the common Glauber model approach (which predicts epsilon scaling as A1^(1/3) + A2^(1/3) and only a 30% increase between S+U and Pb+Pb), and instead supports a stronger A^(2/3) dependence for comover density that is compatible with the observed J/psi suppression pattern. The results enable a pQCD-based interpretation of the NA50 anomalous J/psi suppression data and suggest that mini-jet production at SPS is dominated by quark-quark scattering, making shadowing effects negligible for pT >= 1 GeV.
