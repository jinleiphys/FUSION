# Three-body force effect on neutrino emissivities of neutron stars within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> The three-body force (TBF) effect on the neutrino emissivity in neutron star matter and the total neutrino emissivity of neutron stars have been investigated within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach by adopting the AV18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic TBF. The neutrino emissivity from the direct Urca process turns out to be much larger than that from the modified Urca process. Inclusion of the TBF reduces strongly the density thresholds of the direct Urca processes involving electrons and muons. The TBF effect on the total neutrino emissivity of neutron stars is shown to be negligibly weak for neutron stars with small masses. For neutron stars with large masses, the TBF effect becomes visible and inclusion of the TBF may enhance the total neutrino emissivity by about 50% for neutron stars with a given mass of $M=1.6M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim
Inclusion of a microscopic three-body force (TBF) within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach reduces the density thresholds for direct Urca processes involving electrons and muons, but the resulting enhancement of the total neutrino emissivity of a neutron star is only about 50% for a star of mass 1.6 solar masses, and is negligible for low-mass stars.

## Method
The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix using the Argonne AV18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic TBF constructed via the meson-exchange current approach. They then computed the equation of state of beta-stable npeμ matter, the symmetry energy, and the proton fraction, and used these to calculate neutrino emissivities from the direct Urca and modified Urca processes. Finally, they solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star density profiles and integrated the emissivity over the star to obtain total neutrino emissivities for stars of different masses.

## Key numbers
- Threshold density for the electron direct Urca process: reduced from 0.67 fm⁻³ (AV18 alone) to 0.38 fm⁻³ (AV18 + TBF).
- Threshold density for the muon direct Urca process: reduced from 0.76 fm⁻³ (AV18 alone) to 0.42 fm⁻³ (AV18 + TBF).
- For a neutron star of mass 1.6 solar masses, inclusion of the TBF enhances the total neutrino emissivity by about 50%.
- For a neutron star of mass 1.2 solar masses, the TBF effect on total neutrino emissivity is negligibly small.
- Radius range where direct Urca processes are allowed (R^DU) for a 1.6 solar mass star: 6.40 km (AV18 alone) vs. 7.09 km (AV18 + TBF), an increase of about 10%.
- Central density of a 1.6 solar mass star: 1.19 fm⁻³ (AV18 alone) vs. 0.50 fm⁻³ (AV18 + TBF).

## Context
This work extends previous BHF studies of neutron star structure by Zhou (2004) and Li (2008), which showed that TBF increases the maximum neutron star mass. It also builds on the standard cooling scenario based on the modified Urca process (Chiu, 1964; Friman, 1979; Maxwell, 1987) and the non-standard direct Urca process (Lattimer, 1991; Prakash, 1992). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling curves by quantifying how TBF affects the onset density of rapid neutrino emission, particularly for massive stars.
