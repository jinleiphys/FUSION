# Renormalization group analysis of resonantly interacting anyons

> We formulate a field theory for resonantly interacting anyons, that enables us to perform a perturbative calculation near the fermionic limit. We derive renormalization group equations for three-body and four-body couplings at one-loop order. In addition to two fixed points, we find a limit cycle behavior in the four-body coupling, which implies an infinite set of bound states in the four-anyon system.

# Renormalization group analysis of resonantly interacting anyons

## Key claim
Near the fermionic limit (statistics parameter |α| ≪ 1), a field theory of anyons with two-body contact interaction tuned to resonance can be treated perturbatively; the renormalization group equations for three-body and four-body couplings reveal a limit cycle in the four-body coupling, implying an infinite set of discrete bound states in the four-anyon system, but only when both two-body and three-body interactions are simultaneously tuned to resonance.

## Method
The author formulates a nonrelativistic field theory of fermionic fields ψ minimally coupled to a Chern-Simons gauge field, plus a bosonic dimer field φ representing a p-wave bound pair of anyons. The two-body coupling g is tuned to a nontrivial fixed point g*² = 2π|α| that describes anyons at the two-body resonance. One-loop renormalization group equations are then derived for the three-body coupling v₃ and four-body coupling v₄, with the statistics parameter α serving as a small expansion parameter.

## Key numbers
- Fixed point for two-body coupling: g*² = 2π|α|, which is O(α) and thus small near the fermionic limit |α| ≪ 1
- Two fixed points for three-body coupling: v₃* = π|α| (stable, regular wave function) and v₃* = 10π|α| (unstable, three-body resonance)
- When v₃ is at the stable fixed point π|α|, the four-body coupling has fixed points at v₄* = −2π|α| (stable) and v₄* = 4π|α| (unstable, four-body resonance)
- When v₃ is at the three-body resonance fixed point 10π|α|, the four-body coupling exhibits a limit cycle with primitive period π/(6|α|) in RG time s
- Ratio of successive four-anyon binding energies near the fermionic limit: E_{n+1}/E_n → exp[−π/(3|α| + O(α²))]
- Ground state energy of two anyons in a harmonic potential: E = (1 + |α|)ω, matching the exact quantum mechanical result

## Context
This work extends the study of anyons with contact interactions beyond the bosonic limit, where previous field-theoretical treatments (Bergman 1993, Amelino-Camelia 1994) allowed perturbative calculations. The author draws an analogy to two-component fermions at infinite scattering length near four spatial dimensions (Nussinov 2006, Nishida 2006, Nikolic-Sachdev 2007). The predicted limit cycle and geometric spectrum of four-anyon bound states resemble Efimov states in three-body systems at infinite scattering length in three dimensions (Efimov 1970, Bedaque 1998), recently observed in cold atoms (Kraemer 2006). The work enables future perturbative studies of many-body properties of resonantly interacting anyons, including the ground state energy ratio ξ = E/E₀ near the fermionic limit and possible superfluidity.
