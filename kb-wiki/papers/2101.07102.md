# Diffuseness parameter as a bottleneck for accurate half-life calculations

> An investigation of the calculated $α$ decay half-lives of super heavy nuclei (SHN) reveals that the diffuseness parameter is a great bottleneck for achieving accurate results and predictions. In particular, when universal proximity function is adopted for nuclear potential, half-life is found to vary significantly and nonlinearly as a function of diffuseness parameter. To overcome this limiting hurdle, a new semiempirical formula for diffuseness that is dependent on charge and neutron numbers is proposed in this work. With the model at hand, half-lives of 218 SHN are computed, for 68 of which there exists available experimental data and 150 of which are predicted. The calculations of half-lives for 68 SHN are compared against experimental data and the calculated data obtained by using deformed Woods-Saxon, deformed Coulomb potentials model, and six semiempirical formulas. The predictions of 150 SHN are compared against the predictions of seven of the current best semiempirical formulas. Calculations of the present study are in good agreement with the experimental half-lives outperforming all but ImSahu semiempirical formula. Moreover, the predictions of our model are consistent with predictions of the semiempirical formulas. We strongly conclude that more attention should be directed toward obtaining accurate diffuseness parameter values for using it in nuclear calculations.

# Diffuseness parameter as a bottleneck for accurate half-life calculations

## Key claim

The diffuseness parameter in the universal proximity potential causes calculated alpha decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei to vary significantly and nonlinearly, unlike the linear variation seen with Woods-Saxon potentials. The authors propose a new semiempirical formula for effective diffuseness dependent on charge and neutron numbers, and their model outperforms all but the ImSahu semiempirical formula when compared against experimental data for 68 superheavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors adopted the universal proximity potential for the nuclear interaction between the alpha particle and daughter nucleus, assuming spherical symmetry with no deformation effects and no angular momentum. They used the WKB approximation to calculate half-lives, with a preformation factor formula from the literature, and mapped Woods-Saxon diffuseness values from prior work into effective diffuseness values for the proximity potential using the ansatz a_eff = (a_alpha + a_WS)/2 with a_alpha = 0.3238 fm. A linear fit to the effective diffuseness versus charge and neutron numbers yielded a new semiempirical formula, which was then used to predict half-lives for 150 superheavy nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For nucleus 256Rf, varying a_eff from 0.22 to 0.61 fm changed log10(T_1/2) from 2.1 to -0.1, a change of two orders of magnitude in half-life
- Varying a_eff by 0.1 fm from 0.22 to 0.32 fm for 256Rf induced a change of 0.457 in log10(T_1/2); varying from 0.5 to 0.6 fm induced a change of 0.69
- The proposed semiempirical formula is a_eff = -1.09535 + 0.012063 Z + 0.0019759 N, with an rms error of 0.065 fm from true a_eff values
- The rms error of log10(T_1/2) for the Prox model over 68 superheavy nuclei was 0.41, compared to 0.79 for the deformed Woods-Saxon model with constant diffuseness a_WS = 0.54 fm
- The mean absolute deviation for Prox was 0.36, versus 0.67 for the Woods-Saxon model
- The difference between maximum and minimum error for Prox was 1.65, versus 3.44 for the Woods-Saxon model

## Context

This work extends the investigation by Dehghani et al. (2020), who studied the role of the diffuseness parameter in deformed Woods-Saxon and deformed Coulomb potentials for 68 superheavy nuclei and found that a constant a = 0.54 fm minimized rms error to 0.787. The present study generalizes these results to the universal proximity potential, finding that the half-life varies nonlinearly with diffuseness (unlike the linear behavior in the Woods-Saxon case) and that the constant diffuseness approximation is particularly problematic for systems with a_eff greater than 0.5 fm. The new semiempirical formula enables half-life predictions for 150 superheavy nuclei without requiring experimental data, and the results are compared against seven semiempirical formulas including Viola-Seaborg, Royer, ImSahu, SemFIS, and UNIV. The authors conclude that accurate diffuseness values are more important than including deformation effects for half-life calculations.
