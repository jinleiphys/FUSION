# Nonperturbative renormalization of the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction up to next-to-next-to-leading order

> We study the nonperturbative renormalization of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) of chiral effective field theory. A systematic variation of the cutoff parameter is performed for values below the chiral symmetry breaking scale of about 1 GeV. The accuracy of the predictions is determined by calculating the $χ^2$ for the reproduction of the NN data for energy intervals below pion-production threshold. At NLO, the NN data are described well up to about 100 MeV laboratory energy and, at NNLO, up to about 200 MeV---with, essentially, cutoff independence for cutoffs between about 450 and 850 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonperturbative renormalization of the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction works well for cutoffs between about 450 and 850 MeV, but only when the cutoff is kept below the chiral symmetry breaking scale of about 1 GeV, contrary to approaches that take the cutoff to infinity.

## Method

The authors constructed chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using Weinberg counting, then solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation nonperturbatively with a regulator function f(p',p) = exp[-(p'/Lambda)^(2n)-(p/Lambda)^(2n)]. They varied the cutoff parameter Lambda from about 350 to 900 MeV, refitting the S-wave and P-wave counterterms at each cutoff value, and quantified the accuracy by calculating chi-squared per datum for reproduction of neutron-proton elastic scattering data in several energy intervals below pion-production threshold.

## Key numbers

- At NLO, chi-squared per datum for the 2-35 MeV energy bin ranges from 1.03 (Lambda=500, 600 MeV) to 3.33 (Lambda=900 MeV) with SFR parameter 700 MeV
- At NLO, chi-squared per datum for the 35-125 MeV bin reaches a minimum of 3.98 at Lambda=700 MeV, rising to 6.93 at Lambda=900 MeV
- At NNLO, chi-squared per datum for the 2-35 MeV bin is 0.87 for Lambda values from 500 to 850 MeV with SFR parameter 700 MeV
- At NNLO, chi-squared per datum for the 35-125 MeV bin reaches a minimum of 1.88 at Lambda=600 MeV
- At NNLO, chi-squared per datum for the 125-183 MeV bin reaches a minimum of 4.33 at Lambda=600 MeV
- At NNLO, chi-squared per datum for the 183-290 MeV bin reaches a minimum of 18.0 at Lambda=600 MeV
- The cutoff independence is essentially achieved for cutoffs between about 450 and 850 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of applying chiral effective field theory to nuclear forces as developed by Weinberg (1990) and others, and directly addresses the renormalization problems identified by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996). It contrasts with the infinite-cutoff renormalization approach of Nogga et al. (2005) and Valderrama (2011), which the authors argue produces interactions with a wound integral of about 40% in nuclear matter, making them impractical for many-body calculations. The paper follows the philosophy of Lepage (1997) that cutoffs should be kept below the hard scale of the effective theory, and enables the construction of chiral potentials that are both renormalized and usable in few- and many-body nuclear structure calculations.
