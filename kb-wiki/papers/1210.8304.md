# Causality constraints for charged particles

> In quantum systems with short-range interactions, causality imposes nontrivial constraints on low-energy scattering parameters. We investigate these causality constraints for systems where a long-range Coulomb potential is present in addition to a short-range interaction. The main result is an upper bound for the Coulomb-modified effective range parameter. We discuss the implications of this bound to the effective field theory for nuclear halo systems. In particular, we consider several examples of proton--nucleus and nucleus--nucleus scattering. For the bound-state regime, we find relations for the asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) of nuclear halo states. As an application of these relations, we extract the ANCs of the excited 2+ and 1- states in O-16 from alpha--C-12 scattering data.

## Key claim

For systems with both short-range interactions and a long-range Coulomb potential, causality imposes an upper bound on the Coulomb-modified effective range parameter, which can be used to define a "causal range" that serves as a diagnostic tool for the convergence of halo effective field theory calculations.

## Method

The authors derive causality bounds for two charged particles with an arbitrary finite-range interaction by extending the Wronskian identity approach of Hammer and Lee (2010) to include Coulomb effects. They construct zero-energy solutions of the Coulomb Schrodinger equation using modified Bessel functions (for repulsive Coulomb) or ordinary Bessel functions (for attractive Coulomb), then compute the function b_l^C(r) that appears in the bound r_l^C <= b_l^C(r) for all r >= R. The causal range R_c is defined as the value of r where b_l^C(R_c) - r_l^C = 0.

## Key numbers

- For the constant term Z_l in the causality bound function, Z_0 = 0, Z_1 = gamma*(1/6 - 2*gamma_E/9), and Z_2 = gamma^3*(79/21600 - gamma_E/360), where gamma_E = 0.577216... is the Euler-Mascheroni constant and gamma = 2*mu*alpha*Z1*Z2 is the Coulomb parameter.
- The causality bound for S-wave (l=0) is given by b_0^C(r) expressed in terms of hypergeometric and Meijer G-functions, with explicit forms provided for both repulsive and attractive Coulomb cases.
- The derivative of b_l^C(r) is non-negative: d/dr b_l^C(r) = 2*(g_0(r) - (1/a_l^C)*f_0(r))^2.

## Context

This work extends the causality bounds previously derived by Wigner (1955) for finite-range interactions and by Phillips and Cohen (1996) for S-wave scattering, as well as the general treatment by Hammer and Lee (2010) for arbitrary angular momentum without Coulomb. The results are directly applicable to halo effective field theory (halo EFT), which has been used to describe neutron-alpha scattering (Bertulani 2002, Bedaque 2003), alpha-alpha scattering (Higa 2008), and bound single-neutron halo systems such as 11Be (Hammer 2011) and 8Li (Rupak 2011, Fernando 2011). The causality bounds enable estimation of the natural ultraviolet cutoff of the effective theory as R_c^{-1}, and provide constraints on asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for nuclear halo states, with application to extracting ANCs of excited 2+ and 1- states in 16O from alpha-12C scattering data.
