# Vortical fluid and $Λ$ spin correlations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Fermions become polarized in a vortical fluid due to spin-vorticity coupling. The spin polarization density is proportional to the local fluid vorticity at the next-to-leading order of a gradient expansion in a quantum kinetic theory. Spin correlations of two $Λ$-hyperons can therefore reveal the vortical structure of the dense matter in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We employ a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from A MultiPhase Transport (AMPT) model to calculate the vorticity distributions and $Λ$ spin correlations. The azimuthal correlation of the transverse spin is shown to have a cosine form plus an offset due to a circular structure of the transverse vorticity around the beam direction and global spin polarization. The longitudinal spin correlation shows a structure of vortex-pairing in the transverse plane due to the convective flow of hot spots in the radial direction. The dependence on colliding energy, rapidity, centrality and sensitivity to the shear viscosity are also investigated.

## Key claim

The spin polarization density of fermions in a vortical fluid is proportional to the local fluid vorticity at next-to-leading order in a gradient expansion, and this relationship allows the vortical structure of the quark-gluon plasma to be mapped via azimuthal correlations of Lambda hyperon spins, but the authors neglect magnetic field effects and assume zero baryon chemical potential, limiting predictions to high-energy collisions.

## Method

The authors employed the (3+1)D CLVisc viscous hydrodynamic model with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from the AMPT model, using the Kurganov-Tadmor algorithm for hydrodynamic evolution and Cooper-Frye particlization on GPUs. They calculated the vorticity distributions from the hydrodynamic evolution and then computed the spin polarization vector for Lambda hyperons at freeze-out using the Cooper-Frye formalism, integrating over the freeze-out hypersurface at temperature 137 MeV. The spin correlations of two Lambdas were computed as functions of the azimuthal angle difference of their momenta, with the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio set to 0.08 or 0.0.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 137 MeV
- Initial thermalization time: tau_0 = 0.4 fm at RHIC energies, tau_0 = 0.2 fm at LHC energies
- Grid spacing: Delta x = Delta y = 0.1 fm, Delta eta = 0.1, with 301 x 301 x 181 grid points
- Time step: Delta tau = 0.005 fm
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios studied: eta_v/s = 0.08 and 0.0
- Collision systems and energies: Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 and 200 GeV; Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Centralities studied: semi-peripheral (20-30%) and central (0-5%)
- Rapidity ranges for spin correlations: |Y| in [2,3] for transverse, |Y| in [0,1] for longitudinal
- Spatial rapidity for vorticity plots: eta = 4, proper time tau = 3 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of fermion polarization in vortical fluids by Liang (2004), Becattini (2013), and Fang (2016), who established the spin-vorticity coupling mechanism and the next-to-leading order gradient expansion result. It also builds on the Chiral Vortical Effect framework of Kharzeev (2007) and Fukushima (2008). The paper enables experimental measurements of Lambda spin correlations at RHIC and LHC to map the vortical structure of the quark-gluon plasma, providing constraints on initial conditions and transport properties such as shear viscosity.
