# Low-energy dipole strength in 8He

> In this work, we present new ab initio coupled-cluster calculations of dipole-excited state properties of 8He based on the chiral effective field theory interaction 1.8/2.0 (EM). We focus on the dipole polarizability, and compare the results to our previous study [Phys. Rev. C 105, 034313 (2022)] and subsequent theoretical work. With the aim of connecting the presence of low-lying dipole strength to structure properties of 8He, we compute the point-neutron radius, finding excellent agreement with available experimental data, and investigate its correlation with the dipole polarizability.

## Key claim

New coupled-cluster calculations with the chiral interaction 1.8/2.0 (EM) confirm the presence of low-lying dipole strength in 8He at around 5 MeV, but the dipole polarizability shows significant model dependence across different chiral forces, with the 1.8/2.0 (EM) prediction being more than 20% higher than the NNLOsat result.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio coupled-cluster calculations using the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method combined with equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) theory, employing either the CCSD or CCSDT-1 approximation schemes. They computed the discretized dipole response function and the dipole polarizability for 8He using the chiral effective field theory interaction 1.8/2.0 (EM), which contains SRG-evolved two-nucleon forces at N3LO and three-body forces at NNLO. They also calculated the point-neutron radius and investigated its correlation with the dipole polarizability across four different chiral interactions.

## Key numbers

- Dipole polarizability for 1.8/2.0 (EM): 0.48(3) fm3
- Dipole polarizability for ΔNNLOGO(450): 0.42(3) fm3
- Dipole polarizability for ΔNNLOGO(394): 0.40(3) fm3
- Dipole polarizability for NNLOsat: 0.37(3) fm3
- RPA predictions for dipole polarizability range from 0.220 to 0.262 fm3 across three relativistic mean-field functionals
- EMPM prediction for dipole polarizability with NNLOsat: 0.206 fm3
- Low-lying dipole strength appears at around 5 MeV for the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction
- Theoretical uncertainty for coupled-cluster results ranges between 6% and 8% of the central value for different interactions
- Theoretical uncertainty for point-neutron radius predictions varies between 1% and 2% for different interactions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-cluster study (Bonaiti 2022) by adding calculations with the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction, which includes higher-order chiral contributions than the NNLO forces used previously. The results support the presence of a soft dipole mode also found by cluster orbital shell model (Myo 2022) and equation-of-motion multiphonon approach (De Gregorio 2023) calculations, but contradict random-phase approximation within density functional theory (Piekarewicz 2022) which attributes such strength to spurious center-of-mass contaminations. The correlation found between dipole polarizability and point-neutron radius parallels similar correlations observed in medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei like 48Ca (Simonis 2019) and 68Ni (Kaufmann 2020), and the new Coulomb excitation data from RIKEN (Lehr 2022) are expected to resolve the ongoing experimental controversy about the soft dipole mode.
