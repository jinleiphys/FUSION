# Spin alignment of vector mesons in heavy-ion collision

> We give a brief review on the spin alignment induced by the strong field and the shear-stress tensor. In experiments, a significant positive deviation from 1/3 is observed for the $ϕ$ meson, which can be explained by the anisotropy of the strong field fluctuation in the meson's rest frame, while the anisotropy is mainly a consequence of the motion of meson relative to the quark-gluon plasma. On the other hand, the shear-induced spin alignment is of the order $10^{-4}\sim10^{-5}$ if the magnitude of thermal shear tensor is $10^{-2}$.

## Key claim

The observed significant positive deviation from 1/3 for the $\phi$ meson spin alignment can be explained by the anisotropy of strong field fluctuations in the meson's rest frame, where this anisotropy is mainly a consequence of the meson's motion relative to the quark-gluon plasma; the shear-induced contribution is orders of magnitude smaller.

## Method

The authors review two theoretical approaches: a relativistic quark coalescence model with spin degrees of freedom, where the spin alignment of vector mesons is derived from the spin polarizations of constituent quarks and antiquarks, and a linear response theory based on the Kubo formula, where the spin alignment is calculated as a response to the thermal shear tensor. For the strong field mechanism, the vector $\phi$ field captures the strong interaction between strange quarks in the surrounding medium and the constituent quarks inside the $\phi$ meson, with field fluctuations treated as parameters fitted to STAR data. For the shear mechanism, spectral functions for longitudinally and transversely polarized $\phi$ mesons are parameterized by finite width and energy shift, evaluated numerically in a quark-meson interaction model.

## Key numbers

- The shear-induced spin alignment is of order $10^{-4}\sim10^{-5}$ if the magnitude of the thermal shear tensor is $10^{-2}$.
- The electromagnetic field contribution to spin alignment is $\mathcal{O}(10^{-3}\sim 10^{-4})$, much smaller than experimental observations.
- The strong force field coupling constant $\alpha_\phi=g_\phi^2/(4\pi)\sim \mathcal{O}(1)$ is much larger than $\alpha_\text{EM}=e^2/(4\pi)\approx1/137$.
- Components of the coefficient $C^{\mu\nu}$ for shear-induced spin alignment are of order $10^{-2}$ or smaller for the whole considered momentum region at temperature $T=158.4$ MeV and chemical potential $\mu_s=7.4$ MeV (freezeout conditions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=200$ GeV).

## Context

This work reviews and extends the authors' previous theoretical framework (Sheng 2022, Sheng 2023) that explains the $\phi$ meson spin alignment observed by the STAR collaboration (STAR 2022) in Au+Au collisions, which could not be accounted for by conventional mechanisms like vorticity or magnetic fields (Yang 2017, Sheng 2020). The strong field fluctuation mechanism successfully reproduces the experimental data, while the shear-induced contribution calculated via Kubo formula (Dong 2023) is found to be negligible. The model predicts specific azimuthal angle and rapidity dependencies that can be tested in future experiments.
