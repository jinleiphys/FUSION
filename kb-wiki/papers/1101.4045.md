# On the exact solubility in momentum space of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential

> The Schrodinger equation with the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential in flat three dimensional Euclidean space, E3, and its exact solutions are shown to be also exactly transformable to momentum space, though the resulting equation is purely algebraic and can not be cast into the canonical form of an integral Lippmann-Schwinger equation. This is because the cotangent function does not allow for an exact Fourier transform in E3. In addition we recall, that the above potential can be also viewed as an angular function of the second polar angle parametrizing the three dimensional spherical surface, S3, of a constant radius, in which case the cotangent function would allow for an exact integral transform to momentum space. On that basis, we obtain a momentum space Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation, though the corresponding wavefunctions have to be obtained numerically.

# On the Exact Solubility in Momentum Space of the Trigonometric Rosen-Morse Potential

## Key claim

The trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential (Rosen-Morse I) can be exactly Fourier transformed to momentum space when treated as a central potential in flat three-dimensional Euclidean space (E3), but the resulting equation is purely algebraic and cannot be cast into the canonical integral Lippmann-Schwinger form because the cotangent function does not allow an exact Fourier transform in E3. When the potential is instead viewed as an angular function on the three-dimensional spherical surface S3 of constant radius, the cotangent function does allow an exact integral transform to momentum space, yielding a Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation whose wavefunctions must be obtained numerically.

## Method

The authors start from the Schrodinger equation with the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential in flat E3 space (parametrized as chi = r/d) and in the equatorial disk D3 of S3 (parametrized as chi = sin^{-1}(r/R)). They perform ordinary three-dimensional Fourier transformations of the known exact position-space wavefunctions for the E3 case, obtaining closed-form algebraic expressions for the momentum-space wavefunctions and for the integral F(p) appearing in the momentum-space Schrodinger equation. For the D3 case, they Fourier transform the full equation including the kinetic and interaction terms, using the previously derived E4 Fourier transform of the cotangent potential restricted to S3 via a delta function, to obtain an integral momentum-space equation.

## Key numbers

- The trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential is given by V(chi) = -2B cot chi + (hbar^2/(2 mu d^2)) l(l+1) csc^2 chi, where chi is a dimensionless angular variable, mu has the dimensionality of mass, and d is a matching length parameter.
- The free geodesic motion on S3 has spectrum E = (hbar^2/(2 mu R^2)) K(K+2) = (hbar^2/(2 mu R^2))(K+1)^2 - (hbar^2/(2 mu R^2)), with K = 0,1,2,...,infinity.
- The spectrum of the interacting system on S3 is E = (hbar^2/(2 mu R^2)) K(K+2) - (G^2 2 mu)/(hbar^2 (K+1)^2).
- In the limit R -> infinity, K -> infinity such that (K+1)/R -> k = constant, the energies approach E -> (hbar^2 k^2)/(2 mu), the scattering continuum of the Coulomb potential.
- The momentum-space wavefunction for the K=0 S-wave state is phi_{0,0,0}(p-bar) = (2bd/hbar)^(3/2) (b^2+1)^(1/2) / (pi) * 1/(p-bar^4 + 2(b^2-1)p-bar^2 + 2b^4 + 1), where p-bar = pd/hbar and b = 2B mu d^2/hbar^2.
- The F(p) function for the K=0 S-wave state is F_{0,0,0}(p-bar) = (1/pi) (2bd/hbar)^(3/2) (b^2+1)^(1/2) * (1 - b^2 - p-bar^2)/(p-bar^4 + 2(b^2-1)p-bar^2 + 2b^4 + 1).

## Context

This work extends the known exact solvability of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential in position space (previously studied by Levai, Dutt, and by Compean and Kirchbach (2006, 2007) using Romanovski polynomials) to momentum space. The potential is of interest for describing confinement phenomena ranging from electrons in quantum dots to quarks in hadrons, as noted by references to quantum dot literature and by Kirchbach and Compean (2010). The results enable momentum-space many-body frameworks based on the T-matrix to incorporate this potential, and the algebraic momentum-space equation for the E3 case provides a simpler alternative to the integral equation approach.
