# A New Extraction of Neutron Structure Functions from Existing Inclusive DIS Data

> A recent reanalysis of world proton and deuteron structure function measurements showed that a significant amount of the apparent model dependence in the extraction of the neutron structure function was related to inconsistencies between the kinematics of the data and those assumed for the calculation, suggesting that the true model dependence is smaller than commonly believed. We present a detailed comparison of the neutron structure function as extracted using different models, with care taken to ensure that all other aspects of the comparison are done consistently. The neutron structure function is extracted using a fit to these data evaluated at fixed Q^2_0=16 GeV^2. We compare the results obtained using a variety of N--N potentials and deuteron binding models to determine the model dependence of the extraction. As in the recent extraction, F_{2n}/F_{2p} falls with x with no sign of plateau and follows the low edge of the wide range of earlier F_{2n} extractions. The model-dependent uncertainty in F_{2n}/F_{2p} is shown to be considerably smaller than previously believed, particularly at large-x.

## Key claim

The model-dependent uncertainty in the extracted neutron-to-proton structure function ratio \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) is considerably smaller than previously believed, particularly at large \(x\), provided that the analysis is performed consistently at a fixed \(Q^2\) and that only modern, realistic deuteron models (including Fermi motion, binding, and off-shell effects) are used, excluding models with an explicit "EMC effect" for the deuteron that are not implemented in a realistic fashion.

## Method

The authors began with a fit to world proton and deuteron inclusive DIS data, interpolating the deuteron-to-proton structure function ratio \(F_{2d}/F_{2p}\) to a common \(Q_0^2 = 16\) GeV\(^2\) to minimize \(x\)-\(Q^2\) correlations. They then extracted \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) using a light-cone impulse approximation with the CD-Bonn N-N potential as a baseline, parameterizing the ratio with a flexible functional form and tuning parameters via MINUIT to reproduce the deuteron data. To assess model dependence, they varied the N-N potential (using a single baseline nuclear model) and separately varied the nuclear model (using the CD-Bonn potential as a baseline), taking the full range of results as the one-sigma systematic band for each source of uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The analysis was performed at a fixed \(Q_0^2 = 16\) GeV\(^2\), chosen to match the kinematics of the large-\(x\) measurements where the \(Q^2\) dependence is largest.
- The input \(F_{2d}/F_{2p}\) data covered a \(Q^2\) range from 3 to 230 GeV\(^2\); data points with \(6 < Q^2 < 30\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W^2 > 3.5\) GeV\(^2\) were interpolated to a common \(Q_0^2\) of 12 GeV\(^2\) in the baseline extraction, with a typical correction of 0.5%.
- The extracted \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) falls with \(x\) with no sign of a plateau and approaches a value consistent with 1/4 (and thus the \(d(x)/u(x)\) ratio approaching zero) as \(x\) becomes very large.
- The total systematic uncertainty from experimental extraction and model/potential dependence was combined in quadrature, yielding a significantly narrower band than the range of prior extractions.

## Context

This work extends the reanalysis of Arrington (2009), which showed that much of the apparent model dependence in neutron structure function extractions arose from inconsistent treatment of kinematics, by performing a systematic comparison of multiple N-N potentials and deuteron binding models. It contradicts the earlier conclusion that model dependence was so large as to preclude reliable information on the neutron at large \(x\), as seen in the wide spread of prior extractions from Melnitchouk (1996), Whitlow (1991), and Frankfurt (1988). The results enable improved constraints on the \(d/u\) ratio at large \(x\) for parton distribution function analyses such as CTEQ6X, and motivate future model-independent measurements planned for the 12 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Lab to test for exotic contributions beyond the models considered here.
