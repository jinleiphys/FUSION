# Nakanishi integral representation for the quark-photon vertex

> Using a nonperturbative framework of Dyson-Schwinger equations a class of Nakanishi's like integral representations for the transverse part of the quark-photon vertex is derived. For this but also for its own purpose the two and single variable integral representations for untruncated quark-antiquark-photon vertex is proposed as well. To exhibit the adequacy of proposed representation, the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the vertex is transformed into the equivalent set of coupled integro-differential equations for Nakanishi weight functions: functions that appear linearly in the numerator of given integral representation. Their knowledge then provide self-consistent nonperturbative solution for the vertex in the entire Minkowski space.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Nakanishi integral representation (NIR) for the transverse part of the quark-photon vertex within the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework, but the representation is proposed as an ansatz whose numerical solution is left for future work; the derivation assumes a specific Rainbow-Ladder truncation and a mean-z approximation where only the dominant transverse component \(T_1 = \gamma_T\) is retained.

## Method

The author starts from the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark-photon proper vertex in Minkowski space, using a Rainbow-Ladder truncation with a gluon propagator modeled by a difference of two scalar propagators (Eq. 4). The untruncated vertex is expressed via a Salam-type integral representation (Eq. 2) that separates a Gauge Technique longitudinal part from transverse terms. Substituting this representation into the vertex DSE and converting the momentum integrals into Nakanishi form yields coupled integro-differential equations for the Nakanishi weight functions \(\rho^i(a,z;Q^2)\) and the associated single-variable functions \(\tau_{v,s}(o)\) derived from the weight functions.

## Key numbers

- The gluon kernel parameters are: \(\alpha_g = c_v/(4\pi) = 22.62\), \(g^2\xi/(4\pi) = 2.13\), \(\mu_g^2/\Lambda_g^2 = 0.2667\), and \(\mu_g = 144\ \text{MeV}\).
- These parameters produce the correct pion mass \(m_\pi\) and pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) via the Bethe-Salpeter equation.
- The constant \(C\) appearing in the vertex NIR is \(C = e_q c_v/(4\pi)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi integral representation formalism previously applied to the quark propagator in Minkowski space by Sauli (VHP) and to meson transition form factors by Ding (2019). It addresses the missing inclusion of transverse vertices in Minkowski-space DSE studies, which is a known limitation compared to Euclidean-space calculations (e.g., Maris and Tandy (1999), Kacka (2005)). The derived equations enable future numerical solutions for the vertex in the entire Minkowski domain, which would allow calculation of hadron production amplitudes and form factors without analytic continuation from Euclidean space.
