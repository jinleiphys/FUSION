# An Introduction to Few Nucleon Systems in Effective Field Theory

> Progress in the Effective Field Theory of two and three nucleon systems is sketched, concentrating mainly on the low energy version in which pions are integrated out as explicit degrees of freedom. Examples given are: the extraction of nucleon polarisabilities from deuteron Compton scattering at very low energies; the energy dependence of the nucleon polarisabilities; three body forces and the triton; and $\mathrm{nd}$ partial waves at momenta below the pion cut.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Effective Field Theory (EFT) provides a systematic, model-independent framework for describing few-nucleon systems at low energies, but the power counting scheme must accommodate unnaturally large scattering lengths and bound states; the choice between iterating only contact interactions (KSW counting) or also iterating one-pion exchange (Weinberg counting) depends on the channel, and neither scheme is universally consistent across all partial waves.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of an EFT for two- and three-nucleon systems, focusing on the version where pions are integrated out as explicit degrees of freedom (ENT(π̸)). The method involves writing the most general chirally invariant Lagrangian with contact interactions between non-relativistic nucleons, establishing a power counting scheme based on the typical momentum Q relative to the breakdown scale Λ_NN, and using dimensional regularization with Power Divergence Subtraction to handle ultraviolet divergences. For three-body systems, the Faddeev equations are solved numerically after summing bubble-chain sub-graphs into the deuteron propagator.

## Key numbers

- The scattering lengths in the S-wave channels are 1/a(¹S₀) = -8.3 MeV and 1/a(³S₁) = 36 MeV, with the deuteron binding energy B = 2.225 MeV and binding momentum γ = √(M B) ≈ 46 MeV
- The expansion parameter in both KSW and Weinberg counting is found to be of order 1/3, so NLO calculations are accurate to about 10% and N²LO calculations to about 4%
- From deuteron Compton scattering data at ω_Lab = 49 MeV, the extracted iso-scalar polarisabilities (with Baldin sum rule constraint) are α₀ = 7.2 ± 2.1(exp) ± 1.6(theor) and β₀ = 6.9 ∓ 2.1(exp) ∓ 1.6(theor), each in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- In the triton channel, the leading-order ENT(π̸) calculation gives a triton binding energy of 8.0 MeV at LO and 8.8 MeV at NLO
- The ultraviolet behavior of the Faddeev equation in the doublet S-wave (triton) channel yields an exponent s₀(l=0, s=½) = ±1.0062 i, leading to a limit cycle that requires promoting the three-body force to leading order

## Context

This review extends the EFT approach from Chiral Perturbation Theory and Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (Weinberg 1990) to the few-nucleon system, building on the KSW counting scheme (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise 1998) and van Kolck's work (1997). The paper discusses how the three-body force in the triton channel explains the Phillips line correlation between triton binding energy and three-body scattering length observed in potential models (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck 1999). The results enable model-independent extraction of neutron properties from deuteron experiments and provide a framework for systematically including higher-order corrections with controlled error estimates.
