# Light quark and antiquark constraints from new electroweak data

> We present a new parton distribution function analysis which includes new data for W boson production in proton-proton collisions and lepton pair production in proton-proton and proton-deuteron collisions. The new data provide strong constraints on the light antiquark parton distribution functions in the proton. We identify an interesting correlation between the $d/u$ ratio and the $\bar{d}/\bar{u}$ ratio which leads to a modification of our previous results for the $d/u$ ratio as the parton momentum fraction $x \rightarrow 1.$

## Key claim

The new CJ22 global fit reveals a previously underappreciated anticorrelation between the $d/u$ and $\bar{d}/\bar{u}$ ratios in lepton pair production and weak boson production data, which modifies the extrapolation of the $d/u$ ratio as $x \rightarrow 1$ compared to the earlier CJ15 results, but only when the more flexible parametrization of the light antiquark sea is adopted.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading order global QCD analysis (CJ22) using the ACOT-$\chi$ heavy quark scheme, including new data from the SeaQuest experiment on lepton pair production in $pp$ and $pd$ collisions and from the STAR experiment on $W$ boson production in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=510$ GeV. The light antiquark sea was parametrized via the $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ difference with a flexible functional form, allowing the $\bar{d}/\bar{u}$ ratio to vary freely at large $x$, while higher-twist corrections and nucleon off-shell corrections in deuterium were fitted simultaneously with the PDF parameters using a local tolerance criterion with $T=1.645$.

## Key numbers

- The SeaQuest data cover the kinematic range $0.1 < x < 0.45$, extending the large-$x$ reach from the earlier E866 experiment
- The STAR $W$ boson data at $\sqrt{s}=510$ GeV probe parton momentum fractions $0.05 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.25$ via the $W^+/W^-$ ratio, with midrapidity corresponding to $x_1=x_2\approx 0.16$
- The $\chi^2$/datum for the SeaQuest data improved from 3.19 to 1.25 after including the new data in the fit, while the E866 $\chi^2$/datum increased only from 1.63 to 1.93
- The total fit includes 4557 data points with a total $\chi^2$ of 4936.6 (or 4573 points with 4948.6 including normalization uncertainties)
- The $\bar{d}/\bar{u}$ ratio from CJ22 remains above 1 within uncertainties for $x \gtrsim 0.25$, in contrast to the CJ15 parametrization which forced the ratio to approach 1 as $x\rightarrow 1$

## Context

This work extends the CJ collaboration's global PDF analyses (CJ15, Accardi 2016; CJ15-a, Accardi 2019) by incorporating new SeaQuest data (SeaQuest 2021) and STAR $W$ boson data (STAR 2020), which provide stronger constraints on the light antiquark sea than the earlier E866 experiment (NuSea 1998). The results are consistent with parallel analyses by the CT (Guzzi 2021) and JAM (Cocuzza 2021) collaborations, and the improved constraints on $\bar{d}/\bar{u}$ at $x \gtrsim 0.3$ enable more reliable predictions for large-$x$ parton distributions relevant to future experiments at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider.
