# QCD phase diagram from finite energy sum rules

> We study the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential by relating the behavior of the light-quark condensate to the threshold energy for the onset of perturbative QCD. These parameters are connected to the chiral symmetry restoration and the deconfinement phase transition, respectively. This relation is obtained in the framework of finite energy QCD sum rules at finite temperature and density, with input from Schwinger-Dyson methods to determine the light-quark condensate. Results indicate that both critical temperatures are basically the same within some 3% accuracy. We also obtain bounds for the position of the critical end point, mu_{B c} >~ 300 MeV and T_c <~ 185 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the critical temperatures for chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement are essentially identical to within about 3% accuracy across a range of baryon chemical potentials, but this result is conditional on the use of a specific meromorphic parametrization of the quark propagator fitted to lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential and extended to finite density via Schwinger-Dyson equations.

## Method

The authors use finite energy QCD sum rules (FESR) for the axial-vector current correlator to relate the perturbative QCD threshold energy \(s_0\) (a deconfinement parameter) to the pion decay constant, which is then linked to the light-quark condensate via the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. The light-quark condensate at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential is computed from a meromorphic parametrization of the quark propagator, fitted to reproduce lattice QCD data at \(\mu_B = 0\) and extended to finite \(\mu_B\) using the Schwinger-Dyson equations framework. The critical temperatures for both transitions are identified from the maxima of the heat capacities \(-\partial \langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle/\partial T\) and \(-\partial s_0/\partial T\).

## Key numbers

- The vacuum light-quark condensate is taken as \(\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle|_0 = -(0.241\ \text{GeV})^3\).
- The pion decay constant at zero temperature and chemical potential is \(f_\pi(0,0) = 92.21 \pm 0.14\ \text{MeV}\).
- The critical temperatures for the quark condensate and the PQCD threshold \(s_0\) are identical within a window of roughly \(3\ \text{MeV}\) around \(T = 185\ \text{MeV}\) for all values of \(\mu_B\) up to \(300\ \text{MeV}\).
- Bounds for the critical end point are \(\mu_{Bc} \gtrsim 300\ \text{MeV}\) and \(T_c \lesssim 185\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature analysis of the PQCD threshold \(s_0\) from Dominguez (2008) to finite baryon chemical density, using the Schwinger-Dyson equations framework to compute the in-medium quark condensate. It addresses the long-standing question of whether chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement occur at the same temperature, a topic where lattice QCD at \(\mu_B = 0\) gives consistent results (Lattice Collaboration, 2009; Lattice Collaboration, 2010) but where model calculations and Monte Carlo methods at finite \(\mu_B\) are in conflict, particularly regarding the existence and location of the critical end point. The results enable future refinements of the quark propagator parametrization to more precisely locate the critical end point, and provide an alternative to Polyakov-loop-based approaches for studying deconfinement.
