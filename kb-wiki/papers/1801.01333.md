# Chronology of the three-body dissociation of $^8$He

> The space and time configurations of the dissociation of $^8$He into $^6$He+$n$+$n$, on C and Pb targets, have been explored simultaneously for the first time. The final-state interactions in the $n$-$n$ and $^6$He-$n$ channels are successfully described within a model that considers independent emission of neutrons from a Gaussian volume with a given lifetime. The dissociation on C target exhibits a dominant sequential decay through the ground state of $^7$He, consistent with neutrons being emitted from a Gaussian volume of $r_{nn}^{\rm{rms}}=7.3\pm0.6$~fm with a $n$-$n$ delay in the sequential channel of $1400\pm400$~fm/$c$, in agreement with the lifetime of $^7$He. The lower-statistics data on Pb target correspond mainly to direct breakup, and are well described using the $n$-$n$ volume measured, without any $n$-$n$ delay. The validity of the phenomenological model used is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper simultaneously measures the spatial and temporal characteristics of the three-body dissociation of ⁸He into ⁶He+n+n for the first time, showing that the sequential decay through ⁷He on a C target produces a neutron emission delay consistent with the ⁷He lifetime, while direct breakup on a Pb target shows no such delay.

## Method
The experiment used a 15 MeV/nucleon ⁸He secondary beam from SPIRAL at GANIL incident on C and Pb targets. The ⁶He fragments were identified with Si strip detectors and CsI crystals from the CHARISSA array, while neutrons were detected with 90 elements of the DEMON array. The analysis employed an interacting phase-space model with a Gaussian space-time source, fitting the Dalitz plots and invariant-mass projections to extract the n-n root-mean-square distance and the neutron emission delay, using the formalism of Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1982) for the n-n final-state interaction.

## Key numbers
- n-n root-mean-square distance: r_nn^rms = 7.3 ± 0.6 fm for the dissociation on C target
- Neutron emission delay in the sequential channel: τ = 1400 ± 400 fm/c for the C target
- Lifetime of ⁷He: ℏ/Γ = 1320 ± 180 fm/c (from Γ = 150 ± 20 keV)
- Sequential decay probability on C target: P(⁷He) = 70^{+10}_{-5}%
- Beam energy: 15 MeV/nucleon
- Target thicknesses: 95 mg/cm² C and 284 mg/cm² Pb

## Context
This work extends the approach of Marqués et al. (2000, 2001) on two-neutron halo breakup, where the n-n correlation function was used to extract source sizes for ⁶He, ¹¹Li, and ¹⁴Be. It also relates to the study of sequential decay effects in ¹⁴Be by Marqués et al. (2001), where a delay of 150^{+250}_{-150} fm/c was attributed to unresolved resonances in ¹³Be. The present measurement provides the first simultaneous determination of both spatial and temporal parameters in a three-body decay, enabled by the well-defined narrow width of the ⁷He ground state. The results enable future studies of heavier two-neutron halo systems such as ¹⁷B, where the extremely narrow ¹⁶B resonance (Γ < 100 keV) would produce an even larger delay (τ > 2000 fm/c).
