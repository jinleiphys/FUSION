# XTE J1814-338: A potential hybrid star candidate

> A recent analysis on the properties of the XTE J1814-338 pulsar yielded a small radius value around ~ 7 km. Notably, this estimation is significantly lower compared to the currently inferred values for the radius of neutron stars (as they are derived from both theoretical calculations and corresponding observations). In this paper, we focus on the construction of hybrid equations of state (EOSs) for the possible reconciliation of the exotic XTE J1814-338 properties. Our analysis indicates that an equation of state involving a sufficiently strong phase transition could potentially lead to the explanation of the size and mass of such a compact object. By examining the sign of the $dM/dP_c$ slope, we found that stellar configurations compatible to XTE J1814-338 are only stable for a "stiff" low density phase. For a "soft" hadronic EOS selection the stability of the resulting configurations may not be satisfied in terms of the turning point criterion. However, a complementary analysis, for the radial oscillations of the aforementioned configurations, indicated their stability when a slow phase conversion is considered.

# XTE J1814-338: A potential hybrid star candidate

## Key claim
Hybrid equations of state involving a sufficiently strong first-order phase transition with large energy density jumps can potentially explain the extremely small radius (~7 km) of the XTE J1814-338 pulsar, but only if the low-density hadronic phase is stiff (e.g., DD2) for stability under the turning point criterion; for a soft hadronic EOS (APR), stability requires a slow phase conversion scenario at the hadron-quark interface.

## Method
The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by combining two hadronic models (APR and DD2) with the constant speed of sound (CSS) model for quark matter, using Maxwell construction for the phase transition. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to generate mass-radius relations, and assessed stellar stability both via the turning point criterion (sign of dM/dP_c) and by solving the radial oscillation equations of Chandrasekhar using the Gondek formalism, applying appropriate junction conditions at the hadron-quark interface for slow and fast phase conversion scenarios.

## Key numbers
- XTE J1814-338 mass: M = 1.21^{+0.05}_{-0.05} M_sun (1 sigma level)
- XTE J1814-338 radius: R = 7.0^{+0.4}_{-0.4} km (1 sigma level)
- Energy density jump range explored: [Delta_E_cr + 900, Delta_E_cr + 2100] MeV fm^{-3}, where Delta_E_cr is the Seidov critical jump
- For DD2 EOS with transition mass 2.0 M_sun: terminal (last slow stable) configuration mass = 1.197 M_sun, radius = 6.68 km
- For DD2 EOS with transition mass 2.1 M_sun: terminal configuration mass = 1.202 M_sun, radius = 6.89 km
- For DD2 EOS with transition mass 2.2 M_sun: terminal configuration mass = 1.218 M_sun, radius = 7.20 km

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Kini et al. (2024), who reported the puzzlingly small radius of XTE J1814-338, and builds on prior studies of hybrid stars by Li et al. (2023), who showed that hybrid EOSs consistent with LIGO and NICER measurements could support stable stars with extremely small radii. The authors also compare with alternative explanations: Pitz and Schaffner-Bielich (2024) proposed a bosonic star with nuclear matter core, while Yang et al. (2024) investigated a strange star admixed with mirror dark matter. The results enable future observational constraints on the stiffness of the nuclear EOS to potentially distinguish between slow and fast phase conversion dynamics in hybrid stars.
