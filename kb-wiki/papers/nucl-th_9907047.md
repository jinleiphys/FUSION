# Isoscalar Hamiltonians for light atomic nuclei

> The charge-dependent realistic nuclear Hamiltonian for a nucleus, composed of neutrons and protons, can be successfully approximated by a charge-independent one. The parameters of such a Hamiltonian, i.e., the nucleon mass and the NN potential, depend upon the mass number A, charge Z and isospin quantum number T of state of the studied nucleus.

## Key claim

The charge-dependent realistic nuclear Hamiltonian for a nucleus composed of neutrons and protons can be successfully approximated by a charge-independent (isoscalar) Hamiltonian, but the parameters of this Hamiltonian (nucleon mass and NN potential) must depend on the mass number A, charge Z, and isospin quantum number T of the specific nuclear state under study, rather than being universal constants.

## Method

The authors derive an optimal nucleon mass as the expectation value of a nucleon mass operator in the nuclear state, yielding m = (Nm_n + Zm_p)/A. They then construct an equivalent charge-independent NN potential as a weighted linear combination of the nn, np, and pp potentials in each two-nucleon channel, with coefficients determined by the number of nucleon pairs of each type in the nucleus. The method is tested using large-basis no-core shell-model calculations for A=3 and A=6 systems with the CD-Bonn potential, comparing exact charge-dependent results with the approximate isoscalar Hamiltonian results.

## Key numbers

- The small parameter δ = (m_n - m_p)/(m_n + m_p) ≈ 6.887 × 10^{-4}
- The difference between the reduced mass μ and the average mass ṁ for the two-nucleon system is approximately -4.5 × 10^{-4} MeV/c²
- The expectation value of the kinetic energy difference ⟨K - K⁰⟩ is less than or equal to 10^{-5} MeV for light nuclei
- The no-core shell-model calculations used N_max = 8 for A=3 systems and N_max = 6 for A=6 systems
- The harmonic-oscillator frequency was ℏΩ = 19 MeV for A=3 systems and ℏΩ = 17.2 MeV for A=6 systems, following the formula ℏΩ = 45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3} MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Friar and Gibson (1978) and Friar (1987) for three-nucleon systems to general light nuclei, providing a systematic method for constructing charge-independent Hamiltonians that best approximate realistic charge-dependent ones. The method enables perturbative treatment of charge-dependent effects in modern ab initio calculations of light nuclei, which previously used realistic charge-dependent potentials such as Nijmegen (Nijmegen group, 1990s), Argonne (Argonne group, 1990s), and CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 1990s). The results are tested within the no-core shell-model approach of Navratil and Barrett (1996).
