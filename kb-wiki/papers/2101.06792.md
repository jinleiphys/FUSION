# The relevance of pion-exchange contributions versus contact terms in the chiral effective field theory description of nucleon-nucleon scattering

> The standard way to demonstrate the relevance of chiral symmetry for the NN interaction is to consider higher partial waves of NN scattering which are controlled entirely by chiral pion-exchanges (since contacts vanish). However, in applications of NN-potentials to nuclear structure and reactions, the lower partial waves are the important ones, making the largest contributions. Lower partial waves are sensitive to the short-range potential, and so, when the short-range contacts were to dominate over the chiral pion-contributions in lower partial waves, then the predictions from "chiral potentials" would have little to do with chiral symmetry. To address this issue, we investigate systematically the role of the (chiral) one- and two-pion exchanges, on the one hand, and the effect of the contacts, on the other hand, in the lower partial waves of NN scattering. We are able to clearly identify the signature of chiral symmetry in lower partial waves. Our study has also a pedagogical spin-off as it demonstrates in detail how the reproduction of the lower partial-wave phase shifts comes about from the various ingredients of the theory.

## Key claim
The paper systematically investigates whether chiral symmetry is genuinely relevant for the lower partial waves of nucleon-nucleon scattering, or whether short-range contact terms (which have no connection to chiral symmetry) dominate and override the chiral pion-exchange contributions in those waves. The authors find that chiral symmetry's signature can be clearly identified even in lower partial waves, contrary to the suspicion that contacts might render "chiral potentials" essentially equivalent to phenomenological ones.

## Method
The authors construct chiral NN potentials order by order from leading order (LΩ) through N⁴LΩ, separating the long-range pion-exchange contributions (governed by chiral symmetry) from the short-range contact terms (constrained only by parity, time-reversal, and non-relativistic invariances). They then systematically fit the contact low-energy constants (LECs) to reproduce NN phase shifts under various scenarios: using contacts only, using pion contributions alone, and using combinations of both, to assess what each component contributes to reproducing empirical phase shifts in lower partial waves (S, P, and D waves). The πN LECs (c_i and d̅_i) are held fixed at values determined from πN scattering, while the NN contact LECs are the only free parameters.

## Key numbers
* The breakdown scale for the NN problem is Λ_b ~ 0.6 GeV, with the regulator cutoff set to Λ = 500 MeV.
* The spectral-function regularization cutoff is set to Λ̃ = 650 MeV, chosen to lie below the ρ(770) and ω(782) meson masses but above the f₀(500) resonance.
* At leading order (LΩ), two contact terms contribute in S-waves; at next-to-leading order (NLΩ) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLΩ), nine contacts contribute up to P-waves; at N³LΩ, 24 contacts contribute up to D-waves.
* The πN LECs used include c₁ = -0.74(2) GeV⁻¹ (NNLΩ) and -1.07(2) GeV⁻¹ (N³LΩ), c₃ = -3.61(5) GeV⁻¹ (NNLΩ) and -5.32(5) GeV⁻¹ (N³LΩ), and c₄ = 2.44(3) GeV⁻¹ (NNLΩ) and 3.56(3) GeV⁻¹ (N³LΩ), among others.

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT NN potential constructions of Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) and others (Piarulli 2015, 2016; Carlsson 2016; Reinert, Krebs, and Epelbaum 2018; Ekström 2018) by systematically disentangling the roles of pion exchanges versus contact terms in lower partial waves. The standard demonstration of chiral symmetry's relevance has relied on higher partial waves where contacts vanish (Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise 1997; Entem 2015a, 2015b), but this study addresses the concern that lower partial waves, which dominate nuclear structure and reaction calculations, might be governed primarily by contacts. The analysis enables a clearer understanding of whether the success of chiral potentials in nuclear structure calculations genuinely stems from their chiral symmetry foundation or merely from flexible contact-term fitting.
