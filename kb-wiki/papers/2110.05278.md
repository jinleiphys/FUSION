# Nonperturbative two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the nonperturbative two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions to the $NN$ interaction in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. We study how the nonperturbative resummation affects the $NN$ phase shifts for partial waves with $J \geq 3$ and $L \leq 6$. No significant differences are observed between the nonperturbative phase shifts and perturbative ones for most partial waves except for $^3D_3$, for which the nonperturbative resummation greatly improves the description of the phase shifts. However, a significant cutoff dependence is found for this partial wave and a reasonable description of the phase shifts can only be obtained with a particular cutoff. Furthermore, we compare the so-obtained nonperturbative phase shifts with those obtained in the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We show that the contributions from relativistic nonperturbative TPE are more moderate than those from the nonrelativistic TPE obtained in the dimensional regularization scheme. A proper convergence pattern is observed for most of the partial waves studied except for $^3F_3$, $^3F_4$, and $^3H_6$, for which the subleading TPE contributions are a bit strong. We find that for $H$ and $I$ partial waves, the OPE alone can already describe the phase shifts reasonably well.

## Key claim

The nonperturbative (resummed) two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory show no significant differences from perturbative results for most partial waves with \(J \geq 3\) and \(L \leq 6\), except for the \(^3D_3\) channel where resummation greatly improves the description, but only with a particular cutoff value due to significant cutoff dependence.

## Method

The authors calculated the leading-order (LO, \(O(p^0)\)) one-pion exchange and the next-to-leading-order (NLO, \(O(p^2)\)) and next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO, \(O(p^3)\)) two-pion exchange (TPE) potentials from covariant chiral Lagrangians, using the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme to remove power-counting-breaking terms. They resummed the potential nonperturbatively by solving the Blankenbecler-Sugar (BbS) scattering equation in the partial-wave \(LSJ\) representation, with a sharp cutoff regulator \(\Lambda\) varied between 600 and 1000 MeV, and subtracted the iterated one-pion exchange contribution to avoid double counting. Phase shifts were computed for partial waves with \(J \geq 3\) and \(L \leq 6\) and compared to perturbative results, nonrelativistic dimensional-regularization results, and the Nijmegen PWA93 phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 139\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and axial coupling \(g_A = 1.29\) were used as input parameters.
- Low-energy constants: \(c_1 = -1.39\), \(c_2 = 4.01\), \(c_3 = -6.61\), \(c_4 = 3.92\) (all in units of GeV\(^{-1}\)).
- The cutoff was varied between 600 and 1000 MeV; the best description of phase shifts was obtained with a cutoff around 900 MeV.
- For the \(^3D_3\) partial wave, when the cutoff was varied from 800 to 1000 MeV, the phase shifts at \(T_{\text{lab}} = 280\) MeV changed approximately from 10 to \(-5\) degrees.
- Phase shifts were plotted up to \(T_{\text{lab}} = 280\) MeV, where the first inelastic channel opens.
- The nonrelativistic comparison used a sharp cutoff \(\Lambda = 875\) MeV from Epelbaum (1999).

## Context

This work extends the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory framework for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, previously developed at leading order by Ren (2016) and at perturbative TPE order by Xiao (2020), by performing a nonperturbative resummation of the TPE contributions. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory approach of Epelbaum (1999), which used dimensional regularization, finding that relativistic nonperturbative TPE contributions are more moderate. The results enable future inclusion of higher-order contact terms (e.g., at N\(^3\)LO) to reduce the observed cutoff dependence in channels like \(^3D_3\) and to improve convergence, as was done in nonrelativistic studies by Entem (2002, 2003).
