# High-spin structures of the near-spherical nuclei $^{91,92}$Zr

> In the present work, we have interpreted recently available experimental data for high-spin states of the near-spherical nuclei $^{91,92}$Zr, using the shell-model calculations within the full $f_{5/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $g_{9/2}$ model space for protons and valence neutrons in $g_{9/2}$, $g_{7/2}$, $d_{5/2}$ orbits. We have employed a truncation for the neutrons due to huge matrix dimensions, by allowing one neutron excitation from $g_{9/2}$ orbital to $d_{5/2}$ and $g_{7/2}$ orbitals. Results are in good agreement with the available experimental data. Thus, theoretically, we have identified the structure of many high-spin states, which were tentatively assigned in the recent experimental work. The $^{91}$Zr $21/2^+$ isomer lies at low-energy region due to fully aligned spins of two $g_{9/2}$ protons and one $d_{5/2}$ neutron.

## Key claim

The paper interprets recently available experimental high-spin data for the near-spherical nuclei ⁹¹Zr and ⁹²Zr using shell-model calculations, but the model employs a truncation that allows only one neutron excitation from the g₉/₂ orbital to the d₅/₂ and g₇/₂ orbitals due to huge matrix dimensions, meaning the results are not from a full unrestricted calculation.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the NuShellX code with the GWBXG effective interaction, taking ⁶⁸Ni as the core. The model space included the full f₅/₂, p₃/₂, p₁/₂, g₉/₂ orbitals for protons and the g₉/₂, g₇/₂, d₅/₂ orbitals for valence neutrons, with a truncation allowing only one neutron excitation from g₉/₂ to d₅/₂ and g₇/₂. The calculated energy levels and wave functions were compared with experimental data, and electromagnetic properties (B(E2) and B(M1) values, quadrupole and magnetic moments) were computed using two sets of effective charges.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹¹Zr: the calculated 7/2₁⁺ state lies 282 keV above the experimental value; the 9/2₁⁺ state lies 139 keV above the experimental value.
- For ⁹¹Zr: the experimentally observed plausible (13/2₁⁻) state at 2765 keV was predicted at 2924 keV; the calculated 35/2₁⁻ state is 181 keV lower than the experimental value.
- For ⁹¹Zr: the 15/2₁⁻ isomeric state was calculated at 2376 keV (experimental at 2287.8 keV).
- For ⁹²Zr: the high-spin isomer 17₁⁻ observed at 8.041 MeV was calculated at 7.781 MeV.
- For ⁹²Zr: the shell model predicts the first three 14⁺ states at 4618, 6591, and 7063 keV.
- B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) for ⁹²Zr: experimental 6.4(6) W.u.; calculated 4.67 W.u. (Set I) and 7.94 W.u. (Set II).
- Magnetic moment μ(2₁⁺) for ⁹²Zr: experimental -0.360(20) μ_N; calculated -0.458 μ_N (Set II with g_s^eff = 0.7 g_s^free).

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of nuclei near the N=50 shell closure, following experimental high-spin data for ⁹¹,⁹²Zr reported in a recent experimental paper (cited as 9192Zr). The calculations use the GWBXG effective interaction, which had previously been applied to ⁸⁹Rb, ⁹²Y, and ⁹³Y (cited as gwbxg_cite), and the results are compared with earlier restricted-space calculations by Gloeckner (cited as int3) that did not include neutron excitation from g₉/₂ to d₅/₂. The work enables identification of the structure of tentatively assigned high-spin states and highlights the need for neutron excitations across N=50 to reproduce states above about 4.5 MeV.
