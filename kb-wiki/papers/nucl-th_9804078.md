# Low-energy and low-momentum representation of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude

> We perform an expansion of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude for low energies and low momenta and show that this expansion covers the transition from the regime to be investigated in the scheduled photon electroproduction experiments to the real Compton scattering regime. We discuss the relation of the generalized polarizabilities of virtual Compton scattering to the polarizabilities of real Compton scattering.

# Low-energy and low-momentum representation of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude

## Key claim
The paper shows that a simultaneous expansion of the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude in both the final-photon energy ω' and the initial-photon three-momentum q̅ covers the transition region between real Compton scattering (RCS) and the VCS regime probed in scheduled photon electroproduction experiments, but only if terms of higher order in ω' are included beyond the linear-in-ω' truncation used by Guichon et al. (1995).

## Method
The authors perform a covariant expansion of the 12 invariant functions f_i describing the non-pole part of the VCS amplitude through O(k³) in the four-momenta, then expand the center-of-mass amplitudes A_i through O(r⁴) in the spin-independent sector and O(r⁵) in the spin-dependent sector (r ∈ {ω', q̅}). They further expand in 1/M to leading order, relating the generalized polarizabilities (GPs) of Guichon et al. (1995) to the invariant functions and deriving explicit expressions for the GPs as Taylor series in q̅². The RCS limit is obtained by setting q̅ = ω'.

## Key numbers
- The expansion covers terms through O(r⁴) in the spin-independent sector and O(r⁵) in the spin-dependent sector, where r ∈ {ω', q̅}.
- At O(r²) there are 2 structure constants (f_{1,0}, f_{2,0}); at O(r³) there are 6 additional constants; at O(r⁴) there are 7 new constants; at O(r⁵) there are 21 new structure constants.
- The electric polarizability α and magnetic polarizability β of RCS are related to the GPs at q̅=0 by: α = -(e²/4π)√(3/2) P^{(01,01)0}(0), β = -(e²/4π)√(3/8) P^{(11,11)0}(0).
- The spin polarizabilities γ₃ and γ₂+γ₄ of Ragusa (1993) are related to GPs at q̅=0 by: γ₃ = -(e²/4π)(3/√2) P^{(01,12)1}(0), γ₂+γ₄ = -(e²/4π)(3√3/(2√2)) P^{(11,02)1}(0).
- The forward spin polarizability γ = γ₁ - γ₂ - 2γ₄ is not contained in the GP formalism of Guichon et al. (1995).

## Context
This work extends the VCS framework of Guichon et al. (1995), which kept only terms linear in ω' and assumed q̅ >> ω', by providing a simultaneous expansion in ω' and q̅ that covers the transition to RCS. It builds on the covariant approach of Drechsel et al. (1997) which reduced the number of independent GPs from ten to six. The results enable comparison with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations such as those of Hemmert et al. (1997) and provide a basis for analyzing the momentum evolution of polarizabilities in the transition region between VCS and RCS, relevant for experiments at MAMI, Jefferson Lab, and MIT Bates.
