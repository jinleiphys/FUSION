# Charged Particle Scattering in Renormalizable Pionless Effective Field Theory at Next-to-Leading Order: The $pd$, $dd$, and $p^3\mathrm{He}$ Case

> We formulate a renormalizable pionless effective field theory (Pionless EFT) with a non-perturbative treatment of the Coulomb interaction up to next-to-leading order (NLO) for few-nucleon systems. We extract scattering observables for charged clusters by employing two-, three-, and four-body contact interactions and using the stochastic variational method with a Coulomb-corrected harmonic oscillator trap. Our NLO results yield a $pd$ spin-quartet scattering length and effective range of $a_{pd}^{3/2} = 12.76(29)\,\mathrm{fm}$ and $r_{pd}^{3/2} = 1.17(7)\,\mathrm{fm}$; for $dd$ scattering in the spin-quintet channel, we find $a_{dd}^{2} = 6.26(3)\,\mathrm{fm}$ and $r_{dd}^{2} = 1.41(7)\,\mathrm{fm}$; and for $p^3\mathrm{He}$ scattering, the spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels are characterized by $a_{p^3\mathrm{He}}^0 = 11.26(4)\,\mathrm{fm}$, $r_{p^3\mathrm{He}}^0 = 1.65(26)\,\mathrm{fm}$ and $a_{p^3\mathrm{He}}^1 = 9.06(4)\,\mathrm{fm}$, $r_{p^3\mathrm{He}}^1 = 1.36(25)\,\mathrm{fm}$, respectively. Our predictions exhibit mild cutoff dependence and agree well with existing experimental phase shift analyses and potential model calculations. This demonstrates the predictive power of (Pionless EFT) for charged few-nucleon systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a renormalizable pionless effective field theory (Pionless EFT) with non-perturbative Coulomb treatment can predict charged few-nucleon scattering observables at next-to-leading order (NLO), but the spin-doublet pd phase shifts converge slowly within the EFT expansion and require a dedicated ppn three-body force to achieve proper renormalization.

## Method

The authors constructed local Pionless EFT potentials up to NLO, treating the Coulomb interaction non-perturbatively by splitting the two-body spin-singlet isospin-triplet channel into nn, np, and pp components with separate low-energy constants (LECs). The LO strong interaction was iterated through all orders via the Schrodinger equation, while NLO corrections were included within first-order perturbation theory. Scattering observables were extracted using the stochastic variational method with a correlated Gaussian basis and a Coulomb-corrected harmonic oscillator trap technique to obtain phase shifts.

## Key numbers

*   pd spin-quartet scattering length: a_pd^(3/2) = 12.76(29) fm at NLO
*   pd spin-quartet effective range: r_pd^(3/2) = 1.17(7) fm at NLO
*   dd spin-quintet scattering length: a_dd^2 = 6.26(3) fm at NLO
*   dd spin-quintet effective range: r_dd^2 = 1.41(7) fm at NLO
*   p^3He spin-singlet scattering length: a_p^3He^0 = 11.26(4) fm at NLO
*   p^3He spin-singlet effective range: r_p^3He^0 = 1.65(26) fm at NLO
*   p^3He spin-triplet scattering length: a_p^3He^1 = 9.06(4) fm at NLO
*   p^3He spin-triplet effective range: r_p^3He^1 = 1.36(25) fm at NLO
*   LO extrapolated ^3He binding energy: B(^3He) = 7.7085(4) MeV, compared to experimental 7.718 MeV
*   LO extrapolated ^4He binding energy: B(^4He) = 28.11(3) MeV, compared to experimental 28.296 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Pionless EFT studies by Schafer et al. (2023) and Bagnarol et al., which did not include Coulomb interactions, to charged systems (pd, dd, p^3He). It builds on the non-perturbative Coulomb treatment developed by Kong and Ravndal (1999, 2000) for pp scattering and by Vanasse et al. (2014) for ^3He and pd scattering, but uses a potential formulation with local regulators rather than the dibaryon field formalism. The results enable systematic EFT calculations of charged few-nucleon scattering up to NLO and can be extended to higher orders or more complex systems.
