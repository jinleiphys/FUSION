# Direct photon production from viscous QGP

> We simulate direct photon production in evolution of viscous QGP medium. Photons from Compton and annihilation processes are considered. Viscous effect on photon production is very strong and reliable simulation is possible only in a limited $p_T$ range. For minimally viscous fluid $η/s$=0.08), direct photons can be reliably computed only up to $p_T \leq$ 1.3 GeV. With reduced viscosity ($η/s$=0.04), the limit increases to $p_T \leq $2GeV.

## Key claim

Viscous effects on direct photon production from a QGP medium are very strong, and reliable hydrodynamic simulation of Compton and annihilation photons is only possible in a limited transverse momentum range; for a minimally viscous fluid with η/s=0.08, reliable computation is limited to pT ≤ 1.3 GeV, and even with reduced viscosity η/s=0.04 the limit only increases to pT ≤ 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors solved second-order Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamic equations (using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code) for a baryon-free QGP fluid in √sNN=200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, assuming boost-invariance and a lattice-based equation of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration. Photon production rates from Compton and annihilation processes were computed including both the equilibrium distribution function and a non-equilibrium correction δfneq = (1/(2(ε+p)T²)) pμ pν πμν, with the shear stress tensor evolved dynamically. The fluid was initialized at τi=0.6 fm with central temperature T0=350 MeV, and simulations were performed for η/s values of 0, 0.04, 0.08, and 0.12.

## Key numbers

- For minimally viscous fluid (η/s=0.08), direct photons from Compton and annihilation processes can be reliably computed only up to pT ≤ 1.3 GeV (assuming δNneq/Neq = 0.5 as reliability threshold).
- With reduced viscosity η/s=0.04, the reliable pT limit increases to pT ≤ 2 GeV.
- For η/s=0.12, the reliable pT limit decreases to pT ≤ 1.3 GeV.
- The non-equilibrium contribution equals the equilibrium contribution at pT ≈ 1.7 GeV for η/s=0.08.
- Photon elliptic flow v2(pT) in ideal fluid evolution reaches a maximum less than about 2% at pT ≈ 2 GeV.
- Inverse slope parameter Teff from exponential fits in the range 1.5 ≤ pT ≤ 2.5 GeV varies by approximately 20-80 MeV depending on initial time for η/s=0.04 ± 0.04.
- Initial time dependence was studied for τi = 0.2, 0.6, 1.0, and 1.4 fm with fixed siτi = 60 fm⁻².

## Context

This work extends earlier ideal hydrodynamic studies of photon production at RHIC (Alam 2007, Chatterjee 2005) by including shear viscosity, and corrects an inconsistency in the earlier viscous study by Dusling (2009) which included non-equilibrium corrections to the distribution function but neglected the modification of fluid evolution due to viscosity. The results show that viscous effects on photons are much stronger than on charged particles, because photons are emitted throughout the evolution when shear stress tensors are large, whereas charged particles are emitted from the freeze-out surface where stress tensors are smaller. The limited pT range of applicability raises questions about whether photons in the 1.5-3 GeV range can be considered thermal or are of pQCD origin, and suggests that simultaneous explanation of charged particle and photon production may require QGP viscosity much less than the AdS/CFT lower bound.
