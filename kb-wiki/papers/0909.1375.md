# CIPANP 2009: Closing Talk

> CIPANP 2009 is the tenth meeting of this series. I look back at some of the key events of past meetings, comment on a few of the presentations of this meeting, and look foward to the next CIPANP gathering, when first data from the LHC will be in hand.

## Key claim

The paper is a conference summary talk that reviews highlights from the tenth CIPANP meeting and identifies future research directions; it does not present a single original claim but rather synthesizes the state of the field in 2009, with the caveat that many results (such as the possible GZK cutoff) are preliminary and not yet definitive claims by the collaborations themselves.

## Method

The author reviews the history of the CIPANP conference series from 1984 to 2009, then summarizes selected presentations from the 2009 meeting organized around three themes: inner space/outer space (neutrinos, ultra-high-energy cosmic rays, solar neutrinos), low-energy/high-energy intersections (CP violation, flavor physics, dark matter), and theory/computation. The talk uses retrospective slides and commentary on specific experimental results and theoretical developments presented at the meeting.

## Key numbers

- The atmospheric neutrino mass splitting gives m_nu^(3) ~ sqrt(m_23^atmos) ~ 0.05 eV; combining this with m_D^(3) ~ m_top quark ~ 180 GeV yields m_R ~ 0.3 x 10^15 GeV, close to the SUSY grand-unified scale of ~10^16 GeV
- The best laboratory limit on the nu_e mass from tritium beta decay is 2.2 eV, equivalent to sum_i m_i < 6.6 eV; KATRIN could lower the m_nu_e limit to about 0.2 eV, giving sum ~ 0.6 eV
- Cosmological limits give sum_i m_i < 0.7 eV, equivalent to rho_nu < 0.013 rho_crit; to measure nu mass at the 0.05 eV atmospheric lower bound requires sensitivity to nu dark matter at ~0.001 rho_crit
- The free-streaming wavenumber for neutrinos is k_free_streaming ~ 0.004 sqrt(m_nu/0.05 eV) Mpc^-1
- The 199Hg electric dipole moment limit improved from 2.1 x 10^-28 e cm to 3.1 x 10^-29 e cm (factor of seven improvement)
- Neutron edm limit is 2.9 x 10^-26 e cm; new ultracold neutron experiments should improve this to ~5 x 10^-28 e cm by 2015
- Electron edm limit is 1.9 x 10^-27 e cm (from atomic 205Tl); proton edm limit is 6.5 x 10^-23 e cm (from molecular TlF)
- The 5/2+ - 5/2- 160 eV ground state parity doublet in 229Pa could produce an edm enhancement of ~10,000
- The muon to electron conversion experiment at Fermilab with an 8 GeV proton beam could reach branching ratio sensitivity of ~4 x 10^-17, an improvement of four orders of magnitude over current bounds; JPARC's experiment with 40 GeV protons might reach ~5 x 10^-19
- Current LFV decay bounds include: mu+ -> e- gamma at 1.2 x 10^-11 (MEGA/LAMPF, 2002); mu+ -> e-e+e- at 1.0 x 10^-12 (SINDRUM I/PSI, 1988); mu- Ti -> e- Ti at 6.1 x 10^-13 (SINDRUM II/PSI, 1998); mu- Au -> e- Au at 7.0 x 10^-13 (SINDRUM II/PSI, 2006)
- The GZK cutoff appears at about 10^19.5 eV from Pierre Auger results (not a definite claim by the collaboration)

## Context

This closing talk reviews the tenth meeting of the CIPANP conference series, which began in 1984 in Steamboat Springs. It extends the tradition of summarizing intersections between nuclear, particle, and astrophysics, and looks forward to the LHC era. The talk highlights specific experimental programs including Majorana/GERDA, CUORE, EXO, and SNO+ for neutrinoless double beta decay; Double Chooz and Daya Bay for reactor neutrino experiments; and Fermi/GLAST, IceCube, and Pierre Auger for high-energy astrophysics. It also discusses the solar metallicity problem arising from revised photospheric abundance analyses (Serenelli, 2009) and the potential for future solar neutrino experiments like SNO+ and a deeper Borexino to measure CN solar neutrinos and constrain core metallicity to ~10% accuracy. The talk enables planning for the next CIPANP meeting in 2012, when first LHC data would be available.
