# Bounds on the presence of quantum chaos in nuclear masses

> Differences between measured nuclear masses and those calculated using the Finite Range Droplet Model are analyzed. It is shown that they have a well defined, clearly correlated oscillatory component as a function of the proton and neutron numbers. At the same time, they exhibit in their power spectrum the presence of chaos. Comparison with other mass calculations strongly suggest that this chaotic component arises from many body effects not included in the mass formula, and that they do not impose limits in the precision of mass calculations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while mass deviations in the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) exhibit a power spectrum slope consistent with quantum chaos (near -1), this chaotic component arises from neglected many-body effects and does not impose a fundamental limit on mass calculation precision; local Garvey-Kelson relations achieve r.m.s. deviations below 100 keV with white-noise-like residuals.

## Method

The authors analyzed differences between measured nuclear masses and those calculated using the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM), the Duflo-Zuker (DZ) mass formula, and the liquid drop model (LDM). They performed Fourier analysis on mass deviations along isotopic chains and along a boustrophedon ordering of all 1654 nuclei with measured masses, fitting the squared Fourier amplitudes to a power law \(|F(\omega)|^2 \sim \omega^m\). They also systematically applied Garvey-Kelson (GK) relations to predict masses from neighboring nuclei, using up to 12 independent estimates per nucleus.

## Key numbers

- FRDM isotopic chain power spectrum slope: \(m^{(1)}_{FRDM} = -1.18 \pm 0.17\)
- DZ isotopic chain power spectrum slope: \(m^{(1)}_{DZ} = -0.67 \pm 0.16\)
- FRDM boustrophedon ordering power spectrum slope: \(m^{(2)}_{FRDM} = -0.91 \pm 0.05\)
- DZ boustrophedon ordering power spectrum slope: \(m^{(2)}_{DZ} = -0.51 \pm 0.05\)
- r.m.s. mass deviation for FRDM: 669 keV
- r.m.s. mass deviation for DZ: 346 keV
- r.m.s. mass deviation for GK relations (all nuclei): 189 keV
- r.m.s. mass deviation for GK-12 (12 relations applied, A >= 60): 80 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Hirsch 2004, Hirsch 2004b) on correlations in mass errors and responds to the proposal by Abe (2002) and Bohigas (2002) that chaotic motion might limit nuclear mass calculation accuracy. The analysis compares global mass formulas (FRDM by Moller 1995, DZ by Duflo and Zuker 1994) with local Garvey-Kelson relations (Garvey 1966), demonstrating that the chaotic signal in FRDM deviations diminishes in more microscopic approaches. The results enable continued use of mass predictions for nuclear astrophysics without a fundamental precision barrier, and suggest that further inclusion of many-body correlations could improve global mass formulas.
