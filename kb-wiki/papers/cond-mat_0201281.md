# Universal Equation for Efimov States

> Efimov states are a sequence of shallow 3-body bound states that arise when the 2-body scattering length is large. Efimov showed that the binding energies of these states can be calculated in terms of the scattering length and a 3-body parameter by solving a transcendental equation involving a universal function of one variable. We calculate this universal function using effective field theory and use it to describe the three-body system of 4He atoms. We also extend Efimov's theory to include the effects of deep 2-body bound states, which give widths to the Efimov states.

## Key claim
The paper calculates the universal function \(\Delta(\xi)\) that appears in Efimov's transcendental equation for the binding energies of Efimov states, using effective field theory. The authors also extend Efimov's original theory to include the effects of deep 2-body bound states, which give finite widths to the Efimov states, introducing an additional inelasticity parameter \(\eta\).

## Method
The authors used effective field theory (EFT) with zero effective range to solve the 3-body equation for three identical bosons, calculating the binding energies of the three lowest Efimov states for both signs of the scattering length \(a\). From these numerical results, they extracted the universal function \(\Delta(\xi)\) by solving Efimov's equation for the middle branch of states, and provided analytic parametrizations for \(\Delta(\xi)\) in three intervals of the angular variable \(\xi\). They then applied the universal equation to the \(^4\)He trimer system, using the calculated dimer binding energy \(B_2\) and the excited trimer binding energy \(B_3^{(1)}\) to determine the 3-body parameter \(\Lambda_*\) and predict the ground-state trimer binding energy \(B_3^{(0)}\) and the atom-dimer scattering length \(a_{12}\).

## Key numbers
- The universal constant \(s_0 \approx 1.00624\) appears in the radial equation for the 3-body system in the region \(l \ll R \ll |a|\).
- The asymptotic ratio of successive Efimov binding energies approaches \(\exp[2\pi/s_0] \approx 515\) as \(B_3 \to \infty\).
- For \(a>0\), the sequence of binding energies \(B_3\) in units of \(\hbar^2/ma^2\) is 1, 6.8, \(1.4 \times 10^3\), ...; the ratio of the two shallowest states can range from 6.8 to 210.
- For \(a<0\), the sequence of binding energies \(B_3\) in units of \(\hbar^2/ma^2\) is 0, \(1.1 \times 10^3\), \(6.0 \times 10^5\), ...; the ratio of the two shallowest states can range from \(\infty\) to 550.
- For the \(^4\)He trimer with the LM2M2 potential, the calculated dimer binding energy \(B_2 = 1.303\) mK, the excited trimer binding energy \(B_3^{(1)} = 2.28\) mK, and the ground-state trimer binding energy \(B_3^{(0)} = 125.9\) mK; the universal equation predicts \(B_3^{(0)} = 130.3\) mK (1-4% higher than the calculated value).
- The parametrizations of \(\Delta(\xi)\) deviate from the numerical results in Table 1 by less than 0.013, with discontinuities at \(\xi = -3\pi/8\) and \(\xi = -5\pi/8\) of less than 0.016.

## Context
This work extends Efimov's original theory (Efimov 1971) by calculating the universal function \(\Delta(\xi)\) using effective field theory, following the approach of Braaten, Hammer, and Kusunoki (1999) which introduced the 3-body parameter \(\Lambda_*\). The results enable the prediction of low-energy 3-body observables (such as atom-dimer scattering length and 3-body recombination rates) for systems with large scattering length, using only the scattering length \(a\) and the 3-body parameter \(\Lambda_*\) as inputs. The application to the \(^4\)He trimer demonstrates that the ground state can be described by Efimov's equation, with predictions within 1-4% of accurate Faddeev calculations by Motovilov et al. (2001) for various model potentials. The generalization to include deep 2-body bound states via an inelasticity parameter \(\eta\) extends the theory to systems like Na and Rb atoms near Feshbach resonances, where many deep 2-body states exist.
