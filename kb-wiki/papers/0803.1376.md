# Deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model for exotic nuclei

> A deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (DRHB) model is developed aiming at a proper description of exotic nuclei, particularly deformed ones with large spatial extension. In order to give an adequate description of both the contribution of the continuum and the large spatial distribution in exotic nuclei, the DRHB equations are solved in a Woods-Saxon basis in which the radial wave functions have proper asymptotic behaviors at large distance from the nuclear center which is crucial for the formation of halo. The formalism and the numerical procedure of the DRHB model in a Woods-Saxon basis are briefly presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (DRHB) model solved in a Woods-Saxon basis, claiming that this basis provides proper asymptotic behavior of radial wave functions at large distance from the nuclear center, which is crucial for describing halo formation in exotic nuclei. The authors hedge that the model is presented as a formalism and numerical procedure with only preliminary results for a spherical test case, not yet for deformed exotic nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations by expanding quasiparticle wave functions in a Woods-Saxon basis, where the basis states are obtained from solving the spherical Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon-like potentials. The potentials and densities are expanded in Legendre polynomials for axially deformed nuclei with spatial reflection symmetry, and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force is used in the particle-particle channel. The numerical procedure introduces an energy cutoff for positive energy states in the Woods-Saxon basis and a box boundary condition to discretize the continuum.

## Key numbers

- Box size: \( R_\mathrm{max} = 4r_0 A^{1/3} \) with \( r_0 = 1.2 \) fm, step size \( \Delta r = 0.1 \) fm, \( \lambda_\mathrm{max} = 4 \), and \( E^+_\mathrm{cut} = 100 \) MeV give relative deviations of binding energy, rms radius, and quadrupole moment smaller than 0.1% for light nuclei.
- Pairing strength \( V_0 = 374 \) MeV fm\(^3\) and quasiparticle cutoff \( E^\mathrm{q.p.}_\mathrm{cut} = 60 \) MeV reproduce the proton pairing energy in spherical \(^{20}\)Mg from a spherical RHB calculation with Gogny force.
- For \(^{20}\)Mg with \( E^+_\mathrm{cut} = 100 \) MeV, accuracies in total binding energy and proton pairing energy are about a hundred keV, and in rms radius around 0.002 fm.
- Saturation density \( \rho_0 = 0.152 \) fm\(^{-3}\) is used for the pairing force.

## Context

This work extends the spherical relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov models of Meng (1996, 1998) and the spherical relativistic Hartree model in a Woods-Saxon basis of Zhou (2003) to deformed nuclei, addressing the need for deformed halo descriptions raised by Li (1996), Misu (1997), and others. The Woods-Saxon basis approach reconciles the harmonic oscillator basis method (used by Vretenar (1999), Lalazissis (1999), Niksic (2002, 2004) for deformed RHB) with coordinate-space solutions, enabling future studies of deformed exotic nuclei near the neutron drip line where deformed halos may appear.
