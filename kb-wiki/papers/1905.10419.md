# Rescattering effects in antiproton-induced exclusive $J/ψ$ and $ψ^\prime$ production on the deuteron

> On the basis of the generalized eikonal approximation we study the exclusive reactions $\bar p d \to J/ψ\, n$ and $\bar p d \to ψ^\prime\, n$ in vicinity of the thresholds for charmonium production on a free proton target. It is shown that the rescattering of the incoming antiproton and outgoing charmonium on the spectator neutron leads to a depletion of the charmonium production at low- and to an enhancement at high transverse momenta. This is in qualitative agreement with previous studies of hard proton knockout in proton-deuteron collisions. We analyze different physical sources of uncertainty which may influence the extraction of the total charmonium-neutron cross section. The color transparency effect for the incoming $\bar p$ largely compensates the influence of charmonium rescattering both at low and high transverse momenta. Different choices of the deuteron wave function lead to significant uncertainties at high transverse momenta. As an outcome of the calculations of charmonium production, we also provide predictions on the production of open charm hadrons due to the dissociation of the charmonium on the neutron. It is shown that the open charm production cross section is proportional to the total charmonium-nucleon cross section and quite stable with respect to the variation of other parameters of the model. We thus suggest that open charm channels are most suited for future studies of charmonium-nucleon interactions at PANDA with a deuteron target.

## Key claim

The authors show that rescattering of the incoming antiproton and outgoing charmonium on the spectator neutron in exclusive $\bar p d \to J/\psi\, n$ and $\bar p d \to \psi^\prime\, n$ reactions leads to depletion at low transverse momenta and enhancement at high transverse momenta, but the color transparency effect for the incoming antiproton largely compensates the influence of charmonium rescattering at both low and high transverse momenta. The open charm production cross section from charmonium dissociation is proportional to the total charmonium-nucleon cross section and is stable with respect to variation of other model parameters, making it the most suitable observable for future studies at PANDA.

## Method

The authors employed the generalized eikonal approximation to multiple-scattering theory, summing coherently the impulse approximation diagram and diagrams with elastic rescattering of the charmonium and antiproton on the spectator neutron. The elementary $\bar p p \to \psi$ transition amplitude was described using an effective Lagrangian with Dirac and Pauli couplings, with coupling constants fixed by the charmonium total width, branching fraction to $\bar p p$, and the anisotropy parameter $\alpha$ from $e^+ e^- \to \psi \to p \bar p$ angular distributions. Color transparency effects were included via the quantum diffusion model, replacing the total cross sections in the elastic scattering amplitudes with effective cross sections that depend on the distance traveled from the hard interaction point.

## Key numbers

* The threshold antiproton beam momentum for $J/\psi$ production on a free proton is $p_{\rm thr}=4.07$ GeV/c; for $\psi^\prime$ it is $p_{\rm thr}=6.23$ GeV/c.
* The r.m.s. distance between $c$ and $\bar c$ from the Cornell potential is 0.376 fm for $J/\psi(1S)$ and 0.760 fm for $\psi^\prime(2S)$; the calculated charmonium-nucleon total cross sections are 3.62 mb for $J/\psi$ and 20.0 mb for $\psi^\prime$ (Gerland 1998).
* The coupling constants for the effective Lagrangian are $\kappa=-0.089$, $g=1.79 \times 10^{-3}$ for $J/\psi$ and $\kappa=-0.259$, $g=1.35 \times 10^{-3}$ for $\psi^\prime$, with anisotropy parameters $\alpha=0.595\pm0.012\pm0.015$ for $J/\psi$ and $\alpha=1.03\pm0.06\pm0.03$ for $\psi^\prime$.
* The default charmonium-neutron scattering parameters used are $\sigma_{J/\psi n}^{\rm tot}=4$ mb, $\sigma_{\psi^\prime n}^{\rm tot}=20$ mb, $B_{J/\psi n}=B_{\psi^\prime n}=3$ GeV$^{-2}$, and $\rho_{J/\psi n}=\rho_{\psi^\prime n}=0.2$.
* The charmonium coherence lengths are estimated as $l_{J/\psi} \simeq 2p_{J/\psi}/(m_{\psi^\prime}^2-m_{J/\psi}^2)$ and $l_{\psi^\prime} \simeq 2 l_{J/\psi} \simeq 6$ fm $\times (p_{\psi^\prime}/30$ GeV$)$.
* The internal transverse momentum scales used for color transparency are $\sqrt{\langle k_{J/\psi t}^2\rangle}=0.8$ GeV/c and $\sqrt{\langle k_{\psi^\prime t}^2\rangle}=0.4$ GeV/c; the antiproton coherence length uses $\Delta M^2=0.7$ GeV$^2$, $n_{\bar p}=3$, and $\sqrt{\langle k_{\bar p t}^2\rangle}=0.35$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hard proton knockout in proton-deuteron collisions to the charmonium sector, and improves upon the earlier treatment by Cassing (1999) which performed incoherent summation of partial amplitudes and neglected antiproton rescattering. The authors demonstrate that coherent summation and inclusion of antiproton rescattering are essential, and that color transparency effects largely compensate charmonium rescattering. The results enable future experimental studies at PANDA with a deuteron target, where open charm production channels are suggested as the most robust observable for extracting the charmonium-nucleon total cross section, which remains poorly constrained with predictions ranging from 2-8 mb from meson-exchange models (Sibirtsev 2000, Oh 2007) to dynamically-generated resonance peaks near 8 mb (Molina 2012, Xiao 2015) and lattice QCD results giving up to approximately 50 mb at threshold (Sugiura 2017).
