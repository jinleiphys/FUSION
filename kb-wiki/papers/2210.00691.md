# Electromagnetic response in an expanding quark-gluon plasma

> The validity of conventional Ohm's law is tested in the context of a rapidly evolving quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. Here we discuss the electromagnetic response using an analytical solution in kinetic theory. As conjectured previously, after switching on an electric field in a nonexpanding plasma, the time-dependent current is given by $\mathbf{J}(t)=(1-e^{-t/τ_0}) σ_{0} \mathbf{E}$, where $τ_0$ is the transport relaxation time and $σ_{0}$ is the steady-state electrical conductivity. Such an incomplete electromagnetic response reduces the efficiency of the magnetic flux trapping in the quark-gluon plasma and may prevent the observation of the chiral magnetic effect. Here we extend the study to the case of a rapidly expanding plasma. We find that the decreasing temperature and the increasing transport relaxation time have opposite effects on the electromagnetic response. While the former suppresses the time-dependent conductivity, the latter enhances it.

## Key claim

The conventional Ohm's law is violated on time scales shorter than the transport relaxation time in a quark-gluon plasma, and in a rapidly expanding plasma the decreasing temperature suppresses the time-dependent conductivity while the increasing transport relaxation time enhances it, with the net result depending on the expansion rate.

## Method

The author solved the Boltzmann kinetic equation in the relaxation-time approximation for a single charged particle species, first for a nonexpanding plasma and then for a plasma with Bjorken-like power-law expansion. The time-dependent electrical conductivity was extracted analytically from the linear-response current induced by a suddenly switched-on electric field, with the expansion modeled by a time-dependent temperature following a power law and a time-dependent transport relaxation time proportional to the inverse temperature.

## Key numbers

* For a nonexpanding plasma, the time-dependent conductivity is given by sigma(t) = (1 - e^{-t/tau_0}) sigma_0, where sigma_0 = (tau_0/9) e^2 T_0^2 for massless particles.
* For an expanding plasma with Bjorken expansion (alpha = 1/3), the temperature scales as T_t = T_0 ((t_0 + t)/t_0)^{-alpha}, with the initial time parameter t_0 set to either 0.1 tau_0 ("fast" expansion) or 0.5 tau_0 ("slow" expansion).
* The transport relaxation time in the expanding plasma is modeled as tau(t) = tau_0 ((t_0 + t)/t_0)^{beta} with beta = alpha = 1/3, based on lattice estimates that sigma_0/T_0 has weak temperature dependence in the near-critical region.

## Context

This work extends the study of incomplete electromagnetic response in a quark-gluon plasma, previously investigated by Wang (2021) and Grayson (2022), from the nonexpanding case to a rapidly expanding plasma relevant to heavy-ion collisions. The results challenge the assumption that conventional Ohm's law applies in the short-lived QGP, which was used in earlier magnetic flux trapping arguments by Tuchin (2013) and Gursoy (2014). The finding that a sufficiently fast expansion could enhance the effective conductivity at intermediate times opens the possibility of slowed magnetic field decay, potentially improving the prospects for observing the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions.
