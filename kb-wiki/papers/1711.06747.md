# Energy dependence of non-local potentials

> Recently a variety of studies have shown the importance of including non-locality in the description of reactions. The goal of this work is to revisit the phenomenological approach to determining non-local optical potentials from elastic scattering. We perform a $χ^2$ analysis of neutron elastic scattering data off $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb at energies $E \approx 5-40$ MeV, assuming a Perey and Buck or Tian, Pang, and Ma non-local form for the optical potential. We introduce energy and asymmetry dependencies in the imaginary part of the potential and refit the data to obtain a global parameterization. Independently of the starting point in the minimization procedure, an energy dependence in the imaginary depth is required for a good description of the data across the included energy range. We present two parameterizations, both of which represent an improvement over the original potentials for the fitted nuclei as well as for other nuclei not included in our fit. Our results show that, even when including the standard Gaussian non-locality in optical potentials, a significant energy dependence is required to describe elastic-scattering data.

## Key claim

Even when a standard Gaussian non-locality is included explicitly in the optical potential, a significant energy dependence in the imaginary part is still required to describe neutron elastic-scattering data across the 5–40 MeV range; the specific parameters of that energy dependence are not uniquely constrained by elastic data alone.

## Method

The authors performed a χ² analysis of 24 sets of neutron elastic-scattering data on ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb at energies E ≈ 5–40 MeV, using either the Perey and Buck (PB) or Tian, Pang, and Ma (TPM) non-local form for the optical potential. They introduced linear energy and asymmetry dependencies in the imaginary depths (surface term Wₛ and volume term Wᵥ) and refit the five parameters to all data simultaneously, keeping the real part energy-independent and all other parameters at their original values.

## Key numbers

- The original PB potential gives summed χ²ₜₒₜ = 136 for energies <20 MeV and 465 for energies ≥20 MeV; the energy-dependent PB-E fit improves these to 121 and 136, respectively (data with θ<100°).
- The original TPM potential gives summed χ²ₜₒₜ = 230 for energies <20 MeV and 182 for energies ≥20 MeV; the energy-dependent TPM-E fit improves these to 158 and 177, respectively (data with θ<100°).
- For the PB-E parameterization, the energy slope of the imaginary volume term is d = 0.34 ± 0.011 (dimensionless), and the asymmetry coefficient for the surface term is b = 0.74 ± 0.46 MeV.
- For the TPM-E parameterization, the energy slope of the imaginary surface term is a = 0.20 ± 0.0040 (dimensionless), and the asymmetry coefficient for the surface term is b = 4.5 ± 0.5 MeV.
- The energy slopes a and d in both non-local parameterizations are of the same order of magnitude as those in the local Becchetti and Greenlees (BG) potential.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological non-local optical potentials of Perey and Buck (1962) and Tian, Pang, and Ma (2015), which assumed energy-independent parameters, by demonstrating that elastic-scattering data require an explicit energy dependence even when Gaussian non-locality is included. The results contradict the common belief that non-locality alone can remove the energy dependence of optical potentials, and they agree with expectations from microscopic theories such as the dispersive optical model (Mahaux and Sartor, 1991) and ab initio coupled-cluster calculations (Rotureau et al., 2017). The study enables the development of a new global non-local energy-dependent optical potential and calls for future work incorporating non-Gaussian non-locality from ab initio theories and proton data to reliably constrain the asymmetry term.
