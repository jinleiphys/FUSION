# Intermittency route to chaos for the nuclear billiard - a qualitative study

> We analyze on a simple classical billiard system the onset of chaotical behaviour in different dynamical states. A classical version of the "nuclear billiard" with a 2D deep Woods-Saxon potential is used. We take into account the coupling between the single-particle and the collective degrees of freedom in the presence of dissipation for several vibrational multipolarities. For the considered oscillation modes an increasing divergence of the nucleonic trajectories from the adiabatic to the resonance regime was observed. Also, a peculiar case of intermittency is reached in the vicinity of the resonance, for the monopole case. We examine the order-to-chaos transition by performing several types of qualitative analysis including sensitive dependence on the initial conditions, single-particle phase space maps, fractal dimensions of Poincare maps and autocorrelation functions.

## Key claim

For a classical single-nucleon in a 2D Woods-Saxon potential, the onset of deterministic chaos occurs earlier at the resonance frequency (Ω_res = 0.145 c/fm) than at any adiabatic vibration frequency, and a peculiar intermittency regime (alternating laminar and turbulent phases) appears specifically for the monopole case at Ω = 0.1 c/fm, prior to full resonance.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Hamilton equations for a single nucleon and a collective vibrational degree of freedom using a Runge-Kutta algorithm (order 2-3) with an optimized step size, ensuring absolute error below 10⁻⁶ per variable and total energy conservation to a relative error of 10⁻⁸. They analyzed the order-to-chaos transition for monopole (L=0), dipole (L=1), and quadrupole (L=2) vibrational modes of the Woods-Saxon potential well, comparing the adiabatic regime (Ω_ad ≤ 0.05 c/fm) with the resonance regime (Ω_res = 0.145 c/fm). Qualitative diagnostics included sensitive dependence on initial conditions (Δr = 0.01 fm perturbation), 1D phase-space maps, fractal dimensions of Poincaré maps computed via box counting, and autocorrelation functions of both single-particle and collective variables.

## Key numbers

- Woods-Saxon potential depth V₀ = -1500 MeV, diffusivity a = 0.01 fm, radius R₀ = 6 fm
- Nucleon mass m = 938 MeV, apparent temperature T = 36 MeV
- Adiabatic wall frequency Ω_ad ≤ 0.05 c/fm, corresponding wall period τ_wall ≥ 125.66 fm/c
- Single-particle period τ_part = 2R₀/v ≈ 43.33 fm/c (for T = 36 MeV, R₀ = 6 fm)
- Resonance frequency Ω_res = 0.145 c/fm
- Intermittency observed at Ω = 0.1 c/fm for the monopole case
- Fractal dimensions d_f of (r ↔ p_r) Poincaré maps for uncoupled equations: 0.1194 (Ω=0.010 c/fm), 0.1666 (Ω=0.050 c/fm), 0.1840 (Ω=0.100 c/fm), 0.2227 (Ω=0.145 c/fm)
- Fractal dimensions d_f for monopole case: 0.1262 (Ω=0.010 c/fm), 0.1870 (Ω=0.050 c/fm), 0.1790 (Ω=0.100 c/fm), 0.2010 (Ω=0.145 c/fm)
- Poincaré map analysis used ε_θ = 10⁻⁴ radians thickness and Δt = 5×10⁶ fm/c evolution time
- Initial perturbation of collective variable: α(t=0) = ᾱ + 0.15

## Context

This work extends the classical nuclear billiard model introduced by Burgio, Baldo et al. (1995-1998), who studied damping and order-chaos transitions in a Woods-Saxon container with nucleons kicking the walls. It also follows the formalism of Papachristou et al. (2008) on the decay width of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance. The authors show that intermittency, previously known for mushroom billiards (Bunimovich, 2001-2003) and annular billiards (Saito, 1982), also appears in a Woods-Saxon billiard with inelastic particle-wall interactions. This enables future quantitative studies using power spectra, Shannon entropies, and Lyapunov exponents (Felea et al., 2009b) to further characterize the chaos transition in nuclear systems.
