# Effects of reflective scattering in nuclear collisions

> We discuss effects of reflective scattering for heavy nuclei collisions and the deconfined matter formation at the LHC and asymptotical energies. Reflective scattering in hadron collisions leads to decreasing matter density with energy beyond the LHC energies. Limiting form of energy dependence of hadron density is obtained.

# Effects of reflective scattering in nuclear collisions

## Key claim

Reflective scattering in hadron collisions, where the elastic S-matrix becomes negative at small impact parameters, reduces the available volume for hadron occupation, thereby lowering the hadron density needed to trigger percolation-driven deconfinement. This effect becomes noticeable at LHC energies around √s ≈ 5 TeV in Pb+Pb collisions and leads to a limiting form of hadron density at asymptotically high energies.

## Method

The authors apply a van der Waals-type correction to the hadron density by replacing the geometric volume V with V − p_R(s) V_R(s) N/2, where p_R(s) is the probability of reflective scattering averaged over the reflective volume V_R(s). The reflective scattering mode is implemented via a U-matrix unitarization scheme, in which the elastic S-matrix becomes negative when the input generalized reaction matrix U(s,b) exceeds unity at b=0. The radius R(s) of the reflective region grows logarithmically with energy, R(s) ~ (1/M) ln s, with M related to the pion mass.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for the onset of reflective scattering is √s_R ≈ 2 TeV; below this energy α(s)=0 and no density corrections occur.
- At the LHC energy √s ≈ 5 TeV in Pb+Pb collisions, reflective scattering effects are expected to be noticeable.
- At asymptotically high energies (s → ∞), the hadron density under spherical symmetry scales as n_R(T,μ) ~ M³/ln³ s; without spherical symmetry, the transverse-plane density scales as n_R(T,μ) ~ M²/ln² s.

## Context

This work extends the percolation mechanism of deconfinement proposed by Satz (2000s) by incorporating the reflective scattering mode, which was previously developed for hadron collisions by the same authors and others (Troshin and Tyurin, 2000s). The reflective scattering interpretation provides a dynamical origin for a repulsive-core-like effect in high-energy nuclear collisions, in contrast to static repulsive-core models used for baryons in percolation studies. The results enable predictions for initial-state dynamics and deconfined matter searches at LHC energies, introducing the concept of an energy-dependent limiting density for strongly interacting matter.
