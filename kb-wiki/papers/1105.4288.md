# Magnetic and electric contributions to the energy loss in a dynamical QCD medium

> The computation of radiative energy loss in a finite size QCD medium with dynamical constituents is a key ingredient for obtaining reliable predictions for jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. It was previously shown that energy loss in dynamical QCD medium is significantly higher compared to static QCD medium. To understand this difference, we here analyze magnetic and electric contributions to energy loss in dynamical QCD medium. We find that the significantly higher energy loss in the dynamical case is entirely due to appearance of magnetic contribution in the dynamical medium. While for asymptotically high energies, the energy loss in static and dynamical medium approach the same value, we find that the physical origin of the energy loss in these two cases is different.

## Key claim

The significantly higher radiative energy loss in a dynamical QCD medium compared to a static QCD medium is entirely due to the appearance of a magnetic (transverse gluon exchange) contribution in the dynamical case; while for asymptotically high energies the total energy loss in static and dynamical media approach the same value, the physical origin of the energy loss in these two cases is different.

## Method

The author decomposes the effective cross-section for gluon exchange in a dynamical QCD medium into longitudinal (electric) and transverse (magnetic) parts using hard-thermal-loop (HTL) gluon self-energies. These components are inserted into the previously derived first-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss formula for a finite-size dynamical medium, allowing separate numerical evaluation of the electric and magnetic contributions. The asymptotic high-energy limit is also derived analytically by taking the limit \(E^+ \rightarrow \infty\) and performing the integrations over transverse momenta and longitudinal momentum fraction.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC conditions (\(T = 225\) MeV, \(L = 5\) fm), the electric contribution in the dynamical medium is similar to (though somewhat smaller than) the total energy loss in a static QCD medium for light, charm, and bottom quarks.
- For LHC conditions (\(T = 400\) MeV, \(L = 5\) fm), the same qualitative behavior is observed: the electric contribution in the dynamical medium is comparable to the static medium energy loss.
- In the asymptotic high-energy limit, the ratio of magnetic to electric contributions to the dynamical energy loss is exactly \(1/2\), meaning two thirds of the energy loss comes from the electric (longitudinal) contribution and one third from the magnetic (transverse) contribution.
- The magnetic contribution to the exchanged gluon spectrum comes almost exclusively from low momentum transfers where \(|\mathbf{q}| \lesssim 1\) GeV.
- The ratio of magnetic to electric contributions decreases with increasing jet energy for all three quark types, approaching the asymptotic limit of \(1/2\) (data not shown in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous formalism for radiative energy loss in a dynamical QCD medium (Djordjevic 2009, Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) by decomposing the loss into electric and magnetic components to explain the 50-70% increase over the static medium result. It directly contradicts the implicit assumption that the static and dynamical energy losses have the same physical origin, showing instead that the dynamical case includes a significant magnetic contribution absent in the static case. The paper opens the question of whether a finite magnetic mass (suggested by non-perturbative approaches such as Maezawa 2010, Nakamura 2004, Hart 2000, and Bak 2000) can be consistently included in dynamical energy loss calculations, which would modify the results presented here.
