# Probing neutrino-nucleus interaction in DUNE and MicroBooNE

> The neutrino experiments utilize heavy nuclear targets to achieve high statistics neutrino-nucleus interaction event rate, which leads to systematic uncertainties in the oscillation parameters due to the nuclear effects and uncertainties in the cross-section. Understanding the interaction of neutrinos with the nucleus becomes crucial in determining the oscillation parameters with high precision. We investigate the uncertainty in quasi-elastic interaction due to nuclear effects by selecting exactly 1 proton, 0 pions, and any number of neutrons in the final state in DUNE and MicroBooNE detectors, and the effects on the neutrino oscillation in the DUNE detector. The calorimetric method with this selection can be used for accurate neutrino energy reconstruction in the quasi-elastic channel where the nuclear effects are inevitable.

## Key claim

The calorimetric method for neutrino energy reconstruction, when combined with a final-state selection of exactly 1 proton, 0 pions, and any number of neutrons, can reduce the discrepancy between reconstructed and true neutrino energy to less than 100 MeV in DUNE and MicroBooNE detectors, but this holds only under the condition that the selection criteria and detector thresholds are applied; the abstract does not specify the magnitude of the residual shift.

## Method

The authors simulated charged-current muon neutrino interactions on argon and carbon targets using the GENIE v3.01.00 and NuWro-21.09.2 Monte Carlo event generators, applying the Valencia model for quasi-elastic scattering and the Berger-Sehgal model for resonance production. They selected events with exactly 1 muon, 1 proton, 0 pions, and any number of neutrons in the final state, applied realistic detector thresholds for DUNE near detector (muon total energy 226 MeV, proton kinetic energy 3 MeV), DUNE far detector (muon kinetic energy 30 MeV, proton kinetic energy 50 MeV), and MicroBooNE (muon momentum 100 MeV/c, leading proton momentum 300 MeV/c), and reconstructed the neutrino energy using the calorimetric method with a Gaussian distribution for neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

* For DUNE near detector without event selection, the reconstructed energy distribution is shifted by approximately 0.4 GeV toward lower energies relative to the true energy distribution.
* With the selection (1 proton, 0 pions, any number of neutrons) and detector cuts applied, the shift between reconstructed and true neutrino energy is reduced to less than 100 MeV for both DUNE near and far detectors.
* For MicroBooNE detector without selection, the reconstructed energy shows a shift of approximately 0.2 GeV relative to true energy.
* The absolute value of the first oscillation maximum in the muon neutrino survival probability drops by approximately 10% when the selection is applied, compared to the true energy case.
* The axial mass for CCQE was set to 0.96 GeV/c^2 in both GENIE and NuWro, with vector mass 0.84 GeV/c^2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear effects on neutrino energy reconstruction for long-baseline experiments, particularly following the approach of Mosel (2013) for LBNE using the GiBUU model and Coloma et al. (2013) for T2K and MiniBooNE. The results demonstrate that the calorimetric method with a strict final-state selection can achieve the 100 MeV energy resolution required for DUNE physics studies as specified by LBNE (2013), and the analysis quantifies the impact of final-state interactions on the reconstructed oscillation probability, showing a notable effect on the mixing angle with comparatively lesser effect on the mass-squared difference.
