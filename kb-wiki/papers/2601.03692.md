# Role of $Σ(1660)$ in the $K^- p \toπ^0π^0Σ^0$ reaction

> The processes of $K^-p \to π^0 π^0 Σ^0$ and $K^- p \to π^0 Λ(1405)$ are studied within the effective Lagrangian approach. In addition to the ``background" contribution from the $u$-channel nucleon pole term, contribution from the $Σ(1660)$ resonance with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$ is also considered. For the $K^-p \to π^0 π^0 Σ^0$ reaction, we perform a calculation for the total and differential cross sections by considering the contribution from the $Σ(1660)$ intermediate resonance decaying into $π^0 Λ(1405)$ with $Λ(1405)$ decaying into $π^0 Σ^0$. With our model parameters, the available experimental data on both the $K^-p \to π^0 π^0 Σ^0$ and $K^- p \to π^0 Λ(1405)$ reactions can be fairly well reproduced. It is shown that we really need the contribution from the $Σ(1660)$ resonance, and that these experimental measurements could be used to determine some properties of the $Σ(1660)$ resonance.

# Wiki Page: Role of Σ(1660) in the K⁻ p → π⁰ π⁰ Σ⁰ reaction

## Key claim
The Σ(1660) resonance with Jᴾ = 1/2⁺ is necessary to reproduce the available experimental data on both the K⁻ p → π⁰ π⁰ Σ⁰ and K⁻ p → π⁰ Λ(1405) reactions within the effective Lagrangian approach, but the evidence is conditional on the model parameters and the current data have large uncertainties. The authors state that the Σ(1660) contributes mainly in the high-energy region of the total cross section and that more precise experimental data are needed to confirm its properties.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to calculate the total and differential cross sections for K⁻ p → π⁰ π⁰ Σ⁰, considering an s-channel Σ(1660) resonance decaying into π⁰ Λ(1405) (with Λ(1405) subsequently decaying into π⁰ Σ⁰) and a u-channel nucleon pole term as the non-resonant background. The product of coupling constants g_{Σ(1660)K̄N} × g_{Σ(1660)πΛ(1405)} was determined by a combined fit to 77 data points from the Crystal Ball Collaboration measurements of the K⁻ p → π⁰ π⁰ Σ⁰ reaction and total cross section data for K⁻ p → π⁰ Λ(1405), with all cutoff parameters fixed to 1000 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The product of coupling constants g_{Σ(1660)K̄N} × g_{Σ(1660)πΛ(1405)} = 1.81 ± 0.10, determined from a fit with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.46.
- The coupling constant g_{Σ(1660)K̄N} = 2.49 ± 0.88, derived using the PDG branching fraction B[Σ(1660) → K̄N] = 0.10 ± 0.05 and Σ(1660) mass 1660 ± 20 MeV and width 200 ± 100 MeV.
- The coupling constant g_{Σ(1660)πΛ(1405)} = 0.73 ± 0.26, obtained from the product and the extracted g_{Σ(1660)K̄N}.
- The branching fraction B[Σ(1660) → π⁰ Λ(1405)] = (7.2 ± 6.3)%, consistent with the PDG value (4.0 ± 2.0)% within uncertainties.
- The Σ(1660) mass was taken as 1660 MeV and its total decay width as 200 MeV; the Λ(1405) mass was 1405 MeV with width 50.5 MeV.
- The kaon momentum range studied was 0.4 to 1.0 GeV for the three-body reaction (center-of-mass energy 1.52 to 1.79 GeV) and 0.5 to 2.0 GeV for the two-body reaction (1.56 to 2.23 GeV).
- A significant peak in the K⁻ p → π⁰ Λ(1405) total cross section was predicted around p_{K⁻} = 0.75 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of the Σ(1660) resonance, which has a three-star rating in the PDG but whose existence has been questioned in some analyses: Gao (2012) found support for Σ(1660) in K⁻ n → π⁻ Λ and K⁻ p → π⁰ Λ reactions, while Zhong (2013) using a chiral quark-model approach found no clear evidence in K⁻ p → Λ π⁰, and Fernandez-Ramirez (2015) found no evidence in a unitary multichannel model of K̄N scattering. The present work builds on the Crystal Ball Collaboration (2004) measurements of K⁻ p → π⁰ π⁰ Σ⁰ with high statistics at kaon momenta from 514 to 750 MeV/c, and on the Faddeev equation results of Martinez Torres (2007) which found a peak at √s = 1656 MeV in the ππΣ system associated with Σ(1660). The results enable future experimental programs such as the KLF Collaboration at JLab using the GlueX spectrometer and the proposed Huizhou Hadron Spectrometer at HIAF to determine the coupling strength for Σ(1660) → π Λ(1405) and confirm the resonance properties.
