# Universal and non-universal finite-volume effects in the vicinity of chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD

> In this proceeding, we discuss the finite-size scaling analysis of the order parameter related to the chiral phase transition in QCD with two massless quarks. We use data obtained in lattice QCD calculations performed with highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) for a range of light quark masses, $1/240 \leq m_\ell/m_s \leq 1/27$ for different spatial volumes ($N_σ$) on Euclidean lattices with temporal extent $N_τ=8$, satisfying $3\,N_τ\leq N_σ\leq 10\,N_τ$. We observe that infinite volume extrapolated data for the order parameter agree reasonably well with the expected $O(2)$ scaling behavior even for physical ratios of the light-to-strange quark mass ratio. We quantify deviations from asymptotic scaling and perform a detailed analysis of the influence of finite-size effects in terms of temperature and quark masses at a fixed lattice cutoff. This is crucial for improving the reliability of the infinite-volume extrapolated estimate of the chiral order parameter and for a more precise determination of chiral phase transition temperature from direct Lattice QCD simulations.

# Universal and non-universal finite-volume effects in the vicinity of chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD

## Key claim

The infinite volume extrapolated improved order parameter for the chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD agrees reasonably well with the expected O(2) scaling behavior even at physical light-to-strange quark mass ratios, but only after accounting for finite-volume corrections that are close to quartic in the inverse volume scaling variable; the analysis is performed at a single lattice cutoff (Nτ=8) and does not yet provide continuum extrapolated results.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) with an O(a²) Symanzik-improved gauge action on Euclidean lattices with fixed temporal extent Nτ=8 and spatial extents Nσ satisfying 3Nτ ≤ Nσ ≤ 10Nτ. They computed the renormalization group invariant improved chiral order parameter M = Mℓ - H χℓ for light-to-strange quark mass ratios H = mℓ/ms ranging from 1/240 to 1/27, at three temperatures near the chiral phase transition temperature (142.8, 145.1, and 147.4 MeV). The finite-size dependence of the scaled order parameter M/H^(1/δ) was fitted as a function of the bare finite-volume scaling variable zL,b using an ansatz M = M0 + ac zL,b^c, with the exponent c either fixed to 3 or 4 or treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponents used for the 3-d O(2) universality class: β = 0.34864(7), δ = 4.7798(5)
- Previous estimate of chiral transition temperature on Nτ=8 lattices: Tc^(Nτ=8) = 143.7(2) MeV (Ding 2024)
- New preliminary estimate of chiral transition temperature on Nτ=8 lattices: Tc^(Nτ=8) = 144(4) MeV
- To reduce finite-size effects on the improved order parameter below 1%, aspect ratios Nσ/Nτ larger than 6 are needed for physical light-to-strange quark mass ratio H = 1/27 ≈ 0.037
- At H = 1/80 (corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass of about 80 MeV), an aspect ratio of about 7.5 is needed
- Fit results for H = 1/27: M0(T=145.1 MeV) = 15.46(7), ac = -0.95(8), c = 3.77(18)
- Fit results for H = 1/40: M0(T=145.1 MeV) = 13.66(5), ac = -0.56(8), c = 4.39(37)
- Fit results for H = 1/80: M0(T=145.1 MeV) = 11.40(4), ac = -0.23(4), c = 5.16(43)
- Non-universal parameter h0^(-1/δ) ≈ 38, obtained from the limit as H→0 of M(144 MeV, H)/[H^(1/δ)(1-1/δ)]
- Non-universal parameter z0 ≈ 1.2, obtained from a fit to data at H = 1/240

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling analysis of the QCD chiral phase transition initiated by Karsch (2023) and the authors' previous studies (Mitra 2024, 2025). It provides a direct lattice QCD determination of finite-volume effects on the improved chiral order parameter without presuming the underlying universality class, in contrast to earlier studies that assumed O(4) or O(2) scaling and constructed universal scaling ansatze. The results enable more precise determinations of the chiral phase transition temperature from lattice QCD simulations and provide quantitative guidance on the required aspect ratios for future calculations at smaller quark masses, including those needed to locate the QCD critical point in the phase diagram (Ding 2024, Goswami 2025).
