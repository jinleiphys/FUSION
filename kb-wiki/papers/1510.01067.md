# Spectrum of heavy baryons in the quark model

> Single- and double- heavy baryons are studied in the constituent quark model. The model Hamiltonian is chosen as a standard one with two exceptions : (1) The color-Coulomb term depend on quark masses, and (2) an antisymmetric $LS$ force is introduced. Model parameters are fixed by the strange baryon spectra, $Λ$ and $Σ$ baryons. The masses of the observed charmed and bottomed baryons are, then, fairly well reproduced. Our focus is on the low-lying negative-parity states, in which the heavy baryons show specific excitation modes reflecting the mass differences of heavy and light quarks. By changing quark masses from the SU(3) limit to the strange quark mass, further to the charm and bottom quark masses, we demonstrate that the spectra change from the SU(3) symmetry patterns to the heavy quark symmetry ones.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the low-lying negative-parity spectra of single- and double-heavy baryons smoothly transition from SU(3) symmetry patterns to heavy quark symmetry patterns as the heavy quark mass is increased from the SU(3) limit (equal masses) through strange, charm, and bottom masses, but only when an antisymmetric spin-orbit (ALS) force is introduced to guarantee that heavy quark spin dependence vanishes in the infinite-mass limit.

## Method

The authors solve a non-relativistic constituent quark model using the Gaussian expansion method for three-quark systems, with a Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, linear confinement, and short-range interactions (color-Coulomb, spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit forces). The color-Coulomb coupling strength is made dependent on the reduced mass of each quark pair, and an antisymmetric LS force is added to ensure heavy quark spin symmetry in the infinite-mass limit. All 10 model parameters are first fitted to reproduce the strange baryon spectra (Λ, Σ, Ξ), then applied without change to predict charmed and bottom baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- Strange baryon parameters: light quark mass m_q = 300 MeV, strange quark mass m_s = 510 MeV, charm quark mass m_c = 1750 MeV, bottom quark mass m_b = 5112 MeV
- String tension parameter b = 0.165 GeV², Coulomb strength parameter K = 90 MeV
- Spin-spin coupling α^ss = 1.2, spin-orbit coupling α^so = 0.077 (set equal to tensor coupling α^ten)
- Constant term C = -1139 MeV, smearing parameter Λ = 3.5 fm⁻¹
- Predicted mass differences for Σ baryon spin doublets: Σ_s(3/2⁺) − Σ_s(1/2⁺) = 174 MeV, Σ_c(3/2⁺) − Σ_c(1/2⁺) = 63 MeV, Σ_b(3/2⁺) − Σ_b(1/2⁺) = 22 MeV

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model studies of heavy baryons by Copley (1979) and Roberts and Pervin (2007), but adds a systematic interpolation between the SU(3) limit and the heavy quark limit by varying the heavy quark mass continuously. The introduction of a mass-dependent color-Coulomb term follows suggestions from lattice QCD calculations by Kawanai (2011), while the antisymmetric LS force is introduced to enforce heavy quark spin symmetry. The results enable future experimental studies of baryon structure planned at J-PARC and FAIR, particularly for understanding the roles of λ-mode and ρ-mode excitations in heavy baryon spectra.
