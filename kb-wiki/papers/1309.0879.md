# Charge Symmetry Breaking and Parity Violating Electron Scattering

> I review the effects of charge symmetry breaking CSB on electromagnetic form factors and how that influences extraction of information regarding nucleon strangeness content and the weak mixing angle. It seems that CSB effects are very modest and should not impact the analysis of experiments.

# Charge Symmetry Breaking and Parity Violating Electron Scattering

## Key claim
Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) effects on nucleon electromagnetic form factors are likely very modest (of order 0.1% of the anomalous magnetic moment) and should not impact the analysis of parity-violating electron scattering experiments, but the uncertainty in CSB effects from chiral perturbation theory calculations (Kubis and Lewis, 2006) is large enough to hinder extraction of nucleon strangeness content and the weak mixing angle unless constrained by nucleon-nucleon scattering data.

## Method
The author reviews his own 1998 non-relativistic quark model calculation of CSB effects on proton electromagnetic form factors using perturbation theory with the light quark mass difference and one gluon exchange operators, then compares it to the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of Kubis and Lewis (2006) which includes pion loop graphs and resonance saturation via rho-omega mixing. The author further evaluates the Kubis-Lewis parameters against constraints from nucleon-nucleon scattering data on CSB potentials and discusses a relativistic chiral perturbation theory approach as an alternative.

## Key numbers
- The 1998 quark model calculation found CSB effects on electromagnetic form factors less than about 1% relative to the values of G_E and G_M.
- Kubis and Lewis (2006) found delta G_M approximately 0.02 ± 0.02 for Q^2 between 0 and 0.3 GeV^2.
- The vector coupling constant from Kubis-Lewis parameters is g_omega^2/4π ~ 150, compared to typical nucleon-nucleon scattering values of g_omega^2/4π ~ 10-20.
- Preliminary relativistic chiral perturbation theory results show contributions to delta G_M of about 0.01 at small Q^2.
- The author's 1998 calculation found CSB effects less than 0.002 on electromagnetic form factors.
- The scale of CSB is typified by the neutron-proton mass difference to proton mass ratio of about one part in 1000.

## Context
This work extends the author's own quark model calculation (Miller, 1997) and addresses the chiral perturbation theory calculation of Kubis and Lewis (2006), which found larger CSB uncertainties. The paper connects CSB in electromagnetic form factors to the broader context of nucleon-nucleon scattering CSB studies (Henley, 1979; Miller, 1990, 1994, 2006) and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Coon, 1987; Blunden, 1987). The results enable improved extraction of nucleon strangeness content and the weak mixing angle from parity-violating electron scattering experiments, as reviewed by Armstrong and McKeown (2012).
