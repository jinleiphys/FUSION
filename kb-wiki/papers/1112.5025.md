# Remarks on the complex branch points in pi-N scattering amplitude and the multiple poles structure of resonances

> A simple heuristic argument to understand the existence of branch points in the unphysical sheet for pi-N scattering amplitude is presented. It is based on a hypothesis that the singularity structure of the pi-N scattering amplitude is a smooth varying function of the pion mass. We find that, in general, multiple poles structure of a resonance is a direct mathematical consequence when additional Riemann surface is included in the study and the two-pole structure found to correspond to the Roper resonance is a good example.

# Remarks on the complex branch points in pi-N scattering amplitude and the multiple poles structure of resonances

## Key claim

The two-pole structure found to correspond to the Roper resonance is a direct mathematical consequence of including additional Riemann surfaces in the analysis, and in general multiple poles (either two or four) will be associated with any resonance when multi-Riemann sheets are considered, though the physical significance of poles lying in sheets not directly reachable from the physical sheet remains to be further investigated and established.

## Method

The author presents a heuristic argument based on the hypothesis that the singularity structure of the pi-N scattering amplitude varies smoothly with the pion mass, using lattice QCD results for the masses of the nucleon, Delta(1232), pi-N, and pi-Delta as functions of pion mass to illustrate how the pi-Delta branch point moves into the complex plane as the pion mass decreases. The author then analyzes simple mathematical functions of the form f(z) = sqrt(z - z_a) + sqrt(z - z_b) + g(z)/(z - z_0) to demonstrate that a pole in the presence of two additive square-root branch cuts will appear in multiple Riemann sheets, with the number of sheets and poles depending on the functional form of the denominator.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic channel pi-pi-N appears at center-of-mass energy W = 1216 MeV
- Lattice QCD data show that the lines representing M_Delta and m_pi + M_N cross at around m_pi = 300 MeV
- An experimental value of Gamma_Delta = 120 MeV for the width of the Delta is assumed for illustration of the LQCD result
- Two almost degenerate poles near the pi-Delta threshold and one higher mass pole in the pi-N P_11 channel all evolve from a single bare state according to the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels model

## Context

This work extends the long history of including complex cuts in pion-nucleon partial wave analysis, which began with Cutkovsky (1979) who treated three-body pi-pi-N channels via coupled-channels approaches with quasi two-body channels like pi-Delta, eta-N, and rho-N. The two-pole structure for the Roper resonance was first noticed in SAID's pion-nucleon partial-wave analysis by Arndt (1985) and later confirmed by Cutkovsky and Wang (1990), who found two and four nearly degenerate poles at 1470 and 1700 MeV respectively. The EBAC group (Suzuki 2010) demonstrated via the dynamical coupled-channels model of Julia-Diaz (2007) that these poles evolve from a single bare state, while the DMT model (Kamalov 2001) and Sato-Lee model (Sato 1996) find only one pole for the Roper because they do not include the pi-Delta complex branch cut. This work enables further investigation into the physical significance of poles lying in Riemann sheets not directly reachable from the physical sheet.
