# Conformal Symmetry in nuclear structure

> The purpose of this paper is to introduce the unitary limit as applied in systems of cold atoms into collective states of heavy, even-even nuclei and to identify a related physical example. This is accompanied by the determination of observables of conformal symmetry in nuclear structure. A Hamiltonian is defined that governs the scattering process of an incident pair of two slow neutrons onto a heavy, even-even target nucleus in the framework of the Interacting Boson Model of nuclear structure. A unitary pair-collective state interaction is introduced along with the effective range expansion of a pair-collective state scattering length. The solutions to the coupled channels equations provide a scattering state for the two neutrons that couples with intermediate states of the $A+2n$ compound nucleus. The unitary limit manifests itself when the intermediate states correspond to pair-collective state resonances the positions of which coincide with the energies of IBM states of the closed channels. The position and the width of each resonance is measurable via the fluctuation of the cross section which tunes the pair-collective state scattering length. Conformal symmetry is represented in a tower of equally spaced states that emerges at the unitary limit from two-boson excitations on the IBM state of the closed channel. These tower states are experimentally testable as a regularity pattern of fluctuations of the cross section, in terms of regular positions and widths. The manifestation of the unitary limit and conformal symmetry via fluctuations of the cross section indicates the $A+2n$ compound nucleus as a physical laboratory for the examination of the BCS-BEC crossover, of an underlying critical point and of algebras with infinite number of generators.

## Key claim

This paper introduces the unitary limit, a concept from cold-atom physics where the scattering length becomes infinite, into the collective states of heavy, even-even nuclei, and identifies the compound nucleus formed by two slow neutrons incident on a heavy target as a physical system where conformal symmetry can be observed. The claim is hedged by the condition that the unitary limit manifests only when intermediate states of the compound nucleus correspond to pair-collective state resonances whose positions coincide with the energies of Interacting Boson Model (IBM) states of the closed channels.

## Method

The author constructs a Hamiltonian for the scattering of two slow neutrons on a heavy, even-even target nucleus within the framework of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) in its O(6) dynamical symmetry limit. The pair-collective state interaction is approximated by a zero-range delta interaction, and the Feshbach formalism is used to couple an open channel (target ground state) with closed channels (excited IBM states). The coupled channels equations are solved to obtain a scattering state for the neutron pair, and the unitary limit is reached when the pair-collective state scattering length diverges, which occurs when the positions of intermediate-state resonances coincide with the energies of IBM states in the closed channels.

## Key numbers

* The s-wave scattering amplitude reaches a maximum value of \(|f_{k_r}| \leq 8/k_r\) when \(1/a_r(k_r)=0\), where \(k_r\) is the conjugate wavenumber of the pair relative to the collective state.
* The cross section for the s-wave component is given by \(\sigma = (4\pi)^3 \sin^2\delta_0 / k_r^2 = (4\pi)^3 / (1/a_r^2(k_r) + k_r^2)\), with \(\delta_0\) the s-wave phase shift.
* The width of the resonance formed by the incident pair with the collective state is \(\Gamma = \hbar^2 k_r / (M r^*)\), where \(r^*\) is the effective range of the pair-collective state interaction.
* The effective range expansion for the inverse scattering length is \(1/a_r(k_r) = 1/a_r - \frac{1}{2} k_r^2 r^* + \cdots\), valid for low \(k_r\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of the unitary limit and conformal symmetry, previously applied to cold atoms (Randeria, Son) and light nuclei (Konig), into the domain of heavy even-even nuclei by combining the Feshbach formalism (Feshbach) with the Interacting Boson Model (IBM). The paper identifies the compound nucleus formed in two-neutron transfer reactions as a physical laboratory for studying the BCS-BEC crossover and critical-point symmetries such as E(5), which already implies conformal invariance at the critical point of a second-order quantum phase transition between the U(5) and O(6) limits of the IBM.
