# Electroweak Interactions in a Chiral Effective Lagrangian for Nuclei

> We have studied electroweak (EW) interactions in quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) effective field theory (EFT). The Lorentz-covariant EFT contains nucleon, pion, $Δ$, isoscalar scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) fields, and isovector vector ($ρ$) fields. The lagrangian exhibits a nonlinear realization of (approximate) $SU(2)_L \otimes SU(2)_R$ chiral symmetry and incorporates vector meson dominance. First, we discuss the EW interactions at the quark level. Then we include EW interactions in QHD EFT by using the background-field technique. The completed QHD EFT has a nonlinear realization of $SU(2)_L \otimes SU(2)_R \otimes U(1)_B$ (chiral symmetry and baryon number conservation), as well as realizations of other symmetries including Lorentz-invariance, $C$, $P$, and $T$. Meanwhile, as we know, chiral symmetry is manifestly broken due to the nonzero quark masses; the $P$ and $C$ symmetries are also broken because of weak interactions. These breaking patterns are parameterized in a general way in the EFT. Moreover, we have included the $Δ$ resonance as manifest degrees of freedom in our QHD EFT, with a discussion of the irrelevance of the well-known pathologies involving high-spin fields from the modern EFT perspective. This enables us to discuss physics at the kinematics where the resonance becomes important. As a result, the effective theory uses hadronic degrees of freedom, satisfies the constraints due to QCD (symmetries and their breaking pattern), and is calibrated to strong-interaction phenomena. Applications to (anti)neutrino scattering are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete treatment of electroweak interactions in a chiral effective Lagrangian for nuclei (quantum hadrodynamics EFT) that includes nucleon, pion, Delta resonance, and meson fields, with the claim that this framework respects all relevant QCD symmetries and their breaking patterns, but the actual construction relies on the background-field technique and the assumption that the Lagrangian can be truncated at low orders using naive dimensional analysis and naturalness, with the caveat that "seagull" terms of higher order in the couplings are not discussed.

## Method

The authors construct a Lorentz-covariant effective field theory containing nucleon, pion, Delta (1232), isoscalar scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) fields, and isovector vector (rho) fields, with a nonlinear realization of approximate SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R chiral symmetry and vector meson dominance. They introduce electroweak interactions by applying the background-field technique, matching the QCD-level background fields to Standard Model electroweak gauge bosons (W, Z, photon) through the electroweak synthesis relations, and then constructing hadronic interaction terms that transform appropriately under chiral, C, P, and T symmetries. The Lagrangian is truncated using power counting (index nu = d + n/2 + b, where d is derivatives, n is fermion fields, b is heavy meson fields) and naive dimensional analysis, with parameters calibrated to strong-interaction phenomena.

## Key numbers

- The metric convention is g_{mu nu} = diag(1, -1, -1, -1)_{mu nu} and the Levi-Civita symbol convention is epsilon^{0123} = 1.
- The baryon number B = 1/3 is assigned to quarks in the background-field construction.
- The weak mixing angle relations are g sin(theta_w) = g' cos(theta_w) = e, where g and g' are the SU(2)_L and U(1)_Y gauge couplings.
- The pion decay constant f_pi appears in the definition of the chiral field U = exp[2i pi_i t^i / f_pi] and in the mesonic Lagrangian term (1/4) f_pi^2 Tr[partial_mu U (partial^mu U)^dagger].
- The pion mass m_pi appears in the mesonic Lagrangian term (1/4) f_pi^2 m_pi^2 Tr(U + U^dagger - 2).
- The rho meson mass m_rho and vector meson mass m_v appear in the mesonic Lagrangian with coupling modifications (1 + eta_rho g_s phi/M) m_rho^2 Tr(rho_mu rho^mu) and (1 + eta_1 g_s phi/M + eta_2 g_s^2 phi^2/(2M^2)) m_v^2 V_mu V^mu.
- The scalar meson mass m_s appears in the term -(1/2 + kappa_3/3! g_s phi/M + kappa_4/4! g_s^2 phi^2/M^2) m_s^2 phi^2.
- The nucleon mass M appears throughout as the natural scale for dimensionless couplings, e.g., in the tensor couplings -f_rho g_rho/(4M) psibar{N} rho_{mu nu} sigma^{mu nu} N and -f_v g_v/(4M) psibar{N} V_{mu nu} sigma^{mu nu} N.

## Context

This work extends the QHD effective field theory framework developed by Furnstahl, Serot, and collaborators (Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1995-1998) by providing a complete treatment of electroweak interactions including the Delta resonance as explicit degrees of freedom, following the background-field method of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984). The formalism enables calculations of (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections needed for neutrino-oscillation experiments and neutrino astrophysics, and it builds on earlier applications of the QHD EFT to the isovector axial-vector current in nuclei by Ananyan, Serot, and Walecka (2002). The paper also addresses technical issues with spin-3/2 fields in relativistic quantum field theory following Pascalutsa and Vanderhaeghen (2008) and Krebs, Gasparyan, and Epelbaum (2010).
