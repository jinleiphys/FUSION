# A Euclidean Monte-Carlo-informed route to ground-state preparation for quantum simulation of scalar field theory

> Quantum simulators hold great promise for studying real-time (Minkowski) dynamics of quantum field theories. Nonetheless, preparing non-trivial initial states remains a major obstacle. Euclidean-time Monte-Carlo methods yield ground-state spectra and static correlation functions that can, in principle, guide state preparation. In this work, we exploit this classical information to bridge Euclidean and Minkowski descriptions for a (1+1)-dimensional interacting scalar field theory. We propose variational ansatz families which achieve comparable ground-state energies, yet exhibit distinct correlations and local non-Gaussianity. By optimizing selected wavefunction moments with Monte-Carlo data, we obtain ansatzes that can be efficiently translated into quantum circuits. Our algorithmic cost analysis shows these circuits' gate complexity scales polynomially in system size. Our work paves the way for systematically leveraging classically-computed information to prepare initial states in quantum field theories of interest in nature.

# A Euclidean Monte-Carlo-informed route to ground-state preparation for quantum simulation of scalar field theory

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that ground-state correlation functions from Euclidean path-integral Monte Carlo can be used to optimize variational wavefunction ansatzes for a (1+1)-dimensional interacting scalar field theory, producing quantum circuits whose gate complexity scales polynomially with system size. However, the method assumes that the stellar rank R and spatial spread Q of the ansatz remain much smaller than the system size N, and the authors note that larger R and Q values may be needed as the continuum limit is approached.

## Method

The authors discretize (1+1)D phi^4 theory on a periodic lattice with N=10 sites and use path-integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) to compute ground-state two-point correlation functions and local moment ratios. They introduce the (R,Q) ansatz, a finite stellar-rank bosonic state consisting of a product of single-mode squeezing operators applied to a core state generated by a polynomial in the field operators with maximum degree R and spatial spread Q. The ansatz parameters are optimized using an objective function that combines the energy expectation value with weighted penalties for deviations from PIMC-computed target moments. The optimized ansatz is then mapped to a qubit-based quantum circuit using a sparse state preparation algorithm and digitized squeezing operators.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size N = 10 sites, with periodic boundary conditions
- Four values of (m^2, lambda) are taken approaching (0,0) along a straight line in the symmetric phase, with specific values including (m^2, lambda) = (0.6, 1.5)
- The (R,Q) = (2,2) ansatz is used for moment optimization, with target sets including local moments up to phi^10 and the two-point function at separation 4
- The core state preparation circuit uses O(N^2 n_q(R) |c|_{R,Q}) CNOT gates, where |c|_{R,Q} is a combinatorial factor depending on R and Q
- The squeezing operator implementation uses O(N Lambda K) CNOT gates for the unary mapping and O(N Lambda K log Lambda) for the binary mapping, where Lambda is the Fock-space truncation and K is the number of Trotter layers

## Context

This work extends the stellar hierarchy approach for bosonic systems introduced by Chabaud (2020) to the context of quantum field theory ground-state preparation. It addresses the major obstacle of preparing non-trivial initial states for quantum simulation of real-time dynamics, which has been identified as a bottleneck by Jordan (2011, 2012) and others. The method contrasts with the Gaussian Effective Potential (GEP) ansatz of Stevenson (1984, 1985), which reproduces two-point functions well but cannot capture non-Gaussian correlations. The work enables a systematic classical determination of quantum circuits for preparing ground states of field theories, with the long-term goal of extending to theories with fermions and gauge bosons, including lattice QCD.
