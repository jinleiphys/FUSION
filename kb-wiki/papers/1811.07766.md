# The QCD Phase Diagram from Statistical Model Analysis

> In high multiplicity nucleus-nucleus collisions baryon-antibaryon annihilation and regeneration occur during the final hadronic expansion phase, thus distorting the initial equilibrium multiplicity ratios. We quantify the modifications employing the hybrid UrQMD transport model and apply them to the grand canonical partition functions of the Statistical Hadronization Model(SHM). We analyze minimum bias and central Pb+Pb collision data at SPS and LHC energy. We explain the Pion to Proton ratio puzzle. We also reproduce the deuteron to proton ratio at LHC energy by the SHM, and by UrQMD after attaching a phase space coalescence process. We discuss the resulting (T,$μ_{B}$) diagram.

## Key claim
The paper argues that post-hadronization baryon-antibaryon annihilation in high-multiplicity nucleus-nucleus collisions distorts the primordial hadro-chemical equilibrium ratios, and that correcting for these distortions with UrQMD transport model modification factors raises the extracted hadronization temperature from about 155 MeV to about 164 MeV at zero baryochemical potential, resolving the pion-to-proton ratio puzzle.

## Method
The authors used the hybrid UrQMD transport model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at SPS and LHC energies, comparing multiplicities directly after hadronization (Cooper-Frye sampling with subsequent strong decays) to those after the full hadronic afterburner stage. The resulting species-by-species modification factors were applied as multipliers to the grand canonical partition functions of the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM), allowing reconstruction of the "true" hadronization point. For deuteron production, a phase-space coalescence routine was attached to the UrQMD decoupling stage, enforcing closeness in both coordinate and momentum space.

## Key numbers
- Temperature at zero baryochemical potential after correction: 164 ± 3 MeV (from SHM fit to central ALICE Pb+Pb data at 2.76 TeV with UrQMD modification factors)
- Temperature at zero baryochemical potential from quadratic fit to all five data points: 164 ± 2 MeV
- Standard SHM fit without modification factors gave T = 155 ± 4 MeV for central ALICE Pb+Pb data at 2.76 TeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom improved from about 2 (standard SHM) to about 0.5 (with UrQMD modification factors) for central ALICE data
- Curvature coefficient of the QCD pseudo-critical line from quadratic fit: 0.0048 ± 0.0026
- Central pion yield increase of about 5% due to afterburner effects, corresponding to about 110 extra pions
- Energies analyzed: LHC at 2.76 TeV, SPS at 17.3, 8.7, 7.6 GeV, and AGS at 4.75 GeV

## Context
This work extends the Statistical Hadronization Model approach of Becattini (2000s) by incorporating final-state interaction effects previously neglected in A+A collisions, following the authors' earlier demonstrations (Stock et al., 2017-2018) that baryon-antibaryon annihilation distorts multiplicity ratios. The reconstructed hadronization temperatures are higher than lattice QCD predictions based on Polyakov loop or fluctuation observables (Borsanyi et al., 2010s), which the authors attribute to the broad cross-over transition where different observables freeze out at different mean temperatures. The deuteron-to-proton ratio results reconcile the SHM with the coalescence model of Mrowczynski (1990s) and enable a consistent mapping of the QCD phase diagram from AGS through LHC energies.
