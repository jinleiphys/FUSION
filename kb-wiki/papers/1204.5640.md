# The Baryonic Phase in Holographic Descriptions of the QCD Phase Diagram

> We study holographic models of the QCD temperature-chemical potential phase diagram based on the D3/D7 system with chiral symmetry breaking. The baryonic phase may be included through linked D5-D7 systems. In a previous analysis of a model with a running gauge coupling a baryonic phase was shown to exist to arbitrarily large chemical potential. Here we explore this phase in a more generic phenomenological setting with a step function dilaton profile. The change in dilaton generates a linear confining $\bar{q}q$ potential and opposes the screening effect of temperature. We show that the persistence of the baryonic phase depends on the step size and that QCD-like phase diagrams can be described. The baryonic phase's existence is qualitatively linked to the existence of confinement in Wilson loop computations in the background.

## Key claim

The persistence of the baryonic phase in holographic models of the QCD phase diagram depends on the step size of a phenomenological dilaton profile; for sufficiently large changes in the dilaton between the UV and IR, the baryonic phase exists to arbitrarily large chemical potential, but for more modest changes the baryonic phase can be confined to a finite region of the phase diagram that resembles QCD expectations.

## Method

The authors study probe D7 branes in an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background with a step-function dilaton profile that transitions between two conformal regimes, generating a linear confining potential. Baryon vertices are introduced via D5 branes wrapped on the S^5, and the D7 and D5 branes are linked by strings that shrink to a point, with a force balancing condition at the junction. The free energy of the combined D7-D5 system is computed and compared to the quark phase (D7 ending on the black hole horizon) to determine the preferred phase at each temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For dilaton step parameter A < 2.1, the model is always in the chirally symmetric phase with the massless D7 embedding L(ρ)=0 at all temperatures.
- The baryon vertex solutions exist for parameters A=10, λ=1.715, with two possible vertices for each density value; the energetically preferred one is always that with the largest radius.
- At fixed density d=5, A=10, λ=1.715, baryon vertex solutions exist for temperatures w_H=0.1, 0.345, 0.8, and 1.5, with typically two solutions at each temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of the QCD phase diagram using the D3/D7 system, including analyses with a background magnetic field (Evans 2010) and with a running dilaton (Evans 2011, Gwak 2012). The earlier analysis by Gwak (2012) found a baryonic phase that persisted to arbitrarily large chemical potential, similar to the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Bergman 2007). The present paper shows that by tuning the dilaton step size, the baryonic phase can be made to occupy only a finite region of the phase diagram, more closely matching QCD expectations. This enables future construction of more realistic holographic QCD phase diagrams with both chiral and baryonic transitions.
