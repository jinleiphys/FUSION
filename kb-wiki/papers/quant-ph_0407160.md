# An Algebraic Construction of Generalized Coherent States for Shape-Invariant Potentials

> Generalized coherent states for shape invariant potentials are constructed using an algebraic approach based on supersymmetric quantum mechanics. We show this generalized formalism is able to: a) supply the essential requirements necessary to establish a connection between classical and quantum formulations of a given system (continuity of labeling, resolution of unity, temporal stability, and action identity); b) reproduce results already known for shape-invariant systems, like harmonic oscillator, double anharmonic, Poschl-Teller and self-similar potentials and; c) point to a formalism that provides an unified description of the different kind of coherent states for quantum systems.

## Key claim

Generalized coherent states for all shape-invariant potentials can be constructed using an algebraic approach based on supersymmetric quantum mechanics, and these states satisfy the four essential requirements (continuity of labeling, resolution of unity, temporal stability, and action identity) needed for a connection between classical and quantum formulations, provided the functional \( \mathcal{Z}_j \) is chosen appropriately to accommodate the specific potential parameter relations.

## Method

The authors define a generalized coherent state as \( |z;a_j\rangle = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \{z\,\mathcal{Z}_j\,\hat{B}_-^{-1}\}^n |\Psi_0\rangle \), where \(\hat{B}_-\) and \(\hat{B}_+\) are creation and annihilation operators built from the supersymmetric partner Hamiltonians and a similarity transformation that shifts the potential parameters. They then verify that this state satisfies label continuity, overcompleteness (via a moment problem for the weight function), temporal stability (by redefining \(\mathcal{Z}_j\) with a phase factor), and the action identity (showing \(\langle \hat{H} \rangle = \hbar\Omega |z\mathcal{Z}_{j-1}|^2\)). The formalism is applied to shape-invariant systems classified by the Infeld-Hull factorization method (types A, B, C, D) and to self-similar potentials.

## Key numbers

* For type (C) and (D) systems (including the harmonic oscillator), the remainder \(R(a_n) = \gamma\) with \(\gamma = \sqrt{2\hbar/(m\Omega)}\,\beta\), and the coherent state reduces to the standard bosonic form \(|z;a_r\rangle = e^{-|z|^2/2} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (z^n/\sqrt{n!}) |n\rangle\) after redefining \(z \to cz/\sqrt{\gamma}\).
* For type (C) with a specific choice \(\mathcal{Z}_j = \sqrt{g(a_1; -\gamma/\eta, 1)} e^{-i\alpha R(a_1)}\), the normalization factor is \(\mathcal{N}(|z|^2;a_r) = (1-|z|^2)^{-\rho/2}\) with the restriction \(|z| < 1\), where \(\rho = a_1/\eta\) and \(\eta = \sqrt{\hbar/(2m\Omega)}\).
* For type (A) with \(\rho = 1/2\) and \(\mathcal{Z}_j = c\) constant, the coherent state is \(|z;a_r\rangle = \sqrt{\text{sech}(|z|)} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (z^n/\sqrt{(2n)!}) |\Psi_n\rangle\), with the resolution of unity obtained using the measure \(w(|z|^2;a_r) = e^{-|z|}/(2|z|)\).
* For type (A) with a different functional choice, the normalization factor is \(\mathcal{N}(|z|^2;a_r) = (1-|z|^2)^{(\nu+1)/2}\) with \(|z| < 1\), where \(\nu = 2a_1/\eta\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic construction of coherent states for self-similar potentials (Aleixo and Balantekin, earlier works) to all shape-invariant systems classified by Infeld and Hull (1951). It unifies the Barut-Girardello, Perelomov, and intelligent-state definitions under a single formalism based on supersymmetric quantum mechanics. The results enable the systematic construction of coherent states for exactly-solvable potentials beyond the harmonic oscillator, including double anharmonic, Poschl-Teller, and self-similar potentials, and provide a framework for studying classical-quantum correspondence in these systems.
