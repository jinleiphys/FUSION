# Coupling of Dipole Mode to gamma-unstable Quadrupole Oscillations

> The coupling of the high-lying dipole mode to the low-lying quadrupole modes for the case of deformed gamma-unstable nuclei is studied. Results from the geometrical model are compared to those obtained within the dipole boson model. Consistent results are obtained in both models. The dipole boson model is treated within the intrinsic frame, with subsequent projection onto the laboratory frame. As an application, calculations of photonuclear cross-sections in gamma-unstable nuclei are presented.

# Coupling of Dipole Mode to gamma-unstable Quadrupole Oscillations

## Key claim

The coupling of the high-lying dipole mode to low-lying quadrupole oscillations in gamma-unstable nuclei produces a dipole strength distribution that is symmetric around the unperturbed dipole energy only in the limit of tau-degeneracy; for realistic O(6) nuclei where the tau-splitting is non-negligible, the distribution becomes strongly asymmetric with most transition strength concentrated on the lowest states, contradicting the simple adiabatic geometrical model prediction.

## Method

The authors compared two theoretical approaches: the geometrical (hydrodynamical) collective model and the interacting boson model (IBM) extended with a dipole p-boson. For the IBM, calculations were performed in the laboratory frame using numerical diagonalization with the codes GDR and GDRT, and in the intrinsic frame using a boson condensate ground state with subsequent projection onto the laboratory frame via Bes functions. The O(6) limit of IBM (corresponding to gamma-unstable shapes) was studied with the quadrupole operator parameter chi set to zero, and the dipole-quadrupole coupling was taken as a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction.

## Key numbers

- Unperturbed dipole energy epsilon_1 = 15 MeV used in all IBM calculations
- Dipole-quadrupole coupling kappa = 0.2 MeV used in all IBM calculations
- Number of sd bosons N = 15 used in all IBM calculations
- For the tau-degenerate case (b = 0.00015 MeV), the 2_1+ excitation energy is 0.0012 MeV
- For b = 0.0015 MeV, the 2_1+ excitation energy is 0.012 MeV
- For b = 0.015 MeV, the 2_1+ excitation energy is 0.120 MeV
- For b = 0.15 MeV, the 2_1+ excitation energy is 1.2 MeV
- In the geometrical model, the total splitting 2*Delta is proportional to the quadrupole deformation beta_0, with Delta = epsilon_1 * sqrt(2/15) * (kappa_1/C_1) * beta_0
- The IBM intrinsic frame gives Delta_IBM = 2 * sqrt(2/3) * (beta_IBM/(1+beta_IBM^2)) * kappa * N
- For photoabsorption cross-sections, widths used: constant 2.5 MeV, or power-law with Gamma_0 = 0.026 * E_0^delta (MeV) and delta = 1.91, giving widths from about 3.5 to 6.0 MeV for energies 13 to 17 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of dipole-quadrupole coupling from well-deformed (SU(3)) nuclei, previously treated by Scholtz and Hahne (1987), to the gamma-unstable (O(6)) case. The geometrical model treatment follows the dynamic collective model of Danos and Greiner (1964). The authors show that unlike the SU(3) case where rotational excitation energy is low and the adiabatic geometrical model works well, for O(6) nuclei the non-degeneracy in tau is crucial and the simple adiabatic picture fails. The results enable future experimental tests: no photoabsorption data exist for O(6) nuclei such as 196Pt or 134Ba, and such measurements could determine whether the tau-projected dipole boson model predictions are correct.
