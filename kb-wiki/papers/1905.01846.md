# Equation of states and charmonium suppression in Heavy ion collisions

> The present article is the follow-up of our work Bottomonium suppression in quasi-particle model, where we have extended the study for charmonium states using quasi-particle model in terms of quasi-gluons and quasi quarks/antiquarks as a equation of state. By employing medium modification to a heavy quark potential thermodynamic observables {\em viz.} pressure, energy density, speed of sound etc. have been calculated which nicely fit with the lattice equation of state for gluon, massless and as well {\em massive} flavored plasma. For obtaining the thermodynamic observables we employed the debye mass in the quasi particle picture. We extended the quasi-particle model to calculate charmonium suppression in an expanding, dissipative strongly interacting QGP medium (SIQGP). We obtained the suppression pattern for charmonium states with respect to the number of participants at mid-rapidity and compared it with the experimental data (CMS JHEP) and (CMS PAS) at LHC energy (Pb+Pb collisions, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= $2.76$ TeV).

## Key claim

The authors claim that their quasi-particle model, using a medium-modified Cornell potential with a nonvanishing confining string term and a quasi-particle Debye mass, produces thermodynamic observables (pressure, energy density, speed of sound) that fit lattice QCD results for pure gauge, 2-flavor, 3-flavor, (2+1)-flavor, and 4-flavor QGP, and that the resulting charmonium suppression pattern for inclusive J/psi matches CMS experimental data at LHC energy, though the survival probability for sequentially produced J/psi is only "slightly higher" than for directly produced J/psi and is "closer to" (not identical to) the experimental results.

## Method

The authors employed the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus string term) and modified it in-medium using the dielectric permittivity derived from the static limit of the gluon self-energy, obtaining a temperature-dependent potential. They computed the Debye mass from the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) using quasi-parton thermal distributions for two different hot QCD equations of state: EoS1 (O(g^5)) and EoS2 (O(g^6 ln(1/g))). Using this Debye mass, they calculated thermodynamic observables (pressure, energy density, speed of sound) for various flavor numbers and then computed dissociation temperatures for charmonium states (J/psi, psi', chi_c) by solving the Schrodinger equation with the medium-modified potential. Finally, they estimated the centrality-dependent survival probability of charmonium in an expanding, dissipative strongly interacting QGP (SIQGP) for three values of shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio (eta/s = 0, 0.08, and 0.3) and compared with CMS data.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation temperature T_D for J/psi: 1.60 T_c (EoS1) and 1.64 T_c (EoS2) for a 3-flavor QGP
- Dissociation temperature T_D for psi': 1.29 T_c (EoS1) and 1.36 T_c (EoS2) for a 3-flavor QGP
- Dissociation temperature T_D for chi_c: 1.40 T_c (EoS1) and 1.46 T_c (EoS2) for a 3-flavor QGP
- Screening energy density epsilon_s for J/psi: 9.94 (SIQGP) and 9.84 (ideal) for EoS1; 11.05 (SIQGP) and 10.93 (ideal) for EoS2
- Screening energy density epsilon_s for psi': 4.10 (SIQGP) and 4.09 (ideal) for EoS1; 4.99 (SIQGP) and 4.98 (ideal) for EoS2
- Screening energy density epsilon_s for chi_c: 5.63 (SIQGP) and 5.61 (ideal) for EoS1; 6.75 (SIQGP) and 6.72 (ideal) for EoS2
- Speed of sound c_s^2 for J/psi in SIQGP: 0.330 (EoS1) and 0.331 (EoS2)
- Charmonium masses used: m_J/psi = 3.09 GeV, m_psi' = 3.68 GeV, m_chi_c = 3.73 GeV
- String tension sigma = 0.184 GeV^2
- LHC collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratios considered: eta/s = 0 (ideal), 0.08 (AdS/CFT), and 0.3 (pQCD near T_c to 2T_c)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on bottomonium suppression in the quasi-particle model (Nilima and Agotiya, 2019) to charmonium states, using the same framework of a medium-modified Cornell potential with a nonvanishing string term rather than Coulomb-only interactions as in the Bannur model. The approach builds on prior quasi-particle models by Chandra and collaborators and on the strongly coupled QED plasma framework adapted to QCD. The results enable comparison with CMS experimental data on J/psi suppression at LHC energy and allow testing of sequential suppression scenarios for charmonium states, with the authors noting that future work should include bulk viscosity effects.
