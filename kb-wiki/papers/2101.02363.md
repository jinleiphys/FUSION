# Shape changes in the mirror nuclei $^{70}$Kr and $^{70}$Se

> We studied the proton-rich $T_z=-1$ nucleus $^{70}$Kr through inelastic scattering at intermediate energies in order to extract the reduced transition probability, $B(E2;\;0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$. Comparison with the other members of the $A=70$ isospin triplet, $^{70}$Br and $^{70}$Se, studied in the same experiment, shows a $3σ$ deviation from the expected linearity of the electromagnetic matrix elements as a function of $T_z$. At present, no established nuclear structure theory can describe this observed deviation quantitatively. This is the first violation of isospin symmetry at this level observed in the transition matrix elements. A heuristic approach may explain the anomaly by a shape change between the mirror nuclei $^{70}$Kr and $^{70}$Se contrary to the model predictions.

## Key claim

The reduced transition probability $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ for the proton-rich $T_z = -1$ nucleus $^{70}$Kr deviates by more than $3\sigma$ from the linear trend expected from isospin symmetry when compared to the other members of the $A=70$ isospin triplet ($^{70}$Br and $^{70}$Se). The authors propose that a shape change between the mirror nuclei $^{70}$Kr and $^{70}$Se, contrary to model predictions, may explain the anomaly, but no established nuclear structure theory can quantitatively describe the observed deviation.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory using projectile fragmentation of a $^{78}$Kr primary beam at 345 $A$MeV. The $^{70}$Kr beam (average intensity 15 pps) impinged on Au (926 mg/cm$^2$) and Be (703 mg/cm$^2$) targets surrounded by the DALI2 NaI(Tl) detector array; reaction products were identified in the ZeroDegree spectrometer. The exclusive cross sections for excitation of the $2^+_1$ state were extracted from Doppler-corrected $\gamma$-ray spectra, and the nuclear deformation length $\delta_\text{N}$ and $B(E2)$ value were determined simultaneously from the Be and Au target data using a coupled-channels reaction model (FRESCO) with optical model potentials.

## Key numbers

- $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ for $^{70}$Kr: 2726(451) e$^2$fm$^4$ (first measurement).
- $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ for $^{70}$Br: 1454(233) e$^2$fm$^4$ (weighted average with prior data).
- $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ for $^{70}$Se: 1659(659) e$^2$fm$^4$ (weighted average with prior data).
- Proton matrix element $M(E2)$ for $^{70}$Kr: 52.2(43) efm$^2$; for $^{70}$Br: 38.1(31) efm$^2$; for $^{70}$Se: 40.7(82) efm$^2$.
- Extrapolated $M(E2)$ at $T_z = -1$ from linear fit to $^{70}$Br and $^{70}$Se: 35.0(43) efm$^2$; the experimental value for $^{70}$Kr deviates by more than $3\sigma$.
- Excitation energy of the $2^+_1$ state in $^{70}$Kr: 885 keV.
- Cross section for $^{70}$Kr on Au target: 349(36) mb; on Be target: 14.5(46) mb.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin symmetry via electromagnetic matrix elements in $T=1$ triplets, previously performed for $22 \leq A \leq 50$ (Prados-Estevez 2007, Boso 2019, Giles 2019) where no deviation from linearity was detected. The $A=70$ triplet is the heaviest where all three $B(E2)$ values are experimentally determined. The result contradicts predictions from shell-model calculations (GXPF1A, JUN45), HFB-based models, and the complex excited VAMPIR model (Petrovici 2018), none of which reproduce the strong increase in $B(E2)$ toward $^{70}$Kr. The finding enables tests of isospin symmetry in heavier nuclei and challenges current theoretical descriptions of shape coexistence and isospin mixing in the $A=70$ region.
