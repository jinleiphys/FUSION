# Strong two-meson decays of light and charmed vector mesons

> We calculate the strong decay couplings for $ρ\to ππ$, $ϕ\to KK$, $K^* \to Kπ$ and $D^*\to Dπ$ in a unified and consistent approach based on the impulse approximation, nonperturbative solutions of the quark-gap equation and the Poincaré invariant Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes of vector and pseudoscalar mesons. In particular, we obtain the coupling $g_{D^*\!Dπ} = 17.24^{+3.06}_{-2.30} $ in very good agreement with the experimental value by CLEO, which corresponds to a strong effective coupling between heavy vector and pseudoscalar mesons to the pion of $\hat g = 0.58^{+0.10}_{-0.08}$.

## Key claim

The paper calculates strong decay couplings for light and charmed vector mesons using a Poincaré covariant impulse approximation, obtaining a coupling \(g_{D^*\!D\pi} = 17.24^{+3.06}_{-2.30}\) that agrees with the CLEO experimental value, but the authors note that the impulse approximation likely misses some relevant physics for lighter mesons like the \(\rho\), whose decay width is almost 20% of its mass.

## Method

The authors solve the quark gap equation (Dyson-Schwinger equation) in rainbow-ladder truncation to obtain dressed quark propagators, then solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector meson bound-state amplitudes. The strong decay amplitude is computed via a one-loop integral in the impulse approximation, using a flavor-dependent interaction model with a Gaussian infrared term and perturbative QCD continuation, with the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes analytically continued to complex momenta via a Nakanishi-type representation.

## Key numbers

- \(g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 5.13^{+0.24}_{-0.25}\) (theoretical), compared to experimental \(5.94 \pm 0.44\) (13.6% deviation)
- \(g_{\phi KK} = 5.02^{+0.12}_{-0.22}\) (theoretical), compared to experimental \(5.53 \pm 0.31\) (9.2% deviation)
- \(g_{K^*\!K\pi} = 5.10^{+0.22}_{-0.26}\) (theoretical), compared to experimental \(5.47 \pm 0.99\) (6.8% deviation)
- \(g_{D^*\!D\pi} = 17.24^{+3.06}_{-2.30}\) (theoretical), compared to experimental \(17.9 \pm 0.3 \pm 1.90\) from CLEO (3.7% deviation)
- Effective heavy-meson coupling \(\hat g = 0.58^{+0.10}_{-0.08}\) derived from the \(D^*\!D\pi\) coupling
- Computed meson masses: \(\pi\) 0.140 GeV, \(K\) 0.494 GeV, \(D\) 1.867 GeV, \(\rho\) 0.730 GeV, \(\phi\) 1.070 GeV, \(K^*\) 0.883 GeV, \(D^*\) 2.021 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Jarecke et al. (2002) on \(\rho\to\pi\pi\), \(\phi\to\bar KK\), and \(K^*\to K\pi\) decays, updating those results with improved methods. It also improves upon the authors' own previous studies of \(D^*\to D\pi\) (El-Bennich et al., 2010-2016) which used one-covariant models lacking Poincaré invariance, by now employing fully Poincaré covariant Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The results enable more reliable extraction of the universal coupling \(\hat g\) in heavy-meson chiral perturbation theory and provide a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations of charmed meson properties.
