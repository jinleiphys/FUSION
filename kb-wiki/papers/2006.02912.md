# Comment on "Search for $η'$ Bound Nuclei in the $^{12}\mathrm{C}(γ,p)$ Reaction with Simultaneous Detection of Decay Products"

> We comment on a recent paper by the LEPS2/BGOegg Collaboration [Phys. Rev. Lett. 124, 202501 (2020), arXiv:2005.03449].

# Comment on "Search for η' Bound Nuclei in the ¹²C(γ,p) Reaction with Simultaneous Detection of Decay Products"

## Key claim

The LEPS2/BGOegg Collaboration's conclusion about the real part (V₀) of the η'-¹¹B optical potential and the branching fraction Br_{η'N→ηN} should be reconsidered due to unexamined systematic uncertainties in their normalization procedure, specifically the assumption that two normalization factors F⁽¹⁾ and F⁽²⁾ are equal and the evaluation of F⁽²⁾ itself.

## Method

The authors re-examine the normalization procedure used by Tomida et al. (2020) in their missing-mass measurement of the ¹²C(γ,p) reaction near the η' emission threshold. They identify two specific issues: (1) the impact of the imaginary part W₀ of the η'-nucleus potential on the decomposed cross sections for absorption and escape channels, where W₀ was fixed at -12 MeV but has a range of 9 to 17 MeV from the CBELSA/TAPS result; and (2) the wide range of momentum transfer arising from the polar angle coverage (0.9°–6.8°) and incident photon energy range (1.3–2.4 GeV), which makes it inappropriate to evaluate the normalization factor F⁽²⁾ as a ratio of averaged cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The imaginary part W₀ of the η'-nucleus potential was fixed at -12 MeV in the original analysis, while the CBELSA/TAPS result is -13 ± 3 ± 3 MeV, giving a range of |W₀| between 9 and 17 MeV when statistical and systematic errors are added in quadrature.
- The momentum transfer at threshold energy ranges from 0.47–0.49 GeV/c at E_γ = 1.3 GeV to 0.24–0.36 GeV/c at E_γ = 2.4 GeV, for the polar angle coverage of 0.9°–6.8°.
- The statistical uncertainty of (dσ/dΩ)^{η'esc}_{exp} is 26%, while the fluctuation of the cross-section ratio due to W₀ uncertainty is estimated at 16–19% (for V₀ = -20 and -100 MeV respectively), which is comparable to the statistical uncertainty.
- In the inclusive ¹²C(p,d) measurement by the η-PRiME/Super-FRS Collaboration, the momentum transfer at threshold is 0.48–0.49 GeV/c, a much narrower range.

## Context

This comment critiques the analysis by Tomida et al. (2020) from the LEPS2/BGOegg Collaboration, which reported the first missing-mass measurement of ¹²C(γ,p) reactions near the η' emission threshold. The commenters argue that the normalization procedure used to constrain V₀ and Br_{η'N→ηN} suffers from simplifications that may overconstrain these parameters. They contrast this with the inclusive ¹²C(p,d) measurement by the η-PRiME/Super-FRS Collaboration (2016, 2018), which used a fixed beam energy of 2.5 GeV resulting in a narrow momentum transfer range, and where the μ₉₅ parameter (analogous to the normalization factor) was evaluated differently. The comment enables a more careful treatment of systematic uncertainties in future searches for η' bound nuclei.
