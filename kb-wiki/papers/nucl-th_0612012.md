# Study of the ground-state energy of 40Ca with the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential

> We have calculated the ground-state energy of the doubly-magic nucleus 40Ca within the framework of the Goldstone expansion using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The short-range repulsion of this potential has been renormalized by integrating out its high-momentum components so as to derive a low-momentum potential V-low-k defined up to a cutoff momentum Lambda. A simple criterion has been employed to establish a connection between this cutoff momentum and the size of the two-nucleon model space in the harmonic oscillator basis. This model-space truncation approach provides a reliable way to renormalize the free nucleon-nucleon potential preserving its many-body physics. The role of the 3p-3h and 4p-4h excitations in the description of the ground state of 40Ca is discussed.

## Key claim

The ground-state energy of 40Ca calculated with the CD-Bonn potential using the Goldstone expansion up to fourth order converges to (-314 ± 3) MeV, which underestimates the experimental binding energy by 28 MeV (8%), suggesting that a three-body force is needed to compensate for the lack of attraction of the CD-Bonn potential alone.

## Method

The authors renormalized the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential by integrating out its high-momentum components to derive a low-momentum potential V-low-k defined up to a cutoff momentum Λ. They established a connection between this cutoff momentum and the size of the two-nucleon model space in the harmonic oscillator basis using a simple criterion based on the maximum number of HO quanta N_max. They then performed a Hartree-Fock calculation and summed all Goldstone linked diagrams for the ground-state energy up to fourth order in the two-body interaction, using Padé approximants [2|2] to obtain the converged result.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 40Ca with CD-Bonn potential: (-314 ± 3) MeV, converged at ħω = 25.5 MeV
- Fourth-order result is 6 MeV more attractive than the third-order converged value of (-308 ± 3) MeV
- The calculated binding energy underestimates the experimental value by 28 MeV (8% of the experimental binding energy)
- Fourth-order contributions breakdown (in MeV, for N_max=10): 2p-2h = -15, 3p-3h = -16, 4p-4h = +24, total fourth order = -7
- Net repulsive contribution from 3p-3h and 4p-4h excitations combined: 8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of ground-state energies for 4He and 16O using the CD-Bonn potential (Coraggio 2006, 2007) to the heavier doubly-magic nucleus 40Ca, which represents a major step beyond the p-shell mass region accessible to methods like Green's function Monte Carlo (Pieper 2001, 2005) and no-core shell model (Navratil 2000). The study is the first fully microscopic calculation of 40Ca, apart from preliminary work by Kumagai (1997) and Fujii (2005) using the unitary model-operator approach, and it demonstrates that 3p-3h and 4p-4h excitations play a significant role at fourth order, with their net repulsive contribution counterbalancing the 2p-2h contribution. The results enable future non-perturbative ab initio calculations and confirm the need for three-body forces, as also seen in the authors' earlier work on lighter nuclei.
