# Neutron star asteroseismology and nuclear saturation parameter

> Adopting various unified equations of state (EOSs), we examine the quasinormal modes of gravitational waves from cold neutron stars. We focus on the fundamental ($f$-), 1st pressure ($p_1$-), and 1st spacetime ($w_1$-) modes, and derive the empirical formulae for the frequencies and damping rate of those modes. With the resultant empirical formulae, we find that the value of $η$, which is a specific combination of the nuclear saturation parameters, can be estimated within $\sim 30 \%$ accuracy, if the $f$-mode frequency from the neutron star whose mass is known would be observed or if the $f$- and $p_1$-mode frequencies would be simultaneously observed, even though this estimation is applicable only for the low-mass neutron stars. Additionally, we find that the mass and radius of canonical neutron stars can be estimated within a few per cent accuracy via the simultaneous observations of the $f$- and $w_1$-mode frequencies. We also find that, if the $f$-, $p_1$-, and $w_1$-mode frequencies would be simultaneously observed, the mass of canonical neutron stars can be estimated within $2\%$ accuracy, while the radius can be estimated within $1\%$ for the neutron star with $M\ge 1.6M_\odot$ or within $0.6\%$ for the neutron star with $M\ge 1.4M_\odot$ constructed with the EOS constrained via the GW170817 event. Furthermore, we find the strong correlation between the maximum $f$-mode frequency and the neutron star radius with the maximum mass, between the minimum $w_1$-mode frequency and the maximum mass, and between the minimum damping rate of the $w_1$-mode and the stellar compactness for the neutron star with the maximum mass.

## Key claim

The value of eta, a specific combination of nuclear saturation parameters, can be estimated within approximately 30 percent accuracy if the f-mode frequency from a neutron star whose mass is known would be observed or if the f- and p1-mode frequencies would be simultaneously observed, but this estimation is applicable only for low-mass neutron stars.

## Method

The author calculated the complex eigenfrequencies (quasi-normal modes) for cold, non-rotating neutron stars constructed with various unified equations of state (EOSs), including DD2, Miyatsu, Shen, FPS, SKa, SLy4, SLy9, and Togashi. The linear perturbations were solved without the relativistic Cowling approximation, meaning metric perturbations were included, allowing determination of both fluid modes (f- and p1-modes) and spacetime modes (w1-modes) for the l=2 multipole. Empirical formulae for the frequencies and damping rates were derived as functions of the nuclear saturation parameter eta and stellar properties such as average density and compactness.

## Key numbers

* The value of eta can be estimated within approximately 30 percent accuracy from f-mode observations of low-mass neutron stars with known mass, or from simultaneous f- and p1-mode observations.
* The mass and radius of canonical neutron stars can be estimated within a few percent accuracy via simultaneous observations of f- and w1-mode frequencies.
* If f-, p1-, and w1-mode frequencies are simultaneously observed, the mass of canonical neutron stars can be estimated within 2 percent accuracy, while the radius can be estimated within 1 percent for neutron stars with M greater than or equal to 1.6 solar masses, or within 0.6 percent for neutron stars with M greater than or equal to 1.4 solar masses constructed with the EOS constrained via the GW170817 event.
* The empirical formula for the f-mode frequency as a function of x and eta is given by f_f(x,eta) in kHz = 1.5390x + f_0(eta), where f_0(eta) in kHz = 1.2487 eta_100^{-3} - 3.7949 eta_100^{-2} + 3.9618 eta_100^{-1} - 0.7717, with eta_100 = eta / 100 MeV.
* The f-mode frequency for canonical neutron stars can be estimated within a few percent using the empirical formula f_f(C,M) in kHz = [-66.3431 (M/R)^3 + 43.9243 (M/R)^2 + 5.1709 (M/R) + 0.01847] (M/1.4 solar masses)^{-1}.
* The p1-mode frequency for neutron star models between the second and third avoided crossing can be estimated within approximately 10 percent accuracy using the derived empirical formula.
* The values of x_AC and f_f,AC obtained with the Cowling approximation are overestimated up to approximately 50 percent compared to the full perturbation analysis.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Sotani (2020b, 2020c) that used the relativistic Cowling approximation to examine f- and p1-mode frequencies, by now including metric perturbations and the w1-mode. The empirical formulae enable estimation of the nuclear saturation parameter eta from gravitational wave observations, and allow mass and radius determination for canonical neutron stars. The results build on the universal relations for f-mode frequencies as functions of average density found by Andersson and Kokkotas (1996, 1998), and on the characterization of neutron star properties in terms of eta by Sotani, Iida, Oyamatsu, and Ohnishi (2014). Future gravitational wave detections of neutron star oscillations could directly constrain the nuclear saturation parameters and stellar structure.
