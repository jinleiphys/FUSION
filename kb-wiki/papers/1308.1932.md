# Proton-nucleus collisions at LHC energy in the Monte Carlo model

> A Monte Carlo model, initially developed for soft pp and AA collisions at high energy, is applied for proton-lead interaction at the LHC energy. Elementary collisions are implemented at the partonic level and do not involve the usual Glauber supposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions. The average number of participating nucleons and charged multiplicity in p-Pb collisions were calculated and compared with the predictions of Glauber model and experimental data. It was demonstrated that taking into account the energy conservation results in the number of participating nucleons considerably lower than in the Glauber approach. Different ways of centrality determination in pA are discussed and the influence of the methods of centrality definition on mean observables and their variances is studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Monte Carlo model implementing parton-level interactions with energy conservation yields a mean number of participating nucleons in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV that is considerably lower than the standard Glauber model prediction, challenging the Glauber normalization commonly used in experimental multiplicity studies for non-symmetrical colliding systems.

## Method

The model distributes nucleons according to a Woods-Saxon density profile and represents each nucleon as a set of partons (valence quark, diquark, and quark-diquark pairs) with Gaussian transverse positions and momentum fractions assigned via exclusive distributions that enforce energy and angular momentum conservation. Dipole-dipole interaction amplitudes are computed using a confinement-modified form with modified Bessel functions, and charged multiplicity is calculated via a color-string approach with string fusion on a transverse lattice. The model parameters are constrained by pp data on total inelastic cross section and charged multiplicity from ISR to LHC energies, with additional consistency checks against minimum-bias p-Pb and Pb-Pb data.

## Key numbers

* Mean pseudorapidity density of charged particles in NSD p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV: 16.81 ± 0.71 (ALICE experimental result cited in the paper)
* Normalized multiplicity per participant in p-Pb: 2.14 ± 0.17, compared to 2.6 in pp collisions at the same energy
* Woods-Saxon parameters for lead: R = 6.63 fm, d = 0.545 fm
* Model parameters: r₀ = 0.6 fm, α_S = 0.4, r_max/r₀ = 0.5, r_str = 0.2 fm, μ₀ = 1.152 (mean charged multiplicity from one string per rapidity unit)
* Model prediction for mean charged multiplicity in p-Pb: 16.5, consistent with the experimental value of 16.81

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier Monte Carlo model for pp and AA collisions (Kovalenko 2012, 2013) to p-Pb systems, and directly challenges the standard Glauber model (Glauber 1959) that is widely used by RHIC and LHC experiments for centrality determination and multiplicity normalization. The results align with findings from the Modified Glauber approach (Feofilov 2012, Seryakov 2012) and the AMPT model (2013) which also predict reduced participant numbers due to energy conservation effects, and suggest that geometric centrality determination in pA collisions is less reliable than in symmetric systems, requiring careful accounting of the centrality estimator when comparing experimental results.
