# Calculations of direct photon emission in Heavy Ion Collisions at \sqrt{s_NN} = 200 GeV

> Direct photon emission in heavy-ion collisions is calculated within a relativistic micro+macro hybrid model and compared to the microscopic transport model UrQMD. In the hybrid approach, the high-density part of the collision is calculated by an ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic calculation, while the early (pre-equilibrium-) and late (rescattering-) phase are calculated with the transport model. We study both models with Au+Au-collisions at \sqrt{s_NN} = 200 GeV and compare the results to experimental data published by the PHENIX collaboration.

# Calculations of direct photon emission in Heavy Ion Collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV

## Key claim

The hadronic models (pure UrQMD cascade and UrQMD+Hydro hybrid) fail to reproduce the low-\(p_\perp\) direct photon excess measured by PHENIX at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, implying that a significant contribution to the measured spectra must come from non-hadronic, likely partonic (QGP) sources. However, the hybrid model with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage produces significantly higher photon yields than the pure cascade, a difference not seen at lower collision energies.

## Method

Direct photon emission was calculated perturbatively for minimum bias (0–92%) and central (0–10%) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV using two approaches: the pure microscopic transport model UrQMD v2.3, and a hybrid model coupling UrQMD for the pre-equilibrium and late rescattering phases with ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamics for the high-density intermediate stage. Photon emission from the transport phase used cross-sections from Kapusta et al., while emission from the hydrodynamic phase used parametrizations by Turbide, Rapp, and Gale (for hadron gas) and Arnold et al. (for QGP). The hybrid model used a Hadron Gas Equation of State (HG-EoS) matching the degrees of freedom in the transport phase, with the transition to hydrodynamics at \(t = 0.6\) fm and back to cascade when the energy density dropped below \(\epsilon_{\rm crit} \approx 5 \epsilon_0\).

## Key numbers

- Minimum bias (0–92%) and central (0–10%) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV were studied.
- The transition from transport to hydrodynamics occurs at \(t = 0.6\) fm.
- The transition back to cascade occurs when the local rest frame energy density drops below \(\epsilon_{\rm crit} \approx 5 \epsilon_0\).
- The intermediate hydrodynamic phase dominates the hybrid model photon spectrum at low transverse momenta \(p_\perp < 3\) GeV.
- The difference between hybrid and pure cascade spectra was not observed at lower energies \(E_{\rm lab} = 158\) AGeV and \(E_{\rm lab} = 45\) AGeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Baeuchle and Bleicher (2009) at \(E_{\rm lab} = 158\) AGeV and (2010) at \(E_{\rm lab} = 45\) AGeV, where no difference was found between pure transport and hybrid model photon spectra. The present result shows that at the higher RHIC energy of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the hydrodynamic description of the intermediate stage yields a significant excess over the pure cascade, suggesting that the excess depends on collision energy rather than system size. The calculations are compared to experimental data from the PHENIX collaboration (Adler 2005) and to pQCD predictions by Gordon and Vogelsang (1993). The findings motivate future studies with different equations of state (Bag Model EoS, chirally restored phase) and other collision systems (Cu+Cu, lower energies) to further investigate the origin of the low-\(p_\perp\) photon excess.
