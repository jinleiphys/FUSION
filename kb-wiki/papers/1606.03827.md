# Thermal blurring of event-by-event fluctuations provoked by rapidity conversion

> We study the effect of thermal blurring caused by the use of (momentum-space) rapidity as a proxy of coordinate-space rapidity in experimental measurements of conserved charge fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In theoretical studies assuming statistical mechanics, calculated fluctuations are those in a spatial volume. Experiments, on the other hand, can measure fluctuations only in a momentum-space in the final state. In a standard argument to compare experimental results for a momentum space with theoretical studies for a coordinate space, rapidities of particles are implicitly regarded as equivalent to their coordinate-space rapidity. We show that the relation of two fluctuations is significantly altered by the existence of the thermal motion, i.e. thermal blurring. We discuss that the thermal blurring can be regarded as a part of the diffusion process, and the effect can be understood by studying the rapidity window dependences of fluctuations. Centrality dependence of the thermal blurring effect is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using momentum-space rapidity as a proxy for coordinate-space rapidity in measurements of conserved charge fluctuations introduces a "thermal blurring" effect that significantly alters the relation between theoretical and experimental fluctuations, with the effect being strongest for narrow rapidity windows and for particles with small mass-to-temperature ratios like pions.

## Method

The authors model thermal blurring by assuming particles are emitted at kinetic freezeout according to a blastwave model with fixed proper time and constant transverse velocity, using the Cooper-Frye formula with a Boltzmann distribution. They derive the particle distribution per unit rapidity n(y) and compute cumulants of the particle number in a rapidity window Δy using both a binomial distribution approach and a discretized multinomial formalism, showing the two methods give equivalent results.

## Key numbers

- For pions at kinetic freezeout (T_kin ≈ 100 MeV, β = 0.6-0.7), the width σ of the rapidity distribution n(y) is approximately 0.5, which is about half the maximal rapidity window Δy = 1.0 at STAR
- For nucleons with the same freezeout parameters, σ ≈ 0.25
- For the electric charge fluctuation with w = m/T = 1.5 and Δy = 1.0, R_2(Δy) ≈ 0.5, indicating about 50% modification of the second-order cumulant due to thermal blurring
- For Δy = 1.6 (ALICE detector coverage) or w = 9 (net-baryon number), R_2(Δy) is larger than 0.25
- The kurtosis κ of n(y) for parameters relevant to pions and nucleons at kinetic freezeout satisfies |κ| < 0.5

## Context

This work extends earlier qualitative discussions of thermal blurring by Asakawa (2000), Jeon (2000), Shuryak (2000), and Koch (2008) by providing quantitative calculations of the effect on cumulants, including non-Gaussian fluctuations. The authors show that thermal blurring can be treated mathematically as part of the same diffusion process described by the diffusion master equation formalism of Kitazawa (2013) and Kitazawa (2015), enabling direct application of existing results for rapidity window dependence. The analysis enables experimental tests of the thermal blurring picture through simultaneous measurement of second, third, and fourth order cumulants for electric charge and baryon number at different rapidity windows, and suggests that wider rapidity coverage (such as planned for the BES-II program at RHIC) would be valuable for these studies.
