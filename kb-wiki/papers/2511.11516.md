# Exclusive photoproduction of a $π^0γ$ pair in the saturation framework

> We consider the exclusive photoproduction of a $π^0 γ$ pair with large invariant mass, as a promising channel to study the effects of gluon saturation. It has recently been demonstrated that this process is incompatible with a collinear factorization approach in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at the leading twist. In such a situation, a (generalized) $k_T$-dependent factorization at small $x$ is a valid alternative approach. We perform this calculation using the shockwave formalism, which resums multiple gluon exchanges between the projectile and the dense nuclear target. We find that the polarized amplitude changes sign as a function of back-to-back transverse momentum $|\vec{p}_t|$ of the pion-photon pair, resulting in a dip-like structure in the fully differential cross section as a function of $|\vec{p}_t|$.

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a π⁰γ pair with large invariant mass is incompatible with collinear factorization in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at leading twist, but a (generalized) kT-dependent factorization at small x using the shockwave formalism provides a valid alternative; the polarized amplitude changes sign as a function of back-to-back transverse momentum |p⃗_t| of the pion-photon pair, producing a dip-like structure in the fully differential cross section.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for γ + N → γ + π⁰ + N' using the shockwave formalism, which resums multiple gluon exchanges between the projectile and the dense nuclear target via Wilson lines and a dipole operator. The quark-antiquark pair from the incoming photon is projected onto a neutral pion final state using the asymptotic pion distribution amplitude φ_π⁰(z) = 6z(1-z), and the target is modeled with the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff (GBW) dipole model. The calculation is performed in the forward scattering limit (Δ_⟂ = 0) and the photoproduction limit (Q² = 0), with the amplitude expressed as a convolution over the t-channel momentum p⃗₁ and the quark momentum fraction z.

## Key numbers

- The GBW model parameters used for the target impact factor are: σ₀ = 23.02 mb, x₀ = 3.04 × 10⁻⁴, λ = 0.288 (from Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff, 1998).
- The saturation scale is defined via R₀(x_ℙ) = 1/Q_s(x_ℙ) = (x_ℙ/x₀)^(λ/2) / (1 GeV), with Q₀ = 1 GeV.
- The center-of-mass energy s_γN and pion rapidity η are varied; the amplitude is shown for η values between 3 and 4.5 as a function of |p⃗_t|.
- The pion distribution amplitude used is the asymptotic form φ_π⁰(z) = 6z(1-z).

## Context

This work extends the shockwave formalism studies of diffractive processes (Boussarie et al., 2014–2024; Fucilla et al., 2022–2023) to the exclusive photoproduction of a π⁰γ pair, a channel previously shown by Nabeebaccus et al. (2023, 2024) to break collinear factorization due to a Glauber pinch, unlike processes studied by Boussarie et al. (2016), Duplancic et al. (2018–2023), and Qiu et al. (2022) where collinear factorization is valid. The calculation enables future studies of gluon saturation effects in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at facilities such as the EIC and LHC, and the dip structure in the cross section provides a potential observable for distinguishing saturation from non-saturation models.
