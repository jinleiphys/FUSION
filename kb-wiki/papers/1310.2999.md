# Pre-neutron-emission mass distributions for reaction $^{238}$U(n, f) up to 60 MeV

> The pre-neutron-emission mass distributions for reaction $^{238}$U(n, f) up to 60 MeV are systematically studied with an empirical fission potential model. The energy dependence of the peaks and valleys of the pre-neutron-emission mass distributions is described by an exponential form based on the newly measured data. The energy dependence of evaporation neutrons before scission is also considered, which plays a crucial role for the reasonable description of the mass distributions. The measured data for the pre-neutron-emission mass distributions for reaction $^{238}$U(n, f) are reasonably well reproduced up to 60 MeV. The mass distributions at unmeasured energies are also predicted with this approach.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their combination method, which uses a nucleus-nucleus driving potential from the Skyrme energy-density functional together with a phenomenological fission potential, can reasonably reproduce measured pre-neutron-emission mass distributions for 238U(n,f) up to 60 MeV, but the model relies on an empirical exponential fit to the energy dependence of peak and valley heights based on newly measured data, and the number of pre-scission evaporation neutrons is derived from a step-function approximation of multi-chance fission cross sections.

## Method

The pre-neutron-emission mass distributions are described by a phenomenological fission potential consisting of three harmonic-oscillator functions, with the positions of the light and heavy fragment peaks determined from the driving potential of the fissile nucleus after accounting for evaporation neutrons before scission. The number of pre-scission evaporation neutrons is derived from the multi-chance fission cross sections of 238U(n,f) as a step function of incident energy, giving fissile nuclei of 239U, 238U, 237U, and 236U in different energy regions. The heights of the peaks and valleys of the mass distributions are fitted to experimental data using exponential functions of incident neutron energy.

## Key numbers

- The number of evaporation neutrons before scission is taken as 0 for threshold to 6.5 MeV, 1 for 6.5 to 14.5 MeV, 2 for 14.5 to 21.5 MeV, and 3 for 21.5 to 60 MeV.
- The peak height P(A1) is fitted as 3.850 + 2.600 exp(-0.040 En) where En is in MeV.
- The valley height P(A0) is fitted as 0.044 + 4.581 exp(-32.465/En) where En is in MeV.
- The heavy fragment peak position A2 is approximately 140 at low energies and decreases with incident energy; at 60 MeV the experimental heavy fragment peak is at mass 136.
- The light fragment peak position A1 remains roughly 99 from threshold up to 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous combination method (Sun 2012) from low-energy neutron-induced fission to incident energies up to 60 MeV for 238U(n,f). The approach is compared with the TALYS code (Koning 2007) and reproduces experimental data from Zoller (1995), Vives (2000), Ryzhov (2011), and Simutkin (2011). The model enables predictions of pre-neutron-emission mass distributions at unmeasured energies up to 100 MeV, which is relevant for applications such as accelerator-driven systems and thorium-based fuel cycles.
