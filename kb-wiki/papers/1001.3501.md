# Shell-model interpretation of high-spin states in 134-I

> New experimental information has been recently obtained on the odd-odd nucleus 134-I. We interpret the five observed excited states up to the energy of ~3 MeV on the basis of a realistic shell-model calculation, and make spin-parity assignments accordingly. A very good agreement is found between the experimental and calculated energies.

## Key claim
The paper interprets five newly observed excited states in the odd-odd nucleus 134-I up to about 3 MeV using a realistic shell-model calculation, assigning spins and parities. The claimed "very good agreement" between experimental and calculated energies is qualified by an rms deviation of about 100 keV for the high-spin cascade and a maximum discrepancy of 160 keV for the low-lying 8- state.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations treating 132-Sn as a closed core, with valence protons and neutron holes occupying the five orbitals of the 50-82 shell (0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, 0h11/2). The effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential using the V-low-k renormalization approach with cutoff Lambda = 2.2 fm-1, including diagrams up to second order in the Q-box folded-diagram expansion. Single-particle and single-hole energies were taken from experimental spectra of 133-Sb and 131-Sn, and calculations were performed with the OXBASH computer code.

## Key numbers
- The five observed excited states in 134-I lie up to approximately 3 MeV excitation energy
- The rms deviation between calculated and experimental energies for the high-spin yrast cascade is about 100 keV
- The largest discrepancy for low-lying states is 160 keV, occurring for the 8- state (experimental 316 keV vs. calculated 475 keV)
- The 9- state lies about 250 keV below the 10- state, but its population probability from the 10- state is about 30 times smaller than that of the 8- state
- Single-particle energies for protons (in MeV): epsilon_g7/2 = 0.0, epsilon_d5/2 = 0.962, epsilon_d3/2 = 2.439, epsilon_h11/2 = 2.793, epsilon_s1/2 = 2.800
- Single-hole energies for neutrons (in MeV): epsilon^-1_d3/2 = 0.0, epsilon^-1_h11/2 = 0.065, epsilon^-1_s1/2 = 0.332, epsilon^-1_d5/2 = 1.655, epsilon^-1_g7/2 = 2.434
- The oscillator parameter used is hbar omega = 7.88 MeV
- The cutoff parameter for V-low-k is Lambda = 2.2 fm-1

## Context
This work extends previous shell-model studies of nuclei near doubly magic 132-Sn, particularly the authors' earlier calculations for 132-Sb (Coraggio 2002) and 130-Sb, using the same realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential. The experimental data come from prompt gamma-ray measurements following spontaneous fission of 252-Cf reported by Liu (2009) and from 134-Te beta decay data in the ENSDF database. The results enable spin-parity assignments for the newly observed levels in 134-I and provide a test of the proton-neutron hole effective interaction when moving from the one proton-one neutron hole system 132-Sb to the three proton-one neutron hole system 134-I.
