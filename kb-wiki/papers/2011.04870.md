# QCD phase structure in strong magnetic fields

> In this proceedings we discuss the natural connection between the reduction of neutral pion mass in the vacuum, and the magnetic catalysis as well as the reduction of transition temperature in the external magnetic field. We also present the first results on fluctuations of and correlations among conserved charges in strong magnetic fields from lattice QCD computations.

## Key claim

The reduction of the neutral pion mass in a magnetic field is naturally connected to both magnetic catalysis and the reduction of the QCD transition temperature, via the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GMOR) relation and the Ward identity; the authors present first lattice QCD results on fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges in strong magnetic fields, but note that these results are obtained with a pion mass of 220 MeV (larger than the physical value) and thus dynamical quark effects on the observed \(qB\) scaling are negligible.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of \(N_f = 2 + 1\) QCD using highly improved staggered fermions with a pion mass of about 220 MeV and a lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.117\) fm. Zero-temperature results were obtained on \(40^3 \times 96\) and \(32^3 \times 96\) lattices; nonzero-temperature results used a fixed-scale approach with temporal extents \(N_\tau = 16, 12, 10, 8\) and spatial extent \(N_\sigma = 32\), corresponding to temperatures \(T = 140, 168, 210, 280\) MeV at zero magnetic field. They computed chiral condensates, pseudo-scalar meson masses, and second-order fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) as functions of the magnetic field strength \(eB\).

## Key numbers

- The chiral correction \(|\delta|\) to the GMOR relation is at most 6% at \(eB = 0\) and becomes closer to zero as \(eB\) increases.
- The ratio \(\bar{\psi}\psi_{u,d} / (m_{u,d} \chi_{\pi^0_{u,d}})\) deviates from unity by at most 1.2% in nonzero magnetic field.
- Neutral pseudo-scalar meson masses (\(\pi^0_{u,d}\), \(\pi^0\), \(K^0\), \(\eta_s^0\)) monotonically decrease with \(eB\) and appear to saturate at \(eB \gtrsim 2.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- For charged pseudo-scalar mesons (\(\pi^-\), \(K^-\)), the mass-squared difference \(M^2(eB) - M^2(eB=0)\) is well described by the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation for \(eB \lesssim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\), then deviates and decreases after a turning point at \(eB \approx 0.6\) GeV\(^2\).
- At \(T = 140\) MeV and the strongest magnetic field simulated, \(\chi_2^B\) becomes about 75 times its value at \(eB = 0\), and \(\chi_2^Q\) becomes about 28 times its value at \(eB = 0\).
- At the highest temperature (280 MeV), the changes induced by the magnetic field are about a factor of 3 for both \(\chi_2^B\) and \(\chi_2^Q\).
- At \(T = 140\) MeV and \(eB \approx 11 M_\pi^2(eB=0)\), \(\chi_2^B\) is about 6 times and \(\chi_2^Q\) about 2 times their values at \(eB = 0\).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the QCD phase diagram in external magnetic fields, following earlier observations of the reduction of the transition temperature \(T_{pc}\) (Bali et al., 2011) and the phenomenon of inverse magnetic catalysis (Andersen et al., 2014). It builds on the finding that the GMOR relation holds in weak magnetic fields from chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1986; Shushpanov and Smilga, 1997; Agasian and Shushpanov, 2001) and tests it in strong fields. The new results on fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges in strong magnetic fields provide input for heavy-ion collision phenomenology, particularly for understanding how the strong magnetic fields produced in peripheral collisions (Skokov et al., 2009) might affect observables, and they enable comparisons with hadron resonance gas model predictions that require the correct hadron mass spectrum in a magnetic field.
