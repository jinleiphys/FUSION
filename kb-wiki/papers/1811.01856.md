# Anisotropic hydrodynamics for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV

> In this proceedings, we review the basics of quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP). Thenwe present phenomenological comparisons between 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics and experimental data from RHIC experiments at 200 GeV Au-Au collisions. We show that 3+1d aHydroQP model is able to describe the experimental results quite well for the spectra, multiplicity, and elliptic flow for charged particles in different centrality classes.

## Key claim

The 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model is able to describe experimental results "quite well" for the spectra, multiplicity, and elliptic flow of charged particles in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, but only for the specific centrality classes shown and with the extracted fitting parameters listed.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation using a quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics framework, where the one-particle distribution function is parameterized with an anisotropy tensor and a temperature-dependent effective mass tuned to match the QCD equation of state. They performed 3+1d dynamical simulations of Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV and compared the resulting particle spectra, charged particle multiplicity as a function of pseudorapidity, and elliptic flow to experimental data from the PHENIX and PHOBOS collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 455 MeV at proper time tau0 = 0.25 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.179
- Freeze-out temperature T_FO = 130 MeV
- Spectra of pions, kaons, and protons shown for centrality classes 0-5% and 30-40% as a function of transverse momentum pT
- Charged particle multiplicity compared across centrality classes 0-25%
- Elliptic flow v2 for charged particles shown for the 20-30% centrality class

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics approach previously developed by the same group (Alqahtani 2017, Almaalol 2018) to comparisons with RHIC data at 200 GeV, following earlier work on anisotropic hydrodynamics by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010, 2012), and others. The model builds upon earlier ideal and viscous hydrodynamics descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma by Huovinen (2001) and Romatschke (2007), and enables systematic comparison of anisotropic hydrodynamics predictions with a broader set of experimental observables across different collision energies and centrality classes.
