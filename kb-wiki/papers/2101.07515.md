# The equation of state and radial oscillations of neutron stars

> We investigate radial oscillations of pure neutron stars and hybrid stars, employing equations of state of nuclear matter from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory, and of quark matter from the Dyson-Schwinger quark model, performing a Gibbs construction for the mixed phase in hybrid stars. We calculate the eigenfrequencies and corresponding oscillation functions. Our results for the zero points of the first-order radial oscillation frequencies give the maximum mass of stable neutron stars, consistent with the common criterion $dM/dρ_c=0$. Possible observations of the radial oscillation frequencies could help to learn more about the equation of state, predict the maximum mass of neutron stars more precisely, and indicate the presence of quark matter.

## Key claim

The zero points of the first-order radial oscillation frequencies yield the maximum mass of stable neutron stars, consistent with the common criterion dM/dρ_c=0; however, the authors note that this consistency holds for both pure neutron stars and hybrid stars, and that a low fundamental oscillation frequency (f_1 ≲ 1 kHz) together with low compactness (β ≲ 0.3) could indicate the presence of quark matter inside the star.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations for hydrostatic equilibrium using equations of state (EOS) from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory for nuclear matter (with Bonn B and Argonne V18 potentials plus three-body forces) and from the Dyson-Schwinger quark model for quark matter, with a Gibbs construction for the mixed phase in hybrid stars. They then solved the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue equations for radial oscillations to obtain eigenfrequencies and eigenfunctions, imposing boundary conditions at the center and surface.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses: BOB pure NS 2.51 M⊙, V18 pure NS 2.34 M⊙; hybrid stars range from 2.43 M⊙ (BOB+DS0.5) down to 2.02 M⊙ (BOB+DS2)
- For a 1.4 M⊙ NS, fundamental radial oscillation frequency f₁ = 3.16 kHz (BOB) and 3.09 kHz (V18)
- For a 2.0 M⊙ NS, f₁ = 2.73 kHz (BOB pure), 1.25 kHz (BOB+DS2 hybrid), 2.64 kHz (V18 pure), 1.59 kHz (V18+DS1.5 hybrid)
- Radius of a 1.4 M⊙ NS: 12.83 km (BOB), 12.34 km (V18)
- Compactness β = GM/R for maximum-mass stars: 0.326 (BOB), 0.345 (V18), 0.249 (BOB+DS2), 0.266 (V18+DS1.5)
- The condition M_max > 2.1 M⊙ constrains the DSM parameter α < 1.7 for BOB and α < 1.3 for V18 hybrid EOSs

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron star radial oscillations by Chandrasekhar (1964) and subsequent authors (Chanmugam 1977, Gondek 1997, Sagun 2020) by applying the formalism to both pure neutron stars and hybrid stars with quark matter cores. The BHF nuclear EOS approach follows earlier work by Li (2008) and Chen (2011), while the Dyson-Schwinger quark model EOS was developed by Chen (2011, 2015). The results enable future observational discrimination between pure neutron stars and hybrid stars through combined measurements of radial oscillation frequencies and compactness, potentially using next-generation gravitational wave detectors such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer.
