# Models with non-Hermitian Hamiltonian and optical theorem

> The applicability of the optical theorem in the models with the non-Hermitian Hamiltonian is studied. By way of example we consider the $n\bar{n}$ transition in a medium followed by annihilation. It is shown that an application of optical theorem for the non-unitary $S$-matrix leads to the qualitative error in the result. The lower limit on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time $τ$ calculated by means of the model with Hermitian Hamiltonian lies in the range $10^{16}\; {\rm yr}>τ>1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$.

# Models with non-Hermitian Hamiltonian and optical theorem

## Key claim

The optical theorem is inapplicable to models with non-unitary S-matrices derived from non-Hermitian Hamiltonians, and applying it to the n-nbar transition in medium leads to a qualitative error in the calculated oscillation time. The correct lower limit on the free-space n-nbar oscillation time lies in the broad range 10^16 yr > τ > 1.2 × 10^9 s, depending on which unitary model is adopted.

## Method

The author compares two classes of models for n-nbar transitions in a medium followed by annihilation: a standard potential model using a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian with an optical potential for antineutron-medium interactions, and alternative models with Hermitian Hamiltonians where the antineutron annihilation is treated via a field-theoretical approach. For the Hermitian models, the total decay width is calculated directly from off-diagonal matrix elements rather than from the optical theorem, using the antineutron Green function and annihilation amplitudes defined in two different ways (models a and b).

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on free-space n-nbar oscillation time from the potential model (non-Hermitian): τ_pot = 2.36 × 10^8 s
- Lower limit from Hermitian model a: τ_a = 1.2 × 10^9 s (with Γ = 100 MeV and V = 10 MeV)
- Lower limit from Hermitian model b: τ_b = 10^16 yr
- Ratio of decay widths between model a and potential model: r = Γ_a / Γ_opt = 25 (for Γ = 100 MeV, V = 10 MeV)
- The conservative lower limit τ_a = 1.2 × 10^9 s exceeds the Grenoble reactor experiment limit by a factor of 14

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about n-nbar oscillation calculations, specifically challenging the standard optical potential approach used in prior work by several authors (references 4-9 in the paper). The author shows that the optical theorem result Γ_opt ∼ 1/Γ (inverse dependence on annihilation width) is qualitatively wrong, while Hermitian models give Γ_a,b ∼ Γ (linear dependence). The paper identifies that the huge discrepancy between τ_a and τ_b (a factor of roughly 10^17) arises from the 1/V^2 dependence in model a and the fact that V → 0 in model b, and concludes that further investigations are needed to resolve which unitary model is correct.
