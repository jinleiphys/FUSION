# $Y(4260)$ as the first $S$-wave open charm vector molecular state?

> Since its first observation in 2005, the vector charmonium $Y(4260)$ has turned out to be one of the prime candidates for an exotic state in the charmonium spectrum. It was recently proposed that the $Y(4260)$ should have a prominent $D_1\bar{D}+c.c.$ molecular component that is strongly correlated with the production of the charged $Z_c(3900)$. In this paper we demonstrate that the nontrivial cross section line shapes of $e^+e^-\to J/ψππ$ and $h_cππ$ can be naturally explained by the molecular scenario. As a consequence we find a significantly smaller mass for the $Y(4260)$ than previous studied. In the $e^+e^-\to \bar D D^*π+c.c.$ process, with the inclusion of an additional $S$-wave $\bar D^*π$ contribution constrained from data on the $D\bar D^*$ invariant mass distribution, we obtain a good agreement with the data of the angular distributions. We also predict an unusual line shape of $Y(4260)$ in this channel that may serve as a smoking gun for a predominantly molecular nature of $Y(4260)$. Improved measurements of these observables are therefore crucial for a better understanding of the structure of this famous resonance.

# Y(4260) as the first S-wave open charm vector molecular state?

## Key claim

The Y(4260) resonance can be explained as a predominantly D1(2420) anti-D molecular state, provided that a subleading S-wave contribution from anti-D* pi is included to reproduce the observed angular distributions; the fits yield a significantly lower mass for the Y(4260) than previous analyses, namely 4217.2 MeV rather than the nominal 4260 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonrelativistic effective field theory treating the Y(4260) as a D1 anti-D S-wave molecular state, with the Zc(3900) included as a D anti-D* molecule. They performed combined fits to the e+e- to J/psi pi pi and e+e- to hc pi pi cross section line shapes, and separately fitted the D anti-D* invariant mass distribution in e+e- to D anti-D* pi, including an additional S-wave anti-D* pi contribution parameterized with two real constants.

## Key numbers

- Fitted Y(4260) mass: M_Y = (4217.2 +/- 2.0) MeV, significantly below the nominal 4260 MeV
- Residual width (non-D1 anti-D decay channels): Gamma_tilde_Y = (55.91 +/- 2.61) MeV
- Zc(3900) coupling constant: |z| approximately 0.77 GeV^{-1/2}, giving branching ratio B(Zc to D anti-D*) of about 20%
- D1 coupling constant: |h'| = (0.62 +/- 0.08) GeV^{-1}, determined from the D1+ decay width of (25 +/- 6) MeV
- S-wave fit parameters from D anti-D* invariant mass: |alpha| = (6.72 +/- 0.17) GeV^{-1}, beta = (-15.28 +/- 0.01) GeV^2, with chi^2/d.o.f. = 59.02/(57-3)
- Asymmetry parameter A = (n_{>0.5} - n_{<0.5})/(n_{>0.5} + n_{<0.5}) = (0.12 +/- 0.06) from experiment; model gives A = 0.0 (S-wave), 0.11 (D-wave), 0.05 (sum)
- Forward-backward asymmetry A_fb = 0.0 (S-wave), 0.37 (D-wave), 0.16 (sum) when integrating the full kinematic region
- Branching ratio for Y(4260) to D anti-D* pi via intermediate D anti-D1 can be as large as 60%

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation proposed by Wang (2013) that the Y(4260) is a D1 anti-D bound state correlated with the Zc(3900) as a D anti-D* molecule. It contradicts the hybrid interpretation of Zhu (2005), the hadro-charmonium model of Voloshin (2007), and the 4S charmonium assignment of Llanes-Estrada (2005). The results enable experimental tests through the predicted unusual line shape in e+e- to D anti-D* pi and the forward-backward asymmetry in the pi-D helicity angle, which would serve as a smoking gun for the molecular nature.
