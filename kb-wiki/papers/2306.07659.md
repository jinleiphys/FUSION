# R-process beta-decay neutrino flux from binary neutron star mergers and collapsars

> This study investigates the antineutrinos production by $β$-decay of $r$-process nuclei in two astrophysical sites that are capable of producing gamma-ray bursts (GRBs): binary neutron star mergers (BNSMs) and collapsars, which are promising sites for heavy element nucleosynthesis. We employ a simplified method to compute the $β$-decay $\barν_e$ energy spectrum and consider a number of different representative thermodynamic trajectories for $r$-process simulations, each with four sets of $Y_e$ distribution. The time evolution of the $\barν_e$ spectrum is derived for both the dynamical ejecta and the disk wind for BNSMs and collapsar outflow, based on approximated mass outflow rates. Our results show that the $\barν_e$ has an average energy of approximately 3 to 9~MeV, with a high energy tail of up to 20 MeV. The $\barν_e$ flux evolution is primarily determined by the outflow duration, and can thus remain large for $\mathcal{O}(10)$~s and $\mathcal{O}(100)$~s for BNSMs and collapsars, respectively. For a single merger or collapsar at 40~Mpc, the $\barν_e$ flux is $\mathcal{O}(10-100)$~cm$^{-2}$~s$^{-1}$, indicating a possible detection horizon up to $0.1-1$~Mpc for Hyper-Kamiokande. We also estimate their contributions to the diffuse $\barν_e$ background, and find that both sources should only contribute subdominantly to the diffuse background when compared to that expected from core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim

The antineutrino flux from r-process beta-decay in binary neutron star mergers and collapsars is primarily determined by the outflow duration rather than the detailed nuclear physics, but the contribution of both source types to the diffuse antineutrino background remains subdominant compared to core-collapse supernovae, with the caveat that collapsar contributions depend on the still-uncertain rate of collapsars capable of hosting r-process nucleosynthesis.

## Method

The authors employed a simplified beta-decay spectrum calculation using allowed-transition formalism with an effective Q-value reduction factor of P=0.9, combined with r-process nucleosynthesis simulations using a nuclear reaction network of over 7000 isotopes. They used parameterized thermodynamic trajectories with three representative expansion timescales (0.2 ms, 2 ms, and 20 ms) and entropies (1, 20, and 15 k_B per baryon), each with four Gaussian Y_e distributions centered at 0.15, 0.25, 0.35, and 0.45. The time-dependent antineutrino flux was computed for dynamical ejecta (prompt ejection) and disk winds (parameterized mass outflow rates) for both binary neutron star mergers and collapsars.

## Key numbers

- Average antineutrino energy: approximately 3 to 9 MeV, with a high-energy tail up to 20 MeV
- Antineutrino flux duration: O(10) s for binary neutron star mergers and O(100) s for collapsars
- For a single merger or collapsar at 40 Mpc: antineutrino flux is O(10-100) cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- Detection horizon for Hyper-Kamiokande: 0.1-1 Mpc
- Dynamical ejecta mass assumed: 0.01 M_sun for BNSM-dyn. A and BNSM-dyn. B
- Disk outflow mass assumed: 0.05 M_sun for BNSM-disk and 0.50 M_sun for collapsar
- Peak flux values for dynamical ejecta with Y_e,c = 0.15-0.35 at 40 Mpc: approximately 30-100 s^{-1} cm^{-2}
- Plateau flux values for BNSM-disk with Y_e,c = 0.15-0.35 at 40 Mpc: approximately 5-40 s^{-1} cm^{-2}
- Average antineutrino energy during disk outflow: approximately 5-9 MeV for t less than 10 s

## Context

This work extends the earlier estimate by Chen (2023) of r-process beta-decay antineutrino emission from binary neutron star merger dynamical ejecta by including the longer-lasting disk wind components and collapsar outflows, which produce qualitatively different temporal evolution. The study also builds on the proposal by Siegel (2018) that collapsar disks may be r-process sites, while acknowledging the ongoing debate from theoretical works (Miller 2019, Fujibayashi 2020, Zenati 2020) and observational searches (Macias 2019, Bartos 2019, Brauer 2020, Anand 2023). The results enable future searches for r-process antineutrinos with detectors like Hyper-Kamiokande and provide quantitative estimates for the subdominant contribution of these sources to the diffuse supernova neutrino background.
