# The multidimensional model of cluster radioactivity

> The cluster decays $^{228}$Th $\rightarrow \, ^{208}$Pb + $^{20}$O, $^{232}$U $\rightarrow \, ^{208}$Pb + $^{24}$Ne, $^{236}$Pu $\rightarrow \, ^{208}$Pb + $^{28}$Mg, $^{242}$Cm $\rightarrow \, ^{208}$Pb + $^{34}$Si are considered in the framework of the multidimensional cluster preformation model. The macroscopic potential energy surface related to the interaction between the cluster and the residue nucleus is evaluated in the framework of the nonlocal $\hbar^4$ extended Thomas-Fermi approach with Skyrme and Coulomb forces. The shell-correction to the macroscopic potential energy is also taken into account. The dynamical surface deformations of both the cluster and the residue nucleus are taken into consideration at the barrier penetration path. The heights of saddle points related to deformed nuclear shapes are lower than the barrier height between the spherical cluster and residue nuclei; therefore the dynamical deformations of nuclei increase the barrier penetrability and reduce the half-life of cluster decay. The shell correction contribution into the potential energy between cluster and residue nucleus is important for both the potential landscape and the half-life evaluation. The experimental values of cluster decay half-lives are well reproduced in the model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including dynamical surface deformations of both the cluster and the residue nucleus along the barrier penetration path, together with shell-correction contributions to the potential energy, allows the model to well reproduce experimental cluster decay half-lives for four specific decays. The hedge is that the model is tested only for decays where the residue nucleus is always \(^{208}\)Pb and both cluster and residue are even-even nuclei, and the axial symmetry assumption is exact only for two of the four cases considered.

## Method

The macroscopic potential energy surface between the deformed cluster and residue nucleus was evaluated using the nonlocal \(\hbar^4\) extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approach with Skyrme and Coulomb forces, employing a relaxed density parametrization that prevents nucleon density from exceeding equilibrium nuclear matter density. Shell corrections from Strutinsky’s prescription were added to the macroscopic potential, with an exponential reduction of shell-correction magnitude near the touching distance of the two nuclei. The cluster decay half-life was computed via the WKB approximation for barrier penetration along a multidimensional trajectory in the collective coordinates \(R\) (distance between centers), \(\xi_1\) (deformation of residue), and \(\xi_2\) (deformation of cluster), using hydrodynamical mass parameters with enhancement factors derived from experimental quadrupole transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{242}\)Cm \(\rightarrow\) \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{34}\)Si, the macroscopic fission barrier height relative to the ground-state energy of the parent nucleus is close to 2.5 MeV, compared to 2.75 MeV from another macroscopic approach (Sierk).
- For the same decay, the cluster decay barrier height relative to the ground-state energy of the parent nucleus is close to 47 MeV for spherical nuclei.
- The experimental energy released for \(^{242}\)Cm \(\rightarrow\) \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{34}\)Si is \(Q = 96.5\) MeV (Audi and Wang, 2012).
- The binding energy of \(^{208}\)Pb obtained in the energy density approach with the SkM\(^{\star}\) Skyrme force set equals 1603.6 MeV; the binding energy evaluated using the cluster representation (\(^{208}\)Pb \(\rightarrow\) \(^{174}\)Er + \(^{34}\)Si at \(R=0\), \(\xi_1=\xi_2=0\)) equals 1601.2 MeV, a difference of 2.4 MeV.
- The touching distance for spherical \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{34}\)Si is \(R_t(0,0) \approx 10.5\) fm (sum of radii \(R_{\text{Pb}} \approx 6.8\) fm and \(R_{\text{Si}} \approx 3.7\) fm).
- The diffuseness parameter for the shell-correction attenuation was chosen as \(d_{sh} = 0.6\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the multidimensional fusion model (Denisov 1991, 1996) to the inverse process of cluster emission, incorporating dynamical surface deformations of both fragments before and after scission, which had previously been considered only for the pre-scission stage (M. Mirea et al.). The approach combines elements of the fission approximation (Sandulesku, Poenaru, and Greiner 1980) and the cluster-preformation model (many authors), using the same macroscopic-microscopic energy evaluation that has been successfully applied to atomic mass tables (Aboussir et al.) and fission barrier properties (M. Brack et al.). The results enable future application of this multidimensional framework to a broader range of cluster decays and to the study of mutually inverse heavy-ion fusion reactions.
