# Isovector proton-neutron pairing and Wigner energy in Hartree-Fock mean field calculations

> We propose a new approach for the treatment of isovector pairing in self-consistent mean field calculations which conserves exactly the isospin and the particle number in the pairing channel. The mean field is generated by a Skyrme-HF functional while the isovector pairing correlations are described in terms of quartets formed by two neutrons and two protons coupled to the total isospin T=0. In this framework we analyse the contribution of isovector pairing to the symmetry and Wigner energies. It is shown that the isovector pairing provides a good description of the Wigner energy, which is not the case for the mean field calculations in which the isovector pairing is treated by BCS-like models.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that isovector proton-neutron pairing correlations, treated via a quartet condensation model (QCM) that exactly conserves isospin and particle number, can provide a good description of the Wigner energy in self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) mean field calculations, which is not the case when isovector pairing is treated by BCS-like models.

## Method

The authors perform self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme SLy4 functional, with the isovector pairing correlations described by a quartet condensation model (QCM) that conserves isospin and particle number exactly. The ground state for N=Z nuclei is a condensate of alpha-like quartets (two neutrons and two protons coupled to T=0), while for N>Z nuclei a condensate of excess neutron pairs is appended to the quartet condensate. The HF and QCM calculations are iterated together until convergence, with the pairing energy averaged over the QCM state added to the mean-field energy.

## Key numbers

* The strength of the isovector pairing force is fixed as g = 9.6 / A^(3/4) [MeV], calibrated to reproduce the T=0 even-even to odd-odd mass difference along the N=Z line.
* For each isobaric chain, the quantities Phi and X are extracted from the binding energies of three nuclei with T = |Tz| = 0, 2, 4 (i.e., N-Z = 0, 4, 8).
* The QCM calculations use 10 single-particle states each for protons and neutrons above a self-conjugate core; for an isobaric chain of mass A with A/2 even, the core has Nc = Zc = A/2 - 6.
* For the N=Z nucleus this corresponds to the QCM state with n_q = 3 quartets; for T=2, n_q = 2 and n_N = 2; for T=4, n_q = 1 and n_N = 4.

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation model (QCM) developed in Refs. [qcm1, qcm2] (Negrea and Sandulescu, 2013-2014) to self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations, addressing the failure of BCS-like models to describe the Wigner energy as noted by Bentley et al. (2013-2014) and Satula (2001). The results are comparable in accuracy to those of Bentley et al. (2013-2014), which used a phenomenological T^2 interaction to simulate isospin dependence of single-particle levels, but here the isospin dependence is consistently taken into account. The approach enables future studies of the influence of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing on the Wigner energy.
