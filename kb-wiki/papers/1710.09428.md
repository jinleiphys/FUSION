# 12C within the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model

> The Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) is applied to 12C as a system of three alpha- clusters. The microscopic model space, which observes the Pauli-Exclusion-Principle (PEP), is constructed. It is shown that the 12C nucleus can effectively be treated as a two-cluster system 8Be+alpha. The experimental spectrum is well reproduced. The geometrical mapping is discussed and it is shown that the ground state must correspond to a triangular structure, which is in agreement with other microscopic calculations. The non-zero B(E2; 0_2+ --> 2_1+) transition requires a mixing of SU(3) irreducible representations (irreps) whose consequences are discussed. The Hoyle state turns out to contain large shell excitations. The results are compared to another phenomenological model, which assumes a triangular structure and, using simple symmetry arguments, can reproduce the states observed at low energy. This model does not observe the PEP and one objective of our contribution is to verify the extend of importance of the PEP.

## Key claim

The Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) can reproduce the experimental spectrum of 12C well when treated as a three-alpha cluster system, but the Pauli Exclusion Principle (PEP) has significant effects that are ignored in simpler phenomenological models; the Hoyle state requires large shell excitations (4ħω) and the non-zero B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) transition demands mixing of SU(3) irreducible representations.

## Method

The SACM model space was constructed by first treating the two-alpha subsystem (⁸Be) with the Wildermuth condition fixing the minimal relative oscillation quanta to nρ0 = 4, then adding the third alpha particle with nλ0 = 4 relative quanta to the ⁸Be center of mass. The SU(3) content of the model space was tabulated up to 6ħω excitations, and a Hamiltonian combining a dominant SU(3) part with an O(4) symmetry-breaking mixing term was applied. Three sample calculations were performed: a pure SU(3) limit, a case where B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) was adjusted to 1 WU, and a case where it was adjusted to the experimental value of 8 WU.

## Key numbers

- The minimal number of relative oscillation quanta for the two-alpha subsystem (⁸Be) is nρ0 = 4, and for the ⁸Be+α system is nλ0 = 4
- The harmonic oscillator energy ħω is fixed at 14.89 MeV for ¹²C via the formula 45 × A⁻¹/³ − 25 × A⁻²/³
- The ratio λ₀/ρ₀ = √3/2 confirms the triangular structure of the ground state
- B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺): experimental = 4.65 WU; SU(3) limit = 4.65 WU; case-1 = 5.18 WU; case-8 = 3.41 WU
- B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺): experimental = 8.0 WU; SU(3) limit = 0.0 WU; case-1 = 0.97 WU; case-8 = 8.33 WU
- B(E3; 3₁⁻ → 0₁⁺): experimental = 12.0 WU; SU(3) limit = 6.32 WU; case-1 = 12.23 WU; case-8 = 8.64 WU
- The Hoyle state (0₂⁺) comes from a 4ħω excitation, specifically the (12,0) SU(3) irrep contributes 58% in case-1 and 33% in case-8
- The ground state (0₁⁺) is dominated by the (0,4) irrep at 4ħω: 62% in case-1 and 82% in case-8
- Spectroscopic factors for ⁸Be+α → ¹²C include: 0₁⁺ = 0.0866, 2₁⁺ = 0.0218, 0₂⁺ = 0.0417, 1₁⁻ = 0.0160

## Context

This work extends the SACM framework (Hess 1998, 2000) to ¹²C as a three-alpha cluster system and directly compares to the phenomenological oblate symmetric top model of Bijker (2010, 2014), which assumes a triangular structure but ignores the PEP. The results contradict the claim in Bijker (2010) that no 1⁺ cluster states are possible, and show that the PEP eliminates certain parity doublets and the predicted low-lying 5⁻ state. The calculation also relates to no-core shell model calculations by Draayer (2012) for the Hoyle state, confirming that large shell excitations (4ħω) are required. The work enables future improvements via multi-channel symmetry (Hess 2015) to reduce free parameters.
