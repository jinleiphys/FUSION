# Magnetic Screening in Thermal Yang-Mills Theories

> We develop a semiclassical method to calculate the density of magnetic monopoles in non-abelian gauge theories at finite temperature in the dilute gas approximation. This quantity is related to the inverse magnetic screening length for which we obtain $μ_M = 0.255 g^2T$ in SU(2).

## Key claim

The authors develop a semiclassical method to calculate the magnetic monopole density in non-abelian gauge theories at finite temperature, obtaining an inverse magnetic screening length of μ_M = 0.255 g²T for SU(2) in the dilute gas approximation, which is "amazingly close" to lattice simulation results but relies on the assumption that quantum corrections do not contribute at leading order in g.

## Method

The authors connect the magnetic screening mass to the magnetic monopole charge density via linear response theory, then calculate the monopole partition function from a canonical partition sum using a saddle-point evaluation in the space of radially symmetric monopole field configurations. They introduce a stabilizing scale-breaking term via a unit factor insertion to circumvent the absence of a stable classical monopole solution in pure SU(2) gauge theory, perform Gaussian integration over the w and φ fields, and include zero-mode contributions from translations and rotations using collective coordinates. The functional integral over the remaining dynamical field u(r) is evaluated around the lowest-energy stationary solution of the stabilized action, with the scale parameter λ integrated out at the end.

## Key numbers

- Inverse magnetic screening length for SU(2): μ_M = 0.255 g²T
- Monopole density: ρ = 0.0326 (g²T)³
- Ground state energy scaling constant from numerical integration: a = 1.469
- Scale-breaking term constant: b = 0.695
- Bound state eigenvalue for radial fluctuations: ω₀² = 0.950
- Determinantal factor from radial fluctuations: Δ⁻¹/² ≈ 1.03
- For QCD at T = 300 MeV and g = 2 (α_s ≈ 0.32): magnetic screening mass μ_M ≈ 375 MeV, screening length ≈ 0.53 fm
- Average monopole-soliton size: R_M = 6.10/(g²T) ≈ 1 fm at T = 300 MeV
- Average monopole mass: ⟨E⟩ = 4 T
- Average interaction energy between monopoles: E_int ≈ 0.02 T

## Context

This work extends earlier arguments by Linde (1980) and Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981) that the inverse magnetic screening length must be of order g²T, and it partially supports lattice gauge theory results by Billoire, Lazarides, and Shafi (1981) and DeGrand and Toussaint (1982) which gave μ_M = (0.27 ± 0.03) g²T for SU(2). The method circumvents the lack of a stable classical monopole solution in pure gauge theory (the Wu-Yang monopole being unstable) by introducing a stabilizing term inspired by the t'Hooft-Polyakov monopole solution in spontaneously broken gauge theories. The result enables a semiclassical understanding of static magnetic screening in thermal gauge theories and provides numerical estimates for conditions achievable in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies, though the authors note the dilute gas approximation may be borderline because the average monopole size exceeds the average inter-monopole distance.
