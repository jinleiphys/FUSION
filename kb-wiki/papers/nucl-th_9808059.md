# The nucleus as a fluid of skyrmions: Energy levels and nucleon properties in the medium

> A model of a fluid of skyrmions coupled to a scalar and to the $ø$ meson mean fields is developed. The central and spin-orbit potentials of a skyrmion generated by the fields predict correct energy levels in selected closed shell nuclei. The effect of the meson fields on the properties of skyrmions in nuclei is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a model treating the nucleus as a fluid of skyrmions (topological solitons) coupled to scalar and omega meson mean fields can predict correct single-particle energy levels in selected closed-shell nuclei, but this result hinges on a dilute fluid approximation and a pre-chosen Thomas-Fermi distribution function, which break down at short internucleon distances and for light nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian coupling skyrmions to scalar and omega meson mean fields, then apply a product ansatz for the skyrmions and an additive ansatz for the meson fields. They use a Thomas-Fermi distribution to average over skyrmion positions and momenta, solve the omega meson equation with measured nuclear densities as input, and derive an effective Schrodinger equation for the skyrmion center-of-mass coordinate. The spin-orbit interaction is obtained from the coupling of the rotating skyrmion to the omega meson field in a rotating nucleus, rather than from Dirac wave function components as in standard Walecka models.

## Key numbers

* Carbon-12: calculated 1s1/2 binding energy 36.3 MeV vs experimental 35.2 MeV; calculated 1p3/2 binding energy 15.7 MeV vs experimental 16.9 MeV
* Oxygen-16: calculated 1s1/2 binding energy 37.1 MeV vs experimental 43 +/- 5 MeV; calculated 1p3/2 binding energy 20.5 MeV vs experimental 20.1 MeV; calculated 1p1/2 binding energy 15.6 MeV vs experimental 13.9 MeV
* Calcium-40: calculated 1s1/2 binding energy 48 MeV vs experimental 50 +/- 10 MeV; calculated 1d5/2 binding energy 21 MeV vs experimental 18.5 MeV; calculated 2s1/2 binding energy 15.7 MeV vs experimental 14.5 MeV; calculated 1d3/2 binding energy 14.8 MeV vs experimental 12 MeV
* The omega meson coupling constant used is g_V = 7.31
* Nuclear matter incompressibility was fitted to around 270 MeV in prior work by the same author

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach of Walecka (known as the Walecka model) by replacing pointlike Dirac nucleons with finite-size skyrmions, building on the Skyrme model developed by Skyrme (1960s) and revived by Witten (1980s) in the large N_c limit of QCD. It contrasts with static crystal-like skyrmion solutions found by Houghton et al. and Piette et al., instead treating the nucleus as a dynamical aggregate of nucleons. The approach enables future investigation of how the meson fields modify nucleon properties (mass, moment of inertia) inside the nuclear medium, and leaves open the question of whether quarks become partially deconfined inside the nucleus.
