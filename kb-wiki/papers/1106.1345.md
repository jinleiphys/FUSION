# Nonperturbative Effects on the Ferromagnetic Transition in Repulsive Fermi Gases

> It is generally believed that a dilute spin-1/2 Fermi gas with repulsive interactions can undergo a ferromagnetic phase transition to a spin-polarized state at a critical gas parameter $(k_{\rm F}a)_c$. Previous theoretical predictions of the ferromagnetic phase transition have been based on the perturbation theory, which treats the gas parameter as a small number. On the other hand, Belitz, Kirkpatrick, and Vojta (BKV) have argued that the phase transition in clean itinerant ferromagnets is generically of first order at low temperatures, due to the correlation effects that lead to a nonanalytic term in the free energy. The second-order perturbation theory predicts a first-order phase transition at $(k_{\rm F}a)_c=1.054$, consistent with the BKV argument. However, since the critical gas parameter is expected to be of order O(1), perturbative predictions may be unreliable. In this paper we study the nonperturbative effects on the ferromagnetic phase transition by summing the particle-particle ladder diagrams to all orders in the gas parameter. We consider a universal repulsive Fermi gas where the effective range effects can be neglected, which can be realized in a two-component Fermi gas of $^6$Li atoms by using a nonadiabatic field switch to the upper branch of a Feshbach resonance with a positive s-wave scattering length. Our theory predicts a second-order phase transition, which indicates that ferromagnetic transition in dilute Fermi gases is possibly a counterexample to the BKV argument. The predicted critical gas parameter $(k_{\rm F}a)_c=0.858$ is in good agreement with the recent quantum Monte Carlo result $(k_{\rm F}a)_c=0.86$ for a nearly zero-range potential [S. Pilati, \emph{et al}., Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 105}, 030405 (2010)]. We also compare the spin susceptibility with the quantum Monte Carlo result and find good agreement.

## Key claim

The authors predict a second-order ferromagnetic phase transition in dilute repulsive Fermi gases, contradicting the Belitz-Kirkpatrick-Vojta (BKV) argument that such transitions are generically first order. This result is conditional on using a nonperturbative ladder resummation method rather than perturbation theory, which the authors argue is unreliable at the critical gas parameter of order O(1).

## Method

The authors summed particle-particle ladder diagrams to all orders in the gas parameter \(k_{\rm F}a\), corresponding to the leading order of a \(1/{\cal D}\) expansion where \({\cal D}=2^{D/2}\) with \(D\) being the space-time dimension. They used dimensional regularization with the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme to handle divergences and considered a universal repulsive Fermi gas with negligible effective range, corresponding to the upper branch of a Feshbach resonance with positive s-wave scattering length. The energy density as a function of magnetization \(x=(n_\uparrow-n_\downarrow)/(n_\uparrow+n_\downarrow)\) was evaluated from the summed ladder diagrams, and the critical gas parameter was determined from the divergence of the spin susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- Critical gas parameter \((k_{\rm F}a)_c = 0.858\) for the ferromagnetic phase transition, where the spin susceptibility diverges.
- The second-order perturbation theory predicts a first-order phase transition at \((k_{\rm F}a)_c = 1.054\).
- The predicted critical gas parameter agrees with the quantum Monte Carlo result \((k_{\rm F}a)_c = 0.86\) from Pilati et al. (2010).
- In the first-order perturbation (Hartree-Fock), the ferromagnetic phase transition occurs at \(k_{\rm F}a = \pi/2\).
- The coefficient of the nonanalytic term \(x^4\ln|x|\) in the second-order perturbation expansion is \(\upsilon = 40(k_{\rm F}a)^2/(243\pi^2)\), which is positive.
- The in-medium bound state has negative energy \(E(0) < 0\) only for \(0 < k_{\rm F}a < \pi/2\), and the condition \(E({\bf P}) < {\bf P}^2/(4M)\) is fulfilled for \(k_{\rm F}a < 1.34\).

## Context

This work extends the study of itinerant ferromagnetism in dilute Fermi gases beyond the perturbative regime, where previous predictions based on second-order perturbation theory (Kanno, 1966; Huang, 1987) gave a first-order transition at \((k_{\rm F}a)_c=1.054\). The authors challenge the general argument by Belitz, Kirkpatrick, and Vojta (1997) that clean itinerant ferromagnets undergo first-order transitions at low temperatures. The results enable comparison with quantum Monte Carlo simulations by Pilati et al. (2010) and provide a theoretical framework for interpreting experiments on repulsive Fermi gases of \(^6\)Li atoms using Feshbach resonances.
