# Effects of dark matter on the in-spiral properties of the binary neutron stars

> Using the relativistic mean-field model, we calculate the properties of binary neutron star (BNS) in the in-spiral phase. Assuming the dark matter (DM) particles are accreted inside the neutron star (NS) due to its enormous gravitational field, the mass $M$, radius $R$, tidal deformability $λ$ and dimensionless tidal deformability $Λ$ are calculated at different DM fractions. The value of $M$, $R$, $λ$ and $Λ$ decreases with the increase of DM percentage inside the NS. The in-spiral phase properties of the BNS are explored within the post-Newtonian (PN) formalism, as it is suitable up to the last orbits in the in-spiral phase. We calculate the strain amplitude of the polarization waveform $h_+$ and $h_\times$, (2,2) mode waveform $h_{22}$, orbital phase $Φ$, frequency of the gravitational wave $f$ and PN parameter $x$ with DM as an extra candidate inside the NS. The magnitude of $f$, $Φ$ and $x$ are almost the same for all assumed forces; however, the in-spiral time duration in the last orbit is different. We find that the BNS with soft equation of state and a high fraction of DM sustains more time in their in-spiral phase. We suggest that one should take DM inside the NS when they modelling the in-spiral waveforms for the BNS systems.

# Effects of dark matter on the in-spiral properties of the binary neutron stars

## Key claim

The presence of dark matter inside neutron stars reduces their mass, radius, and tidal deformability, and binary neutron star systems with a soft equation of state and a high fraction of dark matter sustain more time in their in-spiral phase; however, the gravitational wave frequency, orbital phase, and post-Newtonian parameter remain almost unchanged regardless of dark matter content.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with three parameter sets (NL3, G3, and IOPB-I) to calculate neutron star equations of state, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities. Dark matter (neutralino WIMPs of mass 200 GeV) was added via Higgs-mediated nucleon-DM interactions, and the in-spiral phase of equal-mass binary neutron stars was studied using the post-Newtonian TaylorT4 formalism up to 3.5PN order with tidal contributions included at 1PN accuracy.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of NS without DM: NL3 predicts 2.775 solar masses (stiffest), G3 predicts 1.979 solar masses (softest), IOPB-I predicts 2.149 solar masses
- For IOPB-I with DM momentum 0.03 GeV: maximum mass decreases to 2.051 solar masses; with DM momentum 0.05 GeV: maximum mass decreases to 1.788 solar masses
- Radius at 1.35 solar masses for IOPB-I without DM: 13.168 km; with DM3: 12.047 km; with DM5: 10.204 km
- Dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda at 1.35 solar masses for IOPB-I without DM: 855.304; with DM3: 577.237; with DM5: 210.387
- Lambda at 1.4 solar masses for IOPB-I+DM3: 473.22; for IOPB-I+DM5: 169.644 (both satisfy the GW170817 constraint of Lambda_{1.4}=190_{-120}^{+390})
- Last orbital in-spiral time for NL3: 0.0583 seconds; for G3: 0.0597 seconds; for IOPB-I: 0.0593 seconds; for IOPB-I+DM3: 0.0597 seconds; for IOPB-I+DM5: 0.0603 seconds
- Gravitational wave frequency in last orbit for IOPB-I: 2343.66 Hz; for IOPB-I+DM5: 2365.90 Hz
- Orbital phase in last orbit for IOPB-I: 105.178 radians; for IOPB-I+DM5: 108.224 radians

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter effects on neutron star structure by Das et al. (2020, 2021) and Quddus et al. (2020) to the binary in-spiral phase, incorporating the post-Newtonian formalism developed by Blanchet (2006) and tidal interaction methods from Vines et al. (2011) and Baiotti et al. (2011). The results are constrained by observational data from the GW170817 event (Abbott et al., 2017, 2018), which provided limits on tidal deformability, and by massive pulsar measurements PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie et al., 2019) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013). The work enables improved modeling of gravitational waveforms from binary neutron star mergers that accounts for possible dark matter content, which could affect the interpretation of future LIGO, Virgo, and KAGRA detections.
