# Dynamic Jet Charge

> We propose a modified definition of the jet charge, the "dynamic jet charge", where the constant jet momentum fraction weighting parameter, $κ$, in the standard jet charge definition is generalized to be a function of a dynamical property of the jet or the individual jet constituents. The dynamic jet charge can complement analyses based on the standard definition and give improved discrimination between quark and gluon initiated jets and between jets initiated by different quark flavors. We focus on the specific scenario where each hadron in the jet contributes to the dynamic jet charge with a $κ$-value dynamically determined by its jet momentum fraction. The corresponding dynamic jet charge distributions have qualitatively distinct features and are typically characterized by a multiple peak structure. For proton-proton collisions, compared to the standard jet charge, the dynamic jet charge gives significantly improved discrimination between quark and gluon initiated jets and comparable discrimination between $u$- and $d$-quark initiated jets. In Pythia simulations of heavy ion collisions, the dynamic jet charge is found to have higher jet discrimination power compared to the standard jet charge, remaining robust against the increased contamination from underlying event. We also present phenomenological applications of the dynamic jet charge to probe nuclear flavor structure.

## Key claim
The authors propose a "dynamic jet charge" where the constant weighting parameter κ in the standard jet charge definition is replaced by a function of a dynamical property of each hadron in the jet, specifically the hadron's momentum fraction z_h. They claim this modified definition gives significantly improved discrimination between quark and gluon initiated jets compared to the standard jet charge in Pythia simulations, while providing comparable discrimination between u- and d-quark initiated jets.

## Method
The authors define the dynamic jet charge as Q_dyn = Σ_h z_h^{κ(z_h)} Q_h, where κ(z_h) is a step function with parameters ξ_cut, k_<, and k_>. They perform Pythia 8.240 simulations of dijet events in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV and PbPb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV (using the Angantyr model), with anti-k_T jets of radius R = 0.4. Jet discrimination is quantified using Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curves comparing quark-initiated jets (from HardQCD:qq2qq=on) versus gluon-initiated jets (from HardQCD:gg2gg=on).

## Key numbers
- For pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV, jets are selected with 200 GeV < p_T < 300 GeV and |η| < 2.1; for PbPb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV, jets are selected with 80 GeV < p_T < 150 GeV and |η| < 0.9.
- The default dynamic jet charge parameters are ξ_cut = 0.3, k_< = 1.0, and k_> = 0.3.
- The standard jet charge distributions are shown for κ = 0.3, 0.5, and 0.7, with jet charge bins of size 0.1 in units of the proton electric charge e.
- The dynamic jet charge distributions are shown for k_> = 0.3, 0.5, and 0.7 with ξ_cut = 0.3 and k_< = 1.0.

## Context
This work extends the standard jet charge definition introduced by Field and Feynman (1977) and developed theoretically by Krohn et al. (2012) and Waalewijn (2012), which has been applied in ATLAS measurements (Aad et al., 2015) and CMS analyses (Sirunyan et al., 2017). The dynamic jet charge is presented as a complement to existing jet substructure tools reviewed by Larkoski et al. (2017), and is motivated by the finding of Chien et al. (2018) that quark-gluon jet discrimination power of various substructure techniques typically worsens in heavy ion collisions. The authors suggest future studies using JEWEL (Zapp et al., 2013) or JETSCAPE (Putzschke et al., 2019) simulations that include hot nuclear medium effects, which are absent in the Pythia/Angantyr model used here.
