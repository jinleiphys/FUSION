# Effective Operators for Double-Beta Decay

> We use a solvable model to examine double-beta decay, focusing on the neutrinoless mode. After examining the ways in which the neutrino propagator affects the corresponding matrix element, we address the problem of finite model-space size in shell-model calculations by projecting our exact wave functions onto a smaller subspace. We then test both traditional and more recent prescriptions for constructing effective operators in small model spaces, concluding that the usual treatment of double-beta-decay operators in realistic calculations is unable to fully account for the neglected parts of the model space. We also test the quality of the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation and examine a recent proposal within that framework to use two-neutrino decay to fix parameters in the Hamiltonian. The procedure eliminates the dependence of neutrinoless decay on some unfixed parameters and reduces the dependence on model-space size, though it doesn't eliminate the latter completely.

## Key claim

The paper uses a solvable SO(5) model to test whether standard prescriptions for constructing effective double-beta-decay operators in truncated shell-model spaces can accurately reproduce exact results, concluding that the usual treatment is unable to fully account for neglected parts of the model space, and that simple multiplicative renormalization requires different factors for two-neutrino and neutrinoless decay.

## Method

The authors diagonalize an SO(5)-based Hamiltonian in a model space consisting of a degenerate lower shell (pfg9/2) and a higher-energy degenerate upper shell (sdg7/2), representing 76Ge and 76Se. They project the exact wave functions onto the lower shell only, then test both traditional effective-operator prescriptions (multiplicative renormalization and two-valence-nucleon-based effective operators) and a Lanczos-based nonperturbative method against the exact full-space results. They also perform Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations in the same model and test a proposal to fix QRPA parameters by fitting two-neutrino decay data.

## Key numbers

- The short-range correlations and average excitation energy shrink the 0ν matrix element by a factor of about 1.5, almost independently of gpp
- At gpp=0.9 with ε=10G and gph=0, the renormalization factor for 0ν decay must be 1.8, while for 2ν decay it is 1.3; with ε=20G these numbers become 1.5 and 1.1 respectively
- The model uses a harmonic oscillator length parameter b=2.12 fm for single-particle wave functions
- The short-range correlation function uses γ1=1.1 fm−2, γ2=0.68 fm−2, and average excitation energy Ē=15 MeV
- The full paired-state space has dimension 1042 for 76Ge and 1347 for 76Se
- The energy splitting ε is set so that either about 20% or about 6% of particles occupy the upper shell; one test uses a very large ε giving 1% occupation

## Context

This work extends earlier SO(5) model studies of two-neutrino decay by Civitarese (1997) to the neutrinoless mode, which requires operators not built from SO(5) generators. It tests the proposal of Rodin (2003) to renormalize the QRPA Hamiltonian by fitting to measured 2ν decay, finding the procedure works less well in this model than in more realistic calculations. The paper enables improved understanding of how neglected single-particle levels affect neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements, and demonstrates that effective-operator methods successful for few-valence-nucleon systems fail to fully correct for truncation in heavier nuclei like 76Ge.
