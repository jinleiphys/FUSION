# Pion photo- and electroproduction with chiral MAID

> We present a calculation of pion photo- and electroproduction in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to and including order $q^4$. We fix the low-energy constants by fitting experimental data in all available reaction channels. Our results can be accessed via a web interface, the so-called chiral MAID. We explain how our program works and how it can be used for further analysis.

## Key claim

The paper presents a full calculation of pion photo- and electroproduction in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (RChPT) up to and including order \(q^4\), extending previous work on neutral pion photoproduction on the proton. The results are made available through a web interface called chiral MAID, but the calculation is restricted to energies below the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance and to momentum transfers \(Q^2 \leq 0.1\)–\(0.2\) GeV\(^2\) for a conservative estimate.

## Method

The authors compute the invariant amplitudes for pion photo- and electroproduction using manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to order \(q^4\), including both mesonic and pion-nucleon Lagrangians. They fix the low-energy constants (LECs) by fitting experimental data across all available reaction channels, using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme to restore power counting in loop diagrams. The resulting multipole amplitudes are implemented in a web-accessible program, chiral MAID, which provides numerical results for the various reaction channels.

## Key numbers

- The pion-decay constant in the chiral limit is \(F_\pi = 92.2\) MeV (with \(F_\pi = F[1+O(\hat m)]\)).
- The upper limit for momentum transfers is conservatively estimated as \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) and optimistically as \(Q^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The chiral expansion is performed up to order \(q^4\), where \(q\) denotes a small momentum or pion mass relative to a scale of about 1 GeV.
- The fine-structure constant is taken as \(\alpha = e^2/(4\pi) \approx 1/137\).

## Context

This work extends the results of Hilt et al. (2013) for neutral pion photoproduction on the proton to all reaction channels of pion photo- and electroproduction at order \(q^4\) in RChPT. It builds on earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard et al. (1992–2001) and addresses the power-counting problem using the EOMS scheme of Gegelia (1999) and Fuchs et al. (2003). The chiral MAID web interface enables further analysis and comparison with experimental data from threshold measurements by groups such as Beck (1990), Mazzucato (1986), and many others cited in the paper.
