# Exact solutions and attractors of higher-order viscous fluid dynamics for Bjorken flow

> We consider causal higher order theories of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics in the limit of one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion and study the associated dynamical attractor. We obtain evolution equations for the inverse Reynolds number as a function of Knudsen number. The solutions of these equations exhibit attractor behavior which we analyze in terms of Lyapunov exponents using several different techniques. We compare the attractors of the second-order Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS), transient DNMR, and third-order theories with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation. It is shown that for Bjorken flow the third-order theory provides a better approximation to the exact kinetic theory attractor than both MIS and DNMR theories. For three different choices of the time dependence of the shear relaxation rate we find analytical solutions for the energy density and shear stress and use these to study the attractors analytically. By studying these analytical solutions at both small and large Knudsen numbers we characterize and uniquely determine the attractors and Lyapunov exponents. While for small Knudsen numbers the approach to the attractor is exponential, strong power-law decay of deviations from the attractor and rapid loss of initial state memory is found even for large Knudsen numbers. Implications for the applicability of hydrodynamics in far-off-equilibrium situations are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for Bjorken flow, the third-order hydrodynamic theory provides a better approximation to the exact kinetic theory attractor from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation than both the second-order Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) and transient DNMR theories, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of shear relaxation rate and the conformal, boost-invariant system studied.

## Method

The authors derived evolution equations for the inverse Reynolds number as a function of the inverse Knudsen number for MIS, DNMR, and third-order hydrodynamic theories, simplified for (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken flow with longitudinal boost-invariance and conformal symmetry. They solved these equations numerically and analytically for three different choices of the time dependence of the shear relaxation rate, and compared the resulting attractors with the exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation. Lyapunov exponents were computed using several different techniques to characterize the attractor behavior.

## Key numbers

- The first non-vanishing coefficient in the gradient expansion is \(c_1 = a = 4/15\) for all three theories (MIS, DNMR, third-order).
- For MIS theory, the Lyapunov exponent extracted from the effective linearized equation is \(\Lambda = -3/2\), indicating exponential approach to the attractor with decay time \(2/3 \tau_\pi\).
- The ratio of consecutive gradient expansion coefficients \(|c_{n+1}/c_n|\) grows proportionally to \(n\) for \(n \gtrsim 5\), confirming factorial growth and zero radius of convergence.
- The attractor and all solutions join the asymptotic Navier-Stokes behavior after \(\bar{\tau} \gtrsim 3\), corresponding to Knudsen number \(\mathrm{Kn} \lesssim 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hydrodynamic attractors in Bjorken flow (Heller and Spalinski 2015, Romatschke 2017, Denicol et al. 2018) by providing new analytical solutions for the energy density and shear stress for three different forms of the shear relaxation time. It also builds on the derivation of third-order hydrodynamics from kinetic theory by Jaiswal (2013) and the DNMR formulation by Denicol et al. (2012). The results enable a more precise characterization of the hydrodynamic attractor and its Lyapunov exponents, with implications for understanding the applicability of hydrodynamics in far-off-equilibrium situations such as those encountered in heavy-ion collisions.
