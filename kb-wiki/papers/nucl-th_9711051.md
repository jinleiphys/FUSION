# Density Fluctuations in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Using the kinetic theory we discuss how the particle and energy densities of the quark-gluon plasma fluctuate in a space-time cell. The fluctuations in the equilibrium plasma and in that one from the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are estimated. Within the physically interesting values of the parameters involved the fluctuations appear sizeable in both cases.

# Density Fluctuations in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

Using kinetic theory, the particle and energy density fluctuations in a space-time cell are estimated to be sizeable for both equilibrium quark-gluon plasma and the nonequilibrium parton system from the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the results should be treated as a guide for more quantitative study because the approach is simplistic and assumes noninteracting, classical, massless partons.

## Method

The density correlation function for a classical system of noninteracting particles is coarse-grained over a Gaussian space-time cell of root-mean-square size Δx and Δt to obtain analytic expressions for the relative fluctuations of particle density and energy density. The formulas are then applied to two cases: an equilibrium quark-gluon plasma described by a Bose-Einstein plus Fermi-Dirac distribution at temperature T, and a nonequilibrium parton system from the early stage of Au-Au collisions at RHIC and LHC, using a flat rapidity distribution and perturbative gluon densities from Bir92.

## Key numbers

- For equilibrium plasma at T = 200 MeV and Δx = 1 fm: energy density fluctuations are about 14%
- For equilibrium plasma at T = 200 MeV and Δx = 0.5 fm: energy density fluctuations increase to 41%
- For equilibrium plasma at critical temperature T_c = 150 MeV with Δx identified as the inverse confinement scale (Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV): energy density fluctuations are about 22%
- For RHIC (√s = 200 GeV per N-N collision, central Au-Au): average gluon density ≈ 4.4 fm⁻³; energy density fluctuations are 13% for Δx = 1 fm and 38% for Δx = 0.5 fm
- For LHC (√s = 6 TeV per N-N collision, central Au-Au): average gluon density ≈ 63 fm⁻³; energy density fluctuations are smaller than at RHIC by a factor of 2.7
- Average particle density in equilibrium plasma: ⟨ρ⟩ ≈ 4.14 T³
- Average energy density in equilibrium plasma: ⟨ε⟩ ≈ 12.2 T⁴
- De Broglie wavelength in equilibrium plasma: λ ≈ 0.292 / T

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier argument (Mrowczynski 1997) that color current fluctuations can initiate plasma instabilities, and complements the finding by Gyulassy, Rischke and Zhang (1997) that initial energy density fluctuations significantly influence hydrodynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. The derived formulas provide a criterion for choosing the elementary fluid cell size in numerical hydrodynamical calculations and suggest that sizeable fluctuations near the deconfinement phase transition could speed up hadronization; the results are also relevant to density-dependent processes such as J/ψ dissociation in the plasma (Kar96).
