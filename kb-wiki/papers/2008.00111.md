# Three-body model for $K(1460)$ resonance

> The three-body $KK\bar K$ model for the $K(1460)$ resonance is developed on the basis of the Faddeev equations in configuration space. A single-channel approach is using with taking into account the difference of masses of neutral and charged kaons. It is demonstrated that a splitting the mass of the $K(1460)$ resonance takes a place around 1460 MeV according to $K^0K^0{\bar K}^0$, $K^0K^+K^-$ and $K^+K^0{\bar K}^0$, $ K^+K^+K^-$ neutral and charged particle configurations, respectively. The calculations are performed with two sets of $KK$ and $K\bar K$ phenomenological potentials, where the latter interaction is considered the same for the isospin singlet and triplet states. The effect of repulsion of the $KK$ interaction on the mass of the $KK\bar K$ system is studied and the effect of the mass polarization is evaluated. The first time the Coulomb interaction for description of the $K(1460)$ resonance is considered. The mass splitting in the $K$(1460) resonances is evaluated to be in range of 10 MeV with taking into account the Coulomb force. The three-body model with the $K\bar K$ potential, which has the different strength of the isospin singlet and triplet parts that are related by the condition of obtaining a quasi-bound three-body state is also considered. Our results are in reasonable agreement with the experimental mass of the $K(1460)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $K(1460)$ resonance can be described as a three-body $KK\bar K$ system using Faddeev equations in configuration space, but the agreement with experiment is qualified by the use of phenomenological potentials and a single-channel approximation that neglects some isospin-dependent effects.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for a bosonic $AAB$ system (two identical kaons plus one antikaon) using $s$-wave isospin-dependent $KK$ and $K\bar K$ potentials parameterized as one-range Gaussians with two sets of parameters (sets A and B). They considered four particle configurations ($K^0K^0{\bar K}^0$, $K^0K^+K^-$, $K^+K^0{\bar K}^0$, $K^+K^+K^-$) with different kaon masses and included the Coulomb interaction perturbatively for charged configurations where the $AAB$ symmetry was preserved.

## Key numbers

- The three-body quasi-bound state energy for the $KK\bar K$ system with averaged kaon mass 496.0 MeV using potential set A is $E_3 = -19.8$ MeV, yielding a total mass of 1468.2 MeV.
- For the $K^+K^+K^-$ configuration with potential set B including Coulomb force (B$_c$), the mass is 1457.8 MeV, the lowest value obtained.
- The mass splitting among the four configurations is evaluated to be in the range of 10 MeV when the Coulomb force is included.
- The two-body $K\bar K$ binding energy $E_2$ is -11.25 MeV for set A with averaged kaon mass 496.0 MeV.
- The relative contribution of mass polarization $\delta$ ranges from 23.9% to 30.0% across configurations and potential sets.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $KK\bar K$ system by Mart and Jido (2014) using a variational method and by Kanada and Jido (2008) using Faddeev equations in momentum space, but applies the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the first time to this system. The results are compared with experimental masses from the SLAC collaboration (Brandenburg, 1976), the ACCMOR collaboration (Daum, 1981), and the LHCb collaboration (2018). The approach enables future inclusion of more realistic isospin-dependent $K\bar K$ potentials and Coulomb effects in three-kaon systems.
