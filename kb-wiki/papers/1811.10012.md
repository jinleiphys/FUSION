# Topology change and nuclear symmetry energy in compact-star matter

> We show that the cusp-like structure in the nuclear symmetry energy, a consequence of topology change, visible in the skyrmion lattice description of dense matter is extremely robust. It is present in the Skyrme model -- with the pion field only -- and is left unscathed by massive degrees of freedom such as the vector mesons $ρ$ and $ω$ and also the scalar meson introduced as a dilaton. It leads to the emergence of parity-doubling at the skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition and impacts on the nuclear symmetry energy "wilderness" landscape, weeding out roughly half of the wilderness. It is shown that a pseudo-conformal sound velocity arises at a precocious density at which the cusp is formed. It is suggested that the topology change, being robust, could impact on the tidal deformability observed in gravity waves

## Key claim

The cusp-like structure in the nuclear symmetry energy, arising from a topology change at the skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition, is extremely robust and persists even when massive degrees of freedom such as vector mesons (rho and omega) and a scalar dilaton are included, not just in the minimal Skyrme model with only the pion field.

## Method

The authors computed the nuclear symmetry energy by collective-quantizing pure neutron matter in a skyrmion crystal lattice, using hidden local symmetric (HLS) Lagrangians that incorporate pion, rho, and omega meson fields. They performed numerical simulations on the crystal lattice to determine the static field configurations and then calculated the moment of inertia in isospace, from which the symmetry energy was obtained as E_sym = 1/(8 lambda_I). The calculations were carried out for several versions of the HLS Lagrangian: with pions only, with pions and rho mesons, and with pions, rho, and omega mesons, including a full O(p^4) treatment with parameters fixed from the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model.

## Key numbers

- The topology change density n_{1/2} is estimated to lie in the range 2 less than or similar to n_{1/2}/n_0 less than or similar to 4, where n_0 is approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The symmetry energy exhibits a cusp at the skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition density n_{1/2}, decreasing as density approaches n_{1/2} from below and then increasing as density increases away from n_{1/2}.
- The closure-sum approximation indicates that the net tensor force from pion and rho exchange would go to zero at approximately n similar to 3n_0 if naively extrapolated, but the topology change prevents this by suppressing the rho tensor at n_{1/2}.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by LPR (2015) that a topology change in skyrmion matter produces a cusp in the nuclear symmetry energy, and demonstrates that this feature is not an artifact of the minimal Skyrme model but survives the inclusion of vector mesons. The result impacts the "symmetry energy wilderness" landscape compiled by Chen (2015), which shows hundreds of model predictions diverging at high densities, by weeding out roughly half of those predictions. The robust cusp structure suggests that a pseudo-conformal sound velocity arises at a precocious density and could affect the tidal deformability observed in gravitational waves from neutron star mergers such as GW170817.
