# Strange quark matter with dynamically generated quark masses

> Bulk properties of strange quark matter (SQM) are investigated within the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. In the chiral limit the model behaves very similarly to the MIT bag model which is often used to describe SQM. However, when we introduce realistic current quark masses, the strange quark becomes strongly disfavored, because of its large dynamical mass. We conclude that SQM is not absolutely stable.

## Key claim

Strange quark matter (SQM) is not absolutely stable when realistic current quark masses are introduced into the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, because the strange quark acquires a large dynamical mass that makes it energetically disfavored; this conclusion is robust against parameter variations within the model.

## Method

The authors investigate bulk properties of SQM at zero temperature using the SU(3) NJL model in mean-field (Hartree) approximation, with a sharp three-momentum cutoff regularization. They solve the gap equation self-consistently for dynamical quark masses as functions of density, then compute energy density and pressure including a dynamically generated bag pressure. For beta-equilibrated charge-neutral matter, they include electrons as a free gas and impose chemical equilibrium via weak interactions.

## Key numbers

- In the chiral limit with the adopted parameter set (cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 1.835, K*Lambda^5 = 12.36), vacuum dynamical masses are equal for all flavors at 310.6 MeV, and the vacuum bag pressure B0 = 57.3 MeV/fm^3.
- With finite current quark masses (m0_u = 5.5 MeV, m0_s = 140.7 MeV), vacuum dynamical masses are m_u = 367.6 MeV and m_s = 549.5 MeV; the vacuum bag pressure B0 = 291.7 MeV/fm^3.
- For non-strange isospin-symmetric quark matter (r_s = 0) with finite current masses, the dynamical masses in stable matter are m_u* = 52.6 MeV and m_s* = 464.4 MeV, with a Fermi energy of 361.1 MeV.
- The minimum energy per baryon for beta-equilibrated SQM occurs at baryon density rho_B = 2.25 rho_0, with E/A = 1102 MeV, compared to approximately 930 MeV for an iron nucleus.
- Strange quarks appear only above a threshold density of approximately 3.85 rho_0.
- To obtain non-zero strangeness at the minimum of E/A, the current strange quark mass must be at most 85 MeV; to reach E/A approximately 930 MeV requires m0_s = 10 MeV or m0_s = 25 MeV with m0_u = m0_d = 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NJL model study of non-strange quark matter (Buballa, 1996) to the three-flavor case, directly contrasting with the MIT bag model calculations of Farhi and Jaffe (1984) that found a parameter window for absolutely stable SQM. The results contradict the original conjecture of Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984) that SQM could be the absolute ground state of strongly interacting matter, and they rule out the simple Pauli-principle argument for SQM stability. The paper enables future work to explore whether more sophisticated mechanisms, such as color superconductivity or color-flavor locking (Alford, 1998), could alter this conclusion.
