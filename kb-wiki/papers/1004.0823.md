# Spin and pseudospin symmetries in the antinucleon spectrum of nuclei

> Spin and pseudospin symmetries in the spectra of nucleons and antinucleons are studied in a relativistic mean-field theory with scalar and vector Woods-Saxon potentials, in which the strength of the latter is allowed to change. We observe that, for nucleons and antinucleons, the spin symmetry is of perturbative nature and it is almost an exact symmetry in the physical region for antinucleons. The opposite situation is found in the pseudospin symmetry case, which is better realized for nucleons than for antinucleons, but is of dynamical nature and cannot be viewed in a perturbative way both for nucleons and antinucleons. This is shown by computing the spin-orbit and pseudospin-orbit couplings for selected spin and pseudospin partners in both spectra.

## Key claim

Spin symmetry is almost exact for antinucleons in the physical region (where the vector potential strength is near 292 MeV), but this symmetry is of perturbative nature; conversely, pseudospin symmetry is better realized for nucleons than for antinucleons, but is of dynamical (non-perturbative) nature in both cases.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically for neutrons and antineutrons using spherical Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials with fixed range (7.0 fm) and diffusivity (0.6 fm), varying the vector potential strength V0 from 0 to 300 MeV while keeping the scalar depth S0 fixed at -358.0 MeV. They computed the spin-orbit and pseudospin-orbit coupling contributions to the energy splittings for selected spin and pseudospin partner states, using the second-order differential equations to identify the relevant coupling terms.

## Key numbers

- For neutrons at the physical point (V0 = 292.0 MeV), the spin partner splitting between 1p1/2 and 1p3/2 is 0.4983 MeV, while the pseudospin partner splitting between 1h9/2 and 1h11/2 is 4.4206 MeV.
- For antineutrons at V0 = 292.0 MeV, the spin partner splitting between 1p1/2 and 1p3/2 is 0.0140 MeV, while the pseudospin partner splitting between 1h9/2 and 1h11/2 is 0.1354 MeV.
- For antineutrons at V0 = 292.0 MeV, the spin partner splitting between 2s1/2 and 1d3/2 is 4.7148 MeV, and the pseudospin partner splitting between 2g9/2 and 1i11/2 is 13.6806 MeV.
- At V0 = 0, neutron and antineutron binding energies are identical; for example, epsilon(2s1/2) = -322.5962 MeV for both.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin and pseudospin symmetries from nucleons to antinucleons using the charge-conjugation transformation, which exchanges the roles of the sum (Sigma = V+S) and difference (Delta = V-S) potentials. It builds on prior relativistic mean-field calculations for nucleons in 208Pb by the same group (Lisboa et al., 2001; Alberto et al., 2002) and on the work of Zhou et al. (2003) who performed self-consistent calculations for antinucleons. The results show that antinucleon spin symmetry becomes nearly exact in the physical region because the Sigma potential becomes small, while pseudospin symmetry for antinucleons is strongly broken, opposite to the nucleon case. This clarifies the non-perturbative nature of pseudospin symmetry and the perturbative nature of spin symmetry in both sectors.
