# On the light front surfaces in the phase space of hadrons in heavy-ion collisions

> Surfaces corresponding to the constant values of the light front variable forms paraboloids in the phase space of the inclusively produced hadrons in heavy-ion collisions. In this paper, it is demonstrated with the simulated Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV using the UrQMD event generator that the paraboloidal surface defined by a certain constant value of the light front variable divides the particles in the phase space into two groups such that the distributions of the square of the transverse momentum, the polar angle and the light front variable of particles belonging to one among them can be described with the Boltzmann distribution parameterised by the mass-dependent temperature.

## Key claim
Using the UrQMD event generator for simulated Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the light front analysis scheme can successfully identify a paraboloidal surface in phase space that separates a thermalised group of particles, and this scheme works for particles heavier than pions, contradicting earlier arguments that the observed isotropisation was fortuitous and should disappear for higher-mass particles.

## Method
The analysis used 55,000 simulated Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with the UrQMD event generator (version 3.4). For each hadron species, the inclusive single-particle distribution of the light front variable |zeta| was fitted with an integral relation assuming a Boltzmann energy distribution; the lowest |zeta| value yielding a successful fit (chi-squared per degree of freedom near 1.0) was taken as the critical value zeta_c. The |cos(theta)| and p_T^2 distributions of particles inside the paraboloid defined by zeta_c were then independently fitted with the same Boltzmann-based integral relations to confirm thermalisation.

## Key numbers
- For pi+/- mesons: zeta_c = 6.50; temperatures extracted: T_zeta = 96 +/- 4 MeV, T_pT2 = 87 +/- 1 MeV, T_cos(theta) = 72 +/- 1 MeV
- For K+/- mesons: zeta_c = 5.50; temperatures: T_zeta = 183 +/- 6 MeV, T_pT2 = 147 +/- 3 MeV, T_cos(theta) = 129 +/- 2 MeV
- For eta0 mesons: zeta_c = 5.40; temperatures: T_zeta = 187 +/- 5 MeV, T_pT2 = 147 +/- 1 MeV, T_cos(theta) = 123 +/- 2 MeV
- For protons and antiprotons: zeta_c = 4.85; temperatures: T_zeta = 295 +/- 3 MeV, T_pT2 = 235 +/- 1 MeV, T_cos(theta) = 200 +/- 2 MeV
- For Lambda0 baryons: zeta_c = 4.90; temperatures: T_zeta = 221 +/- 14 MeV, T_pT2 = 177 +/- 4 MeV, T_cos(theta) = 161 +/- 6 MeV
- For Sigma0 baryons: zeta_c = 4.55; temperatures: T_zeta = 291 +/- 9 MeV, T_pT2 = 199 +/- 4 MeV, T_cos(theta) = 142 +/- 5 MeV

## Context
This work extends the light front analysis scheme previously applied to low-energy hadron-hadron and nucleus-nucleus collisions by Garsevanishvili (1978, 1979), Amaglobeli (1999), and Djobava (2003) to RHIC energies and heavier hadron species within the UrQMD model. It directly contradicts the criticism by Levchenko that the observed isotropisation was fortuitous due to low pion mass and low centre-of-mass energy. The results endorse the known understanding of thermalisation at low transverse momentum from blast wave models (Schnedermann 1993, Siemens 1979) and hydrodynamical simulations, while suggesting that further experimental studies at RHIC and LHC are needed to explore the relationship between the light front surfaces and quark-gluon plasma formation.
