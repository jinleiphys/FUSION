# Chiral restoration in excited nucleons versus SU(6)

> We compare axial charges of excited nucleons, as predicted by the chiral symmetry restoration picture, with the traditional, moderately successful for the ground-state baryons SU(6) symmetry. The axial charges of excited nucleons can (and will) be measured in lattice QCD simulations, and comparison of the lattice results with the two different symmetry schemes will give an insight on the origins of the excited hadron masses as well as on interrelations of chiral symmetry and confinement.

# Chiral restoration in excited nucleons versus SU(6)

## Key claim
The paper argues that diagonal axial charges of excited nucleons should be near zero if chiral symmetry is approximately restored in those states, whereas SU(6) symmetry predicts large nonzero values for several of them; the actual hedge is that chiral restoration is never complete, so the axial charges should only approximately vanish, with deviations proportional to the residual chiral symmetry breaking from the quark condensate.

## Method
The authors compare two symmetry schemes for excited nucleons: the chiral restoration picture, where approximate parity doublets have vanishing diagonal axial charges and unit off-diagonal axial charges, and the SU(6) x O(3) constituent quark model, where axial charges are computed from nonrelativistic single-quark Gamov-Teller operators acting on SU(6) wave functions. They calculate the diagonal axial charges for specific excited states (N(1440), N(1535), N(1650), N(1710), N(1520)) using explicit SU(6) wave functions and contrast these with the chiral restoration predictions.

## Key numbers
- SU(6) prediction for the diagonal axial charge of the Roper resonance N(1440): G^A = 5/3, identical to the nucleon axial charge.
- SU(6) prediction for N(1710): G^A = 1/3.
- SU(6) prediction for N(1535): G^A = -1/9.
- SU(6) prediction for N(1650): G^A = 5/9.
- SU(6) prediction for the 3/2^- N(1520) diagonal axial charge matrix element: (sqrt(5)/3) times the appropriate Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.
- Chiral restoration prediction for diagonal axial charges of parity-doubled states: G^A_+ = G^A_- = 0.
- Chiral restoration prediction for off-diagonal axial charges within a chiral doublet: G^A_{+-} = G^A_{-+} = 1.
- Empirical nucleon axial charge: G^A_N = 1.26, compared to the SU(6) prediction of 5/3.

## Context
This work extends the chiral restoration scenario for excited hadrons proposed by Glozman (2007) and others, which suggests that highly excited baryons form approximate parity doublets decoupled from the quark condensate, in contrast to the SU(6) x O(3) constituent quark model that has been moderately successful for ground-state baryons since the work of Gell-Mann (1964) and others. The paper enables lattice QCD calculations to distinguish between the two symmetry schemes; first unquenched lattice results for the N(1535) axial charge by Takahashi and Kunihiro (2008) already show a very small value supporting chiral restoration, and further lattice measurements of other excited states are now underway.
