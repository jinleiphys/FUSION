# Quark-Gluon Plasma: from lattice simulations to experimental results

> Theoretical studies of quarkonia can elucidate some of the important properties of the quark--gluon plasma, the state of matter realised when the temperature exceeds 150 MeV, currently probed by heavy-ion collisions experiments at BNL and the LHC. We report on our results of lattice studies of bottomonia for temperatures in the range 100 MeV < T < 450 MeV, introducing and discussing the methodologies we have applied. Of particular interest is the analysis of the spectral functions, where Bayesian methods borrowed and adapted from nuclear and condensed matter physics have proven very successful.

# Quark-Gluon Plasma: from lattice simulations to experimental results

## Key claim

Lattice simulations of bottomonia using non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the bottom quarks and full relativistic dynamics for light quarks show that S-wave states (Υ and η_b) survive up to temperatures of approximately 2.1 T_c, while P-wave states (χ_b1 and related channels) melt immediately above T_c, with the caveat that the apparent width of the ground state peaks at zero temperature is likely a lattice/MEM artefact rather than a physical effect.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on highly anisotropic lattices with dynamical light Wilson-type quark flavors, using NRQCD for bottom quarks with a mean-field improved action including terms up to O(v^4) where v^2_b ≈ 0.1. Correlators were computed for S-wave states (Υ, η_b) and P-wave states (χ_b0, χ_b1, χ_b2, h_b) at temperatures from 100 MeV to 450 MeV, and spectral functions were extracted using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) adapted from nuclear and condensed matter physics. The analysis included effective power plots, effective mass plots, and comparison with free lattice spectral functions to distinguish bound state survival from melting.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 100 MeV ≲ T ≲ 450 MeV (approximately 0.5 T_c to 2.1 T_c, with T_c ≈ 220 MeV on these lattices)
- Crossover temperature from ordinary matter to quark-gluon plasma: approximately 155 MeV (citing Borsanyi 2010 and Bazavov 2011)
- QCD coupling α_s at relevant scales: as large as 0.5 or larger, with perturbative estimates yielding α_s ≈ 0.4 from the observed width-to-temperature ratio Γ/T ∼ 1
- Bottom quark velocity squared in bottomonium: v^2_b ≈ 0.1
- Largest accessible momentum on lattice: |p| ≈ 1.73 GeV, corresponding to v ≈ 0.2 for Υ and η_b
- Ground state masses used for velocity calculations: M_Υ = 9.460 GeV, M_η_b = 9.438 GeV
- Thermal mass shift predicted by weak-coupling expansion: δE_thermal/M = 5.93 α_s (T_c/M)^2 (T/T_c)^2 ≈ 0.0046 (T/T_c)^2, consistent with lattice results within large errors
- Width predicted by leading-order weak coupling: Γ/T = (1156/81) α_s^3 ≈ 14.27 α_s^3

## Context

This work extends the FASTSUM collaboration's previous lattice studies of bottomonia (Aarts 2010, Aarts 2011, Aarts 2012, Aarts 2013) and updates earlier reviews (Aarts 2012, Allton 2013). The results support the sequential suppression picture of quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma, with P-wave states melting immediately above T_c while S-wave ground states persist. The findings are compared with analytic predictions from effective field theories (Brambilla 2010, Escobedo 2011) and with recent CMS results on sequential Upsilon suppression in PbPb collisions at LHC energies (Chatrchyan 2011, Chatrchyan 2012). The work enables quantitative comparison between lattice QCD predictions and experimental heavy-ion collision data, though the authors caution that the systems differ fundamentally (thermal equilibrium QCD versus an expanding fireball).
