# Triplet of kindred prompt-2p emitters in mass-8 proton-rich nuclei

> A triplet of kindred prompt-2p emitters in A=8 nuclei has been demonstrated. Two of these are the ground state of 8C and its isobaric analog state in 8B, both of which are analogs of the halo or thick-skinned nucleus 8He. The third member is the recently found fourth 1+ state in 8B. This new 8B state at E*=8.4 MeV was observed to decay to the ground state of 6Li by 2p emission. Momentum correlations between the decay products indicate that it is not a sequential 2p decay through a 7Be intermediate state, but indicative of prompt 2p emission with correlations similar to those of the other members of the triplet. Ab initio calculations with the symmetry-adapted nocore shell model indicate that these three states have very similar spatial wavefunctions, but with nucleons coupling to different spins, isospins, and isospin projections. The triplet of 2p emitters all have oblate shapes and by stripping two protons equatorially, decay to states which have prolate shapes.

# Triplet of kindred prompt-2p emitters in mass-8 proton-rich nuclei

## Key claim

A triplet of prompt two-proton (2p) emitters in A=8 nuclei has been identified, consisting of the ground state of 8C, its isobaric analog state in 8B, and the newly observed fourth 1+ state in 8B at E*=8.4 MeV; the claim is hedged by the fact that the 1+4 assignment for the new state is the best candidate among three possible spin assignments (1+4, 2+4, and 3+3) based on ab initio calculations, and the correlations are strikingly similar but not expected to be completely identical due to differing decay energies (Q2p = 1.3, 2.1, and 2.7 MeV for the three members).

## Method

Invariant-mass spectroscopy was performed using neutron knockout from an E/A=69 MeV 9C beam on a 9Be target to produce 8C states, and proton knockout from the same beam to produce 8B states decaying via the 2p+6Li exit channel. A second experiment using fragmentation products from an E/A=65-MeV 13O beam on a 9Be target confirmed the new 8B state. Momentum correlations between decay fragments were analyzed to distinguish prompt 2p emission from sequential 2p decay through intermediate 7Be states. Ab initio calculations using the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with the NNLOopt chiral potential were performed to calculate excitation spectra and intrinsic structures of the A=6-8 states of interest.

## Key numbers

- The new 8B state has excitation energy E* = 8.40(4) MeV and width Gamma = 0.88(7) MeV (weighted mean from two data sets).
- The 8B(0+2) isobaric analog state has excitation energy 10.619 MeV, determined by adding the 3.562-MeV gamma-ray energy from the 0+1 state in 6Li to the measured decay energy.
- Decay energies for the triplet: Q2p = 1.3 MeV (0+2 8B), 2.1 MeV (0+1 8C), and 2.7 MeV (1+4 8B).
- For the data set with the 9C beam, an invariant-mass peak for the p+3He+alpha exit channel was found at E* = 8.23(18) MeV with Gamma = 1.30(33) MeV, with a yield 1.2(4) times that of the 2p branch.
- The SA-NCSM spectroscopic factors for proton decay of candidate states to low-lying states in 7Be are less than 8%.
- The 1+4 state is predominantly L=0 (87%) with a moderate L=2 projection (11%); the 2+4 state has significant L=2 projection (39%); the 3+3 state is predominantly L=3 (79%).

## Context

This work extends the known phenomenon of prompt 2p emission from ground states of even-Z isotopes beyond the proton drip line (Pfutzner 2023) to an excited state inside the drip line where competing 1p decay channels exist, similar to the second 0+ state in 10C (Charity 2022). The triplet connects to the previously studied isobaric analog states of 8C and 12O in 8B and 12N (Charity 2010, Brown 2014, Webb 2019), and the mirror 1+4 level in 8Li was previously studied via beta-delayed triton emission of 8He (Borge 1993, Barker 1996, Grigorenko 1996). The results enable further investigation of the relationship between halo systems and prompt 2p emitters, and demonstrate that ab initio SA-NCSM calculations can explain the exotic decay mode through the identification of oblate parent states decaying to prolate daughter states via equatorial two-proton stripping.
