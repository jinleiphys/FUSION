# Final-state interactions and superscaling in the semi-relativistic approach to quasielastic electron and neutrino scattering

> The semi-relativistic approach to electron and neutrino quasielastic scattering from nuclei is extended to include final-state interactions. Starting with the usual non-relativistic continuum shell model, the problem is relativized by using the semi-relativistic expansion of the current in powers of the initial nucleon momentum and relativistic kinematics. Two different approaches are considered for the final-state interactions: the Smith-Wambach 2p-2h damping model and the Dirac-equation-based potential extracted from a relativistic mean-field plus the Darwin factor. Using the latter the scaling properties of $(e,e')$ and $(ν_μ,μ^-)$ cross sections for intermediate momentum transfers are investigated.

## Key claim

The semi-relativistic (SR) approach, when combined with the Dirac-equation-based (DEB) potential plus Darwin factor (DEB+D) for final-state interactions, can reproduce the asymmetric shape of the experimental scaling function from quasielastic electron scattering, whereas the Smith-Wambach 2p-2h damping model fails to produce this asymmetry at intermediate-to-high momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors start from a non-relativistic continuum shell model with Woods-Saxon potentials and relativize it using the semi-relativistic expansion of the electromagnetic and weak currents in powers of the initial nucleon momentum (p/m_N) while retaining relativistic kinematics. Two final-state interaction models are implemented: the Smith-Wambach damping model, which folds the bare response with a Lorentzian function derived from a complex self-energy parametrization, and the DEB+D approach, where the final nucleon wave functions are obtained by solving a Schrodinger equation with a local potential derived from the Dirac equation and multiplied by a Darwin factor. The scaling functions f_L(psi') and f_T(psi') are computed for 12C at momentum transfers q = 0.5, 0.7, and 1 GeV/c and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers studied: q = 0.5, 0.7, and 1 GeV/c for the scaling function calculations
- Smith-Wambach damping model parameters: alpha = 10.75 MeV, epsilon_0 = 18 MeV, epsilon_1 = 110 MeV for the imaginary self-energy parametrization
- Effective mass in the SWD model: M*/m_N approximately 0.8-0.9 for high momentum transfers
- The SR expansion is performed to first order in eta = p/m_N, where eta < 1/4 for typical bound nucleon momenta

## Context

This work extends the semi-relativistic approach previously developed by the same authors (Amaro et al., 1996-2005) for electron and neutrino quasielastic scattering to include final-state interactions. It builds on the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model results of Caballero et al. (2005, 2006), which showed that using the same Dirac-Hartree potential for bound and continuum states produces the asymmetric scaling function seen in experimental data from Donnelly et al. (1999) and Maieron et al. (2002). The Smith-Wambach damping model was originally developed for low-to-intermediate momentum transfers by Smith and Wambach (1988) and Co et al. (1988). The DEB+D approach enables the translation of the successful RMF description into the computationally simpler SR framework, and the results support the superscaling analysis (SuSA) method proposed by Amaro et al. (2005) for predicting neutrino cross sections from electron scattering data.
