# Vector analyzing powers in the $d(p,pp)n$ and $d(p,{\rm{^{2}He}})n$ channels at 135 MeV

> Vector analyzing powers, $A_x$ and $A_y$, of the proton-deuteron break-up reaction have been measured by using a polarized-proton beam at 135 MeV impinging on a liquid-deuterium target. For the experiment, the Big Instrument for Nuclear-polarization Analysis (BINA) was used at KVI, Groningen, the Netherlands. The data are compared to the predictions of Faddeev calculations using state-of-the-art two- and three-nucleon potentials. Our data are reasonably well described by calculations for the kinematical configurations at which the three-nucleon force (3NF) effect is predicted to be small. However, striking discrepancies are observed at specific configurations, in particular in the cases of symmetric configurations, where the relative azimuthal angle between the two protons is small which corresponds to the $d(p,{\rm{^{2}He}})n$ channel. The vector analyzing powers of these configurations are compared to the proton deuteron elastic scattering to study the spin-isospin sensitivity of the 3NF models. The results are compared to the earlier results of the proton-deuteron break-up reaction at 190 MeV proton-beam energy \cite{Mardanpour2010}. A disagreement is observed for both proton-beam energies between data and calculations which points to a deficiency in the treatment of spin-isospin part of the 3NF.

## Key claim

The measured vector analyzing powers \(A_x\) and \(A_y\) for the proton-deuteron break-up reaction at 135 MeV are reasonably described by Faddeev calculations using two-nucleon forces alone for configurations where three-nucleon force effects are predicted to be small, but striking discrepancies appear at symmetric configurations with small relative azimuthal angle between the two protons (the \(d(p,{}^{2}\mathrm{He})n\) channel), and these discrepancies persist at both 135 MeV and 190 MeV beam energies, pointing to a deficiency in the spin-isospin part of the three-nucleon force models.

## Method

A polarized proton beam at 135 MeV was directed onto a liquid deuterium target at KVI, Groningen, using the Big Instrument for Nuclear-polarization Analysis (BINA). The forward wall of BINA measured the energies and positions of the two outgoing protons for polar angles between \(10^\circ\) and \(35^\circ\) and full azimuthal coverage. The vector analyzing powers \(A_x\) and \(A_y\) were extracted from the asymmetry of events for spin-up and spin-down beam polarization states as a function of the arclength \(S\) along the kinematic curve for each angular configuration \((\theta_1, \theta_2, \phi_{12})\). The data were compared to Faddeev calculations using two-nucleon potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18) and two-nucleon plus three-nucleon force models (CDB+\(\Delta\), CDB+TM99, AV18+UIX).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 135 MeV; average beam current: 15 pA; target thickness: 3.85 mm; energy resolution: 5%.
- Beam polarization values: up state \(0.57 \pm 0.03\), down state \(-0.70 \pm 0.04\).
- For the configuration \((\theta_1, \theta_2, \phi_{12}) = (24^\circ \pm 2^\circ, 24^\circ \pm 2^\circ, 180^\circ \pm 5^\circ)\), the kinematic slice width \(\Delta S = 4.75\) MeV was used for event selection.
- For the \(d(p,{}^{2}\mathrm{He})n\) channel, the relative energy of the two protons at the center of the \(S\)-curve is less than 1 MeV.
- Systematic uncertainty from beam polarization is approximately 6%.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of the proton-deuteron break-up reaction at 190 MeV by Mardanpour (2010), which reported a spin-isospin anomaly in \(A_y\) at small azimuthal opening angles. The present results at 135 MeV confirm that the same deficiency persists at lower energy, ruling out a simple energy-dependent resolution of the discrepancy. The data are compared to elastic \(p d\) scattering results from Ramazani (2008) to study the spin-isospin sensitivity of three-nucleon force models. The findings challenge current three-nucleon force treatments (e.g., CDB+\(\Delta\), CDB+TM99, AV18+UIX) and motivate improved theoretical descriptions of the spin-isospin part of the three-nucleon interaction.
