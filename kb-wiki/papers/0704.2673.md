# Chiral symmetry and the string description of excited hadrons

> A large symmetry group is perhaps experimentally observed in excited hadrons which includes the chiral group U(2)_L x U(2)_R as a subgroup. To possess this large symmetry a dynamical model for excited hadrons, presumably a string model, should explain formation of chiral multiplets and, at the same time, predict coinciding slopes of the angular and radial Regge trajectories. This is possible only if both the dynamics of the string and the chirality of the quarks at the ends of the string are considered together. We construct a model-independent unitary transformation from the relativistic chiral basis to the ^{2S+1}L_J basis, commonly used in hadronic phenomenology as well as in the string models, and demonstrate that a hadron belonging to the given chiral representation is a fixed superposition of the basis vectors with different L's and S's. Thus the description of highly excited hadron in terms of a fixed L is not compatible with chiral symmetry and has to be disregarded in favour of the description in terms of the total hadron spin J. Therefore, dynamics of the string must deliver the principal quantum number ~n+J, in order chiral multiplets with different spins to become degenerate, as required by the large symmetry group.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a description of highly excited light-quark hadrons in terms of a fixed orbital angular momentum L is incompatible with the approximately restored chiral symmetry observed in these states; instead, the chiral basis must be used, and the string dynamics must deliver a principal quantum number proportional to n+J to achieve the observed degeneracy of states with different spins.

## Method

The authors construct a model-independent unitary transformation from the relativistic chiral basis (based on representations of the SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R chiral group) to the nonrelativistic {2S+1}L_J basis commonly used in hadronic phenomenology and string models. They derive the transformation coefficients using Wigner D-functions and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, showing that a hadron belonging to a given chiral representation is a fixed superposition of basis vectors with different L values. The transformation is applied to specific meson states to demonstrate that fixed-L descriptions fail for states with approximately restored chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For the axial-vector meson a1, the chiral vector |(0,1)+(1,0); 1 1++⟩ maps to a single term: |1; 3P1⟩.
- For the h1 meson, the chiral state |(1/2,1/2)_b; 0 1+-⟩ maps to |0; 1P1⟩.
- For the ρ meson, two distinct chiral representations exist: |(0,1)+(1,0); 1 1--⟩ = sqrt(2/3) |1; 3S1⟩ + sqrt(1/3) |1; 3D1⟩, and |(1/2,1/2)_b; 1 1--⟩ = sqrt(1/3) |1; 3S1⟩ - sqrt(2/3) |1; 3D1⟩.
- The paper references experimental data from pbar-p annihilation at LEAR (CERN) showing equality of slopes of angular and radial Regge trajectories, and clustering of many states with different spins, parities, and isospins around definite energies at masses M ~ 2.3 GeV, M ~ 2 GeV, and M ~ 1.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons observed in Crystal Barrel data from pbar-p annihilation at LEAR (Buggs, Anisovich, and collaborators, early 2000s) and the Generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Glozman, Nefediev, and collaborators, 2000s), which reproduces chiral restoration but fails to give equal slopes for angular and radial Regge trajectories. The paper contradicts the traditional naive quark model classification of light hadrons in terms of fixed L and S, which has been used for thirty years, and calls for revision of string models to incorporate quark chirality. It enables a new approach where the string energy depends only on the principal quantum number n+J and the chiral index, potentially explaining the large experimental degeneracy of mesons with different spins, isospins, and parities.
