# Probing coalescence of light nuclei via femtoscopy and azimuthal anisotropies

> The production mechanism of light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions is vital to understanding the intricate details of nucleon-nucleon interactions. The coalescence of nucleons is a well-known mechanism that attempts to explain the production mechanism of these light clusters. This work investigates the formation mechanism of these nucleon clusters with a combination of coalescence and femtoscopy of nucleons and nuclei. It is achieved by appending a coalescence and correlation afterburner (\texttt{CRAB}) to the \texttt{SMASH} transport model. To have a proper view of the anisotropy of light nuclei clusters, a mean-field approach to \texttt{SMASH} is applied. The anisotropic coefficients of various light nuclei clusters are calculated and compared to experimental measurements. To incorporate hydrodynamics into the picture, the anisotropic measurements are completed in a hybrid \texttt{SMASH}+\texttt{vHLLE} mode. In both approaches, the femtoscopy of nucleons and light nuclei is performed, reported with CRAB, and compared to the latest experimental measurements. An insight into cluster formation time is drawn by extracting the emission source size with the Lednický-Lyuboshits (LL) model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combining a coalescence afterburner with the SMASH transport model, using either a hard equation-of-state mean-field approach or a hybrid hydrodynamic mode, can simultaneously describe femtoscopic correlations and anisotropic flow of light nuclei in Au+Au collisions at 3 GeV, but only the hard EoS mean-field approach reproduces the negative elliptic flow (out-of-plane squeeze-out) observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors simulated Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV using the SMASH hadronic transport model in two configurations: a mean-field mode with a hard Skyrme equation of state, and a hybrid mode coupling SMASH to the vHLLE 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic code. A Wigner-function coalescence afterburner was applied to nucleon phase-space at cluster formation times of 20 and 30 fm/c to produce deuterons, tritons, and helium-3 nuclei. The CRAB correlation afterburner then generated momentum correlation functions for proton-deuteron and deuteron-deuteron pairs, which were fitted with the Lednicky-Lyuboshits model to extract emission source sizes.

## Key numbers

- Source radii for p-d pairs extracted from LL fits range between 3–3.5 fm across centralities; for d-d pairs, source radii range between 1.8–2 fm.
- At 20 fm/c cluster formation time, SMASH+Coal+CRAB with hydro mode reproduces p-d source sizes; at 30 fm/c, source sizes overestimate data by up to 30%.
- For d-d pairs, hydro SMASH at 30 fm/c gives the closest description; hard EoS shows ~10% overestimation for central collisions.
- The slope dv1/dy for deuterons from hard EoS SMASH shows about 6% deviation from STAR data; hydro mode shows ~9% deviation; for triton and helium-3, deviations reach up to ~25%.
- Hydro SMASH overestimates measured v2 of all light nuclei species by almost a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends coalescence studies by the STAR collaboration (Mi, 2022) on p-d and d-d femtoscopy in Au+Au at 3 GeV, and anisotropic flow measurements of light nuclei by STAR (2021). It builds on transport model calculations with SMASH (SMASH Collaboration, 2016) and earlier work using UrQMD (Bass, 1998) and AMPT (Lin, 2004). The results enable future femtoscopy studies of other light and hypernuclei species (p-3He, p-t, t-t, p-Lambda-3H) and systematic scans of emission source sizes across collision energies, which could further constrain nucleon-nucleon interactions and cluster formation times.
