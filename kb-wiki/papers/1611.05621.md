# Relativistic Effects and Three-Nucleon Forces in Nuclear Matter and Nuclei

> We review a large body of predictions obtained within the framework of relativistic meson theory together with the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach to nuclear matter and finite nuclei. The success of this method has been largely related to its ability to take into account important three-body effects. Therefore, the overarching theme of this article is the interpretation of the so-called "Dirac effects" as an effective three-nucleon force. We address the equation of state of isospin symmetric and asymmetric nucleonic matter and related issues, ranging from proton and neutron density distributions to momentum distributions and short-range correlations. A central part of the discussion is devoted to the optical model potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering. We also take the opportunity to explore similarities and differences with predictions based on the increasingly popular chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "Dirac effects" in the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, specifically the enhancement of the small component of the nucleon Dirac spinor in the nuclear medium, can be interpreted as an effective three-nucleon force (3NF), and that this mechanism alone yields the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter without introducing phenomenological 3NF parameters, though the authors acknowledge that this interpretation relies on treating the medium dependence consistently with rearrangement terms.

## Method

The authors review the DBHF approach, which starts from a realistic one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) and solves the relativistic Bethe-Goldstone (Thompson) equation to obtain the G-matrix in the nuclear medium. The nucleon self-energy is decomposed into scalar, time-like vector, and space-like vector components, and the Dirac equation is solved with density-dependent spinors to obtain the single-particle potential and total energy. For finite nuclei, most DBHF studies employ a local density approximation (LDA), though a recent calculation for 16O solves the DBHF equations directly. The authors compare DBHF predictions with chiral effective field theory (EFT) calculations at NLO, N2LO, N3LO, and N4LO, using both two-nucleon forces alone and including the leading (N2LO) 3NF where available.

## Key numbers

- The scalar component of the self-energy around saturation density is approximately -300 MeV, mainly from sigma meson exchange, compensated by a repulsive time-like vector component to yield a single-particle potential near -40 MeV.
- The empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter is -16 MeV per nucleon at a nuclear density around 0.16 fm^-3.
- The repulsive energy shift from the Dirac effect (Z-diagram) scales approximately as ΔE ∝ (ρ/ρ0)^(8/3).
- The hard core radius in early local NN potentials is r_c ≈ 0.4 fm.
- The D-state probability in the deuteron is used as an indicator for the position along the Coester band, with smaller D-state probability corresponding to softer interactions and larger binding energy/density.

## Context

This review extends the DBHF approach beyond infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei, neutron skins, momentum distributions, and the optical model potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering, building on earlier work by Brockmann and Machleidt (1984), Horowitz and Serot (1987), and van Dalen et al. (2004-2007). The paper directly compares DBHF predictions with chiral EFT calculations from the Idaho group (Entem and Machleidt, 2003; Machleidt and Entem, 2011) and the Bochum group (Epelbaum et al., 2000), showing that DBHF predictions fall within the uncertainty band of chiral EFT when 3NF are included. The work enables future direct DBHF calculations for finite nuclei without LDA, as recently demonstrated for 16O by Ring and collaborators (2016), and provides a framework for interpreting relativistic effects as effective three-nucleon forces that can be compared with explicit 3NF models like the Urbana force.
