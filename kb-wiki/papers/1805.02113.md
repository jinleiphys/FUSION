# What is the right formalism to search for resonances? II. The pentaquark chain

> We discuss the differences between several partial-wave analysis formalisms used in the construction of three-body decay amplitudes involving fermions. Specifically, we consider the decay Lambda_b -> psi p K- , where the hidden charm pentaquark signal has been reported. We analyze the analytical properties of the amplitudes and separate kinematical and dynamical singularities. The result is an amplitude with the minimal energy dependence compatible with the S-matrix principles.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that differences between partial-wave analysis formalisms for three-body decays involving fermions are dynamical rather than kinematical in nature, and that the commonly held belief that the LS formalism is inherently nonrelativistic is unjustified. The authors specifically analyze the decay $\Lambda_b \to \psi p K^-$ to show that a properly constructed amplitude with minimal energy dependence compatible with S-matrix principles requires careful separation of kinematical and dynamical singularities, with the latter being the only place where physical differences between formalisms appear.

## Method

The authors analyze the decay $\Lambda_b \to \psi p K^-$ by considering the equivalent s-channel scattering process $\Lambda_b \psi \to p K^-$ and performing a partial-wave expansion of helicity amplitudes. They construct parity-conserving helicity amplitudes (PCHAs) and isolate kinematical singularities by factoring out half-angle factors, threshold factors, and K-factors, then compare this canonical approach with a covariant projection method (CPM) using a basis of six independent Lorentz structures. The matching between the kinematical-singularity-free PCHAs and the covariant scalar functions yields conspiracy relations that must be satisfied by the isobar amplitudes at threshold and pseudothreshold to ensure regularity.

## Key numbers

- The decay $\Lambda_b \to \psi p K^-$ involves masses $m_b$ ($\Lambda_b$), $m_p$ (proton), $m_\psi$ ($J/\psi$), and $m_K$ (kaon), with the s-channel threshold at $s = (m_p + m_K)^2$ and pseudothreshold at $s = (m_b \pm m_\psi)^2$
- The partial-wave expansion runs over spin $j$ with minimum value $M = \max(|\lambda|, |\lambda^\prime|)$, where $\lambda = \lambda_b - \lambda_\psi$ and $\lambda^\prime = \lambda_p$, and the sum is truncated at finite $J_\text{max}$
- The K-factors for natural parity ($\eta=+$) and unnatural parity ($\eta=-$) are given explicitly: $K^+_{MN} = (p\sqrt{s}/m_\psi)^{M-3/2} (q\sqrt{s}/m_p)^{M+1/2} (1/\sqrt{s})^{M-N} Q^+$ and $K^-_{MN} = (p\sqrt{s}/m_\psi)^{M-1/2} (q\sqrt{s}/m_p)^{M-1/2} (-1/\sqrt{s})^{M-N} Q^-$, where $M=3/2$ and $N=1/2$ for the dominant partial waves
- The conspiracy relation for natural isobars at $p=0$ (threshold) is: $\hat A^{j+}_{+,++} / [\langle 1/2,1/2;1,-1|3/2,-1/2\rangle \langle 3/2,-1/2;j-3/2,0|j,-1/2\rangle] = (\sqrt{s}/m_p m_\psi) \hat A^{j+}_{+,+-} / \langle 3/2,3/2;j-3/2,0|j,3/2\rangle$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the $B \to \psi \pi K$ decay (Mikhasenko et al., 2017) to the more complicated fermion-boson case, specifically addressing the $\Lambda_b \to \psi p K^-$ decay where LHCb reported pentaquark signals (Aaij et al., 2015, 2016). The paper clarifies that the LS formalism, contrary to common wisdom, is not inherently nonrelativistic, and provides a practical parameterization suitable for data analysis of three-body decays involving baryons. This enables rigorous amplitude analysis of hidden-charm pentaquark candidates in final states containing a nucleon, a light meson, and a charmonium.
