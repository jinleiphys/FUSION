# In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group for Closed and Open-Shell Nuclei

> We present a pedagogical introduction to the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IM-SRG) framework for ab initio calculations of nuclei. The IM-SRG performs continuous unitary transformations of the nuclear many-body Hamiltonian in second-quantized form, which can be implemented with polynomial computational effort. Through suitably chosen generators, it is possible to extract eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian in a given nucleus, or drive the Hamiltonian matrix in configuration space to specific structures, e.g., band- or block-diagonal form. Exploiting this flexibility, we describe two complementary approaches for the description of closed- and open-shell nuclei: The first is the Multireference IM-SRG (MR-IM-SRG), which is designed for the efficient calculation of nuclear ground-state properties. The second is the derivation of nonempirical valence-space interactions that can be used as input for nuclear Shell model (i.e., configuration interaction (CI)) calculations. This IM-SRG+Shell model approach provides immediate access to excitation spectra, transitions, etc., but is limited in applicability by the factorial cost of the CI calculations. We review applications of the MR-IM-SRG and IM-SRG+Shell model approaches to the calculation of ground-state properties for the oxygen, calcium, and nickel isotopic chains or the spectroscopy of nuclei in the lower $sd$ shell, respectively, and present selected new results, e.g., for the ground- and excited state properties of neon isotopes.

## Key claim
The In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IMSRG) provides a framework for ab initio nuclear calculations that can extract eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian or drive it to block-diagonal form with polynomial computational effort, but the practical applicability of the IMSRG+Shell model approach is limited by the factorial cost of the configuration interaction calculations.

## Method
The IMSRG performs continuous unitary transformations of the nuclear many-body Hamiltonian in second-quantized form using flow equations with an anti-Hermitian generator. Two complementary approaches are implemented: the Multireference IMSRG (MR-IMSRG) for ground-state properties of closed- and open-shell nuclei using correlated reference states, and the derivation of nonempirical valence-space interactions via IMSRG for use as input in nuclear Shell model (configuration interaction) calculations.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state properties were calculated for oxygen, calcium, and nickel isotopic chains using the MR-IMSRG approach
- Spectroscopy of nuclei in the lower sd shell was studied using the IMSRG+Shell model approach
- Selected new results were presented for ground- and excited state properties of neon isotopes
- The triton ground-state energy shows a 5-6% dependence on the SRG flow parameter λSRG when only NN interactions are used and the flow equations are truncated at the two-body level
- The triton ground-state energy becomes invariant under SRG flow when three-body interactions are included, closely reproducing the experimental binding energy

## Context
This work extends the free-space Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) methodology developed by Wegner (1994) and Glazek and Wilson (1993) to in-medium applications for nuclear structure. The IMSRG approach is related to Coupled Cluster theory (Shavitt, 2009; Hagen, 2014), canonical transformation theory (White, 2002; Yanai, 2006), and the Irreducible Contracted Schrodinger Equation approach (Nakatsuji, 1976; Mazziotti, 2006), but differs by focusing on Hamiltonian evolution rather than wave functions. The method enables ab initio calculations of heavier nuclei than previously accessible with methods like the No-Core Shell Model, and provides a systematic framework for deriving nonempirical valence-space interactions that can replace phenomenological interactions like those of Brown (2006) for the sd shell.
