# Modification of hyperon masses in nuclear matter

> We discuss the properties of baryons within the framework of the in-medium modified SU(3) Skyrme model. The modification is performed by a minimal way, the medium functionals in the SU(2) sector being introduced. These functionals are then related to nuclear matter properties near the saturation point. The modifications in the SU(3) sector are performed with the additional changes of kaon properties in nuclear matter. The results show that the properties of baryons in the strange sector are sensitive to the in-medium modifications of the kaon properties. The consistency of the in-medium modifications of hadron properties in the present approach with other model approaches and phenomenological indications is also discussed.

## Key claim

The mass shifts of hyperons in nuclear matter are sensitive to the in-medium modification of kaon properties, but the authors treat the kaon mass modification parameter \(C\) as an arbitrary external parameter rather than a fixed prediction, noting that a more consistent approach would require adjusting it to kaon-nucleus scattering or kaonic atom data.

## Method

The authors extend an in-medium modified SU(2) Skyrme model to SU(3) by trivial embedding of the SU(2) chiral soliton into SU(3), introducing three independent density-dependent functionals \(f_1, f_2, f_3\) fixed to reproduce the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter near saturation density. The kaon properties in nuclear matter are then modified via a linear-density approximation \(F_K^* m_K^* = F_\pi m_K (1 - C\lambda)\), where \(\lambda = \rho/\rho_0\) and \(C\) is a free parameter, and the collective Hamiltonian is diagonalized to obtain density-dependent baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
- Input parameters in free space: pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 108.783\ \mathrm{MeV}\), Skyrme parameter \(e = 4.854\), pion mass \(m_\pi = 134.976\ \mathrm{MeV}\), kaon mass \(m_K = 495\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Density-dependent parameters fixed to reproduce nuclear matter EoS: \(C_1 = -0.279\), \(C_2 = 0.737\), \(C_3 = 1.782\)
- At \(\rho_0\): \((F_{\pi,s}^*/F_\pi)^2 \approx 0.52\) (spatial pion decay constant squared ratio), \((F_{\pi,t}^*/F_\pi)^2 \approx 0.14\) (temporal pion decay constant squared ratio), \((e^*_s/e)^{-2} \approx 1\) (spatial Skyrme parameter inverse-square ratio), \((e^*_t/e)^{-2} \approx 0.26\) (temporal Skyrme parameter inverse-square ratio)

## Context

This work extends the in-medium modified SU(2) Skyrme model of Yakhshiev (2013) and Yakhshiev (2015) to the strange sector, following the SU(3) Skyrme model formalism of Westerberg (1994). The results are compared qualitatively with chiral perturbation theory (Meissner 2001, Kirchbach 1997), QCD sum rules (Kim 2003), and phenomenological indications from hyperon-nucleon interactions studied via lattice QCD (Beane 2012), effective field theory with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Haidenbauer 2014, Petschauer 2015), and density functional theories (Lenske 2018). The approach enables future studies of hyperon properties in dense matter relevant to neutron stars and hypernuclei, with the kaon mass modification parameter \(C\) left to be constrained by future experimental data.
