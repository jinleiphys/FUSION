# Liquid-Gas Phase Transition and Instabilities in Asymmetric Two Component Systems

> The liquid-gas phase transition and associated instability in two component systems are investigated using a mean field theory. The importance of the role of both the Coulomb force and symmetry energy terms are studied. The addition of the Coulomb terms bring asymmetry into a mean field and thus result in important differences with previous approaches which did not include such terms. The Coulomb effects modify the chemical instability and mechanical instability domains shifting many features away from proton fraction point y=1/2 to a value closer to the valley of βstability. These features are discussed in detail.

# Liquid-Gas Phase Transition and Instabilities in Asymmetric Two Component Systems

## Key claim

The authors show that including the Coulomb force in a mean-field treatment of the liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric two-component systems (neutrons and protons) shifts the equal-concentration point and the peaks of the instability surfaces away from proton fraction y=1/2 to a smaller value y_E, which is close to the proton fraction in the valley of beta stability; this shift does not occur in previous approaches that omitted Coulomb terms.

## Method

The authors used a mean-field theory with a Skyrme-like effective interaction, including symmetry energy, Coulomb energy (approximated as a uniformly charged sphere of fixed radius R=8 fm), and surface energy terms. They derived the mechanical instability boundary (spinodal, from dP/dρ=0) and the chemical instability boundary (from dμ_q/dy=0 at fixed T and P) for a two-component system of protons and neutrons, and solved the resulting quadratic and cubic equations to map these surfaces in (ρ, y, T) and (P, y, T) space. Calculations included first-order degeneracy corrections and used force parameters from prior work (Lee and Mekjian, 2001).

## Key numbers

- The equal-concentration proton fraction y_E = 0.4256 at T = 10 MeV when Coulomb effects are included (with R = 8 fm, C = 231 MeV/fm³); without Coulomb, y_E = 0.5.
- The Coulomb coefficient used was C = 231 MeV/fm³, chosen to be appropriate for systems near uranium.
- The critical temperature for mechanical instability at a given y is T_c(y) = [a_0 - (2/3)(1/2 + x_0)a_0(2y-1)² - C y²]²/(6a_3), which is maximum at y = y_E.
- The mechanical and chemical instability boundaries coincide at y_E = 0.5/(1 + C/[(8/3)(1/2+x_0)a_0]) in the simplified (no degeneracy) case.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of two-component liquid-gas phase transitions (Lee and Mekjian, 2001; earlier work cited as Ref. 4) that included symmetry energy and surface effects but not Coulomb interactions. It also builds on studies of isospin distillation and fractionization in nuclear collisions (V. Baran et al., cited as Ref. 7; Refs. 8 and 9) which were based solely on symmetry energy effects. The results enable more realistic modeling of phase transitions in neutron-rich nuclear matter, relevant for radioactive beam experiments and neutron star interiors, and show that the Coulomb force introduces a new pair of coexistence densities with larger proton concentration in the gas phase than in the liquid phase.
