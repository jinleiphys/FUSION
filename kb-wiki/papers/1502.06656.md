# The light-front gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor

> We provide for the first time a complete parametrization for the matrix elements of the generic asymmetric, non-local and gauge-invariant canonical energy-momentum tensor, generalizing therefore former works on the symmetric, local and gauge-invariant kinetic energy-momentum tensor also known as the Belinfante-Rosenfeld energy-momentum tensor. We discuss in detail the various constraints imposed by non-locality, linear and angular momentum conservation. We also derive the relations with two-parton generalized and transverse-momentum dependent distributions, clarifying what can be learned from the latter. In particular, we show explicitly that two-parton transverse-momentum dependent distributions cannot provide any model-independent information about the parton orbital angular momentum. On the way, we recover the Burkardt sum rule and obtain similar new sum rules for higher-twist distributions.

## Key claim

This paper provides the first complete parametrization of matrix elements for the generic asymmetric, non-local, and gauge-invariant canonical energy-momentum tensor on the light front, generalizing previous work that only considered local symmetric tensors. The author shows that two-parton transverse-momentum dependent distributions cannot provide any model-independent information about parton orbital angular momentum, contrary to what some earlier work had suggested.

## Method

The author constructs a generic light-front gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor using Wilson lines along the light-front direction, then parametrizes its matrix elements for a spin-1/2 target using 32 complex-valued scalar functions of the skewness variable ξ and momentum transfer t. The constraints from linear momentum conservation, angular momentum conservation, and light-front conditions are imposed to reduce the number of independent functions, and the relations between these scalar functions and two-parton generalized parton distributions and transverse-momentum dependent distributions are derived.

## Key numbers

- The parametrization of the generic light-front gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor for a spin-1/2 target involves 32 complex-valued scalar functions (reduced to 29 independent functions due to a light-front constraint)
- The local energy-momentum form factors are identified: A_q(t) = A^{e,1}_2(0,t), A_G(t) = A^{e,2}_2(0,t), B_q(t) = A^{e,1}_4(0,t) + A^{e,1}_5(0,t) - A^{e,1}_2(0,t), B_G(t) = A^{e,2}_4(0,t) + A^{e,2}_5(0,t) - A^{e,2}_2(0,t), C_q(t) = A^{e,1}_3(0,t), C_G(t) = A^{e,2}_3(0,t), D_q(t) = A^{e,1}_4(0,t) - A^{e,1}_5(0,t)
- The forward limit of the generic tensor reduces to an expression with terms proportional to 2M^2 g^{μν}, 2P^μ P^ν, 2N^μ N^ν, 2P^μ N^ν, and 2P^ν N^μ, plus naive T-odd spin-dependent terms

## Context

This work extends earlier parametrizations of the symmetric local gauge-invariant Belinfante-Rosenfeld energy-momentum tensor given by Ji (1996) and Polyakov (2002), and the asymmetric local gauge-invariant Ji tensor parametrized by Bakker (2004). It also addresses the failed attempt by Leader (2012) to parametrize the local gauge non-invariant Jaffe-Manohar tensor, showing that the failure arose from neglecting the dependence on the light-front direction n. The results enable connections between the canonical energy-momentum tensor and generalized parton distributions as well as transverse-momentum dependent distributions, and recover the Burkardt sum rule while obtaining new sum rules for higher-twist distributions.
