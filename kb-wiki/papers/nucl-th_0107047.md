# Isospin Symmetry Breaking within the HLS Model: A Full ($ρ, ω, ϕ$) Mixing Scheme

> We study the way isospin symmetry violation can be generated within the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Model. We show that isospin symmetry breaking effects on pseudoscalar mesons naturally induces correspondingly effects within the physics of vector mesons, through kaon loops. In this way, one recovers all features traditionally expected from $ρ-\omg$ mixing and one finds support for the Orsay phase modelling of the $e^+e^- \ra π^+ π^-$ amplitude. We then examine an effective procedure which generates mixing in the whole $ρ$, $\omg$, $ϕ$ sector of the HLS Model. The corresponding model allows us to account for all two body decays of light mesons accessible to the HLS model in modulus and phase, leaving aside the $ρ\ra ππ$ and $K^* \ra K π$ modes only, which raise a specific problem. Comparison with experimental data is performed and covers modulus and phase information; this represents 26 physics quantities successfully described with very good fit quality within a constrained model which accounts for SU(3) breaking, nonet symmetry breaking in the pseudoscalar sector and, now, isospin symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

Isospin symmetry breaking in the vector meson sector can be generated naturally within the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model through kaon loop effects, by allowing different renormalization conditions for charged and neutral kaon loops, without requiring explicit isospin breaking in the vector meson tree-level masses.

## Method

The authors introduce isospin symmetry breaking by allowing the subtraction polynomials for charged and neutral kaon loops to differ (c± ≠ c0), which generates transition amplitudes among all three neutral vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) through kaon loop effects. They diagonalize the one-loop corrected HLS Lagrangian using analytic field transformations that depend on the Mandelstam variable s, producing complex mixing angles that account for the observed ρ-ω mixing phase. The model incorporates previously established SU(3) breaking via the BKY mechanism and nonet symmetry breaking in the pseudoscalar sector, then fits 26 physics quantities simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The ρ-ω mixing amplitude Πρω ≈ -4300 MeV² is small compared to mω², but the narrow ω width Γω = 8.4 MeV allows the isospin-violating contribution to be sizeable
- The ω→2π branching fraction attenuation factor is Γω/Γρ ≈ 0.05
- The real part of the pion loop in the ρ-ω peak region R(s ≈ mρ²) ≈ -(1.1 to 1.9) × 10⁻² GeV², compared with the imaginary part I(s ≈ mρ²) ≈ mρΓρ = 0.12 GeV²
- The Orsay phase φ = Arg[tan2δ(s ≈ mρ²)] = 100.7 ± 0.7 degrees, compared with a previous HLS fit value of 104.7° ± 4.1°
- The ratio |tan2δ|² ≈ Γ(ω → π⁺π⁻)/Γ(ρ → π⁺π⁻) = (1.24 ± 0.17) × 10⁻³, corresponding to an imaginary mixing angle of about 1 degree
- The ρ-ω mixing angle magnitude is comparable to the real ω-φ mixing angle of about 3 degrees

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on symmetry breaking within the HLS model (Benayoun et al., 1995-2000), which had already incorporated SU(3) breaking via the BKY mechanism (Bando, Kugo, Yamawaki, 1988) and nonet symmetry breaking in the pseudoscalar sector. It builds on the ω-φ mixing mechanism from kaon loop effects (mixing, 1999) and provides a unified framework that accounts for isospin violation alongside these earlier symmetry breakings. The model successfully describes 26 physics quantities including two-body decays of light mesons (VPP, VPγ, VVP, Pγγ, Ve⁺e⁻) in both modulus and phase, leaving aside only ρ→ππ and K*→Kπ modes as a separate problem. This enables a consistent treatment of isospin violation in applications such as the pion form factor, B meson decays, and precision tests of the standard model.
