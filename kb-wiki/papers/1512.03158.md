# Impact of nuclear mass uncertainties on the $r$-process

> Nuclear masses play a fundamental role in understanding how the heaviest elements in the Universe are created in the $r$-process. We predict $r$-process nucleosynthesis yields using neutron capture and photodissociation rates that are based on nuclear density functional theory. Using six Skyrme energy density functionals based on different optimization protocols, we determine for the first time systematic uncertainty bands -- related to mass modeling -- for $r$-process abundances in realistic astrophysical scenarios. We find that features of the underlying microphysics make an imprint on abundances especially in the vicinity of neutron shell closures: abundance peaks and troughs are reflected in trends of neutron separation energy. Further advances in nuclear theory and experiments, when linked to observations, will help in the understanding of astrophysical conditions in extreme $r$-process sites.

## Key claim

The paper shows that systematic uncertainties in nuclear mass predictions from six different Skyrme energy density functionals produce non-uniform uncertainty bands in r-process abundance calculations, with the band width depending strongly on mass number rather than being homogeneous as found in earlier sensitivity studies that varied individual masses randomly.

## Method

The authors computed r-process nucleosynthesis yields for neutron star merger and jetlike supernova scenarios using a complete nuclear reaction network, where neutron capture and photodissociation rates were based on masses from six Skyrme energy density functionals (SkM*, SkP, SLy4, SV-min, UNEDF0, and UNEDF1). Maxwellian-averaged (n,γ) reaction rates were calculated with the TALYS code using the statistical model, while photodissociation rates were determined by detailed balance; beta decay and fission rates were kept at default values and not varied between the mass models.

## Key numbers

- The functional UNEDF0 has an rms deviation from experimental masses of 1.45 MeV, while SkM* and SLy4 yield rms deviations around and greater than 5 MeV
- The best overall agreement with experimental masses using Skyrme EDF is around 600 keV, achieved with several phenomenological corrections
- The approximate r-process path during (n,γ)-(γ,n) equilibrium corresponds to a two-neutron separation energy of S2n/2 = 1.5 MeV
- The uncertainty band is narrow for the second r-process peak (A ~ 130) and broadens before the third peak (A ~ 195) in neutron star mergers
- For UNEDF0, the N=82 shell closure is quenched for very neutron-rich nuclei with Z ≤ 40

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear mass uncertainties in r-process nucleosynthesis by using correlated mass predictions from microscopic density functional theory rather than randomly varying individual masses as done by Mumpower (2015, 2016). The approach is complementary to sensitivity studies by Farouqi (2009) and Arcones and Martinez-Pinedo (2011) that considered mass models based on vastly different physical approaches. The results enable future work to improve other microphysics input such as fission yields and beta decay rates, and to explore additional astrophysical environments, with the goal of using observations to constrain the astrophysical conditions of r-process sites.
