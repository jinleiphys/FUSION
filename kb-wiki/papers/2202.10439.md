# Pion-induced radiative corrections to neutron beta-decay

> We compute the electromagnetic corrections to neutron beta decay using a low-energy hadronic effective field theory. We identify and compute new radiative corrections arising from virtual pions that were missed in previous studies. The largest correction is a percent-level shift in the axial charge of the nucleon proportional to the electromagnetic part of the pion-mass splitting. Smaller corrections, comparable to anticipated experimental precision, impact the $β$-$ν$ angular correlations and the $β$-asymmetry. We comment on implications of our results for the comparison of the experimentally measured axial charge with first-principle computations using lattice QCD and on the potential of $β$-decay experiments to constrain beyond-the-Standard-Model interactions.

## Key claim

The authors identify and compute new radiative corrections to neutron beta decay arising from virtual pions that were missed in previous studies; the largest correction is a percent-level shift in the axial charge of the nucleon proportional to the electromagnetic part of the pion-mass splitting, while smaller corrections impact the beta-neutrino angular correlations and the beta-asymmetry at levels comparable to anticipated experimental precision.

## Method

The authors adopt Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBχPT) with dynamical photons and leptons, expanding the neutron decay amplitude in the Fermi constant, the fine structure constant, and the chiral expansion parameter, while keeping all orders in the ratio of external momentum to the pion mass. They compute the amplitude to chiral dimension three (O(G_F α)) and four (O(G_F α ε_χ) and O(G_F α ε_{/π})), including one-loop diagrams with virtual nucleons, pions, photons, and charged leptons, and match the result onto a lower-energy pionless effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- The largest correction is a percent-level shift in the nucleon axial charge, proportional to the electromagnetic part of the pion-mass splitting (m_{π±}² - m_{π0}² = 2 e² F_π² Z_π).
- The pion mass splitting is given by m_{π±}² - m_{π0}² = 2 e² F_π² Z_π, where F_π = 92.4 MeV.
- The LECs c₃ and c₄, which contribute to the largest part of the correction, are precisely extracted from pion-nucleon scattering data.
- The Fermi function F(E_e) and the radiative correction δ^{(1)}_α(E_e) appear in the electron spectrum w = |p_e| E_e (E_0 - E_e)² F(E_e) (1 + α/(2π) δ^{(1)}_α(E_e)), where E_0 = (m_n² - m_p² + m_e²)/(2m_n) is the maximal electron energy.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hayen (2020) and Gorchtein (2021), who used current algebra and dispersion techniques to evaluate axial contributions from vertex corrections and the γW box diagram, but only considered asymptotic and elastic contributions assuming isospin symmetry led to a vanishing three-point function. The present calculation finds much larger than anticipated isospin-breaking corrections by explicitly treating the vertex corrections through one-loop diagrams in HBχPT. The results enable improved comparison of experimentally measured axial charge with lattice QCD computations and strengthen the potential of beta-decay experiments to constrain beyond-the-Standard-Model interactions, while also providing the first step toward a full EFT treatment of radiative corrections to multi-nucleon beta decays.
