# Bottom and charmed hadron spectroscopy from lattice QCD

## Key claim
Lattice QCD simulations of bottom and charmed hadron masses are broadly consistent with experimental measurements where available, but systematic uncertainties from different quark actions, lattice spacings, and chiral extrapolations remain significant; the lattice results favor the CDF measurement of the Ωb mass over the D0 result.

## Method
The survey compiles lattice QCD calculations using multiple bottom-quark actions (NRQCD, Fermilab, static) and light-quark actions (nonperturbatively-tuned clover, improved staggered, chirally-improved, domain wall, twisted mass) on various gauge configurations (Iwasaki, one-loop Symanzik/Lüscher-Weisz). Charmed baryon calculations use the Fermilab action for charm quarks with staggered sea quarks and either domain wall or staggered valence quarks. Chiral extrapolations toward physical u/d quark masses reach minimum pion masses ranging from 275 MeV to 600 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Ωbbb mass prediction: M = 14.371 ± 0.004_stat ± 0.011_syst ± 0.001_exp GeV (Meinel, using NRQCD bottom quarks on dynamical configurations)
- Minimum pion mass reached in bottom baryon studies: 275 MeV (DLW), 290 MeV (NG, LCMO), 331 MeV (MDLW), 461 MeV (BHLLS), 600 MeV (LW)
- Charmed baryon simulations use lightest u/d masses corresponding to mπ = 290 MeV (NG, LLOW)
- Dong et al. charmed meson study: lattice spacing 0.08 fm, volume (2.7 fm)^3, pion mass as light as 331 MeV

## Context
This survey reviews lattice QCD results from the three years preceding MENU 2010, extending earlier quenched calculations of charmed baryons (MLW, FMT, CH) to full dynamical simulations. The work enables comparison of lattice predictions with recent experimental discoveries of Σb, Σb*, Ξb, and Ωb baryons by CDF and D0 collaborations. Future opportunities include further charmed meson spectroscopy, motivated by questions about the nature of the Ds0*(2317) state.
