# Dynamical freeze-out phenomena: The case of $K^\pm, ϕ$ transverse momentum spectra in collisions of Au(1.23 A GeV) + Au

> We argue on a continuous (dynamical) kinetic freeze-out of $K^\pm, ϕ$ observed at midrapidity in collisions Au(1.23 A GeV) + Au. The simulations by means of a transport model of BUU type point to time independent transverse momentum slope parameters after 20 fm/c. The complex interplay of expansion dynamics and strangeness production/exchange/absorption as well as elastic scatterings involved in the reaction network does not support the previous interpretation of a late freeze-out of $K^-$ due to larger cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the transverse momentum slope parameters of \(K^\pm\) and \(\phi\) mesons in Au(1.23 A GeV) + Au collisions become time-independent after 20 fm/c, representing a continuous (dynamical) kinetic freeze-out, and that this behavior invalidates the previous interpretation of a later, cooler freeze-out of \(K^-\) due to larger cross sections.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model simulations of Au(1.23 A GeV) + Au collisions, tracking the time evolution of the transverse momentum spectra of \(K^\pm\) and \(\phi\) mesons at midrapidity. They extracted slope parameters \(T\) by fitting the transverse mass spectra with a Boltzmann-like function \(\frac{1}{m_t^2} \frac{d^2 N}{dm_t dy} = C \exp(-(m_t - m_0)/T)\) for times up to 60 fm/c and impact parameters from 1 to 10 fm.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameters \(T_{K^\pm, \phi}\) become approximately constant for \(t > 20\) fm/c for all impact parameters.
- The experimental slope parameters from HADES are about 91 MeV for \(K^+\) (centrality 30-40%) and \(69 \pm 7\) MeV for \(K^-\) (centrality 20-40%).
- The maximum nucleon density reaches about \(2 \rho_0\) (or \(2.75 \rho_0\) for \(b = 1\) fm) at \(t \approx 12\) fm/c, with the high-density stage (\(\rho \ge \rho_0\)) lasting from \(t = 7\) to 20 fm/c.
- The \(K^-\) from \(\phi\) decays contribute \(26 \pm 8\%\) of the total \(K^-\) yield, and their slope parameter is approximately \(\frac{2}{3} T_\phi\).
- Scaling up the total \(K^- +\) anything cross section by a factor of 10 increases the maximum of \(T_{K^-}\) by 15 MeV; switching off absorption channels lets the maximum of \(T_{K^-}\) increase towards 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical freeze-out concept familiar from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (Kolb and Turner) to strangeness-carrying mesons in heavy-ion collisions. It directly contradicts the interpretation of the KaoS Collaboration (Forster, 2003) that \(T_{K^+} > T_{K^-}\) arises from later decoupling of \(K^-\) due to larger cross sections, and instead supports the HADES Collaboration (Adamczewski-Musch, 2017) interpretation that the difference arises from \(\phi \to K^+ K^-\) feed-down, though with a more complex dynamical mechanism. The results provide a reference for future CBM experiments at SIS100 and for analogous studies of charm degrees of freedom in compressed baryon matter.
