# K-K-pp - an important gateway toward multi-kaonic nuclei

> Based on our Faddeev-Yakubowsky calculations of four-body kaonic nuclear systems it was revealed that the structure of K-K-pp is well approximated by two Lambda*=K-p's with strong mutual attraction. It is vitally important to study this nucleus, as it is an essential gateway toward multi-Lambda* nuclei. Two experimental proposals are presented: i) production of K-K-pp by p+p reactions at T_p = 7 GeV, and ii) search for K-K-pp from the invariant mass of M(Lambda-Lambda) around 2.6 GeV/c2 in high-energy HI reactions.

## Key claim

Based on Faddeev-Yakubovsky four-body calculations, the structure of the $K^-K^-pp$ system is well approximated by two $\Lambda^* = K^-p$ clusters with strong mutual attraction, but this conclusion depends on using an enhanced $\bar{K}N$ interaction strength (17% more attractive than the PDG $\Lambda(1405)$ ansatz) to reproduce the observed binding energy of $K^-pp$ at DISTO.

## Method

The authors performed Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the four-body kaonic nuclear system $K^-K^-pp$, varying the $(\bar{K}N)_{I=0}$ interaction strength over a wide range. They extracted wavefunctions to study the correlation between two $\Lambda^*$ clusters inside $K^-K^-pp$ and compared energy levels across multiple few-body kaonic nuclei ($K^-p$, $K^-pp$, $K^-K^-p$, $K^-ppn$, $K^-K^-pp$). Two experimental production mechanisms were proposed: direct production via $p+p$ reactions at 7 GeV incident energy, and indirect search via the $\Lambda\Lambda$ invariant mass around 2.6 GeV/$c^2$ in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $K^-K^-pp$ drops to -190 MeV for the DISTO-compatible interaction that reproduces the observed $K^-pp$ level.
- The $K^-p$ binding energy from the PDG $\Lambda(1405)$ ansatz is 27 MeV, leading to a predicted $K^-pp$ binding energy of about 50 MeV, roughly half the observed value.
- A 17% enhanced $\bar{K}N$ attraction accounts for the observed binding energy of $K^-pp$.
- The proposed $p+p$ production reaction uses incident proton kinetic energy $T_p = 7.0$ GeV.
- The expected invariant mass $M(\Lambda\Lambda)$ from $K^-K^-pp$ decay is around 2.6 GeV/$c^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier theoretical predictions for $K^-pp$ (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002; Yamazaki and Akaishi, 2002) and the experimental observation of high-density $K^-pp$ at DISTO (Yamazaki et al., 2010) and J-PARC E27 (Ichikawa et al., 2015). The calculations contradict the chiral predictions of Barnea et al. (2012), which yield shallow energy levels even for $K^-K^-pp$. The proposed direct production mechanism builds on the reaction mechanism for $K^-pp$ production (Yamazaki and Akaishi, 2007) and detailed cross-section calculations by Hassanvand et al. (2011). The paper enables experimental searches for multi-$\Lambda^*$ nuclei and tests of chiral symmetry restoration in the $\bar{K}N$ interaction.
