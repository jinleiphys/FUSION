# Are there near-threshold Coulomb-like Baryonia?

> The $Λ_c(2590) Σ_c$ system can exchange a pion near the mass-shell. Owing to the opposite intrinsic parity of the $Λ_c(2590)$ and $Σ_c$, the pion is exchanged in S-wave. This gives rise to a Coulomb-like force that might be able to bind the system. If one takes into account that the pion is not exactly on the mass shell, there is a shallow S-wave state, which we generically call the $Y_{cc}(5045)$ and $Y_{c\bar c}(5045)$ for the $Λ_c(2590) Σ_c$ and $Λ_c(2590) \barΣ_c$ systems respectively. For the baryon-antibaryon case this Coulomb-like force is independent of spin: the $Y_{c\bar c}(5045)$ baryonia will appear either in the spin $S=0$ or $S=1$ configurations with G-parities $G=(-1)^{L+S+1}$. For the baryon-baryon case the Coulomb-like force is attractive in the spin $S=0$ configuration, for which a doubly charmed molecule is expected to form near the threshold. This type of spectrum might be very well realized in other molecular states composed of two opposite parity hadrons with the same spin and a mass difference close to that of a pseudo-Goldstone boson, of which a few examples include the $Λ(1405) N$, $Λ(1520) Σ^*$, $Ξ(1690) Σ$, $D_{s0}^*(2317) D$ and $D_{s1}^*(2460) D^*$ molecules.

# Wiki Page: Near-Threshold Coulomb-like Baryonia

## Key claim
The $\Lambda_c(2590) \Sigma_c$ and $\Lambda_c(2590) \bar{\Sigma}_c$ systems can form shallow S-wave bound states (denoted $Y_{cc}(5045)$ and $Y_{c\bar{c}}(5045)$) via a Coulomb-like one-pion exchange potential, but only if the pion is not exactly on the mass shell; the finite effective pion mass limits the number of bound states to at most a few, with only the n=0 S-wave state surviving in concrete calculations.

## Method
The authors derived the one-pion exchange (OPE) potential for the $\Lambda_{c1} \Sigma_c$ and $\Lambda_{c1} \bar{\Sigma}_c$ systems using the Lagrangian of Cho (1994) and the formalism of Valderrama (2012) and Lu (2017), obtaining a Yukawa potential with an effective pion mass $\mu_\pi$ determined by the mass difference $\omega_\pi = m_{\Lambda_{c1}} - m_{\Sigma_c}$. They solved the Schrödinger equation with a delta-shell short-range potential at a cutoff radius $R_c = 1$ fm to compute binding energies, and introduced a complex short-range potential to model baryon-antibaryon annihilation effects.

## Key numbers
- Bohr radius: $a_B = 4.4^{+1.2}_{-0.8}$ fm for the $\Lambda_{c1} \Sigma_c$ system
- Bohr momentum: $\gamma_B = 45^{+10}_{-10}$ MeV
- Effective pion mass for charged pion exchange: $\mu_{\pi^\pm} \simeq 18$ MeV, giving eight times the standard range of OPE
- Binding energy in absence of short-range forces: $E = -0.8^{+0.4}_{-0.3}$ MeV
- For a hard-core at $R_c = 1$ fm ($c_0 \to -\infty$), ground state binding energy: $E_B = -0.09^{+0.06}_{-0.08}$ MeV
- For $c_0 = 1$ (threshold bound state from short-range alone), OPE shifts binding to $E_B = -1.9^{+0.5}_{-0.6}$ MeV
- Excited state appears for $c_0 > 0.9^{+0.2}_{-0.4}$
- Decay width of $\Lambda_{c1}^+ \to \Sigma_c^+ \pi^0$: $2.1^{+0.5}_{-0.4}$ MeV
- Dimensionless ratio $\lambda$ for $Y_{c\bar{c}}^+ \to \Sigma_c^+ \bar{\Sigma}_c^0 \pi^0$: $\lambda = 0.24$ (central value), range $0.18-0.30$
- $\lambda = 0.034$ for $Y_{c\bar{c}}^+ \to \Lambda_{c1}^{+} \bar{\Lambda}_c^{-} \pi^+$ and $\lambda = 0.045$ for $Y_{c\bar{c}}^+ \to \Lambda_{c}^+ \bar{\Sigma}_c^0 \pi^+ \pi^-$
- Energy gap to $\Sigma_c^+ \bar{\Sigma}_c^0 \pi^0$ channel: $4.37 \pm 0.49$ MeV below threshold
- OBE model with $\Lambda = 0.9-1.2$ GeV gives shallow bound state at $0.1-0.2$ MeV and deep bound state at $15-130$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the molecular explanation for the $P_c(4450)^+$ pentaquark proposed by Geng (2017), which involved a $\Lambda_{c1} \bar{D}$ component alongside the usual $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$ molecule. The mechanism is analogous to the $X(3872)$ as a $D^0 \bar{D}^{0*}$ molecule, but with a Coulomb-like potential instead of a tensor force. The authors note that the same binding mechanism applies to other systems of two opposite-parity hadrons with the same spin and a mass difference close to a pseudo-Goldstone boson, including $\Lambda(1405) N$, $\Lambda(1520) \Sigma^*$, $\Xi(1690) \Sigma$, $D_{s0}^*(2317) D$, and $D_{s1}^*(2460) D^*$ molecules. The work enables experimental searches for these predicted baryonia and baryon-antibaryon molecules near the $\Lambda_c(2590) \Sigma_c$ threshold, and suggests that a full coupled-channel calculation including $\Lambda_{c1} \bar{\Sigma}_c$, $\Sigma_c \bar{\Lambda}_{c1}$, $\Sigma_c \bar{\Sigma}_c$, and $\Sigma_c \bar{\Sigma}_c \pi$ channels would be valuable, similar to the treatment of the $X(3872)$ by Baru (2011).
