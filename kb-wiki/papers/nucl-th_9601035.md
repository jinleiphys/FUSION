# The eta-meson light nucleus resonances and quasi-bound states

> The position and movement of poles of the amplitude for elastic eta-meson scattering off the light nuclei d, t, ^3He, and ^4He are studied. It is found that, within the existing uncertainties for the elementary eta-N interaction, all these nuclei can support a quasi-bound state. The values of the eta-nucleus scattering lengths corresponding to the critical eta-N interaction which produces a quasi--bound state are given.

## Key claim

Within the existing uncertainties in the elementary ηN interaction, all light nuclei from deuterium to ⁴He can support a quasi-bound state; the critical ηN attraction required to produce such a state lies within the range of ηN scattering lengths reported in the literature (Re a_ηN between 0.27 fm and 0.98 fm), meaning the existence of these states is possible but not guaranteed.

## Method

The authors used the Finite-Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian, treating the η-meson and nucleon motions separately, to calculate the η-nucleus T-matrix at complex energies. They solved coupled integral equations for the Faddeev-type components of an auxiliary T-operator, using realistic nuclear ground-state wave functions for A=2,3,4 obtained via the Integro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) with the Malfliet–Tjon I–III nucleon-nucleon potential. The ηN amplitude was taken as a separable form dominated by the N*(1535) S₁₁ resonance, with three different range parameters (α = 2.357, 3.316, and 7.617 fm⁻¹) and the ηN scattering length parametrized as a_ηN = (g·0.55 + i g'·0.30) fm, where g and g' were varied to locate poles of the elastic amplitude in the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

- For the η⁴He system with α = 2.357 fm⁻¹ and g = g' = 1, the pole lies in the quasi-bound region (above the diagonal in the complex p-plane), while for ηd, ηt, and η³He the poles lie in the resonance region.
- The ηd resonance pole crosses the diagonal (becoming a quasi-bound state) when the enhancing factor g = 1.6536 (with g' = 1).
- The nuclear binding energies used were 2.272 MeV for deuterium, 8.936 MeV for A=3 nuclei, and 30.947 MeV for ⁴He; the corresponding r.m.s. radii were 1.976 fm, 1.685 fm, and 1.431 fm, respectively.
- The ηN scattering length was parametrized as a_ηN = (g·0.55 + i g'·0.30) fm, with the real part range from the literature being 0.27–0.98 fm and the imaginary part 0.19–0.37 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier optical-model estimates (Haider and Liu) that placed a lower bound of A ≥ 12 for η-nucleus bound states, and contradicts the experimental null results from Chrien et al. for η bound states with lithium, carbon, oxygen, and aluminum. It improves upon the authors' own preliminary FRA calculations (Rakityansky et al., 1995) that used simple Gaussian nuclear wave functions, by employing more realistic wave functions from the IDEA method. The results enable experimental searches for η-nucleus quasi-bound states in light systems (A ≥ 2), particularly noting that only the η⁴He system is predicted to have small widths, making it the most promising candidate for detection.
