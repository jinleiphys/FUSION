# Comment on "Negative heat ..." by Moretto et al

> The recent paper nucl-th/0012037 by Moretto et al. is commented

## Key claim

Moretto et al. claim that the first-order phase transition and backbending of the caloric curve in small systems like fragmenting nuclei is trivial; however, they miss essential facts about microcanonicity and the role of partition entropy, making their analysis too simplistic and, in key respects, wrong.

## Method

Gross critiques the Moretto et al. paper by contrasting the microcanonical ensemble (fixed energy, no heat bath or vapor pressure) relevant to nuclear multifragmentation experiments with the canonical (T,P,N) ensemble used in their analysis. He argues that the backbending of the caloric curve is a specific feature of microcanonicity, not a trivial artifact, and that the increase in the number of intermediate fragments and total surface area (including partition entropy) is essential to multifragmentation, a feature lost in Moretto’s liquid-drop scenario.

## Key numbers

- The example nucleus with 20 nucleons is far from a macroscopic liquid drop, having nearly no volume part.
- The paper references experimental backbending of the caloric curve T(E) in nuclear multifragmentation, e.g., Pochodzalla (1995) and D’Agostino (2000).

## Context

This comment extends the debate on phase transitions in small, non-extensive systems such as hot nuclei, atomic clusters, and biological or astrophysical systems, building on Gross’s own prior work (Gross 1997, Gross 1998). It directly contradicts the trivial interpretation of Moretto et al. (2000) and argues that the backbending is a nontrivial microcanonical signature, not reproducible in the canonical ensemble with fixed temperature and pressure. The work enables further investigation into how phase transitions can be defined unambiguously in small systems beyond the thermodynamic limit.
