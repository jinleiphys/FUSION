# QCD Scales and Chiral Symmetry in Finite Nuclei

> We report on our progress in the calculation of nuclear ground-state properties using effective Lagrangians whose construction is constrained by QCD scales and chiral symmetry. Good evidence is found that QCD and chiral symmetry apply to finite nuclei.

# QCD Scales and Chiral Symmetry in Finite Nuclei

## Key claim
The paper presents evidence that effective Lagrangians whose construction is constrained by QCD scales and chiral symmetry can describe finite nuclei, but the authors explicitly state that their work does not test QCD or chiral symmetry themselves, only effective Lagrangians constrained by them. The goal of constructing a Lagrangian whose coupling constants are all natural and whose predictive power is superior to the original NHM Lagrangian has not yet been achieved.

## Method
The authors performed Dirac-Hartree calculations in mean field approximation using relativistic point coupling models with contact interactions, solving the model equations for several nuclei simultaneously in a nonlinear least-squares adjustment algorithm (Levenberg-Marquardt type) with respect to measured nuclear ground-state observables. They scaled the empirically determined coupling constants according to the QCD-based Lagrangian of Manohar and Georgi (1984) and applied the chiral symmetry constraints of Weinberg (1990) to test whether the dimensionless coefficients become natural (of order unity). The original nine-parameter Lagrangian was extended to ten parameters by adding an isovector-scalar term of order Lambda^{-1}.

## Key numbers
- The nine original NHM coupling constants span 13 orders of magnitude in their dimensional form
- After QCD scaling, six of the nine dimensionless coefficients c_lmn are of order 1, two are of order 10, and one is of order 10^{-2}
- The original NHM Lagrangian achieved average absolute deviations of 2.52 MeV for binding energies (34 cases) and 0.020 fm for rms charge radii (17 cases)
- The pion decay constant used for scaling is 92.4 MeV and the pion mass is 139.6 MeV
- The QCD large-mass scale Lambda used is 1 GeV
- Three separate minima were found for the isospin-dependent coupling constants, with sums of the two coefficients being 0.57, 0.55, and 0.38
- The extended 10-parameter Lagrangian (calculation d) achieved 8 natural coupling constants with average absolute deviations of 2.60 MeV for binding energies and 0.022 fm for rms charge radii
- Constraining the eight-fermion interactions to be equal (calculations e and f) forced all coupling constants to be natural but increased chi-squared/pt to 57.61 and 68.92 respectively

## Context
This work extends the Dirac-Hartree calculation of Nikolaus, Hoch, and Madland (1992) for 57 nuclei, which used empirically motivated improvements to the Walecka scalar-vector model. The observation by Friar, Madland, and Lynn (1996) that the NHM coupling constants become natural when QCD-scaled motivated the present systematic exploration. The approach applies the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1990) and the dimensional power counting of Manohar and Georgi (1984) to finite nuclei. The work enables future development of more complete effective Lagrangians that may include additional isospin dependence, tensor terms, and explicit pions to achieve both naturalness and superior predictive power.
