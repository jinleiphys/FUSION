# A Monte-Carlo model for elastic energy loss in a hydrodynamical background

> We present a computation of elastic energy loss of hard partons traversing the bulk hydrodynamical medium created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The model is based on perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (pQCD) cross sections for $2\to 2$ processes in which a hard incoming parton is assumed to interact with a thermal parton from the medium. We model the interactions of this type in a Monte-Carlo framework to account properly for exact energy-momentum conservation, non-eikonal parton propagation, parton conversion reactions and the possibility to create additional hard recoiling partons from the medium. For the thermodynamical properties of the medium we use a hydrodynamical evolution model. We do not aim at a full description of high transverse momentum ($P_T$) observables at this point. Rather, we view the model as a starting point in obtaining a baseline of what to expect under the assumptions that the medium is describable by thermal quasifree partons and that their pQCD interactions with the high-energy partons are independent. Deviations from this baseline then call for more sophisticated medium description, as well as inclusion of higher-order processes and coherence effects in the pQCD scatterings.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Monte-Carlo baseline calculation of elastic energy loss for hard partons in a hydrodynamical medium, but explicitly states that this model does not aim for a full description of high transverse momentum observables; rather, it establishes what to expect under the assumptions that the medium consists of thermal quasifree partons and that their pQCD interactions with high-energy partons are independent, with deviations from this baseline calling for more sophisticated medium descriptions and inclusion of higher-order processes.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte-Carlo simulation where hard partons propagate through a 3-dimensional hydrodynamical medium, interacting via leading-order pQCD 2-to-2 scattering processes with thermal partons from the medium. The scattering rates were computed using spin- and colour-summed matrix elements with a thermal-mass-like cutoff scale m = s_m * g_s * T to regularize forward/backward singularities, and the medium evolution was modeled using a 1+1 dimensional azimuthally symmetric, longitudinally boost-invariant hydrodynamical framework with an EoS containing QGP, mixed, and hadron gas phases. The simulation accounted for exact energy-momentum conservation, non-eikonal parton propagation, parton conversion reactions, and the possibility of creating additional hard recoiling partons, with the highest-energy final-state parton selected as the new hard parton for continued propagation until the medium cooled below the critical temperature T_C = 165 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The initial hydrodynamical evolution time for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV was set to tau_0 = 0.17 fm/c
- The critical temperature for the phase transition was T_c = 165 MeV, with a bag constant B^(1/4) = 239 MeV
- The decoupling temperature for terminating hydrodynamic evolution was T_dec = 150 MeV
- For a static homogeneous QGP with L = 5 fm, alpha_s = 0.3, and s_m = 1, the estimated number of elastic scatterings ranged from 14-5 for gluons and 6-2 for quarks at T = 500-165 MeV
- In the high-energy limit, the average scattering angle between hard and thermal partons was approximately 110 degrees, with average s ≈ 8*E_1*T and average E_2 ≈ 3T
- The lower bound for parton transverse momentum was set to p_T_min = 5.0 GeV
- About 13% of punch-through partons originated from outside the final rapidity interval |y| ≤ 0.35
- In a simplified homogeneous static plasma cylinder (L = 5 fm, T = 500 MeV), up to 40% of original high-p_T gluons that became punch-through partons converted to quarks, but in the full 3-d hydrodynamical simulation only about 4% of punch-through partons underwent flavour conversion
- With alpha_s = 0.3 and s_m = 1.0, the hadronic R_AA was approximately 0.6, consistent with the AMY formalism calculation by Ruppert using alpha_s = 0.27

## Context

This work extends prior elastic energy loss calculations by Thoma, Mustafa, Djordjevic, and others by moving beyond the eikonal approximation and including exact energy-momentum conservation, parton conversion reactions, and non-eikonal propagation in a Monte-Carlo framework. The model uses a realistic 3-d hydrodynamical background (based on the EKRT saturation model and the Hydro framework) rather than the static medium used in JEWEL, and shares features with parton cascade models like those by Geiger and Müller while assuming the bulk medium is thermalized. The results provide a baseline for elastic energy loss that can be compared with radiative energy loss calculations and experimental data from PHENIX, enabling future work to identify where more sophisticated medium descriptions, higher-order processes, and coherence effects are needed.
