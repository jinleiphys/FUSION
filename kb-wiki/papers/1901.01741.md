# Observation of excited states in $^{20}$Mg sheds light on nuclear forces and shell evolution

> The exotic Borromean nucleus $^{20}$Mg with $N$ = 8, located at the proton drip-line provides a unique testing ground for nuclear forces and the evolution of shell structure in the neutron-deficient region. We report on the first observation of proton unbound resonances together with bound states in $^{20}$Mg from the $^{20}$Mg($d$,$d'$) reaction performed at TRIUMF. Phenomenological shell-model calculations offer a reasonable description. However, our experimental results present a challenge for current first-principles nuclear structure approaches and point to the need for improved chiral forces and {\it ab initio} calculations. Furthermore, the differential cross section of the first excited state is compared with distorted-wave Born approximation calculations to deduce a neutron quadrupole deformation parameter of $β_n$=0.46$\pm$0.21. This provides the first indication of a possible weakening of the $N$ = 8 shell closure at the proton drip-line.

## Key claim

The first observation of proton unbound resonances in the Borromean nucleus $^{20}$Mg, combined with bound-state measurements, challenges current first-principles nuclear structure approaches and suggests a possible weakening of the $N=8$ shell closure at the proton drip-line, though the neutron quadrupole deformation parameter of $\beta_n = 0.46 \pm 0.21$ carries a large uncertainty.

## Method

The experiment used the $^{20}$Mg($d$,$d'$) reaction at TRIUMF's IRIS facility with a 8.5$A$ MeV radioactive beam of $^{20}$Mg impinging on a thin windowless solid deuterium target. Deuterons scattered from the target were detected using silicon-CsI(Tl) detector telescopes covering laboratory angles of 30.1$^\circ$ to 56.2$^\circ$, and the excitation energy spectrum was reconstructed via the missing mass technique. The differential cross sections were compared with distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations to extract deformation parameters, and the experimental results were compared with phenomenological shell-model calculations and multiple ab initio approaches (VS-IMSRG, CCEI, and NCSM) using various chiral NN+3N interactions.

## Key numbers

- First excited state (bound) in $^{20}$Mg observed at 1.65$^{+0.02}_{-0.10}$ MeV, consistent with prior gamma-ray measurements at 1.598(10) MeV (GA07) and 1.61(6) MeV (IW08).
- First proton unbound resonance observed at 3.70$^{+0.02}_{-0.20}$ MeV with an apparent resonance width of 0.47$\pm$0.06 MeV after unfolding the detection resolution.
- A small structure observed at 5.37$\pm$0.02 MeV (uncertainty from fitting only).
- Neutron quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_n = 0.46 \pm 0.21$ deduced from the differential cross section of the first excited state, using a proton deformation parameter of 0.44(4) from Coulomb excitation (IW08) and a proton radius of 3 fm.
- Deformation length $\delta = 1.33 \pm 0.23$ fm for the first excited state.
- The two-proton separation energy of $^{20}$Mg is $S_{2p} = 2.337(27)$ MeV (AME12).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the $N=8$ shell closure, which is known to disappear at the neutron drip-line (forming the two-neutron halo in $^{11}$Li), by providing the first experimental data on excited states above the proton threshold in the mirror nucleus $^{20}$Mg at the proton drip-line. The results challenge current ab initio calculations using chiral forces (VS-IMSRG and CCEI frameworks), which systematically underpredict the observed resonance energies, and point to the need for improved chiral forces and inclusion of continuum coupling effects. The measured neutron deformation provides the first indication of possible weakening of the $N=8$ shell closure at the proton drip-line, and the resonance data may inform studies of the hot CNO cycle breakout path via two-proton capture on $^{18}$Ne in Type-I x-ray bursts.
