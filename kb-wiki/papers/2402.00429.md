# Electromagnetic and gravitational form factors of the nucleon

> Form factors are Lorentz invariant functions describing the internal structure of a system. In particular, they encode how physical properties like, e.g., charge, energy, momentum, and pressure are spatially distributed. While nucleon electromagnetic form factors have been studied for a long time, the first extraction of nucleon gravitational form factors from experimental data was reported in 2018, triggering a lot of enthusiasm and attention in the hadronic community. In this contribution we review some theoretical bases, discuss recent developments regarding the physical interpretation of these form factors, and give a glimpse of what can be learned about the mass and spin structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim
This review argues that relativistic spatial distributions of nucleon properties are inherently frame-dependent, and that the phase-space formalism provides a natural quasi-probabilistic framework that interpolates between the Breit frame and light-front representations, reconciling their apparent differences.

## Method
The author applies a phase-space (Wigner distribution) formalism to the matrix elements of the electromagnetic current and the energy-momentum tensor for a nucleon target. The formalism is used to derive elastic-frame (EF) distributions that depend on the average longitudinal momentum \(P_z\), connecting the Breit-frame (BF) limit \(P_z \to 0\) to the infinite-momentum-frame (IMF) limit \(P_z \to \infty\). The spin structure of the nucleon is explicitly included via Wigner spin rotation matrices, showing how boosts mix electric and magnetic contributions and distort the spatial distributions.

## Key numbers
- The total electric charge \(\mathcal Q = \int d^2b_\perp\, J^0_\text{EF}(\vec b_\perp;P_z)\big|_{s'=s} = \frac{\langle p,s|j^0(0)|p,s\rangle}{2p^0}\) is independent of \(P_z\), confirming correct normalization.
- The gravitational form factors satisfy \(A_q(0)+A_g(0)=1\) (linear momentum conservation), \(J_q(0)+J_g(0)=\tfrac{1}{2}\) (angular momentum conservation), and \(\bar C_q(Q^2)+\bar C_g(Q^2)=0\) (mechanical equilibrium).
- The von Laue condition \(\int d^3r\,p(r)=0\) ensures mechanical equilibrium, and the quantity \(D_q(0)+D_g(0)=M\int d^3r\,r^2\,p(r)\) is suggested to be negative for stability.
- The first experimental extraction of the pressure distribution inside the proton from deeply virtual Compton scattering was reported in Burkert (2018).

## Context
This review extends the long-standing study of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Sachs, 1962; Ernst, 1960) to the gravitational form factors of the energy-momentum tensor, whose first extraction from experimental data was reported in 2018. The phase-space approach (Lorce, 2018) reconciles the Breit-frame and light-front interpretations of spatial distributions, which had previously shown conflicting features such as the negative center of the neutron's light-front charge distribution. The formalism enables future experimental programs at the Electron-Ion Collider (AbdulKhalek, 2021) and provides a framework for interpreting lattice QCD calculations (Alexandrou, 2020; Pefkou, 2021; Wang, 2021) of gravitational form factors.
