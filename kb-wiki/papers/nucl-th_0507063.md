# Cherenkov Radiation from Jets in Heavy-ion Collisions

> The possibility of Cherenkov-like gluon bremsstrahlung in dense matter is studied. We point out that the occurrence of Cherenkov radiation in dense matter is sensitive to the presence of partonic bound states. This is illustrated by a calculation of the dispersion relation of a massless particle in a simple model in which it couples to two different massive resonance states. We further argue that detailed spectroscopy of jet correlations can directly probe the index of refraction of this matter, which in turn will provide information about the mass scale of these partonic bound states.

## Key claim

Cherenkov-like gluon bremsstrahlung in dense QCD matter can occur only if the medium contains partonic bound states that provide an attractive interaction via resonant scattering; the observation of such radiation would therefore serve as a signal for the presence of bound states in the quark-gluon plasma, but the effect is sensitive to the specific mass scales of those states and is not present in a plasma described by the Hard Thermal Loop approximation alone.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop thermal self-energy of a massless scalar field (representing a radiated gluon) coupled to two massive scalar fields (representing a bound state and its excitation) in a simple model Lagrangian. They extract the real part of the self-energy to obtain the in-medium dispersion relation and determine the index of refraction, from which the Cherenkov angle and energy loss are derived. The calculation is performed at finite temperature using thermal (Bose-Einstein) distributions, with masses and coupling expressed in units of temperature.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the QCD phase transition is taken as \(T_c \simeq 170\) MeV (from lattice QCD).
- For the model parameters \(m_1 = T\), \(m_2 = 3T\), and \(g = 2T\), the dispersion relation becomes space-like at low momenta, enabling Cherenkov radiation.
- The Cherenkov energy loss is estimated as \(dE/dx \sim 0.1\) GeV/fm for a temperature \(T \sim 300\) MeV.
- The typical energy scale for soft modes where Cherenkov radiation can occur is \(p_0 \sim T\).

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of Cherenkov gluon radiation (Mueller; Dremin) by identifying the necessary condition of partonic bound states, which had not been addressed in the Hard Thermal Loop approximation (HTL) or in quenched lattice QCD calculations (Petreczky 2001). The proposed mechanism is contrasted with Mach cones (Shuryak; Stoecker) and with Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal-induced bremsstrahlung (Vitev; Majumder and Wang 2005). The results enable experimental searches for bound states in the quark-gluon plasma via momentum-dependent cone angles in two-particle correlations at RHIC, and they motivate further work on collision-induced Cherenkov-like bremsstrahlung to simultaneously explain jet energy loss and conical emission patterns.
