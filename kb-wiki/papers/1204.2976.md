# Collective modes of an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma induced by relativistic jets

> We discuss the characteristics of collective modes induced by relativistic jets in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma(AQGP). Assuming a tsunami-like initial jet distribution, it is found that the dispersion relations for both the stable and unstable modes are modified substantially due to the passage of jet compared to the case when there is no jet. It has also been shown that the growth rate of instability first increases compared to the no jet case and then completely turned into damping except the case when the jet velocity is perpendicular to the wave vector in which case the instability always grows. Thus, the introduction of the jet in the AQGP, in general, might to faster isotropization for the special case when the wave vector is parallel to the anisotropy axis.

# Collective modes of an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma induced by relativistic jets

## Key claim

The introduction of a relativistic jet into an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (AQGP) modifies the dispersion relations of both stable and unstable collective modes, and the growth rate of the Weibel-type instability first increases compared to the no-jet case and then turns into damping, except when the jet velocity is perpendicular to the wave vector ($\theta_{jet} = \pi/2$), in which case the instability always grows. This suggests that jet-induced effects may lead to faster isotropization specifically when the wave vector is parallel to the anisotropy axis ($\hat{k} \parallel \hat{n}$).

## Method

The authors use a Vlasov-type transport theory in the weak-coupling regime ($g \ll 1$), neglecting collision terms under the assumption that the time scale for collective mode evolution is much shorter than the inter-particle collision time. The anisotropic plasma is described by a momentum-space distribution with anisotropy parameter $\xi$, and the jet is modeled by a colorless tsunami-like distribution $f_{jet}(\mathbf{p}) = \bar{n} \bar{u}^0 \delta^{(3)}(\mathbf{p} - \Lambda \bar{\mathbf{u}})$. The total polarization tensor is taken as the sum of the plasma and jet contributions, and the dispersion relations for stable and unstable modes are obtained by finding the poles of the gluon propagator in temporal-axial gauge.

## Key numbers

- The isotropic Debye mass $m_D$ is defined by $m_D^2 = -\frac{g^2}{2\pi^2} \int_0^\infty dp \, p^2 \frac{df_{iso}(p^2)}{dp}$, where $f_{iso}$ is the isotropic distribution function.
- The jet plasma frequency is $\omega_{jet}^2 = \frac{g^2 \bar{n}}{2\Lambda}$, where $\bar{n}$ is a density parameter and $\Lambda$ sets the energy scale of jet particles.
- The parameter $\eta = \omega_{jet}^2 / \omega_t^2$ is introduced, with $\omega_t^2 = \omega_{jet}^2 + m_D^2 / 3$; numerical results are shown for $\eta = 0.02$ and $\eta = 0.2$.
- The anisotropy parameter $\xi$ ranges over $\xi = 1$ and $\xi = 10$ in the numerical results.
- Jet velocities considered include $v_{jet} = 0.5$, $0.7$, and $0.999$ (in natural units).
- The maximum wave number for the unstable mode at $\theta_{jet}=0$ is given by $k_{max}^2 = \omega_{jet}^2 + m_D^2 \frac{\sqrt{\xi} + (\xi-1)\arctan(\sqrt{\xi})}{4\sqrt{\xi/(\xi+1)}}$.
- The time scale for plasma instabilities to fully develop is $t \sim (6.7-12.5)/\omega_t$, corresponding to $1-2$ fm/c at $T \sim 350$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of jet-induced collective modes in equilibrated isotropic QGP (Refs. prd74, prd76, prd77) and of gluonic collective modes in anisotropic plasma without jets (Refs. prd68, prd70) to the combined system of an AQGP with a relativistic jet. The analysis builds on the tsunami-like jet distribution introduced in hep-ph971 and on the Vlasov transport framework from prc49 and pr183. The results enable further investigation of how jet-induced instabilities may accelerate isotropization in the early-stage anisotropic QGP produced in heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that the behavior for arbitrary angle $\theta_n$ between the wave vector and the anisotropy axis remains to be studied.
