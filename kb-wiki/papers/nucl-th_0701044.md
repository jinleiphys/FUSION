# Operation of Faddeev-Kernel in Configuration Space

> We present a practical method to solve Faddeev three-body equations at energies above three-body breakup threshold as integral equations in coordinate space. This is an extension of previously used method for bound states and scattering states below three-body breakup threshold energy. We show that breakup components in three-body reactions produce long-range effects on Faddeev integral kernels in coordinate space, and propose numerical procedures to treat these effects. Using these techniques, we solve Faddeev equations for neutron-deuteron scattering to compare with benchmark solutions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a practical method to solve Faddeev three-body integral equations in coordinate space for energies above the three-body breakup threshold, extending previous work that was limited to bound states and scattering below breakup. The key hedge is that the method requires careful numerical treatment of long-range effects in the integral kernel caused by breakup components, and the accuracy is demonstrated to be better than 1% for most benchmark comparisons, with a noted 2% discrepancy for one parameter.

## Method

The author solves the Faddeev integral equations for wave functions in coordinate space using an iterative method called the method of continued fraction (MCF). The kernel operation involves a permutation operator for particle exchange, followed by a Green's operator that is handled by a subtraction method to extract the elastic contribution and a Fourier-Bessel transformation for breakup and closed-channel components. Long-range effects from breakup channels are treated by fitting asymptotic forms of the source functions and using a least-square fit to obtain converged values for the elastic T-matrix and the Fourier-Bessel integrals.

## Key numbers

- Incident nucleon energy of E_Lab = 14.1 MeV gives K_0 = 0.416 fm^{-1}, p_c = 0.480 fm^{-1}, and p_0 = 0.550 fm^{-1}
- The oscillation length of the elastic source function omega^{(e)}(y) is 2*pi/p_c, about 13 fm
- The wave length of the slow convergence in T(y) is 2*pi/(p_0 - p_c) = 90 fm
- The fitting coefficient t_0 for T(y) converges to Re[t_0] = -6.5448 with y_fit^{max} = 1000 fm, achieving five digits of accuracy
- The range for the function xi_alpha(x,y) is reduced to {0 <= x <= 85 fm, 0 <= y <= 47.5 fm} by limiting chi_alpha(x,y) to x_M = 10 fm and y_M = 80 fm
- The Fourier-Bessel integral requires y_inf = 4 x 10^5 fm to achieve convergence for the breakup component
- Phase shift parameters for neutron-deuteron doublet state at E_Lab = 14.1 MeV: Re(delta) = 105.5 degrees, eta = 0.466
- Phase shift parameters for neutron-deuteron quartet state at E_Lab = 14.1 MeV: Re(delta) = 69.1 degrees, eta = 0.976
- Phase shift parameters for neutron-deuteron doublet state at E_Lab = 42.0 MeV: Re(delta) = 41.6 degrees, eta = 0.498
- Phase shift parameters for neutron-deuteron quartet state at E_Lab = 42.0 MeV: Re(delta) = 37.8 degrees, eta = 0.889

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space Faddeev integral equation method previously applied to three-nucleon bound states and low-energy scattering below breakup threshold (Sasakawa 1986, Ishikawa 1986) to energies above the three-body breakup threshold. The results are compared with benchmark calculations from the Utrecht, Juelich/NM, Bochum, LA/Iowa, and Hosei(Q) groups (Friar 1990, Friar 1995), which used momentum-space integral equations or differential equation approaches. The method enables accurate treatment of three-body breakup reactions in coordinate space, with potential extensions to include spin degrees of freedom, three-body forces, and the long-range Coulomb interaction.
