# Unveiling the properties of the dimuonium at the energies available at the Large Hadron Collider at CERN

> We study the production of the dimuonium (also known as true muonium) in two and three photon fusion processes in nucleus--nucleus collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. A new formalism is introduced for the production process and valuable new information is extracted which will be helpful in proposals of future experiments. We explore the phase space constraints, the reaction mechanisms, and how the dimounium decay observables might be jeopardized by other physical processes. We show that the energies available at the large hadron collider at CERN might lead to the first identification of the dimounium in a terrestrial laboratory.

## Key claim
The authors show that the dimuonium (para-dimuonium, specifically) could be produced at the LHC via two-photon fusion in ultra-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, but they hedge that the observation requires innovative detector adaptations and that existing LHC detectors may not resolve the low-energy 2P-1S transition; the claim is that the LHC "might lead to the first identification" rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method
The authors use first-order perturbation theory with a projection technique (Novikov et al., 1978) to convert tree-level Feynman diagrams for free muon pair production into bound-state production amplitudes for the spin-singlet paradimuonium (¹S₀). They compute the probability distribution as a function of impact parameter, transverse momentum, and rapidity, then integrate over impact parameters with an absorption factor (Woods-Saxon nuclear density and nucleon-nucleon cross section) to obtain cross sections. The results are compared to the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) method.

## Key numbers
- Dimuonium Bohr radius: a₀ = 512 fm (using ℏ = c = 1, m_μ = 105.66 MeV/c²)
- Wavefunction at origin for ground state: Ψ(0) = 4.8 × 10⁻⁵ fm⁻³/² (for n=1)
- Total cross section for ground-state paradimuonium production in Pb+Pb at LHC (√s_NN = 5.5 TeV/nucleon, γ = 2941): 2.32 μb
- Total cross section including all excited states (sum over 1/n³ factor of 1.202): σ_PM = 2.86 μb
- Paradimuonium lifetime: τ_PM = 0.602 ps, corresponding to decay length λ = 0.181 mm
- Orthodimuonium (³S₁) lifetime: τ_OM = 1.81 ps
- Hyperfine splitting (leading order): ΔE = 7 m_μ α⁴ / 12
- Event rate at LHC nominal luminosity (10³⁴ cm⁻² s⁻¹): 28.6 × 10⁻³ per second per high-luminosity experiment; 0.906 × 10⁻⁹ events per beam crossing
- Light-by-light scattering background cross section: σ_D ∼ 30 nb (two orders of magnitude smaller than PM production)
- Bremsstrahlung background: smaller than PM cross section by factor 10⁻⁴

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of dimuonium production in e⁺e⁻ and heavy ion collisions (e.g., Bander and Soni (1962), Moffat (1975), Ginzburg et al. (1998), Berestetsky and Lifshitz (2009), d'Enterria and da Silveira (2020)) by introducing a projection technique previously applied to positronium production (Bertulani and Navarra (2002)). It contrasts with the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) method (Bertulani and Baur (1988)), showing EPA overestimates cross sections by up to 15% at small transverse momenta. The results enable experimental searches at LHCb, ATLAS, and CMS, and could inform proposals for dedicated detectors (e.g., LHCf modifications) to detect the dimuonium via its two-photon decay, similar to π⁰ reconstruction techniques used by the LHCf collaboration.
