# Octupole deformation in quasiparticle states of odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei

> As a follow up of [Phys. Scr. 99 055305 (2024)], where we studied axial octupole shapes in two-quasiparticle states of even-even nuclei, we investigate this type of shapes in odd-mass and odd-odd well-deformed nuclei, using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-BCS approach with selfconsistent blocking and a constraint on the expectation value $Q_{30}$ of the axial octupole moment operator. To interprete the pattern of the resulting deformation energy curve as a function of $Q_{30}$, we extend the perturbative mechanism of Ref. [1]. We deduce selection rules which can predict, from the single-particle spectra at $Q_{30} = 0$, whether in a given multiquasiparticle state the deformation energy curve has a local minimum at a vanishing or a finite value of $Q_{30}$. The predictions of this perturbative mechanism are compared with actual Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations with a constraint on the expectation value $Q_{30}$. Overall we obtain a qualitative agreement and we show that quantitative predictions are limited by the role of pairing correlations and strong octupole coupling between quasi-degenerate members of a single-particle parity doublet.

# Octupole deformation in quasiparticle states of odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei

## Key claim

The perturbative mechanism developed previously for even-even nuclei (Kontowicz et al., Phys. Scr. 99 055305, 2024) can be extended to odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei to predict, from single-particle spectra at zero octupole deformation, whether a given multiquasiparticle configuration will exhibit a local minimum at finite or vanishing axial octupole deformation; however, quantitative predictions are limited by pairing correlations and strong octupole coupling between quasi-degenerate members of a single-particle parity doublet.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations with selfconsistent blocking and a constraint on the expectation value of the axial octupole moment operator Q30, using the SIII parametrization of the Skyrme energy-density functional and constant pairing matrix elements. They extended the perturbative mechanism of Bonneau (2024) to odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei by introducing an even-even reference nucleus and using Koopmans approximation to express the energy of a multiquasiparticle configuration as a sum of single-particle energies. The perturbing potential was approximated by a Woods-Saxon potential expanded to first order in beta3, and selection rules for the Q30 operator between asymptotic Nilsson configurations were derived to predict the sign of the excitation-energy correction.

## Key numbers

- In 144Ba, the neutron 5/2-[523] orbital has a single-particle energy correction of -66 keV, while the 3/2+[651] orbital has +126 keV; the proton 5/2-[532] orbital has -40 keV and the 3/2+[411] orbital has +260 keV.
- In 228Ra, the neutron 7/2-[743] orbital has a correction of -71 keV, the 5/2-[752] orbital has +51 keV, the 3/2+[631] orbital has +120 keV, and the 5/2+[633] orbital has -108 keV; the proton 1/2-[530] orbital has +524 keV and the 1/2+[640] orbital has -526 keV.
- The axial octupole moment step used in constrained calculations was dQ30 = (3/4pi) A^2 r0^3 dbeta3 with r0 = 1.2 fm and dbeta3 = 0.02.
- Basis parameters for A ~ 150 nuclei: N0 = 14, b = 0.495, q = 1.18 for 150Sm and 154Gd; for A ~ 230 nuclei: N0 = 16, b = 0.48, q = 1.20.
- Pairing strengths in A ~ 150 nuclei: G0(n) = 16.26 MeV, G0(p) = 15.27 MeV; in A ~ 230 nuclei: G0(n) = 16.0 MeV, G0(p) = 14.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative mechanism for axial octupole deformation developed by Bonneau (2024) for two-quasiparticle states in even-even nuclei to odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei in the A ~ 150 and A ~ 230 mass regions, where octupole correlations are known to occur near neutron numbers N = 88, 138 and proton numbers Z = 56, 88 (Butler, 1996). The study enables systematic predictions of octupole shapes in multiquasiparticle configurations based solely on single-particle spectra at zero deformation, and identifies the limitations of the perturbative approach due to pairing and parity-doublet coupling.
