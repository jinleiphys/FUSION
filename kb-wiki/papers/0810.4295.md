# The thermal evolution of nuclear matter at zero temperature and definite baryon number density in chiral perturbation theory

> The thermal properties of cold dense nuclear matter are investigated with chiral perturbation theory. The evolution curves for the baryon number density, baryon number susceptibility, pressure and the equation of state are obtained. The chiral condensate is calculated and our result shows that when the baryon chemical potential goes beyond $1150 \mathrm{MeV}$, the absolute value of the quark condensate decreases rapidly, which indicates a tendency of chiral restoration.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory (χPT) at zero temperature, the authors find that the baryon number density, pressure, and susceptibility remain zero until the baryon chemical potential reaches the nucleon mass (938 MeV), and that the quark condensate begins to decrease rapidly when the chemical potential exceeds 1150 MeV, indicating a tendency toward chiral restoration. The abstract glosses over the fact that the results are only applicable for chemical potentials below 1150 MeV, where spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking still holds, and that the calculation neglects binding energy contributions and pion-loop effects.

## Method

The authors introduce the baryon chemical potential as an external field coupled to the U(1)B current in the QCD Lagrangian, then map this to an effective chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian for pions and nucleons at order O(p4). They compute the baryon number density from the derivative of the generating functional with respect to the chemical potential, using the nucleon propagator modified by the chemical potential shift p0 → p0 + μb, and evaluate the resulting loop integrals via contour integration in the complex p0 plane. The quark condensate is obtained similarly from the derivative with respect to the quark mass, and the difference from its zero-chemical-potential value is extracted.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass in the chiral limit is taken as m = 896 MeV, with the physical nucleon pole at 938 MeV (the "a" pole).
- The baryon number density and pressure are zero for baryon chemical potential μb below 938 MeV; at μb = 938 MeV a singularity appears.
- The quark condensate remains constant at its zero-chemical-potential value for μb below the nucleon mass, then decreases rapidly for μb above 1150 MeV.
- The critical point for the hadron-to-QGP transition is estimated at μb ≈ 1150 MeV, corresponding to a quark chemical potential μq = μb/3 ≈ 365 MeV, consistent with NJL model predictions.
- The susceptibility shows a peak near μb = 1150 MeV, indicating a second-order phase transition associated with excited nucleon modes at p_b = 1152 ± i337 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory to cold dense nuclear matter at zero temperature, following the framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985, 1988) for introducing external fields. The results are consistent with the low-temperature evolution of the quark condensate studied by Martin (2006) using heavy-baryon χPT, and with the model-independent prediction of a singularity at μ = μ0 and T = 0 from Halasz et al. (1998). The calculation enables a determination of the range of applicability of χPT for dense matter (μb < 1150 MeV) and provides a benchmark for nonperturbative approaches such as Dyson-Schwinger equations, which are needed to study the region above the chiral restoration transition.
