# On the Time-Modulation of the beta^+$-Decay Rate of H-like 140Pr58+ Ion

> According to recent experimental data at GSI, the rates of the number of daughter ions, produced by the nuclear K-shell electron capture (EC) decays of the H-like ions 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+, are modulated in time with periods T_(EC) simeq 7 s and amplitudes a_(EC) simeq 0.20. We study a possible time-dependence of the nuclear positron (beta^+) decay rate of the H-like 140Pr58+. We show that the time-dependence of the beta^+ decay rate of the H-like 140Pr58+ ion as well as any H-like heavy ions cannot be observed. This result can be used as a prediction for future analysis of the time-dependence of the beta^+ decay rates of the H-like heavy ions 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ at GSI for the test of the measuring method.

## Key claim

The authors show that the time-dependence of the beta+ decay rate of the H-like 140Pr58+ ion, and of any H-like heavy ion, cannot be observed experimentally, even though the analogous electron-capture decay rate exhibits a clear time modulation with period ~7 s and amplitude ~0.20.

## Method

The authors apply time-dependent perturbation theory with massive neutrino mixing, using the same theoretical framework previously developed for the electron-capture decays of H-like 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ at GSI. They compute the beta+ decay amplitude as a coherent sum over neutrino mass eigenstates, using neutrino wave packets, and integrate over the phase space including the Fermi function for Coulomb repulsion between the positron and the daughter nucleus. The time-dependent part of the beta+ decay rate is then evaluated numerically using neutrino masses derived from the GSI electron-capture data and the KamLAND results.

## Key numbers

- The experimental beta+ decay rate of H-like 140Pr58+ is lambda_exp_beta+ = 0.00161(10) s^-1, with the EC decay rate lambda_exp_EC = 0.00219(6) s^-1 and ratio R_exp_EC/beta+ = 1.36(9).
- The theoretical prediction for the ratio R_th_EC/beta+ = 1.40(4) agrees with experiment.
- The Q-values are Q_H = (3348 +/- 6) keV for the EC decay and Q_beta+ = (3396 +/- 6) keV for the beta+ decay of H-like 140Pr58+.
- The time-averaging interval for the beta+ decay measurement is Delta T = 5 x 64 ms = 0.32 s, over which the rapid time variations of the beta+ decay rate average out.
- The neutrino mass-squared difference from the GSI data is (Delta m^2_21)_GSI = 2.22(4) x 10^-4 eV^2, compared to (Delta m^2_21)_KamLAND = 0.80(+0.06/-0.05) x 10^-4 eV^2.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Ivanov et al. (2008) that explained the time-modulated electron-capture decays of H-like 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ observed at the GSI storage ring, where periods T_EC ~ 7 s and amplitudes a_EC ~ 0.20 were measured. The authors apply the same massive-neutrino mixing formalism to predict that the beta+ decay channel, unlike the EC channel, will show no observable time modulation due to the rapid variation of the time-dependent term relative to the experimental time resolution. This prediction can serve as a test of the single-ion Schottky mass-measurement method at GSI for future experiments on both 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+.
