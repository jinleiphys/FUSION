# Two-color QCD in a strong magnetic field: The role of the Polyakov loop

> We study two-color QCD in an external magnetic backround at finite temperature using the Polyakov-loop extended two-flavor two-color NJL model. At T=0, the chiral condensate is calculated and it is found to increase as a function of the magnetic field $B$. In the chiral limit the deconfinement transition lies below the chiral transition for nonzero magnetic fields $B$. At the physical point, the two transitions seem to coincide for field strengths up to $|qB|\approx 5m_π^2$ whereafter they split. The splitting between the two increases as a function of $B$ in both the chiral limit and at the physical point. In the range from zero magnetic field and $|qB|=20 m_π^2$, the transition temperature for the chiral transition increases by approximately 35 MeV, while the transition temperature for deconfinement is essentially constant.

# Two-color QCD in a strong magnetic field: The role of the Polyakov loop

## Key claim

Using the Polyakov-loop extended two-flavor two-color NJL model, the chiral transition temperature increases by approximately 35 MeV from zero field to |qB|=20 M_π^2, while the deconfinement transition temperature remains essentially constant; at the physical point, the two transitions coincide for field strengths up to |qB|≈5 M_π^2 and then split, with deconfinement occurring first.

## Method

The authors study two-color QCD in a constant external magnetic field at finite temperature using the Polyakov-loop extended two-flavor two-color Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The thermodynamic potential is computed in the mean-field approximation, with the quark contribution regulated via a combination of dimensional regularization and a sharp three-momentum cutoff Λ=657 MeV. The parameters G, m_0, and Λ are fixed at T=0 by scaling three-color values by N_c factors to obtain f_π=75.4 MeV and m_π=93.3 MeV, while the Polyakov-loop potential parameters a and b are set using the pure-glue deconfinement temperature T_c=270 MeV and the condition that the chiral and deconfinement transitions coincide at B=0.

## Key numbers

- At T=0, the chiral condensate increases monotonically with magnetic field, showing magnetic catalysis; the condensate grows quadratically with |qB| for small fields in the chiral limit.
- From zero magnetic field to |qB|=20 M_π^2, the chiral transition temperature increases by approximately 35 MeV.
- The deconfinement transition temperature is essentially constant over the range |qB|=0 to |qB|=20 M_π^2.
- At the physical point, the chiral and deconfinement transitions coincide for |qB| up to approximately 5 M_π^2 (where M_π=140 MeV), then split.
- The width of the deconfinement transition band (defined by 0.4<Φ<0.6) is approximately 30 MeV.
- The ultraviolet cutoff is Λ=657 MeV, with coupling G=7.23 GeV^{-2} and bare quark mass m_0=5.4 MeV at the physical point; in the chiral limit, G=7.00 GeV^{-2} and m_0=0 MeV.
- The pure-glue deconfinement temperature is T_c=270 MeV, giving a=858.1 MeV; the parameter b=(210.5)^3 MeV^3.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-color QCD in magnetic fields, which had been investigated on the lattice in the quenched approximation by Braguta et al. (2010) and with dynamical fermions by Ilgenfritz et al. (2012). It also builds on three-color PNJL model calculations by Gatto et al. (2011) and Fukushima et al. (2010) that predicted a splitting between chiral and deconfinement transitions. The results enable comparison with lattice simulations of two-color QCD and provide a framework for studying the interplay between magnetic catalysis and the Polyakov loop in a theory where the sign problem is absent at finite baryon density.
