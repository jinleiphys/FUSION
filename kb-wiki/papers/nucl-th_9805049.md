# Large Shell Model Calculations for Calcium Isotopes: Spectral Statistics and Chaos

> We perform large shell model calculations for Calcium isotopes in the full fp shell by using the realistic Kuo-Brown interaction. The Calcium isotopes are especially interesting because the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution P(s) of low-lying energy levels shows significant deviations from the predictions of the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble of random--matrix theory. This contrasts with other neighbouring nuclei which show fully chaotic spectral distributions. We study the chaotic behaviour as a function of the excitation energy. In addition, a clear signature of chaos suppression is obtained when the single-particle spacings are increased. In our opinion the relatively weak strength of the neutron-neutron interaction is unable to destroy the regular single-particle mean-field motion completely. In the neighbouring nuclei with both protons and neutrons in valence orbits, on the other hand, the stronger proton-neutron interaction would appear to be sufficient to destroy the regular mean-field motion.

# Large Shell Model Calculations for Calcium Isotopes: Spectral Statistics and Chaos

## Key claim
Calcium isotopes show significant deviations from the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) predictions for nearest-neighbour spacing distributions, unlike neighboring nuclei that exhibit fully chaotic spectra. The authors attribute this to the relatively weak neutron-neutron interaction being unable to completely destroy the regular single-particle mean-field motion, whereas the stronger proton-neutron interaction in neighboring nuclei is sufficient to do so.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations for calcium isotopes from 44Ca to 50Ca in the full fp shell (f7/2, p3/2, f5/2, p1/2) using a minimally modified Kuo-Brown realistic interaction with monopole improvements, assuming a 40Ca inert core. They constructed and diagonalized Hamiltonian matrices using a modified version of the computer code ANTOINE, then analyzed the unfolded energy level spectra by fitting the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution P(s) to the Brody distribution, which interpolates between Poisson (regular, omega=0) and Wigner (chaotic GOE, omega=1) statistics.

## Key numbers
- Brody parameter omega for combined 46Ca+48Ca+50Ca up to 4 MeV above yrast: 0.56; up to 5 MeV: 0.60; up to 6 MeV: 0.61
- Brody parameter omega for combined 46V+46Ti+46Sc up to 4 MeV above yrast: 0.92; up to 5 MeV: 0.93; up to 6 MeV: 0.95
- Brody parameter omega for full spectra of individual Ca isotopes: 44Ca = 0.69, 45Ca = 0.75, 46Ca = 0.99, 47Ca = 0.98, 48Ca = 0.95, 49Ca = 1.00, 50Ca = 0.87
- For 48Ca, it required including levels up to about 14 MeV above yrast to obtain Brody parameter omega greater than 0.9
- For 50Ca, the number of states was 17,276, enabling separate analysis by J value
- For J=0 states in both 48Ca and 50Ca, Brody parameter omega was approximately 0.8

## Context
This work extends earlier statistical analyses of shell-model energy levels in the fp shell by the same authors (Manfredi, Gomez, Salasnich, 1997) and contrasts with prior studies in the sd shell region where chaotic behaviour was found both near the yrast line and at higher energies (referenced as b2 in the paper). The results enable further investigation of the order-to-chaos transition in nuclei as a function of excitation energy, angular momentum, and single-particle energy spacings, and suggest that the proton-neutron interaction strength is a key factor determining the degree of chaoticity in nuclear spectra.
