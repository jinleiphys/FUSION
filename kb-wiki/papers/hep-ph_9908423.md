# Origins of the baryon spectrum

> I begin with a key problem of light and strange baryon spectroscopy which suggests a clue for our understanding of underlying dynamics. Then I discuss spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in QCD, which implies that at low momenta there must be quasiparticles - constituent quarks with dynamical mass, which should be coupled to other quasiparticles - Goldstone bosons. Then it is natural to assume that in the low-energy regime the underlying dynamics in baryons is due to Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) between constituent quarks. Using as a prototype of the microscopical quark-gluon degrees of freedom the instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction I show why the GBE is so important. When the 't Hooft interaction is iterated in the qq t-channel it inevitably leads to a pole which corresponds to GBE. This is a typical antiscreening behavior: the interaction is represented by a bare vertex at large momenta, but it blows up at small momenta in the channel with GBE quantum numbers, explaining thus a distinguished role of the latter interaction in the low-energy regime. I show how the explicitly flavour-dependent short-range part of the GBE interaction between quarks, perhaps in combination with the vector-meson exchange interaction, solves a key problem of baryon spectroscopy and present spectra obtained in a simple analytical calculation as well as in exact semirelativistic three-body approach.

# Origins of the baryon spectrum

## Key claim

The ordering of low-lying positive and negative parity states in the nucleon, Δ-resonance, and Λ-hyperon spectra cannot be explained by perturbative one-gluon exchange or by the 't Hooft instanton-induced interaction between valence quarks alone; instead, the short-range part of the Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) interaction between constituent quarks, which is explicitly flavour-dependent, provides the correct level ordering and splittings when combined with a confining interaction.

## Method

The author presents a schematic analytical model with harmonic confinement and a flavour-spin hyperfine interaction of the form \( H_\chi = -\sum_{i<j} C_\chi \, \vec \lambda^{\rm F}_i \cdot \vec \lambda^{\rm F}_j \, \vec \sigma_i \cdot \vec \sigma_j \), where the constant \( C_\chi \) is extracted from the Δ–N splitting. Expectation values of this operator are evaluated analytically for the known \( SU(6) \) wave functions of all low-lying baryon states. The author also references exact semirelativistic three-body calculations from prior work that include linear confinement and, in some cases, pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchanges with spin-spin, tensor, and central force components.

## Key numbers

- The constant \( C_\chi \) extracted from the Δ–N splitting is 29.3 MeV.
- The oscillator parameter \( \hbar\omega \) characterizing the effective confining interaction is determined as approximately 250 MeV from the mass difference between the first excited \( \frac{1}{2}^+ \) states and the ground states of baryons with the same flavour-spin symmetry (e.g., N(1440) – N, Λ(1600) – Λ, Σ(1660) – Σ).
- For N, Λ, and Σ sectors, the predicted mass difference between the lowest excited \( \frac{1}{2}^+ \) states and the \( \frac{1}{2}^- – \frac{3}{2}^- \) negative parity pairs is \( m(\frac{1}{2}^+) – m(\frac{1}{2}^- – \frac{3}{2}^-) = 250\ \text{MeV} – C_\chi (14 – 2) = -102\ \text{MeV} \).
- For the Λ system, the mass difference between the lowest \( \frac{1}{2}^+ \) state Λ(1600) and the lowest negative parity pair Λ(1405) – Λ(1520) is \( m(\frac{1}{2}^+) – m(\frac{1}{2}^- – \frac{3}{2}^-) = 250\ \text{MeV} – C_\chi (14 – 8) = 74\ \text{MeV} \).
- For the Δ spectrum, the mass difference between the lowest positive parity state Δ(1600) and the lowest negative parity excitations Δ(1620) – Δ(1700) is \( m(\frac{3}{2}^+) – m(\frac{1}{2}^- – \frac{3}{2}^-) = 250\ \text{MeV} – C_\chi (4 + 4) = 15\ \text{MeV} \).
- The quark condensate value is given as \( \langle \bar q q \rangle \sim -(250\ \text{MeV})^3 \).
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is given as \( \Lambda_\chi \sim 1\ \text{GeV} \).

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach by replacing the colour-magnetic one-gluon exchange interaction, which is flavour-independent and fails to explain the opposite ordering of levels in N and Λ spectra (as shown in Fig. 2), with a Goldstone boson exchange interaction that arises from spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. The author argues that the perturbative gluon exchange picture (RGG, MIT, IK) and the direct application of the 't Hooft instanton-induced interaction between valence quarks (KOCH, SR, BLASK) both fail to reproduce the observed pattern. The GBE interaction is motivated by the instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction (HOOFT) and its iteration in the t-channel, which produces a pole corresponding to Goldstone boson exchange. The approach enables future work on higher Fock components (QQQπ, QQQK, etc.) in a coupled-channel framework, and has been implemented in semirelativistic three-body calculations (GPVW, WGPV). Similar conclusions about the GBE force being responsible for the Δ–N splitting have been obtained in a recent lattice study (LIU), and phenomenological analyses of L=1 negative parity spectra (CG) as well as \( 1/N_c \) expansion studies (CARONE) also support the interaction.
