# Excited states and precision results for nucleon charges and form factors

> The exponentially falling signal-to-noise ratio in all nucleon correlation functions, and the presence of towers of multihadron excited states with relatively small mass gaps makes extraction of matrix elements of various operators within the ground state nucleon challenging. Theoretically, the allowed positive parity states with the smallest mass gaps are the $N(\bm p)π(-\bm p)$, $N(\bm 0)π(\bm 0)π(\bm 0)$, $N(\bm p)π(\bm 0)$, $N(\bm 0)π(\bm p),\ \ldots$, states. A priori, the contribution of these states arises at one loop in chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT), however, in many cases the contributions are enhanced. In this talk, I will review four such cases: the correlation functions from which the axial form factors, electric and magnetic form factors, the $Θ$-term contribution to neutron electric dipole moment (nEDM), and the pion-nucleon sigma term are extracted. Including appropriate multihadron states in the analysis can lead to significantly different results compared to standard analyses with the mass gaps taken from fits to 2-point functions. The $χ$PT case for $N π$ states is the most clear in the axial/pseudoscalar form factors which need to satisfy the PCAC relation between them. Our analyses, supported by $χ$PT, suggests similarly large effects in the calculations of the $Θ$-term and the pion-nucleon sigma term that have significant phenomenological implications.

## Key claim

Including multihadron excited states (particularly \(N\pi\) and \(N\pi\pi\) states) in the spectral decomposition analysis of nucleon three-point correlation functions can change extracted ground-state matrix elements very significantly compared to standard analyses that use mass gaps from two-point function fits, but the current lattice data often cannot distinguish between the two analysis strategies based on \(\chi^2\) alone, requiring chiral perturbation theory guidance.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions using clover fermion ensembles with pion masses down to 135 MeV and lattice spacings from 0.04 to 0.15 fm, with up to 3000 configurations and 320 measurements each. They extracted ground-state matrix elements by fitting the spectral decomposition of correlation functions using two strategies: a "standard" analysis with excited-state energies taken from fits to the two-point function, and an "\(N\pi\)" analysis where the first excited-state energy was set to the non-interacting energy of an \(N\pi\) state as motivated by chiral perturbation theory. The analysis was applied to axial form factors, electromagnetic form factors, the \(\Theta\)-term contribution to the neutron electric dipole moment, and the pion-nucleon sigma term.

## Key numbers

- The standard analysis of axial form factors fails the PCAC relation by about 50% at \(M_\pi = 135\) MeV, while including the \(N\pi\) state significantly improves agreement.
- For the pion-nucleon sigma term, the standard analysis gives \(\sigma_{\pi N} \approx 40\) MeV, while including \(N\pi\) and \(N\pi\pi\) states gives \(\sigma_{\pi N} \approx 60\) MeV, consistent with phenomenological extractions.
- For the \(\Theta\)-term contribution to nEDM, the ground-state matrix element extracted with \(N\pi\) states included is vastly different from the standard analysis, with comparable \(\chi^2\)/dof between the two fits.
- Electromagnetic form factors show only small (approximately 5%) differences between standard and \(N\pi\) analyses, consistent with chiral perturbation theory predictions by Bar (2021).
- The tensor charge \(g_T\) for the quark EDM operator has been determined with less than 5% uncertainty and shows small excited-state contamination insensitive to \(N\pi\) inclusion.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculations of nucleon matrix elements by the authors' collaboration (Park 2021, Bhattacharya 2021, Gupta 2021) and addresses the long-standing challenge of excited-state contamination in nucleon correlation functions. The results contradict the assumption that standard two-point function fits capture the relevant excited-state spectrum, and instead support the chiral perturbation theory analysis by Bar (2018) showing that \(N\pi\) states give enhanced contributions to axial and scalar matrix elements. The findings enable future precision calculations of the pion-nucleon sigma term (resolving the tension between lattice estimates around 40 MeV and phenomenological values around 60 MeV from Hoferichter (2015) and Ruiz de Elvira (2017)), the neutron electric dipole moment from the \(\Theta\)-term, and axial form factors, provided that either increased statistics or novel methods such as variational bases with multihadron operators or analytic continuation techniques (Wagman 2016, Detmold 2021) can resolve the excited-state spectrum directly from data.
