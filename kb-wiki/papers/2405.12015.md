# Deciphering Hypertriton and Antihypertriton Spins from Their Global Polarizations in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Understanding the properties of hypernuclei is crucial for constraining the nature of hyperon-nucleon ($Y\text{-}N$) interactions, which plays a key role in determining the inner structure of compact stars. The lightest hypernuclei and antihypernuclei are the hypertriton ($^3_Λ\text{H}$), which consists of a pair of nucleons and a $Λ$ hyperon, and its antinucleus (${^3_{\barΛ}}\overline{\rm H}$). Significant knowledge has recently been acquired regarding the mass, lifetime, and binding energy of $^3_Λ\text{H}$. However, its exact spin, whether $\frac{1}{2}$ or $\frac{3}{2}$, remains undetermined in both experimental and theoretical studies. Here, we present a novel method of using the hypertriton global polarization in heavy-ion collisions to decipher not only its total spin but also its internal spin structure. This method is based on the finding that its three different spin structures exhibit distinct beam energy dependence of its global polarization when it is produced in these collisions from the coalescence of proton, neutron and $Λ$. Future observations of the hypertriton and antihypertriton global polarizations thus provide the opportunity to unveil the spin structures of hypertriton and antihypertriton and their production mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the global polarization of hypertriton and antihypertriton in noncentral heavy-ion collisions can determine their total spin (whether 1/2 or 3/2) and internal spin structure, based on the finding that three different spin structures exhibit distinct beam energy dependence of global polarization when produced via coalescence of polarized nucleons and Lambda hyperons.

## Method

The authors extended a blast-wave model to include particle spin in the one-body phase-space distribution and calculated hypertriton polarization from the coalescence of polarized protons, neutrons, and Lambda hyperons using the spin-dependent Wigner function formalism. They derived the angular distributions of decay products for the two-body decay channel hypertriton to pi- plus He-3, considering three hypothetical spin structures: spin-parity 1/2+ with the neutron-proton pair in a spin-triplet state, 1/2+ with the neutron-proton pair in a spin-singlet state, and 3/2+ with the neutron-proton pair in a spin-triplet state. Using measured Lambda and anti-Lambda polarizations from STAR, ALICE, and HADES collaborations, they predicted the beam energy dependence of hypertriton and antihypertriton polarizations from a few GeV to several TeV.

## Key numbers

- For hypertriton with spin-parity 1/2+ and neutron-proton pair in spin-triplet state, the decay parameter alpha_H is approximately -1/2.58 times alpha_Lambda, where alpha_Lambda = 0.732 plus or minus 0.014
- The magnitude of alpha_H times P_H reaches about 2 percent in collisions at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair less than 7.7 GeV
- For hypertriton with spin-parity 3/2+, the combined spin density matrix element rho_{1/2,1/2} plus rho_{-1/2,-1/2} minus 1/2 reaches about -4 times 10^{-3} for center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair minus twice the nucleon mass less than or equal to 1 GeV
- The Lambda separation energy of hypertriton is B_Lambda = 0.17 plus or minus 0.06 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of hadron spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which became active following the discovery of hyperon spin polarization by STAR (2017) and vector meson spin alignment by STAR (2022) and ALICE (2019, 2022). The method contrasts with previous attempts to determine hypertriton spin from the ratio of partial decay widths, which remained inconclusive due to large experimental errors (STAR, 2017), and with lattice QCD calculations at unphysical SU(3)-flavor symmetric point that suggested spin 3/2+ (NPLQCD, 2012). The results can be tested by upcoming measurements from HADES, STAR, and ALICE collaborations, enabling determination of hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant for understanding compact star structure.
