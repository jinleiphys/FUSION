# Angular Structure of Jet Quenching Within a Hybrid Strong/Weak Coupling Model

> Within the context of a hybrid strong/weak coupling model of jet quenching, we study the modification of the angular distribution of the energy within jets in heavy ion collisions, as partons within jet showers lose energy and get kicked as they traverse the strongly coupled plasma produced in the collision. To describe the dynamics transverse to the jet axis, we add the effects of transverse momentum broadening into our hybrid construction, introducing a parameter $K\equiv \hat q/T^3$ that governs its magnitude. We show that, because of the quenching of the energy of partons within a jet, even when $K\neq 0$ the jets that survive with some specified energy in the final state are narrower than jets with that energy in proton-proton collisions. For this reason, many standard observables are rather insensitive to $K$. We propose a new differential jet shape ratio observable in which the effects of transverse momentum broadening are apparent. We also analyze the response of the medium to the passage of the jet through it, noting that the momentum lost by the jet appears as the momentum of a wake in the medium. After freezeout this wake becomes soft particles with a broad angular distribution but with net momentum in the jet direction. We show that the particles coming from the response of the medium to the momentum and energy deposited in it leads to a correlation between the momentum of soft particles well separated from the jet in angle with the direction of the jet momentum, and find qualitative but not quantitative agreement with experimental data on observables designed to extract such a correlation. By confronting the results that we obtain upon introducing transverse momentum broadening and the response of the medium to the jet with available jet data, we highlight the importance of these processes for understanding the internal, soft, angular structure of high energy jets.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, due to the quenching of parton energy within jet showers, jets that survive with a specified final energy in heavy ion collisions are narrower than jets with that same energy in proton-proton collisions, even when transverse momentum broadening is nonzero; this makes many standard jet observables remarkably insensitive to the magnitude of broadening, but a new differential jet shape ratio observable can reveal its effects.

## Method

The authors extend their hybrid strong/weak coupling Monte Carlo model, which uses Pythia 8.183 for the perturbative DGLAP shower evolution and applies a holographically derived energy loss rate for each parton traversing the strongly coupled plasma, by adding two new physics processes: (1) transverse momentum broadening, implemented via a Gaussian distribution of transverse momentum kicks with width governed by a parameter K = q̂/T³, where q̂ is the momentum broadening parameter and T is the local plasma temperature; and (2) a simplified model for the collective response of the medium (the wake), where the energy and momentum lost by the jet are assumed to thermalize quickly into a hydrodynamic wake that after freezeout becomes soft hadrons with a broad angular distribution but net momentum along the jet direction. The hard jet events are embedded in an expanding, cooling droplet of plasma described by relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, with the energy loss rate evaluated in the local fluid rest frame.

## Key numbers

- The holographic energy loss rate for a light quark jet in strongly coupled N=4 SYM is dE/dx = -(4/π) E_in x² / (x_stop² √(x_stop² - x²)), with x_stop = (1/(2κ_sc)) E_in^(1/3) / T^(4/3) and κ_sc = 1.05 λ^(1/6).
- The transverse momentum broadening parameter is defined as q̂ = K T³, with K a free parameter; in N=4 SYM theory with λ=10, K_N=4 ≈ 24, while perturbative analyses from the JET Collaboration yield K_pert ≈ 5.
- The minimum transverse momentum for the DGLAP shower evolution is pT_min = 1 GeV.
- The freezeout temperature T_c is varied over the range 145 < T_c < 170 MeV.
- The paper reports qualitative but not quantitative agreement with CMS data on "missing-pT" distributions, finding that the simplified wake treatment produces slightly too many very soft particles (pT < 2 GeV) at large angles and not enough particles with momenta in the 2-4 GeV range.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid strong/weak coupling model introduced by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2014, 2015), which had previously been fitted to a single data point and successfully described many single jet, dijet, and photon+jet observables. The finding that energy loss causes jets with a given energy to be narrower in heavy ion collisions than in proton-proton collisions has also been observed in fully strong-coupling calculations by Chesler and Rajagopal (2015, 2016) and in weak-coupling Monte Carlo calculations by Milhano and Zapp (2015). The paper enables future experimental extraction of the transverse momentum broadening parameter K via the proposed differential jet shape ratio, which would be a step toward using jets to resolve the microscopic structure of the quark-gluon plasma.
