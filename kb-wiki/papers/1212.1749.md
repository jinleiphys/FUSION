# Connecting neutron star observations to the high density equation of state of quasi-particle model

> The observation of $1.97\pm0.04$ solar-mass neutron-like star gives constraint on the equation of state (EOS) of cold, condensed matter. In this paper, the EOS for both pure quark star and hybrid star with a quark core described by quasi-particle model are considered. The parameters of quasi-particle model which affect the mass of both quark star and hybrid star can be constrained by the observation.

## Key claim

The quasi-particle model with a bag constant can describe both pure quark stars and hybrid stars that are consistent with the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses), but only for specific ranges of the model parameters a, c, and B; the parameters are constrained by requiring that the maximal mass of the star reaches at least the observed lower bound.

## Method

The authors use a quasi-particle model for quark matter at zero temperature and finite chemical potential, where the effective quark mass and running coupling constant depend on two phenomenological parameters a and c. They compute the equation of state from the quark number density using a model-independent formula, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for both pure quark stars and hybrid stars (quark core with an APR hadronic crust). The parameters are constrained by requiring that the maximal stellar mass reaches at least 1.93 solar masses, the lower bound of the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230.

## Key numbers

- Observed mass of PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Demorest et al., 2010)
- For pure quark stars with central chemical potential mu = 0.45 GeV, the allowed range of the running coupling constant alpha_s is 0.659 to 1.135
- The range of the critical chemical potential mu_0 (where quark number density vanishes) is 0.21 GeV to 0.327 GeV, consistent with the model-independent prediction of 307 MeV from Halasz et al. (1998)
- For hybrid stars in the large B region, the maximal mass tends to 2.2 solar masses
- Example parameter sets shown: a = 12/GeV, c = -0.05 GeV; a = 13/GeV, c = -0.1 GeV; a = 12/GeV, c = -0.1 GeV; a = 16/GeV, c = -0.1 GeV
- Example bag constant values shown: B^(1/4) = 130.58 MeV, 137 MeV, 140.32 MeV, 148.37 MeV, 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle model approach previously applied to finite-temperature QCD (Peshier et al., 2000; Bluhm and Kampfer, 2008) to zero-temperature dense matter relevant for neutron stars. It uses the same model-independent formula for the equation of state developed by Zong and Sun (2008) and applied by Zhao et al. (2010). The results are compared with the MIT bag model study of hybrid stars by Weissenborn et al. (2011), which used TM1 and NL3 RMF equations of state for the hadronic crust. The paper enables future work to use neutron star observations to constrain the parameters of quasi-particle models, which are otherwise poorly determined due to the lack of experimental data at high density and zero temperature.
