# In-medium properties of kaons in a chiral approach

> The first order self-energy corrections of the kaon in the symmetric nuclear matter are calculated from kaon-nucleon scattering matrix elements using a chiral Lagrangian within the framework of relativistic mean field approximation. It shows that the effective mass and the potential of K^+ meson are identical with those of K^- meson in the nuclear matter, respectively. The effective mass of the kaon in the nuclear matter decreases with the nuclear density increasing, and is not relevant to the kaon-nucleon Sigma term. The kaon-nucleus potential is positive and increases with the nuclear density. Moreover, the influence of the resonance $Λ(1405)$ on the $K^-$-nucleus potential due to the re-scattering term is discussed. Our results indicate the K^- meson could not be bound in the nuclei even if the contribution of $Λ(1405)$ resonance is considered.

# In-medium properties of kaons in a chiral approach

## Key claim
The effective mass and potential of K⁺ and K⁻ mesons in symmetric nuclear matter are identical, and both are positive and increase with density, meaning neither K⁺ nor K⁻ can form bound states in nuclei, even when the Λ(1405) resonance contribution is included. This result depends on the specific chiral KN interaction Lagrangian used and contradicts many earlier models that predicted attractive K⁻-nucleus potentials.

## Method
The authors calculated the first-order self-energy correction of kaons in symmetric nuclear matter from kaon-nucleon scattering matrix elements using a chiral Lagrangian (including Tomozawa-Weinberg vector, scalar, and off-shell terms) within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation. The nucleon mean fields were described by the Walecka model with σ and ω mesons, and the kaon self-energy was obtained via the Dyson equation. The influence of the Λ(1405) resonance was also evaluated through a second-order re-scattering term.

## Key numbers
- At saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, the effective kaon mass m_K* = 473 MeV, about 20 MeV less than the vacuum value.
- At ρ = ρ₀, the kaon-nucleus potential U_K = 44 MeV for Σ_KN = 450 MeV, and U_K = 6.5 MeV for Σ_KN = 280 MeV.
- At ρ = 3ρ₀, U_K = 70 MeV for Σ_KN = 450 MeV, and U_K = 8 MeV for Σ_KN = 280 MeV.
- The Λ(1405) resonance contribution to the K⁻-nucleus potential at saturation density is U_KΛ ≈ 0.57 MeV.
- The kaon decay constant used is f_K ≈ 93 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of in-medium kaon properties using chiral Lagrangians and RMF models (Waas and Weise, 1997; various authors using Walecka-type models). It directly contradicts predictions of deeply bound kaonic atoms from fits to K⁻ atomic data (Friedman et al., 1999) and the possibility of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986). The results also differ from chiral unitary approaches (Ramos and Oset, 2000; Waas et al., 1996) that predict attractive K⁻-nucleus potentials of 50-70 MeV depth. The paper suggests that if the chiral KN Lagrangian used is correct, neither kaon bound states nor kaon condensation can occur in nuclear matter.
