# Spectroscopic factors, overlaps, and isospin symmetry from an $R$-matrix point of view

> Background: Spectroscopic factors, overlaps, and isospin symmetry are often used in conjunction with single-particle wave functions for the phenomenological analysis of nuclear structure and reactions. Many differing prescriptions for connecting these quantities to physically relevant asymptotic normalization constants or widths are available in the literature, but their relationship and degree of validity are not always clear. Purpose: This paper derives relationships among the above quantities of interest using well-defined methodology and starting assumptions. Method: $R$-matrix theory is used as the primary tool to interoperate between the quantities of interest to this work. Particular attention is paid to effects arising from beyond the nuclear surface, where isospin symmetry is strongly violated. Results: Relationships among the quantities of interest are derived. Example applications of these methods to mirror levels in nucleon+${}^{12}{\rm C}$, nucleon+${}^{16}{\rm O}$, and nucleon+${}^{26}{\rm Al}$ are presented. A new approach to multi-level mirror symmetry is derived and applied to the first three $2^+$ states of ${}^{18}{\rm O}$ and ${}^{18}{\rm Ne}$. Conclusions: The relationship between the quantities of interest is clarified and certain procedures are recommended. It is found that the asymptotic normalization constant of the second $2^+$ state in ${}^{18}{\rm Ne}$ deduced from the mirror state in ${}^{18}{\rm O}$ is significantly larger than found in previous work. This finding has the effect of increasing the ${}^{17}{\rm F}(p,γ){}^{18}{\rm Ne}$ reaction rate in novae.

## Key claim

The paper derives consistent relationships among spectroscopic factors, overlaps, asymptotic normalization constants (ANCs), and widths using R-matrix theory, with particular attention to isospin symmetry violation beyond the nuclear surface. The author finds that the asymptotic normalization constant of the second 2+ state in 18Ne deduced from the mirror state in 18O is significantly larger than found in previous work, which increases the 17F(p,γ)18Ne reaction rate in novae.

## Method

The author uses R-matrix theory as the primary framework to connect single-particle wave functions, reduced-width amplitudes, and spectroscopic factors to physically relevant ANCs and widths. Numerical calculations are performed using Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters (radius parameter r_n = r_C = 1.25 fm, diffuseness a_n = 0.65 fm) for nucleon + 12C, nucleon + 16O, and nucleon + 26Al systems, with the radial Schrodinger equation solved via the Numerov method. A new multi-level mirror symmetry approach is derived and applied to the first three 2+ states of 18O and 18Ne, accounting for mirror symmetry breaking beyond the channel radii.

## Key numbers

- For p+12C with nℓ=2s: S-matrix resonance energy E_S = 420.5 keV, R-matrix resonance energy E_R = 424.0 keV, K-matrix resonance energy E_K = 424.1 keV; widths: Γ_S = 37.50 keV, Γ_R = 39.17 keV, Γ_K = 39.23 keV
- For p+14N with nℓ=2s: E_S = E_R = E_K = 259.3 keV; Γ_S = Γ_R = Γ_K = 1.173 keV
- For p+26Al with nℓ=2s: E_S = E_R = E_K = 126.8 keV; Γ_S = Γ_R = Γ_K = 4.96 × 10^(-9) keV
- The channel radius used for p+12C calculations is a = 3.51 fm (R_n + a_n)
- The outer matching radius b = 20 fm is used for all calculations
- For p+12C, the single-particle width is just under 10% of the resonance energy

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix formalism developed by Lane and Thomas (1958) and applies it to clarify relationships among spectroscopic factors, overlaps, and isospin symmetry that have been treated with differing prescriptions in the literature. The paper enables more reliable determination of astrophysical reaction rates from mirror symmetry arguments, particularly for the 17F(p,γ)18Ne reaction important in nova nucleosynthesis, and provides a new multi-level approach that improves upon previous single-level mirror symmetry treatments.
