# Calculating QCD Phase Diagram Trajectories of Nuclear Collisions using a Semi-analytical Model

> At low to moderate collision energies where the parton formation time $τ_F$ is not small compared to the nuclear crossing time, the finite nuclear thickness significantly affects the energy density $ε(t)$ and net conserved-charge densities such as the net-baryon density $n_B(t)$ produced in heavy ion collisions. As a result, at low to moderate energies the trajectory in the QCD phase diagram is also affected by the finite nuclear thickness. Here, we first discuss our semi-analytical model and its results on $ε(t)$, $n_B(t)$, $n_Q(t)$, and $n_S(t)$ in central Au+Au collisions. We then compare the $T(t)$, $μ_B(t)$, $μ_Q(t)$, and $μ_S(t)$ extracted with the ideal gas equation of state (EoS) with quantum statistics to those extracted with a lattice QCD-based EoS. We also compare the $T-μ_B$ trajectories with the RHIC chemical freezeout data. Finally, we discuss the effect of transverse flow on the trajectories.

## Key claim

The finite nuclear thickness significantly alters the time evolution of energy density and net-baryon density at low to moderate collision energies, which in turn shifts the QCD phase diagram trajectories; the effect is most pronounced when the parton formation time is not small compared to the nuclear crossing time.

## Method

The authors developed a semi-analytical model that treats produced partons as free-streaming but includes the finite longitudinal width and time of primary nucleon-nucleon collisions, integrating over the production area in the x-z0 plane. They then extracted temperature and chemical potentials from the resulting densities using both an ideal gas equation of state with quantum statistics and a lattice QCD-based equation of state with Taylor-expanded pressure up to fourth order in μ/T. Transverse expansion was incorporated by increasing the transverse overlap area over time according to a data-based parameterization of the transverse flow velocity.

## Key numbers

- Maximum energy density ε^max increases with √s_NN while maximum net-baryon density n_B^max decreases, for central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7, 19.6, and 39 GeV with τ_F = 0.3 fm/c
- For the lattice EoS, μ_B first decreases before increasing with time because the lattice EoS smoothly merges with the hadron resonance gas model at T ≲ 135 MeV
- μ_Q^max from the lattice EoS can be a factor ~2 larger than from the quantum EoS, while μ_S^max values from the two EoS are within ~20% of each other
- μ_B^max extracted with the lattice EoS is significantly larger (~10%) at low to moderate collision energies than with the quantum EoS, with essentially no difference at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- The lattice EoS has no T-μ_B solution for central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 2.0 GeV at any time in the evolution
- The transverse flow parameterization uses β_T,f = [ln(√s_NN/E_0) / (64.7 + ln(√s_NN/E_0))]^0.202, where E_0 = 2m_N is the threshold energy

## Context

This work extends the semi-analytical model of Mendenhall and Lin (2020, 2021) by comparing trajectories extracted with an ideal gas EoS to those from a lattice QCD-based EoS parameterized by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2019). The results are compared to RHIC chemical freezeout data from the STAR collaboration (2017) and to the functional renormalization group crossover curve of Fu et al. (2019). The model enables rapid exploration of QCD phase diagram trajectories without full hydrodynamic simulations, and the authors have created a web interface for calculating densities and trajectories.
