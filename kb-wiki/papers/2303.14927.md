# $α$ + core structure described with an additional interaction in the nuclear matter saturation region

> In a phenomenological approach, the $α$ + core structure is investigated in the $^{20}$Ne, $^{44}$Ti, $^{94}$Mo, $^{104}$Te, and $^{212}$Po nuclei through the local potential model using a double-folding nuclear potential with effective nucleon-nucleon interaction of M3Y + $c_{\mathrm{sat}}δ(s)$ type, where the term $c_{\mathrm{sat}}δ(s)$ acts only between the saturation regions of $α$-cluster and core. Properties such as energy levels, $α$-widths, $B(E2)$ transition rates, and half-lives are calculated, and good level of agreement with experimental data is obtained in general. It is shown the inclusion of the term $c_{\mathrm{sat}}δ(s)$ is determinant for a better description of the experimental energy levels compared to the ground state bands produced with the simple M3Y interaction. The properties calculated for $^{104}$Te reinforce the superallowed $α$-decay feature for this nucleus, as indicated in previous studies.

## Key claim
The paper claims that adding a contact term \(c_{\mathrm{sat}}\delta(s)\) to the M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, which acts only when both the alpha-cluster and core densities exceed the nuclear matter saturation density of 0.17 fm\(^{-3}\), reproduces the effect of the phenomenological (1+Gaussian) factor used in earlier Woods-Saxon based potentials. This modification allows the double-folding potential to describe ground-state bands from \(0^{+}\) to \(8^{+}\) without requiring an angular-momentum-dependent intensity parameter, though the description is rougher for higher spins.

## Method
The authors use the local potential model with a double-folding nuclear potential for the \(\alpha\) + core systems in \(^{20}\)Ne, \(^{44}\)Ti, \(^{94}\)Mo, \(^{104}\)Te, and \(^{212}\)Po. The effective nucleon-nucleon interaction is the M3Y-Reid form supplemented by a contact term \(c_{\mathrm{sat}}\delta(s)\) that is nonzero only where both the alpha and core matter densities are at or above the saturation density of 0.17 fm\(^{-3}\). The parameters \(\lambda\) (intensity) and \(c_{\mathrm{sat}}\) are fitted to reproduce the \(0^{+}\) and \(4^{+}\) levels of each ground-state band, and the radial Schrodinger equation is solved to obtain energy levels, alpha-decay widths, and half-lives.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density threshold: \(\rho_{\mathrm{sat}} \approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- M3Y-Reid parameters: \(V_1 = 7999\) MeV, \(V_2 = 2134\) MeV, \(\mu_1 = 4\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\mu_2 = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\); fixed \(\hat{J}_{00} = -276\) MeV.
- Fitted \(c_{\mathrm{sat}}\) values for individual nuclei: \(^{20}\)Ne: \(-2020\) MeV; \(^{44}\)Ti: \(-3200\) MeV; \(^{94}\)Mo: \(-5650\) MeV; \(^{212}\)Po: \(-5370\) MeV; \(^{104}\)Te: \(-4870\) MeV.
- Global fit value minimizing \(S^2\) for \(0^{+}\) to \(8^{+}\) levels of \(^{20}\)Ne, \(^{44}\)Ti, \(^{94}\)Mo, and \(^{212}\)Po: \(c_{\mathrm{sat}} = -4870\) MeV.
- Intensity parameter \(\lambda\) ranges from 0.65310 (\(^{104}\)Te) to 0.88720 (\(^{20}\)Ne).
- Coulomb radius parameter \(r_{0\,\mathrm{Coul.}} = R / A_T^{1/3}\) ranges from 1.177 fm (\(^{212}\)Po) to 1.289 fm (\(^{44}\)Ti).
- For \(^{104}\)Te: calculated \(\alpha\)-decay width \(\Gamma_{\alpha} = 1.0188 \times 10^{-13}\) MeV at \(E(0^{+}) = 5.22\) MeV; corresponding half-life \(T_{1/2,\alpha} \approx 4.478\) ns, compatible with experimental upper limit \(T_{1/2}^{\mathrm{exp}} < 18\) ns (Auranen et al., 2018).
- For \(^{212}\)Po with M3Y only (no \(c_{\mathrm{sat}}\) term): calculated \(10^{+}\) level at \(E_x = 0.273\) MeV, far below experimental energy.

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of \(\alpha\) + core structures in nuclei above double-shell closures, previously addressed by Souza and Miyake (2019, 2021) using a (1+Gaussian)\(\times\)(W.S.+W.S.\(^3\)) potential, and by other authors using double-folding potentials with DDM3Y interaction (e.g., Kobos et al., 1984; Ohkubo, 1995) that required \(L\)-dependent intensity parameters. The proposed modification to the M3Y interaction provides a physical interpretation of the short-range attractive enhancement needed to reproduce energy bands, and the results for \(^{104}\)Te reinforce the superallowed alpha-decay feature indicated by Auranen et al. (2018). The approach enables systematic description of multiple nuclei with fixed interaction parameters, though it may not be directly applicable to alpha scattering data.
