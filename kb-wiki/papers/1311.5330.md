# Color spin wave functions of heavy tetraquark states

> Using the variational method, we calculate the mass of the J^P=1^+ (ud)bar(bb) tetraquark containing two identical heavy antiquarks in a nonrelativistic potential model with color confinement and spin hyperfine interaction. In particular, we extend a previous investigation of the model by Brink and Stancu by investigating the effect of including the color anti-sextet component of the diquark configuration as well as using several more Gaussian parametrization for the L=0 part of the spatial wave function. We find that for the heavy tetraquark, the 6bar{6} component among the color singlet bases is negligible and that the previously used specific Gaussian spatial configuration is good enough in obtaining the ground state energy.

## Key claim

The color anti-sextet component of the diquark configuration, \((q_1q_2)^{6}\otimes(\bar{q}_3\bar{q}_4)^{\bar{6}}\), contributes negligibly to the mass of the \(J^P=1^+\) \(ud\bar{b}\bar{b}\) tetraquark, and the single Gaussian spatial wave function used by Brink and Stancu (1998) is sufficient for obtaining the ground state energy.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic potential model with color confinement and spin hyperfine interaction, employing the variational method with Gaussian trial spatial wave functions. They extended the work of Brink and Stancu (1998) by explicitly including the color anti-sextet component in the color-spin basis and by testing several generalized Gaussian parametrizations (schemes I–VII) for the \(L=0\) spatial wave function, including multiple Gaussian terms. The Hamiltonian matrix was diagonalized and the lowest eigenvalue minimized with respect to variational parameters to obtain the ground state mass of the \(T_{bb}^1\) tetraquark.

## Key numbers

* Mass of the \(ud\bar{b}\bar{b}\) tetraquark (\(J^P=1^+\)) in scheme 0 with a single Gaussian: 10576.6 MeV (with the color anti-sextet component included) and 10577.7 MeV (with the anti-sextet component neglected).
* Binding energy \(B_T\) for \(ud\bar{b}\bar{b}\): -101.6 MeV (with anti-sextet) and -100.5 MeV (without anti-sextet), indicating the state is bound relative to the two-meson threshold.
* Mass of the \(ud\bar{c}\bar{c}\) tetraquark in scheme 0: 4036.4 MeV (with anti-sextet) and 4043.9 MeV (without anti-sextet), with binding energies +97.9 MeV and +105.4 MeV respectively, indicating an unbound state.
* Variational parameters for \(ud\bar{b}\bar{b}\) (with anti-sextet): \(C^s_{11}=2.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\), \(C^s_{22}=18.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\), \(C^s_{33}=2.7\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\).
* Quark masses used: \(m_u=m_d=337\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_b=5259\ \mathrm{MeV}\); potential parameters: \(\kappa=102.67\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}\), \(a_0=0.0326\ (\mathrm{MeV^{-1}\ fm})^{1/2}\), \(D=913.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(r_0=0.4545\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the variational calculation of Brink and Stancu (1998) for the \(T_{QQ}^1\) tetraquark by testing two proposed improvements: including the color anti-sextet component and using more flexible spatial wave functions. The results confirm the earlier finding that the \(ud\bar{b}\bar{b}\) state is stable against strong decay, while the \(ud\bar{c}\bar{c}\) state is not, consistent with the stability predictions of Lee (2007, 2009) and earlier quark model studies by Zouzou (1986) and Silvestre-Brac and Semay (1993). The negligible role of the anti-sextet component justifies the simpler basis used in prior work and enables future studies to focus on the dominant color-triplet diquark configuration.
