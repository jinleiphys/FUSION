# Electroweak interactions in a relativistic Fermi gas

> We present a relativistic model for computing the neutrino mean free path in neutron matter. Thereby, neutron matter is described as a non-interacting Fermi gas in beta-equilibrium. We present results for the neutrino mean free path for temperatures from 0 up to 50 MeV and a broad range of neutrino energies. We show that relativistic effects cause a considerable enhancement of neutrino-scattering cross-sections in neutron matter. The influence of the $Q^2$-dependence in the electroweak form factors and the inclusion of a weak magnetic term in the hadron current is discussed. The weak-magnetic term in the hadron current is at the origin of some selective spin dependence for the nucleons which are subject to neutrino interactions.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects cause a considerable enhancement of neutrino-scattering cross-sections in neutron matter compared to non-relativistic calculations, but this result is conditional on the description of neutron matter as a non-interacting relativistic Fermi gas in beta-equilibrium, without inclusion of nucleon correlations.

## Method

The authors model neutron matter as a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) and compute neutrino mean free paths and cross sections for neutral-current (ν+n→ν+n, ν+p→ν+p) and charged-current (ν+n→p+e⁻) interactions. They retain the full electroweak hadron current with Q²-dependent form factors (Dirac, weak-magnetic, axial, and pseudoscalar) and compare results against non-relativistic calculations from Reddy (1997) and analytical limits for scattering on a single nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino mean free path computed for temperatures from 0 up to 50 MeV and a broad range of neutrino energies, at baryon density n_B = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For the dipole parametrization of vector form factors: M_V² = 710649 MeV²; for axial form factor: M_a² = 1065024 MeV², g_a = 1.262.
- Weak mixing angle sin²θ_W = 0.2312.
- At low neutrino energies (ε ≪ M_N), the analytical approximation σ(ε) = (1/4)(4G_F² m_e² ħ² / π c²)(ε/m_e)² is valid; at higher energies the deviation grows and the weak-magnetic term enhances cross sections while Q²-dependent form factors reduce them.
- In the classical (non-degenerate) limit, the relativistic dynamical form factor S(ω,|q|) contains terms proportional to ω and (kT)² that are absent in the non-relativistic expression S(ω,|q|) = (2π/|q|) kT m_b² exp((μ_b - e_-)/kT).
- For a hadron gas at low temperatures in beta equilibrium at density 0.16 fm⁻³, the chemical potential is about 60 MeV; for a neutrino energy of 150 MeV the chemical potential is 114 MeV.
- Charged-current cross sections are suppressed by several orders of magnitude compared to neutral-current at low energies (kT = 5 MeV) due to Pauli blocking of outgoing electrons.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Fermi gas calculations of Tubbs and Schramm (1975) and the analytical treatment of Schinder (1990) by incorporating relativistic kinematics and the full Q²-dependent electroweak form factors. It also goes beyond the four-point Fermi-interaction approximation used in earlier studies by including the weak magnetic term, which Vogel (1983) showed to be non-negligible. The results enable more accurate neutrino transport simulations for core-collapse supernova modeling, where the shock wave problem may be attributed to incomplete weak interaction descriptions as noted by Buras (2003). Future work is planned to implement nucleon correlations.
