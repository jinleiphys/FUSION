# Electroweak interactions in a relativistic Fermi gas

> We present a relativistic model for computing the neutrino mean free path in neutron matter. Thereby, neutron matter is described as a non-interacting Fermi gas in beta-equilibrium. We present results for the neutrino mean free path for temperatures from 0 up to 50 MeV and a broad range of neutrino energies. We show that relativistic effects cause a considerable enhancement of neutrino-scattering cross-sections in neutron matter. The influence of the $Q^2$-dependence in the electroweak form factors and the inclusion of a weak magnetic term in the hadron current is discussed. The weak-magnetic term in the hadron current is at the origin of some selective spin dependence for the nucleons which are subject to neutrino interactions.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects cause a considerable enhancement of neutrino-scattering cross-sections in neutron matter compared to non-relativistic calculations, but this result is conditional on the description of neutron matter as a non-interacting relativistic Fermi gas in beta-equilibrium, without inclusion of nucleon correlations.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Fermi gas calculations of Tubbs and Schramm (1975) and the analytical treatment of Schinder (1990) by incorporating relativistic kinematics and the full QВІ-dependent electroweak form factors. It also goes beyond the four-point Fermi-interaction approximation used in earlier studies by including the weak magnetic term, which Vogel (1983) showed to be non-negligible. The results enable more accurate neutrino transport simulations for core-collapse supernova modeling, where the shock wave problem may be attributed to incomplete weak interaction descriptions as noted by Buras (2003). Future work is planned to implement nucleon correlations.
