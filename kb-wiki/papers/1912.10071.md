# Hadron multiplicity calculation: a configurational entropy approach to the saturation scale in QCD

> This paper investigates the configurational entropic content of hadron-nucleus collisions. Hadron multiplicities and Au nuclei are employed to compute the critical points of the configurational entropy as a function of the saturation scale in deep inelastic scatterings, in QCD. The results match phenomenological data to the precision of 0.39%.

## Key claim

The configurational entropy (CE) approach predicts the saturation scale parameter λ = 0.253 for central Au-Au collisions at rapidity y=0, matching phenomenological data to 0.39% precision, but this result is conditional on using the hadron multiplicity as the localized function and fixing the rapidity to y=0, which is the only value with available experimental data for comparison.

## Method

The author computed the configurational entropy by taking the Fourier transform of the hadron multiplicity distribution (Eq. finres) as a function of the saturation scale parameter λ, for fixed rapidity values. The modal fraction was then constructed from the Fourier-transformed distribution, and the CE was calculated as the Shannon-type entropy of this modal fraction. The global minima of the CE were found numerically for y=0 and y=0.2, with the minimum at y=0 corresponding to the most stable nuclear configuration.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale parameter λ = 0.253 at y=0, matching the value λ ≈ 0.252 from Kharzeev (2001) within 0.39% precision
- For y=0.2, the CE global minimum occurs at λ = 0.271
- The CE value at the minimum is 0.92 for y=0 and 1.51 for y=0.2
- The saturation scale energy dependence parameter λ = 0.288 ± 0.03 (from Eq. QS) and λ ≈ 0.252 (from Eq. f1f)
- Λ²_QCD = 0.04 GeV²
- The CE valley for y=0 spans the range 0.2 ≲ λ ≲ 0.299; for y=0.2, the valley spans 0.23 ≲ λ ≲ 0.32
- The analysis uses Au nucleus at fixed energy E₀ = 130 GeV, for 0-6% most central collisions

## Context

This work extends the configurational entropy approach, previously applied to mesons, glueballs, and quark-gluon plasma in AdS/QCD, to the Color Glass Condensate framework for hadron-nucleus collisions. It corroborates the saturation scale predictions of Kharzeev (2001) for central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, and demonstrates that the CE global minimum selects the most stable nuclear configuration. The method enables prediction of the saturation scale for other rapidity values (e.g., y=0.2) where experimental data are not yet available, and can be extended to other nuclear configurations and field-theoretical effects.
