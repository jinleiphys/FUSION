# Influence of the Pauli principle on the dynamics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> A simple parton cascade model for the ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is proposed to investigate the Pauli blocking at the transition from hadronic to partonic state, i.e. QGP. A boost invariant study of the Pauli blocking is implemented in the Monte Carlo simulation for the first time. It turns out that the higher reaction energy the stronger the partonic Pauli effect is. It amounts to about ten percent at SPS and RHIC energies but nearly reaches twenty percent at LHC energy. Thus the effect is relevant to the formation and evolution of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The Pauli blocking effect on partons in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is below ten percent at SPS and RHIC energies but reaches nearly twenty percent at LHC energy, contrary to the expectation that the effect would be negligible due to the large momentum phase space available.

## Method
The authors developed a simple parton cascade model based on the JPCIAE multiple mini-jet generator (itself based on PYTHIA) with string fragmentation switched off, so that produced particles are quark pairs, diquark pairs, and gluons. Diquarks are split into quarks and gluons into quark pairs randomly, and a boost-invariant Pauli blocking check is applied for the first time in Monte Carlo simulation by evaluating occupation numbers in six-dimensional phase-space cells of size (1 fm × 1.24 GeV/c)³. The partonic system is then hadronized by JETSET after nucleon-nucleon collisions cease, and results with and without Pauli blocking are compared.

## Key numbers
- Pauli blocking reduces particle yields by about 1.7% for pions in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_nn = 17.3 GeV (SPS energy), e.g., π⁺ yield drops from 427.8 to 416.5.
- At RHIC energy (Au+Au at √s_nn = 200 GeV), the reduction is about 6.7% for pions: π⁺ yield drops from 1352 to 1261.
- At LHC energy (Pb+Pb at √s_nn = 5500 GeV), the reduction reaches about 17.2% for pions: π⁺ yield drops from 7291 to 6034.
- For strange particles at LHC energy, the total strangeness multiplicity decreases from 2863 to 2383, a reduction of 16.8%.
- The charge multiplicity in Au+Au collisions at √s_nn = 200 GeV with Pauli blocking is 3063, compared to the experimental data of 4630±370.

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum statistical effects in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, following the formal treatment of partonic transport equations by Geiger (1995) and the pioneering parton cascade model by Boal (1986). It is the first Monte Carlo simulation to implement Pauli blocking in a boost-invariant manner, in contrast to earlier boost-uninvariant treatments at low and intermediate energies by Cugnon (1981), Bertsch (1984), and others. The results suggest that more refined models of QGP dynamics must include Pauli blocking, and that experimental information on phase-space density might be obtained from baryon-baryon coincidence measurements, especially neutrals.
