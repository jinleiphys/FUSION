# Sigma and omega meson propagation in a dense nuclear medium

> The propagation of the scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) mesons in nuclear matter is studied in detail using the Walecka model over a wide range of densities and including the effects of a finite $σ$ width through the inclusion of a two-pion loop. We calculate the dispersion relation and spectral functions of the $σ$ and (transverse and longitudinal) $ω$ mesons, including the effect of $σ$-$ω$ mixing in matter. It is shown that the mixing effect is quite important in the propagation of the (longitudinal) $ω$ and $σ$ mesons above normal nuclear matter density. We find that there is a two-peak structure in the spectral function of the $σ$ channel, caused by $σ$-$ω$ mixing.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that σ-ω mixing in dense nuclear matter causes a level-level repulsion between the longitudinal ω and σ modes, producing a two-peak structure in the σ spectral function at finite three-momentum transfer, but only at densities above approximately normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀).

## Method

The authors used the Walecka model (QHD-I) in the relativistic Hartree approximation for the nuclear ground state, then computed meson propagators by summing ring diagrams in the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA). They included a finite σ width through a two-pion loop contribution, added a free-space ω width of 9.8 MeV, and constructed a 5×5 matrix propagator to handle σ-ω mixing. The dispersion relations and spectral functions were obtained by finding zeros of the dielectric functions for transverse, longitudinal, and scalar modes.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, with binding energy per nucleon of −15.7 MeV at saturation
- Effective nucleon mass M*/M = 0.73 at ρ₀
- Incompressibility K = 452 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_s² = 101.50, g_v² = 72.296, g_{σπ} = 18.33
- Free hadron masses: M = 939 MeV, m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV
- Free σ width Γ_σ⁰ = 300 MeV
- Free ω width Γ_ω⁰ = 9.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Williams (1996) on the ω meson invariant mass in nuclear matter by including free-space widths for both σ and ω and a detailed treatment of σ-ω mixing as a function of density. It improves upon Wolf et al. (1990), who calculated only the lowest-order mixing diagram without nucleon-loop contributions to the meson propagators. The results enable future experimental searches for medium-modified meson spectral functions in dilepton production at TJNAF and GSI, and provide a baseline for comparison with quark-meson coupling model calculations that include hadron internal structure.
