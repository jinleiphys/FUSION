# The quark-gluon plasma at RHIC

> I present a theory-guided review of RHIC data, arguing that they provide strong evidence for formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma at RHIC. Strong radial flow reflects high thermal pressure in the reaction zone. Large elliptic flow proves that the pressure builds up quickly and the system thermalizes on a very short time scale of less than 1 fm/c. The observed hadron yields are consistent with statistical hadron formation from a quark-gluon plasma, followed by immediate chemical decoupling due to strong radial expansion. The observed suppression of jets appears to confirm the predicted large energy loss suffered by hard partons moving through a quark-gluon plasma; more work is required to quantitatively understand this effect. Source size measurements using two-particle correlations do not seem to fit into this picture; the origin of this discrepancy (``HBT puzzle'') is presently not understood.

# The quark-gluon plasma at RHIC

## Key claim

The paper argues that RHIC data provide strong evidence for the formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma (QGP), but the claim is hedged by noting that the HBT puzzle (discrepancy between measured source sizes and hydrodynamic predictions) is "presently not understood" and that quantitative understanding of jet suppression requires more work.

## Method

The author presents a theory-guided review of RHIC hadron production data, comparing measured transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow coefficients, particle ratios, and high-pT suppression patterns to hydrodynamic and statistical model predictions. The analysis uses the Bjorken formula to relate measured transverse energy to initial energy density, and constrains the thermalization time scale by comparing measured elliptic flow (v2) to hydrodynamic calculations that assume different sound velocities and thermalization times.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time scale of less than 1 fm/c is required to reproduce the measured elliptic flow, with successful hydrodynamic predictions using tau_therm = 0.6 fm/c
- Initial energy density in central collisions at tau_therm ≈ 0.6 fm/c is about 25 GeV/fm^3, more than an order of magnitude above the critical value for deconfinement (about 1 GeV/fm^3)
- Critical temperature for color deconfinement T_c ≈ 170 MeV from lattice QCD
- Chemical freeze-out temperature extracted from particle ratios is consistent with T_c ≈ 170 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T_f = 120-130 MeV with average transverse flow velocity <beta_perp> = 0.4-0.5 from SPS data; RHIC gives similar values with either T_f or <beta_perp> about 5-10% higher
- For T ≥ 2 T_c, lattice QCD shows about 80-85% of the ideal gas sound speed squared (c_s^2 = 1/3) is reached
- High-pT particle production in central Au+Au collisions scales with N_part (number of wounded nucleons) rather than with number of binary collisions, indicating suppression by a factor N_part^(1/3)

## Context

This review extends the line of evidence for QGP formation from SPS experiments (Heinz 2000) to RHIC, where larger elliptic flow and jet quenching provide stronger signals. The paper contrasts hydrodynamic predictions (Kolb 2000, Huovinen 2001) with microscopic models (Molnar 2001, Lin 2001) that require unusually strong rescattering to approach the hydrodynamic limit. The HBT puzzle remains an open problem that challenges current dynamical models. The work enables future "jet tomography" studies (Gyulassy 2001) and motivates high-luminosity measurements of thermal dileptons, direct photons, and heavy quarkonium production.
