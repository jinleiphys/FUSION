# Single- & double-strangeness hypernuclei up to $ A=8 $ within chiral effective field theory

> We investigate $S=-1$ and $-2$ hypernuclei with $A=4-8$ employing the Jacobi-NCSM approach and in combination with baryon-baryon interactions derived within the frame work of chiral effective field theory. The employed interactions are transformed using the similarity renormalization group (SRG) so that the low- and high-momentum states are decoupled, and, thereby,convergence of the binding energies with respect to model space can be significantly speeded up. Such an evolution is however only approximately unitary when the so-called SRG induced higher-body forces are omitted. We first explore the impact of the SRG evolution on the $Λ$ separation energies $B_Λ$ in $A=3-5$ hypernuclei when only SRG-evolved two-body and when both two- and three-body forces are included. For the latter scenario, we thoroughly study predictions of the two almost phase-equivalent NLO13 and NLO19 YN potentials for $A=4-7$ hypernuclei. The NLO19 interaction yields separation energies that are comparable with experiment, whereas NLO13 underestimates all the systems considered. We further explore CSB splittings in the $A=7,8$ multiplets employing the two NLO YN potentials that include also the leading CSB potential in the $Λ$N channel, whose strength has been fitted to the presently established CSB in $A=4$. Finally, we report on our recent study for $Ξ$ hypernuclei based on the $Ξ$N interaction at NLO.

## Key claim

The NLO19 chiral YN potential yields Λ separation energies for A=4-7 hypernuclei that are comparable with experiment (only slightly above), whereas the almost phase-equivalent NLO13 potential substantially underestimates all the systems considered, highlighting the importance of chiral YNN forces for properly describing light hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors employed the Jacobi no-core shell model (NCSM) with baryon-baryon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory, using similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution to decouple low- and high-momentum states and accelerate convergence. They solved flow equations for two-body NN and YN interactions and three-body YNN interactions in momentum space, then computed binding and separation energies for hypernuclei with A=4-8 including both SRG-induced and chiral three-body forces. For charge symmetry breaking (CSB) studies, they extracted CSB results perturbatively using hypernuclear and nuclear wave functions computed at the largest accessible model space.

## Key numbers

- Λ separation energy for ^4_ΛH(0^+): NLO13 gives 1.551 ± 0.007 MeV, NLO19 gives 1.514 ± 0.007 MeV, experiment is 2.16 ± 0.08 MeV (Juric 1979)
- Λ separation energy for ^4_ΛH(1^+): NLO13 gives 0.823 ± 0.003 MeV, NLO19 gives 1.27 ± 0.009 MeV, experiment is 1.07 ± 0.08 MeV (Juric 1979)
- Λ separation energy for ^5_ΛHe: NLO13 gives 2.22 ± 0.06 MeV, NLO19 gives 3.32 ± 0.03 MeV, experiment is 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV (Juric 1979)
- Λ separation energy for ^7_ΛLi: NLO13 gives 5.28 ± 0.68 MeV, NLO19 gives 6.04 ± 0.30 MeV, experiment is 5.58 ± 0.03 MeV (Juric 1979) or 5.85 ± 0.13 MeV (Agnello 2012)
- CSB splitting for ^7_ΛBe-^7_ΛLi* with NLO13-CSB: -40 keV; with NLO19-CSB: -35 keV; experiment: -100 ± 90 keV (Botta 2019)
- CSB splitting for ^8_ΛBe-^8_ΛLi with NLO13-CSB: 178 ± 50 keV; with NLO19-CSB: 143 ± 50 keV; experiment: 40 ± 60 keV (Botta 2016)
- Ξ separation energy for ^4_ΞH(1^+,0): 0.48 ± 0.01 MeV with decay width 0.74 MeV
- Ξ separation energy for ^5_ΞH: 2.16 ± 0.10 MeV with decay width 0.19 MeV
- Ξ separation energy for ^7_ΞH: 3.50 ± 0.39 MeV with decay width 0.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Le (2020) that qualitatively explored NLO13 and NLO19 effects on light hypernuclei with A=4-7, now including chiral and SRG-induced three-body forces for quantitative comparison with experiment. The CSB analysis builds on the leading CSB potential derived by Haidenbauer (2020) fitted to A=4 mirror hypernuclei, and the results for A=7,8 multiplets are compared with cluster-model calculations by Hiyama (2009) and shell-model estimates by Gal (2015). The Ξ hypernuclei study extends the work of Le (2021) and provides predictions that can be tested against future experiments, with results comparable to HAL QCD potential calculations by Hiyama (2019) but differing from the ESC08c Nijmegen potential.
