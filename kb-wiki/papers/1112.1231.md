# Fragmentation of electric dipole strength in N=82 isotones

> Fragmentation of the dipole strength in the N=82 isotones 140Ce, 142Nd and 144Sm is calculated using the second random-phase approximation (SRPA). In comparison with the result of the random-phase approximation (RPA), the SRPA provides the additional damping of the giant dipole resonance and the redistribution of the low-energy dipole strength. Properties of the low-energy dipole states are significantly changed by the coupling to two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) states, which are also sensitive to the correlation among the 2p2h states. Comparison with available experimental data shows a reasonable agreement for the low-energy E1 strength distribution.

## Key claim

The second random-phase approximation (SRPA) successfully produces both the spreading of the giant dipole resonance and the concentration of dipole strength in the low-energy region for N=82 isotones, but the transition strength of the first dipole state is overestimated by a factor of 2.7–3.5, indicating the need for a more elaborate treatment of states with two-phonon character.

## Method

The authors performed SRPA calculations for the N=82 isotones 140Ce, 142Nd, and 144Sm using the Skyrme SIII interaction, incorporating two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) configurations explicitly while respecting the Pauli principle. The single-particle space included states with angular momenta j ≤ 15/2 up to 30 MeV, yielding about 800 1p1h and 9000 2p2h amplitudes, with the residual interaction strength renormalized by a factor f=0.73 for 142Nd to approximately reproduce the spurious center-of-mass mode at zero energy.

## Key numbers

- The energy-weighted dipole strength summed up to 50 MeV exhausts 87% of the energy-weighted sum-rule value including the enhancement term from momentum-dependent parts of the Skyrme interaction.
- For 140Ce, the mean energy of low-energy dipole states shifts from 7.53 MeV (RPA) to 6.47 MeV (SRPA), compared to the experimental value of 6.28 MeV; the summed B(E1)↑ increases from 0.021 e²fm² (RPA) to 0.219 e²fm² (SRPA), versus 0.308 e²fm² experimentally.
- For 142Nd, the SRPA mean energy is 6.31 MeV (experiment: 6.07 MeV) with summed B(E1)↑ of 0.224 e²fm² (experiment: 0.184 e²fm²).
- For 144Sm, the SRPA mean energy is 6.04 MeV (experiment: 5.69 MeV) with summed B(E1)↑ of 0.233 e²fm² (experiment: 0.208 e²fm²).
- The lowest 1⁻ state in 140Ce has SRPA excitation energy 3.54 MeV (experiment: 3.644 MeV) but B(E1)↑ of 0.076 e²fm² versus 0.0217 e²fm² experimentally; similar overestimates occur for 142Nd (0.074 vs 0.0211 e²fm²) and 144Sm (0.068 vs 0.0248 e²fm²).
- The residual interaction renormalization factor f=0.73 was obtained for 142Nd, and the zero-range interaction parameter v₀=-570 MeV fm³ was fixed by reproducing the lowest 1⁻ state in 142Nd.

## Context

This work extends earlier random-phase approximation (RPA) and quasiparticle RPA calculations by Yoshida (2009) that failed to produce low-energy dipole strengths at Ex=5.5–8 MeV in 142Nd and 144Sm, and provides a complementary approach to the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) used by Enders (2003) and Savran (2006) which successfully reproduced low-energy strengths but violates the Pauli principle through multi-phonon assumptions. The results enable future studies using extended RPA formalisms with ground-state correlations, as proposed by Tohyama and Schuck (2007), and dressed-four-quasi-particle approaches by Kanesaki (1979), to better describe two-phonon character states.
