# K_{l3} transition form factors

> The rainbow truncation of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is combined with the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the meson bound state amplitudes and the dressed quark-W vertex in a manifestly covariant calculation of the K_{l3} transition form factors and decay width in impulse approximation. With model gluon parameters previously fixed by the chiral condensate, the pion mass and decay constant, and the kaon mass, our results for the K_{l3} form factors and the kaon semileptonic decay width are in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, with model parameters fixed only by the chiral condensate, pion mass and decay constant, and kaon mass, yields K_{l3} form factors and semileptonic decay widths in good agreement with experimental data without any readjustment of parameters; however, the scalar form factor f_0(t) should not be trusted quantitatively in the timelike region beyond the Callan-Treiman point because the ladder approximation is inadequate for scalar channels.

## Method

The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation was solved in rainbow truncation, then used to solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar bound states and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-W vertex, both in ladder truncation. The K_{l3} transition form factors f_+(t) and f_-(t) were calculated in impulse approximation using these dressed propagators and amplitudes, with the effective quark-antiquark interaction containing an infrared Gaussian term plus the perturbative QCD running coupling at large momenta.

## Key numbers

- f_+(0) is of order 1 (normalized to 1 at t=0)
- f_0(m_K^2 - m_pi^2) = 1.18 at the Callan-Treiman point, compared to the current algebra prediction f_K/f_pi = 1.22
- f_K = 109 MeV from the model, compared to the experimental value 113 MeV
- Monopole mass for f_+(t) fit: m^2 = 0.87 GeV^2
- Vector bound state (K*) mass in the model: m^2 = 0.876 GeV^2 (experimental m_K*^2 = 0.796 GeV^2)
- Scalar u-bar-s bound state mass in the model: m^2 = 0.80 GeV^2
- Physical region for t: m_l^2 < t < (m_K - m_pi)^2 approximately 0.13 GeV^2
- Model parameters: omega = 0.4 GeV, D = 0.93 GeV^2, Lambda_QCD = 0.234 GeV, renormalization scale mu = 19 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach previously applied to pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors by Maris and Tandy (2000) to the semileptonic K_{l3} decay, using the same model parameters without readjustment. It improves upon earlier DSE calculations by Kalinovsky et al. (1997) by dressing the quark-W vertex through solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation rather than using phenomenological parametrizations, and by using actual DSE solutions for propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The results are similar to those from light-front calculations by Ji and Choi (2000) for f_+(t), but the manifestly covariant approach automatically includes nonvalence contributions that complicate light-front calculations. The work enables future incorporation of meson loop corrections to generate resonance widths rather than poles in the timelike region.
