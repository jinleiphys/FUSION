# Singular Character of Critical Points in Nuclei

> The concept of critical points in nuclear phase transitional regions is discussed from the standpoints of Q-invariants, simple observables and wave function entropy. It is shown that these critical points very closely coincide with the turning points of the discussed quantities, establishing the singular character of these points in nuclear phase transition regions between vibrational and rotational nuclei, with a finite number of particles.

## Key claim
The paper shows that critical points in nuclear phase transitions between vibrational and rotational nuclei (U(5)↔SU(3) and U(5)↔O(6)) coincide very closely with the turning points (extrema of first derivatives) of Q-invariants, simple observables, and wave function entropy, establishing the singular character of these points for finite boson numbers.

## Method
The authors used the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model in the extended consistent Q formalism (ECQF) to span the transition regions between U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetries. They calculated Q-invariants (q₂, K₄, σ_γ), simple observables (R₄/₂ and B(E2:2⁺₁→0⁺₁)), and wave function entropy for N=10 bosons as functions of the control parameter ζ, and compared the turning points of these quantities to the critical points ζ_c derived from the coherent state formalism of the IBA.

## Key numbers
- For the U(5)↔SU(3) transition with N=10 bosons: turning points ζ_t = 0.54 for q₂, 0.53 for K₄, 0.52 for σ_γ, and 0.54 for both R₄/₂ and B(E2); the coherent state critical point ζ_c = 0.54.
- For the U(5)↔O(6) transition with N=10 bosons: turning points ζ_t = 0.60 for q₂ and 0.56 for K₄; the coherent state critical point ζ_c = 0.56.
- For the U(5)↔SU(3) transition, the maximum first derivative (dq₂/dζ)_max ≈ 800; for U(5)↔O(6), it is ≈ 200.
- For the wave function entropy ratio r^B, turning points ζ_t = 0.52 (U(5)↔SU(3)) and ζ_t = 0.59 (U(5)↔O(6)), compared to ζ_c = 0.54 and 0.56 from the coherent state formalism.
- The critical point ζ_c for the U(5)↔SU(3) transition in the large N limit approaches 0.5.

## Context
This work extends the concept of critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) proposed by Iachello (2000, 2001) for infinite nucleon numbers to finite boson numbers using the IBA model. It also builds on the identification of O(6) as a critical point symmetry by Jolie and collaborators (2001). The paper connects three themes: phase transitional regions and critical point nuclei, the behavior of Q-invariants (previously studied by Joliet et al. 1997 and Palchikov et al. 1998), and the study of chaos and entropy in nuclear systems (Alhassid et al. 1991, Cejnar and Jolie 1998). The results enable the identification of critical points in real nuclei using accessible observables and allow investigation of finite valence particle number effects on phase transition singularities.
