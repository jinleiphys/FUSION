# Spin polarised nuclear matter and its application to neutron stars

> An equation of state(EOS) of nuclear matter with explicit inclusion of a spin-isospin dependent force is constructed from a finite range, momentum and density dependent effective interaction. This EOS is found to be in good agreement with those obtained from more sophisticated models for unpolarised nuclear matter. Introducing spin degrees of freedom, it is found that at density about 2.5 times the density of normal nuclear matter the neutron matter undergoes a ferromagnetic transition. The maximum mass and the radius of the neutron star agree favourably with the observations. Since finding quark matter rather than spin polarised nuclear matter at the core of neutron stars is more probable, the proposed EOS is also applied to the study of hybrid stars. It is found using the bag model picture that one can in principle describe both the mass and size as well as the surface magnetic field of hybrid stars satisfactorily.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a finite-range, momentum- and density-dependent effective interaction can produce a ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter at a density of about 2.5 times normal nuclear matter density, but the authors hedge that quark matter rather than spin-polarised nuclear matter is more probable at neutron star cores, so they also apply their equation of state to hybrid stars.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state for nuclear matter using a modified Seyler-Blanchard effective interaction that explicitly includes spin-isospin dependent channels, with parameters fitted to reproduce the saturation density, volume energy, symmetry energy, spin symmetry energies, surface energy coefficient, and breathing-mode energies of normal nuclear matter. They then calculated the energy per particle for spin-polarised and unpolarised neutron matter at zero temperature, determined the magnetic susceptibility to identify the ferromagnetic transition density, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star masses, radii, and moments of inertia. For hybrid stars, they combined this nuclear equation of state with the MIT bag model for three-flavour quark matter (u, d, s quarks) under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of normal nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K = 240 MeV
- Volume energy of symmetric nuclear matter: E_V = −16.1 MeV
- Symmetry (isospin) energy: E_X = 34.0 MeV
- Spin symmetry energy: E_Y = 31.5 MeV
- Spin-isospin symmetry energy: E_Z = 35.0 MeV
- Ferromagnetic transition occurs at ρ ∼ 2.5 ρ₀ (approximately 2.4 ρ₀ from susceptibility calculation)
- For the interaction with incompressibility K = 304 MeV, transition density decreases to ∼ 2.3 ρ₀
- Maximum beta-equilibrium proton fraction for unpolarised matter with electrons only: ∼ 0.085 at ρ ≃ 3 ρ₀
- Maximum beta-equilibrium proton fraction for unpolarised matter with electrons and muons: ∼ 0.11
- Measured mass of 4U 0900-40: (1.85 ± 0.3) M⊙
- For B¹/⁴ = 155 MeV: unpolarised NM favoured up to ρ_b ≃ 0.255 fm⁻³, then quark matter is lowest energy state
- For B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV: polarised NM exists between ρ_FM ≃ 0.405 fm⁻³ and ρ_HQ ≃ 1.05 fm⁻³
- Quark masses used: m_u = 5 MeV, m_d = 10 MeV, m_s = 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic studies of nuclear matter equations of state by Friedmann and Pandharipande (1981) using the v₁₄+TNI interaction and by Bethe and Johnson using a correlated basis function approach, with which the present interaction shows good agreement for symmetric nuclear matter. It contrasts with the Hartree-Fock calculations of Pfarr (1976) and the constrained variational method of Forseth and Ostgaard (1986), which found no ferromagnetic transition at realistic densities, and with the relativistic σ+ω model of Niembro et al. (1991) that predicted no transition, while the improved model of Marcos et al. (1991) including π and ρ mesons found a transition at ρ ∼ 3.5 ρ₀ but with too high incompressibility (∼ 450 MeV). The paper enables future studies of whether hybrid stars with spin-polarised nuclear matter layers can simultaneously explain neutron star masses, radii, and surface magnetic fields, though it concludes that large regions of spin-polarised matter are ruled out by the observational mass limit.
