# Statistical analysis of pQCD energy loss across system size, flavor, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$, and $p_T$

> We present suppression predictions from our pQCD-based energy loss model, which receives small system size corrections, for high-$p_T$ $π$, $D$ and $B$ meson $R_{AB}$ as a function of centrality, flavor, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$, and $p_T$ from large to small collision systems at RHIC and LHC. A statistical analysis is used to constrain the effective strong coupling in our model to available high-$p_T$ suppression data from central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, yielding good agreement with all available data. We estimate two important theoretical uncertainties in our model, stemming from: the transition between vacuum and hard thermal loop propagators in the collisional energy loss, and from the angular cutoff on the radiated gluon momentum. We find, consistently, that the extracted $α_s$ remains relatively unchanged across heavy- and light-flavor final states and across central, semi-central, and peripheral collisions. We make predictions from our large-system-constrained model for small systems and find good agreement with photon-normalized $R^{π^0}_{d \text{Au}} \simeq 0.75 $ in $0-5\%$ centrality $d$ + Au collisions by PHENIX. However, we find strong disagreement with the measured $R^{h^{\pm}}_{p \text{Pb}} \gtrsim 1$ in $0-5\%$ centrality $p$ + Pb collisions by ALICE and ATLAS; we argue that this disagreement is due, in large part, to centrality bias. We make predictions for the ratio of suppression in ${}^3$He + Au and $p$ + Au collisions, which may in the future be used to disentangle final- from initial-state suppression in small systems. We then compare our results to various subsets of data, which allows us to estimate the preferred: low-$p_T$ scale at which non-perturbative processes become important, scales at which the strong coupling runs, and scale at which vacuum propagators transition to thermally modified propagators in collisional energy loss.

## Key claim

The authors' pQCD-based energy loss model, which includes small system size corrections, can simultaneously describe high-pT suppression data across system sizes, flavors, and collision energies when the effective strong coupling is extracted statistically, but only if the large formation time approximation is enforced via a kinematic cutoff on radiated gluon transverse momentum; the model agrees with PHENIX d+Au data but disagrees with ALICE and ATLAS p+Pb data, which the authors attribute primarily to centrality bias rather than to a failure of the energy loss mechanism.

## Method

The authors used the DGLV opacity expansion framework for radiative energy loss and the Braaten-Thoma formalism for collisional energy loss, both including short path length corrections. They imposed a combined collinear and large formation time kinematic cutoff on the radiated gluon transverse momentum to ensure self-consistency of the theoretical approximations. A chi-squared statistical analysis was performed to extract the effective strong coupling constant by comparing model predictions to high-pT suppression data from central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, with two theoretical uncertainties estimated: the transition between vacuum and hard thermal loop propagators in collisional energy loss, and the angular cutoff on radiated gluon momentum.

## Key numbers

- Extracted effective strong coupling at RHIC: alpha_s^eff = 0.41(14:10) (with systematic and statistical uncertainties)
- Extracted effective strong coupling at LHC: alpha_s^eff = 0.37(11:8) (with systematic and statistical uncertainties)
- Model uncertainties lead to O(30%) uncertainty in extracted alpha_s^eff, but final uncertainty on constrained R_AB and extrapolations is less than or equal to 20%
- Photon-normalized R^{pi^0}_{dAu} approximately 0.75 predicted for 0-5% centrality d+Au collisions at PHENIX
- Measured R^{h^pm}_{pPb} greater than or approximately 1 in 0-5% centrality p+Pb collisions at ALICE and ATLAS, in strong disagreement with model predictions
- Approximately 30% suppression predicted in minimum bias O+O and Ne+Ne collisions

## Context

This work extends the WHDG energy loss model (Wicks, Horowitz, Djordjevic, Gyulassy, 2005) by incorporating short path length corrections from Kolbe and Horowitz (2015) and enforcing the large formation time approximation via a kinematic cutoff proposed in Faraday and Horowitz (2023). It provides fully constrained, zero-parameter predictions for peripheral heavy-ion and small-system collisions, enabling a test of QGP formation in small systems. The ratio of suppression in He3+Au to p+Au collisions is proposed as a future observable to disentangle final-state energy loss from initial-state effects such as those described by the color fluctuation model of Alvioli et al. (2014, 2017).
