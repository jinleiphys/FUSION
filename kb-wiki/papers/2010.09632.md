# Pairing in pure neutron matter

> We review the long standing problem of superfluid pairing in pure neutron matter. For the $s$-wave pairing, we summarize the state of the art of many-body approaches including different $nn$ interactions, medium polarization, short-range correlations and BCS-BEC crossover effects, and compare them with quantum Monte Carlo results at low-densities. We also address pairing in the $p$-wave, which appears at higher densities and hence has large uncertainties due to the poorly constrained interactions, medium effects and many-body forces.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the current understanding of superfluid pairing in pure neutron matter, but emphasizes that while s-wave pairing at low densities is relatively well understood with moderate screening effects, p-wave pairing at higher densities remains highly uncertain due to poorly constrained interactions, medium effects, and many-body forces.

## Method

The authors review the BCS gap equation for s-wave singlet pairing and compare results from different many-body approaches including screening corrections via induced interactions (diagrams with particle-hole bubbles and RPA resummation), short-range correlations from self-consistent Green's function theory, and BCS-BEC crossover effects using the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach. For the screening calculations, they use renormalization-group-based interactions (Vlowk and Vsrg) with a density-dependent cutoff, and Skyrme energy-density functionals for the particle-hole interaction in the RPA. For triplet pairing, they discuss the phase shifts and uncertainties from different nn interactions at higher densities.

## Key numbers

- The nn scattering length in the s-wave is approximately -18.5 fm, signalling a nearly bound state
- The Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) result gives Delta/Delta_BCS = (4e)^{-1/3} approximately 0.45 in the limit |k_F a_nn| much less than 1
- For all considered Skyrme forces, the maximum of the screened s-wave gap lies between 2.3 and 2.5 MeV
- When short-range correlations are included in addition to screening, the maximum gap is further reduced to approximately 1.8 MeV
- The BCS relation T_c = 0.57 Delta(T=0) is a good approximation at all values of the chemical potential in neutron matter
- The s-wave gap maximum occurs near k_F approximately 0.8 fm^{-1}, corresponding to number densities above 0.017 fm^{-3} or mass densities above 2.9 x 10^{13} g/cm^3
- Triplet pairing is supposed to occur at k_F greater than approximately 1.3 fm^{-1}, corresponding to number densities above 0.07 fm^{-3} or mass densities above 1.2 x 10^{14} g/cm^3

## Context

This review extends the long-standing problem of neutron superfluidity first pointed out around 1960 by Migdal (1959) and Ginzburg (1964), and connects to observational evidence from pulsar glitches and neutron star cooling. The authors compare screening calculations by Cao et al. (2006) and their own work (Ramanan 2018, Urban 2020) with quantum Monte Carlo results from Gandolfi et al. (2008), Abe and Seki (2009), and Gezerlis and Carlson (2010). The review enables future work on reconciling screening calculations with QMC results at low densities and constraining p-wave pairing at higher densities, which is crucial for understanding neutron star crust and core physics.
