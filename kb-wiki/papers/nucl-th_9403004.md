# Polarizing Stored Beams by Interaction with Polarized Electrons

> A polarized, internal electron target gradually polarizes a proton beam in a storage ring. Here, we derive the spin-transfer cross section for $\vec e\,(p,\vec p\, )e$\ scattering. A recent measurement of the polarizing effect of a polarized atomic hydrogen target is explained when the effect of the atomic electrons is included. We also consider the interaction of a stored beam with a pure electron target which can be realized either by a comoving electron beam or by trapping of electrons in a potential well. In the future, this could provide a practical way to polarize antiprotons.

# Polarizing Stored Beams by Interaction with Polarized Electrons

## Key claim
A polarized internal electron target can gradually polarize a stored proton beam via spin transfer in $\vec e\,(p,\vec p\,)e$ scattering, and this electronic contribution explains a recent measurement of beam polarization from a polarized atomic hydrogen target at the TSR ring when combined with the effect of the target protons. The spin transfer cross section is surprisingly large because it arises from interference between the Coulomb and hyperfine amplitudes, enhanced by the singularity at zero momentum transfer and the large proton-to-electron mass ratio.

## Method
The authors derived the spin-transfer cross section for $\vec e\,(p,\vec p\,)e$ scattering in the center-of-mass frame using plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA), then incorporated Coulomb distortions via distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) with a correction factor $D$ involving the Coulomb parameter $\eta$ and the Coulomb wave function at the origin $C_0^2$. The total spin cross section $\sigma_{nn}$ was obtained by integrating over scattering angles with a cutoff at a minimum angle $\theta_{min}$ determined by screening: the Bohr radius for atomic hydrogen targets or the Debye length for pure electron plasma targets.

## Key numbers
- Measured polarization rate for a 23 MeV proton beam at the TSR ring: $dP_B/dt = 0.0124 \pm 0.0006$ per hour, using a polarized atomic hydrogen target of thickness $6 \times 10^{13}$ atoms per cm$^2$ (Rathmann et al., 1993).
- Spin-dependent part of the strong pp total cross section at 23 MeV: $\sigma(\uparrow\uparrow)-\sigma(\downarrow\uparrow) = -122$ mb.
- Calculated electronic spin transfer cross section for the TSR conditions: $\sigma_{nn} = -140$ mb, giving $\hat\sigma = -70$ mb.
- Maximum scattering angle of a proton from an electron at rest: $m_e/m_p = 0.54$ mrad.
- Screening distance for atomic hydrogen target: $\Lambda_A = a_0 = 52900$ fm.
- Estimated Debye screening length for a pure electron target with $kT \approx 0.1$ eV and $n_e \approx 10^{10}$ cm$^{-3}$: $\Lambda_D \approx 10^{10}$ fm.
- Extrapolated target thickness for a trapped electron device: $10^{12}$ electrons per cm$^2$.

## Context
This work extends the "filter method" for polarizing stored beams, previously tested at the TSR ring by Rathmann et al. (1993), by identifying the previously overlooked contribution from atomic electrons. The authors show that selective beam loss from the spin-dependent pp total cross section alone overestimates the observed polarization by about a factor of two, and that including the $\vec e\,(p,\vec p\,)e$ spin transfer resolves the discrepancy. The method enables a practical path toward polarizing antiprotons, which has been a long-standing challenge (Workshop on Polarized Antiprotons, 1985), by using pure electron targets to avoid the large annihilation cross section that would occur with nuclear targets.
