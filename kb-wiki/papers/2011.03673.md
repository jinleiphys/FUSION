# Magnetic field dependence of the neutral pion mass in the linear sigma model with quarks: The strong field case

> We use the linear sigma model with quarks to find the magnetic field-induced modifications to the neutral pion mass at one-loop level. The magnetic field effects are introduced by using charged particle propagators in the presence of a magnetic background in the strong field regime. We show that when accounting for the effects of the magnetic field on the model couplings, the vacuum sigma field and the neutral pion self-energy, the neutral pion mass decreases monotonically as a function of the field strength. We find an excellent qualitative and quantitative agreement with recent lattice QCD calculations, reproducing the monotonically decreasing trend with the field strength as well as the decrease when lattice data approaches the physical vacuum pion mass from larger values.

## Key claim

The neutral pion mass decreases monotonically as a function of magnetic field strength when one accounts for the combined effects of magnetic field corrections to the boson self-coupling, the fermion-boson coupling, the vacuum expectation value of the sigma field, and the neutral pion self-energy, all computed within the linear sigma model with quarks (LSMq) in the strong field regime using the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation.

## Method

The authors computed the neutral pion mass in the LSMq at one-loop order in the presence of a constant external magnetic field, working in the strong field limit where only the lowest Landau level (LLL) contributes. They calculated three magnetic field-dependent ingredients: the neutral pion self-energy from quark-antiquark and charged pion loops, the vacuum expectation value of the sigma field from the one-loop effective potential, and the magnetic field corrections to the boson self-coupling and fermion-boson coupling. The renormalization scale was set to \(\mu^2 = 2|eB| + m_0^2\) to ensure it exceeds the LLL gap, and vacuum stability conditions were enforced to isolate purely magnetic effects.

## Key numbers

- The neutral pion mass decreases monotonically with magnetic field strength, reproducing the trend from lattice QCD calculations by Endrodi and Ding.
- The renormalization scale is chosen as \(\mu^2 = 2|eB| + m_0^2\), where \(m_0 = 140\) MeV is the vacuum pion mass.
- The model parameters used are \(\lambda = 3.67\), \(g = 0.46\), yielding \(m_\sigma = 435\) MeV, \(a = 256\) MeV, and \(v_0 = 152\) MeV.
- The charged boson loop contribution to the self-energy is \(\Pi_{\pi^\pm} = -\frac{\lambda|eB|}{4\pi^2} \ln\left(\frac{|eB| + m_0^2}{2|eB| + m_0^2}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ayala et al. (2020) on magnetic field-modified couplings in the LSMq, confirming that the neutral pion mass decreases monotonically with field strength, in agreement with lattice QCD results from Endrodi and Ding. It contradicts the findings of Das (2019) and Scoccola (2019) using the NJL model, which found a non-monotonic behavior with an intermediate increase, attributed to a different vacuum subtraction procedure. The results enable further study of hadron properties in strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions and neutron star physics.
