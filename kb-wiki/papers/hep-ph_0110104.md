# Semi-classical transport theory for non-Abelian plasmas

> We review a semi-classical transport theory for non-Abelian plasmas based on a classical picture of coloured point particles. Within this formalism, kinetic equations for the mean particle distribution, the mean fields and their fluctuations are obtained using an ensemble-average in phase space. The framework permits the integrating-out of fluctuations in a systematic manner. This leads to the derivation of collision integrals, noise sources and fluctuation-induced currents for the effective transport equations of QCD. Consistency with the non-Abelian gauge symmetry is established, and systematic approximation schemes are worked out. In particular, the approach is applicable to both in- and out-of-equilibrium plasmas. The formalism is applied explicitly to a hot and weakly coupled QCD plasma slightly out of equilibrium. The physics related to Debye screening, Landau damping or colour conductivity is deduced in a very simple manner. Effective transport equations are computed to first and second order in moments of the fluctuations. To first order, they reproduce the seminal hard-thermal-loop effective theory. To second order, the fluctuations induce collisions amongst the quasi-particles, leading to a Langevin-type transport equation. A complementary Langevin approach is discussed as well. Finally, we show how the approach can be applied to dense quark matter systems. In the normal phase, the corresponding kinetic equations lead to the hard-dense-loop effective theory. At high density and low temperature diquark condensates are formed, changing the ground state of QCD. In the superconducting phase with two massless quark flavours, a transport equation for coloured excitations is given as well. Possible future applications are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper presents a semi-classical transport theory for non-Abelian plasmas based on classical coloured point particles, claiming that this framework can systematically derive effective transport equations for QCD by integrating out fluctuations, with the hedge that the approach is valid only in the domain where the underlying point particle picture is applicable.

## Method

The authors extend the Klimontovitch procedure to non-Abelian gauge theories, treating colour charges as dynamical non-canonical phase space variables and introducing an ensemble average over phase space to obtain kinetic equations for mean particle distributions, mean fields, and their fluctuations. They then systematically integrate out fluctuations to derive collision integrals, noise sources, and fluctuation-induced currents, applying the formalism explicitly to a hot, weakly coupled QCD plasma slightly out of equilibrium and to dense quark matter systems including the superconducting phase with two massless quark flavours.

## Key numbers

- Debye screening mass scale: ~ gT for chromo-electric fields in a hot QCD plasma
- Damping rate of hard gluons: ~ g²T ln(1/g), which sets the dynamical scale γ
- Magnetic mass scale: ~ g²T, below which perturbative methods fail due to non-perturbative effects
- Effective expansion parameter for integrating out modes with momentum |p|: ~ g²T/|p|
- For dense quark matter: Fermi energy EF = μ, where μ is the quark chemical potential

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical transport approach of Heinz (1983-1986) and Kelly et al. (1994) by incorporating non-Abelian fluctuations beyond the Vlasov approximation, addressing gaps left by Selikhov (1993) and Markov and Markova (1994) regarding gauge consistency and colour charge dynamics. The formalism reproduces the hard-thermal-loop effective theory of Pisarski (1989) and Braaten and Pisarski (1990) at first order, and at second order yields the Boltzmann-Langevin equation derived by Bodeker (1998) for hot QCD plasmas, enabling systematic computation of transport coefficients and application to out-of-equilibrium plasmas and colour superconducting phases.
