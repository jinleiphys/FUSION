# Lorentz-violating contributions to the nuclear Schiff moment and nuclear EDM

> In the context of an atom endowed with nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM), we consider the effects on the Schiff moment of $CPT$-even Lorentz-violating (LV) terms that modify the Coulomb potential. First, we study the modifications on the Schiff moment when the nucleus interacts with the electronic cloud by means of a Coulomb potential altered only by the $P$-even LV components. Next, by supposing the existence of an additional intrinsic LV EDM generated by other LV sources, we assess the corrections to the Schiff moment when the interaction nucleus-electrons runs mediated by a Coulomb potential modified by both the $P$-odd and $P$-even LV components. We then use known estimates and EDM measurements to discuss upper bounds on the new Schiff moment components and the possibility of an intrisic nuclear EDM component ascribed to LV effects.

## Key claim

The authors show that Lorentz-violating (LV) terms from the CPT-even sector of the Standard Model Extension (SME) modify the nuclear Schiff moment, producing a component that is not aligned with the nuclear spin, and that this misalignment could in principle be detected in existing EDM experiments, provided the nuclear spin is prepared along the magnetic field axis.

## Method

The authors start from a modified Coulomb potential derived from the CPT-even LV tensor \((K_F)^{\alpha\beta\mu\nu}\), which contains both parity-even (\(\kappa^{ij}\)) and parity-odd (\(\kappa^j\)) coefficients. They expand the electrostatic interaction between the nucleus and the electronic cloud in powers of the inverse electron-nucleus distance, retaining terms up to the Schiff moment order. Using a displacement operator formalism, they compute the commutator with the full atomic Hamiltonian to isolate the residual interaction that survives the Schiff screening, obtaining modified Schiff moment expressions for three cases: (i) only parity-even LV terms, (ii) parity-even LV terms plus an intrinsic LV nuclear EDM, and (iii) parity-odd LV terms plus an intrinsic LV nuclear EDM.

## Key numbers

* Upper bound on the LV Schiff moment component in the Earth frame: \(|\langle\mathbb{S}_{(1)}^{x}\rangle_{z\pm}| = |\tilde{S}\kappa^{13}| \lessapprox 2.6\times10^{-12}\ e\,\text{fm}^3\), derived from the experimental EDM limit \(|d(^{199}\text{Hg})| < 3.1\times10^{-29}\ e\,\text{cm}\) (expEDM, 2016) and the theoretical relation \(d(^{199}\text{Hg}) = -2.8\times10^{-17}\,(S/e\ \text{fm}^3)\ e\,\text{cm}\) from Dzuba (2002).
* After sidereal transformation to the Sun-centered frame, the bound on the diagonal LV coefficient becomes \(|\tilde{S}\cos\chi\sin\chi|\,|\kappa^{ZZ}| \lessapprox 1.7\times10^{-13}\ e\,\text{fm}^3\), where \(\chi\) is the laboratory colatitude.
* Assuming the usual Schiff moment magnitude \(\tilde{S} \sim 10^{-12}\ e\,\text{fm}^3\), the resulting constraint on the LV coefficient is \(|\kappa^{ZZ}| \lesssim 0.17\).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of the nuclear Schiff moment (Schiff, 1963; Flambaum, 1994; Dzuba, 2002) by incorporating Lorentz-violating modifications to the Coulomb potential from the CPT-even sector of the SME (Kostelecky and Mewes, 2002; Bailey and Kostelecky, 2004). It builds on prior studies of LV effects in atomic systems, such as constraints from nuclear quadrupole moments (LVquad, 2017) and neutron EDM searches (Altarev, 2009). The results enable experimental searches for LV contributions to atomic EDMs by reinterpreting existing EDM data, and motivate further theoretical work on the structure of intrinsic LV nuclear EDMs arising from nonminimal couplings or LV quark-sector interactions.
