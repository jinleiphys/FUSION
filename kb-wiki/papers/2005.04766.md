# Thermal Fluctuations in Nuclear Pasta

> Despite their astrophysical relevance, nuclear pasta phases are relatively unstudied at high temperatures. We present molecular dynamics simulations of symmetric nuclear matter with several topologies of `lasagna' at a range of temperatures to study the pasta-uniform transition. Using the Minkowski functionals we quantify trends in the occupied volume, surface area, mean breadth, and Euler characteristic. The amplitude of surface displacements of the pasta increase with temperature which produce short lived topological defects such as holes and filaments near melting, resulting in power laws for increasing surface curvature with temperature. We calculate the static structure factor and report the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of pasta, finding that the shear viscosity is minimized at the melting temperature. These results may have implications for the thermoelastic properties of nuclear pasta and finite temperature corrections to the equation of state at pasta densities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermal fluctuations in nuclear pasta phases produce short-lived topological defects (holes and filaments) near the melting temperature, resulting in power-law increases in surface curvature with temperature, and that shear viscosity is minimized at the melting temperature, but these results are obtained for symmetric nuclear matter (equal proton and neutron numbers) at a density of 0.05 fm⁻³, which may not directly correspond to the low proton fractions found in neutron star crusts.

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations of symmetric nuclear matter using the semi-classical IUMD code with a two-body potential reproducing nuclear matter properties near saturation. They simulated three topologically distinct configurations of the planar "lasagna" phase (defects, nonparallel, and parallel) at temperatures from 0.70 to 1.16 times the melting temperature Tm = 1.72 MeV, using 102,400 nucleons in a cubic volume at nucleon density n = 0.05 fm⁻³. The Minkowski functionals (occupied volume, surface area, mean breadth, and Euler characteristic) were computed from charge density isosurfaces to quantify geometric and topological changes, and the static structure factor was calculated to obtain shear viscosity and thermal conductivity.

## Key numbers

- Melting temperature Tm = 1.72 MeV, resolved from heating simulations from T = 1.7 MeV to T = 1.8 MeV with increments of ΔT = 10⁻⁴ MeV every 10³ timesteps
- Nucleon density n = 0.05 fm⁻³ (approximately one-third of nuclear saturation density) in cubic simulation volumes with 102,400 total nucleons
- Approximately 41% of the simulation volume contains condensed nuclear matter below the melting temperature
- Surface area density A/Vtot increases by about 20% between T/Tm = 0.70 and T/Tm = 0.99
- Mean breadth follows a power law B/A ∝ T⁹·⁷ for configurations without helicoids
- Euler characteristic follows a power law −χ/A ∝ T¹²·⁵ for configurations without helicoids
- The first peak in neutron-proton pair correlations broadens by approximately 0.1 fm when increasing T/Tm from 0.70 to 0.87

## Context

This work extends prior molecular dynamics studies of nuclear pasta by Schneider et al. (2016) and Horowitz et al. (2015) to finite temperatures near the melting point, and complements elastic property studies by Caplan et al. (2018). The results provide finite-temperature corrections to the nuclear equation of state at pasta densities and transport properties (shear viscosity and thermal conductivity) that may be relevant for numerical simulations of neutron star merger remnants, where Hanauske et al. (2019) predict pasta densities at temperatures between 10 and 20 MeV for tens of milliseconds post-merger.
