# Re-evaluation of the $^{16}$O($n$,$γ$)$^{17}$O cross section at astrophysical energies and its role as neutron poison in the $s$ process

> The doubly-magic nucleus $^{16}$O has a small neutron capture cross section of just a few tens of microbarn in the astrophysical energy region. Despite of this, $^{16}$O plays an important role as neutron poison in the astrophysical slow neutron capture ($s$) process due to its high abundance. We present in this paper a re-evaluation of the available experimental data for $^{16}$O($n,γ$)$^{17}$O and derive a new recommendation for the Maxwellian-averaged cross sections (MACS) between $kT$= 5$-$100 keV. Our new recommendations are lower up to $kT$= 60 keV compared to the previously recommended values but up to 14\% higher at $kT$= 100 keV. We explore the impact of this different energy dependence on the weak $s$-process during core helium- ($kT$= 26 keV) and shell carbon burning ($kT$= 90 keV) in massive stars where $^{16}$O is the most abundant isotope.

## Key claim

The new recommended Maxwellian-averaged cross sections (MACS) for ¹⁶O(n,γ)¹⁷O are lower than previously recommended values up to kT = 60 keV, but up to 14% higher at kT = 100 keV, altering the energy dependence of the cross section in a way that affects weak s-process calculations in massive stars.

## Method

The authors re-evaluated all available experimental data for the ¹⁶O(n,γ)¹⁷O reaction, including direct capture measurements and resonance parameters, using a direct capture model with spectroscopic factors adjusted to fit the experimental cross sections. The cross section was calculated as the sum of contributions from s-wave, p-wave, and d-wave capture to final states in ¹⁷O, with the 3/2⁻ resonance at 411 keV added as a Breit-Wigner resonance including interference. The resulting cross sections were Maxwellian-averaged over kT = 5–100 keV and renormalized using the updated ¹⁹⁷Au(n,γ)¹⁹⁸Au standard cross section from KADoNiS v1.0.

## Key numbers

- MACS at kT = 30 keV: 34.9 μb (with asymmetric uncertainty +7.0/–3.5 μb), compared to the previous KADoNiS v0.3 value of 38 μb
- MACS at kT = 100 keV: 80.3 μb, compared to the previous KADoNiS v0.3 value of 69 μb (ratio 1.164, i.e., 16.4% higher)
- MACS at kT = 5 keV: 14.9 μb, compared to the previous value of 16 μb (ratio 0.933)
- Thermal capture cross section (25.3 meV): weighted average 190 ± 18 μb from Jur63 and McD77, with a dominating 82% branch to the 1/2⁻ state at 3055 keV and 18% branch to the 1/2⁺ state at 871 keV
- Spectroscopic factor for p-wave capture to the 5/2⁺ ground state: C²S = 0.93 ± 0.12
- Spectroscopic factor for p-wave capture to the 1/2⁺ state at 871 keV: C²S = 0.87 ± 0.06
- Ground state radiation width of the 3/2⁻ resonance at 411 keV: Γγ,0 = 0.30 ± 0.07 eV from neutron capture, compared to 0.42 eV from photodisintegration (Holt 1978)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutron poisons in the weak s-process in massive stars, following earlier discussions by Beer and Goriely (1985) for ²²Ne and by Pignatari et al. (2010) for the propagation effect of light neutron poisons. The new MACS values, implemented in the KADoNiS v1.0 database, provide a different energy dependence compared to the previous KADoNiS v0.3 recommendation (Bao et al. 2000), which affects the neutron economy during core helium burning (kT ≈ 26 keV) and shell carbon burning (kT ≈ 90 keV) in massive stars. The results enable more accurate weak s-process calculations, particularly for the production of isotopes with A < 90, and highlight the need for independent experimental confirmation of the neutron capture data, as the only independent check (the ground state radiation width from photodisintegration by Holt 1978) shows a discrepancy of about 40%.
