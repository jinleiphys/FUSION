# Photon-Photon and Pomeron-Pomeron Processes in Peripheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> We estimate the cross sections for the production of resonances, pion pairs and a central cluster of hadrons in peripheral heavy-ion collisions through two-photon and double-pomeron exchange, at energies that will be available at RHIC and LHC. The effect of the impact parameter in the diffractive reactions is introduced, and imposing the condition for realistic peripheral collisions we verify that in the case of very heavy ions the pomeron-pomeron contribution is indeed smaller than the electromagnetic one. However, they give a non-negligible background in the collision of light ions. This diffractive background will be more important at RHIC than at LHC.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that for very heavy ions (e.g., uranium or lead) at RHIC and LHC energies, the double-pomeron exchange contribution to peripheral heavy-ion collisions is at least two orders of magnitude below the photon-photon contribution for resonance production, once a realistic impact-parameter cut (b_min > 2R) is imposed; however, for light ions (e.g., silicon) the diffractive background becomes non-negligible and can be as large as half the electromagnetic rate, and this diffractive background is more important at RHIC than at LHC.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections for resonance production, pion-pair production, and multiparticle hadron cluster production in peripheral heavy-ion collisions via two-photon and double-pomeron exchange, using the equivalent-photon (Weizsäcker-Williams) approximation in impact-parameter space for photons and the Donnachie-Landshoff model for pomerons. They enforce the condition for realistic peripheral collisions either by a geometrical cut requiring the minimum impact parameter b_min > 2R (where R is the nuclear radius) or by introducing an absorption factor computed in the Glauber approximation, and compare the two approaches for identical ions at RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/nucleon) and LHC (sqrt(s) = 6300 GeV/nucleon).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Baur (1990s), Cahn and Jackson (1990), and Klein (1990s) on peripheral heavy-ion collisions, and directly addresses the claim by Engel et al. (1990s) that diffractive production of hadrons may be a background for photonic processes at LHC. The authors show that the geometrical cut on impact parameter (b_min > 2R) reduces the diffractive contribution significantly for heavy ions, contrary to earlier estimates by Natale (1990s) that did not include this cut. The results enable future experimental separation of photon-photon and pomeron-pomeron processes at RHIC and LHC, particularly for light-ion collisions where the diffractive background cannot be neglected.
