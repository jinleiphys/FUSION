# Semiclassical transport of particles with dynamical spectral functions

> The conventional transport of particles in the on-shell quasiparticle limit is extended to particles of finite life time by means of a spectral function A(X,\vec{P},M^2) for a particle moving in an area of complex self-energy Σ^{ret}_X = Re Σ^{ret}_X -i Γ_X/2. Starting from the Kadanoff-Baym equations we derive in first order gradient expansion equations of motion for testparticles with respect to their time evolution in \vec{X}, \vec{P} and M^2. The off-shell propagation is demonstrated for a couple of model cases that simulate hadron-nucleus collisions. In case of nucleus-nucleus collisions the imaginary part of the hadron self-energy Γ_X is determined by the local space-time dependent collision rate dynamically. A first application is presented for A + A reactions up to 95 A MeV, where the effects from the off-shell propagation of nucleons are discussed with respect to high energy proton spectra, high energy photon production as well as kaon yields in comparison to the available data from GANIL.

## Key claim

The paper extends conventional on-shell quasiparticle transport to particles with finite lifetime by deriving semiclassical equations of motion that include a dynamical spectral function, but the derivation assumes a first-order gradient expansion and restricts to local collision terms, with the condition that energy conservation for individual test particles must be restored by an additional term when the width has explicit time dependence.

## Method

Starting from the Kadanoff-Baym equations, the authors performed a Wigner transformation and first-order gradient expansion to obtain generalized transport and mass-shell equations for the Green functions. They introduced a testparticle ansatz in an 8-dimensional phase space (position, momentum, and squared mass) and derived equations of motion for the testparticle coordinates that include forces from both the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy. The collisional width was determined dynamically from the local space-time dependent collision rate, and the approach was implemented within the HSD transport framework for nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- Nucleus-nucleus reactions up to 95 A MeV were studied, comparing to experimental data from GANIL.
- For the model studies, the Woods-Saxon potential parameters were R=5 fm and a0=0.6 fm, with vacuum widths as small as ΓV=0.8 MeV and as large as ΓV=160 MeV.
- In the model case with W0=50 MeV and ΓV=2 MeV, the spectral function width increased from 1 MeV in vacuum to 102 MeV (=2W0+ΓV) at the center of the absorptive potential.
- The time step used in numerical simulations was Δt=0.5 fm/c, with coordinate space cells of size 1 fm³ for averaging the collisional width.

## Context

This work extends the formal transport theory of Botermans and Malfliet (1990) and Henning (1995) by providing a practical semiclassical implementation of dynamical spectral functions, going beyond the on-shell quasiparticle limit used in standard approaches such as those of Bertsch and Das Gupta (1988) or the HSD model of Cassing and Bratkovskaya (1999). The approach enables studies of subthreshold meson production and in-medium modifications of hadron properties in heavy-ion collisions, addressing whether enhanced yields arise from mass shifts or spectral broadening, and provides a framework for future dilepton studies of vector mesons in nuclear matter.
