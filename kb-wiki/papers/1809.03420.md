# Influence of the interactions of scalar mesons on the behavior of the symmetry energy

> Symmetry energy behavior of scalar mesons interactions is analyzed within the framework of the standard relativistic mean field model. Whereas the presence of the $δ$ meson itself makes the symmetry energy stiffer, the crossing term $δ\textrm{-}σ$ allows its slope to decrease to the suggested experimental value. Moreover, such controlling of the symmetry energy does not significantly affect the stiffness of the equation of state and acceptable neutron star masses result. Interestingly, for the most plausible value of the symmetry energy slope, the phase transition occurs in the neutron star core.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a scalar meson crossing term (σ-δ) in the standard relativistic mean field model allows the symmetry energy slope L to be reduced to experimentally suggested values (around 50 MeV), but only when the σ-δ coupling constant takes negative values; this control of the symmetry energy does not significantly soften the equation of state, and for the most plausible slope values a phase transition occurs in the neutron star core.

## Method

The authors extended the standard relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian by adding a σ-δ meson interaction term, considered in both linear (σ δ²) and quadratic (σ² δ²) forms. They solved the mean-field equations for asymmetric nuclear matter, adjusting the isovector coupling constants C_ρ², C_δ², and the σ-δ coupling g_α to fix the symmetry energy at saturation to 30 MeV while allowing the slope L to vary between 48 and 140 MeV. Neutron star masses and radii were then computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy B = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 230 MeV.
- Symmetry energy at saturation fixed to E_sym(n₀) = 30 MeV.
- For the linear model with g_{σδ²} = -0.009 fm⁻¹ and C_δ² = 3.5 fm², the slope L = 47.2 MeV.
- For the quadratic model with g_{σ²δ²} = -0.004 and C_δ² = 3.5 fm², the slope L = 54.4 MeV.
- Maximum neutron star masses: 2.17–2.21 M_⊙ (linear model) and 2.17–2.22 M_⊙ (quadratic model), with radii 11.09–11.53 km and central densities 0.97–1.05 fm⁻³.
- Critical density for direct URCA cooling: 0.25–0.3 fm⁻³, corresponding to neutron star masses of 1.01–1.06 M_⊙.

## Context

This work extends the standard RMF model of Walecka (1974) by incorporating σ-δ meson interactions, building on earlier studies of δ meson effects by Kubis (1997) and of ρ-σ and ρ-ω crossing terms by Horowitz (2001), Todd-Rutel (2005), and Fattoyev (2010). The authors show that scalar meson coupling can reduce the symmetry energy slope to values consistent with experimental constraints from Oertel (2016), unlike pure ρ-meson RMF models which predict L above 90 MeV. The super-soft symmetry energy behavior obtained with quadratic coupling, previously seen only in Skyrme and MDI models as reviewed by Li (2008), opens the possibility of studying phase transitions in neutron star cores, which the authors plan to address in a separate work.
