# Alpha-decay branching ratios of near-threshold states in 19Ne and the astrophysical rate of 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne

> The 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne reaction is one of two routes for breakout from the hot CNO cycles into the rp process in accreting neutron stars. Its astrophysical rate depends critically on the decay properties of excited states in 19Ne lying just above the 15O + alpha threshold. We have measured the alpha-decay branching ratios for these states using the p(21Ne,t)19Ne reaction at 43 MeV/u. Combining our measurements with previous determinations of the radiative widths of these states, we conclude that no significant breakout from the hot CNO cycle into the rp process in novae is possible via 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne, assuming current models accurately represent their temperature and density conditions.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that no significant breakout from the hot CNO cycle into the rp process in novae is possible via 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne, but this conclusion is conditional on assuming current models accurately represent the temperature and density conditions of novae.

## Method

The authors populated excited states in 19Ne just above the 15O + alpha threshold using the p(21Ne,t)19Ne reaction in inverse kinematics at a 21Ne beam energy of 43 MeV/u. They detected either 19Ne recoils (gamma-decays) or 15O decay products (alpha-decays) in coincidence with tritons in the Big-Bite Spectrometer at 0 degrees, using phoswich detectors for particle identification and achieving 100% geometric efficiency for excitation energies up to 5.5 MeV. Branching ratios were extracted from fits to excitation energy spectra, and upper limits were determined using Bayesian statistical analysis.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on alpha-decay branching ratio for the 4.033 MeV 3/2+ state: ≤ 4.3 × 10^-4 at 90% confidence level
- Upper limit on alpha-decay branching ratio for the 4.379 MeV 7/2+ state: ≤ 3.9 × 10^-3 at 90% confidence level
- Alpha-decay branching ratio for the 4.600 MeV state: 0.32 ± 0.04
- Alpha-decay branching ratio for the 4.712 MeV state: 0.85 ± 0.04
- Alpha-decay branching ratio for the 5.092 MeV state: 0.90 ± 0.06
- Upper limit on alpha-width for the 4.033 MeV state: ≤ 0.011 meV at 90% confidence level
- Upper limit on alpha-width for the 4.379 MeV state: ≤ 2.4 meV at 90% confidence level
- Experimental resolution: 90 keV FWHM

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to measure alpha-decay branching ratios in 19Ne using the 19F(3He,t)19Ne reaction by Magnus (1990), which lacked sensitivity to measure the critical 4.033 MeV state. The authors' upper limit on B_alpha for the 4.379 MeV state is a factor of 11 smaller than the central value reported by Magnus (1990), which they attribute to imperfect background subtraction in that earlier work. The results enable the first calculation of the 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne reaction rate at temperatures ≤ 0.5 GK based on direct experimental measurements, and combined with independent determinations of the 18Ne(alpha,p)21Na reaction rate by Wiescher (1999), Bradfield-Smith (1999), and Chen (2001), the authors rule out appreciable breakout from the hot CNO cycle in novae.
