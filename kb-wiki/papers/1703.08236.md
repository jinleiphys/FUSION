# Generalized Nambu-Goldstone pion in dense matter: a schematic NJL model

> Chiral symmetry is always broken in cold, dense matter, by chiral condensation at low densities and by diquark condensation at high density. We construct here, within a schematic Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the corresponding generalized Nambu-Goldstone pion, $π_G$. As we show, the $π_G$ mode naturally emerges as a linear combination of the $\langle \bar{q}q\rangle$ vacuum pion $π$ and the $\langle qq \rangle$ diquark condensate pion $\tildeπ$, with $q$ the quark field, and continuously evolves with increasing density from being $π$-like in the vacuum to $\tildeπ$-like in the high density diquark pairing phase. We calculate the density-dependent mass, decay constant, and coupling to quarks of the $π_G$, and derive a generalized Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner (GMOR) relation in the presence of a finite bare quark mass $m_q$. We briefly discuss the implications of the results to possible Bose condensation of $π_G$ in more realistic models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in cold dense matter, the Nambu-Goldstone pion continuously evolves from a vacuum pion (fluctuation of the chiral condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q\rangle\)) at low density to a diquark-condensate pion (fluctuation of the \(\langle qq\rangle\) diquark condensate) at high density, with a generalized pion \(\pi_G\) emerging as a linear combination of both modes in the coexistence region; this result is derived within a simplified single-flavor, single-color NJL model and is conditional on the existence of a coexistence phase where both condensates are finite.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar and pseudoscalar four-quark interactions (couplings \(G\) and \(H\)) and solve it in the mean-field approximation using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism. They derive the gap equations for the chiral condensate \(\sigma = 2G\langle \bar{q}q\rangle\) and the diquark condensate \(\Delta_s = 2H\langle \bar{q}i\gamma_5 C \bar{q}^T\rangle\) in the scalar ground state, compute the quasiparticle spectrum, and analyze the phase diagram to identify a stable coexistence region. The collective modes are obtained by expanding the thermodynamic potential around the mean-field solution, yielding the mass matrix for the two pseudoscalar pionic modes \(\pi\) and \(\tilde{\pi}\), from which the generalized pion \(\pi_G\) and its properties are extracted.

## Key numbers

- The lower bound for the chiral coupling to have a non-vanishing chiral condensate in vacuum is \(G_{c1} = \pi^2 / \Lambda^2\), where \(\Lambda\) is the three-momentum cutoff.
- The upper bound for stability against density fluctuations is \(G_{c2} = \pi^2 / [(1 - e^{-2}) \Lambda^2]\), with backbending first occurring at critical Fermi momentum \(p_{Fc} = \Lambda / e\).
- A coexistence phase requires \(H < G\); the authors use a representative parameter set \(G = 11 \Lambda^{-2}\) and \(H = 6 \Lambda^{-2}\) to produce the phase diagram.
- The squared mass of the massive pionic mode \(\pi_M\) is proportional to \(M^2 \Delta^2\), where \(M\) is the effective quark mass and \(\Delta\) is the BCS gap, and is always positive in the coexistence phase.

## Context

This work extends prior NJL studies of chiral NG mesons in the low-density \(\bar{q}q\) condensed regime (Bernard 1986, Buballa 2000) and high-density pure BCS \(qq\) paired regime (Ruggieri 2007, Ebert 2008) by providing a quantitative description at intermediate densities where both condensates coexist. The mixing mechanism for the \(\pi\) and \(\tilde{\pi}\) modes is a concrete example of the generalized meson framework proposed by Hatsuda (Collective modes in dense matter). The results enable future studies of meson mass ordering reversal in dense matter (dtsonReversal) and implications for neutron star interiors and cooling (Yakovlev 2001).
