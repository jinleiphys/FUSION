# A unified nucleosynthetic site for the production of heavy isotopes and p-nuclei

> Current r-process models under-produce A<130 nuclei. P-process models either underproduce A<100 and 150<A<165 p-nuclei, or have other limitations. We argue that the puzzles in these two mechanisms can be solved by heavy-ion spallation. Spallation happens in the explosive phase transition from a neutron star to a quark star - the quark-nova. The quark-nova triggers the r-process which creates abundant A>130 nuclei, and spallation fragments these isotopes into A<130 nuclei and all 35 p-nuclei. Our model is universal in relation to a star's age, metallicity, and chemistry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that heavy-ion spallation occurring in the dual-shock Quark Nova (dsQN) can simultaneously solve the underproduction of A<130 nuclei in r-process models and the underproduction of certain p-nuclei in p-process models, but only if the time delay between the initial supernova and the subsequent quark-nova is short enough (a few days) to allow the relativistic QN ejecta to interact with the supernova's nickel layer.

## Method

The authors model the spallation of a beam of A>130 r-process isotopes (produced in the Quark Nova ejecta) against a static target of 56Ni from the inner shell of a Type II supernova. They use parameterized partial cross sections for proton-nucleus reactions scaled to nucleus-nucleus cross sections, and simulate the beam's fragmentation as it traverses virtual layers of the target until the beam's displacement exceeds the nickel layer's thickness. The simulation is run for different time delays between the supernova and the Quark Nova (2, 3, and 5 days), which determines the target density at the time of collision.

## Key numbers

- The QN ejecta mass is approximately 10^-3 solar masses, with the r-process beam modeled as a pulse of mass ~10^-5 solar masses.
- The beam energy is E ~ 10 GeV per nucleon, corresponding to the typical nucleon energy in the QN ejecta.
- The target is a 56Ni layer with mass 0.1 solar masses, expanding at v_sn = 5000 km/s.
- The simulation stops when the beam displacement x exceeds the nickel layer thickness ΔR, with the average free path λ calculated per virtual layer.
- For time delays of 5, 3, and 2 days, the spallation yields show different slopes: higher time delays produce flatter isotope distributions, while lower time delays produce a negative slope favoring lighter nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the dual-shock Quark Nova model previously explored by Ouyed (2011) for spallation, but now specifically considers fragmentation of A>130 r-process isotopes produced in the QN to cover a wider mass spectrum. It addresses the long-standing underproduction of A<130 nuclei in r-process models (such as neutron star mergers, which underproduce elements lighter than A~140 as shown by Korobkin 2012) and the underproduction of p-nuclei in the mass ranges A≤100 and 150<A<165 in supernova p-process models (as noted by Rayet 1995 and others). The model offers a potential explanation for the rare isotopes 138La and 180mTa without requiring the neutrino-process, and its predicted "double-hump" light curve has been observed in SN 2009ip and SN 2010mc, with Cas A suggested as a candidate dsQN remnant with t_delay ~ 5 days.
