# Peeling away neutron skin in ultracentral collisions of relativistic nuclei

> In heavy nuclei the ratio between local densities of neutrons and protons increases towards the nuclear periphery. The excess of neutrons is known as the neutron skin (NS) with a subtle difference ($<0.5$~fm) between the r.m.s radii of the distributions of neutrons and protons. We show that the presence of NS in $^{208}$Pb leads to extra spectator neutrons in ultracentral $^{208}$Pb--$^{208}$Pb collisions at the CERN SPS and LHC. The yields of spectator neutrons and protons were calculated within a new version of Abrasion-Ablation Monte Carlo for Colliders model (AAMCC-MST) taking into account NS and pre-equilibrium clustering of spectator matter. While the average numbers of spectator neutrons and protons in ultracentral collisions vary insignificantly, the cross sections of emission of certain numbers of spectator neutrons in events with 0,1,...5 spectator protons are changed by 50--250\%, depending on the thickness of NS. These cross sections are less sensitive to other parameters in calculations, and their measurements in the ALICE experiment at the LHC will make it possible to restrict the existing variety of neutron density parameterizations in $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

The presence of neutron skin in 208Pb leads to extra spectator neutrons in ultracentral 208Pb-208Pb collisions, but the average numbers of spectator neutrons and protons vary insignificantly; instead, the cross sections of emission of certain numbers of spectator neutrons in events with 0,1,...5 spectator protons are changed by 50-250%, depending on the thickness of the neutron skin, and these cross sections are less sensitive to other parameters in calculations.

## Method

The authors used a new version of the Abrasion-Ablation Monte Carlo for Colliders model (AAMCC-MST) that incorporates neutron skin effects and pre-equilibrium clustering of spectator matter via a minimum spanning tree algorithm. The Glauber Monte Carlo model simulated the initial geometry of 208Pb-208Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.21 GeV (CERN SPS) and 5.02 TeV (LHC), with six different parameterizations of neutron and proton density distributions in 208Pb. The excitation energy of spectator prefragments was calculated using a hybrid approach combining the Ericson formula and ALADIN parameterization, and the subsequent decay of clusters was simulated via evaporation, Fermi Break-up, and Statistical Multifragmentation models from Geant4.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness values for the six parameterizations range from Delta_r_np = 0.115 fm (NFB-8 profile) to 0.321 fm (NL1 profile)
- The Pbpnrw profile (default in Glauber MC) gives Delta_r_np = 0.15 fm, while the PREX profile gives Delta_r_np = 0.283 fm
- The cross sections of emission of certain numbers of spectator neutrons in events with 0,1,...5 spectator protons are changed by 50-250% depending on the thickness of the neutron skin
- The maximum impact parameter b_max for 0-5% centrality at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.21 GeV ranges from 3.32 fm (NFB-8) to 3.46 fm (PREX1); at 5.02 TeV it ranges from 3.45 fm (NFB-8) to 3.59 fm (PREX1)
- The hybrid excitation energy parameterization uses a switching point at alpha_sw = 0.9287 for 208Pb projectiles

## Context

This work extends proposals by De (2017), Paukkunen (2015), and Alvioli (2019) to probe neutron skin in peripheral 208Pb-208Pb collisions at the LHC, and complements studies by Fang (2011, 2010), Yan (2019), Aumann (2017), and Bertulani (2019) that showed sensitivity of reaction cross sections to neutron skin in lower-energy collisions with neutron-rich isotopes. The authors' suggestion to study ultracentral collisions was first formulated in their earlier papers (Pshenichnov 2021, Dmitrieva 2021, Kozyrev 2021, Pshenichnov 2022) based on an early version of the AAMCC model, and was independently considered by Liu (2022) for isobaric collisions. The measurements proposed could be performed with the neutron and proton Zero Degree Calorimeters in the ALICE experiment at the LHC, enabling restriction of the existing variety of neutron density parameterizations in 208Pb.
