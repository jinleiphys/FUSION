# The role of three-body collisions in phi-meson production processes near threshold

> The amplitude of subthreshold phi-meson production is calculated using dominant tree-level diagrams for three-body collisions. It is shown that the production can overwhelmingly be described by two-step processes. The effect of the genuine three-body contribution (i.e. the contribution which cannot be factorized) is discussed. The production rate of phi-mesons is presented for proton induced reactions on carbon.

## Key claim

Subthreshold phi-meson production in proton-nucleus collisions can overwhelmingly be described by two-step processes (sequential two-nucleon collisions), while genuine three-body contributions are not important and can even become negative when in-medium effects are included. The claim is conditional on the specific tree-level diagrams and effective parameters chosen, and on the treatment of intermediate particles with collision broadening.

## Method

The authors calculated the invariant amplitude for the reaction p + 2N → φ + 3N using tree-level diagrams with one-boson exchange, restricting to diagrams with nucleon propagators for the intermediate particle X and pion exchange between nucleons. They separated the total cross section into a two-step part (where intermediate pions or nucleons go on-shell and propagate as real particles) and a genuine three-body part, using a phenomenological collision width Γ = Γ₀ + Γ_coll to treat in-medium propagation. The cross section was evaluated for a proton hitting a target nucleon embedded in nuclear matter at density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with Fermi motion and separation energy included for nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- The three-body threshold for φ meson production is 1.8 GeV, compared to the two-nucleon threshold of 2.6 GeV.
- At an energy of 82 MeV above the two-nucleon production threshold, the calculated cross section is 0.04 μb using the chosen coupling constants and cut-off parameters.
- The coupling constants used: f_πNN = 0.989, g_ρNN = 3.718, f_ρNN = 6.1 × g_ρNN, f_πρφ = 1.04.
- Cut-off parameters: Λ^π_πNN = 1.6 GeV, Λ^ρ_ρNN = 1.3 GeV, Λ^π_πρφ = 0.95 GeV, Λ^ρ_πρφ = 1.2 GeV.
- The total cross section for intermediate pions and ρ mesons in the medium was taken as 25 mb.
- The relative velocity for a pion creating a φ meson in collision with a nucleon at rest is v̂ ∼ 11.
- Fermi momentum used: k_Fermi = 0.27 GeV; average separation energy: E_sep = 20 MeV.
- For p + ¹²C, an effective particle number A = 6 was used.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of subthreshold φ meson production using two-nucleon collisions (Chung et al., 1997; Titov et al., 1999) and addresses the discrepancy where transport models based on two-particle cross sections underestimated φ multiplicities measured in Ni+Ni and Ru+Ru collisions (Herrmann et al., 1999). The authors build on the approach of Danielewicz (1984) for many-body collisions in cumulative backward emission and on the treatment of off-shell propagation in transport models (Leupold, 1999). The results enable improved implementation of multi-step processes in transport codes for subthreshold heavy-meson production, particularly for interpreting upcoming K⁻ measurements at COSY (ANKE collaboration) and existing data from KaoS at SIS.
