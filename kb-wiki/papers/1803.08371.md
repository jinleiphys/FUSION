# Angular Momentum Sum Rule Violation in QCD Due to Confinement and The Proton Spin Crisis

> The proton spin crisis remains an unsolved problem in physics. In this paper we find that due to the confinement of partons inside the hadron the angular momentum sum rule in QCD is violated. Hence we find that the non-vanishing angular momentum flux contribution of the partons in QCD should be added to the spin and angular momentum of the partons to solve the proton spin crisis.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard angular momentum sum rule for the proton is violated because confinement forces the chromo-electric field to fall off no faster than \(1/r^{3/2}\), which produces a non-vanishing boundary surface term at infinity. This yields a gauge-invariant angular momentum flux contribution that must be added to the spin and orbital angular momentum of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons to recover the proton spin of \(1/2\).

## Method

The author derives the angular momentum conservation equation from the gauge-invariant Noether theorem in classical Yang-Mills theory, then extends it to QCD by promoting fields to operators. The key step is to argue that confinement requires the chromo-electric field to decay as \(1/r^{3/2}\) or slower, which makes the boundary surface term at spatial infinity non-zero. This non-zero term defines an angular momentum flux operator \(\hat{J}_{\rm flux}\), whose expectation value is then added to the standard parton spin and orbital angular momentum contributions.

## Key numbers

* The chromo-electric field produced by confined quarks and/or antiquarks cannot fall faster than \(1/r^{3/2}\) at large distance (Section IV).
* The proton spin sum rule is given by \(\frac{1}{2} = \sum_q \langle \hat{S}_q \rangle + \sum_q \langle \hat{L}_q \rangle + \sum_{\bar q} \langle \hat{S}_{\bar q} \rangle + \sum_{\bar q} \langle \hat{L}_{\bar q} \rangle + \sum_g \langle \hat{J}_g \rangle + \langle \hat{J}_{\rm flux} \rangle\) (Equation labeled \ref{fnef}).
* World data on spin-dependent parton distribution functions indicate that about fifty percent of the proton spin is carried by the spin of the partons (Section I).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier studies on gauge invariance and boundary surface terms in Yang-Mills theory (Nayak, 2004-2007) and contrasts with the standard parton-model sum rule proposed by Jaffe and Manohar (1990). It directly contradicts the assumption in relativistic quark models with confinement (e.g., the MIT bag model, as in work by Thomas and collaborators) that no extra surface term is needed. The paper claims that those models are not fundamental QCD and therefore their agreement with data does not invalidate the necessity of the angular momentum flux term. The result is intended to resolve the proton spin crisis by adding the flux contribution, but no numerical value for \(\langle \hat{J}_{\rm flux} \rangle\) is computed.
