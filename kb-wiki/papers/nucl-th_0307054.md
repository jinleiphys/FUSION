# Structure of $^8B$ and astrophysical $S_{17}$ factor in Skyrme Hartree-Fock theory

> We investigate the ground state structure of $^8$B within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock framework where spin-orbit part of the effective interaction is adjusted to reproduce the one-proton separation energy of this nucleus. Using same set of force parameters, binding energies and root mean square radii of other light p-shell unstable nuclei, $^8$Li, $^7$B,$^7$Be, and $^9$C, have been calculated where a good agreement with corresponding experimental data is obtained. The overlap integral of $^8$B and $^7$Be wave functions has been used to determine the root mean square radius of the single proton in a particular orbit and also the astrophysical S factor ($S_{17}$) for the $^{7}$Be($p, γ)^{8}$B radiative capture reaction. It is found that the asymptotic region (distances beyond 4 fm) of the p-shell single proton wave function contributes more than half to the calculated value (4.93 fm) of the corresponding single particle root mean square radius. We determine a $S_{17}$ 21.1 eV.b which is in good agreement with the recommended value for near zero energy $S_{17}$ of $19.0^{+4.0}_{-1.0}$ eV.b.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the astrophysical S-factor for the \(^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}\) reaction can be reproduced within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock framework by adjusting the spin-orbit part of the effective interaction to match the one-proton separation energy of \(^{8}\text{B}\), yielding a value of 21.1 eV·b that agrees with the recommended near-zero-energy value of \(19.0^{+4.0}_{-1.0}\) eV·b.

## Method

The ground state structure of \(^{8}\text{B}\) was calculated using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock method with a modified spin-orbit interaction tuned to reproduce the experimental one-proton separation energy of \(^{8}\text{B}\). The same force parameters were then used to compute binding energies and root mean square radii for the light p-shell unstable nuclei \(^{8}\text{Li}\), \(^{7}\text{B}\), \(^{7}\text{Be}\), and \(^{9}\text{C}\). The overlap integral of the \(^{8}\text{B}\) and \(^{7}\text{Be}\) wave functions was employed to determine the root mean square radius of the single proton in a particular orbit and to calculate the astrophysical S-factor \(S_{17}\) for the radiative capture reaction.

## Key numbers

* The calculated root mean square radius of the single proton in its orbit is 4.93 fm; more than half of this value comes from the asymptotic region beyond 4 fm.
* The calculated astrophysical S-factor \(S_{17}\) is 21.1 eV·b.
* The recommended near-zero-energy value of \(S_{17}\) is \(19.0^{+4.0}_{-1.0}\) eV·b.

## Context

This work extends the application of Skyrme Hartree-Fock theory to the structure of \(^{8}\text{B}\) and the calculation of the \(S_{17}\) factor, which is important for understanding the solar neutrino flux. The authors achieve good agreement with experimental binding energies and radii for several light p-shell unstable nuclei, including \(^{8}\text{Li}\), \(^{7}\text{B}\), \(^{7}\text{Be}\), and \(^{9}\text{C}\). The approach enables further studies of radiative capture reactions in light nuclei using self-consistent mean-field methods.
