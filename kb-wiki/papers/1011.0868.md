# Symmetry energy of dense matter in holographic QCD

> We study the nuclear symmetry energy of dense matter using holographic QCD. To this end, we consider two flavor branes with equal quark masses in a D4/D6/D6 model. We find that at all densities the symmetry energy monotonically increases. At small densities, it exhibits a power law behavior with the density, $E_{\rm sym} \sim ρ^{1/2}$.

## Key claim

The authors find that the nuclear symmetry energy in the D4/D6/D6 holographic QCD model increases monotonically with density at all densities, exhibiting a stiff dependence, and at low densities follows a power law \(E_{\rm sym} \sim \rho^{1/2}\); however, they caution that the holographic approach does not yet encode attractive scalar contributions essential for realistic nuclear matter, and that back-reaction effects at extreme densities are neglected.

## Method

The authors use a holographic QCD model (D4/D6/D6) with two flavor branes of equal quark masses, representing up and down quarks, and compact D4 branes as baryon vertices. They compute the total energy of the brane configuration as a function of baryon number \(Q\) and asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\) (related to neutron-proton asymmetry), imposing a force-balance condition at the cusp where the D6 branes meet the compact D4 brane. The symmetry energy \(S_2\) is extracted as the coefficient of the quadratic term in the asymmetry expansion of the energy per nucleon, and its density dependence is obtained by relating \(Q\) to the baryon density \(\rho\).

## Key numbers

- At low densities, the symmetry energy follows \(S_2 \sim \rho^{1/2}\); with \(\lambda=6\) and \(M_{KK}=1.04\) GeV, the fitted coefficient is \(c \simeq 27.7\) MeV and exponent \(\gamma \simeq 0.5\), compared to the experimental range \(\gamma = 0.5\)–\(0.7\) and \(c \simeq 31.6\) MeV.
- The normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\) corresponds to \(\tilde{Q} \sim 1.2\) in the model with the chosen parameters.
- The quark mass is taken as \(M_q \sim 41\) MeV and the non-anomalous \(\eta'\) mass as \(\sim 390\) MeV to fix the model parameters \(M_{KK}=1.04\) GeV and \(\lambda=6\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to dense matter developed by Seo (2008) and Kim, Shin, Sin (2010) by calculating the symmetry energy, a key quantity for neutron star properties and heavy-ion collisions. The result of a stiff symmetry energy (monotonically increasing with density) contrasts with some phenomenological models that allow a soft behavior at supra-saturation densities, as discussed in reviews by Li (2010) and others. The low-density power law \(S_2 \sim \rho^{1/2}\) differs from the kinetic-energy-dominated \(\rho^{2/3}\) behavior in conventional approaches (e.g., Danielewicz, 2008), suggesting either an anomalous dispersion relation or a fuzzy Fermi surface due to strong interactions, which the authors propose as a future study.
