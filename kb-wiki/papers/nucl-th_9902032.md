# dππdecay of the d* dibaryon

> The $d^* \to d ππ$ partial decay width has been calculated in a wave-function model for $d^*$ and $d$. It is found to be smaller than a previous estimate by a factor of 7. A previously proposed dependence on dibaryon sizes is confirmed. Additional dependence on the high-momentum components of dibaryon wave functions is found.

# d* → d ππ decay of the d* dibaryon

## Key claim

The d* → d ππ partial decay width is smaller than the previous estimate by Goldman et al. (1989) by a factor of 7, with about half of this reduction coming from a smaller volume ratio and the other half from the previously neglected requirement of momentum matching among wave functions in the pion emissions.

## Method

The decay width is calculated using lowest-order perturbation theory with a Fermi golden rule approach, treating the decay as two off-shell Δ baryons emitting pions. The calculation uses a wave-function model for both d* and d, with the d* described by single-Gaussian and two-center Gaussian baryon wave functions and the deuteron described by both a single Gaussian and a three-term Gaussian fit to the realistic Bonn C wave function. The Green functions for the two time-ordered diagrams include a total width Γtot for d* to avoid zeros in energy denominators.

## Key numbers

- The calculated d* → d ππ partial decay width at m* = 2250 MeV/c² is about 3 eV using the Bonn C deuteron wave function and a single-Gaussian d* wave function, smaller than the Goldman et al. (1989) estimate of about 20 eV by a factor of 7.
- For a single-Gaussian deuteron wave function with r* = 0.7 fm, the width at m* = 2250 MeV/c² is 2.0 eV, ten times smaller than the Goldman et al. (1989) estimate.
- The deuteron radius used is rd = 1.967 fm; the d* radius used is r* = 0.7 fm (with additional calculations at 0.5 fm and 0.9 fm).
- The volume ratio (r*/rd)³ is smaller than that used by Goldman et al. (1989) by a factor of 3.
- The parameter α0 describing quark wave functions inside baryons is 0.12 fm².
- The Δ width ΓΔ = 120 MeV is used in the calculation.
- The total width Γtot of d* is taken as 10 MeV, rising from about 1 MeV at m* = 2100 MeV/c² to about 10 MeV at m* = 2350 MeV/c².
- For the two-center Gaussian d* wave function with radius 0.72 fm, the calculated widths are about 20% smaller than for the single-Gaussian d* wave function.

## Context

This work extends the earlier estimate by Goldman et al. (1989) (GMSSW) of the d* → d ππ partial decay width, which used an effective dd*ππ vertex with a volume scaling factor (r*/rd)³. The present calculation confirms the size dependence proposed by Goldman et al. (1989) but finds it appears in a more complicated form that also depends on other wave function details. The work builds on the author's previous perturbative method for estimating NN and πNN partial widths of d* (Wong, 1998). The results enable future quantitative calculations that would need to include higher-order Feynman diagrams, quark antisymmetrization and delocalization effects, deuteron D-state contributions, and improved treatment of energy denominators.
