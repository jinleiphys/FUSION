# Comment on "On the implementation of CVC in weak charged-current proton-neutron transitions" by C. Giunti, arXiv: 1602.00215 [hep-ph]

> We show that the term maintaining conservation of the charged vector current for the transitions "neutron <-> proton" even for different masses of the neutron and proton (see T. Leitner et al., Phys. Rev. C {\bf 73}, 065502 (2006) and A. M. Ankowski, arXiv:1601.06169 [hep-ph]) is related to the first class current contribution but not to the second class one as has been pointed out by C. Giunti, arXiv: 1602.00215 [hep-ph].

## Key claim

The term \((- q_{\mu}\hat{q}/q^2)F_1(q^2)\) used by Leitner et al. (2006) and Ankowski (2016) to maintain conservation of the charged vector current (CVC) for neutron-proton transitions with unequal masses is a first class current contribution, not a second class one as claimed by Giunti (2016); therefore, Giunti's argument that the term should be prohibited does not hold.

## Method

The author analyzes the G-parity transformation properties of four currents that generate the Lorentz structures \(\gamma_{\mu}\), \(i\sigma_{\mu\nu}q^{\nu}\), \(q_{\mu}\hat{q}\), and \(q_{\mu}\) in the matrix element of the \(p \to n\) transition. Using the transformation rules for nucleon fields under G-parity (where \(G = C e^{i\pi I_2}\)), the author shows that the currents \(\bar{N}(x)\gamma_{\mu}\tau^{(\mp)}N(x)\), \(\partial^{\nu}(\bar{N}(x)\sigma_{\mu\nu}\tau^{(\mp)}N(x))\), and \(\partial_{\mu}\partial^{\nu}(\bar{N}(x)\gamma_{\nu}\tau^{(\mp)}N(x))\) are first class currents, while \(\partial_{\mu}(\bar{N}(x)\tau^{(\mp)}N(x))\) is a second class current. The author then demonstrates that the term \(q_{\mu}\hat{q}\) arises from a first class current, whereas the term \(q_{\mu}\) arises from a second class current, so they cannot be equated.

## Key numbers

- The relation \(F_3(q^2) = - (m_n - m_p)/q^2 \cdot m_N F_1(q^2)\) is derived by Giunti (2016) from the Dirac equation, requiring CVC for unequal nucleon masses.
- The author proposes \(F_4(q^2) = - (m_N^2/q^2) F_1(q^2)\) as the condition for CVC when the \(q_{\mu}\hat{q}\) term is included, without invoking the Dirac equation.

## Context

This comment corrects the classification by Giunti (2016) of the CVC-preserving term introduced by Leitner et al. (2006) for quasi-elastic neutrino scattering \(\nu_{\ell} + n \to p + \ell^-\) and used by Ankowski (2016) for inverse beta decay \(\bar{\nu}_e + p \to n + e^+\). The analysis extends the original G-parity classification of hadronic weak currents by Weinberg (1958) and shows that the \(q_{\mu}\hat{q}\) Lorentz structure belongs to the first class, enabling its continued use in calculations of neutron beta decay and neutrino-nucleon reactions without violating CVC.
