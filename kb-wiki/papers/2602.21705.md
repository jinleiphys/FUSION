# Phase diagram of the single-flavor Gross--Neveu--Wilson model from the Grassmann corner transfer matrix renormalization group

> We investigate the phase structure of the single-flavor Gross--Neveu model with Wilson fermions using the Grassmann corner transfer matrix renormalization group (CTMRG). The path integral is formulated as a two-dimensional Grassmann tensor network and approximately contracted by the Grassmann CTMRG algorithm. We investigate the phase diagram by varying the fermion mass and the four-fermion coupling, using the pseudoscalar condensate as an order parameter for the $\mathbb{Z}_{2}$ parity symmetry breaking phase. The universality classes of the phase boundaries are identified through the central charge $c$ obtained via scaling analysis of the entanglement entropy. Furthermore, we extract the quantity related to the entanglement spectrum from the converged CTMRG environments, allowing us to distinguish the topological insulator phase and the trivial phase. The resulting phase structure suggests that the Aoki phase is separated from the other phases by critical lines characterized by $c=1/2$, while the critical lines with $c=1$ separate the topological insulating and trivial phases. Our numerical results also indicate that the Aoki phase does not persist in the strong-coupling regime for the single-flavor theory.

## Key claim

The paper claims to provide the first comprehensive numerical phase diagram of the single-flavor Gross--Neveu--Wilson model in the Lagrangian formulation using tensor networks, but the results are qualified by the use of a finite bond dimension D as a hyperparameter controlling accuracy, and the phase boundaries are identified through scaling analysis of entanglement entropy rather than direct extrapolation to the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The path integral of the (1+1)-dimensional single-flavor Gross--Neveu model with Wilson fermions was formulated as a two-dimensional Grassmann tensor network and contracted using the Grassmann corner transfer matrix renormalization group (CTMRG) algorithm. The phase diagram was mapped by varying the fermion mass m and the four-fermion coupling g^2, using the pseudoscalar condensate as an order parameter for Z_2 parity symmetry breaking, and identifying universality classes via the central charge c obtained from finite-bond-dimension scaling of the entanglement entropy. The topological insulator phase was distinguished from the trivial phase by extracting quantities related to the entanglement spectrum from the converged CTMRG environments.

## Key numbers

* The bond dimension of the local fundamental tensor is d=4, while the virtual bond dimension D is a hyperparameter controlling accuracy, with computational cost scaling as O(d^3 D^3).
* The central charge c=1/2 characterizes the critical lines separating the Aoki phase from other phases, while c=1 characterizes the critical lines separating the topological insulating and trivial phases.
* The effective correlation length xi_D is defined as 1/xi_D = ln(lambda_1/lambda_2), where lambda_1 and lambda_2 are the leading and sub-leading eigenvalues of the row-to-row or column-to-column transfer matrix.
* The entanglement entropy S_D is computed as S_D = -sum_i theta_i log theta_i, where theta_i are the eigenvalues of the reduced density matrix rho_D with bond dimension D.

## Context

This work extends the large-N_f analysis of the Gross--Neveu--Wilson model originally performed by Aoki (1983), which predicted that the Aoki phase extends deeply into the strong-coupling region. The numerical results contradict that prediction for the single-flavor case, showing that the Aoki phase does not persist in the strong-coupling regime. The study also complements the Hamiltonian formalism results of Bermudez et al. (2018), which revealed the presence of the Aoki phase, a trivial band-insulating phase, and a symmetry-protected topological phase using matrix product state simulations. The Grassmann tensor network approach is free from the sign problem that plagues Monte Carlo simulations of Wilson fermions with odd flavors, opening a pathway toward future investigations of lattice QCD with Wilson fermions.
