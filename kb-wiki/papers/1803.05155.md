# Limiting fragmentation in high-energy nuclear collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> The hypothesis of limiting fragmentation (LF) or it is called otherwise recently, as extended longitudinal scaling, is an interesting phenomena in high energy multiparticle production process. This paper discusses about different regions of phase space and their importance in hadron production, giving special emphasis on the fragmentation region. Although it was conjectured as a universal phenomenon in high energy physics, with the advent of higher center-of-mass energies, it has become prudent to analyse and understand the validity of such hypothesis in view of the increasing inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section ($σ_{\rm in}$). In this work, we revisit the phenomenon of limiting fragmentation for nucleus-nucleus (A+A) collisions in the pseudorapidity distribution of charged particles at various energies. We use energy dependent $σ_{\rm in}$ to transform the charged particle pseudorapidity distributions ($dN^{\rm AA}_{ch}/dη$) into differential cross-section per unit pseudorapidity ($dσ^{\rm AA}/dη$) of charged particles and study the phenomenon of LF. We find that in $dσ^{\rm AA}/dη$ LF seems to be violated at LHC energies while considering the energy dependent $σ_{\rm in}$. We also perform a similar study using A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) Model with string melting scenario and also find that LF is violated at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that limiting fragmentation (LF) in the differential cross-section per unit pseudorapidity of charged particles appears to be violated at LHC energies (2.76 and 5.02 TeV) when an energy-dependent inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section is used, whereas LF is observed in the pseudorapidity distribution itself up to 2.76 TeV. The violation is conditional on the choice of observable and the inclusion of the energy dependence of the inelastic cross-section.

## Method

The authors transform experimental charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions ($dN_{\rm ch}^{\rm AA}/d\eta$) for A+A collisions at energies from 19.6 GeV to 5.02 TeV into differential cross-sections per unit pseudorapidity ($d\sigma^{\rm AA}/d\eta$) using the relation $d\sigma^{\rm AA}/d\eta = [A^2 \sigma_{\rm in} (dN_{\rm ch}^{\rm AA}/d\eta)] / [(1-x)\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle/2 + x\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle]$, where $\sigma_{\rm in}$ is the energy-dependent inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section. They also perform the same analysis using the AMPT model with string melting scenario. A double Gaussian function is used to extrapolate the experimental data into the fragmentation region at LHC energies where full phase-space data are unavailable.

## Key numbers

* Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section ($\sigma_{\rm in}$) shows a slow rise at lower collision energies up to top RHIC energy, but deviates from a logarithmic function at LHC energies; a hybrid function A + B ln($\sqrt{s}$) + C($\sqrt{s})^\alpha$ fits the data from lower to higher energies.
* For the double Gaussian fit parameters at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV: $A_1 = 2592.29 \pm 311.56$, $A_2 = 959.59 \pm 304.26$, $\sigma_1 = 3.27 \pm 0.13$, $\sigma_2 = 1.67 \pm 0.23$.
* For the double Gaussian fit parameters at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV: $A_1 = 2102.16 \pm 28.39$, $A_2 = 1817.56 \pm 37.90$, $\sigma_1 = 4.75 \pm 0.01$, $\sigma_2 = 0.61 \pm 0.14$.
* LF is observed in $dN_{\rm ch}^{\rm AA}/d\eta$ from $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 19.6 GeV to 2.76 TeV, but appears violated at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV.
* In $d\sigma^{\rm AA}/d\eta$, LF appears violated at both $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV when energy-dependent $\sigma_{\rm in}$ is used.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of limiting fragmentation at RHIC by the PHOBOS collaboration (2005) and at LHC by ALICE (2013), which reported LF in pseudorapidity distributions of charged particles. It contradicts the expectation of universal longitudinal scaling by showing that the energy dependence of the inelastic cross-section breaks the scaling in the differential cross-section observable at LHC energies. The study uses the AMPT model (Lin, 2004) for comparison and notes that Landau hydrodynamics (Landau, 1953) predicts LF naturally, but its failure at higher energies (absence of Gaussian rapidity distribution) together with the varying cross-section may explain the violation. The work calls for forward detectors at LHC to validate the hypothesis experimentally.
