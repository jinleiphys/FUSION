# Predictions for $R_{AA}$ in 5.36 TeV C+C, O+O, and Ne+Ne collisions at the LHC

> Experiments on collisions of light nuclei at $\sqrt{s}=5.36$ TeV have recently begun at the LHC. In this regard we make predictions for nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ in 5.36 TeV C+C, O+O, and Ne+Ne collisions for scenarios with and without quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions. We find a sizeable difference in $R_{AA}$ for these two scenarios, which grows with decreasing atomic number. This says that data on $R_{AA}$ for light nuclei could potentially give information on the presence of jet quenching in $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ in 5.36 TeV C+C, O+O, and Ne+Ne collisions will differ noticeably between scenarios with and without mini-quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in $pp$ collisions, with the difference growing as atomic number decreases; this difference could potentially reveal whether jet quenching occurs in $pp$ collisions, but the claim is conditional on obtaining accurate experimental data.

## Method

The author uses the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission, treating collisional energy loss as a perturbation, with a temperature-dependent running coupling $\alpha_s(Q,T)$ that has a plateau around $Q \sim \kappa T$ motivated by lattice results. The single free parameter $\kappa$ in the $\alpha_s$ parametrization is fitted to LHC data on $R_{AA}$ in 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, and then $R_{AA}$ for light nuclei is calculated without further free parameters. The QGP fireball is modeled with Bjorken 1+1D hydrodynamics, using the optical Glauber wounded nucleon model with a binary collision term to compute charged multiplicity densities, and the nuclear modification of parton distribution functions is included via LO EPS09 corrections.

## Key numbers

- For minimum bias C+C and O+O collisions, the difference $\Delta R_{AA}$ between the scenarios with and without mini-QGP formation in $pp$ collisions is $\sim 0.1-0.15$ at $p_T \sim 10-20$ GeV.
- The $R_{pp}$ factor (medium modification in $pp$ collisions) grows from $\approx 0.78$ at $p_T \approx 10$ GeV to $\approx 0.92$ at $p_T \approx 50$ GeV.
- For minimum bias C+C collisions, the ratio $|R_{AA}^{pdf}-1|/|R_{AA}-1| \lesssim 0.2-0.3$ at $p_T \lesssim 30$ GeV, indicating nuclear PDF uncertainties are not a strong obstacle.
- The initial QGP temperatures for minimum bias collisions (with $\tau_0=0.5$ fm, $k=2$) are $T_0=258$ (286) MeV for C, $263$ (291) MeV for O, and $261$ (289) MeV for Ne (ideal gas and lattice entropy values respectively).
- The midrapidity charged multiplicity densities in minimum bias jet events are $dN_{ch}(AA)/d\eta \approx 51.02$, 64.15, and 82.13 for C, O, and Ne nuclei respectively.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous global analysis (Zakharov, 2023) of $R_{AA}$ in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, which found that data for heavy ions could be described equally well with or without mini-QGP formation in $pp$ collisions, but that for 7 TeV O+O collisions a noticeable difference appeared. The present paper provides predictions for the newly accessible 5.36 TeV light-ion collisions at the LHC (CERN-OO536 experiment). The results enable experimental tests of whether jet quenching occurs in $pp$ collisions, a question motivated by observations of the ridge effect in $pp$ collisions (CMS, ATLAS), strangeness enhancement (ALICE), and anomalous multiplicity dependence of $\langle p_T \rangle$ (Campanini et al.).
