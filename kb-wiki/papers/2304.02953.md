# B meson production in Pb+Pb at 5.02 ATeV at LHC: estimating the diffusion coefficient in the infinite mass limit

> In the last decade a Quasi-Particle Model (QPM) has been developed to study charm quark dynamics in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions supplying a satisfactory description of the main observables for $D$ meson and providing an estimate of the space-diffusion coefficient $D_s(T)$ from the phenomenology. In this paper, we extend the approach to bottom quarks describing their propagation in the quark-gluon plasma within an event-by-event full Boltzmann transport approach followed by a coalescence plus fragmentation hadronization. We find that QPM approach is able to correctly predict the first available data on $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and $v_{2}(p_T)$ of single-electron from B decays without any parameter modification w.r.t. the charm. We show also predictions for centralities where data are not yet available for both $v_{2}(p_T)$ and $v_{3}(p_T)$. Moreover, we discuss the significant breaking of the expected scaling of the thermalization time $τ_{th}$ with $M_Q/T$, discussing the evolution with mass of $D_s(T)$ to better assess the comparison to lQCD calculations. We find that at $T=T_c$ charm quark $D_s(T)$ is about a factor of 2 larger than the asymptotic value for $M \rightarrow \infty$, while bottom $D_s(T)$ is only a $20-25\%$ higher. This implies a $D_{s}(T)$ which is consistent within the current uncertainty to the most recent lattice QCD calculations with dynamical quarks for $M \rightarrow \infty$.

## Key claim

The Quasi-Particle Model (QPM) approach, without any parameter modification from the charm sector, correctly predicts the first available data on the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of electrons from B decays in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 ATeV; however, the spatial diffusion coefficient for bottom quarks is only 20-25% higher than the asymptotic infinite-mass limit, while for charm quarks it is about a factor of 2 larger, indicating a significant breaking of the expected mass scaling of the thermalization time.

## Method

The authors employed an event-by-event full Boltzmann transport approach to describe bottom quark propagation in the quark-gluon plasma, using the same Quasi-Particle Model (QPM) interaction matrices previously applied to charm quarks. The bulk medium evolution was simulated with thermal massive quarks and gluons according to QPM, which reproduces the lattice QCD equation of state, while heavy quarks interacted via two-body collisions with gluons and light quarks. Hadronization was performed via a hybrid coalescence plus fragmentation approach, with bottom quarks that did not coalesce hadronizing through the Kartvelishvili fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- At T = T_c = 0.155 GeV, the charm quark spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(T) is about a factor of 2 larger than the asymptotic value for M → ∞, while bottom D_s(T) is only 20-25% higher.
- The ratio D_s(M_charm)/D_s(M → ∞) ≈ 1.9 in the QPM approach, compared to approximately 1.4 in pQCD at T = T_c.
- The thermalization time for bottom quarks in QPM is τ_th(b) ∼ 11 fm/c, significantly smaller than the 16.5 fm/c expected from a simple M/T scaling of the charm thermalization time of about 5 fm/c.
- The efficiency of conversion of initial eccentricity to elliptic flow v_2 for bottom quarks is about 15% smaller than for charm in 0-10% centrality and about 40% smaller at 30-50% centrality; for triangular flow v_3, the efficiency is about 30% smaller at both centralities.
- The initial eccentricities used were ε_2^(0-10%) ≈ 0.13, ε_2^(30-50%) ≈ 0.42, ε_3^(0-10%) ≈ 0.11, and ε_3^(30-50%) ≈ 0.21.

## Context

This work extends the Quasi-Particle Model approach, previously successful for D mesons (Scardina 2017, Das 2015), to the bottom quark sector at LHC energies. The study addresses the mass dependence of the spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(T), enabling a more appropriate comparison to lattice QCD calculations that are performed in the infinite mass limit, particularly the recent 2+1 flavor dynamical fermion results from Altenkort (2023). The authors explicitly quantify for the first time the breaking of the M/T scaling of thermalization time and show that once finite-mass effects are accounted for, the QPM D_s(T) agrees better with dynamical lattice QCD calculations than with older quenched approximations.
