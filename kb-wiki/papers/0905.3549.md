# Neutrino oscillations, global analysis and theta(13)

> At the previous Venice meeting NO-VE 2008, we discussed possible hints in favor of a nonzero value for the unknown neutrino mixing angle theta(13), emerging from the combination of solar and long-baseline reactor data, as well as from the combination of atmospheric, CHOOZ and long-baseline accelerator nu_mu->nu_mu data. Recent MINOS 2009 results in the nu_mu->nu_e appearance channel also seem to support such hints. A combination of all current oscillation data provides, as preferred range, sin^2 theta(13) = 0.02 +- 0.01 (1σ). We review several issues raised by such hints in the last year, and comment on their possible near-future improvements and tests.

## Key claim
A global combination of all neutrino oscillation data available in 2008–2009, including preliminary MINOS νμ→νe appearance results, provides a preferred range of sin²θ₁₃ = 0.02 ± 0.01 (1σ), representing a ~2σ indication in favor of a nonzero θ₁₃ rather than a definitive discovery.

## Method
The authors performed a global χ² analysis combining solar neutrino data (Super-Kamiokande, SNO phases I–III), long-baseline reactor data (KamLAND), atmospheric neutrino data (Super-Kamiokande phase I), short-baseline reactor data (CHOOZ), and long-baseline accelerator data (K2K, MINOS νμ disappearance and νe appearance). All three-flavor oscillation parameters were marginalized, and the Δχ² profile as a function of sin²θ₁₃ was computed for different data set combinations.

## Key numbers
- sin²θ₁₃ = 0.021 ± 0.017 (1σ) from solar + KamLAND data alone (2008 analysis)
- sin²θ₁₃ = 0.012 ± 0.013 (1σ) from atmospheric + LBL + CHOOZ data alone (2008 analysis)
- sin²θ₁₃ = 0.016 ± 0.010 (1σ) from all data available in 2008 (global combination)
- sin²θ₁₃ ≃ 0.05 ± 0.03 (1σ) from preliminary MINOS 2009 νe appearance data alone
- sin²θ₁₃ = 0.02 ± 0.01 (1σ) from all data including MINOS 2009, representing a ~2σ (95% C.L.) indication

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous global analyses (Fogli et al., 2008) and independent analyses by other groups (Schwetz et al., 2008; Valle et al., 2008) that found weak hints of θ₁₃ > 0 at the 1–1.5σ level from solar and KamLAND data. The inclusion of preliminary MINOS 2009 νe appearance results raises the combined significance to ~2σ. The results motivate near-future tests by the SNO and KamLAND collaborations (joint 3ν analysis), the Super-Kamiokande collaboration (full 3ν analysis of phases I–III), and upcoming reactor experiments (Daya Bay, Double Chooz) and accelerator experiments (T2K, NOνA) that could achieve a >3σ discovery and enable searches for leptonic CP violation.
