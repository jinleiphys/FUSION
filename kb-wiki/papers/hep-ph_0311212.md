# Chiral Dynamics: Theory and Experiment (CD2003)

> These are the proceedings of the fourth international workshop on "Chiral Dynamics: Theory and Experiment" which was held at the University of Bonn, September 8-13, 2003. The workshop concentrated on the various experimental andtheoretical aspects of chiral dynamics, including for the first time in this series detailed discussions of lattice gauge theory. It consisted of an introductory lecture, plenary talks, working group talks and working group summaries. Included is a short contribution per talk.

# Chiral Dynamics: Theory and Experiment (CD2003)

## Key claim
This is a conference proceedings volume, not a research paper presenting a single new result. The workshop aimed to survey the current experimental and theoretical status of chiral dynamics, with the novel inclusion of detailed lattice gauge theory discussions for the first time in this series.

## Method
The workshop consisted of an introductory lecture by John Donoghue, 20 plenary talks in morning sessions, and four parallel working groups in afternoons covering Goldstone boson dynamics, meson-baryon dynamics, few-body dynamics and hadronic atoms, and chiral lattice dynamics. Working group convenors summarized results on the final day. The proceedings contain one short contribution per talk, with about 90 working group talks and 140 participants from 20 countries.

## Key numbers
- 140 participants from 20 different countries attended the workshop
- 20 plenary talks were given in morning sessions
- Approximately 90 talks were presented across four working groups
- The workshop was the fourth in a series, following MIT (1994), University of Mainz (1997), and Jefferson Laboratory (2000), with the fifth planned at TUNL-Duke in 2006

## Context
This proceedings volume documents the fourth international workshop in a series that began at MIT in 1994, following earlier meetings at Mainz (1997) and Jefferson Laboratory (2000). The workshop extended previous discussions of Goldstone boson and meson-baryon physics to include few-body systems, hadronic bound states, and for the first time, detailed lattice gauge theory discussions. The proceedings enable quick access to the status of the field as of 2003, with individual talks available as PDF or PS files on the conference website.
