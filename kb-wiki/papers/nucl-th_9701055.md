# QRPA and its extensions in a solvable model

> An exactly solvable model is introduced, which is equivalent to the exact shell-model treatment of protons and neutrons in a single j-shell for Fermi-type excitations. Exact energies, quasiparticle numbers and double beta decay Fermi amplitudes are computed and compared with the results of both the standard quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and the renormalized one (RQRPA), and also with those corresponding to the hamiltonian in the quasiparticle basis (qp). A zero excitation energy state is found in the exact case, occuring at a value of the residual particle-particle interaction at which the QRPA collapse. The RQRPA and the qp solutions do not include this zero-energy eigenvalue in their spectra, probably due to spurious correlations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the collapse of the standard quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) correlates with the appearance of a zero-excitation-energy state in the exact solution of the model, but the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) and the quasiparticle-basis (qp) solutions do not reproduce this zero-energy eigenvalue, likely due to spurious correlations introduced by particle-number fluctuations.

## Method

The authors constructed an exactly solvable shell-model Hamiltonian for protons and neutrons in a single j-shell (j=19/2) with pairing, particle-hole, and particle-particle interactions, which is equivalent to an SO(5) algebra. They diagonalized this Hamiltonian exactly and compared the resulting energies, quasiparticle numbers, and double-beta-decay Fermi matrix elements with those obtained from the standard QRPA, the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA), and the Hamiltonian in the quasiparticle basis (qp), both in its full form and in a linearized version that retains only pair-creation and pair-annihilation terms.

## Key numbers

* For the chosen parameters (j=19/2, N=20, e_p=0.69 MeV, e_n=0.0 MeV, G_p=G_n=0.2 MeV, chi=0 or 0.025 MeV, 0 <= kappa <= 0.1), the exact excitation energy of the lowest 0+ state in the odd-odd intermediate nucleus (N_n=13, N_p=7) goes to zero when 4*kappa/G is approximately 1.3 for chi=0.
* The exact double-beta-decay matrix element M_{2nu} vanishes at the point 4*kappa/G = 1.
* The QRPA collapses (its eigenvalues become complex) near the point where the exact excitation energies become negative, which occurs for 4*kappa/G approximately 1.3-1.8 depending on the case.
* The average number of proton-quasiparticles in the ground state of the even-even nucleus (N_p=6, N_n=14) shows a saturation in the full-qp case for 4*kappa/G approximately 1.8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study using a schematic proton-neutron Lipkin model (Hirsch 1996), which showed that the RQRPA violates the Ikeda sum rule. The paper directly compares the exact solutions of a solvable model with the QRPA, RQRPA, and qp approaches used in realistic double-beta-decay calculations by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986), Engel, Vogel, and Zirnbauer (1988), and Civitarese, Faessler, and Tomoda (1987). The results enable a critical assessment of the RQRPA as a cure for QRPA instabilities, as proposed by Toivanen and Suhonen (1995), and highlight the need for additional work to clarify the role of spurious states in extended QRPA methods.
