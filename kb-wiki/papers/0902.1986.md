# Chiral Effective Field Theory for Nuclear Matter with long- and short-range Multi-Nucleon Interactions

> We derive a novel chiral power counting scheme for in-medium chiral perturbation theory with explicit nucleonic and pionic degrees of freedom coupled to external sources. It allows for a systematic expansion taking into account local as well as pion-mediated inter-nucleon interactions. Based on this power counting, one can identify classes of non-perturbative diagrams that require a resummation. Within this scheme, the pion self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter is analyzed and calculated up-to-and-including next-to-leading order (NLO). It is shown that the corrections involving in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions cancel between each other at NLO. As a result, there are no corrections up to this order to the linear density approximation for the in-medium pion self-energy.

## Key claim

The paper derives a novel chiral power counting scheme for in-medium chiral perturbation theory that simultaneously incorporates local multi-nucleon interactions and pion-mediated interactions, and shows that at next-to-leading order (NLO) the corrections involving in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions cancel each other, leaving the linear density approximation for the in-medium pion self-energy unchanged up to that order.

## Method

The authors developed a new chiral power counting for in-medium chiral perturbation theory by treating all nucleon propagators as order p^{-2} (non-standard counting) from the start, and by including both bilinear pion-nucleon vertices and multi-nucleon interactions mediated by heavy meson fields. They applied this counting to calculate the pion self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter up to NLO (order p^5), evaluating contributions from meson-baryon vertices, pion-loop nucleon self-energy diagrams, and in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The temporal weak pion decay constant in medium decreases linearly with density as f_t = f_pi (1 - (0.26 ± 0.04) rho/rho_0), where f_pi = 92.4 MeV is the vacuum value and rho_0 is nuclear matter saturation density.
- The in-medium quark condensate decreases linearly with density as 1 - (0.35 ± 0.09) rho/rho_0.
- The leading contribution to the pion self-energy from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term is Sigma_1 = (-i q^0 / (2 f^2)) epsilon_{ij3} (rho_p - rho_n), where rho_p and rho_n are proton and neutron densities.
- The NLO contribution from diagram 6 is Sigma_4 = (-2 delta_{ij} / f^2) (2 c_1 m_pi^2 - q_0^2 (c_2 + c_3 - g_A^2/(8m)) + c_3 q^2) (rho_p + rho_n).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium chiral perturbation theory framework of Annp (2002) and Prcoller (2002), which only considered pion-mediated interactions, by incorporating local multi-nucleon interactions from four-nucleon Lagrangians and heavy meson exchanges. The calculation of the pion self-energy addresses open problems in pionic atoms and the pion-nucleus optical potential, particularly the missing S-wave repulsion and renormalization of the isovector scattering length discussed by Chanfray (2003), Oset (2004), and Girlanda (2004). The result enables systematic inclusion of multi-nucleon forces in nuclear matter calculations and provides a foundation for future studies of pion properties in dense matter.
