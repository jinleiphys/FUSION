# Conserved charges in general relativity

> We present a precise definition of a conserved quantity from an arbitrary covariantly conserved current available in a general curved spacetime with Killing vectors. This definition enables us to define energy and momentum for matter by the volume integral. As a result we can compute charges of Schwarzschild and BTZ black holes by the volume integration of a delta function singularity. Employing the definition we also compute the total energy of a static compact star. It contains both the gravitational mass known as the Misner-Sharp mass in the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation and the gravitational binding energy. We show that the gravitational binding energy has the negative contribution at maximum by 68% of the gravitational mass in the case of a constant density. We finally comment on a definition of generators associated with a vector field on a general curved manifold.

## Key claim

The paper presents a manifestly covariant definition of a conserved charge from any covariantly conserved current in a general curved spacetime with a Killing vector, which requires no specific asymptotic conditions or subtraction of boundary terms; the definition reproduces known black hole charges and reveals that the total energy of a static compact star contains both the gravitational mass (Misner-Sharp mass) and a gravitational binding energy contribution.

## Method

The authors define a conserved charge as the volume integral over a time slice of the covariant current density \(Q(t) = \int_{M_t} d^{d-1}\vec x\, \sqrt{|g|} J^0(t,\vec x)\), where \(J^\mu\) is a covariantly conserved current. For systems with a Killing vector \(\xi^\mu\), they construct the current as \(J^\mu = T^\mu{}_\nu \xi^\nu\) using the matter energy-momentum tensor. They apply this formula to compute charges of Schwarzschild, Reissner-Nordstrom, and BTZ black holes by evaluating the volume integral of the energy-momentum tensor, which contains a delta-function singularity at the origin, and to compute the total energy of a static compact star described by the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

## Key numbers

* Schwarzschild black hole energy: \(E = \rho V_{d-2}\) where \(\rho = (d-2)M/(8\pi)\) and \(V_{d-2}\) is the volume of the Einstein manifold; reproduces known results.
* BTZ black hole mass: \(E = M/4\); angular momentum: \(P_\phi = J/8\).
* For a constant-density compact star in \(d=4\), the gravitational binding energy fraction \(F(R) = \Delta M / M(R)\) is a monotonically decreasing function from \(F(0)=0\) to \(F(R_*) \simeq -0.68\), meaning the binding energy can reach at most 68% of the gravitational mass.
* Reissner-Nordstrom black hole electric charge: \(Q_c = V_{d-2} (d-3) q\); at \(d=4\) for \(k>0\), \(Q_c = 4\pi q\).

## Context

This work extends the definition of conserved charges from covariantly conserved currents found in Fock (1959) and Trautman (2002), and improves upon the Komar integral (Komar, 1962) and the approach of Abbott and Deser (1982) for special backgrounds. The authors note that their definition differs from quasi-local approaches such as Brown and York (1993) and Balasubramanian and Kraus (1999), and from the Ashtekar and Das (2000) surface integral method, particularly in how it handles the cosmological constant and the energy of compact stars. The paper enables future applications to black holes with non-point-like singularities and to systems without Killing vectors via a proposed generic conservation equation.
