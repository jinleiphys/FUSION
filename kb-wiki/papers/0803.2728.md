# Multi-Pion States in Lattice QCD and the Charged-Pion Condensate

> The ground-state energies of systems containing up to twelve $π^+$'s in a spatial volume V ~ (2.5 fm)^3 are computed in dynamical, mixed-action lattice QCD at a lattice spacing of ~ 0.125 fm for four different values of the light quark masses. Clean signals are seen for each ground state, allowing for a precise extraction of both the $π^+π^+$ scattering length and $π^+π^+π^+$-interaction from a correlated analysis of systems containing different numbers of $π^+$'s. This extraction of the $π^+π^+$ scattering length is consistent with than that from the $π^+π^+$-system alone. The large number of systems studied here significantly strengthens the arguments presented in our earlier work and unambiguously demonstrates the presence of a low energy $π^+π^+π^+$-interaction. The equation of state of a $π^+$ gas is investigated using our numerical results and the density dependence of the isospin chemical potential for these systems agrees well with the theoretical expectations of leading order chiral perturbation theory. The chemical potential is found to receive a substantial contribution from the $π^+π^+π^+$-interaction at the lighter pion masses. An important technical aspect of this work is the demonstration of the necessity of performing propagator contractions in greater than double precision to extract the correct results.

# Multi-Pion States in Lattice QCD and the Charged-Pion Condensate

## Key claim

The ground-state energies of systems containing up to twelve π⁺ mesons in a finite volume can be computed with sufficient precision to extract both the π⁺π⁺ scattering length and a non-zero three-pion interaction, but only when propagator contractions are performed in greater than double precision; the results agree with leading-order chiral perturbation theory for the isospin chemical potential, though the three-body contribution becomes substantial at lighter pion masses.

## Method

The authors used mixed-action lattice QCD on coarse MILC gauge configurations with asqtad-improved staggered sea quarks and domain-wall valence quarks at a single lattice spacing of approximately 0.125 fm in a spatial volume of approximately (2.5 fm)³. They computed correlation functions for n-π⁺ systems (n = 1 through 12) by rewriting the contractions in terms of traces over 12×12 spin-color matrices, reducing the computational cost from factorial scaling to approximately 2000 operations per configuration. Ground-state energies were extracted from correlated fits and analyzed using finite-volume energy shift formulas at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the large-volume expansion, including the three-body interaction parameter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier letter (Beane 2007) which studied up to five pions and found indications of a three-pion interaction; the present calculation increases the maximum number of pions to twelve and uses an improved finite-volume formula at one order higher in the L⁻¹ expansion (Detmold 2008). The results enable future lattice QCD studies of multi-boson systems and provide a benchmark for chiral perturbation theory calculations of the pion gas equation of state, with implications for heavy-ion collisions and neutron star interiors where charged-pion condensates may form (Sawyer 1972, Scalapino 1972).
