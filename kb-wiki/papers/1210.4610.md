# Narrowing the uncertainty on the total charm cross section and its effect on the J/ψ cross section

> We explore the available parameter space that gives reasonable fits to the total charm cross section to make a better estimate of its true uncertainty. We study the effect of the parameter choices on the energy dependence of the J/ψ cross section.

# Narrowing the uncertainty on the total charm cross section and its effect on the J/ψ cross section

## Key claim

The authors show that by fitting the factorization and renormalization scales to existing total charm cross section data, rather than using the standard FONLL fiducial parameter set, the uncertainty band on the total charm cross section can be substantially narrowed, particularly at collider energies above 200 GeV; however, the best-fit scale parameters depend strongly on which data sets are included, and the resulting uncertainty remains asymmetric.

## Method

The authors performed chi-squared fits of next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations of the total charm production cross section in pp collisions to subsets of fixed-target data (beam energies 250-920 GeV) and RHIC data at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV from PHENIX and STAR, varying the charm quark mass between 1.18 and 1.54 GeV and the ratios mu_F/m and mu_R/m over wide ranges. They applied a penalty to the chi-squared for deviations from the PDG charm quark mass of 1.27 +/- 0.09 GeV, and used the resulting best-fit scale parameters to calculate J/psi cross sections in the color evaporation model.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters with m = 1.27 GeV for fixed-target plus PHENIX plus STAR (2011) data: mu_F/m = 2.1 (+2.55/-0.85), mu_R/m = 1.6 (+0.11/-0.12), chi-squared/dof = 1.16
- Best-fit parameters with m = 1.27 GeV for fixed-target data only: mu_F/m = 1.1 (+1.00/-0.40), mu_R/m = 1.6 (+0.13/-0.08), chi-squared/dof = 1.03
- PHENIX total charm cross section at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: 0.551 (+0.203/-0.231) mb
- STAR final (2012) total charm cross section at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: 0.797 +/- 0.210 (+0.208/-0.262) mb
- Using the final STAR 2012 value instead of the preliminary 2011 value changes the calculated upper limit on the total cross section by 0.77% at 200 GeV and 0.70% at 7 TeV, and the lower limit by -3.36% at 200 GeV and -12.65% at 7 TeV
- The central NLO cross section with m = 1.5 GeV and mu_F/m = mu_R/m = 1 gives chi-squared = 142.6 for the LHC points, while the fit including PHENIX and STAR (2011) gives chi-squared = 3.9 for the LHC points

## Context

This work extends previous uncertainty estimates on the total charm cross section by Vogt (2008) and Cacciari et al. (2012) that used a fiducial parameter set centered on m = 1.5 GeV with mu_F/m = mu_R/m = 1, which produced an uncertainty band too wide to have predictive power at collider energies. The authors show that the FONLL fiducial set gives a central cross section a factor of approximately 3 below LHC data at 2.76 and 7 TeV, while their fitted parameters bring the calculation into agreement. The results enable more precise predictions of J/psi production in the color evaporation model and demonstrate that the factorization scale uncertainty dominates the energy dependence of the total charm cross section, while the renormalization scale acts primarily to fine-tune the overall magnitude.
