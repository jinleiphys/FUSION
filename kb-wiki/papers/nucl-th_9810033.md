# Response function beyond mean field of neutron-rich nuclei

> The damping of single-particle and collective motion in exotic isotopes is a new topic and its study may shed light on basic problems of nuclear dynamics. For instance, it is known that nuclear structure calculations are not able, as a rule, to account completely for the empirical single-particle damping. In this contribution, we present calculations of the single-particle self-energy in the case of the neutron-rich light nucleus $^{28}$O, by taking proper care of the continuum, and we show that there are important differences with the case of nuclei along the valley of stability.

# Response function beyond mean field of neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim

The coupling of single-particle motion to collective vibrations accounts for only about 50% of the empirical damping of single-particle motion in stable nuclei, and this paper shows that in the neutron-rich light nucleus ²⁸O the imaginary part of the optical potential rises much faster near threshold (as (ω−ω_th)⁴) than in stable nuclei, while the real part produces a 330 keV positive shift that may push the loosely bound d₃/₂ orbital toward unbound.

## Method

The authors solve Hartree-Fock equations with the Skyrme SIII interaction on a radial mesh of 250 points with 0.1 fm step, then compute the imaginary part of the single-particle self-energy (Eq. ImV) by coupling the particle motion to 2p-1h doorway states, treating the continuum properly at the 2p-1h level for the first time in exotic nuclei. The transition densities δϱ(r) are taken from continuum-RPA calculations of the isoscalar 2⁺, 3⁻ and isovector 1⁻ strength functions, and the radial integrals extend to 25 fm with 0.5 fm step. The real part of the self-energy is obtained via a dispersion relation (Eq. ReV) and used to compute the energy shift of the neutron d₃/₂ hole state.

## Key numbers

- The highest occupied neutron orbital d₃/₂ in ²⁸O has a Hartree-Fock binding energy of about 1.1 MeV, while all protons are bound by more than 30 MeV.
- The imaginary part of the optical potential W at r=r′=3.2 fm follows a power law (ω−ω_th)⁴ near threshold, confirmed by a fit to the microscopic calculation.
- The real-part energy shift ΔE for the loosely bound d₃/₂ neutron hole orbital is +330 keV, compared with the unperturbed HF energy E⁽⁰⁾ = −1.1 MeV.
- This +330 keV shift results from a cancellation between two diagrammatic contributions: 930 keV minus 600 keV.
- The continuum-RPA strength functions are computed with energy bins of 1 MeV for ω_λ.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the quadrupole response in ²⁸O by Ghi (1996) that included both escape and spreading widths but were considered model-dependent; the present calculation treats the continuum properly at the 2p-1h level for the first time in exotic nuclei. The results confirm analytical expectations from Colo (1998) about the threshold behavior of the optical potential. The finding that the spreading width is as important as the escape width in ²⁸O, and that the real-part shift of 330 keV may affect drip-line predictions, raises questions about the reliability of simple mean-field methods for neutron-rich nuclei and motivates further work beyond standard RPA approaches such as second RPA.
