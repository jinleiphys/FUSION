# Coulomb Distortion Effects for Electron or Positron Induced $(e,e')$ Reactions in the Quasielastic Region

> In response to recent experimental studies we investigate Coulomb distortion effects on $(e,e')$ reactions from medium and heavy nuclei for the case of electrons and positrons. We extend our previously reported full DWBA treatment of Coulomb distortions to the case of positrons for the $^{208}Pb(e,e')$ reaction in the quasielastic region for a particular nuclear model. In addition, we use previously reported successful approaches to treating Coulomb corrections in an approximate way to calculate the Coulomb distortion effects for $(e,e')$ reactions for both electrons and positrons for the case of a simple nuclear model for quasielastic knock-out of nucleons. With these results in hand we develop a simple {\em ad-hoc} approximation for use in analyzing experiments, and discuss methods of extracting the ``longitudinal structure function" which enters into evaluation of the Coulomb sum rule. These techniques are generally valid for lepton induced reactions on nuclei with momentum transfers greater than approximately 300 $MeV/c$.

# Coulomb Distortion Effects for Electron or Positron Induced $(e,e')$ Reactions in the Quasielastic Region

## Key claim

Coulomb distortion effects in quasielastic $(e,e')$ reactions on medium and heavy nuclei can be treated with an improved ad-hoc approximation that permits Rosenbluth-like separation into longitudinal and transverse structure functions, but this separation requires a nuclear model and the ad-hoc procedure is not accurate in the wings of the cross section distributions, particularly for electrons at lower energies.

## Method

The authors extended their existing full distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) treatment of Coulomb distortions to positron-induced reactions on $^{208}Pb$ in the quasielastic region, using a relativistic $\sigma-\omega$ model for nucleons. They also developed an approximate treatment using a local effective momentum approximation with a parametrized elastic scattering phase shift function, and tested this against a simple harmonic oscillator "toy" model for the longitudinal structure function. The ad-hoc approximation replaces the angular-dependent phase factors in the distorted wave with angle-averaged values, enabling multipole expansion of the matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The static Coulomb potential at the surface of $^{208}Pb$ is of order 25 MeV.
- The approximate DW treatment reproduces exact DWBA results at about the 1-2% level near the peaks of $(e,e'p)$ cross sections for heavy nuclei such as $^{208}Pb$.
- The ad-hoc parameter $\Delta$ uses the value $a = -\alpha Z (16 \text{ MeV}/c / p)^2$, where 16 MeV/c was determined by comparison with exact radial wave functions.
- The phase shift parametrization uses nuclear radius $R = 1.2 A^{1/3} - 0.86 / A^{1/3}$.
- For $^{208}Pb(e_-,e'_-)$ at incident energy 310 MeV and scattering angle $\theta = 143^\circ$, the ad-hoc approximation agrees well with full DWBA, with longitudinal contribution at the few percent level.
- For $^{208}Pb(e_+,e'_+)$ at 485 MeV and $\theta = 60^\circ$, the longitudinal response contributes about 40% of the cross section.
- For $^{208}Pb(e_-,e'_-)$ at 383 MeV and $\theta = 60^\circ$, the electron DWBA result is approximately 15-20% larger than the Saclay experimental data.
- For $^{208}Pb(e_-,e'_-)$ at 224 MeV and $\theta = 143^\circ$, the electron DWBA result is in quite good agreement with Saclay data.
- The techniques are generally valid for momentum transfers greater than approximately 300 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Ohio University group's earlier DWBA treatments of Coulomb distortions for $(e,e')$ and $(e,e'p)$ reactions (Jin et al., 1992-1994) to include positron projectiles, and improves upon their earlier ad-hoc procedures (Kim et al., 1996-1999) which were not well-determined for the longitudinal structure function. The approximate method builds on the work of Knoll (1973) and Lenz and Rosenfelder (1971), and improves upon earlier attempts by Giusti and Pacati (1987) and Trani et al. (1988). The results enable investigation of the Coulomb sum rule through extraction of the longitudinal structure function from $(e,e')$ data on medium and heavy nuclei, though the authors note that a Rosenbluth-like separation is not the best procedure and suggest instead fitting normalization factors to a semi-realistic nuclear model.
