# Quarkyonic picture of isospin QCD

> We suggest a new look onto the thermodynamics of cold isospin QCD matter described within the quarkyonic framework, when confining forces are essential only for the momentum states close to the Fermi sphere and lead to formation of the quark-antiquark correlations with quantum numbers of pions. Within this picture cold isospin QCD matter is constituted by the Bose-Einstein condensate of pions and free (anti)quarks. We develop a simple confining model of pions existing as the lowest energy $P$-state of in-medium three-dimensional harmonic oscillator and use it to show that under the conditions of isospin QCD quarkyonic matter can onset at densities significantly smaller than the overlap density of pions. Using the experimentally established pion charge radius we demonstrate that the onset density can be even lower than two nuclear saturation densities. To analyze the lattice QCD data we develop a field-theory inspired model of quark-antiquark-pion matter with vector-isovector repulsion among pions and (anti)quarks. The quark substructure of pions, which is one of the key elements of the model, is accounted for within the one-loop no-sea approximation and leads to an effective medium dependence of the pion mass. The latter is shown to be crucial for providing asymptotic vanishing of the pion density and pion condensate, which reflects pion dissociation due to asymptotic freedom of QCD. The proposed quarkyonic picture of isospin QCD agrees with the results of perturbative calculations on reaching the conformal limit of QCD and with the data of lattice simulations, which supports its viability and physical relevance.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that cold isospin QCD matter can be described within a quarkyonic framework where confining forces are essential only for momentum states close to the Fermi sphere, leading to formation of quark-antiquark correlations with quantum numbers of pions; the onset density of this quarkyonic matter can be significantly smaller than the overlap density of pions and even lower than two nuclear saturation densities, contrary to previous estimates of about five nuclear saturation densities.

## Method

The author develops a simple confining model treating pions as the lowest energy P-state of an in-medium three-dimensional harmonic oscillator, with the oscillation frequency adjusted so that the mean quark-antiquark separation matches the experimentally established pion charge radius. A field-theory inspired model of quark-antiquark-pion matter with vector-isovector repulsion among pions and (anti)quarks is then constructed, incorporating the quark substructure of pions within a one-loop no-sea approximation that leads to an effective medium dependence of the pion mass. The momentum states of (anti)quarks are partitioned into free states below a Fermi momentum k_q and bound states in a shell between k_q and k_I, with the condition μ_π = 2μ_q enforcing equilibrium between pions and their constituent (anti)quarks.

## Key numbers

* Onset density of quarkyonic matter: n_onset = 0.23 fm^{-3}, derived from the harmonic oscillator model with pion charge radius R_π = 0.66 fm
* Onset isospin chemical potential: μ_onset = 238 MeV, obtained using leading-order chiral perturbation theory with m_π = 140 MeV and f_π = 92 MeV
* QCD energy scale extracted from the onset condition: Λ = 519 MeV, using n_onset = N_c N_f Λ^3 / (48π^2) with N_c = 3 and N_f = 2
* High-density asymptote of pion mass: M_π^2 → 4k_q^2 as k_q → ∞, derived from the polarization function in the no-sea approximation
* ρ-meson mass used in the model: m_ρ = 775 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept, originally proposed by McLerran and collaborators (2007) for baryon-rich QCD, to the isospin QCD regime for the first time with a constructed equation of state. It contrasts with previous approaches such as chiral perturbation theory (Son, 2000), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Barducci, 2004; Ebert, 2005), and the quark-meson model (Chiba, 2023; Kojo, 2024), which either ignore hadron dissociation at high densities or predict speed of sound behavior inconsistent with perturbative QCD results (Kurkela, 2009; Gorda, 2021). The model enables comparison with lattice QCD simulations of isospin QCD (Brandt, 2021; Abbott, 2023) and provides a framework for constraining the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter via the universal character of strong interactions (Fujimoto, 2023).
