# Color randomization of fast gluon-gluon pairs in the quark-gluon plasma

> We study the color randomization of two-gluon states produced after splitting of a primary fast gluon in the quark-gluon plasma. We find that for the LHC conditions the color randomization of the $gg$ pairs is rather slow. At jet energies $E=100$ and $500$ GeV, for typical jet path length in the plasma in central Pb+Pb collisions, the $SU(3)$-multiplet averaged color Casimir of the $gg$ pair differs considerably from its value $2N_c$ for a fully randomized $gg$ state. Our calculations of the energy dependence for generation of the nearly collinear decuplet $gg$ states, that can lead to the baryon jet fragmentation, show that the contribution of the anomalous decuplet color states to the baryon production should become small at $p_T\gtrsim 10$ GeV.

## Key claim

The color randomization of gluon-gluon pairs produced from splitting of a primary fast gluon in the quark-gluon plasma is rather slow under LHC conditions, meaning that for jet energies of 100 and 500 GeV over typical jet path lengths in central Pb+Pb collisions, the SU(3)-multiplet averaged color Casimir of the gg pair remains considerably different from the fully randomized value of 2N_c.

## Method

The author formulates an evolution equation for the color density matrix of the two-gluon system, describing the in-medium color randomization via the diffraction operator for scattering of a fictitious color-singlet four-gluon system on QGP constituents through two-gluon t-channel exchanges. The calculation uses the rigid geometry approximation with straight parton trajectories and describes the QGP as static Debye-screened scattering centers, with the evolution solved by expressing the diffraction operator in the t-channel basis where it becomes diagonal with eigenvalues given by dipole cross sections for each color multiplet.

## Key numbers

- For jet energies E = 100 GeV and E = 500 GeV, the SU(3)-multiplet averaged color Casimir of the gg pair differs considerably from the fully randomized value 2N_c for typical jet path lengths in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC.
- The contribution of anomalous decuplet color states to baryon production should become small at p_T greater than or approximately 10 GeV.
- For RHIC and LHC conditions, the formation length for induced gluon emission for soft gluons with energy omega approximately 3-5 GeV may be rather large, around 2-5 fm, with the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression factor typically 0.3-0.5 for a gluon quasiparticle mass m_g of about 400 MeV.
- The QGP production time tau_0 is approximately 0.5-1 fm for RHIC and LHC conditions.

## Context

This work extends the qualitative analysis of baryon production via anomalous decuplet color states performed by Zakharov (2017) (Ref. AZ_baryon in the text), which assumed fast color randomization of two-parton states in the QGP. The present paper provides a quantitative analysis of the color randomization rate, showing that the fully randomized approximation becomes invalid at sufficiently high energies. The results are relevant for understanding the role of color decoherence in in-medium gluon emission from two-parton antennas, studied by Iancu (2014), Salgado (2013), and others, and may inform Monte-Carlo models of in-medium parton cascading such as those by Wiedemann (2000) and the JETSCAPE collaboration.
