# Classical Gluon Radiation in Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions: Space-Time Structure, Instabilities, and Thermalization

> We investigate the space-time structure of the classical gluon field produced in an ultrarelativistic collision between color charges. The classical solution which was computed previously in a perturbative approach is shown to become unstable on account of the non-Abelian self-interaction neglected in the perturbative solution scheme. The time scale for growth of the instabilities is found to be of the order of the distance between the colliding color charges. We argue that these instabilities will eventually lead to thermalization of gluons produced in an ultrarelativistic collision between heavy nuclei. The rate of thermalization is estimated to be of order $g^2 μ$, where $g$ is the strong coupling constant and $μ^2$ the transverse color charge density of an ultrarelativistic nucleus.

## Key claim

The classical gluon field produced in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, previously computed in a perturbative approach that neglected non-Abelian self-interactions, is shown to become unstable when those self-interactions are included; the growth rate of the instability is of order the inverse distance between color charges, which the authors argue sets the time scale for thermalization of the produced gluon field.

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis of the perturbative classical gluon field solution for a collision between two color charges in SU(2) gauge theory, computing the determinant of the fluctuation matrix M(k,A₀) at a symmetry point halfway between the charges in the transverse plane. They solve the resulting sixth-order polynomial for complex frequencies ω to identify unstable modes, and examine the time evolution of the growth rate for times t > b/2.

## Key numbers

- The growth rate of the instability at early times t ∼ b is of order b⁻¹, where b is the transverse distance between the two colliding color charges
- The field energy density at the symmetry point drops as t⁻⁴
- For a constant color-magnetic field H, the instability growth rate is 2¹/⁴ √(gH)
- The growth rate scales approximately as Γ ∼ Λ^α with α ∼ 1/2, where Λ is the color field strength parameter

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and the perturbative classical gluon field solutions of Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995) and Yuri, Kovner, and Rischke (1995) by demonstrating that the non-Abelian self-interactions neglected in those perturbative schemes lead to dynamical instabilities. The results connect to earlier studies of classical instabilities in non-Abelian gauge theories by Gong (1990) and the decay of constant color-magnetic fields by Sikivie (1984), and suggest that thermalization of the classical gluon field may occur on time scales set by the transverse color charge density μ, enabling estimates of equilibration times in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
