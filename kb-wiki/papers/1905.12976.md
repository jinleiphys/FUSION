# Two-particle transfer processes as a signature of shape phase transition in Zirconium isotopes

> We explore two-particle transfer reactions as a unique probe of the occurence of shape coexistence in shape phase transitions. The (t,p) reactions to the ground state and to excited $0^+$ states are calculated for the isotope chain of even-even Zirconium isotopes starting from stable nuclei up to beyond current experimental limits. Two-particle spectroscopic factors derived from Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations are used, together with the sequential description of the two-particle transfer reaction mechanism. The calculation shows a clear signature for a shape phase transition between $^{98}$Zr and $^{100}$Zr, which displays coexistence of a deformed ground state with an excited spherical $0^+$ state. Furthermore, we show that there is a qualitative difference with respect to the case of a normal shape phase transition that can be discriminated with two-neutron transfer reactions.

# Two-particle transfer processes as a signature of shape phase transition in Zirconium isotopes

## Key claim
Two-neutron transfer reactions can discriminate between a normal shape phase transition and a shape phase transition driven by shape coexistence, based on whether the pair strength becomes fragmented across multiple states or remains concentrated in a single state. The calculation shows a clear signature for a shape phase transition between ⁹⁸Zr and ¹⁰⁰Zr that displays coexistence of a deformed ground state with an excited spherical 0⁺ state, but the authors note that the amount of particle-hole excitations in the microscopic calculation exceeds the simple 2p-2h picture.

## Method
The authors performed second-order DWBA calculations using the code FRESCO for (t,p) reactions on even-even Zirconium isotopes at 20 MeV, incorporating correlated sequential single-particle transfer through all intermediate states in the A+1 odd system, simultaneous transfer of the two neutrons, and non-orthogonality terms. Two-particle spectroscopic amplitudes from Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) calculations were used as nuclear structure input, with optical model parameters taken from Flynn (1975) and single-particle wave functions generated from a Saxon-Woods potential. The authors also compared the microscopic results with schematic Interacting Boson Model (IBM) calculations for both a sharp U(5) to SU(3) transition and a shape coexistence scenario with configuration mixing.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction ⁹⁶Zr(t,p)⁹⁸Zr, the largest two-particle transfer amplitude is 0.90 for the 2s₁/₂ orbit, while the 1d₅/₂ amplitude drops to 0.13 (compared to 0.86 for ⁹⁴Zr→⁹⁶Zr)
- For ⁹⁸Zr(t,p)¹⁰⁰Zr ground state, all listed two-particle amplitudes are approximately 0.0, while for the 0⁺₄ excited state the largest amplitude is 0.90 for the 1d₃/₂ orbit
- For ¹⁰⁰Zr(t,p)¹⁰²Zr, the largest two-particle amplitude is 0.55 for the 0h₁₁/₂ orbit
- The (t,p) reactions were calculated at E=20 MeV, with differential cross sections evaluated at the maximum around 40 degrees

## Context
This work extends the Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations of Tsunoda et al. (2014) that indicated shape coexistence in Zirconium isotopes with a sharp transition between ⁹⁸Zr and ¹⁰⁰Zr. The authors build on earlier IBM studies of shape phase transitions by Fossion et al. and schematic configuration mixing approaches, and compare with experimental data from Flynn (1975) for lighter systems up to ⁹⁶Zr(t,p). The results enable future experimental studies using two-neutron transfer reactions with rare-isotope beams to probe the structure of neutron-rich nuclei such as ⁹⁸Zr and ¹⁰⁰Zr, complementing recent experimental searches for shape coexistence in ⁹⁸Zr by Singh et al. (2018) and ⁹⁶Zr by Kremer et al. (2016).
