# 7Be(p,gamma)8B S-factor from ab initio wave functions

> Nuclear structure of 7Be, 8B and 7,8Li is studied within the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM). Starting from the high-precision CD-Bonn 2000 nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, wave functions of 7Be and 8B bound states are obtained in basis spaces up to 10 hbar Omega and used to calculate channel cluster form factors (overlap integrals) of the 8B ground state with 7Be+p. Due to the use of the harmonic oscillator (HO) basis, the overlap integrals have incorrect asymptotic properties. We fix this problem in two alternative ways. First, by a Woods-Saxon (WS) potential solution fit to the interior of the NCSM overlap integrals. Second, by a direct matching with the Whittaker function. The corrected overlap integrals are then used for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B S-factor calculation. We study the convergence of the S-factor with respect to the NCSM HO frequency and the model space size. Our S-factor results are in agreement with recent direct measurement data.

# 7Be(p,γ)8B S-factor from ab initio wave functions

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of the 7Be(p,γ)8B S-factor using ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) wave functions derived from the CD-Bonn 2000 nucleon-nucleon potential, but the S-factor results depend on correcting the incorrect asymptotic behavior of the harmonic oscillator basis overlap integrals using either a Woods-Saxon potential fit or direct Whittaker function matching, with the final S-factor value at 10 keV quoted as 22.1 ± 1.0 eV b.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations for 7Be, 8B, 7Li, and 8Li using the CD-Bonn 2000 NN potential in model spaces up to 10ħΩ (Nmax=10) for a range of HO frequencies, with selected calculations at 12ħΩ. They computed channel cluster form factors (overlap integrals) for the 8B ground state with 7Be+p, then corrected the incorrect asymptotic behavior of these HO-basis overlap integrals using two alternative procedures: fitting Woods-Saxon potential solutions to the interior (0-4 fm) of the NCSM overlap integrals under the constraint of reproducing the experimental 7Be+p separation energy of 0.137 MeV, and direct matching of logarithmic derivatives with the Whittaker function. The corrected overlap integrals were then used to calculate the 7Be(p,γ)8B S-factor, with continuum wave functions for s and d waves obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential model.

## Key numbers

- 7Be ground state energy: 33.972 MeV (Nmax=10, ħΩ=12 MeV) compared to experimental 37.6004(5) MeV; CD-Bonn 2000 underbinds by about 3-5 MeV
- 8B ground state energy: 32.367 MeV (Nmax=10, ħΩ=12 MeV) compared to experimental 37.7378(11) MeV; CD-Bonn 2000 predicts 8B unbound contrary to experiment
- 7Be point-proton radius: 2.342 fm (Nmax=10, ħΩ=12 MeV) compared to experimental 2.36(2) fm
- 8B point-proton radius: 2.487 fm (Nmax=10, ħΩ=12 MeV) compared to experimental 2.45(5) fm
- Dominant spectroscopic factor for 8B|7Be+p (I1π=3/2-, l=1, j=3/2): 0.960 (Nmax=10, ħΩ=12 MeV)
- Experimental 7Be+p separation energy: E0=0.137 MeV
- Whittaker function matching radius: Rm=4.05 fm for the dominant p-wave j=3/2 overlap
- S-factor at 10 keV: S17(10 keV)=22.1 ± 1.0 eV b (NCSM result combining both correction methods)
- S-factor values at 10 keV range from 16.81 eV b (Whittaker, ħΩ=15 MeV, Nmax=6) to 23.06 eV b (WS fit, ħΩ=11 MeV, Nmax=10)
- Asymptotic normalization constants from Whittaker matching: C1,3/2=0.704 fm^{-1/2} and C1,1/2=0.247 fm^{-1/2} (Nmax=10, ħΩ=12 MeV)
- Total inclusive cross section for 8B+12C at 936 MeV/nucleon: theoretical 99.66 mb compared to experimental 94±9 mb
- Total inclusive cross section for 8B+9Be at 41 MeV/nucleon: theoretical 82.96 mb (experimental given in arbitrary units)

## Context

This work extends ab initio NCSM calculations to the astrophysically important 7Be(p,γ)8B capture reaction, which is a key input for understanding the solar neutrino flux measured by SNO with ~9% precision, while previous theoretical predictions had uncertainties of ~20% (Adelberger 1998; Couvidat, Turck-Chieze, and Kosovichev 2003; Bahcall and Pinsonneault 2004). The calculation uses the CD-Bonn 2000 NN potential (Machleidt 2001) and corrects the HO basis asymptotic problem that previous NCSM calculations faced, enabling comparison with direct measurement data from Baby (2003), Seattle (Junghans et al. 2002), and indirect Coulomb dissociation experiments. The results enable future inclusion of three-nucleon forces to better reproduce experimental thresholds and provide a framework for ab initio predictions of radiative capture reactions relevant to nuclear astrophysics.
