# Two-particle photodisintegration of He-4: He-4(gamma,d)d, He-4(gamma,p)T, He-4(gamma,n)He-3

> Using a covariant diagram technique and the concept of a nucleus as an elementary particle we calculated differential cross-sections for two-particle photodisintegration reactions of the He-4. The only functional parameter was the vertex structure function, which described the "collapse" of a He-4 nucleus and the nucleon remnants. The interaction of a real photon was determined by the value of particles charge, since the electromagnetic form factors were calculated in the photon point. The inseparability property of the electric charge from the particle mass allowed us to match the energy-momentum and charge conservation laws in the interaction. Therefore, the requirement of gauge symmetry was immediately satisfied. The covariant amplitude of the process equaled to the sum of pole diagrams and regular part.

## Key claim

Using a covariant diagram technique with the nucleus treated as an elementary particle, the authors achieved a close fit to experimental differential and total cross sections for two-particle photodisintegration of He-4, but only when the regular (contact) part of the amplitude was included alongside the pole diagrams; the pole diagrams alone did not provide an adequate description.

## Method

The covariant amplitude for each reaction was constructed as the sum of pole diagrams (s-, t-, u-channel) and a regular (contact) diagram, with the vertex structure function describing the "collapse" of He-4 into fragments as the only functional parameter. Electromagnetic vertices were determined by particle charges and magnetic moments, with form factors evaluated at the photon point, and the regular part was derived from an integral over the derivative of the vertex function to enforce gauge invariance. Differential and total cross sections were then calculated for the reactions He-4(γ,d)d, He-4(γ,p)T, and He-4(γ,n)He-3 and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For He-4(γ,d)d at Eγ = 40 MeV (lab system), the calculated differential cross section shows a minimum at υ = 90° and maxima at υ = 45° and 135°.
- For He-4(γ,N)T reactions, differential cross sections were calculated and compared with experimental data at photon energies from 22.5 to 75.0 MeV (lab system).
- Total cross sections for He-4(γ,N)T were calculated over the photon energy interval from 20 to 44 MeV.
- The vertex function parametrization for the He-4 → dd channel used coefficients: a1 = 316.513, a2 = 0.484×10⁻³, a3 = -0.0138, a4 = 0.543×10⁻⁴, a5 = -6.21×10⁻⁸; b1 = -0.0272, b2 = 0.373×10⁻², b3 = 0.117×10⁻³, b4 = -0.6847×10⁻⁶, b5 = 9.696×10⁻¹⁰.

## Context

This work extends the covariant diagram approach developed by Kasatkin (2004, 2009) for describing electromagnetic interactions of nonlocal bound systems, applying it specifically to two-particle photodisintegration of He-4. The vertex functions were parameterized using the Urbana potential from Schiavilla (1986) and compared with Argonne parametrization from Zayats (1994). The results demonstrate that the regular part of the amplitude, which arises from the nonlocality of the bound state, is essential for reproducing experimental data, enabling future application of this gauge-invariant framework to other photonuclear processes.
