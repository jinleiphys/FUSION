# Isoscalar and Isovector spin response in $sd-$ shell nuclei

> The spin magnetic dipole transitions and the neutron-proton spin-spin correlations in $sd-$shell even-even nuclei with $N=Z$ are investigated using shell model wave functions taking into accout enhanced isoscalar (IS) spin-triplet pairing as well as the effective spin operators. It was shown that the IS pairing and the effective spin operators gives a large quenching effect on the IV spin transitions to be consistent with observed data by $(p,p')$ experiments. On the other hand, the observed IS spin strength show much smaller quenching effect than expected by the calculated results. The IS pairing gives a substantial quenching effect on the spin magnetic dipole transitions, especially on the isovector (IV) ones. Consequently, an enhanced isoscalar spin-triplet pairing interaction enlarges the proton-neutron spin-spin correlation deduced from the difference between the isoscalar (IS) and the IV sum rule strengths. The beta-decay rates and the IS magnetic moments of $sd-$shell are also examined in terms of the IS pairing as well as the effective spin operators.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an enhanced isoscalar (IS) spin-triplet pairing interaction, combined with effective spin operators, produces a large quenching effect on isovector (IV) spin transitions consistent with experimental data, but the observed IS spin strength exhibits much smaller quenching than the shell-model calculations predict, implying a differential quenching mechanism between IS and IV channels.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the full sd-shell model space using the USDB effective interaction for N=Z even-even nuclei (12C, 20Ne, 24Mg, 28Si, 32S, 36Ar). The IS spin-triplet pairing matrix elements were enhanced by factors of 1.1 or 1.2 relative to the original USDB interaction, and effective spin operators with quenching factors (e.g., f_s^IS = 0.9 for IS, and Towner's effective GT operator for IV) were introduced to account for higher-order configuration mixings, meson-exchange currents, and Delta-isobar coupling. The accumulated IS and IV spin-M1 transition strengths up to Ex=16 MeV were compared with experimental data from high-resolution proton inelastic scattering at Ep=295 MeV.

## Key numbers

- In 28Si, the empirical IV summed strength below Ex=16 MeV is B_exp(sigma tau: Ex <= 16 MeV) = 4.59 +/- 0.222, while the calculated USDB value is 7.34, yielding a quenching factor q_s^IV(eff) = 0.79.
- In 28Si, the calculated IS summed strength below Ex=16 MeV is B_cal(sigma: Ex <= 16 MeV) = 6.816 for USDB, decreasing to 4.562 for USDB3 (33% quenching), while the empirical value is 6.489 +/- 1.604.
- In 32S, the observed IS sum rule strength below Ex=16 MeV is B_exp(sigma: Ex <= 16 MeV) = 6.414 +/- 1.227, with a calculated value of 7.623, giving f_s^IS(eff) = 0.92.
- In 32S, the IV summed strength is B_exp(sigma tau: Ex <= 16 MeV) = 4.120 +/- 0.407, while the calculated USDB value is 7.993, yielding f_s^IV(eff) = 0.72.
- In 24Mg, the experimental summed IS strength below Ex=16 MeV is B_exp(sigma: Ex <= 16 MeV) = 5.061 +/- 1.166, compared to the calculated USDB sum of 4.256.
- In 24Mg, the experimental IV summed strength below Ex=16 MeV is B_exp(sigma tau: Ex <= 16 MeV) = 3.180 +/- 0.236, while the calculated USDB value is 3.855 (about 20% quenching).
- In 12C, the experimental B(M1) values from (e,e') are 0.0402 (IS) and 2.679 (IV) in units of nuclear magneton squared, while shell model with SFO gives 0.0131 and 2.515, respectively.
- In 12C, the (p,p') data give B(sigma) = 3.174 +/- 0.842 at Ex=12.71 MeV and B(sigma tau) = 1.909 +/- 0.094 at Ex=15.11 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-isospin response in sd-shell nuclei, following experimental findings by Matsubara et al. (2015) that IS quenching is substantially smaller than IV quenching for N=Z sd-shell nuclei, contrary to earlier theoretical expectations from Richter et al. (2008) that quenching of IS and IV spin operators should be similar. The paper builds on shell-model calculations using the USDB interaction of Brown and Richter (2006) and incorporates effective operators from Towner (1987) and Arima (1987). The results enable quantitative understanding of proton-neutron spin-spin correlations in ground states and have implications for astrophysical processes such as neutrino mean free paths in dense neutron matter and core-collapse supernova dynamics.
