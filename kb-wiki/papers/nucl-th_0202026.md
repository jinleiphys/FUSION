# Spectral Function of rho in Dense and Hot Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The nucleon-antinucleon polarization (Dirac sea) in hot/dense hadronic matter, mediated by a decreasing effective nucleon mass within the QHD-I model, makes the rho meson spectral function very sharp and shifted significantly toward low invariant mass, contrary to the hot pion gas effect which changes the spectral function only slightly. The result is conditional on using a point-like meson-nucleon interaction in QHD; inclusion of a phenomenological monopole form factor attenuates the magnitude of the mass shift.

## Method

The rho meson self-energy in medium is calculated from nucleon-loop diagrams using an effective QHD-I Lagrangian with both vector and tensor rho-nucleon couplings. The nucleon effective mass and effective chemical potential are determined self-consistently from the mean-field theory (MFT) of QHD-I, solving the coupled gap equations for scalar and baryon densities at finite temperature and density. The spectral function is obtained as the imaginary part of the retarded rho propagator after analytical continuation, with the divergent vacuum part of the nucleon-loop regularized by a phenomenological subtraction condition at the vacuum nucleon mass and free rho mass.

## Key numbers

- The peak position of the rho spectral function shifts from 0.77 GeV in vacuum to about 0.63 GeV in medium at baryon density rho_N/rho_0 = 0.1 and momentum k = 0.75 GeV.
- At low density (rho_N/rho_0 = 0.1), the rho meson width is already reduced to about half of that in vacuum.
- At high density (rho_N/rho_0 = 1.5), the spectral function approaches a delta-like function.
- The normal nuclear density is taken as rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The free rho mass is m_rho = 770 MeV; the free nucleon mass is M_N = 938 MeV.
- The QHD-I parameters used: g_s^2 = 109.626, g_v^2 = 190.431, m_s = 520 MeV, m_v = 783 MeV.
- The rho-nucleon coupling constants: g_{rho NN}^2 = 6.91, kappa_rho = 6.1.
- The monopole form factor cutoff for the rho-NN vertex is Lambda_rho = 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-density study of Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) on effective rho meson mass in nuclear matter to finite temperature, focusing on the spectral function rather than just the pole mass. It contrasts with the results of Rapp and Wambach (1999, 2000) and Rapp et al. (1997), which did not include the effects of decreasing effective nucleon mass; the present calculation finds a sharpening and low-mass shift of the spectral function, whereas those prior works obtained broader spectral functions from other mechanisms. The results enable further comparison with QCD sum rule approaches (e.g., Leupold, Klingl, Hatsuda) and with dilepton data from heavy-ion collisions such as those from CERES-NA45, though the relation to QCD sum rules in the low-density region remains to be clarified.
