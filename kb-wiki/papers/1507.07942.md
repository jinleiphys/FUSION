# Deep inelastic scattering cross sections from the gauge/string duality

> Differential cross sections of deep inelastic scattering of charged leptons from hadrons are investigated by using the gauge/string duality. We consider vector mesons derived from different holographic dual models obtaining a general expression. We focus on the strongly coupled regime of dual gauge theories for different values of the Bjorken parameter. We find new predictions which are particularly interesting for differential scattering cross sections of polarized leptons scattered off polarized vector mesons. We also carry out a detailed comparison of the moments of the structure functions with lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper derives general expressions for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) differential cross sections of charged leptons from polarized vector mesons using gauge/string duality, but the authors emphasize that the comparison with lattice QCD is only qualitative because the holographic dual models (D3D7, D4D6, D4D8) do not lead to gauge theories with exactly the same properties as QCD in the planar limit, and the calculations and existing phenomenology belong to different parametric regimes.

## Method

The authors construct the DIS differential cross section from the leptonic and hadronic tensors for spin-one hadrons, decomposing the hadronic tensor into symmetric and antisymmetric parts that yield eight structure functions (F1, F2, b1-b4, g1, g2). They then specialize to the strongly coupled regime of dual gauge theories using the gauge/string duality, considering three parametric regimes of the Bjorken parameter x: 1/√λ ≪ x < 1 (supergravity regime), e^{-√λ} ≪ x ≪ 1/√λ (excited string production), and x ∼ e^{-√λ} (strings comparable to AdS scale). The cross sections are calculated for unpolarized, longitudinally polarized, transversally polarized, and partially polarized hadron beams.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for unpolarized hadrons is given by dσ/(dx dy dφ) = (MEe^4)/(4π^2 q^4) { xy^2 F1 + (1-y)F2 }, where the b_i structure functions contribute zero because ⟨qq⟩_unpol = 1
- For longitudinally polarized hadrons, the cross section contains terms with b1 and b2 structure functions: dσ/(dx dy dφ) = (MEe^4)/(4π^2 q^4) { xy^2[F1 - (1/3)b1] + (1-y)[F2 - (1/3)b2] - H_l H_h (2-y)yx g1 + O(t) }
- For transversally polarized hadrons, ⟨qq⟩_TP ≈ 3/2, giving the factor ⟨qq⟩ - 1 ≈ 1/2 in the b_i contribution
- For partial longitudinal polarization with fraction H_L, the b_i contribution carries factor (2/3 - H_L^2); for partial transversal polarization with fraction H_T, the factor is -(1/3 - H_T^2/2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of DIS from scalar and polarized vector mesons at strong coupling (Koile 2011, Koile 2013, Koile 2014), which were inspired by methods developed by Polchinski and Strassler (2002) for DIS from glueballs in N=1* SYM theory. The holographic models considered include the D3D7-brane model (Kruczenski 2003) corresponding to N=2 SYM with fundamental quarks, the D4D6-brane model (Kruczenski 2003), and the D4D8-brane Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai 2004) which realizes spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The results enable qualitative comparison with lattice QCD data for moments of structure functions of the pion and rho meson, though the authors note that quantitative comparison is difficult because the calculations and lattice QCD belong to different parametric regimes.
