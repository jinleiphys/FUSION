# Calculations of Polarization Observables in Pseudoscalar Meson Photo-production Reactions

> In preparation for the extraction of pseudoscalar meson photo-production amplitudes from a new generation of complete experiments, we assemble the relations between experimental observables and the Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu amplitudes. We present expressions that allow the direct calculation of matrix elements with arbitrary spin projections and uses these to clarify sign differences that exist in the literature. Comparing to the MAID and SAID analysis codes, we have found that the implied definitions of six double-polarization observables are the negative of what has been used in comparing to recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper provides a consistent set of relations between experimental polarization observables and the Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu (CGLN) amplitudes for pseudoscalar meson photo-production, resolving sign ambiguities in the literature. The authors find that six double-polarization observables (H, L_x', C_x', C_z', O_x', O_z') as defined in the MAID and SAID analysis codes have the opposite sign compared to what is used in comparing to recent experimental data.

## Method

The authors derive explicit formulae for calculating matrix elements with arbitrary spin projections using helicity states related to z-axis quantization by rotations, then use these to evaluate all 16 polarization observables from the four CGLN F_i amplitudes. They compare their analytic expressions to those from the MAID and SAID analysis codes by downloading multipoles, constructing CGLN amplitudes via standard multipole expansions, and computing observables to identify sign differences.

## Key numbers

- 16 possible observables in single-pseudoscalar meson photo-production: the cross section (σ), three single polarization asymmetries (Σ, T, P), and three sets of four asymmetries for beam-target (BT), beam-recoil (BR), and target-recoil (TR) combinations
- 6 observables (H, L_x', C_x', C_z', O_x', O_z') found to have sign differences between the authors' expressions and those in the MAID and SAID codes
- 37 Fierz identities interrelating the 16 polarization observables, with corrected signs provided in an appendix
- C_z' constrained by angular momentum conservation to the value of +1 at 0 degrees, with recent CLAS measurements on K+Λ production at W=1680 MeV and W=1940 MeV clearly showing this asymmetry approaching +1 at 0 degrees, while MAID predictions approach -1

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Chew, Goldberger, Low, and Nambu (1957) for pseudoscalar meson photo-production and resolves sign ambiguities between two groups of prior expressions: those from Adel and Fasano (1994) and those from Drechsel and Tiator (1992) and Knochlein (1995). The corrected relations are essential for the current generation of "complete experiments" underway at Jefferson Lab (FROST and HDice experiments) and at facilities in Bonn and Mainz, which aim to directly determine photo-production amplitudes for channels such as KΛ and πN.
