# Employing Helicity Amplitudes for Resummation in SCET

> Helicity amplitudes are the fundamental ingredients of many QCD calculations for multi-leg processes. We describe how these can seamlessly be combined with resummation in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), by constructing a helicity operator basis for which the Wilson coefficients are directly given in terms of color-ordered helicity amplitudes. This basis is crossing symmetric and has simple transformation properties under discrete symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper describes how helicity amplitudes can be seamlessly combined with resummation in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) by constructing a helicity operator basis whose Wilson coefficients are directly given in terms of color-ordered helicity amplitudes; this basis is crossing symmetric and has simple transformation properties under discrete symmetries, but the matching equation (Eq. 6) holds exactly only at tree level, while at loop level the Wilson coefficients are given by the infrared-finite part of the renormalized QCD helicity amplitude.

## Method

The authors construct quark and gluon fields with definite helicity in SCET, using spinor helicity formalism for quarks and polarization vectors for gluons, and define fermion vector currents of definite helicity. They then build a helicity operator basis for specific processes (e.g., \(gg q\bar q H\)) and decompose the color structure using a color basis. The matching from QCD onto SCET is performed by equating the tree-level matrix element of the SCET hard-scattering Lagrangian to the Wilson coefficient, which at higher orders is given by the infrared-finite part of the renormalized QCD helicity amplitude after accounting for wave function renormalization and the renormalization factor of the Wilson coefficient.

## Key numbers

- For the \(gg q\bar q H\) process, the helicity basis consists of a total of six independent operators (Eq. 7).
- The color basis for \(gg q\bar q H\) has three color structures: \((T^a T^b)_{\alpha\bar\beta}\), \((T^b T^a)_{\alpha\bar\beta}\), and \(\tr[T^a T^b] \delta_{\alpha\bar\beta}\) (Eq. 10).
- The Wilson coefficients for \(pp\to H + 0,1,2\) jets, \(pp\to W/Z/\gamma + 0,1,2\) jets, and \(pp\to 2,3\) jets were obtained at (next-to-)leading order (cited as Moult (2015)).

## Context

This work extends the application of helicity amplitudes, which are fundamental to many QCD calculations for multi-leg processes, into the framework of SCET resummation. It builds on prior work by Moult (2015) on constructing helicity operators for matching, and enables the systematic construction of subleading-power operators as pursued by Kolodrubetz (2016), as well as matching between different SCET theories for describing nearby jets as in Bauer (2011) and Pietrulewicz (2016).
