# Gluon Bremsstrahlung in Weakly-Coupled Plasmas

> I report on some theoretical progress concerning the calculation of gluon bremsstrahlung for very high energy particles crossing a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma. (i) I advertise that two of the several formalisms used to study this problem, the BDMPS-Zakharov formalism and the AMY formalism (the latter used only for infinite, uniform media), can be made equivalent when appropriately formulated. (ii) A standard technique to simplify calculations is to expand in inverse powers of logarithms $\ln(E/T)$. I give an example where such expansions are found to work well for $ω/T \gtrsim 10$ where $ω$ is the bremsstrahlung gluon energy. (iii) Finally, I report on perturbative calculations of $\hat q$.

## Key claim

The BDMPS-Zakharov and AMY formalisms for gluon bremsstrahlung in weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasmas can be made equivalent when the BDMPS-Zakharov formalism is rewritten in terms of the elastic scattering rate rather than the gluon mean free path, which is zero in hard-thermal-loop resummed perturbation theory.

## Method

The author demonstrates that by reformulating the BDMPS-Zakharov formalism in terms of the elastic scattering rate Γ_el instead of the product of density n and cross-section σ, and by avoiding normalization by the mean free path λ, the BDMPS-Zakharov approach reproduces the AMY formalism in the uniform, infinite medium limit. The large logarithm approximation is tested by comparing next-to-leading-log (NLL) analytic results to full leading-order numerical evaluations of the g→gg bremsstrahlung rate. For the jet broadening parameter q̂, the author presents a perturbative calculation using the full form of the elastic scattering rate including Bose enhancement and Pauli blocking factors.

## Key numbers

- The large logarithm expansion to NLL is good to less than or equal to 20% for ω/T greater than or equal to 10, where ω is the bremsstrahlung gluon energy
- For ω/T approximately equal to 1, the NLL result differs from the full small-coupling result by roughly a factor of two
- For a purely gluonic plasma, the UV-regulated jet broadening parameter is: q̂_g(Λ) = [ζ(3) ln(Λ/(cT)) + ζ(2) ln(cT/m_D) - σ_+] × (9 g^4 T^3)/π^3, where c = 2 exp(1/2 - γ_E) and σ_+ = 0.386043817389949...
- Corrections to q̂ that are formally higher-order in coupling, of order m_D/T = O(g), are of order 100% for realistic couplings

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Zakharov formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné and Schiff, 1996-1998) and the AMY formalism (Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe) by proving their equivalence and removing limitations of each approach. The AMY formalism was previously limited to uniform, infinite-volume systems, while BDMPS-Zakharov implicitly treated plasma particles as static scatterers and normalized results by the gluon mean free path λ, which is zero in perturbation theory. The results enable calculations of gluon bremsstrahlung in non-uniform media using the AMY formalism and in systems with non-static scatterers using the BDMPS-Zakharov formalism. The perturbative calculation of q̂ extends earlier work by Caron-Huot (2008) and provides a benchmark for understanding the magnitude of higher-order corrections.
