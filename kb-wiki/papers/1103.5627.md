# E, B, μ, T Phase Structure of the D3/D7 Holographic Dual

> The large N_c N=4 gauge theory with quenched N=2 quark matter displays chiral symmetry breaking in the presence of a magnetic field. We previously studied the temperature and chemical potential phase structure of this theory in the grand canonical ensemble - here we, in addition, include the effect of an electric field which acts to counter chiral symmetry breaking by disassociating mesons. We compute using the gravity dual based on the D3/probe-D7 brane system. The theory displays two transition at one of which chiral symmetry is restored. At the other transition density switches on, the mesons of the theory become unstable and a current forms, making it a conductor-insulator transition. Through the temperature, electric field, chemical potential volume (at fixed magnetic field parallel to the electric field) these transitions can coincide or separate at critical points, and be first order or second order. We map out this full phase structure which provides varied computable examples relevant to strongly coupled gauge theories and potentially condensed matter systems.

## Key claim

The paper maps the full phase structure of a large N_c N=4 gauge theory with quenched N=2 quark matter in the presence of a magnetic field, now including an electric field parallel to the magnetic field; the electric field acts to counter chiral symmetry breaking by disassociating mesons, and the phase diagram in the temperature, chemical potential, and electric field volume shows that the two transitions (chiral symmetry restoration and conductor-insulator transition) can coincide or separate at critical points, and be first order or second order.

## Method

The authors use the D3/probe-D7 brane holographic dual, introducing three bulk fields on the D7 world volume: an embedding scalar L(rho), a gauge field A_t(rho) for chemical potential, and a gauge field A_x(rho) for the electric field and induced current. They solve the DBI action with a Legendre transform to handle conserved quantities (density d and current J), and classify phases by the three order parameters (condensate c, density d, current J) being on or off, scanning the 3D parameter space of temperature T, chemical potential mu, and electric field E at fixed magnetic field B and zero quark mass.

## Key numbers

* The singular shell radius at zero temperature is w_s = sqrt(E) R (in units where B=1), and at zero electric field it merges into the horizon w_s = w_H.
* The second order chiral symmetry breaking transition occurs at E ~ 0.2054 (in units scaled by B) at zero temperature and chemical potential.
* The first order transition between the chiral symmetry broken insulator and the chiral symmetry broken conductor occurs around E ~ 0.1 (in units scaled by B) at zero temperature and chemical potential.
* The maximal current for a given E at zero temperature and density is J = sqrt((1+E^2)E) (in scaled units), achieved by the flat embedding with theta_s = 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the T-mu phase diagram (Evans 2011) by adding an electric field parameter, and builds on prior holographic studies of electric fields in probe brane systems (Andy 2007, Hall 2008, Erdmenger 2008, Veselin 2008, Andy 2009, Mas 2009, Andy 2010). The results provide computable exemplars of phase structures relevant to strongly coupled gauge theories and potentially condensed matter systems, showing how the competition between B (favouring chiral symmetry breaking) and E, T, mu (favouring symmetry restoration and current formation) generates rich phase diagrams with critical points and transitions of varying order.
