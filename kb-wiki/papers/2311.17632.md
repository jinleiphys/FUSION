# Ellipticity of dilepton production from a hot and magnetized hadronic medium

> We study the azimuthal angle and transverse momentum dependence of dilepton production from hot and magnetized hadronic matter using $ρ^0$-meson dominance. The thermomagnetic spectral function of the $ρ^0$ is evaluated using the real time method of thermal field theory and Schwinger proper-time formulation. A continuous spectrum is obtained in which there is sizeable Landau cut contributions in the low invariant mass region as a consequence of finite background field. The emission rate of the dileptons is found to be significantly anisotropic in this region and the later effectively increases with the strength of the background field. In addition, we also evaluate the elliptic flow parameter ($v_2$) as a function of invariant mass for different values of magnetic field and temperature. We find that in low invariant mass region $v_2$ remains positive at lower values of $eB$ signifying that the production rate could be larger along the direction transverse to the background field. This behaviour is consistent with the angular dependence of dilepton production rate.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the low invariant mass region, the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) for dileptons from a hot and magnetized hadronic medium remains positive at lower values of \(eB\), indicating that the production rate could be larger along the direction transverse to the background field; this behavior is presented as consistent with the angular dependence of the dilepton production rate, but the authors note that the nonsmooth nature of the spectrum due to Landau level thresholds does not lead to any generic trend in the \(\phi\)-dependence of the rate.

## Method

The authors evaluate the thermomagnetic spectral function of the \(\rho^0\) meson using the real time formalism of thermal field theory and the Schwinger proper-time formulation, incorporating a constant external magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis. The one-loop \(\rho^0\) self energy is calculated from the interaction Lagrangian with pions, and the Dyson-Schwinger equation is solved using a tensor decomposition of the self energy in a thermomagnetic background. The dilepton production rate is then obtained via vector meson dominance, and the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) is computed from the azimuthal angle dependence of the rate at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant \(g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 20.72\) GeV\(^{-2}\) is used, calculated from the vacuum decay width \(\Gamma_{\rho\to\pi\pi} = 155.8\) MeV.
- The vector-meson--photon coupling parameter \(F_\rho = 156\) MeV is used in the dilepton production rate formula.
- Two representative magnetic field strengths are considered: \(eB = 0.02\) GeV\(^2\) and \(eB = 0.05\) GeV\(^2\).
- Two medium temperatures are considered: \(T = 130\) MeV and \(T = 160\) MeV.
- Numerical calculations include up to 500 Landau levels to ensure convergence.
- The total differential rate is shown for transverse momenta \(q_T = 0.3\) GeV and \(q_T = 0.5\) GeV, and for invariant masses \(M = 0.1\) GeV and \(M = 0.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of dilepton production from a hot and magnetized hadronic medium previously performed by Mondal et al. (2023) by investigating the angular dependence and ellipticity of the emission. It complements studies of dilepton ellipticity from a magnetized quark-gluon plasma by Wang et al. (2022) and addresses a gap in the literature, as such an analysis had not yet been performed for the hadronic phase. The results enable future work using magneto-hydrodynamic simulations to perform full space-time evolution of the rate and extract meaningful estimates of effective temperature modifications due to the magnetic field.
