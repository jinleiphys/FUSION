# The reaction $^2H(γ,π^0)np$ in the threshold region

> Neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the inelastic channel is studied in the threshold region from 142 to 160 MeV. The calculation is based on the use of the diagrammatic approach. Contributions from the pole diagrams, one-loop diagrams both with $n$-$p$ and $π$-$N$ rescattering as well as two-loop diagrams with simultaneous inclusion of these rescattering mechanisms are taken into account. We have found a reasonable agreement with a very simple calculation by J.C. Bergstrom et al. [Phys. Rev. C 57 (1998) 3203]. It is shown that the `effective' electric dipole deuteron amplitude $E_d$ is negative in agreement with a prediction of ChPT. We conclude that a deviation of about 20% from a ChPT prediction for $E_d$ discovered in an experiment on coherent $π^0$ photoproduction off the deuteron cannot be attributed to inadequate estimates of inelastic channel contributions. We suggest that $π$-$N$ rescattering is responsible for the deviation of the free-nucleon $p_1$ threshold amplitude from the value measured in that experiment

## Key claim

The paper shows that the approximately 20% deviation of the measured deuteron electric dipole amplitude \(E_d\) from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions in coherent \(\pi^0\) photoproduction cannot be explained by inadequate treatment of the inelastic channel \(^2H(\gamma,\pi^0)np\); instead, the deviation of the free-nucleon \(p_1\) threshold amplitude is attributed to \(\pi\)-\(N\) rescattering.

## Method

The calculation uses a diagrammatic approach for neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the inelastic channel, including pole diagrams, one-loop diagrams with \(n\)-\(p\) and \(\pi\)-\(N\) rescattering, and two-loop diagrams combining both rescattering mechanisms. The elementary \(\pi^0\) photoproduction amplitudes are parametrized using both ChPT and dispersion relation (DR) values for the threshold multipoles, while charged-channel amplitudes and higher-energy neutral amplitudes are taken from the Blomqvist-Laget model. The differential cross section is integrated over the final neutron momentum to obtain the angle-dependent cross section, with numerical integrations up to nine dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The threshold photon energy for the reaction is \(E_\gamma^{thr} = 142.2\) MeV, and calculations are performed up to 160 MeV.
- The ChPT prediction for the deuteron electric dipole amplitude is \(E_d \approx -1.8 \pm 0.2\) (in units of \(10^{-3}/\mu_{\pi^+}\)), while the measured value from SAL is \(-1.45 \pm 0.09\).
- The effective modification of the isospin-averaged threshold amplitude due to \(\pi\)-\(N\) rescattering is \(\Delta E_{0+}^{(+)} \simeq -1.1\) (same units), and \(\Delta p_1^{(+)} \simeq 4.1 \pm 0.6\), yielding a total \(p_1 = 13.1 \pm 0.6\) that agrees with the experimental value \(p_1^{exp} = 12.88 \pm 0.28\) from Bergstrom (1998).
- Parametrized differential cross section coefficients at 145 MeV: \(A_1 = 0.0043 \pm 0.0014\) \(\mu\)b/sr, \(A_2 = -0.0007\) \(\mu\)b/sr; at 160 MeV: \(A_1 = 0.095 \pm 0.012\) \(\mu\)b/sr, \(A_2 = -0.041\) \(\mu\)b/sr.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of \(\gamma d \to \pi^0 np\) at 200–400 MeV (Levchuk, 1996) down to threshold, and directly addresses the model of Bergstrom (1998) which used a simplified square-well deuteron wave function and effective range approximation. The calculation shows that the inelastic channel contribution cannot explain the 20% discrepancy between the ChPT prediction for \(E_d\) and the SAL measurement, but confirms that \(\pi\)-\(N\) rescattering renormalizes the \(p_1\) amplitude to match the experimental value. The results provide parametrized cross sections useful for experimentalists estimating inelastic channel backgrounds in coherent \(\pi^0\) photoproduction experiments.
