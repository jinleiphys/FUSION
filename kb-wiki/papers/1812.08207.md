# QCD Sum Rules Approach to the $X,~Y$ and $Z$ States

> In the past decade, due to the experimental observation of many charmonium-like states, there has been a revival of hadron spectroscopy. In particular, the experimental observation of charged charmonium-like, $Z_c$ states, and bottomonium-like, $Z_b$ states, represents a challenge since they can not be accommodated within the naive quark model. These charged states are good candidates of either tetraquark or molecular states and their observation motivated a vigorous theoretical activity. This is a rapidly evolving field with enormous amount of new experimental information. In this work, we review the current experimental progress and investigate various theoretical interpretations of these candidates of the multiquark states. The present review is written from the perspective of the QCD sum rules approach, where we present the main steps of concrete calculations and compare the results with other approaches and with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the application of QCD sum rules to the X, Y, and Z exotic hadron states, arguing that while many of these states can be described as either tetraquark or molecular configurations within this approach, the results depend on the specific interpolating currents chosen and the inclusion of higher-order perturbative corrections, which modify coupling constants more significantly than masses.

## Method

The authors perform QCD sum rule calculations for molecular and tetraquark interpolating currents describing charmonium-like and bottomonium-like states, including next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) perturbative QCD corrections. They assume factorization of four-quark correlators into convolutions of two-quark bilinear currents for the higher-order corrections, and apply stability criteria with respect to the Borel mass parameter, continuum threshold, and renormalization scale to extract hadron masses and couplings.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the $\bar DD$ molecular state at N2LO: $3898 \pm 36$ MeV, with coupling $170 \pm 15$ keV
- Mass of the scalar $S_c$ tetraquark state at N2LO: $3898 \pm 54$ MeV, with coupling $191 \pm 20$ keV
- Mass of the $\bar D^\ast D$ molecular state ($1^{+\pm}$): $3903 \pm 62$ MeV, with coupling $161 \pm 17$ keV
- Mass of the $\bar D_s D_s$ molecular state: $4169 \pm 48$ MeV, with coupling $167 \pm 18$ keV
- Mass of the $\bar D_s^\ast D_s$ molecular state ($1^{+\pm}$): $4188 \pm 67$ MeV, with coupling $156 \pm 17$ keV
- Mass of the $S_{cs}$ tetraquark state: $4233 \pm 61$ MeV, with coupling $187 \pm 19$ keV
- Charm quark running mass used: $\overline{m}_c(m_c) = 1261 \pm 12$ MeV
- Gluon condensate used: $\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle = (7 \pm 3) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^4$

## Context

This review extends the body of QCD sum rule calculations for exotic hadrons, building on the SVZ sum rule framework originally developed by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov, and on earlier applications to heavy-light systems. The work enables systematic comparison of molecular versus tetraquark interpretations for observed states such as X(3872), Z_c(3900), Z_c(4020), Z_c(4200), and Y(3940), and provides N2LO results that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations and experimental data from BESIII, Belle, and LHCb.
