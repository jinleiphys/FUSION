# Odd-even mass staggering with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> We have studied odd-even nuclear mass staggering with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory by employing isoscalar and isovector contact pairing interactions. By reproducing the empirical odd-even mass differences of the Sn isotopic chain, the strengths of pairing interactions are determined. The optimal strengths adjusted in this work can give better description of odd-even mass differences than that fitted by reproducing the experimental neutron pairing gap of $^{120}$Sn.

# Odd-even mass staggering with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

## Key claim
The pairing strengths in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory, when refitted to reproduce the empirical odd-even mass differences of the Sn isotopic chain (rather than the average pairing gap of a single nucleus, $^{120}$Sn), yield a better description of odd-even staggering across the isotopic chain. The optimal strengths for odd-even staggering differ from those that best fit binding energies themselves.

## Method
Self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed using the HFBTHO solver with five Skyrme energy functionals (SLy4, SkP, SkM*, UNEDF0, UNEDF1) in the particle-hole channel. In the particle-particle channel, isoscalar density-dependent delta pairing interactions (volume, mixed, surface) and two isovector pairing interactions (MSH and YS) were employed. Odd-A nuclei were treated using the equal filling approximation with a blocking energy window of $25 / \sqrt{A}$ MeV, and the minimum-energy configuration was taken as the binding energy.

## Key numbers
- Optimal mixed pairing strengths (MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$) for Sn isotopes: SLy4 = 310, SkP = 240, SkM* = 270, UNEDF0 = 225, UNEDF1 = 245.
- Optimal MSH pairing strengths: SLy4 = 400, SkP = 325, SkM* = 360, UNEDF0 = 300, UNEDF1 = 325.
- Optimal YS pairing strengths: SLy4 = 325, SkP = 260, SkM* = 290, UNEDF0 = 245, UNEDF1 = 260.
- Standard pairing strengths (fitted to $^{120}$Sn average pairing gap): SLy4 = 283.3, SkP = 213.3, SkM* = 233.9 (UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 not listed).
- For UNEDF0+Mixed at the optimal OES strength of 225 MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$, the root mean square deviation $\sigma$ of binding energies was 3.18 MeV.
- The neutron number range for Sn isotopes studied was 49 to 85.

## Context
This work extends prior HFB studies of odd-even staggering (OES) by Satula (1998), Xu (1999), Duguet (2001), and Bertsch (2009), which found that standard pairing strengths fitted to the average pairing gap of $^{120}$Sn (Dobaczewski, 1995) gave OES values too small for global comparison. The authors also compare with the pairing strengths fitted by Bertsch (2009) from overall systematics. The results enable improved global calculations of OES across the nuclear chart and show that isospin-dependent pairing interactions (MSH from Margueron, 2007; YS from Yamagami, 2009) flatten the odd-even mass differences as a function of neutron number, consistent with Margueron (2008).
