# Polynomiality sum rules for generalized parton distributions of spin-1 targets

> We present the polynomiality sum rules for all leading-twist quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of spin-1 targets such as the deuteron nucleus. The sum rules connect the Mellin moments of these GPDs to polynomials in skewness parameter $ξ$, which contain generalized form factors (GFFs) as their coefficients. The decompositions of local currents in terms of generalized form factors for spin-1 targets are obtained as a byproduct of this derivation.

## Key claim

The paper derives polynomiality sum rules for all leading-twist quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of spin-1 targets, such as the deuteron, by counting the number of independent generalized form factors (GFFs) that appear in the Mellin moments of these GPDs, with the caveat that the counting depends on the specific operator type (vector, axial vector, or transversity) and the Mellin moment index s.

## Method

The authors used the method of Ji and Lebed (2000) to count independent GFFs by matching the J^{PC} quantum numbers of local operators (which appear in the Mellin moments of bilocal correlators) with the J^{PC} quantum numbers of hadron-antihadron states in the crossed channel. For spin-1 targets, they enumerated the allowed J^{PC} sequences for two-boson states with total spin S=0, 1, and 2, then matched these against the J^{PC} decompositions of vector, axial vector, quark transversity, and gluon transversity operators to obtain explicit counting formulas for the number of GFFs as a function of the Mellin moment index s.

## Key numbers

* For vector operators (helicity-independent) of spin-1 targets, the number of GFFs follows the sequence 3, 7, 8, 12, 13, ... for s = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, ..., given by the formula N(s) = 3(1 + floor((s+1)/2)) + s.
* For axial vector operators (helicity-dependent) of spin-1 targets, the number of GFFs is N(s) = 2(s+1), producing the sequence 2, 4, 6, 8, ... for s = 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
* For quark transversity operators of spin-1 targets, the number of GFFs follows the sequence 5, 8, 14, 17, 23, ... for s = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, ..., given by N(s) = 5 + 3(s + floor(s/2)).
* For gluon transversity operators of spin-1 targets, the number of GFFs follows the sequence 6, 9, 15, 18, 24, ... for s = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, ..., given by N(s) = 6 + 3(s + floor(s/2)).
* For spin-0 targets, the vector operator yields GFF counts of 1, 2, 2, 3, ... for s = 0, 1, 2, 3, ..., matching existing literature on pion GFFs (Broniowski, 2008).
* For spin-0 targets, the quark transversity operator yields GFF counts of 1, 1, 2, 2, ..., matching results from Dorokhov (2011).

## Context

This work extends the polynomiality sum rules and GFF counting previously performed for spin-0 and spin-1/2 targets (Ji, 2000; Hagler, 2004) to the case of spin-1 targets such as the deuteron. The results enable future studies of the energy-momentum tensor for spin-1 systems, including the distribution of mass, angular momentum, and forces (shear and pressure) inside the hadron, where a curious feature is the appearance of two independent D-like terms in the second Mellin moment, contrasting with spin-0 and spin-1/2 cases. The authors note that this work could be extended to spin-3/2 nuclei such as Lithium-7 for studies of the polarized EMC effect.
