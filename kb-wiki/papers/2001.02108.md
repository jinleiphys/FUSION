# Frame dependence of transition form factors in light-front dynamics

> We study the radiative transitions between vector and pseudoscalar quarkonia in the light-front Hamiltonian approach, and investigate the effects of using different current component and different reference frames. In practical calculations with truncated Fock spaces, transition form factors may acquire current component dependence and frame dependence, and such dependences could serve as a measure for the Lorentz symmetry violation. We suggest using the transverse current with $m_j=0$ state of the vector meson, since this procedure employs the dominant spin components of the light-front wavefunctions and is more robust in practical calculations. We calculate the transition form factor between vector and pseudoscalar quarkonia and investigate the frame dependence with light-front wavefunctions calculated from the valence Fock sector. We suggest using frames with minimal longitudinal momentum transfer for calculations in the valence Fock sector, namely the Drell-Yan frame for the space-like region and a specific longitudinal frame for the timelike region; at $q^2=0$ these two frames give the same result.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in practical light-front calculations with truncated Fock spaces, transition form factors acquire spurious dependence on the chosen current component and reference frame; the authors suggest using the transverse current with the \(m_j=0\) state of the vector meson and frames with minimal longitudinal momentum transfer as a more robust procedure, but note that this recommendation is conditional on working within the valence Fock sector only.

## Method

The authors compute the magnetic dipole (M1) transition form factor \(V(q^2)\) for vector-to-pseudoscalar quarkonia (\(\mathcal{V} \to \mathcal{P} \gamma\)) using light-front wavefunctions from the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach, which includes holographic QCD and one-gluon exchange with a running coupling. They extract the form factor from different current components (\(J^+, J^R, J^L, J^-\)) and different magnetic projections (\(m_j = 0, \pm 1\)) of the vector meson, and evaluate the frame dependence by sampling over the longitudinal momentum transfer \(z\) and transverse momentum transfer \(\vec\Delta_\perp\) in the valence Fock sector under the impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\Upsilon\) (bottomonium) vector meson with \(m_j=0\), the dominant spin component proportion is 100%; for \(m_j=1\) it is 98.5%. For the \(\eta_b\) pseudoscalar, the dominant component is 96.6%.
- For the \(J/\psi\) (charmonium) vector meson with \(m_j=0\), the dominant spin component proportion is 99.9%; for \(m_j=1\) it is 95.9%. For the \(\eta_c\) pseudoscalar, the dominant component is 88.0%.
- For the \(\rho\) (light meson) vector meson with \(m_j=0\), the dominant spin component proportion is 98.8%; for \(m_j=1\) it is 84.2%. For the \(\pi\) pseudoscalar, the dominant component is 62.9%.
- At \(q^2=0\), the Drell-Yan frame and the specific longitudinal frame give the same result for the transition form factor.

## Context

This work extends the study of Lorentz symmetry violation in truncated light-front calculations, following earlier investigations by Li (2018, 2019) on transition form factors and by Brodsky (1999), Ji (2000), and Bakker (2003) on frame dependence of elastic and transition form factors. The results enable more reliable extraction of radiative transition form factors from valence-sector light-front wavefunctions, and the authors plan to include higher Fock sectors in future work to further investigate rotational symmetry in radiative transitions.
