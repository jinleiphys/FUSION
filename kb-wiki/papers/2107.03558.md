# What hath Weinberg wrought? Reflections on what Weinberg's papers on 'Nuclear Forces from Chiral Lagrangians' did and did not accomplish

> I discuss selected legacies of Weinberg's application of chiral Lagrangians to nuclear physics: (1) the use of the chiral expansion to organize the interaction of pions and photons with a nucleus; (2) the much-debated question of why and how the potential derived from a chiral Lagrangian should be inserted in the Schrodinger equation; (3) the emergence of "pionless EFT" as a tool for diagnosing universal correlations that are present in quantum few-body systems of very different sizes, and, perhaps most important of all, (4) an epistemological shift in what is expected of a nuclear-physics calculation.

## Key claim

Weinberg's application of chiral Lagrangians to nuclear physics launched the chiral effective field theory (χEFT) paradigm, but the paper argues that several foundational issues remain unresolved: the justification for iterating the NN potential in the Schrödinger equation is not fully established, and the counting of the nucleon mass to justify iteration is "rather unsatisfactory."

## Method

The paper is a reflective review that examines four legacies of Weinberg's work: (1) using the chiral expansion to organize pion and photon interactions with nuclei, illustrated through pion-deuteron scattering and Compton scattering on deuterium; (2) analyzing the formal justification for why the χEFT-derived potential should be iterated (i.e., inserted into the Schrödinger equation) rather than treated perturbatively; (3) tracing how Weinberg's toy model for singular potentials evolved into "pionless EFT" as a tool for universal few-body physics; (4) assessing the epistemological shift in expectations for nuclear-physics calculations. The author uses specific examples from Weinberg (1990, 1991, 1992) and subsequent work by Kaplan, Savage, Wise (1998), Birse (2005), and others.

## Key numbers

- Weinberg's prediction for the pion-deuteron scattering length was −0.05 m_π^{-1}, consistent with the experimental value of −0.056 ± 0.009 m_π^{-1}
- The scale Λ_NN ≡ 16π f_π^2/(g_A^2 M_N) ≈ 300 MeV, below which one-pion exchange can be treated perturbatively and above which it must be iterated
- In the ^3P_0 channel of NN scattering, Λ_NN is of order 150 MeV, i.e., comparable to m_π
- NN scattering lengths: 5.4 fm in the ^3S_1 channel and −23 fm in the ^1S_0 channel, both markedly larger than 1/m_π
- The triton binding energy from local NN potentials that reproduced NN data with χ^2/d.o.f. ≈ 1 fell short by 10%

## Context

This paper reflects on the legacy of Weinberg's three papers from 1990–1992 that launched χEFT for nuclear forces, extending the earlier meson-exchange phenomenology (e.g., Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force from Ellis (1984), Urbana-IX from Carlson (1983)) that lacked systematic organization. The work discusses how χEFT enabled consistent construction of NN and three-nucleon forces, and how the subsequent development of pionless EFT by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999) provided a rigorously renormalized framework for few-body systems with large scattering lengths. The paper identifies unresolved questions about power counting and iteration of the potential that remain open for future work.
