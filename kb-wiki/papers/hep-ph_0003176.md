# Does the quark-gluon plasma contain stable hadronic bubbles?

> We calculate the thermodynamic potential of bubbles of hadrons embedded in quark-gluon plasma, and of droplets of quark-gluon plasma embedded in hadron phase. This is a generalization of our previous results to the case of non-zero chemical potentials. As in the zero chemical potential case, we find that a quark-gluon plasma in thermodynamic equilibrium may contain stable bubbles of hadrons of radius $R \simeq 1$ fm. The calculations are performed within the MIT Bag model, using an improved multiple reflection expansion. The results are of relevance for neutron star phenomenology and for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Within the MIT Bag model using an improved multiple reflection expansion, a quark-gluon plasma in thermodynamic equilibrium may contain stable bubbles of hadrons of radius approximately 1 fm even at non-zero chemical potentials, though the minimum in the thermodynamic potential is less pronounced than in the zero chemical potential case and the effect may be model-dependent.

## Method

The authors calculate the thermodynamic potential of hadron bubbles embedded in quark-gluon plasma and of quark-gluon plasma droplets embedded in hadron phase, generalizing their previous zero-chemical-potential work to finite chemical potentials. They use the MIT Bag model with three quark flavors (massless u and d, s quark mass 150 MeV) and a modified multiple reflection expansion (MMRE) for the gluon density of states, which truncates the density of states to avoid negative values present in the standard MRE. For the hadron phase, all hadrons with masses below 1.2 GeV are included with a volume-only density of states, and an excluded volume correction from Hagedorn and Rafelski (1981) is applied to the hadron gas pressure.

## Key numbers

- Hadron bubble radius at the thermodynamic potential minimum: R approximately 1 fm, found for several points on the phase equilibrium line
- s quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV
- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential: T = 150 MeV, giving a Bag constant B = (221 MeV)^4
- Phase equilibrium points studied include (mu_q, T) = (320, 106.1) MeV, (450, 41.0) MeV, and (470.0, 3.28) MeV for equal quark chemical potentials
- For mu_u = 100 MeV, mu_s = 400 MeV, the phase equilibrium temperature is T_0 = 119.5 MeV; for mu_u = 400 MeV, mu_s = 100 MeV, T_0 = 100.0 MeV
- Mean number of quarks in a hadron bubble of radius R = 1 fm is typically 3-4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Neergaard and Madsen, 1999) from zero chemical potential to finite chemical potentials, making the results relevant for neutron star phenomenology and ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. It confirms and generalizes the earlier finding by Mardor and Svetitsky (1991) that hadron bubbles in QGP can have a minimum in free energy at finite radius, contrary to standard nucleation theory where an energy barrier must be overcome. The results suggest that if QGP forms in heavy ion collisions, it would contain pre-formed hadronic bubbles that could blur plasma signatures, though the authors note that lattice QCD calculations would be needed to confirm whether the effect is physical or a model artifact.
