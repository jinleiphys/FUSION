# Baryon Stopping at HERA: Evidence for Gluonic Mechanism

> Recent results from the H1 experiment [1] have confirmed the existence of a substantial baryon asymmetry of the proton sea at small $x$ with magnitude predicted in hep-ph/9607486. This is a strong support for the idea [3] that baryon number can be transferred by gluons through a large rapidity interval without attenuation. In this paper we calculate the dependence of baryon asymmetry on associated multiplicity of produced hadrons which turns out to be very sensitive to the underlying dynamics. Comparison with data [1] confirms the dominance of the gluonic mechanism of baryon number transfer and excludes any substantial contribution of valence quark exchange.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed baryon asymmetry in photon-proton collisions at HERA is dominated by a gluonic mechanism of baryon number transfer, which is rapidity-independent, and that the valence quark exchange mechanism, which would produce a constant asymmetry with multiplicity, is excluded by the data. The hedge is that the magnitude of the asymmetry alone could not distinguish between the two mechanisms; it is the dependence on associated particle multiplicity that discriminates them.

## Method

The authors calculated the dependence of the baryon asymmetry on the associated multiplicity of charged hadrons using the AGK cutting rules, modeling the multiplicity distribution with a double-Pomeron contribution. They assumed that the mean associated multiplicity for net baryon number production is 5/4 times larger for the gluonic mechanism than for baryon-symmetric events, while it is equal for the valence quark exchange mechanism. They then compared their predicted asymmetry as a function of multiplicity to the H1 data.

## Key numbers

- Baryon asymmetry measured by H1: \(A = (8.0 \pm 1.0 \pm 2.5)\%\) for protons and antiprotons with small momentum in the laboratory frame in \(\gamma p\) collisions at HERA.
- The asymptotic annihilation cross section for \(\bar pp\) is \(\sigma_{ann}(\bar pp) \approx 1.5 \pm 0.1\) mb, which is nearly energy independent.
- The mean multiplicity of produced particles corresponding to one cut pomeron is \(\langle n \rangle = 5.2\), and the weight of the double-pomeron contribution is \(\delta = 0.02\).
- The mean associated multiplicity for net baryon number production is \(K = 5/4\) times larger for the gluonic mechanism and \(K = 1\) for the valence quark exchange mechanism.

## Context

This work extends the prediction of a gluonic mechanism for baryon number transfer, first suggested by Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1991), which provides a rapidity-independent probability of baryon stopping. It contrasts with the valence quark exchange mechanism, which was previously used to explain baryon asymmetry in \(pp\) collisions at the ISR (1970s-1980s) and in heavy ion collisions at the SPS. The results enable future experiments to use multiplicity dependence as a diagnostic tool to identify the dominant mechanism of baryon number transfer in high-energy collisions.
