# Progress in the Nucleon Electric Dipole Moment Calculations in Lattice QCD

> Electric dipole moments (EDMs), which are sought as evidence of CP violation, require lattice calculations to connect constraints from experiments to limits on the strong CP violation within QCD or CP violation in new physics beyond the standard model. Nucleon EDM calculations on a lattice are notoriously hard due to large statistical noise, chiral symmetry violating effects, and potential mixing of the EDM and the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon. In this report, details of ongoing lattice calculations of proton and neutron EDMs induced by the QCD $θ$-term and the quark chromo-EDM, the lowest-dimension effective CP-violating quark-gluon interaction are presented. Our calculation employs chiral-symmetric fermion discretization. An assessment of feasibility of nucleon EDM calculations at the physical point is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that after correcting for a previously overlooked parity-mixing effect, all earlier lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon electric dipole moment (EDM) induced by the QCD theta-term become consistent with zero within uncertainties; the authors present a very preliminary ballpark estimate for the neutron EDM form factor that should be taken with a 100% uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon electric dipole form factors (EDFF) using lattice QCD with chiral-symmetric (Mobius domain wall) fermions on ensembles generated by the RBC/UKQCD collaboration. They introduce CP-odd interactions (the QCD theta-term and the quark chromo-EDM) as first-order perturbations to nucleon correlation functions, and extract the EDFF F3 from three-point functions using the vector current. For the theta-term, they restrict the topological charge sampling to a cylindrical spacetime volume around the nucleon to reduce statistical noise, and they explicitly account for the parity-mixing angle alpha_5 that contaminates the EDM extraction.

## Key numbers

* The heavy-pion ensemble has m_pi = 340(2) MeV, lattice spacing a = 0.1105(6) fm, and nucleon mass m_N = 1.178(10) GeV; the physical-point ensemble has m_pi = 139.2(4) MeV, a = 0.1141(3) fm, and m_N = 0.945(6) GeV.
* A very preliminary ballpark estimate for the neutron EDFF induced by the theta-term at the heavy pion mass is hat_F3n(0) approximately 0.05, with a 100% uncertainty.
* Leading-order chiral extrapolation to the physical point yields hat_F3n_phys approximately 0.01, corresponding to |d_n_phys| approximately 0.001 e.fm.
* At the physical point with approximately 33,000 statistical samples, the neutron EDFF signal is consistent with zero with statistical uncertainty delta_F3n = 0.05 to 0.10, requiring a factor of 5 to 10 improvement in signal-to-noise ratio (25 to 100 times more statistics).
* The corrected value from earlier Wilson fermion calculations (Guo 2015) constrains |hat_F3(0)| less than 0.06 at m_pi approximately 360 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculations of nucleon EDMs by correcting a systematic error from parity mixing that affected all previous calculations (Shintani 2005, Berruto 2005, Aoki 2008, Guo 2015, Shindler 2015, Alexandrou 2015, Shintani 2015); after correction those earlier results become consistent with zero. The paper enables future constraints on the QCD theta-term and on CP-violating new physics from next-generation EDM experiments, but notes that direct calculations at the physical pion mass are at the limit of current computing capabilities and may require dynamical theta-term simulations (Aoki 2008) or chiral perturbation theory extrapolations.
