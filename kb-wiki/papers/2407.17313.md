# Probing collectivity in heavy-ion collisions with fluctuations of the $p_T$ spectrum

> Event-by-event fluctuations in the initial stages of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions depend little on rapidity. The hydrodynamic expansion which occurs in later stages then gives rise to correlations among outgoing particles which depend weakly on their relative rapidity. Azimuthal correlations, through which anisotropic flow ($v_n(p_T)$) is defined, have been the most studied. Here we study a new observable introduced in 2020 by Schenke, Shen and Teaney and dubbed $v_0(p_T)$, which quantifies the relative change in the $p_T$ spectrum induced by a fluctuation. We describe how it can be measured. Using hydrodynamic simulations, we make quantitative predictions for $v_0(p_T)$ of charged and identified hadrons. We then discuss how $v_0(p_T)$ relates to two phenomena which have been measured: The increase of the mean transverse momentum in ultracentral collisions, and the event-by-event fluctuations of the transverse momentum per particle $[ p_T]$. We show that $v_0(p_T)$ determines the dependence of these quantities on the $p_T$ cuts implemented in the analysis. We quantitatively explain the rise of $σ_{p_T}$ observed by ATLAS as the upper $p_T$-cut is increased from $2$ to $5$~GeV/$c$.

# Probing collectivity in heavy-ion collisions with fluctuations of the $p_T$ spectrum

## Key claim

The observable $v_0(p_T)$, which quantifies the relative change in the $p_T$ spectrum induced by a fluctuation, is driven primarily by initial temperature fluctuations and determines the dependence of mean transverse momentum and its fluctuations on the $p_T$ cuts used in experimental analyses; the scaled quantity $v_0(p_T)/v_0$ is largely independent of system size, centrality, and initial-state fluctuations at a given collision energy.

## Method

The authors perform two sets of hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV using the MUSIC code with TRENTo initial conditions: one set with 100 fluctuating initial density profiles evolved through hydrodynamics, and a second set using a smooth initial profile obtained by averaging over $10^4$ fluctuating conditions, where two calculations differing by a global factor of 1.05 in initial entropy density isolate the effect of temperature fluctuations. They implement both ideal and viscous hydrodynamics with constant shear viscosity over entropy ratios $\eta/s=0.08$ or $0.16$ and a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity parametrization, with particlization via the Cooper-Frye prescription at $T=130$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The standard deviation of dynamical fluctuations of transverse momentum per particle, $\sigma_{p_T}$, is of order $1\%$ in central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
- The hydrodynamic calculation reproduces quantitatively the increase by a factor $\sim 2$ of $\sigma_{p_T}$ observed by ATLAS as the upper $p_T$-cut is raised from $2$ to $5$ GeV/$c$.
- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at proper time $\tau_0=0.4$ fm/$c$.
- The initial entropy density fluctuation parameter is $k=2.5$.
- The freeze-out temperature is $T=130$ MeV, corresponding to an energy density of $0.18$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- The smooth initial condition calculations use a global factor of $1.05$ to create two events with different $[p_T]$.
- The collision energy for the main simulations is $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV.
- The PHENIX data comparison uses Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV, with a lower momentum cut of $(p_T)_{\min}=0.2$ GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the observable $v_0(p_T)$ introduced by Schenke, Shen and Teaney (2020) to quantify event-by-event fluctuations of the $p_T$ spectrum in heavy-ion collisions. It relates $v_0(p_T)$ to two previously measured phenomena: the increase of mean transverse momentum in ultracentral collisions studied by Gardim (2019), Nijs (2023), and CMS (2024), and the event-by-event fluctuations of transverse momentum per particle measured by ATLAS (2022) and PHENIX (2003). The paper shows that $v_0(p_T)$ determines how these quantities depend on experimental $p_T$ cuts, and demonstrates that the scaled observable $v_0(p_T)/v_0$ is largely independent of initial-state modeling and centrality, similar to scaled anisotropic flow $v_n(p_T)/v_n$ studied by ATLAS (2018) and scaled $p_T$ spectra studied by Muncinelli (2024).
