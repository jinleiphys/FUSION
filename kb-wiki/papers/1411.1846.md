# Thomas-Ehrman effect in a three-body model: $^{16}$Ne case

> The dynamic mechanism of the Thomas-Ehrman shift is studied in three-cluster systems by example of $^{16}$Ne and $^{16}$C isobaric mirror partners. We predict configuration mixings for $0^+$ and $2^+$ states in $^{16}$Ne and $^{16}$C. Large isospin symmetry breaking on the level of wave function component weights is demonstrated for these states and discussed as three-body mechanism of Thomas-Ehrman shift. It is shown that the description of the Coulomb displacement energies requires a consistency among three parameters: the $^{16}$Ne decay energy $E_T$, the $^{15}$F ground state energy $E_r$, and the configuration mixing parameters for the $^{16}$Ne/$^{16}$C $0^+$ and $2^+$ states. Basing on this analysis we infer the $^{15}$F $1/2^+$ ground state energy to be $E_r=1.39-1.42$ MeV.

## Key claim

The Thomas-Ehrman shift in the three-body systems \(^{16}\)Ne and \(^{16}\)C arises not only from the conventional static effect of different radial extents of \(s\)-wave and \(d\)-wave orbitals, but also from a dynamic three-body mechanism that significantly alters the configuration mixing weights between the mirror partners, with the dynamic contribution estimated at 45–60% of the total shift.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for the discrete spectrum of \(^{16}\)C and the continuum spectrum of \(^{16}\)Ne using a hyperspherical harmonic method with a core+\(N\)+\(N\) model. They adjusted core-\(N\) potentials to reproduce known \(^{15}\)C and \(^{15}\)F resonance positions, then tuned a phenomenological three-body potential \(V_3(\rho)\) to match the experimental energies of the \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states in \(^{16}\)C, and finally computed the corresponding states in \(^{16}\)Ne. The dependence of the results on the unknown charge radius of \(^{14}\)O and on the \(^{15}\)F ground-state energy \(E_r\) was systematically explored.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{15}\)F \(1/2^+\) ground-state energy is inferred to be \(E_r = 1.39\)–\(1.42\) MeV from the consistency of the three-body model with experimental \(^{16}\)Ne energies.
- For the \(0^+\) state, the static TES (pure \([s^2]\) configuration) is \(\sim 230\) keV and the static TES (pure \([d^2]\) configuration) is \(\sim 120\) keV, while the realistic TES including dynamic effects varies between \(\sim 300\) and \(\sim 400\) keV.
- The \(^{16}\)C \(0^+\) ground-state energy is fixed at \(E_T = -5.469\) MeV and the \(2^+\) state at \(E_T = -3.703\) MeV.
- The experimental \(^{16}\)Ne \(0^+\) state energy is \(E_T = 1.466(20)\) MeV and the \(2^+\) state energy is \(E_T = 3.16(2)\) MeV (from Brown 2014).
- The width of the \(^{16}\)Ne \(0^+\) state ranges from 0.323 keV (set P1) to 3.67 keV (set P3); the \(2^+\) state width ranges from 40.6 keV (P1) to 51.4 keV (P3).
- The weight of the \([s^2]\) configuration in the \(^{16}\)C \(0^+\) state varies from 44.2% (P1) to 51.4% (P3); in \(^{16}\)Ne it varies from 69.0% (P3) to 71.7% (P1).
- The weight of the \([sd]\) configuration in the \(^{16}\)C \(2^+\) state is about 78–81%; in \(^{16}\)Ne it is about 87%.

## Context

This work extends the three-body treatment of the Thomas-Ehrman shift previously introduced by Grigorenko (2002) for the \(^{12}\)O/\(^{12}\)Be and \(^{16}\)Ne/\(^{16}\)C mirror pairs, now including the first excited \(2^+\) states and using improved experimental data from Mukha (2008), Wamers (2014), and Brown (2014). The analysis demonstrates that simultaneous description of the \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states in \(^{16}\)Ne provides a stringent consistency check that can resolve the experimental controversy on the \(^{15}\)F ground-state energy (Fortune 2006). The results enable more precise predictions for the structure of proton-rich \(sd\)-shell nuclei beyond the dripline and for the role of three-body dynamics in isospin symmetry breaking.
