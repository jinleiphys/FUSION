# Connection between soft and hard probes of small collision systems at RHIC and LHC

> The expected connections between signatures in the soft and hard sectors of small collision systems, and the status of experimental attempts to identify them, are discussed. These proceedings summarize the talk as given at the International Symposium on Multi-Particle Dynamics in September 2019 in Santa Fe, NM (ISMD19). As such, the choice of content and focus are selective and not intended to be comprehensive.

## Key claim

The expected connections between soft-sector signatures (collective flow at low transverse momentum) and hard-sector signatures (high-transverse-momentum particle production) in small collision systems like proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions are discussed, but the talk was selective and not intended to be comprehensive, and the tension between observed high-transverse-momentum azimuthal anisotropy and the absence of nuclear modification in minimum-bias proton-nucleus collisions remains unresolved.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from RHIC and LHC on azimuthal anisotropy coefficients (v_n) and nuclear modification factors in small collision systems, comparing soft-sector (p_T less than 3 GeV) and hard-sector (p_T greater than 10 GeV) measurements. It examines specific data from the ATLAS, ALICE, and CMS collaborations on jet-triggered events, heavy flavor muon anisotropies, and photo-nuclear collisions, and discusses theoretical calculations from hydrodynamic models and parton energy loss frameworks.

## Key numbers

- In 0-1% centrality 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions, v_2 was suggested to have an appreciable value even at p_T approximately 10 GeV (Aad 2014).
- In 8.16 TeV p+Pb data, a non-zero azimuthal anisotropy was observed for particles up to p_T approximately 50 GeV, far from the hydrodynamic regime (Aad 2019).
- A calculation by Romatschke (2018) indicates that appreciable v_2 values on the order of 2% for p_T greater than 10 GeV particles should accompany a non-trivial v_2 at low p_T.
- Current data on heavy flavor quark production in p+A collisions indicates no modification at the 10% level in production spectra compared to pp collisions (Adam 2016).
- In 91 GeV e+e- collisions with ALEPH at LEP, upper statistical limits were set on the possible magnitude of v_2 (Badea 2019).
- In photo-nuclear events analyzed by ATLAS (2019), v_2 values were compatible with those in pp collisions but lower than those in p+Pb.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of collective behavior in small systems from the soft sector (where hydrodynamic models describe v_n values across pp, p+A, and A+A collisions, as shown by Weller 2017) into the hard sector, where the connection is less clear. It highlights tension between the observation of significant azimuthal anisotropy at high p_T in p+Pb collisions (Aad 2019) and the absence of detectable nuclear modification in minimum-bias p+A collisions, and it identifies heavy flavor measurements (particularly bottom quarks, which show zero anisotropy in ATLAS data from Aad 2019) as key discriminants for future work. The paper enables further experimental studies in novel collision systems (such as O+O or Ar+Ar at RHIC and LHC, and ultra-peripheral collisions) and calls for precision jet-coincidence and jet-structure measurements guided by theoretical approaches that correctly describe A+A collisions.
