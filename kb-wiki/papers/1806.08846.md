# Semiclassical description of chiral geometry in triaxial nuclei

> A triaxial particle-rotor Hamiltonian for three mutually perpendicular angular momentum vectors corresponding to two high-$j$ quasiparticles and the rotation of a triaxial collective core, is treated within a time-dependent variational principle. The resulting classical energy function is used to investigate the rotational dynamics of the system. It is found that the classical energy function exhibits two minima starting from a critical angular momentum value which depends on the single-particle configuration and the asymmetry measure $γ$. The emergence of the two minima is attributed to the breaking of the chiral symmetry. Quantizing the energy function for a given angular momentum, one obtains a Schrödinger equation with a coordinate dependent mass term for a symmetrical potential which changes from a single to a double well shape as the angular momentum pass the critical value. The energies of the chiral partner bands for a given angular momentum are then given by the lowest two eigenvalues. The procedure is exemplified for maximal triaxiality and two $h_{11/2}$ quasiparticles, with the results used for the description of the chiral doublet bands in $^{134}$Pr.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a semiclassical treatment of a triaxial particle-rotor Hamiltonian, using a time-dependent variational principle, can reproduce the emergence of chiral doublet bands. The key condition is that the classical energy function exhibits two minima (indicating chiral symmetry breaking) only above a critical angular momentum value, which depends on both the single-particle configuration and the triaxiality parameter $\gamma$.

## Method
The author starts with a quantum particle-rotor Hamiltonian for two high-$j$ quasiparticles coupled to a triaxial core. This Hamiltonian is dequantized into a classical energy function using a time-dependent variational principle with spin coherent states. The classical energy surface is analyzed in terms of canonical variables to find planar and aplanar (chiral) stationary points. The classical energy function is then quantized with respect to a chiral variable, yielding a Schrodinger equation with a coordinate-dependent mass term, whose lowest two eigenvalues give the energies of the chiral partner bands.

## Key numbers
- The minimal classical critical angular momentum ($I_{c}^{min}$) for the $\pi h_{11/2} \otimes \nu h_{11/2}^{-1}$ configuration (relevant for $^{134}$Pr) is 10.87.
- For the $^{134}$Pr application, the fitted inertial constant is $\mathcal{J}_{0}=33.196$ MeV$^{-1}$ and the reference energy is $E_{0}=2.746$ MeV, yielding an rms deviation of 59.28 keV.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter used for $^{134}$Pr is $Q=3.5\, e\,b$.
- The critical angular momentum for the $\pi g_{9/2} \otimes \nu g_{9/2}^{-1}$ configuration (A~80) is 8.99.

## Context
This work extends the standard particle-rotor model (PRM) and tilted axis cranking (TAC) approaches to chirality by providing a semiclassical bridge between them. It is directly comparable to the collective Hamiltonian approach of Refs. [col1, col2], but derives both the kinetic and potential terms from a single quantum Hamiltonian rather than separate procedures. The formalism is applied to the chiral doublet bands in $^{134}$Pr, a well-known case studied experimentally by Tonev (2007) and others, and the results are compared to prior PRM calculations from Refs. [PRM5, PRM7, Qi].
