# A combined analysis of PandaX, LUX, and XENON1T experiments within the framework of dark matter effective theory

> Weakly interacting massive particles are a widely well-probed dark matter candidate by the dark matter direct detection experiments. Theoretically, there are a large number of ultraviolet completed models that consist of a weakly interacting massive particle dark matter. The variety of models makes the comparison with the direct detection data complicated and often non-trivial. To overcome this, in the non-relativistic limit, the effective theory was developed in the literature which works very well to significantly reduce the complexity of dark matter-nucleon interactions and to better study the nuclear response functions. In the effective theory framework for a spin-1/2 dark matter, we combine three independent likelihood functions from the latest PandaX, LUX, and XENON1T data, and give a joint limit on each effective coupling. The astrophysical uncertainties of the dark matter distribution are also included in the likelihood. We further discuss the isospin violating cases of the interactions. Finally, for both dimension-five and dimension-six effective theories above the electroweak scale, we give updated limits of the new physics mass scales.

## Key claim

The paper presents combined exclusion limits on dark matter effective field theory operators from three leading direct detection experiments (PandaX, LUX, and XENON1T), finding that the combined constraint improves the spin-independent cross section limit by a factor of approximately 1.3 compared to XENON1T alone, though the authors note their binned likelihood method is more conservative than the official unbinned analyses.

## Method

The authors reconstructed likelihood functions for the PandaX-II, LUX, and XENON1T experiments using published event counts and background estimates, then combined them into a joint likelihood for spin-1/2 dark matter within the non-relativistic effective theory framework of 14 operators. They included astrophysical uncertainties from the dark matter velocity distribution (local velocity, escape velocity, and local density) as nuisance parameters using a profile likelihood approach, and considered both isospin-conserving and isospin-violating scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Combined 95% CL upper limit on spin-independent WIMP-nucleon cross section improves by a factor of ~1.3 compared to XENON1T alone
- For PandaX-II: observed events 2 (Run 8, 5845 kg-day exposure) and 1 (Run 9, 27155 kg-day exposure), with expected backgrounds of 2.4 ± 0.8 and 2.4 ± 0.7 respectively
- For XENON1T: observed event 1 with background 0.36 (+0.11/-0.07) over 34.2 live days with ~1042 kg fiducial mass
- For a WIMP mass of ~30 GeV, XENON1T sets upper limit on spin-independent cross section to ~10^(-46) cm^2
- Local dark matter density adopted: 0.32 ± 0.02 GeV/cm^3
- Local velocity adopted: 240.0 ± 6.0 km/s
- Escape velocity adopted: 541.3 (+16.6/-12.2) km/s
- Dark matter mass range considered: 5 GeV to 1 TeV
- Higgs vacuum expectation value used as scale: Λ = 246 GeV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Anand (2013) and Fitzpatrick (2012) by combining the three most recent xenon-based direct detection experiments (PandaX-II, LUX, and XENON1T) into a single joint analysis with proper treatment of astrophysical uncertainties. The results enable model-independent comparisons of any ultraviolet-completed dark matter model against the combined experimental data, and the likelihood functions are incorporated into the LikeDM tool developed by Huang (2016).
