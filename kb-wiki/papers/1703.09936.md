# Properties of non-local wave function equivalent potential with generalized derivative expansion

> We examine the properties of the wave-function-equivalent potentials which HAL QCD collaboration has introduced. We generalize the derivative expansion, and then apply it to energy-independent and non-local potentials in a coupled-channel model. We observe that the expansion converges by comparing the scattering phase shifts computed from these potentials with the exact one. We have also found that the convergence can be improved by either varying the choice of interpolating fields or tuning the expansion scale in the generalized derivative expansion. The results will be utilized in future lattice QCD calculations, allowing for further application of the HAL QCD method.

## Key claim

The generalized derivative expansion for non-local HAL QCD potentials converges, and its convergence can be improved by either varying the choice of interpolating fields (via a field admixture parameter \(q\)) or tuning the Gaussian expansion scale \(\rho\), though the abstract does not specify that the demonstration is performed in a 1+1 dimensional coupled-channel model rather than in full lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors employed a 1+1 dimensional coupled-channel model with three scalar fields (analogous to proton, neutron, and an excited neutron state) and an attractive square-well interaction plus a contact coupling. They constructed Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions from the analytic solutions of the coupled-channel Schrödinger equations, then applied a generalized derivative expansion using a Gaussian smearing function \(\delta_\rho(x-x')\) to avoid singularities at the square-well edges and delta-function coupling. The coefficient functions \(v_n(x)\) were determined from the NBS wave functions for the \((N+1)\) lowest-lying energy levels in a finite box with twisted boundary conditions, and the resulting HAL QCD potentials were used to compute scattering phase shifts via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for comparison with the exact analytic solution.

## Key numbers

- Bound state energy: \(E_0 \simeq -33.7\) (in units where \(R=1\) and \(M=1\)).
- 15 excited states exist below the threshold \(E = \Delta = 6/R^2\) (with \(R=1\) and \(M=1\)).
- The potential is effectively zero for \(|x| > 3\) (in units of \(R=1\)), and treated as zero for \(|x| > 4\) in phase shift calculations.
- The exact phase shift at zero energy is \(\delta(E=0) = \pi/2\), corresponding to a single bound state in 1+1 dimensions.
- Model parameters: \(MV_0 = 1/R^2\), \(M\Delta = 6/R^2\), \(Mg = 6/R\), with \(R=1\) and \(M=1\).
- Box size: \(L = 10\) with twisted boundary condition \(\theta = \pi/2\).

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method for deriving nuclear forces from lattice QCD, which was introduced by the HAL QCD collaboration (HAL-PRL2007, HAL-PTP2010). It directly addresses the validity of the derivative expansion for non-local potentials, which was previously studied indirectly by Murano (2011) using only two local lowest-order potentials at energies \(E \sim 0\) MeV and \(E \sim 45\) MeV. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to use the generalized derivative expansion with tunable parameters to improve convergence without additional computational cost.
