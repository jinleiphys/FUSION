# Bayesian analysis of density profile of light dark matter elucidating the properties of dark matter admixed neutron stars in the presence of hyperons

> We study the impact of symmetry energy ($S$), hyperons, and dark matter (DM) on structural and oscillatory properties of neutron stars (NSs). Uncertainty from hadronic equation of state for NSs is considered with 15 relativistic mean field models having slope parameter ($L_0$) of $S$ in range $40-120$ MeV. DM admixed NSs (DMANSs) are described with feeble interaction between light DM fermions ($χ$) with hadronic matter in the presence of hyperons via scalar ($η$) and vector ($ξ$) dark mediators. The masses $m_χ$, $m_η$ and $m_ξ$ are related by self-interaction constraints from bullet cluster. DM self-interaction couplings are related to $m_χ$ by relic density constraint. The DM density is taken as an exponential function of baryon density with a free parameter $α$. Uncertainty from DM model is incorporated by exploring the dependence on $m_χ$ and $α$. Several DM search experiments have almost ruled out the existence of massive DM ($\gtrsim$ GeV). Lately, pursuit for sub-GeV DM has attracted significant attention. Therefore, we consider $m_χ<$ 1 GeV and $α\leq$ 0.1 such that the contribution of DM to the total mass of the DMANSs is $<10\%$. Comparing our results with various astrophysical constraints, we find that the HESS J1731-347 and GW170817 data are very important in determining the presence of light DM in NSs in moderate amount, relevant in the range $L_0\lesssim$ 58 MeV. Employing models of DMANSs that satisfy several observational data, we infer with Bayesian analysis, the likely ranges of $m_χ$ and $α$ are almost independent of the underlying hadronic model within 40 MeV $\lesssim$ $L_0$ $<$ 58 MeV. In the absence of DM and with the most probable values of $m_χ$ and $α$ obtained from the Bayesian inference, we calculate the frequencies of non-radial $f$- and $p_1$-modes oscillation of NSs/DMANSs.

## Key claim

The paper claims that light dark matter (DM) with sub-GeV mass can be present in neutron stars in moderate amounts, but only if the slope parameter of the symmetry energy ($L_0$) is less than about 58 MeV; the Bayesian analysis shows that the most probable DM parameters are almost independent of the hadronic model within the range 40 MeV $\lesssim L_0 <$ 58 MeV, a condition that is not stated in the abstract's broader claim about the importance of HESS J1731-347 and GW170817 data.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for dark matter admixed neutron stars (DMANSs) by combining 15 relativistic mean field (RMF) hadronic models (with slope parameter $L_0$ ranging from 40 to 120 MeV) with a light fermionic dark matter component ($m_\chi < 1$ GeV) interacting via scalar and vector mediators. The dark matter density was taken as an exponential function of baryon density with a free parameter $\alpha$, constrained so that the DM contribution to total mass is less than 10%. They solved the TOV equations for static stars and used the RNS code for rotating stars, then performed a Bayesian inference to determine the likely ranges of $m_\chi$ and $\alpha$ that satisfy observational constraints from pulsar masses/radii and GW170817 tidal deformability data.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter mass $m_\chi$ is considered below 1 GeV (sub-GeV regime), with the DM contribution to total mass of the DMANSs restricted to less than 10%.
- The slope parameter $L_0$ of the symmetry energy is considered in the range 40–120 MeV, with the relevant range for DM presence being $L_0 \lesssim$ 58 MeV.
- 15 RMF models were used: 9 non-linear (NL) models (GM1, BigApple, NL3, NL3$\omega \rho 1$ through NL3$\omega \rho 6$) and 6 density-dependent (DD) models (DDLZ1, DD2, DDME1, DDME2, DDMEX, PKDD).
- The parameter $\alpha$ is restricted to $\alpha \leq 0.1$ such that the DM fraction $f$ does not exceed 10%.
- The Bayesian analysis yields likely ranges of $m_\chi$ and $\alpha$ that are almost independent of the hadronic model within 40 MeV $\lesssim L_0 <$ 58 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of DM admixed neutron stars by Sen, Guha, and collaborators (2021, 2024) that assumed a constant DM number density, which introduced thermodynamic inconsistency; here the DM density is instead taken as an exponential function of baryon density, a novel approach. The paper also addresses the PREX-CREX dilemma for the symmetry energy slope parameter, noting that Lattimer (2023) found $L_0 = 53 \pm 13$ MeV optimally satisfies both experiments, and the present results are consistent with that range. The findings enable future gravitational wave observations (e.g., by Advanced LIGO, A+, Cosmic Explorer, Einstein Telescope) to potentially distinguish DM effects from other uncertainties in non-radial oscillation spectra.
