# Perspectives of Light-Front Quantized Field Theory: Some New Results

> Some basic topics in the light-front (LF) quantization of relativistic field theory are reviewed. It is argued that the LF quantization is equally appropriate as the conventional one and that they lead, assuming the micro- causality principle, to the same physical content. This is confirmed in the studies on the LF of the spontaneous symmetry breaking (SSB), of the degenerate vacua in Schwinger model (SM) and Chiral SM (CSM), of the chiral boson theory, and of the QCD in covariant gauges among others. The discussion on the LF is more economical and more transparent. In the context of the Dyson-Wick pertur- bation theory the relevant popagators in the front form theory are causal. The Wick rotation can then be performed to employ the Euclidean space integrals in momentum space. The lack of manifest covariance becomes tractable, and still more so if we employ, as discussed in the text, the Fourier transform of the fermionic field based on a special construction of the LF spinor. The fact that the hyperplanes $x^{pm}=0$ constitute characteristic surfaces of the hyper- bolic partial differential equation is found irrelevant in the quantized theory.

## Key claim

Light-front (LF) quantization is equally appropriate as conventional equal-time quantization and leads to the same physical content, assuming the microcausality principle; the hyperplanes \(x^{\pm}=0\) being characteristic surfaces of hyperbolic partial differential equations is found irrelevant in the quantized theory, as it is sufficient to quantize on one of the characteristic hyperplanes.

## Method

The paper reviews the Dirac method for constrained dynamical systems applied to light-front quantization, where the canonical momenta produce constraints on the phase space that are eliminated via Dirac brackets to obtain the reduced Hamiltonian and equal-LF-time commutators. The Fourier transform of fields on the LF uses the covariant phase space factor \(\theta(p^{+})/(2p^{+})\) and a special construction of the LF spinor for fermionic fields, and the Dyson-Wick perturbation theory is developed with causal propagators that permit Wick rotation to Euclidean space.

## Key numbers

- The equal-LF-time commutator for a real scalar field is \([\phi(\tau,x),\phi(\tau,y)] = -\frac{i}{4} \epsilon(x^{-}-y^{-}) \delta^{2}(x^{\perp}-y^{\perp})\), showing nonlocality in the longitudinal direction \(x^{-}\).
- The LF phase space factor for a massive particle is \(\int d^{2}p^{\perp} dp^{+} \theta(p^{+})/(2p^{+})\), compared to the conventional \(\int d^{3}\vec{p}/(2E_{p})\) with \(E_{p}=+\sqrt{\vec{p}^{2}+m^{2}}>0\).
- Seven out of ten Poincare generators are kinematical on the LF: \(P^{+}, P^{1}, P^{2}, M^{12}=-J_{3}, M^{+-}=M^{03}=-K_{3}, M^{1+}=(K_{1}+J_{2})/\sqrt{2}\), and \(M^{+2}=(K_{2}-J_{1})/\sqrt{2}\).
- The scalar field propagator in momentum space is \(\frac{i}{(2\pi)^{4}} \int d^{4}p \frac{e^{-ip\cdot x}}{(p^{2}-m^{2}+i\epsilon)}\), identical to the conventional form after setting \([\theta(p^{+})+\theta(-p^{+})]=1\).
- Length dimensions in LF theory are distinguished: \([x^{\perp}]=L_{\perp}\), \([x^{-}]=L_{\|}\), \([p^{+}]=1/L_{\|}\), \([p^{-}]=L_{\|}/(L_{\perp})^{2}\), \([x^{+}]=(L_{\perp})^{2}/L_{\|}\), and \([\phi]=1/L_{\perp}\).

## Context

This review extends Dirac's (1949) original proposal of the front form theory and connects it to the infinite momentum frame (IMF) methods used by Weinberg (1966) and the parton model of Feynman. It enables the application of light-front Tamm-Dancoff methods for nonperturbative QCD bound-state calculations (Brodsky, Pauli, and collaborators) and provides a framework for studying vacuum structures in soluble models such as the Schwinger model and chiral Schwinger model, where the LF vacuum is simpler than in conventional equal-time quantization.
