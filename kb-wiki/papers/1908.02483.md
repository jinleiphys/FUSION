# In-medium bound states of two bosonic impurities in a one-dimensional Fermi gas

> We investigate the ground-state energy of a one-dimensional Fermi gas with two bosonic impurities. We consider spinless fermions with no fermion-fermion interactions. The fermion-impurity and impurity-impurity interactions are modelled with Dirac delta functions. First, we study the case where impurity and fermions have equal masses, and the impurity-impurity two-body interaction is identical to the fermion-impurity interaction, such that the system is solvable with the Bethe ansatz. For attractive interactions, we find that the energy of the impurity-impurity subsystem is below the energy of the bound state that exists without the Fermi gas. We interpret this as a manifestation of attractive boson-boson interactions induced by the fermionic medium, and refer to the impurity-impurity subsystem as an in-medium bound state. For repulsive interactions, we find no in-medium bound states. Second, we construct an effective model to describe these interactions, and compare its predictions to the exact solution. We use this effective model to study non-integrable systems with unequal masses and/or potentials. We discuss parameter regimes for which impurity-impurity attraction induced by the Fermi gas can lead to the formation of in-medium bound states made of bosons that repel each other in the absence of the Fermi gas.

# In-medium bound states of two bosonic impurities in a one-dimensional Fermi gas

## Key claim

For attractive interactions, the energy of the impurity-impurity subsystem lies below the bound state energy that exists without the Fermi gas, which the authors interpret as attractive boson-boson interactions induced by the fermionic medium. For repulsive interactions, no in-medium bound states are found within the Bethe-ansatz-solvable case where impurity and fermion masses are equal and impurity-impurity and fermion-impurity interactions are identical.

## Method

The authors model a one-dimensional Fermi gas of spinless non-interacting fermions with two bosonic impurities using Dirac delta interactions for both fermion-impurity and impurity-impurity couplings. For the symmetric case of equal masses and equal interaction strengths, the system is solved exactly via the Bethe ansatz by solving the Bethe-ansatz equations for the quasi-momenta using Newton's method with initial estimates from the weak-coupling limit. For non-integrable cases with unequal masses or unequal interaction strengths, an effective Hamiltonian is constructed using either a zero-range potential matched to single-phonon exchange or a Born-Oppenheimer potential derived from static impurities, and the binding energies are obtained by exact diagonalization of the effective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For the Bethe-ansatz-solvable case with attractive interactions ($c<0$), the in-medium binding energy $E$ is negative, indicating a bound state; for repulsive interactions ($c>0$ up to $c\lesssim 2$), $E=0$ within numerical accuracy, meaning no in-medium bound state exists.
- The effective mass renormalization for the impurity is given by $m_{\mathrm{eff}}/m \simeq 1 + c^2/\pi^4 + (2/\pi^6 - 1/(6\pi^4))c^3 + (4/\pi^8 - 1/(2\pi^6))c^4$, with corrections of order $O(c^5)$ neglected for $c\in(-2,0)$.
- For the zero-range effective potential, the single-phonon exchange gives $\kappa = 2c^2/\pi^2 \simeq 0.202 c^2$ for $c\to 0$, and the bound state energy is $E_{\mathrm{eff}}^{ZR} = -(m_{\mathrm{eff}}/2m)(c - c^2/\pi^2)^2$.
- For the Born-Oppenheimer potential, $|\int E_{BO}(y)\mathrm{d}y| \simeq 0.22 c^2$ for $c\to 0$, slightly larger than the zero-range value.
- For $m=M$, $c=-2$, and $c_{II}=0$, the in-medium binding energy is $E\simeq -0.06$, which for $^6$Li atoms with $\rho=3/(\mu\mathrm{m})$ translates to approximately 22 nK $\times k_B$.
- The critical impurity-impurity interaction strength for bound state formation is $c^{cr}_{II} \simeq c^2/\pi^2$.

## Context

This work extends studies of impurity-impurity correlations induced by a medium in cold atom systems, following prior work on induced interactions by Bruderer (2007), Schecter (2014), and others. The one-dimensional geometry enhances interaction effects compared to higher dimensions, as noted by Takada (1982), and any attractive potential in 1D supports at least one bound state. The results enable experimental tests in ultracold Bose-Fermi mixtures, for example by measuring spectroscopic response or studying collapse dynamics as in Chin (2019), and can be applied to systems such as $^{133}$Cs impurities in a $^6$Li Fermi gas.
