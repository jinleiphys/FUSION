# Coupled baryon, electric charge and strangeness fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We present for the first time quantitative results for the coupled dynamics of second order fluctuations in the three conserved charges of QCD based on stochastic diffusion equations for a Bjorken-type expanding hadronic medium.

# Coupled baryon, electric charge and strangeness fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The authors present the first quantitative results for the coupled dynamics of second-order fluctuations in the three conserved charges of QCD (baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness) using stochastic diffusion equations in a Bjorken-type expanding hadronic medium. The key hedge is that these fluctuations are necessarily driven out of equilibrium during the hadronic phase, meaning that final fluctuation observables do not simply reflect equilibrium conditions at chemical freeze-out.

## Method
The authors solve stochastic diffusion equations in Milne coordinates (proper time τ and spatial rapidity y) for the coupled net-charge densities n_i (i = B, Q, S), incorporating a charge diffusion coefficient matrix κ_ij and noise cross-correlations between different charges. They use realistic diffusion coefficients from kinetic theory for a hadron resonance gas of the 19 lightest hadron species (Greif 2017), with a Hubble-like temperature evolution T(τ) = T(τ₀)τ₀/τ starting from τ₀ = 1 fm/c and T(τ₀) = 500 MeV, and study equilibration at fixed temperature followed by dynamic expansion and cooling from T = 160 MeV to T = 100 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Equilibration time for the local variance of net-baryon density σ_BB² is of order 20 fm/c at the temperatures studied.
- Initial conditions: n_i(τ,y) = 0 fm⁻², implying μ_j = 0 MeV for chemical potential profiles; initial local variances and co-variances set to zero.
- Temperature evolution follows T(τ) = T(τ₀)τ₀/τ with τ₀ = 1 fm/c and T(τ₀) = 500 MeV.
- Dynamic expansion studied from T = 160 MeV down to T = 100 MeV.
- At μ_B = 0 MeV, the diffusion coefficients and susceptibilities are evaluated as functions of temperature for the hadron resonance gas.

## Context
This work extends the deterministic coupled diffusion study of Fotakis (2019) by adding stochastic noise and cross-correlations to model fluctuation dynamics quantitatively. It contradicts the common assumption that fluctuation measurements at freeze-out reflect equilibrium conditions, showing instead that rapid expansion drives fluctuations out of equilibrium even when starting from an equilibrated state at T = 160 MeV. The results call for reassessment of previous freeze-out analyses based on fluctuation data, such as those by Alba (2014) and Bluhm (2018), and suggest that critical fluctuation signals from the QCD phase transition (Kitazawa 2020) may survive the rapid hadronic expansion.
