# Lattice QCD at nonzero temperature and density

> We discuss some selected recent developments in the field of lattice QCD at nonzero temperature and density, describing in particular the transition from the hadronic gas to the quark-gluon plasma, as seen in simulations using Wilson fermions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that lattice QCD simulations using Wilson fermions, although not yet at the physical point and continuum limit simultaneously, show an emerging consistency with staggered-fermion results for the thermal transition, and that the pseudocritical temperatures shift to lower values as the pion mass is reduced, with a reduced spread among observables that may indicate proximity to a genuine phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed fixed-scale lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f=2+1\) flavors of Wilson clover fermions on anisotropic lattices (renormalised anisotropy \(\xi \approx 3.45\), temporal cutoff \(a_\tau^{-1} \approx 6\) GeV) at two pion masses: \(m_\pi = 384(4)\) MeV (Generation 2) and \(m_\pi = 236(2)\) MeV (Generation 2L). They computed quark number susceptibilities, the renormalised chiral condensate, the chiral susceptibility, and a parity-doubling ratio \(R\) for octet and decuplet baryons, extracting pseudocritical temperatures from inflection points of cubic spline fits.

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperature from light quark number susceptibility: \(157(1)\) MeV for \(m_\pi = 236(2)\) MeV; \(166(6)\) MeV for \(m_\pi = 384(4)\) MeV.
* Pseudocritical temperature from renormalised chiral condensate: \(164(2)\) MeV for \(m_\pi = 236(2)\) MeV; \(181(2)\) MeV for \(m_\pi = 384(4)\) MeV.
* Pseudocritical temperature from chiral susceptibility: \(165(2)(2)\) MeV for \(m_\pi = 236(2)\) MeV; \(170(3)(2)\) MeV for \(m_\pi = 384(4)\) MeV.
* Extrapolated pseudocritical temperature at the physical point from chiral condensate: \(159(6)\) MeV, using a combined fit with twisted-mass fermion data and an O(4) scaling ansatz with \(\Delta = 1.833\) fixed, yielding \(T_0 = 147(4)\) MeV.
* Parity-doubling inflection temperature for the nucleon channel: \(157(2)\) MeV (Gen 2L) and \(169(1)\) MeV (Gen 2).
* Parity-doubling inflection temperature for the \(\Delta\) channel: \(158(3)\) MeV (Gen 2L) and \(168.8(5)\) MeV (Gen 2).

## Context

This work extends studies of the QCD thermal transition using Wilson fermions, complementing staggered-fermion results from the Budapest-Wuppertal collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2010) and hotQCD (2018) which obtained \(T_{\rm pc} \approx 155-156.5\) MeV at the physical point. The parity-doubling analysis builds on prior work by Datta et al. (2012) and the authors' own series (Aarts et al., 2015, 2017, 2018). The results enable future continuum-extrapolated studies at the physical point with Wilson fermions and provide a complementary window into chiral symmetry restoration via baryonic observables.
