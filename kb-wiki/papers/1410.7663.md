# Neutrinos and the synthesis of heavy elements: the role of gravity

> The synthesis of heavy elements in the Universe presents several challenges. From one side the astrophysical site is still undetermined and on other hand the input from nuclear physics requires the knowledge of properties of exotic nuclei, some of them perhaps accessible in ion beam facilities. Black hole accretion disks have been proposed as possible r-process sites. Analogously to Supernovae these objects emit huge amounts of neutrinos. We discuss the neutrino emission from black hole accretion disks. In particular we show the influence that the black hole strong gravitational field has on changing the electron fraction relevant to the synthesis of elements.

## Key claim

The strong gravitational field of a black hole alters the neutrino fluxes from accretion disk outflows by redshifting the high-energy tails of the spectra and deforming the solid angle subtended by the source, which changes the electron fraction enough to alter nucleosynthesis yields; the effect is time-dependent because the neutrino decoupling surfaces shrink as matter is accreted.

## Method

The authors used a time-dependent hydrodynamical simulation of matter accreting around a 3 solar mass black hole with spin a=0.8 to define the neutrino decoupling surfaces at t=20 ms and t=60 ms. They computed null geodesics in the Schwarzschild metric to connect each point on an outflow trajectory to each point on the neutrino surfaces, obtaining the observed neutrino fluxes including redshift and solid-angle deformation. The electron fraction was then derived from the neutrino and antineutrino absorption rates on nucleons, using the observed fluxes with weak magnetism corrections.

## Key numbers

* At t=20 ms, for an outflow with entropy per baryon s/k=30, the Newtonian electron fraction near x=z=100 km is 0.47, while the general relativistic value is 0.49.
* At t=60 ms, for the same outflow conditions, the Newtonian electron fraction is approximately 0.53, while the general relativistic value is approximately 0.56.
* The black hole mass is 3 solar masses with spin a=0.8.
* The outflow trajectory starts at r=30 km from the black hole.
* The entropy per baryon s/k was sampled from 20 to 80, and the dynamic timescale tau from 10 to 100 ms.
* The constant b in the absorption rate formulas is 9.704 x 10^{-50} cm^2 keV^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of general relativistic effects on neutrino emission in black hole accretion disk outflows (Caballero et al., 2014; Caballero et al., 2012) and builds on hydrodynamical simulations of accretion disks (Just et al., 2014). It contrasts with Newtonian calculations that neglect gravitational redshift and ray-bending, showing that general relativity systematically increases the electron fraction and enhances the production of proton-rich nuclei such as Ni-56. The results enable more realistic nucleosynthesis calculations for merger-type accretion disks, which have been proposed as r-process sites, and highlight the need to include time-dependent neutrino surfaces and general relativity in future simulations.
