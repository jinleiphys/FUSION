# Skewness of mean transverse momentum fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We propose the skewness of mean transverse momentum, $\langle p_t \rangle$, fluctuations as a fine probe of hydrodynamic behavior in relativistic nuclear collisions. We describe how the skewness of the $\langle p_t \rangle$ distribution can be analyzed experimentally, and we use hydrodynamic simulations to predict its value. We predict in particular that $\langle p_t \rangle$ fluctuations have positive skew, which is significantly larger than if particles were emitted independently. We elucidate the origin of this result by deriving generic formulas relating the fluctuations of $\langle p_t \rangle$ to the fluctuations of the early-time thermodynamic quantities. We postulate that the large positive skewness of $\langle p_t \rangle$ fluctuations is a generic prediction of hydrodynamic models.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the probability distribution of mean transverse momentum fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions has a positive skewness that is significantly larger than the baseline from independent particle emission, and argues this is a generic prediction of hydrodynamic models, though the specific quantitative results may be overestimated because the hydrodynamic calculation overestimates the magnitude of the fluctuations themselves.

## Method

The authors used existing event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, starting from TRENTO initial conditions with a specific shear viscosity of 0.047 and a lattice QCD equation of state, evolving with the V-USPHYDRO code. They computed the distribution of mean transverse momentum for each event by integrating the continuous momentum distribution from hydrodynamics, then calculated the standardized skewness and intensive skewness of these distributions across centrality bins. They also derived analytic formulas relating the skewness of mean transverse momentum fluctuations to the skewness of initial energy fluctuations using a perturbative expansion of the initial entropy density field.

## Key numbers

- Standardized skewness of mean transverse momentum in 5-10% central Pb+Pb collisions: gamma_pt = 0.26 +/- 0.05
- Standardized skewness of mean transverse momentum in 50-55% peripheral Pb+Pb collisions: gamma_pt = 0.89 +/- 0.08
- Intensive skewness in 5-10% central Pb+Pb collisions: Gamma_pt = 7.5 +/- 1.5
- Intensive skewness in 50-55% peripheral Pb+Pb collisions: Gamma_pt = 10.6 +/- 0.9
- Baseline intensive skewness from independent particle emission (mixed events) ranges from 2.8 to 3.0 in Au+Au at 200 GeV and from 3.2 to 3.7 in Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV
- Standardized skewness from STAR data on Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV in 0-5% centrality: (7.3 +/- 1.0) x 10^{-2}
- The ratio of relative fluctuations of mean transverse momentum to relative fluctuations of initial energy is approximately 1.24 from thermodynamic relations

## Context

This work extends studies of mean transverse momentum fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, which have been measured by STAR (Adams 2005) and ALICE (Abelev 2014) but have received less theoretical attention than anisotropic flow. The paper builds on the idea from Gardim (2020) and Giacalone (2020) that mean transverse momentum fluctuations correlate with initial energy fluctuations, and uses the perturbative approach to initial density fluctuations developed by Blaizot (2014) and Gronqvist (2016). The results enable experimental measurement of the skewness as a new observable that can distinguish hydrodynamic behavior from independent particle emission, and may help constrain models of initial conditions and the equation of state of the quark-gluon plasma.
