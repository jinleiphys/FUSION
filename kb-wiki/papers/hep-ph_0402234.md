# NJL-model analysis of dense quark matter

> Investigations of deconfined quark matter within NJL-type models are reviewed, focusing on the regime of low temperatures and ``moderate'' densities, which is not accessible by perturbative QCD. Central issue is the interplay between chiral symmetry restoration and the formation of color superconducting phases. In order to lay a solid ground for this analysis, we begin with a rather detailed discussion of two and three-flavor NJL models and their phase structure, neglecting the possibility of diquark pairing in a first step. An important aspect of this part is a comparison with the MIT bag model. The NJL model is also applied to investigate the possibility of absolutely stable strange quark matter. In the next step the formalism is extended to include diquark condensates. We discuss the role and mutual influence of several conventional and less conventional quark-antiquark and diquark condensates. As a particularly interesting example, we analyze a spin-1 diquark condensate as a possible pairing channel for those quarks which are left over from the standard spin-0 condensate. For three-flavor systems, we find that a self-consistent calculation of the strange quark mass, together with the diquark condensates, is crucial for a realistic description of the 2SC-CFL phase transition. We also study the effect of neutrality constraints which are of relevance for compact stars. Both, homogeneous and mixed, neutral phases are constructed. Although neutrality constraints generally tend to disfavor the 2SC phase we find that this phase is again stabilized by the large values of the dynamical strange quark mass which follow from the self-consistent treatment. Finally, we combine our solutions with existing hadronic equations of state to investigate the existence of quark matter cores in neutron stars.

# NJL-model analysis of dense quark matter

## Key claim

This review argues that NJL-type models, despite their well-known shortcomings (lack of confinement, no gluon degrees of freedom), are justified for studying cold deconfined quark matter at moderate densities where both confinement and gluon degrees of freedom are of minor importance; the central finding is that a self-consistent calculation of the dynamical strange quark mass together with diquark condensates is crucial for a realistic description of the 2SC-CFL phase transition, and that the large values of the dynamical strange quark mass from self-consistent treatment can stabilize the 2SC phase even under neutrality constraints that otherwise disfavor it.

## Method

The author reviews investigations using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models with point-like four-fermion (and sometimes six-fermion) interactions, first analyzing two- and three-flavor NJL models and their phase structure without diquark pairing, then extending the formalism to include diquark condensates. The study examines the competition and mutual influence of conventional and less conventional quark-antiquark and diquark condensates, including a spin-1 diquark condensate as a possible pairing channel for quarks left over from the standard spin-0 condensate, and constructs both homogeneous and mixed neutral phases relevant for compact stars.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: baryon density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}, with binding energy (16 +/- 1) MeV per nucleon and Fermi momentum k_F = (1.35 +/- 0.05) fm^{-1}
- Baryon number chemical potential at saturation: mu_B = m_N - E_b = 923 MeV, corresponding to quark number chemical potential mu = 308 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV; pion-nucleon sigma term: sigma_{pi N} = 45 MeV
- QCD scale parameter: Lambda_QCD = 200 MeV for five flavors in the MS-bar scheme
- Critical temperature for chiral phase transition at mu=0: T_c = (173 +/- 8) MeV for two flavors, (154 +/- 8) MeV for three flavors in the chiral limit; pure SU(3) gauge theory without quarks gives T_c = (269 +/- 1) MeV
- Curvature of phase boundary at mu=0 from Taylor expansion: T_c (d^2T_c/dmu^2)|_{mu=0} = -0.14 +/- 0.06, giving T_c(mu) = T_c(0) - (0.07 +/- 0.03) mu^2/T_c(0)
- Lattice determination of critical endpoint (Fodor and Katz, improved calculation): T = (162 +/- 2) MeV and mu_B = (360 +/- 40) MeV
- Color superconducting gaps can be of order 100 MeV

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the work on NJL-model analyses of dense quark matter that began with the renewed interest in color superconductivity sparked by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998), who found gaps of order 100 MeV. The paper builds on earlier schematic phase diagrams from Rajagopal (1999), Alford (2003), and Schafer (2003), and compares NJL-model results with lattice QCD calculations at zero chemical potential (Laermann and Philipsen, 2003; Karsch and Laermann, 2003) and with chiral perturbation theory results for in-medium reduction of the quark condensate (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1989; Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel, 1992). The work enables future investigations of the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star interiors by combining NJL-model solutions with existing hadronic equations of state.
