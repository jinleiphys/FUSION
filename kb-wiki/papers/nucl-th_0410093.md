# Virtual photon asymmetry for confined, interacting Dirac particles with spin symmetry

> We study the Bjorken x dependence of the virtual photon spin asymmetry in polarized deep inelastic scattering of electrons from hadrons. We use an exactly solved relativistic potential model of the hadron, treating the constituents as independent massless Dirac particles bound to an infinitely massive force center. The potential is chosen to have spin symmetry and a linear radial dependence with spherical symmetry. The effect of interactions of the struck constituent with the remainder of the target on the longitudinal photon asymmetry is demonstrated. In particular, the small-x suppression of the photon asymmetry observed in polarized deep inelastic scattering from the proton is shown to be a consequence of these interactions. The effect of p--wave components of the Dirac wave function, long known to give an important contribution to the spin of hadrons, is explicitly demonstrated through their interference with the s--wave term.

## Key claim

The small-\(x\) suppression of the virtual photon spin asymmetry \(A_1^q(\xi)\) observed in polarized deep inelastic scattering from the proton is shown to arise from the interference between \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave components of the Dirac wave function of a confined, interacting quark, a mechanism that is absent in the cavity approximation to the bag model.

## Method

The authors use an exactly solved relativistic potential model in which a single massless Dirac particle is confined by a linear potential with a half-vector plus half-scalar structure, chosen to preserve spin symmetry. The virtual photon helicity cross sections \(\sigma_{1/2}\) and \(\sigma_{3/2}\) are computed exactly from the eigenstates of the Hamiltonian for excitation energies up to \(\sim 12\) GeV, and the asymmetry \(A_1^q = (\sigma_{1/2} - \sigma_{3/2})/(\sigma_{1/2} + \sigma_{3/2})\) is evaluated as a function of the scaling variable \(\xi\). The exact results are compared with the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and with the cavity approximation to the bag model.

## Key numbers

- String tension \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 1\) GeV/fm, used to set the confining potential.
- Ground state energy of the confined quark: \(E_0 = 840\) MeV.
- Target mass used to convert \(\tilde{y}\) to \(\xi\): \(M_T = 2.5\) GeV \(\sim 3E_0\).
- The calculated structure functions are very close to zero at \(\tilde{y} = -2000\) MeV, corresponding to \(\xi \approx 0.8\).
- In the bag model comparison, the cavity radius is chosen as \(R = 0.65\) fm (the rms radius of the ground state in the linear confinement model), and the bag model ground state energy is 0.62 GeV.
- The PWIA reproduces the exact asymmetry to within about ten percent.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic potential model of Paris (2003) to polarized structure functions, and contrasts with earlier bag model calculations by Jaffe (1975), Thomas (1991), Jaffe and Ji (1992), and Song (1994), which treated the struck constituent as a free particle in a cavity and neglected the confining interaction. The exact calculation demonstrates that the \(p\)-wave components of the Dirac wave function, long known to reduce the valence quark contribution to the nucleon spin (Bogoliubov 1968, Kuti 1971, Close 1974, Chodos 1974, Jaffe 1975), produce a dynamical suppression of \(A_1^q\) at small \(\xi\) when interactions are included. This suggests that the observed small-\(x\) suppression of the proton asymmetry is a consequence of interactions among relativistic fermionic constituents, and it motivates more refined treatments of spin asymmetries in hadronic models.
