# Application of the functional renormalization group to Bose gases: from linear to hydrodynamic fluctuations

> We study weakly interacting Bose gases using the functional renormalization group with a hydrodynamic effective action. We use a scale-dependent parametrization of the boson fields that interpolates between a Cartesian representation at high momenta and an amplitude-phase one for low momenta. We apply this to Bose gases in two and three dimensions near the superfluid phase transition where they can be described by statistical O(2) models. We are able to give consistent physical descriptions of the infrared regime in both two and three dimensions. In particular, and in contrast to previous studies using the functional renormalization group, we find a stable superfluid phase at finite temperatures in two dimensions. We compare our results for the superfluid and boson densities with Monte-Carlo simulations, and we find they are in reasonable agreement.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a scale-dependent interpolation between Cartesian and amplitude-phase (AP) field representations within the functional renormalization group (FRG) yields a stable superfluid phase at finite temperatures in two dimensions, which previous FRG studies using only the Cartesian representation had failed to achieve without fine-tuning or unphysical approximations.

## Method

The authors apply the Wetterich FRG to a classical O(2) model describing weakly interacting Bose gases near the superfluid phase transition in two and three dimensions. They use a derivative expansion truncated to quartic order in the fields and introduce a scale-dependent parametrization of the boson fields that interpolates between a Cartesian representation at high regulator scales and an amplitude-phase (hydrodynamic) representation at low scales, controlled by a function \(b_k\) that tends to infinity at high \(k\) and to \(\sqrt{\rho_0}\) at \(k\to 0\). The flow equations are derived from the modified Wetterich equation that accounts for the explicit scale dependence of the fields, and are integrated numerically using the optimized Litim regulator.

## Key numbers

- In three dimensions, the superfluid density \(\rho_s = Z_m \rho_0\) at \(k=0\) is compared with Monte-Carlo simulations of Prokofev (2002, 2004), finding reasonable agreement across the temperature range below \(T_c\).
- In two dimensions, the quasi-condensate density \(\rho_q\) (identified with \(\rho_0\) in the AP representation) remains finite at \(k=0\) for temperatures below the BKT transition, in contrast to Cartesian FRG calculations where the flow becomes unstable and drives the system to the normal phase.
- The interpolation scale is set by the healing (hydrodynamic) momentum scale, which is of order \(\sqrt{2m g \rho_0}\) in the broken phase; the results are stable when the switch between representations occurs near this scale.
- The boson density \(n_0\) at \(k=0\) is compared with Monte-Carlo data for both two and three dimensions, showing reasonable agreement without fine-tuning of the interpolation parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of O(2) models and Bose gases that used only the Cartesian representation, such as those by Dupuis (2009, 2011) and Floerchinger and Wetterich (2008), which suffered from infrared instabilities in two dimensions and required stopping the flow at a finite scale. It builds on the interpolating representation idea proposed by Lamprecht and Pawlowski (2007) and contrasts with the AP-only FRG approach of Defenu (2017), which used an uncontrolled ultraviolet renormalization. The method enables consistent treatment of both ultraviolet and infrared regimes within a single FRG framework, and opens the way to studying two-dimensional superfluid systems with explicit vortex physics and the BKT transition using derivative expansions.
