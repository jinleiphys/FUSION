# D Mesons in Nuclear Matter: A DN Coupled-Channel Equations Approach

> A set of coupled two-body scattering equations is solved for the DN system embedded in an iso-symmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium behavior of charmed D mesons: (D^+,D^0), is investigated from the self-consistent solution within this scheme. The effective meson-baryon Lagrangian in charm quantum number one sector, the key ingredient in the present study, is adopted from a recent model by Hofmann and Lutz which has aimed at combining the charmed meson degree of freedom in a consistent manner with chiral unitary models. After a critical examination, the original model is modified in several important aspects, such as the method of regularization, in order to be more consistent and practical for our objective. The resultant interaction is used to reproduce the position and width of the s-wave Λ_c(2593) resonance in the isospin zero DN channel. In the isospin one channel, it generates a rather wide resonance at \~2770 MeV. The corresponding in-medium solution is then sought by incorporating Pauli blocking and the D- and π-meson dressing self-consistently. At normal nuclear matter density, the resultant Λ_c (2593) is found to stay narrow and shifted at a lower energy, while the I=1 resonance is lowered in position as well and broadened considerably. The possible implication of our findings on the J/Ψsuppression, etc. in relativistic heavy ion collisions is briefly discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a self-consistent coupled-channels approach, modified from the Hofmann-Lutz model by introducing a cut-off regularization and an extra phenomenological scalar-isoscalar attraction in the diagonal DN channel, can reproduce the Λ_c(2593) resonance and predict in-medium D meson behavior that differs qualitatively from static mean-field or QCD sum-rule results.

## Method
The authors solve coupled two-body Bethe-Salpeter equations for the DN system in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, using an effective meson-baryon Lagrangian based on t-channel vector meson exchange with universal coupling. They modify the Hofmann-Lutz model by changing the regularization method and adding a scalar-isoscalar attraction in the diagonal DN channel, then incorporate Pauli blocking and self-consistent D- and π-meson dressing to obtain in-medium solutions.

## Key numbers
- The Λ_c(2593) resonance in the isospin zero DN channel is reproduced with a binding energy of approximately 200 MeV and a width of about 3 MeV in free space.
- In the isospin one channel, the model generates a rather wide resonance at approximately 2770 MeV in free space.
- At normal nuclear matter density, the Λ_c(2593) resonance stays narrow and is shifted to a lower energy, while the I=1 resonance is lowered in position and broadened considerably.
- The QCD sum-rule approach gives an isospin-averaged D meson mass shift of approximately -50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, with an additional ±25 MeV from the vector condensate.
- The NMFA approach gives a mass shift of about 70 MeV for D and 20 MeV for anti-D at normal nuclear matter density.
- The Hofmann-Lutz model applied to nuclear matter gives a D meson pole shifted upward by about 30 MeV with a finite width.

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent coupled-channels method (SCCM) previously applied to anti-kaon nuclear physics (Koch 1994, Oset 1998, Lutz 2002, Ramos 2000, Tolos 2002) to the charmed D meson sector, modifying the Hofmann-Lutz model (Hofmann and Lutz 2005) which had aimed to combine charmed meson degrees of freedom with chiral unitary models. The results contradict the static mean-field approaches (Kaplan 1986, Nelson 1987) and QCD sum-rule methods (Hilger 2000, Morath 2002) that predicted a downward mass shift, instead finding an upward shift of the D meson pole. The work enables future studies of J/Ψ suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions and possible D-nuclear bound states.
