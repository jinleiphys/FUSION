# Low energy analysis of nu N --> nu N gamma in the Standard Model

> The production of single photons in low energy (~1 GeV) neutrino scattering off nucleons is analyzed in the Standard Model. At very low energies, E(nu) << 1 GeV, a simple description of the chiral lagrangian involving baryons and arbitrary SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y gauge fields is developed. Extrapolation of the process into the ~1-2 GeV region is treated in a simple phenomenological model. Coherent enhancements in compound nuclei are studied. The relevance of single photon events as a background to experimental searches for nu(mu) --> nu(e) is discussed. In particular, single photons are a plausible explanation for excess events observed by the MiniBooNE experiment.

## Key claim
Single-photon production in low-energy (~1 GeV) neutrino-nucleon scattering, mediated by pseudo-Chern-Simons interactions between the Z boson, photon, and vector mesons, provides a plausible explanation for the excess electron-like events observed by the MiniBooNE experiment, though the extrapolation to the 1-2 GeV region relies on a phenomenological model rather than a rigorous chiral expansion.

## Method
The analysis develops a chiral Lagrangian for baryons and electroweak gauge fields through three-derivative order, incorporating the full SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y gauge symmetry and the unique operator induced by pseudo-Chern-Simons interactions. The low-energy constants are fixed using known nucleon form factors (dipole parameterization with axial mass m_A ≈ 1.2 GeV), and the extrapolation to E_ν ~ 1-2 GeV is treated via a phenomenological model including t-channel ω(780) and ρ meson exchange, s-channel Δ(1232) resonance production, and generalized Compton scattering with an intermediate nucleon.

## Key numbers
- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A ≈ 1.26
- Weinberg angle: sin²θ_W = 0.231
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: a_p = 1.79
- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: a_n = -1.91
- Pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 93 MeV
- Nucleon mass: m_N ≈ 940 MeV
- Axial mass parameter: m_A ≈ 1.2 GeV
- Dipole form factor scale: 0.71 GeV²
- ω meson mass: m_ω ≈ 782 MeV
- ρ meson mass: m_ρ ≈ 770 MeV
- Δ(1232) resonance mass: approximately 1232 MeV
- Coefficient c₄⁽³⁾(ω) from ω exchange: ~1.5
- Coefficient c₅⁽³⁾(ρ) from ρ exchange: ~0.2

## Context
This work extends the analysis of pseudo-Chern-Simons interactions previously identified by Hill (2009) in the context of Z-boson-photon-vector meson couplings, and builds on the chiral Lagrangian formalism of Gasser (1987), Weinberg (1991), and Bernard (1995) by systematically including U(1) factors. The results enable quantitative predictions for single-photon backgrounds in neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE, and may have applications in astrophysical contexts including neutron star cooling and supernova dynamics.
