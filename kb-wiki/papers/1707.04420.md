# Transverse Momentum Fluctuations and Correlations

> We study fluctuations and correlations of the average transverse momentum of particles emitted in heavy-ion collisions. Fluctuations of the average transverse momentum are related to event-by-event fluctuations of the size and entropy of the initial source. Hydrodynamic calculations using a Glauber model with quark degrees of freedom reproduce the data. We study correlations of the average transverse momentum in different rapidity bins. We propose a definition of the observable that can be directly related to correlations of the collective flow variables.

# Transverse Momentum Fluctuations and Correlations

## Key claim
Hydrodynamic calculations using a Glauber model with quark degrees of freedom reproduce the ALICE data on transverse momentum fluctuations, but the Pearson correlation coefficient for transverse flow in different pseudorapidity bins is dominated by statistical fluctuations from finite multiplicity events and cannot be directly interpreted as a measure of collective flow correlations.

## Method
The authors performed viscous hydrodynamic simulations with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from both nucleon Glauber and quark Glauber Monte Carlo models. They computed the variance of the average transverse momentum using the estimator that excludes self-correlations, and calculated correlation coefficients and covariances of the average transverse momentum in different pseudorapidity bins. They also introduced a modified correlation coefficient that removes statistical fluctuation contributions by using the variance estimator without self-correlations.

## Key numbers
- The scaled variance of transverse momentum fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies shows a multiplicity dependence that deviates from the simple scaling C_{p_T}^{1/2}/<[p_T]> proportional to (dN/deta)^{-1/2}
- The predicted transverse flow-transverse flow correlation coefficient rho([p_T]_eta,[p_T]_-eta) is close to one (rho > 0.8), significantly larger than the Pearson correlation coefficient b([p_T]_F,[p_T]_B)
- The covariance of the average transverse momentum in two pseudorapidity bins, scaled by the product of square roots of charged particle densities and inverse average transverse momenta, shows a maximum for semi-central collisions
- The factorization breaking coefficient r_{p_T}(eta) shows small decorrelation that increases with pseudorapidity separation

## Context
This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of transverse momentum fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (Bozek 2012) and builds on the wounded quark model revived for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC (Bialas 1977, Adler 2013, Adare 2015). The results are compared to ALICE Collaboration data (Abelev 2014) and preliminary ALICE data on transverse flow correlations. The proposed correlation coefficient rho([p_T]_F,[p_T]_B) enables direct interpretation of collective flow correlations, while the factorization breaking observable r_{p_T}(eta) adapts the CMS Collaboration approach for azimuthal flow decorrelations (Khachatryan 2015) to transverse momentum fluctuations.
