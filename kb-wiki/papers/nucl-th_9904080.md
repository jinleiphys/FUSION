# Deuterons and space-momentum correlations in high energy nuclear collisions

> Using a microscopic transport model together with a coalescence after-burner, we study the formation of deuterons in Au + Au central collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200 AGeV$. It is found that the deuteron transverse momentum distributions are strongly affected by the nucleon space-momentum correlations, at the moment of freeze-out, which are mostly determined by the number of rescatterings. This feature is useful for studying collision dynamics at ultrarelativistic energies.

## Key claim

Deuteron transverse momentum distributions in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies are strongly affected by nucleon space-momentum correlations at freeze-out, which are mostly determined by the number of rescatterings; this sensitivity makes deuterons a useful probe for studying collision dynamics, but the effect is only clearly visible when comparing randomized versus normal events, not when comparing aligned versus normal events.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD v2.4) transport model, a semi-classical microscopic approach combining classical propagation with stochastic interactions, to simulate central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV. Since RQMD does not include light cluster production, a coalescence after-burner was applied to calculate deuteron yields from the nucleon phase-space distribution at freeze-out. The space-momentum correlations were artificially altered by randomizing or aligning the transverse space and momentum vectors of nucleons, and by scrambling the vector amplitudes, to isolate the effect of correlations on observables.

## Key numbers

- At mid-rapidity, the average transverse momentum values are: pions 0.4 GeV/c, kaons 0.6 GeV/c, protons 0.85 GeV/c, and deuterons about 1.4 GeV/c.
- The difference in mean pT between proton and deuteron decreases from about 550 MeV/c at mid-rapidity to 150 MeV/c at y >= 4 in normal events.
- After randomization of space-momentum correlations, the splitting between deuterons and protons in mean pT becomes constant at about 150 MeV/c across all rapidities.
- The calculated mean cosine of the angle between transverse space and momentum vectors is about 0.9 at mid-rapidity.
- The Gaussian size parameter Rg converges to about 9.5 fm at mT approaching the proton mass (pT approaching 0).
- RQMD predicts an average transverse collective velocity of about 0.6c at mid-rapidity at RHIC energy, compared to 0.4-0.45c at SPS energy (sqrt(s) approximately 20 AGeV).
- In the no-rescattering calculation, the number of collisions per nucleon is about 2.5 and the collective velocity is about zero over the whole rapidity range.

## Context

This work extends previous observations of mass-dependent slope parameters in transverse mass distributions (NA44 Collaboration, 1997) and momentum-dependent source sizes from two-particle correlations (NA44 and NA49 Collaborations, 1996) by proposing deuteron distributions as an alternative probe of space-momentum correlations. The study uses RQMD, which has been successfully applied at AGS and SPS energies, and finds that the collective flow velocities of pions, kaons, and nucleons are similar at RHIC energies, which Levai and Muller (1991) argued could indicate an earlier deconfined phase in baryon-poor regions. The results enable future measurements at RHIC experiments such as STAR to use deuteron yields and transverse momentum distributions to test whether new physics modifies the space-momentum structure at ultrarelativistic energies.
