# Does HESS J1731-347 have a thick crust ?

> A relativistic mean-field model with a crossing term of isovector-scalar and isoscalar mesons, the Cracow crossing terms (CCT) model, has previously been shown to be capable of explaining the light and compact object HESS J1731-347. Here, the model is supplemented with a correct treatment of the phase transition. Applying the thermodynamically consistent Gibbs conditions shows that a mixed-phase system occurs in the core of the neutron star over a wide range of densities, extending up to the neutron star crust, leading to an intriguing stellar structure with a thick and massive crust.

## Key claim

The CCT relativistic mean-field model, previously shown to explain HESS J1731-347 using the Maxwell construction, produces a thick and massive neutron star crust when the thermodynamically consistent Gibbs conditions are applied instead, because the mixed-phase system extends from the core down to densities as low as the inner crust.

## Method

The authors apply the Gibbs construction for phase coexistence (equality of pressure and chemical potentials for all particle species) to the CCT model with sigma-delta and omega-delta meson crossing terms, using three parameter sets with symmetry energy slope L=40 MeV and varying the sigma coupling Cs. They compute the two-phase equilibrium between a neutron-lepton phase and a neutron-proton-lepton phase, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and crustal properties, joining the inner crust with the SLy4 equation of state at the matching density.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for two-phase system formation: 0.3038 fm^-3 (Cs=12), 0.2849 fm^-3 (Cs=13), 0.2610 fm^-3 (Cs=14)
- Crust-core transition density reaches approximately 2 times saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- For HESS J1731-347, the crust is about 50% thicker than in standard models, with crustal mass and moment of inertia several times larger
- Joining densities with SLy4 crust: 0.0025 fm^-3 (Cs=12), 0.0656 fm^-3 (Cs=13 and 14)
- Symmetry energy slope fixed at L = 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CCT model of Kubis (2023) by replacing the Maxwell construction with the thermodynamically consistent Gibbs conditions, following the framework of Glendenning (1997) for systems with two conserved charges. The resulting thick crust scenario differs from typical nuclear models where the crust-core transition density lies between 0.03 and 0.08 fm^-3, and from the earlier two-phase analysis of Kubis (2020) where the mixed phase disappeared near saturation density. The model enables predictions for thermal evolution and rotational properties of HESS J1731-347 that could be tested against future observations.
