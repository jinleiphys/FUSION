# Deformations of the fermion realization of the sp(4) algebra and its subalgebras

> With a view towards future applications in nuclear physics, the fermion realization of the compact symplectic sp(4) algebra and its q-deformed versions are investigated. Three important reduction chains of the sp(4) algebra are explored in both the classical and deformed cases. The deformed realizations are based on distinct deformations of the fermion creation and annihilation operators. For the primary reduction, the su(2) sub-structure can be interpreted as either the spin, isospin or angular momentum algebra, whereas for the other two reductions su(2) can be associated with pairing between fermions of the same type or pairing between two distinct fermion types. Each reduction provides for a complete classification of the basis states. The deformed induced u(2) representations are reducible in the action spaces of sp(4) and are decomposed into irreducible representations.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a complete fermion realization of the sp(4) algebra and its q-deformed versions, showing that the deformed induced u(2) representations are reducible in the action spaces of sp(4) and must be decomposed into irreducible representations, with the deformation parameter q providing a new degree of freedom that may explain nonlinear effects in nuclear structure.

## Method

The authors begin with the standard fermion realization of sp(4) using creation and annihilation operators c_{m,sigma}^dagger and c_{m,sigma} for particles of type sigma = +/-1 in a j-shell of dimension 2*Omega_j = 2j+1, constructing the ten generators A_{sigma,sigma'}, B_{sigma,sigma'}, and D_{sigma,sigma'}. They then investigate three reduction chains of sp(4) to its u(2) subalgebras: the primary chain where su(2) corresponds to spin/isospin/angular momentum, a second chain where su^0(2) describes pairing between two distinct fermion types, and a third chain where su^+(2) and su^-(2) describe pairing between fermions of the same type. For the deformed case, they introduce q-deformed fermion operators via a transformation alpha_{m,sigma} = theta^{N_sigma/2} c_{m,sigma} and construct the corresponding deformed generators F_{sigma,sigma'}, G_{sigma,sigma'}, and E_{1,-1}, then analyze the same reduction chains for the q-deformed algebra.

## Key numbers

- The fermion space dimension for a given sigma is 2*Omega_j = 2j+1, where j = (2k+1)/2 for k = 0,1,2,...
- The normalization constant eta = 1/sqrt(2*Omega_j) and xi_{sigma,sigma'} = eta / sqrt(1 + delta_{sigma,sigma'})
- The maximum number of particles in a representation of Sp(4) is 4*Omega_j
- The eigenvalue of the second order Casimir operator of Sp(4) on the basis states is (Omega_j + 3)
- The number of particles n = 2(n_1 + n_0 + n_{-1}) takes values n = 0, 2, 4, ..., 4*Omega_j
- The tau quantum number takes values tau = tilde{n}/2, tilde{n}/2 - 2, ..., 1 (odd) or 0 (even), where tilde{n} = min{n, 4*Omega_j - n}
- The seniority quantum number in the SU^0(2) reduction is nu = 2(n_1 + n_{-1})_{max} = 2|i|
- The d quantum number in the SU^+-(2) reduction is d = (Omega_j - n_{0 max})/2

## Context

This work extends the boson realization of sp(4,R) and its deformations previously studied by the same group (Debre, 2000) to the fermion case, which has more direct application in nuclear theory. The sp(4) algebra has been used to explore pairing correlations in nuclei (Ginocchio, 1980; Hecht, 1965; Flowers, 1952) and is related to the SO(5) group. The paper provides the algebraic framework for classifying basis states in three different coupling schemes, enabling future applications to charge-independent pairing, the Lipkin model, and two-dimensional rotations and vibrations in nuclear structure calculations.
