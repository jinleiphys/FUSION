# Leading-Order QCD Equation of State in Strong Magnetic Fields at Nonzero Baryon Chemical Potential

> We present continuum-estimated $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD results for the leading-order Taylor expansion coefficients of the equation of state in strong magnetic fields and at nonzero baryon chemical potential. Simulations employ the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with physical pion masses on lattices of temporal extent $N_τ= 8,\,12$, covering $145 \lesssim T \lesssim 165~\mathrm{MeV}$ and $eB \lesssim 0.8~\mathrm{GeV}^2$, imposing strangeness neutrality with baseline results at electric charge to baryon number ratio $r = 0.4$. We determine the $T$--$eB$ dependence of $q_1$ and $s_1$ (electric charge and strangeness chemical potential ratios), pressure coefficient $P_2$, baryon number density coefficient $N_1^{\rm B}$, and energy-like coefficients $Θ_2$ (trace anomaly), $ε_2$ (energy density), and $σ_2$ (entropy density). Magnetic fields induce temperature-band crossings for $q_1$ and $P_2$ and non-monotonic structures in the energy-like coefficients, with $Θ_2$ at strong fields possibly vanishing or turning negative at higher $T$, indicating dominance of the pressure term over the energy contribution. We also examine the $r$-dependence, finding that $r=0$ (charge-neutral matter) shows the most muted magnetic-field enhancement of $P_2$ despite larger $|q_1|$, providing a useful reference for neutron-star-like conditions. Comparisons with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model show qualitative agreement at low $T$ and weak $eB$, with clear deviations near the crossover and at strong fields. These results provide useful input for constraining models and effective theories of QCD matter in strong magnetic fields at finite baryon density.

## Key claim
The paper presents continuum-estimated (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD results for leading-order Taylor expansion coefficients of the equation of state in strong magnetic fields at nonzero baryon chemical potential, but only for temperatures between 145 and 165 MeV and magnetic fields up to 0.8 GeV², with the caveat that results are leading-order only and rely on Taylor expansion around zero baryon chemical potential to circumvent the sign problem.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with physical pion masses on lattices of temporal extent Nτ = 8 and 12, covering temperatures 145 ≲ T ≲ 165 MeV and magnetic fields eB ≲ 0.8 GeV². They imposed strangeness neutrality with baseline results at electric charge to baryon number ratio r = 0.4, and computed second-order conserved-charge susceptibilities from which they derived the leading-order Taylor expansion coefficients for pressure (P₂), baryon number density (N₁ᴮ), trace anomaly (Θ₂), energy density (ε₂), and entropy density (σ₂) as functions of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range: 145 ≲ T ≲ 165 MeV
- Magnetic field range: eB ≲ 0.8 GeV² (approximately 45 Mπ²)
- Lattice temporal extents: Nτ = 8, 12
- Isospin parameter for baseline results: r = 0.4 (corresponding to Pb/Au collisions)
- The coefficient Θ₂ at strong fields possibly vanishes or turns negative at higher T, indicating dominance of the pressure term over the energy contribution
- For r = 0 (charge-neutral matter), the magnetic-field enhancement of P₂ is most muted despite larger |q₁|

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of magnetic field effects on the QCD equation of state at vanishing baryon chemical potential by Bali et al. (2014) and at finite lattice spacing by Borsanyi et al. (2023, 2025). It builds on the authors' prior calculations of conserved-charge fluctuations at physical pion mass (Ding et al., 2025) and provides first-principles input for constraining effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and hadron resonance gas model, as well as for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions and neutron star modeling.
