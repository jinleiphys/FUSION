# Quarkonium-Nucleus Bound States from Lattice QCD

> Quarkonium-nucleus systems are composed of two interacting hadronic states without common valence quarks, which interact primarily through multi-gluon exchanges, realizing a color van der Waals force. We present lattice QCD calculations of the interactions of strange and charm quarkonia with light nuclei. Both the strangeonium-nucleus and charmonium-nucleus systems are found to be relatively deeply bound when the masses of the three light quarks are set equal to that of the physical strange quark. Extrapolation of these results to the physical light-quark masses suggests that the binding energy of charmonium to nuclear matter is B < 40 MeV.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations demonstrate that both strangeonium-nucleus and charmonium-nucleus systems are relatively deeply bound at the flavor-SU(3) symmetric point where the light quark masses equal the physical strange quark mass, but extrapolation to physical light-quark masses suggests the charmonium-nuclear matter binding energy is less than 40 MeV, with the caveat that the systematic uncertainties from the observed relative-velocity dependence of extracted binding energies are not yet fully quantified.

## Method

The NPLQCD Collaboration performed lattice QCD calculations of quarkonium-nucleus correlation functions for atomic numbers A=1 through 4 at a single lattice spacing with multiple lattice volumes (spatial extents L=24 and L=32), using the same lattice technology and parameters as in previous light-nuclei calculations. The calculations were performed at the flavor SU(3)-symmetric point with light quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of approximately 805 MeV, and binding energies were extracted from the center-of-mass frame energies after removing the rest masses of the constituents.

## Key numbers

- At the SU(3) symmetric point (M_pi = M_K ~ 805 MeV), charmonium-nuclear matter binding energy is inferred to be B^NM ~ 60 MeV, assuming saturation of interactions for A=3 and A=4 systems
- Extrapolation to physical light-quark masses yields an estimated charmonium-nuclear matter binding energy of B^NM_phys less than 40 MeV
- For the N-eta_c system, the scattering length is found to be a ~ 1 fm when higher order terms in the effective range expansion are ignored
- The expected energy difference between the lowest-lying continuum states in L=24 and L=32 volumes is delta E ~ 7 MeV, larger than the observed difference of ~ 2 MeV between extracted ground state energies
- The estimated contamination from scattering states would make the binding energy approximately 1.5 MeV deeper than the central values reported

## Context

This work provides the first lattice QCD demonstration of quarkonium-nucleus bound states for A less than 5, extending prior phenomenological predictions by Brodsky, Schmidt and de Teramond (1990) who predicted bound states for A greater than or equal to 3 using a Yukawa toy model, and by Wasson (1991) who found smaller binding energies after accounting for the extended volume of large nuclei. The calculations enable future lattice QCD studies at smaller light-quark masses and lattice spacings to determine whether these systems remain bound at the physical point, which would guide experimental searches at Jefferson Lab (ATHENNA), FAIR (PANDA), and J-PARC.
