# Elastic electromagnetic form factors of vector mesons

> A symmetry-preserving approach to the two valence-body continuum bound-state problem is used to calculate the elastic electromagnetic form factors of the $Žü$-meson and subsequently to study the evolution of vector-meson form factors with current-quark mass. To facilitate a range of additional comparisons, $K^\ast$ form factors are also computed. The analysis reveals that: vector mesons are larger than pseudoscalar mesons; composite vector mesons are non-spherical, with magnetic and quadrupole moments that deviate $\sim 30$\% from point-particle values; in many ways, vector-meson properties are as much influenced by emergent mass as those of pseudoscalars; and vector meson electric form factors possess a zero at spacelike momentum transfer. Qualitative similarities between the electric form factors of the $Žü$ and the proton, $G_E^p$, are used to argue that the character of emergent mass in the Standard Model can force a zero in $G_E^p$. Morover, the existence of a zero in vector meson electric form factors entails that a single-pole vector meson dominance model can only be of limited use in estimating properties of off-shell vector mesons, providing poor guidance for systems in which the Higgs-mechanism of mass generation is dominant.

## Key claim

The paper argues that vector meson electric form factors possess a zero at spacelike momentum transfer, and that qualitative similarities between the electric form factors of the rho meson and the proton suggest that emergent mass in the Standard Model can force a zero in the proton's electric form factor. The authors qualify that a single-pole vector meson dominance model can only be of limited use for off-shell vector mesons, particularly in systems where the Higgs mechanism of mass generation is dominant.

## Method

The authors employed a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to compute the elastic electromagnetic form factors of vector mesons. They solved the dressed-quark gap equation, the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed-quark-photon vertices, and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the vector meson bound-state amplitudes, then combined these elements to extract the form factors F1, F2, and F3 as functions of momentum transfer. The interaction kernel used was a two-parameter model that preserves one-loop renormalization group behavior of QCD, with parameters fitted to reproduce the pion's mass and leptonic decay constant for light quarks and a separate parameter set for heavy quarks.

## Key numbers

* Rho meson charge radius: r_rho = 0.72 fm; magnetic moment: mu_rho = 2.01; quadrupole moment: Q_rho = -0.36
* K*+ meson charge radius: r_K*+ = 0.64 fm; magnetic moment: mu_K*+ = 2.22; quadrupole moment: Q_K*+ = -0.31
* Rho_c (charm-like vector meson) charge radius: r_rho_c = 0.24 fm; magnetic moment: mu_rho_c = 2.12; quadrupole moment: Q_rho_c = -0.33
* For the rho meson, the compositeness-induced deviations from point-particle values are 1-kappa_gamma = 0.32 and lambda_gamma = 0.32, indicating roughly 30% deviation in both magnetic and quadrupole moments
* The product f_V * r_V is approximately constant at 0.53(3) for systems composed of light quarks with current masses less than or equal to the strange quark mass
* The zero in the proton's electric form factor is estimated at Q_p^2 approximately 8.7 GeV^2 from linear extrapolation of experimental data

## Context

This work extends previous DSE calculations of vector meson form factors by Bhagwat (2006) and lattice QCD simulations by Owen (2015) and Dudek (2006), providing a systematic study of how form factors evolve with current-quark mass from light to heavy quarks. The results enable comparisons between vector and pseudoscalar meson properties, showing that vector mesons are larger than their pseudoscalar counterparts, and that the zero in the electric form factor moves toward smaller momentum transfer as current-quark mass increases. The analysis also provides a theoretical foundation for understanding the potential zero in the proton's electric form factor observed at Jefferson Lab, using the simpler two-body vector meson system as a proxy for the three-body proton problem.
