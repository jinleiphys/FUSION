# New Calculations of Recombination Rates for Cold $^4$He Atoms and Determination of Universal Scaling Functions

> Three-body recombination rates for cold $^4$He are calculated with a new method which exploits the simple relationship between the imaginary part of the atom-dimer elastic scattering phase shift and the $S$-matrix for recombination. The elastic phase shifts are computed above breakup threshold by solving a three-body Faddeev equation in momentum space with inputs based on a variety of modern atom-atom potentials. Recombination coefficients for the HFD-B3-FCII potential agree very well with the only previously published results. Since the elastic scattering and recombination processes for $^4$He are governed by "Efimov physics", they depend on universal functions of a scaling variable. The newly computed recombination coefficients for potentials other than HFD-B3-FCII make it possible to determine these universal functions for the first time.

# New Calculations of Recombination Rates for Cold $^4$He Atoms and Determination of Universal Scaling Functions

## Key claim

Three-body recombination coefficients for cold $^4$He can be accurately computed from the imaginary part of the atom-dimer elastic scattering phase shift above breakup threshold, and the newly computed values for multiple atom-atom potentials (HFD-B, LM2M2, TTY) allow the first determination of the universal scaling functions $h_1$ and $h_2$ that govern recombination when Efimov physics dominates, provided the breakup energy remains below the Stueckelberg minima where the universal assumptions break down.

## Method

The author solves the momentum-space Faddeev equation for three-body scattering above breakup threshold using a separable atom-atom interaction with a finite-range form factor, then extracts the complex elastic atom-dimer phase shift via the Hetherington-Schick contour deformation method. The recombination coefficient $K_3$ is obtained from the inelastic $S$-matrix element, which is directly related to the imaginary part of the phase shift. Calculations are performed for four atom-atom potentials (HFD-B3-FCII, HFD-B, LM2M2, TTY) using both finite-range form factors and effective theory with effective range expansion (ET-ERE) at LO, NLO, and NNLO.

## Key numbers

- Recombination coefficient for HFD-B3-FCII at zero breakup energy: $K_3^{(0)}(L=0) = 7.06 \times 10^{-28}$ cm$^6$/s, in agreement with the previously published value of $7.09 \times 10^{-28}$ cm$^6$/s from Suno et al. (2002)
- Recombination coefficients for other potentials at zero breakup energy: HFD-B gives $4.10 \times 10^{-28}$ cm$^6$/s, LM2M2 gives $24.7 \times 10^{-28}$ cm$^6$/s, TTY gives $22.7 \times 10^{-28}$ cm$^6$/s
- Atom-dimer scattering lengths from form-factor calculations: HFD-B3-FCII = 12.017 nm, HFD-B = 12.149 nm, LM2M2 = 11.422 nm, TTY = 11.548 nm
- Ratio ${\bar a}_2/a_{0*}$ for HFD-B3-FCII = 1.1477, consistent with the value of approximately 1.15 reported by Braaten, Kang, and Platter (2006)
- Imaginary part of the $L=0$ phase shift near threshold is typically of order $10^{-5}$ degree
- Dimer binding energies: HFD-B3-FCII = 1.58730 mK, HFD-B = 1.68541 mK, LM2M2 = 1.30348 mK, TTY = 1.30962 mK
- Trimer excited state binding energies: HFD-B3-FCII = 2.624 mK, HFD-B = 2.74 mK, LM2M2 = 2.28 mK, TTY = 2.28 mK

## Context

This work extends the only previous "ab initio" calculation of $^4$He three-body recombination coefficients, which was performed by Suno et al. (2002) using only the HFD-B3-FCII potential, by providing results for three additional atom-atom potentials (HFD-B, LM2M2, TTY). The new results enable the first extraction of the universal functions $h_1$ and $h_2$ introduced by Braaten, Kang, and Platter (2006), which are essential for universality-based analyses of Efimov physics in systems such as $^{133}$Cs studied by the Innsbruck group (Naegerl, 2006). The method also confirms the unusual low-energy behavior of $p \cot\delta_0$ for $^4$He atom-dimer scattering noted by Penkov (2003), and the atom-dimer scattering lengths agree well with those of Roudnev (2002), Penkov (2003), and Platter and Phillips (2006).
