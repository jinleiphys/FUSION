# Fission fragment mass yields of Th to Rf even-even nuclei

> Fission properties of the actinide nuclei are deduced from theoretical analysis. We investigate potential energy surfaces and fission barriers and predict the fission fragment mass-yields of actinide isotopes. The results are compared with experimental data where available. The calculations were performed in the macroscopic-microscopic approximation with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) for the macroscopic part and the microscopic energy corrections were evaluated in the Yukawa-folded potential. The Fourier nuclear shape parametrization is used to describe the nuclear shape, including the non-axial degree of freedom. The fission fragment mass-yields of considered nuclei are evaluated within a 3D collective model using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the macroscopic-microscopic model with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) macroscopic energy and Yukawa-folded single-particle potential, combined with a 3D collective model using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, can reproduce the main features of fission fragment mass yields for even-even actinide nuclei from Th to Rf, but the agreement is quantitatively less satisfactory for thorium isotopes unless the two adjustable parameters (zero-point energy and neck-breaking half-width) are refitted specifically to the Th data.

## Method

The authors used the macroscopic-microscopic approximation with the LSD model for the macroscopic part and Yukawa-folded potential for microscopic shell and pairing corrections, with a Fourier nuclear shape parametrization including non-axial degrees of freedom. They solved a 3D collective model using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to separate the slow fission motion (elongation coordinate q2) from fast vibrations in the mass-asymmetry (q3) and neck (q4) coordinates, approximating the probability distribution by a Wigner function with a generalized temperature. The fission fragment mass yields were obtained by integrating the probability distribution over the neck parameter and applying a neck-rupture probability that depends on the neck radius, with two free parameters: the zero-point energy E0 and the half-width d of the neck-breaking probability.

## Key numbers

* Optimized global values of the two adjustable parameters: zero-point energy E0 = 2.2 MeV and neck-breaking half-width d = 1.6 fm, obtained by minimizing the overlap integral between theoretical and experimental yields across all nuclei.
* For thorium isotopes only, a refit gave E0 = 1.5 MeV and d = 2.5 fm, yielding much more satisfactory agreement with experimental data.
* The fission barrier heights obtained from the topographical theorem using the LSD model deviate from experimental data by only 310 keV on average.
* The universal pairing strength used is G N^(2/3) = 0.28 hbar omega0, with hbar omega0 = 41/A^(1/3) MeV, and the pairing window contains 2 sqrt(15 N) single-particle energy levels closest to the Fermi level.
* The average slope of the fission valley potential from the last saddle to scission in thorium nuclei is almost three times smaller than in nobelium.
* In 218Th, the barrier towards symmetric fission from the third minimum at q2 approx 1.2 is less than 0.5 MeV, while the barrier towards asymmetric fission is about 1 MeV and thicker.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on fission fragment mass yields for Pu isotopes (Pomorski et al., 2017, 2018) and Pt to Ra isotopes (Pomorski et al., 2020) to the broader actinide region from Th to Rf. It uses the same Fourier shape parametrization (Pomorski et al., 2015) and macroscopic-microscopic approach with LSD energy (Pomorski and Dudek, 2003) and Yukawa-folded potential (Davies et al., 1976; Dobrowolski et al., 2016) that were previously applied to lighter systems. The results enable systematic predictions of fission fragment mass yields for nuclei where experimental data are scarce, and the identified discrepancies in Th isotopes point to the need for more advanced dynamical models such as Langevin dynamics or improved quantum molecular dynamics models to account for energy dissipation and the full fission dynamics.
