# Unpolarized Quark and Gluon TMD PDFs and FFs at N$^3$LO

> In this paper we calculate analytically the perturbative matching coefficients for unpolarized quark and gluon Transverse-Momentum-Dependent (TMD) Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs) and Fragmentation Functions (FFs) through Next-to-Next-to-Next-to-Leading Order (N$^3$LO) in QCD. The N$^3$LO TMD PDFs are calculated by solving a system of differential equation of Feynman and phase space integrals. The TMD FFs are obtained by analytic continuation from space-like quantities to time-like quantities, taking into account the probability interpretation of TMD PDFs and FFs properly. The coefficient functions for TMD FFs exhibit double logarithmic enhancement at small momentum fraction $z$. We resum such logarithmic terms to the third order in the expansion of $α_s$. Our results constitute important ingredients for precision determination of TMD PDFs and FFs in current and future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the perturbative matching coefficients for unpolarized quark and gluon Transverse-Momentum-Dependent Parton Distribution Functions and Fragmentation Functions through Next-to-Next-to-Next-to-Leading Order in QCD, with the TMD Fragmentation Functions obtained by analytic continuation from space-like to time-like quantities, and the coefficient functions for TMD FFs exhibit double logarithmic enhancement at small momentum fraction z that is resummed to third order in the strong coupling expansion.

## Method

The N$^3$LO TMD PDFs are calculated by solving a system of differential equations for Feynman and phase space integrals, following the method of Luo (2019). The TMD FFs are obtained by analytic continuation from space-like quantities to time-like quantities using the approach of Chen (2020), which exploits the fact that space-like and time-like splitting amplitudes are analytic functions, with the triple real and double real-virtual parts having trivial continuation while the double virtual-real and virtual squared-real parts are calculated using the corresponding splitting amplitudes. The results are expressed in terms of harmonic polylogarithms up to transcendental weight 5, with numerical fits provided for the range $0 < x, z < 1$ accurate to 0.1 percent.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop scale-independent coefficient functions: $I^{(1)}_{qq}(x) = 2.66667 \bar{x}$, $I^{(1)}_{qg}(x) = 2 x \bar{x}$, $I^{(1)}_{gq}(x) = 2.66667 x$, $I^{(1)}_{gg}(x) = 0$
- The two-loop threshold limit for quark TMD PDFs: $\mathcal{I}^{(2)}_{qq}(z) = \frac{1}{(1-z)_+} \left[ \left(28 \zeta_3 - \frac{808}{27}\right) C_A C_F + \frac{224}{27} C_F N_f T_F \right]$
- The three-loop threshold limit for quark TMD PDFs: $\mathcal{I}^{(3)}_{qq}(z) = \frac{1}{(1-z)_+} \left[ \left(-\frac{1648 \zeta_2}{81} - \frac{1808 \zeta_3}{27} + \frac{40 \zeta_4}{3} + \frac{125252}{729}\right) C_A C_F N_f T_F + \left(-\frac{176}{3} \zeta_3 \zeta_2 + \frac{6392 \zeta_2}{81} + \frac{12328 \zeta_3}{27} + \frac{154 \zeta_4}{3} - 192 \zeta_5 - \frac{297029}{729}\right) C_A^2 C_F + \left(-\frac{608 \zeta_3}{9} - 32 \zeta_4 + \frac{3422}{27}\right) C_F^2 N_f T_F + \left(-\frac{128}{9} \zeta_3 - \frac{7424}{729}\right) C_F N_f^2 T_F^2 \right]$
- The threshold limit exhibits Casimir scaling up to three loops: $\lim_{z\to1} \frac{\mathcal{I}^{(n)}_{gg}(z)}{\mathcal{I}^{(n)}_{qq}(z)} = \lim_{z\to1} \frac{\mathcal{C}^{(n)}_{gg}(z)}{\mathcal{C}^{(n)}_{qq}(z)} = \frac{C_A}{C_F}$

## Context

This work extends the first N$^3$LO calculation of unpolarized quark TMD PDFs by Luo (2019) to include unpolarized gluon TMD PDFs and both quark and gluon TMD FFs, building on previous NNLO calculations by Gehrmann (2012, 2014), Echevarria (2016), and Luo (2019). The results provide essential ingredients for precision determination of TMD PDFs and FFs in current and future experiments, enabling N$^3$LL resummation for large logarithms at small transverse momentum, as previously achieved by Chen (2018), Bizon (2018), and others at lower orders.
