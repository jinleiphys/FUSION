# Influence of the projectile description on breakup calculations

> Calculations of the breakup of 8B and 11Be are performed with the aim of analyzing their sensitivity to the projectile description. Several potentials adjusted on the same experimental data are used for each projectile. The results vary significantly with the potential choice, and this sensitivity differs from one projectile to the other. In the 8B case, the breakup cross section is approximately scaled by the asymptotic normalization coefficient of the initial bound state (ANC). For $^{11}$Be, the overall normalization of the breakup cross section is no longer solely determined by the ANC. The partial waves describing the continuum are found to play a significant role in this variation, as the sensitivity of the phase shifts to the projectile description changes with the physical constraints imposed to the potential.

## Key claim

The breakup cross section is not universally proportional to the square of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the initial bound state; for 11Be, the continuum description (partial waves and phase shifts) introduces variations of up to 20% among potentials fitted to the same bound-state energies, and up to 40% when a potential that does not reproduce the excited bound state is used.

## Method

The authors performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations for 8B breakup on 58Ni at 25.75 MeV and time-dependent semiclassical calculations for 11Be breakup on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon. For each projectile, they used several core-fragment potentials (five for 8B, six for 11Be) that were all adjusted to reproduce the same bound-state energies, and compared the resulting breakup cross sections after dividing by the square of the ANC to isolate the effect of the continuum description.

## Key numbers

- For 8B on 58Ni at 25.75 MeV, the breakup cross sections divided by the square of the ANC differ by approximately 4% near the maximum around 0.5 MeV relative energy when using potentials T1–T5.
- For 8B, the p1/2 resonance energy varies from 1.3 MeV (T5) to 2.8 MeV (T1), with widths from 0.7 MeV to 2.2 MeV, causing a bump in the cross section whose location shifts with potential choice.
- For 11Be on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon, potential V6 (which reproduces only the ground state) gives a breakup cross section 40% larger than potentials V1–V5.
- For 11Be, potentials V1–V5 (all fitted to the same three lowest levels) produce breakup cross sections that vary by up to 20% after dividing by the square of the ANC.
- The 8B ground state binding energy is 137 keV for all potentials T1–T5; the 11Be ground state binding energy is 0.504 MeV for all potentials V1–V6.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Typel and Baur (2004, 2005), who used effective-range theory to show that electromagnetic strengths of halo nuclei depend on both bound-state properties (like the ANC) and continuum parameters (like scattering lengths). It also follows the study by Moro and Nunes (2005) on the sensitivity of transfer cross sections to single-particle parameters. The results question the standard method of extracting spectroscopic factors from breakup data, as done by Fukuda et al. (2004) for 11Be, and suggest that the continuum description must be carefully constrained before reliable ANCs or spectroscopic factors can be obtained.
