# Three-nucleon force contribution to the deuteron channel in $(d,p)$ reactions

> The contribution of a three-nucleon (3N) force, acting between the neutron and proton in the incoming deuteron with a target nucleon, to the deuteron-target potential in the entrance channel of the $A(d,p)B$ reaction has been calculated within the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA). Four different 3N interaction sets from local chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) were used. Strong sensitivity of the adiabatic deuteron-target potential to the choice of the 3N force format has been found, which originates from the enhanced sensitivity to the short-range physics of nucleon-nucleon (NN) and 3N interactions in the ADWA. Such a sensitivity is reduced when a Watanabe folding model is used to generate $d$-$A$ potential instead of ADWA. The impact of the 3N force contribution on $(d,p)$ cross sections depends on assumptions made about the $p$-$A$ and $n$-$A$ optical potentials used to calculate the distorted $d$-$A$ potential in the entrance channel. It is different for local and nonlocal optical potentials and depends on whether the induced three-body force arising due to neglect of target excitations is included or not.

## Key claim

The contribution of a three-nucleon (3N) force to the deuteron-target adiabatic potential in $(d,p)$ reactions is strongly sensitive to the choice of the 3N force format and to the short-range physics of nucleon-nucleon and 3N interactions, but this sensitivity is reduced when a Watanabe folding model is used instead of the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA); the impact on cross sections further depends on whether local or nonlocal optical potentials are employed and on whether an induced three-body force from target excitation is included.

## Method

The authors calculated the 3N force contribution to the deuteron-target potential in the entrance channel of $A(d,p)B$ reactions within the ADWA, using four different 3N interaction sets from local chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) taken from Lynn et al. (2016). They evaluated the effective $d$-$i$ potential by folding the 3N interaction with the deuteron wave function and vertex function, then constructed the adiabatic $d$-$A$ potential by folding this effective interaction with target densities from electron scattering data. The calculations were performed for $^{40}$Ca and $^{27}$Al targets using a range of nucleon-target optical potentials, and were also repeated within the Watanabe folding model for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For a zero-range hypercentral 3N force with volume integral $I_3 = 2186$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^6$, the adiabatic potential at the origin is $U_{3N}(0) \approx -33$ MeV using $\phi_1(0) = -1.17$ fm$^{-3/2}$ and $\phi_0(0) = 0.0796$ fm$^{-3/2}$ from the N2LO $\chi$EFT NN model, with average target density $\rho_A(0) \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The depth of $U_{3N}(R)$ for the $d+^{26}$Al system decreases from 33 MeV to 0 MeV as the hyperradius $\rho_0$ increases from 0 to $\sim 0.5$ fm, then changes sign and becomes repulsive up to $\sim 5.7$ MeV for $0.5 \lesssim \rho_0 \lesssim 0.8$ fm.
- The volume integrals of the $\lambda=0$ components of the effective $d$-$i$ potential for contact 3N forces are 31.1 MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ for the $E\tau$ format (set II) and 37.4 MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ for the $E1$ format (set I), a difference of about 20%.
- The deuteron $d$-state contributes about 50% to the $U_{3N}$ potential for the contact 3N force in the $d-^{40}$Ca system.

## Context

This work extends the study of 3N force effects in direct reactions beyond the simplified hypercentral contact interaction model used by Timofeyuk (2018), which assessed the 3N force contribution through an additional term in the DWBA or ADWA $T$-matrix element. The present calculations address the $n$-$p$-$A$ three-body force arising from bare 3N interactions, which had not been considered previously and complements the effective induced three-body force studied by Dinmore et al. (2019) that arises from neglect of target excitation. The results enable future development of deuteron stripping and pickup reaction theory by including 3N forces consistently, as listed among outstanding tasks by Timofeyuk (2020).
