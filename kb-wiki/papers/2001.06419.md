# Beam energy dependence of net-$Λ$ fluctuations measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC

> The measurements of particle multiplicity distributions have generated considerable interest in understanding the fluctuations of conserved quantum numbers in the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) hadronization regime, in particular near a possible critical point and near the chemical freeze-out. We report the measurement of efficiency and centrality bin width corrected cumulant ratios ($C_{2}/C_{1}$, $C_{3}/C_{2}$) of net-$Λ$ distributions, in the context of both strangeness and baryon number conservation, as a function of collision energy, centrality and rapidity. The results are for Au + Au collisions at five beam energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV) recorded with the Solenoidal Tracker at RHIC (STAR). We compare our results to the Poisson and negative binomial (NBD) expectations, as well as to Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) and Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model predictions. Both NBD and Poisson baselines agree with data within the statistical and systematic uncertainties. The ratios of the measured cumulants show no features of critical fluctuations. The chemical freeze-out temperatures extracted from a recent HRG calculation, which was successfully used to describe the net-proton, net-kaon and net-charge data, indicate $Λ$ freeze-out conditions similar to those of kaons. However, large deviations are found when comparing to temperatures obtained from net-proton fluctuations. The net-$Λ$ cumulants show a weak, but finite, dependence on the rapidity coverage in the acceptance of the detector, which can be attributed to quantum number conservation.

## Key claim

The measured cumulant ratios (C2/C1, C3/C2) of net-Lambda distributions in Au+Au collisions at five beam energies show no features of critical fluctuations, and both Poisson and negative binomial baselines agree with the data within statistical and systematic uncertainties; however, the UrQMD model describes the measured net-Lambda C1 and C3 at 200 GeV reasonably well but deviates from C2, with the deviation increasing as a function of collision energy.

## Method

The STAR experiment reconstructed Lambda and anti-Lambda baryons via their decay into p + pi- and anti-p + pi+, respectively, using the Time Projection Chamber for tracking and particle identification via ionization energy loss. Event-by-event net-Lambda multiplicities were measured in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, with efficiency corrections applied per centrality bin using a pT-dependent method and centrality bin width correction applied to account for volume fluctuations. Cumulant ratios C2/C1 and C3/C2 were calculated from the first three cumulants of the net-Lambda distributions and compared to Poisson and negative binomial baselines, as well as to UrQMD and Hadron Resonance Gas model predictions.

## Key numbers

*   Five beam energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions.
*   Number of events analyzed: 16 x 10^6 (19.6 GeV), 33 x 10^6 (27 GeV), 77 x 10^6 (39 GeV), 27 x 10^6 (62.4 GeV), and 199 x 10^6 (200 GeV).
*   Lambda reconstruction efficiency in 0-5% most central collisions ranged from 0.127 (200 GeV) to 0.183 (19.6 GeV) for pT between 0.9 and 2.0 GeV/c.
*   Typical systematic uncertainties: 15% for C1, 18% for C2, and 30% for C3; for the cumulant ratios, systematic uncertainties were 2.4% for C2/C1 and 22.7% for C3/C2 at 200 GeV in 0-5% centrality.
*   Signal purity greater than 90% for all collision energies achieved through topological cuts.

## Context

This work extends the STAR Beam Energy Scan program's fluctuation measurements from net-protons and net-kaons to net-Lambdas, which carry both baryon number and strangeness. The results are compared to previous STAR net-proton and net-kaon fluctuation measurements (Adamczyk et al., 2013) and to HRG model calculations (Bellwied et al., 2018) that successfully described net-proton, net-kaon, and net-charge data; the extracted chemical freeze-out temperatures for Lambdas are similar to those of kaons but show large deviations from net-proton temperatures, supporting the concept of sequential hadronization. The measurements enable future studies of the off-diagonal baryon-strangeness correlator and, when combined with net-kaon and net-proton results, provide a more complete picture of net-strangeness and net-baryon number fluctuations.
