# Nuclear surface studies with antiprotonic atom X-rays

> The recent and older level shifts and widths in pbar atoms are analyzed. The results are fitted by an antiproton-nucleus optical potential with two basic complex strength parameters. These parameters are related to average S and P wave scattering parameters in the subthreshold energy region. A fair consistency of the X-ray data for all Z values, stopping pbar data and the Nbar-N scattering data has been achieved. The determination of neutron density profiles at the nuclear surface is undertaken, and the determination of the neutron R_{rms} radii is attempted. Uncertainties due to the input data and the procedure are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that antiprotonic atom X-ray data can be used to extract neutron density profiles and root-mean-square (rms) radii at the nuclear surface, but the extracted radii depend strongly on the choice of input charge density profiles and on assumptions about the isospin dependence of the antiproton-nucleon absorption amplitudes, with a persistent discrepancy between the absorption ratio \(R_{n/p}\) inferred from atomic data and that measured in chamber experiments.

## Method

The authors constructed a complex optical potential for antiprotons bound in atomic states, consisting of local \(S\)-wave and gradient \(P\)-wave terms with strengths related to subthreshold \(\bar{N}N\) scattering amplitudes. They fitted this potential to 117 X-ray data points (level shifts and widths) across 37 isotopes from N to U, using charge densities from muonic atom data (Fricke et al., 1995) and parametrizing neutron densities with either variable diffuseness or half-density radius. Constraints from antiprotonic atoms of light nuclei (H, He) and from the Paris \(\bar{N}N\) potential model were applied, and separate fits to lower and upper atomic levels were performed to account for energy-dependent absorption.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit optical potential parameters: \(a_S(\text{"lower"}) = -1.60 - i\,1.10 f_i\) fm, \(a_S(\text{"upper"}) = -1.60 - i\,0.74 f_i\) fm, \(a_P = -0.0 - i\,0.39\) fm\(^3\), folding range \(r_o = 0.79\) fm, with \(f_i = 4/3\) for protons and \(2/3\) for neutrons.
- Total \(\chi^2 = 247\) for 117 data points (\(\chi^2/N = 2.11\)) for the best-fit potential consistent with chamber constraints.
- Best-fit neutron-proton rms radius difference trend: \(\Delta R_{np} = -0.10(2) + 1.65(5)\,\delta\), where \(\delta = (N-Z)/(N+Z)\).
- Average capture ratio from the best-fit potential: \(R_{n/p} = 0.86(4)\) for the radiochemical region.
- Recoil energy in heavy nuclei: \(<E_{\text{rec}}> \approx 11\) MeV, with a 5 MeV difference between lower and upper atomic states.
- Average antiproton absorption radii in lower atomic orbits correspond to charge densities of about 5% of central density; upper level radii are larger by 0.2–0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological optical potential fits to antiprotonic atom data by Batty et al. (1997) and Friedman et al. (2005), incorporating a larger data set, recoil effects in the \(P\)-wave interaction, and constraints from light antiprotonic atoms and the Paris \(\bar{N}N\) potential model. The extracted neutron radii are compared with trends from hadronic scattering data (Jastrzebski et al., 2004) and pionic atom analyses. The paper enables improved determination of neutron density distributions at nuclear surfaces and addresses whether antiprotonic data can distinguish a neutron skin from a neutron halo as defined in Trzcinska et al. (2001).
