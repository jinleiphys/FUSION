# Three-body problem with velocity-dependent optical potentials: a case of $(d,p)$ reactions

> The change in mass of a nucleon, arising from its interactions with other nucleons inside the target, results in velocity-dependent terms in the Schrödinger equation that describes nucleon scattering. It has recently been suggested in a number of publications that introducing and fitting velocity-dependent terms improves the quality of the description of nucleon scattering data for various nuclei. The present paper discusses velocity-dependent optical potentials in a context of a three-body problem used to account for deuteron breakup in the entrance channel of $(d,p)$ reactions. Such potentials form a particular class of nonlocal optical potentials which are a popular object of modern studies. It is shown here that because of a particular structure of the velocity-dependent terms the three-body problem can be formulated in two different ways. Solving this problem within an adiabatic approximation results in a significant difference between the two approaches caused by contributions from the high $n$-$p$ momenta in deuteron in one of them. Solving the three-body problem beyond the adiabatic approximation may remove such contributions, which is indirectly confirmed by replacing the adiabatic approximation by the folding Watanabe model where such contributions are suppressed. Discussion of numerical results is carried out for the $^{40}$Ca($d,p)^{41}$Ca reaction where experimental data both on elastic scattering in entrance and exit channels and on nucleon transfer are available.

# Three-body problem with velocity-dependent optical potentials: a case of $(d,p)$ reactions

## Key claim
Velocity-dependent optical potentials in the three-body problem for $(d,p)$ reactions can be formulated in two different ways, and solving within an adiabatic approximation produces significant differences between the two approaches due to contributions from high $n$-$p$ momenta in the deuteron; however, solving beyond the adiabatic approximation may remove such contributions, as indirectly confirmed by the folding Watanabe model where these contributions are suppressed.

## Method
The paper formulates the three-body $A+n+p$ problem with velocity-dependent $n-A$ and $p-A$ optical potentials in two equivalent representations: one using first derivatives only (Case I) and another using both first and second derivatives (Case II). The adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) is applied to each case, and the resulting deuteron adiabatic potentials are used to calculate $(d,p)$ transfer cross sections for the $^{40}$Ca($d,p)^{41}$Ca reaction at 20 MeV deuteron incident energy, using the code TWOFNR with externally modified distorted waves and Perey factors.

## Key numbers
- The $^{40}$Ca($d,p)^{41}$Ca reaction was calculated at a deuteron incident laboratory energy of **20 MeV** in the zero-range approximation.
- The velocity-dependent optical potential used a surface form with parameters $\rho_0 = 0.4$, $r_{\rho} = 1.63$ fm, and $a_{\rho} = 0.90$ fm, fitted to improve elastic scattering data for neutrons at **10 MeV** and protons at **10 and 22 MeV** on $^{40}$Ca.
- The overlap integral between $^{41}$Ca and $^{40}$Ca ground states used a spectroscopic factor $S = 0.73$ from the nonlocal dispersive optical model (NLDOM) of Mahzoon (2014) and updated by Waldecker (2016).
- Multiplication of deuteron distorted waves by the Perey factor reduced $(d,p)$ cross sections by approximately **8%** without noticeable shape changes; additional multiplication of proton distorted waves by the nucleon Perey factor reduced cross sections further by about **5%**.
- The local equivalent potentials from the velocity-dependent potential of Jagiello (2012a) produced a repulsive narrow peak of height approximately **60 MeV** in the surface region, deemed unphysical.

## Context
This work extends the study of velocity-dependent optical potentials, previously introduced by Jagiello (2011, 2012a, 2012b), Zurek (2013), and Ghabar (2015) for nucleon elastic scattering, into the three-body problem for $(d,p)$ reactions. It builds on the Johnson-Tandy adiabatic distorted wave approximation (1974) and addresses the sensitivity to high $n$-$p$ momenta previously identified by Bailey (2016) in adiabatic treatments of nonlocal potentials. The results enable future development of continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations for velocity-dependent potentials, which could be simpler than extending CDCC beyond leading order for general nonlocal potentials, as noted by Gomez-Ramos (2018) and Deltuva (2018).
