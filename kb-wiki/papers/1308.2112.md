# Three-body calculation of the Delta-Delta dibaryon candidate D(03) at 2.37 GeV

> The D(03) dibaryon is generated dynamically as a resonance pole in a pion-nucleon-Delta' three-body model, where Delta' is a stable Delta baryon. Using separable interactions dominated by the Delta(1232) isobar for pion-nucleon and by the D(12) isobar for nucleon-Delta', where D(12) is the nucleon-Delta 2150 MeV dibaryon deduced in and constrained by 1D2 pp scattering, the model reduces to an effective two-body problem for Delta-Delta' which is solved. The mass and width of D(03) are found close to those of the I(JP)=0(3+) resonance peak observed by WASA@COSY in pion-production pn collisions at 2.37 GeV.

# Three-body calculation of the Delta-Delta dibaryon candidate D(03) at 2.37 GeV

## Key claim

The D(03) dibaryon at 2.37 GeV is generated dynamically as a resonance pole in a pi N Delta' three-body model, where Delta' is a stable Delta baryon, and the model reduces to an effective two-body problem for Delta-Delta' which is solved. The calculated mass and width agree with the WASA@COSY observed resonance peak, but the authors note that the calculated Breit-Wigner mass and width values for comparison with experiment should each be about 10 MeV higher than the pole values reported.

## Method

The authors constructed a pi N Delta' three-body model using separable pairwise interactions: a p-wave pi N interaction dominated by the Delta(1232) isobar, and an s-wave N Delta' interaction dominated by the D(12)(2150) dibaryon isobar, with the pi Delta' interaction neglected. The three-body Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics were reduced to an effective two-body Delta-Delta' eigenvalue problem, which was solved numerically by extending the integral equation into the complex plane to search for resonance poles of the form W = M - i (Gamma/2). The Delta' spectator mass in the in-medium pi N propagator was replaced by a physical Delta effective complex mass with a 2/3 suppression factor accounting for Delta decay phase space in the I(J^P)=0(3+) system.

## Key numbers

- D(03) pole mass: M = 2363 ± 20 MeV, averaged over best-fit solutions
- D(03) pole width: Gamma = 65 ± 17 MeV, averaged over best-fit solutions
- D(12) dibaryon pole from the same model: E = 2151(2) - i 60(3) MeV
- Delta-Delta threshold: 2460 MeV
- pi-D(12) threshold: 2290 MeV
- WASA@COSY observed resonance width: approximately 70 MeV
- D(03) mass values from individual fits ranged from 2343 to 2392 MeV depending on form factor choices
- D(03) width values from individual fits ranged from 24 to 52 MeV depending on form factor choices

## Context

This work extends earlier SU(6) symmetry arguments by Dyson (1964) predicting D(12) and D(03) dibaryon candidates, and provides the first quantitative phenomenological few-body calculation confronting the WASA@COSY 2.37 GeV resonance peak observed by Bashkanov et al. (2009). The model builds on the authors' previous pi NN three-body calculation of the D(12) dibaryon (Gal and Garcilazo, 2014) and contrasts with earlier quark-model calculations by Mulders et al. (1980, 1983), Oka and Yazaki (1980), Maltman (1985), and Goldman et al. (1989) that used constituent quarks to generate Delta-Delta interaction potentials, as well as earlier OBE calculations by Kamae and Fujita (1977) and Swart and Sebille (1983). The approach enables future inclusion of the Delta' decay width and extension to coupled-channel calculations between the Delta-Delta and pi-D(12) channels.
