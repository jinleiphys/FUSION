# Long-distant contribution and $χ_{c1}$ radiative decays to light vector meson

> The discrepancy between the PQCD calculation and the CLEO data for $χ_{c1}\to γV$ ($V=ρ^0,\,ω,\,ϕ$) stimulates our interest in exploring extra mechanism of $χ_{c1}$ decay. In this work, we apply an important non-perturbative QCD effect, i.e., hadronic loop mechanism, to study $χ_{c1}\to γV$ radiative decay. Our numerical result shows that the theoretical results including the hadronic loop contribution and the PQCD calculation of $χ_{c1}\to γV$ are consistent with the corresponding CLEO data of $χ_{c1}\to γV$. We expect further experimental measurement of $χ_{c1}\to γV$ at BES-III, which will be helpful to test the hadronic loop effect on $χ_{c1}$ decay.

## Key claim

The hadronic loop mechanism, a non-perturbative QCD effect, can resolve the order-of-magnitude discrepancy between the perturbative QCD (PQCD) calculation and the CLEO experimental data for the branching ratios of \(\chi_{c1}\to \gamma V\) (\(V=\rho^0,\,\omega,\,\phi\)), provided the cutoff parameter \(\alpha\) in the dipole form factor is chosen within a common range of \(2.18 < \alpha < 2.28\) for all three decay channels.

## Method

The authors computed the hadronic loop contribution to \(\chi_{c1}\to \gamma V\) by considering intermediate charmed meson loops (\(D\bar{D}^*+h.c.\) for \(\rho^0\) and \(\omega\), and \(D_s\bar{D}_s^*+h.c.\) for \(\phi\)). They used effective Lagrangians for the \(\chi_{c1} \mathcal{D}\mathcal{D}^{*}\), \(\mathcal{D}^{(*)}\mathcal{D}^{(*)}V\), and \(\mathcal{D}^{(*)}\mathcal{D}^{(*)}\gamma\) vertices, and introduced a dipole form factor \(\mathcal{F}(q^2)=(\Lambda^2-m^2)/(\Lambda^2-q^2)^2\) with \(\Lambda = m + \alpha \Lambda_{QCD}\) (\(\Lambda_{QCD}=220\) MeV) to regularize the loop integrals. The total decay amplitude was obtained by summing the hadronic loop amplitude with the existing PQCD result from Gao (2006).

## Key numbers

- The PQCD-only predictions from Gao (2006) were \(\mathcal{B}(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \rho^0)= 1.4 \times 10^{-5}\), \(\mathcal{B}(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \omega) =1.6 \times 10^{-6}\), and \(\mathcal{B}(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \phi) =3.6 \times 10^{-6}\).
- The CLEO experimental measurements (Bennett, 2008) were \(\mathcal{B}(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \rho^0) =243 \pm 19 \pm 22 \times 10^{-6}\), \(\mathcal{B}(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \omega) =85 \pm 15 \pm 12 \times 10^{-6}\), and \(\mathcal{B}(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \phi) =12.8 \pm 7.6 \pm 1.5 \times 10^{-6}\).
- The \(\alpha\) ranges where the combined (hadronic loop + PQCD) result overlaps the CLEO data are \(2.18 < \alpha < 2.35\) for \(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \rho^0\), \(2.06 < \alpha < 2.28\) for \(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \omega\), and \(1.16 < \alpha < 2.77\) for \(\chi_{c1} \to \gamma \phi\); a common range \(2.18 < \alpha < 2.28\) exists for all three channels.
- The coupling constants used include \(g_{\chi_{c1 DD^{*}}} = -21.44\) GeV, \(g_{\mathcal{D} \mathcal{D} \mathcal{V}} = 3.71\), and \(f_{\mathcal{D}^{*} \mathcal{DV}} = 2.31\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The radiative coupling constants were fixed as \(|g_{D^{*+} D^{+} \gamma}|=0.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(|g_{D^{*0}D^{0}\gamma}| = 2.0\) GeV\(^{-1}\), with signs taken as \(g_{D^{*+} D^{+} \gamma} = -0.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(g_{D^{*0}D^{0}\gamma} = 2.0\) GeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the hadronic loop mechanism, previously applied by Chen (2010) and Liu (2010) to explain the OZI-suppressed \(\chi_{c1}\to VV\) decays observed by BES-III, to the radiative decays \(\chi_{c1}\to \gamma V\). It addresses the large discrepancy between the PQCD predictions of Gao (2006) and the CLEO measurements of Bennett (2008). The success of the mechanism in both \(\chi_{c1}\to VV\) and \(\chi_{c1}\to \gamma V\) provides a test of the hadronic loop model and motivates further experimental studies of \(\chi_{cJ}\) decays at BES-III.
