# Disentangling baryon stopping and neutron skin effects in heavy-ion collisions

> We analyse the net baryon (B) and net electric charge (Q) stopping in heavy-ion collisions using the statistical model. Focusing first on isobar collisions $\rm{Ru}+\rm{Ru}$ and $\rm{Zr}+\rm{Zr}$ at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$~GeV, we show that the excess baryon-stopping parameter $γ_B \equiv (N_B/N_Q)\,(Z/A)$ can be quantitatively extracted from forthcoming RHIC measurements of charge- and baryon-stopping ratios. We then generalize the approach to other collision systems at RHIC and LHC energies and introduce an oxygen-baseline observable, $r^{OX}$, whose central-to-peripheral ratio exhibits strong and systematic sensitivity to the neutron-skin thickness of the target nucleus $X$.

# Disentangling baryon stopping and neutron skin effects in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The excess baryon-stopping parameter gamma_B can be quantitatively extracted from forthcoming RHIC measurements of charge- and baryon-stopping ratios, but only if nuclear-structure effects (particularly neutron-skin thickness) are disentangled from stopping dynamics using a statistical model framework that accounts for participant proton fraction and centrality dependence.

## Method
The authors used the Thermal-FIST hadron resonance gas model to compute midrapidity hadron yields at chemical freeze-out, imposing a charge-to-baryon ratio constraint that incorporates an excess baryon-stopping parameter gamma_B and the participant proton fraction from 3D Glauber model simulations. They calibrated gamma_B by comparing thermal-model predictions with hydrodynamic simulations from Pihan (2024), and generalized the isobar ratio observable r to arbitrary nuclear pairs, introducing an oxygen-baseline observable r^{OX} whose centrality dependence isolates neutron-skin effects.

## Key numbers
- Temperature fixed at T = 158 MeV for all systems studied
- Baryon chemical potential values: mu_B = 21.2 MeV for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV; mu_B = 0.90 MeV for Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV; mu_B = 0.84 MeV for O+O at 5.36 TeV
- Reference volume for 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC: dV/dy = 1000 fm^3
- Neutron-skin thickness values used: Delta_R_np = 0.05 fm for Ru, 0.18 fm for Zr, 0.20 fm for Au, 0.24 fm for U, 0.08 fm for Cu (estimated from linear fit), and two benchmarks for Pb: 0.17 fm (ab initio) and 0.28 fm (PREX-II)
- Maximum relative difference between proxy-based and direct conserved-charge reconstruction: 0.3% for net baryon number, 2.2% for net electric charge difference

## Context
This work extends the STAR Collaboration's (2024) proposed strategy for probing baryon-junction stopping mechanisms in isobar collisions by developing a framework that separates baryon-stopping physics from neutron-skin effects. It builds on hydrodynamic simulations by Pihan (2024) and uses the 3D Glauber model of Shen (2020) for nuclear geometry. The approach enables systematic studies of junction-induced baryon stopping at RHIC and LHC energies, and introduces oxygen as a baseline nucleus to constrain neutron-skin thickness from heavy-ion measurements, complementing existing constraints from PREX-II (2021) and ab initio calculations by Hu (2021).
