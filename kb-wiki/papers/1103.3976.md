# Eikonal Reaction Theory for Neutron-Removal Reaction

> We present an accurate method of treating the one-neutron removal reaction at intermediate incident energies induced by both nuclear and Coulomb interactions. In the method, the nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes are consistently treated by the method of continuum discretized coupled channels without making the adiabatic approximation to the Coulomb interaction, so that the removal cross section calculated never diverges. This method is applied to recently measured one-neutron removal cross section for $^{31}$Ne+$^{12}$C scattering at 230 MeV/nucleon and $^{31}$Ne+$^{208}$Pb scattering at 234 MeV/nucleon. The spectroscopic factor and the asymptotic normalization coefficient of the last neutron in $^{31}$Ne are evaluated.

## Key claim

The paper presents an accurate method (Eikonal Reaction Theory, ERT) for treating one-neutron removal reactions at intermediate incident energies that consistently handles both nuclear and Coulomb breakup without the adiabatic approximation for the Coulomb interaction, thereby avoiding the divergence that plagues the standard Glauber model when Coulomb is included. The method is applied to determine the spectroscopic factor and asymptotic normalization coefficient of the last neutron in \({}^{31}\)Ne, but the authors note that these quantities are sensitive to the poorly-known one-neutron separation energy \(B_n\).

## Method

The three-body (core+neutron+target) Schrödinger equation is solved using the eikonal approximation, but the S-matrix is separated into a neutron part (treated with the adiabatic approximation for the short-range nuclear interaction) and a core part (treated without the adiabatic approximation using eikonal continuum discretized coupled channels, eikonal-CDCC). The one-neutron removal cross section is then obtained as the sum of the elastic breakup (diffractive) cross section and the neutron-stripping cross section, calculated from the S-matrix elements of the full system and of the core-only subsystem.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{31}\)Ne+\({}^{12}\)C scattering at 230 MeV/nucleon with the \(1p3/2\) orbit: \(\sigma_{-n} = 114\) mb (theory) vs. 79 mb (experiment); spectroscopic factor \(\mathcal{S} = 0.693\).
* For \({}^{31}\)Ne+\({}^{208}\)Pb scattering at 234 MeV/nucleon with the \(1p3/2\) orbit: \(\sigma_{-n} = 1044\) mb (theory) vs. 712 mb (experiment); spectroscopic factor \(\mathcal{S} = 0.682\).
* For the \(0f7/2\) orbit: \(\mathcal{S} = 2.47\) for \({}^{12}\)C target and 5.65 for \({}^{208}\)Pb target.
* Asymptotic normalization coefficient for the \(1p3/2\) orbit at \(B_n = 0.33\) MeV: \(C_{\rm ANC} = 0.320 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.028\) fm\(^{-1/2}\) for \({}^{12}\)C and \(0.318 \pm 0.008 \pm 0.029\) fm\(^{-1/2}\) for \({}^{208}\)Pb.
* The error due to the adiabatic approximation for the neutron-nucleus potential is 0.2% for \(\sigma_{\rm R}\), 1.9% for \(\sigma_{\rm bu}\), 4.1% for \(\sigma_{\rm str}\), and 3.3% for \(\sigma_{-n}\) for \({}^{31}\)Ne(\(1p3/2\))+\({}^{208}\)Pb.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model and continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) by removing the adiabatic approximation for the Coulomb interaction, which previously caused divergences in the removal cross section. The method is compared to the Coulomb-corrected eikonal model (CCE) of Ibrahim and Capel (2008) and to earlier Glauber model calculations for \({}^{31}\)Ne by the same group (2010). The results enable more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients for halo nuclei, particularly for heavier systems such as \({}^{31}\)Ne, and suggest that the last neutron in \({}^{31}\)Ne predominantly occupies the \(1p3/2\) orbit, implying deformation.
