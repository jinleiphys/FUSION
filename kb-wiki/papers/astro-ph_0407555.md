# Prospects for obtaining an r-process from Gamma Ray Burst Disk Winds

> We discuss the possibility that r-process nucleosynthesis may occur in the winds from gamma ray burst accretion disks. This can happen if the temperature of the disk is sufficiently high that electron antineutrinos are trapped as well as neutrinos. This implies accretion disks with greater than a solar mass per second accretion rate, although lower accretion rates with higher black hole spin parameters may provide viable environments as well. Additionally, the outflow from the disk must either have relatively low entropy, e.g. around s = 10, or the initial acceleration of the wind must be slow enough that it is neutrino and antineutrino capture as opposed to electron and positron capture that sets the electron fraction.

# Prospects for obtaining an r-process from Gamma Ray Burst Disk Winds

## Key claim

R-process nucleosynthesis may occur in winds from gamma ray burst accretion disks, but only if the disk temperature is high enough that both electron antineutrinos and neutrinos are trapped, which requires accretion rates greater than about one solar mass per second, or alternatively lower accretion rates with higher black hole spin parameters. Additionally, the outflow must have either relatively low entropy (s ~ 10) or slow enough initial acceleration that neutrino and antineutrino capture, rather than electron and positron capture, sets the electron fraction.

## Method

The authors used accretion disk models from DiMatteo, Perna and Narayan (2002) to calculate neutrino and antineutrino trapping surfaces and spectra for disks with different accretion rates and black hole spin parameters. They parameterized the wind outflow velocity with a power-law function controlled by a parameter beta, and calculated the electron fraction evolution under the influence of neutrino and antineutrino capture reactions, electron and positron capture, and electron degeneracy. Nucleosynthesis yields were computed for selected outflow trajectories that produced low electron fractions.

## Key numbers

- Accretion rate threshold for antineutrino trapping: greater than 1 solar mass per second (M_sun/s)
- High accretion rate model studied: M_dot = 10 M_sun/s, black hole spin parameter a=0
- Moderate accretion rate model studied: M_dot = 1 M_sun/s, a=0
- Low accretion rate model referenced: M_dot = 0.1 M_sun/s, a=0.95 (from Popham, Woosley, Fryer 1999)
- Electron neutrino temperature at neutrino surface: T_MeV ~ 2.5 to 4.5
- Electron antineutrino temperature at antineutrino surface: T_MeV ~ 3.6 to 5.1
- Outflow velocity used in nucleosynthesis calculations: v_infinity = 3 x 10^4 km/s
- Low entropy outflow that produced r-process: s=10, beta=0.8
- High entropy outflow with slow acceleration that produced r-process: s=40, beta=2.5
- Electron fraction in low accretion rate model with slow outflow: driven up to Y_e ~ 0.6
- Electron fraction in moderate accretion rate model with slow outflow: very proton rich, producing primarily nickel and proton-rich nuclei such as Cu-58, Zn-59, Fe-50, Fe-52

## Context

This work extends the search for r-process sites beyond the traditional candidates of neutrino-driven winds from Type II supernovae (Meyer 1992, Woosley 1994) and neutron star merger ejecta (Meyer 1989, Freiburghaus 1999, Rosswog 1999). The supernova neutrino-driven wind was found to be a near miss for producing r-process elements (Hoffman 1997, Fuller and Meyer 1995), and self-consistent neutrino treatment showed electron neutrino capture drove the electron fraction too high (McLaughlin, Fuller, and Meyer 1998). Neutron star mergers face difficulties reconciling with metal-poor star observations (Argast 2004). This work enables future studies of nucleosynthesis in gamma ray burst accretion disk winds, with the key parameters being accretion rate and black hole spin, and suggests that both low-entropy outflows and slow-acceleration high-entropy outflows from high accretion rate disks can produce r-process elements.
