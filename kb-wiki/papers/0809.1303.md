# Current conservation, screening and the magnetic moment of the $Δ$ resonance: 2. Formulation with quark degrees of freedom 3. Magnetic moment of the $Δ^o$ and $Δ^-$ resonances

> Our previous paper \cite{MFNEW} is generalized within the field theoretical formulation with the quark degrees of freedom \cite{HW,H,N,Z}, where pions and nucleons are treated as the bound systems of quarks. It is shown that relations generated by current conservation for the on shell $πN$ bremsstrahlung amplitude with composite nucleons and pions have the same form as in the usual quantum field theory \cite{IZ,BD} without quark degrees of freedom \cite{MFNEW}. Consequently, the model independent relations for the magnetic dipole moments of the $Δ^+$ and $Δ^{++}$ resonances in \cite{MFNEW} remain be the same in the quantum field theory with the quark degrees of freedom. These relations are extended for the magnetic dipole moments of the $Δ^o$ and $Δ^-$ resonances which are determined via the anomalous magnetic moment of the neutron $μ_n$ as $μ_{Δ^o}={{M_Δ}\over {m_p}} μ_n$ and $μ_{Δ^{-}}={3\over 2}μ_{Δ^o}$.

## Key claim

The model-independent relations for the magnetic dipole moments of the Δ⁺ and Δ⁺⁺ resonances derived in the authors' previous paper remain valid when the formalism is extended to include quark degrees of freedom, and these relations are extended to the Δ⁰ and Δ⁻ resonances using the anomalous magnetic moment of the neutron.

## Method

The authors generalize their previous field-theoretical treatment of πN bremsstrahlung to a formulation where pions and nucleons are treated as bound states of quarks, using the Huang-Weldon approach and the Haag-Nishijima-Zimmermann formalism. They construct creation and annihilation operators for composite hadrons from quark field operators, derive the Ward-Takahashi identity for the on-shell πN bremsstrahlung amplitude with composite particles, and show that current conservation yields the same form of relations as in the theory without quark degrees of freedom. They then extract the Δ-pole contributions and equate the Δ-γΔ vertex functions from the external and internal radiation amplitudes to obtain relations between the Δ magnetic moments and the nucleon anomalous magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- μ_Δ⁰ = (M_Δ / m_p) μ_n, where μ_n is the anomalous magnetic moment of the neutron (−1.91 μ_B in units of the nuclear magneton e/2m_p)
- μ_Δ⁻ = (3/2) μ_Δ⁰
- μ_p = 2.79 μ_B for the proton anomalous magnetic moment
- μ_n = −1.91 μ_B for the neutron anomalous magnetic moment

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (Machavariani and Faessler, 2007) which derived model-independent relations for the magnetic moments of Δ⁺ and Δ⁺⁺ from current conservation in πN bremsstrahlung. The present paper generalizes those results to include quark degrees of freedom following the composite particle formalism of Huang and Weldon (1975) and the Haag-Nishijima-Zimmermann approach, and extends the relations to the Δ⁰ and Δ⁻ resonances. The results enable model-independent predictions for the magnetic moments of all four Δ charge states that can be compared with experimental determinations and lattice QCD calculations.
