# SAID Results for GDH-Related Sum Rules

> Model-dependence of the single-pion contribution to the GDH, Baldin, and forward spin-polarizability sum rules is explored, using the most recent SAID multipole analysis. Results from SAID and MAID are compared.

## Key claim

The single-pion contribution to the GDH, Baldin, and forward spin-polarizability sum rules is model-dependent, with differences between the SAID and MAID multipole analyses appearing mainly above 450 MeV, though the total integrals are more consistent than their individual pieces due to cancellations.

## Method

The authors used the most recent SAID multipole analysis (SM02) of single-pion photoproduction data to calculate the GDH, Baldin, and forward spin-polarizability integrals from threshold to 2 GeV. Results were compared with the MAID2000 analysis, and the integrals were evaluated separately for the threshold region (to 200 MeV) and the full energy range to identify where differences are most pronounced.

## Key numbers

- Proton GDH integral (SAID/MAID): -187/-168 microbarns (threshold to 2000 MeV); 28/31 microbarns (threshold to 200 MeV)
- Neutron GDH integral (SAID/MAID): -137/-120 microbarns (threshold to 2000 MeV); 41/46 microbarns (threshold to 200 MeV)
- Proton Baldin integral (SAID/MAID): 11.5/11.6 x 10^-4 fm^3 (threshold to 2000 MeV); 1.3/1.3 x 10^-4 fm^3 (threshold to 200 MeV)
- Neutron Baldin integral (SAID/MAID): 12.9/13.4 x 10^-4 fm^3 (threshold to 2000 MeV); 1.8/1.9 x 10^-4 fm^3 (threshold to 200 MeV)
- Proton forward spin polarizability gamma_0 (SAID/MAID): -0.85/-0.68 x 10^-4 fm^4 (threshold to 2000 MeV); 0.94/0.98 x 10^-4 fm^4 (threshold to 200 MeV)
- Neutron forward spin polarizability gamma_0 (SAID/MAID): -0.08/0.14 x 10^-4 fm^4 (threshold to 2000 MeV); 1.35/1.49 x 10^-4 fm^4 (threshold to 200 MeV)
- For pi^0 p channel: GDH -142/-150 microbarns, Baldin 4.7/4.7 x 10^-4 fm^3, gamma_0 -1.40/-1.47 x 10^-4 fm^4 (threshold to 2000 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous sum-rule calculations using the older SAID fit SM95 and the SX99 parametrization, showing that both changes in phenomenology and an improved database have modified the integrals. The comparison with MAID2000 (developed by the Mainz group) reveals that the single-pion contribution is negligible above 2 GeV for both the GDH and forward spin-polarizability integrals. The results enable future work to identify discrepancies in photoproduction data and to constrain models of nucleon structure through sum-rule consistency checks.
