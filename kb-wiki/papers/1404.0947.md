# Theoretical study of a $d^{*}$ resonance in $^{3}G_{3}$ partial wave of nucleon-nucleon scattering

> Inspired by the recent results of the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration, in which they found a resonance pole in the coupled $^{3}D_{3}$ - $^{3}G_{3}$ partial waves as expected from the $d^{*}$ resonance hypothesis, we calculated the resonance structure in the coupled $^{3}D_{3}$ - $^{3}G_{3}$ partial wave phase shifts of nucleon-nucleon scattering in the framework of two constituent quark models: the quark delocalization color screening model and the chiral quark model. Our results show that there is a resonance $^{7}S_{3}^{ΔΔ}$ in the coupled $^{3}D_{3}^{NN}$ and $^{3}G_{3}^{NN}$ partial waves in both of these two models, which is in accordance with the expectation from the $d^{*}$ resonance structure. The resonance shape in the $^{3}D_{3}^{NN}$ partial wave is remarkable, whereas in the $^{3}G_{3}^{NN}$ phase shifts there is a small rise around the resonance energy. This result is in agreement with the recent experimental observations of WASA-at-COSY Collaboration.

# Theoretical Study of a $d^{*}$ Resonance in $^{3}G_{3}$ Partial Wave of Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

## Key claim
The authors claim that a resonance in the coupled $^{3}D_{3}^{NN}$ and $^{3}G_{3}^{NN}$ partial waves of nucleon-nucleon scattering arises from coupling to a $^{7}S_{3}^{\Delta\Delta}$ state, consistent with the $d^{*}$ resonance hypothesis. However, the resonance structure is only "remarkable" in the $^{3}D_{3}^{NN}$ partial wave, while the $^{3}G_{3}^{NN}$ phase shifts show only a "small bump" around the resonance energy, and the theoretical partial-wave amplitudes for $^{3}G_{3}^{NN}$ differ qualitatively from experimental measurements.

## Method
The authors performed resonating group method (RGM) calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts in the coupled $^{3}D_{3}$ - $^{3}G_{3}$ partial waves, using two constituent quark models: the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) and the chiral quark model (ChQM). They included the $^{7}S_{3}^{\Delta\Delta}$ channel as a resonance state and computed phase shifts for single-channel, two-channel, and three-channel coupling scenarios, comparing results to the SP07 partial-wave solution and WASA-at-COSY data.

## Key numbers
- Resonance mass in QDCSM: $\sim 2360$ MeV, which is lower than the experimental value of $\sim 2380$ MeV from WASA-at-COSY.
- Resonance mass in ChQM: $\sim 2410$ MeV, which is higher than the experimental value by $\sim 30$–$50$ MeV.
- The $^{3}D_{3}^{NN}$ phase shifts rise through $\pi/2$ at a resonance mass $\sim 2360$ MeV in QDCSM when the $^{7}S_{3}^{\Delta\Delta}$ channel is coupled.
- The $^{3}D_{3}^{NN}$ and $^{3}G_{3}^{NN}$ phase shifts from both models fit the SP07 data well for center-of-mass energies $E_{CM} < 400$ MeV in single-channel calculations.
- The $\sigma$ meson mass used in ChQM is 675 MeV; the color screening constant $\mu$ in QDCSM is 0.45 fm$^{-2}$.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies by the same group (Ping et al., 2013; Huang et al., 2013) that calculated the $d^{*}$ resonance only in the $^{3}D_{3}$ partial wave, by now including the coupled $^{3}D_{3}$ - $^{3}G_{3}$ channels as suggested by Pricking et al. (2013) and as observed by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (2014). The results support the $d^{*}$ resonance hypothesis originally proposed by Dyson and Xuong (1964) and later predicted by Goldman et al. in quark models. The study enables future work to refine the description of the $^{3}G_{3}^{NN}$ partial-wave amplitudes, where the theoretical results currently disagree with experimental data, and to explore hidden-color channel coupling effects that may lower the ChQM resonance mass by 10–20 MeV.
