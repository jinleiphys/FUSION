# Chiral extrapolations for nucleon magnetic moments

> Lattice QCD simulations have made significant progress in the calculation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the chiral regime in recent years. With simulation results achieving pion masses of order ~180 MeV, there is an apparent challenge as to how the physical regime is approached. By using contemporary methods in chiral effective field theory, both the quark-mass and finite-volume dependence of the isovector nucleon magnetic moment are carefully examined. The extrapolation to the physical point yields a result that is compatible with experiment, albeit with a combined statistical and systematic uncertainty of 10%. The extrapolation shows a strong finite-volume dependence; lattice sizes of L > 5 fm must be used to simulate results within 2% of the infinite-volume result for the magnetic moment at the physical pion mass.

## Key claim

The chiral extrapolation of the isovector nucleon magnetic moment to the physical point using finite-range regularized chiral effective field theory yields a result compatible with experiment, but with a combined statistical and systematic uncertainty of ±10%, and the extrapolation shows a strong finite-volume dependence requiring lattice sizes larger than 5 fm to achieve results within 2% of the infinite-volume value.

## Method

The authors applied finite-range regularized (FRR) chiral effective field theory using a dipole regulator to analyze nine lattice QCD results from the QCDSF Collaboration for the isovector nucleon magnetic moment, with pion masses down to approximately 180 MeV and lattice sizes from 1.7 fm to 2.9 fm satisfying L > 1.5 fm and mπL > 3. They performed a renormalization flow analysis of the leading-order low-energy coefficient c0 as a function of the regularization scale Λ to identify the optimal regularization scale, then used this optimal scale to perform chiral extrapolations including finite-volume corrections evaluated at a fixed scale Λ' = 2.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The optimal regularization scale for a dipole regulator is Λ^(scale) = 0.82^(+0.14)_(-0.13) GeV, obtained from a χ² analysis of the renormalization flow curves.
- Lattice sizes of L > 5 fm must be used to simulate results within 2% of the infinite-volume result for the magnetic moment at the physical pion mass.
- The extrapolation to the physical point yields a result compatible with experiment with a combined statistical and systematic uncertainty of ±10%.
- The smallest total error bar occurs when all nine lattice points are included, corresponding to a maximum pion-mass value of mπ,max² = 0.81 GeV².
- The lattice results analyzed have pion masses as low as approximately 180 MeV, with lattice sizes varying from 1.7 fm to 2.9 fm.

## Context

This work extends the FRR chiral extrapolation methodology previously applied to the nucleon mass by Hall, Leinweber, and Young (2010) to the isovector nucleon magnetic moment, using lattice QCD results from the QCDSF Collaboration (Collins et al., 2011) that probe into the chiral regime. The analysis addresses the persistent difficulty in achieving consistent quantitative description of the pion-mass dependence of magnetic moments between the physical point and lightest simulation results, which had been noted across multiple simulations including those by Gockeler et al. (2003) and Boinepalli et al. (2006). The results enable more reliable extraction of nucleon electromagnetic properties from lattice QCD simulations and provide guidance on required lattice volumes for future calculations.
