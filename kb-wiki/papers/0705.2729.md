# Tunneling of a composite particle: Effects of intrinsic structure

> We consider simple models of tunneling of an object with intrinsic degrees of freedom. This important problem was not extensively studied until now, in spite of numerous applications in various areas of physics and astrophysics. We show possibilities of enhancement for the probability of tunneling due to the presence of intrinsic degrees of freedom split by weak external fields or by polarizability of the slow composite object.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the tunneling probability of a composite particle can be exponentially enhanced by its intrinsic degrees of freedom, but only under specific conditions such as a sufficiently large splitting between internal states (e.g., \(2a\Delta \gg 1\)) or when the composite particle is loosely bound and its internal wave function can adjust adiabatically as it approaches the barrier.

## Method
The authors solve one-dimensional Schrödinger equations for a composite particle with two intrinsic states, using a rectangular barrier model. They treat the intrinsic degrees of freedom via a spinor wave function (spin-1/2 analogy) with a magnetic field splitting inside the barrier, and also develop an adiabatic approach for a two-body composite (e.g., deuteron) where only one constituent feels the barrier, solving the coupled center-of-mass and relative motion equations numerically.

## Key numbers
- For an electron tunneling through a rectangular barrier of width \(a = 10\) nm and height \(U_0 - E = 1\) eV, the condition for enhancement requires \(|h|/(U_0 - E) > 1/25\), i.e., \(|h| > 0.04\) eV, where \(h\) is the splitting field strength.
- In the deuteron model, the intrinsic potential \(V(r)\) has depth \(V_0 = 11.4\) MeV and width \(r_0 = 2\) fm, reproducing the deuteron binding energy \(E_0 = -2.225\) MeV.
- The barrier parameters for the deuteron model are \(U_0 = 5.5\) MeV and \(a = 2\) fm; at \(R = 0\) the effective potential height is \(\tilde{U}(0) \approx 2.21\) MeV, with binding energy \(\epsilon(0) = 0.01\) MeV.
- The transmission probability for the composite deuteron is considerably enhanced compared to the simple tunneling formula \(T_0 = \frac{16k^2\kappa_0^2}{(\kappa_0^2 + k^2)^2} e^{-2\kappa_0 a}\) (see Fig. 3).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of composite particle tunneling by Saito (1994), Sato (2002), and Goodvin (2005), and addresses the lack of detailed theoretical treatment noted in those papers. It also builds on the polarizability effect pointed out by FZ05 (2005) and the virtual radiation processes discussed by BPZ99 (1999) and FZ99 (1999). The results enable more realistic modeling of subbarrier nuclear fusion reactions, especially for loosely bound systems like the deuteron, and have implications for astrophysical reaction rates and condensed matter transport phenomena.
