# The Hadron-Quark Crossover in Neutron Star within Gaussian Process Regression Method

> The equations of state of the neutron star at the hadron-quark crossover region are interpolated with the Gaussian process regression (GPR) method, which can reduce the randomness of present interpolation schemes. The relativistic mean-field (RMF) model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model are employed to describe the hadronic phase and quark phase, respectively. In the RMF model, the coupling term between $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons is considered to control the density-dependent behaviors of symmetry energy, i.e. the slope of symmetry energy, $L$. Furthermore, the vector interaction between quarks is included in the NJL model to obtain the additional repulsive contributions. Their coupling strengths and the crossover windows are discussed in the present framework under the constraints on the neutron star from gravitational wave detections, massive neutron star measurements, mass-radius simultaneous observation of NICER collaboration, and the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb from PREX-II. It is found that the slope of symmetry energy, $L$ should be around $50-90$ MeV and the crossover window is $(0.3,~0.6)~\rm fm^{-3}$ with these observables. Furthermore, the uncertainties of neutron star masses and radii in the hadron-quark crossover regions are also predicted by the GPR method.

## Key claim

The Gaussian process regression (GPR) method can reduce the randomness of present interpolation schemes for the hadron-quark crossover equation of state in neutron stars, and under constraints from gravitational wave detections, massive neutron star measurements, NICER observations, and the PREX-II neutron skin thickness, the slope of symmetry energy \(L\) should be around \(70\sim 90\) MeV and the crossover window is \((0.3,~0.6)~\rm fm^{-3}\).

## Method

The hadronic phase was described using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the IUFSU parameter set, including a coupling term between \(\omega\) and \(\rho\) mesons to control the density-dependent behavior of symmetry energy. The quark phase was described using a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector repulsion. The equations of state in the hadron-quark crossover region were interpolated using the Gaussian process regression (GPR) method with a squared exponential covariance kernel, treating the pressure as a function of baryon density and using the hadronic and quark phase results as training data to predict the pressure in the crossover density window.

## Key numbers

- The slope of symmetry energy \(L\) is constrained to around \(70-90\) MeV under the combined astrophysical and nuclear observables.
- The crossover window for the hadron-quark transition is \((0.3,~0.6)~\rm fm^{-3}\).
- The IUFSU model yields a saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.155~\rm fm^{-3}\), binding energy per nucleon \(E_0 = -16.397\) MeV, incompressibility \(K = 230.749\) MeV, symmetry energy \(E_{\rm sym} = 31.336\) MeV, and slope \(L = 47.165\) MeV at saturation.
- For the IUFSU parameter sets with different \(L\) values, the maximum masses of neutron stars are all less than \(2 M_\odot\); radii corresponding to the maximum mass range from \(11.24\) km to \(11.71\) km, and radii of \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron stars range from \(12.74\) km to \(13.54\) km.
- The neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}{\rm Pb}\) from PREX-II is \(R_{\rm skin}^{208}=0.283\pm0.071\) fm (Adhikari 2021), and the symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to \(E_{\rm sym}=38.1\pm4.7\) MeV (Reed 2021).

## Context

This work extends previous interpolation approaches for the hadron-quark phase transition, such as the hyperbolic function interpolation by Masuda et al. (2013) and the polynomial interpolation by Kojo et al. (2015), by applying Gaussian process regression to reduce the randomness and quantify uncertainties in the interpolated equation of state. The results are constrained by the latest multi-messenger observations including GW170817, NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the PREX-II neutron skin thickness measurement. This framework enables more reliable predictions of neutron star properties in the crossover region and can be used to further constrain the density dependence of symmetry energy and the nature of dense matter.
