# The quark-meson coupling model for Lambda, Sigma and Xi hypernuclei

> The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which has been successfully used to describe the properties of both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei, is applied to a systematic study of $Λ, Σ$ and $Ξ$ hypernuclei. Assumptions made in the present study are, (i) the (self-consistent) exchanged scalar, and vector, mesons couple only to the u and d quarks, and (ii) an SU(6) valence quark model for the bound nucleons and hyperon. The model automatically leads to a very weak spin-orbit interaction for the $Λ$ in a hypernucleus. Effects of the Pauli blocking at the quark level, particularly in the open, coupled, $ΣN - ΛN$ channel (strong conversion), is also taken into account in a phenomenological way.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which assumes that scalar and vector mesons couple only to u and d quarks and uses an SU(6) valence quark model for bound nucleons and hyperons, automatically produces a very weak spin-orbit interaction for the Λ in a hypernucleus without requiring large SU(3) breaking or additional tensor couplings, though the authors note that Pauli blocking effects at the quark level in the coupled ΣN-ΛN channel must be included phenomenologically for realistic results.

## Method

The authors applied the QMC model, previously used for infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei, to systematically study Λ, Σ, and Ξ hypernuclei by deriving mean-field equations of motion using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, where quasi-particles are three-quark clusters. They solved self-consistent Dirac equations for nucleons and hyperons coupled to scalar (σ) and vector (ω, ρ) meson fields, with the hyperon effective masses calculated from the MIT bag model including the scalar field dependence. The spin-orbit potentials for each hyperon were derived from the quark structure, including Thomas precession and anomalous contributions from finite-size effects, and were evaluated for the 1p3/2 hyperon configuration in 41YCa and 209YPb hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- Bag parameters: nucleon bag radius RN = 0.8 fm in free space, bag pressure B = (170 MeV)4, current quark masses mu = md = 5 MeV and ms = 250 MeV
- Free-space hyperon masses fitted: Λ = 1115.7 MeV, Σ = 1193.1 MeV, Ξ = 1318.1 MeV, with corresponding bag radii RΛ = 0.806 fm, RΣ = 0.827 fm, RΞ = 0.820 fm
- Slope parameters for effective coupling: aN = 8.8 × 10-4 MeV-1, aΛ = 9.3 × 10-4 MeV-1, aΣ = 9.5 × 10-4 MeV-1, aΞ = 9.4 × 10-4 MeV-1
- Scaling relation for effective mass variation: δMΛ*/δMN* ≈ δMΣ*/δMN* ≈ 2/3, δMΞ*/δMN* ≈ 1/3
- Normal nuclear matter density ρ0 ≈ 0.15 fm-3, with the parametrization for C(σ) working well up to about three times normal nuclear density (ρB ≈ 3ρ0)

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, originally suggested by Guichon and previously applied to finite nuclei by the same authors (Tsushima, Saito, Haidenbauer, Thomas, 1997), to hypernuclei with strangeness S = -1 and S = -2. The model provides a quark-level explanation for the weak spin-orbit interaction in Λ hypernuclei, which was first phenomenologically suggested by Bouyssy and Hüfner and later explained by Brockman and Weise in a relativistic Hartree model requiring strong SU(3) breaking, while Pirner offered an explanation in terms of quark and gluon dynamics. The results enable systematic predictions for Σ and Ξ hypernuclei, where experiments at CERN, Brookhaven, and KEK have confirmed Σ hypernuclei existence and revealed narrower-than-expected widths due to ΣN-ΛN conversion, and where renewed interest in Ξ hypernuclei connects to the H dibaryon suggested by Jaffe based on the MIT bag model.
