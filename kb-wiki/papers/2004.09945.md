# Nucleons pair shell model in M-scheme

> The nucleon pair shell model (NPSM) is casted into the so-called M-scheme for the cases with isospin symmetry and without isospin symmetry. The odd system and even system are treated on the same foot. The uncoupled commutators for nucleon-pairs, which are suitable for M-scheme, are given. Explicit formula of matrix elements in M-scheme for overlap, one-body operators, two-body operators are obtained. It is found that the $cpu$ time used in calculating the matrix elements in M-scheme is much shorter than that in the J-scheme of NPSM.

## Key claim

The nucleon pair shell model (NPSM) is reformulated in the M-scheme, which avoids the need to calculate 9j symbols and coefficients of fractional parentage, and the authors claim that the CPU time used in calculating the matrix elements in M-scheme is much shorter than that in the J-scheme of NPSM, though the paper does not provide a quantitative speedup factor.

## Method

The authors derived uncoupled commutators for nucleon-pair creation and annihilation operators, one-body operators, and two-body operators, suitable for an M-scheme basis where states are labeled by the total angular momentum projection M rather than the total angular momentum J. They then used these commutators to obtain explicit recursive formulas for the overlap matrix elements and the matrix elements of the pairing interaction, one-body operators, and multipole-multipole interactions, treating both even and odd systems on the same footing. The formalism is presented for cases both without and with isospin symmetry, and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in an orthonormal basis obtained by orthogonalizing the non-orthonormal M-scheme basis.

## Key numbers

* The shell model space for medium and heavy nuclei is about $10^{14} - 10^{18}$, which is too large for direct diagonalization (from the introduction).
* The maximum number of nucleon-pairs that the NPSM in J-scheme can handle is 5 for identical nucleon systems (from the introduction).
* The overlap for a one-pair state in M-scheme is given by $\langle r_1 \mu_1 | s_1 \nu_1 \rangle = 2\delta_{r_1,s_1} \delta_{\mu_1,\nu_1} \sum_{ab} y(abr_1)y(abs_1)$.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon pair shell model (NPSM), originally formulated in the J-scheme by Chen (1993) and Zhao (2000), into the M-scheme, which is the basis used by most large-scale shell model codes (Whitehead, 1977). The M-scheme avoids the recoupling of angular momenta required in the J-scheme, which is too time-consuming for systems with more than 5 nucleon pairs. This reformulation enables the NPSM to handle larger model spaces and more nucleon pairs, potentially allowing the study of medium and heavy nuclei that are inaccessible to the J-scheme NPSM.
