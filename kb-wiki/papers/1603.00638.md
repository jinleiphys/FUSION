# Investigating the alpha-clustering on the surface of $^{120}$Sn via ($p$,$pα$) reaction and the validity of the factorization approximation

> The $^{120}$Sn($p$,$pα$)$^{116}$Cd reaction at 392 MeV is investigated with the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework. We show that this reaction is very peripheral mainly because of the strong absorption of $α$ by the reaction residue $^{116}$Cd, and the $α$-clustering on the nuclear surface can be probed clearly. We investigate also the validity of the so-called factorization approximation that has frequently been used so far. It is shown that the kinematics of $α$ in the nuclear interior region is significantly affected by the distortion of $^{116}$Cd, but it has no effect on the reaction observables because of the strong absorption in that region.

## Key claim
The authors show that the factorization approximation, which replaces local momenta with asymptotic momenta in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA), is valid for calculating the triple differential cross section of the $^{120}$Sn($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{116}$Cd reaction at 392 MeV, even though the approximation fails for the alpha particle's distorted wave in the nuclear interior, because strong absorption of the alpha particle by the residual nucleus $^{116}$Cd suppresses contributions from that region.

## Method
The authors performed DWIA calculations for the $^{120}$Sn($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{116}$Cd reaction at 392 MeV, using a local semi-classical approximation (LSCA) to treat the distorted waves of the outgoing alpha particle. They compared the full DWIA result with one using the factorization approximation, which replaces the local momentum of the alpha particle with its asymptotic momentum. The alpha particle bound state was described by a 4S orbit in a Woods-Saxon potential with a separation energy of 4.81 MeV, and the alpha spectroscopic factor was taken as 0.022. The $p$-$\alpha$ differential cross section was calculated with a microscopic single folding model, and the alpha-nucleus distorting potential was obtained from a double folding model with no free parameters.

## Key numbers
- Incident proton energy: 392 MeV.
- Alpha separation energy of $^{120}$Sn: 4.81 MeV.
- Alpha spectroscopic factor $S_\alpha$ for $^{120}$Sn: 0.022.
- The calculated TDX at $p_{\rm R}=0$ using the factorization approximation differs from the LSCA result by only 6% at most.
- A 10% change in the Woods-Saxon radius parameter $r_0$ (from 1.27 fm to 1.40 fm or 1.14 fm) changes the magnitude of the TDX by about a factor of three.
- The reaction probes the alpha-cluster wave function in the radial region of 6-9 fm on the nuclear surface.

## Context
This work extends the study of alpha-clustering from light nuclei to heavy nuclei, specifically $^{120}$Sn, following theoretical predictions by Typel (2014) of alpha-clustering on the surface of Sn isotopes. The authors use the ($p$,$p\alpha$) knockout reaction as an alternative to alpha transfer reactions like ($^6$Li,$d$), which were applied to $^{20}$Ne by Fukui (2016). The study validates the factorization approximation used in prior knockout reaction analyses by Samanta (1986) and others, showing it is reliable for this heavy system due to strong absorption. The results enable future experimental probes of alpha-clustering on heavy nuclear surfaces using ($p$,$p\alpha$) reactions.
