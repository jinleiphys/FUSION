# Relativistic and Polarization Phenomena in NN -> Dπprocesses

> A detailed analysis of the processes of the type NN -> Dπis presented taking into account the exchange graphs of a nucleon and a pion. A large sensitivity of polarization observables to the off-mass shell effects of nucleons inside the deuteron is shown. The influence of the inclusion of the P-wave in the Deuteron Wave Function (DWF) is studied. We have analysed the sensitivity of all the observables to the form of πNN - current taking into account the off-shellnis of the nucleon and the choice of the DWF relativistic form. One can see the very large sensitivity of all the observables, especialy of the polarization characteristics to the choice of the DWF form. The inclusion of the P-wave contribution to the DWF within framework of Gross's approach results in a better description of the experimental data on the differential cross section and the polarization observables.

## Key claim

The paper shows that polarization observables in \(NN\to d\pi\) processes are highly sensitive to the off-mass-shell effects of nucleons inside the deuteron and to the inclusion of \(P\)-wave components in the deuteron wave function (DWF), with some polarization characteristics able to change sign when these effects are included. The authors qualify that the one-nucleon exchange and pion rescattering graphs alone are insufficient to describe all data without also accounting for the off-shell structure of the \(\pi NN\) vertex and the relativistic form of the DWF.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant relativistic amplitude for \(NN\to d\pi\) using six invariant amplitudes, then derive helicity amplitudes and partial-wave expansions. They compute the contributions from the one-nucleon exchange (pole) graph and the pion rescattering (triangle) graph, using a pseudoscalar/pseudovector mixing parameter \(\lambda\) for the \(\pi NN\) vertex and two forms of the deuteron wave function: Locher's nonrelativistic form and Gross's relativistic form which includes \(P\)-wave (small) components. The observables (differential cross section, asymmetries \(A_{y0}\), \(A_{xx}\), \(A_{yy}\), \(A_{zz}\), and deuteron tensor polarizations \(iT_{11}\), \(T_{20}\), \(T_{21}\), \(T_{22}\)) are calculated numerically for the reaction \(pp\to d\pi^+\) at a proton kinetic energy of 578 MeV and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Proton kinetic energy \(T_p = 578\) MeV (corresponding to pion kinetic energy \(T_\pi = 147\) MeV) is the energy at which calculations are performed.
- The cut-off parameter for the pion formfactor is \(\Lambda\) (value not specified in text) and the mixing parameter \(\lambda\) is varied between 0.0 and 1.0; best description of the differential cross section is found for \(\lambda = 0.6\)–\(0.8\).
- The total probability of small components of the DWF (from Gross's approach) increases monotonically from approximately \(0.03\%\) for \(\lambda = 0\) to approximately \(1.5\%\) for \(\lambda = 1\).
- The square of the virtual pion four-momentum in the triangle graph lies in the range \(0 > q^2 > -0.3\) GeV\(^2\); the off-shell nucleon momentum squared is in the range \(0.83 > k^2 > 0.63\) GeV\(^2\).
- The maximum loop momentum for the triangle integral at \(T_p = 0.578\) GeV is \(\eta_m \approx 0.366\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic studies of \(NN\to d\pi\) (e.g., Watson and Rosen, Mandel, Niskanen, Green, Koltun and Rinat, Blankleider) and relativistic approaches by Yao, Schiff, Barry, and Locher (Locher 1981, 1983, 1984) by systematically including off-mass-shell effects and \(P\)-wave components in the deuteron wave function using Gross's relativistic formalism (Buck and Gross 1979, Gross 1982). The authors show that Locher's DWF form fails to reproduce the differential cross section and gives the wrong sign for the vector polarization \(iT_{11}\), whereas Gross's DWF with \(\lambda = 0.6\)–\(0.8\) yields a better description of both cross section and polarization data. The work enables further tests of the off-shell \(\pi NN\) vertex and deuteron structure at short distances, and the authors note that future studies should include initial \(NN\) and final \(d\pi\) state interactions.
