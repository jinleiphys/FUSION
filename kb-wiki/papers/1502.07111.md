# Calculating broad neutron resonances in a cut-off Woods-Saxon potential

> In a cut-off Woods-Saxon (CWS) potential with realistic depth $S$-matrix poles being far from the imaginary wave number axis form a sequence where the distances of the consecutive resonances are inversely proportional with the cut-off radius value, which is an unphysical parameter. Other poles lying closer to the imaginary wave number axis might have trajectories with irregular shapes as the depth of the potential increases. Poles being close repel each other, and their repulsion is responsible for the changes of the directions of the corresponding trajectories. The repulsion might cause that certain resonances become antibound and later resonances again when they collide on the imaginary axis. The interaction is extremely sensitive to the cut-off radius value, which is an apparent handicap of the CWS potential.

# Calculating broad neutron resonances in a cut-off Woods-Saxon potential

## Key claim

In a cut-off Woods-Saxon (CWS) potential, the positions of broad neutron resonances and their trajectories depend on the unphysical cut-off radius parameter, and the repulsive interaction between nearby poles, which can cause resonances to become antibound and later resonances again, is extremely sensitive to this cut-off radius value, representing a handicap of the CWS potential.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schroedinger equation for a spin-less neutral particle scattered on a spherically symmetric target nucleus represented by a real CWS potential. They locate S-matrix poles by searching for the absolute minimum of a function F(k^R, k^I) that compares logarithmic derivatives of the internal and external solutions over a wide radial region, using Powell's method. Pole trajectories are generated by varying the potential depth V_0 while keeping the shape parameters fixed, and the landscape of the function -F(k) is plotted over the complex wave number domain to identify approximate pole positions.

## Key numbers

- For the 208Pb+n system with l=2, V_0=44.4 MeV, R=r_0*208^(1/3) with r_0=1.27 fm, a=0.7 fm, and R_max=15 fm, the three lowest-lying poles have k values: m=1: |k^R|=0.076 fm^-1, k^I=-0.147 fm^-1; m=2: |k^R|=0.438 fm^-1, k^I=-0.603 fm^-1; m=3: |k^R|=0.752 fm^-1, k^I=-0.449 fm^-1
- The repulsion between d_2 and d_3 resonances occurs at V_0=51.5 MeV, with steps of Delta V=0.1 MeV
- Changing R_max from 15 fm to 15.1 fm reduced the closest distance between the repelling resonances to half of the original distance
- The d_2 antibound pole meets the c_1 antibound pole at approximately k=(0,-0.1) fm^-1 on the imaginary axis at V_0=66.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pole trajectories in CWS potentials, particularly the observation by Salamon and Vertse (2008) that broad resonance positions depend on the cut-off radius and the strange circulating shapes reported by Vertse et al. (2012). The authors compare with the SV potential (Salamon and Vertse, 2008) which goes smoothly to zero and lacks these circulating shapes. The results are relevant for Gamow shell model calculations using Berggren ensembles, as in the analysis of 7Be(p,gamma)8B and 7Li(n,gamma)8Li reactions by Fossez et al. (2015), where knowing pole locations is crucial for choosing the integration path. The authors suggest using a relatively small cut-off radius to reduce pole interactions, and note that similar avoided crossing phenomena were observed in axially deformed Woods-Saxon potentials by Fossez et al. (1997) when deformation was varied.
