# Proton Spin in Chiral Quark Models

> The spin and flavor fractions of constituent quarks in the proton are obtained from their chiral fluctuations involving Goldstone bosons. SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking suggested by the mass difference between the strange and up, down quarks is included, and this improves the agreement with the data markedly.

## Key claim

Including SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking via a hypercharge generator term proportional to the strange–up/down quark mass difference brings the chiral quark model's predictions for proton spin fractions and sea quark observables into much closer agreement with experimental data, though the axial vector coupling constant \(g_A^{(3)}\) remains slightly low and the \(\bar u/\bar d\) ratio stays at 3/4 unless the \(\eta\) meson is assumed to couple only to strange quarks.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(3)-symmetric chiral quark model by adding a symmetry-breaking term proportional to \(\lambda_8\) in the quark–Goldstone-boson interaction Lagrangian, parameterized by a single parameter \(\epsilon\). They compute the transition probabilities for chiral fluctuations \(q_{\uparrow,\downarrow} \rightarrow q_{\downarrow,\uparrow} + \text{Goldstone boson}\) for u, d, and s quarks, then derive the resulting spin fractions \(\Delta u, \Delta d, \Delta s\) and antiquark fractions \(\bar u, \bar d, \bar s\) to first order in the coupling \(a = g_8^2\). Numerical results are obtained for \(\epsilon = 0.2\) and \(a = 0.12\), and compared with experimental data from deep inelastic scattering and weak decays.

## Key numbers

* \(\Delta_3/\Delta_8 = 2.12\) for \(\epsilon = 0.2\), compared to the experimental value \(2.09 \pm 0.13\) (E143) and the SU(3)-symmetric value 5/3.
* \(f_3/f_8 = 0.24\) for \(\epsilon = 0.2\), compared to the experimental value \(0.23 \pm 0.05\).
* \(g_A^{(3)} = {\cal F}+{\cal D} = 1.217\) for \(\epsilon = 0.2\), below the experimental value \(1.2573 \pm 0.0028\) (PDG).
* \(\bar u/\bar d = 0.75\) for \(\epsilon = 0.2\), compared to the experimental value \(0.51 \pm 0.09\) (E143).
* \(\Delta \Sigma = 0.36\) for \(\epsilon = 0.2\), compared to the experimental value \(0.30 \pm 0.06\) (E143) and the NQM value 1.
* \(\Delta s = -0.07\) for \(\epsilon = 0.2\), compared to the experimental value \(-0.08 \pm 0.05\) (E143).
* Gottfried sum rule \(I_G = 0.27\) for \(\epsilon = 0.2\), compared to the experimental value \(0.235 \pm 0.026\) (E143).

## Context

This work extends the SU(3)-symmetric chiral quark model of Eichten, Hinchliffe, and Quigg (1990) and Cheng and Li (1995) by incorporating SU(3) breaking via the strange–up/down quark mass difference, which was previously omitted. The results improve upon the symmetric model's predictions for \(\Delta_3/\Delta_8\) and \(f_3/f_8\), and show that including the \(\eta'\) meson (as in Cheng and Li 1995) does not resolve the remaining discrepancy in \(g_A^{(3)}\). The framework enables future inclusion of relativistic corrections, which are expected to further affect \(g_A^{(3)}\), and suggests that a modified \(\eta\) coupling to strange quarks alone may improve the \(\bar u/\bar d\) ratio.
