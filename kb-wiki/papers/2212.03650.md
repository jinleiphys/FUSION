# Transfer Reactions in Nuclear Astrophysics

> Transfer reactions are an important tool in nuclear astrophysics. These reactions allow us to identify states in nuclei and to find the corresponding energies, to determine if these states can contribute to astrophysical nuclear reactions and ultimately to determine the strength of that contribution. In this paper, the basic details of how transfer reactions may be used in nuclear astrophysics are set out along with some common pitfalls to avoid.

## Key claim

Transfer reactions are a powerful indirect tool for determining thermonuclear reaction rates in nuclear astrophysics, but the information extracted is model-dependent and requires careful selection of appropriate reaction models to avoid deficient conclusions.

## Method

The paper reviews how transfer reactions (single-nucleon, cluster, or charge-exchange) are used to identify nuclear states, determine their energies via two-body kinematics, constrain spins and parities from differential cross sections, and extract spectroscopic factors or asymptotic normalisation coefficients (ANCs) through Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations. These quantities feed into calculations of direct-capture and resonance-capture cross sections, which are folded with Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions to obtain thermonuclear reaction rates.

## Key numbers

- Transfer reactions occur on timescales of approximately 10^-22 seconds, dominated by peripheral interactions at the nuclear surface.
- The direct-capture cross section is calculated as σ_DC(E_c.m.) = Σ_i (C²S)_i σ_i,DC(E_c.m.), where (C²S)_i is the spectroscopic factor of the i-th state.
- The resonance cross section follows the Breit-Wigner form: σ_res(E_c.m.) = ω (πℏ²)/(2μE) × (Γ_in Γ_out)/[(E_c.m. - E_R)² + Γ²/4], where ω = (2J+1)/[(2j₁+1)(2j₂+1)].
- The partial width for resonance capture is given by Γ = 2P_ℓ(E,R)(ℏ²R)/(2μ) C²S |φ(R)|², where P_ℓ is the penetrability at angular momentum ℓ.
- A factor 3 change in the ANC for ¹³C+α was observed between the works of Johnson and Avila, the latter correcting for beam energy reduction due to target build-up.

## Context

This paper extends the pedagogical and methodological discussions found in the book by Iliadis and the work by Thompson and Nunes, providing a concise overview of transfer reaction techniques for nuclear astrophysics. It enables practitioners to avoid common pitfalls such as overinterpreting state non-existence from single reactions, misidentifying spins due to insensitivity to total spin in single-nucleon transfer, and underestimating model dependencies in spectroscopic factor extraction. The paper specifically references the ⁵⁰Cr(p,t)⁴⁸Cr study at iThemba LABS for state identification relevant to the ⁴⁴Ti(α,p)⁴⁷V reaction in core-collapse supernovae, and the ²⁵Mg(d,p)²⁶Mg study by Chen compared to neutron-capture measurements by Massimi, illustrating cases where transfer reactions may not fully specify the reaction rate when gamma-ray partial widths are rate-limiting.
