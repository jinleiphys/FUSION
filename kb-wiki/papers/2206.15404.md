# Inferring the nuclear symmetry energy at supra saturation density from neutrino cooling

> An ambitious goal of the astrophysical community is not only to constrain the equation of state (EOS) of neutron star (NS) matter by confronting it with astrophysics observations, but ultimately also to infer the NS composition. Nevertheless, the composition of the NS core is likely to remain uncertain unless we have an accurate determination of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra saturation density ($ρ>ρ_0$). We investigate how the nucleonic direct Urca (dUrca) processes can be used as an effective probe to constraint the high density nuclear symmetry energy. A large number of minimally constrained EOSs has been constructed by applying a Bayesian approach to study the correlations of the symmetry energy at different densities with a few selected properties of a NS. The nuclear symmetry energy above the baryon density 0.5 fm$^{-3}$ ($\sim 3 ρ_0$) is found to be strongly correlated with NS mass at which the onset of nucleonic dUrca neutrino cooling takes place in the core. This allows us to constrain the high density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy within narrow bounds. {The pure neutron matter pressure constraint from chiral effective field theory rules out the onset of nucleonic dUrca in stars with a mass $\lesssim$ 1.4 $M_\odot$.} The onset of dUrca inside 1.6 M$_\odot$ to 1.8 M$_\odot$ NS implies a slope of the symmetry energy $L$ at $\sim 2.5~ρ_0$, respectively, between 54 and 48 MeV.

## Key claim

The nucleonic direct Urca (dUrca) neutrino cooling process can serve as an effective probe to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities (above 0.5 fm$^{-3}$), but this constraint is conditional on the assumption that only nucleons are present in the neutron star core; the presence of hyperons or pairing effects would shift the inferred bounds.

## Method

The authors constructed a large set of minimally constrained equations of state (EOS) using a Bayesian inference approach applied to relativistic mean-field models with density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings (DDH models). They introduced a modified parametrization for the density dependence of the $\rho$-meson coupling to allow nucleonic dUrca to occur, then studied correlations between the symmetry energy at different densities and the neutron star mass at which dUrca cooling turns on (M$_{\rm dUrca}$). Additional Bayesian analyses were performed imposing pseudo-constraints on M$_{\rm dUrca}$ at 1.4, 1.6, 1.8, and 2.0 M$_\odot$.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear symmetry energy above baryon density 0.5 fm$^{-3}$ ($\sim 3 \rho_0$) is strongly correlated with M$_{\rm dUrca}$, with linear regression coefficients $a(\rho)/{\rm MeV} = -56.8095~\rho/{\rm fm}^{-3} + 4.8828$ and $b(\rho)/{\rm MeV} = 166.0152~\rho/{\rm fm}^{-3} + 30.1419$ for the relation $S(\rho) = a(\rho) \cdot {\rm M}_{\rm dUrca}/M_\odot + b(\rho)$.
- For dUrca onset in stars with masses between 1.6 M$_\odot$ and 1.8 M$_\odot$, the slope of the symmetry energy $L$ at $\sim 2.5~\rho_0$ lies between 54 and 48 MeV, with the anti-correlation given by $L/{\rm MeV} = -31.224~{\rm M}_{\rm dUrca}/M_\odot + 104.339$.
- The 90% confidence intervals for the nuclear symmetry energy $S(\rho)$ at $\rho = 0.56$ fm$^{-3}$ are [74, 82] MeV for M$_{\rm dUrca} = 1.6$ M$_\odot$ and [69, 76] MeV for M$_{\rm dUrca} = 1.8$ M$_\odot$.
- The pure neutron matter pressure constraint from chiral effective field theory rules out the onset of nucleonic dUrca in stars with mass $\lesssim 1.4$ M$_\odot$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Klahn (2006), Cavagnoli (2011), Providencia (2013, 2018), and Fortin (2016) that showed the dUrca process is strongly influenced by the density dependence of the symmetry energy. It specifically addresses the limitation that DDH relativistic mean-field models with standard $\rho$-meson couplings (such as DD2 and DDME2) do not predict nucleonic dUrca inside neutron stars. The results enable future work to use observational estimates of M$_{\rm dUrca}$ from neutron star cooling curves, as studied by Beznogov (2015), to place quantitative bounds on the high-density symmetry energy, though the authors caution that hyperonic dUrca channels and pairing effects must be included in future studies.
