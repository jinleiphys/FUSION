# Low-temperature triple-alpha rate in a full three-body model

> A new full three-body method is introduced to compute the rate of the triple-alpha capture reaction which is the primary source of $^{12}$C in stars. In this work, we combine the Faddeev hyperspherical harmonics and the R-matrix method to obtain a full solution to the three-body $α+α+α$ continuum. Particular attention is paid to the long range effects caused by the pairwise Coulomb interactions. The new rate agrees with the NACRE rate for temperatures greater than 0.07 GK, but a large enhancement at lower temperature is found ($\approx 10^{14}$ at 0.02 GK). Our results are compared to previous calculations where additional approximations were made. We show that the new rate does not significantly change the evolution of stars around one solar mass. In particular, such stars still undergo a red-giant phase consistent with observations, and no significant differences are found in the final white dwarfs.

# Low-temperature triple-alpha rate in a full three-body model

## Key claim
The new hyperspherical harmonic R-matrix (HHR) rate agrees with the NACRE rate for temperatures above 0.07 GK, but shows a large enhancement at lower temperatures (approximately 10^12 at 0.02 GK). However, this enhancement does not significantly change the evolution of stars around one solar mass, which still undergo a red-giant phase consistent with observations, unlike the case with the CDCC rate from Ogata (2009) which predicted a disappearance of the red-giant phase.

## Method
The authors combine the Faddeev hyperspherical harmonics method with the R-matrix method to solve the full three-body alpha+alpha+alpha continuum scattering problem. They use an R-matrix propagation technique with screening of off-diagonal Coulomb couplings to handle the long-range Coulomb interactions out to hyper-radii of 3000 fm. The bound 2_1^+ state of 12C and the 0^+ continuum states are obtained from the same three-body Hamiltonian, and the electromagnetic transition strength dB(E2)/dE is computed to determine the reaction rate.

## Key numbers
- The new HHR rate shows an enhancement of approximately 10^12 at 0.02 GK compared to the NACRE rate
- The HHR rate agrees with NACRE for temperatures greater than 0.07 GK
- At T = 0.02 GK, the temperature sensitivity d ln<R>/d ln T is 23.3 for HHR versus 45.5 for NACRE
- At T = 0.08 GK, the temperature sensitivity is 51.7 for HHR versus 48.3 for NACRE
- The calculations include up to K = 26 in the hyperspherical harmonic expansion, with 50 poles in the R-matrix expansion, an R-matrix box size of 50 fm, and propagation out to 3000 fm
- A screening radius of 800 fm and diffuseness of 10 fm were used for the off-diagonal Coulomb couplings
- The results with K_max = 26 differ from the extrapolation to infinite K by at most a factor of 2

## Context
This work extends the three-body approach to the triple-alpha reaction previously attempted by the Kyushu group using CDCC (Ogata, 2009) and the Madrid-Aarhus collaboration using hyperspherical adiabatic expansion (Alvarez-Rodriguez, 2007; Diego, 2010), as well as the three-body Breit-Wigner extrapolation by Garrido (2011). The CDCC results predicted a 20 order of magnitude increase at 0.02 GK and would suppress He flashes and eliminate the red-giant phase in low-mass stars (Dotter, 2009; Suda, 2011). The present HHR rate gives a more moderate enhancement and shows that standard stellar evolution is preserved for solar-mass stars. The method enables reliable calculations down to 0.01 GK, which was not possible with previous hyperspherical techniques.
