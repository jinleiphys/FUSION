# The Quark-Meson Model and the Phase Diagram of Two-Flavour QCD

> We study the qualitative features of the QCD phase diagram in the context of the linear quark-meson model with two flavours, using the exact renormalization group. We identify the universality classes of the second-order phase transitions and calculate critical exponents. In the absence of explicit chiral-symmetry breaking through the current quark masses, we discuss in detail the tricritical point and demonstrate that it is linked to the Gaussian fixed point. In its vicinity we study the universal crossover between the Gaussian and O(4) fixed points, and the weak first-order phase transitions. In the presence of explicit chiral-symmetry breaking, we study in detail the critical endpoint of the line of first-order phase transitions. We demonstrate the decoupling of pion fluctuations and identify the Ising universality class as the relevant one for the second-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the qualitative features of the QCD phase diagram for two quark flavours can be reproduced within the linear quark-meson model using the exact renormalization group, but only for parameter values that are not realistic for phenomenology, as the treatment becomes too complicated for realistic values and little analytical progress can be made.

## Method

The author studies the linear quark-meson model with two flavours using the exact renormalization group (effective average action formalism), deriving and solving the evolution equation for the scale-dependent potential at non-zero temperature and chemical potential. The analysis considers both the case without explicit chiral symmetry breaking (zero current quark masses) and with explicit breaking, focusing on the fixed-point structure and universal behaviour near critical points. The evolution equation is cast in a scaling form that permits identification of fixed points, and the fermionic contributions are assumed to decouple at scales below the temperature.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent beta takes the mean field value beta = 1/2 for second-order phase transitions when the quartic coupling lambda is positive, and beta = 1/4 at the tricritical point where lambda = 0
- The number of colours N_c = 3 is used throughout the fermionic contributions
- The threshold functions L^d_n(w;T) satisfy L^d_n(w;T) = L^d_n(w,0) for T much less than k, and L^d_n(w;T) = (v_{d-1}/v_d)(T/k)L^{d-1}_n(w) for T much greater than k
- The leading perturbative result for the critical temperature includes a term 6T^2/24 in the renormalized potential

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the quark-meson model by Berges, Jungnickel, and Wetterich (1997-1998) that established the model's formulation for non-zero temperature and chemical potential, and by Tetradis and Wetterich (1992-1993) on the exact renormalization group evolution equations. The paper identifies the Ising universality class as relevant for the critical endpoint of first-order phase transitions in the presence of explicit chiral symmetry breaking, and demonstrates the decoupling of pion fluctuations. The results enable future studies with realistic parameter values and the generalization to three flavours, though the author notes that colour superconducting or colour-flavour locked phases require additional degrees of freedom not included in this model.
