# Deciphering the coalescence behavior of Coulomb-Schrödinger atomic wave functions from an operator product expansion

> We revisit the coalescence behavior of the atomic Schrödinger wave functions from the angle of an operator product expansion (OPE) within the nonrelativistic Coulomb-Schrödinger effective field theory. We take the electron-nucleus coalescence as an explicit example to demonstrate our formalism, where the celebrated Kato's cusp condition can be easily reproduced. An exact OPE relation is rigorously proved to all orders in perturbation theory. Our approach can be readily extended to ascertain the multi-particle coalescence behaviors of atomic wave functions, as well as to take relativistic effects into account.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the coalescence behavior of atomic Schrödinger wave functions can be derived from an operator product expansion (OPE) within the nonrelativistic Coulomb-Schrödinger effective field theory, with the proof holding to all orders in perturbation theory in the fine-structure constant. The abstract glosses over the fact that the OPE relation is proven specifically for the case where the hard momentum scale is much larger than the inverse Bohr radius but still smaller than the electron mass, and that the proof relies on the specific causal structure of nonrelativistic propagators which eliminates crossed ladder, self-energy, and vertex-correction diagrams.

## Method

The authors construct a Coulomb-Schrödinger effective field theory from NRQED and heavy nucleus effective theory (HNET), retaining only the instantaneous Coulomb interaction. They prove an exact OPE relation for the product of an electron field and a nucleus field by analyzing the large-momentum behavior of amputated four-point Green's functions, using a perturbative induction method on Coulomb ladder diagrams. The Wilson coefficients for the S-wave and P-wave composite operators are extracted from the tree-level diagram and shown to receive no further radiative corrections from higher-order Coulomb exchanges.

## Key numbers

- The electron-nucleus coalescence condition yields a linear term with coefficient \(1/a_0\) where \(a_0 \equiv (mZ\alpha)^{-1}\) is the Bohr radius; for S-wave hydrogenic states, \(R_{n0}(r) \to R_{n0}(0)(1 - r/a_0)\) as \(r \to 0\)
- For P-wave hydrogenic states, \(R_{n1}(r) \to r R'_{n0}(0)(1 - r/(2a_0))\) as \(r \to 0\)
- The momentum-space wave function scales as \(\widetilde R_{nl}(q) \propto 1/q^{l+4}\) in the large-momentum regime \(1/a_0 \ll q \lesssim m\)
- The OPE in momentum space gives the leading Wilson coefficient \(8\pi m Z\alpha / \mathbf{q}^4\) for the S-wave operator and \(-16 i \pi m Z\alpha / \mathbf{q}^6\) for the P-wave operator

## Context

This work extends the OPE approach previously applied to the unitary Fermi gas by Braaten and Platter (2008) and to the jellium model by Hofmann et al. (2013), now applied to atomic Coulomb systems with a direct connection to QED. The results reproduce Kato's cusp condition (1957) and Löwdin's formula for hydrogen-like atoms (1954), while providing a field-theoretic framework that can be extended to multi-particle coalescence and relativistic corrections, potentially addressing a 90-year-old puzzle about the coalescence behavior of Dirac hydrogen atom wave functions.
