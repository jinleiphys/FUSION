# Two-solar-mass hybrid stars: a two model description with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio quark model

> Hybrid stars with a quark phase described by the Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model are studied. The hadron-quark model used to determine the stellar matter equation of state favors the appearance of quark matter: the coincidence of the deconfinement and chiral transitions and a low vacuum constituent quark mass. These two properties are essential to build equations of state that predict pure quark matter in the center of neutron stars. The effect of vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector terms is discussed, and it is shown that the vector-isoscalar terms are necessary to describe 2$M_\odot$ hybrid stars, and the vector-isovector terms result in larger quark cores and a smaller deconfinement density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hybrid stars with a pure quark core described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model can reach two solar masses, but only if the vector-isoscalar coupling is sufficiently strong and the vacuum constituent quark mass is low (around 313 MeV), and if an effective bag constant is introduced to make the deconfinement and chiral transitions coincide.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for hybrid stars by combining a relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic model (NL3ωρ) with quark matter described by the SU(2) and SU(3) NJL models including vector interactions. They imposed Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium and fixed an effective bag constant B* so that the deconfinement transition coincides with the chiral symmetry transition. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations were then solved to obtain stellar mass-radius relations for different values of the vector coupling ratio ξ = G_V/G_S.

## Key numbers

* The hadronic model NL3ωρ alone gives a maximum neutron star mass of 2.75 M⊙, well above the 2 M⊙ constraint.
* The SU(2) NJL model with ξ = 0.75 and the NJL(V+P+VI+PI) vector scenario yields a maximum hybrid star mass of 2.02 M⊙, satisfying the PSR J0348+0432 constraint (2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙).
* The SU(3) NJL model with ξ = 0.75 and the NJL(V+P+VI+PI) scenario produces a maximum hybrid star mass of 2.04 M⊙, also above the 2 M⊙ threshold.
* The vacuum constituent quark mass for the SU(2) parametrization is M_u,d = 312.6 MeV, and for the SU(3) parametrization it is M_u,d = 312.2 MeV.
* The SU(2) NJL model is valid up to baryon densities of about 11 ρ0 (where ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is saturation density), and the SU(3) model up to at least 15 ρ0.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Pagliara et al. (2007) and Bonanno et al. (2011) that used the NJL model for hybrid stars but did not reach two solar masses, or did so only with color-superconducting phases. It contrasts with Schertler et al. (1999) and Baldo et al. (2002), who found that pure quark phases did not occur or that maximum masses remained below 1.8 M⊙. The results enable future studies of hybrid stars with strangeness and vector interactions, and show that the vector-isovector coupling produces larger quark cores and lower deconfinement densities.
