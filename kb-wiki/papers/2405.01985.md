# Systematic study of capture thresholds with time dependent Hartree-Fock theory

> With the time dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, capture thresholds $E_{\rm cap}$ for 144 fusion systems with nearly spherical nuclei are systematically studied for the first time. We find that for the reactions between doubly-magic nuclei, the calculated $E_{\rm cap}$ are very close to the extracted barrier heights from measured fusion excitation functions. For the fusion reactions with nearly spherical nuclei, an excitation energy of about 1 MeV at the capture position need to be considered to better reproduce the data due to the lower excitation threshold. The rms deviation with respect to the barrier heights is only 1.43 MeV from the TDHF calcualtions, which is smaller than the results from three empirical nuclear potentials. Together with Siwek-Wilczyński formula in which the three parameters are determined by the TDHF calculations, the measured fusion cross sections at energies around the barriers can be well reproduced for seven fusion reactions $^{40}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb, $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr, $^{28}$Si+$^{96}$Zr and $^{132}$Sn+$^{40,48}$Ca.

## Key claim

The TDHF capture thresholds for 144 fusion systems with nearly spherical nuclei are systematically calculated for the first time, and the rms deviation from extracted barrier heights is 1.43 MeV, which is smaller than that from three empirical nuclear potentials; however, for reactions with nearly spherical (open shell) nuclei, an excitation energy of about 1 MeV at the capture position must be considered to better reproduce the data, whereas for doubly-magic nuclei the calculated thresholds are very close to the extracted barrier heights without such correction.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional TDHF calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional with parameter set SLy6, with head-on collisions on a coordinate-space grid of varying size depending on nuclear charge. The capture threshold was defined as the lowest incident energy at which the composite system remains in contact for at least 2 zeptoseconds (approximately 600 fm/c) after projectile-target contact, using the DBSCAN algorithm to identify clusters. For deformed nuclei, capture thresholds were calculated at four extreme orientation configurations and combined via a weighted average formula to obtain the most probable capture threshold.

## Key numbers

- Capture threshold for $^{40}$Ca + $^{48}$Ca: $51.95 \pm 0.05$ MeV, determined from TDHF calculations with a binary search terminated at energy intervals smaller than 0.2 MeV.
- RMS deviation between TDHF-calculated barrier heights and extracted barrier heights for 144 fusion systems: 1.43 MeV, compared to 2.11 MeV (Bass potential), 1.67 MeV (BW91), and 1.53 MeV (MWS).
- Relative deviation between TDHF capture thresholds and extracted barrier heights: rms error of 2.48% for the 144 systems.
- Barrier radius from frozen Hartree-Fock calculations with SLy6: 10.2 fm for $^{40}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca and 11.6 fm for $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb.
- Standard deviation $W$ of the Gaussian barrier distribution from TDHF: 2.87 MeV for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr, 3.24 MeV for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca, and 1.92 MeV for $^{132}$Sn+$48$Ca.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of fusion barriers by Chen (2023), who extracted barrier heights for over 300 fusion systems by fitting measured excitation functions, and applies TDHF theory to 144 of those systems with nearly spherical nuclei. The TDHF results improve upon empirical nuclear potentials such as the Bass potential (Bass, 1974), the BW91 Woods-Saxon potential (Brack and Werner, 1991), and the MWS potential (Wang, 2008), and enable more accurate predictions of fusion cross sections using the Siwek-Wilczynski formula with TDHF-determined parameters. The approach enables systematic microscopic predictions for fusion reactions involving neutron-rich nuclei and can be applied to reactions relevant for superheavy element synthesis, where quasi-fission complicates traditional barrier penetration models.
