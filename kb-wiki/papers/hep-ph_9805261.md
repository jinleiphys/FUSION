# Anomaly Induced Domain Formation of Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> We discuss the effect of chiral anomaly as a possible mechanism for triggering formation of domains of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The anomalous $π^0 \to 2 γ$ coupling and the strong, Lorentz contracted electromagnetic fields of the heavy ions combine to produce the ``anomaly kick'' to the field configuration of the neutral pion field. We implement the effect of anomaly kick in our numerical simulation of the linear sigma model in a schematic way which preserves its characteristic features: the effect is coherent over a large region of space but is opposite in sign above and below the ion scattering plane. We demonstrate by detailed simulations with longitudinal expansion that the DCC domain formation is dramatically enhanced by the anomaly kick in spite of its small absolute magnitude. We examine the behavior of various physical quantities such as pion fields, the axial vector currents, and their correlation functions. Our results also provide useful insight into the mechanism and properties of DCC domain formation, in general. Finally, we discuss some experimental observables which can signal the anomaly induced formation of DCC.

## Key claim

The chiral anomaly, via the Wess-Zumino term coupling the neutral pion field to electromagnetic fields, can trigger the formation of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domains in relativistic heavy ion collisions, even though the absolute magnitude of the "anomaly kick" to the conjugate pion field is small (approximately 0.03 mπ⁻² at RHIC). The effect is coherent over nuclear dimensions but opposite in sign above and below the scattering plane, and numerical simulations show it dramatically enhances DCC domain formation despite its small amplitude.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of the linear sigma model on a 128×128 lattice with periodic boundary conditions, assuming longitudinal boost invariance and using a quench initial condition. The effect of the chiral anomaly was implemented as a coherent initial kick to the conjugate field of the neutral pion (π₃), with opposite sign in the upper and lower half-spaces relative to the scattering plane, using the Adams-Bashforth method and staggered leapfrog/Crank-Nicholson methods for time integration. The initial field configurations were randomly distributed according to a Gaussian form with correlation length ℓcorr = 2a, and the virial theorem was used to relate initial field fluctuations to conjugate field fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Anomaly kick magnitude estimate: ΔΠ₃ ∼ 0.03 mπ⁻² in the central region of a non-central collision at RHIC (z ∼ 0, b ∼ r⊥ ∼ RA, γ ∼ 100)
- Anomaly kick parameter used in simulations: an = 0.1 (similar order but slightly larger than RHIC estimate, much smaller than LHC estimate)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.25 fm
- Initial proper time: τ₀ = 1 fm
- Time step: Δτ = 1/1800 fm or smaller
- Linear sigma model parameters: λ = 19.97, v = 87.4 MeV, H = (119 MeV)³, corresponding to mπ = 135 MeV, mσ = 600 MeV, fπ = 92.5 MeV at T=0
- Initial field fluctuation: δ₀² = v²/16
- Transient region thickness: Γ = 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the quench scenario for DCC formation proposed by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and the numerical simulations with longitudinal expansion by Asakawa, Huang, and Wang (1995). It addresses the question raised by Minakata and Muller (1996) about whether the chiral anomaly could trigger DCC formation, which had remained unanswered due to the small magnitude of the effect. The results enable experimental searches for anomaly-induced DCC signatures through observables such as the axial current A₀³ asymmetry between upper and lower half-spaces, and provide insight into the general mechanism of DCC domain formation through the effective decoupling of low-momentum Nambu-Goldstone modes.
