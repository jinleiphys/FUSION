# The Pentaquark $螛^+$ in $nK^+$ and $pK^0_S$, and its $危^+_5$ partner

> A systematic lowering in mass of strangeness ambiguous $pK^0_S$ peaks relative to the $nK^+$ is noted. We discuss how measurement of $纬p\to 螛^+ K^0_S\to p K^0_S K^0_S \to p蟺^+蟺^-蟺^+蟺^-$ in association with $纬p\to 螛^+ K^0_S\to n K^+ K^0_S$ can help to clarify the nature of the narrow baryon state claimed in $p K^0_S$ invariant mass distributions. This can both establish the $螛^+$ in a single experiment and also reveal, or place severe limits on, the existence of an associated $危_5$ pentaquark.

# The Pentaquark $\Theta^+$ in $nK^+$ and $pK^0_S$, and its $\Sigma^+_5$ partner

## Key claim

The paper notes a systematic mass shift between $pK^0_S$ peaks (lower mass) and $nK^+$ peaks (higher mass) in claimed pentaquark signals, and proposes that measuring $\gamma p \to \Theta^+ K^0_S \to p K^0_S K^0_S$ alongside $\gamma p \to \Theta^+ K^0_S \to n K^+ K^0_S$ in a single experiment could distinguish whether the narrow state is the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark with strangeness $S=+1$ or a $\Sigma_5$ pentaquark, or reveal kinematic artifacts.

## Method

The authors analyze the systematic mass difference between $nK^+$ and $pK^0_S$ signals reported in prior experiments, compiling data from multiple collaborations. They propose a specific photoproduction reaction $\gamma p \to p K^0_S K^0_S \to p\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-$, which is free from the $\phi$ meson background ambiguity that affects the $nK^+$ channel, and argue that comparing the $pK^0_S$ and $nK^+$ invariant mass spectra from the same experiment can test for the existence of both the $\Theta^+$ and a predicted $\Sigma_5$ pentaquark partner.

## Key numbers

- The $\Theta^+$ is claimed at a mass around 1.54 GeV in the $nK^+$ channel.
- The $pK^0_S$ peaks appear systematically lower in mass than the $nK^+$ peaks, with the $pK^0_S$ signal at approximately 1.53 GeV in several experiments.
- The $\Sigma_5$ pentaquark is predicted to have a mass within 100 MeV of the $\Theta^+$.
- The predicted photoproduction rate of $\Sigma_5$ relative to $\Theta^+$ is about $1/6$ to $1/2$.
- The width of the $\Theta^+$ from $NK$ scattering analyses is constrained to be narrower than 1 MeV.
- The HERMES collaboration reported a width of 17 MeV in the $pK^0_S$ channel.

## Context

This work extends the discussion following the claimed observation of the $\Theta^+(1540)$ pentaquark by several experiments including SAPHIR (2003), CLAS (2003), DIANA (2003), HERMES (2004), and others, and addresses concerns raised by Rosner (2004) and Dzierba et al. (2004) about possible kinematic reflections and background processes mimicking a resonance. The paper proposes a new experimental channel to resolve ambiguities in strangeness assignment and to test predictions from the chiral soliton model of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and quark model calculations by Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) and Close (2003), which predict a $\Sigma_5$ pentaquark partner. The proposed measurements could establish the $\Theta^+$ definitively or place severe limits on the existence of the $\Sigma_5$.
