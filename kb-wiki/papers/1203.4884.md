# Five-body resonances of 8C using the complex scaling method

> We study the resonance spectroscopy of the proton-rich nucleus 8C in the alpha+p+p+p+p cluster model. Many-body resonances are treated on the correct boundary condition as the Gamow states using the complex scaling method. We obtain the ground state of 8C as a five-body resonance for the first time, which has dominantly the sub-closed (p_{3/2})^4 configuration and agrees with the recent experiment for energy and decay width. We predict the second 0+ state with the excitation energy of 5.6 MeV, which corresponds to the $2p2h$ state from the ground state. We evaluate the occupation numbers of four valence-protons in the 8C states and also the J^πdistribution of proton-pair numbers of the two 0+ states of 8C. The ground state involves a large amount of the 2+ proton-pair fraction, while the excited 0+_2 state almost consists of two of the 0+ proton pairs, which can be understood from the (p_{3/2})^2(p_{1/2})^2 configuration. We also discuss the mirror symmetry between 8C and 8He with an alpha+four nucleon picture. It is found that the 0+ states retain the mirror symmetry well for the configuration properties of two nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors claim to obtain the ground state of ⁸C as a five-body resonance for the first time using the complex scaling method, with the caveat that the calculated decay width of 0.072 MeV is slightly smaller than the experimental value of 0.130(50) MeV, and the calculated resonance energy of 3.32 MeV agrees with the experimental value of 3.449(30) MeV.

## Method

The authors employ the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) for the ⁴He+4p five-body system, treating ⁴He as a (0s)⁴ harmonic oscillator core and expanding the relative wave functions of valence protons using Gaussian basis functions with angular momenta ℓ ≤ 2. Many-body resonances are treated as Gamow states using the complex scaling method (CSM), where the relative coordinates are transformed by a scaling angle θ to obtain resonance eigenvalues E = E_r − iΓ/2 from diagonalization of the complex-scaled Hamiltonian. The Hamiltonian uses the KKNN potential for the ⁴He-p interaction and the Minnesota potential for the p-p interaction, with the repulsive strength adjusted to 173.7 MeV to reproduce the two-neutron separation energy of ⁶He.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of ⁸C: resonance energy E_r = 3.32 MeV, decay width Γ = 0.072 MeV (experimental: E_r = 3.449(30) MeV, Γ = 0.130(50) MeV)
- Excited 0⁺₂ state of ⁸C: resonance energy 8.88 MeV above the ⁴He+4p threshold, decay width 6.64 MeV, corresponding to excitation energy 5.6 MeV from the ground state
- Ground state of ⁸C: dominant (p₃/₂)⁴ configuration with complex squared amplitude 0.878 − i0.005; 2p2h excitations mix with total squared amplitude about 0.12
- Excited 0⁺₂ state of ⁸C: dominant (p₃/₂)²(p₁/₂)² configuration with complex squared amplitude 0.934 − i0.012
- Matter radius of ⁸C ground state: R_m = 2.81 − i0.08 fm; charge radius: R_ch = 3.18 − i0.09 fm
- Matter radius of ⁸He ground state: R_m = 2.52 fm (experimental: 2.49(4) fm from Tanihata (1992), 2.53(8) fm from Alkhazov (1997), 2.49(4) fm from Kiselev (2005))
- Relative distance between ⁴He core and four valence nucleons: 2.36 − i0.03 fm for ⁸C, 2.05 fm for ⁸He
- Displacement energies due to Coulomb repulsion: 2.4 MeV for ⁶Be from ⁶He, 3.9 MeV for ⁷B from ⁷He, 6.5 MeV for ⁸C from ⁸He
- Pair numbers in ⁸C ground state: 0⁺ proton pair number about 1.1 (real part), 2⁺ proton pair number about 4.6 (real part)
- Pair numbers in ⁸C 0⁺₂ state: 0⁺ proton pair number about 2.0 (real part), 2⁺ proton pair number about 2.6 (real part)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of ⁷B with the ⁴He+3p model (Myo, 2011) and their studies of neutron-rich He isotopes using the ⁴He+4n model (Myo, 2010), applying the same complex-scaled COSM framework to the proton-rich nucleus ⁸C for the first time as a five-body resonance calculation. The results enable comparison of mirror symmetry between ⁸C and ⁸He, showing that the 0⁺ states retain good mirror symmetry in their configuration properties, with the ground states dominated by the (p₃/₂)⁴ sub-closed configuration and the excited 0⁺₂ states dominated by the (p₃/₂)²(p₁/₂)² configuration in both nuclei. The calculated pair numbers in the ⁸C ground state are consistent with the experimental observation by Charity (2010, 2011) that the decay proceeds through the ⁶Be(0⁺₁)+2p channel with probability 0.92(5).
