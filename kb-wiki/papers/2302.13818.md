# Proton Charge Radius from Lepton Scattering

> Protons are bound states of the strong interaction governed by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Its charge radius ($r_{E}^{p}$) is an important quantity as it characterizes the spatial distribution of the proton's charge, which is carried by the quarks. On the other hand, the proton charge radius is an essential physical input for the bound-state Quantum Electrodynamic (QED) calculations for the hydrogen atomic energy levels. Nevertheless, the large discrepancy between $r_{E}^{p}$ measurements from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy, and those from $ep$ elastic scattering and ordinary hydrogen spectroscopy, have been puzzling physicists for over a decade. Tremendous efforts, in both theoretical and experimental sides, have been dedicated to providing various insights into this puzzle, yet certain issues still remain unresolved, particularly in the field of lepton scatterings. This review will focus on $r_{E}^{p}$ measurements using lepton scatterings, the recent theoretical and experimental developments in this field, as well as future experiments using this technique.

## Key claim
This review paper surveys the proton charge radius (r_E^p) measurements from lepton scattering, noting that despite extensive theoretical and experimental efforts, certain issues in the field of lepton scatterings remain unresolved regarding the long-standing discrepancy between results from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy and those from electron-proton elastic scattering and ordinary hydrogen spectroscopy.

## Method
The authors review the theoretical and experimental developments in proton charge radius determinations using lepton scattering techniques, covering elastic electron-proton (ep) scattering experiments, the extraction of the form factor and its extrapolation to zero momentum transfer (Q^2 = 0), and the associated systematic uncertainties. They also discuss recent experimental developments and future experiments planned using this technique.

## Key numbers
Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context
This review addresses the "proton radius puzzle," a decade-old discrepancy between the r_E^p value from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (approximately 0.84 fm) and the values from ep elastic scattering and ordinary hydrogen spectroscopy (approximately 0.88 fm). The paper extends earlier reviews of the subject by focusing specifically on lepton scattering measurements and their recent developments. It enables future work by cataloging ongoing and planned experiments that aim to resolve the puzzle, such as the PRad experiment at Jefferson Lab and the MUSE experiment at PSI.
