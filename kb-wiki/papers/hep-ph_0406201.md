# Leading-particle suppression in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Parton energy loss effects in heavy-ion collisions are studied with the Monte Carlo program PQM (Parton Quenching Model) constructed using the BDMPS quenching weights and a realistic collision geometry. The merit of the approach is that it contains only one free parameter that is tuned to the high-pt nuclear modification factor measured in central Au-Au collisions at sqrt{s_NN} = 200 GeV. Once tuned, the model is coherently applied to all the high-pt observables at 200 GeV: the centrality evolution of the nuclear modification factor, the suppression of the away-side jet-like correlations, and the azimuthal anisotropies for these observables. Predictions for the leading-particle suppression at nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energies of 62.4 and 5500 GeV are calculated. The limits of the eikonal approximation in the BDMPS approach, when applied to finite-energy partons, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a single-parameter Monte Carlo model (PQM), tuned to the central Au-Au nuclear modification factor at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, can coherently describe multiple high-pt observables, but only if the transport coefficient takes very large values (average hat-q approximately 14 GeV^2/fm) that push the BDMPS eikonal approximation to its limits when applied to finite-energy partons.

## Method

The authors constructed the Parton Quenching Model (PQM) by generating hard partons with PYTHIA, modifying their energies using BDMPS quenching weights from Salgado and Wiedemann (2003), and hadronizing them independently via KKP fragmentation functions. The collision geometry was implemented using a Glauber model with Wood-Saxon nuclear density profiles, providing realistic spatial distributions of parton production points and the amount of matter traversed by each parton. The single free parameter k, which sets the scale of the transport coefficient, was tuned to reproduce the measured nuclear modification factor in central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Average transport coefficient in central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: average hat-q approximately 14 GeV^2/fm (using alpha_s = 1/3); if alpha_s = 1/2 were used, average hat-q approximately 10 GeV^2/fm
* Nuclear modification factor R_AA in central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: approximately 0.2 at high pt
* Maximum escape path length L_max_escape at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: approximately 2.5 fm from central (0-5%) to semi-peripheral (40-60%) collisions
* Maximum escape path length at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV: approximately 3 fm in central collisions
* Maximum escape path length at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV (LHC): approximately 1.5 fm in central collisions
* Average relative energy loss for gluons in central collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: 70-80% (non-reweighted approach)
* Average relative energy loss for quarks in central collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: 50-70% (non-reweighted approach)

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS parton energy loss formalism (Baier et al., 1997) combined with quenching weights from Salgado and Wiedemann (2003) by incorporating realistic collision geometry via a Glauber model, following the approach of Drees, Feng and Jia (2004) who showed the importance of geometry. The model enables predictions for leading-particle suppression at LHC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV) and intermediate RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV), and the results differ substantially from predictions by Vitev and Gyulassy (2002) for LHC, showing a much stronger suppression that extends to very high transverse momenta.
