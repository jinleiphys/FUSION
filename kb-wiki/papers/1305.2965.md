# Spins and Magnetic Moments of $^{49}$K and $^{51}$K: establishing the 1/2$^+$ and 3/2$^+$ level ordering beyond $N$ = 28

> The ground-state spins and magnetic moments of $^{49,51}$K have been measured using bunched-beam high-resolution collinear laser spectroscopy at ISOLDE-CERN. For $^{49}$K a ground-state spin $I = 1/2$ was firmly established. The observed hyperfine structure of $^{51}$K requires a spin $I > 1/2$ and from its magnetic moment $μ(^{51}\text{K})= +0.5129(22)\, μ_N$ a spin/parity $I^π=3/2^+$ with a dominant $π1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ hole configuration was deduced. This establishes for the first time the re-inversion of the single-particle levels and illustrates the prominent role of the residual monopole interaction for single-particle levels and shell evolution.

# Spins and Magnetic Moments of $^{49}$K and $^{51}$K: establishing the 1/2$^+$ and 3/2$^+$ level ordering beyond $N$ = 28

## Key claim

The ground-state spin of $^{49}$K is firmly established as $I = 1/2$, and the ground-state spin/parity of $^{51}$K is deduced as $I^\pi = 3/2^+$ with a dominant $\pi 1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ hole configuration, demonstrating for the first time a "re-inversion" of the single-particle level ordering beyond $N = 28$ where the $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ levels invert back to normal ordering as neutrons fill the $\nu 2p_{3/2}$ orbit.

## Method

Bunched-beam high-resolution collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at ISOLDE-CERN on $^{49,51}$K$^+$ beams produced by 1.4 GeV protons impinging on a thick UC$_x$ target, surface ionized, accelerated to 40 kV, mass separated, and bunched by the ISCOOL gas-filled Paul trap. The 769.9 nm $4s\;{^2S_{1/2}} \rightarrow 4p\;{^2P_{1/2}}$ atomic transition in neutralized K atoms was scanned by applying a tunable voltage of $\pm 500$ V on the charge exchange cell, and fluorescence was detected by photomultiplier tubes gated to the $\sim 6$ $\mu$s bunch passage period. Hyperfine spectra were fitted using MINUIT to extract the hyperfine parameters $A(^{2}S_{1/2})$ and $A(^{2}P_{1/2})$, from which magnetic moments were deduced relative to the precisely known $^{39}$K reference.

## Key numbers

- $^{49}$K ground-state spin: $I = 1/2$ (firmly established from the three-transition hyperfine pattern)
- $^{51}$K magnetic moment: $\mu(^{51}\text{K}) = +0.5129(22)[15]\, \mu_N$ (statistical error in parentheses, hyperfine anomaly uncertainty in square brackets)
- $^{49}$K magnetic moment: $\mu(^{49}\text{K}) = +1.3386(8)[40]\, \mu_N$
- $^{51}$K $g$ factor: $g_{\text{exp}} = +0.3420(15)[10]$, consistent with the effective single-particle value for a $\pi 1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ hole ($g_{\text{eff}} = +0.25$)
- $^{49}$K $g$ factor: $g_{\text{exp}} = +2.6772(16)[80]$, suggesting a mixed wave function
- $^{51}$K beam intensity: $\sim 4000$ ions/s
- Background reduction factor from bunching: 15000 (ISCOOL accumulation time divided by ion bunch temporal width)

## Context

This work extends the study of single-particle level evolution in neutron-rich K isotopes beyond $N = 28$, following earlier measurements that established the $1/2^+$ ground state in $^{47}$K (Touchard 1982) and the inversion of $\pi 2s_{1/2}$ and $\pi 1d_{3/2}$ levels near $N = 28$ explained by Otsuka et al. (2005) via the monopole interaction. The results provide the first experimental evidence for a "re-inversion" of these levels as the $\nu 2p_{3/2}$ orbit is filled, enabling further constraints on the residual monopole interaction (central, spin-orbit, and tensor terms) in shell-model effective interactions such as SDPF-NR, SDPF-U, and the recently developed SDPF-MU interaction based on the universal monopole interaction $V_{\text{MU}}$ (Otsuka 2010). Spectroscopy of excited states in $^{51}$K is identified as the next step to improve effective interactions in this region.
