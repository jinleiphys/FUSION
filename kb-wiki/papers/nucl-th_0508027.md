# The nuclear moment of inertia and spin distribution of nuclear levels

> We introduce a simple model to calculate the nuclear moment of inertia at finite temperature. This moment of inertia describes the spin distribution of nuclear levels in the framework of the spin-cutoff model. Our model is based on a deformed single-particle Hamiltonian with pairing interaction and takes into account fluctuations in the pairing gap. We derive a formula for the moment of inertia at finite temperature that generalizes the Belyaev formula for zero temperature. We show that a number-parity projection explains the strong odd-even effects observed in shell model Monte Carlo studies of the nuclear moment of inertia in the iron region.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a simple model based on a deformed single-particle Hamiltonian with pairing interaction treated in the static path approximation that can reproduce the nuclear moment of inertia at finite temperature, including the strong odd-even effects observed in shell model Monte Carlo studies, but only under the assumption that the spin distribution follows the spin-cutoff model and at temperatures that are not too low where deviations from that model appear.

## Method

The authors use the static path approximation to the BCS Hamiltonian, which includes time-independent fluctuations of the pairing field, to calculate the grand-canonical partition function and moment of inertia for an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential with orbital-independent pairing. They derive a finite-temperature generalization of the Belyaev formula for the moment of inertia and apply number-parity projection to capture odd-even differences, then integrate over the pairing field magnitude to obtain the final moment of inertia. The model is applied to nuclei in the iron region using the pfg9/2 shell with single-particle energies and wave functions from a deformed Woods-Saxon potential.

* The pairing gap Δ versus temperature T is shown for ⁵⁶Fe with deformation parameter β₂ = 0.24, where the pairing strengths are determined from zero-temperature BCS to reproduce experimental gap values using the second difference formula.
* The number-parity projected BCS equations yield a strong suppression of the pairing gap for the odd projection compared to the even projection and the unprojected BCS solution.
* In the T → 0 limit for odd number-parity projection, the moment of inertia around the symmetry axis becomes ℐ^{η=-1}_{zz} → β |μ₀|², where |k₀⟩ = |q, μ₀⟩ is the state with lowest quasiparticle energy E_{k₀}.

## Context

This work extends the Belyaev formula (Belyaev, 1961) for zero-temperature moment of inertia to finite temperature and generalizes it with number-parity projection to explain odd-even effects seen in shell model Monte Carlo calculations of nuclei in the A ∼ 50–70 mass region. The model is intended for global calculations of spin distributions below the neutron separation energy, which are needed for theoretical estimates of nucleosynthesis reaction rates (Rauscher and Thielemann, 1997). The approach builds on prior number-parity projection methods (Goodman, 1981; Rossignoli et al., 1998; Baldo et al., 1999; Flocard and Onishi, 2001) and the static path approximation (Mühlschlegel et al., 1972; Alhassid et al., 1984; Lauritzen et al., 1988).
