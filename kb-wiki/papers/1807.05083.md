# Status of the Chiral Magnetic Effect Search in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Quark interactions with topological gluon fields in QCD can yield local $\mathcal{P}$ and $\mathcal{CP}$ violations which could explain the matter-antimatter asymmetry in our universe. Effects of $\mathcal{P}$ and $\mathcal{CP}$ violations can result in charge separation under a strong magnetic field, a phenomenon called the chiral magnetic effect (CME). Experimental measurements of the CME-induced charge separation in heavy-ion collisions are dominated by physics backgrounds. Major theoretical and experimental efforts have been devoted to eliminating or reducing those backgrounds. We review the current status of these efforts in the search for the CME in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The observed charge-dependent three-particle correlator (Δγ) in heavy-ion collisions, which has been interpreted as a possible signature of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), is dominated by physics backgrounds from resonance decays and local charge conservation coupled with elliptic flow, and the CME signal fraction is consistent with zero within current uncertainties.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of the three-particle γ correlator (γ = ⟨cos(φα+φβ-2ψRP)⟩) and its opposite-sign minus same-sign difference Δγ in Au+Au, Cu+Cu, Pb+Pb, p+Pb, p+Au, and d+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It examines background suppression techniques including event-by-event selection on elliptic flow (v2) and event shape engineering (ESE) using the q2 flow vector magnitude, where the Δγ correlator is studied as a function of v2 within narrow centrality bins to decouple magnetic field effects from flow-induced backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, the Δγ difference between opposite-sign and same-sign correlators decreases with increasing centrality, consistent with combinatorial dilution and decreasing magnetic field strength
- At √sNN = 7.7 GeV in Au+Au collisions, the difference between opposite-sign and same-sign γ correlators disappears, possibly indicating CME disappearance where hadronic interactions dominate
- In Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV from ALICE, the CME fraction extracted from ESE measurements combining 10-50% centrality gives fCME = 0.10 ± 0.13 (MC-Glauber), 0.08 ± 0.10 (MC-KLN CGC), and 0.08 ± 0.11 (EKRT), all consistent with zero
- The 95% confidence level upper limits on fCME for 10-50% centrality in Pb+Pb are 33% (MC-Glauber), 26% (MC-KLN CGC), and 29% (EKRT)

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on CME searches by Kharzeev (2004-2015) and the initial STAR measurements (Abelev 2009) that found qualitative consistency with CME expectations. It directly addresses the background contamination identified by Wang (2009), Pratt (2010), and Bzdak (2010), who showed that resonance decays and local charge conservation coupled with elliptic flow can account for most of the observed Δγ signal. The small-system control experiments from CMS (Khachatryan 2016) in p+Pb collisions demonstrated that similar charge-dependent correlations exist where CME should be absent, challenging the CME interpretation. The ESE results from ALICE (Acharya 2017) provide the most quantitative constraint to date, enabling future measurements with higher statistics and improved background subtraction techniques to search for a residual CME signal.
