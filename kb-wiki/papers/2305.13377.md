# How Macroscopic Limits on Neutron Star Baryon Loss Yield Microscopic Limits on Non-Standard-Model Baryon Decay

> We investigate how our baryon-loss limits from anomalous binary-pulsar period lengthening can be interpreted microscopically to yield specific constraints on the particle physics of baryon number violation within a neutron star. We focus on the possibility of anomalous baryon disappearance via dark baryon processes and on scenarios in which the produced dark-sector particles do not survive to influence the response of the star to baryon-number-violating effects. We flesh out the conditions for which this may occur, as well as other key assumptions. We then turn to the analysis of particle processes in the dense nuclear medium found at the core of a neutron star, employing the techniques of relativistic mean-field theory. Using our study of in-medium effects and limits on macroscopic baryon number violation we extract limits on in-vacuum baryon-number-violating processes, and we determine them for various equations of state. We conclude by noting the implications of our results for models of dark-sector-enabled baryogenesis.

## Key claim

The authors translate previously established macroscopic limits on baryon number violation (BNV) from binary-pulsar period lengthening into microscopic constraints on specific particle-physics models where baryons decay to dark-sector fermions, under the condition that the dark-sector particles do not survive to influence the star's response to BNV and that Standard Model dynamics control the star's evolution.

## Method

The authors employ relativistic mean-field theory (RMF) to describe the dense nuclear medium of a neutron star in beta-equilibrium, evaluating in-medium effects on baryon-to-dark-baryon decay processes such as n → χγ and Λ → χγ. They then use previously derived macroscopic limits on baryon loss from binary-pulsar timing observations to extract limits on the in-vacuum mixing parameters ε_{Bχ} for various equations of state, assuming that the dark baryon χ is efficiently removed from the star via annihilation or decay to other dark-sector particles.

## Key numbers

- Lower bound on dark baryon mass from ⁹Be stability: m_χ > 0.937993 GeV
- Upper bound on χ mass from successful baryogenesis in the dark-sector scenario: m_χ ≤ 1.07784 GeV
- Existing limit on exotic neutron decay branching ratio: Br(n → exotics) < 0.16% (95% one-sided CL)
- Neutron lifetime anomaly is roughly a 1% effect
- The most precise measurement of the A correlation coefficient yields |λ| = 1.27641(56)
- Rotation effects on neutron star structure are less than 3% for the fastest spinning pulsar considered (J1614−2230)
- Internal neutron star temperatures T ≲ 10¹¹ K are much less than Fermi energies E_F ≲ GeV within a minute after formation

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier macroscopic BNV limits from binary-pulsar observations (Berryman 2022) by developing a microscopic framework to constrain specific particle models, including those connected to the neutron lifetime anomaly (Fornal 2018) and dark-sector-enabled baryogenesis (Davoudiasl 2012, Alonso-Alvarez 2021). The analysis employs RMF techniques developed by Walecka (1974), Serot (1984, 1997), and Glendenning (1997) to describe dense matter, and it provides the first in-depth quantitative study of non-Standard-Model baryon decay processes within dense nuclear matter, enabling future constraints on models of co-genesis of dark matter and the cosmic baryon asymmetry.
