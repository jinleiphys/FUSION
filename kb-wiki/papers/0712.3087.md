# Real stabilization method for nuclear single particle resonances

> We develop the real stabilization method within the framework of the relativistic mean field (RMF) model. With the self-consistent nuclear potentials from the RMF model, the real stabilization method is used to study single-particle resonant states in spherical nuclei. As examples, the energies, widths and wave functions of low-lying neutron resonant states in $^{120}$Sn are obtained. These results are compared with those from the scattering phase shift method and the analytic continuation in the coupling constant approach and satisfactory agreements are found.

# Real stabilization method for nuclear single particle resonances

## Key claim

The real stabilization method (RSM), when combined with self-consistent relativistic mean field (RMF) potentials, can reliably extract single-particle resonance energies and widths for neutron states in spherical nuclei, with results that agree with the scattering phase shift method and the analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) approach. The method works best for narrow resonances and requires careful convergence checks on the box size, with widths assigned only when they converge to within 1% between adjacent box-size curves.

## Method

The Dirac equation for neutrons is solved in coordinate space under a spherical box boundary condition, using self-consistent vector and scalar potentials from the RMF model with the PK1 and NL3 parameter sets. The box size R_max is varied over a large range (7 fm to 60 fm, and up to 65 fm for convergence checks), and resonant states are identified by their stability against changes in R_max. The resonance energy E_gamma is determined from the condition d^2E/dR_max^2 = 0, and the width Gamma is extracted from the energy derivative at that point using a formula derived from the phase shift behavior under the box boundary condition.

## Key numbers

- Neutron i13/2 resonant state in 120Sn: E_gamma = 3.469 MeV, Gamma = 0.005 MeV (with PK1 parameter set); E_gamma = 3.266 MeV, Gamma = 0.004 MeV (with NL3 parameter set)
- Neutron f5/2 resonant state in 120Sn: E_gamma = 0.870 MeV, Gamma = 0.064 MeV (PK1); E_gamma = 0.674 MeV, Gamma = 0.030 MeV (NL3)
- Neutron i11/2 resonant state in 120Sn: E_gamma = 9.811 MeV, Gamma = 1.275 MeV (PK1); E_gamma = 9.559 MeV, Gamma = 1.205 MeV (NL3)
- Neutron j15/2 resonant state in 120Sn: E_gamma = 12.865 MeV, Gamma = 1.027 MeV (PK1); E_gamma = 12.564 MeV, Gamma = 0.973 MeV (NL3)
- For the i13/2 state, the width converges to within about 1% at R_max approximately 63 fm, with values ranging from 0.00327 MeV at R_max = 20.1 fm to 0.00483 MeV at R_max = 63.2 fm

## Context

This work extends the real stabilization method, originally developed by Hazi (1970) and Maier (1980) for atomic and molecular systems, into the relativistic mean field framework for nuclear physics. It provides a bound-state-like alternative to the complex scaling method (Kruppa, 1988) and the ACCC approach (Kukulin, 1977; Kukulin, 1989) that was previously applied within the RMF model by Yang (2001) and Zhang (2004). The method enables systematic study of single-particle resonances in exotic nuclei where the Fermi surface lies close to the particle continuum, and can handle both narrow and wide resonances by using a sufficiently large box size.
