# Initial temperature and EoS of quark matter from direct photons

> The time evolution of the quark gluon plasma created in gold-gold collisions of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) can be described by hydrodynamical models. Distribution of hadrons reflects the freeze-out state of the matter. To investigate the time evolution one needs to analyze penetrating probes, such as direct photon spectra. Distributions of low energy photons was published in 2010 by PHENIX. In this paper we analyze a 3+1 dimensional solution of relativistic hydrodynamics and calculate momentum distribution of direct photons. Using earlier fits of this model to hadronic spectra, we compare photon calculations to measurements and find that the initial temperature of the center of the fireball is at least 519+-12 MeV, while for the equation of state we get c_s= 0.36+-0.02.

## Key claim

The initial temperature of the fireball center in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is at least 519 ± 12 MeV, and the speed of sound in the quark-gluon plasma is \(c_s = 0.36 \pm 0.02\), but these values depend on the assumption that the initial time of the hydrodynamic evolution is at most 0.7 fm/\(c\) (a 95% confidence upper limit).

## Method

The authors used a 3+1 dimensional relativistic, ellipsoidally symmetric, exact solution of perfect fluid hydrodynamics (the Hubble-flow type model of Csorgo (2003)) to compute the invariant transverse momentum distribution and elliptic flow of thermal direct photons. They fixed the freeze-out parameters (central freeze-out temperature, proper-time, eccentricity, and expansion rates) from earlier fits of the same model to hadronic spectra (Csanad (2009)), then fitted the remaining free parameters, the equation-of-state parameter \(\kappa\) and the initial time \(t_i\), to the PHENIX direct photon data from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV Au+Au collisions (Adare (2008)). The photon source function was integrated using a second-order saddle-point approximation.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature lower bound: \(T_i > 519 \pm 12\) MeV, derived from the maximum acceptable initial time of 0.7 fm/\(c\) (95% confidence).
- Speed of sound in the quark-gluon plasma: \(c_s = 0.36 \pm 0.02\), corresponding to \(\kappa = 7.7 \pm 0.7\) (where \(\kappa = 1/c_s^2\)).
- Freeze-out proper-time (fixed from hadronic fits): \(\tau_0 = 7.7\) fm/\(c\).
- Central freeze-out temperature (fixed from hadronic fits): \(T_0 = 204\) MeV.
- Fit quality: \(\chi^2 = 7.0\) for 5 data points with 2 fitted parameters, giving a confidence level of 7.2%.

## Context

This work extends the exact hydrodynamic solution of Csorgo (2003), previously applied only to hadronic observables by Csanad (2009), to penetrating probes (direct photons) for the first time, enabling a simultaneous constraint on the initial temperature and equation of state. The extracted speed of sound agrees with lattice QCD calculations (Borsanyi (2010)) and with experimental results from hadronic data (Adare (2006); Lacey (2006)). The initial temperature lower bound is consistent with the 300–600 MeV range reported by other models (Adare (2008)). The approach is distinct from numerical hydrodynamic calculations because it uses analytic model results fitted directly to data.
