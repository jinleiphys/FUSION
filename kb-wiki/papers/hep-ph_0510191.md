# Multihadron production features in different reactions

> We consider multihadron production processes in different types of collisions in the framework of the picture based on dissipating energy of participants and their types. In particular, the similarities of such bulk observables like the charged particle mean multiplicity and the pseudorapidity density at midrapidity measured in nucleus-nucleus, (anti)proton-proton and electron-positron interactions are analysed. Within the description proposed a good agreement with the measurements in a wide range of nuclear collision energies from AGS to RHIC is obtained. The predictions up to the LHC energies are made and compared to experimental extrapolations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the charged particle mean multiplicity and midrapidity pseudorapidity density in different collision systems (electron-positron, proton-antiproton, and central nucleus-nucleus) can be unified by rescaling the center-of-mass energy according to the number of constituent quarks that deposit energy into the thermalized interaction volume, with the hedge that this scaling works only when the participant type (structureless electron, constituent quark, or nucleon) is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors compare published experimental data on charged particle mean multiplicity and pseudorapidity density at midrapidity from electron-positron, proton-antiproton, and central nucleus-nucleus collisions across a wide energy range (AGS to RHIC). They apply a rescaling where the center-of-mass energy for proton-antiproton collisions is divided by 3 (to account for one interacting constituent quark pair out of three) when comparing to electron-positron data, and multiplied by 3 for nucleus-nucleus collisions (to account for all three constituent quarks per nucleon depositing energy) when comparing to proton-antiproton data. They then incorporate a Landau hydrodynamical correction factor for the midrapidity density that depends on the participant mass (proton mass for nucleus-nucleus, one-third proton mass for proton-antiproton).

## Key numbers

* Mean multiplicity in electron-positron collisions at center-of-mass energy 130 GeV: 23.35 ± 0.20 ± 0.10
* Mean multiplicity in electron-positron collisions at center-of-mass energy 200 GeV: 27.62 ± 0.11 ± 0.16
* Predicted pseudorapidity density per participant pair at midrapidity for LHC nucleus-nucleus collisions: approximately 7.7
* Predicted pseudorapidity density per participant pair at midrapidity for LHC proton-antiproton collisions: approximately 6.1 (from CDF fit extrapolation)
* Predicted mean multiplicity per participant pair for LHC nucleus-nucleus collisions: about 10% above the proton-antiproton fit prediction and about 3.3 times larger than the RHIC nucleus-nucleus value at center-of-mass energy per nucleon 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the observation by PHOBOS (2004) that mean multiplicity and midrapidity density in nucleus-nucleus and electron-positron collisions show similarity when scaled by number of participants, but it resolves the discrepancy that proton-antiproton data give lower values. The authors build on the additive quark model and the Landau hydrodynamical approach to multiparticle production, and their predictions for LHC energies are compared to independent extrapolations by PHENIX (2004) and ATLAS Monte Carlo tunes. The description enables unified predictions for bulk observables across all collision systems and identifies two distinct energy regimes in nucleus-nucleus collisions (below and above SPS energies).
