# On the Neutron Transition Magnetic Moment

> We discuss the possibility of the transition magnetic moments (TMM) between the neutron n and mirror neutron n', its hypothetical sterile twin from parallel particle "mirror" sector. The neutron can be spontaneously converted into mirror neutron via these TMM's (in addition to the more conventional transition channel due to n-n' mass mixing) interacting with the magnetic field B as well as with mirror magnetic field B'. We derive analytic formula for the average probability of n-n' oscillation and consider possible manifestations of the neutron TMM effects. In particular, we discuss potential role of these effects in the neutron lifetime measurement experiments leading us to new, testable predictions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a transition magnetic moment (TMM) between the neutron and its hypothetical mirror twin can induce neutron-to-mirror-neutron conversion, in addition to the previously considered mass-mixing channel. The authors derive analytic formulas showing that the TMM contribution to the oscillation probability can dominate over mass mixing in certain magnetic field regimes, and that the two effects can be distinguished experimentally by comparing neutron losses in zero and non-zero magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors construct a 4×4 Hamiltonian describing the time evolution of the neutron–mirror-neutron system, including mass mixing, transition magnetic moments coupling to both ordinary and mirror magnetic fields, Fermi potentials from matter, and possible mass splitting. They derive analytic expressions for the average oscillation probability in the limit of many oscillations, separating the contributions from mass mixing and TMM, and analyze the dependence on applied magnetic field strength, mirror magnetic field strength, and the angle between them. They also consider the cases of zero and non-zero mirror magnetic field, and the possibility of resonant enhancement by tuning the applied magnetic field or introducing matter potentials.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of neutron–mirror-neutron oscillations originally proposed by Berezhiani (2005, 2008), which considered only mass mixing as the conversion mechanism. The introduction of a transition magnetic moment provides a new channel for n–n' conversion that can be distinguished experimentally by its opposite magnetic field dependence: mass mixing is suppressed in non-zero fields while TMM is enhanced. The results enable experimental searches using ultra-cold neutrons in traps, where comparing neutron loss rates at different applied magnetic fields can separate the two contributions, and are relevant to interpreting anomalies in existing neutron lifetime measurements (e.g., the 5.2σ deviation reported by Nesti). The formalism also applies to regeneration experiments like those discussed by Berezhiani (2017) and Broussard (2017).
