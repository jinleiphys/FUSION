# Glueball masses and Regge trajectories for the QCD-inspired potential

> Bound state of two massive constituent gluons is studied in the potential approach. Relativistic quasi-classical wave equation with the QCD-inspired scalar potential is solved by the quasi-classical method in the complex plane. Glueball masses are calculated with the help of the universal mass formula. The hadron Regge trajectories are given by the complex non-linear function in the whole region of the invariant variable $t$. The Chew-Frautschi plot of the leading glueball trajectory, $α_P(t)$, has the properties of the t-channel Pomeron, which is dual to the glueball states in the s channel. The imaginary part of the Pomeron is also calculated.

# Glueball masses and Regge trajectories for the QCD-inspired potential

## Key claim
Glueball masses and the leading Pomeron Regge trajectory can be derived from a relativistic quasi-classical wave equation with a QCD-inspired scalar potential that includes a running strong coupling, provided one uses a two-point Padé approximant to interpolate between the short-distance Coulombic and long-distance linear solutions; the resulting trajectory is non-linear and saturating, in agreement with HERA data, but the calculation assumes two massive constituent gluons and does not account for spin-dependent corrections.

## Method
The author solved the relativistic quasi-classical (QC) wave equation for two equal-mass constituent gluons bound by a funnel-type potential consisting of a Coulombic term with a running strong coupling α_s(r) and a linear confining term. Exact analytic solutions were obtained separately for the short-distance Coulombic part and the long-distance linear part using contour integration in the complex plane. These two asymptotic solutions were then joined via a two-point Padé approximant (K=3, N=2) to produce an interpolating mass formula. The squared total energy eigenvalues were shifted by a constant C² to obtain physical meson masses, and the resulting mass formula was inverted to yield the analytic dependence J(M_n²) for Regge trajectories, including the leading glueball (Pomeron) trajectory α_P(t).

## Key numbers
- String tension for the gluon-gluon system: σ_a = (9/4)σ^{fund}, where σ^{fund} ≈ 0.15 GeV².
- Strong coupling in the adjoint representation: α_a ≡ α^{adj} = 3α_s^{fund}.
- Dynamical gluon mass: μ_g = 500 ± 200 MeV, with QCD scale Λ = 300 MeV (Cornwall, 1982).
- Frozen strong coupling in the non-perturbative regime: α_0 ≡ α_s(0) = 1 / [2b₀ ln(2μ_g/Λ)].
- Classic soft Pomeron parameters: intercept α_P(0) = 1, slope α_P′(0) = 0.25 (GeV/c)⁻².
- Soft supercritical Pomeron intercept: α_P(0) ≈ 1.08.
- Quadratic Pomeron parametrization from UA8/ISR data: α_P(t) = 1.10 + 0.25t + α_P″(0)t² (Brandt et al.).
- Predicted excited glueball masses from Burakovsky et al. (phenomenological string model): M(2^{++}) = 2.38 ± 0.12 GeV, M(4^{++}) = 4.21 ± 0.21 GeV, M(6^{++}) = 5.41 ± 0.28 GeV.
- The interpolating mass formula yields squared energy eigenvalues: E_n(J)² = 8σ̃(2n_r + J + 3/2 − α̃₀), where α̃₀ = kα₀.
- The saturating Pomeron trajectory satisfies α_P(t → −∞) → −1.

## Context
This work extends the potential-model approach to glueballs previously applied by Sergeenko (2011) and others, using the Cornell-type potential with a constant strong coupling, by introducing a running strong coupling α_s(r) derived from Cornwall's (1982) Dyson-Schwinger solution for the gluon propagator. The saturating Regge trajectories obtained here contrast with the classic linear soft Pomeron (Donnachie and Landshoff, 1992) and the BFKL Pomeron, and are consistent with non-linear trajectories inferred from ZEUS and H1 data on exclusive ρ and φ photoproduction. The results enable a unified description of glueball masses and the Pomeron trajectory that can be tested against lattice QCD calculations (Lattice Glueball Collaboration) and future Jefferson Lab experiments, though the model neglects spin-dependent corrections and mixing with quark-based states.
