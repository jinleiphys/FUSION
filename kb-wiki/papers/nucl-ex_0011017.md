# Proton-deuteron elastic scattering at low energies

> We present measurements of differential cross sections and the analyzing powers A_y, iT11, T20, T21, and T22 at E_c.m.=431.3 keV. In addition, an excitation function of iT11(theta_c.m.=87.8 degrees) for 431.3 <= E_c.m. <= 2000 keV is presented. These data are compared to calculations employing realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, both with and without three-nucleon forces. Excellent agreement with the tensor analyzing powers and cross section is found, while the Ay and iT11 data are found to be underpredicted by the calculations.

# Proton-deuteron elastic scattering at low energies

## Key claim

The paper reports that at E_c.m. = 431.3 keV, the measured cross section and tensor analyzing powers (T20, T21, T22) agree well with calculations using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus the Urbana-IX three-nucleon force, but the vector analyzing powers A_y and iT11 are underpredicted by approximately 40% even when three-nucleon forces are included, confirming the persistence of the "A_y puzzle" at very low energies.

## Method

Differential cross sections and analyzing powers A_y, iT11, T20, T21, and T22 were measured for proton-deuteron elastic scattering at E_c.m. = 431.3 keV using polarized and unpolarized proton and deuteron beams from the TUNL atomic-beam polarized ion source, incident on thin hydrogenated or deuterated carbon foils. An excitation function of iT11 at theta_c.m. = 87.8 degrees was also measured for center-of-mass energies from 431.3 keV up to 2000 keV. The experimental results were compared to theoretical calculations using the Pair-Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic basis and the Kohn variational principle with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential alone and with the addition of the Urbana-IX three-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

* Differential cross section and analyzing powers measured at E_c.m. = 431.3 +/- 0.8 keV, the lowest energy at which a complete set of these observables has been obtained for nucleon-deuteron scattering.
* Absolute p-d cross sections determined at theta_lab = 25 degrees and 35 degrees relative to p-p scattering at a nominal proton beam energy of 2600 keV, with a systematic uncertainty of +/- 1.1%.
* For the N = 94 data points at 431.3 keV, the AV18 calculation yields chi-squared = 844, while the AV18+UR-IX calculation yields chi-squared = 268.
* The vector analyzing powers A_y and iT11 are underpredicted by the calculations by approximately 40%.
* The iT11 excitation function was measured for 431.3 <= E_c.m. <= 2000 keV at theta_c.m. = 87.8 degrees, with the AV18+UR-IX potential consistently underpredicting the data across the entire energy range below the deuteron breakup threshold.
* The AV18-only calculation differs from the cross section measurements by 3-4%, a small but statistically significant amount.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of p-d scattering at E_c.m. = 2 MeV by Knutson (1993) and Shimizu (1995), where the same underprediction of vector analyzing powers was observed, and provides the first complete set of observables below that energy. The data place strong constraints on proposed solutions to the "A_y puzzle," which has been studied by Witala and Gloeckle (1991), Tornow and Witala (1998), and Kievsky (1999). The results enable tests of new theoretical approaches, such as the chiral perturbation theory NN interaction of Epelbaum (2000), and provide input for determining p-d scattering lengths as discussed by Black (1999).
