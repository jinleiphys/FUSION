# Lambda-Sigma^0 mixing in finite nuclei

> Properties of hypernuclei are studied in the context of a chiral Lagrangian which successfully describes ordinary nuclei. Lambda-Sigma^0 mixing arises from nondiagonal vertices in flavor space induced by the vector mesons and by the electromagnetic field. The set of Dirac equations for the coupled hyperon system is discussed. Results are presented for energy spectra and electromagnetic properties of hyperons in the nuclear environment. Simple estimates suggest that flavor mixing can lead to sizable changes in the lifetimes of Lambda hypernuclei.

## Key claim

Lambda-Sigma^0 flavor mixing in hypernuclei is a real but small effect driven by the nuclear medium (specifically the rho meson mean field), not by vacuum mass differences; the paper shows that while the mixing shifts single-particle energies by less than 0.01 MeV and alters magnetic moments at the 2% level, it could dramatically shorten the lifetimes of excited Lambda hypernuclei by up to seven orders of magnitude due to the vastly different decay widths of the two hyperon species.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian including the baryon octet, vector meson nonet, and a light scalar singlet, with D-type couplings that generate nondiagonal self-energies in the Lambda-Sigma^0 flavor sector. They solve the coupled Dirac equations for the hyperon system in a mean-field approximation using a Pb-208 core nucleus, treating the hyperon as a valence particle and neglecting core response. The vector coupling constants are fixed via SU(3) symmetry from the nucleon-sector parameter set G1 of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997), while scalar couplings are fit to reproduce the lowest Lambda level in O-17 and the Sigma^0 potential in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Energy level shifts from flavor mixing are less than 0.01 MeV for the lowest Lambda-like states in a Pb-208 core, compared to typical hypernuclear spin-orbit splittings of less than 0.5 MeV.
- The Sigma^0 admixture in the lowest s_{1/2} Lambda-like state is 0.003% (N_Sigma / N_Lambda ratio).
- Deviations of hypernuclear magnetic moments from their single-particle values due to mixing are approximately 2% for realistic mixing strength (zeta=1).
- Two very narrow Sigma-like resonances appear at energies of approximately 1174.16 MeV and 1184.64 MeV in the Pb-208 core, with widths of about 1 keV.
- The estimated lifetime modification factor for excited Lambda-like states is Gamma_Lambda approximately 10^7 times Gamma^0_Lambda, based on the in-medium Sigma^0 width being roughly 10^12 times the Lambda width.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matter analysis of Mueller (1999) to finite nuclei, adding tensor couplings and electromagnetic couplings relevant for hypernuclei. It builds on the chiral effective Lagrangian approach of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997) that successfully describes ordinary nuclei, and generalizes it to strange systems. The flavor mixing mechanism is analogous to the MSW effect for neutrinos (Wolfenstein 1978, Mikheev and Smirnov 1985) but is a true many-body effect arising from the nuclear medium rather than vacuum mass differences. The results enable future experimental searches for mixing signatures in hypernuclear magnetic moments (proposed by Nakai 1996) and suggest that lifetime measurements of excited Lambda hypernuclei could reveal the effect, though the authors note that current experimental data on light hypernuclei (Bhang 1998) show weakly mass-dependent lifetimes that do not yet support their simple estimate.
