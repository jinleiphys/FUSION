# Is $J^{PC}=3^{-+}$ molecule possible?

> The confirmation of charged charmonium-like states indiates that heavy quark molecules should exist. We here discuss the possibility of a molecule state with $J^{PC}=3^{-+}$. In a one-boson-exchange model investigation for the S wave $C=+$ $D^*\bar{D}_2^*$ states, one finds that the strongest attraction is in the case $J=3$ and $I=0$ for both $Ï€$ and $Ïƒ$ exchanges. Numerical analysis indicates that this hadronic bound state might exist if a phenomenological cutoff parameter around 2.3 GeV (1.5 GeV) is reasonable with a dipole (monopole) type form factor in the one-pion-exchange model. The cutoff for binding solutions may be reduced to a smaller value once the $Ïƒ$ exchange contribution is included. If a state around the $D^*\bar{D}_2^*$ threshold ($\approx$4472 MeV) in the channel $J/ÏˆÏ‰$ (P wave) is observed, the heavy quark spin symmetry implies that it is not a $c\bar{c}$ meson and the $J^{PC}$ are likely to be $3^{-+}$.

# Wiki Page: Is $J^{PC}=3^{-+}$ molecule possible?

## Key claim

A hadronic molecule with exotic quantum numbers $J^{PC}=3^{-+}$ might exist as an S-wave $D^*\bar{D}_2^*$ bound state, but only if a phenomenological cutoff parameter around 2.3 GeV (dipole form factor) or 1.5 GeV (monopole form factor) is considered reasonable in the one-pion-exchange model; the conclusion is highly sensitive to this cutoff and to the poorly-constrained sigma meson coupling constants.

## Method

The authors employed a one-boson-exchange model to study S-wave $C=+$ $D^*\bar{D}_2^*$ systems, deriving potentials from scattering amplitudes using heavy quark effective theory Lagrangians for pion and sigma exchanges. They solved the SchrÃ¶dinger equation with dipole and monopole type form factors to regularize short-distance singularities, scanning over phenomenological cutoff parameters $\Lambda$ to find binding solutions with root-mean-square radius above 0.8 fm and cutoff below 4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- $D^*\bar{D}_2^*$ threshold is approximately 4472 MeV
- For the $X_3$ state ($I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(3^{-+})$) with OPEP and dipole form factor: binding energy 0.6 MeV at $\Lambda=2.3$ GeV ($r_{rms}=3.8$ fm); 19.8 MeV at $\Lambda=2.6$ GeV ($r_{rms}=0.8$ fm)
- For $X_3$ with OPEP and monopole form factor: binding energy 4.5 MeV at $\Lambda=1.5$ GeV ($r_{rms}=1.5$ fm)
- With OPEP+OsEP (dipole FF, Set 1: $g_\sigma=2.621$, $h_\sigma'=0$): $X_3$ binding possible for $\Lambda=1.7$ to 2.2 GeV
- With OPEP+OsEP (monopole FF, Set 3: $g_\sigma=2.621$, $h_\sigma'=2.621$): $X_3$ binding possible for $\Lambda\sim0.8$ GeV
- Pion coupling constant $g=0.59\pm0.07\pm0.01$ from $D^*\to D\pi$ decay
- $h_\chi=0.55$ GeV$^{-1}$ for the $D^*D_2^*\pi$ coupling
- $m_{D^*}=2008.63$ MeV, $m_{D_2}=2463.5$ MeV, $m_\pi=137.27$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of charged charmonium-like states such as $Z(4430)$ (near $D^*D_1$ threshold) and $Z_c(3900)$ (near $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold) to a higher-spin exotic quantum number $J^{PC}=3^{-+}$, which cannot be a conventional $q\bar{q}$ meson. The study follows the approach of Liu et al. (2008) for $D^*\bar{D}_1$ bound states and uses similar one-boson-exchange formalism as in nuclear force models (Bonn model, Nijmegen model). If a state near 4472 MeV is observed in $J/\psi\omega$ (P-wave), heavy quark spin symmetry would identify it as a molecule rather than a $c\bar{c}$ meson, enabling experimental tests of meson exchange models in the heavy quark sector.
