# Neutron Moderation Theory Taking into Accout the Thermal Motion of Moderating Medium Nuclei

> In this paper we present the analytical expression for the neutron scattering law for an isotropic source of neutrons, obtained within the framework of the gas model with the temperature of the moderating medium as a parameter. The obtained scattering law is based on the solution of the kinematic problem of elastic scattering of neutrons on nuclei in the L-system in the general case. I.e. both the neutron and the nucleus possess the arbitrary velocity vectors in the L-system. For the new scattering law the flux densities and neutron moderation spectra depending on the temperature are obtained for the reactor fissile medium. The expressions for the moderating neutrons spectra allow reinterpreting the physical nature of the underlying processes in the thermal region.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical neutron scattering law that includes the moderating medium temperature as a parameter, based on averaging over the thermal motion of moderator nuclei and isotropic neutron source conditions, rather than assuming stationary target nuclei as in traditional theory.

## Method

The kinematic problem of elastic neutron scattering on a nucleus in the laboratory system was solved for the general case where both neutron and nucleus have arbitrary velocity vectors. The authors introduced two laboratory coordinate systems (L-system and L'-system, where the nucleus is at rest in L') and used the center-of-mass system to derive the velocity relationships. The resulting expression for neutron energy after scattering was averaged over the spherically symmetric distributions of the thermal motion of moderator nuclei (Maxwellian distribution) and the isotropic neutron source, yielding a scattering law that depends on the moderator temperature T.

## Key numbers

- The neutron energy threshold for anisotropic scattering is given by E_B ~ 10 MeV / A^(2/3), where A is the mass number of the scattering nucleus
- For hydrogen (A=1), the entire fission spectrum range (0-10 MeV) is in the spherically symmetric scattering regime
- For carbon (A=12), E_B ~ 2 MeV, comparable to the average fission neutron energy (~2 MeV)
- The maximum relative energy loss in a single scattering is (1 - alpha), where alpha = ((A-1)/(A+1))^2
- For A > 50, (1 - alpha) ≈ 4/A, and for A > 200, (1 - alpha) < 2%
- The average kinetic energy of moderator nuclei from the Maxwell distribution is (3/2)kT

## Context

This work extends the traditional neutron moderation theory developed by Weinberg (1961), Akhiezer (2002), Galanin (1971), Feinberg (1978), and others, which neglected thermal motion of moderator nuclei and was only valid for neutron energies much higher than the thermal motion energy. The traditional approach artificially supplemented the Fermi spectrum with a Maxwell-type spectrum in the thermal region using an empirical formula T_n = T[1 + 1.8 * Sigma_a(kT) / (xi * Sigma_s)] from Weinberg (1961). The new scattering law enables a consistent theory of neutron moderation applicable to reactor emergency modes, traveling-wave reactors, pulsed reactors, boosters, subcritical assemblies, and natural nuclear reactors such as the georeactor.
