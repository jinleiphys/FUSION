# Energy loss of a fast moving parton in Gribov-Zwanziger plasma

> The Gribov-Zwanziger prescription applied within Yang-Mills theory is demonstrated to be an efficient method for refining the theory's infrared dynamics. We study the collisional energy loss experienced by a high-energetic test parton as it traverses through the Grivov plasma at finite temperature. To achieve this, we employ a semi-classical approach that considers the parton's energy loss while accounting for the back-reaction induced by the polarization effects due to its motion in the medium. The polarization tensor of the medium is estimated within a non-perturbative resummation considering the Gribov-Zwanziger approach. The modification of the gluon and ghost loops due to the presence of the Gribov parameter plays a vital role in our estimation. We observe that the non-perturbative interactions have a sizable effect on the parton energy loss. Further, we discuss the implications of our findings in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The Gribov-Zwanziger non-perturbative resummation significantly enhances the leading-order pQCD estimate of collisional energy loss for a fast parton in a quark-gluon plasma, with the effect being more pronounced for massive partons even at high momentum, contrary to the pQCD expectation that mass effects become negligible.

## Method

The authors employed a semi-classical approach using Wong equations coupled with linearized Yang-Mills equations to compute the collisional energy loss of a high-energy test parton, accounting for the back-reaction from polarization effects in the medium. The gluon self-energy was constructed from one-loop diagrams where the gluon and ghost loops were modified by the Gribov parameter γ_G, while the quark loop remained unchanged; the effective gluon propagator was then obtained via the Dyson-Schwinger equation. The temperature dependence of γ_G was fixed from the gap equation, and the resulting energy loss was used as input to Langevin dynamics to study momentum evolution and the nuclear suppression factor R_AA.

## Key numbers

- The Gribov parameter γ_G is given by γ_G = [(d-1)/d] * [N_c/(4√2 π)] * g² T, where d is the spacetime dimension, N_c = 3, and T is the temperature.
- For the massive parton case, the mass was chosen as M = 1.25 GeV (charm quark mass).
- The nuclear suppression factor R_AA was computed for charm quarks with a final evolution time τ_f = 5 fm/c.
- The ghost loop contribution to the gluon self-energy involves the numerical factor 7ζ(3)/(8π² T²) appearing in the expressions for I^c_μμ and I^c_00.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger approach, previously applied to improve the infrared behavior of Yang-Mills theory (Gribov 1977, Zwanziger 1989) and recently used to improve the pQCD estimate of the charm quark diffusion coefficient (Madni 2022), to the problem of parton energy loss in a hot QCD medium. The results show that non-perturbative effects are essential for consistency with lattice-based estimates, as pQCD calculations (e.g., for the diffusion coefficient) have shown notable discrepancies with lattice data (Dong 2019). The study enables future work on energy loss in an evolving Gribov plasma using second-order viscous hydrodynamics (Jaiswal 2020) and on the influence of chromodynamic field fluctuations during early-stage evolution.
