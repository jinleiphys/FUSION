# Study of some properties of deconfined matter: Resummed perturbation theory and beyond

> The present thesis is devoted to the study of some of the features of the deconfined state of matter dubbed Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). The different properties of the extreme matter that have been studied in this thesis are next-to-leading order dispersion properties for soft-moving quarks, screening masses of mesons, drag and diffusion properties of heavy quarks, the specific shear viscosity of QGP and energy loss of heavy quarks propagating in QGP background. The theoretical techniques which are utilized in order to study these properties are mainly hard thermal loop effective theory, which is required when one is interested in studying the soft scale physics of the underlined theory QCD at finite temperatures, and the other techniques, which are utilized here is the nonperturbative resummation approach dubbed as Gribov quantization. This nonperturbative resummation deals with the magnetic scale of the theory through the Gribov mass parameter.

## Key claim

This thesis claims that incorporating nonperturbative Gribov quantization, which addresses the magnetic scale of QCD, improves the description of several quark-gluon plasma properties beyond what standard hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory provides, particularly in the low-temperature regime where perturbative results deviate from lattice measurements.

## Method

The author computed next-to-leading order (NLO) quark self-energy and dispersion relations for soft-moving quarks using hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory in the real-time formalism, including both three-point and four-point vertex diagrams. For mesonic screening masses, the author employed Gribov quantization with a nonperturbative resummation approach, following analogies with non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) effective theory for both quenched and (2+1) flavor QCD. Heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients were calculated using Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) propagators for mediator gluons, and the resulting coefficients were used to estimate heavy quark energy loss and the specific shear viscosity of the QGP.

## Key numbers

- Thermal quark mass defined as \(m_q = \sqrt{C_F/8}\,gT\), where \(C_F\) is the Casimir factor and \(g\) the coupling strength
- The magnetic scale is addressed through a Gribov mass parameter of order \(g^{2}T\)
- For \(N_c=3\) and \(N_f=2\), the gluon mass parameter \(m_g^2 = 4\) (in appropriate units) is used in the HTL gluon propagator calculations
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was found to comply with the AdS/CFT bound over a larger temperature range compared to earlier perturbative expectations

## Context

This thesis extends the program of understanding quark-gluon plasma properties using HTL effective theory, as developed by Bellac (2011), Kapusta (2006), and Mustafa (2022), and incorporates the Gribov quantization approach to handle the nonperturbative magnetic scale that standard HTL resummation cannot address. The work on mesonic screening masses improves upon earlier perturbative results and shows agreement with recent lattice measurements, while the heavy quark dynamics results provide nonperturbative estimates relevant for interpreting data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN. The findings enable more accurate modeling of heavy quark energy loss and transport coefficients in the temperature regime accessible to current heavy-ion experiments.
