# Extraction of the pion distribution amplitude from polarized muon pair production

> We consider the production of muon pairs from the scattering of pions on longitudinally polarized protons. We calculate the cross section and the single spin asymmetry for this process, taking into account pion bound state effects. We work in the kinematic region where the photon has a large longitudinal momentum fraction, which allows us to treat the bound state problem perturbatively. Our predictions are directly proportional to the pion distribution amplitude. A measurement of the polarized Drell-Yan cross section thus allows the determination of the shape of the pion distribution amplitude.

## Key claim

The single spin asymmetry in polarized Drell-Yan production of muon pairs from pions on longitudinally polarized protons is directly proportional to the pion distribution amplitude, allowing its shape to be determined experimentally, provided one works in the kinematic region where the photon has a large longitudinal momentum fraction so that the bound state problem can be treated perturbatively.

## Method

The authors calculate the hadronic tensor for the Drell-Yan process π⁻ + p → γ* + X → μ⁺μ⁻ + X, including pion bound state effects via a convolution of the partonic amplitude with the pion distribution amplitude φ(z, Q̃²). The imaginary part arises from the on-shell internal quark line in the hard scattering diagrams, and the angular coefficients μ̄ and ν̄, which are nonzero only for polarized targets, are expressed in terms of integrals over φ and the polarized and unpolarized quark distribution functions of the proton.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 133 MeV
- Numerical results presented for s = 20² GeV², Q = 3 GeV, and Q_T = 0.9 GeV (giving ρ = Q_T/Q = 0.3) and Q_T = 0.06 GeV (giving ρ = 0.06)
- For the parton model at s = 20² GeV², Q = 3 GeV, ρ = 0.3: μ̄(s_ℓ=+1) ∼ 0.12 α_s ∼ 0.036
- For the parton model at s = 20² GeV², Q = 3 GeV, ρ = 0.06: μ̄(s_ℓ=+1) ∼ 0.034 α_s ∼ 0.01
- The relation ν̄ = 2ρ μ̄ holds in the bound state model; in the parton model ν̄ ∼ 3ρ μ̄

## Context

This work extends the bound state model for the pion previously applied to unpolarized Drell-Yan angular distributions by Brandenburg, Brodsky, Khoze, and Muller (1994), which successfully described data from the NA10 Collaboration and Conway et al. (1989) that the naive parton model (Chiappetta and Le Bellac, 1986) failed to reproduce. The calculation enables experimental determination of the pion distribution amplitude shape through measurements of the single spin asymmetry in polarized Drell-Yan production, complementing earlier perturbative QCD studies of this asymmetry by Pire and Ralston (1983) and Carlitz and Willey (1992).
