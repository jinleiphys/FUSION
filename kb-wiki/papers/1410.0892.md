# Isoscalar and isovector pairing in a formalism of quartets

> Isoscalar (T=0,J=1) and isovector (T=1,J=0) pairing correlations in the ground state of self-conjugate nuclei are treated in terms of alpha-like quartets built by two protons and two neutrons coupled to total isospin T=0 and total angular momentum J=0. Quartets are constructed dynamically via an iterative variational procedure and the ground state is represented as a product of such quartets. It is shown that the quartet formalism describes accurately the ground state energies of realistic isovector plus isoscalar pairing Hamiltonians in nuclei with valence particles outside the 16O, 40Ca and 100Sn cores. Within the quartet formalism we analyse the competition between isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations and find that for nuclei with the valence nucleons above the cores 40Ca and 100Sn the isovector correlations account for the largest fraction of the total pairing correlations. This is not the case for sd-shell nuclei for which isoscalar correlations prevail. Contrary to many mean-field studies, isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations mix significantly in the quartet approach.

# Isoscalar and isovector pairing in a formalism of quartets

## Key claim
The ground state of isovector plus isoscalar pairing Hamiltonians in self-conjugate nuclei can be accurately represented as a product of alpha-like quartets (two protons and two neutrons coupled to total isospin T=0 and total angular momentum J=0), with relative errors under 1% compared to exact diagonalization. However, the dominance of isovector versus isoscalar correlations depends strongly on the nuclear shell and on the spin-orbit interaction, with isoscalar correlations prevailing only in sd-shell nuclei and becoming dominant in pf-shell nuclei only when spin-orbit splittings are removed.

## Method
The authors constructed a quartet formalism where the ground state of an even-even N=Z nucleus is approximated as a product of generalized quartets, each built from a superposition of isovector quartets (two coupled T=1,J=0 pairs) and isoscalar quartets (two coupled T=0,J=1 pairs). The quartet amplitudes were determined through an iterative variational procedure: starting from one quartet (exact for 2 protons and 2 neutrons), additional quartets were added sequentially, with each quartet updated via diagonalization in a space of non-collective quartets while holding the others fixed, iterated until energy convergence. The method was tested against exact diagonalizations for nuclei with valence particles outside the 16O, 40Ca, and 100Sn cores, using pairing strengths extracted from the USDB, KB3G, and G-matrix interactions respectively.

## Context
This work extends the quartet formalism previously developed for isovector pairing only (Sambataro, 2013; Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2013) to include both isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions. It contrasts with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approaches (Goodman, 1990; Gezerlis, 2011) which violate particle number and isospin conservation and typically find that isovector and isoscalar correlations do not mix significantly. The results confirm earlier shell-model studies (Poves and Martinez-Pinedo, 1998; Pittel and Sandulescu, 2006) showing that isoscalar correlations are strongly suppressed by the spin-orbit interaction in pf-shell nuclei. The formalism enables systematic study of pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei while conserving all symmetries, and suggests that T=0, J=0 quartets play a role analogous to Cooper pairs in like-particle pairing systems.
