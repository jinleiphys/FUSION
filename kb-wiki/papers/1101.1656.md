# Landau parameters for asymmetric nuclear matter with a strong magnetic field

> The Landau Fermi Liquid parameters are calculated for charge neutral asymmetric nuclear matter in beta equilibrium at zero temperature in the presence of a very strong magnetic field with relativistic mean-field models. Due to the isospin structure of the system, with different populations of protons and neutrons and spin alignment to the field, we find non-vanishing Landau mixing parameters. The existence of quantized Landau levels for the charged sector has some impact on the Landau parameters with the presence of discretized features in those involving the proton sector. Using the Fermi liquid formalism singlet and triplet excited quasiparticle states are analyzed, and we find that in-medium effects and magnetic fields are competing, however, the former are more important in the interaction energy range considered. It is found that for magnetic field strengths Log$_{10}$ B (G) $\le 17$ the relative low polarization of the system produces mild changes in the generalized Landau parameters with respect to the unmagnetized case, while for larger strengths there is a resolution of the degeneracy of the interaction energies of quasiparticles in the system.

# Landau parameters for asymmetric nuclear matter with a strong magnetic field

## Key claim
For magnetic field strengths Log₁₀ B (G) ≤ 17, the relatively low polarization of the system produces only mild changes in the generalized Landau parameters compared to the unmagnetized case, while for larger strengths the degeneracy of the interaction energies of quasiparticles is resolved; however, in-medium effects are more important than magnetic field effects in the interaction energy range considered.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations for charge-neutral asymmetric nuclear matter in beta equilibrium at zero temperature under a uniform magnetic field along the z-axis, using the TM1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model. They calculated the Landau Fermi Liquid parameters as second derivatives of the energy density with respect to quasiparticle occupation numbers, accounting for quantized Landau levels for protons and continuous Fermi surfaces for neutrons, including anomalous magnetic moments for both nucleons. The dimensionless Landau parameters were obtained by multiplying the interaction matrix elements by the density of states at each Fermi level, with angular averages performed over the cylindrical Fermi surfaces for protons and ellipsoidal Fermi surfaces for neutrons.

## Key numbers
- Critical electron magnetic field: Bᵉ_c = 4.414 × 10¹³ G, used to define B* = B/Bᵉ_c
- Saturation density: n₀ = 0.145 fm⁻³
- Nucleon masses: m_n = m_p = 938 MeV
- Anomalous magnetic moments: κ_p = 1.79285 (proton), κ_n = -1.91315 (neutron)
- For densities n_B/n₀ = 0.1, 0.5, 2, and 4, the proton fraction shows rapid increase (decrease) of spin-up (spin-down) protons for Log₁₀ B(G) > 16
- At low density n_B = 0.0145 fm⁻³ and fields Log₁₀ B(G) > 16, complete alignment of the proton sector occurs with all protons in the n=0 Landau level
- For n_B/n₀ = 0.5, the Landau parameters F^{ss'}_{ij} are negative (attractive interaction); for n_B/n₀ = 2, they are positive (repulsive) for the magnetic field strengths considered
- Results for Log₁₀ B(G) > 18 should be treated with caution

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Landau parameters for non-magnetized symmetric nuclear matter or neutron matter by Caillon et al. and Matsui, and for magnetized matter in non-relativistic formalisms by Angeles et al. or in relativistic treatments without exchange terms by Nino. The paper enables future investigation of how strong magnetic fields affect the quasiparticle interaction structure in neutron star interiors, particularly regarding the competition between magnetic polarization and in-medium effects. The results are relevant for understanding the equation of state of compact stellar objects such as magnetars, which may have surface magnetic fields B ≈ 10¹⁵ G and interior fields up to 10¹⁸ G according to the scalar virial theorem.
