# Radial and orbital decomposition of charge radii of Ca nuclei:Comparative study of Skyrme and Fayans functionals

> We investigate the charge and point-proton radii of the Ca nuclei in detail in the density functional theory framework. As the Fayans energy density functional provides characteristic $N$-dependence, successfully describing the parabolic behavior of the differential charge radii in $20\leq N\leq 28$, we pose our particular focus on its physics origin, by decomposing them into the radial and orbital contributions. The results are compared with those from the Skyrme plus usual pairing functional, which is taken as a representative of the functionals having normal pairing channels. We point out that, because the enhancement of the differential charge radii in $N<20$ with the Fayans functional, which is contradictory with the data, has the origin parallel to the parabolic behavior in $20\leq N\leq 28$, it is significant to describe both $N$ regions simultaneously.

## Key claim

The Fayans energy density functional reproduces the parabolic behavior of differential charge radii in Ca isotopes for 20 ≤ N ≤ 28 through its specific pairing channel, but the same mechanism causes an incorrect enhancement of charge radii for N < 20, meaning that no existing energy density functional successfully describes the charge radii across the full 36–48 Ca region simultaneously.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the coordinate-basis code HFBrad, implementing both the Fayans FaNDF⁰ functional and several Skyrme functionals (SkM*, SLy4, UNEDF1, M3Y-P6). They decomposed the charge and point-proton radii into radial contributions from individual proton single-particle orbits (1s₁/₂, 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 1d₅/₂, 1d₃/₂, 2s₁/₂) by computing the differences in orbital root-mean-square radii relative to ⁴⁰Ca. The role of the rearrangement term from the pairing functional in the central potential was isolated by comparing calculations with and without this term.

## Key numbers

- Experimental differential charge radius δ⟨r²⟩₍ₕ⁴⁰,³⁸ = −0.080 fm²; FaNDF⁰ gives +0.095 fm² (overestimates), while SkM* and M3Y-P6 reproduce the experimental value well.
- At ⁴⁸Ca, the proton 1d₃/₂ orbit shrinks by 0.061 fm and the 2s₁/₂ orbit shrinks by 0.101 fm relative to ⁴⁰Ca, canceling the expansion of other orbits and yielding δ⟨r²⟩₍ₕ⁴⁰,⁴⁸ ≈ 0.
- The pairing strength for the Skyrme mixed-type pairing is Vₙ = −283.7 MeV fm³, adjusted to the neutron gap of 1.245 MeV in ¹²⁰Sn.
- The box size was set to Rₘₐₓ = 15 fm with lattice spacing Δr = 0.1 fm; pairing cutoff was 15 MeV for Fayans and 60 MeV for Skyrme functionals.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Reinhard (2017), who showed that the Fayans functional's pairing channel produces the parabolic charge radius behavior in ⁴⁰–⁴⁸Ca, by decomposing the effect into individual orbital contributions and comparing with Skyrme functionals. The results contradict the shell-model suggestion of Caurier (2001) that proton excitation across Z=20 causes the parabola, since the Fayans functional reproduces it without such excitations. The study enables future experimental discrimination between energy density functionals by identifying that the same pairing rearrangement mechanism responsible for the successful N=20–28 description also produces the incorrect anti-kink at N<20 noted by Nakada (2019), making simultaneous description of both regions a critical test for next-generation functionals.
