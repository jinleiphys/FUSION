# Multi-Physics Bayesian Analysis of Neutron Star Crust Using Relativistic Mean-Field Model

> We study the properties of neutron-star crust within a Bayesian framework based on a unified relativistic mean-field (RMF) description of dense matter. The analysis focuses on the posterior distributions of crust properties, constrained by nuclear experimental data, chiral effective field theory, and multimessenger neutron-star observations. In the inference, the outer crust is fixed using the AME2020 nuclear mass table, supplemented by Hartree--Fock--Bogoliubov mass models, while the inner crust is described using a compressible liquid-drop model consistently coupled to the RMF interaction. The same RMF framework is used to describe the uniform core, ensuring a unified treatment across all density regimes. From the resulting posteriors, we extract key crustal observables, including the crust--core transition density and pressure, crust thickness, crust mass, and the fractional crustal moment of inertia. We find that the transition density is primarily governed by the symmetry-energy slope $L$ and curvature $K_{\rm sym}$ evaluated at sub-saturation densities, while the transition pressure plays a central role in determining global crustal properties. The inner-crust equation of state reflects a collective interplay between isovector nuclear-matter properties rather than a dependence on any single parameter. We also assess the impact of using matched crust--core constructions and show that they can introduce systematic differences in predicted neutron-star properties when compared with fully unified treatments.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the crust-core transition density in neutron stars is primarily governed by the symmetry-energy slope \(L\) and curvature \(K_{\rm sym}\) evaluated at sub-saturation densities, while the transition pressure plays a central role in determining global crustal properties; however, the inner-crust equation of state reflects a collective interplay between isovector nuclear-matter properties rather than a dependence on any single parameter.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using a unified relativistic mean-field (RMF) description of dense matter, treating the parameters of the RMF Lagrangian as random variables constrained by nuclear experimental data, chiral effective field theory calculations, and multimessenger neutron-star observations including GW170817 tidal deformability and NICER mass-radius measurements. The outer crust was fixed using the AME2020 nuclear mass table supplemented by Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models, while the inner crust was described using a compressible liquid-drop model consistently coupled to the RMF interaction, with the same RMF framework used for the uniform core to ensure a unified treatment across all density regimes. For each posterior sample, the surface parameters of the compressible liquid-drop model were refitted to the AME2020 atomic mass evaluations, and approximately 40,000 unified equations of state were generated using the nested-sampling Monte Carlo framework MLFriends via the UltraNest package.

- Symmetry energy at saturation: median \(J = 33.361\) MeV, 68% CI 31.996--35.139 MeV, 95% CI 30.801--37.170 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy: median \(L = 67.041\) MeV, 68% CI 61.118--76.106 MeV, 95% CI 56.753--89.122 MeV
- Curvature of symmetry energy: median \(K_{\rm sym} = -43.44\) MeV, 68% CI \(-66.15\) to \(-14.99\) MeV, 95% CI \(-79.78\) to 17.94 MeV
- Maximum neutron-star mass: median \(M_{\rm max} = 2.102 M_\odot\), 95% CI 2.004--2.422 \(M_\odot\)
- Radius of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star: median \(R_{1.4} = 12.933\) km, 68% CI 12.760--13.123 km, 95% CI 12.610--13.347 km
- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star: median \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 603.85\), 95% CI 511.63--744.37
- Normalized moment of inertia for a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) star: median \(I_{1.4}/MR^2 = 0.3539\), 95% CI 0.3448--0.3591
- Moment of inertia for PSR J0737-3039A (1.338 \(M_\odot\)): median \(I_{1.338} = 1.545 \times 10^{45}\) g cm\(^2\), 95% CI 1.479--1.632 \(\times 10^{45}\) g cm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends Bayesian studies of neutron-star crusts by Carreau (2019), Dinh (2021), and Burrello (2025) by using a fully unified relativistic mean-field framework rather than stitching together different models for the outer crust, inner crust, and core, which the authors show can introduce systematic differences in predicted neutron-star properties. The results are compared with the recent meta-model analysis of Tsang (2024), showing overall consistency in nuclear-matter parameters and neutron-star observables, though the RMF-based analysis favors more negative values of the symmetry incompressibility \(K_{\rm sym}\). The study enables future direct measurements of the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A via relativistic spin-orbit coupling to serve as an independent probe of the neutron-star equation of state.
