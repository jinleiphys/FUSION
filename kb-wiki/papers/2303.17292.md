# Effective field theory with resonant P-wave interaction

> A new effective field theory has been developed to describe shallow $P$-wave resonances using nonlocal, momentum-dependent two-body potentials. This approach is expected to facilitate many-body calculations and has been demonstrated to converge and to be renormalizable in perturbative calculations at subleading orders. The theory has been applied to the neutron-alpha system, with good agreement found between its predictions and a phase-shift analysis of neutron-alpha elastic scattering. In the three-body system consisting of two neutrons and an alpha particle, the nonlocal potential in this framework has been found to recover the same qualitative features as previously shown with energy-dependent formulations.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new effective field theory for shallow P-wave resonances using nonlocal, momentum-dependent two-body potentials rather than energy-dependent ones, with the claim that this approach converges and is renormalizable in perturbative calculations at subleading orders. The key hedge is that the theory is demonstrated to work for the neutron-alpha system and the three-body neutron-neutron-alpha system, but the authors note that the special scaling of low-energy constants (where only one parameter needs fine-tuning) makes the resonance "easier" for the underlying theory to form, and they continue to count the other parameters as low-energy scales for the rest of the paper.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order P-wave potential using a nonlocal, separable form with an auxiliary field that does not propagate in time, then solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain the T-matrix. Subleading corrections are added systematically through derivative expansions of the form-factor function and expansions of the bare parameters, with renormalization performed by absorbing divergences into counterterms. The theory is applied to neutron-alpha elastic scattering by fitting low-energy constants to empirical phase shifts, and to the three-body neutron-neutron-alpha system using coupled-channel Faddeev equations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-alpha P3/2 resonance pole positions: k± = (±34.5 − i6.2) MeV, obtained from ERE parameters a1 = −62.951 fm³ and r1 = −0.8819 fm⁻¹ (Arndt 1973)
- Low-energy constants for neutron-alpha: λR⁻¹ = −87.0 MeV, γ = 99.4 MeV, η = 12.4 MeV, setting breakdown scale Mhi ≈ 90 MeV
- Three-body binding energy of ⁶He: 0.969 MeV (ground state)
- Neutron-neutron ¹S0 scattering length: a0 = −18.6 fm (used at LO)
- Cutoff used in phase shift calculations: Λ = 0.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal potential framework previously developed for S-wave interactions with large scattering length and effective range (Peng 2021, Beane 2021, vanKolck 2022) to describe shallow P-wave resonances, with immediate application to the neutron-alpha system. The approach avoids the complications of energy-dependent potentials used in earlier treatments of P-wave resonances (Bertulani 2002, Bedaque 2003, Epelbaum 2021) and dimeron field formulations (Long 2013), which require modified orthogonality and closure conditions for many-body calculations (Gobel 2019). The three-body application to the neutron-neutron-alpha system (Rotureau 2012, Ji 2014, Ryberg 2017) demonstrates that the nonlocal potential recovers the same qualitative features as energy-dependent formulations, and the observed cutoff dependence suggests that a three-body force is needed at leading order for renormalization, similar to the Thomas collapse phenomenon.
