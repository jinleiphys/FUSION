# $J/ψ$ suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> At high temperatures, strongly interacting matter becomes a plasma of deconfined quarks and gluons. In statistical QCD, deconfinement and the properties of the resulting quark-gluon plasma can be investigated by studying the in-medium behaviour of heavy quark bound states. In high energy nuclear interactions, quarkonia probe different aspects of the medium formed in the collision. So, we first reviewed the fate of quarkonia in the different stages of the (dynamical) system produced at the collision. We have then presented our present work on the dissociation of the heavy quarkonium states in a hot QCD medium by investigating the medium modifications to heavy quark potential. In contrast to the usual screening picture, interestingly our theory gives rise the screening of the charge, not the range of the potential.

## Key claim

The authors propose that medium modifications to the heavy quark potential lead to screening of the effective color charge (inversely proportional to the square of the Debye mass) rather than screening of the range of the potential, in contrast to the usual Debye-screened Yukawa form employed in most of the literature.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium heavy quark potential by taking the Fourier transform of the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear string term) and dividing by a dielectric permittivity derived from the static limit of the longitudinal gluon self-energy. They solve the Schrödinger equation using the resulting long-range Coulombic potential to obtain binding energies for charmonium and bottomonium states, then determine dissociation temperatures by imposing the condition that the binding energy equals the mean thermal energy of partons (3T). Three forms of the Debye mass are employed: leading-order perturbative (m_D^LO), full non-perturbative (m_D^NP), and a lattice-parametrized form (m_D^L), for pure gluonic, 2-flavor, and 3-flavor QCD media.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of J/ψ at zero temperature: approximately 640 MeV
- String tension used: σ = 0.184 GeV²
- Critical temperatures used: 270 MeV (pure gluonic), 203 MeV (2-flavor), 197 MeV (3-flavor QCD)
- Dissociation temperature for J/ψ with m_D^LO: 1.1 T_c (pure QCD), 1.3 T_c (N_f=2), 1.2 T_c (N_f=3)
- Dissociation temperature for J/ψ with m_D^L: 0.8 T_c (pure QCD), 0.9 T_c (N_f=2), 0.9 T_c (N_f=3)
- Dissociation temperature for Υ with m_D^LO: 1.4 T_c (pure QCD), 1.7 T_c (N_f=2), 1.6 T_c (N_f=3)
- Dissociation temperature for Υ with m_D^L: 1.0 T_c (pure QCD), 1.2 T_c (N_f=2), 1.2 T_c (N_f=3)
- Dissociation temperature for ψ' with m_D^LO: 0.8 T_c (pure QCD), 0.9 T_c (N_f=2), 0.9 T_c (N_f=3)
- Dissociation temperature for ψ' with m_D^L: 0.5 T_c (pure QCD), 0.7 T_c (N_f=2), 0.6 T_c (N_f=3)
- Non-perturbative coefficients for SU(3): c_N = 2.46 ± 0.15, b_N = -0.49 ± 0.15

## Context

This work reviews and extends the study of quarkonium dissociation in hot QCD media, building on the original proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that J/ψ suppression signals deconfinement. The authors contrast their approach with standard potential model calculations based on lattice free and internal energies (Wong 2007, Alberico 2007) and with lattice spectral function studies (Mocsy and Petreczky). The results for dissociation temperatures with the leading-order Debye mass are reasonably close to the findings of Mocsy and Petreczky but significantly smaller than predictions by Satz and Alberico (2007). The work enables systematic comparison of perturbative versus non-perturbative contributions to Debye screening and their effects on charmonium and bottomonium melting patterns across different numbers of light quark flavors.
