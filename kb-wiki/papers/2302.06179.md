# Bottom energy loss and non-prompt $J/ψ$ production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the momentum and centrality dependence of the non-prompt $J/ψ$ nuclear modification factors ($R_{AA}$), which comes from the $B$ hadrons decay, in Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Bottom quarks are produced in the parton hard scatterings and suffer energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma and the hadronic gas, where the spatial and time evolution of the medium is described with the hydrodynamic equations. Medium-induced elastic scatterings and the radiation in bottom quarks are included in the energy loss of bottom quarks. The hadronization process of bottom quarks is described with the instantaneous coalescence model. After considering both cold and hot nuclear matter effects in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, we calculated the $R_{AA}$ and also the elliptic flows of non-prompt $J/ψ$ from the decay of $B$ mesons at different centralities and transverse momentum bins. The $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ of non-prompt $J/ψ$ sensitive to the hot medium reflect and centrality supply an opportunity to study the bottom quarks energy loss in the hot medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) of non-prompt $J/\psi$ from $B$ hadron decay in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV can be described by a model combining Langevin dynamics for bottom quark energy loss (including both elastic collisions and medium-induced radiation) with hydrodynamic medium evolution and instantaneous coalescence hadronization, but the theoretical calculations slightly underestimate the experimental $v_2$ data at high transverse momentum, possibly due to neglecting momentum dependence in the spatial diffusion coefficient and event-by-event fluctuations.

## Method

The authors simulate bottom quark evolution in the quark-gluon plasma using a Langevin equation that includes both drag and stochastic terms (with spatial diffusion coefficient $\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T)=7$ in QGP) plus medium-induced gluon radiation via perturbative QCD. The hot medium expansion is modeled with 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with Bjorken longitudinal expansion, using a first-order phase transition at critical temperature $T_c=165$ MeV and initial central temperature $T_0=510$ MeV at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c. Bottom quarks hadronize into $B$ mesons via instantaneous coalescence with thermal light quarks at the hadronization hypersurface, and cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing) are included using the EPS09 parametrization with a global shadowing factor of $0.86-0.95$.

## Key numbers

- Spatial diffusion coefficient in QGP: $\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T)=7$; in hadronic medium: $\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T)=9$
- Bottom quark mass: $m_b=4.5$ GeV
- Critical temperature for deconfinement phase transition: $T_c=165$ MeV
- Initial central temperature at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c: $T_0=510$ MeV
- Global shadowing factor for bottom quarks: $0.86-0.95$
- Root-mean-square radius of $B$ meson: $\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle_B}=0.43$ fm
- Thermal mass of light quarks in coalescence: $m_2=0.3$ GeV
- Fitted parameters for non-prompt $J/\psi$ $p_T$ distribution in pp collisions: $n=3.2$, $\langle p_T^2\rangle_{pp}=19.5$ (GeV/c)$^2$
- Coalescence dominates $B$ meson production for $p_T\lesssim 5$ GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma (Cao 2013, He 2012) by specifically focusing on non-prompt $J/\psi$ from $B$ hadron decay as a probe of bottom quark dynamics, complementing measurements of $D$ meson $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ from ALICE (2013, 2020) and CMS (2020). The calculations use transport coefficients determined in prior work (Chen 2017) and the EPS09 shadowing parametrization (Eskola 2009), and the results enable future extraction of momentum-dependent spatial diffusion coefficients for bottom quarks by comparing with CMS (2017, 2022) and ATLAS (2018) data on non-prompt $J/\psi$ at LHC energies.
