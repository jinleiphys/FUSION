# Spin polarizations under a pseudovector interaction between quarks with the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft term in high density quark matter

> A possibility of a quark spin polarization originated from a pseudovector condensate is investigated in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction which leads to flavor mixing. It is shown that a pseudovector condensate related to the strange quark easily occurs compared with pseudovector condensate related to light quarks. Further, it is shown that the pseudovector condensate related to the strange quark appears at a slightly small chemical potential by the effect of the flavor mixing due to the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a pseudovector condensate (quark spin polarization) related to the strange quark appears more readily than one for light quarks, and that the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft flavor-mixing interaction lowers the chemical potential threshold for the strange-quark pseudovector condensate compared to the case without flavor mixing.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector-pseudovector-type four-point interactions and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft six-point determinant interaction, treated in the mean-field approximation. They derive the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature and finite quark chemical potential, then solve the coupled gap equations self-consistently for the chiral condensates (dynamical quark masses) and the pseudovector condensates (spin polarizations) for light quarks (u,d) and the strange quark, with the pseudovector coupling strength \(G_P\) treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

* The three-momentum cutoff is \(\Lambda = 0.6314\) GeV, with scalar coupling \(G_S = 3.666/\Lambda^2\) and determinant coupling \(G_D = -9.288/\Lambda^5\) for the standard parameter set.
* The dynamical quark masses in vacuum are \(M_q = 0.335\) GeV for light quarks and \(M_s = 0.527\) GeV for the strange quark.
* For model GP2 (\(G_P = 2G_S\)), the strange-quark pseudovector condensate appears at \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_s1 \approx 0.486\) GeV with the determinant interaction, compared to \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_s1 \approx 0.541\) GeV without it.
* For model GP4.1 (\(G_P = 4.1G_S\)), the light-quark pseudovector condensate appears in the narrow range \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_q1 \approx 0.342\) GeV to \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_q2 \approx 0.343\) GeV, while the strange-quark condensate appears at \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_s1 \approx 0.441\) GeV with the determinant interaction versus \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_s1 \approx 0.482\) GeV without it.
* For model GP5 (\(G_P = 5G_S\)), the light-quark pseudovector condensate appears from \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_q1 \approx 0.333\) GeV to \(\mu_{\rm cr}U_q2 \approx 0.358\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-flavor NJL study (Morimoto, 2019) where pseudovector condensates appeared only in a narrow region before chiral restoration, to the three-flavor case including the strange quark. It incorporates the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant interaction (Kobayashi and Maskawa, 1970; 't Hooft, 1976) which introduces flavor mixing, and shows that this interaction lowers the chemical potential threshold for the strange-quark spin polarization. The results suggest that spin-polarized phases in dense quark matter could contribute to the strong magnetic fields observed in compact stars such as neutron stars and magnetars, as previously explored in tensor-interaction studies (Tsue et al., 2015, 2016) and pseudovector-interaction studies (Morimoto, 2019).
