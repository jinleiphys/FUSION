# Fluid dynamics of out of equilibrium boost invariant plasmas

> By solving a simple kinetic equation, in the relaxation time approximation, and for a particular set of moments of the distribution function, we establish a set of equations which, on the one hand, capture exactly the dynamics of the kinetic equation, and, on the other hand, coincide with the hierarchy of equations of viscous hydrodynamics, to arbitrary order in the viscous corrections. This correspondence sheds light on the underlying mechanism responsible for the apparent success of hydrodynamics in regimes that are far from local equilibrium.

## Key claim

By solving a kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation for a specific set of moments of the distribution function, the authors establish that the resulting hierarchy of equations coincides exactly with viscous hydrodynamics to arbitrary order in viscous corrections, explaining why hydrodynamics succeeds even far from local equilibrium. The key qualifier is that this correspondence relies on the specific choice of moments (the ${\cal L}_n$ moments) and the Bjorken flow geometry.

## Method

The authors study a massless particle system undergoing Bjorken flow, described by a kinetic equation with a relaxation time approximation for the collision kernel. Instead of solving the full distribution function, they focus on a particular set of moments ${\cal L}_n$ defined by integrating the distribution weighted by $|{\bf p}|^2 P_{2n}(p_z/|{\bf p}|)$, where $P_{2n}$ are Legendre polynomials. Using recursion relations among Legendre polynomials, they convert the kinetic equation into an infinite hierarchy of coupled ordinary differential equations for these moments, which can be truncated at any order. They then analyze the fixed points of this hierarchy: a free-streaming fixed point where all moments decay as $1/\tau$, and a hydrodynamic fixed point corresponding to the gradient expansion in powers of $\tau_R/\tau$.

## Key numbers

- The free-streaming fixed point gives $g_n(\tau) = -1$ for all $n$, where $g_n = \tau \partial_\tau \ln {\cal L}_n$, corresponding to ${\cal L}_n(\tau) \propto 1/\tau$.
- The hydrodynamic fixed point gives $g_n(\tau) = -(4+2n)/3$ for conformal invariant setting with $\tau_R T(\tau)$ constant.
- The leading order coefficient for the first moment is $\alpha_1^{(0)} = -b_1 \tau_R \varepsilon = -2\eta$, where $\eta$ is the shear viscosity.
- The coefficient $\alpha_2^{(0)} = \frac{64}{105} \varepsilon \tau_R^2 = \frac{4}{3}(\lambda_1 + \eta \tau_\pi)$, with $\lambda_1$ and $\tau_\pi$ as defined in Baier (2007).
- The coefficient $\alpha_1^{(1)} = -\frac{32}{315} \varepsilon \tau_R^2 = \frac{4}{3}(\lambda_1 - \eta \tau_\pi)$.
- The truncation at $n<20$ gives the free-streaming fixed point at $g_n = -1.00294$ (slightly different from the exact $-1$ due to truncation).
- The first order viscous hydrodynamics gives $\Pi \simeq 4\eta/(3\tau) = (16/45) \varepsilon (\tau_R/\tau)$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Blaizot and Yan (2017) on ${\cal L}$-moments and provides an alternative perspective to the attractor approach of Heller and Spalinski (2015) for understanding hydrodynamization in Bjorken flow. The results contradict the traditional view that hydrodynamics requires local equilibrium, instead showing that viscous hydrodynamic equations can exactly capture kinetic theory dynamics through the moment hierarchy. The paper enables a systematic way to compute higher-order viscous corrections (e.g., third order as in Jaiswal (2013)) and introduces a renormalization of $\eta/s$ that reproduces the effect first pointed out by Lublinsky and Shuryak (2007), where out-of-equilibrium systems effectively have reduced viscosity.
