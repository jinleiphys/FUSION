# Universal features of the order-parameter fluctuations : reversible and irreversible aggregation

> We discuss the universal scaling laws of order parameter fluctuations in any system in which the second-order critical behaviour can be identified. These scaling laws can be derived rigorously for equilibrium systems when combined with the finite-size scaling analysis. The relation between order parameter, criticality and scaling law of fluctuations has been established and the connexion between the scaling function and the critical exponents has been found. We give examples in out-of-equilibrium aggregation models such as the Smoluchowski kinetic equations, or of at-equilibrium Ising and percolation models.

# Universal features of the order-parameter fluctuations: reversible and irreversible aggregation

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal scaling laws for order-parameter fluctuations can be derived for any system exhibiting second-order critical behavior, including out-of-equilibrium aggregation models, but this holds only when the system is at the critical or pseudo-critical point and the observable is the order parameter or a shifted version thereof; the scaling form depends on whether the system is exactly at criticality, near it, or far from it.

## Method

The authors derive scaling relations for order-parameter fluctuations using finite-size scaling analysis combined with Widom's hypothesis for equilibrium systems, then extend the formalism to non-equilibrium systems. They introduce the concept of Δ-scaling, which generalizes the first-scaling law (Δ=1) and second-scaling law (Δ=1/2) into a continuous family of scaling forms, and analyze the tail behavior of the scaling function. The theoretical framework is applied to several models: the Landau-Ginzburg theory as an exactly solvable example, the non-critical fragmentation model, the Potts model, reversible aggregation modeled by percolation (both 3-dimensional bond percolation and mean-field percolation on the Bethe lattice), and irreversible aggregation described by the Smoluchowski kinetic equations.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous dimension g for equilibrium systems at a second-order phase transition lies between 1/2 and 1.
- The exponent ν̂ governing the tail of the scaling function (Φ(z) ∼ exp(-a z^{ν̂})) is always larger than 2 for critical systems, with ν̂ = 1/(1-g) = δ+1 = (2-α)/β.
- For the Landau-Ginzburg model at the critical point, the average order parameter scales as <m> ∼ N^{3/4}.
- For the Landau-Ginzburg model at the pseudo-critical point, the deviation from the critical point scales as ε = -6(Γ[3/4]/Γ[1/4])(b/β_T N)^{1/2}.
- For the Landau-Ginzburg model far from criticality in the disordered phase, <m> ∼ N^{1/2}.
- The exponent τ of the power-law cluster-size distribution at a second-order equilibrium phase transition is constrained to 2 < τ < 3.
- The size of the largest cluster scales as <s_max> ∼ N^{1/(τ-1)}.

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling analysis of equilibrium phase transitions, building on Widom's hypothesis (Widom, 1965) and the finite-size scaling framework of Fisher and Barber (1972), to encompass both equilibrium and non-equilibrium systems. The first-scaling law is shown to be equivalent to the Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling proposed in relativistic field theory (Koba, Nielsen, and Olesen, 1972). The paper provides a unified framework for analyzing fluctuations in diverse systems including hadronization in strong interaction physics, polymerization, colloid aggregation, aerosol coalescence, and formation of large-scale structures in the Universe. The results enable experimental identification of critical behavior in finite systems through the measurement of fluctuation scaling exponents, without requiring knowledge of the detailed microscopic dynamics.
