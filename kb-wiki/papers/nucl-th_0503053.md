# Electron scattering on halo nuclei

> The inelastic scattering of electrons on weakly-bound nuclei is studied with a simple model based on the long range behavior of the bound state wavefunction and on the effective-range expansion for the continuum wavefunctions. Three mechanisms have been considered: (a) dissociation of halo nuclei by high energy electrons, (b) dissociation by electrons present in a fixed target, and (c) Coulomb dissociation. It is shown that the properties of halo nuclei can be studied in electron-radioactive beam colliders using the electro-disintegration process. A comparison with fixed-target experiments is also performed.

# Electron scattering on halo nuclei

## Key claim

The electro-disintegration cross section for halo nuclei in an electron-radioactive beam collider reaches only about 1 mb for a separation energy of 1 MeV, meaning such experiments are feasible only if the radioactive beam intensity is very large or if the separation energy is extremely small (below about 50 keV), contrary to the abstract's implication that such studies are straightforwardly enabled.

## Method

The author uses a simple single-particle model for a one-neutron halo nucleus, representing the bound state by a Hankel function determined by the neutron separation energy and the continuum by spherical Bessel functions with phase shifts parameterized via effective-range expansion. The electron scattering cross section is computed in first Born approximation for longitudinal (Coulomb) excitations at small momentum transfers, with analytic form factors derived for s-wave ground states and final-state angular momenta l=0,1,2. For fixed-target dissociation by atomic electrons, the Thomas-Fermi model describes the target electron momentum distribution, Lorentz-boosted into the projectile frame.

## Key numbers

- Electro-disintegration cross section for a halo nucleus with S_n = 100 keV and E_e = 10 MeV is approximately 25 mb (from the analytic formula, eq. (sigef)).
- For S_n = 1 MeV and E_e = 0.5 GeV (collider mode at GSI), the cross section is of order 1 mb, yielding an estimated 100 events/second at a luminosity of 10^29 /cm^2.s.
- For S_n less than about 50 keV, the electro-disintegration cross section becomes larger than 1 mb at electron bombarding energies of 0.1–0.5 MeV (Figure 1).
- Coulomb correction factor Q for electron scattering on 11Li at K_e = 100 MeV is 0.949; for 19C at the same energy it is 0.903 (Table I).
- For a halo nucleus with S_n = 100 keV incident on a Pb target at 10 GeV/nucleon, the dissociation cross section by atomic electrons is of order 1 barn (Figure 2).
- Coulomb dissociation cross section for the same system (10 GeV/nucleon on Pb) is approximately 24 barns, about 5% of which comes from electron dissociation in the target.

## Context

This work extends the single-particle model previously used for Coulomb excitation of halo nuclei by Bertsch and Bertulani (1986) and by Bertulani and Sustich (1992) to the case of electron-induced dissociation. It provides analytic estimates for cross sections relevant to the planned electron-radioactive beam collider at GSI/Darmstadt, where 0.5 GeV electrons will cross 740 MeV/A radioactive beams. The results show that electron dissociation in fixed targets (via atomic electrons) is a non-negligible background to Coulomb dissociation experiments, comprising about 5% of the total breakup cross section on heavy targets. The paper also notes implications for stellar environments where free electrons can dissociate weakly-bound nuclei during the r-process.
