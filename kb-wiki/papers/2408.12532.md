# Spectral eigenfunction decomposition of a Fokker-Planck operator for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A spectral solution method is proposed to solve a previuously developed non-equilibrium statistical model describing partial thermalization of produced charged hadrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, thus improving the accuracy of the numerical solution. The particle's phase-space trajectories are treated as drift-diffusion stochastic process, leading to a Fokker-Planck equation (FPE) for the single-particle probability distribution function. The drift and diffusion coefficients are derived from the expected asymptotic states via appropriate fluctuation-dissipation relations, and the resulting FPE is then solved numerically using a spectral eigenfunction decomposition. The calculated time-dependent particle distributions are compared to Pb-Pb data from the ATLAS and ALICE collaborations at the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a spectral eigenfunction decomposition method to solve the Fokker-Planck equation for charged-hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which improves numerical accuracy at larger transverse momenta compared to previous finite-difference methods, but the authors note that the theoretical justification may be limited for light hadrons like pions and kaons when their in-medium widths become comparable to or larger than their masses.

## Method

The authors model particle trajectories as a drift-diffusion stochastic process in phase space, leading to a Fokker-Planck equation (FPE) for the single-particle probability distribution function in transverse and longitudinal rapidity coordinates. The drift and diffusion coefficients are derived from the expected asymptotic states via fluctuation-dissipation relations, and the FPE is solved numerically using a spectral eigenfunction decomposition with non-classical orthogonal polynomials as basis functions. The calculated time-dependent particle distributions are compared to Pb-Pb collision data from the ATLAS and ALICE collaborations at the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies considered: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC
- Gluon saturation scale parametrization uses lambda = 0.2 and (4/9)Q_0^2 x_0^lambda = 0.09 GeV^2, consistent with prior work by Hoelck (2020) and Rizzi and Wolschin (2024)
- The power-law exponent n = -8 was adopted in prior work for p_T <= 2 GeV following analysis of ALICE p-p data by Aamodt (2011), but the authors note this overestimates particle yield at high p_T
- The asymptotic distribution uses a generalized Fokker-Planck solution by Bonasera (2020) with parameters c, d, and a temperature-like quantity hat-T

## Context

This work extends the relativistic diffusion model (RDM) previously developed by Wolschin (1999), Biyajima (2002), and Wolschin (2016), which was placed on a firm nonequilibrium-statistical foundation by Rizzi and Wolschin (2024). The spectral method improves upon the finite-difference numerical approach used in that prior work, which prevented comparison to experimental data at large transverse momenta where particle yield is small but measured with high accuracy. The model sits between equilibrium-statistical models proposed by Fermi (1950) and Hagedorn (1968) and more detailed numerical nonequilibrium models using Color Glass Condensate initial states and viscous relativistic hydrodynamics.
