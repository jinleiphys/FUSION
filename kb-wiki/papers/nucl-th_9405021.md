# Boson-fermion Dyson mapping and supersymmetry in fermion systems

> We demonstrate that exact supersymmetry can emerge in a purely fermionic system. This "supersymmetry without bosons" is unveiled by constructing a novel boson-fermion Dyson mapping from a fermion space to a space comprised of collective bosons and ideal fermions. In a nuclear structure context the collective spectra of even and odd nuclei can then be unified in a supersymmetric description with Pauli correlations still exactly taken into account.

## Key claim

Exact supersymmetry can emerge in a purely fermionic system, contrary to the usual expectation that supersymmetry requires mixing bosonic and fermionic degrees of freedom. The claim is hedged by the condition that the fermion system must have a collective spectrum generating algebra that allows construction of a specific boson-fermion Dyson mapping, and the supersymmetry is demonstrated for a particular nuclear model (SO(8)⊗SO(5)).

## Method

The authors construct a novel boson-fermion Dyson mapping from a fermion space to a space of collective bosons and ideal fermions, using a similarity transformation that eliminates redundant ideal-fermion-pair components while preserving all commutation and anticommutation relations. They apply this mapping to the SO(8) Ginocchio model extended by an SO(5) algebra of multipole operators, and show that the mapped boson-fermion Hamiltonian exhibits the same group chain and energy formula as the phenomenological Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) with U(6/20) supersymmetry, but with Pauli correlations exactly taken into account.

## Key numbers

- For the SO(8)⊗SO(5) supersymmetric energy formula, Hamiltonian parameters B=35 keV, B̃=12.63 keV, and C=18 keV are chosen so that the even part coincides with the spectrum of Ling et al. (1984).
- The SO(8) model includes 2Ω = (2i+1)(2k+1) creation and annihilation operators, with active angular momentum i=3/2 and inactive angular momentum k=0 (giving j=3/2) or k=2 (giving j=1/2, 3/2, 5/2, 7/2).
- For k=2, the fermion sector has 20 states, leading to an overall IBFM U(6/20) supersymmetry realized in the Au-Pt isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) supersymmetry approach of Iachello (1980) and Iachello and van Isacker (1991) by providing a microscopic derivation that fully incorporates Pauli correlations, which the IBFM does not. It builds on prior boson-fermion mapping techniques by Dobaczewski, Scholtz, and Geyer (1993) and the SO(8) model of Ginocchio (1980), and enables a unified supersymmetric description of even and odd nuclei starting from a purely fermionic microscopic collective model. The result parallels recent demonstrations of supersymmetry in purely bosonic models by Brzezinski et al. (1993) and Plyushchay (1994).
