# Description of the Giant Monopole Resonance in the Even-A $^{112-124}$Sn Isotopes within the Microscopic Model Including Quasiparticle-Phonon Coupling

> We have calculated the strength distributions of the giant monopole resonance in the even-A tin isotopes (A = 112-124) which were recently measured in inelastic $α$-scattering. The calculations were performed within two microscopic models: the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and the quasiparticle time blocking approximation which is an extension of the QRPA including quasiparticle-phonon coupling. We used a self-consistent calculational scheme based on the HF+BCS approximation. The single-particle continuum was exactly included on the RPA level. The self-consistent mean field and the effective interaction were derived from the Skyrme energy functional. In the calculations, two Skyrme force parametrizations were used. The T5 parametrization with comparatively low value of the incompressibility of infinite nuclear matter ($K_{\infty}$ = 202 MeV) gives theoretical results in good agreement with the experimental data including the resonance widths.

## Key claim

The T5 Skyrme force parametrization, with a comparatively low nuclear matter incompressibility of K∞ = 202 MeV, gives theoretical results in good agreement with experimental data for the giant monopole resonance in tin isotopes, including the resonance widths, when quasiparticle-phonon coupling is included via the quasiparticle time blocking approximation.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent HF+BCS calculations using Skyrme energy functionals (T5 and T6 parametrizations) to compute ground state properties, then calculated isoscalar giant monopole resonance strength distributions using both the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA), which extends QRPA by including quasiparticle-phonon coupling. The single-particle continuum was exactly included at the RPA level, and the effective residual interaction was derived from the Skyrme energy functional. The phonon space included collective modes with spins L = 2-9 and natural parity, with phonon characteristics adjusted to reproduce experimental energies of the 2+1 and 3-1 levels.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility K∞ = 202 MeV for the T5 Skyrme force, which gave good agreement with experiment
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K∞ = 236 MeV for the T6 Skyrme force, which overestimated experimental centroid energies by more than 1 MeV
- For 112Sn: QTBA centroid energy m1/m0 = 16.0 MeV (experiment: 16.2 ± 0.1 MeV), width Γ = 3.9 MeV (experiment: 4.0 ± 0.4 MeV)
- For 120Sn: QTBA centroid energy m1/m0 = 15.5 MeV (experiment: 15.7 ± 0.1 MeV), width Γ = 4.3 MeV (experiment: 4.9 ± 0.5 MeV)
- For 124Sn: QTBA centroid energy m1/m0 = 15.3 MeV (experiment: 15.3 ± 0.1 MeV), width Γ = 4.4 MeV (experiment: 4.5 ± 0.5 MeV)
- QRPA widths ranged from 1.9 MeV (112Sn) to 2.6 MeV (124Sn), strongly underestimating experimental values
- QTBA widths ranged from 3.9 MeV (112Sn) to 4.4 MeV (124Sn), in good agreement with experiment
- Smearing parameter Δ = 500 keV, approximately matching experimental resolution
- Pairing force strength V0 = -210 MeV·fm3

## Context

This work extends self-consistent RPA calculations of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance to open-shell nuclei by including quasiparticle-phonon coupling through the QTBA, which was previously developed by the same group (QTBA1, QTBA2). The results contradict recent findings from Colo et al. (2004) and Shlomo et al. (2006) that favor K∞ around 230-240 MeV based primarily on 208Pb data, suggesting instead that doubly magic nuclei may not be the best candidates for determining nuclear matter incompressibility due to strong shell effects. The approach enables systematic investigation of how the ISGMR mean energy depends on neutron excess across an isotopic chain, and demonstrates that including quasiparticle-phonon coupling is essential for reproducing experimental resonance widths.
