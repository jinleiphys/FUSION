# Transition Radiation in QCD matter

> In ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions a finite size QCD medium is created. In this paper we compute radiative energy loss to zeroth order in opacity by taking into account finite size effects. Transition radiation occurs on the boundary between the finite size medium and the vacuum, and we show that it lowers the difference between medium and vacuum zeroth order radiative energy loss relative to the infinite size medium case. Further, in all previous computations of light parton radiation to zeroth order in opacity, there was a divergence caused by the fact that the energy loss is infinite in the vacuum and finite in the QCD medium. We show that this infinite discontinuity is naturally regulated by including the transition radiation.

## Key claim

This paper computes the zeroth-order radiative energy loss in a finite-size QCD medium, showing that transition radiation at the medium-vacuum boundary reduces the difference between medium and vacuum energy loss compared to the infinite-medium case, and that this transition radiation naturally regulates the infinite discontinuity between medium and vacuum light parton energy losses that appeared in previous calculations.

## Method

The author computes the squared amplitude for one-gluon radiation from a quark produced inside a static QCD medium of finite length L, using light-cone coordinates and a position-dependent gluon mass that takes the value m_{g,p} inside the medium and m_{g,v} outside. The matrix element is derived from a Feynman diagram where a source produces an off-shell jet that subsequently radiates a gluon, with the gluon wave function satisfying the Klein-Gordon equation with a step-function mass profile. The fractional energy loss is obtained by integrating the squared amplitude over transverse momentum and longitudinal momentum fraction x, using the "Frozen alpha" running coupling model with beta_0 = 28/3 and Lambda_QCD approximately 0.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

* For charm quarks in a 5 fm medium, transition radiation lowers the Ter-Mikayelian effect from 30% to 15-20% reduction of the vacuum energy loss.
* For medium thickness greater than 4 fm, the transition radiation becomes approximately independent of the medium thickness for charm quarks.
* For light quarks with m_{g,v}=0 and m_{g,p}=0.35 GeV, the difference I_med^(0)-I_vac^(0) increases approximately linearly with L, with the analytic approximation I_med^(0)-I_vac^(0) approximately (C_R alpha_S/2) (m_{g,p}^2 L/E) ln[E/(2 m_{g,p})].
* For bottom quarks (M=4.5 GeV) with m_{g,p}=0.35 GeV and m_{g,v}=0, the difference I_med^(0)-I_vac^(0) is negligible across the entire jet energy range.
* For charm quarks with vacuum gluon mass in the range 0 to 0.7 GeV, the difference between medium and vacuum energy loss is in the range of about plus or minus 5%.
* For light quarks with vacuum gluon mass in the range 0 to 0.7 GeV, the difference between medium and vacuum energy loss is in the range of about plus or minus 15%.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation of the QCD Ter-Mikayelian effect for heavy quarks (Djordjevic 2003, 2004), which assumed an infinite QCD medium, to the more realistic finite-size medium created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The computation generalizes the approach of Zakharov (1996) for transition radiation in QCD, and the results for probes produced far outside the medium agree with the QED transition radiation results of Ter-Mikayelian (1961) and Jackson (1999). The paper shows that for light quarks with a finite gluon mass in the vacuum larger than in the medium, the light quarks can leave a medium smaller than 3 fm essentially unquenched, which may help address the puzzle posed by Mioduszewski (2004) regarding pion suppression data.
