# Effective Lagrangian Approach to the Theory of Eta Photoproduction in the $N^{*}(1535)$ Region

> We investigate eta photoproduction in the $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance region within the effective Lagrangian approach (ELA), wherein leading contributions to the amplitude at the tree level are taken into account. These include the nucleon Born terms and the leading $t$-channel vector meson exchanges as the non-resonant pieces. In addition, we consider five resonance contributions in the $s$- and $u$- channel; besides the dominant $N^{*}(1535)$, these are: $N^{*}(1440),N^{*}(1520),N^{*}(1650)$ and $N^{*}(1710)$. The amplitudes for the $π^\circ$ and the $η$ photoproduction near threshold have significant differences, even as they share common contributions, such as those of the nucleon Born terms. Among these differences, the contribution to the $η$ photoproduction of the $s$-channel excitation of the $N^{*}(1535)$ is the most significant. We find the off-shell properties of the spin-3/2 resonances to be important in determining the background contributions. Fitting our effective amplitude to the available data base allows us to extract the quantity $\sqrt{χΓ_η} A_{1/2}/Γ_T$, characteristic of the photoexcitation of the $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance and its decay into the $η$-nucleon channel, of interest to precise tests of hadron models. At the photon point, we determine it to be $(2.2\pm 0.2)\times 10^{-1} GeV^{-1}$ from the old data base, and $(2.2\pm 0.1) \times 10^{-1} GeV^{-1}$ from a combination of old data base and new Bates data. We obtain the helicity amplitude for $N^{*}(1535)\rightarrow γp$ to be $A_{1/2}=(97\pm 7)\times 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}$ from the old data base, and $A_{1/2}=(97\pm 6)\times 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}$ from the combination of the old data base and new Bates data, compared with the results of the analysis of pion photoproduction yielding $74\pm 11$, in the same units.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the product of the photoexcitation amplitude and the eta-nucleon decay amplitude for the N*(1535) resonance from tree-level effective Lagrangian fits to available data, but the eta-nucleon pseudoscalar coupling constant g_eta is not well-determined by the data, yielding a broad range of 0.2 to 6.2.

## Method

The authors construct a tree-level effective Lagrangian amplitude for gamma p -> eta p including nucleon Born terms (with both pseudoscalar and pseudovector couplings), t-channel rho and omega vector meson exchanges, and s- and u-channel exchanges of five nucleon resonances: N*(1440), N*(1520), N*(1535), N*(1650), and N*(1710). They fit this amplitude to the existing differential cross-section data base (137 older data points plus 15 from Homma et al. and 12 new Bates data points) to extract the N*(1535) photocoupling parameters and the eta-nucleon coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Extracted quantity sqrt(chi Gamma_eta) A_{1/2}/Gamma_T at the photon point: (2.2 +/- 0.2) x 10^{-1} GeV^{-1} from the old data base, and (2.2 +/- 0.1) x 10^{-1} GeV^{-1} from the combination of old data and new Bates data
- Helicity amplitude A_{1/2} for N*(1535) -> gamma p: (97 +/- 7) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} from the old data base, and (97 +/- 6) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} from the combined data, compared to 74 +/- 11 from pion photoproduction analysis
- Extracted range for the eta-nucleon pseudoscalar coupling constant: 0.2 <= g_eta <= 6.2
- Threshold for eta photoproduction: photon lab energy 709.3 MeV, corresponding to c.m. energy W = 1487.1 MeV
- N*(1535) lies only 48 MeV above the eta-nucleon threshold

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied to pion photoproduction (Mukhopadhyay and collaborators, 1980s) to the eta photoproduction channel, where the N*(1535) resonance dominates. It contrasts with the coupled-channel isobar model of Tiator, Bennhold and Kamalov (1994), who extracted bare couplings not directly comparable to hadron model predictions, and with Breit-Wigner parameterizations by Homma et al. (1980) and Hicks et al. (1980). The extracted photocoupling amplitudes provide tests for QCD-inspired hadron models and enable future experimental studies at CEBAF and Mainz, particularly for polarization observables.
