# Normalization discrepancies in photoproduction reactions

> Recent CLAS photoproduction results using a tagged bremsstrahlung photon beam for the ground-state pseudoscalar meson photoproduction channels ($K^+ Λ$, $K^+ Σ^0$, $ηp$, $π^+ n$ and $π^0 p$) show a normalization discrepancy with older results from SLAC, DESY and CEA that used an untagged bremsstrahlung beam. The CLAS results are roughly a factor of two smaller than the older data. The CLAS $K^+Λ$ and $K^+Σ^0$ results are in excellent agreement with the latest LEPS results that also employed a tagged beam. For the vector meson ($ωp$ and $ϕp$) channels, CLAS agrees with SLAC results that employed a linearly polarized beam using laser back-scattering, as well as Daresbury data that also came from tagged photon experiment. We perform a global survey of these normalization issues and stress on their significant effect on the coupling constants used in various partial wave analyses.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that CLAS photoproduction cross sections for pseudoscalar meson channels ($K^+\Lambda$, $K^+\Sigma^0$, $\eta p$, $\pi^+ n$, $\pi^0 p$) are systematically lower by roughly a factor of two compared to older SLAC, DESY, and CEA results that used untagged bremsstrahlung beams, while vector meson channels ($\omega p$, $\phi p$) show no such discrepancy; the authors stress that this normalization issue significantly affects the coupling constants extracted in partial wave analyses.

## Method

The authors performed a global survey comparing CLAS g11a and g1c tagged-photon data (covering $\sqrt{s}$ from near-threshold to 2.84 GeV) with older untagged-beam results from SLAC, DESY, CEA, and Daresbury, as well as recent tagged-beam data from LEPS and CB-ELSA. They examined differential cross sections for pseudoscalar channels ($K^+\Lambda$, $K^+\Sigma^0$, $\eta p$, $\pi^+ n$, $\pi^0 p$) and vector meson channels ($\omega p$, $\phi p$), using scaled cross sections ($s^2 \times d\sigma/dt$) to compare shape and normalization across different energy regimes. Internal consistency checks within CLAS included comparing two-track and three-track topologies, comparing g11a with g1c results, and verifying that the $\omega p$ channel (which agrees with older data) shares the same normalization procedure as the pseudoscalar channels.

## Key numbers

- CLAS pseudoscalar meson cross sections are roughly a factor of two smaller than older SLAC/DESY/CEA untagged-beam data
- CLAS g11a photon energy range: 0.808 to 3.811 GeV, corresponding to $\sqrt{s}$ up to approximately 2.84 GeV
- CLAS g1c maximum $\sqrt{s}$: approximately 2.55 GeV
- Quoted systematic uncertainties in most datasets are of order approximately 10%
- CLAS g11a recorded approximately 20 billion event triggers
- CLAS g11a used a 17.85 cm cryotarget; g1c used a 40 cm cryotarget
- CLAS g11a energy binning: 10-MeV-wide $\sqrt{s}$ bins for vector meson channels
- Older experiments had energy bin widths $\Delta E_\gamma \sim \mathcal{O}(1~\text{GeV})$

## Context

This work extends the discussion of normalization discrepancies previously noted by Sibirtsev et al. (2010) for the $\eta p$ channel, and contrasts with the conjecture by Yu et al. (2011) that tensor exchanges could resolve the discrepancy. The paper shows that CLAS $K^+\Lambda$ and $K^+\Sigma^0$ results agree with LEPS data from Sumihama et al. (2006) which also used a tagged beam, while CLAS $\eta p$ results disagree with CB-ELSA data from Crede et al. (2009) and Crede et al. (2005). The vector meson channels agree with SLAC data from Ballam et al. (1973) and Daresbury data from Barber et al. (1984) and Barber et al. (1978). The findings enable future partial wave analyses to properly account for normalization when extracting coupling constants, and highlight the need for new tagged-beam experiments to resolve the CLAS-ELSA discrepancy.
