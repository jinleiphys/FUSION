# Effect of nuclear matter incompressibility on the 16O+208Pb system

> To analyze the property of nuclear matter in the 16O+208Pb collision system, the internuclear potential of the fusion reaction is calculated by using the Skyrme forces associated with an extensive nuclear matter incompressibility K range in the semiclassical energy density formalism. Comparison of the experimental fusion cross sections and those obtained by using potentials derived from different forces with various K values shows that the incompressibility of nuclear matter changes during the fusion process at different bombarding energies. The results indicate that, as the energy increases, the nuclear matter becomes more incompressible.

## Key claim

The nuclear matter incompressibility in the 16O+208Pb system is not a fixed value but varies with bombarding energy, increasing from approximately 234-248 MeV at subbarrier energies to above 300 MeV at above-barrier energies, as inferred from which Skyrme force best reproduces the experimental fusion cross sections at each energy.

## Method

The internuclear potential for the 16O+208Pb fusion reaction was calculated using the semiclassical energy-density formalism with five different Skyrme forces (SkSC4, Es, SKXce, E, and SI) that yield nuclear matter incompressibility values ranging from 234 to 370 MeV. Neutron and proton density distributions for both nuclei were obtained from self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using each respective Skyrme force. Fusion cross sections were then computed with the CCFULL code, including couplings to the 2+ and 3- states of both projectile and target, and compared to experimental data across subbarrier, near-barrier, and above-barrier energies.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility values of the selected Skyrme forces range from 234 MeV (SkSC4) to 370 MeV (SI).
- Skyrme forces with K values of approximately 234-248 MeV reproduce experimental fusion cross sections at energies below and near the barrier.
- The Skyrme force with K=270 MeV explains experimental data at energies in the vicinity and nearly above the barrier.
- Forces with K greater than 300 MeV predict fusion cross sections at energies above the barrier and at higher energies.
- All selected Skyrme forces reproduce experimental binding energies and root-mean-square charge radii of 16O and 208Pb with relative deviations less than 4.69% and 2.88%, respectively.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the 16O+208Pb system that used static and dynamic approaches (including earlier work by the same group cited as reference 30) by systematically varying the nuclear matter incompressibility across a wide range to probe its energy dependence. The results enable future studies to treat nuclear matter incompressibility as an energy-dependent quantity in fusion calculations, and the method is demonstrated to be applicable to other systems such as 40Ca+90Zr. The approach provides a static-model alternative to dynamic methods like quantum molecular dynamics and time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory for analyzing the nuclear matter equation of state in fusion reactions.
