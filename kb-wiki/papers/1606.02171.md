# Three pion nucleon coupling constants

> There exist four pion nucleon coupling constants, $f_{π^0, pp}$, $-f_{π^0, nn}$, $f_{π^+, pn} /\sqrt{2}$ and $ f_{π^-, np} /\sqrt{2}$ which coincide when up and down quark masses are identical and the electron charge is zero. While there is no reason why the pion-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants should be identical in the real world, one expects that the small differences might be pinned down from a sufficiently large number of independent and mutually consistent data. Our discussion provides a rationale for our recent determination $$f_p^2 = 0.0759(4) \, , \quad f_{0}^2 = 0.079(1) \,, \quad f_{c}^2 = 0.0763(6) \, , $$ based on a partial wave analysis of the $3σ$ self-consistent nucleon-nucleon Granada-2013 database comprising 6713 published data in the period 1950-2013.

## Key claim

The four pion-nucleon coupling constants are not identical in the real world due to isospin breaking from up/down quark mass differences and electromagnetic effects, and the Granada-2013 database of 6713 self-consistent nucleon-nucleon scattering data provides sufficient statistical power to resolve these small differences at the 1-2% level, though the ordering pattern predicted by chiral quark models remains unconfirmed.

## Method

The authors performed a partial wave analysis of the Granada-2013 database comprising 6713 pp and np scattering data below 350 MeV laboratory energy, collected between 1950-2013. They used a coarse-grained potential approach with Dirac delta-shells at distances below 3 fm, treating the potential values at sampled points as fitting parameters (approximately 50 parameters), while fixing the long-range part to unregularized one-pion exchange plus electromagnetic interactions (Coulomb, vacuum polarization, magnetic moments, relativistic corrections). A self-consistent 3σ rejection criterion was applied iteratively to remove incompatible data sets, reducing the initial 8173 data points to 6713.

## Key numbers

- $f_p^2 = 0.0759(4)$ (proton-proton neutral pion coupling squared)
- $f_0^2 = 0.079(1)$ (mixed proton-neutron neutral pion coupling squared)
- $f_c^2 = 0.0763(6)$ (charged pion coupling squared)
- $g_p^2/(4\pi) = 13.72(7)$ (proton-proton pseudoscalar coupling)
- $g_n^2/(4\pi) = 14.91(39)$ (neutron-neutron pseudoscalar coupling)
- $g_c^2/(4\pi) = 13.81(11)$ (charged pion pseudoscalar coupling)
- Database: 6713 data points (from initial 8173) after 3σ self-consistent selection
- Maximum laboratory energy: 350 MeV
- Coarse-graining resolution: Δr = 0.6 fm
- Outer cutoff radius: r_c = 3 fm

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen partial wave analyses (Stoks 1993, de Swart 1997) which recommended a single value $f^2 = 0.0750(9)$ under charge symmetry breaking assumptions, by using a significantly larger database (6713 vs 4313 data points) to resolve the three distinct coupling constants. The results confirm the Nijmegen group's 1997 premonition that charge-independence breaking effects could be checked with improved data. The determination enables more accurate nuclear structure calculations and provides empirical constraints for lattice QCD calculations of the pion-nucleon coupling constant, which currently achieve values around $g_{\pi NN} \sim 13(1)$ at unphysical pion masses (Aoki 2009, Alexandrou 2007).
