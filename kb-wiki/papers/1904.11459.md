# Determining the Diffusivity for Light Quarks from Experiment

> Charge balance functions reflect the evolution of charged pair correlations throughout the stages of pair production, dynamical diffusion, and hadronization in heavy-ion collisions. Microscopic modeling of these correlations in the full collision volume shows that the balance functions are sensitive to the diffusivity of light quarks when studied as functions of relative azimuthal angle. By restricting our analysis to K+,K- and p,pbar pairs, we find that the diffusivity of light quarks, a fundamental property not currently well understood, can be constrained by experimental measurement.

## Key claim

By analyzing charge balance functions for K⁺K⁻ and p\(\bar{p}\) pairs binned by relative azimuthal angle, the diffusivity of light quarks in the quark-gluon plasma can be constrained to better than a factor of two, provided various systematic uncertainties (initial thermalization time, viscous effects, \(\phi\) meson contributions) can be reduced to the 15% level.

## Method

The authors simulate the evolution of charge correlations by superimposing a diffusion equation for the non-local part of the charge correlator onto a hydrodynamic description of the collision (based on Shen (2014) for the QGP phase) and a hadronic simulation for the hadronic stage. The diffusion equation is solved via a Monte Carlo procedure where sample charge pairs are created according to a source function derived from the evolution of the susceptibility (from lattice calculations by Borsanyi (2011) and Bellwied (2015)), then propagated as random walks with mean free paths consistent with the chosen diffusion constant. The resulting hadrons from each charge pair are tracked through a hadronic simulation, and balance functions are constructed for K⁺K⁻ and p\(\bar{p}\) pairs as functions of relative rapidity and relative azimuthal angle.

## Key numbers

- The diffusivity from lattice calculations (Aarts 2014) is used as the baseline; calculations are repeated with half, double, and quadruple that value.
- The initial spread of the balancing correlation is given a Gaussian width of \(\sigma_0 = 0.5\) units of spatial rapidity at 0.6 fm/\(c\) after the beginning of the collision.
- The \(\phi\) meson decay width is 4.3 MeV, and its contribution to the K⁺K⁻ balance function can be subtracted to ensure normalizations are uncertain at the 5% level.
- If the height of the experimental balance function is changed by 15%, the extracted diffusivity would be modified by roughly 50%.
- The analysis is restricted to 0-5% central events.

## Context

This work extends prior modeling of charge balance functions by Pratt (2018) and Pratt (2017) to specifically constrain the light-quark diffusivity, a transport coefficient not yet well determined experimentally. It contrasts with lattice QCD calculations (Aarts 2014; Amato 2013), perturbative EQCD (Ghiglieri 2018), and AdS/CFT predictions (Policastro 2002) which give values near \(1/(2\pi T)\), as well as hadron-gas estimates with 25 mb cross sections (Greif 2017). The proposed method enables experimental extraction of the diffusivity with precision comparable to that achieved for shear viscosity, and is motivated by existing STAR and LHC measurements of balance functions (Wang 2012; Abelev 2010; Adams 2003).
