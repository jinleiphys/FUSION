# Light Scalars in Field Theory

> Outline: 1. Introduction, 2. Confinement, chiral dynamics and light scalar mesons, 3. Chiral shielding of σ(600), chiral constraints, σ(600), f_0(980) and their mixing in ππ\toππ, ππ\to K\bar K, and ϕ\toγ\pi0\pi0, 4. The ϕmeson radiative decays on light scalar resonances. 5. Why a0(980) and f0(980) are not the K\bar K molecules. 6. Light scalars in γγcollisions. Evidence for four-quark components of light scalars is given. The priority of Quantum Field Theory in revealing the light scalar mystery is emphasized.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the light scalar mesons (σ(600), κ(700-900), a₀(980), f₀(980)) are not ordinary quark-antiquark states but have a dominant four-quark (q²q̄²) component, with the chiral shielding mechanism hiding the σ meson in ππ scattering because a negative background phase prevents the phase shift from reaching 90° at the resonance mass.

## Method
The author uses the SU_L(2)×SU_R(2) Linear Sigma Model (LSM) to describe ππ→ππ scattering, separating the amplitude into a background phase and a resonance phase. The same framework is extended to γγ→ππ, φ→γπ⁰π⁰, and φ→γπ⁰η decays, with the K⁺K⁻ loop model used to describe the production of a₀(980) and f₀(980) in radiative φ decays. The amplitudes are unitarized via a K-matrix approach, and the pole positions in the complex s-plane are extracted from the scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- σ meson Breit-Wigner mass M_res = 0.43 GeV, width Γ_res(M²_res) = 0.67 GeV, with m_σ = 0.93 GeV (the mass shift from the BW mass is 0.50 GeV)
- σ pole position: √s_R = (0.52 - i0.25) GeV, with residue g²_π = (0.12 + i0.21) GeV²
- ππ scattering lengths: a⁰₀ = 0.18 m_π⁻¹, a²₀ = -0.04 m_π⁻¹
- σ→γγ decay width from the pole residue: Γ(σ→γγ) ≈ 2 keV
- Direct (non-rescattering) σ→γγ width: Γ⁰_σγγ ≈ 0.0034 keV
- Phenomenological chiral shielding results: m_σ = 679 MeV, Γ_σ = 498 MeV, m_f₀ = 989 MeV, a⁰₀ = 0.223 m_π⁺⁻¹
- Average σ→π⁺π⁻→γγ width from Belle data: ⟨Γ(σ→π⁺π⁻→γγ)⟩ ≈ 0.45 keV
- Average f₀→K⁺K⁻→γγ width from Belle data: ⟨Γ(f₀→K⁺K⁻→γγ)⟩ ≈ 0.2 keV
- a₀→K⁺K⁻→γγ width: ⟨Γ(a₀→K⁺K⁻→γγ)⟩ ≈ 0.3 keV
- Direct (non-rescattering) widths for σ, f₀, a₀→γγ: all ≪ 0.1 keV

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies (Achasov and Shestakov, 1994, 2007) on chiral shielding of the σ meson and the four-quark interpretation of light scalars. It directly contradicts the K̄K molecular interpretation of a₀(980) and f₀(980) by showing that the K⁺K⁻ loop model requires gauge-invariant regularization with high-momentum contributions, incompatible with a nonrelativistic molecular picture. The analysis of Belle data on γγ→ππ (Achasov and Shestakov, 2005, 2008) provides new evidence that light scalars are produced via rescattering (γγ→π⁺π⁻→σ, γγ→K⁺K⁻→f₀/a₀) rather than direct γγ→q̄q couplings, consistent with the four-quark model predictions from 1982 (Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov). The results enable future studies of scalar meson production mechanisms beyond simple mass spectrum fits.
