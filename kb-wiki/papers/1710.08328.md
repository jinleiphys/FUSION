# Octet Baryon Masses and Magnetic Moments in Hot and Dense Isospin Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> Medium modification of the magnetic moments of octet baryons in the isospin asymmetric nuclear medium at finite temperature has been calculated using medium modified quark and baryon masses derived in chiral SU(3) quark mean field model. The magnetic moments of baryons are found to vary significantly as a function of density of nuclear medium as well as with the increase in isospin asymmetry of the medium. The rise of temperature is found to decrease the effect of isospin asymmetry on masses and magnetic moments of baryons. The results of present investigation may be helpful to understand the experimentally observed values of octet baryon magnetic moments at different experimental facilities.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of octet baryons vary significantly with nuclear medium density and isospin asymmetry, but increasing temperature reduces the effect of isospin asymmetry on both masses and magnetic moments; the results are obtained within the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model including the scalar isovector delta meson field, which is important for strongly asymmetric matter at higher densities.

## Method

The authors use the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model with mean field approximation, incorporating scalar isoscalar fields (sigma, zeta), a scalar dilaton field (chi), a scalar isovector delta meson field, and vector meson fields (omega, rho). Effective quark masses are obtained from the meson mean fields at finite temperature and density, and baryon masses are computed from these quark masses including a spin-spin interaction correction and removal of spurious center-of-mass motion. The total effective magnetic moment of each octet baryon is then calculated by summing contributions from valence quarks, sea quarks, and the orbital angular momentum of sea quarks.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum masses of quarks are taken as m_u = m_d = 313 MeV and m_s = 489 MeV.
- The scalar isovector delta meson field is included; the coupling constants satisfy g_delta^u = -g_delta^d = g_sigma^u = g_sigma^d = (1/sqrt(2)) g_zeta^s.
- The confining potential coupling constant k_c is taken as 100 MeV.fm^{-2}.
- The spin-spin correction energies for octet baryons are: E_N_spin = -482 MeV, E_Lambda_spin = -756.9 MeV, E_Sigma_spin = -531 MeV, E_Xi_spin = -705 MeV.
- The vacuum expectation values of scalar meson fields are sigma_0 = -93.49 MeV and zeta_0 = -97.98 MeV, with dilaton field chi_0 = 254.6 MeV and coupling g_4 = 37.4.
- Saturation density is taken as rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of octet baryon magnetic moments in symmetric nuclear matter (Singh, Kumar, Dahiya, 2017) to the case of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, incorporating the scalar isovector delta meson field which was not included in that earlier study. The approach builds on the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model developed by Wang and collaborators, and the inclusion of sea quark and orbital angular momentum contributions follows the method used by Cheng and Li for free-space baryon magnetic moments. The results are relevant for interpreting data from heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as FAIR, RHIC, and LHC, and for understanding baryon properties in neutron star matter.
