# Nuclear y and x scaling

> The basic approximations leading to non-relativistic and relativistic nuclear y and x scaling are recalled. The general and systematic features of y-scaling structure functions are pointed out, and a recently proposed novel approach to y-scaling, based on a global scaling variable, y_G, which incorporates the effect of the momentum dependence of the nucleon removal energy, and therefore allows the establishment of a direct link between scaling functions and momentum distributions, is illustrated and applied to the analysis of a large body of data. The phenomenon of nuclear x-scaling, based on a proper analysis of nuclear quasi-elastic data in terms of the Bjorken scaling variable x_B, is shown to occur at high values of the four-momentum transfer Q^2; the usefulness of nuclear x scaling is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a newly defined "global" scaling variable \(y_G\), which incorporates the momentum dependence of the nucleon removal energy, allows a direct extraction of the longitudinal momentum distribution from experimental scaling functions without requiring theoretical binding corrections, unlike the standard \(y\)-scaling approach where the scaling function \(F(y)\) differs from the longitudinal momentum distribution \(f(y)\) due to binding effects.

## Method

The authors recall the exact expression for the nuclear structure function in inclusive lepton-nucleus scattering and derive the non-relativistic and relativistic approximations leading to \(y\)-scaling and \(x\)-scaling. They introduce a global scaling variable \(y_G\) by replacing the excitation energy of the residual \(A-1\) system with an average value that depends on the nucleon momentum, obtained from realistic spectral functions, and apply this variable to analyze experimental data for nuclei from deuteron to nuclear matter. The \(x\)-scaling analysis uses the Bjorken variable \(x_B\) and examines the shape of the reduced structure function \(\nu W\) around \(x_B \simeq 1\).

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron, \(f(0) \sim 10\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\); for heavy nuclei, \(f(0) \sim 3\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\).
- For \(y \sim 0\), \(f(y) \sim (\alpha^2 + y^2)^{-1}\) with \(\alpha\) ranging from \(\sim 45\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(A=2\) to \(\sim 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(A=56\).
- For \(50\ \mathrm{MeV} \le |y| \le 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(F(y) \sim e^{-a^2 y^2}\) with \(a\) ranging from \(\sim 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(A=2\) to \(\sim 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(A=56\).
- For \(|y| \ge 400\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(f(y) \sim C_2 e^{-b|y|}\) with \(b = 6 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\), independent of \(A\); \(C_2\) ranges from \(2.5 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) for \(A=2\) to \(1 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) for \(A=56\).
- For \(^4\mathrm{He}\), the normalization of the mean-field contribution \(S_0 = 0.76\) and the correlation contribution \(S_1 = 0.24\) from the fit, compared to theoretical values \(S_0 \sim 0.8\) and \(S_1 \sim 0.2\).
- The width of the Lorentzian in \(x\)-scaling converges to \(\alpha^2/M^2 = 2E_{\mathrm{min}}/M\) as \(Q^2 \rightarrow \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the standard \(y\)-scaling approach developed by West (1975) and others, which required theoretical binding corrections to extract momentum distributions, by introducing a global scaling variable \(y_G\) that directly links scaling functions to longitudinal momentum distributions. The analysis of \(x\)-scaling using the Bjorken variable \(x_B\) provides a complementary method to study nucleon momentum distributions at high four-momentum transfer. The results enable future experiments at higher \(Q^2\) to test the predicted saturation of the width in \(x\)-scaling and to further investigate the universal nature of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations across all nuclei.
