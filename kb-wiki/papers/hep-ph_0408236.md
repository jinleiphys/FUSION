# Glueball filled with the quark field as a model of nucleon

> On the basis of the non-perturbative Heisenberg's quantization scheme and using some simplifications and assumptions the reduction from the gluon-quark Lagrangian to a scalar-fermion Lagrangian is made. The corresponding field equations have a regular spherically symmetric solution which can be interpret as a simplified model of nucleon. Some properties of such "nucleon" are discused: the presence of a mass gap and spin $\neq \hbar /2$. The mass gap describes the difference between the "nucleon" and the "nucleon" without quark field (a scalar glueball). The presence of spin $\neq \hbar /2$ can be connected with the spin problem of nucleon.

## Key claim

Using a non-perturbative Heisenberg quantization scheme with simplifying assumptions (closure of the infinite Green's function hierarchy via one-function and product approximations), the gluon-quark Lagrangian is reduced to a scalar-fermion Lagrangian whose regular spherically symmetric solutions can be interpreted as a simplified model of the nucleon, but the resulting object has spin not equal to ℏ/2, which the author suggests may be connected to the nucleon spin problem.

## Method

The author starts from the SU(3) gauge field Lagrangian interacting with quarks and averages it over a quantum state, keeping only 2-point and 4-point Green's functions. The 2-point gluon Green's function is approximated as a product of scalar fields φ^A, and the 4-point function is written as a symmetrized product of two 2-point functions; odd Green's functions vanish because ⟨Â⟩ = 0. The quark Lagrangian is approximated by a classical field plus an interaction term α (ψ̄ ψ)(φ^A φ^A) inserted by hand to couple quarks to gluon fluctuations. The resulting coupled scalar and Dirac equations are solved numerically as a nonlinear eigenvalue problem in spherical symmetry using an iterative scheme: starting from a guessed f₀(x), one solves for φ₁, then f₁, then the Dirac spinor components h₁, g₁, and repeats until convergence.

## Key numbers

* The iterative eigenvalues converge to m* ≈ 1.6327, μ* ≈ 1.5030, and E* ≈ 0.9405 (dimensionless units, after rescaling by φ(0)).
* The dimensionless energy integral I₁ ≈ 0.395, giving total energy W = 4π φ(0) × 0.395.
* The z-projection of the full angular momentum is M_z = (1/2) × 4π I₂, where I₂ = ∫ x² [(h*)² + (g*)²] dx, yielding spin ≠ ℏ/2.
* Boundary conditions used: φ(0) = 1.0, f(0) = √0.6, g(0) = 0.1, with parameters G = 2.0, M = 0.2, λ₁ = 1.0, λ₂ = 0.1, α = 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier scalar glueball model (Dzhunushaliev, 2003) by filling that glueball with quarks, using the same non-perturbative Heisenberg quantization technique previously applied to nonlinear spinor fields by Heisenberg. The solution avoids Derrick's theorem because the scalar fields tend to a local minimum (φ → m*, f → 0) rather than the global minimum at infinity. The result that the "nucleon" has spin ≠ ℏ/2 is presented as a possible connection to the long-standing nucleon spin problem, and the mass gap between this object and the pure scalar glueball (without quarks) is identified as a key feature.
