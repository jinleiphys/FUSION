# Half-lives of rp-process waiting point nuclei

> We give results of microscopic calculations for the half-lives of various proton-rich nuclei in the mass region A=60-90, which are involved in the astrophysical rp-process, and which are needed as input parameters of numerical simulations in Nuclear Astrophysics. The microscopic formalism consists of a deformed QRPA approach that involves a selfconsistent quasiparticle deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock basis and residual spin-isospin separable forces in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. The strength of the particle-hole residual interaction is chosen to be consistent with the Skyrme effective force and mean field basis, while that of the particle-particle is globally fixed to 0.07 MeV after a judicious choice from comparison to experimental half-lives. We study and discuss the sensitivity of the half-lives to deformation and residual interactions.

## Key claim

The deformed QRPA approach, using a selfconsistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock basis with a globally fixed particle-particle coupling strength of 0.07 MeV, reproduces experimental half-lives of rp-process waiting point nuclei when the selfconsistent equilibrium deformations from energy minimization are used, but the results are sensitive to both deformation and the residual interaction strength.

## Method

The authors performed deformed HF+BCS calculations with Skyrme forces (Sk3 and SG2) to generate the mean field and determine equilibrium deformations selfconsistently. They then applied a proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with separable spin-isospin residual interactions in both the particle-hole (ph) and particle-particle (pp) channels, where the ph strength was derived consistently from the Skyrme force and the pp strength was fixed globally to 0.07 MeV by fitting experimental half-lives. The Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition strengths were calculated and summed with phase space factors to obtain beta-decay half-lives for the N=Z waiting point nuclei from A=64 to A=92.

## Key numbers

- The particle-particle coupling strength was globally fixed to $\kappa = 0.07$ MeV after comparison to experimental half-lives.
- For $^{64}$Ge, the Sk3 force gives a quadrupole deformation $\beta = 0.199$ and SG2 gives $\beta = -0.192$.
- For $^{68}$Se, Sk3 gives $\beta = -0.216$ and SG2 gives $\beta = -0.219$.
- For $^{72}$Kr, Sk3 gives $\beta = -0.268$ and SG2 gives $\beta = -0.246$.
- For $^{76}$Sr, Sk3 gives $\beta = 0.408$ and SG2 gives $\beta = 0.416$.
- For $^{80}$Zr, Sk3 gives $\beta = 0.431$ and SG2 gives $\beta = 0.002$.
- For $^{84}$Mo, Sk3 gives $\beta = 0.005$ and SG2 gives $\beta = 0.001$.
- For $^{88}$Ru, Sk3 gives $\beta = 0.019$ and SG2 gives $\beta = 0.038$.
- For $^{92}$Pd, Sk3 gives $\beta = 0.111$ and SG2 gives $\beta = 0.103$.
- Charge root mean square radii range from 3.985 fm ($^{64}$Ge, Ref. [ring]) to 4.458 fm ($^{92}$Pd, Sk3 this work).
- The HF+BCS half-lives are systematically lower than QRPA results, sometimes by one order of magnitude.
- Deformation changes half-lives by factors up to four.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations of beta-decay properties using deformed HF+BCS+QRPA with Skyrme forces (Sarriguren et al., 2003) to the specific N=Z waiting point nuclei of the rp-process, which were previously studied with phenomenological potentials (Moller et al., 1997) and relativistic mean field models (Ring et al., 2001). The results enable more reliable input for rp-process network simulations, particularly for nuclei where experimental half-lives are unknown, and for calculations under varying astrophysical conditions of density and temperature.
