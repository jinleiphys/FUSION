# Nucleon Axial Matrix Elements

> Current issues associated with nucleon axial matrix elements are studied, including the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, the induced pseudoscalar, and SU(3) chiral perturbation theory.

# Nucleon Axial Matrix Elements

## Key claim
The nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\) has apparently increased by about 7% over a decade (from 1.17±0.02 in 1959 to 1.266±0.004 in the latest Grenoble experiment), but this "time dependence" is facetious; the real physics lies in the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, which depends on the precise value of the pion-nucleon coupling constant and may favor either conventional or generalized chiral perturbation theory depending on which analysis of \(g_{\pi NN}\) is adopted.

## Method
The paper reviews three topics: (1) the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy \(\Delta_\pi = 1 - M_N g_A(0)/[F_\pi g_{\pi NN}(m_\pi^2)]\), evaluated using the Dashen-Weinstein theorem in SU(3) and compared to two competing determinations of \(g_{\pi NN}(m_\pi^2)\) (Karlsruhe value 13.4 vs. VPI value 13.05); (2) the induced pseudoscalar coupling \(g_P(q^2)\) extracted from ordinary muon capture on H, \(^3\)He, and \(^{12}\)C, from radiative muon capture on hydrogen, and from threshold pion electroproduction; (3) SU(3) axial couplings in hyperon semileptonic decays, where one-loop heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory is regularized with a cutoff (300–600 MeV) to account for finite hadronic size, replacing dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers
- \(g_A/g_V = 1.266 \pm 0.004\) (Grenoble, latest published value used in the paper)
- Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy: \(\Delta_\pi = 4.1\%\) for \(g_{\pi NN}=13.4\) (Karlsruhe), implying \(m_s/\hat{m} \approx 48\); \(\Delta_\pi = 1.5\%\) for \(g_{\pi NN}=13.05\) (VPI), implying \(m_s/\hat{m} \approx 17\)
- Dashen-Weinstein theorem prediction: \(\Delta_\pi^{\text{theo}} \simeq 0.028\) (2.8%)
- Induced pseudoscalar ratio \(r_P = [m_\mu/(2M g_A(0))] g_P(q^2=-0.9 m_\mu^2) = 6.7\) (chiral prediction)
- Experimental \(r_P\) values: \(6.5 \pm 2.4\) (hydrogen), \(6.9 \pm 0.2\) (\({}^{3}\)He), \(9.0 \pm 1.7\) (\({}^{12}\)C)
- Muon capture rate on \({}^{3}\)He: \(\Gamma_\mu(^{3}\text{He}) = 1496 \pm 4 \text{ s}^{-1}\) (PSI, 3% precision)
- Radiative muon capture on hydrogen: \(r_P = 9.8 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.3\) (TRIUMF, at variance with chiral prediction at 3\(\sigma\))
- SU(3) chiral loop corrections to \(g_A\) for various transitions: in dimensional regularization, corrections are large (e.g., \(g_A(\bar{p}n)=1.72\)); with cutoff regularization they reduce to 0.37–0.84 for \(\Lambda=300\)–600 MeV

## Context
This review extends the classic Goldberger-Treiman relation (Goldberger and Treiman, 1958) and the Dashen-Weinstein theorem (Dashen and Weinstein, 1969) by confronting them with modern precision data on \(g_A\), \(F_\pi\), and \(g_{\pi NN}\). It discusses the induced pseudoscalar in the context of muon capture experiments (H, \(^3\)He, \(^{12}\)C) and the controversial TRIUMF radiative muon capture result. The SU(3) chiral perturbation theory analysis with a finite-size cutoff (Holstein and collaborators) resolves the apparent failure of one-loop chiral corrections (which gave 30–50% modifications) and brings results into agreement with chiral bag models such as the cloudy bag model. The work enables refined tests of chiral symmetry in the nucleon sector and motivates new measurements of the induced pseudoscalar via polarized muon capture on polarized \(^3\)He.
