# Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of a relativistic Hagedorn resonance gas

> The new state of matter produced at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider reveals a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma with an extremely small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s. We calculate the eta/s of an equilibrated hadron matter characterized by a relativistic hadron resonance gas with a Hagedorn mass spectrum that grows exponentially with the hadron mass. We find with increase in temperature of the system the eta/s value decreases due to rapid increase in the multiplicity of massive resonances. In the vicinity of the critical temperature for deconfinement transition, the minimum value of eta/s in the Hagedorn resonance gas is found to be consistent with the current estimates for a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of an equilibrated hadron matter with a Hagedorn mass spectrum decreases with temperature and reaches a minimum of approximately 0.34 at the critical temperature for deconfinement, which is consistent with the upper bound of current estimates for a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma but significantly above the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) lower bound of 1/4π.

## Method

The author uses a Monte Carlo sequential binary emission model to simulate an infinite equilibrated hadron gas in a box with periodic boundary conditions, including both low-lying measured hadrons (mass less than 2 GeV) and Hagedorn states (mass greater than 2 GeV) with an exponentially growing mass spectrum. The shear viscosity is extracted via the Green-Kubo relation by integrating the time correlation function of the shear-stress tensor, while the entropy density is computed from the Gibbs formula using excluded-volume corrections to account for repulsive interactions among finite-size hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Minimum shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the Hagedorn resonance gas at zero baryon chemical potential: (η/s)min ≈ 0.34 at Tc = 196 MeV
- Speed of sound drops from c²s ≈ 0.18 at T ≈ 100 MeV to about 0.09 at Tc
- In the statistical model with Hagedorn states, the speed of sound at Tc is c²s ≈ 0.07
- The UrQMD model (without Hagedorn states) yields η/s ≈ 0.9 at T ≈ 125-166 MeV, compared to the Hagedorn gas value of approximately 0.34 at Tc
- The Hagedorn temperature is set to TH = 196 MeV, consistent with the lattice QCD critical temperature
- The exponent α = 2.44 in the Hagedorn mass spectrum density formula
- The effective MIT bag constant used is B^(1/4) = 0.40 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of η/s in hadron matter that used chiral perturbation theory, the linearized Boltzmann equation (Dobado, Chen, Itakura), and microscopic transport theory (Muronga). It directly contrasts with the UrQMD model calculation by Bass (2009) which included only hadrons up to mass ~2 GeV and found η/s ≈ 0.9, significantly higher than the viscous hydrodynamic estimates of η/s ~ 0.08-0.24 from RHIC data. The study also builds on the rate equation approach by Noronha (2009) that demonstrated η/s reduction near Tc with Hagedorn states. The results enable improved viscous hydrodynamic modeling of the hadronic after-burning phase in heavy ion collisions, suggesting a continuous transition of η/s between the deconfined and confined phases near Tc rather than a sharp increase.
