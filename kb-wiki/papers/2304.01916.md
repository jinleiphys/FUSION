# New model comparison for semi-inclusive charged-current electron and muon neutrino scattering by $^{40}$Ar in the energy range of the MicroBooNE experiment

> In this work we present a comparison of semi-inclusive muon and electron neutrino cross sections with $^{40}$Ar target measured by the MicroBooNE Collaboration with the predictions of an unfactorized model based on the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) and the SuSAv2-MEC model implemented in the neutrino event generator GENIE. The predictions based on the RDWIA approach, with a realistic description of the initial state and a phenomenological relativistic complex optical potential for the description of final state interactions, better describe the measured cross sections than GENIE-SuSAv2 and RDWIA with a purely real potential.

## Key claim

The relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) with a phenomenological relativistic complex optical potential (ROP) for final state interactions provides better overall agreement with MicroBooNE semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-argon cross section data than either the GENIE-SuSAv2 model or RDWIA with a purely real energy-dependent relativistic mean field (ED-RMF) potential, achieving an average chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.95 across all measurements compared to 2.84 for ED-RMF.

## Method

The authors compared MicroBooNE semi-inclusive muon and electron neutrino cross sections on argon-40 with predictions from two RDWIA approaches (using ROP and ED-RMF for final state interactions) and the SuSAv2-MEC model implemented in GENIE. The RDWIA calculations used a relativistic mean field description of the initial nuclear state with a continuous missing energy profile parameterized from seven independent-particle shell model states plus a background component, with uncertainties estimated by sampling the profile parameters. Two-particle-two-hole meson exchange current contributions and pion absorption contributions were calculated with GENIE and added to the quasielastic cross sections for comparison to the CC0piNp and CC0pi1p topology measurements.

## Key numbers

- Average chi-squared per degree of freedom across all measurements: 0.95 for ROP model, 2.84 for ED-RMF model
- For the muon CC0piNp reconstructed muon momentum distribution, ROP achieved chi-squared of approximately 10 for 6 degrees of freedom
- The Booster Neutrino Beamline energy extends to 7 GeV and peaks around 0.7 GeV
- Muon momentum threshold for CC0piNp topology: greater than 0.1 GeV
- Proton momentum range for muon CC0piNp: 0.3-1.2 GeV
- Muon momentum range for CC0pi1p: 0.1-1.5 GeV
- Proton momentum range for CC0pi1p: 0.3-1.0 GeV
- Electron energy threshold for electron CC0piNp: greater than 30.5 MeV
- Proton momentum threshold for electron CC0piNp: greater than 0.3105 GeV
- Argon binding energy assumed for neutrino energy reconstruction: 40 MeV
- Background occupancy parameter Sb = 3.05, resulting in approximately 4.3 neutrons in the background out of 22 total neutrons

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RDWIA analysis of semi-inclusive neutrino-carbon scattering (Franco-Patino et al., 2022) to argon-40 targets relevant for the MicroBooNE, SBN, and DUNE experiments. The study compares the RDWIA approach with the SuSAv2 inclusive model implemented in GENIE (based on superscaling work by Amaro et al., 2004) and provides validation of nuclear models against exclusive measurements that are sensitive to final state interactions. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-argon interactions for oscillation experiments and suggest that future microscopic semi-inclusive two-particle-two-hole calculations could be validated against additional exclusive measurements like the CC0pi topology with one muon and two protons.
