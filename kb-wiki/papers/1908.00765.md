# Normal-ordered $k$-body approximation in particle-number-breaking theories

> The reach of ab initio many-body theories is rapidly extending over the nuclear chart. However, dealing fully with three-nucleon, possibly four-nucleon, interactions makes the solving of the A-body Schrödinger equation particularly cumbersome, if not impossible beyond a certain nuclear mass. Consequently, ab initio calculations of mid-mass nuclei are typically performed on the basis of the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation that captures dominant effects of three-nucleon forces while effectively working with two-nucleon operators. A powerful idea currently employed to extend ab initio calculations to open-shell nuclei consists of expanding the exact solution of the A-body Schrödinger equation while authorizing the approximate solution to break symmetries of the Hamiltonian. In this context, operators are normal ordered with respect to a symmetry-breaking reference state such that proceeding to a naive truncation may lead to symmetry-breaking approximate operators. The purpose of the present work is to design a normal-ordering approximation of operators that is consistent with the symmetries of the Hamiltonian while working in the context of symmetry broken (and potentially restored) methods. Focusing on many-body formalisms in which U(1) global-gauge symmetry associated with particle number conservation is broken (and potentially restored), a particle-number-conserving normal-ordered k-body (PNOkB) approximation of an arbitrary N-body operator is designed on the basis of Bogoliubov reference states. A numerical test based on particle-number projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations permits to check the particle-number conserving/violating character of a given approximation to a particle-number conserving operator. Using the presently proposed PNOkB approximation, ab initio calculations based on symmetry-breaking and restored formalisms can be safely performed.

## Key claim

The paper designs a particle-number-conserving normal-ordered k-body (PNOkB) approximation for arbitrary N-body operators when using symmetry-breaking Bogoliubov reference states, demonstrating that naive extensions of the standard normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation produce operators that violate particle-number conservation, whereas the proposed PNOkB procedure yields approximate operators that commute with the particle-number operator.

## Method

The authors formulate the normal ordering of an arbitrary N-body operator with respect to a particle-number-breaking Bogoliubov reference state using Wick's theorem, expressing the operator in both single-particle and quasiparticle bases. They then define the PNOkB approximation by recursively constructing particle-number-conserving effective k-body matrix elements from the normal-ordered fields of the original operator, ensuring that the truncated operator retains the same normal fields as the full operator up to rank k while discarding higher-rank terms. A numerical test based on particle-number projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations is employed to verify the particle-number conserving or violating character of the approximate operators.

## Key numbers

* The error induced by the NO2B approximation to the Hamiltonian in large-scale no-core shell model calculations was estimated to be of the order of 1-3% up to the oxygen region (citing RoBi12 and Geb16).
* For a two-body operator, the naive NO1B approximation produces non-zero two-particle-addition and two-particle-removal contributions proportional to (1/2)tr[o^{22}κ] and (1/2)tr[o^{22}κ*] respectively, explicitly demonstrating particle-number violation.
* The correction terms in the PNOkB procedure become non-zero only for initial N-body operators with N ≥ 4, meaning that for up to an initial three-body operator, no extra terms beyond the original fields are needed.

## Context

This work extends the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation, previously formulated for symmetry-conserving Slater determinant reference states and used successfully in no-core shell model calculations (RoBi12, Geb16), to the context of symmetry-breaking Bogoliubov reference states employed in methods such as Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (Tichai 2018), Bogoliubov coupled cluster (Signoraccia 2014), and Gorkov self-consistent Green's function (Soma 2011, Soma 2013). The PNOkB approximation enables ab initio calculations based on symmetry-breaking and restored formalisms to be performed safely, and the authors envision future formulation of an angular-momentum-conserving normal-ordered k-body approximation based on deformed Slater determinant or Bogoliubov reference states.
