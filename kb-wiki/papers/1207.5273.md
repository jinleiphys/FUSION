# Quarkonium+$γ$ production in coherent hadron - hadron interactions at LHC energies

> In this paper we study the $H + γ$ ($H = J/Ψ$ and $Υ$) production in coherent hadron - hadron interactions at LHC energies. Considering the ultrarelativistic protons as a source of photons, we estimate the $γ+ p \rightarrow H + γ+ X$ cross section using the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization formalism and considering different sets of values for the matrix elements. Our results for the total $ p + p \rightarrow p + H + γ+ X$ cross sections and rapidity distributions at $\sqrt{s} = 7$ and 14 TeV demonstrate that the experimental analysis of the $J/Ψ+ γ$ production at LHC is feasible.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the experimental analysis of \(J/\Psi + \gamma\) production in coherent proton-proton collisions at LHC is feasible, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of NRQCD matrix elements; using the MEHEN set yields cross sections a factor of about 3 larger than the more recent BK set, and the predictions are strongly dependent on the assumed charm quark mass and hard scale.

## Method

The total cross section for \(p + p \rightarrow p \otimes H + \gamma + X\) (where \(H = J/\Psi\) or \(\Upsilon\) and \(\otimes\) denotes a rapidity gap) is computed by factorizing the process into an equivalent photon flux from one proton and the \(\gamma + p \rightarrow H + \gamma + X\) cross section. The photoproduction cross section is calculated using the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization formalism, with the partonic differential cross section for \(\gamma + g \rightarrow H + \gamma\) taken from Mehen (1997) and the gluon distribution from the CTEQ6 parametrization. The authors consider several sets of color-octet matrix elements for both charmonium and bottomonium, and evaluate the rapidity distributions and total cross sections at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) and 14 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for \(J/\Psi + \gamma\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV: \(1.23 \pm 0.50\) pb (BK matrix elements) and 3.62 pb (MEHEN matrix elements).
- Total cross section for \(J/\Psi + \gamma\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV: \(1.90 \pm 0.32\) pb (BK) and 5.60 pb (MEHEN).
- Total cross section for \(\Upsilon + \gamma\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV: \(1.45 \pm 0.13\) fb (BSV matrix elements) and 5.46 fb (BFL matrix elements).
- Event rates for \(J/\Psi + \gamma\) at design luminosity \({\cal L} = 10^7\) mb\(^{-1}\)s\(^{-1}\): approximately \(1 \times 10^5\) events/year at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV and \(2 \times 10^5\) events/year at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV (BK matrix elements).
- Event rates for \(\Upsilon + \gamma\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV: approximately \(1 \times 10^2\) events/year.
- The rapidity distribution at \(Y = 0\) for \(J/\Psi + \gamma\) using the MEHEN matrix element is a factor of about 3 larger than that using the BK matrix element.
- The maximum photon-proton center-of-mass energy at LHC is \(W_{\gamma p} \approx 8390\) GeV (Lorentz factor \(\gamma_L = 7455\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent hadron-hadron interactions at LHC energies, previously applied to exclusive vector meson production (e.g., Vicmagarin and collaborators, 2009) and inclusive heavy quark production, to the new final state of quarkonium plus a photon. The results are compared to inclusive hadroproduction (Li and Wang, 2009) and single diffractive \(H + \gamma\) production via Pomeron exchange (Xu and collaborators, 2009; Machado and collaborators, 2009), where the photoproduction cross sections are a factor of about \(10^3\) smaller than inclusive hadroproduction but a factor of about 8 smaller than diffractive predictions. The analysis enables future experimental separation of these processes via event multiplicity and transverse momentum cuts, and may help constrain the NRQCD color-octet matrix elements and the proton gluon distribution at small \(x\).
