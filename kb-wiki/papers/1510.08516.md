# Nonextensive Nambu Jona-Lasinio model of QCD matter revisited

> We present a revisited version of the nonextensive QCD-based Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model describing the behavior of strongly interacting matter proposed by us some time ago. As before, it is based on the nonextensive generalization of the Boltzmann-Gibbs (BG) statistical mechanics used in the NJL model to its nonextensive version based on Tsallis statistics, but this time it fulfils the basic requirements of thermodynamical consistency. Different ways in which this can be done, connected with different possible choices of the form of the corresponding nonextensive entropies, are presented and discussed in detail. The corresponding results are compared, discussed and confronted with previous findings.

## Key claim

The authors present a thermodynamically consistent reformulation of their earlier nonextensive Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, correcting the previous oversight that the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) must be treated as variable rather than constant, particularly when describing both particles and antiparticles simultaneously.

## Method

The authors generalize the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistical mechanics underlying the standard NJL model to Tsallis statistics, replacing the usual Fermi-Dirac distribution with a \(q\)-deformed version \(\exp_q(-X/T)\). They implement three distinct entropy functionals \(\tilde{S}_q^{(R)}\) (labeled \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\)) corresponding to different choices of the nonextensivity parameter for particles versus antiparticles, and verify thermodynamic consistency by checking the differential relations \(d\varepsilon_q = T ds_q + \mu d\rho_q\) and \(dP_q = s_q dT + \rho_q d\mu\) numerically.

## Key numbers

* Thermodynamic consistency was verified numerically at \(\mu = 322\) MeV and \(T = 60\) MeV for cases with \(q = 1.1\), \(q = 0.9\), and \(q = 1.1\) (case \(c\)), as well as for the extensive case \(q = 1\); all relations were satisfied with accuracy better than \(1\%\).
* The consistency checks were extended over a range of \(q\) values from \(q = 0.8\) to \(q = 1.2\) and remained satisfied.
* The schematic entropy calculations used a \(q\)-independent quark mass \(M_u = 0\) MeV, temperature \(T = 60\) MeV, and chemical potential \(\mu = 322\) MeV.
* Additional entropy calculations used \(q\)-independent quark masses \(M_u = 296\) MeV and \(M_u = 367\) MeV (the maximum obtainable \(u\)-quark mass in this approach).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier \(q\)-NJL model (Rozynek and Wilk, 2015) by correcting its thermodynamic inconsistency, which arose from assuming a constant exponent \(q\) for both particles and antiparticles. The corrected formulation follows the thermodynamic consistency framework of Martinez et al. (2000) and the dual-parameter approach (\(q\) for particles, \(2-q\) for antiparticles) developed by Deppman (2012) and others. The results enable future comparisons with Polyakov-loop extended NJL models and provide a foundation for studying nonextensive effects on susceptibilities and heat capacity in quark matter.
