# The fermion self-energy and damping rate in a hot magnetized plasma

> We derive a general expression for the fermion self-energy in a hot magnetized plasma by using the Landau-level representation. In the one-loop approximation, the Dirac structure of the self-energy is characterized by five different functions that depend on the Landau-level index $n$ and the longitudinal momentum $p_z$. We derive general expressions for all five functions and obtain closed-form expressions for their imaginary parts. The latter receive contributions from three types of on-shell processes, which are interpreted in terms of Landau-level transitions, accompanied by a single photon (gluon) emission or absorption. By making use of the imaginary parts of the self-energy functions, we also derive the Landau-level dependent fermion damping rates $Γ_{n}(p_z)$ and study them numerically in a wide range of model parameters. We also demonstrate that the two-spin degeneracy of the Landau levels is lifted by the one-loop self-energy corrections. While the spin splitting of the damping rates is small, it may be important for some spin and chiral effects. We argue that the general method and the numerical results for the rates can have interesting applications in heavy-ion physics, astrophysics, and cosmology, where strongly magnetized QED or QCD plasmas are ubiquitous.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general expression for the fermion self-energy in a hot magnetized plasma using the Landau-level representation, but the one-loop results are obtained in the Feynman gauge and the authors note that a more refined analysis would require hard-thermal and hard-magnetic loop resummations, which are beyond the scope of this exploratory study.

## Method

The authors start from the coordinate-space representation of the fermion self-energy in a hot magnetized QED-like plasma, remove the Schwinger phase, and perform a Fourier transform on the translation-invariant part. They then project the result onto the Landau-level basis using orthogonality properties of Laguerre polynomials, obtaining closed-form expressions for the imaginary parts of five Dirac-structure functions. The imaginary parts receive contributions from three on-shell processes: transitions to lower Landau levels with photon emission, transitions to higher Landau levels with photon absorption, and particle-antiparticle annihilation with photon emission.

## Key numbers

- Plasma temperature T is assumed in the range 200 MeV to 400 MeV for numerical studies
- Magnetic field strength is taken as |qB| ~ m_π^2, where m_π = 135 MeV
- The fermion mass m̄_0 and charge q are used, with coupling constant α = q^2/(4π)
- For QCD plasma, the coupling constant α is replaced with α_s C_F, where α_s = g_s^2/(4π) and C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)
- The magnetic length ℓ = 1/√|qB| appears throughout the formalism

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the fermion self-energy in strongly magnetized vacuum by Tsai (1974), Jancovici (1969), Gepraegs (1994), Gusynin (1998), and Machet (2015), as well as more recent efforts by Das (2017), Ayala (2021), and Chaudhuri (2023) who calculated the Fourier transform of the translation-invariant part but stopped short of projecting onto Landau levels. The methodology follows an approach previously developed for the quantum Hall effect in graphene by Gorbar (2011) and Shovkovy (2015), and was also used in studies of chiral asymmetry in magnetized QED at nonzero density by Gorbar (2013). The results enable calculation of fermion damping rates, mean free paths, and transport properties in strongly magnetized plasmas relevant to heavy-ion physics, astrophysics, and cosmology.
