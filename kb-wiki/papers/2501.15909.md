# Core screening effect in knockout reactions

> The systematic quenching of spectroscopic factors in terms of separation energy asymmetry in single-nucleon knockout reactions remains a puzzle. We propose a core screening effect to consider the hindrance when strongly bound nucleons in the projectile nucleus are removed by the heavy-ion target. The core screening effect is simulated as a density dependent suppression of single-particle wave functions inside the core of projectile. Our study shows that the parameterized core screening effect can significantly reduce the isospin dependence of quenching factors, offering insights into nuclear reaction mechanisms.

# Core screening effect in knockout reactions

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a parameterized "core screening effect", a density-dependent suppression of single-particle wave functions inside the projectile core, can significantly reduce the isospin dependence of quenching factors in single-nucleon knockout reactions, but the effect is introduced phenomenologically with two free parameters rather than derived from first principles.

## Method
The authors modify the standard eikonal stripping cross-section formula by multiplying the valence nucleon wave function by a density-dependent screening factor f(r) = 1 - α(ρ(r)/ρ₀)^γ, where ρ(r) is the core density and ρ₀ its maximum value. They apply this to knockout reactions of carbon isotopes on heavy-ion targets, using Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions constrained by separation energies and Hartree-Fock radii, with spectroscopic factors from shell model calculations using the WBP interaction.

## Key numbers
- The optimized screening parameters are α = 0.95 and γ = 3.52 for carbon isotope knockout reactions
- The inner part of the core (r < 2 fm) is almost completely screened, while screening vanishes for r > 5 fm
- Without core screening, the slope of the quenching factor R_s versus separation energy asymmetry ΔS is -0.010 MeV⁻¹
- Quenching factors R_s for deeply bound nucleons decrease to 0.2–0.4 compared to approximately 1 for weakly bound nucleons

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of systematic quenching of spectroscopic factors in single-nucleon knockout reactions, where the quenching factor R_s depends on separation energy asymmetry ΔS, a dependence not observed in transfer reactions, (p,pN) reactions (Gomez Ramos, 2018; Paschalis, 2020), or electron-induced knockout (Kramer, 2001). The core screening effect is distinct from the recently proposed core destruction effect (Gomez Ramos, 2023), which Bertulani (2023) found insufficient to explain the binding energy dependence. The approach enables future improvements such as double-folding of target and core densities, and suggests that finite-size effects of the reaction probe are crucial for understanding knockout reactions with heavy-ion targets.
