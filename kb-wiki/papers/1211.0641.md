# An Analytical Solution To A Special Case Of The Weber-Schafheitlin Integral

> Special cases of Weber-Schafheitlin type integrals are evaluated analytically.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed-form analytical expressions for two special cases of the Weber-Schafheitlin integral involving spherical Bessel functions, specifically integrals of the form \(\int_0^\infty j_{\lambda_1}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_2}(k_2 r) / r^{\lambda_3} \, dr\) and \(\int_0^\infty r^{1-\lambda_3} j_{\lambda_1}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_2}(k_2 r) \, dr\), under the condition that the angular momenta \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \lambda_3\) satisfy the triangular conditions imposed by the 3j symbol.

## Method

The author uses the closure relation of spherical Bessel functions to rewrite the target integrals as a double integral, then substitutes a known result for the integral over three spherical Bessel functions from Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane (1991). A standard integral from Gradshteyn and Ryzhik (1965) is used to evaluate the inner radial integral, and the remaining integration over the intermediate wavenumber \(k_3\) is transformed into an integration over \(\Delta = (k_1^2 + k_2^2 - k_3^2)/(2k_1 k_2)\), which is evaluated using the generating function for Legendre polynomials.

## Key numbers

* For the case \(\lambda_3 = 0\) and \(\lambda_1 = \lambda_2 = \lambda\): \(\int_0^\infty j_\lambda(k_1 r) j_\lambda(k_2 r) \, dr = \frac{\pi}{2(2\lambda + 1)} \frac{k_<^\lambda}{k_>^{\lambda+1}}\), where \(k_<\) and \(k_>\) are the smaller and larger of \(k_1\) and \(k_2\).
* For the case \(\lambda_2 = 0\) and \(\lambda_3 = \lambda_1 = \lambda\) with \(k_1 = k_2 = k\): \(\int_0^\infty r^{-\lambda} j_\lambda(kr) j_0(kr) \, dr = \frac{\pi}{2} \frac{k^{\lambda-1}}{(2\lambda + 1)!!}\).
* For the case \(\lambda_3 = 0\) and \(\lambda_1 = \lambda_2 = \lambda\) in the second integral: \(\int_0^\infty r j_\lambda(k_1 r) j_\lambda(k_2 r) \, dr = \frac{1}{2k_1 k_2} Q_\lambda\left(\frac{k_1^2 + k_2^2}{2k_1 k_2}\right)\), where \(Q_\lambda\) is a Legendre function of the second kind.

## Context

This work extends the classic treatment of the discontinuous Weber-Schafheitlin integral found in Watson (1944) and the earlier analytical work by Dixon and Ferrar (1930). The results are derived using the same closure-relation technique previously applied by Mehrem (2011) to integrals over three spherical Bessel functions. The analytical expressions enable direct evaluation of these integrals without numerical quadrature, which is useful in applications involving spherical Bessel functions in quantum scattering and nuclear reaction theory.
