# Effects of the next-to-leading order terms in the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian on the strangeness -1 s-wave meson-baryon interactions

> The meson-baryon interactions in s-wave in the strangeness S=-1 sector are studied using a chiral unitarity approach based on the next-to-leading order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. The model is fitted to the large set of experimental data in different two-body channels. Particular attention is paid to the $Ξ$ hyperon production reaction, $\bar{K} N \rightarrow K Ξ$, where the effect of the next-to-leading order terms in the Lagrangian play a crucial role, since the cross section of this reaction at tree level is zero.

# Effects of the next-to-leading order terms in the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian on the strangeness -1 s-wave meson-baryon interactions

## Key claim
Including next-to-leading order (NLO) terms from the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian in a unitarized coupled-channels approach improves agreement with experimental data for s-wave meson-baryon interactions in the strangeness S=-1 sector, and is essential for describing the $\Xi$ hyperon production reactions $\bar{K} N \rightarrow K \Xi$, whose cross sections are zero at tree level. However, the 7 new NLO parameters are not yet well controlled, and the final goal is to find trustable restrictions on them.

## Method
The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in coupled channels for the 10 possible meson-baryon channels in the S=-1 sector, using a dimensional regularization scheme for the loop function. The interaction kernel is computed from the chiral Lagrangian up to NLO, introducing 7 new parameters ($b_0$, $b_D$, $b_F$, $d_1$, $d_2$, $d_3$, $d_4$) in addition to the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term and 6 subtraction constants. They perform fits to experimental cross sections and branching ratios, comparing a 7-parameter leading-order (WT) fit with a 14-parameter NLO fit.

## Key numbers
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is predicted to be a superposition of two states: one at approximately 1390 MeV with a width of approximately 130 MeV coupling to $\Sigma \pi$, and another at approximately 1420 MeV with a width of approximately 30 MeV coupling to $\bar{K} N$.
- The pion decay constant used in leading-order calculations is taken as $f = 1.15 - 1.2 f_{exp}$, where $f_{exp} = 93.4$ MeV.
- The NLO fit gives branching ratios at threshold: $\gamma = \Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \pi^+ \Sigma^-)/\Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \pi^- \Sigma^+) = 2.36$, $R_n = \Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \pi^0 \Lambda)/\Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \text{neutral states}) = 0.197$, and $R_c = \Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \pi^+ \Sigma^-,\pi^- \Sigma^+)/\Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \text{inelastic channels}) = 0.659$, compared to experimental values $2.36 \pm 0.04$, $0.189 \pm 0.015$, and $0.664 \pm 0.011$ respectively.
- The $\bar{K} N \rightarrow K \Xi$ cross sections are zero at tree level, making them extremely sensitive to NLO corrections.

## Context
This work extends the unitary extension of Chiral Perturbation Theory (U$\chi$PT) originally proposed in the 1990s, which has been used to study the dynamically generated $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance. Previous studies by other groups (ref2, ref3, ref4, ref22 in the text) have begun using NLO terms, but this work is the first to include the $\Xi$ hyperon production reactions $\bar{K} N \rightarrow K \Xi$, which were not considered in those prior studies. The results indicate that higher-spin resonances such as $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$ may need to be included via a special treatment analogous to that performed in ref23 and ref24, which is work in progress.
