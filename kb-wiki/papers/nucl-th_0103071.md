# Baryon Number Fluctuation and the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We show that $ω_B$ or $ω_{\bar B}$, the squared baryon or antibaryon number fluctuation per baryon or antibaryon, is a possible signature for the quark-gluon plasma that is expected to be created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, as it is a factor of three smaller than in an equilibrated hadronic matter due to the fractional baryon number of quarks. Using kinetic equations with exact baryon number conservation, we find that their values in an equilibrated matter are half of those expected from a Poisson distribution. Effects due to finite acceptance and non-zero net baryon number are also studied.

# Baryon Number Fluctuation and the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The squared baryon or antibaryon number fluctuation per baryon or antibaryon (ω_B or ω_anti-B) is a factor of three smaller in an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma than in an equilibrated hadronic matter, due to the fractional baryon number of quarks (b_0 = 1/3 versus b_0 = 1). However, the authors qualify that these fluctuations will be modified during hadronization and subsequent hadronic scatterings, and that ω_anti-B is preferred over ω_B due to contamination from projectile and target nucleons.

## Method
The authors derive a master equation with exact baryon number conservation for baryon-antibaryon pair production and annihilation (m_1 m_2 ↔ B anti-B) in a hadronic matter of net baryon number s. They solve for the equilibrium multiplicity distribution and its generating function, obtaining analytic expressions for the mean baryon/antibaryon numbers and their squared fluctuations. They then generalize the master equation to multiple baryon and antibaryon species to study effects of finite acceptance and non-zero net baryon number.

## Key numbers
- ω_B,eq ≈ b_0/2 in an equilibrated matter, where b_0 = 1/3 in quark-gluon plasma and b_0 = 1 in hadronic matter, giving a factor of 3 difference between the two phases.
- The squared baryon number fluctuation per baryon from a Poisson distribution is ω_B^Poisson = b_0, which is a factor of 2 larger than the equilibrium value of b_0/2.
- For finite acceptance, the correction factor to ω_B_i,eq is (1 - h_i/2) and to ω_anti-B_j,eq is (1 - f_j/2), where h_i and f_j are the fractions of total baryons or antibaryons observed.
- For net baryon number s, the leading corrections to the mean baryon number are ⟨B_i⟩_eq ≈ b_0 h_i √ε (1 + (2s-1)/(4√ε)) and to the mean antibaryon number ⟨anti-B_j⟩_eq ≈ b_0 f_j √ε (1 - (2s+1)/(4√ε)), valid when s/√ε ≪ 1.

## Context
This work extends the proposal of net baryon number fluctuations as a quark-gluon plasma signature by Yuki (2000) and net charge fluctuations by Jeon (2000), focusing instead on baryon and antibaryon number fluctuations which do not require finite phase space due to conservation of total net baryon number. The authors note that a similar result for the factor of 2 reduction from Poisson statistics was obtained by Gavin and Pruneau based on thermodynamic considerations. The paper enables quantitative studies of how hadronization (e.g., via quark coalescence or string fragmentation) and hadronic rescatterings modify the fluctuations, and identifies the need to account for gluonic baryon junctions as a potential complication.
