# Cooling of Neutron Stars and 3P_2 neutron gap

> We study the dependence of the cooling of isolated neutron stars on the magnitude of the $3P_2$ neutron gap. It is demonstrated that our ``Nuclear medium cooling scenario'' is in favor of a suppressed value of the $3P_2$ neutron gap.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario, which successfully fits neutron star cooling data when the $3P_2$ neutron pairing gap is suppressed (by a factor of 0.1), fails to fit the data when the $3P_2$ neutron gap is enhanced by factors up to 50, as suggested by the work of KCTZ (2004). The authors conclude that their scenario favors a suppressed $3P_2$ neutron gap, but note that the result depends on the specific parameterization of the effective pion gap and the crust model used.

## Method

The authors incorporated enhanced $3P_2$ neutron pairing gaps (by factors $f(3P_2,n)=1$ to $100$) and partially suppressed $1S_0$ proton gaps (by factors $f(1S_0,p)=0.1$ to $0.5$) into their existing "nuclear medium cooling" scenario code, using two different gap models (model I from YGKLP (2003) and model II from TT (2004)). They computed cooling curves for isolated neutron stars of various masses (primarily $1.4 M_\odot$ and up to $1.84 M_\odot$) using the HHJ fit of the Urbana-Argonne equation of state, and compared the results against observational cooling data for different choices of the $T_{\rm in}-T_{\rm s}$ relation in the envelope.

## Key numbers

- The $3P_2$ neutron pairing gap was enhanced by a factor $f(3P_2,n)=50$ in the main test cases, with the range $1$ to $100$ explored.
- The $1S_0$ proton gap was suppressed by factors $f(1S_0,p)=0.1$ and $0.5$ in the main test cases.
- For stars with $M \lesssim 1.6 M_\odot$, complete freezing of neutron degrees of freedom occurred for $f(3P_2,n) \gtrsim 20$.
- The density $4.5 n_0$ (where $n_0$ is nuclear saturation density) is achieved in the center of a neutron star of mass $M = 1.7 M_\odot$, above which the $3P_2$ neutron gap reaches zero.
- The critical density for pion condensation assumed by KCTZ (2004) is $n_c = 2 n_0$, which is achieved in the central region of a neutron star with mass $M \sim 0.8 M_\odot$.
- The effective pion gap in the authors' scenario was assumed to have $\widetilde{\omega}^2 \simeq 0.3 m_\pi^2$ (positive) and $\widetilde{\omega}^2 \simeq -0.1 m_\pi^2$ (negative) at the critical point, with a jump.
- The emissivity of the medium-modified proton pair breaking and formation process is given by $\varepsilon_{\nu} \sim 10^{29} (m_N^*/m_N) [p_{F,p}/p_{F,n}(n_0)] [\Delta_{pp}/\text{MeV}]^7 [T/\Delta_{pp}]^{1/2} \xi_{pp}^2$ erg cm$^{-3}$ s$^{-1}$ for $T < T_{cp}$.
- The specific heat of electrons is $c_e \sim 6 \times 10^{19} (n_e/n_0)^{2/3} T_9$ erg cm$^{-3}$ K$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario developed by BGV (2004) and SVSWW (1997), which previously showed that suppressed $3P_2$ neutron gaps (motivated by Schwenk and Friman (2003)) could fit the full set of neutron star cooling data. The paper directly tests the alternative hypothesis of KCTZ (2004), who argued for dramatically enhanced $3P_2$ neutron gaps (1 to 10 MeV) due to pion softening, and finds that such enhancement produces cooling curves that are too rapid to match observations. The results enable future work to focus on the suppressed-gap branch of the nuclear medium cooling scenario, while noting that internal heating mechanisms (as in T (2004)) or quark cores (as in BGV (2004)) could be explored separately.
