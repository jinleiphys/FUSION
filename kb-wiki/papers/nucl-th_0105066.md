# Retardation effects from quark confinement on low-energy nucleon dynamics

> Retardation effects from quark confinement on low-energy nucleon dynamics are investigated within the generalized quantum dynamics (GQD). It is shown that the GQD provides an extension of Hamiltonian dynamics which can describe the evolution of systems of hadrons whose interaction is nonlocal in time due to quark-gluon retardation effects. This is illustrated by the example of the dynamics of nucleons with internal structure described by a constitute quark model. It is shown that quark-gluon retardation effects result in an anomalous off-shell behavior of the two-nucleon amplitudes, and in a lack of continuity of the evolution operator describing nucleon dynamics; for this reason low-energy nucleon dynamics is not governed by the Schr{ö}dinger equation. This is shown to have substantial effects on the dynamics of many nucleon systems. It is also shown that the GQD allows one to take into account retardation effects from quark confinement in describing the short-range part of the $NN$ interaction. PACS number(s): 24.85.+p, 13.75.Cs, 21.30.-x

## Key claim

Quark-gluon retardation effects from confinement cause the evolution operator describing low-energy nucleon dynamics to lack strong continuity, meaning that this dynamics is not governed by the Schrödinger equation and instead requires the generalized quantum dynamics (GQD) framework for a unitary description.

## Method

The authors apply the generalized quantum dynamics (GQD) formalism, which extends Hamiltonian dynamics by replacing the interaction Hamiltonian with a generalized interaction operator \(H_{int}(t_2,t_1)\) that depends on the duration of interaction. They solve the GQD equation of motion for a separable-potential model where the form factor \(\varphi(\mathbf{p})\) has power-law behavior \(|\mathbf{p}|^{-\alpha}\) at large momentum, and they analyze the conditions under which the generalized interaction operator must be nonlocal in time versus instantaneous. The model is used to construct the T-matrix and evolution operator for two-nucleon systems in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- For form factors with \(\alpha > 1/2\), the generalized interaction operator reduces to the instantaneous form \(-2i\delta(t_2-t_1)H_I(t_1)\), yielding standard Hamiltonian dynamics with a separable potential.
- For form factors with \(0 < \alpha < 1/2\), the generalized interaction operator must take the nonlocal form \(a_1 \tau^{-\alpha-1/2} + a_2 \tau^{-2\alpha}\), where \(\tau = t_2 - t_1\).
- The leading asymptotic coefficient for the nonlocal case is \(b_1 = -\frac{1}{2} \cos(\alpha\pi) \pi^{-2} |c_1|^{-2} (2\mu)^{\alpha-3/2}\) for the T-matrix behavior \(t(z) \sim b_1 (-z)^{\alpha-1/2}\) as \(z \to -\infty\).
- The parameter \(a_1\) in the nonlocal interaction is given by \(a_1 = \frac{i}{2}\pi^{-2}|c_1|^{-2}(2\mu)^{\alpha-3/2}\Gamma^{-1}(1-2\alpha)\cos(\alpha\pi)\exp[i(-\alpha/2+1/4)\pi]\).

## Context

This work extends the separable-potential model of nucleon-nucleon interactions to include nonlocality in time, addressing a limitation of quark models (e.g., Refs. [2-9] cited in the paper) that miss retardation effects from gluon exchange. The GQD framework was previously developed by Gainutdinov (Ref. [21]) to handle unitary evolution with nonlocal-in-time interactions, which cannot be described by the Schrödinger equation. The paper enables future studies of three- and many-nucleon systems where quark-gluon retardation may have significant effects, and it provides a foundation for constructing short-range NN interaction models that incorporate confinement-induced retardation.
