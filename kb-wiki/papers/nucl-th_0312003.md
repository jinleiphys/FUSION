# Standard Model Masses and Models of Nuclei

> We note an intriguing coincidence in nuclear levels, that the subshells responsible for doubly magic numbers happen to bracket nuclei at the energies of the Standard Model bosons. This could show that these bosons actually contribute to the effective mesons of nuclear models.

# Standard Model Masses and Models of Nuclei

## Key claim
The subshells responsible for doubly magic numbers (100Sn, 132Sn, 208Pb) happen to start their filling at nuclear masses that coincide with the masses of Standard Model bosons (W, Z, Higgs, and top-quark composites), suggesting these bosons may contribute to the effective mesons of nuclear models via an undetermined many-body effect. The author explicitly states this could be a random numerological coincidence, but notes it covers all cases where naive shell-plus-spin-orbit models do not suffice to justify the observed magicity.

## Method
The author plots diagonal isobaric lines for the masses of W (80.423 GeV), Z (91.1876 GeV), a conjectured Higgs (115 GeV from LEP-2), and the top quark (174.3 GeV) over the traditional N-P plot of nuclides, comparing them to the subshell filling rectangles for the spin-orbit depressed subshells (5g9/2, 6h11/2, 7i13/2) that generate the doubly magic numbers at N,Z = 50, 82, 126. The author also examines error plots from the macroscopic-microscopic mass formula FRDM-1992 and other mass models for anomalies near these mass numbers.

## Key numbers
- Top quark mass: 174.3 GeV (about 187.1 amu), occurring slightly before the doubly magic nucleus 208Pb
- W boson mass: 80.423 GeV (about 86.3 amu), occurring inside the 5g9/2 subshell square for N=50, Z=50
- Z boson mass: 91.1876 GeV (about 97.9 amu), occurring inside the 5g9/2 subshell square for N=50, Z=50
- Higgs boson conjectured mass: 115 GeV (about 123.46 amu) from LEP-2 preliminary measurement, occurring near the doubly magic number 132Sn
- The average of W and Z masses (92.1 amu) roughly closes the diagonal of the 5g9/2 subshell square for N=50, Z=50
- FRDM-1992 mass errors in the W/Z region show black areas at +1.5 MeV and white areas at -2.0 MeV

## Context
This work extends the observation that doubly magic numbers 50, 82, and 126 require abnormally high spin-orbit splitting, which traditional phenomenological Hamiltonians (with empirical spin-orbit coupling and Nilsson terms) and relativistic meson-exchange approaches have struggled to reproduce for high nucleon numbers. The author notes that modern HFB mass formulae fail to reproduce the narrow overlap and priority of certain subshells (e.g., 4d3/2 over 6h11/2). The paper suggests that if the LEP-2 event is confirmed as the Higgs scalar, it could couple to J=0 scalar channels, potentially interfering with the scalar sigma meson (f0(600)) that is a crucial ingredient of relativistic mean-field models. The work enables future tests: either no particle appears near 115 GeV, or quantum hadrodynamics proves able to reproduce spin couplings for double magicity, which would invalidate or make accidental the coincidence. The author also notes that error analysis of mass models by Huertas and others shows slope changes at these mass numbers.
