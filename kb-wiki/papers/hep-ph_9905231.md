# Finding the Pion in the Chiral Random Matrix Vacuum

> The existence of a Goldstone boson is demonstrated in chiral random matrix theory. After determining the effective coupling and calculating the scalar and pseudoscalar propagators, a random phase approximation summation reveals the massless pion and massive sigma modes expected whenever chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that chiral random matrix theory (χRMT) contains a massless pseudoscalar (pion) Goldstone boson and a massive scalar (sigma) mode, but only when the Banks-Casher relation Σ = πρ(0) is used to cancel the constant term in the pseudoscalar propagator; the scalar mass prediction of M² = 6Σ² is explicitly stated as not a precise numerical prediction due to scale ambiguity.

## Method

The authors compute the effective four-quark coupling from the χRMT partition function by integrating over random matrix elements, then calculate the bare scalar and pseudoscalar quark-antiquark correlation functions from the microscopic two-eigenvalue spectral correlation function in the limit of small momentum transfer q → 0. They perform a random phase approximation (RPA) summation of the geometric series Π = g/(1 − Π₀g) to obtain the dressed correlation functions, using the Banks-Casher relation to identify the massless pole in the pseudoscalar channel.

## Key numbers

- Effective coupling strength: g = (2 N_f Σ²)⁻¹, where Σ is the chiral condensate and N_f is the number of flavors.
- Bare scalar correlator (small q): Π₀^S(q²) = +2N_f(π⁴ρ(0)⁴/(3Σ⁴) q² − π²ρ(0)²) + O(q⁴)
- Bare pseudoscalar correlator (small q): Π₀^PS(q²) = −2N_f(π⁴ρ(0)⁴/(3Σ⁴) q² − π²ρ(0)²) + O(q⁴)
- Scalar propagator pole: finite q², with effective mass M² = 6Σ² (not a precise numerical prediction due to scale ambiguity)
- Pseudoscalar propagator: Π^PS(q²) = C/q², exhibiting a pole at q² = 0 (massless Goldstone boson), where C = 3Σ⁴/[2N_fπ⁴ρ(0)⁴]

## Context

This work extends the mean-field treatment of chiral symmetry restoration in χRMT developed by Halasz, Jackson, Shrock, Stephanov, and Verbaarschot (1998) by recovering the Goldstone mode that is expected whenever chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken. The RPA summation method is analogous to that used in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model and instanton vacuum models, where a gap equation conspires with the pseudoscalar correlator to leave a massless mode. The results enable future studies of mesonic excitations within the χRMT framework and confirm that the pattern of spontaneous symmetry breaking in χRMT matches that of dynamical field theoretic approaches.
