# Dilepton production and resonance properties within a new hadronic transport approach in the context of the GSI-HADES experimental data

> The dilepton emission in heavy-ion reactions at low beam energies is examined within a hadronic transport approach. In this article the production of electron-positron pairs from a new approach named SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) is introduced. The dilepton emission is consistently taken into account below the hadronic threshold. The calculations are systematically confronted with HADES data in the kinetic energy range of $1 - 3.5A$ GeV for elementary, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions. The present approach employing a resonance treatment based on vacuum properties is validated by an excellent agreement with experimental data up to system sizes of carbon-carbon collisions. After establishing this well-understood baseline in elementary and small systems, the significance of medium effects is investigated with a coarse-graining approach based on the same hadronic evolution. The effect of explicit in-medium modifications to the vector meson spectral functions is important for dilepton invariant mass spectra in ArKCl and larger systems, even though the transport approach with vacuum properties reveals similar features due the coupling to baryonic resonance and the intrinsically included collisional broadening. This article provides a comprehensive comparison of our calculations with published dielectron results from the HADES collaboration. In addition, the emission of dileptons is predicted in gold-gold and pion-beam experiments for which results are expected soon.

## Key claim

The SMASH hadronic transport approach, using resonance properties based on vacuum spectral functions, achieves excellent agreement with HADES dilepton data for elementary and small systems up to carbon-carbon collisions, but explicit in-medium modifications to vector meson spectral functions become important for larger systems such as ArKCl, even though the vacuum transport already captures similar features through baryonic resonance coupling and collisional broadening.

## Method

The authors implemented dilepton production (electron-positron pairs) within the SMASH hadronic transport framework, which models heavy-ion collisions via the relativistic Boltzmann equation with binary collisions and resonance excitation/decay. All vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) are treated with spectral functions extending below the hadronic threshold down to the dielectron mass, and the dilepton yield is computed perturbatively using the time integration (shining) method. For larger systems, a coarse-graining approach based on the same hadronic evolution is employed to incorporate explicit in-medium spectral functions for vector mesons, allowing direct comparison with the vacuum transport results.

## Key numbers

* Dielectron invariant mass spectra for pp collisions at E_Kin = 1.25 GeV and 2.2 GeV are compared to HADES data (Agakishiev 2009, Agakishiev 2012).
* The eta production cross section in pp collisions is enhanced by a factor of 6.5 for the pn channel relative to pp, based on experimental evidence from Calen (1998).
* The rho meson spectral function shows a kink at approximately 0.3 GeV, corresponding to the hadronic threshold at 2m_pi for the rho -> pi+ pi- decay.
* The eta' form factor is approximated as F_eta'(m_ll) = 1 (QED approximation), while the eta form factor uses Lambda_eta = 0.716 GeV from Arnaldi (2009).
* The omega Dalitz decay form factor uses Lambda_omega = 0.65 GeV and Gamma_omega = 75 MeV from Bratkovskaya (1996).

## Context

This work extends previous dilepton studies using GiBUU (Weil 2012), HSD (Bratkovskaya 2013), and UrQMD (Schmidt 2008) by introducing the SMASH approach with consistent treatment of sub-threshold dilepton contributions for all vector mesons, which UrQMD neglects and GiBUU only applied to the rho meson. The direct comparison between vacuum transport and coarse-grained in-medium results (based on Endres 2015) enables systematic investigation of medium effects on vector meson spectral functions, and the validated baseline provides a foundation for future studies at higher energies (e.g., CBM at FAIR) and for hybrid approaches combining hydrodynamics with hadronic transport.
