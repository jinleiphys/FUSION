# The non-equilibrium attractor for kinetic theory in relaxation time approximation

> I demonstrate that the concept of a non-equilibrium attractor can be extended beyond the lowest-order moments typically considered in hydrodynamic treatments. Using a previously obtained exact solution to the relaxation-time approximation Boltzmann equation for a transversally homogeneous and boost-invariant system subject to Bjorken flow, I derive an equation obeyed by all moments of the one-particle distribution function. Using numerical solutions, I show that, similar to the pressure anisotropy, all moments of the distribution function exhibit attractor-like behavior wherein all initial conditions converge to a universal solution after a short time with the exception of moments which are sensitive to modes with zero longitudinal momentum and high transverse momentum. In addition, I compute the exact solution for the distribution function itself on very fine lattices in momentum space and demonstrate that (a) an attractor for the full distribution function exists and (b) solutions with generic initial conditions relax to this solution, first at low momentum and later at high momentum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the non-equilibrium attractor concept, previously established for low-order moments such as pressure anisotropy, extends to all moments of the one-particle distribution function in relaxation-time approximation kinetic theory under Bjorken flow, with the exception of moments sensitive to modes with zero longitudinal momentum and high transverse momentum, which exhibit slower convergence.

## Method

The author uses a previously obtained semi-analytic exact solution to the 0+1d conformal RTA Boltzmann equation for a transversally homogeneous and boost-invariant system subject to Bjorken flow. This solution takes the form of a one-dimensional integral equation for the energy density, which is solved iteratively on a logarithmically-spaced grid in proper-time using a CUDA-based GPU code. Once the effective temperature is obtained, the author derives and evaluates an integral equation obeyed by all moments of the one-particle distribution function, and also reconstructs the full distribution function on fine momentum-space lattices.

## Key numbers

* The integral equation for the effective temperature was iterated until convergence to sixteen digits at all values of proper time.
* For the attractor solution, 4096 points spaced logarithmically between tau = 0.001 and 1000 fm/c were used, with initial anisotropy tuned to alpha_0 approximately 0.0025.
* For solutions with different initial conditions, 2048 points spaced logarithmically between tau = 0.1 and 100 fm/c were used, with initial temperature T_0 = 1 GeV at tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c.
* For the full distribution function reconstruction, a 500 x 500 grid in scaled transverse and longitudinal momentum was used with |p_T,z|/T less than or equal to 50.
* Generic solutions for moments with m not equal to 0 visibly merge with the attractor after scaled time w-bar approximately greater than 3, while moments with m=0 (such as M^30) merge only after w-bar approximately greater than 6.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the non-equilibrium attractor in relativistic kinetic theory and hydrodynamics, which had previously restricted attention to the pressure anisotropy and low-order moments (Heller 2015, Romatschke 2017, Strickland 2017). The paper uses the exact solution method developed by Florkowski (2013) for the RTA Boltzmann equation. The finding that all moments exhibit attractor behavior on similar timescales (except m=0 moments) suggests that the full one-particle distribution function itself hydrodynamizes, enabling future work to characterize thermalization in heavy-ion collisions more completely.
