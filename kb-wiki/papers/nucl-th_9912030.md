# Low- and intermediate-energy nucleon-nucleon interactions and the analysis of deuteron photodisintegration within the dispersion relation technique

> The nucleon-nucleon interaction in the region of the nucleon kinetic energy up to 1000 MeV is analysed together with the reaction $Œ≥d \to pn$ in the photon energy range $E_Œ≥=0-400$ MeV. Nine nucleon-nucleon $s$-channel partial amplitudes are reconstructed in the dispersion relation $N/D$ method: $^1S_0$, $^3S_1-^3D_1$, $^3P_0$, $^1P_1$, $^3P_1$, $^3P_2$, $^1D_2$, $^3D_2$ and $^3F_3$. Correspondingly, the dispersive representation of partial amplitudes $NŒî\to pn$, $NN^* \to pn$ and $NNœÄ\to pn$ is given. Basing on that, we have performed parameter-free calculation of the amplitude $Œ≥d \to pn$, taking into account: $(i)$ pole diagram, $(ii)$ nucleon-nucleon final-state rescattering $Œ≥d \to pn \to pn$, and $(iii)$ inelastic final-state rescatterings $Œ≥d \to NŒî(1232) \to pn$, $Œ≥d \to NN^*(1400) \to pn$ and $Œ≥d \to NNœÄ\to pn$. The $Œ≥d \to pn$ partial amplitudes for nine above-mentioned channels are found. It is shown that the process $Œ≥d \to pn \to pn$ is significant for the waves $^1S_0$, $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$, at $E_Œ≥ =50 -100$ MeV, while $Œ≥d \to NŒî\to pn$ for the waves $^3P_2$, $^1D_2$,$^3F_3$ dominates at $E_Œ≥ > 300$ MeV. Meson exchange current contributions into the deuteron disintegration are estimated: they are significant at $E_Œ≥=100-400$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dispersion relation N/D method can simultaneously describe nucleon-nucleon scattering up to 1000 MeV and deuteron photodisintegration up to 400 MeV, with the key condition that the inelasticities in the nucleon-nucleon amplitude are primarily due to baryon excitations (such as NŒî and NN*) rather than prompt meson production, and that the photodisintegration calculation is parameter-free once the nucleon-nucleon vertex functions are fixed from phase shift data.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation analysis of deuteron photodisintegration previously carried out by Anisovich and collaborators (Alex1, Alex2) from low energies into the region EŒ≥ ‚âà 250‚Äì400 MeV where Œî(1232) and N*(1400) production becomes important. It complements the extensive studies of deuteron photodisintegration using quantum mechanical and Feynman diagram approaches by Gross (GrossHol), Laget (Laget), and Levchuk (Levchuk), particularly by providing a framework where meson exchange currents arise naturally from anomalous t- and u-channel singularities rather than being inserted as separate diagrams. The successful description of deuteron elastic form factors using the same dispersion relation technique (Akms) motivates this application to the two-nucleon breakup reaction, and the approach enables future extension to other two-nucleon processes with controlled analytic properties.
