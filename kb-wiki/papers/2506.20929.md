# Iterative Harrow-Hassidim-Lloyd quantum algorithm for solving resonances with eigenvector continuation

> We propose a novel quantum algorithm for solving nuclear resonances, which is based on the iterative Harrow-Hassidim-Lloyd algorithm and eigenvector continuation with complex scaling. To validate this approach, we compute the resonant states of $α-α$ system and achieve results in good agreement with traditional methods. Our study offers a new perspective on calculating eigenvalues of non-Hermitian operators and lays some groundwork for further exploration of nuclear resonances using quantum computing.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a quantum algorithm that combines an iterative Harrow-Hassidim-Lloyd (IHHL) algorithm with eigenvector continuation and complex scaling to solve for nuclear resonant states, but the validation is limited to a quantum simulation of the alpha-alpha system rather than execution on actual quantum hardware, and the bound-state training vectors used for eigenvector continuation are obtained from classical methods rather than from quantum algorithms.

## Method

The authors transform the non-Hermitian complex-scaled Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem into an iterative linear system by constructing a Hermitian matrix A from the non-Hermitian Hamiltonian and using the HHL algorithm to solve for updated wave functions until convergence. They combine this iterative HHL approach with eigenvector continuation, where bound-state wave functions from a training parameter range are used to construct a reduced-dimension complex-symmetric matrix via the complex scaling method, with the inner product replaced by the c-product. The algorithm is tested on the alpha-alpha system using the pyqpanda quantum simulation interface with 1-bit precision.

## Key numbers

- The target resonant state of the alpha-alpha G-wave system has energy 11.8079 - 1.8085i MeV as computed by the R-matrix method.
- The iterative HHL algorithm converges to the first eigenvalue after 6 iterations and to the second eigenvalue after 5 iterations, both to a precision of epsilon = 10^{-4} MeV.
- The iterative HHL yields the resonant energy as 11.8211 - 1.8107i MeV, compared to 11.8079 - 1.8110i MeV from direct diagonalization.
- The complex scaling angle is set to 20 degrees, with 8 training parameters uniformly distributed in the interval [1.45, 1.75] for eigenvector continuation.
- The Gaussian potential parameters are V0 = -122.6225 MeV and a = 2.132 fm, taken from Buck (1977).

## Context

This work extends prior efforts in applying quantum computing to nuclear physics, particularly building on the authors' own previous work on non-Hermitian nuclear resonant states (Zhang 2024). The algorithm combines the complex scaling method, originally developed by Aguilar (1971) and Balslev (1971), with eigenvector continuation as introduced by PhysRevLett.121.032501, which has been used for building emulators in nuclear scattering by Melendez (2022) and Furnstahl (2020). The approach enables future application of the trap method for scattering phase shifts in the complex domain and offers a pathway for studying nuclear resonances on quantum computers, though actual hardware implementation remains an open challenge.
