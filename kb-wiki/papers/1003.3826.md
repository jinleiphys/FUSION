# Towards a high precision calculation for the pion-nucleus scattering lengths

> We calculate the leading isospin conserving few-nucleon contributions to pion scattering on $^2$H, $^3$He, and $^4$He. We demonstrate that the strong contributions to the pion-nucleus scattering lengths can be controlled theoretically to an accuracy of a few percent for isoscalar nuclei and of 10% for isovector nuclei. In particular, we find the $π$-$^3$He scattering length to be $(62 \pm 4\pm 7)\times 10^{-3} m_π^{-1}$ where the uncertainties are due to ambiguities in the $π$-N scattering lengths and few-nucleon effects, respectively. To establish this accuracy we need to identify a suitable power counting for pion-nucleus scattering. For this purpose we study the dependence of the two-nucleon contributions to the scattering length on the binding energy of $^2$H. Furthermore, we investigate the relative size of the leading two-, three-, and four-nucleon contributions. For the numerical evaluation of the pertinent integrals, aMonte Carlo method suitable for momentum space is devised. Our results show that in general the power counting suggested by Weinberg is capable to properly predict the relative importance of $N$-nucleon operators, however, it fails to capture the relative strength of $N$- and $(N+1)$-nucleon operators, where we find a suppression by a factor of 5 compared to the predicted factor of 50. The relevance for the extraction of the isoscalar $π$-N scattering length from pionic $^2$H and $^4$He is discussed. As a side result, we show that beyond the calculation of the $π$-$^2$H scattering length is already beyond the range of applicability of heavy pion effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the strong contributions to pion-nucleus scattering lengths can be controlled theoretically to an accuracy of a few percent for isoscalar nuclei and 10% for isovector nuclei, but only after identifying that Weinberg counting (rather than Q-counting) provides a reliable power counting scheme, and that the suppression between N- and (N+1)-nucleon operators is a factor of 5 rather than the factor of 50 predicted by Weinberg counting.

## Method

The authors calculated the leading isospin-conserving few-nucleon contributions to pion scattering on deuterium, helium-3, and helium-4 using chiral perturbation theory. They evaluated expectation values of tree-level pion-nucleus amplitudes with few-nucleon wave functions obtained from both phenomenological potentials (AV18, Nijmegen 93, CD-Bonn) and chiral nuclear interactions at LO, NLO, and N2LO, using a Monte Carlo method suitable for momentum space integrals. The binding energy dependence of two-nucleon contributions was studied by artificially varying the deuteron binding energy to test the predictions of Weinberg counting versus Q-counting.

## Key numbers

- The π-³He scattering length is (62 ± 4 ± 7) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹, where the first uncertainty comes from ambiguities in the π-N scattering lengths and the second from few-nucleon effects.
- The leading two-nucleon diagram contributes about 20 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ to the π-²H scattering length.
- The estimated uncertainty for isoscalar targets is 1 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ for π-²H and 2 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ for π-⁴He scattering.
- The suppression factor between N- and (N+1)-nucleon operators is found to be 5, compared to the predicted factor of 50 from Weinberg counting.
- The binding momenta γ for ²H, ³He, and ⁴He are 46 MeV, 82 MeV, and 167 MeV respectively.
- The relative suppression of the triple scattering diagram compared to the leading two-nucleon diagram is of order m_π²/(16f_π²) ≈ 0.14.

## Context

This work extends the systematic chiral perturbation theory approach to pion-nucleus scattering initiated by Weinberg (1992) and refined by Beane (2002), who proposed Q-counting based on the small binding energy of the deuteron. The authors resolve a controversy by showing that Weinberg counting is more reliable than Q-counting, contrary to claims in Beane (2002). The results enable a more accurate extraction of the isoscalar π-N scattering length from pionic deuterium and helium-4 data, and provide the first explicit calculation of three-nucleon corrections to pion-nucleus scattering. As a side result, the authors show that the physical deuteron binding energy is already beyond the range of applicability of heavy pion effective field theory for the π-²H scattering length calculation.
