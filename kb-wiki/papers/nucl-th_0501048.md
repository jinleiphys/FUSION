# Anomalous behavior of the first excited 0$^{+}$ state in $N \approx Z$ nuclei

> A study of the energies of the first excited $0^+$ states in all even-even $Z$ $\geq$ 8 nuclei reveals an anomalous behavior in some nuclei with $N$ = $Z$, $Z$ $\pm$ 2. We analyze these irregularities in the framework of the shell model. It is shown that proton-neutron correlations play an important role in this phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper identifies an anomaly in the ratio \(R_{0/2} = E(0_2^+)/E(2_1^+)\) for certain light even-even nuclei with \(N = Z\) or \(N = Z \pm 2\), but explicitly notes that not all such nuclei exhibit the anomaly; it occurs only when there is an open \(1d_{5/2}\) or \(1f_{7/2}\) shell (except for \(N,Z = 22\)), and the anomaly is attributed to the combined effects of the \(T=0\) and \(T=1\) components of the proton-neutron interaction.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental energies of the first excited \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states for all even-even nuclei with \(Z \geq 8\) and plotted the ratio \(R_{0/2}\) against \(E(2_1^+)\) and against \(R_{4/2} = E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)\). They performed full shell model calculations using the Wildenthal interaction for \(sd\)-shell nuclei (model space: \(1d_{5/2}, 2s_{1/2}, 1d_{3/2}\)) and the KB3 interaction for \(fp\)-shell nuclei (model space: \(1f_{7/2}, 2p_{3/2}, 1f_{5/2}, 2p_{1/2}\)), with an extrapolation method for the heaviest Fe isotopes. They then decomposed the shell model interaction into isoscalar (\(T=0\)) and isovector (\(T=1\)) parts and computed correlation energy differences \(\delta \langle O \rangle = \langle O \rangle_{ex} - \langle O \rangle_{gr}\) for the ground and first excited \(0^+\) states.

## Key numbers

- Seven nuclei were identified as anomalous: \(^{20,22}\)Ne, \(^{22,24}\)Mg, \(^{48,50}\)Cr, and \(^{52}\)Fe, with \(Z\) and/or \(N = 10, 12, 24, 26\).
- The ratio \(R_{0/2}\) for \(^{20,22}\)Ne and \(^{48,50}\)Cr is "almost two times" that of the heavier Ne and Cr isotopes.
- For the \(N = Z\) \(sd\)-shell nuclei, \(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{24}\)Mg exhibit large \(R_{0/2}\) ratios compared to neighboring \(N = Z\) nuclei such as \(^{36}\)Ar.
- The \(T=1\) component of the correlation energy difference in Ne isotopes remains relatively constant at \(\sim 3.5\) MeV for \(N = 10-14\), dropping to \(\sim 1\) MeV for \(N = 16\).
- The \(R_{4/2}\) values for the anomalous nuclei are all greater than 2.4, indicating softness to deformation.

## Context

This work extends the study of excited \(0^+\) states in light nuclei, which have traditionally been interpreted as \(\beta\) vibrations in deformed nuclei (BandM) or as collective excitations built on the \(\gamma\) vibration (RandB, Burke, Kumar, Gunther, Garrett). The paper builds on prior studies of proton-neutron correlations in \(N \approx Z\) nuclei, including singularities in the \(p-n\) interaction energy (Zhang 1999, Zamfir 1999, Kaneko 2002), alpha-like correlations (Hasegawa 1990), the Wigner energy (Satula 1997), and degenerate \(T=0\) and \(T=1\) states in odd-odd \(N=Z\) nuclei (Zeldes 1993, Brentano 1994, Vogel 1996, Macchiavelli 2000, Satula 1998, Kaneko 2004). The findings enable future exotic beam experiments to search for additional examples of this anomaly in \(N \approx Z\) nuclei and to test the role of \(T=0\) correlations in shell model calculations.
