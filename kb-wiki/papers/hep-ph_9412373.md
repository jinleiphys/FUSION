# From Effective Lagrangians, to Chiral Bags, to Skyrmions with the Large-N_c Renormalization Group

> We explicitly relate effective meson-baryon Lagrangian models, chiral bags, and Skyrmions in the following way. First, effective Lagrangians are constructed in a manner consistent with an underlying large-N_c QCD. An infinite set of graphs dress the bare Yukawa couplings at *leading* order in 1/N_c, and are summed using semiclassical techniques. What emerges is a picture of the large-N_c baryon reminiscent of the chiral bag: hedgehog pions for r > 1/Λpatched onto bare nucleon degrees of freedom for r < 1/Λ, where the ``bag radius'' 1/Λis the UV cutoff on the graphs. Next, a novel renormalization group (RG) is derived, in which the bare Yukawa couplings, baryon masses and hyperfine baryon mass splittings run with Λ. Finally, this RG flow is shown to act as a *filter* on the renormalized Lagrangian parameters: when they are fine-tuned to obey Skyrme-model relations the continuum limit Λ--> \infty exists and is, in fact, a Skyrme model; otherwise there is no continuum limit.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a precise equivalence between effective meson-baryon Lagrangian models and Skyrme models at leading order in the 1/N_c expansion, but only when the renormalized Lagrangian parameters are fine-tuned to obey Skyrme-model relations; otherwise the continuum limit (cutoff removed) does not exist, with the large-N_c renormalization group acting as a "filter" that blocks the path to the continuum except for a measure-zero subset of parameter space.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians for mesons and baryons consistent with large-N_c QCD selection rules, then sum the infinite set of leading-order Feynman diagrams (meson trees attached to a baryon line) using semiclassical techniques. They introduce an ultraviolet cutoff Λ (interpreted as the inverse bag radius) and derive a renormalization group flow for the bare Yukawa couplings, baryon masses, and hyperfine mass splittings as functions of Λ. The RG flow is studied in a series of models with different mesonic Lagrangians (free pion, nonlinear sigma model, and Skyrme term), and the existence of a continuum limit Λ → ∞ is examined.

## Key numbers

- Critical cutoff value for the nonlinear sigma model (no Skyrme term): Λ_crit ≅ 340 MeV, beyond which the large-N_c renormalization group cannot be pushed.
- In the analytically soluble 2+1 dimensional O(3) sigma model, the bare coupling g_bare(Λ) flows to zero like Λ^{-4}.
- The Δ → Nπ decay width calculated from the Yukawa coupling is within a few MeV of its experimental value of 120 MeV.
- Higher baryon widths: Γ_{5/2 → Δπ} ≈ 800 MeV, Γ_{7/2 → 5/2 + π} ≈ 2600 MeV, Γ_{9/2 → 7/2 + π} ≈ 6400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the large-N_c approach to baryons pioneered by 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979), and builds on the authors' earlier Letter (Dorey, Hughes, Mattis, 1994). It connects to the chiral bag models of Chodos and Thorn (1975), Rho, Goldhaber, and Brown (1983), and Goldstone and Jaffe (1983), as well as the Cheshire cat principle of Nielsen and collaborators. The paper enables a systematic understanding of when effective Lagrangian descriptions of baryons can be replaced by soliton models, and provides a renormalization-group interpretation of the bag radius in chiral bag models.
