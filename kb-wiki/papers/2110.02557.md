# Understanding the systematic differences in extractions of the proton electric form factors at low-$Q^2$

> Systematic differences exist between values of the proton's electric form factors in the low-$Q^2$ region extracted by different experimental and theoretical groups, though they are all making use of basically the same electron-proton scattering data. To try understand the source of these differences, we make use of the analytically well-behaved rational (N=1, M=1) function, a predictive function that can be reasonably used for extrapolations at $Q^{2} \rightarrow 0$. First, we test how well this deceptively simple two-parameter function describes the extremely complex and state-of-the-art dispersively improved chiral effective field theory calculations. Second, we carry out a complete re-analysis of the 34 sets of eletron-proton elastic scattering cross-section data of the Mainz A1 Collaboration with its unconstrained 31 normalization parameters up to $Q^{2} = 0.5~{\rm (GeV/c)^{2}}$. We find that subtle shifts in the normalization parameters can result in relatively large changes in the extracted physical qualities. In conclusion, we show that by simply using a well-behaved analytic function, the apparent discrepancy between recent form-factor extractions can be resolved.

## Key claim

By re-analyzing the Mainz A1 electron-proton elastic scattering cross-section data using a simple two-parameter rational function (Rational (1,1)) and allowing subtle shifts in the 29 independent normalization parameters, the apparent discrepancy between the proton electric form factor extracted by the Mainz A1 and PRad collaborations can be resolved.

## Method

The authors first validated the Rational (1,1) function by fitting it to pseudo-data generated from the dispersively improved chiral effective field theory (DIχEFT) parametrizations of Alarcon et al. (2017, 2018) over a range of proton radii from 0.80 fm to 0.88 fm. They then performed a complete re-analysis of 1285 out of 1422 Mainz A1 cross-section data points (up to Q² = 0.5 (GeV/c)²) using the Rational (1,1) form for both G_E^p and G_M^p, fitting with 29 unconstrained normalization parameters.

## Key numbers

- The Mainz A1 data set contains 1422 cross-section data points in the Q² range from 0.0038 to 0.9772 (GeV/c)², with 538 data points in the overlap Q² region with PRad (0.0038 to 0.0582 (GeV/c)²).
- The re-analysis used 1285 out of 1422 cross-section points (90% of the data) up to Q² = 0.5 (GeV/c)², with 29 independent normalization parameters.
- The PRad experiment measured down to a forward scattering angle of approximately 0.7° and covered Q² from 0.0002 to 0.0582 (GeV/c)².
- In the overlap Q² region above 0.0038 (GeV/c)², G_E^p from fits to Mainz and earlier data falls about 1.5% faster than the PRad data.
- The two-photon-exchange correction in the PRad kinematic range was estimated to be less than 0.2% of the elastic scattering cross section.

## Context

This work extends the analysis framework developed by the PRad collaboration (Xiong et al., 2019, 2020; Yan et al., 2018) and addresses the discrepancy between the Mainz A1 (Bernauer et al., 2010, 2013) and PRad extractions of the proton electric form factor. The study is motivated by the DIχEFT calculations of Alarcon et al. (2017, 2018, 2020), which provide a state-of-the-art theoretical parametrization of nucleon form factors. The results enable a consistent extraction of the proton charge radius and suggest that upcoming experiments such as PRad-II may further resolve remaining uncertainties.
