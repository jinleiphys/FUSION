# Coulomb interacting Bose-Einstein correlations in Fourier space

> In high-energy heavy-ion physics experiments, a state of matter is created that existed in the early Universe: the quark-gluon plasma. This strongly interacting matter exists in today's experiments only within a range of a few femtometers and for a duration of a few femtometers per speed of light, making its resolution with optical tools impossible. However, there is a method that allows for a closer look into the structure of the quark-gluon plasma: femtoscopy. Initially used in astronomy, femtoscopy is based on the quantum mechanical indistinguishability of identical particles, which causes them to arrive at detectors in a correlated manner. The measurable correlation is related to the spacetime structure of the particle-emitting source, which in heavy-ion physics is the quark-gluon plasma created in collisions. For free particles, a relatively simple relationship exists between the source and the correlation (essentially a Fourier transform). However, this relationship becomes complex when accurately accounting for the repulsive Coulomb interaction between final-state electrically charged particles. Our paper presents a new method that is more precise than previously used ones, yet less computationally demanding, especially for the exotic source function shapes. Mathematically, the method is interesting because it exactly handles many emerging integrals and limits. Practically, it is ready for use in experimental analyses.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for calculating Coulomb-interacting Bose-Einstein correlation functions that is more precise than previously used approaches yet less computationally demanding, specifically for exotic source function shapes such as Lévy-stable distributions. The method works by first performing the Fourier transform of the interacting two-particle wave function and then applying this result to the Fourier transform of the source function, rather than performing a direct numerical integration.

## Method

The authors start from the Koonin-Pratt formula for the correlation function, which involves an integral over the pair distribution function and the squared two-particle Coulomb wave function. They express the pair distribution function as the Fourier transform of another integrable function, then interchange the order of integration using a regularization factor \(e^{-\lambda r}\) and apply the Lebesgue and Fubini theorems. For the spherically symmetric case, they perform the angular integrals analytically and then evaluate the resulting expressions using techniques from Nordsieck (1954) and Nagy (2023).

## Key numbers

* Correlation functions for pions and kaons were calculated for source radii \(R\) ranging from \(3\text{ fm}\) to \(12\text{ fm}\) and Lévy stability indices \(\alpha\) from \(0.6\) to \(2\).
* The difference \(\Delta C_2(Q)\) between the numerical table method (Kincses, 2019) and the new wave-function Fourier method is shown for 6 different \(\alpha\) values and two \(R\) values, for both pions and kaons.
* With the new method, correlation functions can be calculated in just a few minutes on a desktop computer, compared to days for the previously used numerical integration approach.

## Context

This work extends the use of Lévy-stable source functions in femtoscopy, which were previously applied to provide statistically acceptable descriptions of experimental data (Csörgő, 2003; Csanád, 2024). The new method improves upon the Gamow-factor approximation (adequate for small sources) and the Koonin-Pratt formula (Koonin, 1977) by offering a computationally efficient alternative to the direct numerical integration approach (Kincses, 2019). The method is currently limited to spherically symmetric sources, but a more general procedure exists and its numerical implementation is identified as future work.
