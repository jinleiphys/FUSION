# Electromagnetic Reactions and Few-Nucleon Dynamics

> We present an update on recent theoretical studies of electromagnetic reactions obtained by using the Lorentz integral transform method. The 4He nucleus will be the main focus of this report: results for the photo-disintegration and the electro-disintegration processes will be shown, as well as a recent calculation of polarizability effects in muonic atoms. We also discuss the exciting possibility to investigate inelastic reactions for medium-mass nuclei in coupled-cluster theory, highlighted by the recent application to the 16O photo-nuclear cross section.

## Key claim
The Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method, combined with hyper-spherical harmonics or coupled-cluster theory, enables ab-initio calculations of electromagnetic response functions for light and medium-mass nuclei, but the theoretical sensitivity to three-body forces in the 4He photo-absorption cross section (about 10% at the peak) is smaller than the spread among existing experimental data, preventing discrimination among three-nucleon force models.

## Method
The LIT method avoids explicit calculation of continuum final states by introducing an integral transform of the response function with a Lorentzian kernel, converting the problem into a bound-state-like Schrödinger equation. For few-nucleon systems (A=3–7), the LIT equation is solved using hyper-spherical harmonics expansions with an effective interaction (EIHH) and the Lanczos algorithm. For medium-mass nuclei, the LIT method is merged with coupled-cluster theory using singles-and-doubles excitations, solving both left and right Schrödinger-like equations with a generalized Lanczos algorithm for non-symmetric matrices.

## Key numbers
- For 4He photo-absorption, the variation of the cross section with different Hamiltonians (AV18+UIX, chiral N3LO+N2LO, central 3NF) is about 10% at the peak, while the data from Shima et al. are a factor of 2 smaller than all other measurements.
- For muonic 4He+ nuclear polarization corrections: δ(0)E1 = -4.418 meV (AV18+UIX, Kmax=22) and -4.701 meV (chiral N3LO+N2LO, Kmax=20); δ(0)C = 0.512 meV (AV18+UIX) and 0.546 meV (chiral). The numerical error from model-space truncation is below 1%, while the potential dependence is roughly 6%.
- For 4He longitudinal response at q=50 MeV/c, three-body forces lead to up to a 50% quenching effect; a 10% difference in SL is obtained using different three-body Hamiltonians.
- For 16O photo-nuclear cross section, the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule [59.74 × NZ/A MeV mb] is exhausted by integrating up to 40 MeV; integrating up to 100 MeV gives an enhancement factor of 0.57–0.58.

## Context
This work extends previous LIT calculations for few-nucleon systems (A=3–7) using hyper-spherical harmonics and no-core shell model approaches, and presents the first application of the LIT method merged with coupled-cluster theory to reach medium-mass nuclei (16O). The 4He transition form factor calculation shows dramatic dependence on the three-body Hamiltonian, motivating new experimental activity at LNS Catania with the MAGNEX spectrometer. The muonic atom polarizability corrections improve on earlier estimates by Bernabeu (1973), Rinker (1976), and Friar (1977), which had 20% errors based on experimental data. The 16O results are compared to experimental data from Ahrens (1975) and Ishkhanov (2002), and to self-consistent mean-field calculations such as Erler (2011) and Ly (2012).
