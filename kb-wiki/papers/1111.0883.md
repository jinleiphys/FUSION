# Effects of the U-boson on the inner edge of neutron star crusts

> We explore effects of the light vector $U$-boson, which is weakly coupled to nucleons, on the transition density $ρ_{t}$ and pressure $P_{t}$ at the inner edge separating the liquid core from the solid crust of neutron stars. Three methods, i.e., the thermodynamical approach, the curvature matrix approach and the Vlasov equation approach are used to determine the transition density $ρ_{t}$ with the Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interactions. We find that the $ρ_{t}$ and $P_{t}$ depend on not only the ratio of coupling strength to mass squared of the $U$-boson $g^{2}/μ^{2}$ but also its mass $μ$ due to the finite range interaction from the $U$-boson exchange. In particular, our results indicate that the $ρ_{t}$ and $P_{t}$ are sensitive to both $g^{2}/μ^{2}$ and $μ$ if the $U$-boson mass $μ$ is larger than about 2 MeV. Furthermore, we show that both $g^{2}/μ^{2}$ and $μ$ can have significant influence on the mass-radius relation and the crustal fraction of total moment of inertia of neutron stars. In addition, we study the exchange term contribution of the $U$-boson based on the density matrix expansion method, and demonstrate that the exchange term effects on the nuclear matter equation of state as well as the $ρ_{t}$ and $P_{t}$ are generally negligible.

## Key claim

The transition density and pressure at the inner edge of neutron star crusts depend not only on the ratio of coupling strength to mass squared of the U-boson (g²/μ²) but also on its mass μ itself when μ exceeds about 2 MeV, due to the finite-range interaction from U-boson exchange; the exchange term contribution of the U-boson to the nuclear matter equation of state and to the transition density and pressure is generally negligible for the parameter values considered.

## Method

The authors used three methods to determine the core-crust transition density ρt in neutron stars: the thermodynamical approach, the curvature matrix approach, and the Vlasov equation approach, all based on Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interactions. The U-boson contribution was incorporated by adding a Yukawa potential to the standard Skyrme interaction, with the direct term treated exactly and the exchange term approximated using the density matrix expansion method. The calculations were performed for β-equilibrium npe matter with electrons treated as a free Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

- The transition density ρt and pressure Pt are sensitive to both g²/μ² and μ when the U-boson mass μ is larger than about 2 MeV.
- The neutron drip-out density ρout is relatively well determined to be about 4×10¹¹ g/cm³ (Rus06).
- The incompressibility K0 of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ρ0 has been determined to be 240±20 MeV from analyses of nuclear giant monopole resonances.
- The slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy at ρ0 is constrained to about 60±30 MeV from various experimental data.
- The nuclear symmetry energy at ρ0 is known to be around 30 MeV from the empirical liquid-drop mass formula.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on U-boson effects on nuclear matter equation of state and neutron star structure by Kri09, Wen09, Zha11, and Wen11, which only considered the direct term contribution and found the EOS depends only on g²/μ². The present paper introduces the exchange term contribution via the density matrix expansion method and demonstrates its negligible effect, while showing that the finite-range nature of the U-boson interaction introduces a dependence on μ itself. This enables more precise constraints on U-boson properties from neutron star observations, particularly the mass-radius relation and crustal fraction of total moment of inertia, and connects to constraints from neutron-proton and neutron-lead scatterings (Nes08, Kam08) and spectroscopy of antiproton atoms (Pok06).
