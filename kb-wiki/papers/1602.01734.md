# A model for particle production in nuclear reactions at intermediate energies: application to C-C collisions at 95 MeV/nucleon

> A model describing nuclear collisions at intermediate energies is presented and the results are compared with recently measured double differential cross sections in C-C reactions at 95 MeV/nucleon. Results show the key role played by geometrical effects and the memory of the entrance channel, in particular the momentum distributions of the two incoming nuclei. Special attention is paid to the description of processes occurring at mid-rapidity. To this end, a random particle production mechanism by means of a coalescence process in velocity space is considered in the overlap region of the two interacting nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper presents a semi-microscopic model (SLIIPIE) that reproduces measured double differential cross sections for C-C collisions at 95 MeV/nucleon, with the key hedge being that the model assumes a "sudden and frozen approximation" where particle production occurs so rapidly that momentum distributions retain strong memory of the entrance channel, rather than requiring full thermalization.

## Method
The model uses a participant-spectator geometrical separation based on impact parameter, then applies a random coalescence process in momentum space with a cutoff parameter p_cut = 225 MeV/c to form clusters from nucleons in the overlap region. In-medium nucleon-nucleon collisions are applied with an average of x_coll = 1.5 collisions per participant, using free nucleon-nucleon angular distributions corrected by Pauli blocking. Excited fragments then decay via a Fermi Break-Up model using discrete known excited states and statistical phase space sampling.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 95 MeV/nucleon for C-C collisions
- Coalescence momentum cutoff: p_cut = 225 MeV/c (close to Fermi momentum ~250 MeV/c)
- Average number of collisions per participant: x_coll = 1.5 (determined from comparison with proton angular distributions)
- Free nucleon-nucleon cross section at this energy: sigma_NN_free = 56.15 mb (average of sigma_nn = 32.04 mb and sigma_np = 80.25 mb)
- Pauli blocking factor: alpha_Pauli = 0.44, giving medium cross section sigma_NN_medium = 24.8 mb
- Temperature parameterization: T_0 = 7.5 MeV (b=0) and T_bmax = 4.5 MeV (b=bmax)
- Nuclear radius formula: R = 1.28*A^(1/3) - 0.76 + 0.8*A^(-1.3) fm
- For b=0 in C-C: R_max = 2.52 fm, rho_0 = 0.168 fm^(-3)
- Model achieves the best chi-square among four models compared (QMD, INCL, HIPSE, SLIIPIE)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous benchmarking study (Dudouet 2014) which showed that existing GEANT4 models (QMD and INCL) had difficulty reproducing mid-rapidity particle production in C-C reactions at 95 MeV/nucleon. The model improves upon fully dynamical approaches by using a sudden coalescence mechanism rather than treating cluster formation on longer time scales. The results enable future extension to other nuclear systems and energies, and suggest that the "sudden approximation" is a key ingredient for reproducing mid-rapidity data that dynamical models miss.
