# Multiple Parton Scattering in Nuclei: Parton Energy Loss

> Multiple parton scattering and induced parton energy loss is studied in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei. The effect of multiple scattering of a highly off-shell quark and the induced parton energy loss is expressed in terms of the modification to the quark fragmentation functions. We derive such modified quark fragmentation functions and their QCD evolution equations in DIS using generalized factorization of higher twist parton distributions. We consider double-hard, hard-soft parton scattering as well as their interferences in the same framework. The final result, which depends on both the diagonal and off-diagonal twist-four parton distributions in nuclei, demonstrates clearly the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal interference features and predicts a unique nuclear modification of the quark fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

Multiple parton scattering and induced gluon radiation in deeply inelastic scattering off nuclei modify the quark fragmentation functions through an additional term in the DGLAP evolution equations, with the nuclear modification depending quadratically on nuclear size due to Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal interference, provided the formation time of the emitted gluon is not larger than the nuclear size.

## Method

The authors derive modified quark fragmentation functions and their QCD evolution equations for deeply inelastic scattering off nuclei using generalized factorization of twist-four parton distributions, considering double-hard, hard-soft, and interference contributions from double parton scattering in an axial gauge. They perform collinear expansion of the partonic hard part around zero relative transverse momentum, convert gluon fields into field strength tensors, and evaluate contour integrations over momentum fractions to isolate the leading higher-twist contributions that are enhanced by nuclear size. The calculation includes 23 cut diagrams representing double scattering processes, their interferences, and interferences between single and triple scattering, retaining only leading logarithmic contributions in the limit of small gluon transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The parameter for twist expansion of fragmentation processes inside a nucleus is \(\alpha_s A^{2/3}/Q^2\), compared to \(\alpha_s A^{1/3}/Q^2\) for processes with large final transverse momentum as studied by Luo, Qiu and Sterman (1998).
- The minimum transverse momentum of the radiated gluon due to LPM interference is \(\ell_T^2 \sim Q^2/MR_A \sim Q^2/A^{1/3}\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass and \(R_A\) is the nuclear radius.
- The formation time of the emitted gluon is \(1/x_L p^+ = 2q^- z(1-z)/\mu^2\), where \(\mu\) is the factorization scale and \(z\) is the fractional momentum of the emitted gluon.
- The color factor for quark-gluon coupling is \(C_F = (N_c^2-1)/2N_c = 4/3\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized factorization approach of Luo, Qiu and Sterman (1998) on nuclear dependence of jet cross sections in deeply inelastic scattering, but focuses on soft gluon emission responsible for DGLAP evolution of fragmentation functions rather than large transverse momentum jet production. The study differs from earlier parton energy loss models by Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and others that considered on-shell partons in hot partonic matter, as it treats off-shell quarks in cold nuclear matter where colors are confined to nucleon size. The results enable quantitative predictions for nuclear modification of fragmentation functions in electron-nucleus collisions and provide a framework for studying how parton energy loss depends on medium properties, with expected differences between confined hadronic matter and deconfined quark-gluon plasma.
