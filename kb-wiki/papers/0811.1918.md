# Interplay of the emission from thermal and direct sources in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The separation of the source created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions into a thermalized dense core and an outer mantle consisting of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions is discussed. Evidence for such a two component picture is found in transverse mass spectra of kaon, protons and antiprotons produced in Au-Au collisions at 200GeV. Estimates of the sizes of the thermal and direct sources are compared to models separating the interaction zone into a core and a corona, according to the density of participants or to the number of collisions. Consequences for the modeling of the dynamics of the small size, thermalized core are described. New initial conditions corresponding to the dense core lead to a stronger azimuthal asymmetry of the hydrodynamically expanding fireball, pressure gradients also increase. 2+1-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations are presented starting from all the matter in the interaction region or from the dense, thermal part of the source. We find faster transverse expansion and stronger elliptic flow for dense core initial conditions. For different impact parameters we find very similar spectra of the thermal part of the source and only adding particles emitted directly from nucleon-nucleon collisions in the corona the experimentally observed softening of the spectra with increasing impact parameter is reproduced. The elliptic flow is stronger for particles emitted from a source separated into a core and a corona.

# Interplay of the emission from thermal and direct sources in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed centrality dependence of transverse mass spectra for kaons, protons, and antiprotons in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV can be explained not by changing the freeze-out parameters of a single thermal source, but by varying the proportion of particles emitted from a thermalized dense core versus those emitted directly from independent nucleon-nucleon collisions in an outer corona. The thermal core spectra are approximately universal across centralities, and only by adding the corona contribution does the experimentally observed softening of spectra with increasing impact parameter emerge.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared fit of the two-component emission model (Eq. 1) to STAR Collaboration data for K+, K-, protons, and antiprotons across 7 centrality bins from 5-10% to 60-70%, extracting the core and corona participant numbers for each centrality. They then compared the extracted core sizes to two geometric models: one defining the core by a minimal participant density threshold (rho_crit = 1.2 fm^-2) in the transverse plane, and another defining the core as nucleons undergoing more than one collision. Finally, they performed 2+1-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, using a realistic equation of state interpolating lattice QCD data above T_c = 170 MeV and a noninteracting hadron gas below, starting from either the full Glauber participant density or the dense-core-only density, with an initial time tau_0 = 0.25 fm/c and freeze-out at T_f = 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The critical density threshold for the core in the density-based model is rho_crit = 1.2 fm^-2 (smaller than the value 2 fm^-2 used in Bozek (2005) due to different assumptions about corona absorption).
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for Au (A=197) are: central density rho_0 = 0.161 fm^-3, radius R_A = 6.5 fm, diffuseness a = 0.535 fm.
- The nucleon-nucleon cross section used is sigma = 41 mb.
- The initial entropy density at the center of the fireball in central collisions corresponds to a temperature of 500 MeV.
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is tau_0 = 0.25 fm/c.
- The freeze-out temperature is T_f = 150 MeV.
- The number of participants in the corona for the 0-5% centrality bin is taken as N_corona = 29, based on Bozek (2005).
- The emission per participant pair from the core is a factor alpha = 1.65 larger than from the corona.
- Hydrodynamic simulations were performed at impact parameters b = 2.23, 4.08, 5.78, 7.48, 8.86, 10.0, 11.1, and 12.1 fm, corresponding to centrality bins from 0-5% to 60-70%.

## Context

This work extends the core-corona picture introduced by Bozek (2005), which explained centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicity and strangeness suppression (K/pi ratio) using a minimal participant density threshold. It also relates to the analysis by Becattini and Manninen (2008), who studied strangeness suppression using a different definition of the thermalized fireball based on the number of collisions per participant. The hydrodynamic simulations build on the successful description of soft observables at RHIC achieved by Chojnacki (2007), Broniowski (2008), and Kisiel (2008), which assumed early thermalization and an equation of state without a soft point. The results enable more refined modeling of initial conditions for hydrodynamic calculations, particularly for peripheral collisions where the core-corona separation becomes significant, and suggest that event-by-event fluctuations of the core size (shown to have a scaled standard deviation that differs from the full participant distribution) could modify conclusions about collective flow and final spectra for smaller systems such as Cu-Cu collisions.
