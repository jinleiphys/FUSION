# On the appearance of hyperons in neutron stars

> By employing a recently constructed hyperon-nucleon potential the equation of state of β-equilibrated and charge neutral nucleonic matter is calculated. The hyperon-nucleon potential is a low-momentum potential which is obtained within a renormalization group framework. Based on the Hartree-Fock approximation at zero temperature the densities at which hyperons appear in neutron stars are estimated. For several different bare hyperon-nucleon potentials and a wide range of nuclear matter parameters it is found that hyperons in neutron stars are always present. These findings have profound consequences for the mass and radius of neutron stars.

# On the appearance of hyperons in neutron stars

## Key claim

Hyperons (specifically Lambda and Sigma-minus) appear in neutron star interiors at densities around twice nuclear saturation density for all hyperon-nucleon interaction models and nuclear matter parameter ranges considered, but the identity of the first-appearing hyperon depends sensitively on whether the Sigma-minus single-particle potential is attractive or repulsive. The authors find that hyperons are always present in neutron stars, yet the maximum neutron star masses obtained with all hyperon-nucleon interactions remain below the precisely measured 1.44 solar masses of the Hulse-Taylor binary pulsar.

## Method

The authors construct effective low-momentum hyperon-nucleon potentials (V_low_k) via a renormalization group framework starting from several bare hyperon-nucleon interactions: Nijmegen NSC89, Nijmegen NSC97a-f, Julich J04, and chiral effective field theory with a 600 MeV cutoff. Using the Hartree-Fock approximation at zero temperature, they calculate single-particle potentials for hyperons in symmetric nuclear matter and then determine particle concentrations in beta-equilibrated, charge-neutral matter by solving the coupled equations for chemical potentials and charge neutrality. The nucleonic part of the equation of state is replaced by a parameterization fitted to variational calculations with the Argonne V18 interaction including three-body forces, allowing controlled variation of the incompressibility K0 between 200 and 300 MeV and the symmetry energy a_t between 28 and 36 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Lambda hyperon single-particle potential at rest in symmetric nuclear matter: approximately -30 MeV (empirical value), with most potentials reproducing this value except Julich J04 (stronger binding) and Nijmegen NSC89 (weaker binding)
- Sigma-minus hyperon threshold densities range from 1.4 rho_0 to 2.4 rho_0 for all interactions except chiral EFT600, where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is nuclear saturation density
- Lambda hyperon threshold densities range from 1.7 rho_0 to 4.5 rho_0 depending on K0, a_t, and the hyperon-nucleon interaction
- Nuclear saturation parameters used: E_0 = -15.8 MeV (binding energy), S_0 = 32 MeV (symmetry energy), gamma = 0.6, delta = 0.2
- Incompressibility K0 varied between 200 MeV and 300 MeV; symmetry energy a_t varied between 28 MeV and 36 MeV
- Maximum neutron star masses with hyperons remain below 1.44 solar masses (the Hulse-Taylor binary pulsar mass)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hyperonic matter in neutron stars dating back to Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960) and subsequent calculations by Pandharipande (1971), Glendenning (1984), and Prakash (1996). It differs from prior Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Baldo, 1998) by using a renormalization-group-based low-momentum potential approach and systematically comparing six different hyperon-nucleon interaction models. The results enable future studies of neutron star structure with strangeness, though the authors note that including hyperon-hyperon interactions (neglected here) and three-body forces would be necessary next steps. The finding that maximum masses fall below 1.44 solar masses contradicts observations of heavier neutron stars and points to the need for additional repulsion at high densities, possibly from three-body forces or quark matter.
