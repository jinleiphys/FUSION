# Towards tomography of quark-gluon plasma using double inclusive forward-central jets in Pb-Pb collision

> We propose a new framework, merging High Energy Factorization with final-state jet quenching effects due to interactions in a quark-gluon plasma, to compute di-jet rates at mid- and forward rapidity. It allows to consistently study the interplay of initial-state effects with medium interactions, opening the possibility for understanding the dynamics of hard probes in heavy-ion collisions and the QGP evolution in rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new framework that merges High Energy Factorization (HEF) with final-state jet quenching effects to compute di-jet rates at mid- and forward rapidity in Pb-Pb collisions, but the authors explicitly state that the factorization formula is a "conjecture" and can only be justified when the jets are hard enough that the medium modifies their properties only slightly.

## Method

The authors combine the hybrid HEF formalism, which uses one off-shell and one on-shell incoming parton with transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton densities, with medium-induced radiative energy loss implemented via energy-loss probability distributions. The medium is modeled using a transport coefficient $\hat q$ linked to energy density via $\hat q = 2 K \varepsilon^{3/4}$, with a rapidity-dependent profile $H(\eta)$ fitted to LHC charged-particle data. The vacuum cross section uses nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs for the collinear parton and a nuclear TMD gluon density constructed via the KMRW procedure, while medium effects are applied by shifting the final-state jet transverse momenta according to energy-loss probabilities derived from the BDMPS-Z spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Central jet transverse momentum cut: $p_{t,c} > 100$ GeV
- Forward jet transverse momentum cut: $p_{t,f} > 30$ GeV
- Central jet rapidity range: $-1 < \eta_c < 1$
- Forward jet rapidity range: $2 < \eta_f < 3$
- Medium length: $L = 5$ fm
- Transport coefficient at mid-rapidity: $\hat q = 1$ GeV$^2$/fm (for $K=1$)
- Total energy density: $\epsilon_\text{tot} \approx 143$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Characteristic gluon energy: $\omega_c \approx 62.5$ GeV
- Parameter $r = \hat q L^3/2 \approx 1560$
- Fitting parameters for rapidity profile: $a_1=2108.05$, $b_1=3.66935$, $a_2=486.368$, $b_2=1.19377$
- Strong coupling constant: $\alpha_s = 1/3$ (default)

## Context

This work extends the hybrid HEF framework previously developed for vacuum di-jet production (Catani 1990, Deak 2009, Kutak 2012) by incorporating medium-induced energy loss, building on earlier studies of di-jet azimuthal decorrelation due to transverse momentum broadening (Mueller 2016). The framework enables tomographic studies of the rapidity profile of the quark-gluon plasma using forward-central jet pairs, and the authors plan to extend it to include saturation effects and final-state momentum broadening in the spirit of Mueller (2016).
