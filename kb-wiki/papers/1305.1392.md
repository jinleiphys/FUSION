# Effects of the Detection Efficiency on Multiplicity Distributions

> In this paper we investigate how a finite detection efficiency affects three popular multiplicity distributions, namely the Poisson, the Binomial and the Negative Binomial distributions. We found that a multiplicity-independent detection efficiency does not change the characteristic of a distribution, while a multiplicity-dependent detection efficiency does. We layout a procedure to study the deviation of moments and their derivative quantities from the baseline distribution due to a multiplicity-dependent detection efficiency.

## Key claim
A multiplicity-independent detection efficiency does not change the type of a Poisson, Binomial, or Negative Binomial distribution, only its mean and width; however, a multiplicity-dependent detection efficiency distorts the original distribution type, making recovery of the original distribution difficult.

## Method
The authors applied the cluster decay theorem by replacing the probability-generating function variable \(z\) with \(g(y) = (1-\epsilon) + \epsilon y\) for a multiplicity-independent efficiency \(\epsilon\). For the multiplicity-dependent case, they derived expressions for factorial moments \(F_i\) as functions of \(\epsilon(k)\) and used these to compute the mean, variance, third and fourth central moments, and cumulants for each distribution type.

## Key numbers
- For a Poisson distribution with multiplicity-independent efficiency, the mean is reduced from \(\lambda\) to \(\lambda \epsilon\).
- For a Binomial distribution with multiplicity-independent efficiency, the parameter \(p\) is replaced by \(p' = p\epsilon\) and the mean becomes \(\mu' = \mu\epsilon\).
- For a Negative Binomial distribution with multiplicity-independent efficiency, the parameter \(p\) is replaced by \(p' = \frac{p}{\epsilon + p - p\epsilon}\) and the mean becomes \(\mu' = \mu\epsilon\); the parameter \(r\) is unchanged.
- For a Binomial distribution, the quantity \(\kappa\sigma^2 = C_4/C_2\) becomes \(1 - 6p\epsilon + 6p^2\epsilon^2\) with finite efficiency.
- For a Negative Binomial distribution, \(\kappa\sigma^2 = C_4/C_2\) becomes \(\frac{6 - 6p' + p'^2}{p'^2}\) with \(p' = \frac{p}{\epsilon + p - p\epsilon}\).

## Context
This work addresses a practical concern in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments searching for the QCD phase boundary and critical end point, as discussed by Stephanov (2004, 2009), Fodor (2004), and others. The paper extends prior treatments of detection efficiency effects, such as the procedure proposed by Adam (2012), by providing explicit formulas for three common multiplicity distributions and for the difference distribution of two independent variables. The results enable experimentalists to quantify how finite detection efficiency distorts higher-order moments and cumulants of conserved quantities like net-baryon number, net-charge, and net-strangeness, helping to avoid misinterpreting detector effects as signals of phase transitions.
