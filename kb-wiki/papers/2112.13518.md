# Effects of the momentum dependence of nuclear symmetry potential on pion observables in Sn + Sn collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon

> Within a transport model, we study effects of the momentum dependence of nuclear symmetry potential on pion observables in central Sn + Sn collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon. To this end, a quantity $U_{sym}^{\infty}(ρ_{0})$, i.e., the value of nuclear symmetry potential at the saturation density $ρ_{0}$ and infinitely large nucleon momentum, is used to characterise the momentum dependence of nuclear symmetry potential. It is shown that with a certain $L$ (i.e., slope of nuclear symmetry energy at $ρ_{0}$) the characteristic parameter $U_{sym}^{\infty}(ρ_{0})$ of symmetry potential affects significantly the production of $π^{-}$ and $π^{+}$ as well as their pion ratios. Moreover, through comparing the charged pion yields, pion ratios as well the spectral pion ratios of theoretical simulations for the reactions $^{108}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn with the corresponding data in S$π$RIT experiments, we find that our results favor a constraint on $U_{sym}^{\infty}(ρ_{0})$, i.e., $-160^{+18}_{-9}$~MeV, and the $L$ is also suggested within a range, i.e., $62.7<L<93.1$~MeV. In addition, it is shown that the pion observable of $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at 400~MeV/nucleon also supports the extracted value for $U_{sym}^{\infty}(ρ_{0})$.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential, characterized by the parameter \(U_{sym}^{\infty}(\rho_{0})\) (the symmetry potential at saturation density and infinite nucleon momentum), significantly affects charged pion yields and ratios in Sn+Sn collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon, and the authors find that a constraint on this parameter of \(-160^{+18}_{-9}\) MeV, combined with a slope parameter \(L\) in the range \(62.7 < L < 93.1\) MeV, allows their transport model simulations to fit the SπRIT experimental data.

## Method

The authors used an isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, improved from the IBUU04/IBUU11 frameworks, with a separate density-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon interaction (IMDI). They simulated central (\(b=3\) fm) collisions of \(^{108}\)Sn + \(^{112}\)Sn and \(^{132}\)Sn + \(^{124}\)Sn at 270 MeV/nucleon, varying the parameter \(U_{sym}^{\infty}(\rho_{0})\) and the slope \(L\) of the symmetry energy at saturation density, and compared the calculated charged pion multiplicities, single \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratios, and double ratios with SπRIT experimental data to extract constraints.

## Key numbers

- The extracted constraint on the symmetry potential parameter is \(U_{sym}^{\infty}(\rho_{0}) = -160^{+18}_{-9}\) MeV.
- The slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to \(62.7 < L < 93.1\) MeV.
- The saturation density used in the model is \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation is \(-16\) MeV, the incompressibility is \(K_0 = 230\) MeV, and the symmetry energy at saturation is \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 32.5\) MeV.
- The maximum compression density reached in the central region of \(^{132}\)Sn + \(^{124}\)Sn collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon is approximately \(1.5\rho_0\).
- The transition kinetic energy for the symmetry potential at \(1.5\rho_0\) with \(L=62.7\) MeV and \(U_{sym}^{\infty}(\rho_0) = -240\) MeV is about 81 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the SπRIT collaboration (Jhang, 2021) and the transport model comparison project, which found that seven models could not quantitatively reproduce both pion yields and ratios simultaneously. The study specifically addresses the role of the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential, a factor highlighted by Yong (2021) regarding high-momentum nucleons from short-range correlations. The results provide a combined constraint on the symmetry energy slope \(L\) and the momentum dependence parameter \(U_{sym}^{\infty}(\rho_0)\), and are further supported by comparison with pion data from \(^{197}\)Au + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, enabling more precise modeling of the isovector part of the nuclear equation of state at suprasaturation densities.
