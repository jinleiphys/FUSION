# Screened potential and quarkonia properties at high temperatures

> We perform a quark model calculation of the quarkonia $b\overline{b}$ and $c\overline{c}$ spectra using smooth and sudden string breaking potentials. The screening parameter is scale dependent and can be related to an effective running gluon mass that has a finite infrared fixed point. A temperature dependence for the screening mass is motivated by lattice QCD simulations at finite temperature. Qualitatively different results are obtained for quarkonia properties close to a critical value of the deconfining temperature when a smooth or a sudden string breaking potential is used. In particular, with a sudden string breaking potential quarkonia radii remain almost independent of the temperature up to the critical point, only well above the critical point the radii increase significantly. Such a behavior will impact the phenomenology of quarkonia interactions in medium, in particular for scattering dissociation processes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that quarkonia properties near the deconfining temperature depend qualitatively on whether the string breaking potential is smooth or sudden: with a sudden potential, quarkonia radii remain almost independent of temperature up to the critical point and then increase abruptly, whereas with a smooth potential the radii change continuously with temperature.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger equation for charmonium and bottomonium using two phenomenological potentials: a smooth screened potential that saturates exponentially and a sudden potential that saturates sharply at a breaking distance. They varied the screening parameters (mu for smooth, 1/r_b for sudden) to mimic temperature dependence, and computed bound state masses, r.m.s. radii, and wavefunctions at the origin for S- and P-wave states. The temperature dependence of the screening parameters was motivated by relating them to an effective running gluon mass with a finite infrared fixed point.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium with sudden breaking, the r.m.s. radius of the J/Psi state remains at 0.4596 fm for 1/r_b values from 0.44 to 0.67 fm^-1, then increases to 0.8268 fm at 1/r_b = 1.67 fm^-1 before the state melts
- For bottomonium with sudden breaking, the Upsilon(1S) r.m.s. radius stays at 0.2136 fm for 1/r_b from 0.44 to 1.11 fm^-1, then increases to 0.4590 fm at 1/r_b = 4.00 fm^-1 before melting
- The smooth potential string tension is sigma = 1470 MeV fm^-1, while the sudden potential uses sigma = 800 MeV fm^-1
- The sudden potential breaking distance parameter at T=0 is 1/r_b = 0.44 fm^-1, corresponding to a breaking distance of about 2.27 fm
- The smooth potential screening parameter at T=0 is mu = 0.71 fm^-1
- Charm quark mass is 1264 MeV (smooth) or 1385 MeV (sudden); bottom quark mass is 4724 MeV (smooth) or 4820 MeV (sudden)
- The high-energy elastic cross section scales as 1/lambda ~ 1/<r^2>, predicting a factor of five decrease for charmonium and a factor of two for bottomonium as the critical point is crossed

## Context

This work extends earlier quark model studies using screened potentials by Gonzalez (2003) and Vijande (2003) to finite temperature, and contrasts with lattice QCD results from Umeda (2002), Asakawa (2003), and Datta (2003) that showed charmonium spectral peaks persisting above T_c. The results directly impact calculations of quarkonium dissociation cross sections in heavy-ion collisions, such as those relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR, and build on the constituent interchange mechanism used by Hadjimichef (1998), Wong (1999), Hilbert (2007), and Haidenbauer (2007). The temperature dependence of screening is motivated by the running gluon mass approach of Cornwall and lattice estimates by Satz (2005) and Kar (1988).
