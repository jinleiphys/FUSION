# CPV Phenomenology of Flavor Conserving Two Higgs Doublet Models

> We analyze the constraints on CP-violating, flavor conserving Two Higgs Doublet Models (2HDMs) implied by measurements of Higgs boson properties at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and by the non-observation of permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs) of molecules, atoms and the neutron. We find that the LHC and EDM constraints are largely complementary, with the LHC studies constraining the mixing between the neutral CP-even states and EDMs probing the effect of mixing between the CP-even and CP-odd scalars. The presently most stringent constraints are implied by the non-observation of the ThO molecule EDM signal. Future improvements in the sensitivity of neutron and diamagnetic atom EDM searches could yield competitive or even more severe constraints. We analyze the quantitative impact of hadronic and nuclear theory uncertainties on the interpretation of the latter systems and conclude that these uncertainties cloud the impact of projected improvements in the corresponding experimental sensitivities.

## Key claim

The LHC and electric dipole moment (EDM) constraints on CP-violating, flavor-conserving Two Higgs Doublet Models (2HDMs) are largely complementary, with the most stringent present limits coming from the ThO molecule EDM non-observation; however, hadronic and nuclear theory uncertainties cloud the interpretation of projected improvements in neutron and diamagnetic atom EDM searches.

## Method

The authors analyzed a Z2-symmetric 2HDM with a single physical CP-violating phase in the scalar potential, parameterized by the mixing angle αb that governs the CP-odd admixture of the 125 GeV scalar. They computed constraints from LHC Higgs boson property measurements and from EDM limits on the ThO molecule, the neutron, and 199Hg, using renormalization group evolution of low-energy effective operators and accounting for hadronic and nuclear many-body uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The ThO molecule EDM non-observation provides the most stringent present constraint on the CPV parameter space, with the ACME collaboration reporting a ten times tighter limit on the electron EDM (Baron 2013).
- A factor of 100 more sensitive neutron EDM search would restrict |sin αb| to less than a few × 10−3 in the event of a null result.
- The vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV is used as the combined vev of the two neutral scalars.
- The charged scalar mass is given by m²H+ = (1/2)(2ν − λ4 − Re λ5)v², where ν ≡ Re m²12 csc β sec β / (2v²).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of CPV in 2HDMs (e.g., Branco 2011, Accomando 2006) by simultaneously incorporating LHC Higgs property constraints and EDM limits from multiple systems. The results enable future comparisons between type I and type II 2HDM scenarios, showing that a non-zero result from future neutron, mercury, or radium EDM searches would indicate a type II rather than type I model, while also highlighting the need for improved hadronic and nuclear many-body computations to resolve interpretation uncertainties.
