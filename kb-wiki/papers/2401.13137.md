# Collectivity inside high-multiplicity jets in high-energy proton-proton collisions

> We present the first study of collectivity inside jets with high charged multiplicity $N^j_{\rm ch}$ in proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. By incorporating final-state partonic and hadronic interactions through cascade models among jet shower partons and final hadrons, we investigate and compare to the CMS experimental data on multiplicity distribution, pseudorapidity distribution, and elliptic anisotropy coefficient $v^{j}_2$ of two-particle correlations within the jet. We show that final-state partonic interactions are essential for producing the flow-like long-range correlation, which leads to the enhanced tail in the $N^j_{\rm ch}$ dependence of $v^{j}_2$ above the non-flow correlation from jet parton showering at high multiplicities ($N^j_{\rm ch}\gtrsim70$) as observed in the CMS experimental data. In addition, we provide predictions for the pseudorapidity-gap dependence of $v^{j}_2$ that can be tested in future experimental measurements.

# Collectivity inside high-multiplicity jets in high-energy proton-proton collisions

## Key claim

Final-state partonic interactions are essential for producing the flow-like long-range correlation that leads to the enhanced tail in the multiplicity dependence of the elliptic anisotropy coefficient $v^j_2$ inside high-multiplicity jets, but only when the charged multiplicity within the jet exceeds approximately 70; hadronic rescatterings alone are insufficient to generate this effect.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA8 with the CP5 tune to generate initial jet shower partons, then incorporated final-state partonic rescatterings via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) model and hadronic rescatterings via the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. Jet reconstruction was performed using the anti-$k_T$ algorithm with cone size $R = 0.8$ and jet transverse momentum $p_T^{\rm jet} > 550$ GeV/$c$, and the elliptic anisotropy coefficient $v^j_2$ was computed using the Q-cumulant method with pseudorapidity gaps in the jet coordinate frame.

## Key numbers

- Jet transverse momentum threshold: $p_T^{\rm jet} > 550$ GeV/$c$ for jet reconstruction with cone size $R = 0.8$
- Charged hadron kinematic cuts: $\vert\eta\vert < 2.4$ and $p_T > 0.3$ GeV/$c$ in the laboratory frame
- Onset of collectivity occurs at $N^j_{\rm ch} \sim 70$, comparable to the multiplicity range where flow-like signals have been observed in high-multiplicity $p+p$ collisions
- At $N^j_{\rm ch} \approx 100$, $v^j_2$ for $0.5 < j_T < 3.0$ GeV/$c$ is approximately 0.17 for calculations with $\sigma_p = 3.0$ mb and hadronic rescatterings, compared to approximately 0.10 for calculations without final-state interaction
- Partonic cross section $\sigma_p$ was varied, with a value of 3.0 mb used for the primary comparison

## Context

This work extends the search for collective phenomena from large heavy-ion collision systems (where quark-gluon plasma behaves as a nearly perfect liquid) to the smallest possible system: a single high-multiplicity jet in proton-proton collisions. It directly addresses the CMS Collaboration measurement (CMS, 2023) which showed a rising tail in the multiplicity dependence of $v^j_2$ that could not be described by PYTHIA8 or SHERPA simulations. The study enables future experimental tests of pseudorapidity-gap dependence of $v^j_2$ and can be extended to $e^{+}+e^{-}$ collisions to further understand the space-time structure and interactions among shower partons inside energetic jets.
