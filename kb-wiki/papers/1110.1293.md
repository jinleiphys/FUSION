# Impurity effect of $Λ$ hyperon on shape-coexistence nucleus $^{44}$S in the energy functional based collective Hamiltonian

> The non-relativistic Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) based collective Hamiltonian, that takes into account dynamical correlations related to the restoration of broken symmetries and fluctuations of quadrupole collective variables, is applied to quantitatively study the impurity effect of $Λ$ hyperon on the collectivity of $^{44}$S. Several Skyrme forces for both the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and $Λ$-nucleon ($ΛN$) interactions are used. The influence of pairing strengths on the polarization effect of $Λ$ hyperon is also examined. It is found that although these Skyrme forces with different pairing strengths give somewhat different low-lying spectra for $^{44}$S, all of them give similar and generally small size of $Λ$ reduction effect (within 5%) on the collective properties.

## Key claim

The paper finds that although different Skyrme forces and pairing strengths produce somewhat different low-lying spectra for the normal nucleus \(^{44}\)S, all of them give a similar and generally small reduction effect (within 5%) of the \(\Lambda\) hyperon on the collective properties of the shape-coexistence nucleus \(^{44}\)S.

## Method

The authors applied a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) based on the non-relativistic Skyrme energy density functional (EDF), which includes dynamical correlations from symmetry restoration and quadrupole fluctuations. Parameters for the collective Hamiltonian (potential, mass parameters, moments of inertia) were determined from constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS (SHF+BCS) calculations for both \(^{44}\)S and the hypernucleus \(^{45}_{\Lambda}\)S. Several Skyrme forces for the nucleon-nucleon (\(NN\)) interaction (SGII, SLy5, SIII, SKM*, T6, SkP, SLy4) and for the \(\Lambda\)-nucleon (\(\Lambda N\)) interaction (No.1 set from Yamamoto (1988) and HP120310 from Guleria (2011)) were used, and the pairing strength for nucleons was varied.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Lambda\) hyperon reduces the \(B(E2:2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) value and other spectroscopic observables for the nuclear core of \(^{45}_{\Lambda}\)S relative to \(^{44}\)S by within 5%, across all Skyrme forces and pairing strengths tested.
* The excitation energy of the \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{44}\)S calculated with the SGII, SLy5, and SIII forces is much smaller than that with SKM*, T6, and SkP forces; the experimental value is indicated by a vertical dotted line in Fig. 2.
* The pairing strength \(g\) for nucleons (varied around a reference value of 1000 MeV fm\(^3\)) significantly changes the excitation energies of the \(2^+_1\), \(0^+_2\), and \(2^+_2\) states in \(^{44}\)S, but the \(\Lambda\) impurity effect on collectivity remains within 5% for different pairing strengths.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior 5DCH study of \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg (Yao (2011)) to the shape-coexistence nucleus \(^{44}\)S, using multiple Skyrme forces and pairing strengths to test the robustness of the \(\Lambda\) polarization effect. It contrasts with earlier static mean-field studies (e.g., Zhou (2007), Schulze (2010), Win (2011)) that found generally small shape polarization but did not include dynamical correlations from symmetry restoration and quadrupole fluctuations. The results enable systematic studies of \(\Lambda\) impurity effects across different mass regions and may help constrain \(\Lambda N\) effective interactions in both non-relativistic and covariant energy density functionals.
