# Experimental Signatures for Identifying Distinct Origins of Color Field Generation

> Signatures for non-abelian dynamics have long been central to QCD and QGP. Equally important are they in spin systems and laser-plasma interactions, where they emerge as effective interactions. Distinguishing experimentally gauge inequivalent sources (and hence potentials) that produce the same field tensor is one major task in this endeavour. As a step in this direction, this paper investigates how physically distinct sources which produce the same color electric field (uniform and constant) may be distinguished experimentally in a gauge-invariant manner. We first study the motion of a test particle in such fields and show that the resultant trajectories are counterintuitive. We then examine the radiation emitted - both gluonic and photonic and show that each source (independent non-abelian configuration) leaves a unique signature in the energy spectra, laying the ground for application to specific physical systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that physically distinct, gauge-inequivalent potentials (parameterized by a continuous parameter α) which produce the same uniform, constant color electric field can be distinguished experimentally through the motion of a test particle and the resulting bremsstrahlung radiation spectra, despite the Wu-Yang ambiguity that makes the field tensor alone insufficient.

## Method

The authors consider test particle dynamics in a static, uniform color electric field generated by a one-parameter family of gauge-inequivalent potentials for an SO(3) gauge group. They solve the coupled Lorentz and Wong equations for the particle's momentum and color charge evolution, then compute both gluonic and electromagnetic bremsstrahlung radiation from the resulting periodic motion, analyzing the power spectra for signatures of the underlying potential parameter α.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless coupling parameter k = gq²E/(mc²)² characterizes the nonabelian dynamics and the system energy scale q√(gE) relative to the particle rest mass energy.
- The effective potential coefficients are k₁ = (α² + 1/α²), k₂ = (1/α²)ε, and k₃ = α²Π, where α > 0 is the potential parameter, ε is total energy, and Π is canonical momentum.
- The dominant frequency ω_d resolves α almost completely, with only a twofold discrete ambiguity remaining.
- The power spectrum is asymmetric under α → α⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the qualitative discussion of nonabelian test particle dynamics by Brown (1978) to quantitative predictions for experimental signatures. It directly addresses the Wu-Yang ambiguity (Wu and Yang, 1975) by showing that gauge-inequivalent potentials producing identical field tensors can be distinguished through particle dynamics and radiation. The results are expected to apply to synthetic nonabelian gauge fields in spintronics and cold atom-laser interactions (Berche, 2013; Leurs, 2007), as well as to quark-gluon plasma dynamics in heavy ion collisions (Huet, 1997; Buchmuller, 1998).
