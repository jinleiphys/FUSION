# Comparison of Relativistic and Non-relativistic Faddeev calculations for Proton-Deuteron Elastic Scattering

> This investigation compares non-relativistic and relativistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials in the context of pd scattering. Conventional NN potentials (e.g., CDBonn, AV18) rely on the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation, whereas the Kharkiv potential is intrinsically relativistic. We employ the Coester-Pieper-Serduke (CPS) and Kamada-Glöckle (KG) conversion methods to construct a Pseudo-Relativistic Potential (PRP) from a realistic NN potential, preserving the deuteron binding energy and phase shifts. Calculations of the differential cross section using the relativistic Faddeev equation show that relativistic effects particularly the deviation at the backward angle become pronounced at 135 MeV. The differences in the forward angle were attributed to the characteristics of the Kharkiv potential itself. The reverse transformation of the Kharkiv potential into a Pseudo-Non-Relativistic Potential (PNRP) confirms that the backward-angle relativistic effect increases with energy in the range from 100 MeV to 400 MeV. Comparisons of the polarization observables indicate that relativistic effects, as well as the discrepancy between the CPS and KG transformations, become significant above 300 MeV. Nevertheless, non-relativistic calculations using the PRP remain generally reliable for polarization observables below 300 MeV.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in proton-deuteron elastic scattering become pronounced at backward angles at 135 MeV and increase with energy from 100 MeV to 400 MeV, but non-relativistic calculations using pseudo-relativistic potentials remain generally reliable for polarization observables below 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Faddeev three-body equation using the fully relativistic Kharkiv potential and compared the results with non-relativistic Faddeev calculations using conventional realistic potentials (CDBonn, AV18, Nijmegen). They employed the Coester-Pieper-Serduke (CPS) and Kamada-Glöckle (KG) conversion methods to construct pseudo-relativistic potentials from the non-relativistic potentials, and also applied the inverse transformations to convert the Kharkiv potential into pseudo-non-relativistic potentials for systematic comparison.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section at 135 MeV shows clear relativistic effect at backward scattering angles, with differences between the Kharkiv potential and conventional potentials becoming pronounced
- Backward-angle relativistic effect increases with energy in the range from 100 MeV to 400 MeV, as confirmed by the reverse transformation of the Kharkiv potential into a pseudo-non-relativistic potential
- Relativistic effects and discrepancies between CPS and KG transformations become significant above 300 MeV for polarization observables
- Non-relativistic calculations using pseudo-relativistic potentials remain generally reliable for polarization observables below 300 MeV
- The Kharkiv potential's NN database extends only up to about 350 MeV in the laboratory frame

## Context

This work extends previous studies of relativistic Faddeev calculations for three-body systems, including triton binding energy calculations by Glöckle (1986) and Kamada (2002), and earlier analyses of elastic electron-deuteron scattering by Allen (2000). The CPS transformation was developed by Coester, Pieper, and Serduke (1975), while the KG transformation was introduced by Kamada and Glöckle (1998). The Kharkiv potential, constructed via the unitary clothing transformations method by Shebeko and collaborators, provides a fully relativistic alternative to conventional meson-theoretical potentials like CDBonn (Machleidt) and AV18 (Wiringa). The results enable future work including calculations of the Kharkiv three-nucleon force within the UCT framework.
