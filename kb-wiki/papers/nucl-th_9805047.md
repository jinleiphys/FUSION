# Solution of relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in configurational representation: spherical neutron halo nuclei

> The scaled harmonic oscillator basis (SHO) is derived by a local scaling-point transformation of the spherical harmonic oscillator radial wave functions. The unitary scaling transformation produces a basis with improved asymptotic properties. The SHO basis is employed in the solution of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations in configurational space. The model is applied in the self-consistent mean-field approximation to the description of the neutron halo in Ne isotopes. It is shown that an expansion of nucleon spinors and mean-field potentials in the SHO basis reproduces the asymptotic properties of neutron densities calculated by finite element discretization in the coordinate space. In the RHB description of neutron skins and halos, SHO bases in two or three dimensions can be a useful alternative to technically complicated solutions on a mesh in coordinate space.

## Key claim

The scaled harmonic oscillator (SHO) basis, derived by a local-scaling point transformation of spherical harmonic oscillator radial wave functions, can reproduce the asymptotic properties of neutron densities in halo nuclei calculated by finite element discretization in coordinate space, provided the basis parameters are chosen so that neutron densities decrease exponentially outside the nuclear volume; the method is presented as a useful alternative to technically complicated coordinate-space solutions for deformed nuclei, not as a universal replacement.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations in configurational representation by expanding nucleon spinors and mean-field potentials in a scaled harmonic oscillator (SHO) basis. The SHO basis was constructed by applying a unitary local-scaling point transformation to standard spherical harmonic oscillator radial wave functions, with the transformation function chosen to produce exponential asymptotic decay. The RHB calculations used the NL3 parameter set for the mean-field Lagrangian and the D1S Gogny interaction for pairing, and results were compared with finite element discretization solutions in coordinate space for a series of even-even Ne isotopes from 30Ne to 40Ne.

## Key numbers

* Neutron rms radii for Ne isotopes calculated with the SHO basis (20 oscillator shells) "almost exactly reproduce" the values from finite element discretization in coordinate space, whereas HO basis calculations with 12 or 20 shells could not reproduce the coordinate space results.
* The coordinate space calculations were performed in a box of finite dimension 20 fm, and near this boundary a "very steep decrease" in densities was observed as an artifact of the finite mesh.
* The sudden increase in neutron rms radii, interpreted as evidence for multi-particle halo formation, occurs for Ne isotopes with neutron number N greater than approximately 22.
* In the RHB description, the neutron halo formation is related to the quasi-degeneracy of the triplet of states 1f7/2, 2p3/2, and 2p1/2, where pairing promotes neutrons from the 1f7/2 orbital to the 2p levels.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations of Poschl, Vretenar, Lalazissis, and Ring (1997) on neutron halo nuclei in Ne isotopes, which used finite element methods in coordinate space, by implementing a configurational representation approach. The SHO basis was previously suggested by Stoitsov, Nazarewicz, and Pittel (1998) for non-relativistic HFB calculations, and the present work applies it to the relativistic framework. The method enables future applications to deformed halo nuclei, where coordinate space discretization algorithms become technically very complicated, and provides a basis that avoids the Gaussian asymptotic behavior of standard harmonic oscillator expansions that produce incorrect densities for halo nuclei.
