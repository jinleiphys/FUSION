# Application of the optimized-basis generator coordinate method to low-lying excited states of sd-shell nuclei

> We apply the optimized-basis generator coordinate method (OptGCM) to sd-shell nuclei, $^{20}$Ne, $^{24}$Mg, and $^{28}$Si. This method variationally optimizes both the basis Slater determinants in the generator coordinate method (GCM) and the corresponding weight coefficients. To analyze the low-lying excited states of those nuclei, we implement the angular momentum projection. With the Skyrme interaction, we show that the simultaneous optimzation of the basis functions and the weight factors lowers the energy of the excited states and at the same time leads to an appreciable effect on transition probabilities. These results highlight the effectiveness of the OptGCM method.

# Application of the optimized-basis generator coordinate method to low-lying excited states of sd-shell nuclei

## Key claim
The optimized-basis generator coordinate method (OptGCM), which variationally optimizes both the basis Slater determinants and their weight coefficients, lowers the energies of low-lying excited states and appreciably affects transition probabilities in sd-shell nuclei compared to conventional GCM, but only when the basis states are first optimized for the intrinsic ground state and then angular momentum projection is applied afterward (variation before projection), rather than optimizing for each angular momentum individually.

## Method
The authors applied the OptGCM to 20Ne, 24Mg, and 28Si using the SIII Skyrme interaction with an axial harmonic oscillator basis of 14 major shells, imposing axial and reflection symmetries and omitting pairing correlations and time-odd terms. Basis Slater determinants and weight coefficients were optimized simultaneously for the intrinsic ground state using the conjugate gradient method, with initial determinants constructed from Woods-Saxon potentials at various quadrupole deformations. After optimization, angular momentum projection was performed and the Hill-Wheeler equation was solved for each angular momentum to obtain spectra and B(E2) transition probabilities, using a variation-before-projection (VBP) approach.

## Key numbers
- For 20Ne: intrinsic ground state energy decreases by 0.56 MeV from HF (-153.28 MeV) to GCM (-153.84 MeV), and further by 0.87 MeV from GCM to OptGCM (-154.71 MeV)
- For 24Mg: intrinsic ground state energy decreases by 0.74 MeV from HF (-191.21 MeV) to GCM (-191.95 MeV), and further by 0.91 MeV from GCM to OptGCM (-192.86 MeV)
- For 28Si: intrinsic ground state energy decreases by 1.19 MeV from HF (-228.74 MeV) to GCM (-229.93 MeV), and further by 0.78 MeV from GCM to OptGCM (-230.71 MeV)
- B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) in 20Ne: OptGCM gives 54.9 e2fm4, GCM gives 61.1 e2fm4, HF gives 38.1 e2fm4, compared to experimental 65 +/- 3 e2fm4
- B(E2; 4+ -> 2+) in 20Ne: OptGCM gives 65.5 e2fm4, GCM gives 70 e2fm4, HF gives 55 e2fm4, compared to experimental 71 +/- 6 e2fm4
- B(E2; 6+ -> 4+) in 20Ne: OptGCM gives 50.3 e2fm4, GCM gives 44.3 e2fm4, HF gives 61.6 e2fm4, compared to experimental 64 +/- 10 e2fm4
- B(E2; 8+ -> 6+) in 20Ne: OptGCM gives 31.4 e2fm4, GCM gives 16.9 e2fm4, HF gives 67.9 e2fm4, compared to experimental 29 +/- 4 e2fm4
- 10 Slater determinants were superposed for 20Ne and 28Si; 8 Slater determinants for 24Mg

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous application of OptGCM to intrinsic ground states of 16O and 28Si (Matsumoto 2023, Matsumoto 2024) by implementing angular momentum projection to study low-lying excited states and transition probabilities. The method addresses a longstanding limitation of conventional GCM identified by Ring and Schuck, where the selection of collective coordinates (typically multipole moments) relies on empirical knowledge and may be insufficient, as demonstrated by Hizawa (2021, 2022) and Kumar (2023). The approach is related to the Monte-Carlo shell model (Shimizu 2012) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Myo 2023, Hasegawa 2020), which attempt similar simultaneous optimization. The results enable future extensions including optimization for each angular momentum (as in MCSM), inclusion of pairing correlations via HFB states, removal of axial/reflection symmetry constraints, and extension to time-dependent frameworks for nuclear dynamics such as fusion and fission.
