# Stability of the homogeneous Bose-Einstein condensate at large gas parameter

> The properties of the uniform Bose gas is studied within the optimized variational perturbation theory (Gaussian approximation) in a self-consistent way. It is shown that the atomic BEC with a repulsive interaction becomes unstable when the gas parameter gamma=rho a^3 exceeds a critical value gamma_{crit} ~ 0.01. The quantum corrections beyond the Bogoliubov-Popov approximation to the energy density, chemical potential and pressure in powers of gamma expansions are presented.

## Key claim

The authors show that a homogeneous atomic Bose-Einstein condensate with repulsive interactions becomes unstable when the gas parameter gamma = rho a^3 exceeds a critical value gamma_crit approximately 0.01, but this instability is conditional on the specific self-consistent Gaussian approximation scheme used, which incorporates two chemical potentials to satisfy both the Hugenholtz-Pines theorem and particle number conservation simultaneously.

## Method

The authors reformulate the field-theoretical Gaussian (variational) approximation following Yukalov's prescription, introducing separate chemical potentials for the condensed fraction (mu_0) and the uncondensed fraction (mu_1), plus a Lagrange multiplier to enforce the condition that the fluctuating field has zero expectation value. They derive gap equations from the principle of minimal sensitivity, solve them at zero temperature using dimensional regularization, and impose the normalization condition that the sum of condensed and uncondensed densities equals the total density.

## Key numbers

- Critical gas parameter: gamma_crit approximately 0.01, above which the homogeneous BEC becomes unstable
- Maximum experimental gas parameter achieved in Rb-85 experiments (Cornish et al.): gamma_max approximately 0.01, corresponding to scattering length a approximately 4500 angstrom
- The dimensionless sound velocity is given by c^2 = Sigma_bar_12 / m, where Sigma_bar_12 = g rho (1 + 2 nbar_1) at the stationary point
- The uncondensed fraction at zero temperature is N_1 = (1/8) I_{1,1}(X_1), where I_{1,1}(X_1) = V (2m X_1)^(3/2) / (3 pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian variational approach first applied to uniform BEC by Bijlsma and Stoof (2000), which Andersen (2004) noted did not satisfy the Hugenholtz-Pines theorem. The authors adopt Yukalov's (2005) prescription of two chemical potentials to make the approximation both conserving and gapless, in contrast to the Bogoliubov-Popov approximation and one-loop approximations. The results enable calculation of quantum corrections beyond the Bogoliubov-Popov approximation for energy density, chemical potential, and pressure in powers of sqrt(gamma) expansions, and provide a theoretical framework for understanding the stability limit observed experimentally by Cornish et al. (2000) with Rb-85.
