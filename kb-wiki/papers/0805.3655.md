# Renormalization-group analysis for low-energy scattering of charged particles

> The low-energy scattering of two charged particles is analyzed using a renormalization group approach based on dimensional regularization with power-divergence subtraction. A nontrivial solution with a marginally unstable direction is found, corresponding to a system with a bound state at zero energy. For purely energy-dependent perturbations around this solution, the power counting agrees with that from Wilsonian methods. These terms in the effective potential are in direct correspondence with the the terms in the Coulomb-distorted effective-range expansion. We also study perturbations that depend on off-shell momenta as well as energy, and we show that these affect only the off-shell form of the scattering matrix. These terms are of higher order that the corresponding energy-dependent ones and so terms in the potential that depend only on the off-shell momenta do not have definite orders in power counting.

## Key claim

For low-energy scattering of charged particles with a bound state near threshold, momentum-dependent short-range potentials have a higher RG eigenvalue (ν=2) than the corresponding energy-dependent ones (ν=1), meaning they appear at higher order in the power counting and affect only the off-shell form of the scattering matrix, not on-shell observables.

## Method

The authors applied a renormalization-group analysis using dimensional regularization with power-divergence subtraction (PDS) and modified minimal subtraction (MS-bar) to S-wave scattering by a combination of Coulomb and short-range potentials. They expanded the short-range potential around a nontrivial marginally unstable solution corresponding to a zero-energy bound state, and studied both purely energy-dependent perturbations and the simplest momentum-dependent perturbation (second order in off-shell momenta). The RG equations were obtained by requiring the off-shell scattering matrix to be independent of the subtraction scale μ.

## Key numbers

- The inverse Bohr radius κ = αM/2, which for two protons is approximately 3 MeV
- For two α particles, κ is about 60 MeV, comparable to mπ
- The RG eigenvalue for the leading momentum-dependent perturbation (second order in momenta) is ν=2, compared to ν=1 for the corresponding energy-dependent term proportional to on-shell energy
- The derivative of the Riemann zeta function ζ'(-2) = -0.0304 appears in the expression for ΔJ2

## Context

This work extends the RG analysis of pure short-range interactions by Birse, McGovern, and Richardson (1999) to include the Coulomb potential, and directly addresses discrepancies between the Wilsonian RG approach of Birse and Barford (2003) and the pionless EFT calculations of Kong and Ravndal (1999, 2001) and Ando et al. (2007). The results explain why momentum-dependent potentials used in those earlier EFT approaches mix different RG eigenfunctions, causing higher-order terms to significantly affect lower-order coefficients, and why different momentum structures (e.g., k⁴+k'⁴ vs. k²k'²) renormalize lower-order terms differently as shown numerically by Ando et al. (2007). The analysis enables a systematic power counting for charged-particle scattering that separates energy-dependent and off-shell momentum-dependent terms.
