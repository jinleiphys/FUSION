# Shell-model structure of exotic nuclei beyond 132Sn

> We report on a study of exotic nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn in terms of the shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The short-range repulsion of the bare potential is renormalized by constructing a smooth low-momentum potential, V-low-k, that is used directly as input for the calculation of the effective interaction. In this paper we focus attention on the nuclei 134Sn and 135Sb which, with an N/Z ratio of 1.68 and 1.65, respectively, are at present the most exotic nuclei beyond 132Sn for which information exists on excited states. Comparison shows that the calculated results for both nuclei are in very good agreement with the experimental data. We present our predictions of the hitherto unknown spectrum of 136Sn.

# Shell-model structure of exotic nuclei beyond 132Sn

## Key claim
The shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the V-low-k renormalization method reproduce the observed spectra of 134Sn and 135Sb in very good agreement with experiment, without requiring any shell-structure modifications or adjustments to single-particle energies beyond those taken from experiment.

## Method
The authors derived a two-body effective interaction from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential by first constructing a smooth low-momentum potential V-low-k with a cutoff momentum of 2.2 fm^-1, then applying a folded-diagram method (Q-box including diagrams up to second order) summed via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. Single-particle energies were taken from experimental spectra of 133Sb and 133Sn, with the exception of the proton s1/2 and neutron i13/2 energies taken from prior work. Calculations assumed a 132Sn closed core with valence neutrons occupying the 0h9/2, 1f7/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2, and 0i13/2 orbitals, and for 135Sb the odd proton occupied the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals.

## Key numbers
- The first 2+ state in 134Sn lies at 726 keV excitation energy, the lowest first-excited 2+ level observed in a semi-magic even-even nucleus across the chart of nuclides.
- Calculated B(E2;0+ -> 2_1+) for 134Sn is 0.033 e^2b^2, in excellent agreement with the experimental value of 0.029(4) e^2b^2, using an effective neutron charge of 0.70 e.
- The calculated 5/2+ state in 135Sb lies at 0.391 MeV, within 100 keV of the experimental value of 0.282 MeV.
- Calculated B(M1;5/2+ -> 7/2+) for 135Sb is 4.0 x 10^-3 mu_N^2, compared to the experimental upper limit of 0.29 x 10^-3 mu_N^2.
- For 136Sn, the predicted excitation energies are: 2+ at 0.715 MeV, 4+ at 1.020 MeV, and 6+ at 1.163 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei around 132Sn (Coraggio 2005, Coraggio 2006, Covello 2005a) and addresses the suggestion by Shergur (2002) that the low position of the 5/2+ state in 135Sb requires a 300 keV downshift of the d5/2 proton level relative to g7/2 due to shell-structure modification. The results argue against such modifications, showing that a single Hamiltonian with experimental single-particle energies and a realistic effective interaction can reproduce the data. The predictions for 136Sn provide a benchmark for future experiments on this highly neutron-rich nucleus (N/Z ratio of 1.72).
