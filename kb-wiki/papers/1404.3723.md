# QCD and strongly coupled gauge theories: challenges and perspectives

> We highlight the progress, current status, and open challenges of QCD-driven physics, in theory and in experiment. We discuss how the strong interaction is intimately connected to a broad sweep of physical problems, in settings ranging from astrophysics and cosmology to strongly-coupled, complex systems in particle and condensed-matter physics, as well as to searches for physics beyond the Standard Model. We also discuss how success in describing the strong interaction impacts other fields, and, in turn, how such subjects can impact studies of the strong interaction. In the course of the work we offer a perspective on the many research streams which flow into and out of QCD, as well as a vision for future developments.

## Key claim

This paper is a comprehensive community review that highlights progress, current status, and open challenges in QCD-driven physics, rather than presenting a single new result; it argues that the strong interaction is intimately connected to a broad sweep of physical problems ranging from astrophysics and cosmology to strongly-coupled complex systems in particle and condensed-matter physics, as well as to searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors, a large international collaboration of theorists and experimentalists, conducted a multi-chapter survey of the field of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and strongly coupled gauge theories. The review covers both theoretical approaches (including lattice QCD, effective field theories, perturbative QCD, and holographic methods) and experimental programs (at facilities such as the LHC, RHIC, Jefferson Lab, and various flavor factories), synthesizing the state of knowledge across hadron spectroscopy, heavy-ion collisions, parton distribution functions, and the QCD phase diagram.

## Key numbers

Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This work extends and updates earlier community reviews and white papers on QCD, such as the "Quark Confinement and the Hadron Spectrum" series, by providing a broader perspective that connects QCD to astrophysics, cosmology, and condensed-matter physics. It enables future coordinated efforts across theoretical and experimental communities by identifying the most pressing open questions, including the nature of confinement, the structure of the QCD phase diagram at high density, the origin of hadron masses, and the role of strong interactions in searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
