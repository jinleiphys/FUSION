# The $ωNN$ couplings derived from QCD sum rules

> The light cone QCD sum rules are derived for $ωNN$ vector and tensor couplings simultaneously. The vacuum gluon field contribution is taken into account. Our results are $g_ω=(18\pm 8), κ_ω=(0.8\pm 0.4)$.

# The ωNN couplings derived from QCD sum rules

## Key claim
The light cone QCD sum rules yield vector and tensor ωNN couplings of g_ω = 18 ± 8 and κ_ω = 0.8 ± 0.4, but these values depend strongly on the choice of vector meson wave functions; using asymptotic wave functions instead of model wave functions gives g_ω = 10 ± 2 and κ_ω = 1.0 ± 0.4, and the quoted central results represent an average treating the two wave-function sets as opposite limits.

## Method
Light cone QCD sum rules were derived for the ωNN vector and tensor couplings simultaneously, including the vacuum gluon field contribution which cancels in the analogous ρNN case due to isospin symmetry. The sum rules were evaluated at the symmetric point u_0 = 1/2 with Borel parameters M_1^2 = M_2^2 = 2M^2, using vector meson light cone wave functions at the scale μ = 1 GeV from Braun (1998). The continuum subtraction employed the factor f_n(x) with threshold s_0, and the ratio of two sum rules provided a stable determination of 1 + κ_ω.

## Key numbers
- g_ω (1 + κ_ω) = 45 ± 9, from the combined sum rule analysis
- g_ω = 26 ± 6, from the tensor sum rule alone
- 1 + κ_ω = 1.7 ± 0.4, from the ratio of sum rules
- κ_ω = 0.7 ± 0.4, derived from the ratio
- Final adopted values: g_ω = 18 ± 8, κ_ω = 0.8 ± 0.4, after averaging over wave-function uncertainties
- With asymptotic wave functions: g_ω = 10 ± 2, κ_ω = 1.0 ± 0.4
- Comparison with dispersion analysis by Mergell et al.: g_ω = 20.86 ± 0.25, κ_ω = −0.16 ± 0.01

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum-rule formalism previously applied to ρNN couplings by Zhu to the ωNN case, where vacuum gluon fields contribute significantly unlike the isospin-symmetric ρ case. The results disagree with the naive relation g_ω = 3g_ρ and with the dispersion-theoretical extraction by Mergell et al. which found a much smaller and negative κ_ω. The calculation has no free parameters once the vector meson wave functions at u_0 = 1/2 are fixed, and it enables future studies of the role of vacuum gluon fields in vector meson nucleon interactions.
