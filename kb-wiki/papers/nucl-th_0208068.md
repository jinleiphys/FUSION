# Collective flow and HBT radii from a full 3D hydrodynamic model with early chemical freeze out

> We investigate the effect of early chemical freeze-out on hydrodynamic flow and HBT radii at the RHIC energy with a genuinely three dimensional hydrodynamic model. It is found that the property of early chemical freeze out reduces radial flow, elliptic flow, R_out and R_long.

## Key claim
Early chemical freeze-out (where chemical composition freezes before thermal freeze-out) reduces radial flow, elliptic flow, and the HBT radii \(R_{\mathrm{out}}\) and \(R_{\mathrm{long}}\) in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC, but this effect alone does not fully resolve the RHIC HBT puzzle, as transverse HBT radii remain unreproduced.

## Method
The authors constructed two hadronic equations of state for a full 3D hydrodynamic model: one assuming full chemical equilibrium (model CE) and one implementing partial chemical equilibrium (model PCE) where only strong-interaction resonances remain equilibrated while weak-decay particles are chemically frozen. They simulated Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130\) GeV with initial conditions tuned to reproduce charged hadron pseudorapidity distributions, then compared the resulting transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow \(v_2\), and HBT radii to STAR and PHENIX data.

## Key numbers
- Maximum initial energy density for central collisions: 35 GeV/fm\(^3\) at initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- Chemical freeze-out temperature range from statistical models: \(T^{\mathrm{ch}} \sim 160\)–\(200\) MeV; thermal freeze-out temperature range: \(T^{\mathrm{th}} \sim 100\)–\(140\) MeV.
- Phase transition temperature used in both models: \(T_{\mathrm{c}} = 170\) MeV.
- Model PCE reproduces \(p_t\) spectra below 1.0–1.5 GeV/\(c\) but underestimates data above that range; hard contributions become important above 1.5–2.0 GeV/\(c\).
- Model PCE reproduces the \(K_T\) dependence of \(R_{\mathrm{long}}\) but not the transverse radii.

## Context
This work extends prior hydrodynamic studies by Teaney (2002) and Hirano (2001) that first incorporated early chemical freeze-out into the hadronic equation of state, here applied to a full 3D hydrodynamic model at RHIC energies. It contradicts the conventional assumption of equal chemical and thermal freeze-out temperatures used in earlier models, showing that chemical non-equilibrium suppresses both radial and elliptic flow. The results enable future studies with non-zero baryon chemical potential (\(\mu_{\mathrm{B}} \neq 0\)) to simultaneously describe particle ratios and spectra across different collision energies, and highlight that the RHIC HBT puzzle requires additional mechanisms beyond early chemical freeze-out.
