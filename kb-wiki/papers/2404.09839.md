# A machine learning-based study of open-charm hadrons in proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> n proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions, the study of charm hadrons plays a pivotal role in understanding the QCD medium and provides an undisputed testing ground for the theory of strong interaction, as they are mostly produced in the early stages of collisions via hard partonic interactions. The lightest open-charm, $D^{0}$ meson ($c\Bar{u}$), can originate from two separate sources. The prompt $D^{0}$ originates from either direct charm production or the decay of excited open charm states, while the nonprompt stems from the decay of beauty hadrons. In this paper, using different machine learning (ML) algorithms such as XGBoost, CatBoost, and Random Forest, an attempt has been made to segregate the prompt and nonprompt production modes of $D^{0}$ meson signal from its background. The ML models are trained using the invariant mass through its hadronic decay channel, i.e., $D^{0}\rightarrowπ^{+} K^{-}$, pseudoproper time, pseudoproper decay length, and distance of closest approach of $D^{0}$ meson, using PYTHIA8 simulated $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13~\rm{TeV}$. The ML models used in this analysis are found to retain the pseudorapidity, transverse momentum, and collision energy dependence. In addition, we report the ratio of nonprompt to prompt $D^{0}$ yield, the self-normalized yield of prompt and nonprompt $D^{0}$ and explore the charmonium, $J/ψ$ to open-charm, $D^{0}$ yield ratio as a function of transverse momenta and normalized multiplicity. The observables studied in this manuscript are well predicted by all the ML models compared to the simulation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that machine learning models (XGBoost, CatBoost, Random Forest) trained on PYTHIA8-simulated proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV can classify prompt and nonprompt D⁰ mesons with high accuracy, and these models retain their predictive power across different collision energies (5.02 TeV and 900 GeV) without retraining, though the authors note that PYTHIA8 systematically underestimates the absolute production cross-sections compared to ALICE data by factors of 2.0 for prompt and 2.8 for nonprompt D⁰ mesons.

## Method

The authors generated 2 billion minimum-bias proton-proton collision events at √s = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8 with the 4C tune, and additional 1 billion events each at 5.02 TeV and 900 GeV. They trained three supervised machine learning algorithms (XGBoost, CatBoost, Random Forest) on topological features of reconstructed π⁺K⁻ pairs: invariant mass (m_πK), pseudoproper time (t_z), pseudoproper decay length (cτ), and distance of closest approach (DCA) of the D⁰ meson. The class imbalance problem (background:prompt:nonprompt ratio of 50:20:1) was addressed using the Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE) before training, and the data was split into 8:1:1 train:test:validation sets.

## Key numbers

- Training used 600 million minimum-bias pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV from PYTHIA8
- XGBoost and CatBoost achieved 99% classification accuracy for separating prompt from nonprompt D⁰ mesons; Random Forest achieved 97% accuracy
- All three ML models separated background π⁺K⁻ pairs from signal with 100% accuracy
- The background:prompt:nonprompt ratio before SMOTE oversampling was 50:20:1, which changed to 15:5:1 after oversampling
- PYTHIA8 underestimates ALICE data by a factor of 2.0 for prompt D⁰ yield and 2.8 for nonprompt D⁰ yield
- The D⁰ meson mass used in calculations was m_D⁰ ≈ 1865 MeV (from Particle Data Group)
- A p_T cutoff of 0.5 GeV/c was implemented to prevent divergence of QCD processes
- Primary vertex z-component was restricted to |V_z| < 10 cm following experimental methods

## Context

This work extends the application of machine learning techniques to heavy-flavor hadron classification, following similar approaches used for prompt and nonprompt J/ψ separation in forward rapidity by Prasad (2023) and for electron source identification by ATLAS (2023). The study enables separate measurements of charm and beauty production dynamics in proton-proton collisions across the full LHC energy range, which is essential for understanding heavy quark energy loss mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma when compared to heavy-ion collision data from ALICE, CMS, and STAR experiments. The authors note that PYTHIA8 with the 4C tune and gluon-move color reconnection mode provides comparable spectral shapes to ALICE data (2021, 2023) but requires scaling factors to match absolute yields.
