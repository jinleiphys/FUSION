# Spin response of neutron matter in ab initio approach

> We propose a general method embedded in the ab initio nuclear framework to reconstruct linear response functions and calculate sum rules. Within our approach, based on the Gaussian integral transform, we consistently treat the groundstate and the excited spectrum. Crucially, the method allows for a robust uncertainty estimation of the spectral reconstruction. We showcase it for the spin response in neutron matter. Our calculations are performed using state-of-the-art many-body coupled-cluster method and Hamiltonians derived in the chiral effective field theory, emphasizing the analysis of finite-size effects. This work serves as a stepping stone towards further studies of neutrino interactions in astrophysical environments from first principles.

# Spin response of neutron matter in ab initio approach

## Key claim
The authors present the first consistent ab initio calculation of the dynamical spin response and sum rules in pure neutron matter using chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians, with a method based on the Gaussian integral transform that allows for robust uncertainty estimation of the spectral reconstruction. The approach is limited to the long-wavelength limit (q → 0) and uses the coupled-cluster doubles approximation, which the authors note yields a small truncation error of approximately 5% of the correlation energy.

## Method
The authors employ the coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) method for nuclear matter on a cubic lattice in momentum space with twisted angle boundary conditions, using a model space of (2n_max+1)^3 momentum points with n_max=3. They reconstruct the spin response function using a Gaussian integral transform expanded in Chebyshev polynomials, where the Chebyshev moments are obtained through an equation-of-motion technique applied to the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian. The method avoids the inversion procedure required by Lorentz or Laplace integral transforms and provides rigorous error bounds on the spectral reconstruction.

## Key numbers
- For the NNLOΔ_GO(394) Hamiltonian at density ρ=0.08 fm⁻³ with N=114 neutrons: Q⁰_σ = 0.0359⁺⁰·⁰⁰⁰⁵_₋₀.₀₀₁₀, Q¹_σ = 2.954⁺⁰·⁰⁵⁹_₋₀.₀₅₉ MeV, ω₁ = 82.26⁺¹·⁴³_₋₀.₈₉ MeV
- For the NNLOΔ_GO(394) Hamiltonian at density ρ=0.12 fm⁻³ with N=114 neutrons: Q⁰_σ = 0.0357⁺⁰·⁰⁰⁰⁶_₋₀.₀₀₁₆, Q¹_σ = 3.376⁺⁰·⁰⁶⁰_₋₀.₀₇₂ MeV, ω₁ = 94.79⁺²·¹⁰_₋₀.₂₄ MeV
- For the NNLOΔ_GO(394) Hamiltonian at density ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ with N=114 neutrons: Q⁰_σ = 0.0298⁺⁰·⁰⁰⁰⁸_₋₀.₀₀₁₉, Q¹_σ = 3.011⁺⁰·⁰⁶⁵_₋₀.₁₀₃ MeV, ω₁ = 101.37⁺²·⁷⁶_₋₀.₉₀ MeV
- The total strength Q⁰_σ is approximately five times lower than values reported in prior work by Shen (2012)
- The first excited state lies approximately 30 MeV above the groundstate at ρ=0.12 fm⁻³ for N=66 particles
- The truncation error in the CCD expansion is approximately 5% of the correlation energy, translating to around 0.04 MeV for the ground-state energy at saturation density

## Context
This work extends the Gaussian integral transform method previously developed by Roggero (2020) and Sobczyk (2021) to the spin response of neutron matter, providing the first ab initio calculation with consistent uncertainty estimation. The results differ substantially from earlier work by Shen (2012) using Quantum Monte Carlo with the AV8 potential, where the total strength is around five times lower and the average excitation energy is considerably higher in the present calculation, which the authors attribute primarily to the different nuclear Hamiltonian. The framework enables future studies of neutrino interactions in astrophysical environments from first principles, with the authors noting that systematic uncertainty from the choice of nuclear interaction remains to be quantified in follow-up work.
