# Reaction models to probe the structure of light exotic nuclei

> We review here theoretical models for describing various types of reactions involving light nuclei on the driplines. Structure features to be extracted from the analysis of such reaction data, as well as those that need to be incorporated in the reaction models for an adequate description of the processes, are also under focus. The major unsolved theoretical issues are discussed, along with some suggestions for future directions of the field.

## Key claim

This review argues that while high-energy reaction models (eikonal, adiabatic, Glauber) have been sufficient to extract general structural features of light dripline nuclei, a new era of low-energy experiments will be required to obtain detailed structure information, even though low-energy reactions are harder to model because adiabatic and eikonal simplifications no longer hold and couplings become more important.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical models for reactions involving light nuclei on the driplines, covering the continuum discretised coupled channels (CDCC) method, the adiabatic (sudden) approximation, few-body Glauber methods including the optical limit, time-dependent semiclassical approaches, and models for total reaction cross sections, elastic and inelastic scattering, breakup, knockout, transfer, and fusion reactions. For each method, the formalism is summarised and the conditions under which approximations are valid are discussed, with emphasis on how projectile few-body structure (weak binding, extended tails, continuum coupling) must be incorporated.

## Key numbers

* Total reaction cross sections for neutron-rich isotopes of He, Li, and Be were used to extract matter radii; the dashed line showing the expected mean-field scaling is \(R_0 A^{1/3}\) with \(R_0 = 1.2\) fm.
* Elastic scattering of \(^6\)He+\(^{12}\)C was measured at 38.3 MeV/nucleon (data from Lapoux).
* Halo nuclei binding energies are typically of the order of 1 MeV or less, making breakup effects even more important than for deuteron or \(^{6,7}\)Li projectiles.
* The eikonal approximation requires \(K_0 R_0 \gg 1\) and \(E \gg |V_0|\) for validity; in practice it works well even when \(E \approx |V_0|\) provided the short-wavelength condition holds and forward-angle scattering is considered.
* The few-body Glauber approach can be applied at energies below 20 MeV/A when corrections to the straight-line assumption are included.

## Context

This review extends earlier experimental reviews on light exotic nuclei (Tanihata, Hansen, Orr) that focused on experimental techniques rather than reaction theory, and conference proceedings (ISOL, IAN, JAKENAM, JAKHIRSH) that gave limited attention to reaction models. It builds on the early work of Tanihata (1985, 1988) that used total reaction cross sections to infer large matter radii, and on the work of Al-Khalili (1993) showing that including granular structure in reaction models leads to even larger deduced halo sizes than one-body density models. The paper enables future work by identifying open theoretical issues, including the need for four-body CDCC for Borromean nuclei, better treatment of low-energy reactions, and theoretical developments for reactions with electron beams.
