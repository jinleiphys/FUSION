# The Hadronic Gas Chiral Phase Transition within Generalized Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study the temperature evolution of the quark-antiquark condensate below the chiral phase transition. The hadronic gas is described using a virial expansion within Generalized Chiral Perturbation Theory. In such way, we can implement both the large or small chiral condensate scenarios and analyze the condensate dependence on the values of the lightest quark masses.

## Key claim

The temperature evolution of the quark-antiquark condensate below the chiral phase transition depends significantly on the actual value of the zero-temperature condensate and the light quark mass ratio \(r = m_s/\hat{m}\), with lower condensate scenarios yielding systematically lower critical temperatures.

## Method

The hadronic gas was described using a relativistic virial expansion truncated at second order, with pion phase shifts obtained from one-loop \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitudes within Generalized Chiral Perturbation Theory (GChPT). The pressure was computed as a function of temperature, and the quark condensate was obtained by differentiating the free energy with respect to the light quark mass \(\hat{m}\), including the dependence of \(M_\pi\), \(M_K\), \(F_\pi\), and \(F_K\) on \(\hat{m}\). Heavier meson states were added as a free gas with Boltzmann suppression.

## Key numbers

* For \(r = 8\) (low condensate scenario), the critical temperature \(T_C\) is around 130 MeV; for \(r \geq 20\) (standard large condensate scenario), \(T_C\) is above 200 MeV.
* The uncertainty in \(T_C\) from parameters in the \(\pi\pi\) phase shifts is \(\pm 5\) MeV at \(r = 26\) and \(\pm 3\) MeV at \(r = 8\).
* The zero-temperature condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_0 \simeq -(280)^3\) MeV\(^3\) for \(\hat{m} = 7\) MeV (standard ChPT value), and vanishes around \(\hat{m} \simeq 30\) MeV.
* The allowed range for the quark mass ratio \(r\) after including one-loop corrections is \(r^*_1 \simeq 8 \leq r \leq r^*_2\) (with \(r^*_2\) up to 39).
* The phenomenological parameter \(\Omega = 4.7 \pm 0.7\) describes the condensate via \(\hat{m}\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_0 \simeq -F_\pi^2 [M_\pi^2 - 4\hat{m}^2 \Omega]\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature treatment of the chiral condensate by Gerber and Leutwyler (1989) from standard ChPT to Generalized Chiral Perturbation Theory, allowing both large and small condensate scenarios. It enables future experimental discrimination between these scenarios through precise \(\pi\pi\) scattering length measurements from \(\pi^+\pi^-\) atom decay (DIRAC experiment). The approach is consistent with earlier GChPT studies by Stern and collaborators (1993-1995) and uses quark mass difference determinations from \(m_s - m_u = (184 \pm 32)\) MeV that do not rely on a large condensate assumption.
