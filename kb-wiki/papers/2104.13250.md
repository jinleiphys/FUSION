# Dynamical exploring the QCD matter at finite temperatures and densities-a short review

> We provide a concise review on recent theory advancements towards full-fledged (3+1)D dynamical descriptions of relativistic nuclear collisions at finite baryon density. Heavy-ion collisions at different collision energies produce strongly-coupled matter and probe the QCD phase transition at the crossover, critical point, and first-order phase transition regions. Dynamical frameworks provide a quantitative tool to extract properties of hot QCD matter and map fireballs to the QCD phase diagram. Outstanding challenges are highlighted when confronting current theoretical frameworks with current and forthcoming experimental measurements from the RHIC beam energy scan programs.

## Key claim

This review argues that while significant progress has been made toward (3+1)D dynamical descriptions of relativistic nuclear collisions at finite baryon density, outstanding challenges remain when confronting current theoretical frameworks with experimental measurements from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan programs, particularly regarding the proper encoding of QCD phase transitions (crossover, critical point, and first-order) into dynamical models.

## Method

The authors survey recent theory developments for dynamical modeling of heavy-ion collisions across the QCD phase diagram, organized by the type of phase transition probed. For the crossover region (collision energies \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \ge 20\) GeV, \(\mu_B \le 250\) MeV), they review dynamical initialization schemes, finite-density equations of state from lattice QCD, transport coefficients including baryon diffusion, and hadronic transport models. For the critical point region, they review equilibrium critical phenomena (universal scaling, multiplicity fluctuations) and dynamical models including Model A, Model B, non-equilibrium chiral fluid dynamics, fluctuating hydrodynamics, hydrodynamics-kinetics, and Hydro+. For the first-order phase transition region, they review nucleation theory and spinodal decomposition approaches.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies for the crossover region: \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \ge 20\) GeV, with net baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B \le 250\) MeV
- RHIC Beam Energy Scan collision energy range: \(3 \sim 200\) A GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Overlap time estimate for \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 20-60\) GeV collisions: \(\tau_\mathrm{overlap} \sim 2R / \sqrt{(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}/(2m_N))^2 - 1}\)
- Lattice QCD constraints suggest the critical point may exist in the region \(T < 140\) MeV, \(\mu_B > 300\) MeV
- Strangeness neutrality condition for heavy-ion collisions: \(n_Q \simeq 0.4 n_B\)
- Out-of-equilibrium corrections to generalized entropy in Hydro+ simulations: within 0.1%

## Context

This review extends the line of work on integrated hybrid models developed for top RHIC and LHC energies (Song 2010, Shen 2014, Bernhard 2016, Everett 2020) toward lower collision energies where baryon density becomes significant. It builds on lattice QCD results for the crossover at \(\mu_B \simeq 0\) (Aoki 2006, Bazavov 2019) and effective model predictions for a first-order phase transition at large chemical potential (Fukushima 2010, Fischer 2018). The review enables future Bayesian inference analyses of RHIC Beam Energy Scan data to extract \(\mu_B\)-dependent QGP transport coefficients, and highlights the need for further development of dynamical models near the first-order phase transition region where the validity of hydrodynamics remains poorly understood.
