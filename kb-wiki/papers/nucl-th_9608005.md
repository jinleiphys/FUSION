# Superheavy Nuclei in the Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> We have carried out a study of superheavy nuclei in the framework of the Relativistic Mean-Field theory. Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations have been performed for nuclei with large proton and neutron numbers. A finite-range pairing force of Gogny type has been used in the RHB calculations. The ground-state properties of very heavy nuclei with atomic numbers Z=100-114 and neutron numbers N=154-190 have been obtained. The results show that in addition to N=184 the neutron numbers N=160 and N=166 exhibit an extra stability as compared to their neighbors. For the case of protons the atomic number Z=106 is shown to demonstrate a closed-shell behavior in the region of well deformed nuclei about N=160. The proton number Z=114 also indicates a shell closure. Indications for a doubly magic character at Z=106 and N=160 are observed. Implications of shell closures on a possible synthesis of superheavy nuclei are discussed.

# Superheavy Nuclei in the Relativistic Mean Field Theory

## Key claim

The Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) theory with a finite-range Gogny pairing force predicts that neutron numbers N=160 and N=166 exhibit extra stability relative to their neighbors in addition to the well-known N=184 shell closure, and that Z=106 shows closed-shell behavior in the deformed region around N=160, with indications of doubly magic character at Z=106 and N=160, but these findings depend on the specific RMF force NL-SH and the spherical or deformed configuration assumed, and the deformed calculations use constant-gap BCS pairing rather than the full Gogny force.

## Method

Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations were performed for even-even superheavy nuclei with atomic numbers Z=100-118 and neutron numbers N=154-190 using the NL-SH parameter set of the RMF theory, with a finite-range Gogny-type pairing force (parameter set D1) in the particle-particle channel for spherical calculations. For axially deformed configurations, the calculations used the constant-gap BCS approximation with pairing gaps from the prescription of Moller and Nix (1992), employing 20 oscillator shells for both spherical and deformed bases. Ground-state properties including two-neutron and two-proton separation energies, pairing energies, alpha-decay half-lives (via the Viola-Seaborg formula with parameters from Sobiczewski et al., 1989), quadrupole and hexadecupole deformations, and single-particle spectra were computed.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing energy vanishes at N=184 for all Z=100-114, confirming a strong magic number at N=184.
- Neutron pairing energy at N=164 is about -1.0 MeV for Z=114, indicating minimal pairing and relatively strong magicity.
- Proton pairing energy vanishes at Z=106 for most nuclides with N=156 to N=164, with pairing energy 4-6 MeV larger at Z=104 or Z=108.
- Alpha-decay half-lives show enhancement at N=164 and a strong plateau up to N=184, with a drastic dip at N=186 for nuclei below Z=112.
- Measured alpha-particle energy for 266106 is 8.63 MeV; calculated Q_alpha values from deformed RMF for Z=106 isotopes are about 7-8 MeV, and for Z=108 isotopes about 9-10 MeV.
- Measured alpha-particle energy for Z=108 is between 9.7 and 9.87 MeV.
- Binding energies from RMF for N=184 isotones (A=290, 292, 294, 298) agree with ETF-SI within 1-2 MeV and with FRDM within a few MeV.
- Quadrupole deformations for N=184 isotones are very close to zero in RMF, indicating spherical shape.

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of shell effects across the periodic table (Lalazissis and Sharma, 1995; Lalazissis, Sharma, and Ring, 1995) into the superheavy region, and provides a microscopic alternative to macroscopic-microscopic approaches such as the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller and Nix (1994) and the Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations of Patyk and Sobiczewski (1991) and Sobiczewski (1994). The predictions of extra stability at N=164 and N=166 and the doubly magic character at Z=106, N=160 differ from FRDM, which predicts shell gaps at Z=104, 106, 108, and 110 consecutively, and from the ETF-SI model (Audi, Pearson, and Dutta, 1992), which shows no clear magicity at N=162 but does show a kink at N=184. The results enable targeted experimental searches for superheavy nuclei in the deformed region around Z=106 and N=160-166, complementing ongoing efforts at GSI, GANIL, Berkeley, and Dubna.
