# Semileptonic decays $D\toπ^+π^-e^+ν_e$ and $D_s\toπ^+π^-e^+ν_e$ as the probe of constituent quark-antiquark pairs in the light scalar mesons

> Decays $D\toπ^+π^-e^+ν_e$ and $D_s\toπ^+ π^-e^+ν_e$ serve as probes that check the existence of constituent $q\bar q$ components in the wave functions of scalar mesons decaying into $π^+π^-$. There exists a great deal of concrete evidence in favor of the exotic four-quark nature of light scalars. At the same time, the further expansion of the area of the $q^2\bar q^2$ model validity for light scalars on ever new processes seems extremely interesting and important. We analyze the BESIII and CLEO data on the decays $D^+\to π^+π^- e^+ν_e$ and $D^+_s\to π^+π^- e^+ν_e$ and show that the results of these experiments together can be interpreted in favor of the four-quark nature of light scalar mesons $σ(500)$ and $f_0(980)$. Our approach can also be applied to the description of other similar decays involving light scalars.

# Semileptonic Decays $D\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e$ and $D_s\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e$ as a Probe of Constituent Quark-Antiquark Pairs in Light Scalar Mesons

## Key claim

The combined BESIII and CLEO data on the $\pi^+\pi^-$ mass spectra in $D^+\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e$ and $D_s^+\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e$ can be interpreted in favor of the four-quark ($q^2\bar q^2$) nature of the light scalar mesons $\sigma(500)$ and $f_0(980)$, while a model assuming direct $q\bar q$ couplings to these scalars sharply contradicts the BESIII data at $\sqrt{s}<1$ GeV.

## Method

The authors analyzed the $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distributions from BESIII (2019) for $D^+\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e$ and CLEO (2009) for $D_s^+\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e$, comparing two production mechanisms. First, they tested a direct $q\bar q$ coupling model where $\sigma(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ couple to $d\bar d$ and $s\bar s$ pairs created in the semileptonic decays, using a mixed-resonance propagator formalism with parameters from Achasov and Kiselev (2006). Second, they implemented a four-quark production mechanism where seed $q\bar q$ fluctuations ($d\bar d\to\pi\pi$, $d\bar d\to K\bar K$, $s\bar s\to K\bar K$) are dressed by final-state strong interactions, using known $S$-wave $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitudes and $K\bar K\to\pi\pi$ transition amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction for $D_s^+\to f_0(980)e^+\nu_e$, $f_0(980)\to\pi^+\pi^-$: $(20\pm3\pm1)\times10^{-4}$ (CLEO), corresponding to a width $\Gamma = (39.7\pm6.3)\times10^8$ s$^{-1}$.
- Branching fraction for $D^+\to\sigma(500)e^+\nu_e$, $\sigma(500)\to\pi^+\pi^-$: $(6.30\pm0.43\pm0.32)\times10^{-4}$ (BESIII), corresponding to $\Gamma = (6.06\pm0.51)\times10^8$ s$^{-1}$.
- Upper limit for $D^+\to f_0(980)e^+\nu_e$, $f_0(980)\to\pi^+\pi^-$: $\mathcal{B} < 0.28\times10^{-4}$ (BESIII), corresponding to $\Gamma < 0.27\times10^8$ s$^{-1}$.
- Ratio $\Gamma_{D_s^+\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e} / \Gamma_{D^+\to\pi^+\pi^-e^+\nu_e}$: $6.55\pm1.18$ from data; the direct $q\bar q$ model yields approximately 5.62; the four-quark model yields approximately 6.55.
- The four-quark model uses subtraction constants $\tilde{C}_{\pi^+\pi^-}=1.8$ and $\tilde{C}_{K^+K^-}=1.0$ to achieve simultaneous description of both mass spectra.
- The ratio of Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix elements: $|V_{cs}|/|V_{cd}| \simeq 20.92$.

## Context

This work extends the program proposed by Achasov and Kiselev (2012, 2014) to probe the internal structure of light scalar mesons via semileptonic $D$ and $B$ decays. It directly contradicts the predictions of a naive $q\bar q$ constituent model for $\sigma(500)$ and $f_0(980)$, and instead supports the four-quark ($q^2\bar q^2$) picture originally proposed by Jaffe (1977). The analysis enables future application of the same four-quark production formalism to other semileptonic decays involving light scalars, such as $D^0\to\pi^-\eta e^+\nu_e$ and $B$ meson decays.
