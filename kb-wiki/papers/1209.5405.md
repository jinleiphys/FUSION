# Heavy quark energy loss and thermalization in hot and dense nuclear matter

> We study heavy quark energy loss and thermalization in hot and dense nuclear medium. The diffusion of heavy quarks is calculated via a Langevin equation, both for a static medium as well as for a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium generated by a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model. We investigate how the initial configuration of the QGP and its properties affect the final state spectra and elliptic flow of heavy flavor mesons and non-photonic electrons. It is observed that both the geometric anisotropy of the initial profile and the flow profile of the hydrodynamic medium play important roles in the heavy quark energy loss process and the development of elliptic flow. Within our definition of the thermalization criterion and for reasonable values of the diffusion constant, we observe thermalization times that are longer than the lifetime of the QGP phase.

## Key claim
Within the authors' definition of thermalization and for the diffusion coefficient value that describes experimental data, charm quarks may remain far off equilibrium during the entire QGP lifetime; the strong coupling revealed by heavy flavor suppression and elliptic flow is insufficient to conclude that heavy quarks actually thermalize with the medium.

## Method
Heavy quark diffusion was simulated via a Langevin equation including only collisional energy loss, both in a static medium at fixed temperature and in a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamic model of the QGP. The temperature parameters of the heavy quark ensemble were extracted from both energy and momentum spectra and compared to the medium temperature to test thermalization. Final-state heavy flavor mesons and non-photonic electrons were obtained via Pythia 6.4 fragmentation and decay.

## Key numbers
- Charm quarks initialized with 5 GeV/c momentum in a static medium at 300 MeV required about 25 fm/c to reach equilibrium (temperature parameters from different methods merge and approach medium temperature).
- With diffusion coefficient D = 6/(2πT), charm quarks in a realistic QGP medium (200 GeV Au-Au, impact parameter 2.4 fm) never thermalized during the QGP phase; with D = 1.5/(2πT), thermalization was achieved within the QGP lifetime.
- Charm quarks with initial momentum below 1 GeV/c could thermalize within the QGP temperature range and lifetime; those with initial momentum above 3 GeV/c took much longer.
- A difference of 0.7% in the charm-to-bottom mixing ratio led to a variation of approximately 25% in electron v2 for D = 6/(2πT) and over 30% for D = 1.5/(2πT).

## Context
This work extends the Langevin-based heavy quark transport framework of Svetitsky (1987) and Moore (2004) by coupling it to a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model from Nonaka (2006). It contradicts the assumption that observed large elliptic flow and strong suppression of heavy mesons (Adare 2010) imply thermalization. The results enable systematic separation of geometric versus flow contributions to heavy quark observables and motivate inclusion of radiative energy loss and hadronic interactions in future studies.
