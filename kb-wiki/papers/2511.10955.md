# Constraining Neutron Capture Cross Sections for $^{88}\mathrm{Y}$ with Gamma-ray Strength Function in $(p,p^\primeγ)$ Surrogate Reaction

> We demonstrate to extract $^{88}\mathrm{Y}(n,γ)$ cross sections using the $(p,p'γ)$ surrogate reaction with proper treatment of the spin-parity distribution of the compound nucleus $^{89}\mathrm{Y}$. Experimental data of both $γ$-decay probability and $γ$-ray strength function are used to constrain the nuclear model parameters within a computational framework combining the Bayesian optimization and Markov chain Monte Carlo method, which helps to significantly reduce the $(n,γ)$ data uncertainty. The $^{88}\mathrm{Y}(n,γ)$ cross sections are then extracted with a narrow uncertainty of 7.6\%-23.1\% within neutron energy range of 0.01 to 3.0 MeV for the first time, where no experimental data are available. Moreover, our method is verified with the $^{88}\mathrm{Sr}(p,γ)$ reaction, of which the measured data are available for comparison. This work opens interesting perspectives on the matter of extracting ($n,γ$) reaction cross sections on unstable nuclei as surrogate reaction experiments are becoming widely available.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that simultaneously constraining nuclear model parameters using both experimental γ-decay probability and γ-ray strength function data, rather than only the γ-decay probability as in prior surrogate-reaction studies, reduces the uncertainty of the extracted ⁸⁸Y(n,γ) cross sections by a factor of 1.8, yielding the first reported cross sections for this reaction with uncertainties of 7.6%-23.1% in the neutron energy range 0.01-3.0 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the ⁸⁹Y(p,p'γ) surrogate reaction to populate the compound nucleus ⁸⁹Y, measuring the γ-decay probability Pγ(E*) with silicon telescopes and HPGe detectors at the HI-13 tandem accelerator. They calculated the spin-parity distribution of the compound nucleus via coupled-channel calculations with FRESCO, then performed a joint optimization of eight parameters (E1 and M1 γ-ray strength function resonance parameters, level density parameter a, and shell damping parameter γ) using a Bayesian optimization and Markov chain Monte Carlo framework to simultaneously reproduce experimental Pγ(E*) and γSF data. The optimized parameters were validated by calculating ⁸⁸Sr(p,γ) cross sections and comparing with direct measurements, then used to extract ⁸⁸Y(n,γ) cross sections via the Hauser-Feshbach formalism.

## Key numbers

- The ⁸⁸Y(n,γ) cross sections were extracted with uncertainties of 7.6% at neutron energy En=0.01 MeV and 23.1% at En=3.0 MeV, for the first time in this energy range.
- The optimized γSF parameters were: E_E1=17.04 MeV, σ_E1=219.15 mb, Γ_E1=5.23 MeV; E_M1=7.82 MeV, σ_M1=0.75 mb, Γ_M1=2.61 MeV; NLD parameters a=11.67 and γ=0.053 MeV, yielding χ²_w=6.6.
- The measured Pγ(E*) data covered excitation energies E* from 7.6 to 13.3 MeV; the γSF data were fitted over Eγ from 2 to 17 MeV.
- The average spin of the compound nucleus from coupled-channel calculations was J̄=3.4 ħ, compared to J̄=4.3 ħ from TALYS for the neutron-induced reaction.
- The ⁸⁸Y half-life is 107 days; the neutron separation energy S_n for ⁸⁹Y is 11.48 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the surrogate reaction method beyond the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation, which failed for spherical nuclei like Y and Zr isotopes as shown by Escher et al. (2012) and Boutoux et al. (2012). It improves upon the Oslo method used by Larsen et al. (2016) for ⁸⁸Y(n,γ), which had larger uncertainties, by adding γSF constraints to the Pγ-only fitting used in prior surrogate studies by Escher et al. (2018), Ratkiewicz et al. (2019), and Perez-Sanchez et al. (2020). The validated approach enables reliable (n,γ) cross section extraction for unstable nuclei where direct measurements are impossible, with implications for s-process nucleosynthesis models and nuclear energy applications.
