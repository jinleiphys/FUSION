# Towards a unified realistic shell-model Hamiltonian with the monopole-based universal force

> We propose a unified realistic shell-model Hamiltonian employing the pairing plus multipole Hamiltonian combined with the monopole interaction constructed starting from the monopole-based universal force by Otsuka it et al. (Phys. Rev. Lett. 104, 012501 (2010)). It is demonstrated that the proposed PMMU model can consistently describe a large amount of spectroscopic data as well as binding energies in the pf and pf5/2g9/2 shell spaces, and could serve as a practical shell model for even heavier mass regions.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a Hamiltonian combining pairing-plus-multipole terms with the monopole interaction derived from the monopole-based universal force (V_MU) can serve as a unified realistic shell-model interaction for different mass regions, but only after refitting some monopole terms and adjusting strength parameters separately for each model space.

## Method

The PMMU model combines a pairing-plus-multipole Hamiltonian (including J=0 and J=2 pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole, and octupole-octupole terms) with monopole matrix elements constructed from the monopole-based universal force of Otsuka et al. (2010), using a Gaussian parameter of μ=1.0 fm and harmonic oscillator basis with ℏω=41A^{-1/3}. The monopole matrix elements were modified (labeled V_m^{MU'}) by refitting 10 monopole terms for the pf shell and 14 monopole terms for the pf5/2g9/2 shell, with all monopole matrix elements scaled by factors (42/A)^{0.3} and (58/A)^{0.3} respectively. Shell-model calculations were performed with the code MSHELL64, fitting binding energies from 95 nuclei in the pf shell and 91 nuclei in the pf5/2g9/2 shell.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation for binding energies in the pf shell: 707 keV (from 95 nuclei: Ca, Sc, Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni isotopes)
- RMS deviation for binding energies in the pf5/2g9/2 shell: 691 keV (from 91 nuclei: Ni, Cu, Zn, Ga, Ge, As, Se isotopes)
- pf shell strength parameters: g0=20.2/A, g2=228.2/A^{5/3}, χ2=228.2/A^{5/3}, χ3=0.0
- pf5/2g9/2 shell strength parameters: g0=18.0/A, g2=0.0, χ2=334.0/A^{5/3}, χ3=259.2/A^{2}
- pf shell single-particle energies (MeV): ε_f7/2=-7.78, ε_p3/2=-5.68, ε_f5/2=-1.28, ε_p1/2=-3.88
- pf5/2g9/2 shell single-particle energies (MeV): ε_p3/2=-9.40, ε_f5/2=-8.29, ε_p1/2=-7.49, ε_g9/2=-5.70
- Calculated B(E2;2_1^+→0_1^+) for 56Ni: 181.2 e^2fm^2 (experiment: 120±24 e^2fm^2)
- Calculated B(E2;2_1^+→0_1^+) for 72Ge: 462.0 e^2fm^2 (experiment: 418.1±72 e^2fm^2)
- Calculated B(E2;4_1^+→2_1^+) for 72Ge: 712.1 e^2fm^2 (experiment: 658.3±88 e^2fm^2)

## Context

This work extends the EPQQM model (extended pairing-plus-quadrupole-quadrupole with monopole corrections) previously applied by the same group to various mass regions (Hasegawa 2001, Kaneko 2002, Kaneko 2008, Kaneko 2011), which relied on phenomenological monopole adjustments and could not describe binding energies or unusual structures like the first excited 0+ state in Zn and Ge isotopes near N=40. The PMMU model replaces those phenomenological corrections with the monopole-based universal force of Otsuka et al. (2010), which was previously applied only to light nuclei (Yuan 2012, Utsuno 2012). The monopole matrix elements show striking similarity to those of the GXPF1A interaction (Honma 2004) for the pf shell and JUN45 (Honma 2009) for the pf5/2g9/2 shell, suggesting the PMMU model could enable unified shell-model calculations for heavier systems such as the A~100 mass region.
