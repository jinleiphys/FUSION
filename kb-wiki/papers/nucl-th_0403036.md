# Recombination Models

> We review the current status of recombination and coalescence models that have been successfully applied to describe hadronization in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies. Basic concepts as well as actual implementations of the idea are discussed. We try to evaluate where we stand in our understanding at the moment and what remains to be done in the future.

## Key claim

Recombination/coalescence models successfully describe hadronization in dense parton systems at RHIC, but only for hadrons at intermediate transverse momenta (roughly 2–5 GeV/c for mesons and up to 6 GeV/c for baryons); the simple recombination formula does not describe bulk hadron production, and the model becomes increasingly difficult below 2 GeV/c.

## Method

The paper reviews four implementations of recombination/coalescence (ReCo): Duke/Minnesota/Kyoto, Ohio State/Wayne State, Oregon, and Texas A&M/Budapest. The Duke and Oregon groups evaluate the recombination yield analytically using a convolution of one-particle parton distributions with hadron wave functions in longitudinal momentum space, while the Ohio and TAM groups use Monte Carlo implementations that can be connected to string or parton cascade models. The Duke group strictly separates soft (thermal) partons for recombination from hard (minijet) partons for fragmentation, whereas the TAM group includes coalescence of soft and hard partons, and the Oregon group replaces fragmentation functions by a scenario where minijet partons develop a shower that subsequently recombines.

## Key numbers

- Proton/pion ratio ~1 observed in central Au+Au collisions between P_T = 1.5 and 4 GeV/c (PHENIX, 2003)
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA for baryons is close to 1 up to 4 GeV/c (PHENIX, STAR, 2003)
- Duke group uses thermal distribution with temperature T = 175 MeV and average radial flow velocity v = 0.55 c for soft partons in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Recombination dominates over fragmentation at intermediate transverse momenta between 2 and 5 GeV/c for mesons
- Elliptic flow scaling laws v_2^M(P_T) = 2 v_2(P_T/2) for mesons and v_2^B(P_T) = 3 v_2(P_T/3) for baryons are confirmed by experimental data for P_T > 1 GeV/c (STAR, PHENIX, 2003)
- Kink in spectra data around P_T = 4 GeV/c for mesons and 6 GeV/c for baryons marks the transition from soft (recombination) to hard (perturbative) particle production

## Context

This review extends the recombination concept first formulated by Hwa and collaborators (1977) for hadron-hadron collisions and later applied to heavy ion collisions by Gupta (1983) and the ALCOR model (Biro, Levai, Zimanyi, 1995). The recent revival was triggered by Voloshin (2002) showing that elliptic flow v_2 at RHIC obeys valence quark scaling, and by Fries, Muller, Nonaka, Bass (2003) and Greco, Ko, Levai (2003) explaining baryon enhancement and the absence of nuclear suppression in baryon spectra. The paper identifies open questions including the role of resonances, charm hadron systematics, soft-hard coalescence, hadron-hadron correlations, and extension to LHC energies where increased jet quenching and a brighter thermal source may push the recombination-dominated region to even higher P_T.
