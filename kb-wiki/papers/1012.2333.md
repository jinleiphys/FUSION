# Latent heat of nuclear matter

> We study the latent heat of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter using self-consistent mean-field calculations with a few Skyrme forces. The temperature dependence of the latent heat is rather independent of the mean-field parametrization and can be characterized by a few parameters. At low temperatures, the latent heat tends to the saturation energy. Near the critical point, the latent heat goes to zero with a well-determined mean-field critical exponent. A maximum value of the latent heat in the range l ~ 25-30 MeV is found at intermediate temperatures, which might have experimental relevance. All these features can be explained from very basic principles.

## Key claim

The latent heat of symmetric nuclear matter, computed with Skyrme mean-field forces, shows a temperature dependence that is largely independent of the specific parametrization, with a maximum in the range 25–30 MeV at intermediate temperatures; this behavior can be explained from basic quantum statistical mechanics arguments rather than from details of the effective interaction.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using four Skyrme effective interactions (BSk17, ZR1, SLy9, and LNS). They solved the coexistence conditions (equal chemical potential and pressure between liquid and gas phases) at each temperature, then computed the latent heat per particle from the entropy difference between the two phases, l = T(s_g − s_l). Analytical low-temperature and near-critical limits were derived using Sommerfeld expansions for the liquid and classical ideal-gas expressions for the dilute gas phase.

## Key numbers

- For BSk17, the latent heat maximum is l_max = 29.9 MeV at T = 8.7 MeV, with a critical temperature T_c = 15.6 MeV.
- For all Skyrme forces studied, the reduced latent heat peaks at T/T_c ∼ 0.5–0.6 with l_max/e_0 ∼ 1.7–2.
- The zero-temperature limit of the latent heat equals the saturation energy: l(T→0) = e_0.
- The simple analytical model predicts a maximum at T_max ≈ 11 MeV with l_max ≈ 30 MeV.
- Experimental latent heat values extracted from caloric curves are l ∼ 4–8 MeV (Pochodzalla 1995, Lee 1997, Natowitz 2002), from heat capacity measurements l ∼ 2–4 MeV (D'Agostino 2000), and from bimodality analysis l ∼ 8 MeV (Bonnet 2009).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter using Skyrme and Gogny mean-fields (Rios 2010) by focusing specifically on the latent heat, a quantity that had received little prior attention. The results provide a theoretical baseline for interpreting experimental latent heat extractions from heavy-ion collisions, such as those from caloric curves (Pochodzalla 1995) and bimodality (Bonnet 2009). The reduced scaling behavior suggests a degree of universality across different mean-field parametrizations, enabling future comparisons with microscopic many-body calculations (Baldo 1999, Rios 2008) and with experimental data from finite nuclear systems.
