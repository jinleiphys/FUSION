# Time and space dependence of electromagnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Exact analytical solution for the space-time evolution of electromagnetic field in electrically conducting nuclear matter produced in heavy-ion collisions is discussed. It is argued that the parameter that controls the strength of the matter effect on the field evolution is $σγb$, where $σ$ is electrical conductivity, $γ$ is the Lorentz boost-factor and $b$ is the characteristic transverse size of the matter. When this parameter is of the order one or larger, which is the case at RHIC and LHC, space-time dependence of electromagnetic field is completely different form that in vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parameter controlling the strength of matter effects on electromagnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions is σγb (electrical conductivity times Lorentz factor times characteristic transverse size); when this parameter is of order one or larger, which occurs at RHIC and LHC, the space-time dependence of the electromagnetic field is completely different from the vacuum case.

## Method

The author solves Maxwell's equations in a conducting medium for a point charge moving at relativistic speed, assuming ε=μ=1 but finite electrical conductivity σ. The solution is obtained via Fourier transform, and the late-time behavior is extracted from the pole structure in the complex frequency plane. The point-charge solution is then integrated over the nuclear charge distribution to obtain the electromagnetic field of two colliding nuclei at finite impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC: γ=100, σ≈5.8 MeV, b=7 fm yields γσb=19, placing the system firmly in the diffusive regime
- For γσb≫1, the magnetic field decays as 1/x_-² at late times (diffusive solution), compared to 1/x_-³ in vacuum
- The diffusive solution (Eq. a21) completely overlaps with the exact numerical solution for σ≈5.8 MeV at all times except t<0.1 fm
- At artificially reduced conductivity σ≈0.01 MeV, the field initially follows the vacuum solution before transitioning to diffusive behavior at later times

## Context

This work extends the author's previous results (Tuchin 2010, Tuchin 2013) showing that electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma dramatically alters electromagnetic field evolution, and disagrees with claims made by McLerran (2013). The analytical solution enables calculation of electromagnetic field components at any point in the transverse plane and at any time, which is essential for understanding observables sensitive to the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, such as the chiral magnetic effect. The paper provides the mathematical framework to compute fields for realistic nuclear charge distributions rather than point charges.
