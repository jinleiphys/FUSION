# Jost function for coupled channels

> Recently, we proposed an exact method for direct calculation of the Jost function for central potentials (which may have Coulombic tails) and the Jost matrix for non-central short range potentials. This method works for all real or complex momenta of physical interest, including the spectral points corresponding to bound and Siegert states. In this work we extend it for potentials which couple channels with different thresholds. The proposed method is based on simple differential equations of the first order, which can be easily solved numerically. Thus, the spectrum generated by any given potential can be thoroughly investigated. At the same time the physical wave function can be obtained having the correct asymptotic behaviour.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed exact method for direct calculation of the Jost function (for central potentials) and Jost matrix (for non-central short-range potentials) to the case of coupled channels with different thresholds, claiming the method works for all real or complex momenta of physical interest including bound, scattering, and resonance (Siegert) states.

## Method

The authors introduce an ansatz expressing the regular solution matrix of the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation in terms of Riccati-Hankel functions multiplied by unknown matrix functions \({\cal F}^{(\pm)}(E,r)\). Substituting this ansatz yields a system of first-order differential equations for \({\cal F}^{(\pm)}(E,r)\) which are integrated from \(r=0\) outward; at large distances where the potential vanishes, these functions become constant and coincide with the Jost matrix and its counterpart. For resonance states in the complex energy plane, a complex rotation of the radial coordinate \(r = x e^{i\theta}\) is performed to ensure the asymptotic limit exists.

## Key numbers

- For a two-channel S-wave potential with thresholds \(E_1=0\) and \(E_2=0.1\) (atomic units), the first resonance energy is \(E_r = 4.7681968188\) a.u. with total width \(\Gamma = 0.0014201920\) a.u.
- The partial widths for the first resonance are \(\Gamma_1 = 0.000051103\) a.u. and \(\Gamma_2 = 0.001368733\) a.u.
- The Noro and Taylor (1993) reference values for comparison are \(E_r = 4.7682\) a.u., \(\Gamma = 0.001420\) a.u., \(\Gamma_1 = 0.000059\) a.u., and \(\Gamma_2 = 0.001361\) a.u.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier single-channel Jost function method (Rakityansky and Sofianos, 1997) and their non-central short-range potential Jost matrix method (Rakityansky and Sofianos, 1998) to coupled channels with different thresholds. The approach reproduces results from Noro and Taylor (1993) who used square-integrable function expansions, and additionally locates three bound states and several broad resonances not previously reported. The method enables straightforward calculation of partial widths, which the authors note is difficult with most prior approaches.
