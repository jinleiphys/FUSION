# Regge analysis of pion-pion (and pion-kaon) scattering for energy s^{1/2}>1.4 GeV

> We perform a detailed Regge analysis of NN, pion-N and pion-pion scattering. From it, we find expressions that represent the pion-pion scattering amplitudes with an accuracy of a few percent, for exchange of isospin zero, and $\sim15%$ for exchange of isospin 1, and this for energies s^{1/2}> 1.4 GeV and for momentum transfers |t|^{1/2}< 0.4 GeV. These Regge formulas are perfectly compatible with the low energy (s^{1/2}< 1.4 GeV) scattering amplitudes deduced from pion-pion phase shift analyses as well as with higher energy (s^{1/2}>1.4 GeV) experimental pion-pion cross sections. They are also compatible with NN and pion-N experimental cross sections using factorization, a property that we check with great precision. This contrasts with results from phase shift analyses of the pion-pion scattering amplitude which bear little resemblance to reality in the region $1.4<s^{1/2}<2 GeV$, as they are not well defined and increasingly violate a number of physical requirements as energy grows. Pion-kaon scattering is also considered, and we present a Regge analysis for these processes valid for energies s^{1/2}>1.7 GeV. As a byproduct of our analysis we present also a fit of $NN$, $πN$ and $KN$ cross sections valid from c.m. kinetic energy $E_{\rm kin}\simeq1$ GeV to multi TeV energies.

# Regge analysis of pion-pion (and pion-kaon) scattering for energy s^{1/2}>1.4 GeV

## Key claim

The Regge formulas presented represent the pion-pion scattering amplitudes with an accuracy of a few percent for isospin zero exchange and approximately 15% for isospin one exchange, for energies s^{1/2}>1.4 GeV and momentum transfers |t|^{1/2}<0.4 GeV, but only when the problematic low-energy phase shift analyses (which the authors argue are unreliable in the region 1.4<s^{1/2}<2 GeV) are replaced by Regge behavior that is compatible with experimental cross sections and factorization.

## Method

The authors performed a detailed Regge analysis of NN, pion-N, KN, pion-pion and pion-K scattering, using factorization to relate the Regge residues across different reactions. They fitted experimental total cross section data for NN, pion-N and pion-pion scattering simultaneously, using the Pomeron, P' (associated with the f2(1270) resonance) and rho Regge poles, with the rho trajectory taken as degenerate with the P' trajectory. The analysis was extended to multi-TeV energies using a modified parametrization compatible with the Froissart bound.

## Key numbers

- The Pomeron intercept is fixed at α_P(0)=1, with slope α'_P=0.2±0.1 GeV^{-2}
- The rho trajectory intercept is α_ρ(0)=0.52±0.02, with slope α'_ρ=0.90 GeV^{-2} and curvature α''_ρ=-0.3 GeV^{-4}
- The best-fit Pomeron residue is β_P=2.56±0.03, the P' residue is β_{P'}=1.05±0.05, and the rho residue is β_ρ=0.94±0.14
- The isospin-2 exchange residue is β_2=0.2±0.2
- The ratio of nucleon-to-pion couplings is f_{N/π}=1.408±0.005
- The predicted total pp cross section at the LHC is σ_pp=116±4 mb
- The universal diffractive slope parameter is b=(2.4±0.2) GeV^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the Regge analysis approach of Rarita et al. (1970s) and Froggatt and Petersen (1977) to provide accurate high-energy input for dispersive studies of pion-pion scattering, specifically for use with Roy equations. It directly contradicts the high-energy input used by Ananthanarayan, Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler (ACGL, 2001) and subsequent works by Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler (2001) and Descotes et al., which the authors argue uses physically unacceptable amplitudes that violate Regge theory and are incompatible with experimental pion-pion cross sections. The results enable improved low-energy analyses of pion-pion scattering via Roy equations and sum rules, and provide a parametrization of NN, pion-N and KN cross sections valid from approximately 1 GeV to multi-TeV energies.
