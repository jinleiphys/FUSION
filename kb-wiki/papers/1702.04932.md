# Muon-neutrino-induced charged current pion production on nuclei

> [Background] Long-Baseline experiments such as T2K, NOvA or the planned Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) require theoretical descriptions of the complete event in a neutrino-nucleus reaction. Since nuclear targets are used this requires a good understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions. [Purpose] One of the dominant reaction channels in neutrino-nucleus interactions is pion production. This paper aims for a coherent view on all charged current charged pion production data that are avaible from the experiments MiniBooNE, the near detector experiment at T2K and MINERvA. [Methods] Pion production is treated through excitations of nucleon resonances, including background terms, and deep inelastic scattering. The final state interactions of the produced pions are described within the Giessen-Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) implementation of quantum-kinetic transport theory. [Results] Results are given for MiniBooNE, the near detector experiment at T2K and for MINERvA. While the theoretical results for MiniBooNE differ from the data both in shape and magnitude, their agreement both with the T2K and the MINERvA data is good for all pion and lepton observables. Predictions for pion spectra are shown for MicroBooNE and NOvA. [Conclusions] Based on the GiBUU model of lepton-nucleus interactions a consistent, good theoretical description of CC charged pion production data from the T2K ND and the MINERvA experiments is possible, without any parameter tunes. The MiniBooNE data cannot be reproduced.

# Muon-neutrino-induced charged current pion production on nuclei

## Key claim
The GiBUU transport model, without any parameter tuning, provides a consistent and good theoretical description of charged-current charged pion production data from T2K ND and MINERvA, but the MiniBooNE pion data cannot be reproduced in either shape or magnitude, indicating an incompatibility between the MiniBooNE dataset and the other experiments.

## Method
Pion production on nuclei was treated as a two-step process: first, pion production on a nucleon via a superposition of nucleon resonance excitations (including background terms) and deep inelastic scattering, with quantum mechanical interference maintained; second, final-state interactions of the produced pions were simulated using the Giessen-Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) implementation of quantum-kinetic transport theory, including elastic scattering, resonance excitations, charge transfer, and absorption. All calculations used the same code version (GiBUU 2016) with the same parameter set, with the ANL data set used to determine nonresonant contributions and without in-medium broadening of the Delta resonance.

## Key numbers
- MiniBooNE: theory fails to reproduce both the absolute magnitude and shape of the pion kinetic energy spectrum; the data show a shape feature at about 0.18 GeV kinetic energy that is not seen in the calculation.
- T2K ND (water target): pion momentum spectrum described very well except at the lowest pion momentum (below 0.2 GeV); the discrepancy at low Q2 is about 0.2 x 10^-40 cm^2 per nucleon, compatible with the experimentally measured coherent cross section of about 3 +/- 1 x 10^-40 cm^2 on carbon.
- MINERvA (CH target, W < 1.4 GeV cut): good agreement for the kinetic energy spectrum except at the lowest energy point; angular distribution shows discrepancy at angles below about 40 degrees.
- MINERvA (CH target, W_rec < 1.8 GeV cut): good agreement for kinetic energy spectrum except at T_pi = 0.0475 GeV; multi-pion events increase the cross section by about 50% compared to 1-pion events; muon momentum and angular distributions show perfect agreement over the full range.
- NOvA prediction: pion kinetic energy distribution peaks at about 0.08 GeV, independent of beam energy; cross section for multiple pi+ production is much larger than single pion production at NOvA energies.
- MicroBooNE prediction: pion production is dominated by 1-pi events due to the relatively low neutrino energy.

## Context
This work extends the GiBUU model comparisons previously reported by Lalakulich et al. (2012) and Mosel (2015) for MiniBooNE and MINERvA, using an updated version with improved nuclear ground state treatment, free Delta width, and different DIS onset. The paper addresses the long-standing discrepancy between MiniBooNE pion data and theoretical calculations noted by Leitner et al. (2008), Lalakulich et al. (2011), and Hernandez et al. (2013), and the incompatibility between MiniBooNE and MINERvA datasets previously identified by Sobczyk (2014) and Alvarez-Ruso (2016). The results enable reliable predictions for upcoming MicroBooNE and NOvA experiments, and support the use of GiBUU as a neutrino event generator for long-baseline experiments such as T2K, NOvA, and DUNE.
