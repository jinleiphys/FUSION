# On the eta-eta' complex in the SD-BS approach

> The bound-state Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter (SD-BS) approach is chirally well-behaved and provides a reliable treatment of the eta-eta' complex although a ladder approximation is employed. Allowing for the effects of the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking in the quark-antiquark annihilation, leads to the improved eta-eta' mass matrix.

## Key claim

The bound-state Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter (SD-BS) approach, despite employing a ladder approximation that cannot calculate the gluon axial anomaly contribution from first principles, provides a reliable treatment of the eta-eta' complex when the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking in the quark-antiquark annihilation is parameterized via a strangeness attenuation factor X, leading to an improved eta-eta' mass matrix.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Schwinger-Dyson equation for dressed quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for q\bar q bound states in a rainbow-ladder approximation with an infrared-enhanced gluon propagator. They introduce a strangeness attenuation parameter X into the eta-eta' mass matrix in the f\bar f basis to suppress the nonperturbative quark annihilation amplitude when strange quarks are involved, estimating X from the ratio of reduced two-photon decay amplitudes T_{s\bar s}(0,0)/T_{u\bar u}(0,0) calculated within the same SD-BS model. The free parameter 3\beta (the gluon anomaly contribution to the singlet eta_0 mass squared) is then fixed by requiring the trace of the 2x2 mass matrix to match the empirical m_eta^2 + m_eta'^2.

## Key numbers

- Nonstrange-to-strange constituent mass ratio from SD-BS model: M_u(0)/M_s(0) = 0.615, with M_u(0) = 375 MeV (bare mass 3.1 MeV) and M_s(0) = 610 MeV
- Strangeness attenuation parameter X = 0.663, estimated from the ratio of reduced two-photon amplitudes T_{s\bar s}(0,0)/T_{u\bar u}(0,0)
- Chiral-limiting singlet mass squared: 3\beta = 0.832 GeV^2 = (912 MeV)^2, just 0.5% below the phenomenological value of (917 MeV)^2
- Resulting mixing angles: phi = 41.3 degrees, theta = -13.4 degrees
- Resulting masses: m_eta = 588 MeV, m_eta' = 933 MeV (compared to experimental 547 MeV and 958 MeV)
- For comparison, fitting both 3\beta and X as free parameters gives 3\beta = 0.753 GeV^2 = (868 MeV)^2, X = 0.835, but theta = -17.9 degrees

## Context

This work extends the SD-BS approach of Klabucar and Kekez (1997) which could not satisfactorily reproduce the eta' mass when successfully fitting the eta mass and gamma-gamma widths, because it lacked the strangeness attenuation factor X. The results agree with the phenomenological mass-matrix analysis using the Gell-Mann-Okubo formula (giving m_eta8 approx 567 MeV and m_eta0 approx 947 MeV) and with the Feldmann-Kroll-Stech two-mixing-angle scheme (Feldmann 1999) which finds phi approx 42 degrees. The approach also shows reasonable agreement with UKQCD lattice results (2000) which give mixing parameters x_ss = 0.13 GeV^2, x_nn = 0.292 GeV^2, x_ns = 0.218 GeV^2 and a mixing angle theta = -10.2 degrees. The authors propose extending this work to finite temperatures to study the temperature dependence of the eta-eta' mass matrix and the U_A(1) symmetry restoration.
