# Description of Nuclear Structure Effects in Subbarrier Fusion by the Interacting Boson Model

> Recent theoretical developments in using the Interacting Boson Model to describe nuclear structure effects in fusion reactions below the Coulomb barrier are reviewed. Methods dealing with linear and all orders coupling between the nuclear excitations and the translational motion are discussed, and the latter is found to lead to a better description of the barrier distribution data. A systematic study of the available data (cross sections, barrier and spin distributions) in rare-earth nuclei is presented.

# Description of Nuclear Structure Effects in Subbarrier Fusion by the Interacting Boson Model

## Key claim
The Interacting Boson Model (IBM) can describe subbarrier fusion cross sections, barrier distributions, and spin distributions in rare-earth nuclei, but only when coupling between nuclear excitations and translational motion is treated to all orders (not just linear coupling) does the model yield a good representation of the barrier distribution data. The abstract's claim of "systematic study" glosses over the fact that the all-orders coupling approach ignores excitation energies in the target nucleus, and that subbarrier fusion probes overall coupling strength but is not sensitive to details of nuclear wavefunctions.

## Method
The authors use the path integral formulation of multidimensional quantum tunneling, employing the influence functional method to couple the relative motion of projectile and target to the internal degrees of freedom described by the Interacting Boson Model. For linear coupling, the interaction Hamiltonian is taken as a one-body transition operator in the SU(6) algebra of the sd-IBM, and the two-time influence functional is evaluated as a group matrix element. For all-orders coupling, the authors introduce a Green's function method that diagonalizes the coupling operator via a unitary transformation, allowing calculation of eigenchannel cross sections within the WKB approximation, with the quadrupole and hexadecapole operators taken from the consistent-Q formalism of Casten and Warner.

## Key numbers
- The Coulomb coupling potential is given by $V_{\text{Coul}} = Z_1 Z_2 e^2 / r \times (1 + (3/5)(R_1^2/r^2) \hat O)$ for $r > R_1$, and $V_{\text{Coul}} = Z_1 Z_2 e^2 / r \times (1 + (3/5)(r^2/R_1^2) \hat O)$ for $r < R_1$.
- The nuclear potential is taken as a Woods-Saxon form: $V_{\text{nuc}} = -V_0 [1 + \exp((r - R_0 - R_1 \hat O)/a)]^{-1}$.
- The possible multipole transition operators in the standard sd-IBM have $k = 2,4$; $k=0$ and odd-$k$ values are excluded.
- The quadrupole operator is $T^{(2)} = [s^\dagger \tilde d + d^\dagger s]^{(2)} + \chi [d^\dagger \tilde d]^{(2)}$, and the hexadecapole operator is $T^{(4)} = [d^\dagger \tilde d]^{(4)}$.
- The model was applied to systems including $^{16}$O + $^{148}$Sm, $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Sm, and $^{16}$O + $^{186}$W, with data from the Australian National University.
- Predictions were made for $^{40}$Ca + $^{194}$Pt and $^{40}$Ca + $^{194}$Os systems, later confirmed by measurements (Bierman).

## Context
This work extends earlier path integral formulations of subbarrier fusion (Balantekin and Takigawa, 1985) and the SU(3) limit of IBM (Balantekin, Bennett, and Kuyucak, 1991) to general IBM Hamiltonians using a 1/N expansion technique. It directly addresses the need for realistic calculations away from the three symmetry limits of IBM, particularly for nuclei with arbitrary quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations. The systematic study of rare-earth nuclei enables predictions for transitional Os-Pt nuclei, where shape-phase transitions are expected to produce sharp changes in barrier distributions, providing a sensitive test for competing nuclear structure models. The authors note that subbarrier fusion, like static quantities (energy levels, electromagnetic transition rates), does not constitute a dynamic probe of nuclei, in contrast to proton scattering.
