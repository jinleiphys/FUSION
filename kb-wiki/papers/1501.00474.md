# Caloron correction to the effective potential in thermal gluodynamics

> The effective potential in thermal gluodynamics has stable minima in perturbation theory. There the Wilson line is center group valued and eigenvalues of the Wilson line stay clustered. Calorons with non-trivial holonomy contribute to one loop order a linear term in the holonomy. The sign is such that the center group minimum stays stable.

## Key claim

Calorons with non-trivial holonomy contribute a linear term in the holonomy \(q\) to the one-loop effective potential in thermal gluodynamics, and the sign of this term is positive, meaning the center-group minimum at \(q=0\) remains stable to leading order in the running coupling \(g(T)\).

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop fluctuation determinant around the caloron (KvBLL) solution for gauge group \(SU(2)\) at finite temperature, expanding the caloron gauge field to first order in the holonomy \(q\) around the Harrington-Shepard (HS) caloron at \(q=0\). They show that the non-rotationally-invariant terms in the variation vanish upon tracing in the determinant, so the linear term in \(q\) reduces to the derivative of the HS determinant with respect to the caloron size \(r_{12}\), multiplied by \(r_{12}^2\). The resulting linearized caloron density \(n_1\) is then integrated over the size parameter \(r_{12}\) using an analytic fit to the HS determinant from Altes (2014).

## Key numbers

* The integrated caloron density at leading order is \(\int_0^\infty dr_{12}\, n_0(r_{12}) = C \times 2.83854\), where \(C = 2^{-8/3} \mathcal{N} \pi^4\) and \(\mathcal{N}\) is the volume of the \(SU(2)\) instanton.
* The coefficient of the linear term in the integrated density is \(\int_0^\infty dr_{12}\, n_1(r_{12}) = C \times (-17.5653)\), giving a negative contribution to the density.
* The total free energy including the caloron correction is \(f_{tot} = f_{tr} - 2 T^4 \left( \frac{4\pi^2}{g^2(T)} \right)^4 \exp\left( -\frac{8\pi^2}{g^2(T)} \right) C \left( 2.83854 - 17.5653\,q + \cdots \right)\), with \(f_{tr} = f_{SB} + \frac{4\pi^2}{3} T^4 q^2 (1-|q|)^2\).
* The Stefan-Boltzmann free energy is \(f_{SB} = -\frac{\pi^2}{15} T^4\).

## Context

This work extends the seminal calculation of the instanton density at finite temperature by Gross, Pisarski and Yaffe (1980) (GPY), who computed the coefficient \(n_0\) for the Harrington-Shepard caloron at zero holonomy. The present paper computes the previously unknown linear coefficient \(n_1\) for the caloron with non-trivial holonomy, using an analytic approach that avoids the large-size expansion employed by Diakonov, Gromov, Petrov and Slizovskiy (2004-2005) (DGPS). The result shows that, contrary to the expectation that calorons might destabilize the perturbative Stefan-Boltzmann minimum, the linear term is positive and thus stabilizes it at leading order; the authors note that non-leading \(\mathcal{O}(g^2)\) effects, as considered by DGPS, could potentially reverse this sign at lower temperatures.
