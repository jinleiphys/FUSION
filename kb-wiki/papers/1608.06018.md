# Quark self-energy in an ellipsoidally anisotropic quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate the quark self-energy in a quark-gluon plasma that possesses an ellipsoidal momentum-space anisotropy in the local rest frame. By introducing additional transverse momentum anisotropy parameters into the parton distribution functions, we generalize previous results which were obtained for the case of a spheroidal anisotropy. Our results demonstrate that the presence of anisotropies in the transverse directions affects the real and imaginary parts of quark self-energy and, consequently, the self-energy depends on both the polar and azimuthal angles in the local rest frame of the matter. Our results for the quark self-energy set the stage for the calculation of the effects of ellipsoidal momentum-space anisotropy on quark-gluon plasma photon spectra and collective flow.

## Key claim

The quark self-energy in a quark-gluon plasma with ellipsoidal momentum-space anisotropy depends on both the polar and azimuthal angles in the local rest frame, generalizing previous spheroidal results; the authors demonstrate that transverse anisotropies affect both the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy, with the imaginary part showing larger modifications than the real part.

## Method

The authors calculate the retarded quark self-energy in the hard-loop (HL) approximation using a partial-fraction decomposition of the angular integrals, starting from a generalized distribution function that includes three independent anisotropy parameters (ξ_x, ξ_y, ξ_z) corresponding to ellipsoidal momentum-space isosurfaces. For a conformal system, they reduce the problem to two physical anisotropy parameters (ξ_1 for longitudinal and ξ_2 for transverse anisotropy) and evaluate the resulting one-dimensional integrals over the polar angle numerically. They cross-check their results using an alternative derivation presented in an appendix and analytic small-anisotropy expansions.

## Key numbers

- The quark self-energy components are normalized by m_q²/k, where m_q² = (g² C_F)/(8π²) ∫₀^∞ dp p f_iso(p/λ) defines the effective quark mass scale.
- Results are presented for ξ_1 = 10, with transverse anisotropy parameter ξ_2 taking values {-0.2, 0, 1, 3} at fixed angles θ_k = π/3 and φ_k = π/6.
- Results are shown for ξ_1 = 10, ξ_2 = 3, θ_k = π/3, with azimuthal angle φ_k taking values {0, π/6, π/4, π/3}.
- The isotropic limit (ξ = 0) gives analytic forms: Σⁱˢᵒ_0 = (m_q²/(2k)) log[(ω+k)/(ω-k)] and Σⁱˢᵒ_s = (m_q²/k)[(ω/(2k)) log[(ω+k)/(ω-k)] - 1].

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the quark self-energy in a spheroidally anisotropic QGP by Schenke, Strickland, and collaborators (2006), which used a single anisotropy direction and did not account for transverse momentum anisotropies. The spheroidal parametrization was introduced by Romatschke and Strickland (2003) for gluonic collective modes and applied to photon production by Schenke, Greiner, and Strickland (2007). The present results enable self-consistent calculation of photon spectra and collective flow (including elliptic flow) from anisotropic hydrodynamics codes that already incorporate ellipsoidal anisotropies, as developed by Tinti, Nopoush, Strickland, and collaborators (2014-2015).
