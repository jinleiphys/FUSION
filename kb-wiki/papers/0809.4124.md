# Bubbles in $^{34}$Si and $^{22}$O?

> Bubble nuclei are characterized by a depletion of their central density. Their existence is examined within three different theoretical frameworks: the shell model as well as non-relativistic and relativistic microscopic mean-field approaches. We propose $^{34}$Si and $^{22}$O as possible candidates for proton and neutron bubble nuclei, respectively. In the case of $^{22}$O, we observe a significant model dependence, thereby calling into question the bubble structure of $^{22}$O. In contrast, an overall agreement among the models is obtained for $^{34}$Si. Indeed, all models predict a central proton density depletion of about 40%. This result provides strong evidence in favor of a proton bubble in $^{34}$Si.

## Key claim

The paper proposes $^{34}$Si and $^{22}$O as candidates for proton and neutron bubble nuclei, respectively, but with a strong hedge: while all three theoretical frameworks (shell model, non-relativistic mean-field, and relativistic mean-field) agree on a central proton density depletion of about 40% in $^{34}$Si, the results for $^{22}$O show significant model dependence and are weakened by pairing correlations, calling into question its bubble structure.

## Method

The authors employed three complementary theoretical frameworks: shell-model calculations using the SDPF-NR interaction with the ANTOINE code, non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SLy4) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, and relativistic mean-field calculations using NL3, FSUGold, and DD-ME2 parameter sets with relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov pairing. For each nucleus, the central depletion fraction F was computed as (ρ_max − ρ_c)/ρ_max from the calculated density profiles, and occupation numbers of the relevant s₁/₂ orbitals were extracted.

## Key numbers

- For $^{34}$Si, the central proton depletion fraction F is about 40% across all models: 43% (shell model), 38% (SLy4-HF), 37% (NL3 RMF), 42% (FSUGold RMF), and 36% (DD-ME2 RMF/RHB).
- For $^{22}$O, the neutron depletion fraction F varies widely: 25% (shell model), 13% (SLy4-HF), 0% (SLy4-HFB with pairing), 28% (NL3 RMF), 34% (FSUGold RMF), 29% (DD-ME2 RMF without pairing), and 10% (DD-ME2 RHB with pairing).
- The shell-model occupation number of the π2s₁/₂ orbital in $^{34}$Si is 0.09, compared to 1.88 in $^{36}$S; the experimental value for $^{36}$S is 1.63(32).
- The shell-model occupation number of the ν2s₁/₂ orbital in $^{22}$O is 0.34, compared to 1.89 in $^{24}$O.
- The experimental two-proton separation energy of $^{34}$Si is 33.74 MeV, reproduced by HF without pairing as 35.19 MeV.
- The experimental binding energy per nucleon for $^{34}$Si is 8.34 MeV; NL3 predicts 8.36 MeV and FSUGold predicts 8.28 MeV.
- The experimental binding energy per nucleon for $^{36}$S is 8.58 MeV; NL3 predicts 8.50 MeV and FSUGold predicts 8.42 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for bubble nuclei beyond earlier candidates such as $^{46}$Ar (Todd-Rutel 2004, Khan 2008) and $^{205}$Tl (Cavedon 1982), where the depletion was limited by near-degeneracy of s and d orbitals or by pairing correlations. The study exploits the large subshell gaps at Z=14 and N=14/16 to identify $^{34}$Si and $^{22}$O as cleaner candidates, and uses multiple theoretical frameworks to test robustness. The results enable experimental searches via electron scattering at facilities like FAIR (EXL) and RIKEN (RIBF), or via transfer reactions such as $^{34}$Si(d,$^{3}$He)$^{33}$Al to confirm the near-zero occupancy of the 2s₁/₂ proton orbital.
