# Pion Scalar Density and Chiral Symmetry Restoration at Finite Temperature and Density

> This paper is devoted to the evaluation of the pionic scalar density at finite temperature and baryonic density. We express the latter effect in terms of the nuclear response evaluated in the random phase approxima- tion. We discuss the density and temperature evolution of the pionic density which governs the quark condensate evolution. Numerical evalua- tions are performed.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates the pionic scalar density (which governs the quark condensate evolution) at finite temperature and baryonic density, finding that the additive approximation which simply sums the pure-heat-bath and cold-baryonic-medium contributions is a good approximation, deviating from the exact calculation by no more than 15%, with the maximum deviation around T ≈ 50 MeV where the additive approximation slightly overestimates the pionic density.

## Method

The authors express the pionic scalar density in terms of the nuclear response to a pion-like excitation, evaluated in the random phase approximation (RPA) including the Delta resonance and two particle-two hole (2p-2h) excitations. They introduce temperature via the Matsubara formalism, replacing Heaviside functions with Fermi-Dirac distributions for nucleons and deltas, and incorporate thermal pions through the Bose-Einstein factor. The calculation is performed for infinite nuclear matter using a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV and Landau-Migdal parameters g'_NN = 0.7, g'_NΔ = g'_ΔΔ = 0.5.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon sigma commutator: Σ^π_N = 30 MeV (with N_π = 0.40)
- At normal nuclear density (ρ_0): effective sigma commutator per nucleon Σ̃^π_N = 38.5 MeV, scalar pion number per nucleon N^π_A/A = 0.55
- Cutoff parameter: Λ = 1 GeV; coupling ratio (g_πNΔ/g_πNN)^2 = 3.8
- Momentum and energy integration limits: q = 1 GeV, ω = 1 GeV
- At T ≈ 50 MeV, the additive approximation deviates from the exact result by at most 15%
- At nuclear matter density, virtual nuclear pions dominate the scalar pion density up to at least T ≈ 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatment by Chanfray and Ericson (1990s) of the quark condensate modification in cold nuclear matter, incorporating both temperature and baryonic density simultaneously. The RPA enhancement of the low-energy response was previously introduced by Alberico et al. (1980s), and the zero-order response functions including 2p-2h excitations follow the method of Delorme and Guichon (1990s). The results enable more realistic calculations of chiral symmetry restoration in conditions relevant to relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the fireball contains both thermal pions and a residual baryonic background.
