# A neutron star model in the nonlinear Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

> 4 pages, 4 figures, Proceedings of the 17th Divisional Conference on Nuclear Physics in Astrophysics (NPDC17), 30th September-4th October 2002, ATOMKI, Debrecen, Hungary, to appear in Nuclear Physics A

# A neutron star model in the nonlinear Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

## Key claim

The inclusion of the delta meson and additional nonlinear vector meson interactions in the relativistic mean-field model softens the equation of state and decreases neutron star parameters, but the specific parameter set including the delta meson, quartic rho meson term, and nonlinear vector meson interactions yields a maximum radius consistent with observations of RX J185635-3754.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian in the relativistic mean-field approximation that includes nucleons, leptons, and four meson types: isoscalar sigma and omega mesons, and isovector rho and delta mesons, with additional nonlinear vector meson interaction terms. Field equations were derived at the mean-field level and solved for homogeneous infinite matter, with parameters chosen to reproduce equilibrium properties of symmetric nuclear matter. The resulting equation of state was used as input for the Oppenheimer-Tolman-Volkoff equations to calculate hydrostatically stable neutron star configurations and mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for TM1 parameter set: 2.20 solar masses, with radius 13.8 km
- Maximum mass for GM3 parameter set: 2.17 solar masses, with radius 12.9 km
- Maximum mass for nonlinear TM1 parameter set (including delta meson and nonlinear vector interactions): 2.30 solar masses, with radius 14.0 km
- Minimum mass for all three parameter sets: 0.2 solar masses, with radii between 12.2 and 12.8 km
- Gravitational binding energy for TM1: -0.617; for GM3: -0.602; for nonlinear TM1: -0.586
- Observed values for RX J185635-3754: mass 1.7 plus or minus 0.4 solar masses, radius 11.4 plus or minus 2 km

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic mean-field models of Walecka (1974) by incorporating the delta meson and nonlinear vector meson interactions, which are relevant for highly asymmetric neutron star matter. The results are compared with the Zimanyi-Moszkowski model and the quark mean-field model for quark stars. The inclusion of the delta meson causes nucleon mass splitting and alters particle abundances, and the configuration with the delta meson is energetically favorable compared to configurations without it, though at higher densities the Zimanyi-Moszkowski and quark mean-field models give better results.
