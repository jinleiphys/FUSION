# Open charm phenomenology with a multi-stage approach to relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study open charm flavor observables in Pb+Pb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76$ TeV within the MARTINI framework. The space-time expansion of the quark-gluon plasma is described using the hydrodynamical approach-MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions. The model parameters, including the viscous coefficients, were obtained from a recent Bayesian model-to-data comparison. We evolve heavy quarks in this background using Langevin dynamics while incorporating their collisional and radiative processes in the medium. The sensitivity of charm observables to the IP-Glasma initial state, bulk evolution, and centrality of the collision is studied. We find that the elliptic flow of open charm flavor has a strong dependence on the fluctuating initial conditions in addition to the strength of the interaction of heavy quarks with the medium constituents. Within this framework, the nuclear suppression factor and elliptic flow of D-mesons act as efficient probes to study the initial stages of heavy-ion collisions, transport coefficients associated with QGP medium as well as heavy quark interactions.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of open charm mesons has a strong dependence on fluctuating IP-Glasma initial conditions, and the net effect of these fluctuations on charm flow is opposite to that observed in light-flavor jets: increased flow from fluctuations is more than compensated by increased decorrelation between the charm and light-flavor event planes, suppressing the measured $v_2$.

## Method

The authors use the MARTINI framework to simulate charm quark production via PYTHIA8.2 with EPS09 nuclear PDFs, then evolve the charm quarks through a QGP medium described by the viscous hydrodynamics code MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions. The heavy quark dynamics are modeled using Langevin equations that include both collisional and radiative processes, with drag and diffusion coefficients computed using an effective coupling and a hard-thermal-loop parameterization of the infrared regulator. Three setups for the heavy quark transport coefficients are compared: a constant spatial diffusion coefficient ($2\pi D_sT=3.0$), temperature-dependent coefficients, and fully momentum- and temperature-dependent coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV
- Charm quark mass used: $m_{HQ} = 1.25$ GeV
- For the constant setup (Setup I): $2\pi D_sT = 3.0$
- For the temperature-dependent setup with effective coupling and radiative processes: $(2\pi T)D_s \approx 2-7$
- For comparison, pQCD elastic collisional processes alone give $(2\pi T)D_s$ in the range $30-40$
- QCD parameter $\Lambda = 0.2$ GeV; $\kappa = 0.2$ in the IR regulator $\mu^2_{IR}(t, T)=\kappa\, 4\pi \Big(1+\frac{N_f}{6}\Big)\alpha_{\text{eff}}\,(t) T^2$
- 100 hydrodynamic events per 10% centrality, with 50000 charm configurations per event

## Context

This work extends prior heavy-flavor studies that used smooth initial conditions or simpler hydrodynamical models (e.g., Young 2011, He 2013, Sarkar 2018, Thakur 2020, Prakash 2021) by incorporating fluctuating IP-Glasma initial conditions and viscous hydrodynamics tuned via Bayesian analysis (Heffernan 2023). It contrasts with the behavior observed in light-flavor jets (Noronha-Hostler 2016), where enhanced flow from fluctuations dominates. The results enable future model-to-data comparisons to extract heavy quark transport coefficients and highlight the need to include pre-equilibrium interactions (Das 2017, Carrington 2022, Boguslavski 2023) and coalescence hadronization (Fries 2008) for a simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$.
