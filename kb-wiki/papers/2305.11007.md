# Direct mapping of tidal deformability to the iso-scalar and iso-vector nuclear matter parameters

> Background: The equations of state (EoSs) which determine the properties of neutron stars (NSs) are often characterized by the iso-scalar and iso-vector nuclear matter parameters (NMPs). Recent attempts to relate the radius and tidal deformability of a NS to the individual NMPs have been inconclusive. These properties display strong correlations with the pressure of NS matter which depends on several NMPs. The knowledge of minimal NMPs that determine the NS properties will be necessary to address any connection between NS properties (e.g., tidal deformability) and that of finite nuclei. Purpose: To identify the important NMPs required to describe the tidal deformability of neutron star for astrophysically relevant range of their gravitational masses (1.2 -- 1.8 M$_\odot$) as encountered in the binary neutron star merger events. Method: We construct a large set of EoSs using four iso-scalar and five iso-vector NMPs. These EOSs are employed to perform a systematic analysis to isolate the NMPs that predominantly determine the tidal deformability, over a wide range of NS mass. The tidal deformability is then directly mapped to these NMPs. Results: The tidal deformability of the NS with mass 1.2-1.8 M$_\odot$ can be determined within 10$\%$ directly in terms of four nuclear matter parameters, namely, the incompressibility $K_0$ and skewness $Q_0$ of symmetric nuclear matter, and the slope $L_0$ and curvature parameter $K_{\rm sym,0}$ of symmetry energy. Conclusion: A function that quickly estimates the value of tidal deformability in terms of minimal nuclear matter parameters is developed. Our method can also be extended to other NS observables.

## Key claim

The tidal deformability of neutron stars with masses 1.2–1.8 M$_\odot$ can be determined within 10% directly in terms of four nuclear matter parameters, the incompressibility $K_0$ and skewness $Q_0$ of symmetric nuclear matter, and the slope $L_0$ and curvature parameter $K_{\rm sym,0}$ of symmetry energy, but only when the equations of state are constrained by chiral effective field theory at low density and by a maximum mass condition; without those constraints, the accuracy degrades to 10–30%.

## Method

The authors constructed a large set of equations of state (approximately 10$^4$) by randomly drawing nine nuclear matter parameters (four iso-scalar: $e_0$, $K_0$, $Q_0$, $Z_0$; five iso-vector: $J_0$, $L_0$, $K_{\rm sym,0}$, $Q_{\rm sym,0}$, $Z_{\rm sym,0}$) from uniform prior distributions. They solved the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations to compute tidal deformability $\Lambda$ for each equation of state, then performed multi-parameter correlation studies to identify the minimal subset of parameters that predominantly determine $\Lambda$. They fitted linear and quadratic polynomial functions of the identified parameters to the computed $\Lambda$ values and validated the fitted functions against independent equation-of-state samples, including those constrained by chiral effective field theory up to $2\rho_0$ and by a central density condition for 1.8 M$_\odot$ neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Tidal deformability for neutron stars of mass 1.2–1.8 M$_\odot$ can be determined within 10% using the four-parameter function $\Lambda_{q_4+l_5}$ for equations of state constrained by chiral effective field theory and with central density $\rho_c(1.8) \leq 3.5\rho_0$; the standard deviation of the ratio $\Lambda_{TOV}/\Lambda_{func}$ for this function is 0.01–0.02 across all masses in that range.
- For the linear four-parameter function $\Lambda_{l_4}$ with the same constraints, the standard deviation of the ratio is 0.04–0.08 for masses 1.2–1.8 M$_\odot$.
- For the linear two-parameter function $\Lambda_{l_2}$ (using only $L_0$ and $K_{\rm sym,0}$) with all nine parameters varied, the correlation coefficient with $\Lambda_{TOV}$ drops to $r \sim 0.68$ for a 1.8 M$_\odot$ neutron star.
- The correlation between $\Lambda_{TOV}$ and pressure of $\beta$-equilibrated matter peaks at approximately $1.25\rho_0$ for a 1.2 M$_\odot$ neutron star and at approximately $2\rho_0$ for a 1.8 M$_\odot$ neutron star.
- For the $\Lambda_{l_4}$ function with chiral effective field theory constraint, approximately 9% of equations of state for a 1.2 M$_\odot$ neutron star and 34% for a 1.8 M$_\odot$ neutron star have the ratio $\mathcal{R}$ outside the range 0.9–1.1; for $\Lambda_{q_4+l_5}$ these fractions reduce to 0.5% and 3.8%, respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to relate neutron star radius and tidal deformability to individual nuclear matter parameters, which had been inconclusive (Patra 2023). Prior studies had established robust correlations between neutron star radii and pressure at $\sim 2\rho_0$ (Lattimer 2000) and between tidal deformability and pressure at $\approx 2\rho_0$ (Tsang 2019, Tsang 2020, Patra 2022), but those relations could not separately constrain iso-scalar and iso-vector components. The present paper provides direct empirical relations that map tidal deformability to a minimal set of four nuclear matter parameters, enabling computationally efficient Bayesian inference of these parameters from gravitational-wave observations without repeated solution of the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations. The approach can be extended to other neutron star observables and to different equation-of-state models.
