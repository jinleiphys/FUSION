# Projectile breakup dynamics for $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co: kinematical analysis of $α$-$d$ coincidences

> A study of the kinematics of the $α$-$d$ coincidences in the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system at a bombarding energy of $E_{lab} = 29.6$ MeV is presented. With exclusive measurements performed over different angular intervals it is possible to identify the respective contributions of the sequential projectile breakup and direct projectile breakup components. A careful analysis using a semiclassical approach of these processes provides information on both their lifetime and their distance of occurrence with respect to the target. Breakup to the low-lying (near-threshold) continuum is delayed, and happens at large internuclear distances. This suggests that the influence of the projectile breakup on the complete fusion process can be related essentially to direct breakup to the $^6$Li high-lying continuum spectrum. %

## Key claim

The influence of projectile breakup on complete fusion in the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system is essentially due to direct breakup to the $^{6}$Li high-lying continuum spectrum, because both sequential breakup and direct breakup to low-lying (near-threshold) continuum states occur at large internuclear distances and are therefore delayed.

## Method

Exclusive $\alpha$-$d$ coincidence measurements were performed for the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system at $E_{lab} = 29.6$ MeV using 11 triple telescopes covering angular intervals from $-45^\circ$ to $-15^\circ$ and $15^\circ$ to $75^\circ$. A 3-body kinematics analysis distinguished sequential projectile breakup (SBU) from direct projectile breakup (DBU) and incomplete fusion/transfer (ICF/TR) contributions. A semiclassical approach using Coulomb trajectories was applied to estimate lifetimes and distances of occurrence from the target for both breakup components, with CDCC calculations providing theoretical comparisons.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: $E_{lab} = 29.6$ MeV ($E_{cm} = 26.9$ MeV), approximately twice the Coulomb barrier energy ($E_{cm} \approx 12$ MeV)
- Experimental SBU cross section for the first resonant state (2.186 MeV, $3^+$): $20.6 \pm 4.0$ mb (CDCC prediction: 22.1 mb)
- Experimental DBU cross sections: $3.04 \pm 0.41$ mb ($E^* = 1.66$–$2.10$ MeV), $6.8 \pm 4.2$ mb ($E^* = 2.20$–$2.40$ MeV), $4.45 \pm 0.94$ mb ($E^* = 3.10$–$3.25$ MeV)
- SBU lifetime: $\tau_{SBU} = 2.73 \times 10^{-20}$ s, corresponding to $\Gamma_{SBU} = 0.024 \pm 0.002$ MeV
- SBU distance of occurrence from target: $r = 831.0 \pm 1.3$ fm at $R_{min}^{MP} = 8.5 \pm 0.4$ fm
- DBU distances of occurrence: $r = 143.1 \pm 1.5$ fm ($E^* = 1.7$ MeV), $r = 19.8 \pm 0.8$ fm ($E^* = 2.3$ MeV), $r = 14.3 \pm 1.8$ fm ($E^* = 3.2$ MeV)
- Coulomb barrier height between $\alpha$ and $d$: $V_B = 0.576$ MeV at $R_{\alpha d} = 5.0$ fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous inclusive measurements of the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system at four bombarding energies (Souza 2009) by providing exclusive coincidence data that separate SBU and DBU contributions. The results contradict the assumption that non-resonant breakup structures arise exclusively from DBU, as assumed for $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi by Santra (2009), by showing that ICF/TR contributions are also present. The finding that low-lying continuum breakup occurs at large distances supports the classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup developed by Diaz-Torres (2007) and suggests that the above-barrier complete fusion suppression observed in reactions with weakly bound projectiles (Dasgupta 2004, Hinde 2002) arises primarily from direct breakup to high-lying continuum states.
