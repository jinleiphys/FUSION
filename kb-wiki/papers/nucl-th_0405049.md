# Wobbling excitations and tilted rotation in 163-Lu

> Using a microscopic self-consistent model, we analyse wobbling excitations built upon the rotational band in $^{163}$Lu, which is identified with a rotation of a triaxial, strongly deformed shape. We find that the presence of pairing correlations substantially affects the energy of the wobbling excitations. Our calculations predict an onset of a tilted rotation at a critical rotational frequency where the energy of the wobbling excitations approaches zero.

# Wobbling excitations and tilted rotation in 163-Lu

## Key claim
Using a self-consistent cranking plus random phase approximation (CRPA) model, the authors find that pairing correlations substantially affect the wobbling excitation energy in 163Lu, contrary to a prior claim that pairing does not affect the wobbling phonon. The calculations predict an onset of tilted rotation at a critical rotational frequency where the wobbling excitation energy approaches zero, but the authors note that the collapse of the static pair field at high frequency happens too abruptly, which may explain why the reduction of wobbling energy comes too fast compared to experiment.

## Method
The authors employ a pairing-plus-quadrupole (QQ) Hamiltonian and solve Hartree-Bogoliubov equations for the TSD1 rotational band in 163Lu, with and without pairing fields, using a spherical modified oscillator basis with major shells N=4-6 for protons and neutrons. They then apply the quasi-boson approximation and random phase approximation (RPA) to construct vibrational wobbling excitations from the self-consistent mean-field solutions, diagonalizing the full RPA determinant rather than a reduced dispersion equation. The rotational frequency vector is treated with Euler tilt angles (theta, phi) without restriction to signature symmetry.

## Key numbers
- The proton pair field disappears at approximately hbar*omega = 0.25 MeV, and the neutron pair field disappears at approximately hbar*omega = 0.4 MeV, due to gradual breaking of quasiparticle pairs.
- For hbar*omega < 0.5 MeV, principal axis rotation is found as the lowest energy solution.
- The wobbling excitation energy is substantially larger at small rotational frequencies when pairing is included, and after the collapse of both pair fields at hbar*omega ≈ 0.4 MeV, the paired and unpaired calculations predict similar results.
- The ratio of inter-band to intra-band E2 transitions is calculated using effective charges of 0.5 for neutrons and 1.5 for protons to compensate for the restricted configuration space.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic analysis of wobbling excitations in 163Lu, which were first experimentally identified by Odegard et al. (2001) and Jensen et al. (2002) as the TSD2 band built on the triaxial strongly deformed (TSD1) band. The authors directly contradict the conclusion of Matsuyanagi et al. (2004) that pairing correlations do not affect the wobbling phonon, showing instead that pairing changes the gamma-deformation and thereby substantially alters the wobbling energy. The study also follows the analysis of Frauendorf (2001) and investigates the possible transition from wobbling to tilted rotation predicted by Frauendorf (2001) and Hamamoto (2002). The results enable future work with particle number projection to resolve the discrepancy between the calculated and experimental rates of wobbling energy reduction.
