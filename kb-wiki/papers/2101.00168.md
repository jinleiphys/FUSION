# A matter of shape: seeing the deformation of atomic nuclei at high-energy colliders

> This work establishes a deep connection between two seemingly distant branches of nuclear physics: nuclear structure and relativistic heavy-ion collisions. At the heart of this connection is the recent discovery made at particle colliders that the elliptic flow of outgoing hadrons in central nucleus-nucleus collisions is strongly impacted by the quadrupole deformation of the colliding nuclear species. I review the physics of the soft sector of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and I explain that the interpretation of elliptic flow data in central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au, $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U, and $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions requires a deep understanding the structure of these ions. Subsequently, I introduce a technique that permits one to isolate collision configurations in which the deformed shapes of the colliding nuclei maximally break rotational (azimuthal) symmetry in the interaction region. This allows me to construct observables that possess an unparalleled sensitivity to the quadrupole deformation of the colliding ions, and thus to conclude that nuclear experiments at high energy can be used to place new quantitative constraints on the deformation of atomic nuclei. I emphasize the great opportunities offered by potential collider experiments aimed at the systematic study of nuclear deformation across the valley of stability.

## Key claim

This work establishes that relativistic heavy-ion collisions can serve as a quantitative probe of nuclear quadrupole deformation, provided that collision geometries where deformed nuclei maximally break azimuthal symmetry can be isolated experimentally through event selection based on average transverse momentum.

## Method

The author analyzes experimental elliptic flow data from RHIC and LHC collisions of 197Au, 238U, and 129Xe nuclei using hydrodynamic model comparisons within the Glauber Monte Carlo framework. A technique is introduced to select collision events based on the correlation between average transverse momentum and elliptic flow at fixed multiplicity, which isolates configurations where deformed nuclear shapes maximally break rotational symmetry in the interaction region.

## Key numbers

- 238U nuclei show clear evidence of ellipsoidal shape from RHIC data, with the analysis providing the first experimental indication that 197Au nuclei are nearly spherical
- 129Xe nuclei exhibit significant deformation in LHC data, appearing in tension with predictions from nuclear structure models
- Ultrarelativistic 208Pb+208Pb collisions release 2000-3000 particles within a volume of order 1000 fm3, corresponding to 2-3 particles per fm3 (over one order of magnitude larger than nuclear matter density of 0.16 particles per fm3)
- The quark-gluon plasma reaches local thermal equilibrium within approximately 1 fm/c (3×10-24 s) at a temperature T ≈ 1012 K

## Context

This work extends the connection between nuclear structure and relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the discovery that elliptic flow in central nucleus-nucleus collisions is strongly impacted by quadrupole deformation. The analysis of 197Au data contradicts empirical estimates from nuclear data tables based on mean-field approximations, while the 129Xe results suggest the first phenomenological manifestation of shape coexistence effects in high-energy experiments. The technique introduced enables quantitative studies of nuclear deformation that were previously thought impossible due to difficulties in selecting appropriate collision geometries.
