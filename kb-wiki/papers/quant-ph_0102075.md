# Correlation induced collapse of many-body systems with zero-range potentials

> The zero-range potential is customarily employed in various mean-field calculations of many-body systems in atomic and nuclear physics within, correspondingly, Gross-Pitaevskii and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach. We argue, however, that a many-body system with zero-range potentials is unstable against clusterization into collapsed three-body subsystems. We show that neither the density dependence of the potential nor an additional repulsive three-body potential can prevent this unexpected correlational collapse if the potentials are of zero range. Therefore the zero-range potential can only be used in many-body calculations where all three-body correlations are explicitly excluded.

# Correlation induced collapse of many-body systems with zero-range potentials

## Key claim

The authors argue that many-body systems with zero-range potentials are fundamentally unstable against clusterization into collapsed three-body subsystems, and that neither density-dependent potentials nor repulsive three-body zero-range potentials can prevent this correlational collapse. The zero-range potential can therefore only be used in many-body calculations where all three-body correlations are explicitly excluded from the variational model space.

## Method

The authors analyze a three-body system with zero-range potentials using the hyper-spheric adiabatic expansion method, solving the Faddeev equations in the adiabatic approximation. They derive the eigenvalue equation for the hyper-angular quantum number nu as a function of hyper-radius rho, and examine the behavior of the effective hyper-radial potential at small distances (rho much less than the scattering length a). They then extend the analysis to show that the same collapse mechanism persists when repulsive three-body zero-range potentials or density-dependent zero-range potentials are added to the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The imaginary root of the eigenvalue equation at small distances is nu_0 = i b, where b = 1.006
- The effective hyper-radial potential at small distances behaves as -C/rho^2, where C = 1.262
- For C greater than 1/4, the hyper-radial wavefunction acquires an imaginary exponent, leading to infinitely many nodes at small distances
- The number of bound states in a regularized three-body system is approximately ln(a/R), where R is the finite-range cutoff scale

## Context

This work extends the known Thomas effect (Thomas, 1935) for three-body systems with zero-range potentials to the many-body context, showing that the same correlational collapse occurs in systems described by Gross-Pitaevskii and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approaches. The paper contradicts the common assumption that density-dependent potentials or three-body zero-range potentials (as used in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vautherin and Brink, 1972) are sufficient to stabilize many-body systems against all forms of collapse. The result implies that existing mean-field calculations using zero-range potentials remain valid only as long as the model space is restricted to Hartree-Fock product wavefunctions, and that going beyond this approximation requires regularization of the zero-range potential by introducing a finite length scale.
