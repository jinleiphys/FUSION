# Effects of imaginary and real rotations on QCD matters

> Inspired from perturbative calculations, this work introduces imaginary ($Ω_{\rm I}$) and real ($Ω$) rotation effects to the pure $SU(3)$ gauge potentials simply through variable transformations: The empirical Polyakov loop (PL) potentials can be rewritten as functions of the imaginary chemical potentials of gluons and ghosts $(q_{\rm ij})$, and the transformations are taken as $q_{\rm ij}\rightarrow q_{\rm ij}\pmΩ_{\rm I}/T$ and $q_{\rm ij}\rightarrow q_{\rm ij}\pm i\,Ω/T$, respectively. For the PL potential of Fukushima $(V_1)$, a smaller imaginary rotation $Ω_{\rm I}$ tends to suppress PL at all temperature and the deconfinement transition keeps of first order. However, for the PL potential of Munich group $(V_2)$, $Ω_{\rm I}$ tends to enhance PL at low temperature $T$, consistent with lattice simulations; but suppress PL at high $T$, consistent with perturbative calculations. Moreover, the deconfinement alters from first order to crossover with increasing $Ω_{\rm I}$ as is expected from lattice simulations. On the other hand, the real rotation $Ω$ tends to enhance PL at relatively low $T$ for both potentials, and the (pseudo-)critical temperature decreases with $Ω$ as expected. Therefore, we find that analytic continuation of the phase diagram from imaginary to real rotation is not necessarily valid in the non-perturbative region. Finally, we apply the more successful PL potential $V_2$ to the Polyakov--Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and discover that $Ω_{\rm I}$ tends to break chiral symmetry while $Ω$ tends to restore it. Especially, the modified model is even able to qualitatively explain the lattice result that a larger $T$ would catalyze chiral symmetry breaking for a large $Ω_{\rm I}$.

## Key claim

This work introduces imaginary (\(\Omega_{\rm I}\)) and real (\(\Omega\)) rotation effects into pure \(SU(3)\) gauge potentials through variable transformations on the imaginary chemical potentials of gluons and ghosts, finding that analytic continuation of the phase diagram from imaginary to real rotation is not necessarily valid in the non-perturbative region, contrary to common assumption.

## Method

The authors rewrite empirical Polyakov loop (PL) potentials (Fukushima's \(V_1\) and Munich group's \(V_2\)) as functions of the imaginary color chemical potentials \(q_{\rm ij}\), then apply the transformations \(q_{\rm ij}\rightarrow q_{\rm ij}\pm\Omega_{\rm I}/T\) for imaginary rotation and \(q_{\rm ij}\rightarrow q_{\rm ij}\pm i\,\Omega/T\) for real rotation, averaging over both signs. They minimize the resulting thermodynamic potentials with respect to the angles \(\phi_1\) and \(\phi_2\) to obtain the Polyakov loop magnitude \(|L|\) and determine the deconfinement transition temperature. The more successful potential \(V_2\) is then applied to the three-flavor Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to study chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For the \(V_2\) potential, \(\Omega_{\rm I}=0.05, 0.1, 0.15, 0.2~{\rm GeV}\) are used; \(\Omega_{\rm I}\) enhances \(|L|\) at low \(T\) and suppresses it at high \(T\), with the deconfinement transition changing from first order to crossover.
- For the \(V_2\) potential, real rotations \(\Omega=0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.15~{\rm GeV}\) are used; the (pseudo-)critical temperature decreases with \(\Omega\) and the transition becomes crossover except near \(\Omega\sim0\).
- For the \(V_1\) potential, \(\Omega_{\rm I}=0, 0.05, 0.1~{\rm GeV}\) and \(\Omega=0, 0.05, 0.1~{\rm GeV}\) are used; the deconfinement transition remains first order in all cases.
- The scale temperature \(T_0=0.27~{\rm GeV}\) is used for the reduced temperature \(\tilde{T}\equiv T/T_0\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations by Chen (2022) that introduced rotation effects to gluons and ghosts, and addresses contradictions between lattice QCD simulations (Braguta 2021, Yang 2023) and effective model studies (Jiang 2016, Chen 2015, Ebihara 2016) regarding whether rotation enhances or suppresses confinement. The results show that the Munich group's PL potential \(V_2\) (Ratti 2005) reproduces lattice QCD trends for imaginary rotation, while Fukushima's potential \(V_1\) (Fukushima 2003) does not; the modified PNJL model can qualitatively explain lattice results on chiral symmetry breaking under large imaginary rotation. This enables future studies of real rotation effects on QCD phase diagrams without relying on analytic continuation from imaginary rotation data.
