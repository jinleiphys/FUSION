# Conformal symmetry algebra of the quark potential and degeneracies in the hadron spectra

> The essence of the potential algebra concept [3] is that quantum mechanical free motions of scalar particles on curved surfaces of given isometry algebras can be mapped on 1D Schroedinger equations with particular potentials. As long as the Laplace-Beltrami operator on a curved surface is proportional to one of the Casimir invariants of the isometry algebra, free motion on the surface is described by means of the eigenvalue problem of that very Casimir operator and the excitation modes are classified according to the irreps of the algebra of interest. In consequence, also the spectra of the equivalent Schroedinger operators are classified according to the same irreps. We here use the potential algebra concept as a guidance in the search for an interaction describing conformal degeneracies. For this purpose we subject the so(4) isometry algebra of the S^3 ball to a particular non-unitary similarity transformation and obtain a deformed isometry copy to S^3 such that free motion on the copy is equivalent to a cotangent perturbed motion on S^3, and to the 1D Schroedinger operator with the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential as well. The latter presents itself especially well suited for quark-system studies insofar as its Taylor series decomposition begins with a Cornell-type potential and in accord with lattice QCD predictions. We fit the strength of the cotangent potential to the spectra of the unflavored high-lying mesons and obtain a value compatible with the light dilaton mass. We conclude that while the conformal group symmetry of QCD following from AdS_5/CFT_4 may be broken by the dilaton mass, it still may be preserved as a symmetry algebra of the potential, thus explaining the observed conformal degeneracies in the unflavored hadron spectra, both baryons and mesons.

## Key claim
The conformal group symmetry of QCD, as suggested by the AdS₅/CFT₄ duality, may be broken by the dilaton mass at the level of the group but preserved as a symmetry algebra of the quark potential, thereby explaining the observed conformal degeneracies in unflavored hadron spectra. The key hedge is that this preservation occurs only in the specific "potential algebra" representation, not as a full group symmetry.

## Method
The author applies the potential algebra concept, where free motion on a curved surface with a given isometry algebra is mapped to a 1D Schrödinger equation with a specific potential. The so(4) isometry algebra of the S³ ball is subjected to a non-unitary similarity transformation, producing a deformed isometry copy of S³ such that free motion on the copy is equivalent to a cotangent-perturbed motion on S³ and to the 1D Schrödinger operator with the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential. The strength of the cotangent potential is then fitted to the spectra of unflavored high-lying mesons.

## Key numbers
- The fitted potential strength b = 3.2793 ± 0.0697, which is approximately isospin independent.
- The quantity ℏc√b/R = 673.7 MeV, which matches the predicted light dilaton mass from the literature.
- The temperature T = ℏc/R = 373 MeV, which is larger than Λ_QCD ≈ 175 MeV.
- The anomalous gap between Δ(1232) and the first excited state is approximately 500 MeV, while subsequent gaps between ferneons differing by two spin units are approximately 200 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the potential algebra concept pioneered by Alhassid, Gürsey, and Yachello (1983) and applies it to the conformal symmetry of QCD. It builds on prior observations by Kirchbach and collaborators that unflavored baryon spectra show conformal degeneracies distributed over so(4) levels, and on the AdS₅/CFT₄ duality of Maldacena (1998). The paper enables a new interpretation where conformal degeneracies in hadron spectra arise from a broken conformal group symmetry that survives as a potential algebra, suggesting generalizations of traditional algebraic Hamiltonians beyond the simple sum-of-Casimir approach of Yachello.
