# Electrical and thermal conductivities of hot and dense hadron gas

> We estimate the electrical and thermal conductivities of hot and dense hadronic matter in the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann equation. We estimate the thermodynamical quantities of hot and dense hadronic matter within the ambit of the excluded volume hadron resonance gas model. The relaxation time for all the hadrons is estimated assuming the constant cross section with uniform as well as mass dependent hard-core radius. We compare our results with various existing results. Finally we give an estimate of electrical and thermal conductivities in the context of heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The electrical and thermal conductivities of hot and dense hadronic matter are estimated using the excluded volume hadron resonance gas model with a constant cross-section approximation; the authors note that their electrical conductivity values are higher than those from kinetic theory estimates (Greif 2016) because their assumed cross sections (approximately 10 mb) are smaller than the species-dependent cross sections used in that work.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a multicomponent hadronic medium. Thermodynamic quantities are computed using the excluded volume hadron resonance gas model with two parametrization schemes: a uniform hard-core radius of 0.3 fm for all hadrons, and a mass-dependent excluded volume parameter with energy density parameter epsilon_0 = 2 GeV/fm^3. The relaxation time for each hadron species is obtained from the thermal average of constant cross sections times relative velocity, using Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics.

## Key numbers

* Electrical conductivity sigma_el/T at zero chemical potential is compared across models; the EHRGM with uniform excluded volume gives values higher than kinetic theory results (Greif 2016) and lattice QCD data (Aarts 2014).
* The uniform hard-core radius is set to r_h = 0.3 fm based on nucleon-nucleon scattering analysis.
* The mass-dependent excluded volume parameter uses epsilon_0 = 2 GeV/fm^3.
* The hadron resonance gas cutoff is Lambda = 2.25 GeV, including all mesons and baryons listed in Amsler (2008).
* Electrical conductivity is shown as a function of temperature for baryon chemical potentials mu = 0.1, 0.2, and 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior estimates of transport coefficients in the hadronic phase using the excluded volume hadron resonance gas model, which has previously been applied to shear and bulk viscosity (Kadam 2015). The results are compared with chiral perturbation theory (Fernandez-Fraile 2006), kinetic theory (Greif 2016), lattice QCD (Aarts 2014), and holographic models (Rougemont 2015, Finazzo 2013). The authors note that assigning repulsive interactions only between baryons and antibaryons while keeping mesons non-interacting (Vovchenko 2016) could improve the model, and that including Hagedorn states would better reproduce lattice data up to 160 MeV but is not done here because their electric charges are unknown.
