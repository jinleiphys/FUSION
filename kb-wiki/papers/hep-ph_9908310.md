# Magnetic Interactions, the Renormalization Group and Color Superconductivity in High Density QCD

> We investigate the effect of long range magnetic interactions on the renormalization group (RG) evolution of local Cooper pairing interactions near the Fermi surface in high density QCD. We use an explicit cut-off on momentum modes, with special emphasis on screening effects such as Landau damping, to derive the RG equations in a gauge invariant, weak coupling expansion. We obtain the Landau pole $Δ\sim μg^{-5} \exp(- \frac{3 π^2}{\sqrt{2} g})$, although the structure of our equations differs from previous results. We also investigate the gap equation, including condensates of higher angular momentum. We show that rotational invariance is unbroken at asymptotically high density, and verify that $Δ$ is the correct value of the gap when higher modes are included in the analysis.

# Magnetic Interactions, the Renormalization Group and Color Superconductivity in High Density QCD

## Key claim
Long range magnetic interactions modify the renormalization group evolution of Cooper pairing interactions near the Fermi surface, yielding a Landau pole gap of $\Delta \sim \mu g^{-5} \exp(-3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}g)$, but the structure of the RG equations differs from previous results by Son (1998) because applying the RG directly to scattering amplitudes presents conceptual problems.

## Method
The authors adopt a Wilsonian RG procedure with a hard infrared cutoff on spatial momentum, expanding the magnetic gluon form factor into Legendre components with definite angular momentum. They compute tree-level one-gluon exchange and one-loop box diagram contributions using screened gluon propagators that incorporate Landau damping, deriving coupled RG equations for the A-type (temporal) and B-type (spatial) four-fermion coupling coefficients. The gap equation is then solved including condensates of higher angular momentum to test rotational symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers
- Landau pole gap: $\Delta \sim \mu g^{-5} \exp(-3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}g)$, where $\mu$ is the chemical potential and $g$ is the gauge coupling
- Debye screening mass: $m_D^2 = N_f g^2 \mu^2 / 2\pi^2$, where $N_f$ is the number of quark flavors
- The spin-0 combination $S_l \equiv A_l - 3B_l$ reaches a Landau pole at the scale $\Delta \sim \mu g^{-5} \exp(-3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}g)$
- The spin-1 combination $T_l \equiv A_l + B_l$ reaches an asymptotic value of $T_l(t \rightarrow \infty) = \sqrt{2}\pi g / 3\mu^2$
- The constant term in the $B_l$ RG equation is $g^2 / 27\mu^2$

## Context
This work extends the RG analysis of color superconductivity originally developed by Evans, Hsu, and Schwetz (1999) for local interactions, incorporating the long-range magnetic interaction effects first identified by Son (1998). The authors show that despite differences in the RG equation structure, the Landau pole result agrees with Son's finding. The paper demonstrates that rotational invariance remains unbroken at asymptotically high density, contradicting the possibility of higher angular momentum condensates suggested by some analyses. This enables a consistent treatment of the gap equation in the leading logarithmic approximation for high-density QCD.
