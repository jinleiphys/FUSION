# Path-integral hadronization for the nucleon and its interactions

> Nucleon structure and the origin and nature of the nuclear force are investigated in the context of a QCD-based effective field theory and the path-integral method of hadronization. We start from a microscopic model of quarks and diquarks where the gluons have been integrated out. In particular, we use the chiral Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to describe quark dynamics and assume that the nucleon can be conceived as a quark-diquark relativistic bound state. The hadronization method is then used to rewrite the problem in terms of the physical meson and nucleon degrees of freedom. Next, by employing a loop and derivative expansion of the resulting quark/diquark determinants, we arrive at an effective chiral meson-nucleon Lagrangian. Nucleon properties such as mass, coupling constants, electromagnetic radii, anomalous magnetic moments, and form factors are derived using a theory of at most two free parameters.

## Key claim

The paper derives an effective chiral meson-nucleon Lagrangian from a microscopic quark-diquark model using path-integral hadronization, claiming that nucleon properties can be calculated with at most two free parameters; however, the numerical study presented is restricted to the scalar-diquark-only case, and the full treatment including axial-vector diquarks is deferred to future work.

## Method

Starting from the chiral Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quarks, the authors introduce scalar and axial-vector diquark fields as elementary degrees of freedom and assume the nucleon is a quark-diquark relativistic bound state. They apply a two-step path-integral hadronization: first bosonization to introduce meson fields as quark-antiquark correlations, then "fermionization" to introduce nucleon fields as quark-diquark correlations. A loop and derivative expansion of the resulting quark/diquark determinants yields an effective chiral meson-nucleon Lagrangian, with divergent integrals regularized using the Pauli-Villars method to preserve gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass \(m_q\) and scalar diquark mass \(M_S\) are basic quantities in the model (specific numerical values are not given in the provided text).
* Pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 93\) MeV is used in the nonlinear parameterization of meson fields.
* The number of colors \(N_c = 3\) appears in the nucleon self-energy expression.
* The Goldberger-Treiman relation is derived as \(g_{\Phi N N} = (M_B / F_\pi) g_A\), linking the pion-nucleon coupling constant, nucleon mass, and axial-vector coupling constant.
* The paper states that axial-vector diquark correlations contribute at most no more than 10% to the structure of the nucleon, based on a recent Faddeev equation calculation (MBIY02).

## Context

This work extends the path-integral hadronization approach introduced by Cahill (1989), Reinhardt (1990), and Ebert and collaborators (1992), and specifically builds upon an earlier attempt by Ebert and Jurke (1998) that used only scalar diquarks but had issues with gauge invariance and regularization. The present paper improves on that by including both scalar and axial-vector diquarks, employing a gauge-invariant Pauli-Villars regularization, and verifying the Ward-Takahashi identity and Goldberger-Treiman relation. The formalism parallels Faddeev equation approaches (e.g., Ishii et al., 1993; Meyer, 1994) and Bethe-Salpeter equation treatments (e.g., Hellstern et al., 1997; Oettel et al., 1998) for quark-diquark systems, but additionally yields a quantum hadrodynamics-type Lagrangian describing meson-nucleon interactions. The numerical results for the scalar-diquark case enable future extension to include axial-vector diquarks and application to other baryons such as the \(\Delta\) particle.
