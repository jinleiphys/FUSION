# Tidal stretching of gravitons into classical strings: application to jet quenching with AdS/CFT

> Previous work has shown that the standard supergravity approximation can break down when using AdS/CFT duality to study certain top-down formulations of the jet stopping problem in strongly-coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills (SYM) plasmas, depending on the virtuality of the source of the "jet." In this paper, we identify the nature of this breakdown: High-momentum gravitons in the gravitational dual get stretched into relatively large classical string loops by tidal forces associated with the black brane. These stringy excitations of the graviton are not contained in the supergravity approximation, but we show that the jet stopping problem can nonetheless still be solved by drawing on various string-theory methods (the eikonal approximation, the Penrose limit, string quantization in pp-wave backgrounds) to obtain a probability distribution for the late-time classical string loops. In extreme cases, we find that the gravitons are stretched into very long folded strings which are qualitatively similar to the folded classical strings originally used by Gubser, Gulotta, Pufu and Rocha to model the jet stopping problem. This makes a connection in certain cases between the different methods that have been used to study jet stopping with AdS/CFT and gives a specific example of a precise N=4 SYM problem that generates such strings in the gravity description.

## Key claim

High-momentum gravitons in the gravitational dual of strongly-coupled N=4 SYM plasmas get stretched into relatively large classical string loops by tidal forces from the black brane, but this stringy excitation of the graviton does not necessarily invalidate the λ=∞ results for the stopping distance because the ratio of the stretched string size to the stopping distance is parametrically small (∼λ^{-1/4}) for large but finite λ, except when the logarithm in Eq. (1.13) becomes exponentially large.

## Method

The authors identify the breakdown of the supergravity approximation for jet stopping in the regime λ^{-1/6}ℓ_max ≪ ℓ_stop ≲ ℓ_max by analyzing the tidal stretching of gravitons as they fall in the AdS₅-Schwarzschild background. They treat the graviton as a tiny closed string loop and use the Penrose limit to approximate the background metric near a null geodesic as a pp-wave background, where string quantization is tractable. They then compute the probability distribution for the late-time classical string loops using string-theory methods including the eikonal approximation, the Penrose limit, and string quantization in pp-wave backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- The maximum stopping distance scales as ℓ_max ∼ E^{1/3}/T^{4/3} (Eq. 1.1).
- The stopping distance for a null geodesic is ℓ_stop ≃ [Γ²(1/4)/(4π)^{1/2}] (E²/(-q²))^{1/4} (1/(2πT)) (Eq. 3.2).
- The ratio of the stretched string size in the direction of motion to the stopping distance is (δx³)_string/ℓ_stop ∼ λ^{-1/4} ln^{1/2}(λ^{-1/6}ℓ_max/ℓ_stop) (Eq. 1.13).
- The horizon location is z_h = 1/(πT) (Eq. 2.2).
- The characteristic turning point scale is z_⋆ ≡ z_h (-q²/|q|²)^{1/4} ≪ z_h (Eq. 3.3).
- The AdS/CFT dictionary gives α'/R_ads² = λ^{-1/2} (Eq. 2.5).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Arnold, Szepietowski, Vaman 2012) of finite-λ corrections to jet stopping distances, which found that the formal expansion in 1/√λ breaks down for ℓ_stop ≲ λ^{-1/6}ℓ_max. The paper makes a connection between the supergravity-based approach to jet stopping and the folded classical string configurations originally used by Gubser, Gulotta, Pufu and Rocha (2006) to model the jet stopping problem. The results enable a unified understanding of different methods for studying jet stopping with AdS/CFT and provide a specific N=4 SYM problem that generates classical strings in the gravity description.
