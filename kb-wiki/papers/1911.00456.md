# Charmed hadron chemistry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We develop for charmed hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions a comprehensive coalescence model that includes an extensive set of $s$ and $p$-wave hadronic states as well as the strict energy-momentum conservation, which ensures the boost invariance of the coalescence probability and the thermal limit of the produced hadron spectrum. By combining our hadronization scheme with an advanced Langevin-hydrodynamics model that incorporates both elastic and inelastic energy loss of heavy quarks inside the dynamical quark-gluon plasma, we obtain a successful description of the $p_\mathrm{T}$-integrated and differential $Λ_c/D^0$ and $D_s/D^0$ ratios measured at RHIC and the LHC. We find that including the effect of radial flow of the medium is essential for describing the enhanced $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We also find that the puzzling larger $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio observed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC than in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC is due to the interplay between the effects of the QGP radial flow and the charm quark transverse momentum spectrum at hadronization. Our study further suggests that charmed hadrons have larger sizes in medium than in vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the puzzling larger $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio observed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC compared to Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC can be explained by the interplay between the radial flow of the quark-gluon plasma and the charm quark transverse momentum spectrum at hadronization, rather than by flow strength alone.

## Method

The authors developed a comprehensive coalescence model that includes both $s$ and $p$-wave charmed hadron states with strict energy-momentum conservation, ensuring boost invariance of coalescence probabilities and the thermal limit of hadron spectra. They combined this hadronization scheme with a Langevin-hydrodynamics model that simulates both elastic and inelastic energy loss of heavy quarks in a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic medium, using FONLL+EPPS16 initial charm quark spectra and a Monte-Carlo-Glauber model for initial spatial distributions.

## Key numbers

- In-medium radius of $D^0$: $r = 0.97$ fm, corresponding to oscillator frequency $\omega = 0.24$ GeV, compared to vacuum radius $r = 0.83$ fm with $\omega = 0.33$ GeV
- Constituent quark masses used: $m_{u,d} = 0.3$ GeV, $m_s = 0.4$ GeV, $m_c = 1.8$ GeV
- Spatial diffusion coefficient values: $D_s(2\pi T) = 3.5$ for RHIC and $4$ for LHC
- Coalescence dominates $D^0$ production up to 5 GeV and $\Lambda_c$ up to 8 GeV in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV with QGP flow
- Temperature of QGP boundary (hadronization hypersurface): $T_c = 160$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous coalescence models (Oh 2009, Plumari 2017, Cho 2019) by including all $s$ and $p$-wave charmed hadron states and strict energy-momentum conservation, overcoming the long-standing problem of broken boost invariance in earlier approaches. It provides the first simultaneous description of charmed hadron chemical compositions at both RHIC and the LHC, and suggests that charmed hadrons have larger sizes in medium than in vacuum, consistent with findings by Shi (2019). The model enables future tests of in-medium hadron sizes through hadronic model calculations.
