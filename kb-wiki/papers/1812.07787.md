# Electromagnetic Excitations and Responses in Nuclei from First Principles

> We discuss the role of clustering on monopole, dipole, and quadrupole excitations in nuclei in the framework of the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM). The SA-NCSM starts from nucleon-nucleon potentials and, by exploring symmetries known to dominate the nuclear dynamics, can reach nuclei up through the calcium region by accommodating ultra-large model spaces critical to descriptions of clustering and collectivity. The results are based on calculations of electromagnetic sum rules and discretized responses using the Lanczos algorithm, that can be used to determine response functions, and for 4He are benchmarked against exact solutions of the hyperspherical harmonics method. In particular, we focus on He, Be, and O isotopes, including giant resonances and monopole sum rules.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) combined with the Lanczos response method can, for the first time, decompose nuclear response functions into contributions from individual intrinsic deformations, but the results for \({}^{16}\)O and \({}^{10}\)Be are only in qualitative agreement with existing literature and require further systematic study.

## Method

The authors used the ab initio SA-NCSM with the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon potential to compute ground-state wave functions for \({}^{4}\)He, \({}^{16}\)O, and \({}^{10}\)Be. They then constructed a pivot vector from the ground state and the desired excitation operator (isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, or isoscalar quadrupole) and applied the Lanczos algorithm to generate discretized response functions and sum rules via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method. The SA-NCSM basis states are labeled by SU(3) quantum numbers \((\lambda\ \mu)\) that describe deformation, allowing the authors to examine the contribution of each deformation to peaks in the response.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{4}\)He, the ground state energy and rms radius calculated by SA-NCSM, conventional NCSM, and hyperspherical harmonics (HH) are in good agreement as a function of \(K_{\mathrm{max}}\) or \(N_{\mathrm{max}}\) using the JISP16 interaction (Figure 1).
- For \({}^{16}\)O, the monopole and quadrupole response functions were computed with a \(\left < 4 \right >8\) model space, \(\Gamma = 2\) MeV, and \(\hbar\Omega = 15\) MeV (Figure 3).
- For \({}^{10}\)Be, the dipole response and energy-weighted dipole response were computed with a \(\left < 4 \right >10\) model space, \(\Gamma = 2\) MeV, and \(\hbar\Omega = 20\) MeV (Figure 4).
- In the \({}^{16}\)O monopole response, more than 50% of the lower-energy peak is comprised of spin-0 configurations of \((\lambda\ \mu) = (2\ 0)\) and \((4\ 2)\); the main contributions (\(\sim 30\%\)) to the higher-energy peak are a spin-0 configuration of \((2\ 0)\) and a spin-2 configuration of \((4\ 2)\).
- In the \({}^{16}\)O quadrupole response, more than 25% of the lowest-energy peak is comprised of \((4\ 2)\), spin-0; the main peak is composed (\(\sim 30\%\)) of \((2\ 0)\) spin-0 and \((4\ 2)\) spin-2 configurations.
- In the \({}^{10}\)Be dipole response, the first peak is more than 35% comprised of \((3\ 2)\), spin-0; the second peak is more than 30% a combination of \((3\ 2)\), spin-0 and spin-1.

## Context

This work extends the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (Launey, Dytrych) by merging it with the Lanczos response method (Efros, Miorelli) to study electromagnetic excitations, building on prior studies of clustering and giant resonances (Youngblood 1977, Youngblood 1998, Lui, Gupta) and theoretical cluster-structure work (KE_Be, KE_C, Yamada, Kawabata, Chiba, Kimura, Suzuki). The approach is benchmarked against exact hyperspherical harmonics calculations (Efros, Barnea) for \({}^{4}\)He and shows qualitative agreement with results for \({}^{16}\)O (Stumpf) and \({}^{10}\)Be (KE_Be). It enables future first-principles identification of giant resonance characteristics in nuclei up through the calcium region.
