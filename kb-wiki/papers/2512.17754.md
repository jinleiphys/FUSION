# Unified study of $B_s^0 \to X(3872) π^+π^- (K^+ K^-)$ and $B_s^0 \to ψ(2S) π^+π^- (K^+ K^-)$ processes

> We perform a unified description of the experimental data of the $π^+π^-$ invariant mass spectra of $B_s^0 \to ψ(2S) π^+π^-$, the $π^+π^-$ and $K^+ K^-$ invariant mass spectra of $B_s^0 \to X(3872) π^+π^- (K^+ K^-)$, and the ratio of branch fractions $\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872)(K^+K^-)_{{\rm non-}ϕ}]/ \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872)π^+ π^-)$. The strong final state interactions between the two pseudoscalars are taken into account using a parametrization fulfilling unitarity and analyticity. We find that there is universality in the coupling constants for $B_s^0 \to ψ(2S) π^+π^-$ and $B_s^0 \to J/ψπ^+π^-$ processes. While the couplings of $B_s^0 \to X(3872) π^+π^-$ are about half of magnitude smaller than the couplings of $B_s^0 \to ψ(2S) π^+π^-$, which indicates that the $X(3872)$ is different from a pure charmonium state. Furthermore, we find that the $f_0(1500)$ plays an important role in the $B_s^0 \to ψ(2S) π^+π^-$ and the $B_s^0 \to X(3872) π^+π^- (K^+ K^-)$ processes, though the phase space of $B_s^0 \to X(3872) f_0(1500)$ is small. Also we predict the ratio of branch fractions $\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to ψ(2S)(K^+K^-)_{{\rm non-}ϕ}]/ \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to ψ(2S)π^+ π^-]$ and the $K^+ K^-$ invariant mass distribution of $B_s^0 \to ψ(2S)K^+K^-$.

## Key claim

The coupling constants for $B_s^0 \to X(3872) \pi^+\pi^-$ are about half the magnitude of those for $B_s^0 \to \psi(2S) \pi^+\pi^-$, indicating that the $X(3872)$ is different from a pure charmonium state, though the abstract's claim of "universality" applies only to the comparison between $B_s^0 \to \psi(2S) \pi^+\pi^-$ and $B_s^0 \to J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-$ processes.

## Method

The author performed a simultaneous fit to experimental data of the $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass spectra of $B_s^0 \to \psi(2S) \pi^+\pi^-$, the $\pi^+\pi^-$ and $K^+ K^-$ invariant mass spectra of $B_s^0 \to X(3872) \pi^+\pi^- (K^+ K^-)$, and the ratio of branching fractions $\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872)(K^+K^-)_{{\rm non-}\phi}]/ \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872)\pi^+ \pi^-)$. The strong final-state interactions between the two pseudoscalars were treated using a parametrization fulfilling unitarity and analyticity, which matches a dispersive description at low energies and a phenomenological isobar model at higher energies. Chiral contact Lagrangians were constructed for the $B_s^0 \psi(2S) PP$ and $B_s^0 X(3872) PP$ vertices, and three-channel ($\pi\pi$, $K\bar{K}$, effective $4\pi$) $S$-wave rescattering was included with the $f_0(1500)$ resonance parametrized as a bare pole.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant $g_1 = (2.77 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{10}~\text{GeV}^{-1}$ (Fit I) and $(2.91 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{10}~\text{GeV}^{-1}$ (Fit II) for $B_s^0 \to \psi(2S) \pi^+\pi^-$
- Coupling constant combination $(\tilde{g}_1-\sqrt{2}\tilde{g}_8) = (1.88 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{10}~\text{GeV}^{-1}$ (Fit I) and $(1.95 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{10}~\text{GeV}^{-1}$ (Fit II) for $B_s^0 \to X(3872) \pi^+\pi^-$
- Bare $f_0(1500)$ mass $m_r = 1.45 \pm 0.10~\text{GeV}$ (Fit I) and $1.47 \pm 0.10~\text{GeV}$ (Fit II)
- Ratio $\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872)(K^+K^-)_{{\rm non-}\phi}] / \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872)\pi^+ \pi^-] = 1.38 \pm 0.23$ (Fit I) and $1.37 \pm 0.21$ (Fit II), compared to the PDG value $2.05 \pm 1.16$
- Predicted ratio $\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \psi(2S)(K^+K^-)_{{\rm non-}\phi}] / \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \psi(2S)\pi^+ \pi^-] = 2.22 \pm 0.30$ (Fit I) and $2.09 \pm 0.29$ (Fit II)
- $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 150.2/(117-9) = 1.39$ (Fit I) and $130.6/(117-9) = 1.21$ (Fit II)

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Ropertz et al. (2018) for studying $B_s^0 \to J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-$ and $B_s^0 \to J/\psi K^+ K^-$ to the $\psi(2S)$ and $X(3872)$ channels. It contrasts with the study by Wang (2024) which used a chiral unitary approach and considered $f_0(1500)$ only for the $\psi(2S)$ processes, whereas this work includes $f_0(1500)$ effects for both $\psi(2S)$ and $X(3872)$ processes in a unitary and analytic framework. The finding that the $X(3872)$ couplings are about half those of $\psi(2S)$ suggests the $X(3872)$ is not a pure charmonium state, and the predicted $K^+K^-$ invariant mass distribution for $B_s^0 \to \psi(2S) K^+ K^-$ provides a testable target for future LHCb measurements.
