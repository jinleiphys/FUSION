# Near threshold laser-modified proton emission in nuclear photoeffect

> The change of the probability of proton emission in nuclear photoeffect due to an intense coherent (laser) field is discussed near the threshold, where the hindering effect of the Coulomb field of the remainder nucleus is essential. The ratio of laser-assisted and laser free differential cross section is deduced and found to be independent of the polarization state of the $γ$ field and the two types of initial nuclear state considered. The numerical values of this ratio are given at some characteristic parameters of the intense field.

# Near threshold laser-modified proton emission in nuclear photoeffect

## Key claim

The ratio of laser-assisted to laser-free differential cross sections for proton emission in the nuclear photoeffect near threshold is independent of the polarization state of the gamma field and of the two types of initial nuclear state considered, depending only on laser parameters; however, this ratio shows only a moderate increase (R < 1.28) over the parameter range studied, contrary to the symmetric behavior and R = 1 result found in the high-energy limit by Dadi (2012).

## Method

The authors calculate the gauge-invariant S-matrix element for laser-assisted nuclear photoeffect using Coulomb-Volkov wave functions for the final-state proton in the radiation (pA) gauge, within the long wavelength approximation and near threshold where the Coulomb field of the remainder nucleus strongly hinders emission. The initial nuclear state is taken either as a one-proton halo state (modeled on 8B) or a nu s1/2 shell model state, and the ratio R of laser-assisted to laser-free differential cross sections is derived analytically and evaluated numerically for a laser photon energy of 1.65 eV and intensities up to 10^20 W/cm^2.

## Key numbers

- For a remainder nucleus charge Z = 4, with Delta = 50 keV and laser intensity I = 10^20 W/cm^2, the ratio R increases linearly from R = 1.00 at I = 10^18 W/cm^2 to R = 1.12 at I = 10^20 W/cm^2.
- The maximum value of the total ratio R is less than 1.28 across the parameter range studied (Fig. 2).
- The Sommerfeld parameter eta(Q) = Z * alpha_f * m * c / (hbar * Q), where alpha_f is the fine structure constant.
- The parameter delta = e * A_0 / (hbar * c * Q) is approximately 0.05 near Delta = 50 keV.
- The separation energy of the 8B one-proton halo state is E_b = 0.137 MeV, with beta_1 = 1.495 x 10^12 cm^-1.
- The laser photon energy used in numerical calculations is hbar * omega_0 = 1.65 eV.
- The epsilon_n values range from epsilon_{-11000} = 0.761 to epsilon_{18000} = 1.240 for the parameters in Fig. 1.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of laser-assisted nuclear photoeffect (LANP) by Dadi (2012), who investigated the process far from threshold and neglected the Coulomb field of the remainder nucleus, finding symmetric n-dependence and total cross section unaffected by the laser (R = 1). The present paper incorporates the Coulomb hindrance near threshold, following the gauge-invariant approach of Kalman (1988) for the analogous laser-assisted x-ray photo effect. The results enable quantitative predictions for near-threshold photonuclear experiments with intense laser fields, though the authors note significant experimental challenges including synchronization of gamma and laser pulses, background from hot electron plasmas, and the short half-life of 8B (770 ms).
