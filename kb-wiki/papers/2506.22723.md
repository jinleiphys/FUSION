# Study on Pentaqaurks by Solving Schrodinger Equation in the Non-Hermitian Quantum Mechanics

> The interaction of the charmed baryon and the anticharmed meson is assumed to be realized by exchanging a scalar meson of $f_0(500)$, and then these systems are studied by solving the Schrodinger equation, respectively. When the pentaquarks $P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)^{+}$, $P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)^{+}$, $P_{c\bar{c}s}(4338)^0$, and $P_{c\bar{c}s}(4459)^{0}$ are treated as $Σ_c \bar{D}$, $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$, $Ξ_c \bar{D}$ and $Ξ_c \bar{D}^*$ bound states, four resonance states of them are obtained as solutions of the Schrodinger equation under the outgoing wave condition, respectively. The resonance state of $Σ_c \bar{D}$ might correspond to the particle $P_{c\bar{c}}(4440)^{+}$, while the other three resonance states have no counterparts in the review of the Particle Data Group(PDG). Although the binding energy of the bound state is only several MeVs, all these resonance states are more than 100 MeV higher than their corresponding thresholds, respectively. The calculation results indicate that the spectrums of the strange and non-strange pentaquarks are symmetric to each other.

## Key claim
The paper claims that pentaquark states observed by LHCb can be described as molecular bound states and resonance states of a charmed baryon and an anticharmed meson interacting via scalar meson exchange, but only one of the four calculated resonance states (the $\Sigma_c \bar{D}$ resonance near 4440 MeV) has a possible experimental counterpart in the Particle Data Group review; the other three predicted resonance states have no known experimental matches.

## Method
The author assumes a Yukawa-type potential mediated by scalar meson $f_0(500)$ exchange between charmed baryons ($\Sigma_c$, $\Xi_c$) and anticharmed mesons ($\bar{D}$, $\bar{D}^*$). The radial Schrodinger equation for S-wave ($l=0$) is solved analytically by transformation to a Bessel equation. Bound state energies are fitted to known pentaquark masses to determine the coupling constant, then the outgoing wave condition (using the second kind Hankel function) yields complex energies $E = M - i\Gamma/2$ for resonance states.

## Key numbers
- $P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)^{+}$ treated as $\Sigma_c^{+} \bar{D}^0$ bound state with binding energy 5.59 MeV yields a $\Sigma_c^{+} \bar{D}^0$ resonance at $4440 - i34$ MeV (with $m_\sigma = 440$ MeV)
- $P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)^{+}$ treated as $\Sigma_c^{++} D^-$ bound state with binding energy 11.57 MeV yields a $\Sigma_c^{++} D^-$ resonance at $4445 - i64$ MeV (with $m_\sigma = 440$ MeV)
- $P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)^{+}$ treated as $\Sigma_c^{+} \bar{D}^{*0}$ bound state with binding energy 2.2 MeV yields a $\Sigma_c^{+} \bar{D}^{*0}$ resonance at $4575 - i9$ MeV (with $m_\sigma = 440$ MeV)
- $P_{c\bar{c}s}(4338)^0$ treated as $\Xi_c^{+} D^-$ bound state with binding energy 0 MeV yields a $\Xi_c^{+} D^-$ resonance at $4451 - i1$ MeV (with $m_\sigma = 440$ MeV)
- $P_{c\bar{c}s}(4459)^0$ treated as $\Xi_c^{0} \bar{D}^{*0}$ bound state with binding energy 18.49 MeV yields a $\Xi_c^{0} \bar{D}^{*0}$ resonance at $4591 - i90$ MeV (with $m_\sigma = 440$ MeV)
- All resonance states are more than 100 MeV above their corresponding thresholds

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of pentaquarks, following the LHCb discoveries of $P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)^{+}$, $P_{c\bar{c}}(4440)^{+}$, and $P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)^{+}$ (LHCb 2019) and the strange pentaquarks $P_{c\bar{c}s}(4338)^0$ (LHCb 2022) and $P_{c\bar{c}s}(4459)^0$ (LHCb 2020). The method is similar to Sun (2024) where $K\bar{K}^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ systems were studied with one-pion exchange, but here scalar meson exchange replaces pion exchange. The results differ from unitary coupled-channel approaches (Wu 2010, Chen 2016, Shen 2019, Xiao 2019, Liu 2019, Wang 2019, Xiao 2020, Yalikun 2021) where bound and resonance states appear only near threshold; here resonance states appear more than 100 MeV above threshold. The predicted symmetry between strange and non-strange pentaquark spectra suggests that only non-strange meson exchange is involved.
