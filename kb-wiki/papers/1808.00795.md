# Initial correlations of the Glasma energy-momentum tensor

> We present an analytical calculation of the covariance of the energy-momentum tensor associated to the gluon field produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at early times, the Glasma. This object involves the two-point and single-point correlators of the energy-momentum tensor ($\langle T^{μν}(x_{\perp})T^{σρ}(y_{\perp})\rangle$ and $\langle T^{μν}(x_{\perp})\rangle$, respectively) at proper time $τ\!=\!0^+$. Our approach is based on the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, which allows us to map the fluctuations of the valence color sources in the colliding nuclei to those of the energy-momentum tensor of the produced gluon fields via the solution of the classical equations of motion in the presence of external currents. The color sources in the two colliding nuclei are characterized by Gaussian correlations, albeit in more generality than in the McLerran-Venugopalan model, allowing for non-trivial impact parameter and transverse dependence of the two-point correlator. We compare our results to those obtained under the Glasma Graph approximation, finding agreement in the limit of short transverse separations. However, important differences arise at larger transverse separations, where our result displays a slower fall-off than the Glasma Graph result ($r^{-2}$ vs $r^{-4}$ power-law decay), indicating that the color screening of the correlations in the transverse plane occurs at distances larger than $1/Q_s$ by a logarithmic factor sensitive to the infrared. In the Glasma flux tube picture, this implies that the color domains are larger than originally estimated.

## Key claim

The covariance of the Glasma energy-momentum tensor at proper time τ=0⁺ decays as 1/r² at large transverse separations r ≫ 1/Qₛ, which is slower than the 1/r⁴ decay predicted by the Glasma Graph approximation, implying that color screening occurs at distances larger than 1/Qₛ by a logarithmic factor sensitive to the infrared cutoff.

## Method

The authors performed an analytical calculation of the two-point and single-point correlators of the energy-momentum tensor of the gluon field produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at proper time τ=0⁺, using the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. They solved the classical Yang-Mills equations in the presence of two external color sources representing the colliding nuclei, with the sources characterized by Gaussian correlations more general than the McLerran-Venugopalan model, allowing non-trivial impact parameter and transverse dependence. The calculation involved evaluating the covariance ⟨T^{μν}(x_⟂)T^{σρ}(y_⟂)⟩ − ⟨T^{μν}(x_⟂)⟩⟨T^{σρ}(y_⟂)⟩ and comparing the result to the Glasma Graph approximation.

## Key numbers

- The large-distance limit of the covariance of the energy density (T⁰⁰ component) in the MV model is given by: lim_{rQₛ≫1} Cov[T⁰⁰_{MV}](0⁺;x_⟂,y_⟂) = [2(N_c²−1)(4π ∂²L(0_⟂))² (Q̄ₛ₁⁴ Qₛ₂² + Q̄ₛ₂⁴ Qₛ₁²)] / [g⁴ N_c² r²], where r = |x_⟂ − y_⟂|.
- The covariance decays as 1/r² at large transverse separations, compared to the 1/r⁴ power-law decay of the Glasma Graph approximation.
- The dimensionless ratio characterizing eccentricity fluctuations is given by (8π/(N_c²−1))[Q̄ₛ₁²(x_⟂)+Q̄ₛ₂²(x_⟂)] / ∫ d²x_⟂ Q̄ₛ₁²(x_⟂) Q̄ₛ₂²(x_⟂), which is larger in the middle of the overlap region than near the edge.
- The integrated covariance normalized by the average energy density scales as (Q̄ₛ₁²(x_⟂)+Q̄ₛ₂²(x_⟂))/(αₛ N_c).

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of Glasma correlations beyond the Glasma Graph approximation used in earlier calculations (Muller and Schaffer; Lappi, 2009), which assumed Gaussian statistics for the Weizsacker-Williams fields rather than for the color sources themselves. The result challenges the standard flux tube picture of Dumitru and collaborators (2008), where color domains were expected to have transverse size 1/Qₛ, by showing that correlations extend further due to a logarithmic enhancement ln(Qₛ/m) from the infrared cutoff. The analytical expressions can be directly applied in Bayesian multi-parameter fits to determine medium properties (Bernhard et al., 2016), in mode-by-mode studies of hydrodynamic propagation (Florkowski and collaborators, 2014), and in determining initial eccentricity fluctuations (Blaizot and collaborators, 2014).
