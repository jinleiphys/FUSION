# Reinterpreting the weak mixing angle from atomic parity violation in view of the Cs neutron rms radius measurement from COHERENT

> Using the model independent average neutron rms radius of $^{133}\text{Cs}$ and $^{127}\text{I}$ obtained from the analysis of the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data of the COHERENT experiment, we remove the long-standing $1.5\,σ$ tension between the Standard Model prediction and the weak mixing angle measurement from the atomic parity violation (APV) in caesium. The updated APV result becomes $\sin^2 \vartheta_{\textrm{W}}=0.239{}^{+0.006}_{-0.007}$, to be compared with the Standard Model prediction at low momentum transfer, $\sin^2 \vartheta_{\text{W}}^{\textrm{SM}} = 0.23857(5)$. Moreover, exploiting the fact that the APV result is highly sensitive to the caesium neutron rms radius, $R_{n}$, and assuming that the Standard Model is correct, we combine the APV and the COHERENT measurements in order to get a better determination of $R_{n}$. The value of $R_{n}=5.42\pm 0.31\,\textrm{fm}$ is obtained, improving significantly the current uncertainty. This result allows to infer a meaningful value of the caesium neutron skin, the difference between the neutron and proton distribution radii, equal to $ΔR_{np}=0.62\pm 0.31\,\text{fm}$, showing for the first time a $2\, σ$ deviation from zero.

# Reinterpreting the weak mixing angle from atomic parity violation in view of the Cs neutron rms radius measurement from COHERENT

## Key claim

Using a model-independent average neutron rms radius of Cs-133 and I-127 derived from COHERENT coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data, the authors remove the long-standing 1.5 sigma tension between the Standard Model prediction and the weak mixing angle extracted from atomic parity violation in caesium, but the resulting uncertainty is significantly enlarged compared to previous determinations that did not use direct experimental input.

## Method

The authors first remove the neutron-skin correction previously applied to the atomic parity violation (APV) weak charge of Cs-133, which had been based on extrapolated antiprotonic atom data, and replace it with a correction derived from the COHERENT measurement of the average neutron rms radius of Cs-133 and I-127. They then perform a combined least-squares fit of the COHERENT data and the APV measurement, assuming the Standard Model value of the weak mixing angle, to obtain an improved determination of the caesium neutron rms radius and neutron skin.

## Key numbers

- Updated weak mixing angle from APV after COHERENT-based correction: sin^2 theta_W = 0.239 (+0.006 / -0.007), compared to the Standard Model prediction at low momentum transfer sin^2 theta_W^SM = 0.23857(5)
- Combined fit of APV and COHERENT yields caesium neutron rms radius R_n = 5.42 +/- 0.31 fm
- Caesium neutron skin (difference between neutron and proton distribution radii): Delta R_np = 0.62 +/- 0.31 fm, showing a 2 sigma deviation from zero
- Weak charge of Cs-133 after correction: Q_W^Cs = -73.3 (+1.3 / -1.6), with difference from Standard Model delta Q_W^Cs = -0.1 (+1.3 / -1.6)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the COHERENT experiment's coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data by Cadeddu et al. (2018) and addresses the long-standing tension between the APV measurement on caesium by Wood et al. (1997) and the Standard Model prediction. The previous APV analysis by Dzuba et al. (2012) had used antiprotonic atom x-ray data to estimate the neutron skin correction, which the present work replaces with a direct experimental input. The improved neutron radius determination enables better constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy and its slope parameter L, which are relevant for neutron star equation of state studies following the LIGO/Virgo gravitational-wave observations. The results also inform estimates of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering backgrounds for future dark matter detectors such as XENONnT, DARWIN, and LZ.
