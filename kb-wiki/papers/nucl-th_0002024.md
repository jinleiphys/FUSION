# Finite T meson correlations and quark deconfinement

> Finite temperature spatial $\bar q q$ correlation modes in the $π$ and $ρ$ channels are studied with the rainbow-ladder truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Matsubara formalism. To retain the finite range of the effective interaction while facilitating summation over fermion Matsubara modes necessary to ensure continuity at {T=0}, a separable kernel is used. The model is fixed by {T=0} properties and it implements dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and quark confinement. Above and below the deconfinement and chiral restoration transitions, we study $M_π(T)$, $f_π(T)$ and the 3-space transverse and longitudinal masses $M_ρ^T(T)$ and $M_ρ^L(T)$. For the $ρ$ mode we also study the strong and electromagnetic partial widths to determine the $T$-dependence that is generated by the quark content. The $ρ\to ππ$ width decreases sharply just above the chiral restoration transition leaving the $ρ$ with only its narrow {$e^+ e^-$} width. We discuss improvements needed by this model, especially in regard to the high $T$ behavior of the masses where we compare to lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a separable-rank Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter model, which implements both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and quark confinement at zero temperature, can describe the temperature dependence of pion and rho meson masses, decay constants, and widths across the deconfinement and chiral restoration transitions, but the authors note that the model requires improvements for high-temperature behavior where they compare to lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The authors use the rainbow-ladder truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Matsubara formalism, employing a separable kernel (rank-1 and rank-2) to retain the finite range of the effective interaction while facilitating summation over fermion Matsubara modes. The model parameters are fixed by zero-temperature pion and rho/omega properties, and the finite-temperature extension is accomplished by transcribing Euclidean quark 4-momentum to discrete Matsubara frequencies. The solutions for the dressed quark propagator yield three amplitudes (A, B, C) due to the loss of O(4) symmetry, and the Bethe-Salpeter equations for pion and rho modes are solved to extract masses, decay constants, and partial widths.

## Key numbers

*   Pion mass at T=0: M_pi = 0.140 GeV (rank-1) and 0.139 GeV (rank-2), compared to experimental 0.1385 GeV.
*   Pion decay constant at T=0: f_pi = 0.104 GeV (rank-1) and 0.093 GeV (rank-2), compared to experimental 0.093 GeV.
*   Rho/omega mass at T=0: M_rho/omega = 0.783 GeV (rank-1) and 0.784 GeV (rank-2), compared to experimental 0.770/0.783 GeV.
*   Rho to pi pi width at T=0: Gamma_rho->pipi = 137 MeV (rank-1) and 221 MeV (rank-2), compared to experimental 151 MeV.
*   Rho to e+e- width at T=0: Gamma_rho0->e+e- = 6.76 keV (rank-1) and 4.22 keV (rank-2), compared to experimental 6.77 keV.
*   Dynamical quark mass at p^2=0 and T=0: m(p^2=0) = 0.685 GeV (rank-1) and 0.405 GeV (rank-2).
*   Chiral condensate at T=0: -langle qbar q rangle^0 = (0.248 GeV)^3 (rank-1) and (0.203 GeV)^3 (rank-2), compared to experimental (0.236 GeV)^3.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to finite temperature, building on zero-temperature studies by Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999) that successfully described light meson masses and decays. It contrasts with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (as referenced by Florkowski and Friman, 1994) which lacks quark confinement and leads to unphysical thresholds for qbar-q dissociation, and with earlier infrared-dominant models (as referenced by Munczek and Nemirovsky) that provided only qualitative information. The results enable future studies of vector meson modifications in hot dense matter relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding di-lepton spectra as a signal of the quark-gluon plasma.
