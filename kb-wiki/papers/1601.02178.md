# Cigar-shaped quarkonia under strong magnetic field

> Heavy quarkonia in a homogeneous magnetic field are analyzed by using a potential model with constituent quarks. To obtain anisotropic wave functions and corresponding eigenvalues, the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method is applied, where the anisotropic wave functions are expanded by a Gaussian basis in the cylindrical coordinates. Deformation of the wave functions and the mass shifts of the $S$-wave heavy quarkonia ($η_c$, $J/ψ$, $η_c(2S)$, $ψ(2S)$ and bottomonia) are examined for the wide range of external magnetic field. The spatial structure of the wave functions changes drastically as adjacent energy levels cross each other. Possible observables in heavy-ion collision experiments and future lattice QCD simulations are also discussed.

# Cigar-shaped quarkonia under strong magnetic field

## Key claim

Heavy quarkonia (charmonia and bottomonia) in a homogeneous magnetic field develop cigar-shaped (prolate) wave functions and exhibit mass shifts that depend strongly on spin state and excitation level, with the excited states being more sensitive to the magnetic field than ground states. The authors qualify that their nonrelativistic potential model neglects relativistic corrections, anisotropic confinement potential modifications, and continuum threshold changes, which would be needed for more quantitative results.

## Method

The authors solve the nonrelativistic two-body Schrödinger equation with the Cornell potential (linear confinement plus Coulomb term) and a Gaussian spin-spin interaction, using the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method (CGEM) to expand anisotropic wave functions in Gaussian bases on the transverse plane and along the longitudinal axis separately. For spin-singlet and longitudinal spin-triplet states, they solve a coupled-channel equation that includes mixing via the quark magnetic moment term \(-\boldsymbol{\mu}_i \cdot \boldsymbol{B}\), while transverse spin-triplet states are treated with a single-channel calculation. The charm quark mass, string tension, and Coulomb parameter are taken from lattice QCD fits, with remaining parameters tuned to reproduce experimental \(\eta_c\) and \(J/\psi\) masses.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark kinetic mass: \(m_c = 1.7840\ \mathrm{GeV}\); string tension: \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 0.402\ \mathrm{GeV}\); Coulomb parameter: \(A = 0.713\)
- Mass shifts at \(eB = 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\): \(\eta_c(1S)\): \(-9.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(J/\psi\): \(+12.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(\eta_c(2S)\): \(-16.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(\psi(2S)\): \(+28.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Mass shifts at \(eB = 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (LHC-relevant field): transverse \(J/\psi_{|1\pm1\rangle}\): \(12.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\); transverse \(\psi(2S)_{|1\pm1\rangle}\): \(50.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\); longitudinal \(J/\psi_{|10\rangle}\): \(79.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\); longitudinal \(\psi(2S)_{|10\rangle}\): \(121.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Mass splitting between transverse and longitudinal components at \(eB = 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\): \(67.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(J/\psi\); \(70.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(\psi(2S)\)
- Maximum magnetic field studied: \(eB = 10\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (compared to LHC maximum \(\sim 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\))
- For bottomonia, longitudinal \(\Upsilon(1S)\) approaches \(\eta_b(2S)\) at about \(eB = 5.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)

## Context

This work extends previous potential model studies of ground-state quarkonia in magnetic fields by Alford and Strickland (2013) and Bonati et al. (2015), which used the finite difference time domain method and were limited to ground states only. The CGEM approach enables simultaneous treatment of ground and excited states, including coupled-channel mixing between spin-singlet and spin-triplet states. The results provide predictions for heavy-ion collision observables such as dilepton spectra from vector quarkonia, where the mass splitting between transverse and longitudinal components could serve as a sensitive probe of the magnetic field strength, and offer benchmarks for future lattice QCD simulations.
