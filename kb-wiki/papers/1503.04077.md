# Test of the Atiyah-Singer Index Theorem for Fullerene with a Superconducting Microwave Resonator

> Experiments have been performed using a spherical superconducting microwave resonator that simulates the geometric structure of the C60 fullerene molecule. The objective was to study with very high resolution the exceptional spectral properties emerging from the symmetries of the icosahedral structure of the carbon lattice. In particular, the number of zero modes has been determined to test the predictions of the Atiyah-Singer index theorem, which relates it to the topology of the curved carbon lattice. This is, to the best of our knowledge, the first experimental verification of the index theorem.

## Key claim

The authors report the first experimental verification of the Atiyah-Singer index theorem for fullerenes, but with the hedge that the six near-zero modes (two triplets) approach zero energy only in the thermodynamic limit of an infinite number of carbon atoms; in the finite C60 molecule they lie close to but not exactly at the Dirac frequency.

## Method

A spherical superconducting microwave resonator was constructed with 60 circular cavities (vertices) and 90 rectangular channels (edges) milled into a brass sphere and coated with lead, replicating the truncated icosahedral structure of C60. Transmission spectra were measured below 4.2 K using a vector network analyzer, and the resonance frequencies in the first band (8.254–8.779 GHz) were compared to tight-binding model (TBM) calculations that included next-nearest, second-nearest, and third-nearest neighbor couplings. The TBM was then used to compute spectra for larger fullerene molecules (C180, C240, C540, C720) to study the approach to the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- The first band contains 60 resonances in the frequency interval [8.254, 8.779] GHz, with the Dirac frequency determined as f_D = 8.504 GHz from extrapolation.
- The TBM fit yielded for the isolated cavity frequency f_0 = 8.515 GHz and coupling parameters t_1 = -0.0929 GHz, t_2 = 0.0035 GHz, and t_3 = 0.0005 GHz.
- The two triplets of zero modes are located slightly above the Dirac frequency; for C60 the lower triplet lies at approximately 8.504 GHz and the upper triplet at approximately 8.508 GHz (from Fig. 5).
- The extrapolation fit f(n) = f_D + a/n^b gave f_D = 8.504 GHz, with a = 1.188 GHz, b = 0.661 for the lower zero modes and a = 1.211 GHz, b = 0.869 for the upper ones.
- The resonator quality factor was Q > 10^5, and the cutoff frequency for the first propagating mode in the waveguides was f_c^1 ≳ 10.714 GHz.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical predictions by Pachos (2007) and Pachos (2007a) that derived an index theorem for graphene sheets deformed by pentagons, and by Gonzalez (1992, 1993), Kolesnikov (2006), and Vozmediano (2010) who predicted zero modes in continuum models of icosahedral fullerenes. It also builds on the authors' previous experiments with flat superconducting microwave Dirac billiards (Bittner 2012, Dietz 2015, Dietz 2015a) and on TBM calculations for C60 by Manousakis (1991). The results enable experimental tests of topological effects in curved graphene structures and provide a method to determine the Dirac frequency of fullerene molecules.
