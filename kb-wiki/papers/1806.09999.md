# Constraints on the equation of state from the stability condition of neutron stars

> The stellar equilibrium and collapse, including mainly white dwarfs, neutron stars and supper massive stars, is an interplay between general relativistic effects and the equation of state of nuclear matter. In the present work, we use the Chandrasekhar criterion of stellar instability by employing a large number of realistic equations of state (EoS) of neutron star matter. We mainly focus on the critical point of transition from stable to unstable configuration. This point corresponds to the maximum neutron star mass configuration. We calculate, in each case, the resulting compactness parameter, $β=GM/c^2R$, and the corresponding effective adiabatic index, $γ_{\rm cr}$. The role of the trial function $ξ(r)$ is presented and discussed in details. We found that it holds a model-independent relation between $γ_{\rm cr}$ and $β$. This statement is strongly supported by the large number of EoS and it is also corroborated by using analytical solutions of the Einstein's field equations. In addition, we present and discuss the relation between the maximum rotation rate and the adiabatic index close to the instability limit. Accurate observational measurements of the upper bound of the neutron star mass and the corresponding radius, in connection with the present predictions, may help to impose constraints on the high density part of the neutron star equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a model-independent relation between the critical adiabatic index and the compactness parameter at the maximum mass configuration of neutron stars, but this universality is established only for the specific trial function TF-1 and the relation becomes more complicated when using other trial functions.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for 14 realistic equations of state based on various nuclear models, then applied the Chandrasekhar variational stability criterion to compute the critical adiabatic index and compactness parameter at the onset of instability (maximum mass configuration). They compared these numerical results with four analytical solutions of Einstein's field equations (Schwarzschild constant-density, Tolman VII, Buchdahl, and Nariai IV) and examined the dependence of the maximum rotation frequency on the critical adiabatic index.

## Key numbers

- The Tolman VII solution gives the onset of instability at compactness beta = 0.3475 and critical adiabatic index gamma_cr = 3.85
- The maximum rotation frequency has an absolute lower upper bound close to 1460 Hz
- From GW170817 constraints, the maximum compactness is beta_max = 0.333_{-0.005}^{+0.001}, corresponding to gamma_cr,max = 3.381_{-0.095}^{+0.020}
- The fastest known pulsar PSR J1748-244ad rotates at 716 Hz
- The observed mass limits are M = 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses (PSR J1614-2230) and M = 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses (PSR J0348+0432)
- From GW170817, the radius R_max of the nonrotating maximum-mass configuration must be larger than 9.6_{-0.04}^{+0.15} km, and M_max is constrained to be less than or equal to 2.17 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the Chandrasekhar (1964) stability criterion from the post-Newtonian regime to the fully relativistic regime by using realistic equations of state, finding that the linear relation between gamma_cr and beta in the Newtonian limit becomes exponential at high compactness. The results enable constraints on the high-density equation of state through combined measurements of neutron star mass and radius, as demonstrated using the GW170817 constraints from Bauswein et al. (2017) and Margalit and Metzger (2017). The Tolman VII analytical solution is shown to reproduce the numerical results from realistic equations of state particularly well for high compactness values.
