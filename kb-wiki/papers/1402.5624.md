# Effective Field Theory for Neutron Stars with Genuine Many-body Forces

> The aim of our contribution is to shed some light on open questions facing the high density nuclear many-body problem. We focus our attention on the conceptual issue of naturalness and its role for the baryon-meson coupling for nuclear matter at high densities. As a guideline for the strengths of the various couplings the concept of naturalness has been adopted. In order to encourage possible new directions of research, we discuss relevant aspects of a relativistic effective theory for nuclear matter with natural parametric couplings and genuine many-body forces. Among other topics, we discuss in this work the connection of this theory with other known effective Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) models found in literature and how we can potentially use our approach to describe new physics for neutron stars. We also show some preliminary results for the equation of state, population profiles and mass-radius relation for neutron stars assuming local charge neutrality and beta equilibrium.

# Effective Field Theory for Neutron Stars with Genuine Many-body Forces

## Key claim

The paper argues that applying the naturalness condition, requiring all dimensionless coupling coefficients to be of order unity, to a relativistic effective field theory for nuclear matter can systematically incorporate genuine many-body forces, and that this approach yields neutron star masses up to 1.97 solar masses that agree with recent observations, but only for a specific choice of the parameter λ that produces a stiffer equation of state.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) Lagrangian density in the mean-field approximation that includes the full baryon octet (n, p, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Λ, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰) and meson fields (σ, σ*, ω, φ, ρ, δ), with effective baryon-meson couplings parameterized by a set of exponents (ξ, κ, η, ς). They focus on a scalar version (ς ≠ 0, ξ = κ = η = 0) where the effective baryon mass depends on a single parameter λ, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations under local charge neutrality and beta equilibrium to obtain equations of state, particle populations, and mass-radius relations for neutron stars with hyperon content.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass of 1.97 M⊙ for λ = 0.06, in agreement with observations by Demorest et al. (2010)
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and binding energy ε_B = −16.0 MeV adopted as nuclear matter saturation properties
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation ranges from 0.70 to 0.78 (in units of bare mass) for λ between 0.06 and 0.14
- Symmetry energy coefficient a_asym = 32.5 MeV used to fix the isovector coupling
- Compressibility modulus K₀ values: 262 MeV (λ = 0.06), 226 MeV (λ = 0.10), 216 MeV (λ = 0.14)
- Hyperon potential depths: U_Λ = U_Σ = −30 MeV, U_Ξ = 30 MeV
- Σ⁻ fraction saturates at about 0.3 in the particle population

## Context

This work extends the Quantum Hadrodynamics framework of Serot and Walecka (1997) by incorporating the naturalness condition from Georgi (1993) and Manohar (1996) as an organizing principle for coupling constants, and generalizes earlier parameterized coupling models by Taurines et al. (2001) and Vasconcellos et al. (2012). The approach connects to the Boguta and Bodmer (1997) model when the expansion is truncated to cubic and quartic σ self-interactions, and enables systematic inclusion of many-body forces that shift hyperon thresholds to higher densities, with implications for neutron star structure at densities ρ ≥ 5 ρ₀.
