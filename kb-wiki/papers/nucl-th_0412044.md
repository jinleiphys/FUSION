# Surface diffuseness anomaly in heavy-ion potentials for large-angle quasielastic scattering

> Recent high precision experimental data for heavy-ion fusion reactions at subbarrier energies systematically show that a surprisingly large surface diffuseness parameter for a Woods-Saxon potential is required in order to fit the data. We point out that experimental data for quasi-elastic scattering at backward angles also favor a similar large value of surface diffuseness parameter. Consequently, a double folding approach fails to reproduce the experimental excitation function of quasielastic scattering for the $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Sm system at energies around the Coulomb barrier. We also show that the deviation of the ratio of the quasielastic to the Rutherford cross sections from unity at deep subbarrier energies offers an unambiguous way to determine the value of the surface diffuseness parameter in the nucleus-nucleus potential.

# Surface diffuseness anomaly in heavy-ion potentials for large-angle quasielastic scattering

## Key claim

Experimental data for quasielastic scattering at backward angles favor a large surface diffuseness parameter (around 1.05 fm) for the Woods-Saxon potential, consistent with the large diffuseness required by subbarrier fusion data, rather than the commonly accepted value of around 0.63 fm from elastic scattering analyses. The deviation of the quasielastic-to-Rutherford cross section ratio from unity at deep subbarrier energies provides an unambiguous way to determine the surface diffuseness parameter.

## Method

The authors calculated fusion and quasielastic cross sections for the 16O + 154Sm system using an orientation-integrated formula in the isocentrifugal approximation, with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential including quadrupole (beta2=0.306) and hexadecapole (beta4=0.05) deformations. They compared results for diffuseness parameters of 1.05 fm and 0.65 fm, and also computed cross sections using a double folding potential with the density-dependent M3Y interaction. A short-range imaginary potential was used to simulate compound nucleus formation, with absorption cross sections identified as fusion cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Surface diffuseness parameter of 1.05 fm (large value) fits both quasielastic and fusion data for 16O + 154Sm, while 0.65 fm (standard value) does not
- Standard diffuseness from elastic scattering is around 0.63 fm, while fusion data require values ranging from 0.75 to 1.5 fm
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters for the large-diffuseness case: V0=165 MeV, R=0.95 x (A_P^(1/3)+A_T^(1/3)) fm, a=1.05 fm
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters for the standard-diffuseness case: V0=220 MeV, R=1.1 x (A_P^(1/3)+A_T^(1/3)) fm, a=0.65 fm
- Quasielastic data measured at theta_lab=170 degrees for 16O + 154Sm show clear inconsistency with a=0.65 fm at energies between 35 and 55 MeV
- Double folding potential yields results similar to the Woods-Saxon potential with a=0.65 fm, failing to reproduce experimental excitation functions

## Context

This work extends the "surface diffuseness anomaly" previously identified in heavy-ion fusion reactions (Hagino 2002, Hagino 2004, Newton 2001, Hinde 2003) to quasielastic scattering at backward angles. The standard Woods-Saxon diffuseness of about 0.63 fm from elastic scattering analyses (Brock 1991, Christensen 1976, Love 1980) is inconsistent with both fusion and quasielastic data. The finding that double folding potentials fail to reproduce subbarrier quasielastic excitation functions contradicts the success of double folding for angular distributions in many systems, and the authors suggest further investigation into the energy dependence of double folding potentials due to exchange contributions. This work enables systematic determination of the surface diffuseness parameter through quasielastic measurements at deep subbarrier energies, which are experimentally easier than fusion measurements.
