# Direct Urca processes involving singlet proton superfluidity in neutron star cooling

> A detailed description of the baryon direct Urca processes A: $n\rightarrow p+e+\barν_{e}$, B: $Λ\rightarrow p+e+\barν_{e}$, C: $Ξ^{-}\rightarrowΛ+e+\barν_{e}$ related to the neutron star cooling is given in the relativistic mean field approximation.The contributions of the reactions B and C on the neutrino luminosity are calculated by means of the relativistic expressions of the neutrino energy losses.Our results show that the total neutrino luminosities of the reactions A, B, C within the mass range 1.603-2.067$M_{\odot}$ (1.515-1.840$M_{\odot}$ for TM1 model) for GM1 model are larger than the corresponding values for neutron stars in npe$μ$ matter. Although the hyperon direct Urca processes B and C reduce the neutrino emissivity of the reaction A, it illustrates the reactions B and C still make the total neutrino luminosity enhancement in the above mentioned areas.Furthermore, when we only consider the $^{1}S_{0}$ proton superfluidity in neutron star cooling, we find that although the neutrino emissivity of the reactions A and B is suppressed with the appearance of $^{1}S_{0}$ proton superfluidity, the total contribution of the reactions A, B, C can still quicken a massive neutron star cooling.These results could be used to help prove appearing hyperons in PSR J1614-2230 and J0348+0432 from neutron star cooling perspective.

## Key claim

The total neutrino luminosity from the combined baryon direct Urca processes A (n→p+e+ν̄e), B (Λ→p+e+ν̄e), and C (Ξ−→Λ+e+ν̄e) is enhanced relative to npeμ matter within specific mass ranges (1.603–2.067 M⊙ for GM1 model; 1.515–1.840 M⊙ for TM1 model), even though the hyperon processes B and C individually reduce the neutrino emissivity of process A. When ¹S₀ proton superfluidity is included, the neutrino emissivities of processes A and B are suppressed, but the total contribution of all three processes can still accelerate the cooling of massive neutron stars.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation with GM1 and TM1 parameter sets, using both SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry and SU(3) flavor symmetry for the vector meson-hyperon coupling constants. They calculated the neutrino emissivities and luminosities for baryon direct Urca processes A, B, and C using relativistic expressions for neutrino energy losses, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to obtain mass-radius relations. The ¹S₀ proton superfluidity was incorporated using the Reid soft core (RSC) potential for the pp pairing gap, with the critical temperature Tcp = 0.57 Δ(k) and corresponding reduction factors for neutrino emissivity and thermal capacity.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses from GM1 model: 1.820 M⊙ (case i), 1.863 M⊙ (case ii), 2.141 M⊙ (case iii, SU(3) flavor symmetry)
- Maximum neutron star masses from TM1 model: 1.686 M⊙ (case i), 1.729 M⊙ (case ii), 2.038 M⊙ (case iii, SU(3) flavor symmetry)
- Mass ranges for reactions B and C in case (iii) with GM1 model: 1.671–2.141 M⊙ and 1.888–2.141 M⊙, respectively
- Mass ranges for reactions B and C in case (iii) with TM1 model: 1.579–2.038 M⊙ and 1.849–2.038 M⊙, respectively
- Total neutrino luminosity enhancement mass range for GM1 model: 1.603–2.067 M⊙
- Total neutrino luminosity enhancement mass range for TM1 model: 1.515–1.840 M⊙
- Critical temperature crossover density for ¹S₀ proton SF: ρB = 0.454 fm⁻³ (GM1 model) and ρB = 0.418 fm⁻³ (TM1 model), below which Tcp in npheμ matter is lower than in npeμ matter and above which it is higher
- Observed masses used as references: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ (Demorest 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ (Antoniadis 2013)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star cooling by Prakash (1992) and Levenfish (1994) on baryon direct Urca processes, and by Yakovlev (1999) on superfluid effects, by specifically calculating the combined contributions of hyperon direct Urca processes B and C alongside the nucleon process A. The use of SU(3) flavor symmetry (following Weissenborn 2013 and Miyatsu 2013) allows the equation of state to support the observed massive neutron stars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, which SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry could not explain. The results enable future work on proving the presence of hyperons in massive neutron star cores through cooling observations, and the authors note that effects of the neutron star crust and hyperon superfluidity were not considered and remain open questions.
