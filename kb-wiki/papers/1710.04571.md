# Dissecting the hadronic contributions to $(g-2)_μ$ by Schwinger's sum rule

> The theoretical uncertainty of $(g-2)_μ$ is currently dominated by hadronic contributions. In order to express those in terms of directly measurable quantities, we consider a sum rule relating $g-2$ to an integral of a photo-absorption cross section. The sum rule, attributed to Schwinger, can be viewed as a combination of two older sum rules: Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Burkhardt-Cottingham. The Schwinger sum rule has an important feature, distinguishing it from the other two: the relation between the anomalous magnetic moment and the integral of a photo-absorption cross section is linear, rather than quadratic. The linear property makes it suitable for a straightforward assessment of the hadronic contributions to $(g-2)_μ$. From the sum rule we rederive the Schwinger $α/2π$ correction, as well as the formula for the hadronic vacuum-polarization contribution. As an example of the light-by-light contribution we consider the single-meson exchange.

## Key claim

The Schwinger sum rule, which relates the anomalous magnetic moment linearly (rather than quadratically) to an integral of a photo-absorption cross section, can be used to express the hadronic contributions to $(g-2)_\mu$ in terms of directly measurable quantities, provided one considers the spin-structure functions of the muon in the quasi-real-photon limit.

## Method

The authors derive the Schwinger sum rule as a linear combination of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) and Burkhardt-Cottingham (BC) sum rules, and apply it to the muon. They separate hadronic contributions into two types: (i) hadron photo-production off the muon (e.g., $\gamma \mu \to \pi^0 \mu$), which can be accessed experimentally via the spin-structure function combination $g_1+g_2$; and (ii) hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contributions to non-hadronic channels such as Compton scattering, which require only the HLbL amplitude with two virtual photons rather than three. The method is validated by reproducing the Schwinger $\alpha/2\pi$ correction and the standard hadronic vacuum-polarization (HVP) formula.

## Key numbers

* The Schwinger correction $\alpha/2\pi$ is reproduced from the sum rule using tree-level Compton scattering cross sections.
* The HVP contribution is expressed as $\varkappa^{\mathrm{HVP}} = \frac{\alpha}{\pi^2}\int_{4m_\pi^2}^\infty ds\, K(s/m^2) \, \frac{\mathrm{Im}\,\Pi^{\mathrm{had}}(s)}{s}$, where $K(s/m^2) = \int_0^1 dx \frac{x^2(1-x)}{x^2 + (1-x)(s/m^2)}$.
* The hadronic contribution to $\varkappa_\mu$ is of order $10^{-8}$, starting at $O(\alpha^2)$.
* The GDH sum rule involves $\varkappa^2$, making the lhs $O(\alpha^5)$ while hadronic photo-production cross sections start at $O(\alpha^3)$, requiring cancellation to at least 5 orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach of Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (1965-1966) and Burkhardt-Cottingham (1970) by using Schwinger's linear sum rule (1975) to access hadronic contributions to $(g-2)_\mu$. It contrasts with the standard dispersive approach for HVP (Jegerlehner, 2017; Davier, 2016) and the more complex dispersive analyses for HLbL (Colangelo et al., 2014-2017; Pauk, 2014), as the Schwinger sum rule requires only the transition form factor for one virtual photon (e.g., $M\to \gamma\gamma^*$) rather than two virtual photons. The method enables a data-driven evaluation of hadronic contributions that could be measured at an electron-muon collider with polarized beams, and may also apply to axion contributions to $g-2$.
