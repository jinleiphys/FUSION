# Maximum Azimuthal Anisotropy of Neutrons from Nb-Nb Collisions at 400 AMeV and the Nuclear Equation of State

> We measured the first azimuthal distributions of triple--differential cross sections of neutrons emitted in heavy-ion collisions, and compared their maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratios with Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (BUU) calculations with a momentum-dependent interaction. The BUU calculations agree with the triple- and double-differential cross sections for positive rapidity neutrons emitted at polar angles from 7 to 27 degrees; however, the maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio for these free neutrons is insensitive to the size of the nuclear incompressibility modulus K characterizing the nuclear matter equation of state.

## Key claim

The maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio for free neutrons emitted in Nb-Nb collisions at 400 AMeV is insensitive to the nuclear incompressibility modulus K, contrary to earlier theoretical expectations; however, the full one-body BUU calculations that include composite fragments do exhibit sensitivity to K, suggesting that composites rather than free neutrons carry the equation-of-state information.

## Method

The experiment measured triple-differential cross sections for neutrons emitted from semi-central Nb-Nb collisions at 400 AMeV using an array of 18 time-of-flight scintillation detectors spanning polar angles from 3 to 90 degrees, with a plastic-wall array of 184 detectors for charged fragments to determine the reaction plane via the transverse-velocity method. The measured azimuthal distributions were fit with a cosine function and corrected for the finite dispersion in the reaction plane determination (approximately 40 degrees). The data were compared with Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model calculations that included a momentum-dependent nuclear mean field and a correction for cluster contamination by rejecting neutrons within 2.5 fm of another nucleon from the same BUU ensemble.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 400 AMeV with an energy spread of ±41 AMeV at the center of the Nb target (physical thickness 2.04 g/cm²)
- Reaction plane dispersion Δφ_R approximately 40 degrees for all rapidity cuts, with uncertainties of about 5%
- Multiplicity threshold M ≥ 26 selected about 22% of the total geometric cross section, corresponding to a ratio of maximum impact parameter to nuclear diameter b₀/2R = 0.47
- Flight paths to the 18 neutron detectors ranged from 5.91 to 8.38 m; flight paths to the 184 plastic-wall detectors ranged from 4.0 to 5.0 m
- Velocity uncertainty for charged fragments detected in the plastic wall was typically 3% for 400 AMeV fragments
- Statistical uncertainties in the triple-differential cross sections were typically about 10% per 18-degree φ-bin at polar angles from 7 to 15 degrees, increasing to about 14% for φ-φ_R > 90 degrees at θ = 18-24 degrees
- The critical distance d used to identify free neutrons in BUU calculations was 2.5 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of azimuthal distributions for charged particles (Beavis et al., 1985; Doss et al., 1987; Gutbrod et al., 1989; Gosset et al., 1989) and theoretical proposals by Welke et al. (1988) suggesting the maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio as a probe of the nuclear equation of state. The finding that free neutrons are insensitive to K while composites carry the sensitivity enables future work focusing on azimuthal anisotropy of composite fragments, with preliminary results reported as encouraging by Gale, Pan, and Danielewicz (1993).
