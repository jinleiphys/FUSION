# Realistic Shell-Model Calculations for Nuclei in the Region of Shell Closures off Stability

> We have performed realistic shell-model calculations for nuclei around doubly magic 100Sn and 132Sn using an effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential. The results are in remarkably good agreement with the experimental data showing the ability of our effective interaction to provide an accurate description of nuclear structure properties.

## Key claim

Realistic shell-model calculations using an effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential achieve remarkably good agreement with experimental data for nuclei around doubly magic 100Sn and 132Sn, but the quality of agreement depends on the correct treatment of the Pauli exclusion operator in the G-matrix calculation and on the choice of single-particle energies.

## Method

The authors derived a two-body effective interaction from the Bonn A potential using a G-matrix folded-diagram formalism that included renormalizations from core polarization and folded diagrams, with the Q-box composed of G-matrix diagrams through second order in G. They performed shell-model calculations for N=50 isotones (98Cd, 97Ag, 96Pd) treating 100Sn as an inert core with proton valence holes, and for N=82 isotones (134Te, 135I) and the doubly odd nucleus 132Sb using a 132Sn core with valence protons and neutrons in a model space of five single-particle orbits (0g7/2, 1d5/2, 2s1/2, 1d3/2, 0h11/2).

## Key numbers

- For 98Cd, the rms deviation sigma between calculated and experimental yrast states (2+, 4+, 6+, 8+) is 105 keV.
- For 97Ag, the rms deviation sigma is 121 keV; the 13/2+ state lies 213 keV above the observed value.
- For 96Pd, the rms deviation sigma is 130 keV; the 2+ state lies 281 keV above the observed value.
- For 134Te, the rms deviation sigma is 106 keV; 9 out of 15 states have discrepancies smaller than 50 keV.
- For 135I, the rms deviation sigma is 58 keV.
- For 132Sb positive-parity states, the rms deviation sigma is 32 keV; the largest discrepancy is 77 keV for the 5+1 state.
- The calculated B(E2; 8+ -> 6+) in 98Cd is 0.67 W.u. using bare proton charge, compared to the experimental value of 0.44(+0.20/-0.10) W.u.
- For 134Te, using effective proton charge 1.55e, the calculated B(E2; 4+1 -> 2+1) is 4.2 W.u. (experiment: 4.3 +/- 0.3 W.u.) and B(E2; 6+1 -> 4+1) is 1.9 W.u. (experiment: 2.05 +/- 0.03 W.u.).
- The oscillator parameter hbar-omega was 8.5 MeV for A=100 and 7.88 MeV for A=132.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier realistic shell-model calculations for light Sn isotopes (Andreozzi 1996, Covello 1997) to nuclei further from stability, using the Bonn A potential which had shown better agreement than the Paris potential due to its weaker tensor force component. The study of 132Sb provides a direct test of T=0 effective interaction matrix elements, addressing a known difficulty where earlier calculations with different NN potentials (Jiang 1992) had insufficient attraction in the T=0 channel. The improved treatment of the Pauli exclusion operator compared to the authors' earlier work (Andreozzi 1997) yields substantially better agreement for 134Te and 135I, and the results enable future studies of nuclear structure properties near shell closures off stability.
