# Gapless neutron superfluidity can explain the late time cooling of transiently accreting neutron stars

> The current interpretation of the observed late time cooling of transiently accreting neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries during quiescence requires the suppression of neutron superfluidity in their crust at variance with recent ab initio many-body calculations of dense matter. Focusing on the two emblematic sources KS~1731$-$260 and MXB~1659$-$29, we show that their thermal evolution can be naturally explained by considering the existence of a neutron superflow driven by the pinning of quantized vortices. Under such circumstances, we find that the neutron superfluid can be in a gapless state in which the specific heat is dramatically increased compared to that in the classical BCS state assumed so far, thus delaying the thermal relaxation of the crust. We have performed neutron-star cooling simulations taking into account gapless superfluidity and we have obtained excellent fits to the data thus reconciling astrophysical observations with microscopic theories. The imprint of gapless superfluidity on other observable phenomena is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The observed late-time cooling of transiently accreting neutron stars KS 1731-260 and MXB 1659-29 can be explained by gapless neutron superfluidity in the crust, driven by a superflow from pinned quantized vortices, without requiring the suppression of neutron superfluidity that contradicts recent ab initio many-body calculations. The authors treat the effective superfluid velocity as a free parameter in their fits.

## Method

The authors used the crustcool code to solve the heat diffusion equation in the neutron star crust, incorporating modifications for superfluid neutron diffusion (following Gusakov and Chugunov 2020, 2021) and implementing gapless superfluidity with a specific heat that depends on the effective superfluid velocity relative to the Landau critical velocity. They performed Markov Chain Monte Carlo simulations to fit the effective surface temperature evolution of KS 1731-260 and MXB 1659-29, treating the impurity parameter, base temperature, core temperature, and superfluid velocity as free parameters, using realistic pairing calculations from Gandolfi (2022).

## Key numbers

- For KS 1731-260 with gapless superfluidity: best-fit superfluid velocity Vn = 1.21 (+0.10, -0.11) times the Landau critical velocity VLn; impurity parameter Qimp = 5.80 (+1.68, -1.25); base temperature Tbase = 3.13 (+0.19, -0.20) x 10^8 K; core temperature Tcore = 3.99 (+0.26, -0.34) x 10^7 K
- For MXB 1659-29 first outburst with gapless superfluidity: best-fit Vn = 1.23 (+0.09, -0.11) VLn; Qimp = 17.16 (+3.49, -3.70); Tbase = 3.14 (+0.16, -0.15) x 10^8 K; Tcore = 0.85 (+0.59, -0.54) x 10^7 K
- For KS 1731-260 with BCS superfluidity (no superflow): Qimp = 10.56 (+2.15, -1.97); Tbase = 2.45 (+0.19, -0.18) x 10^8 K; Tcore = (4.69 +/- 0.14) x 10^7 K
- For MXB 1659-29 first outburst with BCS superfluidity: Qimp = 8.05 (+0.71, -0.61); Tbase = (3.11 +/- 0.13) x 10^8 K; Tcore = (2.69 +/- 0.15) x 10^7 K
- The gapless state occurs for superfluid velocities between VLn and Vcn, where Vcn is approximately exp(1)/2 times VLn
- The maximum superfluid velocity from pinning forces is estimated as Vcr ~ 10^7 cm/s for a pinning force of 10^18 dyn/cm; VLn is of order 10^8 cm/s
- The neutron star mass is assumed to be 1.62 solar masses and radius 11.2 km, with a constant accretion rate of 10^17 g/s

## Context

This work resolves a tension between astrophysical observations of quasipersistent soft X-ray transients and microscopic theories of neutron superfluidity. Previous studies (Turlione 2015) required artificially suppressing superfluidity in most of the crust to fit the cooling data, while Deibel (2017) assumed neutrons remain normal in the deep crust based on extrapolations of quantum Monte Carlo calculations that were later ruled out by Gandolfi (2022). The gapless superfluidity mechanism, whose microscopic theory was developed by Allard and Chamel (2023), provides an alternative that reconciles the cooling observations with realistic pairing calculations and with the independent requirement of neutron superfluidity for pulsar glitch interpretation. The work enables future tests through continued monitoring of KS 1731-260 and MXB 1659-29, and application to other sources such as HETE J1900.1-2455, which appears unusually cold and may require similar physics.
