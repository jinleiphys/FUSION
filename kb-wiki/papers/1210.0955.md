# Effect of superfluidity on neutron star crustal oscillations

> We consider how superfluidity of dripped neutrons in the crust of a neutron star affects the frequencies of the crust's fundamental torsional oscillations. A nonnegligible superfluid part of dripped neutrons, which do not comove with nuclei, act to reduce the enthalpy density and thus enhance the oscillation frequencies. By assuming that the quasi-periodic oscillations observed in giant flares of soft gamma repeaters arise from the fundamental torsional oscillations and that the mass and radius of the neutron star is in the range of 1.4 < M/M_SUN < 1.8 and 10 km < R < 14 km, we constrain the density derivative of the symmetry energy as 100 MeV < L < 130 MeV, which is far severer than the previous one, L > 50 MeV, derived by ignoring the superfluidity.

## Key claim

Neutron superfluidity in the crust enhances the frequencies of fundamental torsional oscillations by reducing the effective enthalpy density, and when the observed quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) from soft gamma repeaters are identified with these modes, the density derivative of the symmetry energy is constrained to 100 MeV < L < 130 MeV for neutron stars with masses 1.4-1.8 solar masses and radii 10-14 km. This is a stricter constraint than the previous lower limit of L > 50 MeV derived without considering superfluidity.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations inward from the surface for various mass-radius combinations and crust equations of state parameterized by the symmetry energy slope L. They computed eigenfrequencies of fundamental torsional oscillations using the relativistic Cowling approximation, replacing the enthalpy density H with an effective enthalpy density that subtracts the superfluid neutron component not comoving with nuclei, using superfluid fractions from band-structure calculations by Chamel (2012). The predicted frequencies were compared to low-lying QPO frequencies observed in giant flares from SGR 1806-20 (18, 26, 30, 92.5 Hz) and SGR 1900+14 (28, 54, 84 Hz) to determine the allowed range of L.

## Key numbers

- The fundamental torsional frequency for a star with M=1.8 solar masses and R=14 km increases by up to 66-82% for L less than or equal to 76.4 MeV and up to 131% for L=146.1 MeV when superfluidity is included, compared to the non-superfluid case.
- The lower limit on L (L_min) from the 18 Hz QPO alone is 47.6 MeV without superfluidity, rising to 125.9 MeV with full superfluidity (N_s/N_d=1), and 55.2 MeV using the realistic superfluid fractions from Chamel (2012).
- For a star with M=1.4 solar masses and R=12 km, the best-fit L value reproducing the SGR 1806-20 QPOs (identified as ell=3,4,5,15) is L=127.1 MeV, with calculated frequencies of 18.5 Hz (ell=3), 24.9 Hz (ell=4), 31.0 Hz (ell=5), and 90.3 Hz (ell=15), all within 5% of observations.
- The combined constraint from both SGR 1806-20 and SGR 1900+14 yields an allowed range of 100 MeV < L < 130 MeV for stars with 1.4-1.8 solar masses and 10-14 km radius.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Sotani et al. (2012) that ignored superfluidity and obtained only a lower limit L > 50 MeV. It incorporates the band-structure calculations of superfluid neutron fractions by Chamel (2012) and is consistent with earlier studies by Aguilera et al. (2009), Sotani et al. (2009), and Passamonti and Andersson (2012) that examined superfluidity effects for specific crust models. The resulting constraint on L is tighter than most experimental determinations, which tend to favor smaller L values, and enables more precise predictions for the extent of pasta phases in the crust as discussed by Oyamatsu and Iida (2007).
