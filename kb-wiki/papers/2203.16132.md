# Towards (semi) exclusive cross section measurements and modelling

> Semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering, corresponding to the simultaneous detection of a lepton and one or more hadrons in the final state, is shown to be much more sensitive to nuclear effects than the inclusive process. The theoretical description of the semi-inclusive process is illustrated and some comparisons of relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) predictions with recent MicroBooNE, T2K and MINERvA data on argon and carbon are shown. The merit of new variables constructed to enhance particular nuclear effects is also illustrated and future developments going beyond the RPWIA approach are outlined.

# Towards (semi) exclusive cross section measurements and modelling

## Key claim
Semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering is shown to be much more sensitive to nuclear effects than inclusive scattering, but the Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA) calculations, while reproducing single differential cross sections reasonably well, fail to match observables based on combined lepton/hadron variables (single transverse kinematic imbalances) that are particularly sensitive to final state interactions and two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) effects.

## Method
The author presents the formalism for semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering within the Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA), using either a Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) or an Independent Particle Shell Model (IPSM) based on relativistic mean field theory for the nuclear initial state, with the final proton state approximated by a plane wave. The 6th-differential cross section is computed and compared with recent MicroBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA data on argon and carbon targets, including comparisons using single transverse kinematic imbalance (STKI) variables.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum for argon in the RFG calculation: k_F = 0.241 GeV/c
- Phase-space restrictions for MicroBooNE comparison: k' > 0.1 GeV/c and 0.3 < p_N < 1.2 GeV/c
- T2K operates at average neutrino energy ⟨E_ν⟩ ∼ 0.6 GeV
- MINERvA operates at average neutrino energy ⟨E_ν⟩ ∼ 3 GeV
- Fixed muon kinematics for cross section calculations: k' = 1.5 GeV/c, θ = 30 degrees, φ_N = π

## Context
This work extends the SuSAv2 model, previously validated for inclusive electron and neutrino scattering, to the semi-inclusive case. It builds on the formalism developed by Moreno (2014), VanOrden (2019), and Franco-Patino (2020) for semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus reactions. The paper shows that while inclusive models (such as those by Martini (2009), Nieves (2011), and Benhar (2005)) produce similar predictions with 10-20% spread, semi-inclusive observables can discriminate between nuclear models. The results enable future implementation of final state interactions using complex optical potentials, as previously applied to exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, and will help reduce nuclear uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE and T2HK.
