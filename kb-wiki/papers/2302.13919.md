# Decoding the flow evolution in Au+Au reactions at $1.23 A$ GeV using hadron flow correlations and dileptons

> We investigate the development of the directed, $v_1$, and elliptic flow, $v_2$, in heavy ion collisions in mid-central Au+Au reactions at $E_\mathrm{lab}=1.23 A$ GeV. We demonstrate that the elliptic flow of hot and dense matter is initially positive ($v_2>0$) due to the early pressure gradient. This positive $v_2$ transfers its momentum to the spectators, which leads to the creation of the directed flow $v_1$. In turn, the spectator shadowing of the in-plane expansion leads to a preferred decoupling of hadrons in the out-of-plane direction and results in a negative $v_2$ for the observable final state hadrons. We propose a measurement of $v_1-v_2$ flow correlations and of the elliptic flow of dileptons as methods to pin down this evolution pattern. The elliptic flow of the dileptons allows then to determine the early-state EoS more precisely, because it avoids the strong modifications of the momentum distribution due to shadowing seen in the protons. This opens the unique opportunity for the HADES and CBM collaborations to measure the Equation-of-State directly at 2-3 times nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim
The paper argues that in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, the initially positive elliptic flow ($v_2>0$) generated by pressure gradients during the compression phase is the source of directed flow ($v_1$), and that spectator shadowing subsequently converts this into the negative $v_2$ observed in final-state hadrons. The authors propose that dilepton elliptic flow and $v_1$-$v_2$ correlations can directly probe the early-stage equation of state at 2-3 times nuclear saturation density, avoiding the shadowing effects that obscure hadronic flow measurements.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v3.5) model with a chiral mean field equation of state to simulate 20-30% peripheral Au+Au collisions at a kinetic beam energy of 1.23$A$ GeV. They extracted the time evolution of directed flow ($v_1^*$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) for all baryons in the system and for emitted nucleons at freeze-out. Dilepton emission was calculated using a coarse-graining method with state-of-the-art vector meson spectral functions, and the elliptic flow of dileptons at invariant masses of 0.15 GeV and 0.6 GeV was compared to hadronic flow.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: $E_\mathrm{lab}=1.23 A$ GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Centrality: 20-30% peripheral collisions (impact parameter range $6.6\leq b\leq8.1$ fm)
- Maximum compression density: $\rho_\mathrm{B}/\rho_0\approx2-3$ (2-3 times nuclear saturation density)
- Directed flow plateau value: $v_1^*\approx0.18$ for all baryons after 15 fm/c
- Dilepton elliptic flow: increases up to 3% at 1 GeV transverse momentum for both invariant masses (0.15 GeV and 0.6 GeV)
- Time of maximum dilepton emission: centered at 10-12 fm/c
- Time of nucleon kinetic freeze-out peak: 18-20 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the understanding of flow development at low beam energies, building on previous studies of elliptic flow at SIS energies by FOPI (2004, 2011), EOS/E895 (1999, 2000), and E877 (1997), as well as recent HADES measurements (2020, 2022). The authors note that similar time evolutions of elliptic flow were presented by LeFevre (2016, 2019) and Gao (2022) using QMD models at lower energies, but those studies did not link the anisotropic expansion dynamics to experimentally accessible observables. The paper proposes two new observables, $v_1$-$v_2$ correlations and dilepton elliptic flow, to directly probe the equation of state at 2-3 times saturation density, which could be measured by the HADES and CBM collaborations at FAIR.
