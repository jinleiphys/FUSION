# Explaining higher-order correlations between elliptic and triangular flow

> The ALICE and CMS Collaborations have analyzed a number of cumulants mixing elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$), involving up to $8$ particles, in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. We unravel an unexpected simplicity in these complex mathematical quantities for collisions at fixed impact parameter. We show that as one increases the order in $v_2$, for a given order in $v_3$, the changes in the cumulants are solely determined by the mean elliptic flow in the reaction plane, which originates from the almond-shaped geometry of the overlap area between the colliding nuclei. We derive simple analytic relations between cumulants of different orders on this basis. These relations are in good agreement with recent data from the CMS Collaboration. We argue that agreement will be further improved if the analysis is repeated with a finer centrality binning. We make quantitative predictions for cumulants of order 10 which have not yet been analyzed.

# Explaining higher-order correlations between elliptic and triangular flow

## Key claim
The authors show that for collisions at fixed impact parameter, the changes in mixed harmonic cumulants as one increases the order in elliptic flow ($v_2$) for a given order in triangular flow ($v_3$) are solely determined by the mean elliptic flow in the reaction plane, which originates from the almond-shaped geometry of the overlap area. The derived analytic relations between cumulants of different orders are in good agreement with recent CMS data, but the authors argue that agreement will be further improved if the analysis is repeated with a finer centrality binning.

## Method
The authors derive analytic expressions relating experimental mixed harmonic cumulants (MHC) measured in the laboratory frame to cumulants defined in the "intrinsic frame" where the impact parameter direction is fixed. They apply a power-counting scheme based on the typical magnitude of flow fluctuations ($\mathcal{F}$) and the mean elliptic flow in the reaction plane ($\bar V_2$), truncating expressions to leading order in the small parameter $\mathcal{F} \sim \bar V_2$. By eliminating intrinsic cumulants through linear combinations of measured cumulants, they obtain simple ratio predictions that are compared with ALICE and CMS data from Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- $MHC(v_2^6, v_3^2) / [v_2\{4\}^2 \cdot MHC(v_2^4, v_3^2)] = -6$: predicted ratio from leading-order theory, in good agreement with CMS data
- $MHC(v_2^8, v_3^2) / [v_2\{4\}^2 \cdot MHC(v_2^6, v_3^2)] = -11$: predicted ratio for cumulants of order 10 not yet analyzed
- $MHC(v_2^4, v_3^2) / [v_2\{4\}^2 \cdot MHC(v_2^2, v_3^2)] \approx -2$: predicted ratio valid above 5% centrality where $\bar V_2 > \mathcal{F}$
- $[MHC(v_2^4, v_3^4) + 2 v_2\{4\}^2 MHC(v_2^2, v_3^4)] / MHC(v_2^2, v_3^2)^2 \approx -4$: predicted ratio involving cumulants with $v_3^4$
- $v_2\{4\}^4 MHC(v_2^2, v_3^6) / [MHC(v_2^2, v_3^2)(MHC(v_2^4, v_3^4) + 6 v_2\{4\}^2 MHC(v_2^2, v_3^4))] \approx 9/8$: order-of-magnitude prediction for cumulants with $v_3^6$
- $v_2\{4\}^2 MHC(v_2^6, v_3^4) + 6 v_2\{4\}^4 MHC(v_2^4, v_3^4)] / MHC(v_2^4, v_3^2)^2 = 6$: predicted ratio for cumulants of order 10 not yet measured

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Alqahtani and Ollitrault, 2025) which showed that the lowest-order mixed harmonic cumulant (the symmetric cumulant) is driven by the correlation between elliptic flow in the reaction plane and $v_3^2$. The paper builds on measurements by ALICE (2021) and CMS (2025) of higher-order mixed harmonic cumulants involving up to 8 particles in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The results enable future precision studies of subdominant non-Gaussian fluctuations (such as the mixed kurtosis $c_{1111}$) and provide testable predictions for cumulants of order 10 that have not yet been analyzed experimentally. The authors note that full hydrodynamic calculations with fluctuating initial conditions (iEBE-VISHNU and IP-GLASMA+MUSIC+URQMD frameworks) should be checked against these analytic results, particularly for elliptic flow above 30% centrality where deviations from linear hydrodynamic response are significant.
