# Photon polarization tensor in a magnetized plasma: Absorptive part

> We calculate the absorptive part of the photon polarization tensor in a hot magnetized relativistic plasma. In the derivation, we utilize a Landau-level representation for the fermion Green's function in a mixed coordinate-momentum space and obtain a closed-form expression for the one-loop polarization tensor. At the leading order in the coupling, its absorptive part is determined by particle and antiparticle splitting processes ($e^{-} \leftrightarrow e^{-}+γ$ and $e^{+} \leftrightarrow e^{+}+γ$, respectively), as well as by particle-antiparticle annihilation processes ($e^{-} + e^{+}\leftrightarrow γ$). The interpretation in terms of quantum transitions between Landau levels is also given. By making use of the photon polarization tensor, we study the differential photon emission rate in the quantum limit of magnetized relativistic plasma. At low energies, the photon emission has a prolate profile with the symmetry axis along the line of the magnetic field. At high energies, on the other hand, the photon emission has an oblate profile. The underlying reasons for such emission profiles are given in both regimes. The general result for the photon polarization tensor is also used to calculate the longitudinal and transverse components of magneto-optical conductivity.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed-form analytical expression for the absorptive (imaginary) part of the one-loop photon polarization tensor in a hot magnetized relativistic plasma, but only for the quantizing regime where Landau level quantization effects are important, not the semiclassical weak-field regime.

## Method

The authors used a Landau-level representation for the fermion Green's function in mixed coordinate-momentum space within the Matsubara finite-temperature formalism, performing the Dirac traces and transverse spatial integrations analytically. They extracted the imaginary part of the retarded polarization tensor by analytic continuation \(i\Omega_m \to \Omega + i\epsilon\) and evaluated the resulting delta function constraints by solving the energy conservation equation for the longitudinal momentum \(p_z\). The final expression involves sums over Landau level indices \(n, n'\) and contributions from particle splitting (\(e^{-} \to e^{-} + \gamma\)), antiparticle splitting (\(e^{+} \to e^{+} + \gamma\)), and particle-antiparticle annihilation (\(e^{-} + e^{+} \to \gamma\)) processes.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength for quantum electrodynamics vacuum birefringence: \(B_c \simeq m_e^2/e \approx 4.414 \times 10^{13}~\text{G}\)
- Magnetic field strength in noncentral heavy-ion collisions: \(B \gtrsim m_\pi^2/e \approx 3.1 \times 10^{18}~\text{G}\)
- Electron mass used for QED plasma calculations: \(m = 0.511~\text{MeV}\)
- The transverse momentum threshold for splitting processes: \(k_{-} = \left|\sqrt{m^2 + 2n|qB|} - \sqrt{m^2 + 2n'|qB|}\right|\)
- The transverse momentum threshold for annihilation processes: \(k_{+} = \left|\sqrt{m^2 + 2n|qB|} + \sqrt{m^2 + 2n'|qB|}\right|\)

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the photon polarization tensor in strong magnetic fields beyond the lowest Landau level approximation (Bandyopadhyay 2016) and the weak-field limit (Das 2019, Ghosh 2019), which were limited to those regimes. It also goes beyond vacuum polarization results from Tsai (1974) and Shabad (1975) by including finite temperature effects. The result enables calculation of differential photon emission rates and magneto-optical conductivity in strongly magnetized relativistic plasmas, with applications to heavy-ion collision phenomenology (as used in Wang 2020 to address the photon \(v_2\) puzzle) and to understanding synchrotron emission in the quantum limit.
