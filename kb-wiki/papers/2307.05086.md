# Neutron star equation of state: identifying hadronic matter characteristics

> The general behavior of the nuclear equation of state (EOS), relevant for the description of neutron stars (NS), is studied within a relativistic mean field description of nuclear matter. Different formulations, both with density dependent couplings and with non-linear mesonic terms, are considered and their predictions compared and discussed. A special attention is drawn to the effect on the neutron star properties of the inclusion of exotic degrees of freedom as hyperons. Properties such as the speed of sound, the trace anomaly, the proton fraction and the onset of direct Urca processes inside neutron stars are discussed. The knowledge of the general behavior of the hadronic equation of state and the implication it has on the neutron star properties will allow to identify signatures of a deconfinement phase transition discussed in other studies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with different density dependencies (density-dependent couplings versus non-linear mesonic terms) can all reproduce current neutron star observations, but they predict distinct nuclear matter properties and neutron star characteristics that could be distinguished by future measurements; the authors hedge that the composition of neutron stars, including proton fraction and hyperon content, cannot be determined from agnostic EOS descriptions alone and requires a microscopic model.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference calculations using three classes of relativistic mean-field models: density-dependent couplings with exponential parametrization (DDB), density-dependent couplings with rational function parametrization (DDH), and non-linear mesonic terms (NL). They constrained the model parameters using nuclear saturation properties (binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy), pure neutron matter pressures from chiral effective field theory at three densities (0.08, 0.12, and 0.16 fm⁻³), and the requirement that the maximum neutron star mass exceeds 2.0 solar masses. The posterior distributions were sampled using the nested sampling algorithm PyMultinest, generating 25287 equations of state for each model class.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian inference studies of RMF models by Traversi (2020), Sun (2022), Beznogov (2022), and Huang (2023) by comparing three distinct RMF formulations (DDB, DDH, NL) under identical observational constraints. The study complements agnostic EOS inference approaches such as piecewise polytropic interpolation (Kurkela 2014, Annala 2021), spectral interpolation (Lindblom 2010), and speed-of-sound interpolation (Annala 2019, Altiparmak 2022, Somasundaram 2022) by providing a microscopic framework that can predict composition, including proton fraction and hyperon content. The results enable future identification of deconfinement phase transitions by comparing the hadronic matter signatures (speed of sound, trace anomaly, polytropic index) with predictions from studies of quark matter, and can be tested by upcoming missions such as eXTP and STROBE-X.
