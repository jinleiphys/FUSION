# Short-range Effects on Nuclear Pairing

> We determine the influence of the three-body force and the medium modification of meson masses on pairing in nuclear and neutron matter. A reduction of the pairing gap is found and increasing with density.

# Short-range Effects on Nuclear Pairing

## Key claim
The three-body force and medium modification of meson masses both reduce the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in neutron and nuclear matter, with the reduction increasing with density; however, the authors state that no definite quantitative conclusions can be drawn due to too many theoretical uncertainties, and the effects appear small compared to polarization corrections.

## Method
The authors solved the BCS gap equation for $^1S_0$ pairing using the Argonne $V_{18}$ potential as the bare two-body force, adding an effective two-body force derived from a meson-exchange model of the three-body force. They also performed calculations with the Bonn-B potential, varying the $\omega$ meson mass both independently of density and according to the Brown-Rho scaling model, using a free single-particle spectrum.

## Key numbers
- The saturation density of nuclear matter is given as $\rho_0 \approx 0.15-0.17\;\rm fm^{-3}$.
- The peak domain of the energy gap in neutron matter is at $\rho\approx 0.02\;\rm fm^{-3}$.
- The three-body force $V_3(r)$ is effective mainly at short range $r \lesssim 1\;\rm fm$.
- The Brown-Rho scaling model uses $m^*_{\omega}/m_{\omega} = 1/(1 + \alpha(\rho/\rho_0))$, where $\alpha$ is a constant and $\Lambda$ is the mass cutoff.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of pairing in nuclear and neutron matter that showed the sensitivity of the gap equation to the short-range part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, as discussed in Lombardo (1999) and the review by Lombardo and Schulze (2001). The three-body force treatment follows the approach of Lejeune et al. (1986) and the meson mass scaling follows Brown and Rho (1991). The results indicate that short-range effects from three-body forces and meson mass changes reduce the gap, but the reduction is smaller than that from polarization corrections (Lombardo and Schulze, 2001), and further theoretical work is needed to reduce uncertainties.
