# Formation of quark phases in protoneutron stars: the transition from the 2SC to the normal quark phase

> We study the process of formation of quark phases in protoneutron stars. After calculating the phase transition between nucleonic matter and the 2SC phase at fixed entropy and lepton fraction, we show that an unpairing transition between the 2SC phase and the normal quark phase occurs for low lepton fractions. We then calculate the process of diffusion of neutrinos in protoneutron stars and show that for intermediate values of the mass of the star, the deleptonization triggers the phase transition between the two quark phases after a temporal delay of a few seconds. In less massive stars instead only the normal quark phase is formed at the end of the deleptonization stage. We also discuss the possible astrophysical implications of our scenario.

# Formation of quark phases in protoneutron stars: the transition from the 2SC to the normal quark phase

## Key claim
The deleptonization of a protoneutron star can trigger a first-order phase transition from the 2SC (two-flavor color superconducting) quark phase to the normal (unpaired) quark phase, but only for intermediate stellar masses; in less massive stars only the normal quark phase forms at the end of deleptonization, and the timing estimate of a few seconds is only an order-of-magnitude estimate because reheating effects were neglected.

## Method
The author computed the equation of state for protoneutron star matter using a relativistic mean field model (TM1 parameterization) for nucleonic matter and a modified bag model including color superconductivity for quark matter, with the phase transition calculated via the Gibbs construction under conditions of fixed entropy per baryon (S/N=1) and varying lepton fraction (Y_L from 0.4 to 0.04). The neutrino diffusion equation was solved numerically in the diffusion approximation, coupled with the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for quasi-static equilibrium, using absorption-dominated mean free paths for degenerate neutrinos in both nucleonic and quark phases.

## Key numbers
- Initial lepton fraction: Y_L = 0.4, with corresponding electron/proton fraction Y_e = Y_p ~ 0.3 at the star center
- Bag constant B fixed to 155 MeV^(1/4); diquark coupling G_D = 3/4 G_S
- Maximum mass for cold catalyzed hybrid stars: 1.94 M_sun, consistent with the measured mass of 1.97 ± 0.04 M_sun (Demorest 2010)
- Onset of phase transition occurs at twice nuclear saturation density for initial lepton fraction and entropy configurations
- For a hybrid star with baryonic number N_B = 2.29 × 10^57: initial gravitational mass M = 1.78 M_sun, central pressure p_c = 118 MeV/fm^3, initial central neutrino density n_nu0 = 3.44 × 10^(-2) fm^(-3)
- For a neutron star with N_B = 2.29 × 10^57: central pressure p_c = 83 MeV/fm^3, gravitational mass ~1.78 M_sun
- For a hybrid star with N_B = 1.74 × 10^57: initial gravitational mass 1.38 M_sun, initial central neutrino density n_nu0 = 1.66 × 10^(-2) fm^(-3)
- Superconducting gap Delta ~ 100 MeV
- The unpairing transition occurs at t ~ 4 s for the intermediate-mass hybrid star case
- Neutron star deleptonization time: central neutrino density halves after t* ~ 7 s

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of quark phase formation in protoneutron stars by Steiner (2002), Ruester (2005), Sandin (2007), and Lugones (2010), who argued that the 2SC phase might form during the protoneutron star stage while the CFL phase appears later. It builds on the calculation of neutrino mean free paths in color superconducting matter by Carter (2000) and on the analysis of the unpairing transition in asymmetric fermionic systems by Bedaque (1999), Kiriyama (2001), Lawley (2005), and Ruester (2005). The work enables future studies of observable signatures in the neutrino signal from protoneutron stars and motivates a full three-component mixed phase calculation including nucleonic matter, 2SC, and normal quark phases.
