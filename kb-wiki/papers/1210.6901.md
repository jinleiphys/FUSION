# Thermodynamics of the QCD transition from lattice

> We discuss recent developments in lattice QCD for the bulk thermodynamics of the transition. We review the current status of the equation of state, the possible implications of a magnetic field and the fluctuations of conserved charges, like the net electric charge or baryon number. We also show predictions for higher cumulants, which will be experimentally available in near future.

## Key claim

The QCD transition at zero chemical potential is a crossover, not a first-order phase transition, and the transition temperature depends on the observable chosen, spanning a broad range rather than a single value.

## Method

The Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration performed lattice QCD simulations with physical quark masses using the stout staggered fermion formulation, with lattice resolutions from Nt=6 to Nt=16. They extracted the transition temperature from the inflection point of the chiral condensate, and also computed the equation of state, fluctuations of conserved charges, and the effects of a magnetic field, all extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from chiral condensate inflection point: Tc = 155(3)(3) MeV (first error statistical, second systematic)
- Normalized energy density inflection point: 157(4)(3) MeV
- Trace anomaly inflection point: 154(4)(3) MeV
- Speed of sound minimum: approximately 145(5) MeV
- Curvature of the transition line in the T-mu_B phase diagram: kappa = 0.0066(20) from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration
- Curvature from O(4) scaling analysis on Nt=8 lattices: kappa = 0.0066(2)(4)
- Curvature from strange susceptibility: 0.0089(14)
- HotQCD transition temperature: 154(8)(1) MeV based on O(4) scaling

## Context

This work extends the earlier continuum result of 192(4)(7) MeV by the RBC-Bielefeld group, which was based on coarser lattices and heavier quarks, and the discrepancy was resolved by the adoption of the HISQ action by HotQCD. The results enable precise comparisons with heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, provide input for relativistic hydrodynamics, and allow extrapolation of the equation of state to finite chemical potential. The finding that the transition temperature decreases with magnetic field contradicts earlier model expectations from PNJL models and exploratory lattice simulations by Cea (2007).
