# Monte Carlo methods and applications for the nuclear shell model

> The shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) technique transforms the traditional nuclear shell-model problem into a path-integral over auxiliary fields. We describe below the method and its applications to four physics issues: calculations of sdpf- shell nuclei, a discussion of electron-capture rates in pf-shell nuclei, exploration of pairing correlations in unstable nuclei, and level densities in rare earth systems.

## Key claim

The shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method provides a practical alternative to direct diagonalization for very large nuclear model spaces, but it cannot yield detailed spectroscopic information such as individual excitation energies and wave functions; instead it computes thermal expectation values and response functions, which are complementary to direct diagonalization for modest spaces and the only viable method for very large problems.

## Method

The SMMC technique transforms the nuclear shell-model Hamiltonian into a path integral over auxiliary fields via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, replacing the two-body interaction with a superposition of one-body evolutions in fluctuating external fields. The imaginary-time evolution operator \(e^{-\beta H}\) is discretized into time slices, and for each slice a Gaussian integral over auxiliary fields linearizes the two-body term. Observables are computed as ratios of field integrals using Metropolis Monte Carlo sampling, with a sign-problem extrapolation method that varies the strength of "bad" (positive \(V_\alpha\)) interaction components to reach the physical Hamiltonian from a sign-free region.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{32}\)Mg, the experimental \(B(E2) = 454 \pm 78 \, e^2\text{fm}^4\) yields a deformation parameter \(\beta_2 = 0.513\) (using \(R_0 = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm).
- SMMC calculations with effective charges \(e_p = 1.5\) and \(e_n = 0.5\) give \(B(E2)\) values for \(^{32}\)Mg and \(^{30}\)Ne of 177.1 and 143.2 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) respectively when restricted to the \(sd\)-shell (USD values), far below the full \(sd\)-\(pf\) results.
- For electron capture on \(^{56}\)Ni at \(\rho_7 = 5.86\), \(T_9 = 3.40\), \(Y_e = 0.47\), the SMMC total rate is \(3.47 \times 10^{-2}\) s\(^{-1}\), compared to \(1.60 \times 10^{-2}\) s\(^{-1}\) from Aufderheide et al.
- For \(^{56}\)Fe at \(\rho_7 = 10.7\), \(T_9 = 3.65\), \(Y_e = 0.455\), the SMMC total rate is \(9.80 \times 10^{-6}\) s\(^{-1}\), versus \(4.68 \times 10^{-7}\) s\(^{-1}\) from Aufderheide et al.
- For odd-\(A\) nuclei such as \(^{55}\)Co at the same conditions, the SMMC rate is \(3.89 \times 10^{-4}\) s\(^{-1}\), while the Aufderheide total rate is \(1.41 \times 10^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\).
- The SMMC level density for \(^{162}\)Dy agrees well with experimental data from Tveter et al. (1996) in the 1–3 MeV excitation range, but increases more rapidly than data from 3–5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SMMC formalism developed by Lang et al. (1993), Koonin et al. (1997), and Ormand et al. (1994) to four new application areas: neutron-rich \(sd\)-\(pf\) nuclei, electron-capture rates in \(pf\)-shell nuclei, pairing correlations near \(N=Z\), and level densities in rare-earth nuclei. The electron-capture rate comparisons directly challenge the rates used in supernova collapse calculations by Aufderheide et al. and Fuller et al., showing that for odd-\(A\) nuclei the Gamow-Teller contribution has been significantly overestimated in prior work. The level-density results for \(^{162}\)Dy provide the first SMMC test of the pairing-plus-quadrupole interaction in the rare-earth region and show that the static path approximation (SPA) used by Rossignoli et al. does not yield consistently reliable results.
