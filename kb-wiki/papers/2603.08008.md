# Physics-Informed Global Extraction of the Universal Small-$x$ Dipole Amplitude

> We extract the universal small-$x$ dipole scattering amplitude $N(r,x_B)$ from a global analysis based on a physics-informed neural network (PINN), without imposing a priori MV-type parametrization of the initial condition. The network provides a smooth and differentiable surrogate for $N(r,x_B)$, whose rapidity dependence is constrained by the collinearly improved Balitsky--Kovchegov evolution equation, while its functional form is simultaneously constrained by Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) data for the reduced total and charm cross sections, exclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction measurements, and a positivity requirement for the momentum-space dipole amplitude. The resulting single universal amplitude consistently describes all fitted observables within a unified framework, alleviating the long-standing tension between total and charm channels encountered in conventional small-$x$ fits based on rigid parametric ansätze. Within the fitted kinematic domain, the best extracted PINN solution yields a smooth, non-negative momentum-space dipole over the full transverse-momentum range examined. Our results provide a robust and well-behaved input for Color Glass Condensate phenomenology across a broad class of high-energy processes.

# Physics-Informed Global Extraction of the Universal Small-$x$ Dipole Amplitude

## Key claim
The authors extract a universal small-$x$ dipole scattering amplitude $N(r,x_B)$ using a physics-informed neural network (PINN) that simultaneously satisfies the collinearly improved Balitsky-Kovchegov (ciBK) evolution equation and fits deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and exclusive $J/\psi$ photoproduction data, without imposing a pre-defined McLerran-Venugopalan (MV)-type parametrization for the initial condition. The extracted amplitude alleviates the long-standing tension between total and charm cross section channels that has persisted in conventional small-$x$ fits based on rigid parametric ansätze, but the fit is restricted to $x_B < 0.004$ to avoid the transitional region where DGLAP and nonlinear dynamics overlap.

## Method
The dipole amplitude $N(r,Y)$ is represented as a smooth, differentiable neural-network surrogate $\mathcal{N}_\theta(r,Y)$ across the full $(r,x_B)$ domain, with rapidity $Y = \ln(x_0/x_B)$ and starting point $x_0 = 0.03$. The network is trained by minimizing a composite loss function that includes: (1) the integro-differential residual of the ciBK evolution equation as a physics penalty; (2) a negative log-likelihood data term for HERA reduced total cross section $\sigma_r$, reduced charm cross section $\sigma_r^{c\bar{c}}$, and exclusive $J/\psi$ photoproduction cross sections; and (3) physical penalty terms enforcing the black-disk limit at large dipole sizes, color transparency at small dipole sizes, and Fourier-positivity of the momentum-space dipole $S$-matrix. The network parameters $\theta$ and three physical parameters (proton size $R_p$, $J/\psi$ normalization factor $K_{\text{factor}}^{VM}$, and infrared-frozen coupling $\alpha_{fr}$) are optimized simultaneously using the AdamW algorithm with cosine-annealing learning-rate scheduling.

## Key numbers
- Global fit to HERA reduced total cross section $\sigma_r$ yields $\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 1.150$ at all available energies ($\sqrt{s} = 224.9$, $251.5$, $300.3$, and $318$ GeV).
- Reduced charm cross section $\sigma_r^{c\bar{c}}$ at $\sqrt{s} = 318$ GeV yields $\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 1.546$.
- Exclusive $J/\psi$ photoproduction yields $\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 0.474$ across ALICE, H1, ZEUS, and LHCb data.
- Extracted physical parameters (deep-ensemble $1\sigma$): $R_p = 0.809 \pm 0.010$ fm, $\alpha_{fr} = 0.702 \pm 0.070$, $K_{\mathrm{factor}}^{VM} = 0.902 \pm 0.008$.
- Fitting an MV-type parametrization to the PINN-extracted amplitude at $x_B = 0.01$ yields $(\gamma,\ Q_{s0}^2,\ \Lambda,\ e_c) = (1.110,\ 0.074\ \mathrm{GeV}^2,\ 0.089\ \mathrm{GeV},\ 0.300)$, with the fitted $\Lambda = 0.089$ GeV being unrealistically small.
- The momentum-space dipole $S(k_T,x_B)$ remains non-negative over $k_T \in [0, 100]$ GeV for $x_B \le 0.01$.
- Fit restricted to data with $x_B \le 0.004$ and $2 < Q^2 < 45$ GeV$^2$.

## Context
This work extends global analyses of small-$x$ dipole amplitudes that have used Bayesian fits with MV-type initial conditions (Casuga 2025, Hänninen 2022, Ducloué 2019) and addresses the persistent tension between total and charm DIS channels (Albacete 2009, 2010; Lappi 2013; Mäntysaari 2018). The PINN approach provides a non-parametric alternative to rigid analytic ansätze, enabling simultaneous description of inclusive DIS, charm production, and exclusive $J/\psi$ photoproduction while enforcing Fourier-positivity of the momentum-space dipole, a requirement that has been violated in previous fits (Albacete 2009, Giraud 2016, Ducloué 2019, Casuga 2025). The extracted amplitude can serve as input for Color Glass Condensate phenomenology across high-energy processes, and the framework is designed to be extended to impact-parameter dependent amplitudes and NLO impact factors for confrontation with future Electron-Ion Collider data.
