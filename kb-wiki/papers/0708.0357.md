# Analysis of Coulomb breakup experiments of 8B with a dynamical eikonal approximation

> Various measurements of the Coulomb breakup of 8B are analyzed within the dynamical eikonal approximation using a single description of 8B. We obtain a good agreement with experiment for different observables measured between 40 and 80 MeV per nucleon. A simple 7Be-p potential model description of 8B seems sufficient to describe all observables. In particular, the asymmetry in parallel-momentum distributions due to E1-E2 interferences is well reproduced without any scaling. The projectile-target nuclear interactions seem negligible if data are selected at forward angles. On the contrary, like in previous analyzes we observe a significant influence of higher-order effects. The accuracy of astrophysical S factors for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction at stellar energies extracted from breakup measurements therefore seems difficult to evaluate.

# Analysis of Coulomb breakup experiments of 8B with a dynamical eikonal approximation

## Key claim

The dynamical eikonal approximation (DEA), using a simple 7Be-p potential model of 8B without any parameter fitting, reproduces the asymmetry in parallel-momentum distributions from Coulomb breakup experiments at 44 and 81 MeV per nucleon without requiring any scaling of the E2 strength, contrary to previous analyses. However, the accuracy of astrophysical S factors extracted from such breakup measurements remains difficult to evaluate due to significant higher-order effects.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrodinger equation using the dynamical eikonal approximation, which applies the eikonal approximation without the adiabatic approximation, treating the projectile-target interaction as a time-dependent potential along straight-line trajectories. They compute breakup cross sections in both center-of-mass and laboratory frames, applying kinematical relativistic corrections for the projectile velocity and final-state momenta. The calculations use a simple 8B model consisting of a valence proton loosely bound to a 7Be core, with the core-fragment interaction described by a Woods-Saxon potential plus spin-orbit coupling, and projectile-target interactions simulated with optical potentials from the literature.

## Key numbers

- 8B ground state binding energy: 137 keV (one-proton separation energy)
- Central nuclear potential depth adjusted to reproduce the 0p3/2 bound state: V0 = 44.65 MeV
- Woods-Saxon radius: R0 = 2.391 fm; diffuseness: a = 0.52 fm
- Spin-orbit coupling strength: VLS = 19.59 MeV fm^2
- Scattering length from the model: a0 = 5.9 fm (compared to 25 +/- 9 fm for S=1 channel and -7 +/- 3 fm for S=2 channel)
- Experimental resolution for longitudinal momentum distributions: 5 MeV/c
- Shift applied to 44 MeV/nucleon calculations: -5 MeV/c; shift for 81 MeV/nucleon: -9 MeV/c
- At 81 MeV/nucleon, DEA calculations are 10 to 25% too high in magnitude compared to experiment
- First-order perturbation theory overestimates the DEA cross section by 9% at 0.5 MeV relative energy for the 83 MeV/nucleon data

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of 8B Coulomb breakup experiments at RIKEN (52 MeV/nucleon) and MSU (44, 81, and 83 MeV/nucleon) by applying a single theoretical model without parameter fitting, unlike earlier studies that focused on individual experiments. The DEA improves upon first-order perturbation theory (Esbensen and Bertsch, 1996) and previous semiclassical methods by including quantal effects such as interferences and providing differential cross sections. The results confirm the necessity of including both E1 and E2 multipoles and higher-order dynamical effects, as previously noted by Davids and Typel (2003) and Mortimer, Thompson, and Tostevin (2002), but contradict the need for E2 strength scaling suggested in those earlier works. This analysis enables a consistent comparison between different experimental datasets and provides a benchmark for extracting the astrophysical S17 factor for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction.
