# Hydrodynamical description of a hadron-quark first-order phase transition

> Solutions of hydrodynamical equations are presented for the equation of state of the Var der Waals type allowing for the first order phase transition. Attention is focused on description of the hadron-quark phase transition in heavy ion collisions. It is shown that fluctuations dissolve and grow as if the fluid is effectively very viscous. Even in spinodal region germs are growing slowly due to viscosity and critical slowing down. This prevents enhancement of fluctuations in the near-critical region, which is frequently considered as a signal of the critical point in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to expectations that fluctuations near the critical point of a hadron-quark first-order phase transition would be enhanced and serve as a signal, such fluctuational effects can hardly be pronounced in heavy ion collisions because all relevant processes are frozen as the temperature approaches the critical temperature, while the system passes this region during a finite time.

## Method

The authors solve the non-ideal non-relativistic Navier-Stokes, continuity, and heat transport equations numerically in two spatial dimensions and analytically for arbitrary dimensions, using a modified Van der Waals equation of state parameterized near the critical point. They expand the Helmholtz free energy in density and temperature deviations, linearize the hydrodynamical equations, and derive a wave equation for the density perturbation that differs from the standard Landau equation for a non-conserving order parameter. They then solve this equation for germ (droplet/bubble) evolution in various regimes and apply the results to the hadron-quark gluon plasma transition using lattice-derived parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: \(T_{cr} \simeq 162\) MeV; critical density: \(n_{cr}/n_{sat} \simeq 1.3\), from lattice calculations.
- For the hadron-QGP transition with minimal shear viscosity \(\eta_{\rm min} = s/(4\pi) \simeq 60\) MeV/fm\(^2\) and \(\sigma_0 \simeq 40\) MeV/fm\(^2\), the dimensionless parameter \(\beta_{\rm QGP}^{\rm max} \simeq 0.015q\) (with \(q = m/(300\ \text{MeV})\)), corresponding to effectively very large viscosity.
- Time scale for aerosol formation: \(t_{\rm aer}^{\eta} \simeq |\delta{\cal{T}}|^{-1}\) fm; typical germ size in aerosol: \(R^{\eta}_{\rm aer} \simeq 0.24 |\delta{\cal{T}}|^{-1/2}\) fm.
- Critical germ size: \(R_{cr} \simeq 0.1 |\delta{\cal{T}}|^{-1}\) fm; fog size: \(R_{\rm fog} \simeq 0.1 q^{-1/2} |\delta{\cal{T}}|^{-1}\) fm \(\lsim R_{cr}\).
- For \(\beta = 0.015\): \(t_0 \simeq 2 |\delta{\cal{T}}|^{-1}\) fm, \(t_{\rho} \simeq 2.6 R q^{1/2} |\delta{\cal{T}}|^{-1}\), \(t_{\rm dis} \simeq 14 q R_0 (R_0/\text{fm})\).
- Reynolds numbers are \(\lsim 1\), much smaller than the critical value (\(\gsim 1000\)).

## Context

This work extends analytical and fragmentary two-dimensional numerical solutions for first-order phase transitions (e.g., Onuki, and earlier work by PS and MSTV90) to the specific case of the hadron-quark phase transition in heavy ion collisions. It contradicts the expectation from Stephanov that the system may linger near the critical point due to diverging susceptibilities, instead showing that viscosity and critical slowing down prevent fluctuation enhancement. The results enable quantitative estimates of time scales for germ growth, aerosol formation, and fog development, showing that the hadron-quark system behaves as an effectively very viscous fluid, with implications for the search for the critical point in heavy ion experiments.
