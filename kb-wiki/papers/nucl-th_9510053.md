# Nucleon Structure Functions at Moderate Q**2: Relativistic Constituent Quarks and Spectator Mass Spectrum

> We present a model description of the nucleon valence structure function applicable over the entire region of the Bjorken variable x, and above moderate values of Q**2 (> 1 GeV**2). We stress the importance of describing the complete spectrum of intermediate states which are spectator to the deep-inelastic collision. At a scale of 1 GeV**2 the relevant degrees of freedom are constituent quarks and pions. The large-x region is then described in terms of scattering from constituent quarks in the nucleon, while the dressing of constituent quarks by pions plays an important role at intermediate x values. The correct small-x behavior, which is necessary for the proper normalization of the valence distributions, is guaranteed by modeling the asymptotic spectator mass spectrum according to Regge phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a constituent quark model, when augmented with a complete description of the spectator mass spectrum (including low-mass diquark states, intermediate-mass pion-dressed states, and a high-mass Regge continuum), can describe nucleon valence structure functions over the entire Bjorken x region at a starting scale of Q² ≈ 1 GeV², where perturbative QCD evolution becomes justifiable, unlike previous models that required evolution from much lower, non-perturbative scales of 0.1–0.3 GeV².

## Method

The authors decompose the spectator mass spectrum into three regions: (A) low-mass diquark spectators (s ≲ 1 GeV²) described by relativistic quark–diquark–nucleon vertex functions with scalar and pseudovector diquarks; (B) intermediate-mass spectators (1 GeV² ≲ s ≲ 2 GeV²) modeled by dressing constituent quarks with pions; and (C) high-mass spectators (s ≳ 2 GeV²) treated via Regge phenomenology to generate the correct small-x behavior. The constituent quark distributions are calculated in the impulse approximation using parametrized vertex functions with monopole/dipole form factors, and the resulting valence quark distributions are evolved from μ² = 1 GeV² to Q² = 10 GeV² using perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass: m_Q ≈ 450 MeV.
* Scalar diquark mass: m₀ ≈ 1.0 GeV; pseudovector diquark mass: m₁ ≈ 1.2 GeV (with alternative values m₀ ≈ 0.6 GeV, m₁ ≈ 0.8 GeV tested for comparison).
* Cut-off parameters for vertex functions: Λ₀ = 1.0 GeV (scalar vertex), Λ₁ = 1.2 GeV (γ₅γ_α vertex).
* Vertex function exponents: n₀ = 2.0 (scalar), n₁ = 3.5 (pseudovector), giving quark distributions q^{(0,1)}(x) → (1−x)^{3,4} as x → 1.
* Model scale: μ² ≈ 1 GeV², associated with constituent quark size R_Q ≈ 0.2 fm from lattice color correlation length.
* Intermediate-mass region cutoff: s₀ ≈ 2 GeV².
* Regge intercept: α_R ≈ 1/2, giving small-x behavior ∼ x^{−α_R}.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to connect constituent quark models to deep-inelastic structure functions, such as bag and non-relativistic quark models (Author, Year), which failed to reproduce the correct small-x behavior and required evolution from extremely low scales (Q² ∼ 0.1–0.3 GeV²) where perturbative QCD is questionable. The paper builds on the dispersion representation approach of Landshoff, Polkinghorne, and Short (1971) and the work of Close and Thomas (1988) on SU(4) symmetry breaking, and uses Regge phenomenology (Author, Year) to model the high-mass spectator continuum. The approach enables a consistent description of valence quark distributions from a scale where perturbative QCD evolution is reliable, and provides a framework for connecting low-energy constituent quark models to high-Q² DIS data.
