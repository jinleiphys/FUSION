# On the Generalization of Radamacher's Proof to $\vartheta_1(z,τ)$

> In this paper, we generalize Rademacher's proof of the transformation law of the eta function to the Jacobi theta function using Residue calculus.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Rademacher's residue-calculus proof of the transformation law of the Dedekind eta function to the Jacobi theta function \(\vartheta_1(z,\tau)\), under the condition that the proof holds for \(v\) real and positive with \(v > |\Im(z)| > 0\) before extending by analytic continuation.

## Method

The authors construct a special function \(F_N(x)\) (equation 15) whose poles include a third-order pole at \(x=0\) and simple poles at \(x = in/N\) and \(x = -n/(Nv)\). They compute the residues at all poles, sum them, and apply the residue theorem over a parallelogram contour \(C\). Taking the limit \(m \to \infty\) and using Arzela's bounded convergence theorem, they show the contour integral equals \(-\log(v)\), which together with the residue sums yields the desired transformation law (equation 6) for \(\vartheta_1(z,\tau)\) under any element \(A \in \mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})\) with \(c>0\).

## Key numbers

- The residue at \(x=0\) of the function \(\frac{1}{4ix}\coth(\pi Nx)\cot(\pi Nvx)\) is \(\frac{i}{12}\left(v-\frac{1}{v}\right)\).
- The residue at \(x=0\) of the sub-function \(A_N(x)\) summed over \(\mu=1\) to \(k-1\) yields \(\left(-\frac{1}{12}+\frac{1}{12k}\right)vi + s(h,k) + \left(-\frac{1}{12}+\frac{1}{12k}\right)\frac{1}{vi}\).
- The total residue of \(F_N(x)\) at \(x=0\) is \(\frac{z^2k}{vi} - \frac{z}{vi} + z + \frac{i}{4k}\left(v-\frac{1}{v}\right) + 3s(h,k)\).
- The contour integral \(\lim_{m\to\infty} \int_C F_N(x)\,dx\) evaluates to \(-\log(v)\).

## Context

This work extends Rademacher's proof (Rademacher, 1973) of the eta-function transformation law to the Jacobi theta function \(\vartheta_1(z,\tau)\), following earlier generalizations by Iseki and Gordon. It provides an alternative to proofs using the theory of quadratic forms, such as those by Richter (2010) and Yurii (2006), and aims to enable further generalizations to other Jacobi forms, as connections between \(\vartheta_1\) and Jacobi forms of weight 10 and -2 are noted from Dabholkar, Murthy, and Zagier (2012).
