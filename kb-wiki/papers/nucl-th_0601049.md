# Elliptic Flow Analysis at RHIC with the Lee-Yang Zeroes Method in a Relativistic Transport Approach

> The Lee-Yang zeroes method is applied to study elliptic flow ($v_2$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200A$~GeV, with the UrQMD model. In this transport approach, the true event plane is known and both the nonflow effects and event-by-event $v_2$ fluctuations exist. Although the low resolutions prohibit the application of the method for most central and peripheral collisions, the integral and differential elliptic flow from the Lee-Yang zeroes method agrees with the exact $v_2$ values very well for semi-central collisions.

# Elliptic Flow Analysis at RHIC with the Lee-Yang Zeroes Method in a Relativistic Transport Approach

## Key claim

The Lee-Yang zeroes method reproduces the exact elliptic flow values with better than 2% accuracy for semi-central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy, but only when the resolution parameter chi is above about 0.5; the method is inapplicable for most central (0-5%) and peripheral (above 40%) collisions where chi is too small.

## Method

The Lee-Yang zeroes method was applied to UrQMD model events for Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV, using the generating function G^theta(ir) to find the first zero r0^theta and extract integral v2 = j01/r0^theta, where j01 = 2.40483. The integral v2 was averaged over 5 equally spaced values of theta from 0 to 4pi/5, and differential v2 as a function of pT and pseudorapidity was also extracted. The true reaction plane angle was known by construction in the UrQMD model, allowing direct comparison of the extracted v2 to the exact v2 = <cos2(phi - Phi_R)>.

## Key numbers

- The resolution parameter chi is about 1 in the semi-central bin (20-30%), while it is close to 0.5 in the most central (0-5%) and peripheral bins (above 40%)
- The Lee-Yang zeroes method reproduces the exact v2 in the 20-30% centrality bin with better than 2% accuracy
- More than 1.3 x 10^6 minimum bias events were used in the integral v2 analysis
- More than 6 x 10^5 semi-central events (impact parameters 6.7 to 8.3 fm, corresponding to about 20% to 30% of total cross section) were used for differential flow analysis
- The first root of the Bessel function J0(x) used is j01 = 2.40483

## Context

This work extends the testing of the Lee-Yang zeroes method, previously applied at SIS/GSI energies by Bastid (2005), to RHIC energies using the UrQMD model. It builds on prior work by Zhu (2005) showing that 4- and 6-particle cumulants give true v2 values with better than 5% accuracy for semi-central collisions in the same model. The results demonstrate that the Lee-Yang zeroes method is not sensitive to nonflow correlations or v2 fluctuations for semi-central bins, though the method fails for central and peripheral bins where the resolution parameter is too low. The authors note that the UrQMD model underpredicts RHIC v2 data by about 40%, suggesting the method will work over a broader range of centrality bins in actual experiments at RHIC or LHC.
