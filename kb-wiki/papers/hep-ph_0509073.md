# The phase diagram of neutral quark matter: The effect of neutrino trapping

> We study the effect of neutrino trapping on the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter within the framework of a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. In the analysis, dynamically generated quark masses are taken into account self-consistently. The phase diagrams in the plane of temperature and quark chemical potential, as well as in the plane of temperature and lepton-number chemical potential are presented. We show that neutrino trapping favors two-flavor color superconductivity and disfavors the color-flavor-locked phase at intermediate densities of matter. At the same time, the location of the critical line separating the two-flavor color-superconducting phase and the normal phase of quark matter is little affected by the presence of neutrinos. The implications of these results for the evolution of protoneutron stars are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Neutrino trapping favors two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) and disfavors the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase at intermediate densities of quark matter, but the critical line separating the 2SC phase from the normal quark phase is little affected by the presence of neutrinos.

## Method

The authors used a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with dynamically generated quark masses treated self-consistently, including scalar diquark condensates and quark-antiquark condensates in the mean-field (Hartree) approximation. They imposed local charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, solving the gap equations and neutrality conditions simultaneously for eight possible phases, then comparing pressures to determine the ground state. The lepton sector was treated as an ideal gas of electrons, muons, and their corresponding neutrinos, with the muon lepton-number chemical potential set to zero.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters include a cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, scalar coupling G_S * Lambda^2 = 1.835, 't Hooft coupling K * Lambda^5 = 12.36, strange quark current mass m_s = 140.7 MeV, and up/down quark current masses m_{u,d} = 5.5 MeV
- The diquark coupling was set to G_D = (3/4) G_S, called "intermediate coupling"
- The quark chemical potential was restricted to mu less than or approximately 500 MeV and the neutrino chemical potential to mu_{nu_e} less than or approximately 400 MeV
- At mu_{L_e} = 0 and T = 0, the ratio of down to up quark chemical potentials in a toy model is mu_d/mu_u approximately 1.256, corresponding to n_u/n_d approximately 0.504 and n_e/n_d approximately 0.003
- At mu_{L_e} = mu, the up and down Fermi momenta become equal (mu_d = mu_u)
- The lepton fraction Y_{L_e} in protoneutron stars is expected to be of order 0.4 right after core collapse, corresponding to mu_d/mu_u approximately 1.1
- The critical lepton-number chemical potential for CFL phase stability is mu_{L_e}^{(cr)} approximately Delta_2 - M_s^2/(2 mu)
- For typical values mu = 500 MeV and mu_{L_e} less than or approximately Delta approximately 50-100 MeV, the lepton fraction in the CFL phase satisfies Y_{L_e} less than or approximately 10^{-4} to 10^{-3}
- At mu = 400 MeV, the low-temperature normal phase is replaced by the (g)2SC phase at mu_{L_e} approximately 110 MeV
- The electric charge chemical potential mu_Q reaches its minimum around mu approximately 440 MeV at T = 0

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the phase diagram of neutral quark matter (Ruester et al., 2005) by including the effect of a nonzero lepton-number chemical potential, which is relevant for protoneutron stars where neutrinos are trapped during the first few seconds of stellar evolution. It builds on earlier work by Steiner, Reddy, and Prakash (2002) who first found that neutrino trapping favors the 2SC phase and disfavors the CFL phase, but extends that analysis by performing a more systematic survey including gapless phases and self-consistent dynamical quark masses. The results enable more realistic modeling of the early evolution of protoneutron stars, where the lepton fraction can reach up to about 0.4 before deleptonization occurs over approximately a dozen seconds.
