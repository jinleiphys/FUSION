# Jets and produced particles in pp collisions from SPS to RHIC energies for nuclear applications

> Higher-order pQCD corrections play an important role in the reproduction of data at high transverse momenta in the energy range 20 GeV $ \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 200$ GeV. Recent calculations of photon and pion production in $pp$ collisions yield detailed information on the next-to-leading order contributions. However, the application of these results in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions is not straightforward. The study of nuclear effects requires a simplified understanding of the output of these computations. Here we summarize our analysis of recent calculations, aimed at handling the NLO results by introducing process and energy-dependent $K$ factors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that higher-order pQCD corrections in pp collisions can be effectively captured by a process- and energy-dependent K factor, but this simplification is only valid within a specific definition (the ratio of the full NLO cross section to the Born cross section) and requires careful choice of scales and jet cone parameters.

## Method

The authors used the Ellis-Kunszt-Soper (EKS) public FORTRAN code to compute Born and NLO jet cross sections, employing MRST (central gluon) parton distribution functions at NLO level with factorization/renormalization scale Q = p_T/2 and collinear singularity scale Q_collinear = p_T. They then extracted K factors for jets at two cone sizes (R=0.7 and R=1.0) and extended the analysis to hadron production by convolving with NLO fragmentation functions from BKK, and to photon production using the code of Aurenche et al.

## Key numbers

- Energy range studied: 20 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 200 GeV
- At √s = 200 GeV, the K factor varies less than 5% from p_T = 3 GeV to p_T = 10 GeV
- At √s = 20 GeV, the K factor varies on the order of 20% from p_T = 2.5 GeV to p_T = 4 GeV
- At RHIC energies (√s = 140-200 GeV) with Q = p_T/2 and R = 0.7, the K factor is approximately constant at K ≈ 1.2
- The hadron-level K factor is larger than the jet-level counterpart by 10-20%
- Parameterization for R=0.7: K_jet(s,p_T) = 1. + 65./(√s+160.) + 2./(√s-6.) * p_T
- Parameterization for R=1.0: K_jet(s,p_T) = 1.6 + 20./√s - 24./(√s-10.)^2 * p_T + 6./(√s-10.)^2 * p_T^2

## Context

This work extends earlier pQCD calculations by the Ellis-Kunszt-Soper group (1992) and the Aversa-Chiappetta-Greco-Guillet group, as well as recent NLO studies by Aurenche et al. (1999) and Kidonakis (1999). The parameterized K factors are intended for use in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, where full NLO calculations are too complex; the authors plan to apply them in the energy range 20-60 GeV and transverse momenta 3-7 GeV as an improvement on their earlier work (Levai et al., 2000).
