# Non-equilibrium Phase Transitions in Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Highly excited nuclear matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions possibly reaches the phase of quark deconfinement. It quickly cools down and hadronises. We explain that the process of hadronisation may likely be connected with disintegration into fragments. Observable signals of such a scenario are proposed.

# Non-equilibrium Phase Transitions in Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
Hadronisation of deconfined quark-gluon matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions may proceed via spinodal fragmentation into droplets rather than smooth phase conversion, provided the expansion rate exceeds the bubble nucleation rate. This scenario may apply even at RHIC and LHC energies if a sharp peak in bulk viscosity at the crossover transition causes the fireball to tear apart.

## Method
The authors apply the van der Waals equation of state to illustrate spinodal fragmentation of a rapidly expanding system that supercools through a first-order phase transition. They estimate fragment size from the balance between dissipated energy and kinetic energy using a one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion model, and simulate particle emission from droplets with a Monte Carlo generator to compute proton-proton correlation functions.

## Key numbers
- Expansion time scale of the deconfined fireball is of order 10 fm/c, or 10^{-22} s.
- Characteristic fragment size in one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion is given by L^2 = 24 ζ_c τ_c / ε_c, where τ_c is the time when crossover is reached, ε_c the energy density at that point, and ζ_c a scaling factor of bulk viscosity.
- Proton-proton correlation functions were simulated for events with 900 hadrons, with droplets distributed according to a Gaussian with rapidity width of 1.3.

## Context
This work extends the concept of spinodal fragmentation from multifragmentation in nuclear collisions at energies around 100 MeV per nucleon (Chomaz, 2004; multifragmentation literature) to the ultrarelativistic regime. It builds on model studies with the linear sigma model coupled to quarks (Scavenius, 2001) that indicate realistic expansion rates exceed bubble nucleation rates. The proposed fragmentation scenario could reconcile hydrodynamic simulations with femtoscopy data, which currently show sharp disagreement (Giorgio, 2006), and is consistent with PHOBOS observations of multiplicity fluctuations and two-particle correlations (PHOBOS Collaboration, 2005, 2006) as well as non-statistical mean p_t fluctuations at RHIC (Brahms Collaboration, 2005).
