# Finite-density QCD, $\mathcal{PT}$ symmetry, and exotic phases

> We study the phase structure of effective models of finite-density QCD using analytic and lattice simulation techniques developed for the study of non-Hermitian and $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric QFTs. Finite-density QCD is symmetric under the combined operation of the charge and complex conjugation operators $\mathcal{CK}$, which falls into the class of so-called generalized $\mathcal{PT}$ symmetries. We show that $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric quantum field theories can support patterned ground-state field configurations in the vicinity of a critical endpoint. We apply our methods to a lattice heavy quark model at nonzero chemical potential that displays patterning behavior for a range of parameters. We derive a simple approximate criterion for the formation of these patterns, which can be used with lattice results.

## Key claim

The paper shows that finite-density QCD, due to its invariance under the combined charge and complex conjugation operator (a generalized PT symmetry), can support patterned ground-state field configurations near a critical endpoint; this behavior is demonstrated in a lattice heavy quark model and a simple approximate criterion for pattern formation is derived that can be used with lattice results.

## Method

The authors perform a stability analysis of the mass matrix of a generic PT-symmetric scalar field theory, classifying the ground state as normal, unstable, PT-broken (sinusoidally-modulated), or patterned (inhomogeneous) based on the eigenvalues. They apply this analytic method to a two-component PT-symmetric phi^4 model and confirm the phase structure with lattice simulations using a Fourier-transform technique that recasts the action into a real, simulatable form. They then apply the same mass-matrix analysis to a heavy quark effective model of finite-density QCD with a Polyakov loop parametrized as P = e^{chi + i phi}.

## Key numbers

- For the PT-symmetric phi^4 model, the mass matrix eigenvalues become complex for sufficiently large couplings g; the phase diagram is computed at fixed m_chi^2 = 0.5, lambda = 0.1, and v = 3.
- Lattice simulations of the phi^4 model were performed on a 64^2 lattice after a hot start followed by 20,000 sweeps.
- For the heavy quark model, the action includes a coefficient z = e^{beta(mu - m)} and the Polyakov loop interactions are split into attractive and repulsive potentials V = V_A + V_R.
- The stability criterion against pattern formation is given by 1 + beta * chi_Q * tilde{V}_{qq}(k) > 0, where chi_Q is the quark number susceptibility and tilde{V}_{qq} is the quark-quark potential.

## Context

This work extends the application of PT-symmetric quantum field theory techniques, developed by Bender (1998) and others, to finite-density QCD, which was previously shown to be invariant under the CK operator by Meisinger (2012) and Schindler (2021). The patterned phases found here are related to previously studied inhomogeneous behavior in QCD, including LOFF phases in color superconductors (Alford, 2007), chiral spirals (Kojo, 2009), and sinusoidally-modulated Polyakov loop correlators predicted by Patel (2011, 2012) and observed in Polyakov-Nambu-Jona Lasinio models (Nishimura, 2016) and Z(3) spin models (Akerlund, 2016). The results enable lattice calculations of the stability criterion for pattern formation using quantities computable on the lattice, and suggest that similar inhomogeneous phases may appear around the critical endpoint of finite-density QCD.
