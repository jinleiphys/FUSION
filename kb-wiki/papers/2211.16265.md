# Quark-Hadron Transition and Entanglement

> The dual holographic description has enjoyed many successes in explaining fundamental properties of the early stages of relativistic heavy ion collisions up to the formation of a minimal-viscosity quark-gluon fluid. However, there have been few attempts to extend its application beyond this stage. Here we explore the prospects for such an extension beyond the time of hadronization. Our discussion makes use of recent insights into the duality of entanglement properties of field theory states in the edge of Anti-de Sitter space and non-trivial topologies of horizons in the bulk, often referred to as ER = EPR duality. We discuss this topic from the point of view of heavy-ion phenomenology, review several relevant concepts, and map out a path toward combining them into a comprehensive, at least semiquantitative description of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We outline possible next steps in this direction.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions never becomes a truly thermal state with large von Neumann entropy, but instead remains a highly entangled pure quantum state throughout its evolution, with apparent thermal behavior arising from the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis (ETH) and the fact that detectors act as an incoherent environment only at the final measurement stage.

## Method

The paper reviews and synthesizes concepts from AdS/CFT duality, entanglement entropy, the Page curve, and the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis to propose a holographic framework for describing the complete heavy ion collision, including the hadronization transition. The authors divide the collision into four stages (hydrodynamization, QGP expansion with surface hadronization, bulk hadronization, and expansion of entangled hadrons) and propose distinct holographic dual descriptions for each stage, including a Hawking-Page type transition for bulk hadronization and an analogy to Hawking radiation for surface hadronization.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_CF = 156.6 ± 1.7 MeV (Andronic 2021)
- QCD crossover transition temperature from lattice QCD: T_c = 158 ± 0.6 MeV (Borsanyi 2020) or T_c = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV (HotQCD 2018)
- Intrinsic width of QCD crossover transition: 15 ± 1 MeV (Borsanyi 2020)
- Single-particle entropy per unit rapidity at top RHIC energy: dS_sp/dy ≈ 5,000 (Pal 2003, Muller 2005)
- Single-particle entropy per unit rapidity at top LHC energy: dS_sp/dy ≈ 11,500 (Hanus 2019)
- Hypertriton binding energy: 0.4 MeV, much smaller than T_CF
- Transverse fluctuation scale: inverse saturation scale 1/Q_s ∼ 0.2 fm
- Diffusion coefficient estimate in QCD: D ∼ (πT)^{-1}
- With R_T ≈ 5 fm and T ≈ 300 MeV: Thouless time t_Th ∼ 120 fm/c, much longer than QGP lifetime
- Speed of information transport at strong coupling in d=4 holographic models: v_E ≈ 0.62c (Liu 2013)
- Estimated fraction of QGP that decays via surface hadronization: roughly 20%

## Context

This work extends the successful application of holographic methods to early stages of heavy ion collisions, including rapid thermalization (Lin 2006, Balasubramanian 2010, Chesler 2010), hydrodynamic flow (Janik 2005, Heller 2011), and the small specific shear viscosity (Kovtun 2004), to the later hadronization stage, which has rarely been explored holographically. The authors build on recent resolutions of the black hole information paradox (Maldacena 2013, Almheiri 2019, Penington 2019) and the ER=EPR duality to propose that the hadronization process can be understood analogously to black hole evaporation, with the Page curve (Page 1993) describing the entanglement entropy of emitted hadrons. The paper enables a path toward a unitary, fully quantum description of heavy ion collisions that reconciles the apparent thermal behavior of final-state hadrons with the unitarity of the QCD S-matrix, and suggests that the observed deviation of hypertriton yields in p-Pb collisions from the thermal model (ALICE 2021) may be explained by the failure of ETH to fully establish in small collision systems.
