# Debye screening mass near deconfinement from holography

> In this paper the smallest thermal screening mass associated with the correlator of the $CT$-odd operator, $\sim {\rm Tr}F_{μν}\tilde{F}^{μν}$, is determined in strongly coupled non-Abelian gauge plasmas which are holographically dual to non-conformal, bottom-up Einstein+scalar gravity theories. These holographic models are constructed to describe the thermodynamical properties of $SU(N_c)$ plasmas near deconfinement at large $N_c$ and we identify this thermal mass with the Debye screening mass $m_D$. In this class of non-conformal models with a first order deconfinement transition at $T_c$, $m_D/T$ displays the same behavior found for the expectation value of the Polyakov loop (which we also compute) jumping from zero below $T_c$ to a nonzero value just above the transition. In the case of a crossover phase transition, $m_D/T$ has a minimum similar to that found for the speed of sound squared $c_s^2$. This holographic framework is also used to evaluate $m_D$ as a function of $η/s$ in a strongly coupled conformal gauge plasma dual to Gauss-Bonnet gravity. In this case, $m_D/T$ decreases with increasing $η/s$ in accordance with extrapolations from weak coupling calculations.

## Key claim

The smallest thermal screening mass associated with the CT-odd operator TrFμνF̃μν is identified with the Debye screening mass mD in strongly coupled non-Abelian gauge plasmas, but this identification holds only within the holographic framework where the bulk axion spectrum is gapped, positive, and discrete in the deconfined phase, and the behavior of mD/T depends on the order of the deconfinement phase transition.

## Method

The authors use the gauge/gravity duality to compute the Debye screening mass from the lowest lying mass in the spectrum of axion fluctuations in the bulk of 5-dimensional Einstein+scalar gravity theories. They consider three classes of bottom-up holographic models: Model A (an analytical Improved Holographic QCD model with a first order transition), Model B (Einstein+scalar models with potentials fitted to lattice QCD thermodynamics), and a conformal Gauss-Bonnet gravity model to study the dependence of mD on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s.

## Key numbers

* In Model A with a first order deconfinement transition at Tc, mD/T jumps from zero below Tc to a nonzero value just above the transition, following the behavior of the Polyakov loop expectation value.
* In the crossover case (Model B), mD/T displays a minimum similar to that found for the speed of sound squared cs2 near the transition.
* For the conformal Gauss-Bonnet gravity plasma, mD/T decreases with increasing η/s, consistent with extrapolations from weak coupling calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic calculations of the Debye screening mass in conformal plasmas (Bak, Karch, and Yaffe 2007) and in N=2* supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma (Hoyos, Paik, and Yaffe 2011) to non-conformal strongly coupled plasmas with properties similar to QCD. The results enable comparisons with lattice QCD calculations of screening masses (Kajantie et al. 1997; Datta and Gupta 2000; Laine and Philipsen 1999) and provide a holographic framework for understanding how the Debye mass behaves near the deconfinement transition, which is relevant for heavy quarkonium melting in heavy ion collisions.
