# The $Λ(1405)$ in resummed chiral effective field theory

> We study the unitarized meson-baryon scattering amplitude at leading order in the strangeness $S=-1$ sector using time-ordered perturbation theory for a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of chiral effective field theory. By solving the coupled-channel integral equations with the full off-shell dependence of the effective potential and applying subtractive renormalization, we analyze the renormalized scattering amplitudes and obtain the two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. We also point out the necessity of including higher-order terms.

## Key claim

Using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of chiral effective field theory with time-ordered perturbation theory and subtractive renormalization, the authors obtain the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) resonance at leading order with no free parameters, but note that higher-order terms are necessary to fully describe the data, particularly the isoscalar K̅N scattering length.

## Method

The authors solve coupled-channel integral equations for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=-1 sector at leading order, using time-ordered perturbation theory applied to a Lorentz-invariant chiral Lagrangian that explicitly includes low-lying vector mesons. The effective potential incorporates vector-meson exchange (replacing the Weinberg-Tomozawa contact term), Born terms, and crossed-Born terms, with the full off-shell dependence retained. Subtractive renormalization is applied to obtain finite on-shell T-matrices that are independent of the momentum cutoff, and the amplitudes are analytically continued to find resonance poles in the complex s-plane.

## Key numbers

- Lower pole of Λ(1405) at 1337.7 - i 79.1 MeV (using F₀ = F_π = 92.07 MeV)
- Higher pole of Λ(1405) at 1430.9 - i 8.0 MeV (using F₀ = F_π = 92.07 MeV)
- Lower pole at 1348.2 - i 120.2 MeV and higher pole at 1436.3 - i 0.7 MeV (using F₀ = 103.4 MeV, the SU(3)-average value)
- Isoscalar K̅N scattering length a₀ = -2.50 + i 1.37 fm
- Isovector K̅N scattering length a₁ = 0.33 + i 0.72 fm
- Coupling strengths: lower pole couples predominantly to πΣ (|g| = 2.64) while higher pole couples strongly to K̅N (|g| = 2.31)

## Context

This work extends the renormalizable approach previously applied to pion-nucleon scattering by Ren et al. (2020) to the three-flavor sector with strangeness S=-1. It addresses the ongoing debate about whether the Λ(1405) has one or two poles, which was first reported by Oller (2000) and has been studied extensively in chiral unitary approaches by groups such as Ikeda (2011, 2012), Guo (2012), and Mai (2014). The key advance is that the framework yields cutoff-independent results without the on-shell factorization approximation used in traditional chiral unitary models, and the leading-order results are pure predictions with no free parameters. The next step is to include next-to-leading order corrections to improve the description of the isoscalar scattering length and to describe additional experimental data from JLab, SIDDHARTA, and kaonic nuclear bound state experiments.
