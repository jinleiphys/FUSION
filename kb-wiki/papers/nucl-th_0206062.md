# Heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies in a quark-gluon exchange framework

> Heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies can be studied in the context of the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) model. One of the main features in this model is the nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross section in the collisional term. Quark interchange plays a role in the NN interaction and its effect can be observed in the cross section. We explore the possibility that quark interchange effects can appear in observables at energies lower than RHIC.

## Key claim
The paper explores whether quark interchange effects, which are known to influence the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction at medium energies, can appear in heavy-ion collision observables at intermediate energies (lower than RHIC) when the NN cross section in the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) model is replaced by one derived from the Fock-Tani formalism. The hedge is that the effect is only observable if the nucleon bag radius parameter is chosen in a specific range (0.3 to 0.4 fm) that differs from the range used to fit free NN scattering.

## Method
The authors modify the VUU transport equation by replacing the standard NN cross section in the collisional term with a cross section derived from the Fock-Tani formalism, which incorporates quark interchange via a one-gluon exchange potential (OGEP). The VUU equation is solved using Monte Carlo simulations with an ensemble of events, and the resulting transverse momentum distributions as a function of rapidity are computed for Nb+Nb collisions at a given impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- The Fock-Tani cross section depends on the nucleon root-mean-square radius parameter \(a\); in heavy-ion collisions, values of \(a\) from 0.3 to 0.4 fm produce results that approach the standard VUU simulations.
- For free NN scattering with quark interchange, the range of \(a\) needed to fit lower partial waves is 0.45 to 0.6 fm.
- Larger bag radii of 0.7 to 0.8 fm produce results that are far off the original VUU calculation.

## Context
This work extends the VUU model (Stocker, 1980s) by incorporating a microscopic quark-interchange mechanism (the Fock-Tani formalism) into the NN cross section, rather than using a purely phenomenological cross section. The approach contrasts with earlier studies (da Silva and Hadjimichef, 2000; Hadjimichef, 1997) that applied quark interchange to free NN scattering; here the authors find that the effective nucleon radius in the nuclear medium must be smaller (0.3–0.4 fm) than in free space (0.45–0.6 fm). The results suggest that quark interchange effects could be probed in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, potentially enabling a connection between nuclear equation-of-state studies and quark-level dynamics.
