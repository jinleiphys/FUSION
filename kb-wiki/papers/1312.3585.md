# Scattering and reflection positivity in relativistic Euclidean quantum mechanics

> In this paper I discuss a formulation of relativistic few-particle scattering theory where the dynamical input is a collection of reflection-positive Euclidean covariant Green functions. This formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics has the advantage that all calculations can be performed using Euclidean variables. The construction of the Hilbert space inner product requires that the Green functions are reflection positive, which is a non-trivial constraint on the dynamics. In this paper I discuss the structure of reflection positive Green functions. I show that scattering wave operators exist when the dynamics is given by a Green function with the structure discussed in this work. Techniques for computing scattering observables in this Euclidean framework are discussed.

## Key claim

This paper exhibits a specific class of reflection-positive Euclidean invariant four-point functions that can serve as dynamical input for a Poincaré-invariant quantum theory, and demonstrates that scattering wave operators exist for this class without requiring analytic continuation to Minkowski space. The hedge is that the construction does not provide a general representation for all reflection-positive four-point functions, but rather a representative class motivated by a one-dimensional structure theorem (Widder's theorem).

## Method

The author constructs reflection-positive connected four-point Euclidean Green functions by generalizing Widder's one-dimensional representation theorem for reflection-positive kernels to a four-dimensional Euclidean covariant form, expressing the connected four-point function as an integral over three momenta and three mass parameters with a kernel that must be positive and symmetric under exchange of initial and final variables. The existence of scattering wave operators is then demonstrated by applying the Cook condition to these Green functions, showing that the connected part of the four-point function plays the role of the interaction and yields a time decay of \(t^{-3/2}\) in the relevant integrals, sufficient for strong convergence of the wave operator limits.

## Key numbers

- The connected four-point Green function is expressed as an integral over three momenta \(\p_1, \p_2, \p_3\) and three mass parameters \(m_a, m_c, m_b\) with propagator denominators \((\p_1^2 + m_a^2)(\p_2^2 + m_c^2)(\p_3^2 + m_b^2)\).
- The large-time falloff of the Cook condition integrand is \(t^{-3/2}\), identical to the behavior found in non-relativistic scattering theory.
- The previous paper (Kopp and Polyzou, 2011) demonstrated convergence to exact transition matrix elements for energies between 50 MeV and 2 GeV using a solvable model.

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics developed by Kopp and Polyzou (2011), which introduced methods to compute sharp-momentum transition operators using matrix elements of \(e^{-\beta H}\) in normalizable states without analytic continuation. The paper addresses two open questions from that earlier work: whether non-trivial reflection-positive multipoint Green functions exist, and whether such Green functions lead to non-trivial scattering theory. The construction is motivated by Widder's theorem (Widder, 1941) on the general structure of reflection-positive kernels in one dimension, and the scattering analysis uses the Cook condition (Cook, 1957) and techniques from Ruelle (1962). The results enable scattering calculations in Euclidean space for relativistic quantum models, with potential applications to QCD and systems with arbitrary spin states.
