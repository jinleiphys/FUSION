# Phenomenological bound on the viscosity of the hadron resonance gas

> We have explored some phenomenological issues during calculations of transport coefficients for hadronic matter, produced in the experiments of heavy ion collisions. Here, we have used an ideal hadron resonance gas model to demonstrate the issues. On the basis of dissipation mechanism, the hadronic zoo is classified into resonance and non-resonance members, who participate in dissipation via strong decay and scattering channels respectively. Imposing our phenomenological restriction, we are able to provide a rough upper and lower bound estimations of transport coefficients. Interestingly, we find that our proposed lower limit estimation for shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is little larger than its quantum lower bound. By taking a simple example, we have demonstrated how our proposed restriction help to tune any estimation of transport coefficients within its numerical band, proposed by us.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when calculating transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity) for the hadronic matter produced in heavy-ion collisions, one must impose a "finite size dissipation" restriction: only hadrons whose mean life time or relaxation length is smaller than the fireball's lifetime (approximately 10 fm) should be included. This restriction yields a narrower numerical band for the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio than previously reported, with a lower bound that is "little larger" than the quantum lower bound of 1/(4π).

## Method

The authors use an ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to compute thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, entropy density, speed of sound) and transport coefficients (shear viscosity η, bulk viscosity ζ) within the relaxation time approximation (RTA). Hadrons are classified into "non-resonance" members (pions, kaons, nucleons) whose dissipation comes from strong scattering, and "resonance" members (all other hadrons) whose dissipation comes from strong decays. The finite size restriction is implemented by (i) including only resonances with mean life times less than 10 fm, and (ii) imposing a temperature-dependent upper momentum cutoff on pions and kaons such that their relaxation lengths do not exceed 10 fm. The transport coefficients are then folded with hadronic spectral functions to account for finite widths.

## Key numbers

*   The fireball lifetime is taken as approximately **10 fm**; this is used as the upper limit for relaxation times of non-resonance particles and as the cutoff for resonance mean life times.
*   At T = 0.160 GeV, the proposed band for the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is **0.3 < η/s < 0.85**.
*   At T = 0.100 GeV, the proposed band for η/s is **0.007 < η/s < 0.4**.
*   The quantum lower bound (KSS bound) for η/s is **1/(4π) ≈ 0.08**.
*   The isospin-averaged cross sections used for scattering calculations are: σ_ππ = **17.3 mb**, σ_πN = **16.1 mb**, σ_NN = **53.4 b**, σ_KN = **4.7 mb**, σ_Kπ = **12.2 mb**.

## Context

This work extends the long list of microscopic calculations of η/s for hadronic matter (citing Prakash, Itakura, Dobado, Nicola, Weise1, Ghosh_piN, Gorenstein, HM, Hostler, Bass, Muronga, Plumari, Pal, and others) by pointing out that many prior estimates fall outside the proposed band because they did not account for finite size dissipation. The paper specifically notes that at T = 0.160 GeV, values from Bass and Pal (≈1), HM (≈0.2), and Ghosh_piN (≈0.13) lie outside the band, while values from Itakura (≈0.8), Gorenstein (≈0.45), Weise1 (≈0.32), and Nicola and Hostler (≈0.3) lie within it. The work enables future calculations to tune their transport coefficient estimates within a narrower numerical band by properly imposing the finite size dissipation restriction.
