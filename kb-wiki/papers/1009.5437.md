# Energy dependence of pbar/p ratio in p+p collisions

> We have compiled the experimentally measured pbar/p ratio at midrapidity in p+p collisions from \sqrt{s} = 23 to 7000 GeV and compared it to various mechanisms of baryon production as implemented in PYTHIA, PHOJET and HIJING/B-Bbar models. For the models studied with default settings, PHOJET has the best agreement with the measurements, PYTHIA gives a higher value for \sqrt{s} < 200 GeV and the ratios from HIJING/B-Bbar are consistently lower for all the \sqrt{s} studied. Comparison of the data to different mechanisms of baryon production as implemented in PYTHIA shows that through a suitable tuning of the suppression of diquark-antidiquark pair production in the color field relative to quark-antiquark production and allowing the diquarks to split according to the popcorn scheme gives a fairly reasonable description of the measured pbar/p ratio for \sqrt{s} < 200 GeV. Comparison of the beam energy dependence of the pbar/p ratio in p+p and nucleus-nucleus (A+A) collisions at midrapidity shows that the baryon production is significantly more for A+A collisions relative to p+p collisions for \sqrt{s} < 200 GeV. We also carry out a phenomenological fit to the y_beam dependence of the pbar/p ratio.

## Key claim

The paper compiles experimental data on the $\bar{p}/p$ ratio at midrapidity in $p$+$p$ collisions from $\sqrt{s}$ = 23 to 7000 GeV and finds that, among the models studied with default settings, PHOJET gives the best agreement with measurements, while PYTHIA overestimates the ratio for $\sqrt{s} < 200$ GeV and HIJING/B-$\bar{\mathrm B}$ consistently underestimates it across all energies; a tuned version of PYTHIA using diquark-antidiquark suppression and the popcorn scheme can describe the data for $\sqrt{s} < 200$ GeV.

## Method

The authors compiled published experimental $\bar{p}/p$ ratios at midrapidity from ISR (23, 31, 45, 53, 63 GeV), STAR at RHIC (200 GeV), and ALICE at LHC (900 GeV and 7 TeV). They compared these data to the default settings of the event generators PYTHIA (Ver. 6.4), PHOJET (Ver. 1.12), and HIJING/B-$\bar{\mathrm B}$ (Ver. 1.34), and also to various baryon production schemes within PYTHIA by varying the MSTJ(12) parameter and the diquark suppression factor. A phenomenological fit to the asymmetry $(N_p - N_{\bar{p}})/(N_p + N_{\bar{p}})$ as a function of $\sqrt{s}$ was performed, and a Regge-model inspired fit to the $\bar{p}/p$ ratio as a function of $y_{\mathrm{beam}}$ was carried out for both $p$+$p$ and A+A collisions.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar{p}/p$ ratio at midrapidity in $p$+$p$ collisions increases from approximately 0.46 at $\sqrt{s}$ = 23 GeV to 0.991 $\pm$ 0.005(stat.) $\pm$ 0.014(syst.) at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV.
- The asymmetry $(N_p - N_{\bar{p}})/(N_p + N_{\bar{p}})$ decreases from about 46% at $\sqrt{s}$ = 23 GeV to 0.5% at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV.
- The best fit to the asymmetry data yields parameters $A = 6.7 \pm 0.9$ and $B = 0.85 \pm 0.04$ for the function $A e^{-B \log \sqrt{s}}$, with $\chi^2/ndf = 3/4$.
- The best fit to the $\bar{p}/p$ ratio in $p$+$p$ collisions using a Regge-inspired function gives $\alpha_J - \alpha_P = -0.97 \pm 0.05$ and $C = 35 \pm 7$, with $\chi^2/ndf = 4/5$.
- For A+A collisions, the $\bar{p}/p$ ratio is 0.77 $\pm$ 0.14 at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV.
- The tuned PYTHIA parameter for diquark-antidiquark suppression was set to 0.05, compared to the default value of 0.1.

## Context

This work extends prior compilations of $\bar{p}/p$ ratios in $p$+$p$ collisions and provides a systematic comparison to the PYTHIA (Sjostrand et al.), PHOJET (Engel et al.), and HIJING/B-$\bar{\mathrm B}$ (Vance et al.) models. It enables future tuning of baryon production mechanisms in event generators, particularly for lower energies, and provides constraints on Regge-model descriptions of baryon transport, such as the string-junction picture proposed by Kharzeev (1996) and Kaidalov (2003). The comparison of $p$+$p$ and A+A data highlights the enhanced baryon production in heavy-ion collisions below $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV, motivating further measurements at LHC energies.
