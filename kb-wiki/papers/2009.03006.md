# Little-Bang and Femto-Nova in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We make a theoretical and experimental summary of the state-of-the-art status of hot and dense QCD matter studies on selected topics. We review the Beam Energy Scan program for the QCD phase diagram and present the current status of search for QCD Critical Point, particle production in high baryon density region, hypernuclei production, and global polarization effects in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The available experimental data in the strangeness sector suggests that a grand canonical approach in thermal model at high collision energy makes a transition to the canonical ensemble behavior at low energy. We further discuss future prospects of nuclear collisions to probe properties of baryon-rich matter. Creation of a quark-gluon plasma at high temperature and low baryon density has been called the "Little-Bang" and, analogously, a femtometer-scale explosion of baryon-rich matter at lower collision energy could be called the "Femto-Nova", which may possibly sustain substantial vorticity and magnetic field for non-head-on collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the state-of-the-art status of hot and dense QCD matter studies, arguing that the transition from a grand canonical ensemble description at high collision energies to a canonical ensemble behavior at low energies is suggested by available experimental data in the strangeness sector, and that a femtometer-scale explosion of baryon-rich matter at lower collision energy (termed "Femto-Nova") may sustain substantial vorticity and magnetic field for non-head-on collisions.

## Method

The authors provide a theoretical and experimental summary of selected topics in hot and dense QCD matter, reviewing the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at RHIC for the QCD phase diagram. They analyze experimental data on particle production, hypernuclei yields, and global polarization effects in nucleus-nucleus collisions, comparing measurements to thermal model calculations (grand canonical and canonical ensemble approaches), hadron resonance gas (HRG) model predictions, and transport model (UrQMD) simulations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice-QCD simulation gives the pseudo-critical temperature for the QCD crossover as \(T = (156.5 \pm 1.5)\) MeV at vanishing baryon chemical potential (Bazavov 2018).
- The saturation density around the center of heavy nuclei is \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\) nucleons/fm\(^3\), corresponding to energy density \(\epsilon_0 \simeq 0.15\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The Stefan-Boltzmann law converts energy density \(\epsilon = 0.8-1.0\) GeV/fm\(^3\) to \(T = 150-160\) MeV, above which a quark-gluon plasma is realized.
- The baryon number density along the Chemical Freeze-out line is maximized around \(T \simeq 150\) MeV and \(\mu_B \simeq 400\) MeV, between \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3-19.6\) GeV.
- The \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio peaks around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \simeq 8\) GeV.
- Central \(\kappa\sigma^2\) data for net-proton distributions show a non-monotonic variation with collision energy at a significance of \(\sim 3\sigma\) (Adam 2020).
- The global average lifetime of the hypertriton is \(206^{+15}_{-13}\) picoseconds, about 22% shorter than the free \(\Lambda\) lifetime of \(263.2 \pm 2.0\) picoseconds.
- The angular momentum imparted to the system is of the order of \(10^7\hbar\), leading to thermal vorticity of the order of \(10^{21}\) per second for QCD matter.
- The magnetic field generated at LHC is of the order of \(10^{15}\) T, lasting for a short time of the order of \(\sim 0.1\) fm/\(c\).

## Context

This review extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, which builds on earlier work by Cleymans (2005) on the Chemical Freeze-out line and by Stephanov (2008, 2011) on non-Gaussian fluctuations as signatures of the QCD Critical Point. The paper discusses the transition from grand canonical to canonical ensemble behavior in strangeness production, which was previously explored by Hagedorn (1984) and Andronic (2017). The results enable future experimental programs at FAIR, JPARC-HI, and NICA to probe baryon-rich matter, and provide constraints for models of neutron star interiors through hyperon-nucleon interaction measurements from hypernuclei lifetime studies.
