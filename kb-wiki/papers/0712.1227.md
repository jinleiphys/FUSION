# Effective Field Theories for Heavy Quarkonium

> We briefly review how nonrelativistic effective field theories give us a definition of the QCD potentials and a coherent field theory derived quantum mechanical scheme to calculate the properties of bound states made by two or more heavy quarks. In this framework heavy quarkonium properties depend only on the QCD parameters (quark masses and $\als$) and nonpotential corrections are systematically accounted for. The relation between the form of the nonperturbative potentials and the low energy QCD dynamics is also discussed.

## Key claim

Nonrelativistic effective field theories (NR EFTs) provide a rigorous field-theory-derived definition of QCD potentials and a quantum mechanical scheme for calculating heavy quarkonium properties that depends only on QCD parameters (quark masses and $\alpha_s$), with nonpotential corrections systematically accounted for; however, the applicability of perturbative or nonperturbative methods depends on whether the soft scale $m_Q v$ is larger than or comparable to $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$, which varies across different quarkonium systems.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of a hierarchy of effective field theories for heavy quarkonium: Nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) is obtained by integrating out hard modes at the scale $m_Q$, and potential NonRelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) is obtained by further integrating out soft modes at the scale $m_Q v$, leaving only ultrasoft degrees of freedom ($Q\bar{Q}$ colour singlets, $Q\bar{Q}$ colour octets, and low-energy gluons) dynamical. The matching coefficients of pNRQCD are the QCD potentials, which can be calculated either perturbatively (when $m_Q v \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$) or nonperturbatively via Wilson loop expectation values (when $m_Q v \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$), with Poincare invariance imposing exact relations among the potentials such as the Gromes relation.

## Key numbers

- For $b\bar{b}$ systems: $v^2 \sim 0.1$; for $c\bar{c}$ systems: $v^2 \sim 0.3$; for $t\bar{t}$ systems: $v \sim 0.1$
- The static singlet $Q\bar{Q}$ potential is known at three loops apart from the constant term
- The $1/m_Q$ singlet potential is known at two loops; the $1/m_Q^2$ singlet spin-dependent and spin-independent potentials are known at one loop
- Nonpotential nonperturbative contributions to energy levels start at order $m_Q \alpha_s^5$
- The M1 transition rate $\Gamma (J/\psi \to \gamma \eta_c)$ was calculated in pNRQCD and found compatible with experimental data

## Context

This review extends the framework of NR EFTs for heavy quarkonium developed by Caswell (1985), Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1994), and Manohar (1997) for NRQCD, and by Pineda (1997) and Brambilla (1999) for pNRQCD. It contrasts with phenomenological potential models and constituent quark model descriptions by providing a systematic field-theory foundation where potentials are matching coefficients rather than ad hoc inputs, and it enables precision calculations of quarkonium spectra, decays, and transitions that depend only on Standard Model parameters, allowing extraction of $\alpha_s$ and quark masses from experimental data. The framework also provides a way to relate nonperturbative potentials to QCD vacuum structure and lattice calculations, as pursued by Bali (1997) and Koma (2007).
