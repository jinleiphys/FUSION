# Multiplicity dependent $p_T$ distributions of identified particles in pp collisions at 7 TeV within HIJING/$B\bar{B}$ v2.0 model

> Effects of strong longitudinal color fields (SCF) on the identified (anti)particle transverse momentum ($p_T$) distributions in $ pp$ collision at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV are investigated within the framework of the {\small HIJING/BB v2.0} model. The comparison with the experiment is performed in terms of the correlation between mean transverse momentum ($\langle p_t\rangle$) and multiplicity (N$_{ch}^*$) of charged particles at central rapidity, as well as the ratios of the $p_T$ distributions to the one corresponding to the minimum bias (MB) pp collisions at the same energy, each of them normalized to the corresponding charged particle density, for high multiplicity $\mbox{(HM, N$_{ch}>100$)}$ and low multiplicity $\mbox{(LM, N$_{ch}<100$)}$ class of events. The theoretical calculations show that an increase of the strength of color fields (as characterized by the effective values of the string tension $κ$), from $κ$ = 2 GeV/fm to $κ$ = 5 GeV/fm from LM to HM class of events, respectively, lead to a ratio at low and intermediate $p_T$ (i.e., \mbox{$ 1$GeV/{\it c} $< p_{T} < 6 $ GeV/{\it c}}) consistent with recent data obtained at LHC by the ALICE Collaboration.

## Key claim

The HIJING/BB v2.0 model can reproduce the ratio of normalized transverse momentum distributions for identified particles in high-multiplicity (Nch > 100) pp collisions at 7 TeV only if the effective string tension is increased from κ = 2 GeV/fm (used for low-multiplicity events) to κ = 5 GeV/fm, matching the value previously used for central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV; however, the model still underestimates the mean transverse momentum of kaons and multi-strange particles at high multiplicity.

## Method

The HIJING/BB v2.0 model was used to simulate pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV, incorporating strong longitudinal color field effects through an effective string tension κ that controls quark-pair production rates via the Schwinger mechanism. Events were generated with κ = 2 GeV/fm and κ = 5 GeV/fm, and the resulting identified particle (π, K, p, Λ, Ξ, Ω) transverse momentum spectra were compared to ALICE data using the ratio Rmb of normalized pT distributions for high-multiplicity (Nch > 100) and low-multiplicity (Nch < 100) event classes relative to minimum bias events.

## Key numbers

- Effective string tension increased from κ = 2 GeV/fm for low-multiplicity (Nch < 100) events to κ = 5 GeV/fm for high-multiplicity (Nch > 100) events in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV
- The value κ = 5 GeV/fm matches the string tension used for central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, derived from κ(s,A)LHC = κ0 (s/s0)0.04 A0.167 GeV/fm
- For pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV, the default energy-dependent string tension is κ = 2.0 GeV/fm from κ(s) = κ0 (s/s0)0.04 GeV/fm
- The integrated charged particle density at mid-pseudorapidity for minimum bias events is (dNchth/dη)MB = 5.7, close to the experimental value of approximately 6.0
- For high-multiplicity class I (200 ≤ Nch < 300), dNchth/dη = 30.9 for κ = 2 GeV/fm and 25.2 for κ = 5 GeV/fm
- The ratio Rmb at low and intermediate pT (1 GeV/c < pT < 6 GeV/c) is consistent with ALICE data when using κ = 5 GeV/fm for high-multiplicity events
- Increasing κ from 2 to 5 GeV/fm increases the mean pT of kaons by only 10-15%

## Context

This work extends the HIJING/BB v2.0 model studies of identified particle production in small collision systems at LHC energies, following previous successful descriptions of global observables in pp, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions by Topor Pop et al. (2010-2013). The results contradict the interpretation that collective hydrodynamic flow is necessary to explain the observed multiplicity dependence of mean transverse momentum, instead supporting initial-state dynamics from strong color fields as seen in the EPOS model of Pierog et al. (2013) and the DIPSY model of Bierlich et al. (2017). The finding that κ = 5 GeV/fm describes both high-multiplicity pp and central Pb-Pb collisions suggests that system size plays a minor role at LHC energies, with global features determined primarily by the properties of initial chromoelectric flux tubes.
