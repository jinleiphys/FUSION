# Dynamical Freeze-out in 3-Fluid Hydrodynamics

> Freeze-out procedure accepted in the model of 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) is analyzed. This procedure is formulated in terms of drain terms in hydrodynamic equations. Dynamics of the freeze-out is illustrated by 1-dimensional simulations. It is demonstrated that the resulting freeze-out reveals a nontrivial dynamics depending on initial conditions in the expanding ``fireball''. The freeze-out front is not defined just ``geometrically'' on the condition of the freeze-out criterion met but rather is a subject the fluid evolution. It competes with the fluid flow and not always reaches the place where the freeze-out criterion is met. Dynamics of the freeze-out in 3D simulations is analyzed. It is demonstrated that the late stage of central nuclear collisions at top SPS energies is of the form of three (two baryon-rich and one baryon-free) fireballs separated from each other.

## Key claim

The freeze-out front in the 3-fluid dynamics model is not determined purely geometrically by a fixed criterion (e.g., energy density threshold) but instead emerges dynamically from competition between the fluid expansion and the freeze-out process, meaning the front may not reach the region where the criterion is met if the fluid flow is supersonic.

## Method

The freeze-out procedure is formulated as drain terms in the hydrodynamic equations, removing matter from the fluid evolution when the total energy density falls below a critical value (ε_frz) and the collective velocity points outward, with an additional anti-bubble condition preventing freeze-out inside dense matter. One-dimensional simulations of a step-like slab of nuclear matter and three-dimensional simulations of central nuclear collisions at top SPS energies were performed using the 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, with the freeze-out front treated as a discontinuity at the system surface. Observable spectra were calculated using Milekhin's method on a discontinuous hypersurface, where each tiny hypersurface element has a normal vector coinciding with the local 4-velocity.

## Key numbers

- Critical freeze-out energy density: ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm³ for most incident energies, with lower values of 0.3 GeV/fm³ at E_lab = 2A GeV and 0.2 GeV/fm³ at E_lab = 1A GeV.
- One-dimensional simulations were performed for head-on collisions of two 1D slabs at E_lab = 10A GeV, with the freeze-out hypersurface illustrated in Figure 1.
- The ground-state cold nuclear matter has energy density ε ≈ 0.15 GeV/fm³, which is below the freeze-out threshold but protected by the additional expansion criterion.

## Context

This work extends the 3-fluid dynamics model (3FD) previously developed by the authors (Russkikh and Ivanov, 2006) for simulating heavy-ion collisions from AGS to SPS energies, and it contrasts with standard freeze-out schemes such as the Cooper-Frye prescription (Cooper and Frye, 1974) and its modifications (e.g., cut-Jüttner distribution by Bugaev (1996) and Csernai (1997)). The dynamical freeze-out approach is similar to the "continuous emission" method proposed by Sinyukov (2002) and is inspired by experimental studies of evaporation from superheated classical fluids (evap76, evap82, evap92, evap99). This work enables more realistic modeling of freeze-out dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly explaining the incident-energy dependence of kaon inverse-slope parameters observed at AGS and SPS energies, which was previously attributed to deconfinement phase transitions by Gorenstein (2003) and Mohanty (2003).
