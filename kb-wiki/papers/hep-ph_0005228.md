# Neutrino Propagation In Color Superconducting Quark Matter

> We calculate the neutrino mean free path in color superconducting quark matter, and employ it to study the cooling of matter via neutrino diffusion in the superconducting phase as compared to a free quark phase. The cooling process slows when quark matter undergoes a second order phase transition to a superconducting phase at the critical temperature $T_c$. Cooling subsequently accelerates as the temperature decreases below $T_c$. This will directly impact the early evolution of a newly born neutron star should its core contain quark matter. Consequently, there may be observable changes in the early neutrino emission which would provide evidence for superconductivity in hot and dense matter.

## Key claim

The cooling of quark matter via neutrino diffusion slows when it undergoes a second-order phase transition to a color superconducting phase at the critical temperature \(T_c\), but subsequently accelerates as the temperature drops below \(T_c\); this effect arises primarily from the peak in specific heat at the transition rather than from large changes in the neutrino mean free path near \(T_c\).

## Method

The authors calculated the neutrino mean free path in color superconducting quark matter using linear-response theory with a BCS-type superconducting background, computing the vector, axial-vector, and mixed vector-axial quark polarization tensors for two-flavor quark matter with an energy gap \(\Delta\). They then applied these results to a simplified spherical model of quark matter cooling via neutrino diffusion, using BCS temperature dependences for the gap and specific heat to derive a characteristic cooling time scale \(\tau_c(T) = C_V(T) R^2 / (c \langle \lambda(T) \rangle)\).

## Key numbers

- Neutrino mean free path in superconducting quark matter increases non-linearly for \(\Delta/T \sim 1\) and exponentially for \(\Delta/T \gtrsim 5\), for ambient conditions \(\mu_q = 400\) MeV and \(T = 30\) MeV.
- The critical temperature is related to the zero-temperature gap by \(T_c \simeq 0.57 \Delta_0\), taken from BCS theory.
- For a typical quark chemical potential \(\mu_q \sim 400\) MeV, models predict an energy gap \(\Delta \sim 100\) MeV.
- The vector-longitudinal response at low energy transfer reduces to \(\lim_{q_0 \rightarrow 0} \text{Im}~\Pi^V_L(\Delta) = \frac{\mu^2 q_0}{2\pi q} \frac{1}{1 + e^{\beta\Delta}}\).
- The axial-longitudinal response at low energy transfer scales as \(\text{Im}~\Pi^A_L(\Delta) \simeq \frac{\Delta}{2T} \,\text{sech}^2\left(\frac{\Delta}{2T}\right) \ln\left(\frac{\Delta}{q_0}\right) \Pi^A_L(\Delta = 0)\).
- Proto-neutron star conditions considered: temperature \(T \sim 20-30\) MeV, baryon density \(n_B \sim 2-3~n_0\) where \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), and neutrino mean energy \(\langle E_\nu \rangle \sim 20\) MeV emitted over a diffusion time scale of about 10-20 seconds.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of color superconductivity in dense QCD (Gap, 1969; Son, 1999; BBS, 1999) and applies them to neutrino transport in proto-neutron stars, building on earlier calculations of neutrino mean free paths in normal quark matter (RPL, 1994). The results enable future studies of how a superconducting transition in a neutron star core could alter the temporal profile of supernova neutrino emission, though the authors note that realistic systems require accounting for ungapped color-3 quarks, chemical potential mismatches that may drive first-order transitions (Paulo, 2000), and the presence of strange quarks (Strange Quark, 1999).
