# Impact of Dynamical Fermions on the Centre Vortex Gluon Propagator

> The impact of $SU(3)$ centre vortices on the Landau-gauge gluon propagator is calculated in the presence of dynamical fermions and compared to the pure Yang-Mills case. The presence of dynamical fermions is found to alter the behaviour of the centre vortex propagator when compared to the established pure-gauge result. The gluon spectral representation is also explored from the centre vortex perspective, where centre vortices are shown to exhibit clear signs of positivity violation, which is an indicator of confinement. Vortex removal subsequently restores positivity, demonstrating the crucial role centre vortices play in the confinement of gluons.

## Key claim

Centre vortices in the presence of dynamical fermions capture the non-perturbative physics of QCD more effectively than in pure Yang-Mills, with vortex removal becoming far more effective at removing infrared strength from the gluon propagator; however, the origin of the differences between pure-gauge and dynamical cases remains unknown.

## Method

The authors generated three ensembles of 200 32^3 x 64 lattice gauge fields: one pure Yang-Mills ensemble and two (2+1) flavour dynamical ensembles from the PACS-CS collaboration with pion masses of 701 MeV and 156 MeV. They identified centre vortices by fixing configurations to maximal centre gauge and projecting links onto nearest SU(3) centre elements, producing untouched (UT), vortex-only (VO), and vortex-removed (VR) ensembles. The Landau-gauge gluon propagator was calculated on each ensemble and renormalised using both a standard MOM scheme and a best-fit reconstruction approach, and the Euclidean correlator was computed to test positivity violation.

## Key numbers

- Pure gauge ensemble: lattice spacing a = 0.100 fm, beta = 2.58
- Heavy dynamical ensemble: a = 0.102 fm, beta = 1.9, kappa_ud = 0.13700, m_pi = 701 MeV
- Light dynamical ensemble: a = 0.093 fm, beta = 1.9, kappa_ud = 0.13781, m_pi = 156 MeV
- MOM renormalisation constants Z3_UT: pure gauge = 7.112, m_pi=701 MeV = 9.316, m_pi=156 MeV = 11.51
- Best-fit renormalisation constants (zeta_VO, zeta_VR): pure gauge = (0.5543, 0.8985), m_pi=701 MeV = (0.6916, 0.8251), m_pi=156 MeV = (0.5834, 0.8780)
- Renormalisation scale mu = 5.5 GeV applied at largest available momentum on all ensembles

## Context

This work extends previous pure-gauge centre vortex studies by Biddle (2018) and Bowman (2010) which showed that vortex removal suppresses the infrared gluon propagator but leaves residual infrared strength. It builds on recent dynamical fermion studies by Biddle (2022) and Virgili (2022) that demonstrated quantitative agreement between vortex-only results and unmodified gauge fields for the static quark potential. The results enable further investigation into the geometric structure of centre vortices in full QCD and suggest that the presence of dynamical fermions fundamentally alters how centre vortices partition the vacuum into long-range and short-range physics.
