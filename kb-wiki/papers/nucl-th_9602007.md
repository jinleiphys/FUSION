# Renormalized QRPA and double beta decay: a critical analysis of double Fermi transitions

> The proton-neutron monopole Lipkin model, which exhibits some properties which are relevant for those double beta decay ($ββ$) transitions mediated by the Fermi matrix elements, is solved exactly in the proton-neutron two-quasiparticle space. The exact results are compared with the ones obtained by using the Quasiparticle Random Phase (QRPA) and renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) approaches. It is shown that the RQRPA violates the Ikeda Sum Rule and that this violation may be common to any extension of the QRPA where scattering terms are neglected in the participant one-body operators as well as in the Hamiltonian. This finding underlines the need of additional developments before the RQRPA could be adopted as a reliable tool to compute $ββ$ processes.

## Key claim

The renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) violates the Ikeda Sum Rule by nearly 50% at large values of the particle-particle strength parameter, and this violation may be common to any extension of the QRPA where scattering terms are neglected in the participant one-body operators as well as in the Hamiltonian, indicating that additional developments are needed before the RQRPA can be adopted as a reliable tool to compute double beta decay processes.

## Method

The authors constructed a solvable proton-neutron monopole Lipkin model in a single-shell space with j=9/2, Z=4, and N=6, which is exactly solvable via a Holstein-Primakoff mapping. They compared exact solutions of the model Hamiltonian (with scattering terms neglected) against results from the QRPA and the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) for excitation energies, ground state boson number, Fermi beta transition amplitudes, double-beta decay matrix elements, and the Ikeda Sum Rule as functions of the particle-particle strength parameter kappa'.

## Key numbers

- The model space uses j = 9/2, Z = 4, N = 6, with quasiparticle energy epsilon = 1 MeV.
- The QRPA collapses at kappa' approximately equal to 1 (for both chi' = 0 and chi' = 0.5).
- The RQRPA violates the Ikeda Sum Rule by nearly 50% at large values of kappa' (shown for chi' = 0.5).
- The double-beta decay amplitude M_{2nu} is calculated with Delta = 0.5 MeV.
- The RQRPA ground state shows about twice the exact number of bosons for large kappa' values.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of QRPA and RQRPA methods for double beta decay, previously applied to realistic calculations by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986), Engel, Vogel, and Zirnbauer (1988), and Civitarese, Faessler, and Tomoda (1987), as well as the RQRPA reformulation by Catara, Dinh Dang, and Sambataro (1994) and its application by Toivanen and Suhonen (1995). The paper demonstrates that while the RQRPA avoids the collapse of the QRPA, it overestimates ground state correlations and violates the Ikeda Sum Rule, suggesting that future work must include scattering terms (one-quasiparticle terms) in both the transition operators and the Hamiltonian, requiring an SO(5) algebra rather than the SU(2) quasispin algebra used here.
