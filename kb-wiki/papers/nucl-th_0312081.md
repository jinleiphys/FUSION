# Elliptic flow of resonances at RHIC: probing final state interactions and the structure of resonances

> We propose the measurement of the elliptic flow of hadron resonances at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider as a tool to probe the amount of hadronic final state interactions for resonances at intermediate and large transverse momenta. This can be achieved by looking at systematic deviations of the measured flow coefficient $v_2$ from the scaling law given by the quark recombination formalism. Our method can be generalized to explore the structure of exotic particles, such as the recently found pentaquark $Θ^+ (1540)$.

## Key claim

Measuring the elliptic flow $v_2$ of hadron resonances at RHIC can probe the amount of hadronic final state interactions at intermediate and large transverse momenta, by looking for systematic deviations from the scaling law predicted by quark recombination. The method can also distinguish between compact five-quark states and molecular states for exotic particles like the pentaquark $\Theta^+(1540)$, provided the measurement is performed in the transverse momentum range where the two scenarios differ by up to 20%.

## Method

The authors calculate $v_2$ for resonances such as $\Sigma^*(1385)$ and $K^*_0(892)$ under two limiting scenarios: primordial production from a hadronizing quark-gluon plasma (QGP) via parton recombination, and secondary production via hadron-hadron coalescence in the hadron gas (HG). They use the quark recombination scaling law $v_2(P_T) = n v_2^q(P_T/n)$ for the QGP case and extend the same additive coalescence formalism to hadron-hadron coalescence for the HG case, then combine the two contributions via $v_2^{\rm measured}=r(P_T) v_2^{\rm QGP} + (1-r(P_T)) v_2^{\rm HG}$ where $r(P_T)$ is the fraction of resonances escaping hadronization without rescattering. For the pentaquark $\Theta^+(1540)$, they compare a compact five-quark state (scaling with $n=5$) against a molecular $KN$ bound state (using wave functions with momentum fraction $x_K \approx 0.35$).

## Key numbers

- For conventional resonances, the difference between the QGP and HG limiting cases for $v_2$ can be as large as 30% between 2 and 6 GeV.
- For the pentaquark $\Theta^+(1540)$, above $P_T = 3$ GeV the difference between the compact five-quark scenario and the $KN$ molecular scenario is up to 20%.
- The scaling law $v_2(P_T) = n v_2^q(P_T/n)$ applies for $P_T < 4$ GeV for mesons and $P_T < 6$ GeV for baryons, and for $P_T > 2$ GeV where mass effects are suppressed.
- For a molecular $\Theta^+$, the kaon momentum fraction is $x_K = M_K/(M_K + M_N) \approx 0.35$.

## Context

This work extends the quark recombination formalism developed by the Duke group (2002-2004) and the scaling law confirmed by RHIC measurements of pions, kaons, protons, $\Lambda$, and $\Xi$ (2003). It proposes a new observable, resonance elliptic flow, to quantify hadronic rescattering, which is complementary to resonance yield measurements via invariant mass reconstruction. The method can be applied to determine the internal structure of exotic candidates like the $\Theta^+(1540)$ (discovered by Nakano et al., 2003) and other possible molecular states such as $\Lambda(1405)$, $a_0(980)$, and $f_0(980)$, with the deuteron serving as a benchmark molecular state already measured by the PHENIX collaboration (Sakai, 2003).
