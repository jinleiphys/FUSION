# Coulomb Corrections to Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering and the Proton Charge Radius

> Recent values of the proton charge radius derived from the Lamb shift in electronic hydrogen tend to be larger than those from electron scattering. Therefore low-momentum-transfer scattering data from different groups and laboratories have been reanalyzed with Coulomb and recoil corrections included. This was done by calculating at each scattering angle and scattering energy the corresponding correction obtained from a partial wave program which includes recoil approximately. Corrections due to magnetic scattering were also made before the rms-radius was determined by a fit which allows free normalization for each experiment. It is shown that an analysis of electron scattering data including Coulomb corrections lowers the $χ^2$ of the fit and increases the proton radius by about (0.008 - 0.013) fm depending on the fit strategy. A value of r_p = 0.880 (15) fm is obtained which is in good agreement with the one extracted from Lamb shift measurements.

# Coulomb Corrections to Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering and the Proton Charge Radius

## Key claim
Including Coulomb corrections in the analysis of low-momentum-transfer elastic electron-proton scattering data increases the extracted proton charge radius by 0.008–0.013 fm, yielding a value of r_p = 0.880 (15) fm that agrees with the radius derived from Lamb shift measurements in electronic hydrogen, whereas the original Mainz analysis without such corrections gave 0.862 (12) fm.

## Method
The author reanalyzed published and unpublished elastic electron-proton cross section data from Mainz and Saskatoon at momentum transfers below Q^2 < 1 fm^{-2}. For each scattering angle and energy, Coulomb corrections were calculated using a partial wave program that solves the Dirac equation in the electrostatic potential of the proton, with recoil included approximately by transforming to the center-of-mass system but neglecting dynamical recoil corrections of order V_c^2/M_p. Magnetic scattering corrections were applied separately, and the electric form factor was fitted to a quadratic expansion in Q^2 with free normalization factors for each experiment, using a chi-squared function that includes normalization error penalties.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius without Coulomb corrections (fit to Mainz data): 0.862 (12) fm
- Proton charge radius with Coulomb corrections included: 0.880 (15) fm
- Increase in extracted radius due to Coulomb corrections: 0.008 – 0.013 fm (range depends on fit strategy)
- Coulomb corrections to individual cross sections: 0.4% – 0.9% (positive, i.e., increasing the cross section)
- Lamb shift proton radius used for comparison: 0.883 (14) fm
- Wong's earlier reanalysis result: 0.877 (24) fm
- Error on Coulomb corrections from phase shift comparison: at most 2 × 10^{-4}
- Coefficient a_2/a_0 in form factor fit: 0.014 ± 0.004 fm^4

## Context
This work extends the approach of Foldy et al. (1950s) and Friar (1970s) for including Coulomb distortion in electron scattering, which had previously been applied to the deuteron by the same author (Rosenfelder, 1999) showing an increase of 0.012–0.015 fm in the deuteron radius. The analysis resolves a discrepancy between the proton radius from electron scattering (Simon et al., 1980, giving 0.862 fm) and from Lamb shift measurements in electronic hydrogen (1990s, giving 0.883 fm). The result enables comparison with the upcoming muonic hydrogen experiment at PSI, which aims to determine the proton charge radius with tenfold higher accuracy.
