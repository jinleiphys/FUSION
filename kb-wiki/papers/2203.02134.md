# Stochastic hydrodynamics and hydro-kinetics: Similarities and differences

> The hydro-kinetic formalism has been used as a complementary approach to solving the Stochastic Differential Equations (SDE) corresponding to noisy hydrodynamics. The hydro-kinetic formalism consists of a deterministic set of relaxation type equations that tracks the evolution of 2-point correlation functions of stochastic hydrodynamic quantities. Hence they are comparatively easier to solve than the SDEs, which are computationally intensive and need to deal with arbitrarily large gradients. This work compares the two approaches for the propagation and diffusion of conserved charge fluctuations in the Bjorken hydrodynamic model. For white noise, the two approaches agree. For colored Catteneo noise, which is causal, the two approaches diverge. This is because white noise only induces two-point correlations, while Catteneo noise also induces higher-order correlations. This difference is quantified from the effects of causal evolution and influence from higher-order correlations induced by the Catteneo noise.

## Key claim

The hydro-kinetic formalism (deterministic relaxation equations for 2-point correlation functions) agrees exactly with direct stochastic hydrodynamic simulations for white noise, but diverges for colored Catteneo noise because the latter introduces higher-order correlations that the hydro-kinetic approach does not track.

## Method

The authors compared two approaches for propagating conserved charge fluctuations in a 1+1D Bjorken-expanding fluid with transverse dynamics: (1) direct numerical solution of the stochastic differential equations (SDEs) for charge density fluctuations, and (2) the hydro-kinetic equation, a deterministic evolution equation for the 2-point correlation function N(τ,k). For white noise, they solved the SDE analytically via Green's functions and compared with numerical solutions of the hydro-kinetic equation. For colored Catteneo noise, they solved the SDE numerically on a discrete lattice using an auxiliary Langevin equation to generate the noise, and compared with the white-noise hydro-kinetic result.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between stochastic hydrodynamics and the hydro-kinetic formalism previously developed by Akamatsu (2016), Stephanov (2017), and Martinez (2017) for expanding systems. The authors build on their own prior work (De 2020) on colored noise in 1+1D Bjorken flow, now including transverse dynamics. The results show that hydro-kinetics cannot capture colored noise effects because Catteneo noise generates non-Gaussian correlations; this motivates developing extended hydro-kinetic equations that include higher-order cumulants, particularly important for studying fluctuations near the QCD critical point as discussed by Bzdak (2019) and Bluhm (2020).
