# Octet Baryon Magnetic Moments from Lattice QCD: Approaching Experiment from a Three-Flavor Symmetric Point

> Lattice QCD calculations with background magnetic fields are used to determine the magnetic moments of the octet baryons. Computations are performed at the physical value of the strange quark mass, and two values of the light quark mass, one corresponding to the three-flavor symmetric point, where the pion mass is 800 MeV, and the other corresponding to a pion mass of 450 MeV. The moments are found to exhibit only mild pion-mass dependence when expressed in terms of appropriately chosen magneton units- the natural baryon magneton. A curious pattern is revealed among the anomalous baryon magnetic moments which is linked to the constituent quark model, however, careful scrutiny exposes additional features. Relations expected to hold in the large-Nc limit of QCD are studied; and, in one case, a clear preference for the quark model over the large-Nc prediction is found. The magnetically coupled Lambda-Sigma system is treated in detail at the three-flavor symmetric point, with the lattice QCD results comparing favorably with predictions based on SU(3)F symmetry. This analysis enables the first extraction of the isovector transition magnetic polarizability. The possibility that large magnetic fields stabilize strange matter is explored, but such a scenario is found to be unlikely.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of the octet baryons exhibit only mild pion-mass dependence when expressed in natural baryon magneton units, but this conclusion is based on calculations at only two pion masses (800 MeV and 450 MeV) and does not include quark-disconnected contributions at the lighter pion mass, which are estimated to be small but not quantified.

## Method

Lattice QCD calculations with background magnetic fields were performed on three ensembles of gauge configurations generated with a tadpole-improved clover-fermion action. Two ensembles (I and II) used three degenerate dynamical quark flavors at the SU(3)F-symmetric point with pion masses of approximately 800 MeV and 760 MeV respectively, while Ensemble III used 2+1 dynamical flavors with physical strange quark mass and a pion mass of approximately 450 MeV. The magnetic moments were extracted from the Zeeman splitting between spin-up and spin-down energy eigenstates of each baryon in uniform background magnetic fields, using ratios of spin-projected correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- At the SU(3)F-symmetric point (m_pi ~ 800 MeV), the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton in natural baryon magnetons [nBM] is 2.052(14)(34), and of the neutron is -1.982(03)(19)
- At m_pi ~ 450 MeV, the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton in [nBM] is 1.895(22)(51), and of the neutron is -1.908(08)(37)
- The isovector combination (p-n) extrapolated to the physical pion mass gives 3.71(12) [nBM], matching the experimental value of 3.71(00) [BM]
- The Sigma^- anomalous magnetic moment at the SU(3)F-symmetric point is -0.136(14)(32) [nBM], and at m_pi ~ 450 MeV is -0.206(21)(43) [nBM]
- The Xi^- anomalous magnetic moment at m_pi ~ 450 MeV is 0.049(16)(34) [nBM], compared to the experimental value of 0.0834(35) [BM]
- The lattice spacing on Ensemble I is 0.1453(16) fm, on Ensemble II is 0.1036(11) fm, and on Ensemble III is 0.1167(16) fm

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon and light nucleus magnetic moments by Beane et al. (2014) and Chang et al. (2015) to the full octet of baryons, finding that natural baryon magneton units capture the quark-mass dependence for the entire multiplet. The results enable comparison with SU(3)F symmetry predictions from Coleman (1961), Okubo (1963), Beg (1964), and Sakita (1964), and with large-Nc relations from Dashen (1994) and Jenkins (2011), showing a preference for the nonrelativistic quark model over large-Nc predictions in one case. The first extraction of the isovector transition magnetic polarizability is enabled, and the possibility of magnetic field stabilization of strange matter is ruled out for neutron star conditions.
