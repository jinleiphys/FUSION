# Tentative estimates of $\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to蟺^0蟺^0蠂_{c1})$ and $\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to蟺^+蟺^-蠂_{c1})$

> The rates of the $X(3872)\to蟺^0蟺^0蠂_{c1}$ and $X(3872)\to 蟺^+蟺^-蠂_{c1}$ decays are estimated in the model of the triangle loop diagrams with charmed $D^*\bar DD$ and $\bar D^*D\bar D$ mesons in the loops. There are the triangle logarithmic singularities in the physical region of the $X(3872)\to蟺^0蟺^0 蠂_{c1}$ decay which manifest themselves as narrow peaks in the $蟺^0蠂_{c1}$ mass spectrum near the $D^0\bar D^0$ threshold. The model predicts approximately the same branching fractions of the $X(3872)\to蟺^0蟺^0 蠂_{c1}$ and $X(3872)\to蟺^+蟺^-蠂_{c1}$ decays at the level of about (0.8-1.7)$\times10^{-4}$. A distinct prediction of the model is the value of the ratio $\mathcal{R}= \mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to蟺^+蟺^-蠂_{c1})/\mathcal{B}(X(3872) \to蟺^0蟺^0蠂_{c1})\approx1.1$. It weakly depends on the $X(3872)$ resonance parameters and indicates a significant violation of the isotopic symmetry according to which one would expect $\mathcal{R}=2$.

## Key claim

The branching fractions of \(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^0\chi_{c1}\) and \(X(3872)\to\pi^+\pi^-\chi_{c1}\) are predicted to be approximately equal, at the level of about \((0.8\text{--}1.7)\times10^{-4}\), with a ratio \(\mathcal{R}\approx1.1\) that indicates significant violation of the isotopic symmetry expectation \(\mathcal{R}=2\). This prediction depends on the chosen values of the \(X(3872)\) coupling constant \(g_X^2/(16\pi)\) and the non-\((D^*\bar D+\bar D^*D)\) width \(\Gamma_{\text{non}}\), which are varied within reasonable ranges based on current experimental information.

## Method

The authors model the decays via triangle loop diagrams with charmed \(D^*\bar D D\) and \(\bar D^* D\bar D\) mesons in the loops, treating the \(X(3872)\) as a \(\chi_{c1}(2P)\) charmonium state with equal couplings to \(D^{*0}\bar D^0\) and \(D^{*+}D^-\) channels. They compute the scalar triangle amplitudes using nonrelativistic formalism, including finite widths of the \(D^{*0}\) (55.6 keV) and \(D^{*+}\) (83.6 keV) mesons, and estimate the effective \(D\bar D\pi\chi_{c1}\) coupling constant from \(D^*\) exchange with a form factor. The branching fractions are obtained by integrating the energy-dependent decay width over the resonance distribution of the \(X(3872)\).

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^0\chi_{c1})\) ranges from \(0.77\times10^{-4}\) to \(1.63\times10^{-4}\) depending on \(g_X^2/(16\pi)\) (0.25, 0.5, or 0.671 GeV\(^2\)) and \(\Gamma_{\text{non}}\) (1 or 2 MeV).
- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to\pi^+\pi^-\chi_{c1})\) ranges from \(0.86\times10^{-4}\) to \(1.77\times10^{-4}\) for the same parameter sets.
- Ratio \(\mathcal{R} = \mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to\pi^+\pi^-\chi_{c1})/\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^0\chi_{c1})\) is predicted to be between 1.07 and 1.22 across all parameter variations, compared to the isotopic symmetry expectation of 2.
- Invariant phase volumes: \(\text{PV}(m_X;m_{\pi^0},m_{\pi^0},m_{\chi_{c1}}) = 0.0049718\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(\text{PV}(m_X;m_{\pi^+},m_{\pi^-},m_{\chi_{c1}}) = 0.00407956\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- Energy releases: \(\text{T}_n = 91.0264\ \text{MeV}\) for \(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^0\chi_{c1}\) and \(\text{T}_c = 81.8392\ \text{MeV}\) for \(X(3872)\to\pi^+\pi^-\chi_{c1}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the \(X(3872)\) as a \(\chi_{c1}(2P)\) charmonium state (Achasov and Shestakov, 2024; Achasov et al., 2019) and provides predictions for three-body decays that have not yet been measured. It contrasts with earlier leading-order estimates by Fleming and Mehen (2008, 2012) who found very different ratios for \(\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to\pi^0\pi^0\chi_{c1})/\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to\pi^0\chi_{c1})\); the present model predicts branching fractions two orders of magnitude smaller than the 2008 estimate. The results enable future experimental searches at BESIII and Belle II to test the charmonium interpretation of the \(X(3872)\) and the role of triangle singularities in its decays.
