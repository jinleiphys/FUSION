# Extended study for unitary fermions on a lattice using the cumulant expansion technique

> A recently developed lattice method for large numbers of strongly interacting nonrelativistic fermions exhibits a heavy tail in the distributions of correlators for large Euclidean time τ and large number of fermions N, which only allows the measurement of ground state energies for a limited number of fermions using standard techniques. In such cases, it is suggested that measuring the log of the correlator is more efficient, and a cumulant expansion of this quantity can be exactly related to the correlation function. The cumulant expansion technique allows us to determine the ground state energies of up to 66 unpolarized unitary fermions on lattices as large as 72$\times$14^3, and up to 70 unpolarized unitary fermions trapped in a harmonic potential on lattices as large as 72$\times$64^3. We have also improved our lattice action with a Galilean invariant form for the four-fermion interaction, which results in predictive volume scaling of the lowest energy of three fermions in a periodic box and in good agreement of our results for N \leq 6 trapped unitary fermions with those from other benchmark calculations.

## Key claim

The cumulant expansion technique, which extracts ground state energies from the log of the correlator rather than the correlator itself, overcomes the heavy-tailed distribution overlap problem that previously limited lattice simulations of unitary fermions to at most N=38 untrapped or N=20 trapped particles, enabling calculations for up to 66 unpolarized untrapped and 70 trapped unitary fermions.

## Method

The authors simulate N nonrelativistic two-component fermions on a T x L^3 Euclidean lattice with periodic spatial and open temporal boundary conditions, using Z_2 auxiliary fields on time-like links to generate the four-fermion contact interaction. They tune the interaction strength C(q) using a Galilean invariant form with up to five operator coefficients to match the lowest energy eigenvalues of the two-body transfer matrix to Luscher's formula at unitarity. Ground state energies are extracted via a cumulant expansion of ln C(tau), where the generalized effective mass is constructed from partial sums of cumulant differences and plateau fits are performed with bootstrap resampling and correlated chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of three untrapped unitary fermions in a zero total momentum state: 0.3735^{+0.0014}_{-0.0007} in units of the noninteracting three-fermion energy, agreeing with Pricoupenko and Castin (2004) within ~0.3% uncertainty
- Preliminary Bertsch parameter xi = 0.399 +/- 0.002, obtained by averaging constant fits of xi(N) for N >= 38 untrapped unitary fermions on lattices up to 72 x 14^3
- Trapped unitary fermion ground state energies for N=3: 4.243^{+0.037}_{-0.034} omega; N=4: 5.071^{+0.032}_{-0.075} omega; N=5: 7.511^{+0.051}_{-0.091} omega; N=6: 8.339^{+0.080}_{-0.066} omega, agreeing with Blume (2007) at 1% level
- Lattice parameters: temporal spacing omega b_tau = 0.005 for trapped simulations; spatial lattice sizes up to 72 x 64^3 for trapped and 72 x 14^3 for untrapped

## Context

This work extends the lattice method for nonrelativistic fermions with four-fermion contact interactions presented by Endres (2010) and applied to unitary fermions by Lee (2010) and Nicholson (2010), which were limited to N=20 trapped and N=38 untrapped fermions due to a statistical overlap problem. The cumulant expansion technique, introduced in Noise (2011) and Trapped (2011), enables reaching N~70 and extracting the Bertsch parameter for comparison with Quantum Monte Carlo results (xi <= 0.383 (3) from the most recent QMC calculations) and experimental measurements (xi=0.39 (2) from Duke group, 0.41 (1) from Paris group). The method enables future calculations of the pairing gap in slightly polarized unitary Fermi gases and the integrated contact density near unitarity.
