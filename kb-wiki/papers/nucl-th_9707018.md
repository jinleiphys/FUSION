# Bremsstrahlung Pair Production In Relativistic Heavy Ion Collision

> We calculate production of electron- and muon-pairs by the bremsstrahlung process in hadron collisions and compare it with the dominant two-photon process. Results for the total cross section are given for proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions at energies of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

Bremsstrahlung pair production contributes less than 3 × 10⁻³ to the total electromagnetic lepton-pair production rate in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, despite the Z⁶ scaling of its cross section; the authors emphasize that their calculation provides an upper limit, so the true cross section is expected to be even smaller.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross section for electron-positron and muon-antimuon pair production via the bremsstrahlung process in lowest-order perturbation theory, using Feynman diagrams where one nucleus emits a virtual photon that creates the lepton pair. They included elastic form factors for the colliding nuclei: a dipole form factor for protons and a Gaussian form factor for heavy ions, and for proton-proton collisions they also applied a vector-meson-dominance (VMD) form factor in the time-like region using the ω-meson. The total cross sections were obtained by Monte Carlo integration (VEGAS) over the four-particle final-state phase space.

## Key numbers

- For electron-positron pair production at RHIC (γ = 100) with gold beams: σ(bremsstrahlung) = 3.08 × 10⁻⁴ barn, σ(two-photon) = 2.74 × 10⁴ barn, ratio = 1.1 × 10⁻⁸
- For electron-positron pair production at LHC (γ = 2900) with lead beams: σ(bremsstrahlung) = 8.73 × 10⁻⁴ barn, σ(two-photon) = 2.05 × 10⁵ barn, ratio = 4.3 × 10⁻⁹
- For muon-antimuon pair production at RHIC (γ = 100) with gold beams: σ(bremsstrahlung) = 4.77 × 10⁻⁵ barn, σ(two-photon) = 4.66 × 10⁻¹ barn, ratio = 1.0 × 10⁻⁴
- For muon-antimuon pair production at LHC (γ = 2900) with lead beams: σ(bremsstrahlung) = 2.02 × 10⁻⁴ barn, σ(two-photon) = 4.60 × 10⁰ barn, ratio = 4.4 × 10⁻⁵
- For muon-antimuon pair production with protons at LHC (γ = 7500) including VMD form factor: σ(bremsstrahlung) = 5.7 × 10⁻¹⁰ barn, σ(two-photon) = 1.36 × 10⁻⁷ barn, ratio = 1.2 × 10⁻³
- The Gaussian form factor for heavy ions uses λ² = 6/⟨r²⟩ with √⟨r²⟩ = 1.0 A^(1/3) fm
- The dipole form factor for protons uses Λ² = 0.71 GeV²

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates by Ginzburg (1973) and a detailed calculation by Panella (1995) for e⁺e⁻ collisions at LEPII energies, providing the first systematic comparison of bremsstrahlung and two-photon pair production for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The results show that bremsstrahlung production is negligible for the use of muon pairs as a luminosity monitor at the LHC (where the rate must be known to better than 1%), and that the largest uncertainty comes from the poorly known time-like form factor of the proton and nuclei. The authors suggest that measurements of virtual Compton scattering and the Bethe-Heitler process, as proposed by Scholten (1997), could improve the calculation.
