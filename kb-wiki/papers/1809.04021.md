# Rho Meson spectral function in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> The rho meson spectral function in hot and dense asymmetric nuclear matter (number densities of proton and neutron different) is evaluated in an effective chiral SU(3) model within the mean field approximation (MFA). The dependence of the vector meson masses on density and temperature, their variation with the asymmetry parameter, the form of the spectral function when the p <-> n symmetry is broken are studied. One can observe clear splitting of the longitudinal and the transverse modes among the rho isospin multiplets. The role of a running tensor coupling on the spectral function is also studied.

## Key claim

The rho meson spectral function in hot and dense asymmetric nuclear matter shows a clear splitting of longitudinal and transverse modes among the rho isospin multiplets when proton-neutron symmetry is broken, with the magnitude of splitting depending on the asymmetry parameter and density; however, the study is limited to mean field approximation (MFA) without considering nucleon-antinucleon pair production effects, and only the effects of decreasing neutron and proton effective masses on the spectral function are examined.

## Method

The rho meson spectral function was evaluated using an effective chiral SU(3) model within the mean field approximation, where the meson fields are treated as classical fields and only scalar and vector fields contribute to the baryon-meson interaction. The in-medium self-energy of the rho meson was calculated from nucleon-loop contributions using the finite temperature field theory, with the polarization tensor modified for charged rho mesons to restore current conservation in asymmetric matter by adding a term proportional to the difference between neutron and proton number densities. The spectral function was obtained from the imaginary part of the retarded propagator for both longitudinal and transverse modes.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- For symmetric matter (eta = 0) at rho_B = rho_0, |k| = 0.75 GeV, T = 0.15 GeV: longitudinal peak at 0.77 GeV, transverse peak at 0.78 GeV
- For asymmetry eta = 0.1 at rho_B = rho_0, |k| = 0.75 GeV, T = 0.15 GeV: longitudinal spectral function peaks for rho^0 and rho^- at 0.77 GeV, but for rho^+ at 0.74 GeV
- Input parameters: m_pi = 0.1396 GeV, m_K = 0.498 GeV, m_omega = 0.783 GeV, f_pi = 0.0933 GeV, f_K = 0.122 GeV, m_rho = 0.77 GeV, g_rhoNN^2 = 6.96, kappa_rhoNN = 6.1

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector meson properties in nuclear matter, particularly the work by Gale and Kapusta on rho meson self-energy in isospin-symmetric pion media, and the study by Mazumder on asymmetry effects on rho meson masses. It builds on the chiral SU(3) model framework previously used for nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and hyperonic matter studies by Zschiesche and collaborators. The results enable predictions for dilepton production rates in heavy-ion collisions where isospin asymmetry is significant, and provide a baseline for comparing mean field approximation results with those including Dirac sea effects.
