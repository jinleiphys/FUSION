# Unveiling the origin of shape coexistence in lead isotopes

> The shape coexistence in the nuclei $^{182-192}$Pb is analyzed within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach with the effective Gogny force. A good agreement with the experimental energies is found for the coexisting spherical, oblate and prolate states. Contrary to the established interpretation, it is found that the low-lying prolate and oblate $0^+$ states observed in this mass region are predominantly characterized by neutron correlations whereas the protons behave as spectators rather than playing an active role.

## Key claim

Contrary to the traditional interpretation that shape coexistence in neutron-deficient lead isotopes arises from proton 2p-2h and 4p-4h excitations, the authors find that the low-lying prolate and oblate 0+ states are predominantly characterized by neutron correlations, with protons acting as spectators rather than playing an active role.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the finite-range density-dependent Gogny force (D1S parametrization) in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis containing thirteen major shells. They constrained the expectation value of the mass quadrupole moment to generate potential energy surfaces as functions of deformation for the isotopes 182-192Pb, and analyzed the separate contributions of proton and neutron energies, pairing energies, and single-particle occupancies to identify the origin of the deformed minima.

## Key numbers

- The prolate minima in 182-186Pb have deformation parameters β2 = 0.29, 0.32, and 0.29 respectively; the oblate minima have β2 = -0.18, -0.18, and -0.20 respectively.
- For 188-192Pb, the first excited states are oblate with β2 = -0.20, -0.20, and -0.17; the second excited states are prolate with β2 = 0.28, 0.25, and 0.22 respectively.
- The crossing of theoretical prolate and oblate states occurs at A=188, two mass units higher than the experimental crossing at A=186.
- In 186Pb, the proton wave function of the prolate minimum corresponds to a 7p-7h configuration relative to the spherical state; the oblate minimum corresponds to a 4.5p-4.5h configuration.
- The neutron wave function of the prolate minimum in 186Pb corresponds to a 5.5p-5.5h configuration; the oblate minimum corresponds to a 4.4p-4.4h configuration.
- The lowest two-quasiparticle state for protons lies at E_p-h^min = 5.7 MeV; for neutrons at E_2qp^min = 3.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of shape coexistence in lead isotopes using the Strutinsky method (Moller and Nix, 1977; Bengtsson and Nazarewicz, 1989) and schematic SU(3) shell model calculations (Heyde et al., 1987), as well as more recent beyond-mean-field calculations with the Gogny force (Champagne et al., 2001) and Skyrme interaction (Dobaczewski et al., 2003). The authors reinterpret the mechanism of shape coexistence, showing that the proton shell closure at Z=82 drives the spherical ground state while the neutrons, favored by the mid-shell N=104 configuration, are responsible for the deformed minima, with the proton-neutron interaction and pairing providing the remaining ingredients that bring all three 0+ states within 1 MeV.
