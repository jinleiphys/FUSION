# On the Size of the Chiral Condensate, Generalized Chiral Perturbation Theory and the DIRAC experiment

> In the near future, the DIRAC collaboration will measure pion-pion scattering lengths with great precision. Those measurements are likely to shed some light on the problem of the size of the chiral quark-antiquark condensate. Although it is usually assumed to be as a large as (-225 MeV)^3, in the last years a more general approach, has been developed to accommodate either a large or a small alternative (-100 MeV)^3. Such a low value would also modify the standard temperature estimate at which the chiral phase transition occurs. In this work we briefly review the basic theoretical ideas related to this issue as well as the experiment that could help to establish any of the two scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the possibility that the chiral quark-antiquark condensate could be as small as approximately (-100 MeV)^3 rather than the standard value of approximately (-225 MeV)^3, and argues that the DIRAC experiment's upcoming precise measurement of pion-pion scattering lengths could distinguish between these two scenarios, though neither is ruled out by current experimental evidence.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of standard Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) and Generalized Chiral Perturbation Theory (GChPT), comparing their predictions for meson masses, scattering lengths, and the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula. They then describe how the DIRAC experiment at CERN will produce pionium atoms (pi+ pi- bound states) using a 24 GeV proton beam, measure the pionium lifetime via a double-arm magnetic spectrometer, and extract the combination of scattering lengths (a_00 - a_20) from the lifetime to 5% precision.

## Key numbers

- Standard condensate value: approximately (-225 MeV)^3; alternative small condensate value: approximately (-100 MeV)^3
- Pion decay constant F approximately 90 MeV
- Standard scenario B_0 approximately 1.3 GeV
- Standard scenario quark mass ratio r = m_s/m_hat approximately 26; GChPT allows range 6.3 less than or equal to r less than or equal to 26
- Standard ChPT two-loop prediction for scattering length a_00 = 0.217; GChPT two-loop fit gives a_00 = 0.263; experimental value 0.26 plus or minus 0.05
- Standard ChPT two-loop prediction for a_20 = -0.0413; GChPT two-loop fit gives a_20 = -0.027; experimental value -0.028 plus or minus 0.012
- Standard ChPT two-loop prediction for a_11 = 0.040; GChPT two-loop fit gives a_11 = 0.037; experimental value 0.038 plus or minus 0.002
- DIRAC experiment will measure pionium lifetime to 10% precision, determining (a_00 - a_20)^2 to 5% precision; lifetime is of order 10^{-15} s
- Strong Lamb shift for pionium: Delta E_{2s-2p} approximately -0.3 eV
- Standard scenario critical temperature T_c approximately 200 MeV for two flavors; GChPT extreme case could give T_c below 150 MeV

## Context

This review extends the theoretical discussion of the chiral condensate size, contrasting the standard large-condensate Chiral Perturbation Theory (Weinberg 1979, Gasser and Leutwyler 1984) with the Generalized Chiral Perturbation Theory developed by Stern and collaborators (1993) that allows a small condensate. The paper discusses how lattice calculations (various authors, 1997) support the standard scenario, while some deviations in the pi N Goldberger-Treiman relation motivated the generalized approach. The DIRAC experiment (proposed at CERN) will provide decisive data on pion-pion scattering lengths, which could distinguish between the two scenarios and also impact estimates of the chiral phase transition temperature relevant for heavy ion physics.
