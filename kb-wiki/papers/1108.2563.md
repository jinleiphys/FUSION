# Neutral Current $ν$ Induced Reactions in Nuclei at Supernova Neutrino Energies

> We calculate cross sections for the neutral current induced neutrino/antineutrino reaction from $^{208}Pb$ target and applied it to study Supernova neutrino event rates. The calculations are done in local density approximation taking into account Pauli blocking, Fermi motion effects and renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium. The numerical results for the neutrino nucleus total cross sections have been averaged over the various Supernova neutrino/antineutrino fluxes available in literature.

## Key claim

The paper calculates neutral-current neutrino/antineutrino cross sections on \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) at supernova energies, but the reported event rates depend strongly on both the inclusion of random-phase approximation (RPA) correlations and the choice of supernova neutrino flux model, with RPA effects reducing event rates by about 65%.

## Method

The cross sections were computed using a Fermi gas model with local density approximation (LDA), incorporating Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and renormalization of weak transition strengths via RPA correlations. The hadronic current included vector, axial, and pseudoscalar form factors with a strange quark contribution (\(\Delta s = -0.15\)), and the total cross section was averaged over three different supernova neutrino flux models (Duan, Kneller, and Livermore) to obtain event rates for a 1 kT lead target.

## Key numbers

- For neutrinos on \(^{208}\text{Pb}\), inclusion of Pauli blocking and Fermi motion reduces the cross section from the free case by about 90% at \(E_\nu = 10\text{–}20\ \text{MeV}\), 82% at \(E_\nu = 50\ \text{MeV}\), and 65% at \(E_\nu = 100\ \text{MeV}\).
- Including RPA correlations further reduces the cross section by about 60–65% in the energy range 10–100 MeV.
- Event rates for 1 kT of lead target over 1 second, using the Duan flux with RPA: \(\nu_e\) = 14 events, \(\bar\nu_e\) = 44 events, \(\nu_\mu\) = 71 events, \(\bar\nu_\mu\) = 15 events, \(\nu_\tau\) = 57 events, \(\bar\nu_\tau\) = 35 events.
- Using the Kneller flux with RPA: \(\nu_e\) = 339 events, \(\bar\nu_e\) = 217 events, \(\nu_\mu\) = 337 events, \(\bar\nu_\mu\) = 226 events, \(\nu_\tau\) = 337 events, \(\bar\nu_\tau\) = 217 events.
- Using the Livermore flux with RPA: \(\nu_e\) = 315 events, \(\bar\nu_e\) = 665 events, \(\nu_\mu\) = 990 events, \(\bar\nu_\mu\) = 360 events, \(\nu_\tau\) = 990 events, \(\bar\nu_\tau\) = 665 events.

## Context

This work extends earlier neutral-current neutrino-nucleus calculations by applying LDA and RPA to \(^{208}\text{Pb}\), a target relevant for detectors like HALO. The results show that nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, RPA) significantly reduce cross sections compared to free-nucleon calculations, and that event rates vary widely depending on the supernova flux model used (e.g., Duan, Kneller, Livermore). This enables more realistic predictions for supernova neutrino detection in lead-based detectors and highlights the need for improved flux modeling.
