# Special Solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian Related to Shape Phase Transitions in Nuclei

> Nuclei exhibit quantum phase transitions (earlier called ground state phase transitions) between different shapes as the number of nucleons is modified, resulting in changes in the ground and low lying nuclear states. Special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian appropriate for the critical point of such shape phase transitions, as well as other special solutions applicable to relevant nuclear regions are described.

# Special Solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian Related to Shape Phase Transitions in Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper describes special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian that serve as critical point symmetries for nuclear shape phase transitions, but explicitly states that it is "not a review article but rather a biased brief account of topics related to the authors' work," meaning the selection of solutions and experimental examples reflects the authors' own research focus rather than a comprehensive survey.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian for various potential shapes in the collective coordinates β (deformation) and γ (triaxiality), using infinite square wells, harmonic oscillators, Davidson potentials, and power-law potentials to model the critical points of shape phase transitions. For axially symmetric cases (X(5) and related models), the γ-degree of freedom is treated with a harmonic oscillator approximation near γ=0, while for triaxial cases (Z(5) and Z(4)), the focus is near γ=π/6. Exact separation of variables is achieved in some cases (E(5), X(3), Z(4)), while approximate separation is used in others (X(5), Z(5)) by replacing β² denominators with their average values.

## Key numbers

- E(5) model: R₄/₂ = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 2.199, parameter-free up to an overall scale factor fixed by the 2₁⁺ energy
- X(5) model: R₄/₂ = 2.904, with ground state and β band spectra parameter-free but γ bandheads parameter-dependent
- X(3) model (γ frozen to 0, infinite square well in β): R₄/₂ = 2.44
- Z(5) model (triaxial, γ≈π/6, infinite square well in β): ground state band R₄/₂ = 2.350
- Z(4) model (γ frozen to π/6, infinite square well in β): R₄/₂ = 2.226
- E(5)-β⁴ potential: R₄/₂ = 2.093; E(5)-β⁶: R₄/₂ = 2.135; E(5)-β⁸: R₄/₂ = 2.157
- X(5)-β² potential: R₄/₂ = 2.646; X(5)-β⁴: R₄/₂ = 2.769; X(5)-β⁶: R₄/₂ = 2.824; X(5)-β⁸: R₄/₂ = 2.852
- U(5) harmonic oscillator (β²/2 potential): R₄/₂ = 2.0, spectrum E_N = N + 5/2 with N = 2ν + τ

## Context

This work extends the critical point symmetries E(5) introduced by Iachello (2000) and X(5) introduced by Iachello (2001), which describe nuclei at the second-order phase transition between U(5) and O(6) and the first-order transition between U(5) and SU(3), respectively, within the Interacting Boson Model framework of Arima and Iachello. The paper surveys a family of related solutions including Davidson potentials bridging U(5) to O(6) (Elliott, 1970s), power-law potentials bridging U(5) to E(5) (Bender, 2000s), confined β-soft rotor models bridging to rigid rotor limits, and the newer Z(5) and Z(4) models for triaxial shapes near γ=π/6. Experimental candidates identified include ¹³⁴Ba and ¹⁰²Pd for E(5), ¹⁵²Sm and ¹⁵⁰Nd for X(5), and ¹⁹²⁻¹⁹⁶Pt for Z(5), with ongoing measurements at Jyväskylä and Stuttgart providing new data.
