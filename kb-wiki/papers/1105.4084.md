# On the formulation of functional theory for pairing with particle number restoration

> The restoration of particle number within Energy Density Functional theory is analyzed. It is shown that the standard method based on configuration mixing leads to a functional of both the projected and non-projected densities. As an alternative that might be advantageous for mass models, nuclear dynamics and thermodynamics, we propose to formulate the functional in terms directly of the one-body and two-body density matrices of the state with good particle number. Our approach does not contain the pathologies recently observed when restoring the particle number in an Energy Density Functional framework based on transition density matrices and can eventually be applied with functionals having arbitrary density dependencies.

## Key claim

The standard method for particle-number restoration in Energy Density Functional (EDF) theory, based on configuration mixing and transition density matrices, leads to a functional that depends on both the projected and non-projected densities; the authors propose an alternative formulation using the one- and two-body density matrices of the particle-number-conserving state directly, which avoids the pathologies (divergences and discontinuities) recently observed in the standard Multi-Reference EDF approach and can be applied to functionals with arbitrary density dependencies, including non-analytical ones.

## Method

The authors analyze the standard Multi-Reference EDF (MR-EDF) approach for particle-number projection, showing that the energy functional can be expressed in terms of the one- and two-body density matrices of the projected state plus additional terms involving the non-projected reference state. They then propose a "Symmetry-Conserving" EDF theory where the functional is constructed directly from the one-body density matrix of the projected state, replacing the problematic terms with expressions that depend only on the projected-state occupation numbers. This approach is tested on the deformation energy curve of 18O using the Skyrme interaction SIII and a delta pairing interaction, comparing the standard MR-EDF (with and without regularization) to the new formulation.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of particle-number restoration in EDF theory, following the identification of pathologies (divergences and jumps) in the standard MR-EDF approach by Anguiano (2001), Dobaczewski (2007), Lacroix (2009), and Duguet (2010). The regularization procedure proposed by Lacroix (2009) and Bender (2009) is shown to be almost equivalent to a functional of the projected-state densities, but the authors' new formulation removes the residual dependence on the non-projected reference state. This enables a consistent functional framework that can be applied to functionals with non-analytical density dependencies (e.g., non-integer powers used in Skyrme and Gogny interactions), which cannot be safely regularized in the standard MR-EDF approach, and opens the way for applications to mass models, nuclear dynamics, and thermodynamics.
