# Measurement of global spin alignment of vector mesons at RHIC

> We report the measurements of spin alignment ($ρ_{00}$) for $K^{*0}$, $\overline{K^{*0}}$, $K^{*+}$, and $K^{*-}$ vector mesons in RHIC isobar collisions (Zr+Zr and Ru+Ru) at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. We observe the first non-zero spin alignment for $K^{*\pm}$ in heavy-ion collisions. The $K^{*\pm}$ $ρ_{00}$ is about 3.9$σ$ larger than that of $K^{*0}$. The observed difference and the ordering between $K^{*\pm}$ and $K^{*0}$ are surprising, and require further inputs from theory. When comparing between the isobar and Au+Au collisions, no significant system size dependence in $K^{*0}$ $ρ_{00}$ is observed within uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of non-zero spin alignment for charged $K^{*\pm}$ vector mesons in heavy-ion collisions, with the charged species' $\rho_{00}$ being about 3.9$\sigma$ larger than that of neutral $K^{*0}$, a result that is "surprising" and "opposite to the naive expectation from $B$-field" based on the magnetic moments of the constituent quarks.

## Method

The STAR collaboration measured the spin alignment ($\rho_{00}$) of $K^{*0}$, $\overline{K^{*0}}$, $K^{*+}$, and $K^{*-}$ vector mesons in RHIC isobar collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV, using about 1.8 and 2.0 billion minimum-bias events respectively. The $K^{*}$ mesons were reconstructed via their hadronic decay channels, with the angular distribution of decay daughters measured relative to the second-order event plane, and the observed $\rho_{00}$ corrected for event plane resolution using $R_{2,\mathrm{TPC}} \sim 64\%$ in mid-central collisions.

## Key numbers

- $K^{*\pm}$ $\rho_{00}$ is about 3.9$\sigma$ larger than that of $K^{*0}$ in 20-60% central isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV
- Event plane resolution in mid-central isobar collisions: $R_{2,\mathrm{TPC}} \sim 64\%$
- Data sample: approximately 1.8 billion good minimum-bias events for Ru+Ru and 2.0 billion for Zr+Zr collisions
- Analysis performed for $1.0 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 5.0$ GeV/$c$ and mid-rapidity ($|y|<1.0$) in 20-60% centrality

## Context

This work extends the STAR collaboration's previous measurements of $\phi$ and $K^{*0}$ $\rho_{00}$ from the first phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES-I) in Au+Au collisions (STAR, 2022), which revealed a large positive deviation for $\phi$ mesons (8.4$\sigma$ significance) but no obvious deviation for $K^{*0}$. The isobar data enable high-precision comparison between charged and neutral $K^{*}$ species, and the observed ordering opposite to naive magnetic field expectations challenges current theoretical mechanisms including those from Liang (2005), Yang (2018), and Sheng (2020), requiring further theoretical inputs to interpret the results.
