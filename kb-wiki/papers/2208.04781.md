# Momentum broadening of heavy quarks and jets in the Glasma from classical colored particle simulations

> We investigate the effects of the pre-equilibrium Glasma stage of heavy-ion collisions on the broadening of momentum for heavy quarks and jets using classical colored particle simulations based on Wong's equations. Confirming previous studies, we find that these probes accumulate momentum on the order of the saturation scale $Q_s$ and that the color field of the Glasma induces an anisotropy with more broadening along the beam axis. For quark jets the anisotropy is more pronounced at lower energies.

# Momentum broadening of heavy quarks and jets in the Glasma from classical colored particle simulations

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium Glasma stage of heavy-ion collisions induces momentum broadening on the order of the saturation scale \(Q_s\) for both heavy quarks and jets, with an anisotropy that favors broadening along the beam axis; for quark jets this anisotropy is more pronounced at lower energies, and the effect persists when full classical dynamics of the probes is taken into account rather than only the eikonal (Wilson loop) approximation.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations for the Glasma color fields using McLerran-Venugopalan initial conditions, then propagate heavy quarks (charm and beauty) and quark jets through these fields as classical test particles using Wong's equations in a colored particle-in-cell (CPIC) approach. The momentum broadening \(\delta p_\mu^2(\tau)\) is computed as the difference between squared momentum components at time \(\tau\) and at the particle formation time, with averaging over random color charges drawn from distributions matching fundamental (quark) and adjoint (gluon) representations. The classical results are matched to pQCD eikonal broadening via a normalization factor \(1/D_R\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale of the Glasma: \(Q_s = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Charm quark mass: \(m_c = 1.27\ \mathrm{GeV}\); beauty quark mass: \(m_b = 4.18\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Heavy quark formation time: \(\tau_{c,b} = 1 / (2 m_{c,b})\)
- Heavy quark initial transverse momentum range: \(p_T \in [0, 10]\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Jet initial momentum-to-mass ratio range: \(p^x/m \in [1, 10]\)
- Heavy quarks and jets accumulate squared momenta of approximately \(4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) within \(\tau \lesssim 0.5\ \mathrm{fm}/c\)
- For dynamic heavy quarks, longitudinal broadening \(\langle \delta p_L^2 \rangle\) increases by up to \(50\%\) and transverse broadening \(\langle \delta p_T^2 \rangle\) by up to \(30\%\) relative to the static (infinite mass) limit
- For jets, \(\langle \delta p_z^2 \rangle\) and \(\langle \delta p_y^2 \rangle\) differ from the lightlike limit by at most \(30\%\); the anisotropy can increase by up to \(60\%\) for jets with small \(p^x/m\) ratio

## Context

This work extends previous studies of momentum broadening in the Glasma by Ipp (2020), which used Wilson loop (eikonal) calculations for lightlike probes, to include the full classical dynamics of massive heavy quarks and finite-momentum jets via Wong's equations. The results confirm that the anisotropy in momentum broadening, originally traced to characteristic correlations among Glasma color fields by Ipp (2020), persists for both heavy quarks and jets when backreaction is neglected. The findings enable future computation of additional observables such as angular correlations of quark-antiquark pairs and gauge-invariant force correlators along probe trajectories, which could help isolate Glasma signatures in heavy-ion collision data from LHC and RHIC.
