# Dynamical simulation on production of ${\rm W}^{\pm}$ and ${\rm Z}^{0}$ bosons in p-p, p-Pb (Pb-p), and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV with PACIAE

> In this paper, production of ${\rm W}^{\pm}$ and ${\rm Z}^{0}$ vector bosons in p-p, p-Pb (Pb-p), and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV is dynamically simulated with a parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE. ALICE data of ${\rm Z}^{0}$ production is found to be reproduced fairly well. A prediction for ${\rm W}^{\pm}$ production is given in the same collision systems, at the same energy and at the same energy. An interesting isospin-effect is observed in the sign-change of $μ^{\pm}$ charge asymmetry in pp, pn, np, and nn collisions and in minimum bias p-Pb, Pb-p and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV, respectively.

## Key claim
The PACIAE parton and hadron cascade model reproduces ALICE Z⁰ boson data in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV fairly well, and predicts W± boson production in the same collision systems. The observed sign-change of μ± charge asymmetry across different collision systems is attributed to an isospin effect from varying valence u/d quark abundances, though the authors note this is a simulation-based observation that warrants further investigation.

## Method
The PACIAE model, based on PYTHIA 6.4.28, simulates partonic hard scatterings followed by parton rescattering using LO-pQCD cross sections, Lund string fragmentation, and hadronic rescattering. For heavy-ion collisions, initial nucleon positions follow a Woods-Saxon distribution with participant nucleons determined by the Glauber model. W/Z boson production is simulated using a triggered approach with specific subprocesses activated, and results are presented as rescaled distributions R(X) = X/X_ref to enable comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers
- W± boson mass: m_W± = 80.39 GeV/c²; Z⁰ boson mass: m_Z⁰ = 91.19 GeV/c²
- W± decay time: t_W± = 0.0922 fm/c; Z⁰ decay time: t_Z⁰ = 0.0791 fm/c
- Z⁰ production yield: dN(Z⁰)/dy ~ 10⁻⁹ at mid-rapidity in the most 20% central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- For Pb-Pb centrality classes 0-5%, 0-10%, 0-20%, 0-40%, and 0-90%, the corresponding impact parameter intervals are [0, 3.5], [0, 4.94], [0, 6.98], [0, 9.88], and [0, 14.96] fm, with average Npart values of 378.6, 348.3, 298.1, 219.9, and 111.7 respectively
- A rapidity shift Δy = 0.465 is used to account for asymmetric beam energy configurations in p-Pb and Pb-p collisions

## Context
This work extends previous measurements by CMS and ATLAS of W± and Z⁰ production in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (CMS 2011, ATLAS 2011) and more recent ALICE and ATLAS measurements at √sNN = 5.02 TeV (ALICE 2016, ATLAS 2017). The PACIAE dynamical simulation approach provides a complementary method to the LO and NLO pQCD calculations using CT14 PDF and EPPS16 nPDF sets that have been used to describe existing data. The results enable future studies of partonic transport effects and medium-induced higher order effects in W/Z boson production.
