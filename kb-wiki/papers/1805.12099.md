# Can Long-Range Nuclear Properties Be Influenced By Short Range Interactions? A chiral dynamics estimate

> Recent experiments and many-body calculations indicate that approximately 20\% of the nucleons in medium and heavy nuclei ($A\geq12$) are part of short-range correlated (SRC) primarily neutron-proton ($np$) pairs. We find that using chiral dynamics to account for the formation of $np$ pairs due to the effects of iterated and irreducible two-pion exchange leads to values consistent with the 20\% level. We further apply chiral dynamics to study how these correlations influence the calculations of nuclear charge radii, that traditionally truncate their effect, to find that they are capable of introducing non-negligible effects.

## Key claim

Using chiral dynamics to account for neutron-proton short-range correlated (SRC) pair formation via iterated and irreducible two-pion exchange yields SRC probabilities consistent with the measured 20% level, and these correlations can introduce non-negligible effects on nuclear charge radii calculations that traditionally truncate them, though the sign and magnitude of the effect depend on the specific orbital configuration.

## Method

The authors use a chiral dynamics interaction dominated by iterated one-pion exchange (OPEP) in the spin-triplet channel, following the framework of Kaiser (1997) and Kaiser (2001), to compute the short-range correlation probability for neutron-proton pairs. They apply this interaction within a reference spectrum approximation for the propagator, using harmonic oscillator single-nucleon wave functions with frequency Omega = 10.3 MeV matched to the measured charge radius of 48Ca, and evaluate the change in mean-square charge radius when a neutron is added to either the 0f5/2 or 1p3/2 orbital outside a 48Ca core.

## Key numbers

- The short-range correlation probability is found to be P_SRC ≈ 0.2 ± 0.02 for all orbitals considered, consistent with the measured 20% level for medium and heavy nuclei (A ≥ 12)
- For the 0f5/2 neutron orbital, the change in mean-square charge radius is ΔR²(0f5/2) = 0.17 ± 0.12 fm²
- For the 1p3/2 neutron orbital, the change in mean-square charge radius is ΔR²(1p3/2) = -0.8 fm²
- The harmonic oscillator parameter b² = 1/(MΩ) = 4.02 fm², corresponding to Ω = 10.3 MeV
- The effective Fermi momentum is taken as k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹, with effective mass M* = 0.6M and energy denominator parameter A₂ ≈ 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral dynamics approach of Kaiser (1997) and Kaiser (2001), which showed that iterated OPEP accounts for nuclear binding and saturation, to estimate SRC effects on charge radii. It addresses the systematic underestimate of nuclear charge radii in ab initio calculations using soft interactions, as highlighted by Garcia Ruiz (2016) for calcium isotopes, where calculations with interactions such as NNLO_sat, SRG1, and SRG2 obtained smaller charge radii than measured. The results suggest that including SRC effects, which are typically truncated in soft-interaction calculations, may help resolve discrepancies between theory and experiment, and motivate future ab initio calculations that properly include unitary transformations on operators.
