# A unitary isobar model for pion photo- and electroproduction on the proton up to 1 GeV

> A new operator for pion photo- and electroproduction has been developed for nuclear applications at photon equivalent energies up to 1 GeV. The model contains Born terms, vector mesons and nucleon resonances ($P_{33}(1232)$, $P_{11}(1440)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, and $D_{33}(1700)$). The resonance contributions are included taking into account unitarity to provide the correct phases of the pion photoproduction multipoles. The $Q^2$ dependence of electromagnetic resonance vertices is described with appropriate form factors in the electromagnetic helicity amplitudes. Within this model we have obtained good agreement with the experimental data for pion photo- and electroproduction on the nucleon for both differential cross sections and polarization observables. The model can be used as a starting point to predict and analyze forthcoming data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unitary isobar model for pion photo- and electroproduction on the proton up to 1 GeV that achieves good agreement with experimental data, but the model's success is qualified by the need to use a hybrid pseudovector-pseudoscalar pi-NN coupling that transitions between the two forms depending on energy, and by the fact that agreement in the pi0 p channel is only satisfactory up to 900 MeV due to inconsistent data sets.

## Method

The model combines Born terms, vector meson exchange (omega and rho), and six nucleon resonances (P33(1232), P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535), F15(1680), D33(1700)) with Breit-Wigner energy dependence. The nonresonant background is fixed by analyzing s- and p-wave multipoles, using a hybrid pi-NN coupling that starts as pseudovector at low energies and transitions to pseudoscalar at high energies with a mixing parameter Lambda_m = 450 MeV. The resonance contributions are unitarized by adjusting a phase parameter phi to match the pion-nucleon scattering phases from VPI analysis, and the Q^2 dependence of electromagnetic vertices is described using form factors in the helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Energy range: photon equivalent energies up to 1 GeV
- Mixing parameter for pi-NN coupling: Lambda_m = 450 MeV
- Damping parameter for resonance widths: X = 500 MeV for all resonances
- Delta(1232) resonance: Gamma_exp = 115 MeV used in relation between M_{1+}^{(3/2)} and G_M^* form factor
- Best fit parameter for Q^2 dependence of Delta resonance: gamma = 0.24 (c/GeV)^2
- Harmonic oscillator parameter for Roper resonance: alpha = 0.370 GeV
- Nucleon form factors at Q^2=0: F_1^p(0)=1, F_pi(0)=1, F_1^n(0)=0, F_2^p(0)=kappa_p=1.79, F_2^n(0)=kappa_n=-1.97
- pi-NN coupling constant: g^2/4pi = 14.28

## Context

This work extends earlier isobar models by Blomqvist and Laget (late 1970s) that only covered the first resonance region, and provides a simpler alternative to more sophisticated coupled-channels approaches by Garcilazo, Guidal, and Mosel that were too complicated for nuclear applications. The model is designed for use in nuclear calculations and can serve as a starting point to analyze forthcoming data from Jefferson Lab, MAMI Mainz, ELSA Bonn, LEGS Brookhaven, and GRAAL Grenoble, as well as to predict pion production on neutron targets.
