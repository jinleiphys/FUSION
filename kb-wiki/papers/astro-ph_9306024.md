# Vacuum-Polarization Corrections to Solar-Fusion Rates

> The vacuum-polarization corrections to rates for nuclear-fusion reactions in the $pp$ chain and in the CNO cycle are calculated. For the reactions of particular importance to the solar-neutrino problem, the $\Hethree$, $\Hefour$, $\Be$, and $\Ni$ reactions, we find the magnitude of the effect to be less than 2\%. The effect of VP on all the other reaction rates is expected to be of a similar order of magnitude. We discuss how these results affect the predicted fluxes of solar neutrinos.

# Vacuum-Polarization Corrections to Solar-Fusion Rates

## Key claim

Vacuum-polarization corrections to the electrostatic potential between reacting nuclei change solar fusion reaction rates by less than 2% for the reactions most important to the solar-neutrino problem, and the effect on predicted solar-neutrino fluxes is correspondingly small. The magnitude of the correction is much smaller than the uncertainty in the measured low-energy cross-section factors.

## Method

The authors calculated the Uehling potential (the vacuum-polarization correction to the Coulomb potential) and used a WKB approximation to determine its effect on the Gamow penetration factor for nuclear reactions. For four key reactions (³He+³He, ³He+⁴He, ⁷Be+p, and ¹⁴N+p), they re-analyzed existing experimental data by including vacuum polarization self-consistently in the fitting procedure, then compared the resulting solar-energy cross sections with the standard analysis that omits vacuum polarization.

## Key numbers

- For the ³He+³He reaction, vacuum polarization decreases the reaction rate by 0.2%.
- For the ³He+⁴He reaction, vacuum polarization decreases the reaction rate by 1.6%.
- For the ⁷Be+p reaction, vacuum polarization decreases the reaction rate by 0.1%.
- For the ¹⁴N+p reaction, vacuum polarization decreases the reaction rate by 0.8%.
- For the initial p+p reaction, vacuum polarization decreases the rate by 0.6%.
- The predicted flux of ⁸B neutrinos increases by only 0.2% when vacuum polarization is included.
- The predicted flux of ⁷Be neutrinos decreases by 0.7%.
- The predicted event rate changes by +0.01 SNU for the chlorine experiment and -0.11 SNU for the gallium experiments.
- The vacuum-polarization correction Δ(E) at solar energies ranges from 1.4% (p+p) to 5.5% (¹⁶O+p), but the net effect on rates after self-consistent analysis is always much smaller.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Gould (1990), who estimated a 4.6% correction for the ³He+⁴He reaction without including vacuum polarization in the data-fitting procedure. The authors show that self-consistent inclusion of vacuum polarization reduces the correction to 1.6%, resolving the discrepancy. The results are relevant to solar-neutrino flux predictions from standard solar models as described in Bahcall (1989) and to stellar-evolution calculations of main-sequence stars as reviewed by Parker and Rolfs (1992). The small size of the corrections means that vacuum polarization does not significantly affect the solar-neutrino problem.
