# Cluster configurations in Li isotopes in the variation of multi-bases of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> We investigate the cluster configurations in Li isotopes, which are described in the optimization of the multi-Slater determinants of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. Each Slater determinant in the superposition is determined simultaneously in the variation of the total energy. The configurations of the excited states are obtained by imposing the orthogonal condition to the ground-state configurations. In Li isotopes, various cluster configurations are confirmed and are related to the thresholds of the corresponding cluster emissions. For $^5$Li, we predict the $^3$He+$d$ clustering in the excited state as well as the mirror state of $^5$He with $^3$H+$d$. For $^{6-9}$Li, various combinations of the clusters are obtained in the ground and excited states, and the superposition of these basis states reproduces the observed energy spectra. For $^9$Li, we predict the linear-chain states consisting of various cluster configurations at 10--13 MeV of the excitation energy.

## Key claim

The authors predict that various cluster configurations in Li isotopes, including unbound subsystems such as $^5$He and $^{4,5}$H, appear automatically in the variation of total energy using the multicool method, and these configurations are related to the thresholds of the corresponding cluster emissions. For $^9$Li, linear-chain states consisting of various cluster configurations are predicted at 10--13 MeV of excitation energy, but this prediction depends on the specific strength of the pseudo potential used to generate excited-state configurations.

## Method

The authors use antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with multi-Slater determinants, where each Slater determinant is optimized simultaneously through the multicool method (an extension of the cooling method to multi-basis states). The ground-state configurations are obtained by minimizing the total energy of the superposed wave function, while excited-state configurations are generated by imposing an orthogonal condition to the ground-state configurations using a pseudo potential with variable strength $\lambda$. After generating basis states with both spin-fix and spin-free conditions, angular-momentum projection is performed and the generalized eigenvalue problem (Hill-Wheeler equation) is solved to obtain the final energy spectra.

## Context

This work extends the multicool method previously applied to $^{10}$Be (Myo 2023) to the Li isotope chain, demonstrating that the method automatically generates various cluster configurations without prior assumptions. The results relate to the Ikeda threshold rule (Ikeda 1968) for unstable nuclei, showing that cluster configurations appear near their corresponding emission thresholds. The method enables systematic investigation of clustering in light nuclei and has been combined with neural network approaches for applications to $\Lambda$ hypernuclei of Be isotopes (Tian 2024) and $3\alpha$ resonances of $^{12}$C (Cheng 2024). The $^4$He $0^+_2$ state results are consistent with ab-initio four-body calculations (Hiyama 2004, Horiuchi 2008) and the Gamow shell model (Michel 2023) regarding the importance of the $3N$+$N$ structure.
