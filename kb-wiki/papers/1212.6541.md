# Nuclear modification of vector boson production in proton-lead collisions at the LHC

> In anticipating the upcoming proton-lead run at the LHC in the near future, we present predictions for the nuclear modification factor of transverse momentum spectrum of $Z^0$ production and transverse momentum broadening of vector boson ($J/ψ$, $Υ$, $W/Z^0$) production in proton-lead collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5$ TeV, respectively. We find that the measurement of nuclear modification factor of $Z^0$ production provides a clean and unambiguous test of the nuclear anti-shadowing proposed in the recent EPS09. In addition, the dramatic difference in transverse momentum broadening between the heavy quarkonium and $W/Z^0$ production could be a signature prediction of QCD parton multiple scattering, and it provides valuable informations on color neutralization of a produced heavy quark pair when it transmutes into a physical quarkonium.

# Nuclear modification of vector boson production in proton-lead collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ of $Z^0$ production in proton-lead collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5$ TeV provides a clean and unambiguous test of nuclear gluon anti-shadowing as proposed in the EPS09 parametrization, but only if the transverse momentum is above 20 GeV where gluon-initiated subprocesses dominate; the enhancement at high $p_T$ is a signature of EPS09's gluon anti-shadowing and disappears when using the nDS parametrization which has no such anti-shadowing region.

## Method
The authors used the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) resummation formalism to calculate the full transverse momentum spectrum of $Z^0$ production in both p+p and p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5$ TeV and mid-rapidity $y=0$, employing CTEQ6M NLO parton distribution functions with factorization scale $\mu = M_T/2 = \sqrt{M_Z^2 + p_T^2}/2$. For nuclear effects, they incorporated EPS09 and nDS nuclear PDFs for leading-twist nuclear modifications and modeled nuclear-size-enhanced power corrections by scaling the non-perturbative parameter $g_2$ by $A^{1/3}$. For transverse momentum broadening of vector bosons ($J/\psi$, $\Upsilon$, $W/Z^0$), they derived expressions within the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) framework, accounting for initial-state multiple scattering for all processes and additional final-state rescattering for heavy quarkonium production.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s}=5$ TeV for proton-lead collisions at the LHC
- $Z^0$ mass $M_Z$ used as the hard scale; at $y=0$, the minimum parton momentum fraction $x = M_Z/\sqrt{s} \sim 0.018$
- For p+p collisions, the effective non-perturbative parameter $G_2^{pp} = 0.324$ GeV$^2$ (using $\bar{g}_2 = 0.25 \pm 0.05$ GeV$^2$, $g_2 = 0.01 \pm 0.005$ GeV$^2$, $b_{max} = 0.5$ GeV$^{-1}$)
- For p+Pb collisions, the effective parameter becomes $G_2^{pPb} = 0.689$ GeV$^2$ after scaling $g_2$ by $A^{1/3}$
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ for $Z^0$ production with EPS09 shows suppression at $p_T < 10$ GeV and enhancement at high $p_T$ up to 70 GeV; with nDS, the enhancement disappears
- For heavy quarkonium broadening, the leading contribution in the gluon-gluon dominated region is $\Delta\langle q_T^2\rangle_{\rm HQ}^{\rm CEM} \approx 2 C_A \left( \frac{8\pi^2\alpha_s}{N_c^2-1} \lambda^2 A^{1/3} \right)$
- For p+Pb collisions at the LHC, the minimum bias number of collisions $N_{\rm coll}(b_{\rm min.bias}) \sim 7$ from a Glauber model with $\sigma_{\rm NN}^{\rm in} = 70$ mb at $\sqrt{s}=5$ TeV

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear effects in vector boson production to the upcoming LHC proton-lead run, building on the CSS resummation formalism developed by Collins, Soper, and Sterman and applied to $Z^0$ production in p+p collisions by Qiu and Zhang (2000). The authors contrast two nuclear PDF parametrizations: EPS09 by Eskola, Paukkunen, and Salgado (2009), which features gluon anti-shadowing in the region $x \sim (0.005, 0.2)$, and nDS by de Florian and Sassot (2003), which has no such anti-shadowing. The transverse momentum broadening predictions for heavy quarkonia extend earlier work by Kang, Qiu, and collaborators (2008, 2011) and Xing, Kang, and Qiu (2012), and the dramatic difference between quarkonium and $W/Z^0$ broadening is presented as a signature prediction of QCD multiple scattering that can probe color neutralization timescales. A concurrent preprint by Guzey et al. (2012) studied $Z^0$ nuclear modification focusing on the small-$x$ shadowing region, while this work emphasizes the anti-shadowing region.
