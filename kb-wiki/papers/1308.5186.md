# Baryon masses at nonzero isospin/kaon density

> We present a lattice QCD calculation of the ground-state energy shifts of various baryons in a medium of pions or kaons at a single value of the quark mass corresponding to a pion mass of m_π~390 MeV and a kaon mass of m_K~540 MeV, and in a spatial volume V (4fm)^3. All systems are created using a canonical formalism in which quark propagators are contracted into correlation functions of fixed isospin/kaon density. We study four different systems, Σ^+(π^+)^n, Ξ^0(π^+)^n, p(K^+)^n, and n(K^+)^n, for up to n=11 mesons. From the ground-state energy shifts we extract two- and three-body scattering parameters, as well as linear combinations of low-energy constants appearing in tree-level chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of ground-state energy shifts for baryons in a medium of pions or kaons at a single quark mass (pion mass ~390 MeV, kaon mass ~540 MeV) and a single spatial volume of (4 fm)^3, from which two- and three-body scattering parameters and low-energy constants are extracted, but the results are limited to this specific unphysical quark mass and volume, and the systems are chosen to avoid annihilation diagrams between valence quarks.

## Method

The authors use a canonical formalism on anisotropic 2+1-flavor dynamical clover fermion gauge configurations with spatial lattice spacing 0.1227(8) fm, computing correlation functions for systems containing a single baryon (Sigma+, Xi0, proton, or neutron) with up to 11 mesons (pions or kaons). Quark propagator contractions are performed using a matrix-based method that packages mesons into 12x12 matrices and baryons into larger matrices, enabling efficient computation of all required correlation functions through traces of matrix products. Ground-state energy shifts are extracted from ratios of correlation functions using constant-plus-exponential fits, and scattering parameters are obtained by fitting the volume-dependent energy shifts to the Luscher finite-volume formula for mixed-species boson-fermion systems.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi ~ 386 MeV; kaon mass: m_K ~ 543 MeV (from the lattice ensemble)
* Spatial lattice spacing: b_s = 0.1227(8) fm; renormalized anisotropy: xi = 3.469(11)
* Energy shift for Sigma+ + 1 pion: 0.00219(22)(22) in lattice units; for Sigma+ + 8 pions: 0.0192(14)(14) in lattice units
* Energy shift for Xi0 + 1 pion: 0.00057(19)(13) in lattice units; for Xi0 + 7 pions: 0.0080(21)(15) in lattice units
* Energy shift for proton + 1 kaon: 0.00242(22)(21) in lattice units; for proton + 9 kaons: 0.0275(24)(20) in lattice units
* Energy shift for neutron + 1 kaon: 0.00068(25)(30) in lattice units; for neutron + 5 kaons: 0.0048(12)(12) in lattice units
* Inverse scattering phase shift for pi+ Sigma+: bar_a_MB = 4.63(33) in lattice units
* Inverse scattering phase shift for pi+ Xi0: bar_a_MB = 1.89(71) in lattice units
* Inverse scattering phase shift for K+ proton: bar_a_MB = 5.36(41) in lattice units
* Inverse scattering phase shift for K+ neutron: bar_a_MB = 2.49(88) in lattice units
* Three-body parameter for pi+ pi+ Xi0: bar_eta_MMB = 8.0(2.7) x 10^5 in lattice units
* Three-body parameter for K+ K+ neutron: bar_eta_MMB = 8.6(4.5) x 10^5 in lattice units

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of multiple-meson systems by Detmold et al. (2008, 2011, 2012) and Beane et al. (2007) to include a single baryon alongside up to 11 mesons, enabling the first lattice extraction of meson-meson-baryon three-body interactions. The results enable comparison with tree-level heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory predictions by Bedaque et al. (2009) for baryon masses at finite isospin chemical potential, and provide input for understanding pion and kaon condensation in neutron star interiors as proposed by Kaplan and Nelson (1986, 1987).
