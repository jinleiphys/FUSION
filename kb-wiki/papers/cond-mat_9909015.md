# Exact Study of the Effect of Level Statistics in Ultrasmall Superconducting Grains

> The reduced BCS model that is commonly used for ultrasmall superconducting grains has an exact solution worked out long ago by Richardson in the context of nuclear physics. We use it to check the quality of previous treatments of this model, and to investigate the effect of level statistics on pairing correlations. We find that the ground state energies are on average somewhat lower for systems with non-uniform than uniform level spacings, but both have an equally smooth crossover from the bulk to the few-electron regime. In the latter, statistical fluctuations in ground state energies strongly depend on the grain's electron number parity.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations in ultrasmall superconducting grains never vanish for any level spacing, even in the presence of random level statistics; the crossover from the bulk superconducting regime to the fluctuation-dominated regime is completely smooth for both uniformly-spaced and randomly-spaced levels, contrary to earlier grand-canonical mean-field predictions of an abrupt vanishing at a critical spacing.

## Method

The authors used Richardson's exact solution of the reduced BCS model (the "picket-fence model" from nuclear physics) to compute ground state energies for half-filled bands of up to 260 spin-degenerate levels, both uniformly spaced and randomly spaced according to the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble. For randomly-spaced levels, they generated sets by diagonalizing random matrices, applied a rescaling to ensure unit average spacing, and computed ensemble averages and variances over 150-1000 realizations for each system size. They calculated condensation energies, spectroscopic gaps, and the Matveev-Larkin parity parameter, comparing exact results against previous grand-canonical BCS, projected BCS (PBCS), Lanczos exact diagonalization, band-width rescaling, and density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) approaches.

## Key numbers

- For uniformly-spaced levels with coupling constant lambda = 0.224, the exact results confirm the leading bulk limit behavior E^C_b ~ -Delta^2/(2d) as d approaches 0, with corrections of order d^0.
- The DMRG approach reproduces the exact results with a relative error of less than 10^{-4} for systems with up to n = 400 pairs.
- For randomly-spaced levels, the variance in even condensation energy is approximately delta E^C_0 ~ Delta/2, and delta E^C_0 ~ 2 * delta E^C_1.
- The ensemble size was 1000 for systems with 24 <= N <= 40, and varied between 700 and 150 for 40 <= N <= 260.
- The variances delta E^C_b are essentially d-independent in the range 24 <= N <= 260.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing correlations in ultrasmall superconducting grains, following experiments by Ralph, Black and Tinkham (1996) on Al grains. It corrects the earlier grand-canonical BCS results of von Delft et al. (1996) and Smith and Ambegaokar (1996), which predicted an abrupt vanishing of pairing correlations at a critical level spacing; the latter had found critical spacings of d^c_1 ~ 0.89 Delta for odd grains and d^c_0 ~ 3.6 Delta for even grains with uniform spacings, and ensemble-averaged values of <d^c_1> = 1.8 Delta and <d^c_0> ~ 14 Delta for random spacings. The exact solution confirms the smooth crossover found by Dukelsky and Sierra (1999) using DMRG, and validates the band-width rescaling method of Berger and Halperin (1998) as remarkably accurate, while showing that the PBCS approach of Braun and von Delft (1998) is unreliable in the crossover region. This enables exact studies of the interplay between randomness and pairing correlations in a non-trivial model, and provides a benchmark for future approximations in related problems where no exact solution exists.
