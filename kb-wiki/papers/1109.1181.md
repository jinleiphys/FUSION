# Energy loss due to field fluctuations in a two-stream QCD plasma

> We derive the expression for the collisional energy loss in two stream plasma induced by the fluctuating chromoelectric field. It is revealed that the main contribution here comes from the unstable modes which grow exponentially with time. A strong direction dependence of the energy loss has also been demonstrated.

## Key claim
The paper derives an expression for collisional energy loss due to chromoelectric field fluctuations in a two-stream QCD plasma, showing that the dominant contribution comes from unstable modes that grow exponentially with time. The authors hedge that this calculation is performed for a simplified two-stream plasma rather than a realistic anisotropic momentum distribution, and that only longitudinal chromoelectric fields are considered.

## Method
The authors start from the classical equation of motion for a charged particle in a chromoelectric field and compute the statistical average of the energy loss per unit time, including contributions from field fluctuations and velocity-field correlations. They consider a two-stream plasma with a delta-function momentum distribution, solve for the longitudinal dielectric function to identify unstable modes where the frequency becomes imaginary, and evaluate the energy loss expression by keeping only the fastest-growing unstable mode contributions. The calculation is performed for light partons with the stream velocity set to u=1, considering two cases: jet velocity parallel and perpendicular to the anisotropy vector.

## Key numbers
- The plasma temperature used for numerical estimates is T ~ 400 MeV
- The critical temperature for QGP formation is given as T_c ~ 170 MeV
- The initial energy density at RHIC is stated as 5 GeV/fm^3
- The threshold energy density for QGP formation is given as 1 GeV/fm^3

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of energy loss in anisotropic plasmas by Romatschke and Strickland (2003, 2004), who showed that unstable modes exist in anisotropic systems, and by Chakraborty et al. (2007), who found that field fluctuations could lead to energy gain for heavy quarks in isotropic QGP. The present calculation shows that in a two-stream plasma, light partons lose energy due to field fluctuations, with a strong dependence on the direction of propagation relative to the anisotropy vector. The authors note that this contribution should be added to the usual polarization loss and may be important at early times when large anisotropy exists in heavy ion collisions.
