# $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ suppression by comovers in Pb+Pb collisions

> Measurements of $ψ$ and $ψ'$ production at the CERN SPS are compared to predictions based on a hadronic model of charmonium suppression. Detailed information is presented to facilitate comparison to other analyses. Sensitivity of these conclusions to the model parameters is discussed.

# J/ψ and ψ' suppression by comovers in Pb+Pb collisions

## Key claim
The apparent threshold effect in the ψ-to-continuum ratio as a function of mean path length L reported by NA50 is not a deconfinement signal but instead reflects the geometric saturation of L in symmetric Pb+Pb collisions; the data vary smoothly with transverse energy ET and can be described by a hadronic comover model without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation. However, the authors caution that their comover interpretation is not unambiguous and that model parameters such as comover density should not be taken literally.

## Method
The authors calculate charmonium survival probabilities by combining nucleon absorption (using a ψ-nucleon absorption cross section σψN ≈ 4.8 mb fixed from pA data) with comover scattering (using a dissociation cross section σco ≈ 2σψN/3 and a comover density that scales with ET). They compute ψ and ψ' production cross sections in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon and S+U collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon using the color evaporation model for the elementary NN cross sections, with GRV LO parton distributions, and fold these with a Gaussian probability distribution P(ET,b) to obtain ET-dependent yields. The continuum dimuon yield is calculated via the Drell-Yan process in the mass range 2.9 < M < 4.5 GeV, and the ET scale is adjusted to the NA50 calorimeter acceptance (1.1 < η < 2.3).

## Key numbers
- σψN ≈ 4.8 mb (absorption cross section for ψ-nucleon interactions, fixed from pA data)
- σco ≈ 2σψN/3 ≈ 3.2 mb (comover dissociation cross section)
- Initial comover density n0 = 0.8 fm⁻³ (chosen to fit the central S+U datum)
- Formation time τ0 ≈ 2 fm; freezeout time τF ≈ RT/vrel with vrel ≈ 0.6
- RT ≈ 6.6 fm for Pb+Pb vs. 3.6 fm for S+U (transverse size, leading to ≈ 60% increase in the logarithmic comover suppression factor)
- Total (σ/AB)ψ = 0.97 nb for S+U at 200 GeV and 0.54 nb for Pb+Pb at 158 GeV (in NA50 acceptance, 0.4 > xF > 0, -0.5 < cosθ < 0.5)
- Experimental total cross sections: S+U = 1.03 ± 0.04 ± 0.10 nb; Pb+Pb = 0.44 ± 0.005 ± 0.032 nb
- Drell-Yan cross section (σ/AB)DY = 35.2 pb (calculated) vs. NA50 value 32.8 ± 0.9 ± 2.3 pb
- Bσψ'NN/BσψNN = 0.02 (from pp data)
- σψ' ∼ 2.1 σψ for singlet model; σψ'co ≈ 12 mb for octet model
- χ²/d.o.f. = 0.34 for σψN = 6.2 mb and 1.3 for σψN = 4.8 mb (fits to pA data)
- Comovers contribute at ≈ 18% level to total S+U cross section

## Context
This work extends the hadronic comover model developed by Gavin and Vogt (1995) and Gavin, Gyulassy, and Vogt (1995) to the new NA50 Pb+Pb data at 158 GeV per nucleon. It directly contradicts the interpretation of Blaizot and Ollitrault (1996) and Kharzeev and Satz (1996) that a larger σψN ≈ 6.2 mb without comovers can describe the data; the authors show that such a value fails to reproduce the ET-dependent ratios. The paper enables future searches for deconfinement at RHIC and LHC by establishing a hadronic baseline for charmonium suppression, and suggests that SPS inverse-kinematics experiments and AGS pA studies near the ψ threshold (Seth, 1996) could help resolve model uncertainties.
