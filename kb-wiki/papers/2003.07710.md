# Ab initio study of $\boldsymbol{(ν_\ell,\ell^-)}$ and $\boldsymbol{(\overlineν_\ell,\ell^+)}$ inclusive scattering in $^{12}$C: confronting the MiniBooNE and T2K CCQE data

> We carry out an ab initio calculation of the neutrino flux-folded inclusive cross sections, measured on $^{12}$C by the MiniBooNE and T2K collaborations in the charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) regime. The calculation is based on realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions, and on a realistic nuclear electroweak current with one-and two-nucleon terms that are constructed consistently with these interactions and reproduce low-energy electroweak transitions. Numerically exact quantum Monte Carlo methods are utilized to compute the nuclear weak response functions, by fully retaining many-body correlations in the initial and final states and interference effects between one- and two-body current contributions. We employ a nucleon axial form factor of the dipole form with $Λ_A = 1.0$ or $1.15$ GeV, the latter more in line with a very recent lattice QCD determination. The calculated cross sections are found to be in good agreement with the neutrino data of MiniBooNE and T2K, and antineutrino MiniBooNE data, yielding a consistent picture of nuclei and their electroweak properties across a wide regime of energy and momenta.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their *ab initio* quantum Monte Carlo calculation, which fully retains many-body correlations and interference between one- and two-body current contributions, yields flux-folded inclusive cross sections on 12C that agree well with the MiniBooNE and T2K charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) data, but this agreement is conditional on using a nucleon axial form factor dipole cutoff Lambda_A of either 1.0 or 1.15 GeV, with the latter value being more consistent with recent lattice QCD determinations.

## Method

The authors used Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods to compute the nuclear weak response functions for 12C, employing the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon interactions, along with one- and two-body electroweak currents constructed consistently with these interactions. They calculated the Euclidean response functions via Laplace transforms of the current-current correlators, then performed maximum-entropy inversion to obtain the energy-dependent response functions for momentum transfers from 100 to 700 MeV, and used scaling properties to extrapolate to higher momentum transfers needed for flux-folded cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The axial form factor dipole cutoff Lambda_A was set to either 1.0 GeV or 1.15 GeV, the latter more in line with a recent lattice QCD determination.
- The GFMC response functions were calculated for momentum transfers q in the range 100–700 MeV in steps of 100 MeV.
- The computational cost was approximately 130 million core hours on the MIRA supercomputer at Argonne National Laboratory.
- The two-body current contributions accounted for more than 50% of the total calculated strength in the R_zz response function.
- The antineutrino cross sections are a factor of about 2 to 10 smaller than the neutrino cross sections as the muon scattering angle increases from 0 degrees to 90 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantum Monte Carlo studies of 12C electromagnetic ground-state structure (Lovato 2013) and longitudinal and transverse response functions (Lovato 2016) to the weak sector, confronting neutrino and antineutrino data from MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010, 2013) and T2K (Abe 2016). It provides a more microscopic alternative to phenomenological approaches such as the relativistic Fermi gas (VanOrden 1978, Alberico 1988), relativistic mean field (Walecka 1974, Amaro 2006), random-phase approximation (Martini 2009, Nieves 2004), SuperScaling approach (Barbaro 2006, Amaro 2013), and spectral function methods (Benhar 1989, Rocco 2018), by using realistic interactions and currents with no free parameters in the vector sector and only one fitted parameter in the axial sector.
