# Virial expansion for the Tan contact and Beth-Uhlenbeck formula from 2D SO(2,1) anomalies

> The relationship between 2D $SO(2,1)$ conformal anomalies in nonrelativistic systems and the virial expansion is explored using recently developed path-integral methods. In the process, the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for the shift of the second virial coefficient $δb_2$ is obtained, as well as a virial expansion for the Tan contact. A possible extension of these techniques for higher orders in the virial expansion is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the shift of the second virial coefficient δb₂ for a 2D Fermi gas with contact interactions is produced entirely by the SO(2,1) conformal anomaly, and that the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for δb₂ follows directly from the anomaly analysis without requiring an explicit expression for the interaction contribution to the grand potential δΩ₂.

## Method

The authors use a path-integral formulation of the partition function for a 2D dilute gas of nonrelativistic spin-1/2 fermions with contact interactions, regularized by a momentum cutoff Λ. They derive the scaling anomaly by relating the energy density and pressure through dimensional analysis, identifying the anomaly with the Tan contact. The virial expansion is introduced for the grand potential, and a general formula linking the shift of the nth virial coefficient δbₙ to the derivative of δΩₙ with respect to the bound state energy E_b is obtained. For the n=2 case, the discontinuity of the logarithm of the inverse pair propagator is computed to extract ∂δΩ₂/∂E_b, which is then integrated over inverse temperature β to yield δb₂.

## Key numbers

* The bound state energy E_b appears as the single energy scale in the system; the coupling constant c is related to E_b via 1/c = (1/4π) ln(E_b/Λ²) plus a finite constant.
* The shift of the second virial coefficient is given by δb₂ = e^{βE_b} − ∫₀^∞ (dy/y) [2e^{−βE_b y²}] / [π² + 4 ln² y], where β = 1/(k_B T).
* The second-order term in the virial expansion of the Tan contact is I₂ = (E_b/βπ) e^{βE_b} [1 + 2∫₀^∞ dy y e^{−βE_b(y²+1)} / (π² + 4 ln² y)].
* The thermal wavelength is λ = √(2πβ), and the relation ln(βE_b) = 2 ln(λ/a_{2D}) − ln(2π) connects to the 2D scattering length a_{2D}.

## Context

This work extends the path-integral approach to conformal anomalies developed by Ordóñez et al. (2015) and Lin et al. (2016) to the virial expansion of ultracold atomic Fermi gases in two dimensions. It provides a derivation of the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for δb₂ that is simpler than the explicit calculation of δΩ₂ performed by Chafin and Schäfer (2013), and it reproduces results for the second virial coefficient that agree with Parish (2011). The framework enables a systematic procedure for computing higher-order virial coefficients δbₙ (n ≥ 3) using the Hubbard-Stratonovich representation of the partition function, with partial results for δb₃ reported as work in progress.
