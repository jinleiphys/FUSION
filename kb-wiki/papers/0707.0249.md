# Eccentricity fluctuations from the Color Glass Condensate at RHIC and LHC

> In this brief note, we determine the fluctuations of the initial eccentricity in heavy-ion collisions caused by fluctuations of the nucleon configurations. This is done via a Monte-Carlo implementation of a Color Glass Condensate $k_t$-factorization approach. The eccentricity fluctuations are found to nearly saturate elliptic flow fluctuations measured recently at RHIC. Extrapolations to LHC energies are shown.

# Eccentricity fluctuations from the Color Glass Condensate at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach predicts eccentricity fluctuations that nearly saturate the experimentally measured elliptic flow fluctuations at RHIC, but the measured $\sigma_{v_2}/v_2$ should be viewed only as an upper limit for $\sigma_{\varepsilon_\mathrm{part}}/\varepsilon_\mathrm{part}$ because non-flow effects and hydrodynamic fluctuations may also contribute to $\sigma_{v_2}$.

## Method

The authors implemented a Monte Carlo version of the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) model using $k_t$-factorization to compute gluon production for each nucleon configuration in the colliding nuclei, with the saturation scale proportional to local nucleon density. They improved on their earlier model by rejecting nucleon pairs with separation greater than $r_{\mathrm{max}} = \sqrt{\sigma_{\mathrm{inel}}/\pi}$, and calculated the participant eccentricity $\varepsilon_\mathrm{part}$ and its fluctuations $\sigma_{\varepsilon_\mathrm{part}}$ for given centrality classes defined by number of participants. Results were compared to a simple Glauber model where gluon density scales with number of participants.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic cross section at full RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV): $\sigma_{\mathrm{inel}} = 42$ mb
- Inelastic cross section at LHC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5500$ GeV): $\sigma_{\mathrm{inel}} = 66$ mb
- In the $p+p$ limit, the improved pair-rejection procedure yields an additional factor of 0.58, close to values found in Broniowski (2007) and Kharzeev (2004)
- Fixed coupling evolution of saturation scale uses $\lambda = 0.28$ for the extrapolation to LHC energy
- The proportionality constant between elliptic flow and initial eccentricity in ideal hydrodynamics is roughly $c = 0.2$, following Ollitrault (1992)

## Context

This work extends the MC-KLN model introduced by Drescher (2006) to study eccentricity fluctuations, and compares results to standard Glauber model calculations from Bhalerao (2006) and Broniowski (2007). The eccentricity fluctuations are compared to elliptic flow fluctuation measurements from the PHOBOS collaboration (Alver, 2007) and the STAR collaboration (Sorensen, 2006). The authors note that the CGC approach gives slightly lower relative fluctuations than the Glauber model for semi-central collisions, primarily due to a larger average eccentricity in the CGC approach, and that hydrodynamic fluctuations (Vogel, 2007) may also contribute to measured $v_2$ fluctuations.
