# Meson-exchange Currents and Quasielastic Neutrino Cross Sections

> We illustrate and discuss the role of meson-exchange currents in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering induced by charged currents, comparing the results with the recent MiniBooNE data for differential and integrated cross sections.

# Meson-exchange Currents and Quasielastic Neutrino Cross Sections

## Key claim

Meson-exchange currents (MEC) producing two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) final states can significantly increase the predicted quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross section and help resolve the apparent need for an anomalously large axial mass (M_A = 1.35 GeV/c^2) in the MiniBooNE data, but the agreement is incomplete: the MEC contribution alone is insufficient at large scattering angles (cosθ ≲ 0.6) and low muon kinetic energies, and correlation diagrams required by gauge invariance are not yet included in the neutrino-scattering implementation.

## Method

The authors construct a fully relativistic model of pion-mediated meson-exchange currents (seagull, pion-in-flight, and Δ-resonance diagrams) using pseudo-vector nucleon-pion coupling and a Rarita-Schwinger propagator for the Δ, then embed these two-body currents into the superscaling approach (SuSA) framework. The SuSA model replaces the free Fermi gas scaling function with a phenomenological scaling function extracted from electron scattering data; the MEC are added only in the 2p-2h sector, affecting primarily the transverse vector response R_T^{VV}, and the results are flux-averaged over the MiniBooNE neutrino energy spectrum (0 to 3 GeV) for comparison with the measured charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross sections on carbon-12.

## Key numbers

- The universally accepted nucleon axial mass is M_A ≈ 1 GeV/c^2; fitting MiniBooNE data within the relativistic Fermi gas model requires M_A = 1.35 GeV/c^2, significantly larger.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is f^2/4π = 0.08.
- For electron scattering on iron-56, the transverse response is shown at momentum transfers q = 550 and 1140 MeV/c; the MEC produce a large peak near ω = (m_Δ^2 + q^2)^{1/2} - m_N.
- The regularization parameter for correlation diagrams in the Fermi gas model is taken as ε ≈ 200 MeV.
- In the double differential cross section for ν_μ-^12C, the SuSA+MEC model yields reasonable agreement with MiniBooNE data for cosθ ≥ 0.6, but disagreement grows at larger angles (cosθ ≲ 0.6).
- At the most forward scattering angles, 30-40% of the cross section corresponds to energy transfers below 50 MeV, where impulse approximation is inadequate.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling approach (SuSA) of Amaro (2004) and the relativistic MEC calculations of Amaro (2002, 2003, 2010) to neutrino scattering, directly addressing the discrepancy between MiniBooNE CCQE data and standard nuclear models that led to the axial-mass puzzle. The results are consistent with the multinucleon emission explanations of Martini (2009) and Nieves (2011), and with the relativistic Green's function framework of Meucci (2011), all of which can describe the data without modifying the axial mass. The paper identifies the need to include correlation diagrams (required by gauge invariance) in the neutrino-scattering calculation, as well as the need for a consistent treatment of low-energy collective excitations at forward angles, which are outside the impulse approximation regime.
