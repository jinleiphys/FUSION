# Solution of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations and fitting procedure using N2LO Skyrme pseudo-potential in spherical symmetry

> We present the development of the extended Skyrme N2LO pseudo-potential in the case of spherical even-even nuclei calculations. The energy density functional is first presented. Then we derive the mean-field equations and discuss the numerical method used to solve the resulting fourth-order differential equation together with the behaviour of the solutions at the origin. Finally, a fitting procedure for such a N2LO interaction is discussed and we provide a first parametrization. Typical ground-state observables are calculated and compared against experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first parametrization of the N2LO Skyrme pseudo-potential for finite nuclei, but the authors explicitly neglect tensor terms and state that the resulting functional is a preliminary exploration, not a fully optimized interaction.

## Method

The authors derive the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for the N2LO Skyrme pseudo-potential in spherical symmetry, which involves solving a fourth-order differential equation in coordinate space rather than using a harmonic-oscillator basis. They then develop a fitting procedure to determine the coupling constants of the functional and apply it to calculate ground-state observables of spherical even-even nuclei, comparing the results against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The N2LO pseudo-potential includes gradient terms up to 6th power (order 2ℓ with ℓ=2), extending the standard N1LO Skyrme interaction which includes gradients up to 2nd order.
- The central term of the N2LO interaction contains five parameter pairs: t0, t1, t2, t1^(4), t2^(4) with corresponding x0, x1, x2, x1^(4), x2^(4) exchange parameters.
- The spin-orbit term V_N1LO^LS and density-dependent term V_N1LO^DD retain the same structure as in the standard Skyrme interaction, with no additional parameters from the N2LO extension.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme N2LO pseudo-potential, previously only parametrized using infinite nuclear matter properties (Davesne et al., 2015; Davesne et al., 2015a), to finite nuclei calculations. It contrasts with the HOSPHE code (Carlsson et al., 2008) which uses a harmonic-oscillator basis for N3LO functionals, by working in coordinate space which is more convenient for fitting procedures. The paper responds to the conclusion of the UNEDF collaboration (Kortelainen et al., 2013) that the standard Skyrme functional has reached its limits, and aims to improve nuclear observables by exploring additional gradient terms rather than multi-reference approaches.
