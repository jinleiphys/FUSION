# Probing neutron-skin thickness with total reaction cross sections

> We analyze total reaction cross sections, $σ_R$, for exploring their sensitivity to the neutron-skin thickness of nuclei. We cover 91 nuclei of O, Ne, Mg, Si, S, Ca, and Ni isotopes. The cross sections are calculated in the Glauber theory using the density distributions obtained with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method in 3-dimensional coordinate space. Defining a reaction radius, $a_R=\sqrt{σ_R/π}$, to characterize the nuclear size and target (proton or $^{12}$C) dependence, we find an empirical formula for expressing $a_R$ with the point matter radius and the skin thickness, and assess two practical ways of determining the skin thickness from proton-nucleus $σ_R$ values measured at different energies or from $σ_R$ values measured for different targets.

## Key claim

Total reaction cross sections on a proton target are sensitive to neutron-skin thickness, while those on a 12C target are not; the sensitivity arises from the energy-dependent difference between proton-neutron and proton-proton total cross sections below about 550 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated total reaction cross sections for 91 even-even nuclei (O, Ne, Mg, Si, S, Ca, Ni isotopes) using the Glauber theory with the optical limit approximation for proton targets and the nucleon target formalism for 12C targets. Nuclear densities were generated by the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method in 3-dimensional coordinate space using two parameter sets, SkM* and SLy4. They defined a reaction radius a_R = sqrt(sigma_R/pi) and derived an empirical linear formula relating a_R to the point matter radius and the neutron-skin thickness, with coefficients fitted as functions of incident energy.

## Key numbers

- 91 nuclei were analyzed, covering mass numbers A = 14-86 for elements with Z = 8-16, 20, and 28
- At 100 MeV, the proton-neutron total cross section is about three times larger than the proton-proton total cross section; the difference vanishes at about 550 MeV
- The root-mean-square deviation of the fitted formula from the calculated a_R values is 0.052 fm for SkM* and 0.049 fm for SLy4, approximately 1% of the mean a_R value (4.68 fm and 4.70 fm, respectively)
- For p+40Si at 100 MeV, Fermi distributions with different diffuseness parameters give cross sections deviating from the Hartree-Fock result by at most 1.6%
- For 12C+40Si at 100 MeV, the same Fermi distributions give cross sections deviating from the Hartree-Fock result by about 5%

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of total reaction cross sections for unstable nuclei, building on prior measurements for neutron-rich Ne and Mg isotopes on 12C targets by Takechi (2010) and Kanungo (2011). The authors propose that proton-target measurements at two different energies (e.g., below 200 MeV and above 550 MeV) can extract both matter radius and skin thickness, complementing parity-violating electron scattering measurements such as the PREX result for 208Pb (0.33+0.16-0.18 fm) and dipole polarizability measurements by Tamii (2011). The approach enables skin-thickness determination for unstable nuclei where traditional methods like elastic electron scattering or isotope shift measurements are difficult to apply.
