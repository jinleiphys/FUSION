# Fine structure of the pygmy quadrupole resonance in $^{112,114}$Sn isotopes

> The electric quadrupole response in $^{112,114}$Sn isotopes is investigated by energy-density functional (EDF) and three-phonon quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) theory with special emphasis on 2$^+$ excitations located above the first collective quadrupole state and below 5 MeV. Additional quadrupole strength clustering as a sequence of states similar to the recently observed pygmy quadrupole resonance in $^{124}$Sn is found. The spectral distributions and transition densities of these 2$^+$ states show special features being compatible with oscillations of a neutron skin against the isospin-symmetric nuclear core. Furthermore, two new ($p$, $p' γ$) Doppler-shift attenuation (DSA) coincidence experiments were performed at the SONIC@HORUS setup. Quadrupole states with excitation energies up to 4.2 MeV were populated in $^{112,114}$Sn. Lifetimes and branching ratios were measured allowing for the determination of the reduced quadrupole transition strengths to the ground state. A stringent comparison of the new data to EDF+QPM theory in $^{112}$Sn and $^{114}$Sn isotopes hints at the occurrence of a low-energy quadrupole mode of unique character which could be interpreted as pygmy quadrupole resonance.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a sequence of low-energy 2⁺ states in ¹¹²Sn and ¹¹⁴Sn, located above the first collective quadrupole state and below 5 MeV, can be interpreted as a pygmy quadrupole resonance (PQR) mode, but the identification is qualified as a "hint" based on comparison between new experimental data and EDF+QPM theory, with the caveat that the neutron excess in these isotopes is relatively small (N/Z = 1.24 and 1.28).

## Method

The authors performed energy-density functional (EDF) theory combined with the three-phonon quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) to calculate the electric quadrupole response in ¹¹²Sn and ¹¹⁴Sn. Two new (p, p'γ) Doppler-shift attenuation (DSA) coincidence experiments were conducted at the SONIC@HORUS setup at the University of Cologne, using 8 MeV protons to populate quadrupole states up to 4.2 MeV excitation energy. Lifetimes and γ-decay branching ratios were measured to determine reduced quadrupole transition strengths to the ground state and to the first 2⁺ state.

## Key numbers

- Total experimental summed B(E2) strength between 2 MeV and 4.2 MeV: 224(19) e²fm⁴ in ¹¹²Sn and 151(16) e²fm⁴ in ¹¹⁴Sn
- For firm spin assignments only: 84(7) e²fm⁴ in ¹¹²Sn and 97(14) e²fm⁴ in ¹¹⁴Sn
- EDF+QPM calculated total PQR strength (E_x ≈ 2–5 MeV): B(E2) = 179 e²fm⁴ in ¹¹²Sn and 188 e²fm⁴ in ¹¹⁴Sn
- Pure one-phonon QRPA PQR strength: B(E2) = 111 e²fm⁴ in ¹¹²Sn and 120 e²fm⁴ in ¹¹⁴Sn
- B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁): 12.5(8) W.u. in ¹¹²Sn and 11.1(7) W.u. in ¹¹⁴Sn
- B(E2; 2⁺₃ → 0⁺₁): 0.082(13) W.u. in ¹¹²Sn and 0.023(9) W.u. in ¹¹⁴Sn
- B(E2; 4⁺₁ → 2⁺₁): 5.6(8) W.u. in ¹¹²Sn and 5.9(5) W.u. in ¹¹⁴Sn

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical predictions of a pygmy quadrupole resonance (PQR) in ¹²⁴Sn (Tsoneva 2011, Pellegri 2015, Spieker 2016) to the lighter stable tin isotopes ¹¹²Sn and ¹¹⁴Sn, which have smaller neutron excess. The study provides the first detailed investigation of the fragmentation of PQR strength and the role of multi-phonon coupling in these nuclei, using both new experimental data and advanced EDF+three-phonon QPM calculations. The results enable future studies of how the PQR mode evolves with neutron excess and how branching ratios can serve as sensitive probes of the microscopic structure of low-energy quadrupole excitations.
