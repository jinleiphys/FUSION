# Kaon-nucleon interaction in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model

> The chiral SU(3) quark model is extended to include the coupling between the quark and vector chiral fields. The one-gluon exchange (OGE) which dominantly governs the short-range quark-quark interaction in the original chiral SU(3) quark model is now nearly replaced by the vector-meson exchange. Using this model, the isospin I=0 and I=1 kaon-nucleon S, P, D, F wave phase shifts are dynamically studied by solving the resonating group method (RGM) equation. Similar to those given by the original chiral SU(3) quark model, the calculated results for many partial waves are consistent with the experiment, while there is no improvement in this new approach for the P_{13} and D_{15} channels, of which the theoretical phase shifts are too much repulsive and attractive respectively when the laboratory momentum of the kaon meson is greater than 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The extended chiral SU(3) quark model, in which one-gluon exchange (OGE) is nearly replaced by vector-meson exchange, yields calculated kaon-nucleon phase shifts for many partial waves that are similar to those from the original chiral SU(3) quark model and consistent with experiment; however, the new approach does not improve the description of the P13 and D15 channels, where the theoretical phase shifts remain too repulsive and too attractive, respectively, for kaon laboratory momenta above 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the chiral SU(3) quark model by including coupling between quarks and vector chiral fields, nearly replacing the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) short-range interaction with vector-meson exchange. They solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation to dynamically calculate the S-, P-, D-, and F-wave kaon-nucleon phase shifts for isospin I=0 and I=1 channels. The model parameters were fixed by reproducing the ground-state masses of octet and decuplet baryons, with the sigma meson mass set to 675 MeV and two possible scalar mixing angles (35.264° and -18°) considered.

## Key numbers

- The up (down) quark mass is taken as 313 MeV and the strange quark mass as 470 MeV.
- The harmonic-oscillator width parameter b_u is 0.45 fm in the extended model (compared to 0.50 fm in the original chiral SU(3) quark model).
- The OGE coupling constants in the extended model are g_u^2 = 0.0748 and g_s^2 = 0.0001, much smaller than in the original model (g_u^2 = 0.7704, g_s^2 = 0.5525).
- The vector-meson coupling constant g_chv is taken as 2.351.
- The sigma meson mass is set to 675 MeV.
- The cutoff mass Lambda is 1500 MeV for the kappa meson and 1100 MeV for all other mesons.
- The calculated baryon masses (in MeV) are: N=939, Sigma=1194, Xi=1335, Lambda=1116, Delta=1232, Sigma*=1370, Xi*=1511, Omega=1684.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model developed by Zhang et al. (1997), which had successfully described baryon ground states, nucleon-nucleon scattering, and hyperon-nucleon cross sections. It builds on the authors' previous RGM calculations of KN phase shifts (Huang and Zhang, 2004) and addresses the ongoing debate about whether OGE or vector-meson exchange governs short-range quark-quark interactions, as discussed by Glozman and Riska (1996, 2000) versus Isgur (2000, 2001). The results enable future investigations of the more complicated antikaon-nucleon system and the possible inclusion of inelastic channel effects.
