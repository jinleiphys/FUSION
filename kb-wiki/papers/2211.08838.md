# Two-loop form factors for pseudo-scalar quarkonium production and decay

> We present the analytic expressions for the two-loop form factors for the production or decay of pseudo-scalar quarkonia, in a scheme where the quarks are produced at threshold. We consider the two-loop amplitude for the process $γγ\leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}$, that was previously known only numerically, as well as for the processes $gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}$, $γg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}$ and $gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}$, which have not been computed before. The two-loop corrections to $gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}$ are the last missing ingredients for a full NNLO calculation of $η_Q$ hadro-production. We discuss how the singularity structure of the amplitudes is affected by the threshold kinematics, which in particular introduces Coulomb singularities. In this context, we first show how the usual structure of the infrared singularities degenerates at threshold kinematics, and then extract the anomalous dimensions governing the Coulomb singularities for colour-singlet and octet channels, the latter being presented here for the first time. We give high-precision numerical results for the hard functions, which can be used for phenomenological studies of $η_Q$ production and decay at NNLO.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first analytic two-loop form factors for pseudo-scalar quarkonium production and decay in the channels \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\), \(\gamma g \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}\), and \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}\), while also providing analytic results for the previously numerically known \(\gamma\gamma \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\) channel, all under the condition that the heavy-quark pair is produced at threshold kinematics (i.e., with zero relative velocity).

## Method

The authors work within the framework of Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and compute the two-loop scalar form factors for the production or decay of a pseudo-scalar \(S\)-wave quarkonium state in both colour-singlet and colour-octet configurations. They generate Feynman diagrams using FeynArts, perform colour and Lorentz algebra with FeynCalc, project the final-state heavy-quark pair onto the \(^1S_0^{[1,8]}\) state using standard projection operators, and employ the 't Hooft-Veltman scheme for \(\gamma_5\). The form factors are reduced to a basis of 76 master integrals using integration-by-parts identities implemented in FIRE or KIRA, and the master integrals are evaluated both analytically and numerically as described in a companion paper (Abreu 2022).

## Key numbers

*   For the two-loop form factor \(\mathcal{F}^{(2)}_{FF}\) in the \(\gamma\gamma \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\) channel, the finite piece (\(\epsilon^0\)) is \(-9.58245\).
*   For the two-loop form factor \(\mathcal{F}^{(2)}_{FA}\) in the \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\) channel, the finite piece is \(6.32284 - i\, 12.72196\).
*   For the two-loop form factor \(\mathcal{F}^{(2)}_{AA}\) in the \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\) channel, the finite piece is \(3.45556 + i\, 24.90661\).
*   For the two-loop form factor \(\mathcal{F}^{(2)}_{FA}\) in the \(\gamma g \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}\) channel, the finite piece is \(11.58260\).
*   For the two-loop form factor \(\mathcal{F}^{(2)}_{FA}\) in the \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}\) channel, the finite piece is \(13.9607 - i\, 6.3610\).
*   The light-by-light contribution to the two-loop form factor for \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\) is \((0.64697 + i\, 2.07358) C_F T_F n_h + (0.73128 - i\, 1.79085) C_F T_F \tilde{n}_l + (0.17355 + i\, 0.27096) C_A T_F n_h + (-0.27562 + i\, 0.56535) C_A T_F \tilde{n}_l\).

## Context

This work extends the previous numerical computation of the two-loop form factor for \(\gamma\gamma \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\) by Czarnecki (2001) and Feng (2015) to analytic form, and provides the first results for the \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\), \(\gamma g \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}\), and \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[8]}}\) channels. The two-loop corrections to \(gg \leftrightarrow {^1S_0^{[1]}}\) are the last missing ingredient for a full next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) computation of \(\eta_Q\) hadro-production. The paper also extracts the anomalous dimensions governing Coulomb singularities for colour-octet channels for the first time, and provides high-precision numerical results for the hard functions that can be used in phenomenological studies of \(\eta_Q\) production and decay at NNLO.
