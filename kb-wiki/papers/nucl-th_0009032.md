# Interpretation of the First Data on Central Au+Au Collisions at Square-Root of s = 56 and 130 A GeV

> We compare three semi-microscopic theories to the first data on particle production in central Au+Au collisions taken at RHIC by the PHOBOS collaboration as well as to existing data on central Pb+Pb collisions taken at the SPS by the NA49 collaboration. LEXUS represents the SPS data quite well but not the RHIC data, whereas the wounded nucleon model does the opposite. The collective tube model fails to describe any of the data. This suggests a transition in the dynamics of particle production between \sqrt{s} = 17 and 56 A GeV as one goes from the SPS to RHIC.

# Interpretation of the First Data on Central Au+Au Collisions at √s = 56 and 130 A GeV

## Key claim

The transition in the dynamics of particle production between SPS (√s = 17 A GeV) and RHIC (√s = 56 and 130 A GeV) is suggested by the fact that LEXUS fits SPS data but overpredicts RHIC data by about 60%, while the Wounded Nucleon Model fits RHIC data but underpredicts SPS data by about 30%; the authors note that this is not the only possible interpretation, and that final-state interactions could modify the observed particle numbers.

## Method

The authors compared three semi-microscopic theories, LEXUS (Linear EXtrapolation of Ultrarelativistic nucleon-nucleon Scattering), the Wounded Nucleon Model (WNM), and the Collective Tube Model (CTM), to charged hadron yields from central Au+Au collisions at √s = 56 and 130 A GeV measured by PHOBOS and to central Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 17 A GeV measured by NA49. All three theories use the same nucleon-nucleon input and the same geometrical folding for nucleus-nucleus collisions, with no free parameters, differing only in the assumed dynamics of particle production in multiple collisions.

## Key numbers

- PHOBOS measured dNch/dη averaged over |η| < 1 for the 6% most central Au+Au collisions: 408 ± 12 (stat) ± 30 (syst) at √s = 56 A GeV, and 555 ± 12 (stat) ± 35 (syst) at √s = 130 A GeV.
- LEXUS predicts about 60% more particles than measured by PHOBOS at both RHIC energies.
- The Wounded Nucleon Model predicts only about 70% of the particles observed by NA49 at √s = 17 A GeV.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used was 24 mb for LEXUS and 30 mb for WNM and CTM; the dependence of dNch/dy on this choice is a few percent.
- The conversion factor from pseudo-rapidity to rapidity density at mid-rapidity is v = 0.95, assuming pions with average transverse momentum of 3 times their mass.

## Context

This work extends comparisons of semi-microscopic models from SPS energies (NA49 data on Pb+Pb at √s = 17 A GeV) to the first RHIC data from PHOBOS, finding that LEXUS (Jeon and Kapusta, prior work) describes SPS but not RHIC, while the Wounded Nucleon Model (Bialas, Bleszynski, and Czyz, 1976) does the opposite. The Collective Tube Model fails at all energies. The results suggest a change in particle production dynamics between SPS and RHIC, and the authors note that the HIJING model had already been compared to the PHOBOS data. Future identification of charged hadrons and measurements of other observables at RHIC are needed to clarify the underlying mechanism.
