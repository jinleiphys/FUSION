# Hybrid Renormalization for Quasi Distribution Amplitudes of A Light Baryon

> We develop a hybrid scheme to renormalize quasi distribution amplitudes of a light baryon on the lattice, which combines the self-renormalization and ratio scheme. By employing self-renormalization, the UV divergences and linear divergence at large spatial separations in quasi distribution amplitudes are removed without introducing extra nonperturbative effects, while making a ratio with respect to the zero-momentum matrix element can properly remove the UV divergences in small spatial separations. As a specific application, distribution amplitudes of the $Λ$ baryon made of $uds$ are investigated, and the requisite equal-time correlators, which define quasi distribution amplitudes in coordinate space, are perturbatively calculated up to the next-to-leading order in strong coupling constant $α_s$. These perturbative equal-time correlators are used to convert lattice QCD matrix elements to the continuum space during the renormalization process. Subsequently, quasi distribution amplitudes are matched onto lightcone distribution amplitudes by integrating out hard modes and the corresponding hard kernels are derived up to next-to-leading order in $α_s$ including the hybrid counterterms. These results are valuable in the lattice-based investigation of the lightcone distribution amplitudes of a light baryon from the first principles of QCD.

## Key claim

The authors develop a hybrid renormalization scheme for quasi distribution amplitudes of a light baryon that combines self-renormalization at large spatial separations with a ratio scheme at short spatial separations, thereby removing ultraviolet divergences and linear divergences without introducing extra nonperturbative effects, provided that the logarithmic divergences in the three scales \(z_1^2\), \(z_2^2\), and \((z_1-z_2)^2\) can be factorized separately (which is verified at one-loop order but requires further investigation at higher orders).

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative calculation of the equal-time correlators defining quasi distribution amplitudes for the \(\Lambda\) baryon (composed of \(uds\) quarks) up to next-to-leading order in the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\), working in coordinate space to separate short-distance and long-distance behavior. At short distances, they divide the lattice matrix element by the zero-momentum matrix element (ratio scheme) to cancel logarithmic UV divergences; at large distances, they use self-renormalization with a renormalization factor \(Z_R\) fitted from zero-momentum lattice matrix elements at small lattice spacings. The renormalized quasi distribution amplitudes are then matched onto lightcone distribution amplitudes via a hard kernel derived up to next-to-leading order in \(\alpha_s\) including hybrid counterterms.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop perturbative zero-momentum matrix element in coordinate space is \(\hat{M}_{p}(z_1, z_2, 0, 0, \mu) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2 \pi} \left[ \frac{7}{8} L_1^{\text{UV}} + \frac{7}{8} L_2^{\text{UV}} + \frac{3}{4} L_{12}^{\text{UV}} + 4 \right]\), where \(L_i^{\text{UV}} = \ln\left(\frac{1}{4}\mu_{\text{UV}}^2 z_i^2 e^{2\gamma_E}\right)\) and \(L_{12}^{\text{UV}} = \ln\left(\frac{1}{4}\mu_{\text{UV}}^2 (z_1-z_2)^2 e^{2\gamma_E}\right)\).
* The one-loop hard kernel in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme is given by \(\mathcal{C}_{\rm \overline{MS}}(x_1, x_2, y_1, y_2, P^z, \mu) = \delta(x_1-y_1)\delta(x_2-y_2) + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{8\pi} \times [C_2 \delta(x_2-y_2) + C_3 \delta(x_3-y_3) + \{x_1 \leftrightarrow x_2, y_1 \leftrightarrow y_2\}]_{\oplus}\), with explicit piecewise expressions for \(C_2\) and \(C_3\) containing terms such as \(\frac{2 x_1 \ln \frac{4 x_1 (x_3-y_3) P_z^2}{\mu^2}}{y_1 (x_3-y_3)}\) and \(\frac{x_1 \ln \frac{4 x_1 x_3 P_z^2}{\mu^2}}{y_1 (y_1+y_3)}\) in the region \(0 < x_1 < y_1\).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid renormalization scheme, first proposed by Ji (2020) for quasi-parton distribution functions, to the case of baryon quasi distribution amplitudes, addressing the problem that the RI/MOM scheme introduces uncontrollable nonperturbative effects as shown by the Lattice Parton Collaboration (LPC) (2021). The method builds on the LaMET framework of Ji (2013, 2014) and the previous one-loop matching calculation for baryon quasi-DAs by Deng (2023), which used the RI/MOM scheme. The results enable a practical lattice QCD calculation of the full shape of lightcone distribution amplitudes for light baryons from first principles, overcoming the limitation of previous lattice studies (RQCD, 2019) that only extracted the first few moments.
