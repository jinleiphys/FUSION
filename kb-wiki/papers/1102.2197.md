# Baryons and baryonic matter in the large Nc and heavy quark limits

> This paper explores properties of baryons and finite density baryonic matter in an artificial world in which Nc, the number of colors, is large and the quarks of all species are degenerate and much larger than Λ_QCD. It has long been known that in large Nc QCD, baryons composed entirely of heavy quarks are accurately described in the mean-field approximation. However, the detailed properties of baryons in the combined large Nc and heavy quark limits have not been fully explored. Here some basic properties of baryons are computed using a variational approach. At leading order in both the large Nc and heavy quark expansions the baryon mass is computed explicitly as is the baryon form factor. Baryonic matter, the analog of nuclear matter in this artificial world, should also be well described in the mean-field approximation. In the special case where all baryons have an identical spin flavor structure, it is shown that in the formal heavy quark and large Nc limit interactions between baryons are strictly repulsive at low densities. The energy per baryon is computed in this limit and found to be exponentially small. It is shown that when the restriction to baryons with an identical spin-flavor structure is dropped, a phase of baryonic matter exists with a density of 2Nf times that for the restricted case but with the same energy (where Nf is the number of degenerate flavors). It is shown that this phase is at least metastable.

## Key claim

In the combined large \(N_c\) and heavy quark limits, baryons are accurately described by a mean-field approximation, and the baryon mass is given by \(M_{\text{baryon}} \approx N_c M_Q (1 - 0.05426 \, \tilde{\alpha}_s^2)\) at leading order, where \(\tilde{\alpha}_s \equiv N_c \alpha_s\). The authors show that baryonic matter with identical spin-flavor structure has strictly repulsive interactions at low densities, with an exponentially small energy per baryon, and that a distinct phase with density \(2 N_f\) times larger exists and is at least metastable.

## Method

The authors use a variational approach with a trial class of spherically symmetric wave functions constructed from s-wave harmonic oscillator eigenfunctions. They minimize the mean-field energy functional for a system of \(N_c\) heavy quarks interacting via a color-Coulomb potential, treating the quarks as bosons due to color antisymmetry. For baryonic matter, they extend the mean-field treatment to infinite matter, analyzing the low-density regime where interactions arise from the Pauli principle.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass at leading order: \(M_{\text{baryon}} \approx N_c M_Q \left(1 - 0.05426 \, \tilde{\alpha}_s^2\right)\), where \(\tilde{\alpha}_s \equiv N_c \alpha_s\).
- The binding energy term is proportional to \(\tilde{\alpha}_s^2 M_Q \frac{\mathcal{V}^2}{4\mathcal{T}}\), with \(\mathcal{T}\) and \(\mathcal{V}\) dimensionless functionals of the wave function shape.
- The density of the alternative baryonic matter phase is \(2 N_f\) times that of the restricted identical-spin-flavor phase, with the same energy per baryon.

## Context

This work extends Witten's (1979) mean-field analysis of baryons in large \(N_c\) QCD by numerically solving the variational equations for the first time, providing explicit results for the baryon mass and form factor. It also generalizes the treatment to cold baryonic matter, showing that at low densities the interactions are repulsive due to the Pauli principle, with an exponentially small energy per baryon. The results enable future studies of whether baryonic matter in this limit saturates (is self-bound) and provide a tractable regime for understanding nuclear matter from QCD, complementing studies of the 't Hooft model by Bringoltz and Narayanan (2008).
