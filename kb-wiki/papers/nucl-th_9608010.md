# Pion electromagnetic form factor at finite temperature

> Temperature effects on the electromagnetic couplings of pions in hot hadronic matter are studied with an effective chiral Lagrangian. We show that the Ward-Takahashi identity is satisfied at non-zero temperature in the soft pion limit. The in-medium electromagnetic form factor of the pion is obtained in the time-like region and shown to be reduced in magnitude, especially near the vector-meson resonance region. Finally, we discuss the consequences of this medium effect on dilepton production from hot hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The pion electromagnetic form factor at finite temperature is reduced in magnitude, especially near the vector-meson resonance region, due to the mixing of vector and axial-vector currents and the broadening of the rho meson in hot hadronic matter; the reduction is primarily a consequence of the suppression of the photon-rho-meson coupling and modifications to the rho meson self-energy, with the Ward-Takahashi identity satisfied at non-zero temperature in the soft pion limit.

## Method

The authors used an effective chiral Lagrangian based on hidden local symmetry (HLS) that includes pions, rho mesons, and photons, with the parameter choice a=2 to enforce vector meson dominance (VMD) at zero temperature. They calculated one-loop thermal corrections to the pion-photon vertex, the rho meson self-energy, the pion self-energy, and the direct pion-photon coupling in hot hadronic matter, treating the thermal bath as a pion gas and expanding in powers of T²/f_π² in the soft pion limit or using full propagators near the vector resonance region.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV (used as input parameter)
- The photon-rho-meson coupling modification at finite temperature: g_γρ(T) = (1 + ε) g_γρ(0), with ε = -T²/(12 f_π²) in the chiral limit
- The rho-pion-pion coupling at finite temperature: g_ρππ(T) = g_ρππ(0) × (1 - (5T²/(12 f_π²)) g_0(m_π²/T²)), where g_0 is a thermal integral function
- The direct coupling contribution to the form factor in the soft pion limit: F_π^direct = - (T²/(4 f_π²)) g_0(m_π²/T²)
- Pion wave function renormalization: Z_π^{-1} = 1 - (2T²/(3 f_π²)) g_0(m_π²/T²)
- The rho meson self-energy at finite temperature: Π_ρ(T) = (g²/(3π²)) ∫ (|l|² d|l| / (ω(e^{ω/T} - 1))) × (|l|² / (ω² - k₀²/4)), where ω = √(|l|² + m_π²)

## Context

This work extends the study of temperature effects on vector meson properties in hot hadronic matter, following the prediction by Dey, Eletsky, and Ioffe (1990) that up to T² there is no change in vector meson masses but only mixing between vector and axial-vector correlators. It is consistent with effective chiral Lagrangian approaches by Song (1993) and Pisarski (1994) that found vector meson masses do not change appreciably unless the temperature is very close to the critical temperature. The paper enables more accurate calculations of dilepton production rates from hot hadronic matter produced in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, where previous studies had assumed the pion form factor to be temperature-independent.
