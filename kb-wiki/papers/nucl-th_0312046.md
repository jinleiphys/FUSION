# Thermodynamic properties of spherical nuclei

> The effect of the residual interaction between the nucleons (quasiparticles) on the thermodynamic behavior of spherical nuclei is considered. Thermodynamic relations are derived for the simplest model of the residual interaction that has the necessary macroscopic structure previously established. The range of applicability of the theory is discussed.

## Key claim

The residual interaction between nucleons in spherical nuclei leads to a quadratic dependence of entropy on temperature (S ∝ T²), in contrast to the linear dependence (S ∝ T) characteristic of normal Fermi liquids and nonspherical nuclei, but this result is valid only at low temperatures where T is much smaller than the characteristic width of the diffuse region of the Fermi distribution (T ≪ δ̄ε) and, for nuclei near the phase transformation point, T ≪ T_C.

## Method

The author uses the Racah-Mottelson model for the residual interaction between quasiparticles on the same j level, deriving the degree of degeneracy Γ(s, Ω) of energy levels with seniority s and total angular momentum projection M. The entropy is calculated from the logarithm of the number of states, and the free energy is minimized with respect to the distribution functions for paired (b_j) and unpaired (s_j) quasiparticles, using a Lagrange multiplier for the fixed number of quasiparticles. The resulting distribution functions are then transformed into macroscopic variables (ρ, β̃) and integrated to obtain thermodynamic quantities such as entropy, energy, heat capacity, and moment of inertia.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic width of the diffuse region of the Fermi distribution δ̄ε ranges from 1.9 MeV (heavy nuclei) to 5.2 MeV (light nuclei) for neutron magic nuclei, and from 1.6 MeV (heavy nuclei) to 3.8 MeV (light nuclei) for proton magic nuclei.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the temperature fluctuations ΔT = T/√C = 1/√b amount to approximately 0.6 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at an excitation energy of 5 MeV, the theory gives J̃² ≈ 4, whereas the rigid-body moment of inertia with T ≈ 0.4 MeV gives J̃² ≈ 160, and the experimental value is J̃² ≈ 10.
- The entropy of a spherical nucleus is given by S = (b/2)T², where b = (π³/2)(R⁴m*²/ℏ⁴)(1/ρ_f^N g_N + 1/ρ_f^Z g_Z).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Kamchatnov (1975) and Nosov (1975) on the macroscopic structure of the residual interaction in spherical nuclei, particularly the quadratic dependence of the diffuse region width on orbital angular momentum (δϵ ∝ l²). The paper provides a thermodynamic framework for spherical nuclei that contrasts with the normal Fermi liquid theory of nonspherical nuclei, as described in standard references (Landau and Lifshitz, 1958; Lifshitz and Pitaevskii, 1978). The results enable calculation of level densities, moments of inertia, and angular momentum distributions for spherical nuclei, and suggest a possible mechanism for the phase transformation from the magic (spherical) phase to the normal (nonspherical) phase as temperature increases.
