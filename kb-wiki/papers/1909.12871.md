# Radiative quark $p_\perp$-broadening in a quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC energies

> We study the radiative correction to $p_\perp$-broadening of a fast quark in a quark-gluon plasma beyond the soft gluon approximation. We find that the radiative contribution to quark $\langle p_\perp^2\rangle$ for RHIC and LHC conditions is negative.

# Radiative quark \(p_\perp\)-broadening in a quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC energies

## Key claim
The radiative correction to the mean squared transverse momentum \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle\) of a fast quark in a quark-gluon plasma is negative for RHIC and LHC conditions, contradicting earlier expectations of a large positive contribution. This result is obtained beyond the soft gluon approximation and includes contributions from virtual processes that were omitted in prior work.

## Method
The analysis uses the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to compute the radiative contribution to \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle\) from the \(q\to qg\) process, including both real gluon emission and the virtual \(q\to qg\to q\) process. The calculation goes beyond the soft gluon and logarithmic approximations by using an oscillator form for the dipole cross section \(\sigma_{q\bar{q}}(\rho)=C\rho^2\) with \(C=\hat{q}/2n\), and by separating the medium-induced contribution from the vacuum decay via a decomposition of the Green function. The transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) is adjusted to reproduce the quark energy loss from a running-coupling model, and the cutoff for the \(\Delta z\) integral is set at \(\Delta z_{min}=1/m\) with \(m=300\) MeV.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC conditions at \(E=30\) GeV: \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{rad}/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0} \approx -0.598\) with \(r=1.94\), where \(r=\hat{q}'/\hat{q}\) is the ratio of transport coefficients for Glauber factors versus Green functions.
- For LHC conditions at \(E=100\) GeV: \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{rad}/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0} \approx -0.629\) with \(r=2.13\).
- With \(r=1\) (ignoring energy dependence of \(\hat{q}\)): \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{rad}/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0} \approx -0.397\) for RHIC and \(-0.192\) for LHC.
- The individual contributions for RHIC: \([I_1,I_2,I_3]/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0}\approx [0.417/r,-0.213,-0.601]\).
- The individual contributions for LHC: \([I_1,I_2,I_3]/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0}\approx [0.823/r,-0.107,-0.908]\).
- Adjusted transport coefficients: \(\hat{q}\approx 0.12\) GeV\(^3\) at \(E=30\) GeV for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV; \(\hat{q}\approx 0.14\) GeV\(^3\) at \(E=100\) GeV for Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV.
- Fixed parameters: \(\alpha_s=1/3\), \(L=5\) fm, quasiparticle masses \(m_q=300\) MeV and \(m_g=400\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of radiative \(p_\perp\)-broadening beyond the soft gluon approximation used by Mueller (2000) and others, who found a large positive double-logarithmic contribution \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{rad}\sim (\alpha_s N_c \hat{q} L/\pi)\ln^2(L/l_0)\). The present calculation includes the \(I_2\) and \(I_3\) terms from virtual processes that were omitted in Mueller (2000), and finds that these terms are negative and dominate, leading to an overall negative radiative contribution. The result is consistent with the STAR measurement of hadron-jet correlations showing no evidence for large-angle jet scattering in the medium. The approach enables more realistic modeling of jet quenching by incorporating radiative corrections to transverse momentum broadening beyond leading order.
