# The Two-Body Random Ensemble in Nuclei

> Combining analytical and numerical methods, we investigate properties of the two-body random ensemble (TBRE). We compare the TBRE with the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble of random matrices. Using the geometric properties of the nuclear shell model, we discuss the information content of nuclear spectra, and gain insight in the difficulties encountered when fitting the effective interaction. We exhibit the existence of correlations between spectral widths pertaining to different quantum numbers. Using these results, we deduce the preponderance of spin-zero ground states in the TBRE. We demonstrate the existence of correlations between spectra with different quantum numbers and/or in different nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) is fundamentally different from the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) in information content, and that the preponderance of spin-zero ground states in the TBRE can be understood through the geometric structure of the shell model, specifically the eigenvalues of the correlation matrix of the two-body interaction operators.

## Method

The authors combine analytical group-theoretical arguments with numerical diagonalization of the matrices \(S_{\alpha\beta}(\mathbf{J}) = d^{-1}(\mathbf{J})\,\mathrm{Trace}[C(\mathbf{J},\alpha)C(\mathbf{J},\beta)]\) for the \(sd\)-shell nuclei \({}^{20}\)Ne and \({}^{24}\)Mg (isospin \(T=0\)) and for a single \(j=19/2\) shell with \(m=6,7,8\) identical nucleons. They diagonalize these matrices to obtain eigenvalues \(s^2_\alpha(\mathbf{J})\) and the corresponding orthonormal basis matrices \(B_{\mu\nu}(\mathbf{J},\alpha)\), then analyze the largest eigenvalue and its associated eigenvector (the monopole operator) as a function of total spin \(J\) and nucleon number.

## Key numbers

* For \(m=6\) identical nucleons in a single \(j=19/2\) shell, the largest eigenvalue root \(s_1(J)\) is approximately 6.3 (analytical estimate) and changes very slowly with \(J\), increasing almost monotonically with increasing \(J\).
* For \(m=8\) identical nucleons in a single \(j=19/2\) shell, the analytical estimate gives \(s_1 \approx 11.4\).
* For the single \(j=19/2\) shell with \(m=6\) and \(m=8\), the overlap function of the leading eigenvector between different \(J\) values remains larger than 0.8 even for \(|J-J'| = 20\).
* For the single \(j=19/2\) shell, the overlap function of the leading eigenvector between different nucleon numbers (\(m=6\) and \(m=8\)) is larger than 0.995 for all \(J\) values except for pairs involving \(m=6, J=1\).
* For the \(sd\)-shell nucleus \({}^{20}\)Ne, the largest eigenvalue root \(s_1(J)\) is not as dominant over the other roots as in the single \(j\)-shell or in \({}^{24}\)Mg.
* For the \(sd\)-shell nucleus \({}^{24}\)Mg, the largest eigenvalue root \(s_1(J)\) shows a clear separation from the remaining roots, similar to the single \(j\)-shell case.
* For each spin value \(J\), at least one eigenvalue \(s^2_\alpha(\mathbf{J})\) vanishes identically; the corresponding operator is \(\hat{J}^2 - J(J+1)\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of the two-body random ensemble (TBRE), which was previously studied numerically by French and Wong (1970) and Bohigas and Flores (1971) who showed that TBRE spectral fluctuations agree with GOE predictions. The paper builds on the geometric approach introduced by Papenbrock and Weidenmueller (2004, 2005) and provides a framework to understand the preponderance of spin-zero ground states discovered by Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998). The results enable a systematic identification of which linear combinations of two-body matrix elements are best determined by experimental data, addressing practical difficulties encountered in fitting effective interactions as noted by Honma et al. (2002, 2004) for the \(fp\)-shell.
