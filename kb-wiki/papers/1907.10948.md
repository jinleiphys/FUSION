# Primordial fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We present a simple description of the energy density profile created in a nucleus-nucleus collision, motivated by high-energy QCD. The energy density is modeled as the sum of contributions coming from elementary collisions between localized charges and a smooth nucleus. Each of these interactions creates a sharply-peaked source of energy density falling off at large distances like $1/r^2$, corresponding to the two-dimensional Coulomb field of a point charge. Our model reproduces the one-point and two-point functions of the energy density field calculated in the framework of the color glass condensate effective theory, to leading logarithmic accuracy. We apply it to the description of eccentricity fluctuations. Unlike other existing models of initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions, it allows us to reproduce simultaneously the centrality dependence of elliptic and triangular flow.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple model of event-by-event energy density fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions that reproduces the one-point and two-point functions of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory to leading logarithmic accuracy, and unlike other existing models, it simultaneously reproduces the centrality dependence of elliptic and triangular flow.

## Method

The energy density is modeled as the sum of contributions from elementary collisions between localized color charges (sources) and a smooth nucleus, where each source creates a sharply-peaked energy density profile falling off like \(1/r^2\) at large distances (a two-dimensional Coulomb field). The positions of sources are sampled randomly over the nuclear area according to a density proportional to \(Q_A^2/\ln(1+Q_A^2/m^2)\), and the source profile is given by \(\Delta_A(\mathbf{r}) = (8/g^2 N_c) \cdot 1/(|\mathbf{r}|^2 + Q_A^{-2})\) for \(|\mathbf{r}| < 1/m\) and zero otherwise. The model is implemented in a Monte Carlo code named \texttt{magma}, and anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) are computed from the initial eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) assuming linear response \(v_n \simeq \kappa_n \varepsilon_n\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum at the center of the nucleus: \(Q_{s0} = 1.24\) GeV.
- Infrared cutoff: \(m = 0.14\) GeV.
- Nuclear radius parameter for \(^{208}\)Pb: \(R = 6.62\) fm.
- Coupling constant: \(g^2 = \pi\), corresponding to \(\alpha_s = 0.25\).
- Mean energy density at the center of a central Pb+Pb collision at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV: \(131\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The model reproduces experimental data on \(v_2\{2\}\), \(v_2\{4\}\), and \(v_3\{2\}\) from the ATLAS Collaboration in 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based analytical calculations of Albacete et al. (2018) and Giacalone et al. (2019) by providing a simple Monte Carlo implementation that captures the same leading-logarithmic behavior of the energy density two-point function. It differs from the IP-Glasma model of Schenke et al. (2012), which samples nucleon positions and evaluates saturation momentum locally, by instead sampling sources directly within each nucleus. The model enables fast and robust initial conditions for pre-equilibrium and hydrodynamic studies, and it naturally reproduces the centrality dependence of the ratio \(v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\}\), which is typically missed by hydrodynamic calculations (Alba et al., 2017).
