# Coexisting partial dynamical symmetries and multiple shapes

> We present an algebraic procedure for constructing Hamiltonians with several distinct partial dynamical symmetries (PDSs), of relevance to shape-coexistence phenomena. The procedure relies on a spectrum generating algebra encompassing several dynamical symmetry (DS) chains and a coherent state which assigns a particular shape to each chain. The PDS Hamiltonian maintains the DS solvability and quantum numbers in selected bands, associated with each shape, and mixes other states. The procedure is demonstrated for a variety of multiple quadrupole shapes in the framework of the interacting boson model of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algebraic procedure for constructing Hamiltonians that exhibit several distinct partial dynamical symmetries (PDSs) simultaneously, enabling the description of shape coexistence in nuclei where the abstract's claim of "relevance to shape-coexistence phenomena" is qualified by the requirement that the multiple minima in the energy surface be near-degenerate at the critical point.

## Method

The authors construct Hamiltonians using an intrinsic-collective resolution, where the intrinsic part annihilates selected basis states of specific irreducible representations of incompatible dynamical symmetry chains (e.g., U(5), SU(3), \(\overline{\text{SU(3)}}\), SO(6)), and the collective part consists of Casimir operators of common sub-algebras. The procedure is demonstrated within the interacting boson model (IBM) for multiple quadrupole shapes, requiring the intrinsic Hamiltonian to simultaneously satisfy zero-energy conditions for states belonging to different symmetry chains.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical and \(\gamma\)-unstable deformed (S-G) coexistence Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}'\) with \(r_2=10\), \(\rho_5=\rho_3=0\), and \(N=20\), the energy surface \(\tilde{E}(\beta) = r_2\beta^2(\beta^2-1)^2 (1+\beta^2)^{-3}\) has degenerate global minima at \(\beta=0\) and \(\beta^2=1\) with \(\tilde{E}=0\), and a local maximum at \(\beta_*^2=1/5\) with barrier height \(\tilde{E}=2r_2/27\).
- For large \(N\), the normal mode frequencies about the deformed and spherical minima are \(\epsilon_\beta = 2r_2 N^2\) and \(\epsilon = r_2 N^2\), respectively.
- The \(B(E2)\) value for the transition from the pure spherical state \((n_d=1, L=2)\) to \((n_d=0, L=0)\) satisfies \(B(E2) = e_B^2 N\).
- The \(B(E2)\) values for intraband transitions in the deformed ground band follow the SO(6)-DS expressions: \(B(E2; g, \tau+1, L'=2\tau+2 \to g, \tau, L=2\tau) = e_B^2 \frac{\tau+1}{2\tau+5} (N-\tau)(N+\tau+4)\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS), previously developed by Leviatan (2011) and Alhassid (1992), from single-shape systems to multiple-shape coexistence, building on earlier studies of quantum phase transitions by Leviatan (2007) and Macek (2014). The procedure enables algebraic benchmarks for shape coexistence phenomena observed in nuclei such as neutron-rich Sr isotopes, \(^{96}\)Zr, Kr, Se, and Hg isotopes, and provides analytic expressions for spectroscopic observables that can be tested against experimental data.
