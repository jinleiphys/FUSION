# Addendum: Triton and hypertriton binding energies calculated from SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interactions

> Previously we calculated the binding energies of the triton and hypertriton, using an SU_6 quark-model interaction derived from a resonating-group method of two baryon clusters. In contrast to the previous calculations employing the energy-dependent interaction kernel, we present new results using a renormalized interaction, which is now energy independent and reserves all the two-baryon data. The new binding energies are slightly smaller than the previous values. In particular the triton binding energy turns out to be 8.14 MeV with a charge-dependence correction of the two-nucleon force, 190 keV, being included. This indicates that about 350 keV is left for the energy which is to be accounted for by three-body forces.

## Key claim
Using a renormalized, energy-independent interaction kernel derived from the SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2, the triton binding energy is 8.326 MeV, which is 193 keV less bound than the previous energy-dependent calculation and leaves about 350 keV to be accounted for by three-body forces.

## Method
The authors replaced the previous energy-dependent resonating-group method (RGM) kernel with an energy-independent renormalized RGM kernel, constructed by eliminating the energy dependence through a standard procedure involving the exchange normalization kernel and a Pauli projection operator. They solved the three-cluster Faddeev equations in the Pauli-allowed model space for the triton (up to 50 channels with total angular momentum J <= 6) and hypertriton (up to 150 channels with J <= 6), using the fss2 and FSS quark-model interactions.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy with fss2: 8.326 MeV (50-channel calculation, J <= 6), which is 156 keV above the experimental value of 8.482 MeV
- Charge-dependence correction of the two-nucleon force: 190 keV
- Remaining energy to be accounted for by three-body forces: 346 keV (approximately 350 keV)
- Lambda separation energy of hypertriton with fss2: 262 keV (150-channel calculation, J <= 6), compared to the experimental value of 130 +/- 50 keV
- Lambda separation energy with modified fss2 (kappa meson mass increased from 936 MeV to 995 MeV): 134 keV
- Sigma NN admixture probability in hypertriton with fss2: 0.83%
- Sigma NN admixture probability in hypertriton with FSS: 1.43%

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations using energy-dependent RGM kernels (Fujiwara et al., 2004; 2005) by applying a renormalization procedure previously used for the 3-alpha system (Kurokawa and Kamimura, 2005). The new results show that the quark-model interactions fss2 and FSS predict less binding than standard meson-exchange potentials such as CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, and AV18 (Wiringa et al., 1995; Machleidt, 2001; Stoks et al., 1994), with the missing three-body force contribution being about half of that predicted by meson-exchange models (Nogga et al., 2000). The hypertriton results enable future refinement of the Lambda-N interaction, particularly by adjusting the kappa meson mass to match the experimental separation energy.
