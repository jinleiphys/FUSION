# Exploring Origins for Correlations between Flow Harmonics and Transverse Momentum in Small Collision Systems (Unambiguous Ambiguity)

> High statistics data sets from experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) with small and large collision species have enabled a wealth of new flow measurements, including the event-by-event correlation between observables. One exciting such observable $ρ(v^{2}_{n},[p_{T}])$ gauges the correlation between the mean transverse momentum of particles in an event and the various flow coefficients ($v_n$) in the same event [1]. Recently it has been proposed that very low multiplicity events may be sensitive to initial-state glasma correlations [2] rather than flow-related dynamics. We find utilizing the IP-JAZMA framework that the color domain explanation for the glasma results are incomplete. We then explore predictions from PYTHIA-8, and the version for including nuclear collisions called PYTHIA-ANGANTYR, which have only non-flow correlations and the AMPT model which has both non-flow and flow-type correlations. We find that PYTHIA-ANGANTYR has non-flow contributions to $ρ(v^{2}_{n},[p_{T}])$ in p+O, p+Pb, O+O collisions that are positive at low multiplicity and comparable to the glasma correlations. It is striking that in PYTHIA-8 in p+p collisions there is actually a sign-change from positive to negative $ρ(v^{2}_{n},[p_{T}])$ as a function of multiplicity. The AMPT results match the experimental data general trends in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, except at low multiplicity where AMPT has the opposite sign. In p+Pb collisions, AMPT has the opposite sign from experimental data and we explore this within the context of parton geometry. Predictions for p+O, O+O, and Xe+Xe are also presented.

## Key claim

The authors find that non-flow correlations from PYTHIA-8 and PYTHIA-ANGANTYR produce positive $\rho(v^{2}_{n},[p_{T}])$ values in p+O, p+Pb, and O+O collisions at low multiplicity that are comparable in magnitude to predicted glasma correlations, meaning the glasma color domain explanation is incomplete and non-flow effects must be fully accounted for before claiming evidence of initial-state correlations.

## Method

The authors ran millions of events using the PYTHIA-8 and PYTHIA-ANGANTYR models (which contain only non-flow correlations) and the AMPT model (which contains both non-flow and flow-type correlations) for multiple collision systems at various energies. They applied the three-subevent method with pseudorapidity regions $-2.5 < \eta_a < -0.75$, $|\eta_b| < 0.5$, and $0.75 < \eta_c < 2.5$ to calculate $\rho(v^{2}_{n},[p_{T}])$ using charged particles with $0.3 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV, with event multiplicity defined by $|\eta| < 2.5$ and $0.5 < p_T < 5.0$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- In PYTHIA-8 for p+p collisions at 5 TeV and 13 TeV, $\rho(v^{2}_{2},[p_{T}])$ is positive for $N_{\rm ch} < 30$ and changes sign to negative for $N_{\rm ch} > 30$
- PYTHIA-ANGANTYR results for p+O, p+Pb, and O+O collisions show $\rho$ correlations that are positive for all $N_{\rm ch}$ and around 10%
- AMPT results for Pb+Pb at 5 TeV match ATLAS data qualitatively but are approximately a factor of two higher in magnitude
- In p+Pb collisions at 5 TeV, AMPT gives positive $\rho$ values while ATLAS data show consistently negative $\rho$ values
- AMPT predictions for O+O at 200 GeV (RHIC) and 5 TeV (LHC) show positive $\rho$ correlator with very similar shape and magnitude after rescaling the $x$-axis

## Context

This work extends studies of the $\rho(v^{2}_{n},[p_{T}])$ correlator first measured by ATLAS (Aad, 2019) in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions, and directly addresses the proposal by Giacalone (2020) that glasma correlations would produce a sign change at very low multiplicities ($dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta < 10$). The authors show that PYTHIA-ANGANTYR non-flow correlations produce similar positive signals, contradicting the claim that such a sign change would be definitive evidence for glasma correlations. The results enable future experimental comparisons in p+O, O+O, and Xe+Xe collisions to disentangle non-flow, flow, and glasma contributions.
