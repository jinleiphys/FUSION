# Probing vortical structures in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC-BES energies through helicity polarization

> We investigate the hydrodynamic helicity polarization of $Λ$ hyperons, defined as the projection of the spin polarization vector along the directions of particle momenta, at RHIC-BES energies by utilizing the relativistic (3+1)D CLVisc hydrodynamics framework with SMASH initial conditions. As opposed to local spin polarization at high energy collisions, our hydrodynamic simulations demonstrate that the helicity polarization induced by the kinetic vorticity dominates over other contributions at intermediate and low collision energies. Our findings provide an opportunity to probe the fine structure of local kinetic vorticity as a function of azimuthal angle at intermediate and low collision energies by mapping our predictions to the future measurements in experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that at intermediate and low RHIC-BES collision energies (7.7, 19.6, and 39 GeV), the helicity polarization of Lambda hyperons induced by kinetic vorticity dominates over contributions from shear viscous tensor, fluid acceleration, and gradients of baryon chemical potential by approximately one order of magnitude, with the other contributions nearly canceling each other out. This dominance persists regardless of initial conditions (SMASH or AMPT) and baryon diffusion coefficient values.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic (3+1)D hydrodynamic simulations using the CLVisc framework with SMASH initial conditions to model Au+Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies. They computed the helicity polarization of Lambda hyperons from the freeze-out hypersurface by evaluating the spin polarization vector from chiral kinetic theory up to order hbar, decomposing it into contributions from thermal vorticity (further split into kinetic vorticity and temperature gradient terms), shear viscous tensor, fluid acceleration minus temperature gradient, and gradient of baryon chemical potential over temperature. The helicity polarization was then projected as a function of azimuthal angle and integrated over transverse momentum 0.5-3 GeV and rapidity -1 to 1.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV Au+Au collisions in 20-50% centrality, the magnitude of helicity polarization induced by kinetic vorticity (P_H^omega) is approximately 10 times greater than that induced by other sources (shear, acceleration, chemical potential gradient, temperature gradient).
- The helicity polarization induced by kinetic vorticity dominates at all three studied collision energies: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 19.6, and 39 GeV.
- The first Fourier sine coefficient of helicity polarization, <P_H sin phi_p>, increases with growing transverse momentum p_T in the range 0.5-3 GeV at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV.
- The dominance of kinetic vorticity-induced helicity polarization holds for both SMASH and AMPT initial conditions, and for baryon diffusion coefficient values C_B = 0 and C_B = 1.2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Yi et al. (2021) on helicity polarization at 200 GeV to lower RHIC-BES energies, where the authors previously found that thermal vorticity dominates helicity polarization at high energies. The study addresses the challenge that local spin polarization measurements at low energies are complicated by multiple contributing sources beyond thermal vorticity, as noted in prior work by Fu et al. (2021), Becattini et al. (2021), and Ryu et al. (2021). The findings enable a new approach to probe the fine structure of kinetic vorticity as a function of azimuthal angle in low-energy collisions, and provide a baseline for investigating local parity violation through helicity polarization correlations as proposed by Becattini et al. (2020) and Gao et al. (2021).
