# A new insight into neutrino energy loss by electron capture of iron group nuclei in magnetars surface

> Based on the relativistic mean-field effective interactions theory, and Lai dong model \citep{b37, b38, b39}, we discuss the influences of superstrong magnetic fields (SMFs) on electron Fermi energy, nuclear blinding energy, and single-particle level structure in magnetars surface. By using the method of Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC), and the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) theory, we detailed analyze the neutrino energy loss rates(NELRs) by electron capture (EC) for iron group nuclei in SMFs.

## Key claim

The paper claims that superstrong magnetic fields (SMFs) significantly alter neutrino energy loss rates (NELRs) by electron capture for iron group nuclei in magnetar surfaces, but the effect is strongly conditional: at fields below \(B_{12} < 100\) and low temperature (\(T_9 = 0.233\)) the influence is slight, while at high temperature (\(T_9 = 15.53\)) the NELRs increase by more than four orders of magnitude; at fields above \(B_{12} > 100\) the NELRs decrease by more than three orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors combined the relativistic mean-field effective interactions theory with the Lai-Dong model to compute electron Fermi energy, nuclear binding energy, and single-particle level structure in superstrong magnetic fields. They then applied the Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method to obtain Gamow-Teller strength distributions and the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) theory to calculate neutrino energy loss rates by electron capture for iron group nuclei (isotopes of Fe, Co, Ni) in the presence of superstrong magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- For \(B_{12} < 100\) at \(T_9 = 15.53\), the NELRs for most iron group nuclei (e.g., \(^{52-61}\)Fe, \(^{55-60}\)Co, \(^{56-63}\)Ni) increase by more than four orders of magnitude.
- For \(B_{12} > 100\) at \(T_9 = 15.53\), the NELRs decrease by more than three orders of magnitude.
- For \(\rho_7 \leq 10^3\) at a fixed magnetic field and temperature, the NELRs increase by more than four orders of magnitude; for \(\rho_7 > 10^3\), density has almost no influence on NELRs.
- At density \(\rho_7 = 10^2\) and \(B_{12} \geq 10^{3.5}\), there is an abrupt increase in NELRs.
- Without SMFs at low temperature (\(T_9 = 1\)), the authors' rates are about five orders of magnitude lower than those of FFN and NKK.
- Without SMFs at high temperature (\(T_9 = 3\)), the authors' results agree with NKK but are about one order of magnitude lower than FFN.
- With SMFs, the authors' NELRs for some iron group nuclei can be about five orders of magnitude higher than those of FFN and NKK.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of electron capture and neutrino energy loss rates by FFN (Fuller, Fowler, and Newman) and NKK (Nabi, Klapdor-Kleingrothaus, and collaborators), which did not consider superstrong magnetic fields. It also differs from the Brink-Hypothesis-based approach of earlier studies (e.g., Liu and collaborators, 2014-2015) by using the SMMC method and RPA theory, which treat nuclear temperature exactly and avoid the crude approximation that Gamow-Teller strength on excited states is identical to that on the ground state. The results are intended to inform models of thermal and magnetic evolution, cooling mechanisms, and numerical simulations of neutron stars and magnetars.
