# Entropy, Quantum Decoherence and Pointer States in Scalar ``Parton'' Fields

> Entropy arises in strong interactions by a dynamical separation of ``partons'' from unobservable ``environment'' modes due to confinement. For interacting scalar fields we calculate the statistical entropy of the observable subsystem. Diagonalizing its functional density matrix yields field pointer states and their probabilities in terms of Wightman functions. It also indicates how to calculate a finite geometric entropy proportional to a surface area.

# Entropy, Quantum Decoherence and Pointer States in Scalar "Parton" Fields

## Key claim
Entropy in strong interactions arises from environment-induced quantum decoherence due to confinement, where almost constant QCD field configurations form an unobservable environment that interacts with the observable parton subsystem. The claim is conditional on the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approximation for interacting scalar fields, which preserves a Gaussian structure of the wave functionals.

## Method
The author models a system of two coupled real scalar fields representing partons and their non-perturbative environment, using the functional Schrodinger picture with Dirac's time-dependent variational principle to derive the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) effective action. The functional density submatrix for the observable parton subsystem is obtained by tracing over the unobservable environment degrees of freedom, and the von Neumann statistical entropy is calculated by diagonalizing this density matrix to obtain field pointer states and their eigenvalues. The "replica trick" is employed to compute the statistical entropy from the eigenvalues of the density matrix, which are expressed in terms of Wightman functions (two-point correlation functions) of the fields.

## Key numbers
- The short time scale for non-perturbative gluonic environment interactions is given as much less than 1 fm/c.
- The high energy density threshold for thermalization is given as much greater than 1 GeV/fm^3.
- The geometric entropy is found to be proportional to a surface area A divided by the square of a short-distance cutoff L, with the approximate form S(t) proportional to -(A/L^2) * Tr_L ln((1-G_1 G_12 G_2 G_12)/(1+G_1 Sigma_12 G_2 Sigma_12)).
- The linear entropy lower bound for the statistical entropy is given by S(t) >= -(1/2) Tr ln((1-G_1 G_12 G_2 G_12)/(1+G_1 Sigma_12 G_2 Sigma_12)).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous analysis (Elze, 1994) of the entropy puzzle in high-multiplicity strong interaction events, which dates back to Fermi and Landau. It builds on analogies with the quantum measurement process and environment-induced decoherence (Zurek, 1981) and related problems in quantum cosmology and string theory (Ellis, Hagelin, Nanopoulos, and Srednicki, 1984). The Feynman-Vernon influence functional technique for quantum Brownian motion (Caldeira and Leggett, 1983) previously showed that in the short-time strong-coupling limit, a model parton behaves like a classical particle with friction and localization. The results generalize Srednicki's (1993) work on geometric entropy and can be applied to the moving mirror model approximating thermal entropy outside a black hole, as well as to parton-model applications to complex hadronic or nuclear reactions.
