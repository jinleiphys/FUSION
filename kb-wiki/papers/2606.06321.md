# Equilibrium state of a Fermi system in the diffusion approximation of kinetic theory

> A consistent transformation from momentum space to energy space is performed for the diffusion equation within kinetic theory, with particle-number conservation explicitly preserved. Under the assumption of a constant single-particle level density, the equation reduces to a one-dimensional diffusion equation in energy space with consistent kinetic coefficients. The equivalence of the definitions of the equilibrium temperature in momentum space and in energy space is demonstrated. It is established that the inclusion of the energy dependence of the kinetic coefficients leads to an energy-dependent equilibrium temperature and a modification of the distribution function. The obtained results may be used to analyze relaxation processes in atomic nuclei and nonequilibrium dynamics of Fermi systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the diffusion equation in momentum space can be consistently transformed into a one-dimensional diffusion equation in energy space while preserving particle-number conservation, and that the equilibrium temperature definitions in both spaces are equivalent. However, this equivalence holds only under the assumption of a constant single-particle level density near the Fermi surface, and the inclusion of energy-dependent kinetic coefficients leads to an energy-dependent equilibrium temperature and a modified distribution function.

## Method

The authors start from the nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation in momentum space for a spherically symmetric Fermi system modeling an atomic nucleus in the infinite nuclear matter approximation. They perform a coordinate transformation from momentum to energy space using the single-particle level density and group velocity, explicitly preserving particle-number conservation. Under the approximation of constant single-particle level density near the Fermi surface, the equation reduces to a one-dimensional diffusion equation in energy space with transformed kinetic coefficients. The equilibrium temperature is derived from the stationarity condition in both momentum and energy spaces, and the equivalence is verified algebraically.

## Key numbers

- Diffusion coefficient at the Fermi surface at zero temperature: \(D_{p}^{(0)}(p_F) \approx 3.38 \times 10^{-22}\ \text{MeV}^{2}\,\text{fm}^{-2}\,\text{s}\)
- Drift coefficient at the Fermi surface at zero temperature: \(K_{p}^{(0)}(p_F) \approx -2.76 \times 10^{-23}\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-2}\,\text{s}\)
- Equilibrium temperature from constant-coefficient approximation using these values: \(T_\text{eq}\approx 12~\text{MeV}\), approximately three times larger than the phenomenological estimate of \(T_\text{eq}=4\)~MeV from Wo (1982) and BaWo (2019)
- Critical temperature of nuclear matter: \(T_\text{C}=18\)~MeV from RaPeLa (1983)
- Fermi energy for step distribution at density \(\rho=0.16\,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): \(\epsilon_F \approx 37.1\)~MeV; for Fermi distribution at \(T=4\)~MeV: \(\epsilon_F \approx 36.8\)~MeV; at \(T=8\)~MeV: \(\epsilon_F \approx 35.6\)~MeV
- Nucleon-nucleon interaction parameters: \(r_0 = 0.8\)~fm, \(v_0 = -33\)~MeV, yielding in-medium total cross section \(\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}} \simeq 20~\textrm{mb}\)

## Context

This work extends the diffusion-approximation approach developed by KoLu (2014, 2015) and Lu (2023) for momentum-space kinetic equations to an energy-space formulation, which is more natural for problems involving the single-particle level density near the Fermi surface. The transformation resolves an apparent ambiguity in the definition of equilibrium temperature between momentum and energy spaces, demonstrating their equivalence. The results enable analysis of relaxation processes in atomic nuclei and nonequilibrium dynamics of Fermi systems, particularly relevant to studies of nuclear structure and heavy-ion collision dynamics as discussed by No (1974), Wo (1982), and BaWo (2019).
