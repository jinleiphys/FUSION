# Production of Heavy Clusters with an Expanded Coalescence Model in CEM

> The production of heavy clusters in nuclear reactions is important in a wide variety of applications: radiation shielding, space engineering and design, medical physics, accelerator design, and more. According to the Cascade Exciton Model (CEM), there are three ways high-energy heavy clusters can be produced. The first way is via coalescence of nucleons produced in the IntraNuclear Cascade (INC). The second way is via the preequilibrium model. The last way is via Fermi breakup. Previous work in CEM examines the impact of expansions of the preequilibrium model and Fermi breakup model on heavy cluster production. The present work studies the impact of expanding the coalescence model on heavy cluster spectra. CEM03.03, the default event generator in the Monte Carlo N-Particle transport code version 6 (MCNP6) for intermediate-energy nuclear reactions, is capable of producing light fragments up to He4 in its coalescence model. In the present study, we have expanded the coalescence model to be able to produce up to Be7. Preliminary results are promising.

## Key claim

Expanding the coalescence model in CEM to produce clusters up to Be7 (instead of only up to He4) yields increased production of heavy clusters in spallation reactions and improves agreement with experimental data, but the results are described as "preliminary" and the paper presents only a few example comparisons.

## Method

The authors extended the coalescence model in CEM03.03 (the default event generator in MCNP6) to allow formation of He6, Li6, Li7, and Be7 from cascade nucleons and preexisting coalesced groups (pairs, tritons, He3, and He4) outside the target nucleus. They defined new coalescence momentum parameters p_c(LF) for these heavy clusters and increased the p_c for He4 to compensate for alpha particles lost to heavy-cluster formation. The expanded model was tested by comparing simulated fragment production spectra with experimental data for proton-induced reactions on Au and Ag targets.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence radii for incident energies below 300 MeV or above 1000 MeV: p_c(d) = 90 MeV/c, p_c(t) = p_c(He3) = 108 MeV/c, p_c(He4) = 130 MeV/c, p_c(LF) = 175 MeV/c.
- Coalescence radii for incident energies between 300 MeV and 1000 MeV: p_c(d) = 150 MeV/c, p_c(t) = p_c(He3) = 175 MeV/c, p_c(He4) = 205 MeV/c, p_c(LF) = 250 MeV/c.
- The expansion requires an insignificant amount (2-3 percent) of increased computation time.
- Comparisons are shown for the reaction 1200 MeV p + Au197 producing He6 and Li6, and for 480 MeV p + natAg producing Li7, Be7, and Li6.

## Context

This work extends prior expansions of the preequilibrium model and Fermi breakup model in CEM (Kerby and Mashnik, previous works) by now expanding the coalescence model. It builds on the coalescence approach of Toneev (1983) and KKG+Schulz (1983) used in CEM03.03, which previously produced only fragments up to He4. The expanded model is part of a larger project to improve light-fragment production in spallation reactions, and the authors plan to implement these upgrades into MCNP6. The approach differs from the Liege intranuclear cascade (INCL) coalescence, which coalesces nucleons inside the nucleus rather than outside.
