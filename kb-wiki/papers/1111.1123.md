# The Schwinger Model in Point Form

> We attempt to solve the Schwinger model, i.e. massless QED in 1+1 dimensions, by quantizing it on a space-time hyperboloid x_μx^μ=τ^2. The Fock-space representation of the 2-momentum operator is derived and its algebraic structure is analyzed. We briefly outline a solution strategy.

## Key claim

The authors attempt to solve the Schwinger model (massless QED in 1+1 dimensions) by quantizing on a space-time hyperboloid (point form), but they only derive the Fock-space representation of the 2-momentum operator and outline a solution strategy; they do not actually solve the model or present a spectrum.

## Method

The Dirac field and photon field are Fourier-expanded in plane waves on the hyperboloid \(x_\mu x^\mu = \tau^2\), and the free fermion and photon 2-momentum operators are obtained by integrating the stress-energy tensor over the hyperboloid surface element. The interaction part of the 2-momentum operator is derived and expressed in terms of fermion bilinears and photon creation/annihilation operators, using the covariant distribution \(W_\nu(q)\) to evaluate the surface integrals. The resulting eigenvalue problem for the total 2-momentum operator is written down, and a solution strategy is proposed that involves compactifying the spatial direction to a deSitter space and truncating the mode expansion to a finite number of modes.

## Key numbers

- The free fermion 2-momentum operator in point form is \(P^\mu_e = \int \frac{dp^1}{2p^0} p^\mu \bigl(c^\dagger(p)c(p) + d^\dagger(p)d(p)\bigr)\), identical to the instant-form result.
- The free photon 2-momentum operator in point form is \(P^\mu_\gamma = \sum_{\kappa=0}^1 \int \frac{dk^1}{2k^0} k^\mu g^{\kappa\kappa} a^\dagger_\kappa(k) a_\kappa(k)\), identical to the equal-time quantization result.
- The interaction part of the 2-momentum operator is given by \(P^\mu_{\mathrm{int}} = -e \sum_{\kappa=0}^1 \int \frac{dk^1}{2k^0} \bigl(\mathcal{A}(X_{\kappa}^\mu)(k) a_{\kappa}(k) + \mathcal{A}^\dagger(X_{\kappa}^\mu)(k) a^\dagger_{\kappa}(k)\bigr)\).

## Context

This work extends the point-form quantization of free field theories on the hyperboloid, as developed by Biernat et al. (2011), to the interacting Schwinger model. It contrasts with prior solutions of the Schwinger model obtained via equal-time quantization (Manton, 1985; Link, 1990) and light-cone quantization (Lenz et al., 1991), as well as the original functional methods of Schwinger (1962) and operator solutions of Lowenstein and Swieca (1971). The proposed approach aims to test point-form quantum field theory as an alternative for non-perturbative studies, with the eventual goal of applying it to 4-dimensional quantum field theories.
