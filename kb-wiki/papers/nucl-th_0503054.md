# Probing the QCD equation of state with thermal photons in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC

> Thermal photon production at mid-rapidity in Au+Au reactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV is studied in the framework of a 2D+1 hydrodynamical model that describes efficiently the bulk identified hadron spectra at RHIC. The combined thermal plus NLO pQCD photon spectrum is in good agreement with the yields measured by the PHENIX experiment for all Au+Au centralities. Within our model, we demonstrate that the correlation of the thermal photon slopes with the charged hadron multiplicity in each centrality bin provides direct empirical information on the underlying degrees of freedom and on the equation of state, $s(T)/T^3$, of the strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim

The correlation between thermal photon slopes and charged hadron multiplicity in different centrality bins of Au+Au collisions at RHIC can provide direct empirical information on the underlying degrees of freedom and the equation of state \(s(T)/T^3\) of strongly interacting matter, but only within the specific framework of a 2D+1 hydrodynamical model that assumes Bjorken boost invariance and a first-order phase transition with a bag constant EoS.

## Method

The authors implemented a 2D+1 relativistic hydrodynamical model with cylindrical symmetry and longitudinal boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion, using a first-order phase transition from a QGP to a hadron resonance gas (HRG) at \(T_{crit}\) = 165 MeV with latent heat \(\Delta\varepsilon\approx\) 1.4 GeV/fm\(^3\). The QGP was modeled as an ideal gas of massless quarks (\(N_f\) = 2.5 flavours) and gluons with degeneracy \(g_{QGP}\) = 42.25, while the HRG was modeled as a non-interacting gas of \(\sim\)400 hadrons and resonances below 2.5 GeV/\(c^2\). Initial energy density was distributed according to a Glauber prescription with 75% of entropy from soft processes (scaling with \(N_{part}\)) and 25% from hard processes (scaling with \(N_{coll}\)), and the system evolved from \(\tau_0\) = 0.15 fm/\(c\) with maximum initial energy density \(\varepsilon_0\) = 220 GeV/fm\(^3\) for central collisions. Thermal photon emission rates used the full leading-order calculations from Arnold et al. for the QGP phase and the improved parametrization from Turbide et al. for the HRG phase, combined with NLO pQCD prompt photon predictions from Vogelsang scaled by the nuclear overlap function.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density for central (\(b\) = 0 fm) Au+Au collisions: \(\varepsilon_0\) = 220 GeV/fm\(^3\) (average \(\langle\varepsilon_0\rangle\) = 72 GeV/fm\(^3\)) at \(\tau_0\) = 0.15 fm/\(c\)
- Critical temperature for phase transition: \(T_{crit}\) = 165 MeV with latent heat \(\Delta\varepsilon\approx\) 1.4 GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Bag constant: \(B\approx\) 0.38 GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: \(T_{chem}\) = 150 MeV with baryochemical potential \(\mu_B\) = 25 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: \(T_{fo}\) = 120 MeV (energy density \(\varepsilon_{fo}\) = 0.10 GeV/fm\(^3\))
- Initial entropy density for central collisions: \(s_0\) = 590 fm\(^{-3}\) (average \(\langle s_0\rangle\) = 378 fm\(^{-3}\))
- Initial temperature for central collisions: \(T_0\) = 498 MeV (average \(\langle T_0\rangle\) = 190 MeV)
- Energy density at end of pure QGP phase: \(\varepsilon_{QGP}^{min}\) = 1.7 GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Energy density at beginning of pure HRG phase: \(\varepsilon_{HRG}^{max}\) = 0.35 GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Effective number of degrees of freedom in HRG at \(T_c\): \(g_{HRG}\approx\) 12
- Sound velocity in QGP phase: \(c_s^2 = 1/3\); in HRG phase: \(c_s^2\approx\) 0.15

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamical studies of thermal photon production at SPS energies (Srivastava et al., Alam et al., Peressounko) and at RHIC (Kolb and Heinz, Teaney, Hirano) by using the most up-to-date photon emission rates from Arnold et al. (2001) for the QGP and Turbide et al. (2004) for the HRG, combined with NLO pQCD prompt photon calculations from Vogelsang that successfully reproduce the p+p baseline at RHIC. The paper enables a new method to extract the QCD equation of state from experimental data by correlating the thermal photon slope with charged hadron multiplicity across centrality bins, which could provide direct empirical evidence for the degrees of freedom in the deconfined phase, complementing lattice QCD predictions (Karsch et al., 2001) and statistical model analyses (Andronic et al., 2005).
