# Bayesian estimation of the low-energy constants up to fourth order in the nucleon-nucleon sector of chiral effective field theory

> We use Bayesian methods and Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC) sampling to infer the posterior probability density function (PDF) for the low-energy constants (LECs) up to next-to-next-to-next- to-leading order (N3LO) in a chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) description of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. In a first step, we condition the inference on neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data and account for uncorrelated $χ$EFT truncation errors. We demonstrate how to successfully sample the 31-dimensional space of LECs at N3LO using a revised HMC inference protocol. In a second step we extend the analysis by means of importance sampling and an empirical determination of the neutron-neutron scattering length to infer the posterior PDF for the leading charge-dependent contact LEC in the $^{1}S_0$ neutron-neutron interaction channel. While doing so we account for the $χ$EFT truncation error via a conjugate prior. We use the resulting posterior PDF to sample the posterior predictive distributions for the effective range parameters in the $^{1}S_0$ wave as well as the strengths of charge-symmetry breaking and charge-independence breaking. We conclude that empirical point-estimate results of isospin breaking in the $^{1}S_0$ channel are consistent with the PDFs obtained in our Bayesian analysis and that, when accounting for $χ$EFT truncation errors, one must go to next-to-next-to-leading order to confidently detect isospin breaking effects.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Bayesian inference with Hamiltonian Monte Carlo can successfully sample the 31-dimensional posterior of low-energy constants at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, and that when accounting for chiral truncation errors one must go to at least next-to-next-to-leading order to confidently detect isospin breaking effects in the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors used Hamiltonian Monte Carlo sampling to infer the posterior probability density for all low-energy constants up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, conditioning on neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data from the Granada database while accounting for uncorrelated chiral effective field theory truncation errors. They then extended the analysis via importance sampling, using the empirical neutron-neutron scattering length of -18.9 ± 0.4 fm to infer the posterior for the leading charge-dependent contact low-energy constant in the 1S0 neutron-neutron channel, with the chiral truncation error modeled via a conjugate inverse-gamma prior on the variance of the expansion coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron scattering length used as data: -18.9 ± 0.4 fm (experimental uncertainty 0.4 fm)
- The cutoff used for nonlocal regulators: 450 MeV (with exponent n=3)
- The breakdown scale for the chiral expansion parameter: 600 MeV
- The number of low-energy constants sampled at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order: 31
- The integrated autocorrelation times for Hamiltonian Monte Carlo chains: 0.71 at next-to-leading order, 0.93 at next-to-next-to-leading order, and 3.33 at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order
- The mode of the marginal posterior for the variance of chiral expansion coefficients (cbar-squared): 11.5
- The prior mode for cbar-squared: 6.0 (inverse-gamma prior with shape 1.0 and rate 12.0)
- The number of expansion coefficients used to inform the truncation error posterior: 5
- The maximum a posteriori prediction for the neutron-proton scattering length at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order: -23.733 fm (empirical: -23.740 ± 0.020 fm)
- The maximum a posteriori prediction for the proton-proton scattering length at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order: -16.544 fm (empirical: -17.3 ± 0.4 fm)
- The maximum a posteriori prediction for the neutron-proton effective range at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order: 2.693 fm (empirical: 2.77 ± 0.05 fm)
- The maximum a posteriori prediction for the proton-proton effective range at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order: 2.867 fm (empirical: 2.85 ± 0.04 fm)

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference framework for chiral effective field theory low-energy constants previously developed by Svensson et al. (2021) from next-to-next-to-leading order to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, addressing the significantly more challenging 31-dimensional sampling problem. The authors build on the truncation error model of Furnstahl et al. (2015) and Wesolowski et al. (2018), and use pion-nucleon priors from the Roy-Steiner analysis of Siemens et al. (2016). The results enable quantified uncertainty predictions for isospin breaking effects in the 1S0 channel and provide posterior distributions that can be used in ab initio calculations of nuclei and nuclear matter, particularly toward the driplines where isospin breaking becomes important.
