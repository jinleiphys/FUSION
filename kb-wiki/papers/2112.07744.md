# Toward an automated-algebra framework for high orders in the virial expansion of quantum matter

> The virial expansion provides a non-perturbative view into the thermodynamics of quantum many-body systems in dilute regimes. While powerful, the expansion is challenging as calculating its coefficients at each order $n$ requires analyzing (if not solving) the quantum $n$-body problem. In this work, we present a comprehensive review of automated algebra methods, which we developed to calculate high-order virial coefficients. The methods are computational but non-stochastic, thus avoiding statistical effects; they are also for the most part analytic, not numerical, and amenable to massively parallel computer architectures. We show formalism and results for coefficients characterizing the thermodynamics (pressure, density, energy, static susceptibilities) of homogeneous and harmonically trapped systems, and explain how to generalize them to other observables such as the momentum distribution, Tan's contact, and the structure factor.

## Key claim

The authors present a comprehensive review of automated algebra methods they developed to calculate high-order virial coefficients for quantum many-body systems, claiming these methods are computational but non-stochastic (avoiding statistical effects), mostly analytic rather than numerical, and amenable to massively parallel computing; however, the approach relies on Suzuki-Trotter factorization with an imaginary-time discretization that requires extrapolation to the continuous limit, and the interaction must be renormalized by tuning to reproduce the exact second-order Beth-Uhlenbeck result.

## Method

The method factorizes the Boltzmann weight \(e^{-\tau \hat H}\) into kinetic and potential parts using Suzuki-Trotter decomposition, then computes canonical partition functions \(Q_N\) for fixed particle numbers by evaluating matrix elements of the factorized transfer matrix on a spatial lattice. For homogeneous spin-1/2 fermions with zero-range interaction, the matrix elements factorize into products of non-interacting Boltzmann weights \(K(\mathbf{p}) = e^{-\tau \mathbf{p}^2/(2m)}\) plus interaction terms proportional to \(C = e^{\tau g/\ell^3} - 1\), where the coupling is tuned to reproduce the exact second-order virial coefficient. The canonical partition functions are computed at progressively larger values of the imaginary-time steps \(N_\tau\), and the continuous-time limit is taken by extrapolation.

## Key numbers

* For non-interacting non-relativistic fermions in \(d\) dimensions, the virial coefficients are \(b_k^{(0)} = (-1)^{k+1} k^{-(d+2)/2}\).
* The second-order virial coefficient for 3D spin-1/2 fermions with zero-range interaction is \(\Delta b_2^\text{3D} = \frac{e^{\lambda_3^2}}{\sqrt{2}} \left[ 1 + \erf(\lambda_3) \right]\), where \(\lambda_3 = \sqrt{\beta}/a_0\) with \(a_0\) the s-wave scattering length.
* For 2D fermions, \(\Delta b_2^\text{2D} = e^{\lambda_2^2} - 2 \int_0^{\infty} \frac{dp}{p} \frac{2 e^{-\lambda_2^2 p^2}}{\pi^2 + 4 \ln^2(p^2)}\), with \(\lambda_2 = \sqrt{\beta E_B}\) where \(E_B\) is the two-body binding energy.
* For 1D fermions, \(\Delta b_2^\text{1D} = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}} + \frac{e^{\lambda_1^2/4}}{2\sqrt{2}} \left[ 1 + \erf(\lambda_1/2) \right]\), with \(\lambda_1 = 2\sqrt{\beta}/a_0\).

## Context

This work extends the virial expansion framework beyond the second-order Beth-Uhlenbeck formula (1937) and the third-order analytic results of Leyronas (2011) and Kaplan and Sun (2011), as well as the exact diagonalization approaches of Blume and collaborators (2009-2016) that reached third order and partially fourth order. The automated algebra methods enable calculation of higher-order coefficients (beyond third order) with controlled systematic error, making resummation techniques practical and meaningful for extending the domain of applicability of the virial expansion, as demonstrated by Hou et al. (2020).
