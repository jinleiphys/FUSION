> We present a superior proof that the results for summed strength isovector dipole, spin dipole, and orbital dipole excitations are independent of deformations at the Delta N = 0 level.

# Further Comments On The Effects of Deformation on Isovector Electromagnetic and Weak Transition Strengths

## Key claim
The summed strength for isovector dipole, spin-dipole, and orbital dipole excitations is independent of deformation at the ΔN = 0 level, but only when using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions and considering only a single excitation energy of 1ℏω; the proof relies on the fact that for dipole transitions there is only one excitation energy, unlike for E2 or E3 transitions.

## Method
The authors present a new proof using energy-weighted sum rules (EWSR) for the isovector dipole operator rY¹t₊, showing that the summed strength can be expressed as EWS/ℏω. They then extend the analysis to spin-dipole and orbital-dipole operators by relating their energy-weighted sum rules to the ordinary dipole EWS through spin and orbital angular momentum factors. The effect of different oscillator frequencies in the x, y, and z directions is incorporated by replacing 1/ℏω with the average (1/3)(1/ℏωₓ + 1/ℏωᵧ + 1/ℏω_z).

## Key numbers
- For ordinary dipole in ¹²C: the quantity 4π SUM (Mω/ℏ) equals 27, confirming results from Zamick and Auerbach (1999)
- For spin-dipole: the same quantity equals 20.25, which is exactly 3/4 of the ordinary dipole value (27 × 3/4 = 20.25)
- For orbital dipole: the quantity equals 48, all coming from the 0p shell
- For ¹²C in the asymptotic (oblate) limit with volume conservation: ωₓ = 0.8434 ω₀ and ω_z = 1.4057 ω₀, giving the replacement factor 1.0275/ℏω₀, a very small change in overall strength
- With different frequencies, 2/3 of the strength shifts down to 0.8434 ℏω₀ and 1/3 shifts up to 1.4057 ℏω₀

## Context
This work extends the earlier proof by Zamick and Auerbach (1999) that summed isovector dipole strengths are independent of deformation in a ΔN = 0 Nilsson model for ¹²C. The new proof avoids explicit use of the rotational model and instead employs energy-weighted sum rules following the approach of Lipparini and Stringari (1989). The results enable future studies of deformation effects on weak transition strengths relevant to neutrino absorption reactions such as νₑ + ¹²C → ¹²N + e⁻, and clarify that while individual multipole strengths differ between asymptotic and spherical limits, the total summed strength remains invariant.
