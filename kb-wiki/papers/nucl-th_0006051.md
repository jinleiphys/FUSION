# Dynamics of low-energy nuclear forces for electromagnetic and weak reactions with the deuteron in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei

> A dynamics of low-energy nuclear forces is investigated for low-energy electromagnetic and weak nuclear reactions with the deuteron in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei by example of the neutron-proton radiative capture (M1-capture) n + p -> D + photon, the photomagnetic disintegration of the deuteron photon + D -> n + p and weak reactions of astrophysical interest. These are the solar proton burning p + p -> D + positron + nu_e, the pep-process p + electron + p -> D + nu_e and the neutrino and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron caused by charged nu_e + D -> electron + p + p, anti-nu_e + D -> positron + n + n and neutral nu_e(anti-nu_e) + D -> nu_e(ant-nu_e) + n + p weak currents.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei (NNJL model) can describe low-energy electromagnetic and weak reactions with the deuteron using one-nucleon loop exchanges, with the deuteron treated as a neutron-proton collective excitation (Cooper np-pair), and the model claims to reproduce experimental cross sections and astrophysical factors without free parameters, though the description of the M1-capture requires including chiral one-meson loop corrections and the Delta(1232) resonance to match the experimental value.

## Method

The authors applied the NNJL model, which treats the deuteron as a neutron-proton collective excitation from a phenomenological local four-nucleon interaction, to calculate cross sections for the neutron-proton radiative capture n + p -> D + gamma (M1-capture), photomagnetic disintegration of the deuteron, and weak reactions including solar proton burning p + p -> D + e+ + nu_e, the pep-process, and neutrino/anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron. They evaluated effective Lagrangians using one-nucleon loop diagrams at leading order in the 1/M_N expansion (justified by the large N_C approach to QCD), and included contributions from chiral one-meson loop corrections and the Delta(1232) resonance for the electromagnetic reactions. For weak reactions, they calculated astrophysical factors and cross sections relative to the S_pp(0) value.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross section for neutron-proton radiative capture at thermal neutron kinetic energy T_n = 0.0252 eV: sigma(np -> D gamma)_exp = (334.2 +/- 0.5) mb
- Effective coupling constant C_NN for strong low-energy n + p -> n + p transition in the 1S0 state: C_NN = 3.27 x 10^{-3} MeV^{-2}
- S-wave scattering length of elastic np scattering in the 1S0 state: a_np = (-23.75 +/- 0.01) fm
- Effective range of low-energy elastic np scattering: r_np = 2.75 +/- 0.05 fm
- Astrophysical factor for solar proton burning obtained in the NNJL model: S_pp(0) = 4.08 x 10^{-25} MeV b, compared to the recommended value S_pp(0) = 4.00 x 10^{-25} MeV b
- Proton magnetic dipole moment: mu_p = 2.793 nuclear magnetons; neutron magnetic dipole moment: mu_n = -1.913 nuclear magnetons
- Deuteron electric quadrupole moment: Q_D = 0.286 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei (NNJL model) previously developed by the authors (Ivanov et al., 2000) to electromagnetic and weak reactions with the deuteron, providing an alternative to the Potential Model Approach (Austern, 1952; Riska and Brown, 1972) and Effective Field Theory approaches (Weinberg, 1990; various authors, 1997-1999) which gave contradictory results for the M1-capture cross section (ranging from 297.2 mb to 334 mb). The results enable predictions for solar neutrino experiments at the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) and for reactor anti-neutrino experiments, and the calculated S_pp(0) value agrees with the recommended value used in the Standard Solar Model for normalizing the solar luminosity.
