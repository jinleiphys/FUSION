# Effective Q-Q Interactions in Constituent Quark Models

> We study the performance of some recent potential models suggested as effective interactions between constituent quarks. In particular, we address constituent quark models for baryons with hybrid Q-Q interactions stemming from one-gluon plus meson exchanges. Upon recalculating two of such models we find them to fail in describing the N and Δspectra. Our calculations are based on accurate solutions of the three-quark systems in both a variational Schrödinger and a rigorous Faddeev approach. It is argued that hybrid {Q-Q} interactions encounter difficulties in describing baryon spectra due to the specific contributions from one-gluon and pion exchanges together. In contrast, a chiral constituent quark model with a Q-Q interaction solely derived from Goldstone-boson exchange is capable of providing a unified description of both the N and Δspectra in good agreement with phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hybrid constituent quark models combining one-gluon exchange (OGE) with meson exchanges fail to describe the N and Delta spectra when solved accurately, contrary to claims in prior work by Valcarce et al. (1996) and Dziembowski et al. (1996). In contrast, a chiral constituent quark model with interactions derived solely from Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) can provide a unified description of both spectra without needing a sizeable OGE contribution.

## Method

The authors recalculated the N and Delta spectra for two hybrid models (VGFV and DFM) using two independent methods: a precise solution of the Faddeev equations via high-rank separable expansions and a stochastic variational solution of the Schrodinger equation. They then constructed a semirelativistic GBE constituent quark model using a relativistic kinetic energy operator and a Q-Q interaction from pseudoscalar meson exchange (pi, K, eta, eta'), solved via the stochastic variational method in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- For the VGFV model, the recalculated N-Delta splitting is larger than 2000 MeV, with the Delta ground state at approximately 3200 MeV (compared to the empirical 1232 MeV).
- For the DFM model, the recalculated N-Delta splitting is 670 MeV, more than two times larger than the empirical value.
- In the VGFV model, the lowest negative-parity 1/2- state appears at approximately 1550 MeV, with the S=3/2, L=1 excitation at approximately 3500 MeV.
- The GBE model uses a constituent quark mass of 340 MeV and a confinement strength C of approximately 2.3 fm^-2.
- The pion-quark coupling constant is fixed to g_piQ^2/4pi = 0.67, derived from the Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Context

This work extends the critique of OGE-based models by Glozman and Riska (1996) and contradicts the claims of Valcarce et al. (1996) and Dziembowski et al. (1996) that hybrid models with OGE plus meson exchange could describe baryon spectra. The authors show that those prior results were not converged due to inadequate basis sets in the hyperspherical-harmonics method. The GBE model enables a unified description of light and strange baryon spectra without OGE, and the semirelativistic framework avoids the unphysical v/c > 1 found in nonrelativistic treatments.
