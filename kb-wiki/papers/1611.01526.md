# Uncertainty Quantification for Optical Model Parameters

> Although uncertainty quantification has been making its way into nuclear theory, these methods have yet to be explored in the context of reaction theory. For example, it is well known that different parameterizations of the optical potential can result in different cross sections, but these differences have not been systematically studied and quantified. The purpose of this work is to investigate the uncertainties in nuclear reactions that result from fitting a given model to elastic-scattering data, as well as to study how these uncertainties propagate to the inelastic and transfer channels. We use statistical methods to determine a best fit and create corresponding 95\% confidence bands. A simple model of the process is fit to elastic-scattering data and used to predict either inelastic or transfer cross sections. In this initial work, we assume that our model is correct, and the only uncertainties come from the variation of the fit parameters. We study a number of reactions involving neutron and deuteron projectiles with energies in the range of 5-25 MeV/u, on targets with mass $A$=12-208. We investigate the correlations between the parameters in the fit. The case of deuterons on $^{12}$C is discussed in detail: the elastic-scattering fit and the prediction of $^{12}$C(d,p)$^{13}$C transfer angular distributions, using both uncorrelated and correlated $χ^2$ minimization functions. The general features for all cases are compiled in a systematic manner to identify trends. Our work shows that, in many cases, the correlated $χ^2$ functions (in comparison to the uncorrelated $χ^2$ functions) provide a more natural parameterization of the process. These correlated functions do, however, produce broader confidence bands. Further optimization may require improvement in the models themselves and/or more information included in the fit.

## Key claim

This work systematically quantifies uncertainties in nuclear reaction predictions arising from fitting optical model parameters to elastic-scattering data, but only under the assumption that the reaction model itself is correct and all uncertainty comes from parameter variation. The correlated chi-squared fitting method produces broader and more realistic 95% confidence bands than the standard uncorrelated approach, though further optimization requires improved models or additional data.

## Method

The authors fit optical model parameters to elastic-scattering angular distribution data using both uncorrelated and correlated chi-squared minimization functions, then propagated the parameter uncertainties to predict inelastic-scattering or transfer cross sections. For the uncorrelated case, parameter sets were drawn from a multivariate Gaussian approximation around the best fit; for the correlated case, where model correlations between angles were accounted for via a model covariance matrix, parameters were drawn from the empirical chi-squared distribution when the parameter space was non-Gaussian. The reaction calculations used the coupled-channels Born approximation (CCBA) for neutron elastic and inelastic scattering, and the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) for deuteron elastic scattering and (d,p) transfer reactions, all implemented in the FRESCO code with MINUIT minimization.

## Key numbers

- For the detailed example of 11.8 MeV deuterons on 12C: uncorrelated fit gave elastic-scattering average band width 1.2211 (ratio to Rutherford) and predicted transfer band width 0.75735 mb/sr; correlated fit gave elastic band width 16.606 and transfer band width 6.2968 mb/sr.
- Extracted spectroscopic factor for 12C(d,p)13C(g.s.): uncorrelated fit gave S_exp = 0.435 (+0.019/-0.017); correlated fit gave S_exp = 0.352 (+0.223/-0.050).
- For the 12C(d,d)12C elastic fit: uncorrelated chi-squared per data point chi2_UC/M = 4.513; correlated chi2_C/M = 0.283.
- For the 54Fe(n,n)54Fe elastic fit: uncorrelated chi2_UC/M = 158.098; correlated chi2_C/M = 1.080.
- For the 208Pb(n,n)208Pb elastic fit: uncorrelated chi2_UC/M = 3.678; correlated chi2_C/M = 1.731.
- Deformation parameters used: delta_2 = 1.0852 fm for 12C, 0.85 fm for 48Ca, 0.967 fm for 54Fe; for 208Pb, delta_3 = 0.296 fm (uncorrelated) and 0.230 fm (correlated).

## Context

This work extends uncertainty quantification methods from nuclear structure theory (e.g., Bayesian parameter estimation in effective field theories by Wesolowski (2016) and Schindler (2009), and density functional theory by McDonnell (2015) and Schunck (2015)) into nuclear reaction theory for the first time. It contrasts with previous ad-hoc comparisons of two optical model parameterizations by providing a systematic statistical framework for generating confidence bands. The results enable future work to incorporate model-form uncertainty (beyond parameter variation) and to include additional data constraints, as the authors note that the correlated fits produce broader bands that better reflect true uncertainties but still may require improved reaction models.
