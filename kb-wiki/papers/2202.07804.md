# Decays of Roper-like singly heavy baryons in a chiral model

> We present a chiral model describing decays of the Roper-like $Œõ_c(2765)$ and $Œû_c(2970)$ as well as those of the heavy quark spin-doublet $\{Œ£_c(2455),Œ£_c(2520)\}$ and $\{Œû_c',Œû_c(2645)\}$, by focusing on chiral representations of diquarks inside the heavy baryons. Based on our chiral model together with the heavy quark spin symmetry, we derive a relation satisfied by axial charges of the heavy baryons that controls magnitude of their one pion (kaon) decays. Besides, it is shown that the large decay widths of the Roper-like $Œõ_c(2765)$ and $Œû_c(2970)$, which cannot be implemented by conventional non-relativistic quark models, are explained by reasonable values of the axial charges. In addition, we apply the chiral model to bottom baryons and predict decay widths of the undiscovered Roper-like $Œû_b$. We expect that our present investigation leads to elucidation of dynamical properties of the excited heavy baryons from the viewpoint of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The large decay widths of the Roper-like baryons Œõc(2765) and Œûc(2970), which conventional non-relativistic quark models cannot reproduce (giving widths of only a few MeV), can be explained by a chiral model with reasonable values of the axial charges, provided that the decays proceed sequentially via heavy quark spin-doublet baryons rather than through direct two-pion emission.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral Lagrangian invariant under SU(3)L √ó SU(3)R chiral symmetry, heavy quark spin symmetry, and parity, describing interactions among HQS-singlet baryons (ground-state and Roper-like) and HQS-doublet baryons (Œ£c and Œûc') with pseudo-scalar mesons. The Roper-like and ground-state baryons were treated as superpositions of two types of diquark fields with flipped chiral representations, with mixing angles determined by the axial charges gA and gA'. Decay widths were computed analytically from the Lagrangian for both charmed and bottom baryon sectors.

## Context

This work extends the chiral model approach of Suenaga (2021) by incorporating heavy quark spin-doublet baryons and focusing on chiral representations of diquarks. It addresses the failure of conventional non-relativistic quark models (Copley 1979, Yoshida 2015) and the non-relativistic quark model of Nagahiro (2016) to explain the large decay widths of Roper-like baryons, which were previously addressed by relativistic quark models (Arifi 2021) and the 3P0 model (Chen 2016). The results enable predictions for the undiscovered Roper-like Œûb baryon in the bottom sector and provide a framework for understanding excited heavy baryon dynamics from chiral symmetry.
