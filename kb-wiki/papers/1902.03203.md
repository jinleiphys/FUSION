# Thermal contribution of unstable states

> Within the framework of the Lee model, we analyze in detail the difference between the energy derivative of the phase shift and the standard spectral function of the unstable state. The fact that the model is exactly solvable allows us to demonstrate the construction of these observables from various exact Green functions. The connection to a formula due to Krein, Friedal, and Lloyd is also examined. We also directly demonstrate how the derivative of the phase shift correctly identifies the relevant interaction contributions for consistently including an unstable state in describing the thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The correct thermodynamic description of an interacting system containing an unstable state requires using the energy derivative of the scattering phase shift (the effective spectral function \(B(E)\)) rather than the standard spectral function of the unstable particle alone, because the nonresonant two-body interaction contribution is generally not negligible and can even dominate at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors employed an exactly solvable Lee Model Hamiltonian describing a system of stable pions (\(\pi\)) and an unstable rho meson (\(\rho\)) that decays into two pions via \(\rho \rightarrow \pi\pi\). They analytically computed the \(\rho\)-propagator, the two-pion propagator, the scattering phase shift, and various spectral functions, then derived the relation \(B(E) = A_\rho(E) + \sum_q \Delta A_{2\pi}(E;q)\) connecting the effective spectral function to the \(\rho\) spectral function and the modification of the two-pion spectral function. Numerical solutions were obtained on a momentum grid with 800 states and a box size of approximately 800 fm, using P-wave coupling with a Gaussian form factor.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant used in the numerical exercise was \(g = 23.5\ \text{GeV}^{-3/2}\), with bare resonance energy \(\Delta_0 = 0.64\ \text{GeV}\) and form factor cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.4\ \text{GeV}\).
* The momentum grid used \(1+N_q = 800\) states with box size \(L \approx 800\ \text{fm}\) and an imaginary increment \(\epsilon = 0.01\ \text{GeV}\).
* In the large bare mass limit (\(\Delta_0 \gg E\)), the effective spectral function \(B\) scales as \(B \approx 2 a_l \times \frac{\partial}{\partial E} q^{2l+1}\) near threshold, where \(a_l\) is the scattering length and \(l\) is the relative orbital angular momentum.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix formulation of statistical mechanics by Dashen, Ma, and Bernstein (1969) and the discussion by Weinhold (1998), demonstrating explicitly within an exactly solvable model why the spectral function of the unstable state alone (Scheme A, used in many hadron resonance gas models such as those by Satz (2006) and the Wroclaw group) is insufficient. The results enable proper inclusion of both resonant and nonresonant contributions in thermal models of hadron gas, with implications for states like \(f_0(500)\), \(\kappa(700)\), \(N^*\) and \(\Delta\) resonances, and exotic states such as \(X(3872)\) as studied by Ortega (2017).
