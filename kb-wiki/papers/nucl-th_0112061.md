# Continuum HFB calculations with finite range pairing interactions

> A new method of calculating pairing correlations in coordinate space with finite range interactions is presented. In the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach the mean field part is derived from a Skyrme-type force whereas the pairing field is constructed with a Gogny force. An iterative scheme is used for solving the integro-differential HFB equations via the introduction of a local equivalent potential. The method is illustrated on the case of the nucleus $^{18}$C. It is shown that the results are insensitive to the cut off value in the quasiparticle spectrum if this value is above 100 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for solving Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate space with finite-range pairing interactions, demonstrating that results become insensitive to the cutoff in the quasiparticle spectrum above 100 MeV, in contrast to the divergent behavior seen with zero-range pairing interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled integro-differential HFB equations in coordinate space by using a Skyrme-type force (SLy4) for the mean field and a finite-range Gogny force (D1S) for the pairing field. They transform the integro-differential equations into differential ones by introducing a local equivalent potential for each quasiparticle wave function, following the method of Vautherin and Veneroni (1987), and solve the system iteratively. The continuum states are treated exactly without box boundary conditions, and the local equivalent potentials are regularized near their poles using a linear interpolation procedure.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of \(^{18}\)C converges to -130.65 MeV at a cutoff of 100 MeV when using the Gogny D1S pairing interaction.
- Pairing correlation energy for \(^{18}\)C reaches a stable value of 16.04 MeV with the Gogny interaction.
- Mean square radius of the pairing density converges to about 34.5 fm\(^2\) at a cutoff of 95 MeV for the Gogny interaction.
- The shortest range of the two gaussian form factors in the Gogny force is 0.7 fm, corresponding to a kinetic energy of about 160 MeV; combined with a mean field depth of about 40 MeV, this yields a quasiparticle energy around 120 MeV.
- Zero-range pairing interaction parameters used for comparison: \(V_0 = -2488.9\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(\gamma = 1/6\), \(\rho_C = 0.133\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space HFB method of Dobaczewski et al. (1996) and Grasso et al. (2001), which previously used zero-range interactions in both channels, to incorporate finite-range Gogny pairing forces. It directly addresses the well-known pathology of zero-range pairing interactions producing diverging contributions without a cutoff, as discussed by Dobaczewski et al. (1996), and contrasts with regularization schemes proposed by Bulgac (2002). The method enables accurate treatment of pairing correlations in weakly bound nuclei near the drip lines where continuum effects are important, and the authors note that a further step toward full self-consistency using the same finite-range force for both mean field and pairing field is in progress.
