# Predictiveness of Effective Field Theory in Nuclear Physics

> We discuss the role effective field theory plays in making predictions in nuclear physics in an approach that combines both the high sophistication of the standard nuclear many-body approach and the power of systematic higher chiral-order account in chiral perturbation theory. The main idea of this approach is illustrated with a selected number of cases involving few-body systems, the measurement of some of which poses an experimental challenge and will be of value to solar neutrino studies.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) in nuclear physics can be made genuinely predictive only when combined with the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA), rather than pursued in isolation as a "rigorous EFT" (RigEFT) or "pionless EFT" (π̸EFT). The author argues that the "more effective effective theory" (MEEFT) strategy, which exploits both the accurate wave functions from SNPA and the systematic chiral counting of EFT, enables parameter-free predictions with error estimates that neither approach can achieve separately.

## Method

The author adopts Weinberg's counting rule, which defines the nuclear potential as the sum of 2-particle irreducible diagrams truncated at some chiral order, then uses that potential to solve the Lippman-Schwinger or Schrödinger equations. The MEEFT strategy then computes response functions by calculating irreducible contributions to current vertex functions to the order correctly implemented in the wave functions, using accurate SNPA wave functions that possess correct long-distance properties governed by chiral symmetry. The approach separates processes into those protected by the "chiral filter" mechanism (accurately calculable via soft-pion theorems) and those not protected (requiring more careful treatment but still tractable with SNPA input).

## Key numbers

- The N³LO chiral perturbation calculation of the two-body potential fits np scattering below 290 MeV with χ²/datum = 1.10, while the phenomenological AV18 potential fits with χ²/datum = 1.04
- At NLO and NNLO, the χ²/datum values are 36.2 and 10.1 respectively
- The SNPA two-nucleon potentials fit scattering data up to momenta ∼300 MeV with χ²/datum ≲ 1.4
- SNPA describes nuclei up to mass number A=10 with ground and excited state energies within ∼1–2%
- In Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of A≤12 nuclei, the expectation value of the one-pion-exchange potential is typically 70–75% of the total potential energy
- The π̸EFT calculation of np capture at thermal energy works fairly well up to Eγ ∼ 10 MeV

## Context

This paper extends the line of work following Weinberg's "folk theorem" (Weinberg, 1979) on applying EFT to nuclear physics, and contrasts with the "rigorous EFT" approach (e.g., pionless EFT as developed by Kaplan and others) which the author argues lacks predictiveness beyond reproducing SNPA results. The MEEFT approach builds on the SNPA framework reviewed by Wiringa and collaborators (2006) and the chiral perturbation theory potentials reviewed by Machleidt (2006). The work enables systematic calculation of processes relevant to solar neutrino studies, particularly the hep and hen processes involving four-body interactions that are difficult for RigEFT approaches.
