# Revisiting the post-glitch relaxation of the 2000 Vela glitch with the neutron star equation of states in the Brueckner and relativistic Brueckner theories

> We revisit the short-term post-glitch relaxation of the Vela 2000 glitch in the simple two-component model of pulsar glitch by making use of the latest realistic equations of states from the microscopic Brueckner and the relativistic Brueckner theories for neutron stars, which can reconcile with the available astrophysical constraints. We show that to fit both the glitch size and the post-glitch jumps in frequency derivatives approximately one minute after the glitch, the mass of the Vela pulsar is necessarily small, and there may be demands for a stiff equation of state (which results in a typical stellar radius larger than $\sim\negthickspace12.5\,\rm km$) and a strong suppression of the pairing gap in the nuclear medium. We discuss the implications of this result on the understanding of pulsar glitches.

## Key claim

The authors show that fitting both the glitch size and the post-glitch jumps in frequency derivatives approximately one minute after the 2000 Vela glitch requires the Vela pulsar mass to be necessarily small, and may demand a stiff equation of state (stellar radius larger than approximately 12.5 km) and a strong suppression of the pairing gap in the nuclear medium, but these conclusions depend on the specific treatment of the core superfluid coupling and the polarization effect on pairing gaps.

## Method

The authors applied the snowplow two-component glitch model of Pizzochero (2011) to the 2000 Vela glitch, using neutron star equations of state and proton fractions consistently calculated from the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne V18 potential and from the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) approach with pvCD-Bonn A potentials. They computed the pinning force, Magnus force, and critical angular momentum lag in the inner crust, then determined the fraction of coupled core vorticity (Y_gl) required to reproduce the observed glitch size and the post-glitch jump in spin-down rate.

## Key numbers

- The glitch activity parameter for Vela is A_g = 1.91 × 10^{-9} per day, giving a constraint I_n/I_p ≳ 1.6% from glitch observations.
- The characteristic age of Vela is approximately 11.3 kyr, and the interglitch time is approximately 2 yr.
- The maximum pinning force occurs at densities around 0.0544 fm^{-3} (approximately 0.32 ρ_0) for the BHF EOS with M = 1.2 M_sun.
- The BHF EOS gives a maximum gravitational mass M_TOV = 2.20 M_sun and a radius for a 1.4 M_sun star of 11.09 km; the RBHF EOS gives M_TOV = 2.21 M_sun and R_1.4 = 12.34 km.
- The saturation density for BHF is ρ_0 = 0.186 fm^{-3} with binding energy -14.47 MeV; for RBHF it is ρ_0 = 0.192 fm^{-3} with binding energy -16.83 MeV.
- The symmetry energy parameters are E_sym = 33.0 MeV and L = 72.8 MeV for BHF; E_sym = 36.8 MeV and L = 80.5 MeV for RBHF.
- The observed post-glitch jump in spin-down rate for the 2000 Vela glitch is shown as a horizontal line with 1σ deviation in the figures, but no explicit numerical value for this jump is given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the snowplow model studies of Pizzochero (2011) and Seveso et al. (2012), who used polytropic and relativistic mean-field equations of state, by employing consistent microscopic equations of state and proton fractions from the Brueckner and relativistic Brueckner theories. The authors address the "glitch crisis" previously identified by Li et al. (2015, 2016) in the Brueckner framework, showing that assuming a partially coupled core (rather than fully coupled) can resolve the crisis, consistent with arguments by Gugercinoglu et al. (2014) and Haskell et al. (2015). The results enable future work to test whether the simple two-component model can be applied to other glitching pulsars and to constrain neutron star microphysics from glitch observations.
