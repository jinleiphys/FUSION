# Nuclear contacts of unstable nuclei

> Nuclear contact is a key quantity to describe the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (SRCs). While they have been determined by electron scattering experiments for selected stable nuclei, nuclear contacts are largely unknown for unstable nuclei. In this work, we study nuclear contacts for a number of nuclei in the vicinity of the doubly magic $^{132}$Sn from the theoretical perspective, with special emphasis on unstable nuclei. We find that the proton-proton contact generally gets suppressed by the excess neutrons for the Sn isotopes, resembling the suppression of $α$-cluster formation reported recently for the same isotopic chain [J. Tanaka $\textit{et al}.$, Science $\textbf{371}$, 260 (2021)]. This indicates a hidden universal aspect of SRCs and $α$ clustering, two different kinds of nuclear correlations. Meanwhile, a linear relation is found between the proton-proton contact and the proton number for the $N=82$ isotones. Our results can be helpful for future experimental studies of SRCs in unstable nuclei at advanced facilities worldwide.

## Key claim

The authors find that the proton-proton contact for Sn isotopes is suppressed by excess neutrons, resembling the suppression of alpha-cluster formation reported by Tanaka et al. (2021) for the same isotopic chain, and they identify a model-independent nearly linear correlation between the pp contact and the root-mean-square radius of the proton density distribution.

## Method

The authors combine the generalized contact formalism (GCF) with the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) model to extract the spin-zero s-wave pp contact for Sn isotopes (A=100-140) and N=82 isotones. The point-proton densities from the Skyrme HFB model (using SLY4, SIII, and SKM* parameter sets) and experimental charge densities from elastic electron scattering are used to compute the two-proton density, from which the pp contact is extracted at a matching radius r0 using the universal two-body function calculated with the AV18 potential.

## Key numbers

* The pp contact for Sn isotopes decreases consistently as mass number increases from A=100 to A=140, with no obvious shell effects at the magic neutron number N=82.
* A quadratic fit between the pp contact and the RMS proton radius for Sn isotopes yields C⁰_pp = 5.35611 - 1.86809 r_p + 0.17212 r_p² with a coefficient of determination R² = 0.99902.
* For N=82 isotones, the pp contact shows a linear trend with proton number Z: C⁰_pp = -0.19089 + 0.01141A with a correlation coefficient r = 0.99742.
* The error on the extracted pp contact is estimated to be of the order of 10%, originating from the infinite nuclear-matter approximation and the selection of r0.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear contacts, previously determined for stable nuclei via electron scattering and ab initio calculations limited to A ≤ 40, to unstable nuclei in the ¹³²Sn region. The results connect to the suppression of alpha-cluster formation observed by Tanaka et al. (2021) in (p,pα) experiments on Sn isotopes, suggesting a universal aspect between short-range correlations and alpha clustering. The findings enable future experimental extraction of pp contacts for unstable nuclei using elastic electron scattering at facilities like RIKEN, where experiments on ¹³⁷Cs and nuclei around ¹³²Sn are ongoing.
