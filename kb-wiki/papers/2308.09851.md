# Strongly hyperbolic quasilinear systems revisited, with applications to relativistic fluid dynamics

> We revisit the theory of first-order quasilinear systems with diagonalizable principal part and only real eigenvalues, what is commonly referred to as strongly hyperbolic systems. We provide a self-contained and simple proof of local well-posedness, in the Hadamard sense, of the Cauchy problem. Our regularity assumptions are very minimal. As an application, we apply our results to systems of ideal and viscous relativistic fluids, where the theory of strongly hyperbolic equations has been systematically used to study several systems of physical interest.

## Key claim

The paper provides a self-contained and simple proof of local well-posedness for first-order quasilinear systems with diagonalizable principal part and only real eigenvalues (strongly hyperbolic systems) under very minimal regularity assumptions, specifically requiring only that distinct eigenvalues are continuously differentiable and do not meet each other.

## Method

The authors develop a novel energy estimate for the linear system associated with the quasilinear equation using a Fourier multiplier operator constructed from the symmetrizer matrix \(\cS^*\cS\), which is homogeneous of degree zero in the spatial frequency variable. They then construct an approximating sequence of smooth solutions via an inductive scheme, prove uniform bounds in Sobolev spaces \(H^r\) with \(r > N/2+1\), and establish convergence of the sequence using an energy estimate for the difference of two solutions. The existence time is shown to depend only on the initial data norm and the distance from the initial image to the boundary of the domain \(U\).

## Key numbers

* Regularity threshold: \(s > N/2 + 1\) is required for the Sobolev space index in the main well-posedness theorem and energy estimates.
* The energy estimate for the solution satisfies \(\|u(t)\|_r^2 \leq F_I(\|u\|_{C(I\times \mathbb{T}^N)}) e^{|t| F_I(\|u\|_{\mathbb{B}(I)})} \left( \|u_0\|_r^2 + |t| F_I(\|u\|_{\mathbb{B}(I)}) \right)\) for \(t \in [-T,T]\).
* The linear energy estimate (Proposition 3.1) gives \(\|u(t)\|_s^2 \leq F_I(\|v(t)\|_\infty) e^{t F_I(\|v\|_{\mathbb{E}^s(I)})} \left[ \|u_0\|_s^2 + \int_0^t \left( C_I + F_I(\|v(\tau)\|_\infty)\|v(\tau)\|_s^2 \right) d\tau \right]\) for \(s > N/2+1\).

## Context

This work extends the classical theory of symmetric and symmetrizable hyperbolic systems developed by Friedrichs (1954), Lax (1955), Kato (1975), and Taylor (1996) to systems where only a mild regularity condition on the eigenvalues is assumed, rather than requiring a full symmetrizer. The theory was implicitly used in previous work by Bemfica, Disconzi, Rodriguez, and Shao (2021) to establish local well-posedness of a relativistic viscous fluid model, and the present paper provides a systematic and self-contained development. The results enable the study of relativistic fluid dynamics (both ideal and viscous) where strongly hyperbolic formulations have been systematically employed.
