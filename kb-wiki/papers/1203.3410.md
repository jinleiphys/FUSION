# A study of the anisotropy associated with dipole asymmetry in heavy ion collisions

> The anisotropy associated with the initial dipole asymmetry in heavy ion collisions is studied via the two-particle relative azimuthal azimuthal angle (Δϕ=ϕ^a-ϕ^b) correlations, within a multi-phase transport model. For a broad selection of centrality, transverse momenta (pT^{a,b}) and pseudorapidity (η^{a,b}), a fitting method is used to decompose the anisotropy into a rapidity-even component, characterized by the Fourier coefficient v1, and a global momentum conservation component. The extracted v1 values are negative for pT<=0.7-0.9 GeV, reach a maximum at 2-3 GeV, and decreases at higher pT. The v1 values vary weakly with ηand centrality, but increases with collision energy and parton cross-section. The extracted global momentum conservation component is found to depend on Δη= η^a-η^b for Δη<3.

## Key claim

The rapidity-even dipole anisotropy \(v_1\) extracted from the AMPT model via a fitting method shows a sign change at low transverse momentum, with the crossing point at \(p_{\mathrm{T},0}=0.7-0.8\) GeV at RHIC energy and 0.9 GeV at LHC energy, qualitatively agreeing with hydrodynamic predictions but with the caveat that the extracted \(v_1\) values depend on the strength of final-state interactions and the model fails to describe data for \(\pT > 3\) GeV.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi Phase Transport) model, which combines HIJING initial conditions with parton and hadron transport, to generate Monte Carlo events for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=0.2\) TeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. They constructed two-particle correlation functions in relative azimuthal angle \(\Delta\phi\) and extracted the Fourier coefficient \(v_{1,1}\) for bins in transverse momentum (\(\pT^{\mathrm{a}},\pT^{\mathrm{b}}\)) and pseudorapidity (\(\eta^{\mathrm{a}},\eta^{\mathrm{b}}\)). A least-square fit of the \(v_{1,1}\) values to the functional form \(v_{1,1}(\pT^{\mathrm{a}},\pT^{\mathrm{b}}) = v_1(\pT^{\mathrm{a}})v_1(\pT^{\mathrm{b}}) - c\,\pT^{\mathrm{a}}\pT^{\mathrm{b}}\) was used to simultaneously determine the rapidity-even \(v_1(\pT)\) and the global momentum conservation coefficient \(c\), with the fit extended to a 4-D parameterization to extract \(v_1(\pT,\eta)\).

## Key numbers

- The crossing point where \(v_1\) changes sign is \(p_{\mathrm{T},0}=0.7-0.8\) GeV at RHIC energy (Au+Au, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=0.2\) TeV) and 0.9 GeV at LHC energy (Pb+Pb, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV).
- The extracted \(v_1\) reaches a maximum value of approximately 0.1 at \(\pT = 2-3\) GeV for both RHIC and LHC energies.
- Changing the strong coupling constant from \(\alpha_s=0.47\) (set-A) to \(\alpha_s=0.33\) (set-B) leads to an approximately 60% increase in the peak value of \(v_1\).
- The parton cross-section was varied from 1.5 mb to 10 mb by adjusting the Debye screening mass \(\mu\), with the magnitude of \(v_1\) growing with increasing cross-section.
- The global momentum conservation coefficient \(c\) depends on \(|\Delta\eta|\) for \(|\Delta\eta| < 3\) and flattens at larger \(|\Delta\eta|\); at LHC energy, the value of \(c\) at large \(|\Delta\eta|\) is three times larger than that calculated from Eq.~\ref{eq:v1b} assuming \(K=1\).

## Context

This work extends the ATLAS Collaboration (2012) analysis of dipole asymmetry in heavy-ion collisions by applying their fitting method to the AMPT model, and compares with hydrodynamic predictions from Gardim et al. (2011) which predicted a sign change in \(v_1\) at low \(\pT\). The study enables quantitative predictions for how \(v_1\) varies with transverse momentum, pseudorapidity, centrality, collision energy, and parton cross-section, while also revealing limitations of the AMPT model at high \(\pT\) where jet quenching physics is important. The extended method to extract \(v_1(\pT,\eta)\) provides a framework for validating the event plane method proposed by Luzum (2010) in future experimental analyses.
