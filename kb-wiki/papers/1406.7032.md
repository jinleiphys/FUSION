# Nonlinear electromagnetic response in quark-gluon plasma

> We perform the first systematic study of the nonlinear electromagnetic currents induced by external electromagnetic field in quark-gluon plasma, both in the two cases that the inhomogeneity of electromagnetic field is small (large) so that the collision effect is important (negligible). In the former case, we list and classify possible components of the currents in a systematic way, and make an order estimate of each component by using the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. In the latter case, we explicitly calculate the quadratic current by using the Vlasov equation, and find that the current generated by the chiral magnetic effect and the quadratic current can have the same order of magnitude by using the Kadanoff-Baym equation. We also demonstrate this property by using a possible configuration of electromagnetic field realized in heavy ion collision.

## Key claim

This paper performs the first systematic study of nonlinear (specifically quadratic) electromagnetic currents in quark-gluon plasma, showing that under conditions where the electromagnetic field inhomogeneity is large enough to neglect collisions, the quadratic current can have the same order of magnitude as the current from the chiral magnetic effect, provided the chemical potential is not much larger than the temperature.

## Method

The author analyzes two regimes separately: (1) when the electromagnetic field varies slowly so that collisions dominate, the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation is used to classify and estimate the order of all possible linear and quadratic current components allowed by charge conjugation and parity symmetries; (2) when the field varies rapidly so that collisions are negligible, the Vlasov equation and the Kadanoff-Baym equation with gradient expansion are used to calculate the quadratic current explicitly at next-to-leading order. The equivalence between the Vlasov and Kadanoff-Baym results for the quadratic current is demonstrated.

## Key numbers

* Ohmic (linear) conductivity: \(\sigma_e = \tau m_D^2 / 3\), where \(m_D \equiv e\sqrt{T^2/3 + \mu^2/\pi^2}\) is the Debye mass, and the current is of order \(e^2 \tau T^2 F_{\mu\nu}\).
* Quadratic Hall-type current (zeroth order in \(\tau\partial_X\)): \(\mathbf{j}_2 = (e^3 \tau^2 \mu / 3\pi^2) \, \mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{B}\), of order \(e^3 \tau^2 \mu (F_{\mu\nu})^2\).
* Quadratic current at first order in \(\tau\partial_X\): of order \(e^3 \tau^3 \mu \, \partial_X (F_{\mu\nu})^2\), with explicit form given in Eq. (13) of the paper.
* In the collisionless regime, the quadratic current from the Vlasov equation is of order \(e^3 \mu \, \partial_X^{-2} (F_{\mu\nu})^2\).
* The chiral magnetic effect current is of order \(e^2 \mu_5 F_{\mu\nu}\).
* The condition for the quadratic current to match the linear current in magnitude in the collision-dominated regime is \(F_{\mu\nu} \sim T^2 / (e \mu \tau)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of electromagnetic response in quark-gluon plasma beyond the well-known linear Ohmic current and the chiral magnetic effect current (Fukushima 2008, Kharzeev and Warringa, Son and Surowka 2009). It systematically classifies nonlinear terms that become important when the electromagnetic field is strong, as expected in non-central heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future calculations of nonlinear electromagnetic signatures in heavy-ion collision experiments and provide a framework for comparing the relative importance of different current components under realistic field configurations.
