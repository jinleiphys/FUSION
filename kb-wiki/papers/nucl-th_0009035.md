# Discontinuities in Relativistic Hydrodynamics Across Space-like and Time-like Hypersurfaces

> Discontinuities in relativistic hydrodynamics - shock waves and freeze-out shocks - are considered across both space-like and time-like hypersurfaces. We analyze the peculiar features of the freeze-out discontinuities and their connection to the shock-wave phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper argues that freeze-out (FO) discontinuities on time-like hypersurfaces in relativistic hydrodynamics are physically distinct from normal shock waves: while stable normal shock waves are compression shocks with final velocity lower than initial velocity, stable FO shocks are rarefaction shocks with final velocity higher than initial velocity, provided the fluid velocity exceeds the speed of sound.

## Method

The authors analyze discontinuities across both space-like and time-like hypersurfaces using the conservation laws for energy-momentum and baryonic current, expressed via the Taub adiabate. For freeze-out shocks, they consider a massless ideal gas with zero baryonic number and a Juttner distribution, imposing a theta-function cutoff to remove unphysical negative particle contributions from the Cooper-Frye formula. They derive analytical expressions for the gas velocity, temperature ratio, and entropy production as functions of the fluid velocity in the shock rest frame.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound for the ideal gas equation of state: \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\).
- Minimal fluid velocity for both shock types: \((v_f)_{\text{min}} = p_f/\varepsilon_f = 1/3\).
- For the freeze-out shock, the gas velocity as a function of fluid velocity: \(v_g = (9v_f^2 - 8v_f + 3)/(3v_f^2 + 1)\).
- For the freeze-out shock, the temperature ratio: \(R \equiv (T_f/T_g)^4 = 4(3v_f^2 - 2v_f + 1)^2 (v_f + 1) / [(3v_f - 1)(3v_f^2 + 1)^2]\).
- For the normal shock wave, the gas velocity: \(v_g = 1/(3v_f)\).
- For the normal shock wave, the temperature ratio: \(R \equiv (T_f/T_g)^4 = 3(1 - v_f^2)/(9v_f^2 - 1)\).
- Entropy production in the freeze-out shock: \(s_g^\mu d\sigma_\mu / s_f^\mu d\sigma_\mu = (1/(2v_f)) \left[ (3v_f^2 + 1)^2 (3v_f - 1)/(1 + v_f) \right]^{1/4}\).
- Entropy production in the normal shock wave: \(s_g^\mu d\sigma_\mu / s_f^\mu d\sigma_\mu = (1/(3^{3/4} v_f)) \left[ (9v_f^2 - 1)/(1 - v_f^2) \right]^{1/4}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic shock-wave formalism of Taub (1948) and the freeze-out procedure of Cooper and Frye (1974) to treat discontinuities on time-like hypersurfaces, a possibility first suggested by Csernai (1992). It resolves the problem of unphysical negative particle numbers in the Cooper-Frye formula for time-like freeze-out surfaces by introducing a cutoff distribution, and provides analytical solutions for the freeze-out shock in a massless ideal gas. The results enable self-consistent hydrodynamical modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for hybrid approaches that combine hydrodynamics with microscopic hadron transport as in the work of Bass et al. (1998).
