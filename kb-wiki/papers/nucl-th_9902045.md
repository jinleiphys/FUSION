# Space-time versus particle-hole symmetry of collisions in a non-local kinetic equation for dense Fermi systems

> Binary collisions in Fermi systems obey two fundamental symmetries corresponding to the space and time inversion and to the interchange of particles and holes. We show that beyond the local and instant approximation of scattering-in and -out integrals of a kinetic equation, only one of symmetries can be explicit while the other has to be covered by a constraint. This constraint, derived from the optical theorem, allows one to convert at need an explicit particle-hole symmetric form to the space-time symmetric form and vice versa. We implement this constraint to heavy ion reactions, where simulation algorithms require the space-time symmetry while former theories offer kinetic equations with the explicit particle-hole symmetry.

# Space-time versus particle-hole symmetry of collisions in a non-local kinetic equation for dense Fermi systems

## Key claim

For binary collisions in dense Fermi systems treated beyond the local and instant approximation, the scattering-in and scattering-out integrals cannot simultaneously exhibit explicit space-time symmetry and explicit particle-hole symmetry; only one symmetry can be explicit, while the other is enforced by a constraint derived from the optical theorem, which allows conversion between the two forms as needed.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym equation for non-equilibrium Green's functions and examine the anticommutator structure that yields scattering-in and -out integrals. They apply the optical theorem for the two-particle T-matrix, which relates causal and anti-causal expansions, to rearrange the operator order in the scattering-out term. Using the quasiclassical and quasiparticle approximations, they derive a kinetic equation with non-local corrections expressed as gradients of the scattering phase shift, and show that flipping the sign of the phase shift (by interchanging retarded and advanced T-matrices) reverses all non-local corrections, thereby exchanging which symmetry is explicit.

## Key numbers

- Non-local corrections are linear in the phase shift derivatives: time shift Δ_t = ∂φ/∂Ω, momentum shift Δ_K = (1/2)∂φ/∂r, and spatial displacements Δ_2, Δ_3, Δ_4 given by combinations of ∂φ/∂k, ∂φ/∂p, ∂φ/∂q.
- BUU simulation results are shown for central collision of ²⁰⁹Bi → ¹³⁶Xe at 55 MeV/A, with enhanced production of high-energy protons due to non-local corrections, more pronounced for larger impact parameter and forward/backward angles.

## Context

This work extends the kinetic equation derived by Spicka, Lipavsky and Morawetz (1998) which included Landau-type mean-field and in-medium non-local collisions but lacked explicit space-time symmetry needed for numerical simulations. The constraint resolves a conflict between the classical Enskog theory of gases (Chapman and Cowling, 1990), which has explicit space-time symmetry, and quantum statistical approaches (Baerwinkel, 1969; Laloe, 1989; Nacher, Tastevin and Laloe, 1991; Bornath, Kremp, Kraeft and Schlanges, 1996) that have explicit particle-hole symmetry. The result enables implementation of non-local corrections into Boltzmann equation and quantum molecular dynamics simulations of heavy ion reactions, using the Bruckner approximation for the T-matrix.
