# Analyzing power in elastic scattering of 6He from polarized proton target at 71 MeV/nucleon

> The vector analyzing power has been measured for the elastic scattering of neutron-rich 6He from polarized protons at 71 MeV/nucleon making use of a newly constructed solid polarized proton target operated in a low magnetic field and at high temperature. Two approaches based on local one-body potentials were applied to investigate the spin-orbit interaction between a proton and a 6He nucleus. An optical model analysis revealed that the spin-orbit potential for 6He is characterized by a shallow and long-ranged shape compared with the global systematics of stable nuclei. A semimicroscopic analysis with a alpha+n+n cluster folding model suggests that the interaction between a proton and the alpha core is essentially important in describing the p+6He elastic scattering. The data are also compared with fully microscopic analyses using non-local optical potentials based on nucleon-nucleon g-matrices.

## Key claim

The vector analyzing power for elastic scattering of neutron-rich 6He from polarized protons at 71 MeV/nucleon reveals that the spin-orbit potential for 6He is shallower and longer-ranged than the global systematics for stable nuclei, but the data have large error bars that prevent definitive observation of differences between the analyzing power of p+6He and p+4He.

## Method

The experiment used a solid polarized proton target operated at 0.1 T and 100 K, enabling detection of low-energy recoil protons under inverse kinematics conditions at the RIKEN Accelerator Research Facility. Two local one-body potential approaches were applied: a phenomenological optical model analysis to extract spin-orbit potential parameters, and a semimicroscopic alpha+n+n cluster folding model analysis. The data were also compared with fully microscopic non-local optical potentials based on nucleon-nucleon g-matrices using a large-basis shell model for 6He.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 70.6 +/- 1.4 MeV/nucleon at the center of the secondary target
- Maximum target polarization: 20.4 +/- 3.9% achieved after 40 hours of buildup
- Average polarization during measurement: 13.8 +/- 2.7%
- Target crystal thickness: 116 mg/cm^2 (14 mm diameter, 1 mm thick disk)
- Number of hydrogens per unit area: 4.29 +/- 0.13 x 10^21 /cm^2
- Magnetic field strength: 91 mT applied to the target crystal
- Target operating temperature: 100 K
- Differential cross section at theta_cm = 42.1 degrees: 5.02 +/- 0.52 mb/sr
- Differential cross section at theta_cm = 87.2 degrees: 0.038 +/- 0.012 mb/sr
- Analyzing power at theta_cm = 37.1 degrees: 0.242 +/- 0.069 (statistical) with additional 19% scale error from target polarization uncertainty
- Analyzing power at theta_cm = 74.3 degrees: -0.27 +/- 0.27 (statistical) with additional 19% scale error

## Context

This work extends measurements of vector analyzing power to unstable neutron-rich nuclei, specifically 6He, which has a spatially extended neutron distribution due to its small binding energy. The data are compared with previous measurements for p+4He at 72 MeV (Burzynski 1989) and p+6Li at 72 MeV (Henneck 1994), showing that the analyzing power for 6He resembles that of 4He rather than 6Li, suggesting the importance of the alpha core in describing p+6He elastic scattering. The results enable quantitative evaluation of spin-orbit interactions for unstable nuclei and demonstrate the applicability of the solid polarized proton target for radioactive ion beam experiments.
