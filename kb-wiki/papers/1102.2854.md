# The $\bar{K}NN$ system with chiral dynamics

> We have performed a calculation of the scattering amplitude for the three body system $\bar{K}NN$ assuming $\bar{K}$ scattering against a $NN$ cluster, using the Fixed Center approximation to the Faddeev equations. The $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes, which we take from chiral unitary dynamics, govern the reaction and we find a $\bar{K}NN$ amplitude that peaks around 40 MeV below the $\bar{K}NN$ threshold, with a width in $|T|^2$ of the order of 50 MeV for spin 0 and has another peak around 27 MeV with similar width for spin 1. The results are in line with those obtained using different methods but implementing chiral dynamics. The simplicity of the approach allows one to see the important ingredients responsible for the results. In particular we show the effects from the reduction of the size of the $NN$ cluster due to the interaction with the $\bar{K}$ and those from the explicit considiration of the $ πΣN $ channel in the three body equations.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the $\bar{K}NN$ system exhibits a resonant amplitude with binding energies of approximately 40 MeV (spin 0) and 27 MeV (spin 1) below the $\bar{K}NN$ threshold, but these results depend on the assumed size of the $NN$ cluster and the inclusion of the $\pi \Sigma N$ channel, with the reduced $NN$ radius from Dote (2008) increasing binding by about 5-10 MeV.

## Method

The authors apply the Fixed Center approximation to the Faddeev equations, treating the $\bar{K}$ as scattering against a $NN$ cluster. The $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes are taken from chiral unitary dynamics, and the three-body scattering amplitude $T$ is computed as a function of total energy for spin 0 and spin 1 configurations, using the deuteron form factor and a modified form factor with reduced $NN$ radius from Dote (2008). The $\pi \Sigma N$ channel is explicitly introduced into the three-body system via $\bar{K}N \rightarrow \pi\Sigma$ transitions followed by $\pi N$ rescattering and $\pi\Sigma \rightarrow \bar{K}N$ recombination.

## Key numbers

- For spin 0 ($S=0$): the peak in $|T|^2$ lies at about 40 MeV below the $\bar{K}NN$ threshold with a width of about 50 MeV (using reduced $NN$ radius).
- For spin 1 ($S=1$): the peak lies at about 27 MeV below the $\bar{K}NN$ threshold with a width of about 50 MeV (using reduced $NN$ radius).
- For spin 1 with the deuteron form factor: the peak is at $\sqrt{s}=2350$ MeV, about 22 MeV below threshold, with a width of about 50 MeV.
- The $|T|^2$ strength for spin 0 is 4.5 times larger than for spin 1.
- The reduced $NN$ radius from Dote (2008) is about 2.2 fm, compared to the deuteron r.m.s. radius of 3.98 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior Faddeev calculations by Ikeda (2007), Shevchenko (2006, 2007), and variational calculations by Dote (2008) that used chiral dynamics, all of which found $\bar{K}NN$ binding energies ranging from 20-70 MeV. The Fixed Center approximation provides a simpler, more transparent approach that relies only on on-shell two-body amplitudes, avoiding uncertainties from off-shell extrapolations that affect the AGS-based calculations. The results enable future studies of $\bar{K}NN$ production in reactions and clarify the role of the $\pi \Sigma N$ channel, supporting the conjecture of Schick and Gibson (1978) that its explicit inclusion has small effects.
