# Locating the freeze-out curve in heavy-ion collisions

> Based on transport equations we argue that the chiral dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at high collision energies effectively decouples from the thermal physics of the fireball. With full decoupling at LHC energies the chiral condensate relaxes to its vacuum expectation value on a much shorter time scale than the typical evolution time of the fluid dynamical fields and their fluctuations. In particular, the net-baryon density remains coupled to the bulk evolution at all collision energies. As the mass scales of the hadrons are controlled by the chiral condensate, it is reasonable to employ vacuum masses in the statistical description of the hadron production at the chemical freeze-out for high collision energies. We predict that at lower collision energies the coupling of the chiral condensate to the thermal medium gradually increases with consequences for the related hadronic masses. A new estimate for the location of the freeze-out curve takes these effects into account.

## Key claim

Based on transport equations, the chiral dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at high collision energies effectively decouples from the thermal physics of the fireball, with full decoupling at LHC energies; however, at lower collision energies the coupling of the chiral condensate to the thermal medium gradually increases, with consequences for the related hadronic masses and the location of the freeze-out curve.

## Method

The authors analyze the mean-field evolution equations for the chiral condensate and net-baryon density in the fluid dynamical limit, derived from transport approaches. They examine the relaxation timescales and coupling terms, particularly the dependence on fireball volume and net-baryon density, to determine whether the chiral condensate relaxes to its vacuum value or to its in-medium equilibrium value at chemical freeze-out. Using the proton mass as a representative hadron, they estimate the reduction in freeze-out temperature required when in-medium effects on hadron masses are taken into account.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature at vanishing baryon chemical potential: \(T_c \approx 156\) MeV
- At LHC energies (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2760\) GeV), freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm fo} = 156.5 \pm 1.9\) MeV and baryon chemical potential \(\mu_{B,{\rm fo}} = 0.7 \pm 3.8\) MeV
- At top RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV), freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm fo} = 162 \pm 3.5\) MeV and baryon chemical potential \(\mu_{B,{\rm fo}} = 24^{+3}_{-4}\) MeV
- At SPS energy (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 17.3\) GeV), freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm fo} = 154 \pm 3.1\) MeV and baryon chemical potential \(\mu_{B,{\rm fo}} = 230^{+15}_{-10}\) MeV
- Fireball volume at chemical freeze-out: \(V = 5084\) fm\(^3\) at SPS, \(V = 13440\) fm\(^3\) at top RHIC, \(V = 53856\) fm\(^3\) at LHC
- Coefficient \(\kappa_\sigma = 0.214 \pm 0.045\) determined by requiring \(T_{\rm fo}^{\rm STAR} \leq T_{\rm fo}^{\rm LHC}\)
- At freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm fo} \approx 156\) MeV and zero density, the chiral condensate in equilibrium is \(\sigma(T_{\rm fo},0) \approx 1/2 \, \bar\sigma_0\) (half its vacuum value)

## Context

This work extends the understanding of chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions by addressing a long-standing puzzle: why thermal fits using vacuum hadron masses work well despite in-medium QCD calculations showing significant modifications of hadron properties near the crossover temperature. The authors build on prior studies of chiral dynamics near the phase transition (Nahrgang 2011, Bluhm 2018) and functional QCD results (Fu 2019) to argue that the chiral condensate decouples from the medium at high energies. This provides a theoretical justification for the statistical hadronization model approach (Andronic 2017) at LHC energies, while predicting modified freeze-out conditions at lower energies that could reconcile discrepancies observed at HADES energies. The work enables future system-size scans at LHC to test the volume dependence of freeze-out temperatures.
