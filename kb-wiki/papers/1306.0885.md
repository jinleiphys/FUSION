# Open Charm Production in $p + p$ and Pb + Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Effects of strong longitudinal colour electric fields (SCF), shadowing, and quenching on the open prompt charm mesons (D$^0$, D$^+$, D$^{*+}$, D${_s}{^+}$) production in central Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV are investigated within the framework of the {\small HIJING/BB v2.0} model. We compute the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm PbPb}^{\rm D}$, and show that the above nuclear effects constitute important dynamical mechanisms in the description of experimental data. The strength of colour fields (as characterized by the string tension $κ$), partonic energy loss and jet quenching process lead to a suppression factor consistent with recent published data. Predictions for future beauty mesons measurements have been included. Ratios of strange to non-strange prompt charm mesons in central Pb + Pb and minimum bias (MB) $ p + p$ collisions at 2.76 TeV are also discussed. Minimum bias $p + p$ collisions which constitute theoretical baseline in our calculations are studied at the centre of mass energies $\sqrt{s}$ = 2.76 TeV and 7 TeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strong longitudinal colour electric fields (SCF), characterized by an effective string tension κ that increases with collision energy and nuclear size, can produce open charm mesons via the Schwinger mechanism at rates comparable to perturbative QCD predictions, offering an alternative explanation for the observed suppression of heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions without requiring a quark-mass hierarchy in energy loss.

## Method

The authors use the HIJING/BB v2.0 model, a two-component model combining soft string/rope fragmentation with semi-hard minijet production computed via collinear factorized pQCD with DGLAP evolution. They introduce an energy-dependent string tension κ(s) = κ₀ (s/s₀)^0.04 GeV/fm for p+p collisions and κ(s,A) = κ₀ (s/s₀)^0.04 A^0.167 GeV/fm for A+A collisions, and compute the nuclear modification factor R_PbPb for prompt D mesons (D⁰, D⁺, D*⁺, D_s⁺) in central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, including shadowing and jet quenching effects.

## Key numbers

- String tension in p+p collisions increases from κ = 1.5 GeV/fm at √s = 0.2 TeV (top RHIC energy) to κ = 2.0 GeV/fm at √s = 7 TeV
- String tension in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV is κ ≈ 5 GeV/fm
- For vacuum string tension κ₀ = 1 GeV/fm, the suppression of heavier quark production follows u : d : qq : s : c ≈ 1 : 1 : 0.02 : 0.3 : 10⁻¹¹
- With κ = 3.0 GeV/fm, the charm pair production rate per unit volume increases from ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻¹² fm⁻⁴ to ≈ 3.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻⁴ compared to κ = 1.0 GeV/fm
- The power law for string tension energy dependence uses exponent 0.04, consistent with the square root of charged particle density at midrapidity showing a power law proportional to s^0.05 for inelastic p+p interactions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of strong colour field effects on open charm production at RHIC energies (Pop 2009) and on strange particle production at LHC energies (Topor Pop 2010, 2011). It addresses the "open charm RHIC puzzle" where heavy quarks show similar suppression to light quarks, contradicting expectations from the dead-cone effect (Dokshitzer 2001) and mass-dependent energy loss (Armesto 2005). The model offers an alternative to FONLL pQCD predictions (Cacciari 2012) and GM-VFNS calculations (Kniehl 2012) that overpredict data, while providing predictions for future p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV to disentangle initial-state effects.
