# Enhancement of Intermediate Mass Dileptons From Charm Decays At SPS Energies

> We study the dimuon yield from open charm decays in the intermediate mass region(IMR) in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN-SPS energy. We find that final state rescatterings which broaden the $m_T$ spectrum of charmed mesons can enrich the part of the phase space covered by the NA50 experiment, thus leading to an apparent enhancement of IMR dileptons within the acceptance of the experiment. Such an enhancement increases with the dimuon invariant mass and the single muon energy cut-off.

## Key claim

Final state rescatterings that broaden the transverse-mass spectrum of charmed mesons can produce an apparent enhancement of intermediate-mass dileptons within the NA50 acceptance, even though the total charm yield and total dimuon yield remain unchanged. The enhancement factor depends on the strength of rescatterings and increases with dimuon invariant mass and the single muon energy cut-off.

## Method

The authors modeled D-meson production in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV using PYTHIA with MRS D-prime parton distribution functions, intrinsic parton kt of 1 GeV/c, and charm quark mass of 1.3 GeV/c^2. Final state rescatterings were simulated by thermalizing D-mesons in their local rest frame with a temperature parameter T (default 150 MeV), then boosting back to the lab frame, which broadens the mT spectrum to an inverse slope Teff of about 290 MeV. The dimuon yield from semileptonic D-meson decays (12% branching ratio) was then computed within an approximate NA50 acceptance including cuts on invariant mass, pair rapidity, Collins-Soper angle, and single muon angle and energy.

## Key numbers

- Charm pair cross section in pp collisions: 9.5 ± 2 μb at ELab = 200 GeV, giving 6.8 μb at ELab = 158 GeV
- Overlap function for central Pb+Pb collisions: T_PbPb(0) = 30.4/mb
- D-meson mT inverse slope Teff increases from 160 MeV (initial) to 290 MeV (after rescatterings with T = 150 MeV)
- Enhancement factor R = 3.0 for dimuons from charm decays inside NA50 acceptance with T = 150 MeV (Teff = 290 MeV)
- Initial multiple scatterings give dimuon enhancement less than 10%; anti-shadowing gives enhancement no more than 20%

## Context

This work addresses the observed enhancement of intermediate-mass dileptons (1.5-2.5 GeV/c^2) in NA38/NA50 experiments for S+U and Pb+Pb collisions compared to p+p and p+A collisions. Previous explanations included enhanced charm production from secondary parton scattering (negligible at SPS energies per estimates by Shuryak and others) or secondary meson-meson interactions (Gale and Li, 1995). The authors propose that the enhancement is an acceptance effect from D-meson mT broadening due to final state rescatterings, similar to the collective transverse flow observed for protons and phi-mesons by NA44 (where Teff for protons increases from 148 MeV in pp to 289 MeV in central Pb+Pb). Direct measurements of D-meson spectra are identified as the ultimate test of this model.
