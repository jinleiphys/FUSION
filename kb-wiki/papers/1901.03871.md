# Precision Analysis of the $^{136}$Xe Two-Neutrino $ββ$ Spectrum in KamLAND-Zen and Its Impact on the Quenching of Nuclear Matrix Elements

> We present a precision analysis of the $^{136}$Xe two-neutrino $ββ$ electron spectrum above 0.8 MeV, based on high-statistics data obtained with the KamLAND-Zen experiment. An improved formalism for the two-neutrino $ββ$ rate allows us to measure the ratio of the leading and subleading $2νββ$ nuclear matrix elements (NMEs), $ξ^{2ν}_{31} = -0.26^{+0.31}_{-0.25}$. Theoretical predictions from the nuclear shell model and the majority of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations are consistent with the experimental limit. However, part of the $ξ^{2ν}_{31}$ range allowed by the QRPA is excluded by the present measurement at the 90% confidence level. Our analysis reveals that predicted $ξ^{2ν}_{31}$ values are sensitive to the quenching of NMEs and the competing contributions from low- and high-energy states in the intermediate nucleus. Since these aspects are also at play in neutrinoless $ββ$ decay, $ξ^{2ν}_{31}$ provides new insights towards reliable neutrinoless $ββ$ NMEs.

# Precision Analysis of the $^{136}$Xe Two-Neutrino $\beta\beta$ Spectrum in KamLAND-Zen and Its Impact on the Quenching of Nuclear Matrix Elements

## Key claim
The ratio of subleading to leading two-neutrino double-beta nuclear matrix elements, $\xi^{2\nu}_{31}$, is measured to be $-0.26^{+0.31}_{-0.25}$ from the $^{136}$Xe electron spectrum above 0.8 MeV, with a 90% confidence level upper limit of $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} < 0.26$. This measurement excludes part of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) parameter space at high effective axial-vector coupling values, but the result is conditional on the energy scale systematic uncertainty being the dominant limitation on sensitivity.

## Method
The KamLAND-Zen detector, consisting of 13 tons of xenon-loaded liquid scintillator, collected data over 534.5 days with a fiducial exposure of 126.3 kg yr in $^{136}$Xe. A likelihood fit was performed to the binned energy spectrum of selected candidate events between 0.8 and 4.8 MeV, using an improved formalism for the $2\nu\beta\beta$ rate that includes both leading ($M_{GT}^{2\nu}$) and subleading ($M_{GT-3}^{2\nu}$) nuclear matrix elements. The ratio $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} = M_{GT-3}^{2\nu}/M_{GT}^{2\nu}$ was extracted from the spectral shape, with theoretical predictions from the nuclear shell model (using GCN and MC interactions) and QRPA (using Argonne V18 and CD-Bonn potentials) compared to the experimental limit.

## Key numbers
- $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} = -0.26^{+0.31}_{-0.25}$ (best-fit value from KamLAND-Zen data)
- 90% C.L. upper limit: $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} < 0.26$
- $^{136}$Xe $2\nu\beta\beta$ half-life: $T_{1/2}^{2\nu} = 2.23 \pm 0.03(\text{stat}) \pm 0.07(\text{syst}) \times 10^{21}$ yr
- Fiducial exposure: $(126.3 \pm 3.9)$ kg yr in $^{136}$Xe
- Total systematic uncertainty on $2\nu\beta\beta$ rate: 3.1%
- Shell model predictions: $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} = 0.17$ (GCN interaction) and $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} = 0.10$ (MC interaction)
- QRPA ranges: $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} = (0.11, 0.29)$ for Argonne potential; $\xi^{2\nu}_{31} = (0.11, 0.35)$ for CD-Bonn potential
- QRPA excluded at 90% C.L. for $g_A^{\text{eff}} \gtrsim 1.14$ (Argonne) and $g_A^{\text{eff}} \gtrsim 1.00$ (CD-Bonn)

## Context
This work extends the high-statistics $2\nu\beta\beta$ analysis of KamLAND-Zen (Gando 2016) by introducing the novel observable $\xi^{2\nu}_{31}$, which probes the competition between low- and high-energy intermediate states in the $^{136}$Cs nucleus. The measurement tests nuclear many-body calculations from the nuclear shell model (Caurier 2011, Horoi 2013) and QRPA (Simkovic 2011, Simkovic 2013), and provides constraints on the effective axial-vector coupling $g_A^{\text{eff}}$ that are relevant for neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element calculations. Future experiments such as KamLAND2-Zen (Inoue 2013) with improved resolution and reduced backgrounds promise enhanced sensitivity to further distinguish between theoretical predictions.
