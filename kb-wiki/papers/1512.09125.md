# Acceptance dependence of fluctuation measures near the QCD critical point

> We argue that a crucial determinant of the acceptance dependence of fluctuation measures in heavy-ion collisions is the range of correlations in the momentum space, e.g., in rapidity, $Δy_{\rm corr}$. The value of $Δy_{\rm corr}\sim1$ for critical thermal fluctuations is determined by the thermal rapidity spread of the particles at freezeout, and has little to do with position space correlations, even near the critical point where the spatial correlation length $ξ$ becomes as large as $2-3$ fm (this is in contrast to the magnitudes of the cumulants, which are sensitive to $ξ$). When the acceptance window is large, $Δy\ggΔy_{\rm corr}$, the cumulants of a given particle multiplicity, $κ_k$, scale linearly with $Δy$, or mean multiplicity in acceptance, $\langle N\rangle$, and cumulant ratios are acceptance independent. While in the opposite regime, $Δy\llΔy_{\rm corr}$, the factorial cumulants, $\hatκ_k$, scale as $(Δy)^k$, or $\langle N\rangle^k$. We demonstrate this general behavior quantitatively in a model for critical point fluctuations, which also shows that the dependence on transverse momentum acceptance is very significant. We conclude that extension of rapidity coverage proposed by STAR should significantly increase the magnitude of the critical point fluctuation signatures.

## Key claim

The acceptance dependence of fluctuation measures near the QCD critical point is primarily determined by the range of correlations in momentum space (rapidity), $\Delta y_{\rm corr} \sim 1$, which is set by the thermal rapidity spread of particles at freezeout and has little to do with the spatial correlation length $\xi$, even when $\xi$ becomes as large as 2–3 fm near the critical point.

## Method

The authors model critical point fluctuations using a blast-wave freezeout surface with Bjorken boost invariance, approximating spatial correlations as local (delta-function) on scales where the system size $R \gg \xi$. They compute the critical contribution to cumulants of proton multiplicity by integrating over phase space with thermal distributions, using the coupling of the critical mode $\sigma$ to particle distributions via $\delta f_A = -(\chi_A/\gamma_A) g \sigma(\bm x_A)$, and evaluate the integrals numerically for different rapidity and transverse momentum acceptance windows.

## Key numbers

- The spatial correlation length $\xi$ near the critical point can become as large as $2-3$ fm, but the rapidity correlation range $\Delta y_{\rm corr} \sim 1$ is determined by thermal broadening, not by $\xi$.
- For $\Delta y \ll \Delta y_{\rm corr}$, factorial cumulants $\hat\kappa_k$ scale as $(\Delta y)^k$ or $\langle N \rangle^k$; for $\Delta y \gg \Delta y_{\rm corr}$, cumulants $\kappa_k$ scale linearly with $\Delta y$ and $\omega_k = \kappa_k/\langle N \rangle$ saturates.
- The Bjorken rapidity correlation length from spatial correlations is $\Delta \eta_{\rm corr} \approx \xi/\tau_{\rm f} \sim 0.1-0.3$ (with $\tau_{\rm f} \sim 10$ fm), much smaller than $\Delta y_{\rm corr} \sim 1$.
- The freezeout temperature used is $T \approx 160$ MeV and baryochemical potential $\mu_B \approx 200$ MeV for $\sqrt s = 19.6$ GeV.
- The surface velocity parameter $\beta_s \approx 0.6$ is determined by fitting the inclusive single-particle distribution.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of acceptance dependence for critical fluctuations by Stephanov (2001, 2004) to higher-order cumulants, and complements studies of non-critical correlations by Pruneau (2002), Bzdak (2012), Kitazawa (2014, 2015), and others. The results show that extending rapidity coverage (as proposed by the STAR iTPC upgrade) should significantly increase the magnitude of critical point fluctuation signatures, especially for higher-order cumulants, and that the transverse momentum acceptance dependence is also very significant.
