# Bimodality as a signal of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition

> Here we present an explicit counterexample to a bimodality concept as the unique signal of first order phase transition. Using an exact solution of the simplified version of the statistical multifragmentation model we demonstrate that the bimodal distributions can naturally appear in infinite system without a phase transition in the regions of the negative values of the surface tension coefficient. Also we propose a new parameterization for the compressible nuclear liquid which is consistent with the L. van Hove axioms of statistical mechanics. As a result the proposed model does not lead to the irregular behaviour of the isotherms in the mixed phase region which is typical for mean-field models. Peculiarly, the suggested approach to account for the nuclear liquid compressibility automatically leads to an appearance of an additional state that in many respects resembles the physical antinuclear matter.

# Bimodality as a signal of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that bimodal fragment size distributions can appear in an infinite system without a first-order phase transition, specifically in the region where the surface tension coefficient becomes negative. This provides a counterexample to the claim that bimodality is a unique signal of a first-order phase transition in finite or infinite systems.

## Method
The authors solve the constrained statistical multifragmentation model (CSMM) exactly in the thermodynamic limit using the grand canonical ensemble. They introduce a compressible nuclear liquid parameterization for the liquid phase pressure and a temperature-dependent surface tension coefficient that becomes negative above a critical temperature of 18 MeV. The fragment size distributions are then computed and compared between the gaseous phase and the supercritical temperature region where no phase transition exists by construction.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density at T=0 and zero pressure: ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm³
- Nucleon mass: m ≈ 940 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon at T=0: W₀ = 16 MeV
- Critical temperature for surface tension: T_cep = 18 MeV
- Surface tension coefficient at T=0: σ₀ = 18 MeV
- Fisher topological exponent: τ ≈ 1.825
- Constants for liquid pressure parameterization: a₂ ≈ 1.233·10⁻² MeV⁻¹, a₄ ≈ 4.099·10⁻⁷ MeV⁻³
- Tricritical endpoint appears at one third of the normal nuclear density

## Context
This work extends the constrained statistical multifragmentation model (CSMM) developed by Bugaev (2000, 2005, 2007, 2013) by incorporating a compressible nuclear liquid and a more realistic temperature-dependent surface tension based on an exact solution of the partition function of surface deformations. The results contradict the bimodality concept proposed by Chomaz (2003), Gulminelli (2007), and Hill (who argued that bimodality signals a first-order phase transition in finite systems). The model also produces fragment size distributions resembling those observed in dynamical simulations of nuclear multifragmentation by Campi (2003). The compressible liquid parameterization avoids the irregular isotherm behavior typical of mean-field models and automatically generates an additional state resembling antinuclear matter.
