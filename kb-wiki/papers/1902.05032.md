# Quenching of hadron spectra in XeXe and PbPb collisions at the LHC

> The $p_\perp$ dependence of the nuclear modification factor $R_{\text{AA}}$ measured in PbPb collisions at the LHC exhibits a universal shape, which can be very well reproduced in a simple energy loss model based on the BDMPS medium-induced gluon spectrum. We update a former study by including in the analysis the recent CMS measurements on $J/ψ$ production in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV and charged hadron production in XeXe collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.44$ TeV. The average parton energy loss extracted from minimum bias XeXe collisions is reduced typically by 20% compared to PbPb collisions, consistent with a length dependence $\langle\barε\rangle \propto L^{1.3\pm0.5}$ with $L\propto A^{1/3}$.

# Quenching of hadron spectra in XeXe and PbPb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The universal scaling of the nuclear modification factor R_AA as a function of p_T / (n * omega_c) holds across different collision systems (PbPb and XeXe) and particle species (charged hadrons, D mesons, J/psi), but only for p_T above approximately 10 GeV; scaling violations appear below this threshold, signaling the onset of other phenomena.

## Method
The authors fit a BDMPS-based energy loss model to CMS measurements of R_AA for charged hadrons in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, charged hadrons in XeXe collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.44 TeV, and J/psi and D mesons in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV. The model uses a single free parameter, the energy loss scale omega_c (or equivalently the mean energy loss <epsilon_bar>), determined from 1-parameter fits to each data set for p_T >= 10 GeV (or p_T >= 15 GeV for the most central classes). The shape of R_AA(p_T) is fully predicted once the pp power-law index n is known from fits to pp data.

## Key numbers
- Mean energy loss in most central (0-5%) PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV: <epsilon_bar> = 6.9 GeV
- Mean energy loss in most central XeXe collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.44 TeV: <epsilon_bar> = 5.6 GeV
- Mean energy loss in centrality-integrated (0-100%) PbPb collisions: <epsilon_bar>_PbPb = 4.5 +/- 0.2 GeV
- Mean energy loss in centrality-integrated (0-80%) XeXe collisions: <epsilon_bar>_XeXe = 3.7 +/- 0.2 GeV
- Mean energy loss in minimum bias XeXe is reduced by approximately 20% compared to PbPb
- Extracted length dependence of average energy loss: <epsilon_bar> proportional to L^(1.3 +/- 0.5) with L proportional to A^(1/3)
- Mean energy loss from J/psi data in PbPb: <epsilon_bar>^J/psi_PbPb = 4.3 +/- 0.2 GeV
- Mean energy loss from D meson data in PbPb: <epsilon_bar>^D_PbPb = 4.1 +/- 0.7 GeV
- Mean energy loss in PbPb at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV is roughly 10-20% larger than at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV
- Power law index n ranges from 5.2 to 5.9 depending on particle species and collision energy

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Arleo (2017) which demonstrated universal scaling of charged hadron R_AA in PbPb collisions, by now including recent CMS measurements of J/psi production in PbPb at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV (Sirunyan 2017) and charged hadron production in XeXe collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.44 TeV (Sirunyan 2018). The results support the interpretation that radiative energy loss is the dominant mechanism for hadron quenching at large p_T across all hadron species, including heavy quarkonia. The observed 20% reduction in energy loss from PbPb to XeXe systems, consistent with a length dependence L^(1.3 +/- 0.5), enables future systematic studies of the pathlength and system-size dependence of parton energy loss, and the extracted energy loss values can be compared to transport coefficient estimates from multiplicity density measurements by ALICE (Adam 2015).
