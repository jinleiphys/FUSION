# Fragment productions in DJBUU and SQMD: comparative study

> We study $^{208}$Pb+$^{40,48}$Ca reactions at $E_{\rm beam}$ = 50, 100 AMeV with DJBUU and SQMD transport codes. We compare the large primary fragments from the two codes at the end of the simulation time. We observe that overall the two models produce similar fragments. However, we see a noticeable difference between DJBUU and SQMD at $E_{\rm beam}=100$ AMeV with the impact parameter $b$ = 0 fm and discuss this difference in terms of the difference in the equation of state adopted in the two models and the difference in stability inherent to the BUU-type and QMD-type models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that overall the DJBUU and SQMD transport codes produce similar primary fragments for the reactions studied, but a noticeable difference appears at the higher beam energy of 100 AMeV with a central impact parameter of 0 fm, which the authors attribute to both the difference in the equation of state adopted in the two models and the difference in stability inherent to BUU-type and QMD-type models.

## Method

The authors simulated 208Pb+40,48Ca reactions at beam energies of 50 and 100 AMeV with impact parameters of 0, 3, and 6 fm using the DJBUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck type) and SQMD (Quantum Molecular Dynamics type) transport codes. They compared the largest primary fragments produced by each code at the end of the simulation time (300 fm/c), using ten runs for DJBUU and ten thousand runs for SQMD, with different cluster identification methods: a density-based cell method for DJBUU and a Minimum Spanning Tree algorithm for SQMD.

## Key numbers

*   The nuclear saturation density used in both models is rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3.
*   For DJBUU, the position profile function parameter a_x = 4.2 fm and the momentum profile parameter a_p = s*hbar/a_x with s approximately 0.6.
*   For SQMD, the width of a single nucleon in coordinate space is sigma_r = 1.3 fm.
*   For SQMD, the Skyrme potential parameters are alpha = -218 MeV, beta = 164 MeV, and gamma = 4/3, yielding an incompressibility K = 236 MeV and binding energy of -16 MeV at saturation density.
*   In SQMD, two nucleons are not connected in the cluster algorithm if their minimum distance is longer than 3.5 fm.
*   At 50 AMeV with b = 0 fm, the produced fragments from both models are in the range of A = 162 to 168 with Z = 70 to 74.
*   For the 208Pb+40Ca reaction at 100 AMeV with b = 0 fm, DJBUU produced BFs around A = 121-124 (e.g., 123Ba, 124Ba) while SQMD produced BFs around A = 78-124 (e.g., 78As, 114Sn, 124Xe).
*   For the 208Pb+48Ca reaction at 100 AMeV with b = 0 fm, DJBUU produced BFs around A = 112-116 (e.g., 113Sb, 115Te) while SQMD produced BFs around A = 58-107 (e.g., 58Mn, 74Ge, 107Pd).
*   The symmetry energy density dependence uses sigma = 1 for DJBUU and sigma = 0.7 for SQMD.

## Context

This work compares two transport models, DJBUU and SQMD, which were developed primarily to support physics outcomes at the RAON rare isotope facility in Korea. The study extends prior work on fragment production simulations in heavy ion reactions, referencing studies by Mocko (2008), Gaitanos (2007), Napolitani (2009), and Liu (2014). The authors note that after improvements such as including a surface term in DJBUU and a time-dependent width of nucleons in SQMD, these models will play important roles in studying heavy ion reactions and related physics.
