# Selected Science Opportunities for the EicC

> An electron ion collider has been proposed in China (EicC). It is anticipated that the facility would provide polarised electrons, protons and ion beams, in collisions with large centre-of-mass energy. This discussion highlights its potential to address issues that are central to understanding the emergence of mass within the Standard Model, using examples that range from the exploration of light-meson structure, through measurements of near-threshold heavy-quarkonia production, and on to studies of the spectrum of exotic hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the EicC (Electron Ion Collider in China) could address fundamental questions about the emergence of hadronic mass within the Standard Model, but the discussion is explicitly selective rather than comprehensive, focusing on "a few of the many opportunities" that might be exploited with the facility, subject to its currently anticipated design specifications.

## Method

The paper presents a theoretical survey combining Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) calculations with projected experimental capabilities of the proposed EicC facility, comparing its anticipated luminosity and center-of-mass energy coverage against existing and planned facilities such as JLab at 12 GeV, the EIC at BNL, and COMPASS++/AMBER. The authors use continuum bound-state methods that produced the renormalization group invariant process-independent running coupling to make predictions for observables including the pion electromagnetic form factor, kaon form factor ratios, and near-threshold heavy-quarkonium production.

## Key numbers

- The Higgs boson is responsible for less than 2% of the visible mass in the Universe, with the remainder contained in nuclei
- Each nucleon has a mass m_N approximately 1 GeV, roughly 2000 times the electron mass m_e
- The renormalization group invariant gluon mass-scale m_0 is approximately m_N/2
- The process-independent running coupling at infrared momenta saturates at hat_alpha(0) = pi x 0.97(4)
- The screening mass m_G is approximately 1.4 times Lambda_QCD
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 92.2 MeV
- At Q^2 = 2.45 GeV^2, the empirical value of Q^2 F_pi(Q^2) is 0.41 (+0.04/-0.03), which is a factor of 2.7 larger than the asymptotic pQCD prediction of 0.15 at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2
- The pion mass m_pi is approximately 0.15 m_N, and the rho-meson mass m_rho is greater than 5 m_pi
- The kaon mass m_K is approximately 0.5 GeV

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework developed by Cui et al. (2020) and Rodriguez-Quintero et al. (2018) for QCD's process-independent effective charge, and builds upon the Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of hadron structure by Maris and Tandy (2000), Chang et al. (2013), and Gao et al. (2017). The work positions the proposed EicC to fill a gap between the 12 GeV JLab facility (Dudek et al., 2012; Burkert et al., 2018) and the EIC at Brookhaven National Laboratory, while developing synergy with COMPASS++/AMBER at CERN (Denisov et al., 2018). The paper enables future experimental tests of emergent hadronic mass through measurements of pion and kaon form factors, near-threshold heavy-quarkonium production, and exotic hadron spectroscopy.
