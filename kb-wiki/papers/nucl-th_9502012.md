# Relativistic Effects in Heavy-Ion Collisions at SIS Energies

> The covariant and non-covariant Quantum Molecular Dynamics models are applied to investigate possible relativistic effects in heavy ion collisions at SIS energies. These relativistic effects which arise due to the full covariant treatment of the dynamics are studied at bombarding energies E$_{lab.}$ = 50, 250, 500, 750, 1000, 1250, 1500, 1750 and 2000 MeV/nucl.. A wide range of the impact parameter from b = 0 fm to b = 10 fm is also considered. In the present study, five systems $^{12}$C-$^{12}$C, $^{16}$O-$^{16}$O, $^{20}$Ne-$^{20}$Ne, $^{28}$Si-$^{28}$Si and $^{40}$Ca-$^{40}$Ca are investigated. The full covariant treatment at low energies shows quite good agreement with the corresponding non-covariant approach whereas at higher energies it shows less stopping and hence less thermal equilibrium as compared to the non-covariant approach. The collisions dynamics is less affected. The density using RQMD rises and drops faster than with QMD. The relativistic effects show some influence on the resonance matter production. Overall, the relativistic effects at SIS energies ($\leq$ 2000 MeV/nucl.) are less significant.

## Key claim

At SIS energies (up to 2000 MeV/nucleon), relativistic effects arising from a full covariant treatment of the dynamics are present but "less significant" overall; the covariant approach shows less stopping and less thermal equilibrium than the non-covariant approach at higher energies, while at low energies the two approaches agree closely.

## Method

The authors compared the non-covariant Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model with its covariant extension, Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD), which is based on Constraint Hamiltonian Dynamics. They simulated five symmetric systems (12C-12C, 16O-16O, 20Ne-20Ne, 28Si-28Si, 40Ca-40Ca) at nine bombarding energies from 50 to 2000 MeV/nucleon and impact parameters from 0 to 10 fm, using both soft (K=200 MeV) and hard (K=380 MeV) equations of state. The analysis focused on observables least sensitive to model inputs: nucleon density evolution, collision history, resonance (delta) production, and rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energies studied: 50, 250, 500, 750, 1000, 1250, 1500, 1750, and 2000 MeV/nucleon.
- Impact parameter range: b = 0 fm (central) to b = 10 fm (peripheral).
- At 1.5 GeV/nucleon for 40Ca-40Ca, RQMD shows about 12% reduction in delta population compared to QMD (calculated as (Delta(RQMD)-Delta(QMD))/Delta(QMD) x 100).
- In central collisions of 40Ca-40Ca, about 30% of nuclear matter is converted into delta matter.
- For 40Ca-40Ca with hard EOS, QMD gives 23% delta population (about 18 deltas for 80 baryons).
- At 1 GeV/nucleon, the boost velocity is about 85% of the speed of light.
- The Gaussian wave-packet width parameter L = 1.08 fm^2, corresponding to a nucleon wave-packet root mean square radius of 1.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own development of the RQMD model (Lehmann 1994) and compares it with the standard QMD model of Aichelin (1991). It builds on earlier RQMD work by the Frankfurt group (Sorge 1989) and addresses the question of whether relativistic effects are important at SIS energies, where previous studies had focused on the equation of state. The findings that relativistic effects reduce stopping and delta production have implications for subthreshold production of kaons and antiprotons, as studied by Huang (1994), Spieles (1993), Teis (1992), and Batko (1994), and for the concept of "resonance matter" proposed by Ehehalt (1993), Mosel (1993), and Mina (1994).
