# Low density instability in a nuclear Fermi liquid drop

> The instability of a Fermi-liquid drop with respect to bulk density distortions is considered. It is shown that the presence of the surface strongly reduces the growth rate of the bulk instability of the finite Fermi-liquid drop because of the anomalous dispersion term in the dispersion relation. The instability growth rate is reduced due to the Fermi surface distortions and the relaxation processes. The dependence of the bulk instability on the multipolarity of the particle density fluctuations is demonstrated for two nuclei $^{40}Ca$ and $^{208}Pb$.

# Low density instability in a nuclear Fermi liquid drop

## Key claim

The presence of a sharp surface in a finite Fermi-liquid drop strongly reduces the growth rate of bulk density instabilities compared to infinite nuclear matter, due to the anomalous dispersion term in the dispersion relation; this reduction is further enhanced by Fermi-surface distortion effects and relaxation processes, with the dominant decay mode (binary fission vs. multifragmentation) depending on the multipolarity of the density fluctuation.

## Method

The authors derived a linearized fluid-dynamic equation of motion for small density fluctuations in a finite Fermi-liquid drop with a sharp surface, using the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation for kinetic energy and Skyrme-type forces (SIII) for the interparticle interaction. They obtained dispersion relations for both stable and unstable regimes, incorporating Fermi-surface distortion effects via a pressure tensor with a relaxation time parameter, and applied a boundary condition balancing surface pressure with sound pressure at the free surface. The instability growth rate was then calculated as a function of wave number and multipolarity for the nuclei ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- The saturated nuclear density used was ρ_sat = 0.1453 fm⁻³; calculations were performed at ρ₀ = 0.3 ρ_sat.
- The relaxation time was taken as τ = ℏ α / T² with α = 9.2 MeV and α = 2.6 MeV; calculations were shown at T = 6 MeV.
- The surface tension parameter was 4π r₀² σ = 17.2 MeV.
- The maximum growth rate occurred at q_max/k_F = 0.69 for the conditions studied.
- For ⁴⁰Ca, the lowest multipolarities L ≤ 3 contributed to the instability growth rate, indicating instability with respect to fission.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, multipolarities up to L ≤ 8 contributed, indicating instability with respect to multifragmentation.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear instabilities beyond the infinite nuclear matter treatment of Pethick and Ravenhall (1987) by incorporating finite-size effects and Fermi-surface distortions in a Fermi-liquid drop. It builds on the fluid-dynamic approach of Kolomietz, Mishustin, and Platonov (1984) and the Skyrme-force framework of Engel, Brink, and Goeke (1975). The results enable predictions of whether a hot nucleus will undergo binary fission or multifragmentation based on its mass and excitation conditions, with implications for heavy-ion reaction dynamics and nuclear fission processes.
