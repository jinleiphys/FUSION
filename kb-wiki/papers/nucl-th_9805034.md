# Isotopic and Microcanonical Temperatures in Nuclear Multifragmentation

> A systematic comparison of different isotopic temperatures with the thermodynamical temperature of a multifragment system is made on the basis of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model. It is demonstrated that isotopic temperatures are strongly affected by the secondary decays of hot primary fragments and the population of particle-stable excited states in final fragments. The He-Li temperatures, measured recently by the ALADIN group, are reproduced fairly well both as a function of excitation energy and bound charge. Our analysis confirms the anomaly in the nuclear caloric curve.

# Isotopic and Microcanonical Temperatures in Nuclear Multifragmentation

## Key claim
Isotopic temperatures extracted from fragment yields deviate significantly from the true microcanonical temperature of the multifragment system due to secondary decays and population of particle-stable excited states; however, the ALADIN He-Li temperature data are consistent with the anomaly in the nuclear caloric curve, provided the ensemble of emitting sources is properly reconstructed.

## Method
The authors used the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to simulate the break-up of excited $^{197}$Au nuclei and ensembles of projectile spectator sources from peripheral Au+Au collisions. They calculated microcanonical temperatures by solving the energy balance equation for each break-up channel and then averaging over many channels, while isotopic temperatures were extracted from final (post-secondary-decay) isotope yields using the Albergo double-ratio formula. The model includes both sequential evaporation from heavy fragments and simultaneous Fermi break-up of light fragments ($A \leq 16$), with the free volume parametrized as a function of fragment multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear caloric curve shows a plateau at about 5–6 MeV for excitation energies between 3 and 10 MeV/nucleon in the standard SMM parametrization.
- The maximum fragment charge $Z_{max}$ drops rapidly from 60 to about 6 as excitation energy increases from 3 to 10 MeV/nucleon.
- The number of Intermediate Mass Fragments (IMFs, $3 \leq Z \leq 20$) reaches a maximum of $N_{imf} \approx 8$ at the end of the transition region.
- The He-Li temperature measured for central Au+Au collisions at 35 AMeV is about 4.6 MeV, compared to the SMM microcanonical temperature of about 6 MeV.
- Excited state energies included for light fragments: 3.56 MeV for $^{6}$Li, 0.48 MeV for $^{7}$Li, 0.43 MeV for $^{8}$Li, and 3.37, 5.96, 6.18, and 6.26 MeV for $^{10}$Be.
- The free volume increases to about $2V_0$ at $\varepsilon^* \approx 10$ MeV/nucleon, where $V_0$ is the compound nucleus volume at normal density.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model predictions of Bondorf et al. (1995) for the nuclear caloric curve and provides a systematic comparison with the isotopic temperature method of Albergo et al. (1985). It addresses the ALADIN experimental data of Pochodzalla et al. (1995) and earlier calculations by Kolomiets et al., Huang et al., and Gulminelli et al. that demonstrated the importance of secondary decays. The analysis enables future studies to separate pre-equilibrium emission contributions and to use kinematical analysis of emitting sources for more reliable temperature determination in nuclear multifragmentation experiments.
