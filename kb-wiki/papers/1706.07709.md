# Finite size effect of hadronic matter on its transport coefficients

> We have theoretically investigated the finite system size effect of hadronic matter on its transport coefficients like shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and electrical conductivity. We have used a Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to calculate the thermodynamical quantities like entropy density, speed of sound and also the above transport coefficients. All these quantities are found to be sensitive to finite system size effects of hadronic matter. The effect of finite system size is found to be more when the system is at low temperatures and gets reduced at high temperatures. Owing to the intimate linking between system size and centrality, we have presented the centrality dependence of transport coefficients. We have also explored to link of our results with the macroscopic picture of hydrodynamical evolution.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transport coefficients of hadronic matter (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and electrical conductivity) are sensitive to finite system size effects, with the effect being more pronounced at low temperatures and diminishing at high temperatures; however, this conclusion is hedged by the use of a simplified lower momentum cut-off approximation rather than a full discrete momentum summation, and the relaxation time is arbitrarily fixed at 1 fm for absolute value comparisons.

## Method

The authors used a non-interacting Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model incorporating all hadrons up to 3 GeV mass from the particle data book, implementing finite system size via a lower momentum cut-off \(k_{min} = \pi/R\) for a cubic volume \(V \sim R^3\). Transport coefficients (shear viscosity \(\eta\), bulk viscosity \(\zeta\), and electrical conductivity \(\sigma\)) were calculated using standard kinetic theory expressions in the relaxation time approximation with \(\tau = 1\) fm, integrating momenta from the cut-off to infinity for finite systems and from zero to infinity for infinite systems. The ratios of finite-size to infinite-size transport coefficients were then computed for system radii \(R = 2, 2.5, 3, 4, 5, 6\) fm at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For \(R = 2\) fm at \(T = 160\) MeV, the suppression relative to infinite matter is approximately \(1\%\) for shear viscosity \(\eta\), \(8\%\) for bulk viscosity \(\zeta\), and \(20\%\) for electrical conductivity \(\sigma\).
- The freeze-out system sizes studied range from \(R = 2\) fm to \(R = 6\) fm, motivated by HRG model fits to particle yields in high energy heavy ion collisions.
- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the centrality dependence covers \(N_{\rm part} = 350\) to \(N_{\rm part} = 40\), corresponding to freeze-out radii \(R = 7\) fm to \(R = 3\) fm, temperatures \(T = 0.168\) GeV to \(T = 0.164\) GeV, and baryon chemical potentials \(\mu_B = 0.027\) GeV to \(\mu_B = 0.019\) GeV.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for entropy density used for normalization is \(s_{SB} = \frac{19\pi^2}{9}T^3\) for 3 flavor quarks.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of finite volume effects in the quark-hadron phase transition (Bhattacharyya 2012, Bhattacharyya 2014, Bhattacharyya 2015, Redlich 2016) by applying the same lower momentum cut-off technique to transport coefficient calculations within the HRG model. The results suggest that hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions should consider size-dependent (along with temperature-dependent) values of \(\eta/s\) and \(\zeta/s\) during the complete evolution, particularly for non-central collisions where freeze-out sizes are smaller. The paper also compares its entropy density and speed of sound results with lattice QCD data from the WB group (Borsanyi 2013) and Hot QCD group (Bazavov 2014), finding reasonable agreement.
