# Microscopic mechanism of charged-particle radioactivity and generalization of the Geiger-Nuttall law

> A linear relation for charged-particle emissions is presented starting from the microscopic mechanism of the radioactive decay. It relates the logarithms of the decay half-lives with two variables, called $χ'$ and $ρ'$, which depend upon the $Q$-values of the outgoing clusters as well as the masses and charges of the nuclei involved in the decay. This relation explains well all known cluster decays. It is found to be a generalization of the Geiger-Nuttall law in $α$ radioactivity and therefore we call it the universal decay law. Predictions on the most likely emissions of various clusters are presented by applying the law over the whole nuclear chart. It is seen that the decays of heavier clusters with non-equal proton and neutron numbers are mostly located in the trans-lead region. The emissions of clusters with equal protons and neutrons, like $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, are possible in some neutron-deficient nuclei with $Z\geq54$.

## Key claim

The authors derive a linear relation (the Universal Decay Law, UDL) for charged-particle radioactivity half-lives that generalizes the Geiger-Nuttall law, but the relation holds under the approximation that the formation amplitude depends linearly on the variable ρ' and that higher-order terms in the Coulomb penetration expansion can be neglected for medium and heavier nuclei.

## Method

Starting from the R-matrix expression for the decay width, the authors approximated the Coulomb-Hankel function for the l=0 channel using an analytic formula and expanded the exponent in powers of cosβ, retaining terms up to third order. They defined two dimensionless variables, χ' = ZcZd√(A/Qc) and ρ' = √(AZcZd(Ad^(1/3)+Ac^(1/3))), and showed that log T1/2 depends linearly on χ' and ρ', with the formation amplitude also contributing linearly to the ρ' dependence. The coefficients a, b, and c in the resulting linear relation log T1/2 = aχ' + bρ' + c were determined by fitting to experimental α-decay data (78 ≤ Z ≤ 108) and 11 measured heavier cluster decays (from 14C to 34Si).

## Key numbers

- For α-decay fitting with a as a free parameter: a = 0.4065, b = -0.4311, c = -20.7889, with rms deviation σ = 0.3436
- For α-decay fitting with a fixed at its calculated value (a = 0.4314): b = -0.4608, c = -21.9453, with σ = 0.4606
- For combined α and cluster decay fitting with a fixed: a = 0.4314, b = -0.4087, c = -25.7725, with σ = 0.7631
- The α-decay formation amplitudes log |RFc(R)| for even-even emitters lie in the range -1.5 to -0.75 fm^(-1/2), corresponding to Fc(R) = (0.03 to 0.18)/R fm^(-3/2)
- For heavier cluster decays, log |RFc(R)| ranges from -9 to -3, corresponding to Fc(R) = (10^(-9) to 10^(-3))/R fm^(-3/2)
- The χ' and ρ' values for all observed α and cluster decays span ranges 105 < χ' < 640 and 60 < ρ' < 660, with half-lives covering -8 < log T1/2 < 28 (in seconds)
- For the 14C decay of 220Ra, the predicted half-life is log T1/2 = 14.776 s with -log brel. = 16.142

## Context

This work extends the Geiger-Nuttall law (Geiger and Nuttall, 1911) and the Viola-Seaborg systematics (Viola and Seaborg, 1966) by providing a microscopic derivation that unifies α decay and heavier cluster decay into a single linear relation. The UDL improves upon macroscopic approaches that require effective interactions and spectroscopic factors (Buck and collaborators, 1989-1993; Brown, 1992; Poenaru and collaborators, 2002-2006) by starting from the R-matrix formalism of Thomas (1954). The predictions enable systematic searches for new cluster decay modes, particularly in the trans-lead region for clusters with non-equal proton and neutron numbers, and in neutron-deficient nuclei with Z ≥ 54 for Nc = Zc clusters like 12C and 16O.
