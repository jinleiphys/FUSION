# Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics of N particles

> A Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics for systems of a finite number of degrees of freedom is discussed. Relativistic treatments of quantum theory are needed to study hadronic systems at sub-hadronic distance scales. While direct interaction approaches to relativistic quantum mechanics have proved to be useful, they have two disadvantages. One is that cluster properties are difficult to realize for systems of more than two particles. The second is that the relation to quantum field theories is indirect. Euclidean formulations of relativistic quantum mechanics provide an alternative representation that does not have these difficulties. More surprising, the theory can be formulated entirely in the Euclidean representation without the need for analytic continuation. In this work a Euclidean representation of a relativistic $N$-particle system is discussed. Kernels for systems of N free particles of any spin are given and shown to be reflection positive. Explicit formulas for generators of the Poincaré group for any spin are constructed and shown to be self-adjoint on the Euclidean representation of the Hilbert space. The structure of correlations that preserve both the Euclidean covariance and reflection positivity is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics for N-particle systems that can be constructed entirely in the Euclidean representation without analytic continuation, overcoming difficulties with cluster properties for more than two particles and providing a direct relation to quantum field theory, though the formulation remains phenomenological with kernels that are in principle models of exact Euclidean Green functions of the field theory.

## Method

The authors construct positive-mass irreducible unitary representations of the Poincaré group and use them to build equivalent Lorentz covariant and Euclidean covariant representations for massive particles of any spin. Kernels for systems of N free particles are given and shown to be reflection positive, and explicit formulas for the Poincaré group generators (Hamiltonian, momentum, angular momentum, and boost operators) are constructed and demonstrated to be self-adjoint on the Euclidean representation of the Hilbert space. The structure of correlations that preserve both Euclidean covariance and reflection positivity is discussed.

## Key numbers

- The Euclidean covariant kernel for a particle of mass m and spin s is given by \( S^s_{e}(x_e,\mu;y_e,\nu) := \int d^4p \frac{2}{(2\pi)^4} \frac{e^{i p_e \cdot (x_e - y_e)}}{p_e^2 + m^2} D^s_{\mu \nu}(p_e \cdot \sigma_e) \)
- The mass-squared invariant is \( M^2 = (P^0)^2 - \mathbf{P}^2 = -P^{\mu}P_{\mu} \)
- The spin-squared operator is defined as \( S^2 = W^2/M^2 \) for \( M \neq 0 \), where \( W^{\mu} \) is the Pauli-Lubanski vector
- The basis state normalization is \( \langle (m',s')p',\mu' | (m,s)p,\mu \rangle = \delta_{m'm}\delta_{s's} \delta(\mathbf{p}'-\mathbf{p}) \delta_{\mu' \mu} \)

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean approach to quantum field theory developed by Osterwalder and Schrader (1973, 1974), who identified properties of Euclidean covariant distributions sufficient to reconstruct a relativistic quantum field theory, and builds on earlier Euclidean formulations advocated by Schwinger, Symanzik (1966, 1968), and Nelson (1973). The paper addresses limitations of direct interaction approaches to relativistic quantum mechanics, specifically the difficulty of realizing cluster properties for systems of more than two particles (Sokolov 1977, Coester 1982, Keister 1991) and the indirect relation to quantum field theory. The formulation enables a class of relativistic quantum mechanical models with a more direct connection to lattice calculations and Schwinger-Dyson methods, which are normally formulated in a Euclidean representation.
