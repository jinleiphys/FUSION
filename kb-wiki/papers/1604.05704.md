# The Impact of New Polarization Data from Bonn, Mainz and Jefferson Laboratory on $γp \to πN$ Multipoles

> New data on pion-photoproduction off the proton have been included in the partial wave analyses Bonn-Gatchina and SAID and in the dynamical coupled-channel approach Jülich-Bonn. All reproduce the recent new data well: the double polarization data for E, G, H, P and T in $γp \to π^0 p$ from ELSA, the beam asymmetry $Σ$ for $γp \to π^0 p$ and $π^+ n$ from Jefferson Laboratory, and the precise new differential cross section and beam asymmetry data $Σ$ for $γp \to π^0 p$ from MAMI. The new fit results for the multipoles are compared with predictions not taking into account the new data. The mutual agreement is improved considerably but still far from being perfect.

## Key claim

The inclusion of new double-polarization and beam-asymmetry data from Bonn, Mainz, and Jefferson Laboratory improves the agreement between the multipoles from the Bonn-Gatchina, SAID, and Jülich-Bonn partial-wave analyses, but the mutual agreement among the different approaches remains far from perfect even after refitting.

## Method

The Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa) group used a relativistic operator expansion method with a K-matrix/P-vector approach, recently upgraded to an N/D dispersion-relation technique, to fit photoproduction data. The Jülich-Bonn (JüBo) group employed a dynamical coupled-channel approach solving a Lippmann-Schwinger equation for hadronic scattering and a separate equation for photoproduction, with resonance poles extracted from the complex energy plane. The SAID group used a Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix formalism extended to include an electromagnetic channel, with hadronic parameters fixed from πN elastic scattering fits and only the γN coupling polynomials varied in the photoproduction fit. All three groups refitted their models after including new double-polarization data (E, G, H, P, T) for γp → π⁰p from ELSA, beam asymmetry Σ data for γp → π⁰p and π⁺n from Jefferson Laboratory, and differential cross section and beam asymmetry Σ data for γp → π⁰p from MAMI.

## Key numbers

- The new data included double polarization observables E, G, H, P, and T for γp → π⁰p from ELSA, beam asymmetry Σ for γp → π⁰p and π⁺n from Jefferson Laboratory, and differential cross section and beam asymmetry Σ for γp → π⁰p from MAMI.
- The BnGa analysis used a Blatt-Weisskopf form factor with range parameter r = 0.8 fm to regularize phase volumes at large energies.
- The MAID2007 version describes all 13 four-star resonances below W = 2 GeV, including Δ(1232)3/2⁺, N(1440)1/2⁺, N(1520)3/2⁻, N(1535)1/2⁻, Δ(1620)1/2⁻, N(1650)1/2⁻, N(1675)5/2⁻, N(1680)5/2⁺, Δ(1700)3/2⁻, N(1720)3/2⁺, Δ(1905)5/2⁺, Δ(1910)1/2⁺, and Δ(1950)7/2⁺.
- The MAID approach is fitted up to photon virtualities of Q² ≈ 5 GeV², and for the Δ(1232)3/2⁺ region even up to Q² = 8 GeV².
- The JüBo approach includes hadronic channels πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ and effective three-body channels πΔ, σN, and ρN in the coupled-channel equations.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing effort to resolve the "missing resonance" problem in baryon spectroscopy, where many more three-quark states are predicted by quark models (Capstick 1986, Loring 2001, Giannini 2015) than observed experimentally. The paper compares the multipole predictions from four leading partial-wave analysis groups (BnGa, JüBo, MAID, and SAID) before and after including new polarization data from experiments at Bonn (CBELSA/TAPS), Mainz (MAMI), and Jefferson Laboratory (CLAS). The improved data sets are expected to help determine resonance masses, widths, and helicity amplitudes more reliably, and the convergence of different analyses toward a common multipole solution is a necessary step toward a unified picture of the N* and Δ* resonance spectrum.
