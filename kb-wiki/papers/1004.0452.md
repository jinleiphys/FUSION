# Confinement and Chiral Symmetry in a dense Matter

> 6 pp, Talk presented at workshops "Excited QCD 2010", 31 January - 6 February, 2010, Tatra National Park, Slovakia and "Strongly Interacting Matter under Extreme Conditions", Hirschegg, Kleinwalsertal, Austria, January 17 - 23, 2010

# Confinement and Chiral Symmetry in a Dense Matter

## Key claim
A confining but chirally symmetric subphase of quarkyonic matter may exist at large baryon densities and low temperatures, where the quark-antiquark condensate vanishes due to Pauli blocking while confinement persists because the linear confining potential is not yet Debye screened. This conclusion is drawn from a model with a linear instantaneous Coulomb-like potential in 3+1 dimensions, which is a generalization of the 't Hooft model and is acknowledged to be a great oversimplification of real QCD.

## Method
The author generalizes the 't Hooft model (large N_c QCD in 1+1 dimensions) to 3+1 dimensions by using a model with a linear instantaneous Coulomb-like potential between quarks, neglecting all other gluonic interactions. A gap equation for a probe quark is solved in the presence of a dense baryon matter with a well-defined quark Fermi sea, where all intermediate quark levels below the Fermi momentum P_f are Pauli blocked and do not contribute to the gap equation. The infrared regularization of the linear potential ensures that color-singlet quantities are finite while single quarks have infinite energy, manifesting confinement.

## Key numbers
- The chiral restoration phase transition occurs at sufficiently large Fermi momentum P_f, where the required phase space to create a nontrivial solution with broken chiral symmetry is removed by Pauli blocking.
- Lattice results for N_c=2 QCD suggest that a deconfining transition could occur at densities of the order 100 times nuclear matter density (cited from Ha, no year given in text).
- No explicit numerical values for the chiral restoration density or Fermi momentum are provided in the paper.

## Context
This work extends the concept of quarkyonic matter proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (no year given in text), which describes a strongly interacting matter with confinement and a well-defined Fermi sea of baryons or quarks in the large N_c limit. It contradicts the Skyrmionic description of dense matter that predicts a crystalline structure (cited as S, no year given in text), and instead argues for a liquid phase based on lattice results for N_c=2 QCD (cited as Ha, no year given in text). The paper enables future investigation of whether chiral density waves near the Fermi surface (cited as Ru, no year given in text) can occur in a system with confinement, and opens the question of whether a deconfining transition to quark matter with uncorrelated single quark excitations exists in the real N_c=3 world at densities relevant to future experiments and astrophysics.
