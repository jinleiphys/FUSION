# Phonon contributions to ab initio double mass differences of magic nuclei

> Odd-even double mass differences (DMD) of magic nuclei are found within the approach starting from the free $NN$ interaction with account for particle-phonon coupling (PC) effects. We consider three PC effects: the phonon induced effective interaction, the renormalization of the "ends" due to the $Z$-factor corresponding to the pole PC contribution to the nucleon mass operator and the change of the single-particle energies. The perturbation theory in $g^2_L$, where $g_L$ is the vertex of the $L$-phonon creation, is used for PC calculations. PC corrections to single-particle energies are found self-consistently with an approximate account for the tadpole diagram. Results for magic $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{56,78}$Ni, $^{100,132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb nuclei are presented. For lighter part of this set of nuclei, from $^{40}$Ca till $^{56}$Ni, the cases divide approximately in half between those where the PC corrections to DMD values make agreement with the data better and the ones with the opposite result. In the major part of the cases of worsening of description of DMD, a poor applicability of the perturbation theory for the induced interaction is the most probable reason of the phenomenon. For intermediate nuclei, $^{78}$Ni and $^{100}$Sn, there is no sufficiently accurate data on masses of nuclei necessary for finding DMD values. Finally, for heavier nuclei, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb, PC corrections always make agreement with the experiment better.

## Key claim

The paper finds that particle-phonon coupling (PC) corrections to double mass differences (DMD) of magic nuclei improve agreement with experimental data for heavier nuclei (132Sn and 208Pb), but for lighter nuclei (40Ca to 56Ni) the corrections make agreement better in only about half of the cases, with poor applicability of perturbation theory for the induced interaction being the most probable reason for the worsening of description in the other cases.

## Method

The authors start from the free NN interaction and solve the in-medium Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-particle states in magic nuclei using a semi-microscopic effective pairing interaction (EPI) that combines an ab initio term (solved via the Local Potential Approximation) with a small phenomenological correction parameter gamma = 0.06. They then add three particle-phonon coupling effects in perturbation theory in g_L^2: the phonon induced interaction, the renormalization of the ends via the Z-factor from the pole PC contribution to the nucleon mass operator, and the change of single-particle energies (including the tadpole diagram self-consistently).

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca (proton-proton, D2-): D2^(0) = 3.001 MeV, total PC correction delta D2^PC = -0.866 MeV, giving D2^PC = 2.135 MeV versus experimental D2^exp = 1.94683(19) MeV
- For 40Ca (neutron-neutron, D2-): D2^(0) = 4.064 MeV, delta D2^PC = -1.454 MeV, giving D2^PC = 2.610 MeV versus experimental D2^exp = 2.3395(9) MeV
- For 208Pb (proton-proton, D2-): D2^(0) = 1.004 MeV, delta D2^PC = -0.260 MeV, giving D2^PC = 0.744 MeV versus experimental D2^exp = 0.792(5) MeV
- For 208Pb (neutron-neutron, D2-): D2^(0) = 1.381 MeV, delta D2^PC = -0.331 MeV, giving D2^PC = 1.050 MeV versus experimental D2^exp = 1.007(5) MeV
- For 132Sn (neutron-neutron, D2-): D2^(0) = 1.518 MeV, delta D2^PC = -0.297 MeV, giving D2^PC = 1.221 MeV versus experimental D2^exp = 1.207(5) MeV
- The 3- phonon in 40Ca has theoretical energy omega_L^th = 3.335 MeV (experimental 3.73669(5) MeV) and B(E3) = 1.52 x 10^4 e^2 fm^6 (experimental 1.24 x 10^4 e^2 fm^6)
- The 3- phonon in 208Pb has theoretical energy omega_L^th = 2.684 MeV (experimental 2.615 MeV) and B(E3) = 7.093 x 10^5 e^2 fm^6 (experimental 6.12 x 10^5 e^2 fm^6)

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic model for effective pairing interaction developed by the Moscow-Catania group (Pankr1, Pankr2, Sap1) and its application to double mass differences in non-superfluid nuclei (Gnezd1, Gnezd2, Gnezd3). It builds on earlier ab initio BCS gap calculations by the Milan group (milan1, milan2, milan3) and the low-k force approach of Duguet and Losinsky (Dug1, Dug2), while using the Fayans energy density functional (Fay1, Fay4, Fay5, Fay) with the DF3-a parameter set (Tol-Sap). The results enable systematic inclusion of phonon corrections in calculations of nuclear masses and pairing properties across the nuclear chart, particularly for magic and semi-magic nuclei.
