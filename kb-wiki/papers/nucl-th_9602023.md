# Coherent $π^0$ and $η$ Photoproduction on the Deuteron

> Coherent pion photoproduction, $d(γ,π^0)d$, is calculated in a coupled channels approach in momentum space using an elementary production operator that includes Born terms, vector meson exchange and $M1/E2$ $Δ$-resonance excitation. The final state interaction is treated in the KMT multiple scattering approach that describes $πd$ elastic scattering well. Differential cross sections are found to agree well with existing ($γ, π^0$) data. The polarization observables $Σ, iT_{11}$ and the tensor analyzing powers are predicted for forthcoming experiments. A number of transparent relationships between polarization observables and the elementary production operator is derived. Finally, calculations are performed for coherent eta photoproduction, $d (γ, η)d$, in the threshold region which agree well with a very recent Mainz experiment.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their coupled-channels KMT multiple scattering approach, which neglects coupling to break-up channels, reproduces existing coherent $\pi^0$ photoproduction data on the deuteron and predicts polarization observables, but they note that near threshold the ground-state contribution is very small and coupling to break-up channels becomes important, which may explain why their full calculations are about 15% larger than experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculate coherent $\pi^0$ and $\eta$ photoproduction on the deuteron using a momentum-space coupled-channels approach based on the KMT multiple scattering theory, with an elementary production operator that includes Born terms, vector meson exchange, and $M1/E2$ $\Delta$-resonance excitation. The final state interaction is treated via the KMT multiple scattering approach that describes $\pi d$ elastic scattering, using a unitary version of the Blomqvist-Laget amplitude for the elementary process. The formalism is implemented in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with a factorization approximation for nucleon Fermi motion.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon scattering length used is $a_{\pi N}^{\pi^0\pi^+} = 0.122/m_{\pi}$
- The expectation value $\langle 1/r \rangle = 0.65/m_{\pi}$ for the deuteron ground state
- The isoscalar $s$-wave multipole $E_{0+}^{(+)}$ is found to have a preferred value of $-0.5 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi}$ from comparison with total cross section data
- The elementary amplitude model is valid for photon energies up to $E_{\gamma} = 500$ MeV
- The $D$-wave contribution becomes manifest at momentum transfers $Q^2 > 3.1$ fm$^{-2}$ for energies $E_{\gamma} > 300$ MeV
- At forward and backward angles, the tensor analyzing power $T_{20}(0^\circ) \approx T_{20}(180^\circ) \approx -\sqrt{2}/4$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous KMT multiple scattering approach for pion scattering (Kamalov, Tiator, Bennhold, 1993, 1995) and pion photoproduction on $^3$He (Kamalov, Tiator, Bennhold, 1991, 1992) to coherent photoproduction on the deuteron. It contrasts with the Faddeev approach of Wilhelm and Arenhoevel (1994, 1995) and the relativistic Feynman diagram method of Garcilazo and de Guerra (1995), finding that their results are lower than Garcilazo and de Guerra's and agree better with data except at forward angles. The calculations enable predictions for polarization observables ($\Sigma$, $iT_{11}$, $T_{20}$, $T_{21}$, $T_{22}$) for forthcoming experiments at Mainz, Bonn, Saskatoon, and CEBAF, and provide a framework for separating isospin components of nucleon resonances through eta photoproduction.
