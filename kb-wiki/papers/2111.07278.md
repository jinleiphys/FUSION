# Crustal properties of a neutron star within an effective relativistic mean-field model

> We use the effective relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model to study the crustal properties of the neutron star. The unified equations of state (EoS) are constructed using recently developed E-RMF parameter sets, such as FSUGarnet, IOPB-I, and G3. The outer crust composition is determined using the atomic mass evaluation 2020 data [Chinese Physics C {\bf 45}, 030002 (2021)] along with the available Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 88}, 024308 (2013)] for neutron-rich nuclei. The structure of the inner crust is estimated by performing the compressible liquid drop model calculations using the same E-RMF functional as that for the uniform nuclear matter in the liquid core. Various neutron star properties such as mass-radius ($M-R$) relation, the moment of inertia ($I$), the fractional crustal moment of inertia ($I_{crust}/I$), mass ($M_{crust})$ and thickness ($l_{crust}$) of the crust are calculated with three unified EoSs. The crustal properties are found to be sensitive to the density-dependent symmetry energy and slope parameter advocating the importance of the unified treatment of neutron star EoS. The three unified EoSs, IOPB-I-U, FSUGarnet-U, and G3-U, reproduced the observational data obtained with different pulsars, NICER, and glitch activity and are found suitable for further description of the structure of the neutron star.

# Crustal properties of a neutron star within an effective relativistic mean-field model

## Key claim
The unified equations of state constructed from the E-RMF parameter sets FSUGarnet, IOPB-I, and G3 reproduce observational data from pulsars, NICER, and glitch activity, but only when the same functional is used consistently for the outer crust, inner crust, and liquid core, the crustal properties are sensitive to the density-dependent symmetry energy and slope parameter, which the unified treatment preserves.

## Method
The authors constructed three unified equations of state (IOPB-I-U, FSUGarnet-U, G3-U) by combining: (1) outer crust composition determined via the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland variational formalism using the AME2020 atomic mass evaluation and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models (BSk14, BSk24, BSk26) for neutron-rich nuclei; (2) inner crust structure calculated with a compressible liquid drop model using the same E-RMF functional as the core; and (3) the liquid core described by homogeneous matter in beta-equilibrium using the same E-RMF parameter sets. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations and moment of inertia, with crustal moment of inertia computed from the transition radius to the stellar surface.

## Context
This work extends the unified treatment of neutron star crusts pioneered by Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (1971) and Baym-Bethe-Pethick (1971), and builds on the compressible liquid drop model approach of Carreau et al. (2019, 2020). It differs from prior work by using the exact E-RMF formalism rather than meta-modeling for the inner crust, preserving the underlying properties of each parameter set. The results enable further studies of pulsar glitch behavior through the fractional crustal moment of inertia and provide three unified EoSs suitable for describing neutron star structure from surface to core.
