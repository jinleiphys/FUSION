# Extending the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation to finite temperatures

> In view of the properties of mesons in hot strongly interacting matter the properties of the solutions of the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator at finite temperatures within the rainbow-ladder approximation are analysed in some detail. In Euclidean space within the Matsubara imaginary time formalism the quark propagator is not longer a O(4) symmetric function and possesses a discrete spectra of the fourth component of the momentum. This makes the treatment of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations conceptually different from the vacuum and technically much more involved. The question whether the interaction kernel known from vacuum calculations can be applied at finite temperatures remains still open. We find that, at low temperatures, the model interaction with vacuum parameters provides a reasonable description of the quark propagator, while at temperatures higher than a certain critical value $T_c$ the interaction requires stringent modifications. The general properties of the quark propagator at finite temperatures can be inferred from lattice QCD calculations. We argue that, to achieve a reasonable agreement of the model calculations with that from lattice QCD, the kernel is to be modified in such a way as to screen the infra-red part of the interaction at temperatures larger than $T_c$. For this, we analyse the solutions of the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation with existing interaction kernels in a large temperature range with particular attention on high temperatures in order to find hints to an adequate temperature dependence of the interaction kernel to be further implemented in to the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons. This will allow to investigate the possible in medium modifications of the meson properties as well as the conditions of quark deconfinement in hot matter.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the vacuum interaction kernel for the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation can provide a reasonable description of the quark propagator at low temperatures, but above a certain critical temperature $T_c$ the kernel requires stringent modifications, specifically screening of the infra-red part, to achieve agreement with lattice QCD results.

## Method

The authors solve the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator at finite temperatures within the rainbow-ladder approximation using the Matsubara imaginary-time formalism in Euclidean space. They employ two interaction kernel models (Maris-Tandy and Alkofer-Watson-Weigel) with several parameter sets, introducing a temperature-dependent Debye screening mass $m_g$ in the longitudinal part of the kernel, and solve the coupled system of equations for the dressing functions $A$, $B$, and $C$ by iteration with a cubic spline interpolation procedure.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass is given by $m_g^2 = \alpha_s \frac{\pi}{3} [2 N_c + N_f] T^2$, with the running coupling $\alpha_s$ in one-loop approximation
- Parameter sets used: $\omega = 0.5$ GeV, $D = 1$ GeV$^2$ (AWW and MT1); $\omega = 0.4$ GeV, $D = 0.93$ GeV$^2$ (MT2); bare quark masses $m_u = 5$ MeV, $m_s = 115$ MeV
- Vacuum quark condensate values for all models lie in the narrow corridor $-\langle q \bar{q} \rangle_0 = (0.0145 - 0.0159)$ GeV$^3$
- For finite quark masses, the inflection method determines pseudo-critical temperatures approximately 50% smaller than those from lattice QCD calculations
- A decrease of $m_g^2$ by a factor of 2 results in approximately 15% shift of the solution to larger temperatures
- Summation over Matsubara frequencies truncated at $N_{max} \sim 320$ for low temperatures and $N_{max} \sim 250$ at temperatures above 80-100 MeV
- Cutoff $\Lambda = \mathbf{k}_{max} \simeq 10^3$ GeV/c used in numerical calculations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Dyson-Schwinger equation at finite temperatures by Kitazawa et al. (2006) and Roberts et al. (2007), who found that the infra-red term must vanish abruptly near the critical temperature. The paper aims to find a smooth parametrization of the temperature-dependent interaction kernel that can be implemented into the Bethe-Salpeter equation for studying meson properties in hot matter, addressing the limitation of approaches that use discontinuous step-like modifications or rely on point-by-point fitting of lattice QCD results as done by Fischer et al. (2014).
