# Bohr Hamiltonian with Hulthén plus ring-shaped potential for triaxial nuclei with deformation-dependent mass term

> In this work, we solve the eigenvalues problem with the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for triaxial nuclei within Deformation-Dependent Mass formalism (DDM) using the Hulthén potential. We shall call the solution developed here Z(5)-HDDM. Analytical expressions for energy spectra are derived by means of a recent version of the Asymptotic Iteration Method. The calculated numerical results are compared with the experimental data, and the model Z(5)-H using the Hulthén potential without DDM formalism as well as theoretical predictions of Z(5)-DDDM model with Davidson potential.

# Bohr Hamiltonian with Hulthén plus ring-shaped potential for triaxial nuclei with deformation-dependent mass term

## Key claim

The Z(5)-HDDM model solves the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for triaxial nuclei using a deformation-dependent mass (DDM) formalism with the Hulthén potential, yielding analytical energy spectra that improve agreement with experimental data compared to the constant-mass Z(5)-H model, except for isotopes where the deformation parameter a equals zero, where the results are identical.

## Method

The authors separate the Bohr Hamiltonian into β and γ-angular parts, using a deformation function f(β) = 1 + aβ² (with a << 1) for the mass parameter B(β) = B₀/(f(β))². The β-part is solved with the Hulthén potential u(β) = -1/(e^{τβ} - 1) using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM), while the γ-part uses a ring-shaped potential w(γ) = (c + s cos²(3γ))/sin²(3γ). The free parameters τ, c, s, and a are fitted to experimental data via least-squares minimization for eight isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the Z(5) critical point symmetry framework introduced by Bonatsos et al. (2004) for triaxial nuclei, which describes the transition from prolate axially symmetric SU(3) nuclei to oblate shapes. It builds on the Z(5)-H model of Chabab et al. (2015) that used the Hulthén potential with constant mass, and the Z(5)-DDDM model of Bonatsos et al. (2011) that used the Davidson potential with deformation-dependent mass. The Z(5)-HDDM model improves accuracy for ¹³²Xe, ¹³⁴Xe, and all three Pt isotopes compared to Z(5)-DDDM, while confirming the vibratory nature of ¹²⁸,¹³⁰,¹³²,¹³⁴Xe isotopes. The approach enables testing other flexible potentials for better description of experimental data in triaxial nuclei.
