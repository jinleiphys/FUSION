# Spin and Weak Interactions in Atoms and Nuclei

> The use of spin observables to study the semi-leptonic and non-leptonic weak interaction in atoms and nuclei is surveyed. In particular, the use of semi-leptonic neutral current scattering and atomic parity violation to search for physics beyond the Standard Model is reviewed. The status of nuclear parity violation as a probe of the weak N-N interaction is surveyed. Possible atomic and nuclear signatures of parity conserving, time-reversal violating interactions are also discussed.

## Key claim

Low-energy semi-leptonic parity-violating observables in atoms and nuclei can constrain certain extensions of the Standard Model (such as an unmixed additional neutral gauge boson or fermion compositeness) with sensitivities competitive with or exceeding those from high-energy colliders, provided experimental and theoretical precision can be improved to the 1% level.

## Method

The paper surveys three areas: (1) neutral-current studies using atomic parity violation (APV) in cesium and parity-violating electron scattering from a (0⁺,0) nucleus to extract the nuclear weak charge Q_W and constrain new physics; (2) nuclear parity violation (PV) analyzed via a meson-exchange model with π, ρ, ω exchange to extract weak meson-nucleon couplings h_i from observables such as PV γ decays in ¹⁸F and ¹⁹F, polarized epithermal neutron scattering, and the nuclear anapole moment; (3) parity-conserving time-reversal-violating (PCTV) interactions studied through neutron electric dipole moments (EDMs), atomic EDMs, and the five-fold correlation in epithermal neutron scattering.

## Key numbers

- Present cesium APV determination of Q_W(Cs) has combined experimental and theoretical error of about 2.5%; this constrains the Z_χ mass to greater than about 0.5 TeV.
- A future 1% determination of Q_W(Cs) would push the Z_χ mass bound to 0.8 TeV; a 1% measurement of Q_W(0⁺,0) from PV electron scattering would yield a bound of 0.9 TeV.
- Present cesium results require compositeness scale Λ > 10 TeV; a 1% determination of Q_W(Cs) or Q_W(0⁺,0) would place this lower bound at about 16 TeV.
- Direct CDF searches yield a lower bound on M_{Z'} between 500 and 585 GeV for the Z_χ.
- Upper bounds on the PCTV coupling \bar{g}_ρ from detailed balance and the five-fold correlation are about 2.5 and 22, respectively; limits from the neutron EDM give \bar{g}_ρ < 10^{-3}.
- The cesium APV systematic error is already at the 0.5% level; the present atomic theory error is 1.2%.

## Context

This survey extends the long history of using atomic and nuclear processes to study weak interactions, complementing high-energy Z-pole studies at LEP, SLC, and the Tevatron. It reviews the status of nuclear PV as a probe of the weak N-N interaction, noting that a consistent set of weak couplings h_i has not yet been achieved from existing data, with a discrepancy between constraints from ¹⁸F PV and those from \vec{p}+α, ¹⁹F, and ²¹Ne experiments (Holstein 1995). The work enables future improved constraints on Standard Model extensions, including additional gauge bosons, compositeness, and oblique corrections parameterized by S and T (Peskin and Takeuchi 1990; Marciano and Rosner 1990), as well as on PCTV interactions via neutron and atomic EDM measurements.
