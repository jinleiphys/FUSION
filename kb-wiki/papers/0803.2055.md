# Quark-hadron duality and truncated moments of nucleon structure functions

> We employ a novel new approach to study local quark-hadron duality using "truncated" moments, or integrals of structure functions over restricted regions of x, to determine the degree to which individual resonance regions are dominated by leading twist. Because truncated moments obey the same Q^2 evolution equations as the leading twist parton distributions, this approach makes possible for the first time a description of resonance region data and the phenomenon of quark-hadron duality directly from QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that "truncated moments" (integrals of structure functions over restricted regions of Bjorken $x$) obey the same $Q^2$ evolution equations as leading twist parton distributions, enabling for the first time a quantitative QCD-based test of local quark-hadron duality in individual nucleon resonance regions, with the caveat that the analysis assumes the proton structure function is well approximated by its nonsinglet component at moderate to large $x$.

## Method

The authors apply DGLAP evolution equations to doubly-truncated moments of the proton $F_2$ structure function, evolving leading twist moments from a reference scale of $Q_0^2 = 25$ GeV$^2$ down to lower $Q^2$ values (1–6 GeV$^2$) using next-to-leading order (NLO) nonsinglet evolution with target mass corrections. They compare these evolved leading twist moments to truncated moments computed directly from Jefferson Lab resonance region data (experiment E91-110) across three specific resonance regions: the $\Delta(1232)$ region ($W_{\rm th}^2 \leq W^2 \leq 1.9$ GeV$^2$), the $S_{11}(1535)$ region ($1.9 \leq W^2 \leq 2.5$ GeV$^2$), and the $F_{15}(1680)$ region ($2.5 \leq W^2 \leq 3.1$ GeV$^2$), as well as combinations thereof.

## Key numbers

- The $n=2$ truncated moment of the entire resonance region data ($W_{\rm max}^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$) deviates from leading twist behavior by $\lesssim 15\%$ for all $Q^2$ values from 1 to 6 GeV$^2$, with the data being leading twist dominated at the level of 85–90% over the entire $Q^2$ range.
- Higher twist contributions to the $n=2$ moment for the entire resonance region change sign around $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$, going from approximately $-10\%$ at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$ to approximately $+10$–15% for $Q^2 \approx 5$ GeV$^2$.
- In the $\Delta$ resonance region, higher twist contributions decrease from approximately $-15\%$ of the data at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$ to values consistent with zero at larger $Q^2$.
- In the $S_{11}$ resonance region, higher twists vary between approximately $-15\%$ of the data at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$ and 20–25% at $Q^2 \sim 5$ GeV$^2$.
- For the $n=4$ moment at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$, the higher twist component of the $W^2 < 4$ GeV$^2$ region is approximately 15–20%; for the $n=6$ moment it is approximately 20–30%.
- The nonsinglet evolution approximation introduces errors of order 2–4% for $1.2 \lesssim W_{\rm max} \lesssim 2$ GeV at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$ for the $n=2$ moment, and $\lesssim 2$–3% for $n=4$ and $n=6$ moments.
- Target mass corrections reduce the difference between leading twist moments and data by approximately 40% for large $W_{\rm max}$ at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Bloom-Gilman duality, first observed by Bloom and Gilman (1970), by providing a quantitative QCD framework to test local duality in individual resonance regions, going beyond earlier qualitative analyses by Niculescu et al. (2000) and the operator product expansion approach of De Rujula, Georgi, and Politzer (1977). The truncated moment formalism, originally developed by Forte et al. (2001) to handle small-$x$ extrapolation problems and later reformulated by Kotlorz and Kotlorz (2003), is here extended to doubly-truncated moments for studying the large-$x$ resonance region. This enables future work to systematically quantify higher twist contributions in other nucleon structure functions and to test duality predictions from QCD-inspired models of the nucleon.
