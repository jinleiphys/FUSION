# The range of elementary interactions: a criticism of the standard classroom approach

> The finite scope of the some elementary interactions is usually presented to Physics students as a natural consequence of the time-energy uncertainty relation. It is demonstrated that this heuristic derivation is not a priori valid. Accordingly, it is argued the doubtful usefulness of this "proof" in the classroom, for it may lead to misconceptions.

# The range of elementary interactions: a criticism of the standard classroom approach

## Key claim
The standard textbook derivation of the finite range of elementary interactions from the time-energy uncertainty relation is not a priori valid, because the uncertainty relation provides only a lower bound on the lifetime of a virtual particle, not an upper bound. The usual heuristic therefore requires an additional assumption extraneous to the uncertainty principle itself, and its interpretation is confusing for students.

## Method
The author examines the standard textbook argument that the range of an interaction mediated by a massive particle of mass *m* is given by *l*ₘₐₓ = ℏ/(2*mc*), derived from ΔE = *mc*² and Δt = ℏ/(2ΔE) via the time-energy uncertainty relation. He then applies the correct form of the time-energy uncertainty relation, ΔE Δt ≥ ℏ/2, to show that it yields only a lower bound on Δt and therefore a lower bound on the range *l* ≥ *v*ℏ/(2ΔE), not an upper bound. The author demonstrates that recovering the standard formula requires the extraneous assumption that the actual range is of the order of this lower bound, and that the resulting expression is an upper bound for a lower bound, not a direct upper bound on the range.

## Key numbers
- For a massive mediator of mass *m*, the standard textbook formula gives *l*ₘₐₓ = ℏ/(2*mc*) as the claimed maximum range.
- The correct inequality from the time-energy uncertainty relation is ΔE Δt ≥ ℏ/2, leading to a lower bound *l* ≥ *v*ℏ/(2ΔE) for the range, where *v* is the average speed of the virtual particle and *v* < *c*.

## Context
This paper criticizes a heuristic derivation found in introductory textbooks such as those by Alford and in advanced texts by Krauss and by Hamilton (all cited in the paper). The argument extends to the electromagnetic interaction where the exchanged virtual photon has energy ΔE. The work shows that the standard classroom approach may lead students to incorrectly remember the uncertainty relation as Δt ≤ ℏ/(2ΔE) rather than the correct ΔE Δt ≥ ℏ/2. The paper does not propose an alternative derivation but argues that the standard heuristic should be used carefully or avoided in teaching.
