# Dissecting nucleon transition electromagnetic form factors

> In Poincaré-covariant continuum treatments of the three valence-quark bound-state problem, the force behind dynamical chiral symmetry breaking also generates nonpointlike, interacting diquark correlations in the nucleon and its resonances. We detail the impact of these correlations on the electromagnetically-induced nucleon-$Δ$ and nucleon-Roper transitions, providing a flavour-separation of the latter and associated predictions that can be tested at modern facilities.

## Key claim

The force behind dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) generates nonpointlike, interacting diquark correlations in the nucleon and its resonances, and these correlations have observable consequences for nucleon-to-resonance transition form factors that can be tested at modern facilities, with the caveat that meson-baryon final-state interactions obscure the dressed-quark core at low momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors employed a Poincaré-covariant continuum Faddeev equation approach to describe baryons as bound states of three dressed quarks, with the Faddeev kernel generating nonpointlike diquark correlations. They computed the electromagnetic transition currents for nucleon-to-Delta and nucleon-to-Roper transitions, dissecting the form factors into contributions from different diquark types (scalar, pseudovector, and mixed) and different scattering mechanisms (photon striking a bystander quark, a diquark, or a quark in-flight during diquark breakup).

## Key numbers

- The scalar diquark contributes 62% of the normalisation for both the nucleon and Roper resonance.
- The gamma-p to Delta+ transition has been measured on a domain extending to Q^2 approximately 6 GeV^2.
- The electric quadrupole form factor G_E* for the gamma-p to Delta+ transition possesses a zero at x_Delta = Q^2/m_Delta^2 approximately 3.
- The dressed-quark core of the Roper resonance is revealed to probes with x_N = Q^2/m_N^2 greater than or approximately 3.
- For the flavour-separated Pauli transition form factor of the gamma-p to Roper+ reaction, kappa_u* = F_{2,u}*(0) = -0.91 and kappa_d* = F_{2,d}*(0) = 0.14.
- The ratio of rescaling factors satisfies |kappa_d*/kappa_u*| less than 1/6 for the Roper transition, compared to approximately 2/5 in the elastic case.

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation studies of Segovia (2014) and Segovia (2015) on nucleon-to-resonance transitions, providing a detailed dissection of the form factors into diquark and scattering contributions. The predictions can be tested at Jefferson Lab after the 12 GeV upgrade, particularly with CLAS12, and address whether DCSB shows environment sensitivity and whether quark-quark correlations are essential in all baryons. The results contradict the dynamical coupled-channels analysis of Julia-Diaz (2006) for the electric quadrupole form factor, where the authors argue their Poincaré-covariant result is a better estimate of the dressed-quark core contribution.
