# Application of density functional theory to line broadening: Cs atoms in liquid helium

> We test the applicability of density functional theory (DFT) to spectral perturbations taking an example of a Cs atom surrounded by superfluid helium. The atomic DFT of helium is used to obtain the distribution of helium atoms around the impurity atom, and the electronic DFT is applied to the excitations of the atom, averaging over the ensemble of helium configurations. The shift and broadening of the D1 and D2 absorption lines are quite well reproduced by theory, suggesting that the DFT may be useful for describing spectral perturbations in more complex environments.

## Key claim

Density functional theory (DFT) can reproduce the shift and broadening of the D1 and D2 absorption lines of a Cs atom in superfluid helium, but the calculated peak shift for the D1 line comes out about 20% lower than the measured value, suggesting that either the asymptotic wave function of the Cs atom or the calculated helium bubble radius may be slightly inaccurate.

## Method

The authors used the Orsay-Paris density functional for liquid helium to compute the equilibrium helium density profile around a Cs impurity atom, then randomly sampled helium atom positions from that density distribution to generate an ensemble of configurations. For each configuration, they calculated the first-order perturbation energy shift of the Cs valence electron orbitals (s1/2, p1/2, p3/2) using a contact electron-helium interaction with strength derived from the electron-helium scattering length, and constructed the absorption line shapes by averaging over 100,000 configurations. They also tested the effect of including short-range He-He correlations by imposing a minimum separation distance between helium atoms.

## Key numbers

- The calculated oscillator strength for the D1 and D2 transitions is 1.034, in good agreement with the measured value of 1.058.
- At zero pressure, the effective number of helium atoms contributing to the perturbation (Meff) is approximately 8 for the s1/2 state, increasing to 11-12 at 25 atm.
- The average number of helium atoms in the first shell (r < 8.5 Å) is 27 at P=0 and 45 at P=25 atm.
- Including a van der Waals potential reduces the helium bubble radius by about 0.3 Å at zero pressure and increases the blue shift by about 35 cm^-1.
- With He-He correlations included at P=20 atm, the full width at half maximum of the D1 line is 150 cm^-1, which agrees with the experimental value of 140 cm^-1.
- The helium density maximum at P=20 atm is about 0.0275 Å^-3 at r=7.2 Å.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to model Cs spectra in helium, specifically improving upon the collective vibration bubble model of Kinoshita et al. (1995, 1996) which reproduced average peak shifts but gave line widths less than half the observed values. It also provides a simpler and less computationally expensive alternative to the Path-Integral Monte Carlo approach of Ogata (1999), which succeeded in reproducing the D2 line profile but was very costly. The method enables future application of DFT-based spectral perturbation calculations to more complex systems such as molecules and clusters embedded in liquid helium.
