# Hard exclusive neutrino production of a light meson

> We update the leading order in $α_s$ QCD amplitude for deep exclusive neutrino and antineutrino production of a light meson on an unpolarized nucleon. The factorization theorems of the collinear QCD approach allow us to write the amplitude as the convolution of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. We study both the pseudoscalar meson and longitudinally polarized vector meson cases. It turns out that, contrarily to the electroproduction case, the leading twist scattering amplitudes for $π$ and $ρ_L$ productions are proportional to one another, which may serve as an interesting new test of the leading twist dominance of exclusive processes at medium scale. The dominance of the gluonic contribution to most cross sections is stressed.

## Key claim

The leading-twist QCD amplitudes for exclusive neutrino production of a pion and a longitudinally polarized rho meson are proportional to one another, unlike in electroproduction, which provides a new test of leading-twist dominance at medium scales; the gluonic contribution dominates most cross sections, contrary to previous studies that omitted it.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order-in-αs QCD amplitude for deep exclusive neutrino and antineutrino production of a light pseudoscalar (π) or longitudinally polarized vector (ρL) meson on an unpolarized nucleon. They apply collinear QCD factorization to write the amplitude as a convolution of chiral-even quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) with perturbatively calculable coefficient functions, using the handbag diagrams. The cross sections are evaluated for specific kinematics (s = 13 and 20 GeV², y = 0.3–0.7, Q² up to ~10 GeV²) using the asymptotic pion distribution amplitude and various models for the gluon GPD Eg.

## Key numbers

- fπ = 0.131 GeV and fρ = 0.216 GeV are the meson decay constants used.
- The multiplicative factor for the ρL cross section relative to π is (fρ/fπ)² ≈ 2.72.
- For non-asymptotic pion distribution amplitudes, the cross-section scaling factor |K|² is ≈ 3.2 (Schwinger-Dyson model), ≈ 1.2 and 0.96 (light-front quark model), and ≈ 1.8 (AdS-CFT prediction).
- The differential cross section d³σ/(dy dQ² dt) for ν p → l⁻ π⁺ p at s = 20 GeV², y = 0.7, ΔT = 0 is shown in figures with values up to ~10⁻⁴ pb GeV⁻⁴; the gluon contribution dominates over the quark contribution by a large factor.
- For ν n → l⁻ π⁰ p at s = 20 GeV², ΔT = 0, the cross section is much smaller (no gluon contribution), with values below ~10⁻⁵ pb GeV⁻⁴.

## Context

This work updates earlier calculations by Kopeliovich et al. (2012) that omitted the leading-order gluon contributions, which the authors show dominate the cross section for charged-meson production. The result that π and ρL amplitudes are proportional at leading twist contrasts sharply with the electroproduction case, where different GPDs contribute and the phenomenology has been controversial (as discussed in the literature on pion and rho electroproduction). The study enables future GPD extraction programs (e.g., PARTONS) and can be extended to two-pion production via generalized distribution amplitudes, backward kinematics via transition distribution amplitudes, and nuclear targets with color transparency effects, with relevance to experiments at NOvA, MINERνA, and MINOS+.
