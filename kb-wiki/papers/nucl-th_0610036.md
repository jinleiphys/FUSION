# The Spectator Electromagnetic Effect on Charged Pion Spectra in Peripheral Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We estimate the electromagnetic effect of the spectator charge on the momentum spectra of $π^+$ and $π^-$ produced in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies. We find that the effect is large and results in strongly varying structures in the $x_F$ dependence of the $π^+/π^-$ ratio, especially at low transverse momenta where a deep valley in the above ratio is predicted at $x_F \sim$ 0.15 -- 0.20. It appears that the effect depends on initial conditions. Thus, it provides new information on the space and time evolution of the non-perturbative pion creation process.

# The Spectator Electromagnetic Effect on Charged Pion Spectra in Peripheral Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The electromagnetic field from spectator protons in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies produces large, strongly varying structures in the $x_F$ dependence of the $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratio, especially a deep valley at $x_F \sim 0.15$–0.20 at low transverse momenta. The effect depends on the assumed pion emission time $t_E$, meaning it carries information about the space-time evolution of the non-perturbative pion creation process, but the paper only demonstrates this sensitivity within a simplified model that reduces the emission region to a single point.

## Method
The authors model a peripheral Pb+Pb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17$ GeV with 60 participating nucleons and spectator charge $Q = 70$ elementary units, using a geometrical Monte Carlo simulation with nuclear density profiles from the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach. They reduce the pion emission region to a single point at the interaction point, assume identical initial spectra for $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$ based on a smooth parametrization of nucleon-nucleon data, and numerically trace charged pion trajectories in the combined electric and magnetic fields of the two moving spectator spheres (modelled as uniform spheres with $\rho = 0.17$/fm$^3$) using the Lorentz force with full relativistic retardation, until the pions reach 10,000 fm from the interaction point.

## Key numbers
- Spectator charge per system: $Q = 70$ elementary units, for a collision with $N_{part} = 60$ participating nucleons at impact parameter $b = 10.61$ fm.
- Distance of closest approach between spectator centers of gravity: $b' = 12.13$ fm, after accounting for a displacement $\Delta b = 0.76$ fm of spectator proton centers.
- Spectator sphere radius: $R_s = 6.3$ fm, derived from $N_{spec}$ and $\rho = 0.17$/fm$^3$.
- Spectator velocity: $v_S = 0.994c$, corresponding to $\gamma_s = (1 - v_S^2/c^2)^{-1/2}$.
- Electrostatic constant: $k \approx 1.44$ MeV$\cdot$fm/$e^2$.
- Initial spectator c.m.s. electrostatic potential on a pion: $\frac{kQq}{R} \approx 17$ MeV.
- Pion emission times studied: $t_E = 0, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2$ fm/c; changes of 0.5 fm/c produce visible changes in the $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratio.
- Deep valley in $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratio predicted at $x_F \sim 0.15$–0.20 at low $p_T$ (e.g., $p_T = 25$ MeV/c), with the valley still visible at $p_T = 175$ MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of Coulomb effects in heavy-ion collisions, which at SPS energies had focused on mid-rapidity ($x_F \sim 0$) electromagnetic fields from participant particles (NA44, E866, WA98; theoretical work by Osada et al., 1996). The only published measurement of spectator-induced effects at forward angles ($p_T = 0$) was from NA52. The paper provides the first theoretical description of the spectator electromagnetic effect across the full $x_F$–$p_T$ plane for peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, and demonstrates that the effect is sensitive to the pion formation time, enabling future experimental tests with the NA49 apparatus to constrain the space-time evolution of pion production.
