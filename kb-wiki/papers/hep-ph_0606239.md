# Soliton solutions of the improved quark mass density-dependent model at finite temperature

> The improved quark mass density-dependent model (IQMDD) based on soliton bag model is studied at finite temperature. Appling the finite temperature field theory, the effective potential of the IQMDD model and the bag constant $B(T)$ have been calculated at different temperatures. It is shown that there is a critical temperature $T_{C}\simeq 110 \mathrm{MeV}$. We also calculate the soliton solutions of the IQMDD model at finite tmperature. It turns out that when $T<T_{C}$, there is a bag constant $B(T)$ and the soliton solutions are stable. However, when $T>T_{C}$ the bag constant $B(T)=0$ and there is no soliton solution, therefore, the confinement of quarks are removed quickly.

# Soliton solutions of the improved quark mass density-dependent model at finite temperature

## Key claim

The improved quark mass density-dependent model (IQMDD) exhibits a deconfinement phase transition at a critical temperature of approximately 110 MeV, above which the bag constant vanishes and no stable soliton solutions exist, but this result depends on the specific set of four adjustable parameters chosen (a=70.0 fm^{-2}, b=-2201.8 fm^{-1}, c=20000, g=9.8) and on the one-loop approximation that ignores quantum corrections to the scalar field.

## Method

The authors extended the IQMDD model, which couples quark fields to a nonlinear scalar sigma field via a Friedberg-Lee type potential, to finite temperature by replacing the classical potential U(sigma) with a temperature-dependent effective potential V(sigma;beta) computed under the one-loop approximation using finite temperature field theory following Dolan and Jackiw (1974). They numerically solved the coupled nonlinear differential equations for the sigma field and quark Dirac spinors in spherical symmetry with mean-field approximation, imposing boundary conditions where sigma approaches the temperature-dependent physical vacuum sigma_v(beta) for T below T_C and sigma approaches zero for T above T_C. The bag constant B(T) was calculated as the difference between the effective potential values at the perturbative vacuum (sigma=0) and the physical vacuum (sigma=sigma_v).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_C approximately 110 MeV, defined as the temperature where the two minima of the effective potential become equal
- At T = 149.7 MeV, the second minimum of the effective potential at sigma approximately sigma_v disappears entirely
- The bag constant at zero temperature B_0 is fitted with the analytical formula B_3(T) = B_0 [1 - c1 (T/T_C)^2 - c2 (T/T_C)^4] where c1 = -0.113 and c2 = 1.113
- The fitted parameters for the alternative formula B_1(T) are A_1 approximately -0.67 and A_2 approximately 1.55
- At T = T_C, the total energy of the soliton system decreases to 74% of its zero-temperature value E_0
- The soliton bag radius increases with temperature up to T_C, where the bag disappears

## Context

This work extends the IQMDD model previously developed by Wu et al. (2005) for nucleon properties at zero temperature to finite temperature, and addresses the difficulty that the original QMDD model could not produce the correct T-rho deconfinement phase diagram of QCD because quark masses diverge as density goes to zero (Zhang et al., 2002). The results are qualitatively similar to those obtained in the conventional soliton bag model at finite temperature by Gao et al. (1992), Li et al. (1987), and Wang et al. (1989). Unlike the quark mass density- and temperature dependent (QMDTD) model where B(T) was introduced as an ansatz with adjustable parameters (Zhang et al., 2001; Zhang et al., 2002), this work calculates B(T) directly from the effective potential. The approach enables future study of strange quark matter at finite temperature and extension to chiral soliton models with pion fields as studied by Birse and Banerjee (1983, 1984) and Kahana, Ripka, and Soni (1984).
