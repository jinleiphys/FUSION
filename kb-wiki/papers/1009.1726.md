# Consistent alpha-cluster description of the 12C (0^+_2) resonance

> The near-threshold 12C (0^+_2) resonance provides unique possibility for fast helium burning in stars, as predicted by Hoyle to explain the observed abundance of elements in the Universe. Properties of this resonance are calculated within the framework of the alpha-cluster model whose two-body and three-body effective potentials are tuned to describe the alpha - alpha scattering data, the energies of the 0^+_1 and 0^+_2 states, and the 0^+_1-state root-mean-square radius. The extremely small width of the 0^+_2 state, the 0_2^+ to 0_1^+ monopole transition matrix element, and transition radius are found in remarkable agreement with the experimental data. The 0^+_2-state structure is described as a system of three alpha-particles oscillating between the ground-state-like configuration and the elongated chain configuration whose probability exceeds 0.9.

## Key claim
The alpha-cluster model, with two-body and three-body effective potentials tuned to reproduce alpha-alpha scattering data and the energies and radius of the lowest 12C states, can calculate the extremely small width of the 12C(0+2) resonance and the monopole transition matrix element in agreement with experimental data, provided the 0+2-state energy is precisely fixed to the experimental value.

## Method
The three-body Schrödinger equation for three alpha-particles was solved by expanding the wave function in hyperspherical harmonics on a hypersphere, reducing it to a system of coupled hyperradial equations. The two-body alpha-alpha interaction used a simple Ali-Bodmer form with parameters fitted to low-energy elastic-scattering phase shifts up to 12 MeV and to the experimental energy and width of the 8Be ground state; a Woods-Saxon three-body potential was then tuned to fix the 0+1 and 0+2 state energies and the 0+1 root-mean-square radius at experimental values. The 0+2 resonance was treated using a two-cluster (alpha + 8Be) asymptotic boundary condition for the first-channel hyperradial function, with the width obtained by fitting the scattering phase shift to the Wigner energy dependence.

## Key numbers
- 0+2 state width: Γ = 7.92–8.71 eV across five potential combinations (experimental value: 8.5 ± 1.0 eV)
- Monopole transition matrix element: M12 = 5.270–5.449 fm² (experimental: 5.47 ± 0.09 fm² from Chernykh (2010))
- Transition radius: R12 = 4.836–4.852 fm (experimental: 4.59 ± 0.16 fm from Chernykh (2010))
- 0+2-state root-mean-square radius: R2 ≈ 3.57 fm (no direct experimental value available)
- 0+1-state root-mean-square radius: R1 = 2.48 ± 0.002 fm (experimental, used as input)
- 8Be ground state energy: E2α = 92.04 ± 0.05 keV (experimental, used as input)
- 8Be ground state width: γ = 5.57 ± 0.25 eV (experimental, used as input)
- Probability of elongated chain configuration in 0+2 state: exceeds 0.9

## Context
This work extends earlier alpha-cluster calculations by Fedotov (2004, 2005) that achieved only factor-of-two agreement with experimental width and transition matrix element, by using improved potentials and precise three-body solution methods. The results directly support the sequential decay mechanism (12C → alpha + 8Be → 3α) observed by Freer (1994) and enable future unified treatment of the resonant and non-resonant triple-alpha reaction at low energies, which is important for astrophysical scenarios such as accretion on white dwarfs and neutron stars as discussed by Cameron (1959).
