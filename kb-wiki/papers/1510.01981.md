# Heavy hypernuclei with $A=3$ in a relativistic quark-gluon model

> We generalized our approach to the hypernuclei with $A=B=3$ containing one charm or one bottom quark. We derive the relativistic nine-quark equations using the dispersion relation technique. The hypernuclei as the system of interacting quarks and gluons are considered. The relativistic nine-quark amplitudes of hypernuclei, including the constituent quarks with the charm or bottom are calculated. The approximate solutions of these equations are obtained using a method based on the extraction of leading singularities of the amplitudes. The poles of the multiquark amplitudes allow us to determine the masses and the binding energy of hypernuclei with the $A=3$. We predict the mass spectrum of hypernuclei with $A=3$, which is valuable to further experimental study of the hypernuclei with charm and bottom.

## Key claim
The authors predict masses and binding energies for heavy hypernuclei with baryon number A=3 containing one charm or one bottom quark, using a relativistic quark-gluon model based on dispersion relations. The predictions are approximate because the method extracts only leading singularities of the nine-quark amplitudes, and the gluon coupling constant for bottom hypernuclei is fixed by assuming the mass of the heaviest bottom state (7400 MeV) rather than from experimental data.

## Method
The authors derive relativistic nine-quark equations using the dispersion relation technique, generalizing the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach to systems of nine interacting constituent quarks. Two-particle quark-quark interactions are calculated via the dispersion N/D method with an input four-fermion interaction carrying gluon quantum numbers, using an iterative bootstrap procedure. The system of 23 coupled integral equations for the reduced amplitudes (representing diquark-plus-seven-quark, two-diquark-plus-five-quark, and three-baryon configurations) is solved approximately by extracting two-particle singularities and treating higher partial singularities in the coupled equations, reducing the problem to algebraic equations.

## Key numbers
- Charmed hypernucleus $^3_{\Lambda_c}H$ ($pn\Lambda_c$): mass = 4102 MeV, binding energy = 63 MeV
- Charmed hypernucleus $^3_{\Sigma_c^{++}}He$ ($pp\Sigma_c^{++}$): mass = 4044 MeV, binding energy = 286 MeV
- Charmed hypernucleus $^3_{\Sigma_c^0}He$ ($pp\Sigma_c^0$): mass = 4144 MeV, binding energy = 186 MeV
- Bottom hypernucleus $^3_{\Lambda_b}H$ ($pn\Lambda_b$): mass = 7330 MeV, binding energy = 168 MeV
- Bottom hypernucleus $^3_{\Sigma_b^+}H$ ($pn\Sigma_b^+$): mass = 7366 MeV, binding energy = 323 MeV
- Bottom hypernucleus $^3_{\Sigma_b^-}He$ ($pp\Sigma_b^-$): mass = 7400 MeV, binding energy = 292 MeV
- Constituent quark masses used: $m_{u,d}=495$ MeV, $m_c=1655$ MeV, $m_b=4840$ MeV
- Gluon coupling constant: $g=0.2122$ for charmed hypernuclei, $g=0.1682$ for bottom hypernuclei

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous dispersion-relation approach to light hypernuclei with strangeness (Gerasyuta and Matskevich, 2013) and to the three-nucleon system $^3He$ (Gerasyuta and Matskevich, 2012), now incorporating charm and bottom quarks. The method contrasts with lattice QCD calculations, which the authors note have not yet reached physical light-quark masses for multi-nucleon systems, and with other theoretical approaches such as the chiral SU(3) quark model, the flavor SU(3) skyrmion model, the quark-delocation model, and the quark cluster model. The predicted mass spectra provide targets for experimental programs at DA$\Phi$NE, FAIR, Jefferson Lab, J-PARC, and Mainz.
