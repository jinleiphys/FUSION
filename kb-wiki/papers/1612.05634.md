# Systematic procedure for analyzing cumulants at any order

> We present a systematic procedure for analyzing cumulants to arbitrary order in the context of heavy-ion collisions. It generalizes and improves existing procedures in many respects. In particular, particles which are correlated are allowed to belong to different phase-space windows, which may overlap. It also allows for the analysis of cumulants at any order, using a simple algorithm rather than complicated expressions to be derived and coded by hand. In the case of azimuthal correlations, it automatically corrects to leading order for detector non-uniformity, and it is useful for numerous other applications as well. We discuss several of these applications: anisotropic flow, event-plane correlations, symmetric cumulants, net baryon and net charge fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic procedure for analyzing cumulants to arbitrary order in heavy-ion collisions that generalizes existing frameworks by allowing particles from different, possibly overlapping phase-space windows and by providing a simple algorithm rather than pre-derived expressions, though the practical feasibility at very high orders is ultimately limited by statistical uncertainty rather than the formalism itself.

## Method

The authors develop a general method to express multiparticle correlations (moments) as functions of single-particle sums (flow vectors) by subtracting self-correlations using a partition-based algorithm with weights given by \((-1)^{k-1}(k-1)!\) for blocks of \(k\) identical particles. They then define cumulants (connected correlations) from these moments using a Möbius inversion formula that also sums over set partitions, with weights \((-1)^{n-1}(n-1)!\) where \(n\) is the number of blocks. The procedure is implemented by generating all partitions of the set of particle groups using an algorithm by Orlov, then applying the appropriate weight formulas.

## Key numbers

- The weight for a block of \(k\) elements in self-correlation subtraction is \((-1)^{k-1} (k-1)!\); for \(k=1,2,3\) the weights are \(1, -1, 2\) respectively.
- The weight for a partition with \(n\) blocks in the cumulant expansion is \((-1)^{n-1} (n-1)!\); for \(n=2,3,4\) the weights are \(-1, 2, -6\) respectively.
- The normalization coefficients \(a_{2k}\) for converting cumulants to flow coefficients are: \(a_2=1\), \(a_4=-1\), \(a_6=4\), \(a_8=-33\), \(a_{10}=456\), \(a_{12}=-9460\).
- The factorial moment \(F_n \equiv \langle M(M-1)\cdots(M-n+1)\rangle\) counts the average number of \(n\)-tuples; for a Poisson distribution \(F_n = \langle M \rangle^n\).

## Context

This work extends the cumulant analysis framework of Bilandzic et al. (2010) by removing the assumption of azimuthal symmetry and allowing particles from different rapidity and transverse momentum bins, which enables study of longitudinal decorrelation effects observed by Khachatryan et al. (2015) and Adamczyk et al. (2013). It also generalizes the older generating-function approach of Borghini et al. (2001) by providing exact algebraic expressions to arbitrary order. The framework enables new analyses of anisotropic flow up to arbitrarily high harmonic orders, event-plane correlations, symmetric cumulants, and net baryon and net charge fluctuations, with the practical limit set only by statistical precision.
