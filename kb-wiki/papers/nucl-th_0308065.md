# Chiral effective field theory of Delta(1232) and Compton scattering

> A new power-counting scheme for a chiral effective field theory that includes Delta degrees of freedom is briefly described and applied to the nucleon Compton scattering.

## Key claim
A new power-counting scheme for chiral effective field theory that includes explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom extends the applicability of chiral perturbation theory from photon energies around 100 MeV up into the Delta-resonance region around 293 MeV, provided the hierarchy m_pi << Delta << Lambda (approximately 600 MeV) is recognized and one-Delta-reducible graphs are resummed near the Delta pole.

## Method
The authors introduce a single small expansion parameter delta = Delta/Lambda ~ m_pi/Delta based on a three-scale hierarchy (m_pi << Delta << Lambda). They derive a counting index alpha that takes different forms depending on whether the photon energy omega is near m_pi or near Delta, with one-Delta-reducible graphs receiving an additional enhancement of two negative powers of delta in the Delta region. ## Key numbers
- Delta = M_Delta - M_N ≈ 293 MeV, the excitation energy of the Delta(1232) resonance
- The breakdown scale Lambda ≈ 600 MeV, set by the next excitation above the Delta
- The pi N Delta coupling h_A ≈ 2.8, fixed from the Delta width
- The gamma N Delta couplings: g_M = 2.6, g_E = -6, adjusted to reproduce proton Compton scattering data in the Delta region
- Chiral perturbation theory without Delta degrees of freedom agrees with experiment up to photon energy omega ≈ 100 MeV but fails above 150 MeV

## Context
This work extends chiral perturbation theory (which integrates out the Delta and is limited to photon energies below about 100 MeV) by introducing explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, contrasting with older schemes by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and the small-scale-expansion approach that treat m_pi and Delta as similar scales. The new power-counting enables calculations of nucleon Compton scattering in the Delta-resonance region, as demonstrated by the authors' next-to-leading order results agreeing with experimental data from SAL93, LEGS97, and LEGS01 across a broad energy range.
