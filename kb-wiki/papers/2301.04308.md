# Self-consistent thermodynamic potential for magnetized QCD matter

> Within the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, we derive a self-consistent thermodynamic potential $Ω$ for a QCD matter in an external magnetic field $B$. To be consistent with Schwinger's renormalization spirit, counter terms with vacuum quark mass are introduced into $Ω$ and then the explicit $B$-dependent parts can be regularized in a cutoff-free way. Following that, explicit expressions of gap equation and magnetization can be consistently obtained according to the standard thermodynamic relations. The formalism is able to reproduce the paramagnetic feature of a QCD matter without ambiguity. For more realistic study, a running coupling constant is also adopted to account for the inverse magnetic catalysis effect. It turns out that the running coupling would greatly suppress magnetization at large $B$ and is important to reproduce the temperature enhancement effect to magnetization. The case with finite baryon chemical potential is also explored: no sign of first-order transition is found by varying $B$ for the running coupling and the de Haas-van Alphen oscillation shows up in the small $B$ region.

## Key claim

Within the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, the authors derive a thermodynamic potential for magnetized QCD matter that is self-consistent with Schwinger's renormalization spirit, using counter terms with the vacuum quark mass to regularize the explicitly magnetic-field-dependent parts in a cutoff-free way. The formalism reproduces the paramagnetic feature of QCD matter without ambiguity, but the key hedge is that the running coupling constant is required to reproduce both the inverse magnetic catalysis effect and the temperature enhancement of magnetization.

## Method

The authors start from the two-flavor NJL Lagrangian in Euclidean space with a baryon chemical potential and a magnetic field introduced via minimal coupling. They perform a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce auxiliary fields, integrate out the quark degrees of freedom in the mean-field approximation, and use Schwinger's proper-time method to evaluate the fermion propagator in a magnetic field. The divergent thermodynamic potential is regularized by subtracting counter terms constructed with the vacuum quark mass, following Schwinger's renormalization spirit, and then finite-temperature and finite-chemical-potential contributions are added via the Landau-level representation. The gap equation and magnetization are obtained from the thermodynamic relations ∂Ω/∂m = 0 and ℳ = −∂Ω/∂(eB).

## Key numbers

* Vacuum quark mass m_v = 0.313 GeV, obtained from model parameters m_0 = 5 MeV, Λ = 653 MeV, G(0)Λ² = 2.10, fitted to chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = −2 × (250 MeV)³, pion mass m_π = 135 MeV, and pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV.
* Running coupling form: G(eB) = G(0) / (1 + 0.524 eB²), fitted to LQCD π⁰ mass data for eB ≥ 0.6 GeV².
* At zero temperature and chemical potential, the self-consistent regularization and the vacuum magnetic regularization (VMR) scheme give consistent results with LQCD data for 0 ≤ eB ≤ 0.6 GeV², but diverge for larger B.
* For the running coupling at finite baryon chemical potential, the de Haas–van Alphen oscillation shows up in magnetization at eB ≈ 0.167 GeV² for μ_B = 1 GeV and at eB ≈ 0.375 GeV² for μ_B = 1.5 GeV, determined by √(2q_d|B|) ≈ μ_B/3.
* No sign of first-order transition is found by varying B for the running coupling at finite baryon chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetization in chiral models such as the two-flavor chiral perturbation theory (Hofmann, 2021; Hofmann, 2020), three-flavor Polyakov-linear-sigma model (Tawfik, 2017), and two- and three-flavor (Polyakov-)NJL model (Avancini, 2020; Tavares, 2021). It addresses the regularization problem in the (P)NJL model where previous work (Avancini, 2020) required a cutoff for explicitly magnetic-field-dependent terms and an additional renormalization scheme from LQCD simulations (Bali, 2013) to obtain the correct paramagnetic feature. The self-consistent formalism enables consistent derivation of gap equation and magnetization from the same thermodynamic potential, and the running coupling allows the model to reproduce both the inverse magnetic catalysis effect from LQCD (Bali, 2012) and the temperature enhancement of magnetization found in LQCD simulations (Bali, 2013).
