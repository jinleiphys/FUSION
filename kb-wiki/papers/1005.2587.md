# Holographic Anomalous Conductivities and the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> We calculate anomaly induced conductivities from a holographic gauge theory model using Kubo formulas, making a clear conceptual distinction between thermodynamic state variables such as chemical potentials and external background fields. This allows us to pinpoint ambiguities in previous holographic calculations of the chiral magnetic conductivity. We also calculate the corresponding anomalous current three-point functions in special kinematic regimes. We compare the holographic results to weak coupling calculations using both dimensional regularization and cutoff regularization. In order to reproduce the weak coupling results it is necessary to allow for singular holographic gauge field configurations when a chiral chemical potential is introduced for a chiral charge defined through a gauge invariant but non-conserved chiral density. We argue that this is appropriate for actually addressing charge separation due to the chiral magnetic effect.

## Key claim

By making a clear conceptual distinction between thermodynamic state variables (chemical potentials) and external background gauge fields, the authors show that previous holographic calculations of the chiral magnetic conductivity suffered from ambiguities; when this distinction is maintained, the holographic model reproduces the weak-coupling result for the chiral magnetic effect, but only if singular holographic gauge field configurations are allowed when a chiral chemical potential is introduced for a gauge-invariant but non-conserved chiral density.

## Method

The authors use a holographic model consisting of two Maxwell actions for left and right gauge fields plus Chern-Simons terms in a five-dimensional AdS black hole background, with the Bardeen counter-term enforcing vector gauge invariance. They compute holographic two-point functions of vector and axial currents via Kubo formulas in the zero-frequency, small-momentum limit, and extract anomalous conductivities from the correlators evaluated at vanishing external background fields. They also compute the corresponding three-point functions in special kinematic regimes and compare the holographic results to weak-coupling calculations using both dimensional regularization and cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

* Chiral magnetic conductivity: sigma_CME = N_c / (2 pi^2) * mu_5, where mu_5 is the chiral chemical potential and N_c is the number of colors.
* Axial conductivity: sigma_axial = N_c / (2 pi^2) * mu, where mu is the vector chemical potential.
* Axial-axial conductivity: sigma_55 = N_c / (2 pi^2) * mu_5.
* Three-point function <J^i(k) J^j(-k) J_5^0(0)> = -i k * (N_c / (2 pi^2)) * epsilon_{ij}, independent of chemical potentials and temperature.
* Three-point function <J_5^i(k) J_5^j(-k) J_5^0(0)> = -i k * (1/3) * (N_c / (2 pi^2)) * epsilon_{ij}.
* The Chern-Simons coupling is fixed to kappa = -N_c / (24 pi^2) for a dual strongly coupled SU(N_c) gauge theory with a massless Dirac fermion in the fundamental representation.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of anomalous conductivities in models of QCD, specifically addressing ambiguities in prior calculations by Lifschytz and Yee (2009) and Yee (2009) that did not properly distinguish chemical potentials from background gauge fields. It also corrects the vanishing result for the electromagnetic current found by Rebhan (2009) when using the Bardeen counter-term in the Sakai-Sugimoto model. The results enable a consistent holographic treatment of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, where a chiral chemical potential models the imbalance between left- and right-handed fermions induced by topologically nontrivial gluon configurations, and the authors argue that singular gauge field configurations in the bulk are appropriate for addressing charge separation in inhomogeneous situations.
