# Power spectrum of flow fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We carry out hydrodynamical simulation of the evolution of fluid in relativistic heavy-ion collisions with random initial fluctuations. The time evolution of power spectrum of momentum anisotropies shows very strong correspondence with the physics of cosmic microwave anisotropies as was earlier predicted by some of us. In particular our results demonstrate suppression of superhorizon fluctuations and the correspondence between the location of the first peak in the power spectrum of momentum anisotropies and the length scale of fluctuations and expected freezeout time scale (more precisely, the sound horizon size at freezeout).

# Power spectrum of flow fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Hydrodynamic simulations confirm that the power spectrum of momentum anisotropies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions exhibits a strong correspondence with the cosmic microwave background radiation power spectrum, including suppression of superhorizon fluctuations and acoustic peak structure, but the detailed nature of dissipation and statistical fluctuations has not been analyzed sufficiently to determine whether coherence will produce a visible multiple-peak structure in heavy-ion data.

## Method
The authors performed 2+1 dimensional and full 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions with random initial fluctuations generated from a Wood-Saxon background plus Gaussian fluctuations, using a lattice equation of state for the 2+1D code and an ideal gas equation of state for 2 massless flavors for the 3+1D code. They calculated root-mean-square values of the Fourier coefficients of momentum anisotropies ($v_n^{rms}$) and spatial anisotropies ($F_n^{rms}$) in a lab-fixed frame, then compared the time evolution of these power spectra for different initial fluctuation sizes and evolution times.

## Key numbers
- Initial time scale $\tau_0 = 0.3$ fm with initial temperature $T_0 = 500$ MeV
- Results presented for 150 events, with evolution carried out up to maximum time $\tau = 3.24$ fm
- Wood-Saxon background radius of 2.5 fm with skin thickness of 0.3 to 0.5 fm
- Gaussian fluctuation widths varied from 0.25 fm to 1.6 fm
- For $n$ smaller than about 3-4, the plot of $v_n^{rms}$ drops below the monotonically rising $F_n^{rms}$ plot, demonstrating superhorizon suppression
- For $n$ larger than about 4, both spatial and momentum power spectra show similar patterns
- Oscillations develop early for larger $n$ values (10, 11) compared to smaller $n$ values (7, 9)
- The ratio of first peak to second peak height at $\tau = 3.24$ fm varies with initial fluctuation size $\sigma$

## Context
This work extends the analogy between relativistic heavy-ion collision flow anisotropies and cosmic microwave background anisotropies first proposed by the same authors in their earlier work (Saumia and Srivastava, 2015), where they predicted that the power spectrum of all flow coefficients (including odd ones) should be studied rather than only the first few even coefficients. The results confirm predictions of superhorizon suppression and acoustic peak structure, building on subsequent measurements of odd flow coefficients such as triangular flow $v_3$ by other groups (Sorenson, 2015). The findings enable future studies of viscosity, equation of state constraints, and species-specific power spectra that could probe different fluid components of the quark-gluon plasma, analogous to how CMBR peak ratios reveal baryon-to-dark-matter ratios.
