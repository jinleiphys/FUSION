# Thermal properties of hot and dense medium in interacting hadron resonance gas model

> The meson exchange interaction based on relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory has been introduced in the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, called interacting HRG (iHRG) model. This model can be used to explain the experimental data both at finite temperature ($T$) with finite chemical potential ($μ_B$) and finite temperature at vanishing chemical potential. The nuclear matter equation of state also can be explained at zero temperature with finite baryon density (finite chemical potential) due to the presence of attractive and repulsive interactions between the hadrons in the iHRG model. Similarly, the lattice equation of state is well described at $μ_B$ = 0 and finite temperature by the iHRG model. In the present study, we have calculated the thermodynamical quantities as a function of temperature and chemical potential using both HRG and iHRG models. Also, we have presented the isothermal compressibility ($k_T$), specific heat ($C_V$), and speed of sound ($c_s^2$) as a function of $μ_B$, $T$, and center of mass energies. The effect of kinematic acceptance on these quantities are also presented as a function of $μ$ and $T$. Results from this study on $k_T$ are compared with results from other heavy-ion transport models and experimental data up to LHC energies.

## Key claim

The interacting hadron resonance gas (iHRG) model, which incorporates meson exchange interactions via relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, can simultaneously describe nuclear matter properties at zero temperature with finite baryon density and lattice QCD equation of state at vanishing chemical potential with finite temperature, but the model's prediction for the proton-to-(Lambda+Sigma0) ratio shows the opposite trend to ALICE experimental data, suggesting that non-resonant interactions and coupled-channel effects absent in the RMF-based iHRG model may be needed.

## Method

The authors combined the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with the chiral sigma (CS) model, a relativistic mean-field theory, to create the interacting HRG (iHRG) model. They used the STO-5 parameter set from prior work to define the scalar and vector meson coupling constants, fitting saturation values of nuclear matter binding energy per nucleon (~-14.5 MeV) and baryon density (~0.14 fm^-3). Thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy density, entropy density, specific heat, speed of sound, and isothermal compressibility were calculated as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential for both the ideal HRG and iHRG models, with comparisons to lattice QCD data from HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations and to ALICE experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: approximately -14.5 MeV, with baryon density approximately 0.14 fm^-3, used as fitting targets for the STO-5 parameter set
- Landau mass: 0.85 times the nucleon mass (0.85 m_N), used as a fitting target
- Effective mass of baryons remains at vacuum values up to T ≈ 150 MeV for baryon chemical potential up to μ ≈ 200 MeV; for higher μ ≈ 500 MeV, effective mass starts reducing even at lower T ≈ 70-80 MeV
- For T = 100 MeV, effective mass remains unchanged up to μ ≈ 400 MeV, with sudden decrease at higher chemical potential
- For T ≥ 150 MeV, there is 2-3% reduction of effective mass up to μ = 200 MeV
- The proton-to-(Lambda+Sigma0) ratio from the ideal HRG model at T = 150-160 MeV is slightly above 2.0; the iHRG model increases this ratio further, while ALICE data for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV gives a value around 1.3
- Thermodynamic parameters (p/T^4, epsilon/T^4, s/T^3, C_V/T^3) are the same for any μ up to 200 MeV in the ideal HRG model, with significant differences at higher chemical potential

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cassing (2018) and Huovinen (2017) who included baryon-baryon interactions in hadron gas models, and the chiral sigma model of Sahu (1993, 2010) which described nuclear matter and finite nuclei properties. The iHRG model aims to bridge the gap between the ideal HRG model (which works well at small μ_B and high T up to ~170 MeV but fails at nuclear matter saturation) and RMF models (which describe nuclear matter at zero temperature but fail at finite temperature QCD matter). The results enable future studies of thermodynamic response functions like isothermal compressibility and specific heat as probes of the QCD critical point, and comparisons with upcoming data from FAIR and NICA experiments.
