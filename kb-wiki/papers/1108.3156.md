# Nuclear medium effects in $ν(\barν)$-nucleus deep inelastic scattering

> We study the nuclear medium effects in the weak structure functions $F_2(x,Q^2)$ and $F_3(x,Q^2)$ in the deep inelastic neutrino/antineutrino reactions in nuclei. We use a theoretical model for the nuclear spectral functions which incorporates the conventional nuclear effects, such as Fermi motion, binding and nucleon correlations. We also consider the pion and rho meson cloud contributions calculated from a microscopic model for meson-nucleus self-energies. The calculations have been performed using relativistic nuclear spectral functions which include nucleon correlations. Our results are compared with the experimental data of NuTeV and CDHSW.

## Key claim

The nuclear medium effects on weak structure functions F2 and F3 in neutrino/antineutrino deep inelastic scattering are different from each other, contrary to most phenomenological analyses that assume the same nuclear correction factor for both; the authors find that meson cloud contributions significantly affect F2 at low and intermediate x but are absent in F3, and that the target mass corrections and kinematic factors also differ between the two structure functions.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic nucleon spectral function calculated from nuclear many-body theory in nuclear matter, implemented in finite nuclei via the local density approximation, to describe Fermi motion, binding, and nucleon correlations. They added pion and rho meson cloud contributions to F2 using a microscopic model for meson-nucleus self-energies, incorporated target mass corrections following the formalism of Schienbein et al., and included shadowing effects following the model of Kulagin and Petti. The calculations used CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions at both leading order and next-to-leading order, and the results were compared with experimental data from the NuTeV and CDHSW collaborations for iron targets.

## Key numbers

- Shadowing effect is about 3-5% at x=0.1, Q2=1-5 GeV2 and 1-2% at x=0.2, Q2=1-5 GeV2, dying out with increasing x and Q2.
- A 20% variation in the cutoff parameters Lambda and Lambda_rho (tested at 0.8 GeV, 1.0 GeV, and 1.2 GeV) results in a 4-6% change in the cross section at low x, decreasing to 2-3% around x=0.4-0.5.
- Non-isoscalarity correction is around 2-3%.
- The ratio R(x,Q2)=2F2Fe/(A F2D) and R(x,Q2)=2F3Fe/(A F3D) were calculated at Q2=5 GeV2 and compared with results from Tzanov et al., Hirai et al., Eskola et al., and Schienbein et al.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful application of the same formalism to electromagnetic structure functions (Haider et al., 2011), which agreed with JLab data from Seely et al. and earlier experiments. It contrasts with phenomenological nuclear PDF analyses by groups such as Hirai et al., Eskola et al., and Schienbein et al., which typically use charged lepton and Drell-Yan data and often assume the same nuclear correction for F2 and F3. The results provide predictions for carbon targets that will be testable by the NOMAD collaboration and the proposed NuSOnG experiment, and they demonstrate that separate nuclear correction factors are needed for F2 and F3 in weak interaction analyses.
