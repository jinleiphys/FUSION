# Determination of the nuclear incompressibility from the rapidity-dependent elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies 0.4\emph{A} - 1.0\emph{A} GeV

> Heavy-ion-collision measurements in combination with transport model simulations serve as important tools for extracting the nuclear incompressibility. However, uncertainties in transport models (or model dependence) partly affect the reliability of the extracted result. In the present work, by using the recently measured data of rapidity-dependent flows, we constrain the incompressibility of nuclear matter and analyse the impact of model uncertainties on the obtained value. The method is based on the newly updated version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model in which the Skyrme potential energy-density functional is introduced. Three different Skyrme interactions which give different incompressibilities varying from $K_0$=201 to 271 MeV are adopted. The incompressibility is deduced from the comparison of the UrQMD model simulations and the FOPI data for rapidity-dependent elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at beam energies 0.4\emph{A} - 1.0\emph{A} GeV. The elliptic flow $v_2$ as a function of rapidity $y_0$ can be well described by a quadratic fit $v_2=v_{20} + v_{22}\cdot y_0^2 $. It is found that the quantity $v_{2n}$ defined by $v_{2n}=|v_{20}|+|v_{22}|$ is quite sensitive to the incompressibility $K_0$ and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, but not sensitive to the slope parameter $L$ of the nuclear symmetry energy. With the FU3FP4 parametrization of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, an averaged $K_0 = 220 \pm 40$~MeV is extracted from the $v_{2n}$ of free protons and deuterons. However, remaining systematic uncertainties, partly related to the choice of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, are of the same magnitude ($\pm 40$~MeV). Overall, the rapidity dependent elliptic flow supports a soft symmetric-matter equation-of-state.

## Key claim

The nuclear incompressibility \(K_0\) is extracted from the rapidity-dependent elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions, but the result carries a systematic uncertainty of \(\pm 40\) MeV that is comparable to the statistical uncertainty, partly because the choice of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section shifts the central value by about 35–50 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model with a Skyrme potential energy-density functional, adopting three Skyrme interactions (Skxs15, MSK1, SKX) that give incompressibilities from 201 to 271 MeV. They compared the model simulations to FOPI data for the elliptic flow \(v_2\) as a function of normalized rapidity \(y_0\) in Au+Au collisions at beam energies 0.4\(A\)–1.0\(A\) GeV. The observable \(v_{2n} = |v_{20}| + |v_{22}|\) was constructed from a quadratic fit \(v_2 = v_{20} + v_{22} \cdot y_0^2\) and used to extract \(K_0\) by linear interpolation between the model results and the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The three Skyrme interactions used have incompressibilities \(K_0 = 201\) MeV (Skxs15), 234 MeV (MSK1), and 271 MeV (SKX).
- With the FU3FP4 in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, the extracted \(K_0\) from free protons is \(240 \pm 20\) MeV; from deuterons it is \(190 \pm 10\) MeV; the average is \(K_0 = 220 \pm 40\) MeV.
- With the FU3FP5 parametrization (stronger reduction of the in-medium cross section), the extracted \(K_0\) from free protons is \(275 \pm 25\) MeV; from deuterons it is \(225 \pm 20\) MeV.
- The extracted \(K_0\) from free protons increases with beam energy: \(180 \pm 20\) MeV at 0.4\(A\) GeV and \(280 \pm 17\) MeV at 1.0\(A\) GeV (UrQMD with FU3FP4).
- The slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy differs by 47 MeV between SV-sym34 (\(L = 80.95\) MeV) and MSK1 (\(L = 33.92\) MeV), yet the \(v_{2n}\) values are nearly identical, confirming insensitivity to \(L\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Le Fevre et al. (2015), who used the IQMD model to extract \(K_0 = 190 \pm 30\) MeV from the same \(v_{2n}\) observable. The present study uses the UrQMD model with a Skyrme functional and systematically examines the impact of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, finding that the extracted \(K_0\) shifts by about 35–50 MeV depending on the cross-section parametrization. The results support a soft symmetric-matter equation of state up to about twice saturation density, consistent with constraints from giant monopole resonance studies (Khan et al., 2013) and from kaon yields (Hartnack et al., 2005). The work highlights the need for further model comparisons to reduce systematic uncertainties in the extracted incompressibility.
