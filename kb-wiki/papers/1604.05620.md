# Deuteron potential using the noncritical holographic model

> Deuteron has been studied using a noncritical holographic quantumchromodynamics model constructed in the six-dimensional Anti-de-Sitter supergravity background. The nucleus potential energy is calculated. The binding energy has been estimated as the minimum of the potential energy. The roles of various mesons in producing the deuteron potential energy are discussed in details.

## Key claim
The deuteron binding energy can be reproduced by a noncritical holographic QCD model with a single free parameter \(M_{KK}\), but the result is conditional on tuning that parameter to 372 MeV to match the experimental binding energy of \(-2.2246\) MeV.

## Method
The authors use a noncritical holographic QCD model based on a compactified six-dimensional Anti-de-Sitter (\(AdS_6\)) supergravity background with a constant dilaton. They derive a nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential from the model by expanding baryon and gauge fields in Kaluza-Klein (KK) modes and including exchanges of pseudoscalar (\(\pi\)), isovector vector (\(\rho\)), isoscalar vector (\(\omega\)), and isovector axial-vector (\(a\)) mesons. The deuteron potential is then computed by applying the spin and isospin selection rules for the deuteron (\(S=1\), \(T=0\)) to the holographic NN potential, and the binding energy is taken as the minimum of this potential.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron binding energy is reproduced as approximately \(-2.2246\) MeV when the model's single free parameter, the mass scale \(M_{KK}\), is set to 372 MeV.
- The minimum of the deuteron potential occurs at a separation \(rM_{KK} = 7.62\) (dimensionless product), with a depth of about 2.225 MeV.
- The nucleon mass is taken as \(m_N = 920\) MeV, and the number of KK modes included in the sum is \(P = 10\).
- The number of colors is set to \(N_c = 3\).

## Context
This work extends the noncritical holographic QCD approach of previous studies (e.g., the \(AdS_6\) model of the same group, 2015) to the deuteron, a two-nucleon bound state. It contrasts with phenomenological one-boson-exchange potentials such as AV18, CD-Bonn, and Reid93, which contain many adjustable parameters, by offering a model with a single free parameter. The results enable further holographic studies of nuclear structure and interactions, though the model's predictive power is limited by the need to tune \(M_{KK}\) to the experimental binding energy.
