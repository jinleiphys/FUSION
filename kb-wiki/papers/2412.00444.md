# Impact of nonextensivity on the transport coefficients of strongly interacting QCD matter

> Tsallis nonextensive statistics is applied to study the transport coefficients of strongly interacting matter within the Polyakov chiral SU(3) quark mean field model (PCQMF). Nonextensivity is introduced within the PCQMF model through a dimensionless $q$ parameter to examine the viscous properties such as shear viscosity ($η$), bulk viscosity ($ζ_b$), and conductive properties, including electrical conductivity ($σ_{el}$) and thermal conductivity ($κ$). Additionally, some key thermodynamic quantities relevant to the transport coefficients, like the speed of sound ($c_{sq}^2$) and specific heat at constant volume ($c_{vq}$), are calculated. The temperature dependence of the transport coefficients is explored through a kinetic theory approach with the relaxation time approximation. The results are compared to the extensive case where $q$ approaches 1. The nonextensive $q$ parameter is found to have a significant effect on all transport coefficients. We find that the nonextensive behaviour of the medium enhances both specific shear viscosity $η/s_q$ as well as conductive coefficients $σ_{el}/T$ and $κ/T^2$. In contrast, the normalised bulk viscosity $ζ_b/s_q$ is found to decrease as the nonextensivity of the medium increases. We have also studied the transport coefficients for finite values of chemical potentials. The magnitude of $η$, $σ_{el}$, and $κ$ increases at lower temperatures while $ζ$ is found to decrease for systems with non-zero chemical potential.

# Impact of nonextensivity on the transport coefficients of strongly interacting QCD matter

## Key claim
The Tsallis nonextensive q-parameter significantly modifies all transport coefficients of strongly interacting QCD matter within the Polyakov chiral SU(3) quark mean field model (PCQMF), but the effect is conditional: specific shear viscosity η/s_q and conductive coefficients σ_el/T and κ/T^2 increase with nonextensivity, while normalized bulk viscosity ζ_b/s_q decreases as the medium becomes more nonextensive, with the magnitude of these effects depending on temperature and chemical potential.

## Method
The authors incorporate Tsallis nonextensive statistics into the Polyakov chiral SU(3) quark mean field model (q-PCQMF) through a dimensionless q parameter, modifying the thermodynamic potential density and the quark distribution functions. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity η, bulk viscosity ζ_b, electrical conductivity σ_el, and thermal conductivity κ) are computed using kinetic theory with the relaxation time approximation, where the relaxation time τ depends on the strong coupling constant α_S(T,μ). The coupled equations for scalar fields (σ, ζ, δ, χ), vector fields (ω, ρ, φ), and Polyakov loop fields (Φ, Φ̄) are solved by minimizing the thermodynamic potential density, and the quark backreaction is included by replacing the Polyakov loop potential with the QCD glue potential.

## Key numbers
- At zero baryon chemical potential and T = 200 MeV, the σ field magnitude decreases from 38.97 MeV at q = 1 to 34.49 MeV at q = 1.05 and 30.34 MeV at q = 1.10; the ζ field decreases from 72.49 MeV at q = 1 to 67.27 MeV at q = 1.05 and 61 MeV at q = 1.10.
- At μ_B = 600 MeV, μ_I = -30 MeV, μ_S = 125 MeV, and T = 120 MeV, the σ field magnitude decreases from 76.73 MeV at q = 1 to 70.22 MeV at q = 1.05 and 64.46 MeV at q = 1.10; the ζ field decreases from 92.51 MeV at q = 1 to 90.11 MeV at q = 1.05 and 87.75 MeV at q = 1.10.
- At T = 200 MeV, μ_B = 600 MeV, and q = 1, the effective mass of the u quark is approximately 123 MeV while the d quark effective mass is approximately 119 MeV.
- The relaxation time is given by τ = 1 / [5.1 T α_S² log(1/α_S) (1 + 0.12(2N_f + 1))], with Λ_T = 70 MeV in the strong coupling constant expression.
- The model parameters include vacuum values σ_0 = -93 MeV, ζ_0 = -96.87 MeV, χ_0 = 254.6 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, f_K = 115 MeV, m_π = 139 MeV, m_K = 496 MeV, m_ω = m_ρ = 783 MeV, and m_φ = 1020 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the application of Tsallis nonextensive statistics to transport coefficients within the PCQMF framework, following prior studies that incorporated nonextensivity into effective QCD models such as the q-PNJL model (Zhao 2020, 2021, 2023) and the q-NJL model (Rozy 2009). The results are compared to the extensive case (q → 1) and to lattice QCD calculations (Nakamura, Meyer) that show η/s_q decreasing and ζ_b/s_q increasing near T_c. The study enables future investigations of nonextensive effects on hydrodynamic evolution of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions at facilities like LHC, RHIC, FAIR, and NICA, particularly for systems with finite baryon chemical potential where the q-parameter captures temperature fluctuations and non-equilibrium effects.
