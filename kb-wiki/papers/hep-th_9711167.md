# The Quantum Aspects of Relativistic Fermion Systems with Particle Condensation

> A consistent local approach to the study of interacting relativistic fermion systems with a condensation of bare particles in its ground or vacuum state, which may has a finite matter density, is developed. The attention is payed to some of the not so well explored quantum aspects that survive the thermodynamic limit. A 4-vector local field, called the primary statistical gauge field, and a statistical blocking parameter are introduced for a consistent treatment of the problem. The effects of random fluctuations of the fields on local observables are discussed. It is found that quasiparticle contributions are not sufficient to saturate local observables. The property of the primary statistical gauge field are discussed in some detail. Two models for the strong interaction are then introduced and studied using the general framework developed. Four possible phases for these models are found. The possibility of spontaneous CP violation and local fermion creation in two of the four phases is revealed. The implications of the finding on our understanding of some of the strong interaction processes are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in relativistic fermion systems with particle condensation, the conventional quasiparticle picture and global chemical potential approach are insufficient to saturate local observables, necessitating a local formalism with a primary statistical gauge field and a statistical blocking parameter to capture quantum fluctuation effects that survive the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The author develops a local quantum field theory framework using an 8-component "real" representation for fermion fields, introducing a 4-vector primary statistical gauge field μ^α(x) to replace the global chemical potential and a statistical blocking parameter ε. The formalism distinguishes between Minkowski and Euclidean spacetime formulations, where the Euclidean approach captures quantum penetration effects into classically forbidden field configurations that the quasiparticle approximation misses. Two 4-fermion interaction models for strong interactions are then analyzed using this framework to determine their vacuum phase structures.

## Key numbers

- Four possible phases are found for the two strong interaction models: the O phase (trivial/bare vacuum), the α phase (fermion-antifermion pair condensation), the β phase (fermion pair condensation), and the ω phase (antifermion pair condensation)
- Spontaneous CP violation is revealed to occur in two of the four phases (the β and ω phases)
- Local fermion creation is shown to appear naturally in the β and ω phases

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Ying, 1995; Ying, 1996) where fermion pair condensation leading to the β phase within the chiral symmetry breaking chain SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R → SU(2)_V was demonstrated. The paper addresses limitations of the grand canonical ensemble treatment of finite density problems in relativistic QFT, particularly the formal breakdown of equivalence between apparent and absolute particle numbers in the thermodynamic limit. The framework enables systematic study of vacuum phases with macroscopic particle condensation, with implications for understanding strong interaction processes and cosmological matter creation.
