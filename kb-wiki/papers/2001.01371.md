# Vorticity in low-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We study the kinematic and thermal vorticities in low-energy heavy-ion collisions by using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. We explore their time evolution and spatial distribution. We find that the initial vorticities have a non-monotonic dependence on the collision energy $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$: as $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ grows the vorticities first increase steeply and then decrease with the turning point around $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\sim 3-5$ GeV depending on the centrality.

## Key claim

The initial kinematic and thermal vorticities in low-energy heavy-ion collisions exhibit a non-monotonic dependence on collision energy, first increasing steeply and then decreasing, with a turning point around 3-5 GeV depending on centrality; this behavior is inferred from UrQMD simulations and is consistent with the trend of global Lambda polarization measurements from HADES, STAR, and ALICE, but the authors caution that the actual calculation of Lambda polarization requires more detailed study because the mean freeze-out time is not short and a non-equilibrium treatment is needed at low energies.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 1.9 to 50 GeV per nucleon pair. From the simulated particle positions and momenta, they computed the fluid three-velocity and energy density using Gaussian smearing functions, then derived the kinematic vorticity (a covariant generalization of the usual vorticity) and the thermal vorticity (which at global equilibrium determines spin polarization density). The temperature was obtained from the energy density via a relation epsilon = c T^4 with c chosen to match the thermalized quark-gluon matter relation.

## Key numbers

* Initial kinematic vorticity at mid-rapidity shows a non-monotonic dependence on collision energy, with the turning point around sqrt(s_NN) ~ 3-5 GeV depending on centrality (impact parameters b = 5, 8, and 10 fm were studied).
* The kinematic vorticity decays with time, with faster decay rates at higher collision energies (e.g., comparing sqrt(s_NN) = 2.5 GeV and 10 GeV).
* Initial thermal vorticity at mid-rapidity also exhibits a non-monotonic energy dependence, with the turning point around sqrt(s_NN) ~ 3-5 GeV.
* At late times (e.g., t = 14 fm), the thermal vorticity is roughly a decreasing function of sqrt(s_NN).
* The smearing width parameter was set to sigma = 1.48 fm for baryons and sigma = 0.98 fm for mesons.
* The prefactor c in the energy-temperature relation was chosen as c = pi^2 (16 + 10.5 N_f)/30 ~ 15.6 with N_f = 3.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fluid vorticity at high-energy heavy-ion collisions (Jiang 2016, Deng 2016, Wei 2018, Becattini 2015) into the low-energy regime (sqrt(s_NN) = 1.9-50 GeV), complementing studies at NICA and FAIR energies (Teryaev 2015, Xie 2016, Ivanov 2017, Kolomeitsev 2018). The results are consistent with the non-monotonic energy dependence of global Lambda polarization observed by HADES at 2.4 GeV, STAR at 7.7-200 GeV, and ALICE at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. The findings provide theoretical background for future experimental programs including the RHIC beam energy scan phase II, FAIR, NICA, and HIAF.
