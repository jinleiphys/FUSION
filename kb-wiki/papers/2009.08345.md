# Deciphering the mechanism of near-threshold $J/ψ$ photoproduction

> The photoproduction of the $J/ψ$ off the proton is believed to deepen our understanding of various physics issues. On the one hand, it is proposed to provide access to the origin of the proton mass, based on the QCD multipole expansion. On the other hand, it can be employed in a study of pentaquark states. The process is usually assumed to proceed through vector-meson dominance, that is the photon couples to a $J/ψ$ which rescatters with the proton to give the $J/ψp$ final state. In this Letter, we provide a compelling hint for and propose measurements necessary to confirm a novel production mechanism via the $Λ_c \bar D^{(*)}$ intermediate states. In particular, there must be cusp structures at the $Λ_c \bar D^{(*)}$ thresholds in the energy dependence of the $J/ψ$ photoproduction cross section. The same mechanism also implies the $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering lengths of order 1 mfm. Given this, one expects only a minor contribution of charm quarks to the nucleon mass.

# Deciphering the mechanism of near-threshold J/psi photoproduction

## Key claim

The near-threshold J/psi photoproduction off the proton may be dominated by a coupled-channel mechanism involving Lambda_c D-bar(*) intermediate states rather than the traditionally assumed vector-meson dominance (VMD) process, which would obscure the direct connection between the J/psi photoproduction cross section and the trace anomaly contribution to the nucleon mass. The authors provide a "compelling hint" for this novel mechanism but state that further measurements are necessary to confirm it.

## Method

The authors calculate the J/psi photoproduction cross section using a box diagram where the photon couples to Lambda_c D-bar(*) intermediate states through effective Lagrangians with couplings fixed from experimental data on D* decays, SU(4) symmetry, and VMD. The amplitude is evaluated using a dispersion relation with a cutoff at about 1 GeV, and the exchanged particles are augmented with a single-pole form factor where the cutoff parameter Lambda is set to the mass of the lowest neglected exchange particle plus Lambda_QCD (250 MeV). Only S-wave contributions are retained for the open-charm system near threshold, while both S and D waves are considered for the J/psi p and gamma p systems.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda_c+ D-bar0 threshold lies 116 MeV above the J/psi p threshold; the Lambda_c+ D-bar*0 threshold lies 258 MeV above the J/psi p threshold.
- The J/psi-nucleon scattering lengths are predicted to be |a^{J=1/2}| = 0.2 to 3.1 mfm and |a^{J=3/2}| = 0.2 to 3.0 mfm, varying the form factor parameter eta between 0.5 and 2.
- The coupling constants used include g_{gamma DD*} = 0.134 GeV^{-1}, g_{gamma D*D*} = 0.641, g_{DN Lambda_c} = -4.3, g_{D*N Lambda_c} = -13.2, g_{psi Lambda_c Lambda_c} = -1.4, and g_{psi DD} = 7.44.
- The cross section calculation is compared with GlueX data up to E_gamma = 10.2 GeV, with qualitative agreement up to the highest GlueX data point at 11.6 GeV.

## Context

This work challenges the standard VMD approach to J/psi photoproduction, which was previously used to relate the cross section to the nucleon matrix element of two-gluon operators and the trace anomaly contribution to the nucleon mass (Kharzeev 1995, Kharzeev 1998). The proposed mechanism also provides an alternative to the VMD-based analyses of P_c pentaquark searches in photoproduction (Cao 2019, Winney 2019). The authors note that the cross sections for the analogous strangeness-sector reactions gamma p to K+ Lambda and gamma p to K+ Sigma0 are much larger than that for near-threshold phi meson production (Bradford 2005, Bradford 2006, McCracken 2009, Dey 2010, Paterson 2016). The predictions of cusp structures at the Lambda_c D-bar and Lambda_c D-bar* thresholds can be tested by ongoing J/psi photoproduction measurements in Hall C at Jefferson Laboratory (Meziani 2016) and by lattice QCD calculations of the J/psi-nucleon scattering lengths.
