# Scrutinizing CKM unitarity with a new measurement of the $K_{μ3}/K_{μ2}$ branching fraction

> Precision tests of first-row unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix currently display two intriguing tensions, both at the $3σ$ level. First, combining determinations of $V_{ud}$ from superallowed $β$ decays with $V_{us}$ from kaon decays suggests a deficit in the unitarity relation. At the same time, a tension of similar significance has emerged between $K_{\ell 2}$ and $K_{\ell 3}$ decays. In this Letter, we point out that a measurement of the $K_{\mu3}/K_{μ2}$ branching fraction at the level of $0.2\%$ would have considerable impact on clarifying the experimental situation in the kaon sector, especially in view of tensions in the global fit to kaon data as well as the fact that the $K_{\mu2}$ channel is currently dominated by a single experiment. Such a measurement, as possible for example at NA62, would further provide important constraints on physics beyond the Standard Model, most notably on the role of right-handed vector currents.

## Key claim

A new measurement of the \(K_{\mu3}/K_{\mu2}\) branching fraction with 0.2% precision could resolve or corroborate the current \(3\sigma\)-level tensions in first-row CKM unitarity, specifically the tension between \(K_{\ell2}\) and \(K_{\ell3}\) decays, but only if the measurement's central value deviates from the current fit expectation by at least \(2\sigma\).

## Method

The authors performed an updated global fit to kaon decay data (including \(K_S\), \(K_L\), and charged-kaon decays) using the most recent measurements, radiative corrections, and hadronic matrix elements from lattice QCD. They then simulated the impact of adding a hypothetical measurement of the \(K_{\mu3}/K_{\mu2}\) branching fraction at 0.5% and 0.2% precision, with central values either matching the current fit or shifted by \(\pm 2\sigma\) of its error. The resulting changes in CKM elements and unitarity deviations were quantified, and the implications for right-handed vector currents were analyzed using a two-parameter (\(\epsilon_R, \Delta\epsilon_R\)) framework.

## Key numbers

* Current global fit to kaon data: \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 25.5/11\), \(p\)-value = 0.78%
* Current \(V_{us}^{K_{\ell3}} = 0.22330(53)\) and \((V_{us}/V_{ud})|_{K_{\ell2}/\pi_{\ell2}} = 0.23108(51)\)
* Current CKM unitarity deficit from kaon-only input: \(\Delta_\text{CKM}^{(3)} = -0.0164(63)\), significance \(2.6\sigma\)
* With a 0.2% \(K_{\mu3}/K_{\mu2}\) measurement at \(+2\sigma\) shift: \(\Delta_\text{CKM}^{(3)} = -0.0083(62)\), significance \(1.4\sigma\)
* With a 0.2% \(K_{\mu3}/K_{\mu2}\) measurement at \(-2\sigma\) shift: \(\Delta_\text{CKM}^{(3)} = -0.0233(62)\), significance \(3.8\sigma\)
* Current right-handed current parameters: \(\epsilon_R = -0.69(27)\times 10^{-3}\) (2.5\(\sigma\)), \(\Delta\epsilon_R = -3.9(1.6)\times 10^{-3}\) (2.4\(\sigma\))
* With 0.2% measurement at \(+2\sigma\): \(\Delta\epsilon_R = -1.8(1.6)\times 10^{-3}\) (1.1\(\sigma\))
* With 0.2% measurement at \(-2\sigma\): \(\Delta\epsilon_R = -5.7(1.6)\times 10^{-3}\) (3.5\(\sigma\))

## Context

This work extends the ongoing precision tests of first-row CKM unitarity, which currently show two \(3\sigma\) tensions: a deficit in \(|V_{ud}|^2+|V_{us}|^2+|V_{ub}|^2=1\) from combining superallowed beta decays (Hardy and Towner, 2020) with kaon decays, and a tension between \(K_{\ell2}\) and \(K_{\ell3}\) decays. The proposed measurement would clarify whether the \(K_{\ell2}\)-\(K_{\ell3}\) tension is experimental or indicates new physics, particularly right-handed currents. The measurement is feasible at NA62, which previously measured \(K_{e2}/K_{\mu2}\) to 0.4% precision (NA62, 2012), and would enable more robust constraints on beyond-Standard-Model interpretations, especially for right-handed vector currents in the strange sector.
