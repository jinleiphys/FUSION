# Short-range Correlations in a CBF description of closed-shell nuclei

> The Correlated Basis Function theory (CBF) provides a theoretical framework to treat on the same ground mean-field and short-range correlations. We present, in this report, some recent results obtained using the CBF to describe the ground state properties of finite nuclear systems. Furthermore we show some results for the excited state obtained with a simplified model based on the CBF theory.

# Short-range Correlations in a CBF description of closed-shell nuclei

## Key claim
The Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory, combined with the Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) approximation, can treat mean-field and short-range correlations on the same footing for finite closed-shell nuclei, but the FHNC/0 approximation (neglecting elementary diagrams) produces binding energies that differ from Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) results by about 10% in ¹⁶O, and this discrepancy is largely resolved by including the lowest-order elementary diagram.

## Method
The authors applied the CBF variational approach using a trial wave function consisting of a Slater determinant multiplied by a symmetrized product of two-body correlation functions. They evaluated expectation values of the Hamiltonian using cluster expansion techniques and the FHNC integral equations to sum nodal diagrams, first in the FHNC/0 approximation (neglecting elementary diagrams) and then including the lowest-order elementary diagram (FHNC-1). Calculations were performed for closed-shell nuclei using the Brink and Boeker B1 interaction and the S3 force of Afnan and Tang, with Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions, and results were compared to VMC calculations for model nuclei.

## Key numbers
- In ¹⁶O, the FHNC/0 binding energy differed from VMC results by about 10%.
- The spin sum rule, which should equal -1 for spin-saturated systems, was violated by 10% in FHNC/0 calculations but satisfied at the 1% level after including the lowest-order elementary diagram (FHNC-1).
- In ¹²C, the contribution of the elementary diagram to the binding energy was of the order of 10% or more.
- In ²⁰⁸Pb, the contribution of the elementary diagram was a few parts per thousand.
- For ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb, the nuclear interaction contribution to binding energy was about the same across these nuclei, indicating saturation.

## Context
This work extends the application of CBF-FHNC theory, previously successful for few-body systems and nuclear matter (reviewed by Pandharipande (1990)), to medium and heavy finite nuclei. The authors had previously begun this program (Co' (1992), Co' (1994)). The FHNC/0 approximation, which neglects elementary diagrams, was tested against VMC calculations (Buehler (1992)) and found to require inclusion of elementary diagrams for accurate results in light nuclei, consistent with nuclear matter studies where elementary diagrams are small (Pandharipande (1990), Wiringa (1995)). The work enables development of simplified models for interpreting upcoming two-nucleon knockout experiments at electron accelerators.
