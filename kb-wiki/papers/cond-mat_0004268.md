# Micro-canonical Statistical Mechanics of some Non-Extensive Systems

> Non-extensive systems do not allow to go to the thermodynamic limit. Therefore we have to reformulate statistical mechanics without invoking the thermodynamical limit. I.e. we have to go back to Pre-Gibbsian times. We show that Boltzmann's mechanical definition of entropy S as function of the conserved ``extensive'' variables energy E, particle number N etc. allows to describe even the most sophisticated cases of phase transitions unambiguously for ``small'' systems like nuclei, atomic clusters, and selfgravitating astrophysical systems: The rich topology of the curvature of S(E,N) shows the whole ``Zoo'' of transitions: transitions of 1.order including the surface tension at phase-separation, continuous transitions, critical and multi-critical points. The transitions are the ``catastrophes'' of the Laplace transform from the ``extensive'' to the ``intensive'' variables. Moreover, this classification of phase transitions is much more natural than the Yang-Lee criterion.

## Key claim

Boltzmann's micro-canonical definition of entropy as a function of conserved mechanical variables (energy, particle number) can describe all types of phase transitions unambiguously for finite, non-extensive systems like nuclei and atomic clusters, without requiring the thermodynamic limit or extensivity. The author argues that Lieb's claim that "extensivity is essential for thermodynamics to work" is wrong and contradicts Boltzmann's original formulation.

## Method

The paper analyzes the topology of the curvature of the micro-canonical entropy surface \(s(e,n) = S(E,N,V)/V\) as a function of energy density \(e\) and particle density \(n\), using the determinant of the curvature matrix \(d(e,n) = s_{ee}s_{nn} - s_{en}^2\) and its eigenvalues. This geometric approach is applied to a 2-dimensional 3-state diluted Potts model on a finite \(50^2\) lattice with periodic boundaries, and to simulations of 200-3000 sodium atoms at constant external pressure of 1 atm.

## Key numbers

- For the 2-dim Potts lattice-gas: the multi-critical point \(P_m\) is located at \(e_m \sim -1\), \(n_m \sim 0.6\) or \(\beta_m = 1.48 \pm 0.03\), \(\nu_m = 2.67 \pm 0.02\)
- For the ordinary (\(q=3\)) Potts model at zero vacancy concentration (\(n=1\)): the continuous phase transition occurs at \(e_c = 1 + 1/\sqrt{q} \approx 1.58\) in the infinite volume limit
- For 1000 sodium atoms at 1 atm: transition temperature \(T_{tr} = 990\) K, latent heat \(q_{lat} = 0.91\) eV, entropy gain per atom \(s_{boil} = 10.7\), surface entropy loss \(\Delta s_{surf} = 0.56\), surface tension \(\sigma/T_{tr} = 5.68\)
- For 3000 sodium atoms at 1 atm: transition temperature \(T_{tr} = 1095\) K, latent heat \(q_{lat} = 0.94\) eV, surface tension \(\sigma/T_{tr} = 7.07\), compared to bulk values of 1156 K, 0.923 eV, and 7.41 respectively

## Context

This work extends the micro-canonical approach of Boltzmann (1877) and Einstein (1903, 1904) to non-extensive systems where the thermodynamic limit does not exist, contradicting the conventional Yang-Lee criterion (1952) that phase transitions require singularities in the thermodynamic limit. The paper shows that phase transitions in finite systems can be defined topologically by regions of non-negative curvature of the entropy surface, and that the convex intruder in \(s(e,n)\) gives the surface tension at phase separation. This approach contrasts with the Tsallis (1988) formalism for non-extensive systems, which the author suggests may be relevant for systems that do not populate the energy manifold densely.
