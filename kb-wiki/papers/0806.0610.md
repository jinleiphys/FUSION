# Photons from nuclear collisions at RHIC energies

> We model the hot and dense strongly interacting mater produced in high energy heavy ion collisions using relativistic hydrodynamics. Several different sources of real photons produced during these collisions are considered and their relative importance is assessed. We include contributions from QCD jets, which are allowed to loose and gain energy as they proceed through the hot matter. This is treated within the AMY formalism. We obtain photon spectra, R_{AA}, and v_2 in agreement with measurements performed by the PHENIX collaboration.

# Photons from nuclear collisions at RHIC energies

## Key claim
The authors model photon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions using relativistic hydrodynamics combined with the AMY formalism for jet energy loss and gain, and obtain agreement with PHENIX data for photon spectra, R_AA, and v_2 without tuning parameters to electromagnetic observables. However, the data do not yet permit an accurate determination of model parameters and ingredients, and the net photon v_2 is essentially zero for p_T > 4 GeV due to the small initial geometric anisotropy and the use of a realistic Woods-Saxon jet profile.

## Method
The authors use a two-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical model (AZHYDRO) to describe the space-time evolution of the hot and dense strongly interacting matter produced in heavy ion collisions. They calculate photon production from multiple sources: prompt photons from initial nucleon-nucleon collisions (using NLO QCD with K-factors), thermal photons from the partonic and hadronic phases, and jet-thermal photons from interactions between QCD jets and thermal partons. The jet energy profiles are evolved in time using the AMY formalism, which solves coupled Fokker-Planck rate equations that include both energy loss and gain channels, with the strong coupling alpha_s adjusted to reproduce R_AA for neutral pions.

## Key numbers
- Photon spectra calculated for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV at RHIC, for two different centrality classes, compared to PHENIX data.
- The prompt photon spectrum in proton-proton collisions uses renormalization and factorization scales set to p_T / sqrt(2), yielding good agreement with observation.
- R_AA for photons is less than 1 at high p_T, attributed to fragmentation photons being affected by jet energy loss and isospin effects.
- Photon azimuthal anisotropy v_2 is essentially zero for p_T > 4 GeV, with jet-plasma photons contributing a negative component.
- The strong coupling alpha_s is a free parameter adjusted to reproduce R_AA for neutral pions (no specific numerical value given in the text).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of photon production in heavy ion collisions by treating jet energy loss and gain consistently within the AMY formalism, building on prior work by Gale, Turbide, Frodermann, and Heinz (2006) and the AZHYDRO hydrodynamical model by Kolb and Heinz. The results show that jet-plasma photons produce a negative contribution to v_2, as originally suggested by Turbide, Gale, and Frodermann. The approach is being generalized to include energy loss by elastic collision processes and a 3D+1 hydrodynamical evolution, and precise measurements of photon v_2 have the potential to stringently constrain early-time dynamics in relativistic nuclear collisions.
