# Effects of nuclear re-interactions in quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering

> The effects of nuclear re-interactions in the quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering are investigated with a phenomenological model. We found that the nuclear responses are lowered and their maxima are shifted towards higher excitation energies. This is reflected on the total neutrino-nucleus cross section in a general reduction of about 15% for neutrino energies above 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear re-interactions (final state interactions) lower the quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross section by about 15% for neutrino energies above 300 MeV, but this reduction depends on the choice of the parameterization for the single-particle width and the effective nucleon mass, with the maximum difference between parameterizations being 5%.

## Method

The authors applied a phenomenological folding model, originally developed for electron scattering, to correct the Fermi gas (FG) mean-field response functions for final state interactions (FSI). The folding model convolves the FG response with a Lorentzian function whose width is derived from empirical single-particle energy widths measured in nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering and knock-out reactions, and the effective nucleon mass is taken as M*/M = 0.8. The calculations were performed for a Fermi momentum of k_F = 220 MeV/c, corresponding to the average density of nuclei with mass number 15 < A < 50.

## Key numbers

- The total neutrino-nucleus cross section is reduced by about 15% for neutrino energies above 300 MeV due to nuclear re-interactions.
- The Fermi momentum used in all calculations is k_F = 220 MeV/c, corresponding to the average density of nuclei with mass number 15 < A < 50.
- The effective nucleon mass ratio used is M*/M = 0.8.
- The single-particle width parameterization uses a = 11.5 and b = 18 for the function gamma(epsilon) = a * epsilon^2 / (epsilon^2 + b^2) * h(epsilon), with h(epsilon) either 1 or c^2/(epsilon^2 + c^2) where c = 110.
- The maximum difference between the two choices of h(epsilon) on the cross section is 5%.
- Above a muon energy threshold of 100 MeV, the integral muon flux from atmospheric neutrinos is reduced by about 5% due to the lowering of the quasi-elastic peak.
- The axial mass used is M_A = 1.00 GeV/c^2, with an uncertainty of about 15% that produces changes in the cross section of the same order.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological folding model developed by Smith and Wambach (1988) and Co et al. (1988) for electron scattering to the case of quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering. It addresses the inadequacy of mean-field models (such as the Fermi gas model of Smith and Moniz (1972)) in describing experimental data, where mean-field calculations overestimate charge responses and underestimate current responses. The results enable more accurate predictions of neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for the analysis of atmospheric and long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments, particularly for reconstructing neutrino direction and rejecting backgrounds in nu_tau detection.
