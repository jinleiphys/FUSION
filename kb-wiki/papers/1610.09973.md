# Nuclear and Quark Matter at High Temperature

> We review important ideas on nuclear and quark matter description on the basis of high- temperature field theory concepts, like resummation, dimensional reduction, interaction scale separation and spectral function modification in media. Statistical and thermodynamical concepts are spotted in the light of these methods concentrating on the - partially still open - problems of the hadronization process.

## Key claim

This review argues that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is far from a free gas of colored charges, and that the transition from hadronic matter to QGP is a continuous crossover rather than a first-order phase transition, with no uniquely fixed transition temperature point.

## Method

The authors review high-temperature field theory concepts including resummation of hard thermal loops (HTL), dimensional reduction of the 4-dimensional Euclidean path integral to a 3-dimensional partition sum at high temperature, and spectral function analysis to describe quasiparticle properties in the medium. They also present a simple string-based thermodynamic model where the free energy density includes a string tension term proportional to \( \sigma n^{2/3} \), and analyze the resulting non-equilibrium chemical potential to derive a condition for stable QGP equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement temperature \( T_c \) shows a lowering trend from 175 MeV through 165 MeV and recently down to 158 MeV, with the width of the transition zone about 15-35 MeV.
- The perturbative regime where color freedom is expressed requires relative momenta ranging from \( 3T_c \) to \( 6T_c - 10 T_c \).
- Hadron-like correlations survive well in the temperature zone of \( T_c - 4T_c \) according to modern lattice data.
- In the Boltzmann approximation with 37 effective light degrees of freedom for QGP, the critical temperature for string stability is \( T_{\text{QGP}} \ge 0.623 \sqrt{\sigma} \), where \( \sigma \) is the string tension.
- The static magnetic gluon mass is of order \( g^2 T \), and the gluon density from classical-order configurations is estimated as \( n \sim (g^2 T)^3 \).
- The Debye screening mass from quantum-order configurations is of order \( m \sim gT/\sqrt{\hbar} \).

## Context

This review extends the long line of work on QGP phase transitions from the MIT bag model (BAG, BAGB) and Hagedorn temperature ideas (HAGEDORN) to modern lattice QCD calculations (LATTICE, LATTICEB) that show a continuous crossover. It addresses open problems in hadronization, the applicability of hydrodynamics at early and late stages of heavy-ion collisions (ILLUSION), and the role of non-perturbative infrared effects (ALLCONF, ALLCONFB). The paper enables further investigation of the quarkyonic phase at low temperature and high baryon density, and the relationship between color deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration.
