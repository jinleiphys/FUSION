# Algorithm for generating new explicitly solvable Schrodinger type equations

## Key claim

The authors present an algorithm that generates new explicitly solvable Schrodinger-type equations by applying a change-of-variable technique (due to Derezinski and Wrochna) to known solvable hypergeometric-type equations, but the resulting "new" equations are only solvable because the eigenvalue parameter in the starting equation depends on the potential parameters in a specific way, a condition that limits the generality of the approach.

## Method

The authors start from the hypergeometric-type equation σ(s)y''(s) + τ(s)y'(s) + λy(s) = 0, where σ is at most second-degree and τ at most first-degree, and use the associated orthogonal polynomials and special functions to construct Schrodinger-type operators. They then apply a change-of-variable transformation (x → r) such that x'(r) = 1/√(I_k(x(r))) for certain functions I_k, converting the known Schrodinger-type equation into a new one with a different potential form. The algorithm exploits the parameter dependence of the eigenvalues in the starting system to ensure the transformed equation remains exactly solvable.

## Key numbers

- The parameter Λ that determines the number of orthogonal polynomials is Λ = ∞ for σ(s) ∈ {1, s, 1−s²} and Λ = (1−α)/2 for σ(s) ∈ {s²−1, s², s²+1}
- For the case σ(s)=1, the potential takes the form V_m(x) = (α²/4)x² + (αβ/2)x + β²/4 + α/2 − αm, with α < 0
- The eigenvalue for the transformed system with potential c₁(3r/2)^{2/3} + c₂(2/(3r))^{2/3} − 5/(36r²) is E_n^± = ±2√(c₁c₂ + c₁√c₁(1+2n)) for any n ∈ {0,1,2,...} satisfying n ≥ −c₂/(2√c₁) − 1/2
- The eigenfunctions ψ_n^±(r) are expressed in terms of Hermite polynomials H_n, with the explicit form involving factors exp(−(3/4)∛(3/2)√c₁ r^{4/3} ± ∛(9/4)√(c₂+√c₁(1+2n)) r^{2/3})

## Context

This work extends the method of Derezinski and Wrochna (DW) for generating new exactly solvable Schrodinger equations by applying it to the broader class of hypergeometric-type equations. The authors build on the theory of orthogonal polynomials and associated special functions developed by Nikiforov, Suslov, and Uvarov (NSU) and on prior work by Cotfas (NC02, NC04, NC06) relating hypergeometric-type operators to Schrodinger-type operators. The algorithm enables the systematic generation of new solvable quantum systems, though the authors note that the resulting formulas can become complicated (e.g., requiring solving cubic equations) for cases beyond the simplest examples.
