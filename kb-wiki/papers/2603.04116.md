# Topological observables and domain wall tension from finite temperature chiral perturbation theory

> Within the framework of SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, we derive the general solution of the QCD $θ$-vacuum for an arbitrary vacuum phase, explicitly incorporating isospin-breaking effects from the light quark mass difference, and compute the temperature dependence of the topological susceptibility, higher-order cumulants, and the domain wall tension up to next-to-leading order. We find that the topological susceptibility agrees with lattice data at low temperatures but deviates at higher temperatures as expected from the breakdown of the chiral expansion; moreover, we demonstrate that the normalized fourth-order cumulant and the domain wall tension decrease monotonically with increasing temperature, while the normalized sixth-order cumulant exhibits the opposite behavior. These results extend earlier analyses by showing how isospin breaking reshapes the full hierarchy of topological charge cumulants and the dynamics of $θ$-vacuum domain walls, thereby offering new theoretical input on the $θ$-vacuum properties, which are relevant for axion-related effective theories in hot QCD matter.

# Topological observables and domain wall tension from finite temperature chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The authors derive the general solution of the QCD θ-vacuum within SU(2) chiral perturbation theory for an arbitrary vacuum phase, explicitly incorporating isospin-breaking effects from the light quark mass difference, and compute the temperature dependence of topological observables up to next-to-leading order. The topological susceptibility agrees with lattice data at low temperatures but deviates at higher temperatures as expected from the breakdown of the chiral expansion, while the normalized fourth-order cumulant and domain wall tension decrease monotonically with increasing temperature and the normalized sixth-order cumulant exhibits the opposite behavior.

## Method

The authors work within SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, deriving the general ground-state solution for the QCD θ-vacuum by parameterizing the vacuum state as a diagonal matrix and solving the stationary condition for the vacuum phase angle φ. They compute the vacuum free energy density at finite temperature up to next-to-leading order by combining tree-level contributions from the O(p²) and O(p⁴) chiral Lagrangians with one-loop contributions evaluated using dimensional regularization, incorporating thermal effects through the replacement of the zero component of momentum by discrete Matsubara frequencies. The topological susceptibility, normalized fourth-order and sixth-order cumulants, and domain wall tension are then obtained from derivatives of the free energy density with respect to the vacuum angle θ.

## Key numbers

- Zero-temperature topological susceptibility in the isospin-breaking case: χ_t^(1/4) = 75.5(5) MeV, in agreement with lattice result 75.6(2) MeV from Borsanyi (2016)
- Zero-temperature topological susceptibility in the isospin-symmetric limit: χ_t^(1/4) = 77.9(4) MeV, in agreement with lattice result 78.1(2) MeV from Borsanyi (2016)
- Normalized fourth-order cumulant at zero temperature in the isospin-breaking case: b₂ = -0.029(2)
- Normalized fourth-order cumulant at zero temperature in the isospin-symmetric limit: b₂ = -0.022(1)
- Normalized sixth-order cumulant at zero temperature in the isospin-breaking case: b₄ = -0.00028(7)
- Normalized sixth-order cumulant at zero temperature in the isospin-symmetric limit: b₄ = -0.00017(6)
- Input parameters: F_π = 92.2 MeV, M_π = 134.98 MeV, light-quark mass ratio z = m_u/m_d = 0.48 ± 0.03
- Low-energy constants: l₇ = (7 ± 4) × 10⁻³, h₁ʳ - h₃ - l₄ʳ = (4.8 ± 1.4) × 10⁻³
- Asymptotic value of b₂ from dilute instanton gas model: b₂^(inst) = -1/12 ≈ -0.083
- Domain wall tension drops by about 8-10% relative to its zero-temperature value near T ~ 120-150 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier SU(2) chiral perturbation theory analyses by GrillidiCortona (2015) that computed the topological susceptibility and leading non-Gaussian cumulant b₂ at zero temperature, incorporating isospin-breaking corrections. The authors systematically compute the temperature evolution of higher-order cumulants (b₂ and b₄) and domain wall tension within a unified framework, comparing isospin-symmetric and isospin-breaking cases. The results provide theoretical input for axion phenomenology and the physics of topological defects in hot QCD matter, complementing lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2016) at low temperatures and dilute instanton gas model predictions at high temperatures.
