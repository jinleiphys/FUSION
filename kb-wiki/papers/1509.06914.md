# Glueball-baryon interactions in holographic QCD

> Studying the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with type IIA string theory, we find the glueball-baryon interaction is predicted in this model. The glueball is identified as the 11D gravitational waves or graviton described by the M5-brane supergravity solution. Employing the relation of M-theory and type IIA string theory, glueball is also 10D gravitational perturbations which are the excited modes by close strings in the bulk of this model. On the other hand, baryon is identified as a D4-brane wrapped on $S^{4}$ which is named as baryon vertex, so the glueball-baryon interaction is nothing but the close string/baryon vertex interaction in this model. Since the baryon vertex could be equivalently treated as the instanton configurations on the flavor brane, we identify the glueball-baryon interaction as "graviton-instanton" interaction in order to describe it quantitatively by the quantum mechanical system for the collective modes of baryons. So the effective Hamiltonian can be obtained by considering the gravitational perturbations in the flavor brane action. With this Hamiltonian, the amplitudes and the selection rules of the glueball-baryon interaction can be analytically calculated in the strong coupling limit. We show our calculations explicitly in two characteristic situations which are "scalar and tensor glueball interacting with baryons". Although there is a long way to go, our work provides a holographic way to understand the interactions of baryons in hadronic physics and nuclear physics by the underlying string theory.

## Key claim

The paper claims that glueball-baryon interactions exist in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto (WSS) model and can be described quantitatively by a holographic quantum mechanical system for baryon collective modes, but only in the strong coupling limit (t'Hooft coupling λ ≫ 1) and only for the two-flavor case (N_f = 2), because the analytical instanton configuration for generic numbers of flavors is not known.

## Method

The author identifies glueballs as 10D gravitational perturbations (gravitons) in the bulk of the WSS model and baryons as D4'-branes wrapped on S⁴ (baryon vertex), which are equivalently described by BPST instanton configurations in the world-volume theory of flavor D8/anti-D8 branes. The glueball-baryon interaction is then treated as a "graviton-instanton" interaction by adding linear gravitational perturbations h_MN to the background metric and evaluating the perturbed Yang-Mills action on the flavor branes. The resulting time-dependent Hamiltonian for the baryon collective coordinates is used to compute transition amplitudes and selection rules via standard time-dependent perturbation theory in quantum mechanics.

## Key numbers

- The scalar glueball mass spectrum from WKB approximation is given by M_E(j) ≃ (8.12/β̄) √[j(j + 5/2)] in units of M_KK = U_KK = 1, with β̄ = 2π.
- For scalar glueball-baryon transitions, the ratio E(l=1,3; n_ρ=3; n_z=0,1,2,3) - E(l=1,3; n_ρ=0; n_z=0,1,2,3) divided by M_E(j=1) is approximately 1.013.
- For scalar glueball-baryon transitions, the ratio E(l=1,3; n_ρ=5; n_z=0,1,2,3) - E(l=1,3; n_ρ=0; n_z=0,1,2,3) divided by M_E(j=2) is approximately 1.053.
- The transition amplitude for n_ρ = 3 → n_ρ' = 0 (with l = l', n_z = n_z') in the small k limit is approximately -243 A κ C_1 / (44800 a² π² m_y⁴ ω_ρ⁴ λ) + O(k²).
- The transition amplitude for n_ρ = 5 → n_ρ' = 0 (with l = l', n_z = n_z') in the small k limit is approximately -81 A κ C_1 / (63760 a² π² m_y⁴ ω_ρ⁴ λ) + O(k²).

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of glueball-meson interactions previously developed by the same author and others (Li, 2015; earlier works cited as key-020 through key-023) to include baryons, building on the baryon vertex construction of Witten (1998) and the instanton description of baryons in the WSS model developed by Hata et al. (2007). The paper shows that the tensor glueball-baryon interaction vanishes at linear order in gravitational perturbation, implying it must be a nonlinear effect. Combined with prior work on glueball-meson interactions, this provides a complete holographic description of glueball-meson-baryon interactions in the strong coupling limit, though the author notes that high-energy baryon states may not be consistently described in this low-energy effective model.
