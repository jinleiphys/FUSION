# Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and nucleon structure

> A modification of the Gerasimov--Drell--Hearn sum rule suggested by the current experimental data is presented. Within the conventional theoretical framework, we find it necessary to consider the possibility of the presence of a localized region inside a nucleon, in which the electromagnetic (EM) gauge symmetry is spontaneous broken down, if the constraints of the gauge invariance, Lorentz invariance and the assumption of the commutativity of the EM charge density operator at equal-time are considered. We also discuss the propagation of a virtual photon inside a nucleon under such a scenario. A comment on some of the recent model independent works on the same subject is provided.

# Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and nucleon structure

## Key claim

A modification of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule is proposed that can reconcile the sum rule with current experimental data, provided that a localized region exists inside a nucleon in which electromagnetic gauge symmetry is spontaneously broken. This modification is conditional on the assumptions of gauge invariance, Lorentz invariance, and commutativity of the electromagnetic charge density operator at equal-time being maintained.

## Method

The author derives the constraints imposed by gauge invariance on the invariant amplitudes parameterizing the forward Compton scattering amplitude, expressed through the Ward identity. By analyzing the large-energy behavior of these amplitudes and defining an order parameter for spontaneous EM gauge symmetry breaking inside a nucleon, the author obtains a modified GDH sum rule with an additional term arising from a constant asymptotic contribution to the spin-dependent amplitude. The propagation of a photon inside a finite superconducting region is then studied using a Schwinger-Dyson equation approach adapted to a spherical cavity of finite radius.

## Key numbers

- The order parameter for the proton is extracted as $\rho_\infty^p = 2.9 \times 10^{-2} \text{ fm}^2$
- The order parameter for the neutron is extracted as $\rho_\infty^n = -2.5 \times 10^{-2} \text{ fm}^2$
- The scale $\Lambda$ defined by $\rho_\infty^{p,n} = 1/\Lambda^2$ corresponds to $\Lambda \sim 1 \text{ GeV}$, matching the natural scale of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking
- The photon mass inside the superconducting phase is estimated to be of order $10 \text{ MeV}$ based on a diquark condensation scenario from Ying (1998)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, originally derived by Gerasimov (1965) and Drell and Hearn (1966), in response to experimental analyses by Workman and Arndt (1992) and Sandorfi, Whisnant, and Khandaker (1994) suggesting a violation of the sum rule using single-pion photoproduction data below 1.7 GeV. The proposed mechanism provides an alternative to earlier approaches that assumed a fixed J=1 pole in the helicity amplitude (Ahmadzadeh and Gunion, 1971; Fox and Freedman, 1976) or modified the commutator between electromagnetic charge density operators (Kawarabayashi and Suzuki, 1973; Creutz, 1972). The modified sum rule enables experimental tests through planned measurements at BNL LEGS, Mainz MAMI, CEBAF, Bonn ELSA, and Grenoble GRAAL facilities, and predicts a phenomenon of virtual photon transparency at high energies if a nucleon contains a superconducting phase.
