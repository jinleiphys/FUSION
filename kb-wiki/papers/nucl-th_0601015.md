# Cooperative Enhancement Mechanisms of Low Energy Nuclear Reactions Using Superlow Energy External Fields

> We proposed a new mechanism of LENR: cooperative processes in whole system -- nuclei+atoms+condensed matter can occur at smaller threshold energies then corresponding ones on free constituents. The cooperative processes can be induced and enhanced by low energy external fields. The excess heat is the emission of internal energy and transmutations at LENR are the result of redistribution inner energy of whole system.

# Cooperative Enhancement Mechanisms of Low Energy Nuclear Reactions Using Superlow Energy External Fields

## Key claim

The authors propose that low-energy nuclear reactions (LENR) can occur in whole systems (nuclei+atoms+condensed matter) at threshold energies significantly lower than those for free constituents, and that these cooperative processes can be induced and enhanced by superlow energy external fields, without violating known fundamental physical laws.

## Method

The paper derives classical Hamiltonian mechanics for the hydrogen atom to show that proton and electron motions are synchronized with equal frequency, impulse, and de Broglie wavelength, establishing that nuclear and atomic processes must be treated as a unified whole. The authors then apply the universal resonance synchronization principle to argue that weak interaction processes such as electron capture by protons (p + e⁻ → n + νₑ) and neutron decay can have their threshold energies drastically reduced in condensed matter systems, with external fields (electromagnetic, acoustic, discharge) acting as triggers to extract internal binding energy from the whole system.

## Key numbers

- The rest mass difference between neutron and (proton+electron) is 0.7823 MeV/c², which is the threshold energy for electron capture by free protons.
- The kinetic energy ratio of electron to proton in a hydrogen atom is 4.46 × 10⁻⁴.
- The threshold for electron capture by ¹⁴N (producing ¹⁴C) is 156.41 keV, compared to 782.3 keV for free proton capture.
- The threshold for electron capture by ⁵⁸Ni (68.27% abundance) is 0.37766 keV, compared to 0.7823 MeV for free protons (ratio Q₂/Q₁ ≈ 2).
- The rest mass difference m(¹¹⁰Pd) − m(¹¹⁰Cd) = 1.9989 MeV/c².
- The rest mass difference m(⁵⁸Ni) − m(⁵⁸Fe) = 1.92642 MeV/c².
- The rest mass difference m(¹⁸⁶W) − m(¹⁸⁶Os) = 0.47302 MeV/c².
- The rest mass difference m(¹⁹⁸Pt) − m(¹⁹⁸Hg) = 1.05285 MeV/c².
- The total number of neutrons produced by one typical lightning discharge was estimated as 2.5 × 10¹⁰.
- The half-life of radiocarbon ¹⁴C is 5730 years.
- The threshold for electron capture by ³²S (producing ³²P) is 1.710 MeV; for ³³S (producing ³³P) it is 0.240 MeV.

## Context

This paper extends the authors' earlier work on the universal resonance synchronization principle (Gareev, 2004) and their review of LENR stimulation mechanisms (Gareev and Zhidkova, 2005). It directly challenges the conventional nuclear physics assumption that electron-volt-scale external fields cannot influence mega-electron-volt-scale nuclear processes. The work enables a proposed experimental program using condensed matter discharge (breakdown, spark, arc) with Ni, Pd, Pt, and W electrodes to produce specific isotopes (⁵⁸Fe, ¹¹⁰Cd, ¹⁸⁶Os, ¹⁹⁸Hg) and excess heat, and suggests that double beta decay experiments could be performed more cheaply using external field enhancement rather than waiting for spontaneous decays with half-lives of 10¹⁸–10³⁰ years.
