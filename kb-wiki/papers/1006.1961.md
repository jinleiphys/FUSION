# K*0 production in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at \sqrt{s_NN} = 62.4 GeV and 200 GeV

> We report on K*0 production at mid-rapidity in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4 and 200 GeV collected by the Solenoid Tracker at RHIC (STAR) detector. The K*0 is reconstructed via the hadronic decays K*0 \to K+ pi- and \bar{K*0} \to K-pi+. Transverse momentum, pT, spectra are measured over a range of pT extending from 0.2 GeV/c to 5 GeV/c. The center of mass energy and system size dependence of the rapidity density, dN/dy, and the average transverse momentum, <pT>, are presented. The measured N(K*0)/N(K) and N(ϕ)/N(K*0) ratios favor the dominance of re-scattering of decay daughters of K*0 over the hadronic regeneration for the K*0 production. In the intermediate pT region (2.0 < pT < 4.0 GeV/c), the elliptic flow parameter, v2, and the nuclear modification factor, RCP, agree with the expectations from the quark coalescence model of particle production.

## Key claim
The measured N(K*0)/N(K) and N(phi)/N(K*0) ratios favor the dominance of re-scattering of decay daughters of K*0 over hadronic regeneration for K*0 production, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the hadronic phase after chemical freeze-out is the primary determinant of the observed yields rather than initial production mechanisms.

## Method
K*0 and anti-K*0 mesons were reconstructed at mid-rapidity via their hadronic decay channels K*0 to K+ pi- and anti-K*0 to K- pi+ using the STAR detector at RHIC. Transverse momentum (pT) spectra were measured over a range from 0.2 GeV/c to 5 GeV/c in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at center-of-mass energies of 62.4 GeV and 200 GeV. The rapidity density dN/dy, average transverse momentum <pT>, elliptic flow parameter v2, and nuclear modification factor RCP were extracted from the data.

## Key numbers
- pT spectra measured from 0.2 GeV/c to 5 GeV/c for K*0 in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV and 200 GeV
- In the intermediate pT region (2.0 < pT < 4.0 GeV/c), the elliptic flow parameter v2 and nuclear modification factor RCP agree with expectations from the quark coalescence model of particle production
- The N(K*0)/N(K) and N(phi)/N(K*0) ratios were measured and found to favor re-scattering over regeneration as the dominant mechanism for K*0 production

## Context
This work extends previous STAR measurements of strange and multi-strange hadron production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies, providing systematic comparisons between Cu+Cu and Au+Au systems at two different beam energies. The results on K*0 production and its ratios to kaons and phi mesons constrain models of the hadronic phase evolution after chemical freeze-out, particularly the competition between resonance regeneration and daughter-particle re-scattering. The intermediate-pT v2 and RCP results support the quark coalescence picture previously applied to other hadron species at RHIC, as developed in theoretical work by Molnar and Voloshin (2003) and others.
