# Scalar field, nucleon structure and relativistic chiral theory for nuclear matter

> The work that Peter Schuck and I carried out during the nineties in collaboration with the Lyon and Darmstadt theory groups is summarized. I retrace how our theoretical developments combined with experimental results concerning the in-medium modification of the pion-pion interaction allowed a clarification of the chiral status of the sigma meson introduced in relativistic theories of nuclear matter. This enabled us to build a relativistic chiral theory, now called the chiral confining model of nuclear matter, which includes the effect of the nucleon substructure, namely the response of the nucleon to the nuclear scalar field generating an efficient and natural contribution to the saturation mechanism. Using parameters from a QCD-connected version of the chiral confining model, I describe the relative roles of the chiral scalar field and two-pion (or two-rho) exchange for the in-medium $NN$ attractive interaction and the associated sources of three-body interactions needed for the saturation mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "nuclear physics sigma meson" of relativistic mean-field theories should be identified not with the chiral partner of the pion, but with the chiral-invariant scalar field associated with radial fluctuations of the chiral condensate; this identification, combined with a nucleon response to the scalar field arising from confinement, resolves long-standing problems with nuclear saturation and lattice QCD constraints on the nucleon's scalar susceptibility.

## Method

The author develops the chiral confining model of nuclear matter, which couples nucleons to scalar (s), vector (omega, rho), and pion fields via a Lagrangian with standard Yukawa couplings, but incorporates two key ingredients beyond simple relativistic mean-field theory: (1) an in-medium nucleon mass that depends quadratically on the scalar field through a nucleon scalar susceptibility parameter C, reflecting the nucleon's quark substructure; and (2) a chiral effective potential for the scalar field, initially taken from the linear sigma model (LσM) and later replaced by a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) version that includes an additional parameter C_χ reducing the attractive tadpole diagram. The model's parameters are constrained by lattice QCD data on the nucleon mass expansion in the pion mass squared, and the nuclear matter equation of state is computed including ring summation over pion and rho exchange with short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless nucleon response parameter C is found to be 0.32 from the QCD-connected confining model, while the NJL chiral potential parameter C_χ is 0.488.
- The effective sigma mass from the NJL model is M_σ = 716.4 MeV.
- The scalar coupling constant from the confining model is g_S = 6.52.
- The lattice QCD parameters from the model are a_2 = 1.175 GeV^{-1} and a_4 = -0.615 GeV^{-3}, yielding a non-pionic sigma commutator σ^{(s)} = 23.5 MeV.
- The NJL model parameters derived from QCD inputs (string tension σ = 0.18 GeV^2, gluon condensate G_2 = 0.025 GeV^4) give a gluon correlation length T_g = 0.286 fm, a constituent quark mass M_0 = 356.7 MeV, pion decay constant F_π = 91.9 MeV, and quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(241.1 MeV)^3.
- The cutoff parameters for monopole form factors in scalar, pion, and vector channels are all approximately Λ = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral approach to nuclear matter initiated by Walecka and collaborators (Serot and Walecka, 1986) and addresses the chiral status of the sigma meson first questioned by Birse. It builds on the Quark Meson Coupling model of Guichon (1988) by incorporating the nucleon's scalar response, and uses lattice QCD results from the Adelaide group (Leinweber et al., 2003-2010) to constrain the nucleon's scalar susceptibility. The model enables a consistent description of nuclear saturation, the in-medium NN interaction from two-pion exchange, and the chiral properties of nuclear matter, with applications to neutron star equations of state.
