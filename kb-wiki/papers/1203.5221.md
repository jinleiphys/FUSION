# Heavy quark energy loss and D-Mesons at RHIC and LHC energies

> We obtain the radiative energy loss of a heavy quark in a deconfined medium due to radiation of gluons off them using a recently derived generalised gluon emission spectrum. We find that the heavy flavour loses energy almost in a similar fashion like light quarks through this process. With this, we further analyse the nuclear modification factor for D-meson at LHC and RHIC energies. In particular, the obtained result is found to be in close agreement with the most recent data from ALICE collaboration at 2.76 ATeV Pb-Pb collisions. We also discuss the nuclear modification factor due to the collisional energy loss. Furthermore, the result of non-photonic single electron from the decay of both D and B mesons is compared with the RHIC data at 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions, which is also in close agreement.

## Key claim

The authors claim that heavy quarks lose energy through radiative gluon emission almost as efficiently as light quarks, but only when the heavy quark energy is large compared to its mass (E ≥ 10 GeV); at lower energies the dead-cone suppression at small angles still reduces heavy quark energy loss relative to light quarks.

## Method

The authors used a recently derived generalized gluon emission spectrum for heavy quarks (Abir 2012) that includes a dead-cone factor valid over the full range of gluon emission angles, not just the forward direction. They computed the radiative energy loss in a Bethe-Heitler approximation using a canonical approach based on the mean energy of emitted gluons and the mean free path of the traversing quark, with kinematic cuts derived from the hierarchy √s, E ≫ q⊥ ≫ ω > k⊥ ≫ mD. The nuclear modification factor RAA for D-mesons was calculated by combining this radiative energy loss with collisional energy loss from Peigne and Peshier (2008), using leading-order pQCD for heavy quark production with Peterson fragmentation and EKS98 shadowing.

## Key numbers

- Average path length ⟨L⟩ = 5.78 fm for central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV and 6.14 fm for central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV and 5.5 ATeV
- Initial temperature T0 = 400 MeV at 200 AGeV, 525 MeV at 2.76 ATeV, and 590 MeV at 5.5 ATeV, assuming initial time τ0 = 0.2 fm/c
- Critical temperature Tc ≈ 170 MeV; time to reach Tc is ≈ 2.627 fm/c at 200 AGeV, 5.9038 fm/c at 2.76 ATeV, and 8.375 fm/c at 5.5 ATeV
- Gluon multiplicity dNg/dy ≈ 1125 for Au+Au at 200 AGeV, ≈ 2855 for Pb+Pb at 2.76 ATeV, and ≈ 4050 for Pb+Pb at 5.5 ATeV
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: εc = 0.06 for charm quarks into D-mesons, εb = 0.006 for bottom quarks into B-mesons
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.5 GeV for energy loss calculations

## Context

This work extends the generalized gluon emission spectrum derived by Abir (2012) to compute heavy quark energy loss and nuclear modification factors, addressing the long-standing puzzle that single electron data at RHIC showed similar suppression for heavy and light hadrons despite the expected dead-cone effect. The results differ significantly from the DGLV formalism of Djordjevic, Gyulassy, Levai and Vitev (2000, 2007), which constrained gluon emission only to forward angles, while the present calculation includes the full angular range. The nuclear modification factor for D-mesons with radiative energy loss alone shows close agreement with ALICE data at 2.76 ATeV, and the non-photonic single electron results at 200 AGeV require contributions from both collisional energy loss and B-meson decay to match RHIC data from STAR and PHENIX.
