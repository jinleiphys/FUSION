# The impact of hierarchy upon the values of neutrino mixing parameters

> A neutrino-oscillation analysis is performed of the more finely binned Super-K atmospheric, MINOS, and CHOOZ data in order to examine the impact of neutrino hierarchy in this data set upon the value of $θ_{13}$ and the deviation of $θ_{23}$ from maximal mixing. Exact oscillation probabilities are used, thus incorporating all powers of $θ_{13}$ and $ε:=θ_{23}-π/4$. The extracted oscillation parameters are found to be dependent on the hierarchy, particularly for $θ_{13}$. We find at 90% CL are $Δ_{32} = 2.44^{+0.26}_{-0.20}$ and $2.48^{+0.25}_{-0.22}\times 10^{-3} {\rm eV}^2$, $ε=θ_{23}-π/4=0.06^{+0.06}_{-0.16}$ and $0.06^{+0.08}_{-0.17}$, and $θ_{13}=-0.07^{+0.18}_{-0.11}$ and $-0.13^{+0.23}_{-0.16}$, for the normal and inverted hierarchy respectively. The inverted hierarchy is preferred at a statistically insignificant level of 0.3 $σ$.

## Key claim

The extracted values of the neutrino mixing parameters, particularly $\theta_{13}$, depend on whether the neutrino mass hierarchy is normal or inverted, but the preference for the inverted hierarchy over the normal hierarchy is statistically insignificant at only 0.3 sigma for a three-parameter fit.

## Method

The authors performed a neutrino-oscillation analysis using exact oscillation probabilities (incorporating all powers of $\theta_{13}$ and $\varepsilon = \theta_{23} - \pi/4$) on the more finely binned Super-K atmospheric data combined with MINOS and CHOOZ data, fixing solar parameters at their best-fit values from Schwetz (2008). They minimized $\chi^2$ over the three varied parameters ($\Delta_{32}$, $\varepsilon$, and $\theta_{13}$) separately for the normal hierarchy and inverted hierarchy assumptions, using a two-density model of the Earth (mantle density 4.5 g/cm³, core density 11.5 g/cm³, core radius 3486 km) to compute oscillation probabilities including matter effects.

## Key numbers

- $|\Delta_{32}| = 2.44^{+0.26}_{-0.20} \times 10^{-3} \text{ eV}^2$ for the normal hierarchy and $2.48^{+0.25}_{-0.22} \times 10^{-3} \text{ eV}^2$ for the inverted hierarchy, at 90% CL
- $\varepsilon = \theta_{23} - \pi/4 = 0.06^{+0.06}_{-0.16}$ for the normal hierarchy and $0.06^{+0.08}_{-0.17}$ for the inverted hierarchy, at 90% CL
- $\theta_{13} = -0.07^{+0.18}_{-0.11}$ for the normal hierarchy and $-0.13^{+0.23}_{-0.16}$ for the inverted hierarchy, at 90% CL
- The inverted hierarchy is preferred at a statistically insignificant level of 0.3 sigma (difference in $\chi^2$ of 0.9 between the two hierarchy minima)
- Fixed solar parameters: $\Delta_{21} = 7.65 \times 10^{-5} \text{ eV}^2$ and $\theta_{12} = 0.584$, from Schwetz (2008)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Escamilla-Roa et al. 2009, 2009wp) by explicitly examining how the neutrino mass hierarchy assumption affects parameter extraction from atmospheric and long-baseline data, using exact oscillation probabilities rather than approximate formulae. The results show that the atmospheric data's preference for negative $\theta_{13}$ (equivalent to setting $\delta = \pi$) persists regardless of hierarchy, originating from an excess of sub-GeV e-like events, while the inverted hierarchy yields larger error bars on $\theta_{13}$ because the MSW resonances occur in the subdominant antineutrino channel. The authors estimate that if $\theta_{13}$ is as large as $\pm 0.15$, reducing statistical errors by a factor of two (or increasing total events by a factor of four) could begin to produce statistically significant hierarchy indications from atmospheric data.
