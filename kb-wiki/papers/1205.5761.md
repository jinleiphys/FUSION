# Anisotropic flow of charged hadrons, pions and (anti-)protons measured at high transverse momentum in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> The elliptic, $v_2$, triangular, $v_3$, and quadrangular, $v_4$, azimuthal anisotropic flow coefficients are measured for unidentified charged particles, pions and (anti-)protons in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV with the ALICE detector at the Large Hadron Collider. Results obtained with the event plane and four-particle cumulant methods are reported for the pseudo-rapidity range $|η|<0.8$ at different collision centralities and as a function of transverse momentum, $p_{\rm T}$, out to $p_{\rm T}=20$ GeV/$c$. The observed non-zero elliptic and triangular flow depends only weakly on transverse momentum for $p_{\rm T}>8$ GeV/$c$. The small $p_{\rm T}$ dependence of the difference between elliptic flow results obtained from the event plane and four-particle cumulant methods suggests a common origin of flow fluctuations up to $p_{\rm T}=8$ GeV/$c$. The magnitude of the (anti-)proton elliptic and triangular flow is larger than that of pions out to at least $p_{\rm T}=8$ GeV/$c$ indicating that the particle type dependence persists out to high $p_{\rm T}$.

## Key claim
The elliptic (v2) and triangular (v3) flow coefficients for charged hadrons, pions, and (anti-)protons in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV are non-zero and depend only weakly on transverse momentum for pT above 8 GeV/c, with the particle-type dependence (protons showing larger flow than pions) persisting out to at least pT = 8 GeV/c. The small difference between event-plane and four-particle cumulant results for elliptic flow suggests a common origin of flow fluctuations up to pT = 8 GeV/c, though the paper does not claim this holds at higher pT.

## Method
The ALICE Collaboration measured the elliptic (v2), triangular (v3), and quadrangular (v4) anisotropic flow coefficients using both the event-plane method and the four-particle cumulant method. The analysis covered unidentified charged particles, pions, and (anti-)protons in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, within the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 0.8, across different collision centralities and as a function of transverse momentum up to pT = 20 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- Elliptic flow (v2) and triangular flow (v3) are measured for charged particles out to pT = 20 GeV/c, with weak pT dependence observed for pT > 8 GeV/c.
- The difference between elliptic flow from event-plane and four-particle cumulant methods is small up to pT = 8 GeV/c, indicating common flow fluctuations in that range.
- (Anti-)proton elliptic and triangular flow magnitudes are larger than those of pions out to at least pT = 8 GeV/c.
- The analysis covers the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 0.8 at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.

## Context
This work extends previous measurements of anisotropic flow at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) by pushing the transverse momentum reach to 20 GeV/c and by separately identifying pions and (anti-)protons. The results enable tests of partonic energy loss models and the path-length dependence of jet quenching in the quark-gluon plasma, and they provide constraints on the initial-state geometry fluctuations that generate higher-order flow harmonics. The persistence of particle-type dependence to high pT contradicts expectations from simple hydrodynamical scaling and points to the importance of hadronization mechanisms such as recombination at intermediate pT.
