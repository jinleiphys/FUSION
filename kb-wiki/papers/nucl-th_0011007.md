# Airy-like patterns in heavy ion elastic scattering

> A semiclassical analysis of an optical potential cross section is presented. The cross section considered is characterized by the appearance of an Airy-like pattern. This pattern is similar to that which is present in many cross sections, which fit the recent measurements of light heavy ion elastic scattering, and is considered as a manifestation of a rainbow phenomenon. The semiclassical analysis shows that, in the case considered, the oscillations arise from the interference between the contributions from two different terms of a multi-reflection expansion of the scattering function, and, therefore, cannot be associated with the rainbow phenomenon.

# Airy-like patterns in heavy ion elastic scattering

## Key claim

The Airy-like oscillatory pattern observed in the far-side cross section of heavy ion elastic scattering, which has been interpreted as a rainbow phenomenon, actually arises from interference between contributions from two different terms of a multi-reflection expansion, specifically, a saddle point from the second term (trajectories refracted in the internal region) interfering with a contribution from the first term (trajectories that do not penetrate the internal region), and therefore cannot be associated with the rainbow phenomenon.

## Method

The author performed a uniform semiclassical multi-reflection expansion analysis of the scattering function for an optical potential that fits the elastic scattering cross section of 16O + 12C at Elab = 132 MeV. The expansion decomposed the scattering amplitude into a barrier term (first term, describing non-penetrating trajectories) and higher-order terms (describing trajectories refracted in the internal region). The author then compared the separate cross sections of the first two terms with the complete cross section and its near-side and far-side components to identify the origin of the oscillatory pattern.

## Key numbers

- The optical potential parameters used: V0 = 282.2 MeV, Rv = 2.818 fm, dv = 0.978 fm (real part); W0 = 13.86 MeV, Rw = 5.689 fm, dw = 0.656 fm (imaginary part), for 16O + 12C at Elab = 132 MeV.
- The classical deflection function (from the real potential alone) has a minimum of about -310.15 degrees at lambda approximately 23.56.
- The rainbow singularity from the classical deflection function minimum is expected at an angle of 49.85 degrees in the near-side contributions.
- The moduli of the first and second terms of the multi-reflection expansion become equal at l approximately 21.

## Context

This work extends the multi-reflection expansion approach developed by Knoll and Schaeffer (1976) and Brink and Takigawa (1977) for potentials with multiple turning points, and builds on the author's own prior work (Anni and Renna, 1981) on approximate multi-reflection expansions. The analysis directly addresses the interpretation of oscillatory patterns observed in recent measurements of 16O+16O (Khoa et al., 2000; Nicoli et al., 2000) and 16O+12C (Nicoli et al., 1999; Ogloblin et al., 2000) elastic scattering, which had been claimed as evidence of rainbow scattering. The result enables future analyses to correctly distinguish between true rainbow phenomena (arising from saddle point coalescence within a single term of the multi-reflection expansion) and interference patterns arising from different terms, and confirms that one of the two interfering contributions is a saddle point associated with trajectories that penetrate the internal region.
