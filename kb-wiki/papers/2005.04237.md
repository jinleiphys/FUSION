# Dark matter or correlated errors: Systematics of the AMS-02 antiproton excess

> Several studies have pointed out an excess in the AMS-02 antiproton spectrum at rigidities of 10-20 GV. Its spectral properties were found to be consistent with a dark-matter particle of mass 50-100 GeV which annihilates hadronically at roughly the thermal rate. In this work, we reinvestigate the antiproton excess including all relevant sources of systematic errors. Most importantly, we perform a realistic estimate of the correlations in the AMS-02 systematic error which could potentially "fake" a dark-matter signal. The dominant systematics in the relevant rigidity range originate from uncertainties in the cross sections for absorption of cosmic rays within the detector material. For the first time, we calculate their correlations within the full Glauber-Gribov theory of inelastic scattering. The AMS-02 correlations enter our spectral search for dark matter in the form of covariance matrices which we make publicly available for the cosmic-ray community. We find that the global significance of the antiproton excess is reduced to below 1 $σ$ once all systematics, including the derived AMS-02 error correlations, are taken into account. No significant preference for a dark-matter signal in the AMS-02 antiproton data is found in the mass range 10-10000 GeV.

## Key claim

The global significance of the AMS-02 antiproton excess at rigidities of 10–20 GV is reduced to below 1 sigma once all systematic errors, including the newly derived correlations in the AMS-02 systematic error, are taken into account; no significant preference for a dark-matter signal is found in the mass range 10–10000 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of the antiproton-carbon and proton-carbon absorption cross sections within the full Glauber-Gribov theory of inelastic scattering, using the multimodal nested sampling algorithm MultiNest to sample the 15- to 17-dimensional input parameter space. They then derived covariance matrices for the absorption cross-section uncertainties at the rigidity bins of AMS-02, which were combined with estimates of correlations from the effective acceptance correction and subleading systematic contributions to build the full AMS-02 covariance matrices for the antiproton flux and antiproton-to-proton ratio. Cosmic-ray fits were performed independently in two complementary cosmic-ray propagation setups.

## Key numbers

* The antiproton excess at rigidities of 10–20 GV has a global significance below 1 sigma after including all systematics and the derived AMS-02 error correlations.
* The dark-matter mass range considered in the search is 10–10000 GeV.
* The unfolding error for the antiproton flux is 1% at rigidities below 200 GV and 1.5% at 450 GV; for the antiproton-to-proton ratio it is 1% at 1 GV and 0.5% at rigidities above 2 GV.
* The nominal value for the mean square charge radius of carbon is 6.1 fm², with an estimated uncertainty of 0.44 fm².
* The asymptotic nucleon-nucleon cross section is given by 36.04 + 0.304 ln²(s/33.1 GeV²) mb.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the AMS-02 antiproton excess (Cuoco 2016, Cui 2016, Reinert 2017, Cuoco 2019, Cholis 2019, Lin 2019) by including, for the first time, a realistic estimate of correlations in the AMS-02 systematic error derived from the Glauber-Gribov theory of inelastic scattering. It contradicts earlier claims of a significant dark-matter signal by showing that the excess is consistent with systematic effects. The publicly available covariance matrices enable future cosmic-ray analyses to properly account for correlated systematic errors.
