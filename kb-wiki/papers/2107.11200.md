# Matrix Model: Emergence of a Quantum Number in the Strong Coupling Regime

> We continue here to study simple matrix models of quantum mechanical Hamiltonians. The eigenvalues and eigenfunctions were associated energy levels and wave functions. Whereas previously we considered the weak coupling limits of our models, we here address the more difficult strong coupling limits. We find that the wave functions fall into 2 classes and we can assign a quantum number to distinguish them. Implications for transition rates are also discussed.

## Key claim

In the strong coupling limit of a tridiagonal matrix model, wave functions fall into two classes distinguished by a quantum number \( s(m) = a(m,10)/a(m,0) \) that takes values \(+1\) for even \(m\) and \(-1\) for odd \(m\); this classification explains the even-odd staggering of transition amplitudes from the ground state, where transitions to odd \(m\) states are much larger than to even \(m\) states.

## Method

The authors diagonalize an 11×11 tridiagonal Hamiltonian matrix with off-diagonal coupling \(v\) and diagonal terms \(nE\) (with \(E=1\)). They study the strong coupling regime by taking \(v\) large (up to \(10^6\)) and compare results to the asymptotic limit where diagonal terms are set to zero. Transition amplitudes \(O(m)\) from the ground state to excited states are computed using the T1 operator, and first-order perturbation theory is applied treating the diagonal terms as a perturbation on the asymptotic Hamiltonian \(H_a\).

## Key numbers

* For \(v=10^4\), the ground state energy is \(E_0 = -1.9314 \times 10^4\) and the highest state energy is \(E_{10} = 1.9324 \times 10^4\).
* For \(v=10^4\), the transition amplitude from ground to first excited state is \(O(1) = 2.1611 \times 10^{-4}\), while to the second excited state it is \(O(2) = 1.0815 \times 10^{-7}\).
* For \(v=10^6\), the transition amplitude \(O(1) = 2.1611 \times 10^{-6}\) and \(O(2) = 1.0814 \times 10^{-11}\).
* The matrix element \(\langle 0|V_{\text{pert}}|1\rangle = -2.16113\) and \(\langle 0|V_{\text{pert}}|2\rangle = 0\) in the asymptotic basis.
* For large \(v\) (100 and above), increasing \(v\) by a factor of 10 decreases odd-\(m\) \(O(m)\) by about a factor of 10 and even-\(m\) \(O(m)\) by about a factor of 100.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of simple tridiagonal and pentadiagonal matrix models (Fan and Zamick, earlier works) from the weak coupling limit to the strong coupling limit. The motivation comes from realistic nuclear shell-model calculations using NushellX with the GXPF1A interaction (Kingan, Ma, and Zamick, 2020) that showed exponential decreases in B(M1) strength with excitation energy. The strong coupling results enable a classification of states by a new quantum number analogous to the change from \(J, m_J\) to \(m_l, m_s\) quantum numbers in the Zeeman effect for atomic hydrogen.
