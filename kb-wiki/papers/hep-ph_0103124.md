# Thermal versus Direct $J/Ψ$ Production in Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The production of $J/Ψ$ mesons in central collisions of heavy nuclei is investigated as a function of collision energy. Two contributions are considered simultaneously: early (hard) production coupled with subsequent suppression in a Quark-Gluon Plasma, as well as thermal recombination of primordially produced $c$ and $\bar c$ quarks at the hadronization transition. Whereas the former still constitutes the major fraction of the observed $J/Ψ$ abundance at SpS energies, the latter dominates the yield at RHIC. The resulting excitation function for the number of $J/Ψ$'s over open charm pairs exhibits nontrivial structure around $\sqrt{s} \simeq 30$ AGeV, evolving into a significant rise towards maximal RHIC energy. We study this feature within different (thermal) scenarios for $J/Ψ$ suppression, including parton-induced quasifree destruction as a novel mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that at RHIC energies, thermal recombination of charm and anticharm quarks at hadronization will dominate the observed J/Ψ yield, whereas at SPS energies the majority still comes from suppressed primordial production; the resulting excitation function for J/Ψ over open charm pairs exhibits a nontrivial minimum around √s ≃ 30 AGeV before rising significantly toward maximal RHIC energy.

## Method

The authors model central Pb-Pb (Au-Au) collisions using a thermal fireball with isentropic expansion, tracking a thermodynamic trajectory through a mixed phase at the critical temperature. Primordial J/Ψ production is calculated from hard nucleon-nucleon collisions, then subjected to nuclear absorption (Glauber model with σ_ΨN ≃ 5.8 mb) and QGP dissociation via quasifree parton scattering (g,q + J/Ψ → g,q + c + c̅) using leading-order QCD with in-medium binding energies. Thermal J/Ψ production is computed from statistical coalescence of primordial c and c̅ quarks at hadronization, enforcing exact charm conservation within a canonical-ensemble treatment.

## Key numbers

- At SPS (√s = 17.3 GeV): direct J/Ψ yield after suppression N_J/Ψ^dir = 0.45 × 10⁻³, thermal yield ⟨J/Ψ⟩ = 0.16 × 10⁻³ per central collision (N_p = 360)
- At RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): direct yield N_J/Ψ^dir = 3.25 × 10⁻³, thermal yield ⟨J/Ψ⟩ = 45.22 × 10⁻³ per central collision
- QGP suppression factor S_QGP: 0.66 at √s = 17.3 GeV, decreasing to 0.23 at √s = 200 GeV
- Charm quark fugacity γ_c: 0.82 at SPS, rising to 5.62 at full RHIC energy
- Initial fireball temperatures: T_0 ≃ 205 MeV at SPS, T_0 ≃ 390 MeV at RHIC
- Pure QGP lifetime: ∼1.5 fm/c at SPS, ∼3.5 fm/c at RHIC; mixed phase until ∼5 fm/c (SPS) and ∼7.5 fm/c (RHIC)
- J/Ψ dissociation energy in medium: E_diss(T=180 MeV) ≃ 220 MeV, dropping to ∼100 MeV at T=240 MeV

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach of Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000), who proposed statistical recombination of c and c̅ quarks at hadronization, by simultaneously including primordial J/Ψ production with QGP suppression. It contrasts with the standard suppression-only picture used to interpret NA50 data at the SPS (e.g., Matsui and Satz 1986). The predicted rise in J/Ψ yield at RHIC enables a systematic test of hadronization dynamics at the QCD phase transition, provided accurate knowledge of primordial charm production; the authors note that their description implies significant changes in J/Ψ transverse-momentum distributions, with thermal shapes expected at highest RHIC energies versus hard production at the SPS.
