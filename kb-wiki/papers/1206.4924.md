# Phase transition towards strange matter

> The phase diagram of a system constituted of neutrons and $Λ$-hyperons in thermal equilibrium is evaluated in the mean-field approximation. It is shown that this simple system exhibits a complex phase diagram with first and second order phase transitions. Due to the generic presence of attractive and repulsive couplings, the existence of phase transitions involving strangeness appears independent of the specific interaction model. In addition we will show under which conditions a phase transition towards strange matter at high density exists, which is expected to persist even within a complete treatment including all the different strange and non- strange baryon states. The impact of this transition on the composition of matter in the inner core of neutron stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a system of neutrons and Λ-hyperons exhibits a first-order phase transition towards strange matter at high density, but this transition is not model-independent; it depends on the specific form of the ΛΛ and nΛ interactions, and some models (e.g., G-matrix models without ΛΛ interaction) do not show it.

## Method

The authors model a two-component system of neutrons and Λ-hyperons using a non-relativistic mean-field approximation with an energy density functional from Balberg and Gal (1997), employing four parameter sets (BGI, BGII, BGIII, and 220g2.8). They perform a convexity analysis of the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature to identify spinodal instability regions, then apply Gibbs construction and Maxwell construction in the chemical potential ensemble to determine the phase coexistence boundaries. The analysis is extended to finite temperature by computing the Helmholtz free energy and performing the same Gibbs construction.

## Key numbers

- For parameter set (BGI), the lowest energy corresponds to a symmetric mixture ρ_n = ρ_Λ, with a bound state predicted for Λ fraction Y_Λ between 0.19 and 0.85.
- The critical temperature for the strangeness phase transition under the condition μ_n = μ_Λ is T_c = 14.8 MeV for parameter set (BGI).
- The phase coexistence region at zero temperature extends from below nuclear saturation density to high density, with the high-density transition involving a discontinuity in strangeness content.
- At zero temperature under μ_n = μ_Λ, the Λ-rich phase contains approximately 25% hyperons at the lower boundary of the coexistence region.

## Context

This work extends the study of hyperonic matter equations of state, which have been modeled by many authors including Glendenning (1982), Balberg and Gal (1997), and Oertel (2012), but typically without considering phase transitions. The finding that the opening of strangeness at high density corresponds to a first-order phase transition rather than a continuous increase contradicts the standard mean-field treatment used in many neutron star models (e.g., Massot 2012). The results enable future studies incorporating all hyperons and resonances, and suggest that neutron star and supernova matter should be modeled using Gibbs construction rather than simple mean-field equations of state.
