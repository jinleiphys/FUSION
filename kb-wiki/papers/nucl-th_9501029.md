# Double beta decay to excited states in Nd150

> The pseudo SU(3) model is used to study the double beta decay of Nd150 to the ground and excited states of Sm150 . Low lying collective excitations of Sm150 and its BE(2) intensities are well reproduced. Expressions for the two neutrino double beta decay to excited states are developed and used to describe the decay of Nd150 . The existence of selection rules which strongly restrict the decay is discussed.

## Key claim

The pseudo SU(3) model predicts that the two-neutrino double beta decay of Nd150 to the first excited 0+ state of Sm150 is forbidden by an exact selection rule, and decays to other excited states are strongly suppressed, contradicting earlier speculations that the decay to the first excited 0+ state could have similar intensity to the ground-state decay.

## Method

The pseudo SU(3) shell model was used to construct wave functions for the ground state of Nd150 and the low-lying collective states of Sm150, with occupation numbers determined from Nilsson levels at assumed deformations (β ≈ 0.28 for Nd150, β ≈ 0.19 for Sm150). Expressions for the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements to excited 0+ and 2+ states were derived using the summation method, and the nuclear matrix elements were evaluated in the restricted Hilbert space where only the highest spatial symmetry configurations and seniority-zero abnormal parity states are considered.

## Key numbers

- The predicted half-life for the two-neutrino double beta decay of Nd150 to the ground state of Sm150 is in reasonable agreement with experimental data, which vary between 9 and 17 × 10^18 years.
- The decay to the second excited 0+ state has a predicted half-life four orders of magnitude larger than that of the decay to the ground state.
- The matrix element for the decay to the first excited 2+ state, M_{2ν}(2+_{g.s.}), is three orders of magnitude smaller than the matrix element for the decay to the ground state, M_{2ν}(0+_{g.s.}).
- The energy gap between the two excited 0+ states is predicted to be 125 eV, while the experimental value is 454 eV.
- The Hamiltonian parameters fitted to reproduce the first 2+ states in Sm150 are: χ = 3.47 eV, ζ1 = 215 keV, ζ2 = 50.4 keV.
- The polarization charge used for BE(2) calculations is e_pol = 0.93 e.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo SU(3) model, previously applied to ground-state-to-ground-state double beta decays of six heavy deformed nuclei (Cas93, Cas94, Hir94), to decays into excited states of Sm150. It contradicts earlier estimates by Bar90 and Pie94, who speculated that the decay to the first excited 0+ state could have similar intensity to the ground-state decay, and differs from QRPA calculations for other nuclei such as 100Mo (Gri92, Suh94) and 116Cd (Pie94) where the matrix element to the first excited 0+ state was found to be enhanced. The results suggest that future experiments searching for double beta decay to excited states in Nd150 should expect strongly suppressed rates.
