# Inertial parameters and superfluid-to-normal phase transition in superdeformed bands

> The quasiclassically exact solution for the second inertial parameter $\cal B$ is found in self-consistent way. It is shown that superdeformation and nonuniform pairing arising from the rotation induced pair density significantly reduce this inertial parameter. The different limiting cases for $\cal B$, which allow to study an interplay between rapid rotation, pairing correlations, and mean field deformation, are considered. The new signature for the transition from pairing to normal phase is suggested in terms of the variation of ${\cal B}/{\cal A}$ versus spin. Experimental data indicate the existence of such transition in the three superdeformed mass regions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neither superdeformation nor nonuniform pairing (arising from rotation-induced pair density) is responsible for the extreme regularity of superdeformed rotational spectra; instead, the regularity is explained by a transition from the superfluid to normal phase, signaled by a characteristic variation of the ratio \( {\cal B}/{\cal A} \) with spin.

## Method

The author derived a quasiclassically exact, self-consistent expression for the second inertial parameter \( {\cal B} \) (or equivalently \( \beta \)) in the superfluid phase using a cranking expansion with gauge-invariant pairing fields, including nonuniform pairing corrections \( \Delta^{(1)} \) and \( \Delta^{(2)} \). The calculation was performed for an axially deformed oscillator potential, summing over single-particle transitions within and across oscillator shells in the Thomas-Fermi approximation. Separate expressions were obtained for the normal phase (\( \Delta = 0 \)) and for the combined effect of centrifugal stretching, and the ratio \( {\cal B}/{\cal A} \) was extracted from experimental \( \gamma \)-ray energies using formulas relating transition energies to the inertial parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the superfluid phase in the oscillator model, \( \beta_s \sim \hbar^4 (A/\varepsilon_F)^3 \), and with \( {\cal A} \sim \varepsilon_F A^{-5/3} \), the ratio \( {\cal B}/{\cal A} \sim A^{-2} \), which overestimates the minimal value in all superdeformed mass regions.
- In the normal phase, the limiting ratio for a nucleus with \( Z \) protons and \( N \) neutrons is \( {\cal B}_n/{\cal A}_n = -2.56 \, (k^4 - 10k^2 + 1) k^{2/3} / [(k^2+1)^3 A^{8/3}] \times [ (2Z/A)^{1/3} + (2N/A)^{1/3} ] \), giving values \( \sim 10^{-6} \) for the 130 and 150 mass regions.
- The convergence radius of the two-parameter formula \( {\cal B}/{\cal A} \) is of order 100 for bands in the 80 and 150 mass regions and 40 in the 130 and 190 mass regions.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of the second inertial parameter \( {\cal B} \) for normal-deformed nuclei by Marshalek (1960s) and by Ginzburg and Peierls (1960s), which were limited to monopole pairing or neglected major-shell coupling. It also builds on the quasiclassical method of Ginzburg and Peierls and on the nonuniform pairing treatment of Hamamoto and Nazarewicz (1990s). The paper provides a new signature for the superfluid-to-normal phase transition in superdeformed bands, applicable to data in the 80, 130, and 150 mass regions, and suggests a method to extract proton and neutron pairing gaps from \( \gamma \)-ray energies, though the oscillator potential overestimates the inertial parameters and a more realistic potential is needed.
