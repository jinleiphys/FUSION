# Toward the Analytic Bootstrap of Energy Correlators

> In this paper, we present a framework for the analytic bootstrap of three-point energy correlators, a crucial observable in $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills theory and quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Our approach combines spherical contour techniques, general physical constraints such as pole cancellations, and power correction data in the singular limits to determine its analytic expression. In contrast to previous bootstrap studies restricted to scattering amplitudes for supersymmetric theories, our framework makes use of the properties of Feynman integrals, marking a significant step toward bootstrapping realistic QCD observables. Using this method, we derive analytic expressions for leading-order three-point energy correlators with equal and unequal energy weights, where the latter are crucial ingredients for projected $N$-point energy correlators. We also apply the recently developed technique of analytic regression with lattice reduction as a way to bypass needing explicit expressions for the singular limits. Bridging theoretical advances in scattering amplitudes with the renewed interest in weighted cross-sections, our work opens the door to precision tests of QCD dynamics through analytic event-shape predictions.

## Key claim

The authors present a bootstrap framework for determining analytic expressions of three-point energy correlators at leading order that, unlike previous bootstrap studies restricted to scattering amplitudes in supersymmetric theories, makes use of the properties of Feynman integrals and can be applied to both \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory and quantum chromodynamics, though the method is currently demonstrated only in the homogeneous collinear limit where all angles are sent to zero simultaneously.

## Method

The authors factorize the leading-order three-point energy correlator in the triple collinear limit into a product of the Born cross-section, a collinear phase space, and tree-level \(1 \to 3\) splitting functions. They compute the leading transcendental weight of the resulting integral using spherical contour methods, which extract discontinuities and thereby the symbol of the highest-weight polylogarithms. The lower-weight remainder is then fixed by imposing physical constraints such as pole cancellations and power correction data from operator product expansion limits in the singular kinematic limits.

## Key numbers

* The collinear three-point correlator at leading order involves four final-state particles (\(m=4\) in the definition) and the \(1 \to 4\) tree-level matrix element squared.
* The \(\mathcal{N}=4\) splitting function is proportional to \(N_c^2\) and contains terms with denominators \(s_{13} s_{23}\), \(s_{12} z_3\), and their permutations.
* The collinear Gram determinant factorizes as \(\Delta_3^{\text{coll}} = (z_1 z_2 z_3)^2 \tilde{\Delta}_3\) where \(\tilde{\Delta}_3 = x_{12}^2 + x_{13}^2 + x_{23}^2 - 2x_{12}x_{13} - 2x_{13}x_{23} - 2x_{12}x_{23}\).
* The projected 4-point correlator receives contributions from energy weight combinations \(\{a,b,c\} = \{2,1,1\}, \{1,2,1\}, \{1,1,2\}\) where \(a+b+c=4\).

## Context

This work extends the amplitude bootstrap program of Dixon (2016, 2020, 2022) and Caron-Huot (2016, 2019) from scattering amplitudes to energy correlators, and also draws on the Landau bootstrap approach of Chicherin (2017), Caron-Huot (2018), and Henn (2018) for Feynman integrals. The collinear limit of the three-point correlator was previously computed in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory, quantum chromodynamics, and Higgs decays by Yan (2022), Yang (2022, 2024), and the analytic results have been used for light-ray operator product expansion analysis by Chen (2020, 2021) and for the most precise \(\alpha_s\) extraction in jet substructure by the CMS collaboration (2024). The framework enables future computation of higher-loop and higher-point energy correlators, particularly the projected \(N\)-point correlators proposed by Chen (2020) that are widely used in LHC precision studies.
