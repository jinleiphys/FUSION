# Spin contribution to the dissociation of bound states in rotating medium in magnetic field

> Magneto-rotational dissociation is the decay, by the way of tunneling, of a rotating bound state in the magnetic field. The corresponding probability was recently computed in the quasi-classical approximation using the Imaginary Time Method and was shown to increase with the angular velocity and decrease with the magnetic field strength \cite{Tuchin:2021lxl}. This letter reports the calculation of the quantum correction to the dissociation probability associated with the spin of the tunneling particle. The quasi-classical motion of spin is described by the Bargmann--Michel--Telegdi equation in the rotating frame. It is shown that the spin contribution significantly increases the dissociation probability. Applications to the Quark-Gluon Plasma are touched upon.

## Key claim

The spin contribution to the magneto-rotational dissociation probability is the leading quantum relativistic correction (order ℏ/c) to the quasi-classical result, and it significantly increases the dissociation probability, though the qualitative features of the dissociation (increase with angular velocity, decrease with magnetic field) remain essentially the same as in the prior quasi-classical calculation.

## Method

The author computes the dissociation probability of a bound state (modeled as a heavy-light meson) in a rotating medium with a constant magnetic field using the Imaginary Time Method. The classical sub-barrier trajectory of the tunneling quark is solved from the equations of motion in the rotating frame, and the spin contribution to the action is calculated by solving the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi (BMT) equation for the Pauli-Lubanski spin vector along that trajectory. The total dissociation probability is the exponential of the sum of the classical restricted action (from Tuchin (2021)) and the spin action, evaluated in the non-relativistic and quasi-classical limits.

## Key numbers

- The spin contribution to the action in the strong-field, non-rotating limit is given by \( \im S' \approx -\frac{\hbar}{2c} a^z \frac{B}{E} \left( \frac{2\e_b}{m}\right)^{1/2} \), where \( \e_b \) is the binding energy, \( a^z \) is the spin projection, and \( E \) and \( B \) are the electric and magnetic fields in the hadron comoving frame.
- The adiabaticity parameter is defined as \( \gamma = \sqrt{\frac{2\e_b}{m}} \frac{B}{E} \).
- The magnetic field must satisfy the condition \( B \ll \e_b/\mu \) for the quasi-classical approximation to hold, where \( \mu = e\hbar/2mc \) is the quark magnetic moment; this maximal field is comparable to the critical Schwinger value \( B_c = m^2c^3/e\hbar \).
- The dissociation probability is plotted as a function of \( \Omega/\omega_B \) for values of \( V = E/B \) and \( x = |e|B/m^2 \), with binding energy set to \( \e_b = mc^2/2 \) (giving \( \gamma = 1/V \)) and initial spin polarization \( a^z = 1 \).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-classical calculation of magneto-rotational dissociation by Tuchin (2021) by adding the quantum spin correction, which is the leading relativistic correction. The results apply to any rotating medium in a magnetic field, with specific motivation from Quark-Gluon Plasma phenomenology in heavy-ion collisions, where the vorticity and magnetic field are aligned. The author notes that at collision energies around \( \sqrt{s} \sim 50-130 \) GeV, the cyclotron frequency \( |\omega_B| \) is of the same order as the angular velocity \( \Omega \), and the dissociation is strongest when \( \Omega \gg |\omega_B| \), which occurs at lower collision energies. The work provides a benchmark for future phenomenological studies of the magneto-rotational effect on heavy hadrons.
