# Back-to-back correlations of boson-antiboson pairs for anisotropic expanding sources

> In the hot and dense hadronic sources formed in high energy heavy ion collisions, the particle interactions in medium might lead to a measurable back-to-back correlation (BBC) of boson-antiboson pairs. We calculate the BBC functions of $ϕ\,ϕ$ and $K^+K^-$ for anisotropic expanding sources. The dependences of the BBC on the particle momentum and source expanding velocity are investigated. The results indicate that the BBC functions increase with the magnitude of particle momentum and exhibit an obvious dependence on the direction of the momentum for the anisotropic sources. As the source expanding velocity decreases, the BBC function decreases when the particle momentum is approximately perpendicular to the source velocity, and the BBC function increases when the particle momentum is approximately parallel to the source velocity.

# Back-to-back correlations of boson-antiboson pairs for anisotropic expanding sources

## Key claim
The back-to-back correlation (BBC) functions of boson-antiboson pairs from anisotropic expanding hadronic sources depend not only on the magnitude of particle momentum but also on its direction relative to the source velocity, with the BBC function increasing when particle momentum is perpendicular to the source velocity and decreasing when momentum is parallel to the source velocity, as the source expansion velocity increases.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic Monte Carlo algorithm to calculate the BBC functions for phi-phi and K+K- pairs, extending previous spherical-source formalisms to anisotropic Gaussian sources with transverse and longitudinal radii (RT=5 fm, RL=4 fm) and linear velocity profiles parameterized by ax, ay, az. The BBC functions were computed from the chaotic and squeezed amplitudes derived from a Bogoliubov transformation between in-medium quasiparticles and free observable particles, assuming sudden freeze-out with exponential emission time distribution (Δt=2 fm/c) and fixed freeze-out temperatures (Tf=140 MeV for phi, Tf=170 MeV for K).

## Key numbers
- Source Gaussian radii: RT = 5 fm (transverse), RL = 4 fm (longitudinal)
- Freeze-out temperature for phi meson: Tf = 140 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature for K meson: Tf = 170 MeV
- Emission time distribution parameter: Δt = 2 fm/c
- BBC functions of phi-phi were calculated at m* = 1050 MeV (approximate peak position)
- BBC functions of K+K- were calculated at m* = 650 MeV (approximate peak position)
- Momentum regions for averaged BBC functions: 0 < k < 500 MeV/c and 500 < k < 1000 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous BBC calculations for spherical hadronic sources by Padula (2006, 2010) and Zhang (2014) to the more realistic case of anisotropic expanding sources formed in high-energy heavy ion collisions. The momentum-direction dependence of the BBC function for anisotropic sources provides additional signals for experimental detection at RHIC and LHC, complementing the earlier spherical-source formalisms that lacked such directional sensitivity.
